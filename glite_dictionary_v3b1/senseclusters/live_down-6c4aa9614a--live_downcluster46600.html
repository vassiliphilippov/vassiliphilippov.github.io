live down

verb
make people forget a previous mistake or shame
Pronunciation: / lɪv daʊn / (AmE), / lɪv daʊn / (BrE)
to behave or continue to live so that people eventually forget or forgive a past mistake, failure, or embarrassment

Examples

To live down a mistake
After making that embarrassing speech, he knew he might never live down the incident.
It took years before the scandal finally started to live down in the public's memory.
She worked hard to live down her early mistakes at the company.
No matter how hard he tried, Mark could never live down his embarrassing fall at the wedding.
She hopes that volunteering in the community will help her live down her past mistakes.
Their company is still living down the scandal from several years ago.

  • synonym: get over - to recover from an unpleasant experience or emotion
  • synonym: outlive - to live longer than something or someone
