comparative

adjective
relating to comparison
1.6
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpær.ə.tɪv /; / kəmˈpæɹ.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpær.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or based on comparison between two or more things; considered in relation to something else rather than absolutely

Examples

Comparative study
He wrote a comparative analysis of French and Spanish grammar.
In English, adjectives like 'bigger' and 'smarter' are in the comparative form.
The comparative study of different cultures can reveal surprising similarities.
After the accident, he returned to a comparative state of health, though not fully recovered.
Living in the countryside offered them a comparative sense of peace compared to the city.
The report discussed the comparative merits of the two proposals without drawing firm conclusions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolute - not dependent on or referring to anything else; not comparative
  • synonym: relative - considered in relation or in proportion to something else
