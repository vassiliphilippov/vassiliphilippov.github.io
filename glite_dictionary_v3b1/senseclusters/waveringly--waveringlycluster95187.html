waveringly

adverb
in an unsteady way
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈweɪ.vər.ɪŋ.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈweɪ.vər.ɪŋ.lɪ/ (BrE)
in an unsteady, trembling, or uncertain way, showing hesitation or lack of firmness (physically or emotionally)

Examples

To speak waveringly
She spoke waveringly, her voice betraying her nervousness in front of the audience.
The candle's flame flickered waveringly in the cold night breeze.
He reached out waveringly, unsure if he should take the offered hand.
The candle burned waveringly as the wind blew through the open window.
Her voice sounded waveringly as she tried to explain what had happened.
The signal appeared waveringly on the monitor, sometimes strong and sometimes faint.
He answered waveringly, not sure if he was making the right decision.
The candlelight flickered waveringly in the cold night breeze.
She looked at him waveringly, torn between hope and doubt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firmly - in a strong, steady, or confident way
  • antonym: steadily - in a way that is regular, constant, or unchanging
  • synonym: uncertainly - in a way that shows lack of confidence or sureness
  • synonym: unsteadily - in a way that lacks firmness or stability
