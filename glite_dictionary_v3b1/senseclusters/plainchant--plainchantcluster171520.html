plainchant

noun
unaccompanied liturgical chant
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪnˌtʃænt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌtʃɑːnt / (BrE)
a style of liturgical music featuring a single, unaccompanied melodic line, especially as used in the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

A plainchant melody
Many early religious compositions were based on the structure of plainchant.
The ethereal sound of plainchant echoed through the ancient cathedral.
The monks gathered in the chapel to sing plainchant during the morning service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gregorian chant - a form of plainchant used in the Western Church
