precontact

adjective
before cultural contact
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈkɑːntækt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkɒntækt / (BrE)
relating to the time before two or more groups or cultures first met or interacted

Examples

Precontact cultures
The museum displays artifacts from the precontact era of the region's history.
Researchers analyzed precontact languages to understand how communities communicated before outside influence.
Many traditions from the precontact period have been preserved by local elders.
The museum features precontact pottery made by indigenous peoples before European arrival.
Researchers study precontact societies to understand their traditions and way of life.
Many precontact languages have been lost due to colonization and cultural change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postcontact - relating to the period after two or more groups first met
  • synonym: precolonial - relating to the period before colonization
