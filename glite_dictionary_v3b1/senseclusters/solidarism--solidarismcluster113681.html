solidarism

noun
social solidarity theory
8.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈlɪd.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /sə.ˈlɪd.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief, theory, or practice that emphasizes social solidarity, mutual support, and cooperation among members of a group or society

Examples

Solidarism in society
The government adopted policies inspired by solidarism to promote cooperation among citizens.
Many social reformers in history have supported solidarism as a way to reduce inequality.
Debates about solidarism often arise when discussing the balance between community and individual rights.
The philosophy of solidarism encourages people to work together for the common good.
Many social movements are inspired by the principles of solidarism and collective responsibility.
During difficult times, solidarism can strengthen the bonds within a community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collectivism - a system that prioritizes the group over the individual
  • antonym: individualism - a belief in the importance of individual rights and independence
  • synonym: solidarity - unity or agreement of feeling or action among individuals with a common interest
