horizonless

adjective
without event horizon
Pronunciation: / həˈraɪzənləs / (AmE), / həˈraɪzənləs / (BrE)
lacking an event horizon, especially in reference to a compact object in astrophysics that does not have a boundary beyond which nothing can escape

Examples

Horizonless black hole
Some scientists study horizonless objects to better understand the nature of gravity.
A horizonless compact object would not trap light in the same way as a black hole.
The theory suggests that certain stars could collapse into horizonless remnants instead of black holes.
