angle of incidence

noun
angle between incoming line and perpendicular to surface
5.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈæŋ.gəl əv ˈɪn.sɪ.dəns/ /; / /ˈæŋ.gəl əv ˈɪn.sə.dəns/ (AmE), /ˈæŋ.gəl əv ˈɪn.sɪ.dəns/ /; / /ˈæŋ.gəl əv ˈɪn.sə.dəns/ (BrE)
the angle that a ray, beam, or line makes with a line perpendicular to the surface at the point where it meets the surface

Examples

The angle of incidence on wing
Pilots adjust the angle of incidence on an aircraft's wing for optimal lift.
The angle of incidence affects how much light is reflected from the surface.
When the angle of incidence increases, the intensity of the reflected ray often changes.
