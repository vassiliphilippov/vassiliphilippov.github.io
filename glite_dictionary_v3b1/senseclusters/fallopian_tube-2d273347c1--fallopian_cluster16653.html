Fallopian tube

noun
tube connecting ovary to uterus
5.5 0.68/m
Pronunciation: / f…ôÀàlo äpi…ôn tuÀêb / (AmE), / f…ôÀàl…ô äpi…ôn tjuÀêb / (BrE)
either of a pair of slender tubes in the female reproductive system that transport eggs from the ovary to the uterus

Examples

The Fallopian tube transports eggs
An egg released by the ovary travels through the Fallopian tube toward the uterus.
Blockage of a Fallopian tube can affect a woman's fertility.
During ovulation, fertilization usually occurs within the Fallopian tube.

Synonyms

  • uterine tube
    4.6 0.00/m
    uterine tube is the scientific name used in medicine for the same organ, while Fallopian tube is the common everyday name.
  • oviduct
    8.1 0.05/m
    oviduct names the egg-carrying tube in many female animals, sometimes opening outside the body, while Fallopian tube refers to the same tube in mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oviduct - a tube through which an ovum or egg passes from an ovary
  • synonym: uterine tube - another term for Fallopian tube
