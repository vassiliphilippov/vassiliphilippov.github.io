Gulf of Aegina

proper noun
gulf of the Aegean Sea in Greece
Pronunciation: / ɡʌlf əv əˈdʒinə / (AmE), / ɡʌlf əv iˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
A gulf of the Aegean Sea on the southeastern coast of Greece, lying between the Attica peninsula and the island of Aegina.

Examples

The Gulf of Aegina
Fishermen often set out early in the morning onto the Gulf of Aegina to catch fresh seafood.
Several ancient Greek cities were established along the shores of the Gulf of Aegina.
The Gulf of Aegina is a popular sailing destination due to its sheltered waters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Saronic Gulf - another name for the Gulf of Aegina, an inlet of the Aegean Sea
