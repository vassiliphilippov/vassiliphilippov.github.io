narrow-leaved strap fern

noun
fern species with narrow leaves
Pronunciation: / ˌnæroʊ livd ˈstræp fɝn / (AmE), / ˌnærəʊ liːvd ˈstræp fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of fern with shorter and narrower leaves than the Florida strap fern, found from Florida to the West Indies, Mexico, and south to Argentina.

Examples

The narrow-leaved strap fern plant
Gardeners appreciate the delicate look of the narrow-leaved strap fern in subtropical landscapes.
The narrow-leaved strap fern is often found growing in shaded hammocks across southern Florida.
Unlike its broad-leaved relative, the narrow-leaved strap fern has much slimmer fronds.
