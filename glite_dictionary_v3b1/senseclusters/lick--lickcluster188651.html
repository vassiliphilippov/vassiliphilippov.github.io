lick

verb
defeat thoroughly (informal)
Pronunciation: / lɪk / (AmE), / lɪk / (BrE)
(informal) to defeat someone thoroughly in a competition, fight, or argument.

Examples

To lick the other team
Our basketball team licked the defending champions in the finals.
The experienced lawyer easily licked his opponent in court.
They were determined not to be licked in the rematch.
