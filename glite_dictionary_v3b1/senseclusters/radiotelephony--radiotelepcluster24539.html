radiotelephony

noun
voice transmission by radio waves
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊtəˈlɛfəni / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊtəˈlɛfəni / (BrE)
the transmission of speech by radio waves instead of by wire, especially for communication over distance

Examples

Radiotelephony communication
Before the widespread use of satellites, ships used radiotelephony to keep in contact with shore.
Modern air traffic control relies heavily on radiotelephony to coordinate aircraft movement.
The military adopted radiotelephony for secure and rapid field communication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radio communication - communication by transmission over radio waves
  • antonym: wired telephony - transmission of speech over wires (traditional telephony)
