epithetic

adjective
added ending sound
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
describing a sound or element added to the end of a word, often to make pronunciation easier

Examples

Epithetic vowel ending
Some dialects use an epithetic vowel at the end of words to make them easier to pronounce.
The addition of an epithetic consonant can help avoid awkward word endings in speech.
Linguists study epithetic elements to understand how languages adapt to pronunciation challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epenthetic - describing a sound inserted within a word
