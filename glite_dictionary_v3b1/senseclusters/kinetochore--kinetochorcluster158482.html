kinetochore

noun
protein structure on chromosomes for spindle attachment
Pronunciation: / kɪˈnɛtəˌkɔr / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtəkɔː / (BrE)
a protein structure forming on the centromere of a chromosome during cell division, to which spindle fibers attach

Examples

The kinetochore protein
A defect in the kinetochore can cause errors in chromosome separation.
Scientists have studied the proteins that assemble at the kinetochore during cell division.
When chromosomes divide during mitosis, spindle fibers attach to the kinetochore for proper segregation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centromere - region of chromosome where kinetochore forms and sister chromatids are joined
