troublemaking

adjective
causing problems
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌbəlˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəlˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
causing problems, mischief, or disturbances, especially deliberately.

Examples

Troublemaking kids
The teacher quickly separated the troublemaking students before the situation got worse.
His troublemaking behavior often led to arguments with his classmates.
The manager warned that any troublemaking employees would face disciplinary action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disruptive - causing interruption or disorder
  • synonym: mischievous - causing or showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful way
  • antonym: well-behaved - behaving in a polite or proper way
