electrooptic

adjective
electric and light interaction
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈɑptɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈɒptɪk / (BrE)
relating to the interaction between electric fields and light in materials

Examples

Electrooptic effect crystal
The electrooptic effect allows certain crystals to change their optical properties when an electric field is applied.
Modern fiber optic communication systems often use electrooptic modulators to control light signals.
Researchers are developing new electrooptic materials for faster and more efficient data transmission.

Related Terms

  • synonym: optoelectronic - relating to devices that use both light and electronics
