beak

noun
beaklike mouthpart of non-bird animals
Pronunciation: / bik / (AmE), / biːk / (BrE)
the hard, beaklike mouthpart of animals other than birds, such as turtles or some mollusks

Examples

A turtle's beak
A squid's beak is strong enough to break through shells.
Some mollusks use their sharp beak to tear food apart.
The snapping turtle has a powerful beak to catch its prey.
