preinstall

noun
software installed in advance
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪnˈstɔl / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪnˈstɔːl / (BrE)
software or applications that are installed on a device before it is sold or used, typically by the manufacturer

Examples

Default preinstalls
The laptop came with several preinstalls that took up a lot of storage space.
Many users delete unnecessary preinstalls to free up memory on their new devices.
Manufacturers often include security apps as preinstalls to protect users from the start.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloatware - unwanted software pre-installed on a device
  • antonym: uninstall - to remove software from a device
