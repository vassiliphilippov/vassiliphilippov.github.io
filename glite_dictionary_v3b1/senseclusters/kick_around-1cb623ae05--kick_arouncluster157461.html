kick around

verb
treat badly or unfairly
2.4
Pronunciation: / kɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to treat someone badly or unfairly, often repeatedly; to abuse or mistreat

Examples

To kick around a person
After years of being kicked around at work, he decided to stand up for himself.
I won't let them kick around my little brother anymore.
She felt as though her coworkers only wanted to kick around someone weaker than them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abuse - to use in a way that is harmful or morally wrong; treat cruelly
  • antonym: cherish - to care for and protect lovingly
  • synonym: mistreat - to treat someone badly or cruelly
