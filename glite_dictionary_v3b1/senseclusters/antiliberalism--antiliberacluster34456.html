antiliberalism

noun
opposition to free-market policies
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈlɪbərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈlɪbərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
opposition to economic liberalism, especially to policies that support free markets, deregulation, privatization, or free trade

Examples

Economic antiliberalism
The rise of antiliberalism has influenced many countries to adopt more restrictive trade policies.
Debates about antiliberalism often center on the role of government in regulating the economy.
Some political movements promote antiliberalism as a response to globalization and economic inequality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberalism - a political and economic philosophy supporting free markets and individual freedoms
  • synonym: protectionism - the policy of protecting domestic industries from foreign competition
