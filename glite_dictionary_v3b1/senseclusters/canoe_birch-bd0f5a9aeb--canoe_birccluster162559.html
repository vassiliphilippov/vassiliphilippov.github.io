canoe birch

noun
North American birch tree with white bark
Pronunciation: / kəˈnuː bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / kəˈnuː bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A North American birch tree with white, peeling bark, commonly used by indigenous peoples for making canoes, baskets, and other items.

Examples

The canoe birch tree
In early spring, sap from the canoe birch can be collected much like that of the sugar maple.
Native Americans used the bark of the canoe birch to build lightweight canoes and containers.
The smooth, white bark of the canoe birch peels in sheets and is often used in traditional crafts.
