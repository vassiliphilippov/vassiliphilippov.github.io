fenster

noun
geological window
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛnstər / (AmE), / ˈfɛnstə / (BrE)
an area of younger rock exposed and surrounded by older rocks, typically formed by erosion or faulting

Examples

Geological fenster
The geologist identified a fenster where younger rocks were visible through the older formation.
A fenster can reveal important information about the geological history of a region.
During the field trip, students observed a large fenster surrounded by ancient rocks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: klippe - an isolated block of rock surrounded by younger rocks
  • synonym: window - an opening that allows something to be seen through
