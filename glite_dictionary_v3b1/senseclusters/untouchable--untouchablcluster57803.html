untouchable

adjective
beyond criticism or attack
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtʌtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃəbl / (BrE)
impossible to criticize, attack, defeat, or harm; immune to negative judgment or action

Examples

Untouchable leader
For years, the company president seemed untouchable, no matter how unpopular his decisions were.
Many believed the star athlete was untouchable until investigations uncovered evidence of wrongdoing.
The senator was once untouchable, but a recent scandal damaged his reputation.
After winning twenty games in a row, the team seemed untouchable to their rivals.
For many years, the company was untouchable in the tech industry due to its innovations.
The governor's popularity made her almost untouchable during the election campaign.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage
  • synonym: unassailable - impossible to attack, defeat, or criticize
  • antonym: vulnerable - open to harm, criticism, or attack
