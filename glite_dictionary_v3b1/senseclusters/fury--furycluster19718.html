fury

noun
violent or turbulent force
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfjʊri / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəri / (BrE)
violent or intense force, especially wild or turbulent energy, as in storms or emotions

Examples

The fury of a storm
She spoke with such fury that everyone in the room fell silent.
The fury of the hurricane left the entire town in ruins.
Waves crashed against the rocks in a display of nature’s fury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - the absence of disturbance or violence
  • synonym: violence - the use of physical force or intensity
