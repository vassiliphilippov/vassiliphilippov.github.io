Pick's disease

noun
progressive dementia affecting frontal and temporal lobes
9.4
Pronunciation: / pɪks dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / pɪks dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare progressive form of dementia, typically affecting middle-aged and elderly people, marked by degeneration of the frontal and temporal lobes and resulting in loss of intellectual ability and language problems.

Examples

Pick's disease symptoms
Her grandmother was diagnosed with Pick's disease after displaying changes in personality and speech difficulties.
Progressive degeneration in the frontal lobe is a hallmark of Pick's disease.
Researchers are studying the genetic factors that contribute to Pick's disease and other forms of dementia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frontotemporal dementia - a group of disorders caused by progressive nerve cell loss in the brain's frontal lobes or its temporal lobes
