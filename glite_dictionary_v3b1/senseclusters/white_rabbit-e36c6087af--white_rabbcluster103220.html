white rabbit

noun
a person who is always late; a symbol for adventure into the unknown
7.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /waɪt ˈræ.bɪt/ /; / /hwaɪt ˈræ.bɪt/ (AmE), /waɪt ˈræ.bɪt/ (BrE)
A person who is always late, or a symbol of embarking on an adventure into the unknown; a reference to the White Rabbit character from Lewis Carroll's 'Alice's Adventures in Wonderland.'

Examples

The white rabbit character
He rushed into the meeting like a white rabbit, apologizing for his tardiness.
She joked that she was the family's white rabbit, always lagging behind on their outings.
The novel uses the white rabbit as a metaphor for stepping into new and uncertain experiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latecomer - a person who habitually arrives late
