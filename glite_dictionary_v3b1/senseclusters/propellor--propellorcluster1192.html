propellor

noun
rotating device that generates thrust
7.8 3.9
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɛlər / (AmE), / prəˈpɛlə / (BrE)
A mechanical device with blades that rotates to push against air or water, generating thrust to move a vehicle such as a boat or aircraft.

Examples

An airplane propellor
An airplane's propellor spins rapidly during takeoff and landing.
He carefully inspected the propellor before starting the engine.
The ship's propellor became tangled in seaweed, causing it to stop moving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propeller - another spelling; device that provides thrust via rotation
