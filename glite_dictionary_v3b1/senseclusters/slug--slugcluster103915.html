slug

noun
a gulp or portion of liquor
Pronunciation: / slʌɡ / (AmE), / slʌɡ / (BrE)
a small amount of an alcoholic drink, especially one that is poured or quickly swallowed in one go

Examples

A slug of whiskey
He poured himself a generous slug of whiskey and sat down to relax.
She took a quick slug from her flask before stepping outside.
With a slug of rum, he tried to steady his nerves.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: sip - a small mouthful of liquid
  • synonym: swig - a large or hurried swallow of drink
