prying

adjective
offensively curious; intrusive
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) showing too much interest in other people's private affairs, especially in a way that is annoying or offensive.

Examples

Prying eyes
I caught a prying glance from the man across the street as I entered my house.
Journalists can sometimes be prying, digging into details best left private.
She avoided her prying relatives who always asked personal questions about her life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nosy - showing too much interest in other people's affairs
