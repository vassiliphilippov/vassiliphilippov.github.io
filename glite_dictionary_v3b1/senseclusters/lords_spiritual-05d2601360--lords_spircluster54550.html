Lords Spiritual

noun
Church of England bishops in the House of Lords
Pronunciation: / lɔrdz ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / lɔːdz ˈspɪrɪtʃuːəl / (BrE)
the bishops of the Church of England who have seats in the House of Lords as spiritual representatives

Examples

Lords Spiritual in Parliament
Debates in the House of Lords often include contributions from the Lords Spiritual on ethical issues.
Historically, the Lords Spiritual have influenced decisions regarding education and social welfare policies.
Twenty-six Lords Spiritual currently sit in the House of Lords alongside the Lords Temporal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Lords Temporal - members of the House of Lords who are not bishops, i.e., secular peers
