steam coal

noun
coal used for steam boilers
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: / stiːm koʊl / (AmE), / stiːm kəʊl / (BrE)
A type of coal suitable for burning in steam boilers to generate steam power.

Examples

Steam coal for boilers
Historically, steam coal fueled locomotives throughout the heyday of rail travel.
Many power plants rely on steam coal to operate their steam boilers efficiently.
The price of steam coal has fluctuated with changes in global energy demand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bituminous coal - a type of coal less hard than anthracite, often used for power generation
