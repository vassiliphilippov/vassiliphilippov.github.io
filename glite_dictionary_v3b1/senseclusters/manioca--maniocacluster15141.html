manioca

noun
starch from cassava root, staple food
8.0 6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmæniˈoʊkə / (AmE), / ˌmaniˈəʊkə / (BrE)
A starchy substance prepared from the root of the cassava plant, used as a staple food in tropical regions and as the source of tapioca.

Examples

The manioca root
Some cultures use manioca as their principal source of carbohydrates.
The chef thickened the soup with manioca for a unique, smooth texture.
You can substitute manioca flour for traditional wheat flour in gluten-free recipes.
