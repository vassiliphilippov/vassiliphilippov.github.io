antidumping

noun
trade import tax
6.0
Pronunciation: /æn.tiː.ˈdʌm.pɪŋ/ /; / /æn.taɪ.ˈdʌm.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈdʌm.pɪŋ/ /; / /æn.taɪ.ˈdʌm.pɪŋ/ (BrE)
a tax or official measure imposed on imported goods to prevent them from being sold below fair market value

Examples

Antidumping duties
The government imposed antidumping duties to protect local steel producers from unfair competition.
Many countries use antidumping laws to prevent foreign companies from selling products at very low prices.
The company faced an antidumping investigation after exporting goods below market value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subsidy - government financial support to help an industry
  • synonym: tariff - a tax on imported or exported goods
