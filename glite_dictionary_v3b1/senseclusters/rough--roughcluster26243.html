rough

adjective
unpleasantly harsh or severe
1.9
Pronunciation: / rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
harsh, severe, or difficult; causing or experiencing hardship or adversity

Examples

Rough times
His childhood was rough, marked by strict discipline and little comfort.
Living in the desert can be a rough experience for those not used to harsh conditions.
The coach's rough attitude motivated the players but also intimidated them.
It was a rough year for many small businesses.
She's been going through a rough patch since she lost her job.
They had a rough time adjusting to life in a new country.
It's best not to go sailing during rough weather.
The captain warned us that the rough seas would make for a difficult crossing.
The ferry ride was canceled due to rough conditions on the water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - (of weather or sea) not disturbed by wind or waves; peaceful
  • synonym: difficult - not easy; requiring much effort
  • antonym: easy - not difficult; achieved without great effort
  • antonym: gentle - kind, soft, and mild in manner or effect
  • synonym: hard - not easy; involving a lot of effort or pain
  • synonym: harsh - extremely unpleasant, severe, or cruel; stern
  • synonym: stormy - (of weather) characterized by strong winds and heavy rain
