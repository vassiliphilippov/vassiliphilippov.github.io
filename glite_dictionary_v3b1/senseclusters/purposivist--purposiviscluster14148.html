purposivist

noun
believer in purpose-based explanation
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɜːr.pə.ˈsɪ.vɪst/ /; / /pɜːr.pə.ˈsaɪ.vɪst/ (AmE), /pɜː.pə.ˈsɪ.vɪst/ /; / /pɜː.pə.ˈsaɪ.vɪst/ (BrE)
a person who interprets or explains things (especially laws or texts) by focusing on their purpose or intended goal

Examples

A purposivist judge
The judge, a well-known purposivist, interpreted the law based on its intended purpose.
As a purposivist, she believed that animal behaviors are best understood by their goals.
Many philosophers debate whether a purposivist approach is valid in scientific explanations.
The Supreme Court justice was considered a leading purposivist in her approach to statutory interpretation.
As a purposivist, he argued that the law should be read in light of its broader objectives.
Legal scholars often debate whether a purposivist or textualist method leads to fairer outcomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mechanist - a person who explains phenomena by physical or mechanical causes, not purposes
  • synonym: teleologist - a person who explains phenomena by their ends or purposes
  • antonym: textualist - a person who interprets laws or texts based strictly on the wording
