short-term

adjective
lasting a short period
3.3 21/m
Pronunciation: / Àà É…îrt t…úrm / (AmE), / Àà É…îÀêt t…úÀêm / (BrE)
lasting or intended to last for only a brief or limited period of time.

Examples

A short-term loan
She decided to take a short-term loan to cover her unexpected expenses.
The company hired additional workers for a short-term project that would last only two months.
The government implemented short-term measures to address the immediate economic crisis.

Synonyms

  • temporary
    3.0 35/m
    temporary lasts for a limited time and is common in everyday speech, while short-term labels things not meant to last long.
  • non-permanent
    3.8 0.00/m
    non-permanent describes something not intended to last long, while short-term often refers to a brief planned period or effect.
  • short-run
    4.1 3.99/m
    short-run refers to a limited time frame, often in economics, while short-term covers brief situations in many areas.
  • short-lived
    3.9 4.88/m
    short-lived lasts only a short period and often describes objects or fame, while short-term means not lasting long generally.
  • transient
    7.0 4.07/m
    transient is more formal and describes people or states that pass quickly, while short-term is more general and neutral in tone.
  • fleeting
    6.3 4.61/m
    fleeting passes very quickly and feels sudden, while short-term can mean a longer but still limited period.
  • momentary
    4.8 3.87/m
    momentary lasts for a single very short moment and feels instant, while short-term can cover days or weeks, not just moments.
