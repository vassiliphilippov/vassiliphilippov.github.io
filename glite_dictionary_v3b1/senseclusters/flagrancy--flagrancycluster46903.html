flagrancy

noun
blatant wrongdoing
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪɡrənsi / (AmE), / ˈfleɪɡrənsi / (BrE)
the quality or act of being openly and shockingly wrong, offensive, or blatant

Examples

The flagrancy of foul
The referee could not ignore the flagrancy of the foul committed during the match.
Such flagrancy in breaking the law shocked the entire community.
The manager was fired for the flagrancy of his repeated violations of company policy.
The flagrancy of the violation shocked everyone in the courtroom.
His flagrancy in breaking the rules could not be ignored by the officials.
The flagrancy of her disregard for the law led to public outrage.

  • antonym: innocence - the state of being free from wrongdoing
  • synonym: misconduct - improper or illegal behavior
  • synonym: outrage - a shocking or morally offensive act
  • antonym: subtlety - the quality of being delicate or not obvious
