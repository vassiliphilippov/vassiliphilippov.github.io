spread-eagle

verb
stand or lie with arms and legs spread out
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsprɛd ˌiɡəl /; / ˈsprɛd ˌiːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɛd ˌiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
to stand, lie, or position oneself or someone else with arms and legs stretched out wide and apart

Examples

To spread-eagle yourself
He spread-eagled himself on the grass, staring up at the sky.
Police ordered the suspect to spread-eagle against the wall for a search.
The gymnast spread-eagled on the mat to begin her routine.
He spread-eagled his arms and legs to show how much space he could cover.
She was spread-eagled on the beach towel, soaking up the sun.
The guards spread-eagled the suspect on the ground before handcuffing him.

Related Terms

  • synonym: splay - to spread or extend (things, especially limbs or fingers) outward or apart
