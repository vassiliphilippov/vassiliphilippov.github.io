esotropia

noun
eyes turning inward (medical)
6.4 8.7
Pronunciation: /iː.sə.ˈtroʊ.pi.ə/ /; / /ɪ.sə.ˈtroʊ.pi.ə/ (AmE), /iː.sə.ˈtrəʊ.pi.ə/ /; / /ɪ.sə.ˈtrəʊ.pi.ə/ (BrE)
a form of strabismus in which one or both eyes turn inward toward the nose

Examples

Esotropia eye condition
Children with esotropia may require corrective lenses or surgery to improve their vision alignment.
Early detection of esotropia is important to prevent long-term vision problems in children.
The doctor diagnosed her with esotropia when he noticed her left eye turning toward her nose.
