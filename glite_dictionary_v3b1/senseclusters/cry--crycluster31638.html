cry

verb
to utter a characteristic sound
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / kraɪ / (AmE), / kraɪ / (BrE)
to make a loud sound, shout, or call, often to express emotion or to communicate

Examples

To cry for help
At dawn, the rooster cries to greet the morning.
The baby cried loudly for its mother.
The injured animal was crying in pain near the bushes.
He would often cry 'Watch out!' before something unexpected happened.
If you see smoke, cry for help immediately so others can hear you.
She couldn't believe the news and began to cry out his name in joy.
He cried aloud as the heavy box fell on his foot.
She cried out in pain when she touched the hot stove.
The children cried for help when they got lost in the woods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: call - to utter a sound or voice to attract attention or communicate
  • synonym: exclaim - to speak suddenly and loudly, often expressing surprise or emotion
  • degree higher: scream - to make a loud, high sound with the voice
  • synonym: shout - to speak or call out loudly, often to be heard at a distance
  • degree lower: whimper - to make a series of low, feeble sounds
  • synonym: yell - to shout loudly, especially when in pain, angry, or excited
