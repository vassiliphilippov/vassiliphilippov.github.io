water hole

noun
natural hollow filled with water
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə həʊl / (BrE)
a natural hollow or depression in the ground where water collects, often serving as a drinking place for animals

Examples

A water hole in ground
During the drought, the water holes dried up, forcing animals to migrate elsewhere.
Lions often wait near the water hole for their prey to appear.
The elephants gathered at the water hole to drink after the long, dry day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pond - a small body of still water, typically smaller than a lake
