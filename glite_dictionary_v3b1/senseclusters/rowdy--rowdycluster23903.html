rowdy

noun
noisy troublemaker
4.0 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆraÊŠdi / (AmE), / ËˆraÊŠdi / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a noisy, rough, or disruptive way, especially in a group

Examples

A rowdy at a party
The rowdy was escorted out of the stadium after starting a fight in the stands.
A group of rowdies disrupted the quiet evening with loud music and shouting.
Teachers struggled to control the rowdy during the school assembly.

Synonyms

  • hooligan
    6.3 1.01/m
    a hooligan is often violent and causes public trouble, while a rowdy is simply noisy and disruptive in a group.
  • rollicker
    8.2 0.01/m
    a rollicker is lively and boisterous at parties, while a rowdy is rougher and more likely to cause real disruption.

Related Terms
