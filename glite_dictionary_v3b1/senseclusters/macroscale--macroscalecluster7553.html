macroscale

noun
large observable scale
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmækroʊˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmakrəʊskeɪl / (BrE)
a scale large enough to be seen or measured directly, as opposed to microscopic or molecular levels

Examples

At the macroscale
The researchers observed the chemical reaction at the macroscale to study its visible effects.
Unlike microscopic analysis, this study focuses on changes occurring at the macroscale.
Engineers often design structures by considering forces acting on the macroscale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microscale - a scale so small it can only be seen with a microscope
  • antonym: nanoscale - a scale measured in nanometers, extremely small
