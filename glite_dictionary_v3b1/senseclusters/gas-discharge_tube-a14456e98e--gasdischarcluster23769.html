gas-discharge tube

noun
sealed tube with electric discharge in gas
Pronunciation: / ɡæs dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ tuːb / (AmE), / ɡas dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ tjuːb / (BrE)
a sealed tube in which an electric discharge passes through a gas, often used in lighting, electronics, or scientific applications

Examples

A gas-discharge tube light
One of the most common uses of a gas-discharge tube is in fluorescent lighting systems.
Scientists studied the behavior of electrons inside a gas-discharge tube.
The old sign was illuminated using a gas-discharge tube filled with neon.
