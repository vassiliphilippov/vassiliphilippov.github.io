undrive

verb
to remove driven object
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈdraɪv / (AmE), / ʌnˈdraɪv / (BrE)
to remove something that has been driven in, such as a nail or stake, by pulling it out

Examples

To undrive a nail
They had to undrive the old nails before repairing the wooden fence.
To recycle the wood, she carefully undrove each screw from the planks.
The workers used a special tool to undrive the metal stakes from the ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extract - to pull something out, especially with effort
  • antonym: insert - to put something into something else
