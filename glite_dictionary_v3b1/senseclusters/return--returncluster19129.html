return

verb
deliver a verdict or judgment formally

to formally announce or submit something in an official or legal context, such as a verdict, election, or document

Examples

To return a verdict
After careful consideration, the panel returned its decision late Friday evening.
The judge returned judgment in favor of the defendant.
The jury returned a guilty verdict after several hours of deliberation.
She hopes the voters will return her to office for a second term.
The popular candidate was returned to Parliament by a large majority.
The region has returned the same party to power for the past decade.
After careful review, the governor returned the proposal to the legislature for further amendment.
The auditor will return her report to the board by the end of the month.
The committee was required to return its findings to the council within two weeks.

Synonyms

  • bring in
    6.0 0.34/m
    bring in is used when a jury officially announces a verdict in court, while return is a formal legal submission.
  • deliver
    2.6 41/m
    deliver is to present or hand an official decision to a group or court, while return means to formally announce or submit it.
  • render
    3.9 4.92/m
    render is to give or provide something formally, often as a service or payment, while return means to officially announce or submit a decision.
