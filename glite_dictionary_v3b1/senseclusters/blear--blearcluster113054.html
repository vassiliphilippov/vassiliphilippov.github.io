blear

verb
make dim or indistinct
Pronunciation: / blɪr / (AmE), / blɪə / (BrE)
to make something (especially vision or outlines) dim, blurred, or less clear

Examples

To blear the view
Cigarette smoke began to blear the outlines of the figures in the room.
Our memories of that event are bleared by the passage of time.
The long night had bleared his tired eyes.
The smoke from the campfire bleared her eyes, making it hard to see.
After crying for hours, his vision was bleared and unfocused.
Long hours at the computer bleared my eyesight by the end of the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - make or become unclear or less distinct
  • antonym: clear - to make something easy to see or understand
  • antonym: sharpen - make or become clear or more distinct
