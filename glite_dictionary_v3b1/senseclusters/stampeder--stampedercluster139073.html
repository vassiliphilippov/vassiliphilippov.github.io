stampeder

noun
instigator of stampede
8.8
Pronunciation: /stæm.ˈpiː.dər/ (AmE), /stæm.ˈpiː.də/ (BrE)
a person or animal that causes others to rush forward in a stampede, often by starting or provoking it

Examples

A stampeder starts panic
The frightened cow became the stampeder, causing the rest of the herd to bolt across the field.
During the gold rush, every stampeder hoped to be the first to find riches.
The sudden noise turned the horse into a stampeder, setting off chaos among the animals.
