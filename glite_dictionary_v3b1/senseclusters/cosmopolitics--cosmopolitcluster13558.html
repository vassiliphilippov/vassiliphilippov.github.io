cosmopolitics

noun
politics including nonhumans
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑzməpəˈlɪtɪks / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməpəˈlɪtɪks / (BrE)
a theoretical approach to politics that considers nonhuman entities, such as animals, plants, or technologies, as participants in political processes

Examples

Cosmopolitics with animals
The philosopher argued that cosmopolitics challenges traditional ideas about who can participate in political life.
Recent debates in environmental ethics often reference cosmopolitics to include animals and ecosystems as stakeholders.
Her research explores how cosmopolitics can reshape our understanding of democracy and responsibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anthropocentrism - the belief that humans are the central or most important entities
  • synonym: ecopolitics - political theory focusing on ecological or environmental issues
