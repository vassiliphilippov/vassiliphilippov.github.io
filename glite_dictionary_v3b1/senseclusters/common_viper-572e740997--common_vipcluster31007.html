common viper

noun
a venomous snake of northern Eurasia (Vipera berus)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmən ˈvaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈvaɪpə / (BrE)
a small venomous snake (Vipera berus) found throughout northern Eurasia, known for its zigzag pattern and mildly poisonous bite

Examples

The common viper snake
Despite its venom, the bite of a common viper is rarely fatal to humans.
The common viper is the only venomous snake native to Britain.
We spotted a common viper basking on a rock during our hike in Sweden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adder - another name for the common viper in Britain
