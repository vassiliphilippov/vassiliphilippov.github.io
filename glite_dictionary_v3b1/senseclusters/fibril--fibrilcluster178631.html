fibril

noun
a slender fiber or threadlike structure
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbrɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪbrɪl / (BrE)
a very slender threadlike fiber, especially found in biological tissues or as a synthetic material

Examples

Muscle fibrils
Muscle cells contain many fibrils that are essential for contraction.
Scientists have developed a new fibril made from synthetic polymers for use in medicine.
Under the microscope, each fibril was clearly visible in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiber - a thread or filament forming a tissue or material
