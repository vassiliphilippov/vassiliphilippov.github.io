Kodiak

noun
large brown bear of Alaska and British Columbia
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊdiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdiːˌæk / (BrE)
a large subspecies of brown bear native to the coastal regions of Alaska and British Columbia, especially Kodiak Island

Examples

Kodiak bear
A zoo recently welcomed a new Kodiak as part of its bear exhibit.
The Kodiak is among the largest bears in the world by size and weight.
While hiking in Alaska, they spotted a massive Kodiak fishing for salmon.
