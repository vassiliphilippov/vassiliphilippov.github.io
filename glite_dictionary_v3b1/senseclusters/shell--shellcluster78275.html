shell

verb
to hit a pitcher hard and often (baseball)
9.5
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl / (AmE), / ʃɛl / (BrE)
(of a baseball team or player) to hit a pitcher's deliveries hard and frequently, often resulting in many runs scored.

Examples

Fans cheered wildly as the lineup shelled the visiting team for ten runs.
The home team shelled their rival's ace pitcher early in the game.
