trustless

adjective
lacking trust
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌstləs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌstləs / (BrE)
lacking trust, confidence, or reliability; not deserving of trust

Examples

A trustless relationship
After the scandal, meetings became trustless, with colleagues avoiding honest conversations.
A trustless environment can make teamwork and collaboration very difficult.
The negotiations failed because both sides remained trustless and unwilling to compromise.
After several broken promises, his friends considered him trustless.
A trustless employee can damage the reputation of an entire company.
She learned to avoid trustless people after being deceived multiple times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distrustful - not trusting; lacking trust in others
  • antonym: reliable - able to be trusted or depended on
  • synonym: suspicious - feeling or showing distrust of someone or something
  • antonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful
  • synonym: untrustworthy - not able to be relied on as honest or truthful
