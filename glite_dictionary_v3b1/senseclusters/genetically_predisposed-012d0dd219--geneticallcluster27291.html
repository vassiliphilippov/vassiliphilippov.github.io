genetically predisposed

adjective
having an inherited tendency; prone by genetics
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪkli priːdɪsˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛtɪkli priːdɪsˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
Having an inherited tendency to develop certain conditions or diseases.

Examples

To be genetically predisposed
Being genetically predisposed does not guarantee you will develop the disease.
Scientists study how people are genetically predisposed to certain mental health conditions.
Some individuals are genetically predisposed to high blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predisposed - having a tendency or inclination toward something
