sharply

adverb
suddenly or steeply
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑːrpli / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːpli / (BrE)
suddenly or intensely, with a quick or noticeable change in direction, degree, or amount

Examples

Prices rise sharply
Temperatures dropped sharply overnight, leading to frost in the morning.
The car turned sharply to avoid the obstacle.
The road bends sharply to the left after the bridge.
After the announcement, the company's stock price fell sharply.
The number of applicants has increased sharply over the past year.
Traffic accidents rise sharply during periods of heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abruptly - suddenly and unexpectedly
  • antonym: gently - in a mild or gradual way
  • antonym: gradually - slowly, over a long period of time
