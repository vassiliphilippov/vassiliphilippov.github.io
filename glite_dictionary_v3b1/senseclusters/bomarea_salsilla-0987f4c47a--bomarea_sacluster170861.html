Bomarea salsilla

noun
tropical vine with edible roots, Colombia
Pronunciation: / boʊˈmɑːriə sælˈsɪlə / (AmE), / bəʊˈmɑːriə sælˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A tropical vine native to Colombia, producing umbels of small purple flowers and edible roots that are sometimes cooked as a potato substitute.

Examples

Bomarea salsilla vine
Bomarea salsilla thrives in tropical environments and is valued for both its flowers and edible tubers.
Botanists admire the striking purple umbels produced by Bomarea salsilla.
The roots of Bomarea salsilla are sometimes cooked and eaten as a potato substitute in Colombia.
