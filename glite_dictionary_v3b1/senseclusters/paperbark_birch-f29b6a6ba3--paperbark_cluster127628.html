paperbark birch

noun
North American birch tree with peeling white bark
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪpərˌbɑrk bɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəbɑːk bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a small North American birch tree (Betula papyrifera) with white, peeling bark often used for making baskets and canoes

Examples

Paperbark birch tree
A stand of paperbark birch trees brightened the forest with their pale, papery trunks.
Native Americans traditionally used paperbark birch bark to craft canoes and containers.
The paperbark birch is easily recognized by its striking white bark that peels away in thin layers.
