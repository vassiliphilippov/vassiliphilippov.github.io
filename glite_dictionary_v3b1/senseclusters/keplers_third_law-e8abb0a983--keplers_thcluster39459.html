Kepler's third law

noun
planetary law relating period and orbit size
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkep.lərz θɜːrd lɑː/ /; / /ˈkep.lərz θɜːrd lɔː/ (AmE), /ˈkep.ləz θɜːd lɔː/ (BrE)
the law in astronomy stating that for all planets, the square of the period of revolution is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis of its orbit

Examples

Kepler's third law in astronomy
According to Kepler's third law, the more distant a planet is from the Sun, the longer it takes to orbit it.
Astronomers confirmed Kepler's third law by measuring planetary orbits in our solar system.
Students used Kepler's third law to calculate the orbital period of Mars.
