wirephoto

noun
electronically sent photo
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪərˌfoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəˌfəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
a photograph sent electronically over telegraph or telephone lines, especially for news publication or reporting

Examples

A wirephoto of news
The editor received a wirephoto of the disaster just in time for the morning edition.
Before digital cameras, newspapers relied on wirephotos to get images from distant locations quickly.
