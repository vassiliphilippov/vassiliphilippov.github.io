counter-reform

verb
to reverse reforms
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntər rɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntə rɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to oppose, undo, or reverse reforms by restoring previous policies or practices

Examples

To counter-reform education
The government decided to counter-reform the education system, bringing back older teaching methods.
After the election, the party began to counter-reform many of the previous administration's policies.
Some leaders threatened to counter-reform the new environmental regulations to favor traditional industries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve something
  • synonym: repeal - to officially cancel a law or regulation
  • synonym: reverse - to change something back to its previous state
