law of closure

noun
Gestalt psychology principle: perceiving incomplete figures as complete
7.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / l…î …ôv Ààklo ä í…ö / (AmE), / l…îÀê …ôv Ààkl…ô ä í…ô / (BrE)
In Gestalt psychology, the principle that people tend to perceive incomplete figures as complete by mentally filling in gaps or missing information.

Examples

The law of closure principle
Designers often rely on the law of closure to create visually appealing graphics that suggest rather than depict full shapes.
The law of closure explains why we see a complete shape even when parts are missing.
When observing a dotted circle, the law of closure leads us to perceive it as a whole.
