dispersal

noun
the act of spreading or scattering
8.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈspɜːr.səl/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈspɜː.səl/ (BrE)
the act of spreading things or people over a wide area; the process of being scattered or distributed.

Examples

Rapid dispersal of information
Modern technology has led to the rapid dispersal of information worldwide.
Seed dispersal by the wind is common among many plant species.
The dispersal of protesters happened peacefully after the demonstration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentration - the action or state of being brought together tightly in one place
  • synonym: dispersion - the process or result of being spread out or scattered
