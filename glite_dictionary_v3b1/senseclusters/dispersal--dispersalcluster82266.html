dispersal

noun
the act of spreading or scattering
5.2 2.04/m
Pronunciation: / d…™Ààsp…ùÀês…ôl / (AmE), / d…™Ààsp…úÀês…ôl / (BrE)
the act of spreading things or people over a wide area; the process of being scattered or distributed.

Examples

Rapid dispersal of information
Modern technology has led to the rapid dispersal of information worldwide.
Seed dispersal by the wind is common among many plant species.
The dispersal of protesters happened peacefully after the demonstration.

Synonyms

  • dispersion
    6.5 1.11/m
    dispersion means spreading or scattering things over an area, while dispersal stresses movement outward from a central place.
  • scattering
    4.2 0.27/m
    scattering is the act of breaking into small parts and spreading them, while dispersal often refers to moving people or things far apart.
  • distribution
    3.0 18/m
    distribution shows how things are placed or shared among places or people, while dispersal focuses on scattering items widely and quickly.
  • spreading
    2.3 0.02/m
    spreading is a general process of extending or increasing over an area, while dispersal implies scattering from a point or group.
  • diffusion
    5.8 2.30/m
    diffusion describes a slow, natural movement of substances or ideas through space, while dispersal often means active scattering outward.
  • spread
    2.5 9.56/m
    spread can mean the result of something reaching many places over time, while dispersal stresses the act of scattering outward.
