bug-out

verb
to protrude suddenly
8.2
Pronunciation: / bʌɡ aʊt / (AmE), / bʌɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to stick out or bulge, especially of the eyes, usually because of surprise or shock

Examples

Eyes bug-out
Her eyes would bug-out whenever she heard shocking news.
The children watched the magician and their eyes began to bug-out in amazement.
He was so surprised that his eyes seemed to bug-out of his head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulge - to swell or stick out
  • antonym: recede - to move back or become less prominent
