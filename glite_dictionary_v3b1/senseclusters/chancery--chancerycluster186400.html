chancery

noun
a court of equity
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.sə.ri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːn.sər.i / (BrE)
a court of equity, historically responsible for administering justice according to principles of fairness, distinct from courts of common law

Examples

Chancery court case
He studied the procedures unique to chancery during his legal training.
Historic cases of trusts and estates were often decided by chancery.
The dispute was settled in chancery rather than in a court of law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: court of law - a court that applies statutes and legal precedents rather than principles of equity
  • synonym: equity court - a court that decides cases on principles of equity
