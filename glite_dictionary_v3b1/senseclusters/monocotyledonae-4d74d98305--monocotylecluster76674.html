Monocotyledonae

noun
class of plants with one seed leaf
9.8 9.3
Pronunciation: /mɑː.nə.kɑː.tɪ.ˈliː.niː/ /; / /mɑː.nə.kɑː.tə.ˈliː.niː/ (AmE), /mɒ.nə.kɒt.ɪ.ˈliː.niː/ /; / /mɒ.nə.kɒt.ə.ˈliː.niː/ (BrE)
a large class of flowering plants (monocots) whose seeds have a single cotyledon (embryonic leaf), including grasses, lilies, orchids, and palms

Examples

The Monocotyledonae plants
Many staple crops, such as rice and wheat, belong to the Monocotyledonae.
Palm trees are classic examples of plants classified under the Monocotyledonae.
Unlike dicotyledons, members of the Monocotyledonae typically have parallel leaf veins.
Most grasses and some common garden plants, such as lilies, belong to the Monocotyledonae.
Orchids are one of the largest families within the Monocotyledonae.
The structure of their leaves helps to distinguish Monocotyledonae from other classes of flowering plants.
