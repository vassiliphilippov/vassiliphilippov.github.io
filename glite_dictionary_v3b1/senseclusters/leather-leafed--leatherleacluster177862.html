leather-leafed

adjective
having thick, leathery leaves
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛðɚ ˌliːft / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə ˌliːft / (BrE)
(of a plant) having leaves which are thick, tough, and leathery in texture.

Examples

Leather-leafed plants
Many evergreens are known for their leather-leafed foliage, which helps prevent water loss.
The leather-leafed shrubs in the garden are well-adapted to dry climates.
This species is easily identified by its leather-leafed appearance and robust stems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leathery - having a tough, thick texture like leather (especially of leaves)
