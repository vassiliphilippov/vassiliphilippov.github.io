upthrust

noun
upward force in fluid
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌθrʌst / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌθrʌst / (BrE)
an upward force that pushes an object up, especially in water, air, or earth

Examples

Water upthrust
The upthrust from the water kept the boat afloat during the storm.
A helium balloon rises because the upthrust from the air is greater than its weight.
Divers must consider upthrust when calculating how much weight to carry underwater.
The upthrust of the water kept the boat afloat during the storm.
Scientists measured the upthrust acting on the rock submerged in the tank.
Without enough upthrust, the object quickly sank to the bottom of the pool.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buoyancy - the ability of an object to float in water or air
  • antonym: gravity - the force that pulls objects toward the center of the earth
