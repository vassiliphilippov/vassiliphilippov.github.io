expedience

noun
practical advantage, often regardless of ethics
8.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspiː.dɪ.əns/ /; / /e.k.ˈspiː.dɪ.əns/ (AmE), /e.k.ˈspiː.dɪ.əns/ /; / /ɪk.ˈspiː.dɪ.əns/ (BrE)
the quality of being convenient and practical, even if it may not be morally right

Examples

Practical expedience
His decision was guided more by expedience than by moral principle.
In politics, expedience sometimes triumphs over honesty and integrity.

Sometimes, political decisions are made based on expedience rather than principle.
The committee chose expedience over tradition in order to meet the deadline.
