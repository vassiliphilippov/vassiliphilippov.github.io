archeological

adjective
relating to archaeology
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrkioʊˈlɑːdʒɪkəl /; / ˌɑr.ki.əˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiəˈlɒdʒɪkəl /; / ˌɑː.ki.əˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the study of human history and prehistory through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts and other physical remains.

Examples

An archeological survey
Experts launched an archeological survey before the construction project began.
Her archeological research uncovered new information about the lost city.
The museum houses many archeological artifacts from ancient civilizations.
Recent archaeological findings have shed new light on the city's history.
She is pursuing an archaeological career and hopes to work on excavations in Egypt.
The team made an archaeological survey of the ancient site before construction began.
