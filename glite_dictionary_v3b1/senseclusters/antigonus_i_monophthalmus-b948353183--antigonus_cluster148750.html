Antigonus I Monophthalmus

proper noun
general and king in Ancient Macedonia
9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈtɪɡ.ə.nəs ðə fɜːrst mɑː.nəf.ˈθæl.məs/ (AmE), /æn.ˈtɪɡ.ə.nəs ðə fɜːst mɒ.nəf.ˈθæl.məs/ (BrE)
(c. 382–301 BC) A Macedonian general under Alexander the Great who later became king of Macedonia; known for losing one eye and for being killed at the Battle of Ipsus.
