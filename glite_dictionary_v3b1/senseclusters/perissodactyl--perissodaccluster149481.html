perissodactyl

noun
odd-toed hoofed mammal (e.g., horse, rhino)
5.6 9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.rɪs.ə.ˈdæk.təl/ /; / /pə.rɪs.ə.ˈdæk.tɪl/ (AmE), /pə.rɪs.ə.ˈdæk.təl/ /; / /pə.rɪs.ə.ˈdæk.tɪl/ (BrE)
a hoofed placental mammal with an odd number of toes on each foot, belonging to the order Perissodactyla, such as horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs

Examples

A perissodactyl animal

Rhinoceroses and tapirs are both modern perissodactyls with unique skeletal features.
The horse is a classic example of a perissodactyl, having a single hoof on each foot.
