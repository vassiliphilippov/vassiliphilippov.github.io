metabolism

noun
life-sustaining chemical processes
Pronunciation: / məˈtæbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈtæbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The physical and chemical processes in an organism by which food is converted into energy and waste products, and which are necessary for maintaining life.

Examples

Metabolism rate
All living organisms rely on metabolism to convert nutrients into energy.
Regular exercise can help speed up your metabolism and increase energy levels.
The doctor explained how a slow metabolism might contribute to weight gain.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anabolism - the synthesis phase of metabolism
  • degree lower: catabolism - the breakdown phase of metabolism
