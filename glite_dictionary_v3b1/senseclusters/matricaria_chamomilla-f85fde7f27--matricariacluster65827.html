Matricaria chamomilla

noun
Eurasian herb (German chamomile) used in herbal medicine
8.3
Pronunciation: /mæ.trɪ.ˈkær.ɪə kə.məˈmɪl.ə/ /; / /mæ.trɪ.ˈkær.ɪə kæ.məˈmɪl.ə/ (AmE), /mæ.trɪ.ˈkær.ɪə kə.məˈmɪl.ə/ /; / /mæ.trɪ.ˈkær.ɪə kæ.məˈmɪl.ə/ (BrE)
A Eurasian annual herb, also known as German chamomile, used for its fragrant flowers in herbal medicine and teas, though its taste is more bitter and its effects considered inferior to true chamomile.

Examples

Matricaria chamomilla herbal tea
Many herbal teas use Matricaria chamomilla as a calming ingredient, although its flavor is distinctly bitter.
The flowers of Matricaria chamomilla are gathered each summer for their medicinal properties.
While Matricaria chamomilla is often called chamomile, some people prefer the milder taste of the Roman variety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chamaemelum nobile - species known as 'Roman chamomile', related plant with milder taste
  • synonym: chamomile - common name for similar medicinal plants
