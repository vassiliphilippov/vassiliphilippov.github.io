herpangia

noun
viral infection with mouth ulcers
Pronunciation: / hɜrˈpændʒiə / (AmE), / hɜːˈpandʒɪə / (BrE)
a viral infection, especially in children, causing sore throat, fever, and small ulcers in the mouth and throat

Examples

Herpangia infection
Most cases of herpangia resolve within a week without complications.
Symptoms of herpangia often include fever, sore throat, and painful ulcers in the throat.
The pediatrician diagnosed the child with herpangia after examining the sores in his mouth.
