cancer drug

noun
drug used in cancer treatment
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.sɚ drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.sə drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug used to treat cancer, typically by controlling or destroying cancerous cells, often as part of chemotherapy

Examples

Cancer drug treatment
Many patients experience hair loss as a side effect of their cancer drug treatments.
Researchers are working to develop a cancer drug with fewer side effects.
The doctor prescribed a new cancer drug that targets specific tumor cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemotherapy - the treatment of cancer with drugs that destroy cancer cells
