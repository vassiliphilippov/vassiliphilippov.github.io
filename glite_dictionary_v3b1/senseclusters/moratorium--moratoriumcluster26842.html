moratorium

noun
temporary suspension or pause
6.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /mɔːr.ə.ˈtɔːr.iː.əm/ /; / /mɔːr.ə.ˈtɔːr.ɪ.əm/ (AmE), /mɒ.rə.ˈtɔː.rɪ.əm/ /; / /mɔː.rə.ˈtɔː.rɪ.əm/ (BrE)
an official or legal temporary stopping or suspension of an activity or obligation

Examples

A moratorium on operations
After the accident, the nuclear plant announced a moratorium on operations for safety checks.
Many activists called for a moratorium on evictions during the economic crisis.
The government imposed a moratorium on logging to protect the endangered forest.
During the recession, several banks announced a moratorium on home foreclosures.
The government imposed a moratorium on new construction to protect the wetlands.
The university called for a moratorium on tuition increases while reviewing its budget.
