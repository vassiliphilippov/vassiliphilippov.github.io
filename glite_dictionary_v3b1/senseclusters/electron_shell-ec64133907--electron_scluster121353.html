electron shell

noun
grouping of electrons around an atom's nucleus
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛk.trɑn ʃɛl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn ʃɛl / (BrE)
a grouping of electrons in an atom that surround the nucleus at a similar energy level

Examples

Electron shell energy level
Electrons in the same electron shell have similar energy levels.
The first electron shell can hold up to two electrons in total.
When the outermost electron shell is full, the atom is chemically stable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electron orbital - region of space where an electron is likely to be found
  • degree higher: valence shell - outermost electron shell of an atom
