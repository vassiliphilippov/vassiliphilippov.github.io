devolutionary

adjective
causing decline
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌdiːvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to or causing a decline, degeneration, or reversal in development or complexity.

Examples

Devolutionary changes in health
Some scientists argue that certain diseases have a devolutionary effect on the human body.
The collapse of infrastructure was seen as a devolutionary process by many experts.
Critics described the new law as a devolutionary step that could reverse years of progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evolutionary - relating to gradual development or advancement
