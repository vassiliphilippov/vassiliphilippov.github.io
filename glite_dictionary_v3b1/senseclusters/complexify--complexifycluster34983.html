complexify

verb
make or become more complex
5.6
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛksɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛksɪˌfaɪ /; / kəmˈplɛksɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become more complex; to increase the complexity of something

Examples

To complexify a problem
Attempts to solve one problem may sometimes complexify another.
New regulations will only complexify the tax process.
Their interactions tended to complexify the project rather than simplify it.
Instead of solving the problem, his suggestions seemed to complexify it further.
She tends to complexify simple tasks by adding unnecessary steps.
The new regulations only complexify the process instead of making it simpler.
