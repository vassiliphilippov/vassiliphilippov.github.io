glottal catch

noun
a stop sound made by closing the glottis
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɑtəl kætʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒtəl kætʃ / (BrE)
A type of stop consonant produced by closing and then rapidly opening the vocal cords (glottis), creating a sudden sound, as in the abrupt onset of a vowel in some English dialects.

Examples

A glottal catch sound
Phonetics classes often teach students how to articulate a glottal catch correctly.
Some English accents use a glottal catch instead of the 't' sound in 'bottle'.
The glottal catch can be heard at the beginning of the word 'apple' in some dialects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glottal stop - a stop consonant made by blocking airflow at the glottis
