nonadditive

adjective
not additive in effect
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈædətɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈædətɪv / (BrE)
not involving simple addition or not producing a combined effect equal to the sum of individual effects

Examples

Nonadditive effects
The researchers found that the drug interactions were nonadditive and did not simply sum up their effects.
In genetics, some traits show nonadditive inheritance patterns due to complex gene interactions.
The combined impact of the two policies was nonadditive, leading to unexpected results.
