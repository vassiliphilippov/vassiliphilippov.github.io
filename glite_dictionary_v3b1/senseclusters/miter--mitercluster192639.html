miter

noun
beveled end surface for a joint
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə / (BrE)
a joint or beveled surface made by cutting two pieces (usually wood or metal) at an angle so they fit together, commonly used in carpentry

Examples

Miter joint
A clean and precise miter is essential for strong and attractive joints.
Before gluing the frame, he carefully checked the miter for a perfect fit.
She sanded each miter to ensure the corners would meet perfectly.
A flawed miter can result in a gap at the corner of the finished product.
He learned how to make a miter joint in his woodworking class.
The picture frame was constructed with perfectly cut miter joints at each corner.
Always check that the mitre is perfectly cut to ensure a tight joint.
Before attaching the pieces, he applied glue to the mitre for a secure bond.
Excess glue can squeeze out along the mitre when clamping the frame together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bevel - an edge cut at an angle other than a right angle
  • antonym: butt joint - a joint made by placing two pieces of wood end to end without any special shaping
  • synonym: miter joint - a joint made by joining two beveled ends, usually at a right angle
