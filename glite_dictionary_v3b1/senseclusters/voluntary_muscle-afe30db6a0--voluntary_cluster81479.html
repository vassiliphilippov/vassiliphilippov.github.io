voluntary muscle

noun
muscle under conscious control
1.1 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːlənˌtɛri ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ən.tər.i ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
A muscle that is under conscious control, typically referring to skeletal muscle as opposed to involuntary muscles.

Examples

Voluntary muscles control
Because the biceps are voluntary muscles, you can flex your arm whenever you choose.
Skeletal muscles are a type of voluntary muscle that move the bones of the body.
Unlike the heart, which is involuntary, voluntary muscle action requires conscious effort.

Related Terms

  • antonym: involuntary muscle - muscle that cannot be controlled consciously
  • synonym: skeletal muscle - muscle attached to skeleton; under voluntary control
