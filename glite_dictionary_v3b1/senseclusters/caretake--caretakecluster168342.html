caretake

verb
to act as caretaker
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌteɪk / (BrE)
to act as a caretaker by looking after a person, property, or responsibility temporarily

Examples

To caretake a house
She was hired to caretake the house while the family traveled abroad.
During the summer, he will caretake the school grounds until classes resume.
They needed someone trustworthy to caretake their pets during the holiday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: look after - to take care of someone or something
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly
  • synonym: supervise - to watch over and direct someone or something
