actual eviction

noun
physical removal of tenant from property
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tʃu.əl ɪˈvɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃu.əl ɪˈvɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
the physical removal of a tenant from a rented property by the landlord or through legal action, after which the tenant is no longer required to pay rent

Examples

An actual eviction notice
After an actual eviction, the tenant is no longer responsible for paying rent for the property.
The court ordered the actual eviction of the tenant due to multiple violations of the lease agreement.
When a landlord changes the locks and denies entry, this is considered an actual eviction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructive eviction - when a landlord's actions or neglect force a tenant to leave, even if not physically removed
