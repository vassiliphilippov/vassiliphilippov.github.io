nonspecific

adjective
not caused by a particular agent
4.2
Pronunciation: / nɑn.spəˈsɪf.ɪk / (AmE), / nɒn.spəˈsɪf.ɪk / (BrE)
Not caused by or attributable to a particular or identified agent or factor; not definite or specific.

Examples

A nonspecific change
Some laboratory tests reveal nonspecific changes that don't point to a single disease.
The doctor said the patient had a nonspecific infection that could not be traced to one cause.
This medication is used for the treatment of nonspecific symptoms such as general pain or fatigue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: specific - clearly defined or identified; relating to a particular agent or condition
