Synonyms

  • unnecessary
    1.6 14/m
    unnecessary is a common, neutral word for something not needed, while redundant often implies extra and sometimes repeats information.
  • unneeded
    2.3 0.3/m
    unneeded is a simple everyday adjective for something not required, while redundant often suggests extra parts or repeated items.
  • superfluous
    7.0 1/m
    superfluous sounds formal and suggests needless excess, while redundant often focuses on being extra or repeating material.
  • surplus to requirements
    6.1 0.01/m
    surplus to requirements is a British phrase for items no longer needed, while redundant names extra things that serve no purpose.
  • supernumerary
    9.0 0.02/m
    supernumerary is a formal term for people or items in excess, while redundant is used more commonly for extra or repeated things.
  • surplus
    3.4 0.07/m
    surplus means extra quantity or stock, while redundant describes parts or roles that are not needed and are wasted.
  • nonessential
    5.6 0.05/m
    nonessential is used for things not needed for a main purpose, while redundant often points to needless or repeated elements.
  • inessential
    5.3 0.03/m
    inessential means not necessary and often minor, while redundant suggests extra material or roles that add no value.
  • unessential

  • duplicative
