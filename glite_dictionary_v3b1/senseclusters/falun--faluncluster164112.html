falun

noun
shell-rich rock
Pronunciation: / faˈlʌn / (AmE), / faˈlœ̃ / (BrE)
a type of sedimentary rock composed mainly of shell fragments, found especially in France

Examples

French falun rock
The geologist collected samples of falun from the coastal cliffs in western France.
Layers of falun are often rich in fossilized shells and marine remains.
Many old buildings in the region were constructed using blocks of local falun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate
  • antonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock formed from sand-sized mineral particles
