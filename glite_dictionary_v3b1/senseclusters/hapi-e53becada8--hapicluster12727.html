Hapi

proper noun
Egyptian funerary deity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpi / (BrE)
in ancient Egyptian religion, one of the four sons of Horus, a funerary deity who protected the lungs of the deceased

Examples

Hapi protects lungs
Ancient Egyptians believed that Hapi watched over the lungs of the dead during mummification.
Priests placed canopic jars dedicated to Hapi inside the tombs of pharaohs.
The image of Hapi often appears on ancient Egyptian burial artifacts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Duamutef - another son of Horus, protector of the stomach in Egyptian mythology
  • synonym: Horus - an important ancient Egyptian god, often depicted as a falcon
  • synonym: Imsety - another son of Horus, protector of the liver in Egyptian mythology
  • synonym: Qebehsenuef - another son of Horus, protector of the intestines in Egyptian mythology
