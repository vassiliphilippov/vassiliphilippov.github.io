overmatch

verb
to be too strong
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈmætʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈmætʃ / (BrE)
to be much stronger or more capable than an opponent, especially in a contest or competition

He realized he was overmatched and decided to withdraw from the competition.

Despite their efforts, the smaller team was overmatched by the defending champions.
He hoped his experience would help him overmatch his rival in the debate.
The experienced chess player easily overmatched his younger rival in the tournament.
Despite their efforts, the team was overmatched by the defending champions.
She knew she could overmatch any competitor in the spelling bee with her preparation.
