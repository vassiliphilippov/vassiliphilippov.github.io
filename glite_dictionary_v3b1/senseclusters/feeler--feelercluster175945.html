feeler

noun
tentative suggestion to test reactions
4.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfiː.lər/ (AmE), /ˈfiː.lə/ (BrE)
a tentative suggestion or a way of testing people's reactions or feelings; the ability to sense subtle information or signals

Examples

Drop a feeler
Before making any decision, he dropped a feeler to test how the team might react.
She put out a feeler about organizing a joint project, but got little response.
The company sent out a feeler to see if there was interest in a merger.
His political feeler tells him that public opinion is shifting.
She seems to have a remarkable feeler for people’s true intentions.
Writers often rely on a well-developed feeler for cultural trends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity or awareness to subtle cues
  • synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively, without conscious reasoning
  • synonym: overture - an approach or proposal made to initiate discussion or action
  • synonym: proposal - a plan or suggestion put forward for consideration
