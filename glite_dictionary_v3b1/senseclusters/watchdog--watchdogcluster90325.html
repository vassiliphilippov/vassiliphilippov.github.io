watchdog

verb
to monitor closely
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑtʃˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌdɒɡ / (BrE)
to monitor or supervise someone or something closely to ensure proper conduct or detect problems

Examples

To watchdog elections
Several organizations are watchdogging the election process to ensure fairness.
The committee has been watchdogging the company's financial practices for years.
Local residents started watchdogging the construction project after safety concerns were raised.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
  • synonym: monitor - to observe and check the progress or quality of something over a period of time
  • synonym: oversee - to supervise or watch over an activity or person
