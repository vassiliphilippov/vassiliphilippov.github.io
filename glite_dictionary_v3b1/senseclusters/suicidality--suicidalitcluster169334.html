suicidality

noun
risk of suicide
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːɪsɪˈdæləti /; / ˌsuːɪsaɪˈdæləti / (AmE), / ˌsuːɪsɪˈdælɪti /; / ˌsuːɪsaɪˈdælɪti / (BrE)
the condition or risk of having thoughts, plans, or behaviors related to ending one's own life, including suicide attempts

Examples

Suicidality risk assessment
The therapist assessed the patient's suicidality during the initial consultation.
High levels of stress and isolation can increase suicidality among young adults.
Doctors are trained to recognize signs of suicidality in their patients.
Mental health professionals assess suicidality to determine the level of care a patient needs.
Open conversations about suicidality can help reduce stigma and encourage people to seek help.
The study found a strong link between social isolation and increased suicidality among teenagers.
