Flaviviridae

noun
family of viruses transmitted by arthropods
Pronunciation: / fleɪˌvaɪvəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / flaˌvɪvɪˈriːdɪi / (BrE)
a family of viruses carried by arthropods such as ticks and mosquitoes, known to cause diseases in humans and animals

Examples

Flaviviridae virus family
Dengue virus is a well-known member of the Flaviviridae family, responsible for millions of infections worldwide.
Many important human pathogens, including the Flaviviridae family, are transmitted by mosquitoes.
Researchers are studying the genetic makeup of Flaviviridae to develop more effective vaccines.
