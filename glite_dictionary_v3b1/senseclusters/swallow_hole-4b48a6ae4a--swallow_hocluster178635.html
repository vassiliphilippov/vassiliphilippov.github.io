swallow hole

noun
natural depression leading to underground passage
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑloʊ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ həʊl / (BrE)
A depression or hole in the ground that leads to a subterranean passage, typically found in limestone areas and formed by the dissolution of rock or the collapse of a cavern roof.

Examples

A swallow hole in limestone
Explorers discovered a vast network of caves beneath the swallow hole in the field.
Heavy rain caused water to gush into the swallow hole, disappearing underground within minutes.
The landscape is dotted with swallow holes formed over thousands of years in the limestone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sinkhole - a cavity in the ground caused by water erosion, often similar to a swallow hole
