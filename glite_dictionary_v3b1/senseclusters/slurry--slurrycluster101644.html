slurry

verb
to mix with liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈslɜːri / (AmE), / ˈslʌri / (BrE)
to mix a solid substance with liquid to create a thick, muddy mixture

Examples

To slurry the clay
The workers slurried the clay before using it to make bricks.
To prepare the mixture, she slurried the powder with water until smooth.
Engineers often slurry soil to stabilize it during construction projects.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dilute - to make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another liquid
  • degree higher: thicken - to make a liquid more dense or viscous
