khandha

noun
Buddhist aggregate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌndə / (AmE), / ˈkʌndə / (BrE)
in Buddhism, one of the five aggregates or components that constitute a sentient being's existence

Examples

The five khandhas
The Buddha taught that understanding the five khandhas is essential to realizing the nature of self.
Each khandha represents a different aspect of human experience in Buddhist philosophy.
Meditation practices often focus on observing the khandhas to gain insight into impermanence.
