unfilmable

adjective
impossible to film
7.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfɪlməbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪlməbəl / (BrE)
too difficult or impossible to adapt into a movie or film successfully

Examples

Unfilmable novel
The author believed her complex novel was unfilmable due to its shifting perspectives.
Some stories are considered unfilmable because of their abstract or internal nature.
Despite being labeled unfilmable, the book was eventually turned into a successful movie.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adaptable - able to be changed or modified for a different use or format
  • degree higher: cinematic - suitable for being made into a film
