self-assertive

adjective
confidently forceful; assertive
1.0 5.6
Pronunciation: /self.ə.ˈsɜːr.tɪv/ (AmE), /self.ə.ˈsɜː.tɪv/ (BrE)
confidently and forcefully expressing your own opinions or desires, sometimes in an aggressive or unwelcome way

Examples

A self-assertive child
A self-assertive child may challenge authority more frequently than a shy one.
He adopted a self-assertive tone during the debate, refusing to back down.
Her self-assertive attitude often made her stand out in group discussions.
Her self-assertive attitude sometimes comes across as aggressive in team meetings.
Some admired his self-assertive nature, while others found it a bit overbearing.
The self-assertive child challenged every rule the teacher made.
