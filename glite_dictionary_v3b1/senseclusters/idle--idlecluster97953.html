idle

noun
state of a running engine at minimal speed
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
the state of an engine or other machine running without load and at low speed, especially while not in use

Examples

Engine idle
After starting the car, let it run at idle for a minute.
He adjusted the idle to ensure the lawnmower didn't stall.
The mechanic listened to the engine's idle to check for any unusual noises.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idling - operation of an engine without load or propulsion
