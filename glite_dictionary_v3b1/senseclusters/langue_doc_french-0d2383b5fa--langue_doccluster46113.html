Langue d'oc French

noun
medieval southern French dialects
9.9
Pronunciation: /lɑːŋg dɑːk frentʃ/ /; / /læŋg dɑːk frentʃ/ (AmE), /lɑːŋg dɒk frentʃ/ /; / /læŋg dɒk frentʃ/ (BrE)
The group of medieval dialects of French formerly spoken in southern France, distinct from northern dialects by their use of 'oc' for 'yes'.

Examples

Langue d'oc French dialects
Linguists study Langue d'oc French to understand the evolution of Romance languages.
Many medieval legal documents from southern France were written in Langue d'oc French.
The poetry of the troubadours was originally composed in Langue d'oc French.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Langue d'oïl - medieval northern French dialects, precursor to modern standard French
  • synonym: Occitan - Romance language descended from Langue d'oc spoken in southern France
