externalism

noun
philosophical theory
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɜrnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical belief that factors outside the mind can determine mental states or knowledge

Examples

Philosophical externalism
In debates about the mind, externalism challenges the idea that thoughts are only shaped by internal factors.
Many cognitive scientists support externalism when discussing how people gain knowledge from their environment.
The professor explained externalism as a key position in the philosophy of mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: internalism - the belief that only factors inside the mind determine mental states or knowledge
