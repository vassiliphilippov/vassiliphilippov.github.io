subordinator

noun
introduces subordinate clause
6.3
Pronunciation: /sə.bɑːr.dɪ.ˈneɪ.tər/ /; / /sə.bɔːr.dɪ.ˈneɪ.tər/ (AmE), /sə.bɔː.dɪ.ˈneɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a word, such as 'because' or 'although', that introduces a subordinate clause in a sentence

Examples

A subordinator word
In the sentence 'She stayed home because it was raining', the word subordinator refers to 'because'.
A subordinator like 'although' can introduce a dependent clause in complex sentences.
Teachers often ask students to identify the subordinator in a given sentence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conjunction - a word used to connect clauses or sentences
  • antonym: coordinator - a word, such as 'and' or 'but', that joins words or groups of equal importance
