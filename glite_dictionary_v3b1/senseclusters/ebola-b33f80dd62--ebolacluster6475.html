Ebola

noun
severe viral disease with fever and bleeding
4.8 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªËˆboÊŠlÉ™ / (AmE), / ÉªËˆbÉ™ÊŠlÉ™ / (BrE)
A severe and often fatal infectious disease in humans and primates, caused by the Ebola virus, marked by fever and internal bleeding, and transmissible by contact with bodily fluids.

Examples

Ebola virus disease
Medical teams took strict precautions to prevent the spread of Ebola.
Researchers are developing new vaccines to protect populations from Ebola.
There was a major Ebola outbreak in West Africa in 2014.

Synonyms

  • Ebola hemorrhagic fever
    5.8 0.01/m
    Ebola hemorrhagic fever is the formal medical term that stresses severe bleeding and organ failure, while Ebola is the general disease name.
  • Ebola fever
    4.6 0.01/m
    Ebola fever is a shorter, often used name in reports and news, while Ebola is the broader, standard disease term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ebola virus - the virus that causes Ebola disease
  • degree lower: Marburg - a similar hemorrhagic fever caused by Marburg virus
