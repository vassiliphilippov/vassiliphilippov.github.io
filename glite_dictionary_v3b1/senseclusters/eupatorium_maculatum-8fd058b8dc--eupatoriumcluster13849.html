Eupatorium maculatum

noun
a North American herb with pink-purple flower heads
Pronunciation: / juːˌpeɪtɔːriəm ˌmækjuˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / juːˌpatɒːrɪəm ˌmakjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A North American herbaceous plant with whorled leaves and clusters of small pink or purple flower heads, commonly known as spotted Joe-Pye weed.

Examples

Eupatorium maculatum plant
Eupatorium maculatum grows naturally in moist meadows and along stream banks across eastern North America.
Gardeners often plant Eupatorium maculatum to attract butterflies with its colorful flower heads.
The tall stems of Eupatorium maculatum make it a distinctive feature in wildflower gardens.
