seat

verb
have enough seats for (number of people)
3.8
Pronunciation: / siːt / (AmE), / siːt / (BrE)
(of a building, vehicle, etc.) to have enough seats for a particular number of people.

Examples

The hall seats 500
The concert hall seats more people after the recent renovations.
The stadium seats 50,000 spectators for major events.
This bus seats up to 40 passengers comfortably.
