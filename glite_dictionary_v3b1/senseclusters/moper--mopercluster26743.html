moper

noun
archaic simpleton
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊpər / (AmE), / ˈməʊpə / (BrE)
an archaic term for a person considered foolish or simple-minded

Examples

An old moper
In the old village, a moper was someone who could not keep up with the others.
The character in the play was mocked as a moper for his silly mistakes.
He was unfairly called a moper despite his honest efforts to help.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual ability
  • synonym: simpleton - a person who is not very intelligent or lacks common sense
