conder

noun
fish spotter
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.dər/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.də/ (BrE)
a person who watches from a high place to spot shoals of fish for fishermen, especially in traditional fishing

Examples

In the old days, a skilled conder was essential for a successful fishing trip.
The fishermen waited for the conder to signal before casting their nets.
