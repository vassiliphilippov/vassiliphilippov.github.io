bolter

noun
plant gone to seed
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊltər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊltə / (BrE)
a plant, especially a vegetable, that has quickly produced a flowering stalk and gone to seed, often becoming bitter and less edible

Examples

Spinach bolter
The spinach became a bolter after the sudden heat wave, making the leaves taste bitter.
Gardeners try to harvest lettuce before it turns into a bolter and loses its tenderness.
Once a plant is a bolter, it usually stops producing edible leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bolt - to produce a flowering stalk and go to seed rapidly
