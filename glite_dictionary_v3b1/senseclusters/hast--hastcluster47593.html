hast

noun
archaic for haste
9.9
Pronunciation: / hæst / (AmE), / hæst / (BrE)
an archaic word meaning haste or great speed, especially in movement or action

Examples

In all hast
They departed, in all hast, before the storm could reach the coast.
With great hast, the messenger delivered the urgent news to the king.
The letter was written in such hast that it was barely legible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haste - speed or urgency of movement or action
  • antonym: slowness - the quality of moving or acting at a slow pace
