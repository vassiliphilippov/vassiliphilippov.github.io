unworked

adjective
not used or active
4.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàw…ùÀêkt / (AmE), /  ånÀàw…úÀêkt / (BrE)
not used, employed, or put to work; idle or unused, especially referring to time, equipment, or capacity.

Examples

Unworked factory hours
After the layoffs, many unworked hours remained in the factory schedule.
The land remained unworked for years before the new owners started farming it.
Several unworked machines stood in the corner, gathering dust.

Synonyms

  • unused
    1.0 2.63/m
    unused is not being used or operated, while unworked describes time, equipment, or capacity left idle and not put to work.
  • unutilized
    5.6 0.02/m
    unutilized sounds formal and means not put to use, while unworked is neutral and refers to idle time, equipment, or capacity.
  • idle
    2.8 4.10/m
    idle means not working or being used in the moment, while unworked emphasizes not being put to work or used.
  • inactive
    2.5 1.77/m
    inactive means not in operation or engaged, often for systems or people, while unworked focuses on unused capacity or time.
  • unexploited
    6.3 0.24/m
    unexploited describes resources or chances not developed or used, while unworked refers more generally to idle equipment, time, or capacity.
  • unengaged
    4.4 0.03/m
    unengaged means not busy or not committed in social or work tasks, while unworked speaks about unused time, tools, or capacity.
