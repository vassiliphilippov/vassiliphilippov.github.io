Bronze Age

noun
mythological third age marked by violence
4.7
Pronunciation: / brɑːnz eɪdʒ / (AmE), / brɒnz eɪdʒ / (BrE)
(In classical mythology) The third age of the world, marked by war, violence, and decline in morality, following the Golden and Silver Ages.

Examples

Mythical Bronze Age
According to Hesiod, humanity descended into the Bronze Age, a time defined by endless strife and warfare.
In the Bronze Age of myth, people were hard and strong but quick to anger.
Poets often reference the Bronze Age to illustrate an era when violence corrupted society.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Iron Age - the fourth and last age of the world in classical mythology, marked by complete decline
  • degree lower: Silver Age - the second age of the world in classical mythology, preceding the Bronze Age
