lady tulip

noun
a species of Eurasian tulip with blotched flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪdi ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A Eurasian species of tulip (Tulipa clusiana) with slender stems and small flowers that are usually blotched at the base.

Examples

A lady tulip flower
She planted a row of lady tulips along the garden path in the spring.
The lady tulip is popular among gardeners for its delicate blossoms and distinctive coloring.
Unlike other varieties, the lady tulip has flowers blotched with a contrasting shade at the base.
