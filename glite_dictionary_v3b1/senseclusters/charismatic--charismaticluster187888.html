charismatic

adjective
possessing charm that attracts others
Pronunciation: / ˌkærɪzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌkærɪzˈmætɪk / (BrE)
Having a compelling charm or appeal that inspires devotion or attracts others.

Examples

A charismatic leader
She has such a charismatic presence that people naturally gravitate toward her at parties.
The company's success is often credited to its charismatic founder.
The crowd was drawn to the charismatic speaker and hung on his every word.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnetic - having an attractive and captivating quality
  • antonym: unremarkable - lacking qualities that attract attention or admiration
