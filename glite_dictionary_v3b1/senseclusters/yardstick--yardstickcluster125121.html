yardstick

noun
three-foot measuring stick
3.2 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / Ààj…ërdÀåst…™k / (AmE), / Ààj…ëÀêdÀåst…™k / (BrE)
A measuring stick or tape, usually three feet (one yard) long, used for measuring length.

Examples

A yardstick for measuring
He keeps a yardstick in his workshop to measure fabric accurately.
Our teacher asked us to bring a yardstick for the math project.
The carpenter measured the board with a yardstick before cutting it.

Synonyms

  • yard measure
    1.8 0.00/m
    yard measure is exactly one yard long and used for measuring fabric, while yardstick names the common one-yard measuring tool.
  • measuring stick
    2.1 0.23/m
    measuring stick is a general ruler or rod marked with units for many lengths, while yardstick is specifically one yard long.
  • measuring rod
    3.4 0.04/m
    measuring rod is a marked device used as a standard to measure distance or length, while yardstick is usually one yard long.
