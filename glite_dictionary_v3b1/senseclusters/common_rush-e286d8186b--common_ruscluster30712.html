common rush

noun
a tall grasslike wetland plant
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən rʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən rʌʃ / (BrE)
a tall perennial rush (Juncus effusus) with soft, erect or arching stems, found in wet habitats in Eurasia, Australia, New Zealand, and widely in North America

Examples

The common rush plant
Gardeners use common rush to add texture and height to wetland gardens.
In marsh restoration projects, common rush is valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion.
The common rush is often found growing along the edges of ponds and streams.

Related Terms
