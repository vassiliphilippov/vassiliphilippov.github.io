dawn on

verb
become clear to someone; realize suddenly
3.7 2.98/m
Pronunciation: / d…în …ën / (AmE), / d…îÀên …ín / (BrE)
(of an idea) to become clear or apparent to someone; to suddenly realize or understand.

Examples

It dawned on her
As the facts were explained, it slowly dawned on her what had happened.
It didn't dawn on me until later that I had left my phone at home.
Only after watching the news did it finally dawn on them how serious the situation was.

Synonyms

  • dawn
    3.6 3.89/m
    dawn describes an idea starting to become clear over time, while dawn on means the idea suddenly becomes clear to someone.
  • realize
    1.1 240/m
    realize means to understand something clearly in general situations, while dawn on suggests the idea suddenly becomes clear to someone.
  • catch on
    3.9 1.52/m
    catch on is used when someone finally understands something after a delay, while dawn on means it suddenly becomes clear.
  • see the light
    5.0 1.66/m
    see the light usually shows someone accepts or understands something after a long time, while dawn on means a sudden realization.
