translucent substance

noun
material allowing light to pass through diffusely
Pronunciation: / trænˈsluːsənt ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / trænˈsluːsənt ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
a material that allows light to pass through but scatters it so that objects on the other side are not clearly visible

Examples

A translucent substance glows
Artists sometimes use a translucent substance to create glowing effects in their paintings.
Frosted glass is a common example of a translucent substance used in bathrooms for privacy.
The lamp was made of a translucent substance that softly diffused the light in the room.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: opaque material - material that does not let light pass through
  • degree higher: transparent material - material that allows light to pass through clearly
