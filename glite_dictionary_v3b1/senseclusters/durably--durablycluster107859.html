durably

adverb
in a lasting way
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdʊ.rə.bliː/ /; / /ˈdʊ.rə.bəl.iː/ (AmE), /ˈdʊə.rə.bliː/ /; / /ˈdjuː.rə.bliː/ (BrE)
in a way that lasts for a long time without breaking, wearing out, or losing effectiveness

Examples

To durably build furniture
The furniture was durably built to survive years of heavy use.
These shoes are durably made and perfect for hiking on rough terrain.
The bridge was durably designed to withstand strong winds and heavy traffic.

Their friendship was durably maintained despite living in different countries.
