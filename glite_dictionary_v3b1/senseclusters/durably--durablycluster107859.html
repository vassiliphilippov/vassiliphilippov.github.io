durably

adverb
in a lasting way
Pronunciation: / ˈdʊrəbli / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəbli / (BrE)
in a way that lasts for a long time without breaking, wearing out, or losing effectiveness

Examples

To durably build furniture
The furniture was durably built to survive years of heavy use.
These shoes are durably made and perfect for hiking on rough terrain.
The bridge was durably designed to withstand strong winds and heavy traffic.
The new policy was durably implemented and continued to benefit the community for years.
Their friendship was durably maintained despite living in different countries.
The agreement was durably supported by all parties involved, ensuring long-term cooperation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragilely - in a way that is easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever or for a very long time
  • synonym: sturdily - in a strong and solid way
  • antonym: temporarily - for a limited or short period of time
