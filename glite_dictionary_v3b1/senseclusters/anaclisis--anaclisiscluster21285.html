anaclisis

noun
strong emotional dependence on another (psychoanalytic)
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈklaɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəˈklaɪsɪs / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) Strong emotional or libidinal dependence on another person, especially a parental figure, forming a basis for subsequent attachments.

Examples

Anaclisis in therapy
Her therapist noted a tendency toward anaclisis in her reliance on authority figures.
Some theories suggest that anaclisis in childhood influences adult attachment styles.
The patient's anaclisis made it difficult for him to form independent relationships outside his family.
