D

noun
a fat-soluble vitamin; prevents rickets
3.5 3.52/m
Pronunciation: / diÀê / (AmE), / diÀê / (BrE)
A fat-soluble vitamin essential for calcium absorption and bone health, deficiency of which causes rickets.

Examples

Vitamin D supplement
Deficiency in vitamin D can lead to rickets in children.
Many people take supplements to ensure they get enough D in winter months.
Milk is often fortified with D to promote healthy bones.

Synonyms

  • vitamin D
    2.5 3.20/m
    vitamin D is the common medical and everyday name for the nutrient, while D is the short label often used in charts or lists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vitamin C - a water-soluble vitamin also involved in bone health; not related to D
  • synonym: vitamin D - a group of fat-soluble secosteroids responsible for increasing intestinal absorption of calcium, magnesium, and phosphate
