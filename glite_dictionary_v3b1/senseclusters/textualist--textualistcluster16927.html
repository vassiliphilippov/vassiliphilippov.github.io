textualist

noun
literal interpreter
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈteks.tjʊ.əl.ɪst/ (AmE), /ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈteks.tjʊ.əl.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who interprets texts, especially laws or scriptures, by focusing strictly on their exact wording

Examples

A textualist judge
The Supreme Court justice is a well-known textualist who bases decisions on the exact words of the Constitution.
As a textualist, she avoids considering the intentions behind the law and focuses only on its language.
Debates between a textualist and a contextualist often center on how to interpret ambiguous statutes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contextualist - a person who interprets texts by considering context and broader meaning
  • synonym: literalist - a person who interprets words in their most basic sense
