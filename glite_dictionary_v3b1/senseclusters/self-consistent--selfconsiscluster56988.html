self-consistent

adjective
logically consistent within itself
Pronunciation: / sɛlf kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / sɛlf kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
(of a set of ideas, theories, statements, etc.) not containing any logical contradictions within itself; consistent with itself.

Examples

A self-consistent theory
Her argument was perfectly self-consistent from start to finish.
It is important that mathematical systems stay self-consistent to avoid paradoxes.
The new theory is self-consistent and agrees with the observed data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coherent - logically connected and consistent
  • antonym: inconsistent - not staying the same or in agreement; containing contradictions
