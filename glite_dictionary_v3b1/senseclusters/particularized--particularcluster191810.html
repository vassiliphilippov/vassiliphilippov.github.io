particularized

adjective
made specific or individualized
8.0 6.5
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈtɪ.kjə.lə.raɪzd/ /; / /pər.ˈtɪ.kjʊ.lə.raɪzd/ (AmE), /pə.ˈtɪ.kjʊ.lə.raɪzd/ /; / /pə.ˈtɪ.kjə.lə.raɪzd/ (BrE)
Directed toward or limited to a specific person, thing, or situation; made specific or individualized; focused on details rather than the general case.

Examples

Highly particularized advice
Her advice was not general but rather highly particularized to each student's needs.
The report offered a particularized account of each incident.
We need a particularized approach instead of a one-size-fits-all solution.
Her particularised approach to the problem addressed every nuance in the data.
Legal arguments must often be supported by particularised evidence rather than general assertions.
The report provides a particularised analysis of the company's financial performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generalized - not specific, covering many cases
  • synonym: specific - clearly defined or identified
