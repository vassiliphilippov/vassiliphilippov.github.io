mirage

noun
optical illusion caused by atmospheric refraction
7.4 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / m…ôÀàr…ë í / (AmE), / m…™Ààr…ëÀê í / (BrE)
An optical illusion caused by atmospheric conditions, especially the appearance of a sheet of water in a desert or on a hot road, caused by the refraction of light from the sky by heated air.

Examples

Desert mirage
After hiking for hours, the exhausted travelers kept chasing a mirage that was never actually there.
Driving through the desert, we saw a shimmering mirage that looked like a lake in the distance.
The oasis turned out to be nothing more than a mirage created by the heat.

Synonyms

  • optical illusion
    4.1 0.47/m
    an optical illusion is a visual trick that fools the eye, while a mirage is a water-like image caused by hot air on roads

Related Terms

  • synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression
