enwomb

verb
to bury or enclose
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈwuːm / (AmE), / ɪnˈwuːm / (BrE)
to enclose or surround something completely as if in a womb, especially to bury or protect it

Examples

To enwomb a relic
The archaeologists decided to enwomb the fragile relics to preserve them for future generations.
Legends say the treasure was enwombed beneath the ancient temple centuries ago.
They hoped to enwomb the time capsule in a safe place for posterity.
The ancient tomb was enwombed by layers of earth and stone for centuries.
Mist enwombed the forest, making it feel mysterious and hidden from the world.
She felt safe, enwombed in the warmth of her thick blanket on a cold night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encase - to enclose in or as if in a case
  • synonym: envelop - to cover or surround completely
  • antonym: exhume - to dig out something buried, especially a body
  • antonym: expose - to leave something uncovered or unprotected
  • synonym: inter - to bury a dead body in the earth
