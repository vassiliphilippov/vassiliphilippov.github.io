enwomb

verb
to bury or enclose
9.9
Pronunciation: /en.ˈwuːm/ /; / /ə.ˈwuːm/ (AmE), /en.ˈwuːm/ /; / /ə.ˈwuːm/ (BrE)
to enclose or surround something completely as if in a womb, especially to bury or protect it

Examples

To enwomb a relic
The archaeologists decided to enwomb the fragile relics to preserve them for future generations.

The ancient tomb was enwombed by layers of earth and stone for centuries.
Mist enwombed the forest, making it feel mysterious and hidden from the world.
She felt safe, enwombed in the warmth of her thick blanket on a cold night.
