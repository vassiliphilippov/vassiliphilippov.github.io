comprehensive

noun
UK secondary school
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a secondary school in the UK that does not select students based on academic ability

Examples

Comprehensive school
Most children in England attend a comprehensive rather than a selective school.
The new comprehensive offers a wide range of subjects for all students.
After moving to London, she enrolled in a local comprehensive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grammar school - a UK secondary school that selects students based on academic ability
  • synonym: secondary school - a school for students aged 11 to 16 or 18
