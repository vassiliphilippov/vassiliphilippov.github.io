tyrant

noun
cruel and oppressive ruler
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪərənt / (BrE)
a person who uses power or authority in a cruel or oppressive way

Examples

Cruel tyrant
Many people fled the region to escape the oppression of a tyrant.
The country suffered for decades under the rule of a brutal tyrant.
Throughout history, tyrants have often been overthrown by popular uprisings.
Her boss acted like a tyrant, demanding absolute obedience from every employee.
Many people feared him, calling him a tyrant behind his back.
The country suffered for years under the rule of a tyrant who silenced all opposition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefactor - someone who gives help, often in a position of authority, in a kind way
  • synonym: despot - a ruler with absolute and often oppressive power
  • synonym: dictator - a ruler with absolute power, often obtained by force
  • antonym: liberator - a person who frees others from oppression
