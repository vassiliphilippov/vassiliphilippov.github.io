windowing

noun
adjusting image contrast
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the adjustment of display settings, such as window level and width, to change contrast or brightness in medical images like CT or MRI scans

Examples

Windowing a CT scan
The radiologist used windowing to highlight subtle differences in the CT scan.
Proper windowing can make it easier to detect small tumors in medical images.
Training in windowing techniques is essential for anyone interpreting MRI results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brightness control - altering the overall lightness or darkness of an image
  • synonym: contrast adjustment - changing the difference between light and dark areas in an image
