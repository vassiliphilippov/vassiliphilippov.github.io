rat snake

noun
nonvenomous snake eating rodents
Pronunciation: / ˈræt ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈræt ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a nonvenomous snake, chiefly found in North America and Asia, that preys on rodents

Examples

A rat snake climbs trees
A rat snake can climb trees in search of food or shelter.
Many people mistake a rat snake for a venomous species because of its size and pattern.
The farmer welcomed the presence of a rat snake in his barn to control rodents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: black rat snake - a species of large, nonvenomous rat snake found in North America
  • synonym: corn snake - a species of rat snake native to the southeastern United States
