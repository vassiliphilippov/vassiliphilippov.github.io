gravity gradient

noun
variation in gravity over an object's extent
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrævəti ˈɡrædiənt / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪti ˈɡradɪənt / (BrE)
the difference in gravitational force experienced at different points of a nonspherical object, important in tidal effects and satellite dynamics

Examples

The gravity gradient effect
Engineers must consider the gravity gradient when designing equipment for space missions.
The gravity gradient around the Earth influences the stability of satellites in orbit.
The tidal forces caused by the Moon are a result of the gravity gradient across the Earth.
