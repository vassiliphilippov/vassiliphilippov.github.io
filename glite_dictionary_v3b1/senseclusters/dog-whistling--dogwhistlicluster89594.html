dog-whistling

noun
making statements with hidden or coded meanings
7.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdɔːg.wɪs.lɪŋ/ /; / /ˈdɑːg.wɪs.lɪŋ/ /; / /ˈdɔːg.hwɪs.lɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdɒg.wɪs.lɪŋ/ /; / /ˈdɒg.hwɪs.lɪŋ/ (BrE)
The act of making statements that have a hidden meaning intended to be understood only by a specific group, often to subtly communicate controversial or coded messages.

Examples

Dog-whistling to groups
Many critics accused the speech of dog-whistling to extremist groups.
She denied any intent of dog-whistling, but her message was interpreted differently by various audiences.
Some political advertisements rely on subtle dog-whistling rather than direct statements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coded language - words or phrases with hidden meanings understood by a select group
  • antonym: explicitness - clear and direct communication, the opposite of subtle or coded messaging
