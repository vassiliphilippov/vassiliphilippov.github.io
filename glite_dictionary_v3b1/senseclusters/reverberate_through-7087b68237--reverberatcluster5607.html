reverberate through

verb
to have a lasting impact or echo throughout
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrbəˌreɪt θru / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbəreɪt θruː / (BrE)
to echo or have a continuing impact throughout a place, group, or period of time

Examples

To reverberate through a room
His sudden resignation reverberated through the political world for weeks.
News of the discovery reverberated through scientific circles, sparking new discussions.
The sound of the church bells reverberated through the entire village.
