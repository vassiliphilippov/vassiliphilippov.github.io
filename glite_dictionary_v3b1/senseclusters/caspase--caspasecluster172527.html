caspase

noun
enzyme involved in cell death (apoptosis)
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkæs.peɪz/ (AmE), /ˈkæs.peɪz/ (BrE)
any of a family of enzymes (proteases) that play an essential role in programmed cell death (apoptosis) by cleaving specific proteins in cells.

Examples

A caspase enzyme
Each type of caspase has a unique role in the apoptosis pathway.
Researchers have discovered that caspase activation is a crucial step in cell apoptosis.
The inhibition of caspase enzymes can prevent unwanted cell death in certain diseases.
