fallenness

noun
state of sinfulness
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔlənnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlənnəs / (BrE)
in Christian theology, the state of moral imperfection and separation from God resulting from the original Fall of humanity

Examples

Human fallenness
The doctrine of fallenness explains why humans are believed to need redemption.
Many religious texts discuss the consequences of humanity's fallenness after the original sin.
He reflected on the concept of fallenness during his theology class.
