large

adverb
with wind abaft the beam (nautical)
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
(Nautical, adverb) With the wind blowing from behind the ship at an angle greater than abeam; said of a sailing vessel when the wind is on the quarter or astern.

Examples

Sailing large
The captain gave orders to set full sails while they were sailing large down the coast.
The old clipper ship sailed large towards the open sea as the wind picked up behind her.
With a fair wind, the vessel made excellent speed running large all afternoon.
