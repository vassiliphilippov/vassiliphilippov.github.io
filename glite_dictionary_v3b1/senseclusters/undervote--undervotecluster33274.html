undervote

noun
unmarked ballot choice
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌvoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌvəʊt / (BrE)
a ballot or situation in which fewer votes are cast than allowed, either by leaving a contest blank or selecting fewer choices

Examples

A ballot undervote
The election officials noticed a large number of undervotes in the mayoral race.
If you skip a question on the ballot, it will be counted as an undervote for that contest.
Some voters intentionally cast an undervote to show dissatisfaction with the candidates.
An undervote may happen if a voter leaves some races blank on the ballot.
The election officials noticed a high number of undervotes in the city council race.
If you skip a question on the ballot, it will be counted as an undervote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstention - the act of deliberately not voting in an election or on a particular issue
  • antonym: overvote - a ballot marked for more choices than allowed in a contest
  • synonym: spoiled ballot - a ballot that is invalid due to errors or markings
