symbiotic

adjective
relating to organisms living together closely
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈbaɪ.ɑ.tɪk /; / ˌsɪmbaɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɪ.ɒ.tɪk /; / ˌsɪmbaɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
living together in a close relationship, usually helping or benefiting at least one of them

Examples

A symbiotic relationship
Scientists studied the symbiotic interaction between the fungus and its host tree.
Some bacteria form symbiotic associations with plant roots, aiding in nutrient exchange.
The clownfish and sea anemone have a symbiotic relationship in the coral reef.
The symbiotic relationship between bees and flowers helps both species survive.
Some bacteria have a symbiotic connection with humans, aiding in digestion.
Lichens are formed by a symbiotic partnership between fungi and algae.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: mutualistic - involving mutual benefit between organisms
  • degree lower: parasitic - living as a parasite at the expense of another organism
