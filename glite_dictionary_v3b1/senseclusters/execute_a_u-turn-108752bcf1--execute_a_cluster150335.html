(figurative) to reverse a previously announced policy, stance, or decision; to change course and do the opposite of what was promised or planned.

Examples

Execute a U-turn on policy
The government executed a U-turn on its immigration policy after widespread protests.
After customer complaints, the company is executing a U-turn on the planned fee increases.
Critics said the mayor executed a U-turn when he suddenly supported the development project.
