come

verb
to enter one's mind spontaneously
1.5 64/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm / (AmE), / kÊŒm / (BrE)
(of an idea, thought, or memory) to enter your mind; to be remembered or suggested without deliberate effort.

Examples

An idea comes to you
If a good idea comes to you, write it down before you forget.
It didn't come to him until later that day what she really meant.
The solution suddenly came to me while I was driving.

Synonyms

  • come to mind
    3.0 8.00/m
    come to mind is a common fixed phrase used when an idea appears suddenly, while come is the basic verb for ideas entering your mind.
  • spring to mind
    3.4 0.46/m
    spring to mind is a lively, informal phrase for ideas that pop up quickly, while come is the neutral verb for such thoughts.
  • occur
    2.4 20/m
    occur is more formal and is used in writing for ideas that happen unexpectedly, while come is more common in everyday speech.
  • come to
    3.1 32/m
    come to often appears in phrases like 'it came to me' to show a sudden realization, while come is the simple verb for ideas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember or bring to mind
  • synonym: occur - to come into the mind; to be thought of
