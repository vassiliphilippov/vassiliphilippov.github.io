doorpost

noun
upright post forming the side of a door frame
5.6 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdɔːr.poʊst/ (AmE), /ˈdɔː.pəʊst/ (BrE)
either of the two upright posts on either side of a door, forming part of the frame into which the door is fitted; a door jamb.

Examples

The doorpost frame
He nailed the sign to the doorpost so everyone would see it as they entered.
She stood silently, leaning against the doorpost, waiting for the rain to stop.
The ancient house still had inscriptions carved into the wooden doorpost.
