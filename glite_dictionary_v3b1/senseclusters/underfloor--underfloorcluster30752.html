underfloor

noun
space beneath floor
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌflɔr / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌflɔː / (BrE)
the space or cavity beneath a building's floor, often used for access, storage, or housing pipes and wiring

Examples

A building's underfloor
The plumber accessed the underfloor to check for leaks in the heating pipes.
They stored old boxes in the underfloor to keep the main rooms tidy.
Electricians often run cables through the underfloor to avoid visible wiring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attic - the space or room just below the roof of a building
  • synonym: crawl space - a shallow area beneath a building's floor for access or storage
