peer

noun
device on a peer-to-peer network
6.0
Pronunciation: /pɪr/ /; / /piːr/ (AmE), /pɪə/ /; / /piːə/ (BrE)
a computer or other device on a peer-to-peer (P2P) network that shares resources (files, bandwidth, processing) directly with other devices without a central server.

Examples

A network peer
In the BitTorrent system, every peer contributes bandwidth to the swarm.
The file was downloaded quickly because several peers were seeding it at once.
To join the network, she configured her computer as a peer and opened the app.

Related Terms

  • synonym: node - a point in a network where data can be created, received, or transmitted
  • antonym: server - a central computer that provides services or resources to clients on a network
