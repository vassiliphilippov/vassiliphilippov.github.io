skid

verb
to slide out of control, often when braking
3.5 0.54/m
Pronunciation: / skÉªd / (AmE), / skÉªd / (BrE)
to slide, usually sideways or out of control, especially when a vehicle or wheel loses traction on a slippery surface

Examples

To skid on ice
If you skid on ice, try not to panic and steer gently.
She lost control and skidded into the intersection during the storm.
The car began to skid when he slammed on the brakes.
He lost control and watched his bicycle skid across the gravel path.
If you brake too hard in the rain, your car may skid unexpectedly.
The bus started to skid on the icy hill.
If you brake too hard on gravel, your tires might skid out of control.
She lost her balance and felt her bicycle skid as she turned the corner.
The car began to skid on the icy road, narrowly avoiding an accident.

Synonyms

  • slew
    7.6 1.18/m
    slew means to slide or turn sideways suddenly, while skid means to slide out of control on a slippery surface.
  • slip
    2.3 33/m
    slip means to move or slide unintentionally and often quietly, while skid means to slide sideways out of control on wet surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slide - to move smoothly over a surface while maintaining continuous contact
  • synonym: slip - to slide unintentionally, usually for a short distance
  • synonym: swerve - to turn aside suddenly, often to avoid something
