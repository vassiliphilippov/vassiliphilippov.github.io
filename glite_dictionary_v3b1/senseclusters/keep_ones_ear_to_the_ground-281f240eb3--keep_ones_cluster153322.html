keep one's ear to the ground

verb
stay alert to news or rumors
1.0
Pronunciation: / kip wʌnz ɪr tə ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ɪə tə ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to pay close attention to what is happening or being said, so as to be well-informed about current events, trends, or rumors

Examples

To keep one's ear to the ground
It's important to keep one's ear to the ground if you want to succeed in a fast-changing industry.
Journalists often keep their ears to the ground for the latest news and trends.
She tries to keep her ear to the ground to hear about any upcoming job openings.

  • synonym: stay tuned - to remain attentive for more information
  • antonym: turn a deaf ear - to deliberately ignore what is happening or being said
