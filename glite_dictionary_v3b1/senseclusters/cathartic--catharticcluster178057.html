cathartic

adjective
strong laxative effect
8.0 8.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑrtɪk / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtɪk / (BrE)
(of a medicine or substance) having a strong laxative effect; causing evacuation of the bowels.

Examples

Cathartic medicine
Some herbal teas can have a mild or cathartic effect on the digestive system.
The doctor prescribed a cathartic solution to clear the patient's bowels before the procedure.
This medication is intended for short-term use due to its cathartic properties.
