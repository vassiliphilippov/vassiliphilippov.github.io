obtrude

verb
impose or force upon others
9.9
Pronunciation: / əbˈtruːd / (AmE), / əbˈtruːd / (BrE)
to force oneself or something into a place or situation in an unwelcome or intrusive way

Examples

To obtrude on guests
A good host ensures that their presence does not obtrude on the comfort of their guests.
He tends to obtrude his opinions during every meeting, even when no one asks.
Please don't obtrude your personal matters into our professional discussions.
Bright lights from the street obtruded into the darkness of the bedroom at night.
He tends to obtrude his opinions during meetings, even when no one asks for them.
Tall weeds obtruded from the cracks in the sidewalk, making the path difficult to walk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intrude - to put oneself deliberately into a place or situation where one is unwelcome
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove oneself or something from a place, situation, or involvement
