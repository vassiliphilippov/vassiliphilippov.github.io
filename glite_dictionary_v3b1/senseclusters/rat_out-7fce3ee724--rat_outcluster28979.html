rat out

verb
to betray by informing on someone
Pronunciation: / ræt aʊt / (AmE), / ræt aʊt / (BrE)
to reveal information about someone, especially to the authorities, usually betraying their trust

Examples

To rat out friends
Despite the pressure, she refused to rat out her friends.
He was afraid his accomplice would rat out the whole group to the police.
I can't believe you ratted out your own brother just to avoid trouble.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to be disloyal or reveal someone's secrets
  • synonym: snitch - to secretly inform on someone
