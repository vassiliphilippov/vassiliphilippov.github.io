hardpack

noun
firm packed surface
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌpæk / (BrE)
a surface of snow, dirt, or soil that has been packed down until it is firm and solid

Examples

A hardpack trail
The mountain trail was covered in hardpack, making it easier for hikers to walk.
After several days of use, the snow on the slope turned into hardpack.
Cyclists prefer riding on hardpack because it offers good traction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mud - soft, wet earth or dirt
  • synonym: pavement - a hard, smooth surface for walking or driving
