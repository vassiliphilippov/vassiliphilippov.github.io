Tyrannosaurus rex

noun
large predatory dinosaur from late Cretaceous
Pronunciation: / tɪˌrænəˌsɔrəs ˈrɛks / (AmE), / tɪˌranəˌsɔːrəs ˈrɛks / (BrE)
a very large carnivorous dinosaur of the late Cretaceous period in North America, known for its massive head, powerful jaws with serrated teeth, and bipedal stance

Examples

The Tyrannosaurus rex skeleton
In the museum's dinosaur exhibit, children marveled at the towering skeleton of Tyrannosaurus rex.
Some scientists debate whether Tyrannosaurus rex was a pure predator or also scavenged for food.
The massive skull of the Tyrannosaurus rex is one of the most iconic fossils ever discovered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Velociraptor - a small, agile carnivorous dinosaur
  • synonym: dinosaur - an extinct group of reptiles that lived during the Mesozoic era
  • antonym: herbivore - an animal that eats plants (contrasted with a carnivore like T. rex)
