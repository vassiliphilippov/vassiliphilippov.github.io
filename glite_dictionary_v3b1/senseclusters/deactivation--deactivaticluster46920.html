deactivation

noun
making something inactive or ineffective
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something inactive or ineffective, especially by turning off power or neutralizing a device

Examples

Deactivation of a device
Bomb squads are trained for the safe deactivation of explosive devices.
Proper deactivation of the old software is required before installing the new version.
The deactivation of the alarm system was completed before the police arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activation - the act of making something active or operative
