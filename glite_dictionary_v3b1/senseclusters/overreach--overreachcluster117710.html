overreach

verb
defeat by cleverness or trickery
7.9 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàrit É / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàriÀêt É / (BrE)
to defeat or outwit (someone) by cleverness or cunning, often by exceeding one's own limits or by trickery

Examples

To overreach an opponent
He tried to overreach his opponent in the negotiations, but ended up exposing his own weakness.
If you try to overreach your competitors by cutting corners, it may backfire.
The chess player successfully overreached her rival with a brilliant series of moves.

Synonyms

    4.7 0.73/m

  • outfox
    6.7 0.23/m
    outfox relies on sly cleverness to fool an opponent, while overreach often adds risk by going beyond what you can manage.
  • outsmart
    4.1 1.51/m
    outsmart means using clever thinking or skill to beat someone, while overreach often also involves risky trickery or overstepping limits.
  • outmaneuver
    7.7 0.77/m
    outmaneuver is to gain advantage by skillful planning and action, while overreach often implies cunning plus risky overstepping.
