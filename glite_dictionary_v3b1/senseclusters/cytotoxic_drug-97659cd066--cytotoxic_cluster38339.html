cytotoxic drug

noun
drug toxic to cells (esp. in chemotherapy)
Pronunciation: / saɪtəˈtɑksɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / saɪtəˈtɒksɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that is toxic to cells, used especially in chemotherapy to inhibit the growth of cancer cells

Examples

Cytotoxic drugs in chemotherapy
Doctors monitor blood counts closely when administering cytotoxic drugs during chemotherapy.
Many cytotoxic drugs can cause serious side effects due to their effect on healthy cells.
The patient was prescribed a cytotoxic drug as part of her cancer treatment.
