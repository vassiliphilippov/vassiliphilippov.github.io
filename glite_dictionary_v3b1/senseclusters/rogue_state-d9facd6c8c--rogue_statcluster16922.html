rogue state

noun
uncooperative or disruptive country
Pronunciation: / roʊɡ steɪt / (AmE), / rəʊɡ steɪt / (BrE)
A country that acts outside accepted international norms, often disregarding international law or agreements.

Examples

A rogue state nation
Diplomats debated sanctions against the rogue state at the United Nations.
Some analysts warn that supporting a rogue state could destabilize the entire region.
The country was labeled a rogue state after repeated violations of international treaties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pariah state - a country rejected by the international community
