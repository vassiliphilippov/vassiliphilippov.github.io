high-yield bond

noun
bond with low rating and high interest
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈjiːld bɑnd / (AmE), / haɪ ˈjiːld bɒnd / (BrE)
a bond with a low credit rating (BB or below), offering higher interest rates to compensate for greater risk, commonly called a junk bond

Examples

A high-yield bond rating
Credit agencies downgraded the firm's debt to high-yield bond status after a drop in profits.
Investors looking for higher returns often consider high-yield bonds despite their increased risk.
The company financed its expansion by issuing high-yield bonds to attract speculative investors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: junk bond - a bond with a lower credit rating, higher risk, and higher yield
