makeweight

noun
a weight added to reach a required total
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪkˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkweɪt / (BrE)
a weight that is added to something, especially on a scale, to reach a required total weight

Examples

A makeweight on scales
A makeweight was necessary because the goods did not reach the minimum shipping weight.
He used a small makeweight to bring the balance to exactly five kilograms.
The jeweler placed a makeweight on the scale to ensure an accurate measurement.
