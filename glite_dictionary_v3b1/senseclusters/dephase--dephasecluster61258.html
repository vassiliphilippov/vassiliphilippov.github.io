dephase

verb
to disrupt phase coherence
Pronunciation: / diːˈfeɪz /; / diˈfeɪz / (AmE), / diːˈfeɪz /; / diˈfeɪz / (BrE)
to cause waves, signals, or quantum states to lose synchronization or coherence

Examples

To dephase quantum bits
Environmental noise can dephase quantum bits, making reliable computation difficult.

Strong magnetic fields can dephase the signals in sensitive electronic devices.
If you dephase the two sound waves, the resulting noise will be less clear.
