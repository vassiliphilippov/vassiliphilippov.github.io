activating agent

noun
chemical used in mineral flotation
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.təˌveɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
a surface-active chemical used in the flotation process of mineral extraction to increase attraction to a specific mineral

Examples

An activating agent chemical
A copper sulfate solution is often used as an activating agent to improve the recovery of sphalerite during flotation.
Selecting the appropriate activating agent can significantly affect which minerals are collected in the flotation process.
The addition of an activating agent dramatically increased the separation efficiency in the ore processing plant.
