appendix

noun
small pouch in the lower intestine
Pronunciation: / əˈpɛndɪks / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪks / (BrE)
a small, tube-shaped pouch attached to the lower end of the cecum in the human body, which has no vital function

Examples

Burst appendix
A burst appendix can cause a severe abdominal infection.
Doctors are not certain why the human body still has an appendix.
The surgeon removed her inflamed appendix before it could rupture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cecum - the first part of the large intestine
