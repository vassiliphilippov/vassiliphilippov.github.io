California false morel

noun
a gyromitra mushroom native to California
Pronunciation: / kæləˌfɔrniə fɔls ˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / kælɪˈfɔːniə fɔːls ˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
A species of gyromitra mushroom with a brown, inflated cap and thick, fluted stalk, found under conifers in California.

Examples

The California false morel mushroom
Hikers are warned against consuming the California false morel, as it can be toxic.
Researchers studied the ecology of the California false morel in its native habitat.
The California false morel is often found growing beneath pines after spring rains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gyromitra - a genus of fungi to which the California false morel belongs
  • antonym: true morel - an edible mushroom similar in appearance but taxonomically distinct
