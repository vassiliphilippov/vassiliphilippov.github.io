over

preposition
above or across
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
used to show movement or position above something or across a surface from one side to the other

Examples

Flew over the city
The plane flew over the mountains during the storm.
She spread a blanket over the grass for the picnic.
The bridge stretches over the river, connecting the two towns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: across - from one side to the other side of something
  • antonym: under - in or to a position below something
