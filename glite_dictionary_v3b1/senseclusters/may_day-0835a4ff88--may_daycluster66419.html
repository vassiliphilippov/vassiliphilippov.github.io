May Day

noun
May 1st festival; workers' holiday
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ deɪ / (BrE)
A traditional festival held on May 1st to celebrate the coming of summer, and, in some countries, a day honoring workers and labor movements.

Examples

May Day festival
Every year, people gather in the city square to celebrate May Day with music and dancing.
In many countries, May Day is recognized as an official public holiday.
The origins of May Day festivities can be traced back to ancient pagan traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Labor Day - a public holiday honoring workers, observed in the US and Canada
