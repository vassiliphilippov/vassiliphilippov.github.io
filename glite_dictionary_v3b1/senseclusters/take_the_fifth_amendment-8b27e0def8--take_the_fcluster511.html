take the Fifth Amendment

verb
refuse to answer by claiming constitutional rights
4.5 7.5
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə fɪfθ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fɪfθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
to refuse to answer questions in a legal setting by invoking the constitutional right against self-incrimination, as guaranteed by the Fifth Amendment.

Examples

To take the Fifth Amendment in court
She was advised by her lawyer to take the Fifth Amendment on potentially incriminating questions.
The witness repeatedly took the Fifth Amendment during the congressional hearing.
When questioned by the prosecutor, he decided to take the Fifth Amendment and did not respond.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remain silent - to not speak or answer questions, often for legal protection
  • synonym: self-incrimination - the act of exposing oneself to an accusation or charge of crime
