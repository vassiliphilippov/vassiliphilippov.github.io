outer cassock

noun
voluminous clergy garment worn over the inner cassock
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtər ˈkæsək / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə ˈkæsək / (BrE)
a long, full garment worn by bishops, priests, deacons, and monastics over the inner cassock as their regular outer wear, especially in Eastern Christian traditions

Examples

The outer cassock garment
During processions, the bishop's outer cassock is often beautifully decorated.
Monastics may wear a black outer cassock as part of their daily attire.
The priest put on his outer cassock before leading the evening service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cassock - a long, close-fitting garment worn by members of the clergy
