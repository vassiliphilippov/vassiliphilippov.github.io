feint

noun
deceptive or distracting movement
Pronunciation: / feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
A deceptive or pretended movement, especially one made to distract an opponent in sports, combat, or games.

Examples

A clever feint
During the chess match, she made a feint with her knight before launching her real attack.
The army's sudden feint on the eastern front diverted attention from their true objective.
The boxer used a quick feint to trick his opponent into lowering his guard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attack - A direct and genuine assault or offensive action
  • synonym: fake - A simulated or false action intended to deceive
