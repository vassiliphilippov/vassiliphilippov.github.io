popliteus

noun
knee joint muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɑːpˈlɪtiəs / (AmE), / pɒpˈlɪtɪəs / (BrE)
a small muscle located at the back of the knee joint that helps unlock the knee during movement

Examples

The popliteus muscle
The popliteus plays a key role in unlocking the knee when you begin to walk.
Injuries to the popliteus are rare but can cause pain at the back of the knee.
Physical therapists may target the popliteus when treating certain knee problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hamstring - any of the muscles at the back of the thigh
