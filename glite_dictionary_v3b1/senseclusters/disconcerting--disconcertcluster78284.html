disconcerting

adjective
causing confusion or unease
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.kənˈsɝː.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kənˈsɜː.tɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a feeling of confusion, discomfort, or unease; unsettling.

Examples

A disconcerting stare
His disconcerting stare made her feel uneasy during the interview.
It can be disconcerting when plans change unexpectedly at the last minute.
The sudden silence in the room was extremely disconcerting to everyone present.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or more confident
  • synonym: unsettling - making someone feel uneasy or anxious
