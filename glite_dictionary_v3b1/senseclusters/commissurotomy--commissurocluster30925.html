commissurotomy

noun
brain surgery for epilepsy
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.mɪ.ʃə.ˈrɑː.tə.mi/ (AmE), /kə.mɪ.ʃə.ˈrɒt.ə.mi/ /; / /kɒ.mɪ.ʃə.ˈrɒt.ə.mi/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the connecting fibers between the two hemispheres of the brain are cut, often to treat severe epilepsy

Examples

Brain commissurotomy
After the commissurotomy, the patient's seizures became much less frequent and severe.
Doctors may consider a commissurotomy when medication fails to control epileptic episodes.
The risks and benefits of commissurotomy were carefully explained to the patient's family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epilepsy - a neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent seizures
  • synonym: lobotomy - a surgical operation involving incision into the prefrontal lobe of the brain
