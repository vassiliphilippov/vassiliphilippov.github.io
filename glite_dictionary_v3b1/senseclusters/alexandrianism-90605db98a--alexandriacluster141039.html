Alexandrianism

noun
scholarly literary style
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪɡˈzændriənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændriənɪzəm / (BrE)
a literary or cultural style characterized by scholarly refinement and complexity, associated with ancient Alexandria

Examples

Alexandrianism in poetry
The poet's fascination with Alexandrianism is evident in his use of complex allusions.
Many scholars debate the influence of Alexandrianism on later European literature.
Her writing style, marked by Alexandrianism, appealed to a highly educated audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classicist - a person or style favoring classical forms and traditions
  • antonym: romanticism - a literary and artistic movement emphasizing emotion and individualism
