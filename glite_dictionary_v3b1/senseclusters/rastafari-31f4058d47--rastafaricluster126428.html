Rastafari

noun
Jamaican religious and cultural movement
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrɑstəˈfɑri / (AmE), / ˌrastəˈfɑːri / (BrE)
A religious and cultural movement originating in Jamaica in the 1930s, characterized by beliefs in the divinity of Haile Selassie, distinctive rituals, and particular styles such as dreadlocks and reggae music.

Examples

Rastafari movement
He studied the beliefs and rituals of Rastafari for his cultural anthropology project.
Many people associate Rastafari with dreadlocks, reggae, and a commitment to African identity.
The Rastafari movement has had a profound influence on reggae music worldwide.
