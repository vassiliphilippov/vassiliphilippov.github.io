canon

noun
collection of recognized sacred books
6.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkæn.ən/ (AmE), /ˈkæn.ən/ (BrE)
the collection of official or recognized books, especially those accepted as part of a holy scripture such as the Bible

Examples

Biblical canon

Some early Christian communities debated which texts belonged in the biblical canon.
The Church officially recognized the New Testament canon in the fourth century.
