mandate

noun
League of Nations governed territory
3.3 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
A territory assigned by the League of Nations to be governed by a foreign power following World War I, until considered able to govern itself.

Examples

League of Nations mandate
After World War I, Germany lost several of its colonial possessions as League of Nations mandates.
French authorities governed the Syrian mandate until its independence.
The British administered the Palestinian mandate from 1920 until 1948.

  • synonym: protectorate - a state controlled and protected by another
