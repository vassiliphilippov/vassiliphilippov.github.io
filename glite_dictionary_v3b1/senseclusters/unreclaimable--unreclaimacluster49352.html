unreclaimable

adjective
beyond reform
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈkleɪməbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈkleɪməbəl / (BrE)
not able to be reformed, improved, or brought back to an acceptable state or behavior

Examples

Unreclaimable person
After years in prison, he was still considered unreclaimable by the authorities.
Some believe that no one is truly unreclaimable, no matter their past mistakes.
The teacher refused to give up on a student others called unreclaimable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incorrigible - impossible to correct or reform
  • synonym: irredeemable - impossible to save or improve
  • antonym: reformable - able to be improved or corrected
