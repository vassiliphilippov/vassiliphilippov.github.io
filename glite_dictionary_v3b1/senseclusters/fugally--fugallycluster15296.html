fugally

adverb
in the style of a fugue (music)
3.4 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfjuː.ɡə.li/ /; / /ˈfjuː.ɡə.lɪ/ /; / /ˈfuː.ɡə.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈfjuː.ɡə.li/ /; / /ˈfjuː.ɡə.lɪ/ (BrE)
in the manner of a fugue; in a fugal style, typically referring to music composed with multiple voices interweaving according to the rules of a fugue.

Examples

To play fugally

The choir sang fugally, their voices weaving in intricate counterpoint.
The composer structured the movement fugally, allowing each instrument to introduce the subject in turn.
