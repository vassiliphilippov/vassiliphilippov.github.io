chest register

noun
lower vocal range in singing or speech
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /tʃest ˈredʒ.ɪ.stər/ /; / /tʃest ˈredʒ.ə.stər/ (AmE), /tʃest ˈredʒ.ɪ.stə/ /; / /tʃest ˈredʒ.ə.stə/ (BrE)
the lower range of the voice used in speaking or singing, characterized by a resonance felt in the chest

Examples

The chest register voice
He sang the first verse in his chest register to give it warmth and depth.
In speech, using your chest register often creates a more powerful and authoritative tone.
Many vocal coaches encourage beginners to strengthen their chest register before tackling high notes.
