Calusa

proper noun
Native American group
Pronunciation: / kəˈluːsə / (AmE), / kəˈluːsə / (BrE)
a Native American (Indigenous) people who lived in southwestern Florida before European contact

Examples

The Calusa people
The Calusa built complex villages along the coast of southwestern Florida.
Artifacts from the Calusa reveal their advanced fishing and building techniques.
Historians study the culture and traditions of the Calusa to learn about early Florida.
The Calusa built large shell mounds along the coast of southwestern Florida.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts from the ancient Calusa civilization near the estuaries.
The Calusa were skilled fishers and controlled trade in their region before European arrival.
