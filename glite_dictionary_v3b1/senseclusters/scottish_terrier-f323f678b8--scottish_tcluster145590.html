Scottish terrier

noun
small Scottish breed of terrier dog
Pronunciation: / ˈskɑːtɪʃ ˈtɛr.i.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈtɛr.i.ə / (BrE)
A small, sturdy breed of dog with long hair, usually black, erect tail and ears, originally from Scotland.

Examples

A Scottish terrier dog
My family adopted a Scottish terrier from the local animal shelter last year.
Queen Victoria was known to keep a Scottish terrier as a pet.
The Scottish terrier is known for its distinctive beard and bushy eyebrows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: terrier - a small, active breed of dog originally bred for hunting vermin
