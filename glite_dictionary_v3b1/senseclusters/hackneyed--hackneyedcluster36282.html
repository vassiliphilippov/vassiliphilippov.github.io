hackneyed

adjective
overused and unoriginal
Pronunciation: / ˈhæk.nid / (AmE), / ˈhak.nid / (BrE)
(of a phrase or idea) used so often as to be no longer original or interesting; overfamiliar through overuse.

Examples

A hackneyed phrase
Avoid using hackneyed expressions in your writing if you want to stand out.
His speech felt hackneyed, filled with clichés we've all heard before.
The movie's plot was so hackneyed that everyone could predict the ending.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - new and unusual; not derived from something else
  • synonym: trite - lacking originality due to repeated use
