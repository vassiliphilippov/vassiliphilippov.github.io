clinker block

noun
lightweight concrete block with cinder aggregate
8.8 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈklɪŋ.kər blɑːk/ /; / /ˈklɪŋ.kər blɔːk/ (AmE), /ˈklɪŋ.kə blɒk/ (BrE)
a lightweight concrete block for construction, made using cinder aggregate instead of traditional stone or gravel

Examples

A clinker block wall
Due to its composition, a clinker block is lighter than a traditional concrete block.
Many older industrial buildings feature clinker blocks in their foundations and walls.
The garden wall was built using sturdy clinker blocks that provided both strength and insulation.
