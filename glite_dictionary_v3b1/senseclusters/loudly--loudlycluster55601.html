loudly

adverb
in a noisy, attention-getting manner
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlaʊdli / (AmE), / ˈlaʊdli / (BrE)
in a way that makes a lot of noise or with a high volume

Examples

To speak loudly
He spoke so loudly that everyone in the restaurant turned to look at him.
She protested loudly when she saw her favorite book was missing.
The children laughed loudly as they played in the park.
During the final movement, the brass section entered loudly, filling the concert hall with sound.
She noticed that the instructions marked 'forte' meant the section should be performed loudly.
The orchestra played the overture loudly to emphasize the dramatic opening.
Please do not talk loudly in the library.
The announcement was made loudly enough for everyone to hear.
The children laughed loudly during the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: noisily - in a way that makes a lot of noise
  • antonym: quietly - in a way that makes little or no noise
  • antonym: softly - in a quiet manner or at a low volume (musical direction)
