horn in

verb
intrude or meddle without invitation
7.5
Pronunciation: / hɔrn ɪn / (AmE), / hɔːn ɪn / (BrE)
to intrude or interfere in a situation where one is not wanted or invited, especially in a meddlesome way

Examples

To horn in on talks
He resented it when his parents would horn in on decisions that should be his alone.
I wish you wouldn't always horn in on my conversations with my friends.
Several reporters tried to horn in on the meeting, but they were quickly asked to leave.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butt in - to interrupt or interject oneself into a conversation or situation
  • synonym: meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
