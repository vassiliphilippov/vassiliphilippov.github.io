propodeum

noun
insect body segment
9.9
Pronunciation: / prəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
the first abdominal segment that is fused with the thorax in certain insects, especially wasps and bees

Examples

The propodeum segment
The propodeum of the ant is located just behind its middle pair of legs.
Scientists examined the shape of the propodeum to help identify the wasp species.
In bees, the propodeum forms a distinct part of the body between the thorax and abdomen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mesosoma - the middle section of an insect's body, including the thorax and propodeum
