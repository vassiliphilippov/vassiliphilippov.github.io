a feeling of being very tired, especially after hard work or exertion, leading to a temporary loss of strength and energy

Examples

Deep weariness
After a long day of hiking, a deep weariness settled over the group.
He spoke with a weariness that suggested he had faced many disappointments.
The weariness in her eyes made it clear she needed a break.

Synonyms

  • tiredness
    1.0 1.02/m
    tiredness is the everyday word for low energy after work, while weariness suggests deeper tiredness from heavy physical effort.
  • fatigue
    4.2 9.15/m
    fatigue is used for temporary loss of strength after hard work or study, while weariness names strong tiredness from exertion.
  • exhaustion
    3.6 7.22/m
    exhaustion is extreme, complete tiredness that can leave you unable to work, while weariness is less total and often temporary.
  • lassitude
    9.3 0.33/m
    lassitude is a formal, slow lack of energy and interest, while weariness is a common feeling after hard physical effort.
  • lethargy
    6.3 0.98/m
    lethargy is a heavy, sluggish state often linked to illness or low mood, while weariness follows exertion and is temporary.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: exhaustion - state of being extremely tired
  • synonym: fatigue - extreme tiredness resulting from mental or physical exertion
  • antonym: freshness - the state of feeling energetic and rested
