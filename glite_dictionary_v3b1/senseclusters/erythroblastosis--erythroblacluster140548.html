erythroblastosis

noun
presence of erythroblasts in the blood
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɪθ.roʊ.blæˈstoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθ.rə.blæˈstəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A blood disease characterized by the abnormal presence of immature red blood cells (erythroblasts) in the bloodstream.

Examples

Erythroblastosis in newborns
Doctors monitor high-risk pregnancies for signs of erythroblastosis.
The newborn was diagnosed with erythroblastosis shortly after birth.
Treatment options for erythroblastosis depend on the severity of the disease.
