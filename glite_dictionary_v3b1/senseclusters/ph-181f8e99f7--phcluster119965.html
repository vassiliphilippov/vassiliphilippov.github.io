pH

noun
acidity/alkalinity measurement scale
4.0 26/m
Pronunciation: / pi e…™t É / (AmE), / piÀê e…™t É / (BrE)
a measure of the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, expressed on a scale from 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most alkaline), with 7 being neutral.

Examples

pH level
Swimming pools are regularly tested to ensure the water's pH is safe for swimmers.
The pH of the soil affects which plants can grow in a garden.
Vinegar has a low pH, making it quite acidic.

  • antonym: alkalinity - the quality of being alkaline; the level of base in a substance
