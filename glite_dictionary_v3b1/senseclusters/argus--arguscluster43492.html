argus

noun
large, brilliantly patterned Asian pheasant
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrɡəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəs / (BrE)
a large, brilliantly patterned pheasant native to Southeast Asia, especially known for the male's long tail feathers with eye-like spots.

Examples

The argus pheasant
The intricate patterns on the argus's plumage are truly remarkable.
The male argus displays its spectacular tail feathers during courtship.
We saw an argus in the wildlife reserve in Malaysia.
