defensibility

noun
business competitive advantage (moat)
7.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.fən.sə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /dɪ.fen.sɪ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
In business and strategy, the quality of a company, product, or plan that allows it to keep a sustainable competitive advantage and resist rivals, often through barriers such as patents, network effects, brand strength, or scale.

Examples

Defensibility of a business
Investors assess defensibility to determine whether a startup can fend off competitors.
The product's network effects gave it strong defensibility in an already crowded market.
Without patents, the company's defensibility depended on brand recognition and rapid innovation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: competitive advantage - a feature or asset that lets a company perform better than competitors
  • synonym: moat - a sustainable competitive barrier that protects a business from rivals
  • antonym: vulnerability - the quality of being open to attack or competition; lack of defensibility
