now

noun
the present moment
1.0 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / naÊŠ / (AmE), / naÊŠ / (BrE)
the present moment; the time at which something is happening or being discussed

Examples

The now matters
Now is the perfect opportunity to start your own business.
Call me back in an hour, as I can't talk right now.
She wants an answer this very now, not later.

Synonyms

  • present moment
    2.1 1.45/m
    present moment means the current point in time, while now refers to the same present moment in normal speech.
  • currently
    1.0 104/m
    currently is used in formal or written contexts to describe what is happening, while now is used in everyday speech.
  • at present
    1.2 8.23/m
    at present appears in formal writing to mean now, while now appears more often in speech and informal writing.
  • at this point in time
    3.5 1.70/m
    at this point in time sounds formal and wordy when dating events, while now is short and common in speech.
  • present
    1.0 27/m
    present usually names the whole current period or state, while now points to the exact moment something happens.
  • this minute
    1.1 1.80/m
    this minute stresses the very immediate moment or urgency, while now can mean the present time more generally.
  • presently
    2.6 9.65/m
    presently appears in some contexts to mean soon or now, while now is the clear word for the present moment.
  • here and now
    3.5 3.28/m
    here and now focuses on the present time and place together, while now names the present time without adding place.
