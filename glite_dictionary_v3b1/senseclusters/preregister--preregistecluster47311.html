preregister

verb
to register study plans
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈrɛdʒɪstər / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈrɛdʒɪstə / (BrE)
to formally record the planned methods, hypotheses, or protocol of a scientific study or clinical trial in a public registry before data collection begins

Examples

To preregister a study
Researchers are encouraged to preregister their studies to increase transparency and credibility.
Before starting the experiment, the team decided to preregister their hypotheses and analysis plan.
Many journals now require authors to preregister clinical trials before accepting submissions.

Related Terms
