monitorial

adjective
student-led teaching
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnɪˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / ˌmɒnɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to a system of education in which older or more advanced students help teach younger or less advanced ones

Examples

Monitorial teaching method
The school introduced a monitorial approach to encourage collaboration among students.
In the nineteenth century, many classrooms used monitorial systems to manage large groups of children.
Teachers appreciated the monitorial method for its ability to involve older students in instruction.
