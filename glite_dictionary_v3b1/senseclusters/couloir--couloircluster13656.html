couloir

noun
narrow mountain passage
Pronunciation: / kuːˈlwɑr / (AmE), / kuːˈlwɑː / (BrE)
a steep, narrow gully or passage on a mountainside, often used for climbing or skiing

Examples

A steep couloir
The climbers carefully descended the steep couloir to reach the glacier below.
Snow often accumulates in the couloir, making it a popular route for experienced skiers.
A narrow couloir separated the two towering rock faces along the trail.
The climbers carefully descended the icy couloir early in the morning.
Avalanches are a risk when crossing a snow-filled couloir in spring.
She photographed the dramatic couloir cutting through the mountain's rocky face.
The climbers carefully made their way up the icy couloir before sunrise.
Avalanches are a risk when crossing a steep couloir in winter conditions.
She chose a challenging ski route that included a narrow couloir between the cliffs.
