translucent

adjective
allowing some light through, not fully clear
4.3 3.9
Pronunciation: /trænz.ˈluː.sənt/ /; / /træns.ˈluː.sənt/ (AmE), /trænz.ˈluː.sənt/ /; / /træns.ˈluː.sənt/ (BrE)
allowing light to pass through, but not transparent; so that objects on the other side are not clearly visible

Examples

A translucent material
She wore a translucent blouse that glowed in the sunlight.
The bathroom window is made of translucent glass for privacy.
The jellyfish appeared translucent as it floated through the clear water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opaque - not allowing any light to pass through
  • degree higher: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
