Ninib

proper noun
Mesopotamian solar deity, god of war and agriculture
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnɪ.nɪb/ /; / /nɪ.ˈnɪb/ (AmE), /ˈnɪ.nɪb/ /; / /nɪ.ˈnɪb/ (BrE)
In Mesopotamian mythology, a solar deity, firstborn of Bel, consort of Gula, and god of war, hunting, and agriculture; sometimes equated with the biblical Nimrod.

Examples

Ninib god of war
Ancient texts describe Ninib as a powerful god who protected warriors on the battlefield.
Some scholars equate Ninib with Nimrod, a figure mentioned in the Bible.
The temple was dedicated to Ninib, believed to watch over the crops and ensure a good harvest.
