ethnicization

noun
development of ethnic identity
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθnɪsəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛθnɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something defined by or focused on ethnic identity or ethnicity

Examples

The ethnicization process
The ethnicization of the neighborhood became evident through new cultural festivals and language classes.
Political changes contributed to the ethnicization of local institutions and community organizations.
Rapid ethnicization among the community led young people to reclaim ancestral language and festivals.
The ethnicization of politics can deepen divisions within a society.
Some historians study the ethnicization of conflicts in multiethnic regions.
Media coverage contributed to the ethnicization of the debate over language policy.
The ethnicization of social issues can lead to increased tensions between communities.
Some historians study the ethnicization of national symbols over time.
Critics argue that the ethnicization of education policies may reinforce stereotypes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to another group, often losing distinct identity
  • synonym: identity formation - the development of a distinct individual or group identity
  • antonym: integration - the process of combining different groups into a unified whole
  • synonym: racialization - the process of ascribing racial identities to a group or issue
