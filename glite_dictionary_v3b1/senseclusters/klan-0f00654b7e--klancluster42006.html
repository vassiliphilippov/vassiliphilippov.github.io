Klan

noun
secret white supremacist society in the US
9.9
Pronunciation: /klæn/ (AmE), /klæn/ (BrE)
A secret society of white Southerners in the United States, originally formed in the 19th century to resist the emancipation of slaves, and known for using terrorist tactics to suppress Black people.

Examples

Secret Klan society
Membership in the Klan was often kept secret to avoid prosecution.
The Klan was responsible for many acts of violence in the post-Civil War South.
The history book covered the rise and fall of the Klan during Reconstruction.
