brickmaking

noun
making bricks
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪkˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪkˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or industry of making bricks from clay or other materials

Examples

Traditional brickmaking process
Traditional brickmaking requires careful mixing and firing of clay.
The village has a long history of brickmaking dating back several centuries.
Modern brickmaking uses machines to speed up the production process.
The town became famous for its brickmaking during the industrial revolution.
Advances in technology have modernized traditional brickmaking methods.
Many families in the region have worked in brickmaking for generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brickwork - the bricks used in building or the work of building with bricks
  • synonym: brickworks - a factory where bricks are made
  • synonym: masonry - the craft or work of building with stone, brick, or concrete
  • synonym: pottery - the craft or process of making objects from clay
