parting shot

noun
final, often critical, remark before leaving
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrtɪŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
a final remark or action made as one leaves, often critical, pointed, or meant to have a lasting impact

Examples

A parting shot
His parting shot during the debate drew laughter from the audience.
She couldn't resist delivering a parting shot before walking out of the office for the last time.
With a sarcastic parting shot, he shut the door behind him.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: farewell - an expression of good wishes when parting
  • degree higher: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark
