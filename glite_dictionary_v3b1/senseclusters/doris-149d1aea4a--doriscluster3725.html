Doris

proper noun
sea goddess in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɔːrɪs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the wife of Nereus and mother of the Nereids, sea nymphs.

Examples

Doris the sea mother
According to the myth, Doris was revered as the mother of the Nereids.
Many statues depict Doris alongside Nereus and their fifty daughters.
The ancient Greeks believed Doris dwelled in the depths of the ocean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nereid - a sea nymph, daughter of Doris and Nereus, in Greek mythology
  • synonym: Nereus - a sea god in Greek mythology, husband of Doris
