replaceability

noun
ability to be replaced
Pronunciation: / rɪˌpleɪsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / rɪˌpleɪsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be replaced by something or someone else

Examples

The replaceability of workers
She questioned the replaceability of experienced workers in the company.
The replaceability of parts ensures the machine can be repaired quickly.
The concept of replaceability is important in discussions about sustainability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irreplaceability - the quality of being impossible to replace
