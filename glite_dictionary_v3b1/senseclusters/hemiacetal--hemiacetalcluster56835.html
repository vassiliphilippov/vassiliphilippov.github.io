hemiacetal

noun
organic compound formed from aldehydes/ketones and alcohols
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmiəˈsiːtəl / (AmE), / ˌhɛmiəˈsiːtæl / (BrE)
a compound formed as an intermediate when an aldehyde or ketone reacts with an alcohol, often in the process of forming an acetal

Examples

A hemiacetal compound
In carbohydrate chemistry, the ring form of glucose is actually a hemiacetal.
The reaction produced a hemiacetal as an intermediate before forming the final product.
The stability of a hemiacetal depends on the specific structure of the molecule.
