booty

noun
stolen goods or loot
7.7 4/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbuÀêti / (AmE), / ÀàbuÀêti / (BrE)
valuable goods or money, especially those taken by force or illegally, such as in war or by thieves.

Examples

Pirate booty
Legend says the booty from the treasure ship is still hidden on the island.
The pirates divided the booty among themselves after the raid.
The thieves escaped with a booty valued at over a million dollars.

Synonyms

  • loot
    4.4 2/m
    loot is goods taken in war, riots, or theft and appears in everyday speech, while booty sounds older and literary.
  • plunder
    5.5 1/m
    plunder is goods taken by force during war and sounds formal or historical, while booty is a general term for stolen valuables.
  • pillage
    7.9 0.03/m
    pillage often shows violent looting and destruction during war, while booty names the stolen goods taken in that attack.
  • prize
    9.1 2/m
    prize is legally captured property in war or at sea and appears in formal reports, while booty is more general and informal.
  • swag
    9.4 0.1/m
    swag is informal slang for stolen goods used by thieves or in speech, while booty is a standard word for such valuables.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loot - goods stolen or taken by force
  • synonym: plunder - property taken by force, especially in war
  • synonym: spoils - goods taken as prizes, especially in war
