blondness

noun
stereotypical blonde traits
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈblɑːnd.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈblɒnd.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or state of having blonde hair, or stereotypical traits associated with being blonde, such as naivety or superficiality, often used informally or pejoratively.

Examples

Stereotypical blondness
The character's blondeness was exaggerated for comedic effect in the movie.
She was tired of people making assumptions about her blondeness at work.
In some stories, blondeness is unfairly linked to being naive or superficial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • synonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • synonym: superficiality - lack of depth or thoroughness
