purchase

noun
means of gaining a firm hold or advantage
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝː.tʃəs /; / ˈpɜrt͡ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.tʃəs /; / ˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
a firm hold, grip, or mechanical advantage that helps you move or control something

Examples

Purchase on ice
He could get no purchase on the icy path and kept slipping.
She used her experience to gain purchase in the male-dominated industry.
Without allies, the reformers found it hard to gain any purchase in parliament.
A crowbar provides added purchase when prying up the old floorboards.
She found her foot slipping, unable to get any purchase on the wet stone.
The sailors used a block and tackle to gain enough purchase to hoist the heavy sail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grip - a firm hold; physical grasp, or a figurative position of control
  • synonym: leverage - the power to influence or gain an advantage
