Janissary

noun
elite Ottoman infantry soldier
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒænɪˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒanɪsəri / (BrE)
A member of an elite infantry unit that formed the Ottoman Sultan's household troops and bodyguards, particularly from the 14th to the 19th centuries.

Examples

A Janissary soldier
During the 16th century, a Janissary would be recruited as a young boy through the devshirme system.
Historians often debate the impact of Janissary revolts on the decline of Ottoman power.
The Janissary corps played a crucial role in the expansion of the Ottoman Empire.
