so-called

adjective
doubtfully called or named
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ ˌkɔld / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ ˌkɔːld / (BrE)
Used to show doubt or skepticism about whether a particular name, description, or title is deserved.

Examples

The so-called expert
He was introduced as a so-called friend, but he never acted like one.
I don't trust these so-called experts to give me accurate information.
The so-called miracle cure was quickly proven to be a scam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alleged - said to have happened but not yet proven
  • antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
