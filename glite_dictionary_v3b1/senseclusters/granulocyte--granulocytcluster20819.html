granulocyte

noun
a white blood cell with cytoplasmic granules
9.9 7.1
Pronunciation: /grə.ˈnuː.loʊ.saɪt/ /; / /ˈgræ.njə.loʊ.saɪt/ (AmE), /ˈgræ.njə.ləʊ.saɪt/ /; / /grə.ˈnuː.ləʊ.saɪt/ (BrE)
a type of white blood cell distinguished by the presence of granules in its cytoplasm, involved in the body's immune response

Examples

A granulocyte cell
Certain diseases can lead to a decrease in the number of granulocytes in the body.
Neutrophils are the most abundant type of granulocyte in human blood.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check the patient's granulocyte count.
