lead

noun
adjustment in aiming at a moving target
Pronunciation: / lid / (AmE), / liːd / (BrE)
the distance or angle by which the aim of a firearm is adjusted ahead of a moving target to allow for the target's movement and the projectile's travel time

Examples

Aiming lead
Calculating the correct lead is essential for hitting a moving clay target.
In skeet shooting, mastering the amount of lead makes all the difference.
The hunter had to give the duck more lead to account for its speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deflection - the adjustment of aim to compensate for movement of a target, especially in shooting
