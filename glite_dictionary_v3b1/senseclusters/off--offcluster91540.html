off

adjective
below usual standard
3.5 0.52/m
Pronunciation: / É‘f / (AmE), / É’f / (BrE)
(of a performance, day, etc.) below the usual or satisfactory standard; not as good as normal.

Examples

Off day
Her singing was noticeably off during the performance.
I'm sorry, I'm a bit off today and can't concentrate.
It was an off night for the team, and they lost easily.

Synonyms

  • off (one's) game
    7.9 0.00/m
    off (one's) game is an informal phrase for not performing well in a situation, while off means below the usual standard.
  • subpar
    4.5 1.02/m
    subpar describes something below the expected standard in quality or performance, while off means not as good as usual.
  • below par
    4.8 0.07/m
    below par often describes feeling weak or not at full strength, while off refers to something simply not as good as usual.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bad - of poor quality or a low standard

  • synonym: poor - not as good as expected or required; of a low standard
