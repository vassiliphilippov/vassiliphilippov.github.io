nervus

noun
tendon; strength (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnɜːr.vəs/ /; / /ˈner.vəs/ (AmE), /ˈnɜː.vəs/ /; / /ˈne.vəs/ (BrE)
an archaic or literary term for a tendon or sinew, or figuratively, for strength, vigor, or courage

Examples

Hero's nervus
In ancient poetry, the hero's nervus was praised as a symbol of his power.
The old manuscript described the nervus as the source of both physical and moral strength.
Scholars debated whether the term nervus referred to a tendon or to inner courage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sinew - a tendon; strong tissue connecting muscle to bone
  • synonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong
  • antonym: weakness - the state or condition of lacking strength
