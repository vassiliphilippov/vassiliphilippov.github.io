terabit

noun
unit of 1,000,000,000,000 bits
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛrəˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,000,000,000,000 (10¹²) bits, commonly used to measure data or storage capacity.

Examples

One terabit unit
Cloud storage providers are now offering several terabits of space to meet growing user demand.
Scientists collected more than a terabit of data from the telescope array during the experiment.
The new cable system can transmit data at one terabit per second, greatly increasing internet speeds.
