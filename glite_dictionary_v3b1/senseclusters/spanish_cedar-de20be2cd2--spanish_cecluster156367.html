Spanish cedar

noun
tropical tree with fragrant wood, used for boxes
Pronunciation: / ˈspænɪʃ ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a tropical American tree whose fragrant wood is used especially for making boxes, particularly cigar boxes

Examples

A Spanish cedar tree
Many guitar makers prefer Spanish cedar for its warm tone and aromatic qualities.
The humidor was crafted from Spanish cedar to preserve the cigars' freshness.
They imported Spanish cedar because of its resistance to insects and decay.
