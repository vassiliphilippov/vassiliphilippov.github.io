beach erosion

noun
loss of beach land by natural forces
4.1 3.0
Pronunciation: / bitʃ ɪˈroʊʒən / (AmE), / biːtʃ ɪˈrəʊʒən / (BrE)
the gradual wearing away and loss of sand or land on a beach due to the action of waves, tides, or weather

Examples

Beach erosion by waves
Heavy storms this winter accelerated beach erosion, washing away much of the sandy shore.
Scientists are studying beach erosion to develop better ways to protect shoreline communities.
Severe beach erosion forced the closure of several popular resorts along the coast.
