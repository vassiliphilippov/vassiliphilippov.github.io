exarticulation

noun
joint separation
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksɑːrtɪkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksɑːtɪkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the separation or dislocation of a joint or limb at the joint

Examples

Joint exarticulation
The surgeon explained that exarticulation was necessary to treat the severe injury.
After the accident, the patient suffered an exarticulation of the left knee joint.
Proper care is required after exarticulation to prevent infection and promote healing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amputation - the removal of a limb or part of a limb
  • synonym: dislocation - an injury where a bone is forced out of its joint
