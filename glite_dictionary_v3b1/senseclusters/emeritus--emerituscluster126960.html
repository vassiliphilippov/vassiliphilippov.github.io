emeritus

adjective
retired but retaining honorary title
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɛrɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛrɪtəs / (BrE)
(of a retired person, especially a professor or cleric) having retired but allowed to retain their title as an honor, often used after the title (e.g., 'professor emeritus').

Examples

Emeritus professor
After decades of teaching, he is now a professor emeritus at the university.
As emeritus dean, she continues to contribute to academic affairs.
The board invited the emeritus director to attend the annual meeting.
