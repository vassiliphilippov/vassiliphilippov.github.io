phytate

noun
phytic acid salt
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪteɪt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪteɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of phytic acid, commonly found in plant seeds and grains

Examples

Plant phytate content
Many whole grains contain high levels of phytate, which can reduce mineral absorption in the body.
Researchers are investigating how phytate in seeds affects human nutrition.
Soaking beans overnight can help lower their phytate content.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mineral - a naturally occurring inorganic substance, sometimes affected by phytate
  • synonym: phytic acid - an acid found in plant seeds that stores phosphorus
