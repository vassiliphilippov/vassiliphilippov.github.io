vacatur

noun
annulment of judgment
9.9
Pronunciation: /və.ˈkeɪ.tər/ /; / /və.ˈkæt.ər/ (AmE), /və.ˈkæt.ə/ /; / /və.ˈkeɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a court order that sets aside or annuls a previous judgment or legal decision

Examples

A vacatur order
The judge issued a vacatur of the earlier verdict after reviewing new evidence.
A successful appeal can sometimes result in a vacatur of the original court order.
The attorney filed a motion seeking vacatur of the conviction due to procedural errors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annulment - the act of declaring something invalid
