disk

noun
phonograph record; audio recording medium
2.6 2.85/m
Pronunciation: / dÉªsk / (AmE), / dÉªsk / (BrE)
a flat, circular object used for storing sound recordings, especially a phonograph record with a continuous groove that is played by a stylus

Examples

Music disk
Collectors prize rare jazz disks for their unique cover art and sound quality.
He placed the vintage disk on the turntable and gently set the needle.
The library has an extensive collection of classical music on disk.

She placed the disc carefully on the turntable and started the music.
The old phonograph could still play a disc with remarkable clarity.

Synonyms

  • phonograph record
    6.6 0.12/m
    phonograph record is the standard name for a physical music disc used on a turntable, while disk is a more general flat storage object.
  • phonograph recording disk
    9.9 0.00/m
    phonograph recording disk is a physical disc made for recording and playback on a phonograph, while disk is a broader flat circular storage object.
  • record
    3.5 35/m
    record is the common everyday word for a music disc played on a turntable, while disk sounds more technical and general.
  • phonograph recording
    7.9 0.01/m
    phonograph recording is the captured sound that may appear on a disc, while disk refers to the physical flat circular object.
  • vinyl record
    3.2 0.30/m
    vinyl record is a disc made of vinyl commonly used for music, while disk is a general flat circular storage object without material detail.
  • platter
    7.4 0.33/m
    platter is an informal slang name DJs use for a record, while disk is the neutral term for any flat circular sound storage object.
