Carry Nation

proper noun
US prohibitionist; temperance activist
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(1846–1911) a US prohibitionist famous for raiding saloons and destroying bottles of liquor with a hatchet as part of the temperance movement

Examples

Carry Nation temperance leader
Carry Nation became famous for her bold attacks on bars and saloons during the temperance movement.
Many temperance supporters idolized Carry Nation for her uncompromising stance against alcohol.
The image of Carry Nation wielding a hatchet became a symbol of the prohibition era.
Carry Nation became famous for her bold attacks on illegal saloons during the temperance movement.
History books often tell the story of how Carry Nation used a hatchet to smash bottles of alcohol.
Many people in the early 1900s either feared or admired Carry Nation for her passionate crusade against liquor.
