pouch

verb
to swell or bulge outwards
4.7
Pronunciation: / paʊt͡ʃ /; / paʊtʃ / (AmE), / paʊtʃ / (BrE)
to swell, bulge, or form a pouch-like shape, or to carry something in a pouch (especially in animals)

Examples

Dough pouches up
As the dough rose, it started to pouch over the rim of the bowl.
His cheeks began to pouch as he filled them with air.
The frog's throat would pouch when it croaked loudly.
The kangaroo was pouching her joey as she hopped across the field.
The fabric started pouching after too many items were stuffed inside.
Some birds are known for pouching food in their beaks to feed their young.
