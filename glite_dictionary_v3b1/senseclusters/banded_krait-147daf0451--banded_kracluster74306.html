banded krait

noun
venomous Asian snake with black and yellow bands
Pronunciation: / ˈbændɪd ˌkraɪt / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd ˌkraɪt / (BrE)
a sluggish, highly venomous Asian snake with black and yellow bands, belonging to the species Bungarus fasciatus

Examples

The banded krait snake
Despite its potent venom, the banded krait is usually not aggressive toward humans.
Spotting a banded krait in the wild is rare, as it is primarily nocturnal and shy.
The banded krait is known for its distinctive black and yellow stripes and is found throughout South and Southeast Asia.
