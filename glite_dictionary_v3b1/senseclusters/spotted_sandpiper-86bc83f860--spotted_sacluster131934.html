spotted sandpiper

noun
a North American wading bird species
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑtɪd ˈsæn(d)ˌpaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈsæn(d)ˌpaɪpə / (BrE)
a small North American wading bird with brown upperparts and distinctive black spots on its white underparts, especially in breeding plumage

Examples

The spotted sandpiper bird
During migration, spotted sandpipers can be found in a variety of wetland habitats.
The spotted sandpiper is known for its unique teetering walk.
We saw a spotted sandpiper feeding along the riverbank this morning.
