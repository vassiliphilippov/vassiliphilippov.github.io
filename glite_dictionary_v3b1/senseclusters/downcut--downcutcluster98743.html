downcut

adjective
cutting downward
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (BrE)
describing a cutting tool or motion that removes material downward, pulling chips toward the workpiece to reduce splintering on the top surface

Examples

Downcut blade
A downcut blade helps prevent splintering on the top surface of plywood.
For a cleaner finish, the carpenter used a downcut router bit on the laminate.
When working with delicate veneers, a downcut tool is often recommended.
