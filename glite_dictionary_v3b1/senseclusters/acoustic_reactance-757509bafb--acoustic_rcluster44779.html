acoustic reactance

noun
imaginary part of acoustic impedance in acoustics
8.9
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk riˈæktəns / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk riˈæktəns / (BrE)
The imaginary component of acoustic impedance, representing the opposition to the flow of sound due to inertia and elasticity rather than energy loss.

Examples

Acoustic reactance measurement
An increase in acoustic reactance can reduce the efficiency of loudspeakers.
Engineers measure acoustic reactance to design better soundproofing materials.
The shape of the wall affects the acoustic reactance in a concert hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acoustic impedance - Total opposition to sound flow in a medium
  • degree lower: acoustic resistance - Real component of acoustic impedance, representing energy loss
