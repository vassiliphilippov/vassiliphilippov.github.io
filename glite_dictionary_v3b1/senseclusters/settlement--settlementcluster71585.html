settlement

noun
the act of founding a colony
1.1 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈse.təl.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈse.təl.mənt/ (BrE)
a place where people establish a community and live together, especially in a new or foreign region; or the act of creating such a community

Examples

A new settlement
Archaeologists studied evidence of ancient settlement patterns along the coast.
Dutch settlement in South Africa significantly altered the region's development.
The British settlement of Australia began in the late 18th century.

The first European settlement in the region was established in 1620.
They built a small settlement near the river where families could farm and trade.
New houses have been built around the old settlement in recent years.
She grew up in a rural settlement, far from the nearest city.
The archaeological team discovered a prehistoric settlement near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the process of establishing control over a foreign area and settling it
  • synonym: colony - a territory settled and ruled by people from another land
  • degree higher: town - larger than a village or settlement, but smaller than a city
  • synonym: village - a small group of houses and other buildings in a country area
