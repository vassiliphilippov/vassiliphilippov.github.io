compound lens

noun
an optical system of multiple lenses
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmˌpaʊnd lɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpaʊnd lɛnz / (BrE)
an optical system consisting of two or more lenses aligned on a common axis, used to improve image quality or magnification

Examples

A compound lens system
Cameras with a compound lens can produce clearer, less distorted images.
She studied the structure of a compound lens in her optics class.
The microscope uses a compound lens to achieve higher magnification than a simple lens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: simple lens - a single lens, not a system of lenses
