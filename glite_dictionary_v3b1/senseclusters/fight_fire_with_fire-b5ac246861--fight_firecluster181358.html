fight fire with fire

verb
respond with similar or aggressive tactics
Pronunciation: / faɪt ˈfaɪɚ wɪð ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / faɪt ˈfaɪə wɪð ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to use the same methods as your opponent, especially aggressive or harmful ones, in order to defeat them

Examples

To fight fire with fire
If our competitors are cheating, we may be forced to fight fire with fire to remain in business.
Politicians often fight fire with fire during heated debates.
She wasn't usually confrontational, but this time she decided to fight fire with fire.
