ground

noun
prepared painting surface (art)
6.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /graʊnd/ (AmE), /graʊnd/ (BrE)
the background or base in art, photography, or perception, against which the main subject stands out

Examples

A painted ground
A smooth ground helps the colors appear more vibrant on the canvas.
He prepared the wooden panel with a special ground to ensure the oil paint would adhere properly.
The artist applied gesso to the ground before starting her painting.
In figure-ground perception, the ground refers to the area that appears to recede behind the object in focus.
Psychologists study how we distinguish the ground from the prominent elements in a scene.
The artist deliberately blurred the ground to make the main subject stand out.
A white ground helps colors appear more vibrant in oil painting.
Before painting, it is important to let the ground dry completely.
The artist carefully applied a thin ground before starting to paint the canvas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: background - the part of a scene or picture that is behind the main subject
  • antonym: figure - the object in a visual field that stands out from the background
  • antonym: foreground - the part of a scene or picture that is nearest to and in front of the viewer
  • synonym: primer - preparatory coating put on materials before painting
