brief

adjective
concise; succinct
1.0
Pronunciation: / briːf / (AmE), / briːf / (BrE)
using few words or lasting only a short time; concise or short in duration

Examples

A brief answer
Her explanation was remarkably brief yet conveyed all the essential information.
Please keep your comments brief due to limited time.
The meeting ended early because the agenda was quite brief.
Her visit to Paris was brief, lasting only two days.
The manager made a brief announcement and then left the room.
We had a brief conversation before the meeting started.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • antonym: lengthy - taking a great deal of time or many words
  • antonym: long - lasting or taking a great amount of time
  • synonym: short - lasting or covering a small amount of time or space
