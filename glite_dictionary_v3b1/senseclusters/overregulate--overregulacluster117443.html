overregulate

verb
to impose too many rules
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈrɛɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈrɛɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to control or restrict something by making too many rules or laws, often in a way that is excessive or unnecessary

Examples

Critics claim that new policies will overregulate the technology industry and slow down innovation.

Some believe that overregulating the market can lead to unnecessary bureaucracy and inefficiency.
Some experts warn that governments may overregulate the technology sector, slowing down innovation.
If we overregulate small businesses, it could discourage entrepreneurs from starting new companies.
The new policy aims not to overregulate but to ensure fair competition in the market.
Some experts argue that the government has overregulated the industry, making it hard for new businesses to compete.
If we overregulate small companies, we might discourage innovation and growth.
The new policies risk overregulating the market, leading to unnecessary bureaucracy.
