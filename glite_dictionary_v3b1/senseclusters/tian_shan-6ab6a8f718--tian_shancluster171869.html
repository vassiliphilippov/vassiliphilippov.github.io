Tian Shan

proper noun
mountain range in Central Asia
Pronunciation: / tiˈɑːn ˈʃɑːn / (AmE), / tiˈæn ˈʃæn / (BrE)
a major mountain range in Central Asia, stretching about 1,500 miles (2,400 km) across several countries, including China, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan.

Examples

The Tian Shan mountains
Glaciers in the Tian Shan are a vital source of water for Central Asia.
Many rare plants and animals inhabit the remote valleys of the Tian Shan.
The Tian Shan rises dramatically along the border between China and Kyrgyzstan.
