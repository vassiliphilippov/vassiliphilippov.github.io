ligamentum

noun
anatomical ligament term
9.5
Pronunciation: / lɪɡəˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / lɪɡəˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
a Latin term for a ligament, commonly used in anatomical names to refer to a band of tissue connecting bones or supporting organs

Examples

Ligamentum flavum
The ligamentum flavum connects the vertebrae in the spine.
During surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the ligamentum teres.
An injury to the ligamentum patellae can affect knee stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ligament - a strong band of tissue connecting bones or supporting organs
