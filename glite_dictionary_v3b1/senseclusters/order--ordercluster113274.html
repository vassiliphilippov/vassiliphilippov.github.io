order

noun
style of classical architectural columns
5.3 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.dər/ /; / /ˈɑːr.dər/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
(Architecture) any of the styles of classical Greek or Roman architecture distinguished by their characteristic type of column and entablature, such as the Doric, Ionic, or Corinthian orders.

Examples

Doric order
Roman builders often mixed elements from different orders to create unique designs in their temples.
The Parthenon was built in the Doric order, characterized by sturdy columns and simple capitals.
The architect chose the Corinthian order for the building's grand entrance, adding ornate detail to each column.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Corinthian - the most ornate of the classical orders of architecture
  • degree lower: Doric - the simplest and sturdiest of the classical orders of architecture
  • synonym: Ionic - a classical order of architecture known for its scrolled capitals
