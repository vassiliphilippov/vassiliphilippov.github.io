Dyaus Pita

proper noun
Hindu sky god, father of the gods
Pronunciation: / djaʊs ˈpiːtə / (AmE), / djaʊs ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
A god of the sky in Hindu mythology, regarded as the father of the gods and often equated with the Greek god Zeus.

Examples

Dyaus Pita god
In ancient Vedic texts, Dyaus Pita is frequently mentioned alongside Prithvi, the earth goddess.
Scholars often compare Dyaus Pita to Zeus, highlighting their shared role as sky fathers in Indo-European mythology.
The hymns praise Dyaus Pita for illuminating the heavens and protecting humanity.
