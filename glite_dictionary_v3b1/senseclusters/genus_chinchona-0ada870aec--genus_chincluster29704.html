genus Chinchona

noun
genus of South American medicinal trees
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs tʃɪnˈkoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tʃɪnˈkəʊnə / (BrE)
a genus of trees native to the Andes in South America, known for their medicinal bark, which is a source of quinine

Examples

Genus Chinchona trees
Scientists are studying several species within the genus Chinchona for their pharmacological properties.
The genus Chinchona is native to the Andean forests of South America.
The bark of the genus Chinchona was historically used to treat malaria.
