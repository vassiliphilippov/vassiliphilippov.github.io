hypertrichosis

noun
excessive hair growth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərtrɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpətrɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by excessive hair growth on areas of the body where hair is usually minimal or absent

Examples

Excessive hypertrichosis hair
The doctor explained that hypertrichosis can be present from birth or develop later in life.
Some rare genetic disorders are known to cause hypertrichosis in affected individuals.
Treatment options for hypertrichosis may include hair removal techniques and addressing underlying causes.
