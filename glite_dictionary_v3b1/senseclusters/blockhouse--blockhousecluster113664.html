blockhouse

noun
fortified defensive building
6.2 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈblɑkˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈblɒkˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A small, sturdy building with thick walls, designed for defense against enemy attack, often with openings for firing weapons.

Examples

A blockhouse building
An old stone blockhouse still stands on the hill, a reminder of past conflicts.
The commander ordered everyone into the blockhouse as enemy troops approached.
The soldiers took cover inside the blockhouse during the heavy bombardment.
