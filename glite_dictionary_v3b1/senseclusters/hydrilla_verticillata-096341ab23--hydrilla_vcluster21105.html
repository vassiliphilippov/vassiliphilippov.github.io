Hydrilla verticillata

noun
invasive aquatic plant species

Pronunciation: /haɪ.ˈdrɪl.ə vɜːr.tɪ.ˈsɪl.ə.tə/ /; / /hɪ.ˈdrɪl.ə vɜːr.tɪ.ˈsɪl.ə.tə/ (AmE), /haɪ.ˈdrɪl.ə vɜː.tɪ.ˈsɪl.ə.tə/ /; / /hɪ.ˈdrɪl.ə vɜː.tɪ.ˈsɪl.ə.tə/ (BrE)
An aquatic plant native to the Old World, known for its whorled lanceolate leaves and single flowers, naturalized in the southern United States where it is an invasive species.

Examples

Hydrilla verticillata aquatic plant
Hydrilla verticillata has spread rapidly in Florida, clogging many waterways and disrupting native ecosystems.
Because Hydrilla verticillata reproduces quickly, it is considered one of the most problematic aquatic weeds.
Scientists are studying ways to control the growth of Hydrilla verticillata in lakes and rivers.
