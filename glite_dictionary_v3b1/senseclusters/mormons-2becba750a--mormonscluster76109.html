Mormons

noun
members of the Latter-day Saints church
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔr.mənz / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.mənz / (BrE)
members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, a religious group founded by Joseph Smith in 1830 with headquarters in Salt Lake City, Utah

Examples

Mormons in Utah
Many Mormons live in Utah, where their church has its headquarters.
Several Mormons volunteered to help with the community service event.
The history of the Mormons includes a long period of migration across the United States.
