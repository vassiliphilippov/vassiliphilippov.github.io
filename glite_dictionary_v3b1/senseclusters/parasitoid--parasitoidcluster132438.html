parasitoid

noun
insect that kills host
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌsɪtɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpærəsɪtɔɪd / (BrE)
an organism (usually an insect) whose larvae live in or on another organism (the host) and eventually kill it as part of their development

Examples

A parasitoid insect
The wasp is a well-known parasitoid that lays its eggs inside caterpillars.
Scientists study parasitoids to find natural ways to control agricultural pests.
Unlike a true parasite, a parasitoid eventually kills its host during development.
A parasitoid wasp injects its eggs into a caterpillar, which is eventually killed by the larvae.
Scientists study the life cycle of the parasitoid to understand its impact on pest populations.
Unlike a typical parasite, a parasitoid always kills its host as part of its development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parasite - an organism that lives on or in a host and benefits at the host's expense
  • antonym: predator - an animal that hunts and kills other animals for food
