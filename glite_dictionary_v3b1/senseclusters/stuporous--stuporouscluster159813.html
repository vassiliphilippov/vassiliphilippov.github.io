stuporous

adjective
in a dazed, semi-conscious state
9.6 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàstuÀêp…ôr…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàstjuÀêp…ôr…ôs / (BrE)
in a state of partial unconsciousness or insensibility; stunned, confused, and slow to react, often due to illness, exhaustion, or intoxication

Examples

Stuporous state after accident
After the accident, he was stuporous and unable to answer simple questions.
The exhausted runner became stuporous and needed immediate medical attention.
The patient remained stuporous despite the doctor's efforts to wake him.

Synonyms

  • stupefied
    7.0 0.02/m
    stupefied is frozen by shock or surprise and cannot think clearly, while stuporous describes a medical partial unconsciousness and slow response.
  • groggy
    5.6 1.37/m
    groggy is weak, unsteady, and confused from tiredness or alcohol, while stuporous implies deeper, medically reduced consciousness.
  • dazed
    4.8 4.17/m
    dazed is unable to think clearly after a blow or shock and feels disoriented, while stuporous means partly unconscious medically.
  • stunned
    3.0 1.38/m
    stunned is shocked and slow to react after a sudden event, while stuporous describes a heavier, often medical, reduced consciousness.
  • logy
    9.8 0.04/m
    logy is dull and sluggish in movement or thought from tiredness or illness, while stuporous is a deeper partial unconsciousness.
  • foggy
    3.4 0.88/m
    foggy is mentally unclear or slow to think in a mild, hazy way, while stuporous means almost unconscious and medically serious.
