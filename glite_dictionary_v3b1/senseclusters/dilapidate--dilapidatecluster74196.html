dilapidate

verb
fall into disrepair or ruin
7.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈlæpɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlapɪdeɪt /; / dɪˈlæpɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to fall into a state of ruin or to cause something to become ruined, usually because of neglect

Examples

To dilapidate a building
If left uncared for, even the finest buildings will eventually dilapidate.
The abandoned mansion began to dilapidate after years of neglect.
Without regular maintenance, the bridge started to dilapidate over the decades.
If we do not maintain our public buildings, they will quickly dilapidate and become unsafe.
Prolonged neglect had dilapidated the once-grand estate, leaving it nearly uninhabitable.
The owner allowed the old mansion to dilapidate over the years, ignoring repeated calls for repairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - to rot or decompose through natural processes
  • antonym: restore - to return something to a former condition
  • synonym: ruin - to fall into a state of complete disrepair
