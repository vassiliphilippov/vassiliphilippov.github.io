proposition

verb
to suggest a plan
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to suggest a plan, idea, or proposal to someone for their consideration or agreement

Examples

To proposition a colleague
She decided to proposition her manager with a new marketing strategy.
After much thought, he propositioned the team about changing the project timeline.
It can be difficult to proposition a group with an unfamiliar idea.
He propositioned his colleague with an idea for a new project.
She felt uncomfortable when a stranger propositioned her at the bar.
After much thought, I decided to proposition the manager about changing our work schedule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: offer - to present something for acceptance or rejection
  • synonym: propose - to put forward an idea or plan for consideration
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept a plan or idea
  • synonym: suggest - to mention an idea or plan for someone to think about
