antebellum architecture

noun
pre-Civil War Southern U.S. architecture
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈbɛləm ˌɑrkəˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈbɛləm ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a style of architecture prominent in the southern United States before the Civil War, characterized by grand mansions, columns, and elaborate decorative elements

Examples

Southern antebellum architecture
Her thesis focused on the influence of European styles on antebellum architecture in the American South.
Many tourists visit the city each year to admire the antebellum architecture lining its historic streets.
The plantation's antebellum architecture features grand columns and sweeping verandas.
