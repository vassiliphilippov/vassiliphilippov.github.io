inhibit

verb
make someone unable to act naturally
6.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to make someone unable to act naturally or to restrain their emotions, desires, or behavior

Examples

To inhibit a person
Being watched by the teacher can inhibit students from speaking freely in class.
His strict upbringing tended to inhibit his ability to express emotions.
Worrying about mistakes may inhibit your performance during the interview.
His shyness tended to inhibit him from speaking up in class.
People often inhibit their true feelings in unfamiliar situations.
She tried to inhibit her laughter during the serious meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give someone confidence or support to do something
  • antonym: express - to show or make apparent, especially emotions or thoughts
  • synonym: restrain - to hold something back or keep under control
  • synonym: suppress - forcibly put an end to or prevent the expression of feelings or actions
