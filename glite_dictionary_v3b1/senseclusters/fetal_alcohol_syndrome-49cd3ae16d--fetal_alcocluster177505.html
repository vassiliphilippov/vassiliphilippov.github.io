fetal alcohol syndrome

noun
birth defects caused by prenatal alcohol exposure
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːtəl ˈælkəhɑl ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəl ˈælkəhɒl ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which physical and mental development is impaired due to alcohol exposure by the mother during pregnancy.

Examples

Fetal alcohol syndrome symptoms
Children with fetal alcohol syndrome may have distinctive facial features and growth problems.
Doctors warn that drinking during pregnancy increases the risk of fetal alcohol syndrome.
Early diagnosis of fetal alcohol syndrome can help provide the support a child needs.
