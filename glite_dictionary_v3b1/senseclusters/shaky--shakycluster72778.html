Synonyms

  • trembling
    2.6 0.03/m
    trembling means shaking slightly and uncontrollably, often from fear or cold, while shaky describes general lack of steadiness.
  • unsteady
    3.3 0.7/m
    unsteady means not firm or balanced and likely to fall, while shaky focuses on physical shaking or lack of stability.
  • trembly
    4.4 0.02/m
    trembly describes a slight nervous or weak shaking of the body, while shaky covers general physical unsteadiness.
  • quivery
    5.0 0.01/m
    quivery means trembling in small quick movements from emotion or weakness, while shaky is a broader lack of steadiness.
  • shivering
    2.2 0.1/m
    shivering is shaking mostly from cold or strong feeling, while shaky can mean any physical unsteadiness.
  • wobbly
    3.6 2/m
    wobbly describes moving side to side and often looks unstable, while shaky emphasizes actual trembling or weak balance.
  • tottery
    8.4 0.01/m
    tottery means moving or standing unsteadily, often from old age or weakness, while shaky refers to general physical trembling.
