A feeling of annoyance, dissatisfaction, or lack of approval.

Examples

Great displeasure
His actions caused great displeasure among the staff.
She couldn't hide her displeasure when she saw the messy room.
The teacher expressed her displeasure at the students' behavior.

Synonyms

  • annoyance
    2.8 3.33/m
    annoyance is a sharp, immediate feeling of being bothered, while displeasure is a more general lack of approval or satisfaction.
  • irritation
    3.6 3.48/m
    irritation is a stinging, often repeated feeling of being annoyed, while displeasure is a broader sense of unhappiness or dislike.
  • dissatisfaction
    3.7 5.73/m
    dissatisfaction is unhappiness about unmet needs or results, while displeasure is a general feeling of annoyance or lack of approval.
  • disapproval
    3.0 4.53/m
    disapproval is an expressed judgment or objection to actions or ideas, while displeasure is a quieter personal feeling of dislike.
