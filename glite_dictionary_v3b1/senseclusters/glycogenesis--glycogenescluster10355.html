glycogenesis

noun
conversion of glucose to glycogen
2.0 8.4
Pronunciation: / ɡlaɪkoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs /; / ˌɡlaɪkoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ɡlʌɪkəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs /; / ˌɡlʌɪkəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which glucose is converted into glycogen for storage in the body, especially in the liver and muscles

Examples

Glycogenesis process
Glycogenesis is stimulated by insulin after a carbohydrate-rich meal.
Liver cells play a key role in glycogenesis during energy storage.
The rate of glycogenesis increases when blood glucose levels are high.
After a meal, glycogenesis occurs in the liver, storing excess glucose as glycogen.
Certain hormones regulate glycogenesis to help maintain stable blood sugar levels.
Defective enzymes can disrupt glycogenesis and lead to metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: glycogenolysis - the breakdown of glycogen to glucose
  • antonym: glycolysis - the breakdown of glucose to release energy
