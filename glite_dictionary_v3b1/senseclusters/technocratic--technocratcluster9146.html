technocratic

adjective
favoring technical solutions
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛknəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɛknəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
emphasizing or involving technical experts, methods, or solutions, especially in government or management

Examples

A technocratic government
The new government adopted a technocratic approach to solving economic problems.
Some critics argue that a technocratic system ignores important social factors.
Her technocratic leadership style prioritized data and efficiency over tradition.
The new administration implemented a technocratic system to improve efficiency in public services.
Critics argued that the technocratic government lacked popular support and transparency.
A technocratic approach was chosen to address the country's complex infrastructure problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bureaucratic - focused on administrative procedures and official rules
  • antonym: democratic - relating to government by the people or their elected representatives
  • synonym: technical - relating to practical or mechanical arts or applied sciences
