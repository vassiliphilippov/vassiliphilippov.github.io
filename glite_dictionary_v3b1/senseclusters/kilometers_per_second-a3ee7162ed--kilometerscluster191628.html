kilometers per second

noun
unit of speed (km/s)
Pronunciation: / kɪˈlɑːmɪtɚz pɚ ˈsɛkənd /; / kɪˈlɑːmɪtərz pər səˈkɑːnd / (AmE), / kɪˈlɒmɪtəz pə ˈsɛkənd /; / kɪˈlɒmɪtəz pə səˈkənd / (BrE)
A unit of speed equal to the number of kilometers traveled in one second.

Examples

Kilometers per second speed
Astronomers measured the star's movement at 30 kilometers per second.
Sound in air travels much slower, at about 0.3 kilometers per second.
The spacecraft was traveling at nearly 12 kilometers per second on its approach to Mars.
Light moves through a vacuum at nearly 300,000 kilometres per second.
Scientists measured the meteor's velocity to be around 70 kilometres per second.
The spacecraft traveled at an astonishing speed of 12 kilometres per second.
