reknit

verb
to restore unity
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈnɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈnɪt / (BrE)
to restore or repair unity, relationships, or social bonds, especially after they have been damaged.

Examples

To reknit a friendship
After years of conflict, the new leader tried to reknit the divided community.
It took time and effort to reknit the friendship after their argument.
The organization aims to reknit social ties that were damaged by the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: break - to separate into pieces or damage unity
  • synonym: rebuild - to construct again or restore after damage
  • synonym: repair - to fix or mend something that is broken
