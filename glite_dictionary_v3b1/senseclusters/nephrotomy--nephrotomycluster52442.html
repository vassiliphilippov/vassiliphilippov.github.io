nephrotomy

noun
surgical incision into a kidney
Pronunciation: / nɛˈfrɑtəmi / (AmE), / nɛˈfrɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into a kidney, typically done to remove a kidney stone

Examples

A nephrotomy surgery
Complications after nephrotomy are rare but can include bleeding or infection.
Prior to developing less invasive techniques, nephrotomy was the standard approach for some kidney conditions.
The surgeon performed a nephrotomy to remove the large kidney stone.
