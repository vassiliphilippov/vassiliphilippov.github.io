Sephardic Jew

noun
Jew of Spanish, Portuguese, or North African descent
7.1 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàf…ërd…™k Ààd íu / (AmE), / s…™Ààf…ëÀêd…™k Ààd íuÀê / (BrE)
A Jew descended from the Jews who lived in Spain, Portugal, or North Africa, especially before their expulsion from Spain in the late 15th century.

Examples

Sephardic Jews from Spain
Her grandparents were Sephardic Jews who emigrated from Morocco to France after World War II.
Many Sephardic Jews maintain unique traditions in cuisine and music that differ from those of Ashkenazi Jews.
The synagogue was built by a community of Sephardic Jews who arrived in the 16th century.

Synonyms

  • Sephardi
    9.1 0.11/m
    Sephardi names a person of Spanish, Portuguese, or North African Jewish descent, while Sephardic Jew is a fuller phrase used in formal or descriptive writing.
  • Sephardic
    7.7 0.12/m
    Sephardic often describes the community or tradition from Spain or Portugal, while Sephardic Jew names an individual descendant of that heritage.
