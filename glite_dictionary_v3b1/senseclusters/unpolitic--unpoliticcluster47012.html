unpolitic

adjective
lacking tact
8.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpɑːlətɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɒlɪtɪk / (BrE)
not showing good judgment, tact, or wisdom in dealing with people or situations

Examples

An unpolitic choice
It would be unpolitic to bring up that topic at the meeting.
Her unpolitic remarks offended several members of the team.
Making such an unpolitic decision could damage your reputation.
It would be unpolitic to criticize your boss in front of the entire team.
Making such a statement during negotiations was considered unpolitic by her colleagues.
He realized it was unpolitic to reveal their strategy before the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impolitic - not wise or not showing good judgment in a situation
  • synonym: imprudent - not showing care for the consequences of an action
  • synonym: inexpedient - not suitable or advisable in the circumstances
  • antonym: politic - showing good judgment and practical sense
  • synonym: tactless - not having or showing sensitivity in dealing with others
