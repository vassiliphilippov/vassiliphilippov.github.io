Vespucci

proper noun
Florentine explorer; America named after him
Pronunciation: / veˈspuːtʃi / (AmE), / veˈspuːtʃi / (BrE)
Amerigo Vespucci (1454–1512), a Florentine navigator and explorer after whom the Americas are named; he explored the coast of South America.

Examples

Vespucci voyages
Vespucci's voyages along the South American coast significantly expanded Europe's knowledge of the world.
Many historians credit Vespucci with proving that the New World was not Asia, but a separate continent.
The continent of America derives its name from Vespucci, the renowned Italian explorer.
