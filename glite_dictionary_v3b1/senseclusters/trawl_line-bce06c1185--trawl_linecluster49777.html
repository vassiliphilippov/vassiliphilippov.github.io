trawl line

noun
long fishing line with attached hooks
8.6
Pronunciation: / trɔl laɪn / (AmE), / trɔːl laɪn / (BrE)
A long fishing line with many shorter lines and hooks attached, typically suspended between buoys and used to catch fish.

Examples

A trawl line with hooks
Fishermen set the trawl line in the deep sea, hoping for a big catch overnight.
Inspecting each hook on the trawl line is a time-consuming task for the crew.
The storm snapped their trawl line, and they lost a day's worth of fish.

Related Terms
