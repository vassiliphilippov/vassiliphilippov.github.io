contribute

verb
help to cause a particular result
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈtrɪbjut /; / kənˈtrɪbjuːt / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to give or add something (such as money, help, ideas, or qualities) in order to help achieve a result or as part of a group effort

Examples

Your ideas contribute greatly to the success of the project.
Her cheerful attitude contributes a positive energy to the office environment.
The singer’s voice contributes an emotional depth to the performance.
Unique artwork contributes character and charm to the living room.
