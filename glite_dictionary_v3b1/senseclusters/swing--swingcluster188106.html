swing

verb
alternate dramatically between extremes
Pronunciation: / swɪŋ / (AmE), / swɪŋ / (BrE)
to move quickly and suddenly from one condition, value, or feeling to another, often between extremes

Examples

To swing between moods
Her emotions swing rapidly from excitement to disappointment.
Interest rates may swing up or down depending on economic conditions.
Stock prices can swing dramatically in a single day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluctuate - to rise and fall irregularly in number or amount
  • antonym: stabilize - to make or become unlikely to change, fail, or decline
