seemly

adjective
proper or appropriate
7.4 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêmli / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêmli / (BrE)
conforming to accepted standards of behavior or propriety; appropriate or proper

Examples

A seemly behavior
Her seemly conduct impressed everyone at the formal dinner.
It is not seemly to interrupt someone while they are speaking.
They behaved in a seemly manner at the ceremony.

Synonyms

  • becoming
    4.6 0.00/m
    becoming often means clothing or behavior flatters or suits someone, while seemly simply means proper and socially acceptable.
  • decorous
    7.9 0.48/m
    decorous describes calm, polite, and dignified behavior in formal settings, while seemly means appropriate to general standards of propriety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proper - appropriate, suitable, according to custom
  • antonym: unseemly - not proper or appropriate
