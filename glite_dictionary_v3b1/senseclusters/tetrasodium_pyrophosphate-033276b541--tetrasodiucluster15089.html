tetrasodium pyrophosphate

noun
a sodium salt used in detergents
8.7
Pronunciation: /tet.rə.ˈsoʊ.diː.əm paɪ.rə.ˈfɑːs.feɪt/ (AmE), /tet.rə.ˈsəʊ.diː.əm paɪ.rə.ˈfɒs.feɪt/ (BrE)
a sodium salt of pyrophosphoric acid (Na4P2O7), used mainly as a builder in soaps and detergents and for water softening

Examples

Tetrasodium pyrophosphate in toothpaste
Tetrasodium pyrophosphate is commonly added to toothpaste as a tartar control agent.
Food manufacturers sometimes use tetrasodium pyrophosphate as a food additive to retain moisture.
The cleaning power of some laundry detergents is enhanced by tetrasodium pyrophosphate.
