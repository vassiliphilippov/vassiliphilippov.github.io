ballyhoo

verb
promote noisily or exaggeratedly
Pronunciation: / ˈbæliˌhu / (AmE), / ˈbæliˌhuː / (BrE)
to publicize or praise something loudly, noisily, or with exaggeration, often to attract attention

Examples

To ballyhoo a movie
Critics complained that the festival was ballyhooed as a cultural breakthrough, but failed to deliver on its promises.
The company ballyhooed its latest smartphone with flashy advertisements and events.
The new movie was ballyhooed all over the media before its release.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advertise - to make something known to the public, usually in order to sell it
  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
  • synonym: promote - to support or actively encourage
