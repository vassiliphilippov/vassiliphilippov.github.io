bulb

noun
(archaic) old term for medulla oblongata
6.9 6.2
Pronunciation: /bʌlb/ /; / /bʌb/ (AmE), /bʌlb/ /; / /bʌb/ (BrE)
a rounded swelling or expansion in a canal, vessel, or organ in the body (in anatomy or medicine)

Examples

The brain bulb
In outdated medical texts, the term bulb is sometimes used instead of medulla oblongata.
Some early anatomists referred to the medulla as the bulb of the brain due to its shape.
The vital functions regulated by the bulb include breathing and heart rate.
An ultrasound revealed a cystic structure adjacent to the duodenal bulb.
Aneurysms often present as a bulb along the affected blood vessel.
The aortic bulb is located at the root of the aorta, just above the heart.
