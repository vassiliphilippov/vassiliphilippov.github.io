hydrogenate

verb
add hydrogen to (chemistry)

Pronunciation: /ˈhaɪ.drə.dʒə.neɪt/ (AmE), /ˈhaɪ.drɒ.dʒə.neɪt/ /; / /ˈhaɪ.drə.dʒə.neɪt/ (BrE)
to combine or treat (a substance) with hydrogen, especially to add hydrogen to an unsaturated organic compound

Examples

Many food manufacturers hydrogenate vegetable oils to extend shelf life.
Scientists hydrogenated the compound to create a more stable product.
The process of hydrogenating fats can lead to the formation of trans fats.
