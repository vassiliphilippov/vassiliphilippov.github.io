retrogression

noun
reversion to simpler biological form
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a process in which an organism or system reverts from a more complex to a simpler form, especially in biological development or evolution

Examples

Evolutionary retrogression
Some scientists argue that cave-dwelling animals represent cases of evolutionary retrogression.
The fossil record provides evidence of retrogression in certain organisms under extreme environmental pressures.
The species exhibited retrogression, losing many of the complex features seen in its ancestors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progression - advancement from a simpler to a more complex form
  • synonym: regression - return to an earlier or less developed state
