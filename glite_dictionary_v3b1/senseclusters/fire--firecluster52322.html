the process of burning that produces heat, light, and often smoke; a flame or source of heat (such as a campfire or fireplace); or a destructive burning event

Examples

Camp fire
He roasted the fish over the open fire at the campsite.
Please set the pot on the fire to boil the water.
They gathered around the fire to cook their dinner and share stories.

The old barn was destroyed in a devastating fire last summer.

Synonyms

  • burning
    1.0 11/m
    burning describes the action or process of something being consumed by flame, while fire names the flame, heat source, or destructive event.
  • combustion
    5.6 6.52/m
    combustion is the technical chemical process with oxygen that produces heat and light, while fire is the visible flame or burning event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blaze - a very large or fiercely burning fire
  • synonym: campfire - an outdoor fire used for warmth or cooking
  • synonym: conflagration - an extensive and destructive fire
  • antonym: extinguish - to cause a fire to stop burning
  • synonym: flame - a hot, bright stream of burning gas from something on fire
  • synonym: hearth - the floor of a fireplace, often used to refer to a home fire
  • degree lower: smolder - to burn slowly with smoke but no flame
