substitutionary

adjective
serving as a substitute
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbstɪˈtuːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃənəri / (BrE)
serving as a substitute, especially by taking the place, punishment, or responsibility of another person, often in legal, religious, or formal situations

Examples

Substitutionary atonement
The doctrine of substitutionary atonement is central to some branches of Christianity.
He made a substitutionary payment on behalf of his friend who could not afford the fine.
The lawyer argued for a substitutionary sentence, allowing community service instead of jail time.
The lawyer argued for a substitutionary arrangement in the contract.
Some religious teachings emphasize the importance of substitutionary atonement.
A substitutionary payment was made on behalf of the debtor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not a substitute
  • synonym: substitutive - serving to replace or act as a substitute
  • synonym: vicarious - experienced or done by one person as a substitute for another
