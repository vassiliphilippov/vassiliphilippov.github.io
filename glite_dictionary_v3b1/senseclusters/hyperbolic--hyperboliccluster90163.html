hyperbolic

adjective
exaggerated; overstated
6.7 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pərˈbɑ.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈbɒl.ɪk / (BrE)
(of language, style, or claims) exaggerated to an extreme degree; not meant to be taken literally.

Examples

Hyperbolic language
Critics pointed out the hyperbolic style of his writing, which often relied on exaggeration.
Her hyperbolic descriptions of the storm made it sound worse than it actually was.
The politician’s hyperbolic promises were met with skepticism by the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerated - described in a way that makes something seem better, worse, or larger than it really is
  • antonym: understated - described in a way that makes something seem less important or serious than it really is
