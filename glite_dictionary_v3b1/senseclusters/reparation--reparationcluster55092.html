reparation

noun
compensation for a wrong
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
compensation or action given to make amends for a wrong, injury, or damage

Examples

Make reparation for harm
As an act of reparation, he apologized publicly and donated to charity.
The country agreed to pay reparation to those who suffered during the conflict.
Victims sought reparation for the damage caused by the company.
Many countries have paid reparation after wars as a form of compensation.
She demanded reparation for the damage caused to her reputation.
The government offered reparation to the victims of the accident.
