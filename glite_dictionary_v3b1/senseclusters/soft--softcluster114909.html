soft

adjective
easily damaged or hurt; delicate
Pronunciation: / sɔft / (AmE), / sɒft / (BrE)
not hard or firm; easily yielding to touch, delicate, or physically weak

Examples

Soft skin
A baby's soft cheeks bruise easily if not protected.
After a summer without gloves, his hands were no longer soft.
The nurse handled the newborn's soft skin with extra care.
She prefers soft cheeses over hard ones.
The child picked up the soft toy and hugged it tightly.
The pillow was so soft that my head sank right in.
After months away from the gym, she felt soft and out of shape.
He was too soft to keep up during the rigorous training exercises.
The coach criticized the team for getting soft in the off-season.
