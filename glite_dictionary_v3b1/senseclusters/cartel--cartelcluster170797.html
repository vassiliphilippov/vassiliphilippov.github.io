cartel

noun
organization formed to control competition
7.9 9.43/m
Pronunciation: / k…ëÀêrÀàt…õl / (AmE), / k…ëÀêÀàt…õl / (BrE)
an association of independent businesses or organizations formed to control production, pricing, and marketing of goods or services, often to limit competition

Examples

A cartel of companies
A cartel can reduce consumer choice and lead to higher prices for goods and services.
Several oil-producing countries formed a cartel to manage oil prices around the world.
The government took action to break up the illegal cartel controlling the drug market.

Synonyms

  • trust
    7.0 3.49/m
    a trust is a legal group of companies acting together to control markets, while a cartel is independent firms agreeing to fix prices.
  • combine
    7.1 0.15/m
    a combine is several companies that join to control production and sales, while a cartel is independent firms that secretly agree on prices.
  • corporate trust
    6.9 0.00/m
    a corporate trust is a formal group of companies using shared management to limit competition, while a cartel is independent firms colluding on price.

Related Terms

  • antonym: competition - the activity of competing against others
  • synonym: monopoly - exclusive control over the supply of a product or service
  • synonym: trust - a large business entity formed to monopolize a market
