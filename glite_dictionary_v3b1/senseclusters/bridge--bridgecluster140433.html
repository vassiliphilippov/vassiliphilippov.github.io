bridge

noun
figurative connection or means of transition
Pronunciation: / brɪdʒ / (AmE), / brɪdʒ / (BrE)
something that serves as a means of connection or transition, resembling a bridge in form or function, especially figuratively

Examples

Bridge between cultures
Education can serve as a bridge out of poverty.
Her role as translator provided a bridge across cultural differences.
The new technology acts as a bridge between traditional methods and modern innovation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gap - an unbridgeable difference or space between things, the opposite of a bridge in this sense
  • synonym: link - a means of connecting or joining things
