microstructure

noun
internal organization
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the small-scale internal structure or arrangement of parts within a material or system, often visible only under a microscope

Examples

The microstructure of metal
Scientists examined the microstructure of the metal to understand its strength.
The microstructure of financial markets can influence how quickly prices change.
Understanding the microstructure of an organization helps improve its efficiency.
Scientists examined the microstructure of the metal to understand why it was so strong.
The microstructure of rocks can reveal important information about their formation history.
Changes in heat treatment can alter the microstructure and properties of an alloy.
The microstructure of the steel determines its strength and flexibility.
Researchers used an electron microscope to study the microstructure of the mineral sample.
Changes in temperature can alter the microstructure of certain materials, affecting their properties.
