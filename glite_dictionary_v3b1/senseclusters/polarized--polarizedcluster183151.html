polarized

adjective
light in one plane
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊləˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpəʊləraɪzd / (BrE)
having vibrations or waves, especially of light, restricted to a single direction or plane

Examples

Polarized light
The scientist used a filter to produce polarized light for the experiment.
Many modern sunglasses block polarized light to reduce glare from surfaces like water or roads.
A laser emits highly polarized light, which is useful in many optical applications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unpolarized - having vibrations in multiple directions or planes
