gum arabic

noun
natural gum from acacia trees used as a thickener
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm ˈærəbɪk / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈærəbɪk / (BrE)
a natural gum obtained from acacia trees, used as a thickener in foods, candies, and pharmaceuticals

Examples

Gum arabic thickener
Many confectioners add gum arabic to sweets to give them a smooth texture.
Pharmaceutical companies often use gum arabic as a stabilizing agent in tablets.
Traditional ink recipes frequently list gum arabic as a binding ingredient.
