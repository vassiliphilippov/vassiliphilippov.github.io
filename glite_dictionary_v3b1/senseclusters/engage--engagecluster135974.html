to occupy or absorb all of someone's attention or time; to involve someone deeply

Examples

To engage his mind
His work engages all his attention during the busy season.
She engaged herself entirely in studying for the exams.
The film engaged me so completely that I forgot about the time.

Synonyms

  • engross
    5.8 1.80/m
    engross absorbs all attention and keeps someone focused on one thing, while engage can involve or interest someone more widely.
  • absorb
    3.1 10/m
    absorb means to take someone's full attention quietly and deeply, while engage often suggests active involvement or choice to join.
  • immerse
    4.8 6.14/m
    immerse makes someone fully involved as if underwater in an activity, while engage can be less total and more social.
  • captivate
    4.2 1.59/m
    captivate charms and holds a person's interest with attraction, while engage often means involve someone more generally or practically.
  • enthrall
    6.8 1.95/m
    enthrall fascinates someone strongly, often like a spell or show, while engage can simply involve or interest a person.
  • capture attention
    3.2 0.08/m
    capture attention grabs focus quickly and can be brief, while engage suggests deeper, lasting involvement of attention or time.
  • occupy
    3.5 6.27/m
    occupy keeps someone's mind or time busy, often in a simple way, while engage suggests active interest or deeper involvement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distract - to divert someone's attention away
