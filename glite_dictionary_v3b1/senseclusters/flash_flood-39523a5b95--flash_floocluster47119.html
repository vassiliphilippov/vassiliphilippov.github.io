flash flood

noun
sudden severe local flood
4.4
Pronunciation: / flæʃ flʌd / (AmE), / flæʃ flʌd / (BrE)
A sudden and severe local flood caused by heavy rain or other rapid water release, usually of short duration.

Examples

A flash flood warning
Authorities issued a warning for possible flash floods in the mountainous regions.
He narrowly escaped being trapped in his car during the flash flood.
The town was devastated by a flash flood after hours of torrential rain.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: flood - an overflowing of a large amount of water beyond its normal confines
