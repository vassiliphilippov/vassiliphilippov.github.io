conjugate

adjective
having alternating double and single bonds (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.dʒə.ɡeɪt /; / ˈkɑn.dʒə.ɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒʊ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
(in chemistry) having a structure with alternating single and double bonds, or formed by the union of two or more substances

Examples

Conjugate bonds
Benzene is a classic example of a conjugate hydrocarbon, with alternating single and double bonds forming a ring.
Chemists study conjugate dienes because their structure affects reactivity and stability.
The dye molecule relies on a conjugate system to absorb visible light and produce color.
Bile acids can exist in both free and conjugate forms in the body.
Certain drugs are administered as conjugate compounds to improve their absorption.
Hemoglobin is a conjugate protein composed of heme and globin.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unconjugated - (of a compound) not having alternating double and single bonds
