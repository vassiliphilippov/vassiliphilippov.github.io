teeter-totter

verb
to ride on a seesaw or plank
4.6 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈtiː.tər.tɑː.tər/ /; / /ˈtiː.tər ˈtɑː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈtiː.tə.tɒ.tə/ /; / /ˈtiː.tə ˈtɒ.tə/ (BrE)
(of people, especially children) to ride on a seesaw or similar plank balanced in the middle so that one end goes up as the other goes down.

Examples

To teeter-totter together
The children teeter-tottered for hours at the playground.
They were teeter-tottering and laughing until sunset.
We used to teeter-totter on the old wooden plank behind the house.
