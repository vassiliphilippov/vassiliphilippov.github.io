blackbird

noun
North/Central American black songbird (Icteridae family)
2.9 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈblæk.bɜːrd/ (AmE), /ˈblæk.bɜːd/ (BrE)
A North or Central American songbird of the family Icteridae, the male of which is black or mostly black.

Examples

American blackbird
A flock of blackbirds landed in the field, their dark feathers shining in the sunlight.
Several species of blackbirds can be found in woodlands and marshes across North America.
The male blackbird is easily recognized by its glossy black plumage and bright yellow eyes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grackle - a songbird of the family Icteridae with iridescent black plumage
  • synonym: red-winged blackbird - a North American bird of the Icteridae family noted for the male's red and yellow shoulder patches
