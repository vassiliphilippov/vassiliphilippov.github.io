send

verb
cause to be admitted to an institution
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɛnd / (AmE), / sɛnd / (BrE)
to cause a person to go or be taken to a particular place, especially for work, duty, or because of an order

Examples

To send a worker
He was sent to a reform school after getting into trouble with the law.
Her family had no choice but to send her to a special care facility.
The judge decided to send him to prison for five years.
He was sent to manage the new branch overseas.
The army decided to send reinforcements to the front line.
The company will send her to their London office next year.

  • synonym: dispatch - to send someone or something to a specific place, often quickly or officially
