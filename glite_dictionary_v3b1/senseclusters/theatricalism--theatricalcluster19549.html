theatricalism

noun
emphasis on performance
8.9
Pronunciation: / θiˈætrɪkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / θɪˈætrɪkəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a style in theater that highlights artificiality and performance rather than aiming for realism

Examples

Theatricalism in stage design
The director's bold use of theatricalism challenged the audience's expectations of traditional drama.
Modern productions often incorporate theatricalism to draw attention to the act of performing itself.
Her set designs reflected a love for theatricalism rather than strict realism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expressionism - an artistic style that emphasizes the expression of emotions over realistic depiction
  • antonym: realism - a style in art or theater that aims to represent subjects truthfully
