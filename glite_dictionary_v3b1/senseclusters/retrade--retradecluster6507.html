retrade

verb
to trade again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtreɪd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtreɪd / (BrE)
to trade something again, especially in financial markets, often by renegotiating or reversing a previous deal

Examples

To retrade shares
After the market correction, several investors decided to retrade their positions for better terms.
The company attempted to retrade the contract after new information came to light.
In volatile markets, it is not uncommon for parties to retrade deals before settlement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: renegotiate - to discuss and change the terms of an agreement
  • synonym: trade - to buy and sell goods or assets
