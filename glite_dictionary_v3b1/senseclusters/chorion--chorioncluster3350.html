chorion

noun
outermost embryonic membrane
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːriɒn / (BrE)
the outermost membrane that surrounds the embryo in reptiles, birds, and mammals

Examples

The chorion membrane
In birds, the chorion helps in protecting the embryo and facilitating gas exchange.
Scientists examined the structure of the chorion under a microscope.
The chorion develops villi that later form part of the placenta.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amnion - the innermost membrane that encloses the embryo of a mammal, bird, or reptile
  • synonym: placenta - an organ that connects the developing fetus to the uterine wall
