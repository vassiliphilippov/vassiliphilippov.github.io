unfulfillment

noun
failure to meet obligation
6.8
Pronunciation: /ʌn.fʊl.ˈfɪl.mənt/ /; / /ən.fəl.ˈfɪl.mənt/ (AmE), /ən.fʊl.ˈfɪl.mənt/ /; / /ən.fəl.ˈfɪl.mənt/ (BrE)
the failure to carry out or meet an obligation, condition, or requirement, especially in a contract or agreement

Examples

Unfulfillment of a contract
The company faced penalties due to the unfulfillment of its contractual duties.
His unfulfillment of the agreement led to a legal dispute between the two parties.
The project was delayed because of the unfulfillment of several important conditions.
