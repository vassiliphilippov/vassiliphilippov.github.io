electret

noun
charged material device
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɛt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɛt / (BrE)
a material or device that has a permanent electric charge, often used in microphones and sensors

Examples

Electret microphone
The engineer installed a new electret in the microphone to improve its sensitivity.
Many modern sensors rely on an electret to detect changes in their environment.
A damaged electret can cause audio equipment to malfunction or produce static.
The electret in the microphone allows it to pick up sound with high sensitivity.
Scientists developed a new type of electret for use in medical sensors.
Unlike regular materials, an electret can hold its electric charge for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capacitor - a device that stores electrical energy
  • synonym: dielectric - an insulating material that can be polarized by an electric field
  • degree lower: magnet - an object that produces a magnetic field
