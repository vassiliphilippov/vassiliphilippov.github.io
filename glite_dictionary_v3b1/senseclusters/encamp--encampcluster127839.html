encamp

verb
to set up or live in a camp
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkæmp / (AmE), / ɪnˈkamp / (BrE)
to set up or settle in a camp, especially in tents or temporary shelters for a period of time

Examples

To encamp outside tents
Dozens of protesters encamped outside the parliament building, demanding government action.
The explorers encamped at the base of the mountain before beginning their ascent.
The soldiers encamped by the river for the night.
