blast wave

noun
high-pressure wave from an explosion
4.6
Pronunciation: / blæst weɪv / (AmE), / blɑːst weɪv / (BrE)
a region of very high pressure moving rapidly through a gas, especially following an explosion

Examples

The blast wave damage
Scientists studied the damage caused by the blast wave after the chemical explosion.
The bomb detonated, sending a powerful blast wave through the city streets.
Windows shattered instantly as the blast wave reached the building.
