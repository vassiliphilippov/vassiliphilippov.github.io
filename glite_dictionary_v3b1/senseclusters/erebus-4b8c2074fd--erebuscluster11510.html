Erebus

proper noun
Greek god of darkness and shadow
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrəˌbəs / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪbəs / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) the god of darkness and shadow, a primordial deity who dwelt in the underworld, son of Chaos and brother of Nox.

Examples

Erebus god of darkness
According to legend, Erebus fathered Aether, the spirit of the bright upper air.
In ancient myths, Erebus was said to dominate the gloom of the underworld.
The poet described the descent into Erebus as a journey through endless night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aether - Primordial god of the upper air, Erebus's son
  • synonym: Chaos - Primordial void in Greek mythology, Erebus's parent
  • synonym: Nox - Roman goddess of night, Erebus's sister
