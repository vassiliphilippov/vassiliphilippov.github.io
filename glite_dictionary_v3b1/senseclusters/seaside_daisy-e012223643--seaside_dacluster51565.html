seaside daisy

noun
perennial coastal plant with pink or lavender flowers
6.5 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsiˌsaɪd ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈsiːsaɪd ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial plant with fleshy leaves and hairy stems, each bearing a single pink or lavender daisy-like flower, native to coastal areas from Oregon to southern California

Examples

A seaside daisy flower
A single seaside daisy bloomed among the rocks, its lavender petals bright in the sun.
Gardeners often choose the seaside daisy for its resilience to salty air and sandy soil.
The seaside daisy thrives on the windswept cliffs along California's coast.
