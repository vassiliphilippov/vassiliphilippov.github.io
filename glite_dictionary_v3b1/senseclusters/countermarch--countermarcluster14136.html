countermarch

verb
turn and march back by the same route
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌmɑːrtʃ /; / ˈkaʊntərˌmɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌmɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to march back or reverse direction, especially as a group or troops

Examples

To countermarch the troops
After reaching the end of the street, the soldiers were ordered to countermarch and retrace their steps.
At the sound of the commander’s whistle, the troops began to countermarch to their original position.
The parade unit practiced how to countermarch in perfect unison for the ceremony.
The commander ordered the troops to countermarch in response to the unexpected obstacle ahead.
To confuse the enemy, the soldiers were instructed to countermarch halfway down the street.
When signaled, the regiment would countermarch swiftly to reposition at the rear.
