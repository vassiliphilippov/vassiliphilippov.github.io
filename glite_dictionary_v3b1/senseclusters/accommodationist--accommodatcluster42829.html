accommodationist

noun
supporter of religious accommodation
9.6
Pronunciation: /ə.kə.mə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /ə.kə.mə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ /; / /ə.kɒ.mə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who supports allowing government recognition or support of religious practices rather than strict separation of church and state

Examples

A political accommodationist
The senator, an outspoken accommodationist, advocated for religious holidays to be recognized by public schools.
Debates between the accommodationist and separationist viewpoints have shaped national policy for decades.
As an accommodationist, she believed that government could support faith-based organizations without violating the constitution.
