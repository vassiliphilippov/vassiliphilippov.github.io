geophyte

noun
plant with underground storage organs
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːoʊˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəʊfaɪt / (BrE)
A perennial plant that survives adverse seasons as underground bulbs, tubers, or corms.

Examples

A geophyte plant
Many desert geophytes bloom rapidly after rain, using nutrients stored in their bulbs.
The garden's collection of Mediterranean geophytes includes rare lilies and irises.
Tulips are classic examples of geophytes, storing energy underground to survive winter.
