terminer

noun
court with authority
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜːrmɪnər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪnə / (BrE)
a legal term for a judge or court with the authority to decide cases, especially criminal ones

Examples

A terminer judge
The judge sat as a terminer to rule on the complex criminal case.
In historical records, the court of oyer and terminer handled serious offenses.
The panel acted as a terminer for the duration of the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tribunal - a court of justice or a body established to settle disputes
