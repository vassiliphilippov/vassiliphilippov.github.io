break the Internet

verb
cause massive online reaction or disruption
Pronunciation: / breɪk ði ˈɪntɚˌnɛt / (AmE), / breɪk ði ˈɪntəˌnɛt / (BrE)
to cause a huge surge of online interest or activity, drawing widespread attention and disrupting normal internet traffic

Examples

To break the Internet quickly
The celebrity's outrageous outfit managed to break the Internet within hours of the event.
The surprise announcement from the company almost broke the Internet yesterday.
When the band reunited after two decades, fans joked that it would break the Internet.
