uncountably

adverb
in countless manner
8.3
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈkaʊn.tə.bliː/ /; / /ə n.ˈkaʊn.tə.bliː/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈkaʊn.tə.bliː/ /; / /ə n.ˈkaʊn.tə.bliː/ (BrE)
in a way that cannot be counted or is too numerous to count (including in mathematics)

Examples

Uncountably many stars
The stars in the night sky appear uncountably numerous to the naked eye.
Between any two points on a line, there are uncountably many possible locations.
The grains of sand on the beach are uncountably scattered along the shore.
The set of real numbers is uncountably infinite, unlike the set of natural numbers.
Some mathematical proofs show that certain sets are uncountably large.
In advanced mathematics, we often discuss whether a set is uncountably or countably infinite.

Related Terms

  • antonym: countably - in a way that can be counted
  • synonym: innumerably - in a way that is too many to be counted
