Synonyms

  • entreat
    8.1 0.73/m
    entreat is a formal, calm request spoken politely in serious situations, while beg is more direct and often urgent.
  • plead
    3.9 13/m
    plead is an emotional, sometimes legal appeal to someone, while beg is simpler and often shows strong need.
  • implore
    5.3 2.17/m
    implore is a desperate, urgent plea that seeks immediate help or mercy, while beg can be less intense.
  • beseech
    8.2 1.62/m
    beseech is a strong, old-fashioned plea often found in stories or formal speech, while beg is more common everyday.
  • supplicate
    9.6 0.14/m
    supplicate is a humble prayer-like request to someone with power or in religious settings, while beg is more general.
  • pray
    1.6 8.35/m
    pray is asking in a religious or hopeful way to God or someone, whereas beg usually means urgent asking from people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demand - to ask authoritatively or forcefully
  • synonym: implore - to beg earnestly or desperately
  • synonym: plead - to make an emotional appeal
