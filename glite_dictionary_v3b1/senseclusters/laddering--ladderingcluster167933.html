laddering

noun
staggered investments
Pronunciation: / ˈlædərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlædərɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of investing in similar financial products with different maturity dates to reduce risk and ensure regular returns

Examples

Laddering bond investments
Many investors use laddering to manage their bond portfolios and reduce interest rate risk.
By practicing laddering, she ensured that some of her certificates of deposit matured every year.
Financial advisors often recommend laddering as a strategy for stable and predictable returns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentration - focusing investments in a single asset or type
  • synonym: diversification - spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk
