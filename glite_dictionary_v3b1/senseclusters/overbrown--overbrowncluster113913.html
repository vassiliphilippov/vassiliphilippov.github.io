overbrown

verb
to cook too brown
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbraʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbraʊn / (BrE)
to cook or bake something until it becomes too brown, often resulting in an undesirable color or taste

Examples

To overbrown bread
If you overbrown the bread, the crust will taste bitter.
She accidentally overbrowned the chicken while trying a new recipe.
Watch the oven closely so you do not overbrown the top of the casserole.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: brown - to cook food until it turns brown
  • degree higher: burn - to cook something so much that it becomes blackened or inedible
