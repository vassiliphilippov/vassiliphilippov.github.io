cherry-pick

verb
select only the best or most desirable options
4.0 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛri pɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri pɪk / (BrE)
To selectively choose only the best or most desirable people or things from a group, often for one's own advantage.

Examples

To cherry-pick the best
During the interview process, she cherry-picked candidates with the most impressive resumes.
Investors often cherry-pick stocks that they believe will outperform the market.
The manager tried to cherry-pick the most talented employees for the new project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pick - to choose or select from a group
  • synonym: select - to choose from a number of options
