competency

noun
the quality of being adequately qualified
4.1 17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêm.p…ô.t…ôn.si / (AmE), / Ààk…ím.p…™.t…ôn.si / (BrE)
The ability to do something successfully or efficiently; the quality of being adequately or well qualified physically and intellectually.

Examples

Technical competency
His technical competency impressed everyone at the job interview.
Leadership competency is essential for managers in today's organizations.
The course is designed to improve students' competency in written communication.

Synonyms

  • competence
    4.1 14/m
    competence is the general ability to meet job demands and perform tasks well, while competency focuses on specific qualifications and success.
  • proficiency
    4.7 14/m
    proficiency denotes a high, practiced level in a subject, while competency refers to being adequately or well qualified to do something.
  • skillfulness
    4.1 0.03/m
    skillfulness shows visible skill and careful technique, while competency is broader and covers overall ability to perform successfully.
  • skill
    1.0 221/m
    skill is a learned ability to do a task well after practice, while competency includes knowledge and judgment for success.
  • ability
    1.0 195/m
    ability is the raw power or talent to do something, while competency includes training, qualification, and consistent successful performance.
  • capability
    3.6 35/m
    capability is the potential or capacity to do or develop a skill, while competency means already being able to perform reliably.

  • antonym: incompetence - lack of ability or qualification to do something
