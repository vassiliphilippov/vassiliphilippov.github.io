contrapposto

noun
sculptural pose
9.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀêntr…ôÀàpo ästo ä / (AmE), / Àåk…íntr…ôÀàp…íst…ô ä / (BrE)
a pose in sculpture or art where the body stands with most weight on one leg, causing the shoulders and hips to tilt in opposite directions

Examples

A contrapposto statue
The artist used contrapposto to give the statue a more natural and relaxed appearance.
Many Renaissance sculptures feature contrapposto to create a sense of movement and balance.
You can see the influence of contrapposto in the way the figure's hips and shoulders are angled.
