fermentation

noun
chemical breakdown by microorganisms
Pronunciation: / ˌfɜr.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜː.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A chemical process in which an agent, usually a microorganism, causes an organic substance to break down into simpler substances, especially the anaerobic conversion of sugars into alcohol.

Examples

Alcohol fermentation
During fermentation, sugars are converted into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
Sauerkraut is made through the natural fermentation of cabbage and salt.
The production of wine depends on the fermentation of grape juice by yeast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposition
  • antonym: respiration - the process in living organisms of producing energy, typically with oxygen
