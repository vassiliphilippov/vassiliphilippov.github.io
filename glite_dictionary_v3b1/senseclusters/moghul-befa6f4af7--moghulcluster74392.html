Moghul

noun
member of Muslim dynasty ruling India
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊɡʊl / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡʊl / (BrE)
A member of the Muslim dynasty that ruled most of northern India from the early 16th to the mid-19th century.

Examples

Moghul emperor
Akbar was one of the most famous Moghul emperors in Indian history.
Many policies of the Moghul rulers shaped Indian society for centuries.
The Moghul emperors left a remarkable architectural legacy, including the Taj Mahal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mughal - member of the Muslim dynasty that ruled India; alternative spelling
