charmonium

noun
charm quark particle
Pronunciation: / tʃɑːrˈmoʊniəm / (AmE), / tʃɑːˈməʊniəm / (BrE)
a type of subatomic particle consisting of a charm quark and its corresponding antiquark

Examples

Charmonium particle
Scientists detected charmonium during high-energy collisions in the laboratory.
The discovery of charmonium provided important evidence for the existence of the charm quark.
Researchers analyzed the decay patterns of charmonium to better understand strong nuclear forces.
