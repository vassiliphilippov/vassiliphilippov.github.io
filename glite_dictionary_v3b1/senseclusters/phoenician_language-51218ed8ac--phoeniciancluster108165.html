Phoenician language

noun
extinct Semitic language of the Phoenicians
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɪʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / fəˈniːʃən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the extinct Semitic language spoken by the ancient Phoenicians, used in the eastern Mediterranean region from about the 12th century BCE to the 2nd century CE

Examples

The Phoenician language script
Few people today can read inscriptions in the Phoenician language.
Scholars have studied the Phoenician language to understand ancient Mediterranean trade.
The Phoenician language was written using one of the earliest alphabets.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Aramaic - ancient Semitic language closely related to Phoenician
  • degree lower: Hebrew language - ancient and modern language of the Hebrews
  • synonym: Semitic - relating to a family of languages including Hebrew, Arabic, Aramaic, and Phoenician
