high-level radioactive waste

noun
dangerous byproduct of used nuclear fuel
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ ˌlɛvəl ˌreɪdiˈoʊæktɪv weɪst / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ ˌlɛvəl ˌreɪdiˈəʊaktɪv weɪst / (BrE)
radioactive waste that remains after nuclear fuel has been used in a reactor, highly radioactive and requiring careful disposal

Examples

Deep storage of high-level radioactive waste
A new facility was constructed to hold high-level radioactive waste deep underground for thousands of years.
Scientists are researching safe methods for disposing of high-level radioactive waste.
The storage of high-level radioactive waste remains one of the greatest challenges for the nuclear industry.
