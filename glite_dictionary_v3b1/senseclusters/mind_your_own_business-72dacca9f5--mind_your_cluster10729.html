mind your own business

verb
do not interfere in others' affairs
Pronunciation: / maɪnd jɔr oʊn ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / maɪnd jɔːr əʊn ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
used to tell someone not to interfere in matters that do not concern them

Examples

To mind your own business carefully
If you just mind your own business, things will be much easier for everyone.
She told him to mind your own business and walked away.
Sometimes it's better to mind your own business than offer unwanted advice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butt out - to stop interfering in something that does not concern you
  • antonym: meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
