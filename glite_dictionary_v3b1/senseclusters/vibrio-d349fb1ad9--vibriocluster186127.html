Vibrio

noun
genus of curved, rod-shaped bacteria
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪbrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪbrɪəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of gram-negative, curved, rod-shaped, motile bacteria, some species of which can cause diseases in humans and animals.

Examples

Vibrio bacteria species
Cholera is caused by a species of the Vibrio genus.
Researchers studied how Vibrio bacteria survive in saltwater environments.
The Vibrio found in seafood can sometimes lead to food poisoning.
