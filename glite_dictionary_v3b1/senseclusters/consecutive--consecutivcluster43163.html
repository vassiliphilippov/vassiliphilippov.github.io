consecutive

adjective
following in uninterrupted succession
1.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
following one after another in an unbroken or continuous sequence

Examples

Three consecutive days
She won three consecutive championships in a row.
The company posted profits for five consecutive years.
They worked for ten consecutive hours without a break.
It rained for three consecutive days during our holiday.
She ran five consecutive marathons without a single day of rest.
The team won seven consecutive games, breaking their previous record.
He worked for three consecutive weeks without a day off.
The festival has been held on consecutive weekends throughout the summer.
The team won five consecutive games this season.

  • antonym: simultaneous - occurring at the same time
