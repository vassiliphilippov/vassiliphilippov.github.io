spindly

adjective
long and thin; often weak-looking
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪndli / (AmE), / ˈspɪndli / (BrE)
long, thin, and often weak or fragile in appearance or structure.

Examples

A spindly tower
A spindly tower rose above the rooftops, barely holding its shape.
He had a spindly frame and looked younger than his age.
The tree's spindly branches bent easily in the wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
  • antonym: sturdy - strong and solidly built
