clammy locust

noun
tree with pink flowers and sticky branches
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈklæmi ˈloʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈklæmi ˈləʊkəst / (BrE)
a small tree of the southeastern United States with rough bark, racemes of pink flowers, and sticky branches and seeds

Examples

The clammy locust tree
In late spring, the clammy locust produces clusters of delicate pink flowers.
The clammy locust is often found in the woodlands of the southeastern United States.
The sticky branches of the clammy locust make it easy to distinguish from other trees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: locust - a tree of the genus Robinia, especially the black locust
