chronicity

noun
long-lasting state
8.1
Pronunciation: /krə.ˈnɪ.sɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /krə.ˈnɪ.sɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being persistent or long-lasting, especially in relation to a disease or condition

Examples

The chronicity of pain
The chronicity of her back pain required ongoing management by specialists.
Doctors were concerned about the chronicity of the infection and its impact on recovery.
Researchers studied the chronicity of symptoms in patients with long-term illnesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acuteness - the quality of being severe but short in duration
  • synonym: duration - the length of time something lasts
  • synonym: persistence - the act of continuing for a long time
