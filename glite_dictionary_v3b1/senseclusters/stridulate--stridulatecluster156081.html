stridulate

verb
emit a shrill sound by body movement (esp. insects)
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪdʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstrɪd.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of certain insects and animals) to make a shrill or creaking sound by rubbing together special body parts.

Examples

To stridulate loudly
On warm summer nights, crickets stridulate to attract potential mates.
Scientists recorded how beetles stridulate when threatened by predators.
Some species of grasshoppers stridulate by scraping their hind legs against their wings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirp - make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound
