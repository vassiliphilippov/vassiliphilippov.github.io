breaking and entering

noun
illegal entry with intent to commit a crime
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkɪŋ ənd ˈɛnɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪŋ ənd ˈɛntərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of illegally entering a building or property, typically by force, with the intention to commit a crime, especially theft.

Examples

Breaking and entering a house
A conviction for breaking and entering can lead to serious penalties, including imprisonment.
She testified that she witnessed a case of breaking and entering in her apartment building.
The suspect was charged with breaking and entering after being found inside the locked store.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burglary - illegal entry into a building with intent to commit a crime, especially theft
  • degree lower: trespassing - entering someone's property without permission
