garishness

noun
excessive brightness or showiness
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛrɪʃ.nəs /; / ˈɡɛrɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɛː.rɪʃ.nɪs /; / ˈɡɑːrɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively bright, flashy, or showy, especially in color, decoration, or taste

Examples

Some people love decorating with garishness, while others prefer a more minimalist style.
The garishness of the neon signs made the street hard to look at at night.
Critics condemned the garishness of the new building's facade.
She disliked the garishness of the decorations at the party.
The garishness of the neon signs overwhelmed the small street.
