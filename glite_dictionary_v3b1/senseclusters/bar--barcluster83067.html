bar

verb
officially exclude or expel
6.9
Pronunciation: / bɑr / (AmE), / bɑː / (BrE)
to officially prevent or forbid someone from entering, joining, or taking part in something

Examples

To bar entry
He was barred from attending the conference due to previous misconduct.
Several journalists were barred from entering the press briefing.
The club decided to bar anyone who broke the rules.
He was barred from accessing the confidential files.
Many shops bar entry to customers without a mask.
The player was barred from the tournament after violating the rules.
The club barred anyone under 18 from entering after 9 p.m.
He was barred from participating in the competition due to a rule violation.
Security guards are barring people from entering the building during the emergency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit someone to do something or enter
  • synonym: banish - to send someone away from a place as a punishment
  • synonym: exclude - to prevent someone from entering or joining
