echo sounding

noun
measuring water depth using sound echoes
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊ ˈsaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ ˈsaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of determining the depth of water or the location of underwater objects by measuring the time it takes for an echo to return from a sound pulse sent underwater

Examples

Echo sounding equipment
Before dropping anchor, the captain checked the depth using echo sounding equipment.
The crew used echo sounding to map the seafloor's topography during the voyage.
With advances in echo sounding, scientists can now study deep ocean trenches more accurately.

Related Terms
