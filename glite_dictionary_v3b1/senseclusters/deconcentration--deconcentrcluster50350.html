deconcentration

noun
loss of focus
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkɑːnsənˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
a state in which someone is unable to focus or maintain concentration on a task or subject

Examples

Mental deconcentration
After several hours of work, he noticed a feeling of deconcentration and decided to take a break.
Students may experience deconcentration if the classroom is too noisy or distracting.
Meditation can help reduce deconcentration and improve mental focus over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentration - the ability to focus one's attention on a task
  • synonym: distraction - a state in which attention is diverted from something
