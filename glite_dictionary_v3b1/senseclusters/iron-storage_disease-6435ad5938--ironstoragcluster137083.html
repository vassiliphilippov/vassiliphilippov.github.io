iron-storage disease

noun
disease with excess iron in tissues
6.4 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.ərn ˈstɔːr.ɪdʒ dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ˈaɪrn ˈstɔːr.ɪdʒ dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.ən ˈstɔː.rɪdʒ dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ˈaɪ.ən ˈstɔː.rədʒ dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which excess iron accumulates in body tissues, leading to symptoms such as bronzed skin, enlarged liver, diabetes mellitus, and abnormalities of the pancreas and joints.

Examples

Iron-storage disease symptoms
Early detection of iron-storage disease is important to prevent irreversible organ damage.
Patients with iron-storage disease often develop signs such as bronzed skin and diabetes.
The doctor diagnosed him with iron-storage disease after tests revealed abnormal iron levels in his liver.
