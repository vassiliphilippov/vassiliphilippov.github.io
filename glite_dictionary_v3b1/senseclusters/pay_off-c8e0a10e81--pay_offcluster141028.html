pay off

verb
to bribe someone for favors
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪ ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to give someone money or a benefit in order to influence their actions, usually dishonestly; to bribe.

Examples

To pay off a judge
Rumors spread that the judge had been paid off to rule in their favor.
The company allegedly paid off several inspectors to ignore safety violations.
The mayor was accused of trying to pay off city officials to approve the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bribe - to give someone money or favor to influence them, especially illegally
  • degree lower: reward - to give someone a benefit for doing something good or helpful
