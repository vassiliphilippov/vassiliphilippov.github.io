grievous

adjective
very serious or severe; causing great harm
2.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈgriː.vəs/ (AmE), /ˈgriː.vəs/ (BrE)
very serious, severe, or causing great pain, suffering, or sorrow

Examples

A grievous loss
A grievous loss struck the family, changing their lives forever.
The accident left him with grievous injuries requiring immediate surgery.
They expressed deep regret over the grievous error made by the company.
He was convicted of a grievous crime that left a lasting impact on the victims' families.
Such grievous acts of violence have no place in a civilized society.
The judge described the assault as a grievous attack that appalled the entire community.
She faced a grievous decision that weighed heavily on her mind.
The board discussed several grievous matters at the emergency meeting.
This was a grievous error that affected the entire outcome of the project.
