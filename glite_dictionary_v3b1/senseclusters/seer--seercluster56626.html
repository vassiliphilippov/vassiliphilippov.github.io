seer

noun
person who predicts the future
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːɚ /; / ˈsɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪə / (BrE)
a person who claims to be able to see or predict future events; a prophet or fortune-teller

Examples

A famous seer
He consulted a seer before making the difficult decision about his business.
Legends tell of a seer who correctly predicted the kingdom’s fate.
The ancient village respected the seer for her insights into events yet to come.
In mythology, a seer often warns the hero about dangers that lie ahead.
She was believed to be a seer, capable of predicting events years before they occurred.
The ancient king relied on his trusted seer to guide important decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clairvoyant - a person who claims to have a supernatural ability to perceive events in the future
  • synonym: fortune-teller - a person who tells people what will happen to them in the future
  • synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God
