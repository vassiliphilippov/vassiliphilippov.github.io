freshet

noun
sudden flow of water from rain or melting snow
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɛʃət / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃɪt / (BrE)
a sudden overflow or rush of water caused by heavy rain or melting snow, often resulting in a temporary stream or flood

Examples

A freshet of water
After a heavy rain, a freshet rushed through the normally dry creek.
Sudden freshets can erode riverbanks and damage nearby roads.
The river swelled with the spring freshet after the mountain snow began to melt.
