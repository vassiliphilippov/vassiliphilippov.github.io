paradigmatic

adjective
serving as a typical example
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.əɡ.dɪˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpar.əɡ.dɪˈmat.ɪk / (BrE)
serving as a typical example or pattern; relating to a model or paradigm.

Examples

Paradigmatic example
Her research offers a paradigmatic analysis of social change.
His career trajectory is often cited as paradigmatic among young entrepreneurs.
The case became paradigmatic of successful business transformation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atypical - not representative of a type, group, or class
  • synonym: exemplary - serving as a desirable model; representing the best of its kind
