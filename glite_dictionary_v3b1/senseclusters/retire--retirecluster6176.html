retire

verb
move back; withdraw
5.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtaɪr/ /; / /riː.ˈtaɪr/ /; / /riː.ˈtaɪ.ər/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtaɪ.ə/ /; / / rə.ˈtaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
to move away or withdraw from a place, group, or situation, often to go somewhere private or quiet

Examples

To retire to a room
After the speech, the ambassador retired from the reception hall without making further comments.
At the end of the meeting, she quietly retired from the room, letting others continue the discussion.
The soldiers retired to a safer position as darkness fell.
After the speech, several guests retired to the balcony for some fresh air.
At the conclusion of dinner, the ladies retired to the drawing room.
The board members retired for a private discussion after the main meeting.

  • synonym: withdraw - to move back or away from a place or position
