illusory

adjective
not real; based on illusion
7.0 2.57/m
Pronunciation: / …™ÀàluÀês…ôri / (AmE), / …™ÀàljuÀês…ôri / (BrE)
Not real but seeming to be real; based on or producing an illusion.

Examples

An illusory hope
Many believed in the illusory benefits of the new fad diet, but they saw no results.
The glow on the horizon was merely an illusory sign of hope during the dark night.
The magician created an illusory effect that left the audience amazed.

Synonyms

  • illusional
    9.4 0.04/m
    illusional often describes something that produces an illusion, while illusory describes something that only seems real but is not.
  • illusive
    8.2 0.06/m
    illusive feels deceptive or misleading, as if showing a trick, while illusory describes something that seems real but is not.
  • illusionary
    7.7 0.24/m
    illusionary creates or looks like an illusion and often intends to fool the senses, while illusory means something only seems real.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imaginary - existing only in the mind, not real
  • antonym: real - actually existing or happening and not imagined or false
