hemianopsia

noun
blindness in half of the visual field
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåhiÀêmi.…ôÀàn…ëp.si.…ô / (AmE), / ÀåhiÀê.mi.…ôÀàn…íp.si.…ô / (BrE)
A condition of blindness in one half of the visual field of one or both eyes.

Examples

Hemianopsia visual field
Hemianopsia can result from damage to the optic pathways in the brain.
The patient was diagnosed with hemianopsia following his stroke.
Visual field tests confirmed the presence of hemianopsia in her left eye.

Synonyms

  • hemianopia
    9.9 0.02/m
    hemianopia describes blindness in one half of the visual field often from brain or optic pathway damage, while hemianopsia names the same condition.
  • hemianopic scotoma
    9.9 0.00/m
    hemianopic scotoma is a blind spot that affects part of one visual half and often follows pathway damage, while hemianopsia means loss across that half.
