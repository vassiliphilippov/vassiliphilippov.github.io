convolve

verb
twist or coil together
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɑlv / (AmE), / kənˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to twist or coil together; to wind into a spiral or curved shape

Examples

To convolve a rope
Her hair seemed to convolve into intricate curls with little effort.
The two strands of rope convolve around each other to make a sturdy cord.
Vines convolve elegantly around the old fence, creating a natural lattice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coil - to wind into loops or rings
  • antonym: unwind - to straighten from a coiled or twisted form
