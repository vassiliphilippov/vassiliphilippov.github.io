take up the running of

verb
assume control or management
5.8
Pronunciation: / teɪk ʌp ðə ˈrʌnɪŋ ʌv / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp ðə ˈrʌnɪŋ ɒv / (BrE)
to assume control or management of something, typically after another person leaves or steps down

Examples

To take up the running of a company
After the director retired, Jane decided to take up the running of the company.
He was asked to take up the running of the project after the previous manager resigned.
When the principal left, Mr. Lee stepped in to take up the running of the school.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hand over - to give control or responsibility to someone else
  • synonym: take charge of - to assume control or responsibility for something
