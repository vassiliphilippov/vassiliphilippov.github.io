pyramidal tract

noun
major motor nerve pathway in the central nervous system
9.1 9.5
Pronunciation: / pɪˈræmɪdəl trækt / (AmE), / pɪˈramɪdəl trækt / (BrE)
any of the major bundles of motor nerve fibers that run from the cerebral cortex through the brainstem to motor neurons of the spinal cord, responsible for voluntary movement

Examples

The pyramidal tract fibers
Damage to the pyramidal tract can lead to weakness or paralysis of voluntary muscles.
Neurologists evaluate the integrity of the pyramidal tract during clinical examinations.
The pyramidal tract is essential for controlling fine motor movements in the hands.
