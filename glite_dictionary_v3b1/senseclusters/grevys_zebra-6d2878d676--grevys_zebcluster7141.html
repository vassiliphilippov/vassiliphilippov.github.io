Grevy's zebra

noun
species of zebra with narrow stripes
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɛviz ˈziːbrə / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛviz ˈziːbrə / (BrE)
A large African zebra species (Equus grevyi) characterized by narrow, closely set stripes and a white belly, native to eastern Africa.

Examples

The Grevy's zebra species
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered Grevy's zebra in Kenya.
The Grevy's zebra is easily recognized by its narrow stripes and white belly.
Unlike other zebras, the Grevy's zebra has a larger head and rounder ears.
