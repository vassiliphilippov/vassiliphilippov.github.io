copyleft

adjective
free-use licensing
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑpiˌlɛft / (AmE), / ˈkɒpiˌlɛft / (BrE)
describing a work, license, or approach that allows users to freely use, modify, and share, provided the same rights are preserved in derivative works

Examples

Copyleft license
The software was released under a copyleft license, allowing anyone to modify and share it.
Many educational resources are now available as copyleft materials to promote open access.
By choosing a copyleft approach, the creators ensured their work would remain free for everyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: copyright - the exclusive legal right to reproduce, publish, and sell a work
  • synonym: open-source - describing software with source code that anyone can inspect, modify, and enhance
