underpinning

noun
basic support
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌpɪnɪŋ /; / ˌʌndərˈpɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌpɪnɪŋ /; / ˌʌndəˈpɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a basic support, foundation, or principle that something depends on or is built upon (especially for ideas, systems, or relationships)

Examples

Underpinning of an idea
Trust is the underpinning of any strong relationship.
The theory provides the underpinning for much of modern physics.
Education is often seen as the underpinning of a successful society.
The scientific underpinning of the theory has been questioned by several experts.
Education is the underpinning of a successful and progressive society.
The architect explained the underpinning required to support the new building extension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basis - the main support or starting point for something
  • synonym: foundation - the underlying base or support for something
  • antonym: superstructure - a structure built on top of a foundation
