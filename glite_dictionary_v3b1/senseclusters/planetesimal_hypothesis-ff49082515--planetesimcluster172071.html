planetesimal hypothesis

noun
theory of solar system formation by planetesimals
8.3 8.3
Pronunciation: /plæn.ə.ˈtes.ɪ.məl haɪ.ˈpɑː.θə.sɪs/ (AmE), /plæn.ə.ˈtes.ɪ.məl haɪ.ˈpɒθ.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
a scientific theory proposing that the solar system was formed by the gradual gravitational accumulation of small bodies called planetesimals

Examples

The planetesimal hypothesis theory
Many astronomers in the early 20th century supported the planetesimal hypothesis to explain the origin of the solar system.
Recent research has cast doubt on some aspects of the planetesimal hypothesis but it remains influential in planetary science.
The planetesimal hypothesis suggests that planets grew by accumulating smaller celestial bodies through gravitational attraction.
