selfing

adjective
by self-fertilization
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛlfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlfɪŋ / (BrE)
describing a plant, line, or population that reproduces by self-fertilization or has been produced by self-fertilization

Examples

Selfing line
The researchers selected selfing plants to ensure genetic consistency in the experiment.
Many crops have selfing varieties that do not require pollinators to reproduce.
A selfing population can quickly become genetically uniform over several generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross-pollinated - fertilized by pollen from another individual
  • synonym: self-fertilizing - capable of fertilizing itself without another individual
