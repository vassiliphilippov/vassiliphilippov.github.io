hydrolyze

verb
to break down by reaction with water

(of a substance) to undergo or cause to undergo a chemical reaction in which it is broken down by water, resulting in decomposition into two or more products.

Examples

To hydrolyze a substance
Cellulose does not easily hydrolyze in the human digestive tract.
Some enzymes can hydrolyze fats into glycerol and fatty acids.
The catalyst helps to hydrolyze the peptide bonds in proteins.
Enzymes can hydrolyse proteins into their amino acid components.
In acidic solutions, sucrose will hydrolyse to produce glucose and fructose.
The compound does not hydrolyse easily at room temperature.
