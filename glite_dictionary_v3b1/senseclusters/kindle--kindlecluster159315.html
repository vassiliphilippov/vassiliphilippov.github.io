kindle

verb
to catch fire; begin burning
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪn.dl̩ /; / ˈkɪndl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɪn.dl̩ /; / ˈkɪndl̩ / (BrE)
to start a fire or make something begin to burn

Examples

To kindle a fire
A single match is all it takes to kindle a campfire in dry weather.
The vast wheat field began to kindle under the scorching sun.
When exposed to a spark, the old newspapers quickly kindled.
A sudden idea kindled his imagination, inspiring a new painting.
She used a match to kindle the fireplace logs on a chilly evening.
The poem describes how hope can kindle in even the darkest times.

  • synonym: ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
