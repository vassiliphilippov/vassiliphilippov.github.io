crosstie

noun
beam supporting railroad rails

A heavy wooden or concrete beam laid across railroad tracks to support and anchor the rails.

Examples

A crosstie beam
Each crosstie must be precisely placed to keep the rails at the correct distance apart.
Modern railroads often use concrete crossties instead of wood for added durability.
The workers replaced several rotten crossties along the aging section of track.

Synonyms

  • railroad tie
    4.1 0.26/m
    railroad tie is the common American name for the heavy beam under rails, while crosstie is a variant form used in the same context.
  • sleeper
    6.3 0.17/m
    sleeper is the British term for the heavy beam under railway tracks, while crosstie is the common American name for it.
  • tie
    7.9 1.23/m
