predrill

noun
preliminary hole
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːdrɪl / (AmE), / ˈpriːdrɪl / (BrE)
a hole made in a material in advance to prepare for another operation, such as inserting a screw or dowel

Examples

A predrill hole
The carpenter made a predrill to ensure the screw would go in straight.
Always check the depth of each predrill before inserting fasteners.
A proper predrill can prevent the wood from splitting during assembly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pilot hole - a small hole drilled to guide a larger drill or screw
