bookful

noun
amount to fill a book
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbʊkfʊl / (BrE)
an amount that is enough to fill a book, especially of written material or content

Examples

A bookful of stories
She wrote a bookful of poems during her travels.
After years of research, he finally had a bookful of notes ready to publish.
The historian collected a bookful of letters from the archives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: volume - a book or a collection of written sheets bound together
