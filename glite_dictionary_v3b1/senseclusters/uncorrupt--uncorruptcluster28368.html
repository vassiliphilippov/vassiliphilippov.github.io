uncorrupt

adjective
free from corruption
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌʌnkəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
not altered, damaged, or affected by errors; intact and free from corruption, especially in data or files

Examples

Uncorrupt data files
The backup copy remained uncorrupt after the system crash, saving hours of work.
It is important to verify that your downloaded files are uncorrupt before installing any software.
The document was surprisingly uncorrupt despite being stored on an old hard drive.
