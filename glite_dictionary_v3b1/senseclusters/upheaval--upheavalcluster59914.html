upheaval

noun
a violent or sudden disturbance
7.4 5.5
Pronunciation: /əp.ˈhiː.vəl/ /; / /ʌp.ˈhiː.vəl/ (AmE), /ʌp.ˈhiː.vəl/ /; / /əp.ˈhiː.vəl/ (BrE)
a sudden, violent, or major change or disturbance, especially causing disorder or turmoil

Examples

Political upheaval
After the merger, the organization faced months of internal upheaval before achieving stability.
Economic upheaval can reshape industries and force companies to adapt quickly.
The revolution caused significant upheaval throughout the country, leaving many citizens in fear.
Natural disasters can cause significant upheaval in affected communities.
The collapse of the government created an economic upheaval that lasted for years.
There was widespread social upheaval after the policy reforms were announced.
After years of stability, the sudden upheaval shocked both citizens and officials.
Economic upheaval often leads to widespread social protests and unrest.
The country experienced significant upheaval following the controversial election results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - absence of disturbance or agitation
  • antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or absence of disturbance
  • antonym: stability - the quality or state of being stable or unchanging
  • synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
