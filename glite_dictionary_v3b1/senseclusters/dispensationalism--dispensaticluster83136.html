dispensationalism

noun
theological system of eras
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃənəˌlɪzəm /; / dɪˌspɛnˈseɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪzəm /; / dɪˌspɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a Christian theological system that divides history into distinct periods or 'dispensations' in which God interacts with humanity in different ways

Examples

Dispensationalism theology
The church's teachings are strongly influenced by dispensationalism and its view of biblical history.
Many Bible study groups discuss the different eras described in dispensationalism.
Some theologians debate the accuracy of dispensationalism compared to other interpretations.
Many evangelical churches in the 20th century adopted dispensationalism as their main theological framework.
The study group discussed how dispensationalism interprets biblical prophecy differently from other traditions.
Some critics argue that dispensationalism divides history in a way not found in early Christianity.
Many evangelical churches in the United States teach dispensationalism as part of their doctrine.
The conference featured a debate on the merits of dispensationalism versus other theological systems.
Some scholars criticize dispensationalism for its strict literal interpretation of biblical prophecy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amillennialism - the belief that there will be no literal thousand-year reign of Christ
  • antonym: covenant theology - a Christian theological system emphasizing God's covenants rather than historical periods
  • synonym: premillennialism - the belief that Christ will return before a thousand-year reign
