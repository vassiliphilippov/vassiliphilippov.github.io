in personam

adjective, adverb
legal action against person
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn pɜːr.ˈsoʊ.nəm/ /; / /ɪn pər.ˈsoʊ.nəm/ (AmE), /ɪn pə.ˈsəʊ.nəm/ /; / /ɪn pɜː.ˈsəʊ.nəm/ (BrE)
relating to a legal action or judgment directed at a specific person rather than property

Examples

In personam jurisdiction
The court has in personam jurisdiction over the defendant in this case.
An in personam order requires the individual to comply with the court's decision.
Unlike an action against property, an in personam judgment binds the specific person involved.
The court exercised in personam jurisdiction over the defendant, requiring him to appear in court.
A lawsuit filed in personam seeks to hold a specific individual responsible for damages.
Unlike actions against property, an in personam case targets a particular person.
