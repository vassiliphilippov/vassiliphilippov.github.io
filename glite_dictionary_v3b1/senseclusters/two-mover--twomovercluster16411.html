two-mover

noun
chess problem: mate in two moves
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː ˌmuːvər / (AmE), / ˈtuː ˌmuːvə / (BrE)
A chess problem in which White is required to checkmate Black in two moves, regardless of Black's defense.

Examples

A two-mover chess problem
I spent hours last night trying to work out the solution to this two-mover.
In his new book, the author includes a selection of elegant two-movers for readers to solve.
The final two-mover in the tournament was solved by only a handful of participants.
