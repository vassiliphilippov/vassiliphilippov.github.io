micromanage

verb
to control every detail
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to control every small detail of someone else's work or activities, often in an excessive or unnecessary way

Examples

To micromanage a team
The new manager tends to micromanage every aspect of the team's work.
Employees feel less trusted when their boss starts micromanaging their daily tasks.
If you micromanage your staff, it can lower their motivation and creativity.
Some managers micromanage their teams, making it hard for employees to work independently.
If you micromanage every task, your team may feel frustrated and untrusted.
She realized she needed to stop trying to micromanage every aspect of the project.
If you micromanage your team, they may feel frustrated and lose motivation.
She tends to micromanage every project, checking even the smallest details herself.
Employees appreciate when their boss does not micromanage their daily tasks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delegate - to give responsibility or tasks to others
  • synonym: oversee - to supervise or watch over a task or people
