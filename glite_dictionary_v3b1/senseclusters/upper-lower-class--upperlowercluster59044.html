upper-lower-class

adjective
in highest segment of the lower class
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpɚ ˌloʊɚ ˈklæs / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˌləʊə ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
belonging to the higher segment of the lower social or economic class

Examples

An upper-lower-class family
As an upper-lower-class student, she was the first in her family to attend college.
He grew up in an upper-lower-class neighborhood, where most parents worked in local factories.
Many families in the upper-lower-class struggle with financial insecurity but still have stable housing.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: lower-middle-class - belonging to the lower segment of the middle class
  • synonym: working class - the social group consisting of people who are employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
