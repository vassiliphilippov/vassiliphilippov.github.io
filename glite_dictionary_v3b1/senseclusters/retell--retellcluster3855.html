retell

verb
tell again or narrate anew
2.8 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàt…õl / (AmE), / riÀàt…õl /; / riÀêÀàt…õl / (BrE)
to tell a story, event, or information again, sometimes with changes or to a new audience

Examples

To retell a story
He retold the events so vividly that everyone felt they were there.
She loves to retell old family tales during holidays.
The teacher asked the students to retell the story in their own words.
He retold the incident to the police, making sure not to leave out any details.
She loves to retell stories from her childhood at family gatherings.
The teacher asked the students to retell the fairy tale in their own words.
In her play, she retells historical events with a modern twist.
The author decided to retell the legend as a fantasy novel for young readers.
The movie retells a classic folktale, but adds magical creatures to the plot.

Synonyms

  • recount
    4.1 9.30/m
    recount focuses on telling past events in clear detail from experience, while retell means saying the same story again, possibly with changes.
  • narrate
    4.0 5.62/m
    narrate tells events or stories often in a formal or dramatic way, while retell means saying a story again, possibly changed for listeners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fictionalize - to present a true story or event with invented elements, making it fiction
  • antonym: invent - to create something not previously existing (contrasted with telling what has already occurred)
  • synonym: recount - to tell in detail; narrate
  • synonym: repeat - to say or do something again
  • synonym: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
