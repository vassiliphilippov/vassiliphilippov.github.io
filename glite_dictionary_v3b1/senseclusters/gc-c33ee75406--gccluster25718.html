Gc

noun
unit: one billion cycles per second
8.9
Pronunciation: /dʒiː siː/ /; / /ˈdʒɪ.gə.saɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /dʒiː siː/ /; / /ˈdʒɪ.gə.saɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
a unit symbol representing 1,000,000,000 cycles (periods) per second, used in physics and electronics as gigacycle or gigahertz

Examples

Gc unit
Older documents sometimes refer to frequency as Gc instead of gigahertz.
Scientists measured the signal at exactly 1 Gc.
The processor operates at a speed of 3 Gc.
