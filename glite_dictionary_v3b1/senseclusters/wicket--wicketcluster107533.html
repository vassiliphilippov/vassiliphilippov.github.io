wicket

noun
set of stumps and bails in cricket
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪt / (BrE)
in cricket, a set of three wooden stumps topped with two crosspieces (bails), placed at each end of the pitch and used as a target for the bowler

Examples

Cricket wicket
Before starting the match, the umpire inspected the wicket closely.
Damage to the wicket during play can lead to a delay in the game.
The batsman was bowled out when the ball hit the wicket.
