twiddle

verb
toggle bits (computing)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪd.əl / (BrE)
(in computing) to change, set, or toggle individual bits or small data fields, especially when debugging, optimizing, or configuring hardware or low-level software.

Examples

To twiddle a bit
During the firmware update, she had to twiddle a configuration bit to enable debugging output.
To fix the race condition, the team twiddled a few status bits in the device driver.
He learned to twiddle the bits in the register to optimize power consumption on the chip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flip - to change the value of a bit or switch (e.g., from 0 to 1 or vice versa)
  • synonym: toggle - to switch between two states, often by setting or clearing a bit or flag
