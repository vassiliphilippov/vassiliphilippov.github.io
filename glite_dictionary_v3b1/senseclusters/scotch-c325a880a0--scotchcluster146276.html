Scotch

adjective
relating to Scotland or its culture; often used in product names
6.4 5.9
Pronunciation: /skɑːtʃ/ (AmE), /skɒtʃ/ (BrE)
of or relating to Scotland, its people, or its culture; now chiefly used in established phrases or as the name for certain products (such as whisky) and less commonly used to refer to people, often considered outdated or disliked by Scots themselves

Examples

Scotch broth
He ordered a bowl of Scotch broth to start his meal.
Many Scottish people dislike being referred to as Scotch.
The traditional pattern of Scotch plaid is recognized around the world.

  • synonym: Scottish - relating to Scotland or its people or culture; the current preferred adjective
