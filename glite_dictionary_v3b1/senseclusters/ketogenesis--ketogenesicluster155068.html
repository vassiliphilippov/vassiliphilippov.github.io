ketogenesis

noun
production of ketone bodies
8.1
Pronunciation: /kiː.toʊ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ /; / /kiː.tə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /kiː.təʊ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ /; / /kiː.tə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the metabolic process by which the body produces ketone bodies from fatty acids, usually during low carbohydrate intake

Examples

Ketogenesis process
During prolonged fasting, ketogenesis becomes a major source of energy for the brain.
A ketogenic diet is designed to stimulate ketogenesis by reducing carbohydrate intake.
Doctors monitor ketogenesis in patients with diabetes to prevent dangerous levels of ketone bodies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: glycolysis - the breakdown of glucose to produce energy
  • synonym: lipolysis - the breakdown of fats to release fatty acids
