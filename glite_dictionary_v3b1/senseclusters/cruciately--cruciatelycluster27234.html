cruciately

adverb
with extreme suffering
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːʃiətli / (AmE), / ˈkruːʃiətli / (BrE)
in a manner resembling being crucified; with extreme suffering or torment, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

Suffered cruciately
The prisoner suffered cruciately during his long captivity in the dungeon.
She described her ordeal cruciately, evoking deep sympathy from the listeners.
In the old tale, the hero endures his fate cruciately, never losing hope.
