illocutionary

adjective
relating to intended meaning
Pronunciation: / ˌɪloʊˈkjuːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌɪləˈkjuːʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to the intended meaning or function that a speaker conveys through a spoken statement

Examples

Illocutionary force
The illocutionary force of his statement was to warn, not just inform.
Understanding the illocutionary intent helps avoid miscommunication in conversations.
Her illocutionary act was an apology, even though she never said 'sorry' directly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
  • synonym: pragmatic - relating to language use in context and intended meaning
