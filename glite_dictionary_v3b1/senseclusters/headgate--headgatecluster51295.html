headgate

noun
main mine entrance
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhed.ɡeɪt/ (AmE), /ˈhed.ɡeɪt/ (BrE)
the main entrance, roadway, or airway at the working face of an underground mine, especially in longwall or room-and-pillar mining

Examples

Mine headgate
The miners entered the underground tunnels through the headgate at the start of their shift.
Safety checks are performed daily at the headgate to ensure proper ventilation in the mine.
After the collapse, rescue teams focused their efforts near the headgate to reach trapped workers.

  • antonym: tailgate - the secondary or opposite entry in a mine
