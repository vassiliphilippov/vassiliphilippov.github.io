pontil

noun
glassmaking scar
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːntɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɒntɪl / (BrE)
a rough mark or scar on the base of a hand-blown glass object where the rod was detached

Examples

Pontil mark on glass
The collector examined the pontil to confirm the vase was hand-blown.
A visible pontil mark can increase the value of antique glassware.
You can often spot a pontil on the bottom of traditional glass bottles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punty - the metal rod used to hold glass while shaping it
