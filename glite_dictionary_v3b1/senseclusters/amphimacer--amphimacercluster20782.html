amphimacer

adjective
metrical foot pattern
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæmfɪˌmeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈæmfɪˌmeɪsə / (BrE)
having or relating to a metrical foot with a stressed-unstressed-stressed syllable pattern in poetry

Examples

Amphimacer rhythm
The poet used an amphimacer rhythm to give the line a strong, emphatic sound.
In classical poetry, an amphimacer foot is less common than a dactyl or an iamb.
The teacher explained that an amphimacer consists of three syllables with the stress on the first and last.
