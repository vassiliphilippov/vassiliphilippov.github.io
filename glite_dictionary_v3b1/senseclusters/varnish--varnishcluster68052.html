varnish

verb
apply a coating of varnish
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
to cover or coat a surface with varnish to protect or improve its appearance

Examples

To varnish wood
After sanding the chair, he began varnishing the surface to bring out the wood's natural color.
She carefully varnished each wooden frame to give it a glossy finish.
The carpenter decided to varnish the table to protect it from spills and stains.
After sanding, she began varnishing the chair to protect it from wear.
The craftsman spent hours varnishing the wooden table to give it a shiny finish.
He carefully varnished the picture frame to enhance its appearance and durability.

  • synonym: paint - to cover a surface with a colored liquid
  • antonym: strip - to remove a coating or covering from a surface
