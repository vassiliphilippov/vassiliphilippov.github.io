Still's disease

noun
adult-onset systemic inflammatory disease
7.5
Pronunciation: /stɪlz dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /stɪlz də.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /stɪlz dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /stɪlz də.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
(in adults) Adult-onset Still's disease (AOSD): a systemic inflammatory disorder that begins in adulthood, characterized by high fevers, a salmon-pink skin rash, joint pain, and raised inflammatory markers.

Examples

Adult Still's disease
After weeks of high fevers and joint pain, doctors diagnosed her with Still's disease.
Blood tests showing very high ferritin levels supported the suspicion of Still's disease in the patient.
Treatment for Still's disease often includes anti-inflammatory drugs and sometimes steroids to control symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adult-onset Still's disease (AOSD) - another name for the adult form of Still's disease; used frequently in medical contexts.
  • synonym: rheumatoid arthritis - a chronic inflammatory disorder affecting joints; a related inflammatory rheumatic condition.
