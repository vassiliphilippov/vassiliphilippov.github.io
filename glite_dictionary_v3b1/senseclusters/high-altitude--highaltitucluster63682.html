high-altitude

adjective
at or from a high altitude
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈæltɪˌtjud / (AmE), / haɪ ˈæltɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
(of an activity or object) taking place, operating, or existing at a relatively high altitude, especially above sea level.

Examples

High-altitude hiking
She experienced headaches due to high-altitude hiking in the mountains.
Special high-altitude balloons are used for weather observations.
The scientists conducted high-altitude research to study atmospheric changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-altitude - occurring at or from a relatively low altitude
