(in genetics and epidemiology) a probabilistic predisposition to develop a disease or trait, resulting from the combined effects of genetic and environmental factors.

Examples

Genetic liability
Researchers estimate an individual's liability to develop the disease using family history and genetic markers.
The study found higher liability for autoimmune disorders among participants with certain gene variants.
Public-health models incorporate population liability to predict future disease prevalence and resource needs.
