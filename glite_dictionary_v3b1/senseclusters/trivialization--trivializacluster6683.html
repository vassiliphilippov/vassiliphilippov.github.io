trivialization

noun
making structure simple
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɪviələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrɪviəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
in mathematics, a mapping or process that shows a complex structure is equivalent to a simple or 'trivial' one, often by isomorphism

Examples

Mathematical trivialization
A local trivialization can help mathematicians analyze the properties of a fibre bundle.
The concept of trivialization is essential in understanding certain geometric structures.
By applying a trivialization, the problem became much easier to solve.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simplification - the process of making something simpler or easier to understand
