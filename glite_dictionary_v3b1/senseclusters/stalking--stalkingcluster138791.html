stalking

noun
hunting by stealthy pursuit or ambush
Pronunciation: / ˈstɔkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or method of following or approaching a person, animal, or prey stealthily, especially for hunting or observing

Examples

The stalking of deer
During the stalking of the deer, the hunters moved silently through the underbrush.
He learned stalking from his father, who was an experienced hunter.
The art of stalking requires patience and attention to detail in the wild.
During the safari, we watched the lion's stalking behavior through binoculars.
The photographer studied the fox's stalking before taking the perfect shot.
The tiger's stalking of its prey was silent and methodical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambush - an act of lying in wait to make a surprise attack
  • synonym: hunting - the activity of searching for and killing wild animals
  • synonym: pursuit - the act of chasing or trying to catch someone or something
