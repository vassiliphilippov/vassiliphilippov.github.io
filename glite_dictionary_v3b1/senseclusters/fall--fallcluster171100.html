fall

noun
a sudden decline or collapse
1.0
Pronunciation: / fɔl /; / fɑl / (AmE), / fɔːl / (BrE)
a sudden or significant decrease, decline, or collapse in amount, value, or importance

Examples

A sharp fall in sales
Profits suffered a sharp fall after the new regulations were introduced.
The fall of the Roman Empire marked the end of an era.
The sudden fall in his popularity surprised the political analysts.
A sharp fall in demand led to lower production in the factory.
After the announcement, there was a fall in the company's share price.
The sudden fall in temperature caught many people unprepared.
