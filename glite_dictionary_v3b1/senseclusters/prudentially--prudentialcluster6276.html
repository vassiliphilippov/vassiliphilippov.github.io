prudentially

adverb
with careful judgment
7.7 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / pruÀêÀàd…õn É…ôli / (AmE), / pruÀêÀàd…õn É…ôli / (BrE)
in a careful and wise way, especially to avoid risk or problems (often in financial or practical matters)

Examples

To act prudentially
The company invested prudentially to avoid unnecessary risks during uncertain times.
He always approaches new opportunities prudentially, considering all possible outcomes first.
It is important to act prudentially when making major life decisions.
The bank managed its investments prudentially to avoid unnecessary risks.
Regulators expect financial institutions to act prudentially during times of economic uncertainty.
She always approaches major decisions prudentially, considering all possible outcomes.

Synonyms

  • prudently
    5.0 0.79/m

  • circumspectly
    8.3 0.09/m
    circumspectly means acting cautiously with attention to all consequences, while prudentially stresses formal, risk-avoiding wisdom, often in finance.
