reference work

noun
book used for authoritative information
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛfɚəns ˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈrɛfərəns ˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a book or publication, such as a dictionary or encyclopedia, that is used for consultation on specific information rather than continuous reading

Examples

A reference work book
Encyclopedias and dictionaries are examples of reference works commonly used by researchers.
She consulted a reference work to verify the scientific facts for her report.
The library has an extensive collection of reference works available to students.
