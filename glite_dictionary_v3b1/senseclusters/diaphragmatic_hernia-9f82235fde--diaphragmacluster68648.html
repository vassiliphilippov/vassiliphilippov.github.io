diaphragmatic hernia

noun
hernia through the diaphragm
5.4 7.1
Pronunciation: / daɪˌæfɹəgˈmætɪk ˈhɝniə / (AmE), / daɪˌæfɹəgˈmatɪk ˈhɜːnɪə / (BrE)
a hernia caused by the protrusion of part of the stomach or other abdominal organs through the diaphragm

Examples

A diaphragmatic hernia case
A diaphragmatic hernia can cause breathing difficulties due to pressure on the lungs.
Surgery may be required to repair a diaphragmatic hernia in newborns.
The patient was diagnosed with a diaphragmatic hernia after experiencing severe abdominal pain.
