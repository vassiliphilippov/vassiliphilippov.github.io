wildland

noun
natural undeveloped area
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪlˌlænd /; / ˈwaɪldˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪlˌlænd /; / ˈwaɪldˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of land that remains in its natural, undeveloped state, often protected or conserved

Examples

A wildland area
The government set aside the forest as a protected wildland for future generations.
Many rare species thrive in the remote wildland far from cities.
Hiking through the wildland gave us a sense of peace and adventure.
Firefighters worked hard to protect the wildland from the approaching wildfire.
Many rare species can only be found in untouched wildland far from cities.
Hiking through the wildland gave us a sense of peace and adventure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • antonym: urban area - a region characterized by high human population and development
  • synonym: wilderness - a wild, natural area where few people live
