(of a container or space) to flow or run over the rim, edge, or limit, typically because it is too full.

Examples

To overflow the cup
Crowds would often overflow from the stadium into the surrounding streets after a big match.
If the river keeps rising, it will overflow its banks and flood the nearby fields.

Synonyms

  • spill over
    4.2 1/m
    spill over is when liquid or people go past an edge or limit, while overflow means a container runs over.
  • run over
    3.2 0.3/m
    run over is used for liquid that spills beyond a rim, while overflow describes a container full and pouring out.
  • brim over
    3.6 0.01/m
    brim over shows liquid or strong feeling pouring over a rim, while overflow describes the container actually going past its edge.
  • overtop
    4.8 0.06/m
    overtop means rise or flow over the top of a barrier, while overflow describes a container simply running over its edge.
  • overspill
    4.9 0.02/m
    overspill means liquid or extra things that spill out beyond a limit, while overflow refers to the container itself spilling over.
  • overrun
    5.3 0.3/m
    overrun often means being filled or passed by people, pests, or time, while overflow is specifically liquid running over an edge.
