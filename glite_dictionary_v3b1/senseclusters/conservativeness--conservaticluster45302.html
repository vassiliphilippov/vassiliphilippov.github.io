conservativeness

noun
preference for tradition
6.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɝːvətɪvnəs / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvətɪvnəs / (BrE)
a tendency to favor traditional values, cautious change, and limited government, especially in politics or social matters

Examples

Political conservativeness
His conservativeness was evident in his reluctance to support rapid changes in education policy.
The party's conservativeness appeals to voters who value stability and tradition.
Some criticized her conservativeness as being resistant to necessary social reforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressivism - support for social reform and new ideas
  • synonym: traditionalism - support for maintaining established customs and values
