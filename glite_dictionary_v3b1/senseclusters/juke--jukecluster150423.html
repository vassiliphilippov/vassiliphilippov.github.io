juke

verb
to dodge quickly
8.1
Pronunciation: / dʒuːk / (AmE), / dʒuːk / (BrE)
to make a quick, deceptive movement to avoid someone, especially in sports

Examples

To juke a defender
The running back managed to juke past two defenders and reach the end zone.
She tried to juke her opponent with a sudden step to the left.
He juked around the corner to avoid bumping into the crowd.
The running back managed to juke past two defenders and score a touchdown.
She tried to juke her opponent with a sudden change of direction.
He juked left, then sprinted right to escape the defender.
The wide receiver juked past the defender to gain extra yards.
She juked left, then sprinted right to avoid being tagged.
During the game, he juked his opponent with a sudden change of direction.
