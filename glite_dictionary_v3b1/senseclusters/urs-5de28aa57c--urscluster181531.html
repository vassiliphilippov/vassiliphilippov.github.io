URS

noun
ureter examination procedure
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː ɑːr ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌjuː ɑː ˈrɛs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a thin tube with a camera is inserted into the ureter to examine or treat urinary tract problems

Examples

URS procedure
The urologist scheduled a URS to remove the patient's kidney stone.
After the URS, the doctor explained the findings to the patient.
A URS is often performed to diagnose blockages in the ureter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cystoscopy - a procedure to examine the bladder with a scope
  • synonym: ureteroscopy - a procedure to look inside the ureter using a scope
