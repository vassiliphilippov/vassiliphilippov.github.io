muckraker tactic

noun
strategy to expose corruption
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌkˌreɪkər ˈtæk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈmʌkˌreɪkə ˈtæk.tɪk / (BrE)
a strategy or method employed to expose corruption, wrongdoing, or misconduct, especially in politics or business

Examples

Muckraker tactic exposes corruption
Some critics argue that muckraker tactics can sometimes cross ethical boundaries in pursuit of the truth.
Thanks to her persistent muckraker tactics, several corrupt officials were brought to justice.
The journalist's muckraker tactics led to a major government scandal being revealed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: investigative journalism - the practice of in-depth, systematic investigation, often to expose wrongdoing
