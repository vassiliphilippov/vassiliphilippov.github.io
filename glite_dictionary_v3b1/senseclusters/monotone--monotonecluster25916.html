monotone

noun
unchanging tone of voice
5.3 0.91/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ëÀê.n…ôÀåto än / (AmE), / Ààm…ín.…ô.t…ô än / (BrE)
a way of speaking or singing in which the tone or pitch does not change, making it sound dull or unvaried

Examples

A flat monotone
He read the announcement in a flat monotone, showing no emotion.
She tried to mask her excitement, but her voice slipped into a monotone.
The lecturer's monotone made it difficult for the students to stay awake.
During the reading, the chant followed a monotone that did not change pitch.
In Gregorian chant, a monotone is sometimes used to recite psalms.
The priest recited the prayer in a monotone, as required by the ancient ritual.

Synonyms

  • droning
    6.5 0.65/m
    droning is a continuous low, boring sound used to describe speech or noise, while monotone is an unvaried voice.
  • level tone
    5.1 0.02/m
    level tone is a calm, even way of speaking that shows little feeling, while monotone is flat and dull without pitch change.
  • drone
    5.2 1.39/m
    drone is the long, low sound from instruments or machines, while monotone is a way of speaking with no pitch change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drone - a continuous low monotonous sound or speech
  • antonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice in speaking, opposite of monotone
