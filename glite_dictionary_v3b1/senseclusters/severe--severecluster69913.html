severe

adjective
very strong or intense
Pronunciation: / sɪˈvɪr /; / səˈvɪr / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
very strong, intense, or extremely bad or serious in degree or effect

Examples

Severe pain
She suffered a severe injury during the championship match, which forced her to withdraw.
The building's roof collapsed under the severe weight of the snow after days of heavy storms.
The storm inflicted a severe blow to the coastal village, destroying several homes.
Due to severe weather conditions, the school was closed for the day.
She was suffering from severe back pain after the accident.
The doctor warned that severe symptoms could require hospitalization.
A severe storm caused widespread damage across the coastal region last night.
Doctors warned that any severe allergic reaction must be treated as a medical emergency.
The patient was hospitalized with a severe case of pneumonia requiring intensive care.
