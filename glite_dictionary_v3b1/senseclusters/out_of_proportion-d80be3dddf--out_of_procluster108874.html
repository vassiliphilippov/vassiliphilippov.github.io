out of proportion

adjective
excessively large or small compared to what is reasonable
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv pɹəˈpɔɹʃən / (AmE), / aʊt əv prəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
larger, smaller, or more extreme than what is appropriate or reasonable for a particular situation; exaggerated or disproportionate.

Examples

An out of proportion reaction
His anger over the small mistake was completely out of proportion to what actually happened.
Let's not take her comments out of proportion—she probably didn't mean to offend anyone.
The media coverage blew the incident out of proportion, making it seem much worse than it was.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disproportionate - not in correct relation or balance
