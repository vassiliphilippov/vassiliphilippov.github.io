syntagm

noun
group of words forming a syntactic unit
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnˌtæɡəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪnˌtæɡəm / (BrE)
A group of words that together form a unit within a sentence, as part of a larger syntactic structure, especially in linguistic analysis.

Examples

A syntagm of words
A syntagm can vary in length, from a single word to several words functioning together.
In structural linguistics, every sentence can be analyzed into various syntagms.
The noun and its accompanying adjective form a syntagm within the sentence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phrase - a group of words that function as a single unit in a sentence
