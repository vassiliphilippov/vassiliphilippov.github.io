infringe

verb
break or violate a law or rule
4.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfrɪndʒ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfrɪndʒ/ (BrE)
to break a law, rule, or limit, or to go beyond what is allowed, especially by violating someone's rights or property

Examples

To infringe the law
Any use of the company's trademark without permission would infringe on their intellectual property rights.
The new law protects workers' rights against employers who might infringe them.
You must not infringe upon other people's privacy, even if you disagree with their choices.
Some neighbors felt the new fence infringed beyond the agreed property line.
The company was found to infringe on environmental regulations by expanding its operations beyond permitted boundaries.
When you infringe upon another country’s territory, it can lead to serious conflict.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comply - to act in accordance with a rule, command, or wish
  • synonym: encroach - to gradually go beyond usual or accepted limits
  • antonym: respect - to not go beyond limits or boundaries
  • synonym: violate - to break or fail to comply with (a rule or law)
