texture

noun
musical combination of parts or voices
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstʃə / (BrE)
The way in which different musical parts or voices are combined together to produce a particular overall effect.

Examples

Musical texture
Her new song features a sparse texture with only a piano and vocals.
The texture of the music changes dramatically when the choir joins in.
The orchestra achieved a rich texture by layering strings and woodwinds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harmony - the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes to produce chords and chord progressions
