virtually

adverb
almost; in effect but not in fact
4.3 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝː.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃu.ə.li /; / ˈvɜː.tjʊ.ə.li / (BrE)
almost or nearly; not completely, but very close to being something

Examples

Virtually impossible
After the renovations, the building was virtually unrecognizable from its original form.
She has virtually finished her thesis, with just a few edits left to make.
The team was virtually unstoppable during the championship, winning every game except one.
Virtually everyone in the office attended the meeting this morning.
The storm destroyed virtually every building in the village.
The two computers are virtually identical in terms of performance and features.
