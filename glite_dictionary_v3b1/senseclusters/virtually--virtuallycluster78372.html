virtually

adverb
almost; in effect but not in fact
3.6 51/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ùÀê.t Éu.…ô.li / (AmE), / Ààv…úÀê.t Éu.…ô.li /; / Ààv…úÀê.tj ä.…ô.li / (BrE)
almost or nearly; not completely, but very close to being something

Examples

Virtually impossible
After the renovations, the building was virtually unrecognizable from its original form.
She has virtually finished her thesis, with just a few edits left to make.
The team was virtually unstoppable during the championship, winning every game except one.
Virtually everyone in the office attended the meeting this morning.
The storm destroyed virtually every building in the village.
The two computers are virtually identical in terms of performance and features.

Synonyms

  • almost
    1.0 466/m
    almost means very nearly complete or true, while virtually stresses something is almost so in reality or effect.
  • nearly
    1.0 193/m
    nearly means being very close to something but not quite there, while virtually often reads as almost the same result.
  • practically
    2.5 26/m
    practically is used when something is almost true in practice, while virtually is similar but can sound slightly more formal.
  • just about
    2.7 39/m
    just about is a casual way to say nearly or almost, while virtually is more formal and used in wider contexts.
  • approximately
    3.2 55/m
    approximately is used for numbers and measures to show an estimate, while virtually is broader and not only for amounts.
  • roughly
    1.6 37/m
    roughly gives a coarse or quick estimate, while virtually implies a closer, nearly exact state or condition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actually - in fact; really
  • synonym: almost - very nearly, but not quite
  • synonym: nearly - not quite; almost
