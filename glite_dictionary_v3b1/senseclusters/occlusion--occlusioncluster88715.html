occlusion

noun
hidden parts of a scene
Pronunciation: / əˈkluːʒən / (AmE), / əˈkluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
(in computer graphics and computer vision) the condition in which one object or part of a scene is hidden from the camera or viewer because another object lies between them; also used for techniques and algorithms that detect or handle such hiding (for example, occlusion culling or occlusion detection).

Examples

3D occlusion
The game engine performs occlusion culling to avoid rendering objects blocked by closer geometry.
In computer vision, heavy occlusion of faces makes reliable recognition far more difficult for the algorithm.
Their simulation models light passing through gaps to realistically handle occlusion between moving objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or prevents passage or view
  • antonym: visibility - the state of being able to be seen; the opposite of occlusion in visual contexts
