batta

noun
extra pay allowance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbætə / (AmE), / ˈbætə / (BrE)
an allowance or extra pay given to soldiers or officials, especially in British India, for special duties or conditions

Examples

Monthly batta allowance
During his service in the frontier region, he received a monthly batta for the difficult conditions.
The colonial administration introduced batta to compensate officers stationed far from major cities.
Many officials relied on batta as a significant part of their income while posted in remote areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allowance - a sum of money given regularly for a specific purpose
  • antonym: salary - regular payment for employment
