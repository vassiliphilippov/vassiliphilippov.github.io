scrounger

noun
person who gets things by relying on others
Pronunciation: / ˈskraʊndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈskraʊndʒə / (BrE)
a person who habitually obtains things, especially money or food, by relying on others or by getting them for free

Examples

A scrounger at lunch
Everyone thinks Greg is a scrounger because he never pays for his own lunch.
She didn't want to seem like a scrounger, so she insisted on paying her share.
The flatmates were annoyed at having a scrounger who never contributed to household expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moocher - someone who habitually asks for or takes free things from others
  • synonym: sponger - a person who lives at others' expense
