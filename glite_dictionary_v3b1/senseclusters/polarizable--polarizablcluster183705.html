polarizable

adjective
causing division
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊlərˈaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˌpəʊləˈraɪzəbəl / (BrE)
likely to cause or be divided into two sharply opposing groups, opinions, or positions

Examples

Polarizable issue
The new policy is highly polarizable and has split the community into two opposing sides.
Social media debates on the topic quickly became polarizable, with little room for compromise.
Some leaders avoid polarizable issues to maintain unity within their organizations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divisive - causing disagreement or hostility between people
  • antonym: unifying - bringing people together or making them agree
