to leeward

noun
on/toward the sheltered side from the wind
Pronunciation: / tə ˈliwɚd / (AmE), / tə ˈluː.əd / (BrE)
on or toward the side sheltered from the wind; the direction away from the wind.

Examples

To leeward side
The sailors preferred to sleep to leeward so they would be less exposed to the cold breeze.
The ship drifted to leeward as the storm intensified, sheltering behind the island.
We anchored to leeward of the reef to avoid the strong winds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: windward - the side or direction from which the wind is blowing
