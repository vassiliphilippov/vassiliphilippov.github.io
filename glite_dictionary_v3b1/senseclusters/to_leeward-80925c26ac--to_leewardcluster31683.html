to leeward

noun
on/toward the sheltered side from the wind
8.0 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / t…ô Ààliw…öd / (AmE), / t…ô ÀàluÀê.…ôd / (BrE)
on or toward the side sheltered from the wind; the direction away from the wind.

Examples

To leeward side
The sailors preferred to sleep to leeward so they would be less exposed to the cold breeze.
The ship drifted to leeward as the storm intensified, sheltering behind the island.
We anchored to leeward of the reef to avoid the strong winds.

Synonyms

  • leeward
    6.4 0.01/m
    leeward describes the sheltered side or direction in one word, while to leeward is a phrase meaning on or toward that side.
  • downwind
    2.8 0.63/m
    downwind names the direction the wind carries things in everyday talk, while to leeward is a more nautical phrase for that position.
