isogonal line

noun
line of equal magnetic declination
9.9
Pronunciation: / aɪˈsɑɡənəl laɪn / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒɡənəl laɪn / (BrE)
An imaginary line on the Earth's surface connecting points where the magnetic declination is the same.

Examples

An isogonal line on Earth
By following an isogonal line, pilots can ensure accurate directional readings throughout their journey.
Navigators often use an isogonal line to correct for compass variations while plotting courses.
The isogonal line on the map shows where the magnetic declination is the same across different regions.
