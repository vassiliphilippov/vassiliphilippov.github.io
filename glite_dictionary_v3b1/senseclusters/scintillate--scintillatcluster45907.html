scintillate

verb
emit flashes of light; sparkle brightly
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪn.təˌleɪt /; / ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪt /; / ˈsɪntɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪt /; / ˈsɪntɪleɪt / (BrE)
to emit flashes of light; to sparkle or shine brightly

Examples

Stars scintillate at night
Her diamond earrings scintillated as she turned her head.
Stars scintillate brilliantly against the clear night sky.
The chandelier crystals scintillate whenever the sun shines through the window.
As the chandelier turned, its glass beads began to scintillate, creating a dazzling effect throughout the room.
The stars seemed to scintillate in the clear night sky.
Tiny crystals scintillated on the surface of the snow, catching the morning sun.
Her diamond necklace scintillated under the restaurant's bright lights.
The fireworks scintillated against the night sky, creating a dazzling display.
The scientist observed how the mineral sample would scintillate when exposed to ultraviolet rays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flash - to shine suddenly and briefly
  • synonym: gleam - to shine with a soft or bright light
  • degree lower: glimmer - emit a dim or unsteady light
  • synonym: sparkle - to shine with bright flashes of light
  • synonym: twinkle - to shine with a gleam that varies repeatedly between bright and faint
