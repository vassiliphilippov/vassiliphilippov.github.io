wigeon

noun
a freshwater duck of Eurasia and northern Africa
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪdʒ.ən/ /; / /ˈwɪdʒ.ɪn/ (AmE), /ˈwɪdʒ.ən/ /; / /ˈwɪdʒ.ɪn/ (BrE)
A freshwater duck found in Eurasia and northern Africa, related to mallards and teals, known for its rounded head, distinctive plumage and whistling call.

Examples

A wigeon duck
Birdwatchers often travel to wetlands to observe migratory wigeon during the winter.
The male wigeon is known for its striking chestnut head and creamy crown.

During the winter, large numbers of widgeon can be seen feeding in marshes and wetlands.
The birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare American widgeon mingling with local ducks.
