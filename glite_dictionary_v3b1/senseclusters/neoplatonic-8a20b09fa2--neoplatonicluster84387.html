Neoplatonic

noun
follower of Neoplatonism
9.9
Pronunciation: /niː.oʊ.plə.ˈtɑː.nɪk/ /; / /niː.əʊ.plə.ˈtɑː.nɪk/ (AmE), /niː.əʊ.plə.ˈtɒ.nɪk/ (BrE)
a person who follows or supports Neoplatonism, a philosophical tradition based on the teachings of Plato and later thinkers

Examples

A Neoplatonic thinker
As a Neoplatonic, he believed in the existence of a single source from which all reality emanates.
The Neoplatonic argued that true knowledge comes from spiritual insight rather than sensory experience.
Many medieval philosophers were influenced by Neoplatonic ideas about the nature of the soul.
