out front

adverb
leading; ahead in a competition
Pronunciation: / aʊt frʌnt / (AmE), / aʊt frʌnt / (BrE)
in a position of being ahead or leading, especially in a competition or race.

Examples

Out front in the race
After a strong start, she was out front for most of the marathon.
By halftime, our team was already out front by ten points.
The company is out front in developing innovative green technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ahead - further forward; in advance of someone else in a competition
  • antonym: behind - in a position that is less advanced or less successful
