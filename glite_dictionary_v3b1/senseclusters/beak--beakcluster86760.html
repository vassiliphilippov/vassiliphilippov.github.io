beak

noun
beaklike tip of a plant part
Pronunciation: / biːk / (AmE), / biːk / (BrE)
a beaklike, tapering tip found on certain plant structures, such as fruits, seeds, or leaves

Examples

A seed's beak
Some grasses produce grains with a slender beak at one end.
The fruit's beak helps with dispersal by attaching to animal fur.
The seed pod narrows into a distinctive beak at the tip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rostrum - a beaklike projection, especially in animals or plants
