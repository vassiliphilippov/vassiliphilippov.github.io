fox shark

noun
large shark with a long tail, found in warm seas
6.8 6.9
Pronunciation: / fɑks ʃɑrk / (AmE), / fɒks ʃɑːk / (BrE)
A large shark found in warm seas, characterized by a long, whiplike tail used for herding and catching small fish.

Examples

The fox shark species
Because of its unique tail, the fox shark is easily distinguished from other sharks.
Divers spotted a fox shark in the warm coastal waters near the reef.
The fox shark uses its whip-like tail to stun schools of small fish before feeding.
