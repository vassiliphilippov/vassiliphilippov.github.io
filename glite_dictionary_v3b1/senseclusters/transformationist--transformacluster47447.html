transformationist

noun
advocate of radical change
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænsfərˈmeɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌtrænsfəˈmeɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or advocates for fundamental or radical change, especially in society or institutions

Examples

A transformationist leader
The transformationist presented a bold plan to completely restructure the education system.
As a committed transformationist, she supported policies that aimed to radically improve social equality.
Critics argued that the transformationist approach was too extreme for gradual progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - a person who resists change and prefers traditional views
  • synonym: reformer - a person who works to improve or change something, especially in society
