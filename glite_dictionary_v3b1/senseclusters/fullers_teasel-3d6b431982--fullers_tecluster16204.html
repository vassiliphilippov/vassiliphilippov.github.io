fuller's teasel

noun
a plant formerly used in textile processing
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlɚz ˈtiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.əz ˈtiː.zəl / (BrE)
a tall prickly plant (Dipsacus sativus) with cone-shaped flower heads, once used for raising the nap on cloth; widespread in Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and naturalized in the United States.

Examples

Fuller's teasel plant
Gardeners sometimes plant fuller's teasel for its distinctive prickly appearance and wildlife value.
In medieval Europe, fuller's teasel played a vital role in the cloth finishing process.
The dried flower heads of the fuller's teasel were once commonly used in woolen mills.
