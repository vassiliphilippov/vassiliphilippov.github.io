multinucleated

adjective
with multiple nuclei
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈnuːkliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈnjuːkliˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having more than one nucleus within a single cell

Examples

Multinucleated muscle cell
Skeletal muscle fibers are multinucleated because they form from the fusion of several cells.
Some types of fungi have multinucleated cells that help them grow quickly.
The scientist observed a multinucleated cell under the microscope during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: binucleated - having two nuclei in a single cell
  • antonym: mononucleated - having a single nucleus in a cell
