beneficiary

noun
person/entity benefiting from an action (linguistics)
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛnəˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃəri / (BrE)
In linguistics, the person or entity who receives the benefit from an action, as described by the verb in a sentence.

Examples

Sentence beneficiary
Identifying the beneficiary helps clarify for whom an action is performed.
In the sentence 'She baked him a cake,' 'him' is the beneficiary of the action.
Linguists often analyze who acts as the beneficiary in different sentence structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agent - the doer of an action in linguistics
