pleat

noun
fold in fabric for style or shape
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /pliːt/ (AmE), /pliːt/ (BrE)
A fold formed by doubling fabric back upon itself and securing it by pressing or stitching, used to give shape and style to clothing and textiles.

Examples

A pleat in trousers
A tailor can add a pleat to trousers for extra comfort and style.
She ironed a perfect pleat into the skirt before wearing it to the party.
The dress featured narrow pleats that added movement and volume to the fabric.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fold - a bend or crease in something, especially fabric or paper
