cold rubber

noun
tough synthetic rubber made at low temperatures
Pronunciation: / koʊld ˈrʌbər / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈrʌbə / (BrE)
a synthetic rubber produced at low temperatures, resulting in increased toughness, commonly used in car tires

Examples

Cold rubber tires
Because cold rubber is tougher, it's a popular choice for heavy-duty applications.
Modern car tires are often made with cold rubber for improved durability.
The factory invested in new equipment to produce cold rubber at lower temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural rubber - rubber derived from latex of rubber trees
  • synonym: synthetic rubber - artificial rubber produced from polymers
