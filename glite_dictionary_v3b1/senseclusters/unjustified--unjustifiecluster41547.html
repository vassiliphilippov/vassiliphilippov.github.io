unjustified

adjective
lacking justification or authorization
4.8 1.61/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒÊŒnËˆdÊ’ÊŒstÉªfaÉªd / (AmE), / ËŒÊŒnËˆdÊ’ÊŒstÉªfaÉªd / (BrE)
Not having, or not shown to have, a good reason or legitimate excuse; not justified or authorized.

Examples

An unjustified criticism
Her criticism of the staff was entirely unjustified and hurtful.
Many employees felt that the new restrictions were unjustified and unnecessarily harsh.
The judge ruled that the search was unjustified because there was no evidence of wrongdoing.

Synonyms

  • unwarranted
    7.0 2.62/m
    unwarranted describes actions done without good reason or permission and sounds formal, while unjustified means lacking proper reason.
  • unjustifiable
    6.8 0.54/m
    unjustifiable cannot be made reasonable or defended, while unjustified means it is not shown to have a good reason.
  • unfounded
    5.6 3.01/m
    unfounded has no facts or evidence to support it, while unjustified means it lacks a proper or legal reason.
  • unmerited
    7.6 0.17/m
    unmerited is not deserved by someone, often for praise or blame, while unjustified means lacking a good reason.
  • uncalled for
    5.1 0.72/m
    uncalled for sounds informal and criticizes behaviour as unnecessary or rude, while unjustified focuses on lacking a valid reason.
  • unwarrantable
    9.2 0.03/m
    unwarrantable means impossible to defend or explain, usually in formal criticism, while unjustified simply lacks shown justification.
  • unexcused
    4.6 0.00/m
    unexcused describes an absence or act with no allowed excuse, while unjustified covers any action lacking a proper reason.
