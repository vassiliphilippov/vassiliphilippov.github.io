recursiveness

noun
self-referential quality
8.5
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɜrsɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to repeat or refer to itself, especially in a process or structure

Examples

Recursiveness in functions
Recursiveness allows a function to call itself in computer programming.
The recursiveness of the pattern made the design endlessly complex.
Linguists study the recursiveness of language structures in different cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: iteration - the repetition of a process or set of instructions
  • synonym: recursion - the process of repeating items in a self-similar way
