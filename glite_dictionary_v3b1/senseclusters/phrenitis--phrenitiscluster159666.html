phrenitis

noun
archaic: brain inflammation
9.9
Pronunciation: / frɛˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / frɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
An archaic term for inflammation of the brain, usually caused by infection and accompanied by symptoms such as headache, neck pain, and fever.

Examples

Inflammation of phrenitis
In historical accounts, patients suffering from phrenitis often displayed delirium and persistent fever.
Modern medicine has replaced the term phrenitis with more specific diagnoses like encephalitis.
The physician diagnosed the man with phrenitis, noting severe headache and confusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encephalitis - inflammation of the brain, especially as a modern medical term
