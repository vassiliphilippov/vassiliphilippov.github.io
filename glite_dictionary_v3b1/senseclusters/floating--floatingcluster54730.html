floating

adjective
not committed to a party or policy
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) not definitely committed to a particular party, group, or policy, especially in political contexts.

Examples

Floating voter
Many politicians target their messages to appeal to floating members of the public.
She remained floating on the issue until the end of the campaign.
The election result may depend on how many floating voters turn out on the day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: committed - firmly dedicated to a cause, party, or policy
  • synonym: undecided - not having made a decision or commitment
