turning point

noun
critical moment of significant change
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝnɪŋ ˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnɪŋ ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
an event or moment when a significant change happens, especially one that affects what happens next

Examples

The discovery of penicillin marked a turning point in medical history.
Winning the championship was the turning point in her athletic career.
Her decision to change careers became the turning point in her life.
Losing that crucial match was the turning point of their season.
The invention of the internet marked a turning point in how people communicate worldwide.
