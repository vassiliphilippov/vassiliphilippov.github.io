living will

noun
legal document expressing end-of-life care wishes
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪvɪŋ wɪl / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ wɪl / (BrE)
A legal document in which a person specifies their wishes regarding medical treatment in circumstances in which they are no longer able to express informed consent, often including instructions about life-sustaining measures.

Examples

A living will document
Many people create a living will to ensure their wishes are respected.
She decided to sign a living will after her father’s illness.
The hospital staff checked to see if the patient had a living will on file.
