noncoherent

adjective
lacking logical connection
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkoʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
lacking logical connection or not having a consistent phase, especially in waves or signals

Examples

Noncoherent signal
The scientist explained that a noncoherent signal cannot be used for precise measurements.
Because the waves were noncoherent, the interference pattern was not visible.
A noncoherent argument is difficult for listeners to follow and understand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coherent - logically connected or having a consistent phase
  • synonym: incoherent - not logical or not clearly expressed
