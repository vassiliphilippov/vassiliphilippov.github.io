chemical energy

noun
energy released by chemical reactions
6.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkem.ɪ.kəl ˈen.ər.dʒɪ/ /; / /ˈkem.ɪ.kəl ˈen.ər.dʒiː/ (AmE), /ˈkem.ɪ.kəl ˈen.ə.dʒɪ/ /; / /ˈkem.ɪ.kəl ˈen.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
the energy stored in the bonds of chemical compounds that can be released during a chemical reaction

Examples

Chemical energy stored
Batteries store chemical energy that is converted into electrical energy when used.
Food provides humans with chemical energy necessary for growth and activity.
The explosion released a large amount of chemical energy in the form of heat and light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: potential energy - energy possessed by a body due to its position or condition
