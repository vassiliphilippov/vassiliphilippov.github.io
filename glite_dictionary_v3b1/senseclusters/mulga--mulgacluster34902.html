mulga

noun
Australian acacia tree
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌlɡə / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɡə / (BrE)
a type of Australian acacia tree, especially Acacia aneura, or the dry scrubland where it grows

Examples

Australian mulga tree
The cattle grazed among the mulga in the outback.
Many birds and animals depend on the mulga for food and shelter.
We drove for hours through endless stretches of mulga under the hot Australian sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acacia - a genus of trees and shrubs found in warm climates
  • synonym: scrubland - land covered with low or stunted vegetation
