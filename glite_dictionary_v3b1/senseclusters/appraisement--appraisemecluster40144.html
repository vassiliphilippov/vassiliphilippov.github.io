appraisement

noun
official property valuation
Pronunciation: / əˈpreɪzmənt / (AmE), / əˈpreɪzmənt / (BrE)
the act of officially determining the value of something, especially property or goods

Examples

An appraisement of property
The bank requested an appraisement of the house before approving the loan.
An official appraisement is necessary to determine the value of the artwork for insurance.
The court relied on the appraisement to settle the dispute over the property’s worth.
The appraisement of the house was necessary before putting it on the market.
After the appraisement, the insurance company agreed to cover the full value of the painting.
A professional appraisement can help determine the true worth of family heirlooms.
The appraisement of the antique vase revealed it was worth much more than expected.
Before selling the house, an official appraisement was required by the bank.
The insurance company requested an appraisement of the damaged artwork.
