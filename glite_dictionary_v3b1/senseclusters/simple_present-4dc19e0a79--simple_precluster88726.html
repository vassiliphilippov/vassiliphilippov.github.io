simple present

noun
verb tense for habitual actions and facts
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmpəl ˈprɛzənt / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəl ˈprɛzənt / (BrE)
The verb tense used to describe habitual actions, general truths, and situations that are currently true.

Examples

The simple present tense
In English, we use the simple present to talk about routine activities.
Many beginners confuse the simple present with the present continuous tense.
The simple present is often used in newspaper headlines to make stories more immediate.
