ring-a-rosy

noun
children's circle game
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪŋ ə ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˌrɪŋ ə ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
A children's game in which players join hands, move around in a circle, and at a signal, all squat or fall down together.

Examples

Ring-a-rosy game
During recess, the teacher led the class in a game of ring-a-rosy.
Memories of ring-a-rosy always remind her of her early school days.
The children played ring-a-rosy in the park, laughing loudly as they fell to the ground.
