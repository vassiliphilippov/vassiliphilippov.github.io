backbencher

noun
ordinary member of parliament (not a leader)
6.3 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.ben.tʃər/ /; / /ˈbæk.bə.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.ben.tʃə/ /; / /ˈbæk.bə.tʃə/ (BrE)
a member of a parliament, especially the House of Commons, who does not hold a government office or a leadership position within their party

Examples

A backbencher in parliament
As a backbencher, she did not participate in Cabinet meetings but still played an active role in committees.
Several backbenchers rebelled against their party's stance on the proposed legislation.
The backbencher voiced concerns about the new education bill during the debate.
