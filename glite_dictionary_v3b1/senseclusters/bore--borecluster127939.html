bore

noun
high tidal wave in a river or estuary
9.8 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / b…îr / (AmE), / b…îÀê / (BrE)
a high, often dangerous, wave caused by the sudden tidal flow up a river or estuary, usually occurring where the incoming tide meets a river current in a narrow or shallow channel.

Examples

A tidal bore
Boats were warned to avoid the area due to the approaching tidal bore.
The famous Severn bore attracts surfers and spectators every year.
The river's bore can rise several meters quickly and is very powerful.

Synonyms

  • tidal bore
    8.9 0.03/m
    tidal bore is the exact name for this tidal wave in rivers and estuaries, while bore is the shorter common word.
  • eagre
    9.9 0.00/m
    eagre is an older name for a sudden tidal wave in rivers, while bore is the modern common word.
  • aegir
    9.9 0.00/m
    aegir is a specialist or poetic name for a tidal surge in narrow estuaries, while bore is everyday usage.
  • eager
    9.9 0.00/m
    eager appears as a variant spelling or misprint in some texts for this tidal wave, while bore is the correct term.
