Corypha utan

noun
large tropical palm; leaves used for thatching
Pronunciation: / kɔːˈriːfə uːˈtɑːn / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪfə juːˈtæn / (BrE)
a large palm native to Malaysia, the Philippines, and northern Australia, with broad leaves used for thatching and making containers

Examples

The Corypha utan palm
Researchers studied how the Corypha utan can withstand harsh tropical weather conditions.
The broad leaves of the Corypha utan are traditionally used for making thatched roofs in rural villages.
Villagers plait containers from the tough, fibrous leaves of Corypha utan.
