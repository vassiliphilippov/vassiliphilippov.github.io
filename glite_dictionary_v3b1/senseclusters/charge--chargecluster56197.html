charge

noun
quantity of explosive to be detonated
5.4
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of explosive material intended to be detonated at once, as in ammunition or demolition work.

Examples

An explosive charge
Each shell contains a charge carefully measured for maximum impact.
Inspectors checked the charge before allowing the demolition to proceed.
The engineers set a charge to demolish the old bridge.
