soundly

adverb
in a deep or thorough way
2.7 2.19/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsaÊŠndli / (AmE), / ËˆsaÊŠndli / (BrE)
in a thorough, complete, or decisive way (for example, sleeping deeply or being completely defeated or criticized)

Examples

To sleep soundly
After a long hike, she slept soundly all night.
He soundly defeated his opponent in the debate.
The baby was resting soundly in her crib despite the noise.
She was soundly criticized for her lack of preparation.
The team was soundly beaten in the final match.
They soundly rejected the proposal after much discussion.

Synonyms

  • thoroughly
    3.5 24/m
    thoroughly means doing something completely and carefully, while soundly often means doing it decisively or sleeping very deeply.
  • roundly
    5.8 1.00/m
    roundly means criticizing or praising someone in a direct and strong way, while soundly often suggests complete defeat or deep sleep.
  • resoundingly
    6.2 0.42/m
    resoundingly means doing something in an emphatic, clear way that all notice, while soundly can mean decisive or deep sleep.
