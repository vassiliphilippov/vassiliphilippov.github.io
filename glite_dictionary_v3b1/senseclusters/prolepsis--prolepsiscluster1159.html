prolepsis

noun
literary foreshadowing
Pronunciation: / proʊˈlɛpsɪs / (AmE), / prəʊˈlɛpsɪs / (BrE)
a literary device in which a future event is described as if it has already happened

Examples

Prolepsis in stories
The novel's opening chapter uses prolepsis to reveal the fate of its main character.
Writers often employ prolepsis to build suspense and engage readers.
In the film, a clever use of prolepsis lets the audience know what will happen later.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flashback - a literary device showing past events
  • synonym: foreshadowing - a literary device that hints at future events
