middle ground

noun
compromise or intermediate position
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an intermediate position, compromise, or area of agreement between two opposing views or sides

Examples

Find middle ground
After much debate, both parties agreed on a middle ground that satisfied everyone.
Her proposal offered a middle ground that neither side had considered before.
We need to find a middle ground between spending too much and saving too little.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compromise - an agreement or settlement of a dispute reached by each side making concessions
  • antonym: extreme - one of the two ends or extremes in a range, with no compromise in between
