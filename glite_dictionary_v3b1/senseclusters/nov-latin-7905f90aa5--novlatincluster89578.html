Nov-Latin

noun
artificial language based on Latin
Pronunciation: / noʊv ˈlætɪn / (AmE), / nɒv ˈlætɪn / (BrE)
an artificial language based on Latin, created by Daniele Rosa in 1890 as a proposed international auxiliary language

Examples

Nov-Latin language
Daniele Rosa created Nov-Latin in 1890 as a proposed international language.
Few people today have studied Nov-Latin, but it is still mentioned in linguistic history.
Unlike Esperanto, Nov-Latin never gained widespread popularity.
