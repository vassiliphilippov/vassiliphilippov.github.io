faint

adjective
weak or lacking strength
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /feɪnt/ (AmE), /feɪnt/ (BrE)
very slight, weak, or barely perceptible; not strong, clear, or easily noticed

Examples

Faint sound
Despite his faint efforts, the campaign failed to gain any traction.
She offered a faint smile, too tired to say anything more.

She heard a faint knocking at the door late at night.
The room was lit by a faint glow from the streetlamp outside.
There was only a faint trace of perfume left on her scarf.
From where I sat, I could only see a faint outline of the mountain through the mist.
He had only a faint memory of his early childhood.
There was a faint noise coming from the next room, barely audible over the wind.
