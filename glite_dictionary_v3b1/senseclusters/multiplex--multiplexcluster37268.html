multiplex

verb
to combine signals
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌplɛks / (BrE)
to combine or transmit multiple signals, messages, or streams at the same time over a single channel or medium

Examples

To multiplex data
Modern communication systems multiplex data to increase the efficiency of network usage.
The technician multiplexed several video feeds onto a single cable for the broadcast.
By using advanced equipment, scientists can multiplex tests and analyze multiple samples at once.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combine - to join or merge two or more things together
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart
