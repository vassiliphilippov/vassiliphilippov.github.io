plop

verb
set down suddenly or with a sound
3.6 1.08/m
Pronunciation: / plÉ‘p / (AmE), / plÉ’p / (BrE)
To set something or oneself down heavily or suddenly, often with a soft or audible sound.

Examples

To plop down
He plopped down beside me with a tired sigh.
Please plop the bag on the counter so I can unpack it.
She plopped herself onto the couch after a long day at work.

Synonyms

  • plump
    4.1 0.14/m
    plump is used when something lands down heavily with a small noise, while plop suggests a soft, quick drop.
  • flop
    4.6 0.00/m
    flop describes a sudden, loose fall that makes a soft heavy sound, while plop suggests a short, soft drop.
  • flump
    8.8 0.01/m
    flump is a heavy drop with a dull thud, while plop is lighter and often makes a softer, muffled sound.
  • plunk
    6.0 1.23/m
    plunk is a heavier drop that often makes a loud, sharp sound, while plop is softer and more gentle.
  • plank
    7.2 0.03/m
    plank often implies a firmer, abrupt placement, while plop suggests a softer, small audible drop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drop - let something fall to the ground
  • synonym: dump - put down or unload something quickly or carelessly
