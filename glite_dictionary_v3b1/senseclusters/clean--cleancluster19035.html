clean

adjective
ritually pure or untainted
1.0
Pronunciation: / klin / (AmE), / kliːn / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) free from anything regarded as unclean according to religious or ritual standards; ritually pure.

Examples

A clean sacrifice
According to ancient laws, only those who are clean may enter the sacred temple.
Certain foods were forbidden to keep the community clean in a religious sense.
She performed the purification rites to become clean before the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unclean - ritually impure or not clean
