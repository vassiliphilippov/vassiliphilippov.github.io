windily

adverb
in a verbose or wordy way
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndɪli / (AmE), / ˈwɪndɪli / (BrE)
In a verbose or wordy manner; using more words than necessary.

Examples

To speak windily
He described his weekend windily, leaving his friends impatient for the details.
The politician spoke windily throughout the debate, rarely getting to the point.
The report was written windily, making it difficult to find the main conclusions.
