straighten out

verb
improve behavior or situation
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən aʊt / (BrE)
to change one's behavior or attitude for the better, especially after previously acting poorly or irresponsibly

Examples

To straighten out your life
After getting a warning from his boss, he decided to straighten out his attitude at work.
If you don't straighten out, you could get into serious trouble with your teachers.
The school counselor helped several students straighten out their behavior last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backslide - to revert to a worse or previous state of behavior
  • synonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve behavior or practices
