nominalistic

adjective
relating to nominalism (philosophy)
7.9 9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊ.mə.nəˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒ.mɪ.nəˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of nominalism, the philosophical doctrine that universals or abstract concepts have no independent existence but exist only as names.

Examples

Nominalistic philosophy
His interpretation of language is firmly nominalistic, denying the existence of universals beyond words.
She wrote a thesis examining nominalistic perspectives in contemporary metaphysics.
The debate between nominalistic and realistic philosophies shaped medieval thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nominalism - the doctrine that universals are only names, not real things
  • antonym: realistic - relating to the philosophy of realism, the opposite of nominalism
