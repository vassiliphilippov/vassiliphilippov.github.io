counterplay

noun
chess: attack to counter an advantage
8.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkaÊŠntÉ™rËŒpleÉª / (AmE), / ËˆkaÊŠntÉ™ËŒpleÉª / (BrE)
In chess and other strategic games, an attack or series of moves made to counterbalance or negate an opponent's advantage elsewhere on the board.

Examples

Brilliant counterplay moves
His brilliant counterplay on the kingside kept him in the game despite being down in material.
The player initiated counterplay after losing control of the center squares.
Without sufficient counterplay, her position quickly deteriorated against her opponent's attack.

Synonyms

  • counterattack
    4.3 1.72/m
    counterattack is a sharp attack that responds directly to an opponent's assault, while counterplay is broader strategic moves to neutralize an opponent's advantage.
  • countermove
    6.7 0.10/m
    countermove is a single response move that answers an opponent's action directly, while counterplay is a broader series of strategic replies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterattack - an attack made in response to one's opponent's attack
