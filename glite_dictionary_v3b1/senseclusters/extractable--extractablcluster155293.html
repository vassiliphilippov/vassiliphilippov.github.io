extractable

adjective
able to be extracted or removed
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstræktəbl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktəbl / (BrE)
capable of being extracted; able to be removed, taken out, or obtained from something, especially by effort or by a specific process.

Examples

Oil extractable reserves
Only a small fraction of the oil reserves are extractable with current technology.
Some vitamins are extractable from food using certain solvents.
The minerals in this rock are extractable using modern mining techniques.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inextractable - not able to be extracted
