overstand

verb
to outlast others
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstænd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstænd / (BrE)
to remain standing or survive when others have fallen or disappeared; to outlast others

Examples

To overstand hard times
After the storm, only a few old trees managed to overstand while the rest were uprooted.
The tradition continues to overstand despite many changes in the community.
Some buildings overstand centuries of conflict and still stand today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endure - to continue to exist or last for a long time
  • antonym: perish - to die or be destroyed
  • synonym: survive - to remain alive or in existence after others have gone
