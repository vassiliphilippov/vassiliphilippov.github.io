headland

noun
point of land projecting into the sea
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.lənd / (BrE)
a high area of land with a steep side, projecting out into the sea or other large body of water

Examples

Rocky headland
Erosion has shaped the headland into dramatic cliffs.
The lighthouse stands on a rocky headland overlooking the rough sea.
We hiked along the coast and paused to rest on a grassy headland.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bay - a body of water partially enclosed by land
  • synonym: promontory - a high point of land or rock projecting into a body of water
