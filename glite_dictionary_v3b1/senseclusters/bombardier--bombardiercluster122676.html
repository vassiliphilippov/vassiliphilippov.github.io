bombardier

noun
member of bomber crew who releases bombs
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌbɑːmbɑːrˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌbɒmbəˈdɪə / (BrE)
a member of a bomber aircraft crew responsible for aiming and releasing bombs during a mission

Examples

Airplane bombardier

During World War II, the bombardier played a crucial role in strategic bombing missions.
The bombardier carefully aligned the bombsight with the target before releasing the payload.
