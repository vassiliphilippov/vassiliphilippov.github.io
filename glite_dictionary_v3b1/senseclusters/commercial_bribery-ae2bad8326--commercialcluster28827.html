commercial bribery

noun
bribery to influence business decisions
4.9 7.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɝː.ʃəl ˈbraɪ.bɚ.i / (AmE), / kəˈmɜː.ʃəl ˈbraɪ.bər.i / (BrE)
the act of bribing an agent or employee of a business to influence the awarding of contracts or transactions in favor of the briber

Examples

Commercial bribery laws
Laws against commercial bribery are intended to protect fair competition in the marketplace.
She was dismissed after being implicated in a commercial bribery scheme involving vendor kickbacks.
The company was fined millions for engaging in commercial bribery to secure government contracts.
