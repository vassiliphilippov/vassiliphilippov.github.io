transconductance

noun
current-voltage ratio
9.9
Pronunciation: /træns.kən.ˈdʌk.təns/ /; / /trænz.kən.ˈdʌk.təns/ (AmE), /træns.kən.ˈdʌk.təns/ /; / /trænz.kən.ˈdʌk.təns/ (BrE)
the ratio of the change in output current to the change in input voltage in an electronic device, such as a transistor

Examples

The transconductance of transistor
The transconductance of a transistor affects how much it amplifies a signal.
Engineers measured the transconductance to determine the efficiency of the new circuit design.
A higher transconductance usually means better performance in electronic amplifiers.
