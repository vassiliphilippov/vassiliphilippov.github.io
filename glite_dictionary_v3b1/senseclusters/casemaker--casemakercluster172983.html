casemaker

noun
insect larva in case
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
an insect larva that constructs and lives in a protective case made from silk and surrounding materials

Examples

Insect casemaker
The casemaker carefully gathered tiny pebbles to build its protective home.
Scientists observed the casemaker moving slowly along the stream bed with its case.
A casemaker can use leaves, sand, or twigs to construct its shelter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caddisfly larva - the aquatic larva of a caddisfly, known for building protective cases
