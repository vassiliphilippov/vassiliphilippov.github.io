intercross

verb
to intersect repeatedly
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to cross or pass over each other, especially referring to lines, paths, or routes that intersect.

Examples

Lines intercross each other
The city is full of narrow streets intercrossing in a complex network.
On the map, several railway lines intercross near the central station.
The spider spun a web with threads intercrossing at many points.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point
  • synonym: intersect - to cross or meet at a point
