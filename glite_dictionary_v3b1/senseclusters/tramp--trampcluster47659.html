tramp

noun
long walk for exercise or pleasure
6.4 3.2
Pronunciation: / træmp / (AmE), / træmp /; / tramp / (BrE)
a long walk, especially for exercise or enjoyment, or a person who travels on foot for pleasure or adventure

Examples

A mountain tramp
After a long tramp in the hills, they enjoyed a picnic by the river.
The group organized a Saturday tramp through the woods.
We went for a tramp across the fields last weekend.
As a young man, he spent a summer as a tramp through the Scottish Highlands.
The old railway line is now popular with tramps and hikers alike.
They met a cheerful tramp who shared stories of walking across Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hiker - a person who goes for long walks in the countryside
  • degree lower: stroll - a slow, relaxed walk
  • synonym: wanderer - a person who travels aimlessly without a fixed route
