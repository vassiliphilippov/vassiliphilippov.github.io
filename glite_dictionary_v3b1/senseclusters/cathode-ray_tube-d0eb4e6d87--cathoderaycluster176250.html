cathode-ray tube

noun
type of electronic display tube
5.4 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.θoʊd reɪ tuːb/ /; / /ˈkæ.θoʊd reɪ tjuːb/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.θəʊd reɪ tjuːb/ /; / /ˈkæ.θəʊd reɪ tuːb/ (BrE)
an electronic vacuum tube in which a heated cathode emits a beam of electrons that are accelerated and focused to strike a phosphorescent screen, forming images—commonly used in older television and computer monitors

Examples

A cathode-ray tube screen
A cathode-ray tube works by directing electrons onto a phosphorescent screen to create pictures.
Many computer monitors from the 1990s contained a cathode-ray tube before flat-panel technology became widespread.
The old television set used a cathode-ray tube to display images on the screen.
