catechol

noun
1,2-dihydroxybenzene group
Pronunciation: / ˈkætɪˌkoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkætɪkɒl / (BrE)
a chemical group consisting of a benzene ring with two hydroxyl groups in the 1,2-positions, often found in larger molecules

Examples

Catechol group
The scientist identified a catechol group in the compound's structure.
Many neurotransmitters, such as dopamine, contain a catechol unit.
The presence of a catechol group can affect how a drug interacts with enzymes.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: phenol - a benzene ring with a single hydroxyl group
  • synonym: resorcinol - a benzene ring with hydroxyl groups in the 1,3-positions
