Lake Albert

proper noun
lake on Uganda–Congo border in Africa
Pronunciation: / leɪk ˈælbɚt / (AmE), / leɪk ˈælbət / (BrE)
a large, shallow lake located on the border between Uganda and the Democratic Republic of the Congo, in the Great Rift Valley of Africa

Examples

Lake Albert in Africa
Fishermen on Lake Albert rely on the lake's rich fish stocks for their livelihood.
Rising water levels in Lake Albert have displaced thousands of people in the surrounding area.
The border between Uganda and Congo runs through Lake Albert in the Great Rift Valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lake Edward - lake in Central Africa, located southwest of Lake Albert
  • synonym: Lake Victoria - large lake in East Africa, source of the Nile
