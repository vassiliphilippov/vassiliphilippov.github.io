Second Sino-Japanese War

proper noun
war between China and Japan (1894–1895) over Korea
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkənd saɪnoʊ dʒəˈpæniz wɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd saɪnəʊ dʒəˈpæniːz wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought between China and Japan from 1894 to 1895 over dominance in Korea, resulting in a decisive Japanese victory, especially at Port Arthur

Examples

The Second Sino-Japanese War in Korea
During the Second Sino-Japanese War, Korea's fate was decided by the victories and defeats of both sides.
Historians often analyze the causes and consequences of the Second Sino-Japanese War.
The Second Sino-Japanese War fundamentally shifted the balance of power in East Asia.
