Erwinia

noun
genus of plant-pathogenic bacteria
9.9
Pronunciation: / ərˈwɪniə / (AmE), / ɜːˈwɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped, motile bacteria that infect and cause diseases in plants.

Examples

Erwinia bacteria infection
Erwinia is known for causing soft rot and fire blight in many crops and ornamental plants.
Researchers are studying Erwinia species to understand how they infect fruit trees.
The outbreak of Erwinia devastated the local apple orchards last season.
