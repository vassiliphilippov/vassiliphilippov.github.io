evangelical

adjective
of a Christian movement stressing conversion and the Bible
6.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /iː.væn.ˈdʒel.ɪ.kəl/ /; / /ə.væn.ˈdʒel.ɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /iː.væn.ˈdʒel.ɪ.kəl/ /; / /ə.væn.ˈdʒel.ɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
relating to a branch of the Christian church that emphasizes personal conversion, the authority of the Bible, and the importance of sharing the Christian message.

Examples

Evangelical church
Her views are influenced by her evangelical upbringing and church community.
Many evangelical Christians believe in spreading their faith through missionary work.
The politician addressed a largely evangelical audience during his campaign.
