ell

noun
extension at right angles to main building
Pronunciation: / ɛl / (AmE), / ɛl / (BrE)
an extension at right angles to the main building, typically forming an L-shape

Examples

A house ell
The architect designed an ell to connect the garage to the main structure.
The old farmhouse had an ell attached to provide extra bedrooms for guests.
They renovated the kitchen, which was located in the ell at the rear of the house.
