company

noun
the crew and officers of a ship
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌmpəni /; / ˈkʌm.pə.ni / (AmE), / ˈkʌmpəni /; / ˈkʌm.pəni / (BrE)
a group of people organized for a specific purpose, such as a military unit, a fire crew, or a ship's crew

Examples

A fire company
All members of the company were required to attend the safety drill.
The company celebrated the ship's anniversary with a special meal.
The captain addressed the company before departing from the port.
A ladder company and an engine company were both dispatched to the scene.
Each company in the fire department has specialized tasks and equipment.
The city added a new company to respond more quickly to fires downtown.
Each company is commanded by a captain in the army.
He was assigned to a new company just before deployment.
The entire company assembled for roll call at dawn.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: battalion - a larger military unit made up of several companies
  • synonym: crew - all the people working on a ship except officers
  • degree lower: platoon - a subdivision of a company, usually consisting of two or more squads
