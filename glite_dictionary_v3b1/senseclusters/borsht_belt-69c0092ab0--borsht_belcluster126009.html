borsht belt

noun
resort area in Catskills for Jewish guests
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrʃt ˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːʃt ˌbɛlt / (BrE)
(informal) an area in the Catskill Mountains of New York known for its resorts frequented by Jewish guests, especially popular in the mid-20th century and famous for its influence on American entertainment and comedy.

Examples

The borsht belt resorts
Many famous comedians got their start performing in the borsht belt resorts.
She wrote a memoir about her summers spent in the borsht belt as a child.
The golden era of the borsht belt shaped much of American stand-up comedy.
