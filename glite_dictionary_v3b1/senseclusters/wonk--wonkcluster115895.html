wonk

noun
boringly studious or socially awkward person
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɑŋk / (AmE), / wɒŋk / (BrE)
a person who is very interested in and knowledgeable about a particular subject, especially in a technical or academic field; often seen as studious or nerdy

Examples

A wonk in politics
Although he was considered a wonk, his academic achievements eventually won him respect.
She was labeled a wonk in school because she always finished her homework before anyone else.
The other kids teased him for being a wonk who would rather study than play sports.
He is a computer wonk who spends hours reading about the latest technology.
The conference was filled with policy wonks discussing government reforms.
As a science wonk, she enjoys analyzing research papers in her free time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dunce - a slow-witted or silly person
  • synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • synonym: geek - a person who is very knowledgeable about a particular subject
  • synonym: nerd - a person who is very interested in academic or technical subjects and is often socially awkward
  • antonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field
  • synonym: specialist - someone who focuses on a specific subject or activity
