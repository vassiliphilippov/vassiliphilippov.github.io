clogged

adjective
blocked or obstructed
2.8 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / klÉ‘gd / (AmE), / klÉ’gd / (BrE)
blocked or obstructed so that movement or flow is difficult or impossible

Examples

Clogged nose
Her nose felt clogged due to her cold.
The drain became clogged with hair and soap scum over time.
Traffic was clogged on the highway after the accident.
My nose is clogged because of this terrible cold.
The kitchen sink was clogged with grease after the big family dinner.
Traffic was clogged on the highway due to the accident.

Synonyms

  • blocked
    1.7 0.00/m
    blocked is completely closed so nothing can pass, while clogged suggests something inside slows or stops the flow.
  • obstructed
    4.6 0.01/m
    obstructed is a formal word for being hindered or blocked in passage, while clogged feels more physical and everyday.
  • choked
    4.3 0.00/m
    choked is often used when a pipe or throat is tightly filled and cannot pass material, while clogged is more general.
  • stopped-up
    4.2 0.01/m
    stopped-up describes an opening filled so nothing goes through, while clogged covers many types of blocked flow.
  • congested
    4.4 0.93/m
    congested describes places or bodies too full of people or fluid, while clogged means actually blocked and cannot move.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blocked - unable to pass through because of an obstruction
  • antonym: unclogged - free from obstruction or blockage
