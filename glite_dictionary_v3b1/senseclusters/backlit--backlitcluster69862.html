backlit

adjective
illuminated from behind
Pronunciation: / ˈbæklɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæklɪt / (BrE)
lit from behind, so that the object or surface appears bright or glowing

Examples

Backlit screen
The advertisement was clearly visible at night because it was backlit.
She prefers a backlit keyboard for typing in dimly lit rooms.
The artist displayed her photograph in a backlit frame to enhance its colors.
The photographer captured a stunning image of the backlit tree at sunset.
Her face was softly backlit by the morning light streaming through the window.
The display uses a backlit screen to make the text easier to read in the dark.

Related Terms

  • antonym: front-lit - illuminated from the front
  • synonym: illuminated - lit up or made bright with light
  • synonym: silhouetted - appearing as a dark shape against a lighter background
  • antonym: unlit - not provided with light; not illuminated
