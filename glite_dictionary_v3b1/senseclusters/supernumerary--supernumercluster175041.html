supernumerary

adjective
more than the required number
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈnuːməˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈnjuːmərəri / (BrE)
present in excess of the normal or required number; more than is needed or usual, especially used in technical or formal contexts

Examples

Supernumerary teeth
Many large theatres keep a few supernumerary actors on call for crowd scenes.
The patient was born with a supernumerary toe on her right foot.
The report found several supernumerary staff members whose roles overlapped with existing positions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: necessary - required to be done, achieved, or present
  • synonym: redundant - not or no longer needed or useful
  • synonym: superfluous - unnecessary, especially through being more than enough
