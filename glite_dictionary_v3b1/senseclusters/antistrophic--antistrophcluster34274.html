antistrophic

adjective
relating to an antistrophe (poetry/chord)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tɪˈstroʊ.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌantɪˈstrəʊ.fɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an antistrophe, particularly in poetry or Greek chorus structure.

Examples

Antistrophic poem structure
Her analysis focused on the antistrophic structure of the poem, which answered the initial strophe.
In Greek tragedy, the antistrophic sections often reflect a reversal or response to earlier themes.
The chorus moved in an antistrophic pattern, mirroring their earlier movement across the stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antistrophe - the part of an ancient Greek choral ode answering a previous strophe, sung by the chorus as it moved from left to right
