impaired

adjective
diminished or weakened; not fully functional
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛrd /; / ɪmˈpɛərd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeəd / (BrE)
weakened or damaged, especially in a way that affects normal function or ability

Examples

Impaired mobility
After his accident, his impaired mobility made it difficult to climb stairs.
Hours of loud music can leave you with temporarily impaired hearing.
Many laws exist to protect the rights of impaired individuals in the workplace.
He was found to be impaired and unable to operate heavy machinery safely.
Several programs support people who are hearing impaired in their daily lives.
The medication helped those with impaired vision to see more clearly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damaged - harmed or injured; not in good condition
  • synonym: disabled - having a physical or mental condition that limits movements, senses, or activities
  • antonym: healthy - having good physical or mental health
  • synonym: unfit - not in a suitable condition, especially physically or mentally
  • antonym: unimpaired - not damaged or weakened; in perfect condition
