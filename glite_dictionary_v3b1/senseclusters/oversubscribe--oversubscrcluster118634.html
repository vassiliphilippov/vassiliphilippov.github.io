oversubscribe

verb
to allocate beyond capacity
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərsəbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəsəbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to assign more users or resources to a system or service than it can handle at once, expecting not all will use it simultaneously

Examples

To oversubscribe a network
Internet service providers often oversubscribe bandwidth, assuming not all customers will use maximum speed at once.
The company decided to oversubscribe its servers to maximize efficiency during off-peak hours.
If you oversubscribe resources, performance may drop when too many users are active simultaneously.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overbook - to sell more tickets or places than are available
  • antonym: underutilize - to use less than the available capacity
