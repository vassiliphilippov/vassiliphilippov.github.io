steal

noun
(baseball) act of advancing a base during a pitch
8.7 5.7
Pronunciation: /stiːl/ (AmE), /stiːl/ (BrE)
(in baseball) an act of advancing to the next base while a pitch is being thrown, without the help of a hit, walk, or error.

Examples

A steal of second base
She attempted a bold steal of second base during the playoff match.
The player's quick steal in the ninth inning changed the outcome of the game.
With his record number of steals, he became a legend in baseball history.
