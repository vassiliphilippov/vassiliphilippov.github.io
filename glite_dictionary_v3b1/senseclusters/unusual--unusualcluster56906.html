Synonyms

  • out of the ordinary
    4.1 2/m
    out of the ordinary describes something clearly different from normal or expected, while unusual covers anything not common.
  • atypical
    5.7 3/m
    atypical is used in formal or scientific contexts for things that do not match the norm, while unusual is more general.
  • anomalous
    8.1 2/m
    anomalous sounds formal and often appears in science to mark a clear deviation, while unusual is everyday and broader.
  • non-typical
    4.8 0.00/m
    non-typical labels things that do not follow usual types or patterns, while unusual is a general word for uncommon occurrences.
  • different
    2.0 40/m
    different simply says not the same or typical in some way, while unusual implies being rare or out of the ordinary.
  • uncommon
    4.6 10/m
    uncommon points to low frequency or few examples, while unusual covers both rarity and other kinds of odd or unexpected traits.
  • rare
    4.1 50/m
    rare means very infrequent or scarce in number, while unusual can mean different or surprising without strong focus on frequency.
  • irregular
    3.4 0.9/m
    irregular describes something that does not follow a pattern or rule, while unusual is a broader term for anything not common.
  • peculiar
    4.6 10/m
    peculiar feels odd or strange with a hint of curiosity, while unusual is more neutral and does not always sound odd.
  • odd
    1.0 40/m
    odd suggests something slightly strange or quirky in a casual way, while unusual is more neutral and can be formal or informal.
  • strange
    2.3 80/m
    strange often sounds stronger and makes people feel confused or uneasy, while unusual can simply mean not common or expected.
