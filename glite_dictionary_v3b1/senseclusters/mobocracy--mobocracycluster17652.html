mobocracy

noun
government by the mob or masses
Pronunciation: / moʊˈbɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / məʊˈbɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A political system in which the masses or a disorderly crowd exercise control or power; government by mob rule.

Examples

Mobocracy rule
Fears of mobocracy prompted leaders to call for stricter constitutional limits.
Many historians argue that unchecked social unrest can lead to mobocracy rather than genuine democracy.
The revolution descended into mobocracy as crowds seized control of the government offices.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anarchy - absence of government or law
  • degree lower: democracy - government by the people or their elected representatives
