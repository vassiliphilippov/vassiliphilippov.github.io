bayberry wax

noun
fragrant green wax from bayberry, used in candles
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪˌbɛri wæks / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbəri waks / (BrE)
a fragrant green wax obtained from the bayberry plant (wax myrtle), used for making candles

Examples

Bayberry wax candles
Historically, colonial Americans used bayberry wax to produce cleaner-burning candles than tallow.
It takes many berries to produce even a small amount of bayberry wax for crafts or candle-making.
These handmade candles are made from natural bayberry wax and have a subtle, earthy scent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wax myrtle - the plant from which bayberry wax is obtained
