up to their necks

adjective
deeply involved; entangled
Pronunciation: / ʌp tə ðɛr nɛks / (AmE), / ʌp tə ðeə nɛks / (BrE)
deeply involved or entangled in something, especially something difficult or troublesome

Examples

Up to their necks in trouble
After the company collapsed, they found themselves up to their necks in legal troubles.
She feels up to her neck in debt and doesn't know how to get out.
We are up to our necks in work this month and can't take on more projects.
