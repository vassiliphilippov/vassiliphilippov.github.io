outclimb

verb
to climb out
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈklaɪm / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈklaɪm / (BrE)
to climb out of a confined space or over an obstacle in order to escape or leave

Examples

To outclimb a fence
The children managed to outclimb the locked fence and run to safety.
After hours in the cave, they finally outclimbed the narrow shaft to reach daylight.
She had to outclimb the steep embankment to escape the rising water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descend - to move or climb down from a higher place
  • synonym: escape - to get away from a place or situation
