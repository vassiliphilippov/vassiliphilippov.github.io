account

verb
be the main cause or source of something
6.1 2.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (BrE)
to explain, justify, or be the cause of something

Examples

To account for changes
Rising costs accounted for the company's reduced profits last year.
Student fees account for a substantial part of the university's income.
The new marketing campaign accounts for much of the recent growth in sales.
How do you account for the team's poor performance this season?
She could not account for her sudden change in mood.
The director tried to account for the project delays at the meeting.
