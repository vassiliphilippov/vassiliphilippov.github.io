spread out

verb
distribute over an area
3.1
Pronunciation: / sprɛd aʊt / (AmE), / sprɛd aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange, distribute, extend, or unfold things so that they cover a larger area or surface

Examples

To spread out the papers
Please spread out the photos on the table so we can all see them.
She spread out the picnic blanket beneath the tree.
The documents were spread out across his desk for easy access.
Picnic blankets were soon spread out over the green lawn.
The children began to spread out across the playground as soon as the bell rang.
Troops were ordered to spread out and cover more ground during their search.
As the paint dries, it tends to spread out unevenly if you apply too much.
Maps were spread out across the table as the team planned their route.
They watched the fog slowly spread out over the entire valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to put things in a neat, attractive, or required order

  • synonym: expand - to increase in size, number, or importance; to spread out
  • synonym: extend - to make something longer or wider; expand its area
  • synonym: fan out - to spread outward in the shape of a fan
  • antonym: fold - to bend something so that one part covers another; opposite of spread out
  • synonym: scatter - to throw things loosely about in different directions
