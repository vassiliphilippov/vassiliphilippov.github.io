esker

noun
glacial sediment ridge
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskər / (AmE), / ˈɛskə / (BrE)
(geology) a long, winding ridge of gravel and sediment deposited by meltwater from glaciers or ice sheets.

Examples

A long esker ridge
Glacial meltwater shaped the prominent esker that threads through the valley.
Scientists examined the sediments to learn when the esker was formed.
The hiking trail follows the course of the ancient esker.
