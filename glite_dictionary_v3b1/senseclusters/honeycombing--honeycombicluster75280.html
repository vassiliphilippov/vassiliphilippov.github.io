honeycombing

noun
lung scarring pattern
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌnikəʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌnikəʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
a pattern of clustered, cystic airspaces seen on lung scans, typically caused by long-term scarring in advanced pulmonary fibrosis.

Examples

Lung honeycombing
The radiologist identified honeycombing on the patient's chest CT, suggesting severe lung disease.
Extensive honeycombing is often seen in cases of advanced pulmonary fibrosis.
Doctors use the presence of honeycombing to help diagnose chronic interstitial lung conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fibrosis - the thickening and scarring of connective tissue
  • antonym: normal lung - lung tissue without abnormal scarring or cystic changes
