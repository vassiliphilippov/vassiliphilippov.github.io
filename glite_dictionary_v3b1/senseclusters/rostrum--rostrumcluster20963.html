rostrum

noun
raised platform for speakers or performers
9.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈrɑː.strəm/ (AmE), /ˈrɒ.strəm/ (BrE)
A platform raised above the surrounding level, used to give prominence to the person standing or speaking on it.

Examples

Speak from the rostrum
At the graduation ceremony, each student was called up to the rostrum to receive their diploma.
The politician stepped onto the rostrum to address the enthusiastic crowd.
The winner stood proudly on the highest rostrum, holding her gold medal aloft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dais - a low platform for a lectern, seats of honor, or a throne
  • synonym: podium - a small platform for a speaker, performer, or conductor
