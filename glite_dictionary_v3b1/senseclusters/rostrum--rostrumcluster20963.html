rostrum

noun
raised platform for speakers or performers
7.1 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / Ààr…ëÀêstr…ôm / (AmE), / Ààr…ístr…ôm / (BrE)
A platform raised above the surrounding level, used to give prominence to the person standing or speaking on it.

Examples

Speak from the rostrum
At the graduation ceremony, each student was called up to the rostrum to receive their diploma.
The politician stepped onto the rostrum to address the enthusiastic crowd.
The winner stood proudly on the highest rostrum, holding her gold medal aloft.

Synonyms

  • podium
    3.8 6.72/m
    podium is a small raised platform for a speaker or award winner, while rostrum is a platform that gives a person public prominence.
  • dais
    7.6 1.36/m
    dais is a raised platform at the front of a room for important speakers or guests, while rostrum is used to give prominence to a speaker.
  • platform
    1.6 11/m
    platform is a raised horizontal surface for standing, performing, or work, while rostrum is specifically for highlighting a person who speaks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: podium - a small platform for a speaker, performer, or conductor
