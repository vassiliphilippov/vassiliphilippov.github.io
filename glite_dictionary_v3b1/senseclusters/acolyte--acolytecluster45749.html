acolyte

noun
a person assisting in religious services
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈækəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈækəlaɪt / (BrE)
a person who assists a priest or minister in a liturgical service; in the Roman Catholic Church, a cleric ordained to the highest rank of the minor orders.

Examples

Church acolyte
As an acolyte, he helped prepare the altar before Mass began.
The acolyte carried the candle down the aisle during the service.
The bishop ordained several young men as acolytes last Sunday.
