speech-read

verb
interpret speech by lipreading
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈspiːtʃ ˌrid / (AmE), / ˈspiːtʃ ˌriːd / (BrE)
(of a deaf or hard-of-hearing person) to understand spoken words by visually interpreting the movements of the lips, face, and tongue, also known as lipreading.

Examples

To speech-read lips
During meetings, he relies on his ability to speech-read the speakers.
She learned to speech-read at a young age after losing her hearing.
Some deaf individuals prefer to speech-read rather than use sign language.

Related Terms
