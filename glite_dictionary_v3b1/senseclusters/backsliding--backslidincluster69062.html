backsliding

noun
relapse to a previous state
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌslaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌslaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
A return to a previous, less advanced or less moral state or behavior after making progress.

Examples

A backsliding habit
After months of healthy eating, her backsliding began when she returned to old habits.
He admitted to some backsliding in his commitment to quit smoking, but resolved to try again.
The organization is concerned about the recent backsliding on environmental policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progress - Forward or onward movement toward a better state or condition.
  • synonym: relapse - A return to a previous undesirable state, especially after improvement.
