milliohm

noun
unit of resistance
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlioʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪəʊm / (BrE)
a unit of electrical resistance equal to one thousandth of an ohm

Examples

Five milliohms
The engineer measured the wire's resistance at just five milliohms.
To ensure accuracy, the device can detect changes as small as one milliohm.
A low milliohm value indicates that the material conducts electricity very well.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ohm - the standard unit of electrical resistance
