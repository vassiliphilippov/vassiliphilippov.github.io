impose one's beliefs

verb
force own opinions on others
2.5 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpoʊz wʌnz bɪ.ˈliːfs/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpoʊz wənz bɪ.ˈliːfs/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpəʊz wʌnz bɪ.ˈliːfs/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpəʊz wənz bɪ.ˈliːfs/ (BrE)
to force one's own opinions or convictions on other people, especially in a way that is unwelcome or overbearing

Examples

To impose your beliefs

She tries not to impose her beliefs on her children, allowing them to make their own choices.
