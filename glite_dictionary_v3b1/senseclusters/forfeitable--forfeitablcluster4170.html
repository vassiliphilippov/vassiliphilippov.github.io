forfeitable

adjective
subject to loss
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrfɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːfɪtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be lost or taken away as a penalty for breaking a rule or law

Examples

A forfeitable deposit
The security deposit is forfeitable if the tenant damages the property.
Under the agreement, certain rights are forfeitable if the terms are violated.
Bonuses may be forfeitable if employees leave before the end of the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonforfeitable - not able to be lost or taken away as a penalty
  • synonym: seizable - able to be taken by legal authority
