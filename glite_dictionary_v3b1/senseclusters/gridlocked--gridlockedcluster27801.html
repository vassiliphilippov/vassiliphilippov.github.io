gridlocked

unable to move forward or make progress due to opposing forces blocking each other, especially in politics or negotiations

Examples

Gridlocked negotiations
The city council remained gridlocked over the new housing proposal for months.
Talks between the two companies became gridlocked after both sides refused to compromise.
Progress on the bill was gridlocked by disagreements between political parties.

Synonyms

  • deadlocked
    6.6 0.01/m
    deadlocked describes a complete standstill caused by opposing forces in any situation, while gridlocked focuses on political or negotiation blockage.
  • stalemated
    6.9 0.00/m
    stalemated means being unable to proceed because forces are equally strong, while gridlocked is used more for political or negotiation deadlock.
  • at an impasse
    6.3 0.40/m
    at an impasse describes a situation where no agreement is reached and talks stop, while gridlocked implies political or negotiation blockage.

Related Terms
