lag screw

noun
heavy-duty wood screw with a wrench head
2.9
Pronunciation: / læɡ skru / (AmE), / læɡ skruː / (BrE)
a heavy-duty screw with a square or hexagonal head, designed to be driven with a wrench into wood or other materials

Examples

A lag screw head
A long lag screw can support a lot of weight in heavy construction projects.
Make sure to pre-drill a hole before inserting a lag screw into the wooden post.
The carpenter secured the beams together using a lag screw for extra strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lag bolt - a similar heavy screw, often used interchangeably with lag screw
