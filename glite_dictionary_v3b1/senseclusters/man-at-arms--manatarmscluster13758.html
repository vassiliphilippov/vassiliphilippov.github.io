man-at-arms

noun
heavily armed medieval soldier
6.0
Pronunciation: / mæn ət ˈɑrmz / (AmE), / mæn ət ˈɑːmz / (BrE)
a heavily armed and mounted soldier in medieval times, especially one serving as a professional fighter or member of a military retinue

Examples

A man-at-arms soldier
A group of men-at-arms rode in front, guarding the royal carriage.
During the siege, a single man-at-arms managed to hold the gate against dozens of attackers.
The historian described the life of a man-at-arms in fourteenth-century England.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knight - a man awarded a rank of honor by a monarch or military leader, especially a mounted soldier of noble birth in medieval times
