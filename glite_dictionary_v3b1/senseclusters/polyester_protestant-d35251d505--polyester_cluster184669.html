polyester Protestant

noun
self-consciously superficial Protestant (esp. American)
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɑː.liː.ˈes.tər ˈprɑː.tə.stənt/ (AmE), /pɒl.ɪ.ˈes.tə ˈprɒ.tə.stənt/ (BrE)
A person, especially in American culture, who is perceived as overly superficial or artificial, often characterized by a particular style of dress or appearance associated with certain Protestant communities.

Examples

A polyester Protestant style
Although he grew up in a traditional church, his flashy outfits made people call him a polyester Protestant.
She laughed and said her family had gone from staunch Calvinists to polyester Protestants overnight.
Some critics lampooned suburban pastors as polyester Protestants in the 1970s.
