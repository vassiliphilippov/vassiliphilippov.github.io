paratyphoid fever

noun
infectious disease similar to typhoid fever
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈtaɪfɔɪd ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈtaɪfɔɪd ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an infectious intestinal disease, similar to typhoid fever, caused by certain bacteria of the genus Salmonella

Examples

Paratyphoid fever infection
Doctors suspected paratyphoid fever when the patient presented with high fever and abdominal pain.
The outbreak was traced to contaminated water, resulting in over fifty cases of paratyphoid fever.
Unlike typhoid, paratyphoid fever often begins with milder symptoms but still requires prompt treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: typhoid fever - serious infectious disease caused by Salmonella typhi bacteria
