dialectics

noun
philosophical reasoning by conflicting forces
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪks / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪks / (BrE)
a method of reasoning or argument based on the conflict of opposing forces, especially as used in dialectical materialism to explain change and development

Examples

Marxist dialectics theory
Marxist theory is deeply rooted in the principles of dialectics.
The philosopher explained history through dialectics, focusing on the clash between opposing social classes.
Understanding dialectics is essential for studying materialist philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialectical materialism - philosophical approach emphasizing change through conflict of opposites
