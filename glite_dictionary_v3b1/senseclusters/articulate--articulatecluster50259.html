articulate

verb
connect by a joint
5.7 6.0
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ˈtɪk.jə.leɪt/ /; / /ɑːr.ˈtɪk.jə.lət/ (AmE), /ɑː.ˈtɪk.jʊ.leɪt/ /; / /ɑː.ˈtɪk.jʊ.lət/ (BrE)
to connect by a joint or to be joined by a joint (especially in anatomy or structures)

Examples

Articulate bones together
Many animal skeletons are articulated to demonstrate how their bones connect at the joints.
Modern prosthetic limbs are carefully articulated to mimic natural movement.
The engineer articulated the two metal rods to allow for flexible movement.
In the human knee, the femur and tibia articulate to allow for movement.
Some dinosaurs had jaws that could articulate with the skull to widen their gape.
The ribs articulate with the vertebrae at two distinct points on each rib.
