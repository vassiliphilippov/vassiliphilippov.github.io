Old Bulgarian

noun
earliest written Slavic language, 9th–11th c.
9.9
Pronunciation: / oʊld bʌlˈɡɛriən / (AmE), / əʊld bʌlˈɡɛəriən / (BrE)
the earliest written Slavic language, used in the 9th–11th centuries, especially known for its use in early Bible translations

Examples

Old Bulgarian language
Many medieval manuscripts are preserved in Old Bulgarian.
Scholars study Old Bulgarian to understand the roots of Slavic languages.
The Gospels were first translated into Old Bulgarian in the 9th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Old Church Slavonic - the first Slavic literary language, used in early translations of the Bible
