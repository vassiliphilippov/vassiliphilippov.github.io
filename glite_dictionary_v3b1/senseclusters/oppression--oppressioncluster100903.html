oppression

noun
feeling of being weighed down
Pronunciation: / əˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / əˈprɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A feeling of being weighed down or burdened, especially with anxiety or discomfort.

Examples

A feeling of oppression
During the storm, a strong oppression seemed to fill the air, making it hard to breathe.
She felt a sudden oppression in her chest as the anxiety grew.
The oppressive heat left everyone with a sense of oppression and fatigue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • antonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress
