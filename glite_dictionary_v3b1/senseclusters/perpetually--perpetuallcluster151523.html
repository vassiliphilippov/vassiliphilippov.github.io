perpetually

adverb
continually; endlessly
1.9 3.9
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈpetʃ.uː.ə.lɪ/ /; / /pər.ˈpet.jʊə.lɪ/ (AmE), /pə.ˈpetʃ.ʊə.lɪ/ /; / /pə.ˈpet.jʊə.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that happens all the time, without stopping or changing

Examples

Is perpetually busy
She seems perpetually busy, never taking a moment to rest.
The mountains are perpetually covered with snow, regardless of the season.
The old clock in the hallway ticks perpetually throughout the night.
He feels perpetually busy and rarely finds time to relax.
She seemed perpetually cheerful, regardless of the circumstances.
The machine ran perpetually for years without needing repairs.
