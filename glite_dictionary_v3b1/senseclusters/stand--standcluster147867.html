stand

verb
be in effect; remain in force
8.3
Pronunciation: / stænd / (AmE), / stænd / (BrE)
to be in a particular state, condition, or to remain inactive or stationary

Examples

The rule stands
As long as the regulation stands, all businesses must comply.
The ban on smoking indoors still stands despite recent protests.
The court ruled that the previous decision would stand.
After the heavy rain, pools of standing water collected in the fields.
If milk is left to stand too long, it can spoil.
The car had stood idle in the garage for years before being sold.
After realizing his mistake, he stood corrected before the committee.
She stood ready to help whenever her team needed assistance.
The suspect stands accused of multiple charges in the court case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apply - to be relevant or enforceable
  • antonym: become - to begin to be in a particular state
  • antonym: expire - to become no longer valid or in force
  • antonym: move - to change position or go from one place to another
  • synonym: remain - to continue in the same state or condition
  • antonym: sit - be in a position of rest with the body supported by the buttocks or thighs
