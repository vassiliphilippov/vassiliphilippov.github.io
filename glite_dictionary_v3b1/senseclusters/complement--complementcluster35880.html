complement

noun
something that completes or enhances
5.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.plə.mənt/ /; / /ˈkɑːm.plɪ.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.plɪ.mənt/ /; / /ˈkɒm.plə.mənt/ (BrE)
something that completes, enhances, or goes well with another thing; one of two things that together make a whole

Examples

Complement to a meal
A good sauce can serve as a complement to any meat dish.

The crisp salad made a delightful complement to the rich main course.
Red and green are often said to be complements on the color wheel.
She found a partner whose skills were the ideal complement to her own.
The sauce is the perfect complement to the grilled fish.
