codetermination

noun
worker participation in management
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊdɪˌtɜrmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊdɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a system or process in which workers or different groups participate together in making management or organizational decisions, especially through formal representation

Examples

Codetermination in companies
German companies are well known for their use of codetermination in corporate governance.
Through codetermination, employees can influence important decisions affecting their workplace.
The union advocated for codetermination to give workers a stronger voice in management.
The new policy encourages codetermination between employees and management on workplace issues.
Many European companies practice codetermination to ensure workers have a voice in important decisions.
Successful codetermination can lead to better cooperation and more effective solutions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autocracy - a system where one person has all the decision-making power
  • antonym: autonomy - the right or condition of self-government or independent decision-making
  • synonym: collaboration - the act of working together to achieve a common goal
  • synonym: industrial democracy - a system where employees have a voice in company decisions
