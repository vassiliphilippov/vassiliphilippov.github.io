metathorax

noun
hindmost thorax segment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
the third and hindmost segment of an insect's thorax, to which the hindwings and second pair of legs are attached

Examples

The metathorax segment
The butterfly's hindwings are attached to its metathorax.
Scientists examined the metathorax to identify the species of the beetle.
The metathorax supports important muscles for the insect's movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mesothorax - the middle segment of an insect's thorax
  • antonym: prothorax - the first segment of an insect's thorax
