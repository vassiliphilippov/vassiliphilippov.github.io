formulaic

adjective
produced according to a set formula
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔr.mjəˈleɪ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.mjʊˈleɪ.ɪk / (BrE)
(especially of language, ideas, or artistic work) produced according to a fixed form or set of rules; lacking originality.

Examples

A formulaic phrase
Critics argued that the artist's new work was too formulaic and lacked innovation.
Many TV sitcoms follow a formulaic approach to jokes and plots.
The movie's formulaic plot failed to surprise seasoned viewers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - created directly and not a copy; inventive
  • antonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to what is generally done or believed
