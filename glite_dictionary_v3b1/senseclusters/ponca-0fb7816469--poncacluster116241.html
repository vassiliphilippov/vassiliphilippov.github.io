Ponca

noun
Native American language of the Ponca people
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋ.kə / (BrE)
the Dhegiha dialect of the Siouan language family spoken by the Ponca people, a Native American group originally from the Midwest United States

Examples

Speak Ponca fluently
He learned Ponca to connect more deeply with his heritage.
Linguists have worked to preserve the traditional stories told in Ponca.
Very few people today are fluent speakers of Ponca.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Omaha - the related Dhegiha Siouan dialect spoken by the Omaha people
