manifold

verb
make multiple copies (especially of documents)
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.əˌfoʊld /; / ˈmænɪˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈmæn.ɪ.fəʊld /; / ˈmænɪfəʊld / (BrE)
to make multiple copies of something, especially a document, using carbon paper or a similar method

Examples

To manifold a letter
Before photocopiers existed, offices often manifolded business correspondence to distribute to several departments.
In the early 20th century, secretaries would manifold important letters using carbon paper.
She manifolds each invoice to ensure both the customer and accounting receive a copy.
She spent the afternoon manifolding the reports with an old carbon-copy machine.
Before digital printers, offices often relied on manifolding to create several copies at once.
The secretary was skilled at manifolding important forms for distribution.
