molality

noun
concentration: moles per kilogram of solvent
7.5 7.5
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈlæ.lɪ.tɪ/ /; / /moʊ.ˈlæ.lɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /mə.ˈlæ.lɪ.tɪ/ /; / /mə.ˈlæ.lə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the concentration of a solution expressed as the number of moles of solute per kilogram of solvent

Examples

The molality of solution
The molality of the solution was calculated for accurate measurement of the reaction.
To prepare a solution with a specific molality, you must weigh both the solute and the solvent carefully.
Unlike molarity, molality does not vary with temperature because it depends on mass, not volume.
