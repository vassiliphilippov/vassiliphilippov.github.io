Albigenses

proper noun
medieval Christian sect in southern France
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.bəˈdʒɛn.siz / (AmE), / ˌæl.bɪˈdʒen.siːz / (BrE)
members of a Christian religious sect in southern France during the 12th and 13th centuries, known for beliefs considered heretical by the Catholic Church.

Examples

The Albigenses sect
Many medieval texts reference the Albigenses as a threat to the Catholic orthodoxy of the time.
Scholars continue to debate the origins of the beliefs held by the Albigenses.
The Albigenses were considered heretics and faced severe persecution during the Albigensian Crusade.
