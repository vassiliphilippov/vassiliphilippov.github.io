diffusionism

noun
anthropological theory
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfjuːʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒənɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that cultural traits, ideas, or technologies spread from one society to others through contact or exchange

Examples

In the early 20th century, diffusionism was a popular explanation for similarities between distant cultures.

Related Terms
