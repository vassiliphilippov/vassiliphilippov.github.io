necrological

adjective
relating to death
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛkrəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnɛkrəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to death, obituaries, or the study and recording of deaths

Examples

necrological report
The historian gave a necrological account of the burial customs of ancient civilizations.
A necrological study revealed much about the society's attitudes toward death.
The ceremony included several necrological traditions passed down through generations.
The newspaper's necrological section was updated every Sunday with recent obituaries.
He specialized in writing necrological essays about notable figures who had passed away.
The annual report included a necrological list of members who died that year.
The hospital published a necrological summary detailing the main causes of death last year.
Researchers used necrological data to track changes in mortality rates over several decades.
A necrological analysis can reveal important trends in public health and disease outbreaks.

  • synonym: mortuary - relating to burial or tombs

  • antonym: vital - relating to life or living things
