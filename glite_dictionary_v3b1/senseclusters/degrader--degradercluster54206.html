degrader

noun
biotech agent that degrades proteins
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡreɪdər / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdə / (BrE)
(in biotechnology and pharmacology) a chemical or biological agent that promotes the breakdown or destruction of a specific target molecule, especially a protein, often by recruiting cellular degradation machinery.

Examples

Protein degrader
Researchers designed a degrader to selectively destroy the harmful protein in disease cells.
Clinical trials showed that the novel degrader reduced toxic protein levels without major side effects.
Many modern drug discovery teams focus on creating degraders capable of targeting previously 'undruggable' proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: PROTAC - a proteolysis‑targeting chimera — a designed molecule that induces degradation of a specific protein by linking it to cellular degradation machinery.
  • synonym: degradant - a substance that causes degradation of another substance (general term).
