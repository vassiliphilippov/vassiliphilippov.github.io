acoustic delay line

noun
device for delaying signals via sound waves
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (BrE)
a device that delays an electrical signal by converting it into sound waves, which travel through a medium before being converted back to electrical form

Examples

An acoustic delay line device
Early televisions sometimes incorporated an acoustic delay line for processing sound.
Researchers tested the performance of a new material for use in acoustic delay line components.
The engineer used an acoustic delay line to synchronize the signals in the circuit.
