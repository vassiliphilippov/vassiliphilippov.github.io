legislature

noun
law-making body

A group of people with the power to make, change, or repeal laws in a country or region.

Examples

The legislature passes laws
A two-thirds majority of the legislature is required to change the constitution.
Elections for the national legislature will be held next spring.
The new healthcare bill was approved by the state legislature after weeks of debate.

Synonyms

  • legislative body
    4.5 0.84/m
    legislative body is the general official group that makes, changes, and repeals laws in a political area, while legislature is the same law-making institution.
  • legislative assembly
    5.4 0.10/m
    legislative assembly is an elected group of representatives who make and change laws locally, while legislature is the general law-making body.
  • parliament
    3.5 25/m
    parliament is the main or supreme law-making chamber in some countries, while legislature is the general term for any law-making group.
  • legislative council
    6.8 0.09/m
    legislative council often refers to a specific law-making chamber in some regions, while legislature names the whole law-making institution.
  • general assembly
    4.9 0.42/m
    general assembly is a representative meeting or elected law-making body in governments and organizations, while legislature is the country's law-making group.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congress - the national legislative body of the United States
  • synonym: parliament - an assembly of representatives that makes laws, especially in the UK and Commonwealth
