continuancy

noun
ongoing existence
8.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪnjuənsi / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjuənsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of continuing without interruption; persistence or ongoing existence over time

Examples

Continuancy of tradition
The continuancy of the tradition relies on each generation passing it down.
Without regular maintenance, the continuancy of the system cannot be guaranteed.
Her continuancy in the role has brought stability to the organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continuity - the unbroken and consistent existence of something over time
  • antonym: interruption - a break or pause in continuity or progress
  • synonym: persistence - the quality of continuing steadily despite problems or difficulties
