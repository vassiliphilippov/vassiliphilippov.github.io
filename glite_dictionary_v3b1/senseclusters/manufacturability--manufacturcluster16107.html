manufacturability

noun
ease of manufacturing
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmæn.jəˌfæk.tʃɚ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˌfæk.tʃər.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the degree to which a product or design can be easily and efficiently manufactured

Examples

The manufacturability of product
The manufacturability of the prototype was a key factor in deciding whether to proceed with mass production.
Designers improved the manufacturability of the device by reducing the number of complex parts.
High manufacturability can lower production costs and speed up the time to market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complexity - the state of being complicated or difficult to make
  • synonym: workability - the ease with which something can be worked or shaped
