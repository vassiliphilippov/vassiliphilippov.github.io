glaucous

adjective
having a bluish-gray, powdery coating
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɔːkəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːkəs / (BrE)
covered with a pale bluish or grayish, powdery or waxy coating that gives a frosted appearance, especially on the surface of plants or fruits.

Examples

A glaucous plant
Botanists noted the glaucous coating on the plum's surface during their inspection.
Some grapes have a glaucous skin, which helps to protect them from pests and moisture loss.
The leaves of the plant are glaucous, giving them a silvery sheen in sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waxy - having a coating or texture resembling wax
