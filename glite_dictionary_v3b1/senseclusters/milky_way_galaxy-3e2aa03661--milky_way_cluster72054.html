Milky Way Galaxy

proper noun
our galaxy; band of light in the night sky
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlki weɪ ˈɡæləksi / (AmE), / ˈmɪlki weɪ ˈɡaləksi / (BrE)
the galaxy that contains the solar system, appearing as a faint, milky band of light across the night sky and consisting of hundreds of billions of stars

Examples

The Milky Way Galaxy
On a clear night, you can see the Milky Way Galaxy stretching across the sky.
Scientists estimate that the Milky Way Galaxy contains over 100 billion stars.
The solar system is located in a spiral arm of the Milky Way Galaxy.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Andromeda Galaxy - the nearest large galaxy to the Milky Way
  • synonym: galaxy - a massive system of stars, gas, and dust bound together by gravity
