warlordism

noun
rule by military leaders
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrˌlɔrdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlɔːdɪzəm / (BrE)
a situation in which military leaders control regions by force and do not obey the central government

Examples

Warlordism spreads quickly
After the central government collapsed, warlordism spread throughout the country.
The region's history of warlordism made it difficult to establish lasting peace.
International aid organizations struggled to operate in areas affected by warlordism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anarchy - a state of society without government or law
  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: militarism - the belief in maintaining a strong military and using it aggressively
