bourgeois

adjective
relating to property-owning, capitalist class (Marxist sense)
8.8
Pronunciation: / bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː / (BrE)
(in Marxist thought) Belonging to or characteristic of the property-owning, capitalist class, considered as exploiting the working class.

Examples

Bourgeois class struggle
His tastes were seen as distinctly bourgeois, favoring comfort and security over radical change.
In Marxist theory, bourgeois values are often contrasted with those of the proletariat.
The revolutionaries denounced the bourgeois attitudes of the wealthy elite.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proletarian - relating to or characteristic of the working class (Marxist sense)
