protective embankment

noun
steep artificial defensive slope
Pronunciation: / prəˈtɛktɪv ɛmˈbæŋkmənt / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv ɪmˈbæŋkmənt / (BrE)
A steep artificial slope built in front of a fortification to provide defense against attack.

Examples

A protective embankment wall
A protective embankment was quickly constructed to strengthen the old fortress.
Archaeologists uncovered the remains of a medieval protective embankment near the site.
The soldiers took cover behind the protective embankment during the assault.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parapet - a low protective wall
  • synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or bank of earth
