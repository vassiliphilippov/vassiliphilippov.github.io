minefield

noun
area with buried explosive mines
5.1 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnˌfild /; / ˈmaɪnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnfiːld /; / ˈmaɪn.fiːld / (BrE)
an area of land or sea where explosive mines have been placed, making it dangerous to enter or cross

Examples

A minefield area
Humanitarian groups work to clear old minefields left over from the war.
The soldiers had to carefully cross the minefield to reach their objective.

Many innocent people have been injured while walking in a minefield left from past conflicts.
The soldiers advanced cautiously through the minefield to avoid triggering any hidden explosives.
