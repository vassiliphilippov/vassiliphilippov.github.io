margrave

noun
medieval German frontier governor
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrˌɡreɪv /; / ˈmɑrˌɡreɪv / (AmE), / ˈmɑːɡreɪv / (BrE)
a German nobleman of high rank, often a military governor of a border province (historical title, above a count and similar to a marquess)

Examples

A margrave ruled
Each margrave ruled his territory with considerable independence from the central authority.
In the twelfth century, the power of the margrave was both military and administrative.
The emperor appointed a margrave to defend the eastern frontier of the realm.
After years of service, he was granted the title of margrave by the emperor.
The castle once belonged to a wealthy margrave who protected the frontier.
The powerful margrave ruled over the territory along the empire's eastern border.
