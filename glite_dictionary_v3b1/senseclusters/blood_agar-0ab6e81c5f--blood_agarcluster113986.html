blood agar

noun
agar medium with blood for culturing bacteria
9.9 4.6
Pronunciation: /blʌd ˈeɪ.gər/ /; / /blʌd ˈæ.gər/ (AmE), /blʌd ˈeɪ.gɑː/ /; / /blʌd ˈeɪ.gə/ (BrE)
a culture medium made by adding whole blood to nutrient agar, used to grow and identify bacteria

Examples

Laboratory technicians often use blood agar to grow bacterial cultures from patient samples.
