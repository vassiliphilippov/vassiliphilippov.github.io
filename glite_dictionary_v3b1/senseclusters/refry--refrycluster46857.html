refry

verb
to rework old material
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfraɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfraɪ / (BrE)
to repeat or rework old material and present it again in a slightly changed form, often lacking originality

Examples

To refry old ideas
The director chose to refry the same storylines from his earlier films.
Critics complained that the new album simply refried the band's previous hits.
Instead of creating something new, the article just refried old arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: innovate - to introduce new ideas or methods
  • synonym: rehash - to reuse old ideas or material with little change
