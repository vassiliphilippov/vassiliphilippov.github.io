Woodrow Wilson

proper noun
28th U.S. President (1913–1921)
4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwʊ.droʊ ˈwɪl.sən/ /; / /ˈwʊd.roʊ ˈwɪl.sən/ (AmE), /ˈwʊ.drəʊ ˈwɪl.sən/ /; / /ˈwʊd.rəʊ ˈwɪl.sən/ (BrE)
(1856–1924) The 28th President of the United States, known for leading the country during World War I and for establishing the League of Nations.

Examples

Woodrow Wilson presidency
Woodrow Wilson was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1919 for his efforts to establish the League of Nations.
During Woodrow Wilson's presidency, the United States entered World War I in 1917.
Many historians debate the effectiveness of Woodrow Wilson's Fourteen Points after the war.
