materialize

verb
become real; happen in reality
1.8
Pronunciation: / məˈtɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become real or actual; to happen or take place as planned or hoped for

Examples

Plans materialize
After years of hard work, his plans finally materialized.
Her fears materialized when the rumors turned out to be true.
The solution did not materialize as quickly as we had hoped.
After months of planning, their business idea finally materialised.
The opportunity he was hoping for never materialised.
When the mist cleared, a mysterious figure seemed to materialise in the garden.
