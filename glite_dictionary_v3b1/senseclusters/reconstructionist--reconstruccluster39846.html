reconstructionist

noun
historical scholar
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a scholar who reconstructs earlier forms of languages, texts, or cultural systems using comparative or forensic methods

Examples

Language reconstructionist
The reconstructionist presented new theories about the origins of the ancient language.
As a reconstructionist, she used comparative methods to infer a Proto-language's vocabulary.
Many discoveries in linguistics are made by a dedicated reconstructionist working with old manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaeologist - a person who studies human history through excavation and analysis of artifacts
  • synonym: historian - a person who studies or writes about history
