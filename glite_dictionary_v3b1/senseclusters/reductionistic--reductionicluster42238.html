reductionistic

adjective
simplifying to parts
Pronunciation: / rɪˌdʌkʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / rɪˌdʌkʃəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
describing an approach that explains complex systems by reducing them to their simplest parts or elements

Examples

A reductionistic approach
The scientist was criticized for her reductionistic explanation of human behavior.
A reductionistic view may overlook important interactions within the system.
His reductionistic approach helped clarify the basic components of the problem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holistic - considering the whole system rather than just its parts
  • synonym: simplistic - treating complex issues as if they are much simpler than they really are
