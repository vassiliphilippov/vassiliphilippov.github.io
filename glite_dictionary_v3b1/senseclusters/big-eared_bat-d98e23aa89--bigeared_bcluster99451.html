big-eared bat

noun
a bat with exceptionally large ears
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪɡ ˌɪrd bæt / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡ ˌɪəd bæt / (BrE)
a large Old World bat notable for its very large ears, belonging to several species within the group

Examples

A big-eared bat colony
Researchers found a colony of big-eared bats roosting in the cave's upper chambers.
The big-eared bat can use its enormous ears to detect the faintest sounds of insect prey.
Unlike other species, the big-eared bat relies heavily on its acute sense of hearing while hunting.
