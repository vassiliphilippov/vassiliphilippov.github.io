pleb

noun
an ordinary or unrefined person
9.5
Pronunciation: / plɛb / (AmE), / plɛb / (BrE)
(informal, often derogatory) an ordinary person, especially one regarded as unrefined or of lower social status.

Examples

An ordinary pleb
In the heated debate, she dismissed her opponent as a mere pleb with no real influence.
Some students used the term pleb to mock their classmates for not knowing luxury brands.
They viewed him as just another pleb who didn't belong in their exclusive club.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aristocrat - a member of the upper or noble class
  • synonym: plebeian - an ordinary person of the lower social classes
