cognizant

adjective
having knowledge or awareness
7.5 1.80/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀê…°n…™z…ônt /; / Ààk…ëÀê…°.n…™.z…ônt / (AmE), / Ààk…í…°n…™z…ônt /; / Ààk…í…°.n…™.z…ônt / (BrE)
having knowledge or awareness of something; conscious or informed about a particular fact or situation

Examples

To be cognizant of facts
Judges must be cognizant of their legal and ethical responsibilities.
She is fully cognizant of the possible consequences of her actions.
The company must remain cognizant of environmental regulations when planning new projects.
Doctors should be cognisant of recent advances in medical technology.
She was fully cognisant of the impact her words could have.
The management must be cognisant of the risks involved in the new project.

Synonyms

  • aware
    2.7 112/m
    aware is used for knowing a fact or situation in general, while cognizant sounds more formal and deliberate.
  • conscious
    3.5 8.29/m
    conscious means noticing or feeling something in the moment, while cognizant is formal awareness of a fact or issue.
  • mindful
    4.0 4.64/m
    mindful describes paying careful attention to something, while cognizant means having knowledge or awareness of a situation.
  • informed
    3.7 0.00/m
    informed means having specific facts or news from a reliable source, while cognizant means broadly aware of those matters.
