scoria

noun
waste layer on molten metals
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsk…îri…ô / (AmE), / Ààsk…îÀêri…ô / (BrE)
A layer of slag or waste material formed by oxidation on the surface of molten metals.

Examples

Metal scoria layer
After the metal was refined, the scoria was skimmed from the surface of the molten iron.
The black, glassy scoria cooled quickly after being separated from the metal.
Workers removed the scoria that had formed during the smelting process.

Synonyms

  • slag
    9.5 0.83/m
    slag is a hard stony waste from smelting that forms a crust, while scoria is the oxidized surface waste on molten metal.
  • dross
    9.6 0.35/m
    dross is loose scum and impurities that rise during melting, while scoria is the solid oxidized layer on molten metal.
