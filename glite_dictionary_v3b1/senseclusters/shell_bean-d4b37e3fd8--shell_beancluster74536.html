shell bean

noun
bean grown for its edible seeds
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl biːn / (AmE), / ʃɛl biːn / (BrE)
A bean plant cultivated mainly for its edible seeds rather than the pod, commonly harvested when the seeds are mature but before they dry.

Examples

Shell bean plant
After removing the pods, the shell beans can be simmered in soups or stews.
Fresh shell beans are available at the farmers' market in late summer.
She prefers to cook shell beans because their flavor is richer than that of snap beans.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dry bean - a bean harvested after the seeds are fully mature and dry
  • antonym: snap bean - a bean cultivated for eating the pod and seeds before maturity
