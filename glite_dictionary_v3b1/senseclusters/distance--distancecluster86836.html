distance

verb
to emotionally or socially withdraw
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
to make oneself less involved or emotionally detached from someone or something; to keep apart or withdraw.

Examples

To distance oneself
After the scandal, she tried to distance herself from her former associates.
He found it necessary to distance himself from the heated argument.
It's important to distance your emotions when making business decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detach - to separate oneself from something emotionally
  • antonym: involve - to include as a necessary part or result; to engage fully
