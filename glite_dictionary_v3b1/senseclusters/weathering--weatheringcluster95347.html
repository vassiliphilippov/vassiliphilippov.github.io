weathering

noun
breaking down of rocks
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the natural process by which rocks and other materials are broken down by wind, water, or living things

Examples

Rock weathering process
Over thousands of years, weathering can turn solid rock into soil.
The statue showed signs of weathering after many years exposed to the elements.
Plants and tree roots contribute to the weathering of rocks in forests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deposition - the process by which material is laid down or settles after being transported
  • synonym: erosion - the gradual removal of soil or rock by wind, water, or ice
