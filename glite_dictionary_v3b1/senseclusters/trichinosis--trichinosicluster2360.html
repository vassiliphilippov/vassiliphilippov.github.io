trichinosis

noun
disease from eating infected undercooked meat
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɪkɪˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by ingestion of undercooked meat, especially pork, that contains trichina larvae, which migrate from the intestines to the muscles and encyst.

Examples

Trichinosis infection
Proper cooking of pork can help prevent infections such as trichinosis.
Symptoms of trichinosis include muscle pain, fever, and swelling around the eyes.
The outbreak of trichinosis was traced to undercooked pork served at a local restaurant.
