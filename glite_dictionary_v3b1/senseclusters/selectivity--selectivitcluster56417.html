selectivity

noun
the quality of being selective
1.0
Pronunciation: / səˌlɛkˈtɪvɪti / (AmE), / sɪˌlɛkˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being selective; the degree to which someone or something carefully chooses from among options.

Examples

High selectivity
Her selectivity when making friends ensures she surrounds herself with positive influences.
The new drug's selectivity allows it to target cancer cells while sparing healthy ones.
Universities are known for their selectivity in choosing incoming students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indiscriminateness - lack of selectivity; not being choosy
  • synonym: selectiveness - the state of being selective; similar meaning to 'selectivity'
