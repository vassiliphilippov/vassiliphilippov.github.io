discolored

adjective
changed in color
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌləd / (BrE)
having lost or changed color, often becoming less attractive or stained

Examples

Discolored fabric
The white shirt became discolored after being washed with dark clothes.
Over the years, the pages of the old book grew discolored and brittle.
She noticed that the bathroom tiles were discolored from constant exposure to moisture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clean - free from marks or stains
  • synonym: faded - having lost brightness or color over time
  • synonym: stained - marked or colored in a way that is hard to remove
