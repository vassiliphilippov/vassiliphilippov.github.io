highfalutin

adjective
pretentiously grand or pompous
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪfəˈluːtən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪfəˈluːtɪn / (BrE)
(of language, behavior, or people) pretending to be important, sophisticated, or superior; pompous or pretentious.

Examples

A highfalutin attitude
His highfalutin attitude made it hard for anyone to take him seriously.
She avoided the highfalutin restaurants and preferred simple, cozy places.
The professor's highfalutin language confused most of the students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pompous - having or showing an exaggerated sense of self-importance
  • synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit
  • antonym: unpretentious - not attempting to impress others with an appearance of greater importance, talent, or culture than is actually possessed
