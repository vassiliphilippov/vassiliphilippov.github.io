leper lily

noun
Eurasian ornamental plant with checkered bell-shaped flowers
4.9 8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈle.pər ˈlɪ.liː/ (AmE), /ˈle.pə ˈlɪ.liː/ (BrE)
A Eurasian ornamental plant (Fritillaria meleagris) with nodding, bell-shaped flowers, often checkered with purple or maroon, resembling the bells once carried by lepers in medieval times.

Examples

The leper lily flower
Gardeners often choose the leper lily for shady spots because of its unique, nodding flowers.
The leper lily blooms in early spring, adding a touch of elegance to the garden with its checkered petals.
The medieval legend says the leper lily was named for its resemblance to the bells carried by lepers.
