subtend

verb
be opposite to and form an angle (geometry)
Pronunciation: / səbˈtɛnd / (AmE), / səbˈtɛnd / (BrE)
(of a line, arc, or side) to be opposite to and delimit an angle at a particular point, especially in geometry.

Examples

An arc subtends angle
An arc that subtends an angle of 120 degrees is one third of the circle.
In a circle, the diameter subtends a right angle at any point on the circumference.
The chord subtends an angle of 60 degrees at the center of the circle.
