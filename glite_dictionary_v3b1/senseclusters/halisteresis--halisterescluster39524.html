halisteresis

noun
loss of bone minerals
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhælɪstəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhælɪstəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
the loss or removal of mineral salts, especially calcium, from bone tissue

Examples

Halisteresis in bone
Osteoporosis can lead to halisteresis in elderly patients.
The doctor explained that halisteresis weakens bones and increases the risk of fractures.
Researchers are studying how diet affects halisteresis in postmenopausal women.

Related Terms
