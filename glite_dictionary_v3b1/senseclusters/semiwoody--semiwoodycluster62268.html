semiwoody

noun
partly woody plant
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈwʊdi / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈwʊdi / (BrE)
a plant, especially a small perennial or subshrub, that has stems which are partly woody and partly herbaceous

Examples

A semiwoody plant
The semiwoody plants in the border survived the winter and sprouted new leaves in spring.
Gardeners often choose semiwoody species for their ability to thrive in mixed flower beds.
This semiwoody perennial produces colorful blooms and requires little maintenance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: herbaceous - a plant with non-woody stems
  • synonym: subshrub - a low-growing plant with partly woody stems
  • degree higher: woody - a plant with fully woody stems
