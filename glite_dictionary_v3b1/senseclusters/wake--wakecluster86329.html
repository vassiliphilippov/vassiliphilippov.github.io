wake

verb
arouse feelings or passions
4.5 3.9
Pronunciation: /weɪk/ (AmE), /weɪk/ (BrE)
to make someone aware of something or to arouse strong feelings or emotions

Examples

To wake strong feelings
Her heartfelt speech woke deep emotions in the audience.
Revisiting the old house woke memories he thought were gone forever.
The tragic news woke a sense of compassion throughout the community.
Her experience abroad woke her to the realities of poverty.
His letter woke the community to the need for better education.
The documentary woke many viewers to the urgent problem of climate change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - to warn or make someone aware of a dangerous or important situation
  • synonym: arouse - to make someone have a particular feeling or reaction
  • antonym: calm - to make someone quiet or less excited or angry
  • synonym: excite - to cause strong feelings of enthusiasm or eagerness
