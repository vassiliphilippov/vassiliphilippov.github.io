foretaste

noun
early sign or sample of something to come
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌteɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌteɪst / (BrE)
a slight or preliminary experience or indication of something to come; an early awareness or sample of what will happen or be experienced later

Examples

A foretaste of success
Her warm smile gave him a foretaste of the hospitality he could expect during his stay.
The concert was just a foretaste of the amazing performances to come this season.
The trailer offered a thrilling foretaste of the movie's action-packed storyline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticipation - the act of looking forward to something that will happen
  • synonym: preview - a viewing or experience of something before it officially begins
  • antonym: surprise - something unexpected occurring suddenly
