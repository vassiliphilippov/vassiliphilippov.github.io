person-to-person

adjective, adverb
directly between two people
Pronunciation: / ˌpɝːsən tə pɝːsən /; / ˌpɝːsən tə ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsən tə pɜːsən /; / ˌpɜːsən tə ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
involving direct communication or contact between two people, without intermediaries (face to face)

Examples

A person-to-person talk
He prefers to negotiate person-to-person when making important decisions.
The manager wanted to resolve the conflict person-to-person rather than through email.
They settled their disagreement person-to-person instead of in a group meeting.
A person-to-person meeting can resolve misunderstandings faster than emails or calls.
Many prefer person-to-person counseling for sensitive issues rather than online support.
The bank offers person-to-person payment services that let you send money directly to friends.

  • synonym: one-on-one - between two people, especially in a direct encounter
