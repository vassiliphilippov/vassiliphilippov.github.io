Synonyms

    inconsequential is a formal word for something without real effect, while unimportant is a more general everyday term.
  • insignificant
    4.1 7/m
    insignificant is used for things too small to matter, while unimportant is a broader, everyday way to say the same.
  • trivial
    3.9 9/m
    trivial describes things of little value or interest, while unimportant simply means not worth attention in general.
  • negligible
    5.6 4/m
    negligible means so small or rare it hardly matters, while unimportant covers many things that lack significance.
  • immaterial
    7.2 1/m
