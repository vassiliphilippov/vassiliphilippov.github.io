key

noun
something crucial for achieving or explaining
Pronunciation: / kiː /; / ki / (AmE), / kiː / (BrE)
something that explains, provides an answer, or is crucial for understanding or solving something (such as an answer key, map key, or cryptographic key)

Examples

The key to success
Communication is often the key to a successful relationship.
Hard work is the key to passing your exams.
The key to understanding this theory lies in its basic principles.
He used the key to check his responses before submitting the test.
The teacher distributed the key after everyone had completed the quiz.
Without the key, it was difficult to know which answers were correct.
Always refer to the key to understand what the different colors represent on the map.
The key shows that this symbol means 'hospital' on the chart.
Without the key, it's difficult to interpret the technical diagram accurately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: answer key - a list of the correct answers to a test or exercise
  • synonym: encryption key - a value used to transform plaintext into ciphertext or vice versa; effectively the same concept as a cryptographic key.
  • synonym: legend - explanatory list for symbols on a map or diagram
  • antonym: obstacle - something that prevents progress or achievement
  • synonym: password - a secret word or phrase used for authentication; sometimes used like a key to access protected data, though technically different from cryptographic keys.
  • synonym: solution - an answer to a problem or puzzle
