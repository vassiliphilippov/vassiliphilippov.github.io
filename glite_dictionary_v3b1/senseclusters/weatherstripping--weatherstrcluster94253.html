weatherstripping

noun
material sealing doors or windows
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛðərˌstrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəˌstrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A narrow strip of material used to seal the joint of a door or window to prevent air, water, and dust from entering a building.

Examples

Door weatherstripping
Check the weatherstripping on your windows every year to maintain good insulation.
Installing new weatherstripping helped lower our energy bills by reducing drafts.
The weatherstripping around the front door needs to be replaced before winter.
