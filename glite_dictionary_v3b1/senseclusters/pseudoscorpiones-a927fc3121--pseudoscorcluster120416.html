Pseudoscorpiones

noun
order of false scorpions (arachnids)
Pronunciation: / suːdoʊˌskɔːrˈpiːoʊniːz / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊˌskɔːˈpɪəʊniːz / (BrE)
An order of small arachnids, commonly known as false scorpions, resembling scorpions but lacking a stinger.

Examples

Small Pseudoscorpiones
Scientists discovered several Pseudoscorpiones living under the bark of a fallen tree.
Some Pseudoscorpiones benefit humans by preying on pests such as mites and small insects.
Unlike true scorpions, Pseudoscorpiones have no tail or stinger.

Related Terms
