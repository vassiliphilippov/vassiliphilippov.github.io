voluptas

proper noun
Roman goddess of pleasure
Pronunciation: / voʊˈlʌptəs / (AmE), / vəˈlʌptəs / (BrE)
in Roman mythology, the goddess who personifies pleasure and sensual delight, said to be the daughter of Cupid and Psyche

Examples

Goddess Voluptas
The ancient Romans worshipped Voluptas as the embodiment of pleasure and delight.
A beautiful painting in the gallery depicts Voluptas surrounded by symbols of joy and happiness.
According to myth, Voluptas was the beloved daughter of Cupid and Psyche.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Eros - Greek god of love and desire
  • synonym: Hedone - Greek goddess of pleasure, equivalent to Voluptas
