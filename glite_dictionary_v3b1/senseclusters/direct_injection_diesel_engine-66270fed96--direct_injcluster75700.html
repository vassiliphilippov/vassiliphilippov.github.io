direct injection diesel engine

noun
diesel engine with direct fuel injection
4.5 5.2
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən ˈdiːzəl ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən ˈdiːzəl ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A diesel engine in which fuel is injected directly into the combustion chamber, typically with injectors mounted at the top, allowing for more efficient combustion.

Examples

A direct injection diesel engine
Modern trucks increasingly use the direct injection diesel engine for better performance and lower emissions.
Routine maintenance is crucial for a direct injection diesel engine to keep it running smoothly.
The direct injection diesel engine offers superior fuel efficiency compared to older designs.
