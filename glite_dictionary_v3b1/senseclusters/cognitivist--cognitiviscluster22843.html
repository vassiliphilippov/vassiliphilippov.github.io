cognitivist

adjective
mental process-based
8.4
Pronunciation: /kɑːg.ˈnɪ.tɪ.vɪst/ /; / /ˈkɑːg.nɪ.tɪ.vɪst/ (AmE), /kɒg.ˈnɪ.tɪ.vɪst/ /; / /ˈkɒg.nɪ.tɪ.vɪst/ (BrE)
relating to the theory that mental processes such as thinking and understanding are central to learning and behavior

Examples

Cognitivist approach
The cognitivist perspective emphasizes the importance of mental activities in understanding how people learn.
Many modern teaching methods are based on cognitivist theories of learning and memory.
A cognitivist approach to language focuses on how the mind processes and organizes information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behaviorist - relating to the theory that behavior is learned through interactions with the environment
  • synonym: constructivist - relating to the theory that knowledge is constructed by learners through experience
