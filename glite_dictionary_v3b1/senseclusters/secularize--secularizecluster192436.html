secularize

verb
transfer from religious to civil control
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkjələrˌaɪz /; / ˈsɛkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊləraɪz /; / sɪˈkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to make something non-religious or remove it from religious ownership, control, or influence

Examples

To secularize land
Many monasteries were secularized in the eighteenth century, transferring their land to the state.
The government decided to secularize several church-owned schools during the reform.
The movement aimed to secularize health care, taking hospitals out of religious control.
Many historic churches have been secularized and turned into museums.
Reforms in the country aimed to secularize its laws and institutions.
The new government set out to secularize the nation's education system.
He argued that modern art tends to secularise themes that were once deeply spiritual.
Over the years, many traditional holidays have become more secularised in their celebration.
The government decided to secularise the education system, removing religious instruction from schools.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nationalize - transfer from private or other ownership to state control
  • antonym: sanctify - make holy or sacred
  • synonym: secularize - make something secular; American spelling
