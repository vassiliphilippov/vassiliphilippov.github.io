bowerbird

noun
Australian bird known for decorative courtship bowers
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊ.ɚ.bɝd / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ.ə.bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird native to Australia and nearby regions, known for the male's habit of building and decorating elaborate structures, called bowers, to attract a mate

Examples

The bowerbird builds bowers
The male bowerbird constructs intricate bowers from twigs and decorates them with colorful objects.
Unlike other birds, a bowerbird impresses potential mates by building elaborate structures.
We watched a documentary about the remarkable courtship rituals of the bowerbird.
