peristalsis

noun
wavelike muscle contractions moving food in the gut
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛr.əˈstæl.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪˈstæl.sɪs / (BrE)
the involuntary wavelike contractions of the muscles in the digestive tract that move food or other substances along

Examples

Muscle peristalsis waves
After you swallow food, peristalsis helps push it down your esophagus into your stomach.
Doctors can observe peristalsis in the intestines during an abdominal exam.
Some medications may affect peristalsis, leading to digestive issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peristaltic - relating to or characterized by peristalsis
