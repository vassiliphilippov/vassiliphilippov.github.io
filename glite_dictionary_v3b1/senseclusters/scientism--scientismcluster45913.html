scientism

noun
excessive trust in science
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.ən.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.ənt.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.ən.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.ənt.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief that science or scientific methods are the only or best way to understand everything, often ignoring other perspectives

Related Terms

  • synonym: empiricism - the theory that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
  • antonym: humanism - a philosophy emphasizing human values and concerns, often contrasted with scientific approaches
  • synonym: positivism - the philosophy that only scientific knowledge is valid
  • synonym: rationalism - the belief that reason is the chief source of knowledge
  • antonym: skepticism - a doubting or questioning attitude, especially towards accepted beliefs
