bunchgrass

noun
type of grass that grows in clumps
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌntʃˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbʌntʃˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
Any of several grass species that grow in dense tufts or clumps rather than spreading to form a continuous mat, especially common in the western United States.

Examples

Dense bunchgrass clumps
Cattle grazing patterns can significantly affect the health of bunchgrass communities.
Restoration efforts included planting native bunchgrass species to prevent soil erosion.
The hills were covered with bunchgrass and wildflowers in early spring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sod - grass that forms a dense mat
