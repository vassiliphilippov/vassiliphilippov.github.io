eth

noun
archaic verb ending
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛθ / (AmE), / ɛθ / (BrE)
an archaic verb ending (-eth) used for the third-person singular present tense in older English, as in 'he maketh'.

Examples

Verb ends with eth
In Shakespeare's plays, you often see verbs ending with eth to indicate the third-person singular.
The word 'runneth' uses the eth ending, which is no longer common in modern English.
Some hymns retain the eth ending for a traditional or poetic effect.
