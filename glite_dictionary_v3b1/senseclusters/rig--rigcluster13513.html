rig

verb
equip with sails or masts (nautical)
Pronunciation: / rɪɡ / (AmE), / rɪɡ / (BrE)
to equip, set up, or prepare something (such as a ship, equipment, or a makeshift solution) for use

Examples

To rig a boat
It takes several hours to rig the yacht for a long journey.
The crew worked quickly to rig the ship before the storm approached.
They hired specialists to rig the new sailing vessel with modern equipment.
She quickly rigged a sling out of cloth for his injured arm.
The mechanic rigged a makeshift lever to open the stuck hatch.
They rigged the alarm system to alert them if anyone entered the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attach - to fasten, join, or connect something
  • antonym: detach - to unfasten or disconnect something
