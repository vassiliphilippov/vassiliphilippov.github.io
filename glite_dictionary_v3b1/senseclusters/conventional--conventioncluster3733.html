conventional

adjective
following accepted customs or standards
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈven.ʃən.əl/ (AmE), /kən.ˈven.ʃən.əl/ (BrE)
following accepted customs, standards, or traditions; typical or usual; not new or original

Examples

Conventional ideas
He wore a conventional suit to the interview, as expected.
Her conventional approach to teaching focused on lectures and textbooks.
They held rather conventional views on marriage and family.
His ideas were far from conventional, challenging the norms of the scientific community.
She wore a conventional dress to the ceremony, opting for a safe and classic style.
The company follows very conventional methods, avoiding risks or new approaches in its operations.
She disliked his conventional approach to problem-solving, which rarely produced creative solutions.
The novel's plot was rather conventional, lacking any surprising twists or original ideas.
Their parents have very conventional views about marriage and career choices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceremonious - marked by formality and strict observance of etiquette
  • synonym: customary - according to the customs or usual practices
  • antonym: innovative - featuring new methods or original ideas
  • synonym: orthodox - conforming to traditional or established beliefs or practices
  • synonym: traditional - having long-established customs or beliefs; not new or modern
  • antonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to accepted customs or standards
  • antonym: unorthodox - not conforming to established customs or beliefs
