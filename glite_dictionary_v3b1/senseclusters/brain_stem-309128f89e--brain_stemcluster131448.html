brain stem

noun
central trunk of the brain connecting to spinal cord
6.2 3.4
Pronunciation: /breɪn stem/ (AmE), /breɪn stem/ (BrE)
the central trunk of the mammalian brain, consisting of the medulla oblongata, pons, midbrain, and parts of the hypothalamus, which connects the spinal cord with the brain

Examples

The brain stem controls
Damage to the brain stem can affect vital functions like breathing and heart rate.
Doctors monitored her brain stem activity after the accident.
The brain stem acts as a relay center connecting the cerebrum with the spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cerebrum - the principal and most anterior part of the brain, responsible for voluntary activities
  • synonym: spinal cord - the long, thin, tubular bundle of nerves that extends from the brain stem
