rallying

noun
act of gathering in support or action
Pronunciation: / ˈræliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gathering people or resources together for a common purpose or renewed effort, especially to show support or after a setback

Examples

Rallying of support
A sense of rallying swept through the community after the announcement.
The rallying of local residents led to changes in the council’s decision.
Their rallying behind the cause inspired others to join.
After a period of doubt, her rallying inspired the whole group to keep going.
The general's rallying of his troops turned the tide of the battle.
With a sudden rallying of their supporters, the team made a remarkable comeback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assembly - a group gathered together for a common purpose
  • synonym: counterattack - a tactical attack made in response to an enemy's attack
  • antonym: dispersal - the act of scattering or breaking up a group
