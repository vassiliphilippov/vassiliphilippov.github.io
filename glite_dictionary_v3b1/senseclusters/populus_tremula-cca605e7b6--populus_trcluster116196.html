Populus tremula

noun
a European and Asian aspen tree species
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːpjələs trɪˈmjuːlə / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs trɪˈmjuːlə / (BrE)
A species of aspen tree native to Europe and temperate Asia, known for its broad crown and trembling leaves.

Examples

Populus tremula tree
A botanist collected samples from a mature Populus tremula growing in northern Finland.
Forests dominated by Populus tremula provide essential habitats for many birds.
The leaves of the Populus tremula tremble delicately in the wind.
