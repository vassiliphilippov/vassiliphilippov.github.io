desensitizing

adjective
making less sensitive or responsive
Pronunciation: / diˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪ.zɪŋ /; / dɪˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / diˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪ.zɪŋ /; / dɪˈsɛnsɪtaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something less sensitive or responsive to physical or emotional stimuli

Examples

Desensitizing effect
Repeated exposure to violent media has a desensitizing effect on viewers.
Some training programs aim at desensitizing people to their fears in controlled environments.
The doctor used a desensitizing cream to reduce the patient's discomfort during the procedure.
After years of working in the emergency room, he found the constant trauma oddly desensitising.
Repeated exposure to violent media can have a desensitising effect on viewers.
Some therapies are aimed at desensitising patients to their specific fears.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: insensitive - not sensitive; lacking feeling or responsiveness
  • antonym: sensitising - making more sensitive to stimuli
  • antonym: sensitizing - making more sensitive to stimuli
