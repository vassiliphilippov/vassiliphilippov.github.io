checkbox

noun
selectable box on screen
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌbɒks / (BrE)
a small box that you can select or clear to show a choice or option, usually on a computer screen or form

Examples

A checkbox option
Please tick the checkbox if you want to receive our newsletter.
You must check the checkbox to agree to the privacy policy before continuing.
Each item on the list has a checkbox next to it for easy selection.
Please tick the checkbox if you agree to the terms and conditions.
You can select multiple answers by clicking the appropriate checkboxes on the survey.
The form will not submit unless you check the required checkbox at the bottom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radio button - a small circle on a computer screen used to select one option from a set
  • synonym: toggle - a switch or control that can be turned on or off
