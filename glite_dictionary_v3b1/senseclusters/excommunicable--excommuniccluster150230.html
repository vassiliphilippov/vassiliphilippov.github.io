excommunicable

adjective
subject to excommunication
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkskəˈmjunɪkəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪkəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be excommunicated or formally expelled from a group, especially a religious community

Examples

Excommunicable offenses
Certain actions were considered excommunicable offenses by the church in the past.
The council debated whether the behavior was truly excommunicable under their rules.
Not every mistake is excommunicable, but some violations are taken very seriously.
Any member who violates the code of conduct is considered excommunicable by the organization.
The club's rules clearly state which actions are excommunicable offenses.
He was warned that sharing confidential information would make him excommunicable under the new policy.
