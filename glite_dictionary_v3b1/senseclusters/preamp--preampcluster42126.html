preamp

verb
to amplify with preamplifier
7.4
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈæmp/ (AmE), /priː.ˈæmp/ (BrE)
to amplify a weak electrical or audio signal using a preamplifier before further processing or recording

Examples

To preamp a microphone
Audio engineers often preamp microphones to ensure a clean recording signal.
You should preamp the guitar before sending it to the main amplifier for the best sound.
They forgot to preamp the vocals, resulting in a weak track on the album.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attenuate - to reduce the strength of a signal
  • synonym: boost - to increase the strength or level of something
