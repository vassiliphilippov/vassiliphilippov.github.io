obduction

noun
oceanic crust over continent
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑbˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɒbˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the geological process in which oceanic crust is pushed over the edge of a continental plate

Examples

Geological obduction event
The mountain range formed as a result of obduction millions of years ago.
Geologists study obduction to understand how oceanic rocks end up on land.
The presence of certain minerals indicates that obduction occurred in this region's past.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subduction - the process where one tectonic plate moves under another
