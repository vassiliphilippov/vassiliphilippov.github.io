nagual

noun
spiritual animal counterpart
Pronunciation: / nɑːˈwɑːl / (AmE), / nəˈwɑːl / (BrE)
in Mesoamerican tradition, an animal or spirit believed to be the spiritual counterpart or protector of a person

Examples

A nagual protector
According to legend, every person has a nagual that protects them from harm.
The shaman claimed his nagual appeared as a jaguar in his dreams.
Stories about the nagual are still told in many rural Mexican communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: familiar - a spirit, often in animal form, that assists a witch or magician
  • synonym: spirit animal - an animal believed to embody or guide a person's spirit
