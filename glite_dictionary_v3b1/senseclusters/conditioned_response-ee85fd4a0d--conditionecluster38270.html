conditioned response

noun
learned reaction to a stimulus
1.9 3.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd rɪ.ˈspɑːns/ /; / /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd riː.ˈspɑːns/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd rɪ.ˈspɒns/ (BrE)
a learned reaction to a specific stimulus that occurs after repeated association with that stimulus

Examples

A conditioned response
A phobia is often seen as a conditioned response developed after a traumatic event.
Teachers can use praise as a stimulus to create a conditioned response in students' behavior.
The dog’s salivation became a conditioned response to the sound of the bell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conditioned reflex - a learned response triggered by a specific stimulus
  • antonym: unconditioned response - an automatic reaction to a stimulus without prior learning
