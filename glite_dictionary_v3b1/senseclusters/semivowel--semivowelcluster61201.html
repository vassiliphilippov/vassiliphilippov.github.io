semivowel

noun
sound like a vowel but acts as a consonant
4.5 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmiˌvaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌvaʊəl / (BrE)
a speech sound that is articulated like a vowel but functions as a consonant, such as the sounds represented by 'y' in 'yes' and 'w' in 'wet'.

Examples

A semivowel sound
A semivowel can sometimes appear at the start of a word, such as in 'yellow'.
Linguists classify 'w' as a semivowel because of its similarity to the vowel 'u'.
The English sounds 'w' and 'y' are considered semivowels in phonetics.

  • synonym: glide - a speech sound with similar phonetic characteristics
