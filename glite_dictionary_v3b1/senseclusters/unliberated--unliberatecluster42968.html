unliberated

adjective
not spiritually free
9.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɪbəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪbəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
not spiritually or metaphysically freed; still subject to suffering, ignorance, or rebirth, especially in religious or philosophical contexts

Examples

Unliberated soul
According to the teachings, an unliberated soul continues to experience the cycle of birth and death.
The monk explained that most people remain unliberated due to attachment and ignorance.
In some philosophies, meditation is a path for the unliberated to seek freedom from suffering.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enlightened - having achieved spiritual knowledge or freedom
  • antonym: liberated - spiritually or metaphysically freed from suffering or rebirth
