spasticity

noun
abnormal muscle stiffness or spasms
7.5 7.5
Pronunciation: /spæs.ˈtɪ.sɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /spæs.ˈtɪ.sɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
A condition in which muscles are continuously contracted, leading to stiffness and awkward or jerky movements.

Examples

Muscle spasticity condition
Doctors monitor the degree of spasticity to adjust treatment plans for cerebral palsy.
Muscle spasticity can make daily activities more challenging for patients with neurological disorders.
She underwent physiotherapy to help reduce the spasticity in her legs.
