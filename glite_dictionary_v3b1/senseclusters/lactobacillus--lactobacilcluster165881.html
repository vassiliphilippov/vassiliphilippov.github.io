lactobacillus

noun
lactic acid-producing bacterium
Pronunciation: / ˌlæktəbəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / ˌlaktəʊbəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
A Gram-positive rod-shaped bacterium that produces lactic acid, especially common in milk and used in fermentation.

Examples

A lactobacillus bacterium
A dose of lactobacillus can help restore the natural balance of bacteria in the gut.
Scientists studied the role of lactobacillus in the fermentation process of dairy products.
Yogurt contains live cultures of lactobacillus that are beneficial to digestion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacterium - single-celled microorganism
  • degree higher: yogurt - fermented milk product often containing lactobacillus
