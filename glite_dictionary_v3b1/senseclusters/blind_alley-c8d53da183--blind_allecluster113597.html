blind alley

noun
unproductive course of action; dead end
9.5 5.0
Pronunciation: /blaɪnd ˈæ.liː/ (AmE), /blaɪnd ˈæ.liː/ (BrE)
a situation or course of action that leads nowhere or offers no possibility of success; a dead end (figurative)

Examples

After months of investigation, the police realized they were pursuing a blind alley.
Negotiations with the committee turned out to be a blind alley and made no progress.
She warned that arguing further would only lead them into a blind alley.
