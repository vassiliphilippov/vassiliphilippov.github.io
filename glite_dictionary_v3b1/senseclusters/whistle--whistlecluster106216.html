whistle

verb
give a signal by whistling
3.3 1.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪs.əl/ /; / /ˈhwɪs.əl/ (AmE), /ˈwɪs.əl/ /; / /ˈhwɪs.əl/ (BrE)
to make a high-pitched sound by blowing air through your lips or a device, often to signal, call attention, or play a tune

Examples

To whistle a tune
He whistled for the dog to come back.
She whistled loudly to get her friend's attention across the street.
The referee whistled to start the match.
A bullet whistled past his head during the battle.
The kettle began to whistle loudly as the water reached boiling point.
The wind would often whistle through the cracks in the old cabin.
He likes to whistle his favorite songs while working.
She whistled a happy tune as she walked down the street.
The boy whistled for his dog to come back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hum - to make a continuous low sound
  • antonym: shout - to make a loud call or cry
  • synonym: signal - to communicate a message or instruction, especially by gesture or sound
  • synonym: sing - to make a musical or high-pitched sound
