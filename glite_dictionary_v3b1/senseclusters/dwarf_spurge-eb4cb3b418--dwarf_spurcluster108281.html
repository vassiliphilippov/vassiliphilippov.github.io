dwarf spurge

noun
small annual weed of the spurge family
7.7 7.9
Pronunciation: /dwɔːrf spɜːrdʒ/ (AmE), /dwɔːf spɜːdʒ/ (BrE)
A small, erect or spreading annual weed of the spurge family native to Europe and introduced to parts of North America.

Examples

The dwarf spurge plant
Despite its small size, dwarf spurge can quickly spread in disturbed soil.
Gardeners often remove dwarf spurge because it is considered a weed.
The dwarf spurge can be found growing wild in fields across parts of Europe and North America.
