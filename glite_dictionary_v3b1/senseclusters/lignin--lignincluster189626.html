lignin

noun
woody plant polymer
8.9 9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪɡ.nɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡ.nɪn / (BrE)
a complex organic polymer found in the cell walls of plants, making them rigid and woody by binding with cellulose

Examples

Plant lignin polymer
Paper manufacturing often involves removing lignin from wood pulp to make the product whiter.
Researchers study lignin for its potential in producing renewable biofuels.
Trees owe much of their strength to lignin, which binds the cellulose fibers in their cell walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cellulose - a polysaccharide that forms the main constituent of plant cell walls
