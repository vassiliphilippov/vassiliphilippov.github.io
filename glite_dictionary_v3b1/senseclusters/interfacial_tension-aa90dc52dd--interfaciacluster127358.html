interfacial tension

noun
surface tension between two liquids
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.t̬ɚˈfeɪ.ʃəl ˈten.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈfeɪ.ʃəl ˈten.ʃən / (BrE)
the force of surface tension at the boundary between two non-miscible liquids

Examples

Interfacial tension force
Measuring interfacial tension is important in the formulation of paints and coatings.
Researchers observed that temperature changes altered the interfacial tension at the boundary of the liquids.
The effectiveness of an emulsifier depends on its ability to reduce interfacial tension between oil and water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surface tension - the elastic tendency of a fluid surface that allows it to acquire the least surface area possible
