buffering

noun
protection from harm
3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌf.ə.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbʌf.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of reducing or absorbing the impact of a shock, stress, or harmful influence to provide protection or relief

Examples

Buffering stress
Community programs provide important buffering against the effects of poverty on children's health.
The thick walls offer buffering from the noise of the busy street outside.
Social support can act as a buffering factor during times of personal crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cushioning - the act of softening the effect of something unpleasant
  • antonym: exposure - the state of being unprotected from harm or risk
