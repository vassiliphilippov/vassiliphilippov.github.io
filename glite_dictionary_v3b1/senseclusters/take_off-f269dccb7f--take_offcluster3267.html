take off

verb
become airborne (of aircraft)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɔf / (AmE), / teɪk ɒf / (BrE)
(of an aircraft or bird) to leave the ground and begin to fly.

Examples

The plane takes off
The helicopter takes off from the hospital roof every morning.
The plane took off two hours late.
We watched the birds take off from the lake at sunrise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: land - (of a plane, bird, or spacecraft) to come down through the air and rest on the ground
