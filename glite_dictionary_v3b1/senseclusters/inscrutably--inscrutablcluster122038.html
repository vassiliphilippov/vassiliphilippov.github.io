inscrutably

adverb
in a mysterious or incomprehensible manner
3.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈskruːtəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈskruːtəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is impossible to understand or interpret; mysteriously or enigmatically.

Examples

Answer inscrutably
He answered the question inscrutably, revealing neither agreement nor disagreement.
She smiled inscrutably, leaving everyone to wonder what she was really thinking.
The judge regarded the witness inscrutably, giving nothing away with his expression.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • synonym: mysteriously - in a way that is difficult or impossible to understand or explain
