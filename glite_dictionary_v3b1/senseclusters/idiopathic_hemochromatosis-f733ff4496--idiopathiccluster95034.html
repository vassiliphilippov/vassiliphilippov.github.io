idiopathic hemochromatosis

noun
genetic disorder of excessive iron accumulation
9.0
Pronunciation: /ɪ.di.ə.ˈpæθ.ɪk hiː.moʊ.krə.ˈtoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ɪ.di.ə.ˈpæθ.ɪk hiː.məʊ.krə.ˈtəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
an inherited disorder in which excessive amounts of iron accumulate in the body, potentially causing organ damage

Examples

Idiopathic hemochromatosis disease
Early detection of idiopathic hemochromatosis can help prevent serious complications such as liver damage.
Genetic testing confirmed his condition as idiopathic hemochromatosis, which required lifelong management.
The patient was diagnosed with idiopathic hemochromatosis after routine blood tests revealed elevated iron levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemochromatosis - a disorder caused by excessive absorption of dietary iron
