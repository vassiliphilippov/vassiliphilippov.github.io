overappraisal

noun
an appraisal that is too high
5.4 7.0
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ə.ˈpreɪ.zəl/ /; / /ə.vər.ə.ˈpreɪ.zəl/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.rə.ˈpreɪ.zəl/ /; / /ə.və.rə.ˈpreɪ.zəl/ (BrE)
An appraisal that assesses something as more valuable or important than it actually is; an excessively high valuation.

Examples

An overappraisal of value
Due to an overappraisal of the artwork, the gallery paid far more than its market value.
Regular checks are needed to avoid overappraisal of company assets during audits.
The bank worried that an overappraisal might result in granting a larger loan than the property is worth.
