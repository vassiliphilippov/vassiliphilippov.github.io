alkaline-earth metal

noun
metal element in Group II of periodic table
7.4 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.kə.laɪn ɜːrθ ˈme.təl/ (AmE), /ˈæl.kə.laɪn ɜːθ ˈme.təl/ (BrE)
any of the metallic chemical elements in Group II of the periodic table, including calcium, strontium, barium, magnesium, and beryllium, which are characterized by being bivalent and forming basic oxides.

Examples

The alkaline-earth metal calcium
Beryllium is the lightest alkaline-earth metal and is used in various aerospace applications.
Calcium is an alkaline-earth metal that is essential for bone health.
Magnesium, another alkaline-earth metal, plays a crucial role in biological processes.
