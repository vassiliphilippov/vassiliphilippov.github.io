mutinously

adverb
rebelliously
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtənəsli / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪnəsli / (BrE)
in a rebellious or disobedient way, especially toward authority

Examples

Look mutinously at
The students stared mutinously at the teacher after being given extra homework.
He crossed his arms mutinously and refused to leave the room.
The workers spoke mutinously about the new company policies during their break.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obediently - in a way that follows orders or rules
  • synonym: rebelliously - in a way that resists authority or control
