Kulturkampf

proper noun
German church-state conflict
Pronunciation: / kʊlˈtuːrˌkɑmpf / (AmE), / kʊlˈtʊəˌkampf / (BrE)
the series of policies and conflicts in 1870s Germany led by Bismarck to limit the Catholic Church's influence

Examples

The German Kulturkampf
The Kulturkampf was a turning point in the relationship between the German state and the Catholic Church.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of the Kulturkampf on German society.
Bismarck's Kulturkampf policies faced strong resistance from Catholic communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: secularization - the process of reducing religious influence in society or government
