diminished

adjective
smaller musical interval by a semitone
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a musical interval) made smaller by a semitone from a perfect or minor interval.

Examples

Diminished fifth interval
A tritone is an example of a diminished fifth interval.
In classical music, a diminished seventh chord creates a strong sense of tension.
The composer used a diminished fourth to add dissonance to the piece.

Related Terms

  • antonym: augmented - (of a musical interval) increased by a semitone
