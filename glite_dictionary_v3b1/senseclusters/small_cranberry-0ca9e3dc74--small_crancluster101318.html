small cranberry

noun
a plant bearing small red cranberries
4.8 4.1
Pronunciation: /smɔːl ˈkræn.bər.iː/ (AmE), /smɔːl ˈkræn.bər.ɪ/ (BrE)
a low, trailing plant of Arctic and cool regions in the Northern Hemisphere, producing small red edible berries

Examples

The small cranberry plant
Compared to its larger relative, the small cranberry has a more tangy flavor and is often used in preserves.
In parts of Alaska, people gather small cranberry to make traditional sauces and desserts.
The small cranberry thrives in chilly peat bogs across northern Europe and North America.
