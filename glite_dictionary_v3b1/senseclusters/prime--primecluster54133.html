prime

noun
the peak period of a person's power or vigor
2.3
Pronunciation: / praɪm / (AmE), / praɪm / (BrE)
the period in a person's or thing's life when they are at their most successful, active, or vigorous

Examples

In his prime
He looks back fondly on the days when he was in his physical prime.
Many athletes reach their prime in their twenties or early thirties.
She was in the prime of her career when she received the award.
He looks back on his youth as the prime of his life.
Many athletes are in their prime during their twenties.
The company expanded rapidly in the prime of its business.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - the period after the prime when vigor or success decreases
  • synonym: heyday - period of greatest success or popularity
