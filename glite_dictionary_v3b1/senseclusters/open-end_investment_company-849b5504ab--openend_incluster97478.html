open-end investment company

noun
company issuing/redeeming investment shares on demand
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊpən ˈɛnd ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˌəʊpən ˈɛnd ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a regulated investment company that issues and redeems shares on demand at a price based on the net asset value of its underlying assets

Examples

An open-end investment company share
She invested her savings in an open-end investment company to benefit from professional management.
The open-end investment company allows investors to purchase and redeem shares at any time.
Unlike a closed-end fund, an open-end investment company continually issues new shares to meet demand.
