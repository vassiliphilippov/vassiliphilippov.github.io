wavery

adjective
wavy or undulating
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈweɪvəri / (BrE)
having a wavy or undulating form, outline, or surface

Examples

Wavery line
The artist painted a wavery line to represent the movement of water.
Her handwriting became wavery as her hand started to tremble.
The distant mountains appeared wavery in the heat of the afternoon sun.

Related Terms

  • antonym: straight - having no curves or bends
  • synonym: wavy - having a series of curves or undulations
