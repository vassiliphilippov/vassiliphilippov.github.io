licentiousness

noun
immoral or dissolute sensual behavior
Pronunciation: / laɪˈsɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / laɪˈsɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
immoral or sexually unrestrained behavior; lack of moral discipline, especially in sexual matters

Examples

Licentiousness and corruption
He was dismissed from his post due to allegations of licentiousness and corruption.
Some critics accused the film of promoting licentiousness among young viewers.
The novelist vividly described the licentiousness of the aristocratic parties in eighteenth-century France.
Critics accused the artist’s work of promoting licentiousness among the youth.
He was dismissed from office because of repeated acts of licentiousness and corruption.
The novel shocked readers with its frank portrayal of licentiousness in the royal court.
