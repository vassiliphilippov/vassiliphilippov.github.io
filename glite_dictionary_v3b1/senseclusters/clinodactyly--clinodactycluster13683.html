clinodactyly

noun
curved fingers or toes (congenital)

Pronunciation: /klɪ.nə.ˈdæk.tɪ.liː/ /; / /klaɪ.nə.ˈdæk.tɪ.liː/ (AmE), /klɪ.nə.ˈdæk.tɪ.liː/ /; / /klaɪ.nə.ˈdæk.tɪ.liː/ (BrE)
A congenital abnormality in which one or more fingers or toes are permanently curved or angled to one side.

Examples

Clinodactyly finger curve
Clinodactyly can be present at birth or develop as the child grows, often requiring no treatment unless it causes functional problems.
He has clinodactyly affecting both of his fifth fingers, but it does not interfere with daily activities.
The doctor diagnosed the newborn with clinodactyly after noticing the unusual curvature of her little finger.
