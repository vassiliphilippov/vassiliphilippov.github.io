ground pine

noun
a low-growing aromatic annual plant
4.3
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd paɪn / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd paɪn / (BrE)
A low-growing annual plant with yellow flowers marked with red, faintly aromatic of pine resin, native to Europe, the British Isles, and North Africa.

Examples

Yellow-flowered ground pine
Botanists found ground pine thriving in the chalky soil of northern England.
In spring, the yellow and red flowers of ground pine brighten the coastal fields.
The ground pine gives off a light, pine-like scent as you brush past it in the meadow.
