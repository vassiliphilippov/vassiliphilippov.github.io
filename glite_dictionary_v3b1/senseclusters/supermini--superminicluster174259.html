supermini

noun
small powerful computer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌmɪni / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌmɪni / (BrE)
a small, high-performance computer from the 1960s–1980s, more powerful than a minicomputer but less than a mainframe

Examples

A supermini computer
In the 1970s the university installed a supermini to handle departmental data processing tasks.
Many research labs relied on a supermini for scientific calculations before personal computers became common.
The company upgraded from a minicomputer to a supermini to improve performance and storage capacity.
