four-act play

noun
a play in four acts
Pronunciation: / fɔr ˈækt pleɪ / (AmE), / fɔː ˈækt pleɪ / (BrE)
A play that is divided into four distinct sections or acts, often used in early twentieth-century theatre.

Examples

A four-act play
Her thesis analyzed the dramatic tension in a four-act play compared to a three-act format.
Many of Chekhov's works are structured as a four-act play with a clear progression of action.
The director chose to stage a classic four-act play for the theatre festival.
