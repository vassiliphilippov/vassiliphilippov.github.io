tough

verb
to endure hardship
Pronunciation: / tʌf / (AmE), / tʌf / (BrE)
to endure or deal with something difficult or unpleasant without giving up

Examples

To tough it out
She toughed it out through the difficult winter.
Despite the setbacks, he toughed his way to the finish line.
They tough through challenges by supporting each other as a team.
He had to tough it out when his job became stressful.
Sometimes you just need to tough through the hard times to succeed.
She managed to tough her way through the challenging training program.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endure - to suffer patiently through something difficult
  • antonym: give up - to stop trying or surrender
  • synonym: persevere - to continue trying despite difficulties
  • antonym: quit - to stop trying or give up
