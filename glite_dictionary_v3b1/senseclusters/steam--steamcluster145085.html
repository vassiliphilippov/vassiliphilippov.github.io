steam

verb
(of liquid) rise or give off vapor
Pronunciation: / stiːm / (AmE), / stiːm / (BrE)
to give off or emit steam or vapor, especially when heated

Examples

The road steams after rain
After the rain, the pavement would steam in the afternoon sun.

The water began to steam as soon as it hit the hot pan.

In the cold morning air, the river appeared to steam as mist rose from its surface.
The hot soup continued to steam long after it was served.
