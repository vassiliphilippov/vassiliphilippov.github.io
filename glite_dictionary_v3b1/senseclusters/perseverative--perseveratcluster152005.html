perseverative

adjective
repetitive behavior
Pronunciation: / pərˈsɛvərəˌtɪv / (AmE), / pəˈsɛvərətɪv /; / pəˈsɪvərətɪv / (BrE)
showing or relating to persistent or repetitive behavior, actions, or thoughts, especially when repeated more than is appropriate

Examples

Perseverative behavior
The psychologist noted the child's perseverative actions during the testing session.
Patients with certain neurological conditions may display perseverative speech or movements.
Her perseverative questioning made it difficult to move on in the conversation.
The child displayed perseverative behavior by repeating the same question over and over.
Her perseverative focus on the details sometimes slowed down the team's progress.
Therapists often work with patients to reduce perseverative thinking patterns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - able to change or adapt easily
  • synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action
  • synonym: repetitive - involving doing the same thing again and again
