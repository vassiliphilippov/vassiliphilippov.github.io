chokepoint

noun
narrow passage restricting flow
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃoʊkˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊk.pɔɪnt /; / ˈtʃəʊkˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a narrow or crowded place where movement or flow is slowed down or blocked

Examples

A chokepoint in traffic
During rush hour, the intersection becomes a chokepoint for city traffic.
Inspectors identified a chokepoint in the assembly line that was causing delays.
The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil shipments.
During the morning commute, the tunnel becomes a major chokepoint for city traffic.
In military strategy, controlling a chokepoint can change the outcome of a campaign.
Supply lines slowed at a chokepoint near the border, delaying deliveries for days.
