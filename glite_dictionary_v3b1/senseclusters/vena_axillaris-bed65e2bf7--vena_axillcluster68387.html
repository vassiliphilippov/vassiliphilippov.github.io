vena axillaris

noun
vein in the armpit region
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌviːnə ækˈsɪlərɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə ækˈsɪlərɪs / (BrE)
The large vein in the armpit area that is formed by the union of the basilic and brachial veins, continuing as the subclavian vein.

Examples

The vena axillaris vein
During surgery, the vena axillaris must be carefully identified to prevent accidental injury.
Obstruction of the vena axillaris can cause swelling of the entire arm.
The catheter was inserted through the vena axillaris for central venous access.
