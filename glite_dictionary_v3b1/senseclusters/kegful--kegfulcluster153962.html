kegful

noun
quantity that fills a keg
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛɡ.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɛɡ.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount or quantity that fills a keg.

Examples

A kegful of cider
After the party, there was barely a kegful of cider left.
The brewery delivered a fresh kegful of beer to the pub every week.
They celebrated by drinking an entire kegful of ale.
