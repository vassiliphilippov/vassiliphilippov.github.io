elastic energy

noun
potential energy stored in a deformed object
2.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæstɪk ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the potential energy stored in an object when it is deformed, such as when a spring is compressed or stretched

Examples

Elastic energy stored
Engineers calculate elastic energy to design safer shock-absorbing materials.
The compressed spring in the toy car contains elastic energy that powers its movement.
When you stretch a rubber band, it stores elastic energy that is released when you let go.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kinetic energy - energy due to motion
  • synonym: potential energy - stored energy due to position or condition
