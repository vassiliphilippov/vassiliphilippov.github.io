round-eyed

adjective
having large, round, wide-open eyes
Pronunciation: / raʊnd aɪd /; / ˈraʊnd.aɪd / (AmE), / raʊnd aɪd /; / ˈraʊnd.aɪd / (BrE)
having large, round, wide-open eyes, often suggesting innocence, surprise, or childlike wonder

Examples

Round-eyed stare
She gave him a round-eyed stare of disbelief.
The child looked up at her with round-eyed wonder.
The kitten's round-eyed gaze made everyone smile.
His round-eyed expression revealed a mixture of awe and disbelief.
She listened round-eyed as her grandfather told stories of his youth.
The children looked round-eyed at the magician’s amazing tricks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: childlike - having qualities of a child, such as innocence or trustfulness
  • synonym: dewy-eyed - naively innocent or trusting, like someone seeing things for the first time
  • synonym: wide-eyed - having wide-open eyes, often expressing innocence or surprise
  • antonym: worldly - experienced and knowledgeable about life, not innocent or naïve
