patriliny

noun
father-based descent
Pronunciation: / pəˈtrɪlɪni / (AmE), / pəˈtrɪlɪni / (BrE)
a system in which family descent and inheritance are traced through the father's or male line

Examples

Patriliny system
In many traditional societies, patriliny determines who inherits land and family titles.
Anthropologists study patriliny to understand patterns of inheritance and kinship.
The shift from patriliny to matriliny changed the way property was passed down in the community.
In societies that practice patriliny, family names and property are passed down through sons.
Anthropologists study how patriliny shapes inheritance and social roles in different cultures.
The tradition of patriliny influenced the way land was distributed among male heirs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: matriliny - a system in which descent and inheritance are traced through the mother's line
  • synonym: patriarchy - a social system in which men hold primary power
  • synonym: patrilineal - relating to descent through the male line
