theatrical

adjective
relating to theater or dramatic style
Pronunciation: / θiˈætrɪkəl / (AmE), / θɪˈætrɪkəl / (BrE)
connected with the theater or behaving in a dramatic, exaggerated way, like an actor on stage

Examples

Theatrical performance
His apology sounded so theatrical that nobody believed he was sincere.
She wore a theatrical costume for the party, complete with a feathered mask.
The director favored theatrical gestures to convey intense emotion on stage.
He adopted a theatrical pose for the photographers, as if he were on stage.
Her speech was theatrical, full of dramatic gestures and pauses.
The president's theatrical response drew both criticism and applause from the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dramatic - related to drama or theatrical performance
  • antonym: subdued - restrained, not showy or theatrical in style
  • antonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way, not obvious or showy
