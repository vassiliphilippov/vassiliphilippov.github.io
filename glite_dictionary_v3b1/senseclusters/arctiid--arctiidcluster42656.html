arctiid

noun
stout-bodied moth of the Arctiidae family
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrk.ti.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈɑːk.tɪ.ɪd / (BrE)
A stout-bodied, broad-winged moth with conspicuously striped or spotted wings; its larvae are typically hairy caterpillars, belonging to the family Arctiidae.

Examples

The arctiid moth
Scientists studied the hairy caterpillars of the arctiid to understand their defense mechanisms.
Several species of arctiids are known for their vibrant patterns and striking appearance.
The garden was visited by an unusual arctiid with bright orange markings on its wings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tiger moth - common name for moths of the Arctiidae family with striking patterns
