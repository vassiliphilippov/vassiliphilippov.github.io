certiorari

noun
a legal writ for court review
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜːrtʃiəˈrɛri / (AmE), / ˌsɜːtɪəˈrɑːraɪ / (BrE)
a writ issued by a higher court ordering a lower court to deliver the record of a case for review

Examples

Granting certiorari order
Granting certiorari is a discretionary decision made by the higher court.
The Supreme Court granted certiorari to review the decision of the appellate court.
The petitioners filed for certiorari after losing their appeal in the lower court.
