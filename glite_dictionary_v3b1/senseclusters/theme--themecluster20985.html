theme

noun
recurrent idea in art or literature
1.8
Pronunciation: / θim / (AmE), / θiːm / (BrE)
the main idea, subject, or message in a piece of writing, art, conversation, or creative work

Examples

A theme in literature
Love and loss are common themes in poetry from this period.
The theme of forgiveness runs throughout the entire novel.
The exhibition explores the theme of identity in modern art.
Most of his poems share a common theme of longing and nostalgia.
The theme of the seminar was the importance of mental health awareness.
Their conversation revolved around the theme of forgiveness and second chances.
Art exhibitions often focus on a specific theme to guide the viewer's experience.
Many of Shakespeare's plays explore the theme of identity.
The central theme of the novel is the struggle between good and evil.
