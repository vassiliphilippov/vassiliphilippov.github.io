Jomon

proper noun
ancient Japanese culture
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒoʊˌmɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊˌmɒn / (BrE)
the prehistoric Japanese culture or period known for its distinctive pottery and early settlements, dating from about 14,000 to 300 BCE

Examples

Jomon period
The Jomon people created some of the oldest known pottery in the world.
Archaeologists study Jomon sites to learn about early Japanese society.
Distinctive cord-marked designs are a hallmark of Jomon pottery.
Archaeologists discovered Jomon pottery fragments at the ancient site.
The Jomon period is famous for its distinctive cord-marked ceramics.
Many tools from the Jomon era show the advanced skills of prehistoric Japanese people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Neolithic - the later part of the Stone Age, when ground or polished stone weapons and implements prevailed
  • antonym: Yayoi - the Japanese culture that followed the Jomon period
