vitrify

verb
become glassy; undergo vitrification
6.5 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪ.trɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /ˈvɪ.trɪ.faɪ/ (BrE)
to become or make something glass or glass-like, usually by heating or fusion

Examples

To vitrify ash
Ash can vitrify when subjected to intense heat during industrial processing.
At high temperatures, certain minerals vitrify and become glass-like in appearance.
The ceramic glaze began to vitrify as the kiln reached its peak temperature.
If the sand is heated to a high enough temperature, it will vitrify and become glass.
Scientists have learned how to vitrify waste to safely contain hazardous materials.
The intense heat from the furnace can vitrify certain types of clay.

Related Terms

  • antonym: devitrify - to lose or remove the glassy state of a substance
  • synonym: glassify - to make into glass
