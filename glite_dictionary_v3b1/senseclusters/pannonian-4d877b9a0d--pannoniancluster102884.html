Pannonian

adjective
of the Pannonian stage
Pronunciation: / pəˈnoʊniən / (AmE), / pəˈnəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the Pannonian stage or its sediments in the late Miocene, especially in geology and stratigraphy

Examples

Pannonian sediments
The Pannonian basin contains important fossil records from the late Miocene period.
Geologists analyzed Pannonian sediments to understand ancient climate patterns in Central Europe.
The Pannonian stage is significant for its unique stratigraphic layers found in Hungary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Miocene - a geological epoch from about 23 to 5 million years ago
