deamidation

noun
removal of amide group
Pronunciation: / diˌæməˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌæmɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction where an amide group is removed or changed in a molecule, especially in proteins

Examples

Protein deamidation
The deamidation of certain proteins can lead to changes in their biological activity.
Researchers study deamidation to understand how it affects the stability of therapeutic drugs.
Aging tissues often show increased levels of deamidation in structural proteins.
The deamidation of proteins can affect their stability and biological activity.
Researchers study deamidation to understand how it influences the aging of therapeutic antibodies.
Enzymatic deamidation may play a role in certain diseases by altering protein function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amidation - the process of introducing an amide group into a molecule
  • synonym: hydrolysis - a chemical reaction involving the breaking of a bond using water
