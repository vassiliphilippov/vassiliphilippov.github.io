tolerationist

adjective
supporting acceptance
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑːləˈreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌtɒləˈreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
favoring or supporting the acceptance of different religious or social beliefs and practices

Examples

Tolerationist approach
The new policy reflects a tolerationist attitude toward cultural diversity in the workplace.
Many historians describe the era as marked by tolerationist reforms in religious practice.
Her tolerationist views made her popular among people from different backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intolerant - unwilling to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own
  • synonym: tolerant - willing to accept beliefs or behaviors different from one's own
