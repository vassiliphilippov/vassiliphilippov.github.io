artery of the labyrinth

noun
artery supplying the inner ear
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.tə.riː əv ðə ˈlæ.brɪnθ/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.tə.ri əv ðə ˈlæ.brɪnθ/ (BrE)
an artery branching from the basilar artery that supplies blood to the structures of the inner ear (labyrinth)

Examples

The artery of the labyrinth
Damage to the artery of the labyrinth can result in sudden hearing loss or vertigo.
During surgery near the brainstem, doctors take care to avoid injuring the artery of the labyrinth.
The artery of the labyrinth is essential for providing oxygenated blood to the cochlea and vestibular apparatus.
