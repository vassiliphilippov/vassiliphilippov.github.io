cordillera

noun
mountain range chain
7.9
Pronunciation: /kɔːr.dɪ.ˈle.rə/ /; / /kɔːr.dɪ.ˈleə.rə/ (AmE), /kɔː.dɪ.ˈle.rə/ /; / /kɔː.dɪ.ˈleə.rə/ (BrE)
a long chain or system of connected mountain ranges, especially in the Americas

Examples

Andes cordillera
The Andes form a massive cordillera stretching along the western edge of South America.
Many rivers begin in the high peaks of the cordillera before flowing to the sea.
The explorers crossed a cordillera to reach the remote valley beyond.
The Andes are the longest cordillera in the world, stretching along the western coast of South America.
A cordillera often contains several parallel mountain ranges separated by valleys.
Many rivers originate in the high peaks of a cordillera before flowing down to the plains.
