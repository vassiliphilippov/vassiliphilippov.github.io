thing-in-itself

noun
philosophical term: object as it exists in itself
8.0
Pronunciation: / θɪŋ ɪn ɪtˈsɛlf / (AmE), / θɪŋ ɪn ɪtˈsɛlf / (BrE)
In philosophy, the true nature of a thing as it exists independently of perception or experience.

Examples

The thing-in-itself concept
In his lectures, the professor explained the difference between appearance and thing-in-itself.
Kant believed that the thing-in-itself remains unknowable to human reason.
Many philosophers have debated whether the thing-in-itself can ever be truly apprehended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: noumenon - an object as it is in itself, independent of perception, especially in Kantian philosophy
