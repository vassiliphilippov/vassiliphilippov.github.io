falter

verb
move hesitantly or unsteadily
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltə / (BrE)
to move unsteadily or hesitantly, as if about to fall or lose strength

Examples

Tall stack falters
As the ground shook, the tall stack of books started to falter and sway.
He began to falter as he crossed the rocky path, nearly losing his balance.
The runner seemed to falter at the halfway mark, but she quickly regained her pace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recover - to regain balance or composure after faltering
  • synonym: stumble - to trip or momentarily lose balance while walking or running
