wood hen

noun
flightless New Zealand bird (weka)
Pronunciation: / wʊd hɛn / (AmE), / wʊd hɛn / (BrE)
a flightless bird native to New Zealand, known for its bold and inquisitive behavior, with short wings each bearing a spur used in fighting

Examples

The wood hen bird
Many locals call the wood hen by its Maori name, 'weka.'
Researchers have noted that the wood hen often steals food from campsites.
The wood hen is easily recognized by its bold behavior and distinctive calls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: weka - flightless rail of New Zealand, also known as wood hen
