carrier

noun
inactive vehicle for radioactive tracer
Pronunciation: / ˈkæriər / (AmE), / ˈkæriə / (BrE)
An inactive substance used to transport a radioactive tracer of the same substance, aiding its recovery after a chemical reaction.

Examples

Radioactive carrier
A carrier is often necessary to facilitate detection of small amounts of radioactive isotopes.
After the experiment, the presence of a carrier made it easier to isolate the tracer.
The chemist added a carrier to help recover the radioactive tracer from the solution.
