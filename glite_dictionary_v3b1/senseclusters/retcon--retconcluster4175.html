retcon

noun
storyline revision
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈrɛtˌkɒn / (BrE)
a change or reinterpretation of previously established facts in a story, often to resolve inconsistencies or update the narrative

Examples

A major retcon
The latest comic introduced a major retcon that changed the hero's origin story.
Fans debated whether the show's retcon improved the plot or made it more confusing.
Writers sometimes use a retcon to fix plot holes from earlier episodes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuity - the consistent maintenance of story details over time
  • synonym: revision - a change or alteration to something already written or established
