release

verb
discharge from cells or fluids
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈliːs/ /; / /rə.ˈliːs/ /; / /riː.ˈliːs/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈliːs/ /; / /rə.ˈliːs/ /; / /riː.ˈliːs/ (BrE)
to let a substance, liquid, gas, or energy escape or be discharged

Examples

To release gas
Certain cells release insulin into the bloodstream to help regulate blood sugar levels.
The thyroid gland releases hormones that influence metabolism throughout the body.
When allergens are detected, the body may release histamines as part of its immune response.
Combustion releases energy in the form of heat and light.
The decaying organic matter released methane into the atmosphere.
When vinegar is mixed with baking soda, it releases carbon dioxide gas.
Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.
Some plants release fragrances at night to attract pollinators.
The factory was fined for releasing toxic chemicals into the river.

  • synonym: emit - to produce and discharge something, especially gas or radiation
  • synonym: secrete - to produce and release a substance, such as a hormone, from a cell or gland
