deviationism

noun
departure from party ideology
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːviˈeɪʒ(ə)nɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the act or practice of departing from an official party doctrine or policy, especially in a communist context

Examples

Charges of deviationism
During the purge, several members were expelled on charges of deviationism.
Historical debates often centered on the dangers of ideological deviationism.
The party leadership accused him of deviationism for supporting reformist ideas.
