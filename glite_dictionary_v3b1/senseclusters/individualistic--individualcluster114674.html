individualistic

adjective
favoring minimal restrictions, esp. in commerce
2.3 5.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.dɪ.vɪ.dʒuː.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.vɪ.dʒuː.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
(of a system, policy, or society) characterized by minimally restricted freedom, especially in economic or commercial matters.

Examples

Individualistic society
Critics argue that an individualistic economic policy can increase inequality.
Some see the American economy as more individualistic than the European model.
The country's individualistic approach to the market has encouraged rapid economic growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collectivist - emphasizing group control over individual freedom
  • synonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimal state intervention in economic affairs
