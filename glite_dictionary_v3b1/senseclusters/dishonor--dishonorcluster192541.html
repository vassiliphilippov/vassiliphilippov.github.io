dishonor

verb
refuse to accept or pay (a cheque, etc.)
5.2
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɑːnɚ /; / dɪsˈɑnər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
(of a bank or other institution) to refuse to pay or accept a cheque, bill, or other negotiable instrument.

Examples

Bank dishonors a cheque
If your account lacks funds, your bank may dishonor your payment.
Several merchants refused to accept cards from companies known to dishonor drafts.
The bank will dishonor any check that is not properly signed.
If your account lacks funds, your payment may be dishonoured by the bank.
Several drafts were dishonoured after the company declared bankruptcy.
The bank decided to dishonour the check due to insufficient funds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to agree to receive or pay a cheque, etc.
  • synonym: bounce - to be refused by a bank because of lack of funds (of a check)
