rootsy

adjective
full of roots
Pronunciation: / ˈruːtsi / (AmE), / ˈruːtsi / (BrE)
containing many roots or characterized by the presence of prominent roots, especially in plants or soil

Examples

Rootsy ground
The path was so rootsy that hikers had to watch their step carefully.
After the heavy rain, the garden became especially rootsy and difficult to dig.
We avoided the rootsy section of the forest to prevent tripping over exposed roots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rooted - having roots or fixed firmly by roots
