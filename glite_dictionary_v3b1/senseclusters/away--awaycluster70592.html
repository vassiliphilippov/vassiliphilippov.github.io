away

adverb
so as to be out of mind or attention
Pronunciation: / əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to disappear, be removed, or cease to exist; out of consideration or attention

Examples

Fade away slowly
He brushed his fears away before stepping onto the stage.
If you can put your worries away, you'll feel much better.
When troubling thoughts come, she tries to push them away and focus on her work.
As the hours slipped away, he realized the opportunity was gone.
She explained away the missing money as a simple mistake.
The old paint on the wall faded away over the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aside - so as to be put or kept out of the way, specifically out of one's thoughts; synonym in this figurative sense
  • synonym: gone - no longer present or existing
  • antonym: remain - to continue to exist, especially after other parts have disappeared or been removed
  • synonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
