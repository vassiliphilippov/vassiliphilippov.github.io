slope

noun
slanting side of a hill or mountain
1.4 14/m
Pronunciation: / sloÊŠp / (AmE), / slÉ™ÊŠp / (BrE)
a piece of land that slants upward or downward, especially the side of a hill or mountain

Examples

A grassy slope
After the rain, the muddy slope was difficult to climb.
Children love sledding down the snowy slope in winter.
The cabin is nestled on the slope of a green hillside.

Synonyms

  • hillside
    1.0 7.56/m
    hillside is the sloping side of a hill, while slope is a more general piece of land that leans up or down.
  • sidehill
    4.9 0.12/m
    sidehill is the side or slope of a hill, while slope can mean any land that slants, on hills or mountains.
  • incline
    4.2 2.08/m
    incline is a sloping surface or ramp often made to connect levels, while slope is natural land that leans up or down.

Related Terms
