overmaster

verb
to overcome by superior force
2.7 7.2
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈmæ.stər/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈmɑː.stə/ (BrE)
to overcome, subdue, or overpower someone or something completely

Examples

To overmaster fear
His desire for revenge began to overmaster all rational thought.
She was suddenly overmastered by a wave of fear that left her speechless.
The soldiers overmastered the rebel forces after a fierce battle.
The invading army managed to overmaster the city's defenses within a few days.
She struggled to overmaster her fear before stepping onto the stage.
The team was overmastered by their opponents' superior tactics and skill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conquer - to take control of a place or people by force
  • synonym: overcome - to succeed in dealing with or gaining control of something
  • synonym: overwhelm - to defeat or overcome completely
  • antonym: submit - to yield or surrender to someone else's control
  • antonym: succumb - to stop resisting an opposing force; to yield
