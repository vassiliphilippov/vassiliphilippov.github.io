correlation

noun
mutual or statistical relationship between things
1.8 3.8
Pronunciation: /kɔː.rə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kɑː.rə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒ.rə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things, where changes in one are associated with changes in another

Examples

Strong correlation between events
Analysts observed a negative correlation between unemployment and consumer spending.
The study found a strong correlation between exercise and overall health.
There is little correlation between the color of a car and its speed.
Scientists found a positive correlation between exercise and improved mental health.
The data showed no significant correlation between diet and the disease.
There is a strong correlation between hours studied and exam results.

  • antonym: causation - the action of causing something; the relationship between cause and effect
  • synonym: connection - a relationship in which a person or thing is linked or associated with something else
