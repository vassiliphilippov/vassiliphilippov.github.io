ethnohistorian

noun
scholar of ethnic history
9.9
Pronunciation: /eθ.noʊ.hɪs.ˈtɔːr.iː.ən/ /; / /əθ.noʊ.hɪs.ˈtɔːr.iː.ən/ (AmE), /eθ.nəʊ.hɪs.ˈtɔː.riː.ən/ /; / /əθ.nəʊ.hɪs.ˈtɔː.riː.ən/ (BrE)
a scholar who studies the history and culture of specific ethnic groups, often using both historical records and cultural analysis

Examples

The ethnohistorian studies culture
The ethnohistorian spent years collecting oral histories from the local community.
As an ethnohistorian, she combined archival research with interviews to understand cultural changes.
A skilled ethnohistorian can reveal how traditions have evolved over centuries.
