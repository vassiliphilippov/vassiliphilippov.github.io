narratee

noun
intended audience
Pronunciation: / ˌnærəˈtiː / (AmE), / ˌnærəˈtiː / (BrE)
the person, character, or audience within a story or text to whom the narrative is addressed

Examples

The narratee in story
In literary theory, the narratee is the imagined recipient of the story within the text.
The author crafted the novel with a young adult narratee in mind.
Sometimes the narratee is directly addressed by the narrator in the story.
In some novels, the narratee is a specific character who listens to the story.
The author sometimes addresses the narratee directly, making the narrative more personal.
Literary scholars often analyze the relationship between the narrator and the narratee.
The narratee in the novel is never directly identified, but the narrator often addresses them.
Literary scholars debate whether the narratee is a character or simply the reader.
In some stories, the narratee plays an important role in shaping the narrative's tone.
