rebuff

to reject someone or something in an abrupt or ungracious manner

Examples

To rebuff an offer
He rebuffs any criticism, insisting his decision was correct.
She tried to help, but he rebuffed her offers politely.
The new proposal was rebuffed by the board without discussion.

Synonyms

  • give someone the brush-off
    7.5 0.12/m
    give someone the brush-off is a quick, unfriendly dismissal in casual talk, while rebuff sounds slightly more formal and abrupt.
  • snub
    6.3 0.30/m
    snub is a deliberate act of ignoring or cutting someone off in public, while rebuff is a blunt rejection.
  • spurn
    7.7 2.13/m
    spurn is to refuse with strong disdain or contempt in a clear way, while rebuff often means a curt, cold rejection.
  • cold-shoulder
    7.7 0.02/m
    cold-shoulder is quiet, long-term ignoring to show dislike or hurt, while rebuff is an immediate, sharp rejection.
  • turn down
    2.9 11/m
    turn down is a general refusal of offers, invitations, or requests, while rebuff suggests a harsher, abrupt personal rejection.
