junk science

noun
misleading or false science
Pronunciation: / dʒʌŋk ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
claims, theories, or practices presented as scientific but lacking credibility, validity, or rigorous methodology, often with the intent to deceive or mislead

Examples

Junk science claims
Several experts warned that the report was based on junk science and should not be trusted.
She accused the panel of relying on junk science to support their arguments.
The documentary exposed how junk science can influence public opinion on health issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pseudoscience - a system of theories, assumptions, and methods erroneously regarded as scientific
