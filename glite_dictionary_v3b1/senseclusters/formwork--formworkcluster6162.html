formwork

noun
temporary concrete mold
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmwɜːk / (BrE)
a temporary structure or mold used to hold and shape poured concrete until it hardens

Examples

Concrete formwork
The builders assembled the formwork before pouring the concrete foundation for the new house.
Properly removing the formwork is essential to ensure the concrete sets correctly.
Modern formwork systems can be reused for multiple construction projects, saving time and resources.
The builders removed the formwork after the concrete had fully set.
Steel formwork is often used for large construction projects to ensure stability.
Properly installed formwork is essential for creating strong and accurate concrete structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mold - a hollow container used to give shape to poured material
  • synonym: scaffolding - a temporary structure for supporting workers and materials during construction
