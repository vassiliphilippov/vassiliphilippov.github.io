intracoastal

noun
inland waterway
7.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.trə.ˈkoʊ.stəl/ /; / /ˈɪn.trə.koʊ.stəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.trə.ˈkəʊ.stəl/ /; / /ˈɪn.trə.kəʊ.stəl/ (BrE)
a sheltered inland or coastal waterway used for navigation, often providing a safer route than open sea.

Examples

The intracoastal route
Boaters often prefer the intracoastal for a smoother journey along the coast.
The marina is located right on the intracoastal, making it easy to access by boat.
They traveled the length of the intracoastal to avoid rough ocean conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canal - an artificial waterway for navigation or irrigation
  • antonym: ocean - the vast body of salt water covering most of the earth
  • synonym: waterway - a navigable body of water such as a river or canal
