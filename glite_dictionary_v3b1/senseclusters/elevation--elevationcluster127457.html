elevation

noun
height above sea level or reference point
Pronunciation: / ˌɛləˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the height or highest point of a place or object above a reference level, such as sea level; a raised area of land; the peak or summit of something

Examples

High elevation
At higher elevations, the temperature drops and snow is more likely.
Mount Everest's elevation makes it the tallest mountain on Earth.
The city has an elevation of 2,000 feet above sea level.
Photographers often choose the highest elevation for the best landscape shots.
That region is dotted with many small elevations that rise gently from the plain.
The hikers paused to admire the view from the elevation overlooking the valley.
He aspired to reach the elevation of his profession before retiring.
Her acting reached its elevation in the acclaimed film.
The company is now at the elevation of its influence in the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acme - the highest point or peak of something
  • synonym: altitude - height of an object or point in relation to sea level or ground level
  • synonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain
  • degree higher: mountain - a large natural elevation of the earth's surface
  • antonym: nadir - the lowest point
  • synonym: pinnacle - the most successful point or highest level
  • synonym: summit - the highest attainable point or level
