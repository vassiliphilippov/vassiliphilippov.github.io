quasi-judicial

adjective
like a court
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑːzi dʒuːˈdɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkwɑːzaɪ dʒuːˈdɪʃəl / (BrE)
having powers or functions similar to those of a court of law, but not fully judicial

Examples

Quasi-judicial powers
The regulatory agency has quasi-judicial powers to settle disputes between companies.
A quasi-judicial body can hold hearings and make binding decisions.
The board's quasi-judicial role allows it to interpret and enforce certain laws.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: administrative - relating to the running of an organization or government
  • degree higher: judicial - relating to courts or judges
