air thermometer

noun
temperature-measuring device using gas pressure
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr θərˈmɑːmɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈeə θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a thermometer that measures temperature by detecting changes in the pressure of a gas kept at constant volume

Examples

An air thermometer device
An air thermometer is especially useful in experiments requiring stability at constant volume.
The laboratory used an air thermometer to obtain precise temperature readings.
Unlike a mercury device, an air thermometer responds to the pressure changes of confined gas.
