indexing

noun
passive investing to track an index
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.dek.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.dek.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
the investment practice of constructing a portfolio to replicate the performance of a market index by holding its components in similar proportions (passive investing).

Examples

Stock market indexing
Despite market swings, more retirees embrace indexing for its low costs and steady exposure.
The firm's indexing strategy tracks the S&P 500 by holding the same stocks in proportion.
Many financial advisors recommend combining indexing with targeted active bets to balance risk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active management - an investment approach where managers select assets in an effort to outperform a market index
  • synonym: passive investing - an investment strategy that seeks to match the returns of a market index, typically using index funds or ETFs
