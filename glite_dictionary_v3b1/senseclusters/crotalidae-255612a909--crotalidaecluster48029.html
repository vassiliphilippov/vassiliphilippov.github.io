Crotalidae

noun
family of venomous snakes (pit vipers)
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈtælɪˌdiː / (AmE), / krəʊˈtælɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of venomous snakes commonly known as pit vipers, which includes rattlesnakes, copperheads, and cottonmouths.

Examples

The Crotalidae family
Herpetologists study Crotalidae to better understand venom evolution.
Rattlesnakes are well-known members of the Crotalidae family.
The family Crotalidae includes some of the most dangerous snakes in North America.
