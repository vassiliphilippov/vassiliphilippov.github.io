water mold

noun
fungus-like organism in water or soil
6.3 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər moʊld / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə məʊld / (BrE)
a parasitic or saprobic organism, similar to a fungus, that lives mainly in fresh water or moist soil

Examples

Water mold species
Many species of water mold can infect fish and amphibians in freshwater habitats.
Phytophthora infestans, a notorious water mold, caused the Irish potato famine.
The crops were devastated by a water mold that thrived in damp soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oomycete - a group of fungus-like organisms to which water molds belong
