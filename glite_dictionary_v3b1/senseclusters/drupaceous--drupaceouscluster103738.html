drupaceous

adjective
relating to a drupe (fruit type)
8.7 9.1
Pronunciation: / druˈpeɪʃəs / (AmE), / druˈpeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a fruit or plant) relating to or resembling a drupe, a fruit with a fleshy outer part and a single hard stone containing the seed.

Examples

Drupaceous fruit
Botanists often categorize olives as drupaceous because of their structure.
The drupaceous nature of cherries makes them distinct from berries.
The peach is a classic example of a drupaceous fruit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drupe - a fleshy fruit with a single hard stone (pit)
