Adam's Peak

proper noun
mountain in south central Sri Lanka
Pronunciation: / ˈædəmz piːk / (AmE), / ˈædəmz piːk / (BrE)
a mountain in south central Sri Lanka, rising to 2,243 meters (7,360 feet), noted for its distinctive conical shape and religious significance to Buddhists, Hindus, Christians, and Muslims

Examples

Adam's Peak mountain
At sunrise, the shadow of Adam's Peak creates a perfect triangle across the countryside.
Pilgrims climb Adam's Peak every year to view the sacred footprint at the summit.
The mist-covered slopes of Adam's Peak are home to unique wildlife and rare plants.
