cardiopulmonary arrest

noun
sudden failure of heart and lungs
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrdioʊˌpʊlməˌneri əˈrɛst / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊˌpʌlməˌnɛəri əˈrɛst / (BrE)
a sudden failure of both the heart and lungs to function, resulting in the cessation of breathing and heartbeat

Examples

Cardiopulmonary arrest event
Cardiologists train extensively to respond to cases of cardiopulmonary arrest in emergency settings.
Prompt defibrillation can sometimes reverse cardiopulmonary arrest and save a patient's life.
The patient suffered a cardiopulmonary arrest and required immediate resuscitation.
