geochronology

noun
dating Earth's history
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːoʊkrəˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊkrəˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of determining the age and order of events in Earth's geological history

Examples

Study of geochronology
Advances in geochronology have helped scientists better understand the timing of mass extinctions.
The research team used geochronology to date the volcanic rocks in the region.
Modern geochronology combines radiometric dating with stratigraphic analysis for more accurate results.
Scientists use geochronology to determine when certain rock layers were formed.
Advances in geochronology have improved our understanding of Earth's ancient climate changes.
By applying geochronology, researchers can date volcanic eruptions from millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronology - the arrangement of events in the order they occurred
  • synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of fossils and ancient life
  • synonym: stratigraphy - the study of rock layers and layering
