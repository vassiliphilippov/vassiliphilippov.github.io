thrash about

verb
move violently and uncontrollably
Pronunciation: / θræʃ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / θræʃ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to move or twist in a violent and uncontrolled way, often because of pain, fear, or agitation

Examples

To thrash about wildly
He continued to thrash about in his sleep, dreaming of the storm.
The fish began to thrash about as soon as it was pulled from the water.
Trapped under the blanket, the child started to thrash about until she was free.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flail - to wave or swing about wildly
  • synonym: writhe - to twist and turn with great force or in pain
