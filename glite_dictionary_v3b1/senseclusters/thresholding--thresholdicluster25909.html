thresholding

noun
stimulus detection method
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃˌhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of setting a limit (threshold) to separate, classify, or detect things, such as in sensory testing or image analysis

Examples

Thresholding process
The scientist applied thresholding to identify the lowest light intensity visible to the subject.
In hearing tests, thresholding helps determine the quietest sound a person can detect.
Modern image processing often uses thresholding to separate objects from the background.
In digital photography, thresholding is used to convert grayscale images into black and white.
The software applies thresholding to highlight important features in the data set.
Accurate thresholding can improve the results of image recognition systems.
