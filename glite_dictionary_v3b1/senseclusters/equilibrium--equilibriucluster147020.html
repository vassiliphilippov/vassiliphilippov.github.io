equilibrium

noun
state of balanced distribution
5.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /iː.kwə.ˈlɪb.riː.əm/ /; / /iː.kwɪ.ˈlɪb.riː.əm/ (AmE), /ɛ.kwɪ.ˈlɪb.rɪ.əm/ /; / /iː.kwɪ.ˈlɪb.rɪ.əm/ (BrE)
a state of balance or stability between opposing forces or influences

Examples

Social equilibrium
Social justice depends on an equilibrium in the distribution of wealth and opportunity.
The government aims to achieve equilibrium between supply and demand in the economy.
When the concentrations on both sides are equal, the system has reached equilibrium.
After several minutes, the system finally reached equilibrium and the temperatures stopped changing.
If the scale tips to one side, it means equilibrium has been disturbed.
The concept of equilibrium is fundamental in chemical reactions and economic models alike.

  • synonym: balance - a condition in which different elements are equal or in the correct proportions
  • antonym: disequilibrium - a loss or absence of equilibrium or stability
  • antonym: imbalance - a state in which opposing forces are not balanced
  • synonym: stability - the state of being stable
