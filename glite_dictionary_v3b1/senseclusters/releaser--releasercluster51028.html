releaser

noun
stimulus causing behavior
Pronunciation: / rɪˈliːsər / (AmE), / rɪˈliːsə / (BrE)
an external stimulus that triggers a specific, instinctive behavior in an animal

Examples

A pecking releaser
A red spot on the beak acts as a releaser for pecking behavior in young gulls.
The scent of a female moth serves as a releaser for male moths to begin searching for a mate.
Researchers identified the sound as a releaser that triggered the animal's defensive response.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibitor - something that prevents or restrains a behavioral response
  • synonym: sign stimulus - a specific stimulus that elicits a fixed action pattern in animals
