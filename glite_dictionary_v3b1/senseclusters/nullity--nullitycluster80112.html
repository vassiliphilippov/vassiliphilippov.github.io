nullity

noun
state of nonexistence
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌl.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈnʌl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state of being null or nonexistent; nothingness.

Examples

State of nullity
After years of neglect, the property had fallen into a state of almost complete nullity.
Philosophers have long debated whether true nullity is possible in the universe.
The theory suggests that the concept of nullity underlies certain mathematical abstractions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: existence - the state of being real or living
  • synonym: nothingness - the state of not existing or being nothing
