interactively

adverb
with active participation
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈæktɪvli / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈræktɪvli /; / ˌɪntərˈæktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that involves active participation and real-time communication or response, especially with people or computer systems

Examples

To learn interactively
The new app allows users to learn interactively by solving problems and receiving instant feedback.
During the workshop, participants engaged interactively with the presenter and each other.
Children often learn better when they can explore topics interactively rather than just listening to lectures.
The software allows users to edit images interactively, seeing changes as they make them.
Students can learn more effectively when they participate interactively in online lessons.
You must answer the questions interactively to complete the registration process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: automatically - in a way that happens without user input
  • synonym: collaboratively - in a way that involves working together with others
  • synonym: dynamically - in a way that changes or responds in real time
  • antonym: passively - in a way that does not involve active participation
