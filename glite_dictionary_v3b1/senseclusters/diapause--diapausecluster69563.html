diapause

verb
to suspend development
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌpɔz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəˌpɔːz / (BrE)
to enter or undergo a period of suspended development or metabolic activity, especially in response to environmental conditions

Examples

To diapause in winter
Some insect species diapause during the coldest months to survive harsh winters.
When food becomes scarce, certain fish will diapause until conditions improve.
Researchers observed that the larvae diapause in response to shorter daylight hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activate - to make active or start functioning
  • synonym: hibernate - to spend the winter in a dormant state
