spinifex

noun
coastal dune grass
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈspɪ.nɪ.feks/ (AmE), /ˈspɪ.nɪ.feks/ (BrE)
a tough, spiny grass that grows in sandy or dry areas, especially in Australia and on coastal dunes

Examples

Dense spinifex grass
The sandy beach was covered with dense patches of spinifex that protected the shoreline from erosion.
Birds often build their nests among the tough blades of spinifex on coastal dunes.
Conservationists planted spinifex to help restore the damaged dune ecosystem after the storm.
The harsh Australian outback is covered with spinifex that survives in the dry climate.
Aboriginal people sometimes use spinifex resin as a natural adhesive.
We had to walk carefully to avoid the sharp leaves of the spinifex along the trail.
