lead tree

noun
tree with mimosa-like flowers and long pods
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛd ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈliːd ˌtriː / (BrE)
a low, bushy tree of tropical and subtropical America and Asia, with feathery leaves, white and yellow flowers, and long flattened pods, often grown for fodder or soil improvement

Examples

A lead tree plant
Farmers often plant lead tree to provide shade and improve soil fertility in their fields.
Some gardeners value the lead tree for its rapid growth and attractive, feathery foliage.
The lead tree produces long pods that are used as animal fodder in many tropical regions.
