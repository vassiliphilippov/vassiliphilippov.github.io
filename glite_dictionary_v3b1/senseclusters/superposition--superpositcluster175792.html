superposition

noun
combination of states
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the combination of two or more states, effects, or influences at the same time, especially in physics or mathematics

Examples

Quantum superposition
In quantum mechanics, a particle can exist in a superposition of different states at the same time.
The superposition of sound waves can create complex musical tones.
Engineers use the principle of superposition to analyze forces in structures.
In quantum mechanics, a particle can exist in a superposition of different energy states.
The concept of superposition is fundamental to understanding quantum computing.
Scientists demonstrated superposition by showing an atom behaving as if it were in two places at once.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combination - the act of joining or mixing two or more things together
  • synonym: overlap - to exist in the same place or time as something else
  • antonym: separation - the act of keeping things apart or dividing them
