deserter

noun
person who abandons a duty, especially military
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɝːtər / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːtə / (BrE)
a person who abandons their duty or post, especially someone who leaves military service without permission

Examples

Army deserter
After months on the run, the deserter was finally captured by authorities.
During wartime, being labeled a deserter carried severe consequences.
The army punished the deserter for leaving his post during battle.
