ornithine

noun
non-protein amino acid in the urea cycle
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrnɪˌθin / (AmE), / ˈɔːnɪθiːn / (BrE)
an amino acid that does not occur in proteins but is important in the urea cycle of metabolism

Examples

Ornithine amino acid
Ornithine plays a critical role in removing ammonia from the body during the urea cycle.
High levels of ornithine may indicate a disorder in amino acid metabolism.
Scientists isolated ornithine while studying avian metabolic processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: urea cycle - the metabolic pathway that produces urea from ammonia
