racewalk

verb
to walk competitively
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪsˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈreɪsˌwɔːk / (BrE)
to take part in the sport of racewalking, walking quickly with a specific technique where one foot must appear to be in contact with the ground at all times.

Examples

To racewalk fast
She began to racewalk every morning to prepare for the upcoming competition.
Athletes must learn the correct technique to racewalk without being disqualified.
He decided to racewalk instead of run to avoid putting too much strain on his knees.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: run - to move swiftly on foot so that both feet leave the ground at times
  • degree lower: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
