rose chestnut

noun
tropical ornamental evergreen tree with fragrant flowers and hard wood
Pronunciation: / roʊz ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / rəʊz ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
A handsome evergreen tree native to East India, often planted ornamentally for its fragrant white flowers that yield perfume, and valued for its extremely hard wood used in construction, such as railroad ties.

Examples

The rose chestnut tree
In spring, the rose chestnut produces clusters of white flowers that fill the air with fragrance.
The rose chestnut is prized for its sweetly scented blossoms and evergreen foliage in tropical gardens.
Wood harvested from the rose chestnut is often used for durable structures such as railway sleepers.
