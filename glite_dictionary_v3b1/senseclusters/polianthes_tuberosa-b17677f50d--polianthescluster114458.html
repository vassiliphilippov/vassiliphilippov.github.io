Polianthes tuberosa

noun
highly fragrant Mexican flowering plant
Pronunciation: / poʊliˈænθiːz tuːbəˈroʊsə / (AmE), / pɒliˈænθiːz tjuːbəˈrəʊsə / (BrE)
A tuberous Mexican plant with grasslike leaves, cultivated for its spikes of highly fragrant, waxy, white, lily-like flowers.

Examples

Polianthes tuberosa flowers
Gardeners often plant Polianthes tuberosa to add an exotic scent to their flower beds.
Perfume makers use extracts from Polianthes tuberosa as a key ingredient in several fragrances.
The Polianthes tuberosa is prized for the sweet fragrance of its white blooms.
