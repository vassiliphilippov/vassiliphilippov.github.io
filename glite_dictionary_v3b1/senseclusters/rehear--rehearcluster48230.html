rehear

verb
hear a legal case again
7.2 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / riËˆhÉªr / (AmE), / riËËˆhÉªÉ™ / (BrE)
(law) to hear or try a case again in a court of law, especially after an appeal or when new evidence is introduced.

Examples

Court rehears a case
If the court decides to rehear the trial, both parties will present their arguments again.
The judge agreed to rehear the case after new evidence was discovered.
The motion to rehear was granted due to procedural errors in the initial proceedings.

Synonyms

  • retry
    5.4 0.43/m
    retry is a full new trial of the case after a mistrial or error, while rehear is a court hearing the matter again.
  • reargue
    6.6 0.04/m
    reargue means lawyers present their arguments again in court, while rehear can mean the court reconsiders the whole case, not just arguments.
  • relitigate
    8.0 0.01/m
    relitigate is bringing the same issue back to court as a new suit, while rehear is a formal court review or retrial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appeal - a request for a higher court to review a case
