upset

noun
tool for thickening/spreading metal by forging
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsɛt / (BrE)
A tool used to thicken or spread the end of metal objects, such as a bar or rivet, by forging, hammering, or swaging.

Examples

A metalworking upset
An upset is essential for shaping rivets during metalworking.
The blacksmith used an upset to widen the end of the heated rod.
To create a strong joint, the metalworker applies the upset at the bar’s end.
