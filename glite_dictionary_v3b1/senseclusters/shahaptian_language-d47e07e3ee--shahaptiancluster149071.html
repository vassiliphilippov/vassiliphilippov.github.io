Shahaptian language

noun
a group of indigenous Penutian languages
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈhæpʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʃəˈhæptiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a branch of the Penutian language family comprising languages traditionally spoken by the Shahaptian peoples of the Pacific Northwest, chiefly in present-day Oregon, Washington, and Idaho.
