Wyandot

proper noun
Iroquoian language
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪəndɑːt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəndɒt / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language formerly spoken by the Wyandot (Huron) people of North America

Examples

Wyandot language
Linguists recorded elders speaking Wyandot to document the language before it declined.
Few people today can understand or speak Wyandot, as it is now considered an endangered language.
The museum displayed artifacts labeled with words in Wyandot alongside their English translations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Huron - another name for the Wyandot language or people
  • synonym: Iroquoian - the family of languages to which Wyandot belongs
