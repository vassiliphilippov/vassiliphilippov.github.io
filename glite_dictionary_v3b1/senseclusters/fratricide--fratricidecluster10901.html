fratricide

noun
injury or death of an ally by one's own side (military)
Pronunciation: / ˈfræt.rɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfræt.rɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
the act of accidentally injuring or killing a member of one's own side, especially by military fire; friendly fire

Examples

Military fratricide incident
Military commanders are trained to minimize the risk of fratricide during joint operations.
Modern warfare technology aims to reduce fratricide by improving identification of friendly forces.
The incident resulted in fratricide, as the troops mistakenly fired on their own unit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: friendly fire - unintentional attack on one's own forces
