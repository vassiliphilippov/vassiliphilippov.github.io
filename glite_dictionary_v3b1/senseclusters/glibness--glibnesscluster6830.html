glibness

noun
superficial or insincere fluency
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪbnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪbnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being fluent and persuasive in speech, but often in a superficial or insincere way

Examples

Her glibness in speech
Her glibness made her seem persuasive, but few trusted what she said.
I was annoyed by the glibness with which he dismissed their serious concerns.
The politician's glibness was evident in every answer he gave during the debate.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: fluency - the ability to express oneself easily and articulately
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
