dugong

noun
large marine mammal (Dugong dugon)
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈduːˌgɑŋ /; / duˈɡɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌgɒŋ /; / ˈdjuːɡɒŋ / (BrE)
a large marine mammal, related to the manatee, found in warm coastal waters from eastern Africa to Australia

Examples

A large dugong
A dugong can weigh up to 400 kilograms and live more than 70 years.
Scientists are concerned about declining dugong populations due to habitat loss.
The dugong is often spotted grazing on seagrass in shallow coastal waters.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered dugong populations in coastal regions.
The dugong is sometimes called the 'sea cow' because of its gentle nature.
We spotted a dugong grazing on seagrass near the coral reef.
