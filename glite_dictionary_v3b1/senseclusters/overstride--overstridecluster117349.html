overstride

verb
to take overly long steps
8.3
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈstraɪd/ /; / /ə.vər.ˈstraɪd/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈstraɪd/ /; / /ə.və.ˈstraɪd/ (BrE)
to take steps that are longer than is natural or efficient, especially while walking or running

Examples

To overstride in running
Coaches warn athletes not to overstride during sprints to prevent muscle strain.
If you overstride while running, you may increase your risk of injury.
She realized she was starting to overstride as she tried to keep up with the group.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps
  • antonym: understride - to take steps that are shorter than normal
