terrigenous

adjective
from land origin
9.9
Pronunciation: / təˈrɪdʒənəs / (AmE), / təˈrɪdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
originating from land, especially describing materials or sediments carried to the sea by rivers or wind

Examples

Terrigenous sediments
The delta is formed mainly from terrigenous sediments brought by the river.
Scientists study terrigenous deposits to understand how land materials reach the ocean floor.
The coastline is rich in terrigenous sand, indicating strong river activity in the area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: marine - originating in or produced by the sea
