absoluteness

noun
the quality of being absolute
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.səˌluːt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.ljuːt.nəs /; / ˈab.sə.luːt.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being absolute, complete, or without limitation or exception

Examples

The absoluteness of law
He admired the absoluteness of the law, which applied equally to everyone.
Many philosophers debated the absoluteness of moral principles throughout history.
The absoluteness of her trust left no room for doubt or hesitation.
His rules were characterized by their absoluteness and lack of exceptions.
The absoluteness of her refusal left no room for negotiation.
There was an absoluteness to the judge’s verdict that surprised everyone in the courtroom.
