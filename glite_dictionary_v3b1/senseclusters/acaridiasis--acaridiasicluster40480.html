acaridiasis

noun
infestation by itch mites
Pronunciation: / əˌkærɪˈdaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / əˌkærɪˈdaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
Infestation or infection caused by itch mites, leading to skin irritation and other symptoms.

Examples

Acaridiasis skin infection
Effective treatment for acaridiasis typically involves topical medications to kill the mites.
Farmers may be at higher risk of developing acaridiasis due to frequent exposure to mites.
The dermatologist diagnosed the patient with acaridiasis after observing severe itching and skin lesions.
