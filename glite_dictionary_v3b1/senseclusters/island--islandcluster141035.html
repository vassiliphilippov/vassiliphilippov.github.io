island

verb
to disconnect from grid
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
to become disconnected from the main electrical grid and continue operating independently, especially of a power system or network

Examples

To island a power grid
During the storm, the microgrid islanded and continued to supply power to the hospital.
If the main grid fails, the system can island automatically to maintain local electricity.
Engineers tested how quickly the network islands in response to a blackout.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disconnect - to separate from a source or network
  • antonym: reconnect - to join again with a source or network
