thorium

noun
a radioactive metallic element (Th)
6.2 9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈθɔriəm / (AmE), / ˈθɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white, radioactive metallic element used as a nuclear fuel, especially in isotope 232; symbol: Th.

Examples

Radioactive thorium metal
Large deposits of thorium have been discovered in monazite sands along the coast.
Scientists are exploring the use of thorium in advanced nuclear reactors for safer power generation.
Unlike uranium, thorium is more abundant and produces less long-lived radioactive waste.
