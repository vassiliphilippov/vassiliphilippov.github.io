impermeable

adjective
not allowing liquid or gas through
4.6
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɝːmiəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːmiəbl̩ / (BrE)
not allowing liquid or gas to pass through; impossible to permeate.

Examples

Impermeable to water
Most plastics are nearly impermeable to water and air.
The scientists discovered an impermeable layer of clay beneath the farmland.
This jacket is impermeable to rain, keeping you dry in wet weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permeable - allowing liquids or gases to pass through
