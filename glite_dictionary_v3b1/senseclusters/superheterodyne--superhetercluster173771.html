superheterodyne

noun
radio receiver type
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈhɛtərəˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈhɛtərəˌdaɪn / (BrE)
a type of radio receiver that uses frequency mixing to convert received signals to a fixed intermediate frequency for easier processing

Examples

A superheterodyne radio
The vintage superheterodyne radio still works after all these years.
Engineers prefer the superheterodyne design for its improved selectivity and sensitivity.
He explained how a superheterodyne receiver converts radio signals to an intermediate frequency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: transmitter - a device that sends out radio signals
