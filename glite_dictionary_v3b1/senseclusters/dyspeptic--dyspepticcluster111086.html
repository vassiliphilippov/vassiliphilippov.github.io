dyspeptic

noun
person with indigestion
7.8
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from indigestion or is habitually irritable or gloomy

Examples

A dyspeptic patient
During the medical seminar, a dyspeptic described his daily struggles with digestion.
In the novel, the author portrays one character as a perpetual dyspeptic who is never satisfied with his food.
The dyspeptic complained bitterly about every meal he ate.
The dyspeptic avoided spicy foods to prevent discomfort after meals.
As a known dyspeptic, he often complained about the menu at restaurants.
Doctors recommended a special diet for the dyspeptic to ease his symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypochondriac - a person excessively worried about their health
  • synonym: invalid - a person made weak or disabled by illness or injury
