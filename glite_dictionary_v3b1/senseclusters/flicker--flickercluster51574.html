flicker

verb
move or shine unsteadily or rapidly
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪkɚ /; / ˈflɪk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪkə /; / ˈflɪk.ə / (BrE)
to shine, burn, or move with quick, small, and unsteady movements or light

Examples

To flicker in the wind
A sudden breeze made the flames flicker for a moment before dying down.
She watched the images flicker across the television screen.
The candle flickered in the draughty room, casting unsteady shadows on the wall.
A distant lightning storm made the sky flicker throughout the night.
The candle began to flicker as the breeze stirred the room.
The old fluorescent bulb would flicker before finally turning on.
I watched the old streetlamp flicker before it finally went out.
Lightning made the tv screen flicker momentarily during the storm.
The candles flickered in the gentle evening breeze.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: flare - to burn with a sudden intensity
  • synonym: flash - to shine suddenly for a short time
  • synonym: fluctuate - to rise and fall irregularly in number or amount
  • degree lower: glimmer - to shine faintly or unsteadily
  • degree lower: glow - to produce a steady light without flame
  • synonym: quiver - to tremble or shake with a slight rapid motion
  • synonym: twinkle - to shine with a flickering or sparkling light
