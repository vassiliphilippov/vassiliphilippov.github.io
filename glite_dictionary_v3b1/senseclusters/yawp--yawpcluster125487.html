yawp

verb
make a loud or harsh noise
8.3 8.7
Pronunciation: /jɔːp/ /; / /jɑːp/ (AmE), /jɔːp/ (BrE)
to make a loud, harsh, or raucous noise, often as a complaint or whine

Examples

To yawp loudly
A dog somewhere in the neighborhood yawped into the night, breaking the silence.
The children began to yawp with excitement as the parade marched by.
They could hear protesters yawping loudly outside the city hall.
Even after being given a snack, the child continued yawping for attention.
She often yawps about minor inconveniences at work, annoying her coworkers.
The toddler began to yawp when he couldn't have the toy he wanted.

  • synonym: whine - to complain with a high-pitched or complaining tone
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly
  • synonym: yelp - to give a short, sharp cry or bark
