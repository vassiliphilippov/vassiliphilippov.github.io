hydrophobia

noun
extreme fear of water
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈfəʊbɪə /; / ˌhaɪdrəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
an extreme or irrational fear of water, especially as a symptom of rabies

Examples

Severe hydrophobia in rabies
His doctor explained that hydrophobia is one of the most alarming signs of rabies.
Stories about sailors with hydrophobia circulated widely in the nineteenth century.
The patient developed intense hydrophobia after being bitten by a rabid animal.
Early signs of hydrophobia may include reluctance or inability to swallow fluids.
One classic symptom of rabies is hydrophobia, causing sufferers to panic at the sight of water.
The doctor's diagnosis was confirmed by the patient's severe hydrophobia when given a glass to drink.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aquaphobia - fear of water (psychiatric term)
  • synonym: rabies - a viral disease causing hydrophobia as a symptom
