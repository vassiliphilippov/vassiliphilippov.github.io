continued fraction

noun
mathematical fraction recursively expanded
5.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪn.juːd ˈfræk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.juːd ˈfræk.ʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical expression in which a number is represented as the sum of its integer part and the reciprocal of another number, which can itself be written as a similar sum, forming a sequence that can continue indefinitely or terminate

Examples

A continued fraction expression
A rational number can always be expressed as a continued fraction with a finite number of terms.
Mathematicians study continued fractions to understand complex numbers and their properties.
The continued fraction representation of pi is known for its interesting pattern.
