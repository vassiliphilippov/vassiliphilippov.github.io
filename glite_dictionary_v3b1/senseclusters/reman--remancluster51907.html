reman

verb
to rebuild machinery
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈriːmæn /; / ˌriːˈmæn / (AmE), / ˈriːmæn /; / ˌriːˈmæn / (BrE)
to rebuild or restore a used product, especially machinery or parts, to a like-new condition through manufacturing processes

Examples

To reman an engine
The factory will reman used car engines to meet environmental standards.
Many companies choose to reman equipment instead of buying new machines.
Technicians are trained to reman parts so they function like new.
The factory will reman hundreds of car engines to meet the new environmental standards.
Many companies choose to reman old equipment instead of buying new machines.
After being reman by skilled technicians, the generator worked as if it were brand new.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discard - to throw something away because it is no longer useful
  • synonym: rebuild - to build something again after it has been damaged or worn out
  • synonym: refurbish - to renovate or make like new again
  • synonym: repair - to fix something that is broken or damaged
  • antonym: replace - to put something new in the place of something old
