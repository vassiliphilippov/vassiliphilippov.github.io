effloresce

verb
to form a crust of crystals due to evaporation
Pronunciation: / ˌɛfləˈrɛs /; / ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛfləˈrɛs /; / ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs / (BrE)
(of a substance) to form a crust or powdery surface of crystals, usually as a result of evaporation or exposure to air

Examples

Minerals effloresce on stone
If the water evaporates quickly, minerals may effloresce on the surface of the stone.
Over time, the bricks began to effloresce with a white, powdery crust.
Salt deposits effloresce on the walls of the old cellar during the dry season.
Certain minerals effloresce on rock surfaces, producing distinctive crystal patterns.
If the temperature is low enough, the compound may effloresce during storage.
When exposed to air, the salt will quickly effloresce and form a white powder.
