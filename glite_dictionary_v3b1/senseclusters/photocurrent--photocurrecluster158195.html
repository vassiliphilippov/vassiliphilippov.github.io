photocurrent

noun
current from light
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.toʊ.kʌr.ənt/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.təʊ.kʌr.ənt/ (BrE)
an electric current generated in a material when it is exposed to light

Examples

The photocurrent increases
The solar cell produces a photocurrent when sunlight strikes its surface.
Scientists measured the photocurrent to determine the efficiency of the new sensor.
A strong photocurrent indicates that the material is highly sensitive to light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dark current - an electric current that flows in the absence of light
  • synonym: photoelectric effect - the emission of electrons when light shines on a material
