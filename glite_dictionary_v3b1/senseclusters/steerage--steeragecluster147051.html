steerage

noun
cheapest accommodations on a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɪərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the part of a passenger ship providing the cheapest accommodations, originally located near the steering apparatus

Examples

Travel in steerage
Many immigrants to America traveled in steerage to save money.
She described her journey across the ocean in steerage as uncomfortable but affordable.
The poor conditions in steerage were a stark contrast to the luxury above deck.
