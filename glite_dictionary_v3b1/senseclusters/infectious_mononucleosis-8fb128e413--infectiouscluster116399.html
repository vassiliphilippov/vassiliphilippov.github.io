infectious mononucleosis

noun
disease causing fever and swollen lymph nodes
5.5 3.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃəs mə.nə.njuː.ˈkliː.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃəs mə.nə.njuː.ˈkliː.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
an acute infectious disease marked by fever, swollen lymph nodes, and an increase in certain white blood cells, often caused by the Epstein–Barr virus

Examples

Contracting infectious mononucleosis
College students are particularly at risk of contracting infectious mononucleosis, especially through close contact.
The doctor diagnosed her with infectious mononucleosis after seeing her symptoms and lab results.
Treatment for infectious mononucleosis usually focuses on relieving symptoms such as fever and sore throat.
