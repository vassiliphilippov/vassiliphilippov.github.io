irrigable

adjective
able to be flushed
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrɪɡəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪɡəbəl / (BrE)
able to be washed or flushed with a liquid, especially for cleaning a wound or body cavity

Examples

Irrigable wound
The doctor determined that the wound was irrigable and could be safely cleaned with saline.
Before the procedure, the nurse checked if the cavity was irrigable to prevent infection.
Some injuries are not irrigable due to their location or the risk of further damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonirrigable - not able to be washed or flushed with a liquid
  • synonym: washable - able to be washed or cleaned with water or another liquid
