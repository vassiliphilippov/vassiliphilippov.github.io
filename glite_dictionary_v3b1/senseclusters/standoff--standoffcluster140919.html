standoff

noun
successful defensive stand or resistance
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈstændˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈstændɒf / (BrE)
a situation where two sides oppose each other and neither side wins or makes progress

Examples

Police standoff
After several hours, the standoff between protesters and police ended without violence.
The soldiers held a standoff at the bridge, preventing enemy forces from advancing.
Their defensive strategy resulted in a standoff, stopping the opposition's attack cold.
The standoff between the two countries continued for weeks without resolution.
Negotiators worked late into the night to break the standoff over the new contract.
After hours of discussion, the meeting ended in a standoff with neither side willing to compromise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - a situation where both sides reach a decision together
  • synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made
  • synonym: defense - the act of protecting against attack
  • synonym: impasse - a situation where no agreement can be reached
  • antonym: retreat - an act of withdrawing from an attack or confrontation
