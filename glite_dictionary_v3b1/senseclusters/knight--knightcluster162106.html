knight

noun
medieval mounted nobleman or modern UK honoree
6.5 8.09/m
Pronunciation: / naÉªt / (AmE), / naÉªt / (BrE)
(historical) A man of noble birth who was trained to arms and chivalry and served as a mounted soldier in the Middle Ages; (in modern use) a man who has been honored by the sovereign in the United Kingdom for personal merit and given the title 'Sir'.

Examples

A knight in armor
He was made a knight by the Queen for his services to charity.
In medieval times, a knight was expected to uphold the code of chivalry.
The museum displayed armor once worn by a fifteenth-century knight.

  • synonym: sir - title used for knights in the UK
