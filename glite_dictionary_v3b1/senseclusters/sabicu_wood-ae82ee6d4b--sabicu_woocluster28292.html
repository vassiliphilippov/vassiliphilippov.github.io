sabicu wood

noun
wood resembling mahogany from the sabicu tree
5.8
Pronunciation: / səˈbiːku wʊd / (AmE), / səˈbiːku wʊd / (BrE)
The wood of the sabicu tree, valued for its resemblance to mahogany and used in carpentry and furniture making.

Examples

Sabicu wood furniture
Although less expensive than mahogany, sabicu wood is prized for its similar appearance.
Boat builders once favored sabicu wood for its strength and durability.
The table is crafted from sabicu wood, giving it a warm, reddish hue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown wood used for furniture
