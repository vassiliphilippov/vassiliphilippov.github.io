compulsion

noun
the act of forcing someone to do something
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpʌlʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌlʃən / (BrE)
the action or state of forcing or being forced to do something; constraint or coercion

Examples

Act under compulsion
He acted under compulsion and had no real choice in the matter.
Laws exist to prevent the compulsion of witnesses to testify against themselves.
The school joined the program out of compulsion, not because it wanted to.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by force or threats
