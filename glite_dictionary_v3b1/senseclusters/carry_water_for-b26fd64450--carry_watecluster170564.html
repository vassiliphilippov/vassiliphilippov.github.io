carry water for

verb
support someone without benefit
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ˈwɔtər fər / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈwɔːtə fə / (BrE)
to support or defend someone, especially in an organization or political context, often without receiving any benefit in return

Examples

To carry water for a company
He has spent years carrying water for the company instead of pursuing his own interests.
Many journalists were accused of carrying water for the administration during the scandal.
She refused to carry water for her boss when she knew he was wrong.
