chastening

noun
an experience or rebuke causing humility
9.4 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃeɪ.sən.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈtʃeɪs.tən.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtʃeɪ.sən.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈtʃeɪs.tən.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
A rebuke or experience that makes someone realize their error and often leads to greater humility or self-awareness.

Examples

A chastening experience
His public error resulted in a chastening that changed his attitude.
She received a sharp chastening after breaking the rules.
The defeat was a chastening for the team, forcing them to reconsider their strategy.
