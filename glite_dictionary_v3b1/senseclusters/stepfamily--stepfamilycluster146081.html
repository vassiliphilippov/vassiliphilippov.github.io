stepfamily

noun
family with stepparent
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛpˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈstɛpˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family in which at least one parent has children from a previous relationship or marriage

Examples

A stepfamily household
After her father remarried, she became part of a stepfamily with two new siblings.
Many modern stepfamilies work hard to build strong relationships among all members.
Adjusting to life in a stepfamily can be challenging for both parents and children.
After his mother remarried, he became part of a loving stepfamily.
Many children adjust well to life in a stepfamily with patience and support.
Family holidays can be complicated when you have a large stepfamily to consider.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blended family - a family consisting of a couple and their children from this and previous relationships
  • antonym: nuclear family - a family group consisting of two parents and their children
