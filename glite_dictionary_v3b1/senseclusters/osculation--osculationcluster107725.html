osculation

noun
point where curves or surfaces share a tangent
4.2 9.5
Pronunciation: /ɑːs.kjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɑːs.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɒs.kjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɒs.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(mathematics) the point at which two curves or surfaces touch and share a common tangent.

Examples

Osculation of two curves
At the point of osculation, the two curves not only meet but also share the same direction.
In differential geometry, studying osculation helps in understanding the local behavior of surfaces.
The osculation of the two circles occurs precisely at the point where they are tangent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tangency - the condition of being tangent, or touching at a single point
