expediter

noun
person speeding process
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkspəˌdaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɛkspədaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who helps a process move faster or more efficiently, especially in business, logistics, or restaurants (such as coordinating food delivery in a restaurant)

Examples

The expediter coordinates orders
The expediter coordinated between the kitchen and the servers to ensure quick service.
As an expediter, she made sure all the paperwork was completed on schedule.
A skilled expediter can greatly improve the efficiency of a busy restaurant.
The expediter made sure all the supplies reached the kitchen on time during the busy dinner service.
As an expediter, she coordinated between the warehouse and delivery drivers to avoid delays.
A skilled expediter can greatly improve the efficiency of a restaurant or factory.
The expediter made sure all the dishes for table five were ready at the same time.
During the busy lunch hour, the expediter kept the kitchen running smoothly.
Without a skilled expediter, orders might arrive at customers' tables at different times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chef - a professional cook in a restaurant
  • synonym: coordinator - a person who organizes people or activities
  • synonym: facilitator - a person who makes an action or process easier
  • antonym: obstructor - a person who slows down or blocks a process
  • synonym: server - a person who brings food to customers in a restaurant
