physiological jaundice of the newborn

noun
yellowish skin in newborns, often harmless
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪziəˌlɑdʒɪkəl ˈʤɔndɪs əv ðə ˈnuˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˌfɪziələˌlɒdʒɪkəl ˈdʒɔːndɪs əv ðə ˈnjuːˌbɔːn / (BrE)
a common, usually harmless yellowish discoloration of the skin and eyes in newborns, caused by immature liver function and typically resolving without treatment

Examples

Newborn physiological jaundice of the newborn
Doctors often reassure parents that physiological jaundice of the newborn is harmless and temporary.
Most cases of physiological jaundice of the newborn resolve within two weeks without treatment.
The pediatrician explained that physiological jaundice of the newborn is a normal part of many babies’ development.
