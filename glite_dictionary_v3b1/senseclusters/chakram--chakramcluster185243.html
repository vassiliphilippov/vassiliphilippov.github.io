chakram

noun
South Asian ceremonial disc
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃʌkrəm / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkrəm / (BrE)
a circular disc used as a religious or ceremonial symbol in South Asian traditions, often representing a wheel and associated with deities

Examples

Sacred chakram symbol
The ancient temple displayed a polished golden chakram above the shrine during the festival.
In Hindu iconography, the god Vishnu is often shown holding a chakram in one of his hands.
The chakram is both a weapon and a sacred symbol in various South Asian cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: symbol - an object that represents an idea or concept
  • synonym: wheel - a circular object that revolves on an axle
