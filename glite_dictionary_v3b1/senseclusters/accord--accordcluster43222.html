accord

verb
to be in agreement or harmony
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: / əˈkɔrd / (AmE), / əˈkɔːd / (BrE)
(of ideas, actions, or qualities) to be in agreement or harmony; to go together well.

Examples

To accord with facts
Her actions accord with her beliefs about fairness and honesty.
His goals did not accord with the organization’s mission.
The two stories accord surprisingly well, given their different perspectives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disagree - to not be in harmony or agreement
  • synonym: harmonize - to go together in a pleasing or compatible way
