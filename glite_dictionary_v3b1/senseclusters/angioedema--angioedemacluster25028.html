angioedema

noun
allergic swelling of skin or tissues
7.0 7.7
Pronunciation: /æn.dʒɪ.oʊ.ɪ.ˈdiː.mə/ /; / /æn.dʒiː.oʊ.ɪ.ˈdiː.mə/ (AmE), /æn.dʒɪ.əʊ.ɪ.ˈdiː.mə/ /; / /æn.dʒiː.əʊ.ɪ.ˈdiː.mə/ (BrE)
A condition characterized by sudden, localized swelling of the deeper layers of the skin or mucous membranes, usually as an allergic reaction.

Examples

Severe angioedema attack
Patients with recurrent angioedema may need to carry emergency medication at all times.
She developed angioedema after taking an antibiotic, with her lips and eyelids swelling rapidly.
The doctor diagnosed his facial swelling as angioedema caused by a food allergy.
