charm

verb
attract or enchant someone
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrm / (AmE), / tʃɑːm / (BrE)
to attract, please, or influence someone by being pleasant, engaging, or appealing

Examples

To charm the crowd
He tried to charm the crowd with his wit and humor.
She easily charmed everyone at the party with her warm smile.
They were charmed by his kindness and genuine interest in their stories.
He tried to charm the crowd with his witty stories and warm smile.
She managed to charm her boss into giving her the day off.
They were charmed into helping by her enthusiasm and kindness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or tired
  • synonym: cajole - to persuade someone to do something by sustained coaxing or flattery
  • synonym: coax - to gently persuade someone to do something
  • synonym: enchant - to delight and attract someone completely
