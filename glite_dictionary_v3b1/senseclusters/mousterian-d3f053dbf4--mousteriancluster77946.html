Mousterian

noun
Paleolithic tool industry
9.9
Pronunciation: / muːˈstɪəriən / (AmE), / muːˈstɪəriən / (BrE)
a Middle Paleolithic stone-tool industry associated with Neanderthals and early modern humans, characterized by distinctive flake tools

Examples

Mousterian tools
Archaeologists discovered Mousterian tools in the cave, indicating Neanderthal occupation.
The Mousterian industry is known for its advanced flake tools and scrapers.
Researchers compared Mousterian artifacts from different sites across Europe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Acheulean - an earlier Paleolithic stone-tool industry known for handaxes
  • synonym: Neanderthal - an extinct species of human associated with Mousterian tools
