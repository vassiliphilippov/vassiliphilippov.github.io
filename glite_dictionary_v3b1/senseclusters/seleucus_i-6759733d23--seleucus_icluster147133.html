Seleucus I

proper noun
Macedonian general; founder of the Seleucid dynasty
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈluːkəs ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / sɪˈljuːkəs ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 358–281 BC) Macedonian general who accompanied Alexander the Great into Asia and founded the Seleucid dynasty, ruling much of Asia Minor until 65 BC.

Examples

Seleucus I the general
Seleucus I played a key role in the division of Alexander the Great's empire.
Many ancient coins from Asia Minor bear the image of Seleucus I.
The Seleucid dynasty, founded by Seleucus I, ruled a vast territory for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Alexander the Great - King of Macedonia and military leader who conquered much of the ancient world
