flood

noun
the act of flooding or overflowing
1.0 18/m
Pronunciation: / flÊŒd / (AmE), / flÊŒd / (BrE)
a large amount of water that covers land that is usually dry; the act of water overflowing or inundating an area

Examples

A flood in the street
A broken water main caused a flood in the basement.
The stadium was at risk when the river threatened to flood the surrounding area.
The sudden flood destroyed the crops and forced villagers to evacuate.
After heavy rains, the town was left underwater due to a sudden flood.
Every spring, the plains are rejuvenated by the annual flood.
The river burst its banks, causing a severe flood that damaged many homes.

Synonyms

  • inundation
    7.7 0.29/m
    inundation is the flooding of land that stays under water after rivers rise or heavy rain, while flood is the general overflow.
  • deluge
    6.3 0.84/m
    deluge is a very large sudden flood from heavy rain, while flood is the common word for water covering land.
  • deluge
    6.0 0.12/m
    deluge is to cover something completely with water in a strong way, while flood means water overflows and fills land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deluge - a severe flood
  • antonym: drought - a prolonged period without rain
  • synonym: inundation - an overwhelming overflow of water onto land
