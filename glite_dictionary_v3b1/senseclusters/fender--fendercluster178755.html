fender

noun
inclined metal frame on a locomotive
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a part of a vehicle (such as a car or locomotive) that covers and protects the wheel area or clears obstacles from the track

Examples

Car fender
A broken fender can make the locomotive vulnerable to obstacles on the railway.
Engineers inspected the fender after the locomotive hit a fallen branch on the tracks.
The old steam engine's fender was dented from years of clearing debris.
He polished the fender to remove the mud and keep the car looking clean.
Rain splashed against the fender as the truck drove through the puddle.
The car's fender was dented after the minor accident.
