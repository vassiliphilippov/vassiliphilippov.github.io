fruit fly

noun
small insect; larvae feed on fruit
5.2 2.2
Pronunciation: /fruːt flaɪ/ (AmE), /fruːt flaɪ/ (BrE)
a small flying insect whose larvae feed on and damage fruit, often found near decaying fruit or vegetables

Examples

A fruit fly larva
A bowl of ripe bananas attracted several fruit flies in the kitchen.
If you leave fruit uncovered, fruit flies may appear within a few hours.
Scientists often study the genetics of the fruit fly for research purposes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Drosophila - a genus of small fruit flies commonly used in genetic research
