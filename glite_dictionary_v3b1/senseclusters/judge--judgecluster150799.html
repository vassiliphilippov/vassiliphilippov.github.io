judge

verb
form an opinion after careful consideration
2.2 2.1
Pronunciation: /dʒʌdʒ/ (AmE), /dʒʌdʒ/ (BrE)
to form an opinion or decision about something or someone, especially after considering the facts

Examples

To judge a book
After reading the essay, she judged it to be well-written and thoughtful.
It's difficult to judge a situation without knowing all the facts.
Try not to judge people too quickly based on appearances.
Can you judge how long it will take to finish this project?
I would judge the distance to be about five miles from here.
It's hard to judge how many people will attend the event.
After reviewing all the evidence, the jury had to judge the defendant's innocence or guilt.
I don't want to judge anyone before hearing their side of the story.
It's unfair to judge people solely by their appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assess - to make a judgment about the quality, size, or value of something
  • antonym: condemn - to express complete disapproval of, typically in public
  • synonym: estimate - to roughly calculate or judge the value, number, quantity, or extent of something
  • synonym: evaluate - to assess or determine the value, significance, or extent of something
