Ph.D.

noun
highest academic doctoral degree

a doctoral degree awarded by universities, typically for advanced research in a specific field, usually requiring several years of study and a dissertation

Examples

Earn a Ph.D.
After five years of research, she finally earned her Ph.D. in chemistry.
Dr. Patel holds a Ph.D. from Oxford University specializing in artificial intelligence.
Many academic jobs require applicants to have a Ph.D. or equivalent degree.

Synonyms

  • PhD
    4.8 19/m
    PhD is the common modern form written without dots and used in everyday writing, while Ph.D. is the dotted abbreviation.
  • Doctor of Philosophy
    5.4 0.15/m
    Doctor of Philosophy is the full formal name used on certificates and in official writing, while Ph.D. is its short abbreviation.
  • DPhil
    7.3 0.01/m
    DPhil is the British form used at some universities like Oxford, while Ph.D. is the more common international abbreviation.
