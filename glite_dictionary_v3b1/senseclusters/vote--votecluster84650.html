vote

noun
the right to cast a ballot; enfranchisement
1.0 20/m
Pronunciation: / voÊŠt / (AmE), / vÉ™ÊŠt / (BrE)
the right to participate in choosing a government or making a decision by casting a ballot, especially as a legally established privilege

Examples

The right to vote
Many people campaigned for the right to have the vote in national elections.
The Civil Rights Movement fought to secure the vote for African Americans.
Women gained the vote in many countries during the twentieth century.

Synonyms

  • right to vote
    2.3 3.82/m
    right to vote expresses the legal entitlement to take part in elections by casting a ballot, while vote names that established right.
  • voting right
    3.3 1.08/m
    voting right refers to the official legal power to participate in a decision, while vote here means that specific voting entitlement.
  • suffrage
    7.6 2.50/m
    suffrage means the right to vote in public elections, while vote in this sense means the legal right to take part in choosing government.
  • franchise
    6.6 1.11/m
    franchise is a formal, statutory right often used for voting rights, while vote here describes the legal act or privilege of participating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disenfranchisement - the denial or removal of the right to vote
  • synonym: enfranchisement - the granting of the right to vote
  • synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
