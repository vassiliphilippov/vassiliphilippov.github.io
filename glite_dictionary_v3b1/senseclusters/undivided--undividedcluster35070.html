undivided

adjective
not separated; whole
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.dɪˈvaɪ.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈvaɪ.dɪd / (BrE)
not separated or divided into parts; forming a single whole (especially property, interest, or responsibility)

Examples

Undivided ownership

The land remained an undivided estate for many years.
He inherited an undivided portion of the estate, making him the sole owner.
Parents often feel the pressure of undivided responsibility for their children's wellbeing.
She expects his undivided attention during meetings.
