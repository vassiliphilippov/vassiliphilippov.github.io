afterward

adverb
at a later time
Pronunciation: / ˈæftərwərd /; / ˈæftɚwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəwəd /; / ˈɑːftəwədz / (BrE)
At a later time; subsequently.

Examples

Happened afterward
She felt a great sense of relief afterward.
There was a celebration at the park afterward.
We went for coffee afterward to discuss the meeting.
She was nervous before the exam, but felt relieved afterwards.
We had lunch, and afterwards we went for a walk in the park.
You must submit your application first; decisions will be made afterwards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: before - at an earlier time
  • antonym: beforehand - in advance; ahead of time
  • synonym: later - at a time in the future or after the expected time
