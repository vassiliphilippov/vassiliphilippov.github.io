set apart

verb
separate or distinguish from others
1.5 3.9
Pronunciation: / sɛt əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / sɛt əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to separate or distinguish someone or something from others, often for a special purpose or duty

Examples

To set apart from others
Her exceptional artistic style set apart her work from that of her peers.
Strict hygiene protocols set apart this hospital from others in the area.
The company sets apart a portion of its profits for charity every year.
She was set apart from the rest of the team to handle customer service.
The school decided to set apart one student to represent the class at the ceremony.
They set apart these funds especially for emergency repairs.
