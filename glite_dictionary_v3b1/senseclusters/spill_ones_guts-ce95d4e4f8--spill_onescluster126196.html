spill one's guts

verb
reveal secrets or emotions
Pronunciation: / spɪl wʌnz ɡʌts / (AmE), / spɪl wʌnz ɡʌts / (BrE)
to reveal personal thoughts, emotions, or secrets, often in a candid or unrestrained way

Examples

To spill one's guts honestly
After hours of questioning, he finally spilled his guts and told the truth.
He didn't want to spill his guts about what happened at work, but eventually he did.
Whenever she feels overwhelmed, she calls her best friend to spill her guts.

  • antonym: keep quiet - to not share information; to remain silent
