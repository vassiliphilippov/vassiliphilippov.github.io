Siouan

noun
member of a North American Native American group
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.ən/ (AmE), /ˈsuː.ən/ (BrE)
a member of a Native American group whose peoples historically ranged from Lake Michigan to the Rocky Mountains and spoke a Siouan language

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered tools used by ancient Siouan communities near the Missouri River.
Many Siouan people migrated to new territories during the 18th century.
The museum features artifacts and pottery made by various Siouan tribes.
