independent

adjective
(of a clause) able to stand alone as a sentence
8.3 3.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.dɪ.ˈpen.dənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.ˈpen.dənt/ (BrE)
(of a clause) capable of standing alone as a complete simple sentence, not dependent on another clause.

Examples

An independent clause
An independent clause expresses a complete thought and can function as a sentence on its own.
If you remove the subordinate clause, the independent clause still makes sense by itself.
The teacher asked the students to identify the independent clause in each complex sentence.
