Canada jay

noun
jay bird of northern North America
Pronunciation: / ˈkænədə ˌdʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈkanədə ˌdʒeɪ / (BrE)
a jay native to northern North America, with a black-capped head and no crest, known for its boldness in stealing food

Examples

The Canada jay bird
A Canada jay boldly approached our camp and stole some of our trail mix.
The Canada jay is often found in boreal forests throughout Canada and Alaska.
Unlike other jays, the Canada jay does not have a crest on its head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gray jay - alternative common name for the same bird species
