lantern-fly

noun
tropical insect with snout-like process
Pronunciation: / ˈlæn.tɚn ˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlæn.tən ˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
a large, brightly colored tropical insect with an extended snout-like process, once mistakenly believed to produce light

Examples

The lantern-fly insect
For years, people believed that the lantern-fly could produce light, though this is a myth.
Scientists often collect lantern-flies to study their vibrant patterns and unique physiology.
The lantern-fly is admired for its striking colors and unusual head shape.
