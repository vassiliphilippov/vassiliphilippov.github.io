matrixing

noun
signal routing process
8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmeɪ.trɪk.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈmeɪ.trɪk.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of routing and combining multiple input signals to various outputs using a matrix system, especially in audio or broadcast engineering

Examples

Matrixing audio signals
The technician used matrixing to send different audio feeds to each speaker in the auditorium.
Modern broadcast studios rely on matrixing to manage complex signal paths efficiently.
With advanced matrixing, engineers can quickly adjust which microphones are heard on each channel.
