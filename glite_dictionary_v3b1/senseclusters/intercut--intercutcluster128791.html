intercut

verb
to cut in a pattern
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkʌt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkʌt / (BrE)
to make a series of cuts or slashes in a material so that they alternate or cross, forming a pattern

Examples

To intercut a pattern
The artisan carefully intercut the metal sheet to create an intricate design.
To achieve the desired effect, the tailor intercut the fabric with diagonal slashes.
Ancient craftsmen often intercut leather to produce decorative patterns on belts and bags.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carve - to cut and shape material, especially wood or stone
  • synonym: incise - to cut into a surface with a sharp tool
