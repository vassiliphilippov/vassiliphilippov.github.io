settlement house

noun
community center providing social services
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl.mənt haʊs / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl.mənt haʊs / (BrE)
a community center in an urban, underprivileged area, providing educational, recreational, and other social services to local residents

Examples

A settlement house center
Hull House was a famous settlement house that helped thousands of immigrants in Chicago.
Many cities established a settlement house to offer free classes, childcare, and job training.
Volunteers at the settlement house organized after-school activities for neighborhood children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: community center - a place where people from a particular community can meet for social or educational activities
