army golf tactic

noun
humorous golf term for erratic, zigzag play
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrmi ɡɑlf ˈtæk tɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi ɡɒlf ˈtæk tɪk / (BrE)
a humorous term for a style of golf in which the player frequently hits the ball alternately left and right, referring to the military marching cadence 'left, right, left'

Examples

An army golf tactic
Coach warned them not to rely on an army golf tactic if they wanted to improve their scores.
During the tournament, his army golf tactic kept sending the ball to opposite sides of the fairway.
They laughed about their army golf tactics after hitting shots both left and right.
