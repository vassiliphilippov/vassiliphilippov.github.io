macaron

noun
sweet meringue-based almond confection
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmæk.əˈrɑn / (AmE), / ˌmak.əˈrɒn / (BrE)
A sweet meringue-based confection made with egg white, sugar, almond flour, and food coloring, often featuring a creamy filling sandwiched between two delicate shells.

Examples

A macaron dessert
Each macaron was filled with a delicious raspberry ganache.
I bought a box of colorful macarons from the French bakery.
Making perfect macarons requires patience and precision in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macaroon - a different type of small cake, typically coconut-based
