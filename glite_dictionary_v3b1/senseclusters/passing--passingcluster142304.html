passing

adjective
satisfactory; allowing one to pass (an exam, inspection, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪŋ / (BrE)
satisfactory, especially in allowing one to pass an examination, inspection, or test.

Examples

Passing grade
A score of 60 percent or above is considered passing in this course.
He worked hard all semester to ensure a passing mark in mathematics.
She was relieved to discover that she had received a passing grade on her final exam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: failing - not meeting the required standard; not passing
