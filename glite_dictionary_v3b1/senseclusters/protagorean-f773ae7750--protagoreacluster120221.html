Protagorean

noun
follower of Protagoras
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˌtæɡəˈriːən / (AmE), / prəʊˌtæɡəˈriːən / (BrE)
a person who follows or supports the philosophy of Protagoras, especially the idea that truth is relative to each individual

Examples

A Protagorean thinker
As a dedicated Protagorean, he often debated that everyone's viewpoint holds equal value.
The professor explained how a Protagorean might interpret conflicting accounts of history.
During the discussion, she revealed her Protagorean stance on the nature of truth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolutist - a person who believes in absolute principles in philosophy or ethics
  • synonym: relativist - a person who believes that truth or morality is not absolute
