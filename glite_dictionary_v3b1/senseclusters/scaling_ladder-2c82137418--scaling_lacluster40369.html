scaling ladder

noun
ladder used to climb walls in attacks
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪlɪŋ ˌlædər / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlɪŋ ˌladə / (BrE)
a portable ladder used for climbing over walls, especially in a military attack or siege

Examples

A scaling ladder for walls
A sturdy scaling ladder was essential equipment for medieval armies attempting to storm a city.
The castle's defenders pushed away every scaling ladder their attackers brought forward.
The soldiers used a scaling ladder to breach the fortress walls during the siege.
