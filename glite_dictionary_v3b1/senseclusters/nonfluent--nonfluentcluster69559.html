nonfluent

adjective
slow, effortful speech
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈfluːənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈfluːənt / (BrE)
not able to speak or write smoothly or easily; speech or language that is slow, effortful, or lacking normal flow

Examples

Nonfluent speech
The patient exhibited nonfluent speech after suffering a stroke.
Doctors described her language as nonfluent, with short and broken sentences.
Therapists worked to improve his nonfluent communication abilities during rehabilitation.
He is a nonfluent speaker of French, often searching for the right words.
After her injury, her speech became noticeably nonfluent and hesitant.
The student’s nonfluent writing made the essay difficult to understand.
