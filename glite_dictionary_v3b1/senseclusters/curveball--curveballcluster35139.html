curveball

noun
curving baseball pitch
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝːvˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈkɜːvˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a baseball pitch thrown with spin so that it curves as it approaches the batter

Examples

A fast curveball
The pitcher threw a perfect curveball that fooled the batter completely.
Hitting a curveball requires quick reflexes and good timing.
He practiced his curveball every day to improve his pitching skills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fastball - a baseball pitch thrown at high speed with little curve
  • synonym: slider - a baseball pitch that slides laterally with less downward break
