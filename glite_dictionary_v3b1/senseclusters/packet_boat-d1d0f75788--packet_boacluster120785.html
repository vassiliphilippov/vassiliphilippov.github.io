packet boat

noun
boat for carrying mail and passengers
Pronunciation: / ˈpækət boʊt / (AmE), / ˈpækɪt bəʊt / (BrE)
a boat used for regularly transporting mail, and sometimes passengers or goods, along a set route

Examples

A packet boat route
In the 19th century, the packet boat was a common sight on England's canals, delivering mail and goods.
The invention of the steam-powered packet boat greatly improved mail delivery speed.
They traveled to the city by packet boat, enjoying the scenic views along the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mail boat - a boat for carrying mail, especially in coastal or river areas
