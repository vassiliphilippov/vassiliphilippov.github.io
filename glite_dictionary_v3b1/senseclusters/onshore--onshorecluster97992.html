onshore

adjective, adverb
(of wind) from sea to land
3.5 0.84/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ënÀå É…îr / (AmE), / Àà…ín É…îÀê /; / Àà…ínÀå É…îÀê / (BrE)
on or moving toward the land (especially from the sea), or located on the land near the sea

Examples

Synonyms

  • shoreside
    4.1 0.02/m
    shoreside means located right at the coast edge, while onshore describes things on land near the sea.
  • shoreward
    3.9 0.06/m
    shoreward means moving toward the shore from the water, while onshore can mean either located on land or moving toward land.
  • ashore
    1.5 5.70/m
    ashore means moving onto or standing on the shore from the water, while onshore usually refers to being on land near the sea.
  • landward
    6.7 0.02/m
    landward means directed away from the sea toward land in general, while onshore refers specifically to being on land near the coast.
