onshore

adjective, adverb
(of wind) from sea to land
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑnˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɒnʃɔː /; / ˈɒnˌʃɔː / (BrE)
on or moving toward the land (especially from the sea), or located on the land near the sea

Examples

Onshore wind
Beachgoers were relieved when an onshore wind pushed the smoke from wildfires away from shore.
The onshore breeze brought cool, salty air to the coastal village.
When the onshore gale intensified, boats hurried back to the harbor.
Lifeguards keep a close watch on onshore swimming areas during the busy season.
The onshore factories depend on goods arriving by ship from overseas.
The onshore wind brought the scent of salt and seaweed across the dunes.
After months at sea, the crew was eager to spend time onshore.
Most of the fishermen returned onshore before the storm hit.
The wind began to blow onshore in the early afternoon.

Related Terms
