co-sleeping

verb
to share sleeping space
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈsliːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈsliːpɪŋ / (BrE)
to sleep in close physical proximity to another person, especially sharing the same bed or sleeping area.

Examples

To co-sleep with baby
Many parents practice co-sleeping with their infants to make nighttime feeding easier.
Some families choose co-sleeping for cultural or personal reasons.
Experts debate the safety and benefits of co-sleeping with young children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bed-sharing - to share a bed with another person
  • synonym: room-sharing - to sleep in the same room as another person
  • antonym: sleep alone - to sleep without anyone else nearby
