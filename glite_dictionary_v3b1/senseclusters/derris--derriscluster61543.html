derris

noun
tropical Asian vine genus used for insecticides
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian woody vines whose roots yield the insecticide rotenone and are used as sources of native fish and arrow poisons

Examples

Derris plant roots
Scientists have studied derris extract for its potential agricultural applications.
Some derris species are commonly grown for their use in natural insecticides.
The villagers prepared a poison from the roots of derris to catch fish in the river.
