order Spirochaetales

noun
taxonomic order of spiral-shaped bacteria
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɚ ˌspaɪroʊˈkiːteɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌspaɪrə(ʊ)ˈkiːteɪliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of higher bacteria characterized by slender, spiral, rod-shaped forms, known for including spirochetes.

Examples

The order Spirochaetales
Many pathogenic bacteria, such as those causing Lyme disease and syphilis, belong to the order Spirochaetales.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within the order Spirochaetales to better understand their role in human disease.
The order Spirochaetales includes spirochetes characterized by their unique spiral morphology and motility.
