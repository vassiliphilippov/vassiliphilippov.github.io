crutching

noun
sheep wool removal
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of removing wool from around a sheep's tail and hind legs to maintain hygiene and prevent flystrike

Examples

Sheep crutching
The farmer performed crutching on the flock to help prevent fly infestations during the summer.
Regular crutching is important for the health and cleanliness of sheep.
Before lambing season, the workers completed crutching to ensure the ewes were clean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shearing - the act of cutting off all the wool from a sheep
