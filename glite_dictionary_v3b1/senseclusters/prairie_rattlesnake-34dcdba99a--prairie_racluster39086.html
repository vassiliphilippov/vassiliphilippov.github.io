prairie rattlesnake

noun
a venomous North American rattlesnake species
7.3 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛri ˈrætəlˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri ˈrætəlˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a venomous rattlesnake (Crotalus viridis) widely distributed in grassland regions between the Mississippi River and the Rocky Mountains in North America.

Examples

The prairie rattlesnake
Much of eastern Colorado is home to the prairie rattlesnake, which often basks on rocky outcrops.
Researchers tracked the movement of a single prairie rattlesnake over the course of the summer.
The prairie rattlesnake uses its rattle as a warning to ward off potential threats.
