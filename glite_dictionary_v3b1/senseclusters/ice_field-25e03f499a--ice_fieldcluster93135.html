ice field

noun
large, flat area of floating sea ice
Pronunciation: / aɪs fiːld / (AmE), / aɪs fiːld / (BrE)
a large, flat expanse of floating ice on the sea, larger than an ice floe

Examples

A large ice field
Satellite images revealed a massive ice field drifting toward the shipping lanes.
Seals could often be spotted resting on the flat ice field during the expedition.
The research team set up camp on the vast ice field in the Arctic.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ice floe - a large, flat piece of floating ice smaller than an ice field
