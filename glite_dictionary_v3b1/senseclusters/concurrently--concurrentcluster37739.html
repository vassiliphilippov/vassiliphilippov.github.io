concurrently

adverb
at the same time; simultaneously
5.8 2.64/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàk…ùÀê.…ônt.li / (AmE), / k…ônÀàk år.…ônt.li / (BrE)
at the same time as something else; overlapping in duration

Examples

To work concurrently
She was enrolled in two degree programs concurrently, which kept her very busy.
The software updates will run concurrently on all company computers.
The two projects were developed concurrently to maximize efficiency and resource sharing.

Synonyms

  • simultaneously
    4.2 28/m
    simultaneously happens at the exact same moment, while concurrently often suggests actions overlap over a period of time.
  • at the same time
    1.0 75/m
    at the same time is a common phrase for events happening together, while concurrently often sounds more formal and implies overlapping durations.
  • concomitantly
    8.6 0.43/m
    concomitantly is a formal word used in academic contexts, while concurrently is more common in general use and speech.
  • at once
    1.8 19/m
    at once can mean immediately or together, while concurrently specifically means events occur at the same time and overlap.
