catclaw

noun
tropical shrub with curved seed pods
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌklɔ / (AmE), / ˈkætˌklɔː / (BrE)
An erect shrub native to the West Indies and Florida, with few or no spines, racemes of white to yellow flowers, and curved pointed seed pods.

Examples

The catclaw shrub
Botanists studied the flowering patterns of the local catclaw population.
The curved pods of catclaw plants can easily catch on passing animals.
We discovered a catclaw growing by the edge of the forest trail.
