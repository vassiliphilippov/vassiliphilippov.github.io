Examples

To be distinctly clear
His voice was distinctly heard over the noise of the crowd.
The two species are distinctly different in their appearance and behavior.
The weather turned distinctly colder after the sun went down.
It was distinctly stated in the contract that payment was due by the end of the month.
She could distinctly hear voices coming from the next room, even though the door was closed.
The mountain peak was distinctly visible against the bright morning sky.
He spoke distinctly so that everyone could understand his message.

This painting is distinctly different from his earlier works.

Synonyms

  • clearly
    1.0 102/m
    clearly means something is easy to see or understand in general, while distinctly stresses that it stands out or is particularly noticeable.
  • unmistakably
    4.1 2.05/m
    unmistakably leaves no doubt and feels very strong, while distinctly means clear or easy to notice but is less forceful.
  • plainly
    1.8 5.83/m
    plainly means something is simple, direct, or obvious, while distinctly points to something that stands out and is easy to notice.
