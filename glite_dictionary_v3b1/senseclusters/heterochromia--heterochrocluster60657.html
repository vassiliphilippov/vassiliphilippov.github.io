heterochromia

noun
different colored eyes
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈkroʊmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈkrəʊmiə / (BrE)
a condition in which an individual has different colored eyes or different colors within one eye

Examples

Heterochromia in eyes
The actress's heterochromia made her eyes especially memorable in close-up shots.
Some animals, like huskies, are known for their striking heterochromia.
Doctors confirmed the newborn had heterochromia after noticing one brown and one blue eye.

Related Terms

  • antonym: albinism - a condition causing lack of pigment in the skin, hair, and eyes
  • antonym: aniridia - absence of the iris in the eye
