Progressive Party

proper noun
US political party of 1912, founded by Roosevelt
6.5
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, founded by Theodore Roosevelt in 1912, also known as the Bull Moose Party

Examples

The Progressive Party 1912
In the 1912 election, the Progressive Party promoted social reforms and direct democracy initiatives.
The Progressive Party was established by Theodore Roosevelt after a split in the Republican Party.
The emblem of the Progressive Party was the bull moose, symbolizing strength and vigor.
