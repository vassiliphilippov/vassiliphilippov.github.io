Sium sisarum

noun
Asiatic herb with sweet edible root
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪəm sɪˈserəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪəm sɪˈsarəm / (BrE)
an Asiatic herb cultivated in Europe for its sweet, edible tuberous root, also known as skirret

Examples

Sium sisarum root
Sium sisarum is valued in some cuisines for its sweet, starchy roots.
Historically, Sium sisarum was used as a food source in medieval Europe.
The gardener decided to grow Sium sisarum in the vegetable patch this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skirret - an edible root vegetable derived from Sium sisarum
