rofecoxib

noun

Pronunciation: /roʊ.fə.ˈkɑːk.sɪb/ /; / /roʊ.fə.ˈkɔːk.sɪb/ (AmE), /rəʊ.fə.ˈkɒk.sɪb/ (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (COX-2 inhibitor), formerly marketed as Vioxx, used to relieve pain and inflammation before being withdrawn in 2004 due to safety concerns.
