trichopteran

noun
an insect of the order Trichoptera (caddis fly)
8.0 8.9
Pronunciation: /trɪ.ˈkɑːp.tər.ən/ (AmE), /trɪ.ˈkɒp.tər.ən/ (BrE)
An insect belonging to the order Trichoptera, commonly known as caddis flies, whose aquatic larvae build protective cases.

Examples

A trichopteran insect
A trichopteran can often be distinguished by its hairy wings, unlike most other aquatic insects.
Scientists observed several species of trichopteran in the freshwater stream ecosystem.
The larvae of the trichopteran spend most of their time building protective cases from sand and debris.
