wash

form by erosion (water)

to flow against, over, or carry something away by the action of water; or to gradually shape land by water

Examples

To wash away soil
Flash floods can wash gullies into what was once flat land.
Over time, the ocean has washed deep cliffs into the rocky shore.
The constant current will eventually wash a channel through the sandbar.
Several cabins were washed away by the rising river.

The waves washed the sand farther up the shore.

Waves wash the rocks at the foot of the cliff every morning.
