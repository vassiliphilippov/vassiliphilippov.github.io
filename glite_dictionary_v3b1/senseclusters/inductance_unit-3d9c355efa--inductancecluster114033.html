inductance unit

noun
unit for measuring inductance
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdʌk.təns ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʌk.təns ˈjuː.nət/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdʌk.təns ˈjuː.nɪt/ (BrE)
a unit for measuring the property of an electric circuit that causes an electromotive force to be induced by a changing current

Examples

Inductance unit henry
Engineers often convert between different inductance units, such as henries and millihenries.
The inductance unit in the International System of Units is called the henry.
