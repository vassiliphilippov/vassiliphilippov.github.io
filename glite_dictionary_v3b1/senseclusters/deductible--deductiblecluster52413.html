deductible

noun
amount subtracted from taxable income
7.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʌktəbl / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktəbl / (BrE)
an amount of money that can be subtracted from your total income or tax when calculating the amount of tax you owe, especially for income tax purposes

Examples

Tax deductible
She asked if her charitable donations would count as a deductible this year.
The accountant explained which business expenses were considered deductible for tax purposes.
Your medical expenses may be listed as a deductible on your income tax form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allowance - a portion of income on which no tax is paid
  • synonym: exemption - an amount allowed by law that reduces taxable income
  • antonym: taxable - subject to being taxed; not deductible
