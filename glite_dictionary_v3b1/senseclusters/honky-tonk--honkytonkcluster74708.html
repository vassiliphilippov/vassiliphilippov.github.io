honky-tonk

noun
cheap bar or dance hall
8.4 9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑŋki tɑŋk / (AmE), / ˈhɒŋki tɒŋk / (BrE)
A cheap bar, club, or dance hall, especially one that features country music and dancing.

Examples

A honky-tonk bar
Many old honky-tonks have a lively, welcoming atmosphere.
She first performed in a Texas honky-tonk before finding success in Nashville.
The band played all night at the crowded honky-tonk on Main Street.
