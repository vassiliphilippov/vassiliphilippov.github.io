fissure of Sylvius

noun
groove separating frontal and temporal brain lobes
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃɚ əv ˈsɪl.vi.əs / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə əv ˈsɪl.vɪ.əs / (BrE)
the deep groove separating the frontal and temporal lobes of the brain in both hemispheres; also known as the lateral sulcus

Examples

The fissure of Sylvius
Damage near the fissure of Sylvius can affect language and auditory processing.
During surgery, the fissure of Sylvius is used as a landmark to access deeper brain structures.
The fissure of Sylvius is easily identified on a brain scan as a prominent groove.
