hint

noun
indication of potential opportunity
Pronunciation: / hɪnt / (AmE), / hɪnt / (BrE)
an indirect or subtle suggestion or clue

Examples

A helpful hint
He was grateful for the hint that helped him get an interview for his dream job.
She received a hint from a friend about a job opening at the company.
The detective followed every hint in hopes of finding the missing artifact.
He didn't take the hint that it was time to leave.
If you need help, just give me a hint and I'll assist you.
She dropped a hint about what she wanted for her birthday.
She gave a hint that she wanted to leave the party early.
The sky had a hint of orange as the sun began to set.
There was not even a hint of excitement in his voice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clue - a piece of evidence or information used in the detection of a crime or solving of a mystery
  • antonym: direct statement - a clear and straightforward remark or declaration
  • antonym: explicit statement - a direct and clear statement, opposite of hint
  • synonym: insinuation - an unpleasant hint or suggestion of something bad
  • synonym: lead - information pointing toward a possible opportunity, especially in work or investigation
  • synonym: suggestion - an idea or proposal put forward for consideration
  • synonym: tip - a useful piece of advice, especially about how to do something or about the likely outcome of something
