reedit

verb
to edit again
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛdɪt / (BrE)
to edit something again, usually to improve or correct it

Examples

To reedit a manuscript
After receiving feedback, she decided to reedit her manuscript before sending it to the publisher.
The director had to reedit the film to meet the new guidelines.
He will reedit the report to include the latest data and corrections.
After receiving feedback, she decided to reedit her short story before submitting it again.
The documentary was reedited to include new interviews and updated information.
He had to reedit the video several times to fix technical errors.

  • synonym: revise - to make changes to improve or correct something
