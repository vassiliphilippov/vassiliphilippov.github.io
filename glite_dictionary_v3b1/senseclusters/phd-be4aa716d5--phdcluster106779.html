PhD

noun
doctorate degree; highest academic qualification
4.8 19/m
Pronunciation: / pi e…™t É Ààdi / (AmE), / piÀê e…™t É ÀàdiÀê / (BrE)
a doctorate degree awarded for advanced academic research, typically after several years of postgraduate study and completion of a dissertation

Examples

A PhD degree
Applicants must have a PhD or equivalent experience to qualify for the research grant.
He was awarded an honorary PhD for his contributions to medical science.
She spent five years earning her PhD in physics at a prestigious university.

Synonyms

  • Doctor of Philosophy
    5.4 0.15/m
    Doctor of Philosophy is the full formal name used in diplomas and ceremonies, while PhD is the common academic abbreviation.
  • Ph.D.
    5.5 0.00/m
    Ph.D. is the punctuated form often used in formal writing and older texts, while PhD is the modern, compact abbreviation.
  • DPhil
    7.3 0.01/m
    DPhil is the British term used by some universities like Oxford, while PhD is the widely used international name for the same degree.
