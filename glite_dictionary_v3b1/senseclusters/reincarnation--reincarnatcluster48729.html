reincarnation

noun
the rebirth of a soul in a new body
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˌkɑːrˈneɪʃən /; / ˌriːɪnˌkɑrˈneɪʃən /; / ˌriːɪnkɑrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˌkɑːˈneɪʃ(ə)n /; / ˌriːɪnkɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the belief or concept that a soul or spirit is reborn in a new body after death, especially in religions like Hinduism and Buddhism

Many cultures hold a deep belief in reincarnation and the idea of life after death.

Many religions teach the concept of reincarnation as part of the cycle of life and death.

Buddhist teachings discuss reincarnation and the cycle of existence known as samsara.
Many Hindus believe in reincarnation and hope to be reborn into a better life.
The concept of reincarnation offers a different perspective on life and death.
