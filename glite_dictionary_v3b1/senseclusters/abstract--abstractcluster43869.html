(of art) not representing external reality or concrete objects, but seeking to achieve its effect using shapes, colors, and textures.

Examples

Abstract painting
Her latest exhibition features a striking abstract sculpture in the center of the gallery.
The artist is famous for his abstract paintings that use bold colors and shapes.
Unlike his earlier work, this piece is entirely abstract with no recognizable subjects.

Synonyms

  • nonrepresentational
    8.9 0.09/m
    nonrepresentational art shows no real objects and focuses on shape and color, while abstract art seeks effect through form and texture.
  • nonfigurative
    8.4 0.06/m
    nonfigurative paintings avoid recognizable figures and stress pure forms and colors, while abstract art uses shapes and textures to create effect.
  • nonobjective
    8.3 0.08/m
    nonobjective work contains no clear objects and emphasizes line, color, or pattern, while abstract art still aims to suggest feeling through form.
  • nonmimetic
    9.6 0.03/m
    nonmimetic art does not try to copy nature and often uses design and color experiments, while abstract art seeks visual effect via shapes.
  • abstractionist
    9.5 0.01/m
    abstractionist describes artists or their style who favor abstraction, while abstract refers to art that does not depict real objects.

  • synonym: nonrepresentational - not representing real objects or scenes
