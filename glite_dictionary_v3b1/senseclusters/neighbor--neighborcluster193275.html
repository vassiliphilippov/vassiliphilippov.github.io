neighbor

verb
be situated next to someone or something
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪbər /; / ˈneɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈneɪbə / (BrE)
to be located next to or near another person, place, or thing

Examples

Shops neighbor the park
Several restaurants neighbor the new library, making lunch options plentiful.
The apartment buildings neighbor the hospital in the city center.
Two farms neighbor the town on either side.
France neighbors several countries, including Germany and Spain.
The newly built hospital neighbors several schools and residential areas.
The small village neighbors a scenic national park, making it a popular tourist destination.
A small village neighboured the ancient castle on the hill.
Her house neighboured the city park, filling her mornings with birdsong.
The school neighbours a quaint little bakery frequented by students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjoin - to be next to or joined with
  • synonym: border - to share a boundary with
