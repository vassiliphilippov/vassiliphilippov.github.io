wart

noun
small hard skin growth
3.2 1.3
Pronunciation: / wɔrt / (AmE), / wɔːt / (BrE)
a small, hard, raised lump on the skin or surface of a plant or animal, usually caused by a virus

Examples

Skin wart
A wart can be contagious if touched or scratched.
Children sometimes develop warts on their hands and feet.
He went to the doctor to have a wart removed from his finger.
He found a small wart on the surface of the leaf while gardening.
Some frogs have visible warts on their backs as a natural feature.
The dermatologist examined the wart to determine if it needed to be removed.
She decided to have the wart on her hand removed.
The doctor examined the wart and suggested a simple treatment.
Warts can appear anywhere on the body, but my wart was on my knee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw on the skin or surface
  • synonym: bump - a round swelling on a surface; a lump
  • antonym: mole - a small, dark skin mark or growth, not caused by a virus
  • synonym: nodule - a small swelling or aggregation of cells in the body or on a plant
  • synonym: verruca - a wart, especially on the sole of the foot
