oxidation number

noun
degree of oxidation of an atom or ion
2.8 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɑːk.sə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən ˈnʌm.bər/ /; / /ɑk.sɪ.ˈdeɪ.ʃən ˈnʌm.bər/ (AmE), /ɒk.sɪ.ˈdeɪ.ʃən ˈnʌm.bə/ /; / /ɒk.sə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən ˈnʌm.bə/ (BrE)
a number assigned to an atom in a compound or ion representing the degree of oxidation, equal to the ionic charge for simple atoms or ions

Examples

The oxidation number value
Assigning the correct oxidation number is essential for balancing redox reactions.
In water, the oxidation number of hydrogen is +1, while that of oxygen is -2.
The oxidation number of an element can help predict its chemical behavior.
