drivel

verb
to talk nonsense
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvəl / (BrE)
to talk in a silly, foolish, or meaningless way

Examples

To drivel nonsense
He started to drivel about things no one understood during the meeting.
When she is tired, she sometimes drivels without realizing it.
Please stop driveling and get to the point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish way
  • antonym: make sense - to speak in a clear and logical way
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
