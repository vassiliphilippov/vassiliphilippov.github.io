achromat

noun
color-blind person or lens
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪkroʊˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈeɪkrəʊˌmat / (BrE)
a person who is completely color-blind or a lens designed to correct color distortion in optical devices

Examples

Color-blind achromat
The scientist used an achromat to reduce color errors in the microscope.
People with achromat vision see the world only in shades of gray.
Modern cameras often use an achromat to improve image clarity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: achromatopsia - complete color blindness
  • antonym: chromat - a lens that does not correct color distortion
