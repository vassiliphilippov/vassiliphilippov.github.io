in-law

noun
relative by marriage
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn lɔ / (AmE), / ˈɪn lɔː / (BrE)
A relative by marriage, especially the parent or sibling of one's spouse.

Examples

In-law family
After the wedding, he became my official in-law.
She talks to her in-laws almost every day on the phone.
We're having dinner with my in-laws this weekend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: step-relative - a relative related by the remarriage of a parent
