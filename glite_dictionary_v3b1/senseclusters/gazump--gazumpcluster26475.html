gazump

verb
refuse a sale to take a higher offer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈzʌmp / (AmE), / ɡəˈzʌmp / (BrE)
(British) to refuse to complete a property sale with a previously accepted offer, usually to accept a higher offer from someone else

Examples

To gazump a buyer
Buyers are often frustrated when they are suddenly gazumped during the final stages of purchasing property.
The seller decided to gazump us at the last minute, leaving us without a home.
We thought the house was ours, but we were gazumped by someone offering more money.
It's frustrating when someone tries to gazump you after months of negotiation.
The seller decided to gazump us at the last minute, demanding an extra £10,000 for the house.
We thought the deal was done, but the estate agent informed us we'd been gazumped.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gazunder - to lower a previously agreed price before contract exchange
