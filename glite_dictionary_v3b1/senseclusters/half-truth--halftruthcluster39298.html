half-truth

noun
partially true statement meant to mislead
2.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhæf.truːθ/ (AmE), /ˈhɑːf.truːθ/ (BrE)
A statement that includes some truth but deliberately omits important facts or distorts the truth in order to deceive or mislead.

Examples

A half-truth statement
By sharing only part of the story, she gave a clever half-truth.
He told me a half-truth to avoid getting into trouble.
Political speeches are sometimes filled with half-truths and omissions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lie - a false statement with intent to deceive
  • degree higher: truth - a true statement or the state of being true
