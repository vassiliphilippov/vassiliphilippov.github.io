drove

noun
broad-edged stonemason's chisel
Pronunciation: / droʊv / (AmE), / drəʊv / (BrE)
A stonemason's chisel with a broad edge, used for dressing and smoothing stone surfaces.

Examples

Stonecutter's drove
Each drove needs to be kept sharp for precise stonework.
He demonstrated the use of a drove during the restoration of the old cathedral.
The stonemason selected a drove to smooth the surface of the granite block.
