jib

verb
to shift a sail or boom sharply from side to side (nautical)
9.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /dʒɪb/ (AmE), /dʒɪb/ (BrE)
(of a sail or boom) to shift abruptly from one side of a ship to the other, especially when sailing before the wind.

Examples

The sail jibbed suddenly
As the yacht accelerated, the headsail jibbed and flapped violently in the wind.
The mainsail suddenly jibbed as the wind direction changed.
Watch out when the boom jibs so you don't get hit.
