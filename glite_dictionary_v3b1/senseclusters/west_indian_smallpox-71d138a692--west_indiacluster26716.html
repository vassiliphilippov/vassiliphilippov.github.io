West Indian smallpox

noun
mild smallpox variant in the West Indies
Pronunciation: / wɛst ˈɪndiən ˈsmɔlˌpɑks / (AmE), / west ˈɪndɪən ˈsmɒlˌpɒks / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent strain of the virus, historically observed in the West Indies.

Examples

Mild West Indian smallpox
Doctors studied West Indian smallpox to understand why it was not as lethal as other forms.
During the 18th century, West Indian smallpox was considered less deadly than other strains.
Historical accounts describe West Indian smallpox outbreaks as being much milder in symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smallpox - a serious, contagious viral disease eradicated in the 20th century
