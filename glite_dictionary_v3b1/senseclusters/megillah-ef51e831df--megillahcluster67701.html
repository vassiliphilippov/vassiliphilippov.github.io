Megillah

noun
scroll containing Book of Esther (Judaism)
Pronunciation: / məˈɡɪlə / (AmE), / məˈɡɪlə / (BrE)
A scroll of parchment containing the biblical Book of Esther, traditionally read in synagogues during the Jewish festival of Purim.

Examples

The Megillah scroll
Each family brought their own Megillah to the synagogue for the reading.
The ornate case protected the precious Megillah inside.
The rabbi read the entire Megillah during the Purim service.
