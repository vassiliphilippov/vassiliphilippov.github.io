docklands

noun
urban dock area
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑkˌlændz / (AmE), / ˈdɒkˌlændz / (BrE)
an area of a city, usually near water, where docks and shipping facilities are or were located, often redeveloped for housing or business

Examples

The docklands area
Many cities have redeveloped their docklands into vibrant neighborhoods with shops and restaurants.
The old docklands were once filled with ships and warehouses but are now home to modern offices.
Tourists enjoy walking along the river in the renovated docklands district.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harbor - a place on the coast where ships may moor safely
  • synonym: waterfront - the part of a city or town next to a body of water
