dry walling

noun
installation of gypsum drywall panels
3.4
Pronunciation: /draɪ ˈwɔː.lɪŋ/ /; / /draɪ ˈwɑː.lɪŋ/ (AmE), /draɪ ˈwɔː.lɪŋ/ (BrE)
The installation or construction of interior walls and ceilings using gypsum-based panels (drywall or plasterboard), including fixing, taping, and finishing joints.

Examples

Interior dry walling
The contractor scheduled the dry walling for next week before painting began.
After removing the old lath, they started dry walling the upstairs bedrooms that afternoon.
Skilled carpenters often handle the metal framing used in dry walling to ensure level walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dry-lining - the British term for lining interior walls with plasterboard or similar panels instead of wet plaster
  • synonym: plasterboarding - the process of fixing plasterboard (plasterboard installation) to form internal walls or ceilings
