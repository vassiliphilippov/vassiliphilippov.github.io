poison

noun
something that harms or destroys
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzən / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzn̩ / (BrE)
A substance or influence that causes harm, destruction, or corruption, often used metaphorically.

Examples

Poison of hate
Racism is a poison that damages the fabric of society.
The spread of misinformation acted as a poison, undermining trust in the community.
Unchecked jealousy can become a poison in any relationship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts poison or its effects
  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance, especially produced by living cells or organisms
