unsensational

adjective
not sensational; lacking excitement
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌsɛnˈseɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.sɛnˈseɪ.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
not intended or likely to provoke strong interest, excitement, or emotion; lacking sensational qualities.

Examples

An unsensational story
Although the movie was unsensational, critics praised its realistic portrayal of everyday life.
Her unsensational approach to storytelling brought a refreshing honesty to the documentary.
The journalist's unsensational report focused on the facts rather than exaggeration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sensational - exceptionally exciting or intended to shock or impress
