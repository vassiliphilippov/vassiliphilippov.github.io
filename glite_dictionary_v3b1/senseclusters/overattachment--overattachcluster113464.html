overattachment

noun
excessive legal seizure
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərəˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərəˈtætʃmənt / (BrE)
the legal seizure or attachment of property that exceeds lawful or reasonable limits

Examples

Court overattachment
The court was criticized for its overattachment of the defendant's assets, which far exceeded the debt owed.
Lawyers argued that the overattachment of property violated the principle of fairness in the legal process.
After the overattachment was overturned, the owner regained access to his equipment.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: attachment - the legal seizure of property to secure a judgment
