romance

verb
have a love affair with
1.8 4.3
Pronunciation: /roʊ.ˈmæns/ /; / /rə.ˈmæns/ (AmE), /rəʊ.ˈmæns/ /; / /rə.ˈmæns/ (BrE)
to try to attract, court, or woo someone romantically; to behave in a flirtatious or amorous way

Examples

To romance a girl
He tried to romance her with flowers and poetry.
Many celebrities romance each other on movie sets.
She was romancing an artist she met last summer.
He tried to romance the waitress, but she wasn't interested in his jokes.
She knew he was only romancing her to impress his friends.
Some people romance casually at parties, looking for a little fun.
He spent months trying to romance her with flowers and poetry.
In the movie, the main character boldly romances his childhood sweetheart.
She was unsure whether he was genuinely interested or just romancing her for fun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: court - try to develop a romantic relationship with someone
  • synonym: flirt - to behave as though attracted to someone, without serious intent
  • synonym: woo - try to gain the love of someone, especially with a view to marriage
