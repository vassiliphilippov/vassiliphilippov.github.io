speckled alder

noun
North American shrub with rust-colored downy shoots
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛkəld ˈɔldɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɛkəld ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a common shrub of Canada and the northeastern United States, with shoots scattered with rust-colored down; also known as the tag alder

Examples

The speckled alder shrub
I recognized the speckled alder by its distinctive rusty-brown branches and small oval leaves.
Scientists studied the way speckled alder helps restore riparian habitats after disturbances.
The wetland was dense with speckled alder along its edges, making it a haven for wildlife.
