fragmentize

verb
break into fragments
Pronunciation: / ˈfræɡ.mənˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræɡ.mən.taɪz /; / ˈfræɡ.məntaɪz / (BrE)
to break or cause to break into fragments or pieces

Examples

To fragmentize glass
If you drop that vase, it will fragmentize on the floor.
Over time, the ancient wall began to fragmentize due to harsh weather conditions.
Some artists fragmentize images deliberately to create abstract pieces.
Over time, exposure to harsh conditions can fragmentise ancient pottery.
The bomb explosion fragmentised the old building instantly.
The document was fragmentised into several unrelated sections after the editing process.
