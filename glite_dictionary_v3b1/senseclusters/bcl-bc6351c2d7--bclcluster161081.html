BCL

noun
bacterial cell lysate
Pronunciation: / bi si ɛl / (AmE), / bi si ɛl / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'bacterial cell lysate', a substance made by breaking open bacterial cells, often used in medical research

Examples

BCL in research
The scientists added BCL to the culture to observe its effect on the immune cells.
Many experiments require BCL to analyze bacterial protein interactions.
After preparing the BCL, the team measured its concentration before use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lysate - a fluid containing the contents of lysed cells
