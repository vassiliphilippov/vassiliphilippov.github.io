hypokinesis

noun
reduced movement
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊkɪˈniːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊkɪˈniːsɪs / (BrE)
a condition in which there is reduced movement or activity, especially of muscles or organs

Examples

Reduced hypokinesis movement
The scan revealed hypokinesis in the patient's left ventricle after the heart attack.
Doctors monitor for hypokinesis to assess muscle function in neurological disorders.
Physical therapy can help improve hypokinesis caused by prolonged inactivity.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: akinesis - absence of movement
  • degree higher: hyperkinesis - excessive movement or activity
