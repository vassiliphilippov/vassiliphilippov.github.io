matchbook

noun
small folder of paper matches
Pronunciation: / ˈmætʃˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈmatʃˌbʊk / (BrE)
A small folder or booklet containing a row of paper safety matches, usually with a striking surface on the outside.

Examples

A matchbook cover
He collects vintage matchbooks from old hotels and restaurants.
Many diners in the past handed out free matchbooks as souvenirs.
She pulled a matchbook from her purse to light the candle.
