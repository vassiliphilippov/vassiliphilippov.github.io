bobsled

noun
long, narrow racing sled for multiple people
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑbˌslɛd /; / ˈbɑːbˌslɛd / (AmE), / ˈbɒb.slɛd / (BrE)
a long, narrow sled with runners and steering, used for racing down an ice track, usually carrying two or more people

Examples

Olympic bobsled race
After months of training, she finally got her chance to ride in a real bobsled.
The team raced down the icy track in their sleek bobsled.
Winter Olympic events include both the luge and the bobsled competitions.
In the early days, a bobsled required more teamwork to steer and brake.
The original bobsled was made by joining two toboggans together.
The team restored an antique bobsled for display at the museum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bobsleigh - another name for bobsled in British English
  • synonym: luge - a small, one- or two-person sled ridden in a supine position feet first
  • synonym: skeleton - a small, single-rider sled ridden face down head first
