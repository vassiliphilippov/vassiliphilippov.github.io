Ibn-Roshd

proper noun
Arabian philosopher and commentator on Aristotle
Pronunciation: / ibn ˈroʊʃd / (AmE), / ˌɪbn ˈrɒʃd / (BrE)
(1126–1198) An influential Arabian philosopher born in Spain, known for his detailed commentaries on Aristotle and his impact on medieval European philosophy. Also widely known as Averroes.

Examples

Ibn-Roshd philosopher Spain
Ibn-Roshd played a key role in introducing Aristotle’s ideas to Europe.
Many medieval philosophers were influenced by the works of Ibn-Roshd.
The writings of Ibn-Roshd are still studied by scholars of Islamic and Western philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Averroes - Latinized name of Ibn-Roshd, the philosopher
