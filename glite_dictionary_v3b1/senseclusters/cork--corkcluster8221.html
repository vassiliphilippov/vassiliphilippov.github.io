cork

noun
fishing float made of cork
9.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /kɔːrk/ (AmE), /kɔːk/ (BrE)
A small float, typically made of cork, attached to a fishing line to keep the bait at the desired depth.

Examples

Fishing cork
She watched the red cork bob up and down in the lake.
The fisherman attached a cork to his line to keep the bait floating near the surface.
When the fish bit, the cork dipped suddenly under the water.
