springtail

noun
minute wingless jumping insect
Pronunciation: / ˈsprɪŋˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋˌteɪl / (BrE)
a minute wingless insect with a special abdominal appendage for jumping, found in soil or on snow and water surfaces

Examples

Springtails jump high
Springtails can be seen jumping on the surface of melting snow in early spring.
Gardeners often find springtails in soil rich with organic matter.
When disturbed, a springtail can leap several centimeters using its forked tail-like appendage.
