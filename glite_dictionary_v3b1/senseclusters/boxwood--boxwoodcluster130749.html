boxwood

noun
hard wood from the box tree, used in fine woodworking
4.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːk.swʊd/ /; / /ˈbɑːks.wʊd/ (AmE), /ˈbɒk.swʊd/ /; / /ˈbɒks.wʊd/ (BrE)
the hard, close-grained, light yellow wood from the box tree, used for fine woodwork such as musical instruments, inlays, and engraving blocks

Examples

Boxwood inlay
Because of its hardness, boxwood is prized by craftsmen for delicate inlay work.
Detailed engravings were carved on blocks of boxwood for old printing presses.
The violin maker selected only the finest boxwood for the instrument's pegs.
