scalloping

noun
baking with cream sauce
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈskæləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæləpɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of cooking in which food, especially thinly sliced potatoes, is baked in a shallow dish with cream or sauce

Examples

Scalloping potatoes
The chef demonstrated scalloping by layering potatoes and pouring cream over them before baking.
Many comfort foods, like scalloping potatoes, are popular at family gatherings.
She learned the technique of scalloping vegetables in her grandmother's kitchen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gratin - a dish baked with a browned crust, often using cheese or breadcrumbs
  • antonym: roasting - cooking food uncovered in an oven or over a fire
