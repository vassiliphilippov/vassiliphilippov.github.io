overproof

adjective
strong alcoholic content
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpruːf / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpruːf / (BrE)
containing more alcohol than the standard proof, especially referring to spirits that are stronger than usual

Examples

Overproof rum
The bartender warned that the overproof rum was very strong.
He accidentally bought an overproof bottle, which was much stronger than he expected.
Some cocktails require overproof spirits to achieve their signature flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strong - having a high concentration of alcohol
