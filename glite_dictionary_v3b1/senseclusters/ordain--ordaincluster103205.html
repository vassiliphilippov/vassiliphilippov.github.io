ordain

verb
order or decree officially
6.9 1.79/m
Pronunciation: / …îrÀàde…™n / (AmE), / …îÀêÀàde…™n / (BrE)
to officially order or decree something, especially by authority or law

Examples

To ordain new rules
After much debate, the committee ordained a new set of rules.
The constitution ordains equal rights for all citizens.
The council ordained that no vehicles would be allowed in the square after dark.
A new policy was ordained to ensure equal opportunity for all citizens.
The constitution ordains the separation of church and state.
The council ordained that all public parks would close at dusk.

Synonyms

  • decree
    5.3 3.14/m

  • enact
    4.7 14/m

  • mandate
    4.9 16/m
    mandate is to officially require someone to do something or give authority, while ordain is to officially order or decree.
