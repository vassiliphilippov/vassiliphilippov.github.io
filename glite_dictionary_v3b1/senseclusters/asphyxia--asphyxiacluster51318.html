asphyxia

noun
lack of oxygen; suffocation condition
5.4 6.9
Pronunciation: /æs.ˈfɪk.sɪ.ə/ /; / /ə.ˈsfɪk.sɪ.ə/ (AmE), /æs.ˈfɪk.sɪ.ə/ /; / /ə.ˈsfɪk.sɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A condition caused by a lack of oxygen resulting in unconsciousness or death; commonly due to choking, drowning, or other means that prevent normal breathing.

Examples

Severe asphyxia case
Smoke from the fire caused asphyxia in several victims before help arrived.
The doctor explained that choking could result in asphyxia if the airway remained blocked.
Victims of drowning often die from asphyxia due to lack of oxygen.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hypoxia - deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching the tissues
  • synonym: suffocation - the state or process of dying from being deprived of air or unable to breathe
