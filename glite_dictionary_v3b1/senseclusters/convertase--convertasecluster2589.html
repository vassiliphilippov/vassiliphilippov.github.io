convertase

noun
enzyme that splits compounds into active forms
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝː.teɪs / (AmE), / kənˈvɜː.tæs / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the conversion of a compound, often splitting it into smaller, biologically active fragments

Examples

Complement convertase
Certain complement convertases play a critical role in immune system responses.
Researchers identified the convertase responsible for generating the active protein fragments.
The activation of protein C requires a specific convertase enzyme in the pathway.
