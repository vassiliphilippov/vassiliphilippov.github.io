throbber

noun
pulsating sound or object
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɑbər / (AmE), / ˈθrɒbə / (BrE)
a thing that throbs, especially a steady pulsating sound, vibration, or sensation

Examples

Engine throbber
A low throbber from the old engine made everyone uneasy as the ship approached port.
The throbber in the background of the song gave it a hypnotic quality.
He felt a dull throbber in his temples after the long, stressful day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hum - a low, continuous sound
  • synonym: pulse - a regular beat or rhythm, especially of sound or vibration
