Rio Bravo

proper noun
river forming US-Mexico boundary
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.oʊ ˈbrɑː.voʊ / (AmE), / ˌriː.əʊ ˈbrɑː.vəʊ / (BrE)
a river in North America forming part of the boundary between the United States and Mexico, known as the Rio Grande in the United States, and flowing into the Gulf of Mexico.

Examples

The Rio Bravo river
Farmers depend on the water from the Rio Bravo for irrigation.
Many rare species live along the banks of the Rio Bravo.
The Rio Bravo serves as a natural border between Texas and northern Mexico.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Rio Grande - river forming much of the US-Mexico border (US English)
