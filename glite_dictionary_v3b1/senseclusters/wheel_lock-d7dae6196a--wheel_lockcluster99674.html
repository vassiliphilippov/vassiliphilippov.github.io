wheel lock

noun
early firearm ignition mechanism
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːl lɑk / (AmE), / ˈwiːl lɒk / (BrE)
an early type of gunlock consisting of a rotating wheel that produces sparks to ignite the powder, used in firearms before the flintlock

Examples

A wheel lock gun
Hunters in the early modern period often relied on the reliability of the wheel lock mechanism.
She explained how a wheel lock worked by demonstrating its ability to produce sparks with a rotating steel wheel.
The museum displayed a 16th-century wheel lock pistol among its collection of antique firearms.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: flintlock - a later type of gunlock using a piece of flint to strike a spark
  • degree lower: matchlock - an early type of gunlock using a slow-burning match to ignite the powder
