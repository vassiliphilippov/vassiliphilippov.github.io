hither and thither

adverb
in various directions; from place to place
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪðər ənd ˈðɪðər /; / ˈhɪðɚ ənd ˈðɪðɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪðə ənd ˈðɪðə / (BrE)
in many different directions or places; moving or scattered about

Examples

Hither and thither around
During the move, boxes were stacked hither and thither around the house.
In the busy marketplace, people bustled hither and thither searching for bargains.
The children ran hither and thither across the playground, their laughter echoing.
Cars drove hither and thither as the parade disrupted traffic.
The leaves blew hither and thither in the autumn wind.
Tourists wandered hither and thither through the ancient ruins.
