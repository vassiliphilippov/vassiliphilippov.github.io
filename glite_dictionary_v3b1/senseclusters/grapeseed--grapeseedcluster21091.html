grapeseed

noun
seed of a grape
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪpˌsiːd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpˌsiːd / (BrE)
the small seed found inside a grape, often used to produce oil or extracts

Examples

A grapeseed oil
The chef prefers to use grapeseed oil because of its mild taste.
After eating the grapes, she collected each grapeseed to plant in her garden.
Many skincare products contain extracts from grapeseed for their antioxidant properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pit - the hard stone inside some fruits, such as peaches or cherries
  • synonym: seed - the part of a plant from which a new plant can grow
