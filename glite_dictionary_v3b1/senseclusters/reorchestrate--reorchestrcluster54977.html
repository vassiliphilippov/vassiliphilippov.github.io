reorchestrate

verb
to reorganize structure
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɔːrkɪstreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɔːkɪstreɪt / (BrE)
to reorganize or rearrange the coordination of parts, tasks, or people in a project, system, or process

Examples

To reorchestrate a project
The manager decided to reorchestrate the workflow to make the team more efficient.
After the new software was introduced, they had to reorchestrate their daily routines.
To meet the deadline, the project leader chose to reorchestrate the responsibilities among the staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reorganize - to arrange something again or differently
  • synonym: restructure - to organize something in a new way
