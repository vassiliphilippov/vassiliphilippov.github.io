-minded

x
having a certain attitude
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
used as a suffix to form adjectives meaning having a particular attitude, way of thinking, or interest.

Examples

Business-minded leader
He is very business-minded and always looks for new opportunities.
Her parents are quite narrow-minded about modern art.
The school encourages students to be open-minded and consider different viewpoints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -hearted - suffix forming adjectives meaning having a particular kind of heart or temperament
