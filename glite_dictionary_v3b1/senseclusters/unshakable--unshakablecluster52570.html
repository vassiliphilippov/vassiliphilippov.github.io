unshakable

adjective
firm and resolute
5.1 4.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃeɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃeɪkəbl / (BrE)
so firm, strong, or certain that it cannot be changed, weakened, or doubted

Examples

Unshakable confidence
Despite harsh criticism, he maintained an unshakable confidence in his decision.
Her unshakable faith inspired everyone around her to remain hopeful in difficult times.
The team's unshakable determination led them to victory against all odds.
He presented an unshakable argument that withstood every challenge.
Her unshakable alibi convinced the jury of her innocence.
The contract terms are unshakable, leaving no room for misinterpretation or loopholes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulletproof - impossible to refute or break, especially in arguments or claims
  • synonym: ironclad - utterly secure or unbreakable, without weaknesses
  • synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • antonym: shakable - capable of being shaken
  • synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
  • synonym: watertight - without any possibility of flaws or loopholes, especially arguments or alibis
  • antonym: wavering - not steady or resolute, fluctuating
