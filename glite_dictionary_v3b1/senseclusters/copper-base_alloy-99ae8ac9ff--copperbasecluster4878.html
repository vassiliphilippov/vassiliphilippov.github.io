copper-base alloy

noun
alloy mainly composed of copper
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːpər beɪs ˈælɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒpə beɪs ˈælɔɪ / (BrE)
an alloy whose principal component is copper, often combined with other elements such as zinc or tin to create specific properties

Examples

A copper-base alloy metal
Bronze is a type of copper-base alloy that has been used for thousands of years.
Engineers may choose a copper-base alloy for its corrosion resistance and electrical conductivity.
Modern coins are often made from copper-base alloy for durability and cost-effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brass - an alloy consisting mainly of copper and zinc
  • synonym: bronze - an alloy consisting primarily of copper and tin
