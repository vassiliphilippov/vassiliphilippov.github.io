Elopidae

noun
family of fishes (tarpons, ladyfishes)
Pronunciation: / əˈloʊpɪˌdiː /; / ɪˈlɑːpɪdi / (AmE), / ɛˈlɒpɪdiː /; / ɪˈləʊpɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of fishes that includes tarpons and ladyfishes, found in warm, tropical, or subtropical coastal waters

Examples

The Elopidae family
Many marine biologists study the diversity of Elopidae.
Tarpons and ladyfishes both belong to the family Elopidae.
The Elopidae are often found in coastal tropical and subtropical waters.
Scientists have studied the migratory patterns of several Elopidae species.
Tarpons, belonging to the Elopidae family, are popular game fish in tropical waters.
The Elopidae are distinguished by their elongated, silvery bodies and forked tails.
