European turkey oak

noun
species of large deciduous oak tree
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən ˈtɝːki oʊk / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈtɜːki əʊk / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to central and southern Europe and Asia Minor, known for its lance-shaped leaves with spiked lobes.

Examples

The European turkey oak tree
Botanists studied the European turkey oak to understand its adaptability to dry soils.
The European turkey oak is often planted in parks for its striking foliage and shade.
Unlike native species, the European turkey oak can thrive in a range of continental climates.
