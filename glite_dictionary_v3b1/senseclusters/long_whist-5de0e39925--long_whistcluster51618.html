long whist

noun
traditional four-player card game
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ wɪst / (AmE), / lɒŋ wɪst / (BrE)
a card game for four players who form two partnerships, using a standard deck; each side scores one point for every trick taken above six

Examples

Long whist card game
In Victorian times, long whist was a popular pastime among families and friends.
The rules of long whist differ slightly from the later version known as 'short whist'.
We gathered in the parlor after dinner to play a few hands of long whist.
