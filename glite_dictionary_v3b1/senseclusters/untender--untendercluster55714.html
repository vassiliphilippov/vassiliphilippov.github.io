untender

adjective
not legal payment
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtɛndər / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛndə / (BrE)
not recognized as legal tender and therefore not acceptable as official payment in a given place.

Examples

Untender coins
The shop refused to accept the untender coins as payment for groceries.
Collectors often own rare bills that are now considered untender in most countries.
He tried to pay with an untender note, but the cashier explained it was not valid currency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legal tender - money that is officially recognized for payment of debts
