squeak through

verb
narrowly escape or succeed
8.2
Pronunciation: / skwik θru / (AmE), / skwiːk θruː / (BrE)
to narrowly escape or succeed, often by a very small margin or with little to spare

Examples

To squeak through exam
After a difficult interview, he squeaked through and got the job offer.
She was late to the station but squeaked through the closing doors just in time.
The team managed to squeak through to the finals by just one point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a desired result
  • synonym: scrape through - to manage to succeed with difficulty, especially in passing an examination
