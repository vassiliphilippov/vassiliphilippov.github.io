degradative

adjective
causing breakdown
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡrædətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈɡrædətɪv / (BrE)
causing or relating to the breakdown or decomposition of substances

Examples

Degradative enzymes
The soil contains degradative enzymes that help break down organic matter.
Scientists study degradative processes to understand how pollutants are removed from the environment.
Some bacteria have degradative abilities that are useful in waste treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructive - serving to build up or improve
  • synonym: decomposing - causing something to rot or break down
