submersion

noun
immersion cooling for electronics
7.7
Pronunciation: /səb.ˈmɜːr.ʒən/ (AmE), /səb.ˈmɜː.ʒən/ (BrE)
(in computing and engineering) the practice or system of cooling electronic equipment by immersing components or entire servers in a non-conductive liquid coolant to remove heat.

Examples

Submersion cooling system
The data center adopted submersion cooling to keep servers from overheating.
Engineers tested submersion of the server boards in dielectric fluid during the trial.
Many manufacturers now recommend submersion systems for compact, high-density compute racks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immersion cooling - a cooling method that immerses electronic components in dielectric fluid to dissipate heat
  • synonym: liquid cooling - any cooling technique that uses a liquid to transfer heat away from components
