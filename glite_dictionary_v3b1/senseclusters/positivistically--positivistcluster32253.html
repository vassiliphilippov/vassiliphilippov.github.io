positivistically

adverb
in a positivist manner
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːzətɪˈvɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌpɒzɪtɪˈvɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on the principles of positivism, especially in philosophy or science

Examples

Interpreted positivistically in research
The data was interpreted positivistically to ensure objectivity in the research findings.
Some critics argue that viewing history positivistically ignores important cultural factors.
The philosopher approached the problem positivistically, focusing only on observable facts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scientifically - in a way that relates to science or uses scientific methods
  • antonym: subjectively - in a way based on personal feelings or opinions
