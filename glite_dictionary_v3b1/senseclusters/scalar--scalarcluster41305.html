scalar

noun
quantity with magnitude only, no direction
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪlər / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlə / (BrE)
A quantity represented by a single value (magnitude only) and not by direction, such as temperature or mass.

Examples

Scalar quantity
In mathematics, mass is considered a scalar quantity.
The energy of an object is a scalar, not a vector.
Unlike force, temperature is a scalar and does not have a direction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vector - a quantity having both magnitude and direction
