dissolve

verb
lose emotional control suddenly
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
to suddenly lose control of your emotions, especially by crying or laughing uncontrollably

Examples

To dissolve into tears
He dissolved into tears when he saw the old photographs.
She dissolved into laughter at the comedian’s joke.
The students dissolved into fits of giggles during the assembly.
He often dissolves into giggles whenever he tries to tell a funny story.
The children dissolved into laughter at the comedian's silly jokes.
Upon hearing the bad news, she dissolved into tears in the middle of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break down - to lose control of one's emotions and start to cry
  • synonym: collapse - to break down or give way suddenly, especially emotionally
