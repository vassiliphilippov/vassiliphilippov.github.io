feis

noun
Gaelic public gathering
Pronunciation: / fɛʃ / (AmE), / fɛʃ / (BrE)
a historical Gaelic assembly or public gathering, often for trade, legal matters, or celebration

Examples

Ancient feis gathering
In ancient Ireland, a feis was held to settle disputes and celebrate the harvest.
The king called a grand feis where poets and musicians performed for the court.
Merchants traveled from distant villages to attend the annual feis and exchange goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: festival - a day or period of celebration, often with public events
