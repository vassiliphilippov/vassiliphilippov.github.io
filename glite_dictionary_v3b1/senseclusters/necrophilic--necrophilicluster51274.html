necrophilic

adjective
morbid interest in death
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛkrəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɛkrəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
having a strong, often morbid, interest in or attraction to death, corpses, or funerary practices (not sexual).

Examples

Necrophilic art
The artist's necrophilic themes often shocked visitors to his gallery.
Her necrophilic curiosity led her to study ancient burial customs.
Some horror films appeal to a necrophilic fascination with death and the afterlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macabre - having a fascination with death or the gruesome
  • synonym: morbid - showing an unhealthy interest in disturbing subjects, especially death
