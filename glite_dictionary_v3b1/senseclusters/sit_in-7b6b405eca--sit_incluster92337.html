sit in

verb
attend as a visitor or observer
2.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /sɪt ɪn/ /; / /sɪt ən/ (AmE), /sɪt ɪn/ /; / /sɪt ən/ (BrE)
to attend a meeting, class, or event as a visitor or observer, but not as a regular participant

Examples

To sit in on a class
Anyone interested in the project is welcome to sit in on the planning meetings.
I asked my supervisor if I could sit in on the interview to gain some experience.
Several students came to sit in on the lecture even though they weren't enrolled in the class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: observe - to watch something carefully, especially as part of a process of learning or assessment
  • antonym: participate - to take part in an activity or event
