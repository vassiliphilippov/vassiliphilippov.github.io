swamp red oak

noun
a species of oak tree found in wet lowlands of North America
Pronunciation: / swɑmp rɛd oʊk / (AmE), / swɒmp rɛd əʊk / (BrE)
a large North American deciduous tree (Quercus shumardii), with spreading branches and deeply lobed leaves, found in wet lowland areas from New Jersey to Illinois and southward.

Examples

A swamp red oak tree
Leaves from a swamp red oak can be identified by their deep, narrow lobes.
Lumber from the swamp red oak is valued for its strength and similarity to northern red oak wood.
The swamp red oak thrives in wet bottomlands throughout the southern United States.

Related Terms

  • synonym: red oak - a general term for several oaks with reddish wood, including Quercus rubra and related species
