fanfold

noun
zigzag-folded paper
Pronunciation: / ˈfænˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfænˌfəʊld / (BrE)
paper folded in a continuous zigzag pattern, often used for printers or forms

Examples

A stack of fanfold
The accountant loaded a stack of fanfold paper into the dot matrix printer.
Old computer reports were often printed on fanfold with perforated edges for easy separation.
He carefully tore the fanfold sheets apart along the perforations after printing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continuous form - paper supplied in a long, unbroken sheet with perforations for separation
