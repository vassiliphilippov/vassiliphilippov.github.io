constitutionalize

verb
give a constitution to
3.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈstɪtuːʃənəˌlaɪz /; / kənˌstɪtuːʃə.nəˌlaɪz /; / kənˌstɪtuːʃəˌnəˈlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈstɪtjuːʃənəˌlaɪz /; / kənˌstɪtjuːʃə.nəˌlaɪz /; / kənˌstɪtjuːʃəˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to provide with a constitution or make something part of a constitution

Examples

To constitutionalize a system
It took years of negotiation to constitutionalize the country's political system.
Several former colonies were constitutionalized during the nineteenth century.
The new leaders worked to constitutionalize the nation after the revolution.
Activists are working to constitutionalize the right to access clean water.
After years of debate, the country decided to constitutionalize environmental protection.
The Supreme Court ruling effectively constitutionalized the right to free speech.
Debate continues over whether to constitutionalise the freedom of information in the state's laws.
Efforts are underway to constitutionalise environmental protection in the national charter.
Several countries have moved to constitutionalise the right to education in recent years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolish - to formally end a system or practice, especially a law or constitution
  • synonym: codify - arrange laws or rules into a systematic code
  • synonym: constitutionalize - incorporate into a constitution; make constitutional (American spelling)
  • synonym: entrench - to establish firmly within a constitution or system
  • antonym: invalidate - to make not valid, especially legally
