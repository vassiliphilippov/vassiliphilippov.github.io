retrade

noun
renegotiation after agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈriːtreɪd / (AmE), / ˈriːtreɪd / (BrE)
an attempt to change the agreed terms or price of a deal after it has already been made, often seen as unfair or in bad faith

Examples

A last-minute retrade
The seller was frustrated by the buyer's last-minute retrade of the contract terms.
A sudden retrade can damage trust between business partners and make future deals difficult.
Many consider a retrade to be unprofessional, especially after both sides have agreed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breach - a violation or breaking of an agreement
  • synonym: renegotiation - the process of discussing and changing the terms of an agreement
