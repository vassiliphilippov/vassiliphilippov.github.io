recompose

verb
to regain composure
5.7 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêk…ômÀàpo äz / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêk…ômÀàp…ô äz / (BrE)
to regain self-control or calmness after being upset or disturbed

Examples

To recompose yourself
He took a deep breath to recompose before answering the difficult question.
After hearing the bad news, she needed a moment to recompose herself.
The actor paused backstage to recompose after forgetting his lines.

Synonyms

  • pull oneself together
    5.1 2.75/m
    pull oneself together is an informal phrase used to stop crying or panic and act calmly, while recompose is more formal.
  • regather
    4.6 0.02/m
    regather means collecting your thoughts and feelings after a shock, while recompose means calming and regaining steady self-control.
  • recover
    2.5 43/m
    recover means returning to a normal state or health over time, while recompose focuses on calming your emotions quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose oneself - to calm oneself and regain control of emotions
  • antonym: lose composure - to become upset or lose self-control
