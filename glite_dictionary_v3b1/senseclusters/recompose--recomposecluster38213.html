recompose

verb
to regain composure
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to regain self-control or calmness after being upset or disturbed

Examples

To recompose yourself
He took a deep breath to recompose before answering the difficult question.
After hearing the bad news, she needed a moment to recompose herself.
The actor paused backstage to recompose after forgetting his lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose oneself - to calm oneself and regain control of emotions
  • antonym: lose composure - to become upset or lose self-control
