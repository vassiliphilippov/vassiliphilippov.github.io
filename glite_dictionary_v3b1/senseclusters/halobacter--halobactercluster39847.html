halobacter

noun
salt-loving microorganism
Pronunciation: / ˈhæloʊbækˌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæləʊbæktə / (BrE)
a type of microorganism (archaea) that thrives in extremely salty environments such as salt flats and the Dead Sea

Examples

Halobacter in salt
Scientists study halobacter to understand how life survives in extreme conditions.
The Dead Sea is home to abundant halobacter, coloring the water reddish hues.
When salt concentrations rise, halobacter flourish where other organisms cannot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: halophile - an organism that thrives in salty environments
