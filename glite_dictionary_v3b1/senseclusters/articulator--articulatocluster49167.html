articulator

noun
movable organ for speech production
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a movable organ in the mouth, such as the tongue or lips, used in producing speech sounds

Examples

Tongue articulator
Damage to any articulator can significantly affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
Linguists study how each articulator contributes to the formation of different consonants and vowels.
The tongue is a primary articulator in the production of many speech sounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: articulation - the act of producing speech sounds using speech organs
  • synonym: speech organ - any organ involved in producing speech sounds
