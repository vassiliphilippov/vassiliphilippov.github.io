strike

verb
produce a note on a musical instrument
8.5 4.2
Pronunciation: / straɪk / (AmE), / straɪk / (BrE)
to produce or cause something by striking, such as a sound, fire, musical note, electric arc, or coin

Examples

To strike a note

She strikes each key with such emotion that the melody truly comes alive.

Once you strike an arc, the circuit is completed and light is produced.
The technician carefully struck an arc to start the welding process.
To restore the theater's lighting, it's necessary to strike the arc in each lamp.
I woke up when the clock struck six.
The church bell strikes every hour on the hour.
We listened to the clock striking twelve at midnight.
