refectory table

noun
long narrow dining table with trestle support
Pronunciation: / rəˈfɛktəri ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfɛktəri ˈteɪbl / (BrE)
a long, narrow dining table supported by a stretcher between two trestles, traditionally used in monasteries and large halls

Examples

A refectory table in monastery
An antique refectory table can add character to a modern dining room.
The grand hall was dominated by a massive oak refectory table dating back to the seventeenth century.
The monks gathered around the refectory table for their evening meal in silence.
