Kassite language

noun
extinct language of the Kassites
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsaɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæsʌɪt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct language once spoken by the Kassites in ancient Mesopotamia, known mainly from personal names and terms in cuneiform texts

Examples

The Kassite language texts
Scholars have found only limited evidence of the Kassite language in cuneiform tablets.
Some personal names in Babylonian records are believed to be derived from the Kassite language.
The Kassite language remains poorly understood due to the scarcity of surviving texts.

Related Terms
