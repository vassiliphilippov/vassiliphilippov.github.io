jinx

verb
bring bad luck to
Pronunciation: / dʒɪŋks / (AmE), / dʒɪŋks / (BrE)
to bring bad luck to someone or something, often by saying something or by superstition

Examples

To jinx a team
Don’t say we’re sure to win—you’ll jinx it!
He believed his rival had jinxed his chances of success.
She worried that talking about her plans would jinx them.
Don't say anything before the results are announced—you might jinx it!
He felt that buying the tickets in advance would jinx their chances of winning.
Some people believe that mentioning a no-hitter during a game can jinx the pitcher.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bless - to confer good fortune or happiness upon
  • synonym: curse - to invoke or wish evil upon someone or something
