confirmed

adjective
established as true, or having received confirmation
3.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàf…ùÀêmd / (AmE), / k…ônÀàf…úÀêmd / (BrE)
(of a fact, arrangement, report, or status) established as true or definite; (of a person) having received the Christian rite of confirmation.

Examples

Confirmed facts
She is a confirmed member of the church and attends every Sunday.
The hotel sent an email with my confirmed reservation details.
We received confirmed reports that the event would proceed as planned.

Synonyms

    3.2 0.03/m
    proven is shown true by strong evidence and tests, while confirmed is declared definite or accepted as true.
  • verified
    3.9 0.01/m
    verified is checked carefully by examination or testing, while confirmed is accepted as true or definite after finding proof.
  • validated
    4.2 0.00/m
    validated is formally made legally or officially valid after review, while confirmed is simply established as true or definite.
  • attested
    6.0 0.00/m
    attested is proved genuine often with a witness or document, while confirmed is broadly established as true or definite.
  • settled
    3.4 0.00/m
    settled is decided so there is no further doubt or dispute, while confirmed is recorded or fixed as true or definite.
  • acknowledged
    3.5 0.00/m
    acknowledged is accepted or admitted by others publicly, while confirmed is established as true or definite through proof or decision.
  • approved
    1.5 0.00/m
    approved is officially accepted or allowed by an authority, while confirmed is established as true or definite rather than just permitted.
