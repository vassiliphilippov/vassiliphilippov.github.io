sow one's wild oats

verb
behave recklessly or promiscuously in youth
9.9
Pronunciation: / soʊ wʌnz waɪld oʊts / (AmE), / səʊ wʌnz waɪld əʊts / (BrE)
to behave in a promiscuous or self-indulgent way, especially when young

Examples

To sow one's wild oats
He spent most of his twenties sowing his wild oats before settling down and starting a family.
Many people believe that young adults need to sow their wild oats before taking on serious commitments.
She regrets sowing her wild oats and wishes she had been more responsible in her youth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: settle down - to adopt a quieter, more responsible lifestyle
