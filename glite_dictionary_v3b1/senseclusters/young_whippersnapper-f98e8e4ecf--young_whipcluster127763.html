young whippersnapper

noun
impudent or presumptuous young person
Pronunciation: / jʌŋ ˈwɪpərˌsnæpər / (AmE), / jʌŋ ˈwɪpəsˌnapə / (BrE)
a young person, especially one who is regarded as impudent or presumptuous

Examples

A young whippersnapper talks
Don't let that young whippersnapper lecture you about business!
My grandfather called me a young whippersnapper when I challenged his story.
The boss dismissed the new recruit as just another young whippersnapper.

Related Terms

  • antonym: old-timer - an experienced or long-standing person, often contrasted with 'young whippersnapper'
  • synonym: upstart - a person who has risen suddenly to wealth or high position, often regarded as presumptuous
