neutrophil

noun
type of white blood cell
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəˌfɪl / (BrE)
a type of white blood cell that is mainly responsible for ingesting and destroying bacteria and other foreign particles, and stains with both acidic and basic dyes

Examples

A neutrophil cell
A blood test showed elevated neutrophil levels in response to the infection.
During bacterial infections, the body rapidly increases the production of neutrophils.
The doctor explained that a low neutrophil count could indicate a weakened immune system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eosinophil - a type of white blood cell that stains with eosin dye
  • degree higher: leukocyte - general term for a white blood cell
  • synonym: lymphocyte - a type of white blood cell involved in the immune response
