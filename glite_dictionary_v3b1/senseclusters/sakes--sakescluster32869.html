sakes

noun
emphatic expressions
8.1
Pronunciation: / seɪks / (AmE), / seɪks / (BrE)
used in set expressions to emphasize strong feelings, urgency, or exasperation, often following 'for' and a possessive noun.

Examples

For heaven's sakes
For heaven's sakes, turn down the music before the neighbors complain.
Oh, for Pete's sakes, can you please hurry up?
For goodness' sakes, don't forget to lock the door when you leave.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sake - reason or purpose for doing something
