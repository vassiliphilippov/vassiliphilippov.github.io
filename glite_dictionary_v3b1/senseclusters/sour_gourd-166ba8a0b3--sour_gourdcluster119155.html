sour gourd

noun
African fruit with edible sour pulp
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊɚ ɡɔrd /; / saʊr ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə ɡɔːd /; / saʊə ɡɔːd / (BrE)
an edible, sour-tasting gourd-like fruit found in Africa and Australia

Examples

Chopped sour gourd in curry
Adding chopped sour gourd to the curry gave it a unique, tart flavor.
She made a tangy sauce using the pulp of sour gourd.
The villagers gathered sour gourds from the fields for the evening meal.
Early settlers learned to crack open the hard shell of the sour gourd for its edible seeds.
Local recipes sometimes include pickled sour gourd to add a tangy flavor.
The sour gourd is often harvested in late summer when the fruit is fully ripe.
