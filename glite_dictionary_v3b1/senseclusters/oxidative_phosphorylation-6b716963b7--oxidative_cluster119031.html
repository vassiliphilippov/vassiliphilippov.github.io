oxidative phosphorylation

noun
process producing ATP in cells via oxidation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksɪˌdeɪtɪv ˌfɑsfəˌrɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌdeɪtɪv ˌfɒsfəˌrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an enzymatic process in cell metabolism by which ATP is synthesized from ADP, using energy released by the oxidation of nutrients

Examples

Oxidative phosphorylation process
Oxidative phosphorylation is the primary method by which cells generate ATP during aerobic respiration.
Defects in oxidative phosphorylation can lead to serious metabolic disorders.
Most of the energy produced by our cells is generated through oxidative phosphorylation in the mitochondria.

Related Terms

  • antonym: substrate-level phosphorylation - production of ATP by direct transfer of a phosphate group, not using the electron transport chain
