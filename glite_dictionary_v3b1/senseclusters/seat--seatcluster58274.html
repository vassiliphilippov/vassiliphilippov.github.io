seat

verb
install into an official position
Pronunciation: / siːt / (AmE), / siːt / (BrE)
to place someone ceremoniously or formally in an office or position; to install into an official role

Examples

To seat a judge
He was officially seated as head of the committee after the voting process.
The board will seat the elected members during next week's meeting.
The new university chancellor was seated in a grand inauguration ceremony.
