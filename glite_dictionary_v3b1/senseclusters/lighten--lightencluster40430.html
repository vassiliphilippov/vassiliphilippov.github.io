lighten

verb
make more cheerful
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtn̩ /; / ˈlaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtn̩ /; / ˈlaɪtən / (BrE)
to make or become more cheerful or less serious in mood or atmosphere

Examples

To lighten the mood
A phone call from a friend can lighten your spirits when you're feeling low.
Her jokes always lighten the mood during tense meetings.
The holiday decorations helped lighten the atmosphere in the office.
His expression lightened when he saw his friends arrive.
She watched his face lighten as he heard the good news.
The mood in the office began to lighten after the joke was shared.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheer up - to make someone feel happier
  • antonym: depress - to make someone feel sad or discouraged
