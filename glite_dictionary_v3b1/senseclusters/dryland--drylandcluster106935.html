dryland

noun
arid region
5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdraɪ.lænd/ /; / /ˈdraɪ.lənd/ (AmE), /ˈdraɪ.lænd/ /; / /ˈdraɪ.lənd/ (BrE)
land or area that receives very little rainfall and is not irrigated

Examples

A dryland area
Farmers in the dryland region must use special methods to conserve water.
Many plants in dryland areas have adapted to survive with minimal rainfall.
The government is investing in irrigation projects to support agriculture in dryland zones.
Farmers often grow wheat and barley on dryland because these crops need little water.
The region's dryland is especially vulnerable to drought during the summer months.
Conservation efforts are helping to restore native plants to the dryland areas outside the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arid land - land with very little rainfall and dry conditions
  • synonym: desert - a very dry, barren area with little or no vegetation
  • antonym: wetland - an area of land saturated with water, such as a marsh or swamp
