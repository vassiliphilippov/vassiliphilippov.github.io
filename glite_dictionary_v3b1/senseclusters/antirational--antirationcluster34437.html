antirational

noun
opponent of reason
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˈræʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈræʃənəl / (BrE)
a person who opposes or rejects the use of reason or rational thought in decision-making or beliefs

Examples

An antirational leader
Some critics labeled him an antirational for ignoring scientific evidence in his arguments.
The debate featured a passionate antirational who dismissed logic in favor of personal beliefs.
Being called an antirational can be controversial in academic circles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrationalist - a person who emphasizes instinct or emotion over reason
  • antonym: rationalist - a person who values reason as the main source of knowledge
