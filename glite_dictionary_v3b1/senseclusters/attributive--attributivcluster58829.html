attributive

adjective
modifying noun directly before it
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɪbjətɪv / (AmE), / əˈtrɪbjʊtɪv / (BrE)
(of an adjective or other modifier) directly preceding and modifying a noun.

Examples

Attributive adjective
In the phrase 'a beautiful garden,' the word beautiful is used in an attributive position.
Many grammars distinguish between attributive and predicative uses of adjectives.
Not all adjectives can be used attributively before a noun.

Related Terms

  • antonym: predicative - (of an adjective or phrase) used after a verb, not before the noun it modifies
