archaist

noun
person who uses archaic forms
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːr.keɪ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈɑː.keɪ.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who deliberately uses or imitates archaic language, style, or customs

Examples

Archaist uses old words
As an amateur archaist, she enjoyed reconstructing medieval speech for historical reenactments.
Some critics dismissed him as an archaist for his deliberate imitation of old literary forms.
The poet was known as an archaist, often reviving words lost to modern English.
