keepsake

noun
item kept for its sentimental value
3.9 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkiÀêpÀåse…™k / (AmE), / ÀàkiÀêpse…™k / (BrE)
An object kept as a reminder of a person, place, or event, and cherished for its sentimental value.

Examples

A keepsake gift
After her trip, she brought back a small stone as a keepsake.
He treasured the signed jersey as a keepsake of his first game.
She wore a locket as a keepsake from her grandmother.

Synonyms

  • memento
    6.0 2.38/m
    a memento is a small object kept to remind someone of an event or person, while a keepsake is cherished for its sentimental value.
  • token
    3.4 1.25/m
    a token is a small gift or symbol kept to mark a person or event, while a keepsake is treasured for personal memory.
  • souvenir
    2.3 5.77/m
    a souvenir is often bought while traveling as a reminder of a place or trip, while a keepsake is more personally cherished.

Related Terms
