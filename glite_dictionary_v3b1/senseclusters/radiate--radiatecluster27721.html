radiate

verb
emit light in rays
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪ.di.eɪt /; / ˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.dɪ.eɪt /; / ˈreɪdɪeɪt /; / ˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to send out or emit energy, light, heat, or waves from a central source

Examples

Radiate heat
The crystals radiate a soft glow when exposed to ultraviolet light.
The lamp radiated warmth and light, making the room feel cozy.
The lighthouse radiated a brilliant beam across the bay every night.
The antenna radiates electromagnetic waves in all directions.
The sun radiates heat and light into space.
Warmth radiated from the fireplace, melting the chill in the room.
During the treatment, a special lamp radiates ultraviolet waves over the patient's skin.
The sun radiates enormous amounts of heat and light every day.
Warmth radiated from the fireplace, making the entire room comfortable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in energy, especially heat or light
  • synonym: emit - to give off, send forth, or discharge
