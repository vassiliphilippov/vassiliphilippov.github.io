moneyline

noun
betting odds system
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌniˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a way of betting on sports where you pick the winner of a game, with odds showing which team or player is favored (no point spreads)

Examples

The moneyline bet
The moneyline for the game shows the home team as the favorite.
If you bet on the underdog using the moneyline, your payout could be much higher.
Many American sportsbooks display both the point spread and the moneyline for each match.
He placed a moneyline bet on his favorite team to win the championship.
The moneyline for the underdog offered a much higher payout than the favorite.
Many beginners prefer the moneyline because it is simple to understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: odds - the probability or likelihood of a particular outcome in betting
  • antonym: point spread - a betting system where a margin of victory is set for the favored team
