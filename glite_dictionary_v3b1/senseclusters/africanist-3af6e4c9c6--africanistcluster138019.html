Africanist

noun
Africa studies expert
Pronunciation: / ˈæfrɪkənɪst / (AmE), / ˈæfrɪkənɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies the cultures, history, languages, or politics of Africa

Examples

An Africanist scholar
The Africanist delivered a lecture on the impact of colonialism in East Africa.
As an Africanist, she has traveled extensively throughout the continent for her research.
Many universities offer programs for students who want to become an Africanist.
The museum hired an Africanist to curate its new exhibit on West African art.
Many Africanists attend international conferences to share their research findings.
She became an Africanist after spending several years studying languages in Kenya.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthropologist - a person who studies human societies and cultures
  • synonym: historian - a person who studies or writes about history
