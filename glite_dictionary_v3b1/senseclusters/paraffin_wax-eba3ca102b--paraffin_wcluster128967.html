paraffin wax

noun
waxy solid from petroleum, used for candles
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəfɪn wæks / (AmE), / ˈpærəfɪn wæks / (BrE)
A white or colorless waxy solid derived from petroleum, used in making candles and for various coatings and preservatives.

Examples

Paraffin wax coating
She applied paraffin wax to the cheese to help preserve it during storage.
The candles were made from paraffin wax, giving them a smooth, glossy finish.
The laboratory used blocks of paraffin wax for embedding tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wax - a solid, fatty substance used in candles and coatings
