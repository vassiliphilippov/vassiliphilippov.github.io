attrition

noun
gradual wearing down by friction
5.0 5.9
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈtrɪʃ(ə)n /; / əˈtrɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the process of gradually wearing down or eroding something by friction, rubbing, or natural forces

Examples

Attrition of metal
Metal parts in an engine are subject to attrition due to continuous movement.
