Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show

noun
19th-century American traveling show by Buffalo Bill
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfəloʊ bɪlz waɪld wɛst ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ bɪlz waɪld west ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a famous American traveling show, organized in 1883 by William F. Cody, featuring displays of horseback riding, marksmanship, and reenactments of frontier life; it toured widely in the United States and Europe

Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show performances
