canafistola

noun
tropical tree with medicinal pods
Pronunciation: / ˌkænəˈfɪstələ / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈfɪstələ / (BrE)
A tropical tree with drooping racemes of scented yellow to sepia flowers and pods whose pulp is used medicinally; found in tropical Asia, Central and South America, and Australia.

Examples

The canafistola tree
Bright drooping racemes cover the canafistola during the rainy season in tropical gardens.
Researchers are studying compounds extracted from canafistola for their potential health benefits.
The canafistola is prized in traditional medicine for the pulp found in its long pods.
