cut corners

verb
do something quickly or cheaply, lowering standards
Pronunciation: / kʌt ˈkɔrnərz /; / kʌt ˈkɔrnɚz / (AmE), / kʌt ˈkɔːnəz / (BrE)
to do something quickly or cheaply by not following all the rules or steps, often resulting in lower quality

Examples

To cut corners on work
If we cut corners on this project, the final result won't meet our usual standards.
Some companies try to cut corners to reduce costs, but it can lead to poor quality products.
The builder was accused of cutting corners when he rushed to finish the house ahead of schedule.
If you cut corners when preparing the report, important details may be missed.
The builder tried to cut corners and the result was a poorly constructed house.
They were criticized for cutting corners to finish the project on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skimp - to use less time, money, or material than is necessary, often producing a poorer result
  • synonym: take shortcuts - do something in a quicker or easier way than usual, often with less care
