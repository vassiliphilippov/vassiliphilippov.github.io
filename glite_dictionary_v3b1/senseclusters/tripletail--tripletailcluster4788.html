tripletail

noun
large warm-water fish with three-lobed tail appearance
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪp.lɪ.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.lɪ.teɪl / (BrE)
a large edible fish found in warm seas worldwide, noted for its long dorsal and anal fins, which along with its caudal fin give the appearance of a three-lobed tail

Examples

A tripletail fish
A tripletail can grow up to 90 centimeters in length and is prized for its delicious meat.
Divers spotted several tripletails swimming near the buoy during their afternoon dive.
The fisherman was excited to catch a tripletail off the coast of Florida.
