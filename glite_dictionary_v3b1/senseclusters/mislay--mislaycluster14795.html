mislay

verb
accidentally put out of place; lose temporarily
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈleɪ/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈleɪ/ (BrE)
to put something somewhere and be unable to find it again, usually by accident

Examples

To mislay keys

She mislaid her notebook and couldn't remember where she left it.
Try not to mislay important documents during the move.
