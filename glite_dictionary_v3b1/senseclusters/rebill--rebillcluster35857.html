rebill

verb
to charge again
5.6
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈbɪl/ /; / /rə.ˈbɪl/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈbɪl/ /; / /rə.ˈbɪl/ (BrE)
to charge or invoice again, especially as part of a recurring payment or after a failed payment

Examples

To rebill a customer
If your subscription is not canceled, the company will rebill you automatically each month.
The gym will rebill your credit card annually unless you notify them to stop.
Customers are often surprised when a service tries to rebill after a free trial period ends.
If the payment fails, the company will rebill your account automatically after three days.
The finance department had to rebill the client because the original invoice was incorrect.
We can rebill the shipping charges to your new account if you prefer.
