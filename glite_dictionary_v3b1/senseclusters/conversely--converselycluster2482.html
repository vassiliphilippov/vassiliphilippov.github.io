conversely

adverb
in an opposite or reversed way
4.6 8/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàv…ùÀêsli / (AmE), / k…ônÀàv…úÀêsli / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement or idea that reverses or contrasts with one just made or referred to.

Examples

Used conversely to contrast
If you don't trust him, he will, conversely, find it hard to trust you.
Men are not always fathers; conversely, not all women are mothers.
The south is experiencing record heat; conversely, the north remains unusually cold.
