credit balance

noun
account balance where credits exceed debits
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛdɪt ˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
an account balance in which the amount credited exceeds the amount debited, indicating that money is owed to the account holder by the institution

Examples

A credit balance in account
If your statement shows a credit balance, the bank owes you money.
My savings account currently has a credit balance of $1,500.
The business maintained a healthy credit balance throughout the fiscal year.
