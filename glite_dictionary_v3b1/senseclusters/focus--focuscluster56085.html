focus

verb
make clearer or more concentrated
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.kəs/ /; / /ˈfoʊ.kɪs/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.kəs/ /; / /ˈfəʊ.kɪs/ (BrE)
to direct or adjust your attention, ideas, or emotions toward something specific or to make them clearer

Examples

To focus on work
He tried to focus his emotions and remain calm during the interview.
She needed to focus her thoughts before responding to the difficult question.
The group discussion helped focus everyone's ideas on finding a solution.
During the meeting, the team tried to focus on the most important issues.
If you focus your mind, you will find the solution more quickly.
She needs to focus on her health instead of worrying about work all the time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blur - to make or become unclear or less distinct
  • synonym: concentrate - to direct one's attention or effort towards a particular activity, subject, or problem
  • antonym: distract - to divert attention to something else
