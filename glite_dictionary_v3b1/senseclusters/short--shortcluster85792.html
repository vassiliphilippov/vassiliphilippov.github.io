short

adjective
not owning what one sells in finance
9.5 8/m
Pronunciation: /  É…îrt / (AmE), /  É…îÀêt / (BrE)
(of a person or position in finance) not owning the securities or commodities that are being sold, typically in the expectation that their prices will fall and can later be bought back at a lower price.

Examples

Go short on stocks
After the announcement, she decided to go short on crude oil futures.
Many traders are currently short in the tech sector, expecting prices to decrease.
The company was caught short during the recent market rally.
