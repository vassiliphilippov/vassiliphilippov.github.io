Ordovician period

noun
a major geological period after the Cambrian
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrdəˈvɪʃən ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˌɔːdəˈvɪʃən ˈpɪərɪəd / (BrE)
the geological period, lasting from about 485 to 444 million years ago, following the Cambrian and preceding the Silurian, marked by diverse marine life and the first appearance of many types of organisms

Examples

The Ordovician period fossils
Fossils from the Ordovician period show an abundance of marine life forms.
Geologists study rock layers from the Ordovician period to understand Earth's ancient environments.
The Ordovician period was marked by significant diversification of invertebrate animals.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Silurian period - the geological period following the Ordovician
