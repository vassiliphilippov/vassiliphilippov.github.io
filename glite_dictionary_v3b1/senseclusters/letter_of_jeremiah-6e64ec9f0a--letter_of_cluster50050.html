Letter of Jeremiah

noun
Apocryphal book attributed to Jeremiah
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtər əv dʒɛˈraɪ.ə.mə / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə əv dʒəˈraɪ.ə.mi / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book attributed to the prophet Jeremiah, consisting of a letter warning the Jews in exile in Babylon against idolatry

Examples

The Letter of Jeremiah
Many Bible editions include the Letter of Jeremiah as a separate text.
Scholars discuss the status of the Letter of Jeremiah within the Apocrypha.
The Letter of Jeremiah warns the Jews in Babylon not to worship idols.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Book of Baruch - a related Apocryphal book often grouped with Letter of Jeremiah
