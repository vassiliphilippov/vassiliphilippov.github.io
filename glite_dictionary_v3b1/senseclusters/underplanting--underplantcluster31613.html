underplanting

noun
plants beneath others
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌplæntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌplɑːntɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of planting, or the plants that are grown, beneath existing trees or shrubs to create a lower layer of vegetation

Examples

Underplanting ferns and hostas
The underplanting of ferns and hostas added lush greenery beneath the tall oak trees.
Gardeners often use underplanting to fill empty spaces and prevent weeds from growing.
Colorful bulbs in the underplanting bloom early in spring before the trees leaf out.
Many gardeners use underplanting to create a lush, layered look in their landscapes.
Effective underplanting can help prevent weeds from growing beneath large shrubs.
The shady area beneath the old oak tree was brightened by careful underplanting with colorful flowers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ground cover - low-growing plants used to cover soil under trees or shrubs
  • synonym: groundcover - low-growing plants that cover the soil under taller plants
  • antonym: mulching - covering soil with material to protect or enrich it
  • antonym: overstory - the upper layer of foliage formed by the crowns of trees
