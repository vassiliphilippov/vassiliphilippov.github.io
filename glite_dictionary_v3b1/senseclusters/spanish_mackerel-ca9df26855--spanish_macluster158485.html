Spanish mackerel

noun
flesh of a type of Atlantic fish used for food
Pronunciation: / ˈspænɪʃ ˈmækərəl / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈmækərəɫ / (BrE)
The flesh of a commercially important fish, especially found in the Atlantic coastal waters of America, used as food.

Examples

Grilled Spanish mackerel
Grilled Spanish mackerel is a popular dish at many seaside restaurants.
The chef recommended using fresh Spanish mackerel for the recipe.
We caught several Spanish mackerel during our fishing trip off the coast.

Related Terms
