Tanacetum balsamita

noun
herb used as potherb, tea, or flavoring
Pronunciation: / ˌtænəˈsitəm bælˈsæmɪtə / (AmE), / ˌtænəsˈiːtəm bɔːlˈseɪmɪtə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb, also known as costmary, with a tansy-like scent and yellow buttonlike flowers, sometimes used as a potherb, salad green, in potpourri, tea, or as a flavoring.

Examples

Tanacetum balsamita plant
Botanists sometimes refer to Tanacetum balsamita by its common name, costmary.
She brewed a herbal tea using dried Tanacetum balsamita from her garden.
The leaves of Tanacetum balsamita are often added to salads for a unique flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: costmary - another name for Tanacetum balsamita, especially in culinary use
  • degree lower: tansy - a related aromatic Eurasian herb, Tanacetum vulgare
