gai choi

noun
variety of Asian mustard green
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪ tʃɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ tʃɔɪ / (BrE)
A variety of Asiatic mustard (Brassica juncea) with thick, slightly bitter leaves, commonly used as a leaf vegetable in East Asian cooking.

Examples

Fresh gai choi leaves
My grandmother always added gai choi to her noodle dishes for extra flavor.
Pickled gai choi is a staple ingredient in many traditional Chinese soups.
You can stir-fry gai choi with garlic for a quick and healthy side dish.
