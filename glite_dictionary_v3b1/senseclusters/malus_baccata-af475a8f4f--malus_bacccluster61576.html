Malus baccata

noun
species of Asian wild crab apple
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪləs bəˈkætə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪləs bəˈkatə / (BrE)
An Asian species of wild crab apple, cultivated in many varieties for its small, acidic, usually red fruit used in preserves.

Examples

Malus baccata fruit
Farmers often grow Malus baccata for its hardy fruit, which is popular in jelly making.
Horticulturists recommend planting Malus baccata because it tolerates colder climates than many other apple varieties.
The ornamental Malus baccata is prized for its profusion of white spring blossoms.
