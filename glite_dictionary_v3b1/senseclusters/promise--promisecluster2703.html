promise

verb
to give a commitment to do something
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.mɪs /; / ˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪs /; / ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
to state that you will definitely do something or that something will happen; to make a commitment or assurance

Examples

To promise to help
I can't make it tonight, but I promise I'll visit you tomorrow.
She promised to help me finish the project by next week.
They promised that the repairs would be completed on time.
I promise to finish the report by tomorrow.
She promised him a vacation if he finished his work.
They promise excellent customer service to all their clients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: break - to fail to fulfill a promise or agreement
  • synonym: guarantee - to give a formal assurance, typically in writing, that something will be done
  • synonym: pledge - to make a formal, serious promise
  • antonym: threaten - state one's intention to harm or punish
  • synonym: vow - to make a solemn promise, especially in a formal or religious context
