bivalent

adjective
chromatin with activating and repressive marks
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.və.lənt/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.veɪ.lənt/ /; / /ˈbɪ.və.lənt/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.və.lənt/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.veɪ.lənt/ /; / /ˈbɪ.və.lənt/ (BrE)
(in epigenetics) describing chromatin regions that carry both activating and repressive histone modifications, marking genes as transcriptionally 'poised' for activation.

Examples

Bivalent chromatin marks
In stem cells, bivalent chromatin carries both activating and repressive histone marks.
Researchers found a bivalent domain at the promoter of several developmental genes in embryos.
Loss of a repressive mark converted the locus from bivalent to actively transcribed chromatin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poised - ready for activation; genes with bivalent chromatin are often described as poised.
