reconcentrate

verb
to concentrate again
8.6
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈkɑːn.sə.treɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈkɑːn.sə.treɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈkɒn.sən.treɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈkɒn.sən.treɪt/ (BrE)
to make a liquid or solution more concentrated again, usually by removing water or another solvent

Examples

To reconcentrate a solution
After the initial process, technicians had to reconcentrate the solution to achieve the desired strength.
If the juice is too watery, you can reconcentrate it by gently heating it to evaporate some water.
The laboratory protocol required them to reconcentrate the sample before further analysis.

Related Terms
