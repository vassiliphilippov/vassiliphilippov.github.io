invalidate

verb
to make something invalid or prove untrue
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvæləˌdeɪt /; / ɪnˈvælɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvælɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something no longer legally or officially valid or acceptable

Examples

To invalidate a contract
If the contract is signed under duress, it may invalidate the agreement.
One error in the calculations could invalidate the entire experiment.
The discovery of new evidence could invalidate the previous court ruling.
If you sign the wrong section, it might invalidate your application.
The bank will invalidate the check if it is altered or damaged.
The company had to invalidate the old contracts after changing its policy.
If you sign the document incorrectly, you might invalidate your application.
New evidence could invalidate the agreement between both parties.
The judge decided to invalidate the previous contract due to a legal technicality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nullify - to make something legally null and void
  • antonym: validate - to confirm or prove the validity of something
  • synonym: void - to declare something invalid, especially in law
