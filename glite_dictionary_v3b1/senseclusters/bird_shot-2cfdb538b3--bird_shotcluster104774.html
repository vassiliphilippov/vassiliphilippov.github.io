bird shot

noun
small lead pellets for shotgun shells
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝd ˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːd ˌʃɒt / (BrE)
small lead pellets used in shotgun shells, typically for hunting birds or small game.

Examples

Bird shot pellets
Bird shot is less likely to penetrate walls than larger pellets like buckshot.
The hunter loaded his shotgun with bird shot before heading into the woods.
Using bird shot is common when hunting quail and other small birds.
