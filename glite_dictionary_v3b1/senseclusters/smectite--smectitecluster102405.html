smectite

noun
expandable clay mineral
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɛktaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsmɛktaɪt / (BrE)
a group of clay minerals that can absorb water and expand, often used in soils and industrial applications

Examples

Smectite clay minerals
The geologist identified a layer of smectite in the soil sample from the riverbank.
Because smectite can absorb water, it is often used to improve soil moisture retention.
Engineers use smectite in drilling muds to help control fluid loss during oil exploration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bentonite - a type of clay formed from smectite minerals
  • antonym: kaolinite - a non-expanding clay mineral
