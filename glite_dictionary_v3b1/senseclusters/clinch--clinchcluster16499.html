clinch

verb
hold opponent in boxing to avoid punches
Pronunciation: / klɪntʃ / (AmE), / klɪntʃ / (BrE)
to hold someone or something tightly, especially in an embrace or in sports like boxing

Examples

To clinch an opponent
He was warned by the referee for trying to clinch too many times during the bout.
The tired boxer tried to clinch to avoid further blows in the final round.
When the bell rang, both fighters began to clinch frequently to slow the pace of the match.
He would clinch his jacket tightly around himself when the wind picked up.
She managed to clinch her friend's hand in the crowded station.
The two boxers began to clinch in the final round of the match.
In the final scene, they clinch, sealing their reunion after years apart.
She rushed to clinch her lover before he boarded the train.
The couple clinched passionately under the stars, lost in their own world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embrace - to hold someone closely in one's arms, especially as a sign of affection
  • synonym: grapple - to engage in a close fight or struggle without weapons; wrestle
  • synonym: grip - to take and keep a firm hold of something
  • antonym: release - to let go or set free
