pancreatic juice

noun
digestive fluid from the pancreas
Pronunciation: / pænˌkriːætɪk ˈdʒuːs / (AmE), / paŋˌkriːætɪk ˈdʒuːs / (BrE)
the clear alkaline fluid secreted by the pancreas into the duodenum, containing enzymes that help digest starches, proteins, and fats

Examples

Pancreatic juice secretion
A deficiency in pancreatic juice can lead to problems absorbing nutrients.
Doctors may test the composition of pancreatic juice to diagnose certain digestive diseases.
When food enters the small intestine, pancreatic juice is released to aid digestion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bile - a digestive fluid produced by the liver and stored in the gallbladder
