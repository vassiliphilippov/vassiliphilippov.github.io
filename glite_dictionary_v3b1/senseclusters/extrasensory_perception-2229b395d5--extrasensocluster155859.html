extrasensory perception

noun
supposed ability to sense beyond ordinary perception
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstrəˈsɛnsəri pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈsɛnsəri pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the supposed ability to perceive information or events by means other than the known senses

Examples

Extrasensory perception ability
Many people claim to possess extrasensory perception, but scientific evidence is lacking.
Stories about extrasensory perception often appear in books about the paranormal.
The experiment aimed to test whether animals have a kind of extrasensory perception.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clairvoyance - the supposed ability to gain information about people or events through means other than the known senses
  • synonym: telepathy - the supposed communication of thoughts or ideas by means other than the known senses
