oikonomia

noun
pastoral leniency principle
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɔɪ.kə.ˈnoʊ.miː.ə/ /; / /iː.kə.ˈnoʊ.miː.ə/ (AmE), /ɔɪ.kə.ˈnəʊ.miː.ə/ /; / /iː.kə.ˈnəʊ.miː.ə/ (BrE)
in Eastern Orthodox Christianity, a principle allowing flexibility or leniency in applying church rules for spiritual care

Examples

Oikonomia in church rules
The priest chose to exercise oikonomia when dealing with the parishioner's difficult situation.
In some cases, oikonomia allows for exceptions to strict church rules for the sake of compassion.
The bishop explained the concept of oikonomia to help the congregation understand the church's decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: akribeia - strictness in applying church rules
  • synonym: dispensation - special permission to relax a rule, especially in church law
