single entry

noun
simple bookkeeping method (one account)
7.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.ɡəl ˈen.triː/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.ɡəl ˈen.triː/ (BrE)
A bookkeeping system in which transactions are recorded in only one account rather than in both debit and credit accounts.

Examples

A single entry system
Before adopting a single entry system, consider the limitations compared to more complex accounting methods.
Many small businesses use single entry bookkeeping because it is easier to manage than double entry.
The accountant explained that single entry does not track both sides of each transaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: double entry - a bookkeeping system in which each transaction is recorded twice, as both a debit and a credit
