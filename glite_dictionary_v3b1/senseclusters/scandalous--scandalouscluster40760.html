scandalous

adjective
causing public outrage or disgrace
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈskændələs / (AmE), / ˈskændələs / (BrE)
causing public shock or strong disapproval by offending moral standards or damaging someone's reputation

Examples

A scandalous story
Her scandalous remarks were reported in all the major newspapers.
It was considered scandalous for women to smoke in public back then.
The politician resigned after details of his scandalous actions were made public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admirable - arousing respect and approval
  • synonym: disgraceful - shockingly unacceptable; causing loss of respect
  • synonym: shameful - deserving or causing shame or disgrace
