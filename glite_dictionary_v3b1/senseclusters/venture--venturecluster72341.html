venture

verb
say or suggest something despite risk of being wrong
7.1 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈven.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈven.tʃə/ (BrE)
to say or suggest something, such as a guess or opinion, even though you may be wrong or criticized for it

Examples

To venture an opinion
He ventured that the new policy might not succeed as intended.
I hesitate to venture an opinion in such a complex situation.
She ventured a guess that the meeting would end early.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • synonym: suggest - to mention or introduce (an idea, proposition, plan, etc.) for consideration or possible action
  • antonym: withdraw - to take back or retract (a statement, opinion, or proposal)
