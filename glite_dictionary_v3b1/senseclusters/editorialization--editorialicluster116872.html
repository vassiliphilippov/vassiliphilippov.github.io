editorialization

noun
adding commentary
8.6
Pronunciation: /e.dɪ.tɔːr.i.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.dɪ.tɔːr.i.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /e.dɪ.tɔːr.i.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.dɪ.tɔːr.i.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of adding personal opinions or bias to reporting or writing, instead of staying objective

Examples

Biased editorialization
The article was criticized for its excessive editorialization rather than sticking to the facts.
Some readers appreciate editorialization because it provides context and personal insight.
Academic writing usually avoids editorialization to maintain a neutral and objective tone.
The news anchor was accused of editorialization during the live broadcast.
Some readers prefer articles without any editorialization or personal bias.
Excessive editorialization can undermine the credibility of a news report.
The news report was criticized for its clear editorialization of the facts.
Readers expect objective journalism, not editorialization in every article.
Some believe that editorialization can undermine the credibility of a news source.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - a tendency to favor one side or opinion
  • synonym: commentary - explanatory or critical notes added to a text
  • antonym: objectivity - the quality of being unbiased and not influenced by personal feelings
