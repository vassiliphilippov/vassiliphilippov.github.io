medallion

noun
a large commemorative or award medal
3.3
Pronunciation: / məˈdæliən / (AmE), / mɪˈdalɪən / (BrE)
a large medal or coin, often given as an award or to commemorate an event

Examples

Gold medallion
At the ceremony, each winner was presented with a commemorative medallion.
She wore the gold medallion she had received after winning the marathon.
The medallion hangs proudly on his wall, marking his athletic achievements.
Collectors value an intact Greek medallion for its historical and artistic significance.
The archaeologist discovered a bronze medallion among other ancient coins at the site.
The museum's collection features a rare medallion minted during the reign of Alexander the Great.
