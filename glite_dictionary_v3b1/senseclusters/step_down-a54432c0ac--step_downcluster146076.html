step down

verb
resign from a position

to resign from or leave an important job or official position, especially so someone else can take over

Examples

To step down as leader
After years as CEO, she decided to step down and let someone else lead the company.
Facing mounting criticism, the official finally chose to step down from his post.
The coach announced he would step down at the end of the season.
After years of dedicated service, she decided to step down as chairperson.

Synonyms

  • step aside

    step aside means withdrawing to let someone else take over, while step down means resigning from an important post.
  • resign
    3.7 20/m
    resign is the formal act of leaving a job by notice, while step down is often used for high-level, public departures.
  • leave office
    4.2 2.62/m
    leave office means giving up a position of authority, while step down often implies making way for a successor.
  • quit
    1.2 28/m
    quit means to stop working or give up a job in any way, while step down implies a formal, public resignation.
  • bow out
    4.9 0.79/m
    bow out means leaving politely or with dignity from a role, while step down is the act of resigning from an important job.
  • stand down
    5.1 2.59/m
    stand down means withdraw or stop duties often after an order, while step down is a voluntary resignation from office.
