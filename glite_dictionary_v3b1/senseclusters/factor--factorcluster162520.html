factor

noun
hereditary unit in genetics
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / ˈfæktə / (BrE)
(genetics, historical) a unit of heredity; a segment of DNA involved in producing a polypeptide chain, now referred to as a gene.

Examples

A genetic factor
Mendel referred to genes as factors before the term 'gene' was coined.
Some diseases are caused by mutations in specific genetic factors.
The early geneticists debated how factors are inherited from parents to offspring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gene - unit of heredity on DNA, encoding a polypeptide or RNA
