carcass

noun
dead body of an animal, dressed for food
4.6 6.25/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêr.k…ôs /; / Ààk…ërk…ôs /; / Ààk…ëÀêrk…ôs / (AmE), / Ààk…ëÀê.k…ôs /; / Ààk…ëÀêk…ôs /; / Ààk…ëÀêk…ëÀês / (BrE)
the dead body of an animal, especially one that has been slaughtered and prepared for food; or the remains of something that has been destroyed

Examples

Animal carcass
After the hunt, they brought the deer carcass back to camp to prepare for cooking.
Birds quickly picked at the remains of the sheep's carcass left in the field.
The butcher hung the carcass on a hook before cutting it into steaks.
After the hunt, the lions left the carcass of the antelope in the grass.
Workers removed the animal carcass from the side of the road early in the morning.
The butcher prepared the carcass for meat processing at the shop.
The lion dragged the carcass of its prey into the shade to eat.
After the fire, only the carcass of the old barn remained standing.
Vultures quickly gathered around the carcass lying in the field.

Synonyms

  • dead body
    1.6 5.68/m
    dead body is the whole person or animal after death, while carcass usually means an animal's body used for meat or ruined remains.
  • body
    1.6 108/m
    body can mean a living or dead physical form, while carcass specifically means a dead animal's body often used for meat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carcass - the dead body of an animal, especially one prepared for food (American spelling and general term)
  • synonym: corpse - the dead body of a human being
  • synonym: remains - what is left of a body after death
