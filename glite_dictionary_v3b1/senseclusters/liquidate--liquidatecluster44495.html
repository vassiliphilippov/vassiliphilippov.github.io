liquidate

verb
convert assets into cash
5.9 7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪt /; / ˈlɪkwəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdeɪt /; / ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪt / (BrE)
to sell assets in order to pay off debts, especially when closing a business or during bankruptcy

Examples

To liquidate assets
Investors often liquidate their holdings during periods of economic uncertainty.
She had to liquidate her business after facing financial difficulties.
The company decided to liquidate several assets to pay off its debts.
After years of losses, the board decided to liquidate the business.
The company had to liquidate its assets to pay off creditors.
The court appointed a trustee to liquidate all remaining resources.
After the court order, the firm was forced to liquidate its liabilities quickly.
She managed to liquidate all her outstanding loans in less than a year.
The company had to liquidate its debts before it could be sold to new owners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bankrupt - declare unable to pay debts
  • synonym: dissolve - officially end or close down a business entity
  • antonym: owe - to be under obligation to pay money back
  • synonym: repay - to pay back money that is owed
  • synonym: sell off - to sell assets, especially in large amounts or at a loss, often to raise cash quickly
