circitor

noun
ancient patrol officer
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɜːrˈsaɪtər / (AmE), / sɜːˈkɪtɔː / (BrE)
an officer, especially in ancient times, who patrolled a route or territory to check on guards or security

Examples

The circitor patrols

In Roman camps, a circitor was tasked with inspecting the guard posts.
The role of the circitor was crucial for maintaining discipline among the soldiers.
