revenue sharing

noun
business model for distributing income
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈrev.ə.nuː ˈʃer.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈrev.ə.njuː ˈʃer.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈrev.ə.njuː ˈʃeə.rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈrev.ə.nuː ˈʃeə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a contractual arrangement in which income from a product, service, or platform is distributed among participating parties (for example, creators, developers, or partners) according to agreed terms.

Examples

Platform revenue sharing
Many streaming platforms use revenue sharing to pay creators a percentage of subscription earnings.
The startup negotiated a revenue sharing agreement that gives partners thirty percent of net sales.
Under the new contract, the publisher receives mechanical royalties plus revenue sharing from ad income.

Related Terms

  • synonym: profit sharing - a system for distributing a company's profits among employees or stakeholders
  • synonym: royalty - a payment made to the owner of a copyright or patent for the use of their work
