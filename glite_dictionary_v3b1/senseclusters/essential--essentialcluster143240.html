Synonyms

  • necessary
    1.0 90/m
    necessary means required or unavoidable in a situation, while essential also suggests a basic, central part of something.
  • indispensable
    4.6 2/m
    indispensable is something you cannot do without, while essential means very important or basic but sometimes replaceable.
  • vital
    3.5 19/m
    vital is crucial for life, success, or survival, while essential is more general and can mean basic or necessary.
  • crucial
    3.6 20/m
    crucial is decisive for the final outcome, while essential is about being necessary or forming a basic part.
  • key
    2.0 60/m
    key is a simple, often spoken word for something very important, while essential can also stress a basic, core role.
  • fundamental
    3.5 20/m
    fundamental describes the basic parts that form something, while essential can mean necessary or very important more broadly.
  • integral
    5.3 3/m
    integral describes something that is a needed part of a whole, while essential can be needed or simply very important.
  • requisite
    6.2 1/m
    requisite is a formal word for something required for a particular purpose, while essential is more general and everyday.
  • central
    2.1 20/m
    central means at the heart or main focus of something, while essential stresses being necessary or a basic element.
  • of the essence
    5.3 1/m
    of the essence is a formal phrase saying something is absolutely necessary in a situation, while essential is a common everyday word.
  • all-important
    4.0 1/m
    all-important is an emphatic, often exaggerated way to say something matters a lot, while essential is more neutral and factual.
