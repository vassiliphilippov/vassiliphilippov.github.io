dithyramb

noun
ancient Greek passionate hymn
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪθ.ɪˌræm / (AmE), / ˈdɪθ.ɪ.ræm / (BrE)
a passionate and often improvisational ancient Greek hymn, especially one sung in honor of Dionysus

Examples

Ancient Greek dithyramb
He read a poem structured like a dithyramb, full of exuberant praise and rhythm.
Historians study the dithyramb as an important precursor to ancient Greek drama.
The choir performed a moving dithyramb to celebrate the festival of Dionysus.
