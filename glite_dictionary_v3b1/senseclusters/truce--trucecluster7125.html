truce

noun
temporary peace agreement
6.4
Pronunciation: / truːs / (AmE), / truːs / (BrE)
An agreement between opponents to stop fighting for a certain period, usually while discussing terms for peace.

Examples

A truce agreement
After months of conflict, both sides agreed to a truce to start negotiations.
The truce lasted for several weeks, allowing humanitarian aid into the city.
They called for a truce over the holidays to give civilians a chance to evacuate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: armistice - an agreement to stop fighting, often used in military contexts
  • synonym: ceasefire - a temporary suspension of fighting; a truce
  • antonym: war - a state of armed conflict between different countries or groups
