superexpression

noun
excessive gene activity
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərɪkˈsprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪkˈsprɛʃən / (BrE)
an unusually high level of gene expression resulting in the production of large amounts of a specific protein

Examples

Gene superexpression
Scientists detected superexpression of the gene after introducing the new drug to the cell culture.
The disease is linked to superexpression of certain proteins in the immune system.
Researchers are studying how superexpression affects the development of cancerous tumors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overexpression - the production of a gene product in greater than normal amounts
  • antonym: underexpression - the production of a gene product in less than normal amounts
