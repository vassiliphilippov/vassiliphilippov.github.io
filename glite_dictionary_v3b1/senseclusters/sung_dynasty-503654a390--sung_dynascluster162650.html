Sung dynasty

noun
Chinese imperial dynasty, 960–1279
Pronunciation: / sʌŋ ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / sʌŋ ˈdɪ.nə.sti / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from 960 to 1279, known for its achievements in art, literature, and philosophy

Examples

The Sung dynasty period
Chinese landscape painting reached new heights in the Sung dynasty.
Many important inventions, such as printing, advanced during the Sung dynasty.
The Sung dynasty was renowned for its vibrant artistic and intellectual life.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Tang dynasty - the preceding Chinese imperial dynasty (618–907)
  • degree higher: Yuan dynasty - the succeeding Mongol-ruled Chinese dynasty (1271–1368)
