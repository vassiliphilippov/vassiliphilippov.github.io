scaling

noun
arranging by degree or rank
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of arranging, measuring, or adjusting things according to a scale (such as size, amount, or importance)

Examples

Scaling of values
A clear scaling of difficulty helps learners progress through the material.
Proper scaling of tasks in the project ensures that each phase is manageable.
The new salary system involves the scaling of wages according to job performance.
Accurate scaling is essential when converting blueprints to full-size structures.
Successful scaling of the project required adjusting resources to match the new workload.
The architect used careful scaling to ensure the model reflected the real building's proportions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gradation - a series of gradual changes or stages
  • synonym: ranking - the process of placing in order of importance or quality
