dead

adjective
not working; no longer functioning
Pronunciation: / dɛd / (AmE), / dɛd / (BrE)
no longer alive, active, or functioning; not living or working

Examples

Dead battery
Our internet connection was dead for hours last night.
She picked up the phone but found the line completely dead.
The dead battery left us stranded on the road.
I tried to start my car, but the dead battery wouldn't turn over.
If you leave your phone on all night, the battery will be completely dead by morning.
The flashlight was useless because its batteries were already dead.
After years of debate, the dead issue was finally removed from the agenda.
His proposal became a dead topic after management declined to discuss it any further.
Many consider that tradition a dead practice in modern society.
