carder

noun
bee collecting plant fibers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
a solitary bee that collects plant hairs or fibers to line its nest, often called a wool-carder bee

Examples

Wool-carder bee
The carder carefully gathered soft plant fibers to build a comfortable nest.
Unlike honeybees, a carder works alone and does not live in a hive.
Gardeners often spot a carder buzzing around fuzzy-leaved plants in the summer.
