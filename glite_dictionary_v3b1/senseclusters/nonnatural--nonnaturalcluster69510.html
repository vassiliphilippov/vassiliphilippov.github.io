nonnatural

adjective
not in accordance with nature
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈnætʃərəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈnatʃərəl / (BrE)
not existing in or not in accordance with nature; originating from outside the natural world.

Examples

A nonnatural substance
Certain chemicals are considered nonnatural and may have unknown effects on the environment.
She preferred organic remedies to those containing nonnatural substances.
The philosopher argued that moral values are nonnatural entities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unnatural - contrary to the ordinary course of nature; artificial or abnormal
