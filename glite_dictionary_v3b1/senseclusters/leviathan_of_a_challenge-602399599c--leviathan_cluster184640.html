leviathan of a challenge

noun
a huge or daunting challenge
Pronunciation: / lɪˈvaɪəθən əv ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / lɪˈvaɪəθən əv ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
an extremely large or difficult challenge; a task that is daunting due to its scale or complexity

Examples

A leviathan of a challenge
Climbing Mount Everest is a leviathan of a challenge that only a few people ever conquer.
Launching the new software presented a leviathan of a challenge for the development team.
Managing a global crisis can be a leviathan of a challenge for any leader.
