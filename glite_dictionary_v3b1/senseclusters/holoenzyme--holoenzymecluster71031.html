holoenzyme

noun
complete enzyme complex
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊ.loʊ.en.zaɪm/ /; / /ˈhoʊ.lə.en.zaɪm/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊ.ləʊ.en.zaɪm/ /; / /ˈhəʊ.lə.ən.zaɪm/ (BrE)
a complete enzyme consisting of its protein part and all necessary cofactors required for activity

Examples

The holoenzyme complex
The holoenzyme is only functional when all its cofactors are bound.
Researchers studied the structure of the holoenzyme to understand how it catalyzes reactions.
Without its cofactor, the protein cannot form a holoenzyme and remains inactive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a biological catalyst
