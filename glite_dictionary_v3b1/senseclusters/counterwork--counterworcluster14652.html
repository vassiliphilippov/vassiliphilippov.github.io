counterwork

verb
to oppose or hinder
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌwɜːk / (BrE)
to act against something in order to prevent or hinder its progress or effect

Examples

To counterwork a project
The activists tried to counterwork the spread of misinformation in their community.
New safety measures were implemented to counterwork the risks associated with the machinery.
She developed a strategy to counterwork the negative influence of peer pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to happen
  • synonym: oppose - to act against or resist
  • antonym: support - to help or promote the progress of something
