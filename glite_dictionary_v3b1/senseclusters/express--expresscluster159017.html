express

adjective
clearly and explicitly stated
1.2
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsprɛs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛs / (BrE)
(of a statement or wish) clearly and explicitly stated, not implied or left unspoken.

Examples

Express permission
It was her express wish that no gifts be given.
She left express instructions not to be disturbed.
The contract states his express agreement to the terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail
  • antonym: implicit - implied though not plainly expressed
