US Secret Service

proper noun
U.S. agency for leader protection and financial crimes
Pronunciation: / juː ɛs ˌsiːkrət ˈsɜːrvɪs / (AmE), / juː ɛs ˌsiːkrɪt ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
the United States federal agency responsible for protecting current and former national leaders and their families, foreign dignitaries, and for investigating financial crimes such as counterfeiting and forgery.

Examples

US Secret Service agents
Agents of the US Secret Service thwarted a counterfeiting operation last year.
She was honored to be visited by the director of the US Secret Service during the ceremony.
The US Secret Service is responsible for protecting the president and other top officials.
