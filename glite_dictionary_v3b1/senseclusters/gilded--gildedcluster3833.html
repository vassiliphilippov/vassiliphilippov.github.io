gilded

adjective
deceptively attractive or superficial
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪldɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡɪldɪd / (BrE)
Having a pleasing or showy appearance that is deceptive or superficial; appearing attractive on the surface but lacking real value or sincerity.

Examples

Gilded promise
Behind the gilded facade, the company was struggling financially.
The gilded invitations looked impressive, but the event itself was disappointing.
The politician gave a gilded speech full of empty promises and flashy rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meretricious - apparently attractive but with no real value
