subsist

verb
maintain or support oneself
Pronunciation: / səbˈsɪst / (AmE), / səbˈsɪst / (BrE)
to manage to stay alive, especially with limited resources; to maintain or support oneself at a minimal level

Examples

To subsist on food
During the war, prisoners had to subsist on very little food.
Many rural families subsist on what they can grow in their own fields.
She managed to subsist on a small pension after retirement.
