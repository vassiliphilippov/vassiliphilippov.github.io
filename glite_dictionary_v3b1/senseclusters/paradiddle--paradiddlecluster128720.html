paradiddle

noun
drumming pattern using single and double strokes
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌdɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈpærədɪdəl / (BrE)
A pattern of drumming consisting of a specific sequence of single and double strokes, often producing a rapid, continuous sound on the snare drum.

Examples

A paradiddle pattern
Practicing the paradiddle daily can improve your hand coordination.
She incorporated a complex paradiddle into her solo, impressing the audience.
The drummer showcased his skills with a rapid paradiddle on the snare.
