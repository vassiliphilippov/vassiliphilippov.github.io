hearth

noun
area at base of a fireplace
Pronunciation: / hɑrθ /; / hɑːrθ / (AmE), / hɑːθ / (BrE)
the floor or area at the base or in front of a fireplace, usually made of stone or brick

Examples

Stone hearth
A decorative rug was placed in front of the hearth to add color to the room.
On cold winter evenings, the family gathered around the hearth to enjoy its warmth.
She cleaned the ashes from the hearth before lighting a new fire.
After a long journey, he was finally back at his own hearth and among his family.
Many soldiers yearned for the comfort of their own hearth during the war.
Writers often speak of the hearth as the heart of the home, a place of warmth and comfort.
Ashes from last night's fire still covered the hearth.
Children gathered around the hearth to listen to stories on cold evenings.
They placed the kettle on the hearth to keep it warm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fireplace - a structure for holding a domestic fire, typically framed in brick or stone
  • synonym: fireside - the part of a room near the fireplace, often used figuratively for home life
