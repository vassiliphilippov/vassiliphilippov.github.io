complex absence

noun
absence seizure with additional symptoms
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmˌplɛks æbˈsɛns / (AmE), / ˈkɒmˌplɛks æbˈsɒns / (BrE)
A type of absence seizure characterized by impaired consciousness accompanied by additional symptoms such as muscle weakness, involuntary movements, or changes in blood vessel function.

Examples

Complex absences with muscle weakness
Children with complex absences may experience sudden muscle weakness in addition to impaired awareness.
The doctor diagnosed her with a complex absence after observing the involuntary movements during her episode.
Treatment for a complex absence may involve adjusting anti-seizure medication to control the additional symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absence seizure - a brief seizure involving sudden loss of awareness
