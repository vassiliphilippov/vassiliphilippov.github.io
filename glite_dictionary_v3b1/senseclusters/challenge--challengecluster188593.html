challenge

noun
formal objection to a juror
7.6 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃæl.ɪndʒ/ /; / /ˈtʃæl.əndʒ/ (AmE), /ˈtʃæl.ɪndʒ/ /; / /ˈtʃæl.əndʒ/ (BrE)
the act of questioning, disputing, or formally objecting to something, including in legal contexts

Examples

Legal challenge
During jury selection, a challenge was raised against a candidate with a conflict of interest.
She issued a challenge to the inclusion of the juror, arguing he knew the victim.
The defense attorney made a challenge to one of the potential jurors due to possible bias.
Her challenge of the mayor's decision sparked a lively debate at the town hall meeting.
His unexpected challenge forced the speaker to clarify her position.
The scientist issued a challenge to the traditional theory in her recent paper.
