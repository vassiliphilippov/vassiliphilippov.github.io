lumpfish

noun
northern Atlantic fish; source of caviar roe
8.1 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈlʌmp.fɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈlʌmp.fɪʃ/ (BrE)
A thick-bodied marine fish of northern Atlantic waters, known for carrying edible roe often used as caviar; it has pelvic fins fused into a sucker.

Examples

The lumpfish roe
Fishermen in northern Europe catch lumpfish during its spawning season.
Some aquariums display the lumpfish because of its unusual appearance and sucker-like fins.
The lumpfish is prized for its delicate roe, often used as a caviar substitute.
