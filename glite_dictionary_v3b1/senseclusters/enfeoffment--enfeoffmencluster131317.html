enfeoffment

noun
granting of land under feudal law
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfiːəfmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfiːəfmənt / (BrE)
under the feudal system, the granting of land to a person in exchange for a pledge of service, usually formalized by a legal deed

Examples

The enfeoffment deed
After the enfeoffment, the new lord was required to provide military service to his overlord.
Records of enfeoffment offer valuable insights into medieval land ownership and social structure.
The enfeoffment of the manor was recorded in a royal charter from the 12th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fief - an estate of land, especially one held on condition of feudal service
