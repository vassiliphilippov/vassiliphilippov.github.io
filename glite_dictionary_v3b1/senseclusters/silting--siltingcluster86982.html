silting

noun
accumulation of silt
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪl.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈsɪl.təŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsɪl.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈsɪl.təŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which fine soil or silt builds up in rivers, lakes, or reservoirs, often reducing water flow or capacity

Examples

Silting in rivers
The construction project was delayed due to excessive silting in the nearby river.
Regular dredging is required to prevent silting from blocking the canal.
After the storm, silting made the lake shallower and affected local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sedimentation - the process of particles settling at the bottom of a liquid
