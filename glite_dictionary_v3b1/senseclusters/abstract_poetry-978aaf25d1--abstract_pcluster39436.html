abstract poetry

noun
poetry prioritizing ideas over tangible images
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈæbˌstrækt ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / ˈæbstrækt ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
Poetry that focuses on expressing ideas or emotions rather than concrete imagery or narrative.

Examples

Abstract poetry style
Many modern artists are drawn to abstract poetry for its open-ended interpretation.
She finds meaning in abstract poetry that doesn't follow traditional structures.
The seminar focused on the techniques of writing abstract poetry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concrete poetry - poetry in which the visual arrangement is important to convey meaning
