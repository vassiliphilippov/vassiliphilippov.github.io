cannula

noun
thin tube for medical insertion
1.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkæn.jʊ.lə/ /; / /ˈkæn.jə.lə/ (AmE), /ˈkæn.jʊ.lə/ /; / /ˈkæn.jə.lə/ (BrE)
A thin tube inserted into a body cavity, duct, or vessel to administer medication, drain fluid, or allow access.

Examples

A cannula tube
After surgery, a cannula was used to drain excess fluid from the wound.
Nurses must be trained in the proper technique for inserting a cannula.
The doctor inserted a cannula into the patient's vein to administer fluids.
