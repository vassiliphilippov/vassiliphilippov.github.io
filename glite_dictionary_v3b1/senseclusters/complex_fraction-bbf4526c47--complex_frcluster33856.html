complex fraction

noun
fraction with fractions in numerator or denominator
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛks ˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛks ˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
a fraction in which the numerator, the denominator, or both contain one or more fractions themselves

Examples

A complex fraction in math
Students often find complex fractions challenging on their math exams.
The equation contained a complex fraction that needed to be simplified.
To solve the problem, you must first convert the complex fraction into a simple fraction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: simple fraction - a fraction in which both the numerator and denominator are integers
