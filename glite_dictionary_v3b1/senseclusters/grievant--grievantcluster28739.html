grievant

noun
person filing complaint
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈgriː.vənt/ /; / /ˈgrɪ.vənt/ (AmE), /ˈgriː.vənt/ /; / /ˈgrɪ.vənt/ (BrE)
a person who files a formal complaint or claims to have suffered a wrong, especially in a workplace or legal context

Examples

A workplace grievant
The grievant met with the human resources department to discuss the issue.
After the investigation, the company responded to the grievant's concerns in writing.
Union representatives supported the grievant throughout the complaint process.
The grievant submitted a detailed statement outlining the harm they experienced.
In the arbitration process, the grievant was represented by a union official.
The court listened carefully to the testimony of the grievant before making a decision.
