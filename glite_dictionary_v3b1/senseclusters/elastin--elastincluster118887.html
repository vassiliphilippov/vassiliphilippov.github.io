elastin

noun
a protein of elastic connective tissue
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæstɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪn / (BrE)
a protein that forms the principal constituent of elastic connective tissue, especially in the walls of arteries

Examples

Elastin protein
Arterial walls contain large amounts of elastin to help them stretch and recoil with each heartbeat.
Researchers are studying elastin to develop new treatments for vascular diseases.
The loss of elastin in the skin leads to visible signs of aging such as wrinkles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collagen - a structural protein found in skin and connective tissue
