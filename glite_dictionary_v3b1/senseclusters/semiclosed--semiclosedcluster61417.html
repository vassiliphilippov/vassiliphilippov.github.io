semiclosed

adjective
partly open interval
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈse.mɪ.kloʊzd/ (AmE), /ˈse.mɪ.kləʊzd/ (BrE)
in mathematics, describing an interval that includes one endpoint but not the other

Examples

Semiclosed interval
The solution lies within the semiclosed interval from 2 to 5, including 2 but not 5.
In this graph, the semiclosed interval is marked with a solid dot on one end and an open circle on the other.
Mathematicians often use semiclosed intervals to specify ranges that include only one endpoint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: half-open - an interval including one endpoint but not the other
  • antonym: open - an interval excluding both endpoints
