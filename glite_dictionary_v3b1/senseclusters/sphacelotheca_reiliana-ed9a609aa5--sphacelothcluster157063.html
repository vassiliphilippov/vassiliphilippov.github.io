Sphacelotheca reiliana

noun
smut fungus affecting corn or sorghum
9.9
Pronunciation: / sfæsəˌloʊˈθɛkə raɪˈliːɑːnə / (AmE), / sfəsɪləʊˈθiːkə raɪˈliːənə / (BrE)
a smut fungus that infects the heads of corn or sorghum, causing a type of covered smut disease

Examples

Sphacelotheca reiliana fungus
Sphacelotheca reiliana is a major pathogen in many sorghum-growing regions, reducing crop yield.
Scientists are developing resistant corn varieties to combat Sphacelotheca reiliana infections.
The covered smut caused by Sphacelotheca reiliana can be identified by its characteristic spore masses.
