representationalism

noun
theory of mental images
9.9
Pronunciation: /re.prɪ.zen.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /re.prɪ.zen.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the theory that perception or knowledge of the world is based on mental representations rather than direct experience

Examples

Philosophical representationalism
Many philosophers debate whether representationalism accurately explains how we perceive reality.
According to representationalism, our minds create internal images to interpret the external world.
Critics of representationalism argue that it separates us from direct experience of reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct realism - the theory that perception allows direct awareness of the external world
  • synonym: idealism - the philosophical theory that reality is fundamentally mental or immaterial
