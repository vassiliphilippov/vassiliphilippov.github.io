modularize

verb
to divide into modules
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdʒələrˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊləˌaɪz / (BrE)
to divide something into smaller, self-contained parts or modules to make it easier to manage, update, or understand

Examples

To modularize a system
The company decided to modularize its training program to allow for easier updates.
By modularizing the curriculum, teachers can adapt lessons to different student needs.
Software developers often modularize code to improve maintenance and scalability.
The engineering team decided to modularize the product to simplify future upgrades.
By modularizing the curriculum, the school made it easier for students to choose their courses.
It is common to modularize large software projects to improve maintainability and flexibility.
Software engineers often modularize large programs to make them easier to maintain and update.
If you modularize your project, each team can work on a different part independently.
The teacher decided to modularize the curriculum into smaller, focused units for better learning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrate - to combine parts into a whole
  • synonym: segment - to divide into separate parts or sections
  • synonym: separate - to divide into different parts or groups
