dizzying

adjective
extremely fast or confusing
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪziɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɪziɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely fast, impressive, or overwhelming, making it hard to keep up or think clearly

Examples

Dizzying speed
The city’s dizzying pace of life can be exhausting for newcomers.
She looked down from the dizzying height of the skyscraper’s observation deck.
The technology industry is known for its dizzying rate of change and innovation.
The dizzying speed of technological change can be hard to keep up with.
He looked down from the dizzying heights of the skyscraper and felt a rush of fear.
The museum displayed a dizzying variety of artwork from around the world.
