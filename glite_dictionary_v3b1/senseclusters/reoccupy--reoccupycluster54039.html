reoccupy

verb
to take back possession
5.6 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀà…ëÀêkj…ôÀåpa…™ / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀà…íkj äpa…™ / (BrE)
to take possession of or return to a place, position, or job after having left it

Synonyms

  • re-assume
    5.7 0.00/m
    re-assume is taking a role or duty back after time away, while reoccupy means returning to a job, place, or position.
  • reenter
    3.2 1.23/m
    reenter is going back into a place or situation, while reoccupy implies taking possession or returning to a job or position.
  • retake
    4.6 1.24/m
    retake is to take back control, often by force or quickly, while reoccupy is returning to hold a place or job.
  • regain
    3.3 17/m
    regain is getting back possession or use of something lost, while reoccupy focuses on returning to a place, job, or position.
