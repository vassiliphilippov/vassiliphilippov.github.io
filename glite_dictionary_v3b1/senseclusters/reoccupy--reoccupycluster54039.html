reoccupy

verb
to take back possession
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɒkjʊpaɪ / (BrE)
to take possession of or return to a place, position, or job after having left it

Examples

To reoccupy a building
After the floodwaters receded, residents were able to reoccupy their homes.
The army planned to reoccupy the city after the enemy retreated.
Scientists will reoccupy the research station once the weather improves.
After the floodwaters receded, the villagers were able to reoccupy their homes.
The army planned to reoccupy the territory that had been abandoned during the conflict.

After the floodwaters receded, residents began to reoccupy their homes.
The company plans to reoccupy its downtown office building next month.
Troops were ordered to reoccupy the border region following the peace agreement.
