sodium phosphate

noun
a phosphate salt of sodium, used as a laxative and cleaner
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm ˈfɑsfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm ˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt, Na3PO4, or any of several related phosphates of sodium, used in medicine as a laxative and in industry for cleaning and water treatment

Examples

Sodium phosphate industrial cleaner
Industrial cleaners often contain sodium phosphate to remove grease and stains.
Some processed foods use sodium phosphate as an additive for texture and preservation.
The doctor prescribed sodium phosphate as a laxative before the colonoscopy.
