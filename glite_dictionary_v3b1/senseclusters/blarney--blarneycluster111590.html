blarney

noun
flattering or persuasive talk
8.7 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbl…ëÀêrni / (AmE), / Ààbl…ëÀêni / (BrE)
talk that flatters or deceives in order to gain favor or advantage; smooth, persuasive flattery.

Examples

Sweet blarney
Don't let his charming blarney fool you into trusting him too quickly.
She tried to win the job with a bit of blarney during the interview.
The politician was full of blarney, making promises he couldn't keep.

Synonyms

  • sweet talk
    4.3 0.07/m
    sweet talk is friendly flattering speech used to charm or persuade someone directly, while blarney is smoother, more playful flattery to gain favor.
  • soft soap
    7.6 0.00/m
    soft soap is exaggerated praise often used to trick or influence people, while blarney is smooth flattering talk that seeks favor.
  • coaxing
    4.4 0.44/m
    coaxing is gentle, patient persuasion that may use praise and care, while blarney is more smooth, sly flattery to gain favor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flattery - excessive or insincere praise, especially to further one's own interests
