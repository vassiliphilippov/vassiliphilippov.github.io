ungrounded

adjective
not restricted
6.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡraʊndɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡraʊndɪd / (BrE)
not restricted, barred, or prevented from operating, moving, or participating as usual

Examples

Ungrounded airplane
After the inspection, the airline announced the aircraft would remain ungrounded and continue its schedule.
Because his grades improved, he was ungrounded and allowed to go out with friends again.
The new safety checks meant most planes were ungrounded within a week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grounded - not allowed to move, fly, or participate as a punishment or restriction
