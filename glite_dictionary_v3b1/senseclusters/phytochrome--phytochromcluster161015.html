phytochrome

noun
plant light pigment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪtəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtəˌkrəʊm / (BrE)
a plant pigment that detects light and helps regulate plant growth and development

Examples

Phytochrome pigment
The discovery of phytochrome helped scientists understand how plants respond to light.
Without phytochrome, many plants would not be able to adjust to seasonal changes.
Researchers study phytochrome to learn how light affects plant development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chlorophyll - the green pigment in plants that absorbs light for photosynthesis
