carbolic

noun
antiseptic compound
9.2
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
a corrosive organic compound, also known as phenol or carbolic acid, used as an antiseptic or disinfectant, especially in the past

Examples

A bottle of carbolic
The old hospital used carbolic to clean wounds before the discovery of modern antibiotics.
A strong smell of carbolic filled the air in the early operating rooms.
Nurses would wash their hands with carbolic to prevent the spread of infection.

  • synonym: phenol - a toxic, colorless crystalline compound used as an antiseptic
