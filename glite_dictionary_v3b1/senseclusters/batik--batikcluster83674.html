batik

noun
fabric dyed with removable wax patterns
Pronunciation: / bəˈtik / (AmE), / bəˈtiːk / (BrE)
A fabric that is dyed using a technique in which parts of the material are covered with removable wax to create patterns and prevent those areas from absorbing dye.

Examples

A batik shirt
She wore a beautiful batik dress with intricate floral designs.
The museum displayed an impressive collection of batik fabrics from across Southeast Asia.
Traditional Indonesian batik is known for its detailed and colorful patterns.
