invasive carp

noun
non-native, harmful carp species
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪsɪv kɑrp / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪsɪv kɑːp / (BrE)
a non-native species of carp that spreads rapidly and causes ecological harm by disrupting local ecosystems

Examples

Invasive carp species
Fisheries experts warn that invasive carp pose a threat to native fish populations.
Several barriers were installed to prevent invasive carp from entering the lake.
The government is funding research to limit the spread of invasive carp in major rivers.
