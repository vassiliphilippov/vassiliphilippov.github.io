palpitate

verb
to beat or tremble rapidly and irregularly
Pronunciation: / ˈpæl.pəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈpal.pɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to tremble, throb, or beat rapidly and irregularly, especially from strong emotion or as a physical reaction

Examples

Nostrils palpitate nervously
He watched his reflection as his nostrils palpitated with each anxious breath.
Her heart began to palpitate with excitement when she heard the news.
The bird's wings seemed to palpitate as it prepared to take flight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: throb - to beat or sound with a strong, regular rhythm; to pulsate strongly
