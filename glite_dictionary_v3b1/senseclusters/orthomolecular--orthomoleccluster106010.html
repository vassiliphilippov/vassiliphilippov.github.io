orthomolecular

adjective
relating to biochemical treatment with nutrients
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθoʊməˈlɛkjələr / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊməˈlɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to the treatment of disease by optimizing the concentrations of substances normally present in the body, especially through high doses of vitamins or minerals.

Examples

An orthomolecular treatment
Orthomolecular approaches often involve high doses of vitamins to correct imbalances.
She specialized in orthomolecular medicine to help her patients achieve better mental health.
Some scientists question the effectiveness of orthomolecular therapy for psychiatric disorders.
