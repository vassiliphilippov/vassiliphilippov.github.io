antisense

noun
gene-blocking molecule
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.tiː.sens/ /; / /ˈæn.taɪ.sens/ (AmE), /ˈæn.tɪ.sens/ /; / /ˈæn.taɪ.sens/ (BrE)
a strand or synthetic molecule of nucleic acid (DNA or RNA) that is complementary to a coding or messenger RNA sequence and can bind to it to block or change gene expression

Examples

An antisense molecule
Researchers used an antisense to prevent the production of a harmful protein in the experiment.
The new drug is based on antisense technology to target genetic diseases.
Doctors are hopeful that antisense therapies will offer new treatments for rare disorders.
Researchers designed an antisense molecule to block the expression of the harmful gene.
The antisense strand binds specifically to the messenger RNA, preventing protein production.
Scientists are exploring antisense therapies to treat certain genetic diseases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sense - the nucleic acid strand that carries genetic information for protein synthesis
