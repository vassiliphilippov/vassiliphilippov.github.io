terrorize

verb
intimidate by threats or violence
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛrəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəraɪz /; / ˈtɛrəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to frighten or intimidate someone very much, especially by using threats or violence

Examples

To terrorize a town
She felt terrorized by the constant phone calls and threats.
Some dictators terrorize their citizens to maintain control.
The gang used threats to terrorize the neighborhood for months.
Bullies often terrorize younger students on the playground.
Police arrested a man who had terrorized the neighborhood with threats and violence.
The villagers were terrorized by the bandits for months.
During the war, air raids would terrorise entire cities at night.
The dictator's regime actively sought to terrorise its opponents into silence.
The gang used threats to terrorise the local residents for months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - to make someone feel less afraid or worried
  • synonym: intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone, especially in order to make them do what one wants
  • antonym: reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears
