choke

noun
valve for carburetor air flow control
4.0 6.5
Pronunciation: /tʃoʊk/ (AmE), /tʃəʊk/ (BrE)
a valve that regulates the flow of air into the carburetor of a gasoline engine, used to enrich the fuel mixture for starting or running under certain conditions

Examples

Engine choke valve
A faulty choke can make the engine difficult to start.
He adjusted the choke before starting the cold engine.
Remember to push in the choke once the engine is running smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: throttle - a device controlling the flow of fuel or power to an engine
