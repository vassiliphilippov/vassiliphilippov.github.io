self-reproof

noun
the act of blaming oneself
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf.rɪˈpruf / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.rɪˈpruːf / (BrE)
the act of criticizing or blaming oneself for a fault or mistake

Examples

A self-reproof moment

After the argument, she sat in self-reproof, reflecting on her harsh words.
His constant self-reproof made it difficult for him to move on from his mistakes.
