tidal stream

noun
stream with tidal effects extending upstream
6.1 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààta…™d…ôl strim / (AmE), / Ààta…™d…ôl striÀêm / (BrE)
a stretch of water, usually a stream or river, in which the effects of the tide are observable far upstream, causing periodic changes in flow direction or level

Examples

Synonyms

  • tidal river
    4.7 0.05/m
    tidal river shows the tide rising and falling far upstream in a river, while tidal stream names any stream or river affected by tides.
  • tidewater stream
    7.9 0.00/m
    tidewater stream has flow and water levels changed by incoming sea tides far upstream, while tidal stream is any stream or river affected by tides.
  • tidewater river
    6.9 0.00/m
    tidewater river shows ocean tides reaching far upstream in a river, while tidal stream is a broader term for any watercourse affected by tides.
