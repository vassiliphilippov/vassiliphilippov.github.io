tidal stream

noun
stream with tidal effects extending upstream
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪdəl strim / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdəl striːm / (BrE)
a stretch of water, usually a stream or river, in which the effects of the tide are observable far upstream, causing periodic changes in flow direction or level

Examples

The tidal stream flow
Navigation can be challenging where the tidal stream reverses direction with each tide.
Scientists are studying how the tidal stream influences local ecosystems upstream.
The tidal stream in this river extends nearly twenty miles inland from the coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: estuary - the tidal mouth of a large river, where the tide meets the stream
  • degree higher: river - a large natural stream of water flowing in a channel to the sea, a lake, or another such stream
