waka

noun
Japanese poem form
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːkə / (AmE), / ˈwækə / (BrE)
a traditional Japanese poem with a set number of syllables, typically 31, often expressing personal feelings or nature

Examples

A Japanese waka poem
The poet composed a waka to express her feelings.
Students learned to write a waka as part of their Japanese literature class.
He admired the beauty and simplicity of the waka in the anthology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haiku - a short Japanese poem with 17 syllables in three lines
  • synonym: tanka - a form of waka with five lines and 31 syllables
