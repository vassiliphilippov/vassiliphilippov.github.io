represent

verb
stand for or symbolize something
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprəˈzɛnt /; / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to be a symbol of, stand for, or show something (including in art, models, mathematics, or as a sign)

Examples

To represent an idea
In this painting, the color red represents passion and energy.
The dove represents peace in many cultures.
The lion is often used to represent strength and courage.
Here, a flag can represent a nation or an organization.
In this equation, 'x' represents the total amount of money saved.
The red color on the map represents areas that are highly populated.
A function can represent the relationship between two sets.
Matrices are often used to represent linear transformations in mathematics.
This diagram represents the mapping between the domain and the range.

  • synonym: stand for - to be a symbol or substitute for something
