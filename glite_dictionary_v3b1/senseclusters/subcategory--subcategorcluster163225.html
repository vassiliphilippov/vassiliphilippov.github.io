subcategory

noun
smaller category within another
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbkætəˌɡɔri / (AmE), / ˈsʌbkætɪˌɡɔːri / (BrE)
a category whose objects and morphisms are selected from a larger category and inherit its structure, especially in mathematics

Examples

Mathematical subcategory
In mathematics, a subcategory is defined by selecting certain objects and morphisms from a larger category.
The professor explained how a subcategory inherits properties from its parent category.
Researchers often study a subcategory to focus on specific structures within a broader mathematical framework.
