novobiocin

noun
an antibiotic for Gram-positive bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊvoʊˈbaɪəsɪn / (AmE), / ˌnəʊvəʊˈbaɪəsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic obtained from an actinomycete and used to treat infections caused by Gram-positive bacteria

Examples

Novobiocin antibiotic treatment
Novobiocin is used in laboratory testing to differentiate certain bacterial species.
Researchers study the effectiveness of novobiocin against various Gram-positive bacteria.
The patient was prescribed novobiocin to treat a resistant staph infection.
