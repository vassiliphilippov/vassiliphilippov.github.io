madrono

noun
evergreen North American tree with edible berries
8.4 8.6
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈdroʊ.noʊ/ /; / /mæ.ˈdroʊ.noʊ/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdrəʊ.nəʊ/ /; / /mæ.ˈdrəʊ.nəʊ/ (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for its glossy leathery leaves, orange-red edible berries, and wood used for furniture; its bark is used for tanning.

Examples

The madrono tree
Furniture makers value the wood of the madrono for its attractive grain and durability.
The madrono tree produces bright orange-red berries that are enjoyed by wildlife and people alike.
The bark of the madrono has traditionally been used in the tanning process.
