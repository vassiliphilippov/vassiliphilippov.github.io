ductwork

noun
air duct system
4.6 0.42/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd åktÀåw…úrk /; / Ààd åktÀåw…ùk / (AmE), / Ààd åktÀåw…úÀêk / (BrE)
a system of ducts or tubes used to carry air, fluids, or cables in buildings, vehicles, or machines, or the activity of installing or maintaining them

Examples

Old ductwork system
The old ductwork needed to be replaced to improve the building's air quality.
During renovations, the workers discovered damaged ductwork above the ceiling tiles.
Properly installed ductwork helps maintain a comfortable temperature throughout the house.
The building's ductwork was inspected to ensure proper air circulation throughout all rooms.
Modern cars use complex ductwork to direct cool air to the engine and passengers.
Technicians repaired the damaged ductwork to prevent leaks in the ventilation system.
The old ductwork in the building needed to be replaced to improve air quality.
He spent the afternoon inspecting the ductwork for any signs of leaks or damage.
Modern ductwork is often insulated to increase energy efficiency in homes.

Synonyms

  • ducting
    4.4 0.04/m
    ducting is a network of tubes for carrying air or gas inside a building, while ductwork also covers cables, fluids, vehicles, and their installation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pipeline - a long tube for transporting liquids or gases
  • synonym: pipework - a system of pipes used to carry liquids or gases
  • antonym: plumbing - the system of pipes for water and waste in a building
  • synonym: ventilation - the process of supplying fresh air to a room or building
