reaccumulate

verb
to gather again
6.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê…ôÀàkjuÀêmj…ôÀåle…™t / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê…ôÀàkjuÀêmj äle…™t / (BrE)
to gather or collect again after having been reduced, removed, or lost

Examples

To reaccumulate snow
After the snow was cleared, it started to reaccumulate on the driveway overnight.
If you stop exercising, fat may reaccumulate in certain areas of your body.
The paperwork seemed to reaccumulate on his desk no matter how often he organized it.
