benightedness

noun
ignorance or lack of knowledge
Pronunciation: / bɪˈnaɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / bɪˈnaɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
a state of ignorance or lack of knowledge; the condition of not being enlightened (sometimes also referring to being in darkness, usually figuratively)

Examples

State of benightedness
The reformers worked hard to lift the community out of benightedness through education.
Centuries of benightedness ended when new ideas began to spread across the region.
He blamed the country's problems on the benightedness of its leaders.
The documentary exposed the benightedness that resulted from years of isolation and poor education.
Efforts to reduce benightedness in rural areas focused on improving access to schools and information.
He spoke passionately about overcoming benightedness through cultural exchange and learning.
The benightedness of the remote village made travel at night dangerous.
He lamented the benightedness that kept people from seeking education.
They worked to bring light and knowledge to the benightedness of the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enlightenment - the state of having knowledge or understanding
  • synonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
