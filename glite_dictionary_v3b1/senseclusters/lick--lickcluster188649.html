lick

verb
pass the tongue over something
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / lɪk / (AmE), / lɪk / (BrE)
to pass the tongue over the surface of something, usually to taste, moisten, or clean it

Examples

To lick ice cream
After spilling ice cream on his hand, he quickly licked it off.
She licked the envelope to seal it before mailing the letter.
The puppy licked my face as soon as I walked in the door.
She watched the cat quietly lick the milk from its bowl.
The child tried to lick the last bit of chocolate from the wrapper.
The dog began to lick the spilled ice cream off the floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bite - to use the teeth to cut into something
  • synonym: lap - to drink or take up liquid with the tongue, like an animal
  • synonym: taste - to distinguish the flavor of something by contact with the tongue
