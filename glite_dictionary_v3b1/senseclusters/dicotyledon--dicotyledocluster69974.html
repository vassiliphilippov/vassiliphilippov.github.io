dicotyledon

noun
plant with two seed leaves
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.kɑː.t̬l̩ˈiː.dən / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.kɒ.tɪˈliː.dən / (BrE)
A flowering plant whose seed typically has two embryonic leaves or cotyledons.

Examples

A dicotyledon plant
Many garden flowers, such as roses and sunflowers, are dicotyledons.
The oak tree is a classic example of a dicotyledon with its broad leaves and two seed leaves.
The seed of a dicotyledon splits easily into two halves when opened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monocotyledon - flowering plant with one cotyledon (seed leaf)
