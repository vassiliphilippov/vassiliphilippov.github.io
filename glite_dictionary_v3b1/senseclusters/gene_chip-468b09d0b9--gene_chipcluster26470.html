gene chip

noun
microchip used to analyze gene sequences
Pronunciation: / dʒiːn tʃɪp / (AmE), / dʒiːn tʃɪp / (BrE)
a microchip containing DNA probes used to detect and analyze gene sequences in biological samples

Examples

A gene chip analysis
A gene chip can analyze thousands of genes simultaneously in a single experiment.
Advances in gene chip technology have improved disease diagnostics.
Researchers used a gene chip to identify mutations in the patient's DNA.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microarray - an array of microscopic spots for analysis of DNA or protein
