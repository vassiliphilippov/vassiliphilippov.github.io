treatise

noun
formal written exposition
8.4 3.84/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtriÀêt…™s / (AmE), / ÀàtriÀêt…™s / (BrE)
A formal and systematic written work dealing with a subject in detail.

Examples

A treatise on history
Her latest treatise explores the impact of technology on modern society.
Students were required to read a lengthy treatise about international law for the seminar.
The philosopher published a groundbreaking treatise on ethics in the late 18th century.

Synonyms

  • monograph
    9.3 3.28/m
    A monograph is a focused, detailed study of one specialist subject, while a treatise is a broad, formal work that covers a subject systematically.
  • tractate
    9.9 0.01/m
    A tractate is a formal scholarly or religious study often on a single topic, while a treatise is a long systematic work on a subject.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dissertation - a long essay on a particular subject, especially one written for a university degree
  • degree lower: essay - a short piece of writing on a particular subject
  • degree higher: monograph - a detailed written study of a single specialized subject
