fianchetto

verb
to develop bishop in chess
Pronunciation: / fiˌænˈkɛtoʊ / (AmE), / fiˌænˈkɛtəʊ / (BrE)
to develop a bishop by moving it to the second rank of the adjacent knight's file, usually to control a long diagonal in chess

Examples

To fianchetto a bishop
Many players choose to fianchetto their bishop to control the long diagonal early in the game.
He decided not to fianchetto because he preferred a more direct attack in the center.
If you fianchetto your bishop, your king's side can become much safer after castling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: castle - to move the king and rook in a single move in chess
  • synonym: pin - to restrict a chess piece's movement by threatening a more valuable piece behind it
