mutual opposition

noun
a condition of being in direct opposition
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtʃuəl ˌɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəl ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship in which two attributes or tendencies are directly opposed to each other

Examples

Mutual opposition of emotions
Psychologists often explore the mutual opposition of conflicting emotions within a person.
The mutual opposition of the two parties was evident in every parliamentary debate.
There is a mutual opposition between tradition and innovation in many aspects of society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polarity - the state of having two opposite or contradictory tendencies
