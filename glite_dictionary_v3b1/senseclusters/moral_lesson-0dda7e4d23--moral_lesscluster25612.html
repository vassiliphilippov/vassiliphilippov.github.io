moral lesson

noun
teaching about right and wrong behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrəl ˈlɛsən / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl ˈlɛsən / (BrE)
a teaching or message in a story, event, or experience that conveys what is right or wrong behavior

Examples

A moral lesson in stories
Each fable in the collection contains a valuable moral lesson relevant to everyday life.
Teachers often use stories to illustrate a moral lesson about honesty or kindness.
The fairy tale ended with a clear moral lesson for young readers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lesson - an amount of teaching given at one time; instruction
  • synonym: moral - concerned with principles of right and wrong behavior
