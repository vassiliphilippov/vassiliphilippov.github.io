diode

noun
electronic device allowing one-way current
3.8 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.oʊd/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.əʊd/ (BrE)
an electronic component or device that allows electric current to flow in only one direction

Examples

A diode device
A light-emitting diode is commonly known as an LED.
Silicon diodes are used to protect sensitive electronic equipment from voltage spikes.
The circuit failed because one diode was installed backwards.
A diode allows current to pass in only one direction, preventing backflow.
Early radios used a vacuum tube diode to detect signals.
The technician replaced the faulty diode in the power supply circuit.
