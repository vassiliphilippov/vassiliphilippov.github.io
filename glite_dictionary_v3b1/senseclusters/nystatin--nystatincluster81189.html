nystatin

noun
antifungal and antibiotic medication
6.8
Pronunciation: / naɪˈstætɪn / (AmE), / naɪˈstætɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic and antifungal medication obtained from soil fungi, used to treat infections caused by Candida and other fungi

Examples

nystatin medication
Long-term use of nystatin may sometimes cause mild stomach upset.
Most cases of yeast infection respond well to nystatin cream.
The doctor prescribed nystatin to treat the patient's oral thrush.
