four-tailed bandage

noun
bandage with four tails for securing the jaw
5.0 7.7
Pronunciation: /fɔːr teɪld ˈbæn.dɪdʒ/ /; / /foʊr teɪld ˈbæn.dɪdʒ/ (AmE), /fɔː teɪld ˈbæn.dɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a bandage made from a strip of cloth split lengthwise at both ends to create four tails, used especially to secure the jaw or chin by tying the tails over the head

Examples

A four-tailed bandage for jaw
After the operation, a four-tailed bandage was used to restrict movement of his mandible.
Some dental injuries require a four-tailed bandage for proper immobilization.
The nurse applied a four-tailed bandage to stabilize the patient's injured jaw.
