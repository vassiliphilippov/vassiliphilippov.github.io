Biloxi

noun
member of a southeastern Mississippi Native American people
Pronunciation: / bɪˈlʌksi / (AmE), / bɪˈlɒksi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally living in southeastern Mississippi, belonging to the Siouan language family

Examples

A Biloxi person
A group of Biloxi settled along the Mississippi River in the eighteenth century.
He traces his ancestry back to the Biloxi people.
The Biloxi were known for their distinctive language and culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indigenous languages
