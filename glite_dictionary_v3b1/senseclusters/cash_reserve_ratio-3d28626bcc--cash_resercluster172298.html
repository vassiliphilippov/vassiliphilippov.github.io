cash reserve ratio

noun
bank deposit percentage held as reserve
3.7 5.9
Pronunciation: /kæʃ rɪ.ˈzɜːrv ˈreɪ.ʃoʊ/ /; / /kæʃ riː.ˈzɜːrv ˈreɪ.ʃoʊ/ (AmE), /kæʃ rɪ.ˈzɜːv ˈreɪ.ʃəʊ/ /; / /kæʃ rɪ.ˈzɜːv ˈreɪ.ʃɪ.əʊ/ (BrE)
the percentage of a bank's total deposits that must be held in reserve and not lent out, to ensure liquidity and stability

Examples

The cash reserve ratio requirement
A change in the cash reserve ratio can directly affect the amount of money available for lending.
Banks must always maintain the cash reserve ratio set by financial regulators.
The central bank increased the cash reserve ratio to control rising inflation in the economy.
