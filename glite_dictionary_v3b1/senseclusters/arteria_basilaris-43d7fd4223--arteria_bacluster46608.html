arteria basilaris

noun
the basilar artery of the brain
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪəriə bəˌsɪˈlɛərɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə bəˌsɪˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
the basilar artery; an unpaired artery that supplies blood to the pons, cerebellum, posterior cerebrum, and inner ear

Examples

The arteria basilaris artery
Aneurysms in the arteria basilaris are rare but potentially life-threatening.
Damage to the arteria basilaris can lead to serious brainstem strokes.
The neurologist identified a blockage in the arteria basilaris during the patient's brain scan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basilar artery - the major artery formed by the union of the vertebral arteries at the base of the brain
