loblolly pine

noun
pine tree native to the southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːbˌlɑːli paɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɒbˌlɒli paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree of the southeastern United States, with long needles in groups of three, reddish-brown fissured bark, and a full, bushy crown

Examples

The loblolly pine tree
Many birds build their nests in the thick branches of the loblolly pine.
The loblolly pine is a common sight in southern forests, towering above other trees.
The loblolly pine is valued for its fast growth and quality timber.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shortleaf pine - another native pine species of the southeastern United States
