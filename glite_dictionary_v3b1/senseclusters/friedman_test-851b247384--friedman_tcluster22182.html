Friedman test

noun
historical pregnancy test using a rabbit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdmən tɛst / (AmE), / ˈfriːdmən tɛst / (BrE)
a historical pregnancy test in which a woman's urine is injected into an unmated female rabbit, and pregnancy is indicated by changes in the rabbit's ovaries

Examples

The Friedman test in rabbits
Doctors rarely use the Friedman test now due to more humane and accurate procedures.
The Friedman test involved injecting urine into rabbits to check for signs of pregnancy.
The Friedman test was once a common method to determine pregnancy before modern tests were developed.
