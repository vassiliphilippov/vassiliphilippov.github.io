criminology

noun
scientific study of crime and law enforcement
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪməˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌkrɪmɪˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of crime, criminal behavior, and how laws are enforced

Examples

Study of criminology
Recent advances in criminology have helped improve methods of crime prevention.
She decided to major in criminology because she was fascinated by the causes of crime.
The professor's research in criminology focuses on juvenile delinquency and rehabilitation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criminal justice - the system of practices and institutions for upholding social control, deterring, and mitigating crime
  • degree lower: penology - the study of prison management and the treatment of offenders
  • degree lower: sociology - the study of society and social behavior
