sweatbox

noun
device for sweating tobacco, fruit, or hides
9.1 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈswɛtˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈswɛtˌbɒks / (BrE)
a device in which tobacco leaves, fruit, or animal hides are enclosed to produce sweating for curing or processing

Examples

Tobacco sweatbox
After harvesting, the tobacco was placed in a sweatbox to cure before processing.
Each orchard used a large sweatbox to help ripen fruit before it went to market.
The hides remained in the sweatbox for several days to facilitate easier tanning.
