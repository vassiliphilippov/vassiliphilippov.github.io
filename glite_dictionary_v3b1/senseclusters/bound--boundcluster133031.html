bound

adjective
united or attached (chemically/physically) with another substance
4.7
Pronunciation: / baʊnd / (AmE), / baʊnd / (BrE)
(of a substance or element) held together with another substance or material by chemical or physical means.

Examples

Bound iron
In this experiment, we distinguish between bound and unbound iron.
Researchers measured the amount of bound water in the protein samples.
The calcium in milk can exist in both bound and free forms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not combined or attached to other substances (in chemistry or biology)
