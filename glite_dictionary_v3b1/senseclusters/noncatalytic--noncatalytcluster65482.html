noncatalytic

adjective
lacking catalytic activity
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkəˈtælɪtɪk /; / ˌnɑnkætəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkəˈtælɪtɪk /; / ˌnɒnkætəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
not having the ability to act as a catalyst or not involving a catalyst in a chemical reaction

Examples

A noncatalytic surface
The experiment required a noncatalytic surface to ensure accurate results.
Scientists studied the noncatalytic properties of the new material in various chemical reactions.
A noncatalytic approach was chosen to avoid influencing the reaction rate.
The process followed a noncatalytic route, requiring higher temperatures to proceed.
Researchers compared the efficiency of catalytic and noncatalytic methods in the experiment.
A noncatalytic reaction often takes longer to reach completion than a catalytic one.
