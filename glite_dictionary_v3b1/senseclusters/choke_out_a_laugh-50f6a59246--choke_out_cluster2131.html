choke out a laugh

verb
to laugh with difficulty under emotion
6.8 5.6
Pronunciation: /tʃoʊk aʊt ə læf/ (AmE), /tʃəʊk aʊt ə lɑːf/ (BrE)
to manage to laugh with difficulty, especially when feeling surprised, shocked, or emotional

Examples

To choke out a laugh nervously
Despite the tension in the room, she choked out a laugh at his awkward joke.
He choked out a laugh, trying to hide his shock at the surprise announcement.
Sarah choked out a laugh, not quite believing what she had just heard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: burst out laughing - to start laughing suddenly and loudly
  • synonym: suppress a laugh - to prevent oneself from laughing
