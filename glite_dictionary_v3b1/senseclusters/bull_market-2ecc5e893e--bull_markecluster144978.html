bull market

noun
market with rising prices
4.5 1.07/m
Pronunciation: / b äl Ààm…ërk…™t / (AmE), / b äl Ààm…ëÀêk…™t / (BrE)
A period in the financial markets during which the prices of securities are rising or are expected to rise.

Examples

A bull market rises
Analysts predict that low interest rates could trigger a new bull market in technology shares.
During a prolonged bull market, many investors become overly confident and take greater risks.
The stock exchange enjoyed a strong bull market for more than six months last year.

Synonyms

  • uptrend
    4.0 0.04/m
    an uptrend is a steady technical rise in a single asset or short period, while a bull market is a broader, longer market-wide rise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bear market - a market characterized by falling prices for securities
