calcium stearate

noun
insoluble salt formed in hard water; also industrial additive
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.si.əm ˈstiː.əˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæl.si.əm ˈstɪə.reɪt / (BrE)
an insoluble white powder formed as a calcium salt of stearic and palmitic acids, used as a stabilizer, lubricant, and in soap scum produced by hard water

Examples

Calcium stearate white powder
Calcium stearate can build up as a stubborn white residue in bathroom sinks in hard water areas.
During the soap-making process, calcium stearate sometimes forms as an unwanted byproduct.
Food manufacturers sometimes add calcium stearate as a flow agent in powdered mixes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: soap scum - insoluble deposit formed when soap reacts with hard water
