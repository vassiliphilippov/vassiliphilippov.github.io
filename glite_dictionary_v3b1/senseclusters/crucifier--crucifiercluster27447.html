crucifier

noun
harsh persecutor
9.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkruÀês…™Àåfa…™…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàkruÀês…™Àåfa…™…ô / (BrE)
a person who harshly punishes, criticizes, or oppresses someone, especially in a severe or relentless way

Examples

Media crucifier
The media became her crucifier, criticizing her every move after the controversy.
He felt like a crucifier when he harshly judged his colleague for a minor mistake.
Online trolls can act as crucifiers, relentlessly attacking people on social media.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.01/m

  • oppressor
    7.5 2.29/m
    an oppressor uses power to treat groups cruelly and unfairly, while a crucifier punishes and criticizes people in a relentless, harsh way.
  • persecutor
    7.9 0.67/m
    a persecutor targets people for race, faith, or beliefs and harasses them, while a crucifier punishes others harshly without that specific motive.
  • tormentor
    7.9 0.74/m
    a tormentor causes repeated mental or physical pain to worry someone, while a crucifier focuses on harsh punishment, criticism, or oppression.
  • torturer
    7.7 1.16/m
    a torturer inflicts severe physical pain to force or punish someone, while a crucifier can punish or criticize but not always use physical torture.
  • victimizer
    8.3 0.26/m
    a victimizer harms or exploits people to make them victims, while a crucifier suggests severe punishment, public criticism, or oppressive power.
