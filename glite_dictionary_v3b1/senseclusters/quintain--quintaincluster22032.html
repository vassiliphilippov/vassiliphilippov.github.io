quintain

noun
medieval riding contest
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪnˌteɪn / (AmE), / ˈkwɪnˌteɪn / (BrE)
a medieval contest or training exercise in which a rider on horseback tried to strike a target with a lance

Examples

Medieval quintain contest
The knights practiced at the quintain to improve their accuracy before the tournament.
During the fair, children watched riders compete in the quintain with excitement.
A wooden post served as the quintain for the young squire's training session.

Related Terms

  • synonym: joust - a medieval sporting contest between two horsemen with lances
