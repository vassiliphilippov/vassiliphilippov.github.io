yerk

verb
to jerk suddenly
Pronunciation: / jɜrk / (AmE), / jɜːk / (BrE)
to jerk, strike, or throw with a sudden, sharp movement

Examples

To yerk the reins
The rider gave the reins a sharp yerk to urge the horse forward.
He yerked the door open with a sudden pull.
With a quick yerk, she tossed the ball across the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jerk - to move or make something move with a sudden quick motion
  • synonym: toss - to throw something lightly or carelessly
