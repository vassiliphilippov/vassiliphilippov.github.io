amount

verb
to be equivalent or add up to (verb)
Pronunciation: / əˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / əˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to add up to or be equal to a particular value, effect, or result

Examples

Amount to nothing
All their promises amounted to very little in the end.
Despite his efforts, the project did not amount to a lasting change.
Her actions will never amount to anything significant unless she commits fully.
All the evidence amounts to a strong case against him.
Her yearly expenses amount to only a few hundred dollars.
The final bill amounted to over $3,000, which was more than we expected.
