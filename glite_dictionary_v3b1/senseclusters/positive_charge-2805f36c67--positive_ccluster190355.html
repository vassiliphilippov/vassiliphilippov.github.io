positive charge

noun
electrical charge from lack of electrons
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzətɪv tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an electrical charge resulting from a deficiency of electrons, having a higher electric potential

Examples

A positive charge
A positive charge is attracted to a negative charge, demonstrating the principle of electric attraction.
The balloon stuck to the wall because it had a positive charge.
When an atom loses an electron, it acquires a positive charge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: negative charge - an electrical charge resulting from an excess of electrons
