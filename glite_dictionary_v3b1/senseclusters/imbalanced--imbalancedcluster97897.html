imbalanced

adjective
lacking balance or equilibrium
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbælənst / (AmE), / ɪmˈbælənst / (BrE)
lacking balance or equilibrium; not properly proportioned or arranged.

Examples

An imbalanced gait
A slight injury left her gait imbalanced, making it difficult to walk straight.
His diet was imbalanced and lacked essential nutrients, which affected his health.
The new team structure felt imbalanced, with too many people assigned to one project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: balanced - in a state of equilibrium; properly proportioned or arranged
  • synonym: unstable - prone to change, fail, or fall apart; not stable
