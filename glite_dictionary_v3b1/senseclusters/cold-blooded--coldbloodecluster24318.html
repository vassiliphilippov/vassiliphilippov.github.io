cold-blooded

adjective
having body temperature varying with environment (animals)
1.6 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkoʊld.blʌd.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈkəʊld.blʌd.ɪd/ (BrE)
(of animals) having a body temperature that varies with the environment, not internally regulated.

Examples

Cold-blooded reptiles
A cold-blooded fish's activity level often depends on the water temperature.
Many amphibians are cold-blooded, so they bask in the sun to warm up.
Reptiles like snakes and lizards are cold-blooded animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: warm-blooded - (of animals) having a constant body temperature that is internally regulated
