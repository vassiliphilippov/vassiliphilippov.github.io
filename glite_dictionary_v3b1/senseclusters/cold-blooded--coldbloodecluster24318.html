cold-blooded

adjective
having body temperature varying with environment (animals)

(of animals) having a body temperature that varies with the environment, not internally regulated.

Examples

Cold-blooded reptiles
A cold-blooded fish's activity level often depends on the water temperature.
Many amphibians are cold-blooded, so they bask in the sun to warm up.
Reptiles like snakes and lizards are cold-blooded animals.

Synonyms

  • ectothermic
    9.1 0.03/m
    ectothermic is a scientific term for animals that use environmental heat to control body temperature, while cold-blooded is the common everyday term.
  • poikilothermic
    9.9 0.01/m
    poikilothermic describes animals whose internal temperature varies with the environment, while cold-blooded is the broader everyday label.
  • heterothermic
    9.9 0.00/m
    heterothermic refers to animals that sometimes have changing body temperatures and may exclude birds and mammals, while cold-blooded means not internally regulated.

Related Terms
