longneck

noun
animal with long neck
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌnɛk / (BrE)
an animal, especially a dinosaur or giraffe, that has a very long neck

Examples

A giant longneck
The museum displayed a skeleton of a longneck that once roamed the ancient forests.
Children often call giraffes longnecks because of their tall, slender necks.
In the movie, the friendly longneck helped the other animals cross the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: giraffe - a tall African mammal with a very long neck
  • synonym: sauropod - a large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur
