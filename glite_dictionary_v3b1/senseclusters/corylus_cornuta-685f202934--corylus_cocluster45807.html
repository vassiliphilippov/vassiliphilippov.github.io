Corylus cornuta

noun
A North American hazel species with beaked nuts
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.ɪ.ləs kɔrˈnuː.tə / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ɪ.ləs kɔːˈnjuː.tə / (BrE)
A species of hazel native to North America, known for its nuts enclosed in a long, beak-like husk.

Examples

Corylus cornuta tree
Many forests in western North America contain thriving stands of Corylus cornuta.
The long, beak-like husk distinguishes Corylus cornuta from other hazel species.
The nuts of the Corylus cornuta are prized by both wildlife and foragers alike.
