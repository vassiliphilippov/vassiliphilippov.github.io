overbuy

verb
to buy too much
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbaɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbaɪ / (BrE)
to buy more of something than is needed or can be used

Examples

To overbuy supplies
They overbought raw materials for the factory and now face storage problems.
During the sale, she overbought groceries and had to give some away.
Investors sometimes overbuy stocks when the market is rising quickly.
It is easy to overbuy groceries when shopping while hungry.
Many people overbuy clothes during sales and end up not wearing them.
If you overbuy supplies, you might waste money and storage space.
Many people tend to overbuy food when preparing for a big holiday meal.
If you overbuy clothes during sales, you might end up with items you never wear.
She realized she had overbought supplies for the project and returned the extras.
