social work

noun
profession providing support to disadvantaged individuals or groups
1.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.ʃəl wɜːrk/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.ʃəl wɜːk/ (BrE)
work carried out by trained professionals to help improve the well-being of individuals, families, or communities, especially those who are disadvantaged or vulnerable.

Examples

Social work practice
After graduating, she dedicated her life to social work in underserved communities.
He studied social work because he wanted to make a positive impact on young people's lives.
Many people rely on social work services for assistance with housing or mental health issues.
