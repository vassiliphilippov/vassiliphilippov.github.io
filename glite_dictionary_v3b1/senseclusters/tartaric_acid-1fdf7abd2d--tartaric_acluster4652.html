tartaric acid

noun
acid found in fruits and used in baking
Pronunciation: / tɑrˈtærɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / tɑːˈtærɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline organic acid found in many fruits, used in baking, soft drinks, and as a food additive

Examples

Tartaric acid in baking
Tartaric acid is commonly added to baking powder to help baked goods rise.
Many fruits, especially grapes, contain natural tartaric acid.
Some candies use tartaric acid to provide a tangy, sour flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citric acid - a weak organic acid found in citrus fruits
