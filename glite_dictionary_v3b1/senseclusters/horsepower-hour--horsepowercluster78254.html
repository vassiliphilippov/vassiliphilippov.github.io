horsepower-hour

noun
unit of work (one horsepower for one hour)
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrsˌpaʊ.ɚ aʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌpaʊ.ə aʊə / (BrE)
A unit of work equal to the work performed by one horsepower in one hour, commonly used in engineering contexts.

Examples

One horsepower-hour
The contract stipulated a minimum output of one horsepower-hour per day.
The generator produced over 50 horsepower-hours of energy during its operation.
To calculate the machine's total output, engineers often use horsepower-hour as a standard unit.
