kenaf

noun
fiber plant (Hibiscus cannabinus)
Pronunciation: / kəˈnæf / (AmE), / kəˈnæf / (BrE)
A valuable fiber obtained from the stalks of the tropical plant Hibiscus cannabinus, used in rope, twine, and other products.

Examples

Kenaf fiber rope
Farmers in the region have begun cultivating kenaf as a cash crop.
Ropes made from kenaf are strong and environmentally friendly.
The paper was made from kenaf, a sustainable alternative to wood pulp.

Related Terms
