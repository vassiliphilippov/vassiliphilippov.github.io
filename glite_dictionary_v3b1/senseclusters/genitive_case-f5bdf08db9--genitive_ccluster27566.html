genitive case

noun
possessive grammatical case
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʤɛnətɪv keɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtɪv keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case indicating possession or a close relationship, usually marked in English by the addition of 's or an apostrophe

Examples

The genitive case form
In English, the genitive case is shown by adding 's to a noun, as in 'the dog's leash'.
