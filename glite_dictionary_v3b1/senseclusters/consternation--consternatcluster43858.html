consternation

noun
sudden fear or anxiety
7.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.stər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.stər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒn.stə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.stə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A sudden feeling of anxiety, shock, or confusion, often caused by something unexpected or frightening.

Examples

Great consternation
The unexpected thunderstorm caused consternation among the outdoor wedding guests.
There was widespread consternation when the company announced hundreds of layoffs.
To her consternation, the door was locked and she was trapped inside.
