consternation

noun
sudden fear or anxiety
7.7 1.74/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀênst…ôrÀàne…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåk…ínst…ôÀàne…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
A sudden feeling of anxiety, shock, or confusion, often caused by something unexpected or frightening.

Examples

Great consternation
The unexpected thunderstorm caused consternation among the outdoor wedding guests.
There was widespread consternation when the company announced hundreds of layoffs.
To her consternation, the door was locked and she was trapped inside.

Synonyms

  • dismay
    3.4 5.24/m
    dismay is a strong, often outward feeling of shock or disappointment, while consternation is sudden anxious shock and confusion.
  • disconcertment
    8.4 0.00/m
    disconcertment is an uneasy, embarrassed feeling after something unsettling, while consternation is sudden anxious shock and deep confusion.
