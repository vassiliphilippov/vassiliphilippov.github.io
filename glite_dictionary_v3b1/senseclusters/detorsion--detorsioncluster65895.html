detorsion

noun
untwisting in gastropods
Pronunciation: / diˈtɔrʒən / (AmE), / diˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
the evolutionary or developmental process in gastropods where the body untwists to return to a more symmetrical form

Examples

Detorsion in snails
Scientists observed detorsion in certain snail species as they developed a more balanced body structure.
The process of detorsion can help explain the evolutionary changes in gastropod anatomy.
During embryonic development, some gastropods undergo detorsion to achieve a less twisted body plan.

Related Terms
