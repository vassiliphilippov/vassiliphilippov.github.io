bitterroot

noun
succulent plant of the Rocky Mountains; Montana state flower
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬.ɚˌrut / (AmE), / ˈbɪt.ərˌruːt / (BrE)
a low, succulent plant (Lewisia rediviva) native to the Rocky Mountains, with fleshy edible roots and pink or white flowers; the state flower of Montana.

Examples

The bitterroot plant
Early settlers relied on bitterroot as a food source during harsh winters.
Hiking in Montana, we spotted the state flower, a single bitterroot nestled among the rocks.
The bitterroot blooms each spring, covering the valleys with delicate pink flowers.
