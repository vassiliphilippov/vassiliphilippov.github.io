parasitoid

adjective
host-killing parasitic
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌsɪtɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpærəsɪtɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to a parasite that ultimately kills its host as part of its life cycle.

Examples

Parasitoid wasp behavior
The parasitoid relationship between the wasp and its host is a fascinating subject for entomologists.
Some insects display parasitoid behavior, eventually killing the organisms they inhabit.
Researchers observed the parasitoid effects of the larvae on the caterpillar population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parasitic - living as a parasite on another organism
