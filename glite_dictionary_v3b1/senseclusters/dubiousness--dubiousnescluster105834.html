dubiousness

noun
state of being doubtful or uncertain
7.8 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀêbi…ôsn…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàdjuÀêbi…ôsn…ôs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being uncertain, doubtful, or suspicious about something

Examples

Dubiousness about plans
His dubiousness about the project's chances slowed down the team's progress.
The company's financial statements created considerable dubiousness among the investors.
There was a clear sense of dubiousness in her voice when she agreed to the plan.
His proposal was met with dubiousness by the team, who wanted more details.
The dubiousness of the evidence made the jury hesitant to deliver a verdict.
There was a sense of dubiousness in her voice as she questioned the report's findings.

Synonyms

  • dubiety
    9.9 0.05/m
    dubiety is a formal, rare word for a state of doubt, while dubiousness means the general quality of being unsure or suspicious.
  • doubtfulness
    5.5 0.01/m
    doubtfulness describes a clear feeling of not being convinced, while dubiousness often includes a sense of being suspicious.
  • doubt
    1.6 87/m
    doubt is a personal feeling of uncertainty or lack of belief, while dubiousness names the broader quality of doubt or suspicion.
  • questionability
    7.5 0.01/m
    questionability shows that something can be wrong or unreliable, while dubiousness focuses on doubt or suspicion about that thing.
  • uncertainty
    4.0 27/m
    uncertainty means not knowing what will happen or lacking surety, while dubiousness adds a feeling that something may be suspicious.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - the state of being certain or confident about something
  • synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
