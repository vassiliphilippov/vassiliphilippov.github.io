catalytic cracker

noun
chemical reactor for breaking down oil using a catalyst
Pronunciation: / kəˈtæl.ə.tɪk ˈkræk.ɚ / (AmE), / kəˈtæl.ɪ.tɪk ˈkræk.ə / (BrE)
a chemical reactor used to break down large hydrocarbon molecules in oils into fuels with lower boiling points by using a catalyst

Examples

A catalytic cracker unit
A catalytic cracker plays a crucial role in converting heavy oils into lighter, more valuable fuels.
Regular maintenance of the catalytic cracker ensures efficient operation and product quality.
The refinery installed a new catalytic cracker to increase gasoline production.
