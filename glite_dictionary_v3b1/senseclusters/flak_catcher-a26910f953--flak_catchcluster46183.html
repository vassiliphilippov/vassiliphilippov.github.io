flak catcher

noun
person who deflects criticism for an employer
Pronunciation: / flæk ˈkætʃər / (AmE), / flæk ˈkatʃə / (BrE)
A person, especially a spokesperson, whose role is to receive and deflect criticism directed at their employer or organization.

Examples

A flak catcher spokesperson
As a seasoned flak catcher, she always managed to turn criticism into praise for her boss.
During the press conference, the flak catcher expertly diverted attention from the recent scandal.
The company's flak catcher handled all the harsh questions from reporters with practiced ease.
