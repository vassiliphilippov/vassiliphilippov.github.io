gapers' block

noun
traffic congestion caused by onlookers
7.3 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈgeɪ.pərz blɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈgeɪ.pəz blɒk/ (BrE)
A traffic slowdown or congestion caused by motorists slowing down to look at an incident or spectacle, especially an accident.

Examples

A gapers' block accident
Police urged drivers not to cause a gapers' block by slowing down to watch the fire.
The gapers' block was worse than the accident itself, with cars crawling for miles.
We sat in a gapers' block for nearly thirty minutes after the crash on the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rubbernecking - the act of staring at something of interest, especially accidents, causing traffic delays
  • synonym: traffic jam - a long line of vehicles unable to move or moving very slowly
