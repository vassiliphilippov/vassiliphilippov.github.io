firm

adjective
securely established; stable
Pronunciation: / fɝm /; / fɝːm / (AmE), / fɜːm / (BrE)
secure, stable, or fixed in place; not likely to move, change, or weaken

Examples

Firm foundation
After years of hard work, she now holds a firm position in the company.
His reputation as an expert in the field is firm and well-deserved.
The agreement established a firm foundation for future collaboration.
His voice was firm when he delivered the instructions to the team.
Make sure the chair is standing on a firm surface before sitting down.
She maintained a firm grip on the ladder while cleaning the windows.
She stood by her decision with a firm resolve, refusing to reconsider.
The company made a firm offer to acquire its competitor.
We need a firm commitment before moving forward with the project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - able to change or be modified easily
  • synonym: solid - stable and not likely to move; strong or reliable
  • synonym: stable - not likely to change or fail
  • synonym: steady - not shaking or moving; stable
  • antonym: unstable - not securely fixed; likely to change
  • antonym: unsteady - not firm or stable; shaking or trembling
  • antonym: volatile - liable to change rapidly and unpredictably, especially for the worse
  • antonym: weak - not strong or secure; lacking firmness
