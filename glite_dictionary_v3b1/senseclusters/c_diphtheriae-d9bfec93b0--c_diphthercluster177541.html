C. diphtheriae

noun
bacterium causing diphtheria
9.5
Pronunciation: / si ˌdɪfˈθɪəriˌaɪ / (AmE), / si ˌdɪfˈθɪəriˌiː / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes diphtheria in humans

Examples

C. diphtheriae bacterium
C. diphtheriae is responsible for the symptoms of diphtheria in infected individuals.
Doctors identified the presence of C. diphtheriae in the patient's throat swab.
The vaccine prevents infection by C. diphtheriae and thereby protects against diphtheria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diphtheria - a serious infectious disease caused by Corynebacterium diphtheriae
