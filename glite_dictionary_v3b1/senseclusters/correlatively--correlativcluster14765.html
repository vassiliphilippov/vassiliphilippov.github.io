correlatively

adverb
using correlative pairs
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛlətɪvli / (AmE), / kəˈrɛlətɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that involves or expresses a relationship between two or more elements, especially using correlative pairs in grammar.

Examples

Correlatively joined clauses
The two clauses are joined correlatively by the words 'either' and 'or' in the sentence.
She explained how 'both' and 'and' function correlatively to connect similar ideas.
In English, certain words are always used correlatively to show a balanced relationship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independently - in a way that is not connected or related
  • synonym: mutually - in a way that involves two or more parties equally
