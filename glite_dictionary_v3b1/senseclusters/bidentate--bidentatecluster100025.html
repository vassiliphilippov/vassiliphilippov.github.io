bidentate

adjective
having two toothlike projections
Pronunciation: / baɪˈdɛnteɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈdɛnteɪt / (BrE)
having two points or projections, especially describing something (like a molecule or ligand) that attaches at two places

Examples

Bidentate ligand
Certain ligands in coordination chemistry are described as bidentate because they attach through two donor atoms.
The caterpillar’s bidentate mandibles made it easy to identify the species.
The fossil had a bidentate jaw structure, making it unique among related species.
A bidentate ligand can form two bonds with a single metal ion in a complex.
The stability of the complex increases when a bidentate molecule is used.
Chemists often choose bidentate ligands for their ability to create more secure attachments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monodentate - attaching at a single point
  • degree higher: polydentate - attaching at multiple points
  • degree higher: tridentate - having three toothlike projections or points
  • degree lower: unidentate - having a single toothlike projection or point
