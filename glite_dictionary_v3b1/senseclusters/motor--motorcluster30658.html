motor

adjective
relating to nerves controlling movement
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊtər /; / ˈmoʊ.tər / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə /; / ˈməʊ.tər / (BrE)
relating to movement or to the nerves and muscles that make movement possible

Examples

Motor skills
Certain diseases may impair motor nerves, leading to muscle weakness.
Damage to the motor cortex can affect voluntary muscle movement.
The doctor tested his motor responses to evaluate nerve function.
A motor impulse travels from the brain to the muscles to initiate movement.
The device converts electrical energy into motor energy.
The engine generates motor power that drives the wheels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: motive - (of power or force) causing motion; relating to movement
  • antonym: sensory - relating to nerves transmitting impulses toward the CNS from sense organs
