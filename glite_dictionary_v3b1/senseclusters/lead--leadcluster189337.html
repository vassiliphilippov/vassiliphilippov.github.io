lead

noun
position of initiator or example
4.6 14/m
Pronunciation: / lid /; / liÀêd / (AmE), / liÀêd / (BrE)
the person who plays the main role in a play, film, or show, or the person who initiates or is in charge of something

Examples

Lead actor
She decided to take the lead in organizing the team project.
The company is hoping to set an industry lead in sustainable practices.
When he offered a suggestion, others were quick to follow his lead.
After his impressive audition, he was chosen as the lead in the new musical.
She moved to New York hoping to land a lead in a Broadway show.
The film's lead received an award for her outstanding performance.

Synonyms

    3.9 1.30/m
    leading man plays the male role on stage or screen, while lead refers to the main role or person in charge.
  • leading lady
    3.1 0.64/m
    leading lady plays the female role on stage or screen, while lead refers to the main role or person in charge.
  • principal
    3.6 38/m
    principal is the most important person in an activity or group, while lead often refers to the main role or person in charge.
  • leader
    1.0 252/m
    leader directs and inspires a group or country, while lead can mean the main role or the person who starts or runs something.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follow - to go or come after; to move or act according to the lead of another
  • synonym: star - a famous actor, especially one who plays leading roles
  • antonym: supporting actor - an actor in a secondary role
