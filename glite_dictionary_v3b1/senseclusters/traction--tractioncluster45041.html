traction

noun
medical pulling force for healing bones
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtræk.ʃən / (BrE)
(medicine) the application of a sustained pulling force to a limb or bone, especially to realign or immobilize it during healing.

Examples

Leg in traction
After the accident, his broken leg was placed in traction for two weeks to ensure it healed correctly.
Children with certain bone injuries may require long-term traction in hospital settings.
The doctor recommended traction to realign the patient’s fractured femur.
