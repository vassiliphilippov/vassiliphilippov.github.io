march

verb
walk in a procession or organized group
1.0 19/m
Pronunciation: / m…ërt É / (AmE), / m…ëÀêt É / (BrE)
to walk in a deliberate, regular, or organized way, often as part of a group, demonstration, or to show something off

Examples

To march in step
Graduates marched into the auditorium as the band played.
Protesters marched through the city demanding change.
The soldiers marched proudly down the main street during the parade.
After winning the trophy, they marched it around the field for all to see.
He marched his new dog around the neighborhood, eager to impress his friends.
She marched her children through the store, making sure everyone noticed them.
Activists marched peacefully, carrying banners and chanting slogans.
She plans to march with her friends at the demonstration on Saturday.
Thousands of students marched through the city demanding climate action.

Synonyms

  • parade
    2.9 1.76/m
    parade is a public, often celebratory procession that shows people or things, while march can be a deliberate group walk, often a protest.
  • troop
    3.8 0.66/m
    troop is to move together slowly and in order as a group, while march is a more formal, steady group walk with purpose.
  • process
    7.2 0.55/m
    process is to move in a formal line during a ceremony or procession, while march is used for many organized group walks.
  • parade
    3.9 1.89/m
    parade is to walk or display oneself to attract attention in a showy way, while march is usually an organized group walk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amble - to walk at a slow, relaxed pace
  • synonym: parade - to walk or display oneself in a formal procession
  • synonym: protest - express strong objection publicly
  • antonym: straggle - to move along slowly, scattered rather than in formation
  • synonym: strut - to walk in a proud or confident way
