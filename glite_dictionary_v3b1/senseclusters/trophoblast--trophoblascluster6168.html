trophoblast

noun
membrane forming outer wall of blastocyst
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊfəˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈtrɒfəˌblɑːst / (BrE)
the outer membrane of the blastocyst in early embryonic development that facilitates implantation in the uterine wall and later contributes to the formation of the placenta

Examples

The trophoblast membrane
Once the trophoblast is established, it begins to produce hormones essential for pregnancy.
Researchers studied how abnormalities in the trophoblast can affect placental development.
The trophoblast plays a crucial role in the embryo's attachment to the uterine lining.
