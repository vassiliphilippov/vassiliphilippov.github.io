totalization

noun
making unified whole
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌtoʊtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtəʊtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something complete or unified by combining all parts into a single whole, often reducing differences

Examples

Totalization of departments
The totalization of the company's departments led to a more streamlined but less flexible organization.
Some fear that the totalization of cultural practices erases important local traditions.
The policy encouraged the totalization of data from different sources into a single database.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centralization - the act of bringing activities or authority under a single control
  • antonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into separate or distinct parts
  • synonym: unification - the process of making or becoming united or whole
