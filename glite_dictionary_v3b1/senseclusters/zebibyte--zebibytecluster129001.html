zebibyte

noun
unit of digital information (2^70 bytes)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈziːbɪˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈziːbɪˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 exbibytes or 2^70 bytes (1,180,591,620,717,411,303,424 bytes).

Examples

A zebibyte unit
A zebibyte is equivalent to 1,024 exbibytes when calculating digital storage capacity.
As technology advances, scientists may one day work with datasets measuring in zebibytes.
Modern data centers rarely need a zebibyte of storage, but the figure is useful for future projections.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: exbibyte - unit of digital information equal to 2^60 bytes
  • degree higher: yobibyte - unit of digital information equal to 2^80 bytes
  • synonym: zettabyte - unit of digital information equal to 10^21 bytes (decimal, not binary)
