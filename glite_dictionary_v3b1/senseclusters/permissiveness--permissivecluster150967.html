permissiveness

noun
openness of a license
6.5
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈmɪ.sɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /pə.ˈmɪ.sɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
the degree to which a software or other legal license allows redistribution, modification, and use of the work; the openness of licensing terms.

Examples

License permissiveness
The project's success was partly due to the license's permissiveness, which encouraged widespread reuse and contributions.
When choosing a library, she checked the permissiveness of the license to avoid legal complications later.
Companies debated the balance between innovation and control, arguing about product permissiveness in licensing terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: openness - the quality of allowing free access, modification, or reuse
  • antonym: restrictiveness - the degree to which a license limits redistribution, modification, or reuse
