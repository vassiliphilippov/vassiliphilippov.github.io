acousmatic

noun
music without visible source
Pronunciation: / əˌkuːzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / əˌkaʊzˈmætɪk / (BrE)
a piece of recorded or fixed electroacoustic music, or a sound event, intended to be heard without seeing its source.

Examples

An acousmatic piece
The composer presented an acousmatic that filled the hall with mysterious, unseen sounds.
At the concert, the audience listened to an acousmatic without knowing what instruments created the music.
Many modern festivals feature at least one acousmatic to showcase innovative sound design.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electroacoustic - music involving electronic sound production
  • antonym: live performance - music performed with visible musicians
