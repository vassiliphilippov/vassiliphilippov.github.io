Antiochene

noun
follower of Antiochene tradition
Pronunciation: / ˈæntiəˌkiːn / (AmE), / ˈæntiəˌkiːn / (BrE)
a member or follower of the Antiochene church or theological tradition, especially in early Christianity

Examples

Antiochene theologian
The Antiochene perspective influenced many early church debates about the nature of Christ.
Scholars often compare Antiochene and Alexandrian approaches to biblical interpretation.
He identified himself as an Antiochene during the theological discussions at the council.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Alexandrian - a member or follower of the Alexandrian theological tradition
