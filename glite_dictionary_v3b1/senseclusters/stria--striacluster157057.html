stria

noun
thin groove or ridge, esp. in tissue or rocks
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a thin, narrow line, groove, or band, often one of many, that is different in color, texture, or form from the surrounding area

Examples

A stria in rock
Each stria in the mineral indicated a separate period of deposition.
Glaciers carved a visible stria into the exposed rock face.
The muscle slide revealed a prominent stria running along the tissue sample.
A single stria on the butterfly's wing was bright blue.
Medical scans revealed multiple striae on the patient's liver.
The marble was prized for the golden striae running through its white surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface
  • synonym: stripe - a long, narrow band or strip, typically one of several
