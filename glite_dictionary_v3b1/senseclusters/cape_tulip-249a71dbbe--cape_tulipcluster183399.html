Cape tulip

noun
South African plant with cup-shaped flowers
Pronunciation: / keɪp ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / keɪp ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to South Africa, known for its large reddish-purple-barred leaves and coral-red, cup-shaped flowers with yellow stamens.

Examples

The Cape tulip plant
Farmers in Australia consider the Cape tulip to be an invasive weed toxic to livestock.
The Cape tulip can add a splash of color to your garden in early spring.
We admired the vibrant, coral-red flowers of the Cape tulip during our hike through the botanical reserve.
