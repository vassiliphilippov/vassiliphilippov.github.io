virtus

proper noun
Roman deity of courage
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrtuːs / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtʊs / (BrE)
the Roman personification or deity representing courage, bravery, and military strength

Examples

Virtus as a goddess
In ancient Rome, soldiers prayed to virtus before entering battle.
Temples dedicated to virtus were built to honor bravery and military strength.
The concept of virtus was central to Roman ideals of heroism and honor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery
  • synonym: valor - great courage in the face of danger
