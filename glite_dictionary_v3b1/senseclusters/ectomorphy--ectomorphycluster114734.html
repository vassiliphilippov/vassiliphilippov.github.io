ectomorphy

noun
slender, lightly built body type
9.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.toʊˌmɔr.fi / (AmE), / ˈɛk.təʊˌmɔː.fi / (BrE)
The quality or state of having a slender, lightweight, and lightly muscled body type, as classified in some physical anthropology and physiology systems.

Examples

High ectomorphy body
A person with high ectomorphy tends to have long limbs and little body fat.
Her ectomorphy was a defining feature, making her well-suited for ballet.
The athlete's pronounced ectomorphy gave him an advantage in long-distance running.
