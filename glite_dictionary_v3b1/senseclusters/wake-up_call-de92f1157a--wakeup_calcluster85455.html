wake-up call

noun
event that prompts urgent action
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪk ʌp kɔl / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ʌp kɔːl / (BrE)
an event or situation that makes people realize they need to take action or change something

Examples

A wake-up call for change
For many people, climate change is the ultimate wake-up call demanding immediate action.
Her diagnosis served as a wake-up call to prioritize her health and well-being.
The economic crisis was a wake-up call for governments to reform financial regulations.
Failing that exam was the wake-up call he needed to take his studies seriously.
His heart attack was a real wake-up call to start living healthier.
The recent data breach should be a wake-up call for companies to improve their security.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - a warning or call to attention of danger or a problem
  • synonym: warning - something that serves to warn of danger or problems
