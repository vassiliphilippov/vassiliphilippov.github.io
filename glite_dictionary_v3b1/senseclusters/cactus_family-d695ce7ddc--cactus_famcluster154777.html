cactus family

noun
group of plants comprising the cacti
Pronunciation: / ˈkæktəs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkaktəs ˈfaməli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Cactaceae) consisting of cacti, typically found in arid regions of the Americas, characterized by thick, fleshy stems adapted for water storage

Examples

The cactus family plants
Many species within the cactus family are able to survive long periods without rainfall.
Researchers study how plants in the cactus family store water to survive harsh climates.
The saguaro, an iconic member of the cactus family, can grow up to 12 meters tall.
