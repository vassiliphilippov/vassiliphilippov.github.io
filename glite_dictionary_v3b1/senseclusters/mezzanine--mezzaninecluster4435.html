mezzanine

noun
intermediate floor above ground level
6.7 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…õz.…ôÀånin / (AmE), / Ààm…õz.…ôniÀên / (BrE)
an intermediate floor between main floors of a building, typically just above the ground floor

Examples

Mezzanine level
A spiral staircase connects the ground floor with the mezzanine above.
Our new office occupies the mezzanine overlooking the lobby.
The restaurant added extra seating on a mezzanine level.

Synonyms

  • mezzanine floor
    6.1 0.00/m
    mezzanine floor is an intermediate level that often spans a whole area, while mezzanine usually means a smaller level above the ground floor.
  • entresol
    9.9 0.00/m
    entresol is a low intermediate level found between ground and first floors and often projects into space, while mezzanine is a general intermediate floor.
