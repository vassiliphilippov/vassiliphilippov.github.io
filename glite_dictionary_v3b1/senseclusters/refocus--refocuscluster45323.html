refocus

verb
adjust focus again; renew attention
2.1 3.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈfoʊ.kəs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈfoʊ.kəs/ (AmE), /riː.ˈfəʊ.kəs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈfəʊ.kəs/ (BrE)
to adjust something so it is in focus again, or to concentrate attention or effort again

Examples

To refocus attention
After a short break, the students were able to refocus on their assignments.
The photographer had to refocus the lens to get a sharper picture.
You should refocus your efforts on meeting this month's sales target.
After adjusting the lens, she had to refocus the camera for a clearer image.
He paused to refocus his attention on the speaker after being distracted.
The company decided to refocus its strategy on customer satisfaction.
After a short break, the team was able to refocus on the project and make significant progress.
She had to refocus her attention after being interrupted several times.
The photographer adjusted the lens to refocus on the moving subject.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defocus - to make something less clear or sharp
  • synonym: focus - to adjust in order to make clear or sharp
