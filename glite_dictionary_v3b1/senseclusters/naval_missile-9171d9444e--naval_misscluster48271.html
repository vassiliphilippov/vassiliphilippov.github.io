naval missile

noun
missile launched from a warship
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪvəl ˈmɪsl̩ / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
a missile designed to be launched from a warship as part of naval weaponry

Examples

A naval missile launch
During the conflict, a naval missile was launched to intercept the incoming threat.
The destroyer was equipped with a new naval missile system capable of striking targets over 100 miles away.
The government invested in advanced naval missiles to strengthen its maritime defenses.
