nonconvergence

noun
failure to converge
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈvɝrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the failure to come together, reach agreement, or achieve a common result or point

Examples

Nonconvergence of opinions
The nonconvergence of the two teams' ideas led to a lengthy debate.
In mathematics, nonconvergence can indicate that a sequence does not approach a specific value.
The experiment failed due to the nonconvergence of the results from different trials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convergence - the process of coming together or reaching a common point
  • synonym: divergence - the process of moving apart or becoming different
