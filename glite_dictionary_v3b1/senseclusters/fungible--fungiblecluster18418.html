An item, especially a commodity, that is interchangeable with others of the same kind for practical or legal purposes.

Examples

A fungible commodity
In banking, gold is considered a fungible because one unit can be substituted for another of equal value.
Legal contracts often specify whether goods are fungibles or non-fungibles to clarify exchange terms.
Oil is traded as a fungible on international markets due to its uniform quality.
