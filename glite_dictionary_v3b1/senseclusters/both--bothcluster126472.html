both

determiner
referring to two together
Pronunciation: / boʊθ / (AmE), / bəʊθ / (BrE)
used to refer to two people or things together, seen as a pair.

Examples

Both children
Both children were excited to open their presents.
Both options have their advantages and disadvantages.
She invited both her parents to the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: either - refers to one or the other of two, but not both
