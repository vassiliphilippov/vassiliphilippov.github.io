gabby

adjective
excessively talkative, chatty
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæbi / (AmE), / ˈɡabi / (BrE)
talking a lot, especially about unimportant things; excessively chatty.

Examples

A gabby person
My gabby aunt entertained everyone at dinner with her endless stories.
Sometimes it's hard to focus with such a gabby coworker sitting nearby.
The gabby children kept the classroom buzzing with constant chatter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reserved - reluctant to speak much; shy or quiet
  • synonym: talkative - inclined to talk a lot
