mistral

noun
strong cold northwesterly wind in France
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪstrəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪstrəl / (BrE)
a strong, cold northwesterly wind that blows from southern France into the Mediterranean, most often in winter

Examples

The mistral wind
During the winter, the mistral can blow for days, clearing the skies but dropping temperatures sharply.
Farmers must take the mistral into account when planning their crops near the Rhône Valley.
The mistral swept across the vineyards, bringing a chill to the otherwise mild landscape.
