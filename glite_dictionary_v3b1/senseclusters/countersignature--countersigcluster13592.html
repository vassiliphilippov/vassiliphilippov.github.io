countersignature

noun
second confirming signature
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˈsɪɡnətʃər / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈsɪɡnətʃə / (BrE)
a second signature that confirms and endorses a document already signed by another person

Examples

A countersignature on a check
The bank requested a countersignature to ensure the check's authenticity.
The contract was not valid until the manager added his countersignature.
Without the required countersignature, the application could not be processed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: signature - a person's name written to authorize a document
