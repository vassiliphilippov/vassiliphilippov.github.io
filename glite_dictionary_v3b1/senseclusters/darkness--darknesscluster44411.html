darkness

noun
absence of light
1.0 43/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…ërkn…ôs / (AmE), / Ààd…ëÀêkn…ôs / (BrE)
the total or partial absence of light; a state or area where there is little or no illumination

Examples

Total darkness
As night fell, a thick darkness enveloped the forest.
She was afraid of the darkness outside her window.
The electricity went out, plunging the entire building into darkness.
As night fell, darkness gradually swallowed the entire landscape.
She felt a chill as he disappeared into the darkness behind the house.
We struggled to see each other in the heavy darkness of the cave.

Synonyms

  • blackness
    3.4 4.25/m
    blackness means total, complete absence of light with a heavy, solid feel, while darkness can be partial or less absolute.
  • dark
    1.0 25/m
    dark often describes a place or time with little light and acts as an adjective, while darkness names the state of low illumination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gloom - partial or total darkness; dimness
  • antonym: light - the natural agent that makes things visible
