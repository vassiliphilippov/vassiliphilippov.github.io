gaff-headed sail

noun
quadrilateral sail set from a gaff
Pronunciation: / ɡæf ˌhɛdɪd seɪl / (AmE), / ɡaf ˌhɛdɪd seɪl / (BrE)
A four-sided (quadrilateral) sail set fore-and-aft, supported at its top by a gaff.

Examples

A gaff-headed sail
The schooner unfurled its gaff-headed sail as the wind began to pick up.
Traditionally, fishing boats used a gaff-headed sail for its versatility and ease of handling.
With its distinctive shape, the gaff-headed sail is common on classic yachts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gaff - a spar to support the upper edge of a sail
