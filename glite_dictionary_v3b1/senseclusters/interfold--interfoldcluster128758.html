interfold

verb
to fold together
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈfoʊld / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfəʊld / (BrE)
to fold together or intertwine, especially in layers or structures

Examples

Layers interfold in rocks
The geologist explained how the rock layers interfold to create the mountain's unique shape.
Sheets of paper can interfold to form a compact booklet.
In some plants, the leaves interfold as they grow, protecting the inner parts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intertwine - to twist or weave together
  • antonym: separate - to move or set apart
