scabrously

adverb
in a rough, scaly way
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskæbrəsli / (AmE), / ˈskæbrəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is rough, scaly, or covered with scabs or patches.

Examples

Scabrously repaired wall
The old wall had been repaired scabrously, leaving patches of rough plaster everywhere.
After the accident, his knee healed scabrously, with uneven, flaky skin.
The bark of the tree grew scabrously, making it difficult to touch without scratching your hand.

  • antonym: smoothly - in a smooth or even manner
