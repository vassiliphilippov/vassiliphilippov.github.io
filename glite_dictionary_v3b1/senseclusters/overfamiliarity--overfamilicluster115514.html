overfamiliarity

noun
excessive familiarity
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərfəˌmɪlˈjæɹəti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəfəˌmɪlɪˈærɪti / (BrE)
the state of being excessively familiar with someone, often resulting in a lack of respect or appropriate boundaries

Examples

Overfamiliarity with the boss
His overfamiliarity with the manager made some colleagues uncomfortable.
Teachers should avoid overfamiliarity with students to maintain professional boundaries.
The guest's overfamiliarity at the dinner table surprised everyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: formality - strict adherence to conventional rules or etiquette
