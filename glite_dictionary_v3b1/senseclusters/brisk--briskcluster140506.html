brisk

verb
(of activity) become busy or lively
Pronunciation: / brɪsk / (AmE), / brɪsk / (BrE)
to become or move in a lively, energetic, or quick way

Examples

To brisk business
As the weather improved, business at the café brisked.
Sales began to brisk during the holiday season.
The pace of negotiations brisked up after the new proposal was introduced.
The children were brisking along the path to school on the chilly morning.
After lunch, she likes to brisk around the park to get some fresh air.
The puppy brisked after the ball, full of excitement and energy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dawdle - to move slowly or waste time
  • synonym: hurry - to move or act quickly
  • synonym: quicken - to become faster or livelier
  • synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
