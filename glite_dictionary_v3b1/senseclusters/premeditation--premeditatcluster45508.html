premeditation

noun
advance planning, especially of a crime
5.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /priː.mə.dɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.mə.dɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of planning or thinking about something, especially a crime, before doing it

Examples

Premeditation of crime
Premeditation is often a key factor in determining the severity of a criminal charge.
He denied any premeditation and claimed the act was spontaneous.
The prosecution argued that the murder was committed with premeditation.
His careful preparation showed unmistakable premeditation before the crime occurred.
Murder charges require proof of premeditation rather than a spontaneous act.
The jury found clear evidence of premeditation in the defendant's actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impulse - an unplanned, spontaneous action
  • antonym: impulsiveness - acting on impulse without forethought
  • synonym: planning - the process of making plans for something
