tear

verb
move quickly and forcefully
Pronunciation: / tɛr / (AmE), / tɪə / (BrE)
to move very quickly and with great force or energy

Examples

To tear down the street
As soon as the bell rang, the children tore out of the classroom towards the playground.
Motorcycles tear through the streets in the middle of the night, waking everyone up.
The dog tore after the squirrel as soon as it spotted it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charge - to move forward quickly and aggressively
  • antonym: crawl - to move slowly on hands and knees or with great difficulty
  • synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
