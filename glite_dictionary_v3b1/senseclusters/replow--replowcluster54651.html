replow

verb
to plow again
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈplaʊ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈplaʊ / (BrE)
to plow a road, path, or land again, especially to clear it or improve its condition

Examples

To replow the road
After heavy snowfall overnight, the city had to replow the main streets to keep them clear.
The maintenance team will replow the parking lot if more snow accumulates this afternoon.
Farmers sometimes need to replow their fields if the first attempt did not break up the soil enough.
After the storm, the farmer had to replow the field to break up the hardened soil.
They decided to replow the land to prepare it better for the next crop season.
Sometimes it is necessary to replow if weeds start growing back quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plow - to clear a surface, especially of snow or soil, using a plow
