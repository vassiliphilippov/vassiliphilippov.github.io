backwash

verb
to clean by reversing flow
Pronunciation: / ˈbækwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækwɒʃ / (BrE)
to clean a filter or system by reversing the flow of water through it to remove debris or buildup

Examples

To backwash a filter
The technician backwashed the pool filter to remove built-up dirt and leaves.
It is important to regularly backwash your aquarium filter to keep the water clean.
After a heavy storm, the maintenance crew backwashed the water treatment system to clear out sediment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clog - to block or become blocked so that movement is difficult
  • synonym: flush - to clean something by forcing water or another liquid through it
