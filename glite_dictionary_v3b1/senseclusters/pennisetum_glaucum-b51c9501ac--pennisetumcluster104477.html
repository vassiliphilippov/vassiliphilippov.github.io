Pennisetum glaucum

noun
species of tall grass; pearl millet
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm ˈɡlaʊkəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm ˈɡlaʊkəm / (BrE)
A tall grass native to Africa and Asia, grown for its grain (pearl millet) and as forage; also used in brewing and cooking.

Examples

Pennisetum glaucum grain
Pennisetum glaucum is widely cultivated across India and Africa as a staple grain.
Farmers in arid regions often choose Pennisetum glaucum because of its drought tolerance.
Some traditional African beers are brewed using the grains of Pennisetum glaucum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pearl millet - the common name for the grain produced by Pennisetum glaucum
