eugenist

noun
heredity improvement advocate
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈjuː.dʒə.nɪst/ (AmE), /ˈjuː.dʒə.nɪst/ (BrE)
a person who promotes or applies methods to improve hereditary qualities in animals or plants, often through selective breeding or genetic selection

Examples

Animal eugenist
The eugenist argued for selective breeding to enhance crop yields.
As a prominent eugenist, she published several papers on improving livestock genetics.
Early 20th-century eugenists influenced agricultural practices around the world.
