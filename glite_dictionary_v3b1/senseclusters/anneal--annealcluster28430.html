anneal

verb
heat and cool a material to alter its properties
Pronunciation: / əˈnil / (AmE), / əˈniːl / (BrE)
to heat a material, such as metal or glass, and then allow it to cool slowly, in order to make it stronger, less brittle, or to achieve the desired properties

Examples

To anneal metal
After forming the glass, it must be annealed to prevent cracks from forming.
Engineers routinely anneal copper wires in the manufacturing process for better conductivity.
The blacksmith will anneal the steel blade to improve its strength and flexibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: temper - to improve the hardness and elasticity of metal or glass by reheating and then cooling
