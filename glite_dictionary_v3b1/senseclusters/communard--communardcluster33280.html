communard

noun
Paris Commune member
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmjəˈnɑrd / (AmE), / ˌkɒmjʊˈnɑːd / (BrE)
a supporter or member of the Paris Commune, the revolutionary government that briefly ruled Paris in 1871

Examples

Paris communard
The communard was arrested after the fall of the Paris Commune in 1871.
Many communards faced harsh punishment when the government regained control of Paris.
A famous painting depicts a group of communards defending a barricade in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: counterrevolutionary - a person who opposes a revolution or revolutionary government
  • synonym: revolutionary - a person who supports or takes part in a revolution
