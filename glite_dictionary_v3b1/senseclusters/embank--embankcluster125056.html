embank

verb
enclose or support with a bank (earth/stone)
8.6 8.0
Pronunciation: / əmˈbæŋk /; / ɪmˈbæŋk / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaŋk /; / ɪmˈbæŋk / (BrE)
to build a raised bank of earth or stone along or around something (such as a river or road) to prevent flooding or provide support

Examples

To embank a river
Engineers will embank the new railway line to protect it from seasonal floods.
Over the centuries, people have embanked many streams to reclaim land for farming.
To prevent flooding, the city decided to embank the river with reinforced concrete walls.
Engineers embanked the river to prevent flooding during the rainy season.
The workers embank the sides of the new highway to make it more stable.
After heavy rains, the villagers quickly embanked the stream to protect their fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bank - a raised area of ground, especially along the edge of a river
  • antonym: erode - to gradually wear away soil or land
  • synonym: fortify - to strengthen or protect with defensive works
