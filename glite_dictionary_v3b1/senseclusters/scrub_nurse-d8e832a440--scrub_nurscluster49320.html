scrub nurse

noun
nurse assisting in surgery
3.4
Pronunciation: / skrʌb nɝːs /; / skrʌb nɜrs / (AmE), / skrʌb nɜːs / (BrE)
a nurse who assists the surgeon during an operation by maintaining a sterile environment and handling surgical instruments

Examples

The scrub nurse assists
During the operation, the scrub nurse handed scalpels and forceps to the surgeon with practiced precision.
It is vital for a scrub nurse to maintain strict sterile procedures throughout the operation.
The scrub nurse quickly prepared the instruments before the patient was brought in for surgery.
After several years as a scrub nurse, she decided to pursue further surgical training.
During the operation, the scrub nurse passed the sutures to the surgeon efficiently.
The scrub nurse ensured that all instruments were properly counted before the surgery began.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: circulating nurse - a nurse who assists in surgery by managing overall care but does not handle sterile instruments
