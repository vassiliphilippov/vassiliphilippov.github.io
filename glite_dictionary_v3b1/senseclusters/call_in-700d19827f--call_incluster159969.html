call in

verb
request the return of a product
3.8
Pronunciation: / kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to request or demand the return of something, such as a product or a loan

Examples

To call in a loan
After discovering a fault, the manufacturer had to call in thousands of cars for inspection.
The bank may call in the loan if the conditions are not met.
The company decided to call in all the toys after receiving complaints about safety issues.
During the financial crisis, several institutions began calling in short-term loans from their clients.
If the mortgage company calls in the debt, they expect you to pay it in full at once.
The bank decided to call in the loan after repeated missed payments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recall - to officially ask for a product to be returned, especially because it is found to be defective or unsafe
