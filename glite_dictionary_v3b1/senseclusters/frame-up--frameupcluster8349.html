frame-up

noun
act of falsifying charges against someone
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfreɪm ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈfreɪm ʌp / (BrE)
An act of falsely incriminating someone, especially by creating fabricated evidence or charges.

Examples

A frame-up case
Many innocent people have had their lives ruined by a frame-up in the courtroom.
She spent years in prison because of a political frame-up that was later exposed.
The evidence proved that the accusation against him was a frame-up orchestrated by his rivals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: setup - an act where someone is falsely made to appear guilty
