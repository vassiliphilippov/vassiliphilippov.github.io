bayou

noun
slow-moving, marshy waterway
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.u / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.uː / (BrE)
a slow-moving, marshy body of water, typically an offshoot of a river or lake, especially found in the southern United States

Examples

A bayou in Louisiana
Cypress trees lined the bayou as far as the eye could see.
Many communities in Louisiana are built alongside a bayou, depending on it for fishing and transportation.
The boat glided quietly along the bayou, disturbing a flock of birds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marsh - wetland dominated by herbaceous plants
  • synonym: swamp - area of land permanently saturated with water
