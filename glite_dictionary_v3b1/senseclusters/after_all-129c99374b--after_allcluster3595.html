after all

adverb
used to emphasize a reason or consideration
Pronunciation: / ˌæftɚ ˈɔl /; / ˌæftər ˈɔl /; / ˌæf.tɚ ˈɔl / (AmE), / ˌɑːftə ˈɔːl /; / ˌɑːf.tə ˈɔːl / (BrE)
used to emphasize a reason or to show that something is true or happens despite earlier doubts or expectations

Examples

After all, she stayed
She decided not to go to the party; after all, she wasn't feeling well.
We should help him; after all, he's done so much for us in the past.
You shouldn't be surprised if he disagrees—after all, he warned us about these issues.
He didn't want to join us at first, but he showed up after all.
It seemed like it would rain, but the sun came out after all.
She decided to go to the concert after all, even though she was tired.
He apologized after all, which surprised everyone in the office.
She decided to attend the meeting after all, even though she was hesitant at first.
We thought the store was closed, but it was open after all.
