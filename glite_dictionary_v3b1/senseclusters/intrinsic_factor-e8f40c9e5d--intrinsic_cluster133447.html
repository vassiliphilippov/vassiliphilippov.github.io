intrinsic factor

noun
protein needed for vitamin B12 absorption
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrɪnsɪk ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɪnsɪk ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a glycoprotein produced in the stomach that is essential for the absorption of vitamin B12 in the intestines

Examples

The intrinsic factor protein
A deficiency of intrinsic factor can lead to serious health problems.
Some autoimmune disorders interfere with the production of intrinsic factor.
Vitamin B12 cannot be properly absorbed without intrinsic factor in the stomach.
