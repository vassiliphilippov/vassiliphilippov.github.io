Shoshonean

noun
a Uto-Aztecan language subfamily
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊˈʃoʊniən / (AmE), / ʃəˈʃəʊniən / (BrE)
a subfamily of the Uto-Aztecan languages spoken mainly in the southwestern United States, including languages such as Shoshone, Comanche, and Ute

Examples

Shoshonean languages
Many linguists study Shoshonean languages to understand the history of the Uto-Aztecan family.
Some Shoshonean languages are still spoken in parts of Arizona and Nevada.
The Comanche language is a prominent member of the Shoshonean subfamily.
