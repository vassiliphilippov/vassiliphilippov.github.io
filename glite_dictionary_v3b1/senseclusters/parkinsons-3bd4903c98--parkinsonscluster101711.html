Parkinson's

noun
degenerative neurological disorder (Parkinson's disease)

A progressive neurological disorder of the central nervous system characterized by tremor, muscle rigidity, and impaired movement, usually referring to Parkinson's disease.

Examples

Parkinson's disease
Parkinson's affects millions of people worldwide, often causing shaking and difficulty with movement.
Her grandfather was diagnosed with Parkinson's five years ago.
New treatments for Parkinson's are being developed to help manage its symptoms.

Synonyms

  • Parkinson's disease
    3.1 1.18/m
    Parkinson's disease is the formal medical name used in hospitals and papers, while Parkinson's is the common short name.
  • paralysis agitans
    6.1 0.00/m
    paralysis agitans is the older Latin medical term used by doctors, while Parkinson's is the modern common name.
  • shaking palsy
    9.9 0.00/m
    shaking palsy is an old everyday name that sounds informal and historic, while Parkinson's is the modern disease name.
  • Parkinson's syndrome
    3.5 0.01/m
    Parkinson's syndrome names a set of symptoms seen in different causes, while Parkinson's usually means the classic disease.
