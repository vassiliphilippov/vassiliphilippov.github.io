Sardina pilchardus

noun
species of European sardine/pilchard
Pronunciation: / sɑrˈdinə pɪlˈkɑːrdəs / (AmE), / sɑːˈdiːnə pɪlˈkɑːdəs / (BrE)
a species of small fish found in large schools along European coasts, smaller and rounder than herrings; also known as the European pilchard or sardine

Examples

The Sardina pilchardus fish
Conservation measures are being considered for Sardina pilchardus due to declining populations.
Fishermen caught thousands of Sardina pilchardus off the coast of Portugal this season.
The diet of the Atlantic puffin includes Sardina pilchardus among other small fish.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: herring - a larger, elongated fish related to sardines
  • synonym: sardine - small, silvery fish often canned for food
