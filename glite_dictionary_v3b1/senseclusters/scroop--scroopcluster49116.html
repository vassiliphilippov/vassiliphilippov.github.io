scroop

verb
to make a rustling sound
Pronunciation: / skruːp / (AmE), / skruːp / (BrE)
to make a crisp, rustling, or creaking sound, especially of fabrics or leather when moved or rubbed

Examples

To scroop like leather
The new leather jacket would scroop every time he shifted in his chair.
As she walked across the room, her silk dress began to scroop softly.
He noticed the curtains scrooping as the breeze moved through the open window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creak - to make a long, high sound when something moves or is pressed
  • synonym: rustle - to make a soft, light sound like dry leaves or paper moving
