jet

noun
upward electrical discharge from storm clouds
9.9 0.84/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’É›t / (AmE), / dÊ’É›t / (BrE)
A rare upper atmospheric electrical discharge, appearing as a blue, cone-shaped burst from the top of giant storm clouds.

Examples

Blue jet lightning
A jet flashed upward from the storm cloud, surprising the scientists observing the thunderstorm.
Pilots sometimes report seeing a jet during intense thunderstorms at high altitude.
The blue light of a jet can be seen above active storm systems for just a few milliseconds.

Synonyms

  • blue jet
    9.9 0.01/m
    blue jet is a named upward blue lightning reaching the stratosphere, while jet is a rare cone-shaped storm-top discharge.
  • reverse lightning
    9.9 0.00/m
    reverse lightning is a rare upward blue discharge that lasts a very short time and widens as it flashes, while jet is cone-shaped.
