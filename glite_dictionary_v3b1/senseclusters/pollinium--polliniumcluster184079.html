pollinium

noun
mass of pollen grains (botany)
8.4
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪniəm / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪnɪəm / (BrE)
a coherent mass of pollen grains, especially as found in orchids and certain other plants

Examples

A pollinium mass
A single pollinium can contain thousands of pollen grains in a compact form.
Researchers studied how the pollinium attaches to visiting bees during pollination.
The orchid releases its pollinium when an insect visits the flower.
