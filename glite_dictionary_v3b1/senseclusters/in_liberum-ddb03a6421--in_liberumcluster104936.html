Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈliːbərʊm / (AmE), / ɪn ˈliːbərʊm / (BrE)
a Latin legal term meaning 'in freedom' or 'freely', used to indicate something done without restriction

Examples

In liberum transfer
The judge ruled that the property was transferred in liberum to the new owner.
The agreement allowed the parties to act in liberum regarding the use of the land.
The court recognized the right to dispose of assets in liberum without prior approval.

Related Terms

  • antonym: restricted - limited or controlled by rules
