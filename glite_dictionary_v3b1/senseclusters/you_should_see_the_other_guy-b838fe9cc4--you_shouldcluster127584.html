you should see the other guy

x
used humorously after a fight to imply the other is worse off
1.0
Pronunciation: / ju ʃəd si ði ˈʌðɚ ɡaɪ / (AmE), / ju ʃʊd siː ði ˈʌðə ɡaɪ / (BrE)
(informal) Used to imply that someone else has been hurt or is in a worse condition, typically after a fight or altercation, often humorously to downplay one's own injuries.

Examples

You should see the other guy joke
After coming home with a bruised face, he joked, 'you should see the other guy.'
He broke his arm in a fight, but he told everyone, "you should see the other guy."
When I walked into work with a black eye, I just said, you should see the other guy.
