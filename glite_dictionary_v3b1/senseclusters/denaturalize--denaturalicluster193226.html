denaturalize

verb
deprive of citizenship
9.2 7.8
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈnæt.ʃə.rə.laɪz/ /; / /diː.ˈnæ.tjə.rə.laɪz/ (AmE), /diː.ˈnæt.ʃə.rə.laɪz/ /; / /diː.ˈnæ.tjə.rə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to deprive (someone) of the rights and duties of citizenship, typically by legal process

Examples

To denaturalize a citizen
After his criminal conviction, the government decided to denaturalize him.
Many feared they could be denaturalized during the political crackdown.
The law allows the authorities to denaturalize individuals who obtained citizenship through fraud.
After the investigation, the government decided to denaturalise the individual for lying on his citizenship application.
Several war criminals were denaturalised in the 1980s as new evidence emerged about their past.
The government has strict rules about when it can denaturalise someone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denationalize - to deprive of national rights or characteristics; often used synonymously
  • antonym: naturalize - to grant citizenship to someone
