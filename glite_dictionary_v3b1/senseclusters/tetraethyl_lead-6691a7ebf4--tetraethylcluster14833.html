tetraethyl lead

noun
toxic petrol additive to prevent engine knocking
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈɛθəl lɛd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrᵻˈiːθɪl lɛd / (BrE)
a clear, oily, poisonous liquid formerly added to petrol (gasoline) to prevent engine knocking

Examples

tetraethyl lead additive
Exposure to tetraethyl lead can cause serious health problems, including neurological damage.
Scientists discovered that tetraethyl lead could significantly reduce engine knocking in automobiles.
The use of tetraethyl lead in gasoline has been banned in many countries due to its toxic effects.
