nanotube

noun
cylindrical molecule, often of carbon
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnæn.oʊˌtuːb / (AmE), / ˈnæn.əʊ.tjuːb / (BrE)
A molecule consisting of atoms arranged in a cylindrical or toroidal structure, especially one made of carbon atoms and used in nanotechnology and materials science.

Examples

A nanotube molecule
A single carbon nanotube can be stronger than steel yet much lighter.
Scientists have discovered new applications for nanotubes in electronics and medicine.
The research team observed how nanotubes interact with other molecules at the nanoscale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fullerene - A form of carbon molecule in closed cages or tubes
