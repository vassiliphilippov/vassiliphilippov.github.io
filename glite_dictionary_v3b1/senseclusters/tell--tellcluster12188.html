tell

verb
give evidence or inform on someone
2.7 89/m
Pronunciation: / tÉ›l / (AmE), / tÉ›l / (BrE)
to give evidence or reveal information, especially to an authority; to inform on someone

Examples

To tell the police
After being questioned by the police, he decided to tell what really happened that night.
She refused to tell on her friends, even when pressured by the teacher.
Witnesses were asked to tell everything they knew about the incident in court.

Synonyms

  • tell on
    4.4 0.76/m
    tell on means reporting someone's wrongdoing to an authority, while tell refers generally to giving information or evidence.
  • inform
    6.8 2.19/m
    inform often means secretly giving information to authorities about wrongdoing, while tell is more general and less formal in everyday speech.
  • betray
    4.6 4.84/m
    betray means breaking trust by revealing private or harmful information, while tell focuses on giving evidence to an authority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to reveal information that harms someone who trusts you
  • synonym: inform - to give information, especially to authorities, about someone's wrongdoing
