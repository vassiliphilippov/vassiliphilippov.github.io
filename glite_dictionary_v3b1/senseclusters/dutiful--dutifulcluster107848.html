dutiful

adjective
obedient and responsible; fulfilling duty
3.9 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀêt…™f…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàdjuÀêt…™f…ôl / (BrE)
(of a person) obedient and attentive to their duties or obligations, showing a willingness to fulfill what is expected out of a sense of duty or respect.

Examples

A dutiful child
Even after retiring, he remained a dutiful member of the community.
She is a dutiful daughter who visits her parents every weekend.
The dutiful soldier followed every order without complaint.

Synonyms

  • duteous
    9.9 0.00/m
    duteous is willingly respectful and shows duty to others, while dutiful describes a person who obeys and attends to responsibilities.
  • conscientious
    5.9 3.44/m
    conscientious works carefully and tries to do right by standards, while dutiful focuses more on following duties out of respect.
  • obedient
    1.0 3.14/m
    obedient obeys orders from an authority figure, while dutiful acts from a sense of duty and respect toward responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests
  • antonym: rebellious - resisting authority or control
