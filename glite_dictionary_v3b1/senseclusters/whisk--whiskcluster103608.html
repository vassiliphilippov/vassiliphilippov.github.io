whisk

verb
move swiftly and suddenly
7.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /wɪsk/ /; / /hwɪsk/ (AmE), /wɪsk/ /; / /hwɪsk/ (BrE)
to move, brush, or sweep something or someone quickly and lightly

Examples

To whisk away
He was whisked into the restaurant before anyone noticed him.
Security guards whisk celebrities through the crowds at the airport.
The nurse whisked away the tray after lunch.
He whisked away the papers with a swift motion.
She whisked the children out of the room before they made more mess.
The secretary whisked past the desks, not stopping to chat with anyone.
A gentle breeze whisked the leaves from the porch steps.
He paused to whisk the dust from his jacket sleeve before entering.
She used her hand to whisk the crumbs off the tablecloth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brush - to remove something from a surface with a quick light movement
  • synonym: dart - to move suddenly and quickly in a particular direction
  • antonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary, often slowly
  • synonym: rush - to move or cause to move with urgent haste
