bootstrapping

noun
statistical resampling method
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ / (BrE)
a statistical method that estimates the accuracy of an estimator by repeatedly resampling data with replacement

Examples

Statistical bootstrapping
The scientist applied bootstrapping to assess the reliability of the experimental results.
Using bootstrapping, the analyst generated thousands of simulated samples from the original data.
Many modern statistical software packages include tools for bootstrapping analyses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jackknife - a statistical method for estimating the accuracy of sample statistics
  • synonym: resampling - the process of repeatedly drawing samples from data
