optic axis

noun
direction in crystal with no double refraction
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪk ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪk ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
the line or direction in a crystal, lens, or eye along which light passes without being split or refracted

Examples

The optic axis line
Light passing along the optic axis of a calcite crystal will not be split into two rays.
Mineralogists often determine the number and direction of optic axes to classify crystals.
The optic axis is a fundamental concept in the study of birefringence.
Light passing along the optic axis travels through the lens without changing direction.
Precise alignment of the optic axis is crucial in designing optical instruments.
The optic axis of the eye determines the direction in which we see most clearly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: principal axis - the main axis of symmetry of an optical element
