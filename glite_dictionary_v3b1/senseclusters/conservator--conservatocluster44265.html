conservator

noun
person in charge of preserving a collection
2.6
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɝː.veɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.teə / (BrE)
a person responsible for the care and preservation of a collection, such as in a museum or library

Examples

Museum conservator
As a conservator, her expertise in historic textiles was essential to the museum.
Each painting was examined by a conservator before being displayed in the new exhibition.
The conservator carefully restored several rare manuscripts in the library's collection.
