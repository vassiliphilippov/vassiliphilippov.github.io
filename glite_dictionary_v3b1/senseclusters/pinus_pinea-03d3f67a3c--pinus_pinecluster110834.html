Pinus pinea

noun
European stone pine; source of pine nuts
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈpaɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nəs ˈpiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
a medium-sized pine tree native to southern Europe, known for its umbrella-shaped crown and edible seeds, commonly called pine nuts

Examples

Pinus pinea tree
Ancient Romans cultivated Pinus pinea for both its beauty and its edible nuts.
The characteristic umbrella shape of Pinus pinea makes it easily recognizable in the landscapes of Italy.
The seeds of the Pinus pinea are an important ingredient in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: pine nut - the edible seed produced by Pinus pinea and related trees
  • synonym: stone pine - another common name for Pinus pinea
