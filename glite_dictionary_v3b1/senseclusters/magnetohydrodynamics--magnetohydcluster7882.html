magnetohydrodynamics

noun
study of magnetic effects on conducting fluids
Pronunciation: / ˌmæg.nə.t̬oʊˌhaɪ.droʊ.daɪˈnæm.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nəʊˌhaɪ.drəʊ.daɪˈnæm.ɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that studies the interaction between magnetic fields and electrically conducting fluids, such as plasmas or molten metals

Examples

Magnetohydrodynamics in physics
Engineers use magnetohydrodynamics to design cooling systems for nuclear reactors.
Research in magnetohydrodynamics helps scientists understand the behavior of solar flares.
The study of Earth's core involves the principles of magnetohydrodynamics.
