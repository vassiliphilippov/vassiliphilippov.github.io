preservation

noun
keeping digital information long-term
4.8

(in libraries, archives, and information technology) the set of practices and processes used to maintain and ensure long-term access to digital information, including migration, emulation, integrity checking, and active curation.

Examples

Digital preservation practices
The university invested in preservation systems to ensure students' theses remain accessible for decades.
Digital preservation requires regular migration and checksums to prevent loss of file integrity over time.
The archives team published guidelines on preservation strategies such as emulation and active curation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data curation - the active management and preservation of digital data across its lifecycle to ensure usability and integrity
  • synonym: digital archiving - the practice of storing and maintaining digital records and archives for long-term accessibility
