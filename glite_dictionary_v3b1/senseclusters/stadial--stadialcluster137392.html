stadial

adjective
of a glacial stage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪdiəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdiəl / (BrE)
relating to a specific stage or period, especially a colder phase during a glacial epoch in geology

Examples

Stadial climate period
The stadial climate conditions led to the advance of glaciers across the region.
Scientists identified stadial layers in the sediment, marking periods of colder temperatures.
During the last stadial phase, many animal species migrated south to survive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glacial - relating to or resulting from the presence of ice, especially glaciers
  • antonym: interstadial - relating to a warmer period between two colder glacial stages
