barrage

noun
heavy artillery bombardment
7.8
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈbærɑːʒ / (BrE)
a heavy and continuous artillery bombardment over a wide area, often intended to protect advancing troops or to saturate without aiming at a specific target

Examples

Artillery barrage
Soldiers waited for the barrage to lift before moving forward toward the trenches.
The enemy troops advanced under the cover of a barrage from their own artillery.
The town endured a relentless barrage that lasted throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombardment - continuous attack with bombs or artillery fire
  • antonym: ceasefire - an agreement to stop fighting temporarily
