methemoglobinemia

noun
blood oxygen disorder
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtˌhiːmoʊˌɡloʊbɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmiːtˌhiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈniːmiə / (BrE)
a blood disorder in which an abnormal amount of methemoglobin reduces the blood's ability to carry oxygen to body tissues

Examples

Methemoglobinemia blood disorder
The doctor suspected methemoglobinemia when the patient's lips appeared unusually blue.
Infants are more vulnerable to methemoglobinemia after exposure to certain chemicals in drinking water.
Treatment for methemoglobinemia often involves medication to restore normal blood oxygen levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anemia - a condition in which the blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells
  • synonym: cyanosis - a bluish discoloration of the skin due to lack of oxygen in the blood
