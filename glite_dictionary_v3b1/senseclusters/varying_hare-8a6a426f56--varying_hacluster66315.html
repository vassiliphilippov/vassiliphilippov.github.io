varying hare

noun
North American hare that turns white in winter
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriɪŋ hɛr / (AmE), / ˈvɛːrɪɪŋ hɛə / (BrE)
A large North American hare (Lepus americanus) with large feet that is white in winter, also known as the snowshoe hare.

Examples

The varying hare in winter
During the winter, the varying hare sheds its brown fur and becomes white to blend in with the snow.
The varying hare is well adapted to snowy environments because of its large, fur-covered feet.
We spotted a varying hare darting across the forest trail, nearly invisible against the snowy background.
