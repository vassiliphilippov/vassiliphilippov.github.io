putridness

noun
the condition of being putrid or decaying
8.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpjuÀêtr…™dn…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàpjuÀêtr…™dn…™s / (BrE)
The quality or state of being putrid; a condition of decay accompanied by foul odor.

Examples

The putridness of decay
A sense of putridness lingered in the air after the floodwaters receded.
After weeks without cleaning, the refrigerator was filled with a horrible putridness.
The putridness of the garbage made it impossible to stay in the room.

Synonyms

  • putrescence
    9.9 0.04/m
    putrescence is the slow rotting that gives a strong bad smell, while putridness is the general quality of foul decay.
  • putrefaction
    8.9 0.15/m
    putrefaction is the process of organic matter breaking down with a foul smell, while putridness names that smelly state.
  • rottenness
    4.6 0.11/m
    rottenness is the condition of being rotten and soft, often without a strong smell, while putridness stresses a foul odour.
  • corruption
    5.6 1.11/m
    corruption is a broad word for physical decay or spoilage in things, while putridness refers to decay that smells very bad.
