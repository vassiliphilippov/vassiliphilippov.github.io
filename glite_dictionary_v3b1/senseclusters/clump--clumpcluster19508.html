clump

noun
heavy, dull sound (impact)
4.4 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / klÊŒmp / (AmE), / klÊŒmp / (BrE)
a heavy, dull sound produced by the impact of heavy objects or footsteps.

Examples

A clump on the floor
There was a loud clump as the box fell onto the floor.
We heard the clump of boots echoing down the hallway.
With a clump, the heavy book landed on the table.

Synonyms

  • thud
    2.2 3.85/m
    thud is a single low, heavy sound from a falling object, while clump describes a step or impact that sounds heavy.
  • thump
    3.0 3.09/m
    thump is a firm blow or heavy beat often from a body or object, while clump refers to heavy impacts or footsteps.
  • clunk
    6.0 0.54/m
    clunk is a short heavy sound often from metal or machines, while clump emphasizes a dull step or heavy impact.
  • clonk
    7.9 0.02/m
    clonk is a sharp solid strike sound made when objects hit, while clump names a dull heavy sound from steps or impacts.
