white sweet clover

noun
fragrant biennial honey plant (Melilotus alba)
7.8
Pronunciation: / waɪt swiːt ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / waɪt swiːt ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A biennial plant (Melilotus alba) with fragrant white flowers, widely cultivated as a forage crop and valued as a nectar source for bees.

Examples

White sweet clover plant
Farmers often plant white sweet clover to enrich the soil and provide forage for livestock.
The fields were full of white sweet clover, attracting bees throughout the summer months.
The pleasant fragrance of white sweet clover filled the air near the apiary.
