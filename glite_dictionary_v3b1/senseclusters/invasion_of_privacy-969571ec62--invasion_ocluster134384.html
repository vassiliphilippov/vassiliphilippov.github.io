invasion of privacy

noun
intrusion into one's private affairs
4.2 3.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈveɪ.ʒən əv ˈpraɪ.və.siː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈveɪ.ʒən əv ˈprɪ.və.siː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈveɪ.ʒən əv ˈpraɪ.və.siː/ (BrE)
the act of intruding into someone's personal life or affairs without consent, especially where there is no legitimate public concern

Examples

An invasion of privacy case
Accusing the government of an invasion of privacy, the protesters demanded stronger data protection laws.

She felt that installing security cameras in every room would be an invasion of privacy.
