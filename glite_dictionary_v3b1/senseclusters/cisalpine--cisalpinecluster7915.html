cisalpine

adjective
on the Italian/Roman side of the Alps
Pronunciation: / sɪsˈælpaɪn / (AmE), / sɪsˈælpaɪn / (BrE)
Relating to the Italian or Roman side of the Alps, especially used in historical contexts to refer to areas south or east of the Alps.

Examples

The cisalpine provinces
The cisalpine provinces were strategically important to the Roman Empire.
The Romans referred to northern Italy as cisalpine Gaul.
Trade routes passed through the cisalpine region before reaching the rest of Italy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: transalpine - on the far side of the Alps (from Italy/Rome); opposite of cisalpine
