gallop across

verb
move quickly and smoothly across a space
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæl.əp əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.əp əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to move quickly and smoothly at high speed across a space, especially as a horse does

Examples

To gallop across the field
A herd of deer galloped across the meadow, startling the nearby birds.
The champion horse effortlessly galloped across the finish line, taking first place.
The riders urged their horses to gallop across the wide plain before sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gallop - to run fast, especially used for horses
