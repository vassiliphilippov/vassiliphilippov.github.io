counteraction

noun
act of neutralizing an effect
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntɚˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərˈækʃən / (BrE)
the act of doing something to reduce, neutralize, or prevent the effect of something else

Examples

The counteraction effect
Environmental groups advocate counteraction to reverse the damage caused by pollution.
The company introduced a series of counteractions to mitigate financial losses.
The new medication's counteraction of side effects has improved its safety for patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opposition - the act of resisting or combating something
  • antonym: reinforcement - the act of strengthening or supporting something; can be the opposite of nullifying effects
