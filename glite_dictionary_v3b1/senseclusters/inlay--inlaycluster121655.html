inlay

noun
solid dental filling fitted into a tooth
6.8 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.leɪ / (BrE)
(Dentistry) A solid filling, usually of gold, porcelain, or a composite, made to fit a cavity in a tooth and cemented into place.

Examples

Dental inlay
After the cavity was cleaned, the technician prepared an inlay to perfectly fit the space.
Gold inlays are known for their strength and durability in dental treatments.
The dentist recommended an inlay rather than a standard filling to restore the damaged tooth.
