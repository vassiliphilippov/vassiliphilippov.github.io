antisymmetric

adjective
sign changes on swap
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
describing a function or relation that changes sign when two arguments are swapped, such that f(x, y) = -f(y, x)

Examples

Antisymmetric function property
The matrix is antisymmetric, so its transpose equals its negative.
In physics, certain tensors are antisymmetric under the exchange of indices.
The function f is antisymmetric, meaning f(a, b) = -f(b, a) for all a and b.

Related Terms

  • antonym: symmetric - unchanged when arguments are swapped
