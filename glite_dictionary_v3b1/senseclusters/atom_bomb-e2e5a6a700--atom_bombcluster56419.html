atom bomb

noun
nuclear weapon using fission
Pronunciation: / ˈæt̬əm ˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˈætəm ˌbɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon that releases a massive amount of energy through the process of nuclear fission, usually involving uranium-235 or plutonium-239.

Examples

The atom bomb explosion
Scientists debated the ethical implications of using the atom bomb in warfare.
The city was destroyed when an atom bomb was dropped in 1945.
The film explored the aftermath of an atom bomb explosion.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hydrogen bomb - a more powerful nuclear bomb using fusion
  • synonym: nuclear bomb - a bomb whose explosion is caused by nuclear reaction
