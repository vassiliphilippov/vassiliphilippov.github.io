Maoism

noun
form of communism developed by Mao Zedong
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmaʊ.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a form of communism developed by Mao Zedong in China, emphasizing agrarian reform, peasants' revolutionary role, and continuous class struggle

Examples

Maoism ideology
He became interested in Maoism after reading about the Cultural Revolution.
Many scholars debate the impact of Maoism on China's social and economic development.
The government feared the spread of Maoism among disaffected rural communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communism - a political and economic system with collective ownership and control of property
