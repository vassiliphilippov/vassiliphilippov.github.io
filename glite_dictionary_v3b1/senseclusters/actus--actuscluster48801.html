actus

noun
philosophical actuality
Pronunciation: / ˈæktəs / (AmE), / ˈæktʊs / (BrE)
in philosophy, the state of being fully actual or realized, as opposed to merely potential

Examples

Philosophical actus
In medieval philosophy, actus was contrasted with potentiality to explain change and existence.
The concept of actus is central to understanding Aristotle's theory of being.
Scholars debated how actus relates to the realization of form in matter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actuality - the state of being real or actual
  • antonym: potentiality - the capacity to develop into something in the future
