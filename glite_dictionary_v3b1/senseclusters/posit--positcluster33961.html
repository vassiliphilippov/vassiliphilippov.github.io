posit

noun
assumed proposition in logic
8.5 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.zət/ /; / /ˈpɑː.zɪt/ (AmE), /ˈpɒ.zɪt/ (BrE)
(logic) A proposition that is assumed to be true for the sake of logical reasoning or argument.

Examples

A logical posit
Every argument begins with at least one posit used as a foundation for reasoning.
In philosophical discourse, a posit does not need empirical evidence to be considered in discussions.
The scientist introduced a new posit to explain the unexplained phenomena.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axiom - self-evident truth used as a starting point in reasoning
  • synonym: postulate - a thing suggested or assumed as true for reasoning or discussion
