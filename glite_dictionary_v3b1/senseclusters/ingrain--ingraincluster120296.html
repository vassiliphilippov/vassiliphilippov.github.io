ingrain

verb
to work into deeply and firmly
3.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡreɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪn / (BrE)
to firmly and thoroughly work (something) into the surface or substance of something else, especially so that it becomes difficult to remove

Examples

To ingrain color
The dye was ingrained so deeply in the fabric that it never faded.
Those habits were ingrained in him from childhood.
Years of working outdoors had ingrained dirt into the lines of his hands.
