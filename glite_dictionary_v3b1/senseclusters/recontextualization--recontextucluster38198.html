recontextualization

noun
placing in new context
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˌtɛkstʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˌtɛkstʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of placing something in a new context to give it a different meaning or interpretation

Examples

The recontextualization of events
The recontextualization of historical events can change how we understand the past.
Through recontextualization, the filmmaker gave new meaning to familiar images.
Academic discussions often involve the recontextualization of classic theories in modern settings.
The artist's recontextualization of old advertisements gave them new social meaning.
Through recontextualization, the documentary presented familiar footage in a surprising way.

The recontextualization of historical events in modern films can change how audiences understand the past.
Teachers often use recontextualization to make complex topics more relatable for students.
Social media allows for the rapid recontextualization of news stories, sometimes altering their original intent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contextualization - the act of placing something within a context to aid understanding
  • antonym: decontextualization - the act of removing something from its original context
  • synonym: reinterpretation - the act of interpreting something in a new or different way
