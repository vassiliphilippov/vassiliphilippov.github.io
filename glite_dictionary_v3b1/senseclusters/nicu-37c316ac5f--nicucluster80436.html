NICU

noun
newborn intensive care unit
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪk.ju / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.juː / (BrE)
An intensive care unit in a hospital designed specifically to care for premature or critically ill newborn babies.

Examples

The NICU unit
After her baby was born prematurely, he spent two weeks in the NICU.
Parents can usually visit their child in the NICU around the clock.
The hospital expanded its NICU to accommodate more critically ill infants.
