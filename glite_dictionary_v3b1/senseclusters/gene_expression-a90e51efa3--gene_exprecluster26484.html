gene expression

noun
biological process of using gene information
Pronunciation: / dʒiːn ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (AmE), / dʒiːn ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process by which information from a gene is used to synthesize a functional product, typically a protein, through transcription and translation

Examples

Gene expression process
Alterations in gene expression can lead to a variety of genetic disorders.
Researchers studied how environmental factors influence gene expression in the developing embryos.
The new drug is designed to regulate gene expression and suppress tumor growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transcription - process of copying DNA to RNA
  • synonym: translation - process of synthesizing protein from mRNA
