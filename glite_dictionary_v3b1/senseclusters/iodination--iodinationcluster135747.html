iodination

noun
the process of adding iodine to a compound
6.9 9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪoʊdəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪədəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing iodine atoms into a molecule, especially into an organic compound, by substitution or addition

Examples

Controlled iodination
Controlled iodination can improve the efficacy of certain drugs.
Laboratory protocols outline the safe handling of reagents during iodination reactions.
The iodination of tyrosine residues is a crucial step in thyroid hormone synthesis.
