applied psychology

noun
branch applying psychology to practical problems
1.3
Pronunciation: / əˈplaɪd saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / əˈplaɪd saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology that uses psychological theories and principles to address real-world problems in areas such as education, industry, or marketing

Examples

Applied psychology research
Research in applied psychology has significantly influenced educational practices.
She decided to pursue a career in applied psychology to help organizations improve employee well-being.
The seminar covered recent advances in applied psychology and their impact on public policy.
