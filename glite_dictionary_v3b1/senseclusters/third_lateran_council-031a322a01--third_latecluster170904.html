Third Lateran Council

proper noun
ecumenical church council held in Rome, 1179
Pronunciation: / θɜrd ˈleɪ.t̬ɚ.ən ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈleɪ.tər.ən ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
the ecumenical council held in 1179 at the Lateran in Rome, which condemned the heresies of the Albigenses and the Waldenses and enacted church reforms

Examples

Third Lateran Council decrees
At the Third Lateran Council, new decrees were established to address various heresies.
Historians often study the decisions made at the Third Lateran Council to understand church policy changes.
The Third Lateran Council marked a significant point in medieval church reform.
