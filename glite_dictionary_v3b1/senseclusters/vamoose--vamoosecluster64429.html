vamoose

verb
leave suddenly; go away quickly
9.2
Pronunciation: / vəˈmus / (AmE), / vəˈmuːs / (BrE)
to leave or depart suddenly, especially in a hurry

Examples

To vamoose quickly
As soon as the argument started to get heated, she chose to vamoose.
He vamoosed out the back door to avoid answering difficult questions.
We'd better vamoose before the police arrive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decamp - to leave a place suddenly and secretly
  • antonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary
