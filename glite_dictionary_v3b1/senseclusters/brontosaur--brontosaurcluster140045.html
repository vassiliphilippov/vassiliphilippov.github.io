brontosaur

noun
large Jurassic herbivorous dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɑːn.t̬əˈsɔr / (AmE), / ˌbrɒn.təˈsɔː / (BrE)
A huge quadrupedal herbivorous dinosaur common in North America during the late Jurassic period, also known as Brontosaurus.

Examples

The brontosaur dinosaur
Children are often fascinated by the long neck and tail of the brontosaur.
Paleontologists discovered a nearly complete skeleton of a brontosaur in Wyoming.
The brontosaur was once considered the largest animal ever to walk the earth.
