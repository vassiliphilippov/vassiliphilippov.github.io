manioc

noun
cassava root used as food and for tapioca
Pronunciation: / ˈmæniˌɑk /; / ˈmɑːniˌɑk / (AmE), / ˈmæniˌɒk /; / ˈmæniɒk /; / ˈmaniɒk / (BrE)
a tropical plant with starchy roots, also called cassava, whose root is used as a staple food and to make products like tapioca

Examples

Farmers in the region rely on manioc as one of their main sources of food.

People in Brazil often prepare manioc by boiling or frying it as a side dish.
The chef used manioc to create a gluten-free bread for the restaurant.
