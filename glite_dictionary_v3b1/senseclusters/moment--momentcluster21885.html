moment

noun
statistical measure of deviation (math)
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
In mathematics and statistics, a quantitative measure expressing the expected value of a specified power of the deviations of a random variable from a fixed point, usually its mean.

Examples

Statistical moment
Calculating the third moment can provide information on the skewness of a dataset.
The concept of a moment is fundamental in probability and statistics.
The variance is the second moment about the mean in a probability distribution.
