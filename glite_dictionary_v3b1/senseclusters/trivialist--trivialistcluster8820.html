trivialist

noun
person who downplays importance
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɪ.vɪ.ə.lɪst/ /; / /ˈtrɪ.vɪ.əl.ɪst/ (AmE), /ˈtrɪ.vɪ.ə.lɪst/ /; / /ˈtrɪ.vɪ.əl.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who habitually treats important matters as unimportant or focuses excessively on insignificant details

Examples

A detail-focused trivialist
As a trivialist, she often ignored the real issues and focused on minor details.
The manager was criticized for being a trivialist during important meetings.
Instead of addressing the main problem, the trivialist kept bringing up small, irrelevant points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minimizer - a person who makes something seem less important
  • synonym: nitpicker - a person who finds small or unimportant faults
  • antonym: serious person - someone who treats important matters with proper attention
