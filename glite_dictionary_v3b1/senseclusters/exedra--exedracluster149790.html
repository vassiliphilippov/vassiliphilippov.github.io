exedra

noun
semicircular seating area
Pronunciation: / ɛɡˈziːdrə / (AmE), / ɛkˈsiːdrə / (BrE)
a semicircular architectural space, often with seats, used for gatherings or conversation

Examples

An exedra space
The ancient villa had an exedra where philosophers would gather to discuss ideas.
Visitors rested on the curved benches of the exedra overlooking the fountain.
A marble exedra provided a quiet spot for conversation in the public garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alcove - a small recessed section of a room or garden
  • synonym: niche - a shallow recess in a wall, often for a statue or seat
