bypass capacitor

noun
capacitor for filtering high frequencies in circuits
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.pæs kə.ˈpæs.ə.tər/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.pæs kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.pɑːs kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.pæs kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a capacitor connected in an electronic circuit to provide a low-impedance path for specific high frequencies, often used to filter out noise or stabilize voltage

Examples

A bypass capacitor circuit
A bypass capacitor is placed near the microcontroller to reduce power supply noise.
Engineers recommend using a bypass capacitor to ensure stable operation of sensitive components.
The effectiveness of the bypass capacitor depends on its value and placement in the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decoupling capacitor - capacitor used to decouple one part of a circuit from another, often used synonymously
