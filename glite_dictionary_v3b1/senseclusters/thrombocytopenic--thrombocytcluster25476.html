thrombocytopenic

adjective
low platelets
Pronunciation: / ˌθrɑːmboʊˌsaɪtəˈpiːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌθrɒmbəʊˌsaɪtəˈpiːnɪk / (BrE)
having or relating to an abnormally low number of platelets in the blood

Examples

Thrombocytopenic disorder
The doctor explained that a thrombocytopenic condition could increase the risk of bleeding.
After chemotherapy, some patients become thrombocytopenic and require special care.
A thrombocytopenic patient may need regular blood tests to monitor platelet levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anemic - having a lower than normal number of red blood cells
  • antonym: thrombocytosis - having an abnormally high number of platelets in the blood
