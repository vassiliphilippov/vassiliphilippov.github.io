samphire

noun
succulent coastal plant, used in glass-making
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæmfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsæmfaɪə / (BrE)
a fleshy coastal plant with succulent stems and small scale-like leaves, found in salt marshes and formerly used in glass-making

Examples

Coastal samphire plant
Historically, samphire was collected and burned for its use in making glass.
She picked fresh samphire to add a briny crunch to the seafood dish.
Wild samphire grows along the salt marshes near the coast.
