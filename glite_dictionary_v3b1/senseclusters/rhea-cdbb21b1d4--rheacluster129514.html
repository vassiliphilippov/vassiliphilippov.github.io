Rhea

proper noun
Greek fertility goddess, wife of Cronus
9.8 0.80/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀê…ô / (AmE), / ÀàriÀê…ô / (BrE)
A fertility goddess in ancient Greek mythology, wife of Cronus and mother of Zeus; equated with the Roman goddess Ops and the Phrygian goddess Cybele.

Examples

Rhea goddess mother
Rhea was revered as the mother of many gods in ancient Greek mythology.
According to legend, Rhea saved her youngest son, Zeus, by tricking Cronus.
Priests of Cybele often traced her origins back to Rhea of Greek myth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cronus - Titan, husband of Rhea in Greek mythology
  • synonym: Cybele - Phrygian mother goddess often equated with Rhea
  • synonym: Ops - Roman equivalent of Rhea, goddess of fertility and earth
