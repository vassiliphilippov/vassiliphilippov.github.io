creeping bentgrass

noun
a lawn and pasture grass with spreading runners
Pronunciation: / ˈkripɪŋ ˈbɛntˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈbɛntˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a species of perennial grass (Agrostis stolonifera) used for lawns and pastures, known for its long runners that enable it to form dense mats

Examples

creeping bentgrass lawn
Farmers often choose creeping bentgrass for pastures because it tolerates close grazing and foot traffic well.
Many golf courses use creeping bentgrass for their greens because it provides a smooth, dense surface.
The creeping bentgrass in the park has spread rapidly due to its long runners.
