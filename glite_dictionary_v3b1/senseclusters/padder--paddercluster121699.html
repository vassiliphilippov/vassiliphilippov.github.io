padder

noun
foot-traveling highwayman (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈpædər / (AmE), / ˈpædə / (BrE)
a highwayman who robs people while traveling on foot, rather than on horseback; used mainly in historical contexts

Examples

A foot padder
In the 18th century, a padder was feared by travelers along rural lanes.
Literary works often portrayed the padder as a cunning villain stalking quiet roads.
The notorious padder was finally caught and tried for his crimes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: highwayman - a robber who steals from travelers, especially on horseback
