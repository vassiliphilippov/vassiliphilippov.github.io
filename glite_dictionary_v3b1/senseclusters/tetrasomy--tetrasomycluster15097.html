tetrasomy

noun
four chromosome copies
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtrəˌsoʊmi / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəsəʊmi / (BrE)
a genetic condition in which there are four copies of a particular chromosome instead of the usual two

Examples

A tetrasomy condition
The geneticist confirmed the diagnosis of tetrasomy after analyzing the patient's chromosomes.
Some rare syndromes are caused by tetrasomy of specific chromosomes.
Researchers are studying how tetrasomy affects physical and cognitive development.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: disomy - the normal state of having two copies of a chromosome
  • degree lower: monosomy - a genetic condition with only one copy of a chromosome
  • degree lower: trisomy - a genetic condition with three copies of a chromosome
