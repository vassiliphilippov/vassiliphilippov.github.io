appoggiatura

noun
musical ornament, grace note
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌpɑdʒəˈtʊrə / (AmE), / əˌpɒdʒəˈtjʊərə / (BrE)
A musical ornament consisting of a grace note that is sounded before a main note, usually taking away some value from the main note.

Examples

A appoggiatura note
As she played the piano piece, the delicate appoggiatura gave the passage a graceful touch.
The composer used an appoggiatura to add emotional tension to the melody.
Understanding how to perform an appoggiatura is essential for interpreting baroque music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grace note - a note added as an ornament, not essential to the harmony or melody
