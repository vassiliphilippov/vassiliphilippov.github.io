sloping trough

noun
an inclined channel for descending objects
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsloʊpɪŋ trɔːf / (AmE), / ˈsləʊpɪŋ trɒf / (BrE)
a channel or trough with an inclined surface, designed so that objects or materials can descend by gravity

Examples

A sloping trough channel
Rainwater runs off the roof and into a sloping trough that leads to the drain.
The packaging facility used a sloping trough to guide boxes onto the conveyor belt.
The workers poured grain down the sloping trough to fill the storage bins.
