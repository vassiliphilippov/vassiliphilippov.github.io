divergently

adverb
in a creative way
6.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɜrdʒəntli / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒəntli / (BrE)
in a way that is different from others or goes in different directions, especially in thinking or developing ideas

Examples

To think divergently
The students were asked to think divergently to come up with as many solutions as possible.
Artists often approach problems divergently, exploring multiple creative paths at once.
In brainstorming sessions, it is important to respond divergently rather than settling on one idea too quickly.
The two scientists approached the problem divergently, leading to very different solutions.
Students in the class responded divergently to the new teaching method.
When faced with the same data, analysts often interpret it divergently.
The two research teams approached the problem divergently, leading to unique solutions.
After the meeting, opinions on the project began to develop divergently among the staff.
Over time, the languages evolved divergently due to geographic separation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creatively - in an original or imaginative way
  • synonym: differently - in a way that is not the same as something else
  • antonym: similarly - in a way that is almost the same as something else
