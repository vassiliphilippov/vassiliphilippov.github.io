gramma

noun
grass of American plains used for pasture
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræmə / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə / (BrE)
A pasture grass native to the plains of South America and western North America, important as fodder for livestock.

Examples

Blue gramma grass
Blue gramma is especially valued for its drought resistance.
Conservationists are working to restore native gramma grasses in the prairie ecosystem.
The cattle grazed on the abundant gramma covering the western plains.
