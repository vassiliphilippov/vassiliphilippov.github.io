bronchiectasis

noun
chronic lung disease
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɑːŋkiˈɛktəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrɒŋkiˈɛktəsɪs / (BrE)
a chronic lung disease in which the airways become permanently widened, leading to mucus buildup and breathing difficulties

Examples

Chronic bronchiectasis disease
The doctor explained that bronchiectasis can cause frequent lung infections if not managed properly.
Daily physiotherapy helps people with bronchiectasis clear mucus from their lungs.
After years of coughing and chest infections, she was finally diagnosed with bronchiectasis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asthma - a condition in which airways narrow and swell, causing breathing problems
  • synonym: chronic obstructive pulmonary disease - a group of lung diseases that block airflow and make breathing difficult
