hydrophilicity

noun
attraction to water
8.1
Pronunciation: /haɪ.drə.fɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /haɪ.drə.fɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the property or quality of being attracted to water or able to absorb water easily

Examples

The hydrophilicity of fabric
The hydrophilicity of the fabric makes it ideal for sportswear that absorbs sweat.
Scientists measured the hydrophilicity of the new coating to see how well it attracts water.
Increasing the hydrophilicity of a surface can help prevent water from forming droplets.
The hydrophilicity of cotton makes it ideal for use in towels and bandages.
Scientists measured the hydrophilicity of the new polymer to determine its suitability for medical implants.
Increasing the hydrophilicity of a surface can help prevent the buildup of bacteria.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrophobicity - the property of repelling water
  • synonym: water absorption - the process of taking in water
  • synonym: water-loving - having an affinity for water
