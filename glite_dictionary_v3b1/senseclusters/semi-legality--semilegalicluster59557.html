semi-legality

noun
partial or questionable legality
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmi lɪˈgæləti / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi lɪˈɡæləti / (BrE)
The state or condition of being only partially legal or of uncertain legality.

Examples

State of semi-legality
Many informal workers exist in a state of semi-legality, lacking the full protection of labor laws.
The export of rare plants was conducted under semi-legality, with authorities often turning a blind eye.
The practice operated in a zone of semi-legality due to ambiguous regulations.
