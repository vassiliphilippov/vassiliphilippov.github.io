expense

noun
detriment or sacrifice to achieve something
6.1 3.5
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛns / (BrE)
a loss, detriment, or sacrifice, especially of something valued or desirable, to achieve or obtain something else.

Examples

Expense of time
He finished the project quickly at the expense of quality.
She pursued her career ambitions at the expense of her personal relationships.
The country developed rapidly, but often at the expense of the environment.
