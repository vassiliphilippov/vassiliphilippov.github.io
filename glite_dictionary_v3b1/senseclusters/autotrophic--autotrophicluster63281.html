autotrophic

adjective
able to produce own food from inorganic substances
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtəˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism) able to synthesize its own food from inorganic substances, typically through photosynthesis or chemosynthesis.

Examples

Autotrophic green plants
Green plants are autotrophic, using sunlight to make food from carbon dioxide and water.
Many bacteria are autotrophic and can thrive in environments without organic nutrients.
Unlike animals, autotrophic organisms do not depend on others for food.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterotrophic - (of an organism) requiring organic compounds for nourishment; not self-sustaining
