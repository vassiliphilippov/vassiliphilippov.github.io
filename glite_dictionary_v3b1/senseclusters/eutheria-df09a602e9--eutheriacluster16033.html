Eutheria

noun
group comprising placental mammals
9.9
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈθɪə.ri.ə/ (AmE), /juː.ˈθɪə.ri.ə/ (BrE)
a major group of mammals that includes all placental mammals and excludes monotremes and marsupials

Examples

Eutheria mammals group
Eutheria includes most familiar mammals, such as dogs, whales, and humans.
The evolutionary history of Eutheria has been an important subject in mammalian paleontology.
Unlike monotremes and marsupials, members of Eutheria have complex placental development.
All whales, elephants, and humans belong to the Eutheria subclass.
Fossil records show that Eutheria diversified rapidly after the extinction of dinosaurs.
Researchers are studying how Eutheria evolved distinct reproductive adaptations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: marsupial - a mammal of the group that carries and nourishes young in a pouch, like kangaroos or opossums
  • antonym: monotreme - a primitive egg-laying mammal, such as a platypus or echidna
  • synonym: placental - a mammal of the group characterised by the presence of a placenta
