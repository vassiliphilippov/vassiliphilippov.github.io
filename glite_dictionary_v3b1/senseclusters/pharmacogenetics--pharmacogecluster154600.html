pharmacogenetics

noun
genetic effects on drug responses
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːr.mə.koʊ.dʒəˈnɛ.tɪks / (AmE), / ˌfɑː.mə.kəʊ.dʒəˈnɛ.tɪks / (BrE)
the study of how genetic differences influence individual responses to drugs

Examples

Pharmacogenetics study
Recent advances in pharmacogenetics have improved the effectiveness of medications for certain diseases.
Researchers use pharmacogenetics to determine why some patients have different reactions to the same drug.
The field of pharmacogenetics aims to develop personalized medicines based on genetic profiles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pharmacogenomics - the study of how genes affect a person's response to drugs, often at a larger genomic scale
