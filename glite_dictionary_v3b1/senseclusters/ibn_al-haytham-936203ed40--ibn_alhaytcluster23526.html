Ibn al-Haytham

proper noun
medieval Egyptian scientist and polymath
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɪb.ən əl ˈhaɪ.θəm/ /; / /ˈɪb.ən əl ˈhaɪ.ðəm/ /; / /ˈɪb.ən əl ˈhaɪ.θæm/ (AmE), /ˈɪb.ən əl ˈhaɪ.θəm/ /; / /ˈɪb.ən əl ˈhaɪ.ðəm/ /; / /ˈɪb.ən əl ˈhaɪ.θæm/ (BrE)
(c. 965–1040) An Egyptian polymath and scientist whose pioneering work in geometry and optics profoundly influenced later science, and who was among the first to advocate experimental proof in physics.

Examples

Ibn al-Haytham scientist
Ibn al-Haytham's work was translated into Latin and influenced European scholars for centuries.
Many historians regard Ibn al-Haytham as the father of modern scientific methodology.
The experiments of Ibn al-Haytham revolutionized the study of optics in the Middle Ages.
