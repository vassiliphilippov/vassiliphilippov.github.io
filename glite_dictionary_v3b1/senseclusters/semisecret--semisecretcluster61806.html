semisecret

noun
partly hidden fact
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈsiːkrət / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈsiːkrɪt / (BrE)
a fact, plan, or arrangement that is only partly secret or known to a limited group of people

Examples

A semisecret plan
The company's expansion plans were a semisecret discussed only among top executives.
Their relationship was a semisecret that most of their friends suspected but never confirmed.
The new policy was a semisecret before the official announcement was made.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: open secret - something that is supposed to be secret but is widely known
  • degree higher: secret - something kept hidden or unknown by most people
