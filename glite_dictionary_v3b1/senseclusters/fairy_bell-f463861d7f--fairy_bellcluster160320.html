fairy bell

noun
tall European plant with tubular pink-purple flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛri bɛl / (AmE), / ˈfeəri bɛl / (BrE)
A tall European biennial or perennial plant with clusters of large, tubular pink-purple flowers, whose leaves contain digitalis and are poisonous to livestock.

Examples

Tall fairy bells
Botanists often study fairy bells for their valuable medicinal properties.
Farmers are cautious because the leaves of the fairy bell are toxic to livestock.
The fairy bell is admired for its beautiful pink-purple blooms in cottage gardens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foxglove - plant with tubular flowers and source of digitalis
