conversionism

noun
active religious conversion
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɜːrʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or policy of actively seeking to convert others to a particular religion or belief system

Examples

Active conversionism efforts
The organization is known for its strong conversionism and frequent outreach events.
Some critics argue that conversionism can lead to cultural misunderstandings.
His approach to faith emphasized conversionism over quiet personal belief.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evangelism - the spreading of a religious faith by public preaching or personal witness
  • synonym: proselytism - the act of trying to convert people to another religion or opinion
  • antonym: tolerance - acceptance of different beliefs or practices without trying to change them
