Lawson's cedar

noun
large timber tree of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːsənz ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːsənz ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A large timber tree native to western North America, growing up to 200 feet tall with a trunk diameter of up to 12 feet.

Examples

Lawson's cedar tree
Logging of Lawson's cedar played a significant role in the development of the Pacific Northwest timber industry.
Many gardeners plant Lawson's cedar as an ornamental tree due to its attractive foliage.
The massive trunk of the Lawson's cedar can reach impressive heights in old-growth forests.
