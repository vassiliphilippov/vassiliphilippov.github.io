fraktur

noun
German blackletter typeface
9.9
Pronunciation: / frɑkˈtʊr / (AmE), / frækˈtʊə / (BrE)
a style of blackletter typeface used in old German printing, characterized by its angular and ornate letters

Examples

Fraktur typeface
The old book was printed entirely in fraktur, making it difficult for modern readers to decipher.
Many historical German documents feature the distinctive fraktur typeface.
He learned to recognize fraktur letters while studying medieval manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blackletter - a script style with thick, angular letters used in Western Europe
  • antonym: roman - a standard upright typeface style used in most modern printing
