slip away

verb
leave quietly and secretly
Pronunciation: / slɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / slɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly and secretly, or to disappear without being noticed

Examples

To slip away quietly
During the meeting, he managed to slip away for a quick phone call.
Several guests began to slip away as the evening wore on.
She tried to slip away from the party before anyone noticed her absence.
During the commotion, he managed to slip away without anyone noticing.
She watched her dreams slowly slip away as challenges mounted.
The patient's strength seemed to slip away day by day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sneak out - to leave a place secretly and quietly to avoid being noticed
  • synonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
