settlement

noun
final resolution of a dispute
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl.mənt /; / ˈsɛtəltmənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl.mənt /; / ˈsɛtləmənt / (BrE)
an official agreement or resolution that ends a dispute, conflict, or legal case

Examples

Legal settlement
After months of negotiations, the two companies finally reached a settlement out of court.
The settlement of the contract dispute allowed the project to move forward.
The lawsuit ended in a financial settlement that satisfied both parties.
After months of negotiation, the company and workers reached a settlement that satisfied both sides.
The two parties are hopeful that a settlement will be announced soon to avoid going to court.
Without a fair settlement, the dispute could drag on for years.
