configurationism

noun
psychological theory emphasizing configurational properties
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃənˌɪzəm / (BrE)
A theory in psychology that emphasizes the significance of overall configurational properties, rather than individual elements, in perception and understanding.

Examples

Configurationism in psychology
Configurationism shaped many early 20th-century debates in psychological theory circles.
Her research into perception was heavily influenced by configurationism and Gestalt principles.
Many critics argue that configurationism offers valuable insights into the nature of cognitive organization.
