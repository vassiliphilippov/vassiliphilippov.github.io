hack

noun
politician serving private interests
6.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /hæk/ (AmE), /hæk/ (BrE)
a person who does dull, routine, or uninspired work, especially a writer, journalist, or political worker

Examples

A political hack
Many saw the senator as just another hack doing favors for wealthy donors.
The mayor surrounded himself with a group of loyal hacks who controlled the city council.
Voters are tired of electing hacks who put their own interests above the public's.
Many consider the job to be suitable only for a hack with little ambition.
She didn't want to become a hack, stuck doing the same repetitive tasks every day.
The journalist was dismissed as a hack who never produced original stories.
Despite her success, she refused to become a political hack writing meaningless speeches.
He was dismissed as a mere hack churning out tabloid stories.
Many critics view him as a literary hack rather than a serious novelist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grind - a person who works steadily and hard at dull work
