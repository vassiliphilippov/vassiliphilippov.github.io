reflexivity

noun
the property of relating an element to itself
Pronunciation: / ˌriːflɛkˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌriːflɛkˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
(logic and mathematics) The property of a relation whereby it holds between any element and itself.

Examples

Reflexivity of equality
In mathematics, equality has the property of reflexivity because any number is equal to itself.
The reflexivity of the relation means every set is related to itself.
Understanding reflexivity is crucial when studying equivalence relations in logic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: symmetry - property of a relation holding in both directions
  • synonym: transitivity - property where relation holds between A and C if it holds between A and B and B and C
