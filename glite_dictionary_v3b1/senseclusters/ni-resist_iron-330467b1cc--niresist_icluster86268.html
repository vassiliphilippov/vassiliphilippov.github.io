Ni-resist iron

noun
heat- and corrosion-resistant cast iron alloy
Pronunciation: / ˌnaɪ.rɪˈzɪst ˈaɪ.ərn / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.rɪˈzɪst ˈaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A type of cast iron containing graphite in an austenitic matrix, notable for its resistance to heat and corrosion due to the presence of nickel.

Examples

Ni-resist iron alloy
Because of its corrosion resistance, Ni-resist iron is ideal for marine applications.
Many engine components are made from Ni-resist iron due to its excellent heat resistance.
The foundry specializes in producing Ni-resist iron castings for industrial machinery.
