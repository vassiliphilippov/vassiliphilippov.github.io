Percocet

noun
opioid pain medication
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrkəˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːkəˌsɛt / (BrE)
a prescription painkiller that contains a combination of oxycodone and acetaminophen, sometimes used recreationally or illegally.

Examples

Take Percocet for pain
After his surgery, the doctor prescribed Percocet to help manage the pain.
Some people misuse Percocet for its strong effects, which can be dangerous.
The pharmacy requires a valid prescription before dispensing Percocet to any patient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Vicodin - a prescription painkiller containing hydrocodone and acetaminophen
  • antonym: ibuprofen - a non-opioid pain reliever
  • synonym: oxycodone - a powerful opioid pain medication
