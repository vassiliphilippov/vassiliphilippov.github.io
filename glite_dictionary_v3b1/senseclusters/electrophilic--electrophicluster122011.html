electrophilic

adjective
attracting electrons
9.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
describing a molecule or atom that tends to attract electrons and react with electron-rich substances in chemical reactions

Examples

An electrophilic compound
An electrophilic compound will often react quickly with substances that have extra electrons.
The chemist explained how electrophilic addition occurs in organic reactions.
Many acids act as electrophilic agents in chemical processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nucleophilic - describing a molecule or atom that donates electrons in chemical reactions
