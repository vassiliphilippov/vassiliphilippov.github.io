river shad

noun
a type of shad found in the Mississippi River system
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvər ʃæd / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ʃad / (BrE)
a species of shad that spawns in streams within the Mississippi River drainage, closely resembling the American shad (Alosa sapidissima)

Examples

The river shad fish
Biologists monitor the health of river shad populations to study changes in the river ecosystem.
During spawning season, river shad migrate upstream into smaller tributaries.
The fishermen were excited to catch a large river shad during their trip on the Mississippi.
