fielder's choice

noun
baseball play: choosing to put out a runner instead of the batter
6.3 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfiːl.dərz tʃɔɪs/ (AmE), /ˈfiːl.dəz tʃɔɪs/ (BrE)
a play in baseball where a fielder, after fielding a ground ball, chooses to try to put out a runner advancing to another base rather than the batter.

Examples

A fielder's choice play
During the fifth inning, a fielder's choice allowed the team to advance a runner to third.
The batter reached first safely because the defense opted for a fielder's choice play.
The run was scored on a fielder's choice after the shortstop threw to second base.
