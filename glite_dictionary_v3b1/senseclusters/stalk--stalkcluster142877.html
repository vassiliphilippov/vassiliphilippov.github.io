stalk

verb
follow secretly or persistently
7.4 4.88/m
Pronunciation: / st…ëk / (AmE), / st…îÀêk / (BrE)
to follow or pursue someone stealthily and often with malicious intent, or to recur persistently in someone's thoughts or life

Examples

To stalk a person
He was arrested after he stalked his former colleague to her home.
Terrifying memories of the accident continued to stalk her long after it happened.
The private investigator stalked the suspect for days without being noticed.

Synonyms

  • haunt
    4.9 11/m
    haunt repeatedly appears in someone's mind and causes worry, while stalk often means secretly following or obsessing over a person.
  • shadow
    6.0 1.33/m
    shadow follows someone secretly to watch them, while stalk usually adds harm or strong obsession to that secret following.
  • trail
    2.5 3.51/m
    trail follows someone, often to catch or find them, while stalk suggests secret, threatening pursuit or a repeated worry.
  • hound
    6.1 2.18/m
    hound pursues and harasses someone relentlessly in public, while stalk implies secret, often hidden pursuit or haunting thoughts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haunt - to come back repeatedly to the mind of someone
  • synonym: pursue - to follow someone in order to catch or attack them
