snake-head

noun
showy marshland plant native to North America
7.3 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsneɪk.hɛd/ (AmE), /ˈsneɪk.hɛd/ (BrE)
A showy perennial plant of marshlands in eastern and central North America, with waxy lance-shaped leaves and flowers that are creamy white below and pale pink to deep purple above.

Examples

Snake-head flowers
Gardeners appreciate the striking flowers of the snake-head for ornamental water gardens.
The snake-head thrives in moist, marshy environments across much of eastern North America.
The wetland was full of blooming snake-heads in early summer, adding splashes of color to the landscape.
