oralism

noun
speech-based deaf education
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a method or belief in teaching deaf people to use speech and lip-reading instead of sign language

Examples

Oralism teaching method

Many parents debated whether oralism or sign language would be better for their child.
Critics argue that oralism can limit access to the deaf community's culture and language.
The school adopted oralism to encourage deaf students to use spoken language instead of sign language.
Debates about oralism versus manualism have shaped deaf education for decades.
Some educators believe that oralism helps integrate students into mainstream society.
