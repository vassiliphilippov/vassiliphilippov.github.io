audion

noun
early signal amplifier
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːdiˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiɒn / (BrE)
an early electronic device, a type of vacuum tube, used to amplify electrical signals in radios and other equipment

Examples

The audion tube

Early radio receivers relied on the audion to boost weak signals.
Lee De Forest patented the audion in the early 1900s, changing electronics forever.
