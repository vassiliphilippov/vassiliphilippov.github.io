creaturely

adjective
of animals or instincts
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkriːtʃərli / (AmE), / ˈkriːtʃəli / (BrE)
relating to the physical, instinctive, or limited nature of living beings (especially humans or animals)

Examples

Creaturely desires
The novel explores the creaturely desires that drive human behavior.
Despite his intelligence, he could not escape his creaturely fears in the dark.
She spoke of the creaturely comforts that everyone needs to survive.
The philosopher wrote about our creaturely needs and the importance of humility.
She reflected on her creaturely existence in contrast to the idea of an all-powerful deity.
His poetry often explores the creaturely aspects of human life, such as vulnerability and dependence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animalistic - having qualities typical of animals
  • antonym: divine - of or like a god; not human or mortal
  • antonym: human - relating to people rather than animals
  • synonym: mortal - subject to death; human or finite
