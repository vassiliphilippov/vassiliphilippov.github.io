canine tooth

noun
pointed tooth between incisors and premolars
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪnaɪn tuθ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnaɪn tuːθ / (BrE)
one of the four pointed, conical teeth situated between the incisors and premolars, used for tearing food

Examples

A canine tooth
After chipping his canine tooth, he needed dental treatment.
Many animals use their canine teeth to grasp and tear food.
The dentist noticed that my canine tooth was slightly crooked.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cuspid - another term for canine tooth
  • degree lower: incisor - a front tooth adapted for cutting
  • degree higher: premolar - a tooth situated between the canine and molar teeth
