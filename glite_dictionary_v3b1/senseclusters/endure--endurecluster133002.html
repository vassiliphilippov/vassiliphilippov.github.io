endure

verb
to last or persist over time
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊə / (BrE)
to continue to exist or last for a long time, especially despite difficulties

Examples

Traditions endure for years
Her popularity has endured despite many controversies.
The drought continued to endure for several months.
This tradition will likely endure for generations to come.
Despite centuries of change, many cultural traditions still endure.
Legends often endure because people cherish the stories and pass them on.
The beauty of the ancient ruins continues to endure through the ages.
Good friendships endure through difficult times as well as good ones.
Her reputation endured long after she retired from the public eye.
This bridge has endured for over a century despite harsh weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: last - to continue for a specified time
  • antonym: perish - to cease to exist or to decay completely
  • synonym: persist - to continue to exist especially for longer than expected
  • antonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
