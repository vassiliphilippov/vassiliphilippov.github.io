intrinsic

adjective
essential or inherent quality
4.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrɪnsɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɪn.zɪk / (BrE)
belonging naturally; essential to the basic nature of a person or thing

Examples

Intrinsic value
Curiosity is an intrinsic quality of successful scientists.
The painting's value is not just monetary; it has intrinsic worth to the family.
The risks are intrinsic to the profession and cannot be entirely eliminated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extrinsic - not part of the essential nature; coming from outside
  • synonym: inherent - existing in something as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute
