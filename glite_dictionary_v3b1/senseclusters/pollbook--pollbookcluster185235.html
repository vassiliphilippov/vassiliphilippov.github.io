pollbook

noun
record of voters' choices
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpoʊl.bʊk/ (AmE), /ˈpəʊl.bʊk/ (BrE)
an official record listing how individual voters cast their ballots in an election, especially before secret ballots were used

Examples

Historic pollbook
The historian discovered a nineteenth-century pollbook that revealed how each citizen voted in the election.
Before secret ballots, a pollbook was used to publicly record every voter's choices.
Researchers analyzed the old pollbook to study voting patterns in early American elections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ballot - a process of voting, in writing and typically in secret
  • synonym: voter register - an official list of people eligible to vote
