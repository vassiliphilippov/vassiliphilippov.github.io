sharpen

verb
become sharp or sharper
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑr.pən /; / ˈʃɑːr.pən / (AmE), / ˈʃɑː.pən / (BrE)
to make or become sharp or pointed, especially at the edge or tip (such as a knife or tool)

Examples

To sharpen a knife
As the discussion progressed, opinions began to sharpen on both sides.
Competition in the market tends to sharpen during the holiday season.
The image on the screen slowly sharpened into focus.
He used a whetstone to sharpen his knife after cutting vegetables.
Please sharpen the pencils before the meeting starts.
The image editor can sharpen blurry photographs with a single click.
He sharpened the knife so it would cut more easily.
I need to sharpen my pencil before drawing the sketch.
The worker sharpened the stakes to drive them into the ground.
