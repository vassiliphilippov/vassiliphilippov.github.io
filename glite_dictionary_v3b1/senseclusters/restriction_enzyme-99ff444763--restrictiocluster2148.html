restriction enzyme

noun
enzyme that cuts DNA at specific sites
6.3
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstrɪkʃən ˈɛnzaɪm / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃən ˈɛnzaɪm / (BrE)
an enzyme that cuts DNA at specific sequences, producing defined fragments used in genetic research and biotechnology

Examples

Restriction enzyme cuts DNA
Certain restriction enzymes recognize palindromic DNA sequences and cleave at those sites.
Scientists used a restriction enzyme to cut the plasmid DNA at a specific sequence.
The development of restriction enzymes revolutionized molecular biology and genetic engineering.
