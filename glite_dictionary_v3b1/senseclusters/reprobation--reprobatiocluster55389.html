reprobation

noun
severe disapproval or condemnation
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprəˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛprəˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
strong disapproval or condemnation, especially on moral grounds

Examples

Public reprobation
He risked the reprobation of his family by choosing a different path.
Her actions were met with universal reprobation from her colleagues.
The preacher spoke with utter reprobation of the town's immoral behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approval - the belief that something or someone is good or acceptable
  • synonym: disapproval - a feeling of having a negative opinion of someone or something
