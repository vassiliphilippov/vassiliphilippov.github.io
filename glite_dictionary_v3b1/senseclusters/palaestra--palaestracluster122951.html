palaestra

noun
ancient Greek or Roman sports training ground
5.7
Pronunciation: / pəˈlistrə / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪstrə / (BrE)
an ancient public place, especially in Greece or Rome, used for training wrestlers and other athletes

Examples

Ancient palaestra building
He studied ancient sports by examining the layout of the palaestra at Olympia.
In classical Athens, the palaestra was a hub of athletic and social life.
The remains of the palaestra can still be seen near the ancient stadium.
