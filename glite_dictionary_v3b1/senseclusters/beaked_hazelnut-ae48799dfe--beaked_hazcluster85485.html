beaked hazelnut

noun
hazel with beaklike involucres on its nuts
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbikt ˈheɪzəlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈbiːkt ˈheɪzəlˌnʌt / (BrE)
A type of hazel shrub native to western North America, recognized by its nuts enclosed in beaklike, elongated coverings.

Examples

The beaked hazelnut shrub
Its distinctive involucre makes the beaked hazelnut easy for botanists to identify.
Squirrels often gather beaked hazelnut seeds for food in late summer.
The beaked hazelnut grows in forests across western North America.
