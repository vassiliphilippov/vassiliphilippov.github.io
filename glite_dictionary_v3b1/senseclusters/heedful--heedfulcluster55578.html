heedful

adjective
paying careful attention
6.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈhiːd.fəl/ (AmE), /ˈhiːd.fəl/ (BrE)
paying careful attention; being attentive or mindful, especially of advice, warnings, or details

Examples

Be heedful of advice
Being heedful can prevent many unnecessary mistakes.
Parents should be heedful of their children's online activities.
She listened to his advice in a heedful manner.
Always be heedful of traffic when crossing the street.
He was heedful of the risks involved when making his decision.
The manager praised her for being heedful of the customers' concerns.
He was heedful of every sign that something was amiss.
She remained heedful of the teacher's instructions throughout the experiment.
Travelers should be heedful of local customs to avoid offending residents.
