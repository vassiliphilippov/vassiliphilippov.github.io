nibbana

noun
Buddhist final liberation
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪˈbɑːnə / (AmE), / nɪˈbɑːnə / (BrE)
the ultimate state of perfect peace and freedom from suffering in Buddhism; the final release from the cycle of rebirth

Examples

Buddhist nibbana
The monk devoted his life to meditation in pursuit of nibbana.
According to Buddhist teachings, nibbana is the end of all suffering and rebirth.
Many followers gathered to commemorate the master's nibbana after his peaceful passing.
Many Buddhists dedicate their lives to reaching nibbana through meditation and ethical living.
The teachings describe nibbana as the end of all suffering and desire.
Achieving nibbana is considered the highest goal in Buddhist practice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nirvana - the state of perfect peace and liberation from suffering in Buddhism
  • antonym: samsara - the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth in Buddhism and Hinduism
