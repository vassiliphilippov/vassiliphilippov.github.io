unspoken accusation

noun
implied accusation without being spoken
7.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈspoʊkən ˌækjəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ʌnˈspəʊkən ˌækjʊˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
An accusation that is implied or understood without being stated aloud.

Examples

An unspoken accusation
He felt the unspoken accusation from his colleagues, even though no one said a word.
The tense silence in the meeting carried an unspoken accusation against the absent manager.
There was an unspoken accusation in her eyes as she looked at him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit accusation - an accusation that is stated clearly and in detail
  • synonym: implicit accusation - an accusation that is suggested or implied rather than stated directly
