age-related macular degeneration

noun
age-related eye disease causing vision loss
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒ rɪˌleɪtɪd ˈmækjələr ˌdɛdʒəˈnreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒ rɪˌleɪtɪd ˈmækjʊlə ˌdɛdʒɪnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the macula, part of the retina, degenerates with age, causing loss of central vision

Examples

Advanced age-related macular degeneration
Advanced age-related macular degeneration can make everyday activities like reading and driving very difficult.
Her grandmother was diagnosed with age-related macular degeneration and gradually lost her central vision.
Smoking is recognized as a risk factor for age-related macular degeneration in older adults.
