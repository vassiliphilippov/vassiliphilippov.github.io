inflationism

noun
policy favoring inflation
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or policy that deliberately increasing the money supply and causing inflation is beneficial for the economy

Examples

Inflationism in economics
Some economists argue that inflationism can help reduce unemployment during economic downturns.
The government's inflationism was criticized for leading to higher prices and reduced savings.
Debates about inflationism often arise during periods of economic crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflationism - the policy or belief that reducing the money supply and causing deflation is beneficial
  • synonym: monetarism - an economic theory focusing on controlling the money supply to manage the economy
