felony

noun
serious crime (e.g., murder, arson)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛləni / (AmE), / ˈfɛləni / (BrE)
a serious crime, typically one punishable by imprisonment for more than one year or by death, such as murder or arson

Examples

A felony crime
Arson is considered a felony and carries a severe penalty.
He was convicted of a felony after the trial concluded.
Possession of that drug in large amounts can be charged as a felony in many states.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crime - an act punishable by law; an offense
  • antonym: misdemeanor - a less serious crime than a felony
