sartorius

noun
thigh muscle; longest in human body
Pronunciation: / sɑːrˈtɔriəs / (AmE), / sɑːˈtɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
a long, thin muscle running down the length of the thigh, responsible for rotating the leg and flexing the hip and knee joints; the longest muscle in the human body

Examples

The sartorius muscle
During the examination, the doctor checked the patient's sartorius for signs of injury.
Stretching the sartorius can help improve flexibility in the thigh.
The sartorius muscle allows you to cross one leg over the other.
