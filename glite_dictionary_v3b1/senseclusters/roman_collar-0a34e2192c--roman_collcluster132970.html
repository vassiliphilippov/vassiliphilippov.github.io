Roman collar

noun
distinctive clergyman's stiff white collar
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.mən ˈkɑː.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈkɒl.ə / (BrE)
a stiff, white, band-like collar that has no opening in the front, worn as a distinctive symbol by members of the Christian clergy

Examples

A Roman collar worn
Many members of the clergy are recognized by their distinctive Roman collar.
She noticed the Roman collar as a sign of his profession.
The priest adjusted his Roman collar before entering the church.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clerical collar - a detachable white collar worn by Christian clergy
