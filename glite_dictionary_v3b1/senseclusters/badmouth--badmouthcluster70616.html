badmouth

verb
speak unfavorably about someone or something
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbædˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈbædˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
to criticize or speak unfavorably about someone or something, especially unfairly or maliciously

Examples

To badmouth a person
He was upset to hear that people had badmouthed his project at the meeting.
It's not right to badmouth your colleagues behind their backs.
She often badmouths competitors to get ahead in business.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disparage - to speak of as unimportant or bad; to belittle
  • antonym: praise - to express admiration or approval of someone or something
