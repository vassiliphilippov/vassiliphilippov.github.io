mediatize

verb
to publicize via media
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiətaɪz / (BrE)
to make something widely known or influenced by extensive media coverage

Examples

To mediatize a story
The celebrity's private life was quickly mediatized after the interview aired on television.
Political events are often mediatized to shape public opinion and influence voters.
The charity's efforts were mediatized to encourage more people to donate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden or secret
  • synonym: publicize - to make something widely known to the public
  • synonym: sensationalize - to present information in a way that provokes public interest or excitement
