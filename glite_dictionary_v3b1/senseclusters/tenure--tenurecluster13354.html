tenure

noun
the right to hold property or land
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛn.jɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛnjə / (BrE)
the legal right to hold or occupy property or land, especially as part of a historical system of landholding

Examples

Land tenure
After centuries, the system of land tenure in Europe has fundamentally changed.
The tenure of these estates often came with feudal duties and restrictions.
Under medieval law, tenure determined the obligations of the landholder to their lord.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: freehold - permanent and absolute tenure of land or property with freedom to dispose of it at will
  • degree lower: leasehold - the holding of property by lease
  • synonym: ownership - the state or fact of having control of property
