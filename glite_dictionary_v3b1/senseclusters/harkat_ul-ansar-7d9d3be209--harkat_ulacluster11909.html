Harkat ul-Ansar

proper noun
Pakistani Islamic militant organization active in Kashmir
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɑːrˈkɑːt ʊl ɑːnˈsɑːr / (AmE), / hɑːˈkʌt ʊl ænˈsɑː / (BrE)
An Islamic militant organization founded in Pakistan that fought against Soviet forces in Afghanistan during the 1980s and now operates primarily as a terrorist group in Kashmir, seeking the region's accession to Pakistan.

Examples

Harkat ul-Ansar militant group
During the 1980s, Harkat ul-Ansar played a prominent role in the armed resistance against Soviet forces in Afghanistan.
Reports indicate that Harkat ul-Ansar has been recruiting new members for operations in Kashmir.
The government condemned Harkat ul-Ansar for its involvement in the recent unrest in the region.
