unwitnessed

adjective
not formally attested
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɪtnəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪtnɪst / (BrE)
not seen, observed, or witnessed by anyone (including in legal or official situations)

Examples

An unwitnessed event
The contract was declared invalid because it was unwitnessed at the time of signing.
An unwitnessed event can be difficult to prove in court.
The lawyer explained that an unwitnessed will might not be accepted as legal evidence.
The accident happened in an unwitnessed alley late at night.
Many natural events in remote areas remain unwitnessed by humans.
The document was signed in an unwitnessed room, raising questions about its validity.

  • synonym: unobserved - not watched or noticed by anyone
  • synonym: unsigned - not having a required signature
