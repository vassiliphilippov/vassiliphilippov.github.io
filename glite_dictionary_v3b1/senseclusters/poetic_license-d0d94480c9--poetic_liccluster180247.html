poetic license

noun
freedom to deviate from rules in art for effect
Pronunciation: / poʊˈɛtɪk ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪk ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
The freedom taken by a writer or artist to deviate from conventional rules, facts, or literal accuracy in order to produce a desired artistic effect.

Examples

Poetic license in writing
Filmmakers often use poetic license to create more dramatic scenes.
Rhyming schemes sometimes require poetic license with grammar or pronunciation.
The author exercised poetic license by changing the timeline of real events in her novel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artistic license - freedom to depart from facts or rules for effect in any art form
  • antonym: literalism - strict adherence to exact words or facts
