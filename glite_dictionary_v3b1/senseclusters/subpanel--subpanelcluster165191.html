subpanel

noun
secondary electrical panel
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌpænəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌpænəl / (BrE)
a secondary electrical panel that distributes power to a specific area or group of circuits within a building

Examples

Electrical subpanel
The electrician installed a subpanel in the basement to handle the new lighting circuits.
If you want to add more outlets in the garage, you may need a subpanel for extra capacity.
Each apartment unit has its own subpanel connected to the building's main electrical system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breaker panel - the main electrical panel that distributes power throughout a building
  • synonym: distribution board - a panel that divides electrical power into subsidiary circuits
