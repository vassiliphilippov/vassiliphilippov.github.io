nobility

noun
elevation of character or ideals
Pronunciation: / noʊˈbɪləti / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of having high moral principles, elevated mind, or exalted ideals and conduct.

Examples

Moral nobility
Acts of nobility are often remembered long after personal achievements are forgotten.
He was admired for the nobility of his vision and the integrity of his actions.
Her nobility was evident in the way she treated everyone with kindness and respect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: baseness - lack of moral principles; bad character
  • synonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
