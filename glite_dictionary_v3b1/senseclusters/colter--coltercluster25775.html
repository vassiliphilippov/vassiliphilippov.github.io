colter

noun
blade in front of a plow
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊltər / (AmE), / ˈkɒltə / (BrE)
a sharp steel blade or wedge attached in front of a plow to cut the soil vertically before it is turned over

Examples

A colter blade
Over time, the colter can become dull and must be sharpened for effective plowing.
The old farmer replaced the colter to ensure his plow would slice cleanly through the hard earth.
With a new colter installed, the team finished the field much faster than the year before.
