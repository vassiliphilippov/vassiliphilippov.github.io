outward-moving

adjective
moving away from the center
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtwɚd ˈmuːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtwəd ˈmuːvɪŋ / (BrE)
moving or directed away from the center, especially when spinning or traveling in a curve

Examples

An outward-moving force
A rapidly spinning disk produces an outward-moving force on objects placed near its edge.
As the vehicle turned the corner quickly, passengers felt an outward-moving sensation.
The outward-moving current carried debris away from the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centrifugal - moving or tending to move away from a center
  • antonym: centripetal - moving or tending to move toward the center
