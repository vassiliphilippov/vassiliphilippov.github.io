aberrator

noun
person who strays
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæbəˈreɪtər / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
an archaic term for a person who wanders or strays from the usual path or course

Examples

A wandering aberrator
The old text described an aberrator who left the village to seek his own path.
In ancient stories, an aberrator was often seen as a symbol of rebellion.
Few understood the motives of the aberrator who wandered far from home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: guide - a person who leads or directs others
  • synonym: stray - a person or animal that has strayed from the proper place
  • synonym: wanderer - a person who travels aimlessly or without a fixed course
