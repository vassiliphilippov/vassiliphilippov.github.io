congenital megacolon

noun
enlarged colon present from birth
9.9 8.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˌmɛɡəˌkoʊlən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˌmɛɡəˈkəʊlən / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which the colon lacks the usual network of nerves, resulting in poor bowel movements and abnormal enlargement of the colon.

Examples

A congenital megacolon case
Early intervention is crucial for children affected by congenital megacolon.
Surgery is often required to treat congenital megacolon and restore normal bowel function.
The newborn was diagnosed with congenital megacolon after showing signs of severe constipation.
