security deposit

noun
money paid as a guarantee or collateral
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkjʊrəti dɪˈpɑːzɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊərəti dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
An amount of money paid in advance as a guarantee for the fulfillment of an obligation, such as collateral deposited with a broker when borrowing to buy securities.

Examples

A security deposit refund
If nothing is damaged upon move-out, your entire security deposit will be refunded.
The landlord required a security deposit before handing over the keys to the apartment.
You need to pay a security deposit when opening a margin account with the broker.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deposit - sum of money placed or kept in a bank account, usually to gain interest
