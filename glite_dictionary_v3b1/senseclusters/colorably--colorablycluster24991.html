colorably

adverb
apparently validly
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚəbli / (AmE), / ˈkʌlɚəbli / (BrE)
in a way that seems valid or true on the surface, but may not be so in reality

Examples

To colorably comply
The company was colorably complying with the regulations, but actually ignored several key requirements.
His actions were colorably legal, though many questioned their true legitimacy.
The proposal was colorably justified, but closer inspection revealed serious flaws.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genuinely - in a way that is truly what it is said to be
  • synonym: ostensibly - in a way that appears to be true but may not be
