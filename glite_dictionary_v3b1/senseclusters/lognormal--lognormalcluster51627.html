lognormal

noun
statistical distribution
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈlɑːg.nɔːr.məl/ /; / /ˈlɔːg.nɔːr.məl/ (AmE), /ˈlɒg.nɔː.məl/ (BrE)
a probability distribution or random variable whose logarithm is normally distributed, often used in statistics and finance

Examples

A lognormal variable
The analyst assumed a lognormal for modeling the distribution of income in the population.
Many natural phenomena, such as the size of raindrops, follow a lognormal rather than a normal distribution.
In finance, stock prices are often modeled using a lognormal to account for their multiplicative growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lognormal distribution - a probability distribution of a variable whose logarithm is normally distributed
  • antonym: normal distribution - a bell-shaped probability distribution that is symmetrical about the mean
