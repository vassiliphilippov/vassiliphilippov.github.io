burglarize

verb
enter and steal from a building
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜːrɡlərˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡləraɪz / (BrE)
to enter a building illegally, especially by force, with the intention of stealing

Examples

To burglarize a house
Security cameras showed a man burglarizing several apartments in the complex.
The police arrested him after he tried to burglarize the jewelry store.
Their house was burglarized while they were on vacation last summer.
After they burglarised the flat, the thieves quickly fled the scene.
He was caught on camera attempting to burglarise the jewelry store.
The police reported that three houses were burglarised in the same neighborhood last night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burgle - to break into a building to commit theft (primarily British English)
