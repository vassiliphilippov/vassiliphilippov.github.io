filter

verb
remove impurities by filtering
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
to remove unwanted substances by passing something through a filter, or to pass through a filter (literally or figuratively)

Examples

To filter water
Coffee is usually made by filtering hot water through ground coffee beans.
The scientists filtered the air sample to collect particles for analysis.
You should filter the water before drinking it to remove harmful bacteria.
Rumors about the new policy slowly filtered out to the entire staff.
Sunlight began to filter through the leaves, casting dappled shadows on the ground.
The rainwater will filter down through the soil before reaching the river.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contaminate - make something impure by adding harmful substances
  • synonym: permeate - to spread through; to pass through the pores or openings of something
  • synonym: purify - make something pure by removing contaminants
  • synonym: seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material
