pulse

noun
brief change in electrical signal
5.6 8.30/m
Pronunciation: / pÊŒls / (AmE), / pÊŒls / (BrE)
a brief, sudden change in an electrical signal, often repeated periodically, as used in electronics or telecommunications

Examples

An electrical pulse
A digital signal consists of a series of pulses representing binary information.
The circuit generates a pulse every second to regulate the timing.
The technician measured the length of each pulse using an oscilloscope.

Synonyms

  • pulsation
    6.8 0.10/m

  • impulse
    7.7 2.06/m
    an impulse is a very short, sharp surge of electrical energy, while a pulse often repeats and forms a regular signal shape.
