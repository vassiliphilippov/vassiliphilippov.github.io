francophone

noun
French-speaking person
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfræŋkəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkəfəʊn / (BrE)
a person who speaks French, either as a native language or fluently

Examples

A francophone visitor
The conference attracted many francophones from different parts of the world.
As a francophone, she enjoys reading French literature in its original language.
Several African countries have large francophone populations due to their colonial history.
Canada has a large francophone population, especially in the province of Quebec.
The conference brought together francophone leaders from around the world.
Many African countries have significant francophone communities due to their colonial history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anglophone - a person who speaks English
  • synonym: francophonic - relating to French-speaking people or communities
