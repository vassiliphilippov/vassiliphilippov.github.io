fish steak

noun
cross-sectional slice of a large fish
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ steɪk / (AmE), / fɪʃ steɪk / (BrE)
a cross-sectional slice cut from a large fish, typically served as a portion for cooking and eating

Examples

A grilled fish steak
He ordered a grilled fish steak with a lemon butter sauce at the restaurant.
The chef recommends marinating the fish steak before grilling it for better flavor.
To prepare a fish steak, slice the fish perpendicular to the spine into thick pieces.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fish fillet - a boneless piece of fish meat, usually cut parallel to the backbone
