corporeal

adjective
relating to the physical body
8.4 1.18/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrÀàp…îri…ôl / (AmE), / k…îÀêÀàp…îÀêri…ôl / (BrE)
relating to the body or something physical, not spiritual or intangible

Examples

The corporeal body
Many religions distinguish the corporeal body from the immortal soul.
The pain she felt was entirely corporeal rather than emotional.
Yoga seeks to balance corporeal health with mental well-being.
Her corporeal needs, such as food and shelter, could not be ignored during the retreat.
Philosophers often debate the nature of corporeal versus spiritual existence.
The ghost appeared to have a corporeal presence, surprising everyone in the room.

Synonyms

  • corporal
    6.8 1.78/m
    corporal is used for bodily matters in formal or legal phrases, while corporeal means relating to the body or physical existence.
  • bodily
    3.7 0.40/m
    bodily describes felt or visible parts of a person's frame in everyday speech, while corporeal sounds more formal and means physical.
  • somatic
    7.9 1.93/m
    somatic is the medical word used in science and clinics for body issues, while corporeal means simply physical or bodily.
  • physical
    2.7 84/m
    physical refers to matter and material objects in general, while corporeal focuses on the living body and bodily existence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incorporeal - lacking physical form or substance; immaterial
  • antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul rather than the body
