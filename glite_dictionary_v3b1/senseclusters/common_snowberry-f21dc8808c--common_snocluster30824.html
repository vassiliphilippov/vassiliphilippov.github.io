common snowberry

noun
North American shrub with white berries
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈsnoʊˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈsnəʊˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to western North America, known for its spikes of pink flowers and round white berries.

Examples

The common snowberry shrub
Birds are attracted to the common snowberry because of its clusters of berries.
The common snowberry is often planted in gardens for its ornamental white fruits.
We saw a thicket of common snowberry along the hiking trail in Oregon.

Related Terms
