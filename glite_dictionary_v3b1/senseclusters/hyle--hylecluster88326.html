hyle

noun
philosophical matter
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪli / (AmE), / ˈhɪli / (BrE)
in philosophy, the matter or substance from which things are made

Examples

Basic hyle matter
In ancient Greek philosophy, hyle was considered the basic material of the universe.
Aristotle discussed the relationship between hyle and form in his metaphysical works.
The concept of hyle influenced later theories about the nature of physical reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: form - the essential nature or structure of something, as opposed to its matter
  • synonym: substance - the physical material from which something is made
