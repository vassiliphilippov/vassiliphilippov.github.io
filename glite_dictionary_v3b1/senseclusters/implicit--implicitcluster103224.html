implicit

adjective
complete; without doubt or reservation
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈplɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪsɪt / (BrE)
(of trust, faith, or belief) complete and without any doubt or reservation.

Examples

Implicit trust
His authority was accepted with implicit confidence by everyone on the team.
Parents often have implicit faith that teachers will look after their children.
She placed her implicit trust in her friends, never doubting their loyalty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt
  • synonym: unquestioning - (of belief or trust) not doubting or questioning
