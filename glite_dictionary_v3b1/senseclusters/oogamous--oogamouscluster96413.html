oogamous

adjective
egg-sperm reproduction
Pronunciation: / oʊˈæɡəməs / (AmE), / əʊˈæɡəməs / (BrE)
describing sexual reproduction involving a large non-motile egg and a small motile sperm

Examples

Oogamous reproduction
Many green algae are oogamous, producing large eggs and small, motile sperm.
In oogamous species, fertilization involves a stationary egg and a swimming sperm.
The evolution of oogamous reproduction marked a significant step in the complexity of life cycles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anisogamous - having gametes of different sizes but both motile
  • antonym: isogamous - having gametes of similar size and form
