Synonyms

  • cause
    1.8 80/m
    a cause is something that makes an event happen, while a reason often names the explanation or motive behind it.
  • explanation
    1.0 60/m
    an explanation is a clear statement that helps you understand why, while a reason can be a cause, excuse, or purpose for something.
  • justification
    4.0 18/m
    a justification is a reason given to show an action is right, while a reason can be neutral and not defend the action.
  • ground
    6.2 13/m
    a ground is a formal reason used as a legal or official basis, while a reason can be simple and everyday.
  • rationale
    6.0 11/m
    a rationale is a set of logical reasons explained for a plan, while a reason is often a single, simple cause or idea.
  • purpose
    1.6 140/m
    a purpose is the intended aim or use of something, while a reason may explain or be the motive behind it.
  • motive
    3.0 30/m
    a motive is an internal or hidden reason that makes someone act, while a reason can be a public explanation.

  • synonym: pretext - a reason given in justification of a course of action that is not the real reason
  • antonym: result - a thing that is caused or produced by something else; effect
