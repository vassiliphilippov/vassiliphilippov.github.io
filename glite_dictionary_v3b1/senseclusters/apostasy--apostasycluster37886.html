apostasy

noun
the act of abandoning faith, party, or principle
9.9 2.11/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàp…ëÀêst…ôÀåsi /; / …ôÀàp…ëÀêst…ôsi / (AmE), / …ôÀàp…íst…ôsi / (BrE)
the act of abandoning or renouncing a religious faith, cause, or strongly held belief

Examples

apostasy from religion
After years of loyalty, his apostasy was seen as a significant betrayal.
Her apostasy from the party shocked her colleagues and supporters.
Political apostasy is often driven by deep disagreements or ideological rifts.
After his apostasy, he was shunned by members of his former church.
Her public apostasy from the party shocked many of her longtime supporters.
Throughout history, apostasy has often been met with severe social consequences.

Synonyms

  • renunciation
    8.9 0.53/m
    renunciation is a formal rejection of a belief or way of life often chosen publicly, while apostasy is abandoning a religious faith.
  • abjuration
    9.9 0.06/m
    abjuration is a solemn, often legal withdrawal from beliefs or claims, whereas apostasy is the act of leaving or rejecting a religious faith.

  • antonym: loyalty - faithfulness to a cause or party
