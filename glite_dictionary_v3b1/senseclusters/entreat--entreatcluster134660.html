entreat

verb
ask or beg earnestly
8.1 0.73/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆtriËt / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆtriËt / (BrE)
to ask or beg for something earnestly or urgently

Examples

To entreat a friend
He is entreating his friends not to reveal the secret until tomorrow.
She entreated him to stay a little longer, hoping he would change his mind.
The villagers entreated the mayor for more time to pay their taxes.

Synonyms

  • implore
    5.3 2.17/m
    implore is urgent and often desperate, while entreat is earnest but can be calmer and more formal.
  • beseech
    8.2 1.62/m
    beseech is fervent and old-fashioned in tone, while entreat is earnest and used in formal but modern speech.
  • plead
    3.9 13/m
    plead is often used to argue or ask with emotion, while entreat focuses on gently asking earnestly.
  • beg
    1.0 10/m
    beg is plain and common, used in urgent or desperate requests, while entreat sounds more formal and polite.
  • supplicate
    9.6 0.14/m
    supplicate is humble and often used in prayer or to authorities, while entreat is general earnest asking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beg - to ask someone earnestly for something
  • antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, not earnestly
  • synonym: implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
