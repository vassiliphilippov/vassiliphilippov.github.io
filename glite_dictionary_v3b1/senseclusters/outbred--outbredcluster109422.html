outbred

adjective
bred from unrelated parents
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈbrɛd / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈbrɛd / (BrE)
bred from parents that are not closely related; having ancestors from different families, classes, or tribes, especially to increase genetic diversity.

Examples

Outbred animals
In animal breeding, outbred individuals usually display greater vitality than inbred ones.
Populations that are outbred tend to have greater resistance to disease.
The scientists used outbred mice in their experiments to ensure genetic diversity.
