thud

verb
strike or fall with a dull sound
7.9 2.9
Pronunciation: /θʌd/ (AmE), /θʌd/ (BrE)
to make or cause a dull, heavy sound, especially when something strikes, falls, or moves roughly

Examples

To thud on the floor
Heavy boots thudded across the wooden floor above us.
Raindrops thudded against the window as the storm intensified.
The rock thudded to the ground, sending up a small cloud of dust.
As they forgot to lubricate the machine, it started to thud and grind.
The engine would thud loudly whenever the oil ran low.
The old car began to thud as it sputtered down the road.
Heavy raindrops thudded against the windows throughout the night.
Her heart thudded in her chest as she waited for the results.
The book thudded to the floor, startling everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bang - to strike or hit something with a sudden loud noise
  • antonym: clatter - to make a loud, rattling sound
  • degree lower: tap - make a light, gentle sound when hitting or touching something
  • synonym: thump - to make a heavy, muffled sound by hitting something heavily
