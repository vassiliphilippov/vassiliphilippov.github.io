beget

verb
to father or produce offspring
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡɛt / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɛt / (BrE)
to become the father of a child (especially used in old or biblical language)

Examples

To beget a son
According to the tradition, Abraham begot Isaac in his old age.
It was once believed that gods could beget children with mortals.
Legends say that the king begot many heirs to continue his dynasty.
In the ancient text, it is written that Abraham begat Isaac.
The old stories often say that one king begat another, continuing the royal line.
He begat many sons, according to the family records.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bear - to give birth to a child
  • synonym: father - to become the father of a child
  • synonym: sire - to father or produce offspring (especially of a male animal)
