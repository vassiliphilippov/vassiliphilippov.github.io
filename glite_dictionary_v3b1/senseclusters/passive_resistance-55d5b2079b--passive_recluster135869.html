passive resistance

noun
nonviolent opposition to authority
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪv rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪv rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
a method of opposing authority or a government, especially by refusing to cooperate or by undertaking nonviolent actions such as fasting or boycotts

Examples

Passive resistance movement
Gandhi famously advocated passive resistance as a means to challenge British rule.
Many movements for social change have used passive resistance rather than violent confrontation.
The protesters engaged in passive resistance by sitting in front of the government building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: civil disobedience - refusal to obey laws as a form of peaceful protest
  • antonym: violent resistance - opposition to authority using violence
