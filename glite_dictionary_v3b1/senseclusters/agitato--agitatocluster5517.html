agitato

adverb
musically agitated
Pronunciation: / ˌædʒɪˈtɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌadʒɪˈtɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
in music, played in an agitated, excited, or restless manner

Examples

Agitato passage
The violinist played the section marked agitato with intense energy and passion.
When the score indicated agitato, the orchestra increased both speed and volume.
The conductor signaled for a more agitato interpretation during the dramatic passage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegro - in music, played in a fast and lively manner
  • antonym: calmato - in music, played in a calm manner
