parathion poisoning

noun
poisoning caused by parathion insecticide
8.6
Pronunciation: /pær.ə.ˈθaɪ.ən ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /pær.ə.ˈθaɪ.ən ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
A toxic condition caused by inhaling or ingesting the insecticide parathion, typically resulting in symptoms such as nausea, abdominal pain, headache, convulsions, and sweating.

Examples

Accidental parathion poisoning
Accidental parathion poisoning can occur if the chemical is not handled with proper protective equipment.
Doctors treated the patient for parathion poisoning after he was exposed to contaminated water.
Emergency responders are trained to recognize the symptoms of parathion poisoning in agricultural workers.
