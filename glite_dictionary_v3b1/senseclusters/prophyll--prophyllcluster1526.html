prophyll

noun
small leaflike structure in plants
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊ.fɪl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.fɪl / (BrE)
a small leaflike structure, especially the first leaf or pair of leaves produced at the base of a lateral shoot or flower in some plants

Examples

A prophyll leaf
Botanists study the development and variation of the prophyll in different plant families.
In some plant species, the prophyll is reduced to a tiny, rudimentary form.
The prophyll often appears as a small, scale-like structure at the base of a flower stalk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bract - a modified or specialized leaf associated with a reproductive structure
