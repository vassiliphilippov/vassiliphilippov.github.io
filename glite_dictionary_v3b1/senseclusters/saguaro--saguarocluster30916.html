saguaro

noun
giant cactus of the American Southwest
8.7 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàw…ëÀêro ä / (AmE), / s…ôÀàw…ëÀêr…ô ä / (BrE)
A very large, tree-like cactus native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with a thick, columnar trunk, white flowers, and red edible fruit.

Examples

A saguaro cactus
A single saguaro may weigh several tons when fully grown.
During their bloom, saguaros produce spectacular white flowers that attract bats and birds.
The towering saguaro can live for over 150 years in the harsh desert environment.

Synonyms

  • Carnegiea gigantea
    9.8 0.00/m
    Carnegiea gigantea is the scientific name used by botanists and in books, while saguaro is the common everyday name.
