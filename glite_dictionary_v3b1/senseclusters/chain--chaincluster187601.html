chain

noun
series of bonded atoms (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn / (AmE), / tʃeɪn / (BrE)
(Chemistry) a series of atoms bonded together in a sequence, typically forming the backbone of organic molecules.

Examples

A carbon chain
A polymer is formed when many smaller molecules are linked in a repeating chain.
Proteins are made up of an amino acid chain folded into a complex shape.
The molecule consists of a long carbon chain with several attached groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ring - a group of atoms bonded in a closed loop within a molecule (chemistry)
