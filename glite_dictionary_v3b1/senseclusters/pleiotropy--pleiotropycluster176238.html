pleiotropy

noun
gene affecting traits
9.9
Pronunciation: / plaɪˈɑːtrəpi / (AmE), / plaɪˈɒtrəpi / (BrE)
the phenomenon in genetics where a single gene influences two or more seemingly unrelated traits in an organism

Examples

Pleiotropy in genetics
In genetics, pleiotropy explains how one gene can affect both eye color and blood type.
Researchers discovered that pleiotropy complicates the prediction of genetic diseases.
The concept of pleiotropy is important for understanding complex traits in biology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polygeny - the phenomenon where multiple genes influence a single trait
