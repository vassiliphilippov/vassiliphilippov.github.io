intervenor

noun
party joining a legal proceeding
8.0 7.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈviː.nər/ /; / /ɪn.tə.ˈviː.nər/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈviː.nə/ (BrE)
(law) a person or entity who enters an ongoing legal proceeding to protect an interest or present a viewpoint.

Examples

An intervenor in court
A trade association filed as an intervenor in the environmental lawsuit.
The court allowed an intervenor to participate in the hearing to represent local residents' concerns.
Without the intervenor, important evidence might not have been considered in the final judgment.
The judge permitted an intervener to join the proceedings to represent community interests.
Several organizations applied to become interveners in the high-profile court case.
As an intervener, the company was able to present its own evidence to the court.
The judge permitted the intervener to submit additional documents relevant to the dispute.
As an intervener, the organization aimed to ensure its interests were represented in the trial.
The court considered the arguments presented by the intervener before making its final decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defendant - a person against whom a legal action is brought
  • synonym: litigant - a party engaged in a lawsuit
  • synonym: participant - a person who takes part in something
  • synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
