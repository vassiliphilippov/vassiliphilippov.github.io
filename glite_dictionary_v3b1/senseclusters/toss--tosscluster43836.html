toss

verb
move or stir about violently
1.4 4.03/m
Pronunciation: / tÉ‘s / (AmE), / tÉ’s / (BrE)
to move or stir about violently, especially in a restless or agitated manner

Examples

Boats toss in waves
During storms, small boats toss violently on the open sea.
The restless child would toss and turn all night, unable to sleep.
Unable to calm her mind, she continued to toss on her pillow until morning.

Synonyms

  • thrash
    5.8 3.02/m
    thrash means moving violently and uncontrollably, often whole-body, while toss suggests restless stirring or lighter, agitated movement.
  • flail
    5.8 3.09/m
    flail is waving or swinging wildly, often with arms, while toss is more a restless stirring or unsettled movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thrash - to move about violently and uncontrollably
