hydroscope

noun
underwater viewing device
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəskəʊp / (BrE)
a device used to observe objects below the surface of water, similar to an underwater telescope or viewing tube

Examples

Hydroscope for underwater viewing
The scientist used a hydroscope to study the fish swimming near the riverbed.
With the help of a hydroscope, divers could inspect the underwater structure without getting wet.
The children were fascinated by the view of the submerged plants through the hydroscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: periscope - an optical instrument for viewing objects above the surface, often used in submarines
  • synonym: telescope - an instrument for viewing distant objects, usually in the sky
