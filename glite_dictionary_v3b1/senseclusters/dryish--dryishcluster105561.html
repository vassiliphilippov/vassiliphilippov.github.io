dryish

adjective
somewhat dry
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat dry in manner, style, or humor; mildly unemotional or understated.

Examples

Dryish humor
Her dryish sense of humor made the meeting more enjoyable without being too obvious.
The film's dryish tone appealed to viewers who prefer subtle comedy.
He gave a dryish reply that made everyone smile quietly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dry - unemotional, understated, or lacking obvious emotion
  • antonym: warm - showing friendliness or emotion
  • synonym: wry - showing dry, mocking, or ironic humor
