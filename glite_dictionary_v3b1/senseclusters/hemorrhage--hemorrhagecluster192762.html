hemorrhage

noun
uncontrolled bleeding from a blood vessel
5.3 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhem.ər.ɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈhem.rɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈhem.ə.rɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈhem.rɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a heavy or uncontrolled flow of blood from a ruptured blood vessel, often caused by injury or disease

Examples

A brain hemorrhage
A brain hemorrhage can cause lasting damage if not treated quickly.
Excessive blood loss due to internal hemorrhage is a medical emergency.
The patient suffered a severe hemorrhage after the accident and required immediate surgery.
Doctors worked quickly to stop the internal haemorrhage and stabilize her condition.
If not treated promptly, a brain haemorrhage can cause permanent damage or death.
The patient suffered a massive haemorrhage after the accident and required urgent surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bleeding - the general process of losing blood, usually through a wound
  • antonym: clot - a thick mass of coagulated blood that stops bleeding
  • synonym: hemorrhage - The American English spelling for 'haemorrhage' (loss of blood)
