incrementalist

noun
advocate of gradual change
Pronunciation: / ˌɪŋkrəˈmɛntəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪŋkrɪˈmɛntəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports making changes in small, gradual steps rather than through sudden or radical actions

Examples

An incrementalist thinker
As an incrementalist, she believed that small policy adjustments would be more effective than sweeping reforms.
The mayor's incrementalist strategy focused on improving the city step by step.
Critics argued that the incrementalist approach was too slow to address urgent problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gradualist - a person who believes in slow, step-by-step change
  • antonym: radical - a person who supports major or fundamental change
