used to introduce a new topic or to add further information that is not directly related to what was being said before

Examples

Incidentally, by the way
Incidentally, I met your brother at the conference yesterday.
Incidentally, have you seen the new restaurant on Main Street?
The main meeting will start at 10 a.m.; incidentally, refreshments will be served afterward.

Synonyms

  • by the way
    1.5 13/m
    by the way is a casual spoken phrase to add a new, unrelated point, while incidentally is more formal.
  • parenthetically
    7.5 0.36/m
    parenthetically is used for a quick side remark or within parentheses in writing, while incidentally introduces extra, unrelated comments.
  • in passing
    3.8 2.41/m
    in passing is a brief mention made without much focus during speech or writing, while incidentally signals a clear topic addition.
  • apropos
    8.1 0.00/m
    apropos is used to introduce a relevant or related point in speech, whereas incidentally often adds a new, unrelated side detail.
