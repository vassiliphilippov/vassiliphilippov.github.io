Pipa pipa

noun
South American toad species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːpə ˈpiːpə / (AmE), / ˈpiːpə ˈpiːpə / (BrE)
A species of South American toad known for incubating its young in pits on the skin of its back.

Examples

Pipa pipa toad
Researchers studied the unique reproductive habits of the Pipa pipa in the Amazon rainforest.
The Pipa pipa is remarkable for the way it carries its eggs embedded in its back.
Unlike most amphibians, Pipa pipa mothers protect their young until they are fully formed tadpoles.
