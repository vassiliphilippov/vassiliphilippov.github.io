racial segregation

noun
separation of people by race
7.7 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.ʃəl se.ɡrə.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.ʃəl se.ɡrə.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The separation of people into different groups based on their race, especially as a policy or practice enforced by law or custom.

Examples

Racial segregation policy
Racial segregation was a major issue in the United States during the 20th century.
Many civil rights leaders fought tirelessly to end racial segregation in their communities.
The law prohibited racial segregation in public schools and facilities.
