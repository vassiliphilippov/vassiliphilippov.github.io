travail

verb
work hard; labor intensely
Pronunciation: / trəˈveɪl / (AmE), / trəˈveɪl / (BrE)
to work hard or suffer through difficult or painful labor, especially over a long period

Examples

To travail for hours
For months, she travailed over her research project to ensure its success.
The miners would travail deep underground for hours with little rest.
Writers often travail late into the night to meet looming deadlines.
She travailed for years to complete her research.
The workers travail day and night to meet the deadline.
He travailed through many hardships before achieving success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drudge - to do hard, menial, or dull work
  • antonym: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax
  • synonym: struggle - to make a great effort in the face of difficulty
  • synonym: toil - to work extremely hard or incessantly
