dauphine

noun
French royal title
9.9
Pronunciation: / doʊˈfiːn / (AmE), / doʊˈfiːn / (BrE)
the title given to the wife of the heir apparent to the French throne

Examples

The French dauphine
The young dauphine was admired for her grace and intelligence at the French court.
As dauphine, she played an important role in diplomatic events and royal ceremonies.
Historians often study the influence of the dauphine during the final years of the monarchy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dauphin - the title for the heir apparent to the French throne
  • synonym: queen consort - the wife of a reigning king
