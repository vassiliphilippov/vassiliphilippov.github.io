draw out

verb
remove or extract, often with effort
3.8 1.83/m
Pronunciation: / dr…î ä a ät / (AmE), / dr…îÀê a ät / (BrE)
to remove or extract something (physical or abstract), such as an object, liquid, information, or feelings

Examples

To draw out a nail
He had to draw out the nail that was stuck in the wall.
She tried to draw out some information from her reluctant friend.
The detective managed to draw out the truth during the interview.
A pump is often used to draw out water from flooded basements.
Doctors may need to draw out excess fluid from a patient's lungs.
The nurse carefully drew out the venom from the snake bite using a syringe.
It took several questions to draw out the main point of her argument.
The interviewer managed to draw out some revealing details from the witness.
Through discussion, the teacher helped the students draw out the key themes in the text.
