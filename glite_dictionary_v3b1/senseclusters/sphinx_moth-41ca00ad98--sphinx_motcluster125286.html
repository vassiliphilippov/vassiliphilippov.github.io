sphinx moth

noun
large moth capable of hovering flight
Pronunciation: / sfɪŋks mɑθ / (AmE), / sfɪŋks mɒθ / (BrE)
any of various large moths with long narrow forewings, known for powerful flight and the ability to hover while feeding from flowers

Examples

The sphinx moth flies
Gardeners often notice the sphinx moth visiting their flowers at dusk.
The sphinx moth can hover in front of a blossom much like a hummingbird.
The caterpillar of the sphinx moth is sometimes called a hornworm.
