magnetic declination

noun
angle between magnetic north and true north
6.3 5.4
Pronunciation: / məɡˈnɛtɪk ˌdɛkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the angle between magnetic north and true north at a particular location, used in navigation and map reading

Examples

Magnetic declination angle
Changes in magnetic declination over time can affect compass readings.
Navigators must correct for magnetic declination when charting their course.
The magnetic declination at this location is about 5 degrees east of true north.
