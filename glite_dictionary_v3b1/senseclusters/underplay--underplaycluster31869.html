underplay

verb
act a role with restraint
2.7 5.2
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈpleɪ/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈpleɪ/ (BrE)
(of an actor) to perform a role with subtlety or restraint, avoiding exaggeration or overemphasis.

Examples

To underplay a role
Critics praised his decision to underplay rather than overact during the dramatic moments.
In this film, she skillfully underplays her role, letting the story speak for itself.
The actor chose to underplay the character's emotions, making the scene more powerful.
