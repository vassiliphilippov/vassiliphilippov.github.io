mandibular condyle

noun
rounded projection of the lower jaw
8.0
Pronunciation: / mænˈdɪbjələr kənˈdaɪl / (AmE), / mænˈdɪbjʊlə kɒnˈdaɪl / (BrE)
the rounded projection at the end of the mandible (lower jaw) that articulates with the skull to form the jaw joint

Examples

The mandibular condyle bone
A fracture of the mandibular condyle can affect jaw alignment and function.
On the X-ray, the doctor noted irregularities in the left mandibular condyle.
The mandibular condyle plays a crucial role in the movement of the jaw during chewing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: temporal bone - the bone of the skull that articulates with the mandibular condyle
