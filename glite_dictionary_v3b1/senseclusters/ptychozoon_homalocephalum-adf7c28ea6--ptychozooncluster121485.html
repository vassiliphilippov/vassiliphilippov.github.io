Ptychozoon homalocephalum

noun
gliding gecko species
Pronunciation: / ˌtaɪkəˈzoʊ.ɒn hoʊˌmæloʊˈsɛfələm / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪkəˈzəʊ.ɒn həʊˌmaləʊˈkɛfələm / (BrE)
A species of gecko notable for membranous expansions along its body, limbs, and tail, enabling it to glide short distances.

Examples

Ptychozoon homalocephalum gliding lizard
Anatomical adaptations allow Ptychozoon homalocephalum to cover surprising distances for a lizard.
Researchers observed the Ptychozoon homalocephalum in its natural Southeast Asian habitat.
The Ptychozoon homalocephalum can glide from tree to tree using the flaps along its sides.
