absorb

verb
to devote oneself wholly to
3.1 10/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàz…îrb / (AmE), / …ôbÀàz…îÀêb / (BrE)
to be fully occupied or interested in something; to have all your attention taken up

Examples

To absorb information
He absorbed himself in his studies to prepare for the exam.
She became so absorbed in her book that she lost track of time.
The artist was absorbed in painting for hours without interruption.
He was so absorbed in his work that he forgot to eat lunch.
The fascinating novel absorbed her for hours every evening.
Their conversation about politics absorbed the entire group during dinner.
