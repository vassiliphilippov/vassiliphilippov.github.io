Mediterranean anemia

noun
inherited anemia (thalassemia)
5.1 5.5
Pronunciation: /me.dɪ.tər.ˈeɪ.ni.ən ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ /; / /me.dɪ.tər.ˈeɪ.njən ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /me.dɪ.tə.ˈreɪ.ni.ən ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ /; / /me.dɪ.tə.ˈreɪ.njən ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
an inherited blood disorder characterized by abnormal hemoglobin production, resulting in anemia; also known as thalassemia

Examples

Mediterranean anemia blood disorder
Many cases of Mediterranean anemia are discovered during routine blood tests in childhood.
People with Mediterranean anemia often require regular blood transfusions.
The disorder known as Mediterranean anemia is prevalent in regions bordering the Mediterranean Sea.
Carriers of Mediterranean anaemia may not show symptoms but can pass the gene to their children.
She was diagnosed with Mediterranean anaemia after blood tests revealed abnormal hemoglobin levels.
Treatment options for Mediterranean anaemia often include regular blood transfusions and iron chelation therapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sickle cell anemia - another inherited hemoglobin disorder, not related to Mediterranean anaemia
  • synonym: thalassemia - another name for Mediterranean anemia
