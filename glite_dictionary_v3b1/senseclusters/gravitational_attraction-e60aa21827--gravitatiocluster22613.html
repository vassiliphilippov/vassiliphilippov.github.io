gravitational attraction

noun
force drawing masses toward each other
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl əˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl əˈtrakʃən / (BrE)
the force by which any two masses in the universe are drawn toward each other, especially the force exerted by the Earth that causes objects to fall toward its surface

Examples

Gravitational attraction force
Engineers must account for gravitational attraction when designing satellites and space probes.
On Mars, gravitational attraction is much weaker than on Earth, allowing for higher jumps.
The Moon orbits the Earth because of gravitational attraction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gravity - the force that attracts a body toward the center of the earth
