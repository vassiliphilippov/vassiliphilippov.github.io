nonremedial

adjective
not corrective
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnrɪˈmiːdiəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnrɪˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
not intended to correct, remedy, or address a deficiency or problem

Examples

Nonremedial course
The university offers nonremedial courses for students who meet the standard requirements.
He was placed in a nonremedial English class after passing the entrance exam.
Unlike remedial classes, nonremedial programs focus on advancing existing skills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: remedial - intended to correct or improve a deficiency
