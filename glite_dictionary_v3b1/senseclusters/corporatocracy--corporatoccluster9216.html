corporatocracy

noun
rule by corporations
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrpərəˈtɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpərəˈtɒkrəsi / (BrE)
a society or system in which corporations or their leaders have significant control over government or public policy

Examples

A corporatocracy system
Many critics argue that the country is becoming a corporatocracy where big businesses influence every major decision.
The documentary explores how a corporatocracy can undermine democratic institutions.
Some activists protest against the rise of corporatocracy in modern politics.

The film explores how a corporatocracy can influence political decisions and public policy.
Some believe that unchecked capitalism leads to a corporatocracy rather than a true democracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plutocracy - a society ruled by the wealthy
