reconnect

verb
to connect again
3.7 4.29/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêk…ôÀàn…õkt / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêk…ôÀàn…õkt / (BrE)
to connect or come together again after being separated or disconnected (can refer to things, people, or relationships)

Examples

To reconnect the router
After the power outage, I had to reconnect the router to restore internet access.
They decided to reconnect after years of not speaking to each other.
Please reconnect the cables to ensure the device works properly.
After years apart, he managed to reconnect with his childhood friends.
The technician will reconnect the power once the repairs are finished.
They used social media to reconnect after losing touch in college.
After moving back to her hometown, she wanted to reconnect with old friends.
The technician was able to reconnect the internet after the outage.
They used social media to reconnect after many years apart.

Synonyms

  • relink
    4.1 0.03/m
    relink is used for technical or digital connections like web pages or files, while reconnect applies to people, devices, or relationships.
  • recouple
    5.6 0.00/m
    recouple often refers to joining parts or systems together again, while reconnect is broader and covers people, devices, or relationships.
  • rejoin
    3.5 3.94/m
    rejoin means to join a group or place again after leaving, while reconnect focuses on restoring a broken link or relationship.
  • reunite
    3.2 6.43/m
    reunite brings people or families back together emotionally after separation, while reconnect can mean simply restoring a connection or contact.
  • reestablish
    4.3 3.16/m
    reestablish means to restore something like a system, rule, or relationship after loss, while reconnect is specifically making a link again.
