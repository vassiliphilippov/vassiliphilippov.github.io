hyperacusia

noun
abnormal sensitivity to sound
5.2 9.7
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.ə.ˈkjuː.ziː.ə/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈkjuː.ziː.ə/ (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by extreme sensitivity and intolerance to ordinary levels of sound.

Examples

Severe hyperacusia symptoms

People with hyperacusia often find everyday noises unbearably loud or painful.
