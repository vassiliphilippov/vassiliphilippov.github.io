Issachar

proper noun
tribal ancestor in the Old Testament
Pronunciation: / ˈɪsəˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈɪsəkɑː / (BrE)
(in the Bible) a son of Jacob and Leah, regarded as the ancestor of one of the twelve tribes of Israel.

Examples

Issachar son of Jacob
According to the Old Testament, Issachar was one of Jacob’s twelve sons.
Biblical scholars often discuss the significance of Issachar and his descendants in ancient Israel.
The tribe of Issachar settled in the fertile valley of Jezreel.
