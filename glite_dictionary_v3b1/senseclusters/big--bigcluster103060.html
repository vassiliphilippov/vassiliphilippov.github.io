big

adjective
large in size or amount
Pronunciation: / bɪg / (AmE), / bɪg / (BrE)
of above average size, number, quantity, or extent; large in scale or degree

Examples

A big house
That company is one of the biggest employers in the region.
Their new house is much bigger than the old one.
We saw a big dog standing at the gate.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: huge - extremely large; much bigger than usual
  • synonym: large - of considerable or relatively great size, extent, or capacity
  • antonym: small - of a size that is less than normal or usual
  • degree lower: tiny - very small
