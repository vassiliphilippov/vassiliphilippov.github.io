demandingly

adverb
insistently
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmændɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːndɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that puts strong demands or high expectations on others

Examples

Spoke demandingly to team
She spoke demandingly to the team, expecting them to finish the project by the end of the day.
The teacher looked demandingly at the students, waiting for someone to answer the question.
He waved his hand demandingly to get the waiter's attention in the busy restaurant.
The coach spoke demandingly to the team, pushing them to do their best.
She looked at him demandingly, waiting for an immediate answer.
The teacher assigned homework demandingly, expecting high-quality work from every student.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gently - in a mild or soft manner
  • synonym: insistently - in a way that demands attention or action
  • antonym: leniently - in a way that is not strict or demanding
  • synonym: strictly - in a way that demands obedience or exactness
