engraining

verb
to fix deeply
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
to firmly fix an idea, habit, or quality in someone's mind or character

Examples

To engraining good habits
Parents play a key role in engraining values and manners in their children.
The coach believes in engraining discipline through daily routines and practice.
Repeated exposure is essential for engraining new vocabulary in language learners.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember or lose an idea from memory
  • synonym: implant - to fix an idea or feeling firmly in someone's mind
  • synonym: instill - to gradually establish an idea or attitude in someone's mind
