liquidness

noun
the state of being liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being liquid; the property of a substance that allows it to flow easily

Examples

The liquidness of water
Scientists studied the liquidness of different substances under varying temperatures.
She marveled at the liquidness of the metal when it was heated to high temperatures.
The liquidness of water allows it to adapt to the shape of its container.
Engineers examined the liquidness of the metal to ensure proper casting.
On a warm day, the liquidness of the wax made it easy to mold.
The chef prefers honey for its smooth liquidness in desserts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluidity - the ability of a substance to flow easily
  • synonym: liquidity - the quality of being liquid, especially in finance or substances
