dispatch

verb
kill quickly or efficiently
6.0 6.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ / (BrE)
to kill a person or animal quickly and deliberately

Examples

To dispatch an enemy
He effortlessly dispatched his opponents in the final duel.
The assassin was ordered to dispatch the enemy leader without hesitation.
The general gave the command to dispatch the prisoners before escaping.
He coolly dispatched his rival and walked away without a second thought.
The assassin was paid to dispatch the witness before the trial began.
The wounded animal was dispatched quickly to end its suffering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: execute - to kill someone as a punishment, especially legally
  • synonym: murder - to kill someone unlawfully and with intent
  • synonym: slay - to kill in a violent way
  • antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or harming someone
