suckle

verb
feed by sucking milk
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.əl / (BrE)
to feed (or be fed) with milk from the breast or udder

Examples

To suckle a baby
She sat quietly as her baby suckled at her breast.
The kittens continued to suckle until they were several weeks old.
The newborn calf began to suckle minutes after birth.
In some traditions, wet nurses would suckle the babies of noble families.
She chose to suckle her twins rather than use formula.
The ewe gently suckled her newborn lambs in the barn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nurse - to feed a baby at the breast
  • antonym: wean - to accustom a baby to food other than milk
