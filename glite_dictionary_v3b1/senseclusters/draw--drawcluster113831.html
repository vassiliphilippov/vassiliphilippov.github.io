Examples

To draw attention
His words drew attention to the issue at the meeting.
Lower prices drew more customers to the store this month.
The powerful magnet drew the nails toward it instantly.
Her enthusiasm and charisma always draw attention wherever she goes.
The exhibition is expected to draw thousands of visitors this year.
The new restaurant draws a lot of customers with its unique menu.
Her bold statement drew immediate applause from the audience.
His joke drew laughter from everyone in the room.
The film drew criticism for its portrayal of historical events.

Synonyms

  • attract
    1.9 59/m

  • pull in
    3.5 0.35/m
    pull in brings people, money, or attention into a place or event by strong appeal or effort, while draw simply means to attract.
  • pull
    2.0 37/m
    pull often means to move or attract by force, influence, or power, while draw focuses on attracting attention or reactions.
