aposiopesis

noun
sudden breaking off of speech
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəsiəˈpisɪs / (AmE), / ˌapəsiəˈpiːsɪs / (BrE)
the rhetorical device of breaking off suddenly and leaving a statement unfinished, often to convey emotion or suggest that the speaker is unable or unwilling to continue

Examples

Sudden aposiopesis in speech
During the heated debate, her sudden aposiopesis left the audience wondering what she truly intended to say.
In literature, authors often use aposiopesis to portray a character's overwhelming emotion or reluctance to speak.
When the teacher confronted him, his response was a classic case of aposiopesis: 'I just thought that maybe if... well, never mind.'
