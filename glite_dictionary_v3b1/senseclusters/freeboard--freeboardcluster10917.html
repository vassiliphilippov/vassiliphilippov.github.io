freeboard

noun
safety margin above water
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfriːbɔːd / (BrE)
the vertical distance from the water level to the top edge of a ship, boat, dam, or similar structure, providing a safety margin above the water

Examples

The freeboard height
The engineers increased the freeboard of the dam to improve flood protection.
A ship with a higher freeboard is less likely to take on water in rough seas.
Regulations require a minimum freeboard for all new seawall constructions.
The engineer checked the freeboard to ensure the ship was not overloaded.
A higher freeboard helps protect the deck from waves in rough seas.
Before launching, they measured the boat's freeboard for safety compliance.
