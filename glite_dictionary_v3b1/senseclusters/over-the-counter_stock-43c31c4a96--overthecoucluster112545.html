over-the-counter stock

noun
stock traded outside formal exchanges
6.6 5.3
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ðə.ˈkaʊn.tər stɑːk/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər ðə ˈkaʊn.tər stɑːk/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ðə.ˈkaʊn.tə stɒk/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və ðə ˈkaʊn.tə stɒk/ (BrE)
A stock that is not listed or traded on an organized stock exchange, but is instead bought and sold through a decentralized dealer network.

Examples

Over-the-counter stock trading
Investing in over-the-counter stock can carry greater risks due to less regulatory oversight.
Many small companies raise capital by issuing over-the-counter stock rather than listing on a major exchange.
She bought shares in an over-the-counter stock to diversify her portfolio.
