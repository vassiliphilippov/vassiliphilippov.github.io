gradualism

noun
slow steady change
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrædʒuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or process that change happens slowly and steadily over time rather than suddenly

Examples

Political gradualism
Many political reforms are achieved through gradualism rather than through revolution.
The scientist argued that gradualism best explains the slow changes seen in the fossil record.
Supporters of gradualism believe that steady progress is more effective than sudden shifts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolution - the gradual development of something over time
  • antonym: sudden change - a rapid or abrupt transformation
