slickness

noun
clever or deceptive speech or behavior
5.8 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪknɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being clever or deceptive, especially in speech, intended to take advantage of someone.

Examples

His slickness in deals
She admired his slickness in negotiation, but didn't fully trust him.
The politician's slickness was evident in the way he dodged every tough question.
The salesman's slickness made it hard to tell if he was telling the truth.
