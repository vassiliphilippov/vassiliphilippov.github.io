paradoxical

adjective
seeming contradiction or impossibility
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.əˈdɑk.sɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpær.əˈdɒk.sɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Seeming impossible or self-contradictory, but possibly true or valid.

Examples

A paradoxical idea
Her paradoxical attitude both confused and intrigued her colleagues.
It may seem paradoxical, but sometimes silence communicates more than words.
The medicine has a paradoxical effect: it makes some patients feel more awake rather than sleepy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contradictory - mutually opposed or inconsistent
  • antonym: logical - characterized by clear, sound reasoning
