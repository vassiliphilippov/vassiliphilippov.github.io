nonterminal

noun
grammar symbol
9.5
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈtɜrmɪnəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈtɜːmɪnəl / (BrE)
a symbol in a formal grammar that can be replaced by other symbols or sequences according to production rules

Examples

Nonterminal symbol
In this context-free grammar, every nonterminal must eventually be replaced by terminal symbols.
The parser identifies each nonterminal and applies the appropriate production rules.
A nonterminal can generate a sequence of terminals and other nonterminals in the derivation process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: production - a rule in a grammar that defines how symbols can be replaced
  • antonym: terminal - a symbol in a grammar that cannot be replaced further
