amnionic fluid

noun
fluid surrounding a fetus in the amnion
5.2 5.8
Pronunciation: / æmˈniː.ɒn.ɪk ˈfluː.ɪd / (AmE), / æmˈniː.ɒn.ɪk ˈfluː.ɪd / (BrE)
the clear, watery fluid that surrounds and protects an embryo or fetus inside the amnion during pregnancy

Examples

The amnionic fluid leak
A sudden leak of amnionic fluid may indicate that a woman's water has broken.
Doctors can perform tests on amnionic fluid to check for genetic disorders in the fetus.
During pregnancy, the level of amnionic fluid is closely monitored to ensure the baby's health.
