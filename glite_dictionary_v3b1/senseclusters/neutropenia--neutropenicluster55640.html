neutropenia

noun
low neutrophil count condition
8.6
Pronunciation: / nuː.troʊˈpiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / njuː.trəʊˈpiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally low number of neutrophils, a type of white blood cell crucial for fighting infection.

Examples

Severe neutropenia condition
Chemotherapy may cause neutropenia, making patients more susceptible to infections.
Doctors monitor neutropenia closely in patients with compromised immune systems.
Her neutropenia was discovered during routine blood tests after treatment.
