resumption

noun
starting something again
5.6 1.06/m

The act of starting something again after it has been paused or interrupted.

Examples

The resumption of work
After a brief interruption, the resumption of the meeting was announced by the chairperson.
Following the holiday, there was a resumption of normal business activities.
The peace talks are expected to see a resumption next week after months of delay.

Synonyms

  • recommencement
    8.0 0.00/m
    recommencement is the formal act of beginning again after a stop, while resumption usually means continuing something after a pause.
  • reinitiation
    7.0 0.01/m
    reinitiation is starting a process again after it stopped or finished, while resumption implies picking up where something left off.
  • reactivation
    4.2 0.09/m
    reactivation is making something active again after a period of inactivity, while resumption often means continuing an interrupted action.
  • reinstitution
    6.8 0.04/m
    reinstitution is bringing a law or practice back into use after it ended, while resumption means simply starting again after a pause.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restart - an act of starting something again
  • antonym: suspension - temporary stopping or pausing of something
