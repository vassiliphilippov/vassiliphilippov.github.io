mizzen

noun
rear or third mast of a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪzən / (AmE), / ˈmɪzən / (BrE)
the aft (rearmost) or third mast from the bow in a ship with three or more masts, or the smaller after mast on a yawl or ketch.

Examples

Tall mizzen mast
He adjusted the sails on the mizzen to catch more wind from astern.
The ketch's mizzen mast stood just ahead of the rudder post.
The old sailing ship had a mizzen that was shorter than the mainmast.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: foremast - the mast nearest the bow of a ship
  • degree higher: mainmast - the principal mast of a sailing vessel
