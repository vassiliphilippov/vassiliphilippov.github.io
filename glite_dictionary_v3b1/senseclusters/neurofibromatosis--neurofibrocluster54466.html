neurofibromatosis

noun
genetic disorder with nerve tumors and skin spots
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊrəʊˌfaɪbrəˌmoʊˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˌfaɪbrəˌməʊˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A genetic disorder characterized by the formation of numerous neurofibromas (benign tumors) on nerve tissue, often accompanied by skin spots and developmental abnormalities.

Examples

Neurofibromatosis genetic disorder
People with neurofibromatosis may develop benign tumors along their nerves.
Physicians closely monitor children with neurofibromatosis for any changes in their skin or vision.
The diagnosis of neurofibromatosis was confirmed after genetic testing and clinical examination.
