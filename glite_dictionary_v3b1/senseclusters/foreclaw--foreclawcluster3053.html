foreclaw

noun
front limb claw
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌklɔ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌklɔː / (BrE)
a claw located on the front foot or limb of an animal

Examples

The foreclaw of eagle
The eagle gripped its prey tightly with its powerful foreclaw.
A cat may extend its foreclaw when stretching or climbing.
Scientists studied the shape of the dinosaur's foreclaw to learn about its behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claw - a sharp, curved nail on the foot of an animal
  • antonym: hindclaw - a claw on the back foot or limb of an animal
