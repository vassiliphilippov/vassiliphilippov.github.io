bring down

verb
cause the defeat or removal (especially of a ruler)
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to cause the defeat or removal of someone in a position of power, especially a government or ruler

Examples

To bring down a government
It was the people’s movement that eventually brought down the dictator.
Opposition parties are determined to bring down the corrupt government.
Several scandals threatened to bring down the entire administration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depose - to remove from office suddenly and forcefully
  • synonym: overthrow - to remove forcibly from power
  • synonym: topple - to cause to fall, especially a regime or government
