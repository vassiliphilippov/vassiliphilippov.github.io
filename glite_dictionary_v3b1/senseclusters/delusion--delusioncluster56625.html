delusion

noun
a false or mistaken belief
4.6 5.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈluː.ʒən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈljuː.ʒən/ /; / /dɪ.ˈljuːz.jən/ (BrE)
a false belief or impression that is strongly held even when it is not true

Examples

A delusion of success
He lives under the delusion that he will win the lottery someday.
Her belief that everyone was against her was just a delusion.
It would be a delusion to think that this problem will solve itself.
After months of delusion, she finally accepted the truth.
He lived under the delusion that everyone liked him.
The idea that he could finish the project in one day was a delusion.
He continues to believe, under a delusion, that he will win the lottery despite all evidence.
Living in a delusion can prevent someone from recognizing and addressing real problems.
The patient's delusion that she could fly posed a serious risk to her safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hallucination - an experience involving the apparent perception of something not present
  • synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief, often involving perception
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
