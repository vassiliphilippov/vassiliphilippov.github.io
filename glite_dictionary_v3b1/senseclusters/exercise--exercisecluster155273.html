exercise

verb
engage in or give physical/mental activity
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksərˌsaɪz /; / ˈɛksɚˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛksəsaɪz /; / ˈɛksəˌsaɪz / (BrE)
to do physical activities that improve health and fitness, such as running or swimming

Examples

To exercise regularly
Doctors recommend that you exercise your muscles regularly for better health.
Parents often exercise their infants to help them develop coordination.
This activity will exercise your mind and improve your memory.
After years of inactivity, he decided to exercise every morning before work.
Doctors recommend that adults exercise regularly to maintain good health.
She prefers to exercise outdoors rather than in a gym.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rest - to cease from action or movement
  • synonym: train - to develop skills or fitness by practice or instruction
  • synonym: work out - to do physical exercises, especially in a gym
