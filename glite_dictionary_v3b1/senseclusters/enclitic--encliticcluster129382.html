enclitic

adjective
dependent on previous word
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈklɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈklɪtɪk / (BrE)
describing a word or syllable that is closely connected to the previous word and cannot stand alone in pronunciation

Examples

Enclitic particle
In some languages, an enclitic particle is always attached to the end of the previous word.
The pronoun in this sentence acts as an enclitic, blending with the word before it.
Linguists study how enclitic forms affect the rhythm and flow of speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proclitic - a word or syllable that attaches to the following word
