electrocautery

noun
destruction of tissue by electrically heated needle
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɔtəri / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈkɔːtəri / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which tissue is destroyed or removed using a heated needle powered by an electric current

Examples

Electrocautery medical procedure
After the bleeding started, the doctor relied on electrocautery to stop it quickly and safely.
The surgeon used electrocautery to remove the small tumor during the operation.
Warts can often be treated effectively with electrocautery in a dermatologist's office.
