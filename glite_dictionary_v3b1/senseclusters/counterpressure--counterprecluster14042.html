counterpressure

noun
manual pain relief
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌprɛʃə / (BrE)
manual pressure applied to a part of the body, often during labor, to relieve pain or discomfort

Examples

Counterpressure during labor
The nurse used counterpressure on the patient's shoulder to help reduce discomfort.
Applying gentle counterpressure during contractions can make labor more manageable.
Some people find that counterpressure on the lower back eases muscle pain after exercise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: massage - the act of rubbing and pressing someone's body to relieve pain
  • synonym: pressure - the act of applying force to something
