Queen's crape myrtle

noun
a tropical tree with showy flowers, used ornamentally and for timber
8.8
Pronunciation: /kwiːnz kreɪp ˈmɜːr.təl/ (AmE), /kwiːnz kreɪp ˈmɜː.təl/ (BrE)
A tropical tree native to Asia, Australia, and the East Indies, known for its large, showy flowers and used as an ornamental plant; its timber is called pyinma.

Examples

The Queen's crape myrtle tree
Botanists admire the Queen's crape myrtle for both its ornamental flowers and its adaptability to warm climates.
In Australia, the timber of the Queen's crape myrtle is known as pyinma and is valued for furniture-making.
The Queen's crape myrtle is prized for its stunning purple blooms in summer gardens.
