rumination

noun
regurgitation of food (medical)
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˌruːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌruːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which small amounts of food are regurgitated, commonly seen in some infants after feeding.

Examples

Infant rumination
Rumination is often mistaken for simple spit-up, but it is a more persistent condition.
After feeding, the infant exhibited signs of rumination, prompting concern from the caregiver.
The pediatrician diagnosed the baby's frequent regurgitation as rumination syndrome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regurgitation - the act of bringing swallowed food up again into the mouth
