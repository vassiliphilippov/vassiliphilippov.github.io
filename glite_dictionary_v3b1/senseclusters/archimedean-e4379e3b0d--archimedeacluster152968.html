Examples

Archimedean property
The set of real numbers is an Archimedean ordered field, meaning it contains no infinitesimals.
In contrast to p-adic numbers, the rational numbers are Archimedean.
Mathematicians often study Archimedean and non-Archimedean structures to understand different number systems.
