Archimedean

adjective
no infinitesimals
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.kɪ.ˈmiː.dɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ɑː.kɪ.ˈmiː.dɪ.ən/ (BrE)
having the property that for any two positive elements, some multiple of one exceeds the other; containing no infinitesimal elements

Examples

Archimedean property
The set of real numbers is an Archimedean ordered field, meaning it contains no infinitesimals.
In contrast to p-adic numbers, the rational numbers are Archimedean.
Mathematicians often study Archimedean and non-Archimedean structures to understand different number systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-Archimedean - having infinitesimal elements; not satisfying the Archimedean property
