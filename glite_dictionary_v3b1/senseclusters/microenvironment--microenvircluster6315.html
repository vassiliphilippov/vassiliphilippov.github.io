microenvironment

noun
immediate business context
7.2
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kroʊ.ɪn.ˈvaɪr.ən.mənt/ (AmE), /maɪ.krəʊ.ɪn.ˈvaɪ.rən.mənt/ (BrE)
the immediate surroundings and factors, such as customers, suppliers, and competitors, that directly influence an organization or system

Examples

Company microenvironment
A company's microenvironment includes its customers, suppliers, and direct competitors.
Understanding the microenvironment helps businesses respond quickly to changes in their immediate market.
The success of a new product often depends on the microenvironment surrounding its launch.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: environment - the overall conditions and influences affecting something
  • degree higher: macroenvironment - the broader external factors that affect an organization, such as economic or political conditions
