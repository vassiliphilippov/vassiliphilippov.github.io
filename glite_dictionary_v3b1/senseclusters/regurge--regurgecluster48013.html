regurge

verb
to flow back
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡɜrdʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to flow or surge back in a backward movement, especially of a liquid such as water or blood

Examples

Water regurges back
During the storm, seawater would regurge into the coastal streets, causing flooding.
If the valve is weak, blood may regurge into the heart chamber.
The old pipes sometimes regurge dirty water after heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflux - to flow back, especially of liquid in a body or system
  • antonym: surge - to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward
