vena subclavia

noun
large vein joining jugular vein; drains upper limb
7.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àåve…™n…ô s…ôbÀàkle…™vi…ô / (AmE), / ÀåveÀên…ô s…ôbÀàkle…™v…™…ô / (BrE)
the large vein that is a continuation of the axillary vein and joins with the internal jugular vein to form the brachiocephalic vein, responsible for draining blood from the upper limb back to the heart

Examples

The vena subclavia vein
A blood clot in the vena subclavia can impede blood flow from the arm.
The right and left venae subclaviae each receive blood from the corresponding upper limb.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the vena subclavia during the procedure.
