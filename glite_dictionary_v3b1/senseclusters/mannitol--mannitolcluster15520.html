mannitol

noun
a sugar alcohol and medical diuretic
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪˌtɔl / (AmE), / ˈmænɪtɒl / (BrE)
a sugar alcohol used as a diuretic to promote urine excretion, and in medicine as an osmotic agent

Examples

Mannitol diuretic drug
Mannitol is often administered intravenously to reduce intracranial pressure after head injury.
Due to its strong osmotic properties, mannitol is used to promote the excretion of urine.
The doctor prescribed mannitol as part of the patient's treatment for acute kidney failure.
