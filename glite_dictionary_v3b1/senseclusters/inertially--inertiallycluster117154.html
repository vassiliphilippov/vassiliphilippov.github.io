inertially

adverb
by inertia
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɜrʃəli / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to inertia or to an inertial frame of reference (especially in physics)

Examples

Move inertially downhill
After the engine stopped, the car continued to roll inertially down the hill.
The satellite moved inertially through space once it left Earth's atmosphere.
Particles in the experiment drifted inertially until acted upon by another force.
The experiment was conducted inertially to ensure accurate measurements of the object's motion.
When viewed inertially, the car appears to move at a constant speed along the highway.
The laws of physics are simplest when described inertially rather than in an accelerating frame.

Related Terms

  • antonym: noninertially - in a manner relating to a non-inertial (accelerating) frame of reference
  • synonym: passively - in a way that does not involve active response or resistance
