spur

verb
encourage or incite to action
7.3 3.3
Pronunciation: /spɜːr/ (AmE), /spɜː/ (BrE)
to encourage or motivate someone to take action or to make something happen

Examples

To spur action
His words of praise spurred her to complete the project ahead of schedule.
Public support spurred the volunteers to keep working through the night.
The coach's speech spurred the team on to victory in the final minutes.
Her words managed to spur him into finally finishing his novel.
The coach tried to spur the team on with a passionate speech.
The new tax policy is expected to spur economic growth in the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • synonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
