support

verb
bear the weight of; keep upright
1.0 31/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàp…îrt / (AmE), / s…ôÀàp…îÀêt / (BrE)
to bear the weight of something or keep it upright and prevent it from falling

Examples

To support a building
Bookshelves must be strong enough to support the weight of all those books.
He used a cane to support his injured leg while walking.
The pillars support the entire structure of the bridge.

Synonyms

  • sustain
    4.1 3.09/m
    sustain is to bear weight and keep things from collapsing, while support is the general act of holding something up.
  • hold up
    3.4 8.23/m
    hold up means to carry or prop something so it does not fall, while support means to bear weight generally.
  • prop
    4.1 4.62/m
    prop is to support something by putting an object under or against it, while support is a broader way to keep things upright.
  • hold
    1.0 184/m
    hold is to grasp or carry something in your hands or arms, while support covers bearing weight or keeping things upright.
  • brace
    4.4 9.54/m
    brace is to steady or strengthen something against movement or pressure, while support focuses on bearing weight to prevent falling.
  • buttress
    7.3 0.20/m
    buttress is an external support built against a wall to strengthen it, while support is any way of holding something up.
