upon

preposition
formal preposition
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑn / (AmE), / əˈpɒn / (BrE)
on or immediately after something; used mainly in formal or literary contexts as a preposition.

Examples

Upon the table
He placed the book upon the table before leaving the room.
Upon hearing the news, she immediately called her family.
The agreement will take effect upon signature by both parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: on - physically in contact with and supported by a surface
