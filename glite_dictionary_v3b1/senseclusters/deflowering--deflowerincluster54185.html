deflowering

noun
loss of freshness
8.8
Pronunciation: / diˈflaʊərɪŋ / (AmE), / diˈflaʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of causing something to lose its freshness, beauty, or prime, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

Deflowering the landscape
The rapid urban development led to the deflowering of the once-pristine countryside.
Many critics lamented the deflowering of the city's historic charm after the renovations.
The scandal resulted in the deflowering of the politician's previously untarnished reputation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state
  • synonym: ruin - the act of destroying or spoiling something
