Lao-tzu

proper noun
Chinese founder of Taoism
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààla äÀådzuÀê / (AmE), / Ààla äÀådzuÀê / (BrE)
(c. 6th century BC) A Chinese philosopher considered the founder of Taoism and traditionally held to be the author of the Tao Te Ching.

Examples

Lao-tzu the philosopher
Lao-tzu's Tao Te Ching remains a foundational text in Chinese philosophy.
Many scholars debate whether Lao-tzu was a historical or legendary figure.
The teachings of Lao-tzu emphasize living in harmony with the Tao.

Synonyms

  • Laozi
    8.8 0.01/m
    Laozi is the modern spelling used by scholars and signs, while Lao-tzu is an older form found in older books.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Confucius - Chinese philosopher and contemporary of Lao-tzu
  • synonym: Taoism - Philosophical and religious tradition founded by Lao-tzu
