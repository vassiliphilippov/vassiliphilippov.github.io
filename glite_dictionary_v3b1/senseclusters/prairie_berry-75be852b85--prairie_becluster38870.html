prairie berry

noun
nightshade plant; berry used to curdle milk
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆprÉ›ri ËˆbÉ›ri / (AmE), / ËˆprÉ›É™ri ËˆbÉ›ri / (BrE)
A weedy nightshade plant with silvery foliage, violet, blue, or white flowers, and a roundish berry, found from the central United States to South America; the berry is sometimes used to curdle milk.

Examples

The prairie berry plant
Her research focused on the medicinal properties of the prairie berry found in the Midwest.
The settlers used the juice of the prairie berry to curdle milk for cheese making.
Wild prairie berry bushes grew along the edge of the pasture, attracting birds each spring.
