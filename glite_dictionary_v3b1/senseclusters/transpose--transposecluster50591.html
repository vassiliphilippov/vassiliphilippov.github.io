transpose

noun
matrix with exchanged rows and columns
8.6 8.1
Pronunciation: / trænˈspoʊz / (AmE), / trænˈspəʊz / (BrE)
A matrix formed by interchanging the rows and columns of a given matrix.

Examples

Matrix transpose
Calculating the transpose is a fundamental operation in linear algebra.
The transpose of matrix A is often denoted as A with a superscript T.
When you take the transpose of an m by n matrix, it becomes an n by m matrix.
