Paleocene epoch

noun
geological epoch (66–56 million years ago)
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪ.li.oʊˌsiːn ˈɛp.ək / (AmE), / ˈpæ.li.əʊˌsiːn ˈiː.pɒk / (BrE)
The geological epoch lasting from about 66 to 56 million years ago, marking the first part of the Paleogene Period and characterized by the development of early mammals and birds.

Examples

The Paleocene epoch period
During the Paleocene epoch, Earth's climate was much warmer than today.
Fossils from the Paleocene epoch have been found in several continents, revealing early forms of birds and mammals.
The Paleocene epoch saw the diversification of mammals after the extinction of the dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Eocene epoch - the geological epoch following the Paleocene, about 56–34 million years ago
