rock

verb
to shock or disturb
4.4
Pronunciation: / rɑk / (AmE), / rɒk / (BrE)
to cause a sudden strong emotional effect, disturbance, or shock to someone or something

Examples

To rock the world
The scandal rocked the entire community and left many people in disbelief.
News of the sudden merger rocked the financial markets for days.
Her unexpected announcement rocked her friends and family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shake - to disturb or unsettle someone or something
  • antonym: stabilize - to make something steady or less likely to change
