Nunavut

proper noun
territory in northern Canada
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːnəˌvuːt / (AmE), / ˈnʌnəˌvuːt / (BrE)
a territory in northern Canada, established in 1999, mainly inhabited and governed by the Inuit people

Examples

Nunavut territory
After 1999, Nunavut became the newest and largest territory of Canada.
The majority of Nunavut's population identifies as Inuit.
Tourists are drawn to Nunavut for its Arctic landscapes and unique culture.
