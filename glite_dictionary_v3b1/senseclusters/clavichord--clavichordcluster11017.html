clavichord

noun
early delicate keyboard instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈklæv.ɪˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈklæv.ɪ.kɔːd / (BrE)
an early stringed keyboard instrument, similar to a piano but with a softer, more delicate sound, popular from the 15th to 18th centuries

Examples

A clavichord instrument
During the Baroque era, composers often wrote pieces specifically for the clavichord.
She restored a vintage clavichord and displayed it in her music studio.
The musician played the clavichord with such delicacy that the soft melodies filled the small room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harpsichord - a popular early keyboard instrument whose strings are plucked, not struck
  • degree higher: piano - a modern keyboard instrument with struck strings producing a wide dynamic range
