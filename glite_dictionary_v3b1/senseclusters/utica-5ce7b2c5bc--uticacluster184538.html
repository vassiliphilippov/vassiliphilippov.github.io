Utica

noun
shale formation (natural gas source)
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈjuː.tɪ.kə/ /; / /juː.ˈtiː.kə/ (AmE), /ˈjuː.tɪ.kə/ /; / /juː.ˈtiː.kə/ (BrE)
(geology, energy) a thick, organic-rich shale formation in the Appalachian Basin (the Utica Shale), a major source of natural gas and oil.

Examples

Utica shale formation
Energy analysts point to the Utica shale play as a major source of natural gas in the region.
Several companies began drilling in the Utica after new seismic data showed promising hydrocarbon potential.
Local towns experienced economic growth when investment in the Utica increased drilling and pipeline development.
