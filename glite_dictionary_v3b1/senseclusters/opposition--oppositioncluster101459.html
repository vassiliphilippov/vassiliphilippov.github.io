opposition

noun
group or body opposing something
1.0 2.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A group of people or an organization that acts together to oppose or resist something, especially within politics or debate.

Examples

A strong opposition
A vocal opposition emerged to challenge the controversial building project.
Members of the opposition called for a vote of no confidence in the government.
The new tax proposals faced strong opposition from environmental groups.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resistance - organized effort to oppose or withstand something
  • antonym: support - group or action giving active help or approval
