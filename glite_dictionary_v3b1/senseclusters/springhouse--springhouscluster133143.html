springhouse

noun
building for cooling food
Pronunciation: / ˈsprɪŋˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a small building constructed over a spring, traditionally used to keep food cool before refrigeration

Examples

A springhouse building
The family used the springhouse to store milk and butter during the hot summer months.
Old farmsteads often have a springhouse near a stream or natural spring.
Before refrigerators, people relied on a springhouse to keep their food fresh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: root cellar - an underground storage space for keeping food cool
