atomize

verb
break up into tiny particles
Pronunciation: / ˈæt.əˌmaɪz /; / ˈæ.təˌmaɪz /; / ˈæ.tə.maɪz / (AmE), / ˈæt.ə.maɪz /; / ˈat.ə.maɪz / (BrE)
to break up or reduce something into very small particles or droplets, often by means of a spray or force

Examples

To atomize debris
After the explosion, the debris was almost completely atomized.
Many perfumes are atomized into a fine mist when sprayed from the bottle.
The scientist used a device to atomize the liquid for the experiment.
Modern spray bottles atomise liquids to create a fine mist.
The perfume was atomised into the air, filling the room with a pleasant scent.
When fuel is atomised before combustion, it burns more efficiently.
Modern inhalers work by atomising the medicine so it can be easily breathed in.
She atomised her favourite perfume onto her wrists before leaving the house.
The device is designed to atomise water into a fine mist for humidifying the air.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coagulate - to change from a fluid into a thickened mass
  • antonym: condense - to make something denser or more concentrated (opposite process)
  • synonym: disperse - to scatter or spread over a wide area
  • synonym: pulverize - to crush into a powder or dust
