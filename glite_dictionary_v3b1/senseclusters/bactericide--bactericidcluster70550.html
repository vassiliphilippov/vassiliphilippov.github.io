bactericide

noun
substance that kills bacteria
Pronunciation: / bækˈtɛrəˌsaɪd /; / bækˈtɪr.əˌsaɪd / (AmE), / bækˈtɪərəˌsaɪd /; / bækˈtɪə.rɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
a substance or chemical that destroys bacteria or stops them from growing

Examples

A bactericide chemical
Scientists are developing a new bactericide to combat antibiotic-resistant strains.
The use of a strong bactericide can help prevent the spread of infection in hospitals.
This spray acts as a bactericide, killing harmful germs on kitchen surfaces.
A hospital's cleaning protocol requires a strong bactericide to eliminate harmful germs on surfaces.
The laboratory tested the effectiveness of the new bactericide against several strains of E. coli.
Use a bactericide in the solution to prevent the spread of bacterial infections in the greenhouse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms
  • synonym: antiseptic - a substance that prevents the growth of microorganisms
  • antonym: bacteriostat - an agent that inhibits the growth of bacteria rather than killing them
  • synonym: disinfectant - a chemical liquid that destroys bacteria
