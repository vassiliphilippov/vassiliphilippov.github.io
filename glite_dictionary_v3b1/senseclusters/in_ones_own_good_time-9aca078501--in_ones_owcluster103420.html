in (one's) own good time

adverb
at one's own pace; when one is ready
2.4
Pronunciation: / ɪn oʊn ɡʊd taɪm / (AmE), / ɪn əʊn ɡʊd taɪm / (BrE)
at a time and pace chosen by oneself, without hurrying or being forced by others

Examples

Answer in own good time
He'll answer your question in his own good time, so there's no need to rush him.
She likes to complete her assignments in her own good time, preferring not to be hurried by others.
The children got dressed in their own good time, ignoring their mother's calls to hurry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: at one's leisure - at a time convenient for oneself; without haste
  • synonym: in due course - at the appropriate or proper time
