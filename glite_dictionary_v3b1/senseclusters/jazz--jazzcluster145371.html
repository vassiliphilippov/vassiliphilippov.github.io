jazz

noun
20th-century dance music with improvisation
Pronunciation: / dʒæz / (AmE), / dʒaz /; / dʒæz / (BrE)
a style of music that began in the early 20th century, known for improvisation, lively rhythms, and strong swing

Examples

Classic jazz music
Many musicians were inspired by the energetic rhythms of classic jazz.
She enjoys listening to jazz from the 1920s and 30s.
The band played lively jazz tunes that filled the dance floor.
Many musicians admire the creativity and complexity of jazz.
She's been a jazz singer for over twenty years.
The festival features live jazz performances every evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blues - a music genre that influenced jazz, known for its melancholy sound
  • antonym: classical - a contrasting music genre with structured forms and traditions
  • degree lower: ragtime - a style of early jazz music characterized by syncopated rhythms
  • degree higher: swing - a style of jazz music with a lively rhythm popular in the 1930s and 40s
