biogeny

noun
production of life from existing life
Pronunciation: / baɪˈɑːdʒəni / (AmE), / baɪˈɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the production of living organisms from other living organisms

Examples

Principle of biogeny
According to the principle of biogeny, all living forms arise from pre-existing life.
Recent research provides further evidence supporting biogeny over abiogenesis in explaining cellular reproduction.
The concept of biogeny refutes the spontaneous generation theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abiogenesis - the original evolution of life from non-living matter
