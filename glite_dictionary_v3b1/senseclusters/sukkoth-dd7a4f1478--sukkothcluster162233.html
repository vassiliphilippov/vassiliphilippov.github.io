Sukkoth

noun
a major Jewish festival commemorating the Israelites' shelter in the wilderness
Pronunciation: / suˈkoʊθ / (AmE), / ˈsʊkɒθ / (BrE)
A major Jewish festival starting on the 15th of Tishri, commemorating the shelter of the Israelites during their 40-year journey in the wilderness.

Examples

Sukkoth festival
During Sukkoth, many Jewish families build temporary shelters called sukkahs in their gardens.
Many people invite friends to share meals in the sukkah during Sukkoth.
The community organized a festive meal to celebrate the start of Sukkoth this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Sukkot - alternative transliteration of Sukkoth, same festival
