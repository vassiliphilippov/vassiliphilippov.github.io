gomuti

noun
Malaysian palm tree; source of sago and sap
Pronunciation: / goʊˈmuːti / (AmE), / gəˈmuːti / (BrE)
A Malaysian feather palm whose base is covered with dense fibers, yielding a sweet sap used for making wine and trunk pith used for sago.

Examples

The gomuti palm
Farmers collected the sweet sap from the gomuti to ferment into palm wine.
The fibers of the gomuti are traditionally used to make ropes and brushes.
The sago made from the trunk pith of the gomuti is an important food source in some regions.
