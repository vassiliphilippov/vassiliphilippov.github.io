past participle

noun
verb form expressing completed action
Pronunciation: / pæst pɑːrˈtɪsɪpəl / (AmE), / pɑːst pɑːˈtɪsɪpl̩ / (BrE)
a verb form, typically ending in -ed, -d, -t, -en, or -n, that expresses a completed action or state, used in forming perfect tenses and the passive voice

Examples

The past participle form
Many irregular verbs have unique past participle forms.
The past participle of 'see' is 'seen'.
Use the past participle with 'have' to form the present perfect tense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: present participle - a verb form ending in -ing, expressing ongoing action
