optical fusion

noun
combining images from both eyes
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl ˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process by which images from both eyes combine to form a single visual perception

Examples

Optical fusion process
Optical fusion is essential for depth perception because it allows the brain to merge images from both eyes.
In cases of strabismus, optical fusion may be disrupted and cause double vision.
Scientists study optical fusion to better understand conditions that affect how we perceive three-dimensional space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binocular vision - vision using both eyes, which allows for depth perception
