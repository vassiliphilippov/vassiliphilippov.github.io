lyrebird

noun
Australian bird with lyre-shaped tail feathers
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪɚˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪəˌbɜːd / (BrE)
An Australian bird known for its strikingly long, lyre-shaped tail feathers and its ability to mimic natural and artificial sounds, especially displayed by the male during courtship.

Examples

The lyrebird tail
A lyrebird can imitate the calls of dozens of other bird species with remarkable accuracy.
The male lyrebird is famous for displaying his beautiful tail feathers during mating rituals.
We were amazed to hear the lyrebird mimic the sounds of camera shutters and car alarms in the forest.
