lyrebird

noun
Australian bird with lyre-shaped tail feathers
6.8 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪɚˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪəˌbɜːd / (BrE)
An Australian bird known for its strikingly long, lyre-shaped tail feathers and its ability to mimic natural and artificial sounds, especially displayed by the male during courtship.
