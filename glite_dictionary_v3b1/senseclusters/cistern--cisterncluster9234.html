cistern

noun
body cavity containing fluid
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪs.tərn/ (AmE), /ˈsɪs.tən/ (BrE)
a sac or cavity in the body, especially one containing lymph or cerebrospinal fluid

Examples

Brain cistern
During the operation, they drained fluid from the subarachnoid cistern.
In brain anatomy, a cistern is a space where cerebrospinal fluid accumulates.
The doctor explained that the enlarged cistern was pressing on the patient’s nerves.
