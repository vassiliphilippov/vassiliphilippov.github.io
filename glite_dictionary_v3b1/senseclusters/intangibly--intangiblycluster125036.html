intangibly

adverb
in an undefinable way
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtændʒəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtændʒəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be touched, measured, or easily defined

Examples

Felt intangibly in influence
Her influence was intangibly felt throughout the organization, even though she rarely spoke in meetings.
The atmosphere in the room changed intangibly after the announcement was made.
Success in this field is often intangibly linked to creativity and intuition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immaterially - in a way that is not physical or material
  • antonym: tangibly - in a way that can be touched or clearly measured
