overpotential

noun
extra voltage in reactions
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərpəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəpəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
the extra voltage needed to make an electrochemical reaction occur, above the theoretical equilibrium potential

Examples

High overpotential
Scientists are working to reduce overpotential in fuel cells to make them more efficient.
A high overpotential can lead to energy loss in electrolysis processes.
The new electrode material showed a much lower overpotential during testing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: activation energy - the minimum energy required to start a chemical reaction
  • antonym: equilibrium potential - the voltage at which an electrochemical reaction is balanced
