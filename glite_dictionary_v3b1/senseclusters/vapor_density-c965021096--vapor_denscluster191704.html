vapor density

noun
relative density of a gas to hydrogen
4.1 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpɚ ˈdɛnsɪti /; / ˈveɪpər dɛnˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə ˈdɛnsɪti /; / ˈveɪpə dɛnˈsɪti / (BrE)
the density of a gas or vapor compared to the density of hydrogen under the same conditions

Examples

Vapor density of gas
Knowing the vapor density of a gas can help assess its hazards in the laboratory.
Scientists calculated the vapor density to determine the molecular weight of an unknown substance.
The vapor density of oxygen is approximately 16 compared to hydrogen.
Because hydrogen is the reference, vapour density is always a ratio with no units.
Scientists measured the vapour density of the unknown gas to identify its composition.
The vapour density of oxygen is used to determine its molecular weight in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: density - mass per unit volume of a substance
