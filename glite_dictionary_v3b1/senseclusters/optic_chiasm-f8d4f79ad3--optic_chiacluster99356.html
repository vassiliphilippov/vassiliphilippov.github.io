optic chiasm

noun
X-shaped crossing of optic nerves in the brain
9.3 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːp.tɪk ˈkaɪ.æz.əm/ (AmE), /ˈɒp.tɪk ˈkaɪ.æz.əm/ (BrE)
the X-shaped structure at the base of the brain where the optic nerves from each eye cross over each other

Examples

The optic chiasm structure
A tumor near the optic chiasm may affect both eyes' sight.
Damage to the optic chiasm can lead to loss of peripheral vision.
The optic chiasm is crucial for the transmission of visual information to the brain.
