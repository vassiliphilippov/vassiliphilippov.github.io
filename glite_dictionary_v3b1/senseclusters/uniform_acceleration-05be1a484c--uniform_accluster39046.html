uniform acceleration

noun
constant change in velocity over time
4.4 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈjuː.nə.fɔːrm æk.sə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈjuː.nɪ.fɔːrm æk.sel.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈjuː.nə.fɔːm æk.sə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈjuː.nɪ.fɔːm æk.sel.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a type of motion in which an object's velocity increases or decreases by equal amounts in equal time intervals

Examples

Uniform acceleration motion
In projectile motion with uniform acceleration, the velocity changes evenly each second.
Physics students often use formulas for uniform acceleration to calculate time and distance of falling objects.
The car picked up uniform acceleration as it rolled down the hill, gaining speed at a steady rate.
