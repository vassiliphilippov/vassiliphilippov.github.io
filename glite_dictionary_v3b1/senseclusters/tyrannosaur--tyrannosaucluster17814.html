tyrannosaur

noun
large predatory Cretaceous dinosaur
8.2
Pronunciation: / tɪˈrænəˌsɔr / (AmE), / tɪˈranəsɔː / (BrE)
A large carnivorous bipedal dinosaur with massive, serrated teeth, that lived in North America during the later Cretaceous period.

Examples

A tyrannosaur skeleton
Children were excited to see a reconstructed tyrannosaur skeleton at the museum.
Paleontologists believe that the tyrannosaur may have been both a predator and a scavenger.
The fossilized skull of a tyrannosaur was discovered in Montana.
