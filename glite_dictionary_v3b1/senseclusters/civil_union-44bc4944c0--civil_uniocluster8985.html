civil union

noun
legally recognized partnership, often for same-sex couples
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪvəl ˈjunjən / (AmE), / ˌsɪvəl ˈjuːniən / (BrE)
a legally recognized union similar to marriage, typically between two people of the same sex, granting similar legal rights and responsibilities

Examples

A civil union ceremony
After their civil union, they enjoyed many of the same rights as married couples.
They decided to enter into a civil union to gain legal recognition of their relationship.
Vermont was one of the first states to legalize civil union for same-sex couples.
