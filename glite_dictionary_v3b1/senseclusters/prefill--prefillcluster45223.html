prefill

verb
to fill in beforehand
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈfɪl /; / priːˈfɪl / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈfɪl /; / priːˈfɪl / (BrE)
to fill something in advance, often automatically (such as a form, document, or container)

Examples

To prefill a form
The online application will prefill your name and address using your account details.
If you allow cookies, some websites can prefill forms with your saved information.
The system failed to prefill the required fields, so I had to enter everything manually.
The company will prefill the bottles with water before shipping them to stores.
You can prefill the form online to save time at the appointment.
Volunteers helped prefill sandbags before the storm arrived.

  • antonym: empty - to remove all contents from something

  • synonym: fill - to make something full
