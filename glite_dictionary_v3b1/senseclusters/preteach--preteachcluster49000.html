preteach

noun
preparatory lesson
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːtiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpriːtiːtʃ / (BrE)
a short lesson or session given before the main lesson to introduce or prepare students with necessary knowledge or skills

Examples

A preteach session
The teacher held a preteach to introduce key concepts before the main science lesson.
A short preteach can help students feel more confident about new material.
Before starting the chapter, the class attended a preteach on important vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preview - an introduction or overview before the main event
  • antonym: review - a lesson or session to go over material already taught
