scram

verb
leave immediately (often as an imperative)
Pronunciation: / skɹæm / (AmE), / skɹam / (BrE)
(informal) To leave or go away quickly, often used as an order to tell someone to go away immediately.

Examples

To scram quickly
Kids, you'd better scram before my parents get home.
The guard shouted, "Scram!" when he saw the trespassers.
We need to scram if we want to catch the last train.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leave - to go away from a place
  • antonym: stay - to remain in a place
