hysteresis

noun
lagging of effect behind cause, esp. in magnetism
6.2 9.0
Pronunciation: /hɪ.ˈster.əs/ /; / /hɪ.ˈster.ɪs/ (AmE), /hɪ.ˈste.rəs/ /; / /hɪ.ˈste.rɪs/ (BrE)
The phenomenon in which the effect of a physical process lags behind its cause, especially when the magnetic properties of a material lag behind changes in the magnetic field.

Examples

Hysteresis effect
Engineers must account for hysteresis when designing precise electronic circuits.
Magnetic materials often exhibit hysteresis when subjected to alternating magnetic fields.
The hysteresis observed in the system results in a time delay between input and output.
