dictated but not read

adjective
prepared by dictation without review
3.3 6.9
Pronunciation: /dɪk.ˈteɪ.tɪd bʌt nɑːt red/ /; / /dɪk.ˈteɪ.tɪd bʌt nɑːt riːd/ (AmE), /dɪk.ˈteɪ.tɪd bʌt nɒt red/ /; / /dɪk.ˈteɪ.tɪd bʌt nɒt riːd/ (BrE)
(of a letter, speech, or document) prepared by dictation and sent or issued without being thoroughly reviewed by the author, often implying the work may contain errors or was handled with little care.

Examples

Dictated but not read letter
Many official letters in the archive are marked as dictated but not read by the original sender.
She signed the report, which was dictated but not read, and only noticed the mistakes after it was sent.
The memo was clearly dictated but not read, as it was filled with errors and inconsistencies.
