photorespiration

noun
plant oxygen uptake process
9.9
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.re.spə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.re.spə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a process in plants where oxygen is taken in and carbon dioxide is released in the presence of light, reducing photosynthesis efficiency

Examples

Photorespiration process in plants
Photorespiration can reduce the amount of sugar produced by some plants on hot days.
Scientists are studying ways to minimize photorespiration to improve crop yields.
During photorespiration, plants use oxygen and release carbon dioxide even in the light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: photosynthesis - the process by which plants use sunlight to make food from carbon dioxide and water
