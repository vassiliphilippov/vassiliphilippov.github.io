ensnare

verb
to trap or catch by trickery
7.4
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈsnɛr / (AmE), / ɪnˈsnɛə /; / ɪnˈsneə / (BrE)
to trap or catch someone or something, often by trickery or in a difficult situation

Examples

To ensnare an animal
Her kindness was genuine, not a ploy to ensnare you in her plans.
Many animals are ensnared by nets set along the riverbank each year.
The clever scheme was designed to ensnare unsuspecting tourists.
She found herself ensnared in a web of lies and deceit.
The company used clever marketing tactics to ensnare new customers.
The hunter tried to ensnare the rabbit with a well-hidden net.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entrap - to catch in or as in a trap
  • antonym: free - to release from captivity or a trap
  • synonym: trap - to catch or take by a device or trick
