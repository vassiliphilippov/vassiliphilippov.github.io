adagio

adverb
at a slow tempo (music)
Pronunciation: / əˈdɑdʒioʊ / (AmE), / əˈdɑːdʒiəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) to be performed at a slow tempo; slowly.

Examples

Play adagio softly
In the score, the composer marked the passage as adagio.
She performed her solo adagio, creating a calm and reflective mood.
The second movement should be played adagio for a more expressive sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allegro - in music, a fast tempo
  • synonym: lento - in music, a slow tempo
