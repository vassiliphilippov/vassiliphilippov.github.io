staphylococcus

noun
genus of spherical, cluster-forming bacteria
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˌstæfɪloʊˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌstæfɪləˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a genus of spherical, Gram-positive bacteria that often form irregular clusters and may cause various infections, such as boils and septicemia

Examples

Staphylococcus bacteria infection
Staphylococcus is commonly found on the skin and in the respiratory tract of humans.
Certain antibiotics are used to treat staphylococcus infections effectively.
The doctor explained that the infection was caused by a strain of staphylococcus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: streptococcus - genus of spherical bacteria that form chains, also cause infections
