Indian buffalo

noun
upland buffalo from eastern Asia
1.7 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈbʌfəloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
An upland buffalo native to eastern Asia, especially in regions where true water buffaloes do not thrive, used for draught work and milk production.

Examples

The Indian buffalo milk
Many dairy farmers in the region depend on the milk produced by the Indian buffalo.
The Indian buffalo is valued for its ability to work in hilly terrains where water buffaloes struggle.
Unlike the water buffalo, the Indian buffalo is more suited for upland agricultural tasks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: water buffalo - domesticated buffalo living in marshes and wetlands, common in south and southeast Asia
