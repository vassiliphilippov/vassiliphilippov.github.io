popularist

noun
one appealing to the masses
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːpjələrɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊlərɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices appealing to popular desires or prejudices, often for political gain

Examples

A popularist leader
The journalist accused the leader of being a popularist who avoided difficult decisions.
Critics argued that the party's popularist approach ignored important long-term issues.
He was labeled a popularist for making promises that appealed to public opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: populist - a person who seeks to represent the interests of ordinary people
