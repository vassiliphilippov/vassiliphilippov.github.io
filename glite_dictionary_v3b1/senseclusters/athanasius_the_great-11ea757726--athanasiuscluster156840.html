Athanasius the Great

proper noun
Greek patriarch and Church Father (c. 293–373)
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈneɪʒəs ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈneɪziəs ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(c. 293–373) Greek patriarch of Alexandria, renowned for defending Christian orthodoxy against Arianism; recognized as a Church Father, saint, and Doctor of the Church.

Examples

Athanasius the Great Church Father
During his lifetime, Athanasius the Great faced numerous exiles for his opposition to Arianism.
Many theologians consider Athanasius the Great a key figure in shaping early Christian doctrine.
The legacy of Athanasius the Great continues to influence the teachings of the Eastern Orthodox Church.
