scalded milk

noun
milk heated almost to boiling
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskɔːldɪd mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈskɔːldɪd mɪlk / (BrE)
milk that has been heated to just below the boiling point and then cooled, often used in cooking or baking

Examples

A cup of scalded milk
Before adding yeast, make sure the scalded milk has cooled to a safe temperature.
Many old-fashioned recipes call for scalded milk instead of cold milk for a smoother result.
The custard's texture improves when you start with scalded milk rather than raw milk.
