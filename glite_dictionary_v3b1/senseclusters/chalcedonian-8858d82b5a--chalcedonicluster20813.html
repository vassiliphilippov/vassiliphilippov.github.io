Chalcedonian

noun
follower of Chalcedon doctrine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkælsɪˈdoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌkælsɪˈdəʊniən / (BrE)
a person who accepts or supports the Christian doctrines established by the Council of Chalcedon in 451 AD

Examples

A Chalcedonian believer
The Chalcedonian argued passionately for the council's definition of Christ's two natures.
Many Eastern churches did not accept the Chalcedonian position after the council.
As a Chalcedonian, he studied the historical debates of early Christianity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Monophysite - a person who holds the belief that Christ has only one nature, opposed to Chalcedonian doctrine
  • antonym: Nestorian - a person who follows the teachings of Nestorius, often contrasted with Chalcedonian doctrine
