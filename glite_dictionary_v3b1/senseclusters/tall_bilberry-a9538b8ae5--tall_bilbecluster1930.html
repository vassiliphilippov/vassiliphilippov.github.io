tall bilberry

noun
high-growing North American shrub, source of cultivated blueberries
9.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /tɔːl ˈbɪl.bər.ɪ/ (AmE), /tɔːl ˈbɪl.bər.ɪ/ (BrE)
A high-growing deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, bearing edible bluish to blackish berries with a distinct bloom; the primary source of cultivated blueberries.

Examples

The tall bilberry shrub
Native to North America, the tall bilberry thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
Researchers study the tall bilberry to improve blueberry crops.
The tall bilberry is widely cultivated for its sweet, edible fruit.
