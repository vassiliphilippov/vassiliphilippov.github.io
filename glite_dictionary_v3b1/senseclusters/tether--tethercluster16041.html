tether

verb
tie with a rope or chain to restrict movement
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛðə / (BrE)
to tie (an animal or object) with a rope, chain, or similar restraint to restrict its movement to a certain area

Examples

To tether a goat
During the storm, they tethered the boat to keep it from drifting away.
Please tether your horse outside the barn before coming in.
She carefully tethered the goat to a post in the yard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: untether - to release from a tether; to set free from binding
