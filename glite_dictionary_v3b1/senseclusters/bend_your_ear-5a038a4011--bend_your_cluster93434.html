bend your ear

verb
talk to someone at length
Pronunciation: / bɛnd jɚ ɪr / (AmE), / bɛnd jɔː ɪə / (BrE)
to talk to someone for a long time, often in a way that may be tiresome or persistent

Examples

To bend your ear with complaints
Don't bend my ear with your complaints all afternoon.
She was eager to bend his ear about her new ideas for the company.
Whenever I visit, he loves to bend my ear about his travels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chew the fat - have a long and informal conversation
  • synonym: pester - to bother or annoy with persistent requests or talking
