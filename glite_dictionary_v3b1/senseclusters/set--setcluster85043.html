set

verb
establish a new record or standard
1.0 1.7
Pronunciation: /set/ (AmE), /set/ (BrE)
to establish, decide, or determine something such as a rule, value, or deadline

Examples

Set a deadline
After years of training, she finally set a new world record in the marathon.
He set a personal best at the meet, establishing himself as one of the top athletes.
The company hopes to set the standard for customer service in the industry.
She set a strict deadline for the completion of the project.
The committee will set new guidelines for employee conduct.
The government sets the minimum age for voting.
Experts set the earthquake damage at over a million dollars.
The committee set the project deadline for September 30th.
The judge set bail at $50,000 for the accused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to surpass an existing record or standard
  • antonym: change - to make or become different (the opposite of setting something fixed)
  • synonym: determine - to come to a definite decision about something
  • synonym: establish - to set up or found something firmly
  • synonym: estimate - to roughly judge the value, number, quantity, or extent of something
  • synonym: fix - to set firmly or make definite
