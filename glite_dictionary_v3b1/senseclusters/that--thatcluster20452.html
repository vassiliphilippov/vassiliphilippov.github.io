that

conjunction
introducing a clause
Pronunciation: / ðæt / (AmE), / ðæt / (BrE)
used to introduce a subordinate clause expressing a statement, reason, purpose, or result

Examples

Know that fact
He explained that he could not attend the meeting due to a prior commitment.
The teacher hoped that her students would enjoy the new project.
It is important that everyone arrives on time for the event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: because - used to introduce a reason or explanation
  • synonym: so that - used to introduce a purpose or result
