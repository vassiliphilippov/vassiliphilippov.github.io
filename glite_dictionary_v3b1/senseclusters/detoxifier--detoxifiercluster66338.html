detoxifier

noun
remover of toxins
6.3
Pronunciation: / diːˈtɑksɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / diːˈtɒksɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an organism or substance that removes, breaks down, or absorbs harmful chemicals from the environment or living systems

Examples

Water detoxifier
The scientists added a detoxifier to the water to help remove dangerous chemicals.
Certain plants act as natural detoxifiers by absorbing toxins from the soil.
Using a biological detoxifier can make polluted environments safer for wildlife.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contaminant - a substance that makes something impure or harmful
  • synonym: purifier - something that cleans or removes impurities
