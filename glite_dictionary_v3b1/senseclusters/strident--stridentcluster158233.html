strident

adjective
describing hissing or buzzing speech sounds

(of certain speech sounds) produced by forcing air through a constricted passage, resulting in a hissing or buzzing quality, as in the sounds of 'f', 's', 'z', or 'th'.

Examples

Strident consonant
In linguistics, the strident consonants include sounds like 's' and 'z'.

Synonyms

  • fricative
    8.0 0.06/m
    fricative is made by forcing air through a narrow gap to cause friction in many sounds, while strident stresses a hissing or buzzing quality.
  • spirant
    9.9 0.00/m
    spirant is a linguistic term for sounds made with a narrow channel and friction, while strident focuses on their hissing or buzzing tone.
  • sibilant
    7.9 0.33/m
