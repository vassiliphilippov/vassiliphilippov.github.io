retentiveness

noun
ability to retain liquid or moisture
5.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɛntɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛntɪvnəs / (BrE)
the ability to retain or hold things, such as liquids, moisture, or possessions

Examples

Soil retentiveness
Gardeners prefer soil with low retentiveness to avoid waterlogged plants.
Sandy soil typically has poor retentiveness and dries out quickly after rain.
The clay soil's retentiveness causes water to remain near the roots for a long time.
Her retentiveness made her reluctant to throw away anything, even old newspapers.
Some people are known for their retentiveness when it comes to cherished possessions.
The retentiveness of the collector's habit filled the house with memorabilia from decades past.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorbency - the ability to soak up liquids easily
  • antonym: forgetfulness - tendency to forget or fail to keep things
