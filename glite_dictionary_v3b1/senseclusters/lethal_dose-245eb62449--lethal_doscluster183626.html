lethal dose

noun
quantity of substance causing death
2.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈliː.θəl doʊs/ (AmE), /ˈliː.θəl dəʊs/ (BrE)
the amount of a substance that, when taken or administered, is sufficient to cause death

Examples

A lethal dose of poison
Doctors warned that ingesting even a small amount could approach a lethal dose for humans.
He survived the accident, but was treated for exposure just under the lethal dose level.
The lethal dose of the chemical was determined in laboratory tests on rats.
