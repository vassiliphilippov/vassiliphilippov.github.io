deflowering

noun
removal of flowers
7.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈflaʊ.rɪŋ/ /; / /də.ˈflaʊ.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈflaʊ.rɪŋ/ /; / /də.ˈflaʊ.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act or process of removing flowers from plants, often to promote fruit growth or for harvesting purposes

Examples

Deflowering the plants
The deflowering of the apple trees was done early in the season to improve fruit quality.
Farmers practiced deflowering to ensure the plants put more energy into growing larger fruits.
Careful deflowering can help increase the yield and size of certain crops.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pollination - the transfer of pollen to enable fertilization in plants
  • synonym: pruning - the act of cutting away unwanted parts of a plant
