leukodystrophy

noun
rare brain disease
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌluːkoʊˈdɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ˌluːkəʊˈdɪstrəfi / (BrE)
a rare inherited disease that affects the white matter of the brain, leading to progressive loss of neurological function

Examples

Leukodystrophy disease
The doctors explained that leukodystrophy would gradually affect the child's ability to walk and speak.
Researchers are working to find new treatments for leukodystrophy and related disorders.
Families affected by leukodystrophy often seek support from specialized medical centers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multiple sclerosis - a disease in which the immune system attacks the protective covering of nerves
  • synonym: neurodegeneration - the progressive loss of structure or function of neurons
