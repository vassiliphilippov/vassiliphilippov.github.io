clocker

noun
race time recorder
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈklɒkə / (BrE)
a person who records the times of racehorses or athletes during training or races

Examples

Horse race clocker
The clocker carefully recorded each horse's time during the early morning practice.
As a clocker, she was responsible for timing all the runners in the race.
The trainer relied on the clocker to track improvements in the athletes' performance.
The clocker arrived early to record the horses' morning workouts at the track.
Every clocker must pay close attention to ensure accurate timing during the race.
She worked as a clocker before becoming a trainer in the horse racing industry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: judge - an official who oversees a sporting event
  • synonym: timekeeper - a person who records the time taken in a sporting event
