trim down

verb
reduce size, amount, or extent
Pronunciation: / trɪm daʊn / (AmE), / trɪm daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the size, amount, or extent of something, often by cutting or eliminating parts

Examples

To trim down expenses
He decided to trim down his expenses by eating out less often.
Nutritionists recommend that you trim down your sugar intake for better health.
The company plans to trim down its workforce next year to save money.
