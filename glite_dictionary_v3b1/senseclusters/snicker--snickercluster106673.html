snicker

verb
to laugh quietly or slyly
Pronunciation: / ˈsnɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪkə / (BrE)
to laugh quietly in a partly suppressed way, especially at something secretive or disrespectful

Examples

To snicker at mistakes
He tried not to snicker during the serious meeting, but the joke was too funny.
She snickered quietly at her friend's outrageous outfit.
The students snickered when the teacher made a silly mistake on the board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chuckle - to laugh quietly or inwardly
  • synonym: giggle - to laugh lightly in a silly or nervous way
  • synonym: snigger - to laugh in a half-suppressed, typically scornful way
