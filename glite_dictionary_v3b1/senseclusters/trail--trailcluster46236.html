trail

noun
clues or evidence leading to a discovery
3.5 12/m
Pronunciation: / treÉªl / (AmE), / treÉªl / (BrE)
A series of clues or evidence that may lead to solving a problem or discovering something, especially in an investigation.

Examples

A trail of clues
Even after months, detectives kept searching for a new trail in the missing person's case.
The trail left by the hacker eventually led investigators to a foreign server.
The police followed the trail of evidence all the way to the suspect's hideout.

Synonyms

  • breadcrumb trail
    4.8 0.01/m
    a breadcrumb trail guides you through a clear chain of tiny clues to a solution, while trail names any series of clues.
  • lead
    4.8 11/m
    a lead is a single useful clue that points an investigator toward answers, while trail is a longer series of clues.
  • track
    5.0 17/m
    a track is a single piece of evidence, often a physical mark, while trail is a connected set of clues used in an investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lead - a clue or piece of information that may help solve a case
