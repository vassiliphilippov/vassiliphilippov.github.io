military law

noun
laws governing armed forces conduct
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri lɔː / (BrE)
the body of laws and regulations governing the discipline, conduct, trial, and punishment of members of the armed forces

Examples

Military law rules
After the coup, the country operated under military law rather than civil law.
All members of the armed forces are subject to military law at all times.
The officer faced a court-martial for violating military law by disobeying direct orders.
