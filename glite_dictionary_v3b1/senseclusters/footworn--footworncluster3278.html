footworn

adjective
worn on feet
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊtˌwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌwɔːn / (BrE)
showing signs of wear from being walked on or used by feet (describing objects, surfaces, or paths)

Examples

Footworn path
The archaeologist carefully examined the footworn slippers found in the ancient tomb.
Her favorite boots were so footworn that the soles had started to come apart.
The shop displayed a collection of footworn shoes from different historical periods.
The footworn path led through the old forest.
After years of use, the stone steps became footworn and smooth.
Tourists followed the footworn trail up the hillside to the ancient ruins.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barefoot - without anything covering the feet
  • synonym: footwear - items worn on the feet, such as shoes or sandals
  • antonym: pristine - in its original, unspoiled condition
  • synonym: well-trodden - worn smooth by frequent walking
