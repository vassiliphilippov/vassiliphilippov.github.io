Iseult

proper noun
legendary wife of King Mark of Cornwall
Pronunciation: / ɪˈzuːlt / (AmE), / ɪˈzəʊlt / (BrE)
(in medieval legend) the wife of King Mark of Cornwall, who fell in love with Tristan after they both drank a love potion.

In the story, Iseult is torn between her duty as a queen and her love for Tristan.

The legend of Iseult and Tristan has inspired countless works of art and literature.
