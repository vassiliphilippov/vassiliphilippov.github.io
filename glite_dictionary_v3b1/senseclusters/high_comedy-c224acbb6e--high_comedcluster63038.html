high comedy

noun
witty, sophisticated comedy; often satirical
2.8
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / haɪ ˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
Comedy characterized by wit, elegance, and satirical observation of sophisticated or genteel society.

Examples

High comedy wit
She preferred high comedy to slapstick, enjoying plays that challenged her intellect.
The play's clever dialogue and subtle humor make it a shining example of high comedy.
Writers of high comedy often use satire to critique upper-class manners and society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low comedy - comedy emphasizing physical humor and farce rather than wit
