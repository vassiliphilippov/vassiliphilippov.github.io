recoup

verb
reimburse or recover a loss
6.0 2.07/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààkup / (AmE), / r…™ÀàkuÀêp / (BrE)
to get back money or something lost, especially by recovering costs or compensating for a loss

Examples

To recoup losses
He hopes to recoup his investment within two years of starting the business.
The company managed to recoup some of its expenses through a government grant.
The insurance policy helped recoup her financial losses after the accident.
It may take time to recoup the costs spent on repairs.
She managed to recoup her investment after a year of hard work.
The company hopes to recoup its losses by launching a new product line.

Synonyms

  • reclaim
    4.1 4.77/m
    reclaim is used to take back money or goods you paid or lost, while recoup means getting back costs or losses.
  • recover
    2.5 43/m
    recover often means getting health, control, or items back, while recoup focuses on recovering money or financial loss.
  • regain
    3.3 17/m
    regain means getting possession, use, or control again, while recoup usually refers to recovering money or costs.
  • get back
    1.6 40/m
    get back is a casual phrase for taking something back, while recoup is a more formal term for recovering money.
  • win back
    3.4 1.30/m
    win back implies effort to regain people, customers, or trust, while recoup focuses on recovering money or costs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to be deprived of or cease to have something
  • synonym: recover - to regain possession of something lost or stolen
  • synonym: reimburse - to pay back money to someone
