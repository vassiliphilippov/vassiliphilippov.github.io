recoup

verb
reimburse or recover a loss
8.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈkuːp/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈkuːp/ (BrE)
to get back money or something lost, especially by recovering costs or compensating for a loss

Examples

To recoup losses
He hopes to recoup his investment within two years of starting the business.
The company managed to recoup some of its expenses through a government grant.
The insurance policy helped recoup her financial losses after the accident.
It may take time to recoup the costs spent on repairs.
She managed to recoup her investment after a year of hard work.
The company hopes to recoup its losses by launching a new product line.
