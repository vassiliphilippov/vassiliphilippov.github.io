varix

noun
enlarged or twisted blood vessel
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛrɪks / (AmE), / ˈvɛərɪks / (BrE)
an abnormally enlarged or twisted vein or lymphatic vessel, often associated with conditions such as varicose veins

Examples

An enlarged varix
A varix can develop in the esophagus in patients with certain liver diseases.
The surgeon determined that the patient’s bleeding was caused by a ruptured varix.
Treatment options for a varix depend on its location and the underlying condition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: varicose vein - a vein that is abnormally swollen and twisted, usually in the leg
