engrail

verb
to make scalloped edge
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈɡreɪl / (AmE), / ɛnˈɡreɪl / (BrE)
to decorate or cut an edge with a series of rounded or semicircular indentations, especially in heraldry or ornamentation

Examples

To engrail a border
The jeweler carefully engrailed the silver frame to create a decorative border.
In medieval times, shields were often engrailed to signify noble families.
The craftsman engrails the wooden panel to match the traditional design.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scallop - to form an edge with a series of curves or semicircles
