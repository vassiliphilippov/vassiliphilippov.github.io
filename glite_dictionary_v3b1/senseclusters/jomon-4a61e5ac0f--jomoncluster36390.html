Jomon

noun
ancient Japanese people
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒoʊˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊˌmɒn / (BrE)
the prehistoric people of Japan who lived as hunter-gatherers and are known for their distinctive pottery

Examples

Jomon people
Archaeologists discovered Jomon pottery fragments at the ancient site.
The Jomon people are famous for their intricate cord-marked ceramics.
Researchers believe the Jomon lived in Japan thousands of years ago.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Yayoi - the people who succeeded the Jomon in ancient Japan
