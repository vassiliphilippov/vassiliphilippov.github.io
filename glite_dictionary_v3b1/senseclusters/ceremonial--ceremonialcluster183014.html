ceremonial

adjective
relating to formal ceremonies or rituals

(of an event, behaviour, or object) involving or relating to traditional ceremonies or formality; marked by pomp or ritual.

Examples

Ceremonial dress
The ambassador arrived in ceremonial robes for the state dinner.
The contract signing was a ceremonial occasion attended by officials.
They performed a ceremonial dance to celebrate the festival.

Synonyms

  • ceremonious
    7.1 0.13/m
    ceremonious shows careful, visible etiquette and formal manners, while ceremonial describes events or objects used in ritual and public pomp.
  • ritualistic
    7.2 1.09/m
    ritualistic follows set rites and repeated practices in a strict way, while ceremonial focuses on formal ceremony and public pomp.
  • ritual
    4.5 0.41/m
    ritual is the set action or rite used in a tradition, while ceremonial describes the formal event or object that shows that rite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formal - done in accordance with rules of convention or etiquette
  • antonym: informal - having a relaxed, unofficial, or friendly style, manner, or nature
