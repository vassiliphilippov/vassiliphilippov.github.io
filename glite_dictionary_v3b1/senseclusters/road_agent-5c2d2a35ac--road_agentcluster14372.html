road agent

noun
highway robber (esp. Old West)
7.5
Pronunciation: / roʊd ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / rəʊd ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person, especially in the American West during the 19th century, who stops and robs travelers on the road, often targeting stagecoaches or vehicles

Examples

A road agent robs travelers
As a road agent, he became infamous for his cunning and ruthless tactics.
Legends of the Old West often mention daring road agents preying on gold shipments.
The notorious road agent robbed several stagecoaches before he was finally caught.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandit - an outlaw who robs people, especially in isolated or lawless areas
  • synonym: highwayman - a robber who attacks travelers on a public road
