subfamily

noun
taxonomic rank below family
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌfæmɪli /; / ˈsʌbˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
a group that is part of a larger family, especially in biology (below family and above genus) or in language groups

Examples

A subfamily of insects
Scientists recently discovered a new subfamily of insects based on genetic differences.
The cat subfamily includes both domestic cats and big wild cats like lions and tigers.
Within the rose family, the Rosoideae is a well-known subfamily that contains strawberries and raspberries.
The Indo-Iranian subfamily includes several major languages spoken across Asia.
In zoology, the Felinae subfamily contains small and medium-sized cats.
Researchers studied the characteristics of each subfamily to understand their evolutionary history.
