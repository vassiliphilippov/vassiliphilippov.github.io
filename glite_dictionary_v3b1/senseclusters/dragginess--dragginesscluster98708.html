dragginess

noun
slowness or dullness
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡinəs / (AmE), / ˈdræɡinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being slow, dull, or lacking energy or liveliness

Examples

Dragginess of a speech
The dragginess of the lecture made it hard for students to stay awake.
After a long week, he felt a sense of dragginess on Friday afternoon.
The movie's dragginess caused several people to lose interest before the end.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liveliness - the quality of being full of life and energy
  • synonym: sluggishness - the state of being slow or lacking energy
