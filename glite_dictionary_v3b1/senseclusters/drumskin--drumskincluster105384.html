drumskin

noun
drum covering
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌmskɪn / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmskɪn / (BrE)
the stretched surface or membrane on a drum, usually made of animal hide or synthetic material, that you hit to produce sound

Examples

Traditional drums often use animal drumskins for a richer, deeper tone.

After hours of playing, the drumskin finally split and needed to be replaced.
You can adjust the pitch of a drum by loosening or tightening the drumskin.
