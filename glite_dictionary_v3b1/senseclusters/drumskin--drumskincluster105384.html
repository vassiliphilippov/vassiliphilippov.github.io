drumskin

noun
drum covering
4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdrʌm.skɪn/ (AmE), /ˈdrʌm.skɪn/ (BrE)
the stretched surface or membrane on a drum, usually made of animal hide or synthetic material, that you hit to produce sound

Examples

The drumskin surface
The musician carefully replaced the old drumskin to improve the sound of his instrument.
Traditional drums often use animal drumskins for a richer, deeper tone.
After hours of playing, the drumskin began to show signs of wear.
The drummer tightened the drumskin to get a sharper sound for the performance.
After hours of playing, the drumskin finally split and needed to be replaced.
You can adjust the pitch of a drum by loosening or tightening the drumskin.

  • synonym: drumhead - the membrane stretched over one or both ends of a drum
