stew

noun
state of agitation or worry
7.6
Pronunciation: / stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
a state of agitation, excitement, or worry, especially when someone is anxious or upset about something

Examples

In a stew over work
I could tell by his fidgeting that he was in quite a stew about the interview.
She was in a complete stew after hearing the unexpected news.
Try not to get yourself into a stew over things you can't control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - state of being free from agitation or worry
  • synonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
