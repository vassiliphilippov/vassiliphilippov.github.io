humanism

noun
doctrine emphasizing human welfare and values
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that emphasizes human welfare, values, and the importance of human beings, often stressing reason and rejecting supernatural explanations

Examples

Modern humanism
Her support for education reform is inspired by a strong belief in humanism.
Many Enlightenment thinkers promoted humanism as a fundamental principle of modern society.
The charity is guided by the ideals of humanism, putting people's needs at the center of its work.
Her commitment to humanism guided her work in education and social reform.
Many philosophers argue that humanism encourages critical thinking and ethical responsibility.
Modern humanism promotes the idea that people can lead fulfilling lives without belief in the supernatural.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egoism - the doctrine that self-interest is the foundation of morality
  • synonym: philanthropy - the desire to promote the welfare of others, especially by generous donation
  • synonym: secularism - the principle of separation of the state from religious institutions
  • antonym: spiritualism - belief that the spirit exists as distinct from matter, or the belief in communication with spirits
