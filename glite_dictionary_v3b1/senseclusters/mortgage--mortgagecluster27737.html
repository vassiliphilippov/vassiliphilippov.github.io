mortgage

noun
legal agreement for property loan security
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
A legal agreement by which a bank or creditor lends money at interest in exchange for taking the title of the debtor's property, with the condition that the transfer of title becomes void upon the payment of the debt.

Examples

A mortgage loan
A fixed-rate mortgage provides predictable monthly payments for homeowners.
After years of saving, they finally paid off their mortgage on the house.
The bank foreclosed on the property because he defaulted on his mortgage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loan - money that is borrowed and expected to be paid back with interest
