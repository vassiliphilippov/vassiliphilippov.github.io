white daisy

noun
a perennial plant with white petals and yellow center
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / waɪt ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A tall Eurasian perennial plant with white petals and a yellow center, often found in fields and gardens; widely naturalized and sometimes classified as Chrysanthemum.

Examples

A white daisy flower
A single white daisy bloomed beside the garden path, brightening the verdant grass.
During their hike, they saw a field covered in white daisy blossoms swaying in the breeze.
She picked a bouquet of white daisy flowers to decorate the kitchen table.
