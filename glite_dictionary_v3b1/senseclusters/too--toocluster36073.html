too

adverb
to an excessive degree
1.0 844/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀê / (AmE), / tuÀê / (BrE)
to a degree exceeding what is desirable, necessary, or acceptable; more than is suitable or required

Examples

Too much noise
It's too late to catch the last train tonight.
She found the instructions too complicated to follow without help.
The coffee was too hot for me to drink immediately.

Synonyms

  • overly
    3.5 14/m
    overly sounds slightly formal and emphasizes extra degree in description, while too is a common, simple word for excess.
  • excessively
    3.0 3.81/m
    excessively sounds formal and strong, often in warnings or reports, while too is more casual and common in speech.
  • unduly
    6.9 2.35/m
    unduly suggests something is not fair or justified in law or policy, while too simply means more than needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: also - in addition; as well
  • degree lower: very - to a great degree (but not exceeding a desirable limit)
