Confederacy

noun
southern states that seceded in 1861 (U.S. history)
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛdɚəsi / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərəsi / (BrE)
the group of southern states that seceded from the United States in 1861, forming the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War

Examples

The Confederacy in 1861
After the defeat of the Confederacy in 1865, the southern states were gradually readmitted to the United States.
Historians have extensively studied the economy of the Confederacy during the Civil War.
The Confederacy was formed by eleven southern states following their secession from the Union in 1861.
