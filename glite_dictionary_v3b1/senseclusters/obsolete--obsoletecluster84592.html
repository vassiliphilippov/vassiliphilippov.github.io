obsolete

adjective
no longer in use; out of date
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːb.səˈliːt / (AmE), / ˌɒb.səˈliːt / (BrE)
no longer used, produced, or relevant; out of date

Examples

Obsolete technology
Many scientists consider the theory to be obsolete in the light of recent discoveries.
Some obsolete laws remain on the books, even though they are never enforced.
The company replaced their obsolete computers with the latest technology.
Many old legal terms have become obsolete over the centuries.
She replaced her obsolete computer with a newer model.
The technology in this device is now considered obsolete and rarely used.
