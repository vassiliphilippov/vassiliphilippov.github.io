obsolete

adjective
no longer in use; out of date
5.8 7.45/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…ëÀêb.s…ôÀàliÀêt / (AmE), / Àå…íb.s…ôÀàliÀêt / (BrE)
no longer used, produced, or relevant; out of date

Examples

Obsolete technology
Many scientists consider the theory to be obsolete in the light of recent discoveries.
Some obsolete laws remain on the books, even though they are never enforced.
The company replaced their obsolete computers with the latest technology.
Many old legal terms have become obsolete over the centuries.
She replaced her obsolete computer with a newer model.
The technology in this device is now considered obsolete and rarely used.

Synonyms

  • out-of-date
    4.4 2.23/m
    out-of-date describes items that no longer fit current styles or rules, while obsolete often means they are replaced and unused.
  • outdated
    5.0 5.97/m
    outdated is commonly used for things that are no longer useful or fashionable, while obsolete suggests they are completely out of use or production.
  • outmoded
    6.5 1.32/m
    outmoded refers to ideas or styles that have gone out of fashion, while obsolete suggests total loss of usefulness or relevance.
  • antiquated
    8.2 2.41/m
    antiquated feels very old and from a past era, while obsolete means it is no longer used or produced today.
  • outworn
    7.1 0.04/m
    outworn means worn out by age or use and no longer effective, while obsolete means it is no longer used or relevant.
  • dated
    6.2 0.20/m
    dated looks old-fashioned and shows a past style, while obsolete means it is no longer used or made.
  • archaic
    9.9 3.14/m
    archaic refers to language or things from a much earlier time, while obsolete means they are no longer in everyday use.
  • disused
    4.5 0.15/m
    disused means something has been left unused or abandoned, while obsolete implies it is no longer produced or relevant.
  • defunct
    7.0 2.14/m
    defunct means no longer existing or operating, while obsolete means it is no longer used though it may still exist.
  • superannuated
    9.9 0.14/m
    superannuated means worn out or retired because of age, while obsolete describes things replaced and no longer in use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times; opposite of 'obsolete'
  • synonym: outdated - no longer current; old-fashioned
