countermark

verb
to stamp for validation
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmɑːk / (BrE)
to stamp or mark an object, coin, or document with a special mark to validate, reissue, or indicate ownership

Examples

To countermark a document
The government decided to countermark all old coins with a new symbol after the currency reform.
Collectors value coins that were countermarked during historical events for their rarity.
To prevent forgery, the official will countermark each certificate before distribution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erase - to remove marks or information from something
  • synonym: stamp - to mark something officially with a seal or sign
  • synonym: validate - to confirm or make something officially acceptable
