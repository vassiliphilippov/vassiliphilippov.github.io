elbow

verb
push one's way with the elbows
1.7 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɛlˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛlbəʊ /; / ˈɛl.bəʊ / (BrE)
to push or force your way through a crowd by using your elbow or elbows

Examples

To elbow through crowds
Fans elbowed past each other to get closer to the stage.
He elbowed through the packed hallway, determined not to be late.
She elbowed her way to the front of the crowded bus.
He elbowed his way through the crowded train station to catch his bus.
She elbowed him gently to get his attention during the meeting.
The journalist elbowed past the security guards to get closer to the celebrity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jostle - push or bump against someone roughly
  • synonym: nudge - gently push someone or something with your elbow
