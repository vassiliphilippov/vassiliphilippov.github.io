Western honey mesquite

noun
drought-resistant shrub/tree of the southwestern US and Mexico
9.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwest.ərn ˈhʌn.ɪ mə.ˈskiːt/ /; / /ˈhwest.ərn ˈhʌn.ɪ mə.ˈskiːt/ (AmE), /ˈwest.ən ˈhʌn.ɪ mə.ˈskiːt/ /; / /ˈhwest.ən ˈhʌn.ɪ mə.ˈskiːt/ (BrE)
A thorny, deep-rooted shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its drought resistance, sugar-rich pods used as livestock feed, and tendency to form extensive thickets.

Examples

The Western honey mesquite shrub
Efforts to control Western honey mesquite are ongoing, as it often forms dense thickets on rangelands.
Farmers rely on the pods of Western honey mesquite as a nutritious feed for their cattle.
The Western honey mesquite thrives in arid regions thanks to its deep roots and drought resistance.
