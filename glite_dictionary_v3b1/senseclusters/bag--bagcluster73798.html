bag

verb
capture or kill (in hunting)
3.4 4.9
Pronunciation: / bæɡ / (AmE), / bæɡ /; / baɡ / (BrE)
to capture, obtain, or get something, especially by hunting or by unfair means

Examples

To bag a prize
She hopes to bag a deer during hunting season this year.

They managed to bag three ducks before noon.
He managed to bag a few rare books from the closed section, though it wasn't exactly legal.
Poachers often bag endangered animals despite strict regulations.
She tried to bag the last ticket by sneaking past the staff.
