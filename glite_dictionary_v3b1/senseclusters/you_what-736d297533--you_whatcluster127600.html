you what

interjection
expression of disbelief or request for repetition
9.9 7/m
Pronunciation: / ju wÉ‘t / (AmE), / ju wÉ’t / (BrE)
(informal, British) used to express disbelief or to ask someone to repeat what they have just said, often when surprised or incredulous.

Examples

You what, mate
You what, mate? Could you repeat that?
You what? I can't believe you just said that.
When he told me he quit his job on a whim, I replied, "You what?"

Synonyms

  • say what
    9.8 2/m
    say what is a casual phrase people use to show surprise or disbelief, while you what sounds more British and sharper.
  • huh
    8.0 80/m
    huh is a short, informal sound people use to show confusion or ask someone to repeat, while you what is stronger.
  • say that again
    4.2 0.9/m
    say that again asks someone to repeat words clearly, while you what expresses disbelief and often challenges what was just said.
  • oh really
    7.4 0.1/m
    oh really shows mild surprise or interest in what is said, while you what signals stronger disbelief and a sharper reaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: come again - (informal) used to ask someone to repeat something
  • synonym: pardon - used to politely ask someone to repeat what they said
