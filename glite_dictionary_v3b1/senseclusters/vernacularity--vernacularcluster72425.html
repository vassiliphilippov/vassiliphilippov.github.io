vernacularity

noun
local cultural quality
9.1
Pronunciation: /vɜːr.nə.ˈnæk.jə.lə.tiː/ /; / /vɜːr.nə.ˈnæk.jʊ.lə.tiː/ (AmE), /və.nə.ˈnæk.jə.lə.tiː/ /; / /və.nə.ˈnæk.jʊ.lə.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being native to or characteristic of a particular place, culture, or tradition, especially in art or architecture

Examples

Vernacularity in architecture
The vernacularity of the village houses reflects the traditions of the local community.
Architects often study vernacularity to design buildings that fit naturally into their surroundings.
The artist's work celebrates the vernacularity of regional crafts and materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: localness - the state of being local or specific to a place
  • antonym: universality - the quality of being universal or applicable everywhere
