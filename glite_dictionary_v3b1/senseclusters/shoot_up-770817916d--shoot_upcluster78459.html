shoot up

verb
rise quickly and dramatically
2.7
Pronunciation: / ʃuːt ʌp /; / ʃut ʌp / (AmE), / ʃuːt ʌp / (BrE)
to increase quickly and suddenly in amount, value, or level

Examples

Prices shot up
After the announcement, oil prices shot up within hours.
Attendance numbers always shoot up during the holiday season.
The company's profits shot up last quarter thanks to strong sales.
Attendance figures shot up after the new exhibit opened at the museum.
Fuel prices may shoot up if demand continues to rise throughout the winter.
If interest rates shoot up again, it could affect the housing market.
