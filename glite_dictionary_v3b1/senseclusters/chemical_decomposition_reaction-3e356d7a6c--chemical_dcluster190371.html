chemical decomposition reaction

noun
chemical reaction breaking substance into simpler parts
1.7 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkem.ɪ.kəl diː.kəm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈkem.ɪ.kəl diː.kɒm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which a single compound breaks down into two or more different substances

Examples

A chemical decomposition reaction breaks down
A chemical decomposition reaction occurs when hydrogen peroxide breaks down into water and oxygen.
A classic example of a chemical decomposition reaction is the electrolysis of water.
Heat can often trigger a chemical decomposition reaction in certain compounds.
