pay dividends

verb
yield positive results or benefits
Pronunciation: / peɪ ˈdɪvəˌdɛndz / (AmE), / peɪ ˈdɪvɪˌdɛndz / (BrE)
(of an action, effort, or investment) to yield significant benefits or positive results, especially after some time.

Examples

To pay dividends in career
Hard work and persistence in your studies will pay dividends in your career.
Her patience with the project began to pay dividends as the results improved.
Taking the time to build strong relationships can pay dividends in both business and personal life.
