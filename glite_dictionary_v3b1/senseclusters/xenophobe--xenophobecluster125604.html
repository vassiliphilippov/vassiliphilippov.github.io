xenophobe

noun
person fearing foreigners
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈzɛnəˌfoʊb / (AmE), / ˈzɛnəfəʊb / (BrE)
a person who dislikes or fears people from other countries or cultures

Examples

A xenophobe dislikes foreigners
The politician was accused of being a xenophobe after making several anti-immigrant remarks.
A true xenophobe avoids contact with people from different backgrounds.
Some believe that education can help change the views of a xenophobe.
Some politicians have been labeled xenophobes because of their opposition to immigration.
The new policy was criticized for appealing to the fears of xenophobes in the country.
He was called a xenophobe after making negative remarks about foreign tourists.
It is difficult for a xenophobe to adapt to multicultural environments.
The politician was accused of being a xenophobe after making several controversial statements.
Her open-minded attitude contrasted sharply with her brother, who was a known xenophobe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: internationalist - a person who supports cooperation between countries
  • synonym: racist - a person who believes one race is superior to others
  • antonym: xenophile - a person who is attracted to foreign cultures or people
