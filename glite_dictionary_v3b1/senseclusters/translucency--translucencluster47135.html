translucency

noun
the property of being partly see-through
6.3
Pronunciation: / trænsˈluːsənsi / (AmE), / trænzˈluːsənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of allowing light to pass through diffusely, so that objects on the other side are not clearly defined

Examples

Frosted glass translucency
Frosted glass is often prized for its translucency in modern interior design.
The translucency of the curtain let in a soft, diffused light.
The jellyfish's translucency made it nearly invisible in the water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opacity - the quality of not allowing any light to pass through
  • synonym: transparency - the quality of allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
