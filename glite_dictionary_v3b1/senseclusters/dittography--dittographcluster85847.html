dittography

noun
accidental repetition
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / dɪˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the accidental repetition of a letter, word, or phrase in writing or printing

Examples

A dittography error
The editor noticed a dittography where the word 'and' appeared twice in the sentence.
Ancient manuscripts often contain dittography due to scribes copying lines more than once.
Careful proofreading can help prevent dittography in important documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: error - a mistake in writing or printing
  • antonym: haplography - the accidental omission of a repeated letter or group in writing
