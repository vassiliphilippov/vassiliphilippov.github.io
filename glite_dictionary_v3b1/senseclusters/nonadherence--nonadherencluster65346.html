nonadherence

noun
lack of attachment
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnədˈhɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnədˈhɪərəns / (BrE)
the failure or absence of sticking or attachment between two surfaces, materials, or biological tissues

Examples

Nonadherence of tape
The nonadherence of the bandage made it difficult to keep the wound protected.
Scientists studied the nonadherence of certain coatings to improve product durability.
In surgery, nonadherence between tissues can affect the healing process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adhesion - the process or state of sticking or attaching
