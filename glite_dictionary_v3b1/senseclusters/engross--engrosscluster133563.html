engross

verb
absorb all one's attention
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊs / (BrE)
to absorb all the attention or interest of someone; to occupy someone completely

Examples

To engross the audience
A good movie can easily engross an audience from start to finish.
He was engrossed in his work and didn't notice the time passing.
The novel was so exciting that it engrossed her for hours.
He tends to engross himself in his work and ignore everything else.
She was so engrossed in the novel that she missed her train stop.
The children became engrossed in their game and forgot about dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take in all the attention or interest
  • antonym: distract - to divert attention away
