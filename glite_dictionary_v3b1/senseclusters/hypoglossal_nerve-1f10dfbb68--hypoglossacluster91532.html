hypoglossal nerve

noun
twelfth cranial nerve controlling tongue muscles
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.poʊˈɡlɑ.səl nɝv / (AmE), / ˌhɪ.pəˈɡlɒ.səl nɜːv / (BrE)
the twelfth cranial nerve that controls the muscles of the tongue

Examples

The hypoglossal nerve controls tongue
Damage to the hypoglossal nerve can result in difficulty with speech and swallowing.
During the operation, the surgeon was careful to avoid injuring the hypoglossal nerve.
The hypoglossal nerve is responsible for controlling most of the tongue's movements.
