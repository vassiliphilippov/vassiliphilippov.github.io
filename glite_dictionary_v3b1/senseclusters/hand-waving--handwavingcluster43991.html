hand-waving

noun
vague explanation
7.9
Pronunciation: /hænd ˈweɪ.vɪŋ/ (AmE), /hænd ˈweɪ.vɪŋ/ (BrE)
an explanation or argument that is vague, lacks detail, or avoids addressing important points

Examples

A hand-waving answer
The scientist's response was dismissed as hand-waving because it lacked supporting evidence.
During the meeting, his explanation was mostly hand-waving and did not address the real issues.
Critics accused the author of hand-waving when discussing the book's main argument.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarification - the act of making something clearer or easier to understand
  • synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding a direct answer or clear explanation
