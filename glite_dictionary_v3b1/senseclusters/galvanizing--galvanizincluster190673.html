galvanizing

adjective
causing excitement or motivation
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæl.vəˌnaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.nʌɪ.zɪŋ /; / ˈɡal.vəˌnaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a person or group to become excited or motivated, often suddenly and intensely

Examples

A galvanizing speech
Her galvanizing presence brought hope to the entire community.
The announcement had a galvanizing effect on the employees, who began working with renewed energy.
The coach's galvanizing speech turned the team's attitude around.
Her performance had a galvanising effect on the audience.
The coach made a galvanising speech that inspired the team to victory.
The new legislation acted as a galvanising force for social change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demotivating - making someone less enthusiastic or interested
  • synonym: electric - exciting or thrilling, as if charged with electricity
  • synonym: inspiring - encouraging enthusiasm or creativity
  • antonym: uninspiring - not exciting or stimulating; failing to provoke interest
