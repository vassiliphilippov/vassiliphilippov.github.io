hawkish

adjective
favoring aggressive or hard-line policies
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkɪʃ / (BrE)
favoring aggressive or hard-line policies, especially in foreign affairs or politics; inclined to support the use of force or military intervention

Examples

A hawkish policy
Analysts describe the new government's stance as markedly hawkish compared to its predecessors.
Her hawkish position on military spending sparked debate among her colleagues.
The senator became increasingly hawkish in his statements about national security.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dovish - favoring peace, compromise, or conciliatory policies
