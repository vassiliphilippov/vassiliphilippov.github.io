Matthew Flinders

proper noun
British explorer who mapped Australian coast
Pronunciation: / ˈmæθju ˈflɪndərz / (AmE), / ˈmæθju ˈflɪndəz / (BrE)
(1774–1814) British explorer who was the first to map much of the Australian coastline and popularized the name 'Australia'.

Examples

Matthew Flinders explorer
Matthew Flinders charted large sections of the Australian coastline in the early 19th century.
Many historians credit Matthew Flinders with giving Australia its name.
The statue of Matthew Flinders stands outside the State Library as a tribute to his achievements.
