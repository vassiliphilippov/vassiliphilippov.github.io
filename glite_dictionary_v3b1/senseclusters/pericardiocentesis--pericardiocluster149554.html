pericardiocentesis

noun
removal of heart sac fluid
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˌkɑrdioʊsɛnˈtiːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˌkɑːdɪəʊsɛnˈtiːsɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which fluid is removed from the sac surrounding the heart, usually to relieve pressure or for diagnosis

Examples

Pericardiocentesis procedure
The doctor performed a pericardiocentesis to relieve pressure on the patient's heart.
A pericardiocentesis can help diagnose infections or other causes of fluid buildup around the heart.
After the pericardiocentesis, the patient's breathing improved significantly.
The doctor performed an emergency pericardiocentesis to save the patient's life.
A pericardiocentesis is often needed when fluid builds up around the heart.
After the pericardiocentesis, the patient's blood pressure improved quickly.
The cardiologist performed a pericardiocentesis to treat the patient's cardiac tamponade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cardiac tamponade - a condition where fluid builds up around the heart, causing dangerous pressure
  • antonym: pericarditis - inflammation of the sac around the heart
  • synonym: thoracentesis - removal of fluid from the chest cavity
