raise the spectre

verb
suggest the possibility of trouble
Pronunciation: / reɪz ðə ˈspɛktɚ / (AmE), / reɪz ðə ˈspɛktə / (BrE)
to mention or suggest the possibility of something bad or worrying that might happen

Examples

To raise the spectre of war
His remarks raise the spectre of increased tension between the countries.
Some economists warn that new tariffs could raise the spectre of trade wars.
The failed negotiation raises the spectre of a potential strike.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allay fears - to reduce the level of fear or concern
  • synonym: raise the alarm - to warn others of a danger or problem
