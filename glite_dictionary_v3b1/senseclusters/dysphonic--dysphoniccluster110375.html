dysphonic

adjective
impaired voice
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
having difficulty speaking or an impaired voice due to a physical disorder of the mouth, throat, or vocal cords

Examples

Dysphonic patient
After the operation, the singer became dysphonic and struggled to perform on stage.
The doctor noted that the patient was dysphonic and recommended a visit to a speech therapist.
Teachers who overuse their voices may become dysphonic by the end of the school year.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: aphonic - unable to produce voice
  • antonym: euphonic - having a pleasant or clear sound
  • synonym: hoarse - having a rough or weak voice
