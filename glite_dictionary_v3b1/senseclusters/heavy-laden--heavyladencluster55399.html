heavy-laden

adjective
weighed down; burdened (often with cares)
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛviˈleɪdən / (AmE), / ˌhɛviˈleɪdən / (BrE)
weighed down or burdened, especially with cares, troubles, or responsibilities

Examples

Heavy-laden with worry
After a long and difficult journey, she appeared heavy-laden with worry.
He spoke in a heavy-laden voice, burdened by memories of the past.
The heavy-laden travelers sought shelter from the storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unburdened - not weighed down by cares or worries
  • synonym: weighed down - feeling burdened or oppressed
