neglect

verb
fail to care for properly
Pronunciation: / nɪˈɡlɛkt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛkt / (BrE)
to fail to give proper care or attention to someone or something, or to fail to do something you are responsible for

Examples

To neglect your work
He was accused of neglecting his duties at work.
If parents neglect their responsibilities, children can suffer serious consequences.
She often neglected her plants, so most of them wilted and died.
If you neglect your homework, your grades will suffer.
She often neglects to water her plants and they start to wilt.
The old house had been neglected for years before new owners moved in.
She often neglected to call her parents, even though they worried about her.
They were criticized for neglecting important safety procedures during construction.
You should not neglect your duties at work, as it can have serious consequences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attend - to pay attention to or care for
  • antonym: attend to - to pay attention to or care for
  • synonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
  • synonym: omit - to leave out or fail to include
  • synonym: overlook - fail to notice or do something
