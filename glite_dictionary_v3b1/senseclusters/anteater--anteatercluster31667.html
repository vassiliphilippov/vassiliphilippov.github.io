anteater

noun
mammal that eats ants and termites
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈænˌtitər /; / ˈænˌtiːtər / (AmE), / ˈænˌtiːtə / (BrE)
a mammal with a long snout and tongue, specialized for feeding on ants and termites, especially the giant anteater of Central and South America

Examples

Giant anteater
At the zoo, we watched a giant anteater eating from an anthill.
The anteater uses its long tongue to extract ants from narrow crevices.
Unlike most mammals, an anteater has no teeth but relies on its sticky tongue.
During our trip to the rainforest, we spotted a giant anteater searching for food.
The anteater uses its long tongue to reach insects inside anthills.
Unlike many mammals, the anteater does not have any teeth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aardvark - a nocturnal burrowing mammal that eats ants and termites
  • synonym: pangolin - a scaly mammal also called 'scaly anteater'
