chagigah

noun
Jewish festival sacrifice
9.9
Pronunciation: / xɑːˈgiːgə / (AmE), / xəˈgiːgə / (BrE)
a Jewish festival sacrifice or the festival itself, especially associated with Passover and other pilgrimage holidays

Examples

Passover chagigah sacrifice
During Passover, the family brought a chagigah to the Temple as part of their celebration.
The tradition of the chagigah is mentioned in ancient Jewish texts describing festival rituals.
Many people learn about the chagigah when studying the customs of Jewish pilgrimage holidays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Passover - a major Jewish festival commemorating the Exodus from Egypt
  • synonym: korban - a general term for a Jewish sacrificial offering
