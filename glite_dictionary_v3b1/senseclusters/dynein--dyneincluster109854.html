dynein

noun
cellular motor protein
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪniːn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪniːn / (BrE)
a motor protein in cells that enables movement by moving along microtubules (such as moving cilia, flagella, or transporting materials)

Examples

Dynein motor protein
The movement of cilia depends on the action of dynein inside the cell.
Researchers study dynein to understand how cells transport materials internally.
Mutations in dynein can cause problems with cell movement and function.
Dynein helps transport nutrients and organelles to different parts of the cell.
Scientists study dynein to understand how cells move their internal components.
A defect in dynein can lead to problems with nerve cell function.
