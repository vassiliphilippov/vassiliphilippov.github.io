hitch

verb
fasten or connect to a vehicle or equipment
Pronunciation: / hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
to fasten or connect (a vehicle or animal) to something such as another vehicle or a piece of equipment.

Examples

To hitch a trailer
Before leaving, make sure you have properly hitched the camper to the car.
The farmer hitched the plow to the tractor before starting the day's work.
We need to hitch the trailer to the back of the truck for the move.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attach - to join or fasten to something
  • antonym: unhitch - to disconnect something that has been fastened or attached
