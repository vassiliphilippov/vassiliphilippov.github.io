flyball

noun
governor weight
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a rotating weighted ball on a centrifugal governor, used to regulate an engine's speed by centrifugal force

Examples

Engine flyball
The engineer inspected the flyball to ensure the engine would not run too fast.
A worn flyball can cause the governor to malfunction and affect speed regulation.
During maintenance, the technician replaced the damaged flyball with a new one.
