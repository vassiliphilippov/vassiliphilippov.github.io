panhypopituitarism

noun
pituitary hormone deficiency
9.9
Pronunciation: /pæn.haɪ.poʊ.pɪ.ˈtjuː.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /pæn.haɪ.pəʊ.pɪ.ˈtjuː.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which the pituitary gland fails to produce most or all of its hormones

Examples

panhypopituitarism hormone failure
The doctor explained that panhypopituitarism can affect growth, metabolism, and other vital functions.
After extensive testing, the child was found to have panhypopituitarism and began hormone replacement therapy.
Patients with panhypopituitarism often require lifelong medical management for their hormone deficiencies.
