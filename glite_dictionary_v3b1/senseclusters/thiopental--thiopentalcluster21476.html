thiopental

noun
fast-acting barbiturate anesthetic
7.7 8.5
Pronunciation: / θaɪoʊˈpɛntɔl / (AmE), / θaɪəʊˈpɛntɒl / (BrE)
A fast-acting barbiturate used as a general anesthetic, often in the form of a sodium salt for intravenous injection.

Examples

Thiopental injection
Because of its rapid onset, thiopental is often chosen for the induction of anesthesia.
In some protocols, thiopental is used to induce a medically induced coma in patients with brain injuries.
The anesthesiologist administered thiopental before the surgical procedure began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barbiturate - any of a group of drugs derived from barbituric acid used as sedatives and anesthetics
