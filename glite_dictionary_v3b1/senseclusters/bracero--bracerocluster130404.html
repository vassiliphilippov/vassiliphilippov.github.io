bracero

noun
Mexican temporary laborer in the U.S. (WWII era)
9.3
Pronunciation: / brəˈsɛɾoʊ / (AmE), / brəˈsɛərəʊ / (BrE)
A Mexican laborer who worked in the United States, especially as part of a temporary worker program during World War II to address labor shortages in agriculture and railroads.

Examples

A bracero worker
Historians have documented the experiences and challenges faced by bracero workers during the 1940s.
Many braceros traveled from remote villages to work the fields in California.
The bracero program had a lasting impact on both American agriculture and Mexican migration patterns.
