bracero

noun
Mexican temporary laborer in the U.S. (WWII era)
9.3 8.7
Pronunciation: / brəˈsɛɾoʊ / (AmE), / brəˈsɛərəʊ / (BrE)
A Mexican laborer who worked in the United States, especially as part of a temporary worker program during World War II to address labor shortages in agriculture and railroads.
