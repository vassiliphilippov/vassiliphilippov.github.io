European Economic Community

proper noun
precursor to the European Union; economic alliance
Pronunciation: / jʊˌroʊpiən ɪˈkɑːnəmɪk kəˈmjunəti / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiən iːˈkɒnəmɪk kəˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
an international organization established in 1957 by several European countries to promote economic integration and cooperation, serving as a precursor to the European Union

Examples

The European Economic Community treaty
France was one of the founding members of the European Economic Community.
The European Economic Community eventually evolved into what is now known as the European Union.
The European Economic Community played a key role in integrating the economies of its member states.
