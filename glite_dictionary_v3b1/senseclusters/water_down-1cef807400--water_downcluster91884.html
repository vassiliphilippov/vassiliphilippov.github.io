water down

verb
make less strong or intense
8.6 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər daʊn/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər daʊn/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə daʊn/ /; / /ˈwɒ.tə daʊn/ (BrE)
to make something less strong, intense, or effective, often by adding water or diluting it (can be used for liquids or ideas/statements)

Examples

To water down juice
She always waters down her orange juice to make it less sweet.
The company decided to water down its safety regulations after pressure from investors.
The movie's message was watered down to appeal to a wider audience.
He decided to water down the soup because it was too salty for the children.
If this paint is too thick, you can water down the mixture for a smoother finish.
Some bars have been accused of trying to water down expensive whiskey to increase profits.
Don't water down your feelings—tell him exactly what you think.
She refused to water down her speech for a broader audience.
The company decided to water down the report to avoid controversy.
