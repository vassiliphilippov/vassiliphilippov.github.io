drive in

verb
force (nail, screw, etc.) into a surface
3.5 3.3
Pronunciation: / draɪv ɪn / (AmE), / draɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to force something (such as a nail, screw, or bolt) into a surface, often by pushing or turning it with a tool

Examples

To drive in a nail
Be sure to drive in the nails straight so the wood doesn't split.
He used an electric drill to drive in the screws quickly.
They drove in the bolts to secure the metal frame firmly.
