hawk

noun
bird of prey with long tail
Pronunciation: / hɑk / (AmE), / hɔːk / (BrE)
A diurnal bird of prey with short rounded wings and a long tail, often known for its keen eyesight and hunting skills.

Examples

A wild hawk
A sharp-eyed hawk circled the barn, scaring away smaller birds.
She spotted a hawk perched on a fence post during her morning walk.
The hawk soared high above the fields searching for its next meal.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: eagle - large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings
  • synonym: falcon - diurnal bird of prey with long pointed wings and notched beak
