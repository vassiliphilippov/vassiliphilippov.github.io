oppose

verb
be against; express opposition
2.9 91/m
Pronunciation: / É™ËˆpoÊŠz / (AmE), / É™ËˆpÉ™ÊŠz / (BrE)
to be against, resist, or set in opposition to someone or something; to try to stop, compete with, or disagree with something

Examples

To oppose a plan
Environmental groups have been opposing the construction of the new highway.
Many people oppose the proposed increase in taxes.
She openly opposed the decision during the meeting.
He is not afraid to oppose powerful interests when necessary.
Many citizens oppose the new government policy.
Several groups opposed the construction of the new factory.
Many people opposed the construction of the factory near their homes.
Several lawmakers oppose the new tax proposal and have promised to vote against it.
She is planning to oppose the motion at the next meeting.

Synonyms

  • resist
    2.2 19/m
    resist means acting strongly against change or orders, while oppose is a general word for being against or trying to stop something.
  • fight
    1.0 88/m
    fight means trying hard to stop or win, often with force or strong effort, while oppose is more general and may be peaceful.
  • object
    5.1 20/m
    object means expressing disapproval openly, while oppose means actively being against something and trying to stop it.
  • reject
    1.9 52/m
    reject means refusing to accept an idea, offer, or person, while oppose is being against something and may involve action to stop it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: align - to place or arrange in a line with or alongside; the opposite of setting in opposition
  • synonym: contrast - to compare in such a way as to emphasize differences
  • synonym: match - to oppose or set against for competition
  • synonym: pit - to set in direct competition or conflict with
  • synonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of something
  • antonym: support - to agree with and give encouragement to something or someone
