quarter

noun
unit of weight (25 pounds)
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə / (BrE)
a unit of weight used in Britain, equal to a quarter of a hundredweight (about 25 or 28 pounds), mainly historical

Examples

A quarter of barley
He purchased three quarters of barley from the merchant.
In historical trade, a quarter was commonly used to measure items like flour or coal.
The sack of grain weighed one quarter and was difficult to lift.
Farmers often sold their wheat by the quarter in the nineteenth century.
Records from the market show butter priced per quarter in the old ledgers.
The sack of grain weighed exactly one quarter, making it easy to transport.
