autokinetic

adjective
illusion of movement
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔtoʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing the illusion that a stationary object is moving, especially in darkness

Examples

Autokinetic illusion
In a dark room, a small light may appear autokinetic to observers.
The autokinetic phenomenon can trick pilots into thinking a fixed light is moving.
Researchers studied how the autokinetic effect influences perception in low-light conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illusory - not real, based on illusion
  • antonym: stationary - not moving; still
