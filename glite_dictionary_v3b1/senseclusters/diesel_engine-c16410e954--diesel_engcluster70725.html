diesel engine

noun
internal combustion engine burning heavy oil
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːzəl ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈdiːzəl ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
An internal combustion engine that operates by burning heavy oil, typically diesel fuel, instead of gasoline.

Examples

A diesel engine runs
After the upgrade, the generator's diesel engine ran much more smoothly.
Modern trucks often rely on a diesel engine for better fuel efficiency and torque.
The ship was powered by a massive diesel engine designed for long voyages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gasoline engine - internal combustion engine that uses gasoline as fuel
  • synonym: internal combustion engine - engine in which fuel combustion occurs within the engine itself
