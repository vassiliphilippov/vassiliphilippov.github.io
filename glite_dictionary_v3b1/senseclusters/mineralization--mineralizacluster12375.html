mineralization

noun
fossil-forming process
7.4
Pronunciation: /mɪn.ər.əl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mɪn.ər.əl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which minerals gradually replace or fill the tissues of dead organisms, preserving their structure as fossils

Examples

Fossil mineralization
The mineralization of bones can preserve their shape for millions of years.
Scientists study mineralization to understand how ancient plants and animals became fossils.
Rapid mineralization can turn wood into stone, creating beautiful petrified forests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permineralization - a fossilization process where minerals fill the pores of an organism's remains
  • synonym: petrification - the process of turning organic material into stone by mineral replacement
