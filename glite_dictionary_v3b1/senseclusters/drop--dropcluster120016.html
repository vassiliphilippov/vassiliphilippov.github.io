drop

noun
stage curtain used as scenery
Pronunciation: / drɑp / (AmE), / drɒp / (BrE)
a curtain in a theatre that can be lowered or raised onto the stage from above, often used as background scenery

Examples

Theatre drop curtain
After the final act, the drop was lowered to signal the end of the performance.
Technicians spent hours preparing the elaborate forest drop for the backdrop of the play.
The painted drop descended slowly, revealing a new scene for the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backdrop - a painted cloth hung at the back of a stage as part of the scenery
  • synonym: scrim - a piece of fabric used as a stage curtain or backdrop, sometimes partially transparent
