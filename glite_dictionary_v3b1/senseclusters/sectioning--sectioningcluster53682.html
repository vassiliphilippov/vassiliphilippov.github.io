sectioning

noun
preparing tissue slices
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of cutting very thin slices of biological tissue for examination under a microscope

Examples

Sectioning tissue samples
The scientist began sectioning the tissue sample to prepare it for microscopic analysis.
Proper sectioning is essential to observe the fine details of cells under the microscope.
After sectioning, the slides were stained to highlight different structures in the tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microtomy - the technique of cutting extremely thin slices of material for microscopic examination
  • synonym: staining - the process of adding color to biological tissue to make structures visible under a microscope
