tribalization

noun
the formation or organization of tribes
7.8 6.5
Pronunciation: /traɪ.bə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /traɪ.bə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of organizing or uniting people into tribes or tribal groups, or adopting tribal characteristics

Examples

Tribalization of society
Social media has been accused of encouraging tribalization in public discourse.
Some historians study the tribalization of societies during periods of migration.
The tribalization of the region has led to complex political alliances.
Efforts at tribalisation often undermine national unity and promote factionalism.
Many scholars study the tribalisation of urban communities after civil unrest.
The tribalisation of politics can lead to deep societal divisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tribalism - strong loyalty to one's tribe, emphasizing group identity
