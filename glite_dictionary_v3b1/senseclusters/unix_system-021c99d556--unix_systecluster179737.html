UNIX system

noun
multiuser computer operating system
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːnɪks ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪks ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A powerful, multiuser operating system known for its stability and influence on many modern systems, originally developed in the 1970s.
