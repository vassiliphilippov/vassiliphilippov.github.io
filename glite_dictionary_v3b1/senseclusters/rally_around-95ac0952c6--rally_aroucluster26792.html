rally around

verb
come together to support someone or a cause
Pronunciation: / ˈræli əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈræli əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to come together as a group to give support to someone or something in times of difficulty or need

Examples

To rally around a friend
After the earthquake, neighbors rallied around each other to rebuild their homes.
In times of crisis, people often rally around those who need help the most.
When their friend was diagnosed with an illness, everyone quickly rallied around her family.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to leave someone or something without support
  • synonym: support - to give assistance or backing to someone or something
