Ichthyosaurus

noun
extinct genus of marine reptiles
Pronunciation: / ˌɪkθioʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌɪkθiəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct marine reptiles of the family Ichthyosauridae, known for their fish-like shape and living during the Jurassic period

Examples

The Ichthyosaurus fossil
Many fossils of Ichthyosaurus have been discovered in early Jurassic rock formations in England.
Scientists believe that Ichthyosaurus resembled modern dolphins in body shape and swimming style.
The discovery of a well-preserved Ichthyosaurus skeleton provided new insights into prehistoric marine life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ichthyosaur - any member of the order Ichthyosauria, a group of extinct marine reptiles
