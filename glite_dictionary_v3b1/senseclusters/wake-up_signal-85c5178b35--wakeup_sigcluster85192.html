wake-up signal

noun
signal that wakes a device from sleep
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪk ʌp ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ʌp ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
(in computing and electronics) a digital or hardware signal that causes a device to leave sleep or low-power mode and enter active operation.

Examples

Device wake-up signal
The base station sends a wake-up signal to wake the IoT sensor from sleep.
A short radio wake-up signal reduced the node's average power consumption by extending battery life.
During testing, the engineer configured the controller to ignore false wake-up signal pulses from interference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardware interrupt - a signal to the processor indicating an event that needs immediate attention, often used to wake systems
  • synonym: wake signal - a signal used to wake a sleeping device
