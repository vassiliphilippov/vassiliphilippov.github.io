karyogamy

noun
fusion of cell nuclei
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæriˈɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / ˌkæriˈɒɡəmi /; / ˌkærɪˈɒɡəmi / (BrE)
the process in which two cell nuclei fuse to form a single nucleus, usually during sexual reproduction in cells such as fungi or protists

Examples

karyogamy in fungi
In fungi, karyogamy is a crucial step that leads to the formation of a diploid nucleus.
The process of karyogamy ensures the combination of genetic material from two parent cells.
Researchers observed karyogamy under the microscope during the fertilization of yeast cells.
During sexual reproduction in fungi, karyogamy results in the formation of a diploid nucleus.
Scientists observed karyogamy under the microscope as the two nuclei merged into one.
The process of karyogamy is essential for genetic recombination in many single-celled organisms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mitosis - cell division resulting in two identical nuclei
  • synonym: plasmogamy - the fusion of the cytoplasm of two cells
