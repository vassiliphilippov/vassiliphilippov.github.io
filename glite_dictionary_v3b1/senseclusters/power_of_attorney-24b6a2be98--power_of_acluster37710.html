power of attorney

noun
legal authorization for someone to act for another
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.ɚ əv əˈtɝː.ni / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə rəv əˈtɜː.ni / (BrE)
a legal document authorizing one person to act as another's representative in legal or financial matters

Examples

A power of attorney document
A power of attorney must be signed while the grantor is mentally competent.
Sarah gave her brother power of attorney to manage her finances while she was abroad.
The lawyer explained how to revoke a power of attorney if circumstances change.
