reinvention

noun
making something new again
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of changing something or yourself so much that it seems new or very different

Examples

A reinvention of career
After years in the same job, she sought a reinvention of her career.
The band's latest album is a bold reinvention of their classic sound.
Many cities are undergoing reinvention to attract new businesses and residents.
The company's reinvention of its brand attracted a new generation of customers.
After years in finance, she began a personal reinvention as a chef.
The reinvention of traditional recipes can lead to exciting new dishes.
The company underwent a complete reinvention to stay competitive in the market.
Personal reinvention can be challenging but rewarding for those seeking change.
After the scandal, the politician attempted a public reinvention to regain trust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modification - a change made to something
  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something unchanged
  • synonym: renewal - the process of making something new or fresh again
  • antonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original condition
  • synonym: transformation - a complete change in form or appearance
