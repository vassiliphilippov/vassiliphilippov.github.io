root vegetable

noun
edible underground plant root or tuber
1.0
Pronunciation: / ruːt ˈvɛdʒtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ruːt ˈvɛdʒtəbl̩ / (BrE)
a vegetable that grows underground as a fleshy root or tuber and is eaten as food

Examples

A root vegetable dish
Carrots are a popular root vegetable used in many dishes.
Parsnip is another example of a nutritious root vegetable.
The farmer harvested several types of root vegetables for the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tuber - a thickened underground part of a stem or rhizome, e.g. a potato
