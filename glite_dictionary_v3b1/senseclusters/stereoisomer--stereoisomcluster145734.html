stereoisomer

noun
type of chemical isomer with different spatial arrangement
6.6 8.1
Pronunciation: /ste.rɪ.oʊ.ˈaɪ.sə.mər/ (AmE), /ste.rɪ.əʊ.ˈaɪ.sə.mə/ (BrE)
a chemical isomer in which the atoms are connected in the same sequence but differ in the arrangement of their atoms in space

Examples

A stereoisomer molecule
Chemists analyze stereoisomers to understand how spatial arrangement influences chemical properties.
Glucose and galactose are stereoisomers that differ only in the arrangement of atoms around one carbon atom.
The study of stereoisomers is crucial in pharmaceutical research, as different arrangements can result in different biological effects.
