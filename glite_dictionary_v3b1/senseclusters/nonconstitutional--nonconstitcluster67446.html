nonconstitutional

adjective
not allowed by constitution
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˌstɪtuˈʃənl /; / ˌnɑnkənˌstɪˈtuːʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˌstɪtjuːˈʃənl /; / ˌnɒnkɒnˌstɪˈtjuːʃənəl / (BrE)
not allowed by, not related to, or not established by a constitution

Examples

A nonconstitutional law
The court declared the new law nonconstitutional after reviewing its provisions.
Any nonconstitutional action by the government can be challenged in court.
The committee found the policy to be nonconstitutional and recommended its immediate repeal.
The committee rejected the proposal as nonconstitutional and outside their legal authority.
Any nonconstitutional changes to the system must be carefully reviewed by legal experts.
The judge clarified that the issue was purely nonconstitutional and did not involve any rights protected by law.

  • synonym: unconstitutional - not allowed by a constitution
