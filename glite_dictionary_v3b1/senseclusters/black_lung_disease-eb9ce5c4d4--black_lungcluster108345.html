black lung disease

noun
lung disease from coal dust inhalation
7.5
Pronunciation: / blæk lʌŋ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / blæk lʌŋ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A lung disease caused by long-term inhalation of coal dust, leading to inflammation and fibrosis; common among coal miners.

Examples

Coal miner black lung disease
Many retired miners have suffered from black lung disease after years of work underground.
The doctor explained that black lung disease is preventable with proper respiratory protection.
There are government programs to assist those diagnosed with black lung disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pneumoconiosis - lung disease caused by inhaling dust, especially in mines
