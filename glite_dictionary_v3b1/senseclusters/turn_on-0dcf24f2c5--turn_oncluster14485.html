turn on

verb
depend on; be contingent on
4.1 1.55/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn …ën / (AmE), / t…úÀên …ín / (BrE)
(of a situation, result, etc.) to depend on or be determined by something.

Examples

Success turns on effort
Her decision turned on the information she got from the lawyer.
The success of the project will turn on the amount of funding we receive.
This case turns on one key piece of evidence.

Synonyms

  • depend on
    1.7 67/m
    depend on is the neutral, common verb used in speech and writing, while turn on is a phrasal verb meaning the same in many contexts.
  • hinge on
    4.2 3.10/m
    hinge on is a stronger phrase that suggests a result depends completely on one factor, while turn on is more general.
  • ride
    5.3 9.61/m
    ride often sounds informal and suggests an outcome hangs on luck or chance, while turn on stays neutral and means depend on.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depend on - to be controlled or determined by something
  • synonym: hinge on - to depend entirely on
