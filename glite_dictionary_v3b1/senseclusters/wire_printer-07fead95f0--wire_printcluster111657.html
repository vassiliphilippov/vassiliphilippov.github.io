wire printer

noun
impact printer using wires to form characters
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪər ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
an impact printer in which each character is formed by a pattern of dots produced by wires or styli

Examples

A wire printer machine
A wire printer is typically noisier than modern inkjet models.
Engineers repaired the wire printer after several wires became bent.
The old office used a wire printer for invoices because it could print on multipart forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dot matrix printer - printer using a matrix of dots to form characters and images
