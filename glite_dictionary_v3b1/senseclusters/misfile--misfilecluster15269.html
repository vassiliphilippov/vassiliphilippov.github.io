misfile

verb
to file incorrectly
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈfaɪl / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈfaɪl / (BrE)
to put a document or item in the wrong place or folder by mistake

Examples

To misfile a document
The assistant accidentally misfiled several important contracts in the wrong cabinet.
If you misfile these documents, it will take hours to find them again.
He realized he had misfiled the invoice after searching for it all morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: file - to organize or store documents in a particular order
  • synonym: misplace - to put something in the wrong location and lose track of it
