factor of safety

noun
engineering safety margin ratio
3.1 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfæk.tər əv ˈseɪf.tiː/ /; / /ˈfæk.tər ə ˈseɪf.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈfæk.tə əv ˈseɪf.tɪ/ /; / /ˈfæk.tə ə ˈseɪf.tɪ/ (BrE)
The ratio of the maximum stress that a structure or material can withstand to the maximum stress expected in normal use.

Examples

The factor of safety ratio
A higher factor of safety is often used in buildings located in earthquake-prone regions.
Engineers calculated a factor of safety of 2.5 for the new bridge design.
The factor of safety ensures that structures can withstand unexpected overloads.
