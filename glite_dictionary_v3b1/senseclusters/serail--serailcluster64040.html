serail

noun
women's quarters in a Muslim household
Pronunciation: / səˈraɪl / (AmE), / səˈraɪl / (BrE)
The section of a Muslim household, especially in former times, reserved for wives, concubines, and female relatives.

Examples

The serail section
In the Ottoman Empire, the serail was strictly off-limits to men who were not family members.
She wrote a novel set in a serail, exploring the lives of the women who lived there.
The architecture of the serail featured hidden gardens and elaborate courtyards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harem - the separate part of a Muslim household reserved for wives, concubines, and female servants
