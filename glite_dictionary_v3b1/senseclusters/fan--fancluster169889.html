fan

noun
enthusiastic admirer or supporter
1.0 121/m
Pronunciation: / fÃ¦n / (AmE), / fÃ¦n / (BrE)
a person who is very enthusiastic about and devoted to a particular person, group, team, or activity

Examples

A big fan of music
I've been a huge fan of her music since I was a teenager.
She's a big fan of science fiction movies and rarely misses a new release.
Thousands of fans cheered as the team walked onto the field.
Hundreds of fans waited outside the stadium to catch a glimpse of their favorite player.
She has been a loyal fan of the football team for over ten years.
The concert was sold out because so many fans wanted to see the band perform live.

Synonyms

  • enthusiast
    3.4 6.63/m
    an enthusiast studies and enjoys an activity deeply, while a fan is more casual and excited about a team or star.
  • devotee
    6.0 2.96/m
    a devotee shows intense and steady devotion, while a fan is often more casual and excited about teams or stars.
  • supporter
    1.0 47/m
    a supporter actively backs and helps a team or cause, while a fan mainly cheers and enjoys as an enthusiastic follower.
  • lover
    2.5 18/m
    a lover often means a romantic partner or someone with strong personal feelings, while a fan admires and follows a team or star.
