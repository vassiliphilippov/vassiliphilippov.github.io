PBS

noun
phosphate-buffered saline solution
Pronunciation: / pi bi ɛs / (AmE), / piː biː ɛs / (BrE)
a solution of phosphate-buffered saline, commonly used in biological research to maintain a constant pH.

Examples

A PBS solution
Always prepare fresh PBS to avoid contamination in cell culture work.
Dilute the sample in PBS to ensure stability during the experiment.
The cells were washed twice with PBS before staining.
