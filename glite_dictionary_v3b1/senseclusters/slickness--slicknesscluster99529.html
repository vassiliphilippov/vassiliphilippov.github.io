slickness

noun
superficial smoothness or fluency
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth, fluent, and often superficially attractive, sometimes suggesting a lack of depth or sincerity

Examples

Slickness in answers
His slickness when answering reporters' questions made him seem evasive rather than trustworthy.
She admired the slickness of the new ad campaign, even if it felt a bit superficial.
The lawyer's slickness in handling questions impressed the jury, but some doubted his sincerity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glibness - the quality of being fluent and insincere
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest or genuine
