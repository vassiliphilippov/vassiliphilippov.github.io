baryton

noun
18th-century string instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈbæɹɪˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈbærɪtɒn / (BrE)
a stringed musical instrument similar to the viol, popular in the 18th century and played with a bow

Examples

Play the baryton
The prince enjoyed playing the baryton at court concerts in the 18th century.
Many compositions for the baryton were written by Joseph Haydn.
A rare baryton was displayed in the museum's collection of historical instruments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cello - a large, low-pitched string instrument played with a bow
  • synonym: viol - a bowed string instrument from the Renaissance and Baroque periods
