Zhou dynasty

noun
Chinese dynasty, 1122–221 BC
Pronunciation: / ʒoʊ ˈdaɪ.næ.sti / (AmE), / dʒəʊ ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from around 1122 to 221 BC, notable for its cultural and philosophical developments, including the emergence of Confucianism and Taoism

Examples

The Zhou dynasty period
Artifacts from the Zhou dynasty have been discovered throughout northern China.
The Zhou dynasty is divided into the Western Zhou and Eastern Zhou periods.
The teachings of Confucius became influential during the Zhou dynasty.
