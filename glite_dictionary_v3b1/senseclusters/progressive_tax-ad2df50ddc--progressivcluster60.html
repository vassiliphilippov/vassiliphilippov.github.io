progressive tax

noun
tax with increasing rate as income rises
6.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈgre.sɪv tæks/ (AmE), /prə.ˈgre.sɪv tæks/ (BrE)
a tax system in which the tax rate increases as the amount subject to taxation rises

Examples

A progressive tax system
In a progressive tax system, those with lower incomes pay a smaller percentage in taxes.
Many economists believe a progressive tax helps reduce income inequality.
The government introduced a progressive tax to ensure higher earners pay a larger share.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regressive tax - tax where the rate decreases as the amount subject to taxation increases
