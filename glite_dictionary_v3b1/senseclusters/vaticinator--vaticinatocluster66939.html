vaticinator

noun
one who foretells the future
Pronunciation: / ˌvætɪˈsɪneɪtər / (AmE), / ˌvatɪˈsɪnətə / (BrE)
A person who foretells or predicts future events, especially with authority or as a profession.

The medieval court often sought counsel from its trusted vaticinator.
