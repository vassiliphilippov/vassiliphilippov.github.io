escarpment

noun
steep slope or cliff at plateau edge
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskɑːrmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈskɑːpmənt / (BrE)
a long, steep slope or cliff at the edge of a plateau or separating areas of land at different heights, typically formed by erosion

Examples

Rocky escarpment
A dramatic escarpment separates the highland from the vast plains below.
Erosion gradually shaped the limestone escarpment over millions of years.
The hikers paused to admire the view from the top of the escarpment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cliff - steep rock face, especially at the edge of the sea
