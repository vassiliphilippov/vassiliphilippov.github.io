Examples

Synonyms

  • scarp
    9.1 0.13/m
    scarp is a long, steep slope at a ridge or plateau edge, while escarpment refers to a larger, continuous slope formed by erosion.
  • rimrock
    8.5 0.11/m
    rimrock is a hard rock layer or cliff at a plateau's edge, while escarpment is the long slope that runs below such edges.
  • cliff
    1.0 30/m
    cliff is a high, steep rock face often by a sea or river, while escarpment is a long slope separating land at different heights.
  • precipice
    7.4 1.52/m
    precipice is a very steep, often overhanging rock face that feels dangerous, while escarpment is a long natural slope between land levels.
