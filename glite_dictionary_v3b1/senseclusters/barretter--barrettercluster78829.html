barretter

noun
resistor for compensating circuit changes
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɛtər / (AmE), / bəˈrɛtə / (BrE)
an electrical resistor used in a circuit to compensate for changes, especially due to temperature fluctuations

Examples

A barretter resistor
Because of its temperature-sensitive resistance, a barretter helps maintain consistent performance in electronic equipment.
Older radios sometimes used a barretter to deal with voltage fluctuations.
The engineer replaced the faulty barretter to stabilize the current in the circuit.
