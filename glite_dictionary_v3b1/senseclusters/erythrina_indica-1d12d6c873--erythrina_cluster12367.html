Erythrina indica

noun
thorny Asian/Australian tree with scarlet or crimson flowers
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɪθrɪnə ɪnˈdaɪkə / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɪnə ɪnˈdɪkə / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized thorny tree native to tropical Asia and northern Australia, with dense clusters of scarlet or crimson flowers and black seeds.

Examples

Erythrina indica tree
Farmers often plant Erythrina indica as a protective hedge because of its sharp thorns.
The Erythrina indica brightened the garden with its vibrant, crimson blossoms.
The seeds of Erythrina indica are sometimes used in handcrafts due to their striking color.
