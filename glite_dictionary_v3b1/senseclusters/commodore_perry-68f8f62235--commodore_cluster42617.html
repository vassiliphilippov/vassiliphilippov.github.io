Commodore Perry

proper noun
U.S. naval officer, victorious at Lake Erie (1812)
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːməˌdɔr ˈperi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmədəˌdɔː ˈperi / (BrE)
(Oliver Hazard Perry) A United States naval officer who led American forces to victory over the British at the Battle of Lake Erie during the War of 1812.

Examples

Commodore Perry victory
Commodore Perry achieved a decisive victory at the Battle of Lake Erie during the War of 1812.
Many historians credit Commodore Perry with changing the course of naval warfare in North America.
Schoolchildren learn about Commodore Perry and his famous message, 'We have met the enemy and they are ours.'
