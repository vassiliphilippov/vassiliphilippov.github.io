trihydrate

noun
compound with three waters
7.2
Pronunciation: / traɪˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound that contains three molecules of water combined with another substance

Examples

A trihydrate compound
The antibiotic is often prescribed as a trihydrate to enhance its effectiveness.
Copper(II) sulfate trihydrate is commonly used in laboratory experiments.
Storing the chemical as a trihydrate helps prevent it from decomposing quickly.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dihydrate - a compound with two molecules of water
  • degree lower: monohydrate - a compound with one molecule of water
  • degree higher: tetrahydrate - a compound with four molecules of water
