overtravel

verb
to exceed stop limit
8.4
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈtræv.əl/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.træv.əl/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈtræv.əl/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.træv.əl/ (BrE)
to move a machine part or mechanism beyond its intended stopping point or limit

Examples

To overtravel a lever
If you overtravel the lever, it may damage the internal components of the machine.
The engineer designed a stop to prevent the part from overtraveling during operation.
Careful adjustment is needed so the mechanism does not overtravel and cause a malfunction.
