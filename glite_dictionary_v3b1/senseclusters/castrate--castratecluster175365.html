castrate

verb
deprive of strength or effectiveness
Pronunciation: / ˈkæˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkas.treɪt / (BrE)
to render something powerless or ineffective by depriving it of essential strength or vigor, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

To castrate a movement
Critics argue that censorship can castrate artistic expression, leaving only bland and safe ideas.
Some believe that too many compromises have castrated the original intent of the proposal.
The new regulations will castrate the committee's ability to conduct independent reviews.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emasculate - to make something weaker or less effective; to deprive of strength
