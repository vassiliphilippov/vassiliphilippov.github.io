nonepileptic

noun
seizure-like episode
8.2
Pronunciation: /nʌn.ə.pɪ.ˈlep.tɪk/ /; / /nʌn.e.pɪ.ˈlep.tɪk/ (AmE), /nɒn.ə.pɪ.ˈlep.tɪk/ /; / /nɒn.e.pɪ.ˈlep.tɪk/ (BrE)
an event or episode that resembles an epileptic seizure but is not caused by abnormal electrical activity in the brain

Examples

A nonepileptic event
The neurologist determined that the patient had experienced a nonepileptic event rather than a true seizure.
After several tests, the episodes were classified as nonepileptic and not related to epilepsy.
It is important to distinguish between nonepileptic and epileptic seizures for proper treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epileptic - an event or seizure caused by abnormal brain activity
  • synonym: psychogenic seizure - a seizure-like episode with psychological rather than neurological causes
