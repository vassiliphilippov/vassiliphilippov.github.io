suffocate

verb
to prevent breathing; cause to suffocate
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌfəˌkeɪt /; / ˈsʌfəkeɪt / (BrE)
to be unable to breathe or to die because there is not enough air; to make someone unable to breathe

Examples

To suffocate a person
He tried to suffocate the fire with a blanket to cut off its air supply.
If you block their airways, fish can easily suffocate out of water.
The smoke from the burning building began to suffocate the trapped residents.
During the fire, several people began to suffocate due to the thick smoke.
He was trapped underwater and started to suffocate before the rescuers pulled him out.
If you cover your mouth and nose tightly, you may start to suffocate.
Plastic bags can cause young children to suffocate if left within their reach.
The murderer tried to suffocate his victim by covering her mouth and nose.
The victim was unable to escape the smoke and began to suffocate in the burning building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphyxiate - to cause someone to stop breathing by depriving them of oxygen
  • antonym: breathe - to take air into the lungs and expel it
  • synonym: choke - to have difficulty breathing because of a blockage in the throat
  • synonym: smother - to kill by covering the nose and mouth, preventing breathing
  • synonym: stifle - to feel unable to breathe freely; to suffocate slightly
