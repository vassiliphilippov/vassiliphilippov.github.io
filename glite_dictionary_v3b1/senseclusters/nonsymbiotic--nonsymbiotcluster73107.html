nonsymbiotic

adjective
not living in symbiosis
4.3 6.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.sɪm.baɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /nɒn.sɪm.baɪ.ˈɒt.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of an organism) not living in symbiosis with another; not parasitic or dependent on another organism.

Examples

A nonsymbiotic relationship
In a nonsymbiotic relationship, neither organism relies on the other for survival.
Some species of algae are entirely nonsymbiotic, thriving on their own in various environments.
The bacteria can exist in a nonsymbiotic state, living independently of plants or animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: symbiotic - living in symbiosis with another organism
