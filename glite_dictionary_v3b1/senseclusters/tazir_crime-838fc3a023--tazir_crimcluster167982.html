Tazir crime

noun
minor offense under Islamic law, judge decides penalty
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɑːˈziːr kraɪm / (AmE), / tæˈzɪə kraɪm / (BrE)
(in Islamic law) a minor offense for which punishment is not prescribed in the Koran, allowing the judge discretion in determining the penalty.

Examples

Tazir crime punishment
In some jurisdictions, a judge may impose flexible penalties for Tazir crime.
Modern legal systems in some Muslim countries have codified Tazir crimes in their statutes.
Unlike hudud offenses, a Tazir crime is not subject to a fixed punishment.
