unteachable

adjective
unable to learn
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtiːtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtiːtʃəbl / (BrE)
unable or unwilling to learn or be taught new things

Examples

Unteachable student
The teacher refused to believe that any child was truly unteachable.
Despite many attempts, the dog seemed completely unteachable when it came to following commands.
Some people are considered unteachable because they resist new ideas and feedback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incorrigible - impossible to correct or reform
  • antonym: teachable - able and willing to learn or be taught
