redeem

verb
pay off a loan or debt
3.0 5.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdim / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːm / (BrE)
to pay off a debt, loan, or financial instrument, or to buy back or regain something by payment

Examples

To redeem a loan
He worked overtime for months to redeem the loan before the due date.
She was finally able to redeem her promissory note with a single payment.
The couple is planning to redeem their mortgage next year.
Investors lined up to redeem their certificates when the company announced a buyback.
The coupon may be redeemed for cash at participating stores until the expiration date.
You can redeem this bond at any bank branch for its face value.
He was able to redeem his watch from the pawn shop after paying the loan.
The company offered to redeem coupons in exchange for discounts on their products.
You can redeem your winning ticket for cash at any participating store.

Related Terms

  • antonym: default - to fail to pay a debt
  • antonym: forfeit - to lose or give up something as a penalty or consequence
  • synonym: reclaim - to recover or regain something previously lost or given up
  • synonym: repay - to pay back money that is owed
