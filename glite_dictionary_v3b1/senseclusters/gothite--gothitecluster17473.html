gothite

noun
iron oxide-hydroxide mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊθˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊθ.aɪt / (BrE)
A red, yellow, or brown mineral consisting of iron(III) oxide-hydroxide, commonly found as a component of rust.

Examples

Red gothite mineral
Due to its iron content, gothite is an important source of pigment in natural paints.
Scientists studied the reddish-brown streak left by gothite on a porcelain plate.
The mineral gothite is often found in the oxidized zones of iron ore deposits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hematite - a common iron oxide mineral often associated with rust
  • synonym: limonite - an amorphous form of hydrated iron oxide
