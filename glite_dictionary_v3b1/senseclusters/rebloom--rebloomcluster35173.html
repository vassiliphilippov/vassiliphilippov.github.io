rebloom

noun
second flowering
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbluːm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbluːm / (BrE)
a second or later flowering of a plant after its initial bloom in the same season

Examples

A late rebloom
The roses surprised everyone with a vibrant rebloom in late summer.
After careful pruning, the orchid produced a healthy rebloom in the fall.
Gardeners often hope for a rebloom to extend the beauty of their flower beds.
