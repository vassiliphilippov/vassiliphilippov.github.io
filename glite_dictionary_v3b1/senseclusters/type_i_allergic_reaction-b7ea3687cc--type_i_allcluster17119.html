type I allergic reaction

noun
allergy reaction appearing within minutes
Pronunciation: / taɪp wʌn əˈlɜrdʒɪk riˈækʃən / (AmE), / taɪp wʌn əˈlɜːdʒɪk rɪˈækʃən / (BrE)
an allergic reaction that occurs within minutes after exposure to an allergen in a person who has already been sensitized, typically involving symptoms such as hives, itching, or anaphylaxis.

Examples

A type I allergic reaction symptom
A type I allergic reaction often results in symptoms like itching or swelling minutes after exposure.
Doctors treated the patient for a type I allergic reaction following a bee sting.
Peanut allergies commonly trigger a type I allergic reaction in sensitive individuals.
