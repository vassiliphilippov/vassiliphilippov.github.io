dromedary

noun
one-humped camel of northern Africa and Asia
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɑː.məˌdɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈdrɒm.ɪ.d(ə)r.i / (BrE)
a species of camel with a single hump, found in the hot deserts of northern Africa and southwestern Asia

Examples

A dromedary camel
A dromedary can travel long distances in the desert with little water.
The dromedary is well adapted to survive in arid environments.
Unlike the Bactrian camel, the dromedary has only one hump.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Bactrian camel - camel species with two humps, native to Central and East Asia
