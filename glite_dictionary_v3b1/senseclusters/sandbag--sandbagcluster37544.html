sandbag

verb
hit forcefully, as with a sandbag
Pronunciation: / ˈsændˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈsan(d)bæɡ /; / ˈsan(d)ˌbaɡ / (BrE)
to hit, attack, or treat someone harshly or unfairly, often by surprise or underhanded means

Examples

To sandbag a witness
He claimed the witness was sandbagged during the cross-examination.
She felt as if she'd been sandbagged by the sudden announcement.
The boxer tried to sandbag his opponent with a surprise left hook.
Don't sandbag your team members during meetings; give them a fair chance to respond.
She felt sandbagged by the sudden negative feedback from her manager.
The company tried to sandbag its competitor by releasing misinformation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against someone or something
  • antonym: favor - to treat someone with special kindness or preference
  • synonym: mistreat - to treat someone badly or unfairly
