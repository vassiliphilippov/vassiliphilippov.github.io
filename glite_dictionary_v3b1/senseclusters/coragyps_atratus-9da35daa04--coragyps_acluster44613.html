Coragyps atratus

noun
species of American black vulture
Pronunciation: / kɔːˈrædʒɪps əˈtreɪtəs / (AmE), / kɒˈrædʒɪps əˈtrɑːtəs / (BrE)
A species of American vulture, commonly known as the black vulture, found in the southeastern United States to South America, smaller than the turkey vulture.

Examples

Coragyps atratus bird
Researchers studied the population growth of Coragyps atratus in urban areas.
The Coragyps atratus is often seen soaring in open country and feeding on carrion.
Unlike the turkey vulture, the Coragyps atratus has a shorter tail and wings.
