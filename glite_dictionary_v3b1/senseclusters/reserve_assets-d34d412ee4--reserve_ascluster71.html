reserve assets

noun
capital held as a financial safety reserve
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɝv ˈæsɛts / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv ˈæsɛts / (BrE)
capital or financial resources kept aside by an organization, government, or central bank to meet potential future demands or obligations

Examples

Reserve assets held
Central banks use reserve assets to stabilize the national currency in times of financial crisis.
Countries are required to report their reserve assets to the International Monetary Fund annually.
The company increased its reserve assets to prepare for possible economic downturns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reserves - funds set aside for future use or emergencies
