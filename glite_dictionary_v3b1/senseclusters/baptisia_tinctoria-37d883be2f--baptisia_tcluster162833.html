Baptisia tinctoria

noun
herbaceous plant with yellow flowers, native to eastern US
Pronunciation: / bæpˈtɪʃiə tɪŋkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪsɪə tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A North American herbaceous plant with many branches and bright yellow flowers, native to the eastern United States and known for its dye-producing properties.
