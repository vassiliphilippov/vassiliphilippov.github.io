saccharify

verb
convert to a simple sugar
8.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæk.ə.rəˌfaɪ /; / sæˈkæ.rəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsak.ə.rɪˌfaɪ /; / sæˈkæ.rɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to convert a substance into sugar or make it sweet, especially by a chemical or enzymatic process

Examples

To saccharify plant material
Cellulose in the plant material can be saccharified to produce bioethanol.
Enzymes are used to saccharify starch during the brewing process.
Scientists found an efficient way to saccharify complex carbohydrates in agricultural waste.
Certain enzymes can saccharify starches to produce glucose.
Chemists saccharify cellulose to obtain fermentable sugars for biofuel production.
The brewer explained how to saccharify the grains during the mashing process.
