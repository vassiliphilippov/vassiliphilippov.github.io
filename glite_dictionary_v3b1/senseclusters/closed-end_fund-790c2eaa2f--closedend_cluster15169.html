closed-end fund

noun
investment fund with a fixed number of tradable shares
1.4
Pronunciation: / kloʊzd ˈɛnd fʌnd / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈɛnd fʌnd / (BrE)
a regulated investment company that offers a fixed number of shares, which are traded on a stock exchange like stocks

Examples

A closed-end fund share
Many investors prefer a closed-end fund because its shares trade on the stock market.
She invested in a closed-end fund specializing in municipal bonds.
The manager explained how a closed-end fund differs from an open-end fund.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open-end fund - mutual fund that issues and redeems shares at any time
