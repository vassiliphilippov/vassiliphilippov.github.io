fustian

noun
strong cotton and linen fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌstiən / (AmE), / ˈfʌstɪən / (BrE)
a strong fabric made of cotton and flax, usually with a slight nap, used especially in the past for clothing and upholstery

Examples

Fustian jacket
During the Middle Ages, fustian became popular for making everyday clothing.
He wore a coat made of fustian, sturdy enough for work in the fields.
The merchant displayed bolts of fustian and velvet to the tailor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corduroy - a durable fabric, usually cotton, with a distinctive ribbed surface
