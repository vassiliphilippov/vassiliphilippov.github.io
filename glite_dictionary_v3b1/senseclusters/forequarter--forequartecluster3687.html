forequarter

noun
front half of a side of meat
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌkwɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌkwɔːtə / (BrE)
the front half of a side of animal carcass, especially in butchery, including the shoulder, brisket, and foreleg

Examples

A forequarter of lamb
She ordered a forequarter of lamb for the special family dinner.
The forequarter contains tender cuts such as the rib and brisket.
The butcher cut the forequarter of beef for roasting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hindquarter - the rear half of a side of meat
