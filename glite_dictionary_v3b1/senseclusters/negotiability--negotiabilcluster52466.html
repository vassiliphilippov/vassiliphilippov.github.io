negotiability

noun
ability to be traversed
7.4
Pronunciation: /nə.goʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ /; / /nɪ.goʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /nə.ɡəʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ /; / /nɪ.ɡəʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to be safely traversed, passed, or overcome, especially referring to routes or obstacles

Examples

Negotiability of a road
The negotiability of the mountain trail was questioned after the recent landslide.
Heavy rain reduced the negotiability of the country roads, making travel difficult.
Engineers assessed the negotiability of the river crossing before allowing vehicles to pass.
