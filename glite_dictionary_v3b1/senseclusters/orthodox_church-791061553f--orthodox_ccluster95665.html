Orthodox Church

proper noun
major Christian church of Byzantine tradition
3.5 3.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…îÀêrŒ∏…ôÀåd…ëks t É…ùÀêt É / (AmE), / Àà…îÀêŒ∏…ôd…íks t É…úÀêt É / (BrE)
The branch of Christianity that developed from the Byzantine Church and follows the Byzantine rites, comprising especially the Eastern Orthodox churches.

Examples

The Orthodox Church tradition
Many traditions in the Orthodox Church date back to the early centuries of Christianity.
She attended a service at the local Orthodox Church last Sunday.
The Orthodox Church celebrates Easter according to the Julian calendar.

Synonyms

    3.7 0.03/m
    Eastern Orthodox Church is the group of churches from the Byzantine tradition, while Orthodox Church names that same branch following Eastern rites.
  • Orthodox Catholic Church
    5.3 0.00/m
    Orthodox Catholic Church is an older formal name used for Eastern Orthodox communities, while Orthodox Church is the common modern term.
  • Eastern Orthodox
    5.7 0.43/m
    Eastern Orthodox refers to the beliefs or people of that tradition, while Orthodox Church names the religious body or institution.
  • Eastern Church
    4.6 0.10/m
    Eastern Church refers broadly to churches from the Byzantine tradition and may include different groups, while Orthodox Church means the specific Eastern Orthodox branch.
