Orthodox Church

proper noun
major Christian church of Byzantine tradition
3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.θə.dɑːks tʃɜːrtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.θə.dɒks tʃɜːtʃ/ (BrE)
The branch of Christianity that developed from the Byzantine Church and follows the Byzantine rites, comprising especially the Eastern Orthodox churches.

Examples

The Orthodox Church tradition
Many traditions in the Orthodox Church date back to the early centuries of Christianity.
She attended a service at the local Orthodox Church last Sunday.
The Orthodox Church celebrates Easter according to the Julian calendar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - The largest single Orthodox church, distinguishing it from Oriental Orthodoxy
  • antonym: Protestant Church - Churches that separated from or reformed against the Roman Catholic Church
  • antonym: Roman Catholic Church - The Western Christian church headed by the Pope
