indexicality

noun
linguistic social signaling
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɛksɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɛksɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a word, expression, or feature that points to or signals social meanings such as identity, class, or stance

Examples

Indexicality of an accent
The indexicality of her accent revealed her hometown to everyone in the room.
Researchers study indexicality to understand how language reflects social identity.
In sociolinguistics, indexicality helps explain how speech signals group membership.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indexical - a linguistic form that points to a particular meaning or context
  • antonym: referentiality - the quality of referring to something specific
