sterilant

noun
agent for sterilization
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛrələnt / (AmE), / ˈstɛrɪlənt / (BrE)
a chemical or agent used to destroy all microorganisms and make something completely sterile

Examples

A sterilant chemical
The laboratory uses a strong sterilant to ensure all equipment is free from bacteria and spores.
Before the procedure, the nurse applied a sterilant to the surgical instruments.
Some sterilants are too harsh for use on skin but are effective for cleaning medical devices.
The hospital uses a powerful sterilant to clean surgical instruments before every operation.
A chemical sterilant was applied to the soil to eliminate harmful bacteria and fungi.
Proper use of a sterilant is essential in laboratories to prevent contamination of experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiseptic - a substance that prevents the growth of microorganisms on living tissue
  • synonym: disinfectant - a substance used to kill most harmful microorganisms, but not necessarily all spores
