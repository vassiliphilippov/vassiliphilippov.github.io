squeeze out

verb
force out by pressure or competition
2.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /skwiːz aʊt/ (AmE), /skwiːz aʊt/ (BrE)
to force someone or something out of a position, place, or situation, often by applying pressure or creating difficult conditions

Examples

To squeeze out a rival
Many small businesses were squeezed out by the arrival of large retail chains.
She felt squeezed out of the conversation by her more outspoken colleagues.
The coach squeezed out the team's weakest players during the final selection.
