gentlefolk

noun
people of good family and social status
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛntəlˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntl̩fəʊk / (BrE)
people of good family and breeding, typically considered to have high social status

Examples

The gentlefolk gathered
At the gathering, the gentlefolk were seated at the front, while others filled the back rows.
Stories about English gentlefolk often appear in nineteenth-century novels.
The old mansion once housed a family of gentlefolk who played an important role in the town's history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commoner - a person without noble rank or title
  • synonym: gentry - people of good social position, below the nobility
