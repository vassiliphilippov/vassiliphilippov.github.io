tonsilla pharyngealis

noun
the pharyngeal tonsil in the upper throat
9.0
Pronunciation: /tɑːnˈsɪl.ə fə.rɪnˈdʒiː.ə.lɪs/ /; / /tʌnˈsɪl.ə fə.rɪnˈdʒiː.ə.lɪs/ /; / /tɑːnˈsɪl.ə fə.rɪnˈdʒɪə.lɪs/ (AmE), /tɒnˈsɪl.ə fə.rɪnˈdʒɪə.lɪs/ /; / /tɒnˈsɪl.ə fə.rɪnˈdʒiː.ə.lɪs/ (BrE)
the collection of lymphatic tissue located on the posterior wall and roof of the nasopharynx, behind the uvula; also known as the pharyngeal tonsil or adenoids

Examples

The tonsilla pharyngealis tissue
Doctors may recommend removal of the tonsilla pharyngealis if it causes breathing difficulties in children.
The tonsilla pharyngealis is located at the back of the nasal cavity above the soft palate.
When the tonsilla pharyngealis becomes enlarged, it is commonly referred to as adenoids.
