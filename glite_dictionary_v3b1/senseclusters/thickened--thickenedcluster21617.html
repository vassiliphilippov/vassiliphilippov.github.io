thickened

adjective
made thicker in consistency
1.9 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈθɪk.ənd/ (AmE), /ˈθɪk.ənd/ (BrE)
having become thick or thicker, usually as a result of a process or added substance

Examples

The paint thickened
Over time, the paint in the can thickened and became harder to use.
She added flour to the sauce until it became thickened and smooth.
The soup was thickened with cornstarch to improve its texture.
He examined the patient's thickened arterial walls on the scan.
Heavy rain left the roads covered in thickened mud.
The sauce became thickened after simmering for an hour.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diluted - made thinner or less concentrated
  • degree lower: thick - having a relatively large distance between opposite sides; not thin
  • antonym: thin - having little thickness, the opposite of thick
