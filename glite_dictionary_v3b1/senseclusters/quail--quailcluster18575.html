quail

verb
shrink back in fear
7.2
Pronunciation: / kweɪl / (AmE), / kweɪl / (BrE)
to feel or show fear; to shrink back in fear or apprehension

Examples

To quail in fear
Despite his bold words, he began to quail at the thought of facing his accuser.
Many students quail before their first major public presentation.
She did not quail in the face of danger, but met the crisis with courage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brace - to prepare (oneself) for something difficult or unpleasant
  • synonym: flinch - to make a quick, nervous movement as a reaction to fear or pain
  • synonym: wince - to make a slight involuntary movement due to pain or distress
