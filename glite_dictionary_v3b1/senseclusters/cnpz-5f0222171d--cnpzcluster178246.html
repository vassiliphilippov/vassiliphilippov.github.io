CNPZ

noun
Bolivian Marxist-Leninist terrorist group
Pronunciation: / siː ɛn pi zi / (AmE), / siː ɛn piː zɛd / (BrE)
A Marxist-Leninist terrorist organization in Bolivia serving as an umbrella group for indigenous subversive groups; revived from the original organization established by Che Guevara in the 1960s.

Examples

CNPZ terrorist group
Membership in the CNPZ was composed mainly of indigenous activists influenced by Marxist-Leninist principles.
Scholars have analyzed the ideology and tactics of the CNPZ in relation to other Latin American insurgent movements.
The Bolivian government cracked down on the CNPZ after a series of attacks in the late 1990s.
