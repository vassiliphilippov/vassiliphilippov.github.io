Biblical Latin

noun
form of Latin used in early Christian texts
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪblɪkəl ˈlætɪn /; / ˈbɪb.lɪ.kəl ˈlæt.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɪblɪkəl ˈlatɪn /; / ˈbɪb.lɪ.kəl ˈlæ.tɪn / (BrE)
the form of Latin used in Christian writings and translations of the Bible, especially from the 3rd to the 8th centuries

Related Terms

  • antonym: Classical Latin - the standardized form of Latin used in formal Roman literature
  • synonym: Ecclesiastical Latin - the form of Latin used in the Roman Catholic Church
  • degree lower: Vulgar Latin - the informal spoken form of Latin used by the common people
