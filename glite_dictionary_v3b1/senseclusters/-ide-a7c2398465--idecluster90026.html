a suffix used in chemistry to name negative ions (anions) and simple binary compounds, especially with nonmetals

Examples

The suffix -ide in chemistry
In sodium chloride, the suffix -ide indicates the presence of the chloride ion.
The name 'oxide' uses the -ide ending to show it is a negative oxygen ion.
Chemists add -ide to the root of an element to name its simple anion.
The element sodium forms the compound sodium chloride, where '-ide' shows it is a binary compound.
In chemistry class, we learned that '-ide' is added to the name of the second element in a compound like carbon dioxide.
The suffix -ide appears in words such as 'fluoride' and 'oxide' to indicate certain chemical compounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -ate - a suffix for the names of certain chemical ions, often oxyanions
  • synonym: -ite - a suffix for the names of certain chemical ions, usually with less oxygen than -ate ions
