faddle

verb
to fuss aimlessly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfædəl / (AmE), / ˈfædəl / (BrE)
to waste time on trivial or unimportant matters; to fuss or busy oneself aimlessly

Examples

To faddle with papers
Instead of starting his homework, he continued to faddle with his pens and papers.
She tends to faddle around the kitchen when she is nervous about guests arriving.
If you faddle all morning, you will never finish your chores on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dawdle - to waste time or move slowly
  • antonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort
  • synonym: fuss - to show unnecessary or excessive concern about something
