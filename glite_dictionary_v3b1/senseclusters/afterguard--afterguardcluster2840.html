afterguard

noun
crew at ship's stern
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæftərˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəɡɑːd / (BrE)
the members of a ship's crew responsible for working at the stern, especially handling sails and steering

Examples

The afterguard crew
The experienced afterguard quickly adjusted the sails as the wind changed direction.
During the storm, the captain relied on the afterguard to keep the ship steady.
Training for the afterguard included learning how to handle ropes and steer the vessel.
