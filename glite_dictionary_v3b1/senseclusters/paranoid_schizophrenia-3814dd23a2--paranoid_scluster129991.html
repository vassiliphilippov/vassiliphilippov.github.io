paranoid schizophrenia

noun
a type of schizophrenia with prominent delusions
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˌnɔɪd skrɪtsəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˌpærənɔɪd skrɪtsəˈfriːnɪə / (BrE)
a subtype of schizophrenia marked by prominent delusions, particularly of persecution or grandeur, often accompanied by suspiciousness and anxiety

Examples

Paranoid schizophrenia diagnosis
Her diagnosis of paranoid schizophrenia led to a careful evaluation of both her mental health and support needs.
People living with paranoid schizophrenia can sometimes function normally between episodes of delusional thinking.
The doctor explained that paranoid schizophrenia often involves intense feelings of persecution and suspicion.
