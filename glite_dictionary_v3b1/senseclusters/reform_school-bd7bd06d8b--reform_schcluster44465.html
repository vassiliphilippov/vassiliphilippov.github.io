reform school

noun
institution for juvenile offenders
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrm skuːl / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm skuːl / (BrE)
an institution for the detention, discipline, and training of young or first-time offenders as a corrective measure

Examples

A reform school institution
After repeated run-ins with the law, he was sent to a reform school for two years.
Many parents believed that reform schools would help steer their children onto a better path.
The reform school aimed to rehabilitate troubled teenagers and teach them vocational skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: juvenile detention center - place where young offenders are held in custody
  • degree higher: prison - institution where people are confined as punishment
