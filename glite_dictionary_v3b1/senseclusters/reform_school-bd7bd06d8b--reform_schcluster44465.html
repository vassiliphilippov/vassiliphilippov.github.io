reform school

noun
institution for juvenile offenders
6.8 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààf…îrm skuÀêl / (AmE), / r…™Ààf…îÀêm skuÀêl / (BrE)
an institution for the detention, discipline, and training of young or first-time offenders as a corrective measure

Examples

A reform school institution
After repeated run-ins with the law, he was sent to a reform school for two years.
Many parents believed that reform schools would help steer their children onto a better path.
The reform school aimed to rehabilitate troubled teenagers and teach them vocational skills.

Synonyms

  • reformatory
    8.3 0.16/m
    a reformatory is a more formal, older term for a correctional institution for young offenders, while reform school is the common everyday name.
  • training school
    6.4 0.03/m
    a training school focuses on education and rehabilitation for young offenders, while reform school stresses detention and discipline as corrective measures.
