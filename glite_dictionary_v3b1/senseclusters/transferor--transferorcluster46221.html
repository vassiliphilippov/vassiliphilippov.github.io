transferor

noun
person who transfers property/title
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfɛrər / (AmE), / trænsˈfɛːrɔː / (BrE)
(law) a person who conveys or transfers property, rights, or a legal title to another.

Examples

The transferor of property
If the transferor fails to disclose all encumbrances, the transaction may be voided.
The transferor must sign the deed in the presence of a notary.
The contract identified both the transferor and the transferee of the shares.
