dismissive

adjective
showing indifference or disregard
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
showing that you do not think someone or something is important or worth considering; showing indifference or lack of respect

Examples

Dismissive wave
He gave a dismissive wave of his hand and walked away.
Her dismissive attitude hurt the feelings of her colleagues.
The report was met with a dismissive response from the board.
He gave a dismissive wave of his hand and walked away from the meeting.
Her dismissive attitude made it clear she did not value our feedback.
The manager's response to concerns was surprisingly dismissive, making everyone feel unappreciated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contemptuous - showing scorn or a strong lack of respect
  • antonym: respectful - showing respect or consideration; opposite in attitude
