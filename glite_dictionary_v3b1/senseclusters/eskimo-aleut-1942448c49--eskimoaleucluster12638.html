Eskimo-Aleut

noun
language family including Eskimo and Aleut
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛskɪmoʊ əˈluːt / (AmE), / ˌɛskɪməʊ əˈluːt / (BrE)
the family of languages that includes Eskimo (Inuit and Yupik) and Aleut, spoken primarily in the Arctic regions of North America and northeastern Siberia.

Examples

The Eskimo-Aleut language family
Linguists classify Inuktitut as part of the Eskimo-Aleut language family.
Many Eskimo-Aleut languages are spoken across the polar regions of North America and Siberia.
The Eskimo-Aleut family is distinct from neighboring Native American language groups.
