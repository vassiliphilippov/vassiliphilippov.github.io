hemiola

noun
musical rhythmic pattern
9.9
Pronunciation: /hə.ˈmiː.ə.lə/ /; / /he.mi.ˈoʊ.lə/ (AmE), /hə.ˈmiː.ə.lə/ /; / /he.mi.ˈəʊ.lə/ (BrE)
a musical or poetic rhythm where the usual grouping of beats is shifted to create a 3:2 effect (for example, two beats played in the time of three, or vice versa)

Examples

A hemiola rhythm
The choir clapped along to the lively hemiola in the final chorus.
Many Baroque composers used hemiola to add excitement to their music.
The sudden hemiola surprised the audience and changed the feel of the piece.
The composer introduced a hemiola in the final bars to add excitement to the piece.
Dancers adjusted their steps as the music shifted into a hemiola during the chorus.
In poetry, a hemiola can create a surprising and engaging change in rhythm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polyrhythm - the simultaneous use of two or more conflicting rhythms
  • synonym: syncopation - a shifting of the normal accent in music
