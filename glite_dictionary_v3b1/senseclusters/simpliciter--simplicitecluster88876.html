simpliciter

adverb
without qualification
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈplɪsɪtər / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪsɪtə / (BrE)
simply or without qualification, especially in legal or academic contexts

Examples

Dismissed simpliciter
The judge dismissed the case simpliciter, finding no grounds for further discussion.
In academic writing, a statement made simpliciter is presented without any conditions.
The contract was declared void simpliciter, with no exceptions allowed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolutely - completely or without restriction
  • antonym: conditionally - with limitations or qualifications
