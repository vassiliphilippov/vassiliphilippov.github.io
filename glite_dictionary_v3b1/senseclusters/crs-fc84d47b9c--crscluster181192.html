CRS

noun
tax information standard
9.5
Pronunciation: /siː ɑːr es/ (AmE), /siː ɑː es/ (BrE)
an international standard for the automatic exchange of financial account information between tax authorities to prevent tax evasion

Examples

CRS tax rules
Banks must comply with the CRS by sharing account details with tax authorities in participating countries.
The CRS aims to increase transparency and reduce opportunities for international tax evasion.
Many countries have adopted the CRS to improve cooperation in tax matters.
