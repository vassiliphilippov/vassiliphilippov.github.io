englobe

verb
to enclose completely
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡloʊb / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡləʊb / (BrE)
to completely surround or enclose something, especially in a spherical or rounded way

Examples

To englobe an object
The ancient artifact was carefully englobed in a transparent sphere for display.
Tiny air bubbles became englobed within the amber as it hardened over time.
The city was englobed by a thick layer of fog early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encase - to cover or surround completely
  • synonym: enclose - to surround on all sides
  • antonym: expose - to leave uncovered or unprotected
