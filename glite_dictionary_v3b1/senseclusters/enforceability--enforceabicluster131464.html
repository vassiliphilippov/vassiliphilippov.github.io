enforceability

noun
ability to be enforced
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌfɔrsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌfɔːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be enforced or made effective, especially by law or rule

Examples

The enforceability of rules
The enforceability of the new regulation was challenged by several companies.
Lawyers debated the enforceability of the contract in different jurisdictions.
Before signing, she asked about the enforceability of the agreement's terms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unenforceability - the quality of not being able to be enforced
  • synonym: validity - the state of being legally or officially acceptable
