albuminuria

noun
protein in urine (medical)
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌælb.jə.məˈnʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌal.bjʊ.mɪˈnjʊə.ri.ə / (BrE)
the presence of an abnormally high amount of protein, especially albumin, in the urine, often indicating a kidney disorder

Examples

Persistent albuminuria
Persistent albuminuria is often a sign of chronic kidney disease.
Routine tests revealed the presence of albuminuria in the patient's urine sample.
The doctor explained that albuminuria might require further investigation and treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proteinuria - the presence of too much protein in the urine
