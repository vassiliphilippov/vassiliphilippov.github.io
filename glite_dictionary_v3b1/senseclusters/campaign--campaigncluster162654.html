campaign

noun
video-game main story or missions
3.7
Pronunciation: /kæm.ˈpeɪn/ /; / /kəm.ˈpeɪn/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpeɪn/ /; / /kæm.ˈpeɪn/ (BrE)
(in video games) the main storyline or linked set of missions designed to be played solo or cooperatively, typically distinct from multiplayer modes.

Examples

Game campaign
The game offers a cinematic campaign that follows the hero's journey from start to finish.
They completed the cooperative campaign with three friends in just one weekend.
Players often replay old campaigns to find hidden endings and secret areas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multiplayer - game modes designed for multiple players competing or cooperating online or locally
  • synonym: single-player mode - the part of a game intended for one player, often narrative-focused and played alone
