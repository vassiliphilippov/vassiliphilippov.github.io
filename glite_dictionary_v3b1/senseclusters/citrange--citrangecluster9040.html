citrange

noun
hybrid citrus fruit (orange × trifoliate orange)
7.7
Pronunciation: / sɪˈtreɪndʒ / (AmE), / sɪˈtrɑːndʒ / (BrE)
a hybrid citrus fruit made by crossing a sweet orange with a trifoliate orange, often more aromatic and acidic than regular oranges

Examples

She added slices of citrange to her beverage to give it a unique, zesty flavor.
The citrange is often used to make tangy marmalades due to its high acidity.
