supergroup

noun
large taxonomic category
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌɡruːp / (BrE)
a high-level taxonomic category that unites several related major groups, especially among eukaryotes

Examples

Eukaryote supergroup
Scientists recently classified several protists into a new supergroup based on genetic evidence.
The supergroup includes organisms that were previously thought to be unrelated.
Understanding the relationships within this supergroup helps clarify the evolution of eukaryotes.
