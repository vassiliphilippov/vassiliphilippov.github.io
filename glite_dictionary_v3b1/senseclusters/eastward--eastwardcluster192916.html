eastward

adjective, adverb
toward the east
1.1 1.98/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêstw…öd /; / Ààistw…öd / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêstw…ôd / (BrE)
toward the east or facing/moving in the direction of the east

Examples

Eastward journey
Explorers moved eastward to discover new lands.
The birds travel eastward every spring in search of food.
The winds blew eastward across the open plains.
An eastward wind brought cool air from the ocean.
The eastward migration of the birds was observed every spring.
The train made an eastward journey across the state.

Synonyms

  • east
    1.0 21/m
    east is the basic compass direction used for places and maps, while eastward implies movement or facing toward the east.
  • eastbound
    2.2 0.10/m
    eastbound describes vehicles, trains, or roads that go east, while eastward focuses on general movement or facing toward the east.

Related Terms

  • synonym: east - the direction toward the rising sun
  • synonym: eastbound - (of a vehicle or journey) going toward the east
  • antonym: northward - toward the north
  • antonym: westward - toward the west
