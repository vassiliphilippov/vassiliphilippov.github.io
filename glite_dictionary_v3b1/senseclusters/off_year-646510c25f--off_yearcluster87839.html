off year

noun
year without major elections
9.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /ɔːf jɪr/ /; / /ɑːf jɪr/ (AmE), /ɒf jɪə/ /; / /ɔːf jɪə/ (BrE)
A year in which no major political elections are held, especially between years of general or presidential elections.

Examples

An off year in politics
Congress usually deals with less controversial legislation during an off year.
The mayor used the off year to focus on local projects and community outreach.
Voter turnout tends to be much lower in an off year compared to a presidential election year.
