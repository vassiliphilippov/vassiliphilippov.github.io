colossus

noun
a person who is very large or influential
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɑːsəs / (AmE), / kəˈlɒsəs / (BrE)
a person who is very large, strong, or extremely important or influential

Examples

A political colossus
Everyone admired the colossus for both his towering height and his extraordinary strength.
She faced corporate challenges like a colossus, unfazed by even the largest obstacles.
The legendary wrestler was a true colossus in the ring, intimidating all his opponents.
After decades of leadership, she became a colossus of the tech industry.
He was regarded as a colossus in the world of literature, admired by critics and readers alike.
The scientist was a colossus, his influence shaping generations of research.
