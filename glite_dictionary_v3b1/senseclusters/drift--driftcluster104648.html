drift

verb
change slowly or fluctuate (prices, rates)
Pronunciation: / drɪft / (AmE), / drɪft / (BrE)
(of prices, rates, etc.) to change slowly or gradually, especially in a way that is not controlled or planned.

Examples

Prices drift down
During the afternoon, the currency drifted downward against the dollar.
Interest rates often drift without strong central bank intervention.
Share prices may drift higher or lower depending on market sentiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluctuate - to change frequently, especially from one extreme to another
