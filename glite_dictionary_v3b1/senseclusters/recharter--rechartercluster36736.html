recharter

verb
to renew charter
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
to grant or renew a charter for an organization or entity

Examples

To recharter a club
The university decided to recharter the student organization after reviewing its achievements.
Every five years, the committee must vote to recharter the local club.
Without enough members, the group was unable to recharter for the upcoming year.
The club decided to recharter for another year to continue its activities.
After a successful trip, the company plans to recharter the yacht for future events.
The school board voted to recharter the organization under new guidelines.
