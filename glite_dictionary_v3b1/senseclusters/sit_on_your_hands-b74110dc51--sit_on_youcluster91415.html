sit on your hands

verb
fail to take action; remain inactive
7.1 6.4
Pronunciation: /sɪt ɑːn jɔːr hændz/ /; / /sɪt ɑːn jʊr hændz/ /; / /sɪt ɑːn jɔːr hænz/ (AmE), /sɪt ɒn jɔː hændz/ /; / /sɪt ɒn jə hændz/ (BrE)
to take no action, especially when action is needed; to remain inactive or do nothing in a situation requiring involvement or response

Examples

To sit on your hands
Despite the worsening crisis, the committee continued to sit on their hands and refused to make decisions.
If we sit on our hands, these problems will only get worse.
Many citizens accused the government of sitting on its hands during the emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: do nothing - to not act or intervene in a situation
  • antonym: take action - to act or do something to achieve a result
