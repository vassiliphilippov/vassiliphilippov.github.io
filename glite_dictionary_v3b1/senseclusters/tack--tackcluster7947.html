tack

noun
nautical rope for adjusting a sail
9.9
Pronunciation: / tæk / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
(Nautical) a rope or chain attached to the lower corner of a sail, used to control its angle to the wind.

Examples

Sail tack rope
He adjusted the tack to better catch the wind as the ship changed direction.
Sailors released the tack during the maneuver to let the sail swing freely.
The captain ordered the crew to secure the tack before the storm hit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sheet - a rope used to control a sail on a boat
