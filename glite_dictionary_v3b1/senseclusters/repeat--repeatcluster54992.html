repeat

verb
do or say again
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpit /; / rɪˈpiːt / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːt / (BrE)
to do, say, or perform something again; for something to happen again

Examples

To repeat a question
Can you repeat what you just said more slowly?
He didn’t want to repeat the same mistake twice.
She had to repeat the experiment several times before getting the correct results.
Could you please repeat your last statement so everyone can hear it?
Every morning, she repeats the same routine before leaving for work.
He repeated the instructions to make sure we all understood.
In the second act, the orchestra repeats the opening theme with subtle variations.
The chorus is repeated several times to emphasize the song's central idea.
The composer decided to repeat the main melody at the end of the piece.

  • synonym: reiterate - to say something again or a number of times, usually for emphasis or clarity
  • synonym: replicate - to make an exact copy of; to repeat for verification
  • synonym: reprise - to repeat a passage in music
  • antonym: stop - to cease doing something
