terminalis

noun
end structure (biology)
Pronunciation: / tərˈmɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / təˈmɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a structure or part located at the end or tip of an organ, especially in biological contexts

Examples

Terminalis part location
The terminalis nerve is found at the front of the brain in some animals.
Researchers studied the terminalis segment of the plant root to understand its growth.
Damage to the terminalis region can affect the function of the entire organ.
