clipping

noun
limiting gradient magnitude (machine learning)
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(in machine learning) the practice of limiting the size (magnitude) of gradients during training, to prevent unstable updates such as exploding gradients.

Examples

Clipping gradients
To stabilize training, researchers applied clipping to the gradients during backpropagation.
Gradient clipping prevented the loss from skyrocketing when the network's parameters exploded.
Many tutorials recommend norm-based clipping as a simple fix for exploding gradients in RNNs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clip - to cut off or limit; used figuratively in computing for restricting numerical values
  • antonym: exploding gradient - a problem in neural-network training where gradients become excessively large, which clipping is used to prevent
