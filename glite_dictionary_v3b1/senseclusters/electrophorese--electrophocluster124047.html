electrophorese

verb
to move particles electrically
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrəfəˈris / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəfəˈriːz / (BrE)
to move or separate charged particles or molecules in a medium using an electric field, especially in laboratory experiments

Examples

The lab technician will electrophorese the DNA samples to analyze their composition.
Researchers often electrophorese molecules to study their electrical properties in different solutions.
The technician will electrophorese the DNA samples to analyze their size.
After preparing the gel, she carefully electrophoresed the proteins for the experiment.
Researchers often electrophorese different substances to study their properties in the lab.
