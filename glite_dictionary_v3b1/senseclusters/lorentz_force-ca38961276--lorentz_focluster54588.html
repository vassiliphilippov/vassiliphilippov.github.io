Lorentz force

noun
force on moving charge in a magnetic field
8.7
Pronunciation: / ləˈrɛnts fɔrs / (AmE), / lɒˈrɛnts fɔːs / (BrE)
the force experienced by an electric charge moving through a magnetic and electric field, acting at right angles to both the velocity of the charge and the magnetic field

Examples

The Lorentz force on charge
The Lorentz force determines the path of charged particles in a cyclotron.
The Lorentz force is fundamental to the operation of electric motors.
When a wire carries current through a magnetic field, it experiences the Lorentz force.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic force - the combined force of electric and magnetic fields on charges
