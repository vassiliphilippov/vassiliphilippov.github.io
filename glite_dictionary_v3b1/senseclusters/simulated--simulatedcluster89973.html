simulated

adjective
made to imitate something real
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
made to imitate or look like something real; artificial or reproduced to resemble the real thing

Examples

Simulated enemy attacks
During the exercise, troops responded to simulated enemy attacks.
Medical students practice procedures on simulated patients before treating real ones.
The pilot trained for hours in a simulated cockpit environment.
He wore a watch with a simulated leather band that looked surprisingly authentic.
The decor used simulated wood panels to create a rustic atmosphere without real timber.
The training involved simulated emergency situations to prepare staff for real-life crises.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genuine - actually existing as something real; not imitation
  • synonym: imitation - something made as a copy of something else
  • antonym: real - actually existing, not imitated or artificial
