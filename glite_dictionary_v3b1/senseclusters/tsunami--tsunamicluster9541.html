tsunami

noun
large sea wave caused by seismic activity
3.2 4.0
Pronunciation: /tsuː.ˈnɑː.miː/ /; / /suː.ˈnɑː.miː/ (AmE), /tsuː.ˈnɑː.miː/ (BrE)
A long, high sea wave caused by an earthquake or other disturbance under the sea, often resulting in great destruction when it reaches land.

Examples

A tsunami wave
Scientists are developing early warning systems to predict when a tsunami might strike.
The coastal city was devastated by a powerful tsunami after the undersea earthquake.
The museum displayed pictures of a historic tsunami that altered the region forever.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tidal wave - large sea wave especially as caused by an underwater disturbance; sometimes used interchangeably with tsunami
