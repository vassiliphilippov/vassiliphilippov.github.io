overstir

verb
to mix too much
7.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàst…úr / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàst…úÀê / (BrE)
to mix a substance too much, often resulting in a negative effect on its texture or quality

Examples

To overstir the batter
If you overstir the pancake batter, the pancakes may become tough and chewy.
Be careful not to overstir the soup, or it could lose its texture.
The recipe warns that overstirring can make the cake dense instead of light and fluffy.

Synonyms

  • overmix
    3.9 0.11/m
    overmix means mixing ingredients too much in general, often by beating or folding, while overstir means doing so by stirring.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: stir - to mix a substance by moving it around with a spoon or similar tool
  • antonym: undermix - to mix a substance too little
