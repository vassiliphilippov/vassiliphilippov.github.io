propene

noun
a flammable gaseous hydrocarbon used in chemistry
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌpiːn / (AmE), / ˈprəʊpiːn / (BrE)
A flammable gaseous hydrocarbon obtained by cracking petroleum, used in organic synthesis.

Examples

Propene gas
Propene is essential for the production of polypropylene plastics.
By cracking petroleum, manufacturers can obtain propene for various chemical processes.
The laboratory stores propene in specialized pressurized cylinders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propylene - another name for propene, a flammable gas used in organic synthesis
