Triceratops

noun
genus of horned prehistoric dinosaurs
6.9
Pronunciation: / traɪˈsɛrəˌtɑps / (AmE), / traɪˈsɛrətɒps / (BrE)
a genus of large, herbivorous horned dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period, characterized by three facial horns and a large bony frill at the back of the head

Examples

The Triceratops dinosaur
Many children can easily recognize a Triceratops thanks to its prominent facial features.
Paleontologists discovered a nearly complete Triceratops skeleton in the badlands last year.
The Triceratops was known for its distinctive three horns and large frill.

Related Terms
