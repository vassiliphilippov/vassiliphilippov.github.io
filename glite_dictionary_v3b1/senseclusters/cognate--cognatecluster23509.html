cognate

noun
word sharing ancestry with another
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
a word that has the same linguistic derivation as another; a word descended from the same ancestral language as another word

Examples

A cognate word
Latin 'pater' and English 'father' are cognates.
Linguists study cognates to trace the history of languages.
The English word mother is a cognate of the German word 'Mutter'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: derivative - a word formed from another word or base
