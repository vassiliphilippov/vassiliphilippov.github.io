congestive

relating to or affected by an excessive accumulation of blood or other fluid in a body part or organ, often leading to swelling or impaired function.

Examples

Congestive heart failure
A congestive liver can result from chronic heart disease.
Swelling in the ankles can indicate congestive symptoms in elderly patients.
The patient was diagnosed with congestive heart failure after showing signs of fluid retention.

Synonyms

  • edematous
    9.9 0.07/m
    edematous describes tissue that is medically swollen with fluid, while congestive refers to fluid build-up that harms an organ's function.
  • engorged
    7.9 0.12/m
    engorged means very swollen with blood or fluid and often looks overfull, while congestive implies fluid build-up that impairs an organ.
  • swollen
    3.2 7.26/m
    swollen is a common word for something enlarged by fluid or injury, while congestive implies medical fluid build-up that reduces organ function.
