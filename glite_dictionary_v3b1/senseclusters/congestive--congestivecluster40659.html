congestive

adjective
involving abnormal fluid accumulation
7.1 5.6
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdʒes.tɪv/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdʒes.tɪv/ (BrE)
relating to or affected by an excessive accumulation of blood or other fluid in a body part or organ, often leading to swelling or impaired function.

Examples

Congestive heart failure
A congestive liver can result from chronic heart disease.
Swelling in the ankles can indicate congestive symptoms in elderly patients.
The patient was diagnosed with congestive heart failure after showing signs of fluid retention.
