drippy

adjective
leaking or dripping in drops
6.8 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdrɪp.iː/ /; / /ˈdrɪp.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdrɪp.iː/ /; / /ˈdrɪp.ɪ/ (BrE)
leaking, dripping, or slightly wet, especially from rain or moisture

Examples

Drippy paint
Her painting style is intentionally drippy, with colors running down the canvas.
The drippy faucet kept me awake all night with its constant noise.
We had to put a bucket under the drippy ceiling to catch the water.
After walking the dog on a drippy morning, I had to dry my shoes by the radiator.
She shook her umbrella before entering the hallway, leaving a drippy trail across the tiles.
The drippy weather made our picnic less enjoyable, but we stayed anyway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damp - slightly wet, often in an unpleasant way
  • synonym: drizzly - characterized by light rain; showery
  • synonym: leaky - allowing liquid or gas to escape; not sealed tightly
  • antonym: watertight - closely sealed so that no water can get in or out
  • degree higher: wet - covered with, or saturated with liquid; not dry
