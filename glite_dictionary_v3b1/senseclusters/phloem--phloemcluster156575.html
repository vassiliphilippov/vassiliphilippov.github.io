phloem

noun
nutrient-carrying plant tissue
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊ.əm / (AmE), / ˈfləʊ.əm / (BrE)
the tissue in plants that carries nutrients, especially sugars produced by photosynthesis, from the leaves to other parts of the plant

Examples

Plant phloem tissue
Damage to the phloem can prevent nutrients from reaching the roots and other organs.
The study focused on the movement of molecules through the phloem in flowering plants.
Unlike xylem, phloem transports sugars throughout the plant, ensuring energy reaches all tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: xylem - the plant tissue that carries water and minerals from roots to other parts
