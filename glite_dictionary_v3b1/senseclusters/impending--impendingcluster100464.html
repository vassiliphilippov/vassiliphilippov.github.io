(of something unpleasant or significant) about to happen soon; likely to occur in the near future.

Examples

An impending event
Rumors about an impending merger caused uncertainty among the employees.
She felt anxious about her impending job interview the next morning.
The country was preparing for the impending storm by stocking emergency supplies.

Synonyms

  • impendent
    7.8 0.00/m
    impendent is a less common form used in older texts for events about to happen, while impending is the usual modern adjective.
  • imminent
    4.8 9.12/m
    imminent means happening very soon and often feels more urgent, while impending suggests a near event that may be significant or unpleasant.
  • at hand
    3.2 2.92/m
    at hand means immediately near in time and use, while impending usually points to a coming event that feels threatening or important.
  • close at hand
    4.1 0.09/m
    close at hand suggests something will happen very soon and nearby, while impending often keeps a stronger sense of threat or weight.
  • approaching
    2.3 0.15/m
    approaching describes something moving toward a time or place, while impending focuses on an event that is likely and often unsettling.
  • forthcoming
    4.2 5.35/m
    forthcoming is a formal word for events that will appear soon or be available, while impending stresses a coming event that may be ominous.
  • in the offing
    5.6 0.81/m
    in the offing describes something likely to happen in the near future but often lacks the immediate, threatening tone of impending.
  • coming
    1.0 0.00/m
    coming is a general word for something expected soon and sounds neutral, while impending often implies seriousness or danger.
  • upcoming
    1.4 19/m
    upcoming refers to planned or scheduled events that will take place soon, while impending usually hints at an unwanted or serious event.
