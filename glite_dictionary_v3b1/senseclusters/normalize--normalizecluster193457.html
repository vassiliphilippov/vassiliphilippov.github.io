normalize

verb
make or restore normal; conform to a standard
3.9 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrməˌlaɪz /; / ˈnɔːrməlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something normal or standard, or to become normal or return to a normal state

Examples

To normalize a process
Efforts are underway to normalize data entry procedures across all departments.
Governments often try to normalize diplomatic relations after a period of conflict.
It took several hours for his blood pressure to normalize after the surgery.
After a turbulent year, the economy began to normalize once confidence was restored.
Doctors hope the patient's blood pressure will normalize after treatment.
Relations between the two countries took time to normalize following the conflict.
After an initial period of uncertainty, the situation began to normalise by the end of the year.
As the fever subsided, her body temperature started to normalise again.
It took several months for the market to normalise after the sudden crash.
