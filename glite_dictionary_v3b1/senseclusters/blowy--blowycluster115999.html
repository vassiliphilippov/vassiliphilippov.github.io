Synonyms

  • windy
    1.0 4/m
    windy describes places with a lot of wind or breezes, while blowy covers both strong winds and gentler breezes.
  • breezy
    1.3 0.7/m
    breezy means a place has light, pleasant winds, while blowy also includes stronger or windier weather as well.
  • gusty
    3.4 0.7/m
    gusty means wind comes in sudden, strong bursts, while blowy is broader and can mean steady or many breezes.
  • blustery
    4.4 0.7/m
    blustery describes loud, gusty wind that may feel rough, while blowy is more neutral and can be less noisy.
  • blustering
    6.0 0.09/m
    blustering means wind blows violently in sudden bursts, while blowy can mean either strong winds or just abundant light breezes.
  • blusterous
    7.9 0.00/m
    blusterous means stormy, violent wind that sounds noisy, while blowy can also mean simply windy or with soft breezes.
