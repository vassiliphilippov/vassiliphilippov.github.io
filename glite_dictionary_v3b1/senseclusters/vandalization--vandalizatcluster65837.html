vandalization

noun
deliberate property damage
5.8
Pronunciation: /væn.dəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /væn.dəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /væn.dəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /væn.dəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of deliberately damaging, destroying, or defacing property (including public, private, or digital property)

Examples

Deliberate vandalization of property
The city increased security after repeated vandalization of public monuments.
The vandalization of the school walls was reported to the police.
Community members volunteered to clean up after the vandalization of the playground equipment.
The vandalization of the public park left many benches broken and graffiti on the walls.
Online vandalization can disrupt important information and mislead readers.
After the vandalization of the school mural, students worked together to repaint it.
The vandalization of the school playground shocked the entire community.
After the vandalization of the statue, the city installed security cameras in the park.
The museum closed temporarily due to the vandalization of several valuable exhibits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destruction - the act of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • antonym: protection - the act of keeping something safe from harm or damage
  • antonym: restoration - the act of repairing or returning something to its original state
  • synonym: vandalism - the act of intentionally damaging or destroying property
