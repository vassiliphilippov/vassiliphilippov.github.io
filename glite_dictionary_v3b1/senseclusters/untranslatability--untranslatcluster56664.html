untranslatability

noun
impossibility to translate
Pronunciation: / ˌʌntrænzˌleɪtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌntrænzˌleɪtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible or extremely difficult to translate accurately into another language

Examples

The untranslatability of words
Some cultural concepts have a certain untranslatability that makes them unique to their language.
The untranslatability of poetry often challenges even the most skilled translators.
He discussed the untranslatability of emotions expressed through music compared to written words.
The untranslatability of certain idioms makes them difficult to explain in other languages.
Poetry often contains a degree of untranslatability due to its cultural references and wordplay.
Some words are famous for their untranslatability and unique meaning in their original language.

Related Terms

  • antonym: translatability - the quality of being able to be translated
