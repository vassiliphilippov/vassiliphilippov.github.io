blurry

adjective
indistinct; not clear in outline
3.6 3.35/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbl…ùÀê.i / (AmE), / Ààbl år.i / (BrE)
not clearly defined; indistinct or hazy in outline or appearance.

Examples

A blurry picture
After crying, her makeup looked blurry in the mirror.
His vision became blurry after he took off his glasses.
The photograph was too blurry to make out any details.

Synonyms

  • blurred
    3.4 0.13/m
    blurred is used for things with soft, unclear edges or pictures, while blurry describes a general lack of clear outline.
  • indistinct
    5.3 5.60/m
    indistinct looks vague or hard to see in shape or sound, while blurry focuses on a washed-out, unclear outline.
  • fuzzy
    3.7 5.97/m
    fuzzy feels soft or unclear around edges, often in pictures or memories, while blurry describes hazy outlines.
  • hazy
    3.9 3.45/m
    hazy looks dim like a thin mist or describes weak memories, while blurry describes poor, unfocused outlines.
  • foggy
    1.4 0.31/m
    foggy appears covered by mist or fog in weather, while blurry means lacking a clear or sharp outline.
  • unfocused
    4.2 0.11/m
    unfocused describes images not set to focus, often from a camera, while blurry is a more general unclear outline.
  • unsharp
    4.8 0.05/m
    unsharp names something without a sharp edge or image, while blurry implies a soft, indistinct outline or view.
  • muzzy
    8.9 0.00/m
    muzzy looks unclear or soft-edged and often means a confused head, while blurry only means an indistinct visual outline.
