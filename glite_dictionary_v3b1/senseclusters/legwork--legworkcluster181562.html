legwork

noun
routine preparatory work
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛɡˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡˌwɜːk / (BrE)
practical or routine work that involves effort, travel, or gathering information, often as preparation or investigation

Examples

Do the legwork
She did all the legwork for the research project before the team started writing.
The detective spent hours doing legwork to find new leads in the case.
If you want the job done right, you have to put in the legwork yourself.
The detective spent hours doing legwork to track down the missing person.
Before writing the article, she completed all the necessary legwork by visiting local businesses.
Good research often requires a lot of legwork to gather accurate information.

  • synonym: grunt work - dull or routine work that requires effort
  • synonym: paperwork - routine clerical or administrative work
