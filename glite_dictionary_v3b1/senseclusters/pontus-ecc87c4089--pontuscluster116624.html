Pontus

proper noun
Greek mythological personification of the sea
Pronunciation: / ˈpɒn.təs / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.təs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the ancient personification of the sea, regarded as the father of Nereus.

Examples

Pontus the sea god
Artists often depicted Pontus as a powerful figure emerging from the waves.
In ancient myths, Pontus was revered as the original sea god.
The genealogy of Greek gods lists Pontus as the father of numerous sea deities.
