shaitan

noun
evil spirit in Islamic belief
Pronunciation: / ʃaɪˈtɑːn / (AmE), / ʃaɪˈtɑːn / (BrE)
(in Islamic belief) a rebellious jinni or evil spirit who tempts or leads people astray.

Examples

An evil shaitan
He believed a shaitan was responsible for the series of misfortunes that befell his family.
In religious texts, the shaitan is often portrayed as a tempter leading people away from the right path.
Many stories in Islamic folklore warn of the dangers of listening to a shaitan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: angel - a benevolent spiritual being, often regarded as a messenger of God
  • synonym: demon - an evil spirit or devil, especially one thought to possess a person or act as a tormentor
