inquiring

adjective
eager to learn or know
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkwaɪrɪŋ /; / ɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
showing curiosity or an interest in learning new things or in finding out information; given to inquiry.

Examples

An inquiring mind
During the interview, her inquiring questions impressed the panel.
His inquiring nature led him to travel to distant countries in search of knowledge.
She has an inquiring mind that always wants to understand how things work.
The child looked at the museum exhibit with an enquiring expression on her face.
He asked several enquiring questions during the meeting to better understand the project.
Her enquiring mind led her to explore many different fields of study.
