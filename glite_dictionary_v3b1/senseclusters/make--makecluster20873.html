make

verb
cause someone to do something
3.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /meɪk/ (AmE), /meɪk/ (BrE)
to force, compel, or cause someone to do something

Examples

Make him study
His parents made him finish his homework before going out to play.
Severe weather made us cancel our travel plans.
The coach made the team run five extra laps after practice.
Cold weather can make you shiver if you're not wearing a coat.
Her illness made her stay home from school.
The teacher tried to make the students finish their homework before leaving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to let someone do something
  • synonym: coerce - to persuade or pressure someone to do something by using force or threats
  • synonym: force - to make someone do something by using physical strength, threats, or pressure
  • synonym: persuade - to induce someone to do something by reasoning or argument
  • antonym: prevent - to stop someone from doing something (opposite meaning)
