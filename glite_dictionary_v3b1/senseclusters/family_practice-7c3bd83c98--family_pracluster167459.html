family practice

noun
primary medical care for families
5.7 3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli ˈpræktɪs / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈpraktɪs / (BrE)
A medical practice that offers primary health care to people of all ages and both sexes, with a focus on the family as a unit.

Examples

Family practice doctor
After years of hospital work, Dr. Lee decided to open a family practice in her hometown.
Many people choose a family practice because it provides care for both children and adults.
The clinic recently expanded its family practice to include mental health services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: general practice - medical practice offering general healthcare to anyone; closely related but may not emphasize the family unit
  • degree higher: geriatrics - branch of medicine focused on elderly care
  • degree lower: pediatrics - medical practice focused on the care of children
