grade

noun
ground level or height
3.0
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪd / (AmE), / ɡreɪd / (BrE)
the degree of steepness or incline of a slope, road, or the level of the ground surface

Examples

A steep grade
The architect specified that the garage entrance should be at grade to improve accessibility.
The water table is just two feet below grade at that location, which affects the foundation design.
They installed drainage pipes to prevent flooding when the basement is below grade.
Engineers measured the grade of the new railway to ensure safety.
Snow on the sharp grade caused several trucks to lose traction.
The mountain pass had a very steep grade, making driving difficult.
