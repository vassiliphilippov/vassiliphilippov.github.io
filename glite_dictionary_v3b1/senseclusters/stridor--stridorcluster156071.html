stridor

noun
harsh whistling breathing sound
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈstraɪdə / (BrE)
A harsh, whistling, or grating sound heard when breathing in, caused by obstruction of the air passages, especially the trachea or larynx.

Examples

Harsh stridor sound
A nurse recognized stridor as a sign of airway obstruction.
Emergency treatment was needed because the patient developed severe stridor.
The doctor heard a distinct stridor when the child took a breath.
