facelessness

noun
absence of a face
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪsləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no face or facial features, either literally or as an artistic or symbolic effect

Examples

Facelessness in art
The facelessness of the crowd made it difficult to recognize anyone in the busy station.
Some modern sculptures use facelessness to represent the loss of personal identity.
In the story, the villain's facelessness made him even more mysterious and frightening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anonymity - the state of being unknown by name or identity
  • antonym: identity - the qualities or beliefs that make a person or group different from others
