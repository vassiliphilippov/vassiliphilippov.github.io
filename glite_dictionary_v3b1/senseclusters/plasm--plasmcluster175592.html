plasm

noun
protoplasm in germ cells carrying genetic material
Pronunciation: / ˈplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈplaz(ə)m /; / ˈplæzəm / (BrE)
the fluid substance inside a cell, including the living material that contains genes and cell components

Examples

Damage to the plasm can affect the entire function of the cell.
