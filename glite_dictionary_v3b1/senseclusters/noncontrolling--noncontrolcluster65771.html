noncontrolling

adjective
lacking authority
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈtroʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈtrəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
not having the power to direct, manage, or influence the actions or decisions of something or someone

Examples

Noncontrolling interest
The company sold its noncontrolling stake in the joint venture to focus on core operations.
A noncontrolling shareholder cannot make major decisions for the business.
Their noncontrolling interest means they have limited influence over the board's choices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: controlling - having the power to direct or manage
  • synonym: subordinate - lower in rank or authority
