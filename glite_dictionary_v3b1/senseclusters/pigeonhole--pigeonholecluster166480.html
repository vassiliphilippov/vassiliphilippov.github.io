pigeonhole

verb
classify unfairly; stereotype
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪdʒᵊnˌhoʊl /; / ˈpɪdʒənˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒᵊnhəʊl /; / ˈpɪdʒ.ən.həʊl / (BrE)
to classify or judge someone or something as belonging to a particular category, especially in a way that is too rigid or unfair

Examples

To pigeonhole people
It's unfair to pigeonhole people based on their appearance alone.
She didn't want her work to be pigeonholed as just another romance novel.
The committee was accused of pigeonholing innovative ideas without proper consideration.
It's unfair to pigeonhole children based on their early academic performance.
She hates being pigeonholed as just a romantic comedian.
Some critics pigeonhole his work as only suitable for young audiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classify - to arrange in categories
  • synonym: generalize - to make broad or overly simplistic judgments
  • synonym: label - to describe or classify in a simplistic way
  • synonym: stereotype - to judge or categorize someone based on oversimplified ideas
  • synonym: typecast - to always cast or regard a person as having only one type of character
