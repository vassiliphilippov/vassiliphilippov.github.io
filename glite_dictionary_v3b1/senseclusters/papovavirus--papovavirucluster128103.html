papovavirus

noun
virus group causing papillomas or polyomas
Pronunciation: / pəˌpoʊvəˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / pəˌpəʊvəˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
any of a group of viruses formerly classified together, associated with papillomas or polyomas in animals; includes viruses like papillomavirus and polyomavirus.

Examples

A papovavirus infection
A well-known example of a papovavirus is the human papillomavirus, which can lead to warts.
Researchers are studying how the papovavirus infects its host cells.
The papovavirus group includes viruses that cause tumors in animals.
