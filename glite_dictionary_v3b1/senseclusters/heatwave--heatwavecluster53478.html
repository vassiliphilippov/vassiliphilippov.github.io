heatwave

noun
period of extreme heat
3.0 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàhiÀêtÀåwe…™v / (AmE), / ÀàhiÀêtÀåwe…™v / (BrE)
a long period of unusually hot weather, often causing discomfort or danger

Examples

A heatwave period
During the heatwave, many people stayed indoors to avoid the extreme temperatures.
The heatwave caused power outages as everyone turned on their air conditioners.
Farmers worried that the heatwave would damage their crops and reduce the harvest.

Synonyms

  • heat wave
    3.2 1.68/m
    heat wave is the common two-word form used in weather reports, while heatwave often appears as one word in writing.
  • hot spell
    3.5 0.08/m
    hot spell is a more casual phrase for a short warm period, while heatwave suggests longer, dangerous hot weather.
