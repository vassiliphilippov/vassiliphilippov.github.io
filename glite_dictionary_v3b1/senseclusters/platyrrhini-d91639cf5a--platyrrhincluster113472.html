Platyrrhini

noun
group of New World monkeys
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌplætɪˈraɪnaɪ /; / ˌplætɪˈrɪnaɪ / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈrɪnɪ /; / ˌplætɪˈrɪniː / (BrE)
a group of primates called New World monkeys, native to Central and South America

Examples

Platyrrhini monkeys
All members of the Platyrrhini are native to Central and South America.
Scientists study the evolutionary adaptations of Platyrrhini to their rainforest habitats.
The Platyrrhini are distinguished from Old World monkeys by their widely spaced nostrils.
Marmosets and tamarins are both members of the Platyrrhini.
Scientists study Platyrrhini to understand primate evolution in the Americas.
The Platyrrhini are distinguished by their broad, laterally spaced nostrils.
