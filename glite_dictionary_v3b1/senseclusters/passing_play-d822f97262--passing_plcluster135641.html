passing play

noun
American football play involving a forward pass
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪŋ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪŋ pleɪ / (BrE)
(in American football) a play in which one player throws the ball forward to a teammate.

Examples

A passing play in football
On third down, the quarterback called a passing play to catch the defense off guard.
The team executed a risky passing play in the final seconds of the game.
Their offense relies heavily on the passing play to move the ball down the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pass - an act of throwing the ball to a teammate
  • antonym: running play - a play in which the ball is advanced by running rather than passing
