reflex

adjective
done automatically; involuntary action
4.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêÀåfl…õks / (AmE), / ÀàriÀê.fl…õks / (BrE)
(of an action) performed automatically and without conscious thought, especially as a response to a stimulus.

Examples

Reflex action
Blinking when something approaches your eyes is a reflex response.
The baby's grip on a finger is a natural reflex action.
The doctor noticed a reflex jerk in the patient's leg when he tapped the knee.

Synonyms

  • reflexive
    6.8 0.77/m
    reflexive is an automatic, involuntary bodily response, while reflex is a sudden action caused directly by a physical stimulus.
  • instinctive
    4.1 2.89/m
    instinctive is done from a natural drive or urge, while reflex is a fast automatic action triggered by a stimulus.
  • automatic
    3.7 3.62/m
    automatic means happening without thought or effort, often by habit or machine, while reflex is a quick involuntary response to stimulus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automatic - done or occurring without conscious thought
  • antonym: voluntary - done by choice or intention
