Bohemian waxwing

noun
a large species of waxwing bird
9.3
Pronunciation: /boʊ.ˈhiː.mi.ən ˈwæks.wɪŋ/ /; / /bəʊ.ˈhiː.mi.ən ˈwæks.wɪŋ/ (AmE), /bəʊ.ˈhiː.mi.ən ˈwæks.wɪŋ/ (BrE)
a large waxwing (Bombycilla garrulus) of northern North America and Eurasia, with greyish-brown plumage, a pointed crest, and red-tipped wing feathers

Examples

A Bohemian waxwing bird
A flock of Bohemian waxwings was spotted feeding on berries near the riverbank.
The Bohemian waxwing is known for its striking plumage and bright red tips on some wing feathers.
Unlike the cedar waxwing, the Bohemian waxwing is rarely seen in southern regions.
