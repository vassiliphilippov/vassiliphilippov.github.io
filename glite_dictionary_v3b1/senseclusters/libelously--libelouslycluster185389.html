libelously

adverb
in a defamatory way
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪbələsli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪbələsli / (BrE)
in a way that involves or constitutes libel, especially by making false and damaging statements about someone in writing

Examples

To libelously accuse
The magazine was sued for statements published libelously about the celebrity.
He accused the blogger of writing libelously to harm his reputation.
The company denied that it had acted libelously in its press release.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defamatorily - in a way that damages someone's reputation
  • synonym: slanderously - in a way that makes false spoken statements damaging to someone's reputation
  • antonym: truthfully - in a way that is honest and based on facts
