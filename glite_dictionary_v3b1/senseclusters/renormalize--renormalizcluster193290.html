renormalize

verb
adjust again to conform to a standard
4.7
Pronunciation: / riːˈnɔrməˌlaɪz /; / riːˈnɔːrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / riːˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make normal again or adjust something once more so that it conforms to a norm or standard, especially after an earlier normalization; commonly used in mathematics and physics.

Examples

To renormalize data
After the unexpected result, the researcher decided to renormalize the data for accuracy.
In quantum field theory, scientists often need to renormalize equations to avoid infinite values.
The team had to renormalize the pressure readings after calibrating the instrument.
After the data set was distorted, scientists decided to renormalise the results to allow proper comparison.
Quantum field theory often requires physicists to renormalise certain values to avoid infinite results.
To account for changes in experimental conditions, the team had to renormalise the measurements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deviate - depart from an established standard or norm
  • synonym: normalize - to make something conform to a standard or norm
