fentanyl

noun
powerful synthetic opioid analgesic
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛn.tə.nɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɛn.tə.nɪl / (BrE)
A powerful synthetic opioid used medically as a painkiller and anesthetic; also known for being highly addictive and sometimes misused illicitly.

Examples

Strong fentanyl painkiller
After surgery, the patient was administered fentanyl for pain relief under close medical supervision.
Due to its potency, fentanyl is prescribed only for severe pain that cannot be managed with other opioids.
The authorities warned that street drugs were being laced with fentanyl, increasing the risk of overdose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: morphine - a naturally occurring opioid pain medication
  • antonym: naloxone - a medication that blocks the effects of opioids
  • synonym: opioid - a class of drugs that includes synthetic narcotics and natural opiates
