Scipio

proper noun
Roman general; defeated Hannibal at Zama
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪpiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪpiːəʊ / (BrE)
(full name: Scipio Africanus) a Roman general who led the invasion of Carthage in the Second Punic War and defeated Hannibal at Zama (circa 237–183 BC).

Examples

Scipio Africanus
Scipio is remembered for his brilliant tactics against Hannibal in the Second Punic War.
Historians often compare Scipio to other great military leaders of antiquity.
The victory of Scipio at Zama changed the course of Roman history.
