occupation

noun
a person's job or profession
2.7 15/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…ëÀê.kj…ôÀàpe…™. É…ôn / (AmE), / Àå…ík.j äÀàpe…™. É…ôn / (BrE)
a job or profession; the principal activity that a person does to earn a living

Examples

Your occupation on forms
Please state your occupation on the application form.
She has worked in the medical occupation for over twenty years.
Teaching is a very demanding occupation that requires patience and dedication.

Synonyms

  • job
    1.0 562/m
    job is the regular paid position you do at a company or place, while occupation means your main work to earn a living.
  • line of work
    3.1 2.86/m
    line of work is an informal phrase for the type of work someone does, while occupation sounds more formal and general.
  • profession
    3.0 20/m
    profession is a skilled field that needs special training or study, while occupation covers any work someone does to earn money.
  • business
    1.3 27/m
    business often means the company or trade a person runs or works in, while occupation names their main job to earn a living.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unemployment - The state of not having a paid job
  • synonym: vocation - A person's employment or main occupation
