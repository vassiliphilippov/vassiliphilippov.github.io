serendipity

noun
the occurrence of fortunate, unexpected discoveries
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛrənˈdɪpɪti / (AmE), / ˌserənˈdɪpɪti / (BrE)
the occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way

Examples

Pure serendipity
By pure serendipity, she discovered her favorite author lived next door.
The invention of penicillin was a remarkable example of serendipity in science.
Travelers often find that the best experiences come through a bit of serendipity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fortune - chance or luck as an external, arbitrary force affecting human affairs
  • synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance rather than one's own actions
