back tooth

noun
a tooth at the back of the mouth
Pronunciation: / bæk tuθ / (AmE), / bak tuːθ / (BrE)
a tooth located toward the back of the mouth, such as a molar or premolar

Examples

A back tooth hurts
Chewing tough foods can sometimes cause pain in a back tooth.
She lost a back tooth when she was twelve and had to get a replacement.
The dentist said I had a cavity in my back tooth that needed to be filled.

Related Terms

  • antonym: front tooth - a tooth at the front of the mouth, such as an incisor or canine
  • synonym: molar - a large, flat tooth at the back of the mouth used for grinding food
