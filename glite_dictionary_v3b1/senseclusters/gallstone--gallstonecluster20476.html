gallstone

noun
stone-like mass in the gall bladder
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːlstəʊn / (BrE)
A small, hard mass formed in the gall bladder or its ducts, usually consisting of cholesterol, bile pigments, and calcium salts.

Examples

A gallstone in gallbladder
Imaging tests can help detect the presence of a gallstone blocking the bile duct.
Surgery was required to remove several large gallstones.
The patient was diagnosed with a gallstone that was causing severe abdominal pain.

  • synonym: calculus - a stone or concretion formed in the body
