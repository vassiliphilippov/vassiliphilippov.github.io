vena renalis

noun
vein draining blood from the kidney
9.9 8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.nə rɪ.ˈneɪ.lɪs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə rə.ˈneɪ.lɪs/ (AmE), /ˈviː.nə rɪ.ˈneɪ.lɪs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə rə.ˈneɪ.lɪs/ (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the kidney into the inferior vena cava at the level of the second lumbar vertebra.

Examples

The vena renalis vein
Blockage of the vena renalis can lead to kidney swelling and impaired function.
Each kidney is drained by a single vena renalis, which connects to the inferior vena cava.
The surgeon carefully identified the vena renalis during the operation to avoid damaging it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: renal vein - the large vein that carries blood away from the kidney
