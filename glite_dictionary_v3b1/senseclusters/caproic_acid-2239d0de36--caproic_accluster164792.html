caproic acid

noun
a fatty acid with a goat-like odor
Pronunciation: / kəˈproʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / kəˈprəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a fatty acid present in animal fats and oils, with a strong odor resembling that of goats, used in flavorings and industrial applications

Examples

Caproic acid odor
Caproic acid is often used in the production of artificial flavors and perfumes.
Researchers analyzed the presence of caproic acid in various animal fats.
The strong odor of caproic acid is reminiscent of goats and can be detected in some cheeses.
