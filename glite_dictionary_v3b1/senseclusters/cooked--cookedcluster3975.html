cooked

adjective
falsified; manipulated (informal)
8.8
Pronunciation: /kʊkt/ (AmE), /kʊkt/ (BrE)
(informal) (of numbers, accounts, or data) falsified or manipulated to give a misleading impression.

Examples

Cooked numbers
The auditor discovered the quarterly revenue numbers had been cooked to hide losses from investors.
They accused the company of presenting cooked figures during the merger talks to inflate its value.
After reviewing the spreadsheet, she concluded the sales data looked cooked and needed a full audit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accurate - correct and free from error; not misleading
  • synonym: falsify - to change information so that it is no longer true, often to deceive
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence something, often unfairly or dishonestly
