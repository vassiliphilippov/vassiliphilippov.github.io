Catholic

noun
member of the Roman Catholic Church
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / ˈkæθlɪk / (BrE)
a member of the Roman Catholic Church, or sometimes of any Catholic church

Examples

A Catholic goes to church
Many Catholics attend Mass every Sunday.
She has been a devout Catholic all her life.
The new bishop addressed the concerns of the local Catholic community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Orthodox - a member of the Eastern Orthodox churches
  • antonym: Protestant - a member of the Protestant churches; religious antonym
