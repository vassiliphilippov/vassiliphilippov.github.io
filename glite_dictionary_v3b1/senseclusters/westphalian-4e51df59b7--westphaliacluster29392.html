Westphalian

noun
Carboniferous stage
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɛstˈfeɪliən / (AmE), / wɛstˈfeɪliən / (BrE)
a stage of the Upper Carboniferous period in European geology, characterized by extensive coal-forming swamp forests

Examples

Westphalian stage
The coal deposits in this region were formed during the Westphalian stage.
Many fossils from the Westphalian provide insight into ancient swamp ecosystems.
Geologists identified the rock layer as belonging to the Westphalian period.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Namurian - the stage preceding the Westphalian in the Carboniferous period
  • degree higher: Stephanian - the stage following the Westphalian in the Upper Carboniferous period
