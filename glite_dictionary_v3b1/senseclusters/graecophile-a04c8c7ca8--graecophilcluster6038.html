Graecophile

noun
admirer of Greece or Greek culture
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪkəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡriːkəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
A person who admires Greece, its people, culture, or language.

Examples

A Graecophile scholar
As a self-proclaimed Graecophile, she spent years studying the language and history of ancient Greece.
Many Graecophiles travel to Athens to experience the country's rich cultural heritage firsthand.
The museum's new exhibit was a delight for any Graecophile interested in Hellenistic art.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Graecomania - an excessive admiration or obsession with Greek culture
  • synonym: Hellenophile - a person who admires ancient Greek culture and language
