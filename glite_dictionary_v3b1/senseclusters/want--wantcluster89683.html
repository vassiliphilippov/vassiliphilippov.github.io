want

noun
state of extreme poverty or lack
4.3 2.11/m
Pronunciation: / wÉ‘nt / (AmE), / wÉ’nt / (BrE)
a lack or deficiency of something necessary or desirable; the state of being in need or poverty

Examples

A want of food
His family struggled on, despite years of want and deprivation.
The city was filled with people living in want after the war destroyed their homes.
Without help, many children would grow up in want and hardship.
The relief organization aims to address the wants of those in need.
There was no want of enthusiasm among the volunteers.
Years of poverty left him with a deep want for security and comfort.
After months of drought, there was a desperate want of clean water in the village.
For want of evidence, the case was dismissed by the judge.
Many charities aim to relieve the want and suffering caused by poverty.

Synonyms

  • lack
    1.3 122/m
    lack is the state of not having something needed or desired, while want means a general lack or need.
  • deficiency
    4.5 15/m
    deficiency is a measurable shortage of something necessary, while want means a broader lack or absence of needed things.
  • deprivation
    6.2 7.08/m
    deprivation is the loss or absence of basic needs like food or shelter, while want covers general lack or poverty.
  • privation
    9.3 0.71/m
    privation is severe hardship from missing basic comforts, while want is a general word for lack or need.
  • need
    2.1 43/m
    need is the state of extreme poverty or lacking necessities, while want is a general lack or shortage.
  • poverty
    2.0 52/m
    poverty is the condition of having very little money or goods, while want can mean lack in many areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: need - something that is necessary or required
  • synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • antonym: surfeit - an excessive amount of something, especially something unnecessary
