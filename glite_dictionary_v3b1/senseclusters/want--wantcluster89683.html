want

noun
state of extreme poverty or lack
7.9
Pronunciation: / wɑnt / (AmE), / wɒnt / (BrE)
a lack or deficiency of something necessary or desirable; the state of being in need or poverty

Examples

A want of food
His family struggled on, despite years of want and deprivation.
The city was filled with people living in want after the war destroyed their homes.
Without help, many children would grow up in want and hardship.
The relief organization aims to address the wants of those in need.
There was no want of enthusiasm among the volunteers.
Years of poverty left him with a deep want for security and comfort.
After months of drought, there was a desperate want of clean water in the village.
For want of evidence, the case was dismissed by the judge.
Many charities aim to relieve the want and suffering caused by poverty.

  • synonym: need - something that is necessary or required
  • synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
