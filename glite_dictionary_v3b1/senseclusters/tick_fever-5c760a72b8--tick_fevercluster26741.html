tick fever

noun
disease transmitted by ticks
Pronunciation: / tɪk ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / tɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
Any of several infectious diseases caused by rickettsial bacteria and transmitted to humans or animals by the bite of a tick.

Examples

Tick fever symptoms
Early symptoms of tick fever can include fever, headache, and muscle aches.
Many farmers are wary of tick fever during the summer months when ticks are most active.
Veterinarians recommend checking pets daily to help prevent tick fever.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Lyme disease - tick-borne disease with different cause (Borrelia bacteria), sometimes confused with tick fevers
  • synonym: Rocky Mountain spotted fever - a specific form of tick-borne fever caused by Rickettsia rickettsii
