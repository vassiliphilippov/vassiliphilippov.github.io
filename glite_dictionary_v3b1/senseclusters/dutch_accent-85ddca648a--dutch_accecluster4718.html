Dutch accent

noun
English spoken with influences from Dutch pronunciation
1.0 5.2
Pronunciation: /dʌtʃ ˈæk.sənt/ (AmE), /dʌtʃ ˈæk.sənt/ (BrE)
The manner of speaking English that is typical of native speakers from the Netherlands, influenced by Dutch pronunciation and intonation.

Examples

A Dutch accent
He practiced for weeks to imitate a convincing Dutch accent in the play.
Her English is fluent but she still has a noticeable Dutch accent.
People sometimes mistake my Dutch accent for a German one over the phone.
