baton

noun
rod twirled by drum major or majorette
6.8
Pronunciation: / bəˈtɑn / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ɒn / (BrE)
a thin, hollow metal rod that is twirled or carried by a drum major or majorette as part of a marching band performance

Examples

Majorette baton
After hours of practice, she could toss the baton high in the air and catch it.
Majorettes often perform synchronized routines with their batons during halftime shows.
The drum major skillfully twirled the baton at the front of the parade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baton (conductor's) - a thin stick used by a conductor to direct an orchestra or choir
