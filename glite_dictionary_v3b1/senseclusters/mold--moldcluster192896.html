mold

verb
shape or form a material
5.8
Pronunciation: / moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
to shape or form something (such as clay, glass, or ideas) by hand, with a mold, or by fitting closely to a shape

Examples

To mold clay
She molded the wax into the shape of a delicate flower.
The artist carefully molded a sculpture out of wet clay.
You can mold the dough into any shape you like before baking.
The glass was heated and then molded to the unique curves of the sculpture.
The yoga pants mold to her body, providing both comfort and support.
This wetsuit molds perfectly to your shape after a few uses.
Artists often mold new ideas from their experiences.
She carefully molded the clay into the shape of a bird.
The chef molded the chocolate into delicate flowers for the cake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cast - to form an object by pouring liquid material into a mold
  • antonym: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • synonym: form - to bring together parts to create something
  • synonym: hug - to fit closely (as clothes do)
  • synonym: model - to shape a material into a particular form or figure
  • synonym: mold - (American spelling) fit or shape closely; form according to a contour
  • synonym: shape - to give a particular form to something
