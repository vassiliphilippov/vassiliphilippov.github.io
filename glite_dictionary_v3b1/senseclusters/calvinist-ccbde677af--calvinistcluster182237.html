Calvinist

noun
follower of John Calvin's theology
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.vɪ.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vɪ.nɪst / (BrE)
A person who follows or adheres to the religious teachings and doctrines of John Calvin, especially in relation to Protestant theology.

Examples

A Calvinist preacher
As a Calvinist, he believed strongly in the doctrine of predestination.
She studied the history of Calvinist thought during her theology course.
The Calvinist church in the city held services every Sunday.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Lutheran - an adherent of Martin Luther's theological doctrines
  • synonym: Puritan - a member of a group of English Protestants influenced by Calvinism
