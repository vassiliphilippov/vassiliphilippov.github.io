euthyroid

adjective
normal thyroid function
8.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈθaɪrɔɪd / (AmE), / juːˈθaɪrɔɪd / (BrE)
having normal levels of thyroid hormones in the body

Examples

Euthyroid state
After treatment, the patient became euthyroid and no longer showed symptoms of thyroid disease.
Most people with a healthy thyroid gland are considered euthyroid by their doctors.
The test results confirmed that she was euthyroid despite her earlier concerns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperthyroid - having abnormally high thyroid hormone levels
  • antonym: hypothyroid - having abnormally low thyroid hormone levels
