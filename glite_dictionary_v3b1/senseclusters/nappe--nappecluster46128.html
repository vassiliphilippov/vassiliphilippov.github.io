nappe

noun
sheetlike rock body
9.9
Pronunciation: / næp / (AmE), / nap / (BrE)
a large sheetlike body of rock that has been displaced far from its original position, or a surface in geometry such as one part of a double cone

Examples

Rock nappe
Geologists studied the nappe to understand how the mountain range was formed.
In geometry, a double cone consists of two nappes joined at the vertex.
The nappe extended for miles, showing evidence of ancient tectonic movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticline - an upward-arching fold in rock layers
  • synonym: thrust sheet - a large slab of rock moved by thrust faulting
