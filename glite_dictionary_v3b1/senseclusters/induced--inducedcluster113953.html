induced

adjective
caused or brought about (not spontaneous)
5.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàduÀêst / (AmE), / …™nÀàdjuÀêst / (BrE)
(of a condition or state) brought about or caused, especially by a specific factor or agent, rather than occurring naturally or spontaneously.

Examples

Induced changes
Many chemicals can cause induced changes in cells.
Scientists studied the effects of induced stress on behavior.
The patient developed induced vomiting after taking the medication.

Synonyms

  • evoked
    7.3 0.00/m
    evoked is called forth from a hidden or potential state by stimulation in scientific settings, while induced is caused by a specific agent.
  • elicited
    5.7 0.00/m
    elicited is drawn out as a response to a stimulus, often referring to feelings or answers, while induced means caused into a state by an agent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spontaneous - occurring without external cause or stimulus
