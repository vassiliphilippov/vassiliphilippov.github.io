induced

adjective
caused or brought about (not spontaneous)
5.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈduːst/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdjuːst/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdjuːst/ /; / /ɪn.ˈduːst/ (BrE)
(of a condition or state) brought about or caused, especially by a specific factor or agent, rather than occurring naturally or spontaneously.

Examples

Induced changes
Many chemicals can cause induced changes in cells.

The patient developed induced vomiting after taking the medication.
