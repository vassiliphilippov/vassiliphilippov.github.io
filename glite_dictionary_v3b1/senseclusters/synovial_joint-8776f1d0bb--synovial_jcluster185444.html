a joint in the body that allows free movement, in which the articulating bone surfaces are covered with smooth cartilage and enclosed within a capsule filled with synovial fluid

Examples

A synovial joint cavity
A synovial joint is characterized by the presence of a cavity filled with lubricating fluid.
Injuries to a synovial joint can often result in swelling and pain due to inflammation.
The knee is a classic example of a synovial joint that permits extension and flexion.
