debar

verb
officially exclude temporarily
Pronunciation: / dɪˈbɑr / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑː / (BrE)
to officially prevent or exclude someone from entering a place, taking part in an activity, or holding a position

Examples

To debar from work
Employees who cheat during the examination will be debarred from future promotions.
The committee has the authority to debar members found guilty of misconduct.
The school decided to debar the student for repeated violations of its code of conduct.
Her criminal record could debar her from holding public office.
Individuals under the age of eighteen are debarred from entering the club.
The committee decided to debar the company from future contracts due to misconduct.
Some countries debar convicted criminals from voting in elections.
The committee decided to debar him from attending the conference due to his misconduct.
The rules may debar you from future competitions if you are found cheating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - to allow someone to enter a place or participate
  • synonym: bar - to prohibit or prevent someone from entering or participating
  • synonym: exclude - to prevent someone from entering a place or taking part in something
