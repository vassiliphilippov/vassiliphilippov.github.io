receipt

verb
mark as paid (bill/invoice)
7.1 6.2
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈsiːt/ /; / /riː.ˈsiːt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈsiːt/ (BrE)
to officially mark or record that something (such as a payment or delivery) has been received

Examples

To receipt a bill
Once the customer has paid, the accountant will receipt the bill to confirm it.
Please receipt the invoice after you process the payment.
The clerk forgot to receipt the document, so the records were incomplete.
The warehouse staff are receipting all incoming shipments to ensure nothing is missing.
Please make sure you receipt the payment in the system as soon as it arrives.
After receipting the documents, she filed them for future reference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acknowledge - to accept or admit the existence or truth of something, often in writing
  • synonym: confirm - to state that something is true or has been received
  • synonym: register - to record information officially
