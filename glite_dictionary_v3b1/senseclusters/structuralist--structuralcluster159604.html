structuralist

noun
one who analyzes structures
Pronunciation: / ˈstrʌktʃərəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies, supports, or practices structuralism, especially in fields like philosophy, linguistics, or social sciences

Examples

A famous structuralist
As a leading structuralist, she focused on the patterns within language and culture.
The debate between the structuralist and the poststructuralist shaped much of twentieth-century theory.
Many critics consider him a structuralist because of his emphasis on underlying systems in literature.
As a leading structuralist, she analyzed myths to uncover universal patterns.
Many structuralists believe that language shapes how we perceive the world.
The debate between the structuralist and his critics was lively and thought-provoking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poststructuralist - a person who critiques or moves beyond structuralist ideas
  • synonym: structuralism - an approach that analyzes systems by their structures
