oligotrophic

adjective
low in nutrients
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊlɪɡoʊˈtroʊfɪk /; / əˌlɪɡəˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
describing water, soil, or an environment that has very low levels of nutrients and supports little plant or animal life

Examples

Oligotrophic lake water
The lake is classified as oligotrophic because it contains very few nutrients and little plant growth.
Scientists study oligotrophic environments to understand how life adapts to nutrient scarcity.
Certain fish species thrive in oligotrophic waters where competition for food is less intense.
The mountain lake is considered oligotrophic because it contains very few nutrients.
Scientists study oligotrophic environments to understand how life adapts to nutrient-poor conditions.
Many oligotrophic lakes have exceptionally clear water due to the lack of algae growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eutrophic - rich in nutrients, supporting abundant plant life
  • degree higher: mesotrophic - having a moderate level of nutrients
