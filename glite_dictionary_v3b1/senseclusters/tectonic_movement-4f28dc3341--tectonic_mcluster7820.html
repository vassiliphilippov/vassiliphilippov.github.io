tectonic movement

noun
movement of Earth's crust
4.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / t…õkÀàt…ën…™k Ààmuvm…ônt / (AmE), / t…õkÀàt…ín…™k ÀàmuÀêvm…ônt / (BrE)
the movement of parts of the Earth's crust that causes its deformation, such as shifting of tectonic plates

Examples

tectonic movement of plates
Evidence of tectonic movement can be seen in the fault lines running through California.
Scientists study tectonic movement to better understand earthquake risks.
The Himalayas were formed as a result of tectonic movement millions of years ago.

Synonyms

  • crustal movement
    7.7 0.00/m
    crustal movement is the shifting of Earth's outer layer that causes earthquakes and mountains, while tectonic movement refers to plate-driven crust shifts.
  • tectonism
    9.1 0.03/m
    tectonism is the geological process of building and bending Earth's crust through internal forces, while tectonic movement names plate shifts.
  • diastrophism
    9.9 0.00/m
    diastrophism is large-scale crust deformation that forms continents and mountains, while tectonic movement focuses on plate motion causing such change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plate tectonics - the theory explaining the movement of the Earth's plates
