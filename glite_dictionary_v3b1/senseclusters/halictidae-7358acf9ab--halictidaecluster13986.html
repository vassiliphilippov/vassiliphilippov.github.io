Halictidae

noun
family of small bees, key pollinators
Pronunciation: / həˈlɪktɪˌdi /; / hæˌlɪk.tɪˈdiː / (AmE), / həˈlɪktɪˌdiː /; / hæˌlɪk.tɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, often metallic-colored bees, many of which are important pollinators in agriculture and wild ecosystems

Examples

Halictidae bee family
Many species in the Halictidae family are essential for pollinating crops around the world.
Researchers have discovered several new Halictidae species in tropical forests.
Unlike honeybees, some Halictidae species show diverse social behavior, from solitary to eusocial.
Many crops depend on the Halictidae for effective pollination in the absence of honey bees.
Recent studies show a decline in several Halictidae species due to pesticide use.
The Halictidae are often called sweat bees due to their attraction to perspiration.
