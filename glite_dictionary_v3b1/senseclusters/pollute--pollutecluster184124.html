pollute

verb
to make impure or dirty, especially the environment
3.4 8.41/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôÀàlut / (AmE), / p…ôÀàluÀêt / (BrE)
to make water, air, soil, etc. dirty or harmful by adding waste or poisonous substances

Examples

To pollute the air
Careless disposal of trash can pollute the local environment.
Factories that dump chemicals into rivers pollute the water and harm wildlife.
It is illegal to pollute the air with hazardous fumes.

Synonyms

  • contaminate
    4.9 13/m
    contaminate means making something impure or harmful in any context, while pollute means adding waste or poison to air, water, or soil.
  • taint
    7.3 5.95/m
    taint leaves a visible or hidden trace that makes something impure or damaged, while pollute means adding waste or poison to air, water, or soil.
  • foul
    4.5 1.20/m
    foul makes something dirty, smelly, or blocked with waste, while pollute refers to adding harmful or poisonous substances to nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contaminate - to make something impure or harmful by adding something undesirable
  • antonym: purify - to remove impurities from; to make pure
