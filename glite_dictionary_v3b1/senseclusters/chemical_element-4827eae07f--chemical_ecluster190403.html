chemical element

noun
a basic substance that cannot be broken down chemically
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈke.mɪ.kəl ˈel.ə.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈke.mɪ.kəl ˈel.ɪ.mənt/ (BrE)
A pure substance consisting of atoms with the same number of protons, which cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means; examples include oxygen, gold, and iron.

Examples

A chemical element symbol
Gold is a chemical element with the symbol Au and atomic number 79.
Hydrogen is the lightest chemical element and the most abundant in the universe.
There are 118 known chemical elements listed in the periodic table.
