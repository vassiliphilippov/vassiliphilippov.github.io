West Nile encephalitis virus

noun
virus causing West Nile encephalitis
Pronunciation: / wɛst naɪl ɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / wɛst naɪl ɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
the virus belonging to the flavivirus family that causes West Nile encephalitis in humans and animals

Examples

West Nile encephalitis virus infection
Outbreaks of West Nile encephalitis virus have led to increased public health surveillance in recent years.
Researchers have isolated the West Nile encephalitis virus from infected mosquitoes in several regions.
The West Nile encephalitis virus can be transmitted to humans through the bite of an infected mosquito.
