scarper

verb
run away quickly; flee
Pronunciation: / ˈskɑːrpər / (AmE), / ˈskɑːpə / (BrE)
to run away or leave a place quickly, often to escape trouble; flee

Examples

To scarper quickly
As soon as they heard the sirens, the thieves decided to scarper down the alley.
The fox managed to scarper across the field just in time.
We'd better scarper before anyone notices we're here.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flee - run away from danger or unwanted situation
  • antonym: linger - to stay somewhere longer than necessary
  • synonym: run off - leave a place quickly, especially to escape
