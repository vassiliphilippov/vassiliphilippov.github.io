Babinski sign

noun
reflex of upward toe extension on foot stimulation

a reflex in which the big toe extends upward when the sole of the foot is firmly stroked, normal in infants but indicating neurological damage in older children or adults

Examples

A positive Babinski sign
A positive Babinski sign in adults can indicate a problem with the central nervous system.
Doctors usually observe the Babinski sign in infants as a normal reflex.
The neurologist checked for the Babinski sign during the patient's examination.

Synonyms

  • Babinski reflex
    6.8 0.00/m
    Babinski reflex is the automatic upward movement of the big toe when the sole is stroked, while Babinski sign is the clinical name for that reflex.
  • Babinski
    9.9 0.00/m
    Babinski is an upward extension of the toes when the sole is stroked, while Babinski sign is the fuller clinical term used by doctors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plantar reflex - the normal downward flexion of the toes when the sole is stroked
