pheochromocytoma

noun
tumor of the adrenal gland causing high blood pressure
9.2 9.9
Pronunciation: /fiː.oʊ.kroʊ.moʊ.saɪ.ˈtoʊ.mə/ /; / /fiː.oʊ.kroʊ.mə.saɪ.ˈtoʊ.mə/ (AmE), /fiː.əʊ.krəʊ.mə.saɪ.ˈtəʊ.mə/ /; / /fiː.əʊ.krə.mə.saɪ.ˈtəʊ.mə/ (BrE)
A rare, usually benign tumor of the adrenal gland causing excessive secretion of epinephrine and resulting in intermittent or sustained high blood pressure.

Examples

A pheochromocytoma tumor
Doctors suspected a pheochromocytoma after reviewing unusual hormone levels in the test results.
Surgical removal is often the treatment of choice for a pheochromocytoma.
The patient's sudden episodes of high blood pressure were traced to a pheochromocytoma.
Surgery is often recommended to remove a phaeochromocytoma from the adrenal gland.
The doctor suspected a phaeochromocytoma after the patient's sudden hypertension episodes.
Untreated phaeochromocytoma can lead to life-threatening complications due to high blood pressure.
The surgeon removed an extra-adrenal phaeochromocytoma from the patient's retroperitoneal space near the kidney.
Genetic testing revealed mutations commonly seen in familial phaeochromocytoma and paraganglioma syndromes.
On MRI, the radiologist suspected a catecholamine-secreting phaeochromocytoma outside the adrenal gland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paraganglioma - a tumour arising in paraganglia; often used to describe extra‑adrenal, catecholamine‑secreting chromaffin tumours
  • antonym: phaeochromocytoma (adrenal) - a phaeochromocytoma occurring within the adrenal gland (the classical, intra‑adrenal form)
