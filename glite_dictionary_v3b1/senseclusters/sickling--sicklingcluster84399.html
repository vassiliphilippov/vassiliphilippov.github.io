sickling

noun
crescent-shaped red cells
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which red blood cells become crescent-shaped, especially as a result of sickle cell disease

Examples

Blood sickling
The laboratory test confirmed the presence of sickling in the patient's blood sample.
Doctors monitor sickling to assess the severity of sickle cell disease.
Frequent sickling can lead to blockages in blood vessels and cause pain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normalization - the process of returning to a normal shape or state
  • synonym: sickle cell disease - a genetic disorder causing abnormal, crescent-shaped red blood cells
