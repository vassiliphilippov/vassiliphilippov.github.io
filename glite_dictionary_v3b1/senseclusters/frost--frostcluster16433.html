frost

noun
white ice crystals formed outdoors
1.0 6.04/m
Pronunciation: / frÉ‘st / (AmE), / frÉ’st / (BrE)
a thin layer of ice crystals that forms on surfaces when it is very cold, or the weather condition when this happens

Examples

Morning frost
A thick layer of frost covered the grass in the early morning.
Gardeners worry about frost damaging their plants in late spring.
The windowpanes glistened with frost after the cold night.
During the night, a frost formed on the grass and rooftops.
Farmers worry that a late spring frost could damage their crops.
The first frost of the year covered the fields in a sparkling layer of ice.
A thick layer of frost formed overnight on the grass.
Make sure to protect the plants from frost as the temperature drops.
The car windows were covered with frost when I woke up this morning.

Synonyms

  • hoarfrost
    9.3 0.11/m
    hoarfrost forms feathery white crystals on cold, moist surfaces in calm weather, while frost is the general thin ice layer.
  • rime
    9.6 0.13/m
    rime builds up from freezing fog into hard, icy crusts on exposed surfaces, while frost usually means a thin, soft ice layer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze - to become or be made solid as a result of extreme cold
  • synonym: ice - frozen water; solid state of water
  • antonym: thaw - to become unfrozen or to warm and melt after a period of cold
