anathematization

noun
formal declaration of a curse
9.8 9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌnæθəmətɪˈzeɪʃən /; / əˌnæθəmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌnæθəmətʌɪˈzeɪʃən /; / əˌnaθəmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The formal act of declaring or pronouncing someone or something to be accursed or excommunicated, especially by a religious authority.

Examples

The anathematization of heretics
During the Middle Ages, anathematization was a powerful tool used against heretics.
He feared anathematization more than any earthly punishment.
The anathematization of her ideas by the church led to her social isolation.
In medieval times, anathematisation could lead to social and political isolation.
The council issued a public anathematisation against the teachings deemed heretical.
The priest's anathematisation of the heretics was met with shock by the congregation.
