habilitation

noun
advanced academic qualification
Pronunciation: / həˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / həˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a higher academic or professional qualification, especially in some countries, that certifies the ability to teach and supervise research at the university level

After years of research, she received her habilitation, allowing her to supervise doctoral students independently.
In Germany, habilitation is often required to become a full university professor.

After years of research, he finally completed his habilitation and was promoted to professor.

Her habilitation thesis was highly praised by the academic committee.
