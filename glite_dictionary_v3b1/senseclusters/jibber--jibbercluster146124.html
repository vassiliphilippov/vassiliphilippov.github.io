jibber

verb
to speak unintelligibly
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɪ.bər/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɪ.bə/ (BrE)
to speak rapidly and unintelligibly, making speech difficult to understand

Examples

To jibber nervously
The frightened child began to jibber when he saw the shadow in the hallway.
She tends to jibber nervously whenever she has to speak in public.
Unable to control his excitement, he started to jibber about his new idea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: articulate - to speak clearly and effectively
  • synonym: jabber - to talk quickly and unclearly
  • synonym: mumble - to speak quietly and not clearly
