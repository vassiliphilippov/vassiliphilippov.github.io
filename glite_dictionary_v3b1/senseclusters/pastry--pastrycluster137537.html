pastry

noun
dough of flour, water, and fat for baking
1.0 2.11/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆpeÉªstri / (AmE), / ËˆpeÉªstri / (BrE)
A mixture of flour, water, and fat that is baked and used as the base or covering for pies and other baked goods.

Examples

Pastry dough
He skillfully rolled out the pastry for a homemade apple pie.
The bakery is famous for its delicious pastry doughs and tarts.
Too much handling can make the pastry tough instead of light and flaky.

Synonyms

  • pastry dough
    1.0 0.09/m
    pastry dough is the uncooked mix used to make crusts and tarts, while pastry is the baked base or covering for pies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bread - food made of dough and baked, but typically not rich in fat like pastry
  • synonym: pie crust - the baked outer shell of a pie, often made from pastry
