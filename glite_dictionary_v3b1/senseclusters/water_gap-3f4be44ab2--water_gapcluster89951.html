water gap

noun
pass in a ridge with flowing water
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər ɡæp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ɡæp / (BrE)
a narrow pass or notch in a mountain ridge through which a river or stream flows

Examples

A water gap in mountains
A river carved a deep water gap through the Appalachian Mountains over millions of years.
Geologists studied the formation of the water gap to understand the region's history.
The Delaware water gap is a well-known natural landmark on the border of New Jersey and Pennsylvania.

Related Terms

  • antonym: wind gap - a pass in a mountain ridge through which no water flows
