(in linear algebra and applied mathematics) to write a matrix as a product of two or more simpler matrices (for example LU, QR, or SVD decompositions), often to make calculations or analysis easier.

Examples

To factorize a matrix
In class, we learned to factorize a matrix into LU form to simplify solving linear systems.
The software factorized the large matrix using SVD to improve the accuracy of the model.
Researchers are factorizing user-rating matrices to predict preferences in recommendation systems.
