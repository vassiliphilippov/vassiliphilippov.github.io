spin

verb
invent a story or explanation
5.4
Pronunciation: / spɪn / (AmE), / spɪn / (BrE)
to present information in a way that influences how people see it (often in media or politics); also, to invent a story or explanation

Examples

To spin a story
He spun a wild story about how he got lost in the woods.
Politicians often spin stories to influence public opinion.
She tried to spin an elaborate tale to explain her absence.
Her statement was carefully spun to avoid causing public panic.
The campaign manager is known for his ability to spin negative stories.
The government tried to spin the bad economic figures into something positive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distort - to give a misleading or false account or impression of something
  • synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as being larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • synonym: fabricate - to invent or concoct something, typically with deceitful intent
  • synonym: misrepresent - to give a false or misleading account of the nature of something
  • synonym: twist - to distort or change the meaning of something
