denotation

noun
literal or primary meaning of a word
6.7 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / ÀådiÀêno äÀàte…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀådiÀên…ô äÀàte…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
The literal or primary meaning of a word, distinct from the feelings or ideas that the word suggests; the class of actual things or entities to which a term refers.

Examples

The denotation of a word
In linguistics, denotation refers to the actual objects or concepts a word represents.
It is important to distinguish between the denotation and the connotation of a term.
The denotation of the word 'rose' is a type of flower.

Synonyms

  • extension
    7.6 0.92/m
    extension lists the real objects a word points to in the world, while denotation gives the word's literal, primary meaning.
  • reference
    5.5 17/m
    reference names the object or concept a word stands for or the link between word and world, while denotation is the word's literal primary meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connotation - the ideas or feelings beyond a word's literal meaning
  • synonym: reference - the action of mentioning or alluding to something, especially in language
