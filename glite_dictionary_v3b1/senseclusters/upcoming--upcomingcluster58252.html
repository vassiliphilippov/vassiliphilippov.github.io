Synonyms

  • forthcoming
    4.2 5/m
    forthcoming is used about planned events or announcements and sounds more formal, while upcoming is neutral everyday speech for near future events.
  • coming
    1.0 0.00/m
    coming is an informal, common way to say something will happen soon, while upcoming is slightly more neutral for events.
  • approaching
    2.3 0.2/m
    approaching often describes time or a deadline that draws near, while upcoming is broader for any event happening soon.
  • at hand
    3.2 3/m
    at hand refers to something immediately near in time or space, while upcoming simply means due to happen soon.
  • imminent
    4.8 10/m
    imminent suggests something will happen at once or very soon and may feel urgent, while upcoming is less urgent and general.
  • impending
    4.0 5/m
    impending often suggests an unpleasant event coming close and sounds tense, while upcoming is neutral and used for ordinary future events.
  • in the offing
    5.6 0.8/m
    in the offing suggests something is likely to appear soon but not certain, while upcoming implies a more definite near future event.
