despoiled

adjective
robbed and destroyed by force
6.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɔɪld / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔɪld / (BrE)
Having been robbed, plundered, or stripped of valuables by force and violence.

Examples

A despoiled land
Centuries of conflict had turned the once-lush valley into a despoiled wasteland.
The despoiled temples stood as grim reminders of the invasion.
The army left a trail of despoiled villages in its wake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devastated - severely damaged or destroyed
  • synonym: plundered - robbed or taken by force, especially in war
  • antonym: restored - brought back to former condition, opposite of being despoiled
