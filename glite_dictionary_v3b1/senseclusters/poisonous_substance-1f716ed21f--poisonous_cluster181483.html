poisonous substance

noun
a substance harmful or fatal to living things
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzənəs səbˈstəns / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənəs ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
a substance that is capable of causing injury, illness, or death if absorbed, ingested, or inhaled by a living organism

Examples

A poisonous substance hazard
Some industrial chemicals are considered poisonous substances and must be handled with extreme caution.
Swallowing even a small amount of a poisonous substance can be fatal to children and pets.
The company was fined for illegally dumping poisonous substances into the river.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antidote - a medicine taken to counteract a particular poison
  • synonym: poison - a substance that is capable of causing harm when introduced to the body
  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms
