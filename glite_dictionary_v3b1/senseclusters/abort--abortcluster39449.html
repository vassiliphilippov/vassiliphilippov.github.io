abort

verb
terminate before completion
5.6 2.70/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàb…îrt / (AmE), / …ôÀàb…îÀêt / (BrE)
to stop a process, plan, or activity before it is completed, often deliberately or due to problems

Examples

To abort the mission
He had to abort the experiment after unexpected results appeared.
The engineers decided to abort the launch due to a technical malfunction.
You can abort the download if it is taking too long.

Synonyms

    halt stops something suddenly or temporarily, while abort ends a process before it finishes because of problems.
  • terminate
    4.6 8.77/m
    terminate ends something completely and often officially, while abort means to stop a process early, usually because of problems.
  • stop
    1.0 539/m
    stop brings something to an end in general, while abort usually refers to stopping a plan or process before it completes.
  • cease
    3.6 23/m
    cease means to come to an end, often gradually or formally, while abort means to stop a process prematurely due to issues.
  • discontinue
    4.2 4.49/m
    discontinue stops something being offered or made, while abort stops a process mid-course, often because of a sudden problem.
  • pull the plug
    5.5 2.01/m

  • scrub
    5.6 0.27/m
    scrub cancels a planned attempt at short notice, while abort stops a process before completion, often because something goes wrong.
  • call off
    2.8 2.10/m
    call off cancels a planned event or meeting, often before it starts, while abort means stopping a process already underway.
  • cancel
    1.0 22/m

  • put a stop to
