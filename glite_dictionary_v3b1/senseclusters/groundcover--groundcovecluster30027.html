groundcover

noun
plant covering soil surface
6.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈgraʊnd.kʌv.ər/ (AmE), /ˈgraʊnd.kʌv.ə/ (BrE)
a low-growing plant or group of plants that spreads to cover the ground, often used in gardens or to prevent soil erosion

Examples

Low groundcover plants
Hostas are a popular groundcover in shady gardens because they thrive where grass will not grow.
The landscaper recommended planting a dense groundcover to prevent soil erosion on the hillside.
We chose creeping thyme as a fragrant groundcover for the path edges.
After clearing the weeds, we planted a thick groundcover to protect the soil from erosion.
English ivy is a popular groundcover for shady areas in gardens.
Many forests have a rich groundcover that provides habitat for insects and small animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: turf - grass and the surface layer of earth held together by its roots, often used for lawns
  • synonym: undergrowth - dense growth of shrubs and other plants under trees in a forest
