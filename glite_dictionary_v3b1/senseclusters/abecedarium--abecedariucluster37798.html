abecedarium

noun
alphabetical poem
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪbiːsiˈdɛriəm / (AmE), / ˌeɪbiːsiːˈdɛːrɪəm / (BrE)
a composition, such as a poem or list, in which each line or section begins with successive letters of the alphabet in order

Examples

Abecedarium poem
The teacher asked the students to write an abecedarium using each letter of the alphabet.
An abecedarium can help children learn the order of the letters.
The ancient manuscript included an abecedarium as a learning tool for new readers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acrostic - a poem in which certain letters in each line form a word or message
