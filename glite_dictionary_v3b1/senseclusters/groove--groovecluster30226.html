groove

noun
long, narrow cut or indentation
8.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /gruːv/ (AmE), /gruːv/ (BrE)
a long, narrow cut, channel, or indentation in a surface or body part

Examples

A deep groove
Rainwater had worn a narrow groove into the stone steps over the years.
She carefully placed the needle into the groove of the vinyl record.
The carpenter cut a deep groove along the edge of the wooden plank.
A deep groove runs along the length of the femur in some animals.
The optic nerve passes through a groove in the bone at the back of the eye socket.
The surgeon identified a groove on the patient's rib where the nerve was located.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furrow - a long, narrow trench made in the ground
