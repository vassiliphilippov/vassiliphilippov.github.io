geranyl

noun
hydrocarbon group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛrəˌnɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrənɪl / (BrE)
a univalent hydrocarbon group (C10H17) derived from geraniol, commonly found in natural terpenes and essential oils

Examples

Geranyl group
The geranyl group is important in the synthesis of many fragrances and flavors.
Researchers studied how geranyl compounds affect the aroma of essential oils.
Adding a geranyl unit to the molecule changed its chemical properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geraniol - an alcohol found in essential oils, from which geranyl is derived
  • synonym: terpene - a large class of organic compounds produced by plants
