decurion

noun
Roman officer or senator
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkjʊriən / (AmE), / dɪˈkjʊəriən / (BrE)
an officer in charge of ten men in the Roman army or a member of a Roman municipal senate

Examples

Roman decurion officer
The decurion was responsible for the discipline and training of his small unit.
In the Roman senate, a decurion played an important role in local government.
Archaeologists discovered an inscription honoring a decurion from the ancient city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centurion - an officer in the Roman army in charge of a hundred men
  • synonym: senator - a member of a senate, especially in ancient Rome
