boom

noun
horizontal pole for sails or cargo
Pronunciation: / buːm / (AmE), / buːm / (BrE)
a long, horizontal pole or bar, especially on a boat or used to hold a microphone

Examples

A ship's boom
The crew used a boom to lift the heavy cargo onto the ship.
The mainsail is attached to the boom along its bottom edge.
Watch your head when you walk beneath the boom on the sailboat.
A skilled boom operator ensures that background noises don't interfere with the recording.
Holding the boom steady for long takes can be surprisingly tiring.
The sound engineer carefully positioned the boom to capture dialogue without appearing in the shot.
