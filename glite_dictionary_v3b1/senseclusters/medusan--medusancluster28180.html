medusan

noun
free-swimming stage of a coelenterate
Pronunciation: / məˈduːzən / (AmE), / mɪˈdjuːzən / (BrE)
the free-swimming, sexual stage in the life cycle of a coelenterate, characterized by a gelatinous, umbrella-shaped body and tentacles

Examples

Medusan stage
During its medusan stage, the organism moves freely through the water.
Marine biologists observed the transition from polyp to medusan in the laboratory.
The jellyfish is a classic example of a medusan form in the animal kingdom.
