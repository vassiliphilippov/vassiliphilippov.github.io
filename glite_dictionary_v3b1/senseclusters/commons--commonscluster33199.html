commons

noun
social class below nobility and clergy
6.0 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêm…ônz / (AmE), / Ààk…ím…ônz / (BrE)
the social class consisting of people who are not members of the nobility or clergy, especially as represented in legislative assemblies

Examples

The commons in parliament
During medieval times, the commons had little political power compared to the nobility.
He came from the commons, but rose to become an influential politician.
The House of Commons was established to give representation to ordinary citizens.
