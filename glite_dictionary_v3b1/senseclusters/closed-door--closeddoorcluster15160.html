closed-door

adjective
not open to the public; private
5.3 0.71/m
Pronunciation: / klo äzd Ààd…îr / (AmE), / kl…ô äzd Ààd…îÀê / (BrE)
(of a meeting or event) not open to the public; held privately or in secret.

Examples

Closed-door meeting
Reporters were not allowed to attend the closed-door negotiations between the countries.
The board held a closed-door session to discuss sensitive business matters.
The decision was made during a closed-door meeting of senior officials.

Synonyms

  • private
    1.8 126/m
    private describes something meant for one person or group, while closed-door describes a meeting kept from the public and secret.
  • closed session
    5.5 0.25/m
    closed session is a formal meeting held not open to the public, while closed-door describes events held privately or in secret.
  • behind closed doors
    4.1 4.29/m
    behind closed doors means doing something in secret or privately, while closed-door describes meetings or events not open to the public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open-door - open to the public; not private or secret
