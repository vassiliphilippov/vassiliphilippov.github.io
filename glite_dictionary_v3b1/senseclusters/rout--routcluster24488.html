rout

verb
cut a groove or channel
6.6
Pronunciation: / raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
to cut a groove, trench, or channel in a surface, especially with a router or similar tool

Examples

To rout a groove
Before laying the wire, the workers needed to rout a channel in the masonry.
The carpenter used a special tool to rout a decorative groove along the edge of the table.
The sign's letters were routed into the wooden board for a professional finish.
