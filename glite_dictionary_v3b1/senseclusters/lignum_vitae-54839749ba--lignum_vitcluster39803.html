lignum vitae

noun
hard wood of Guaiacum trees
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪɡ.nəm ˈvaɪ.ti, ˈvi.taɪ / (AmE), / ˌlɪɡ.nəm ˈvaɪ.ti, ˈviː.taɪ / (BrE)
a very hard, greenish-brown wood from trees of the genus Guaiacum, valued for its density and toughness

Examples

Lignum vitae wood
Artisans prized lignum vitae for its natural oils and extreme durability.
Due to its hardness, lignum vitae was often used in making tool handles and mallets.
The propeller shaft was made from lignum vitae to withstand years of use at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guaiacum - genus of trees producing lignum vitae
