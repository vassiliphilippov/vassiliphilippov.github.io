art rock

noun
experimental subgenre of rock music
8.2 5.7
Pronunciation: /ɑːrt rɑːk/ /; / /ərt rɑːk/ (AmE), /ɑːt rɒk/ /; / /ət rɒk/ (BrE)
A style of rock music originating in the 1970s, characterized by experimental elements and influences from genres such as jazz and classical music, intended more for listening than dancing.

Examples

1970s art rock music
Many critics consider Pink Floyd a prime example of art rock from the 1970s.
The band's latest album explores new creative directions and firmly embraces art rock traditions.
Unlike typical stadium anthems, art rock albums are often designed for attentive, immersive listening.
