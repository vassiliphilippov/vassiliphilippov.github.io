hamada

noun
rocky desert plateau
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɑːˈmɑːdə / (AmE), / həˈmɑːdə / (BrE)
a type of desert landscape made up of bare, rocky plateaus with very little sand

Examples

The hamada desert
The expedition struggled to find water while crossing the vast hamada in North Africa.
Unlike sandy deserts, a hamada is mostly made up of exposed rock and little vegetation.
Travelers must be prepared for extreme heat and rough terrain on a hamada.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erg - a desert area covered with sand dunes
  • synonym: reg - a desert surface covered with gravel or pebbles
