Wycliffe

proper noun
English theologian and reformer (1328–1384)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪk.lɪf/ /; / /ˈwaɪ.k.lɪf/ (AmE), /ˈwɪk.lɪf/ /; / /ˈwaɪ.k.lɪf/ (BrE)
(1328–1384) An English theologian and reformer whose criticisms of Roman Catholic doctrine anticipated the Protestant Reformation.

Examples

Wycliffe the reformer
Wycliffe was one of the earliest critics of the Catholic Church's power in medieval England.
Many historians consider Wycliffe a precursor to the Protestant Reformation.
The translation of the Bible into English was strongly inspired by Wycliffe's teachings.
