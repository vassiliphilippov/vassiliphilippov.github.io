vicar apostolic

noun
Roman Catholic bishop in a missionary territory
9.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪkər əˈpɑːstəlɪk / (AmE), / ˈvɪkə əˈpɒstəlɪk / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic bishop with titular authority over a territory not yet established as a diocese, usually in non-Catholic or missionary areas.

Examples

A vicar apostolic bishop
Historically, a vicar apostolic acted as the highest church authority in areas without an established diocese.
The Pope appointed a vicar apostolic to oversee the church's mission in the remote region.
When the territory grew, the role of vicar apostolic was eventually replaced by a diocesan bishop.
