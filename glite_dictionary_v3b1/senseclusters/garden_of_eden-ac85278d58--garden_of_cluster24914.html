Garden of Eden

proper noun
biblical paradise; idyllic place
5.1 0.97/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°…ërdn …ôv Ààidn / (AmE), / Àà…°…ëÀêdn …ôv ÀàiÀêdn / (BrE)
In the Bible, the paradise where Adam and Eve lived before their expulsion as a consequence of disobedience; also used metaphorically to refer to an unspoiled, idyllic place.

Examples

The Garden of Eden story
He referred to his childhood backyard as his own Garden of Eden.
Scholars have debated the exact location of the Garden of Eden for centuries.
The untouched island reminded her of the Garden of Eden, pure and free from human interference.

Synonyms

  • Eden
    6.3 3.21/m
    Eden is the short biblical name often used in speech and writing, while Garden of Eden is the full, formal name for that paradise.
  • earthly paradise
    5.8 0.11/m
    earthly paradise describes any ideal, beautiful place on Earth in poetic or literary use, while Garden of Eden is the original biblical example.
  • paradise on earth
    4.2 0.09/m
    paradise on earth is a common phrase for a perfect worldly place you might imagine, while Garden of Eden names the specific biblical paradise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hell - the place of eternal punishment; considered the opposite of paradise
  • synonym: paradise - an ideal or idyllic place; often used as a synonym in religious contexts
