hit

verb
suddenly affect (idea, emotion, realization)
Pronunciation: / hɪt / (AmE), / hɪt / (BrE)
to affect someone or something suddenly and strongly, often in a negative or emotional way

Examples

Hit with news
A wave of sadness hit him after he heard the news.
It suddenly hit me that I had left my keys in the car.
The realization of what she had lost hit her all at once.
Many businesses were hit hard by the economic crisis.
The small village was hit by a severe drought that lasted for months.
When the flu season hit, the hospital quickly filled with patients.
