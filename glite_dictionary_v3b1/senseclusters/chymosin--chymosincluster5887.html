chymosin

noun
enzyme causing milk to coagulate
9.2 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkaɪ.mə.sɪn/ (AmE), /ˈkaɪ.mə.sɪn/ (BrE)
an enzyme found in the stomachs of young mammals that causes the coagulation of milk, used in cheese making

Examples

Chymosin enzyme
Chymosin is crucial in the cheese-making process to help milk curdle.
Scientists have developed microbial methods to produce chymosin for commercial use.
Young calves produce chymosin in their stomachs to digest their mother's milk.
