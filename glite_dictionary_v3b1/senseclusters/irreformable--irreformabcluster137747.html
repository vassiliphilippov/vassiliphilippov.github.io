irreformable

adjective
unable to be changed
Pronunciation: / ˌɪriˈfɔrməbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪriˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
not able to be changed, corrected, or reformed

Examples

An irreformable policy
The council declared the new policy irreformable, making further debate impossible.
Some traditions are considered irreformable and must be followed exactly as established.
The law was seen as irreformable by many, despite calls for change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immutable - unable to be changed
  • antonym: reformable - able to be changed or improved
  • synonym: unchangeable - impossible to change or alter
