kiddishness

noun
childlike quality
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪdɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɪdɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being childlike or immature in behavior or attitude

Examples

Childlike kiddishness
Her kiddishness brought a sense of fun to the otherwise serious meeting.
Despite his age, he never lost the kiddishness that made him so approachable.
Some people find a little kiddishness charming in adults.

Related Terms

  • synonym: childishness - the quality of behaving like a child
  • antonym: maturity - the quality of being fully grown or developed in behavior
