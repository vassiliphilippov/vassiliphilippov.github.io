anima

noun
soul or spirit
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪmə / (AmE), / ˈænɪmə / (BrE)
the soul, spirit, or vital force of a person, especially in philosophical or psychological contexts

Examples

Human anima
In Jungian psychology, the anima represents the feminine side of a man's personality.
The philosopher wrote about the anima as the source of human consciousness.
Many ancient cultures believed the anima left the body at death.

Related Terms

  • antonym: body - the physical structure of a person or animal
  • synonym: psyche - the human soul, mind, or spirit
