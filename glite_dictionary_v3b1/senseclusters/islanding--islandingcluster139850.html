islanding

noun
independent power operation
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪləndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪləndɪŋ / (BrE)
the condition or process where part of an electrical power system becomes or is made isolated from the main grid and operates independently

Examples

Islanding in power systems
Engineers monitored the system to prevent islanding during the scheduled maintenance of the main grid.
Unintentional islanding can pose safety risks for utility workers and equipment.
The solar panels enabled islanding when the neighborhood lost connection to the central power supply.
The engineer explained the risks of islanding in the power grid.
During the storm, islanding allowed the local network to operate independently from the main grid.
Proper safeguards are needed to prevent accidental islanding in distributed energy systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blackout - a complete loss of electrical power in an area
  • antonym: connection - the act of joining or linking things together
  • synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
