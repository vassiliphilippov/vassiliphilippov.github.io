cosponsorship

noun
joint sponsorship
5.8
Pronunciation: / koʊˈspɑːnsɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kəʊˈspɒnsəʃɪp / (BrE)
the act of jointly supporting or sponsoring something (such as a bill, proposal, or event) with others

Examples

The cosponsorship of senators
The new law passed easily thanks to the cosponsorship of several influential senators.
Her cosponsorship of the charity event helped attract more donors and volunteers.
Without bipartisan cosponsorship, the bill would have struggled to gain support.
The cosponsorship of the new education bill included members from both major political parties.
Without strong cosponsorship, the proposal struggled to gain enough support in the committee.
Her cosponsorship of the event helped attract more attention and funding from the community.
