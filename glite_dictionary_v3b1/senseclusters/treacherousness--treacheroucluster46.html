treacherousness

noun
quality of being dangerous or hazardous
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛtʃərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃərəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being dangerous due to hidden risks, hazards, or untrustworthiness.

Examples

The treacherousness of roads
Sailors respect the treacherousness of those rocky shores, especially in stormy weather.
The treacherousness of the icy mountain path made the climb exceptionally risky for hikers.
We underestimated the treacherousness of the weather conditions and had to turn back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dangerousness - the quality of being dangerous
  • antonym: reliability - the quality of being dependable or safe
