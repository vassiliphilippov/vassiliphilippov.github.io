monoecy

noun
both sexes on one plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈniːsi / (AmE), / məˈniːsi / (BrE)
the condition in which a single plant has both male and female reproductive organs or flowers

Examples

Plant monoecy
Corn is a common example of a plant that exhibits monoecy.
In monoecy, both male and female flowers grow on the same individual plant.
Botanists study monoecy to understand how plants reproduce and evolve.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dioecy - the condition where male and female organs are on separate plants
