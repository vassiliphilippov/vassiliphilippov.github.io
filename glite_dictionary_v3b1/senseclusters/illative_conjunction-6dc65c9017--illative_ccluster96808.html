illative conjunction

noun
conjunction expressing inference or conclusion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈleɪtɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪtɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a conjunction that indicates that something has been inferred or concluded from a previous statement or fact

Examples

An illative conjunction phrase
In her essay, she introduced each new idea with an illative conjunction to clarify her reasoning.
Teachers explained that 'thus' serves as an illative conjunction to connect evidence with conclusions.
The word 'therefore' is commonly used as an illative conjunction in logical arguments.
