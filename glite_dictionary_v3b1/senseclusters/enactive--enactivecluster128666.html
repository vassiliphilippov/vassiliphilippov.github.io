enactive

adjective
learning by doing
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnæktɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈnæktɪv / (BrE)
relating to learning or knowledge gained through direct action or doing rather than through observation or listening

Examples

Enactive learning method
The enactive approach to education emphasizes hands-on activities and real-world problem solving.
Children often benefit from enactive learning, such as building models or acting out stories.
In science classes, enactive methods help students understand concepts by performing experiments themselves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: experiential - involving or based on personal experience
  • antonym: theoretical - based on theory rather than practical experience
