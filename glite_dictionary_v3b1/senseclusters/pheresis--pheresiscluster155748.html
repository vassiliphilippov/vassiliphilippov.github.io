pheresis

noun
procedure for separating blood components
8.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / fəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which blood is withdrawn, separated into its components, and certain components are retained while the remainder is returned to the donor.

Examples

Pheresis procedure
Pheresis is often used to treat certain autoimmune disorders by removing abnormal antibodies.
Donors who volunteer for pheresis procedures may have a shorter recovery time than those giving whole blood.
The patient underwent pheresis to collect platelets for the planned surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apheresis - the process of removing a specific component of blood and returning the rest
