biodegradation

noun
breakdown by organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊdɪˌɡreɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊdɪˌɡreɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which living organisms, such as bacteria or fungi, break down substances into simpler, natural components

Examples

The biodegradation of waste
The biodegradation of food waste helps reduce landfill pollution and produces useful compost.
Scientists study biodegradation to develop materials that break down more easily in the environment.
Some chemicals resist biodegradation and can remain harmful for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decomposition - the process of rotting or decaying by natural means
  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something from decaying or being destroyed
