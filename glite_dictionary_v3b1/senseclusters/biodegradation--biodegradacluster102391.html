the process by which living organisms, such as bacteria or fungi, break down substances into simpler, natural components

Examples

The biodegradation of waste
The biodegradation of food waste helps reduce landfill pollution and produces useful compost.
Scientists study biodegradation to develop materials that break down more easily in the environment.
Some chemicals resist biodegradation and can remain harmful for many years.

Synonyms

  • decomposition
    5.3 1/m
    decomposition is the breakdown of organic matter by natural organisms, while biodegradation is the biological process that breaks substances into simpler natural parts.
  • decay
    3.4 2/m
    decay is the rotting of organic material caused by bacteria or fungi, while biodegradation is the wider biological breakdown of many substances.
  • rot
    1.6 3/m
    rot is the soft, wet breakdown of plants or food by fungi and bacteria, while biodegradation covers many biological breakdown processes.

Related Terms
