spread

verb
make widely known
1.5 1.5
Pronunciation: /spred/ /; / /sprɪd/ (AmE), /spred/ /; / /sprɪd/ (BrE)
to become known or distributed among many people; to become widespread

Examples

To spread news
It is important not to spread false information during a crisis.
Rumors about the merger began to spread through the office within minutes.
She used social media to spread awareness of the new campaign.
Excitement spread among the students as the concert approached.
News of the victory quickly spread throughout the city.
The fire spread rapidly due to the strong winds.
Rumors about the new policy quickly spread throughout the company.
The news of her engagement spread like wildfire on social media.
Word of the restaurant's reopening began to spread among the locals.

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden from others

  • antonym: confine - to keep within limits, restrict from spreading
  • synonym: disperse - to scatter or distribute over a wide area
  • synonym: disseminate - to spread widely, especially information
