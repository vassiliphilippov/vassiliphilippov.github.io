pub

noun
bar or tavern for drinking and socializing
1.0 7.58/m
Pronunciation: / pÊŒb / (AmE), / pÊŒb / (BrE)
A place, especially in Britain or Ireland, where alcoholic drinks are served and people gather to socialize; often also provides food.

Examples

The pub near home
After the match, fans crowded into the local pub to celebrate.
Let's meet at the pub after work for a drink.
The old village pub serves traditional British meals every evening.

Synonyms

  • public house
    2.0 0.23/m
    public house is a British term for the same local where people drink and eat, while pub is the common short form used daily.
  • tavern
    3.5 6.60/m
    tavern is an older or more rustic place that serves drinks and food, while pub is the usual British community spot.
  • bar
    1.0 74/m
    bar is often just the counter or a room for serving drinks, while pub is a full local for socialising and meals.
  • taphouse
    8.1 0.00/m
    taphouse is a place focused on draft beers and taps, often modern and specialist, while pub is a broader traditional community venue.
  • saloon
    5.8 4.48/m
    saloon is an older American-style bar linked to the past Wild West, while pub is the everyday British local where people meet.
