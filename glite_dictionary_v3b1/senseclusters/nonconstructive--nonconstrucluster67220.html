nonconstructive

adjective
not providing example
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
relating to a proof or method that shows something exists or is true without providing a specific example or construction.

Examples

Nonconstructive proof
The mathematician presented a nonconstructive proof that did not give an explicit solution.
Some logical arguments are nonconstructive and only show that a solution must exist.
A nonconstructive approach can be useful when finding an example is too difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructive - providing a specific example or explicit construction
