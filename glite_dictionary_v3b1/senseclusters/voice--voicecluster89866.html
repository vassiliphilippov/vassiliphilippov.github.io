voice

noun
grammatical category: active or passive
6.7 15/m

(Linguistics) the grammatical category showing the relationship between the subject and the action (such as active or passive); (Phonetics) the quality of speech sounds caused by vibration of the vocal cords

Examples

Passive voice form
In English, the passive voice is used to focus on the receiver of the action.
Some languages have more than two grammatical voices for verbs.
The difference between active and passive voice is often tested in grammar exams.
In English, the difference between voice and voicelessness distinguishes sounds like 'z' and 's'.
Phonetics students learn to analyze the presence or absence of voice in consonants.
The term voice is important when describing how speech sounds are articulated.

Synonyms

  • voiced sound
    4.1 0.01/m
    a voiced sound is produced with vocal cord vibration in speech, while voice is the sound quality caused by that vibration.
  • voicing
    3.9 0.00/m
    voicing is the act of producing sounds with the vocal cords, while voice is the grammatical category and the sound quality from those cords.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: passive voice - A grammatical voice where the subject receives the action
  • degree higher: voiced - (of a sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords
  • degree lower: voiceless - (of a sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords
