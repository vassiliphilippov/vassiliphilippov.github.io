tricksy

adjective
deceptive or cunning
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪksi / (AmE), / ˈtrɪksi / (BrE)
skilled at deceiving; crafty or sly, often in a playful or cunning way

Examples

A tricksy answer
He gave me a tricksy answer that didn’t really address my question.
Her tricksy ways made it hard for anyone to know what she would do next.
The fox is often described as a tricksy animal in folklore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cunning - having or showing skill in achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: sly - having or showing a deceitful or cunning nature
