sound logic

noun
valid or well-founded reasoning
3.9 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / sa änd Ààl…ëÀêd í…™k / (AmE), / sa änd Ààl…íd í…™k / (BrE)
reasoning or arguments that are valid, well-founded, and based on solid principles

Examples

Sound logic and evidence
His proposal was accepted because it was supported by sound logic and evidence.
The professor emphasized the importance of using sound logic in constructing philosophical arguments.
Without sound logic, even the most passionate speech is unconvincing.

Synonyms

  • soundness
    4.9 0.62/m

  • valid argument
    3.4 0.37/m
    a valid argument follows correct logical steps without errors, while sound logic also requires strong, trustworthy starting ideas.
  • airtight argument
    4.8 0.00/m
    an airtight argument seems impossible to disprove because it covers all points, while sound logic is correct and well grounded.
  • logical argument
    4.3 0.42/m
    a logical argument follows a clear system of reasoning, while sound logic is correct and also based on true, solid principles.
