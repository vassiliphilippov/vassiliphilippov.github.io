enabler

noun
person who enables harm
Pronunciation: / ɪˈneɪblər / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪblə / (BrE)
a person who, often without intending to, allows or supports another's harmful or self-destructive behavior by preventing consequences

Examples

A enabler of addiction
By always bailing him out of debts, she became an enabler of his gambling habit.
Family members sometimes act as an enabler without realizing the harm they are causing.
Therapists warn against being an enabler in situations involving addiction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: facilitator - a person who makes an action or process easier
  • antonym: intervener - a person who steps in to stop or change harmful behavior
  • synonym: supporter - a person who gives assistance or encouragement
