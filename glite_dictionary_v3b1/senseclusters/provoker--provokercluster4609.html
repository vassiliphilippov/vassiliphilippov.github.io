provoker

noun
person who deliberately causes trouble
Pronunciation: / prəˈvoʊkər / (AmE), / prəˈvəʊkə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately causes trouble or provokes others to act; an instigator

Examples

The provoker of trouble
As the main provoker of the argument, he was blamed by everyone in the room.
The teacher quickly identified the class provoker and addressed their disruptive behavior.
Throughout history, a provoker often plays a key role in starting major conflicts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instigator - a person who initiates or encourages trouble or conflict
  • antonym: peacemaker - a person who helps to prevent or stop conflict
