slam

verb
throw or put with great force
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /slæm/ (AmE), /slæm/ (BrE)
to close, hit, or throw something with great force, usually making a loud noise

Examples

To slam the door
He slammed the door shut when he left the room.
She angrily slammed her phone onto the desk.
The player slammed the basketball through the hoop with impressive force.
He slammed the basketball through the hoop, thrilling the crowd.
She slammed the door shut as she stormed out of the room.
The book slammed onto the table, startling everyone nearby.
He accidentally slammed the car trunk and startled everyone nearby.
Please don't slam the fridge every time you close it.
She was so angry that she slammed the door behind her.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bang - make a sudden loud noise or hit with force
  • antonym: gently close - to close something softly and quietly
  • synonym: shut - to close something forcefully (such as a door)
  • degree lower: toss - to throw lightly or casually
