arbitration

noun
settlement of a dispute by an impartial decision
2.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.bɪ.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɑːr.bə.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɑː.bɪ.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɑː.bə.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of settling a dispute by having an impartial third party make a decision that is usually binding on all parties involved

Examples

Agree to arbitration
After months of failed negotiations, both companies agreed to arbitration to resolve their dispute.
The contract states that any disagreement must go to arbitration rather than to court.
The employees relied on arbitration to settle their wage claims fairly.
After months of negotiation failed, the labor dispute was finally settled through arbitration.
Many companies now include arbitration clauses in their service agreements to avoid lengthy lawsuits.
The contract required both sides to submit their disagreements to arbitration rather than going to court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mediation - settlement of a dispute by an impartial third party who helps the parties reach a mutual agreement
