cyanogenic

adjective
producing cyanide
3.7
Pronunciation: / saɪˌænəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / saɪˌanəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or organism) capable of producing cyanide, especially as a result of enzymatic breakdown.

Examples

Cyanogenic plants
Cassava roots are cyanogenic, necessitating careful processing to make them safe to eat.
Many plants in the bean family are cyanogenic and can release cyanide when damaged.
The seeds of some fruits are cyanogenic and should not be consumed in large quantities.
