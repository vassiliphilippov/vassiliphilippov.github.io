vena canaliculi cochleae

noun
vein of the cochlear canal
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪ.nə kəˌnæl.ɪˈku.li ˈkɒk.li.i / (AmE), / ˈveː.nə ka.naː.lɪˈkuː.li ˈkɒk.lɪ.iː / (BrE)
a vein located in the canal of the cochlea, responsible for draining blood from the inner ear.

Examples

Vena canaliculi cochleae vein
Damage to the vena canaliculi cochleae can affect hearing and balance.
During surgery, care must be taken not to injure the vena canaliculi cochleae.
The vena canaliculi cochleae plays a crucial role in the venous drainage of the inner ear.
