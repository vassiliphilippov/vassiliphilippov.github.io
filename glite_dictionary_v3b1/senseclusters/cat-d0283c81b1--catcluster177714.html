CAT

noun
medical imaging scan using X-rays and computers
4.6 7.61/m
Pronunciation: / si e…™ ti / (AmE), / siÀê e…™ tiÀê / (BrE)
a medical imaging method in which a computer reconstructs detailed cross-sectional images of the body from X-ray scans taken along a single axis; also known as a CT scan

Examples

A CAT scan
A CAT can help detect tumors and other abnormalities inside the body.
Advancements in CAT technology have greatly improved the accuracy of diagnoses.
The doctor ordered a CAT to get a clearer picture of the patient's head injury.

Synonyms

  • CT
    3.7 1.70/m
    CT is the common short form used in hospitals, while CAT is the older name once used for the same scan.
  • computed tomography
    3.6 0.48/m
    computed tomography is the standard full name used by doctors, while CAT is an older variant that names the same scan.
  • computed axial tomography
    4.3 0.02/m
    computed axial tomography describes images made along a single axis, while CAT is the older phrase with the same technical sense.
  • computerized axial tomography
    4.8 0.00/m
    computerized axial tomography uses computer processing and axial X-rays, while CAT is the older abbreviation for that same imaging method.
  • computerized tomography
    4.1 0.04/m
    computerized tomography names the computer-based X-ray method in plain words, while CAT is the older technical label often used historically.
