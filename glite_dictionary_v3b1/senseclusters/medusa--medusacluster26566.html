medusa

noun
free-swimming coelenterate life stage
6.2
Pronunciation: / məˈduːsə / (AmE), / mɪˈdjuːzə / (BrE)
the free-swimming, sexual stage in the life cycle of coelenterates, characterized by a gelatinous, umbrella-shaped body and tentacles

Examples

A medusa stage
During its medusa stage, the organism can move freely through the water.
Marine biologists study the transition from polyp to medusa in various species.
The jellyfish is an example of a medusa in the phylum Cnidaria.
