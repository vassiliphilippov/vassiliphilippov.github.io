enhancer

noun
DNA region boosting genes
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhænsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɑːnsə / (BrE)
a short region of DNA that increases the activity of a gene by promoting transcription

Examples

Gene enhancer
Scientists discovered an enhancer that significantly increased the expression of the target gene.
The mutation in the enhancer region led to reduced protein production in the cells.
Researchers are studying how each enhancer interacts with specific genes during development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: promoter - a DNA sequence that initiates gene transcription
  • antonym: silencer - a DNA region that decreases gene expression
