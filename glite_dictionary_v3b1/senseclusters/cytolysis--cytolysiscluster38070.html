cytolysis

noun
breakdown of cells by membrane destruction
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /saɪ.tə.ˈlɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /saɪ.tə.ˈlɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
the pathological breakdown of cells caused by the destruction of their outer membrane

Examples

cytolysis of cells
Doctors studied the cytolysis of red blood cells to understand the patient's anemia.
Excessive cytolysis can lead to tissue damage and inflammation in affected organs.
The release of intracellular enzymes is a common consequence of cytolysis during viral infections.
