safe-conduct

noun
official permission for safe passage
Pronunciation: / seɪf ˈkɑn.dʌkt / (AmE), / seɪf ˈkɒn.dʌkt / (BrE)
an official document or permission that allows a person to travel safely through a region, especially during war or conflict, often issued by a government or authority

Examples

A safe-conduct pass
During the war, refugees relied on safe-conduct papers to cross the border unharmed.
The diplomat was granted safe-conduct through enemy territory to attend the negotiations.
Without a safe-conduct, travelers risked being arrested as spies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: passport - an official document issued by a government certifying identity and citizenship and permitting travel abroad
