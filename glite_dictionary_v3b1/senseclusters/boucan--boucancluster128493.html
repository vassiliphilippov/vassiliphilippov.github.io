boucan

noun
smoked dried meat
9.9
Pronunciation: / buːˈkæn / (AmE), / buːˈkæn / (BrE)
meat that has been preserved and flavored by being dried or smoked on a wooden frame called a boucan.

Examples

Dried boucan meat
The explorers packed boucan to sustain them during their long journey through the jungle.
Traditional recipes often call for boucan as a key ingredient in Caribbean stews.
Sailors relied on boucan because it could last for weeks without spoiling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacon - salted and smoked meat from the back or sides of a pig
  • synonym: jerky - meat that has been dried and salted
