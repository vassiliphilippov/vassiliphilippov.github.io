halophile

noun
salt-loving organism
Pronunciation: / ˈhæləˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈhæləʊˌfaɪl / (BrE)
An organism, especially a microorganism, that thrives in environments with very high concentrations of salt.

Examples

A halophile microbe
Researchers discovered a new species of halophile in the salt flats.
Some halophiles can survive in salt concentrations ten times higher than seawater.
The extreme environment favored by a halophile would kill most other organisms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extremophile - organism that thrives in extreme environments
