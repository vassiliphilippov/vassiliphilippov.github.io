hyaluronic acid

noun
a lubricating substance in body tissues and fluids
2.0 4.6
Pronunciation: / haɪəˌlʊrɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪəˌljʊərɪnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a viscous substance found in human and animal tissues, especially in connective tissue, joints, and the eye, valued for its moisture-retaining and lubricating properties

Examples

Hyaluronic acid in skin
Hyaluronic acid naturally occurs in the vitreous humor of the eye, helping to keep it properly lubricated.
Many skin care products contain hyaluronic acid to help maintain hydration and smoothness.
The doctor injected hyaluronic acid into the patient's knee to relieve joint pain.
