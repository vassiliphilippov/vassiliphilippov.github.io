Rutherford atom

noun
model of atomic structure with central nucleus
4.1 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌθ.ər.fərd ˈæt.əm/ /; / /ˈrʌð.ər.fərd ˈæt.əm/ (AmE), /ˈrʌθ.ə.fəd ˈæt.əm/ /; / /ˈrʌð.ə.fəd ˈæt.əm/ (BrE)
a model of atomic structure in which all positive charge and most mass are concentrated in a central nucleus, with electrons orbiting around it

Examples

The Rutherford atom model
In science class, we discussed how the Rutherford atom revolutionized our understanding of atomic structure.
The Rutherford atom helped explain experimental results that the plum pudding model could not account for.
The Rutherford atom model was later refined by Niels Bohr to include quantized electron orbits.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Bohr atom - atomic model with electrons in quantized orbits around the nucleus
  • degree lower: plum pudding model - earlier atomic model with electrons in a positively charged 'pudding'
