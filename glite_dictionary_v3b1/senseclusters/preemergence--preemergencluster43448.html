preemergence

noun
before sprouting
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪˈmɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪˈmɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the period or treatment before something, especially a plant, appears or begins to grow above the ground

Examples

Preemergence treatment
Farmers often use preemergence herbicides to prevent weeds from growing in their fields.
The timing of preemergence treatments is crucial for effective weed control in agriculture.
Applying a preemergence product can help protect crops before any weeds appear.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postemergence - the period or treatment after plants have appeared above the ground
