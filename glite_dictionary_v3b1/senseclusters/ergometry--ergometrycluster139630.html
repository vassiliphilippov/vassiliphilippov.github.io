ergometry

noun
measurement of exercise work
Pronunciation: / ɜːrˈɡɑːmətri / (AmE), / ɜːˈɡɒmətri / (BrE)
the measurement of physical work or energy expended during exercise, often to assess physical performance or health

Examples

Ergometry test
The doctor recommended ergometry to evaluate the patient's cardiovascular response to exercise.
Researchers used ergometry to measure the athletes' endurance and energy output during training.
Modern fitness centers sometimes offer ergometry as part of their health assessment services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exercise testing - the assessment of physical fitness or health through exercise
  • antonym: spirometry - the measurement of lung function, especially the amount and speed of air inhaled and exhaled
