Ishtar

proper noun
Babylonian and Assyrian goddess of love, fertility, and war
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪʃˌtɑr / (AmE), / ˈɪʃtɑː / (BrE)
In Babylonian and Assyrian mythology, the goddess of love, fertility, and war; counterpart to the Phoenician Astarte.

Examples

Ishtar goddess of love
Ancient Mesopotamians worshipped Ishtar as the goddess of love and war.
In myth, Ishtar descended to the underworld in search of her lover.
The temple of Ishtar was one of the most important in Nineveh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Astarte - Phoenician goddess of love and fertility, equivalent to Ishtar
