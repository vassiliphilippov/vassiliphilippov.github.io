nonelastic

adjective
not responsive to change
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
not significantly affected by changes in price, income, or other economic variables; having low elasticity, especially in economics

Examples

Nonelastic demand
The demand for basic medicines is often nonelastic because people need them regardless of price changes.
In many countries, water usage is considered nonelastic since people cannot easily reduce their consumption.
Even after the tax increase, the product's sales remained steady due to its nonelastic demand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elastic - responsive to changes in price or other factors
  • synonym: inelastic - not responsive to changes in price or other factors
