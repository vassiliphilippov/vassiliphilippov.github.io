Quercus borealis

noun
species of northern red oak tree
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs bɔːˈriːəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs bɔːˈrɪːəlɪs / (BrE)
A large deciduous oak tree native to eastern North America, also known as the northern red oak, with broad leaves featuring spiny-tipped lobes and coarse-grained wood.

The Quercus borealis is prized for its shade and autumn foliage in many parks across the northeastern United States.
