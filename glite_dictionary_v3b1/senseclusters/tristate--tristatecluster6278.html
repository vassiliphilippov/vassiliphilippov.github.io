tristate

noun
three-state circuit
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌsteɪt / (BrE)
an electronic circuit or component that can output three states: high, low, or high impedance (disconnected)

Examples

Tristate buffer
The engineer used a tristate buffer to control access to the data bus.
If the tristate is not set correctly, it can cause communication errors in the circuit.
Modern microcontrollers often include tristate outputs for flexible signal management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buffer - an electronic device that isolates or separates signals
  • synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit
