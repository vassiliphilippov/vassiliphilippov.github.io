have a good one

verb
informal farewell wishing enjoyment
Pronunciation: / hæv ə ɡʊd wʌn / (AmE), / hæv ə ɡʊd wʌn / (BrE)
Used to wish someone an enjoyable day, event, or time, often as a friendly farewell or parting phrase.

Examples

To have a good one today
See you tomorrow, and have a good one at the concert tonight.
She waved as she left the shop and said, “have a good one!”
Thanks for your help today—have a good one!

Related Terms

  • synonym: take care - used to wish someone well as they leave
