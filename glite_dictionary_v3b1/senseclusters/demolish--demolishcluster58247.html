demolish

verb
defeat thoroughly and humiliatingly
5.6
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
To defeat (an opponent) thoroughly and humiliatingly.

Examples

To demolish an opponent
She demolished her opponent in the debate with clear arguments and wit.
The defending champions demolished the underdogs in the semifinal match.
The home team demolished their rivals in the finals, winning 6-0.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to be defeated
  • synonym: thrash - to defeat heavily in a contest
  • synonym: trounce - to defeat decisively
