menagerie

noun
collection of wild animals for exhibition
7.1
Pronunciation: / məˈnædʒəri / (AmE), / məˈnædʒəri /; / mɪˈnædʒəri / (BrE)
a collection of wild animals kept for exhibition or study, or the place where they are kept and shown to the public

Examples

A menagerie of animals
Children were fascinated by the elephant in the menagerie at the city fair.
In the 19th century, wealthy collectors often kept a private menagerie on their estates.
The traveling circus arrived in town with a menagerie of exotic animals.
During the 18th century, it was fashionable for the wealthy to maintain a private menagerie.
The children were fascinated by the rare birds in the royal menagerie.
The circus brought its menagerie of lions, tigers, and monkeys to town.
