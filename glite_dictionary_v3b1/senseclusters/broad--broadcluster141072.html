broad

adjective
open-minded; tolerant
Pronunciation: / brɑd / (AmE), / brɔːd / (BrE)
Having or showing willingness to consider different ideas or opinions; open-minded or tolerant.

Examples

Broad mind
His broad approach to politics made him popular with voters from different backgrounds.
She has broad sympathies and accepts people from all walks of life.
The committee took a broad view of the community's needs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liberal - willing to respect or accept behavior or opinions different from one's own
  • antonym: narrow-minded - having or showing a limited outlook
  • synonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow the existence of opinions that one does not necessarily agree with
