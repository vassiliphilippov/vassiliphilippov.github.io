distemper

noun
infectious viral disease affecting animals
7.5
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
an infectious viral disease affecting animals, typically characterized by fever, coughing, and other symptoms; notably seen in dogs and sometimes in cats and other animals.

Examples

Dog distemper virus
Many wild raccoons in the area have been affected by distemper this season.
The veterinarian diagnosed the puppy with distemper after noticing its persistent cough and fever.
Vaccination is essential to prevent distemper outbreaks among shelter animals.
