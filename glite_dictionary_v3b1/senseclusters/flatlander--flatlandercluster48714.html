flatlander

noun
two-dimensional being
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌlændər / (AmE), / ˈflætˌlændə / (BrE)
a fictional being who lives in a two-dimensional world, especially as described in Edwin A. Abbott's novella Flatland

Examples

Flatlander in Flatland
In the story, a flatlander cannot imagine the concept of height or depth.
The scientist used the idea of a flatlander to explain higher dimensions to the students.
A flatlander would see a sphere as a circle that changes size as it passes through their world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Flatland - a novella by Edwin A. Abbott about a two-dimensional world
