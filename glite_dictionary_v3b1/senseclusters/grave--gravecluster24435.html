grave

adjective
serious; causing alarm or danger
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪv / (AmE), / ɡreɪv / (BrE)
serious, important, or showing deep thought or dignity

Examples

Ignoring these grave warnings could have disastrous consequences.

She understood the grave consequences of her actions before making a decision.
The president addressed the nation with a grave expression, aware of the crisis at hand.
We must consider these grave concerns before proceeding with the project.
He delivered the news in a grave and respectful tone.
Her father's grave demeanor set the mood for the ceremony.
With a grave expression, she promised to support her friend no matter what.
