stemma

noun
tree diagram of manuscript transmission
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛmə / (AmE), / ˈstiːmə, ˈstɛmə / (BrE)
A diagram, resembling a tree, that illustrates the hypothesized relationships and transmission of manuscripts of a literary work.

Examples

Manuscript stemma
Building a stemma requires analyzing similarities and differences in the manuscripts.
Scholars constructed a stemma to trace the history of the medieval texts.
The stemma clarified relationships among several corrupted manuscript copies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stemma codicum - A detailed stemma specific to manuscript traditions
