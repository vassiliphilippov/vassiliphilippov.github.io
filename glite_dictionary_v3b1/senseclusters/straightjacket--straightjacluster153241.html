straightjacket

noun
garment used to restrain movement
8.2 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈstreɪt.dʒæk.ɪt/ /; / /ˈstreɪt.dʒæk.ət/ (AmE), /ˈstreɪt.dʒæk.ɪt/ /; / /ˈstreɪt.dʒæk.ət/ (BrE)
A strong garment with long sleeves that can be tied behind the back, used to restrain the arms of a violent or mentally ill person.

Examples

A straightjacket garment
He felt as if the strict rules acted like a straightjacket on his creativity.
The old asylum displayed a straightjacket as part of its medical history exhibit.
The patient was put in a straightjacket after repeated attempts to break free.
