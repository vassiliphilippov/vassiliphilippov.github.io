shallowness

noun
lack of depth in knowledge or feeling
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking depth, especially in knowledge, understanding, or feeling

Examples

Shallowness of ideas
Critics pointed out the shallowness of the film's characters and plot.
His shallowness became obvious during the serious discussion about politics.
She was frustrated by the shallowness of their conversation, which never moved beyond small talk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depth - profoundness; the quality of being deep in knowledge or feeling
  • synonym: superficiality - the quality of being superficial; lack of thoroughness or depth
