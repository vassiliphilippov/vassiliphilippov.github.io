hurrier

noun
mine cart puller
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜriər / (AmE), / ˈhʌriə / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who pulled or pushed coal carts in mines in the past

Examples

A coal mine hurrier
In the 19th century, a hurrier would spend hours underground moving heavy coal tubs.
The museum displayed a photograph of a young hurrier from a Yorkshire coal mine.
Many hurriers were children who worked in dangerous and difficult conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miner - a person who works in a mine extracting minerals
  • synonym: trapper - a child who opened and closed ventilation doors in mines
