disrupted

adjective
marked by breaks or gaps
3.1
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrʌptɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptɪd / (BrE)
broken or interrupted by gaps or breaks; not continuous or smooth.

Examples

Supply chains disrupted
After the earthquake, many supply chains were disrupted, leading to delays in deliveries.
The meeting was disrupted by frequent technical issues, making it hard to follow discussions.
Train services remained disrupted throughout the morning due to severe weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - without interruption or gaps; unbroken
  • synonym: interrupted - stopped for a period or broken in continuity
  • antonym: unbroken - not broken, whole, or uninterrupted
