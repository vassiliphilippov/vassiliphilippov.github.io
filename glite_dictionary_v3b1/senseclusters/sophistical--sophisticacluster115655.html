sophistical

adjective
plausible but misleading; fallacious
3.0
Pronunciation: / səˈfɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
Seemingly plausible but actually fallacious or misleading, especially in reasoning or argument.

Examples

A sophistical argument
His argument may seem convincing, but it is actually sophistical and designed to mislead.
She was wary of sophistical rhetoric meant to obscure the real issue.
The lawyer dismissed the opposing counsel’s sophistical reasoning as irrelevant to the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallacious - based on a mistaken belief
  • antonym: sound - based on valid reasoning
  • synonym: specious - superficially plausible, but actually wrong
