ecumenical council

noun
formal church assembly on doctrine
8.0 7.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kəl ˈkaʊn.səl/ /; / /e.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kəl ˈkaʊn.səl/ (AmE), /ɪ.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kəl ˈkaʊn.səl/ /; / /iː.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kəl ˈkaʊn.səl/ (BrE)
A formal gathering of bishops and church leaders from the whole Christian world convened to decide on issues of doctrine, faith, morals, and church discipline.

Examples

The ecumenical council meeting
Scholars still debate the canons established by the seventh ecumenical council.
The decisions of the first ecumenical council greatly shaped early Christian doctrine.
The idea of an ecumenical council remains central in discussions about church unity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: synod - a council of church officials, usually less universal than an ecumenical council
