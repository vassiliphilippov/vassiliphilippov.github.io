soothing syrup

noun
calming medicinal syrup
Pronunciation: / ˈsuðɪŋ ˈsɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈsuːðɪŋ ˈsɪrəp / (BrE)
a medicinal syrup formulated to have a calming or sedative effect, especially used for children

Examples

Old-fashioned soothing syrup
Old-fashioned soothing syrup was once given to teething infants, though it is rarely used today.
She took a spoonful of soothing syrup before bedtime to ease her cough and help her relax.
The doctor prescribed a soothing syrup to help the child sleep through the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sedative - a substance that calms or induces sleep
  • antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
