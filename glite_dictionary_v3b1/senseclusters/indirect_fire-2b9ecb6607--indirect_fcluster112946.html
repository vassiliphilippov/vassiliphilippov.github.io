indirect fire

noun
military firing at unseen targets
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndəˈrɛkt ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkt ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
fire delivered at a target that is not directly visible to the person aiming the weapon, often using calculations or external observation to adjust aim.

Examples

Indirect fire support
Coordinating indirect fire requires accurate communication with forward observers.
Modern warfare relies heavily on indirect fire from mortars and howitzers.
The artillery unit used indirect fire to strike enemy positions behind the hill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct fire - fire delivered at a visible target
