slapdash

adjective, adverb
carelessly or haphazardly done
Pronunciation: / ˈslæpˌdæʃ / (AmE), / ˈslæp.dæʃ / (BrE)
done in a careless or hurried way, or in a careless and hasty manner

Examples

A slapdash report
He handed in a slapdash report that was full of errors and incomplete sentences.
Her slapdash approach to packing meant she forgot several important items.
The renovations were so slapdash that the paint began peeling within weeks.
She packed her suitcase slapdash before leaving for the airport.
The painting looked slapdash, with paint splatters everywhere.
The repair work was done slapdash and had to be redone soon after.
