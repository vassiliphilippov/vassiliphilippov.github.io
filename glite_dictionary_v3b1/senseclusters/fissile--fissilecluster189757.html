fissile

adjective
capable of undergoing nuclear fission
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪsaɪl / (BrE)
(of a material or atom) capable of undergoing nuclear fission when struck by a neutron, usually releasing energy and neutrons.

Examples

Fissile nuclear material
Only certain heavy nuclei are fissile and suitable for sustaining a chain reaction.
Scientists search for fissile materials to ensure nuclear safety and control.
Uranium-235 is a fissile isotope used in nuclear reactors and weapons.
