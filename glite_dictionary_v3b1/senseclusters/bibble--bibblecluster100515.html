bibble

verb
to babble incoherently
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪbəl / (BrE)
to speak rapidly and incoherently, often saying foolish or meaningless things

Examples

To bibble nonsense
He started to bibble nervously when asked about the missing documents.
After staying up all night, she could only bibble during the morning meeting.
The children began to bibble excitedly about their weekend adventures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: articulate - to express ideas clearly and effectively
  • synonym: babble - to talk quickly in a way that is difficult to understand
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
