big

adjective
serious or severe in effect
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ / (AmE), / bɪɡ / (BrE)
(of an event or feeling) serious or severe; having a significant impact or intensity.

Examples

A big problem
He was in a big panic after losing his passport at the airport.
That was a big mistake, and it affected the whole project.
The company faced a big crisis when its main client left.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minor - not serious or severe; less important
  • synonym: severe - very great; intense (describing degree of seriousness)
