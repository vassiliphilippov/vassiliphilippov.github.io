unsaturated compound

noun
compound with double or triple carbon bonds
4.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd ˈkɑmpaʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsatʃʊˌreɪtɪd ˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing at least one carbon-carbon double or triple bond, such as those found in alkenes or alkynes

Examples

An unsaturated compound
During the experiment, we detected the presence of an unsaturated compound using a bromine water test.
Many plant oils contain a high proportion of unsaturated compounds that are thought to be healthier for the heart.
Unlike saturated fats, unsaturated compounds have at least one double or triple bond between carbon atoms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: saturated compound - a compound in which all carbon atoms are connected by single bonds
