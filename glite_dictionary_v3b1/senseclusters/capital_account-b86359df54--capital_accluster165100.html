capital account

noun
finance: account of business net worth
5.4 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.pɪ.təl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.pɪ.təl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (BrE)
(finance) an account that shows the net worth of a business at a specific date, typically recording the owner's equity or accumulated funds.

Examples

Business capital account
At the end of each accounting period, the capital account indicates the net worth of the partnership.
The auditor reviewed the capital account to ensure all equity transactions were properly recorded.
The company's capital account reflects all owner investments and withdrawals over the fiscal year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: current account - account showing a firm's day-to-day financial transactions
