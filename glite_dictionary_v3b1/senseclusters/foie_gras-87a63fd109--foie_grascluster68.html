foie gras

noun
rich pâté from fattened goose or duck liver
Pronunciation: / fwɑ ˈɡrɑ / (AmE), / fwɑː ˈɡrɑː / (BrE)
A rich pâté made from the liver of a specially fattened goose or duck, often marinated in Cognac and sometimes with truffles.

Examples

Rich foie gras dish
Many gourmets consider foie gras to be the ultimate luxury food.
She tried foie gras for the first time during her trip to Paris.
The chef prepared an exquisite appetizer featuring foie gras and toasted brioche.
