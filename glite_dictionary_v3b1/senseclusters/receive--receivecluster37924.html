receive

verb
accept or believe as true or valid
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsiv / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːv / (BrE)
to accept or believe something as true or valid, especially in a formal or religious context

Examples

To receive the message
During the ceremony, new members are asked to receive the faith.
He chose to receive her explanation without any suspicion.
Many people were hesitant to receive the new doctrine at first.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accept - to consent to receive or undertake something offered
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe, or consider
