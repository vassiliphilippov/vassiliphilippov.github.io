Synonyms

  • twitch
    3.5 3.06/m
    twitch is a sudden small involuntary muscle movement often from nerves, while twitching is repeated or ongoing minor spasms.
  • vellication
    9.9 0.00/m
    vellication is a technical term for a sudden involuntary muscle spasm, while twitching is the common everyday word for that action.
  • fasciculation
    9.9 0.01/m
    fasciculation is a brief involuntary twitch of a small muscle group seen under skin, while twitching often means small visible jerks.
  • myokymia
    9.9 0.03/m
    myokymia is a medical condition causing fine, continuous muscle quivering around the eyes or face, while twitching is more general.
  • myoclonus
    9.9 0.15/m
    myoclonus is a sudden involuntary muscle jerk that can be strong, while twitching usually refers to small, brief muscle spasms.
  • muscle spasm
    3.4 0.39/m
    muscle spasm is a sudden involuntary contraction often causing pain, while twitching usually means small, painless muscle movements.
  • spasm
    3.4 0.37/m
    spasm is a sudden strong contraction that can be painful or long, while twitching is usually a quick, small involuntary movement.
