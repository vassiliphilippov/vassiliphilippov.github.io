Athena

proper noun
Greek goddess of wisdom and war
Pronunciation: / əˈθiːnə / (AmE), / əˈθiːnə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of wisdom, practical arts, and war; protector of Athens, often identified with the Roman goddess Minerva.

Examples

Athena goddess of wisdom
In mythology, Athena sprang fully armed from the head of Zeus.
Many sculptures depict Athena wearing a helmet and holding a shield.
The Parthenon was built in honor of Athena, the patron goddess of Athens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Minerva - Roman goddess of wisdom and arts, equivalent to Athena
