crustal

adjective
relating to a planetary crust
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌstl̩ / (BrE)
relating to the outermost solid layer (crust) of the Earth or another planetary body

Examples

Crustal layers
Geologists study the crustal layers to understand the Earth's history.
Scientists found evidence of crustal changes after the meteorite impact.
The crustal movement in this region has caused frequent earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crust - the outermost solid shell of a planet, such as Earth or Moon
