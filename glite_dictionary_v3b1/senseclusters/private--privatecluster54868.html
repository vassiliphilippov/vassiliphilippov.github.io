private

noun
lowest military rank (soldier)
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvət / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət / (BrE)
a soldier of the lowest enlisted rank in the Army or Marines

Examples

Army private
After training, she was promoted from private to corporal.
As a private, he followed his sergeant's orders closely.
The young private stood at attention during roll call.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: recruit - a newly enlisted soldier, sometimes used as a lower rank
  • degree higher: sergeant - a noncommissioned officer rank above private
