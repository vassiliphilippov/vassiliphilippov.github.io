legal guardian

noun
person or institution appointed to manage another's affairs
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈliɡəl ˈɡɑrdiən / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈɡɑːdiən / (BrE)
a person or institution who has been legally appointed to care for and manage the affairs of another person, especially when that person is unable to manage their own affairs

Examples

A legal guardian signs
Each minor must have a legal guardian sign the consent form before participating.
The court may name a legal guardian to oversee a child's welfare and assets.
Under state law, her uncle was appointed her legal guardian after her parents died.
