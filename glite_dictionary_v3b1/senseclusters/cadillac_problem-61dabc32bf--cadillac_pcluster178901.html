Cadillac problem

noun
too many good choices dilemma
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkædɪˌlæk ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / ˈkædɪlæk ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
a dilemma arising from having several attractive or high-quality options to choose from

Examples

A Cadillac problem dilemma
After getting multiple job offers from top companies, Mark found himself with a Cadillac problem.
Having to choose between three beautiful homes was truly a Cadillac problem.
With so many excellent restaurants nearby, deciding where to eat turned into a Cadillac problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: first-world problem - a trivial issue arising from living in a wealthy or developed society
