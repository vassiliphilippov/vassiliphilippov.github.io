diminishingly

adverb
in a decreasing way
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that becomes less and less, or to an extremely small or negligible degree

Examples

To diminishingly small degree
The benefits of the medication appear diminishingly effective after several months of use.
As the project continued, the team worked diminishingly on the less important details.
The old traditions are observed diminishingly in modern society.
The chances of success became diminishingly small as the deadline approached.
Interest in the old technology is now diminishingly low among young people.
The effect was diminishingly noticeable after several rounds of testing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barely - only just; by a very small amount
  • synonym: hardly - almost not; only just
  • antonym: increasingly - in a way that becomes more or grows over time
