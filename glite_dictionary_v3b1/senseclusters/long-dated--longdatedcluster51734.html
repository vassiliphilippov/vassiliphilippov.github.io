long-dated

adjective
having a long time to redemption (finance)
2.4
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a security) having more than 15 years to run before redemption.

Examples

Long-dated securities
Due to interest rate risk, many portfolios limit their exposure to long-dated securities.
Investors are seeking long-dated bonds to lock in higher yields for the future.
The government issued a series of long-dated gilts to fund infrastructure projects.
