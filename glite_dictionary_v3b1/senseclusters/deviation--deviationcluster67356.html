deviation

noun
compass error from local magnetic effects
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the error in a compass reading caused by local magnetic disturbances, such as those from nearby metal objects or electrical equipment

Examples

Compass deviation
Before setting sail, it's important to account for any possible deviation in the compass readings.
Local magnetic fields can cause a significant deviation that must be considered to ensure accurate navigation.
The ship's navigator corrected for the deviation caused by the metal equipment near the compass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: variation - the angle between true north and magnetic north
