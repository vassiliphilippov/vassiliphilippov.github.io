Examples

Uncontrollable fury
His fury at the unfair accusation was evident to everyone in the office.
In a sudden fury, she slammed the door and stormed out of the room.
The storm raged with all the fury of a wild beast.
His fury subsided only after he had calmed down and apologized.

Synonyms

  • rage
    3.4 23/m
    rage is a sudden, intense and often uncontrollable anger, while fury is a violent, extreme form of that feeling.
  • wrath
    6.8 8.33/m
    wrath is intense, often vengeful anger used in formal or religious language, while fury is raw, violent rage.
  • enragement
    7.9 0.00/m
    enragement is the state of becoming or being very angry and impatient, while fury is more violent and overwhelming.
  • infuriation
    7.9 0.00/m
    infuriation is a strong anger often mixed with impatience at someone, while fury is more violent and extreme.
  • ire
    7.9 1.82/m
    ire is a formal, often milder anger used in speech or writing, while fury is violent and extreme in tone.
