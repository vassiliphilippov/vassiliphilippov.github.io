fury

noun
intense anger; violent agitation
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfjʊri / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəri / (BrE)
violent or intense anger; a state of extreme rage

Examples

Uncontrollable fury
His fury at the unfair accusation was evident to everyone in the office.
In a sudden fury, she slammed the door and stormed out of the room.
The storm raged with all the fury of a wild beast.
His fury subsided only after he had calmed down and apologized.
She was trembling with fury after hearing the unfair decision.
The manager's fury left everyone in the room silent and uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • antonym: calm - a peaceful, relaxed state without agitation
  • synonym: rage - intense, uncontrolled anger
  • degree higher: wrath - extreme anger, often with a desire for revenge
