demarchy

noun
government by lottery
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈmɑːr.kiː/ /; / /də.ˈmɑːr.kiː/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈmɑː.kiː/ /; / /də.ˈmɑː.kiː/ (BrE)
a system of government in which officials are selected by random lot rather than by election or appointment

Examples

Demarchy selects leaders
In a demarchy, leaders are chosen at random rather than through elections.
Some political theorists argue that demarchy could reduce corruption in government.
The ancient city-state experimented with demarchy as an alternative to traditional democracy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: sortition - the selection of officials by random lot
