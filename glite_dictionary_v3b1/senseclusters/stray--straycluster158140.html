stray

verb
to deviate from the main topic
4.0 1.98/m
Pronunciation: / streÉª / (AmE), / streÉª / (BrE)
to move away from the subject or main topic, especially in speaking or writing

Examples

To stray off topic
During the meeting, he began to stray from the main agenda and discussed unrelated issues.
Her thoughts would often stray when she was tired, making it hard to concentrate on her work.
Try not to stray from the topic when answering the exam questions.

Synonyms

  • divagate
    9.9 0.01/m
    divagate is a rare formal verb used in writing to wander from a subject, while stray is the common everyday term.
  • digress
    6.8 1.37/m
    digress means leaving the main subject temporarily in speech or writing, while stray simply describes moving away from the topic.
  • wander
    3.3 3.92/m
    wander often means attention or speech drifts off slowly, while stray refers specifically to moving away from the subject in speech or writing.
  • fly off at a tangent
    8.9 0.00/m
    fly off at a tangent is a sudden, often wild change to an unrelated subject, while stray is a more neutral move away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digress - to depart from the main subject in speech or writing
  • antonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort; opposite of stray in this context
  • synonym: wander - to go off course or deviate from a subject in thought or speech
