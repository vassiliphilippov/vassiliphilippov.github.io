3-hitter

noun
baseball: game allowing only 3 hits
Pronunciation: / ˈθriˌhɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈθriːˌhɪtə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A game in which a pitcher allows the opposing team only three hits.

Examples

A 3-hitter game
Despite several errors in the field, her 3-hitter kept the game out of reach for the opposition.
Fans cheered as he completed his second 3-hitter of the season.
The pitcher threw a remarkable 3-hitter to secure the team's victory.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: 4-hitter - a game in which the pitcher allows four hits
  • degree lower: no-hitter - a game in which no hits are allowed by the pitcher
