Ciconia nigra

noun
species of black stork
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkoʊniə ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / sɪˈkəʊniə ˈnɪɡrə / (BrE)
A species of Old World stork with glossy black upperparts and white underparts, commonly called the black stork.

Examples

The Ciconia nigra bird
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Ciconia nigra in several countries.
During migration, the Ciconia nigra can be spotted in wetlands across southern Europe.
Unlike the white stork, the Ciconia nigra is shy and prefers remote forested areas.
