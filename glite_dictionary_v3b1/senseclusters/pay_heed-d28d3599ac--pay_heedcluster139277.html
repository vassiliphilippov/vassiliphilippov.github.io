pay heed

verb
give close attention
4.1
Pronunciation: / peɪ hid / (AmE), / peɪ hiːd / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to something or someone, especially advice or a warning

Examples

To pay heed to advice
Many people failed to pay heed to the expert's advice on cybersecurity.
She always pays heed to her grandmother's stories, knowing they contain important lessons.
You should pay heed to the weather warnings before planning your hike.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to refuse to pay attention to
  • synonym: take heed - to pay attention or take notice
