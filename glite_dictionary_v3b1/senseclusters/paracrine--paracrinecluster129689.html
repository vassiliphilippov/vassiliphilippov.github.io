paracrine

noun
local signaling substance
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpær.ə.kraɪn/ /; / /ˈpær.ə.krɪn/ (AmE), /ˈpær.ə.kraɪn/ /; / /ˈpær.ə.krɪn/ (BrE)
a signaling substance, such as a hormone or growth factor, released by a cell to affect nearby cells

Examples

A paracrine molecule
The tumor produced a paracrine that stimulated blood vessel growth in nearby tissue.
Researchers studied how the paracrine released by immune cells affected inflammation in the surrounding area.
A paracrine can influence the behavior of neighboring cells without entering the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autocrine - a signaling substance that acts on the same cell that released it
  • antonym: endocrine - a signaling substance that acts on distant cells via the bloodstream
