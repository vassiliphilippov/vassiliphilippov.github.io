turn out

verb
produce quickly or regularly (often with machinery)
3.5 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn a ät / (AmE), / t…úÀên a ät / (BrE)
to produce something, especially quickly or in large quantities, often using machinery

Examples

Factories turn out products
The factory can turn out thousands of units every day.
They managed to turn out enough masks for the entire hospital in just a week.
This company turns out high-quality furniture at an impressive rate.

Synonyms

  • manufacture
    3.3 14/m
    manufacture means making large quantities with machines in a factory, while turn out emphasizes producing quickly or in large numbers.
  • produce
    2.0 143/m
    produce means creating or growing things in general, while turn out focuses on making many items fast, often by machines.
  • yield
    3.6 28/m
    yield gives or results in an amount, often profit or crops, while turn out means physically making many items fast by machines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manufacture - to make goods in large quantities using machinery
  • synonym: produce - to make or grow something for sale or use
