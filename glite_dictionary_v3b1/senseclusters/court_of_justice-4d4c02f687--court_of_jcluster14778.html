court of justice

noun
tribunal administering justice by law
3.8 0.14/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrt …ôv Ààd í åst…™s / (AmE), / k…îÀêt …ôv Ààd í åst…™s / (BrE)
a tribunal presided over by a judge or judges that has authority to decide legal disputes and administer justice according to law

Examples

The court of justice
A court of justice is responsible for ensuring that laws are fairly applied.
International disputes may sometimes be resolved by a special court of justice.
The defendant will appear before the court of justice to answer the charges.

Synonyms

  • court of law
    3.2 2.64/m
    court of law applies formal legal rules and procedures when deciding cases, while court of justice focuses on delivering justice under law.
  • law court
    3.1 0.24/m
    law court hears legal cases and gives formal judgments, while court of justice stresses the authority to administer justice under law.
  • court
    1.4 186/m
    court names the place or group that hears cases, while court of justice names the tribunal that decides disputes and enforces legal justice.
  • tribunal
    7.4 7.07/m
    tribunal is a body or panel that may include non-judge members and handle special disputes, while court of justice is a formal judge-led tribunal that decides legal cases.
