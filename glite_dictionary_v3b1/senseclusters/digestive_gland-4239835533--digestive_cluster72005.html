digestive gland

noun
a gland producing secretions for digestion
2.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʒɛstɪv ɡlænd / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪv ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland that produces and releases substances into the digestive tract to aid digestion

Examples

A digestive gland
Problems with a digestive gland can lead to various digestive disorders.
Salivary glands are also considered major digestive glands because they secrete enzymes that begin the process of digestion.
The human liver is the largest digestive gland in the body.
