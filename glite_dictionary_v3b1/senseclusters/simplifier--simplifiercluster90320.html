simplifier

noun
one who oversimplifies
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪm.plɪ.faɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈsɪm.plɪ.faɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a person who makes something simpler, often by removing important details or distinctions

Examples

A careless simplifier
Some critics accused the author of being a simplifier who ignored the complexity of the issue.
As a simplifier, she tends to leave out important facts in her explanations.
The debate suffered because a simplifier reduced the arguments to black and white terms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complicator - a person who makes something more complex or difficult
  • synonym: reducer - a person who makes something smaller or less complex
