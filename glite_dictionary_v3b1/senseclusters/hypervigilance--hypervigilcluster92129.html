hypervigilance

noun
extreme alertness
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈvɪdʒɪləns / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈvɪdʒɪləns / (BrE)
a state of being extremely alert and watchful, usually because of anxiety, trauma, stress, or a mental health condition

Examples

Extreme hypervigilance
After the accident, his hypervigilance made it hard for him to sleep at night.
People with PTSD may experience hypervigilance in crowded or unfamiliar places.
Her constant hypervigilance led to exhaustion and difficulty concentrating at work.
After the incident, she experienced hypervigilance and was constantly on the lookout for danger.
Security officers are trained to maintain hypervigilance during large public events.
Prolonged hypervigilance can lead to exhaustion and difficulty relaxing.
After the accident, he experienced hypervigilance and was easily startled by loud noises.
Therapists often help patients manage hypervigilance as part of treating post-traumatic stress disorder.
Her hypervigilance made it difficult for her to relax in crowded places.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alertness - the quality of being quick to notice and respond to danger or problems
  • antonym: inattention - lack of attention or focus
  • degree higher: paranoia - an irrational and persistent feeling that people are 'out to get you'
  • degree lower: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for danger or difficulties
