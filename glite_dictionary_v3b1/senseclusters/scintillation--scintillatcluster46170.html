scintillation

noun
a flash or sparkle of light
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪn(t)ɪˈleɪʃən /; / ˌsɪn.t̬ɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a flash or sparkle of light; the quality of shining brightly or with a sparkling effect

Examples

Star scintillation
Stars twinkled with a gentle scintillation in the clear night sky.
The scintillation of sunlight on the water made the lake appear magical.
The scintillation of the diamond caught everyone's eye under the bright lights.
Her eyes had a scintillation that caught everyone's attention in the room.
Radio astronomers must account for the scintillation of signals caused by the atmosphere.
The scintillation of the stars was clearly visible in the mountain sky.
