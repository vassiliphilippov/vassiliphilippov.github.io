relation

noun
legal principle deeming acts retroactively effective
Pronunciation: / rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The principle that an act done at a later date is considered by law to have occurred at an earlier specified time.

Examples

[Law] Doctrine of relation
The judge applied the doctrine of relation to backdate the legal filing.
Their lawyer argued that the amendment's relation ensured it was valid from the original date.
Understanding relation is important when determining the effect of certain legal documents.
