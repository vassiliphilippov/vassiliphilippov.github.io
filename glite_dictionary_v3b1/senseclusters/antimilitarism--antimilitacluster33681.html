antimilitarism

noun
opposition to militarism
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈmɪlɪtəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈmɪlɪtərɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or advocacy of opposition to military power, influence, or institutions

Examples

Strong antimilitarism belief
The organization is known for its strong antimilitarism and support for peaceful solutions.
Many activists in the early 20th century promoted antimilitarism to prevent another world war.
His antimilitarism was shaped by his experiences growing up in a war-torn country.
The rise of antimilitarism in the country led to widespread protests against conscription.
Many political movements in the early twentieth century were influenced by antimilitarism.
His strong antimilitarism made him refuse to participate in any military activities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: militarism - the belief in maintaining a strong military and using it aggressively
  • synonym: pacifism - the belief that all disputes should be settled by peaceful means
