outpass

noun
temporary exit permit
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtpæs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtpɑːs / (BrE)
a written or official permit allowing someone to leave a place temporarily, especially from a school, hospital, or workplace

Examples

A hospital outpass
Before leaving the hospital for a few hours, he requested an outpass from the nurse.
Students must show their outpass to the security guard when exiting the school during class hours.
Her supervisor signed an outpass so she could attend a family emergency outside the workplace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pass - an official document allowing someone to enter or leave a place
  • synonym: permit - an official document giving permission to do something
