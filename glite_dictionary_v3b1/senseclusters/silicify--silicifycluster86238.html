silicify

verb
to impregnate with silica
Pronunciation: / sɪˈlɪsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈlɪsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to turn into or impregnate with silica, especially through natural geological processes

Examples

To silicify wood
Over time, the buried wood began to silicify, resulting in petrified logs.
Geologists study rocks that silicify to understand ancient environmental conditions.
The process can silicify organic material, preserving its microscopic structure for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissolve - to cause a solid to become incorporated into a liquid
  • synonym: fossilize - to preserve an organism by turning it into a fossil
