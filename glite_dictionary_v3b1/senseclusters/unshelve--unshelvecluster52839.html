unshelve

verb
to revive a plan
6.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃɛlv / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɛlv / (BrE)
to bring a plan, idea, or project back into active consideration or use after it was set aside or postponed

Examples

To unshelve a project
The board decided to unshelve the proposal after receiving new funding.
After several years, the museum will finally unshelve its plans for a new exhibit.
The company chose to unshelve the project in response to increased market demand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postpone - to delay or put off to a later time
  • synonym: revive - to bring something back into use or activity
  • antonym: shelve - to set aside or postpone a plan or idea
