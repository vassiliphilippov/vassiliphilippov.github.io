depersonalization

noun
removal of personal identifiers from data
8.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.pɜːr.sə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.pɜːr.sə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.pɜː.sə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.pɜː.sə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of removing or obscuring personal identifiers from data or records so that individuals cannot be readily identified.

Examples

Data depersonalization
The firm performed depersonalization on its customer database to protect users' privacy.
Before sharing research data, the team applied depersonalization to remove direct identifiers.
Many regulations require depersonalization of health records before they are released publicly.
Before publishing the survey results, the team performed depersonalisation to remove all names and contact details.
Under GDPR, researchers must use depersonalisation techniques to protect participants' identities in shared datasets.
The company applied depersonalisation to user logs, replacing email addresses with randomized identifiers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anonymisation - the act of making data anonymous so individuals cannot be identified
  • synonym: anonymization - the process of making data anonymous so that it cannot be linked to a specific person
  • synonym: de-identification - the removal or masking of personal identifiers from data to prevent identification of individuals
  • synonym: pseudonymisation - replacing identifying fields with pseudonyms so data cannot be directly linked to individuals
