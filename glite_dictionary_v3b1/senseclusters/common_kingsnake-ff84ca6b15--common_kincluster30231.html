common kingsnake

noun
a widespread U.S. snake with yellow bands
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈkɪŋsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkɪŋsneɪk / (BrE)
a widespread nonvenomous snake of the United States, typically black or brown with yellow bands, found in various habitats except the northern regions

Examples

The common kingsnake snake
In some areas, the common kingsnake helps control rodent and snake populations.
Many herpetologists admire the striking pattern of the common kingsnake.
The common kingsnake is known for its immunity to the venom of other snakes.

Related Terms
