kick someone when they are down

verb
treat badly when someone is vulnerable
Pronunciation: / kɪk ˈsʌmwʌn wɛn ðeɪ ɑr daʊn / (AmE), / kɪk ˈsʌmwən wɛn ðeɪ ə daʊn / (BrE)
to attack, criticize, or treat someone badly when they are already experiencing difficulties or are in a vulnerable position

Examples

To kick the company when it was down
Critics began to kick the company when it was down after its stock plummeted.
It’s unfair to kick someone when they are down instead of supporting them.
She lost her job and her so-called friends started to kick her when she was down.
