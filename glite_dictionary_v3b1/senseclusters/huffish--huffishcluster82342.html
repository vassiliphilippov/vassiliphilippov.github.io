huffish

adjective
sullen or moody (archaic)
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhʌfɪʃ / (BrE)
sullen, moody, or inclined to take offense easily.

Examples

A huffish mood
After the disagreement, she remained huffish and avoided talking to anyone.
He grew huffish when no one praised his efforts at the meeting.
His huffish attitude made it difficult for others to approach him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: sullen - gloomily silent or moody
