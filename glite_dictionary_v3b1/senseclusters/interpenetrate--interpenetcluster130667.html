interpenetrate

verb
penetrate or blend into each other
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈpɛnəˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈpɛnɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to pass into or through each other so that elements mix, blend, or become interlocked

Examples

To interpenetrate deeply
In some cultures, religious and secular beliefs interpenetrate almost seamlessly.
The themes of love and loss interpenetrate throughout the novel.
The two businesses interpenetrate each other's markets through strategic partnerships.
A sense of anxiety interpenetrated the atmosphere of the courtroom as the verdict was read.
Ancient philosophies often interpenetrate, sharing ideas and influences across cultures.
The blue and red lights interpenetrated to create a mesmerizing purple glow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permeate - to spread throughout or be diffused through something
