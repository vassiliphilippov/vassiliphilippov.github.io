stitchwort

noun
small white-flowered plant
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪtʃˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈstɪtʃwɜːt / (BrE)
A low-growing plant of the carnation family, native to north temperate regions, with small white star-shaped flowers.

Examples

White stitchwort flowers
Gardeners often plant stitchwort for its simple beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
The delicate white flowers of the stitchwort brightened the forest floor in early spring.
Traditional herbalists believed that stitchwort could help relieve side pains.
