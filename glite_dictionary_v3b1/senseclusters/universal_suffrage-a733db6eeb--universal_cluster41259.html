universal suffrage

noun
the right of all adults to vote
4.7
Pronunciation: / juːˈnɪvɚsəl ˈsʌfrɪdʒ / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəsəl ˈsʌfrɪdʒ / (BrE)
The right of almost all adults to vote in political elections, regardless of sex, race, or wealth, except as disqualified by law.

Examples

Universal suffrage right
Many countries introduced universal suffrage after long democratic struggles.
The establishment of universal suffrage marked a turning point in that nation’s political history.
Women's rights activists fought for universal suffrage throughout the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: restricted suffrage - the right to vote limited by qualifications such as property or gender
  • synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in elections
