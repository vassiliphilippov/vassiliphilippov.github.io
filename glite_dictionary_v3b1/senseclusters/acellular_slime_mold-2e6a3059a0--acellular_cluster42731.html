acellular slime mold

noun
type of slime mold (Myxomycetes) with no cellular separation
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛl.jə.lɚ slaɪm moʊld / (AmE), / əˈsɛl.jʊ.lə slaɪm məʊld / (BrE)
a type of slime mold belonging to the class Myxomycetes, characterized by being a single mass of protoplasm containing many nuclei but not divided into separate cells.

Examples

An acellular slime mold network
In the biology lab, we observed an acellular slime mold forming intricate vein-like networks.
Scientists often compare acellular slime molds with cellular slime molds to study their unique life cycles.
The acellular slime mold moves as a giant multinucleate mass, searching for food on decaying logs.

  • antonym: cellular slime mold - a type of slime mold made up of individual amoeboid cells
