curtsy

verb
to bend the knees as a respectful greeting
6.5 0.42/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…úrtsi /; / Ààk…úÀêrtsi /; / Ààk…úÀêrt.si / (AmE), / Ààk…úÀêtsi /; / Ààk…úÀêt.si / (BrE)
to lower your body slightly by bending your knees, usually as a polite greeting or sign of respect (especially by women or girls)

Examples

To curtsy politely
She practiced how to curtsy gracefully for the ceremony.
The queen smiled as the little girl curtsied politely before her.
When introduced to the ambassador, Anne curtsied as a sign of respect.
She practiced curtsying in front of the mirror before the ceremony.
The little girl curtsied politely before leaving the stage.
When introduced to the ambassador, she curtsied gracefully.
At the end of the performance, the ballerina curtseyed gracefully to the audience.
The little girls spent the afternoon practicing how to curtsey properly in dance class.
Whenever she entered the room, she would curtsey before the queen.

Synonyms

  • bob
    8.6 0.02/m
    bob is a quick, small knee bend by a woman as a polite greeting, while curtsy is a fuller, formal knee bend.

Related Terms
