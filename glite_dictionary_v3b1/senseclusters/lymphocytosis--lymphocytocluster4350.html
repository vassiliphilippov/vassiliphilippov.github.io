lymphocytosis

noun
abnormally high lymphocyte count in blood
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪm.foʊ.saɪˈtoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɪm.fəʊ.saɪˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal increase in the number of lymphocytes in the circulating blood, often indicating infection or disease

Examples

High lymphocytosis count
Doctors monitor patients for lymphocytosis as part of routine blood work.
Persistent lymphocytosis may be a sign of chronic lymphocytic leukemia.
The blood test revealed lymphocytosis, suggesting a possible viral infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leukocytosis - an increase in the total number of white blood cells in the blood
  • antonym: lymphopenia - an abnormally low number of lymphocytes in the blood
