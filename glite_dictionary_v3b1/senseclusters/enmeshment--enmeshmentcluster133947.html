enmeshment

noun
being entangled
8.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ /; / /ɛn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ /; / /ɛn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ (BrE)
the state of being deeply involved or entangled in a difficult, complex, or compromising situation

Examples

Enmeshment in trouble
His sudden enmeshment in the corruption investigation shocked colleagues and worried the board.
The family's enmeshment in legal disputes lasted for years and affected their relationships.
She regretted her enmeshment in office politics after it damaged her reputation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entanglement - the state of being caught up in a complicated or difficult situation
  • synonym: involvement - the act of taking part in something, especially a complicated situation
