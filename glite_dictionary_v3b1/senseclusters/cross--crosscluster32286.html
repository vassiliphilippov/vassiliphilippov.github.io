cross

noun
Christian symbol; crucifixion representation
5.5 3.3
Pronunciation: /krɔːs/ /; / /krɑːs/ (AmE), /krɒs/ (BrE)
a structure or symbol consisting of an upright and a crosspiece, especially as a symbol of Christianity; also, a burden or serious difficulty one must endure

Examples

A silver cross
He wore a silver cross around his neck as a sign of his faith.
The church displayed a large cross above the altar.
The red cross on the flag symbolizes the region's Christian heritage.
Churches often display a large cross at the front of the sanctuary.
She wore a silver cross on a chain around her neck.
The prisoner was nailed to a wooden cross during the Roman execution.
Caring for his ill mother was a cross he bore with grace and strength.
Her chronic illness became the cross that shaped her perspective on gratitude.
We all have some cross to carry in life, but overcoming it can make us stronger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blessing - something bringing happiness or good fortune (opposite of affliction)
  • synonym: burden - a heavy load or something difficult to endure
  • synonym: crucifix - a cross with a representation of Jesus's body, especially in Catholic tradition
  • antonym: star of David - a symbol of Judaism; six-pointed star
  • synonym: trial - a test of someone's patience or endurance, a source of suffering
