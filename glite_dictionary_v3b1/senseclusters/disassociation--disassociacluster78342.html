disassociation

noun
psychological separation of mental processes
5.3 6.7
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ə.soʊ.siː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪs.ə.səʊ.siː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a psychological or mental state where a part of someone's experience, memory, or feelings becomes separated or disconnected from the rest

Examples

Emotional disassociation
After the traumatic event, he experienced disassociation and felt detached from his surroundings.
Periods of disassociation can make it difficult to remember important personal information.
Therapists often help patients understand the triggers for their disassociation.
He spoke about his childhood with a sense of disassociation from his family's struggles.
She experienced a feeling of disassociation after the traumatic incident.
The disassociation between the two events made it difficult to draw any connection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissociation - process of separating or disconnecting from something, especially in psychology
