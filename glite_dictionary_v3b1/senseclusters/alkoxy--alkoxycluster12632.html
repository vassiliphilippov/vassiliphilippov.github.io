alkoxy

noun
alkyl-oxygen group
9.4
Pronunciation: /æl.ˈkɑːk.siː/ (AmE), /æl.ˈkɒk.siː/ (BrE)
a chemical group consisting of an alkyl group bonded to an oxygen atom, often written as –OR

Examples

An alkoxy group
The presence of an alkoxy group can increase the solubility of a compound in organic solvents.
Chemists often replace a hydrogen atom with an alkoxy group to modify molecular properties.
The alkoxy substituent plays a key role in the reactivity of this molecule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkyl - a group derived from an alkane by removing one hydrogen atom
  • synonym: ether - an organic compound containing an oxygen atom bonded to two alkyl groups
