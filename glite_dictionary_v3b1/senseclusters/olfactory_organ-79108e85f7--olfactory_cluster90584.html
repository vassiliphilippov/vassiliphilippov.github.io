olfactory organ

noun
organ for the sense of smell
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑlˈfæk.tə.ri ˈɔr.ɡən / (AmE), / ɒlˈfæk.tər.i ˈɔː.ɡən / (BrE)
The anatomical organ responsible for the sense of smell, typically the nose in humans and many animals.

Examples

The olfactory organ in humans
Damage to the olfactory organ can result in a loss of the sense of smell.
Researchers studied how airborne chemicals interact with the olfactory organ.
The olfactory organ in dogs is far more sensitive than in humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nose - the external part of the face used for breathing and smelling
