generatrix

noun
geometry generating line
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒe.nə.rə.trɪks/ /; / /ˈdʒe.nə.rɪ.trɪks/ (AmE), /ˈdʒe.nə.rə.trɪks/ /; / /ˈdʒe.nə.rɪ.trɪks/ (BrE)
a line or curve that, when moved in a specific way, generates a surface or shape in geometry

Examples

Geometric generatrix
In geometry, a generatrix can be used to create a cone by rotating a straight line around an axis.
The generatrix of a cylinder is a straight line that moves parallel to itself along a path.
By moving the generatrix along a curve, you can form complex three-dimensional surfaces.
