Eucharist

noun
Christian sacrament of bread and wine
9.3 3.75/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàjuÀêk…ôr…™st / (AmE), / ÀàjuÀêk…ôr…™st / (BrE)
A Christian sacrament commemorating the Last Supper, in which bread and wine are consecrated and consumed.

Examples

The Eucharist ceremony
Catholics believe that the Eucharist is the real presence of Christ.
Receiving the Eucharist is an essential part of many Christian traditions.
The priest officiated the Eucharist during the Sunday service.

Synonyms

  • sacrament of the Eucharist
    9.8 0.04/m
    sacrament of the Eucharist is the full formal name used in church texts, while Eucharist is the common name for that sacrament.
  • Holy Eucharist
    8.1 0.12/m
    Holy Eucharist is the sacred title used in formal services and writing, while Eucharist is the general name for the same rite.
  • Holy Communion
    6.3 0.62/m
    Holy Communion names both the rite and the act of receiving bread and wine, while Eucharist refers mainly to the sacrament itself.
  • communion
    4.9 0.55/m
    communion is a short, everyday name for sharing bread and wine in church, while Eucharist is the formal sacrament name.
  • Lord's Supper
    7.7 0.45/m
    Lord's Supper is the Protestant phrase for the shared meal of Jesus, while Eucharist is the wider Christian name for the sacrament.
  • Eucharistic liturgy
    9.9 0.09/m
    Eucharistic liturgy is the full church ceremony that includes prayers and rites, while Eucharist names the sacrament of bread and wine.
  • Holy Sacrament
    9.8 0.08/m
    Holy Sacrament is a general reverent label for Christian rites, while Eucharist is the specific sacrament of bread and wine.
  • Mass
    4.9 6.31/m
    Mass is the Catholic service where the Eucharist happens, while Eucharist is the sacrament of consecrated bread and wine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mass - the celebration of the Eucharist, especially in Roman Catholic tradition
