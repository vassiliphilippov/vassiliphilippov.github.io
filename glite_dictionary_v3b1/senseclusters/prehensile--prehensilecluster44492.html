prehensile

adjective
greedy or grasping (figurative use)
7.0
Pronunciation: / prɪˈhɛnsaɪl / (AmE), / prɪˈhɛnsaɪl / (BrE)
(figurative) Immoderately desirous of acquiring, especially wealth or possessions; grasping or greedy.

Examples

Prehensile desire
He showed a prehensile attitude when negotiating for a higher salary, never satisfied with reasonable offers.
Her prehensile interests made her unpopular with her less materialistic friends.
The company's prehensile executives cut costs ruthlessly to maximize profits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • synonym: grasping - eager for gain, greedy or acquisitive
  • synonym: greedy - having an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food
