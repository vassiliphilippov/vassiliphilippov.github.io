Hammurapi

proper noun
Babylonian king and lawgiver
Pronunciation: / ˌhæmʊˈrɑːpi / (AmE), / ˌhæmjuːˈrɑːpi / (BrE)
(c. 1810–1750 BC) A Babylonian king famous for establishing one of the earliest written codes of law in ancient Mesopotamia.

Examples

Hammurapi code
Hammurapi is best known for his code, one of the oldest deciphered writings of significant length in the world.
Many scholars study the reign of Hammurapi to understand the development of early law and governance.
The legal reforms introduced by Hammurapi greatly influenced future civilizations in the region.
