cytokine

noun
protein molecules regulating immune response
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪtəˌkaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪtəkʌɪn / (BrE)
Any of a group of small protein molecules secreted by cells, especially those of the immune system, which act as signals to regulate immune responses and cell communication.

Examples

Immune cytokine signals
Certain autoimmune diseases are linked to abnormal cytokine production.
Researchers measured the levels of cytokine in the blood samples.
The release of cytokines triggers inflammation during infection.
