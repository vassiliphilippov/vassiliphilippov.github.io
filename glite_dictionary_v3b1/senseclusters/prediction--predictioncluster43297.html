prediction

noun
statement about the future
1.4
Pronunciation: / prɪˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
a statement about what will happen in the future, based on knowledge, reasoning, experience, or evidence

Examples

Economic prediction
Economic prediction is always challenging, as markets are influenced by many factors.
Her prediction about the election results turned out to be accurate.
The weather prediction said it would rain today, but the sun is shining.
Her prediction about the outcome of the election turned out to be true.
Many people rely on expert predictions when planning their investments.
The weather forecast made a prediction of heavy rain for tonight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forecast - to estimate or predict something, especially the weather
  • synonym: prophecy - a prediction, especially one inspired by divine influence
  • antonym: retrospect - a review or contemplation of things that have happened in the past
  • antonym: retrospective - looking back on or dealing with past events or situations
