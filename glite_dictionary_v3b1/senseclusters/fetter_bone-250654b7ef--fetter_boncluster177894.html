fetter bone

noun
bone between fetlock and hoof in animals
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛtər ˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈfɛtə ˌbəʊn / (BrE)
the long bone in a horse or similar animal between the fetlock and the hoof, corresponding to the pastern bone

Examples

The fetter bone fracture
Fractures in the fetter bone can cause lameness in racehorses.
Proper care of the hoof helps protect the fetter bone from stress and damage.
The veterinarian examined the horse’s fetter bone for signs of injury.
