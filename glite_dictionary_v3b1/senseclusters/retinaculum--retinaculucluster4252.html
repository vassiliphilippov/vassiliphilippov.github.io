retinaculum

noun
wing-connecting structure
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtɪˈnækjələm / (AmE), / ˌrɛtɪˈnækjʊləm / (BrE)
a hook-like or bristle structure on the forewing of certain moths and insects that connects to the hindwing to hold the wings together during flight

Examples

Moth wing retinaculum
The retinaculum helps certain moths keep their wings aligned during flight.
Scientists studied the retinaculum to understand how insects achieve stable flying patterns.
Without a functioning retinaculum, the moth's wings would not lock together properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frenulum - a small fold or ridge of tissue, also a structure in insect wings that connects forewing and hindwing
