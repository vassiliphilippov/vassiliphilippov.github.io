lather

verb
exude frothy sweat (of animals)
9.9 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈlæð.ər/ (AmE), /ˈlɑː.ðə/ /; / /ˈlæð.ə/ (BrE)
(of a horse or other animal) to produce frothy sweat or foam from exertion or excitement.

Examples

Horse lathers after running
After a long run, the horse began to lather heavily around its neck and flanks.
In hot weather, even a short ride can cause a pony to lather.
You could see the sweat lather on the horse as the race neared its end.
