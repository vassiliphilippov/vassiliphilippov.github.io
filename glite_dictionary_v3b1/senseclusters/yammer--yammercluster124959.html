yammer

verb
complain in a whining manner
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈjæmɚ /; / ˈjæmər / (AmE), / ˈjamə /; / ˈjæmə / (BrE)
to talk or make noise in a loud, repetitive, and annoying way, often complaining or whining

Examples

To yammer loudly
She yammered on about her troubles all evening.
Stop yammering and try to solve the problem.
The children began to yammer when they didn’t get dessert.
All night, the wolves yammered outside the cabin, unsettled by the storm.
In the distance, coyotes could be heard yammering in the moonlight.
The baby yammered for attention until someone picked her up.
The children started to yammer about their day as soon as they got home.
He would yammer on the phone for hours, barely letting anyone else speak.
I wish they would stop yammering and listen for a moment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be quiet - to stop talking or making noise
  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a bad-tempered way
  • synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, doleful cry or wail, like an animal
  • synonym: jabber - to talk quickly and excitedly, often making little sense
  • synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way
