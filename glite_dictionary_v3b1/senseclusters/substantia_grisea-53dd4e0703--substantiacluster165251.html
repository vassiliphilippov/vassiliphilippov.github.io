substantia grisea

noun
gray matter in the nervous system
9.3
Pronunciation: / səbˌstænʃiə ˈɡraɪsiə / (AmE), / sʌbˌstantɪə ˈɡriːsɪə / (BrE)
The grayish nervous tissue in the central nervous system, containing cell bodies as well as fibers, and forming structures such as the cerebral cortex.

Examples

The substantia grisea tissue
Damage to the substantia grisea can result in significant neurological deficits.
Researchers are studying how diseases affect the substantia grisea in the brain.
The substantia grisea of the spinal cord plays a key role in processing information.
