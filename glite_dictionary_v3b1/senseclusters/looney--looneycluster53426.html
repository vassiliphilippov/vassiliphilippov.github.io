looney

noun
insane or deranged person (informal)
9.8 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêni / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêni / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is insane, deranged, or behaving in a very eccentric manner, sometimes suggesting they might be dangerous.

Examples

A looney person
Nobody paid attention to the looney shouting on the street corner.
People thought the old man in the woods was a looney, but he was actually very wise.
She dismissed his wild conspiracy theories as the ramblings of a looney.

Synonyms

  • loony
    9.9 0.6/m
    loony is a common informal spelling used to call someone mad or odd, while looney suggests eccentricity and danger.
  • nutcase
    8.6 1/m
    nutcase is a rude term for someone seen as seriously unstable or dangerous, while looney is a looser informal insult.
  • crazy
    7.2 2/m
    crazy describes wild, unpredictable behaviour or mental illness in casual speech, while looney implies oddness and possible danger.
  • loon
    9.4 1/m
    loon is a blunt insult for someone foolish or crazy and can sound comic, while looney stresses severe eccentricity or danger.
  • weirdo
    7.0 3/m
    weirdo means someone odd or different in habits, whereas looney suggests real mental instability or a real danger.

  • synonym: madman - a man who is mentally ill or behaving wildly
