recalibration

noun
adjustment for accuracy
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkælɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkælɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adjusting or correcting a device, system, or measurement again to ensure or improve accuracy

Examples

Device recalibration
After the device started giving inaccurate readings, a complete recalibration was necessary.

Frequent recalibration helps maintain the precision of scientific instruments.
After several months of use, the equipment required a recalibration to maintain its precision.
The scientist scheduled a recalibration of the sensors before starting the new experiment.
Regular recalibration is necessary to ensure the accuracy of laboratory instruments.
