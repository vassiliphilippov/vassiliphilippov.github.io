netting

noun
loosely woven open fabric
2.7 2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
A fabric made of loosely woven threads with open spaces, often used for veils, curtains, or decorative purposes.

Examples

Protective netting
Gardeners often use netting to protect their plants from birds and insects.
She draped a layer of white netting over the table as a decorative touch.
The bride's veil was made of delicate netting that shimmered in the sunlight.
