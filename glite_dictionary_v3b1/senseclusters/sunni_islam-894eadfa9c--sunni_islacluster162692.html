Sunni Islam

noun
the largest branch of Islam
Pronunciation: / ˈsʊni ɪˈslɑm / (AmE), / ˈsʊni ɪsˈlɑːm / (BrE)
The largest branch of Islam, which accepts the first four caliphs as rightful successors to Muhammad and emphasizes the authority of the community in interpreting Islamic law and tradition.

Examples

Sunni Islam branch
Many countries in the Middle East have a majority population that follows Sunni Islam.
She studied the traditions and teachings specific to Sunni Islam during her religious education.
The differences between Sunni Islam and Shia Islam can be traced back to disagreements over the Prophet's successor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Shia Islam - the second-largest branch of Islam, distinguished by its view on the succession to Muhammad
