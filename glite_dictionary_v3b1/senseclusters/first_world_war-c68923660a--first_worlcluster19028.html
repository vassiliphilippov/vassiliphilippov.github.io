First World War

noun
global war from 1914 to 1918
2.8 2.52/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ùst Ààw…ùld w…îr / (AmE), / Àåf…úÀêst Àåw…úÀêld Ààw…îÀê / (BrE)
the war fought mainly in Europe from 1914 to 1918 between the Allies and the Central Powers; also called World War I.

Examples

The First World War
During the First World War, millions of soldiers fought in the trenches across Europe.
Historians often debate the causes of the First World War and its impact on modern society.
My great-grandfather served in the British Army during the First World War.

Synonyms

  • World War I
    3.0 0.30/m
    World War I is the common modern name used worldwide in history, while First World War is a formal alternative name.
  • Great War
    4.2 1.14/m
    Great War is an older, mainly British name used in history books, while First World War is the more neutral global name.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Second World War - the global conflict that followed, also called World War II
  • synonym: World War I - another common term for the First World War
