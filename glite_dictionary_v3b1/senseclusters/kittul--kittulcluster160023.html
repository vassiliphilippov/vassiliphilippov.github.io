A species of fishtail palm (Caryota urens) native to India and the Malay Peninsula, valued for its sap used to make brown sugar (jaggery) and for its trunk pith which yields sago.

Examples

A tall kittul tree
A tall kittul tree swayed beside the village, its leaves casting intricate shadows on the ground.
Sago is extracted from the trunk pith of the kittul palm in some rural communities.
The local farmers harvest kittul sap to produce traditional sweets like jaggery.
