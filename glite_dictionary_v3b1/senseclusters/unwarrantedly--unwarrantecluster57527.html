unwarrantedly

adverb
without justification
8.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɔːrəntɪdli /; / ʌnˈwɔrəntɪdli / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒrəntɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that is not justified, authorized, or supported by good reason or law

Examples

Blame unwarrantedly
The manager unwarrantedly blamed the team for the project's failure.
She felt that the criticism was unwarrantedly harsh and unfair.
His actions were unwarrantedly aggressive given the situation.
The manager unwarrantedly accused the employee of stealing without any evidence.
She felt her privacy had been unwarrantedly invaded by the constant questions.
The new policy was unwarrantedly strict, causing frustration among the staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illegitimately - in a way that is not allowed by law or rules
  • antonym: justifiably - in a way that can be justified
  • synonym: unjustifiably - in a way that cannot be justified
