reft

verb
to rob or plunder
9.9
Pronunciation: /reft/ (AmE), /reft/ (BrE)
to deprive someone of something by taking it away, robbing, or plundering (archaic)

Examples

They reft the land
The conquerors reft the villagers of their harvest and left them with nothing.
In the old tale, the hero was reft of his sword by a cunning thief.
The storm reft the ship of its sails, leaving it adrift at sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give - to hand over or provide something to someone
  • synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
  • synonym: rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place
