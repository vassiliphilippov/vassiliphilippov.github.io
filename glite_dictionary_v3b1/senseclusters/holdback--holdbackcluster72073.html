holdback

noun
delayed release period
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊldˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌbæk / (BrE)
an amount of money, goods, or a product that is kept back or delayed until certain conditions are met

Examples

A holdback payment
The studio enforced a six-month holdback before the movie could appear on streaming services.
A holdback was put in place to delay the show's international broadcast.
Distributors often negotiate the length of a holdback to maximize profits in different markets.
The car dealer received a holdback from the manufacturer after selling each new vehicle.
Some retailers rely on the holdback to increase their overall profit margins.
The holdback is usually paid several weeks after the initial sale is completed.
The company released the holdback after the project passed its final inspection.
A portion of the payment was kept as a holdback until all repairs were finished.
Contractors often expect a holdback to ensure the work meets all requirements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - a payment made before work is completed
  • synonym: commission - an amount of money paid to someone for selling something
  • synonym: embargo - an official ban on trade or other commercial activity
  • synonym: rebate - a partial refund to someone who has paid too much for something
  • antonym: release - the act of making something available to the public
  • synonym: retainer - a sum of money held until a job is completed
