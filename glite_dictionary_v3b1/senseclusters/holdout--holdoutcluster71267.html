holdout

noun
hiding cards for later use in gambling
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊldˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌaʊt / (BrE)
the act of secretly keeping playing cards aside during a gambling game to use them later for personal advantage

Examples

Card game holdout
He was caught performing a holdout, slipping an extra ace up his sleeve for a later hand.
Strict rules and security measures help prevent the use of holdout techniques among gamblers.
The casino hired professionals to spot anyone attempting a holdout during the poker tournament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheating - dishonest behavior to gain an unfair advantage, especially in games
