lamellate placentation

noun
botanical ovule arrangement with thin placental plates
9.9
Pronunciation: / ləˈmɛlət pleɪˌsɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ləˈmɛlət plæsɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A type of placentation in which ovules are attached to thin plate-like extensions (lamellae) of the placentae that protrude into a compound ovary.

Examples

Lamellate placentation in plants
Botanists use the presence of lamellate placentation as a key trait for plant identification.
In some plant families, lamellate placentation allows for efficient packing of many ovules.
The flower’s compound ovary demonstrates lamellate placentation, visible in cross-section under a microscope.
