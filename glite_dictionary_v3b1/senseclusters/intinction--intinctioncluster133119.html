intinction

noun
brief dipping in liquid
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act of briefly dipping or soaking something into a liquid, especially for a specific purpose

Examples

Intinction of fabric
The artist used intinction to achieve a subtle gradient of color on the canvas.
Traditional dyeing methods often required the careful intinction of cloth into natural pigments.
The chef demonstrated intinction by briefly dipping the bread into olive oil before serving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extraction - the process of removing something, often by soaking
  • synonym: immersion - the act of completely submerging something in a liquid
