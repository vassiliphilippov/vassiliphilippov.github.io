viperine grass snake

noun
a harmless snake resembling a viper
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪ.pəˌrin ˈɡræs ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.pəriːn ˈɡrɑːs ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a small, harmless European snake (Natrix maura) often found near water, resembling a viper in appearance but nonvenomous

Examples

The viperine grass snake swimming
The viperine grass snake is often seen swimming in rivers and streams across southern Europe.
The distinctive zigzag pattern helps the viperine grass snake mimic more dangerous species.
Unlike true vipers, the viperine grass snake poses no threat to humans.
