unlearnable

adjective
impossible to learn
8.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàl…úrn…ôb…ôl / (AmE), /  ånÀàl…úÀên…ôbl / (BrE)
impossible or extremely difficult to learn or master

Examples

Unlearnable subject
For many students, the advanced mathematics course seemed unlearnable at first.
He gave up on the puzzle, declaring it completely unlearnable after hours of trying.
Some ancient scripts are considered unlearnable because no one knows how to read them anymore.

Synonyms

  • unmasterable
    9.5 0.01/m
    unmasterable describes something you cannot fully become skilled at, while unlearnable means it is impossible or very hard to learn.
  • unteachable
    5.4 0.03/m
    unteachable refers to things or people that cannot be taught by any method, while unlearnable focuses on being impossible to learn.
  • uneducable
    8.8 0.04/m
    uneducable often means a person cannot be taught because of limits or unwillingness, while unlearnable describes the extreme difficulty of a skill.
