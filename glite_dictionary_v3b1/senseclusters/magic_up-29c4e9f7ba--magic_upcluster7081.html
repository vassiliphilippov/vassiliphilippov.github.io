magic up

verb
produce/create suddenly or unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / ˈmædʒɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to create or produce something quickly or unexpectedly, as if by magic

Examples

To magic up energy
He seemed to magic up energy whenever it was really needed.
If you can't find a taxi, I'll try to magic up a ride for you.
She managed to magic up a delicious meal from almost nothing in the fridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conjure up - to bring to mind or create as if by magic
  • antonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something
  • synonym: rustle up - to quickly gather or make something, especially food or resources
