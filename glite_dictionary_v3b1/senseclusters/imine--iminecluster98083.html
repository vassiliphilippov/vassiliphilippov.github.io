imine

noun
compound with C=N bond
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmiːn / (AmE), / ˈɪmiːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing a carbon-nitrogen double bond, typically formed by the reaction of an aldehyde or ketone with ammonia or a primary amine

Examples

An imine compound
The chemist isolated the imine after completing the reaction with the aldehyde.
Formation of an imine is a key step in many organic synthesis processes.
Researchers studied the stability of the imine under different temperature conditions.
