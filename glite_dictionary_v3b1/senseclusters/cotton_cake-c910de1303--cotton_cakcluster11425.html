cotton cake

noun
solid residue after extracting cottonseed oil
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtn̩ keɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn keɪk / (BrE)
the solid residue left after oil has been extracted from cottonseeds, often used as animal feed or fertilizer

Examples

The cotton cake residue
After the oil was pressed, the factory workers packaged the cotton cake for transport.
Farmers often use cotton cake as a nutritious feed for livestock.
Some agricultural fertilizers are made with the help of cotton cake due to its nutrient content.
