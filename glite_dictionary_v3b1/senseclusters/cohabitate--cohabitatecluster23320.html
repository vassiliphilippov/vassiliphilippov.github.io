cohabitate

verb
to exist together
5.3
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ˈhæb.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /kə.ˈhæb.ɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ˈhæb.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /kə.ˈhæb.ə.teɪt/ (BrE)
to exist or live together in the same place or context, especially referring to groups, ideas, or systems

Examples

Different cultures cohabitate
In the multicultural city, different traditions cohabitate and shape the community's festivals each year.
Old and new technologies often cohabitate in rapidly developing industries.
Several political ideologies cohabitate within the same organization, leading to lively debates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: separate - to keep apart or not exist together
