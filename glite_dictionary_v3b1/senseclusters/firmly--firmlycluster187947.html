firmly

adverb
in a secure or strong manner
1.0 16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ùÀêmli / (AmE), / Ààf…úÀêmli / (BrE)
in a strong, secure, or definite way; so that something is fixed and unlikely to move or change

Examples

Hold on firmly
He held my hand firmly to reassure me.
She gripped the railing firmly as she climbed the stairs.
You must close the lid firmly so that it seals properly.
Make sure the lid is firmly attached before you shake the bottle.
She held the handlebars firmly as she rode down the hill.
The manager firmly rejected the proposal during the meeting.

Synonyms

  • immovably
    6.0 0.04/m
    immovably is completely fixed so it cannot move, while firmly means held strongly but may allow some movement under force.
  • fast
    1.0 20/m
    fast means held tightly in place and unable to move, while firmly means held with steady strength but not always immobile.
  • securely
    2.9 3/m
    securely keeps something safe and fixed, while firmly focuses on strong grip or position without the safety idea.
  • tightly
    1.0 20/m
    tightly fits or holds by close pressure, while firmly means a steady hold or fixed position without suggesting pressure.
  • stably
    4.3 0.1/m
    stably means staying steady over time, while firmly means being fixed or held strongly in a single moment or place.
  • sturdily
    3.4 0.2/m
    sturdily describes strong, solid build that lasts, while firmly describes how something is held or fixed in place.
