overcontrol

verb
to control excessively
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to control, manage, or regulate someone or something too much, often restricting independence or creativity

Examples

To overcontrol children
Parents who overcontrol their children may prevent them from developing independence.
The manager tends to overcontrol every project, leaving little room for creativity.
If you overcontrol the process, your team might feel frustrated and unmotivated.
Some managers tend to overcontrol their teams, which can reduce creativity and motivation.
If you overcontrol every aspect of the project, your colleagues may feel frustrated.
The teacher realized she had overcontrolled the classroom and decided to allow more student input.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: control - to direct or have power over something or someone
  • synonym: micromanage - to control every small detail of someone's work
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or control something
