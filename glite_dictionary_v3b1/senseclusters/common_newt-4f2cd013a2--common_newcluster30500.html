common newt

noun
a small European semiaquatic salamander
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən nut / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən njuːt / (BrE)
a small semiaquatic salamander found throughout much of Europe, known for its smooth skin and preference for ponds and wetlands

Examples

The common newt in ponds
The common newt is protected by law in several European countries.
We spotted a common newt swimming quietly near the edge of the pond.
Young children gently observed a common newt during their school nature walk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: salamander - an amphibian with a lizard-like appearance
