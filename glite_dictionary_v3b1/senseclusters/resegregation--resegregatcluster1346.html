resegregation

noun
return to separation
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌsɛɡrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌsɛɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of separating groups, especially by race, again after they had previously been integrated

Examples

School resegregation
Recent policy changes have led to the resegregation of some urban school districts.
Activists are concerned about the slow but steady resegregation of neighborhoods once known for their diversity.
The report highlights the negative effects of resegregation on educational opportunities for minority students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integration - the process of combining groups so that they become equal and united
  • synonym: segregation - the act of separating people, often by race or class
