apocalypse

noun
catastrophic destruction by divine power
8.6 4.93/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàp…ëÀê.k…ôÀål…™ps / (AmE), / …ôÀàp…ík.…ô.l…™ps / (BrE)
(in religious or literary contexts) a great and catastrophic event, especially one in which the forces of evil are destroyed by divine power; the end of the world.

Examples

The apocalypse event
Many ancient writings speak of an apocalypse ushered in by divine judgment.
Some believe that an apocalypse will signal the ultimate triumph of good over evil.
The film depicts an apocalypse in which the world is destroyed by supernatural forces.

Synonyms

  • end of the world
    7.9 2.10/m
    end of the world describes the final judgment at history's end, while apocalypse often shows divine destruction and cosmic revelation.
  • Armageddon
    8.4 2.14/m
    Armageddon means a final battle between good and evil, while apocalypse suggests a larger end-time event with divine judgment.
  • doomsday
    7.5 1.20/m
    doomsday names the final day of judgment or great disaster, while apocalypse often frames a full, world-ending revelation.
  • doomsday event
    8.6 0.00/m
    doomsday event means any huge, destructive catastrophe, while apocalypse usually carries religious meaning of divine ending and victory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revelation - the making known of something previously secret or unknown, especially a divine truth
