apocalypse

noun
catastrophic destruction by divine power
1.2 6.8
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑː.kəˌlɪps / (AmE), / əˈpɒk.ə.lɪps / (BrE)
(in religious or literary contexts) a great and catastrophic event, especially one in which the forces of evil are destroyed by divine power; the end of the world.

Examples

The apocalypse event
Many ancient writings speak of an apocalypse ushered in by divine judgment.
Some believe that an apocalypse will signal the ultimate triumph of good over evil.
The film depicts an apocalypse in which the world is destroyed by supernatural forces.
