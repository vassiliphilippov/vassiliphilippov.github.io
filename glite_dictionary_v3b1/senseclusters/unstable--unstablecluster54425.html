unstable

adjective
not stable; prone to change or fall
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ə n.ˈsteɪ.bəl/ /; / /ʌn.ˈsteɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈsteɪ.bəl/ /; / /ə n.ˈsteɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
not steady, secure, or likely to last; likely to change, fail, or fall

Examples

Unstable ground
After the earthquake, the ground remained unstable for several days.
Her mood is unstable, shifting rapidly from happiness to anger.
The old bridge has become unstable and must be repaired before it can be used again.
Her emotions were unstable, shifting from happiness to anger quickly.
The old bookshelf was so unstable that it collapsed when I tried to move it.
The political climate remained unstable for months after the election.
Negotiations produced an unstable agreement that neither side fully trusted.
The situation at the border remains unstable, leaving many residents anxious about their future.
Their financial position is unstable, offering little sense of security.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insecure - not firmly fixed; liable to give way
  • synonym: precarious - not securely held; dangerously likely to fall or collapse; affording no ease
  • antonym: stable - firmly fixed or not likely to change or fail
  • synonym: volatile - liable to change rapidly and unpredictably
