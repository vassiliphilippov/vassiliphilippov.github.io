GHz

noun
unit of frequency (1 billion hertz)
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiː ˌeɪtʃ ˈzɛd / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː ˌeɪtʃ ˈzɛd / (BrE)
A unit of frequency equal to one billion cycles per second; gigahertz.

Examples

GHz unit
Her computer's CPU can be overclocked to 4.5GHz.
The new processor runs at 3.2GHz for faster performance.
Wi-Fi signals typically use either the 2.4GHz or 5GHz frequency bands.

Related Terms
