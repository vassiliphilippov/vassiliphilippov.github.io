picture element

noun
smallest unit of a digital image
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪktʃɚ ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪktʃə ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
(Computer science) The smallest discrete component of a digital image or display, typically represented as a single colored dot.

Examples

A picture element on a screen
Each picture element controls the color at a specific position on the screen.
Modern monitors have millions of picture elements for displaying high-resolution images.
The clarity of the image depends on the number of picture elements per inch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pixel - the smallest unit of a digital image; abbreviation for 'picture element'
