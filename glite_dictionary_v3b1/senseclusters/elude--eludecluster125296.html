elude

verb
escape or avoid
4.9 4.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːd /; / ɪˈlud / (AmE), / ɪˈluːd / (BrE)
to escape from or avoid someone or something, or to fail to be understood, remembered, or achieved

Examples

To elude capture
A clear understanding of the complex theory continues to elude researchers.
The rare bird has eluded all attempts to capture it on camera.
The suspect managed to elude the authorities for months before finally being caught.
Despite years of research, a solution to the problem has eluded scientists.
Her name eluded him just when he needed to introduce her.
The true meaning of the poem continues to elude me, no matter how many times I read it.
Despite repeated attempts, the answer continued to elude him during the interview.
She frequently eludes her responsibilities at work by delegating them to others.
The minister managed to elude questions about the budget during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capture - to catch or seize
  • antonym: comprehend - to understand or grasp mentally
  • antonym: confront - to face up to (something), especially an issue or difficulty
  • synonym: dodge - to avoid (something) by a sudden, quick movement or by cleverness
  • synonym: escape - to get away from a place or danger
  • synonym: evade - to avoid or escape from by cunning
