shellac wax

noun
a hard wax obtained from shellac
4.4
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈlæk wæks / (AmE), / ʃəˈlæk waks / (BrE)
a hard, natural wax separated from shellac by its insolubility in alcohol, used in polishes, cosmetics, and other products

Examples

Shellac wax polish
Manufacturers often use shellac wax in the production of cosmetics and pharmaceuticals.
The furniture was polished with shellac wax to give it a glossy sheen.
You can separate shellac wax from shellac by using alcohol as a solvent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shellac - a resin secreted by the lac bug, used as a wood finish and food glaze
