poler

noun
boat propeller with pole
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
a person who propels a boat or raft by pushing against the bottom with a pole

Examples

River poler
The poler skillfully guided the raft down the shallow river.
Each poler took turns pushing the boat forward through the reeds.
Tourists watched as the experienced poler navigated the narrow canal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punter - a person who propels a flat-bottomed boat with a pole
  • antonym: rower - a person who propels a boat using oars
