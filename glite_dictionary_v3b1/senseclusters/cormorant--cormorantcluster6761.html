cormorant

noun
a large fish-eating seabird
8.8 0.54/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îÀêrm…ôr…ônt / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀêm…ôr…ônt / (BrE)
a large, voracious dark-colored seabird with a long neck and a throat pouch for catching and holding fish, sometimes trained in Asia to catch fish

Examples

A cormorant bird
A cormorant perched on a rock, drying its wings in the sun.
Fishermen in China train cormorants to catch fish for them on rivers.
The dark silhouette of a cormorant was visible against the evening sky.
