fugue

verb
to compose as fugue
9.9
Pronunciation: / fjuːɡ / (AmE), / fjuːɡ / (BrE)
to compose, perform, or arrange music in the style of a fugue, interweaving independent melodic lines in counterpoint.

Examples

To fugue a theme
The composer fugued the final movement, weaving three themes into strict counterpoint.
She skillfully fugued the melody, creating a rich tapestry of sound.
In his latest work, the pianist fugued several motifs to showcase his mastery of counterpoint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose - to create or write a piece of music
  • synonym: imitate - to copy or reproduce the style or manner of something
