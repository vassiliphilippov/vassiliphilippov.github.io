legend of Bucephalus

noun
story of Alexander the Great's horse
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒənd əv bjuːˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒənd əv bjuːˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
the traditional story of Bucephalus, the celebrated horse of Alexander the Great, known for his taming and loyalty

Examples

The legend of Bucephalus
Historians often recount the legend of Bucephalus when discussing figures of ancient Greece.
Many young riders are fascinated by the legend of Bucephalus and dream of such a bond with their own horses.
The legend of Bucephalus is told to illustrate Alexander's extraordinary skill and determination.
