aliquant

noun
number that is not an exact divisor
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌkwɑnt / (AmE), / ˈalɪkwənt / (BrE)
(mathematics) An integer that does not divide another number exactly; a number whose division into another leaves a remainder.

Examples

An aliquant integer
In some tests, if a number is an aliquant of another, the result will include a remainder.
The mathematician explained that 6 is an aliquant of 25 because it does not divide evenly.
When dividing 13 by 4, 4 is an aliquant of 13.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aliquot - an integer that is an exact divisor of another number
