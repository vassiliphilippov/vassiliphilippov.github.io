Democritean

noun
follower of Democritus
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛməˈkraɪtiən / (AmE), / ˌdɛməˈkraɪtiən / (BrE)
a follower or supporter of the ancient Greek philosopher Democritus, especially his doctrine of atomism

Examples

A Democritean philosopher
The ancient scholar was known as a Democritean for his belief in the atomic nature of the universe.
Many early scientists were influenced by Democritean ideas about matter and the void.
He proudly identified himself as a Democritean in philosophical debates about the nature of reality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Epicurean - a follower of Epicurus, another ancient Greek philosopher with similar atomic theories
  • synonym: atomist - a person who believes in or studies the theory that all matter is made of atoms
