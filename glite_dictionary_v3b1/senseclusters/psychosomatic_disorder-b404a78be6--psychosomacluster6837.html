psychosomatic disorder

noun
mental disorder producing physical symptoms
2.3 5.3
Pronunciation: /saɪ.koʊ.sə.ˈmæt.ɪk dɪs.ˈɔːr.dər/ (AmE), /saɪ.kəʊ.sə.ˈmæt.ɪk dɪs.ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
A disorder in which psychological factors cause or contribute to physical symptoms and complaints without a clear medical cause.

Examples

A psychosomatic disorder diagnosis
Doctors found no physical explanation for his pain, suggesting it might be a psychosomatic disorder.
Her chronic headaches were eventually diagnosed as a psychosomatic disorder triggered by stress.
Managing a psychosomatic disorder often requires both psychological therapy and physical care.
