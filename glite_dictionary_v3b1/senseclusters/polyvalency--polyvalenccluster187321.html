polyvalency

noun
capacity to neutralize multiple toxins or antigens
4.0 8.5
Pronunciation: /pɑː.lɪ.ˈvæl.ən.siː/ /; / /pə.lɪ.ˈvæl.ən.siː/ (AmE), /pɒ.lɪ.ˈvæl.ən.siː/ /; / /pə.lɪ.ˈvæl.ən.siː/ (BrE)
the capacity of a substance, especially an antibody or antivenom, to counteract more than one toxin, antigen, or type of microorganism

Examples

Vaccine polyvalency
Because of its polyvalency, the vaccine provides protection against multiple strains of the virus.
Researchers are developing drugs with increased polyvalency to treat a broader range of infections.
The polyvalency of this antivenom makes it effective against bites from several snake species.
