day blindness

noun
inability to see clearly in bright light
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ ˌblaɪndnəs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ˌblaɪndnəs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person is unable to see clearly in bright light.

Examples

Day blindness in bright light
Doctors diagnosed her with day blindness after she mentioned struggling to see outdoors.
He avoided going outside during midday because his day blindness made everything painfully bright.
People with day blindness often wear tinted glasses to cope with sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nyctalopia - night blindness; inability to see in low light
