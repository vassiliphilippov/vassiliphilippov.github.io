rowdy

adjective
noisy and disorderly
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊdi / (AmE), / ˈraʊdi / (BrE)
noisy and disorderly, often disturbing others; rough and loud in behavior

Examples

Rowdy crowd
A rowdy crowd gathered outside the stadium after the match.
The teacher struggled to control the rowdy students during the assembly.
They called the police to disperse a rowdy party next door.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful; rowdy
  • antonym: orderly - behaving in a neat, peaceful, or controlled way
  • synonym: raucous - loud and harsh; noisy
