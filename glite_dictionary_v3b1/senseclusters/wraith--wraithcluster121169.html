wraith

noun
a haunting ghost or spirit
Pronunciation: / reɪθ / (AmE), / reɪθ / (BrE)
A ghost or spirit, especially one that is seen shortly before or after a person's death, often believed to be malevolent or haunting.

Examples

A wraith appears
His face turned pale, as if he had just seen a wraith appear before him.
Many villagers claimed to have seen the wraith lurking in the moonlit cemetery.
The story tells of a wraith that haunts the corridors of the abandoned manor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, especially one that is believed to appear to the living
  • synonym: phantom - a ghost or figment of the imagination
  • synonym: specter - a visible incorporeal spirit, especially one of a terrifying nature; ghost
