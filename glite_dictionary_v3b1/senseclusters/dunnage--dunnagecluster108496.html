dunnage

noun
cargo protection material
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌn.ɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈdʌn.ədʒ/ (AmE), /ˈdʌn.ɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈdʌn.ədʒ/ (BrE)
material used to protect or support cargo during transport, to prevent damage or shifting

Examples

Dunnage protects cargo
The crew added extra dunnage to keep the cargo from shifting during the storm.
Wooden dunnage was placed under the machinery to prevent it from being damaged in transit.
Proper use of dunnage can greatly reduce the risk of goods being broken during shipping.
The workers placed dunnage between the crates to prevent them from shifting during transport.
Proper dunnage is essential to keep fragile goods safe on long sea voyages.
