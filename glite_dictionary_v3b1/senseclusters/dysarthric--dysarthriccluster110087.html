dysarthric

noun
person with dysarthria
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈɑːr.θrɪk/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈɑː.θrɪk/ (BrE)
a person who has dysarthria, a speech disorder caused by weak or uncoordinated muscles used for speaking

Examples

A dysarthric patient

Support groups can help a dysarthric feel less isolated in daily communication.
After the stroke, she became a dysarthric and needed specialized speech therapy.
