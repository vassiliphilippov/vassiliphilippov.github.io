double-deck railcar

noun
railcar with two levels for passengers
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˌdɛk ˈreɪlˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʌbl dɛk ˈreɪlkɑː / (BrE)
a railcar or passenger train carriage with two levels of seating, designed to carry more passengers than a single-level car

Examples

A double-deck railcar design
Many commuter trains in Europe feature at least one double-deck railcar on busy routes.
Passengers enjoyed the view from the upper level of the double-deck railcar as they traveled through the countryside.
The new double-deck railcar increased the train's passenger capacity during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bilevel car - a railcar with two passenger levels
  • antonym: single-deck railcar - a railcar with only one level for passengers
