grape sugar

noun
dextrose found in grapes and fruits
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪp ˌsʌɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪp ˌsʌɡə / (BrE)
another name for dextrose, a naturally occurring form of glucose found especially in grapes, honey, and sweet fruits

Examples

Grape sugar in honey
Honey is a natural source of grape sugar, which gives it its distinctive sweetness.
Some bakers prefer using grape sugar instead of regular table sugar in certain recipes.
The sweetness of ripe grapes is largely due to their high content of grape sugar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dextrose - the dextrorotatory form of glucose; grape sugar
  • synonym: glucose - a simple sugar that is an important energy source in living organisms
