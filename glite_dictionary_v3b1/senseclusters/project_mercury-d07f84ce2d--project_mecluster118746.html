Project Mercury

proper noun
U.S. manned space program, 1961–1963
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːdʒɛkt ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈprɒdʒɛkt ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
the first U.S. manned spaceflight program (1961–1963), aimed at putting a human into orbit around Earth

Examples

Project Mercury spaceflight program
Project Mercury was a crucial step toward achieving President Kennedy's goal of landing a man on the Moon.
Astronaut John Glenn became the first American to orbit the Earth during the Project Mercury mission.
The technology developed during Project Mercury laid the groundwork for later NASA programs like Gemini and Apollo.
