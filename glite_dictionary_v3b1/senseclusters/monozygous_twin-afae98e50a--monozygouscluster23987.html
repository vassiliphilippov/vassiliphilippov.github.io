monozygous twin

noun
one of a pair of identical twins
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊ.nəˈzaɪ.ɡəs twɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈzaɪ.ɡəs twɪn / (BrE)
either of two twins that develop from the same fertilized ovum and share identical genetic material; identical twin.

Examples

An identical monozygous twin
A monozygous twin can serve as a perfect genetic match for organ donation.
Researchers study monozygous twins to better understand the role of genetics in human traits.
She was surprised to learn that her grandmother was a monozygous twin.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dizygous twin - twin developing from two separate fertilized eggs (fraternal twin)
  • synonym: identical twin - twin developed from the same fertilized ovum
