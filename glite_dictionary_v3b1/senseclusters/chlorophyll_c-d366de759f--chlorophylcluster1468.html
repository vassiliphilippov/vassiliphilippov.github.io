chlorophyll c

noun
pigment found in brown algae and some protists
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈklɔːr.ə.fɪl siː/ /; / /ˈkloʊr.ə.fɪl siː/ (AmE), /ˈklɒr.ə.fɪl siː/ /; / /ˈklɔːr.ə.fɪl siː/ (BrE)
a type of chlorophyll found in brown algae, diatoms, and flagellates, involved in photosynthesis

Examples

chlorophyll c pigment
Brown algae owe their color primarily to chlorophyll c and other pigments.
The presence of chlorophyll c helps diatoms adapt to different light conditions.
Unlike chlorophyll a, chlorophyll c is not found in green plants.
