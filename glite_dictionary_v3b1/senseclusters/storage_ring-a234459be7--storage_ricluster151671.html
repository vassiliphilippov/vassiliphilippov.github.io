storage ring

noun
magnet ring for storing charged particles
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈstɔːr.ɪdʒ rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈstɔːr.ədʒ rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈstɔː.rɪdʒ rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈstɔː.rədʒ rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a doughnut-shaped structure of magnets in which charged particles are kept circulating at high speeds, used especially in particle accelerators and synchrotron facilities

Examples

The electrons were accelerated to nearly the speed of light and injected into the storage ring.
