dabble

verb
take part casually in an activity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdæbəl / (AmE), / ˈdæbəl / (BrE)
to take part in an activity in a casual or superficial way, without serious commitment

Examples

To dabble in painting
He began to dabble in gardening after moving to the countryside.
Many people dabble in foreign languages without becoming fluent.
She likes to dabble in painting whenever she has free time.
She enjoys dabbling in painting, but she has never taken a formal class.
Many people dabble in learning new languages without becoming fluent.
He started dabbling in gardening during the summer holidays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: experiment - to try something new to discover what it is like
  • antonym: pursue - to follow or engage in an activity seriously
  • antonym: specialize - to focus on a particular activity or area with deep expertise
