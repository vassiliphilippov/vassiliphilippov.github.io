spite

noun
petty desire to harm or annoy
4.7 3.6
Pronunciation: / spaɪt / (AmE), / spaɪt / (BrE)
a feeling or desire to harm, annoy, or upset someone, often for petty or malicious reasons

Examples

Out of spite
Even though it would hurt her own chances, she acted in spite.
He broke my vase just for spite, not by accident.
She insulted him out of pure spite after their argument.
He ignored her out of pure spite, refusing to admit she was right.
Her actions were motivated by spite rather than any practical reason.
In a moment of spite, he told everyone her secret.
