couch

noun
a bed used for psychoanalytic treatment
4.6
Pronunciation: / kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
a long, narrow bed on which a patient lies during psychiatric or psychoanalytic treatment

Examples

Therapist's couch
Sigmund Freud is famous for using a couch during psychoanalysis sessions.
The patient lay silently on the couch as the therapist took notes.
The traditional psychoanalyst’s office features a comfortable couch for clients to recline on.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divan - a long low sofa without a back or arms, often used as a couch
