invigoration

noun
the act of making something more lively or energetic
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌvɪɡəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɪɡəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or feeling of becoming more lively, energetic, or full of vitality

Examples

A feeling of invigoration
After a few minutes of exercise, she felt a sense of invigoration that lifted her spirits.
The invigoration of the city's economy was made possible by new businesses and investments.
The speech was intended as an invigoration for the tired and disheartened team.
After a brisk morning walk, she felt a sense of invigoration that lasted all day.
The fresh air brought an immediate invigoration to the tired hikers.
There was a new invigoration in the team's efforts after the halftime talk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: debilitation - the process of making someone or something weaker
  • antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • synonym: revitalization - the action of bringing new life or energy to something
  • synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
