clootie

noun
folk healing cloth
Pronunciation: / ˈkluːti / (AmE), / ˈkluːti / (BrE)
a piece of cloth or rag tied to a tree or left near a holy well as a folk offering or healing charm, especially in Scottish tradition

Examples

A clootie on a tree
Pilgrims tied a clootie to the ancient tree, hoping for healing and good fortune.
The branches were covered with colorful clooties left by visitors seeking blessings.
Local tradition says that leaving a clootie at the well can help cure illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amulet - an object believed to have protective powers
  • synonym: offering - something given as a religious or spiritual gift
