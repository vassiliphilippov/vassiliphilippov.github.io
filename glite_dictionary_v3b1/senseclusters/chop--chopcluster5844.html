chop

verb
cut into pieces with repeated blows
4.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /tʃɑːp/ (AmE), /tʃɒp/ (BrE)
to cut or strike something with a sharp blow, especially using an axe, knife, or hand

Examples

To chop wood
He used a heavy axe to chop firewood for the winter.
Please chop the onions finely before adding them to the pan.
The chef quickly chopped the herbs and sprinkled them over the dish.
He chopped the firewood into small pieces for the fireplace.
She chopped the carrots for the stew with a sharp knife.
The chef quickly chops the onions before adding them to the pan.
He raised his hand and chopped the branch in two with a single blow.
She chopped at the ice with an axe to break it apart.
The chef chopped the vegetables swiftly before tossing them in the pan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mend - to repair something that is broken or damaged
  • synonym: slash - to strike with a sweeping or cutting motion
  • synonym: slice - to cut something into thin flat pieces
