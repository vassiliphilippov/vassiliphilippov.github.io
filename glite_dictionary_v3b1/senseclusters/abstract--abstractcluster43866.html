abstract

verb
consider theoretically; remove from specifics
3.7 5.8
Pronunciation: /æb.ˈstrækt/ (AmE), /æb.ˈstrækt/ (BrE)
to consider or think about something in a general or theoretical way, without focusing on specific examples

Examples

To abstract from details
It's often useful to abstract from the details when developing a new theory.
Researchers sometimes abstract general principles from individual cases.
When solving this problem, try to abstract away from the specifics of your own experience.
It's easy to abstract the idea of happiness, but harder to define it in real life.
Philosophers often abstract concepts like justice or freedom from their practical applications.
When we abstract the notion of time, we treat it as an independent entity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concretize - to make something more concrete or specific
  • synonym: generalize - to make a general or broad statement by inferring from specific cases
  • synonym: theorize - to form a theory or set of ideas about something
