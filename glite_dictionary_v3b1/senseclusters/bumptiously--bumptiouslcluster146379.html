bumptiously

adverb
in a self-assertive or conceited way
5.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌmp.ʃəs.lɪ/ /; / /ˈbʌmp.ʃʊs.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈbʌmp.ʃəs.lɪ/ /; / /ˈbʌmp.ʃʊs.lɪ/ (BrE)
In a self-assertive or conceited manner; in a way that shows excessive confidence and lack of humility.

Examples

To speak bumptiously
He spoke bumptiously at the meeting, ignoring everyone else's opinions.
She bumptiously claimed all the credit for the team's success.
The new manager acted bumptiously on his first day, issuing orders without listening.
