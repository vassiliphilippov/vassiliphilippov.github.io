conjunction

noun
a word for linking parts of a sentence
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a word (such as 'and', 'but', or 'because') that connects words, phrases, or clauses, or the grammatical relationship created by such words

Examples

A conjunction word
In this exercise, underline every conjunction you find.
The most common conjunctions in English are 'and', 'but', and 'or'.
Use a conjunction to join the two sentences together.
A coordinating conjunction joins elements of equal grammatical rank.
In the sentence 'She sings and he dances,' the conjunction connects two clauses.
The conjunction creates a relationship between the two phrases in the sentence.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: coordinating conjunction - a conjunction that joins elements of equal grammatical rank
  • synonym: coordination - the linking of words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance
  • degree higher: subordinating conjunction - a conjunction that introduces a subordinate clause
  • antonym: subordination - the linking of clauses where one is dependent on the other
