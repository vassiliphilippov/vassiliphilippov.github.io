Jaghatai language

noun
historical Turkic language of Central Asia
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɑːɡəˈtaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdʒæɡəˈtaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Turkic literary language spoken in medieval Central Asia, named after Jaghatai, a son of Genghis Khan

Examples

Jaghatai language literature
Many classical Uzbek poems were originally written in Jaghatai language.
Scholars still study texts produced in the Jaghatai language to understand Central Asian history.
The Jaghatai language was influential in the development of several modern Turkic languages.
