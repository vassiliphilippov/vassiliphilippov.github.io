P/E ratio

noun
price-to-earnings ratio in stocks
Pronunciation: / piː iː ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / piː iː ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
the ratio of a company's current share price to its earnings per share, used to assess stock valuation

Examples

The P/E ratio value
Before buying shares, she compared the P/E ratios of several tech firms.
Many investors consider a low P/E ratio a sign that a stock is undervalued.
The company's P/E ratio rose sharply after its earnings report exceeded expectations.
