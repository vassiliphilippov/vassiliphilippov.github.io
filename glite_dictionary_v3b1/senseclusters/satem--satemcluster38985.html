satem

noun
Indo-European language group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːtəm / (AmE), / ˈsɑːtəm / (BrE)
a language of the Indo-European family in which original velar consonants changed to sibilant sounds, or the group of such languages

Examples

A satem language
Lithuanian is a classic example of a satem language within the Indo-European family.
The distinction between satem and centum languages is important in historical linguistics.
Scholars debated whether Albanian should be classified as a satem language.
