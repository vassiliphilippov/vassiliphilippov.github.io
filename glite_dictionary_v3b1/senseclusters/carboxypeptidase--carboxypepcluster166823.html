carboxypeptidase

noun
protein-digesting enzyme
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑːrˌbɑksiˈpɛptɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˌbɒksɪˈpɛptɪdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down proteins by removing amino acids from the end of a protein chain

Examples

Carboxypeptidase enzyme activity
The digestive system uses carboxypeptidase to help break down proteins into amino acids.
Researchers discovered a new type of carboxypeptidase in the human pancreas.
A deficiency in carboxypeptidase can affect how the body processes certain proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peptidase - an enzyme that breaks peptide bonds in proteins
  • synonym: protease - an enzyme that breaks down proteins and peptides
