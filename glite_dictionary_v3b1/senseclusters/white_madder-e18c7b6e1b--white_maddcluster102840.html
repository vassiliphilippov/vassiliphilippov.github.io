white madder

noun
Eurasian herb with small white flowers
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈmædɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmadə / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb (Rubia alba) with clusters of small white flowers, commonly naturalized in North America.

Examples

White madder plant
White madder is valued for its attractive white blossoms and vigorous growth.
Botanists discovered wild populations of white madder thriving in several U.S. states.
The garden featured a patch of white madder along the fence line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: madder - a group of plants in the genus Rubia, some with red roots used for dye
