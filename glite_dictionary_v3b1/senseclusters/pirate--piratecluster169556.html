pirate

verb
to seize by force or arbitrarily
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
to take or seize something arbitrarily or by force, especially in the manner of a pirate.

Examples

To pirate a ship
A notorious gang once pirated a luxury yacht off the coast last year.
During the revolution, the rebels pirated a supply ship and redistributed its contents.
The hackers attempted to pirate the company's confidential data for their own use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commandeer - to take arbitrary or forcible possession of something, especially for military purposes
  • synonym: confiscate - to take something away from someone by authority
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly
