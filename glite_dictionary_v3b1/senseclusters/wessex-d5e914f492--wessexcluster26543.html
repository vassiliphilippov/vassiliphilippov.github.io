Wessex

proper noun
historical Saxon kingdom in southwestern England
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛsɛks / (AmE), / ˈwɛsɪks / (BrE)
a historical kingdom in southwestern England, prominent during the early Middle Ages and later the most powerful of the Anglo-Saxon kingdoms by the 10th century

Examples

Kingdom of Wessex
Historians often study the rulers of Wessex to understand the roots of English monarchy.
Several well-known novels by Thomas Hardy are set in a fictionalized Wessex.
The ancient kingdom of Wessex played a crucial role in the formation of England.
