diverter

noun
flow-directing device
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɜrtər / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
a device that changes the direction or path of a flow, such as water, air, or gas

Examples

Water diverter
The plumber installed a diverter to redirect water from the main pipe to the garden hose.
A shower diverter allows you to switch the water flow between the tub and the showerhead.
Engineers used a special diverter to control the airflow in the ventilation system.
