EFLOPS

noun
one quintillion floating-point operations per second (computing)
Pronunciation: / ˈiːˌflɑps / (AmE), / ˈiːˌflɒps / (BrE)
A unit for measuring the speed of a computer system, equal to one quintillion (10^18) floating-point operations per second.

Examples

One EFLOPS speed
Researchers are striving to surpass the EFLOPS barrier in computing power.
The new supercomputer is expected to reach 2 EFLOPS when fully operational.
The project aims to achieve sustained EFLOPS performance for advanced scientific simulations.
