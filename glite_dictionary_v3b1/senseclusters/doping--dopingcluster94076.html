doping

noun
adding impurities to materials
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊpɪŋ / (BrE)
the intentional addition of small amounts of other elements or impurities to a material, especially a semiconductor, to change its electrical or physical properties

Examples

Doping silicon
The process of doping silicon is essential for making modern electronic devices.
Careful doping can significantly improve the conductivity of a semiconductor material.
Researchers are experimenting with new elements for doping to enhance solar cell efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alloying - mixing metals with other elements to improve properties
  • antonym: purification - removing impurities from a material
