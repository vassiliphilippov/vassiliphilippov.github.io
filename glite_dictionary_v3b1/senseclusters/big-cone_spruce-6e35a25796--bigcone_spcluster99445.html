big-cone spruce

noun
tree of California with large cones
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ koʊn sprus / (AmE), / bɪɡ kəʊn spruːs / (BrE)
A North American tree, related to Douglas fir, native to California, notable for its large cones measuring 4–8 inches long.

Examples

The big-cone spruce tree
Botanists study the big-cone spruce to better understand its adaptation to local wildfires.
Hikers in southern California often spot big-cone spruce along dry, rocky slopes.
The big-cone spruce is easily recognized by its unusually large and distinctive cones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Douglas fir - A large evergreen tree (Pseudotsuga menziesii) native to western North America, not a true fir.
