preposition

verb
to arrange in advance
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to place or arrange something in a specific position in advance or beforehand

Examples

To preposition supplies
The emergency supplies were prepositioned at several locations before the storm arrived.
They prepositioned the vehicles near the exit to ensure a quick response.
All necessary materials were prepositioned in the conference room ahead of the meeting.
