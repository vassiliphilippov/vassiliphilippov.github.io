depression

noun
prolonged economic downturn
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
An extended period of serious decline in the economy, marked by high unemployment, falling prices, and low levels of trade and investment.

Examples

The Great Depression
During the depression, many businesses closed and unemployment soared.
Historians still study the effects of the Great Depression on global economies.
The country suffered through a severe depression that lasted almost a decade.

  • degree lower: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
