case

noun
grammatical category showing word relationships
9.9
Pronunciation: / keɪs / (AmE), / keɪs / (BrE)
(Grammar) The grammatical category of a noun, pronoun, or adjective that expresses its relation to other words in a sentence, often shown by inflection.

Examples

Noun case
English pronouns have different forms depending on case, such as 'he' (subject) and 'him' (object).
In Russian, the word endings change to show their case in a sentence.
The teacher asked the students to identify the case of the highlighted noun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: declension - variation of the form of a noun, pronoun, or adjective, to indicate number, case, and gender
