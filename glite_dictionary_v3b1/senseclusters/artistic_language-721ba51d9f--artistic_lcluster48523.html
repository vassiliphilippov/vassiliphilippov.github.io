artistic language

noun
constructed language for artistic purposes
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈtɪstɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪstɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A constructed language created primarily for aesthetic or artistic purposes, often used in works of fiction or for personal enjoyment.

Examples

An artistic language created
She spent years perfecting her own artistic language, complete with unique grammar and vocabulary.
The conference featured a panel on the role of artistic languages in speculative fiction.
Tolkien's Quenya is perhaps the most famous artistic language created for literary purposes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constructed language - an artificially created language
  • antonym: natural language - a language that has developed naturally rather than being consciously designed
