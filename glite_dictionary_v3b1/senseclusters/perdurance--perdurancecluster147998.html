perdurance

noun
persistence by temporal parts
9.9
Pronunciation: / pərˈdʊrəns / (AmE), / pəˈdjʊərəns / (BrE)
the philosophical concept that objects persist over time by having different temporal parts at different times

Examples

Perdurance theory
In metaphysics, perdurance explains how objects can persist through change over time.
The theory of perdurance contrasts with the idea that objects are wholly present at every moment.
Philosophers debate whether perdurance or endurance better describes the persistence of identity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endurance - the ability to last or persist over time
  • degree lower: endure - to continue to exist or last
  • synonym: perdure - to last or continue to exist for a long time
