antiprotease

noun
enzyme inhibitor
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈproʊtieɪz / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈprəʊtiːeɪz / (BrE)
a substance that prevents or reduces the activity of protease enzymes in the body

Examples

An antiprotease drug
Doctors may prescribe an antiprotease to help manage certain lung conditions.
The new drug acts as an antiprotease, blocking harmful enzymes in the body.
Researchers are testing whether an antiprotease can slow the progression of the disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protease - an enzyme that breaks down proteins
  • synonym: protease inhibitor - a substance that blocks the action of protease enzymes
