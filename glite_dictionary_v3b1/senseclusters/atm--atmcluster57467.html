atm

noun
unit of pressure (atmosphere)
4.0
Pronunciation: /eɪ.tiː.ˈem/ /; / /ˈeɪ.tiː.em/ (AmE), /eɪ.tiː.ˈem/ /; / /ˈeɪ.tiː.em/ (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to the pressure exerted by a column of mercury 760 mm high at sea level and 0 degrees Celsius, commonly used in physics and chemistry

Examples

1 atm pressure
At sea level, the standard atmospheric pressure is defined as 1 atm.
The reaction vessel was pressurized to 5 atm to accelerate the chemical process.
The tire pressure should be around 2 atm for optimal performance.
