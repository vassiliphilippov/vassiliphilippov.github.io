mercenariness

noun
the state of being motivated by personal gain
9.5 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmɜːr.sə.ner.ɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈmɜː.sə.nə.ri.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality or state of being motivated chiefly by the pursuit of personal gain, especially money, rather than by principle or loyalty.

Examples

The mercenariness of workers
Accusations of mercenariness damaged the charity's reputation among its donors.
Her mercenariness was evident in every professional decision she made, always choosing the highest bidder.
The politician's mercenariness alienated voters who valued honesty and public service.

Related Terms

  • antonym: altruism - the selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • synonym: selfishness - the quality of being primarily concerned with one's own advantage
