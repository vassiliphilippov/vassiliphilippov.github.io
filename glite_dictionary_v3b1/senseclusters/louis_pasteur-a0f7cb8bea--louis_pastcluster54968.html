Louis Pasteur

proper noun
French chemist and biologist (1822–1895)
5.5
Pronunciation: / luːˌi pæsˈtɜr / (AmE), / luːˌi pæsˈtɜː / (BrE)
(1822–1895) French chemist and biologist, renowned for discovering that fermentation is caused by microorganisms and for developing the process of pasteurization.

Examples

Louis Pasteur discoveries
Louis Pasteur revolutionized science by proving that microorganisms cause disease.
Many medical advancements can be traced back to the pioneering work of Louis Pasteur.
The method of pasteurization is named after Louis Pasteur, who invented it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pasteurization - the process of heating food and liquids to destroy harmful microorganisms
