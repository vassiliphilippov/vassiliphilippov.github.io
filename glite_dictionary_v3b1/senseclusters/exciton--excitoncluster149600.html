exciton

noun
bound electron-hole pair
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈsaɪ.tən/ /; / /ek.ˈsaɪ.tən/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈsaɪ.tɒn/ /; / /ek.ˈsaɪ.tɒn/ (BrE)
a particle in a solid formed when an electron and a hole bind together after absorbing energy

Examples

An exciton particle
Scientists detected an exciton in the semiconductor using advanced imaging techniques.
The energy of an exciton can be transferred between molecules in certain materials.
Understanding how an exciton behaves is important for developing new solar cells.
Scientists study excitons to understand how energy moves through semiconductors.
The efficiency of some solar cells depends on the behavior of excitons within the material.
When light is absorbed, excitons can form and transfer energy across the crystal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: photon - a particle representing a quantum of light
  • synonym: quasiparticle - a particle-like entity in a solid that arises from the collective behavior of electrons
