psychosexual development

noun
progression through stages of sexual/personality development
3.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /saɪ.kə.ˈsek.ʊ.əl dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ (AmE), /saɪ.kə.ˈsek.ʊ.əl dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the process by which personality and sexual behavior mature through a series of developmental stages, namely the oral, anal, phallic, latency, and genital stages.

Examples

Psychosexual development stages
Sigmund Freud's theory of psychosexual development has had a significant impact on modern psychology.
Some therapists consider unresolved conflicts during psychosexual development to be a cause of adult neuroses.
The stages of psychosexual development include oral, anal, phallic, latency, and genital stages.
