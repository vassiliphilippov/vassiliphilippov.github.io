Afroasiatic language

noun
language family of Africa and Western Asia
Pronunciation: / ˌæfroʊ.eɪˈʒæt̬.ɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌæfrəʊ.eɪˈzɪætɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a member of a large family of languages spoken in parts of North Africa and Western Asia, including Arabic, Hebrew, and Amharic

Examples

An Afroasiatic language family
Amharic, spoken in Ethiopia, is classified as an Afroasiatic language.
Arabic is an Afroasiatic language with millions of speakers across the Middle East and North Africa.
Scholars have traced the origins of several Afroasiatic languages back thousands of years.
