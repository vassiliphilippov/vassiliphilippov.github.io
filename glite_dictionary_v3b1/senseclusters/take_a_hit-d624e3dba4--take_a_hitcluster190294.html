take a hit

verb
suffer a loss or setback
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə hɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ə hɪt / (BrE)
to experience a loss, damage, or setback, especially in reputation, finances, or performance

Examples

Company takes a hit
After the new regulations were announced, the entire industry took a hit in the stock market.
His confidence took a hit when he failed the exam on his first try.
Several local restaurants took a hit during the economic downturn.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bounce back - to recover from a setback
  • synonym: suffer a setback - to experience a reversal or defeat
