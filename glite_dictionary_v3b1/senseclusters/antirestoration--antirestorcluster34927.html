antirestoration

adjective
opposed to restoration
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
opposed to the act of restoring something to its previous state or position

Examples

Antirestoration movement
The antirestoration group argued that rebuilding the monument would erase important history.
Many activists adopted an antirestoration stance after learning about the original site's significance.
The policy was criticized for its antirestoration approach to cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - favoring the preservation of existing conditions
  • antonym: restorative - intended to restore or renew
