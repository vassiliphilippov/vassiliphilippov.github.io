demean

verb
lower in dignity or worth
5.1 5.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈmiːn/ /; / /diː.ˈmiːn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈmiːn/ /; / /diː.ˈmiːn/ (BrE)
to lower someone or something in dignity, honor, or worth, especially by words or actions

Examples

To demean someone
His comment was intended to demean her achievements, but she ignored him.
It is wrong to demean others because of their background.
The advertisement was criticized for demeaning women.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degrade - to treat someone with disrespect or lower in dignity
  • antonym: exalt - to raise someone or something to a higher rank or position
  • synonym: humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed and lose respect for themselves
