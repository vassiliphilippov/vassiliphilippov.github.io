A-scan ultrasonography

noun
ultrasound technique to measure eyeball length
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ skæn ˌʌltrəsoʊˈnɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˈeɪ skæn ˌʌltrəsəˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a type of ultrasonography used to measure the length of the eyeball, often to determine intraocular lens power before eye surgery

Examples

Accurate A-scan ultrasonography
Accurate A-scan ultrasonography is crucial in calculating intraocular lens power before implantation.
Technicians receive special training to operate the equipment used in A-scan ultrasonography.
The ophthalmologist performed A-scan ultrasonography to obtain precise measurements for the patient's cataract surgery.
