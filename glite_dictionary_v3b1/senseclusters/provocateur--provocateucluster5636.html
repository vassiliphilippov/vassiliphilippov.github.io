provocateur

noun
person who incites others to commit illegal acts
8.8 0.70/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ôÀåv…ëk…ôÀàt…úr /; / pr…ôÀåv…ëÀêk…ôÀàt…úr / (AmE), / pr…ôÀåv…ík…ôÀàt…úÀê / (BrE)
a person who deliberately provokes others, especially to cause trouble or get a reaction (sometimes to get others to do something wrong)

Examples

An undercover provocateur
An undercover provocateur tried to get activists to commit acts of vandalism.
The police planted a provocateur in the crowd to stir up trouble.
There were suspicions that a provocateur was responsible for the sudden outbreak of violence.
The provocateur made several controversial statements to get a reaction from the audience.
During the protest, a known provocateur tried to incite the crowd to violence.
She was accused of being a provocateur after repeatedly challenging the group's decisions.
