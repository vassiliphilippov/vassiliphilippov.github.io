genus Treponema

noun
bacterial genus (spirochetes; includes syphilis-causing species)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌtrɛpəˈniːmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌtrɛpəˈniːmə / (BrE)
A genus of anaerobic spirochete bacteria, including species that are parasitic in warm-blooded animals and can cause diseases such as syphilis.

Examples

Genus Treponema bacteria
Members of the genus Treponema are known for their distinctive spiral shape.
Researchers discovered a new strain within the genus Treponema that affects livestock.
The bacterium responsible for syphilis belongs to the genus Treponema.

Related Terms
