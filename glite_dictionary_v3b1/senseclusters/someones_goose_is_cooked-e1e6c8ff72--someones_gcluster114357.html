someone's goose is cooked

x
to be in serious trouble
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡus ɪz kʊkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡuːs ɪz kʊkt / (BrE)
said to mean that someone is in serious trouble or is about to be punished or ruined, especially after making a mistake or being caught

Examples

Someone's goose is cooked after mistake
After missing three exams in a row, her goose is cooked for this semester.
If the boss finds out that I lost the client, my goose is cooked.
When the evidence was revealed in court, his goose was cooked.

Related Terms
