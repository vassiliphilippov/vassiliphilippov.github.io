octyl

x
eight-carbon group
Pronunciation: / ˈɑktɪl / (AmE), / ˈɒktɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group or radical derived from octane, consisting of eight carbon atoms

Examples

Octyl radical
The chemist added an octyl group to the molecule to change its properties.
Many surfactants contain an octyl chain to improve their effectiveness.
The presence of an octyl radical can affect the solubility of a compound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkyl - a group derived from an alkane by removing one hydrogen atom
