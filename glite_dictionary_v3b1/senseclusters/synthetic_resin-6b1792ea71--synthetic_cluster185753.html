synthetic resin

noun
man-made resin used in plastics
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
A man-made resin with a polymeric structure, especially in its raw state, used mainly in the production of plastics.

Examples

A synthetic resin material
Polyvinyl chloride is an example of a commonly used synthetic resin in industry.
The company specializes in producing synthetic resin for use in automotive and electronic components.
To create durable plastic goods, manufacturers often start with a liquid form of synthetic resin.
