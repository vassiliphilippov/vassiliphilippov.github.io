Einstein's special theory of relativity

noun
Einstein's 1905 physics theory on relativity and light
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪnstaɪnz ˈspɛʃəl ˈθɪəri əv rɪˈlætɪvɪti / (AmE), / ˈaɪnstaɪnz ˈspɛʃəl ˈθɪəri əv rɪˈlatɪvɪti / (BrE)
the physical theory formulated by Albert Einstein in 1905, asserting that the laws of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers and that the speed of light in a vacuum is constant, forming the basis for special relativity

Examples

Einstein's special theory of relativity in physics
Einstein's special theory of relativity revolutionized physics by describing how time and space are interconnected.
Many experiments have confirmed the predictions of Einstein's special theory of relativity.
The concept of time dilation can be explained using Einstein's special theory of relativity.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: general theory of relativity - Einstein's extension of special relativity to include gravitation and accelerated frames
  • synonym: special relativity - the theory describing the relationship between space and time, based on the constancy of the speed of light
