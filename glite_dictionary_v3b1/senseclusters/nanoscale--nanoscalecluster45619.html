nanoscale

adjective
measured in nanometers
Pronunciation: / ˈnænoʊˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈnanəʊskeɪl / (BrE)
relating to or denoting structures, devices, or processes measured in nanometers, typically on the scale of one billionth of a meter

Examples

Nanoscale devices
Researchers are developing nanoscale materials for use in advanced electronics.
The nanoscale properties of the surface allow water to roll off easily.
At the nanoscale, materials can behave very differently from their bulk forms.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: atomic - relating to atoms or the scale of atoms
  • degree higher: microscale - relating to structures or processes measured in micrometers
