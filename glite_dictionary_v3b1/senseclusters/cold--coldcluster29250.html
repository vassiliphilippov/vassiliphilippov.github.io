cold

adjective
lacking enthusiasm or warmth
3.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /koʊld/ (AmE), /kəʊld/ (BrE)
lacking warmth or friendliness; unemotional, unresponsive, or unfeeling (including sexually unresponsive or emotionally distant)

Examples

A cold reply
He gave a cold response when asked about the team's performance.
Her cold manner made it hard for others to approach her at the party.
The proposal received a cold reception from the committee, who showed little interest in the idea.
He gave his colleague a cold handshake and barely made eye contact.
Her cold response made it clear she wasn't interested in continuing the conversation.
The manager's cold decision to fire several employees upset many in the office.
Beneath his cold exterior, deep resentment simmered.
His cold anger was more frightening than if he had shouted.
She gave him a look of cold contempt before leaving the room.
