interventionist

noun
person who intervenes
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who becomes involved in a situation, especially to help change or improve it

Examples

A school interventionist
The interventionist offered support to students struggling with their studies.
As an experienced interventionist, she helped families find solutions to their problems.
A skilled interventionist can make a big difference in crisis situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bystander - a person who is present but does not take part
  • synonym: mediator - a person who helps others resolve a conflict
