Ficus sycomorus

noun
species of sycamore tree native to Africa and Asia
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪkəs ˌsaɪkəˈmɔrəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪkəs ˌsɪkəˈmɔːrəs / (BrE)
a thick-branched, wide-spreading tree native to Africa and southwestern Asia, often with buttressed roots and clusters of edible but inferior figs; also known as the biblical sycamore

Examples

The Ficus sycomorus tree
Early farmers in Africa valued the Ficus sycomorus for its shade and nourishing but modest figs.
The Ficus sycomorus is often mentioned in biblical texts, where it is referred to as the sycamore tree.
You can find a Ficus sycomorus growing prominently in some ancient village squares.
