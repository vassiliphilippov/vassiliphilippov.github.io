Rheum rhabarbarum

noun
botanical name for common rhubarb
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪəm rəˈbɑːrbərəm / (AmE), / ˈriːəm rəˈbɑːbərəm / (BrE)
The botanical name for the common rhubarb, a plant with edible stalks often used in cooking, especially for pies and sauces.

Examples

Rheum rhabarbarum plant
Botanists sometimes use the name Rheum rhabarbarum instead of simply calling it rhubarb.
Recipes often specify Rheum rhabarbarum as the preferred variety for making traditional rhubarb pie.
The gardener planted several Rheum rhabarbarum crowns in the spring to harvest fresh stalks later in the season.
