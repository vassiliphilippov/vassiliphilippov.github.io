aerosolize

verb
disperse as a fine spray in air
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr.əˌsɑː.laɪz /; / ˈɛr.oʊ.səˌlaɪz /; / ˈɛr.əˌsɑːl.aɪz /; / ˈɛrəsɒˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛə.rəˌsɒ.laɪz /; / ˈɛə.rə.səˌlaɪz /; / ˈɛər.əˌsɒl.aɪz /; / ˈɛərəʊsəlaɪz / (BrE)
to turn something into a fine spray or suspension in air

Examples

To aerosolize infectious agents
It is dangerous to aerosolize infectious agents without proper protection.
Many disinfectants can be aerosolized for use in spraying systems.
The laboratory technician will aerosolize the chemical for analysis.
During the experiment, the researchers aerosolized the chemical to test its effects in the air.
Some viruses can aerosolize and remain infectious for hours.
Water droplets aerosolize when ejected from a spray bottle.
Accidentally aerosolising cleaning chemicals can pose inhalation risks in confined spaces.
Some medical devices are designed to aerosolise medications for patients with respiratory diseases.
The laboratory procedure aerosolised the virus for further study in controlled environments.
