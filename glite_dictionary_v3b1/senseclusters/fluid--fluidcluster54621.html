fluid

noun
substance that flows (liquid or gas)
Pronunciation: / ˈfluː.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈfluː.ɪd / (BrE)
a substance, such as a liquid or gas, that can flow and takes the shape of its container

Examples

A fluid
Engineers study how a fluid moves through pipes and pumps.
The doctor advised her to drink plenty of fluid to stay hydrated.
Water and air are both examples of a fluid.
Air, like water, is considered a fluid because it flows and takes the shape of its container.
Paint is a fluid that can be poured and spread easily over a surface.
The nurse gave him extra fluid to prevent dehydration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gas - a state of matter with no fixed shape or volume
  • synonym: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
  • antonym: solid - state of matter with fixed shape and volume
