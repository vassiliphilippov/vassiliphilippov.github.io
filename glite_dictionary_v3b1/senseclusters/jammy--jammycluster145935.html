jammy

adjective
improvisational music style
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæmi / (AmE), / ˈdʒæmi / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a musical jam session, especially involving informal improvisation.

Examples

Jammy guitar solos
The guitarist led a jammy performance that kept the audience guessing what would come next.
Fans loved the jammy feel of the band's live shows, where no two songs sounded the same.
Their latest album features several jammy tracks with extended solos and creative rhythms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvised - created or performed spontaneously without preparation
  • antonym: structured - organized or arranged in a planned way
