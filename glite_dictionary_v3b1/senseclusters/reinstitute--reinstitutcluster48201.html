reinstitute

verb
to restore or restart
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɪnstɪˌtuːt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪnstɪtjuːt / (BrE)
to bring back or start again something, such as a rule, system, or practice, that was previously stopped

Examples

To reinstitute a rule
After several years, the school decided to reinstitute the uniform policy.
The government may reinstitute travel restrictions if cases rise again.
Many employees welcomed the decision to reinstitute flexible working hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolish - to formally put an end to something
  • synonym: restore - to bring back into existence or use
