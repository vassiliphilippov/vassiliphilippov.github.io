unconscionable

adjective
completely unacceptable; lacking conscience
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkɑnʃənəbəl /; / ʌnˈkɑn.ʃə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃənəbl /; / ʌnˈkɒn.ʃə.nə.bəl / (BrE)
so morally wrong, excessive, or unreasonable that it is completely unacceptable

Examples

An unconscionable act
His unconscionable lies destroyed her reputation.
It was an unconscionable act of greed that harmed many innocent people.
They charged an unconscionable amount of money for a simple repair.
It would be unconscionable to expect people to work under such dangerous conditions.
The company was criticized for its unconscionable profit margins.
Their landlord charged an unconscionable amount of money for a tiny apartment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conscienceless - lacking conscience
  • synonym: exorbitant - (especially of a price or amount) much too high
  • antonym: principled - having strong moral principles
  • antonym: reasonable - fair and sensible; not excessive
