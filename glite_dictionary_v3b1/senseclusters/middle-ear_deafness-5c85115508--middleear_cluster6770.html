middle-ear deafness

noun
hearing loss from middle ear bone issues
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdəl ɪɚ ˈdɛfnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ɪə ˈdɛfnəs / (BrE)
a type of hearing loss caused by problems with the bones of the middle ear

Examples

Middle-ear deafness caused by bones
Children born with certain bone disorders are at higher risk for middle-ear deafness.
Surgery is sometimes recommended to help restore hearing in patients with middle-ear deafness.
The doctor diagnosed him with middle-ear deafness after examining his hearing test results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conductive deafness - hearing loss caused by sound not reaching the inner ear
  • antonym: sensorineural deafness - hearing loss due to problems in the inner ear or nerve
