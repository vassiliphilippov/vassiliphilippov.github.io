primary dentition

noun
the first (deciduous) set of teeth in children
5.5 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri dɛnˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri dɛnˈtɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the first set of teeth in humans, consisting of the deciduous or baby teeth that are eventually replaced by permanent teeth

Examples

The primary dentition set
Children typically develop their primary dentition by the age of three.
Proper care of the primary dentition is important to prevent future dental problems.
The dentist explained the differences between primary dentition and permanent teeth to the parents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanent dentition - the second set of teeth that replace the primary dentition
