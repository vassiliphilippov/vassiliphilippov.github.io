hypochromic

adjective
pale red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈkroʊmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkrəʊmɪk / (BrE)
having less color or appearing paler than normal, especially referring to red blood cells with less hemoglobin

Examples

Hypochromic cells
The doctor noted the presence of hypochromic cells in the patient's blood sample.
A hypochromic appearance in red blood cells often suggests iron deficiency anemia.
Laboratory results showed a high percentage of hypochromic erythrocytes after the illness.
The doctor noted that the patient's red blood cells were hypochromic under the microscope.
A hypochromic appearance in tissue samples can indicate certain types of anemia.
Researchers observed hypochromic regions in the skin affected by the condition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anemic - having a lower than normal number of red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • antonym: hyperchromic - having more color or pigmentation than normal
  • antonym: normochromic - having red blood cells with normal color and hemoglobin content
  • synonym: pale - having a lighter or less intense color
