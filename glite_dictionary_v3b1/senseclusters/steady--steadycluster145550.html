steady

adjective
regular; habitual (person)
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
not changing, regular, or constant in value, behavior, or character

Examples

Steady progress
He has become a steady customer at our bakery, coming in almost every day.
Mike was a steady runner, participating in marathons year after year.
She was known as a steady gambler who visited the casino every weekend.
Despite the crisis, the currency's value stayed steady throughout the quarter.
Investors are attracted to steady markets with minimal fluctuations.
The company's profits remained steady during a period of economic uncertainty.
After years of searching, he finally found a steady job with good benefits.
The boat moved at a steady pace across the calm lake.
The doctor recommended a steady routine to improve her sleep patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constant - occurring continuously over a period of time; unchanging
  • synonym: regular - doing something frequently or habitually
  • synonym: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established
  • antonym: unsteady - not firm or regular; liable to change or fluctuate
  • antonym: volatile - liable to change rapidly and unpredictably
