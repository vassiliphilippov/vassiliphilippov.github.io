irradiation

noun
spread of neural impulses in the cortex (physiology)
Pronunciation: / ɪˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the spreading of effects in the body or mind, such as neural impulses or conditioned responses, beyond the original area

Examples

Neural irradiation effect
In severe cases, sensory irradiation may cause a patient to perceive touch in a much wider area than was stimulated.
Neurologists study irradiation to better understand how brain injuries affect the spread of neural signals.
The irradiation of pain in the cortex can make it difficult to locate the true origin of discomfort.
Early psychologists studied irradiation to understand the spread of conditioned responses.
In classical conditioning, irradiation can cause a dog to salivate at tones similar to the original one.
Through irradiation, children might fear objects resembling the original threatening stimulus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discrimination - the learned ability to distinguish between a conditioned stimulus and similar stimuli
  • synonym: generalization - the tendency to respond in the same way to different but similar stimuli in conditioning
