pilot chart

noun
navigation map showing regional weather and sea conditions
4.0 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpaɪ.lət tʃɑːrt/ (AmE), /ˈpaɪ.lət tʃɑːt/ (BrE)
a map used by navigators to show prevailing meteorological, hydrographic, and navigational conditions, such as winds, currents, and weather patterns for a specific region and time of year

Examples

A pilot chart map
Every year, navigators update their pilot charts with new meteorological data.
Researchers used a pilot chart to analyze prevailing wind and current patterns in the area.
The captain consulted the pilot chart to plan the safest route across the Atlantic Ocean.
