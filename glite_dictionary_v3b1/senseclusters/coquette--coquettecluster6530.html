coquette

verb
act flirtatiously without serious intent
Pronunciation: / koʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / kəʊˈkɛt / (BrE)
to behave flirtatiously or playfully, often without serious intentions

Examples

To coquette with men
Despite her marriage, she continued to coquette with others, enjoying the harmless attention.
He would often coquette with colleagues, making his intentions unclear.
She loved to coquette with her admirers at every party she attended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flirt - to behave as if attracted to someone, usually playfully
