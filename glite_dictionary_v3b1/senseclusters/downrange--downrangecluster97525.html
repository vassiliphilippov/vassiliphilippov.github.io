downrange

adverb
in a combat area
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌreɪndʒ / (BrE)
in or toward an area away from the home base, especially in an operational or combat zone abroad.

Examples

Serving downrange
After the final checks, the soldier told his family he would be downrange for nine months.
Many troops spend long periods downrange before returning home.
She sent care packages to her brother while he was stationed downrange.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deployed - sent to a military area for duty
  • antonym: home - at or near one's home base
