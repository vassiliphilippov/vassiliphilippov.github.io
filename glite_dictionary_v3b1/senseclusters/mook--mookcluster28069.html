mook

noun
low-level criminal
9.9
Pronunciation: / muːk / (AmE), / muːk / (BrE)
a low-level criminal or thug who is hired to carry out violent or illegal tasks for others

Examples

Mob mook
The mook waited outside the building, ready to follow his boss's orders.
In the movie, the hero easily defeated several mooks before reaching the main villain.
Police arrested a mook who was caught trying to break into the warehouse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boss - the leader or person in charge of a criminal group
  • synonym: goon - a hired thug or violent criminal
  • synonym: henchman - a loyal follower or subordinate, especially one who does criminal work
