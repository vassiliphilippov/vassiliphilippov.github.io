Cathar

noun
medieval Christian sect member
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.θər/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.θə/ (BrE)
a member of a medieval Christian sect in southern France that was declared heretical by the Catholic Church

Examples

A medieval Cathar
The Cathar faith spread rapidly in the Languedoc region during the 12th century.
Many Cathars sought refuge in remote castles to escape persecution.
The beliefs of the Cathar community differed greatly from those of the Catholic Church.
