gibber

verb
speak rapidly and unintelligibly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪbər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪbə / (BrE)
to speak or make sounds rapidly and in a way that cannot be understood, often because of nervousness or excitement

Examples

To gibber nervously
He tends to gibber nervously when he's in front of a large crowd.
She could only gibber incoherently after hearing the shocking news.
The toddler began to gibber excitedly when he saw the puppy.
Lost in fright, he could only gibber nonsense instead of explaining what happened.
The monkey began to gibber excitedly when it saw the zookeeper approaching.
Tourists giggled as a group of lemurs gibbered loudly in the treetops above.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
