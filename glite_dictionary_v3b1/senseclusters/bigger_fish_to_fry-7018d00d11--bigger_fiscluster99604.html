bigger fish to fry

x
more important matters to deal with
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪɡɚ fɪʃ tuː fraɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡə fɪʃ tʊ fraɪ / (BrE)
used to say that one has more important or more urgent matters to attend to than the current issue

Examples

Have bigger fish to fry
Although he was annoyed by the error, he knew there were bigger fish to fry at the office.
She refused to join the argument because she had bigger fish to fry.
We can't spend all day on this minor problem; we've got bigger fish to fry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: more pressing matters - matters that are more urgent or important
