seat

verb
to provide with seats or seating
1.8 16/m
Pronunciation: / sit /; / siÀêt / (AmE), / siÀêt / (BrE)
to provide seats for people, arrange where people sit, or show someone to their seat

Examples

To seat the guests
The organizers seated the guests according to their ticket numbers.
The theater can seat over 1,000 people for special events.
This lecture hall seats comfortably up to three hundred students.
Please wait here until the hostess seats you at your table.
The teacher seated the students in alphabetical order for the ceremony.
The usher seated the guests as they arrived at the theater.
He seated the children in a circle before reading the story.
Please seat yourself wherever you like.
The hostess seated the guests near the window.
