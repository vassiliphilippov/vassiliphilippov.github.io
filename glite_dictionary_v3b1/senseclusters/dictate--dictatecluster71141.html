dictate

noun
guiding principle or command
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪkteɪt /; / ˈdɪkˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪkteɪt / (BrE)
a rule, command, or principle that must be followed

Examples

Dictates of reason
He acted according to the dictates of reason rather than emotion.
She always tried to follow the dictates of her conscience.
The company's policies are governed by the dictates of fairness and integrity.
Despite external dictates, the community maintained its traditions.
She refused to ignore the dictates of her conscience.
The company followed the dictates of the new policy without question.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command - an authoritative order
  • synonym: order - a command or directive that must be followed
  • synonym: principle - a fundamental truth or proposition that guides action
  • antonym: suggestion - an idea or proposal put forward for consideration without authority
