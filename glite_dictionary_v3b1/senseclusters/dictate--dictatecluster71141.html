Examples

Synonyms

  • order
    3.0 106/m
    an order is a direct instruction from an authority to be obeyed immediately, while a dictate can mean a rule or guiding principle.
  • rule
    1.0 189/m
    a rule is a general law or standard that guides behavior in many situations, while a dictate is often a single command.
  • fiat
    8.5 3.94/m
    a fiat is an official decree often used by rulers or courts, while a dictate can be any rule or command.
  • diktat
    9.4 0.12/m
    a diktat is an order forced on others without consent and often resented, while a dictate is any rule or command.
  • prescript
    8.9 0.01/m
    a prescript is a formally written rule given to guide conduct in an institution, while a dictate is a general command.
  • direction
    2.0 12/m
    a direction is a practical instruction on how to do something step by step, while a dictate is a rule to be followed.
