princess feather

noun
annual plant with crimson drooping flower spikes
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪnsɪs ˌfɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪs ˌfɛðə / (BrE)
an annual plant with broad ovate leaves and slender, drooping spikes of crimson flowers, native to southeastern Asia and Australia and naturalized in North America, also known as love-lies-bleeding (Amaranthus caudatus)

Examples

Princess feather plant
Many gardeners choose princess feather for its strikingly drooping crimson flower spikes.
The vibrant princess feather added a dramatic splash of color to the summer garden.
While native to Asia and Australia, princess feather has become popular in North American landscapes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: love-lies-bleeding - another common name for Amaranthus caudatus
