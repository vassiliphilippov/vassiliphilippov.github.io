shocked

adjective
feeling surprised and upset
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʃɑkt / (AmE), / ʃɒkt / (BrE)
feeling surprised and upset, often because something unexpected or unpleasant has happened; struck with fear, dread, or consternation

Examples

Look shocked
Everyone looked shocked when the announcement was made.
I felt truly shocked after hearing what he had done.
She was shocked by the news of the accident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not feeling upset or shocked
  • synonym: surprised - feeling or showing surprise
