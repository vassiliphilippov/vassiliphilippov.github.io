sparseness

noun
the state of being sparse or scanty
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑrs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspɑːs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being thinly scattered or insufficient in amount; the state of lacking density or fullness

Examples

Sparseness of information
Critics noted the sparseness of details provided in the official report.
During winter, the sparseness of daylight hours can affect people’s moods.
The sparseness of vegetation in the desert makes survival difficult for many animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: density - the degree to which something is filled or compact
  • synonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply; insufficiency
