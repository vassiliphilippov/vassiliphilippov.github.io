Iris kochii

noun
species of iris with deep blue-purple flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪɹɪs ˈkoʊkiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪɹɪs ˈkɒkiˌaɪ / (BrE)
A species of iris native to northern Italy, known for its deep blue-purple flowers and resembling but smaller than Iris germanica.

Examples

Iris kochii flower
Botanists have noted that Iris kochii is similar to but smaller than the more common Iris germanica.
Many horticulturists prize Iris kochii for its deep blue-purple flowers and compact growth.
The Iris kochii blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to the gardens of northern Italy.
