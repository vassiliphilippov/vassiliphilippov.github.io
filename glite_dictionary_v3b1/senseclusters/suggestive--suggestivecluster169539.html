suggestive

adjective
clearly indicating or revealing something
6.7 4.2
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈdʒes.tɪv/ /; / /sʌ.ˈdʒes.tɪv/ (AmE), /sə.ˈdʒes.tɪv/ (BrE)
making you think of something or implying something without saying it directly

Examples

Suggestive look
His manner was suggestive of someone hiding a secret.
The dark clouds were suggestive of an approaching storm.
The faint aroma in the air was suggestive of freshly baked bread.
Her tone was deliberately suggestive without saying anything outright.
The old ruins were suggestive of a vibrant ancient civilization.
The symbol is highly suggestive of religious meaning.
