Francis Scott Key

proper noun

Pronunciation: /ˈfræn.sɪs skɑːt kiː/ /; / /ˈfræn.səs skɑːt kiː/ (AmE), /ˈfræn.sɪs skɒt kiː/ /; / /ˈfrɑːn.sɪs skɒt kiː/ (BrE)
(1779–1843) An American lawyer and poet best known for writing the poem that became the lyrics of ‘The Star-Spangled Banner’, the national anthem of the United States.
