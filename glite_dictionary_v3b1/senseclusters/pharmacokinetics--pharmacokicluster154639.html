pharmacokinetics

noun
study of how drugs move in the body
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːrməkoʊkɪˈnetɪks / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməkəʊkɪˈnetɪks / (BrE)
the branch of pharmacology concerned with the movement of drugs within the body, including absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion.

Examples

Study of pharmacokinetics
Advances in pharmacokinetics have led to safer and more effective drug therapies.
Modern clinical trials require detailed pharmacokinetics to predict the best dosage for patients.
Understanding pharmacokinetics is crucial for evaluating potential side effects and interactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pharmacodynamics - the study of the effects of drugs on the body
