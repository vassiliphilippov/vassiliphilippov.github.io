convincibility

noun
ability to persuade
7.7
Pronunciation: / kənˌvɪnsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˌvɪnsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to convince others or the capacity to make something believable or persuasive

Examples

Convincibility of evidence
The convincibility of the lawyer's argument swayed the jury in his client's favor.
Researchers tested the convincibility of their new theory with a series of experiments.
Her speech lacked the convincibility needed to change anyone's mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credibility - the quality of being trusted or believed
  • synonym: persuasiveness - the ability to persuade or convince others
  • antonym: unconvincingness - the quality of failing to persuade or convince
