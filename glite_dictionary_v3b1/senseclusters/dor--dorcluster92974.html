dor

noun
large beetle (archaic)
Pronunciation: / dɔr / (AmE), / dɔː / (BrE)
an old term for a large beetle, especially the cockchafer, known for its buzzing flight

Examples

A dor beetle
The buzzing of a dor filled the quiet summer evening.
In old country tales, the dor was often mentioned as a sign of summer.
A dor landed on the window, startling the children with its loud hum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beetle - an insect with a hard shell-like back
  • synonym: cockchafer - a large European beetle known for its buzzing flight
