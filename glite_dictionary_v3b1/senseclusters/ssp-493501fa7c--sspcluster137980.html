SSP

noun
conservation breeding program
Pronunciation: / ɛs ɛs pi / (AmE), / ɛs ɛs pi / (BrE)
an organized conservation breeding program, called Species Survival Plan, run by zoos and wildlife organizations to protect endangered species

Examples

Zoo SSP program
The zoo participates in the SSP to help save endangered tigers from extinction.
Many rare animals are bred under the SSP to maintain healthy populations.
Thanks to the SSP, several species have been successfully reintroduced into the wild.

Related Terms

  • synonym: captive breeding - the process of breeding animals in controlled environments within human care
  • synonym: conservation program - an organized effort to protect and preserve wildlife or natural resources
