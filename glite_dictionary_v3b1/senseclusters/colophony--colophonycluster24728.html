colophony

noun
resin from pine trees used in varnish and on instrument bows
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɑːfəni / (AmE), / kəˈlɒfəni / (BrE)
a translucent, brittle resin obtained from pine trees, used in varnishes, inks, and to increase friction on the bows of stringed instruments

Examples

Colophony resin
Many varnishes are made using colophony as a primary ingredient.
The violinist applied colophony to her bow before the concert began.
Without colophony, the bow would not grip the strings properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rosin - solid form of resin from pines, used similarly to colophony
