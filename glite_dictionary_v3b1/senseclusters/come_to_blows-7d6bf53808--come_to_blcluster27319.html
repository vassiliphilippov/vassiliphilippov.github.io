come to blows

verb
engage in a physical fight
6.7
Pronunciation: / kʌm tu bloʊz / (AmE), / kʌm tuː bləʊz / (BrE)
to begin fighting physically with someone, usually after an argument or disagreement

Examples

To come to blows suddenly
During the stressful meeting, colleagues came to blows over the budget cuts.
Tensions rose so high that they eventually came to blows in the hallway.
The two players nearly came to blows after the controversial call.
