egg-laying mammal

noun
a mammal that lays eggs
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɛɡ ˈleɪ.ɪŋ ˈmæm.əl / (AmE), / ɛɡ ˈleɪ.ɪŋ ˈmam.əl / (BrE)
a primitive mammal that lays eggs, such as those belonging to the subclass Prototheria (e.g., monotremes like the platypus and echidnas)

Examples

An egg-laying mammal species
Echidnas are one type of egg-laying mammal found in the wild.
Scientists study the reproductive systems of egg-laying mammals to understand mammalian evolution.
The platypus is an unusual egg-laying mammal native to Australia.
