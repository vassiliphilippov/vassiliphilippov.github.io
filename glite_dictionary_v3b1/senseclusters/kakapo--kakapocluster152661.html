kakapo

noun
flightless New Zealand parrot
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːkəˌpoʊ /; / ˈkɑkəˌpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːkəˌpəʊ /; / ˈkɑːkəpəʊ / (BrE)
a large, nocturnal, flightless parrot native to New Zealand, known for its green feathers and endangered status

Examples

The kakapo bird

Conservationists work tirelessly to protect the kakapo from extinction.
Unlike most parrots, the kakapo cannot fly and is active mainly at night.
