transmissivity

noun
capacity to transmit water
8.9
Pronunciation: /trænz.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ /; / /træns.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /trænz.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ /; / /træns.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the ability of an aquifer or material to transmit water, calculated as hydraulic conductivity times thickness

Examples

Transmissivity of soil
The transmissivity of the soil determines how quickly groundwater can move through the area.
Scientists tested the transmissivity of several aquifers to assess their suitability for water supply.
Low transmissivity means water will flow very slowly through the underground rock layers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impermeability - the inability of a material to allow fluids to pass through it
  • synonym: permeability - the ability of a material to allow fluids to pass through it
