winter hazel

noun
Asiatic shrub with yellow flowers in early spring
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪntɚ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈheɪzl̩ / (BrE)
Any of several deciduous shrubs of the genus Corylopsis, native to East Asia, known for their drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear.

Examples

Winter hazel shrub
Gardeners often plant winter hazel for early-season color in their landscapes.
In March, the winter hazel was already in bloom, brightening the garden with its vibrant racemes.
The winter hazel produces delicate yellow flowers long before its leaves unfurl.
