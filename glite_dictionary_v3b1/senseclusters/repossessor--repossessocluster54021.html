repossessor

noun
person reclaiming property
Pronunciation: / ˌriːpəˈzɛsər / (AmE), / ˌriːpəˈzɛsə / (BrE)
a person or company that takes back property, such as a car or house, when payments are not made

Examples

The repossessor takes back
The repossessor arrived early in the morning to take back the vehicle.
After missing several mortgage payments, a repossessor was sent to reclaim the house.
The company hired a repossessor to recover unpaid equipment from the business.
