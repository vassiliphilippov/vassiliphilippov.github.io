scrambled

adjective
disorderly or confused
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈskræmbəld / (AmE), / ˈskræmbld / (BrE)
(of things) mixed or thrown together in a confused or disorderly way.

Examples

A scrambled message
After the power outage, the files on my computer became scrambled.
The report was so scrambled that no one could follow the main points.
We had to reorganize the scrambled schedule after the sudden changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disordered - lacking order or organization
  • antonym: orderly - methodically arranged or well organized
