Dalea spinosa

noun
desert shrub of southwestern US and Mexico
Pronunciation: / dəˈliːə spɪˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / dəˈliːə spɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A greyish-green shrub native to desert regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico, with sparse foliage and bluish-violet flowers, and valued locally for producing light-colored honey.

Examples

Dalea spinosa shrub
Beekeepers highly prize honey made from the flowers of Dalea spinosa due to its unique flavor.
In spring, the Dalea spinosa produces striking spikes of bluish-violet blossoms across the arid landscape.
The Dalea spinosa shrub thrives under harsh desert conditions and provides shelter for native wildlife.
