cladophyll

noun
a stem that looks and acts like a leaf
Pronunciation: / ˈklædəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈkladəfɪl / (BrE)
a flattened stem that closely resembles and functions as a leaf, found in certain plants

Examples

A cladophyll stem
In botany class, we studied the structure of a cladophyll in comparison to a typical leaf.
Some desert plants use a cladophyll to carry out photosynthesis instead of ordinary leaves.
The cladophyll of the asparagus plant is often mistaken for a true leaf.
