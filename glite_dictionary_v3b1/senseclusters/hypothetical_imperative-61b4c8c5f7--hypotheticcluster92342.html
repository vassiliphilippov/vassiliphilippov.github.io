hypothetical imperative

noun
principle prescribing actions to reach a goal
4.5 8.6
Pronunciation: / haɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl ɪmˈpɛrətɪv / (AmE), / hʌɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl ɪmˈpɛrətɪv / (BrE)
a principle that prescribes an action to achieve a specific goal or end, typically based on conditional reasoning

Examples

A hypothetical imperative statement
A hypothetical imperative might state, 'If you want to stay healthy, you should exercise regularly.'
Kant distinguished between the hypothetical imperative and the categorical imperative in his moral philosophy.
The concept of a hypothetical imperative helps explain why certain actions are only required in specific circumstances.
