stade

noun
ancient Greek length unit
Pronunciation: / steɪd / (AmE), / steɪd / (BrE)
an ancient Greek unit of length, approximately equal to 185 meters

Examples

A stade long
The ancient race was measured in stades rather than meters.
Historians estimate the city walls stretched for nearly forty stades.
A marathon in ancient Greece was many times longer than a single stade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: meter - the standard unit of length in the metric system
  • synonym: stadium - an ancient Greek or Roman running track, originally the length of a stade
