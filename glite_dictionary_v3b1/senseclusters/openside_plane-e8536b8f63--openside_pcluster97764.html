openside plane

noun
woodworking tool for cutting rabbets
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpənˌsaɪd pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənˌsaɪd pleɪn / (BrE)
a woodworking plane designed to cut rabbets, featuring an open side to allow for wider cuts than a regular plane

Examples

An openside plane tool
An openside plane allows woodworkers to cut rabbets of varying widths without obstruction from the tool's side.
He found that his openside plane worked better than a regular rabbet plane when making wide joints.
The carpenter used an openside plane to create a deep rabbet along the edge of the board.
