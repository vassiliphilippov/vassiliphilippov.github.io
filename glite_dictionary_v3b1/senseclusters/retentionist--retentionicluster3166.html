retentionist

noun
supporter of keeping laws
9.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɛnʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛnʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports keeping a particular law or practice, especially the death penalty

Examples

A retentionist of law
As a retentionist, she spoke out against abolishing the death penalty in her country.
The debate featured a retentionist and an abolitionist discussing the future of capital punishment.
Many retentionists believe that certain laws are necessary for maintaining order in society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolitionist - a person who supports ending a particular law or practice, especially the death penalty
