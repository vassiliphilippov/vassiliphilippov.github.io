formalistically

adverb
in a rule-focused way
8.7
Pronunciation: /fɑːr.mə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /fɔːr.mə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (AmE), /fɔː.mə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (BrE)
in a way that strictly follows rules or structure, without considering creativity or emotion

Examples

To act formalistically
The professor graded the essays formalistically, focusing only on structure and grammar.
Some artists are criticized for working formalistically instead of expressing genuine emotion.
The report was written formalistically, following every guideline but lacking personal insight.
The report was organized formalistically, following every guideline to the letter.
He answered the questions formalistically, without adding any personal insight or opinion.
Some teachers grade formalistically, focusing only on structure rather than content.
