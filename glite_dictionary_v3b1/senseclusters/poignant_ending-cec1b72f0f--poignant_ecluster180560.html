poignant ending

noun
deeply emotional conclusion
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪnjənt ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnjənt ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
An ending that evokes deep emotion, especially sadness or regret.

Examples

A poignant ending
After all the hardship, the story's poignant ending brought tears to her eyes.
Many films strive for a poignant ending to leave a lasting impact on the audience.
The novel's poignant ending left readers reflecting on the nature of loss and hope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: happy ending - an ending with a positive or joyful outcome
