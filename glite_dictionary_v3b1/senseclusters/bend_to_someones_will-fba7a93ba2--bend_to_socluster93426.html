bend to someone's will

verb
submit to another's wishes or authority
Pronunciation: / bɛnd tu ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪl / (AmE), / bɛnd tʊ ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪl / (BrE)
to submit or yield to the wishes, authority, or influence of another person

Examples

To bend to someone's will completely
He tried to bend to her will, but ultimately made his own decision.
She refused to bend to his will and stood firm in her beliefs.
The government would not bend to the protesters' will despite growing pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resist - refuse to accept or comply with something; try to prevent by action or argument
  • synonym: submit - accept or yield to a superior force or to the authority of another
