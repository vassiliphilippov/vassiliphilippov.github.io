clock watcher

noun
employee preoccupied with end of workday
Pronunciation: / klɑk ˈwɑt͡ʃɚ / (AmE), / klɒk ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
an employee who is overly concerned with leaving work at the end of the day rather than being engaged in their job

Examples

A clock watcher at work
Instead of being a clock watcher, try to find more satisfaction in your daily tasks.
No company wants to hire a clock watcher who just does the bare minimum.
Tom has a reputation as a clock watcher who packs up his bag at exactly five o'clock every day.

  • synonym: shirker - a person who avoids work or duty
