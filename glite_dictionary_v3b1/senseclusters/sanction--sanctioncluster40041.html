sanction

noun
official penalty
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
an official penalty or restriction imposed to influence behavior, especially by a government or authority

Examples

Trade sanctions
The government imposed strict sanctions on the company for violating environmental laws.
International sanctions can have a major impact on a country's economy.
The threat of sanction was enough to make the organization change its policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: penalty - a punishment for breaking a law or rule
  • synonym: restriction - a rule or law that limits what people can do
  • antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
