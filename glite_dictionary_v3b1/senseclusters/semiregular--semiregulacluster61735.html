semiregular

adjective
partly regular
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
partly regular in form, pattern, or occurrence but not completely so

Examples

Semiregular meetings
The meetings are held on a semiregular basis, sometimes monthly and sometimes every other month.
She noticed a semiregular pattern in the way the lights flickered each evening.
The club hosts semiregular events that do not follow a strict schedule.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regular - following a consistent pattern or schedule
