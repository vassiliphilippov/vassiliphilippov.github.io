sluice down

verb
flow or pour forcefully like from a sluice
7.6 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / sluÀês da än / (AmE), / sluÀês da än / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to pour or flow forcefully, as if from a sluice.

Examples

To sluice down water
A surge of rainwater sluiced down the city streets after the storm.
Sweat sluiced down his face as he finished the marathon.
The water sluiced down the hillside, carrying mud and debris into the valley.

Synonyms

  • sluice
    7.8 0.18/m
    sluice makes water flow out as a strong steady stream, while sluice down shows that same force directed downward.
  • gush
    3.2 2.00/m
    gush sends liquid out suddenly and with great force, while sluice down stresses a controlled strong flow as from a gate.
  • pour out
    2.9 1.59/m
    pour out means to flow or let liquid leave in large amounts, while sluice down implies a forceful flow down a channel.
