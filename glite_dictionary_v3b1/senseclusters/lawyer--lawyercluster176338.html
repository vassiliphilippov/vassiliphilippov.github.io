lawyer

verb
to outsmart in argument
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔjərd / (AmE), / ˈlɔːjəd / (BrE)
to defeat or outsmart someone in an argument, often by using clever reasoning or legal tactics

Examples

To lawyer an opponent
During the heated discussion, she completely lawyered her opponent with her sharp logic.
I thought I had a good point, but he lawyered me with a clever counterargument.
You really got lawyered in that debate; next time, be better prepared.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to be defeated in an argument or contest
  • synonym: outmaneuver - to gain an advantage over someone by using clever tactics
  • synonym: outwit - to defeat someone by being more clever
