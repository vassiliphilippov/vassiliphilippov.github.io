emulsify

verb
mix into an emulsion
9.7 5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmʌlsɪˌfaɪ /; / ɪˈmʌl.səˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪ /; / ɪˈmʌl.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to mix two liquids (such as oil and water) so that they form a smooth, uniform mixture (an emulsion)

Examples

To emulsify oil
Beat the dressing vigorously to emulsify the oil and vinegar.
Many sauces emulsify when egg yolks are added.
The chef taught us how to emulsify ingredients properly for a smooth texture.
If you add the oil slowly, the mixture will emulsify and become smooth.
Salad dressings often emulsify when you shake them vigorously.
The cream failed to emulsify with the vinegar, resulting in a separated appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combine - to mix two or more substances together
  • synonym: emulsion - a mixture in which one liquid is suspended in another
