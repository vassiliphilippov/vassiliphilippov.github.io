didactically

adverb
in a teaching or instructive manner
7.7 6.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈdæk.tɪ.kəl.ɪ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈdæk.tə.kəl.ɪ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈdæk.tɪ.kəl.ɪ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈdæk.tə.kəl.ɪ/ (BrE)
In a manner intended to teach or instruct, often with an emphasis on educational or moral lessons.

Examples

To speak didactically
She tends to speak didactically, often making her points with detailed examples and explanations.
The book presents its arguments didactically, guiding the reader carefully through complex concepts.
The professor explained the theory didactically, ensuring that every student understood each step.
