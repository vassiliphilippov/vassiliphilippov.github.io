revolutionism

noun
belief in revolutionary principles
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief in or advocacy for the spread of revolutionary principles and radical change, especially in politics or society

Examples

Revolutionism in politics
During the early 20th century, revolutionism became a driving force behind many social movements.
His commitment to revolutionism inspired others to challenge the existing political order.
Some critics argue that revolutionism can lead to instability if not balanced with practical reforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservatism - belief in preserving established traditions and resisting change
  • synonym: revolutionary - advocating or engaged in revolution
