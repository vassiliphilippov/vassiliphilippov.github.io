radio receiver

noun
device for receiving radio signals
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
An electronic device that detects, demodulates, and amplifies radio signals, allowing audio or data to be received.

Examples

A radio receiver device
A new digital radio receiver allows you to listen to hundreds of stations with perfect clarity.
Old radio receivers often required manual tuning and produced static noise.
The engineer adjusted the radio receiver to pick up a weak signal from a distant station.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radio - the transmission or reception of electromagnetic waves of radio frequency
  • antonym: radio transmitter - a device that sends out radio signals
