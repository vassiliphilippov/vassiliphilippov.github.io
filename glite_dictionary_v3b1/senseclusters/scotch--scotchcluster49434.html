scotch

verb
hinder or prevent plans or efforts
7.8
Pronunciation: / skɑtʃ / (AmE), / skɒtʃ / (BrE)
to stop something from happening; to hinder or prevent the realization of a plan, effort, or desire

Examples

To scotch a plan
Her determined opposition scotched his plans for major reform.
Rumors of a merger were quickly scotched by company officials.
The new regulations will scotch any attempts to cheat the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foil - to prevent the success of; to frustrate
  • synonym: frustrate - to prevent a plan or attempted action from being successful
  • synonym: thwart - to prevent someone from accomplishing something
