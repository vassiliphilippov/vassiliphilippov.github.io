piggyback

verb
carry trailers on trains (combined transport)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪɡiˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡiˌbæk / (BrE)
to transport or be transported on top of another vehicle or object, especially as a method of combined transport

Examples

To piggyback a trailer
Freight operators often piggyback containers from ports to inland depots to save on fuel and time.
In recent years, many logistics firms have piggybacked their cargo to reduce highway congestion.
The company began to piggyback truck trailers on railroad cars to optimize delivery routes.
Several shipping containers were piggybacked across the country to save fuel costs.
The company decided to piggyback the new trucks on a freight train for faster delivery.
We can piggyback your trailer to Chicago if you need it delivered by next week.
