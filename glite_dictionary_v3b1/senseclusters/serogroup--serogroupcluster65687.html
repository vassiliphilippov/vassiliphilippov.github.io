serogroup

noun
microbial antigen group
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪroʊˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəʊˌɡruːp /; / ˈsɪərəˌɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of microorganisms (such as bacteria or viruses) classified together because they have the same antigens on their surface

Examples

A serogroup of bacteria
The laboratory identified the bacteria as belonging to a new serogroup not seen before.
Vaccines are often developed to target specific serogroups of a pathogen.
Researchers compared the prevalence of each serogroup in different regions.
The laboratory identified the bacteria as belonging to a rare serogroup not commonly found in the region.
Vaccines are often developed to target the most dangerous serogroup of a particular pathogen.
Outbreaks can sometimes be traced to a single serogroup responsible for multiple cases.
The laboratory confirmed that the outbreak was caused by a new serogroup of bacteria.
Vaccines are often developed to target the most common serogroups responsible for disease.
Researchers compared the characteristics of each serogroup to understand their spread.
