mendicant

adjective
practicing or characteristic of begging
7.1 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmen.dɪ.kənt/ (AmE), /ˈmen.dɪ.kənt/ (BrE)
(of a person or group) practicing begging or depending on charitable donations for support.

Examples

Mendicant friar
He joined an order of mendicant friars and renounced all personal possessions.
In medieval times, mendicant orders were different from traditional monastic communities.
The city was known for its mendicant monks, who depended on alms for their survival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: begging - asking for charity or money, especially as a practice
  • antonym: self-sufficient - independent; not relying on others for support
