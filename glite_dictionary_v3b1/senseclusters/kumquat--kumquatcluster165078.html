kumquat

noun
small oval citrus fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌmˌkwɑt / (AmE), / ˈkʌmˌkwɒt /; / ˈkʌm.kwɒt / (BrE)
a small, oval or round orange citrus fruit with a sweet edible skin and sour pulp, or the tree or shrub that bears this fruit

Examples

Fresh kumquats fruit
She bought a basket of fresh kumquats from the market to use in her marmalade recipe.
The kumquat has a sweet edible skin and a tart flesh, making it unique among citrus fruits.
You can eat a kumquat whole, without peeling, because the rind is so thin and sweet.
I love the contrast of the sweet skin and tart pulp in a kumquat.
She decorated the cake with slices of fresh kumquat to add a citrus flavor.
The kumquat tree in our garden produced fruit all winter long.
