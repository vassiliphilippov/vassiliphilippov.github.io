conceding

noun
the act of admitting defeat or yielding
6.7
Pronunciation: / kənˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of admitting defeat or yielding to an opposing view, claim, or demand

Examples

Her conceding defeat
After much debate, her conceding signaled the end of the argument.
His conceding of the election was received graciously by his opponent.
The company's conceding to the demands averted a strike.
