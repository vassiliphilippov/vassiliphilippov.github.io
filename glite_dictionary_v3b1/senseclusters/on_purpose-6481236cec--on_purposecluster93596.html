Synonyms

  • intentionally
    3.1 7/m
    intentionally is a formal adverb used in writing to show deliberate action, while on purpose is the everyday spoken phrase.
  • purposely
    3.5 3/m
    purposely is a simple, direct adverb used in speech to show deliberate acts, while on purpose is the casual spoken way.
  • purposefully
    3.9 1/m
    purposefully shows a clear aim and determination in action, while on purpose simply says the act was not accidental.
  • deliberately
    3.7 15/m
    deliberately often suggests careful thought or planning before acting, while on purpose only means the action was not accidental.
  • by design
    4.0 1/m
    by design suggests planning or an intended structure behind something, while on purpose is the general phrase for deliberate action.
  • knowingly
    3.9 4/m
    knowingly focuses on acting with full awareness of facts and consequences, while on purpose focuses on doing something deliberately.
  • wittingly
    7.5 0.2/m
    wittingly means acting with full knowledge, often in formal or legal use, while on purpose is common everyday speech.
  • by choice
    3.1 2/m
    by choice highlights a voluntary personal decision without pressure, while on purpose emphasizes the action was done deliberately not accidentally.
  • advisedly
    7.4 0.1/m

  • designedly
