discretionary trust

noun
trust with trustee discretion over payments
8.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈskreʃ.ən.ər.iː trʌst/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈskreʃ.ən.ər.iː trʌst/ (BrE)
a trust in which the trustee has the authority to decide how much of the trust income, if any, is paid to each beneficiary

Examples

A discretionary trust fund
Many families use a discretionary trust as part of their estate planning to provide flexibility.
The wealthy businessman set up a discretionary trust to support his grandchildren.
Under a discretionary trust, the trustee can decide how much money each beneficiary receives.
