cartridge brass

noun
brass alloy for cartridges and tubing
3.1 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrtɹɪdʒ bræs / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtɹɪdʒ brɑːs / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and zinc, containing about 30% zinc, commonly used for making cartridge cases and tubing

Examples

Cartridge brass alloy
Engineers specified cartridge brass for the tubing due to its excellent machinability.
The ammunition factory produces millions of cartridge brass casings every year.
Unlike other alloys, cartridge brass offers a good balance of strength and flexibility.
