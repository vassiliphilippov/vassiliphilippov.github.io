cutbank

noun
steep excavated slope
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌbæŋk / (BrE)
a steep, eroded bank or slope of earth, especially along a river, road, or railway

Examples

A cutbank slope
The construction team stabilized the cutbank to prevent soil erosion near the railway.
Heavy rain caused part of the cutbank beside the road to collapse.
Wildflowers grew along the edge of the cutbank overlooking the river.
The children were warned not to play near the unstable cutbank after the storm.
Erosion had made the cutbank even steeper over the past year.
Wildflowers grew along the top of the cutbank overlooking the river.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cliff - a steep face of rock and earth
  • synonym: embankment - a raised structure of earth or stone built to hold back water or support a road or railway
  • antonym: point bar - the gently sloping deposit of sediment on the inside bend of a river
  • synonym: riverbank - the land along the edge of a river
