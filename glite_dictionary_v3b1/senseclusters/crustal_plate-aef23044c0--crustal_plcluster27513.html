crustal plate

noun
rigid segment of Earth's crust
6.2 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkrʌs.təl pleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈkrʌs.təl pleɪt/ (BrE)
a rigid, discrete segment of the Earth's crust that moves slowly over the underlying mantle, forming part of the planet's lithosphere

Examples

Crustal plates move slowly
Earthquakes often occur along the boundaries of crustal plates.
Scientists study how each crustal plate interacts with its neighbors.
The Pacific crustal plate is one of the largest on Earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tectonic plate - a segment of the Earth's crust and upper mantle that moves as a unit
