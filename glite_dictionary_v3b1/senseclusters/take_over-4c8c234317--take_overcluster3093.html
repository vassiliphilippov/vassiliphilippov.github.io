take over

verb
assume another's expenses or debts
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈoʊvər /; / teɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to assume responsibility for the debts, payments, or obligations of another person or entity

Examples

To take over debts
The government had to take over the hospital's outstanding loans to keep it running.
The new company will take over the debts of the smaller firm.
When her father retired, she agreed to take over his financial obligations.
After the merger, she agreed to take over the lease payments for the office.
Banks sometimes take over loans when smaller lenders go out of business.
The new company will take over all outstanding debts from its predecessor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assume - to take on a responsibility or duty
  • synonym: inherit - to receive money, property, or debts from someone after they die
  • antonym: relinquish - to give up responsibility or power
