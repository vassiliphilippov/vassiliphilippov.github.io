look forward

verb
anticipate with pleasure or excitement
1.4 35/m
Pronunciation: / l äk Ààf…îrw…öd / (AmE), / l äk Ààf…îÀêw…ôd / (BrE)
to anticipate something with pleasure or excitement; to expect or hope for something eagerly

Examples

To look forward to holidays
Children often look forward to their birthdays with great excitement.
I always look forward to spending the holidays with my family.
She said she would look forward to our next meeting.

Synonyms

  • look forward to
    2.4 35/m
    look forward to is used with a thing or event you are eager about, while look forward names that feeling generally.
  • anticipate
    3.6 8.30/m
    anticipate is often used when you expect something with eagerness or worry, while look forward stresses happy, eager hope.
  • await
    3.2 26/m
    await is a formal verb often used in writing about things you wait for, while look forward is casual and shows pleasure.
  • expect
    1.0 267/m
    expect means to think something will happen, usually without emotion, while look forward adds pleasure and eager hope.
  • look for
    1.0 2.14/m
    look for usually means to search for something in the present, while look forward means to await the future with pleasure.
