bloating

noun
excessive expansion
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbloʊ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbləʊ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
an excessive increase in size or complexity, especially in software or systems, often leading to reduced performance

Examples

Software bloating
The software suffered from bloating after too many unnecessary features were added.
Document bloating can make files difficult to open and slow to process.
Regular updates are needed to prevent bloating in large computer systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloat - unnecessary enlargement or complexity, especially in software
  • antonym: streamlining - making something simpler or more efficient
