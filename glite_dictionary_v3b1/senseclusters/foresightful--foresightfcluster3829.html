foresightful

adjective
able to plan for the future
8.1 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îrÀåsa…™tf…ôl / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀê.sa…™t.f äl / (BrE)
Having or showing the ability to anticipate and plan for future needs or events.

Examples

A foresightful plan
His foresightful decisions saved the company from financial trouble.
She is a foresightful manager who always considers potential risks.
They praised his foresightful approach to environmental planning.

Synonyms

  • foresighted
    6.5 0.04/m
    foresighted describes someone who plans carefully ahead in formal contexts, while foresightful sounds more general and less formal.
  • farsighted
    4.6 0.28/m
    farsighted means planning with long-term goals in mind for people or groups, while foresightful focuses on practical near-future needs.
  • longsighted
    6.7 0.01/m
    longsighted suggests thinking ahead over many years in strategy or policy, while foresightful describes careful planning for upcoming needs.
  • farseeing
    7.5 0.00/m
    farseeing often sounds literary and shows wide future vision, while foresightful is more direct about practical planning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farsighted - able to judge what will happen in the future and plan accordingly
  • antonym: shortsighted - lacking consideration for future consequences
