weak-kneed

adjective
lacking determination or willpower
7.1 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / wik nid / (AmE), / wiÀêk niÀêd / (BrE)
Lacking determination, courage, or willpower; easily discouraged or lacking resolve.

Examples

Weak-kneed leader
Only the brave, not the weak-kneed, volunteer for rescue missions in dangerous conditions.
She is too weak-kneed to stand up to her boss when it matters most.
The manager was criticized for being weak-kneed in the face of tough negotiations.

Synonyms

  • fainthearted
    7.7 0.1/m
    fainthearted is easily scared and avoids risks, while weak-kneed means lacking determination and courage when facing pressure.
  • gutless
    6.0 0.7/m
    gutless calls someone a coward in blunt terms, while weak-kneed means lacking resolve or will to act under stress.
  • wimpy
    5.7 1/m
    wimpy sounds informal and childish and shows low strength or courage, while weak-kneed describes lack of determination in hard situations.
  • wishy-washy
    7.3 0.6/m
    wishy-washy describes someone who cannot decide or take a firm stand, while weak-kneed focuses on lack of courage or resolve.
  • faint
    6.1 0.9/m
    faint describes a timid, shaky reaction or voice, while weak-kneed describes a deeper lack of courage and inner resolve in action.
  • namby-pamby
    9.9 0.05/m
    namby-pamby sounds old-fashioned and slightly mocking and means weak or ineffectual, while weak-kneed is a plain term for lacking courage.
