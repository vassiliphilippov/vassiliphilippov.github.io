grayout

noun
temporary vision dimming
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary dimming or partial loss of vision, often caused by reduced blood flow to the brain

Examples

A pilot's grayout
The pilot reported a brief grayout while pulling out of a steep dive.
Some people may experience a grayout if they stand up too quickly after sitting for a long time.
During intense physical exertion, a sudden grayout can be a warning sign to stop and rest.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blackout - a complete loss of consciousness or vision
  • synonym: dizziness - a feeling of lightheadedness or unsteadiness
  • degree higher: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time
