Fourteenth Amendment

proper noun
U.S. constitutional amendment on rights and equality
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈtinθ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
an amendment to the United States Constitution adopted in 1868, granting citizenship and equal protection under the law to all persons born or naturalized in the U.S., and extending the guarantees of the Bill of Rights to the states

Examples

Fourteenth Amendment rights
Many landmark Supreme Court decisions are based on the Fourteenth Amendment.
The Fourteenth Amendment guarantees equal protection of the laws to all citizens.
The rights protected by the Fourteenth Amendment have had a profound impact on American history.
