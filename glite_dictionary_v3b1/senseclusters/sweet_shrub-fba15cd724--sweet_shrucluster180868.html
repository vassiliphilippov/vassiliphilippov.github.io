sweet shrub

noun
fragrant southeastern US shrub
Pronunciation: / swit ʃrʌb / (AmE), / swiːt ʃrʌb / (BrE)
a hardy shrub native to the southeastern United States, known for its clove-scented wood and fragrant reddish-brown flowers

Examples

A sweet shrub flower
A sweet shrub in full bloom filled the garden with a spicy fragrance.
The sweet shrub is prized by gardeners for its unique scent and attractive blooms.
They planted a sweet shrub near the porch to enjoy its aromatic flowers every spring.
