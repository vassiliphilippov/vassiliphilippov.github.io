visual cortex

noun
part of the brain that processes vision
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəl ˈkɔrtɛks / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex that processes visual information received from the eyes via the thalamus

Examples

The visual cortex area
Damage to the visual cortex can lead to partial or complete loss of vision.
Researchers studied how the visual cortex responds to moving images.
The visual cortex is located in the occipital lobe at the back of the brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occipital lobe - the region of the brain containing the visual cortex, located at the back of the head
