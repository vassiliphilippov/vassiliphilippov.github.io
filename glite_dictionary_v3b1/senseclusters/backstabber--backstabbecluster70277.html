backstabber

noun
person who betrays
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstæbər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstæbə / (BrE)
a person who betrays someone who trusts them, especially by acting friendly or secretly working against them

Examples

Office backstabber
After learning the truth, she realized her colleague was a backstabber who had spread rumors behind her back.
No one wanted to trust a known backstabber with important information in the office.
He was hurt to discover that his longtime friend was actually a backstabber all along.
He realized too late that his closest friend was actually a backstabber.
Office politics can be tough when you have to deal with a backstabber on your team.
She warned me about trusting him, saying he had a reputation as a backstabber.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betrayer - someone who is disloyal or betrays trust
  • antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to someone or something
  • synonym: traitor - a person who betrays a friend, country, or principle
