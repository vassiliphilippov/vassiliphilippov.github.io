sacralization

noun
fusion of lumbar vertebra
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌseɪkrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsakrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a condition in which a lumbar vertebra, usually the fifth, is fused to the sacrum, either from birth or acquired later

Examples

Sacralization of vertebra
The X-ray revealed sacralization of the fifth lumbar vertebra, explaining the patient's lower back pain.
Congenital sacralization can sometimes go unnoticed until adulthood when symptoms develop.
Physical therapy was recommended to help manage discomfort caused by sacralization in her lower spine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lumbarization - a condition where the first sacral vertebra is not fused and acts as a lumbar vertebra
