polyimide

noun
heat-resistant polymer
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈɪmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈɪmaɪd / (BrE)
a strong, heat-resistant synthetic polymer used in electronics and industry, known for its high thermal and chemical stability

Examples

Strong polyimide material
The engineer selected polyimide for its excellent thermal stability in the new device.
Flexible circuits often use polyimide films as an insulating layer.
Because of its durability, polyimide is common in aerospace and electronics industries.
A polyimide coating is often used to protect wires in high-temperature environments.
Engineers selected polyimide films for the spacecraft due to their durability and resistance to heat.
Flexible printed circuits are frequently made from polyimide because of its excellent electrical properties.
