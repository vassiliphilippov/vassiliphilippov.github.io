slog

verb
work hard and steadily
3.3
Pronunciation: / slɑg / (AmE), / slɒg / (BrE)
to work hard and steadily over a long period, especially at something difficult or exhausting

Examples

To slog through work
After hours of slogging through paperwork, she finally finished the report.
Despite feeling tired, the team slogged on until the job was done.
He had to slog through deep snow to reach the cabin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grind - to work hard, especially at something monotonous or tedious
  • antonym: rest - to relax or stop working after effort
  • synonym: toil - to work hard and continuously
