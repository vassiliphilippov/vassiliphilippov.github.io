harmfully

adverb
in a way that causes harm
4.3 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërmf…ôli / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêmf…ôli / (BrE)
In a way that causes harm; in a detrimental manner.

Examples

To harmfully affect
She realized her words had harmfully impacted his confidence.
The chemicals can affect the environment harmfully if not disposed of properly.
Using the medicine incorrectly may interact harmfully with other treatments.

Synonyms

  • detrimentally
    7.2 0.08/m
    detrimentally appears in formal or technical contexts to signal a harmful effect, while harmfully is more general and everyday wording.
  • deleteriously
    9.0 0.05/m
    deleteriously is used in science or medicine to show gradual harm, while harmfully is a broader everyday way to say harm.
  • injuriously
    7.6 0.02/m
    injuriously sounds formal or legal and means causing injury, while harmfully is common and covers general harmful actions or effects.
  • damagingly
    4.4 0.01/m
    damagingly describes visible damage or bad effects, often in speech, while harmfully covers any way that causes harm.
  • adversely
    4.9 3.61/m
    adversely is used to say something has a negative effect, often on results or health, while harmfully is more general.
  • noxiously
    9.0 0.00/m
    noxiously often means poisonous, foul, or harmful to health, while harmfully is broader and less about smell or poison.
  • destructively
    4.2 0.24/m
    destructively suggests force or total ruin and strong physical damage, while harmfully can mean any harmful or less strong effect.
