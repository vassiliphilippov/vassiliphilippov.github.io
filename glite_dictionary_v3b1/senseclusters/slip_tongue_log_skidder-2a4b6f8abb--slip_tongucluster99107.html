slip tongue log skidder

noun
logging device with tongs to lift logs
9.9
Pronunciation: /slɪp tʌŋ lɔːg ˈskɪd.ər/ (AmE), /slɪp tʌŋ lɒg ˈskɪd.ə/ (BrE)
A type of skidder used in logging, with dangling tongs between the wheels to lift one end of a log off the ground, especially in the 19th and early 20th centuries.
