scrutinize

verb
examine closely and carefully
Pronunciation: / ˈskruːtəˌnaɪz /; / ˈskruːtɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskruːtənaɪz / (BrE)
to examine something very carefully and thoroughly, especially to find information, check for mistakes, or verify accuracy

Examples

To scrutinize documents carefully
After the accident, investigators scrutinized the train's safety record for possible failures.
She scrutinized the document, searching for any discrepancies in the details.
The teacher will scrutinize every essay for plagiarism before grading.
Every detail was scrutinized during the inspection to ensure quality standards were met.
She scrutinized the fine print of the contract for hidden fees.
The committee will scrutinize the new proposal before making a decision.
Her supervisor scrutinised her work to ensure everything was in order.
Investors carefully scrutinise financial statements before making decisions.
The detective scrutinised every detail of the crime scene for clues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: examine - to look at something closely and carefully
  • antonym: ignore - to intentionally not pay attention to something
  • synonym: inspect - to look at something closely to assess its condition or quality
  • antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or examine
  • synonym: scan - to look at all parts of something carefully in order to detect some feature
  • synonym: scrutinize - the US spelling of 'scrutinise'
