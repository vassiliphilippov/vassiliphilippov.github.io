spray

noun
a burst of small objects or drops in the air
1.0
Pronunciation: / spreɪ / (AmE), / spreɪ / (BrE)
a collection or burst of small drops or particles in the air, such as water, steam, or mist

Examples

A spray of water

The attacker fired a spray of bullets across the courtyard.
The children ran through the spray of foam at the parade.
A spray of water droplets drifted across the garden in the wind.
As the window shattered, a spray of glass filled the air.
The robber fired a spray of bullets toward the guards.
The boat cut through the waves, sending up a fine spray into the air.
The ocean spray cooled our faces as we walked along the beach.
Waterfall spray drifted through the valley, making everything damp and lush.
