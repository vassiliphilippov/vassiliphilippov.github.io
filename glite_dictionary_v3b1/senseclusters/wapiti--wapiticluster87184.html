wapiti

noun
large species of deer, also called elk
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɑːˈpiːti /; / wɑˈpiti / (AmE), / wəˈpiːti / (BrE)
a large species of deer native to North America (also called elk), with large antlers and a light rump patch

Examples

The wapiti deer
Native American folklore often features the wapiti as a symbol of strength.
The park ranger explained the difference between a wapiti and a red deer.
We spotted a majestic wapiti grazing in the early morning light.
A herd of wapiti grazed peacefully in the mountain clearing.
Hikers caught a glimpse of a wapiti crossing the forest stream.
The male wapiti are known for their impressive antlers which are shed and regrown annually.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elk - another name for the wapiti in North America
  • degree lower: red deer - a closely related deer species found in Europe and parts of Asia
