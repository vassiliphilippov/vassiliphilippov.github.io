headhunting

noun
taking enemy heads
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌhʌntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌhʌntɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of taking and preserving the heads of enemies as trophies, often in warfare or ritual contexts

Examples

Tribal headhunting
Some ancient tribes practiced headhunting as a way to display their power over enemies.
The museum displayed artifacts related to headhunting from different cultures around the world.
Stories of headhunting have fascinated historians and anthropologists for decades.
