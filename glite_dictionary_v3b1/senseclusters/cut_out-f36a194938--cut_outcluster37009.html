cut out

verb
form by cutting material away
1.2 3.2
Pronunciation: / kʌt aʊt / (AmE), / kʌt aʊt / (BrE)
to remove, delete, or form something by cutting, especially from a larger piece or from text

Examples

To cut out a picture
He cut out a photograph to use in the collage.
She carefully cut out a star from the colored paper.
The children cut out animal shapes for their art project.
She cut out the recipe from the magazine to save for later.
The editor asked me to cut out several paragraphs from the article.
You should cut out any unnecessary details when writing your report.
He cut out my name from the list after I withdrew my application.
Please cut out any incorrect information before submitting the report.
She decided to cut out the last paragraph from her essay.

  • synonym: remove - to take something away or eliminate it
  • synonym: scratch out - cancel by drawing a line through something
  • synonym: sculpt - to create or represent something by carving, modeling, or shaping
