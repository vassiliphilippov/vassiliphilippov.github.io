bumpy

adjective
having an uneven or rough surface or movement
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌm.piː/ /; / /ˈbʌm.pɪ/ (AmE), /ˈbʌm.pɪ/ /; / /ˈbʌm.piː/ (BrE)
having a surface or movement that is not smooth, with many bumps or jolts

Examples

A bumpy flight
He complained about the bumpy flight, saying the turbulence made him nervous.
She avoided the bumpy path and walked along the smoother trail instead.
The car ride was so bumpy that we nearly spilled our drinks.
After the earthquake, the once smooth path became extremely bumpy and difficult to walk on.
She tried to write a letter on the bumpy bus journey, but it was hard to concentrate.
The car ride was very bumpy because of the old, uneven road.
