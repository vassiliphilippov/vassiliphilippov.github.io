ex nihilo

adverb
from nothing
Pronunciation: / ɛks ˈniːhɪloʊ / (AmE), / ɛks ˈniːhɪləʊ / (BrE)
from or out of nothing; in a way that arises without any obvious cause or prior existence

Examples

Appeared ex nihilo
The philosopher argued that the universe was created ex nihilo by a divine being.
Some artists prefer to build their work ex nihilo rather than using existing materials.
The concept of creation ex nihilo is central to many religious beliefs.
The artist seemed to create masterpieces ex nihilo, astonishing everyone with her originality.
A new theory appeared ex nihilo and quickly gained attention in the scientific community.
Their friendship developed ex nihilo, with no shared history or connections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: from scratch - starting from nothing or no prior preparation
  • synonym: out of nowhere - appearing suddenly and unexpectedly
