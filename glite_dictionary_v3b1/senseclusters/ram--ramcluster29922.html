ram

verb
force into state or action
2.3 4.2
Pronunciation: /ræm/ (AmE), /ræm/ (BrE)
to force something or someone into a particular place, state, or action, either physically or metaphorically

Examples

To ram an idea
He rammed his ideas into the conversation, ignoring everyone else's opinion.
She tried to ram the importance of honesty into her children.
The coach rammed discipline into the team before the match.
