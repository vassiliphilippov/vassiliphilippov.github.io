deselection

noun
removal from a list
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːsɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːsɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing someone or something from a list of candidates, options, or positions, especially in politics or organizations

Examples

Party deselection
The committee's deselection of the candidate surprised many party members.
After the deselection process, only three applicants remained for the final interview.
Controversy arose following the deselection of a long-serving council member.

Related Terms

  • synonym: removal - the act of taking something or someone away
  • antonym: selection - the act of choosing someone or something from a group
