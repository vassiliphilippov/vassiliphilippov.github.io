grab

verb
capture attention or imagination
3.9 1.5
Pronunciation: /græb/ (AmE), /græb/ (BrE)
to capture someone's attention or imagination forcefully and suddenly

Examples

To grab attention
That headline is designed to grab the reader and make them click the article.
The bold colors immediately grab your attention as you walk into the room.
The novel's opening chapter grabbed me and made me eager to read more.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engage - to capture and hold the attention or interest; engross
  • antonym: lose - to fail to keep hold of attention; to no longer have someone's interest
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly; also used figuratively for attention
