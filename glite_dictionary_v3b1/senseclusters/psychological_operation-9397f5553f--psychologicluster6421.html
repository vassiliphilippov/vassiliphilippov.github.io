psychological operation

noun
military action to influence perceptions or behavior
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪ.kəˌlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.kəˌlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl ˌɒ.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a military action designed to influence the perceptions, emotions, and behavior of individuals, groups, or governments, typically as part of information warfare or propaganda efforts

Examples

A psychological operation campaign
Rumors spread through the city were later revealed to be part of a psychological operation.
The army launched a psychological operation to weaken the enemy's morale before the main offensive.
The success of the psychological operation depended on convincing the local population to mistrust their leaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propaganda - information, especially biased or misleading, used to promote a political cause or point of view
