BCE

adverb
before the Common Era (dating system)
4.4 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåbiÀê.siÀêÀàiÀê / (AmE), / ÀåbiÀê.siÀêÀàiÀê / (BrE)
used to identify years occurring before the start of the Common Era (equivalent to BC, but more religiously neutral). Appears after the year number, e.g. 400 BCE.

Examples

Year 400 BCE
Aristotle was born in 384 BCE.
The Great Wall of China was first built in the 7th century BCE.
The Rosetta Stone was inscribed in 196 BCE.

Synonyms

  • B.C.E.
    5.2 0.00/m
    B.C.E. is a punctuated form used in formal writing, while BCE is the common non-punctuated secular abbreviation.
  • BC
    2.9 0.00/m
    BC is the traditional religious label for years before Christ, while BCE is the neutral, modern choice in many texts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: BC - before Christ (traditional dating abbreviation equivalent to BCE)
  • antonym: CE - Common Era (dating abbreviation for years after the beginning of the Common Era)
