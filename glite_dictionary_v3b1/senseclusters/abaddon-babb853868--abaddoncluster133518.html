Abaddon

noun
place of destruction
Pronunciation: / əˈbædən / (AmE), / əˈbædən / (BrE)
a place of destruction or the realm of the dead, often used to mean ruin or annihilation in literature and religious texts

Examples

Pit of Abaddon
In the ancient text, the souls of the wicked were cast into Abaddon for eternity.
The poet described the battlefield as a vision of Abaddon, filled with smoke and ruin.
Some believe that Abaddon represents the ultimate end, where nothing survives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hell - a place regarded in some religions as a realm of punishment after death
  • antonym: paradise - a place or state of perfect happiness, often considered the opposite of hell
