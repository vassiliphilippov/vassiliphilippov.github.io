Hampton Roads

proper noun
naval battle site in the American Civil War (1862)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmptən ˈroʊdz / (AmE), / ˈhæmptən ˈrəʊdz / (BrE)
the site of a notable naval battle during the American Civil War in 1862, known for the clash between the ironclad warships Monitor and Merrimac, which ended indecisively.

Examples

Hampton Roads naval battle
Many historians study Hampton Roads to understand naval strategy in the Civil War.
The Battle of Hampton Roads marked a turning point in naval warfare with the use of ironclad ships.
Tourists visiting Virginia often explore the site where Hampton Roads took place.
