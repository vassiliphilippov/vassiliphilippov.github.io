upthrust

noun
geological elevation rise
8.6 0.02/m

(Geology) A rise of land to a higher elevation, especially as a result of tectonic forces in the process of mountain building.

Examples

Tectonic upthrust
Ancient coral reefs can be found atop mountains created by tectonic upthrust.
Geologists studied the recent upthrust along the fault line to understand seismic activity.
The Himalayas were formed by the upthrust of the Earth's crust millions of years ago.

Synonyms

  • uplift
    7.0 0.41/m
    uplift is a broad geological rise of land over large areas, while upthrust is a localized tectonic lift tied to mountain building.
  • upthrow
    9.9 0.00/m
    upthrow is a fault-related upward movement of one side of rock, while upthrust is a general tectonic uplift in mountains.
  • upheaval
    7.3 0.22/m
    upheaval is a strong, often sudden rise of the earth's crust that reshapes land, while upthrust is specific to mountain-building uplift.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uplift - the process of land being raised to a higher elevation
