upthrust

noun
geological elevation rise
6.6 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌθrʌst / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌθrʌst / (BrE)
(Geology) A rise of land to a higher elevation, especially as a result of tectonic forces in the process of mountain building.

Examples

Tectonic upthrust
Ancient coral reefs can be found atop mountains created by tectonic upthrust.
Geologists studied the recent upthrust along the fault line to understand seismic activity.
The Himalayas were formed by the upthrust of the Earth's crust millions of years ago.

Related Terms
