thyroxine

noun
hormone regulating metabolism
4.4
Pronunciation: / θaɪˈrɑːksin / (AmE), / θaɪˈrɒksiːn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that regulates metabolism by controlling the rate of oxidation in cells

Examples

Thyroxine hormone
A deficiency in thyroxine can cause symptoms of hypothyroidism, such as fatigue and weight gain.
Synthetic thyroxine is often prescribed to patients after thyroid surgery.
The doctor increased her thyroxine dosage to help regulate her metabolism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: triiodothyronine - a related thyroid hormone with similar metabolic effects
