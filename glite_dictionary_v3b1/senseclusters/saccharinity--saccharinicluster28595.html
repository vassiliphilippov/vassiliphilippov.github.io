saccharinity

noun
excessive sweetness
6.3 8.3
Pronunciation: /sæk.ə.ˈrɪn.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /sæk.ə.ˈrɪn.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /sæk.ə.ˈrɪn.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /sæk.ə.ˈrɪn.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
The quality or state of being excessively sweet, especially in relation to saccharin or overly sentimental language or behavior.

Examples

Excessive saccharinity
His poetry was marred by a certain saccharinity that critics found cloying.
Some films suffer from saccharinity, making their messages seem insincere.
The dessert crossed the line into saccharinity with its overuse of artificial sweetener.
