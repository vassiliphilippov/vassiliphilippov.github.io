Al-Jama'a al-Islamiyyah al-Muqatilah bi-Libya

noun
Libyan Islamist militant group (founded 1995)
Pronunciation: / æl dʒæˈmɑː.ə æl ɪsˈlɑː.mij.jə æl muːˈkɑː.tiː.lə biː ˈlɪb.jə / (AmE), / æl dʒæˈmɑː.ə æl ɪsˈlɑː.mɪ.jə æl mjuːˈkɑː.tɪ.lə biː ˈlɪb.jə / (BrE)
a Libyan Islamist militant group founded in 1995, aligned with al-Qaeda, which sought to overthrow the Libyan government and attempted to assassinate Muammar Qaddafi.

Examples

Al-Jama'a al-Islamiyyah al-Muqatilah bi-Libya militant group
Intelligence agencies have monitored Al-Jama'a al-Islamiyyah al-Muqatilah bi-Libya due to its alignment with al-Qaeda.
Reports indicate that Al-Jama'a al-Islamiyyah al-Muqatilah bi-Libya was involved in attempts to overthrow the Libyan regime.
The United Nations imposed sanctions on Al-Jama'a al-Islamiyyah al-Muqatilah bi-Libya for its links to terrorist activities.
