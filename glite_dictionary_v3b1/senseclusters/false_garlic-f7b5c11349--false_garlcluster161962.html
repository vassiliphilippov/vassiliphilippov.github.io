false garlic

noun
wild plant resembling garlic
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
a wild plant resembling garlic, known for its pungent odor, but not a true garlic; often refers to species such as Nothoscordum bivalve or Allium species in the Old World.

Examples

False garlic plant
Gardeners sometimes mistake false garlic for wild onion due to their similar appearance.
In the meadow, clusters of false garlic gave off a sharp, onion-like scent.
Though called false garlic, this plant is not closely related to true garlic nor is it used in cooking.
