sermonic

adjective
like a sermon
Pronunciation: / sərˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / səˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a sermon or preaching, often in a moralizing or instructive way

Examples

Sermonic speech
The professor's sermonic lectures often included lessons about ethics and responsibility.
Her sermonic style made the audience feel like they were in church.
He adopted a sermonic tone when discussing the importance of honesty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: didactic - intended to teach, especially in a moralizing way
  • synonym: preachy - trying to give moral advice in an annoying way
