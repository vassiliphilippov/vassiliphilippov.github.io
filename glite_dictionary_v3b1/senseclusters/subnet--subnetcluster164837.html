subnet

noun
net from another net
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌnɛt / (BrE)
a net formed from another net by reindexing, used in topology as a generalization of a subsequence

Examples

Topology subnet
In topology, every convergent net has a convergent subnet with the same limit.
The proof relies on constructing a subnet that preserves the convergence properties of the original net.
Mathematicians often use a subnet to generalize results about sequences to more abstract spaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: net - a generalization of sequences in topology
  • synonym: subsequence - a sequence derived by selecting elements from another sequence in order
