secretomotor

adjective
stimulating gland secretion
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˌkritoʊˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / sɪˌkriːtəʊˈməʊtə / (BrE)
causing or relating to the stimulation of glandular secretion

Examples

Secretomotor nerves activate glands
The secretomotor fibers of the nerve stimulate the salivary glands to produce saliva.
Damage to secretomotor nerves can reduce the amount of mucus secreted in the respiratory tract.
Doctors studied the secretomotor response of the glands during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excitatory - causing stimulation or increased activity
  • antonym: inhibitory - reducing or preventing activity
