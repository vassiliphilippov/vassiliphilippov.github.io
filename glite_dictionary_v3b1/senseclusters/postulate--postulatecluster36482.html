postulate

noun
proposition assumed true for reasoning
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
A proposition that is assumed to be true without proof and is used as a basis for reasoning, argument, or calculation.

Examples

A mathematical postulate
Before proceeding, the mathematician stated an important postulate that underpins the argument.
Euclid's fifth postulate is central to the development of classical geometry.
The entire theory is built upon a single core postulate accepted without proof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axiom - a statement or principle that is accepted as true without proof
  • degree lower: hypothesis - a supposition made as a basis for reasoning
