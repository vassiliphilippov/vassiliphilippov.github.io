Phoebus

proper noun
another name for Apollo, Greek god of light
9.9 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfiÀêb…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàfiÀêb…ôs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) another name for Apollo, the god of light, prophecy, poetry, music, and healing; son of Zeus and Leto and twin brother of Artemis.

Examples

Phoebus Apollo god
Artists have depicted Phoebus with a radiant halo, symbolizing the sun.
In ancient poetry, Apollo was often called Phoebus to emphasize his role as the god of light.
The prophecy came from Phoebus at Delphi, where his oracle was renowned across Greece.
