North Germanic

noun
northern branch of the Germanic languages
8.2 6.2
Pronunciation: /nɔːrθ dʒər.ˈmæn.ɪk/ /; / /noʊrθ dʒər.ˈmæn.ɪk/ (AmE), /nɔːθ dʒə.ˈmæn.ɪk/ /; / /nɔːθ dʒɜː.ˈmæn.ɪk/ (BrE)
The branch of the Germanic language family that comprises the languages spoken in Scandinavia and Iceland, such as Danish, Swedish, Norwegian, Icelandic, and Faroese.

Examples

North Germanic languages
Danish, Swedish, and Norwegian all belong to the North Germanic group.
Many linguists specialize in the study of North Germanic languages such as Swedish and Icelandic.
The North Germanic family is distinct from West Germanic languages like English and German.
