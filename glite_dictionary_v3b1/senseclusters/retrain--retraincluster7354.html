retrain

verb
to train again or learn new skills
4.2 3.1
Pronunciation: / riˈtreɪn /; / ˌriˈtreɪn / (AmE), / riːˈtreɪn /; / ˌriːˈtreɪn / (BrE)
to learn new skills or be taught new skills, especially to change jobs or adapt to new situations

Examples

Many workers have to retrain to keep up with changing technology.
After losing her job, Maria decided to retrain as a computer programmer.
The company offered to retrain staff so they could work with new technologies.
Workers may need to retrain several times during their careers as industries change.
