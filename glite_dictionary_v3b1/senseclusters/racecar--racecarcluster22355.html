racecar

noun
car for racing
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪsˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈreɪsˌkɑː / (BrE)
a car specially designed and built for racing at high speeds on tracks or circuits

Examples

A racecar on track
The racecar accelerated quickly as it approached the first turn of the track.
He dreamed of driving a professional racecar in the Grand Prix one day.
Engineers spent months perfecting the aerodynamics of the new racecar model.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sedan - a standard passenger car with a separate trunk
  • synonym: sports car - a fast car designed for performance and handling
