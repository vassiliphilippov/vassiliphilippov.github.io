tetrafluoroethylene

noun
a gaseous chemical used for making plastics
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəfluˌɔroʊˈɛθəlin / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəfluːərəʊˈiːθəliːn / (BrE)
a flammable gaseous fluorocarbon (C2F4) used in the manufacture of plastics such as polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) resins

Examples

Tetrafluoroethylene gas
Tetrafluoroethylene is the key monomer used in the production of Teflon and other fluoropolymer plastics.
Manufacturers handle tetrafluoroethylene carefully because it is highly flammable and can pose safety risks.
The chemical industry uses tetrafluoroethylene to create coatings that are resistant to heat and chemicals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polytetrafluoroethylene - a synthetic resin made from tetrafluoroethylene, known for its non-stick properties
