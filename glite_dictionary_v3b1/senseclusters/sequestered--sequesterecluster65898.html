sequestered

adjective

(in climate science and environmental policy) captured and stored so that it is prevented from entering the atmosphere, especially referring to carbon or carbon dioxide.

Examples

Sequestered carbon storage
The policy focuses on increasing sequestered carbon to reduce atmospheric CO2 levels.
During sequestration tests, scientists measured how much sequestered CO2 remained after ten years.
Restoring wetlands increased the amount of sequestered carbon stored in the soil.
