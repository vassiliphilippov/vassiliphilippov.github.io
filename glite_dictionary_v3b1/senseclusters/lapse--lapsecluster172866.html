lapse

verb
to fall to a lower level (morals/standards)
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
to fall or slip back into a previous or less desirable state or behavior

Examples

To lapse into bad habits
After years of sobriety, he began to lapse into old habits.
Even the most disciplined people can occasionally lapse in their standards.
She vowed never to lapse again in her commitment to honesty.
After years of sobriety, he lapsed back into drinking.
Despite months of good behavior, the young man eventually lapsed into trouble with the law again.
When stressed, she sometimes lapses into old eating habits.
As the evening wore on, their conversation lapsed into awkwardness.
He lapsed into unconsciousness before the ambulance arrived.
She lapsed into a deep silence after hearing the news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descend - to move or fall to a lower or less desirable state
  • synonym: relapse - to return to a former, typically worse, state
  • synonym: sink - to gradually go into a lower state or condition
