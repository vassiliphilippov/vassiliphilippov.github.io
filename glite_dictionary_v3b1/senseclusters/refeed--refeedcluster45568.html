refeed

noun
diet calorie increase
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.fiːd/ /; / /ˈrɪ.fiːd/ (AmE), /ˈriː.fiːd/ /; / /ˈrɪ.fiːd/ (BrE)
an act or period of supplying food or material again, especially after a break or during a diet

Examples

A planned refeed
After several days of strict dieting, she scheduled a refeed to help maintain her energy levels.
Many athletes include a weekly refeed to support their training and recovery.
A planned refeed can prevent the body from adapting to a low-calorie diet.
After several hours without food, the athletes had a quick refeed to restore their energy.
The technician performed a refeed of the printer paper when it ran out during the job.
Some diets include a planned refeed day to increase calorie intake temporarily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat day - a day when someone temporarily breaks their diet rules
  • antonym: fasting - the practice of abstaining from food for a period
  • synonym: feeding - the act of giving food
