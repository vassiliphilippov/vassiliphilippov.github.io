invade

verb
enter or intrude without permission
4.1 3.99/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆveÉªd / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆveÉªd / (BrE)
to enter or encroach upon a place, situation, or someone's rights or privacy, often without permission and in an intrusive or unwelcome way

Examples

To invade privacy
I don't like it when someone invades my personal space on the train.
New technologies often invade our privacy in unexpected ways.
She felt her rights were being invaded by the constant surveillance.

Synonyms

  • intrude
    4.4 5.41/m
    intrude means entering a place or conversation without permission or welcome, while invade suggests a larger, forceful, or aggressive entry.
  • encroach
    7.3 2.34/m
    encroach is gradually moving into land, space, or rights without clear permission, while invade often implies sudden, forceful, wide entry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encroach - to gradually or stealthily take possession of something not one's own, especially territory or rights
  • synonym: intrude - to put oneself deliberately into a place or situation where one is unwelcome or uninvited
  • antonym: withdraw - to leave a place or cease to be involved, especially as the opposite of invading or intruding
