Maianthemum bifolium

noun
a small, white-flowered woodland plant
Pronunciation: / maɪˈænθəməm baɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / maɪˈænθɪməm bɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
A small, white-flowered woodland plant native to western Europe and eastward to Japan, also known as false lily of the valley.

Examples

The Maianthemum bifolium plant
Botanists recorded clusters of Maianthemum bifolium in the ancient woodlands near the river.
During her hike, she spotted the delicate Maianthemum bifolium blooming along the shaded forest floor.
The Maianthemum bifolium is often called false lily of the valley due to its similar appearance.
