pour out

verb
gradually pour to leave sediment behind
4.9 2.5
Pronunciation: /pɔːr aʊt/ (AmE), /pɔː aʊt/ (BrE)
to flow or cause to flow out in a large quantity or continuously, especially of a liquid

Examples

To pour out water
She poured out the solution into a new container to separate it from the precipitate at the bottom.
The waiter carefully poured out the aged red wine, making sure no sediment entered the glass.
When serving old port, it's important to pour out slowly to keep the sediment undisturbed.
After the storm, muddy water would pour out from the hillside.
As the bottle tipped, water began to pour out onto the table.
He watched the crowd pour out of the stadium after the game ended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decant - to pour liquid from one container into another, especially to separate sediment
  • degree lower: dribble - flow or let flow in small drops
  • synonym: flow - move along steadily and continuously
