flinch

verb
draw back quickly, as from pain or fear
3.3
Pronunciation: / flɪntʃ / (AmE), / flɪntʃ / (BrE)
to make a quick, involuntary movement or draw back slightly, usually in response to pain, fear, or surprise

Examples

To flinch in pain
He tried not to flinch as the nurse gave him the injection.
She couldn't help but flinch at the loud bang from the fireworks.
Whenever the dentist approached with the drill, he would flinch slightly in the chair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cringe - to react with discomfort or fear by shrinking or recoiling
  • synonym: recoil - to suddenly spring or draw back, especially in fear or disgust
