Mercedarian

noun
religious order member
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɜrsɪˈdɛriən / (AmE), / ˌmɜːsɪˈdɛəriən / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic religious order founded to rescue Christian captives from slavery

Examples

A Mercedarian monk
The Mercedarian dedicated his life to freeing captives during the Middle Ages.
Several Mercedarians established missions in the New World to help the oppressed.
A Mercedarian wore a distinctive white habit as a symbol of his order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: friar - a member of a religious order, especially in the Catholic Church
  • synonym: monk - a man who is a member of a religious community living under vows
