chemosensitivity

noun
detection of chemicals
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˌsɛnsəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a test, instrument, or method to detect or respond to chemical substances, especially at low concentrations

Examples

Chemosensitivity of a sensor
The chemosensitivity of the new sensor allows it to detect even the smallest traces of toxins.
Researchers are improving chemosensitivity in medical tests to identify diseases earlier.
High chemosensitivity is essential for instruments used in environmental monitoring.

  • synonym: sensitivity - the ability to detect or respond to small changes or signals
