pantheon

noun
monument for a nation's dead heroes
Pronunciation: / ˈpænθiˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈpanθɪən / (BrE)
A monument commemorating a nation's most celebrated dead heroes, often serving as their burial place or a site of honor.

Examples

French Pantheon monument
Many heroes of the French Revolution are buried in the Pantheon in Paris.
The country built a grand pantheon to honor its fallen soldiers.
Visiting the national pantheon inspired a sense of pride and remembrance.
