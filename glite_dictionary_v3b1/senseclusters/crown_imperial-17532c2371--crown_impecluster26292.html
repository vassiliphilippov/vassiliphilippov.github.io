crown imperial

noun
a Eurasian plant with orange-red bell flowers
4.8
Pronunciation: / kraʊn ɪmˈpɪriəl / (AmE), / kraʊn ɪmˈpɪəriəl / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant (Fritillaria imperialis) with a cluster of leaves and showy orange-red, bell-shaped flowers at the top of its stem.

Examples

The crown imperial flower
Botanists admire the crown imperial for both its unique appearance and its resistance to pests.
Each spring, the crown imperial adds striking color to the garden with its vibrant orange blooms.
The tall stem of the crown imperial is topped with a distinctive whorl of leaves and bell-shaped flowers.
