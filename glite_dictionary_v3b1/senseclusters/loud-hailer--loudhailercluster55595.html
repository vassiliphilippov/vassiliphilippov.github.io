loud-hailer

noun
person who loudly promotes messages
7.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8la\u028ad\u02cche\u026al\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8la\u028ad\u02cche\u026al\u0259 / (BrE)
A person who loudly promotes, repeats, or broadcasts the views or messages of a group or individual; a public mouthpiece.

Examples

Party loud-hailer
The party's chief loud-hailer repeated the slogan at every rally for weeks.
As a hired loud-hailer, she amplified corporate talking points on morning radio shows.
Public debate suffered because a few loud-hailers drowned out more measured voices.

Synonyms

  • tub-thumper
    9.8 0.01/m
    a tub-thumper speaks loudly and angrily to excite crowds, while a loud-hailer repeats and promotes a group's messages publicly.
  • evangelizer
    7.1 0.02/m
    an evangelizer promotes ideas with strong belief and energy, while a loud-hailer mainly repeats and broadcasts a group's messages loudly.
  • proclaimer
    5.9 0.03/m
    a proclaimer publicly announces beliefs or laws in a formal or religious voice, while a loud-hailer acts as a group's loud public mouthpiece.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mouthpiece - a person or organization that publicly expresses the opinions or policies of another
  • synonym: spokesperson - a person who speaks on behalf of a group or organization
