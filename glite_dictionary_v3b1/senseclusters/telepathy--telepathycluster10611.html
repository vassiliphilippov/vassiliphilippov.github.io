telepathy

noun
direct mind-to-mind communication
4.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /tə.ˈlep.ə.θiː/ /; / /te.ˈlep.ə.θiː/ (AmE), /tə.ˈlep.ə.θi/ /; / /te.ˈlep.ə.θi/ (BrE)
the communication of thoughts or information directly from one person's mind to another's without using speech, writing, or the known senses

Examples

Telepathy communication
In science fiction stories, telepathy is often portrayed as a common superpower.
Many scientists remain skeptical about the existence of telepathy due to the lack of empirical evidence.
The twins claimed that their connection was so strong it sometimes felt like telepathy.
Researchers demonstrated telepathy between participants via brain-computer interfaces in a controlled trial.
In the study, volunteers reported clear telepathy signals when their neural implants were synchronised.
Companies are exploring commercial telepathy systems to let users send thoughts between connected devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brain-to-brain communication - direct exchange of neural information between brains, often using neural interfaces.
  • synonym: brain–computer interface - a device that records brain activity and can transmit or interpret signals to enable communication or control.
  • synonym: clairvoyance - the supposed ability to perceive things beyond normal sensory contact
  • synonym: mind reading - the ability to know what someone is thinking
