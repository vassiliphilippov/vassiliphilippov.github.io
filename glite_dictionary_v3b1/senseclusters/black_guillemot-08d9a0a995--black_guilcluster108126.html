black guillemot

noun
a northern Atlantic seabird
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈɡɪləmət / (AmE), / blæk ˈɡɪlɪmɒt / (BrE)
a medium-sized seabird of the northern Atlantic, with predominantly black plumage and white wing patches, known scientifically as Cepphus grylle

Examples

The black guillemot bird
Conservationists are monitoring black guillemot populations to track changes in the marine environment.
The black guillemot is easily recognized by its striking black body and white wing patches.
We spotted a black guillemot perched on the rocky shoreline during our trip to Iceland.
