nervously

adverb
in a nervous or anxious way
2.3 10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ùÀêv…ôsli /; / Ààn…úrv…ôsli / (AmE), / Ààn…úÀêv…ôsli / (BrE)
in a way that shows nervousness, anxiety, or worry

Examples

To laugh nervously
He laughed nervously when asked about his plans for the future.
She glanced nervously around the crowded room before starting her speech.
The children waited nervously for their test results to be announced.
He glanced nervously at his watch as the interview ran late.
She laughed nervously before giving her speech in front of the class.
The dog barked nervously when it heard a strange noise outside the door.

Synonyms

  • anxiously
    3.2 4.65/m
    anxiously shows strong worry and eagerness for an outcome, while nervously describes general feelings of anxiety or worry.
  • worriedly
    2.0 0.53/m
    worriedly sounds like someone who feels personal worry about a problem, while nervously covers broader anxious behaviour and feelings.
  • uneasily
    4.2 3.13/m
    uneasily suggests physical discomfort or doubt in a situation, while nervously focuses on inner anxiety or worried feelings.
  • tensely
    3.9 0.56/m
    tensely shows muscle tightness or strain under pressure, while nervously means feeling anxious or worried inside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmly - in a calm manner; without nervousness or anxiety
  • antonym: confidently - in a self-assured or certain manner
