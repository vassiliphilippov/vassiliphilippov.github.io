tootle

verb
play music casually (wind instrument)
9.3 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀêt…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàtuÀêt…ôl / (BrE)
to play a musical instrument, especially a wind instrument, in a light, casual, or aimless manner

Examples

To tootle on a flute
In the quiet afternoon, he would tootle on his clarinet by the open window.
She likes to tootle a few cheerful notes on her flute before dinner.
The band tootled happily as the parade passed through town.

Synonyms

  • noodle around
    8.0 0.04/m
    noodle around means playing casually and improvising on an instrument without purpose, while tootle refers to light, aimless playing especially on wind instruments.
  • tweedle
    9.4 0.00/m
    tweedle is to play an instrument in a light, often repetitive way, while tootle means casually playing, often on a wind instrument.
  • toot
    3.3 0.34/m
    toot is to make a short, sharp sound on a horn or whistle, while tootle means to play lightly and aimlessly on an instrument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: toot - to make a short, sharp sound with a horn or similar instrument
