ice

noun
skating/hockey rink with frozen surface
5.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /aɪs/ (AmE), /aɪs/ (BrE)
water that has frozen and become solid, or a surface of frozen water (such as on a lake, river, or rink)

Examples

Skate on ice
Families gathered at the ice on Saturday mornings for public skating sessions.
She practiced her routines on the ice every evening before the competition.
The hockey players stepped onto the ice to start the match.
She put some ice in her glass to chill her drink.
Skating on ice can be both fun and challenging.
The lake was covered with a thick layer of ice in winter.
He could see cracks forming in the ice near the riverbank.
In winter, the ice on the lake was thick enough for skating.
The fisherman drilled a hole through the ice to catch fish below.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ice rink - an enclosed area with a smooth surface of ice for skating or hockey
  • degree lower: snow - atmospheric water vapor frozen into ice crystals and falling as flakes
  • antonym: steam - water in the form of a gas, typically as a result of boiling
  • antonym: water - the liquid state from which ice forms
