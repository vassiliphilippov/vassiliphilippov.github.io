duodecimo

adjective
small book size
Pronunciation: / ˌduːoʊˈdɛsɪˌmoʊ / (AmE), / ˌdjuːəˈdɛsɪməʊ / (BrE)
relating to a book or pamphlet made by folding a sheet of paper to form twelve leaves, resulting in a small size

Examples

Duodecimo format book
The rare book collector prized the duodecimo edition for its compact size and elegant binding.
She found a duodecimo volume of poetry that fit perfectly in her handbag.
Many nineteenth-century novels were published in duodecimo format to make them more affordable.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: octavo - a book size made by folding a sheet into eight leaves
  • degree lower: sextodecimo - a book size made by folding a sheet into sixteen leaves
