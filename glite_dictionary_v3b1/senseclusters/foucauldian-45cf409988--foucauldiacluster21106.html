Foucauldian

adjective
of Foucault's theories
Pronunciation: / fuːˈkoʊldiən / (AmE), / fuːˈkəʊldiən / (BrE)
relating to the theories or ideas of Michel Foucault, especially concerning power, knowledge, and society

Examples

Foucauldian theory
The professor gave a Foucauldian interpretation of how power operates in schools.
Her research uses a Foucauldian framework to analyze social institutions.
Many critics have adopted a Foucauldian approach to studying modern surveillance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Marxist - relating to the theories of Karl Marx
  • synonym: structuralist - relating to the theory that elements of human culture must be understood in terms of their relationship to a larger structure
