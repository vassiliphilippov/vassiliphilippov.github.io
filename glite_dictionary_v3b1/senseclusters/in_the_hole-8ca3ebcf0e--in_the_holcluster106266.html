in the hole

preposition
in debt; financial trouble
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə hoʊl / (AmE), / ɪn ðə həʊl / (BrE)
in debt or in a financially disadvantaged situation

Examples

In the hole for money
After unexpected medical bills, James found himself in the hole for several thousand dollars.
If we spend more than we earn, we'll end up in the hole again.
The small business was in the hole last quarter but expects to turn a profit soon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in the black - not in debt; financially solvent
  • synonym: in the red - in debt or overdrawn
