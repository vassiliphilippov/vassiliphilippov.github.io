backcloth

noun
stage scenery cloth at rear
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈbakˌklɒθ / (BrE)
a large piece of cloth, often painted, hung at the back of a stage as part of the scenery

Examples

A backcloth on stage
A richly colored backcloth can dramatically change the atmosphere on stage.
Crew members carefully hung the backcloth before the next act began.
The backcloth depicted a beautiful mountain landscape for the play's final scene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backdrop - a painted cloth hung at the back of a theater stage as part of the scenery
