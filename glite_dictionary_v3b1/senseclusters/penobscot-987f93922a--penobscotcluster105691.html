Penobscot

proper noun
a Native American people of northern Maine
Pronunciation: / pəˈnɑbˌskɑt / (AmE), / pəˈnɒbˌskɒt / (BrE)
A member of the Native American people belonging to the Algonquian language family, traditionally living in the Penobscot valley of northern Maine.

Examples

Penobscot people
Many members of the Penobscot continue to advocate for their rights and preserve their heritage.
She studied the history and language of the Penobscot during her anthropology course.
The Penobscot have maintained their cultural traditions for centuries in Maine.
