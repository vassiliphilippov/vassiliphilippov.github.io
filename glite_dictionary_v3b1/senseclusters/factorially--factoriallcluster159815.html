factorially

adverb
extremely rapidly
9.2
Pronunciation: / fækˈtɔːriəli / (AmE), / fækˈtɔːriəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to factorials in mathematics, or that increases extremely rapidly like a factorial function

Examples

Increase factorially
The number of users increased factorially after the app was featured on national television.
Rumors about the event spread factorially through social media, reaching thousands in hours.
Interest in the new technology grew factorially, surprising even the developers.
The number of possible seatings increases factorially with each additional guest.
To solve the problem, calculate the outcomes factorially rather than by listing them all.
The complexity of the puzzle grows factorially as more pieces are added.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combinatorially - in a way that relates to combinations or arrangements
  • synonym: exponentially - in a way that increases very quickly or becomes more and more rapid
  • antonym: gradually - in a slow and steady way over time
