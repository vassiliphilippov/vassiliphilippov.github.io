Maori

noun
indigenous people of New Zealand
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊri / (AmE), / ˈmaʊəri / (BrE)
A member of the indigenous Polynesian people of New Zealand, known for their unique language and culture.

Examples

A Maori person
A group of Maori performed a haka to welcome the visitors.
Many Maori traditions are still practiced throughout New Zealand today.
The Maori are known for their rich mythology and distinctive art forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Polynesian - A member of any of the indigenous peoples of Polynesia
  • antonym: Pākehā - A non-Maori New Zealander, usually of European descent
