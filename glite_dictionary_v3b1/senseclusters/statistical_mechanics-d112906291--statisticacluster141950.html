statistical mechanics

noun
physics: prediction of macroscopic systems via statistics
3.5
Pronunciation: / stəˈtɪstɪkəl məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / stəˈtɪstɪkəl mɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that uses statistical methods to predict and explain the behavior of systems composed of many particles, based on probabilistic laws

Examples

Statistical mechanics theory
Statistical mechanics bridges the gap between microscopic and macroscopic perspectives in physics.
Many concepts in statistical mechanics are fundamental to understanding thermodynamics.
Students often struggle with the mathematical rigor required in statistical mechanics.
