short circuit

noun
accidental electric connection causing circuit failure
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an accidental connection allowing current to travel along an unintended path in an electric circuit, often resulting in damage or interruption of function

Examples

A short circuit fault
A sudden short circuit cut power to the entire building.
If you connect the wires incorrectly, you could cause a dangerous short circuit.
The fire started because of a short circuit in the old wiring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open circuit - an electric circuit in which the path is broken, preventing current flow
  • degree higher: overload - excessive electrical load in a circuit
