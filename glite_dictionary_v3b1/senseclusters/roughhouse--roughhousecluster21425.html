roughhouse

verb
engage in rough play
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌfˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌhaʊs / (BrE)
to engage in rough or disorderly play, often play-fighting or tumbling vigorously

Examples

To roughhouse playfully
Even though they were roughhousing, no one got hurt.
The kids began to roughhouse in the living room, knocking over a lamp.
We told them not to roughhouse near the furniture, but they didn't listen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: horse around - to play or fool around in a rough or noisy way
  • degree lower: play - to engage in activity for enjoyment and recreation rather than a serious purpose
