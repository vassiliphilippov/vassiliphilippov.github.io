spleen

noun
large abdominal organ involved in immunity
Pronunciation: / spliːn / (AmE), / spliːn / (BrE)
A large, dark-red organ located on the left side of the abdomen that plays a role in immune response and blood cell maintenance.

Examples

A ruptured spleen
After the car accident, doctors discovered an injury to his spleen that required surgery.
Some medical conditions can cause the spleen to become enlarged and tender.
The spleen is an important part of the body's immune system, helping to fight infections.

  • synonym: pancreas - glandular organ behind the stomach involved in digestion and insulin production
