grass tree

noun
Australian perennial plant with grasslike tufted leaves
4.5 7.5
Pronunciation: / ɡræs tri / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs triː / (BrE)
Any of several Australian evergreen perennial plants with short, thick woody stems, topped with a tuft of grasslike leaves and producing acaroid resins, especially of the genus Xanthorrhoea.

Examples

Australian grass tree
Early settlers used the resin from grass trees as an adhesive and sealant.
Fire helps the grass tree to flower and regenerate in its natural habitat.
The Australian landscape was dotted with distinctive grass trees rising above the scrub.
