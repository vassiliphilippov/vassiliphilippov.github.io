screw wrench

noun
adjustable wrench with screw mechanism
2.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /skruː rentʃ/ (AmE), /skruː rentʃ/ (BrE)
An adjustable wrench with one jaw that is moved by turning a screw, allowing it to fit various sizes of fasteners.

Examples

An adjustable screw wrench
A screw wrench is useful because it can be adjusted to fit different bolt sizes.
He reached into his toolbox and pulled out a screw wrench to loosen the pipe fitting.
The mechanic recommended using a screw wrench for the plumbing job.
