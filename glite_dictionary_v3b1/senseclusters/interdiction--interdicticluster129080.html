interdiction

noun
court order prohibiting an activity
7.0 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tɚˈdɪk.ʃən /; / ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈdɪk.ʃən /; / ˌɪntəˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
an official order that forbids or prohibits something, especially by law or authority

Examples

A interdiction order
A temporary interdiction can be granted while the case is under formal review.
Due to repeated violations, an interdiction was filed against the nightclub's operations.
The court issued an interdiction preventing the company from disposing of its assets.
Military interdiction of supply routes was critical to the success of the operation.
Religious interdiction often shaped local customs and behavior in medieval Europe.
The government imposed an interdiction on the import of endangered animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: injunction - a court order compelling or preventing an action
  • synonym: prohibition - the action of forbidding something, especially by law
