washerwoman

noun
woman who washes clothes for others
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑʃɚˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃəˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to wash other people's clothes, usually for payment

Examples

The washerwoman washes clothes
In Victorian times, being a washerwoman was a common but laborious job for women.
My grandmother once worked as a washerwoman to support her family.
The washerwoman delivered clean linen to the hotel every Monday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laundress - a woman employed to wash and iron clothes
  • antonym: washerman - a man employed to wash clothes, especially professionally
