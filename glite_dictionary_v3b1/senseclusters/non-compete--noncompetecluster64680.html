non-compete

noun
employment restriction
6.0
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.kəm.ˈpiːt/ /; / /nən.kəm.ˈpiːt/ (AmE), /nɒn.kəm.ˈpiːt/ /; / /nən.kəm.ˈpiːt/ (BrE)
a contract or clause that prevents someone from working for competitors for a certain period after leaving a job

Examples

A non-compete clause
The company required all new employees to sign a non-compete before starting work.
Because of his non-compete, he had to wait six months before joining a rival firm.
Her lawyer reviewed the non-compete to ensure it was fair and enforceable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restraint of trade - a legal restriction on a person's ability to do business
