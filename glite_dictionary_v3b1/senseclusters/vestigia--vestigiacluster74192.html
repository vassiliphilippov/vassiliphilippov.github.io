vestigia

noun
traces or remains
Pronunciation: / vɛˈstɪdʒiə / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪdʒiə / (BrE)
traces or remains of something that once existed, often used in literary or scholarly contexts

Examples

Ancient vestigia found
The archaeologists found only vestigia of the ancient settlement.
In the old library, scholars searched for vestigia of lost manuscripts.
The forest path revealed vestigia of a long-forgotten village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evidence - something that shows something existed or happened
  • synonym: remnants - small remaining parts of something
  • synonym: ruins - the remains of something destroyed
