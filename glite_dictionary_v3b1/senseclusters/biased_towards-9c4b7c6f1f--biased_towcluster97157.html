biased towards

adjective
showing favoritism toward something
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪəst təˈwɔrdz / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəst təˈwɔːdz / (BrE)
Showing an unfair preference or favoritism toward a particular person, group, or viewpoint.

Examples

Biased towards certain groups
Many people feel the hiring process is biased towards candidates with certain educational backgrounds.
The article was clearly biased towards the incumbent, presenting only their achievements in a positive light.
The study's methodology has been criticized for being biased towards a Western perspective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biased against - showing unfair dislike or prejudice against someone or something
  • synonym: partial to - having a liking for or preference toward something
