concretion

noun
formation of a stonelike mass in the body
Pronunciation: / kənˈkriːʃən / (AmE), / kənˈkriːʃən /; / kɒnˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
a hard lump or stonelike mass formed by the accumulation of mineral salts in a body organ or duct (medical)

Examples

Kidney concretion
Doctors removed a large concretion during the surgery.
The formation of a concretion in the kidney can lead to severe pain and urinary blockage.
Ultrasound imaging revealed a concretion in the patient's gallbladder.
A large concretion may require medical intervention to prevent complications.
Kidney pain can be caused by a concretion blocking the urinary tract.
The surgeon removed a concretion from the patient's gallbladder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calculus - a stonelike concretion formed in the body, especially in the kidneys or bladder
