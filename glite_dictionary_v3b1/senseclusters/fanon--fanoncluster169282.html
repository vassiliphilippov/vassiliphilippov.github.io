fanon

noun
clerical vestment
Pronunciation: / ˈfænən / (AmE), / ˈfanɒn / (BrE)
a ceremonial vestment, often a shoulder cape, worn by certain clergy in Christian liturgical traditions

Examples

Papal fanon
During the ceremony, the pope placed the fanon over his shoulders as a symbol of his office.
The ornate fanon was reserved for use on the most important feast days.
Historians studied the evolution of the fanon in medieval church attire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chasuble - a sleeveless outer vestment worn by clergy during Mass
  • synonym: stole - a long, narrow strip of cloth worn by clergy as a vestment
