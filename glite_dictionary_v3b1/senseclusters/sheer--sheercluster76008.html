sheer

verb
to swerve or turn aside sharply
Pronunciation: / ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
to turn or swerve suddenly from a course, especially to avoid something

Examples

To sheer away
The boat began to sheer toward the shore as the wind picked up.
The driver had to sheer right to avoid hitting a fallen tree.
When the horse got spooked, it sheered off the path suddenly.
If you sheer too quickly, you might lose control of the car.
Suddenly, the cyclist sheered to the left when he saw the dog.
The bus sheered off the road to avoid a collision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swerve - to change direction abruptly
  • synonym: veer - suddenly change an intended course or direction
