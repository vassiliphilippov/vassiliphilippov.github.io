chess piece

noun
a playing piece in chess
Pronunciation: / tʃɛs pis / (AmE), / tʃɛs piːs / (BrE)
any of the figures, such as a pawn, knight, bishop, rook, queen, or king, used in playing the game of chess

Examples

A chess piece moves
After losing his last chess piece, he conceded the game.
He moved his chess piece to threaten his opponent’s queen.
It's important to understand how each chess piece moves before playing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bishop - a chess piece that moves diagonally
  • synonym: king - the chess piece whose capture decides the game
  • synonym: knight - a chess piece that moves in an L-shape
  • synonym: pawn - the chess piece with the lowest rank
  • synonym: queen - the most powerful chess piece
  • synonym: rook - a chess piece that moves in straight lines
