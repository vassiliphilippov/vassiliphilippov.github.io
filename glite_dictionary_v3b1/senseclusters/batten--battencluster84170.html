batten

verb
to furnish or cover with battens
Pronunciation: / ˈbætən /; / ˈbætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ən /; / ˈbætn̩ / (BrE)
to secure, reinforce, or cover something by fixing battens (strips of material) over it

Examples

To batten the roof
Carpenters will batten the roof to secure the new insulation panels.
It's important to batten windows and doors when preparing for a hurricane.
The crew worked quickly to batten down the hatches before the storm hit.
After hearing the storm warning, the crew hurried to batten down the hatches.
The carpenters will batten the loose panels so they won't move in the wind.
You must batten the shutters before the hurricane arrives.
