potato tuberworm

noun
larva of potato moth; plant pest
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪtoʊ ˈtuːbərˌwɜrm / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ ˈtjuːbəˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of the potato moth that feeds on and mines the leaves and stems of potato, tobacco, and related plants

Examples

The potato tuberworm larva
Damage from the potato tuberworm can significantly reduce crop yields if not controlled effectively.
Researchers are developing new methods to manage potato tuberworm infestations without relying on chemical pesticides.
The potato tuberworm is a serious pest for potato farmers in many regions worldwide.
