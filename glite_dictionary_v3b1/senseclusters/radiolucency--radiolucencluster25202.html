radiolucency

noun
dark area on X-ray
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈluːsənsi /; / ˌreɪdiəˈluːsənsi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈluːsənsi /; / ˌreɪdiəˈluːsənsi / (BrE)
an area or quality on an X-ray image that appears dark because it allows more radiation to pass through, often indicating less dense tissue or a cavity

Examples

A radiolucency area
The dentist noticed a radiolucency near the tooth root, which could indicate an infection.
A small radiolucency was visible on the X-ray, suggesting possible bone loss.
The doctor explained that the radiolucency seen in the scan might be a harmless air pocket.
The X-ray revealed a radiolucency near the root of the tooth, suggesting possible infection.
Doctors look for areas of radiolucency in bone scans to detect abnormalities.
A sudden radiolucency on the chest X-ray prompted further medical investigation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radiopacity - an area on an X-ray image that appears lighter because it blocks more radiation
