calque formation

noun
expression made by direct translation from another language
Pronunciation: / kælk fɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / kalk fɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing an expression into a language by directly translating it from another language

Examples

Calque formation process
Many English expressions originate by calque formation from other languages, notably from Latin and French.
The word 'skyscraper' is a classic example of calque formation from the French 'gratte-ciel'.
Translation students often study calque formation to understand how idioms can be adapted across languages.
