sourdough

noun
naturally fermented bread
3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsaʊr.doʊ/ /; / /ˈsaʊ.ər.doʊ/ (AmE), /ˈsaʊ.ə.dəʊ/ (BrE)
a type of bread made by fermenting dough using naturally occurring wild yeast and bacteria, giving it a tangy flavor

Examples

A sourdough loaf
He bought a loaf of sourdough from the local bakery for their picnic.
The tangy taste of sourdough makes it popular for sandwiches and toast.
Many people enjoy baking their own sourdough at home using a starter culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rye bread - bread made with rye flour, often dense and flavorful
  • antonym: white bread - bread made from refined wheat flour, usually soft and mild
