horizontal parallax

noun
maximum parallax at the horizon (astronomy)
9.9
Pronunciation: /hɔːr.ɪ.ˈzɑːn.təl ˈpær.ə.læks/ /; / /hɔːr.ɪ.ˈzɑːn.təl ˈper.ə.læks/ (AmE), /hɒr.ɪ.ˈzɒn.təl ˈpær.ə.læks/ (BrE)
the maximum parallax of a celestial body as observed from Earth's surface when the body is on the horizon

Examples

The horizontal parallax angle
Astronomers use the horizontal parallax to calculate the distance to nearby celestial objects.
Measuring a planet's horizontal parallax helps determine its actual size and distance.
The horizontal parallax of the Moon is much greater than that of most planets.
