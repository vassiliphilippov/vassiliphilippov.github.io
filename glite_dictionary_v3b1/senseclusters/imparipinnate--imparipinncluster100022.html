imparipinnate

adjective
having a pinnate leaf with a single leaflet at the end
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpɛriˈpɪneɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˌparɪˈpɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a pinnate leaf) having a single terminal leaflet at the tip, resulting in an odd number of leaflets.

Examples

Imparipinnate leaf tip
Many legumes display imparipinnate leaves as a distinguishing feature.
The ash tree has imparipinnate leaves, which means there is a single leaflet at the tip of each leaf.
The botanist identified the species by its imparipinnate leaf arrangement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paripinnate - having an even number of leaflets with no terminal leaflet
