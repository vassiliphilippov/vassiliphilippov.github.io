marketability

noun
ease of being sold
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑrkətəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌmɑːkɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality that makes something easy to sell or attractive to buyers

Examples

The marketability of products
The marketability of the new smartphone depends on its unique features and price.
Designers often consider marketability when creating products for a wide audience.
Improving packaging can greatly increase a product's marketability in stores.
The lawyer checked the marketability of the property before the sale was finalized.
Any defects in the title can affect the marketability of real estate.
Renovations improved the marketability of the house, attracting more potential buyers.
The marketability of the new smartphone helped the company achieve record sales in its first quarter.
Real estate agents often discuss the marketability of a property before listing it for sale.
During economic downturns, the marketability of certain investments can decrease significantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appeal - the quality of being attractive or interesting
  • synonym: desirability - the quality of being wanted or worth having
  • antonym: illiquidity - the difficulty of selling an asset quickly without a loss in value
  • synonym: liquidity - the ability to quickly buy or sell an asset without affecting its price
  • synonym: salability - the quality of being easy to sell
  • antonym: unmarketability - the quality of being difficult to sell or unattractive to buyers
