hylomorphic

adjective
matter and form theory
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪloʊˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɪləʊˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the philosophical theory that everything consists of both matter and form

Examples

Hylomorphic theory
The philosopher presented a hylomorphic explanation for the nature of living beings.
Aristotle's hylomorphic theory influenced many later thinkers in Western philosophy.
Some debates in metaphysics center on the validity of hylomorphic concepts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aristotelian - relating to the philosophy of Aristotle
  • antonym: materialistic - relating to the belief that only matter is real
