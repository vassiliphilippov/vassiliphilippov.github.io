Cheops

proper noun
Egyptian Pharaoh who built the Great Pyramid
Pronunciation: / ˈkiː.ɑps / (AmE), / ˈkiː.ɒps / (BrE)
An Egyptian Pharaoh of the 27th century BC, known for commissioning the Great Pyramid at Giza.

Examples

Cheops pyramid
Archaeologists have long studied the reign of Cheops to understand the construction of the pyramids.
Many historians regard Cheops as one of ancient Egypt’s most influential rulers.
The Great Pyramid of Giza was built as the tomb of Cheops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Khufu - the original Egyptian name of Pharaoh Cheops
