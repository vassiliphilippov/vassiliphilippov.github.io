Arabic numeral

noun
symbol for numbers 0–9
Pronunciation: / əˈræbɪk ˈnuːmərəl / (AmE), / əˈræbɪk ˈnjuːmərəl / (BrE)
any of the ten symbols 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, or 9 used to represent numbers in the decimal system

Examples

Arabic numerals 0 to 9
In mathematics, we use Arabic numerals for calculations and formulas.
Most modern clocks display the time using Arabic numerals.
Please write the date in Arabic numerals rather than Roman numerals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Roman numeral - symbols used in the numeral system of ancient Rome (I, V, X, etc.)
  • synonym: digit - a single symbol used to make up numbers; especially 0–9
