hepatolithiasis

noun
liver stone disease
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɛˌpætəloʊˈθaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / hɛˌpætəʊlɪˈθaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which stones form within the bile ducts of the liver

Examples

Hepatolithiasis in liver ducts
The patient was diagnosed with hepatolithiasis after experiencing persistent abdominal pain.
Surgery may be required to remove stones caused by hepatolithiasis in severe cases.
Doctors monitor patients with hepatolithiasis for possible complications such as infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cholelithiasis - the formation of gallstones in the gallbladder
