population

noun
group of a species in an area
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑː.pjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒp.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A group of organisms of the same species living in a particular area.

Examples

Animal population
A disease outbreak can drastically reduce a fish population in a single lake.
Scientists monitor the local frog population to assess the effects of pollution.
The wolf population in the region has been steadily increasing over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: community - all the different species living together in a particular area
