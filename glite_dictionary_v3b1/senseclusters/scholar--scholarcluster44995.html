scholar

noun
learned person; academic expert
4.1 37/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsk…ëÀêl…ôr / (AmE), / Ààsk…íl…ô / (BrE)
a person who has acquired deep knowledge in a particular field of study through extensive learning and research

Examples

Famous scholar in history
Many scholars have written extensively about the origins of language.
The university appointed her as a visiting scholar in recognition of her achievements.
To become a leading scholar, one must dedicate years to careful research and study.

Synonyms

    4.4 0.04/m

  • savant
    8.9 1.30/m
    a savant shows exceptional, often narrow expertise in one field, while a scholar develops deep knowledge through study and research across a subject.
