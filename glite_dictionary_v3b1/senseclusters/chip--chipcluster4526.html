chip

noun
act of breaking off a small piece
5.2 1.8
Pronunciation: / tʃɪp / (AmE), / tʃɪp / (BrE)
a small piece or fragment broken off from something, or a small mark or indentation left after breaking

Examples

A chip in the cup
A chip on the edge of the cup made it unsafe to drink from.
He gave the statue a small chip when he bumped into it.
Over time, frequent handling can cause a chip in delicate glassware.
A chip in the windshield can quickly grow if not repaired.
He noticed a small chip on his favorite mug.
There's a chip in the edge of the antique table.
A chip of stone hit his shoe during the construction work.
He brushed a chip of glass from the table.
Tiny chips of wood scattered across the workbench after he finished sawing.
