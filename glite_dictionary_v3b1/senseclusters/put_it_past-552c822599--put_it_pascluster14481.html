put it past

verb
consider someone capable of (often negative) action
Pronunciation: / pʊt ɪt pæst / (AmE), / pʊt ɪt pɑːst / (BrE)
to consider someone capable of doing something, especially something bad or unexpected

Examples

To put it past someone
Given his reputation, I wouldn't put it past the company to cut corners.
I wouldn't put it past him to try cheating on the exam.
She lost her keys again—honestly, I don't put it past her at all!

Related Terms

  • synonym: suspect - to believe someone is likely to do something wrong
