thermocouple junction

noun
temperature-measuring device using joined metal wires
Pronunciation: / ˈθɝːmoʊˌkʌpəl ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməkʌpəl ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A device made of two wires of different metals joined at both ends, used to measure temperature by generating a current proportional to the temperature difference between the junctions.

Examples

A thermocouple junction device
In high-temperature environments, a thermocouple junction provides accurate and reliable readings.
The accuracy of a thermocouple junction depends on the types of metals used in the wiring.
The laboratory technician used a thermocouple junction to monitor the reaction temperature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermocouple - a device consisting of two different metals joined at two points to measure temperature
