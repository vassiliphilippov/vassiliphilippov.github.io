hypersensitize

verb
to make overly sensitive
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make a person, animal, or living thing extremely or abnormally sensitive to a substance or stimulus

Examples

To hypersensitize the skin
Certain chemicals can hypersensitize the skin, making it react strongly to sunlight.
Doctors warn that overuse of antibiotics may hypersensitize the immune system to harmless bacteria.
Repeated exposure to allergens can hypersensitize a person's body, leading to severe allergic reactions.
Certain medications can hypersensitize the immune system, leading to allergic reactions.
Repeated loud noises may hypersensitize your hearing over time.
Some treatments are designed to hypersensitize cancer cells to radiation therapy.
