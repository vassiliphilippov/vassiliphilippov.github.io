sense

noun
sound practical judgment
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɛns / (AmE), / sɛns / (BrE)
the ability to make sound practical judgments; good understanding or awareness of what is appropriate or necessary

Examples

Common sense
If you had any sense, you would bring an umbrella on a day like this.
She showed more sense than anyone else in the meeting, quickly defusing the argument.
Tom's decision to save money instead of spending it showed a lot of sense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: judgment - the ability to make considered decisions or come to sensible conclusions
  • antonym: nonsense - lack of good sense or sound judgment; foolishness
