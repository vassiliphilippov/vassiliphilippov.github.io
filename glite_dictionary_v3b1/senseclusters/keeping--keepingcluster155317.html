keeping

noun
the act of retaining or holding something
Pronunciation: / ˈkipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkiːpɪŋ /; / ˈkiː.pɪŋ / (BrE)
the act, state, or responsibility of holding, caring for, or protecting something or someone (custody or care)

Examples

Safe keeping
She entrusted her jewelry to the bank for safe keeping.
The keeping of secrets can be difficult for some people.
The museum was responsible for the keeping of valuable historical documents.
She trusted her children to the keeping of her sister while she was away.
The company left the confidential files in his keeping until the audit was complete.
The museum placed the artifact in the keeping of its most trusted curator.

Related Terms

  • synonym: custody - protective care or guardianship of someone or something
  • synonym: guardianship - the position of being legally responsible for someone
  • antonym: release - the act of allowing something to be free or let go
  • synonym: retention - the continued possession or control of something
