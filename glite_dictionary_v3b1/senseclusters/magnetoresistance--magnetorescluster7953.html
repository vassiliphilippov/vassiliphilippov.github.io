magnetoresistance

noun
change in resistance
Pronunciation: / ˌmægnɪtoʊrɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ˌmægnɪtəʊrɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
a change in the electrical resistance of a material when it is exposed to a magnetic field

Examples

Giant magnetoresistance effect
The discovery of giant magnetoresistance revolutionized data storage technology.
Researchers are studying how magnetoresistance can be used in new types of sensors.
The magnetoresistance of the material increased when a strong magnetic field was applied.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resistivity - the property of a material that resists the flow of electric current
