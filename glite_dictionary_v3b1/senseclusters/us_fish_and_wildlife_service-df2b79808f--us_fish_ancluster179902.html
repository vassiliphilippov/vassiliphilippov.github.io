US Fish and Wildlife Service

proper noun
US agency for wildlife conservation
Pronunciation: / ju ɛs fɪʃ ənd ˈwaɪldlaɪf ˈsɜːrvɪs / (AmE), / juː ɛs fɪʃ ənd ˈwaɪldlaɪf ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
a US government agency within the Department of the Interior responsible for conserving and protecting fish, wildlife, and their habitats, and assessing environmental impacts.

Examples

The US Fish and Wildlife Service agency
New regulations proposed by the US Fish and Wildlife Service aim to restore damaged wetlands.
Researchers from the US Fish and Wildlife Service are studying the effects of climate change on bird populations.
The US Fish and Wildlife Service plays a crucial role in protecting endangered species across the country.
