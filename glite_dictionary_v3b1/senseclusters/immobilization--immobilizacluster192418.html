immobilization

noun
preventing or limiting movement
6.3 6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪˌmoʊbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of preventing movement or making someone or something unable to move

Examples

Immobilization of vehicles
Due to the landslide, immobilization of all vehicles in the area was necessary.
Extended immobilization can sometimes lead to muscle weakness in patients.
The doctor recommended immobilization of his injured leg to promote healing.
Flooding led to the immobilisation of all transport in the region.
Strict immobilisation is necessary after surgery to aid recovery.
The doctor recommended immobilisation of the injured limb using a splint.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the power or right to move freely
  • synonym: immobilization - American English spelling: the act of preventing movement
  • antonym: movement - the act of moving; opposite state of action
  • synonym: restraint - the act of holding something back or limiting its movement
  • synonym: restriction - the act of limiting movement; similar meaning
