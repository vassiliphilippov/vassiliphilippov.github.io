machine

verb
shape or finish using machinery
Pronunciation: / məˈʃin / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
to shape, cut, or finish material using a machine, especially in manufacturing or engineering

Examples

To machine metal
All the components were machined from solid aluminum blocks.
During the workshop, students learned how to machine metal using a lathe.
The company can machine custom parts to very precise specifications.
She spent the afternoon machining precision gears on the lathe.
The company machined thousands of bolts for the new project.
They decided to machine the parts instead of crafting them by hand.
The factory is machining metal parts for the new car model.
Skilled workers are machining the components to precise measurements.
After machining the block, the technician inspected it for any defects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: handcraft - to make something by hand rather than by machine
  • synonym: manufacture - to make (something) on a large scale using machinery
  • synonym: mill - to grind or shape materials using a machine
