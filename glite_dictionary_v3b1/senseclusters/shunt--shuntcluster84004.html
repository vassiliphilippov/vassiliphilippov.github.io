shunt

verb
move a train to a different track
7.2 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÊƒÊŒnt / (AmE), / ÊƒÊŒnt / (BrE)
to move a train or railway vehicle from one track to another, especially to allow others to pass or to organize railway traffic.

Examples

To shunt a train
If there is congestion ahead, the control center will shunt incoming trains to alternative tracks.
The train was shunted onto a side track to allow the express to pass.
Workers quickly shunt the empty wagons to the storage yard every morning.
