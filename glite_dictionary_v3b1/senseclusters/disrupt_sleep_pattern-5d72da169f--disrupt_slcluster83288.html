disrupt sleep pattern

verb
interfere with regular sleep cycles
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrʌpt sliːp ˈpætɚn / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌpt sliːp ˈpætn / (BrE)
to interfere with or interrupt the regularity of a person's usual sleep cycles or habits

Examples

To disrupt sleep pattern badly
Caffeine late in the evening can disrupt sleep pattern and make it hard to feel rested.
Jet lag often disrupts sleep pattern for travelers crossing multiple time zones.
Stressful situations frequently disrupt sleep pattern, leading to fatigue during the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disturb sleep - to prevent someone from sleeping or from sleeping well
