guardian

noun
person legally responsible for another or property
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːrdiən /; / ˈɡɑrdiən / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdiən / (BrE)
a person who protects or is responsible for someone or something, especially a child or property

Examples

A guardian of children
After his parents died, his aunt was appointed as his guardian.
As her guardian, he was responsible for managing her estate until she turned eighteen.
The museum's guardian ensured all the exhibits were safe from damage.
The guardian made sure the children arrived home safely every day.
As a legal guardian, she has the authority to make important decisions for her nephew.
The dog acted as a loyal guardian of the property at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caretaker - a person who takes care of someone or something
  • synonym: protector - someone who protects others
  • antonym: ward - a person under the care of a guardian
