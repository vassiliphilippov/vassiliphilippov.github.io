sassafras

noun
aromatic North American tree
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsæsəˌfræs / (AmE), / ˈsasəfrəs / (BrE)
A North American tree with aromatic leaves and bark, used as a source of sassafras oil and traditionally for flavoring and medicine.

Examples

Sassafras tree leaves
Bottles of old-fashioned root beer often contained extracts from sassafras roots.
Early settlers in North America used sassafras as a medicinal plant.
The leaves and bark of the sassafras are known for their distinctive aroma.
