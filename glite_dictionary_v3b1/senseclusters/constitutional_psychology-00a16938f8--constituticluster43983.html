constitutional psychology

noun
theory linking body type and personality
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.stɪˈtu.ʃə.nəl saɪˈkɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən.əl saɪˈkɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
a former school of psychological thought proposing that a person's character and personality are influenced by their physical body type (somatotype)

Examples

Constitutional psychology theory
Constitutional psychology was influential in the mid-20th century but is now largely rejected by modern science.
Many psychology textbooks discuss constitutional psychology in the context of outdated personality theories.
William Herbert Sheldon is known for developing the theory of constitutional psychology and classifying body types.
