Luddism

noun
opposition to technology
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlʌdɪzəm / (BrE)
opposition to industrialization or new technology, especially in the workplace

Examples

Modern Luddism in offices
Some critics accused the union of Luddism for resisting the introduction of new machinery.
The debate over artificial intelligence sometimes echoes the Luddism of the early industrial era.
Fears of job loss led to a wave of Luddism among factory workers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressivism - support for social reform and new technology
  • synonym: technophobia - fear or dislike of advanced technology
