ABM

noun
account-based marketing (B2B strategy)
7.9
Pronunciation: /eɪ biː em/ (AmE), /eɪ biː em/ (BrE)
a business-to-business marketing strategy that focuses resources on a clearly defined set of target accounts and delivers personalized campaigns tailored to each account.

Examples

ABM marketing strategy
The company adopted ABM to create personalized proposals for its top-tier enterprise clients.
Our marketing team measured conversion rates after launching a focused ABM campaign last quarter.
Agencies skilled in ABM often coordinate sales and marketing to close large corporate deals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: account-based marketing - a marketing approach that treats individual target companies as markets in their own right
  • antonym: mass marketing - a marketing strategy that tries to reach a large audience with a single campaign or message
