misclassification

noun
incorrect categorization
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsklɑːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of incorrectly assigning someone or something to a category, especially regarding employment status, often with legal or tax consequences

Examples

Employee misclassification
The government penalized the company for the misclassification of employees as independent contractors.
Accurate data analysis is impossible if there is significant misclassification in the records.
Legal experts warn that misclassification can lead to costly lawsuits and back taxes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: classification - the process of arranging or assigning into categories
  • synonym: mislabeling - the act of giving something the wrong label or description
