ossified

adjective
rigidly fixed in old ways
9.3 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀê.s…ôÀåfa…™d / (AmE), / Àà…ís.…™.fa…™d / (BrE)
(of ideas, systems, or attitudes) rigidly fixed and unwilling or unable to change; stuck in conventional ways.

Examples

An ossified system
His ossified views on gender roles have not changed in decades.
Many critics argue that the organization has become ossified and resistant to innovation.
Reforms were necessary to break the ossified structures of government bureaucracy.

Synonyms

  • fossilized
    7.0 0.28/m
    fossilized often describes old ideas frozen by long neglect, while ossified suggests rigid, convention-bound beliefs resistant to change.
  • inflexible
    4.7 1.29/m
    inflexible describes a person or rule that will not bend, while ossified means stuck in old conventions and unable to adapt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - willing to change or adapt easily
  • synonym: rigid - not willing to change ideas or behavior
