dominant

adjective
having power or control; most influential
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑm.ɪ.nənt / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.nənt / (BrE)
having power or influence over others; more important, strong, or noticeable than anything else of the same type.

Examples

Dominant position
Her team took a dominant position early in the competition and maintained the lead.
In the local ecosystem, wolves are the dominant predators.
The company's dominant market share makes it difficult for smaller brands to compete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preeminent - surpassing all others; very distinguished
  • antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or position; less important
