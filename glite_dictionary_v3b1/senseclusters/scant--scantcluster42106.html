scant

adjective
barely sufficient; less than needed
9.9 4.4
Pronunciation: /skænt/ (AmE), /skænt/ (BrE)
(of an amount) barely sufficient or less than what is needed, expected, or permitted.

Examples

A scant amount
Due to budget cuts, the committee only gave a scant amount of funding.
She measured out a scant cup of flour for the recipe.
The evidence provided was scant and failed to convince the jury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ample - more than enough; plentiful
  • synonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand
