stay

verb
remain temporarily in a place
1.0 422/m
Pronunciation: / steÉª / (AmE), / steÉª / (BrE)
to remain in a place, state, or condition; not to leave or change

Examples

To stay in a hotel
She stayed in London last summer while attending a course.
We decided to stay for a few extra days to explore the area.
You can stay at my house during your visit to the city.
If you stay calm, everything will work out fine.
The painting stayed untouched for decades.
The weather will stay cold throughout the week.
I will stay here while you get the car.
Please stay where you are until the doctor arrives.
We decided to stay at home instead of going out to dinner.

Synonyms

  • remain
    1.0 316/m
    remain is a formal verb that means to stay in the same place or state for some time, while stay is more general and common.
  • rest
    1.0 1.20/m
    rest describes a thing or state that stays unchanged or calm for a while, while stay covers both not leaving and not changing.
  • stay put
    2.0 4.04/m
    stay put means to remain in the exact spot and not move at all, while stay can mean to remain in place or in a condition more broadly.
  • stay in place
    1.5 0.78/m
    stay in place means to remain physically still at one position, while stay can also mean to continue in a state or condition.
  • continue
    1.0 42/m
    continue means to go on or let something keep happening over time, while stay focuses on remaining and not leaving or changing.
  • stay on
    3.4 6.12/m
    stay on means to remain after others leave or beyond the expected time, while stay simply means to remain or not leave in general.
  • stick around
    2.9 7.31/m
    stick around is informal for staying near while waiting for something to happen, while stay is neutral and fits many situations.
  • persist
    5.2 1.98/m
    persist is formal and often means to continue for a long time despite difficulty, while stay mainly means not to leave or change.
  • stick
    3.5 49/m
    stick can mean to remain with someone or an idea over time, often implying loyalty, while stay mainly means not to leave.
