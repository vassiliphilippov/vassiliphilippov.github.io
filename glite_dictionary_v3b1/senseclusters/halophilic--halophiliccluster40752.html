halophilic

adjective
salt-loving
Pronunciation: / ˌheɪloʊˈfɪlɪk /; / ˌheɪləˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌheɪləˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
able to live or thrive in environments with high salt concentrations (describing certain organisms)

Examples

Halophilic bacteria
Certain bacteria are halophilic and thrive in salt lakes where few other organisms can survive.
The halophilic nature of this protein allows it to function in extremely salty environments.
Researchers study halophilic enzymes to develop industrial processes that work in high-salt conditions.
Certain halophilic bacteria can survive in the extremely salty waters of the Dead Sea.
Researchers study halophilic organisms to understand how life adapts to harsh environments.
The salt flats are home to many halophilic microorganisms that cannot live elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: halotolerant - able to survive in high salt but not requiring it
  • antonym: xerophilic - thriving in very dry environments
