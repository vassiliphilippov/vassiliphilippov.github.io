Olympian Zeus

proper noun
statue of Zeus at Olympia, ancient Greek wonder
Pronunciation: / oʊˌlɪmpiən ˈzuːs / (AmE), / əˌlɪmpiən ˈzjuːs / (BrE)
a massive seated statue of Zeus, the chief god in ancient Greek mythology, created by Phidias for the temple at Olympia; renowned as one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

Examples

The Olympian Zeus statue
Art historians continue to debate the artistic techniques used to create the Olympian Zeus.
Many pilgrims traveled to Olympia just to gaze upon the Olympian Zeus statue.
The Olympian Zeus was considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
