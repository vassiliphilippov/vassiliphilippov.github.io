populate

verb
live in or inhabit a place
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑː.pjə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.jʊ.leɪt /; / ˈpɒp.jə.leɪt / (BrE)
to live in or fill a place with people or animals

Examples

Animals populate the island
After the disaster, only a few hardy species managed to populate the barren land.
As cities grow, new suburbs are quickly populated by young families.
Many migratory birds populate the wetlands throughout the year.
Government programs helped populate the remote regions of the country with farmers.
The island was populated by settlers in the seventeenth century.
Wildlife officials aim to populate the lakes with native fish species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desert - to leave a place so that it is no longer inhabited
  • synonym: inhabit - to live in a place as an inhabitant
