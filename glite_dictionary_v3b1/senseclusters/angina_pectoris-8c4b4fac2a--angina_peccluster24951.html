angina pectoris

noun
heart condition causing chest pain
2.2 4.1
Pronunciation: / ænˈdʒaɪnə ˈpɛktərɪs / (AmE), / ænˈdʒiːnə ˈpɛktərɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by sudden chest pain or discomfort, caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle.

Examples

Angina pectoris pain
His doctor prescribed medication to help manage his episodes of angina pectoris.
If you experience angina pectoris, seek medical attention immediately as it could be a sign of heart disease.
Walking up steep hills sometimes triggered angina pectoris in elderly patients.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: heart attack - a sudden and severe instance of reduced blood flow to the heart causing tissue damage
