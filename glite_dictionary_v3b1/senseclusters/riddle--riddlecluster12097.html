riddle

verb
to fill or permeate throughout (often with problems or holes)
1.5 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈrɪd.əl/ (AmE), /ˈrɪd.əl/ (BrE)
to fill or permeate thoroughly so that something is present in many parts, especially in a way that causes damage or problems; often used in 'riddled with'

Examples

Riddled with problems
Corruption riddled the entire organization, making reforms nearly impossible.
His report was riddled with factual errors and inconsistencies.
The old walls were riddled with bullet holes after the war.
