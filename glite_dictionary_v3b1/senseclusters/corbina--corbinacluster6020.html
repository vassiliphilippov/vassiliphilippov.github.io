corbina

noun
bluish-grey Californian coastal fish
Pronunciation: / kɔːrˈbinə / (AmE), / kɔːˈbiːnə / (BrE)
a bluish-grey coastal fish of the whiting family found along the California coast, valued as a food fish.

Examples

The corbina fish
Biologists study corbina populations to monitor the health of coastal marine environments.
Fishermen along the Southern California shore often catch corbina during the summer months.
The grilled corbina was served with fresh lemon and herbs at the seaside restaurant.
