Aporocactus flagelliformis

noun
species of cactus with showy crimson flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəroʊˈkæktəs fləˌdʒɛlɪˈfɔrmɪs / (AmE), / ˌapərəˈkaktəs fləˌdʒɛlɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of tropical American cactus with slender, creeping stems and large, showy crimson flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.

Examples

The Aporocactus flagelliformis plant
Many gardeners enjoy growing Aporocactus flagelliformis as a trailing plant in hanging baskets.
The Aporocactus flagelliformis is prized for its striking crimson blossoms that appear in late spring.
The slender stems of Aporocactus flagelliformis can reach several feet in length when properly cared for.
