underfill

verb
to fill too little
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈfɪl / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈfɪl / (BrE)
to fill something with less than the required or expected amount

Examples

To underfill a glass
If you underfill the glass, the recipe may not turn out as expected.
The printer will jam if you underfill the paper tray.
Be careful not to underfill the tank before a long drive.
