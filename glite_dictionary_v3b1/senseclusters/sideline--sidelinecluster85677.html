sideline

noun
edge of playing field
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the area just outside the main field of play, especially in sports, where coaches, substitutes, and others stand or sit

Examples

On the sideline
The injured player watched the game from the sideline with his teammates.
Coaches often give instructions to their team from the sideline during a match.
Spectators gathered along the sideline to cheer for their favorite players.

Related Terms

  • antonym: field - the main area where a sport is played
  • synonym: touchline - the line marking the side boundary of a sports field
