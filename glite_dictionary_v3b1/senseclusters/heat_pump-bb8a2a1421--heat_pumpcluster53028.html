heat pump

noun
device for transferring heat for heating buildings
1.5 3.4
Pronunciation: /hiːt pʌmp/ (AmE), /hiːt pʌmp/ (BrE)
A device that transfers heat from a cooler area to a warmer one, typically used to heat buildings by extracting heat from outside sources.

Examples

A heat pump system
Installing a heat pump can significantly reduce your energy bills.
Modern heat pumps work well even in colder climates.
The new house is equipped with a heat pump for efficient winter heating.
