hardening

noun
abnormal thickening of body tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdn̩ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdnɪŋ / (BrE)
Abnormal hardening or thickening of body tissue, often as a result of disease or injury.

Examples

Tissue hardening
Liver hardening, or cirrhosis, is often linked to chronic alcohol abuse.
Long-term exposure to the chemical caused hardening of the skin.
The doctor detected hardening of the arterial walls during the examination.
