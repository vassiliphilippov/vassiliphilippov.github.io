lucid

adjective
expressing thoughts clearly; mentally clear
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsɪd /; / ˈluː.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪd /; / ˈluː.sɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) able to think and express oneself clearly and logically, especially after confusion or illness

Examples

Lucid mind
After her fever broke, she became lucid and could explain what had happened.
During the interview, her responses were surprisingly lucid and insightful.
He offered a lucid account of the events despite the chaos around him.
After waking from surgery, she was briefly lucid before slipping back into confusion.
Despite the severity of the fever, the patient became lucid enough to recognize those around him.
During his illness, he experienced a few lucid moments that gave his family hope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coherent - able to speak or think in a logical and clear way
  • antonym: confused - unable to think clearly or understand what is happening
