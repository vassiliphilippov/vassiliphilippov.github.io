escort

noun
act of accompanying for protection
4.0 6.17/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õsÀåk…îrt /; / Àà…õsk…îrt / (AmE), / Àà…õsk…îÀêt / (BrE)
the act of accompanying someone, or a person or group who accompanies someone, especially to provide protection, assistance, or as a courtesy

Examples

Police escort
A police escort guided the ambulance through the heavy traffic.
The convoy moved under the escort of two fighter jets.
The president traveled with a military escort for added security.
A police escort accompanied the ambassador to the conference.
She worked as an escort, assisting business travelers during their visits to the city.
The celebrity arrived at the premiere with a hired escort for security.
A team of escorts accompanied the president during his visit to the city.
The celebrity arrived at the venue with a personal escort for safety.
The diplomat traveled with an armed escort to ensure her protection.

Synonyms

  • accompaniment
    4.4 0.15/m
    accompaniment is the act of going with someone to help or protect them, while escort often means a person or group who provides that protection.
  • companion
    4.8 0.07/m
    companion is someone who stays with you for company or support, while escort means a more formal, protective or guiding presence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bodyguard - a person employed to protect someone from harm or danger
  • synonym: chaperone - a person who accompanies and looks after another, especially a younger woman
  • synonym: convoy - a group of vehicles or ships traveling together for protection
