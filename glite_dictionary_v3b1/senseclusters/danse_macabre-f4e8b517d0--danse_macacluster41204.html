danse macabre

noun
medieval allegorical dance of death
Pronunciation: / dɑns məˈkɑb / (AmE), / dɑːns məˈkɑːb / (BrE)
A symbolic dance, common in medieval art and literature, in which Death leads people of all ranks to the grave, representing the universality of death.

Examples

The danse macabre scene
Literature from the Middle Ages frequently describes the danse macabre as a somber warning to the living.
Medieval artists often depicted the danse macabre to remind viewers of the inevitability of death.
The painting featured a chilling danse macabre winding through a stormy graveyard.
