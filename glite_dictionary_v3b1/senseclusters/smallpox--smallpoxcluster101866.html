smallpox

noun
contagious viral disease with pustules
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɔlˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlpɒks / (BrE)
A highly contagious and often deadly viral disease, marked by fever and a skin eruption with pustules that form scabs and often leave permanent scars.

Examples

A smallpox vaccination
A smallpox vaccination campaign was launched to protect populations from the deadly disease.
In the eighteenth century, smallpox outbreaks caused widespread fear and thousands of deaths across Europe.
The worldwide eradication of smallpox is considered one of humanity's greatest medical achievements.
