capital

noun
wealth or assets for investment
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl / (BrE)
money, property, or other assets used to produce more wealth or income

Examples

Business capital
Banks provide capital to entrepreneurs to help them launch new businesses.
She invested her capital in a promising start-up.
The company needs more capital to expand its operations overseas.
He invested most of his capital in real estate and technology startups.
The company raised additional capital to finance its expansion plans.
Without sufficient capital, small businesses often struggle to survive their first year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asset - anything valuable owned by a person or organization
  • antonym: liability - financial obligation or debt
