comfort

verb
to soothe or alleviate pain or distress
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌmfərt / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət / (BrE)
to make someone feel better by soothing their pain, worry, or distress

Examples

To comfort a friend
A warm bath can comfort sore muscles after a long run.
He gently comforted the crying child with kind words.
She tried to comfort her friend after the surgery.
After hearing the bad news, he struggled to comfort his sister.
She tried to comfort her friend after the loss of his pet.
The teacher's kind words helped to comfort the anxious student.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravate - to make pain or discomfort worse
  • synonym: console - to provide comfort or solace to someone troubled
  • antonym: distress - to cause someone to feel upset or worried
  • synonym: encourage - to give someone support or confidence
  • synonym: soothe - to gently calm or ease pain or distress
