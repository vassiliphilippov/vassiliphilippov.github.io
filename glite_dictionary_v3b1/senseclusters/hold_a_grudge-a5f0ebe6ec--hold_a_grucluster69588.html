hold a grudge

verb
continue feeling resentment about a past offense
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ə ɡrʌdʒ / (AmE), / həʊld ə ɡrʌdʒ / (BrE)
to continue feeling angry or resentful toward someone for a past offense or slight

Examples

To hold a grudge long
Despite the years that had passed, he still held a grudge against his old friend.
It's better to move on than to hold a grudge over something minor.
She tried not to hold a grudge, even when people disappointed her.
