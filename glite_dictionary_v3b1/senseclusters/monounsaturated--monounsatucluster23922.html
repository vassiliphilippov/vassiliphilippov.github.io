monounsaturated

adjective
fat or oil with one double bond
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˈʌnˌsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈʌnˌsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a fat or oil) containing one double bond in its molecular structure, making it less saturated than fully saturated fats.

Examples

Monounsaturated fat
A diet high in monounsaturated oils may help reduce bad cholesterol levels.
Avocados are a good source of monounsaturated fatty acids.
Olive oil is rich in monounsaturated fats, which are considered healthy for the heart.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: polyunsaturated - fat or oil containing more than one double bond
  • degree lower: saturated - fat or oil without double bonds between carbon atoms
