inventory accounting

noun
accounting for inventory valuation and control
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.vənˌtɔɹ.i əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.vən.t(ə).ri əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of accounting that tracks, values, and manages an organization's inventory of goods or materials

Examples

Accurate inventory accounting
Accurate inventory accounting is essential for determining a business’s true profit margins.
Errors in inventory accounting can lead to significant discrepancies in the balance sheet.
The company improved its financial reporting by implementing a new inventory accounting system.
