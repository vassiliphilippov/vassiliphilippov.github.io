dollar diplomacy

noun
use of financial power in diplomacy
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑlɚ dɪˈploʊməsi / (AmE), / ˈdɒlə dɪˈpləʊməsi / (BrE)
the use of a country's financial power to extend its international influence, especially by encouraging investments and loans to other countries

Examples

Dollar diplomacy policy
Critics argue that dollar diplomacy can undermine local economies in developing countries.
The United States practiced dollar diplomacy in Latin America during the early 20th century.
The ambassador's strategy was characterized more by dollar diplomacy than by military intervention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gunboat diplomacy - the use or threat of military force in international relations
