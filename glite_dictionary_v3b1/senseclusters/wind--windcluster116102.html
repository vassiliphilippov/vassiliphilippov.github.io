wind

noun
a force or tendency influencing events (metaphorical)
Pronunciation: / wɪnd / (AmE), / wɪnd / (BrE)
a tendency, influence, or force that causes or drives change or affects the course of events, often used metaphorically

Examples

Wind of change
Many sensed the wind of discontent blowing through the community.
The wind of reform swept through the company, leading to major changes.
The politician spoke of the winds of opportunity shaping the future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: current - a flow or movement of energy or tendency influencing a situation
