lift

verb
remove root crops from soil
Pronunciation: / lɪft / (AmE), / lɪft / (BrE)
to remove plants or root crops from the ground for harvesting or transplanting

Examples

To lift potatoes
After days of rain, it was finally dry enough to lift the potatoes from the field.
This year, the farmers will lift the carrots before the first frost arrives.
Workers lifted the beets carefully to avoid damaging their roots.
Gardeners often lift tulip bulbs after the leaves have died back.
In autumn, they usually lift and store dahlias to protect them from frost.
It is best to lift young seedlings carefully to avoid damaging the roots.
