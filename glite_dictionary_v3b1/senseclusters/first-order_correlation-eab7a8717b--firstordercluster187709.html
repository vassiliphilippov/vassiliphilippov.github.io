first-order correlation

noun
partial correlation controlling one variable
8.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ùst Àå…îrd…ö Àåk…îr.…ôÀàle…™. É…ôn / (AmE), / Ààf…úÀêst Àå…îÀê.d…ô Àåk…ír.…ôÀàle…™. É…ôn / (BrE)
a partial correlation in which the influence of only one variable is controlled or held constant.

Examples

A first-order correlation analysis
A first-order correlation helps isolate the influence of a single extraneous variable in statistical analysis.
By removing the effect of income, the first-order correlation between education and health became clearer.
The researcher calculated the first-order correlation to examine the relationship after controlling for age.

  • synonym: partial correlation - a correlation between two variables while controlling for the effect of other variables
