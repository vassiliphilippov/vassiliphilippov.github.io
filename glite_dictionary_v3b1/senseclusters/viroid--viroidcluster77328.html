viroid

noun
infectious agent with circular RNA, affects plants
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪ.rɔɪd / (BrE)
A type of infectious agent consisting of a short strand of circular RNA without a protein coat, known to cause disease in plants.

Examples

A viroid infection
Because a viroid contains only RNA, it lacks the protein coat found in typical viruses.
Certain potato diseases are caused by a viroid rather than a conventional virus.
Researchers discovered a new type of viroid affecting tomato plants in South America.
