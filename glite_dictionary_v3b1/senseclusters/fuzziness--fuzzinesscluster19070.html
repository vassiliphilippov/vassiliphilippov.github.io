fuzziness

noun
state of being indistinct or blurred
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌzinəs / (AmE), / ˈfʌzinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being indistinct, blurred, or lacking sharp outlines or clarity

Examples

Image fuzziness
Scientists worked to reduce the fuzziness of the telescope's images.
The fuzziness of the photograph made it hard to recognize anyone in the picture.
There's a certain fuzziness in the rules, which confuses new employees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to see or understand
