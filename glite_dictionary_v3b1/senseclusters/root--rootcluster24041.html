root

verb
originate or have as a cause
5.2 15/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀêt / (AmE), / ruÀêt / (BrE)
(of ideas, problems, etc.) to have as an origin or be caused by something; to originate or be established.

Examples

Problems root in fear
His fear of public speaking roots in a childhood embarrassment.
Many social issues root in systemic inequality.
Their tradition rooted in centuries-old cultural practices.

Synonyms

  • stem
    3.9 13/m
    stem means to arise from a particular cause or situation, while root means to be firmly established as the origin.
  • originate
    3.8 16/m
    originate means to begin or cause something to begin, while root means to be established with a deep origin in something.

Related Terms

  • synonym: originate - to have a specified beginning; to start
