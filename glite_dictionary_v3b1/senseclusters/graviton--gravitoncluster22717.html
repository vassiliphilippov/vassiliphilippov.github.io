graviton

noun
hypothetical quantum particle of gravity
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrævɪˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪtɒn / (BrE)
a hypothetical elementary particle that mediates the force of gravity in quantum theories of gravity

Examples

The graviton particle
If the graviton exists, it could help unite general relativity with quantum mechanics.
Physicists propose that the graviton would carry the force of gravity at the quantum level.
The existence of the graviton remains theoretical as it has never been directly detected.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: boson - category of particles, including force carriers like photons and gluons
  • synonym: photon - elementary particle mediating electromagnetic force
