parallax

noun
apparent shift in position due to viewpoint change
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛr.əˌlæks / (AmE), / ˈpær.əlæks / (BrE)
The apparent shift in the position of an object when viewed from two different places that are not in a straight line with the object, used to measure distance in astronomy and other sciences.

Examples

Astronomical parallax
Because of parallax, the position of the moon appears to shift depending on where you stand on Earth.
Photographers adjust for parallax error when using rangefinder cameras.
The distance to nearby stars can be measured using parallax techniques.
