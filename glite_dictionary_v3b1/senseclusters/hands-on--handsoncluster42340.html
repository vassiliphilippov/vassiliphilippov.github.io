hands-on

adjective
involving active participation
Pronunciation: / ˈhændz ɑn / (AmE), / ˈhændz ɒn / (BrE)
involving active participation rather than just theory or observation; practical

Examples

A hands-on manager
As a hands-on manager, he is always involved with his team.
She prefers a hands-on approach when teaching her students.
The training program offers a hands-on experience with real equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: practical - involving actual doing or use rather than theory
  • antonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application
