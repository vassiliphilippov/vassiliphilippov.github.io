Protestant Church

noun
Christian denominations from the Reformation
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊt̬.ɪ.stənt tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɒt.ɪ.stənt tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the collective body of Christian churches and denominations that separated from the Roman Catholic Church during the Reformation and adhere to Protestant doctrines

Examples

The Protestant Church tradition
Many countries have both Catholic and Protestant Church traditions.
She decided to study the history of the Protestant Church for her thesis.
The Protestant Church has played a significant role in shaping Western education and culture.
