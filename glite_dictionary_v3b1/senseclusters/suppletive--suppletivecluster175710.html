suppletive

adjective
irregular word forms
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈpliːtɪv / (AmE), / səˈpliːtɪv / (BrE)
describing a word form that is replaced by a completely different form in its inflectional paradigm

Examples

Suppletive verb forms
The verb 'be' is suppletive because its forms include 'am', 'is', and 'are'.
In English, 'go' and 'went' show a suppletive relationship in their verb forms.
A suppletive adjective is one whose comparative or superlative form comes from a different root.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irregular - not following the usual rules of grammar or inflection
