juice

verb
to boost artificially
7.8 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / d íuÀês / (AmE), / d íuÀês / (BrE)
to artificially increase or boost something, especially by manipulating numbers or results to make them appear better.

Examples

To juice the numbers
The company juiced its earnings report to impress potential investors.
Some athletes have juiced their performance statistics to gain sponsorships.
He admitted that he juiced the numbers to make the project look more successful.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inflate - to increase something, often dishonestly or artificially
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence something, often in a dishonest way
  • antonym: reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount
