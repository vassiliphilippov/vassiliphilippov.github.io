Landsknecht

noun
mercenary-like person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlændzˌknɛkt / (AmE), / ˈlændzˌknɛkt / (BrE)
a person who acts like a mercenary, motivated mainly by personal gain rather than principles, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

A Landsknecht politician
Some accused the politician of being a Landsknecht who changed sides for personal benefit.
In the business world, a true Landsknecht will work for whoever pays the most.
He was called a Landsknecht because he seemed to care only about profit, not values.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealist - a person guided by principles or ideals rather than personal gain
  • synonym: mercenary - a person motivated only by money, not by loyalty or ideals
