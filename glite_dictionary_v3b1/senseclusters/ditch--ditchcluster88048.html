ditch

verb
cut a trench or channel (for drainage)
3.3
Pronunciation: / dɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪtʃ / (BrE)
to cut a trench or channel in (land), especially to drain water away

Examples

To ditch a field
Before planting, the workers ditched the land to install proper drainage systems.
Farmers often ditch their fields in spring to prevent standing water after heavy rains.
The construction crew is ditching the roadside to guide rainwater away from the highway.
