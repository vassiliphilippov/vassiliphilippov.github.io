spinal cord

noun
major nerve pathway in the spine
3.4 3.35/m
Pronunciation: / Ààspa…™.n…ôl k…îrd / (AmE), / Ààspa…™.n…ôl k…îÀêd / (BrE)
the cylindrical bundle of nerve fibers and associated tissue that is enclosed in the spine and connects nearly all parts of the body to the brain, forming the central nervous system

Examples

The spinal cord injury
A serious injury to the spinal cord can cause paralysis below the site of damage.
Doctors monitor the health of the spinal cord during delicate back surgeries.
The spinal cord transmits signals between the brain and the rest of the body.

Synonyms
