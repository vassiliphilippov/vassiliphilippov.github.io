xerophytic plant

noun
plant adapted to dry conditions
5.7 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌzɪrəˈfɪtɪk plænt / (AmE), / ˌzɪərəˈfɪtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant adapted to survive with limited water, typically found in arid or dry habitats.

Examples

A xerophytic plant survives drought
Botanists study how a xerophytic plant stores water to survive long droughts.
Many xerophytic plants have thick leaves or waxy coatings to reduce water loss.
The cactus is a classic example of a xerophytic plant found in deserts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mesophyte - plant adapted to moderate moisture conditions
