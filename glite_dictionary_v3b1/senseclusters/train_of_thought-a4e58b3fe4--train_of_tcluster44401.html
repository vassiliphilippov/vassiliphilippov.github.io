train of thought

noun
connected sequence of thoughts or ideas
Pronunciation: / treɪn əv θɔt / (AmE), / treɪn əv θɔːt / (BrE)
a series of connected ideas or thoughts that follow one another in a person's mind, especially while speaking or reasoning

Examples

Clear train of thought
It's helpful to write an outline if you want your train of thought to be clear and logical.
She paused for a moment, struggling to regain her train of thought after the sudden interruption.
The noise outside completely broke his train of thought and he had to start the explanation again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thread - an idea or theme that connects the parts of a discussion or argument
