scribble

verb
write carelessly or untidily
Pronunciation: / ˈskrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪbəl / (BrE)
to write or draw something quickly and carelessly, often making it hard to read or recognize

Examples

To scribble a note
He tends to scribble his ideas on napkins during meetings.
She scribbled a quick note before leaving the house.
The teacher could barely read the homework because it was scribbled so messily.
He scribbled his phone number on a scrap of paper for me.
She grabbed a pen and scribbled a note before rushing out the door.
Stop scribbling on your homework and try to write more neatly.
During the meeting, he kept scribbling notes in the margins of his notebook.
Children often scribble on walls when they find a marker or crayon.
She scribbled her phone number on a scrap of paper and handed it to me.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neatly - in a tidy or orderly manner
  • antonym: neatly write - to write in a clear and tidy manner
  • synonym: scrawl - to write in a hurried, careless, or untidy way
