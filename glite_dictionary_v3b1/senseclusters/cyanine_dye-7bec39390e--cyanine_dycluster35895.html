cyanine dye

noun
class of synthetic photographic dyes
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪəˌnin daɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪəniːn daɪ / (BrE)
any of a class of synthetic dyes containing a -CH= group linking two nitrogen-containing heterocyclic rings, used especially as sensitizers in photography.

Examples

Cyanine dye formulas
Researchers are experimenting with new cyanine dye formulas for improved imaging techniques.
The intense color of the cyanine dye makes it valuable in biological staining.
The scientist explained how a cyanine dye can be used to sensitize photographic film.
