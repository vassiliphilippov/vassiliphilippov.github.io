claim jumper

noun
person illegally occupying another's claim
4.1 7.8
Pronunciation: /kleɪm ˈdʒʌm.pər/ (AmE), /kleɪm ˈdʒʌm.pə/ (BrE)
a person who illegally occupies or takes possession of land, especially a mining claim, to which another person has a legal right

Examples

Claim jumpers seize land
During the gold rush, claim jumpers often tried to seize others' hard-earned land.
He guarded his territory carefully to keep out potential claim jumpers.
The court ruled against the claim jumper and restored the property to its rightful owner.
