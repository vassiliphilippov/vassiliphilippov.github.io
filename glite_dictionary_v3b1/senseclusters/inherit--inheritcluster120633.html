inherit

verb
receive from someone after death
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈher.ət/ /; / /ɪn.ˈher.ɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈher.ɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈher.ət/ (BrE)
to receive money, property, a title, or other things (including problems or characteristics) from someone who had them before, especially after their death or from a previous generation

Examples

To inherit a house
After his aunt passed away, he inherited her old farmhouse and land.
Many children inherit fortunes from their parents when they die.
She hopes to inherit her grandmother's jewelry collection some day.
After the merger, the company inherited several unresolved legal disputes.
The new CEO inherited a team of talented but unmotivated employees from her predecessor.
We inherited many traditions and values from our grandparents.
