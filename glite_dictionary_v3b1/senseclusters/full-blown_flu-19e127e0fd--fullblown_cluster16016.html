full-blown flu

noun
severe, advanced influenza
Pronunciation: / fʊl bloʊn flu / (AmE), / fʊl bləʊn fluː / (BrE)
A case of influenza that is fully developed and severe, showing all the major symptoms.

Examples

A full-blown flu case
After a few days of sore throat and sniffles, she realized she had full-blown flu.
Missing the meeting was inevitable once the full-blown flu set in.
The doctor confirmed that his symptoms indicated a case of full-blown flu.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influenza - an infectious viral disease with fever and aches
  • degree lower: mild flu - a less severe case of influenza
