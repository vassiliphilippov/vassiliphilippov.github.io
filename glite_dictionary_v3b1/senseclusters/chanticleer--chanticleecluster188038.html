chanticleer

noun
literary rooster
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæntɪˌklɪr / (AmE), / ˈʃæntɪklɪə / (BrE)
an old or literary word for a rooster, especially as used in fables or stories

Examples

A proud chanticleer
In the old tale, chanticleer outsmarted the cunning fox with his quick thinking.
The fable describes how chanticleer greeted the sunrise with a proud crow.
Children listened as the storyteller spoke of chanticleer and his adventures in the barnyard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hen - an adult female chicken
  • synonym: rooster - an adult male chicken
