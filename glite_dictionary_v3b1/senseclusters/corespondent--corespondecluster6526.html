corespondent

noun
person accused of adultery in divorce case
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊ.rɪˈspɑːn.dənt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.rɪˈspɒn.dənt / (BrE)
a person named as a party in a divorce suit for allegedly having committed adultery with the defendant's spouse

Examples

The corespondent in divorce
Historically, being named as a corespondent could have serious social consequences.
In court, the corespondent denied any wrongdoing and refused to testify.
The divorce petition named a corespondent who was alleged to have had an affair with the wife.
