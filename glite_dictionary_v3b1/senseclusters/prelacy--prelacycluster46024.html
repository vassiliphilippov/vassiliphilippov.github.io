prelacy

noun
office or rank of a prelate
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛləsi / (AmE), / ˈprɛləsi / (BrE)
the office, rank, or system of church government by prelates (high-ranking church officials)

Examples

Church prelacy system
Disputes over prelacy were common during church reformations.
He rose rapidly within the church, eventually attaining a high prelacy.
The bishop held his prelacy with great dignity and devotion.
He criticized the corruption he perceived within the Catholic prelacy.
Many reforms aimed to reduce the power of the prelacy in church affairs.
The medieval church was dominated by the influence of the prelacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: episcopacy - the government of a church by bishops
  • antonym: laity - ordinary people, as distinct from the clergy
  • synonym: prelate - a high-ranking member of the clergy
