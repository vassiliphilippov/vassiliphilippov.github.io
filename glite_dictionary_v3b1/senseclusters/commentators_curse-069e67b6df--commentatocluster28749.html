commentator's curse

noun
event where praise by a commentator brings bad luck
7.3 8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmənˌteɪtɚz ˌkɝːs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənˌteɪtəz ˌkɜːs / (BrE)
the situation in which a commentator's praise is immediately followed by an unexpected or poor performance, suggesting the praise caused bad luck

Examples

The commentator's curse
Many fans blamed the defeat on the commentator's curse after the player missed an easy goal just after being praised.
She refused to say anything positive, fearing the commentator's curse might strike her team again.
The commentator's curse is a widely recognized phenomenon in sports broadcasting.
