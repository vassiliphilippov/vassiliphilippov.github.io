aspirate

verb
inhale liquid, air, or substances
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈæspɚˌeɪt /; / ˈæspəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈaspɪreɪt /; / ˈæspɪreɪt / (BrE)
to accidentally breathe in air, liquid, or another substance into the lungs

Examples

To aspirate food
Doctors warn that young children can aspirate small objects if not supervised.
If food is not swallowed properly, it can be aspirated into the lungs and cause infection.
The patient accidentally aspirated some water while swimming.
The patient accidentally aspirated some water while drinking too quickly.
Doctors warn that young children can easily aspirate small objects like peanuts or beads.
If you aspirate food, it can lead to a serious lung infection called aspiration pneumonia.
