hard water

noun
water with high mineral content
8.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /hɑːrd ˈwɔː.tər/ /; / /hɑːrd ˈwɑː.tər/ (AmE), /hɑːd ˈwɔː.tə/ (BrE)
water that contains high levels of calcium and magnesium salts, which prevent soap from lathering easily

Examples

Hard water minerals
Laundry detergent doesn't work as well with hard water, so clothes may not come out as clean.
She installed a filter to reduce the effects of hard water in her home.
The excessive mineral content in hard water can cause buildup in pipes and household appliances.
