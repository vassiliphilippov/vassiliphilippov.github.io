flexion

noun
bending of a joint
1.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈflek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈflek.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or state of bending a limb or joint, or the position in which it is bent

Examples

Deep flexion of spine
During yoga, deep flexion of the spine should be performed carefully to avoid injury.
Reduced flexion in the fingers can indicate arthritis.
The physiotherapist measured the flexion of her knee after the surgery.
He experienced pain during flexion of his wrist.
Proper flexion of the elbow is essential for lifting weights safely.
The doctor measured the patient's knee flexion after surgery.
In yoga, certain poses require deep flexion of the spine and hips.
Limited flexion in the knee can make walking or climbing stairs difficult.
The physiotherapist measured the flexion of the patient's elbow during the exercise.
