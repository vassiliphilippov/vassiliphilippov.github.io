fibrinolytic

noun
clot-dissolving agent
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbrənoʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbrɪnəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
a drug or enzyme that promotes the breakdown of fibrin in blood clots

Examples

A fibrinolytic drug
The doctor administered a fibrinolytic to help dissolve the patient's blood clot quickly.
Researchers are developing new fibrinolytics to improve stroke treatment outcomes.
A single dose of fibrinolytic therapy can be life-saving in cases of acute heart attack.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticoagulant - a drug that prevents blood clotting
  • synonym: thrombolytic - a drug that dissolves blood clots
