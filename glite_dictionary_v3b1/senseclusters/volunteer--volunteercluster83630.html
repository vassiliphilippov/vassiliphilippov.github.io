volunteer

verb
offer to do something willingly
2.0 17/m
Pronunciation: / Àåv…ëÀêl…ônÀàt…™r / (AmE), / Àåv…íl…ônÀàt…™…ô / (BrE)
to offer to do something, give help, or provide information willingly and without being asked or paid

Examples

To volunteer for work
After hearing about the shortage, several students volunteered to help in the cafeteria.
No one asked him, but he volunteered to take notes during the meeting.
She volunteered to organize the community clean-up event.
Many people volunteer at animal shelters to help care for homeless pets.
She decided to volunteer her time at the local food bank over the summer.
Students are encouraged to volunteer for community service projects during the school year.
During the meeting, he volunteered the details of the project willingly.
She volunteered her opinion even though no one had asked.
The suspect volunteered important information to the police.

Synonyms

  • come forward
    3.2 12/m

  • offer
    1.0 93/m
    offer is to say you are willing to do or give something in many situations, while volunteer means to do so willingly without pay.
