downshift

noun
slowdown in activity or growth
5.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8da\u028an\u02cc\u0283\u026aft / (AmE), / \u02c8da\u028an\u0283\u026aft / (BrE)
a slowdown or deceleration in growth, output, demand, or activity in a business, market, economy, or sector.

Examples

A business downshift
After the pandemic, the company experienced a clear downshift in production and sales this quarter.
Analysts warned of a global downshift in demand that could slow economic recovery next year.
The retailer cited a downshift in consumer spending after interest rates rose sharply.

Synonyms

  • downturn
    4.4 5.61/m
    downturn is a period when economic activity falls, while downshift is a slower deceleration in growth or demand.
  • downswing
    6.1 0.10/m
    downswing is a cyclical fall in business activity, while downshift is a milder slowdown in growth or output.
  • downtrend
    3.9 0.10/m
    downtrend is a steady fall in prices or activity over time, while downshift is a slowdown in growth or momentum.
  • downslide
    5.1 0.03/m
    downslide is a general period of decline in quality or activity, while downshift means a slowing of growth or demand.
  • slump
    4.1 1.10/m
    slump is a sudden large drop in value or activity, while downshift is a softer slowing of growth or demand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upswing - an increase or improvement in economic activity; opposite of a slowdown
