whitespace

noun
blank text characters
4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪt.speɪs/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪt.speɪs/ (BrE)
the characters in text, such as spaces, tabs, or newlines, that create gaps or separate words and are often ignored by computers

Examples

Remove whitespace characters
The program automatically removes extra whitespace from the beginning and end of each line.
If you accidentally add whitespace in your password, it might not work.
Some coding standards require that no trailing whitespace appears at the end of a line.
