aliphatic

adjective
having carbon atoms in open chains
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈfætɪk / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈfætɪk / (BrE)
(of organic compounds) having carbon atoms arranged in open chains rather than rings.

Examples

Aliphatic hydrocarbons
Alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes are all classified as aliphatic hydrocarbons.
Most fuels contain a mixture of aliphatic and aromatic hydrocarbons.
The chemist analyzed the aliphatic compounds present in the sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aromatic - (in chemistry) containing a ring of carbon atoms with delocalized electrons
