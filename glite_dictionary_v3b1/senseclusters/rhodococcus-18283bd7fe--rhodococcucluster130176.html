Rhodococcus

proper noun
soil bacteria genus
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊdəˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a genus of bacteria commonly found in soil, some species of which can cause disease in animals and humans

Examples

Rhodococcus bacteria
Rhodococcus is often found in soil and can sometimes infect animals.
Veterinarians are aware that Rhodococcus can cause pneumonia in young horses.
Researchers are studying how Rhodococcus species break down environmental pollutants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mycobacterium - a genus of bacteria that includes pathogens known to cause serious diseases
  • synonym: Streptococcus - a genus of spherical bacteria, some of which cause disease
