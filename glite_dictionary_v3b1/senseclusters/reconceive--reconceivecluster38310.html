reconceive

verb
to imagine differently
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈsiːv / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈsiːv / (BrE)
to think about, imagine, or plan something in a new or different way

Examples

To reconceive a plan
The architect decided to reconceive the building's design to make it more environmentally friendly.
After hearing new evidence, the scientist began to reconceive her original theory.
The museum has reconceived its main exhibit to attract a younger audience.
After hearing the feedback, the architect decided to reconceive the entire building design.
The director wanted to reconceive the classic play for a modern audience.
Sometimes it is necessary to reconceive your goals when circumstances change.

  • synonym: rethink - to think about something again, especially to reconsider or change it
