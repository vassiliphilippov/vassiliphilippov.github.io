high-octane

adjective
having a high octane rating (gasoline)
3.3
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈɑk.teɪn / (AmE), / haɪ ˈɒk.teɪn / (BrE)
(of gasoline) having a high octane rating; capable of withstanding high compression without knocking.

Examples

High-octane fuel
Because of its high-octane content, the fuel prevents knocking in high-performance vehicles.
Some modern engines are designed to run on high-octane gasoline for better efficiency.
The team uses only high-octane fuel to ensure the car performs at its best.
