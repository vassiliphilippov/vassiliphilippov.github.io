pigeonhole

verb
put into a small compartment or slot
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪdʒənˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪnhəʊl / (BrE)
to put or store something in a small compartment or designated slot, especially in a desk or shelving unit

Examples

To pigeonhole documents
He pigeonholes notes and receipts in the tiny compartments of his desk.
She carefully placed each letter into its own pigeonhole in the organizer.
The librarian pigeonholed the returned books for sorting later.
