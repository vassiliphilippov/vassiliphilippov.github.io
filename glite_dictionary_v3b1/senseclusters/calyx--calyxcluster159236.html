calyx

noun
outer whorl of sepals in a flower
3.8 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkæl.ɪks/ /; / /ˈkeɪ.lɪks/ (AmE), /ˈkeɪ.lɪks/ /; / /ˈkæl.ɪks/ (BrE)
(Botany) The outer whorl of sepals of a flower, collectively forming a protective layer around the developing bud, usually green in color.

Examples

The calyx of flower
Each calyx is made up of sepals arranged in a circle around the base of the flower.
If the calyx is damaged, the developing bud may not open properly.
The rose's calyx protects the delicate petals before the flower blooms.
