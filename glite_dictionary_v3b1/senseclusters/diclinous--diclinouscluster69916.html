diclinous

adjective
having stamens and pistils in separate flowers (botany)
Pronunciation: / daɪˈklɪnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈklɪnəs / (BrE)
(of a plant) having stamens and pistils in separate flowers, rather than together in the same flower.

Examples

Diclinous plants
In diclinous plants, one flower bears only stamens, while another bears only pistils.
Many willow species are diclinous, producing separate male and female flowers on the same or different plants.
Understanding whether a plant is diclinous is important in botanical classification and breeding.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dioecious - having male and female reproductive organs on separate individual plants
  • antonym: monoecious - having both male and female reproductive organs in the same plant
