indeterministic

adjective
involving randomness
9.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.dɪ.tər.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ /; / /ɪn.də.tər.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.tə.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ /; / /ɪn.də.tə.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
not having a single predictable outcome; involving randomness or chance in results or behavior

Examples

An indeterministic process
The results of the indeterministic process can vary each time you run the experiment.
Quantum mechanics is often described as an indeterministic theory because outcomes are not always predictable.
Unlike deterministic systems, indeterministic algorithms may produce different outputs for the same input.
