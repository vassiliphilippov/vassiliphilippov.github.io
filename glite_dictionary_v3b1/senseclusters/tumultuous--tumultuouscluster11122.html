tumultuous

adjective
full of disorder or chaos
7.5 2.73/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀêÀàm ålt Éu…ôs / (AmE), / tjuÀêÀàm åltj ä…ôs / (BrE)
full of confusion, disorder, or uncertainty; involving a lot of loud, excited activity, or strong feelings.

Examples

A tumultuous relationship
His tumultuous relationship with his parents shaped his outlook on life.
The city experienced a tumultuous period following the sudden resignation of its mayor.
There were cheers and applause as the singer took the stage to a tumultuous welcome.

Synonyms

    6.6 2.91/m

  • tempestuous
    8.4 0.48/m

  • riotous
    7.9 0.17/m
    riotous is wild, noisy disorder often with violence in public places, while tumultuous describes loud, chaotic activity or strong feelings.
  • troubled
    3.8 2.36/m
    troubled describes places or times with unrest or problems that cause worry, while tumultuous suggests loud, violent change or strong feelings.
