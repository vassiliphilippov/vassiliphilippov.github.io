war dance

noun
ceremonial dance before/after battle
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔr ˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌdɑːns / (BrE)
a ceremonial dance performed by a group, typically before battle or after a victory, especially among certain Indigenous peoples

Examples

A war dance ceremony
After their victory, the warriors broke into a celebratory war dance to honor their ancestors.
The museum displayed artifacts related to the Native American war dance tradition.
Tribal members gathered to perform the war dance before heading into battle.
