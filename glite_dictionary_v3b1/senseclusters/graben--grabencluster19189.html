graben

noun
sunken crust block
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɑːbən / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːbən / (BrE)
a long, narrow valley formed when a block of the earth's crust drops down between two faults

Examples

A tectonic graben
The East African Rift contains several large grabens formed by tectonic forces.
Geologists studied the graben to understand how the valley developed over millions of years.
A graben can create dramatic landscapes with steep cliffs on either side.
The East African Rift is a well-known graben that stretches for thousands of kilometers.
Geologists studied the formation of the graben to understand tectonic plate movements.
A graben often forms between two parallel faults, creating a distinct valley.

Related Terms

  • antonym: horst - an elevated block of the earth's crust between two faults
  • synonym: rift valley - a large elongated depression with steep walls formed by the downward displacement of a block of the earth's surface
