cholecystectomy

noun
surgical removal of the gall bladder
Pronunciation: / koʊ.lə.sɪsˈtɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.ɪ.sɪsˈtɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove the gall bladder, usually to treat gallstones or inflammation

Examples

A cholecystectomy surgery
A cholecystectomy is often performed when gallstones cause severe pain or complications.
After experiencing repeated gallstone attacks, her doctor recommended a cholecystectomy.
The patient recovered quickly after undergoing a laparoscopic cholecystectomy.
