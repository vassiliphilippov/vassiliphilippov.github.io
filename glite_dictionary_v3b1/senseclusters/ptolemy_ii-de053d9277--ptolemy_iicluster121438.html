Ptolemy II

proper noun
king of Egypt, 3rd century BC
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.lə.miː ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ˈtɑː.lə.mɪ ðə ˈsek.ənd/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.lə.mɪ ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ˈtɒ.lə.miː ðə ˈsek.ənd/ (BrE)
The son of Ptolemy I and king of Egypt from circa 283 to 246 BC, noted for promoting the translation of the Hebrew Bible into Greek (the Septuagint).

Examples

Ptolemy II king Egypt
During his reign, Ptolemy II oversaw major construction projects in Alexandria.
The cultural achievements of Ptolemy II's court attracted scholars from across the ancient world.
The translation of the Hebrew Bible into Greek, known as the Septuagint, was initiated under Ptolemy II.
