stall

noun
seat in the front part of a theater
Pronunciation: / stɔl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
a seat in the front part of the main floor of a theater, especially in the area nearest the stage

Examples

Theater stalls
The stalls offer a close-up view of the stage, making it a popular choice among theatergoers.
Tickets for the stalls are usually more expensive than those for the balcony.
We had excellent seats in the stalls for last night's performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orchestra - main floor seating area in an American theater
