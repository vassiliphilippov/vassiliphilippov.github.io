toppy

adjective
dominant at the top
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑpi / (AmE), / ˈtɒpi / (BrE)
having a prominent or dominant top part, or showing strong features at the beginning, especially in taste or financial markets

Examples

Toppy wine flavor
The wine was described as toppy because its strongest flavors came at the start.
Some investors worry the market is getting toppy and may soon decline.
The cake looked a bit toppy with most of the icing piled on the top layer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: balanced - having equal or proper amounts of all parts
  • synonym: top-heavy - having more weight or emphasis at the top
