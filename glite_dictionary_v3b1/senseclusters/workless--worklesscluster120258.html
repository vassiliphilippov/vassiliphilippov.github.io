workless

adjective
idle or unused
5.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…úÀêrkl…ôs / (AmE), / Ààw…úÀêkl…ôs / (BrE)
not active, operating, or producing work; idle or unused, especially referring to machines, factories, or periods of time

Examples

Workless factory
The factory remained workless for months after the economic downturn.
During the holiday, the usually busy machines were completely workless.
A workless period can be challenging for both workers and management.

Synonyms

  • idle
    2.8 4.10/m
    idle is commonly used for people or machines that sit unused or waiting, while workless stresses not producing work.
  • inactive
    2.5 1.77/m
    inactive describes something not in operation or use over time, while workless often refers to not producing work.
  • unworked
    4.2 0.00/m
    unworked refers to things or time that have not been used or worked on, while workless means not operating or producing work.
  • nonworking
    3.0 0.01/m
    nonworking describes days or periods when no business or work is planned, while workless focuses on not operating or producing work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idle - not active or in use
