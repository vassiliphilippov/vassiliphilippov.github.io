scaremongering

noun
spreading fear
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈskeə.r.mʌŋ.gər.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈskɛr.mʌŋ.gər.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈskeə.mʌŋ.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of deliberately spreading exaggerated or unnecessary fear about something

Examples

Political scaremongering
The politician was accused of scaremongering to gain support for stricter laws.
Some news outlets have been criticized for scaremongering about health risks.
Experts warned that scaremongering could lead to unnecessary panic among the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarmism - the tendency to exaggerate dangers or risks
  • antonym: reassurance - the act of making someone feel less worried or afraid
