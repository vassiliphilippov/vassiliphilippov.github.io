stop

noun
the act of stopping something
Pronunciation: / stɑp / (AmE), / stɒp / (BrE)
the act, instance, or state of stopping, ceasing, or pausing

Examples

A sudden stop
A sudden stop in the assembly line caused production delays throughout the day.
The goalkeeper made an incredible stop to prevent the goal.
They called for an immediate stop to the construction work until safety could be assured.
After a long drive, we decided to make a stop at a roadside café for lunch.
The train made a brief stop at each station along the route.
We had a short stop in Paris before catching our connecting flight.
After the manager's announcement, the discussion came to an abrupt stop.
The train came to a stop just before the crossing.
There was a sudden stop in the music when the power went out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: halt - an act of stopping movement or activity
  • synonym: interruption - an act of stopping something for a time
  • synonym: pause - a temporary stop in action or speech
  • antonym: resumption - the act of beginning again after a stop
  • antonym: start - the beginning of something
  • synonym: stopover - an overnight or short stay in the course of a journey
  • synonym: stoppage - an instance or act of stopping; a cessation of activity
