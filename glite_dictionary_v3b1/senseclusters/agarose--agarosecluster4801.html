agarose

noun
laboratory gel substance
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡəˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˈæɡərəʊz / (BrE)
a jelly-like substance made from seaweed, used in laboratories as a gel to separate DNA, proteins, or other molecules

Examples

agarose gel
The scientist poured the melted agarose into a tray to form a gel for DNA analysis.
After running the experiment, the bands became visible in the agarose gel under UV light.
Students learned how to prepare a 1% agarose solution for their biology class experiment.
The scientist prepared an agarose gel to separate the DNA fragments by size.
After mixing the solution, she poured it into a tray to form an agarose gel.
Many molecular biology experiments rely on agarose for effective separation of biomolecules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agar - a gelatinous substance obtained from seaweed, used as a culture medium
  • synonym: polyacrylamide - a synthetic polymer used as a gel in laboratory techniques
