metazoan

noun
multicellular animal; non-protozoan
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈzoʊ.ən /; / ˌmɛtəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈzəʊ.ən /; / ˌmɛtəˈzəʊən / (BrE)
a multicellular animal (any animal with more than one cell, except protozoans and sponges)

Examples

A metazoan animal
Modern biologists study the development of metazoan embryos to understand multicellularity.
Most animals familiar to us, from insects to mammals, are classified as metazoan organisms.
The discovery of ancient metazoan fossils has shed light on the evolution of early animals.
All mammals, birds, and insects are classified as metazoans because they have multiple cell types.
Scientists study the development of metazoans to understand how complex life evolved.
Sponges are among the simplest metazoans found in the animal kingdom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animal - a living organism that feeds on organic matter
  • antonym: protozoan - single-celled organism of the former group Protozoa
