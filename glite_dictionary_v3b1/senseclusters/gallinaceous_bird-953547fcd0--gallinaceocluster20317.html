gallinaceous bird

noun
heavy, ground-dwelling bird (e.g. chicken, turkey)
Pronunciation: / ˌɡælɪˈneɪʃəs bɜrd / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈneɪʃəs bɜːd / (BrE)
A heavy-bodied, largely ground-dwelling bird such as a chicken, turkey, or pheasant, typically kept for food or sport.

Examples

A gallinaceous bird species
Many gallinaceous birds spend much of their lives foraging on the ground for seeds and insects.
The chicken is perhaps the most familiar gallinaceous bird in the world.
Wild turkeys are a native North American gallinaceous bird species prized by hunters.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: domestic fowl - birds like chickens kept for eggs and meat
  • synonym: game bird - wild birds hunted for sport or food
