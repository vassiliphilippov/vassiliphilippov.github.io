microphthalmos

noun
abnormally small eye
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊfˈθælmoʊs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrɒfˈθælmɒs / (BrE)
a congenital condition in which one or both eyes are abnormally small

Examples

Congenital microphthalmos condition
The specialist confirmed that the baby had microphthalmos after several detailed eye tests.
Children born with microphthalmos may require special care to support their vision.
Surgery can sometimes help improve appearance in cases of microphthalmos.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anophthalmia - absence of one or both eyes
  • antonym: macrophthalmia - abnormally large eyes
