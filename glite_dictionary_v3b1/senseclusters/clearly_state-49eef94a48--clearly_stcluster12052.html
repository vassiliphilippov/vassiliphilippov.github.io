clearly state

verb
express unambiguously
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrli steɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɪəli steɪt / (BrE)
to express something in a way that is easy to understand and leaves no doubt or ambiguity

Examples

To clearly state expectations
She clearly stated her expectations for the new project during the meeting.
The instructions clearly state that you must wear safety gloves at all times.
The policy clearly states that refunds are not available after 30 days.

Related Terms

  • antonym: imply - suggest without stating directly
  • synonym: specify - state something explicitly or in detail
