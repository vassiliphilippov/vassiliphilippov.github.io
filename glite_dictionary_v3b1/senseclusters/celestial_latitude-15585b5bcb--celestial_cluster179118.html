celestial latitude

noun
angular distance from celestial equator (astronomy)
6.2 7.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈles.tʃəl ˈlæt.ɪ.tuːd/ /; / /sə.ˈles.tʃəl ˈlæt.ə.tuːd/ (AmE), /sə.ˈles.tʃəl ˈlæt.ɪ.tjuːd/ /; / /sə.ˈles.tɪ.əl ˈlæt.ɪ.tjuːd/ (BrE)
(astronomy) the angular distance of a celestial body north or south of the celestial equator, measured in degrees; used with right ascension to specify locations in the sky.

Examples

Celestial latitude angle
Astronomers use celestial latitude and right ascension to pinpoint the positions of stars in the sky.
Changes in a star's celestial latitude can indicate its movement relative to the celestial equator.
The celestial latitude of the planet was measured to determine its exact location above the horizon.
