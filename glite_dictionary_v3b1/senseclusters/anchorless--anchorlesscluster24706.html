anchorless

adjective
without a regular host
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋkərləs / (AmE), / ˈæŋkələs / (BrE)
without a regular presenter or anchorperson leading a program, especially a news show or broadcast.

Examples

Anchorless news show
After the main presenter resigned, the news program became anchorless and featured different reporters each night.
The network experimented with an anchorless format to give more voices a chance to present the news.
Some viewers found the anchorless show less engaging without a familiar face leading the broadcast.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hosted - led or presented by a regular host or anchor
