up to mischief

adjective
engaging in playful trouble
7.0
Pronunciation: / ʌp tə ˈmɪstʃɪf / (AmE), / ʌp tə ˈmɪstʃɪf / (BrE)
engaged in playful or mildly troublesome behavior, often in a way that is not serious or malicious

Examples

Boys up to mischief
The teacher caught the boys up to mischief in the hallway during recess.
Those two are always up to mischief when they're together.
Whenever she was quiet for too long, her mother suspected she was up to mischief.
