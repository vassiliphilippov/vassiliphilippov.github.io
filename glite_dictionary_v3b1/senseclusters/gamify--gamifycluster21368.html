gamify

verb
to add game elements
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪməˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪməˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to add game-like features or elements to an activity or process to make it more engaging or motivating

Examples

To gamify a program
The company decided to gamify its employee wellness program to increase participation.
Teachers often gamify lessons to make learning more fun and interactive for students.
Many apps gamify daily tasks by adding points and rewards to keep users motivated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or unengaged
  • synonym: game - an activity with rules and objectives for entertainment or competition
  • synonym: motivate - to encourage someone to take action or participate
