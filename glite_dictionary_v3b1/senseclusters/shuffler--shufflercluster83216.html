shuffler

noun
person who drags feet when walking
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʌflər / (AmE), / ˈʃʌflə / (BrE)
a person who walks without lifting their feet properly from the ground, often dragging or sliding them.

Examples

Slow shuffler
As a shuffler, he often wore out the soles of his shoes quickly.
The old man was a habitual shuffler, dragging his feet along the corridor each morning.
The physical therapist worked with the patient to help him stop being a shuffler.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: walker - a person who walks, especially for exercise or recreation
