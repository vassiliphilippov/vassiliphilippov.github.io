varicose vein

noun
permanently enlarged, twisted vein
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈværɪˌkoʊs veɪn / (AmE), / ˈværɪkəʊs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein, typically in the leg, that is permanently enlarged and twisted due to weakened valves and walls

Examples

A varicose vein in leg
After standing all day at work, she noticed a varicose vein developing on her leg.
Many people experience discomfort and swelling associated with a varicose vein.
Treatment options for a varicose vein range from wearing compression stockings to surgical procedures.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: spider vein - a smaller, milder form of varicose vein
