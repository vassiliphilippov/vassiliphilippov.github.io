homopterous insect

noun
insect with membranous wings (ex-Order Homoptera)
9.9
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑːptəɹəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒptərəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (BrE)
an insect belonging to the former order Homoptera, typically having membranous forewings and hind wings, such as aphids, cicadas, and leafhoppers.

Examples

Homopterous insects like aphids
Many species of homopterous insects are considered agricultural pests due to their feeding habits.
Scientists studied the wing structure of each homopterous insect collected from the field.
The homopterous insect group includes familiar creatures like cicadas and aphids.
