wild parsnip

noun
a poisonous wild plant, ancestor of cultivated parsnip
7.4 6.8
Pronunciation: /waɪld ˈpɑːr.snɪp/ (AmE), /waɪld ˈpɑː.snɪp/ (BrE)
a biennial plant (Pastinaca sativa) found in Europe and America, having large, pinnate leaves, yellow flowers, and a bitter, somewhat poisonous root; the ancestor of the cultivated parsnip

Examples

Wild parsnip plant
Many hikers avoid touching wild parsnip because its sap can cause skin irritation.
The fields near the river are covered with blooming wild parsnip each summer.
Unlike the domesticated version, wild parsnip has a root that is too bitter to eat.
