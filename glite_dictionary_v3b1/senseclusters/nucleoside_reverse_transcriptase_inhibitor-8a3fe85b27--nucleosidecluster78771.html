nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor

noun
antiviral drugs that block HIV replication
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈnuː.kliː.oʊ.saɪd rɪ.ˈvɜːrs træn.ˈskrɪp.teɪs ɪn.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈnjuː.kliː.oʊ.saɪd rɪ.ˈvɜːrs træn.ˈskrɪp.teɪs ɪn.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈnjuː.kliː.əʊ.saɪd rɪ.ˈvɜːs træn.ˈskrɪp.teɪs ɪn.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a class of antiviral drugs used to treat HIV infection; these drugs are incorporated into viral DNA and block the reverse transcriptase enzyme, preventing the virus from replicating
