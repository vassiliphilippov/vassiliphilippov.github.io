herring

noun
edible flesh of a small fish
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
a small, silver fish found in northern Atlantic and Pacific waters, often eaten as food (fresh, smoked, salted, or pickled)

Examples

Pickled herring
In Scandinavia, pickled herring is a traditional dish served during holidays.
She bought smoked herring at the market for dinner.
The appetizer included slices of salted herring with onions and bread.
Fishermen caught a large net full of herring off the Norwegian coast.
Smoked herring is a traditional delicacy in many northern European countries.
The herring migrate in vast schools during the spring and autumn.
