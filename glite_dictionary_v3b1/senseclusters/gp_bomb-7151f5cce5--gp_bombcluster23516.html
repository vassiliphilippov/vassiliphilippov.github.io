GP bomb

noun
a general-purpose aerial bomb
Pronunciation: / dʒi pi bɑm / (AmE), / dʒiː piː bɒm / (BrE)
a general-purpose aerial bomb weighing between 500 and 2,000 pounds, designed to cause both blast and fragmentation effects

Examples

A GP bomb explosion
A GP bomb can inflict significant damage on both structures and personnel.
During the war, GP bombs were frequently used in air raids.
The aircraft was loaded with a 1,000-pound GP bomb for the mission.
