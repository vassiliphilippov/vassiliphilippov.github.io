blasting

noun
use of explosives
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈblæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of using explosives to break apart rock, earth, or other material, especially in mining or construction

Examples

Rock blasting
The blasting at the construction site started early in the morning and lasted for several hours.
Strict safety measures are required during blasting to protect workers and nearby residents.
After the blasting, the workers cleared away the broken rocks from the tunnel entrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detonation - the act of causing something to explode
  • synonym: excavation - the process of removing earth, rock, or other material from a site
