attorney-client relation

noun
legal relationship between a lawyer and their client
Pronunciation: / əˈtɝːni ˈklaɪənt rəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈtɜːni ˈklaɪənt rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the legal relationship in which a lawyer has a duty to act in the best interests of their client

Examples

The attorney-client relation
A serious breach of the attorney-client relation may lead to professional disciplinary action.
The attorney-client relation is built on trust and confidentiality.
Understanding the attorney-client relation is essential for anyone seeking legal advice.
