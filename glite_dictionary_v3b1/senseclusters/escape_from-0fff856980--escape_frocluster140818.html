escape from

verb
get away from a pursuer or unwanted situation
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈskeɪp frʌm/ /; / /ɪ.ˈskeɪp frəm/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈskeɪp frɒm/ /; / /ɪ.ˈskeɪp frəm/ (BrE)
to get away from someone or something that is following, pursuing, or threatening you; to succeed in avoiding an unwanted situation or person.

Examples

To escape from danger
No matter how hard I tried, I couldn't escape from the noise outside my window.
She finally found a way to escape from her stressful job and start a new life.
The suspect managed to escape from the police by hiding in a crowded market.
