antipsychotic

noun
drug for treating psychotic disorders
9.8 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.saɪˈkɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.saɪˈkɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
A drug used to treat psychotic disorders by reducing symptoms such as delusions and hallucinations; often has a calming effect.

Examples

An antipsychotic drug
Many antipsychotic medications can cause side effects such as drowsiness or weight gain.
She responded well to the new antipsychotic after her symptoms became more severe.
The psychiatrist prescribed an antipsychotic to help manage his hallucinations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antidepressant - drug for treating depression, not psychosis
  • synonym: neuroleptic - another term for an antipsychotic drug
