Dichondra micrantha

noun
creeping herb used as grass substitute
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑndrə mɪˈkrænθə / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒndrə mɪˈkrænθə / (BrE)
a creeping perennial herb with hairy stems, round or kidney-shaped leaves, and small white to greenish flowers, often used as a grass substitute in warm regions

Examples

Dichondra micrantha plant
Gardeners often plant Dichondra micrantha as a low-maintenance alternative to traditional lawns.
In warm climates, Dichondra micrantha thrives where many grasses cannot survive.
The small, kidney-shaped leaves are a distinctive feature of Dichondra micrantha.
