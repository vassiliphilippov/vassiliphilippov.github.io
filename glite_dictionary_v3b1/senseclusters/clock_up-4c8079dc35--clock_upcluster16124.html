clock up

verb
accumulate a total amount; record (distance/time)
Pronunciation: / klɑk ʌp / (AmE), / klɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to reach or accumulate a total amount (such as distance, time, or experience) as a result of repeated actions or activity

Examples

To clock up miles
Our old car has clocked up 150,000 miles so far.
She hopes to clock up enough experience to apply for a promotion next year.
The pilot managed to clock up over 10,000 flying hours during his career.
After years of training, he finally clocked up enough experience to become a coach.
She hopes to clock up 1,000 hours of volunteering by next year.
The company has clocked up impressive profits this quarter.
He clocked up a record number of miles on his bicycle last year.
She clocked up over a hundred hours volunteering at the animal shelter.
The new trainee has already clocked up three months of experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pile up - to accumulate in a large quantity
  • synonym: rack up - to accumulate or achieve a number, score, amount, etc.
