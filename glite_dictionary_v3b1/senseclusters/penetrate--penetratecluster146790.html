penetrate

verb
spread or diffuse through
5.0 4.80/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆpÉ›nÉªËŒtreÉªt / (AmE), / ËˆpenÉªtreÉªt / (BrE)
(of a substance or influence) to spread or diffuse through something; to pass into or affect all parts of something.

Examples

To penetrate the crowd
A sense of hope penetrated the crowd despite the difficult circumstances.
The powerful aroma of freshly baked bread penetrated the entire house.
The sound of the alarm penetrated every corner of the building.

Synonyms

  • permeate
    6.0 2.85/m
    permeate spreads into and is present in every part, like a smell filling a room, while penetrate implies entering or affecting deeply.
  • pervade
    6.7 3.84/m
    pervade fills a place or feeling so you notice it everywhere, while penetrate focuses on passing into and affecting all parts.
  • diffuse
    5.5 4.17/m
    diffuse spreads something widely across an area or to many people, while penetrate means to pass into and affect every part.
