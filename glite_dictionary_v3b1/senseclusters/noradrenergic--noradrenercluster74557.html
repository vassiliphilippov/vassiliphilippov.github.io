noradrenergic

adjective
using norepinephrine
Pronunciation: / ˌnɔːrˌædrəˈnɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɔːrˌædrəˈnɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to nerve cells or processes that use norepinephrine as a neurotransmitter

Examples

The noradrenergic system
The noradrenergic system is important for regulating attention and arousal in the brain.
Certain antidepressants work by affecting noradrenergic pathways in the nervous system.
Researchers studied the noradrenergic response to stress in laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adrenergic - relating to nerve cells that use adrenaline (epinephrine) as a neurotransmitter
  • antonym: cholinergic - relating to nerve cells that use acetylcholine as a neurotransmitter
