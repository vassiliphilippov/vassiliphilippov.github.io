special interest

noun
a lobbying group seeking specific benefits
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃl ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
a group of people or an organization that tries to influence government decisions to benefit its own concerns, often through lobbying

Examples

Special interests group
Many politicians are accused of being influenced by special interests during election campaigns.
Some laws are shaped more by special interests than by the needs of ordinary citizens.
The environmental special interest lobbied Congress to pass stricter pollution controls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interest group - an organized group that tries to influence public policy
