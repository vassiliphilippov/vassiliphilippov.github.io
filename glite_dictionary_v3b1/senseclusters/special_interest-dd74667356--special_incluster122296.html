special interest

noun
a lobbying group seeking specific benefits
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃl ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
a group of people or an organization that tries to influence government decisions to benefit its own concerns, often through lobbying

Examples

Some laws are shaped more by special interests than by the needs of ordinary citizens.
