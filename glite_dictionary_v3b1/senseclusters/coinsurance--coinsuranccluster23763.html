coinsurance

noun
insurance jointly issued by multiple underwriters
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˌkəʊɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
a type of insurance in which two or more underwriters jointly cover the same risk or policy

Examples

Coinsurance agreement
Companies often prefer coinsurance when dealing with large or complex assets.
In certain cases, coinsurance allows insurers to share liability and minimize risk.
The property was protected by coinsurance held with several leading firms.
