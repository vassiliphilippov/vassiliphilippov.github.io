proliferative

adjective
rapidly growing
7.8
Pronunciation: / prəˈlɪfərətɪv / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfərətɪv / (BrE)
causing or characterized by rapid growth, increase, or multiplication (of cells, tissues, ideas, or things)

Examples

The artist entered a proliferative period, creating dozens of new paintings in just a few months.
Her proliferative imagination led to many innovative solutions during the brainstorming session.
In biology, a proliferative phase refers to rapid cell growth and division.
The doctor explained that the tumor was in a proliferative stage, growing more quickly than before.
Researchers are studying proliferative changes in tissue to better understand cancer development.
A proliferative response was observed in the patient's immune cells after treatment.
