funny

adjective
strange or unusual bodily sensation
2.0 20/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfÊŒni / (AmE), / ËˆfÊŒni / (BrE)
strange or unusual in a way that is not expected; odd or peculiar

Examples

Funny feeling
I started feeling a funny tingling in my left arm and got worried.
She noticed a funny numbness in her toes and called her doctor.
Sometimes I get a funny sensation in my stomach after eating spicy food.
She felt a funny sensation in her arm after the injection.
The milk tastes a bit funny; I think it might be spoiled.
There's something funny about the way he looked at me.

Synonyms

  • odd
    1.0 41/m
    odd describes something quietly different you notice in daily life, while funny names something surprisingly strange or unexpected.
  • strange
    2.3 84/m
    strange means unusual and a little hard to understand, while funny suggests oddness that catches you by surprise.
  • peculiar
    4.6 11/m
    peculiar sounds more formal and points to a clear unusual trait, while funny is a more general, odd surprise.
  • weird
    3.2 50/m
    weird feels strongly strange and often hard to explain, while funny names oddness that may simply surprise you.
  • bizarre
    5.9 18/m
    bizarre looks wildly strange or shocking to the eye, while funny refers to something unexpectedly odd but less extreme.
  • curious
    4.0 9.88/m
    curious often means interesting or worth noticing, while funny means simply strange or out of the ordinary in a surprising way.
