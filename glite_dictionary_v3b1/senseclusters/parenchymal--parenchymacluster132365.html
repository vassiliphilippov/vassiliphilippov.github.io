parenchymal

adjective
of organ tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛŋkɪməl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛŋkɪməl / (BrE)
relating to the functional tissue of an organ or plant, as opposed to the supporting or connective tissue

Examples

Parenchymal tissue
The scan showed no parenchymal damage in the liver.
Doctors are concerned about parenchymal changes seen in the patient's lung tissue.
A biopsy revealed that the parenchymal cells were functioning normally.
The parenchymal cells in the leaf are responsible for storing water and nutrients.
Damage to parenchymal tissue can affect the plant's ability to perform photosynthesis.
Researchers studied the parenchymal structure to understand how the organ functions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonparenchymal - not relating to the parenchyma; relating to supporting or structural tissue
  • synonym: parenchyma - the functional tissue of an organ
  • antonym: stromal - relating to the supporting tissue of an organ
