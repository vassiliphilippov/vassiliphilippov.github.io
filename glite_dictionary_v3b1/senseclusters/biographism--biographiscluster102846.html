biographism

noun
literary interpretation via author’s biography
8.6 9.0
Pronunciation: /baɪ.ˈɑː.grə.fɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /baɪ.ˈɒ.grə.fɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The practice of interpreting a work of literature primarily in terms of the author's own life and experiences.

Examples

Biographism in literature
His analysis relied on biographism, linking every character to events from the author's personal history.
Modern literary theorists often caution against excessive biographism in interpretation.
Some scholars criticize biographism for overshadowing the text's intrinsic meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: New Criticism - a literary theory that rejects biographical and historical context in favor of close reading of the text itself
  • synonym: biographical criticism - an approach to literary criticism that also interprets works through the author's life
