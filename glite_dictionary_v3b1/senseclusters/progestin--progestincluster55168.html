progestin

noun
synthetic hormone similar to progesterone
6.1 5.6
Pronunciation: /proʊ.ˈdʒes.tɪn/ /; / /prə.ˈdʒes.tɪn/ (AmE), /prəʊ.ˈdʒes.tɪn/ /; / /prə.ˈdʒes.tɪn/ (BrE)
Any of a group of synthetic steroid hormones that have effects similar to those of progesterone, often used in oral contraceptives and hormone therapy.

Examples

Progestin hormone
Doctors may prescribe progestin to help regulate menstrual cycles in some patients.
Some hormone replacement therapies include both estrogen and progestin for balance.
The birth control pill she takes contains a form of progestin to prevent ovulation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: progesterone - a natural steroid hormone essential for pregnancy
