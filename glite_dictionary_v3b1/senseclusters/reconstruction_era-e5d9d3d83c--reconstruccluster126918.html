Reconstruction Era

noun
U.S. post–Civil War period (1865–1877)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.ʃən ˈɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.ʃən ˈɪə.rə / (BrE)
the period in United States history (1865–1877) after the Civil War, characterized by the reorganization and reintegration of the southern states into the Union

Examples

The Reconstruction Era period
Historians debate the successes and failures of the Reconstruction Era in achieving equality.
Many laws and constitutional amendments were passed during the Reconstruction Era to protect the rights of formerly enslaved people.
The political turmoil of the Reconstruction Era had long-lasting effects on Southern society.
