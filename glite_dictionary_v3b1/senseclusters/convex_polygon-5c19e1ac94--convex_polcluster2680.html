convex polygon

noun
polygon with all interior angles less than 180°
7.7 4.6
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈveks ˈpɑːl.ɪ.gən/ /; / /ˈkɑːn.veks ˈpɑːl.ɪ.gən/ (AmE), /kən.ˈveks ˈpɒl.ɪ.gən/ /; / /kɒn.ˈveks ˈpɒl.ɪ.gən/ /; / /kən.ˈveks ˈpɒl.ɪ.gɒn/ (BrE)
A polygon whose interior angles are all less than 180°, and for which every line segment connecting two points in the polygon lies entirely inside it.

Examples

A convex polygon shape
A regular hexagon is an example of a convex polygon.
In geometry class, we learned how to identify a convex polygon by checking its angles.
No line segment between two points inside a convex polygon will ever leave the shape.
