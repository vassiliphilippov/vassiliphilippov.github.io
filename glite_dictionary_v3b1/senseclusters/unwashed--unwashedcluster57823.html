unwashed

adjective
the masses; ordinary people (often disparaging)
6.8 0.5/m
Pronunciation: / ÊŒnËˆwÉ‘Êƒt / (AmE), / ÊŒnËˆwÉ’Êƒt / (BrE)
(usually the unwashed) Used humorously or disparagingly to refer to the masses or the common people considered as a group, especially as lacking refinement or privileges.

Examples

The unwashed masses
He spoke of the unwashed masses with barely disguised contempt.
Some politicians appeal directly to the unwashed in their campaign speeches.
The upscale restaurant was designed to be out of reach for the unwashed.
