hearse

noun
vehicle for transporting a coffin at funerals
Pronunciation: / hɜrs / (AmE), / hɜːs / (BrE)
A vehicle for transporting a coffin to a funeral, typically to a church or cemetery; originally horse-drawn, now usually a motor vehicle.

Examples

A hearse vehicle
A black hearse was parked outside the church entrance, waiting for the mourners.
In Victorian times, a hearse was often drawn by a team of horses.
The hearse slowly made its way to the cemetery, leading the funeral procession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: funeral car - a motor vehicle used as a hearse
