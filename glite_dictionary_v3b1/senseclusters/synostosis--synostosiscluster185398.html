synostosis

noun
fusion of bones
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪ.nə.ˈstoʊ.sɪs/ /; / /sɪn.ə.ˈstoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /sɪ.nɒs.ˈtəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /sɪ.nə.ˈstəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
the fusion of two bones that are normally separate, resulting in a single continuous bone

Examples

Synostosis of bones
The X-ray revealed synostosis between the two finger bones after the injury.
Congenital synostosis can affect the movement of a child's elbow joint.
Surgery may be required if synostosis limits normal bone function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ankylosis - stiffening or immobility of a joint due to fusion of bones
  • antonym: separation - the state of being kept apart or not joined
