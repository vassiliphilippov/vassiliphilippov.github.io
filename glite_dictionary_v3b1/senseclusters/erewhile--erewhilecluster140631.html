erewhile

adjective
former; previous
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌwaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌwaɪl / (BrE)
former or previous; used to describe someone or something that existed or held a role in the past (archaic).

Examples

Erewhile king
The erewhile leader was remembered fondly by the older members of the community.
In the erewhile days of the kingdom, peace was common throughout the land.
The erewhile traditions have faded, replaced by modern customs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: current - happening or existing now
  • synonym: former - having previously filled a particular role or position
