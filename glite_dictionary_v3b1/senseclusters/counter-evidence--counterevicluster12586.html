counter-evidence

noun
evidence that disproves a claim
4.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.ə.və.dəns/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.ev.ə.dəns/ (BrE)
Evidence that tends to disprove or contradict a claim or hypothesis.

Examples

Strong counter-evidence
Despite the mounting counter-evidence, the theory remained widely accepted for years.
Researchers must consider all counter-evidence when evaluating their results.
The lawyer presented new counter-evidence that challenged the prosecution's case.
