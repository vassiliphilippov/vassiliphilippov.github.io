widely

adverb
with much space or distance between
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪdli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdli / (BrE)
with a lot of space or distance between points or objects; far apart from each other

Examples

Widely spaced
At the zoo, the enclosures are widely separated to give animals more privacy.
The houses in the village are widely spaced apart from one another.
The trees in this orchard are widely planted, allowing space for machinery to pass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: narrowly - with small distance or space between points or objects
