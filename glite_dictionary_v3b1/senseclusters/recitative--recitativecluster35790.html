recitative

noun
speech-like vocal style in music
9.9 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / Àår…õs…™t…ôÀàtiv / (AmE), / Àår…õs…™t…ôÀàtiÀêv / (BrE)
A style of musical singing in which a singer delivers narrative text with rhythms and inflections similar to speech, often used in operas, oratorios, and cantatas.

Examples

Recitative style singing
In Baroque music, recitative often connects the main arias and ensembles.
She delivered the recitative with expressive clarity, making the story easy to follow.
The opera opened with a recitative that set the scene for the coming drama.
