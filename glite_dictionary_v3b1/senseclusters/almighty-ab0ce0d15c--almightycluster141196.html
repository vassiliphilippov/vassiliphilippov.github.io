Almighty

noun
God; the supreme being (the Almighty)
8.4 5.93/m
Pronunciation: / …ëlÀàma…™ti / (AmE), / …îÀêlÀàma…™ti / (BrE)
(the Almighty) God; a name or title used especially in the Judeo-Christian tradition for the one supreme being.

Examples

The Almighty God
Many people seek comfort by placing their trust in the Almighty.
She prayed to the Almighty for strength in difficult times.
The old church was dedicated to the Almighty.

Synonyms

  • God Almighty
    4.9 0.41/m
    God Almighty refers directly to the supreme, all-powerful deity, while Almighty is a shorter title often used alone to mean God.
  • God
    2.0 14/m
    God names the all-powerful being in monotheistic faiths, while Almighty is a title that highlights God's supreme power.
  • Lord
    5.5 7.66/m
    Lord is a respectful title used for God or rulers, while Almighty specifically stresses God's all-powerful nature.
  • Divine
    8.6 2.57/m
    Divine describes something godlike or from God, while Almighty is a title that directly names God the all-powerful being.
  • Supreme Being
    7.5 0.26/m
    Supreme Being is a formal phrase for the highest creator, while Almighty is a reverent title often heard in biblical or prayer language.
  • Creator
    7.0 8.62/m
    Creator focuses on God as maker of the world, while Almighty emphasizes God's total power and authority beyond creating.
  • Maker
    9.5 4.38/m
    Maker names God as the one who made the world, while Almighty highlights God's supreme power and rule over everything.
