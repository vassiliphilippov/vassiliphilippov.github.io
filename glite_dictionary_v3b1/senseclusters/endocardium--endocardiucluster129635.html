endocardium

noun
inner lining of the heart
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.doʊˈkɑr.di.əm / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈkɑː.di.əm / (BrE)
the thin membrane that lines the inner chambers of the heart and forms part of the heart valves

Examples

The endocardium membrane
During surgery, the surgeon carefully examined the endocardium for any damage.
The endocardium helps maintain the smooth flow of blood inside the heart.
The endocardium is susceptible to infection in cases of endocarditis.
