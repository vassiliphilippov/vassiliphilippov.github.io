overtake

verb
go past (esp. a vehicle/person)
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈteɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈteɪk / (BrE)
to catch up with and go past someone or something that is moving in the same direction

Examples

To overtake a car
As the runner tired, two competitors managed to overtake him before the finish line.
It can be dangerous to overtake another car on a sharp bend.
She watched the motorcycle overtake the slow-moving truck on the highway.
She tried to overtake her rival in the final lap of the race.
The sports car managed to overtake the truck on the highway.
We had to speed up to overtake the slow-moving tractor ahead of us.
