Leptospira

noun
genus of spiral-shaped bacteria
Pronunciation: / lɛp.toʊˈspaɪ.rə / (AmE), / lɛp.təʊˈspaɪ.rə / (BrE)
A genus of very slender, spiral-shaped, aerobic bacteria, some species of which are free-living while others are parasitic in mammals.

Examples

Leptospira bacteria
Scientists isolated Leptospira from samples taken in tropical wetlands.
The bacteria of the genus Leptospira can cause a serious disease known as leptospirosis.
Veterinarians are alert for cases involving Leptospira infection in livestock.
