clamorously

adverb
in a loud, attention-seeking manner
Pronunciation: / ˈklæm.ɚ.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈklæm.ər.əs.li / (BrE)
in a loud or demanding manner that draws attention, often noisily or insistently

Examples

To clamorously demand
Protesters banged on the doors clamorously, demanding to be heard.
The children pleaded clamorously for sweets at the festival.
The crowd shouted clamorously for the band to perform another song.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obstreperously - in a noisy and difficult to control manner
