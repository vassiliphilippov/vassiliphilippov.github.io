arche

noun
first principle
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈɑːki / (BrE)
the original principle or first cause from which something develops, especially in ancient philosophy

Examples

The arche of the world
In ancient Greek philosophy, the arche was considered the source of all existence.
Thales proposed that water was the arche of the universe.
The concept of arche is central to understanding early metaphysical theories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: end - the final point or conclusion of something
  • synonym: origin - the point or place where something begins
  • synonym: principle - a fundamental truth or proposition
