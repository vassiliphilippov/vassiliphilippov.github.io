sheep

noun
timid or easily led person
4.5
Pronunciation: / ʃip / (AmE), / ʃiːp / (BrE)
a person who is timid, docile, or easily influenced and follows others without thinking for themselves (metaphorical use)

Examples

A sheep in a group
He acts like a sheep in meetings, never giving his own opinion.
Many people blindly follow trends like sheep without questioning.
She was tired of being treated like a sheep and wanted to think for herself.
Don't be a sheep—stand up for your own beliefs!
Many people acted like sheep during the campaign, never questioning what they were told.
The cult members followed their charismatic leader like obedient sheep.

Related Terms

  • synonym: follower - a person who accepts the leadership of another
  • antonym: leader - a person who leads or commands others
