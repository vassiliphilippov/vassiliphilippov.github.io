chemical change

noun
a process transforming one substance into another
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmɪkəl tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a process in which substances are transformed into different substances, typically by altering the atomic or molecular structure

Examples

Chemical change process
A rusting nail undergoes a chemical change as iron turns into iron oxide.
Cooking an egg is a common example of a chemical change in the kitchen.
The explosion caused a rapid chemical change in the fuel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemical reaction - a process that involves rearrangement of the molecular or ionic structure of a substance
  • antonym: physical change - a change affecting the form of a chemical substance, but not its chemical composition
