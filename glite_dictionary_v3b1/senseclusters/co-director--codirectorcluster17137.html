co-director

noun
joint director
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊdəˈrɛktər / (AmE), / ˌkəʊdɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
a person who shares the responsibilities and authority of a director with one or more others

Examples

The co-director role
After years of collaboration, he was promoted to co-director of the research institute.
The film was created by two talented co-directors who shared every decision.
As co-director, she worked closely with her colleague to lead the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: co-manager - a person who shares the role of manager with others
  • degree lower: director - a person in charge of an organization or project
