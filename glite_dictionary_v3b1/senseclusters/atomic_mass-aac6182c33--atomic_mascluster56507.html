atomic mass

noun
mass of an atom in atomic mass units
2.7
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑmɪk mæs / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk mæs / (BrE)
The mass of an atom of a chemical element, typically expressed in atomic mass units (amu).

Examples

Atomic mass unit
Knowing the atomic mass helps chemists calculate the proportions of elements in a compound.
Scientists use the atomic mass to identify elements in the periodic table.
The atomic mass of carbon is approximately 12 atomic mass units.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atomic weight - average mass of atoms of an element relative to carbon-12
  • synonym: mass number - the total number of protons and neutrons in an atomic nucleus
  • degree higher: molecular mass - mass of a molecule
