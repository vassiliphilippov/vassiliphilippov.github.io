cardamom

noun
aromatic spice plant; its seeds used in cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdəməm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdəməm / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to India, whose aromatic seeds are used as a spice in cooking and baking.

Examples

Cardamom plant
He bought a jar of ground cardamom for his curry recipes.
The aroma of cardamom filled the kitchen as she prepared the dessert.
Traditional chai tea often contains spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves.
