lexicalized concept

noun
a concept expressed by a word
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛksɪkəˌlaɪzd ˈkɑnsɛpt / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəlaɪzd ˈkɒnsɛpt / (BrE)
A concept that is represented by a single word or fixed expression in a language.

Examples

A lexicalized concept word
'Schadenfreude' is a lexicalized concept in German, but not in English.
Some languages have a lexicalized concept for 'older brother', while others do not.
The word 'dog' is a lexicalized concept for a domesticated canine.
