ira

noun
wrath; deadly sin of anger
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.rə / (AmE), / ˈɪə.rə / (BrE)
Anger or wrath, one of the Seven Deadly Sins in Christian tradition; intense or violent anger incited by a real or perceived wrong.

Examples

Deadly ira
In medieval texts, ira is depicted as a sinful force that destroys harmony.
Some scholars discuss the depiction of ira in Dante's Divine Comedy.
The vice of ira was condemned by early Christian philosophers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wrath - intense anger; strong vengeful anger
