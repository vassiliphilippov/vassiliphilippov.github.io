subaccount

noun
separate account section
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
a separate account within a main account, used for a specific purpose or to track particular transactions

Examples

A subaccount for investments
Each client has a subaccount for different types of investments.
You can create a subaccount to manage your travel expenses separately from your main funds.
The company uses a subaccount for each project to keep finances organized.

Related Terms

  • synonym: account - a record of financial transactions or a bank arrangement
  • antonym: main account - the primary account containing subaccounts
