somber

adjective
serious or gloomy; not cheerful
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑmbɚ /; / ˈsɑːmbər / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbə / (BrE)
serious, sad, or gloomy in character, mood, or appearance

Examples

Somber mood
She wore a somber dress to the memorial service.
The news cast a somber tone over the entire city.
The room felt somber after everyone had left.
After hearing the bad news, she became quite sombre.
He wore a sombre suit to the important meeting.
The funeral was marked by a sombre silence throughout the hall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: gloomy - dark or causing sadness; downcast in mood
  • synonym: somber - Alternative (American) spelling of 'sombre'
