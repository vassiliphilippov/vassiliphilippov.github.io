truant

noun
student absent from school without leave
6.3 0.37/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtruÀê…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàtruÀê…ônt / (BrE)
a person, especially a student, who stays away from school or duty without permission

Examples

A truant skips school
Being caught as a truant can lead to serious consequences for students.
Habitual truants may face disciplinary action from the school authorities.
The school called his parents after discovering he was a truant.
As a truant, he spent his days wandering around the city instead of studying.
Schools often have policies in place to deal with chronic truants.
The teacher noticed that one truant had not shown up for class in over a week.

Synonyms

  • wagger
    9.9 0.01/m
    wagger is an informal British word for a student who skips school, while truant is the neutral school term used in reports.
  • hooky player
    8.9 0.00/m
    hooky player is someone who plays hooky and skips school for fun, while truant is the formal school term.
  • ditcher
    7.2 0.00/m
    ditcher is a person who deliberately avoids work or school, while truant refers specifically to a student skipping school without permission.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absentee - a person who is expected to be somewhere but is not present
  • antonym: attendee - a person who is present at a place or event
  • synonym: shirker - a person who avoids work or duty
