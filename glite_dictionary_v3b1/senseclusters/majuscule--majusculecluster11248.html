majuscule

adjective
uppercase letter or script
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈdʒʌskjul /; / məˈdʒʌs.kjul / (AmE), / məˈdʒʌskjuːl /; / məˈdʒʌs.kjuːl / (BrE)
written in or relating to large, uppercase (capital) letters, especially in ancient manuscripts

Examples

Majuscule letters
Many ancient Roman inscriptions were written entirely in majuscule script.
The illuminated manuscript featured beautiful majuscule initials at the start of each chapter.
The professor explained the difference between majuscule and minuscule handwriting styles.
Early Christian texts are often written in a majuscule script.
The historian analyzed the majuscule letters in the ancient manuscript.
The transition from majuscule to minuscule writing changed the appearance of medieval books.
