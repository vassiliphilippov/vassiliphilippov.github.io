mapping

noun
locating genes on a chromosome
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmæpɪŋ / (BrE)
In genetics, the process of determining the position of genes on a chromosome.

Examples

Gene mapping
Mapping of the human genome has advanced rapidly over the last two decades.
Gene mapping techniques help scientists trace inherited diseases.
The research team specializes in mapping genes responsible for cancer.
