gypsiferous

adjective
containing gypsum
Pronunciation: / dʒɪpˈsɪfərəs / (AmE), / dʒɪpˈsɪfərəs / (BrE)
containing or producing gypsum, a soft sulfate mineral found in soil or rock

Examples

Gypsiferous rocks
The region is known for its gypsiferous rocks, which are mined for construction materials.
Certain plants thrive in gypsiferous soils that are rich in minerals.
Scientists studied the gypsiferous deposits to understand the area's geological history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calcareous - containing or resembling calcium carbonate or limestone
  • synonym: gypsum - a soft sulfate mineral used in plaster and fertilizer
