harvesting

noun
automated data collection
4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːr.və.stɪŋ/ /; / /ˈhɑːr.vɪ.stɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈhɑː.və.stɪŋ/ /; / /ˈhɑː.vɪ.stɪŋ/ (BrE)
the automated collection of data from multiple sources (for example websites, APIs, or sensors), typically performed by software for analysis, research, or building datasets.

Examples

Data harvesting
Researchers rely on harvesting of public tweets to build training datasets for sentiment analysis.
The company's automated harvesting of web data raised concerns about user privacy and consent.
Academic projects sometimes use large-scale harvesting from online archives to study language change over decades.
