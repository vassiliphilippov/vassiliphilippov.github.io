Egyptian vulture

noun
a small white vulture of Africa and southern Eurasia
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən ˈvʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən ˈvʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
a small mostly white vulture (Neophron percnopterus) native to Africa and southern Eurasia, distinguished by its yellow face and slender bill

Examples

The Egyptian vulture
Conservationists are working to protect the dwindling population of the Egyptian vulture.
Several Egyptian vultures were spotted near the cliffs during migration season.
The Egyptian vulture is easily recognized by its white plumage and yellow face.
