cow-tongue fern

noun
type of fern with strap-shaped leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊ tʌŋ ˌfɝn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ tʌŋ ˌfɜːn / (BrE)
a fern with pale yellow-green, strap-shaped leaves, found as an epiphyte or on the ground from Florida to Uruguay

Examples

The cow-tongue fern plant
Some people grow the cow-tongue fern indoors as an ornamental plant.
The cow-tongue fern is popular in tropical gardens for its striking foliage.
You can often spot the cow-tongue fern growing on tree trunks in the Florida Everglades.
