accord

noun
state of harmony or agreement
4.0 3.90/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàk…îrd / (AmE), / …ôÀàk…îÀêd / (BrE)
a state of agreement or harmony between people, groups, or ideas

Examples

In perfect accord
Despite their differences, the diplomats worked in perfect accord to resolve the crisis.
Her words were in accord with her actions, which gained her everyone's trust.
The new manager brought the team into greater accord with one another.
After months of negotiation, both teams finally reached an accord on the terms of the contract.
Her views are in perfect accord with the principles of the organization.
The changes were made in accord with everyone's wishes, ensuring complete satisfaction.
His views are in accord with the majority opinion.
The two sides reached an accord after lengthy negotiations.
There was an accord among the team members regarding the next steps.
