installment rate

noun
amount paid at regular intervals
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈstɑlmənt reɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɔːlmənt reɪt / (BrE)
the amount of money that must be paid at regular intervals, such as weekly or monthly, usually as part of repaying a loan or purchase.

Examples

High installment rate
Due to the high installment rate, she decided to look for more affordable financing options.
Many car loans allow you to adjust the installment rate to fit your monthly budget.
The bank offered a lower installment rate for clients with excellent credit histories.
