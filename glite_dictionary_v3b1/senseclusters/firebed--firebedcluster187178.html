firebed

noun
layer under fire
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəbɛd / (BrE)
the layer of fuel and ashes at the bottom of a fire, especially in a fireplace or campfire

Examples

Campfire firebed
The campers made sure the firebed was cool before packing up their tents.
A thick firebed of glowing coals kept the cabin warm all night.
Always clear the firebed of ashes before starting a new fire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ash - the powdery residue left after burning
  • synonym: hearth - the floor of a fireplace, often extending into a room
