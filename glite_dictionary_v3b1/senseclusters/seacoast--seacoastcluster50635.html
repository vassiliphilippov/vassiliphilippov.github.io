seacoast

noun
the shore of a sea or ocean
1.0 0.50/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêÀåko äst / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêÀåk…ô äst / (BrE)
the land along the edge of a sea or ocean; the shore of a sea or ocean

Examples

The seacoast line
Fishing communities thrive all along the Mediterranean seacoast.
The storm caused severe erosion along the seacoast.
We spent our vacation exploring the beautiful seacoast of Maine.

Synonyms

  • seashore
    1.0 1.94/m
    seashore is the sandy or pebbly area right beside the sea, while seacoast refers to the wider land along a sea.
  • coast
    1.0 35/m
    coast is the land region near the sea used for towns and travel, while seacoast names the shore itself.
  • shore
    1.0 33/m
    shore is the land beside any large body of water like lakes or rivers, while seacoast refers only to sea or ocean edges.
  • coastline
    1.4 4.84/m
    coastline is the continuous outline of land seen from a map or air, while seacoast means the actual shore by the sea.
  • shoreline
    2.7 7.11/m
    shoreline is the exact line where land meets water and can change with tides, while seacoast refers to the nearby land along a sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inland - situated away from the coast; interior of a country
