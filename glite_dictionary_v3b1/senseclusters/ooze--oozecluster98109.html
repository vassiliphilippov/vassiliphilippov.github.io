ooze

verb
to leak or pass out slowly
3.9 4.24/m
Pronunciation: / uz /; / uÀêz / (AmE), / uÀêz / (BrE)
to leak, pass, or flow out slowly, especially through small openings (of a liquid or substance)

Examples

To ooze out
A sticky syrup oozed from the broken jar onto the table.
Sweat oozed down his forehead as he climbed the steep hill.
Thick black oil began to ooze from the cracks in the engine.
A thick, dark sauce was oozing from the edges of the pie.
Sweat began to ooze from his forehead in the summer heat.
The sap slowly oozed out of the cut in the tree trunk.

Synonyms

  • seep
    2.7 7.15/m
    seep is to move slowly through small openings or pores into nearby places, while ooze implies a thicker, slower flow.
  • exude
    6.5 0.61/m
    exude is to give off a steady small amount like drops, while ooze usually means to leak or flow slowly through holes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drip - to fall or let fall in drops
  • synonym: exude - to discharge (moisture or a smell) slowly and steadily
  • degree higher: gush - to flow out forcefully and in large amounts
  • synonym: seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material
  • synonym: trickle - to flow in a small stream
