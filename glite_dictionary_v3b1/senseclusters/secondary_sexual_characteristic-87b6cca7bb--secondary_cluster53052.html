secondary sexual characteristic

noun
physical sex trait, not directly reproductive
2.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsek.ən.dər.iː ˈsek.ʃuː.əl kær.ək.tə.ˈrɪs.tɪk/ /; / /ˈsek.ən.dər.iː ˈsek.ʃəl kær.ək.tə.ˈrɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /ˈsek.ən.dər.iː ˈseks.juː.əl kær.ək.tə.ˈrɪs.tɪk/ /; / /ˈsek.ən.dər.iː ˈsek.ʃəl kær.ək.tə.ˈrɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
a physical trait that develops at puberty distinguishing the sexes but not directly involved in reproduction, such as body hair or voice pitch

Examples

A secondary sexual characteristic
Body hair is one type of secondary sexual characteristic that develops during puberty.
Breast development in adolescent girls is a secondary sexual characteristic.
In humans, a deeper voice in males is considered a secondary sexual characteristic.
