textualist

noun
text scholar
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛkstʃuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstʃuəlɪst / (BrE)
a scholar or editor who studies and compares texts to establish an accurate or authoritative version

Examples

A textualist scholar
The textualist carefully compared different versions of the ancient poem to find the original wording.
As a textualist, she spent years analyzing manuscripts to produce a reliable edition of the novel.
The conference brought together every leading textualist in the field of Renaissance literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: editor - a person who prepares texts for publication
  • synonym: philologist - a scholar who studies language in written historical sources
