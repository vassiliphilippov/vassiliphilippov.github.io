PBX

proper noun
homeobox gene family
9.9
Pronunciation: /piː.biː.ˈeks/ (AmE), /piː.biː.ˈeks/ (BrE)
a family of homeobox genes, such as PBX1 and PBX2, that encode transcription factors important in development and sometimes linked to leukemia

Examples

PBX gene mutation
Researchers found that mutations in PBX genes can disrupt normal development in mice.
The study showed that PBX proteins interact with other transcription factors during organ formation.
A fusion involving PBX was identified in several leukemia patient samples.
