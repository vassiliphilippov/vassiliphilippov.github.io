spiritual

noun
religious song from Black American tradition
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (BrE)
a religious song of a kind originating among Black people in the southern United States, expressing deep emotion and often featuring call-and-response singing

Examples

A spiritual song
He learned to sing spirituals from his grandmother, who had grown up in the South.
Many spirituals were created as expressions of hope and faith under slavery.
The choir performed an old spiritual during the concert, moving the audience to tears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gospel - a style of religious music closely associated with spirituals but generally more modern and with different musical structure
  • synonym: hymn - a religious song or poem of praise to God, often sung in Christian worship, but generally different in style from spirituals
