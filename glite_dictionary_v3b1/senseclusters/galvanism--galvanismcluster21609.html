galvanism

noun
electricity from chemical reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈɡælvəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
Electricity generated by chemical reactions, especially as observed in a cell or battery.

Examples

Battery galvanism
Mary Shelley was inspired by galvanism when she wrote about reanimating life in her novel 'Frankenstein'.
Scientists in the 18th century were fascinated by galvanism and its potential to animate muscle.
The battery operates by means of galvanism, converting chemical energy into electrical energy.
