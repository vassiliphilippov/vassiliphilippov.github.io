Ayyubid

proper noun
Muslim dynasty member
9.9
Pronunciation: / aɪˈjuːbɪd / (AmE), / aɪˈjuːbɪd / (BrE)
a member of a Muslim dynasty founded by Saladin that ruled parts of the Middle East in the 12th and 13th centuries

Examples

An Ayyubid ruler
The Ayyubid dynasty was known for its architectural achievements in Cairo.
Many historians study the military strategies of the Ayyubid rulers during the Crusades.
Artifacts from the Ayyubid period can be found in several Middle Eastern museums.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Fatimid - a member of the Muslim dynasty preceding the Ayyubids in Egypt
  • synonym: Saladin - the founder of the Ayyubid dynasty
