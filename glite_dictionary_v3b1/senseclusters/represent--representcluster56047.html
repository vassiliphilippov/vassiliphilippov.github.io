represent

verb
draw attention to in protest or objection
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to point out or draw attention to something, especially as a form of protest or objection

Examples

To represent concerns
Several citizens represented their concerns about pollution to the council.
Teachers represented their demands for better resources to the school board.
The lawyer represented to the judge that the evidence was incomplete.
