indent

verb
bind by legal contract (historical)
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛnt / (BrE)
to bind someone by a formal legal agreement, especially as an apprentice or servant

Examples

To indent an apprentice
He regretted having his son indented at such a young age.
Many immigrants were indented to wealthy colonists in exchange for passage to America.
The young man was indented as an apprentice to a master carpenter for seven years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indenture - a formal legal agreement, especially one binding someone as apprentice or servant
