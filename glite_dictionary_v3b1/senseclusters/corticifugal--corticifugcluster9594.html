corticifugal

adjective
nerve fibers from the cerebral cortex
8.6
Pronunciation: / kɔːr.tɪˈsɪf.jʊ.ɡəl / (AmE), / kɔː.tɪˈsɪf.jʊ.ɡəl / (BrE)
(of a nerve fiber) carrying impulses away from the cerebral cortex.

Examples

Corticifugal nerve fibers
Damage to corticifugal pathways can affect voluntary movement control.
Recent studies have mapped the precise origins of corticifugal discharges in the auditory system.
The neurologist explained that corticifugal fibers transmit signals away from the brain's cortex.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corticopetal - (of a nerve fiber) carrying impulses toward the cerebral cortex
