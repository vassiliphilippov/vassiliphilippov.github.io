(of people or groups) moving from place to place rather than settling permanently in one location; migratory.

Examples

Nomadic people
As a nomadic worker, she travels to different cities every few months.
The tribe remained nomadic for centuries, never settling in one region for too long.
Their nomadic lifestyle allowed them to adapt to changing environments.

Synonyms

  • migratory
    5.0 0.33/m
    migratory is used for groups that move regularly, often for seasons or food, while nomadic means a lifestyle of moving rather than settling.
  • itinerant
    8.0 0.18/m
    itinerant describes a person who travels for work from place to place, while nomadic describes whole groups living a moving lifestyle.
  • peripatetic
    9.5 0.00/m
    peripatetic means traveling regularly for work or duty, often by one person, while nomadic describes groups who move to live.
  • roving
    4.1 0.16/m
    roving describes moving about widely and sometimes aimlessly, often alone, while nomadic implies a regular moving lifestyle for groups.
  • wandering
    3.9 0.00/m
    wandering means moving without fixed direction, often slowly or casually, while nomadic means a lifestyle of not settling and moving regularly.
