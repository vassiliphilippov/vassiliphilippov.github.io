rapid climb

noun
a swift upward movement or increase
Pronunciation: / ˈræpɪd ˈklaɪm / (AmE), / ˈræpɪd ˈklaɪm / (BrE)
A swift or sudden upward movement or increase in level, number, or value.

Examples

A rapid climb after takeoff
After takeoff, the pilot initiated a rapid climb to avoid turbulence.
The company experienced a rapid climb in its stock price after the merger was announced.
There has been a rapid climb in the number of new subscribers this quarter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradual rise - a slow or steady increase
  • synonym: sharp rise - a sudden or steep increase
