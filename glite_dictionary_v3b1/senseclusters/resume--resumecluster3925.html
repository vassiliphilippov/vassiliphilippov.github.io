resume

verb
return to a former state
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzum /; / rɪˈzuːm / (AmE), / rɪˈzjuːm / (BrE)
to start something again or return to a previous activity, state, or position after a pause or interruption

Examples

To resume work
After a brief interruption, business operations resumed as usual.
Once peace was restored, the community resumed its normal activities.
The river resumed its course once the blockage was cleared.
After a short break, the meeting will resume at three o'clock.
She plans to resume her studies next semester after taking a year off.
The orchestra resumed playing after the conductor signaled.
After his illness, he was ready to resume his duties as principal.
She will resume her position as chairperson next month.
The company plans to resume operations after the holiday break.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continue - to keep happening or existing without stopping
  • antonym: pause - to stop doing something for a short time before continuing
  • synonym: reclaim - to take back something that was yours
  • antonym: relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim; to give up
  • synonym: revert - to return to a previous state or condition
