similarity

noun
quantitative likeness measure (data science)
4.5
Pronunciation: /sɪ.mə.ˈlær.ə.tɪ/ /; / /sɪ.mɪ.ˈlær.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /sɪ.mə.ˈlær.ə.tɪ/ /; / /sɪ.mɪ.ˈlær.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
(in computing and data science) a quantitative measure or score that indicates how alike two data objects are, often computed by algorithms such as cosine similarity, Jaccard index, or Euclidean-based measures.

Examples

Cosine similarity
The recommender system computes similarity scores between user profiles and items to suggest products.
Researchers measured cosine similarity between document vectors to cluster related news articles.
A higher similarity value indicates the algorithm considers two images more alike.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distance (metric) - a numeric measure of dissimilarity between objects, often used as the inverse concept to similarity
  • synonym: similarity measure - a function or algorithm that computes a numeric value representing likeness between objects
