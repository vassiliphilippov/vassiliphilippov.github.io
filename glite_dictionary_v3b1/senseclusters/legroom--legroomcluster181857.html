legroom

noun
freedom to act
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛɡˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡˌruːm / (BrE)
freedom or flexibility to act or make decisions, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Legroom for ideas
The new manager gave employees more legroom to make decisions about their projects.
With tight deadlines, there is little legroom for unexpected problems.
The contract allows us enough legroom to adjust our strategy if needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latitude - freedom to choose how to act or decide
  • synonym: leeway - freedom to act within certain limits
  • antonym: restriction - a limitation or control on what can be done
