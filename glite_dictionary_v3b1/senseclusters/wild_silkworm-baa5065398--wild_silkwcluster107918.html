wild silkworm

noun
larva of a saturniid moth producing wild silk
5.5 6.2
Pronunciation: /waɪld ˈsɪl.kwɜːrm/ /; / /waɪld ˈsɪlk.wɜːrm/ (AmE), /waɪld ˈsɪl.kwɜːm/ /; / /waɪld ˈsɪlk.wɜːm/ (BrE)
The larva of certain saturniid moths, which produces a large amount of strong silk in its cocoon, especially those not domesticated for commercial silk production.

Examples

The wild silkworm cocoon
Many luxury fabrics are made from the silk of the wild silkworm found in Asian forests.
Researchers are studying the habitat of the wild silkworm to protect endangered silk-producing species.
The wild silkworm spins a tougher, coarser thread than its domesticated counterpart.
