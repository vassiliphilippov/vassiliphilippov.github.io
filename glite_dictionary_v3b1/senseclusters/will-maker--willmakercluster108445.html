will-maker

noun
person making a will
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪlˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who creates or writes a legal will to specify how their property will be distributed after death

Examples

The will-maker signs
The will-maker must be of sound mind when signing the document.
A lawyer explained the responsibilities of a will-maker to the family.
Each will-maker should clearly state their wishes to avoid confusion among heirs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beneficiary - a person who receives something from a will
  • synonym: testator - a person who has made a will
