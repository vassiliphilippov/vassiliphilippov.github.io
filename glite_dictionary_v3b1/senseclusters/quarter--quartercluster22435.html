quarter

noun
one of four equal parts
1.0 1.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɔːr.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkwɔː.tə/ (BrE)
one of four equal parts or periods into which something is divided (including time, sports, or academic terms)

Examples

A quarter of an hour
He spent a quarter of his savings on a new bicycle.
Only a quarter of the students passed the exam this year.
She divided the cake into four quarters so everyone could have an equal share.
He promised to meet me at a quarter past six.
Please wait for another quarter before the next appointment.
She finished her break in a quarter and returned to work.
Each quarter brings a new set of financial results that investors examine closely.
The academic year is divided into four quarters at some universities.
The company reported record profits in the second quarter of the year.

  • synonym: trimester - a period of three months, especially in pregnancy or academic contexts
