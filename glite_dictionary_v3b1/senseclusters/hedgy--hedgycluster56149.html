hedgy

adjective
cautious or defensive
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdʒi / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒi / (BrE)
showing a tendency to avoid risk by being cautious or defensive, especially in financial decisions.

Examples

Hedgy investment
The investor took a hedgy approach, spreading her money across several low-risk funds.
His hedgy attitude meant he rarely made bold moves in the stock market.
Analysts described the company's strategy as hedgy during uncertain economic times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cautious - careful to avoid danger or risk
  • synonym: defensive - intended to protect against risk or attack
  • antonym: reckless - not showing concern about the possible bad results of actions
