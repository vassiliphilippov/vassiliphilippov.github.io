uncountable

adjective
not countable
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkaʊntəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkaʊntəbl / (BrE)
describing a noun that cannot be counted as separate items and does not have a plural form

Examples

Uncountable noun
In English, 'information' is an uncountable noun and does not have a plural form.
Words like 'rice' and 'music' are considered uncountable in English grammar.
You cannot say 'waters' or 'furnitures' because these are uncountable nouns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: countable - able to be counted as separate items
