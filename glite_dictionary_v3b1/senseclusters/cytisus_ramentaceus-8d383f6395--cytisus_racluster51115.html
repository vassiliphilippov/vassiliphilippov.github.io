Cytisus ramentaceus

noun
a shrub native to southeastern Europe with yellow flowers and poisonous seeds
Pronunciation: / saɪˈtaɪsəs ræˌmɛnˈteɪsiəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɪsəs ræˌmɛnˈteɪsiəs / (BrE)
an erect shrub native to southeastern Europe, with large trifoliate leaves and dense clusters of yellow flowers followed by poisonous seeds, sometimes classified under the genus Cytisus

Examples

Cytisus ramentaceus yellow flowers
Cytisus ramentaceus is noted for its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Cytisus ramentaceus should be placed in a different genus.
Farmers in the region avoid grazing livestock where Cytisus ramentaceus grows because its seeds are poisonous.
