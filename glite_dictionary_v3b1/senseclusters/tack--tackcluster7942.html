tack

verb
fasten with tacks
Pronunciation: / tæk / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
to fasten, attach, or join things together, often temporarily, using tacks, nails, or by assembling quickly

Examples

To tack a poster
He tacked the map to the wall so everyone could see it.
Please tack your assignment to the bulletin board when you arrive.
She quickly tacked the fabric together before sewing the final seam.
Before sewing, he decided to tack the fabric pieces together for easier handling.
She used a pushpin to tack the poster to the bulletin board.
You can tack the note onto the door so everyone will see it.
He tacked the broken chair with some spare wood and nails until it could be properly repaired.
She managed to tack together a quick response for the meeting.
The playwright tacked several scenes together to finish the script on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assemble - to bring or put together separate parts to form a whole
  • antonym: detach - to remove or unfasten something that is attached
  • synonym: nail - to fasten with nails
  • synonym: piece - to join or fit together to make a whole
  • synonym: pin - to attach or fasten something with a pin
