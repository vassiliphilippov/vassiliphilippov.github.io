Virginia deer

noun
common North American deer species
9.9
Pronunciation: / vərˈdʒɪniə dɪr / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪnɪə dɪə / (BrE)
A common North American deer species (Odocoileus virginianus) with a tail that has a white underside.

Examples

The Virginia deer
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of the Virginia deer in several states.
During autumn, Virginia deer grow thicker coats to prepare for winter.
The Virginia deer is often seen grazing at dawn in forested areas.
