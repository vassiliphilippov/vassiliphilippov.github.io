hindrance

noun
the act of hindering; obstacle
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪndr(ə)ns /; / ˈhɪndrəns / (AmE), / ˈhɪndrəns /; / ˈhɪndr(ə)ns / (BrE)
something that makes it more difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen; an obstacle or impediment

Examples

A hindrance to progress
Her injury became a hindrance during the competition.
The lack of funds proved to be a serious hindrance to the project's success.
Traffic was a major hindrance to getting to the airport on time.
Heavy traffic is often a serious hindrance to getting to work on time.
His lack of experience proved to be a hindrance during the interview process.
Without her support, every task felt like a greater hindrance than before.
His injured knee was a hindrance during the race.
Procrastination can be a serious hindrance to achieving your goals.
The lack of resources proved to be a major hindrance to the project's success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assistance - the action of helping or supporting
  • antonym: help - something that makes progress easier
  • synonym: impediment - something that hinders or obstructs progress
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or impedes progress
