family Tetraodontidae

noun
the puffer fish family
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli tɛtrəoʊˈdɒntɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɛtrəʊˈdɒntɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of fishes known as puffers, characterized by their ability to inflate their bodies and often possessing poisonous substances.

Examples

family Tetraodontidae puffers
Many members of the family Tetraodontidae possess tetrodotoxin, making them highly poisonous if ingested.
Scientists study the family Tetraodontidae to understand adaptations to extreme environments in fish.
The family Tetraodontidae includes both freshwater and marine species known for their unique defense mechanisms.
