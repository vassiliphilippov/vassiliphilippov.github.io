remembrancer

noun
English legal officer
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɛmbrənsər / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛmbrənsə / (BrE)
a senior English legal officer, historically responsible for keeping records of royal revenue and enforcing debts owed to the Crown

Examples

Crown remembrancer
The remembrancer attended the annual ceremony to present the Crown's accounts.
Historically, the remembrancer played a key role in managing royal finances.
The duties of the remembrancer are now largely ceremonial in modern times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recorder - an official who keeps records, especially in a court
