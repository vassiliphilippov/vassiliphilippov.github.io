hemolyze

verb
to break down red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhiːməˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause red blood cells to break down and release their contents into the surrounding fluid

Examples

To hemolyze red cells
If a blood sample is mishandled, the red cells may hemolyze and affect test results.
Certain diseases can hemolyze red blood cells, leading to anemia.
The laboratory technician checked to ensure the sample did not hemolyze during transport.
If the blood sample is not handled properly, the red cells may hemolyze and affect the test results.
Some diseases can cause red blood cells to hemolyze more easily than normal.
The technician was careful to avoid any procedure that might hemolyze the specimen.
