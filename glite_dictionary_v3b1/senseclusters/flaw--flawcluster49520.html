flaw

noun
imperfection in a plan, theory, or document
3.0 8.94/m
Pronunciation: / fl…î / (AmE), / fl…îÀê / (BrE)
An imperfection or weakness in a plan, theory, or legal document that causes it to fail or makes it less effective.

Examples

A flaw in the plan
Experts pointed out a fundamental flaw in the software design.
The contract had a legal flaw that voided its terms.
There was a major flaw in the argument that made the conclusion unreliable.
