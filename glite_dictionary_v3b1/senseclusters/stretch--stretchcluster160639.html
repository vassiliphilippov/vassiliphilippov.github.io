stretch

noun
extension beyond normal limit, figurative
Pronunciation: / strɛtʃ / (AmE), / strɛtʃ / (BrE)
an idea, claim, or interpretation that is unlikely or exaggerated; something not easily believed or justified

Examples

A big stretch to believe
After an hour of negotiations, their patience was at full stretch.
By any stretch of the imagination, this cannot be considered a fair offer.
The task was a real stretch for someone with little experience.
Calling him a genius might be a stretch, but he's certainly clever.
Saying that eating chocolate makes you healthier is really a stretch.
The story about winning the lottery twice sounded like quite a stretch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggeration - an overstatement; making something seem more important, better, or worse than it really is
  • antonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable
  • synonym: reach - an attempt to justify something unlikely or far-fetched (informal, as in 'a bit of a reach')
