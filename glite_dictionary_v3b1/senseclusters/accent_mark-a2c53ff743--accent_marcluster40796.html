accent mark

noun
diacritical mark used in writing
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.sɛnt ˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈæk.sənt ˌmɑːk / (BrE)
A mark placed over or under a letter, especially a vowel, to indicate stress or a special pronunciation.

Examples

The accent mark above
Remember to add the accent mark above the 'e' in café.
Spanish words often use an accent mark to distinguish between similar words.
The absence of an accent mark can change the meaning of a word.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diacritic - A sign or accent used to alter the sound or value of the letter it is attached to
