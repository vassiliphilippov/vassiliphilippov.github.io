prescriptiveness

noun
rule-setting quality
Pronunciation: / prɪˈskrɪptɪvnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪptɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or tendency of setting rules or dictating how something should be done

Examples

The prescriptiveness of rules
The prescriptiveness of the new policy left little room for flexibility.
Some teachers prefer less prescriptiveness in their classroom rules to encourage creativity.
Linguists often debate the prescriptiveness of traditional grammar guides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptiveness - the quality of describing how things are rather than prescribing rules
  • antonym: proscriptiveness - the quality of forbidding or prohibiting certain actions
