gnosis

noun
esoteric spiritual knowledge
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
Intuitive or esoteric knowledge of spiritual truths, especially as claimed by Gnostics in early Christian times.

Examples

Gnosis of spiritual truths
He devoted his life to the pursuit of gnosis, seeking understanding beyond ordinary knowledge.
Some philosophers distinguish between rational understanding and gnosis as a deeper, intuitive insight.
The ancient sect sought gnosis through meditation and mystical practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agnosis - absence or lack of spiritual knowledge
