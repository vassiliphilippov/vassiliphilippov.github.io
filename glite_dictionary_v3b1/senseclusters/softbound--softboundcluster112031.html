softbound

adjective
with paper cover
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔftˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈsɒftˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
having a flexible paper cover instead of a hard cover, especially for books

Examples

Softbound edition
The new novel is only available in a softbound edition at most bookstores.
Students often prefer softbound textbooks because they are lighter to carry.
She bought a softbound copy of the manual for easier handling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hardbound - having a rigid, hard cover
  • synonym: paperback - a book with a flexible paper cover
