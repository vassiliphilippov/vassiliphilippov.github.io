roughness

noun
the state of being rough or harsh
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌfnɪs /; / ˈrʌfnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being harsh or severe, especially in speech, voice, or behavior

Examples

Roughness in his voice
She was startled by the roughness in his voice during the argument.
The roughness of the fabric made it uncomfortable to wear.
The roughness of the road caused the car to shake violently.
Despite her roughness, she cared deeply for her friends.
People were put off by the roughness of his remarks during the meeting.
The roughness in his tone made the criticism harder to accept.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentleness - the quality of being kind, soft, or mild in manner or approach
  • synonym: harshness - the quality of being unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses, especially sound or manner
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth or even, the opposite of roughness
