bathos

noun
anticlimax or triteness in style
9.0 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.θɑːs/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.θoʊs/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.θɒs/ (BrE)
an unintentional and often ridiculous drop from the serious or important to the trivial or silly, especially in literature or speech

Examples

Unintentional bathos in speech
Critics complained that the film fell into bathos with its over-the-top melodrama.
The novel's emotional moments are ruined by frequent bathos that makes them feel trite.
The speech descended into bathos when the president made an awkward joke.
His speech sank into bathos when he suddenly started quoting greeting card clichés.
The movie's attempt at drama was ruined by accidental bathos in the final scene.
The novel teeters between genuine poignancy and overwrought bathos.
Critics complained that the film's attempt at tragedy resulted in unintended bathos.
His speech, intended to inspire, descended into bathos when he told a joke about lost socks.
The novel's ending was pure bathos, shifting suddenly from high drama to a petty squabble.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticlimax - a disappointing end to an exciting series of events
  • antonym: climax - the most intense or exciting point; the contrast to bathos
  • degree lower: pathos - a quality that evokes pity or sadness
  • synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
