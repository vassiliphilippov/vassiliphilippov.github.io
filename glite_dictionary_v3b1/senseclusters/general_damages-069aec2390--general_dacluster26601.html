general damages

noun
legal compensation for non-monetary losses
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˈdæmɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˈdamɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
(law) compensation awarded by a court for losses that cannot be easily quantified in monetary terms, such as pain and suffering or loss of amenity.

Examples

General damages in law
General damages are often difficult to calculate because they relate to non-economic losses such as emotional distress.
In personal injury cases, plaintiffs may claim both general damages and special damages.
The court awarded general damages to compensate her for the pain she suffered after the accident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: special damages - compensation for losses that can be specifically quantified, such as medical bills or lost earnings
