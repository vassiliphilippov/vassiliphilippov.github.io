misdating

noun
assigning an incorrect date to something
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪsˈdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or result of assigning an incorrect date to something, especially placing it at a time when it could not have existed or occurred

Examples

A misdating error
A simple misdating can lead to significant misunderstandings in archaeological studies.
Due to a misdating of the artifact, researchers believed it was centuries older than it actually was.
The scientist discovered a misdating in the historical records, which altered the entire timeline.
