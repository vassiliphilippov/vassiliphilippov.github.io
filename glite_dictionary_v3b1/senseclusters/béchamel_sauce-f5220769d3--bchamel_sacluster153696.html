béchamel sauce

noun
white sauce of milk, butter, and flour
Pronunciation: / beɪʃəˈmɛl sɔs / (AmE), / beɪʃəˈmɛl sɔːs / (BrE)
A white sauce made by thickening milk with a roux of butter and flour, commonly used as a base in various dishes.

Examples

Classic bechamel sauce
She added a pinch of nutmeg to her béchamel sauce for extra flavor.
The creamy texture of béchamel sauce pairs perfectly with baked vegetables.
To make lasagna, first prepare a béchamel sauce by whisking milk into a butter-flour mixture.

Related Terms
