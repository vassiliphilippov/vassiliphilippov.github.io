King Lear

proper noun
legendary king; Shakespearean tragic character
7.0
Pronunciation: / kɪŋ lɪr / (AmE), / kɪŋ lɪə / (BrE)
a legendary king of ancient Britain and the title character of Shakespeare's tragedy, known for being betrayed by two of his daughters

Examples

King Lear play
In Shakespeare’s play, King Lear descends into madness after being betrayed by his daughters.
Many scholars debate the motivations behind King Lear's tragic decisions.
The role of King Lear is considered one of the greatest challenges for any dramatic actor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lear - the same legendary king or Shakespearean character, sometimes used without 'King'
  • synonym: Macbeth - tragic hero and title character of another Shakespearean play
