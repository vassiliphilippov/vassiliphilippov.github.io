wagger

noun
one who wags
Pronunciation: / ˈwæɡər / (AmE), / ˈwæɡə / (BrE)
a person or animal that wags something, especially a tail, often in a lively or cheerful way

Examples

A tail wagger
The happy dog was a constant wagger whenever its owner came home.
Among all the puppies, Bella was the most enthusiastic wagger in the group.
His reputation as a cheerful wagger made everyone smile at the park.
The friendly dog was a constant wagger whenever someone entered the room.
Among the group of puppies, the smallest was the most enthusiastic wagger.
She called her cat a wagger because it always flicked its tail when happy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-wagger - an animal or person that does not wag
  • synonym: tail-wagger - an animal, especially a dog, that wags its tail
  • synonym: wag - to move something, especially a tail, from side to side repeatedly
