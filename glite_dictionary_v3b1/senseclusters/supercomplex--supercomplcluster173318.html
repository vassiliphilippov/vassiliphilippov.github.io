supercomplex

noun
large protein assembly
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
a large, stable assembly of two or more protein complexes or molecules that function together in a biological system

Examples

Mitochondrial supercomplex
Scientists discovered a new supercomplex in the mitochondria that increases energy production.
The stability of the supercomplex allows multiple enzymes to work together efficiently.
Researchers are studying how the supercomplex assembles and its role in cellular respiration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggregate - a collection of particles or units gathered together
  • degree lower: complex - a group of molecules or proteins bound together
