mycobacteria

noun
group of rod-shaped bacteria; some cause disease
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkoʊbækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkəʊbækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A group of rod-shaped bacteria, some of which live on decaying matter and others cause diseases such as tuberculosis and leprosy.

Examples

Mycobacteria cause diseases
Scientists found mycobacteria in the contaminated water sample.
Some mycobacteria can cause serious diseases in humans, including tuberculosis.
The doctor explained that leprosy is caused by a type of mycobacteria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteria - single-celled microorganisms without a nucleus
