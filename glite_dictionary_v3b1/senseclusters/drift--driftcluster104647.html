drift

verb
cause to be carried by a current
1.5
Pronunciation: / drɪft / (AmE), / drɪft / (BrE)
to cause (something) to be carried along by a current of air or water

Examples

To drift a balloon
The strong wind drifted the balloon far away from its launch site.
The tide began to drift the fishing nets toward the open sea.
They tried to drift the raft closer to shore using the river current.

Related Terms

  • synonym: float - to rest or move on the surface of a liquid without sinking
