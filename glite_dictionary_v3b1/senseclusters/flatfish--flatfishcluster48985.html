flatfish

noun
a fish with a flattened body and both eyes on one side
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflætˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish with a flattened body that swims on its side along the sea floor, with both eyes on the upper side

Examples

A flatfish swims
Because both its eyes are on one side, the flatfish has excellent camouflage.
Fishermen often catch various species of flatfish near the ocean floor.
The flatfish buried itself in the sand to hide from predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish commonly found in coastal waters
  • synonym: halibut - a very large flatfish with a diamond-shaped body
  • synonym: sole - a type of flatfish known for its edible qualities
