equilibrium

noun
state with balanced chemical reaction rates
7.4 1.82/m
Pronunciation: / Àåikw…ôÀàl…™bri…ôm / (AmE), / ÀåiÀêkw…™Ààl…™br…™…ôm / (BrE)
A state in a chemical reaction in which the forward and reverse reactions occur at the same rate, so that the concentrations of the reactants and products remain constant over time.

Examples

Chemical equilibrium
At chemical equilibrium, the concentrations of all reactants and products remain constant over time.
Le Chatelier's principle explains how a system at equilibrium responds to external changes.
When a reaction reaches equilibrium, the rates of the forward and reverse reactions are equal.

Synonyms

  • chemical equilibrium
    5.1 0.04/m
    chemical equilibrium describes the reaction state where forward and reverse rates are equal in chemistry, while equilibrium is a broader term for balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disequilibrium - a state where reaction rates are not balanced in a chemical process
