subscripting

noun
accessing by index
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌskrɪptɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌskrɪptɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of accessing or referring to an element in a sequence, array, or list by its index or position

Examples

Subscripting an array
In many programming languages, subscripting allows you to retrieve specific items from a list or array.
Incorrect subscripting can cause errors if you try to access an index that does not exist.
Learning about subscripting is essential for working with data structures in computer science.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indexing - the process of accessing elements by their position in a data structure
