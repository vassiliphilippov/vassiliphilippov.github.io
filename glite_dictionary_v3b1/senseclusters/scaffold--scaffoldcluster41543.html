scaffold

verb
to support learning
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈskæfəld / (AmE), / ˈskæfəʊld / (BrE)
to support someone’s learning or progress by providing guidance, structure, or assistance as needed

Examples

To scaffold learning
The teacher scaffolded the assignment by breaking it into smaller, manageable steps.
Parents often scaffold their children's learning by offering hints and encouragement.
Effective instructors know when to scaffold and when to let students work independently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assist - to help someone do something
  • synonym: guide - to show or help someone how to do something
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or support someone
