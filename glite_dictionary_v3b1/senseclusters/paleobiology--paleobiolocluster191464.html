paleobiology

noun
study of fossil organisms' biology
7.7 8.8
Pronunciation: /peɪ.li.oʊ.baɪ.ˈɑː.lə.dʒiː/ /; / /pæl.i.oʊ.baɪ.ˈɑː.lə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /peɪ.li.əʊ.baɪ.ˈɒl.ə.dʒiː/ /; / /pæl.i.əʊ.baɪ.ˈɒl.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
The branch of paleontology concerned with the scientific study of fossil organisms, focusing on their origin, development, structure, and biology as living entities.

Examples

paleobiology research
His research in paleobiology focuses on the development of early plant life.
Recent advances in paleobiology have shed new light on the extinction of the dinosaurs.
She decided to pursue a career in paleobiology after becoming fascinated by ancient fossils.
Many advances in palaeobiology rely on computer simulations to reconstruct ancient ecosystems.
Recent discoveries in palaeobiology have changed our understanding of early plant development.
She decided to specialize in palaeobiology because she was fascinated by the evolution of prehistoric life.
