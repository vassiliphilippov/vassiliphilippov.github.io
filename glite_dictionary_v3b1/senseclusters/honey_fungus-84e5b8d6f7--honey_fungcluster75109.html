honey fungus

noun
large clonal Armillaria organism
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈhʌn.ɪ ˈfʌŋ.gəs/ (AmE), /ˈhʌn.ɪ ˈfʌŋ.gəs/ (BrE)
(in ecology/mycology) a single, often very large clonal organism of Armillaria species, made up of interconnected mycelium and rhizomorphs that spread through soil and can kill trees.

Examples

Giant honey fungus network
Scientists discovered a massive honey fungus clone whose mycelium covered nearly the entire woodland.
Foresters warned that the spreading honey fungus had killed dozens of mature trees on the slope.
Genetic tests confirmed the giant honey fungus in Minnesota was a single individual spanning hectares.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Armillaria - a genus of fungi that includes species forming honey fungus; often forms extensive clonal colonies
  • synonym: mycelium - the network of fungal threads (hyphae) that makes up the main body of a fungus; the living tissue of a clonal fungus
