deliberalization

noun
making less liberal
Pronunciation: / diˌlɪbərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌlɪbərələˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making policies, laws, or systems less liberal, especially in politics, economics, or society

Examples

Deliberalization of policies
The country experienced deliberalization as new regulations replaced previous free-market policies.
Critics argue that deliberalization can slow economic growth and reduce individual freedoms.
After years of reform, a wave of deliberalization changed the political landscape.
The new government implemented deliberalization policies that limited freedom of speech and assembly.
Rapid deliberalization led to stricter regulations on the press and public protests.
Many citizens protested against the deliberalization of the country's economic and social systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberalization - the process of making policies or systems more liberal
  • synonym: restriction - the act of limiting or controlling something
