ballade

noun
poem form with 3 stanzas and an envoy
Pronunciation: / bəˈlɑd /; / bəˈlɑːd / (AmE), / bəˈlɑːd / (BrE)
a poem or song, especially in a fixed French verse form with three stanzas and a final short stanza, often telling a story and using a repeated refrain

Examples

A ballade poem
She composed a ballade in honor of her favorite poet.
Students were asked to write a ballade as part of their poetry assignment.
The medieval ballade often featured themes of courtly love.
The poet recited a moving ballade about lost love at the festival.
She composed a ballade that captured the spirit of her hometown.
Medieval musicians often performed a ballade to entertain the royal court.

  • antonym: ode - a lyric poem expressing praise or emotion
