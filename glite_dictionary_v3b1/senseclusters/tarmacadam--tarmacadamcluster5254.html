tarmacadam

noun
paved surface bound with tar and stones
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑːrməˈkædəm /; / ˌtɑːr.məˈkæd.əm / (AmE), / ˌtɑːməˈkædəm /; / ˌtɑː.məˈkæd.əm / (BrE)
a material made from tar and crushed stone, used for making road or pavement surfaces

Examples

Tarmacadam road surface
Early roads were often constructed using tarmacadam instead of modern asphalt.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: macadam - road surface made by compacting small stones without tar
