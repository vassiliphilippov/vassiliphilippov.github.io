preprimary

noun
early childhood school
3.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpriÀêÀàpra…™Àåm…õri / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀêÀàpra…™m…ôri / (BrE)
a school, class, or educational program for children before they begin primary school

Examples

A preprimary class
Many parents enroll their children in a preprimary to help them develop social skills before starting school.
The new preprimary offers a creative curriculum for children aged three to five.
Attending a preprimary can make the transition to primary school much easier for young children.

Synonyms

  • nursery school
    1.6 1.12/m
    nursery school gives planned lessons to prepare children for primary, while preprimary refers to any program before children start primary school.
  • nursery
    1.0 2.96/m
    nursery cares for and sometimes teaches very young children, often acting as childcare, while preprimary means formal programs before primary school.

Related Terms
