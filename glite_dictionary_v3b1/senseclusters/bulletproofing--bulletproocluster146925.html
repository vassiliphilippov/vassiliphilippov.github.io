bulletproofing

noun
making plans robust
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlɪtˌpruːfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪtˌpruːfɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making a plan, argument, or system as strong as possible to prevent criticism or failure

Examples

Bulletproofing a plan
The team spent hours bulletproofing their business plan before presenting it to investors.
Effective bulletproofing of your argument can help you win difficult debates.
She focused on bulletproofing the system to ensure it could handle any unexpected problems.
