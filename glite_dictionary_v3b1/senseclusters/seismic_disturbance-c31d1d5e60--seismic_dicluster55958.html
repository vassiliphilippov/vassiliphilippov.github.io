seismic disturbance

noun
earth's crust agitation (geology)
6.1 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsaÉªzmÉªk dÉªËˆstÉËbÉ™ns / (AmE), / ËˆsaÉªzmÉªk dÉªËˆstÉœËbÉ™ns / (BrE)
a sudden shaking, movement, or agitation of the earth's crust, often caused by natural forces such as an earthquake or volcanic activity.

Examples

A seismic disturbance near volcano
A minor seismic disturbance was recorded near the volcano late last night.
Residents reported feeling a seismic disturbance just before the main earthquake struck.
The scientists analyzed data from the seismic disturbance to predict future activity.

Synonyms

  • seismic event
    4.6 0.12/m
    a seismic event is a natural occurrence releasing energy in the Earth's crust, while a seismic disturbance names the resulting shaking or movement.
  • earthquake
    2.2 26/m
    an earthquake is a sudden strong shaking often causing damage, while a seismic disturbance is a broader term for earth movement.
  • seism
    9.5 0.00/m
    a seism is the scientific term for an earthquake or tremor used in reports, while a seismic disturbance sounds more general and plain.
  • quake
    5.1 6.42/m
    a quake is the informal, short word people use for an earthquake, while a seismic disturbance is more formal and general.
  • shock
    6.0 1.25/m
    a shock is a sudden violent earth movement often linked to an earthquake, while a seismic disturbance covers any sudden ground agitation.
  • tremor
    4.9 0.85/m
    a tremor is a slight, often weak earth shake, while a seismic disturbance can mean stronger or more general ground movement.
