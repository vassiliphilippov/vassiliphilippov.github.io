meat house

noun
building for smoking meat or fish
6.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / mit ha äs / (AmE), / miÀêt ha äs / (BrE)
a small building, often on a farm, used for storing or curing meat (sometimes fish)

Examples

Old meat house
Smoke drifted from the old meat house at the edge of the farm.
The freshly caught fish were hung in the meat house to be cured for winter.
They built a new meat house to preserve their game throughout the hunting season.
During the tour, we saw a 19th-century meat house behind the main farmhouse.
On the old plantation, they kept smoked hams in the meat house all winter.
The farmer repaired the roof of his meat house before stocking it for the season.

Synonyms
