octanol

noun
eight-carbon alcohol
Pronunciation: / ˈɑktəˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˈɒktənɒl / (BrE)
a colorless, oily alcohol with eight carbon atoms, used as a solvent or in chemistry and industry

Examples

Octanol solvent
The chemist measured the partition coefficient using octanol as the organic phase.
Because of its low toxicity, octanol is sometimes used as a solvent in laboratories.
The production of perfumes often involves octanol due to its pleasant scent.
The laboratory used octanol to test how well the new drug dissolves in fats.
Perfume manufacturers often add octanol to create a smooth, long-lasting scent.
The oily texture of octanol makes it useful as a solvent in chemical experiments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: butanol - an alcohol with four carbon atoms
  • degree higher: decanol - an alcohol with ten carbon atoms
  • degree lower: hexanol - an alcohol with six carbon atoms, used as a solvent
