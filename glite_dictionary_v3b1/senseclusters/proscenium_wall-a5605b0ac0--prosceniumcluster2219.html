proscenium wall

noun
wall separating stage from auditorium in a theater
6.6
Pronunciation: / proʊˈsiːniəm wɔl / (AmE), / prəˈsiːniəm wɔːl / (BrE)
the wall that separates the stage from the auditorium in a modern theater, often containing the proscenium arch through which the audience views the stage

Examples

The proscenium wall
Ornate decorations were added to the proscenium wall to enhance the theater's visual appeal.
The actors waited behind the proscenium wall until the curtain rose.
The architect designed a new proscenium wall that improved both acoustics and sightlines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proscenium arch - the arch framing the opening between the stage and the auditorium
