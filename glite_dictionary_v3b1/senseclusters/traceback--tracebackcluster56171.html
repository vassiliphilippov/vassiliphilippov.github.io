traceback

noun
tracking origin
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtreɪs.bæk/ (AmE), /ˈtreɪs.bæk/ (BrE)
the process of tracking something back to its origin or source, often to find the cause of a problem or event

Examples

Traceback to the source
The software generated a traceback to help developers find the error in the code.
After the contamination was discovered, a traceback identified the original supplier.
A detailed traceback can reveal how a product moved through the supply chain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cover-up - an attempt to prevent people from discovering the truth
  • synonym: investigation - the act of examining something to discover facts or causes
  • synonym: source - the place or thing from which something originates
