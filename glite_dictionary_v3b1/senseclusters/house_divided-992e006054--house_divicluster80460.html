house divided

noun
group or organization in conflict
Pronunciation: / haʊs dɪˈvaɪdəd / (AmE), / haʊs dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (BrE)
a group or organization that is internally in conflict or split by opposing opinions

Examples

A house divided group
After the leadership disagreement, the company became a house divided over its future direction.
During the heated campaign, the party turned into a house divided on major policy issues.
The Smiths found themselves a house divided after the children chose different career paths.
