inclusion

noun
added part or feature
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkluʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
something that is added to or contained within something else, often as a part or feature

Examples

Inclusion of breakfast
The price of the tour includes the inclusion of all meals and entrance fees.
Scientists studied the mineral inclusions found inside the ancient rock sample.
Her report was praised for the careful inclusion of relevant statistics and data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole
  • antonym: exclusion - the act of leaving something out
