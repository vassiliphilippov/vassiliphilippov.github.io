microcrack

noun
tiny crack in material
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌkræk /; / ˈmaɪkroʊkræk / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌkræk /; / ˈmaɪkrəʊkræk / (BrE)
a very small crack in a material, often too tiny to see and sometimes an early sign of damage

Examples

A microcrack in glass
The engineer used a microscope to detect a microcrack in the glass panel.
Repeated stress can cause a microcrack to grow into a larger fracture over time.
Manufacturers test materials to ensure no microcrack is present before shipping products.
Engineers detected a microcrack in the bridge support during a routine inspection.
A single microcrack can eventually lead to a major failure if not repaired.
Repeated stress caused several microcracks to appear in the metal component.

  • synonym: fracture - a break or crack in a hard material
