zero point

noun
baseline value on a measurement scale
Pronunciation: / ˈziɹoʊ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the position on a scale or instrument from which measurements are made, usually corresponding to the value zero.

Examples

The zero point on scale
Any deviation from the zero point indicates a change in the system's state.
He marked the zero point on the ruler before beginning his measurements.
The thermometer was calibrated so that the zero point coincided with the freezing point of water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: origin - the starting point or fixed reference in a system of coordinates or measurements
