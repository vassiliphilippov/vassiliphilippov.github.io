elenchus

noun
logical refutation method
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛŋkəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛŋkəs / (BrE)
a logical method of questioning or refutation used to expose contradictions in someone's beliefs or arguments

Examples

Socratic elenchus method
The philosopher used elenchus to reveal inconsistencies in his opponent's argument.
Through elenchus, Socrates encouraged his students to examine their own beliefs critically.
A skilled debater may employ elenchus to challenge assumptions during a discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Socratic method - a form of cooperative argumentative dialogue to stimulate critical thinking
  • synonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong
