whitewasher

noun
team with total victory
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtwɒʃə / (BrE)
a team or competitor that wins every game or match in a series, achieving a complete victory

Examples

Series whitewasher
The visiting team were whitewashers, winning every match in the three-game series without conceding a point.
After their flawless performance, the press called them the tournament's whitewashers.
No one expected the underdogs to become whitewashers by sweeping the finals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loser - a person or team that is defeated in a competition
