pestle

noun
machine for pounding or crushing ores
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛsəl / (BrE)
a machine consisting of a heavy bar that moves up and down to pound or crush ores or other materials

Examples

Mechanical pestle machine
In the metallurgy lab, a mechanical pestle was employed to break down mineral samples efficiently.
The old mining facility still uses a pestle to crush raw ore before further processing.
Workers operated the large pestle with care to avoid any accidents in the crushing room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mortar - a strong bowl in which substances are crushed, often used with a pestle
