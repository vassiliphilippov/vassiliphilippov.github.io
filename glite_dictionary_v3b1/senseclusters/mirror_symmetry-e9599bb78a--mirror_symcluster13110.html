mirror symmetry

noun
symmetry under right-left reflection in physics
4.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪr.ər ˈsɪm.ə.triː/ (AmE), /ˈmɪr.ə ˈsɪm.ə.triː/ (BrE)
The property of a system, especially in physics, in which physical laws or the system itself remain unchanged if left and right are swapped, as if seen in a mirror.

Examples

Mirror symmetry in physics
Mathematicians also study mirror symmetry in the context of geometry and string theory.
Some physical processes violate mirror symmetry, leading to interesting asymmetries in nature.
The concept of mirror symmetry is fundamental in certain areas of particle physics.
