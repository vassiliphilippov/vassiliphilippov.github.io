underripe

adjective
not fully mature
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈraɪp / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈraɪp / (BrE)
not fully developed or mature; not ready to eat or use (especially for fruit, vegetables, or other things)

Examples

An underripe fruit
The underripe tomatoes were too hard to slice for the salad.
If you eat an underripe peach, it might taste sour and be difficult to chew.
She picked the apples too early, and most of them were still underripe.
The peaches were still underripe and too hard to eat.
His underripe plan needed more research before it could be approved.
You should not pick the tomatoes while they are underripe.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: overripe - past the point of being ripe, often too soft or spoiled
  • antonym: ripe - fully developed and ready to eat
  • synonym: unripe - not fully mature or developed
