appellee

noun
person appealed against
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈliː / (AmE), / ˌæpɛˈliː / (BrE)
the person or party in a legal case against whom an appeal is filed

Examples

The appellee in court
The appellee argued that the lower court's decision should be upheld.
After the appeal was filed, the appellee prepared a detailed response for the court.
In this case, the appellee was a large corporation defending its previous victory.
The appellee presented strong arguments to uphold the original court decision.
After the appeal was filed, the appellee had thirty days to respond.
The judge ruled in favor of the appellee after reviewing all the evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appellant - the person who files an appeal in a court case
  • synonym: respondent - the party who responds to an appeal or petition in court
