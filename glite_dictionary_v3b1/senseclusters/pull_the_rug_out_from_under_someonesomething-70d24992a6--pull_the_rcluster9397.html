pull the rug out from under (someone/something)

verb
suddenly withdraw support or stability
8.6
Pronunciation: / pʊl ðə rʌɡ aʊt frəm ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / pʊl ðə rʌɡ aʊt frəm ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to suddenly and unexpectedly withdraw support or assistance from someone, causing them to be in a difficult or unstable situation

Examples

To pull the rug out from under investors
Investors felt the company pulled the rug out from under them after changing its policies overnight.
The management pulled the rug out from under the entire department by announcing the closure with no warning.
When her partner suddenly quit the project, it really pulled the rug out from under her plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sabotage - to deliberately destroy or undermine something or someone; similar effect, but usually intentional harm
  • antonym: support - to give assistance or help; opposite in meaning
  • synonym: undermine - to weaken someone or something gradually or secretly, similar idea but less sudden
