queen bee

noun
fertile female bee in a colony
Pronunciation: / kwiːn biː / (AmE), / kwiːn biː / (BrE)
the fertile female bee in a colony, responsible for laying eggs and reproducing

Examples

A queen bee in the hive
Each spring, the colony may produce a new queen bee to replace the old one.
In a beehive, only the queen bee is capable of laying fertilized eggs.
The queen bee is essential to the survival of the hive, as she lays all the eggs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drone - a male bee whose primary role is to mate with the queen
  • antonym: worker bee - a sterile female bee that performs labor in the colony
