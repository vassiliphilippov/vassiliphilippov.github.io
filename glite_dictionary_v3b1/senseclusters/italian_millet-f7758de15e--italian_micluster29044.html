Italian millet

noun
grain and forage grass species
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæliən ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈtalɪən ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a coarse, drought-resistant annual grass grown for grain, hay, or forage, especially in Europe, Asia, and the United States

Examples

Italian millet grain
Italian millet is valued by farmers for its ability to grow in dry, poor soils.
Many Asian countries cultivate Italian millet as a staple food crop.
The livestock were fed hay made from Italian millet during the drought.
