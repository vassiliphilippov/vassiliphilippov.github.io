relative frequency

noun
ratio of occurrences to total, in statistics
5.1 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / Ààr…õl…ôt…™v ÀàfriÀêkw…ônsi / (AmE), / Ààrel…ôt…™v ÀàfriÀêkw…ônsi / (BrE)
the ratio of the number of occurrences of a particular event or category to the total number of observations, often expressed as a proportion or percentage

Examples

The relative frequency ratio
Histograms often use relative frequency instead of absolute count to compare distributions.
In a survey, the relative frequency of students who prefer coffee was 40%.
To find the relative frequency, divide the category count by the total number of responses.

Synonyms

  • proportion
    3.5 30/m
    proportion describes a part compared to the whole, while relative frequency measures how often an event occurs among all observations.
  • relative incidence
    7.4 0.00/m
    relative incidence compares how often something happens in one group versus another, while relative frequency compares occurrences to total observations.
