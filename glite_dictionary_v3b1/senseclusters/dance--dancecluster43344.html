dance

verb
move rhythmically to music
2.1 1.0
Pronunciation: / dæns / (AmE), / dɑːns / (BrE)
to move your body and feet rhythmically, usually to music, often as a form of expression or entertainment

Examples

To dance to music
After dinner, they were dancing under the stars on the terrace.
Children danced around the room, laughing and clapping their hands.
We love to dance together at parties whenever our favorite song comes on.
Children danced around the playground in a circle.
He dances beautifully, moving across the stage with perfect timing.
The couple loves to dance together at parties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: march - to walk with regular steps, often in time to music
  • synonym: prance - to move with high, springy steps, often showing off or expressing joy
  • synonym: waltz - to dance a particular ballroom dance in triple time
