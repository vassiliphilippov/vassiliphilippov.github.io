constructionism

noun
learning by making
7.7
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌkʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
an educational theory that people learn best by actively creating things that can be shared with others as part of the learning process

Examples

Constructionism in education
The school implemented constructionism by having students design and build their own science projects.
Many educators believe constructionism helps students develop deeper understanding through hands-on activities.
Using constructionism, the teacher encouraged students to create digital stories to demonstrate their learning.
