Yizkor

noun
Jewish memorial prayer
Pronunciation: / ˈjɪzkɔr / (AmE), / ˈjɪzkɔː / (BrE)
a Jewish memorial prayer or service held to remember and pray for deceased relatives, especially during certain religious holidays

Examples

Yizkor memorial prayer
Many families gather at the synagogue to recite Yizkor on Yom Kippur.
She lit a candle and said Yizkor in memory of her late father.
During the service, the congregation stood together for the solemn Yizkor prayer.
Every year on Yom Kippur, the congregation gathers for Yizkor to honor the memory of loved ones.
She lit a candle before attending Yizkor at the synagogue with her family.
During Yizkor, prayers are recited for relatives who have passed away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kaddish - a Jewish prayer recited in memory of the dead
  • synonym: memorial service - a ceremony to remember someone who has died
