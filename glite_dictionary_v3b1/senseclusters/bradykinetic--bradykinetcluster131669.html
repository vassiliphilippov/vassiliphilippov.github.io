bradykinetic

noun
person with slow movement
9.9
Pronunciation: /breɪ.dɪ.kɪ.ˈnet.ɪk/ /; / /bræd.ɪ.kɪ.ˈnet.ɪk/ (AmE), /bræd.ɪ.kɪ.ˈnet.ɪk/ /; / /breɪ.dɪ.kɪ.ˈnet.ɪk/ (BrE)
a person who has abnormally slow movement, typically due to a medical condition such as Parkinson's disease

Examples

A bradykinetic walks
The physical therapist designed special exercises for the bradykinetic to improve mobility.
Doctors observed that the bradykinetic struggled with simple daily activities like buttoning a shirt.
Support groups can help a bradykinetic cope with the challenges of slow movement.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: akinetic - a person who is unable to move voluntarily
  • synonym: bradykinesia - abnormally slow movement, especially as a symptom of disease
  • antonym: hyperkinetic - a person who moves excessively or abnormally fast
