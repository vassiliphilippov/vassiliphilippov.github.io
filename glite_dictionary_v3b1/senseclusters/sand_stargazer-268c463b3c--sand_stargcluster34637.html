sand stargazer

noun
small tropical fish with stalked eyes that burrows in sand
Pronunciation: / sænd ˈstɑrˌɡeɪzər / (AmE), / sand ˈstɑːˌɡeɪzə / (BrE)
a small, pale fish found in tropical waters of the Americas, noted for its eyes on stalks atop the head and a habit of burrowing in sand to ambush prey

Examples

A sand stargazer fish
Divers often spot a sand stargazer ambushing small prey near coral reefs.
The sand stargazer buries itself in the shallow sand, leaving only its stalked eyes above the surface.
The unique appearance of the sand stargazer makes it fascinating to marine biologists studying camouflage.
