shoot holes in

verb
to expose flaws or criticize
Pronunciation: / ʃuːt hoʊlz ɪn / (AmE), / ʃuːt həʊlz ɪn / (BrE)
to find and point out serious faults or flaws in; to criticize effectively by revealing weaknesses, especially in an argument or proposal

Examples

To shoot holes in a plan
Analysts quickly shot holes in the proposal, highlighting its many weaknesses.
The lawyer tried to shoot holes in the witness's testimony during cross-examination.
Whenever I suggest a new idea, someone is ready to shoot holes in it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criticize - to express disapproval of or point out the faults of someone or something
  • synonym: refute - to prove (a statement or theory) to be wrong or false
