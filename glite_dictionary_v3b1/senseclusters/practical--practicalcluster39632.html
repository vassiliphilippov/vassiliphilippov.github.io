practical

adjective
almost; nearly (not strictly but essentially)
Pronunciation: / ˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
Almost or nearly; being such in most respects though not strictly or officially so.

Examples

Practical disaster
After the storm, the town was in practical ruin, though a few buildings remained standing.
Given the delays, finishing on time is practically impossible.
With no money left, their trip was a practical failure before it even began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: virtual - being such in essence or effect though not in name or form
