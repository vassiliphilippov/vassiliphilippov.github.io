prism spectroscope

noun
optical instrument for analyzing light with a prism
5.1 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪz.əm ˈspek.trə.skoʊp/ (AmE), /ˈprɪz.əm ˈspek.trə.skəʊp/ (BrE)
an optical instrument that uses a prism to separate light into its component wavelengths for spectrographic analysis

Examples

A prism spectroscope device
By passing sunlight through a prism spectroscope, they observed the characteristic bands of different elements.
Early astronomers relied on the prism spectroscope to study the spectra of stars.
