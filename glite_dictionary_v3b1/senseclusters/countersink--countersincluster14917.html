countersink

noun
tool for enlarging hole for screw head
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌsɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsɪŋk / (BrE)
A tool or drill bit for enlarging the upper part of a hole to allow the head of a screw to sit flush with or below the surface.

Examples

A countersink tool
He used a countersink to prepare the hole for the screw head.
Make sure to choose the correct countersink size for the hardware you're using.
This toolkit comes with a high-quality countersink for finishing woodwork projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reamer - tool for widening or smoothing a hole
