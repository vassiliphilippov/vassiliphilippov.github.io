puerperal fever

noun
infection after childbirth or abortion
3.2
Pronunciation: / pjʊˈɜrpərəl ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / pjʊəˈpɜːrəl ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a serious infection contracted by a woman during childbirth or abortion, usually caused by unsanitary conditions; now rare due to modern hygiene

Examples

Puerperal fever infection
Before the advent of antiseptic procedures, puerperal fever was a leading cause of maternal deaths in hospitals.
Doctors in the nineteenth century struggled to control outbreaks of puerperal fever among new mothers.
The introduction of handwashing drastically reduced cases of puerperal fever in maternity wards.
