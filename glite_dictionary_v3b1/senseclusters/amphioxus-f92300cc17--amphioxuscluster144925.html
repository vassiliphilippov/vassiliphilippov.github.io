Amphioxus

noun
genus of primitive marine chordates
Pronunciation: / ˌæmfiˈɑksəs / (AmE), / ˌæmfiˈɒksəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, lance-shaped marine animals that are primitive chordates, serving as the type genus of the family Amphioxidae.

Examples

The Amphioxus animal

Scientists often study the Amphioxus to better understand the early evolution of vertebrates.
The Amphioxus is notable for its simple body structure and lack of a true backbone.
