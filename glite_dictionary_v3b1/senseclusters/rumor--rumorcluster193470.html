rumor

verb
spread unverified information
3.9 4.49/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀêm…ö / (AmE), / ÀàruÀêm…ô / (BrE)
to circulate or spread a story or piece of information whose truth is uncertain or not confirmed

Examples

To rumor bad news
It was rumored that the CEO would resign by the end of the month.
News of a possible merger was rumored throughout the office before any official statement was made.
The actress was frequently rumored to be moving abroad, but she never confirmed it.
It was widely rumoured that the company would be sold.
The media rumoured that the actor was secretly married.
Throughout the office, people rumoured about the manager's sudden resignation.

Synonyms

  • bruit
    9.9 0.06/m
    bruit spreads reports widely and often in formal writing, while rumor means spreading uncertain information among people.
  • bandy about
    6.8 0.66/m
    bandy about is used when people pass ideas around casually without care, while rumor focuses on spreading uncertain information.
  • buzz about
    3.4 0.61/m
    buzz about talks excitedly and spreads quickly among people, while rumor is the act of sharing unconfirmed information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirm - to establish the truth or validity of something
  • antonym: deny - to declare something is not true; opposite of spreading rumors
  • synonym: gossip - to spread idle talk or rumors about others
  • synonym: report - to tell or disseminate information; often more formal or official than rumor
