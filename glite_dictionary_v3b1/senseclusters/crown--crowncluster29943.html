crown

noun
wreath worn on head as symbol of victory
2.0
Pronunciation: / kraʊn / (AmE), / kraʊn / (BrE)
a circular ornamental headdress, wreath, or award worn on the head as a symbol of authority, victory, or honor

Examples

A royal crown
In ancient Greece, winning athletes were awarded a crown of olive branches.
The soldier accepted the crown with pride after his victory.
Victors in Roman times often wore a crown to mark their triumph.
After the ceremony, the winner was presented with a jeweled crown as a symbol of her new title.
She admired the golden crown displayed in the museum's royal artifacts collection.

After winning the final match, the team claimed the crown for the third year in a row.
She is determined to win the national crown this season.
