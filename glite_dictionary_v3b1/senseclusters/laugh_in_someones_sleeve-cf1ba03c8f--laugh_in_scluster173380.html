laugh in someone's sleeve

verb

Pronunciation: /læf ɪn ˈsʌm.wənz sliːv/ /; / /læf ɪn ˈsʌm.wʌnz sliːv/ (AmE), /lɑːf ɪn ˈsʌm.wənz sliːv/ /; / /lɑːf ɪn ˈsʌm.wʌnz sliːv/ (BrE)
to secretly find something amusing or take quiet pleasure in someone else's misfortune or mistake without showing it openly
