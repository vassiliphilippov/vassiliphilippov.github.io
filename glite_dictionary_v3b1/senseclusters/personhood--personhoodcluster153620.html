personhood

noun
legal status for artificial entities
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpɜːr.sən.hʊd/ (AmE), /ˈpɜː.sən.hʊd/ (BrE)
(in law, ethics, and technology) the status or recognition of a digital or artificial entity as a person with legal rights, responsibilities, or moral standing.

Examples

AI personhood
Courts are beginning to consider whether advanced AI deserves legal personhood before regulating their actions.
The conference brought philosophers and engineers to debate the consequences of granting robots personhood and voting rights.
Companies argued that recognizing limited digital personhood could simplify liability for autonomous system failures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legal person - an entity recognized by law as having rights and duties, such as corporations or (arguably) machines
  • synonym: moral status - the status of an entity as deserving moral consideration or ethical regard
