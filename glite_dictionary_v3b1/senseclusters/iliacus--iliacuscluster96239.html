iliacus

noun
pelvic muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlaɪ.ə.kəs/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlaɪ.ə.kəs/ (BrE)
a muscle in the pelvis that helps flex the thigh at the hip joint

Examples

The iliacus muscle
The iliacus is essential for movements like walking and running.
An injury to the iliacus can cause pain in the hip area.
Physical therapists often target the iliacus when treating hip flexor strains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: psoas - a muscle that works with the iliacus to flex the hip
