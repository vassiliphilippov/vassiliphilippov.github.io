Cornish fowl

noun
English breed of domestic chicken
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnɪʃ faʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪʃ faʊl / (BrE)
an English breed of compact domestic chicken, raised mainly for crossbreeding to produce meat birds (roasters)

Examples

The Cornish fowl breed
Many supermarkets sell roasters that are crossbreeds of the Cornish fowl.
The Cornish fowl is prized by farmers for its rapid growth and meaty body.
The agricultural show featured a champion Cornish fowl in the poultry section.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broiler - a chicken bred and raised for meat
