unsling

verb
to remove from sling
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈslɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈslɪŋ / (BrE)
to remove something from a sling, or from being slung or hanging (especially from your shoulder, back, or a support)

Examples

To unsling a backpack
The hiker unslung his backpack and set it down on the ground.
She unslung the camera from her shoulder to take a quick photo.
After the long march, the soldiers unslung their rifles to rest.
He unslung his backpack and placed it gently on the bench.
The soldier quickly unslings his rifle as he enters the tent.
After a long hike, she unslung her bag and stretched her shoulders.
She unslung her bag and set it on the table.
The soldier unslung his rifle before entering the tent.
He carefully unslings the camera from his shoulder after a long hike.
