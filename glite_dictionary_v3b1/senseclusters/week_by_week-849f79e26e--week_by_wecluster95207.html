week by week

adverb
on a weekly basis
1.1 0.26/m

happening, changing, or developing with each successive week; every week

Examples

Week by week progress
Her confidence grew week by week throughout her training sessions.
The garden flourished week by week as spring turned into summer.
The situation improved gradually, week by week, until things returned to normal.

Synonyms

  • week after week
    1.0 0.88/m
    week after week is repeated over many successive weeks and often sounds complaining, while week by week shows change or progress each week.
  • each week
    1.0 7.08/m
    each week means something happens once in every individual week, while week by week emphasizes events changing or developing from one week to the next.
  • every week
    1.0 11/m
    every week says an action repeats at weekly intervals, while week by week suggests a step by step change across successive weeks.
  • weekly
    1.0 26/m
    weekly describes a regular, scheduled event or publication once each week, while week by week highlights gradual change from one week to the next.
