kink

verb
to curl or twist tightly
8.8 4.4
Pronunciation: /kɪŋk/ (AmE), /kɪŋk/ (BrE)
to form or cause a sharp twist, bend, or curl in something that is normally straight

Examples

To kink the wire
Be careful not to kink the wire when installing the new lamp.
If you kink your hair too tightly, it may become damaged.
She tried not to kink the hose while watering the garden.
If the hose kinks, the water will stop flowing.
She tried to untangle her necklace, but it kept kinking again.
The telephone cord often kinked after being used.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bend - to change shape so that something is no longer straight
  • synonym: crimp - to make into small folds or ridges, especially in hair or fabric
  • synonym: curl - to form into a spiral or coil
