clon

noun
group of genetically identical cells or organisms
Pronunciation: / kloʊn / (AmE), / kləʊn / (BrE)
a group of genetically identical cells or organisms derived from a single cell or individual by asexual reproduction

Examples

A clon of cells
A cancerous tumor may arise from a single mutated cell, resulting in a clon of identical malignant cells.
Each clon in the colony shared the same genetic makeup as the original organism.
Scientists studied the behavior of the clon under different environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clone - an organism or cell, or group of organisms or cells, produced asexually from one ancestor to which they are genetically identical
