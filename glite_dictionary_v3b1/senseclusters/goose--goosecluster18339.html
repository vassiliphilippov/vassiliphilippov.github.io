goose

noun
large migratory waterbird
2.4 1.0
Pronunciation: /guːs/ (AmE), /guːs/ (BrE)
A large, web-footed, long-necked waterbird, typically migratory and gregarious, usually larger and less aquatic than a duck.

Examples

Wild goose migration
Every autumn, flocks of wild geese migrate south for the winter.
The goose waddled across the field, honking loudly at the passersby.
We fed breadcrumbs to the goose at the pond in the park.
