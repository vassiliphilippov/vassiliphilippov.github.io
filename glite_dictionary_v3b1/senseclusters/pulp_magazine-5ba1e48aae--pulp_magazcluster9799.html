pulp magazine

noun
inexpensive magazine on poor quality paper
3.7 6.2
Pronunciation: /pʌlp ˈmæg.ə.ziːn/ /; / /pʊlp ˈmæg.ə.ziːn/ (AmE), /pʌlp ˈmæɡ.ə.ziːn/ (BrE)
A magazine printed on cheap, rough paper, typically featuring sensational stories and illustrations, especially popular in the early to mid-20th century.

Examples

Old pulp magazines
During the 1930s, pulp magazines were the main source of popular adventure stories.
He collected vintage pulp magazines because he loved their colorful covers and wild tales.
Writers often got their start publishing stories in pulp magazines before moving on to more prestigious publications.
