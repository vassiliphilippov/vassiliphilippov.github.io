dewcap

noun
moss capsule covering
Pronunciation: / ˈduːˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌkæp / (BrE)
a cap-like structure that covers the capsule of certain mosses, protecting developing spores

Examples

The dewcap covers capsule
The dewcap falls off as the moss capsule matures and releases its spores.
Scientists observed the dewcap protecting the young spores during their study of moss reproduction.
Without its dewcap, the moss capsule would be exposed to harsh environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calyptra - a protective cap covering the capsule in mosses
