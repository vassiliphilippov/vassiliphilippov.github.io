ddC

noun
HIV antiviral drug (NARTI)
Pronunciation: / diː diː siː / (AmE), / diː diː siː / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used to treat HIV infection; a nucleoside analog reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NARTI)

Examples

ddC antiviral drug
Dideoxycytidine, also known as ddC, was one of the first NRTIs approved for HIV therapy.
Doctors monitor patients on ddC for possible side effects such as neuropathy.
Treatment regimens including ddC have been effective against some strains of HIV.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stavudine - another antiretroviral drug and NRTI used to treat HIV
  • synonym: zidovudine - another nucleoside analog reverse transcriptase inhibitor used in HIV treatment
