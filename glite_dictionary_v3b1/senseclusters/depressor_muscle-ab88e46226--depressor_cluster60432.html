depressor muscle

noun
muscle that lowers a body part
8.2
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛsər ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a skeletal muscle that pulls a part of the body downward

Examples

The depressor muscle pulls down
A surgeon identified the depressor muscle responsible for moving the lower lip downward.
Damage to the depressor muscle can affect facial expressions.
The depressor muscle in the jaw helps open the mouth during eating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: levator muscle - a muscle that raises a body part
