bridge

verb
cross by means of a bridge
3.3
Pronunciation: / brɪdʒ / (AmE), / brɪdʒ / (BrE)
to build a bridge over or across something, or to cross over something by means of a bridge

Examples

To bridge a river
After the storm, workers managed to bridge the flooded river to deliver supplies.
The engineers planned to bridge the ravine by building a sturdy structure.
Tourists bridge the moat to reach the historic castle.
Engineers managed to bridge the wide river within a year.
The company plans to bridge the canyon to improve access to the town.
They hope to bridge the gap with a new pedestrian walkway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cross - to go across to the other side
  • synonym: span - to extend across or over; often used about bridges or arches
