sublattice

noun
subset lattice structure
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈlætɪs / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈlætɪs / (BrE)
a subset of a lattice that forms a lattice itself under the same operations as the original lattice

Examples

A sublattice in math
In mathematics, a sublattice must satisfy the same properties as the original lattice.
The magnetic ions in the material are arranged in a distinct sublattice within the crystal.
Researchers studied how the sublattice structure affects the material's electronic properties.
