misbinding

noun
incorrect molecular attachment
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈbaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈbaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
an incorrect or unintended attachment between biological molecules that disrupts normal function

Examples

Antibody misbinding
The scientist studied how misbinding of antibodies could lead to autoimmune diseases.
Drug developers work to minimize misbinding to avoid unwanted side effects.
A single instance of misbinding can disrupt the entire biological pathway.

Related Terms

  • antonym: binding - the process of attaching or joining molecules together
  • synonym: misfolding - incorrect folding of a protein molecule
