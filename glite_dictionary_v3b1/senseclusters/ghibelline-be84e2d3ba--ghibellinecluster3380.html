Ghibelline

noun
medieval imperial supporter
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪbəliːn / (AmE), / ˈɡɪbəliːn / (BrE)
a member of a medieval Italian political faction that supported the Holy Roman Emperor against the papal party

Examples

A Ghibelline leader
During the thirteenth century, Florence was divided between the Ghibellines and the Guelfs.
The Ghibelline faction often clashed with papal supporters in northern Italian cities.
Dante's family was aligned with the Ghibellines during the political struggles of medieval Italy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Guelf - a member of the rival medieval Italian faction supporting the Pope
