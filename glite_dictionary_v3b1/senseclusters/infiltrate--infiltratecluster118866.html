to pass into or through something (such as a material or substance), especially gradually or by filtering

Examples

Chemicals can infiltrate the soil and reach the groundwater.
Heavy rain allowed water to infiltrate the basement walls over time.
Light easily infiltrates the thin curtains in the morning.

Synonyms

  • seep into
    3.0 2.20/m
    seep into describes a liquid slowly leaking through a surface, while infiltrate means passing in gradually through tiny gaps.
  • penetrate
    5.0 4.80/m
    penetrate means to go into or through something, often forcefully or fully, while infiltrate implies slow, filtered entry.
  • permeate
    6.0 2.85/m
    permeate means to spread through every part of something, while infiltrate focuses on passing in slowly through small openings.
