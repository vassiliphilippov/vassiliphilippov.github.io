coney

noun
old term for a rabbit
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
an old or archaic term for a rabbit or similar small burrowing mammal, such as a pika

Examples

He read an old English tale featuring a wise coney outsmarting a fox.
In medieval times, people often hunted the coney for meat and fur.
The warren was home to dozens of coneys, scurrying between their burrows.
Many early explorers mistook the coney for a type of rabbit.
The coney darts between the rocks to avoid predators in the mountain meadows.
The coney is well adapted to cold, rocky environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rabbit - a small, burrowing mammal with long ears and soft fur
