permafrost

noun
permanently frozen ground layer
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝː.məˌfrɑːst / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.məˌfrɒst / (BrE)
a layer of soil or rock that remains permanently frozen throughout the year, found especially in polar regions

Examples

Thick permafrost layer
Much of northern Siberia is covered by thick layers of permafrost.
Scientists study permafrost to understand its impact on climate change.
The melting of permafrost can release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.
