safe harbor

noun
business strategy for takeover defense
Pronunciation: / seɪf ˈhɑrbɚ / (AmE), / seɪf ˈhɑːbɑː / (BrE)
a strategy by which a company seeks protection from a hostile takeover by acquiring another company that is heavily regulated or has other attributes making the original company less attractive to the acquirer

Examples

Corporate safe harbor
Analysts discussed whether the new purchase would function as a true safe harbor for the board.
By merging with a highly regulated firm, they created a safe harbor to deter potential buyers.
The acquisition gave the company a safe harbor from aggressive takeover attempts.
