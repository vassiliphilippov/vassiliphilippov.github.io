voyageur

noun
long-distance traveler
9.6
Pronunciation: /vɔɪ.ə.ˈʒɜːr/ /; / /vɔɪ.ə.ˈdʒɜːr/ (AmE), /vɔɪ.ə.ˈʒɜː/ /; / /vɔɪ.ə.ˈdʒɜː/ (BrE)
a person who traveled long distances by canoe in North America, especially for exploration or trade (historically during the fur trade era in Canada)

Examples

The voyageur traveled far
The voyageur navigated the river for weeks, carrying furs to distant trading posts.
In Canadian history, a voyageur was known for his strength and endurance on long journeys.
Many stories celebrate the adventures of the legendary voyageur in the wilderness.
The voyageur paddled across the lake with supplies for the trading post.
In Canadian history, the voyageur played a key role in the fur trade.
A group of voyageurs set out at dawn, their canoes loaded with furs and provisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trader - a person who buys and sells goods, often traveling to do so
