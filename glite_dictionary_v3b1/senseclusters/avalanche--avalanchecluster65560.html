avalanche

verb
(of snow) gather into a mass and slide down
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈævəˌlæntʃ / (AmE), / ˈævəlɑːnʃ / (BrE)
to fall or slide down rapidly in a large mass, like snow, rocks, or earth

Examples

Snow avalanches down
Every spring, as the temperature rises, the snow can avalanche suddenly and without warning.
If the snowpack is unstable, it may avalanche after a loud noise or heavy snowfall.
The rescue team saw a massive wall of snow avalanche down the mountain toward the village.
After the heavy rain, mud and rocks began to avalanche down the hillside.
Snow can avalanche unexpectedly, making mountain travel dangerous in winter.
The books stacked on the shelf suddenly avalanched onto the floor.
After the earthquake, boulders avalanched down the mountain, blocking the road below.
Heavy rain caused mud to avalanche into the valley, damaging several homes.
During the storm, snow avalanched off the roof, startling everyone inside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cascade - to fall or pour down in large quantities
  • synonym: slide - to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact with it
  • synonym: tumble - to fall suddenly and uncontrollably
