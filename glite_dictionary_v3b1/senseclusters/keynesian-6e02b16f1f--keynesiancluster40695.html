Keynesian

noun
supporter of Keynesian economic theory

A person who supports or follows the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes, which advocate government intervention in the economy to achieve full employment and stable growth.

Examples

A Keynesian economist
As a proud Keynesian, she advocated for increased government spending during economic downturns.
His critics accused him of being a die-hard Keynesian unwilling to consider alternative theories.
The policies were heavily influenced by Keynesian economists within the administration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Keynesianism - the economic theories and policy prescriptions advocated by John Maynard Keynes
  • antonym: monetarist - a follower of the economic theories emphasizing the role of money supply in economic stability
