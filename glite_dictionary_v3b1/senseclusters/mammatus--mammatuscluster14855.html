mammatus

adjective
clouds with pouches
Pronunciation: / məˈmeɪtəs / (AmE), / məˈmeɪtəs / (BrE)
having rounded, pouch-like bumps or protuberances on a surface, especially seen in clouds

Examples

Mammatus clouds form
After the thunderstorm, unusual mammatus clouds appeared across the evening sky.
Photographers rushed outside to capture the rare mammatus formations above the city.
The weather report mentioned the presence of mammatus clouds following the severe weather.
The cloud formation was described as mammatus due to its hanging, rounded pouches.
Scientists noted the mammatus texture on the mushroom's cap during their field study.
The geologist identified the rock's surface as mammatus because of its distinctive bulges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cumulonimbus - a type of dense, towering cloud often associated with storms
  • synonym: nodular - having small lumps or nodes
  • antonym: smooth - having an even, flat surface without bumps
