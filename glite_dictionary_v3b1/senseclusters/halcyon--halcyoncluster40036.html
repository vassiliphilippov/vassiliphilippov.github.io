halcyon

adjective
calm and peaceful; idyllic
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhælsiən / (AmE), / ˈhælsiən / (BrE)
calm, peaceful, and happy; especially describing a time in the past that was idyllic and prosperous

Examples

Halcyon days
A halcyon atmosphere filled the garden in the early morning.
The couple remembered their halcyon days spent together by the lake.
The town enjoyed a halcyon period of prosperity and peace.
During those halcyon summers, life seemed simple and worry-free.
Many consider the 1950s to be the halcyon days of their childhood.
The artist longed for a return to the halcyon era when creativity flourished.

Related Terms

  • synonym: golden - denoting a time of happiness, prosperity, or excellence
  • synonym: serene - calm and peaceful
  • synonym: tranquil - free from disturbance; calm
  • antonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
