townhouse

noun
attached urban house
2.0 2.76/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtaÊŠnËŒhaÊŠs / (AmE), / ËˆtaÊŠnËŒhaÊŠs / (BrE)
a house that is attached to other houses in a row, usually in a city and often with several floors

Examples

A townhouse in city
They bought a modern townhouse close to the city center.
Each townhouse in the development has its own small garden.
Living in a townhouse means sharing walls with your neighbors.
They bought a modern townhouse close to the city center for easy commuting.
Each townhouse in the development has its own small garden and garage.
The family moved from a suburban home to a spacious townhouse downtown.

Synonyms

  • townhome
    3.2 0.22/m
    townhome is a modern, often American word for a multi-floor house sharing walls, while townhouse is more general city housing term.
  • row house
    3.4 0.74/m
    row house is one of a line of similar houses sharing walls, while townhouse often implies a taller, city home with several floors.
  • terraced house
    2.5 0.03/m
    terraced house is the British name for houses joined in a continuous row, while townhouse is used more widely in cities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apartment - a set of rooms for living in, usually on one floor of a building
  • antonym: detached house - a house that stands alone and is not connected to another
  • synonym: row house - a house in a row of similar houses joined by shared walls
