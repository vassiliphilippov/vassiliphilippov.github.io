premolar

noun
a tooth between canine and molar teeth
5.5
Pronunciation: / priˈmoʊlər / (AmE), / priˈməʊlə / (BrE)
A tooth with two cusps, located between the canines and molars in mammals and used for grinding food.

Examples

A premolar tooth
Children usually begin to get their premolar teeth around the age of ten.
The dentist explained that my premolar needed a filling due to a cavity.
Unlike the canine, the premolar is broader and aids in grinding food.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: incisor - a front tooth adapted for cutting
  • degree higher: molar - a large grinding tooth at the back of the mouth
