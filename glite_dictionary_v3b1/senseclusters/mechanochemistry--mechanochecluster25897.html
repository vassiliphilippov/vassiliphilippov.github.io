mechanochemistry

noun
chemistry by mechanical force
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛkənoʊˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˌmɛkənəʊˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry that studies how mechanical force (such as grinding or pressing) can cause or influence chemical changes in materials

Examples

Mechanochemistry in chemistry
Researchers used mechanochemistry to create new materials without the need for solvents.
The field of mechanochemistry explores how grinding can speed up chemical reactions.
Advances in mechanochemistry have led to more sustainable manufacturing processes.
Researchers in mechanochemistry investigate how grinding or pressing materials can trigger chemical reactions.
The field of mechanochemistry is important for developing new materials and manufacturing processes.
By applying force, mechanochemistry can create compounds that are difficult to produce by traditional methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mechanical engineering - the branch of engineering dealing with machines and mechanical systems
  • synonym: mechanics - the branch of physics concerned with the motion of bodies under force
  • synonym: physical chemistry - the branch of chemistry dealing with the physical properties of substances
