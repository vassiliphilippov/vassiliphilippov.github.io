otoganglion

noun
autonomic ganglion supplying the parotid gland
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊtəʊˈɡæŋɡliən / (AmE), / ˌəʊtəʊˈɡaŋɡlɪən / (BrE)
A small autonomic ganglion whose postganglionic fibers are distributed to the parotid gland.

Examples

The otoganglion nerve
Damage to the otoganglion can affect saliva production in the mouth.
During dissection, the otoganglion can be identified near the foramen ovale.
The otoganglion plays an important role in the innervation of the parotid gland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ganglion - a structure containing a collection of nerve cell bodies
