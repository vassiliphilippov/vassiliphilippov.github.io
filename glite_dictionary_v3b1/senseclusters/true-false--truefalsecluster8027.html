true-false

adjective
requiring a 'true' or 'false' response
Pronunciation: / truː fɔls / (AmE), / truː fɔːls / (BrE)
(of a test or question) requiring an answer of either 'true' or 'false'.

Examples

A true-false question
A true-false question can sometimes be tricky if the wording is unclear.
Each statement on the true-false exam must be marked with either a T or an F.
The teacher gave us a true-false quiz to review the material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multiple-choice - (of a test or question) offering several possible answers to choose from
