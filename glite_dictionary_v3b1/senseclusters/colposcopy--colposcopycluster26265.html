colposcopy

noun
cervical examination
Pronunciation: / kɑlˈpɑskəpi / (AmE), / kɒlˈpɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical examination of the cervix, vagina, and vulva using a special magnifying instrument (colposcope) to check for disease or abnormalities

Examples

A colposcopy exam
After her abnormal screening, the doctor scheduled a colposcopy to examine her cervix more closely.
A colposcopy can help detect early signs of cervical cancer or other abnormalities.
During the colposcopy, the gynecologist used a colposcope to look for unusual cells.
The doctor recommended a colposcopy after the results of her Pap test were unclear.
During the colposcopy, the gynecologist carefully examined the patient's cervix for any abnormal cells.
A colposcopy is often performed if a routine screening shows possible signs of cervical disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biopsy - the removal of tissue for examination, often to check for disease
  • synonym: pap smear - a test for cervical cancer involving the collection of cells from the cervix
