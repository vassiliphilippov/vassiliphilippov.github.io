Jekyll

proper noun
dual personality figure
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛkɪl / (BrE)
a person who shows a good, respectable side but may have a hidden, darker personality, based on the literary character Dr. Jekyll

Examples

A real Jekyll at work
At work, he was a perfect Jekyll, always polite and helpful to everyone.
Her friends only saw her Jekyll side, never suspecting she could be so different at home.
The politician's public image was pure Jekyll, hiding any trace of controversy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Hyde - a person's hidden, darker side, from the same novel
  • synonym: split personality - a condition or situation where a person has two distinct sides
