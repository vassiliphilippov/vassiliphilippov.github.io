obviator

noun
meeting agent (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑbviˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɒbviˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who goes ahead to meet or welcome someone, often as a representative or emissary (archaic)

Examples

Royal obviator
The obviator was sent ahead to greet the royal guests before their arrival at the palace.
In ancient times, an obviator would meet travelers at the city gates to offer guidance.
The diplomat served as an obviator, welcoming the foreign delegation on behalf of the king.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emissary - a person sent on a special mission, usually as a representative
  • synonym: greeter - a person who welcomes or receives others
