widespread flooding

noun
flooding over a large area
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪd.spɹɛd ˈflʌd.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd.spɹɛd ˈflʌd.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Flooding that affects a large area or region, often causing considerable damage and disruption.

Examples

Widespread flooding after rain
After days of heavy rain, widespread flooding forced hundreds of residents to evacuate their homes.
Satellite images revealed the extent of the widespread flooding throughout the region.
The government issued alerts in anticipation of widespread flooding in the river basin.
