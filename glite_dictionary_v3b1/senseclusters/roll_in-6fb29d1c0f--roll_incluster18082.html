roll in

verb
arrive or flow steadily
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /roʊl ɪn/ (AmE), /rəʊl ɪn/ (BrE)
to arrive, appear, or move in steadily or in large numbers, often in a noticeable or gradual way (used for people, things, weather, etc.)

Examples

Guests roll in
As midnight neared, more guests started to roll in to the party.
Donations continued to roll in after the charity event was televised.
The fog began to roll in from the lake as evening approached.
As the sun set, thick fog began to roll in from the sea.
People watched from the pier as the clouds rolled in over the city skyline.
The cold front rolled in unexpectedly, catching everyone off guard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pour in - to arrive or come in large quantities
  • antonym: roll out - to leave or depart gradually, especially in a dramatic way
  • degree lower: trickle in - to arrive or appear in small numbers or quantities
