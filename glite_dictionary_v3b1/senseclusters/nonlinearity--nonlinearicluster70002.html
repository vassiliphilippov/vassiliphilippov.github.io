nonlinearity

noun
lack of constant rate
8.1
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.lɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /nɒn.lɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or condition of not forming a straight line or not having a direct, proportional relationship between variables (especially in mathematics or science)

Examples

Equation nonlinearity
The nonlinearity of the equation means that small changes can have big effects.
Engineers must consider nonlinearity when designing complex control systems.
Because of its nonlinearity, the model cannot be solved with simple methods.
Scientists study nonlinearity to understand how small changes can lead to unexpected results.
The nonlinearity of the equation makes it difficult to solve using simple methods.
In economics, nonlinearity can explain why markets sometimes behave unpredictably.
