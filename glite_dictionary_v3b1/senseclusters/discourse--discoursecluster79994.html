discourse

noun
extended communication on a topic
2.4 5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪs.kɔɹs /; / ˈdɪsˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɔːs / (BrE)
written or spoken communication or debate, especially formal or extended discussion

Examples

Academic discourse
Academic discourse encourages critical thinking and debate among students.
The politician's discourse on economic policy was widely discussed in the media.
Their dinner table discourse ranged from art to current events.
Her books are known for their critical discourse on gender and society.
Political discourse often shapes public opinion in significant ways.
The professor gave an insightful discourse on the evolution of social norms.

  • synonym: dialogue - conversation between two or more people
  • synonym: discussion - the act of talking about something with others
  • antonym: monologue - a long speech by one person during a conversation
