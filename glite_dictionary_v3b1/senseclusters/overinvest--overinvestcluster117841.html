overinvest

verb
to give too much
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪnˈvɛst / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪnˈvɛst / (BrE)
to give too much emotional energy, importance, or resources to someone or something

Examples

To overinvest in feelings
It is easy to overinvest in a new project if you are passionate about it.
He tends to overinvest emotionally in friendships, which sometimes leads to disappointment.
Many people overinvest their time and energy in work at the expense of their personal lives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcommit - to commit more time or resources than is reasonable
  • antonym: underinvest - to give too little energy, importance, or resources
