dance of death

noun
medieval allegory of death's universality
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdæns əv ˈdɛθ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːns əv ˈdɛθ / (BrE)
A symbolic representation, originating in medieval Europe, in which death, often personified as a skeleton, leads people from all walks of life in a procession toward the grave.

Examples

The dance of death mural
Many medieval churches feature murals of the dance of death as a reminder of mortality.
The dance of death motif became popular in European art after the Black Death.
The painting depicted a dance of death, with skeletons leading nobles and peasants alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: danse macabre - French term for a dance of death, the same medieval allegory
