bitter orange

noun
a type of sour, acidic orange fruit
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬ɚ ˈɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small, highly acidic orange with a bitter taste, often used in marmalade and flavorings

Examples

A bitter orange marmalade
Farmers harvested a large crop of bitter oranges this season, destined for preserves and liqueurs.
Some herbal supplements contain extracts from bitter orange peel.
The traditional recipe for English marmalade uses bitter orange for its distinctive flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Seville orange - another name for the bitter orange, especially in culinary contexts
  • antonym: sweet orange - the more commonly eaten orange, less sour than bitter orange
