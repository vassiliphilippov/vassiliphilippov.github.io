rough pea

noun
Mediterranean-origin annual cover crop plant
Pronunciation: / rʌf piː / (AmE), / rʌf piː / (BrE)
a weak-stemmed winter annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, long established in the southern United States; cultivated as a cover and pasture crop

Examples

The rough pea plant
Rough pea is well adapted to the mild winters of the southern United States.
Some pastures use rough pea to provide forage for grazing animals.
The farmer planted rough pea as a cover crop to improve soil quality over winter.
