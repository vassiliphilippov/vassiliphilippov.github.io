shoeful

noun
amount a shoe can hold
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈʃuːfʊl / (BrE)
The amount that a shoe can contain or hold.

Examples

A shoeful of rice
A single shoeful of rice was all she needed for the experiment.
After the storm, I found a shoeful of sand inside my sneakers.
He poured out a shoeful of muddy water after hiking through the marsh.
