pluperfect

adjective
verb tense before past
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpluːˈpɜrfɪkt / (AmE), / ˌpluːˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
describing a verb tense that indicates an action was completed before another past action

Examples

Pluperfect verb form
In the sentence 'She had finished her work before dinner,' 'had finished' is in the pluperfect tense.
English uses the auxiliary 'had' to form the pluperfect tense in regular verbs.
Students often confuse the pluperfect with the simple past when describing sequences of events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: past perfect - a verb tense showing an action completed before another past action
  • antonym: present perfect - a verb tense showing an action completed at an indefinite time before now
