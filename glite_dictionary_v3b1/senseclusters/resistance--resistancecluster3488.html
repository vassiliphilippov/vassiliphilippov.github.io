resistance

noun
opposition to electrical current
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the opposition that a material offers to the flow of electric current, measured in ohms

Examples

Wire resistance
Increasing the length of the wire increases its resistance to electrical current.
The copper wire has a low resistance, making it ideal for electrical wiring.
The engineer measured the resistance of the circuit with a multimeter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conductance - ability of a material to conduct electric current, reciprocal of resistance
