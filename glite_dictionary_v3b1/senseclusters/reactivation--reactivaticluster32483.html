reactivation

noun
memory recall process
5.6
Pronunciation: /riː.æk.tɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.æk.tɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.æk.tɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.æk.tɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which previously formed memories or neural patterns become active again, often during recall or memory consolidation

Examples

Reactivation of memories
The reactivation of old memories can be triggered by familiar sights or sounds.
Scientists study reactivation during sleep to understand how memories are strengthened.
Therapy sessions often focus on the reactivation of past experiences to help patients heal.
