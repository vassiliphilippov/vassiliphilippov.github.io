Muskogean

noun
language family of southeastern U.S. Native peoples
Pronunciation: / mʌsˈkoʊɡiən / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊɡiən / (BrE)
A family of North American Indigenous languages spoken primarily in the southeastern United States, including Creek, Choctaw, Chickasaw, and others.

Examples

Muskogean languages
Many Muskogean words have found their way into English place names across the Southeast.
Researchers studied the structure of the Muskogean language family to compare it with other Native American languages.
The Muskogean languages include Choctaw and Creek, spoken in the southeastern United States.
