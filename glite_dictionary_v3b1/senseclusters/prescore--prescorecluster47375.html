prescore

noun
pre-event rating
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːskɔːr / (AmE), / ˈpriːskɔː / (BrE)
a score or rating given before an event, test, or competition begins, often to predict or assess expected performance

Examples

Player's prescore
The coach reviewed each player's prescore before finalizing the starting lineup.
Analysts used the prescore to estimate which team was more likely to win.
Her prescore on the practice exam indicated she was well prepared for the real test.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prediction - a statement about what will happen in the future
  • degree higher: score - a number that shows how well someone did in a test or competition
