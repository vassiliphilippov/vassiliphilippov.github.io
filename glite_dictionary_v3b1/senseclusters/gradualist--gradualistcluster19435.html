gradualist

adjective
favoring slow change
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrædʒuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuəlɪst / (BrE)
favoring or relating to slow, step-by-step change or reform rather than sudden action

Examples

Gradualist approach
The committee adopted a gradualist approach to implementing the new policies.
Many experts argue that a gradualist strategy is more sustainable in the long run.
Her gradualist views often put her at odds with more radical reformers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incremental - involving small, gradual increases or changes
  • antonym: revolutionary - favoring or causing sudden, dramatic change
