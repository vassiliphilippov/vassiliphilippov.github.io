changeup

noun
slow deceptive pitch
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪndʒˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒˌʌp / (BrE)
a slow pitch in baseball thrown to deceive the batter by appearing faster than it is

Examples

A fast changeup pitch
The pitcher threw a perfect changeup that completely fooled the batter.
His changeup is so effective that hitters often swing too early.
She practiced her changeup to add variety to her pitching style.
