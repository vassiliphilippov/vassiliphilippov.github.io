reductio ad absurdum

noun
argument showing absurdity of the contrary
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Àåd åktio ä √¶d √¶bÀàs…ùd…ôm / (AmE), / r…™Àåd åkt…™…ô ä √¶d √¶bÀàs…úÀêd…ôm / (BrE)
A method of argument that disproves a proposition by demonstrating that its logical consequences are absurd or contradictory, or proves a proposition by showing that its denial leads to a contradiction.

Examples

Reductio ad absurdum argument
Her claim was refuted with reductio ad absurdum, exposing the absurd outcome of her reasoning.
In mathematics, a reductio ad absurdum proof can sometimes offer the most elegant solution.
The philosopher used reductio ad absurdum to demonstrate that the opposing argument was logically untenable.
