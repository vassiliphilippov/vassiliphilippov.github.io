out-of-date

adjective
no longer valid or fashionable
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊt əv ˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ˈdeɪt / (BrE)
no longer valid, in use, or fashionable; outdated or obsolete.

Examples

An out-of-date map
He found that the map he was using was completely out-of-date and unreliable.
Her kitchen appliances looked very out-of-date compared to the latest models.
Many of the company’s safety guidelines are considered out-of-date and need revision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • synonym: obsolete - no longer produced or used; out of date
  • antonym: updated - having been brought up to date
