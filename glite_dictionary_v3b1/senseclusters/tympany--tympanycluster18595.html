tympany

noun
archaic drum
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪmpəni / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpəni / (BrE)
an old term for a drum or tambourine, used as a percussion instrument

Examples

Medieval tympany
In medieval times, musicians would play the tympany to accompany dancers at celebrations.
The old manuscript described a lively procession with the sound of tympany and flutes.
Few people today know that tympany once referred to a type of drum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drum - a percussion instrument sounded by being struck
  • synonym: tambourine - a small hand-held drum with jingles
