holograph

noun
photographic record for holography
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɒləˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a photographic record containing information for reproducing a three-dimensional image by holography

Examples

Holograph image
A holograph captures both the intensity and the phase of the reflected light.
After exposure, the holograph was illuminated with laser light to reveal the subject in three dimensions.
The scientist carefully developed the holograph before reconstructing the 3D image.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hologram - a three-dimensional image formed by the interference of light beams from a laser or other coherent light source
