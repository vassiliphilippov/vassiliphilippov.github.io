direct sum

noun
a mathematical combination of disjoint sets or spaces

Pronunciation: /də.ˈrekt sʌm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈrekt sʌm/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt sʌm/ (AmE), /də.ˈrekt sʌm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈrekt sʌm/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt sʌm/ (BrE)
A mathematical operation that combines two or more disjoint sets or spaces so that each element is formed as the sum of one element from each set.

Examples

The direct sum of spaces
In linear algebra, the direct sum of vector spaces is a common construction.
Mathematicians use the direct sum to analyze module structures.
The group can be expressed as a direct sum of two subgroups.
