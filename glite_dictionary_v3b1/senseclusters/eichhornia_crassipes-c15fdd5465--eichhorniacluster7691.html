Eichhornia crassipes

noun
invasive tropical aquatic plant (water hyacinth)
9.9
Pronunciation: /aɪk.hɔːr.ˈniː.ə ˈkræs.ɪ.piːz/ (AmE), /aɪk.hɔː.ˈniː.ə ˈkræs.ɪ.piːz/ (BrE)
A tropical aquatic plant, also known as water hyacinth, with floating leaves and spikes of large blue flowers; it is invasive and often clogs waterways, particularly in the southern United States.

Examples

Eichhornia crassipes water hyacinth
Eichhornia crassipes can quickly overtake ponds and rivers, blocking sunlight and depleting oxygen.
Efforts to control Eichhornia crassipes often involve mechanical removal from crowded waterways.
The rapid growth of Eichhornia crassipes has made it a major pest in southern water systems.
