didactic

adjective
intended to teach, often moralizing
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdæktɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈdæktɪk / (BrE)
Intended to teach or instruct, especially in an overly moralistic or patronizing way.

Examples

A didactic lecture
His didactic lectures sometimes felt more like sermons than classes.
The novel takes a didactic approach, making its moral lesson very clear.
The professor's didactic tone annoyed students who preferred open discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: educational - providing education; instructive
  • synonym: pedantic - overly concerned with minor details or rules; showing off learning
