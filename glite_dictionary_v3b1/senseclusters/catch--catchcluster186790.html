catch

verb
attract or win affection
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / katʃ /; / kæʧ / (BrE)
to attract or hold someone's attention, interest, or affection

Examples

To catch attention
His charm instantly caught her interest.
She managed to catch his attention at the crowded party.
The artist's smile caught many hearts at the gallery opening.
A sudden movement in the bushes caught his gaze, and he looked over cautiously.
She managed to catch the attention of everyone with her inspiring speech.
The bright colors of the painting immediately caught my eye as I entered the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attract - to draw attention or interest
  • synonym: capture - to attract or take someone's interest or affection
  • antonym: lose - to fail to maintain someone's attention or interest
