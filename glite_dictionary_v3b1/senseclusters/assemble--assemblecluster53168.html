Examples

To assemble a crowd
A crowd quickly assembled at the scene of the accident.
She assembled the participants in the conference room for the briefing.
The students assembled in the auditorium for the presentation.
Police ordered the crowd not to assemble without a permit.

Synonyms

  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather brings people or things together in a simple, everyday way, while assemble is more formal and planned.
  • get together
    2.8 3.48/m
    get together means friends meet for a social activity, while assemble is used for planned, often formal gatherings.
  • collect
    1.1 40/m
    collect means bring items or people together often by gathering them up, while assemble implies organizing them for a purpose.
  • convene
    5.7 2.50/m
    convene calls people to a meeting or official event, while assemble covers any gathering, formal or informal, for a purpose.
  • muster
    5.4 6.14/m
    muster means to gather people or resources for action often in military or urgent scenes, while assemble is more general.
  • congregate
    4.1 2.55/m
    congregate describes people coming together in a group, often naturally, while assemble suggests arranging them for a purpose.
  • round up
    3.2 5.07/m
    round up means to collect people or animals, sometimes by force or care, while assemble focuses on planned gathering.
  • mass
    4.8 2.78/m
    mass means to form a large crowd or group, often for strength or protest, while assemble simply means to gather.
  • crowd together
    3.0 0.28/m
    crowd together means to press closely in large numbers, while assemble means to bring people together without that close pressure.
  • group
    1.2 6.92/m
    group means to place items or people into smaller sets, while assemble means to bring many into one single place or purpose.
  • meet
    1.0 18/m
    meet means to come together at a time or point, often by chance, while assemble suggests planned bringing for a purpose.
  • coalesce
    7.4 1.54/m
    coalesce means separate parts join and become one over time, while assemble often means people or things are brought together by action.
  • unite
    2.1 253/m
    unite means to join closely often by agreement or feeling, while assemble usually means gathering physically in one place.
