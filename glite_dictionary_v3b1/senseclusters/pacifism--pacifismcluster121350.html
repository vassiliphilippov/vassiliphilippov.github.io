pacifism

noun
belief that all violence is unjustifiable
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpasɪˌfɪzəm /; / ˈpæsɪfɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that violence is wrong and that all disputes should be settled peacefully, without fighting or war

Examples

Belief in pacifism
Gandhi was a famous proponent of pacifism in his fight for Indian independence.
Her commitment to pacifism inspired her work with peace organizations around the world.
Many people adopted pacifism after witnessing the horrors of war.
He embraced pacifism after witnessing the devastation caused by armed conflict.
Her lifelong commitment to pacifism influenced her activism against war.
The organization's principles are rooted in pacifism and diplomacy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: militarism - The belief or desire that a country should maintain a strong military capability
  • synonym: nonviolence - The use of peaceful means, not force, to bring about political or social change
