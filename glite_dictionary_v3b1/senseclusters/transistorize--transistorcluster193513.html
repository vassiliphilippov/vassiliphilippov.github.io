transistorize

verb
equip with transistors
6.9
Pronunciation: / trænˈzɪstɚˌaɪz /; / trænˈzɪstəˌraɪz / (AmE), / trænˈzɪstəˌaɪz /; / trænˈzɪstəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip (an electronic circuit or device) with transistors, often in place of earlier components such as vacuum tubes

Examples

To transistorize a circuit
After they transistorized the circuit, it became much smaller and more reliable.

Engineers are working to transistorise older vacuum tube equipment for greater efficiency.
In the 1960s, many amplifiers were transistorised to enhance performance.
The company decided to transistorise its radios to improve durability and reduce size.
