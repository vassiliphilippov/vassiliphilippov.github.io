taxis

noun
movement of an organism in response to a stimulus
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtæk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈtæk.sɪs / (BrE)
the movement of an organism in response to a stimulus, typically toward or away from it

Examples

Bacterial taxis movement
Certain bacteria exhibit taxis by moving toward sources of nutrients.
Phototaxis is a form of taxis where movement is guided by light.
The scientist observed negative taxis as the organisms moved away from the chemical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tropism - growth or turning movement of a biological organism in response to an environmental stimulus
