British thermal unit

noun
unit of heat energy (BTU)
8.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbrɪ.tɪʃ ˈθɜːr.məl ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ˈbrɪ.tɪʃ ˈθɜːr.məl ˈjuː.nət/ (AmE), /ˈbrɪ.tɪʃ ˈθɜː.məl ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ˈbrɪ.tɪʃ ˈθɜː.məl ˈjuː.nət/ (BrE)
a unit of heat equal to the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit at one atmosphere pressure

Examples

An air conditioner with a higher British thermal unit rating can cool a larger room.
One British thermal unit is approximately equal to 252 calories.
The energy output of the heater is measured in British thermal units per hour.
