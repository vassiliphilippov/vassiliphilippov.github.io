unwarrantably

adverb
in an unjustified manner
3.8 8.0
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈwɔːr.ən.tə.bəl/ /; / /ʌn.ˈwɑːr.ən.tə.bəl/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈwɒr.ən.tə.bəl/ /; / /ʌn.ˈwɔːr.ən.tə.bəl/ (BrE)
In a way that is not justified or is lacking reasonable grounds.

Examples

To be unwarrantably accused
He found himself unwarrantably accused of negligence despite his careful work.
Restrictions were unwarrantably imposed, causing widespread dissatisfaction among the staff.
The report was unwarrantably critical of the new policy without presenting clear evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: justifiably - with good reason; in a justified manner
  • synonym: unjustifiably - in a way not able to be justified
