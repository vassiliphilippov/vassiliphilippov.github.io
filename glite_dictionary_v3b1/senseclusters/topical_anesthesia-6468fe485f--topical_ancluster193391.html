topical anesthesia

noun
localized numbness from surface-applied anesthetic
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːpɪkəl ænəsˈθiːʒə /; / ˈtɑːpɪkəl ˌænəsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪkəl ænɪsˈθiːzɪə /; / ˈtɒpɪkəl ˌanɪsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
loss of sensation in a localized area of the skin or mucous membrane, produced by applying an anesthetic directly to the surface

Examples

Patients with a sore throat sometimes benefit from sprays that provide topical anaesthesia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general anaesthesia - loss of sensation and consciousness throughout the whole body
  • degree higher: general anesthesia - complete loss of sensation including consciousness, usually produced by inhalation or intravenous administration
  • synonym: local anaesthesia - loss of sensation in a specific area by anesthetic injections or applications
  • degree higher: local anesthesia - loss of sensation in a limited area by regional application of an anesthetic
