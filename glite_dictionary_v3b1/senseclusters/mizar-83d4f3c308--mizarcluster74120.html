Mizar

proper noun
star in Big Dipper
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪzɑr / (AmE), / ˈmaɪzɑː / (BrE)
a bright star located in the handle of the Big Dipper constellation in the night sky

Examples

Mizar star
With a clear sky, you can easily spot Mizar in the handle of the Big Dipper.
Astronomers have studied Mizar for centuries because of its brightness and position.
Many people first learn to identify Mizar when exploring the night sky.
