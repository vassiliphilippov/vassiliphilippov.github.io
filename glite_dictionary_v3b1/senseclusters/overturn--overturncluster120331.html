overturn

verb
officially reverse a decision or law
5.6 5.85/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàt…úrn / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàt…úÀên / (BrE)
to officially reverse or cancel a decision, law, or judgment, especially by a higher authority

Examples

To overturn a decision
A new administration might overturn the controversial policy implemented last year.
His legal team hopes to overturn the conviction based on new evidence.
The Supreme Court overturned the lower court's decision after a lengthy appeal process.
If new evidence emerges, a higher authority might overturn the previous verdict.
The Supreme Court overturned the lower court's ruling after a lengthy legal battle.
The appeal was successful, and the decision was overturned by the appellate judge.
