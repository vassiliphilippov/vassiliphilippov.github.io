analbuminemia

noun
abnormally low blood albumin
Pronunciation: / ˌænˌæl.bjuˌmɪˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌænˌæl.bjuː.mɪˈniː.mɪ.ə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by an abnormally low level of albumin in the blood serum

Examples

Analbuminemia blood test
Doctors confirmed the diagnosis of analbuminemia after several blood tests showed undetectable albumin levels.
Patients with analbuminemia may experience swelling and other complications due to the lack of serum albumin.
The rare disorder known as analbuminemia requires careful medical monitoring and treatment.
