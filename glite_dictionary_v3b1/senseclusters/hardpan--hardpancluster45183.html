hardpan

noun
layer of hard compacted subsoil
9.4 0.22/m

A layer of hard, compacted subsoil, often cemented with calcium carbonate, which is difficult for roots and water to penetrate, especially found in arid or semiarid regions.

Examples

A hardpan layer
Heavy machinery is often needed to break through hardpan before construction can begin.
In some deserts, plants cannot grow due to a thick layer of hardpan beneath the soil.
The farmer struggled to dig a well because the hardpan was nearly impenetrable.
