moral principle

noun
standard of right and wrong conduct
2.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔːr.əl ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /ˈmɑːr.əl ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /ˈmɔːr.əl ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈmɒr.əl ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ /; / /ˈmɒr.əl ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ (BrE)
a belief or rule about what is right and wrong, which guides how people behave

Examples

Basic moral principles
Many cultures share similar moral principles about treating others with respect.
She always tried to live by her strong moral principle of honesty.
The company faced criticism for acting against basic moral principles.
It can be difficult to balance profit and moral principle in the business world.
She refused to lie, guided by her strong moral principle.
The company’s policies are based on the moral principle of honesty and fairness.

  • synonym: ethical principle - a general rule about what is morally right or wrong
  • antonym: immorality - the state or quality of being immoral
  • synonym: value - a principle or standard of behavior; one's judgment of what is important in life
