drumroll

noun
series of drumbeats
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌmˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌrəʊl / (BrE)
a rapid series of drumbeats played to build excitement or suspense before an announcement or event

Examples

A drumroll before the winner
The audience waited in silence as a drumroll signaled the start of the ceremony.
With a dramatic drumroll, the magician revealed the final trick of the night.
There was a drumroll before the winner was announced.
The host asked for a drumroll before revealing the winner of the contest.
A dramatic drumroll echoed through the hall as the curtain slowly rose.
Everyone waited in anticipation as the drumroll signaled the big announcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fanfar - a short, lively piece of music played to announce something important
  • antonym: silence - the absence of sound
