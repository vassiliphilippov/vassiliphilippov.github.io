foreign agent

noun
legal designation requiring disclosure
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔː.rən ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ /; / /ˈfɑː.rən ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ /; / /ˈfɔː.rɪn ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ (AmE), /ˈfɒ.rən ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ /; / /ˈfɒ.rɪn ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ (BrE)
A person or organization formally designated by a government as acting on behalf of foreign interests and required by law to register, report, or disclose funding and activities.

Examples

Registered foreign agent
The activist group was declared a foreign agent under the new law, forcing strict financial reporting.
Under the statute, journalists receiving overseas funding may have to register as a foreign agent.
After being labeled a foreign agent, the charity faced audits and restrictions on its activities.
