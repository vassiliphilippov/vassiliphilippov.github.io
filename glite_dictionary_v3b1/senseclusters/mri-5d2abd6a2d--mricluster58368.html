MRI

noun
imaging technique using magnetic fields
5.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈem.ɑːr.aɪ/ (AmE), /ˈem.ɑː.aɪ/ (BrE)
a method of producing detailed images of the inside of the body using magnetic fields and radio waves

Examples

A MRI scan
After reviewing the MRI, the neurologist identified a small lesion in the brain.
Many athletes undergo an MRI after a severe injury to assess the damage.
The doctor ordered an MRI to help diagnose the patient's back pain.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: CT scan - imaging technique using X-rays to create body images
  • degree higher: PET scan - imaging technique using positron emissions for body scans
  • synonym: magnetic resonance imaging - the full form of MRI; the detailed imaging technique used in medicine
