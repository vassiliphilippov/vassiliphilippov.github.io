marshal

noun
law officer enforcing court orders
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
an official responsible for enforcing the orders of a court of law, with duties similar to those of a sheriff

Examples

Court marshal
After the trial, a marshal served the eviction notice as ordered by the judge.
She called the marshal for assistance when court proceedings became disorderly.
The U.S. marshal ensured the safe transport of the prisoner to the courthouse.
A marshal arrested the fugitive on federal charges last night.
The U.S. marshal served the court order to the defendant personally.
The ceremony was overseen by a marshal from the county court.

  • synonym: sheriff - a law officer with similar duties to a marshal
