time and material

noun
work pricing by time spent and materials used
1.0
Pronunciation: / taɪm ənd məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / taɪm ænd məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a method of pricing work or projects in which costs are based on the actual time spent and materials used

Examples

A time and material contract
The contractor used a time and material contract to ensure all costs were covered.
They switched to a time and material agreement because the project scope was unclear.
Under a time and material model, clients pay for work as it progresses.
