extrapolative

adjective
based on generalization
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstræpəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræpələtɪv / (BrE)
involving making predictions or generalizations based on limited or known information

Examples

An extrapolative method
The scientist's extrapolative approach allowed her to predict trends from a small set of data.
His argument was criticized for being too extrapolative and not grounded in enough evidence.
Using an extrapolative method, the analyst estimated next year's sales figures.
The economist's extrapolative model suggested that growth would continue for several more years.
Using extrapolative reasoning, the team estimated the population in 2050.
Her extrapolative analysis relied on trends observed in previous decades.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predictive - relating to or able to predict future events
  • synonym: speculative - based on guesses or incomplete information
