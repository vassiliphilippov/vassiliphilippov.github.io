hominization

noun
becoming human
9.9
Pronunciation: /hɑː.mɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /hɒ.mɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a non-human or divine being becomes human, or develops human qualities such as social, moral, or cultural traits (especially in philosophical or theological contexts)

Examples

Hominization of a being
The philosopher discussed the concept of hominization in relation to the origins of humanity.
Some religious traditions interpret hominization as the moment when a soul is given to a human being.

Anthropologists study the hominization of early primates to understand the origins of human society.
The hominization of ancient communities involved the development of language and shared customs.
Some philosophers debate when hominization truly begins in the evolutionary process.
