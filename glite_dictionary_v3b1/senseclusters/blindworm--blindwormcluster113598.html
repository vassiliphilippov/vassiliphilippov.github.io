blindworm

noun
slender limbless burrowing amphibian
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪndˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A small, slender, limbless burrowing amphibian of the order Gymnophiona, found in moist tropical soil.

Examples

Tropical blindworm
Blindworms are often mistaken for worms or snakes because of their appearance and burrowing habits.
Because a blindworm has no limbs, it moves through wet soil with ease.
The scientist carefully unearthed a blindworm while studying animals in the rainforest.
