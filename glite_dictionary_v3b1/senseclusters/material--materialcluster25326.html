material

adjective
relevant or significant (especially in law)
Pronunciation: / məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
directly relevant or significant to a subject or situation, especially in a legal context

Examples

Material evidence
Her testimony was considered material to the outcome of the case.
Only material facts need to be disclosed during the investigation.
The judge ruled that the contract did not contain any material errors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrelevant - not connected or relevant to something
  • synonym: pertinent - relevant or applicable to a particular matter
