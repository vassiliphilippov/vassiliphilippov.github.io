automobile battery

noun
high-capacity EV propulsion battery
3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.tə.moʊ.bəl ˈbæt.ər.ɪ/ /; / /ɔː.tə.moʊ.ˈbiːl ˈbæt.ər.ɪ/ /; / /ˈɔː.tə.moʊ.bəl ˈbæt.rɪ/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.tə.mə.biːl ˈbæt.ə.rɪ/ /; / /ɔː.tə.mə.ˈbiːl ˈbæt.ə.rɪ/ (BrE)
(in electric and hybrid vehicles) a high-capacity rechargeable battery (typically lithium‑ion) that stores electrical energy to power the vehicle's propulsion system and major electrical loads.

Examples

Electric automobile battery
Many electric cars rely on a large lithium-ion automobile battery to drive the motor.
A damaged automobile battery reduced the vehicle's range, forcing the driver to stop and recharge.
Manufacturers guarantee their high-capacity automobile batteries for eight years or a set number of miles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: EV battery - informal term for the large rechargeable battery pack that powers an electric vehicle
  • synonym: traction battery - a high-capacity battery used specifically to provide power for a vehicle's propulsion system
