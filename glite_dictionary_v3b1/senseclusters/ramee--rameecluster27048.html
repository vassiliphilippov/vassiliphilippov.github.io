ramee

noun
Asian perennial herb grown for its fiber
Pronunciation: / rəˈmi / (AmE), / ˈreɪmi / (BrE)
A tall tropical Asian perennial herb, cultivated for the strong fiber obtained from its woody stems, similar to flax.

Examples

The ramee plant
Ramee thrives best in hot, humid regions with well-drained soil.
Textile manufacturers prize ramee fiber for its strength and silk-like sheen.
The farmer harvested ramee for use in making fine, durable textiles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ramie - The more common variant spelling of ramee, same plant and fiber
