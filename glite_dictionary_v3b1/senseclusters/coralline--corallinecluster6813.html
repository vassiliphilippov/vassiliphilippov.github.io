coralline

noun
calcified red seaweed
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a red, calcified seaweed that forms hard crusts on rocks, shells, or reefs in marine environments

Examples

Coralline seaweed
The divers observed patches of coralline covering the rocks on the ocean floor.
Researchers are studying how coralline helps build and stabilize coral reefs.
A layer of pink coralline can indicate a healthy marine ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alga - a simple, typically aquatic plant-like organism
  • synonym: seaweed - a marine plant or alga that grows in the sea
