climax

verb
reach or bring to a high point or finale
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪˌmæks / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.mæks / (BrE)
(of an event or process) to reach or bring to a decisive or most intense point, often the culmination or conclusion.

Examples

To climax the show
Her career climaxed when she was awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature.
The celebration climaxed with a spectacular fireworks display that left the crowd in awe.
The negotiations climaxed after several days of intense discussion and compromise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culminate - reach a peak or decisive point
  • antonym: peter out - diminish gradually before stopping
