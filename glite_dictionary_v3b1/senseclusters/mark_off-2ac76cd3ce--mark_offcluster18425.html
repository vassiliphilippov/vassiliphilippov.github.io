mark off

verb
indicate checked/completed with a mark
3.2 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / m…ërk …îf / (AmE), / m…ëÀêk …íf / (BrE)
to indicate that something has been checked, noted, or completed, especially by placing a mark next to it

Examples

To mark off tasks
I will mark off the completed tasks on this checklist.
Once you finish each chapter, remember to mark off your progress in the workbook.
She marked off each item as she packed it in her suitcase.

Synonyms

  • check off
    2.5 1.02/m
    check off is used to mark items on a list as completed, while mark off is a general phrase for noting completion.
  • tick off
    3.7 0.23/m
    tick off means marking with a small check to show something is done, while mark off covers any marking to note completion.
  • tick
    2.0 2.03/m
    tick means put a check mark next to an item to show it is done, while mark off is more general marking.
  • check
    1.0 63/m
    check means mark with a tick to show something is chosen or verified, while mark off emphasizes marking something as completed.
  • cross off
    1.8 0.93/m
    cross off means draw a line through an item to remove or finish it, while mark off means simply mark it as checked.
  • chalk off
    6.5 0.00/m
    chalk off means draw a line or note an item, often removing it from a list, while mark off means mark it as completed.
