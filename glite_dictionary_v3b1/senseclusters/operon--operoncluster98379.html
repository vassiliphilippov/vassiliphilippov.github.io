operon

noun
segment of DNA with grouped genes
Pronunciation: / ˈɑː.pəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ər.ɒn / (BrE)
a segment of DNA containing a group of genes, including structural, operator, and regulatory genes, that function together as a single unit of genetic control

Examples

A operon segment
Mutations in an operon can disrupt the coordinated expression of several genes.
Researchers discovered a new operon responsible for antibiotic resistance in the bacterial genome.
The lac operon is one of the most studied regulatory systems in bacteria.
