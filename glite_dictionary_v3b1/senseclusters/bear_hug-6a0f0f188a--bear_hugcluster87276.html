bear hug

noun
attractive takeover bid (business)
4.5 6.4
Pronunciation: /ber hʌg/ /; / /beər hʌg/ (AmE), /beə hʌg/ /; / /bɪə hʌg/ (BrE)
an offer to buy a company at a price so attractive that the company's directors are almost forced to accept it, typically due to shareholder expectations

Examples

A company bear hug
Despite the generous bear hug, the directors hesitated before finally recommending acceptance to the shareholders.
Shareholders were thrilled when the bear hug was announced, as it promised a high return on their investments.
The company received a bear hug from a larger competitor, making rejection almost impossible for the board.
