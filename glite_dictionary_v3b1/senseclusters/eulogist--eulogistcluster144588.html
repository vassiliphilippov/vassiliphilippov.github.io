eulogist

noun
person who gives a eulogy or tribute
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˈjuːlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who delivers a speech or written tribute in praise of someone, especially someone who has recently died

Examples

A eulogist at funeral
As a renowned eulogist, she was often asked to speak at memorial services.
The eulogist shared fond memories of the deceased, bringing both laughter and tears to the audience.
The family chose a close friend to serve as the eulogist at the funeral.

Related Terms

  • antonym: critic - a person who expresses unfavorable or opposing views
  • synonym: orator - a public speaker, especially one who is skilled
