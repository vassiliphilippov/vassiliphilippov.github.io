shad-flower

noun
annual weed with small flowers and oblong seed capsules
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃædˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
an annual weed native to Europe and North America, characterized by a rosette of basal leaves and small flowers followed by oblong seed capsules

Examples

The shad-flower plant
Gardeners often remove shad-flower to prevent it from spreading among their crops.
The distinctive seed capsules make shad-flower easy to identify among other wildflowers.
The field was dotted with clusters of shad-flower in early spring.
