nitrosobacteria

noun
bacteria converting ammonia to nitrites
Pronunciation: / naɪˌtroʊsoʊbækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / nʌɪˌtrəʊsəʊbækˈtɪərɪə / (BrE)
soil bacteria that oxidize ammonia into nitrites as part of the nitrogen cycle

Examples

Nitrosobacteria in soil
Nitrosobacteria play an essential role in soil by converting ammonia into nitrites during the nitrification process.
Scientists discovered a high concentration of nitrosobacteria in the forest soil sample.
Without nitrosobacteria, the natural recycling of nitrogen in ecosystems would be disrupted.
