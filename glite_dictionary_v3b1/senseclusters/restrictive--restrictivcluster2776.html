restrictive

adjective
limiting or controlling
1.0 3.8
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈstrɪk.tɪv/ /; / /riː.ˈstrɪk.tɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈstrɪk.tɪv/ (BrE)
serving to limit or control what is possible or allowed

Examples

A restrictive diet
Some diets are very restrictive and may not provide enough nutrients.
The company adopted a restrictive dress code for all employees.
The new law introduced more restrictive regulations on internet use.
After the introduction of restrictive import quotas, local manufacturers increased production.
Some economists argue that restrictive trade policies can hurt consumers by raising prices.
The government imposed a restrictive tariff to protect domestic industries from foreign competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberal - (of a tariff) allowing free trade, not restrictive
  • synonym: limiting - serving to limit or restrict something
  • antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior
  • synonym: protective - intended to protect (especially of tariffs)
