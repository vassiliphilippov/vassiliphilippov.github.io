disunity

noun
lack of unity or agreement
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈjuːnəti / (AmE), / dɪsˈjuːnɪti / (BrE)
The state of not being united or lacking agreement, often resulting from conflict or difference of opinion.

Examples

Political disunity
He called for an end to disunity among the national leaders.
Political disunity weakened their ability to respond to the crisis.
The ongoing debate led to disunity within the organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: division - the act or process of separating or being separated
  • antonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
