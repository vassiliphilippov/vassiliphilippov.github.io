oversow

verb
to sow on existing plants
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.soʊ/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.səʊ/ (BrE)
to sow seeds over land that already has existing plants or grass, often to improve growth or quality

Examples

To oversow grass
Farmers often oversow clover to improve pasture quality.
To repair thin patches, you can oversow grass seed in the fall.
The gardener decided to oversow wildflowers to add more color to the lawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plant - to put seeds or plants in the ground to grow
  • synonym: reseeding - to plant seeds again in an area to encourage new growth
