bpi

noun
bits per inch (data storage density)
8.9
Pronunciation: / bi pi aɪ / (AmE), / biː piː aɪ / (BrE)
(in computing) a measure of how densely information is packed on a storage medium, expressed as the number of bits per inch along a track.

Examples

Magnetic bpi measure
Engineers are constantly working to improve the bpi of magnetic tapes.
If the bpi increases, more data can be stored in the same amount of space.
The new hard drive boasts a higher bpi than previous models, allowing for greater storage capacity.
