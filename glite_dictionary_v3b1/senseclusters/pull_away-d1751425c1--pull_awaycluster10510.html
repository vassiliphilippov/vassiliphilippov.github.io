pull away

verb
move away or withdraw
Pronunciation: / pʊl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to start moving away from a place, group, or situation; to create distance from others

Examples

To pull away quickly
As the race began, the car quickly pulled away from the rest of the field.
She tried to pull away when he grabbed her arm.
The train pulled away from the station as the passengers waved goodbye.
As the race began, one runner quickly pulled away from the rest of the pack.
She felt the need to pull away from her stressful job for a while.
The moment the doors closed, the bus pulled away from the curb.

  • synonym: withdraw - to move back or away; to retreat
