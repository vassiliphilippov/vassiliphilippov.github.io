penetrant

adjective
able to pass through
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnɪtrənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛnɪtrənt / (BrE)
able to pass through or soak into a material or surface

Examples

Penetrant liquid
The penetrant solution quickly soaked into the porous stone.
A penetrant chemical is used to detect tiny cracks in metal surfaces.
This fabric is not penetrant to water, making it ideal for raincoats.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impermeable - not allowing liquids or gases to pass through
  • synonym: permeable - allowing liquids or gases to pass through
