biosimilar

adjective
similar biological medicine
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈsɪmələr / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈsɪmɪlə / (BrE)
describing a biological medicine that is highly similar to an already approved biological product, with no significant differences in safety, purity, or effectiveness

Examples

Biosimilar medicine
The hospital switched to a biosimilar drug to reduce treatment costs for patients.
Doctors must ensure that a biosimilar medicine meets strict regulatory standards before prescribing it.
A biosimilar product offers the same therapeutic benefits as the original biological medicine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generic - a non-branded version of a drug with the same active ingredients as the original
  • antonym: reference product - the original approved biological medicine to which a biosimilar is compared
