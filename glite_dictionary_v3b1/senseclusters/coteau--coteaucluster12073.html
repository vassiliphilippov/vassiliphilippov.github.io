coteau

noun
vineyard hillside
Pronunciation: / koʊˈtoʊ / (AmE), / kəʊˈtəʊ / (BrE)
a small hill or sloping hillside, sometimes used for vineyards or agriculture

Examples

A coteau hill
The winery planted chardonnay across the sunlit coteau to produce richer grapes this season.
From the top of the coteau, you could see rows of grapevines stretching into the distance.
Many famous French wines come from grapes grown on a coteau with perfect sun exposure.
The farmhouse stood at the base of a grassy coteau overlooking the fields.
Wildflowers covered the gentle coteau that rose above the prairie.
From the top of the coteau, you could see for miles across the open land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knoll - a small, rounded hill
  • antonym: plain - a large area of flat land with few trees
  • synonym: ridge - a long, narrow hilltop
  • synonym: vineyard - a plantation of grapevines, typically for wine production
