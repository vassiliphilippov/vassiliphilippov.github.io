Laius

proper noun
king of Thebes in Greek myth, father of Oedipus
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.əs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the king of Thebes who was unknowingly killed by his son Oedipus, fulfilling a tragic prophecy.

Examples

Laius king of Thebes
According to legend, Laius tried to avoid the prophecy but was eventually slain by his own son.
Many ancient Greek plays recount the story of Laius and his doomed family.
The tragic fate of Laius set the stage for the entire Oedipus myth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Jocasta - wife of Laius and later of Oedipus in Greek mythology
  • synonym: Oedipus - son of Laius in Greek mythology, known for unwittingly killing his father
