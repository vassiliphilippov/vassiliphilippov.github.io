abstract thought

noun
thinking about concepts or ideas, not physical objects
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæbˌstrækt ˈθɔt / (AmE), / ˌæbˌstrɑːkt ˈθɔːt / (BrE)
the mental ability to consider concepts and ideas that are not physically present, often involving coherent and logical reasoning

Examples

Abstract thought development
Children usually develop the ability for abstract thought during adolescence.
Philosophy classes often encourage students to practice abstract thought.
Understanding advanced mathematics requires a high level of abstract thought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concrete thinking - thinking focusing on physical objects or facts rather than ideas or concepts
