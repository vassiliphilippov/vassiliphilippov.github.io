chronic renal failure

noun
long-lasting kidney failure
4.4 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑː.nɪk ˈriː.nəl ˈfeɪ.ljɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪk ˈriː.nəl ˈfeɪ.ljə / (BrE)
A long-term condition in which the kidneys gradually lose their ability to function properly, often as a result of other systemic disorders.

Examples

To treat chronic renal failure
Early detection can help slow the progression of chronic renal failure and improve quality of life.
Managing chronic renal failure often requires a combination of medication, diet changes, and sometimes dialysis.
The patient was diagnosed with chronic renal failure after years of uncontrolled diabetes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kidney failure - loss of kidney function to filter blood normally
