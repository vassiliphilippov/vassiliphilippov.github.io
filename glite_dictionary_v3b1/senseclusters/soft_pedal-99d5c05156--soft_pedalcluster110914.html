soft pedal

noun
piano pedal for softening sound
4.7
Pronunciation: / sɔft ˈpɛdəl / (AmE), / sɒft ˈpɛdəl / (BrE)
a pedal on a piano that, when pressed, moves the action closer to the strings, producing a softer sound

Examples

Press the soft pedal
He pressed the soft pedal to mellow the tone of the music.
Most pianists use the soft pedal when playing gentle passages.
The grand piano's soft pedal can create a more intimate sound in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: una corda - the standard Italian term for the soft pedal on a piano
