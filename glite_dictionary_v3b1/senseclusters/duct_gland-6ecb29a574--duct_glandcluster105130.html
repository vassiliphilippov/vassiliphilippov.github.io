duct gland

noun
gland that secretes via a duct
Pronunciation: / dʌkt ɡlænd / (AmE), / dʌkt ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland that releases its secretion through a duct to an external or internal surface

Examples

duct gland secretion
Sweat glands are classified as duct glands because they secrete sweat through ducts to the skin surface.
The salivary gland is a type of duct gland that produces saliva in the mouth.
Unlike an endocrine gland, a duct gland releases its products externally rather than into the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endocrine gland - a gland that secretes hormones directly into the bloodstream
  • synonym: exocrine gland - a gland that releases its secretion via ducts to an epithelial surface
