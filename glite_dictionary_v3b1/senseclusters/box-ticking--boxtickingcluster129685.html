box-ticking

noun
doing tasks just to meet requirements
5.9
Pronunciation: / bɑks ˈtɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / bɒks ˈtɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of carrying out tasks solely to satisfy formal requirements, without genuine effort or concern for quality; a superficial or perfunctory approach

Examples

A box-ticking exercise
He was tired of the box-ticking exercises required in every training session.
Instead of seeing it as mere box-ticking, she tried to make the process valuable.
The new policy led to more bureaucratic box-ticking and less meaningful change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lip service - insincere expression of agreement or support
  • synonym: perfunctory - done as a routine or duty, with little care
