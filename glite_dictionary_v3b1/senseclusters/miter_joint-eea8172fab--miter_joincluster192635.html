miter joint

noun
joint forming a right-angled corner with bevelled ends
9.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.tər dʒɔɪnt/ /; / /ˈmɪ.tər dʒɔɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.tə dʒɔɪnt/ (BrE)
a joint forming a corner, where both surfaces are bevelled, typically at a 45-degree angle, so they fit together to form a 90-degree corner

Examples

A miter joint corner
A miter joint is commonly used in carpentry to create neatly joined edges on moldings.
Properly cutting a miter joint requires precise measurement and a sharp saw.
The picture frame was assembled using a miter joint at each corner for a clean look.
Carpenters often use glue to reinforce a mitre joint and ensure its stability.
The picture frame was assembled using a mitre joint at each corner to achieve a neat finish.
When making a box, a mitre joint provides a more attractive edge than a simple butt joint.

Related Terms

  • antonym: butt joint - joint where two pieces are joined end to end without bevels
  • antonym: dovetail joint - joint interlocking like a dove's tail, used in woodworking
