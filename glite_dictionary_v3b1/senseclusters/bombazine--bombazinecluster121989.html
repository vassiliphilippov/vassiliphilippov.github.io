bombazine

noun
a fabric of silk and worsted used for dresses
Pronunciation: / ˌbɑːm.bəˈziːn / (AmE), / ˌbɒm.bəˈziːn / (BrE)
a twilled fabric with a silk warp and worsted weft, traditionally used for dresses and especially for mourning garments

Examples

Black bombazine fabric
Museum exhibits included a dress made of black bombazine from the Victorian era.
She ran her fingers over the soft texture of the bombazine fabric.
The tailor recommended using bombazine for the widow's mourning gown.
