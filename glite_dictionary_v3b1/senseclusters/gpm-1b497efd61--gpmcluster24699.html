GPM

noun
gross profit margin
6.9 0.33/m
Pronunciation: / d íi pi …õm / (AmE), / d íiÀê piÀê em / (BrE)
an abbreviation for gross profit margin, a financial ratio showing the percentage difference between sales revenue and the cost of goods sold

Examples

GPM of a company
The company's GPM improved significantly after it reduced production costs.
Analysts compare GPM across different companies to assess profitability.
A higher GPM often indicates that a business is managing its costs well.

Synonyms

  • gross profit margin
    4.1 0.04/m
    gross profit margin is the full term used in finance reports and explanations, while GPM is the short abbreviation used in tables and notes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gross margin - the difference between sales and the cost of goods sold
  • antonym: net profit margin - the percentage of revenue remaining after all expenses are deducted
