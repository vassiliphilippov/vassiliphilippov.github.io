disputatiousness

noun
tendency to argue
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being inclined to argue or engage in disputes

Examples

Her disputatiousness in talks
Her disputatiousness made group discussions lively but sometimes exhausting for others.
The manager grew tired of his constant disputatiousness during team meetings.
A certain level of disputatiousness can be useful in a debate club.

Related Terms

  • synonym: argumentativeness - the tendency to argue frequently
  • antonym: peacefulness - the quality of being calm and avoiding conflict
