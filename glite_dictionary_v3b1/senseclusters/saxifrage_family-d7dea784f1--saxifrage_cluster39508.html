saxifrage family

noun
family of herbaceous plants (Saxifragaceae)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæksɪˌfrædʒ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈsæksɪˌfrɪdʒ ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a large family of mainly herbaceous plants (Saxifragaceae) found chiefly in temperate and cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including the saxifrages and related genera

Examples

The saxifrage family plants
Botanists sometimes debate whether certain genera should be classified within the saxifrage family or in separate families.
Many alpine plants belong to the saxifrage family, thriving in rocky, mountainous environments.
The saxifrage family includes both evergreen and deciduous species found across the Northern Hemisphere.

  • degree higher: hydrangea family - the plant family Hydrangeaceae, sometimes treated as overlapping with saxifrage family
