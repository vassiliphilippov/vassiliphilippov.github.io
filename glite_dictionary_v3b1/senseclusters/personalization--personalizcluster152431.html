personalization

noun
cognitive bias
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpɜrsənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a cognitive bias in which someone believes that events or others' actions are caused by or directed at themselves

Examples

Personalization of criticism
Her constant personalization of every criticism made it hard for her to accept feedback.
Therapists help clients recognize when personalization is affecting their interpretation of events.
Excessive personalization can lead to unnecessary feelings of guilt or responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: egocentrism - the tendency to view everything in relation to oneself
  • antonym: objectivity - the quality of not being influenced by personal feelings or opinions
