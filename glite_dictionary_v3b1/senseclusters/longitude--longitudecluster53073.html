longitude

noun
angular distance from prime meridian
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːndʒəˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈlɒndʒɪtjuːd /; / ˈlɒŋɡɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the distance east or west of the prime meridian, measured in degrees, used to describe a location on the Earth's surface

Examples

Longitude degrees
Early navigators had difficulty determining their exact longitude at sea.
Maps indicate both the latitude and longitude of a location.
The city lies at a longitude of 73 degrees east.
Sailors used the stars to determine their longitude while crossing the ocean.
The city lies at a longitude of 77 degrees east.
Maps show both the latitude and longitude of different locations around the world.
