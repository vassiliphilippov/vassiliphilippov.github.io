Gutierrezia sarothrae

noun
a sticky subshrub of the southwestern US
Pronunciation: / ɡuːˌtɪəriˈɛziə səˈrɒθriː / (AmE), / ɡuːˌtɪəriˈɛziə səˈrɒθriː / (BrE)
A low-growing, sticky subshrub native to the southwestern United States, characterized by narrow, linear leaves on many slender branches and numerous tiny yellow flower heads.
