plant disease

noun
disease affecting plants
6.6
Pronunciation: / plænt dɪˈziz / (AmE), / plɑːnt dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease that affects and harms plants, often caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, or environmental factors.

Examples

Plant disease control
Proper crop rotation can help reduce the risk of plant disease in agricultural systems.
Scientists are developing new ways to detect plant disease before symptoms become visible.
The farmer lost much of his crop to a plant disease that spread rapidly across the field.
