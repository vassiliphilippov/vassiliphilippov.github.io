Dinornithiformes

noun
order of extinct flightless New Zealand birds
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪnɔːrˌnɪθəˈfɔːrmiːz /; / daɪˌnɔrnəˈθɪfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / dɪnɔːˌnɪθɪˈfɔːmiːz /; / daɪˌnɔːnɪˈθɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of recently extinct, large, flightless birds native to New Zealand, also known as moas

Examples

The Dinornithiformes birds
All members of the Dinornithiformes were flightless and native to New Zealand.
Scientists have studied fossils of Dinornithiformes to understand their extinction.
The Dinornithiformes included some of the largest birds to ever live.
Moas, members of the Dinornithiformes, could reach impressive sizes exceeding three meters in height.
Scientists study the fossils of Dinornithiformes to understand their rapid extinction.
The Dinornithiformes were the dominant herbivores in prehistoric New Zealand ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moa - any bird of the Dinornithiformes order
