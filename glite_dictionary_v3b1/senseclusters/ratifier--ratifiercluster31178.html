ratifier

noun
one who approves formally
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrætɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈrætɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person, group, or thing that formally approves or confirms an agreement, law, or decision

Examples

The ratifier signs
The treaty could not take effect until every ratifier had signed the document.
As a ratifier of the new policy, the committee played a crucial role in its adoption.
Each state acted as a ratifier of the constitutional amendment.
The senate acted as the ratifier of the international agreement.
Each state must become a ratifier before the treaty takes effect.
As the official ratifier, the committee reviewed all terms before approval.
The president became the final ratifier of the international treaty.
A notary public often acts as a ratifier for important legal documents.
The council's unanimous vote served as a ratifier of the new policy.
