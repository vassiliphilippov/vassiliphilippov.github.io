cite

verb
refer to as evidence or support
5.4
Pronunciation: / saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
to mention or refer to a source, example, or evidence to support an argument or statement

Examples

To cite a source
In her essay, she cites several recent studies to support her argument.
The lawyer cited previous court cases to back up his point.
You should always cite your sources when writing a research paper.
In her research paper, she cited several leading experts in the field.
The lawyer cited a previous court decision to support his argument.
When challenged, he cited specific passages from the original document.
She cited the author's work in her research paper.
The report cites several recent studies to support its conclusions.
When asked for proof, he could not cite any reliable sources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to refuse to acknowledge or mention
  • synonym: quote - to repeat or copy out (a passage or statement) from a source
  • synonym: reference - to mention or allude to as an example or proof
