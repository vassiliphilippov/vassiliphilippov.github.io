firstness

noun
immediacy or possibility
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrstnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːstnəs / (BrE)
in philosophy, the quality or mode of being that is immediate, possible, or felt, without relation to anything else

Examples

Philosophical firstness
In Peirce's philosophy, firstness represents pure possibility before any interaction or relation occurs.
The concept of firstness helps explain the immediate qualities of experience in philosophical discussions.
Some philosophers debate whether firstness can exist independently of secondness and thirdness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secondness - the mode of being related to actuality or reaction
  • antonym: thirdness - the mode of being related to law, mediation, or generality
