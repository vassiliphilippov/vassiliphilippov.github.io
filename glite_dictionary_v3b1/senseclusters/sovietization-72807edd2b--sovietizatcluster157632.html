Sovietization

noun
adoption of Soviet system
9.9
Pronunciation: /soʊ.vɪ.ə.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /soʊ.vɪ.ə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /səʊ.vɪ.ə.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /səʊ.vɪ.ə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of bringing a country, region, or institution under the political, economic, or cultural influence or systems of the Soviet Union

Examples

The Sovietization of Europe
The Sovietization of Eastern Europe after World War II changed the region's political landscape.
Many traditional customs were suppressed during the Sovietization of local institutions.
Historians debate the long-term effects of Sovietization on education and culture in the affected countries.
The Sovietization of education led to new curricula and teaching methods in the region.
After the war, rapid Sovietization changed the political landscape of several Eastern European countries.
Many traditional customs disappeared during the Sovietization of local culture.
