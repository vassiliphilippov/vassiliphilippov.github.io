Troilus

proper noun
Trojan prince in myth
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊɪləs / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊɪləs / (BrE)
a character from Greek mythology and literature, known as a Trojan prince and tragic lover in the story of Troilus and Cressida

Examples

Troilus of Troy
In Greek legend, Troilus was a young prince of Troy fated to die before his twentieth birthday.
Shakespeare's play explores the tragic love story between Troilus and Cressida during the Trojan War.
Many medieval poets wrote about Troilus as a symbol of doomed romance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cressida - Troilus's lover in medieval and Renaissance literature
  • synonym: Paris - another Trojan prince in Greek mythology
