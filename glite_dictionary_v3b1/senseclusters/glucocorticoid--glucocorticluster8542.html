glucocorticoid

noun
a steroid hormone of the adrenal cortex
5.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /gluː.kə.ˈkɔːr.tɪ.kɔɪd/ /; / /gluː.kə.ˈkɑːr.tɪ.kɔɪd/ (AmE), /gluː.kə.ˈkɔː.tɪ.kɔɪd/ (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex that influences the functioning of gonads and has anti-inflammatory effects

Examples

Natural glucocorticoid hormone
Cortisol is the most important natural glucocorticoid in the human body.
Long-term use of glucocorticoids can suppress the immune system and cause other side effects.
The doctor prescribed a glucocorticoid to reduce inflammation in her joints.

Related Terms
