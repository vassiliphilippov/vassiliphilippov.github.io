principal sum

noun
sum of money originally invested or lent
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪnsəpəl sʌm / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl sʌm / (BrE)
the original amount of money invested or lent, on which interest or income is calculated

Examples

The principal sum invested
Investors must distinguish between the principal sum and the interest earned over time.
She received the principal sum back after the bond matured, along with the accrued interest.
The principal sum will be repaid at the end of the loan term, with interest paid annually.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interest - the income or return earned on capital or principal sum
  • synonym: principal - the original sum of money invested or lent
