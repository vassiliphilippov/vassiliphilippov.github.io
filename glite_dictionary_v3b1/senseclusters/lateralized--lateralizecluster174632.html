lateralized

adjective
controlled by one side
Pronunciation: / ˈlætərəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈlætərəlaɪzd / (BrE)
controlled, perceived, or affecting mainly one side (of the brain, body, or senses) rather than both equally

Examples

Lateralized brain function
In most people, language processing is lateralized to the left hemisphere of the brain.
Some cognitive functions are more lateralized in adults than in children.
The study found that emotional responses can be lateralized depending on the type of emotion.
The sound was lateralized to the right, making it feel like the music was coming from that side.
In some hearing tests, tones are lateralized to help identify ear-specific issues.
The engineer lateralized the vocals to create a more immersive listening experience.
The neurologist noted that the patient's symptoms were lateralized to the left side of the body.
A lateralized lesion in the brain can affect movement on only one side.
Hearing loss may be lateralized, impacting just one ear rather than both.
