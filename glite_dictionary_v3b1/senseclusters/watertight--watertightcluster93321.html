watertight

adjective
flawless; impossible to disprove or question
5.1 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ît…ôrÀåta…™t / (AmE), / Ààw…îÀêt…ôta…™t / (BrE)
(of an argument, case, plan, or alibi) so well put together that it contains no errors, flaws, or loopholes; impossible to disprove or question.

Examples

Watertight argument
His alibi was so watertight that the police could not find any inconsistencies.
She presented a watertight argument that convinced the entire jury.
The company made sure its contract was watertight to prevent legal challenges.

Synonyms

  • airtight
    5.9 0.83/m
    airtight describes an argument with no flaws or loopholes, while watertight describes the same fully secure and flawless case.
  • bulletproof
    5.8 0.48/m
    bulletproof is an informal way to say an argument cannot be disproved, while watertight is a neutral term for no flaws.
  • unassailable
    8.0 0.94/m
    unassailable means it cannot be attacked or defeated, while watertight focuses on having no errors or loopholes to challenge.
  • ironclad
    7.4 0.59/m
    ironclad describes a rule or agreement that is rigid and unchangeable, while watertight focuses on being free of logical flaws.
  • unshakable
    4.8 0.87/m
    unshakable describes a strong belief or certainty that cannot be moved, while watertight describes a flawless argument that cannot be disproved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulletproof - completely safe from flaws; impossible to attack successfully
  • synonym: ironclad - so strong or firm as to be unbreakable; not able to be contradicted
