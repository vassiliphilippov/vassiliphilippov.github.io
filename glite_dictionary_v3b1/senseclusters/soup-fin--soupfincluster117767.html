soup-fin

noun
a Pacific shark valued for its fins and liver
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːp fɪn / (AmE), / ˈsuːp fɪn / (BrE)
a species of Pacific shark valued for its fins, especially for use in Chinese soup, and for its liver, which is rich in vitamin A

Examples

A soup-fin shark
A soup-fin shark's liver is particularly noted for its high vitamin A content.
Regulations now limit the catching of soup-fins to protect shark populations.
The chef selected a soup-fin for tonight's special dish featuring traditional Chinese flavors.
