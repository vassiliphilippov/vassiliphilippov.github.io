carotid plexus

noun
network of nerves around the carotid artery
8.6
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑtɪd ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / kəˈrɒtɪd ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a network of nerves surrounding the internal carotid artery, involved in supplying autonomic nerve fibers to the head and neck

Examples

Carotid plexus nerves
Damage to the carotid plexus can result in various neurological symptoms.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided the carotid plexus to prevent nerve damage.
The carotid plexus plays a role in regulating blood flow to the brain.
