A substance or agent that causes narrowing of blood vessels, thereby increasing blood pressure.

Examples

A vasoconstrictive agent
Cold temperatures act as a vasoconstrictive by causing blood vessels to narrow.
Doctors may use a vasoconstrictive to reduce bleeding during surgery.
Nicotine is a powerful vasoconstrictive that can raise blood pressure.

Synonyms

  • vasoconstrictor
    7.3 0.09/m
    vasoconstrictor is an agent that narrows blood vessels to raise pressure, while vasoconstrictive describes something that has that narrowing effect.
  • vasopressor
    8.1 0.02/m
    vasopressor is a medical drug used to raise blood pressure in patients, while vasoconstrictive is a general property that narrows vessels.
  • pressor
    9.9 0.10/m
    pressor is any agent that raises blood pressure and appears in medical talk, while vasoconstrictive specifically means it narrows blood vessels.
