aggression

noun
act of initiating hostilities
Pronunciation: / əˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of launching hostilities or initiating attack against another, especially between individuals or nations

Examples

Military aggression
The general condemned the country's aggression against its neighbor.
The treaty was intended to prevent further acts of aggression in Eastern Europe.
Unprovoked aggression can lead to broader conflict within the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place
  • antonym: defense - the act of protecting against attack or aggression
