suture

verb
to sew tissue together (medical)
4.8 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsutʃər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
to join tissue or parts of the body together by sewing with a needle and thread, especially after surgery

Examples

To suture a wound
After the operation, the nurse helped to suture the deep cut.
Doctors frequently suture both internal tissues and the skin during procedures.
The surgeon will suture the incision to close the wound.
