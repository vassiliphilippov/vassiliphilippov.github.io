kemp

noun
coarse wool fiber
Pronunciation: / kɛmp / (AmE), / kɛmp / (BrE)
a coarse, wiry fiber in wool that does not absorb dye well and reduces wool quality

Examples

Kemp in wool
The presence of kemp lowers the quality of the wool.
She examined the fleece for kemp before deciding to buy it.
High-quality yarn is made from wool with little or no kemp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiber - a thin thread of natural or artificial material
  • synonym: wool - the soft, curly hair from sheep used for making fabric
