fulsome

adjective
insincerely excessive or flattering
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊl.səm / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.səm / (BrE)
excessively flattering or insincerely lavish in manner or speech, often to an unpleasant degree

Examples

A fulsome praise
Critics found the review so fulsome that it seemed more like flattery than honest praise.
His fulsome speech about the boss made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
She was put off by his fulsome compliments, which sounded insincere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genuine - authentic, sincere
  • synonym: smarmy - excessively ingratiating or insincerely flattering
  • synonym: unctuous - excessively or insincerely flattering or smooth
