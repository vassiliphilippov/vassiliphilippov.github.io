wisp

verb
to float lightly
5.1
Pronunciation: /wɪsp/ (AmE), /wɪsp/ (BrE)
to move or float gently and lightly through the air, like a thin strand or trail

Examples

Smoke wisps upward
A thin trail of smoke wisped up from the campfire into the morning sky.
Mist wisped across the lake as the sun began to rise.
The scent of flowers wisped through the open window on the gentle breeze.
Thin clouds began to wisp across the early morning sky.
The scent of lavender seemed to wisp through the open window on the breeze.
As the candle burned, smoke would wisp upward and disappear into the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: billow - to move or flow outward in a rolling motion, like clouds or smoke
  • synonym: drift - to be carried slowly by air or water
  • synonym: hover - to remain in one place in the air
