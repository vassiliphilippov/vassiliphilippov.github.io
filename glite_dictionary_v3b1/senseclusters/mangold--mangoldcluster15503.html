mangold

noun
beet grown for cattle feed
8.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmænˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəʊld / (BrE)
A variety of beet with a large yellowish root, grown chiefly as cattle fodder.

Examples

A mangold root
After the harvest, piles of mangold were stored in the barn.
The farmer grew mangold in large fields to feed his cattle through the winter.
Unlike sugar beets, mangold is rarely used for human consumption.
