act on

verb
take action in response to something
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈækt ɑn /; / ækt ɑn / (AmE), / ˈakt ɒn /; / ækt ɒn / (BrE)
to do something as a result of receiving information, advice, or a request

Examples

To act on advice
If you receive important information, you should act on it immediately.
She failed to act on his advice, which led to further problems.
The committee decided to act on the recommendations made in the report.
He failed to act on the warning, which led to further problems.
She decided to act on her doctor's advice and started exercising regularly.
The government needs to act on the recommendations in the report.

Related Terms

  • synonym: follow up - to pursue or continue further action on something
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not pay attention to something or not take action on it
