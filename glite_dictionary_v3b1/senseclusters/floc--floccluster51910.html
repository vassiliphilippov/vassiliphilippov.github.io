floc

noun
small mass of suspended particles in liquid
9.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / flÉ‘k / (AmE), / flÉ’k / (BrE)
a small, loosely aggregated mass of particles suspended in or precipitated from a liquid, especially during water treatment or chemical processes

Examples

Water floc
During the water treatment process, floc forms and settles to the bottom of the tank.
Scientists observed the formation of floc when chemicals were added to the solution.
The presence of larger floc can improve filtration efficiency in industrial processes.

Synonyms

  • floccule
    9.9 0.00/m
    floccule is a small clump of fine material often found in liquid, while floc is the water-treatment or chemistry term for such masses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flocculate - to form into small clumps or masses in a liquid
  • synonym: precipitate - to cause a solid to be separated from a solution
