jibber

noun
incoherent talker
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɪ.bər/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɪ.bə/ (BrE)
a person who speaks rapidly and incoherently, making it difficult to understand them

Examples

A fast-talking jibber
The jibber at the party kept talking so fast that no one could follow his stories.
During the interview, the nervous jibber made it hard for the panel to understand his answers.
She was known as a jibber because her words always tumbled out in a confusing rush.
