subdivision

noun
mesh-refinement technique in graphics
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌb.dɪˈvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.dɪˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
(in computer graphics) a technique for refining a polygonal mesh by recursively subdividing its faces to produce a smoother, higher-resolution surface; also used for the resulting surface (subdivision surface).

Examples

Subdivision surface modeling
The 3D artist used subdivision to generate a high-resolution model from a simple mesh.
Modern game engines apply subdivision algorithms to smooth character models during real-time rendering.
By increasing the number of subdivision levels, she produced a much smoother sculpt for printing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mesh smoothing - techniques used to make polygonal meshes appear smoother by altering geometry or topology
  • synonym: subdivision surface - a smooth surface produced by applying subdivision algorithms to a polygonal mesh
