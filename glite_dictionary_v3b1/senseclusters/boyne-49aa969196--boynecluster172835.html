Boyne

proper noun
a 1690 battle in Ireland between William III and James II
Pronunciation: / bɔɪn / (AmE), / bɔɪn / (BrE)
the Battle of the Boyne, a decisive conflict in 1690 near the River Boyne in Ireland, where forces of William III defeated those of the deposed James II, ending the attempt to restore Catholic rule in England.

Examples

The Boyne battle
During his tour of Ireland, he visited the site of the Boyne.
Many historical books detail the events leading up to the Boyne.
The Boyne remains a symbol of Protestant victory in Ireland to this day.
