Cyrus the Younger

proper noun
Persian prince, challenger of Artaxerxes II
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðə ˈjʌŋ.gər/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðiː ˈjʌŋ.gər/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðə ˈjʌŋ.gə/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðiː ˈjʌŋ.gə/ (BrE)
(c. 424–401 BC) A Persian prince and military leader who attempted to seize the throne from his brother Artaxerxes II but was defeated and killed at the Battle of Cunaxa.

Examples

Cyrus the Younger prince
Cyrus the Younger was defeated in 401 BC at the Battle of Cunaxa.
History students often compare Cyrus the Younger to other ambitious Persian princes.
The Greek mercenaries hired by Cyrus the Younger became famous in Xenophon's Anabasis.
