adopted

adjective
acquired and treated as one's own (of place or culture)
Pronunciation: / əˈdɑptɪd / (AmE), / əˈdɒptɪd / (BrE)
(of a place, country, or culture) acquired and treated as one's own, often by choice rather than by birth.

Examples

The adopted country

She calls Paris her adopted city despite being born in Rome.
The author often wrote about her experiences in her adopted state.
