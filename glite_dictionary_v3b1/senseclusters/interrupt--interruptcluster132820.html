interrupt

verb
to stop or disturb the continuity of something

to stop or break the continuity of something, such as an activity, event, or someone speaking

Examples

To interrupt a conversation
A loud noise suddenly interrupted the peaceful evening.
Her phone call interrupted my train of thought while studying.
Please do not interrupt the speaker during the presentation.
New policies were introduced to interrupt the cycle of poverty in the region.
She decided to interrupt her studies to travel the world for a year.
The government hopes to interrupt the spread of disease through early vaccination programs.
He tried to interrupt the meeting with a question, but was asked to wait.
It's rude to interrupt people when they are speaking.
Please don't interrupt me while I'm on the phone.

Synonyms

  • cut off
    3.8 24/m
    cut off stops something suddenly or blocks it from continuing, while interrupt means breaking the continuity of an activity or speech.
  • cut short
    3.5 2.65/m
    cut short ends or stops something before it is finished, while interrupt focuses on breaking continuity in action or speech.
  • bring up short
    7.4 0.25/m
    bring up short stops someone or an action suddenly and unexpectedly, while interrupt means breaking the flow of an activity or speech.
  • cut in
    3.5 0.32/m
    cut in interrupts a person who is speaking by joining the conversation, while interrupt covers stopping any activity or speech.
  • disrupt
    4.4 14/m
    disrupt breaks normal progress and causes disorder in a process, while interrupt more simply stops or breaks continuity.
  • disturb
    2.1 7.20/m
    disturb interrupts or destroys quiet or peace and often means bother, while interrupt is a neutral word for stopping continuity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to go on without stopping or being disturbed
  • synonym: disrupt - to interrupt by causing disorder or confusion
  • synonym: disturb - to interrupt the normal condition or activity of something
  • synonym: pause - to temporarily stop an activity or process
  • antonym: resume - to begin again or continue after a pause or interruption
  • synonym: terminate - bring to an end or halt
