back-to-back

adjective
consecutive; one after the other
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (AmE), / ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (BrE)
happening one after another in a sequence, without interruption

Examples

Two back-to-back meetings
He scheduled two back-to-back meetings in the morning with barely any break in between.
The team won three back-to-back games last week, setting a new record.
We have a series of back-to-back appointments all afternoon.
She scheduled back-to-back meetings all afternoon and had no time for a break.
The team won five back-to-back games this season.
We watched three back-to-back movies on Friday night.
