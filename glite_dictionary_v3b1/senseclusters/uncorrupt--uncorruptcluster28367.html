uncorrupt

adjective
honest and upright
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
not affected by corruption; honest and morally upright in character or actions

Examples

An uncorrupt leader
The new mayor was praised for being completely uncorrupt in all his dealings.
People trusted her because she remained uncorrupt despite the pressures of her position.
It is rare to find an uncorrupt official in such a competitive environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrupt - willing to act dishonestly for personal gain
  • synonym: honest - truthful and morally upright
  • synonym: incorrupt - not susceptible to corruption; pure and honest
