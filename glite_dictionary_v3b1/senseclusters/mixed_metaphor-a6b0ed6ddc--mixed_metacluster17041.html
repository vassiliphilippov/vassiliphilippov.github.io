mixed metaphor

noun
incongruous combination of metaphors
2.1
Pronunciation: / mɪkst ˈmɛtəˌfɔr / (AmE), / mɪkst ˈmɛtəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a figure of speech in which two or more incompatible metaphors are combined, often resulting in a confusing or ludicrous effect

Examples

A mixed metaphor example
His speech was riddled with mixed metaphors that left the audience confused.
The politician’s mixed metaphor—'We'll burn that bridge when we come to it'—drew laughter from the crowd.
Writers are often warned to avoid mixed metaphors in their work to keep their language clear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to something to which it is not literally applicable
