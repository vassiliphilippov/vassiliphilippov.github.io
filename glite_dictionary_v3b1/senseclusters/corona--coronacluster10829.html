corona

noun
the Sun's outer atmosphere, a halo seen at eclipse
7.5 6.6
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈroʊ.nə/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrəʊ.nə/ (BrE)
the outermost region of the Sun's atmosphere, visible as a white halo during a solar eclipse

Examples

Sun's corona
During the total solar eclipse, the corona was clearly visible as a bright, glowing halo around the darkened Sun.
Scientists study the Sun's corona to understand solar winds and other astronomical phenomena.
The temperature of the corona is much higher than that of the Sun's surface, puzzling astronomers.
