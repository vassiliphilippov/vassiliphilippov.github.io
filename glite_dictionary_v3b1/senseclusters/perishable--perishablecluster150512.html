perishable

noun
item that spoils or decays quickly
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛɹ.ɪ.ʃə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛɹ.ɪ.ʃə.bəl / (BrE)
A food or item, especially one that decays, spoils, or loses quality quickly and must be kept cool or used shortly after purchase.

Examples

Fresh perishables
Airlines often restrict the shipping of perishables on long international flights.
During the summer, it's important to transport perishables in a cooler to prevent spoilage.
The grocery store places all the perishables in refrigerated sections to keep them fresh.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonperishable - food that does not spoil or decay quickly
