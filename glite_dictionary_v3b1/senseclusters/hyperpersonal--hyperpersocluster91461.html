hyperpersonal

adjective
excessively personal
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈpɜrsənl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈpɜːsənl / (BrE)
excessively personal, especially by revealing private details or feelings in an inappropriate or intrusive way

Examples

Hyperpersonal questions
The interviewer's hyperpersonal questions made the candidate feel uncomfortable.
Some reality TV shows encourage hyperpersonal sharing of contestants' private lives.
His comments became hyperpersonal, focusing on her childhood rather than her professional skills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impersonal - not showing or involving personal feelings
  • synonym: intrusive - too involved in someone else's private life
