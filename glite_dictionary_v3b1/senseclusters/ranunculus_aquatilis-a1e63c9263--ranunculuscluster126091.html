Ranunculus aquatilis

noun
species of water crowfoot
9.9
Pronunciation: / rəˈnʌŋkjələs ˌækwəˈtaɪlɪs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs ˌakwəˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of water crowfoot with submerged and floating leaves and white flowers, found in ponds and slow streams in Europe and North America.

Examples

Ranunculus aquatilis white flowers
Ranunculus aquatilis often covers the surface of calm ponds with its delicate white flowers.
The floating leaves of Ranunculus aquatilis provide shelter for small aquatic animals.
You can find Ranunculus aquatilis thriving in slow-moving streams across Europe and North America.
