easting

verb
to move eastward
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːstɪŋ / (BrE)
to move or steer toward the east, especially in navigation.

Examples

To start easting
The sailors began easting at dawn to avoid the approaching storm.
After hours of easting, the ship finally spotted land on the horizon.
They spent the afternoon easting along the coast to reach the next port.

Related Terms

  • antonym: westing - to move toward the west
