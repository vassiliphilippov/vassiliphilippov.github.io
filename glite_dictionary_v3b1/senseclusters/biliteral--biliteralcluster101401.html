biliteral

noun
sign for two sounds
9.9
Pronunciation: / baɪˈlɪtərəl / (AmE), / baɪˈlɪtərəl / (BrE)
a sign or written character that represents two letters or two consonantal sounds, especially in ancient scripts

Examples

An ancient biliteral
In ancient Egyptian writing, a biliteral could stand for two consonant sounds together.
The scribe carefully carved a biliteral into the stone tablet to represent the word's root.
Researchers identified a rare biliteral among the hieroglyphic inscriptions in the tomb.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: triliteral - a sign representing three letters or sounds
  • degree lower: uniliteral - a sign representing a single letter or sound
