factor X

noun
blood-clotting protein (coagulation factor)
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tɚ ˈtɛn / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ˈten / (BrE)
a blood-clotting protein (coagulation factor) that is activated to help convert prothrombin to thrombin during blood coagulation

Examples

Factor X blood protein
Factor X plays a crucial role in the blood coagulation pathway.
A deficiency of factor X can lead to serious bleeding disorders.
Laboratory tests confirmed that his factor X levels were unusually low.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prothrombin - a protein involved in blood clotting, converted by factor X
  • synonym: thrombin - an enzyme formed from prothrombin, following activation by factor X
