stepping stone

noun
means of advancement; aid to progress
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛpɪŋ stoʊn /; / ˈstɛpɪŋ ˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈstɛpɪŋ stəʊn /; / ˈstɛpɪŋ ˌstəʊn / (BrE)
an action, event, or experience that helps you make progress towards a goal

Examples

A stepping stone to success
For many new graduates, an internship is often just a stepping stone to a permanent job.
He viewed the project as a valuable stepping stone in his engineering career.
The position served as a stepping stone for her to start her own business.
For many, an internship serves as a stepping stone to permanent employment.
She used her volunteer work as a stepping stone to building a successful career.
Winning the local championship was a stepping stone to competing at national level.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or advancement
  • synonym: springboard - something that helps launch progress to something else
