brownout

noun
partial blackout for camouflage
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊnˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn.aʊt / (BrE)
a reduction or extinguishing of lighting, especially in a city, to make it less visible to enemy aircraft during wartime

Examples

Wartime brownout
During the war, a nightly brownout was enforced to protect the city from enemy bombers.
The brownout left the streets dimly lit but not in complete darkness.
To avoid being targeted, civilians adhered strictly to the brownout regulations.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blackout - a total extinguishing of lights, especially as a wartime precaution
