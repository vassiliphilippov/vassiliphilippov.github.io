tufting

noun
growth in clumps
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtʌf.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtʌf.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
the formation or presence of tufts or clumps, especially as a natural growth pattern on plants or animals

Examples

Tufting on animal fur
The rabbit's fur showed noticeable tufting around its ears and tail.
After the drought, heavy tufting was visible where the lawn's grass grew in dense clumps.
Certain grasses are known for their natural tufting, creating a patchy appearance in fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clumping - the process of forming into clusters or groups
  • antonym: spreading - the process of extending over a larger area
