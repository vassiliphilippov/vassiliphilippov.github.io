chlorinated lime

noun
bleaching and disinfecting powder
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːrəˌneɪtɪd laɪm / (AmE), / ˈklɒrɪˌneɪtɪd laɪm / (BrE)
A white powder containing calcium hydroxide mixed with chloride and hypochlorite, used as a bleaching agent and disinfectant.

Examples

Chlorinated lime powder
After the outbreak, the authorities distributed chlorinated lime for communal use.
The hospital floors were scrubbed with chlorinated lime to prevent the spread of infection.
Workers added chlorinated lime to the water tanks to purify them.
