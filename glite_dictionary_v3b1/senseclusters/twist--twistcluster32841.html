twist

verb
form into a spiral or coil by turning
5.4 1.5
Pronunciation: / twɪst / (AmE), / twɪst / (BrE)
to turn, wind, or form something into a spiral or curved shape; to move or turn with a twisting motion

Examples

To twist the rope
Before baking, you should twist the pastry strips into spirals for a unique look.
He watched the girl twist blades of grass together into a small ring.
She used her hands to twist the ends of the scarf into a decorative knot.
He twisted his body sharply to avoid the oncoming cyclist.
Please twist the handle in the opposite direction to lock the door.
She had to twist her neck to see the screen behind her.
The narrow river twists through the valley, creating beautiful views around every bend.
The road twists sharply to the left just after the old stone bridge.
We followed the trail as it twisted deeper into the dense forest.

  • antonym: straighten - to make straight instead of twisted
