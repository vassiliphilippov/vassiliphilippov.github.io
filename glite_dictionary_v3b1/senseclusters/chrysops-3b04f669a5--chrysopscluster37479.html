Chrysops

proper noun
genus of deer flies
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkraɪsɑps / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsɒps / (BrE)
a genus of biting flies, commonly called deer flies, some species of which can transmit diseases to humans and animals

Examples

Chrysops biting fly
The bite of a Chrysops can be very painful and may cause allergic reactions.
Some species of Chrysops are known to transmit diseases such as tularemia.
Researchers studied the habitat of Chrysops to better understand how to control their population.
