snit

noun
a state of minor irritation or bad temper
Pronunciation: / snɪt / (AmE), / snɪt / (BrE)
A state of irritated agitation, annoyance, or sulky bad temper, typically over something minor.

Examples

In a snit
Don’t get into a snit just because someone disagreed with you.
She left the meeting in a snit after her proposal was rejected.
Whenever he’s in a snit, he refuses to talk to anyone.
