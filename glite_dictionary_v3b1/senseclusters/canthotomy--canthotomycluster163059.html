canthotomy

noun
eye surgery procedure
9.9
Pronunciation: / kænˈθɑtəmi / (AmE), / kænˈθɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the canthus of the eye is cut to relieve pressure or treat injury

Examples

Canthotomy surgical procedure
The emergency team performed a canthotomy to quickly reduce pressure in the patient's eye.
A canthotomy may be necessary when there is severe swelling around the eye after trauma.
Ophthalmologists are trained to carry out a canthotomy in cases of orbital compartment syndrome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canthoplasty - surgical reconstruction of the canthus of the eye
  • synonym: incision - a surgical cut made in skin or tissue
