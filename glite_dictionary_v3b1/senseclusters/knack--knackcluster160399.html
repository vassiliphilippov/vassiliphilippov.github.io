knack

noun
special skill or talent
3.9 3.52/m
Pronunciation: / nÃ¦k / (AmE), / nÃ¦k / (BrE)
an acquired or natural skill at performing a particular task, often involving a special way of doing it

Examples

A knack for cooking
Despite his efforts, he never quite got the knack of cooking pasta al dente.
He quickly developed a knack for solving complex problems under pressure.
She has a knack for making people feel comfortable right away.
