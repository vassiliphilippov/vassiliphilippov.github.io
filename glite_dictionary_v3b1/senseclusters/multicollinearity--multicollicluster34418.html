multicollinearity

noun
correlation issue in regression analysis
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌl.ti.kəˌlɪn.iˈær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪ.kɒ.lɪ.niˈær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A statistical phenomenon in multiple regression where two or more predictor variables are highly correlated, making it difficult to determine their individual effects.

Examples

High multicollinearity problem
A high variance inflation factor indicates possible multicollinearity among the predictors.
Researchers tested for multicollinearity before interpreting the results of the analysis.
Severe multicollinearity can inflate the standard errors of the coefficients in regression models.
