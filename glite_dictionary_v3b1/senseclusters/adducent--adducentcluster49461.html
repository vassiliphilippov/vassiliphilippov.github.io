adducent

adjective
drawing toward the midline (anatomy)
6.7
Pronunciation: / əˈduːsənt / (AmE), / əˈdjuːsənt / (BrE)
(of a muscle) bringing together or drawing toward the midline of the body or toward an adjacent part.

Examples

Adducent muscle action
Certain exercises target the adducent muscles to improve core strength and posture.
Damage to the adducent nerve can impair the ability to move the eye inward.
The adducent muscles of the thigh are important for stabilizing the legs during walking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abducent - (of a muscle) drawing away from the midline of the body
