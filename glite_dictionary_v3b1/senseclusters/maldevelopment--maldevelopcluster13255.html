maldevelopment

noun
harmful development
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmældɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌmældɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
the process of development that is distorted, uneven, or harmful, often resulting in negative social, economic, or environmental effects

Examples

Urban maldevelopment
Rapid urbanization without proper planning can result in maldevelopment and long-term social problems.
Environmental activists warned that unchecked industrial growth would cause serious maldevelopment in the region.
Policies aimed at reducing inequality must address the root causes of maldevelopment in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: development - the process of growth or progress
  • synonym: underdevelopment - a lack of economic or social progress
