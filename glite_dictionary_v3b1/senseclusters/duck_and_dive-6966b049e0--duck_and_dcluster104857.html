duck and dive

verb
evade skillfully; avoid something quickly
5.8
Pronunciation: / dʌk ənd daɪv / (AmE), / dʌk ænd daɪv / (BrE)
to move quickly or skillfully to avoid something or to evade a difficult situation

Examples

To duck and dive quickly
Children duck and dive between the stalls at the busy market.
He managed to duck and dive his way through the complicated bureaucracy.
You have to duck and dive in the job market to stay ahead of the competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dodge - to avoid someone or something by a sudden quick movement
  • synonym: sidestep - to avoid dealing with something directly
