sandal

noun
a light shoe with straps
Pronunciation: / ˈsændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsændl̩ / (BrE)
a light shoe consisting of a sole held to the foot by straps or thongs passing over the instep and around the ankle

Examples

A pair of sandals
He slipped off his sandals before entering the house.
Leather sandal styles are popular during the summer months.
She wore simple sandals while walking along the beach.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: boot - a sturdy shoe covering the ankle or higher
  • degree lower: flip-flop - a light sandal, often with a thong between the toes
