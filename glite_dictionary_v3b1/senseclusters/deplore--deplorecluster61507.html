deplore

verb
express strong disapproval or regret
6.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈplɔːr/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈplɔː/ (BrE)
to strongly disapprove of or express deep regret about something, often in a formal or serious way

Examples

To deplore the problem
Many citizens deplore the lack of affordable housing in the city.
She deplores any form of discrimination in the workplace.
The committee deplored the corruption found within the organization.
Environmental groups deplore reckless waste of natural resources.
Many international leaders deplore the use of violence in the conflict.
She deplored the lack of funding for education in her speech.
