flying reptile

noun
extinct winged reptile (pterosaur)
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈrɛptəl / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈrɛptaɪl / (BrE)
an extinct group of reptiles from the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, characterized by bird-like beaks and membranous wings supported by an elongated fourth finger

Examples

A flying reptile fossil
Paleontologists discovered the fossils of a flying reptile in the limestone quarry.
Scientists believe that the flying reptile's wings were covered in a thin membrane.
The flying reptile soared above the prehistoric landscape, hunting for fish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pterosaur - any extinct flying reptile of the order Pterosauria
