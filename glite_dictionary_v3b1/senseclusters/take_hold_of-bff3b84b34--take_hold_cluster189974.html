take hold of

verb
grasp or seize with hands
Pronunciation: / teɪk hoʊld əv / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld əv / (BrE)
to grasp or seize something or someone deliberately with your hands

Examples

The firefighter took hold of the hose and aimed it at the flames.

  • antonym: release - to stop holding or to let go of something or someone
