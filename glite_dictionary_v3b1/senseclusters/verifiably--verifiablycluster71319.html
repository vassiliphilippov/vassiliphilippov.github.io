verifiably

adverb
provably true
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛrəˈfaɪəbli / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪˈfaɪəbli / (BrE)
in a way that can be shown to be true or accurate through evidence or proof

Examples

To be verifiably true
The scientist's claims were verifiably supported by multiple independent experiments.
All financial transactions must be verifiably recorded for auditing purposes.
The information provided was verifiably accurate, as confirmed by official documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonstrably - in a way that can be clearly shown or proven
  • antonym: unverifiably - in a way that cannot be shown to be true or accurate
