suffix

noun
word ending added to alter meaning
2.8 0.57/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒfÉªks / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒfÉªks / (BrE)
A letter or group of letters added at the end of a word to form a new word or to change its grammatical function.

Examples

The suffix '-ness'
Can you identify the suffix in the word 'careless'?
The suffix '-ness' turns the adjective 'happy' into the noun 'happiness'.
The plural form 'cats' is made by adding the suffix '-s' to the word 'cat'.

Synonyms

  • postfix
    8.3 0.07/m
    postfix is a technical term for an affix added to a word's end to change its meaning, while suffix is the common term.
  • ending
    2.8 3.16/m
    ending is the general final part of a word, often any last letters or inflection, while suffix is a specific added word part.
