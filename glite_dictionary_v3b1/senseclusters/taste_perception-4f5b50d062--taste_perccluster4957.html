taste perception

noun
the process of detecting and interpreting taste
Pronunciation: / teɪst pɚˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / teɪst pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which the taste buds in the tongue and throat detect and interpret flavors from soluble substances

Examples

Taste perception process
Children's taste perception can change as they grow older, making them more open to new foods.
Researchers are studying how genetics influence taste perception and dietary choices.
Smoking can dull your taste perception, making foods seem less flavorful.
