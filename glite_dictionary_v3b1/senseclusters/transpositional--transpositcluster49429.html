transpositional

adjective
relating to DNA movement
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the movement or rearrangement of DNA segments, especially transposons, within a genome.

Examples

Transpositional movement of genes
The transpositional activity of certain DNA elements can lead to genetic variation in bacteria.
Scientists are investigating the transpositional mechanisms that cause mutations in plant genomes.
Understanding transpositional events is important for studying the evolution of genetic material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genetic - relating to genes or heredity
  • antonym: static - not moving or changing
