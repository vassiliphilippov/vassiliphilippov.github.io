Hernando Cortes

proper noun
Spanish conquistador, defeated the Aztecs
Pronunciation: / hɛrˈnɑndoʊ kɔrˈtɛs / (AmE), / hɜːˈnændəʊ kɔːˈteɪz / (BrE)
(1485–1547) A Spanish conquistador known for overthrowing the Aztec Empire and bringing Mexico under Spanish rule.

Examples

Hernando Cortes expedition
Hernando Cortes led the expedition that resulted in the fall of the Aztec Empire.
Many history books recount the controversial legacy of Hernando Cortes.
The conquest of Mexico by Hernando Cortes dramatically changed the region's history.
