counterfeit

verb
make a fake copy to deceive
4.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.fɪt/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.ər.fɪt/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.fɪt/ (BrE)
to make an exact imitation of something, especially money or documents, with the intention to deceive or defraud

Examples

To counterfeit money
He was arrested for attempting to counterfeit official documents.
It is illegal to counterfeit passports or identity cards.
The gang planned to counterfeit hundreds of $100 bills.
