fowl cholera

noun
contagious bacterial disease of birds
7.2
Pronunciation: / faʊl ˈkoʊlɚə / (AmE), / faʊl ˈkɒl(ə)rə / (BrE)
a contagious and often fatal bacterial disease of birds, especially chickens, caused by Pasteurella multocida, characterized by acute diarrhea and septicemia

Examples

Fowl cholera disease
Proper sanitation is essential to control the spread of fowl cholera among birds.
The outbreak of fowl cholera devastated the poultry farm, causing the loss of hundreds of chickens.
Veterinarians recommend vaccination to prevent fowl cholera in commercial flocks.
