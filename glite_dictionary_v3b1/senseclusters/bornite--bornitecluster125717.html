bornite

noun
copper-iron sulfide mineral
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːnaɪt / (BrE)
a reddish-brown mineral consisting of copper and iron sulfides, found in copper deposits and used as an ore of copper

Examples

A bornite mineral
Geologists identified bornite in the rock samples collected from the mine.
Mining companies often extract bornite to obtain valuable copper.
The shiny, iridescent colors of bornite have earned it the nickname 'peacock ore.'
