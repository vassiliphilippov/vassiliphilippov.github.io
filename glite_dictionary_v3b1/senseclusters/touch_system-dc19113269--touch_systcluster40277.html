touch system

noun
method of typing by touch, not sight
2.8
Pronunciation: / tʌtʃ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A method of typing in which the fingers are trained to strike specific keys without looking, allowing the typist to read and type simultaneously.

Examples

He prefers the touch system over hunting and pecking on the keyboard.
Many schools teach the touch system to help students type more efficiently.
Mastering the touch system increased her typing speed dramatically.
