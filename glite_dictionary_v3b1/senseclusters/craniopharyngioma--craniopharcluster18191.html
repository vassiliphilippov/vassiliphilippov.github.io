craniopharyngioma

noun
benign brain tumor
Pronunciation: / ˌkreɪnioʊˌfærɪnˈdʒiːoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌkreɪniəʊˌfærɪnˈdʒiːəmə / (BrE)
a rare, noncancerous tumor that develops near the pituitary gland at the base of the brain

Examples

A craniopharyngioma tumor
A craniopharyngioma can cause headaches and vision problems if it presses on nearby brain structures.
The surgeon explained the risks of removing a craniopharyngioma near the pituitary gland.
Children diagnosed with craniopharyngioma may need long-term hormone therapy after treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: glioma - a type of tumor that occurs in the brain and spinal cord
  • synonym: pituitary tumor - a tumor located in the pituitary gland
