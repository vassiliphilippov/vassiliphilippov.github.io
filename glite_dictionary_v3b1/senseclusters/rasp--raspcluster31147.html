rasp

noun
harsh or irritated utterance
Pronunciation: / ræsp / (AmE), / rɑːsp / (BrE)
a harsh, grating sound or quality, especially in a voice

Examples

A rasp in his voice
A faint rasp escaped him as he struggled to maintain his composure.
His angry rasp cut through the silence in the room.
She spoke with a rasp in her voice, betraying her irritation.
A rasp in her voice hinted at years of heavy smoking.
His laughter had a rasp that set him apart from the others.
The rasp of chalk on the blackboard made everyone cringe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: growl - a low, guttural sound, especially made in anger
  • synonym: scrape - a harsh sound produced by scraping
  • antonym: whisper - speaking very softly without vibration of the vocal cords
