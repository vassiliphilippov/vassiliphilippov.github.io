disorganize

verb
cause to lose order or structure
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz /; / dɪsˈɔr.ɡə.naɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːɡənaɪz /; / dɪsˈɔː.ɡə.naɪz / (BrE)
to cause something to lose its order, structure, or system; to make disordered or chaotic

Examples

To disorganize the office
A sudden change in routine can disorganize even the most efficient office.
He tends to disorganize his desk with piles of unread documents.
Heavy rain and flooding disorganized the delivery schedule for days.
A sudden influx of new information can disorganise even the best planner.
If you change the filing system now, you will completely disorganise our records.
Loud interruptions tend to disorganise the meeting and make it less productive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disorder - to make untidy or disarranged
  • synonym: disorganize - remove the organization from (American spelling)
  • antonym: organise - put into systematic order (British spelling)
  • antonym: organize - to arrange or order systematically
