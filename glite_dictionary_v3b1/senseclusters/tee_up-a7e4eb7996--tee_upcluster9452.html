tee up

verb
make arrangements or preparations
9.6 4.2
Pronunciation: / ti ʌp / (AmE), / tiː ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare or arrange for something to happen, especially to make it ready for the next step

Examples

To tee up a meeting
Let's tee up a meeting with the client to discuss the proposal.
She spent all morning teeing up the project so things would run smoothly.
The marketing team will tee up the campaign launch for next month.
Before the conference, please tee up all the necessary documents for the panelists.
Could you tee up some talking points for tomorrow's interview?
The marketing team will tee up a campaign launch for the new product.

  • synonym: prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
