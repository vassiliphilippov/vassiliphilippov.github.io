naranjilla

noun
South American fruit-bearing shrub
8.4
Pronunciation: / nɑːrɑnˈhiːjə / (AmE), / nərænˈhiːjə / (BrE)
A small perennial shrub native to the Andes of South America, cultivated for its bright orange edible fruits that resemble tomatoes or oranges.

Examples

The naranjilla fruit
Farmers in Ecuador grow naranjilla for its tart, citrus-flavored fruit prized in local drinks.
She added fresh naranjilla juice to the dessert for a tangy twist.
The bright orange naranjilla stood out among the green foliage in the mountain garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tomato - Edible fruit from a related nightshade plant
