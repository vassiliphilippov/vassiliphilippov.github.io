snowshoe

verb

(in cybersecurity) to distribute malicious emails, messages, or malware across many low-volume sender addresses, IPs, or domains to avoid detection and filtering systems.

Examples

To snowshoe spam emails
The attackers snowshoe their phishing campaign across hundreds of domains to dodge filters.
During the investigation, analysts snowshoed malicious emails across many IP addresses to frustrate tracking.
Our threat report showed several groups snowshoeing spam messages to keep each sender below detection thresholds.
