rematerialization

noun
return to physical objects
Pronunciation: / ˌriːməˌtɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːməˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a shift back toward producing, using, or valuing physical objects and materials after a period of digital or immaterial preference

Examples

Rematerialization of books
The rematerialization of printed magazines has surprised many in the publishing industry.
Some artists are leading a rematerialization movement by creating tangible works instead of digital art.
There is growing interest in the rematerialization of traditional crafts and handmade goods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dematerialization - the process of shifting from physical objects to digital or immaterial forms
