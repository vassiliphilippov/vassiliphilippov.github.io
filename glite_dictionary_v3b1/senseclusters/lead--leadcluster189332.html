lead

noun
main news story
Pronunciation: / lid / (AmE), / liːd / (BrE)
the main or most important story in a news broadcast or newspaper, or the opening section of a news article

Examples

Lead story
She reported the lead story for the evening news broadcast.
The editor decided to make the economic crisis the lead for tomorrow's paper.
Tonight's lead focuses on the mayor's unexpected resignation.
A strong lead draws readers into the story and keeps them engaged.
His editor told him to rewrite the lead to make it more attention-grabbing.
The reporter spent hours crafting the perfect lead for her front-page article.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conclusion - the final part of a text that summarizes or wraps up the content
  • synonym: headline - the title of a news article, often summarizing its main point
  • synonym: introduction - the initial section of a piece of writing that sets the stage for what follows
