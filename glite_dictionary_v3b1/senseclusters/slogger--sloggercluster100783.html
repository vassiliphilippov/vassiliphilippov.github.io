slogger

noun
person who walks heavily and laboriously
Pronunciation: / ˈslɑːɡər / (AmE), / ˈslɒɡə / (BrE)
a person who walks in a slow, heavy, and laborious way, as if burdened by effort

Examples

Slow slogger uphill
A group of determined sloggers made their way up the hill despite the pouring rain.
After hours of hiking through the mud, Miles looked every bit the slogger as he trudged onward.
You could tell he was a slogger by the way each step seemed like a mighty effort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plodder - someone who walks or works slowly and steadily, especially in a dull or determined way
