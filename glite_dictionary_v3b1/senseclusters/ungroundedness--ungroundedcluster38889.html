ungroundedness

noun
lack of grounding
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡraʊndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡraʊndɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state of not being connected to the earth (ground), especially in electrical devices, increasing the risk of shock or interference

Examples

Ungroundedness of wiring
The ungroundedness of the old wiring posed a serious safety hazard in the house.
Technicians checked for ungroundedness before installing the new equipment.
Due to its ungroundedness, the device was more likely to experience electrical interference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grounding - the process of connecting an electrical device to the earth for safety
