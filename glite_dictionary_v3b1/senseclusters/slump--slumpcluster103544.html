slump

noun
period of economic decline
4.6 3.70/m
Pronunciation: / slÊŒmp / (AmE), / slÊŒmp / (BrE)
a period or state of decline or deterioration, especially in the economy or performance

Examples

An economic slump
After the housing bubble burst, the industry entered a prolonged slump.
Investors are cautious because of the recent slump in the global economy.
The country is experiencing a severe slump that has put millions out of work.
After a strong start, the company suffered a prolonged slump in profits during the second quarter.
Attendance at the museum fell into a noticeable slump over the winter months.
The baseball player's hitting slump lasted nearly two months before he regained his form.
