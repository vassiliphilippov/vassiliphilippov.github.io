allodial

adjective
land owned freely
Pronunciation: / əˈloʊdiəl / (AmE), / əˈləʊdiəl / (BrE)
relating to land owned outright, free from any obligation to a superior or landlord

Examples

Allodial land ownership
The farmer was proud that his property was allodial and not subject to any rent or dues.
In some countries, allodial land ownership is rare and most land is held under lease.
The concept of allodial title dates back to medieval times when land could be owned absolutely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: feudal - relating to land held under the feudal system with obligations to a lord
