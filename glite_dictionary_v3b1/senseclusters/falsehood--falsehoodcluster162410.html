falsehood

noun
a false statement or lie
4.3 3.48/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îlsÀåh äd / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêlsh äd / (BrE)
a statement that is not true; a lie or untruth

Examples

A falsehood told
His testimony was filled with falsehood and misrepresentation.
It is important to challenge every falsehood that appears in public discourse.
She accused him of spreading falsehoods about her character.

Synonyms

  • untruth
    5.5 1.08/m
    untruth is a simple word for a wrong statement used in speech or writing, while falsehood names a lie or untrue claim.
  • lie
    2.7 56/m
    lie is a false statement told to deceive others, while falsehood can refer to any untrue statement with or without intent.
  • falsity
    7.9 1.08/m
    falsity is a formal word for the quality of being untrue used in writing or law, while falsehood names a false statement.
  • fabrication
    6.5 1.65/m
    fabrication is an invented story or detail made to fool people, while falsehood is any untrue statement or lie.

Related Terms

  • antonym: truth - a true statement; the quality of being true
