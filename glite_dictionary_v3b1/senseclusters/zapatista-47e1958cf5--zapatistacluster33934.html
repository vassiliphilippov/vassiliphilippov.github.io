Zapatista

noun
Mexican activist group member
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌzɑːpəˈtistə / (AmE), / ˌzapəˈtiːstə / (BrE)
a member of a Mexican movement advocating for indigenous rights and social justice, especially in southern Mexico

Examples

The Zapatista movement began in the 1990s to demand rights for indigenous communities.
Many young people were inspired to join the Zapatistas after learning about their cause.

The Zapatista movement began in the southern Mexican state of Chiapas in 1994.
Many Zapatistas wear masks to protect their identities during protests.
International supporters have visited Chiapas to learn from the Zapatistas and their struggle for indigenous rights.
