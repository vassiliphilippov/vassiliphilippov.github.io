conscript

noun
person drafted into the military
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.skrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.skrɪpt / (BrE)
a person who is compulsorily enlisted into military service

Examples

A young conscript
After graduating, he became a conscript and served two years in the army.
The government announced that every conscript would undergo basic training next month.
Unlike professional soldiers, a conscript may have little choice about serving.
