classical

adjective
of ancient Greek and Roman literature
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsɪkəl /; / ˈklæsɪkəl /; / ˈklasɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the culture, literature, language, or art of ancient Greece and Rome, or having qualities associated with classical antiquity

Examples

Classical literature
His interest in classical antiquity led him to study the works of Homer and Virgil.
She is a classical scholar specializing in ancient Greek literature.
The university offers a classical studies program focusing on Latin and Greek texts.
His research focuses on classical Sanskrit poetry from the 5th century.
Many schools once required students to learn at least one classical language such as Latin.
She specialized in classical Greek while studying ancient texts at university.
His research focuses on the influence of classical architecture on modern design.
She has a deep interest in classical literature, especially works by Homer and Virgil.
The museum features several classical sculptures from the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancient - belonging to the distant past, especially before the end of the Western Roman Empire (AD 476)
  • synonym: classicism - a style or outlook inspired by principles of classical antiquity
  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past
