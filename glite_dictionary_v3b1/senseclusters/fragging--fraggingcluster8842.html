fragging

noun
attacking a teammate
Pronunciation: / ˈfræɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfræɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of deliberately attacking or killing a fellow soldier or teammate, especially in a military context

Examples

Military fragging
The army took strict measures after a case of fragging was reported in the unit.
Rumors of fragging spread quickly among the soldiers, causing fear and mistrust.
The investigation revealed that fragging had occurred during the night patrol.

Related Terms

  • antonym: friendly fire - accidental attack on one's own side
  • synonym: mutiny - rebellion against authority, especially by soldiers or sailors
