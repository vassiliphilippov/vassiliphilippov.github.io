drag

noun
something that slows progress
5.7 3.5
Pronunciation: / dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
something that slows, delays, or hinders progress, or an activity or situation that is boring or tedious; also, the resistance experienced by an object moving through air or water

Examples

A drag on progress
Extra paperwork was a drag that slowed their progress considerably.
Having to wait for approval became a drag on the entire project timeline.
The outdated regulations are a real drag on business innovation.
Cars are designed with aerodynamics in mind to decrease drag at high speeds.
Engineers must reduce drag to improve the fuel efficiency of aircraft.
The swimmer streamlined her body to minimize drag in the water.
Filling out all these forms is such a drag.
He finds commuting through heavy traffic every day a real drag.
Waiting in line at the DMV can really be a drag.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aid - something that helps or assists
  • synonym: bore - something or someone that causes boredom
  • synonym: chore - a dull or routine task, especially a household one
  • antonym: delight - something that gives great pleasure
  • synonym: friction - the resistance to motion between two objects in contact
  • synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance or delay to something
  • synonym: impediment - a hindrance or obstruction in doing something
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or progress
