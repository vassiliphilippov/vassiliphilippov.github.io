hash

verb
to encode data
5.4
Pronunciation: /hæʃ/ (AmE), /hæʃ/ (BrE)
to change information, such as a password, into a coded form using a mathematical process, often for security

Examples

To hash a password
Websites often hash your password so that it cannot be easily read by others.
The developer hashed the sensitive data before storing it in the database.
It is important to hash user information to protect privacy online.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decode - to convert coded information back into its original form
  • synonym: encrypt - to convert information into a secret code to prevent unauthorized access
