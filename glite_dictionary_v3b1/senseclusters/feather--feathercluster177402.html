feather

verb
(of a bird) grow feathers
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
to grow feathers or to cover something with feathers

Examples

Chicks feather out
As the ducklings feather, they lose their soft down.
Parrots typically feather earlier than some other bird species.
The chicks began to feather within two weeks of hatching.
Some birds feather their nests with down for added warmth.
The artist decided to feather the mask for a more dramatic effect.
The costume designer will feather the headdress before the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fledge - (of a young bird) to develop wing feathers that are large enough for flight
