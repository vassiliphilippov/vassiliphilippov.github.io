hart

noun
unit of information (logarithmic, base 10)
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɑrt / (AmE), / hɑːt / (BrE)
A logarithmic unit of information or entropy, based on base 10 logarithms and powers of 10.

Examples

A hart of data
Engineers sometimes use harts when calculations involve decimal-based systems.
One hart represents the information content of an event with a probability of exactly one in ten.
The hart is similar to the bit, but uses a decimal rather than a binary base.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bit - a unit of information based on the binary logarithm (base 2)
  • synonym: nat - a unit of information based on the natural logarithm (base e)
