diminution

noun
musical theme played in shorter note values
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪməˈnuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪmɪˈnjuːʃən / (BrE)
In music, the statement of a theme in notes of shorter duration, typically half the length of the original.

Examples

Musical diminution
Her piece demonstrates mastery of diminution and other contrapuntal techniques.
In Baroque music, composers often use diminution to vary familiar melodies.
The fugue subject returns in diminution, doubling the speed of the original theme.
