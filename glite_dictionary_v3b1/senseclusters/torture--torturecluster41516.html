torture

verb
cause intense emotional or mental suffering
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔr.tʃɚ /; / ˈtɔrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.tʃə /; / ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
to cause someone to suffer severe physical or mental pain, especially deliberately

Examples

To torture someone
He couldn't bear to torture himself by looking at old photographs.
She felt as though her guilt would torture her forever.
The uncertainty about the exam results kept torturing her mind all week.
In the novel, the villain would torture his victims for information.
She felt as if fate continued to torture her with one disaster after another.
They threatened to torture the prisoner if he did not confess.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - to ease someone's pain or distress
  • synonym: torment - to cause severe mental or physical suffering
