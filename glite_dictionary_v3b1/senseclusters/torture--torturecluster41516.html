torture

verb
cause intense emotional or mental suffering
5.7 21/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îr.t É…ö /; / Ààt…îrt É…ôr / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀê.t É…ô /; / Ààt…îÀêt É…ô / (BrE)
to cause someone to suffer severe physical or mental pain, especially deliberately

Examples

To torture someone
He couldn't bear to torture himself by looking at old photographs.
She felt as though her guilt would torture her forever.
The uncertainty about the exam results kept torturing her mind all week.
In the novel, the villain would torture his victims for information.
She felt as if fate continued to torture her with one disaster after another.
They threatened to torture the prisoner if he did not confess.

Synonyms

  • torment
    7.2 5.75/m
    torment causes lasting physical or mental pain in people, while torture usually means deliberate, often severe pain used to punish or control.
  • excruciate
    9.9 0.46/m
    excruciate causes extreme, unbearable pain and torment, while torture is the general term for causing severe physical or mental pain.
  • rack
    7.5 2.12/m
    rack means to cause intense physical or mental strain, often like a specific instrument, while torture is the general act of causing severe pain.
  • martyrize
    9.9 0.00/m
    martyrize means to torture or persecute someone to make them a martyr, while torture is causing severe physical or mental pain.
