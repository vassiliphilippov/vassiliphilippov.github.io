lyssavirus

noun
genus of viruses including rabies virus
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪsəˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪsəˌvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of viruses in the family Rhabdoviridae, including the rabies virus, that infect the nervous system of mammals.

Examples

Lyssavirus infection
Bats are known to be carriers of certain types of lyssavirus in the wild.
Protective measures are important to prevent the spread of lyssavirus infections among mammals.
Scientists identified a new lyssavirus that poses a risk to domestic animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rabies - a deadly viral disease affecting the nervous system, caused by a lyssavirus
