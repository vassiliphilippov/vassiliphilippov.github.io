readerly

adjective
conventional in meaning
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdərli / (AmE), / ˈriːdəli / (BrE)
describing a text that presents clear, conventional meaning and encourages passive reading rather than active interpretation

Examples

Readerly novel style
The novel was considered readerly because its plot and characters followed familiar patterns.
Some critics prefer readerly texts that do not challenge traditional storytelling.
Her latest book is more readerly than her earlier, experimental works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: writerly - describing a text that invites active interpretation and creative engagement
