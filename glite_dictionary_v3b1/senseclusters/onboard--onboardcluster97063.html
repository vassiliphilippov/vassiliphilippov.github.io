onboard

verb
to integrate new members
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑnˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌbɔːd / (BrE)
to help a new person become part of a team or organization by providing necessary information and support

Examples

To onboard new staff
The company will onboard all new hires with a week-long training program.
It is important to onboard new volunteers so they understand their roles.
Managers should onboard team members to help them feel comfortable and informed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orient - to familiarize someone with a new situation or environment
  • synonym: welcome - to greet and receive someone into a group or place
