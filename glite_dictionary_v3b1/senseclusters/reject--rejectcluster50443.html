reject

verb
refuse entrance or membership
1.6 1.7
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈdʒekt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈdʒekt/ (BrE)
to refuse to accept, allow, believe, or approve something or someone

Examples

To reject an offer
Applicants without proper identification will be rejected at the door.
Several universities rejected her because she did not meet the entrance requirements.
The club decided to reject his application for membership due to lack of experience.
I had to reject his proposal because it went against our values.
She rejected the idea as being too risky and impractical.
The committee decided to reject the policy on ethical grounds.
Many scientists reject the notion that the theory has been disproven.
She chose to reject the claim, saying there was no supporting evidence.
The committee decided to reject his explanation as unconvincing.
