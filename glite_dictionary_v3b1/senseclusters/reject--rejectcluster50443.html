Synonyms

  • refuse
    1.7 70/m
    refuse means to say no or show you will not accept something, while reject covers not accepting or approving broadly.
  • turn down
    2.9 11/m
    turn down means to say no to an offer or request, while reject can refuse or not accept people or things.
  • decline
    3.4 16/m
    decline means politely refuse an offer or invitation, while reject is a general refusal that can be blunt or formal.
  • deny
    1.6 23/m
    deny means to say something is not true or refuse to give something, while reject means refuse to accept or approve.
  • disallow
    3.9 1.52/m
    disallow means officially forbid or refuse permission, while reject is to refuse or not accept in many informal or formal situations.
  • veto
    4.0 5.93/m
    veto means to officially stop or refuse a law or decision, while reject is a general refusal without formal power.
  • dismiss
    3.0 25/m
    dismiss means decide something is not worth attention, while reject means refuse to accept, allow, or approve it.
  • shoot down
    3.8 1.35/m
    shoot down means to reject an idea quickly and sharply, while reject can be used more generally and calmly sometimes.
  • repudiate
    8.2 3.51/m
    repudiate means to refuse connection with someone or idea in a formal public way, while reject is a general refusal.
  • rule out
    3.5 11/m
    rule out means decide something is not possible or suitable, while reject is a broader refusal to accept or allow options.
  • eliminate
    3.4 22/m
    eliminate means remove someone or something from a contest or choice, while reject means refuse to accept or approve them.
  • winnow out
    7.1 0.06/m
    winnow out means remove items from a group during selection, while reject is a direct refusal to accept or allow something.
  • turn away
    2.8 4.22/m
    turn away means refuse someone entry or membership, while reject can refuse acceptance in many other contexts beyond entry.
