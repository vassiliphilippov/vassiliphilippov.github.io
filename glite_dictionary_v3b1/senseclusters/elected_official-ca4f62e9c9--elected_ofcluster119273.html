elected official

noun
person holding public office after election
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktɪd əˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktɪd əˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
A person who holds a public office as a result of winning an election.

Examples

An elected official serves
Every elected official is expected to act in the public's best interest.
He became an elected official after winning the city council race.
The elected official addressed the concerns of her constituents at the town hall meeting.
