Saint Titus

proper noun
Greek disciple and companion of Saint Paul
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈtaɪtəs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈtaɪtəs / (BrE)
A Greek disciple and missionary companion of Saint Paul in the early Christian Church.

Examples

Saint Titus the disciple
According to the New Testament, Saint Titus played a key role in spreading Christianity in the Mediterranean.
In Crete, Saint Titus is honored as the island's first bishop and patron saint.
The Feast of Saint Titus is celebrated on January 26th by many Christian denominations.
