turn the page

verb
to move on from something or start anew
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /tɜːrn ðə peɪdʒ/ (AmE), /tɜːn ðə peɪdʒ/ (BrE)
to move on to the next stage of something, either literally (such as reading a book) or figuratively (to leave a past event or experience behind and begin anew)

Examples

To turn the page quickly
After the scandal, the company worked hard to turn the page and restore its reputation.
He realized he needed to turn the page and focus on the future instead of the past.
When you are ready, simply turn the page to start the next chapter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: let go - to stop worrying about or being upset by something
  • synonym: move on - to progress to a new situation or activity
