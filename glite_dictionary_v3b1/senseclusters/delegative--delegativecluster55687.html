delegative

adjective
assigning responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛlɪɡeɪtɪv /; / ˈdɛlɪɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪɡətɪv / (BrE)
involving or characterized by giving tasks, responsibilities, or authority to others

Examples

Delegative style
The manager's delegative style encourages employees to take initiative and solve problems independently.
A delegative leader trusts the team to make important decisions without constant supervision.
In a delegative environment, team members are expected to manage their own projects.
The president adopted a delegative approach, making decisions without much input from parliament.
In a delegative democracy, leaders may act with broad authority granted by voters.
Critics argue that a delegative system can undermine accountability and weaken institutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • antonym: authoritative - controlling and making decisions without delegating
  • antonym: democratic - relating to a system where power is exercised by the people or their elected representatives
  • synonym: participative - involving others in decision-making
