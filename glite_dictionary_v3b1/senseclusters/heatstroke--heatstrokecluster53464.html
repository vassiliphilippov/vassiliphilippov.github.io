heatstroke

noun
serious illness from excessive heat exposure
Pronunciation: / ˈhitˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈhiːtstrəʊk / (BrE)
A serious condition caused by prolonged exposure to high temperatures, resulting in failure of the body's temperature-regulating mechanisms.

Examples

Severe heatstroke symptoms
High humidity increases the risk of heatstroke during physical activity.
The marathon runner collapsed from heatstroke after several hours in the sun.
Victims of heatstroke require immediate cooling and medical attention.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: heat exhaustion - a milder form of heat-related illness
