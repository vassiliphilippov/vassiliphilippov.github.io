heatstroke

noun
serious illness from excessive heat exposure
4.0 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàhitÀåstro äk / (AmE), / ÀàhiÀêtstr…ô äk / (BrE)
A serious condition caused by prolonged exposure to high temperatures, resulting in failure of the body's temperature-regulating mechanisms.

Examples

Severe heatstroke symptoms
High humidity increases the risk of heatstroke during physical activity.
The marathon runner collapsed from heatstroke after several hours in the sun.
Victims of heatstroke require immediate cooling and medical attention.

Synonyms

  • heat hyperpyrexia
    9.9 0.00/m
    heat hyperpyrexia is a strict medical term for severe collapse from extreme heat, while heatstroke names the body's failure to cool itself.
  • sunstroke
    5.8 0.21/m
    sunstroke is commonly used for illness after direct sun and heat, while heatstroke emphasizes body temperature control failure.
  • thermic fever
    8.8 0.00/m
    thermic fever describes sudden exhaustion or collapse from extreme heat, while heatstroke specifically means the body's temperature control fails.

  • degree lower: heat exhaustion - a milder form of heat-related illness
