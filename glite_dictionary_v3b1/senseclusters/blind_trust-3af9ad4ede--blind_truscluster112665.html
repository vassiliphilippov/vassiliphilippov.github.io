blind trust

noun
financial arrangement to avoid conflicts of interest
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd trʌst / (AmE), / blaɪnd trʌst / (BrE)
A financial arrangement in which a person transfers assets to a trustee or fiduciary, giving up the right to manage or receive information about those assets, typically to avoid conflicts of interest.

Examples

A blind trust fund
Setting up a blind trust is a common practice for politicians to maintain public trust.
She placed her investments into a blind trust before taking public office to avoid a conflict of interest.
The company CEO managed his assets through a blind trust to ensure unbiased decision-making.
