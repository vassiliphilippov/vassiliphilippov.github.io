rummage

noun
thorough search, often disorderly
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thorough search for something, especially one that causes disorder or confusion

Examples

A rummage for keys
A rummage of the bookshelf turned up an interesting novel.
After a quick rummage through her bag, she found her keys.
He had a rummage in the garage to look for old photographs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: search - an act of looking carefully for something
