double reed

noun
woodwind instrument or reed with two joined reeds
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˌrid /; / ˈdʌb.əl ˈrid / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l ˌriːd /; / ˈdʌb.əl ˈriːd / (BrE)
a pair of reeds joined together that vibrate to produce sound in certain woodwind instruments, such as the oboe and bassoon

Examples

A double reed instrument
He learned how to make a double reed for his bassoon.
Musicians often debate the subtle differences in sound produced by each double reed.
The oboe is a type of double reed instrument found in orchestras.
Making a double reed by hand requires patience and skill.
The oboe is a double reed instrument with a distinct, penetrating sound.
Unlike the clarinet, the bassoon uses a double reed to produce its tone.
