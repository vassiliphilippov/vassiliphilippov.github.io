diamide

noun
compound with two amides
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that contains two amide groups in its structure

A diamide compound

A diamide can be identified by the presence of two amide groups in its molecular structure.
