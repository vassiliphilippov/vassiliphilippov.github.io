Anglish

noun
English without foreign words
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
a form of English, or the movement promoting it, that uses only words of Germanic origin and avoids words from Latin, French, or other sources

Examples

The Anglish movement
Some writers experiment with Anglish to see how English sounds without foreign words.
The Anglish movement encourages people to use only native English words in their writing.
He translated the article into Anglish, replacing all Latin-based words with Germanic ones.
The Anglish movement seeks to replace foreign words in English with native alternatives.
Some writers experiment with Anglish by creating stories using only Germanic-rooted words.
He joined an online Anglish forum to learn more about the history of English vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: English - the language spoken in England and many other countries
  • antonym: loanword - a word adopted from another language
  • synonym: purism - the practice of keeping a language free from foreign influences
