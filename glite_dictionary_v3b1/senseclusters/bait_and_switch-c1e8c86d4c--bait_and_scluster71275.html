bait and switch

noun
deceptive sales practice
Pronunciation: / beɪt ənd swɪtʃ / (AmE), / beɪt ənd swɪtʃ / (BrE)
A method of sales in which customers are enticed by an advertised low-priced item but are then persuaded to purchase a more expensive one.

Examples

A bait and switch tactic
Consumer protection laws prohibit bait and switch practices in most countries.
She realized the company had used a bait and switch strategy when they offered her a different, pricier service.
The store was accused of bait and switch tactics after advertising laptops that were never in stock.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • degree lower: upselling - the practice of persuading a customer to buy a more expensive item or add-ons
