deal someone a bad hand

verb
to place someone in a tough situation
Pronunciation: / diːl ˈsʌmwʌn ə bæd hænd / (AmE), / diːl ˈsʌmwʌn ə bæd hænd / (BrE)
to put someone in a difficult or unfortunate situation through circumstances beyond their control

Examples

To deal someone a bad hand
After losing her job and her apartment, Anna felt life had dealt her a bad hand.
He tried to stay positive, even though fate had dealt him a bad hand.
Sometimes children are dealt a bad hand and must overcome great obstacles.
