backwashing

noun
water moving seaward
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbækwɑːʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækwɒʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the movement of water returning toward the sea or away from the shore after a wave breaks or a boat passes

Examples

Wave backwashing
The backwashing after the wave pulled shells and sand away from the shore.
Swimmers should be cautious of strong backwashing that can make it difficult to return to the beach.
The boat's passage created significant backwashing that disturbed the shallow water near the dock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: undertow - a current of water below the surface moving back out to sea
  • synonym: wash - the movement of water caused by a boat or wave
