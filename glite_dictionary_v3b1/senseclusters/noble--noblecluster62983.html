noble

adjective
having high moral qualities
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
having or showing high moral qualities or elevated character

Examples

Noble intentions
He was admired for his noble intentions, even when his plans failed.
Her noble efforts to help the homeless inspired many others in the community.
It takes a noble spirit to forgive those who have wronged you.

Related Terms

  • antonym: base - lacking moral principles; ignoble
  • synonym: honorable - deserving of honor and respect
