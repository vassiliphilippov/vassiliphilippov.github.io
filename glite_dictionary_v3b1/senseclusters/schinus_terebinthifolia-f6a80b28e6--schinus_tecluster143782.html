Schinus terebinthifolia

noun
Brazilian pepper tree; small resinous tree or shrub
Pronunciation: / ˈskiːnəs ˌtɛrəˌbɪnθɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈskiːnəs ˌtɛrɪˌbɪnθɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A small resinous tree or shrub native to Brazil, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, valued for its ornamental use and sometimes invasive behavior.

Examples

Schinus terebinthifolia tree
Gardeners should be cautious when planting Schinus terebinthifolia due to its rapid spread.
The berries of Schinus terebinthifolia are sometimes used as a pink pepper substitute.
The invasive Schinus terebinthifolia has displaced many native species in Florida.
