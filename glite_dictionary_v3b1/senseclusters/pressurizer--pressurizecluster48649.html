pressurizer

noun
pressure control device
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃəˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a device that maintains or increases the pressure within a system, especially in industrial or technical settings

Examples

Nuclear pressurizer
The pressurizer in the power plant ensures the system operates safely under high pressure.
Engineers checked the pressurizer to make sure it was functioning correctly before starting the test.
A faulty pressurizer can lead to dangerous drops in system pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressor - a machine that increases the pressure of a gas
  • antonym: depressurizer - a device that reduces pressure in a system
