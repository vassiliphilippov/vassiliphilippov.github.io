untied

adjective
not bound; released from restraints
1.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪd / (BrE)
not held by shackles, chains, or other bindings; released from physical restraint.

Examples

Untied hands
Once untied, she stretched her arms to restore circulation to her wrists.
The horse was untied and allowed to wander freely in the field.
The prisoner remained untied but closely watched by the guards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tied - held or fastened with a cord, rope, etc.; bound
