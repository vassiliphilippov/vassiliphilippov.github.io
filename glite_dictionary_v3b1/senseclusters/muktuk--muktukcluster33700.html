muktuk

noun
whale skin and blubber
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌk.tʌk / (AmE), / ˈmʌk.tʌk / (BrE)
the skin and blubber of a whale, traditionally eaten as food by Arctic peoples

Examples

Pieces of muktuk
During the festival, locals shared pieces of muktuk with visitors to introduce them to traditional foods.
Scientists studied the nutritional value of muktuk in the diets of Arctic communities.
She was surprised by the chewy texture of muktuk when she tasted it for the first time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blubber - the thick layer of fat under the skin of marine mammals
