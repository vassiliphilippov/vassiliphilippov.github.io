heighten

verb
become or make more intense
4.4 4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪtən /; / ˈhaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪtn̩ /; / ˈhaɪtən /; / ˈhaɪt(ə)n / (BrE)
to make something (such as a feeling, effect, or situation) more intense, stronger, or more noticeable

Examples

To heighten tension
As the storm approached, the sense of urgency heightened among the residents.
His excitement heightened when he heard he had been accepted into the program.
The suspense in the film heightened as the plot unfolded.
Bad weather will only heighten our worries about the trip.
Strong colors can heighten the impact of a painting.
The discovery served to heighten public interest in the case.
Tensions between the two countries have heightened in recent weeks.
The artist used lighting to heighten the dramatic effect of the painting.
The tragic news only heightened her anxiety about the future.
