get through

verb
spend or pass time, often with effort
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ˈθru / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈθruː / (BrE)
to spend or pass (a period of time), especially with difficulty, boredom, or by some means

Examples

To get through the day
During the long winter evenings, I read books to get through the dark hours.
He barely got through the meeting without falling asleep.
She listened to music at work to get through the boring afternoon shift.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pass the time - to occupy oneself during a period of waiting or boredom
