appeasingly

adverb
in a calming way
6.7
Pronunciation: / əˈpizɪŋli / (AmE), / əˈpiːzɪŋli / (BrE)
in a manner intended to calm, pacify, or please someone to avoid conflict or anger

Examples

To speak appeasingly
He spoke appeasingly to his angry friend, hoping to calm the situation.
The manager smiled appeasingly at the upset customer to prevent an argument.
She nodded appeasingly during the meeting to show she agreed with the suggestions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggressively - in a forceful or confrontational way
  • synonym: soothingly - in a way that calms or comforts
