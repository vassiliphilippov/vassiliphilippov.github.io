(British) Prepared food that is purchased to be eaten elsewhere, not on the premises.

Examples

Order a takeaway
In the UK, there are many Indian and Chinese takeaways on every high street.
She ordered her favorite pizza as a takeaway after a long day at work.
We picked up a takeaway for dinner because neither of us wanted to cook.

Synonyms

  • take-away
    2.3 0.03/m
    take-away is the hyphenated form often seen in writing, while takeaway is the standard unhyphenated British noun used in speech.
  • takeout
    1.5 1.97/m
    takeout is the American word for food to eat elsewhere, while takeaway is the British term for the same thing.
  • carryout
    2.7 0.15/m
    carryout is also used for food or for a shop that sells it, while takeaway refers only to the food taken away.
