modulus

noun
integer dividing a difference exactly
9.1 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ëÀêd.j ä.l…ôs / (AmE), / Ààm…íd.j ä.l…ôs / (BrE)
an integer that divides exactly into the difference between two other integers, with no remainder

Examples

A modulus divides numbers
If a modulus divides evenly into the difference, the numbers are said to be congruent modulo that modulus.
In this case, 6 acts as a modulus for the difference between 12 and 18.
The number 3 is a modulus of 7 and 10, since 10 minus 7 is 3.

  • synonym: divisor - a number by which another number is divided
