cabaletta

noun
fast operatic section
9.9
Pronunciation: /kæ.bə.ˈlet.ə/ (AmE), /kæ.bə.ˈlet.ə/ (BrE)
a fast, lively concluding section of an operatic aria, often following a slower part and showcasing the singer's skill

Examples

The cabaletta section
The soprano's cabaletta thrilled the audience with its energy and speed.
After the slow introduction, the tenor launched into a brilliant cabaletta.
Many operas from the 19th century feature a dramatic cabaletta to end an aria.
The soprano ended her performance with a brilliant cabaletta that brought the audience to their feet.
In many operas, the cabaletta provides a dramatic and energetic conclusion to a character's aria.
The composer's use of a lively cabaletta made the final scene especially memorable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recitative - a style of delivery in opera that imitates speech
