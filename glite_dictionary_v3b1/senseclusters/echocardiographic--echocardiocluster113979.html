echocardiographic

adjective
ultrasound heart imaging
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkoʊˌkɑːrdiəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛkəʊˌkɑːdiəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or produced by ultrasound imaging of the heart (echocardiogram)

Examples

Echocardiographic heart image
The echocardiographic report showed normal heart function after the procedure.
Doctors rely on echocardiographic images to diagnose many heart conditions.
An echocardiographic examination is painless and provides valuable information about heart structure.
The doctor reviewed the echocardiographic images to assess the patient's heart function.
An echocardiographic study revealed abnormalities in the heart's structure.
Accurate diagnosis often depends on high-quality echocardiographic data.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electrocardiographic - relating to the recording of the heart's electrical activity
  • synonym: ultrasonographic - relating to the use of ultrasound for imaging
