arbitrate

verb
settle a dispute as a neutral party
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to act as a neutral third party in order to settle a dispute between others

Examples

To arbitrate a dispute
An independent officer was called in to arbitrate the dispute.
If the two companies fail to agree, a judge may be appointed to arbitrate.
They asked her to arbitrate because she was impartial and respected by both sides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antagonize - to make someone feel hostile instead of resolving a dispute
  • synonym: conciliate - to make peace between arguing groups or people
  • synonym: mediate - to try to end a dispute by acting as an intermediary
