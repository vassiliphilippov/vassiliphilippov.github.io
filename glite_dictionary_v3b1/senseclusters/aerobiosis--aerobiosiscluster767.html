aerobiosis

noun
life sustained in the presence of oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˌɛr.oʊ.baɪˈoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌeə.rəʊ.baɪˈəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
life processes that occur in the presence of air or oxygen, especially by organisms requiring oxygen to live

Examples

Aerobiosis in organisms
Fungi capable of both aerobiosis and anaerobiosis are called facultative anaerobes.
Many bacteria survive through aerobiosis and require oxygen for metabolism.
The process of aerobiosis is essential for animals and plants that rely on oxygen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anaerobiosis - life sustained in the absence of oxygen
