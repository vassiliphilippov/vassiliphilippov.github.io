compost pile

noun
heap of organic material for composting
7.7 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.poʊst paɪl/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.pɒst paɪl/ /; / /ˈkɒm.pəʊst paɪl/ (BrE)
a heap of decaying or decomposing organic material, such as plant matter, food scraps, or manure, used to make compost for fertilizing soil

Examples

A compost pile outdoors
A well-maintained compost pile can significantly reduce household waste.
Kitchen scraps and yard waste were added to the compost pile in the backyard.
The gardener turned the compost pile regularly to help it decompose more quickly.
A well-maintained compost pile produces rich soil for the garden.
She turned the compost pile to aerate it and speed up decomposition.
The compost pile began to steam on the cold morning.
