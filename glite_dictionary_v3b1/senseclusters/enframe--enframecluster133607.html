enframe

verb
to compose within frame
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈfreɪm /; / ɪnˈfreɪm / (AmE), / ɛnˈfreɪm /; / ɪnˈfreɪm / (BrE)
to put something in a frame, surround it with a border, or arrange it so it is enclosed or highlighted (literally or visually)

Examples

To enframe a picture
The director decided to enframe the couple with the city skyline in the background.
Photographers often enframe their subjects using natural elements like trees or windows.
She skillfully enframed the mountain peak between two tall buildings in her shot.
The artist chose to enframe her painting with a gold border to highlight its colors.
They decided to enframe the certificate before hanging it on the wall.
A row of tall trees enframed the garden, creating a sense of privacy.
Tall trees enframe the entrance to the old mansion, creating a dramatic effect.
The window perfectly enframed the view of the mountains in the distance.
A row of flowers enframed the garden path, making it look like a painting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encircle - to form a circle around; to surround
  • synonym: enclose - to surround or close off on all sides
  • antonym: exclude - to leave out or not include in a picture or scene
  • antonym: expose - to leave something uncovered or unprotected
  • synonym: frame - to position or compose within a border or boundary
  • antonym: remove - to take something away or out
