gun jumping

noun
illegal pre‑clearance conduct in mergers
Pronunciation: / ɡʊn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡʊn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
(in competition/antitrust law) illegal conduct by parties to a merger or acquisition that involves prohibited coordination or implementation of the transaction before receiving required regulatory clearance, for example sharing competitively sensitive information, integrating operations, or transferring control.

Examples

[law] Gun jumping in mergers
Regulators fined the company for gun jumping after finding they shared strategic pricing data before clearance.
The merger stalled when officials investigated alleged gun jumping between the buyer and the target's managers.
Their legal team advised strict controls to prevent gun jumping during sensitive pre-approval discussions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antitrust violation - an act that breaches competition law, such as unlawful coordination or market‑sharing
  • synonym: jump the gun - to begin something before the proper or agreed time
