fiddlehead fern

noun
young edible coiled fern frond
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪdəlˌhɛd fɝn / (AmE), / ˈfɪdəlˌhɛd fɜːn / (BrE)
A fern with young, tightly coiled fronds, particularly those of certain species found in North America, which are edible when harvested in early spring.

Examples

Fresh fiddlehead ferns
In the spring, many chefs add fiddlehead ferns to their seasonal menus for a fresh, earthy flavor.
She picked a basketful of fiddlehead ferns during her hike in the woods.
The recipe calls for sautéed fiddlehead ferns as a unique vegetable side dish.
