hear out

verb
listen to someone fully
1.6 3.2
Pronunciation: / hɪr aʊt / (AmE), / hɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to listen to someone until they have finished saying everything they want to say

Examples

To hear out complaints
He patiently heard out all the complaints before responding.
Please hear out my explanation before you make a decision.
She insisted that everyone hear out her side of the story.
Please hear out my proposal before making a decision.
She always makes time to hear out her employees' concerns.
We should hear out both sides of the argument before we judge.
