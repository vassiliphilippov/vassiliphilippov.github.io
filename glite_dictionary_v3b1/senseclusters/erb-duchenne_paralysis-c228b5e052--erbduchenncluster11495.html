Erb-Duchenne paralysis

noun
paralysis of the arm from brachial plexus injury
9.2
Pronunciation: / ɜrb duːˈʃɛn pəˈræləsɪs / (AmE), / ɜːb djuːˈʃɛn pəˈralɪsɪs / (BrE)
A form of paralysis of the arm caused by injury to the brachial plexus, often occurring during childbirth.

Examples

Erb-Duchenne paralysis of arm
Early intervention can greatly help in the recovery of those suffering from Erb-Duchenne paralysis.
Physical therapy is often recommended for children with Erb-Duchenne paralysis to improve mobility.
The infant was diagnosed with Erb-Duchenne paralysis after a difficult delivery.
