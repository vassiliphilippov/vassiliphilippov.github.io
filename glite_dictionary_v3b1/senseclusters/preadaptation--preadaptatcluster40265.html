preadaptation

noun
trait with new function
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌædæpˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌædæpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a trait that originally evolved for one function but later serves a different purpose

Examples

A preadaptation trait
The presence of lungs in some fish is seen as a preadaptation for life on land.
Scientists debate whether feathers were a preadaptation for flight or for temperature regulation.
Opposable thumbs are often cited as a preadaptation that enabled tool use in humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adaptation - a trait that has evolved to suit a particular function
  • synonym: exaptation - a trait that acquires a function for which it was not originally adapted
