remember

verb
show appreciation, often by a gift or bequest
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɛmbɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
to honor, commemorate, or show appreciation for someone or something, especially in a ceremony or by giving a gift

Examples

To remember someone kindly
He generously remembered his old friends in his will.
She made sure to remember her former teacher by sending a thank-you gift.
The company remembered loyal employees with a holiday bonus.
A service was held to remember the soldiers who fell in battle.
Every year, citizens remember those who lost their lives in the disaster.
Many families light candles to remember their ancestors during the festival.
