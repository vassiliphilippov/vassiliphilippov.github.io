discreditable

adjective
deserving discredit; blameworthy
5.5
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkrɛdətəbəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛdɪtəbəl / (BrE)
deserving to bring harm to one's reputation; blameworthy or dishonorable.

Examples

A discreditable act
Despite the discreditable rumors, she continued to work with integrity.
His involvement in the scandal was highly discreditable and damaged his career.
The company tried to cover up several discreditable actions by its executives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creditable - deserving public acknowledgement or praise but not necessarily outstanding
