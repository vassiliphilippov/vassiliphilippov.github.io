satisfise

verb
pursue a minimally satisfactory course of action
Pronunciation: / ˈsætɪsaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsætɪsaɪs / (BrE)
to decide on and pursue a course of action that satisfies the minimum requirements to achieve a goal, rather than seeking the optimal solution

Examples

To satisfise a goal
In situations with limited information, people often satisfise instead of searching for the perfect answer.
Rather than spend hours comparing options, he chose to satisfise and bought a suitable laptop quickly.
She decided to satisfise by choosing the first apartment that met her basic needs.
