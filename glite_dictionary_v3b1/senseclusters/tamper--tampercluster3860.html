tamper

verb
interfere in others' affairs
3.9 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtæm.pər/ (AmE), /ˈtæm.pə/ (BrE)
to interfere with something, especially in a way that is harmful, unwanted, or dishonest

Examples

To tamper with evidence
He was warned not to tamper with the company's financial records.
It's wrong to tamper in other people's family affairs.
You shouldn't tamper in their personal matters without being asked.
Please don't tamper with the settings on my computer without asking me first.
Someone had clearly tampered with the evidence, making it unreliable in court.
The company was fined after it was discovered they had tampered with the safety data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alter - to change something
  • synonym: fiddle - to touch or manipulate something, often restlessly or dishonestly
  • synonym: interfere - to become involved in something that is not your concern, often with a negative effect
  • synonym: meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • antonym: restore - to return something to a former condition
