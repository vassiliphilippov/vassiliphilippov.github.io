overstress

verb
to apply excessive stress
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstrɛs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstrɛs / (BrE)
to put too much mental or physical strain on oneself or something

Examples

To overstress the body
You should not overstress your muscles by exercising too much in one day.
It is easy to overstress about exams, but try to stay calm.
The bridge collapsed because engineers overstressed its main support beam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overburden - to give someone or something too much to carry or deal with
  • antonym: relax - to become less tense or anxious
