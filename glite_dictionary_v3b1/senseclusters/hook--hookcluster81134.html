hook

verb
entice and trap; attract strongly
6.7
Pronunciation: / hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
to attract someone and make them become involved or interested, sometimes by trickery or enticement

Examples

To hook new customers
She tried to hook him with the promise of exclusive information.
The author hooked readers with an exciting opening chapter.
The catchy jingle was designed to hook customers from the very first moment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entice - to attract by offering pleasure or advantage
  • antonym: repel - to drive or force back, discourage involvement
  • synonym: snare - to catch or trap, especially by trickery
