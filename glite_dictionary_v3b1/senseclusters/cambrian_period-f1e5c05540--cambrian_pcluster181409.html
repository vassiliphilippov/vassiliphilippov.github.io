Cambrian period

noun
earliest period of the Paleozoic Era
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæm.bri.ən ˈpɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˈkæm.brɪ.ən ˈpɪə.ri.əd / (BrE)
the first period of the Paleozoic Era, lasting from about 544 to 500 million years ago and marked by the emergence of most major groups of marine invertebrates

Examples

The Cambrian period
Fossil evidence from the Cambrian period reveals the rapid diversification of life forms.
Geologists have discovered rocks that date back to the Cambrian period.
Many marine invertebrates first appeared during the Cambrian period.
