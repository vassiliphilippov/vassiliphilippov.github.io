epigrapher

noun
inscription specialist
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɪɡrəfər / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who studies or interprets inscriptions, especially ancient ones carved or engraved on stone, metal, or other durable materials

Examples

The epigrapher studies inscriptions
The epigrapher spent years deciphering the mysterious symbols on the temple walls.
At the museum, an epigrapher explained the meaning of the ancient Latin inscription.
Becoming an epigrapher requires knowledge of old languages and careful attention to detail.
The ancient tablet was carefully examined by an experienced epigrapher for clues about its origin.
As an epigrapher, she traveled to archaeological sites to document and interpret old inscriptions.
The museum hired a skilled epigrapher to authenticate the newly discovered stone carvings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaeologist - a person who studies human history through excavation and artifacts
  • synonym: epigraphist - a person who studies inscriptions
  • synonym: inscriber - a person who writes or engraves words on a surface
  • synonym: paleographer - a person who studies ancient handwriting and scripts
