Quercus laevis

noun
species of oak tree native to southeastern U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs leɪˈvɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs liːˈvɪs / (BrE)
A small, slow-growing deciduous tree native to dry, sandy barrens of the southeastern United States, with leaves that have bristle-tipped lobes resembling a turkey's toes.

Examples

Quercus laevis tree
Botanists often study Quercus laevis for its adaptation to nutrient-poor soils.
The dry, sandy slopes in the region are home to the Quercus laevis.
The leaves of the Quercus laevis are easily recognized by their bristle-tipped lobes.
