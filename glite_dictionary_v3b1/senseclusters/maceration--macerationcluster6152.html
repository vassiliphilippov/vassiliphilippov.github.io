maceration

noun
softening by soaking
5.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /meɪ.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mə.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /meɪ.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mə.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process of softening or breaking up a substance by soaking or steeping in a liquid.

Examples

Maceration of fruit
After several hours of maceration, the herbs released their full flavor into the oil.
Excessive water can lead to the maceration of delicate plant roots.
The maceration of the fruit in sugar created a delicious syrup.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dehydration - the process of removing moisture or water
  • synonym: infusion - the process of steeping something in liquid to extract flavor or healing properties
