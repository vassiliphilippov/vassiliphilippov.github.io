crinkle

verb
form small wrinkles or creases
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to make or become wrinkled or creased, forming small lines or folds on a surface

Examples

To crinkle paper
Don’t crinkle the paper; I need it smooth for the presentation.
He watched the old man’s eyes crinkle as he smiled.
She likes to crinkle the edges of her drawings for effect.
Her eyes crinkled at the corners when she smiled.
If you wash this shirt in hot water, it may crinkle.
The paper crinkled noisily as he folded it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth - to make even or remove wrinkles
  • synonym: wrinkle - to make or become creased or folded
