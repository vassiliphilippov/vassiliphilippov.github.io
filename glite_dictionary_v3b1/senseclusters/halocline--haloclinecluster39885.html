halocline

noun
salt gradient layer
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈheɪ.loʊ.klaɪn/ /; / /ˈheɪ.lə.klaɪn/ (AmE), /ˈheɪ.ləʊ.klaɪn/ /; / /ˈheɪ.lə.klaɪn/ (BrE)
a layer in a body of water where the salt concentration changes sharply with depth

Examples

A halocline layer
The diver could see a shimmering halocline where the fresh and salt water met in the cave.
Scientists measured the thickness of the halocline to study how saltwater and freshwater mix.
A strong halocline can affect the movement of marine life between different water layers.
