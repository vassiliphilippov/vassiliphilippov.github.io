merciful

adjective
showing mercy or compassion
3.3 2.65/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ùÀês…™f…ôl / (AmE), / Ààm…úÀês…™f…ôl / (BrE)
showing kindness and forgiveness, especially toward someone who is suffering or has done wrong; compassionate and willing to spare others from harm or punishment.

Examples

A merciful act
It was a merciful decision to end the animal's suffering quickly.
She prayed for a merciful outcome to the difficult situation.
The judge was merciful and gave the young man a second chance.

Synonyms

  • forgiving
    3.1 4.06/m
    forgiving forgives past wrongs and lets people move on, while merciful shows kindness and may spare others from punishment.
  • compassionate
    3.7 8.78/m
    compassionate feels deep sympathy and wants to reduce suffering, while merciful also forgives and may spare people from punishment.
  • humane
    3.4 6.30/m
    humane treats people and animals with care to reduce suffering, while merciful focuses on kindness and sparing punishment.
  • clement
    9.1 0.01/m
    clement is a formal, lenient word for showing mercy, while merciful is more common and describes forgiving, kind behaviour.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cruel - willingly causing pain or suffering
