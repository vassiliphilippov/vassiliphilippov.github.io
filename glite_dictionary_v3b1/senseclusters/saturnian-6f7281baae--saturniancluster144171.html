Saturnian

adjective
of Saturn; stern or austere
Pronunciation: / səˈtɝːniən / (AmE), / səˈtɜːniən / (BrE)
relating to Saturn, the Roman god of agriculture and time, or to qualities such as sternness, austerity, or melancholy traditionally associated with him

Examples

Saturnian mood
The ceremony had a Saturnian mood, marked by silence and solemnity.
His Saturnian outlook made him seem distant and serious to his friends.
The old building's Saturnian architecture gave it a sense of ancient dignity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: austere - severe or strict in manner or appearance
  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or gloom
