executive clemency

noun
power to pardon or reduce sentences
4.4 7.9
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv ˈklɛmənsi / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv ˈklɛmənsi / (BrE)
the power held by a president, governor, or other executive official to pardon or reduce the sentence of someone convicted of a crime in their jurisdiction

Examples

Executive clemency power
Activists petitioned the president for executive clemency on behalf of the wrongly convicted individual.
The governor granted executive clemency to several prisoners serving long sentences.
The use of executive clemency is often controversial in high-profile criminal cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commutation - the reduction of a legal penalty to a less severe one
  • synonym: pardon - the official forgiveness of a crime and its penalties
