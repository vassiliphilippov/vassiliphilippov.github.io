microenvironment

noun
cellular surroundings
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊɪnˈvaɪrənmənt / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊɪnˈvaɪrənmənt / (BrE)
the immediate surroundings of a cell or group of cells, including other cells, substances, and signals that affect their behavior

Examples

Cell microenvironment
The microenvironment of a tumor can affect how cancer cells grow and spread.
Researchers study the microenvironment to understand how cells interact with their surroundings.
Changes in the microenvironment may influence the effectiveness of certain medical treatments.
