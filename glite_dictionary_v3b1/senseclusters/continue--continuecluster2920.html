continue

verb
resume after interruption
1.0 88/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàt…™n.ju / (AmE), / k…ônÀàt…™n.juÀê / (BrE)
to start again or resume after a pause or interruption

Examples

To continue after a break
After the rain stopped, the players continued the match.
She paused to answer the phone and then continued cooking dinner.
Work continued on the bridge after a short delay.
Despite the interruption, she continued speaking as if nothing had happened.
He continued, 'Let me clarify what I meant by that statement.'
She paused to collect her thoughts, then continued with her explanation.

Synonyms

  • resume
    3.1 17/m
    resume means to start something again after a pause, while continue covers restarting or moving forward in many situations.
  • recommence
    5.6 0.20/m
    recommence means to begin again in formal or written settings, while continue is the common verb used in everyday speech.
  • restart
    2.3 4.53/m
    restart means to start over a machine or process after stopping, while continue focuses on resuming an action or process.
  • carry on with
    2.6 0.98/m
    carry on with means to keep doing a task after a pause, while continue can also mean to resume or to persist generally.
  • carry on
    2.7 11/m
    carry on means to continue despite problems or distraction, while continue is more neutral about restarting or keeping an action.
  • proceed
    3.0 43/m
    proceed means to move forward or follow a plan, while continue suggests restarting or keeping an activity going.
  • pick up the thread
    4.9 0.12/m
    pick up the thread means to resume a specific discussion or idea after an interruption, while continue is more general.
  • go on
    1.8 67/m
    go on means to continue speaking or tell more after a pause, while continue covers many actions beyond speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cease - to stop doing something
  • synonym: proceed - to go on with a course of action, especially after a pause
  • synonym: resume - to begin again after stopping
  • antonym: stop - to cease from an action, especially speaking
