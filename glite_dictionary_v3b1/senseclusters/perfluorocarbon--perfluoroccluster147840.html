perfluorocarbon

noun
synthetic compound of carbon and fluorine
8.7
Pronunciation: / pɚˌflʊəroʊˈkɑrbən / (AmE), / pɜːˌflʊərəˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
any of a group of synthetic compounds consisting only of carbon and fluorine atoms, some of which are potent greenhouse gases used in industry

Examples

Perfluorocarbon greenhouse gas
Perfluorocarbon emissions are a concern due to their long atmospheric lifetime and strong greenhouse effect.
Scientists are studying the atmospheric impacts of perfluorocarbon gases.
The production of aluminum is a major source of perfluorocarbon emissions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrocarbon - compound consisting entirely of hydrogen and carbon
