skew

verb
to turn or place at an angle
4.8 1.02/m
Pronunciation: / skju / (AmE), / skjuÀê / (BrE)
to turn or place at an angle; to make something not straight or not parallel

Examples

To skew a hat
He skewed his hat to the side for a more casual look.
She tried to skew the picture frame to match the angle of the window.
The data may be skewed if the measurements are not taken properly.

Synonyms

    3.2 3.65/m
    angle is to move or turn something so it sits at a deliberate angle, while skew means make it not straight or parallel.
  • slant
    3.7 3.40/m
    slant is to slope or lean in one direction, while skew means to place or turn something so it is not parallel.
  • cant
    8.0 0.52/m

  • tip
    1.8 10/m
    tip is to tilt or push something from its upright position and maybe overturn it, while skew means to place at an angle.
