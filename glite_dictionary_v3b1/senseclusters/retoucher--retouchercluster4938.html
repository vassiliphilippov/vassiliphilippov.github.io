retoucher

noun
tool for shaping stone
9.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtʌ.tʃər/ /; / /riː.ˈtʌ.tʃər/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtʌ.tʃə/ /; / /riː.ˈtʌ.tʃə/ (BrE)
a small tool, often made of bone, antler, or stone, used to shape or resharpen chipped stone tools in archaeology

Examples

Stone retoucher
The archaeologist identified a retoucher among the ancient tools at the excavation site.
A retoucher made from deer antler was used to refine the edges of flint blades.
Researchers studied the wear patterns on the retoucher to understand how it was used.

  • synonym: pressure flaker - a tool used to shape stone tools by applying pressure
