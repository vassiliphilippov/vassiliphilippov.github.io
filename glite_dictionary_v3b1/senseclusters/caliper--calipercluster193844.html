caliper

noun
instrument for measuring distances
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləpər /; / ˈkælɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪpə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring the distance between two opposite sides of an object, often with precise accuracy

Examples

A caliper measurement
Before ordering the replacement part, she measured the bolt with a caliper.
Digital calipers provide highly accurate measurements for engineering tasks.
The mechanic used a caliper to check the diameter of the piston.
Digital callipers are now commonly used in laboratories for precise measurements.
He checked the diameter of the pipe with his calliper before starting the repair.
The engineer used a pair of callipers to measure the thickness of the metal sheet.
