vena intercapitalis

noun
connecting vein in the hand or foot
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪnə ɪnˌtɜrˌkæpɪˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə ɪnˌtɜːkæpɪˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
a vein that connects the dorsal and palmar veins of the hand or the dorsal and plantar veins of the foot

Examples

Vena intercapitalis vein
Damage to the vena intercapitalis can affect blood flow between the dorsal and palmar veins.
Detailed anatomical diagrams illustrate the location of the vena intercapitalis in both the hand and the foot.
The surgeon carefully avoided the vena intercapitalis during the procedure on the patient's hand.
