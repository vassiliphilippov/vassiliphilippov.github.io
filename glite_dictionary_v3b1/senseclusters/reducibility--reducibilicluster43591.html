reducibility

noun
ability to be transformed
7.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˌduːsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / rɪˌdjuːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or property of something (such as a problem or process) that allows it to be simplified or transformed into a simpler form

Examples

Reducibility of a problem
The reducibility of complex problems is a key concept in computer science.
Mathematicians study the reducibility of equations to find simpler solutions.
Understanding the reducibility between problems helps researchers compare their difficulty.
The reducibility of the problem allows mathematicians to find solutions more easily.
Scientists discussed the reducibility of complex chemical compounds in the experiment.
Understanding the reducibility of these processes is important in computer science.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complexity - the state of being complicated or difficult to understand
  • antonym: irreducibility - the quality of not being able to be reduced or transformed
  • degree higher: simplicity - the quality of being easy to understand or not complicated
  • synonym: transformability - the ability to be changed into a different form
