imagistically

adverb
in an Imagist style
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmædʒɪˈstɪkli / (AmE), / ɪˌmædʒɪˈstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that uses clear, vivid images or creates strong mental pictures

Examples

Describes imagistically with images
The author described the landscape imagistically, painting a clear picture with just a few words.
Her poems are written imagistically, focusing on sharp, concrete images rather than abstract ideas.
Critics praised the novel for its imagistically powerful descriptions of city life.
The author describes the city imagistically, painting a picture with words.
She spoke imagistically about her dreams, making them easy to visualize.
The film unfolds imagistically, relying on strong visual scenes rather than dialogue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstractly - in a way that is not concrete or specific
  • antonym: literally - in a way that uses the exact meaning of words, not imagery
  • synonym: vividly - in a way that produces strong, clear images in the mind
