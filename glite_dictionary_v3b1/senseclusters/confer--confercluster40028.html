confer

verb
discuss or consult with others
4.9 4.32/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàf…ùÀê / (AmE), / k…ônÀàf…úÀê / (BrE)
to discuss something with others or consult, especially in order to make a decision

Examples

To confer with experts
Before making changes, I prefer to confer with my colleagues.
The committee will confer again before announcing its decision.
The doctors conferred privately to discuss the best course of treatment.

Synonyms

  • confer with
    4.5 1.22/m
    confer with is used to discuss something directly with another person or group before deciding, while confer is more formal.
  • talk over
    3.2 2.01/m
    talk over means discussing something carefully with someone before making a choice, while confer sounds more formal and official.
  • discuss
    1.6 177/m
    discuss means talking about details with others to explore ideas, while confer often implies a formal consultation to decide.
  • consult
    3.5 21/m
    consult means asking an expert or source for advice before acting, while confer means jointly discussing matters to decide.
  • confabulate
    8.8 0.01/m
    confabulate is a rare or literary word for chatting or discussing, while confer is a common formal word for consulting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consult - to seek advice or discuss with someone before making a decision
  • synonym: deliberate - to discuss or consider carefully
