allemande

noun
historical dance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈmɑːnd / (AmE), / ˌalɒˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
a lively dance from the 17th or 18th century, often featured in classical music suites

Examples

Baroque allemande
The orchestra performed an allemande as part of the suite.
In her dance class, she learned the steps of the allemande from a Baroque dance manual.
The composer included an allemande as the opening movement of his keyboard suite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minuet - a slow, graceful dance popular in the 17th and 18th centuries
  • synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time
