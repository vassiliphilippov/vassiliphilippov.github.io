uniform

adjective
not varying; consistent; not differentiated
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːnəˌfɔrm /; / ˈjunəˌfɔrm /; / ˈjuːnɪˌfɔrm /; / ˈjuːnɪfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪfɔːm /; / ˈjuːnɪˌfɔːm / (BrE)
not varying; the same in all parts or at all times; showing consistency or sameness (including technical use in computer graphics for something constant)

Examples

Uniform standards
Students are expected to wear uniform clothing during the ceremony.
The paint was applied in a uniform layer across the wall.
The temperature in the room remained uniform throughout the night.
She painted the walls in a uniform shade of blue to create a calm atmosphere.
The bricks must be of uniform size for the wall to be properly constructed.
The temperature in the laboratory was kept uniform throughout the experiment.
The houses on the block are built in a uniform style.
The surface of the cake must be uniform for best results.
The teacher expects uniform standards from all students.

  • synonym: uniform variable - a shader variable declared with the 'uniform' qualifier; holds values provided by the host program

  • antonym: varied - differing from one another; not the same throughout
  • antonym: varying - a shader variable that changes between vertices or fragments and is not constant across invocations
