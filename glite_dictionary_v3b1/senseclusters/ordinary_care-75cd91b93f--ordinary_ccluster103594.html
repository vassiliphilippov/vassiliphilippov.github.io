ordinary care

noun
standard of reasonable care in law
5.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.də.ner.iː keər/ /; / /ˈɔːr.dɪ.nər.iː keər/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.də.nə.riː keə/ /; / /ˈɔː.dɪ.nə.riː keə/ (BrE)
the degree of caution and attention that a reasonable person would use in similar circumstances; the standard for establishing legal responsibility in negligence cases.

Examples

Ordinary care standard
Drivers are expected to use ordinary care to avoid accidents on the road.
Employers have a legal duty to ensure ordinary care is taken to protect their workers.
The court must decide if the defendant failed to exercise ordinary care in maintaining the property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: due care - the effort made by an ordinarily prudent or reasonable party to avoid harm to another party
  • antonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something, leading to damage or injury
