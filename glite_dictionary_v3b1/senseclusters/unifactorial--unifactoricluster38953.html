unifactorial

adjective
single cause
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːniˌfækˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːniˌfækˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
involving or determined by a single factor or cause

Examples

A unifactorial cause
The researchers concluded that the condition was unifactorial in origin.
A unifactorial explanation may be too simple for complex social issues.
Some genetic disorders are unifactorial, caused by a mutation in a single gene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monocausal - having a single cause
  • antonym: multifactorial - involving or determined by many factors or causes
