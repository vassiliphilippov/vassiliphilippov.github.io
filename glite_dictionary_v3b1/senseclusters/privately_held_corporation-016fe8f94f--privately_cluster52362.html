privately held corporation

noun
corporation not publicly traded
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvətli hɛld ˌkɔːrpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvətli hɛld ˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A corporation owned by a small number of shareholders, whose shares are not traded on public stock exchanges.

Examples

A privately held corporation
A privately held corporation does not have to disclose as much financial information as public companies.
After years of steady growth, the privately held corporation is considering going public to raise more capital.
The management team of the privately held corporation meets regularly to discuss long-term business strategies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: publicly traded company - a company whose shares are bought and sold on public stock exchanges
