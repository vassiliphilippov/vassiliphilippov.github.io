valued

adjective
having a specified kind or amount of value (combining form)
Pronunciation: / ˈvæl.juːd / (AmE), / ˈvæl.juːd / (BrE)
(in combination) having a value of a particular kind, amount, or specified property, often used as a combining form (e.g., 'double-valued', 'highly valued').

Examples

Highly valued item
He invested in a rare, highly valued collection of stamps.
In mathematics, a triple-valued function can have three possible results for each input.
The new sensor provides multi-valued output for each measurement.
