expandability

noun
ability to be enlarged
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌspændəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌspændəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of something to be enlarged, extended, or enhanced with new features or capacity

Examples

Software expandability
The expandability of the software allows users to add new modules as needed.
When buying a new phone, many people consider its expandability for extra storage.
The architect praised the building's expandability for future renovations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt easily
  • antonym: limitation - a restriction or lack of ability to expand
  • synonym: scalability - the ability to handle increased work or growth
