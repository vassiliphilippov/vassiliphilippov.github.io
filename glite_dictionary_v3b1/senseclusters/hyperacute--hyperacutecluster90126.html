hyperacute

adjective
extremely sensitive
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːt / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːt / (BrE)
extremely sensitive or highly responsive, especially in detecting small stimuli or subtle differences

Examples

Hyperacute senses
Her hyperacute sense of smell allowed her to notice even the faintest scents in the room.
The detective's hyperacute awareness helped him spot clues that others missed.
Some animals have hyperacute hearing, enabling them to detect sounds humans cannot perceive.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: acute - very sensitive or sharp
  • antonym: insensitive - not sensitive or responsive
