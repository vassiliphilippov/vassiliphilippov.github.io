flatlander

noun
person from flat area
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌlændər / (AmE), / ˈflætˌlændə / (BrE)
a person from a flat or low-lying area, often used by people in hilly or mountainous regions to refer to outsiders

Examples

City flatlander visitor
The mountain residents often refer to city visitors as flatlanders when they arrive for hiking season.
As a proud flatlander, she found the steep hills challenging during her first trip to Vermont.
Locals sometimes tease flatlanders for not being used to the winding mountain roads.
The locals referred to the tourists as flatlanders because they were not used to the steep hills.
As a proud flatlander, she found hiking in the mountains to be a real challenge.
Many flatlanders visit the region each year to enjoy the fresh mountain air.

Related Terms

  • antonym: highlander - a person from a mountainous or highland region
  • antonym: local - a person who lives in a particular area
  • synonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a particular group or place
