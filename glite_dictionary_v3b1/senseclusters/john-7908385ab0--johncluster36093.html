John

proper noun
apostle and author in the New Testament

Pronunciation: / dʒɑn / (AmE), / dʒɒn / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, traditionally regarded as the author of the fourth Gospel, three epistles, and the Book of Revelation.

Examples

John the Apostle

Many scholars debate whether John was actually the author of the Book of Revelation.
The Gospel according to John offers a distinct perspective on the life of Jesus.
