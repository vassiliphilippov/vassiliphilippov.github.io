Claudius

proper noun
Roman Emperor (AD 41–54)
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔdiəs / (AmE), / ˈklɔːdɪəs / (BrE)
(10 BC – AD 54) Roman Emperor from AD 41 to 54, known for consolidating the Roman Empire, conquering southern Britain, and being assassinated by his fourth wife.

Examples

Emperor Claudius
According to legend, Claudius was poisoned by Agrippina to ensure her son's succession.
Many historians credit Claudius with expanding the Roman Empire into Britain.
The reign of Claudius marked a period of significant growth and administrative reform.
