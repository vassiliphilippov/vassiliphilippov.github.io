recall

noun
removal of an official by petition/vote
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔl / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːl / (BrE)
the process by which voters may remove an elected official from office before the end of their term, typically by petition and subsequent vote

Examples

A recall election
A successful recall requires collecting signatures from a set percentage of registered voters.
The city council is considering changes to the recall process to make it more accessible.
The governor faced a recall after widespread public dissatisfaction with his policies.
