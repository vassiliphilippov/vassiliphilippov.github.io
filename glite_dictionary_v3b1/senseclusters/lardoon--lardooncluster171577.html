lardoon

noun
strip of fat for meat
9.2
Pronunciation: / lɑrˈduːn / (AmE), / lɑːˈduːn / (BrE)
a thin strip of fat or bacon inserted into meat to add flavor and moisture during cooking

Examples

Lardoon in beef
The chef inserted several lardoons into the beef to keep it moist while roasting.
Traditional French recipes often call for lardoons to enhance the flavor of stews.
She prepared the duck by threading lardoons through the meat before cooking.
