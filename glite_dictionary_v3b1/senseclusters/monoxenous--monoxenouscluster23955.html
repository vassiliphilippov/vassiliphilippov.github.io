monoxenous

adjective
single-host parasite
9.9
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈnɑːk.sɪ.nəs/ /; / /mə.ˈnɑːk.sə.nəs/ (AmE), /mə.ˈnɒk.sɪ.nəs/ /; / /mə.ˈnɒk.sə.nəs/ (BrE)
describing a parasite that completes its entire life cycle within a single host species

Examples

Monoxenous parasite
A monoxenous parasite can survive and reproduce using only one host species.
Scientists studied the monoxenous life cycle of the protozoan in laboratory mice.
Unlike heteroxenous parasites, monoxenous organisms do not need multiple hosts to complete development.
