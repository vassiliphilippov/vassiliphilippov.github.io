overflap

verb
to fold flap over
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌflæp / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌflæp / (BrE)
to fold or place a flap so that it covers or overlaps another part, usually for protection or closure

Examples

To overflap the envelope
To keep the contents safe, always overflap the envelope before sealing it.
The instructions say to overflap the cardboard panels for extra strength.
Workers overflapped the box lids to make sure nothing would fall out during transport.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overlap - to extend over and cover part of something else
  • antonym: uncover - to remove a cover or not cover something
