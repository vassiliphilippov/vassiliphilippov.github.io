pseudointellectual

noun
pretended intellectual
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be highly intellectual or knowledgeable, often without real understanding

Examples

A pseudointellectual at parties
Many considered him a pseudointellectual because he quoted philosophers without understanding their ideas.
The debate was disrupted by a pseudointellectual who tried to impress the audience with complex words.
She was accused of being a pseudointellectual after her shallow analysis of the novel.
Many pseudointellectuals use complex words to impress others without understanding their meaning.
The debate was filled with pseudointellectuals who cared more about appearances than real ideas.
She quickly recognized him as a pseudointellectual after he failed to answer basic questions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intellectual - a person who is highly knowledgeable or thoughtful
  • synonym: poseur - a person who pretends to be something they are not
