unscored

adjective
without background music
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈskɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈskɔːd / (BrE)
not provided with a musical score or background music, especially in a film, play, or scene

Examples

Unscored movie scene
The opening scene was intentionally unscored to create a sense of realism and tension.
Many directors choose an unscored approach for dramatic moments to let the actors' performances stand out.
The documentary remained unscored, relying only on natural sounds to tell its story.

Related Terms

  • antonym: scored - provided with a musical score or background music
