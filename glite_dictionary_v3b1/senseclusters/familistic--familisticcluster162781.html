familistic

adjective
focused on family
Pronunciation: / ˌfæməˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or emphasizing the importance of family relationships and values

Examples

Familistic values
The village is known for its familistic traditions, where extended families often live together.
A familistic approach to social policy puts the needs of families at the center of decision-making.
Her familistic values influenced her choice to stay close to her relatives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: family-oriented - giving priority to family relationships and activities
  • antonym: individualistic - emphasizing personal independence rather than family or group
