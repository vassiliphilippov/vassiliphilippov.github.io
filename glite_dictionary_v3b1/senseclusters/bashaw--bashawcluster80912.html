bashaw

noun
Ottoman official title
Pronunciation: / bəˈʃɔ / (AmE), / bəˈʃɑː / (BrE)
a former title for a high-ranking Turkish official, especially a pasha in the Ottoman Empire

Examples

Ottoman bashaw
The bashaw was responsible for governing the province under the sultan's authority.
In historical accounts, the bashaw often appears as a powerful and influential figure.
The local people brought their petitions directly to the bashaw for judgment.
