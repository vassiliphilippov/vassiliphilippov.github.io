clearance

noun
space or distance between objects
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪr.əns / (AmE), / ˈklɪə.rəns / (BrE)
the amount of space or distance by which one object is separated from another, allowing passage or preventing contact

Examples

Safe clearance between cars
Aircraft must maintain a safe clearance from other planes during takeoff and landing.
Check the clearance between the door and the floor to ensure it will open smoothly.
The truck barely made it under the bridge due to the low clearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gap - an empty space or interval; a break in continuity
  • antonym: obstruction - something that blocks or fills a passage; an obstacle
