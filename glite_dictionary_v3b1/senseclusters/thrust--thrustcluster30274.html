thrust

noun
force pushing something forward
4.3 4.2
Pronunciation: /θrʌst/ (AmE), /θrʌst/ (BrE)
the force that moves something forward, especially produced by an engine or in physics

Examples

Engine thrust
Modern jets achieve enormous thrust from relatively small engines.
The rocket's engines generate enough thrust to lift it off the ground.
The swimmer felt the thrust of the water as she pushed forward.
Scientists measured the amount of thrust generated during the experiment.
The boat relies on its propeller for thrust through the water.
The rocket engines produce significant thrust to lift the spacecraft off the ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pull - the act of drawing something toward oneself
  • synonym: push - a force moving something away from you
