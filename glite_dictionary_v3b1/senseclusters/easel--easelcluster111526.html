easel

noun
stand for holding artist's canvas
7.7 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈiːzl̩ / (BrE)
a stand, typically with three legs, used to hold an artist's canvas upright while painting or displaying artwork

Examples

A wooden easel
A wooden easel stood in the corner, holding a half-finished portrait.
At the exhibit, each photograph was displayed carefully on an easel.
The artist set her canvas on the easel and began to paint the landscape.
