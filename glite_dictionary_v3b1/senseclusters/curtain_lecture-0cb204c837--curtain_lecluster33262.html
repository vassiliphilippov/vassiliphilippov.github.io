curtain lecture

noun
private reproach or scolding by a wife to her husband
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝː.tən ˌlɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tən ˌlɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
A private reprimand or scolding, especially one given by a wife to her husband, typically in the privacy of their bedroom.

Examples

A curtain lecture at home
After the party, Tom received a curtain lecture about his reckless behavior from his wife.
He dreaded the nightly curtain lectures more than any public argument.
In Victorian novels, the curtain lecture is a recurring motif of marital life.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: lecture - a scolding or moralizing talk
  • synonym: scolding - an angry or critical lecture or reprimand
