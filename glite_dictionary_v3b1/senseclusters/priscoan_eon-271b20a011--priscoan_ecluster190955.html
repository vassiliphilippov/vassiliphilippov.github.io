Priscoan eon

noun
earliest eon in Earth's history
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪskoʊ.ən ˈiː.ɑn /; / ˈprɪs.koʊ.ən ˈi.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈprɪskəʊ.ən ˈiː.ɒn /; / ˈprɪs.kəʊ.ən ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
the earliest eon in Earth's history, spanning from the planet's formation around 4,600 million years ago to about 3,800 million years ago, with no evidence of life

Examples

The Priscoan eon period
During the Priscoan eon, the planet's surface was still forming and cooling.
No rocks have been found that date back to the Priscoan eon, making it largely a theoretical time span.
The Priscoan eon marks the very beginning of the Earth's geological record.
During the Priscoan aeon, the Earth was forming from cosmic dust and debris.
Geologists debate whether the Priscoan aeon should be recognized as a distinct chapter in Earth's history.
The Priscoan aeon predates any known rocks or evidence of life on our planet.
