Daphne mezereum

noun
a toxic European shrub with lilac flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈdæfni məˈzɪriəm / (AmE), / ˈdæfni mɪˈzɪəriəm / (BrE)
A small European deciduous shrub with fragrant lilac-colored flowers and red berries, whose twigs are highly toxic.

Examples

Daphne mezereum shrub
Gardeners should take extra care when handling Daphne mezereum due to its toxic twigs.
In early spring, Daphne mezereum produces clusters of fragrant flowers before its leaves emerge.
The berries of Daphne mezereum are beautiful but extremely poisonous to humans and animals.
