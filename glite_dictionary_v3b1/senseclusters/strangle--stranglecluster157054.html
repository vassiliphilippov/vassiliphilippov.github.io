strangle

verb
to struggle for breath; have difficulty breathing
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstraŋɡ(ə)l / (BrE)
to have difficulty breathing or experience a lack of oxygen, often resulting in choking or gasping

Examples

To strangle on fumes
He started to strangle after inhaling the chemical fumes in the lab.
She began to strangle when a piece of food got stuck in her throat.
The smoke was so thick it made him strangle and cough uncontrollably.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choke - to have severe difficulty breathing because of a constricted or obstructed throat
  • synonym: suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air or inability to breathe
