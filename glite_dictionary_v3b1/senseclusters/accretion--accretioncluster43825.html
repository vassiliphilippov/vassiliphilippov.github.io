accretion

noun
increase in inheritance share (law)
7.6
Pronunciation: / əˈkriːʃən / (AmE), / əˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
(law) an increase in a beneficiary's share of an estate, as when another beneficiary dies, fails to meet a condition, or rejects the inheritance.

Examples

Estate accretion
The will included a clause addressing accretion in case a beneficiary failed to survive the testator.
Under the doctrine of accretion, any unclaimed portion of the estate was distributed among the remaining heirs.
When John refused his inheritance, his share of the estate passed by accretion to his siblings.
