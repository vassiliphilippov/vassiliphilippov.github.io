hole

verb
make a hole in
Pronunciation: / hoʊl / (AmE), / həʊl / (BrE)
to make a hole or holes in something by piercing, cutting, or damaging it

Examples

To hole a roof
Heavy rain caused the wooden roof to hole in several places.
Over the years, insects holed the old fabric, making it unusable.
Workers used a drill to hole the metal sheets for bolts.
The carpenter accidentally holed the wall while installing the new shelf.
A sharp rock holed the tire, causing it to go flat on the highway.
During the storm, debris holed the roof, letting rainwater pour inside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perforate - to pierce and make a hole or holes in
  • synonym: pierce - to make a hole in something with a sharp object
  • antonym: seal - to close or block an opening
