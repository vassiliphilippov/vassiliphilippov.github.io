midleg

noun
middle leg of insect
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌlɛɡ / (BrE)
the middle pair of legs on an insect or arthropod, located between the front and back legs

Examples

The midlegs of insects
The grasshopper uses its midlegs to help balance while jumping.
Scientists observed that the midleg of the beetle was longer than its foreleg.
Damage to the midleg can affect how the insect moves and hunts.
The midleg of the mosquito is longer than its foreleg.
Scientists observed that the midlegs of the beetle help it balance while walking.
Damage to the midleg can affect an insect's ability to move efficiently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foreleg - the front leg of an insect or animal
  • antonym: hindleg - the rear leg of an insect or animal
