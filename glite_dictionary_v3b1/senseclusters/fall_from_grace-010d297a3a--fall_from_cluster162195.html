fall from grace

verb
lose favor or revert to bad behavior
3.3 5.6
Pronunciation: /fɔːl frʌm greɪs/ /; / /fɑːl frʌm greɪs/ (AmE), /fɔːl frɒm greɪs/ (BrE)
to go back to behaving badly after a period of good behavior, or to lose approval or respect after a wrongdoing

Examples

To fall from grace again
After years of sobriety, Mark fell from grace and began drinking again.
The celebrity fell from grace following a scandal that shocked her fans.
The students had been on their best behavior until they fell from grace during the class trip.
