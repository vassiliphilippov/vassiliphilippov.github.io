heavy-handedness

noun
clumsiness or lack of subtlety
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛviˈhændɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛviˈhændɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being clumsy, overly forceful, or lacking subtlety in action or treatment

Examples

The heavy-handedness approach
Critics accused the director of heavy-handedness in portraying the main character's struggles.
Some teachers avoid heavy-handedness by encouraging discussion instead of imposing strict rules.
The film’s heavy-handedness made the message feel forced rather than natural.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subtlety - the quality of being delicate or understated
  • antonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues
