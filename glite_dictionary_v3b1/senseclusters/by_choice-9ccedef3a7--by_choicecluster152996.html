by choice

adverb
intentionally; as a personal decision
3.1
Pronunciation: / baɪ tʃɔɪs / (AmE), / baɪ tʃɔɪs / (BrE)
Intentionally; as a result of one's own decision rather than by accident or compulsion.

Examples

Become by choice
He became a vegetarian by choice, not because of any medical restrictions.
Living alone is something she does by choice, not by necessity.
The employees worked overtime by choice, eager to finish the project ahead of schedule.

Related Terms

  • antonym: by accident - Without intention; unintentionally
  • synonym: intentionally - Deliberately, on purpose
