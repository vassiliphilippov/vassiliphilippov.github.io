digest

verb
mentally absorb or process information
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
to think about and absorb new information, so that it can be understood or remembered

Examples

To digest information
After reading the dense article, he needed time to digest its main points.
It took her several hours to digest all the details from the meeting.
Students often struggle to digest large amounts of complex information in a short period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: process - to deal with or think about information systematically
  • synonym: take in - to absorb and understand information
