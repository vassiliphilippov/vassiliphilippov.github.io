Liber

proper noun
Roman god of wine
9.9 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliÀêb…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêb…ô / (BrE)
an ancient Roman god of fertility, wine, and freedom, often associated with Bacchus or Dionysus

Examples

Liber god of wine
In Roman mythology, Liber was celebrated during spring festivals to ensure a good harvest.
The statue of Liber stood in the center of the ancient temple, surrounded by offerings of wine.
Many ancient texts describe Liber as a symbol of freedom and joyous celebration.
