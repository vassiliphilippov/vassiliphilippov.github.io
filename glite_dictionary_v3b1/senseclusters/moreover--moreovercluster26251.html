moreover

adverb
used to add further information
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /mɔːr.ˈoʊ.vər/ /; / /mə.ˈroʊ.vər/ (AmE), /mɔː.ˈrəʊ.və/ /; / /mə.ˈrəʊ.və/ (BrE)
used to introduce additional information that supports or emphasizes a previous statement

Examples

Moreover, moreover in addition
It was raining heavily; moreover, the roads were very slippery.
The hotel is centrally located and, moreover, staff are extremely helpful.
The new policy is expensive; moreover, it has not been effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furthermore - used to add more information; similar function as 'moreover'
  • antonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
