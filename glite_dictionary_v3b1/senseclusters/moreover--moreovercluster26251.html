moreover

adverb
used to add further information
3.8 47/m
Pronunciation: / m…îÀêrÀào äv…ôr / (AmE), / m…îÀêÀàr…ô äv…ô / (BrE)
used to introduce additional information that supports or emphasizes a previous statement

Examples

Moreover, moreover in addition
It was raining heavily; moreover, the roads were very slippery.
The hotel is centrally located and, moreover, staff are extremely helpful.
The new policy is expensive; moreover, it has not been effective.

Synonyms

  • furthermore
    3.6 35/m
    furthermore links a new strong point often in formal writing, while moreover emphasizes and supports the previous idea.
  • in addition
    1.2 50/m
    in addition introduces another point or fact in speech or writing, while moreover often adds emphasis to support the previous claim.
  • additionally
    3.6 24/m
    additionally gives a neutral extra fact in lists or reports, while moreover usually stresses and supports the point already made.
  • what is more
    3.5 0.60/m
    what is more adds a surprise or stronger point in casual speech, while moreover feels more formal and steady.
  • further
    1.0 0.08/m
    further can mean also or continue an idea in formal text, while moreover specifically adds supporting emphasis to the prior statement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furthermore - used to add more information; similar function as 'moreover'
  • antonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
