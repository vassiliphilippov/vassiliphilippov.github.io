adenosine

noun
intravenous antiarrhythmic drug
7.0
Pronunciation: / 59c8d5bn59ccsin / (AmE), / 59c8did0n59ccsid0n / (BrE)
(medicine) a prescription intravenous drug used to rapidly terminate certain types of fast heart rhythm by briefly slowing conduction through the atrioventricular (AV) node.

Examples

Adenosine injection
In the emergency room the cardiologist quickly gave adenosine to stop the patient's rapid heart rhythm.
During the code, paramedics administered a bolus of adenosine and monitored the heart for rhythm change.
Before the procedure, the team explained that adenosine may briefly slow the heart to restore normal rhythm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adenosine (nucleoside) - the chemical nucleoside component of DNA, RNA, and important biomolecules (ATP, ADP)
  • synonym: antiarrhythmic - a drug or treatment used to correct abnormal heart rhythms
