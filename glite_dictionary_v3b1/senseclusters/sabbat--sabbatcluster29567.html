sabbat

noun
witches' festival
Pronunciation: / ˈsæbət / (AmE), / ˈsæbət / (BrE)
a festival or gathering celebrated by witches or in Wiccan tradition, often marking seasonal changes

Examples

Spring sabbat festival
The coven gathered to celebrate the spring sabbat with music and ritual.
Each sabbat in the Wiccan calendar marks a significant point in the changing seasons.
She prepared a special meal for the upcoming sabbat to honor the tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Sabbath - a day of religious observance and rest
  • antonym: esbat - a Wiccan ritual held at a time other than a sabbat
