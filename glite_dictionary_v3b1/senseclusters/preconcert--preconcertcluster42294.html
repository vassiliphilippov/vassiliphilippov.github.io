preconcert

verb
to plan in advance
8.4
Pronunciation: /priː.kən.ˈsɜːrt/ /; / /prɪ.kən.ˈsɜːrt/ (AmE), /priː.kən.ˈsɜːt/ /; / /prɪ.kən.ˈsɜːt/ (BrE)
to arrange or plan something in advance, especially by agreement among those involved

Examples

To preconcert a plan
The leaders decided to preconcert their statements before the press conference.
They tried to preconcert their actions to ensure the event ran smoothly.
It is unethical to preconcert answers during an examination.
