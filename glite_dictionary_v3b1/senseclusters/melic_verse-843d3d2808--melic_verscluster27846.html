melic verse

noun
songlike poetry, esp. ancient Greek lyric
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛlɪk vɜrs / (AmE), / ˈmiːlɪk vɜːs / (BrE)
Poetry intended to be sung, especially Greek lyric poetry from the seventh to fifth centuries B.C.

Examples

Melic verse poetry
Scholars have studied the structure of melic verse to better understand early Greek song traditions.
The choir specialized in performing ancient melic verse with authentic Greek instrumentation.
Unlike epic poetry, melic verse was typically meant to be sung rather than simply recited.
