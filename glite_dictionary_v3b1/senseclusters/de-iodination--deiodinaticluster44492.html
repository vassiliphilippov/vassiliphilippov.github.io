de-iodination

noun
removal of iodine from a compound
Pronunciation: / diˌaɪ.əˌdɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / diˌaɪ.əʊ.dɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the removal of iodine atoms from a compound, especially an organic molecule, by a chemical process

Examples

De-iodination process
Certain enzymes catalyze de-iodination in the human body.
Researchers studied the de-iodination process to better understand environmental detoxification.
The de-iodination of thyroid hormones is essential for metabolic regulation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: iodination - the process of introducing iodine into a compound
