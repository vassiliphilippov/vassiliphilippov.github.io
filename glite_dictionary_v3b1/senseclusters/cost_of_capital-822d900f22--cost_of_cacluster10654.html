cost of capital

noun
rate a business must earn on investments to attract capital
1.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /kɑːst əv ˈkæp.ɪ.təl/ /; / /kɔːst əv ˈkæp.ɪ.təl/ /; / /kɑːst ə ˈkæp.ɪ.təl/ (AmE), /kɒst əv ˈkæp.ɪ.təl/ /; / /kɒst ə ˈkæp.ɪ.təl/ (BrE)
the rate of return that a business must earn on its investment to maintain its market value and attract funds; typically, the opportunity cost of using funds for a particular investment rather than investing elsewhere with equivalent risk

Examples

The cost of capital rate
A company must ensure that its return on investment exceeds its cost of capital.
Rising interest rates can increase the cost of capital for businesses seeking new funding.
When evaluating new projects, managers compare expected returns to the firm's cost of capital.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opportunity cost - the loss of potential gain from other alternatives when one alternative is chosen
