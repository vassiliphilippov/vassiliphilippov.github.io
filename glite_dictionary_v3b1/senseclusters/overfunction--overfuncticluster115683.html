overfunction

verb
to do more than needed
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
to function or operate at a higher level or intensity than is normal or necessary

Examples

To overfunction at work
When under pressure, she tends to overfunction and take on extra work.
If one part of the system starts to overfunction, it can cause problems elsewhere.
Parents may overfunction for their children by doing tasks the kids could handle themselves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overperform - to perform better or at a higher level than expected
  • antonym: underfunction - to function at a lower level or intensity than normal
