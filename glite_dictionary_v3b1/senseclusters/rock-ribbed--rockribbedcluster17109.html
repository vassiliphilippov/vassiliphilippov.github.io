rock-ribbed

adjective
having many rocky cliffs or escarpments
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑk ˌrɪbd / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌrɪbd / (BrE)
(of a place) abounding in or bordered by rocky cliffs or steep rock formations.

Examples

Rock-ribbed coastline
Her family has a cottage on a rock-ribbed peninsula where the sea crashes against granite cliffs.
Photographers are drawn to the rock-ribbed shores for their dramatic beauty.
The hikers marveled at the rock-ribbed coastline stretching for miles along the bay.
