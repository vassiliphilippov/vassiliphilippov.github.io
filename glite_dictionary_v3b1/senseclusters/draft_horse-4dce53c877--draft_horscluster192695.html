draft horse

noun
large horse bred for heavy work
9.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /dræft hɔːrs/ /; / /dræft hoʊrs/ (AmE), /drɑːft hɔːs/ /; / /dræft hɔːs/ (BrE)
A large, strong horse bred for pulling heavy loads, such as carts or plows.

Examples

A draft horse breed
Belgian and Clydesdale breeds are famous types of draft horse.
She admired the size and strength of the draft horse at the county fair.
The farmer used a draft horse to plow his fields every spring.
In the parade, an impressive draught horse pulled an old wooden cart.
Several breeds of draught horse are popular for their strength and calm temperament.
The farmer relied on his draught horse to plough the fields every spring.
