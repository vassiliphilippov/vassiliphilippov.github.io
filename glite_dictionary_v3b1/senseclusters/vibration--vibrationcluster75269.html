vibration

noun
regular oscillation around a mean (physics)
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
a rapid, repeated movement or shaking, such as when something moves back and forth or up and down

Examples

Strong vibration
Engineers measured the vibration frequency to assess the building's stability.
The vibration of the guitar string produced a clear musical note.
This instrument detects even slight vibration in the surrounding air.
He put his hand on the engine and noticed a strange vibration.
The earthquake caused the entire building to vibrate with a strong vibration.
You can feel a slight vibration when the washing machine is running.
She felt a slight vibration when the subway passed under the building.
The vibration of his hands was noticeable as he signed the document.
The strong vibration from the engine made the glass rattle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oscillation - swinging or movement back and forth at a regular speed
  • antonym: stillness - absence of movement or vibration
