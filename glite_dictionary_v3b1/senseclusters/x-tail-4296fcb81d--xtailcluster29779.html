X-tail

noun
X-shaped aircraft tail configuration
Pronunciation: / ˈɛks teɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛks teɪl / (BrE)
an arrangement of tail control surfaces on an aircraft where four surfaces are set in an X-shaped pattern, replacing the traditional fin and horizontal stabilizers

Examples

An X-tail design
Engineers debated whether to use an X-tail or a conventional tail for the prototype.
The X-tail provides greater yaw stability at high speeds compared to traditional configurations.
The new drone features an X-tail design for improved aerodynamic efficiency.

Related Terms
