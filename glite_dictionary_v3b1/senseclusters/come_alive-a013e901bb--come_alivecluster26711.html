come alive

verb
become lively or full of activity
Pronunciation: / kʌm əˈlaɪv / (AmE), / kʌm əˈlaɪv / (BrE)
to become lively, exciting, or full of activity; to show a marked increase in energy or interest

Examples

To come alive suddenly
Her paintings truly come alive when displayed in natural light.
The old town comes alive every spring with festivals and concerts.
The party came alive after midnight as more guests arrived.
