cert

noun
digital security certificate
7.7 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / s…úrt / (AmE), / s…úÀêt / (BrE)
(informal, computing) a digital certificate used to verify the identity of a website, server, or user and to enable encrypted connections (for example, an SSL/TLS certificate).

Examples

Website cert error
The browser warned that the site's cert had expired, so I didn't enter my password.
Our admin renewed the server's cert overnight to avoid service interruptions tomorrow.
The security team scanned all incoming certs to ensure they used valid root authorities.
