hyperinsulinism

noun
excess insulin production
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈɪnsjələˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərˈɪnsjʊlɪzəm / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the body produces an abnormally high amount of insulin

Examples

Severe hyperinsulinism condition
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hyperinsulinism after several blood tests.
Children with hyperinsulinism may experience frequent episodes of low blood sugar.
Treatment for hyperinsulinism often involves medication to control insulin levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperglycemia - a condition of having high blood sugar levels
  • antonym: hypoinsulinism - a condition in which the body produces too little insulin
