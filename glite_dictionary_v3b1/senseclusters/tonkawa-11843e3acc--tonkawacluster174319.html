Tonkawa

proper noun
Native American group
Pronunciation: / tɑnˈkɑːwə / (AmE), / tɒŋˈkɑːwə / (BrE)
a Native American people originally from central Texas, or the language spoken by this group

Examples

Tonkawa tribe
The Tonkawa were known for their hunting skills and lived in central Texas.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions of the Tonkawa to learn more about their culture.
Few people today speak the Tonkawa language, but efforts are being made to preserve it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Apache - a group of culturally related Native American tribes from the southwestern United States
  • synonym: Comanche - a Native American people from the southern Plains
