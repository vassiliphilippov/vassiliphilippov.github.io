charged

adjective
filled with strong emotion or significance
4.8
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒd / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒd / (BrE)
filled with strong emotion, tension, or likely to cause strong reactions

Examples

Charged atmosphere
Her words had a charged undertone that everyone noticed.
It was a deeply charged moment when the verdict was announced.
The debate created a charged atmosphere in the auditorium.
Abortion is often a charged topic in political discussions.
His speech on immigration was highly charged and sparked intense debate.
The courtroom was charged with tension as the verdict was read.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or strong emotion
  • synonym: emotional - having strong feelings; full of emotion
  • antonym: neutral - not supporting or helping either side in a conflict or disagreement; not emotional
  • synonym: tense - showing signs of nervousness, anxiety, or suspense
