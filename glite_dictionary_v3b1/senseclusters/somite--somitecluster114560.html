somite

noun
segment of an animal's body (biology)
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.maɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.maɪt/ (BrE)
one of a series of similar body segments into which some animals, especially embryos of vertebrates, are longitudinally divided

Examples

Embryo somites form
During development, the embryo forms somites that later differentiate into muscles and vertebrae.
Millipedes are easily recognized by their numerous somites, each bearing pairs of legs.
The number of somites can help scientists determine the stage of embryonic development.
