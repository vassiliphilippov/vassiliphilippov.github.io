wall up

verb
enclose or seal with a wall
Pronunciation: / wɑl ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
to enclose or seal off (an area, opening, or object) by constructing a wall across or around it.

Examples

To wall up a door
During the renovation, they discovered a room that was walled up decades ago.
She decided to wall up the unused fireplace for better insulation.
The workers had to wall up the old doorway to create a new office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open up - to remove barriers or make accessible
  • synonym: seal off - to block access or passage by closing securely
