nonverbal

adjective
lacking verbal skill
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
lacking verbal skill, especially in using spoken or written language; not able to communicate effectively with words.

Examples

Nonverbal children
Many children with autism are considered nonverbal because they have difficulty speaking.
She developed innovative ways to teach literacy to nonverbal learners.
The school offers special support for students who are nonverbal and need alternative communication methods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: verbal - having skill with words, relating to spoken or written language
