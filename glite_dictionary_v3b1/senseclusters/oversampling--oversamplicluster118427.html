oversampling

noun
extra data collection
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsæmplɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of using extra copies of data or collecting more samples than necessary to improve analysis or learning

Examples

Oversampling training data
The researchers applied oversampling to address the imbalance in their training data.
In digital audio, oversampling can help reduce noise and improve sound quality.
Machine learning models often benefit from oversampling when dealing with rare events in datasets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sampling - the process of selecting a subset of data from a larger set
  • antonym: undersampling - the process of using fewer samples or data points than available
