intensification

noun
increasing contrast in photographic film
3.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ten.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ten.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of increasing the contrast or density of a photographic film or image, especially to improve its appearance or clarity

Examples

Photo intensification
After intensification, the image revealed much finer features than before.
Photographers sometimes use intensification to enhance the quality of underexposed negatives.
The intensification of the old film restored its original detail and depth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reduction - the process of decreasing the contrast of photographic film
