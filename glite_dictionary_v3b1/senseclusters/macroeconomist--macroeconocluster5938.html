macroeconomist

noun
an economist specializing in macroeconomics
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊɪˈkɑnəmɪst / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊɪˈkɒnəmɪst / (BrE)
an economist who specializes in the study of the overall functioning and structure of an economy, including issues like inflation, unemployment, and economic growth

Examples

A macroeconomist studies economy
As a macroeconomist, she focuses on national employment rates and inflation policies.
Debates between a macroeconomist and a microeconomist can reveal different economic viewpoints.
The central bank hired a macroeconomist to analyze trends in gross domestic product.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microeconomist - an economist who specializes in microeconomics
