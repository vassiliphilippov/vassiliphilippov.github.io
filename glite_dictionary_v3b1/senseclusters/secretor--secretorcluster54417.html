secretor

noun
organ or gland that secretes substances
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkriːtər / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtə / (BrE)
an organ or gland that produces and releases substances such as hormones or enzymes, either into the bloodstream or through ducts

Examples

A major secretor organ
Damage to any major secretor can disrupt essential bodily functions.
The pancreas is a vital secretor of digestive enzymes.
The study focused on the thyroid as a hormone secretor in mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gland - an organ that secretes particular chemical substances
