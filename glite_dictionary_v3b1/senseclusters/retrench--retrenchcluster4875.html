retrench

verb
reduce costs or cut expenses, esp. staff
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
to reduce costs or spending, especially because of financial difficulty

Examples

To retrench costs
During economic downturns, many firms retrench to minimize their financial losses.
Facing declining profits, the company was forced to retrench and lay off several employees.
The management decided to retrench by cutting unnecessary expenses and freezing new hires.
After the company lost several major clients, it had to retrench and implement strict budget controls.
In tough economic times, families often retrench by cutting down on unnecessary expenses.
The government announced plans to retrench its workforce to save money.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut back - to reduce something, especially spending
  • synonym: downsize - reduce the number of employees to save money
  • antonym: expand - to increase in size, number, or amount
