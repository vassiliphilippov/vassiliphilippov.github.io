mitigator

noun
one who lessens harm
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪtɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something less severe, harmful, or serious

Examples

A mitigator of damage
Planting trees can be a mitigator of air pollution in urban areas.
Wearing seat belts acts as a mitigator of injury during car accidents.
Education is often seen as a mitigator of poverty and inequality.
