bely

verb
to misrepresent
8.7
Pronunciation: / bɪˈlaɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to give a false impression of something or show that something is not true

Examples

To bely feelings
Her cheerful smile bely the sadness she felt inside.
The old house's fresh paint bely its crumbling foundation.
His confident speech bely his nervousness about the presentation.
The witness's statement bely the suspect's version of events.
Her calm appearance bely the anxiety she felt inside.
The data bely the company's claims of rapid growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirm - to show that something is true or correct
  • synonym: contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement or claim
  • synonym: disprove - to show that something is not true
  • synonym: misrepresent - to give a false or misleading account of something
  • antonym: reveal - to make something known or visible
