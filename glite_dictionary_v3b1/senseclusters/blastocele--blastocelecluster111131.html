blastocele

noun
fluid-filled cavity in a blastula
Pronunciation: / ˈblæstəˌsiːl / (AmE), / ˈblastəˌsiːl / (BrE)
The fluid-filled cavity that forms inside a blastula during early embryonic development.

Examples

The blastocele cavity
During gastrulation, cells migrate inward through the blastocele to form the three germ layers.
Researchers observed that improper formation of the blastocele can lead to abnormal embryonic development.
The blastocele enlarges as the blastula develops, allowing more space for cell movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blastula - an early stage of an embryo consisting of a spherical layer of cells surrounding a fluid-filled cavity
