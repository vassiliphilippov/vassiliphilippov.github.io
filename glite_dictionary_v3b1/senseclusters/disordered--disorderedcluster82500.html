disordered

adjective
not arranged in order
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
not organized or arranged; confused, messy, or lacking order

Examples

A disordered room
Her thoughts became disordered as she tried to make sense of the confusing news.
The city was left disordered by the sudden storm, with debris everywhere.
The papers on his desk were completely disordered after the hectic meeting.
After the party, the living room was left completely disordered.
His thoughts were disordered by the sudden bad news.
The files were so disordered that it took hours to find the right one.
His disordered thoughts made it difficult for him to focus during the meeting.
The papers on her desk were so disordered that she couldn't find the report she needed.
We became lost in the disordered streets of the unfamiliar city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaotic - in a state of complete disorder or confusion
  • synonym: messy - untidy or dirty
  • antonym: orderly - arranged in a regular or neat way
