parapodium

noun
fleshy appendage in polychaete worms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌparəˈpəʊdɪəm / (BrE)
each of a pair of fleshy, lateral appendages found in polychaete worms, used for movement and respiration.

Examples

A parapodium on worm
Each parapodium acts like a tiny leg, helping the worm move and breathe.
Scientists observed how the parapodia undulate rhythmically during swimming.
The polychaete worm uses its parapodia to crawl along the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parapodia - the plural form of parapodium; several fleshy appendages in polychaete worms
