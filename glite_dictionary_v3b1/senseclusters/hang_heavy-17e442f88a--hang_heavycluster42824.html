hang heavy

verb
to feel oppressive or burdensome
3.0
Pronunciation: / hæŋ ˈhɛvi / (AmE), / hæŋ ˈhɛvi / (BrE)
(of a feeling, atmosphere, or clouds) to be oppressive, burdensome, or ominously present.

Examples

Silence hangs heavy
A tense silence seemed to hang heavy over the room after the announcement.
Dark thunderclouds hung heavy in the sky, warning of an approaching storm.
Regret can hang heavy on your conscience long after a mistake.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lighten - to make or become less heavy, burdensome, or oppressive
  • synonym: weigh on - to make someone feel worried or burdened
