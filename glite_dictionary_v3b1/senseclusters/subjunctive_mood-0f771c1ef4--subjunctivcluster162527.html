subjunctive mood

noun
verb mood expressing possibility or wishes
9.9 8.8
Pronunciation: / səbˈdʒʌŋktɪv mud / (AmE), / səbˈdʒʌŋktɪv mʊːd / (BrE)
a verb form or grammatical mood used to express wishes, suggestions, demands, or actions that are possible or hypothetical rather than factual

Examples

The subjunctive mood form
If I were you, I would apologize, showing the use of the subjunctive mood.
In English, the subjunctive mood is often used after certain verbs like 'recommend' or 'insist.'
The subjunctive mood appears in statements like 'I suggest that he be on time.'

Related Terms

  • antonym: imperative mood - verb mood used for commands or requests
  • antonym: indicative mood - verb mood expressing statements of fact or reality
