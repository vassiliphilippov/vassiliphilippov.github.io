beaver fur

noun
soft brown fur from a beaver
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːvər fɜr / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə fɜː / (BrE)
the soft, brown fur of a beaver, historically valued for making clothing, hats, and other items

Examples

Beaver fur hat
In the 19th century, top hats made from beaver fur were fashionable among gentlemen.
The coat was lined with luxurious beaver fur to keep out the cold.
The trapper sold several pelts of beaver fur to the trading post.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beaver - a large semiaquatic rodent native to North America and Eurasia
