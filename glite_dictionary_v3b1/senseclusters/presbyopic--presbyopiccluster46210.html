presbyopic

adjective

Able to see distant objects clearly but having difficulty focusing on near objects, typically as a result of aging.

Examples

A presbyopic person
As people age, they often become presbyopic and require reading glasses for close work.
During the eye examination, the doctor confirmed that he was presbyopic and prescribed bifocals.
Her presbyopic condition made it easy to read distant street signs, but hard to see the text on her phone.

Synonyms

  • farsighted
    5.6 0.05/m
    farsighted sees distant things more clearly because of a lens or eye shape, while presbyopic loses near focus due to aging.
  • longsighted
    4.8 0.00/m
    longsighted has better distance sight often from a refractive difference, while presbyopic is age-related loss of near focus.
  • hyperopic
    9.7 0.00/m
    hyperopic is the medical term for farsighted eyes caused by optical refraction, while presbyopic is loss of near focus with age.
  • hypermetropic
    9.9 0.00/m
    hypermetropic is a technical word for farsightedness in eye clinics, while presbyopic refers specifically to age-related near focus loss.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nearsighted - able to see near objects clearly, not distant ones (myopic)
