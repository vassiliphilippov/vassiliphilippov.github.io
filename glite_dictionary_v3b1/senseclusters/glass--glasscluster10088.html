glass

noun
small refracting telescope
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a small refracting telescope, often used for observation, especially at sea or for stargazing

Examples

A sailor's glass
In the 18th century, sailors often relied on a glass to spot approaching ships.
She enjoyed observing the stars at night using her old-fashioned glass.
The captain raised his glass to scan the distant coastline for signs of land.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: binoculars - an optical instrument with two lenses for viewing distant objects
  • synonym: telescope - an instrument for making distant objects appear nearer
