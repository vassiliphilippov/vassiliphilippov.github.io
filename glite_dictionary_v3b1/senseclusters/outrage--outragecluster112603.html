outrage

noun
righteous anger; strong indignation
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.reɪdʒ / (BrE)
a strong feeling of shock and righteous anger, especially caused by something perceived as unjust or offensive

Examples

Public outrage
Many people felt a sense of outrage after reading about the injustice in the news.
She expressed her outrage at the unfair treatment of her colleague.
The announcement of the decision was met with widespread outrage from the public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of tranquility; absence of anger or disturbance
  • synonym: indignation - anger or annoyance provoked by unfair treatment
