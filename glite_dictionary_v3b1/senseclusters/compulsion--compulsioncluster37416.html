compulsion

noun
irrational urge to perform an action
5.2 5.4
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpʌl.ʃən/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpʌl.ʃən/ (BrE)
a strong urge or drive to do something, especially when it is hard to control

Examples

Compulsion to check
His compulsion to keep things perfectly organized often frustrated his family.
She struggled with a compulsion to count objects repeatedly, which disrupted her daily life.
Some people experience a compulsion to check the door locks multiple times every night.
Despite her compulsion to speak, she managed to stay silent during the interview.
He felt a compulsion to check his phone every few minutes, even during meetings.
Many people experience the compulsion to apologize repeatedly, even when it isn't necessary.
