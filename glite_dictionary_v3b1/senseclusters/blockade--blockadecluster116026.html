blockade

verb
impose a blockade on
Pronunciation: / blɑˈkeɪd / (AmE), / blɒˈkeɪd / (BrE)
to seal off a place to prevent people or goods from entering or leaving, often by using barriers or force

Examples

To blockade a city
Authorities decided to blockade the town to cut off all supplies.
During the civil war, government forces attempted to blockade rebel-held cities.
The navy threatened to blockade the enemy's main port until they surrendered.
During the war, several cities were blockaded by enemy forces.
Protesters attempted to blockade the main entrance to the building.
The navy decided to blockade the port to cut off supplies.
Authorities moved quickly to blockade all access points to the quarantined area.
Farmers threatened to blockade the city with their tractors if talks failed.
The protesters decided to blockade the main road to draw attention to their demands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besiege - to surround and blockade a place, especially during war
  • synonym: block - to stop movement through, across, or past
