hedging

noun
financial risk reduction strategy
9.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈhe.dʒɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈhe.dʒɪŋ/ (BrE)
A strategy or technique used in finance to reduce or eliminate risk, often by making counterbalancing investments.

Examples

Hedging investments
By hedging their positions, traders can minimize potential losses in volatile markets.
Currency hedging can help multinational companies manage exchange rate risks.
Many investors use hedging to protect their portfolios from sudden market downturns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: speculation - investment involving high risk for potential gain
