Turdus torquatus

noun
European thrush with a white-neck band
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝːdəs tɔrˈkwɑːtəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdəs tɔːˈkwɑːtəs / (BrE)
A European thrush (ring ouzel) commonly found in rocky areas, with males displaying blackish plumage and a distinctive white band around the neck.

Examples

Male Turdus torquatus bird
Male Turdus torquatus are easily recognized by the white band on their necks.
Ornithologists have studied the migration patterns of the Turdus torquatus extensively.
The Turdus torquatus is often spotted in mountainous regions of northern Europe.
