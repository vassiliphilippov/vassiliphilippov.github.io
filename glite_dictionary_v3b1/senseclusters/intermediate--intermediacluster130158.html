intermediate

noun
middle stage or level
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
a person or thing that is in a middle position or stage between two others, especially in terms of skill or development

Examples

An intermediate student
The language course is designed for intermediate rather than beginner students.
As an intermediate, she found the exercises challenging but not impossible.
The competition has separate categories for beginners, intermediates, and experts.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: advanced - a person or thing at a high level of skill or development
  • degree lower: beginner - a person who is just starting to learn or do something
