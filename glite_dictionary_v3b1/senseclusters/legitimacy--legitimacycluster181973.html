The quality of being lawful, authorized, or in accordance with established rules or laws.

Examples

Legitimacy of the law
Many citizens doubted the legitimacy of the election results after the irregularities were reported.
She argued that the company's actions lacked legitimacy because they violated several regulations.
The judge questioned the legitimacy of the contract before making a final ruling.

Synonyms

  • legality
    4.1 3.03/m
    legality is formal conformity with laws and statutes, while legitimacy also includes being accepted or authorized by people or institutions.
  • lawfulness
    4.7 0.15/m
    lawfulness means following the law in action and intent, while legitimacy adds rightful authority and public acceptance.
  • validity
    4.9 2.47/m
    validity is legal force or binding power often for documents or decisions, while legitimacy covers being authorized and socially accepted.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illegitimacy - the state of not being lawful or authorized
  • synonym: lawfulness - the quality of conforming to law
