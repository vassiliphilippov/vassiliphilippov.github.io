activator

noun
substance that initiates
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈæktɪˌveɪtə / (BrE)
a substance or device that starts or speeds up a process or reaction

Examples

Chemical activator
The scientist added an activator to the solution to speed up the reaction.
In muscle cells, calcium ions act as an activator for contraction.
The new drug works as an activator of certain enzymes in the body.
The scientist added an activator to speed up the experiment.
A small amount of activator is required to start the chemical reaction.
The new cleaning product contains an activator that helps remove tough stains quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction
  • antonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a process or reaction
