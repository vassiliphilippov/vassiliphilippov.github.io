egg cell

noun
female reproductive cell
Pronunciation: / ɛɡ sɛl / (AmE), / ɛɡ sɛl / (BrE)
The female reproductive cell in animals and plants, which can develop into a new individual after fertilization.

Examples

A egg cell released
In mammals, only one egg cell is usually released during each menstrual cycle.
The egg cell is fertilized by a sperm cell during the process of reproduction.
The scientist explained how the egg cell contains half of the genetic information needed to form an embryo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ovum - the mature female reproductive cell; another term for egg cell
  • antonym: sperm cell - the male reproductive cell
