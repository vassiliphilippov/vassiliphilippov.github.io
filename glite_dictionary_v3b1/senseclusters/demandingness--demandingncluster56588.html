demandingness

noun
high expectations
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmændɪŋnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːndɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being demanding; expecting a lot from others or requiring a lot of effort or ability

Examples

High demandingness
The demandingness of the new coach pushed the team to achieve their best results ever.
Parents' demandingness can influence how children approach their schoolwork and responsibilities.
Some employees thrive under a manager's demandingness, while others may feel overwhelmed.
The demandingness of the training program challenged even the most experienced athletes.
Parents sometimes disagree about the appropriate level of demandingness in raising children.
She was surprised by the demandingness of her new job compared to her previous one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: difficulty - the state of being hard to do or deal with
  • antonym: ease - the state of being easy or not difficult
  • antonym: leniency - the quality of being more forgiving or less strict
  • synonym: strictness - the quality of being very firm or severe in demands or rules
