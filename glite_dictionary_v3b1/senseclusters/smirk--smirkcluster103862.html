smirk

noun
a smug or scornful smile
1.8
Pronunciation: / smɝk / (AmE), / smɜːk / (BrE)
A smile that shows smugness, self-satisfaction, or scorn rather than genuine pleasure.

Examples

A sly smirk
His smirk betrayed his true feelings about the situation.
She gave a sly smirk when she realized she was right.
The teacher noticed a smirk on his face after the prank was revealed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frown - A facial expression showing displeasure or concern
  • synonym: grin - A broad smile, often showing the teeth
