cork

verb
to stuff or fill with cork
3.9 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrk / (AmE), / k…îÀêk / (BrE)
to seal or fill something with cork, especially to close a bottle with a cork stopper

Examples

To cork a bottle
She learned how to cork bottles as part of her job at the winery.
Some players illegally cork their bats for an advantage.
The craftsman decided to cork the panel to improve insulation.
After filling the homemade jam jar, she carefully corked it to keep it fresh.
He quickly corked the bottle before traveling to prevent any spills.
Remember to cork the wine bottle tightly after you pour yourself a glass.

Synonyms

  • cork up
    4.9 0.01/m
    cork up means sealing a bottle by pushing in a cork stopper, while cork is the general verb for closing bottles.
  • bung
    9.3 0.00/m
    bung means sealing a container with a stopper for barrels or kegs, while cork refers specifically to using a cork in bottles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uncork - to remove a cork from a bottle
