Ninth of Ab

noun
Jewish fast day mourning temple destruction
Pronunciation: / naɪnθ əv ɑv / (AmE), / naɪnθ əv æv / (BrE)
(Judaism) a major fast day on the Jewish calendar commemorating the destruction of the temples in Jerusalem, observed as a day of mourning.

Examples

The Ninth of Ab fast
Jews around the world observe the Ninth of Ab by fasting and reading the Book of Lamentations.
Many synagogues hold special services on the Ninth of Ab to mark the destruction of the temples.
The Ninth of Ab is considered the saddest day on the Jewish calendar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Yom Kippur - the Day of Atonement, a major Jewish fast day
