declare allegiance

verb
publicly announce loyalty or support
5.5
Pronunciation: / dɪˈklɛr əˈlidʒəns / (AmE), / dɪˈkleə əˈliːdʒəns / (BrE)
to publicly announce loyalty or support for a person, group, or cause

Examples

He refused to declare allegiance to either side in the conflict.
Many team members will declare allegiance to their coach in times of controversy.
