soft scale

noun
a soft-bodied plant pest insect
7.4
Pronunciation: / sɔft skeɪl / (AmE), / sɒft skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale insect of the family Coccidae, distinguished by a soft, leathery protective covering and found on plants throughout all stages of development

Examples

A soft scale insect
Biological controls such as ladybugs are effective against soft scale infestations.
Gardeners often spray horticultural oils to control soft scale on ornamental plants.
The citrus tree was infested with soft scale, damaging its leaves and branches.

Related Terms

  • antonym: armored scale - a scale insect with a hard protective covering (Diaspididae)
