IAA

noun
plant hormone (indole-3-acetic acid)
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ eɪ ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪ eɪ ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for indole-3-acetic acid, a plant hormone that promotes the elongation of stems and roots

Examples

IAA plant hormone
Excessive application of IAA can result in abnormal stem elongation.
Researchers measured the levels of IAA in the leaves after the treatment.
The concentration of IAA influences the development of plant roots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auxin - a class of plant hormones controlling growth and development
