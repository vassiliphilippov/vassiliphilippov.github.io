dabbling

noun
casual participation
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdæblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdæblɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking part in an activity in a casual or superficial way, without serious commitment

Examples

Dabbling in photography
His dabbling in photography never developed into a serious hobby.
After some dabbling in cooking, she decided to take a professional class.
The artist's early dabbling in sculpture influenced her later work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commitment - a serious or dedicated involvement in something
  • synonym: experimenting - trying something new to see what happens
