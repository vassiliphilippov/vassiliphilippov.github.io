mutual induction

noun
generation of electromotive force between two circuits
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuː.tʃu.əl ɪnˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.tʃu.əl ɪnˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which two nearby circuits generate electromotive forces in each other due to changes in current

Examples

Mutual induction in circuits
Engineers must consider mutual induction when designing circuits placed close to each other.
The phenomenon of mutual induction was first demonstrated by Michael Faraday in the 19th century.
Transformers operate on the principle of mutual induction to transfer energy between circuits.
