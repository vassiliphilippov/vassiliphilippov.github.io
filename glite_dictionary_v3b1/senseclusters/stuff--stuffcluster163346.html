stuff

verb
to force something into a space
Pronunciation: / stʌf / (AmE), / stʌf / (BrE)
to fill or cram something tightly with material, or to block a space by packing it

Examples

To stuff a suitcase
He quickly stuffed his clothes into the suitcase before leaving for the airport.
She stuffed the turkey with herbs and vegetables for the holiday meal.
The child stuffed all his drawings into his backpack at the end of art class.
He began stuffing old newspapers into his wet shoes so they would dry faster.
She decided to stuff the cushions with fresh feathers for extra comfort.
They stuffed their backpacks full of snacks and water before leaving for the hike.
After the allergy attack, my nose was completely stuffed and I couldn't breathe easily.
If your ears become stuffed during the flight, try swallowing or yawning to relieve the pressure.
The plumber found that the drain was stuffed with hair and soap residue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - to stop something from moving through a space
  • synonym: cram - to fill (something) completely or to force something into a small space
  • antonym: empty - remove all contents from something; to make empty
  • synonym: fill - make something full; put as much as possible into something
  • synonym: obstruct - to block a passage or opening
  • synonym: thrust - to push something suddenly and strongly
  • antonym: unblock - to remove something that is blocking a passage
