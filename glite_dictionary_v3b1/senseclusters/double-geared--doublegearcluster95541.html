double-geared

adjective
equipped with compound gearing (2 pinions, 2 wheels)
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌbəlˈɡɪrd /; / ˌdʌb.əlˈɡɪrd / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈɡɪəd /; / ˌdʌb.əlˈɡɪəd / (BrE)
(of a machine or mechanism) having two sets of gears to improve performance or control speed

Examples

A double-geared machine
Some older bicycles feature a double-geared system for smoother and more efficient pedaling.
The double-geared lifting mechanism of the crane allowed heavier loads to be raised safely.
The engineers recommended a double-geared winch to improve torque during heavy-duty operations.
The machinist explained that the double-geared system helps prevent overheating during prolonged operations.

To cut threads more accurately, a double-geared mechanism is essential in precision lathes.
