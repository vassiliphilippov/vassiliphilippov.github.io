built-in bed

noun
bed fixed to a wall or built into a room
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪltˈɪn bɛd /; / ˌbɪlt ɪn ˈbɛd / (AmE), / ˌbɪltˈɪn bɛd /; / ˌbɪlt ɪn ˈbɛd / (BrE)
a bed that is built as part of the structure of a room, ship, train, etc., and is fixed in place

Examples

A built-in bed in room
Her childhood bedroom had a cozy built-in bed surrounded by bookshelves.
The small apartment made clever use of space with a built-in bed in the corner.
We decided to install a built-in bed in the guest room for extra storage underneath.
Each passenger was assigned a built-in bed along the compartment wall.
The sleeper train featured built-in beds stacked securely in narrow alcoves.
The small cabin had only a built-in bed and a chair for furniture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bunk - a narrow bed, often one of several stacked in tiers, commonly on ships or trains
