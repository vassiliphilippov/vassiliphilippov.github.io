vena circumflexus femoris

noun
vein serving the hip and thigh
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.nə sɜːr.kəm.ˈflek.səs fə.ˈmɔːr.ɪs/ (AmE), /ˈviː.nə sɜː.kəm.ˈflek.səs fə.ˈmɔː.rɪs/ (BrE)
Either of two veins accompanying the arteries of the same name, serving the hip and thigh.

Examples

The vena circumflexus femoris vein
Anatomical diagrams often highlight the location of the vena circumflexus femoris near its corresponding artery.
Surgeons must be cautious not to injure the vena circumflexus femoris during hip replacement procedures.
The vena circumflexus femoris plays a crucial role in draining blood from the thigh region.
