pervert

verb
to corrupt morally or lead astray
7.9 0.22/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàv…úrt / (AmE), / p…ôÀàv…úÀêt / (BrE)
to corrupt someone morally or lead them into immoral or improper behavior, especially by means of intemperance or sensuality.

Examples

To pervert young minds
Some critics claimed the film would pervert the morals of its young audience.
The teacher warned against books that could pervert impressionable minds.
Unscrupulous leaders may pervert public values for personal gain.
