tenuousness

noun
weakness or fragility
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnjuəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnjʊəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being weak, slight, or lacking strength or substance

Examples

Tenuousness of evidence
The tenuousness of the evidence made it difficult to prove the case in court.
She worried about the tenuousness of their friendship after the argument.
Scientists questioned the tenuousness of the connection between the two phenomena.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • antonym: strength - the quality of being strong or powerful
