compromise

noun
agreement by mutual concession
2.5 23/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêm.pr…ôÀåma…™z /; / Ààk…ëÀêmpr…ôÀåma…™z / (AmE), / Ààk…ím.pr…ô.ma…™z / (BrE)
an agreement or settlement in which each side gives up some demands to reach a solution

Examples

A compromise agreement
After hours of negotiation, they finally reached a compromise acceptable to both sides.
Finding a compromise between the two proposals was challenging but ultimately worthwhile.
She refused to settle for a poor compromise and insisted on a solution that benefited everyone.
After hours of debate, the committee reached a compromise that satisfied both sides.
She refused to accept the compromise because it did not meet her key demands.
Successful relationships often require some level of compromise from both partners.

Synonyms

  • middle ground
    4.2 2.35/m
    middle ground suggests a shared position between sides, while compromise requires each side to give up some demands to agree.
  • accommodation
    5.8 22/m
    accommodation is a calm arrangement to resolve a dispute, while compromise involves both sides making specific concessions.
  • settlement
    3.3 22/m
    settlement is a formal resolution that ends a dispute, while compromise often means each side gives up some demands.
  • bargain
    2.0 13/m
    bargain is a negotiated deal often about trade or price, while compromise stresses mutual concession to reach agreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically binding arrangement between parties
  • synonym: conciliation - the action of stopping someone from being angry; the action of mediating between two disputing people or groups
  • antonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • antonym: disagreement - a lack of consensus or harmony between parties
