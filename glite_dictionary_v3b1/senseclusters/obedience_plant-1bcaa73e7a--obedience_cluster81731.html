obedience plant

noun
West Indian plant yielding arrowroot starch
8.6 8.8
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈbiː.djəns plænt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈbiː.dɪ.əns plɑːnt/ (BrE)
a white-flowered West Indian plant whose root is used to produce arrowroot starch

Examples

Obedience plant root
Farmers in the Caribbean cultivate obedience plant for its starchy rhizomes.
The root of the obedience plant is processed to make arrowroot flour used in cooking.
Traditional remedies sometimes use extracts from the obedience plant root.
