faddishness

noun
temporary popularity
Pronunciation: / ˈfædɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfædɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being fashionable or popular for only a short time; being interested in short-lived trends

Examples

The faddishness of styles
The faddishness of certain clothing styles means they quickly go out of fashion.
Many products rely on faddishness to boost sales for a short time.
He was skeptical of the faddishness surrounding the latest fitness craze.
The faddishness of certain diets means they quickly become unpopular.
Critics argued that the artist's work was marked by faddishness rather than originality.
Some brands rely on faddishness to boost sales for a short period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservatism - the tendency to resist change or stick to traditional styles
  • antonym: timelessness - the quality of not being affected by the passage of time or changes in fashion
  • synonym: trendiness - the quality of being fashionable or following current trends
