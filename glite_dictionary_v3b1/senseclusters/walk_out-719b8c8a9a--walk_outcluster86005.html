walk out

verb
leave suddenly, often in protest or anger
3.4 3.90/m
Pronunciation: / w…îk a ät /; / w…ëk a ät / (AmE), / w…îÀêk a ät / (BrE)
to leave a place or event suddenly and unexpectedly, often because of anger or as a protest

Examples

To walk out angrily
After the heated argument, she walked out and never came back.
Several attendees walked out during the controversial speaker's presentation.
The workers threatened to walk out if their demands were not met.
After the heated argument, she walked out and didn't come back for hours.
He walked out on the meeting when he heard the news.
Many employees threatened to walk out unless their demands were met.

Synonyms

  • walk off
    3.5 2.73/m
    walk off is leaving suddenly to show annoyance or disapproval, while walk out often means leaving as a protest or formal refusal.
  • storm off
    4.5 0.61/m
    storm off means leaving quickly and angrily after being upset, while walk out often involves leaving to protest or show refusal.
  • up and leave
    6.3 1.53/m
    up and leave means depart suddenly without warning or reason, while walk out often suggests leaving to protest or from anger.

Related Terms
