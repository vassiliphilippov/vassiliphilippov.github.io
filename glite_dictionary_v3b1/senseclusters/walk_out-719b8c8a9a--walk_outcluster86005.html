walk out

verb
leave suddenly, often in protest or anger
1.2 2.8
Pronunciation: /wɑːk aʊt/ /; / /wɔːk aʊt/ (AmE), /wɔːk aʊt/ (BrE)
to leave a place or event suddenly and unexpectedly, often because of anger or as a protest

Examples

To walk out angrily
After the heated argument, she walked out and never came back.
Several attendees walked out during the controversial speaker's presentation.
The workers threatened to walk out if their demands were not met.
After the heated argument, she walked out and didn't come back for hours.
He walked out on the meeting when he heard the news.
Many employees threatened to walk out unless their demands were met.
