beadle

noun
minor parish official with ceremonial duties
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːdəl / (AmE), / ˈbiːdəl / (BrE)
a minor official who performs ceremonial or administrative duties, especially in a church, synagogue, or university

Examples

A beadle in church
As a beadle, he was responsible for maintaining order during parish meetings.
The beadle led the procession through the church at the start of the service.
The old beadle had served the cathedral for over thirty years, always in his traditional uniform.
The beadle announced the start of the ceremony in the grand hall.
At the university, the beadle wore a traditional gown during official events.
