interest

noun
charge for borrowing money
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
money paid regularly at a particular rate as a charge for borrowing money or as profit from lending money or investing

Examples

Bank interest rate
He invested his savings to earn interest over time.
Most credit cards charge interest if you do not pay the full balance.
The bank charges a high rate of interest on personal loans.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dividend - a sum of money paid regularly by a company to its shareholders from its profits
  • antonym: principal - the original sum of money lent or invested
  • degree higher: usury - the illegal action or practice of lending money at unreasonably high rates of interest
