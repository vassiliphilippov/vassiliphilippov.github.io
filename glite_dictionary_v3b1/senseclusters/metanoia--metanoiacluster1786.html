metanoia

noun
rhetorical correction
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈnɔɪə / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈnɔɪə / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which a statement is retracted and then restated in a stronger or clearer way

Examples

Rhetorical metanoia
The author used metanoia to clarify his point and make his argument more persuasive.
During the debate, she employed metanoia to retract her earlier statement and present a stronger claim.
In his essay, the student demonstrated metanoia by correcting and then emphasizing his main idea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amplification - a rhetorical device that adds detail or emphasis
  • synonym: correction - the act of making something right or more accurate
