overdramatize

verb
make something seem overly dramatic
3.6 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈdræmətaɪz /; / ˌoʊvɚˈdræməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdræmətaɪz / (BrE)
to present or describe something as more dramatic or serious than it really is

Examples

To overdramatize a story
He tends to overdramatize his achievements when talking to his friends.
The movie overdramatizes what actually happened during the expedition.
Try not to overdramatize the events; it was just a minor disagreement.
It's important not to overdramatise the situation before knowing all the facts.
She tends to overdramatise minor setbacks at work, making them seem like disasters.
The journalist was accused of trying to overdramatise the events to sell more papers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as greater than it is
  • antonym: understate - to describe something as less important or serious than it really is
