throw away

verb
get rid of; remove
1.0
Pronunciation: / θroʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / θrəʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to get rid of something that is no longer wanted or needed, usually by discarding it

Examples

To throw away trash
Don't forget to throw away any expired food from the refrigerator.
She decided to throw away his old letters after the breakup.
You shouldn't throw away opportunities that might lead to your success.
He accidentally threw away an important document with the junk mail.
Please throw away any trash before you leave the room.
We are throwing away old clothes to make more space in the closet.
After cleaning the attic, we had to throw away boxes of useless junk.
It's better to recycle paper than to throw away old newspapers.
She didn't want to throw away the broken chair, hoping it could be fixed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discard - to get rid of something that is no longer useful
  • synonym: dispose of - to get rid of something, especially by throwing it away
  • antonym: keep - to retain or continue to have
