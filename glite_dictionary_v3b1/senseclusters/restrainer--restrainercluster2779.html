restrainer

noun
chemical that slows photographic development
9.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstreɪnər / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnə / (BrE)
a chemical added to a photographic developer to slow down development and reduce fog on film

Examples

Photographic restrainer
Potassium bromide is often used as a restrainer in black-and-white photographic development.
She adjusted the amount of restrainer to fine-tune the image quality.
The addition of a restrainer helps prevent unwanted fogging on the film.
