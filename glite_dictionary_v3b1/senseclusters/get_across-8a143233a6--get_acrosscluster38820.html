Synonyms

  • cross
    1.0 80/m
    cross means to move from one side to the other directly, while get across is a common phrasal verb for the same action.
  • pass
    1.0 100/m
    pass often means move past or across something in motion, while get across focuses on reaching the other side.
  • traverse
    5.6 2/m
    traverse is a more formal word for traveling across wide areas, while get across is informal and used for simple crossings.
  • get over
    3.3 2/m
    get over often means pass above or finish crossing an obstacle, while get across means move from one side to the other.
  • cut across
    3.2 0.7/m
    cut across means to go straight through an area as a shortcut, while get across describes reaching the opposite side.
  • cut through
    3.0 4/m
    cut through means to go through something often by force or directly, while get across simply means to reach the other side.
  • pass over
    3.3 3/m
    pass over means move across or above something, often without touching it, while get across means to go from one side to another.
