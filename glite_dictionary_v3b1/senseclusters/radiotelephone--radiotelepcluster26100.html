radiotelephone

noun
telephone using radio waves
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈtɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈtɛlɪfəʊn /; / ˌreɪdiəʊˈtelɪfəʊn / (BrE)
a telephone that transmits and receives voice or sound using radio waves instead of wires

Examples

A radiotelephone device
Early police cars were equipped with a radiotelephone for quick emergency response.
The researcher made a call from the remote field station using a radiotelephone.
The ship maintained contact with the harbor master using a radiotelephone during the entire voyage.
In remote areas, a radiotelephone can be vital for emergencies.
The ship's captain contacted the harbor using the radiotelephone.
With a radiotelephone, pilots can communicate over long distances without cables.

  • synonym: walkie-talkie - a portable two-way radio communication device
