armor-clad

adjective
covered with heavy protective armor
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrmərˌklæd /; / ˈɑːrmər klæd / (AmE), / ˈɑːməˌklæd /; / ˈɑːmə klæd / (BrE)
Covered with heavy steel or other protective armor, especially for defense.

Examples

Armor-clad knights
Medieval knights sometimes rode armor-clad horses into battle.
The old warship sat in the harbor, its armor-clad hull glinting in the sun.
The soldiers advanced behind an armor-clad vehicle for protection.
Engineers designed an armour-clad vehicle to withstand heavy artillery fire.
The ancient fortress stood armour-clad against every invading army.
The massive armour-clad battleship loomed on the horizon.
