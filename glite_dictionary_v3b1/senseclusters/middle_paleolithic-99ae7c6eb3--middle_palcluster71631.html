Middle Paleolithic

noun
prehistoric period of Neanderthal man
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪdəl ˌpeɪliəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl ˌpælɪəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
the second subdivision of the Paleolithic period, marked by the development of Neanderthal man and lasting until about 35,000 BC

Examples

The Middle Paleolithic period
Many important archaeological discoveries from the Middle Paleolithic have helped us understand Neanderthal life.
Researchers spent years excavating a Middle Paleolithic site in France for clues about early human behavior.
The stone tools of the Middle Paleolithic period were more advanced than those of earlier eras.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Paleolithic - the prehistoric era characterized by the use of stone tools
  • degree higher: Upper Paleolithic - the final subdivision of the Paleolithic period, following the Middle Paleolithic
