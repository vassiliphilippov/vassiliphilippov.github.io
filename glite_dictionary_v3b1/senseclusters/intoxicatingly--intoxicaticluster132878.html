intoxicatingly

adverb
in an exciting way
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪˌkeɪtɪŋli /; / ɪnˈtɒksɪkeɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that is extremely exciting, appealing, or produces a feeling of euphoria, like being intoxicated

Examples

An intoxicatingly fresh air
The air in the mountain village was intoxicatingly fresh and filled her with energy.
He smiled intoxicatingly, making everyone in the room feel drawn to him.
The perfume smelled intoxicatingly sweet, lingering long after she had left.
The perfume lingered intoxicatingly in the air long after she left the room.
He smiled intoxicatingly, making everyone around him feel instantly at ease.
The city lights sparkled intoxicatingly against the night sky, drawing tourists from around the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dully - in a way that is not interesting or exciting
  • synonym: excitingly - in a way that causes excitement
  • synonym: exhilaratingly - in a way that makes you feel very happy and excited
  • synonym: seductively - in a way that is very attractive or tempting
  • antonym: soberly - in a serious or calm way, without excitement or intoxication
