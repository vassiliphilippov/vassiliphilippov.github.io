improperium

noun
liturgical antiphon of reproach in Good Friday service
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈproʊpəriəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpəriəm / (BrE)
(usually plural) An antiphon and response sung in Christian liturgy, especially on Good Friday, in which Christ reproaches his people; particularly known in Latin as 'Improperia'.

Examples

The improperia sung
During the Good Friday liturgy, the choir sang the improperia with haunting reverence.
Many worshippers find the improperia to be deeply moving expressions of Christ's remonstrance.
The improperia are an integral part of traditional Catholic Holy Week services.
