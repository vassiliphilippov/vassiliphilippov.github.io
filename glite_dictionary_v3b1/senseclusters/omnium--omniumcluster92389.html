omnium

noun
the whole; everything
Pronunciation: / ˈɑmniəm / (AmE), / ˈɒmnɪəm / (BrE)
the whole of something; everything considered as a total or entirety, especially in archaic or literary use

Examples

The omnium of life
The philosopher sought to understand the omnium of human experience.
In his speech, he referred to the omnium of the nation's achievements.
Poets sometimes write about the omnium of nature in grand, sweeping terms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: part - a piece or portion of a whole
  • synonym: totality - the state of being total or whole
