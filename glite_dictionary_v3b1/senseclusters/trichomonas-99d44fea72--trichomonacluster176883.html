Trichomonas

noun
parasitic protozoa genus
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɪkəˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
a genus of protozoan parasites, some species of which cause infections in humans and animals

Examples

Trichomonas infection
The doctor explained that Trichomonas is a common cause of certain sexually transmitted infections.
Laboratory tests confirmed the presence of Trichomonas in the patient's sample.
Treatment for Trichomonas usually involves a course of specific antibiotics.

  • synonym: protozoa - single-celled microscopic organisms, some of which cause disease
