samsara

noun
cycle of rebirth in Hinduism and Buddhism
Pronunciation: / sɑmˈsɑrə / (AmE), / sʌmˈsɑːrə / (BrE)
In Hinduism and Buddhism, the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth marked by suffering and illusion.

Examples

The samsara cycle
Many followers of Buddhism seek to escape samsara and attain enlightenment.
The doctrine of samsara teaches that life is an endless cycle of suffering and rebirth.
Understanding samsara is central to several Eastern philosophies and religions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nirvana - state of liberation from the cycle of rebirth and suffering
