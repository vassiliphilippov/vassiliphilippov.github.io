hang in the balance

verb
be in a state of uncertainty or danger
5.3
Pronunciation: / hæŋ ɪn ðə ˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / hæŋ ɪn ðə ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to be in a state of uncertainty or danger, where the outcome or result is not yet decided

Examples

Hang in the balance situation
After the latest development, her future with the company hangs in the balance.
As the negotiations continue, many jobs hang in the balance.
The outcome of the match hangs in the balance until the final whistle.
