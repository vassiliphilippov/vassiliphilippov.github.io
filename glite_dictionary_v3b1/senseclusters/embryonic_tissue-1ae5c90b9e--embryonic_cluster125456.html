embryonic tissue

noun
tissue found in an embryo
3.4
Pronunciation: / ɛmˌbraɪˈɑnɪk ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ɛmˌbrɪˈɒnɪk ˈtɪʃuː / (BrE)
the tissue present in an embryo, from which the organs and structures develop during early stages of growth

Examples

Embryonic tissue development
Damage to embryonic tissue can cause serious developmental abnormalities.
Scientists are studying embryonic tissue to better understand how organs form during early development.
Some stem cell therapies use cells derived from embryonic tissue to treat various diseases.
