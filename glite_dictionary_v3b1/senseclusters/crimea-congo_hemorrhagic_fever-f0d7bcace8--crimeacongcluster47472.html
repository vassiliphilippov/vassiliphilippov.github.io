Crimea-Congo hemorrhagic fever

noun
tick-borne viral hemorrhagic fever
9.3
Pronunciation: /kraɪ.ˈmiː.ə ˈkɑːŋ.ɡoʊ hem.ər.ˈædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.vər/ /; / /kraɪ.ˈmɪə ˈkɔːŋ.ɡoʊ hem.ər.ˈædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.vər/ (AmE), /kraɪ.ˈmiː.ə ˈkɒŋ.ɡəʊ hem.ə.ˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və/ /; / /kraɪ.ˈmɪə ˈkɒŋ.ɡəʊ hem.ə.ˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və/ (BrE)
a severe infectious disease caused by a bunyavirus, transmitted by ticks, and characterized by high fever and bleeding; common in areas of Africa, the Middle East, Asia, and southeastern Europe

Examples

Crimea-Congo hemorrhagic fever outbreak
Several cases of Crimea-Congo hemorrhagic fever were reported after an outbreak among local farm workers.
There is currently no specific treatment for Crimea-Congo hemorrhagic fever, making prevention crucial.
Ticks that feed on livestock can transmit Crimea-Congo hemorrhagic fever to humans.
