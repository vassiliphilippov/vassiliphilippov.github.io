detainer

noun
legal claim for property
9.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈteɪnər / (AmE), / dɪˈteɪnə / (BrE)
a legal order or claim to keep or recover possession of property or land, especially after wrongful occupation

Examples

Detainer for property
The landlord filed a detainer to reclaim the apartment from the former tenant.
A court may issue a detainer when someone refuses to leave a property after their lease ends.
Legal experts discussed the process of obtaining a detainer in property disputes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eviction - the legal removal of a tenant from property
  • synonym: repossession - the act of taking back property after a default
