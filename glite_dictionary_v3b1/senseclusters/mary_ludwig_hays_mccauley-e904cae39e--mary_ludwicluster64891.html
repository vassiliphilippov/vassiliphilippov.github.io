Mary Ludwig Hays McCauley

proper noun
heroine of the American Revolution, known as 'Molly Pitcher'
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛri ˈlʊdɡɪ heɪz məˈkɔːli / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri ˈlʊdɡɪ hɛɪz məˈkɔːli / (BrE)
(1754–1832) A heroine of the American Revolution who reportedly carried water to soldiers during the Battle of Monmouth and took over her husband's cannon after he collapsed from heat.

Examples

Mary Ludwig Hays McCauley bravery
Mary Ludwig Hays McCauley became famous for her bravery at the Battle of Monmouth, where she helped artillerymen under fire.
Many history books tell the story of Mary Ludwig Hays McCauley bringing water to thirsty soldiers in battle.
Some scholars debate how much of Mary Ludwig Hays McCauley's legend is fact and how much is folklore.
