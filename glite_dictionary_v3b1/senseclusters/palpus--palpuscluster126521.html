palpus

noun
insect mouth appendage
Pronunciation: / ˈpælpəs / (AmE), / ˈpalpəs / (BrE)
a sensory appendage near the mouth of some insects and arthropods, used for tasting or feeling

Examples

Insect palpus
The butterfly uses its palpus to taste nectar before feeding.
Scientists observed the movement of the beetle's palpus under a microscope.
Each spider has a pair of palpus near its mouth for sensing its surroundings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antenna - a sensory appendage on the head of insects
