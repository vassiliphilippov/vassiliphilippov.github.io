Synonyms

  • personation
    9.1 0.00/m
    personation is imitating someone's behaviour to trick or entertain, while impersonation covers both playful imitation and deliberate deception of another person's identity.
  • impression
    4.0 1.53/m
    impression copies a person's voice and manner for stage or fun, while impersonation may be used to deceive or entertain in many settings.
  • imposture
    9.5 0.20/m
    imposture involves taking a false identity to trick others, while impersonation can be either a show for fun or a real deceit.
  • imitation
    3.5 1.34/m
    imitation is a copy or exaggerated act often for comic effect, while impersonation also includes serious deception or exact role-playing.
  • put-on
    7.5 0.19/m
    put-on is an informal trick or joke meant to fool people, while impersonation can be a performance or a harmful deception.
  • pretending
    4.1 0.01/m
    pretending is acting as if something is true to fool or play, while impersonation specifically copies another person's identity or manner.
  • playacting
    5.7 0.08/m
    playacting is performing a character on stage or training, while impersonation imitates a real person's identity for show or deceit.
