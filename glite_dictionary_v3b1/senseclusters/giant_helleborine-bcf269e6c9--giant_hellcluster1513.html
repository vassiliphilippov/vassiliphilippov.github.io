giant helleborine

noun
a North American orchid with distinctive flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪənt ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːraɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˌhɛlɪˈbɒraɪn / (BrE)
a species of orchid native to western North America, characterized by leafy stems and greenish-brown and pinkish flowers borne singly in the axils of upper leaves, typically found near streams or ponds

Examples

The giant helleborine orchid
Botanists documented dozens of giant helleborine plants blooming near the river in early summer.
Local conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the rare giant helleborine in the region.
The giant helleborine thrives in moist woodlands along mountain streams.
