sense of the meeting

noun
general consensus in a meeting
Pronunciation: / sɛns əv ðə ˈmitɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛns əv ðə ˈmiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the general agreement or consensus reached among the members of a meeting, often without a formal vote

Examples

The sense of the meeting
He made a summary statement reflecting the sense of the meeting to guide further decisions.
The chairperson asked if anyone objected to the sense of the meeting before moving on to the next agenda item.
Without a formal vote, the proposal was accepted according to the sense of the meeting.
