subclinical absence

noun
brief seizure apparent only on EEG
9.9
Pronunciation: /sʌb.ˈklɪn.ɪ.kəl ˈæb.səns/ (AmE), /sʌb.ˈklɪn.ɪ.kəl ˈæb.səns/ (BrE)
A seizure involving brief impairment of cortical function detectable only by specific EEG patterns, without observable clinical symptoms.

Examples

Subclinical absence seizure
Subclinical absence seizures can complicate the diagnosis of certain epilepsy syndromes.
Routine monitoring revealed subclinical absences that went unnoticed by the patient.
The neurologist identified a subclinical absence on the EEG despite the patient appearing alert.
