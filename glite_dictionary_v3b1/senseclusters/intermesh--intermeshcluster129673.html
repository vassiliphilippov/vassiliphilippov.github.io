intermesh

verb
to fit together closely
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmɛʃ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛʃ / (BrE)
to fit or join together so that parts work closely with each other, often by overlapping or engaging

Examples

Gears intermesh
The two gears intermesh perfectly to ensure smooth operation of the machine.
In this design, the metal rings intermesh to form a strong and flexible chain.
The branches of the shrubs intermesh, creating a dense natural barrier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interlock - to connect or fit together so that movement is restricted
  • antonym: separate - to move or be apart; not joined together
