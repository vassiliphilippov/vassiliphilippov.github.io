The number of electron pairs an atom can share with other atoms, especially as part of covalent bonds in a chemical compound.

Examples

Covalency of atoms
Carbon's covalency enables it to create complex molecular structures such as those found in organic compounds.
Oxygen has a covalency of two, allowing it to form two bonds with hydrogen atoms in water.
The covalency of an element can often be deduced from its position in the periodic table.
