cry

noun
animal's characteristic sound or call
Pronunciation: / kraɪ / (AmE), / kraɪ / (BrE)
a loud sound, shout, or call made by a person or animal, often expressing emotion

Examples

A bird's cry
Each species of bird has its own distinct cry that helps identify it.
The cry of the elephant was heard from miles away.
The wolf's cry echoed through the forest at midnight.
A cry of protest rose from the crowd when the decision was announced.
Her cry for help echoed down the street, drawing attention from passersby.
The demonstrators let out a loud cry against injustice.
A piercing cry echoed through the empty hallway, startling everyone nearby.
She heard the cry of someone in distress and rushed to help.
The wounded animal let out a desperate cry before falling silent.
