monopolar

adjective
depressive only
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
relating to a mood disorder characterized by depressive episodes without any manic or hypomanic episodes.

Examples

Monopolar depression diagnosis
The doctor diagnosed her with monopolar depression after ruling out any history of mania.
Unlike bipolar disorder, monopolar depression involves only depressive episodes.
Researchers are studying the differences between monopolar and bipolar mood disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bipolar - relating to a mood disorder with both depressive and manic episodes
  • synonym: unipolar - relating to depression without manic episodes
