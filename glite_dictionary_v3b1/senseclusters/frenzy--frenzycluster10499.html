frenzy

noun
violent excitement or agitation
4.9 7.41/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfrÉ›nzi / (AmE), / ËˆfrÉ›nzi / (BrE)
A state of uncontrolled excitement, agitation, or wild activity, often marked by intense emotion or violence.

Examples

In a frenzy of excitement
During the sale, shoppers rushed through the store in a frenzy to grab the best deals.
She worked herself into a frenzy of worry before the exam.
The crowd was swept into a frenzy as the band began to play.

Synonyms

  • hysteria
    6.1 4.45/m
    hysteria is sudden, extreme panic or wild emotion in people, while frenzy is violent, fast, uncontrolled activity.
  • madness
    4.9 3.33/m
    madness is extreme, often irrational behavior or mental illness in someone, while frenzy is a short, wild burst of activity.

Related Terms
