tetrazine

noun
nitrogen ring compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtrəziːn / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəziːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound with a six-membered ring structure containing four nitrogen atoms

Examples

A tetrazine compound
The chemist studied the reactivity of the new tetrazine compound in the laboratory.
Many pharmaceuticals are developed using a tetrazine core structure for stability.
Researchers discovered that tetrazine derivatives can be useful in bioorthogonal chemistry.
