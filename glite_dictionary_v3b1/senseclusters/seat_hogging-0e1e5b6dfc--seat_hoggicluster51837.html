seat hogging

noun
taking up extra seats in public
Pronunciation: / siːt ˈhɑɡɪŋ / (AmE), / siːt ˈhɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of occupying more than one seat or more space than necessary, especially in public places

Examples

Seat hogging on buses
On the crowded bus, seat hogging caused several passengers to stand.
Rules against seat hogging are posted in the theater to ensure everyone gets a seat.
The train conductor politely asked people to stop seat hogging during rush hour.

  • synonym: hogging - taking or using more than one's share
