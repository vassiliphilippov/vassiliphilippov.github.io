womanpower

noun
influence of women
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊmənˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwʊmənˌpaʊə / (BrE)
the collective strength, influence, or participation of women, especially in society, politics, or the workforce

Examples

Womanpower in society
The rise of womanpower in government has led to more diverse leadership.
Many companies are recognizing the value of womanpower in their executive teams.
The event celebrated womanpower and encouraged more women to take on leadership roles.
The organization relies on the womanpower of its dedicated female volunteers.
Increasing womanpower in leadership roles has improved the company's performance.
The campaign highlighted the importance of womanpower in driving social change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: girl power - the empowerment and confidence of girls and young women
  • antonym: manpower - the supply of people able to work or contribute
  • synonym: workforce - all the people engaged in or available for work
