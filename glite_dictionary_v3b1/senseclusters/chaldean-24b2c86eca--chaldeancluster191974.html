Chaldean

noun
ancient wise man or occult scholar
6.3
Pronunciation: / kælˈdiːən /; / kælˈdiən / (AmE), / kælˈdiːən /; / kalˈdiːən / (BrE)
a person skilled in occult learning, especially in ancient astrology and magic, often regarded as a wise man in antiquity

Examples

A wise Chaldean
In ancient texts, a Chaldean is often depicted as a master of celestial wisdom.
Some believed the Chaldean possessed secret knowledge far beyond ordinary men.
The king summoned the Chaldean to interpret his troubling dream.
In ancient times, a Chaldaean was revered for his knowledge of the stars and mystical arts.
The king consulted a Chaldaean to interpret the strange dreams that troubled his sleep.
The wise Chaldaean studied sacred texts by candlelight, seeking hidden truths.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magi - a member of a priestly caste skilled in astrology and magic, especially in ancient Persia and Babylonia
  • synonym: magus - a wise man or magician of ancient times
  • synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims or is believed to have magic powers
