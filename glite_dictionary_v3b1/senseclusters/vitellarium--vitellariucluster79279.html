vitellarium

noun
yolk-producing organ in some invertebrates
9.9
Pronunciation: /vɪ.tə.ˈler.iː.əm/ /; / /vɪ.tə.ˈlær.iː.əm/ (AmE), /vɪ.tə.ˈleə.riː.əm/ /; / /vɪ.tə.ˈlæ.riː.əm/ (BrE)
In many flatworms and rotifers, a specialized part of the ovary responsible for producing yolk-filled cells that nourish the developing eggs.

Examples

The vitellarium produces yolk
Damage to the vitellarium can impair egg production in these invertebrates.
Scientists studied the development of the vitellarium in rotifers to understand reproductive strategies.
The vitellarium supplies nutrients to the eggs in many flatworm species.
