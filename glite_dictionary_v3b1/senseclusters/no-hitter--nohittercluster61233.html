no-hitter

noun
baseball game with no hits allowed
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊˌhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌhɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a game in which a pitcher does not allow any hits by the opposing team.

Examples

A no-hitter game
Baseball fans remember where they were when they saw their first no-hitter.
He pitched a no-hitter in last night's game, thrilling the home crowd.
The rookie nearly achieved a no-hitter before giving up a single in the ninth inning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shutout - a game in which one team prevents the other from scoring
