quick temper

noun
tendency to become angry easily
Pronunciation: / kwɪk ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / kwɪk ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
a tendency to become angry easily or suddenly

Examples

A quick temper flare
Although he has a quick temper, he usually calms down quickly.
His quick temper often gets him into arguments at work.
She is known for her quick temper, so people tend to avoid upsetting her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not easily angered; relaxed and peaceful
  • synonym: hot temper - tendency to become angry quickly or passionately
  • synonym: short temper - tendency to become angry easily
