macroglia

noun
large neuroglial cells in nervous tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmæk.roʊˈɡlaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊˈɡlʌɪ.ə / (BrE)
Large neuroglial cells in nervous tissue, including astrocytes and oligodendrocytes, as distinct from microglia.

Examples

The macroglia cells
Astrocytes and oligodendrocytes are types of macroglia present in the central nervous system.
Damage to macroglia has been linked to several neurodegenerative diseases.
Researchers found that macroglia play a key role in maintaining the health of neural tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microglia - small neuroglial cells acting as macrophages in the central nervous system
