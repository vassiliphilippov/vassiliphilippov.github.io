fire control radar

noun
radar controlling weapon targeting and firing
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪɚ kənˈtroʊl ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˌfaɪə kənˈtrəʊl ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
a radar system used to control the aiming and firing of weapons at a military target, typically on ships or in anti-aircraft defense

Examples

Fire control radar system
Engineers upgraded the ship’s fire control radar to improve precision in challenging weather conditions.
Modern fire control radar can simultaneously track multiple targets during combat operations.
The destroyer relies on its fire control radar to accurately guide missiles toward enemy ships.

Related Terms

  • synonym: target acquisition radar - radar used to detect and track targets before engaging them
