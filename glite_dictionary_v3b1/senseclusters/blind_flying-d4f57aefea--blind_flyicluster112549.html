blind flying

noun
aviation: instrument-based flight
6.5 5.9
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd ˈflaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈflaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of flying an aircraft by relying solely on instruments when visual references are unavailable, such as in clouds or fog.

Examples

Blind flying techniques
During the storm, the pilot relied on blind flying techniques to navigate safely to the airport.
Learning the basics of blind flying is essential for any aspiring commercial pilot.
With thick fog covering the runway, only those trained in blind flying could land their planes.
