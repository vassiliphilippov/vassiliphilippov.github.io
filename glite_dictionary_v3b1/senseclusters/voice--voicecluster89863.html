voice

verb
to express or say aloud; to vocalize
1.2
Pronunciation: / vɔɪs / (AmE), / vɔɪs / (BrE)
to express or state something (such as an opinion or feeling) aloud, especially publicly

Examples

To voice an opinion
If you disagree, don't be afraid to voice your opinion.
She finally found the courage to voice her concerns at the meeting.
The actor will voice the main character in the animated film.
He voiced his frustration over the lack of communication.
Many employees voice their concerns about workplace safety.
She voiced her support for the new proposal during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: express - to put (thoughts or feelings) into words; to convey
  • antonym: mute - to silence or make quiet
  • antonym: suppress - to restrain or prevent the expression of (something)
  • synonym: vocalize - to produce sound with the voice
