lawyerly

adjective
like a lawyer
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔjɚli /; / ˈlɔjərli / (AmE), / ˈlɔːjəli / (BrE)
behaving, speaking, or thinking in a way that is typical of a lawyer, especially being precise, cautious, or formal

Examples

A lawyerly approach
Her lawyerly approach to the discussion made everyone choose their words carefully.
He gave a lawyerly answer, avoiding any direct commitment to the proposal.
The contract was reviewed in a very lawyerly manner to ensure every detail was correct.
Her lawyerly approach to negotiations impressed everyone at the meeting.
He answered the questions in a very lawyerly manner, carefully choosing his words.
The document was written in a lawyerly style that was difficult for non-experts to understand.
His explanation was so lawyerly that most people found it hard to follow.
The contract was written in a lawyerly manner, full of technical terms.
She gave a lawyerly response, carefully choosing every word to avoid misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legalistic - focusing strictly on legal rules or details
  • antonym: plain - simple and clear in style
  • antonym: unprofessional - not showing the standards expected of a profession
