reeducation

noun
forced ideological training
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɛdʒuˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɛdjʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forcing people to abandon their beliefs and accept new ones, often through coercion or intensive instruction

Examples

Reeducation camp
Many political prisoners were sent to reeducation camps after the revolution.
The regime used reeducation as a tool to suppress dissent and enforce loyalty.
Stories of reeducation often involve forced confessions and strict ideological training.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brainwashing - the process of making someone adopt radically different beliefs by using systematic pressure
  • synonym: indoctrination - the process of teaching someone to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
