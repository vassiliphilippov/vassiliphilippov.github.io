admissibility

noun
quality of being acceptable or allowed
Pronunciation: / ədˌmɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ədˌmɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being acceptable or allowed, especially as evidence in a court of law or in formal processes

Examples

The admissibility of evidence
Strict rules determine the admissibility of scientific data in academic journals.
The admissibility of his testimony was challenged due to procedural errors.
The judge questioned the admissibility of the new evidence presented by the defense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admissible - acceptable or allowed, especially in a court of law
  • antonym: inadmissibility - the quality or state of not being acceptable or allowed
