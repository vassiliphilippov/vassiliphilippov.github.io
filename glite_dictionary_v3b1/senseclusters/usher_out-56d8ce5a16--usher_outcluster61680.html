usher out

verb
show or lead someone out
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈʌʃɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə aʊt / (BrE)
to show or lead someone politely out of a room or building, especially at the end of an encounter or event

Examples

To usher out guests
At the end of the meeting, the assistant ushered out the remaining guests.
She politely ushered out the reporters when the press conference was over.
The security staff ushered out the crowd after the concert ended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: escort - to accompany someone for guidance or protection
  • synonym: see off - to go with someone to say goodbye as they leave
