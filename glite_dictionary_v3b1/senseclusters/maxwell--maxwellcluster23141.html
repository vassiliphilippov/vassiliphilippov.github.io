maxwell

noun
unit of magnetic flux (cgs system)
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmæksˌwɛl / (AmE), / ˈmæksˌwɛl / (BrE)
a unit of magnetic flux in the centimeter-gram-second (cgs) system, equal to the flux perpendicular to an area of one square centimeter in a magnetic field of one gauss

Examples

One maxwell unit
In the cgs system, one maxwell is the magnetic flux through one square centimeter perpendicular to the field.
Scientists rarely use the maxwell today, preferring the SI unit, the weber.
The strength of the magnetic field was measured in maxwells during the early experiment.
