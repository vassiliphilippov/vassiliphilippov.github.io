budding

noun
asexual reproduction by outgrowth
6.7 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
A form of asexual reproduction in which a new organism develops from an outgrowth or bud on the parent, common in yeasts and some other unicellular organisms.

Examples

Budding in yeast
Budding allows new individuals to form as small growths attached to the parent organism.
The scientist observed the process of budding under the microscope.
Yeast cells reproduce by budding rather than by binary fission.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fission - division of a single organism into two or more parts
