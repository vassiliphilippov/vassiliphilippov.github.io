rekey

verb
to change musical key
7.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàkiÀê / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàkiÀê / (BrE)
to change the musical key of a song or piece to a different pitch level

Examples

To rekey a song
The band decided to rekey the song to make it easier for the new singer.
If the piece is too high, you can rekey it to suit your voice.
The music teacher showed the class how to rekey a melody for different instruments.

Synonyms

  • transpose
    7.2 0.25/m
    transpose raises or lowers a piece so it fits performers, while rekey changes a song's overall key to a different pitch level.
  • modulate
    7.4 0.13/m
    modulate moves between keys inside a piece as part of harmony, while rekey shifts the entire song to a single new pitch level.
