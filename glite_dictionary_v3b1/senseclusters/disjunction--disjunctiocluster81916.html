disjunction

noun
state of being disconnected
7.4
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən /; / dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən /; / dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of being separated or disconnected; a lack of connection between things

Examples

Social disjunction
Social disjunction can be seen when different groups in a society become isolated from each other.
The report highlights a disjunction between theory and practice in modern education.
There is a clear disjunction between the intentions of the law and its actual effects.
Geological events can cause the disjunction of formerly connected landmasses.
The disjunction of the teams made collaboration more difficult.
There was a clear disjunction between the company's policies and its practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connection - a relationship in which things are joined or associated
  • synonym: separation - the state of being apart; the act of moving or being moved apart
