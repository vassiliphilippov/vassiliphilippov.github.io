(informal) Conforming to or characteristic of mainstream trends, especially in culture, media, or entertainment.

Examples

A mainstreamy style
His music used to be unique, but lately it's become surprisingly mainstreamy.
She tends to avoid mainstreamy films and prefers indie productions.
The director's latest movie felt a bit too mainstreamy for fans of his earlier, edgier work.

Synonyms

  • mainstream
    3.4 6.22/m
    mainstream names the dominant, widely accepted ideas or styles, while mainstreamy is an informal word for things that simply follow those trends.
  • popular
    1.0 20/m
    popular describes music or art many people enjoy, while mainstreamy suggests a casual, informal sense of following dominant cultural trends.
  • trendy
    3.1 3.86/m
    trendy means very up-to-date and often short-lived in style, while mainstreamy describes fitting the broad, common tastes of the public.
  • fashionable
    2.6 7.31/m
    fashionable refers to looking stylish or in vogue, while mainstreamy means following wide cultural trends beyond just clothes or style.
