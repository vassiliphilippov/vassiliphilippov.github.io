fluctuate

verb
change frequently; rise and fall
4.0 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈflʌk.tʃʊ.eɪt/ /; / /ˈflʌk.tjʊ.eɪt/ (AmE), /ˈflʌk.tʃʊ.eɪt/ /; / /ˈflʌk.tjʊ.eɪt/ (BrE)
to change continually, especially in level, amount, or value; to go up and down unpredictably

Examples

To fluctuate in value
Her mood tends to fluctuate depending on the weather.
Interest rates may fluctuate as the economy recovers.

The central bank can fluctuate interest rates to control inflation.

Wind patterns fluctuate the water's surface, creating gentle waves on the lake.
During the summer, temperatures can fluctuate between very hot and surprisingly cool within a single week.
His energy levels fluctuate throughout the day depending on how much sleep he had.
The stock market tends to fluctuate significantly during times of economic uncertainty.
