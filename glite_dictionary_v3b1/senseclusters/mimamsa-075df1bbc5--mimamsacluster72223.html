Mimamsa

noun
school of Hindu philosophy focused on Vedic ritual
9.9
Pronunciation: /miː.ˈmɑːn.sə/ /; / /mɪ.ˈmɑːn.sə/ (AmE), /miː.ˈmɑːn.sə/ /; / /mɪ.ˈmɑːn.sə/ (BrE)
One of the six orthodox schools of Hindu philosophy, focusing on the interpretation of Vedic rituals and texts, distinguished from Vedanta, which emphasizes philosophical teachings of the Upanishads.

Examples

Mimamsa school philosophy
Scholars often study Mimamsa to understand ancient Indian perspectives on language, law, and ritual.
The Mimamsa tradition places great emphasis on the correct performance of Vedic rituals.
Unlike Vedanta, Mimamsa regards ritual action as the primary means to spiritual progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Vedanta - School of Hindu philosophy based largely on the Upanishads, emphasizing spiritual knowledge over ritual action
