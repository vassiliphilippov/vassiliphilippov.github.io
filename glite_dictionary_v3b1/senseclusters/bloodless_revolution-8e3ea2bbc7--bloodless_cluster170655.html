Bloodless Revolution

noun
nonviolent political or social change
4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈblʌd.ləs rev.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ /; / /ˈblʌd.ləs rɛv.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈblʌd.ləs rɛv.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ /; / /ˈblʌd.ləs rev.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (BrE)
a major political or social change achieved with little or no violence; used metaphorically for a peaceful or nonviolent transformation.

Examples

A Bloodless Revolution in politics
After the election, many called the peaceful transfer of power a Bloodless Revolution that changed the constitution.
The company's management changes felt like a Bloodless Revolution, transforming operations without layoffs or protests.
Environmentalists hoped for a Bloodless Revolution in policy, moving to renewable energy without social unrest.
