bronchial asthma

noun
allergic respiratory disorder causing wheezing
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑnkiəl ˈæstmə / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋkiəl ˈɑːstmə / (BrE)
A type of asthma characterized by inflammation and narrowing of the airways, causing wheezing and difficulty in breathing, often triggered by allergies.

Examples

Bronchial asthma attack
Pollen is a common trigger for bronchial asthma in both children and adults.
Proper management and medication can help control bronchial asthma symptoms and prevent attacks.
The doctor diagnosed him with bronchial asthma after he experienced frequent episodes of wheezing and shortness of breath.

  • synonym: asthma - chronic respiratory condition with airway narrowing
