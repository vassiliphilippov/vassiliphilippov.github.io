microclimate

noun
localized climate
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌklaɪmət / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌklaɪmət / (BrE)
the climate or weather conditions of a small, specific area that are different from the surrounding region

Examples

Greenhouse microclimate
The greenhouse creates a unique microclimate that allows tropical plants to thrive in winter.
Urban areas often have a warmer microclimate compared to the surrounding countryside.
By planting trees, you can influence the microclimate in your backyard and make it more comfortable.
The tall buildings in the city center create a unique microclimate that is warmer than nearby areas.
Gardeners often use greenhouses to control the microclimate for delicate plants.
A forest clearing can have a different microclimate compared to the shaded areas under the trees.

  • degree lower: weather - the day-to-day state of the atmosphere at a place
