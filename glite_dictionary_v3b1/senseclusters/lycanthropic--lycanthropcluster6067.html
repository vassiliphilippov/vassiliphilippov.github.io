lycanthropic

adjective
wolf-like qualities
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪkənˈθrɑːpɪk / (AmE), / ˌlaɪkənˈθrɒpɪk / (BrE)
having qualities, appearance, or behavior like a wolf, or relating to the transformation into a werewolf (especially in myths or stories)

Examples

Lycanthropic transformation
The actor's lycanthropic transformation in the film amazed the audience.
She wore a mask with a distinctly lycanthropic snout for the costume party.
Legends spoke of villagers who became lycanthropic under the light of the full moon.
The villagers feared the lycanthropic legend that had haunted their town for centuries.
He exhibited lycanthropic behavior whenever the full moon appeared in the sky.
The movie's plot centers on a lycanthropic curse passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: human - relating to or characteristic of people rather than animals
  • synonym: werewolf - a person who transforms into a wolf, especially at a full moon
  • synonym: wolfish - having characteristics or appearance similar to a wolf
