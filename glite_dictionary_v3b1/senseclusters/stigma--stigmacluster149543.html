stigma

noun
mark of shame or disgrace
5.9 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪɡmə / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡmə / (BrE)
A mark of shame or disgrace attached to a person or group because of a particular circumstance, quality, or condition.

Examples

Social stigma
Because of the stigma associated with the disease, many people are reluctant to seek help.
He worked to challenge the stigma surrounding addiction.
There is still a stigma attached to mental health issues in some communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honor - high respect or esteem; opposite of disgrace
