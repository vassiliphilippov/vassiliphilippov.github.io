poller

noun
person conducting surveys
6.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆpoÊŠlÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆpÉ’lÉ™ / (BrE)
a person who conducts opinion polls or surveys to collect people's views or information

Examples

Survey poller
The poller asked each passerby about their favorite local restaurant.
As a poller, she traveled to different neighborhoods to gather public opinions.
The company hired a poller to survey customers about their shopping experiences.

Synonyms

  • pollster
    4.8 7.60/m
    pollster is a professional who runs opinion polls, often about politics, while poller is any person conducting such surveys.
  • poll taker
    3.7 0.05/m
    poll taker is someone whose job is to carry out surveys to measure public opinion, while poller is the general term for such a person.
  • surveyor
    4.1 0.28/m
    surveyor is a person who gathers and analyzes sample data for many studies, while poller focuses on opinion polls of people.
