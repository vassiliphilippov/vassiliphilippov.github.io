in causa

x
Latin legal phrase
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈkaʊzə / (AmE), / ɪn ˈkaʊzə / (BrE)
a Latin legal phrase meaning 'in the case' or 'in the matter of', used to refer to a specific legal case or issue.

Examples

In causa legal case
The judge cited the decision made in causa Brown v. Board of Education.
Legal scholars often discuss the principles established in causa Marbury v. Madison.
The lawyer referenced arguments presented in causa Miller v. California.
