ampliative

adjective
expanding inference
9.9
Pronunciation: /æm.ˈpliː.ə.tɪv/ /; / /æm.ˈplɪ.ə.tɪv/ (AmE), /æm.ˈplɪ.ə.tɪv/ /; / /æm.ˈpliː.ə.tɪv/ (BrE)
describing an inference or argument that extends knowledge beyond what is contained in the premises

Examples

Ampliative reasoning
An ampliative inference allows us to draw new conclusions not strictly contained in the original facts.
Scientists often use ampliative reasoning to develop theories that go beyond observed data.
The philosopher explained that inductive arguments are typically ampliative in nature.
