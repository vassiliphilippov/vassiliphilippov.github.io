malignity

noun
intense ill will; desire to harm
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnəti / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnəti /; / məˈlɪɡnɪti / (BrE)
persistent and intense ill will; a strong desire to cause harm or suffering to others

Examples

Pure malignity toward rivals
His actions were motivated by pure malignity toward his rivals.
The novel portrays the malignity that can lurk beneath a charming exterior.
There was a certain malignity in his words that made everyone uneasy.
Her words dripped with malignity, leaving no doubt about her true feelings.
Stories of malignity and revenge filled the ancient legends they told around the fire.
There was a certain malignity in his glare that made everyone uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benevolence - wishing or doing good to others
  • synonym: malevolence - wishing harm or evil to others
  • synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil
