drag out

verb
prolong unnecessarily
Pronunciation: / dræɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dræɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to make something last longer than necessary, often causing boredom or frustration

Examples

To drag out a meeting
Negotiations were dragged out by both sides, delaying a final agreement.
She tends to drag out her stories, adding irrelevant details.
The meeting was dragged out for hours by unnecessary discussions.
Let’s not drag out this discussion any longer than necessary.
Negotiations can drag out for months without a clear resolution.
The meeting started to drag out by the end, and people grew restless.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prolong - to make something last longer
  • antonym: shorten - to make something shorter or quicker
