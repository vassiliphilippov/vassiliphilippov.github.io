timberline

noun
upper limit of tree growth in mountains
8.7 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪm.bər.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈtɪm.bə.laɪn/ (BrE)
the imaginary line on mountains or in northern regions marking the highest altitude or latitude at which trees are able to grow

Examples

The timberline on mountains
As we climbed higher, we finally reached the timberline, beyond which no trees grew.
Near the timberline, only hardy shrubs and grasses are able to survive.
Snow often covers the ground above the timberline for most of the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tree line - the upper limit where trees grow in mountains or cold regions
