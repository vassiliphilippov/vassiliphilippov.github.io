excludable

adjective
able to be excluded
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːdəbl / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːdəbl / (BrE)
able to be excluded or prevented from being considered, especially in legal or formal contexts

Examples

Excludable evidence
The judge ruled that the evidence was excludable because it was obtained without a warrant.
Certain documents may be excludable from the trial if they violate privacy laws.
The lawyer argued that the testimony was excludable under the current legal standards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admissible - allowed to be considered, especially as evidence
  • synonym: inadmissible - not allowed to be considered, especially as evidence
