Silvanus

proper noun
Roman god of woods and fields
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪlˈveɪnəs / (AmE), / sɪlˈvɑːnəs / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the god of woods, fields, and flocks, often associated with nature and rural life.

Examples

Silvanus god of woods
A statue of Silvanus was discovered among the ruins of an old Roman villa.
In Roman mythology, Silvanus is revered as the guardian of forests and fields.
Many ancient poems celebrate Silvanus as the protector of shepherds and their flocks.
