graceful degradation

noun
controlled decline in function or quality
5.4 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈgreɪs.fəl de.grə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈgreɪs.ʊl de.grə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈgreɪs.fəl de.grə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈgreɪs.ʊl de.grə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a decrease in performance or quality that occurs in a controlled or elegant way, especially the ability of a system to maintain limited functionality when part of it fails

Examples

Graceful degradation in systems
Even with several components malfunctioning, the machine exhibited graceful degradation rather than shutting down completely.
The engineers included graceful degradation so the website would still display basic content during outages.
We aim for graceful degradation in our software, avoiding abrupt crashes when problems arise.

  • antonym: catastrophic failure - sudden and total failure of a system
