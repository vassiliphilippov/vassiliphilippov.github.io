rot

verb
to decay or decompose
1.4 6.78/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ‘t / (AmE), / rÉ’t / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to decay or decompose through the action of bacteria and fungi.

Examples

To rot fruit
If you leave the fruit out in the sun, it will rot quickly.
The old leaves have started rotting on the forest floor.
Too much moisture can cause wood to rot over time.

Synonyms

  • decay
    3.4 1.97/m
    decay describes natural breakdown of organic matter by bacteria and fungi, while rot often suggests a wet, foul breakdown.
  • decompose
    4.4 2.04/m
    decompose means to break organic matter into simpler parts by microbes, while rot usually describes a smelly, visible decay.
  • rot away
    4.2 0.55/m
    rot away means to decay gradually and be destroyed over time, while rot names the general process of decomposition.
  • molder
    7.1 0.20/m
    molder means to decay slowly from moisture or neglect in a quiet way, while rot often implies more active breakdown.
  • biodegrade
    4.4 0.25/m
    biodegrade means to be broken down naturally by living organisms into harmless compounds, while rot refers to organic matter decaying.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - to undergo the process of rotting
  • synonym: decompose - to break down into simpler parts through decay
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something from decaying
