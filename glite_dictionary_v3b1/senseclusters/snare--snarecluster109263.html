snare

noun
a small drum with metal wires across the bottom head
Pronunciation: / snɛr / (AmE), / snɛə / (BrE)
a small drum with wires stretched across the bottom that make a sharp sound, or the wires themselves

Examples

Snare drum
She tuned her snare to get a sharper, brighter sound for the performance.
The drummer played a crisp rhythm on the snare during the solo.
The marching band’s snare was so loud it echoed through the stadium.
She replaced the old snare after noticing it was no longer making a sharp sound.
The drummer adjusted the snare to get a crisper response from his drum kit.
Tightening the snare can significantly change the tone of the drum during a performance.
