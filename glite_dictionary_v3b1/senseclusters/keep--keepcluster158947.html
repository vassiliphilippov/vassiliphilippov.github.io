keep

verb
have or store as a supply
1.0 143/m
Pronunciation: / kip / (AmE), / kiÀêp / (BrE)
to store, have, or retain something for use or as a usual practice

Examples

To keep extra food
She keeps extra canned food in the pantry for unexpected guests.
They kept flashlights and batteries ready for the storm season.
We always keep a first aid kit in the car in case of emergencies.
She always keeps her bicycle in the garage.
We kept the holiday decorations in a box under the stairs.
Where do you keep your keys at home?

Synonyms

  • retain
    3.4 26/m
    retain is more formal and often means you continue to have something or a quality, while keep is everyday and more general.
  • store
    1.0 42/m
    store means putting things away in a safe place for later use, while keep is broader and can mean habitually having or using them.
  • save
    1.0 37/m
    save means setting something aside for future use or to protect it, while keep is more general and ongoing possession or practice.
  • reserve
    3.1 12/m
    reserve means set aside for a particular person or purpose in advance, while keep is a wider, everyday word for having or holding.
  • hold
    1.3 110/m
    hold often means keeping something physically or pausing an action, while keep covers storing, possessing, or doing something as a usual practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discard - to throw something away
  • antonym: lose - to no longer have possession of something
  • synonym: save - to set aside something for future use
  • synonym: store - to keep or accumulate something for future use
