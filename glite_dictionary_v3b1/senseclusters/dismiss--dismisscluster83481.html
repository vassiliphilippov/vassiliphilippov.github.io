dismiss

verb
end or reject a legal case
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪs / (BrE)
to officially end or reject a legal case or petition so it is no longer considered

Examples

Court dismisses a case
If no new facts are presented, the court may dismiss the complaint.
The judge decided to dismiss the case due to insufficient evidence.
The motion to dismiss was granted in the courtroom this morning.
After reviewing the case, the council voted unanimously to dismiss the petition.
The court may dismiss the lawsuit if the claim is found to be invalid.
The judge decided to dismiss the charges due to lack of evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discharge - to officially end or release someone from a duty, legal case, etc.
  • synonym: reject - refuse to accept or consider
  • antonym: uphold - to confirm or support a decision, often in a legal context
