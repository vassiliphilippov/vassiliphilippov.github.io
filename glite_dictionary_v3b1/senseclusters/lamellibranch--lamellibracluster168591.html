lamellibranch

noun
bivalve mollusk with platelike gills
9.8
Pronunciation: / ləˈmɛlɪˌbræŋk / (AmE), / ləˈmɛlɪˌbraŋk / (BrE)
a marine or freshwater mollusk with a soft body and platelike gills, enclosed within two shells hinged together; a bivalve

Examples

A lamellibranch shell
Scientists studied the gill structure of each lamellibranch species in the estuary.
The oyster is a common lamellibranch found in shallow marine waters.
This museum display includes a variety of fossilized lamellibranchs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bivalve - A mollusk with two hinged shells
  • antonym: gastropod - Mollusks such as snails or slugs with a single shell or none
