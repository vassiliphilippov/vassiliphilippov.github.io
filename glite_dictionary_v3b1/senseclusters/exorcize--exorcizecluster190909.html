exorcize

verb
drive out an evil spirit by ritual
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.sɔrˌsaɪz /; / ˈɛksɔrˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɔː.saɪz /; / ˈɛksəsaɪz / (BrE)
to expel an evil spirit or demon from a person or place by prayer, ritual, or religious ceremony

Examples

To exorcize evil spirits
Many religions have specific rituals to exorcize evil spirits from afflicted individuals.
She claimed to have the power to exorcize demons using ancient prayers.
The priest was called to exorcize the haunted house after several strange incidents occurred.
A team was called in to exorcise the demon believed to possess the old building.
The priest attempted to exorcise the haunted house after repeated reports of strange activity.
They believe a shaman can exorcise evil spirits from the afflicted.
