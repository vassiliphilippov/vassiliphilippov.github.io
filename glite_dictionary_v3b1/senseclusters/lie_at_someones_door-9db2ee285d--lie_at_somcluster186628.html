lie at someone's door

verb

Pronunciation: /laɪ ə ˈsʌm.wənz dɔːr/ /; / /laɪ ə ˈsʌm.wʌnz dɔːr/ (AmE), /laɪ ə ˈsʌm.wənz dɔː/ /; / /laɪ ə ˈsʌm.wʌnz dɔː/ (BrE)
(of responsibility, blame, or fault) to be ascribed to someone; to be their responsibility or fault.

Examples

Lie at their door
Responsibility for the failure must lie at their door as they made the key decisions.
The committee decided the error should lie at John's door rather than the team's.
We can't let all the blame lie at her door when others were involved.
