programmatic

adjective
evoking a story or idea
7.0
Pronunciation: /proʊ.ɡræm.ˈmæt.ɪk/ /; / /proʊ.ɡrəm.ˈmæt.ɪk/ (AmE), /prəʊ.ɡræm.ˈmæt.ɪk/ /; / /prəʊ.ɡrəm.ˈmæt.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to music or art that is intended to evoke a story, scene, or idea rather than being purely abstract.

Examples

Programmatic music
The composer wrote a programmatic piece inspired by a mountain landscape.
Many Romantic-era works are highly programmatic, telling stories through music.
Unlike abstract art, programmatic paintings often depict specific events or narratives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstract - not representing a specific story or idea
  • synonym: descriptive - giving details to create a picture or idea
