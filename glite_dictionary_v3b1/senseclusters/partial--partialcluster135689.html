partial

adjective
not complete; only a part
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl / (BrE)
existing or happening only in part; not complete or total

Examples

Partial damage
After the storm, the museum suffered partial damage to its roof.
The report only included a partial list of the available resources.
The scientist observed a partial eclipse through specialized equipment.
A partial eclipse occurs when only a portion of the sun or moon is obscured.
She offered a partial explanation, leaving out key details about what happened.
The committee received only a partial report and requested the remainder of the information.
