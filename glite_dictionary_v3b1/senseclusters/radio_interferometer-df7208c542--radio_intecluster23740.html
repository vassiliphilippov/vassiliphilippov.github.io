radio interferometer

noun
radio telescope using interference patterns
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ ɪn.t̬ɚˌfɪr.əˌmɒt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ɪn.təˌfɪə.rəˌmɒ.tə / (BrE)
A radio telescope consisting of two or more antennas used together to measure interference patterns of incoming radio waves, instead of using a single parabolic antenna.

Examples

A radio interferometer array
A radio interferometer can provide much higher resolution images than a single-dish radio telescope.
Researchers calibrated the radio interferometer before starting the experiment to ensure accurate readings.
The radio interferometer allowed astronomers to observe distant galaxies in greater detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radio telescope - a telescope that detects and collects radio waves from space
