Red Guard

noun
member of radical Chinese youth movement (1960s-70s)
Pronunciation: / rɛd ɡɑrd / (AmE), / rɛd ɡɑːd / (BrE)
a member of a radical Chinese youth movement that supported Mao Zedong and his policies during the Cultural Revolution (1966–1976)

Examples

Red Guard member
During the Cultural Revolution, the Red Guard enforced strict adherence to Maoist ideas throughout China.
Several families were displaced after Red Guard members raided their home.
The influence of the Red Guard dramatically altered the course of Chinese education and culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Maoist - supporter of the doctrines of Mao Zedong
