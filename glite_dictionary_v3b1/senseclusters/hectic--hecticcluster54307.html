hectic

adjective
very busy, excited, or chaotic
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛktɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛktɪk / (BrE)
full of busy activity, excitement, or confusion; marked by intense agitation or emotion.

Examples

A hectic day
After a hectic day at work, she enjoyed a quiet evening at home.
He tried to stay organized despite the hectic atmosphere in the office.
My schedule has been especially hectic this week due to several deadlines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - free from agitation or excitement; peaceful
  • synonym: frantic - conducted in a hurried, excited, or disordered way
