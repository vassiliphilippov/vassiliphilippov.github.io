securities firm

noun
company acting as intermediary in securities trading
2.4 3.5
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈkjʊr.ə.tiːz fɜːrm/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈkjʊə.rə.tiːz fɜːm/ (BrE)
A company that acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers of securities, usually charging a fee or commission for its services.

Examples

A securities firm analyst
He started his career as an analyst at a large securities firm in New York.
Many securities firms expanded their services to include financial consulting and asset management.
The government tightened regulations on securities firms to prevent fraud in the financial markets.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bank - a financial institution that may also deal in securities but provides a broader range of services
  • synonym: brokerage - a company that buys and sells securities on behalf of clients
