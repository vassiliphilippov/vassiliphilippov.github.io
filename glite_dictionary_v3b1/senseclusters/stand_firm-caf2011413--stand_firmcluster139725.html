stand firm

verb
refuse to yield under pressure
1.0
Pronunciation: / stænd fɝm /; / stænd fɝːm / (AmE), / stænd fɜːm / (BrE)
to refuse to change your decision, opinion, or position, even when others try to make you change it

Examples

To stand firm against pressure
He is standing firm against demands for budget cuts.
She decided to stand firm on her beliefs despite criticism from others.
The protesters stood firm even when the police arrived to disperse the crowd.
Despite the criticism, she decided to stand firm in her beliefs.
They stood firm against the pressure to compromise their principles.
We must stand firm if we want to effect real change in the organization.
Despite the mounting pressure, she continued to stand firm in her decision.
It's not always easy to stand firm when others disagree with you.
The company will stand firm on its policy, regardless of criticism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stand one's ground - to maintain your position and not retreat
  • antonym: yield - to give way or surrender to pressure
