reattribution

noun
psychological technique
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
a psychological technique that helps someone change mistaken beliefs about the causes of their symptoms or experiences

Examples

Therapist uses reattribution
The therapist used reattribution to help the patient understand that stress, not illness, was causing her pain.
Through reattribution, clients learn to reconsider the real sources of their symptoms.
A key part of the treatment involved reattribution exercises to challenge mistaken beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cognitive restructuring - a therapy technique for changing negative thought patterns
  • antonym: misattribution - incorrectly identifying the cause of an event or feeling
