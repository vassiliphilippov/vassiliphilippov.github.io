geographics

noun
study of the Earth's surface and human-environment relations
Pronunciation: / dʒiːəˈɡræfɪks / (AmE), / dʒiːəˈɡræfɪks / (BrE)
the study of the Earth's surface, including people's responses to topography, climate, soil, and vegetation

Examples

Study of geographics
Environmental scientists often refer to geographics when analyzing how people adapt to different climates.
The university offers a course in geographics as part of its earth sciences program.
Understanding geographics can help policymakers plan sustainable urban development.
