atop

preposition
on top of
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑp / (AmE), / əˈtɒp / (BrE)
on the top of or above something

Examples

Crown atop his head
A golden crown sat atop the king's head during the ceremony.
The cat perched atop the bookshelf, watching everyone in the room.
Snow piled atop the cars after the heavy winter storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: above - at a higher level or position than something
  • synonym: on - resting or supported by the surface of something
  • antonym: under - directly below something
