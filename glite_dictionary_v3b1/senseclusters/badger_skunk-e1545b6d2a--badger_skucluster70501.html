badger skunk

noun
a large skunk species with white back and tail
Pronunciation: / ˈbædʒər skʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈbadʒə skʌŋk / (BrE)
a large skunk native to southwestern North America and Mexico, characterized by a naked muzzle and white back and tail

Examples

A badger skunk animal
Researchers observed a badger skunk foraging for food along the rocky terrain.
The badger skunk can be found throughout arid regions of southwestern North America.
Unlike other skunks, the badger skunk has a distinctively naked muzzle and a white tail.
