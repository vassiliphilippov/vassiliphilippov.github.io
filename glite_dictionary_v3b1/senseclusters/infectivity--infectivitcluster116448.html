infectivity

noun
ability to cause infection
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɛkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the ability of a disease or microorganism to spread and cause infection in hosts

Examples

The infectivity of virus
Scientists are studying the infectivity of the virus to understand how quickly it can spread.
The infectivity of certain bacteria makes them difficult to control in hospitals.
Public health measures aim to reduce the infectivity of contagious diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagiousness - the quality of being able to be transmitted from one person to another
  • degree higher: virulence - the severity or harmfulness of a disease
