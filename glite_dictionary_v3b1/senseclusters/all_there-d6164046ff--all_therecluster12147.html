all there

adjective
fully present or evident
Pronunciation: / ɔl ðɛr / (AmE), / ɔːl ðeə / (BrE)
fully present, available, or evident; not lacking in any essential quality or attribute.

Examples

To be all there
After his illness, it took months before his energy was all there again.
Her attention during the meeting was all there, which impressed her boss.
I trust his judgment because his sense of responsibility is all there.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absent-minded - lacking concentration or focus; often forgetful
