variable quantity

noun
changeable amount or value
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriəbl ˈkwɑntəti / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbl ˈkwɒntɪti / (BrE)
a quantity that can take on any value within a given set, often used in mathematics and science to represent an unknown or changeable amount

Examples

A variable quantity in math
In this experiment, temperature is treated as a variable quantity that can be adjusted as needed.
Scientists often measure how a variable quantity changes over time.
The value of x in the equation represents a variable quantity rather than a fixed number.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constant - a value that does not change
