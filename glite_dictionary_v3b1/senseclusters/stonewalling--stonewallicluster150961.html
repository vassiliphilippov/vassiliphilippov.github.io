stonewalling

noun
deliberate stalling or refusal to cooperate
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊnˌwɑlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌwɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of deliberately stalling or delaying, especially by refusing to answer questions or cooperate.

Examples

Persistent stonewalling
Her persistent stonewalling made it impossible to get a straight answer in the meeting.
Political stonewalling often leads to lengthy delays in passing important legislation.
The company's stonewalling during the investigation frustrated regulators and the public alike.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cooperation - the act of working together toward a common goal
  • synonym: obstruction - the act of obstructing or hindering progress
