renovate

verb
restore or improve (especially a building)
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛnəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛnəʊveɪt / (BrE)
to repair and improve something (especially a building or room) so it is in good condition or looks new again

Examples

To renovate a house
She hired a contractor to renovate her kitchen with new cabinets and appliances.
The museum will close for a year to renovate its main gallery.
They decided to renovate the old farmhouse rather than tear it down.
After buying the house, she decided to renovate the kitchen and bathrooms.
The city is investing millions to renovate its historic buildings downtown.
They plan to renovate the old library next year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demolish - to completely destroy a building
  • antonym: dilapidate - to bring into a state of decay or partial ruin
  • synonym: refurbish - to make clean, bright, or new again
  • synonym: restore - to bring back to a former condition
