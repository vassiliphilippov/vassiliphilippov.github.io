worm fence

noun
zigzag rail fence; split-rail fence
8.6
Pronunciation: / wɜrm fɛns / (AmE), / wɜːm fɛns / (BrE)
A fence made of rails laid in a zigzag pattern, typical of early American rural areas.

Examples

A worm fence
A worm fence requires no nails and is often seen in traditional American landscapes.
The old farmhouse was surrounded by a worm fence that gave it a rustic charm.
They spent the afternoon repairing the damaged section of the worm fence along the property line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: split-rail fence - a fence made from timber logs split lengthwise into rails
