fourth dimension

noun
the additional dimension beyond length, width, and height
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrθ dɪˈmɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθ dɪˈmɛnʃən / (BrE)
in physics and mathematics, a dimension in addition to the three spatial dimensions, often representing time or a spatial coordinate, needed to define an event or object completely

Examples

The fourth dimension in physics
In Einstein's theory of relativity, time is treated as the fourth dimension of space-time.
Mathematicians often discuss the fourth dimension when working with advanced geometry.
The concept of a fourth dimension can be difficult to visualize using only our everyday experience.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: three dimensions - length, width, and height; usual spatial coordinates
