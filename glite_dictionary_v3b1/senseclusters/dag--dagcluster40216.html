dag

noun
decorative flap on garment edge
Pronunciation: / dæɡ / (AmE), / dæɡ / (BrE)
A decorative flap or hanging point along the edge of a garment, often seen in medieval clothing.

Examples

Medieval dag trim
Medieval tailors would often cut dags into the fabric to create an ornamental effect.
The jester's costume was adorned with colorful dags along the sleeves and hem.
The noblewoman's gown featured elaborate velvet dags that fluttered as she danced.
