ship money

noun
historical English tax for naval defense
Pronunciation: / ʃɪp ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ʃɪp ˈmʌni / (BrE)
a tax levied in England, especially in the 17th century, to raise funds for naval defense

Examples

The ship money tax
Historians regard the controversy over ship money as a key factor leading to the English Civil War.
The court ruled that the Crown's right to levy ship money was not absolute.
The imposition of ship money by Charles I was widely unpopular and contributed to political unrest.
