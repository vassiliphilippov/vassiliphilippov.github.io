bring forward

verb
move something forward
1.6
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to move something to a position farther forward or closer to the front

Examples

To bring forward a chair
Could you please bring forward your chair so we have more space behind you?
She brought forward her suitcase to make room for others boarding the train.
The manager asked to bring forward the meeting table by a few feet.
