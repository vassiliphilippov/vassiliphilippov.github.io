Ledum groenlandicum

noun
evergreen North American shrub used for tea
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈliːdəm ˌɡroʊnlænˈdɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈliːdəm ˌɡrəʊnlænˈdɪkəm / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to eastern North America, with white or creamy bell-shaped flowers and dark green hairy leaves, historically used to make tea during the American Revolution.

Examples

Ledum groenlandicum shrub
