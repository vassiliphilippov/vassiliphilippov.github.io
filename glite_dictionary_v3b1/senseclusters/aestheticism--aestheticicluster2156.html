aestheticism

noun
art for beauty's sake
Pronunciation: / ɛsˈθɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / iːsˈθɛtɪsɪzəm /; / iːsˈθetɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or movement in art and literature that values beauty and artistic form above practical, social, or moral concerns

Examples

Aestheticism movement
The principles of aestheticism influenced many painters and writers in the late nineteenth century.
Oscar Wilde was a famous supporter of aestheticism in English literature.
Some critics argue that aestheticism ignores important social issues in favor of beauty.
The movement of aestheticism influenced many artists in the late nineteenth century.
Her dedication to aestheticism meant she valued beauty above all else in her designs.
Critics argued that aestheticism ignored important social and moral issues in art.

Related Terms

  • synonym: art for art's sake - the belief that art should be valued for its own beauty
  • synonym: hedonism - the belief that pleasure is the most important goal in life
  • antonym: utilitarianism - the belief that art should serve a practical or moral purpose
