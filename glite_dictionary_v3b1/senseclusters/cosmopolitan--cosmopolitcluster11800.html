cosmopolitan

adjective
international; familiar with many cultures
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtən / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən / (BrE)
including or open to people, ideas, or influences from many different countries; having a global outlook

Examples

Cosmopolitan city life
Her cosmopolitan outlook helped her appreciate and adapt to cultures wherever she traveled.
New York is known for being a truly cosmopolitan city, with residents from every continent.
The restaurant's cosmopolitan menu features dishes inspired by cuisines from around the world.
Her cosmopolitan outlook made her comfortable living in many different countries.
It was a cosmopolitan conference, attracting experts from around the globe.
The city has a cosmopolitan atmosphere, with residents from nearly every continent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parochial - limited or narrow in scope, especially regarding local interests
  • antonym: provincial - narrow-minded or limited in outlook, especially from a limited or local perspective
  • synonym: universal - applying everywhere or in all cases
  • synonym: worldly - experienced and knowledgeable about the world
