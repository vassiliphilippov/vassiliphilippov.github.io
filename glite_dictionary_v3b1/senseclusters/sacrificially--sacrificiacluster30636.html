sacrificially

adverb
giving up for advantage
Pronunciation: / ˌsækrəˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / ˌsækrɪˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves giving up something valuable or important for a greater benefit or for others

Examples

Act sacrificially for others
The chess player moved sacrificially to create an opening for a checkmate.
She acted sacrificially, giving up her time to help her friend succeed.
The team played sacrificially, focusing on the group's victory rather than individual achievements.
He sacrificially gave up his free time to care for his sick friend.
The volunteers worked sacrificially to help those affected by the disaster.
Parents often act sacrificially to provide a better life for their children.
