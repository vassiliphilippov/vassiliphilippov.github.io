subjectivization

noun
semantic shift to subjectivity
9.1
Pronunciation: / səbˌdʒɛktɪvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˌdʒɛktɪvaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a word or expression develops a meaning that reflects the speaker's attitudes or viewpoint rather than objective facts

Examples

Subjectivization of adverbs
Linguists have observed the subjectivization of certain adverbs over time.
The subjectivization of language can reveal how speakers express personal attitudes.
Researchers analyze the subjectivization of modal verbs in different languages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subjectification - the process of making something more subjective in meaning
