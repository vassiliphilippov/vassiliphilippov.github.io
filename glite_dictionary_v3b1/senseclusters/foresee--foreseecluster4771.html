foresee

verb
predict or anticipate
3.9 5.45/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrÀàsi / (AmE), / f…îÀêÀàsiÀê / (BrE)
to know, expect, or imagine something before it happens; to anticipate or predict

Examples

To foresee the future
It is difficult to foresee how technology will change our lives in the next decade.
No one could foresee that the peaceful protest would turn violent.
She foresaw the potential risks in investing in that company.

Synonyms

  • predict
    3.0 73/m
    predict states what will happen based on evidence or data, while foresee means to imagine or know something beforehand.
  • anticipate
    3.2 8.77/m
    anticipate expects or prepares for something in advance, while foresee focuses on seeing or imagining events before they happen.
  • forecast
    2.5 5.04/m
    forecast predicts future events using data or signs, while foresee is a general sense of knowing or imagining what will come.
  • expect
    1.0 267/m
    expect thinks something will happen and often waits for it, while foresee suggests seeing or knowing it before it occurs.
  • foretell
    7.0 2.60/m
    foretell says future events often from signs or gifts, while foresee is a more general, non-magical sense of foreseeing things.
  • prognosticate
    9.3 0.21/m
    prognosticate makes formal or learned predictions from signs or data, while foresee stays as a plain verb for imagining future events.
  • foreknow
    9.7 0.05/m
    foreknow means to have prior knowledge, often in old or religious texts, while foresee is a common verb for predicting or imagining events.
