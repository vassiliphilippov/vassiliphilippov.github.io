anacreontic

noun
light lyric poet
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈkriːɑːntɪk / (AmE), / ˌænəˈkriːɒntɪk / (BrE)
a poet or writer who composes light, lyrical poems about love, wine, or similar pleasures

Examples

Famous anacreontic
The famous anacreontic delighted audiences with verses celebrating the joys of wine and love.
As an anacreontic, she often wrote playful poems about the pleasures of life.
Many 18th-century poets admired the style of the original anacreontic and tried to imitate his themes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lyricist - a person who writes the words to songs or poems
  • antonym: satirist - a writer who uses humor or irony to criticize
