subacute sclerosing panencephalitis

noun
rare chronic brain inflammation from measles
Pronunciation: / sʌbəˌkjuːt sklɛˈroʊzɪŋ ˌpænˌɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / sʌbəˌkjuːt sklɪˈrəʊzɪŋ ˌpanˌɛnˌsɛfɪˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A rare, chronic, progressive brain inflammation caused by the measles virus, usually affecting children and adolescents, and often leading to death within a few years.

Examples

Subacute sclerosing panencephalitis disease
Subacute sclerosing panencephalitis is usually fatal, with symptoms worsening progressively over time.
Doctors are working to increase awareness about subacute sclerosing panencephalitis and its link to early childhood measles.
The patient was diagnosed with subacute sclerosing panencephalitis several years after a measles infection.
