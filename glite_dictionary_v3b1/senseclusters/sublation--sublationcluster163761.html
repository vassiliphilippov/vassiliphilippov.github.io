sublation

noun
integration of opposites
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌbˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of resolving a contradiction by integrating and preserving opposing ideas, especially in philosophy

Examples

Hegelian sublation
In Hegelian philosophy, sublation is key to understanding how contradictions are overcome.
The debate ended with a sublation of both viewpoints into a new, unified perspective.
Many philosophical systems use sublation to explain the development of complex ideas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contradiction - a situation in which two or more things are opposed to each other
  • synonym: synthesis - the combination of ideas to form a theory or system
