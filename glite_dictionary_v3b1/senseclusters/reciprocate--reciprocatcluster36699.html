reciprocate

verb
respond in kind; give or feel in return
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɪprəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkeɪt / (BrE)
to respond to an action, feeling, or gesture with a similar one; to give, feel, or do in return

Examples

To reciprocate a favor
If you help your colleagues, they are more likely to reciprocate when you need assistance.
She felt gratitude and tried to reciprocate his kindness with a thoughtful gift.
When he smiled at her, she was happy to reciprocate with a warm smile of her own.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repay - to give back something in compensation or thanks
  • synonym: return - to give back or do something in response
