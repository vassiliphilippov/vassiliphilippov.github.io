preshrinking

noun
fabric pre-treatment
4.2
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈʃrɪŋ.kɪŋ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈʃrɪŋ.kɪŋ/ /; / /prə.ˈʃrɪŋ.kɪŋ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈʃrɪŋ.kɪŋ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈʃrɪŋ.kɪŋ/ /; / /prə.ˈʃrɪŋ.kɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of shrinking fabric before it is made into clothing to prevent further shrinkage after washing

Examples

Preshrinking fabric
Many manufacturers use preshrinking to ensure that garments keep their size after washing.
The preshrinking of fabric helps prevent clothes from becoming too tight after the first wash.
If a fabric has undergone preshrinking, it is less likely to shrink in the laundry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prewashing - washing fabric before use to remove impurities or shrinkage
  • antonym: shrinking - the process of becoming smaller in size, especially after washing
