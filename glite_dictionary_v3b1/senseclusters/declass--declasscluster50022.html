declass

verb
to lower social status
Pronunciation: / diːˈklæs / (AmE), / diːˈklɑːs / (BrE)
to reduce someone or something to a lower social or official class or status

Examples

To declass a noble
The revolution threatened to declass many members of the old aristocracy.
After the scandal, the official was declassed and lost his privileges.
Economic changes can declass entire communities, forcing them into poverty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demote - to move someone to a lower rank or position
  • antonym: promote - to raise someone to a higher rank or position
