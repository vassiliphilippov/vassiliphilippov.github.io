driving wheel

noun
wheel receiving power to drive a vehicle
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪvɪŋ wiːl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ wiːl / (BrE)
a wheel on a motor vehicle that receives power from the engine and transmits it to the ground, propelling the vehicle forward or backward.

Examples

A driving wheel on a car
Many trains rely on a large central driving wheel to transmit the engine's power to the tracks.
Replacing a damaged driving wheel is essential for the safe operation of any vehicle.
The rear driving wheel of the car lost traction on the icy road.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steering wheel - wheel used by the driver to steer a vehicle
