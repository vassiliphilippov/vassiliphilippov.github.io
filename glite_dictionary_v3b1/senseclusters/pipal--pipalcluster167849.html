pipal

noun
sacred Indian fig tree (Ficus religiosa)
6.7 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpiː.pəl/ /; / /ˈpɪp.əl/ (AmE), /ˈpiː.pəl/ /; / /ˈpɪp.əl/ (BrE)
A large fig tree native to India (Ficus religiosa), valued for its longevity and sacred status in Buddhism; unlike the banyan, it lacks prop roots.

Examples

The pipal tree
Buddhist monks gathered daily beneath the pipal to meditate in the tranquil ambiance.
Many people tie strings around the trunk of the pipal as part of religious ceremonies.
The old village square is dominated by a majestic pipal tree, providing shade for everyone.
