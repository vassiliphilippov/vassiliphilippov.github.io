cute

adjective
attractive in a pretty or endearing way
1.0 37/m
Pronunciation: / kjuÀêt / (AmE), / kjuÀêt / (BrE)
attractive in a charming, pretty, or endearing way, especially because of smallness or youthfulness

Examples

A cute puppy
Everyone thought the puppy was cute with its big eyes and floppy ears.
She wore a cute dress to the party last night.
Their new apartment is small but really cute and cozy.

Synonyms

  • adorable
    1.0 8.63/m
    adorable inspires strong affection for small, babylike things, while cute is more general and often lighter in tone.
  • winsome
    7.8 0.49/m
    winsome feels childlike and innocent in a gentle way, while cute often highlights smallness or youthful charm.
  • endearing
    3.5 1.88/m
    endearing makes people feel warm and fond, while cute stresses outward smallness or playful charm in appearance.
  • charming
    1.0 16/m
    charming pleases people with style or manner, while cute usually implies small, youthful, or sweet physical looks.
  • pretty
    1.0 38/m
    pretty shows a delicate, graceful beauty mainly in looks, while cute adds innocence, smallness, or a playful feel.
  • lovely
    1.1 0.38/m
    lovely brings a warm, pleasant feeling and often adult beauty, while cute focuses on charm from smallness or youth.

  • antonym: ugly - unpleasant or repulsive in appearance
