paragram

noun
pun by altering letters of a word
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈpærəɡram / (BrE)
A pun or play on words created by altering the letters of a word, especially the initial letter, often for humorous or rhetorical effect.

Examples

A witty paragram
A witty paragram can transform a simple phrase into a playful joke.
She used a paragram by altering the first letter of 'friend' to create an entirely new meaning.
The poet delighted the audience with a clever paragram that changed 'lover' to 'loafer.'
