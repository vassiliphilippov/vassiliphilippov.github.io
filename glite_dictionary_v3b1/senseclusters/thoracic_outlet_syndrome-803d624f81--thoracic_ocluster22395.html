thoracic outlet syndrome

noun
nerve compression in shoulder causing arm symptoms
7.5
Pronunciation: / θɔˈræsɪk ˈaʊtlɪt sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / θɒˈræsɪk ˈaʊtlɪt ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by compression of nerves or blood vessels between the collarbone and first rib, resulting in pain, tingling, or numbness in the arm and fingers.

Examples

Thoracic outlet syndrome pain
Physical therapy is a common treatment for thoracic outlet syndrome.
She was diagnosed with thoracic outlet syndrome after experiencing persistent numbness in her right hand.
Symptoms of thoracic outlet syndrome may worsen when the arms are raised above the head.
