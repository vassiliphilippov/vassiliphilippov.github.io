repressible

adjective
can be inhibited
Pronunciation: / rɪˈprɛsəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛsəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being inhibited or switched off by a regulatory molecule or mechanism, especially in gene expression or biochemical processes

Examples

Repressible gene
Certain genes are repressible and only become active when specific conditions are met.
The scientist studied a repressible enzyme that could be turned off by a regulatory protein.
In bacteria, some metabolic pathways are repressible depending on the presence of end products.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inducible - capable of being activated or turned on by a specific stimulus
  • synonym: suppressible - able to be stopped or reduced in activity
