scree

noun
slope of loose rocks at cliff base
9.1 0.54/m
Pronunciation: / skri / (AmE), / skri / (BrE)
A slope of loose, broken rocks or small stones that accumulates at the base of a cliff or steep rocky slope.

Examples

A scree slope
A field of scree formed at the foot of the granite cliffs over the centuries.
Climbers had to cross a dangerous section of scree before reaching the summit.
Rainfall caused the scree to shift, making the path below treacherous.

Synonyms

  • talus
    9.3 0.54/m
    talus is a sloping mass formed by falling rock fragments, while scree is the loose stones that collect at a cliff base.

Related Terms

  • synonym: talus - an accumulation of broken rock fragments at the base of a cliff or slope
