noble-minded

adjective
having high moral or intellectual ideals
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊbəlˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˌnəʊbəlˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Having high moral or intellectual ideals; displaying elevated principles or character.

Examples

Noble-minded person
Her noble-minded actions during the crisis earned her universal respect.
In literature, many heroes are portrayed as noble-minded individuals who sacrifice for the greater good.
The leader was admired for his noble-minded commitment to justice and equality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: base-minded - having or showing ignoble motives or low moral standards
  • synonym: exalted - elevated in rank, character, or status
  • synonym: lofty - of imposing height; of a high moral or intellectual level
