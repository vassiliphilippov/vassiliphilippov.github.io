inward-moving

adjective
moving toward the center or axis
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnwərd ˈmuːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnwəd ˈmuːvɪŋ / (BrE)
moving or directed toward the center or axis, especially while spinning or traveling in a curve.

Examples

Inward-moving force
A car taking a sharp turn can experience an inward-moving force pulling it toward the center of the curve.
The inward-moving current carried small fish closer to the riverbank.
The hurricane's inward-moving winds gained strength as they approached the eye of the storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outward-moving - moving or directed away from the center or axis
