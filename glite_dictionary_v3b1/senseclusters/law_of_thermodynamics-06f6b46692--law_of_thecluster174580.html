law of thermodynamics

noun
principle describing energy relationships in physics
Pronunciation: / lɔː əv ˌθɜːrməˌdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˌθɜːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
any of the fundamental principles in physics that describe the relationships between different forms of energy and how energy moves in a closed system

Examples

The law of thermodynamics
Students often struggle to grasp the implications of the law of thermodynamics in their physics classes.
The law of thermodynamics explains how energy is conserved within a closed system.
The development of steam engines was crucially influenced by discoveries related to the law of thermodynamics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: first law of thermodynamics - conservation of energy principle in thermodynamics
  • synonym: second law of thermodynamics - law concerning entropy and energy dispersal
  • synonym: third law of thermodynamics - law stating the entropy of a perfect crystal at absolute zero is zero
