bodyguard

verb
accompany and protect from harm
6.0 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ëdiÀå…°…ërd / (AmE), / Ààb…ídiÀå…°…ëÀêd / (BrE)
to accompany and protect someone from physical harm, usually as a bodyguard

Examples

To bodyguard a celebrity
He hired a professional to bodyguard him during the conference.
They often bodyguard diplomats to ensure their safety abroad.
Two security officers bodyguarded the celebrity as she left the hotel.

Synonyms

  • safeguard
    3.6 0.12/m
    safeguard means to escort and protect someone formally to ensure their safety, while bodyguard protects a person from physical harm.
  • guard
    1.0 15/m
    guard watches over and protects people or places against danger or theft, while bodyguard focuses on close protection from physical harm.
  • escort
    4.0 11/m
    escort goes with someone to protect or guide them, often as a courtesy, while bodyguard stays close to prevent physical harm.
