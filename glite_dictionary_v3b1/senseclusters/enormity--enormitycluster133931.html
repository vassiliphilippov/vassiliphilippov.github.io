enormity

noun
great size or extent; vastness
8.1 0.76/m
Pronunciation: / …™Ààn…îrm…ôti / (AmE), / …™Ààn…îÀêm…™ti / (BrE)
the great size or extent of something; vastness (usage sometimes considered incorrect, as 'enormity' more often refers to wickedness).

Examples

The enormity of the task
Historians debated the enormity of the challenge faced by early settlers.
She was overwhelmed by the enormity of the mountain range stretching before her.
The enormity of the project became clear when we saw the blueprints.

  • enormousness
    4.7 0.01/m

  • hugeness
    1.9 0.10/m

  • tremendousness
    6.9 0.01/m
    tremendousness suggests something impresses or causes awe by its large size, while enormity focuses on simple great size.
  • magnitude
    4.7 15/m
    magnitude refers to size or importance compared to other things, while enormity points mainly to great size.
  • scale
    2.6 88/m

  • largeness
    3.2 0.14/m
    largeness is a plain word for being big in size or scope, while enormity sounds stronger and more dramatic.
  • massiveness
    5.4 0.08/m
