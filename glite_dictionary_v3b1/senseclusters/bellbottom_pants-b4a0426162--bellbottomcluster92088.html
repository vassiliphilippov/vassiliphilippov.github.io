bellbottom pants

noun
pants with flared legs
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛlˌbɑːtəm ˌpænts / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌbɒtəm ˌpænts / (BrE)
Trousers with legs that widen markedly from the knee downward, often associated with sailors and a popular fashion trend in the 1960s and 1970s.

Examples

Bellbottom pants style
During the 1970s, bellbottom pants became popular among both men and women.
She wore her favorite bellbottom pants to the retro-themed party last night.
The navy's sailors are often depicted in classic uniforms with bellbottom pants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flared pants - trousers with legs that widen out from the knee downward
  • antonym: straight-leg pants - pants with legs that remain straight from hip to hem
