phagosome

noun
cellular vesicle
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfæɡəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈfæɡəsəʊm / (BrE)
a vesicle inside a cell that engulfs and digests particles, such as bacteria or debris

Examples

A phagosome inside cell
A phagosome forms when a white blood cell engulfs a harmful bacterium.
The phagosome merges with a lysosome to digest the captured particles.
Researchers observed the movement of the phagosome under a powerful microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endosome - a membrane-bound compartment inside eukaryotic cells
  • synonym: lysosome - an organelle containing digestive enzymes in cells
