fishability

noun
ease of catching fish
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪʃəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌfɪʃəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
how good or suitable a place or conditions are for fishing

Examples

Fishability of lakes
Scientists assess the fishability of different lakes to help manage local fish populations.
Changes in water temperature can affect the fishability of a river throughout the year.
The new fishing gear improved the fishability of offshore areas for commercial boats.
Scientists monitor the fishability of rivers to ensure they can support native fish species.
Restoration projects aim to improve the fishability of degraded lakes and streams.
Changes in water temperature can greatly affect the fishability of a habitat.
The river's excellent fishability makes it a popular spot for anglers year-round.

Local guides rate the lake's fishability as some of the best in the region.

  • antonym: fishless - lacking fish or unable to support fish populations
  • synonym: habitat suitability - the degree to which an environment supports a particular species
  • antonym: unfishable - not suitable for fishing
