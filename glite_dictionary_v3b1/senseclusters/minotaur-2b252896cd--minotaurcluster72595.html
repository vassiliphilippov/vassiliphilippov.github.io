Minotaur

noun
mythical creature with bull's head and man's body
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnəˌtɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɪnətɔː / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a mythical creature with the head of a bull and the body of a man, slain by Theseus.

Examples

The Minotaur in mythology
In modern stories, the Minotaur is often reimagined as a symbol of inner fears.
Many ancient vases depict the battle between Theseus and the Minotaur.
The hero ventured into the labyrinth to confront the Minotaur.
