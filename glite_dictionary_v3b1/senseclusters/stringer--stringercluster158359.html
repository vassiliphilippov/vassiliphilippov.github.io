stringer

noun
longitudinal structural brace in vehicles/boats
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪŋər /; / ˈstrɪŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋə / (BrE)
a long, narrow piece of wood or metal used as a structural support in things like aircraft, boats, buildings, or stairways

Examples

Stair stringer
Engineers inspected every stringer inside the hull for signs of corrosion.
The aluminum stringer adds rigidity to the airplane's fuselage without much extra weight.
The damaged stringer was replaced during the boat's restoration to ensure structural integrity.
A broken stringer can weaken the entire structure of the staircase.
The carpenter installed a stringer along the base to support the new railing.
They used steel stringers to reinforce the bridge's walkway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used in construction
  • antonym: rib - crosswise structural member in aircraft or boats
