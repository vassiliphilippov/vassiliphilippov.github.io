prize

noun
goods or property taken illegally
Pronunciation: / praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)
goods, money, or property taken unlawfully, especially as a result of piracy or during war

Examples

Take prize goods
During the war, soldiers sometimes seized prize from enemy territory.
He was arrested with a trunk full of prize from the raid.
The pirates divided the prize among themselves after capturing the ship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loot - goods taken by force, especially in war
  • synonym: spoils - goods taken from an enemy in war
