mikvah

noun
Jewish ritual bath for purification
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪkvə / (AmE), / ˈmɪkvə / (BrE)
A ritual bath used for purification by Orthodox Jews on certain occasions, such as before the Sabbath or after menstruation.

Examples

The mikvah ritual bath
Before the wedding, she immersed herself in the mikvah as part of the traditional purification process.
Observant Jews visit the mikvah regularly, especially before important religious holidays.
Some communities have built a new mikvah to serve the growing population.
