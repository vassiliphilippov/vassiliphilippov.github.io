mitochondrial

adjective
relating to mitochondria
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪtəˈkɑːndriəl / (AmE), / ˌmɪtəˈkɒndrɪəl / (BrE)
relating to mitochondria (the parts of cells that produce energy, or their genes and DNA)

Examples

mitochondrial DNA
The mitochondrial DNA is inherited almost exclusively from the mother.
Scientists study mitochondrial genes to trace human ancestry and migration patterns.
A mitochondrial disorder can affect the body's ability to produce energy.
Researchers are investigating mitochondrial disorders that affect how cells generate energy.
The mitochondrial DNA is inherited only from the mother in most animals.
Certain mitochondrial diseases can lead to muscle weakness and fatigue.
