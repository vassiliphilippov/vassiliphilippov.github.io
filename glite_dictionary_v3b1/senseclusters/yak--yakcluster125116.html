yak

noun
large long-haired ox of Tibet
Pronunciation: / jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
a large, long-haired wild or domesticated ox native to Tibet and central Asia, valued for its milk, meat, and wool

Examples

A yak herd
The farmer raised several yaks for their milk and warm wool.
Tourists were amazed to see a domesticated yak carrying supplies up the mountain.
Wild yaks roam the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ox - domesticated bovine animal, often used for draft work
