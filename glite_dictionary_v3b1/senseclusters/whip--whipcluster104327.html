whip

verb
strike with a quick, whipping motion
Pronunciation: / wɪp / (AmE), / wɪp / (BrE)
to strike, hit, or punish someone or something with a whip or with a sudden, forceful motion (including harsh criticism)

Examples

To whip a horse
Rain whipped across his face as he walked along the pier.
She whipped her scarf off and tossed it onto the chair.
The wind whipped the branches against the windows all night long.
After the poor performance, the coach publicly whipped the team during the press conference.
During the debate, she whipped her opponent's policies with sharp criticism.
The editor did not hesitate to whip the author for factual errors in the article.
He threatened to whip the boys if they disobeyed his orders again.
The cruel overseer would whip the workers for the slightest mistakes.
The old laws once allowed teachers to whip students as punishment in schools.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flog - to beat someone with a whip or stick as punishment
  • synonym: lash - to strike forcefully with a thin, flexible object
  • antonym: praise - express approval or admiration
  • synonym: scold - reprimand or criticize angrily
  • degree lower: spank - to hit someone, especially a child, on their bottom with an open hand as punishment
