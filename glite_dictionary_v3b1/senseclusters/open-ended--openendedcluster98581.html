open-ended

adjective
allowing changes or lacking a definite limit
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊpənˈɛndɪd /; / ˌoʊpənˈɛndəd /; / ˌoʊ.pənˈɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊpənˈɛndɪd /; / ˌəʊ.pənˈɛn.dɪd / (BrE)
without a fixed limit, conclusion, or answer; allowing for continuation, change, or unrestricted response

Examples

An open-ended question
Her research is intentionally open-ended, allowing for new discoveries to shape its direction.
The contract is open-ended, so either party can make changes as needed.
We entered into an open-ended agreement that could be adjusted depending on circumstances.
She preferred giving open-ended assignments to foster creativity among her students.
The survey consisted mostly of open-ended questions that encouraged thoughtful answers.
Their conversation remained open-ended, leaving room for future ideas and plans.
Her plans for the future are open-ended, with many possibilities still available.
The teacher encouraged open-ended questions that lead to deeper discussion.
We had an open-ended contract that allowed either party to make changes as needed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closed-ended - having a fixed or restricted set of possible responses
  • antonym: fixed-term - having a definite, unchangeable period or structure
  • antonym: restricted - having definite limits or boundaries
  • synonym: unlimited - without any limits or restrictions
