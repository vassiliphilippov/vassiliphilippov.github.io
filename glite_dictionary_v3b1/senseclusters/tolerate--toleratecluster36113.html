tolerate

verb
allow or accept without interference
3.2 5.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ëÀêl…ôÀåre…™t / (AmE), / Ààt…íl…ôre…™t /; / Ààt…íl…ôÀåre…™t / (BrE)
to allow or accept something (such as ideas, practices, or people) even if you do not agree with it

Examples

To tolerate different opinions
She has learned to tolerate the noise from the busy street outside her apartment.
The company does not tolerate harassment in any form.
We cannot tolerate such unfair treatment of our employees.
In a diverse society, it is important to tolerate different cultures and traditions.
She does not tolerate any form of discrimination in her classroom.
They learned to tolerate each other's opinions even when they disagreed.

Synonyms

  • abide
    4.6 2.59/m
    abide is used to mean endure or put up with something unpleasant, while tolerate means allow or accept despite disagreement.
  • accept
    3.5 65/m
    accept is to resign yourself and live with an unpleasant situation, while tolerate is to allow or bear things you disagree with.
  • allow
    1.0 348/m
    allow means give permission or let something happen freely, while tolerate means put up with things you do not agree with.
  • permit
    1.5 41/m
    permit is formal permission often by rules or law, while tolerate means accept or endure things you disagree with personally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forbid - to refuse to allow something
  • synonym: permit - to allow something to happen; grant permission
