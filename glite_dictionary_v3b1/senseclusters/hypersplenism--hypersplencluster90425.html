hypersplenism

noun
enlarged spleen with reduced blood cells
3.7 9.4
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.ˈspliː.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /haɪ.pər.ˈsplen.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈspliː.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /haɪ.pə.ˈsplen.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
An abnormal condition in which the spleen is enlarged and leads to a reduction in one or more types of blood cells, often associated with various underlying disorders.

Examples

Severe hypersplenism condition
Certain blood disorders can be complicated by the onset of hypersplenism.
Her persistent fatigue was traced to hypersplenism, which required further investigation and treatment.
The patient was diagnosed with hypersplenism after tests showed his spleen was enlarged and his blood cell count was low.
