flat

adjective
lying stretched out at full length
1.6 1.0
Pronunciation: /flæt/ (AmE), /flæt/ (BrE)
having a surface that is level, smooth, or not sloping; not raised or uneven

Examples

Flat surface
After tripping on the curb, Marcus ended up flat on the sidewalk.
He was knocked flat by the sudden gust of wind.
She lay flat on the grass to watch the clouds drift by.
Some species of fish have flat bodies adapted for life on the sea floor.
The flat leafstalk provides more surface area for light absorption.
This plant can be recognized by its flat, ribbon-like stems.
Please place the painting on a flat surface to dry.
The countryside here is very flat, with few hills or valleys.
The ground was completely flat after they finished the construction work.
