see-through

adjective
thin enough to be transparent
3.9 0.61/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀåŒ∏ru / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêÀåŒ∏ruÀê / (BrE)
(of cloth or material) so thin that it allows light to pass through and things can be seen through it.

Examples

See-through fabric
He preferred see-through packaging so customers could view the product inside.
She wore a see-through blouse that revealed the outline of her camisole.
The curtains were so see-through that you could easily see outside.

Synonyms

  • transparent
    3.2 6.97/m
    transparent lets you see objects clearly through it, while see-through describes thin fabric that lets shapes show.
  • sheer
    4.0 1.41/m
    sheer is very thin cloth often used in fashion, while see-through is a plain term for fabric you can see through.
  • diaphanous
    9.6 0.48/m
    diaphanous is light, delicate, and almost transparent in a poetic way, while see-through is direct and plain.
  • gauzy
    6.7 0.99/m
    gauzy is thin and soft like medical gauze and often lets light pass partially, while see-through means you can see objects through fabric.
  • gauze-like
    7.1 0.00/m
    gauze-like feels coarse and open like gauze and often looks semi-transparent, while see-through usually means clearly visible behind.
  • filmy
    5.8 0.51/m
    filmy is covered with a thin, cloudy layer that lets some light through, while see-through means you can clearly see through.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • degree lower: translucent - allowing light, but not detailed shapes, to pass through
  • synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects can be seen clearly
