essentialist

adjective
belief in fixed traits
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɛnʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃəlɪst / (BrE)
believing that people or things have fixed, inherent characteristics that define their nature

Examples

Essentialist thinking
The philosopher was criticized for his essentialist approach to human nature.
Some argue that essentialist thinking can lead to stereotypes about different groups.
Her essentialist perspective influenced the way she taught about cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructivist - believing that characteristics are shaped by social or cultural factors
  • synonym: deterministic - believing that outcomes are determined by fixed factors
