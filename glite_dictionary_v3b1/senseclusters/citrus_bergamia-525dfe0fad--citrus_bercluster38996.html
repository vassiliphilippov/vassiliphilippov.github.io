Citrus bergamia

noun
tree yielding bergamot oil, native to Italy
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪtrəs bərˈɡɑːmiə / (AmE), / ˌsɪtrəs bɜːˈɡɑːmɪə / (BrE)
A small tree native to Italy with pear-shaped, yellow or green fruit whose aromatic oil is used in perfumery and flavoring.

Examples

Citrus bergamia oil
Earl Grey tea owes its distinctive flavor to the addition of Citrus bergamia oil.
Farmers in southern Italy cultivate Citrus bergamia for both its fruit and its aromatic oil.
The essential oil extracted from Citrus bergamia is widely used in the perfume industry.
