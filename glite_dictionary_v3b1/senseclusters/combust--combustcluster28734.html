Synonyms

  • catch fire
    2.6 2/m
    catch fire starts burning suddenly, often by accident, while combust is a more formal verb for something that begins to burn.
  • take fire
    4.8 0.01/m
    take fire begins to burn and appears in older or literary texts, while combust is a modern formal verb for catching fire.
  • ignite
    4.0 4/m
    ignite means to cause something to start burning, often intentionally, while combust can mean beginning to catch fire more generally.
  • set on fire
    3.0 3/m
    set on fire means to cause burning, sometimes on purpose or by accident, while combust is a neutral, somewhat formal verb for starting to burn.
  • kindle
    3.7 0.6/m
    kindle means to start a small fire or light slowly for warmth or cooking, while combust is a neutral verb for beginning to burn.
  • burn
    1.0 70/m
    burn describes being on fire or being damaged by heat over time, while combust focuses on the moment something begins to catch fire.
  • conflagrate
    9.9 0.00/m
    conflagrate means to burst into large, destructive flames and sounds formal, while combust is a neutral verb for starting to burn.
