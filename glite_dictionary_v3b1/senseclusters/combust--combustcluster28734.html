combust

verb
catch fire and burn
5.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈbʌst/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈbʌst/ (BrE)
to catch fire, start to burn, or cause something to burn

Examples

To combust quickly
Dry leaves can easily combust on a hot summer day.
If the chemicals are mixed incorrectly, they may combust unexpectedly.
Some gases are able to combust at room temperature.
Chemicals must be stored safely to prevent them from accidentally combusting.
The oily rags began to combust after being exposed to high temperatures.
When mixed with air, certain gases can combust rapidly and cause explosions.
If the chemicals mix, they may combust spontaneously.
Many power plants combust coal to produce electricity.
The engineers designed the engine to combust fuel efficiently.
