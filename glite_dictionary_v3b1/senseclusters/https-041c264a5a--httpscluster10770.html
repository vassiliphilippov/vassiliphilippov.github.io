HTTPS

noun
secure web protocol
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ tiː tiː piː ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ tiː tiː piː ˈɛs / (BrE)
the part at the beginning of a website address that shows the site uses a secure, encrypted connection

Examples

HTTPS address bar
Always look for HTTPS in the address bar before entering sensitive information online.
Websites that use HTTPS help protect your data from being intercepted.
The browser displayed a warning because the site did not have HTTPS enabled.

Related Terms

  • antonym: HTTP - the standard protocol for transferring web pages without encryption
