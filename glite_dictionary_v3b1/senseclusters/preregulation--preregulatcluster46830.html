preregulation

noun
before official rules
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the period or condition existing before official rules or controls are established

Examples

Preregulation era
During the preregulation era, companies operated with little oversight from authorities.
The preregulation phase of the industry saw rapid but sometimes unsafe growth.
Many problems arose in the preregulation market due to the lack of consumer protections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deregulation - the removal of official rules or controls
  • antonym: regulation - the act of controlling or governing by rules
