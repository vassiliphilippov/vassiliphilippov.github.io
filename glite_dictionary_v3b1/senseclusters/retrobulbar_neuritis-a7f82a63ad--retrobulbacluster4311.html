retrobulbar neuritis

noun
inflammation of the optic nerve behind the eye
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈbʌlbɑr nʊˈraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈbʌlbɑː njuːˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the optic nerve located behind the eye, often associated with multiple sclerosis

Examples

Retrobulbar neuritis inflammation
Multiple sclerosis is a common cause of retrobulbar neuritis, leading to impaired vision and pain.
Optical tests confirmed the diagnosis of retrobulbar neuritis in the patient with sudden vision loss.
Treatment of retrobulbar neuritis typically involves corticosteroids to reduce inflammation and preserve eyesight.
