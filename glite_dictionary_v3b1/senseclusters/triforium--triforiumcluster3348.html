triforium

noun
church gallery
9.9
Pronunciation: / traɪˈfɔriəm / (AmE), / trɪˈfɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
a gallery or passage above the arches of the nave and below the clerestory in a large church, often with arches opening to the nave

Examples

The triforium gallery
The sunlight filtered through the triforium of the cathedral, casting patterns on the floor below.
Visitors admired the detailed stonework along the triforium during the guided tour of the ancient church.
From the triforium, the choir could look down on the congregation during special ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: clerestory - the upper part of a church with windows above the nave
  • synonym: gallery - an upper platform or passage inside a building
