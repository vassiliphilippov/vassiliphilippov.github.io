exaggerator

noun
person who overstates
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who makes things seem more important, larger, better, or worse than they really are

Examples

An exaggerator tells stories
Everyone knows Mark is an exaggerator who always makes his stories sound unbelievable.
As a child, she was called an exaggerator because her tales were always bigger than life.
You can’t always trust an exaggerator to give you the plain facts.
Everyone knows Tom is an exaggerator who always makes his stories sound unbelievable.
As an exaggerator, she often turns small problems into major crises.
The article accused the politician of being an exaggerator about the country's economic issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embellisher - a person who adds extra details to make something more interesting
  • synonym: liar - a person who does not tell the truth
  • antonym: minimizer - a person who makes things seem less important or smaller than they are
  • antonym: understater - a person who makes something seem less important or serious than it is
