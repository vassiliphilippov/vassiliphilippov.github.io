recomposition

noun
body transformation process
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of changing body composition, especially by losing fat and gaining muscle at the same time

Examples

Body recomposition
Many fitness enthusiasts aim for recomposition to achieve a leaner and more muscular physique.
Proper nutrition and strength training are essential for successful recomposition.
Her impressive recomposition results inspired others at the gym to focus on both fat loss and muscle gain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bodybuilding - the activity of developing muscles through exercise and diet
  • degree higher: muscle gain - the process of increasing muscle mass
  • degree lower: weight loss - the process of reducing body weight, especially by losing fat
