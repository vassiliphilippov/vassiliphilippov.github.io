facticity

noun
actual conditions of existence
9.9
Pronunciation: / fækˈtɪsəti / (AmE), / fækˈtɪsəti / (BrE)
in philosophy, the concrete facts and conditions of a person's existence that are given and limit freedom or choice

Examples

Facticity of life
Existential philosophers often discuss the facticity of birth and death as limits on human freedom.
Her facticity included her upbringing and the social class she was born into.
Despite his ambitions, the facticity of his physical limitations shaped his choices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actuality - the state of existing in reality
  • antonym: possibility - something that could happen or exist
