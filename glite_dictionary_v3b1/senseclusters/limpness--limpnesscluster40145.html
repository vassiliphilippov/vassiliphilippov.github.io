limpness

noun
softness; lack of firmness
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmp.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪmp.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being soft, lacking firmness or stiffness.

Examples

The limpness of hand
Her handshake was notable for its limpness, lacking any real strength.
The limpness of the fabric caused it to drape beautifully over the chair.
The limpness of the lettuce made it clear that it was past its prime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flaccidity - the state of being soft or lacking firmness
  • antonym: rigidity - the state of being stiff or inflexible
