cutline

noun
photo caption
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a caption or brief explanation placed under a photograph or illustration in a publication

Examples

Cutline under a photo
The cutline under the photograph explained who the people were.
Editors often write a cutline to provide context for images in the article.
Without a cutline, readers might not understand the significance of the picture.
The cutline under the image provided important details about the event.
Editors often write a cutline to give readers more context about the photograph.
Without a cutline, readers might not understand what the picture is showing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caption - a short piece of text describing a picture or illustration
