coordinate bond

noun
covalent bond formed by one atom providing both electrons
7.0 0.00/m

A covalent bond in which both electrons forming the bond are donated by the same atom.

Examples

A coordinate bond in chemistry
A coordinate bond occurs when one atom donates both electrons for a shared pair in a molecule.
Chemists often illustrate a coordinate bond with an arrow pointing from the donor atom to the acceptor.
The ammonium ion is formed through a coordinate bond between ammonia and a hydrogen ion.
