Synonyms

  • parsimonious

    parsimonious is a formal word for extreme thrift, while miserly is more general and can appear in everyday speech.
  • penurious
    9.9 0.01/m
    penurious describes extreme frugality or poverty, while miserly focuses on a person's unwillingness to spend money for others.
  • tightfisted
    8.0 0.05/m
    tightfisted describes someone who refuses to spend money in daily life, while miserly simply means not generous with money.
  • closefisted

  • hardfisted
    9.9 0.00/m

  • penny-pinching

  • pinchpenny
    9.2 0.00/m

  • cheeseparing
    9.9 0.00/m

  • mingy
