miserly

adjective
unwilling to spend money or share

Unwilling to give or spend money; lacking generosity.

Examples

Miserly old man
Even though he was wealthy, his miserly nature stopped him from donating to charity.
She gave her waiter a miserly tip after an excellent meal, which surprised everyone.
The company's miserly budget made it hard to buy new equipment.

Synonyms

  • stingy
    5.2 1.09/m
    stingy is unwilling to give money or time and sounds common in speech, while miserly often suggests hoarding and small generosity.
  • parsimonious
    9.9 0.61/m
    parsimonious is a formal word for extreme thrift, while miserly is more general and can appear in everyday speech.
  • penurious
    9.9 0.05/m
    penurious describes extreme frugality or poverty, while miserly focuses on a person's unwillingness to spend money for others.
  • tightfisted
    8.0 0.16/m
    tightfisted describes someone who refuses to spend money in daily life, while miserly simply means not generous with money.
  • closefisted
    8.2 0.00/m
    closefisted means unwilling to hand out money or gifts in small ways, while miserly means lacking generosity overall.
  • hardfisted
    9.9 0.00/m
    hardfisted often suggests a tough attitude and refusal to spend on others, while miserly focuses on being ungenerous with money.
  • penny-pinching
    7.5 0.02/m
    penny-pinching shows careful saving and small daily cuts, while miserly often feels harsher and focuses on mean giving.
  • pinchpenny
    9.2 0.00/m
    pinchpenny says a person is very unwilling to spend money on small things, while miserly covers general lack of generosity.
  • cheeseparing
    9.9 0.00/m
    cheeseparing paints someone as overly careful with every penny, while miserly is a broader word for not being generous.
  • mingy
    9.1 0.00/m
    mingy is an informal British word for being stingy or mean with money, while miserly is more general and less regional.
  • tight
    7.7 1.29/m
    tight is an informal short word for not wanting to spend money, while miserly sounds more standard and slightly stronger.
  • mean
    2.2 22/m
    mean can mean unkind or cruel as well as not generous, while miserly only describes lack of willingness to spend money.
