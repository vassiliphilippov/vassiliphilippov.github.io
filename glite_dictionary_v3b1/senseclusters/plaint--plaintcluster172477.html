plaint

noun
legal complaint submitted to a court (Brit.)
9.9
Pronunciation: / pleɪnt / (AmE), / pleɪnt / (BrE)
(British, law) a written statement submitted to a court outlining the grounds of a complaint and requesting redress of a grievance.

Examples

Court plaint
The judge reviewed the plaint before deciding whether to admit the case.
The solicitor filed a plaint in the county court on behalf of the client.
Under older British law, a plaint was often the first step in civil proceedings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complaint - a formal expression of dissatisfaction or accusation brought before a court
