ribboned

verb
to win a ribbon
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪbənd / (AmE), / ˈrɪbənd / (BrE)
to be awarded a ribbon as a prize, especially in a show or competition.

Examples

Ribboned for first place
Her painting was ribboned at the local art show for its creativity.
Several students were ribboned for their outstanding science projects.
The dog was proudly ribboned after winning first place in the agility competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to fail to win a prize or position in a competition
  • synonym: place - to win a position or prize in a competition
