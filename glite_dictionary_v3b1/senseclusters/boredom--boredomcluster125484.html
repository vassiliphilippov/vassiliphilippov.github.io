boredom

noun
state of feeling bored
1.3 6.33/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…îrd…ôm / (AmE), / Ààb…îÀêd…ôm / (BrE)
A state of feeling weary and restless due to lack of interest or excitement.

Examples

Feeling boredom
After hours of waiting, boredom began to set in among the children.
Many people experience boredom when they have nothing to do over the weekend.
The lecture filled her with boredom, and she struggled to stay awake.

Synonyms

  • tedium
    6.8 0.90/m
    tedium is the long, dull sameness of a task or job, while boredom is a weary restless lack of interest.
  • ennui
    9.0 0.81/m
    ennui feels like a quiet, empty tiredness that stays, while boredom is more general restlessness from lack of interest.
  • humdrum
    6.7 0.09/m
    humdrum describes a plain, unchanging life or job, while boredom is a felt state of weary restlessness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interest - state of wanting to know or learn about something
  • synonym: tedium - the quality or state of being tedious
