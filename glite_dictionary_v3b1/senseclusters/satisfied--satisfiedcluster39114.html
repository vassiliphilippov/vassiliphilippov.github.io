satisfied

adjective
having a need or desire fulfilled
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsæt̬.ɪs.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ɪs.faɪd / (BrE)
Having a need, desire, or appetite fully fulfilled; having one’s hunger, thirst, or curiosity allayed.

Examples

Satisfied after eating
A small bowl of soup was enough to leave her satisfied for hours.
After a long drink of water, he finally felt satisfied.
Once his hunger was satisfied, he returned to work with renewed energy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unsatisfied - not having one's needs or desires fulfilled
