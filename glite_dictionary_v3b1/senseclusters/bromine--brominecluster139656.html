bromine

noun
a dark, volatile liquid element (Br)
4.9 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.miːn / (BrE)
a dark reddish-brown, poisonous, volatile liquid element (symbol Br) belonging to the halogen group, used in industry and found naturally in sea water

Examples

Liquid bromine element
Exposure to bromine fumes can irritate the eyes and respiratory system.
Sea water is a rich natural source of bromine and other halogens.
The laboratory stored bromine in a tightly sealed glass container due to its volatile nature.
