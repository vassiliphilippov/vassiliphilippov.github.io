obviously

adverb
clearly; plainly; without doubt
1.0 143/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêb.vi.…ôs.li / (AmE), / Àà…íb.vi.…ôs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is easily perceived or understood; clearly or plainly.

Examples

He obviously cares
He obviously cares about his family very much.
She was obviously upset by the outcome of the meeting.
The mistake was obviously a result of miscommunication.

Synonyms

  • distinctly
    3.2 7.47/m

  • evidently
    3.5 13/m

  • unmistakably
    4.1 2.05/m
    unmistakably means it cannot be confused with anything else, while obviously simply says it is clear or plain to see.
  • patently
    5.6 2.34/m
    patently is usually strong or critical, saying something is clearly wrong or true, while obviously is more neutral and common.
  • manifestly
    5.6 1.39/m
    manifestly is formal and often used in writing to stress truth, while obviously fits spoken or general clear statements.
  • self-evidently
    5.5 0.21/m
    self-evidently asserts something needs no proof and is obvious by itself, while obviously just marks that it seems clear.
  • unequivocally
    6.9 2.86/m

  • unambiguously
    5.5 1.40/m
