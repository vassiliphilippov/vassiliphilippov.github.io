Draba verna

noun
a small annual weed with tiny white flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈdreɪbə ˈvɜrnə / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːbə ˈvɜːnə / (BrE)
An annual weed of Europe and North America with a rosette of basal leaves, tiny white flowers, and oblong seed capsules; also known as spring draba or shadflower.

Examples

Draba verna plant
Botanists often find Draba verna growing in disturbed soils across temperate regions.
Gardeners may consider Draba verna a weed, but it adds diversity to early spring plant life.
The tiny white flowers of Draba verna appear early in the spring before many other plants bloom.
