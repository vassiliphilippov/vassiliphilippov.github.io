selective amnesia

noun
amnesia affecting certain events or facts
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛktɪv æmˈniʒə / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv amˈniːziə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person forgets specific events or information, often in a way that seems convenient or intentional.

Examples

Selective amnesia condition
She seemed to suffer from selective amnesia whenever the conversation turned to her mistakes.
Some politicians exhibit selective amnesia when confronted with their past promises.
The witness claimed selective amnesia when asked about his whereabouts that night.
