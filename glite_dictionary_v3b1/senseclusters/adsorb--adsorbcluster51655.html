adsorb

verb
to hold substances on a surface
6.1 7.2
Pronunciation: /əd.ˈzɔːrb/ /; / /əd.ˈsɔːrb/ (AmE), /əd.ˈzɔːb/ /; / /əd.ˈsɔːb/ (BrE)
to collect and hold (liquids or gases) on the surface of a solid or liquid, rather than take them into its interior

Examples

To adsorb gases
Activated charcoal can adsorb toxins from the digestive system.
Certain materials adsorb moisture from the air, helping to control humidity.
Scientists study how different gases adsorb onto the surfaces of catalysts.
