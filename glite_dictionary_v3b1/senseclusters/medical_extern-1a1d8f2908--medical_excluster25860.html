medical extern

noun
medical student working at a hospital without residence
7.1 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmed.ɪ.kəl ek.ˈstɜːrn/ /; / /ˈmed.ə.kəl ɪk.ˈstɜːrn/ (AmE), /ˈmed.ɪ.kəl ek.ˈstɜːn/ /; / /ˈmed.ə.kəl ɪk.ˈstɜːn/ (BrE)
a medical student or doctor who is connected with a hospital for practical experience but does not live there

Examples

A medical extern student
As a medical extern, she observed surgeries and assisted with patient care without living on site.
During his studies, he spent a year as a medical extern at the local hospital.
The responsibilities of a medical extern differ from those of an intern.

Related Terms

  • antonym: medical intern - a medical graduate in training at a hospital, usually residing there
  • degree higher: resident - a doctor who lives and works at a hospital during training
