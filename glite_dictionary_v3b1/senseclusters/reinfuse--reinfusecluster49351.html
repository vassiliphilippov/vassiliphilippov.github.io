reinfuse

verb
to put back in
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈfjuːz / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to put a liquid or substance back into something, especially into the body

Examples

To reinfuse blood
After collecting the blood, the nurse will reinfuse it into the patient.
Doctors sometimes reinfuse fluids to help patients recover from surgery.
The medical team decided to reinfuse the patient's own stem cells after treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infuse - to introduce a liquid or substance into something
  • antonym: remove - to take something out of a place or container
