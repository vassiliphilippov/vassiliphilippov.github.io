interlace

verb
to entwine or lock together
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈleɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈleɪs / (BrE)
to twist, weave, or entwine things together so that they cross each other

Examples

To interlace fingers
He interlaced his hands behind his head and leaned back in his chair.
She interlaced her fingers nervously as she waited for the interview to begin.
The couple interlaced their arms as they strolled through the park.
The artist carefully interlaced strands of gold into the fabric for a shimmering effect.
The branches of the old trees interlace above the path, creating a natural tunnel.
Their fingers interlaced as they walked down the street, hand in hand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entwine - to twist together; to wrap or wind around
  • synonym: intertwine - to twist or twine together
  • antonym: unlace - to untie or loosen laced or interwoven things
