Earth-received time

noun
the UTC time an event is received on Earth
Pronunciation: / ˈɝθ rɪˈsivd taɪm / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ rɪˈsiːvd taɪm / (BrE)
the Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) at which an event is received on Earth, especially as used in astronomy, telecommunications, and space exploration

Examples

Earth-received time in astronomy
Mission control records every event using Earth-received time to maintain synchronization.
The anomaly was first detected at an Earth-received time of 02:45 UTC.
The satellite transmitted its data, but the Earth-received time was several minutes later due to the distance.
