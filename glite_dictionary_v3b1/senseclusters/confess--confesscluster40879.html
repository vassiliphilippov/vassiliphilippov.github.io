confess

verb
admit to a crime or wrongdoing, often under pressure
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛs / (AmE), / kənˈfɛs / (BrE)
to admit that you have done something wrong or committed a crime

Examples

To confess a crime
After hours of questioning, the suspect finally confessed to the robbery.
She refused to confess her involvement, even when presented with the evidence.
The prisoner confessed under pressure from the authorities.
After hours of questioning, he finally confessed to the crime.
I must confess that I was nervous before the interview.
She confessed her mistake in front of the whole team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admit - to acknowledge or disclose something, especially reluctantly
  • antonym: deny - to state that something is not true, especially a crime or allegation
