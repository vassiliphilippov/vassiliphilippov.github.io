orthosis

noun
supportive medical device
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈθoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɔːˈθəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a device used to support, align, or improve the function of a part of the body, especially a limb or the spine

Examples

An orthosis device
The doctor recommended an orthosis to help stabilize her ankle after the injury.
Children with scoliosis may wear an orthosis to improve their posture and spinal alignment.
He was fitted with a custom orthosis to support his weakened wrist during recovery.
The patient wore an orthosis on his leg to improve his walking.
After her injury, the doctor recommended an orthosis to support her ankle.
Children with certain conditions may benefit from using an orthosis to help with movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brace - a device that supports or holds a body part in position
  • antonym: prosthesis - an artificial device that replaces a missing body part
