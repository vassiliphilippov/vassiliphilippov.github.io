Tammuz

proper noun
Sumerian-Babylonian god of vegetation
9.9
Pronunciation: /tə.ˈmuːz/ /; / /ˈtæ.mʊz/ (AmE), /tə.ˈmuːz/ /; / /ˈtæ.mʊz/ (BrE)
an ancient Sumerian and Babylonian god of pastures and vegetation, associated with fertility and the summer month named after him

Examples

God Tammuz
Many ancient rituals in Mesopotamia honored Tammuz as the god who died and was reborn each year.
Some stories describe Tammuz as the husband of the goddess Inanna.
The annual mourning for Tammuz reflected the cycle of life, death, and renewal in nature.
The festival was held in the month of Tammuz according to the Hebrew calendar.
In ancient Mesopotamia, people worshipped Tammuz as a god of fertility and renewal.
The holiday always falls during Tammuz, marking the start of the summer season.
