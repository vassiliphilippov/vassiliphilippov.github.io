ecchymosis

noun
discoloration of skin from bleeding under the surface
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.əˈmoʊ.sɪs /; / ˌɛk.ɪˈmoʊ.səs / (AmE), / ˌɛk.ɪˈməʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a discoloration of the skin caused by bleeding underneath, usually from bruising

Examples

Skin ecchymosis
A yellowish color appeared as the ecchymosis healed over several days.
Elderly patients are more prone to developing ecchymoses after minor injuries.
The doctor examined the patient's arm and noted a large ecchymosis near the elbow.
Bruising and ecchymosis may occur following certain medical procedures.
Multiple ecchymoses on her legs prompted further evaluation for a bleeding disorder.
The physician noted a large ecchymosis on the patient's upper arm after the fall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bruise - an injury causing discoloration without breaking the skin
