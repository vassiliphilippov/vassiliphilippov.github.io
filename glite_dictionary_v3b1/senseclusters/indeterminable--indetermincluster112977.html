indeterminable

adjective
impossible to determine; not able to be calculated
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈtɜrmɪnəbəl /; / ˌɪndɪˈtɝːmɪnəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈtɜːmɪnəbl / (BrE)
impossible to determine or decide with certainty

Examples

Indeterminable cause
The cause of the problem remained indeterminable after weeks of investigation.
The document's true age is indeterminable due to the lack of historical records.
The object was lost at a depth that was indeterminable with current equipment.
Due to conflicting reports, the cause of the fire was deemed indeterminable.
The manuscript's age is indeterminable based on the current evidence.
The outcome of the experiment remained indeterminable after multiple tests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinate - able to be exactly decided or determined
  • synonym: indefinite - vague; not clearly defined or determined
  • synonym: undecidable - not able to be decided
