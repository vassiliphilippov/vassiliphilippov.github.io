chinchona

noun
tree genus; source of quinine
7.3
Pronunciation: / tʃɪnˈkoʊnə / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈkəʊnə / (BrE)
any of several trees in the genus Cinchona, native to South America, whose bark is used to produce quinine.

Examples

The chinchona tree
Botanists studied several varieties of chinchona for their medicinal properties.
Large plantations of chinchona were established in the 19th century for harvesting quinine.
The bark of the chinchona tree was historically used to treat malaria.
