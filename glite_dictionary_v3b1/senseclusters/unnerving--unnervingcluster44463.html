unnerving

adjective
making someone feel nervous or afraid
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈnɜrvɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈnɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone feel nervous, afraid, or uncomfortable; causing a loss of courage or confidence.

Examples

Unnerving experience
It can be unnerving to deliver a speech in front of a large audience for the first time.
The dark, empty corridors had an unnerving effect on the children exploring the old building.
There was something unnerving about the way he stared at me in silence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarming - causing worry or fear
  • antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less afraid
