inertness

noun
lack of ability to move or act
6.7 6.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈɜːrt.nəs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈɜːt.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking the ability or power to move or act; immobility.

Examples

The inertness of objects
His inertness made it difficult for the team to make progress on the project.
The inertness of the chemical kept it from reacting with other substances.
The inertness of the sculpture gave it a sense of tranquility and stillness.
