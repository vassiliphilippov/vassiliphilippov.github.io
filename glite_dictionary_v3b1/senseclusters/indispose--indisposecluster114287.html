indispose

verb
make unfit or unsuitable
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪsˈpoʊz /; / ˌɪndɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪsˈpəʊz /; / ˌɪndɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone unable or unwilling to do something, often because of illness or feeling unwell

Examples

To indispose someone suddenly
A sudden illness can indispose her from attending the conference.
Financial troubles could indispose him for the responsibilities of leadership.
The long journey may indispose them to travel any farther today.
Her illness tended to indispose her to attend social gatherings.
The recent scandals may indispose investors to trust the company.
They feared that the constant criticism would indispose him toward cooperation.
A mild cold may indispose her and keep her from work for a day.
An overheated room can indispose many people and affect their concentration.
Heavy meals late at night sometimes indispose me the next morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disable - make unable to do something, often due to illness or injury
  • synonym: disincline - to make unwilling or reluctant
  • synonym: disqualify - to make ineligible or unsuitable for something
  • antonym: encourage - to give someone support or confidence to do something
