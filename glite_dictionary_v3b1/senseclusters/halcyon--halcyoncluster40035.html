halcyon

noun
mythical bird calming storms
9.9 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhæl.si.ən / (AmE), / ˈhalsɪən / (BrE)
A mythical bird said to breed during the winter solstice in a floating nest and believed to have the power to calm the winds and waves.

Examples

The mythical halcyon
According to legend, the halcyon could calm the sea so she could nest on the waves undisturbed.
During ancient times, sailors prayed for the protection of the halcyon before venturing out in winter.
Poets often use the halcyon as a symbol of peace and tranquility in literature.
