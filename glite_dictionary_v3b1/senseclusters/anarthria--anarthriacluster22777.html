anarthria

noun
loss of articulate speech due to brain lesions
6.0
Pronunciation: / ænˈɑrθriə / (AmE), / ænˈɑːθrɪə / (BrE)
Partial or total loss of the ability to articulate speech, typically caused by lesions of the central nervous system.

Examples

Anarthria speech loss
Anarthria can result from damage to motor areas involved in speech production.
Patients suffering from anarthria may retain comprehension but cannot form intelligible words.
The neurologist diagnosed her with anarthria following her recent stroke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphasia - loss of ability to understand or express speech
