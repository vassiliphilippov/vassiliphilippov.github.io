dovetail joint

noun
interlocking woodworking joint
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊvˌteɪl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvteɪl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a woodworking joint made by interlocking wedge-shaped projections and corresponding slots, creating a strong, durable connection

Examples

A dovetail joint connection
A well-made dovetail joint is both strong and visually attractive in fine furniture.
The cabinetmaker used a dovetail joint to securely connect the drawer's sides.
The instructions recommend practicing the dovetail joint on scrap wood before starting the main project.
