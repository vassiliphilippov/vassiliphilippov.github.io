purple passage

noun
ornately written literary passage
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝpəl ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈpæsɪdʒ / (BrE)
A passage in a literary work characterized by elaborate, ornate, or flowery language, often to the point of excess.

Examples

A purple passage in writing
Everyone remembered the purple passage on the hero's arrival, though some thought it was a bit excessive.
The critic argued that the novel's purple passage distracted from the overall narrative.
Writers are warned to avoid inserting a purple passage simply to show off their vocabulary.

  • synonym: purple patch - an ornate or elaborate literary passage
