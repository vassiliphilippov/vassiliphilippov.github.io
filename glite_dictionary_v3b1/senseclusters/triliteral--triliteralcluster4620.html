triliteral

noun
three-letter root
Pronunciation: / traɪˈlɪtərəl / (AmE), / traɪˈlɪtərəl / (BrE)
a root or morpheme consisting of three letters or consonants, especially in Semitic languages

Examples

A triliteral in Arabic
In Arabic, many verbs are based on a triliteral root formed from three consonants.
The teacher explained how the triliteral system works in Semitic languages.
Researchers studied the patterns of triliteral roots to understand word formation.
