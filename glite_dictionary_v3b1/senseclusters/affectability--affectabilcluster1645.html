affectability

noun
susceptibility to influence
9.6
Pronunciation: / əˌfɛktəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / əˌfɛktəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily influenced or affected by external factors or emotions

Examples

High affectability
Her affectability made her especially sensitive to the opinions of her friends.
The affectability of young children means they can be easily shaped by their environment.
Because of his high affectability, he often changed his mind after hearing others' views.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resilience - the ability to recover quickly or resist being affected
  • synonym: susceptibility - the state of being easily affected or influenced
