avail

verb
make use of something
1.0
Pronunciation: / əˈveɪl / (AmE), / əˈveɪl / (BrE)
to make use of something or to be of use or help (often used as 'avail oneself of')

Examples

Avail yourself of help
Guests are encouraged to avail themselves of the hotel facilities.
If you avail yourself of this offer, you will save money.
She decided to avail herself of the free legal advice.
All his efforts did not avail him in the end.
Logic and reason will avail little against strong emotions.
No amount of training will avail if you lack motivation.
If you do not avail yourself of these opportunities, you may regret it later.
She decided to avail herself of the free counseling services offered by the university.
Visitors are encouraged to avail themselves of the complimentary breakfast each morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benefit - be helpful or provide an advantage to
  • synonym: exploit - to use something for one's own advantage, sometimes unfairly
  • synonym: help - make it easier for someone to do something
  • antonym: hinder - make it difficult for someone to do something
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not take advantage of
  • synonym: utilize - to make practical or effective use of
