hitless

adjective
not achieving a hit (esp. in baseball)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈhɪtlɪs / (BrE)
(in baseball) not achieving or involving any hits, especially for a player, batter, or game

Examples

Hitless baseball game
After three games, she remained hitless despite several good swings.
He was disappointed to go hitless in last night's important match.
The pitcher kept the opposing team hitless for seven innings.
After three hitless games, the batter was eager to break his slump.
The pitcher threw a hitless inning to keep his team in the lead.
The team remained hitless until the seventh inning, when they finally got a single.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hit - to successfully strike the ball and reach base (in baseball)
  • synonym: scoreless - having no points or hits scored
