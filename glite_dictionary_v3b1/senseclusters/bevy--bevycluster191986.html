bevy

noun
a group of birds gathered together
9.5 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛvi / (AmE), / ˈbɛvi / (BrE)
a large group of people, birds, animals, or things of the same kind, especially gathered together

Examples

A bevy of birds
A bevy of geese gathered at the edge of the pond early in the morning.
The children watched as a bevy of sparrows pecked at the ground in the park.
We spotted a bevy of doves resting together under the old oak tree.
A bevy of children rushed into the playground as soon as the bell rang.
A bevy of excited fans waited outside the theater for the celebrities to arrive.
He found himself the center of attention among a bevy of journalists at the press conference.
A bevy of swans glided gracefully across the lake in the early morning.
The celebrity was surrounded by a bevy of enthusiastic fans at the event.
A bevy of new restaurants has opened in the city center this year.
