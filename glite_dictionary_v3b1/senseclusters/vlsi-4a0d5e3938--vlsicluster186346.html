VLSI

noun
integrated circuit chip
9.9
Pronunciation: / viː ɛl ɛs aɪ / (AmE), / viː ɛl ɛs aɪ / (BrE)
an integrated circuit containing a very large number of transistors or components on a single chip

Examples

A VLSI circuit
Modern smartphones rely on VLSI technology to fit powerful processors into small devices.
The development of VLSI has revolutionized the electronics industry by enabling complex circuits on a single chip.
Engineers designed a new VLSI to improve the speed and efficiency of the computer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: IC - abbreviation for integrated circuit, an electronic circuit on a small chip
  • synonym: microchip - a small semiconductor used to relay information in electronic devices
