space-time

noun
four-dimensional continuum in physics
Pronunciation: / ˈspeɪsˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsˌtaɪm / (BrE)
The four-dimensional continuum (three dimensions of space and one of time) in which all physical events occur, as used in the theory of relativity.

Examples

The space-time continuum
According to Einstein's theory, gravity results from the curvature of space-time.
Scientists use the idea of space-time to describe the relationship between matter, energy, and the universe.
The concept of space-time revolutionized modern physics in the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continuum - A continuous sequence or whole without abrupt divisions.
  • synonym: universe - All of space-time and its contents, including planets, stars, and galaxies.
