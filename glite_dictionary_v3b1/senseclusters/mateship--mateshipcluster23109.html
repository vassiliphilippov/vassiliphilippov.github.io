mateship

noun
favoring friends
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmeɪt.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ˈmeɪt.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
the informal practice of favoring friends or close associates, often by giving them special treatment or opportunities

Examples

Mateship at work
Some people believe that mateship in the workplace can lead to unfair hiring practices.
The politician was accused of promoting mateship by appointing his friends to key positions.
Critics argue that mateship undermines merit and equal opportunity in organizations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cronyism - the practice of giving jobs or advantages to friends
  • antonym: fairness - the quality of treating people equally without favoritism
  • synonym: nepotism - favoritism shown to relatives, especially in jobs
