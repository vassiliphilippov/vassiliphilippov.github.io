Japanization

noun
adoption of Japanese ways
8.4
Pronunciation: /dʒə.pæn.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dʒə.pə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dʒæ.pæn.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dʒə.pæn.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of adopting Japanese characteristics, practices, or language in another country or culture

Examples

Cultural Japanization
The Japanization of the company's management style led to more group decision-making.
Some critics argue that Japanization can threaten local traditions and customs.
The rapid Japanization of pop culture is evident in the popularity of anime and manga worldwide.
