teleology

noun
doctrine explaining phenomena by their purposes
9.1 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛliˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
(philosophy) The doctrine that explains phenomena in terms of their ends, goals, or purposes rather than by their causes alone.

Examples

Philosophical teleology
In philosophy, teleology seeks to explain natural phenomena by considering their intended purposes.
Some critics argue that teleology imposes human purpose onto natural events.
The debate between teleology and causality remains central to the philosophy of science.

  • antonym: causality - the relationship between cause and effect
