bivalence

noun
verb with two arguments
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪvəˌlɛns / (AmE), / ˈbaɪvələns / (BrE)
the grammatical property of a verb that requires two arguments, typically a subject and an object

Examples

Bivalence of a verb
The verb 'give' demonstrates bivalence because it requires both a subject and an object.
In linguistic analysis, bivalence helps classify verbs based on the number of arguments they take.
Some verbs lose their bivalence in passive constructions, needing only a subject.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monovalence - the property of taking only one argument
  • degree higher: trivalence - the property of taking three arguments
  • synonym: valency - the number of arguments a verb can take
