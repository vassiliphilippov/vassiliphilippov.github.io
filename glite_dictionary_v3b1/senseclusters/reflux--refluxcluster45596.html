reflux

noun
abnormal backward flow of fluids in the body
5.9 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêÀåfl åks / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêÀåfl åks / (BrE)
an abnormal backward flow of body fluids, especially of stomach contents into the esophagus

Examples

Acid reflux
Many people suffer from acid reflux after eating spicy foods.
Proper medication can help control the symptoms of reflux.
The doctor explained that reflux can cause a burning sensation in the chest.

Synonyms

  • gastroesophageal reflux
    4.5 0.14/m
    gastroesophageal reflux is the formal medical name for stomach contents moving back into the esophagus, while reflux is the general term.
  • esophageal reflux
    4.4 0.02/m
    esophageal reflux names stomach contents flowing back into the esophagus, while reflux is a short, general term used outside medicine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regurgitation - the backward flowing of food or fluid, especially from the stomach to the mouth
