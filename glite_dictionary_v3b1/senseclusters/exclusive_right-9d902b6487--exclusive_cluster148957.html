exclusive right

noun
a right reserved for one person or group
1.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈskluː.sɪv raɪt/ /; / /ɪks.ˈkluː.sɪv raɪt/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈskluː.sɪv raɪt/ /; / /ɪks.ˈkluː.sɪv raɪt/ (BrE)
a right that is reserved to a particular person or group and cannot be exercised by others

Examples

The exclusive right to broadcast
Owning the exclusive right to broadcast the championship gave the network a huge advantage.
The company has the exclusive right to distribute the product in Europe.
The patent gives the inventor an exclusive right to manufacture and sell the device.
