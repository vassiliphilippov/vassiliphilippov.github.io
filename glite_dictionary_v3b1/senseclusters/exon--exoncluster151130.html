exon

noun
gene segment coding for proteins
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkˌsɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛksɒn / (BrE)
a segment of a DNA or RNA molecule containing information coding for a protein or peptide sequence, typically separated by introns within a gene

Examples

An exon segment
Certain disorders result from errors in exon splicing during gene expression.
In most eukaryotic genes, exons and introns alternate along the DNA strand.
The scientist carefully analyzed the exon sequences to identify possible mutations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intron - a segment of a DNA or RNA molecule that does not code for proteins and interrupts the sequence of genes
