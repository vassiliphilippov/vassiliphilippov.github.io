Archean eon

noun
early geological eon; Earth's crust formed
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈkiːən ˈiː.ɑn /; / ɑːrˈkiːən ˈiː.ɑːn / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiː.ən ˈiː.ɒn /; / ɑːˈkiː.ən ˈiː.ən / (BrE)
The geological eon spanning from about 3,800 million to 2,500 million years ago, characterized by the formation of the Earth's crust and the presence of early unicellular life.

Examples

The Archean eon period
During the Archean eon, the first simple life forms appeared on Earth.
Scientists study rocks from the Archean eon to learn about the planet's formation.
The Archean eon marks the earliest known geological eon in Earth's history.
During the Archean aeon, the atmosphere was very different from that of today.
Scientists believe the Archean aeon was a pivotal period for the formation of the Earth’s crust.
The earliest evidence of life comes from rocks that formed in the Archean aeon.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Hadean eon - The earliest geological eon, before the Archean
  • degree higher: Precambrian - the period of time before the Cambrian period, including the Archean eon
  • degree higher: Proterozoic eon - the eon following the Archean eon in Earth's history
