breech

verb
to dress in breeches
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ / (AmE), / briːtʃ / (BrE)
to dress a child, especially a boy, in breeches for the first time, as was customary in past centuries

Examples

To breech a boy
In the eighteenth century, families celebrated when a young boy was breeched for the first time.
The portrait shows the child just after being breeched, wearing his new trousers proudly.
It was a tradition to breech boys at a certain age, marking their transition from infancy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clothe - to put clothes on someone
  • antonym: undress - to remove clothes from someone
