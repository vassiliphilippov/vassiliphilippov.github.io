recomposition

noun
act of composing again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of composing or forming something again or in a different way

Examples

Recomposition of the group
The recomposition of the committee brought fresh ideas to the project.
After the merger, a complete recomposition of the company structure was necessary.
The artist's recomposition of the painting gave it a modern feel.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: composition - the act of putting together or creating something
  • synonym: reorganization - the act of arranging something in a new way
