white-berry yew

noun
Chinese yew with white berries
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt ˌbɛri ˈju / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌbɛri ˈjuː / (BrE)
A yew tree native to southeastern China, distinguished from the common yew by its white berries.

Examples

The white-berry yew tree
Botanists visited southeastern China to study the white-berry yew in its natural habitat.
The white-berry yew is rare in cultivation outside of its native region.
Unlike the familiar red-berried varieties, the white-berry yew produces pale fruits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: yew - an evergreen tree with flat, dark-green needles and red berries
