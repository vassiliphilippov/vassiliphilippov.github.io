reification

noun
treating abstracts as concrete things

The process of considering or treating an abstract concept or idea as if it were a real, concrete thing.

Examples

Concept reification
In sociology, reification can lead to treating social constructs as unchangeable realities.
Some philosophers criticize the reification of concepts such as freedom or justice.
The reification of emotions can make them seem more fixed than they are.

Synonyms

  • hypostatization
    9.9 0.01/m

  • objectification
    8.2 1/m
    objectification treats a person or feeling as a mere object, while reification means treating an abstract idea as a concrete thing.
  • concretization
    7.8 0.01/m
    concretization turns a vague idea into specific details or examples, while reification treats that idea as a real, separate thing.
