extraterritoriality

noun
legal exemption abroad
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstrətɛˌrɪtɔriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrətɛˌrɪtɒriˈælɪti / (BrE)
the condition of being exempt from the jurisdiction of local law, typically due to diplomatic or international agreements

Examples

Diplomatic extraterritoriality
Diplomats often have extraterritoriality, meaning they are not subject to the host country's laws.
The treaty granted extraterritoriality to foreign merchants in the port city.
Because of extraterritoriality, the embassy was considered foreign soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunity - protection from legal duties or penalties
  • antonym: jurisdiction - the official power to make legal decisions and judgments
