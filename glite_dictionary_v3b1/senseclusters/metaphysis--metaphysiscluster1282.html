metaphysis

noun
region between the shaft and end of a long bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈtæfəsɪs / (AmE), / mɛˈtafɪsɪs / (BrE)
The region of a long bone between the shaft (diaphysis) and the end (epiphysis), where growth occurs in children and adolescents.

Examples

The metaphysis of bone
Diseases such as osteomyelitis often affect the metaphysis due to its rich blood supply.
In growing children, the metaphysis is where new bone develops and lengthens the limbs.
X-rays revealed a fracture near the metaphysis of the femur.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diaphysis - the shaft or central part of a long bone
  • synonym: epiphysis - the end part of a long bone
