interrogative pronoun

noun
pronoun used to ask questions
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.təˈrɑ.ɡə.tɪv proʊˈnaʊn / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv prəˈnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun used to ask questions, such as who, what, which, and whose

Examples

Interrogative pronouns who what
English has several interrogative pronouns, including who, what, which, and whose.
In the question 'What is your name?', the word what is an interrogative pronoun.
When forming questions, we often use interrogative pronouns at the beginning of the sentence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: what - interrogative pronoun for things or information
  • synonym: who - interrogative pronoun for persons
