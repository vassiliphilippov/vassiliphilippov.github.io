breach

verb
break a law, rule, contract, or promise
2.2
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ / (AmE), / briːtʃ / (BrE)
to break or violate a law, rule, contract, or promise

Examples

To breach the rules
He was accused of breaching the building’s security regulations.
The company was sued after it breached the terms of the contract.
You must not breach the confidentiality agreement you signed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: observe - to obey or comply with a law, rule, or agreement
  • synonym: violate - to break or fail to comply with a rule or formal agreement
