reconsolidate

verb
to make secure again
7.0
Pronunciation: /riː.kən.ˈsɑː.lə.deɪt/ /; / /rɪ.kən.ˈsɑː.lə.deɪt/ (AmE), /riː.kən.ˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt/ /; / /rɪ.kən.ˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt/ (BrE)
to make something firm, secure, or strong again after it has been weakened or damaged

Examples

To reconsolidate a structure
After the earthquake, engineers worked to reconsolidate the damaged bridge supports.
The company needed to reconsolidate its position in the market after a year of losses.
Efforts to reconsolidate the old walls began as soon as the cracks appeared.
