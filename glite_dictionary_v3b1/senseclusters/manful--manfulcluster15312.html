manful

adjective
showing courage or strength
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.fəl / (AmE), / ˈmæn.fəl /; / ˈman.fəl / (BrE)
showing courage, strength, or determination considered typical of a man

Examples

A manful effort
Despite his injury, he gave a manful performance throughout the match.
He made a manful effort to hold back his tears during the speech.
With manful determination, she pushed through the final mile of the race.
Despite his injury, she admired his manful determination to continue playing.
He made a manful effort to control his emotions during the ceremony.
The team mounted a manful defense, refusing to give up even as they fell behind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: effeminate - having qualities thought to be typical of women; unmanly
  • synonym: manly - having qualities traditionally associated with men, such as strength or courage
  • antonym: womanly - having qualities traditionally associated with women
