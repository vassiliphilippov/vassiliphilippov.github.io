population shift

noun
change in demographic makeup of a population
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən ʃɪft / (BrE)
a change in the distribution or proportions of groups within a population, often referring to movement or migration affecting demographic makeup

Examples

A population shift occurs
A sudden population shift can strain local resources and services.
Recent years have seen a population shift from rural areas to the suburbs.
The city experienced a significant population shift during the industrial revolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: migration - the movement of people from one place to another
