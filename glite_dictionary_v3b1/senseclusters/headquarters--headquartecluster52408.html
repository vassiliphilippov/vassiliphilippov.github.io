headquarters

noun
military unit with commander and staff
3.0 4.95/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…õdÀåkw…îrt…ôrz / (AmE), / Ààh…õdÀåkw…îÀêt…ôz / (BrE)
the place or unit from which military operations are directed, including the commander and their staff

Examples

Army headquarters
An order from headquarters instructed the troops to move out at dawn.
He was assigned to headquarters as part of the command staff last month.
The division's headquarters was responsible for coordinating the various battalions.
Enemy plans were intercepted before they could reach the headquarters of the division.
The general set up a temporary headquarters near the front lines to oversee the battle.
The old mansion was converted into the regiment's headquarters during the war.

Synonyms

  • military headquarters
    3.5 0.16/m
    military headquarters is the main place where commanders oversee and direct armed forces, while headquarters can mean any directing unit.
  • general headquarters
    5.5 0.03/m
    general headquarters is the central command for large military operations, while headquarters can be any unit directing forces.
  • command post
    5.3 1.08/m
    command post is a nearby, often temporary field center for directing troops, while headquarters is a broader, permanent command base.
  • HQ
    3.4 1.55/m
    HQ is the short form used in writing and speech for a command location, while headquarters is the full formal term.
  • GHQ
    7.4 0.06/m
    GHQ is the abbreviation military writers use for a top central command, while headquarters is the general name for any command center.
