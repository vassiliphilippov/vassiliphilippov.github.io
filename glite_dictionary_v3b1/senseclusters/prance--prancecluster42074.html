prance

verb
walk or move with lively, showy steps
8.6 3.9
Pronunciation: / præns / (AmE), / prɑːns / (BrE)
to walk or move in a lively, proud, or showy way, often to attract attention

Examples

To prance down the street
Children pranced happily through the puddles after the rain stopped.
He couldn't help but prance around the office after getting his promotion.
The model began to prance down the runway, captivating the audience with her confident steps.
She pranced onto the stage, full of energy and confidence.
The children pranced in their costumes during the parade.
The young colt loves to prance around the paddock every morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strut - to walk with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant or conceited gait
