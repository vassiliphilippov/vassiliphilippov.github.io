soak

noun
an act of soaking for washing or softening
Pronunciation: / soʊk / (AmE), / səʊk / (BrE)
the act or process of immersing something in liquid to wash, soften, or clean it

Examples

A long soak
After a muddy hike, his boots needed a good soak in warm water.
She gave her stained shirt a long soak before washing it.
The recipe recommends a quick soak of the beans overnight.
After a long hike, a hot bath and a good soak helped relax her muscles.
Heavy rain gave the garden a thorough soak, reviving all the plants.
The beans need a soak in water overnight before cooking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drench - to make thoroughly wet
  • degree lower: rinse - to wash lightly, especially to remove soap
  • synonym: wash - an act of washing something
