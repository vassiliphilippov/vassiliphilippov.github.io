unrooted

adjective
without a root node
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈruːtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈruːtɪd / (BrE)
not having a designated root node, especially in reference to trees in mathematics or biology that do not indicate direction or ancestry.

Examples

Unrooted tree diagram
The researchers constructed an unrooted tree to compare genetic similarities among the species.
In graph theory, an unrooted tree does not specify a starting point or direction.
They preferred to use an unrooted diagram to avoid making assumptions about evolutionary ancestry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rooted - having a designated root node, showing direction or ancestry
