studious

adjective
devoted to study or reading
Pronunciation: / ˈstuːdiəs / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəs / (BrE)
spending a lot of time studying or reading, or showing great care and attention in doing something

Examples

A studious student
Despite his athletic talents, he remained studious and dedicated to his education.
She has always been a studious student, rarely seen without a book in hand.
The studious atmosphere in the library encouraged him to concentrate on his work.
His studious attention to the project impressed his manager.
She made a studious effort to learn every detail about the new process.
With studious care, he arranged the photos on the wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bookish - fond of reading or studying
  • synonym: careful - giving attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • antonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought
  • synonym: diligent - having or showing care and conscientiousness in one's work or duties
  • synonym: industrious - diligent and hardworking
  • antonym: negligent - failing to take proper care in doing something
