refeeding

noun
restoring nutrition
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of gradually reintroducing food to someone after a period of starvation or fasting

Examples

Medical refeeding
After weeks without proper nutrition, careful refeeding was necessary to help the patient recover.
Doctors monitored the refeeding process closely to prevent any complications.
Improper refeeding can lead to serious health risks in malnourished individuals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rehydration - the process of restoring lost fluids to the body
  • antonym: starvation - a state of extreme hunger due to lack of food
