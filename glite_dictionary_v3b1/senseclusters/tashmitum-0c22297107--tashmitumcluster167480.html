Tashmitum

proper noun
Mesopotamian goddess, consort of Nabu
Pronunciation: / tɑʃˈmiːtəm / (AmE), / tæʃˈmɪtəm / (BrE)
In Mesopotamian mythology, the consort of the god Nabu, worshipped as a goddess of wisdom and listening.

Examples

Tashmitum goddess wisdom
A statue believed to represent Tashmitum was discovered among the ruins of a Mesopotamian temple.
Ancient Babylonians worshipped Tashmitum alongside her husband, Nabu, as part of their religious rituals.
The myths describe Tashmitum as a goddess who listens to prayers and bestows wisdom.

Related Terms
