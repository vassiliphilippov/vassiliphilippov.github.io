pay

noun
money earned from work
1.0 48/m
Pronunciation: / peÉª / (AmE), / peÉª / (BrE)
money received for work done or services rendered; wages or salary.

Examples

Weekly pay
He was delighted to receive his first pay at the end of the week.
Most workers expect their pay to reflect their efforts and skills.
She negotiated for a higher pay before accepting the new job.

Synonyms

  • remuneration
    7.6 0.97/m
    remuneration is a formal term for money or benefits paid for work, while pay is the common everyday word.
  • earnings
    2.3 33/m
    earnings are the total money a person gets from work or investments, while pay means money received for work.
  • salary
    1.3 42/m
    salary is a fixed regular payment often paid monthly to professionals, while pay is the general word for money received for work.
  • wage
    1.4 89/m
    wage is a regular payment usually paid hourly or weekly for manual work, while pay means money received for work.
  • compensation
    3.6 20/m
    compensation can mean money or other payment after injury or loss, while pay refers specifically to wages or salary for work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deduction - an amount subtracted from pay, e.g. for taxes
  • synonym: salary - regular payment, typically monthly, for professional work
  • synonym: wage - payment for work, usually based on hours worked
