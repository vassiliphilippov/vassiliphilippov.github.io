terrine

noun
meat, fish, or vegetable loaf cooked in a dish
Pronunciation: / təˈrin / (AmE), / təˈriːn / (BrE)
a dish of seasoned meat, fish, or vegetables, often in the form of a pâté, cooked and served in a deep earthenware dish

Examples

A terrine dish
The chef prepared a rabbit terrine for the appetizer course at the elegant dinner.
Their restaurant specializes in rustic French dishes like duck terrine and country-style pâté.
You should slice the terrine with a sharp knife to keep the layers intact.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pâté - a rich, savory paste made from finely chopped or pureed meat, fish, or vegetables
