two touch drill

noun
soccer exercise limiting players to two touches
7.6 4.4
Pronunciation: /tuː tʌtʃ drɪl/ (AmE), /tuː tʌtʃ drɪl/ (BrE)
A practice exercise, especially in soccer, where players are required to control and pass the ball using no more than two touches.

Examples

The two touch drill practice
Coaches often use the two touch drill to encourage teamwork and accuracy in passing.
During practice, the team worked on their two touch drill to improve passing speed.
The two touch drill helps players develop quick decision-making skills on the ball.
