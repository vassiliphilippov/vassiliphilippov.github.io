no-show

verb
fail to attend (verb)
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈnoʊ.ʃoʊ/ (AmE), /ˈnəʊ.ʃəʊ/ (BrE)
to fail to attend or be present at a scheduled event or appointment, often without giving prior notice.

Examples

To no-show an appointment
She decided to no-show the meeting due to an unavoidable scheduling conflict.
He no-showed the interview, and we couldn't reach him afterward.
Many volunteers no-show without warning, which makes event planning difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attend - to be present at an event or meeting; opposite of fail to appear
  • synonym: miss - to fail to attend or be present for (an event, appointment, etc.)
  • synonym: skip - to intentionally not attend (an event or obligation); informal
