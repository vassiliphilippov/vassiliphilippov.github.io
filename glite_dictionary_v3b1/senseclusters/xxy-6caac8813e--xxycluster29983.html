XXY

noun
genetic condition with an extra X chromosome
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛks ˌɛks ˈwaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛks ˌɛks ˈwaɪ / (BrE)
having an extra X chromosome (resulting in two X chromosomes and one Y); used to describe a genetic condition in males that causes Klinefelter's syndrome.

Examples

The XXY chromosome
Genetic testing confirmed the presence of an XXY chromosome pattern in the patient.
Individuals with XXY may experience learning difficulties and delayed development.
The doctor suspected XXY after observing certain physical traits in the adolescent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Klinefelter's syndrome - genetic condition characterized by the XXY chromosome complement
