piecemeal

adjective, adverb
one thing or step at a time
7.0 1.94/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpiÀêsÀåmiÀêl / (AmE), / ÀàpiÀêsmiÀêl /; / ÀàpiÀêsÀåmiÀêl / (BrE)
done or happening in stages or small pieces, not all at once

Examples

A piecemeal approach
Attempts at solving the problem have been piecemeal and ineffective so far.
She prefers a comprehensive plan rather than the piecemeal approach currently being used.
The reforms were introduced in a piecemeal fashion over several years.
The company is upgrading its computer systems piecemeal, handling one department at a time.
The reforms were introduced piecemeal rather than through a single comprehensive change.
Their house was renovated piecemeal over several years whenever they could afford it.

Synonyms

  • step-by-step
    2.8 2.23/m
    step-by-step is done in a clear order, with each stage finished before the next, while piecemeal means done in parts.
  • stepwise
    6.0 1.29/m
    stepwise describes moving forward in defined steps or stages, whereas piecemeal describes doing work in separate pieces.
  • in stages
    3.5 0.57/m
    in stages means happening gradually with each part completed before the next, while piecemeal means done in small separate pieces.
  • in small stages
    3.9 0.00/m
    in small stages suggests moving forward by small planned steps, whereas piecemeal suggests separate pieces rather than a whole plan.
  • bit by bit
    2.7 0.92/m
    bit by bit means gradually over time and often slowly, while piecemeal emphasizes doing things in separate small parts.
  • little by little
    2.3 2.27/m
    little by little means slowly and steadily increasing or changing, whereas piecemeal focuses on doing something in separate pieces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gradually - in a slow, step-by-step manner
  • antonym: suddenly - quickly and unexpectedly
