hostile witness

noun
witness adverse to questioner's case (law)
7.3 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ‘stÉ™l ËˆwÉªtnÉ™s / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ’staÉªl ËˆwÉªtnÉ™s / (BrE)
A witness in a court case whose testimony is expected to be adverse to the party that called them, and who may therefore be questioned as if under cross-examination.

Examples

Hostile witness in court
Because he was declared a hostile witness, the lawyer was allowed to ask leading questions.
The court recognized him as a hostile witness after he contradicted his previous statement.
The prosecutor treated her as a hostile witness during the cross-examination.

Synonyms
