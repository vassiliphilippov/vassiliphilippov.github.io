to become dry, weak, or shriveled; to lose freshness, strength, or vitality (especially of plants, but also people or things)

Examples

Plants wither quickly
After weeks without rain, the crops began to wither in the fields.
As the years passed, her enthusiasm seemed to wither away.
The flowers will quickly wither if you don't put them in water.
If you don't water the flowers regularly, they will quickly wither and die.
The leaves began to wither under the harsh summer sun.
Time and neglect caused the once-beautiful bouquet to wither in the vase.

Synonyms

  • shrivel
    4.4 2.87/m
    shrivel makes plants or leaves dry and wrinkled from lost moisture, while wither means losing freshness, strength, or life more broadly.
  • shrivel up
    3.5 0.33/m
    shrivel up describes becoming small and wrinkled from no water, while wither can mean losing strength without full drying.
  • dry up
    2.7 1.56/m
    dry up means to lose all moisture and become completely dry, while wither can also mean slow loss of life or strength.
  • fade
    1.2 40/m
    fade means to lose color, brightness, or strength slowly, while wither implies drying, shriveling, or loss of life in plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fade - to gradually lose color, brightness, or strength
  • synonym: shrivel - to become wrinkled and contracted, especially due to loss of moisture
  • antonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful
  • synonym: wilt - to lose strength and become limp, often from lack of water (especially of plants)
