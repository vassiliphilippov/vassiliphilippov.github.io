preclosing

noun
pre-closing procedures
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈkloʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkləʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
the actions, checks, or meeting that occur immediately before the official closing of a transaction, especially in real estate or finance

Examples

Preclosing meeting
The lawyer scheduled a preclosing meeting to review all the necessary documents before the sale.
During the preclosing, the buyer confirmed that all repairs had been completed as agreed.
A thorough preclosing can help prevent last-minute surprises on the day of closing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closing - the final step in completing a transaction, especially in real estate
  • synonym: preparation - the process of getting ready for something
