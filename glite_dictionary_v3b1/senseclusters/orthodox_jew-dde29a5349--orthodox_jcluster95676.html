Orthodox Jew

noun
Jew who strictly follows traditional Jewish law
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.θə.dɑːks dʒuː/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.θə.dɒks dʒuː/ (BrE)
A Jew who strictly observes traditional Mosaic law and religious practices within Orthodox Judaism.

Examples

Orthodox Jew observes Sabbath
As an Orthodox Jew, she observes the Sabbath from Friday evening to Saturday night.
His grandfather was an Orthodox Jew who went to synagogue every morning.
The community center offers resources specifically for Orthodox Jew families.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Reform Jew - A Jew who follows the practices and beliefs of Reform Judaism, a more liberal movement
