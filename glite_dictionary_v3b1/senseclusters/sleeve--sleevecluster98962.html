sleeve

verb
to cover with sleeve
5.5
Pronunciation: / sliːv / (AmE), / sliːv / (BrE)
to put something into a protective cover or sleeve, especially for storage or protection

Examples

To sleeve a card
She carefully sleeved each rare comic book to keep them in perfect condition.
Before shipping, the technician sleeves every circuit board to prevent static damage.
Collectors often spend hours sleeving their trading cards to protect them from wear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encase - to enclose or cover something completely
  • antonym: unwrap - to remove a covering or wrapper from something
