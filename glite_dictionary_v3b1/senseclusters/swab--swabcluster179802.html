swab

noun
absorbent cotton applicator or sampler
Pronunciation: / swɑb / (AmE), / swɒb / (BrE)
A small piece of absorbent material, usually cotton, attached to the end of a stick, used for applying medication, cleaning wounds, or collecting samples.

Examples

A throat swab
A throat swab was taken to test for infection.
He dipped the swab in antiseptic and gently applied it to her cut.
The nurse used a swab to clean the wound before applying the bandage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cotton bud - a stick with absorbent material at each end, mainly for cleaning
  • synonym: gauze - thin transparent fabric used for medical dressings
