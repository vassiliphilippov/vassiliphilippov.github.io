strike

noun
work stoppage by employees in protest
3.7 19/m
Pronunciation: / straÉªk / (AmE), / straÉªk / (BrE)
an organized stoppage of work by employees as a form of protest, typically to demand better pay or working conditions

Examples

A factory strike
During the strike, factories across the city stood silent for days.
Teachers threatened to go on strike unless their demands for better salaries were met.
The union called a strike after negotiations with management broke down.

Synonyms

  • work stoppage
    4.2 0.38/m
    work stoppage is any group refusal to work as protest, while strike is an organized stoppage demanding pay or better conditions.
  • walkout
    4.8 0.84/m
    walkout is leaving a workplace together to protest, while strike is an organized stoppage of work to demand pay or conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lockout - when employers prevent workers from entering the workplace during a dispute
  • synonym: walkout - a sudden refusal to work as a form of protest
