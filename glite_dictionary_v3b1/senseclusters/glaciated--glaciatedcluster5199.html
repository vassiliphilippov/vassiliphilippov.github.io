glaciated

adjective
covered with or shaped by glaciers
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃiˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪsɪeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a region or surface) covered with ice or shaped by the movement of glaciers.

Examples

Glaciated mountain valley
A glaciated landscape often features U-shaped valleys and sharp mountain ridges.
Scientists studied the glaciated terrain to understand past climate changes.
The valleys in this region are deeply glaciated and filled with clear blue lakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unglaciated - not covered by or affected by glaciers
