shovelnose

adjective
broad, flat snout
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈʃʌv.əl.noʊz/ (AmE), /ˈʃʌv.əl.nəʊz/ (BrE)
having a broad, flat snout resembling the shape of a shovel, especially used for certain animals such as fish or rays

Examples

Shovelnose sturgeon
The shovelnose sturgeon is easily recognized by its wide, flattened snout.
Researchers observed a shovelnose ray gliding along the sandy ocean floor.
Many species with a shovelnose adaptation are well suited to digging in soft substrates.
