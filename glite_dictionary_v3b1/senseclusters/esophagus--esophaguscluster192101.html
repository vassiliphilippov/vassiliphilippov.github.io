esophagus

noun
tube linking throat and stomach
3.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɑːfəɡəs /; / iˈsɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / ɪˈsɒfəɡəs /; / iːˈsɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
the muscular tube that carries food and liquids from the throat (pharynx) to the stomach

Examples

The esophagus tube
Acid reflux occurs when stomach acid flows back into the esophagus.
Food passes through the esophagus before reaching the stomach.
The surgeon examined the patient's esophagus for signs of damage.
Acid reflux occurs when stomach acid travels back up the oesophagus.
After swallowing, food moves from the mouth into the oesophagus on its way to the stomach.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's oesophagus for signs of damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: esophagus - the American English spelling of 'oesophagus'; the muscular tube connecting the throat to the stomach
