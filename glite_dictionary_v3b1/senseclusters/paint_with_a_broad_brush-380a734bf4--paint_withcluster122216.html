paint with a broad brush

verb
describe in broad, general terms
7.2 7.2
Pronunciation: / peɪnt wɪð ə brɔd brʌʃ / (AmE), / peɪnt wɪð ə brɔːd brʌʃ / (BrE)
to describe or judge something or someone in very general terms, often overlooking important details or differences

Examples

To paint with a broad brush
Politicians often paint with a broad brush to make their arguments sound simpler.
The article painted with a broad brush and ignored unique local differences.
We shouldn't paint with a broad brush when discussing people's experiences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paint with a fine brush - to pay attention to details or be specific
