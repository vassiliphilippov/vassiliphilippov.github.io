spirit

noun
the mood or atmosphere of a place or event
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪt /; / ˈspɪr.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪt /; / ˈspɪr.ɪt / (BrE)
the essential quality, character, or intended meaning of a place, time, or action

Examples

Spirit of the law
She tried to capture the spirit of the 1960s in her artwork.
The spirit of cooperation was evident throughout the company's annual meeting.
The spirit of the festival was joyful and welcoming, encouraging everyone to participate.
Although the company followed the rules in the spirit of fairness, some employees felt excluded.
The spirit of the agreement was to promote cooperation, not competition.
You should obey the spirit of the law, not just its letter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atmosphere - the pervading tone or mood of a place, situation, or creative work
  • antonym: letter - literal wording of a text or law
  • synonym: mood - a temporary state of mind or feeling; the atmosphere of a place
