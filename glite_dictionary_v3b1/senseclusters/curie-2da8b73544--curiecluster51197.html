Curie

proper noun
French physicist Pierre Curie (1859–1906)
Pronunciation: / ˈkjʊri / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəri / (BrE)
Pierre Curie (1859–1906), a French physicist known for his work on radioactivity and as the husband of Marie Curie.

Examples

Curie and radium
Many textbooks mention Curie as a key figure in the discovery of radium.
Pierre Curie collaborated with his wife, Marie Curie, in pioneering research on radioactivity.
The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Pierre Curie and his wife in 1903.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Marie Curie - Polish-born physicist and chemist, wife of Pierre Curie, noted for research on radioactivity
