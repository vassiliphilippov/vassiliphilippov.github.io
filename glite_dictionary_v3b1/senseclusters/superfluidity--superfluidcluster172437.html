superfluidity

noun
frictionless fluid state
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərfluːˈɪdəti / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəfluːˈɪdəti / (BrE)
a state of matter in which a fluid can flow without friction or resistance, often at very low temperatures

Examples

Superfluidity in helium
Researchers observed superfluidity in helium-4 when it was cooled to extremely low temperatures.
The unique property of superfluidity allows the liquid to climb the walls of its container.
Understanding superfluidity has led to advances in quantum physics and low-temperature research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superfluid - a fluid that exhibits superfluidity
  • antonym: viscosity - the property of a fluid that resists flow
