corrective

noun
device used to correct injury or disease
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktɪv / (BrE)
something (such as a device, action, or policy) that corrects or fixes a problem or fault

Examples

A corrective device
After the injury, he wore a special corrective on his knee for six months.
She was fitted with a corrective to help straighten her spine.
The dentist recommended a dental corrective for her misaligned teeth.
The government implemented several correctives to address the financial crisis.
After the audit, the team proposed a list of correctives to improve efficiency.
Effective correctives can prevent similar mistakes from happening in the future.
