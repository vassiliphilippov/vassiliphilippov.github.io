clientelist

noun
political broker
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪəntəˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈklaɪəntəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who participates in clientelism, especially by exchanging goods, favors, or services for political support or votes

Examples

A clientelist broker
The mayor relied on a trusted clientelist to secure votes in the local community.
A skilled clientelist can influence election outcomes by distributing favors to supporters.
Many accused the politician of being a clientelist who traded jobs for loyalty.
