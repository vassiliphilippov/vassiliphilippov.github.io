rhabdomyosarcoma

noun
malignant muscle tumor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ræbˌdoʊmaɪoʊsɑrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ræbˌdəʊmɪəʊsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a highly malignant tumor originating from striated (skeletal) muscle tissue, most often occurring in children

Examples

A rhabdomyosarcoma tumor
Doctors suspected rhabdomyosarcoma after the patient presented with a rapidly growing mass in his leg.
Research on rhabdomyosarcoma has led to improved survival rates in recent years.
The child's diagnosis of rhabdomyosarcoma prompted immediate treatment with chemotherapy and radiation.
