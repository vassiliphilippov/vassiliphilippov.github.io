preadolescent

noun
older child, not teen
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌædəˈlɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌadəˈlɛsənt / (BrE)
a child who is older than a young child but not yet a teenager, typically between ages 9 and 12

Examples

A preadolescent child
Many preadolescents begin to develop new interests as they approach their teenage years.
The summer camp is designed specifically for preadolescents aged 10 to 12.
Teachers often notice big changes in behavior among preadolescents during the last years of elementary school.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: child - a young person who is not yet an adult
  • degree higher: teenager - a person aged between 13 and 19 years
