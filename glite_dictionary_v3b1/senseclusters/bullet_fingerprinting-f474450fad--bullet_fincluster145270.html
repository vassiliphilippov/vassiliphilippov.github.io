bullet fingerprinting

noun
forensic analysis of bullet/gun marks
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlət ˌfɪŋɡərˌprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt ˌfɪŋɡəˌprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of identifying the firearm that fired a bullet by analyzing the unique marks left on the bullet and shell by the gun

Examples

Bullet fingerprinting process
Advances in bullet fingerprinting have made it easier to solve firearm-related crimes.
Because of bullet fingerprinting, investigators quickly identified the weapon involved in the shooting.
The police used bullet fingerprinting to match the bullets found at the scene to a specific handgun.
