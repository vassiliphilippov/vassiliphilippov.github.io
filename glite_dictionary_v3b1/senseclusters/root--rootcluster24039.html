root

verb
to plant by inserting roots
2.6 1.92/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀêt /; / rut / (AmE), / ruÀêt / (BrE)
to plant or fix a plant in the ground, or to cause a plant or cutting to develop roots and begin to grow

Examples

To root a cutting
Begin by rooting the plant in loose, well-drained soil to help it establish.
In spring, she will root the cuttings in moist soil to encourage new growth.
They carefully rooted the young saplings along the edge of the garden.
He hoped the tropical plant would root successfully in the new climate.
Once transplanted, the shrub should root within a few weeks.
These cuttings will root if you keep them moist and warm.
Gardeners often root new plants from cuttings in water before planting them in soil.
He tried to root the rose bush by placing it in moist sand for several weeks.
Some species are difficult to root and require special growth hormones.

Synonyms

    1.0 27/m
    plant means putting seeds or seedlings into soil so they can grow, while root means causing a cutting to form roots and start growing.
