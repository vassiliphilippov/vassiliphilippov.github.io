druthers

noun
one's preference or choice (informal)
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌðərz / (AmE), / ˈdrʌðəz / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly North American) one's own preference, choice, or way; the right or chance to choose what one would do.

Examples

Given their druthers
Given their druthers, most people would choose to work from home.
If I had my druthers, I'd spend the whole day reading.
You don't always get your druthers at work, so you must compromise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over others
