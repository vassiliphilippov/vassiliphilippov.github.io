de-emphasis

noun
signal noise reduction
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈɛmfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈɛmfəsɪs / (BrE)
the process of reducing or attenuating high-frequency components of a signal, especially to reverse earlier pre-emphasis and reduce noise in audio transmission.

Examples

Audio de-emphasis
The audio engineer applied de-emphasis to the recording to minimize unwanted high-frequency noise.
Modern FM receivers automatically use de-emphasis circuits to improve sound quality.
Without proper de-emphasis, the broadcast would sound harsh and noisy to listeners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attenuation - the reduction of signal strength
  • antonym: pre-emphasis - the process of increasing high-frequency components of a signal before transmission
