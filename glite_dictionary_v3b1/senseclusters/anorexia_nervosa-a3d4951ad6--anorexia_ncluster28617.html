anorexia nervosa

noun
psychological eating disorder marked by self-starvation
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈrɛksiə nɚˈvoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈrɛk.si.ə nɜːˈvəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A serious psychological disorder characterized by an intense fear of gaining weight and a distorted body image, leading to self-starvation and excessive weight loss.

Examples

Anorexia nervosa disorder
Many teenagers with anorexia nervosa struggle to recognize the seriousness of their condition.
The doctor explained that anorexia nervosa can have severe physical and psychological consequences if left untreated.
Treatment for anorexia nervosa often involves therapy, nutritional counseling, and medical monitoring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulimia nervosa - an eating disorder involving binge eating followed by purging
