japery

noun
foolish joking or clownish behavior
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪpəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪpəri / (BrE)
foolish or playful behavior; acting in a joking or clownish manner, often to entertain or amuse others.

Examples

Constant japery
His constant japery at the dinner table had everyone laughing uncontrollably.
There was a hint of japery in his speech, lightening the mood in the crowded room.
Tired of their childish japery, she asked the boys to behave more seriously.
