unrest

noun
state of agitation or disturbance
3.4 7.14/m
Pronunciation: / É™nËˆrÉ›st / (AmE), / ÊŒnËˆrÉ›st / (BrE)
A state in which people are dissatisfied or agitated, often leading to disturbance, disorder, or attempts to bring about change.

Examples

Political unrest
After weeks of political unrest, the situation finally began to stabilize.
Labor unrest often leads to strikes and disruptions in production.
The city experienced widespread unrest following the sudden government announcement.

Synonyms

  • agitation
    4.6 1.23/m
    agitation is noisy or active protest by people, while unrest is a wider state of public dissatisfaction that may lead to disorder.
  • ferment
    7.2 0.73/m
    ferment describes growing excitement and change in society, while unrest is the general dissatisfaction that can cause public disorder.
  • turmoil
    4.8 8.85/m
    turmoil is a state of great confusion and sudden change, while unrest is more focused on public protest and dissatisfaction.
  • disorder
    4.1 6.49/m
    disorder is the visible breakdown of law and order in public places, while unrest is the underlying dissatisfaction that may cause it.
  • discontent
    5.1 4.67/m
    discontent is a private feeling of dissatisfaction or desire for better, while unrest is a public, often noisy state leading to change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disturbance - interruption of a state of peace or quiet
  • antonym: peace - a state of calm or absence of conflict
