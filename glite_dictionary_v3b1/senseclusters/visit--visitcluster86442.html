visit

noun
a temporary stay as a guest
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪzɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪt / (BrE)
an occasion when someone goes to see a person or place, either as a guest, for pleasure, for official or professional reasons, or for a short time

Examples

A visit to Paris
During her visit to France, she stayed with an old friend.
My grandparents' visit lasted for three weeks over the summer.
We really enjoyed your visit and hope you'll come again soon.
During his official visit, the ambassador met with key government officials.
The health inspector made a visit to the restaurant to check its hygiene standards.
The team's annual visit ensures all safety regulations are followed.
She made a visit to her lawyer to discuss the contract.
The doctor’s office charged a fee for each visit.
You should schedule a visit if you have any concerns about your health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appointment - an arrangement to meet with someone at a particular time
  • synonym: call - a short social visit
  • synonym: consultation - a meeting with an expert, especially a doctor, for advice or treatment
  • antonym: departure - the act of leaving a place
  • synonym: inspection - careful examination or official scrutiny
  • synonym: stay - a period spent in a place
