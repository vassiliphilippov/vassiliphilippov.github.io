blinkered

verb
to restrict vision
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪŋkərd / (AmE), / ˈblɪŋkəd / (BrE)
to fit an animal, especially a horse, with blinkers, or to restrict someone's ability to see or consider all options.

Examples

To blinkered a horse
The trainer blinkered the horse to help it focus during the race.
Some leaders have blinkered their teams by ignoring new ideas.
He felt his upbringing had blinkered him to other cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broaden - to make wider or more inclusive
  • synonym: limit - to restrict or confine
