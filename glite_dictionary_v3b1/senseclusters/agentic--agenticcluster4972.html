agentic

adjective
having independent agency
Pronunciation: / eɪˈdʒɛntɪk / (AmE), / eɪˈdʒɛntɪk /; / əˈdʒɛntɪk / (BrE)
showing or having the ability to act independently, make choices, and take initiative

Examples

Agentic person
The psychologist described her as an agentic individual who takes initiative in group projects.
Developing an agentic mindset can help people overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
Children become more agentic as they learn to make decisions for themselves.
Her agentic attitude helped her overcome many challenges at work.
The study focused on how children develop agentic behaviors in new situations.
He took an agentic role in leading the project to success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autonomous - having the freedom to act independently
  • antonym: passive - accepting or allowing what happens without active response
  • synonym: proactive - taking action in advance rather than reacting
