alit

verb
to dismount or disembark
Pronunciation: / əˈlaɪt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪt / (BrE)
to get down from a vehicle, horse, or other form of transport; to disembark or dismount

Examples

He alit from the bus
The passengers alit from the train as it arrived at the station.
After a long journey, he alit from the carriage and looked around.
She alit gracefully from the horse and greeted her friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dismount - to get down from a horse or bicycle
  • antonym: embark - to get on or board a vehicle or ship
