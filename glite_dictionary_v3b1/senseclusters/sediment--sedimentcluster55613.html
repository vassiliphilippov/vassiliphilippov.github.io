sediment

noun
solid matter deposited by natural processes
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛdəmənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛdɪmənt / (BrE)
solid material that settles at the bottom of a liquid or is deposited by water, wind, or ice over time.

Examples

River sediment
After the floodwaters receded, a thick layer of sediment covered the streets.
River sediment can build up over time and change the shape of the riverbed.
Wine bottles often contain sediment at the bottom that should not be poured into the glass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deposit - a layer of accumulated matter left by a natural process
  • antonym: erosion - gradual destruction or removal of soil or rock by natural forces
