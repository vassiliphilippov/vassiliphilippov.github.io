epigenome

noun
chemical DNA modifications
9.9
Pronunciation: /e.pɪ.ˈdʒiː.noʊm/ (AmE), /e.pɪ.ˈdʒiː.nəʊm/ (BrE)
the complete set of chemical modifications to DNA and associated proteins that regulate gene activity in a cell

Examples

The epigenome map
Scientists are mapping the epigenome to better understand how genes are turned on and off.
Changes in the epigenome can influence a person's risk of developing certain diseases.
The study of the epigenome is helping researchers discover new treatments for cancer.
