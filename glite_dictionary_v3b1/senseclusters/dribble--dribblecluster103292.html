dribble

noun
small flow or fall of drops of liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪb.əl /; / ˈdrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪb.əl /; / ˈdrɪbəl / (BrE)
a small, slow flow of liquid or saliva in drops; the action or result of liquid or saliva dripping slowly

Examples

A dribble of water
A dribble of rainwater ran down the windowpane during the storm.
She wiped the dribble of milk from her baby's chin.
There was only a dribble of sauce left at the bottom of the bottle.
A small dribble of saliva escaped as he slept in the chair.
He wiped the dribble from his mouth with a handkerchief.
The baby had a little dribble on her chin after her nap.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drip - the act or sound of falling drops of liquid
  • synonym: drool - let saliva flow from the mouth uncontrollably
