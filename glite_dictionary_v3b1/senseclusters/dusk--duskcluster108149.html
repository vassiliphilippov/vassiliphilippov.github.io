dusk

verb
grow dark at evening
Pronunciation: / dʌsk / (AmE), / dʌsk / (BrE)
(of the day or sky) to become dusk; to grow dark as evening approaches.

Examples

The sky dusks
As the sun set, the sky began to dusk over the quiet town.
It was around eight o'clock when the lake started to dusk and the first stars appeared.
The landscape slowly dusked, hiding the hills in shadow.
