pasquinade

noun
humorous satirical composition
Pronunciation: / ˌpæskwɪˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌpæskwɪˈneɪd / (BrE)
a satire or lampoon, especially one posted in a public place, that humorously imitates or ridicules an individual's style or behavior

Examples

A pasquinade satire
During the festival, several witty pasquinades were posted on the town square for all to read.
His sharp pen produced a pasquinade that left the politician red-faced with embarrassment.
The columnist's latest pasquinade mocked the mayor's extravagant new project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lampoon - a written satire used to ridicule or attack someone
  • synonym: parody - an imitation of the style of someone or something with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
