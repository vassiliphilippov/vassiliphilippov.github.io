biomass

noun
total mass of living matter in an area
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.oʊˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊ.mæs / (BrE)
the total amount of living matter, such as plants and animals, in a given area or volume at a particular time

Examples

Forest biomass
Changes in temperature can affect the biomass of phytoplankton in the ocean.
Researchers measured the forest's biomass to assess its carbon storage capacity.
The wetland supports a large biomass of insects, birds, and amphibians.
