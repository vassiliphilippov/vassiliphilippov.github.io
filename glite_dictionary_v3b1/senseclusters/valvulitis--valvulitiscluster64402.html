valvulitis

noun
inflammation of a valve (esp. cardiac)
9.1
Pronunciation: / vælvjuˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / valvjʊˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of a valve, especially a heart valve, often resulting from diseases such as rheumatic fever.

Examples

Valvulitis of heart
Early diagnosis of valvulitis is crucial for preventing permanent heart damage.
Rheumatic fever can sometimes cause valvulitis in children and young adults.
The patient's symptoms were consistent with valvulitis following a bacterial infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endocarditis - inflammation of the inner lining of the heart, including the heart valves
  • degree lower: myocarditis - inflammation of the heart muscle
