perverse

adjective
resistant to guidance or discipline
7.9 5.76/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàv…úrs /; / p…ôrÀàv…úÀêrs / (AmE), / p…ôÀàv…úÀês / (BrE)
deliberately behaving in a way that is unreasonable, contrary, or against what is accepted or right

Examples

A perverse attitude
Despite knowing the consequences, he adopted a perverse attitude and refused to follow the rules.
Her perverse refusal to accept help frustrated everyone in the group.
In a perverse mood, the child ignored every instruction given to him.
His perverse enjoyment of other people's discomfort disturbed everyone at the meeting.
It was a perverse decision to reward dishonesty rather than hard work.
The judge was frustrated by the perverse behavior of the defendant in the courtroom.
Despite knowing the right answer, he gave a perverse response just to be difficult.
His perverse attitude often led to unnecessary arguments with his colleagues.
She took a perverse pleasure in rejecting every suggestion he made.

Synonyms

  • contrary
    5.4 0.65/m
    contrary behavior is deliberately uncooperative or oppositional, while perverse behavior acts against what is right or accepted.
  • obstinate
    7.5 1.15/m
    obstinate people stubbornly refuse to change opinion or action, while perverse people deliberately behave against what is right or accepted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrary - deliberately doing the opposite of what is expected or desired
  • antonym: cooperative - willing to work together with others
  • synonym: depraved - morally corrupt or wicked
  • antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests
  • synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action
  • antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • synonym: wayward - difficult to control because of willful or perverse behavior
