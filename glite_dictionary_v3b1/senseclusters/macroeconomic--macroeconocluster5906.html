macroeconomic

adjective
relating to large-scale economic factors
Pronunciation: / ˌmæk.roʊˌi.kəˈnɑ.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəʊˌiː.kəˈnɒ.mɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the branch of economics concerned with large-scale or general economic factors, such as interest rates and national productivity.

Examples

Macroeconomic factors
Analysts are concerned about the macroeconomic impacts of rising inflation.
Her research focuses on macroeconomic trends in developing countries.
The government introduced new macroeconomic policies to stabilize the economy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microeconomic - relating to microeconomics or individual economic factors
