Whig

noun
member of historical U.S. political party
8.5
Pronunciation: / wɪɡ / (AmE), / wɪɡ / (BrE)
a member of the Whig Party, a major political party in the United States during the early and mid-19th century, before the Civil War

Examples

US Whig politician
Abraham Lincoln began his political career as a Whig before joining the Republican Party.
During the 1840s, the Whigs opposed the expansion of slavery into new territories.
Many Whig leaders later played significant roles in the formation of the Republican Party.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Democrat - a member of the Democratic Party in the United States
  • synonym: Whig - a member of the British Whig Party, active in the 17th–19th centuries
