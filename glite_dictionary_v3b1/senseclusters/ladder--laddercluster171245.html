ladder

verb
develop a run in fabric by snagging
Pronunciation: / ˈlædər / (AmE), / ˈladə / (BrE)
(of stockings, tights, or similar fabric) to develop a long hole or run as the threads come undone, typically due to a snag.

Examples

Stockings ladder
Be careful not to snag your stockings or they might ladder.
His socks laddered after catching on the rough edge of the bench.
She gasped when she noticed her tights had laddered during the commute.
