conodont

noun
tiny fossil tooth of extinct vertebrate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.nə.dɑnt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.nə.dɒnt / (BrE)
a tiny, cone-shaped fossil tooth belonging to an extinct primitive vertebrate of the order Conodonta, used to study geological time periods

Examples

Conodont fossil tooth
Because of their abundance and diversity, conodonts are useful for dating Paleozoic strata.
Paleontologists discovered several conodont elements in the limestone deposit.
The age of the rock layer was determined by analyzing conodont fossils found within it.
