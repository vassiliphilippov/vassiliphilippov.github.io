unconscientious

adjective
not careful or principled
8.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àå ånÀåk…ëÀên ÉiÀà…õn É…ôs / (AmE), / Àå ånÀåk…ín ÉiÀà…õn É…ôs / (BrE)
Not guided by or showing a sense of right and wrong; not conscientious or careful about doing what is right.

Examples

An unconscientious person
His unconscientious attitude led to frequent mistakes at work.
It is risky to trust important tasks to someone so unconscientious.
The manager was fired for being unconscientious in handling sensitive documents.

Synonyms

  • conscienceless
    9.5 0.04/m
    conscienceless shows a person has no inner sense of right and wrong, while unconscientious means careless about doing what is right.
  • unprincipled
    6.6 0.50/m
    unprincipled lacks moral rules and will ignore honesty for gain, while unconscientious describes carelessness about doing the right thing.
  • unscrupulous
    7.5 1.56/m
    unscrupulous acts without honesty and may exploit others for advantage, while unconscientious is simply careless about right and wrong.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conscientious - wishing to do what is right, especially to do one's work well and thoroughly
