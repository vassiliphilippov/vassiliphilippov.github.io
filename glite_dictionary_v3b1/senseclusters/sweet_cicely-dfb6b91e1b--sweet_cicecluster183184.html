sweet cicely

noun
a European aromatic herb with fern-like leaves
9.1
Pronunciation: / swit ˈsɪsəli / (AmE), / swiːt ˈsɪsɪli / (BrE)
a European herb (Myrrhis odorata) with fern-like leaves, white flowers, and a sweet aniseed flavor, used in gardens, cooking, and as a garnish

Examples

Sweet cicely plant
Gardeners value sweet cicely for its pleasant aniseed scent and delicate foliage.
In the spring, sweet cicely produces clusters of small white flowers that attract pollinators.
The leaves of sweet cicely are often used to sweeten fruit dishes naturally.
Finely chopped sweet cicely gives salads a mild aniseed flavor.
The chef sprinkled sweet cicely leaves over the roasted vegetables as a garnish.
You can use dried sweet cicely seeds to flavor homemade liqueurs.
