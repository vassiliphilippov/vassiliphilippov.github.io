discreetness

noun
quality of being discreet
2.8 6.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskritnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈskriːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being careful, prudent, and tactful in speech or actions to avoid causing offense, embarrassment, or distress

Examples

Show discreetness in speech
In sensitive situations, a little discreetness can prevent unnecessary conflict.
She handled the confidential matter with remarkable discreetness, ensuring no one overheard.
The success of the negotiations was due mainly to his discreetness and tact.
He appreciated her discreetness when discussing sensitive topics at work.
Her discreetness allowed her to manage the difficult situation without offending anyone.
Without discreetness, private matters can easily become public gossip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discretion - the quality of behaving cautiously or with good judgement
  • antonym: indiscretion - behavior or speech that is indiscreet or displays a lack of good judgment
  • synonym: tact - skill and sensitivity in dealing with others or difficult issues
