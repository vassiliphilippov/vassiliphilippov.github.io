alcohol group

noun
chemical group -OH
Pronunciation: / ˈælkoʊˌhɔl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈalkəhɒl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a functional group in chemistry consisting of a hydrogen atom bonded to an oxygen atom, which is attached to a carbon atom (-OH)

Examples

An alcohol group in chemistry
Organic molecules with an alcohol group are classified as alcohols.
The chemist identified an alcohol group attached to the carbon chain.
The presence of an alcohol group makes ethanol soluble in water.
