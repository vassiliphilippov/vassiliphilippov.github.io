zealotry

noun
fanatical intolerance of opposing views
9.5 0.33/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàziÀê.l…ô.tri / (AmE), / ÀàziÀê.l…ô.tri / (BrE)
Fanatical and uncompromising pursuit of religious, political, or other ideals, often with intolerance of differing opinions.

Examples

Zealotry for beliefs
Her zealotry for the cause made it difficult for her to engage in open discussion.
Political zealotry can divide communities and hinder productive debate.
The novel portrays the dangers of religious zealotry through its main antagonist.

Synonyms

  • fanaticism
    7.4 1.69/m
    fanaticism is extreme, blind enthusiasm that rejects other views, while zealotry is a stubborn, intolerant pursuit of beliefs.
  • extremism
    7.4 3.89/m
    extremism means holding very extreme views and pushing harsh actions, while zealotry is obsessive, uncompromising devotion that shuts out different opinions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fanaticism - excessive zeal or obsessiveness, especially in religion or politics
  • antonym: tolerance - willingness to accept views or behaviors different from one's own
