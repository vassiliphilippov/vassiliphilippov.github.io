Renaissance

noun
period of European cultural revival
3.5
Pronunciation: / rɛˈneɪsəns, ˌrɛnəˈsɑns /; / rɛˈneɪsəns / (AmE), / rɪˈneɪsəns, ˌrɛnəˈsɑ̃s /; / rɪˈneɪsəns / (BrE)
the period in European history from the 14th to the 17th century, marked by a revival of art, literature, and learning, bridging the Middle Ages and modern times

Examples

The Renaissance period
Historians often debate the extent of the Renaissance's influence on modern Western culture.
Many famous artists, such as Leonardo da Vinci, lived during the Renaissance.
The Renaissance saw a flourishing of art, science, and philosophy throughout Europe.
Many modern thinkers draw inspiration from the Italian Renaissance.
The Renaissance transformed Europe's cultural landscape through advancements in art and science.
The city became a center of learning during the Renaissance.
