Bankia setaceae

noun
a Pacific coast giant shipworm species
Pronunciation: / ˈbæŋ.ki.ə sɛˈteɪ.si.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.kɪ.ə sɛˈtiː.si.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of large shipworm found along the Pacific coast of North America, known for boring into wood submerged in seawater.

Examples

Bankia setaceae shipworm
An infestation of Bankia setaceae can significantly weaken submerged structures.
Scientists discovered a cluster of Bankia setaceae in the wooden pilings near the pier.
The destructive activities of Bankia setaceae have long threatened wooden ships along the Pacific coast.
