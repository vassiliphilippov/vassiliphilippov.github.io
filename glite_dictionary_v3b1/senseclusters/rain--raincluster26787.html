rain

noun
rapid outpouring or succession (figurative)
1.0 3.94/m
Pronunciation: / reÉªn / (AmE), / reÉªn / (BrE)
a rapid or abundant fall or outpouring of things happening in quick succession, often used figuratively.

Examples

A rain of gifts
After her promotion, she received a rain of congratulations from colleagues.
The new policy led to a rain of complaints at the customer service desk.
The speech triggered a rain of protests from the audience.

Synonyms

  • outpour
    5.7 0.00/m
    an outpour is a sudden large flow of feeling or help, while rain is a general outpouring used in many figurative cases.
  • torrent
    4.4 0.56/m
    a torrent is a sudden strong flow of water or words, while rain is a broader term for many quick outpourings.
  • deluge
    6.3 0.84/m
    a deluge is an overwhelming flood or heavy rain that fills places, while rain can mean any rapid outpouring.
  • shower
    1.0 11/m
    a shower is a short light fall of rain or small items, while rain usually means a larger or repeated outpouring.
  • downpour
    2.7 1.90/m
    a downpour is a heavy sudden rain that soaks quickly, while rain can also mean a quick figurative outpouring.
