sloganeering

noun
use of slogans, often superficial
9.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /sloʊ.ɡə.ˈnɪr.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /sləʊ.ɡə.ˈnɪə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the use of slogans, especially when used to persuade others with superficial or empty phrases rather than substantive arguments

Examples

Sloganeering in politics
Critics accused the company of sloganeering rather than taking meaningful action on environmental issues.
His speech was full of empty sloganeering that failed to address the real problems.
Political campaigns often rely on sloganeering instead of clear policy proposals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: debate - formal discussion or argument involving opposing views
  • synonym: propaganda - biased or misleading information, especially for political purposes
