from

preposition
cause or reason
1.0
Pronunciation: / frəm / (AmE), / frɒm / (BrE)
used to indicate the cause or reason for something happening

Examples

Sick from the flu
She missed the meeting from a severe flu that kept her in bed all week.
His hands were shaking from excitement as he opened the letter.
The road was slippery from the heavy rain that fell overnight.
