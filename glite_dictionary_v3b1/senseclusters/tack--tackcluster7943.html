tack

verb
sew with large, loose stitches
Pronunciation: / tæk / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
to sew together temporarily with large, loose stitches to hold the fabric in place before final sewing

Examples

To tack fabric pieces
He quickly tacked the pieces together and checked the fit.
Please tack the hem so I can see how the dress will look.
She decided to tack the fabric before sewing it permanently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baste - to sew temporarily with long, loose stitches to hold material in place
