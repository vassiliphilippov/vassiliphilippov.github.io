Deuteromycotina

noun
group of fungi with no known sexual stage
Pronunciation: / ˌduːt̬əroʊmaɪˈkoʊtə nə /; / duːˌtɛrəˌmaɪkəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌdjuːtəˌrəʊmaɪˈkəʊtɪnə /; / djuːˌtɛərəˌmʌɪkəˈtʌɪnə / (BrE)
a large group of fungi (historically called 'fungi imperfecti') for which no sexual reproductive stage has been observed

Examples

The Deuteromycotina fungi
Advances in molecular biology have led to the reclassification of some species previously placed in the Deuteromycotina.
Many plant diseases are caused by fungi classified in the Deuteromycotina.
The Deuteromycotina are often referred to as imperfect fungi due to their lack of a known sexual cycle.
Modern classification has largely replaced Deuteromycotina with more genetically accurate groups.
Only asexual reproduction is observed in the fungal species grouped as Deuteromycotina.
Some plant pathogens, formerly classified in Deuteromycotina, are crucial for agricultural research.
