mediately

adverb
indirectly, via intermediary
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiətli / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiətli / (BrE)
by means of something else; not directly but through an intermediary or medium

Examples

To mediately act
The government often acts mediately through various agencies to implement its policies.
Information was passed mediately rather than directly from the manager to the staff.
In some legal systems, justice is delivered mediately by appointed representatives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immediately - without anything in between; directly
  • synonym: indirectly - not in a direct way; through something else
