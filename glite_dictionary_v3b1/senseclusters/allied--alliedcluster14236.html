allied

adjective
joined by treaty or common purpose
Pronunciation: / əˈlaɪd / (AmE), / ˈælaɪd / (BrE)
joined or united with others for a common purpose, especially in a military, political, or organizational context

Examples

Allied forces
During World War II, the Allied forces worked together against the Axis powers.
Several allied nations met to discuss their joint response to the crisis.
The companies became allied in order to better compete in the market.
During the war, many smaller nations remained allied with the Western powers.
Several countries became allied in order to address the environmental crisis.
The two companies are allied against a common competitor in the marketplace.
