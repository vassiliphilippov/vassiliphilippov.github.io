denial

noun
legal rejection of a charge
1.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈnaɪəl / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (BrE)
a defendant's formal statement in court rejecting or disputing the truth of a charge or accusation

Examples

Court denial
A simple denial may not be enough if there is substantial evidence against the accused.
His lawyer filed a formal denial with the court the next morning.
The defendant entered a denial of all the allegations in the indictment.
