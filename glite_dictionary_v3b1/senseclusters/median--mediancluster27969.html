median

noun
middle value in a data set
3.1 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmiː.di.ən/ /; / /ˈmiː.dɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈmiː.di.ən/ /; / /ˈmiː.dɪ.ən/ (BrE)
the value separating the higher half from the lower half of a data set; specifically, the middle number in a sorted list, or the average of the two middle numbers if the list has an even number of values

Examples

The median of numbers
If the list of numbers is 3, 5, and 7, the median is 5.
The median is often used instead of the mean to describe typical income.
When there is an even number of values, the median is the average of the two middle ones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mean - average value, sum divided by count
  • synonym: mode - the value that appears most often in a data set
