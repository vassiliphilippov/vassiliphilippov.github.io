censure

noun
harsh criticism or official disapproval
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɛnʃə / (BrE)
the formal expression of severe disapproval or criticism, especially in an official context

Examples

A formal censure
After the mistake, the board issued a formal censure against the manager.
Public censure can damage a person's reputation for years.
The senator faced strong censure from his colleagues after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approval - the act of accepting or agreeing with something; positive judgment
  • synonym: condemnation - the act of declaring something as wrong or evil; strong disapproval
