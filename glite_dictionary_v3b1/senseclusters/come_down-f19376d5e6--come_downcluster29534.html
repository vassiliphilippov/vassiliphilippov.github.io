come down

verb
stop functioning; become unavailable (systems)
4.2 0.22/m
Pronunciation: / kÉ™m daÊŠn / (AmE), / kÉ™m daÊŠn / (BrE)
(of a computer system, website, service, or network) to stop functioning or become unavailable, often suddenly, because of an error, overload, or outage.

Examples

The website comes down
During the outage, the website can come down for several hours.
Last night the server came down after too many users tried to log in.
If the database keeps coming down, customers will lose trust in the service.

Synonyms

  • crash
    3.2 5.49/m
    crash is a sudden failure of a computer or program, while come down describes a service becoming unavailable, often due to overload.
  • go down
    3.4 6.18/m
    go down means a system or site stops working temporarily, while come down often suggests sudden unavailability from errors or overload.
  • break down
    3.2 8.68/m
    break down is when a machine or system stops working, often gradually or mechanically, while come down refers to sudden service unavailability.

Related Terms
