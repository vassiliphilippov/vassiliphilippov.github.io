noncontrastive

adjective
lacking contrast
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈstræstɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈstrɑːstɪv / (BrE)
not providing or showing a noticeable difference in color, tone, style, or other features

Examples

Noncontrastive colors
The artist used a noncontrastive color scheme to create a peaceful atmosphere in the painting.
Her outfit was intentionally noncontrastive, blending soft shades of beige and cream.
In linguistics, a noncontrastive sound does not distinguish between different words.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contrastive - showing a clear difference in color, tone, or style
  • synonym: monotone - having little or no variation in tone or color
