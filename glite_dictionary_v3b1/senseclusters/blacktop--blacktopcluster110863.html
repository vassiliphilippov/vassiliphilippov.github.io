blacktop

noun
a black paving material for roads
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈblæktɒp / (BrE)
A black bituminous material used for paving roads or other surfaces, typically laid over crushed stone.

Examples

A schoolyard blacktop
Children played basketball on the smooth blacktop behind the school.
The crew laid fresh blacktop on the main street last week.
The parking lot was resurfaced with blacktop after the harsh winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphalt - a mixture of dark bituminous pitch with sand or gravel, used for surfacing roads
  • antonym: concrete - a building material made from a mixture of cement, aggregate, and water
