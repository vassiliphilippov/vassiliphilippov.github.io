semidesert

noun
region between desert and temperate area
2.8 5.0
Pronunciation: /sem.ɪ.ˈdez.ərt/ /; / /ˈsem.ɪ.dez.ərt/ (AmE), /sem.ɪ.ˈdez.ət/ /; / /ˈsem.ɪ.dez.ət/ (BrE)
A region with characteristics similar to a desert but with slightly more moisture, typically found between deserts and more temperate regions.

Examples

A semidesert region
A semidesert region often supports more vegetation than a true desert but remains largely arid.
Rainfall in the semidesert is low, but some hardy plants still manage to survive.
She studied the wildlife of the semidesert bordering the Sahara.
