co-edit

verb
to edit jointly
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈedɪt / (BrE)
to edit a document, publication, or project together with one or more other people

Examples

To co-edit a book
They decided to co-edit the anthology to combine their expertise.
The two professors co-edited a textbook used in universities worldwide.
She was invited to co-edit a special issue of the journal with her colleague.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collaborate - to work together with others on a project
  • synonym: edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting or revising it
