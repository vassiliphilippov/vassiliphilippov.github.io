cholesterol

noun
a fatty substance in animal tissues and blood
1.0 3.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛstəˌrɔl / (AmE), / kəˈlɛstərɒl / (BrE)
A compound found in all animal tissues, especially in the blood and cell membranes, important for cell structure and hormone production but excessive amounts can contribute to heart disease.

Examples

High cholesterol levels
Doctors often recommend a low-fat diet to help lower cholesterol levels.
Eggs and organ meats are particularly rich in cholesterol.
High levels of cholesterol in the blood can increase the risk of heart disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lipid - a group of organic compounds including fats and oils
  • synonym: triglyceride - another type of fat found in blood
