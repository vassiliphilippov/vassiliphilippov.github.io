Examples

Economic reverberation
Global financial markets still felt the reverberation of the economic crisis decades later.
Her resignation sent reverberations through the entire organization, prompting further departures.
The reverberations of the policy change affected the company for years after its introduction.

Synonyms

  • repercussion
    6.7 5.01/m
    repercussion means an unintended consequence that appears later and affects others, while reverberation often suggests indirect results that continue.
  • knock-on effect
    4.7 0.05/m
    knock-on effect describes a chain of indirect impacts spreading to other areas, while reverberation focuses on a single indirect result lasting over time.
  • aftereffect
    5.1 0.95/m
    aftereffect is a delayed result that appears after an event, while reverberation suggests an indirect effect that continues and spreads.
  • lasting effect
    3.7 0.98/m
    lasting effect is a consequence that endures for a long time, while reverberation often refers to an indirect result that ripples outward.
