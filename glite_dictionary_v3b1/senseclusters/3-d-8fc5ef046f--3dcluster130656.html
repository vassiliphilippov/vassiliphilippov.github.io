3-D

adjective
having three dimensions; three-dimensional
Pronunciation: / ˌθriː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌθriː ˈdiː / (BrE)
having three dimensions or appearing to have depth, width, and height; three-dimensional.

Examples

3-D images
She wore special glasses to view the 3-D images on the screen.
The movie was shown in 3-D, making the action scenes feel incredibly realistic.
This printer can create detailed 3-D models for engineering projects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: 2-D - having or appearing to have only two dimensions
  • synonym: three-dimensional - having or appearing to have length, width, and depth
