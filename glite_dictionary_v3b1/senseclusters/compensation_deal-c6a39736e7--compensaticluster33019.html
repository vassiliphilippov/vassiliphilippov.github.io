compensation deal

noun
trade: goods-for-goods export agreement
6.5 4.5
Pronunciation: /kɑːm.pən.ˈseɪ.ʃən diːl/ /; / /kəm.pən.ˈseɪ.ʃən diːl/ (AmE), /kɒm.pən.ˈseɪ.ʃən diːl/ /; / /kɒm.pɛn.ˈseɪ.ʃən diːl/ (BrE)
an arrangement in which an exporter receives goods from an importer as part or all of the payment for exports, rather than cash

Examples

A compensation deal for goods
In a compensation deal, the exporter agreed to receive textiles instead of a cash payment.
Many developing countries prefer a compensation deal when foreign currency is scarce.
The company finalized a compensation deal with the foreign government to exchange machinery for raw materials.
