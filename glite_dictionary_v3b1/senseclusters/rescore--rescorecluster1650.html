rescore

verb
to assign new score
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈskɔr / (AmE), / ˌriːˈskɔː / (BrE)
to give a new score or rating to something after reviewing or correcting the original one

Examples

To rescore an exam

The competition rules allow judges to rescore performances if a mistake is found.
Because of a technical error, the system had to rescore all the entries.
The competition organizers decided to rescore all entries after discovering a calculation error.
If you believe your test was marked incorrectly, you can request to have it rescored.
