prepose

verb
place before in a sentence (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / priˈpoʊz / (AmE), / priˈpəʊz / (BrE)
(in linguistics) to place (a word or phrase) before another in a sentence.

Examples

To prepose a verb
In some questions, the auxiliary verb is preposed before the subject.
Languages like English tend to prepose prepositions, unlike many Asian languages.
The linguist explained how French often preposes adjectives before certain nouns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postpose - to place after another element in a sentence
