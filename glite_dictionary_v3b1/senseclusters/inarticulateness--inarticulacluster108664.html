inarticulateness

noun
lack of clear expression
9.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnɑːrˈtɪkjələtnəs / (AmE), / ɪnɑːˈtɪkjʊlɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unable to express thoughts, feelings, or ideas clearly or effectively, especially in speech

Examples

His inarticulateness in speech
His inarticulateness often made it difficult for him to share his true feelings with friends.
During the interview, her inarticulateness prevented her from explaining her qualifications clearly.
The inarticulateness of the speaker left the audience confused about the main message.
His inarticulateness made it difficult for others to understand his ideas during the meeting.
The actor overcame his childhood inarticulateness through years of speech training.
Some people mistake inarticulateness for a lack of intelligence, but that is not always the case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: articulateness - the ability to express oneself clearly and effectively
  • antonym: eloquence - the ability to express ideas clearly and effectively
  • synonym: incoherence - lack of clarity or logical connection in speech or writing
