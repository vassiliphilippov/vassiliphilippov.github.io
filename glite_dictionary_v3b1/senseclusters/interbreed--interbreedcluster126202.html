interbreed

verb
breed between different varieties or species
6.5 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™nt…ôrÀàbrid / (AmE), / Àå…™nt…ôÀàbriÀêd / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) to breed with individuals of a different variety, race, or species; to mix genetically by crossbreeding.

Examples

To interbreed animals
Farmers sometimes interbreed cattle to improve milk production.
Some plants can interbreed even when they come from distant geographic regions.
The two bird species rarely interbreed in the wild due to different mating calls.

Synonyms

  • crossbreed
    4.6 0.18/m
    crossbreed produces an animal or plant by mating different breeds or species, while interbreed means breeding across varieties or species.
  • hybridize
    7.0 0.56/m
    hybridize means making hybrids by crossing different breeds or species, while interbreed means breeding across varieties or species.
  • cross
    3.9 1.37/m
    cross means to breed using parents from different varieties or species, while interbreed emphasizes mixing genes between varieties or species.
  • cross-fertilize
    7.2 0.00/m
    cross-fertilize means to mix pollen or reproductive cells between individuals, while interbreed refers more broadly to mating and genetic mixing.
