hyperthermic

adjective
abnormally high temperature
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈθɜrmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈθɜːmɪk / (BrE)
having or relating to an abnormally high body temperature, or involving high temperature in medical or scientific contexts

Examples

Hyperthermic patient
The nurse quickly recognized the hyperthermic symptoms and called for immediate treatment.
After running a high fever, the child became hyperthermic and needed to be cooled down.
Certain medications can cause patients to become hyperthermic if not monitored carefully.
The patient was placed in a hyperthermic chamber to enhance the effects of the treatment.
Researchers studied the impact of hyperthermic conditions on cell growth in the laboratory.
Doctors used a hyperthermic solution during surgery to target cancerous tissues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: febrile - having or showing symptoms of a fever
  • antonym: hypothermic - having an abnormally low body temperature
