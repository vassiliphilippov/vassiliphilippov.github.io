narcolepsy

noun
sleep disorder causing sudden deep sleep
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑr.kəˌlɛp.si / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.kəˌlep.si / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by frequent and uncontrollable episodes of deep sleep, often occurring at inappropriate times.

Examples

Severe narcolepsy symptoms
After being diagnosed with narcolepsy, she began treatment to better manage her daytime drowsiness.
People with narcolepsy may experience sudden sleep attacks even while working or driving.
Researchers are still studying the causes of narcolepsy to improve treatments for this condition.
