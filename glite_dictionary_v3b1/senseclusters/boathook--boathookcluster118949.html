boathook

noun
pole with hook
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊthʊk / (AmE), / ˈbəʊthʊk / (BrE)
a long pole with a hook at one end, used for pushing, pulling, or handling boats

Examples

A boathook pole
The sailor reached for the boathook to steady the boat as it approached the dock.
She used a boathook to retrieve the rope that had fallen into the water.
A boathook is an essential tool for anyone working on a marina or pier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boat pole - a pole used for maneuvering boats
  • antonym: oar - a pole with a flat blade used to row or steer a boat
