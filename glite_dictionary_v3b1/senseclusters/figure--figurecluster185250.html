figure

noun
perceived object in psychological perception
2.7 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡə / (BrE)
in psychology, the part of a visual scene that is perceived as the distinct object of attention, standing out from the background (ground)

Examples

Figure and ground
In Gestalt psychology, the figure is what you notice first in a complex image.
The vase in the center of the painting serves as the figure against a dark background.
When viewing optical illusions, your brain may switch between seeing different areas as the figure or the ground.
