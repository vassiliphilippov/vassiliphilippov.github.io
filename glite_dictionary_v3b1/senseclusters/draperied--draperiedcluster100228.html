draperied

adjective
covered with drapes
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdreɪpəriːd / (AmE), / ˈdreɪpəriːd / (BrE)
covered with draperies or having soft, flowing folds like drapery, especially in fabric or decoration

Examples

Draperied curtains
The grand hall was draperied in rich velvet, creating an atmosphere of elegance.
She admired the draperied canopy above the bed, which added a romantic touch to the room.
Sunlight filtered softly through the draperied windows, casting gentle shadows on the floor.
The curtains in the living room were draperied, giving the space an elegant look.
She admired the draperied fabric of the wedding dress as it flowed to the floor.
The artist painted a draperied figure reclining on a velvet sofa.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bare - not covered or decorated
  • synonym: curtained - covered with curtains
  • synonym: draped - arranged loosely in folds
  • antonym: plain - without decoration or folds
  • synonym: pleated - having fabric folded into pleats
