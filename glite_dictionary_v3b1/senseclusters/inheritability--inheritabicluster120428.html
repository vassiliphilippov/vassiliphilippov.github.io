inheritability

noun
ability to be inherited
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌhɛrɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌhɛrɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be passed from parents to offspring through genes

Examples

Inheritability of traits
Scientists study the inheritability of eye color to understand how traits are passed down.
The inheritability of certain diseases can influence medical advice for families.
Genetic counselors often discuss the inheritability of conditions with their clients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquisition - the act of gaining or receiving something not by inheritance
  • synonym: heritability - the extent to which a trait can be inherited
