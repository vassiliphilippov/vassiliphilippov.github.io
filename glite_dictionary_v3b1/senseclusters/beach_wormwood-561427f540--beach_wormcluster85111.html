beach wormwood

noun
North American herb with grey leaves
Pronunciation: / bitʃ ˈwɜːrmwʊd / (AmE), / biːtʃ ˈwɜːmˌwʊd / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant with greyish leaves, found along the east coast of North America and grown as an ornamental plant

Examples

Beach wormwood plant
Beach wormwood thrives in sandy soils along the eastern seaboard of North America.
Many landscapers use beach wormwood as an ornamental due to its attractive greyish leaves.
The coastal garden featured several beach wormwood plants with their distinctive silvery foliage.
