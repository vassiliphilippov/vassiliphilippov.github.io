subjectship

noun
status as subject
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbdʒɛktʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɪktʃɪp / (BrE)
the condition or status of being a subject under a sovereign or government

Examples

Subjectship under the king
His subjectship was recognized by the new government after the treaty was signed.
Debates about subjectship and citizenship were common in the early days of the kingdom.
She renounced her subjectship to seek freedom in another land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a ruler or government
  • antonym: citizenship - the status of being a citizen of a country
