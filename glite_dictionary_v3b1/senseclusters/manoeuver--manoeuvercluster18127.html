manoeuver

verb
act skillfully to achieve a goal
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
to act skillfully or strategically in order to achieve a particular goal

Examples

To manoeuver for power
He manoeuvered skillfully through office politics to secure the promotion.
She manoeuvered herself into a position of influence within the organization.
They manoeuvered to gain control of the board by forming strategic alliances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plot - to plan secretly, typically something harmful or illegal
  • synonym: scheme - to make plans, especially in a cunning way to achieve a goal
