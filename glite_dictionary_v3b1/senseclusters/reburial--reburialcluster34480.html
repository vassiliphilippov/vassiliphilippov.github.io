reburial

noun
the act of burying again
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈbɛriəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbɛərɪəl / (BrE)
The act of burying something or someone again, especially after exhumation or for ceremonial reasons.

Examples

A reburial ceremony
After the family had the grave exhumed, they arranged a proper reburial.
Controversy surrounded the reburial of the monarch's body centuries after his death.
The discovery led to the reburial of the ancient remains in a new location.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: burial - the act or ceremony of placing a dead body in the ground
