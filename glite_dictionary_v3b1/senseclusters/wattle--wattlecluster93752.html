wattle

noun
interwoven stick or branch framework
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtəl / (BrE)
A framework made of interwoven sticks or branches, used especially for fencing or building walls.

Examples

Wattle fence
She admired the skill it took to weave a sturdy wattle from thin branches.
The farmers constructed a wattle fence to keep the sheep inside the pasture.
Traditional houses in the area often use wattle and daub walls for insulation.
