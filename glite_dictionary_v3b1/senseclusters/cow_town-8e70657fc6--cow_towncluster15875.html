cow town

noun
small town in a cattle-raising region
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊ taʊn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ taʊn / (BrE)
a small town in a cattle-raising area, especially in western North America, often historically associated with the cattle trade

Examples

A cow town in America
Dodge City was once a famous cow town on the American frontier.
He grew up in a small cow town where everyone knew each other.
The annual rodeo brings tourists to the quiet cow town every summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cattle town - a town serving as a market center for cattle or the cattle trade
