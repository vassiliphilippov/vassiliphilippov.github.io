behavioristically

adverb
in a behaviorist way
Pronunciation: / bɪˌheɪvjɚˈɪstɪkli / (AmE), / bɪˌheɪvjəˈrɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on behaviorism, focusing on observable actions rather than thoughts or feelings

Examples

To study behavioristically
The psychologist analyzed the data behavioristically, focusing only on the participants' actions.
In the study, responses were interpreted behavioristically rather than considering internal thoughts.
The teacher approached classroom management behavioristically, rewarding observable positive behaviors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: objectively - in a way that is not influenced by personal feelings
  • antonym: subjectively - in a way that is influenced by personal feelings or opinions
