inversion

noun
corporate move to reduce taxes
Pronunciation: / inv5cr9 n / (AmE), / inv5c 3 n / (BrE)
the practice by which a company changes its legal domicile to another country with lower taxes in order to reduce its tax burden.

Examples

Tax inversion
Many multinationals pursued an inversion to reduce their corporate taxes.
The government's new law aims to prevent future inversion deals by tightening tax rules.
After the hostile takeover, the firm abandoned its planned inversion amid public criticism.

Related Terms
