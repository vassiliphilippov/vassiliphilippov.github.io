roister

verb
celebrate in boisterous, noisy manner
Pronunciation: / ˈrɔɪstər / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪstə / (BrE)
to celebrate noisily and in a boisterous, often drunken way

Examples

To roister loudly
During the festival, groups of young people were seen roistering through the town until dawn.
The sailors would roister in the taverns after returning from sea.
They roistered late into the night, filling the streets with laughter and song.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carouse - to drink alcohol and enjoy oneself with others in a noisy, lively way
  • synonym: revel - to enjoy oneself in a lively and noisy way, especially with drinking and dancing
