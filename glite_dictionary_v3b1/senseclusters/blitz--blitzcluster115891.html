blitz

verb
attack suddenly and forcefully
9.0 5.9
Pronunciation: /blɪts/ (AmE), /blɪts/ (BrE)
to attack or destroy a place suddenly and heavily, especially by bombing from the air

Examples

To blitz a city
During the World War, several cities were blitzed overnight, leaving many buildings destroyed.
The army decided to blitz the enemy at dawn, catching them off guard.
The football team blitzed their opponents with a series of unexpected plays.
During the war, enemy planes blitzed the port, leaving much of it in ruins.
The army blitzed the city in a surprise attack at dawn.
In the game, the team blitzed their opponents with a series of rapid moves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against; to assault
  • synonym: bombard - to attack a place continuously with bombs or other weapons
  • antonym: defend - to protect from harm or danger; to guard
  • synonym: raid - to make a sudden attack on a place
