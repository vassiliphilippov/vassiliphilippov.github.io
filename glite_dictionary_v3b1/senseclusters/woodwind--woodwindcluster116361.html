woodwind

noun
musical instrument played by blowing air
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈwʊdwɪnd / (BrE)
a musical instrument that produces sound when air is blown into or across it, usually made of wood or metal and excluding brass instruments

Examples

The woodwind section
During the concert, the woodwind section played a beautiful melody.
He excels at playing several woodwind instruments, including the oboe and flute.
The clarinet is a popular woodwind in classical and jazz music.
