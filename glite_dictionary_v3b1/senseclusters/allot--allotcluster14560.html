allot

verb
to assign or distribute (something)

to give or assign a share, portion, or amount of something to someone or for a particular purpose

Examples

To allot time
Each team was allotted five minutes to present their ideas.
She allotted specific tasks to each member of the group.
The government will allot funds to rebuild the schools damaged by the storm.
Each student was allotted a seat number before the exam began.
The committee will allot funds to different projects based on their needs.
They allotted only ten minutes to each speaker at the conference.
Each group will be allotted a different section of the report to complete.
The teacher decided to allot extra time for the final project.
We must carefully allot our resources to ensure everyone has enough.

Synonyms

    4.5 13/m
    allocate divides resources by plan, often in budgets or projects, while allot gives a share for a particular purpose.
  • distribute
    2.0 8.85/m
    distribute gives or spreads things among people or places widely, while allot assigns specific shares for a set purpose.
  • parcel out
    4.8 0.24/m
    parcel out breaks something into parts and gives them to different people or groups, while allot sets fixed shares.
  • portion
    3.0 0.18/m
    portion divides or gives parts of something to people, usually in shares, while allot assigns parts for a stated use.
  • assign
    2.7 30/m
    assign gives a person a task, duty, or position for work or study, while allot gives a measured share or amount.
  • dole out
    4.0 2.44/m
    dole out gives small amounts, often slowly or as charity, while allot gives set shares for a clear purpose.
  • deal out
    3.2 0.17/m
    deal out hands out items to people, often casually or with cards, while allot assigns planned shares for a purpose.
