contextualize

verb
to provide context
6.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛkstʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈtɛkstjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to place something in its situation or background to make it easier to understand

Examples

To contextualize information
Teachers often contextualize new vocabulary with real-life examples to help students understand.
It is important to contextualize historical events to see their true significance.
The article contextualizes the data by comparing it to previous years.
Teachers often contextualize new vocabulary by using it in real-life situations.
It is important to contextualize statistics before drawing any conclusions.

It is important to contextualize statistics before drawing conclusions from them.
