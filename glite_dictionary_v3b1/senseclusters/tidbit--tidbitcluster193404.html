tidbit

noun
small piece of news
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪdˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪdˌbɪt / (BrE)
a small and interesting piece of information, news, or gossip

Examples

A tidbit of news
She shared a fascinating tidbit about the city's history during the meeting.
Every morning, the newsletter includes a fun tidbit to start your day.
He always has a tidbit of information to make conversations more interesting.
He always has a tidbit of trivia to share at parties.
The article included an interesting tidbit about the author's childhood.
During lunch, she told us a funny tidbit from her recent trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often involving details not confirmed as true
  • synonym: nugget - a small, valuable piece of information
  • antonym: secret - something kept hidden or unknown from others
  • synonym: snippet - a small part or brief extract of something
