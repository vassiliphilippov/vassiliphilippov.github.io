fireball

noun
center of a nuclear explosion
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚˌbɔl /; / ˈfaɪərˌbɑl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a very bright ball of fire or light, especially seen in the sky or caused by an explosion

Examples

Local residents reported seeing a bright fireball followed by a loud boom.

A fireball streaked across the early morning sky, astonishing onlookers around the world.

The explosion sent a massive fireball into the sky, lighting up the night.
