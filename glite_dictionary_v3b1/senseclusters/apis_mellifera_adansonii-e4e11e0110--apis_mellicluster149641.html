Apis mellifera adansonii

noun
a subspecies of aggressive honey bee
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæpɪs məˈlɪfərə ædænˈsoʊniaɪ / (AmE), / ˈæpɪs mɛˈlɪfərə ædænˈsoʊnɪaɪ / (BrE)
A subspecies of honey bee originally crossbred in Brazil in the 1950s, known for its aggressive behavior and rapid spread into the Americas.

Examples

Apis mellifera adansonii aggressive bee
Apis mellifera adansonii is often referred to as the 'Africanized bee' because of its aggressive traits.
Farmers in Texas have learned to manage hives of Apis mellifera adansonii carefully to avoid stings.
The rapid spread of Apis mellifera adansonii has had a major impact on local ecosystems.
