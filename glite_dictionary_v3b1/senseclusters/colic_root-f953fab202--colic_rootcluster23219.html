colic root

noun
plant of genus Aletris, used medicinally
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːlɪk ruːt / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪk ruːt / (BrE)
any of several perennial herbaceous plants of the genus Aletris, having grasslike leaves and bitter roots formerly used as a remedy for colic.

Examples

Colic root plant
He researched traditional uses of colic root among Indigenous peoples in North America.
Herbalists once used colic root to treat digestive discomfort and stomach cramps.
The colic root in the meadow has slender, grasslike leaves and small white flowers.
