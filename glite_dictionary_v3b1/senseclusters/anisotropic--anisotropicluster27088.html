anisotropic

adjective
having different properties in different directions
8.5 8.7
Pronunciation: /æn.ɪ.sə.ˈtrɑːp.ɪk/ /; / /ə.nɪ.sə.ˈtrɑːp.ɪk/ (AmE), /æn.ɪ.sə.ˈtrɒp.ɪk/ /; / /ə.nɪ.sə.ˈtrɒp.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a physical property) having different values when measured in different directions; not exhibiting the same characteristics in all directions.

Examples

Anisotropic material properties
Physicists study anisotropic materials to understand how direction influences their mechanical behavior.
The mineral exhibited anisotropic optical properties under polarized light.
Wood is anisotropic because its strength varies along different grain directions.

  • antonym: isotropic - having identical properties in all directions
