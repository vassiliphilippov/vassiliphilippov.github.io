urban typhus

noun
infectious disease spread by fleas
6.7 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈɜːr.bən ˈtaɪ.fəs/ (AmE), /ˈɜː.bən ˈtaɪ.fəs/ (BrE)
an acute infection caused by rickettsia bacteria and transmitted by the bite of an infected flea, characterized by fever, chills, muscle aches, and a rash

Examples

Urban typhus infection
Cases of urban typhus have been reported in crowded city neighborhoods with poor sanitation.
Improved pest control measures helped reduce outbreaks of urban typhus in the twentieth century.
The doctor diagnosed him with urban typhus after confirming he had been bitten by a flea.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: epidemic typhus - a more severe rickettsial disease spread by lice, not fleas
  • synonym: murine typhus - a synonym for urban typhus, a flea-borne rickettsial infection
