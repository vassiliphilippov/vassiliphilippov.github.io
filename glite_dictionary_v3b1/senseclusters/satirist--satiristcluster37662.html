satirist

noun
person who uses satire to criticize or mock
Pronunciation: / ˈsæt̬.ə.rɪst / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ər.ɪst / (BrE)
a writer, artist, or comedian who uses satire, especially humor, irony, or ridicule, to criticize or expose folly or vice

Examples

Famous satirist writer
Jonathan Swift was a renowned satirist known for works like 'Gulliver's Travels'.
Many comedians today are also regarded as modern satirists for their sharp social commentary.
The famous satirist wrote witty articles that mocked political leaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: humorist - a person who writes or performs humorous material
  • synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or ridicule to expose or criticize
