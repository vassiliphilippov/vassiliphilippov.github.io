oil cake

noun
compressed residue from oilseeds; livestock feed
9.7 5.0
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl keɪk / (AmE), / ɔɪl keɪk / (BrE)
a compressed mass of the residues from oilseeds, such as linseed or cottonseed, left after the oil has been extracted, used as feed for livestock

Examples

After the oil was extracted from the soybeans, the remaining oil cake was sold as cattle feed.
Farmers often use oil cake as a protein-rich supplement for their livestock.
The factory specializes in producing oil cake from sunflower seeds to supply local farms.
