beneficiary

adjective
relating to a benefice; having rights from a benefice
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃiəri / (BrE)
possessing, or relating to, a benefice; having rights or privileges arising from the holding of a benefice, especially in ecclesiastical or historical contexts.

Examples

Beneficiary priest
During the Middle Ages, a beneficiary knight could support himself through his granted lands.
He wore robes reserved for members of the beneficiary clergy.
Only those of beneficiary rank were entitled to income from the church lands.
