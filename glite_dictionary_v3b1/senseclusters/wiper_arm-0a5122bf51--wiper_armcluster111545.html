wiper arm

noun
rotating conductor in electrical contacts
7.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪ.pər ɑːrm/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪ.pə ɑːm/ (BrE)
An electrically conducting lever that rotates to connect with a series of fixed contacts, typically used in devices like potentiometers or switches.

Examples

A wiper arm lever
A worn wiper arm can cause intermittent faults in electronic devices.
The position of the wiper arm determines the output voltage of the potentiometer.
When the wiper arm moves, it makes contact with different terminals, altering the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wiper - a movable electrical contact
