long in the tooth

adjective
old or becoming old (idiom)
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ɪn ðə tuθ / (AmE), / lɒŋ ɪn ðə tuːθ / (BrE)
old or becoming old; past one's prime, often used humorously or mildly disparagingly.

Examples

Long in the tooth horse
Although the house is long in the tooth, it still has a lot of charm.
He admits he's getting a bit long in the tooth for playing competitive football.
This software is starting to look long in the tooth compared to modern alternatives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elderly - old or aged, especially of a person
  • synonym: over the hill - past one's prime; considered old
  • antonym: young - having lived or existed for only a short time; not old
