pentadactyl

noun
five-fingered limb
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛntəˈdæktɪl / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈdæktɪl / (BrE)
an animal or limb that has five fingers or toes

Examples

A pentadactyl animal
The fossil showed a clear pentadactyl structure, indicating five distinct toes.
Most mammals, including humans, have a pentadactyl limb pattern.
Scientists studied the evolution of the pentadactyl limb in vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polydactyl - having more than the usual number of fingers or toes
  • antonym: tetradactyl - having four fingers or toes
