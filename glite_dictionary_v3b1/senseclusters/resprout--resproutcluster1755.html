resprout

noun
new plant shoot
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈspraʊt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈspraʊt / (BrE)
a new shoot or growth that emerges from a plant after it has been cut, damaged, or dormant

Examples

A green resprout
After the wildfire, a single resprout appeared at the base of the old tree.
Gardeners often look for a healthy resprout when trying to revive damaged plants.
The bush produced a strong resprout just weeks after being pruned.
