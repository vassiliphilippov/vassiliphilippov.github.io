Laffer curve

noun
theoretical curve showing tax rates vs. government revenue
7.8 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈlæf.ər kɜːrv/ /; / /ˈlæf.ər kərv/ (AmE), /ˈlæf.ə kɜːv/ (BrE)
a graph illustrating the relationship between tax rates and government revenue, suggesting that revenue increases with higher tax rates up to a certain point, after which it decreases as higher rates discourage economic activity

Examples

The Laffer curve graph
Economists disagree on where the peak of the Laffer curve lies for a given country.
Many policymakers refer to the Laffer curve when debating the impact of raising or lowering tax rates.
The theory behind the Laffer curve suggests that excessive taxation can reduce overall tax revenue.
