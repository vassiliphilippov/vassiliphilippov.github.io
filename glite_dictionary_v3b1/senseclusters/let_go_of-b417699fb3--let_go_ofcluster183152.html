let go of

verb
stop holding; release from grasp
Pronunciation: / lɛt ˈɡoʊ ʌv / (AmE), / lɛt ˈɡəʊ ɒv / (BrE)
to stop holding something; to release it from your grasp

Examples

To let go of the rope
Please let go of my arm so I can move.
She finally let go of all the old letters she had kept for years.
You need to let go of the rope slowly to avoid injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hold on to - continue to keep a firm grip on something
  • synonym: release - allow something to move freely or be set free
