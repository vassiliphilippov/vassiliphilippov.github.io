thatched

adjective
with straw roof
3.9
Pronunciation: / θætʃt / (AmE), / θætʃt / (BrE)
covered with a roof or layer made of straw, reeds, or something similar (literal or resembling thatch)

Examples

Thatched cottage roof
They admired the old thatched cottage at the edge of the village.
Many traditional houses in the countryside are thatched to keep them cool in summer.
The inn was famous for its charming thatched roof and flower-filled garden.
The cottage was thatched with golden straw, giving it a charming, rustic appearance.
His eyebrows were thatched and bushy, making his expressions even more dramatic.
The hillside appeared thatched with wild grasses after the spring rains.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bare - not covered by any layer or growth
  • synonym: roofed - having a roof of any kind
  • synonym: shaggy - having long, thick, or messy hair or fur
  • antonym: tiled - covered with a roof made of tiles
