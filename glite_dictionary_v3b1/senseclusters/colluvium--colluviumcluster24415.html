colluvium

noun
soil at slope base
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈluː.viː.əm/ (AmE), /kə.ˈluː.viː.əm/ (BrE)
loose soil, sand, and rock material that collects at the base of hills or slopes, usually moved by gravity

Examples

Loose colluvium soil
The geologist collected samples of colluvium from the bottom of the steep hillside.
After heavy rain, a thick layer of colluvium formed at the base of the slope.
Farmers often find colluvium in low-lying fields near hills, making the soil rich and fertile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - sediment deposited by flowing water, such as rivers
  • synonym: talus - rock debris that accumulates at the base of a cliff
