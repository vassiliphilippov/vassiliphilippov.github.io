endemicity

noun
disease prevalence rate
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndəˈmɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌɛndɪˈmɪsɪti / (BrE)
the constant or regular presence of a disease or condition in a particular area or population

Examples

The endemicity of malaria
The endemicity of malaria in the region makes year-round prevention efforts necessary.
Researchers monitor the endemicity of certain diseases to track public health trends.
Vaccination programs can help reduce the endemicity of infectious illnesses in vulnerable communities.
The endemicity of certain diseases makes them difficult to eliminate from tropical regions.
Researchers studied the endemicity of the virus in rural communities over several years.
Understanding the endemicity of malaria helps public health officials plan effective interventions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epidemic - a sudden increase in the number of cases of a disease above what is normally expected
  • synonym: incidence - the rate of new cases of a disease in a population over a period
  • synonym: prevalence - the total number of cases of a disease in a population at a given time
