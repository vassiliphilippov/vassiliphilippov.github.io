Janus

proper noun
Roman god of doorways and transitions
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒeɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈdʒeɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the god of doorways, beginnings, and transitions, often depicted with two faces looking in opposite directions.

Examples

Janus with two faces
In art, Janus is often shown with two faces, symbolizing his ability to look both to the past and the future.
Many historians associate Janus with the concept of transition and new beginnings.
The temple of Janus in Rome was kept open during times of war and closed during peace.
