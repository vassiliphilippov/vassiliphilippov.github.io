in commendam

adjective, adverb
temporarily held office
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn kə.ˈmen.dəm/ /; / /ən kə.ˈmen.dəm/ (AmE), /ɪn kə.ˈmen.dəm/ /; / /ən kə.ˈmen.dəm/ (BrE)
temporarily held or entrusted (especially a church office or benefice), usually until a permanent appointment is made

Examples

Held in commendam
The bishop held the abbey in commendam and received its revenues without living there.
Several church offices were granted in commendam to nobles who did not perform the usual duties.
The lands were managed in commendam until a permanent abbot was appointed.
The bishop held the abbey in commendam until a new abbot was appointed.
Several church offices were managed in commendam during the period of transition.
He was granted the benefice in commendam while the position remained vacant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ad interim - for the time being; temporarily
  • synonym: interim - serving temporarily or for the time being
  • antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
