proletarization

noun
growth of working class
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊlətɛrəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊlɪtərəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌprəʊlɪtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which people, groups, or societies become part of the working class, often by losing property, skills, or independence and becoming wage earners

Examples

The proletarization process
The proletarization of rural communities led to major changes in traditional family structures.
Rapid proletarization often accompanies the expansion of factories and urban industries.
Historians debate whether proletarization improved or worsened living conditions for workers.
The rapid proletarization of farmers changed the social structure of the region.
Many historians study the proletarization that occurred during the Industrial Revolution.
Economic shifts can lead to the proletarization of formerly independent workers.
The proletarization of the workforce increased as more jobs became routine and required less training.
Rapid industrialization led to the proletarization of many traditional craftspeople.
Some sociologists study the proletarization of professions that were once considered highly skilled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deskilling - the reduction of skill required to do a job
  • antonym: empowerment - the process of gaining power or control
  • antonym: gentrification - the process of renovating an area so that it becomes more middle-class
  • synonym: industrialization - the development of industries in a region or country
  • synonym: urbanization - the process of people moving to cities and adopting urban lifestyles
