vodka

noun
clear, colorless distilled alcoholic drink
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːdkə / (AmE), / ˈvɒdkə / (BrE)
A clear, colorless, unaged distilled alcoholic drink made chiefly from grain or potatoes, originally developed in Russia and Eastern Europe.

Examples

A glass of vodka
Russian traditions often involve toasts with vodka during celebrations.
She ordered a glass of vodka with ice at the bar.
The recipe calls for a splash of vodka to enhance the flavor of the sauce.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gin - a clear, strong alcoholic drink flavored with juniper berries
  • synonym: whiskey - a distilled alcoholic drink made from grain mash, aged in barrels
