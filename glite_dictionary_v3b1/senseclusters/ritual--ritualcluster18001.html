ritual

noun
repeated pattern of behavior
4.4 9.71/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÉªtÊƒuÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆrÉªtÊƒuÉ™l / (BrE)
a set pattern of actions or behaviors regularly followed, especially as a habit or custom

Examples

Morning ritual
Brushing his teeth before bed had become a nightly ritual for him.
Checking emails as soon as she arrived at work was an unspoken office ritual.
Each morning she followed the same ritual of making coffee and reading the news.

Synonyms

  • routine
    1.0 25/m
    a routine is a practical series of daily actions done for efficiency, while a ritual is often symbolic or formal and follows tradition.
  • pattern
    2.3 93/m
    a pattern is a repeated way of doing something you can observe, while a ritual is a set action often with meaning or ceremony.
  • habit
    1.0 47/m
    a habit is an automatic action you do without thinking, while a ritual is a conscious or formal set of actions with purpose.
  • custom
    1.3 16/m
    a custom is a social practice shared by a group over time, while a ritual is a formal set of acts often tied to religion or ceremony.
  • tradition
    2.0 85/m
    a tradition is a long-held belief or practice passed through generations, while a ritual is a specific set of repeated actions within that tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceremony - a formal religious or public occasion, typically one celebrating a particular event
  • synonym: routine - a regular way of doing things; a standard procedure
  • antonym: spontaneity - the condition of being spontaneous; not planned or patterned behavior
