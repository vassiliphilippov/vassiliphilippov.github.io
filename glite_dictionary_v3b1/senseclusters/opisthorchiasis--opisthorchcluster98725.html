opisthorchiasis

noun
parasitic infection from eating raw fish
6.9
Pronunciation: / oʊˌpɪsθɔːrˈkaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / əʊˌpɪsθɔːˈkaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
a disease caused by infestation with parasitic flukes, typically contracted by eating raw or undercooked fish, common in parts of Asia

Examples

Opisthorchiasis infection
Consuming raw freshwater fish increases the risk of contracting opisthorchiasis.
Doctors diagnosed him with opisthorchiasis after he developed symptoms following a trip to Southeast Asia.
The spread of opisthorchiasis is a major public health concern in some Asian countries.
