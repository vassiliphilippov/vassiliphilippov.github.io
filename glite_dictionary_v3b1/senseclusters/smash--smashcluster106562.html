smash

verb
hit with great force
Pronunciation: / smæʃ / (AmE), / smæʃ / (BrE)
to break, damage, or destroy something with great force, often causing it to shatter or be ruined

Examples

To smash a window
He smashes the ball over the net during the tennis match.
She accidentally smashed the vase while cleaning the shelf.
The protester smashed the window with a brick.
During the storm, a tree branch smashed through the window.
She accidentally smashed her favorite mug on the kitchen floor.
The thief tried to smash the glass door with a hammer.
He used a hammer to smash the old television set.
She smashed the plate on the floor in anger.
The window smashed when the ball hit it with full force.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow or strain
  • synonym: crash - to break with a loud noise, often from impact
  • synonym: destroy - to ruin completely or put an end to something
  • antonym: mend - to repair something that is broken or damaged
  • antonym: repair - to restore something that is broken or damaged
  • synonym: shatter - to break suddenly into many pieces
