inebriety

noun
state of drunkenness

A temporary state of being drunk or intoxicated caused by excessive consumption of alcohol.

Examples

The inebriety state
After an evening of heavy drinking, his inebriety was apparent to everyone.
His obvious inebriety at the banquet caused quite a stir among the guests.
The symptoms of inebriety include slurred speech and impaired motor skills.

Synonyms

  • inebriation
    7.6 0.19/m
    inebriation means the state of being drunk in everyday use, while inebriety sounds more formal or old-fashioned.
  • intoxication
    5.1 0.93/m
    intoxication describes loss of control from alcohol or drugs in formal contexts, while inebriety focuses on alcohol only.
  • drunkenness
    4.4 1.58/m
    drunkenness shows loud or messy behaviour when drunk and everyday speech, while inebriety is a more formal term.
  • insobriety
    9.9 0.00/m
    insobriety appears in legal or formal texts about lack of sobriety, while inebriety is the common noun for being drunk.
