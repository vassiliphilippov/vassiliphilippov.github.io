moray eel

noun
a brightly colored eel of warm seas
9.9
Pronunciation: / mɔˈreɪ iːl / (AmE), / ˈmʌr.eɪ iːl / (BrE)
A type of brightly colored, often voracious eel found in warm coastal waters, generally not aggressive towards humans but some larger species can be dangerous if provoked.

Examples

Bright moray eels
Divers often spot moray eels hiding in crevices along the coral reef.
Some species of moray eel can grow over three meters in length.
The moray eel uses its sharp teeth to catch prey in the dark.
