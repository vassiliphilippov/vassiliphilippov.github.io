luciferase

noun
light-producing enzyme
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈluː.sɪ.fə.reɪs/ (AmE), /ˈluː.sɪ.fə.reɪs/ (BrE)
an enzyme found in some living organisms that produces light and is used in scientific research to study gene activity or cell health

Examples

luciferase enzyme activity
Scientists use luciferase in experiments to study gene expression in cells.
The glow of a firefly is caused by a chemical reaction involving luciferase.
Researchers discovered a new type of luciferase in deep-sea shrimp.
Scientists use luciferase to detect gene expression by measuring the light it produces in cells.
The experiment showed increased activity when the luciferase gene was introduced into the bacteria.
By adding luciferase to the sample, researchers could easily see which cells were alive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioluminescence - the production and emission of light by living organisms
  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that speeds up chemical reactions in living things
  • synonym: reporter gene - a gene used in research to indicate if a certain gene is active
