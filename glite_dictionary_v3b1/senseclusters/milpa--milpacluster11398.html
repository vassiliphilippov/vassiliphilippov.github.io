milpa

noun
Mesoamerican farming system
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlpə / (AmE), / ˈmɪlpə / (BrE)
a traditional Mesoamerican farming system in which maize, beans, squash, and other crops are grown together and plots are rotated to maintain soil fertility

Examples

Milpa farming system
Farmers in the region still use the milpa system to grow a variety of crops together.
The milpa method helps preserve soil fertility and supports local biodiversity.
Learning about the milpa tradition gave her a new appreciation for sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crop rotation - the practice of growing different crops in succession on the same land
  • antonym: monoculture - the cultivation of a single crop in a given area
