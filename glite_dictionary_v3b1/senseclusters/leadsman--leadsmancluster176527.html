leadsman

noun
ship's depth measurer
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdzmən / (AmE), / ˈlɛdzmən / (BrE)
a person on a ship who measures the depth of water using a lead line

Examples

The leadsman measures depth
The leadsman stood at the bow, carefully lowering the line into the water.
Before modern technology, a leadsman was essential for safe navigation in shallow waters.
The captain relied on the leadsman to avoid running aground near the coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: navigator - a person who directs the course of a ship
  • synonym: sailor - a person who works on a ship
