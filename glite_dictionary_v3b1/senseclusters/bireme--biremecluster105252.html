bireme

noun
ancient two-oared ship
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌriːm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌriːm / (BrE)
an ancient ship with two rows of oars on each side, used especially by Greeks and Romans

Examples

An ancient bireme ship
The ancient Greeks built a bireme for fast travel across the Mediterranean Sea.
A detailed model of a bireme was the centerpiece of the maritime museum's exhibit.
Roman soldiers rowed their bireme into battle against enemy fleets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galley - a long, narrow ship powered by oars, used in ancient and medieval times
  • degree higher: trireme - an ancient ship with three rows of oars on each side
