assertion

noun
digitally signed identity statement
7.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsɜːr.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsɜː.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in computing, especially identity and single-sign-on systems) a digitally signed statement issued by an identity provider that asserts information about a user (such as identity or attributes) and is used to authenticate or authorize that user.

Examples

SAML assertion
The service validates the user with a SAML assertion from the identity provider.
After login, the application accepted the assertion and granted access to protected resources.
Engineers inspected the transmitted assertions to ensure signatures and timestamps were valid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claim - a statement about a subject (for example, a user attribute) used by identity systems
  • synonym: token - a digital object used to authenticate or authorize a user or session
