arthrodesis

noun
surgical joint fusion procedure
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrθroʊˈdisɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːθrəʊˈdiːsɪs / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a joint is fixed to promote bone fusion, eliminating movement at the joint

Examples

Arthrodesis surgery
Arthrodesis is sometimes performed when other treatments for severe arthritis have failed.
After years of pain, the surgeon recommended arthrodesis of the ankle to provide stability.
Recovery from arthrodesis can require several months of physical therapy.
