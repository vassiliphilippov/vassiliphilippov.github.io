damage

verb
to suffer harm or injury
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdamɪdʒ / (BrE)
to cause harm or injury to something, making it less valuable, useful, or healthy

Examples

To damage a car
Be careful with the packaging, as the contents can damage during transit.
These old photographs damage easily if exposed to sunlight.
Thin glassware tends to damage more quickly than thicker pieces.
Be careful not to damage the artwork while moving it.
He accidentally damaged his phone by dropping it on the pavement.
Heavy rains damaged several houses in the neighborhood last night.

  • synonym: harm - to physically or emotionally hurt or injure
  • antonym: repair - to restore to good condition after damage
