marsh

noun
low, grassy wetland
7.4
Pronunciation: / mɑrʃ / (AmE), / mɑːʃ / (BrE)
An area of low-lying, wet land often covered with grasses and reeds, usually serving as a transition between land and water.

Examples

A marsh area
Birds nest in the safety of the marsh during spring.
The marsh was teeming with frogs and dragonflies after the rain.
We wore boots as we walked through the muddy marsh to reach the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with decaying plant matter
  • synonym: swamp - an area of low, wet ground, often with trees
