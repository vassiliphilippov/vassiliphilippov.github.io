two-party

adjective
supported by both sides; involving two parties
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː ˌpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈtuː ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
involving, supported by, or relating to two distinct sides, groups, or parties, especially in politics or law

Examples

A two-party system
A two-party system often dominates the political landscape in several countries.
The new legislation received two-party approval, making it more likely to pass quickly.
They negotiated a two-party agreement to resolve the longstanding dispute.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bipartisan - supported by both political parties; involving both parties
