Pre-Raphaelitism

noun
Victorian art and literary movement
Pronunciation: / ˌpriˌræfiˈlitɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌræfəˈliːtɪzəm / (BrE)
A 19th-century artistic and literary movement that sought to return to the detail, intense colors, and complex compositions prevalent in Italian art before Raphael.

Examples

Pre-Raphaelitism art movement
Her research paper explored the connections between Pre-Raphaelitism and the later Arts and Crafts movement.
Museums today display several masterpieces representative of Pre-Raphaelitism in their permanent collections.
The ideals of Pre-Raphaelitism influenced not just painters, but also poets and novelists of the era.
