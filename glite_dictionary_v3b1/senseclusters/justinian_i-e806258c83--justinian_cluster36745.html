Justinian I

proper noun
Byzantine emperor and legal reformer
8.6
Pronunciation: / dʒʌˈstɪniən ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / dʒʌˈstɪniən ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 482–565) Byzantine emperor famous for codifying Roman law and expanding the empire's territory.

Examples

Justinian I emperor
Justinian I is best known for his ambitious project to codify Roman law, known as the Corpus Juris Civilis.
Many historians regard Justinian I as one of the greatest rulers of the Byzantine Empire.
The reign of Justinian I marked a period of military triumphs and extensive legal reforms.
