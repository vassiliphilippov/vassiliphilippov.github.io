birchbark

noun
birch tree outer layer
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrtʃˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɜːtʃˌbɑːk / (BrE)
the thin, papery outer layer of the trunk of a birch tree, often used for crafting or writing

Examples

Birchbark sheet
The canoe was made entirely from birchbark and stitched together with roots.
Early peoples used birchbark to write messages and create containers.
She admired the delicate patterns on the piece of birchbark she found in the forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bark - the outer covering of the trunk, branches, and roots of trees
