refuel

verb
put more fuel into something
3.0 1.25/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàfju…ôl /; / ÀåriÀêÀàfjuÀê…ôl / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàfj ä…ôl / (BrE)
to put more fuel into a vehicle, machine, or person (literally or metaphorically) so it can continue operating or have more energy

Examples

To refuel the ship
After hours at sea, the ship docked to refuel and take on supplies.
The plane will need to refuel halfway through its flight to New York.
We stopped at the service station to refuel before continuing our journey.
After a long drive, we stopped at a service station to refuel.
Ships often refuel at ports during long voyages.
The pilot had to refuel the plane before continuing the flight.
The truck had to refuel at the nearest gas station before continuing its delivery route.
After a long hike, we stopped to refuel with some snacks and water.
Pilots must refuel their planes before every long-distance flight to ensure safety.

Synonyms

  • fuel
    2.0 6.48/m
    fuel means to supply fuel to a machine or vehicle, while refuel stresses adding fuel so it can keep running.
  • gas
    1.3 0.12/m
    gas means to fill a vehicle's tank with petrol or gasoline, while refuel can mean adding any fuel to help it continue.
  • gas up
    1.9 0.25/m
    gas up means to fill a vehicle's tank at a petrol station, while refuel applies to any fuel or energy.
  • fill up
    1.3 3.52/m
    fill up means make a tank or container full, often at a station, while refuel specifically refers to adding fuel.
  • replenish
    4.3 3.23/m
    replenish means restore what has been used, like supplies or energy, while refuel usually means adding fuel to a vehicle or machine.
  • refill
    1.3 3.89/m
    refill means fill something again after use, often liquids or containers, while refuel focuses on adding fuel to keep operation going.
