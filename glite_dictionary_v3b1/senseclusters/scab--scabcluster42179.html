scab

noun
crust forming over a healing wound
Pronunciation: / skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
a hard, dry covering that forms over a healing wound or sore on the skin

Examples

A scab on the knee
After falling off his bike, Tim developed a scab on his knee that itched for days.
The doctor explained that a scab protects the underlying skin as it recovers.
Try not to pick at the scab so your cut can heal properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crust - a hard outer layer or covering, especially over something moist or soft
