nucleus accumbens

noun
brain reward center
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliəs əˈkʌmbɛnz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliəs əˈkʌmbɛnz / (BrE)
a region of the brain involved in reward, motivation, and pleasure processing

Examples

The nucleus accumbens region
The nucleus accumbens is activated when people experience pleasure or receive rewards.
Scientists study the nucleus accumbens to better understand addiction and motivation.
Dopamine release in the nucleus accumbens is linked to feelings of happiness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amygdala - a brain region involved in emotion and motivation
  • synonym: ventral striatum - a part of the brain that includes the nucleus accumbens and is involved in motivation
