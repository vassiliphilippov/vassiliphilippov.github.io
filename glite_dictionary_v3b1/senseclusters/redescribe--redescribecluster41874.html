redescribe

verb
to describe again
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːdɪˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to describe something again or in a different way, often to clarify, reinterpret, or update information

Examples

To redescribe clearly
The teacher asked the student to redescribe the experiment in simpler terms.
After receiving feedback, she decided to redescribe her proposal to make it clearer.
Historians often redescribe past events as new evidence comes to light.
The teacher asked the student to redescribe the process in simpler terms.
After new evidence emerged, the historian decided to redescribe the events of that year.
Sometimes it helps to redescribe a problem to find a better solution.
The scientist decided to redescribe the species based on new evidence.
After hearing new details, she had to redescribe the events of that night.
The teacher asked the student to redescribe the process using simpler language.
