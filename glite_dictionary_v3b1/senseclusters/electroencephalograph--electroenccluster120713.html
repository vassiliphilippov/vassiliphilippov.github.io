electroencephalograph

noun
device for recording brain's electrical activity
9.1 7.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.trə.en.ˈsef.ə.lə.græf/ /; / /ə.lek.trə.en.ˈsef.ə.lə.græf/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trə.en.ˈsef.ə.lə.grɑːf/ /; / /ə.lek.trə.en.ˈsef.ə.lə.grɑːf/ (BrE)
a medical instrument used to record the electrical activity produced by the brain.

Examples

An electroencephalograph machine
After the accident, the hospital performed tests using an electroencephalograph to check for abnormal brain activity.
Technicians are trained to operate an electroencephalograph for diagnostic purposes in neurology departments.
The doctor used an electroencephalograph to monitor the patient's brain waves during the procedure.
