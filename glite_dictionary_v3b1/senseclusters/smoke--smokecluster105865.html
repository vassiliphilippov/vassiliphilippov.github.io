smoke

noun
hint or sign of hidden activity
1.0 7.2
Pronunciation: / smoʊk / (AmE), / sməʊk / (BrE)
an indication or sign of something happening or existing, or something that is just an illusion or insubstantial

Examples

Where there's smoke
If there's so much smoke about, there must be some truth behind the claims.
Journalists are trained to look for signs of smoke before investigating a big story.
There seems to be a lot of smoke around these rumors of corruption.
Her promises turned out to be just smoke with nothing real behind them.
Most of his arguments were merely smoke, meant to distract from the real issue.
They believed his plan would change everything, but it was all smoke and nothing more.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evidence - something that shows or suggests that something exists or is true
  • synonym: illusion - something that is not what it seems; deceptive appearance
  • degree higher: proof - information or evidence showing that something is definitely true or exists
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
