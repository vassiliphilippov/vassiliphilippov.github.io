SLA

proper noun
US alcohol regulator
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs ˌɛl ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˌɛl ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for State Liquor Authority, a government agency in the United States that regulates the sale and distribution of alcoholic beverages

Examples

SLA alcohol rules
The SLA conducted an inspection of the new bar before it opened to the public.
You must apply to the SLA for a license to sell wine and spirits in New York.
The restaurant lost its SLA license after repeated violations of state alcohol laws.
