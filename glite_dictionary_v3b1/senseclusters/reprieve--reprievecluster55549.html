reprieve

noun
postponement of punishment (esp. death sentence)
5.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpriv / (AmE), / rɪˈpriːv / (BrE)
an official order or decision that delays the punishment of a prisoner, especially the death penalty

Examples

A reprieve order
Her family hoped that a last-minute reprieve would save her from the death penalty.
The governor granted a reprieve to the prisoner hours before his scheduled execution.
The lawyer filed for a reprieve while seeking new evidence in the case.
The court's decision offered a welcome reprieve from an otherwise severe sentence.
The family was relieved to hear about the temporary reprieve as they prepared the appeal.
The governor granted a reprieve to the prisoner hours before the execution was to take place.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commutation - a reduction of a sentence or fine
  • antonym: execution - the act of carrying out a death sentence
  • synonym: pardon - an official forgiveness of a crime
  • antonym: sentence - a punishment assigned to a defendant found guilty by a court
