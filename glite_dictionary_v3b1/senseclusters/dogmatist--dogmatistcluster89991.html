dogmatist

noun
stubborn, opinionated person
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɡmətɪst / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡmətɪst / (BrE)
a person who holds to their own opinions or beliefs in an arrogant or stubborn way, without consideration for others' views or evidence

Examples

A dogmatist refuses
As a dogmatist, he refused to listen to any arguments that contradicted his beliefs.
The debate was fruitless because both sides were led by a dogmatist unwilling to compromise.
Unlike a dogmatist, she approached new ideas with curiosity and openness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogmatic - inclined to assert opinions as truth
  • antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; not dogmatic
