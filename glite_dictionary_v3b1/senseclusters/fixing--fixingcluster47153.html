fixing

noun
preservation process in histology
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
the process in histology of preserving and hardening a tissue sample so that it retains its structure as in the living body

Examples

Tissue fixing in labs
Proper fixing of tissue samples is crucial for accurate microscopic analysis.
The laboratory technician ensured the fixing step was performed before staining the slides.
Without adequate fixing, the cellular structures would deteriorate rapidly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fixation - the process of preserving biological tissue to prevent decay
