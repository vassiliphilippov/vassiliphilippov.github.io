key

noun
metal device for opening a lock
Pronunciation: / kiː / (AmE), / kiː / (BrE)
a small device, usually made of metal, used to open or close a lock by fitting into it and turning

Examples

A house key
He forgot his car key at home and couldn't start the engine.
I found the house key under the welcome mat.
She turned the key in the lock and opened the door.
Each employee receives a key to access the building.
I can't get into the house because I've lost my key.
She turned the key in the lock and entered the room quietly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duplicate key - a copy made of an original key
  • antonym: lock - a device that secures something and is opened with a key or other means
  • antonym: passcode - a sequence of characters used to gain access instead of a key
  • degree higher: passkey - a key that opens multiple locks
  • antonym: password - a secret word or phrase used for authentication instead of a key
