deflective

adjective
able to change direction (esp. of waves)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈflɛktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛktɪv / (BrE)
Having the ability to change the direction of something, especially a light or sound wave.

Examples

Deflective properties
Some coatings have deflective properties that scatter incoming sunlight, reducing heat absorption.
The scientist designed a deflective barrier to redirect harmful radiation away from the lab.
The team studied the deflective effect of certain materials on underwater sound waves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflective - capable of sending back light, heat, or sound
