extinguish

verb
bring to an end; terminate or eliminate
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to bring something to an end or eliminate it completely (such as ideas, customs, feelings, or debts)

Examples

To extinguish a debt
Decades of education gradually extinguished prejudice in that community.
The company worked hard to extinguish its financial liabilities.
The government aimed to extinguish certain outdated practices through new legislation.
Her sudden kindness helped to extinguish his feelings of resentment.
The dictator tried to extinguish all opposition to his rule.
The disease threatened to extinguish the entire population of the village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annihilate - to destroy completely; to wipe out entirely
  • antonym: create - to bring into existence; opposite of destroy or extinguish
  • synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of
  • synonym: eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something
  • antonym: preserve - to maintain something in its original state or prevent it from ending
  • synonym: snuff out - to kill or put an end to (figurative)
