disturbingly

adverb
in a way that causes unease or worry
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɝːbɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes anxiety, worry, or unease.

Examples

A disturbingly strong feeling
She found his fascination with crime disturbingly intense.
The film was disturbingly realistic, making the audience feel uneasy.
The report was disturbingly vague about what would happen next.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarmingly - in a way that causes alarm or fear
