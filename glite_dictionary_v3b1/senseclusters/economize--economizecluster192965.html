economize

verb
use resources carefully; avoid waste
5.0 0.60/m
Pronunciation: / …™Ààk…ëÀên…ôÀåma…™z / (AmE), / …™Ààk…ín…ôma…™z / (BrE)
to use money, resources, or time carefully so as to avoid waste or save

Examples

To economize on money
After losing his job, he had to economize on groceries to make ends meet.
Families often economize by cutting out unnecessary expenses during tough times.
To travel more, she tried to economize wherever possible, choosing budget accommodations and cheap flights.
She tries to economize on electricity to lower the monthly bill.
We've had to economize since Jack lost his job.
When traveling, it's wise to economize and avoid unnecessary expenses.
The company encouraged employees to economise wherever possible to cut costs.
The family had to economise after their income was reduced.
To avoid debt, she decided to economise on groceries and utilities.

Synonyms

  • save
    1.0 36/m
    save is using less or keeping money for later, while economize means actively changing habits to avoid waste.
  • conserve
    3.4 5.91/m
    conserve means protecting or managing resources carefully, while economize focuses on using less to save money or time.
  • husband
    8.1 0.25/m
    husband uses resources sparingly and with long-term care, while economize means cutting back or using less to avoid waste.
  • curtail spending
    4.3 0.04/m
    curtail spending reduces how much money is spent quickly, while economize means changing habits to use less over time.
  • cut costs
    3.0 2.86/m
    cut costs means reducing expenses, often sharply for profit reasons, while economize means using resources carefully to avoid waste.
