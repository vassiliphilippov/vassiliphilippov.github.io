economize

verb
use resources carefully; avoid waste
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkɑːnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmaɪz / (BrE)
to use money, resources, or time carefully so as to avoid waste or save

Examples

To economize on money
After losing his job, he had to economize on groceries to make ends meet.
Families often economize by cutting out unnecessary expenses during tough times.
To travel more, she tried to economize wherever possible, choosing budget accommodations and cheap flights.
She tries to economize on electricity to lower the monthly bill.
We've had to economize since Jack lost his job.
When traveling, it's wise to economize and avoid unnecessary expenses.
The company encouraged employees to economise wherever possible to cut costs.
The family had to economise after their income was reduced.
To avoid debt, she decided to economise on groceries and utilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conserve - to use something as little as possible so that it lasts longer
  • synonym: save - to avoid spending money or resources
  • antonym: waste - to use resources carelessly or extravagantly
