duumvir

noun
member of two-person board
Pronunciation: / duːˈʌmvɪr / (AmE), / djuːˈʌmvɪə / (BrE)
one of two people who jointly hold authority or power, especially in ancient Rome

Examples

Ancient duumvir power
In ancient Rome, each duumvir had equal authority in the administration of the city.
The committee was led by a duumvir who shared responsibilities with his colleague.
A duumvir was appointed to oversee the fair distribution of resources during the crisis.
In ancient Rome, each duumvir had equal power in governing the city.
The duumvirs were responsible for organizing public games and festivals.
A duumvir could not make decisions without the agreement of his counterpart.
