virtual version

noun
digital or simulated representation
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrtʃuəl ˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtʃuəl ˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a digital or simulated representation of a person, object, or system, often created using computer technology

Examples

A virtual version of software
Before manufacturing, engineers test a virtual version of the product for any flaws.
She uses a virtual version of her wardrobe to plan outfits online.
The architects presented a virtual version of the new building to the investors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simulation - imitation of a real-world process or system
