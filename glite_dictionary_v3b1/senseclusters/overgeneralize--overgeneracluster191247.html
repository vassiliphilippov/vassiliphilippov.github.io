overgeneralize

verb
make an excessively broad generalization
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz /; / ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to draw a conclusion or make a statement that is too broad or general, often ignoring exceptions or specific details

Examples

To overgeneralize a rule
It is easy to overgeneralize based on a single experience.
Scholars caution not to overgeneralize about an entire culture from a few encounters.
We must be careful not to overgeneralize the results of this study.
It is easy to overgeneralise when discussing cultural differences.
Psychologists warn students not to overgeneralise based on a single experiment.
She tends to overgeneralise from her own experiences to everyone else's.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: generalize - to make broad statements based on limited evidence
  • synonym: overgeneralize - American English spelling of 'overgeneralise'
  • synonym: oversimplify - to simplify too much, ignoring important details
