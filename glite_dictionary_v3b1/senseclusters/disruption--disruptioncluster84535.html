disruption

noun
interruption of continuity
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɹʌp.ʃən /; / dɪsˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌp.ʃən /; / dɪsˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
an interruption, disturbance, or break in the normal flow, order, or process of something

Examples

Any disruption of service
Any disruption of service will be announced on the website.
Frequent phone calls can be a major disruption when you're trying to concentrate.
The construction work caused a significant disruption to traffic flow during rush hour.
Repeated disruptions from the audience forced the speaker to pause several times.
The meeting was thrown into chaos by a sudden disruption in the hallway.
The train was delayed due to a disruption at one of the stations.
New technologies can lead to disruption in traditional industries.
The power outage caused a major disruption to the city's transportation system.
There was significant disruption during the meeting when the fire alarm sounded.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of peace and order, free from disturbance
  • antonym: continuity - the state of being continuous or uninterrupted
  • synonym: disturbance - an interruption of normal activity, often noisy or chaotic
  • synonym: interruption - a break or stoppage in continuity
  • antonym: order - a state in which everything is in its correct place
