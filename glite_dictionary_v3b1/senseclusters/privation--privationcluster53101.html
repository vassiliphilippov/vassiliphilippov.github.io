privation

noun
the state of being deprived of essentials
9.0
Pronunciation: / praɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / praɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of not having the basic things needed for life, such as food, warmth, or money; severe hardship or deprivation

Examples

Extreme privation
Many families suffered extreme privation during the long winter.
The refugees endured years of privation before receiving aid.
Victims of war often experience privation of their basic human rights.
During the war, many families suffered unimaginable privation due to food shortages and harsh winters.
He spoke about his childhood privation and the challenges he overcame to become successful.
The refugees endured years of privation before finally finding safety and comfort.

Related Terms

  • antonym: affluence - the state of having a great deal of wealth or goods
  • synonym: deprivation - the lack or denial of something considered to be a necessity
  • synonym: poverty - the condition of being poor
