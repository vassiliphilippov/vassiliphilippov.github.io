outfeed

verb
to move material out
7.7
Pronunciation: /aʊt.ˈfiːd/ (AmE), /aʊt.ˈfiːd/ (BrE)
to move or transfer material out of a machine or processing system, especially after processing is complete

Examples

To outfeed material
Always outfeed the material slowly to prevent jams in the finishing machine.
The operator must outfeed the boards carefully to avoid damaging the edges.
After cutting, the machine will automatically outfeed the finished pieces onto the conveyor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discharge - to release or send out material from a machine
  • antonym: input - to put material into a machine or system
