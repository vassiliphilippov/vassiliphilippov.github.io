deindustrialize

verb
to remove industry
Pronunciation: / diːɪnˈdʌstriəˌlaɪz /; / diːˈɪndəstrɪəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diːɪnˈdʌstriəlaɪz /; / diːˈɪndʌstrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to reduce or remove industrial activity in a place, economy, or sector, often by closing factories or changing how land is used

Examples

To deindustrialize a city
Many cities began to deindustrialize in the late twentieth century as factories closed.
Government policies can sometimes unintentionally deindustrialize entire regions.
After the plant shut down, the area continued to deindustrialize over the next decade.
After the main factory closed, the town began to deindustrialize rapidly.
Government policies can sometimes unintentionally cause regions to deindustrialize over time.
Many European cities started to deindustrialize in the late twentieth century.
The city decided to deindustrialize the old docklands and build parks and apartments.
Many regions have struggled economically after they began to deindustrialize in the late twentieth century.
Efforts to deindustrialize the area have led to more green spaces and cultural centers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - to decrease or become less in strength or number
  • antonym: industrialize - to develop industry in a country or region
  • synonym: redevelop - to develop land or buildings again for new purposes
