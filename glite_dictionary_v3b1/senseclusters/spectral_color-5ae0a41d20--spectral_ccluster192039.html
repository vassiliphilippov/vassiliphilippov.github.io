spectral color

noun
color seen in the visible spectrum
3.9 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛktrəl ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈspɛktrəl ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
a color that corresponds to a single wavelength of visible light and has a distinct hue, as seen in a rainbow or produced by a prism; not a mixture of different hues.

Examples

A spectral color in rainbow
A rainbow displays the full range of spectral colors visible to the human eye.
Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and violet are all considered spectral colors.
The prism separated the white light into its component spectral colors.
Each spectral colour corresponds to a different wavelength of visible light.
Scientists separated light into its spectral colours using a prism.
The rainbow displays all the spectral colours in their natural order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hue - the attribute of a color by which it is discernible as red, green, etc.
  • synonym: spectral color - American spelling of 'spectral colour'
