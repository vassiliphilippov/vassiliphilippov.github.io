volatility

noun
tendency to vaporize easily
Pronunciation: / ˌvɑːləˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɒləˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the property of a substance that allows it to change readily from a solid or liquid into a vapor

Examples

High volatility of alcohol
Because of its low volatility, water does not evaporate quickly at room temperature.
Gasoline's high volatility makes it useful as a fuel but also dangerous to handle.
The volatility of the chemical means it must be stored in a sealed container.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evaporation - the process of turning from liquid into vapor
