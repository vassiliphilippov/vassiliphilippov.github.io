hardheaded

adjective
practical, unsentimental, realistic
Pronunciation: / ˌhɑrdˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɑːdˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
practical and realistic; guided by experience and facts rather than by theory or emotion

Examples

Hardheaded business decisions
Despite criticism, she remained hardheaded and focused on tangible results.
Her hardheaded approach to negotiations helped the company reach a fair agreement.
You need to be hardheaded when making difficult financial decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hard-nosed - practical and tough-minded
  • antonym: idealistic - guided more by ideals or theory than practicality
  • synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things realistically and sensibly
