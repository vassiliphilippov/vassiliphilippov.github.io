wring

verb
twist and squeeze out of shape
8.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /rɪŋ/ (AmE), /rɪŋ/ (BrE)
to twist and squeeze something, especially to remove liquid

Examples

To wring a wet towel
He wrung his hands nervously while waiting for the results.
She had to wring the wet towel before hanging it to dry.
The laundry was so soaked that I had to wring out each shirt by hand.
After washing the clothes, she carefully wrings them out to remove extra water.
He wrung the mop before cleaning the kitchen floor.
The nurse showed him how to wring the cloth out so it wouldn't drip.
