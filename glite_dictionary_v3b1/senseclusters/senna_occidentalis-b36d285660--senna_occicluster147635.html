Senna occidentalis

noun
leafy tropical shrub, coffee adulterant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.ə ɒkˌsɪˈdɛn.tə.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.ə ɒkˌsɪˈdɛn.təl.ɪs / (BrE)
a leafy tropical shrub with an unpleasant odor, whose seeds have been used as an adulterant in coffee; sometimes classified in the genus Cassia

Examples

Senna occidentalis tropical shrub
Senna occidentalis is known for its strong odor and weedy growth in tropical regions.
Farmers often remove Senna occidentalis to prevent its spread among their crops.
The seeds of Senna occidentalis have historically been mixed with coffee as an adulterant.
