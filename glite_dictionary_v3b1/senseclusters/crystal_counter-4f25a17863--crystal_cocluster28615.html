crystal counter

noun
radiation detector using ionization and conductivity
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstl ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
A device consisting of a counter tube in which an ionizing event increases the conductivity, used for detecting radiation.

Examples

Crystal counter device
Advances in solid-state technology have improved the sensitivity of the crystal counter.
The laboratory equipped the experiment with a crystal counter to measure radioactive emissions.
When the crystal counter detected ionizing particles, it triggered an immediate alarm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Geiger counter - an instrument that detects and measures ionizing radiation
