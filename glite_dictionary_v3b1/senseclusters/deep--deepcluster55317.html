deep

adverb
to a great depth; far down or in
Pronunciation: / dip / (AmE), / diːp / (BrE)
to or at a great distance from a surface, top, or outer part; far down or in

Examples

To go deep
The submarine had to go deep to avoid detection.
They ventured deep into the forest, far from civilization.
You need to dig deep if you want to find water here.
She ventured deep into the forest, far from the main trail.
The diver went deep below the surface to explore the coral reef.
The rescue team searched deep underground for the missing miners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deeply - far down or deeply, often emotionally or physically
  • synonym: far - at, to, or by a great distance (adverb)
  • antonym: shallow - of little depth, not far inwards (adverb)
