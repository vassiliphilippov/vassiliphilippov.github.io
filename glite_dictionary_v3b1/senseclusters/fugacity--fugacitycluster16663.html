fugacity

noun
short-lived nature, especially of plants
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /fjuː.ˈgæs.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /fə.ˈgæs.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /fjuː.ˈgæs.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /fə.ˈgæs.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
The quality of being fleeting or short-lived, especially as applied to plant parts; transience.

Examples

Fugacity of flowers
Botanists note the fugacity of certain flowers, which wither soon after blooming.
The fugacity of cherry blossoms is celebrated as a symbol of life's brief beauty.
The poem draws a parallel between human ambitions and the fugacity of petals falling in spring.
