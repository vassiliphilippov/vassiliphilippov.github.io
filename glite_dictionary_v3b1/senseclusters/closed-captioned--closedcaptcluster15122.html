closed-captioned

adjective
with captions for deaf/hard of hearing viewers
1.7 3.5
Pronunciation: /kloʊzd ˈkæp.ʃənd/ (AmE), /kləʊzd ˈkæp.ʃənd/ (BrE)
(of a television or video broadcast) provided with captions that can be displayed on screen only with special equipment, typically for viewers who are deaf or hard of hearing.

Examples

A closed-captioned video
All public announcements in the theater were closed-captioned for hard-of-hearing patrons.
She prefers to watch movies that are closed-captioned to better understand the dialogue.
The documentary is closed-captioned so viewers can follow the dialogue even in noisy environments.
