fictitiousness

noun
quality of being false
Pronunciation: / fɪkˈtɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / fɪkˈtɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being invented, false, or not real (whether to deceive or simply imaginary)

Examples

The fictitiousness of the story became clear when the facts were checked.
He was criticized for the fictitiousness of his claims about his achievements.
The novel's charm lies in the deliberate fictitiousness of its characters and events.
The fictitiousness of the legend became clear after historians found no supporting evidence.
He was criticized for the fictitiousness of his claims about his past achievements.
Readers enjoyed the fictitiousness of the novel, knowing it was pure imagination.

  • synonym: falsehood - the state of being untrue or a lie
  • synonym: fiction - literature or stories about imaginary events or people
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
