collocation

noun
positioning things together
7.3 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀêl…ôÀàke…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåk…íl…ôÀàke…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the act of placing things together or side by side, especially for comparison or contrast

Examples

Careful collocation of objects
Good lighting depends on the careful collocation of contrasting colors in a room.
The collocation of the two sculptures highlighted their differences.
The artist is known for the bold collocation of shapes in her work.

Synonyms

  • juxtaposition
    9.1 2.88/m
    juxtaposition involves placing things side by side to show differences or similarities, while collocation means putting items together for comparison.
  • apposition
    8.8 0.02/m
    apposition puts words or things next to each other in a close, often grammatical relation, while collocation focuses on general side-by-side placement.
  • bracketing
    6.4 0.02/m
    bracketing groups items as a set for analysis or comparison, whereas collocation simply places things side by side for contrast.
