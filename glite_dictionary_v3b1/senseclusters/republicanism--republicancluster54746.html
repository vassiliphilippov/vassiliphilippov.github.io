republicanism

noun
support for a republican form of government
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpʌblɪkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪkənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief in or support for a republican form of government, where the country is considered a public matter and not the private concern of rulers

Examples

Republicanism belief
Many philosophers have written in defense of republicanism as the preferred system of government.
She studied the origins of republicanism during the Enlightenment period.
The country's shift to republicanism sparked debates among supporters of the monarchy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monarchism - support for a monarchical form of government
