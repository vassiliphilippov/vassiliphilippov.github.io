salmonellosis

noun
food poisoning by Salmonella bacteria
8.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /sæl.mə.nə.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /sæl.mə.nə.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A type of food poisoning caused by infection with Salmonella bacteria, often through contaminated food or water.

Examples

Salmonellosis food poisoning
After eating contaminated chicken, several patients developed symptoms of salmonellosis.
Public health officials warn that salmonellosis can be prevented with proper food handling and hygiene.
The outbreak of salmonellosis was traced back to undercooked eggs served at the restaurant.
