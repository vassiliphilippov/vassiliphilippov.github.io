mid

noun
midfield player
Pronunciation: / mɪd / (AmE), / mɪd / (BrE)
a player who occupies the middle area of the field or court, especially in football (soccer) or hockey

Examples

A skilled mid
The team relied on their experienced mid to control the pace of the game.
After the injury, a substitute mid quickly adapted to the team's strategy.
Coaches often look for a mid who can both defend and attack effectively.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defender - a player whose main role is to prevent the opposing team from scoring
  • antonym: forward - a player whose main role is to attack and try to score goals
  • synonym: midfielder - a player positioned in the middle part of the field
