disposability

noun
ability to be discarded
Pronunciation: / dɪˌspoʊzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌspəʊzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being made to be used once or for a short time and then thrown away

Examples

Disposability of plastic
The disposability of plastic packaging contributes to environmental pollution.
Manufacturers are reconsidering the disposability of their products to promote sustainability.
Some people prefer items with low disposability to reduce waste in their daily lives.
The disposability of single-use cups leads to a lot of unnecessary waste.
Many people are concerned about the disposability of modern consumer products.
Efforts to reduce disposability include encouraging the use of reusable bags and containers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disposable - intended to be used once or a few times and then thrown away
  • antonym: reusability - the quality of being able to be used more than once
