houdah

noun
canopied seat on a camel or elephant
Pronunciation: / ˈhuːdɑː / (AmE), / ˈhuːdɑː / (BrE)
a covered or canopied seat mounted on the back of a camel or elephant, used especially in South Asia and the Middle East for riding or ceremonial purposes

Examples

A houdah seat
Photographs from the expedition show a grand houdah decorated with intricate gold embroidery.
The maharaja waved from his houdah atop the royal elephant during the parade.
They mounted the houdah at sunrise, ready for the long journey across the desert.
