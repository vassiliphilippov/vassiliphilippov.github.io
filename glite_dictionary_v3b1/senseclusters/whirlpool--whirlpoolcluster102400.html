whirlpool

verb
flow in a rapid, circular motion
5.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ùÀêlÀåpuÀêl / (AmE), / Ààw…úÀêlÀåpuÀêl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to move or flow in a rapid, circular motion like that of a whirlpool.

Examples

Water whirlpools down
As the bath drained, I watched the last of the soap bubbles whirlpool away.
Colored dye was poured into the water so we could watch it whirlpool down the drain.
The river would whirlpool around the rocks after a heavy rain.

Synonyms

  • whirl
    2.8 6.28/m
    whirl spins or moves rapidly in a tight circle, while whirlpool means a strong, water-based circular current.
  • eddy
    5.8 0.13/m
    eddy forms a small circular current in water or air, while whirlpool describes a large, powerful water swirl.
  • swirl
    2.7 11/m
    swirl moves in a twisting or circular way, often gently, while whirlpool is a much stronger water whirl.
  • spin
    4.4 0.42/m
    spin turns around a center or axis and often describes objects, while whirlpool refers to a rotating water current.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eddy - to move in a circular motion, especially of water or air
