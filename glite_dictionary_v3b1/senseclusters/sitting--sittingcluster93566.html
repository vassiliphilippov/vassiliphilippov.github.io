sitting

noun
posing session for photo or portrait
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
an occasion or period when people gather and remain seated for a specific purpose, such as a photograph, meeting, meal, or official business

Examples

A sitting for a photo
He had to schedule a sitting with the photographer next week.
The artist requires at least two sittings to complete a detailed painting.
They managed to finish the entire portrait in just one sitting.
Journalists gathered outside for every day of the trial's sitting.
The Supreme Court issued a landmark ruling during its last sitting.
The next sitting of Parliament will begin in September.
At last night's sitting, several participants claimed to have witnessed strange phenomena.
The family attended a sitting with the renowned medium in hopes of contacting their ancestor.
The group scheduled a second sitting after the medium declared she sensed another presence in the room.
