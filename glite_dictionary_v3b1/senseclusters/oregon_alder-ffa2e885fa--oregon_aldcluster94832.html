Oregon alder

noun
tree of Pacific North America with hard red wood
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrɪɡən ˈɔːldər / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a large tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for its hard red wood used especially in furniture making

Examples

Tall Oregon alder trees
Along the riverbank, tall Oregon alder trees provided shade for the hikers.
Furniture makers often choose Oregon alder because its red wood is both attractive and easy to work with.
The craftsman selected Oregon alder for the custom dining table due to its durability and beautiful grain.
