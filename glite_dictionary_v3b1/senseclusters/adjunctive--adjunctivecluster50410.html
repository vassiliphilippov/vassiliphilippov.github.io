adjunctive

adjective
serving as an adjunct; supplementary
Pronunciation: / æˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (AmE), / æˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (BrE)
relating to or forming an adjunct; serving to join or add to something else, especially as a secondary or supplementary element.

Examples

An adjunctive role
In linguistics, certain words have adjunctive functions within a sentence.
In medical treatment, an adjunctive therapy is often used to support the primary approach.
The professor recommended adjunctive reading materials to deepen our understanding of the topic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: primary - first or most important; not supplementary
  • synonym: supplementary - serving as a supplement; additional
