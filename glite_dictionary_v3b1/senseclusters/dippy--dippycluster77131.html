dippy

adjective
silly or eccentric
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpi / (AmE), / ˈdɪpi / (BrE)
behaving in a silly, eccentric, or not very clear-headed way (often in a harmless or amusing manner)

Examples

Dippy behavior
Her dippy ideas always made the meetings more entertaining.
He wore a dippy hat covered in colorful feathers to the party.
Sometimes my brother acts a bit dippy when he is tired.
She felt a bit dippy after forgetting her keys for the third time this week.
His dippy jokes always make the children laugh during class.
I must have looked dippy standing in the rain without an umbrella.
