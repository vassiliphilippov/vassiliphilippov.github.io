pneumoperitoneum

noun
air in abdomen
9.9
Pronunciation: /nuː.moʊ.pər.ɪ.tə.ˈniː.əm/ /; / /nuː.moʊ.per.ɪ.tə.ˈniː.əm/ (AmE), /njuː.məʊ.pə.rɪ.tə.ˈniː.əm/ /; / /njuː.məʊ.per.ɪ.tə.ˈniː.əm/ (BrE)
the presence of gas or air in the abdominal cavity, usually found by medical imaging and often a sign of a medical problem

Examples

X-ray pneumoperitoneum
The surgeon identified a pneumoperitoneum on the patient's X-ray after the accident.
A sudden pneumoperitoneum can indicate a perforated ulcer or other serious abdominal injury.
Doctors use imaging techniques to detect pneumoperitoneum in emergency situations.
The X-ray revealed a pneumoperitoneum that required immediate surgical intervention.
Doctors diagnosed pneumoperitoneum after the patient complained of severe abdominal pain and difficulty breathing.
A sudden pneumoperitoneum can be life-threatening if not treated quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ascites - accumulation of fluid in the abdominal cavity
  • synonym: peritonitis - inflammation of the peritoneum, often due to infection or injury
  • synonym: tension pneumothorax - air trapped in the chest cavity causing pressure on the lungs and heart
