backlight

verb
to light from behind
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbækˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to illuminate a person or object from behind, often to create a silhouette or highlight the subject against the background

Examples

To backlight a subject
The director decided to backlight the actor to create a dramatic silhouette on stage.
Photographers often backlight their subjects to achieve a glowing effect around the edges.
To make the product stand out, the team chose to backlight it during the commercial shoot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: highlight - to make something stand out by lighting or emphasis
  • synonym: illuminate - to light up something
