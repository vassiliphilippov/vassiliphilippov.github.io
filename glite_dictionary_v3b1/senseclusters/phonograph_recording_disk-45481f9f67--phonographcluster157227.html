phonograph recording disk

noun
sound recording disk for a phonograph
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊnəˌɡræf rɪˈkɔrdɪŋ dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəɡrɑːf rɪˈkɔːdɪŋ dɪsk / (BrE)
a flat disk, often coated with cellulose acetate, used for recording and playing back sound on a phonograph.

Examples

A phonograph recording disk
Audio engineers once used a phonograph recording disk to capture live performances before magnetic tape became widespread.
She inherited a collection of vintage phonograph recording disks from her grandfather.
The museum displayed a rare phonograph recording disk from the 1930s.
