which

pronoun
relative pronoun for clauses
Pronunciation: / wɪtʃ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ / (BrE)
a relative pronoun used to introduce a clause that refers to or comments on the whole preceding statement or situation

Examples

Which caused problems
He forgot to set an alarm, which caused him to miss the important morning meeting.
The team won the championship, which surprised everyone in the league.
She passed the exam on her first try, which made her parents very proud.

  • synonym: that - a relative pronoun introducing a clause
