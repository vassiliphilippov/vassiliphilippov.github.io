Noddy

proper noun
children's book character
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑdi / (AmE), / ˈnɒdi / (BrE)
a fictional wooden boy created by Enid Blyton, who is the main character in a series of British children's books

Examples

Noddy stories
Many children grew up reading stories about Noddy and his adventures in Toyland.
The television adaptation brought Noddy and his friends to life for a new generation.
She dressed up as Noddy for the school's book character day.
