dwt

noun
ship carrying capacity
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / diÀê d åb…ôljuÀê tiÀê / (AmE), / diÀê d åb…ôljuÀê tiÀê / (BrE)
an abbreviation for deadweight tonnage, the total weight a ship can safely carry including cargo, fuel, crew, and supplies

Examples

Ship dwt capacity
The new tanker has a dwt of 100,000 tons, allowing it to transport large quantities of oil.
Shipping contracts often specify the required dwt for vessels carrying bulk cargo.
The port can accommodate ships with a maximum dwt of 80,000 tons.

Synonyms

  • deadweight
