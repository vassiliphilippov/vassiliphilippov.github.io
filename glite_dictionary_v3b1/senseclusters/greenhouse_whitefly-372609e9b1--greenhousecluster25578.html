greenhouse whitefly

noun
pest insect found in greenhouses
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrinˌhaʊs ˈwaɪtˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhaʊs ˈwaɪtflaɪ / (BrE)
a small whitefly (Trialeurodes vaporariorum) often found in greenhouses and known for feeding on and damaging plants

Examples

The greenhouse whitefly insect
A single greenhouse whitefly can lay hundreds of eggs on the undersides of leaves.
Gardeners often struggle to control greenhouse whitefly infestations in their tomato plants.
The spread of greenhouse whitefly requires strict hygiene and regular inspections in commercial greenhouses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whitefly - a small white insect that feeds on plant sap
