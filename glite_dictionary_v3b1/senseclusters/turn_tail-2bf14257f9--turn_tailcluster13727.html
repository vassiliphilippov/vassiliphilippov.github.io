turn tail

verb
to flee suddenly in fear or defeat
Pronunciation: / tɝn teɪl / (AmE), / tɜːn teɪl / (BrE)
to run away or flee quickly, especially because of fear or defeat

Examples

To turn tail quickly
Most of the enemy soldiers turned tail when they saw the reinforcements arrive.
Rather than face the consequences, he chose to turn tail and disappear.
When the dog barked, the thief turned tail and fled down the street.
As soon as they heard the siren, the thieves turned tail and disappeared into the alley.
Faced with overwhelming opposition, the politicians had to turn tail and abandon their plans.
The fox turned tail when it spotted the hunter approaching with a rifle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flee - to run away from danger or something frightening
  • synonym: run away - to leave a place or person secretly and suddenly
  • antonym: stand one's ground - to refuse to run away or back down, especially in the face of danger or opposition
