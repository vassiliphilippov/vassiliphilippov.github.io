in for an inch, in for a mile

x
fully committed once started
Pronunciation: / ɪn fəɹ ən ɪnt͡ʃ ɪn fəɹ ə maɪl / (AmE), / ɪn fə ən ɪntʃ ɪn fə ə maɪl / (BrE)
used to say that if you have already started something, you might as well continue and fully commit to it, regardless of the scale or consequences.

Examples

In for an inch, in for a mile
I hadn’t planned to stay all weekend, but in for an inch, in for a mile, so I helped with the whole project.
We’ve already spent so much time fixing this mistake—in for an inch, in for a mile.
When she agreed to help with the event, she thought, in for an inch, in for a mile, and ended up organizing everything.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in for a penny, in for a pound - committed to a venture once started, regardless of how much is at stake
