slide rule

noun
manual calculating instrument with sliding scales
Pronunciation: / slaɪd ruːl / (AmE), / slaɪd ruːl / (BrE)
an analog device with a sliding scale used for rapid mathematical calculations, especially before electronic calculators

Examples

A slide rule device
Before calculators became widespread, engineers relied on the slide rule for complex computations.
Learning to use a slide rule was once a crucial skill for science students.
She inherited an old slide rule from her grandfather, who was an architect.
