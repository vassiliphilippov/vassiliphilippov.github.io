Iphigenia

proper noun
daughter of Agamemnon in Greek mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪfəˈdʒiːniə / (AmE), / ɪfəˈdʒiːniə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the daughter of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra, who was nearly sacrificed by her father to appease Artemis, but was ultimately rescued by the goddess and became a priestess.

Examples

Iphigenia in mythology
Euripides wrote a famous play about the story of Iphigenia at Aulis.
Many scholars debate whether Iphigenia was actually sacrificed or spared by Artemis.
The tragic fate of Iphigenia has inspired many works of literature and art.
