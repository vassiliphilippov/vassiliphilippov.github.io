Mexican husk tomato

noun
small fruit in husk, used in Mexican cooking
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛksɪkən hʌsk təˈmeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən hʌsk təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a small, edible fruit similar to a tomato, yellow to purple in color, enclosed in a papery husk and native to Mexico; often used in Mexican cuisine

Examples

Fresh Mexican husk tomato salsa
After peeling the papery covering, she chopped the Mexican husk tomato for the stew.
Salsa verde is made with Mexican husk tomatoes instead of regular tomatoes.
The market had a fresh batch of Mexican husk tomatoes for making sauces.
