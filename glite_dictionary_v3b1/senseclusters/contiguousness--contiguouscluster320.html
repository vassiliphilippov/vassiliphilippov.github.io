contiguousness

noun
state of being adjacent or touching
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪɡjuəsnəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɪɡjuːəsnəs / (BrE)
the state of being adjacent or touching; the condition of sharing a common border or boundary

Examples

The contiguousness of areas
Effective insulation relies on the contiguousness of all building materials.
The contiguousness of continents allowed species to migrate between them.
The contiguousness of the two properties led to a boundary dispute.
