body of water

noun
large area of water, such as a lake or sea
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑdi əv ˈwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi əv ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
a large area or mass of water, such as a lake, river, sea, or ocean, forming a distinct part of the Earth's surface

Examples

Environmental regulations often vary depending on the type of body of water involved.
Lakes, rivers, and oceans are each considered a body of water.
We went swimming in a nearby body of water during our vacation.
