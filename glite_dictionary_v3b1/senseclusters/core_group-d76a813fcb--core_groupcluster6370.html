core group

noun
central, essential group within a larger whole
Pronunciation: / kɔr ɡruːp / (AmE), / kɔː ɡruːp / (BrE)
a small group of people or things central and essential to a larger organization or activity

Examples

A core group of scientists
A core group of scientists led the groundbreaking research project.
Only a core group of committee members attended every meeting throughout the year.
The success of the event depended on a dedicated core group of volunteers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fringe group - a group on the periphery or margins, not central
  • synonym: nucleus - a central or most important part of something
