deep breath

noun
large inhalation to calm or relax
1.0
Pronunciation: / dip brɛθ / (AmE), / diːp brɛθ / (BrE)
an act of inhaling a large amount of air into the lungs, especially to relax or calm oneself

Examples

Take a deep breath
She took a deep breath before stepping onto the stage.
The doctor advised him to take several deep breaths during the examination.
Whenever I'm stressed, I pause and take a deep breath to calm down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breath - the air taken into or expelled from the lungs
