cyberstalk

verb
to harass online
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.bər.stɑːk/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.bər.stɔːk/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.bə.stɔːk/ (BrE)
to use the internet or electronic communication to follow, monitor, or harass someone persistently and often secretly.

Examples

To cyberstalk a person
The police warned him not to cyberstalk his ex-girlfriend through social media accounts.
She felt unsafe after discovering someone had begun to cyberstalk her online.
Laws have been updated to punish those who cyberstalk others using digital technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harass - to repeatedly bother or attack someone
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to someone or something
  • synonym: stalk - to follow or watch someone secretly, often with harmful intent
