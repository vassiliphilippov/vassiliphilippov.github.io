cured

adjective
rubber processed to improve properties
5.7
Pronunciation: / kjʊrd / (AmE), / kjʊəd / (BrE)
(of materials such as rubber, concrete, tobacco, or plants) treated or processed to improve, harden, or preserve them

Examples

Cured rubber sole
A cured rubber sole lasts much longer than an untreated one.
Certain adhesives contain cured compounds to enhance their strength and flexibility.
The cured rubber sheets are used to manufacture tires for cars and trucks.
If the surface is not cured correctly, the concrete may dry too quickly and become weak.
Properly cured mortar will resist cracking and weathering over time.
The freshly poured concrete must be cured for several days to reach maximum strength.
Only cured leaves are suitable for premium cigars.
Properly cured tobacco develops a richer and smoother flavor.
The tobaccos used in this blend are all cured for several months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dried - (of tobacco) preserved by removing moisture
  • synonym: set - of cement, concrete, etc.: to become hard or solid
  • synonym: vulcanized - (of rubber) treated with sulfur or other agents to improve durability and elasticity
