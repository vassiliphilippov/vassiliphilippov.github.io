euphemic

adjective
mildly expressed
7.9
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈfem.ɪk/ /; / /juː.ˈfiː.mɪk/ (AmE), /juː.ˈfem.ɪk/ /; / /juː.ˈfiː.mɪk/ (BrE)
using or relating to a mild or pleasant word or expression instead of one that may be harsh or unpleasant

Examples

A euphemic expression
The manager chose a euphemic way to announce the layoffs to the staff.
Her euphemic language made the criticism sound less harsh than it really was.
Instead of saying 'fired', he used the euphemic term 'let go'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blunt - saying something in a direct and often harsh way
  • synonym: euphemistic - using or involving a mild or indirect word or expression
