doom

verb
make certain of failure or destruction
5.8 7.58/m
Pronunciation: / duÀêm / (AmE), / duÀêm / (BrE)
to make certain or decide that someone or something will experience failure, destruction, or an unpleasant fate

Examples

To doom a plan
Her constant negativity could doom the project from the start.
The bad weather ultimately doomed their expedition to failure.
The judges' remarks may doom the team's chances of winning.
Fate seemed to doom their love from the very start.
The contract dispute may doom the project before it even begins.
The hero was doomed to fail despite his best efforts.

Synonyms

  • foredoom
    9.9 0.02/m
    foredoom is to condemn someone beforehand to a bad fate, while doom is to make failure or destruction certain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condemn - to sentence someone to a particular punishment, especially death
  • synonym: destine - to intend or choose for a particular purpose or fate
