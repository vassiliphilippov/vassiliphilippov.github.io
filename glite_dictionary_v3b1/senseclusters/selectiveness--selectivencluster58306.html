selectiveness

noun
signal separation ability
7.3
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈlek.tɪv.nəs/ /; / /sə.ˈlek.tɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈlek.tɪv.nəs/ /; / /sə.ˈlek.tɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
the ability of a device, such as a radio receiver, to separate and receive a desired signal while rejecting others

Examples

Radio selectiveness
The new radio's selectiveness made it possible to tune in to distant stations clearly.
Engineers improved the selectiveness of the receiver to reduce background noise.
High selectiveness is important for devices operating in crowded frequency bands.
