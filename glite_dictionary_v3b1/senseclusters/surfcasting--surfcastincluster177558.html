surfcasting

verb
to fish from shore
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːrf.kæst.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsɜːf.kæst.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
to fish by casting a line from the shore into the sea, usually using a rod and reel.

Examples

To surfcast for fish
He spent the morning surfcasting along the rocky coastline, hoping to catch some striped bass.
Many anglers enjoy surfcasting because it allows them to fish without a boat.
During summer evenings, families can often be seen surfcasting on the sandy beaches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angling - to fish with a rod and line
  • antonym: boat fishing - to fish from a boat
