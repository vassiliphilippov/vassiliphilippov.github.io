beyond one's means

adverb
spending more than one can afford
4.6
Pronunciation: / biˈjɑnd wʌnz minz / (AmE), / bɪˈjɒnd wʌnz miːnz / (BrE)
spending more money than one can afford or sustain, often leading to financial difficulty

Examples

Spending beyond your means
It is unwise to buy a car that is beyond your means.
Many families struggle because they live beyond their means and rely on credit cards.
She found herself in debt after years of spending beyond her means.

Related Terms

  • antonym: within one's means - spending only what one can afford
