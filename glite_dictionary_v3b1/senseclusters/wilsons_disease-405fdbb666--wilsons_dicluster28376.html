Wilson's disease

noun
inherited disorder causing copper buildup
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlsənz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈwɪlsənz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a rare inherited disorder in which excess copper accumulates in the liver, brain, and other vital organs

Examples

Wilson's disease diagnosis
Doctors diagnosed him with Wilson's disease after he showed symptoms of liver and neurological problems.
Early detection of Wilson's disease increases the chances of successful treatment.
Untreated Wilson's disease can lead to severe liver damage and neurological impairments.
