director

noun
person in charge of making a film
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛktər, daɪˈrɛktər /; / dəˈrɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktə, daɪˈrɛktə /; / dɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
a person who leads and manages the creative or artistic aspects of a film, play, show, or musical group

Examples

Film director

Many critics praised the film for its talented director and innovative style.
The director worked closely with the actors to bring the script to life.

Orchestra members rely on their director for guidance during both rehearsal and performance.
The director signaled the choir to begin the opening hymn.
As a director, he is known for bringing out the best in his cast.
She won an award for her outstanding work as a director in the theater.
The director called for a break between scenes so the actors could rest.

  • antonym: producer - person responsible for financial and managerial aspects of a film
