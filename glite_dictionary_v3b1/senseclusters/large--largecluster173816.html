large

adjective
lofty or pompous in style
8.2 1.21/m
Pronunciation: / l…ërd í / (AmE), / l…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) using grand, pompous, or lofty language, often with an air of self-importance or exaggeration.

Examples

Large talk
She dismissed his large talk as nothing more than empty boasting.
The essay's large style made it sound more impressive than it truly was.
The senator was known for his large speeches that impressed his supporters but irritated his critics.
