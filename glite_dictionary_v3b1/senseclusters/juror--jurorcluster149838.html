juror

noun
member of a jury
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʊrər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊərə / (BrE)
a member of a jury who is chosen to decide the facts in a legal case

Examples

A juror in court
Each juror must take an oath to deliver a true verdict.
She was selected as a juror for the murder trial.
The juror listened carefully to the evidence given by the witnesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: judge - a person who presides over court proceedings
  • synonym: jury - a group of people selected to decide the facts in a trial
