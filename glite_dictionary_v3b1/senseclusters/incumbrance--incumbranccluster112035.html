incumbrance

noun
legal claim or liability on property
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (BrE)
a legal claim or liability attached to property, such as a lien or mortgage, that may reduce its value or restrict its use

Examples

Property incumbrance
Before purchasing land, make sure to check for any incumbrance such as mortgages or liens.
He could not transfer the title because there was an outstanding incumbrance on the estate.
The property was sold free of any incumbrance or legal claim against it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encumbrance - a claim or liability on property, same as incumbrance
