scruple

verb
hesitate on moral or ethical grounds
9.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈskruː.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈskruː.pəl/ (BrE)
to hesitate or have doubts because something may be morally wrong

Examples

To scruple at lying
He would scruple to cheat, even if no one would ever find out.
She did not scruple to voice her objections at the meeting.
Some journalists fail to scruple over using questionable sources.
He might scruple over taking credit for someone else's work, but others might not.
Many people would scruple at lying under oath, but he did not hesitate.
She did not scruple to speak her mind, even if it offended others.
He scrupled to break his promise, even though it would have benefited him.
She did not scruple to voice her disagreement with the decision.
Some leaders may scruple at taking such drastic measures without proper consent.

  • synonym: hesitate - pause before doing something, often due to uncertainty
