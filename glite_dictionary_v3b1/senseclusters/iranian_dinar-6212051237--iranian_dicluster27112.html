Iranian dinar

noun
former monetary unit of Iran
Pronunciation: / ɪˈreɪniən dɪˈnɑr / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪniən dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
a former monetary unit of Iran, equal to one hundredth of a rial

Examples

The Iranian dinar currency
Before the introduction of the rial, the Iranian dinar was commonly used in financial transactions.
Collectors sometimes seek old coins denominated in Iranian dinar for their rarity.
Historical records show that prices were listed in Iranian dinars in early 20th-century Iran.
