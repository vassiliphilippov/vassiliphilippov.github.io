pleurodont

noun
animal with teeth fused to jaw's inner side
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈplʊr.ə.dɑːnt/ (AmE), /ˈplʊə.rə.dɒnt/ (BrE)
An animal whose teeth are fused to the inner surface of the jawbone without sockets, common in certain reptiles and amphibians.

Examples

A pleurodont reptile
Paleontologists identified the fossil as a pleurodont based on its distinctive dental structure.
Several lizard species are classified as pleurodont due to the way their teeth are fixed to their jaws.
Unlike thecodonts, a pleurodont has teeth that are not set in sockets but attached along the inner jaw.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acrodont - animal with teeth attached to the crest of the jawbone
  • antonym: thecodont - animal with teeth in sockets (contrasting form of tooth attachment)
