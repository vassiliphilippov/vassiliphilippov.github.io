stoical

adjective
enduring pain without showing emotion
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈstəʊɪkəl / (BrE)
enduring pain or hardship without showing feelings or complaining; not affected by pleasure or pain

Examples

A stoical person
Despite the bad news, she remained stoical and composed in front of everyone.
He faced the long recovery with a stoical attitude, never once complaining.
The stoical patient waited quietly as the doctor explained the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emotional - showing strong feelings or emotion
  • synonym: impassive - showing no emotion
  • synonym: stoic - enduring pain or hardship without complaint
