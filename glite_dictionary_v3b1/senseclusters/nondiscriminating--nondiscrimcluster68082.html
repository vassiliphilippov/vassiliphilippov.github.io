nondiscriminating

adjective
not selective
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌdɪˈskrɪməˌneɪtɪŋ /; / ˌnɑn dɪˈskrɪməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌdɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ /; / ˌnɒn dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
not selective or unable to distinguish between different qualities, types, or signals

Examples

Nondiscriminating taste
The restaurant has a nondiscriminating menu that includes dishes from many different cuisines.
He is a nondiscriminating reader who enjoys books of all genres and styles.
Their hiring process was criticized for being nondiscriminating and lacking clear standards.
The nondiscriminating alarm sounded for both harmless and dangerous substances.
Because the filter was nondiscriminating, it let all particles pass through.
A nondiscriminating audience may enjoy a wide variety of performances without preference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discriminating - able to see differences and make careful choices
  • synonym: indiscriminate - done without careful choice or distinction
