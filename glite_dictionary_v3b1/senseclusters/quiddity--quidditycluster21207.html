quiddity

noun
subtle or evasive distinction in argument
7.5 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɪ.dɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈkwɪ.dɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
A subtle or evasive distinction or objection, especially one that distracts from the main point of an argument.

Examples

Legal quiddity
Her argument dissolved into quiddity instead of focusing on the real issue at hand.
Rather than address my concerns, he offered a quiddity about the definition of a single term.
The lawyer's quiddity during cross-examination frustrated the judge and wasted everyone's time.
