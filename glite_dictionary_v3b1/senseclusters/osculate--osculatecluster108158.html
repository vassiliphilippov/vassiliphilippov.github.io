osculate

verb
to share points or contact in mathematics
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀê.skj…ô.le…™t / (AmE), / Àà…ís.kj ä.le…™t / (BrE)
(Mathematics) For two curves or surfaces: to have at least three points, often a point of contact and two additional derivatives, in common at a location.

Examples

Curves osculate at point
In geometry, two circles are said to osculate if they share the same tangent and curvature at a point.
Mathematicians often study how surfaces osculate along a curve in higher dimensions.
The curves osculate at the origin, making their contact particularly strong there.
