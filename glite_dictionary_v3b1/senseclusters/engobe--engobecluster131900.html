engobe

noun
colored clay slip for coating pottery
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈɡoʊb / (AmE), / ɑ̃ˈɡəʊb / (BrE)
a clay slip mixed with colorants, used to coat the surface of pottery before or after firing

Examples

Colored engobe slip
Applying an engobe can help create unique textures and effects on pottery.
Ceramic artists often use engobe to add decorative patterns before glazing.
The engobe on the ancient vase gave it a smooth, colored finish.
