alpha-tocopherol

noun
a form of vitamin E; antioxidant nutrient
Pronunciation: / ˌælfə toʊˈkɑfəˌrɔl / (AmE), / ˌælfə təˈkɒfəˌrɒl / (BrE)
a potent form of vitamin E that occurs naturally in plant oils and is also produced synthetically; important for human nutrition as an antioxidant

Examples

Alpha-tocopherol vitamin E
Deficiency of alpha-tocopherol can lead to neurological problems in humans.
Many dietary supplements contain alpha-tocopherol as the main source of vitamin E.
Sunflower and wheat germ oils are rich in alpha-tocopherol.
