freeness

noun
property of being free
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːnəs / (AmE), / ˈfriːnɪs / (BrE)
the property of an algebraic structure, such as a group or module, of being free, meaning it is generated by a basis with no nontrivial relations among its elements

Examples

Freeness of a group
The freeness of the vector space allows for a unique representation of every element.
Mathematicians often check for freeness when studying modules over a ring.
The proof relied on the freeness of the group to establish the result.

  • synonym: freedom - the state of being free or unrestricted
