sacrist

noun
church caretaker
Pronunciation: / ˈsækrɪst / (AmE), / ˈsækrɪst / (BrE)
a person responsible for the care of sacred vessels, vestments, and other church items

Examples

The sacrist arranges vessels
The sacrist carefully arranged the chalices and candles before the ceremony.
After the service, the sacrist returned the vestments to their proper place.
Each morning, the sacrist unlocked the church and prepared the altar for mass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sacristan - a person in charge of the sacristy and its contents
  • synonym: verger - a church official who assists in religious services
