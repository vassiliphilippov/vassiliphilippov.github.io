Synonyms

  • up to date
    3.4 2/m
    up to date is the same phrase without hyphens often seen in casual writing, while up-to-date is the hyphenated adjective form.
  • up-to-the-minute
    4.4 0.5/m
    up-to-the-minute gives the sense of constant, very recent updates, while up-to-date means generally current or not out of date.
  • latest
    1.0 1/m
    latest refers to the single most recent item or news, while up-to-date means kept current over time.
  • modern
    1.4 50/m
    modern describes styles or ideas from recent times, while up-to-date stresses having the latest information or technology.
  • with-it
    8.1 0.5/m
    with-it describes a person who follows fashion and trends, while up-to-date describes things that have current information or style.
