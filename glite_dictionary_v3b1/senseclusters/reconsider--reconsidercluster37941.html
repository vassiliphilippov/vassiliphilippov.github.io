reconsider

verb
consider again, possibly change decision
3.7 8.43/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàs…™d…ôr / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàs…™d…ô / (BrE)
to think about something again, especially with the possibility of changing a previous decision or opinion

Examples

To reconsider a decision
After new evidence was presented, the committee agreed to reconsider the proposal.
The manager decided to reconsider her earlier decision after receiving additional feedback.
You should reconsider your stance if there are valid arguments on the other side.
After hearing all the facts, the committee agreed to reconsider the proposal.
She decided to reconsider after receiving new information.
You should reconsider your stance before making a final decision.

Synonyms

  • rethink
    3.4 8.84/m
    rethink means to think again and possibly change a decision, while reconsider is the common formal word for that.
  • reevaluate
    5.0 2.46/m
    reevaluate means to judge something again to change a previous decision, while reconsider is used in everyday and formal speech.
  • reassess
    3.7 2.75/m
    reassess means to consider and measure something again, often in detail, while reconsider is more general and covers simple rethinking.
  • reexamine
    3.7 2.09/m
    reexamine means to look at facts or evidence again to reach a new decision, while reconsider covers any second thought.
  • reappraise
    6.7 0.05/m
    reappraise means to judge value or importance again, often of things or ideas, while reconsider covers choices or opinions more broadly.
  • reweigh
    5.5 0.00/m
    reweigh means to weigh pros and cons again before deciding, while reconsider can mean any kind of thinking about a past decision.
  • revisit
    3.7 4.64/m
    revisit means to return to an idea or topic to look at it again, while reconsider focuses on changing a previous decision or opinion.
  • relook
    5.3 0.09/m
    relook means to look at or review something again, often informally, while reconsider implies careful thinking with a possible change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - make a final choice, especially as an antonym for reconsidering
  • synonym: rethink - think about again, often with the intent of changing a previous decision
  • synonym: review - examine or assess again
