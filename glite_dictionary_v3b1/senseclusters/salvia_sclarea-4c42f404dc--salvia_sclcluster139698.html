Salvia sclarea

noun
aromatic European herb; ornamental and potherb
Pronunciation: / ˈsæl.vi.ə sklɛəˈri.ə / (AmE), / ˈsal.vi.ə sklɑːˈrɪə / (BrE)
a species of aromatic herb, native to southern Europe and cultivated for its fragrant leaves and purple flowers, used as a potherb and ornamental plant.

Examples

Salvia sclarea plant
Salvia sclarea is prized for both its beautiful blooms and its aromatic foliage in gardens.
Some herbalists recommend using Salvia sclarea leaves in cooking for their subtle flavor.
The essential oil from Salvia sclarea is popular in aromatherapy and natural skincare products.
