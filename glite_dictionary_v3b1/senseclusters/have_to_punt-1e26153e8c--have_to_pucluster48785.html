have to punt

verb
defer or delay making a decision
Pronunciation: / hæv tə pʌnt / (AmE), / hæv tə pʌnt / (BrE)
to choose to delay or avoid making a decision or taking action, often because of uncertainty or difficulty

Examples

To have to punt a decision
Every time a controversial topic comes up, the committee seems to have to punt.
It’s a tough call, so we may have to punt and revisit the issue later.
The board couldn’t agree on the merger and decided they would have to punt until next month.
