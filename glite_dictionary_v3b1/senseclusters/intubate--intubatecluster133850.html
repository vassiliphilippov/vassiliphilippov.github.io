intubate

verb
insert a tube into a body canal
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.tuˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tjuː.beɪt / (BrE)
to insert a tube into a body canal, especially the trachea, usually to maintain an open airway or administer medication

Examples

To intubate the trachea
Learning how to intubate properly is essential for emergency room physicians.
Paramedics quickly intubated the unconscious man at the scene of the accident.
The doctor needed to intubate the patient to secure the airway during surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extubate - to remove a tube from a body canal, especially the trachea
