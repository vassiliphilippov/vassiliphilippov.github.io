rhizoidal

adjective
relating to a rhizoid
Pronunciation: / raɪˈzɔɪdəl / (AmE), / raɪˈzɔɪdəl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a rhizoid, a root-like structure found in some plants and fungi.

Examples

Rhizoidal filaments
Certain fungi form rhizoidal filaments to absorb nutrients from their environment.
Mosses use their rhizoidal structures primarily for attachment rather than nutrient uptake.
The algae displayed distinct rhizoidal growth at its base, anchoring it tightly to the substrate.
