exoterically

adverb
for general public
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksoʊˈtɛrɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɛksəˈtɛrɪkli /; / ˌɛksəʊˈtɛrɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is intended for or accessible to the general public (not private or secret)

Examples

Explain exoterically to students
The teacher explained the complex theory exoterically so that everyone could understand.
His research was presented exoterically to make it accessible to a broader audience.
The author chose to write exoterically rather than using technical jargon.
The philosopher explained his ideas exoterically so that everyone could understand.
She addressed the audience exoterically, avoiding any private or technical details.
Although he appeared calm exoterically, he was actually very nervous inside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: esoterically - in a way that is intended for or understood by only a small group
  • synonym: openly - in a way that is not secret or hidden
  • synonym: publicly - in a way that is open or accessible to everyone
