blow fly

noun
large metallic blue or green fly
Pronunciation: / bloʊ flaɪ / (AmE), / bləʊ flaɪ / (BrE)
a large, often metallic blue or green fly that lays its eggs in decaying flesh, dung, or wounds

Examples

The blow fly larvae
Blow fly larvae are often used in forensic investigations to estimate the time of death.
A blow fly landed on the meat left on the picnic table.
The presence of blow flies indicated that decomposition had already started.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluebottle - common name for certain blow flies, especially the blue species
