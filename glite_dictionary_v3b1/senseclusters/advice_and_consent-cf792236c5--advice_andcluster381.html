advice and consent

noun
Senate approval of presidential actions (U.S. law)
3.7 6.6
Pronunciation: /əd.ˈvaɪs ənd kən.ˈsent/ /; / /æd.ˈvaɪs ænd kən.ˈsent/ (AmE), /əd.ˈvaɪs ənd kən.ˈsent/ /; / /æd.ˈvaɪs ænd kən.ˈsent/ (BrE)
the legal authority given to the U.S. Senate to approve or reject presidential appointments and treaties as specified in the U.S. Constitution

Examples

Advice and consent power
The Constitution grants the Senate the power of advice and consent regarding judicial appointments.
The President's nominee for Secretary of State required the advice and consent of the Senate prior to confirmation.
Treaties negotiated by the President become effective only with the advice and consent of the Senate.
