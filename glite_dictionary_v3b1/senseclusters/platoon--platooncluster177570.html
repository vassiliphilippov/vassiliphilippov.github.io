platoon

verb
to group into units
7.5
Pronunciation: /plə.ˈtuːn/ (AmE), /plə.ˈtuːn/ (BrE)
to group or operate vehicles together in a coordinated way, often using automation

Examples

To platoon trucks
The trucks were platooned to travel more efficiently along the highway.
During the event, volunteers were platooned to manage different sections of the crowd.
To reduce congestion, the buses platooned as they entered the city center.
The new system allows trucks to platoon safely on long stretches of highway.
By platooning, the vehicles reduce air resistance and save fuel.
Several companies are testing automated technology to enable cars to platoon in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convoy - to travel together in a group, especially for protection or efficiency
  • antonym: disperse - to scatter or separate into different directions
  • synonym: organize - to arrange or form into a structured group
