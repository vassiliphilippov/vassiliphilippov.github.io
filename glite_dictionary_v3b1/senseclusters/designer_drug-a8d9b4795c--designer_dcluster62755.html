designer drug

noun
synthetic drug designed to evade drug laws
7.1 6.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈzaɪ.nər drʌg/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈzaɪ.nə drʌg/ (BrE)
A psychoactive drug that is chemically modified to avoid being classified as illegal, while mimicking the effects of a banned substance.

Examples

A designer drug online
Authorities discovered a new designer drug being sold online that was not yet illegal.
Many designer drugs can be far more dangerous than the substances they are meant to replace.
The chemist was arrested for manufacturing designer drugs intended to mimic ecstasy.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: controlled substance - a drug whose use is regulated by law
  • synonym: synthetic drug - a drug that is artificially created rather than naturally occurring
