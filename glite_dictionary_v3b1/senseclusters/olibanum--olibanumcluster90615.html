olibanum

noun
aromatic resin used in incense and rituals
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ɑː.lɪ.ˈbæn.əm/ (AmE), /ɒ.lɪ.ˈbæn.əm/ (BrE)
an aromatic gum resin obtained from certain trees of the genus Boswellia, used especially in incense, perfumes, and formerly in religious ceremonies and embalming

Examples

Ancient Egyptians used olibanum in their embalming process to preserve bodies.

Traders once traveled long distances to obtain olibanum from Arabian and African regions.
