diameter

noun
length across a circle through the center
5.3
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæmɪtər /; / daɪˈæmətɚ / (AmE), / daɪˈæmɪtə / (BrE)
the straight line that passes through the center of a circle or sphere and touches two points on its edge, or the length of this line

Examples

The diameter of circle
A larger diameter means the wheel can cover more distance per revolution.
The circle has a diameter of 10 centimeters.
We measured the diameter of the pipe before cutting it.
Engineers measured the diameter of the pipe to ensure it would fit the machinery.
The circle has a diameter of 10 centimeters, making its radius 5 centimeters.
The tree trunk was so thick that its diameter exceeded two feet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circumference - the distance around the boundary of a circle
  • degree lower: radius - a straight line from the center to the circumference of a circle
