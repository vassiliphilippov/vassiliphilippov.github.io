corruption

noun
decay; state of putrefaction
4.9 4.6
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈrʌp.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrʌp.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or state of physical decay or decomposition

Examples

Organic corruption
During decomposition, organic matter undergoes gradual corruption and returns to the earth.
He recoiled from the corruption that overtook the forgotten fruit in the cellar.
The meat was left out in the heat until it reached a visible corruption.
Fungi and bacteria speed up the corruption of dead organic material.
The corruption of the ancient manuscript made many passages unreadable.
To prevent corruption of the fruit, it must be kept in a cool and dry place.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - gradual destruction or decomposition of matter
  • antonym: preservation - the act of preventing decay or decomposition
  • synonym: putrefaction - process of decaying or rotting in a body or other organic matter
