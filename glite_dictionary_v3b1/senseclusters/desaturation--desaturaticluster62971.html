desaturation

noun
reducing color intensity
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing the intensity or purity of a color, making it less vivid or bright

Examples

Color desaturation effect
The desaturation of the photograph made the scene appear more somber and timeless.
Graphic designers often use desaturation to create a specific mood in their images.
After desaturation, the colors in the painting looked much softer and less vibrant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fading - the process of becoming less bright or intense in color
  • antonym: saturation - the intensity or purity of a color
