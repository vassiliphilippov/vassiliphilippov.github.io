Eunomian

adjective
of Eunomius or his sect
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈnoʊmiən / (AmE), / juːˈnəʊmiən / (BrE)
relating to Eunomius, his teachings, or the doctrines and practices of the Eunomian sect in early Christianity

Examples

Eunomian doctrine
The Eunomian controversy played a significant role in shaping early Christian theology.
Scholars have studied Eunomian arguments to understand debates about the nature of the Trinity.
Some church councils condemned Eunomian beliefs as heretical in the fourth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arian - relating to the teachings of Arius or Arianism
  • antonym: orthodox - conforming to established or traditional Christian beliefs
