axseed

noun
European herb with pink-and-white flowers
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈækˌsiːd / (AmE), / ˈaksˌiːd / (BrE)
A European herb resembling vetch, with umbels of pink-and-white flowers and sharp-angled pods, naturalized in the eastern United States.

Examples

The axseed plant
Botanists noted that axseed had spread rapidly in the eastern United States.
She picked a sprig of axseed to press in her collection of wildflowers.
The field was dotted with clusters of axseed among the taller grasses.
