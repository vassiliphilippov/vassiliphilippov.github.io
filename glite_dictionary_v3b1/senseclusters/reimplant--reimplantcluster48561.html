reimplant

verb
to restore belief
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪmˈplænt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to restore or establish an idea, feeling, habit, or belief again in someone's mind or within a group

Examples

To reimplant a belief
The teacher tried to reimplant a sense of curiosity in her students after a long break.
After the scandal, the organization worked hard to reimplant trust among its members.
Community leaders hope to reimplant traditional values through new educational programs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implant - to establish an idea or feeling firmly in someone's mind
  • synonym: reinstate - to bring back into use or existence
  • antonym: remove - to take away or eliminate something
