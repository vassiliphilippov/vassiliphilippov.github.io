liberal

adjective
favoring reform and progress
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪb.ɚ.əl /; / ˈlɪb.ər.əl /; / ˈlɪbɚəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪb.ər.əl /; / ˈlɪbərəl / (BrE)
having political or social views that support reform, progress, and individual freedoms; open-minded and tolerant of new ideas

Examples

Liberal views
Her liberal approach to politics emphasizes equal rights and social justice for all.
In a liberal society, people are encouraged to express different opinions.
Many young people hold liberal views on issues like climate change and education.
Her liberal attitude made her open to considering new policies that challenged tradition.
Many people admire societies with liberal values that embrace diversity and change.
The school encourages a liberal outlook among students, promoting tolerance and open-mindedness.
His parents are quite liberal and encourage open discussion at home.
She is known for her liberal views on social issues.
The newspaper has a liberal editorial policy, supporting freedom of expression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broad-minded - willing to accept different opinions or behaviors
  • antonym: conservative - opposed to change; favoring traditional values
  • antonym: narrow-minded - not willing to accept ideas or ways of behaving that are different from your own
  • synonym: progressive - favoring or implementing social reform or new, liberal ideas; advocating change
  • synonym: tolerant - willing to accept feelings, habits, or beliefs that are different from your own
