trimodal

adjective
having three modes
Pronunciation: / traɪˈmoʊdəl / (AmE), / traɪˈməʊdəl / (BrE)
having or involving three distinct modes, forms, or peaks

Examples

A trimodal pattern
The survey results displayed a trimodal pattern, indicating three main groups of responses.
In statistics, a trimodal distribution has three distinct peaks in its data set.
The traffic flow was trimodal, with morning, afternoon, and evening rush hours.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bimodal - having or involving two distinct modes or forms
  • degree lower: unimodal - having or involving a single mode or form
