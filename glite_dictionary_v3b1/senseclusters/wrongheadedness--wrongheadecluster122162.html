wrongheadedness

noun
stubborn misguidedness
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌrɔŋˈhɛdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌrɒŋˈhɛdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having stubborn or misguided opinions or ideas, especially in spite of evidence or reason

Examples

Her wrongheadedness attitude
Her wrongheadedness prevented the team from adopting a more effective strategy.
Despite the evidence, his wrongheadedness led him to stick to his original plan.
The debate was unproductive because of the wrongheadedness shown by both sides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obstinacy - stubborn refusal to change one's opinion
  • antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas
