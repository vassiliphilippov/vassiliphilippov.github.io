bromothymol blue

noun
acid-base indicator dye
Pronunciation: / ˌbroʊmoʊˌθaɪməl ˈbluː / (AmE), / ˌbrəʊməʊˌθʌɪmɒl ˈbluː / (BrE)
a dye used as an acid-base indicator, changing color between pH 6.0 and 7.6

Examples

bromothymol blue indicator
If the pH drops below 6.0, bromothymol blue will change from blue to yellow.
The solution turned green when bromothymol blue was added, indicating a neutral pH.
To test for carbon dioxide, she used bromothymol blue as an indicator in the experiment.
