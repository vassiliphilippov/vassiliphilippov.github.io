sublinear

adjective
slower than linear
8.8
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / sʌbˈlɪniə / (BrE)
growing or increasing at a rate slower than linear (less than a straight line), especially in mathematics or algorithms

Examples

Sublinear growth rate
The new search algorithm operates in sublinear time, making it very efficient for large databases.
A sublinear relationship means the output increases, but not as quickly as the input.
In mathematics, sublinear growth is often preferred for scalable systems.

A sublinear function increases, but not as quickly as a linear one.
Mathematicians often seek sublinear solutions to improve performance in computations.
