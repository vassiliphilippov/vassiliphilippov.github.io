long

adverb
for an extended distance
1.9
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋ / (BrE)
for a great distance or for a long time

Examples

Wait long hours
Her hair grew long down her back.
The river stretches long across the plain.
They drove long through the night before stopping to rest.
He waited long in the cold before the rescue team arrived.
She had long suspected that he was hiding something from her.
This tradition has long been part of our culture.

  • antonym: short - for a small amount of time; not long-lasting
