defensive attribution

noun
bias of blaming victims to protect oneself
4.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛnsɪv əˌtrɪbˈjuʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪv əˌtrɪbˈjuːʃən / (BrE)
A psychological tendency to blame victims for their misfortune in order to feel less vulnerable to similar events.

Examples

Defensive attribution bias
After hearing about the accident, his immediate defensive attribution was to assume the victim was at fault.
Psychologists study defensive attribution to understand why people sometimes lack empathy for those who suffer misfortune.
Research on defensive attribution has shown that people may blame others to protect their own sense of safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: victim blaming - holding victims responsible for their own misfortune
