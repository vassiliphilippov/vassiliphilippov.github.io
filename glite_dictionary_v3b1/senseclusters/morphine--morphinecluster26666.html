morphine

noun
powerful narcotic pain-relief drug
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrˌfiːn / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.fiːn / (BrE)
a powerful narcotic drug, derived from opium, used to relieve severe pain and known for its habit-forming properties

Examples

Strong morphine painkiller
After surgery, she was prescribed morphine to manage her discomfort.
Prolonged use of morphine can lead to physical dependence.
The doctor administered morphine to ease the patient's intense pain.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: codeine - a milder narcotic drug also derived from opium
  • synonym: heroin - a powerful, illegal narcotic derived from morphine
