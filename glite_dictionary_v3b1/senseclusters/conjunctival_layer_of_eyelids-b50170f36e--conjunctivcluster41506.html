conjunctival layer of eyelids

noun
part of eye lining inside the eyelids
8.9
Pronunciation: / kənˌdʒʌŋk.tɪˈvaɪ.əl ˈleɪ.ər əv ˈaɪˌlɪdz / (AmE), / kənˌdʒʌŋk.tɪˈvaɪ.əl ˈleɪ.ə əv ˈaɪˌlɪdz / (BrE)
the part of the conjunctiva that lines the inner surface of the eyelids and is continuous with the bulbar conjunctiva

Examples

The conjunctival layer of eyelids
During an eye examination, the doctor inspected the conjunctival layer of eyelids for any abnormalities.
Swelling in the conjunctival layer of eyelids can be a sign of conjunctivitis.
The conjunctival layer of eyelids helps protect the eye by forming a continuous lining.
