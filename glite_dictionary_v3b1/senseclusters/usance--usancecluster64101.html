usance

noun
period allowed for bill of exchange payment
Pronunciation: / ˈjusəns / (AmE), / ˈjuːzəns / (BrE)
the period of time allowed by commercial practice or law for payment of a bill of exchange, especially in international transactions

Examples

Usance period
A shorter usance may put pressure on buyers to settle quickly.
The usance for bills drawn between these countries is typically sixty days.
Traders must consider the local usance before agreeing on payment terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maturity - the time when a bill of exchange or similar instrument is due for payment
