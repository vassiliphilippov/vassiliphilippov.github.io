perquisite

noun
exclusive right or privilege
9.5 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úrkw…ôz…™t /; / Ààp…úÀêr.kw…™.z…™t / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêkw…™z…™t /; / Ààp…úÀê.kw…™.z…™t / (BrE)
a special benefit or privilege given because of your job or position, in addition to regular pay or rights

Examples

A perquisite of job
Company cars are often considered a perquisite of senior management positions.
Owning land was once the perquisite of nobility in many European societies.
Voting rights were historically a perquisite limited to wealthy property owners.
Executive perquisites often include club memberships and travel expenses.
Generous holiday allowances are considered valuable perquisites by many employees.
One of the perquisites of her position is the use of a company car.

Synonyms

  • perk
    3.5 4.72/m
    perk is an informal, everyday extra benefit often mentioned by workers, while perquisite is a more formal term for job-related privileges.
  • fringe benefit
    3.5 0.54/m
    fringe benefit is a company-provided extra like health insurance or a car, while perquisite covers any job-given privilege.
  • privilege
    3.7 25/m
    privilege is a special right given to a person or group in many settings, while perquisite is specific to a job.
