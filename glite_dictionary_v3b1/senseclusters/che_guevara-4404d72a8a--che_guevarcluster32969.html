Che Guevara

proper noun
Argentine revolutionary leader (1928–1967)
7.7
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪ ɡəˈvɑːrə / (AmE), / tʃeɪ ɡəˈvɑːrə / (BrE)
(1928–1967) An Argentine revolutionary leader and key figure in the Cuban Revolution, known for his role as Fidel Castro's chief lieutenant and for his involvement in various Latin American revolutionary movements.

Examples

Che Guevara revolutionary leader
In his travels, Che Guevara sought to inspire revolutionary movements throughout Latin America.
Many young activists still look up to Che Guevara as a symbol of rebellion.
The image of Che Guevara has become iconic in popular culture around the world.
