transgression

noun
geological covering of land by the sea
9.9
Pronunciation: / trænzˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / tranzˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
In geology, the spreading of the sea over land areas, resulting in the deposition of marine sediments on top of terrestrial sediments.

Examples

A marine transgression
A major transgression caused the coastline to move many kilometers inland.
Geologists identified a transgression by studying layers of marine limestone above sandstone.
The fossil record shows a clear transgression during the Cretaceous period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regression - the retreat of the sea from land, exposing previously submerged areas
