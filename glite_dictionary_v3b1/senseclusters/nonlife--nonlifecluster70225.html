nonlife

noun
absence of life
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌlaɪf / (BrE)
the state or condition of not being alive; things that do not possess life

Examples

Nonlife in nature
Scientists study both nonlife and living organisms to understand the natural world.
The museum exhibit displayed examples of nonlife such as minerals and crystals.
Water, air, and rocks are all considered forms of nonlife in the environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inanimate - not alive, especially not in the manner of animals and humans
  • antonym: life - the condition that distinguishes living things from nonliving things
