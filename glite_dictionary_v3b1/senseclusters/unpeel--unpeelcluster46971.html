unpeel

verb
to remove outer layer
3.4 0.17/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàpiÀêl / (AmE), /  ånÀàpiÀêl / (BrE)
to remove the outer covering, skin, or layer from something (such as fruit, vegetables, or labels)

Examples

To unpeel an orange
She began to unpeel the label from the bottle.
He carefully unpeeled the sticker from his laptop without leaving any residue.
You need to unpeel the protective film before using the new phone.
She was unpeeling the orange when the phone rang.
He carefully unpeeled the label from the jar to reuse it.
After washing the potatoes, start unpeeling them for the soup.

Synonyms

  • peel
    1.0 13/m
    peel is the common everyday action of taking off fruit or vegetable skin, while unpeel names that removal more rarely.
  • skin
    2.7 0.31/m
    skin means taking off the skin, often from animals or fish, while unpeel usually describes taking off outer layers from food or labels.
  • pare
    4.8 0.45/m
    pare is cutting away a thin outer layer to tidy or reduce size, while unpeel means simply removing the outer covering like skin or labels.
