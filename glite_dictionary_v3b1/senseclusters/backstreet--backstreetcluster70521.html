backstreet

noun
small side street
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstriːt / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstriːt / (BrE)
a small, quiet, or less important street away from the main roads in a town or city

Examples

A quiet backstreet
They wandered through a quiet backstreet looking for a shortcut to the station.
Many small shops can be found along the old backstreet behind the market.
The movie was set in the backstreets of a bustling European city.
The old bookstore is tucked away on a quiet backstreet far from the busy center.
They wandered through the narrow backstreets looking for a shortcut to the station.
Many hidden cafes can be found along the city's backstreets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alley - a narrow passageway between or behind buildings
  • antonym: main street - the principal street of a town or city
