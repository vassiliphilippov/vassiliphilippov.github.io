linter

noun
cottonseed fibers
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪntər / (AmE), / ˈlɪntə / (BrE)
the short, fine fibers that remain on cottonseed after the ginning process, often used to make cellulose or paper

Examples

Cottonseed linter
The factory uses linter from cottonseed to manufacture high-quality paper products.
After ginning, the remaining linter is collected and processed for industrial use.
Scientists developed a new method to extract cellulose from linter for biodegradable plastics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cottonseed - the seed of the cotton plant, often used for oil or animal feed
  • synonym: lint - short fibers separated from cotton seeds during ginning
