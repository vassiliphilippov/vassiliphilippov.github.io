vertical integration

noun
combination of all production stages in one company
6.8 0.28/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…úrt…™k…ôl Àå…™nt…ôÀà…°re…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Ààv…úÀêt…™k…ôl Àå…™nt…™Àà…°re…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the combination in one company of two or more stages of production normally operated by separate companies, from raw materials to final product distribution

Examples

Successful vertical integration strategy
Investors praised the manufacturer for its successful vertical integration strategy.
Recent vertical integration has led to greater efficiency and reduced costs.
The company's vertical integration allowed it to control every stage of the supply chain.

Synonyms

  • vertical combination
    7.8 0.00/m
    vertical combination is the merging of companies that handle different production stages, while vertical integration describes combining those stages within one company.
  • verticalism
    8.9 0.00/m
    verticalism is a company policy of controlling many production stages, while vertical integration names the actual combining of these stages into one firm.
