gazetteer

noun
journalist who writes for a gazette (historical)
Pronunciation: / ɡəˌzɛˈtɪr / (AmE), / ɡəˌzɛˈtɪə / (BrE)
(historical) A journalist or writer for a gazette, especially a newspaper or periodical.

Examples

A gazetteer writes news
In the eighteenth century, the gazetteer played a crucial role in shaping public opinion.
Samuel Johnson was once rumored to have been a gazetteer for a London newspaper.
The duties of a gazetteer often included gathering reports from foreign correspondents.
