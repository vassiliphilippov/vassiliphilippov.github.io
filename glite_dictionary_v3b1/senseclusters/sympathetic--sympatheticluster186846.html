sympathetic

adjective
eliciting empathy or approval (of characters)
9.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /sɪm.pə.ˈθe.tɪk/ (AmE), /sɪm.pə.ˈθe.tɪk/ (BrE)
(of a character in a book, play, etc.) eliciting feelings of pity, compassion, or approval from the audience or reader.

Examples

Sympathetic character
Despite his flaws, the main character remained remarkably sympathetic throughout the novel.
The author created sympathetic characters that readers could easily relate to and root for.
The film fails because none of the leads are remotely sympathetic or engaging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: likable - (of a character) easy to like or enjoy
  • antonym: unsympathetic - (of a character) failing to evoke empathy or approval
