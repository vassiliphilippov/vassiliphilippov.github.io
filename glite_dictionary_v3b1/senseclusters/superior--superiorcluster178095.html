superior

noun
person of higher rank or status
3.6
Pronunciation: / səˈpɪriər /; / səˈpɪriɚ / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
a person of higher rank or authority, especially within an organization or religious community

Examples

A superior at work
Employees are expected to follow instructions from their superior.
He reported the incident to his superior immediately after it happened.
If you have any questions, please ask your superior for guidance.
Sister Maria was appointed as the new superior of the convent last year.
The superior is responsible for the spiritual and administrative leadership of the community.
The superior of the monastery addressed the monks after morning prayers.
