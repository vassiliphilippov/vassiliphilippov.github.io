derivational morphology

noun
formation of words by morphological processes
7.6 6.9
Pronunciation: /dɪr.ɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən.əl mɔːr.ˈfɑːl.ə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /dɪr.ɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən.əl mɔː.ˈfɒl.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with how new words are formed by adding prefixes, suffixes, or other morphological processes to existing words

Examples

Derivational morphology in linguistics
A key topic in linguistics, derivational morphology explores how words like 'happiness' are formed from 'happy'.
In English, derivational morphology allows us to create adjectives such as 'modernize' from the noun 'modern'.
She wrote her thesis on derivational morphology in African languages.
