impeachability

noun
state of being subject to impeachment
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpitʃəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpiːtʃəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being liable to impeachment, especially in a legal or constitutional sense

Examples

The impeachability of officials
One's impeachability depends on both legal grounds and the evidence presented.
Scholars continue to discuss the impeachability of high-ranking officials in constitutional law.
The impeachability of the president became a subject of intense national debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immunity - exemption from legal proceedings or charges
  • synonym: impeachable - liable to impeachment; able to be accused or charged formally
