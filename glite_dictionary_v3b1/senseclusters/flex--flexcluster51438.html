flex

verb
contract a muscle by tightening it
Pronunciation: / flɛks / (AmE), / flɛks / (BrE)
to tighten or contract a muscle, often to show strength or for exercise

Examples

To flex your muscles
He likes to flex his biceps in front of the mirror.
She flexed her calf muscles during the warm-up routine.
Try to flex your stomach muscles and hold for five seconds.
The athlete loves to flex his muscles during competitions to intimidate his opponents.
The company decided to flex its financial strength by acquiring several smaller rivals.
The superstar singer flexed her vocal range during the concert's final song.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about achievements or possessions
  • antonym: relax - to make (a muscle) less tight or tense
  • synonym: show off - to display something in a way that is intended to attract attention or admiration
