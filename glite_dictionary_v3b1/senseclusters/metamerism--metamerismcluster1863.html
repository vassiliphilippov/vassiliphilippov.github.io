metamerism

noun
structural isomerism type
9.9
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈtæm.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /mə.ˈtæm.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a type of structural isomerism in organic chemistry where compounds have the same molecular formula but different arrangements of alkyl groups on either side of a functional group

Examples

Chemical metamerism
In organic chemistry, metamerism explains why some compounds have identical formulas but different properties.
The professor illustrated metamerism using examples of ethers with varying alkyl groups.
Understanding metamerism is important for distinguishing between similar-looking chemical compounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isomerism - the existence of compounds with the same molecular formula but different structures
  • synonym: tautomerism - a type of isomerism involving the relocation of a proton and a double bond
