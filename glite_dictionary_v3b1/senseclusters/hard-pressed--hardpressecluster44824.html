hard-pressed

adjective
in difficulty, especially financially
6.0 2.70/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërdÀàpr…õst / (AmE), / h…ëÀêdÀàpr…õst / (BrE)
Experiencing difficulty, especially financial or due to a lack of resources.

Examples

Many hard-pressed families
Many families are hard-pressed to pay their bills during economic downturns.
She was hard-pressed to find a solution to their financial problems.
The company is hard-pressed for cash after several months of poor sales.

Synonyms

  • hard up
    8.0 0.27/m
    hard up is short and informal for having very little money, while hard-pressed describes being in serious difficulty or lack of resources.
  • pinched
    6.5 0.00/m
    pinched means short of money and under financial strain, while hard-pressed covers general difficulty from a lack of resources.
  • distressed
    4.1 0.89/m
    distressed describes strong financial trouble or worry and sounds more formal, while hard-pressed is a broader everyday hardship.
  • struggling
    3.9 0.00/m
    struggling shows someone trying hard to cope or improve, while hard-pressed focuses on being short of money or resources.
  • hard put
    8.3 0.10/m
    hard put often follows be and describes difficulty doing something, while hard-pressed mainly describes lack of money or resources.
