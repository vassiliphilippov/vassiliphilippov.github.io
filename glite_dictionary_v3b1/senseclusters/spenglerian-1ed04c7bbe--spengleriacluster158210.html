Spenglerian

adjective
civilizational decline theory
9.9
Pronunciation: / spɛŋˈlɪriən / (AmE), / spɛŋˈlɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to the theories or worldview of Oswald Spengler, especially the idea that civilizations rise and fall in cycles and inevitably decline

Examples

Spenglerian worldview
The historian offered a Spenglerian perspective on the fate of Western civilization.
Her Spenglerian views led her to believe that all empires eventually collapse.
Critics argue that a Spenglerian approach overlooks the potential for renewal and progress.
The historian presented a Spenglerian analysis of the decline of Western culture.
Her essay takes a Spenglerian approach to the study of ancient empires.
Many critics argue against the Spenglerian idea that all civilizations must eventually fall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclical - recurring in cycles or periods
  • antonym: linear - progressing in a straight, continuous line
  • antonym: optimistic - expecting positive outcomes or improvement
  • synonym: pessimistic - expecting negative outcomes or decline
