noble

noun
a titled member of the aristocracy
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
a person who belongs to the aristocracy, especially one with a hereditary or official title in a country; a titled peer of the realm

Examples

A noble at court
He was made a noble by the king in recognition of his service.
Many reforms were resisted by the nobles who wished to keep their privileges.
The medieval castle was the residence of a powerful noble and his family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocrat - a member of the aristocracy
  • antonym: commoner - a person without noble rank
