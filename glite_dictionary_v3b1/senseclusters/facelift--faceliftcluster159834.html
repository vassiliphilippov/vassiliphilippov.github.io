facelift

noun
surgical procedure to tighten facial skin
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪsˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪslɪft / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove wrinkles and other signs of aging from the face, typically by tightening the skin and removing excess tissue

Examples

Cosmetic facelift
After his facelift, he looked ten years younger and more refreshed.
Many celebrities openly admit to getting a facelift to maintain a youthful appearance.
She decided to have a facelift to reduce the wrinkles around her eyes and mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rhytidectomy - medical term for a facelift (the same cosmetic surgery)
