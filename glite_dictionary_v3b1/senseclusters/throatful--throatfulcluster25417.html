throatful

noun
amount swallowed at once
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈθroʊtfʊl / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊtfʊl / (BrE)
an amount of liquid or substance that fills the throat or can be swallowed in one gulp

Examples

A throatful of water
After running the marathon, she drank a throatful of cold water to refresh herself.
He accidentally swallowed a throatful of pool water while swimming.
The medicine tasted bitter, but he managed to take a throatful without complaining.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gulp - a large and hurried swallow
  • synonym: mouthful - an amount that fills the mouth
