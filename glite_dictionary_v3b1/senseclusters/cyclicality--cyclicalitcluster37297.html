cyclicality

noun
degree of cycles
8.0
Pronunciation: /sɪ.klɪ.ˈkæl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /saɪ.klɪ.ˈkæl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /saɪ.klɪ.ˈkæl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /sɪ.klɪ.ˈkæl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or extent of happening in cycles or being characterized by repeated cycles, especially in economics or other areas

Examples

The cyclicality of markets
Economists study the cyclicality of different industries to predict how they will perform during recessions.
The cyclicality of commodity prices makes long-term planning challenging for producers.
Understanding the cyclicality of employment helps governments design better economic policies.
Economists study the cyclicality of unemployment rates to predict future trends.
The cyclicality of fashion means that old styles often become popular again.
Investors must consider the cyclicality of certain industries before making decisions.

  • antonym: randomness - the quality of lacking any predictable order or plan
  • synonym: recurrence - the act of happening again or repeatedly
  • antonym: stability - the quality of being steady and unchanging
  • synonym: volatility - the tendency to change rapidly and unpredictably
