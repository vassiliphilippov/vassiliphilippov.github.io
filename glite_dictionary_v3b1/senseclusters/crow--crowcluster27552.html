crow

verb
express triumph or satisfaction loudly
Pronunciation: / kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
to talk or speak in a proud, boastful, or triumphant way

Examples

He crows about success
He crows so much about his achievements that it annoys his colleagues.
She couldn't help but crow about her success in the competition.
The team crowed with delight after winning the championship.
After winning the championship, the team crowed over their rivals for weeks.
He likes to crow whenever he gets top marks in class.
She couldn't help but crow about her promotion at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • antonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym: gloat - to dwell with smugness on one's own success
