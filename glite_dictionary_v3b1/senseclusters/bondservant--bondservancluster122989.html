bondservant

noun
devoted religious servant
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑndˌsɝːvənt / (AmE), / ˈbɒndˌsɜːvənt / (BrE)
a person who is bound to serve another, especially in a religious or biblical context, often as a devoted follower or servant of God.

Examples

Bondservant of Christ
In the New Testament, Paul often refers to himself as a bondservant of Christ.
The ancient text described the prophet as a faithful bondservant who obeyed every command.
She saw her role in the community as that of a humble bondservant dedicated to helping others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: master - a person who has control or authority over others
  • synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others
  • synonym: slave - a person who is legally owned by someone else and forced to work for them
