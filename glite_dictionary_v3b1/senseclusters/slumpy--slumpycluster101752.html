slumpy

adjective
performing poorly
Pronunciation: / ˈslʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈslʌmpi / (BrE)
experiencing a period of poor performance, weak activity, or low demand, especially in business or sales.

Examples

Slumpy sales period
The company had a slumpy quarter, with profits falling below expectations.
After a slumpy start to the year, the market began to recover in spring.
Analysts warned that slumpy demand could continue if consumer confidence remains low.

Related Terms

  • antonym: booming - experiencing rapid growth or high activity
  • synonym: sluggish - moving or performing slowly or with little energy
