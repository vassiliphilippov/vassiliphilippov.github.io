outdate

verb
to predate something
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to be older than something else in time; to predate another thing or event

Examples

Outdate the building
These ruins clearly outdate the nearby castle by several centuries.
The family legend claims that their house outdates most buildings in the village.
Some of the artifacts outdate written records, making their origins difficult to trace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postdate - to exist or happen after something else
  • synonym: predate - to exist or happen before something else
