phen-

x
chemistry prefix
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɛn / (AmE), / fɛn / (BrE)
a combining form in chemistry used in the names of compounds containing a phenyl group (C6H5–).

Examples

Phen- group compound
The presence of the phen- prefix in the compound's name indicated a phenyl group was attached.
Students learned that phen- is used in naming aromatic compounds in organic chemistry.
When you see phen- in a chemical name, it usually means a benzene ring is part of the structure.

Related Terms
