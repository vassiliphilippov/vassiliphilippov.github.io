salt

verb
add liveliness or interest
9.3
Pronunciation: / sɔlt / (AmE), / sɒlt / (BrE)
to add interest, liveliness, or small details to something, as if sprinkling salt

Examples

To salt a story
He tends to salt his presentations with funny images to keep the audience engaged.
The author likes to salt her stories with witty remarks and anecdotes.
You can salt your conversations with interesting facts to make them more captivating.
She salted the story with subtle hints about her true feelings.
The enemy salted the road with hidden traps to slow the advancing forces.
The farmer salted the earth with chemicals to prevent anything from growing there.
