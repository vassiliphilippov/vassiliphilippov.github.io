anhidrosis

noun
failure of the sweat glands
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.hɪˈdroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌan.hɪˈdrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which the sweat glands do not produce sweat, leading to an inability to sweat normally.

Examples

Anhidrosis condition
Because of her anhidrosis, she is unable to sweat and must avoid hot environments.
Certain medications can cause anhidrosis as a side effect, making exercise potentially dangerous.
The diagnosis of anhidrosis is important in patients presenting with unexplained overheating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperhidrosis - excessive sweating
