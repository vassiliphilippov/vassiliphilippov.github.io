multistory

adjective
having more than one floor (building)
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈstɔri / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈstɔːri / (BrE)
(of a building) having more than one floor or story.

Examples

A multistory building
A new multistory car park opened near the train station last week.
Firefighters responded quickly to the blaze in the multistory office building.
The city is full of multistory apartment blocks built in the 1970s.
Many modern offices are housed in multistorey buildings to maximize space.
The council approved the construction of a new multistorey car park.
They live in a multistorey apartment complex near the city center.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: high-rise - a building with many stories, typically very tall
  • antonym: single-storey - having only one story or level (of a building)
  • antonym: single-story - (of a building) having only one story or floor
