thrown-away

adjective
discarded; thrown out
Pronunciation: / ˈθroʊn əˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊn əˌweɪ / (BrE)
(of something) discarded or rejected as unwanted or useless.

Examples

Thrown-away clothes
Many thrown-away items end up being recycled or repurposed.
She found a thrown-away bicycle on the sidewalk and fixed it up.
The thrown-away magazines were still in good condition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retained - kept or continued to be held
