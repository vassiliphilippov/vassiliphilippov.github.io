nonsynonymous

adjective
genetic change affecting protein
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌsɪˈnɑnɪməs / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌsɪˈnɒnɪməs / (BrE)
describing a genetic change that alters the amino acid sequence of a protein

Examples

Nonsynonymous mutation
A nonsynonymous mutation can lead to a different protein being produced in the cell.
Researchers found several nonsynonymous changes in the gene that may affect its function.
The study compared the rates of nonsynonymous and synonymous mutations across species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synonymous - describing a genetic change that does not alter the amino acid sequence
