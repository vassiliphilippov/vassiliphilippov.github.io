mechanical stress

noun
force per unit area in a material
7.0 4.1
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈkæn.ɪ.kəl stres/ (AmE), /mə.ˈkæn.ɪ.kəl stres/ (BrE)
The internal force per unit area within a material caused by external forces, including both elastic and viscous effects.

Examples

Mechanical stress on materials
Engineers conducted tests to measure the mechanical stress on the turbine blades.
Repeated bending can cause failure in materials subjected to excessive mechanical stress.
The bridge was designed to withstand high amounts of mechanical stress during storms.
