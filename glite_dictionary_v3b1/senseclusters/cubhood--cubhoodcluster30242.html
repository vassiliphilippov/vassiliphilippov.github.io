cubhood

noun
early stage; inexperience
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌbˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈkʌbˌhʊd / (BrE)
the early stage of life or development, especially the period when someone or something is inexperienced or immature

Examples

Cubhood as a beginner
During his cubhood as a journalist, he learned valuable lessons from his mistakes.
The organization faced many challenges in its cubhood but grew stronger over time.
Just as a lion learns to hunt in its cubhood, young professionals develop skills early in their careers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: maturity - the state of being fully developed or experienced
  • synonym: youth - the time of being young or inexperienced
