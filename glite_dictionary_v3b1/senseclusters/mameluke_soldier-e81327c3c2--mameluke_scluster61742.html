Mameluke soldier

noun
historical Muslim soldier, often in Egypt
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmæ.mə.luːk ˈsoʊl.dʒər/ (AmE), /ˈmæ.mə.luːk ˈsəʊl.dʒə/ (BrE)
A member of a military class originally composed of enslaved people converted to Islam, who served as soldiers and rulers in various Muslim societies, especially in Egypt from the 13th to the early 19th century.

Examples

The Mameluke soldier class
During the Ottoman era, the Mameluke soldier played a vital role in Egyptian society.
Legends tell of the skill and loyalty of every Mameluke soldier.
The Mameluke soldier rose through the ranks to become a respected commander.
