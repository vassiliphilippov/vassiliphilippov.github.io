gyratory

noun
rock-crushing machine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪrəˌtəri / (BrE)
a machine for crushing rock or ore that uses a rotating conical head inside a fixed shell to break material.

Examples

A gyratory crusher
The mining company installed a new gyratory to handle larger volumes of ore.
Engineers inspected the gyratory regularly to ensure it operated safely and efficiently.
A malfunction in the gyratory caused a temporary halt in production at the quarry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crusher - a machine designed to break large rocks into smaller pieces
