bag

verb
to hang loosely like a bag
9.0
Pronunciation: / bæɡ / (AmE), / bæɡ / (BrE)
to hang loosely or bulge out in a way that resembles a bag

Examples

Socks bag at ankles
After a long day, his socks would bag around his ankles.
The old curtains bag in the middle from years of use.
The seat covers have started to bag where people sit most often.
Over time, the old mattress bagged where people slept the most.
The tent's fabric began to bag in the middle under the weight of the rain.
With a strong wind, the sails bag until they are nearly round.
