bop

noun
early form of modern jazz
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɑp / (AmE), / bɒp / (BrE)
an early form of modern jazz, developed around 1940, characterized by fast tempo and improvisation

Examples

Classic bop jazz
Bop revolutionized jazz by introducing complex harmonies and rapid improvisation.
He has an impressive collection of bop records from the genre's early days.
Many jazz musicians of the 1940s were drawn to bop for its creative freedom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bebop - a style of jazz developed in the 1940s with complex harmonies and rhythms
