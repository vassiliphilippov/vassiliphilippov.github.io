specialize

verb
evolve to suit a specific environment
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz /; / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt, develop, or evolve for a specific purpose, function, or environment

Examples

Animals specialize for survival
Amphibians often specialize to exploit specific ecological niches.
Over millions of years, certain birds specialized to survive in desert habitats.
The island's isolated population began to specialize, eventually forming a new species.
Her laboratory decided to specialize its research in renewable energy technologies.
Many hospitals specialize in treating specific types of diseases.
The machine was specialized for high-precision work in electronics manufacturing.
As the climate changed, the mammal population gradually specialised to survive in the new environment.
Over many generations, the finches specialised for different food sources on the islands.
Some insects specialise in feeding on a single type of plant.

  • antonym: generalize - make something more general or less specialized
