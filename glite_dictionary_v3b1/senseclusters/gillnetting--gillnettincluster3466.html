gillnetting

noun
fishing with nets
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈgɪl.net.ɪŋ/ /; / /gɪl.ˈnet.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈgɪl.net.ɪŋ/ /; / /gɪl.ˈnet.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a fishing method in which nets are used to catch fish by their gills as they try to swim through

Examples

Commercial gillnetting
Commercial fishermen often rely on gillnetting to catch large quantities of fish efficiently.
Environmental groups have raised concerns about the impact of gillnetting on marine life.
Regulations now limit gillnetting in certain areas to protect endangered species.
