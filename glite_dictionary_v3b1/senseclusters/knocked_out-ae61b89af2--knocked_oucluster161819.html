knocked out

adjective
unconscious due to a blow
4.6 0.80/m
Pronunciation: / nÉ‘kt aÊŠt / (AmE), / nÉ’kt aÊŠt / (BrE)
Unconscious as a result of a heavy blow, especially to the head.

Examples

He was knocked out
After being hit on the head, he was knocked out and only woke up in the hospital.
She slipped on the ice and was knocked out for several minutes before help arrived.
The boxer was knocked out in the third round by a powerful punch.

Synonyms

  • out
    6.8 0.00/m
    out describes being unconscious after a blow or injury, while knocked out stresses a heavy hit that causes this state.
  • out cold
    6.9 0.79/m
    out cold means completely unconscious or deeply asleep, often from injury or tiredness, while knocked out focuses on a heavy blow.
  • KO'd
    9.6 0.00/m
    KO'd names a boxer sent unconscious by a strong punch, while knocked out is the broader term for such unconsciousness.
  • kayoed
    9.9 0.00/m
    kayoed is slang for being sent unconscious in a fight, while knocked out is neutral and fits many contexts beyond boxing.
