spoilation

noun
stripping or taking by force
Pronunciation: / ˌspɔɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɔɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of stripping or taking something by force; plundering or pillaging

Examples

The spoilation of goods
Evidence of spoilation was found after the village had been ransacked.
Laws were enacted to prevent the spoilation of valuable property by invading armies.
The spoilation of cultural artifacts during the war left many museums nearly empty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pillage - the act of robbing or plundering, especially during war
  • synonym: plunder - the act of stealing goods by force, especially in time of war
  • antonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original owner or condition
