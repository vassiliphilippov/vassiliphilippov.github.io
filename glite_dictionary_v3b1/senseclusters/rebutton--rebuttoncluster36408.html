rebutton

verb
to button again
4.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈbʌt.ən/ /; / /rə.ˈbʌt.ən/ /; / /riː.ˈbʌt.ən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈbʌt.ən/ /; / /rə.ˈbʌt.ən/ /; / /riː.ˈbʌt.ən/ (BrE)
to fasten the buttons on something again after they have been undone or come loose

Examples

To rebutton a shirt
After washing his shirt, he had to rebutton it before hanging it up.
The wind blew her coat open, so she stopped to rebutton it.
He quickly rebuttoned his jacket before entering the meeting room.
After taking off her scarf, she had to rebutton her jacket before going outside.
He quickly rebuttoned his shirt when he realized it was uneven.
The child learned how to rebutton his coat by himself after recess.
After taking off her jacket, she had to rebutton it before going outside again.
He quickly rebuttoned his shirt when he realized it was uneven.
If your coat comes undone, just rebutton it to keep warm.
