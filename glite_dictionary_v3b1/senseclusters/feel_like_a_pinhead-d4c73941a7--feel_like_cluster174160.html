feel like a pinhead

verb
to feel foolish or embarrassed
4.1
Pronunciation: / fil laɪk ə ˈpɪnˌhɛd / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈpɪnhɛd / (BrE)
to feel foolish or embarrassed, often as a result of a mistake or oversight

Examples

To feel like a pinhead after mistake
I felt like a pinhead after spilling coffee all over the table during the meeting.
She felt like a pinhead when she realized she'd been speaking to the wrong person all along.
Whenever he forgets someone's name, he feels like a pinhead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: feel confident - to feel sure of oneself; opposite of feeling foolish
  • synonym: feel foolish - to feel silly or lacking in good judgment
