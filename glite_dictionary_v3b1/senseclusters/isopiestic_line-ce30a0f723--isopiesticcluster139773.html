isopiestic line

noun
line connecting equal groundwater pressure points
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊˈpiːstɪk laɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈpiːstɪk laɪn / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map connecting points where the ground water pressure is equal.

Examples

An isopiestic line on map
After analyzing the pressure data, they connected the dots to create an isopiestic line on the report.
Maps showing isopiestic lines can help engineers locate safe sites for well construction.
The hydrologist drew an isopiestic line to mark areas with identical groundwater pressure across the valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contour line - a line joining points of equal elevation on a map
