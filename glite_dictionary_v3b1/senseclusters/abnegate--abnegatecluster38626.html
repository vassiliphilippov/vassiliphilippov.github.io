abnegate

verb
to deny oneself; to restrain
9.1 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæb.nɪ.geɪt/ /; / /ˈæb.niː.geɪt/ (AmE), /ˈæb.nɪ.geɪt/ (BrE)
to deliberately deny yourself something or give up a desire, habit, or belief

Examples

To abnegate pleasure
He decided to abnegate his desire for sweets during the health retreat.
Many monks abnegate worldly pleasures as part of their spiritual practice.
She chose to abnegate luxury in order to live a simpler life.
After years of addiction, she decided to abnegate harmful habits and start anew.
He chose to abnegate all personal desires for the sake of his family.
Some religious traditions encourage followers to abnegate worldly pleasures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstain - to deliberately refrain from something, especially pleasurable activities
  • antonym: embrace - to accept or support something willingly or enthusiastically
  • antonym: indulge - to allow oneself to enjoy pleasure
  • synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • synonym: renounce - to formally declare one's abandonment of a claim, right, or possession
