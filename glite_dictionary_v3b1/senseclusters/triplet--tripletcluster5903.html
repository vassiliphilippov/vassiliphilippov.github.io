triplet

noun
three-sample unit for metric learning
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪplət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplət / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a group of three related examples — an anchor, a positive example similar to the anchor, and a negative example dissimilar to the anchor — used to train models with triplet loss or other metric-learning objectives.

Examples

A triplet sample
During training, the model was fed a triplet consisting of an anchor, positive, and negative example.
Researchers often mine hard triplet examples to improve embedding quality in retrieval tasks.
When training with triplets, careful selection of positives and negatives is essential for convergence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embedding - a learned vector representation of data items used for similarity and retrieval tasks
  • synonym: triplet loss - a metric-learning loss function that trains models using triplets (anchor, positive, negative) to shape embeddings
