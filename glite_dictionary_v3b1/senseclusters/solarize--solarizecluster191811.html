solarize

verb
expose to sunlight or its effects
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ.ləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ləˌraɪz /; / ˈsəʊ.lə.raɪz / (BrE)
to expose something to sunlight or to affect something by exposure to the sun's rays

Examples

To solarize seeds

Leaving the fabric outside too long can solarize its colors and cause fading.
The artist solarized the prints by leaving them out in direct sunlight for several hours.
Delicate artworks can be damaged if you solarise them excessively.
It is important not to solarise young plants too quickly after moving them outdoors.
To disinfect the soil, they decided to solarise the area for several weeks.
