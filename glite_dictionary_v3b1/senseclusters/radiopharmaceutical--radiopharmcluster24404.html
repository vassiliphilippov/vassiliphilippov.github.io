radiopharmaceutical

noun
radioactive drug for diagnosis or therapy
6.6 6.6
Pronunciation: /reɪ.di.oʊ.fɑːr.mə.ˈsuː.tɪ.kəl/ /; / /reɪ.di.oʊ.fɑːr.mə.ˈsjuː.tɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /reɪ.di.əʊ.fɑː.mə.ˈsjuː.tɪ.kəl/ /; / /reɪ.di.əʊ.fɑː.mə.ˈsuː.tɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
A pharmaceutical drug that contains a radioactive compound and is used in diagnosis or treatment, especially in radiation therapy or medical imaging.

Examples

A radiopharmaceutical drug
A radiopharmaceutical was injected into the patient before the PET scan.
Advances in radiopharmaceutical development are improving cancer diagnosis and treatment.
The hospital uses radiopharmaceuticals to help detect abnormalities during imaging scans.
