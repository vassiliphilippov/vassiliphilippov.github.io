aggravatingly

adverb
in an annoying or irritating way
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡrəˌveɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈaɡrəˌveɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes annoyance or irritation.

Examples

An aggravatingly slow reply
His questions were aggravatingly persistent, refusing to let the topic drop.
She tapped her pen aggravatingly on the desk, distracting everyone in the room.
The meeting was aggravatingly long and unproductive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soothingly - in a manner that calms or soothes
