hothouse conditions

noun
warm, controlled plant-growing environment
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑtˌhaʊs kənˈdɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌhaʊs kənˈdɪʃənz / (BrE)
An environment with high temperature and humidity, maintained artificially to promote plant growth, often used in greenhouses or conservatories.

Examples

Plants need hothouse conditions
Certain exotic plants cannot survive outside of hothouse conditions in colder climates.
Orchids often flourish best under hothouse conditions that mimic their native tropical habitat.
When seedlings are raised in hothouse conditions, they may require gradual exposure to outdoor weather before planting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: greenhouse - a glass building for growing plants in controlled conditions
