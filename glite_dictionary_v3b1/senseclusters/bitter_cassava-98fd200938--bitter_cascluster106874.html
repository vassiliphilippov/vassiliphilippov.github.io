bitter cassava

noun
type of cassava with edible roots
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬ɚ kəˈsɑːvə / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə kəˈsɑːvə / (BrE)
A variety of cassava with long tuberous edible roots and soft brittle stems, high in cyanogenic compounds, used especially to make cassiri and tapioca.

Examples

Bitter cassava roots
Bitter cassava must be processed carefully to remove toxins before it is safe to eat.
Farmers often prefer growing bitter cassava due to its higher pest resistance.
Traditional beverages like cassiri are prepared using bitter cassava roots.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sweet cassava - a variety of cassava with less bitter taste and lower cyanide levels
