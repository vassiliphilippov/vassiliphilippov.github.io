law of action and reaction

noun
Newton's third law of motion
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ˈækʃən ənd riˈækʃən / (AmE), / lɔː ɒv ˈækʃən ənd riˈækʃən / (BrE)
Newton's third law of motion, stating that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Examples

Law of action and reaction in physics
According to the law of action and reaction, pushing on a wall causes the wall to push back with equal force.
Swimmers move forward in water by pushing backward, demonstrating the law of action and reaction.
The law of action and reaction explains why a rocket launches upward when its engines fire downward.
