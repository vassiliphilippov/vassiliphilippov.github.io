isoelectric

adjective
no net electric charge
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (BrE)
having equal electric charge or no net electric charge, especially in a molecule or solution

Examples

Isoelectric point
The solution became isoelectric at a specific pH value during the experiment.
Scientists measured the isoelectric point of the protein to understand its properties.
At the isoelectric point, the molecule does not move in an electric field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: charged - having a net positive or negative electric charge
  • synonym: neutral - having no net electric charge
