systematic desensitization

noun
gradual exposure therapy for phobias
6.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /sɪs.tə.ˈmæt.ɪk dɪ.sen.sɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪs.tə.ˈmæt.ɪk diː.sen.sɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sɪs.tə.ˈmæt.ɪk dɪ.sen.sɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪs.tə.ˈmæt.ɪk diː.sen.sɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A method in behavior therapy for treating phobias and anxiety disorders, in which a person is gradually exposed to the source of fear while practicing relaxation techniques to reduce anxiety.

Examples

Systematic desensitization therapy
Many therapists use systematic desensitization as part of their approach to treating anxiety disorders.
The psychologist recommended systematic desensitization to help her overcome her fear of spiders.
Through systematic desensitization, the patient gradually became comfortable with flying.
Researchers found systematic desensitisation to be effective in treating phobias such as fear of flying.
The psychologist recommended systematic desensitisation to help her overcome her intense fear of dogs.
Through systematic desensitisation, patients gradually learn to confront their anxieties in a controlled environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exposure therapy - A psychological treatment involving direct exposure to feared objects or situations.
  • synonym: flooding - a behavioral therapy exposing patients directly to feared situations
