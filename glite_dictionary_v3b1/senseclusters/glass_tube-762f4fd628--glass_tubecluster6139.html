glass tube

noun
cylindrical glass laboratory container
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs tuːb / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs tjuːb / (BrE)
a cylindrical container or piece of equipment made of glass, typically used in laboratories for holding, mixing, or conducting substances.

Examples

A glass tube container
Broken pieces of glass tube should be disposed of safely to prevent injury.
He heated the glass tube over a Bunsen burner during the experiment.
The scientist carefully poured the chemical into the glass tube for the reaction.
