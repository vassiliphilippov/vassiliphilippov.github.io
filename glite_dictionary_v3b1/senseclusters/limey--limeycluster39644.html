limey

noun
(slang) British person
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪmi / (AmE), / ˈlaɪmi / (BrE)
(slang, sometimes offensive) a British person, especially a sailor, originally referring to Englishmen; historically used in North America and Australia.

Examples

A limey sailor
He didn't mind being called a limey by his American friends, though he knew the history behind the term.
The Australians greeted the visiting limey with a mixture of curiosity and amusement.
The old film featured a gruff limey sea captain who always had a witty insult ready.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Brit - informal term for a British person
