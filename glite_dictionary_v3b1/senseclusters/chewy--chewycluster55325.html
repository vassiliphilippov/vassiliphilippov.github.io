chewy

adjective
requiring much chewing
1.0 1.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt ÉuÀê.i /; / Ààt Éu.i / (AmE), / Ààt ÉuÀê.i / (BrE)
(of food or substances) requiring a lot of chewing because it is tough, elastic, or rubbery

Examples

A chewy texture
Fresh bagels can be pleasantly chewy on the inside.
Some candies are deliberately made chewy to be fun to eat.
The steak was so chewy that it took me minutes to finish one bite.
Children often love chewy candies for their texture and flavor.
The steak was so chewy that it took me several minutes to finish each bite.
These chewy granola bars make a perfect snack for hiking.

Synonyms

  • rubbery
    3.7 0.49/m
    rubbery is bouncy and elastic in texture, while chewy means food that needs a lot of chewing because it is tough or springy.
  • tough
    1.7 2.17/m
    tough describes food that is hard to cut or chew, while chewy describes food you must chew a long time because it is elastic.
