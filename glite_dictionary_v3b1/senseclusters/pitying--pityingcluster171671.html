pitying

adjective
expressing condescending pity
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtiɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtiɪŋ / (BrE)
showing or expressing pity for someone, sometimes in a way that suggests sympathy or condescension

Examples

A pitying look
She gave him a pitying look when he failed the test.
His pitying tone made her feel even worse about her mistake.
They offered a pitying smile, not realizing how patronizing it seemed.
He gave her a pitying smile when he heard about her difficulties.
The teacher looked at the struggling student with a pitying expression.
They offered a pitying glance to the man sitting alone in the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others
  • antonym: indifferent - not showing interest or concern
  • antonym: scornful - showing contempt or disdain
  • synonym: sympathetic - showing understanding or care for someone's troubles
