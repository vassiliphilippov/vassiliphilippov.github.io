tetraploid

noun
organism with four chromosome sets
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtrəˌplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəplɔɪd / (BrE)
an organism or cell that contains four complete sets of chromosomes instead of the usual two

Examples

A tetraploid plant
The scientist identified a tetraploid plant that was larger than its diploid relatives.
Some species of frogs are naturally tetraploid, giving them unique genetic traits.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of tetraploid cells in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diploid - an organism or cell with two complete sets of chromosomes
  • synonym: polyploid - an organism or cell with more than two sets of chromosomes
