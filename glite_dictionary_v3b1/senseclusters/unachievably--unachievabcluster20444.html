unachievably

adverb
in an unattainable manner
8.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: /  ån…ôÀàt Éiv…ôbli / (AmE), /  ån…ôÀàt ÉiÀêv…ôbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be achieved or reached; to an unattainable extent.

Examples

An unachievably high goal
After the deadline changed, finishing the project on time became unachievably difficult for the team.
Many people find the ideal portrayed in the media to be unachievably perfect.
The new height record is almost unachievably high for any athlete.

Synonyms

  • unattainably
    7.5 0.01/m
    unattainably means not possible to reach or gain in real life, while unachievably strongly stresses that it truly cannot be achieved.
  • unreachably
    8.3 0.01/m
    unreachably describes a place or thing that cannot be reached by people or vehicles, while unachievably refers to goals people cannot attain.
  • insurmountably
    8.8 0.03/m
    insurmountably describes a barrier or problem that cannot be overcome, while unachievably describes something no one can achieve.
  • insuperably
    9.9 0.01/m
    insuperably means it cannot be overcome or solved, often in formal speech or writing, while unachievably is more general.
  • impossibly
    5.7 3.29/m
    impossibly often means very unlikely or hard to believe in everyday speech, while unachievably means truly impossible to reach or do.
  • unfeasibly
    6.9 0.01/m
    unfeasibly describes plans or ideas that are not practical or very hard to do, while unachievably means impossible to achieve.
