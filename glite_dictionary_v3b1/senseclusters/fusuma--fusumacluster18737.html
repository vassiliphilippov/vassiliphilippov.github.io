fusuma

noun
Japanese sliding door
Pronunciation: / fuːˈsuːmə / (AmE), / fuːˈsuːmə / (BrE)
a sliding door covered with paper, used to divide rooms in traditional Japanese houses

Examples

A fusuma door
The artist painted a beautiful landscape on the fusuma in the main hall.
Guests entered the tatami room by sliding open the fusuma quietly.
The fusuma separated the living area from the sleeping quarters in the old house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shoji - a Japanese sliding door made of translucent paper on a wooden frame
