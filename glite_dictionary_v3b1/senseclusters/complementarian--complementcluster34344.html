complementarian

adjective
gender role beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpləˌmɛnˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪmɛnˈteəriən / (BrE)
believing that men and women have different but complementary roles, especially in religion or society

Examples

Complementarian beliefs in marriage
The organization has a complementarian view of leadership, assigning different roles to men and women.
Some religious groups promote a complementarian approach to family and community life.
Her beliefs are complementarian, emphasizing distinct but cooperative roles for each gender.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarian - believing in equal rights and roles for all people
