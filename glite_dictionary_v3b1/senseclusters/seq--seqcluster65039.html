seq

noun
Unix number generator
9.6
Pronunciation: / siːk / (AmE), / siːk / (BrE)
a small Unix or Linux command-line program that prints a sequence of numbers, often used in scripts

Examples

Run seq command
The script uses seq to generate a list of numbers from 1 to 10.
You can combine seq with other commands to automate repetitive tasks in Linux.
Typing seq 5 10 will print the numbers from 5 to 10 on separate lines.
