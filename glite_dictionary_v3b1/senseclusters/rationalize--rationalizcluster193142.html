rationalize

verb
think logically or reasonably
5.2 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈræ.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to think in a logical and reasonable way; to use reasoning or logic to make decisions or understand something

Examples

To rationalize a decision
Before making a major decision, it's important to rationalize all your options.
Philosophers often rationalize complex problems to reach logical conclusions.
She tried to rationalize her behavior instead of acting purely on emotion.
If we rationalise this process, we can avoid unnecessary mistakes.
It is important to rationalise your options before making a major life decision.
She tried to rationalise her fears by looking for logical explanations.

  • synonym: reason - to use logical thinking to form conclusions or make decisions
