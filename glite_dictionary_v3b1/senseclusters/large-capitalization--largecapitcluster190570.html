large-capitalization

adjective
stocks of very large companies
3.9 5.6
Pronunciation: /lɑːrdʒ kæp.ɪ.təl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /lɑːdʒ kæp.ɪ.tə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(of stocks) relating to companies with a market capitalization of five billion dollars or more.

Examples

Large-capitalization stocks
Analysts expect large-capitalization firms to outperform smaller companies in the coming year.
Many investors prefer large-capitalization stocks for their stability and lower risk.
The index tracks the performance of large-capitalization companies listed on the exchange.
Investors often diversify by including both small and large-capitalisation companies in their portfolios.
Many institutional investors prefer large-capitalisation stocks due to their stability.
The company's strong profits helped it achieve large-capitalisation status on the stock exchange.
