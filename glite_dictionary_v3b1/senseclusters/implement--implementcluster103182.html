implement

verb
put into effect; carry out
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪˌmɛnt /; / ˈɪmplɪmɛnt / (BrE)
to put a plan, decision, law, or rule into effect; to carry out or fulfill

Examples

To implement a plan
Despite initial resistance, the government successfully implemented the proposed reforms.
It took several months to implement the new guidelines across all departments.
The company will implement a new training program next month.
After the committee's recommendations were accepted, they began to implement them immediately.
It took months of careful preparation before the government could implement the new policy.
The company plans to implement several changes to improve safety procedures this year.
School administrators must implement all safety rules without exception.
The company decided to implement a strict policy on data privacy.
The government will implement the new regulations starting next month.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to give up or stop doing something
  • synonym: apply - to use or bring into operation a rule, law, or policy
  • synonym: carry out - to perform or complete a task, plan, or order
  • synonym: enforce - to make sure a law or rule is obeyed or carried out
  • synonym: execute - to carry out or put into effect
