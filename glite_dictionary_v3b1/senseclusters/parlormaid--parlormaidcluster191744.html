parlormaid

noun
female servant for parlor and table duties
7.7 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːr.lər.meɪd/ (AmE), /ˈpɑː.lə.meɪd/ (BrE)
a female domestic servant whose duties include caring for the parlor, serving at table, and answering the door in a private home

Examples

The parlormaid serves
In Victorian novels, the parlormaid is often depicted wearing a crisp uniform and cap.
She began her career as a parlormaid before eventually becoming the housekeeper.
The wealthy family employed a parlormaid to greet guests and keep the front rooms immaculate.
In Edwardian England, a parlourmaid was responsible for setting the table and serving afternoon tea.
The duties of a parlourmaid included dusting the sitting room and answering the bell.
The wealthy family employed a parlourmaid to greet guests at the front door.
