identity policing

noun
the enforcement of identity norms
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdɛntəti pəˈlisɪŋ / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntəti pəˈliːsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of monitoring, regulating, or enforcing conformity to perceived norms or standards of identity, such as race, gender, sexuality, or culture

Examples

Identity policing of groups
Critics argue that identity policing can stifle individuality and discourage open discussion.
She experienced identity policing when people questioned the authenticity of her cultural background.
The online community often faces issues of identity policing when members challenge each other's self-definitions.
