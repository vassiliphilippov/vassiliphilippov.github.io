presentencing

noun
before sentencing phase
9.1
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈsen.tən.sɪŋ/ /; / /prə.ˈsen.tən.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈsen.tən.sɪŋ/ /; / /prə.ˈsen.tən.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
the period, procedures, or activities that occur before a court announces or imposes a sentence on a defendant

Examples

Presentencing phase
During the presentencing phase, the judge reviews reports and recommendations from probation officers.
The lawyer submitted new evidence in the presentencing process to argue for a lighter punishment.
Victim impact statements are often considered during presentencing hearings in criminal cases.
The judge reviewed the presentencing report before making a final decision on the case.
During the presentencing phase, the probation officer interviewed the defendant's family and employer.
Lawyers often submit evidence during presentencing to influence the court's decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pretrial - the period or activities before a trial begins
  • antonym: sentencing - the official decision by a court stating the punishment for a crime
