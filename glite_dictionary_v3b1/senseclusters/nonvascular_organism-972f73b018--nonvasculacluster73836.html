nonvascular organism

noun
organism lacking vascular tissue
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈvæs.kjə.lər ˈɔːr.gən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /nɑːn.ˈvæs.kjʊ.lər ˈɔːr.gən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈvæs.kjʊ.lə ˈɔː.gən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /nɒn.ˈvæs.kə.lə ˈɔː.gən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
an organism that lacks vascular tissue, such as algae, lichens, fungi, and mosses

Examples

Nonvascular organism like moss
Mosses are a classic example of a nonvascular organism because they lack true roots and stems.
Scientists studied the adaptability of nonvascular organisms in extreme environments such as Antarctica.
Unlike ferns, most algae are considered nonvascular organisms due to the absence of conducting tissues.
