slightness

noun
attractively thin quality
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈslaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈslaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being thin, small, delicately built, or lacking strength

Examples

Her slightness and grace
Her slightness gave her a certain elegance that was hard to ignore.
The model’s slightness made her ideal for certain designer fashions.
There was a graceful slightness to his figure that caught everyone’s attention.
Despite the slightness of the evidence, the police continued their investigation.
His slightness did not prevent him from excelling in sports.
The slightness of her frame made her appear very delicate in the cold wind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and healthy; sturdiness
  • synonym: slenderness - the quality of being gracefully thin
