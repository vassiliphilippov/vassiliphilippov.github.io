crista

noun
mitochondrial fold
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstə / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstə / (BrE)
a ridge or fold, especially one of the internal folds of the inner membrane of a mitochondrion in a cell

Examples

Mitochondrial crista
The crista increases the surface area inside the mitochondrion, allowing for more energy production.
Scientists observed that each crista plays a crucial role in cellular respiration.
Damage to the crista can affect the efficiency of the cell's energy output.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fold - a crease or line made by folding
  • synonym: ridge - a long, narrow raised part or edge
