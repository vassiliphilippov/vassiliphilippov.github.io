re-experience

verb
to feel again
Pronunciation: / ˌriː ɪkˈspɪriəns /; / ˌriːɪkˈspɪriəns / (AmE), / ˌriː ɪkˈspɪəriəns /; / ˌriːɪkˈspɪəriəns / (BrE)
to experience or feel something again, especially from the past

Examples

To re-experience old memories
After hearing the song, he began to re-experience memories from his childhood.
Victims of trauma may re-experience distressing events through flashbacks or dreams.
Traveling to her hometown made her re-experience the emotions she felt years ago.
After watching the movie, he wanted to re-experience the thrill of his childhood adventures.
Many people travel to re-experience the places where they made happy memories.
She hoped to re-experience the excitement of her first concert by attending the band's reunion show.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to be unable to remember something
  • synonym: relive - to experience something again in one's mind
