magnetize

verb
make magnetic; impart magnetism
4.7 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmæɡ.nə.taɪz/ (AmE), /ˈmæɡ.nə.taɪz/ (BrE)
to make (something) magnetic or to give magnetic properties to it

Examples

To magnetize metal
Leaving a screwdriver near a magnet can magnetize it over time.
Scientists need to magnetize the material before it can be used in electronic devices.
The technician used a coil to magnetize the metal rod for the experiment.
Repeatedly rubbing the needle with a magnet will eventually magnetise it.
Scientists can magnetise certain metals by exposing them to a strong magnetic field.
You can magnetise a screwdriver so it picks up small screws easily.
