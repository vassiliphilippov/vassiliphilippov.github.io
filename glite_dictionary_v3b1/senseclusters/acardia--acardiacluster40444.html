acardia

noun
congenital absence of a heart
Pronunciation: / əˈkɑr.di.ə / (AmE), / əˈkɑː.dɪ.ə / (BrE)
A rare congenital condition characterized by the absence of a heart, typically occurring in malformed embryos or twins.

Examples

Rare acardia condition
Acardia is considered extremely rare in prenatal medicine.
Researchers studied several cases of acardia in monozygotic twins.
The fetus was diagnosed with acardia, a condition incompatible with life.
