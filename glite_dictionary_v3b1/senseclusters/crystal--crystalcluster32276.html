crystal

noun
solid with a regular atomic structure
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl /; / ˈkrɪstl / (BrE)
a solid substance with atoms arranged in a highly ordered, repeating pattern, often forming geometric shapes

Examples

A diamond crystal
A diamond is a natural crystal composed entirely of carbon atoms.
Salt forms beautiful crystals when water evaporates from a solution.
Snowflakes are unique crystals of ice that develop in the atmosphere.
Each salt crystal is shaped like a tiny cube under a microscope.
Quartz is a common mineral that often appears as a clear crystal.
The snowflakes formed intricate crystal patterns on the window pane.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amorphous - a solid without a regular atomic structure
  • degree higher: gemstone - a precious or semi-precious stone, often crystalline
