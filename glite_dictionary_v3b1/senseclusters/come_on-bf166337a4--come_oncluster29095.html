come on

verb
begin to be available (of a service)
6.0
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɑn / (AmE), / kʌm ɒn / (BrE)
to start operating, become available, or begin functioning (such as lights, devices, or services)

Examples

The lights come on
After several hours of darkness, the came on and the house was lit again.
As soon as the main switch is flipped, the power will come on in all the rooms.
The heating usually comes on at 6 a.m. during the winter months.
As soon as the power was restored, all the lights came on.
The heater will come on automatically if the temperature drops below 60 degrees.
Wait a second—the computer should come on after you press the button.

Related Terms

  • antonym: go off - to stop operating or being available (especially of electricity, water, etc.)
  • synonym: switch on - to turn on a device by switching it
  • synonym: turn on - to cause a device to begin operating
