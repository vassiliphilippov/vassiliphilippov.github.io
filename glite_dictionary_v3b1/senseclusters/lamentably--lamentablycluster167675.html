In a way that deserves lament or regret; in an unfortunate or deplorable manner.

Examples

A lamentably poor result
Her response was lamentably inadequate to address the seriousness of the issue.
Many historical sites are lamentably neglected by the authorities.
The plan was lamentably executed, resulting in widespread confusion and frustration.

Synonyms

    7.9 0.12/m
    deplorably shows strong condemnation for something very bad or morally wrong, while lamentably means it deserves regret.
  • regrettably
    4.6 1.79/m
    regrettably expresses formal regret about an event or choice, while lamentably feels more literary and blames the situation.
  • unfortunately
    2.9 83/m
    unfortunately states that something happened by bad luck or is regrettable, while lamentably sounds more formal and sorrowful.
  • woefully
    6.5 2.13/m
    woefully describes a very bad or serious state that causes concern, while lamentably focuses on being deserving of regret.
  • sadly
    1.0 17/m

  • sadly
    1.0 6.18/m
    sadly expresses real sorrow or grief, while lamentably emphasizes that something deserves regret or is unfortunate in tone.
