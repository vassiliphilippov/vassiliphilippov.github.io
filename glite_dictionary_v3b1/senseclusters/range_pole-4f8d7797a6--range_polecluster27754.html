range pole

noun
surveying rod with red and white bands
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪndʒ ˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒ pəʊl / (BrE)
a straight rod, typically painted with alternating red and white bands, used by surveyors for sighting and aligning measurements.

Examples

A range pole for surveying
Before beginning the survey, he positioned the range pole at the property boundary.
Brightly colored range poles make it easier to maintain a straight line during construction surveys.
The surveyor used a range pole to mark the precise measurement point in the field.
