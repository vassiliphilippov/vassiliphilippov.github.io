undercolor

noun
base color layer
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.kʌl.ər/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.kʌ.lə/ (BrE)
a layer of color applied beneath another layer in printing or painting to affect the final appearance

Examples

Paint undercolor layer
The artist applied a red undercolor to create warmth beneath the final paint layers.
In printing, a light undercolor can help enhance the vibrancy of the top colors.
She chose a green undercolor to subtly influence the overall tone of her painting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: underpainting - an initial layer of paint applied to a canvas as a base for later layers
