hypostatize

verb
regard an abstract concept as real
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑstəˌtaɪz /; / haɪˈpɑstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒstətaɪz /; / haɪˈpɒstəsaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or regard an abstract concept as if it were a real, concrete entity; to attribute actual existence to a conceptual or non-physical thing

Examples

To hypostatize an idea
It is a mistake to hypostatize the notion of intelligence as a physical entity within the brain.
Some critics argue that social constructs are often hypostatized in modern theory.
The philosopher warned against attempts to hypostatize ideas such as 'justice' or 'freedom'.
Many philosophers warn that one should not hypostatise social constructs as if they were physical realities.
The theory was criticised for its tendency to hypostatise abstract concepts without empirical evidence.
We often hypostatise emotions, speaking of them as tangible objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reify - to make something abstract more concrete or real
