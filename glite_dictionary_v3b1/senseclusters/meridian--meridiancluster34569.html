meridian

adjective
at the highest or best stage
Pronunciation: / məˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / məˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
(literary) At the best or most developed stage; at the height or prime of something.

Examples

Meridian years
In the meridian of youth, life seems full of endless possibilities.
She reached the meridian of her career after years of dedication.
The poet wrote these verses in the meridian of his creative powers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - the process of becoming less strong, healthy, or successful
  • synonym: peak - the highest or best point of something
  • synonym: prime - the state of greatest strength or success
