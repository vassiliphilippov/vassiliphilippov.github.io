religious order

noun
group within a religion following specific rules
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A group within a religion, whose members typically follow specific rules and disciplines, often living communally and devoted to religious life or service.

Examples

A religious order community
Many religious orders dedicate themselves to education or helping the poor.
She decided to join a religious order after finishing school.
The Benedictines are a religious order known for their commitment to prayer and work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monastic order - a type of religious order with members living in monastic communities
