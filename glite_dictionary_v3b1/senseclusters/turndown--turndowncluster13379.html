turndown

noun
act of refusal or rejection
4.6 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ùnÀåda än / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀênÀåda än / (BrE)
an act of refusing or rejecting an offer or request

Examples

A turndown offer
He handled the turndown with grace and professionalism.
Her job application received a turndown by email after the interview.
The company sent a polite turndown to all unsolicited proposals.

Synonyms

  • refusal
    1.4 11/m
    refusal is a clear, everyday saying no to an offer or request, while turndown names that act in casual speech.
  • rejection
    3.0 16/m
    rejection is used for refusing people, ideas, or offers, often in formal contexts, while turndown refers to refusing an offer.
  • declination
    8.7 0.02/m
    declination is a polite, formal refusal often for invitations, while turndown is the more general act of rejecting an offer.
  • nonacceptance
    6.5 0.03/m
    nonacceptance is a formal, technical term for not accepting a proposal, while turndown is the common act of refusal.
  • rebuff
    7.7 0.34/m
    rebuff is a sharp or abrupt refusal that can feel rude, while turndown is a neutral act of rejecting an offer.
