Anthriscus cereifolium

noun
aromatic herb used in cooking (chervil)
Pronunciation: / ænˈθrɪs.kəs ˌsɛr.iˈfəʊ.li.əm / (AmE), / ænˈθrɪs.kəs ˌsɛr.ɪˈfəʊ.li.əm / (BrE)
An aromatic annual herb of the parsley family, native to Europe, cultivated for its finely divided leaves used in cooking, especially in soups and salads.

Examples

Fresh Anthriscus cereifolium leaves
Fresh Anthriscus cereifolium adds a delicate flavor to salads and egg dishes.
Gardeners often grow Anthriscus cereifolium alongside parsley and tarragon for a ready supply of fine herbs.
The finely cut leaves of Anthriscus cereifolium are popular in French cuisine.

  • synonym: chervil - another name for Anthriscus cereifolium, a culinary herb
