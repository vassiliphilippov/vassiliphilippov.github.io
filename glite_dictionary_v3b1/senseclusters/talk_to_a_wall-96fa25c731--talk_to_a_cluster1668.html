talk to a wall

verb
communicate with someone unresponsive
1.0
Pronunciation: / tɔk tə ə wɔl / (AmE), / tɔːk tə ə wɔːl / (BrE)
To attempt to communicate with someone who does not respond or pay attention, as if speaking to an inanimate object.

Examples

To talk to a wall
Every time I suggest a change, it's as if I'm talking to a wall.
Explaining the new policy to the staff felt like talking to a wall.
Trying to negotiate with him was like talking to a wall: nothing got through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall on deaf ears - to be ignored or not listened to
  • antonym: get through to - to succeed in making someone understand or listen
