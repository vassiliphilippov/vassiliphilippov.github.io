aminoalkanoic acid

noun
an organic compound with amino and carboxylic acid groups
5.3
Pronunciation: /ə.miː.noʊ.æl.kə.ˈnoʊ.ɪk ˈæs.əd/ /; / /ə.miː.noʊ.æl.kə.ˈnoʊ.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd/ (AmE), /ə.miː.nəʊ.æl.kə.ˈnəʊ.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ə.miː.nəʊ.æl.kə.ˈnəʊ.ɪk ˈæs.əd/ (BrE)
an organic compound containing both an amino group (–NH₂) and a carboxylic acid group (–COOH), forming a principal type of amino acid

Examples

An aminoalkanoic acid molecule
Glycine, the simplest aminoalkanoic acid, is found in most proteins.
Scientists synthesized a new aminoalkanoic acid to study its potential for drug development.
The structure of an aminoalkanoic acid includes both an amino and a carboxylic acid group.
