public debt

noun
total money owed by government(s)
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpʌb.lɪk det/ (AmE), /ˈpʌb.lɪk det/ (BrE)
The total amount of money owed by a government, including the debts of national, state, and local authorities, typically as a result of borrowing to cover expenditures not supported by taxation.

Examples

The public debt total
Many economists debate the long-term impacts of excessive public debt on future generations.
Rising public debt can lead to higher interest rates and slower economic growth.
The country struggled to reduce its public debt after years of deficit spending.

Related Terms
