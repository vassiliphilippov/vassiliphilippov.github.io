bronchiolitis

noun
inflammation of the bronchioles
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɑːnki.oʊˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrɒŋkɪ.əˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the membranes lining the bronchioles, usually caused by infection.

Examples

Bronchiolitis infection
During the winter months, cases of bronchiolitis increase due to viral infections like RSV.
Hospitalization may be required if bronchiolitis leads to severe respiratory distress in young children.
The pediatrician diagnosed the infant with bronchiolitis after noting wheezing and difficulty breathing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bronchitis - inflammation of the mucous membrane in the bronchial tubes
