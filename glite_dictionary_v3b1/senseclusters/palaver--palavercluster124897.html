palaver

noun
empty or pointless talk
Pronunciation: / pəˈlævər / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
elaborate, insincere, or empty talk, often intended to impress or persuade

Examples

Political palaver
All we got from the spokesperson was a lot of palaver and no real answers.
Instead of action, the debate consisted mainly of political palaver.
The meeting was full of palaver that didn't achieve anything productive.
His endless palaver grew tiring, as everyone could see through his insincere compliments.
I'm not fooled by all your palaver; just tell me the truth.
The salesman tried to win her trust with charming palaver about how wonderful the product was.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - foolish or meaningless talk
  • synonym: cajolery - persuasion by flattery or gentle urging
  • synonym: flattery - excessive praise intended to influence someone
  • synonym: rhetoric - language intended to persuade or impress, often regarded as lacking in sincerity or meaningful content
  • antonym: sense - sound practical judgment; logical meaning
  • antonym: sincerity - honesty and genuineness in expression
