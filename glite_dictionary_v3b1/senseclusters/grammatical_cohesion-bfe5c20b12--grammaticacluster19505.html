grammatical cohesion

noun
cohesion based on grammatical structure
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˌmætɪkəl koʊˈhiʒən / (AmE), / ɡrəˌmætɪkəl kəʊˈhiːʒən / (BrE)
The aspect of cohesion in a written or spoken text that results from the use of grammatical structures, such as reference, substitution, or ellipsis.

Examples

Grammatical cohesion in text
Academic writing often relies on grammatical cohesion to maintain clarity and logical flow between sentences.
The teacher explained that grammatical cohesion helps readers connect ideas beyond word choice.
Using pronouns and conjunctions are common ways to create grammatical cohesion in English texts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lexical cohesion - cohesion achieved by the selection of vocabulary
