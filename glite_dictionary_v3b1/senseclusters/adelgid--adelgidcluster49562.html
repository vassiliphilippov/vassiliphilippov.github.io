adelgid

noun
insect that feeds on conifers
Pronunciation: / əˈdɛl.dʒɪd / (AmE), / əˈdɛl.dʒɪd / (BrE)
a small insect that feeds on conifers and often causes the formation of galls on trees such as pines, spruces, and firs

Examples

The adelgid insect
Gardeners are concerned about the spread of adelgids on ornamental pines.
Scientists discovered a new species of adelgid infesting spruce trees in the region.
The hemlock adelgid has caused significant damage to forests in North America.
