noun

A mechanical diaphragm consisting of thin, overlapping plates that can be adjusted to change the size of a central opening, as used in devices like cameras and microscopes to regulate light.

Examples

Camera iris control
A small iris within the microscope allowed for precise light regulation.
An automatic iris helps modern cameras adapt to changing lighting conditions.
The photographer adjusted the iris of the lens to control the exposure.
