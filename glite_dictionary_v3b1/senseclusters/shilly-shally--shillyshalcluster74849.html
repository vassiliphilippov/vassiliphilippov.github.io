shilly-shally

verb
hesitate or be indecisive
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪli ˌʃæli / (AmE), / ˈʃɪli ˌʃæli / (BrE)
to show hesitation or indecision; to be uncertain and delay making a decision

Examples

To shilly-shally over choice
He shilly-shallied for days before finally reaching a decision.
She tends to shilly-shally whenever it's time to make important choices.
There's no time to shilly-shally—we need an answer now.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - to make a decision, to choose firmly or conclusively
  • synonym: dither - to be indecisive or hesitate
