master

noun
officer in command of a merchant ship
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstər /; / ˈmæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə /; / ˈmɑː.stə / (BrE)
a person who has authority or control over others, or is in charge of a group, place, or activity

Examples

Master of the house
After a thorough inspection, the master decided it was safe to set sail.
He has worked his way up from seaman to master of the vessel.
The master is responsible for the safety of the crew and cargo.
After years of service, she was appointed master of the respected preparatory school.
During the tour, the master explained the school's long-standing traditions to the visitors.
The master of the college addressed the students during the annual ceremony.
He was the undisputed master of the household, making all of the important decisions.
The sailors obeyed their master without hesitation.
Under the old system, each apprentice answered directly to the master.

  • synonym: headmaster - principal or head teacher, especially in a private school

  • synonym: ruler - a person who governs or exercises authority over an area or people
  • antonym: servant - one who is employed to perform duties for others, especially in a household
  • antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority of another
  • synonym: supervisor - a person who oversees others' work
