pasteurize

verb
heat to kill microorganisms (esp. food/drink)
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpæs.tʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.tjə.raɪz /; / ˈpæs.tjə.raɪz / (BrE)
to heat food or drink to a specific temperature for a set period in order to kill harmful microorganisms, extend shelf life, and avoid significantly changing the taste or quality

Examples

To pasteurize milk
Most milk sold in stores has been pasteurized to ensure it is safe to drink.
Some artisanal cheeses are made from unpasteurized milk.
The company pasteurizes all its juices to extend their shelf life.
Before bottling, the cider is pasteurised to destroy any unwanted bacteria.
Fruit juices are often pasteurised to increase their shelf life without adding preservatives.
The dairy now pasteurises all its milk to ensure it is safe to drink.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: sanitize - make clean and hygienic by removing bacteria
  • degree higher: sterilize - make free from all living microorganisms
