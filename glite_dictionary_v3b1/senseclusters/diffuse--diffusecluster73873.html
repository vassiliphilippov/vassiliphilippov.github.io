diffuse

adjective
spread out; not concentrated
5.3 5.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfjuːs/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfjuːz/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfjuːs/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfjuːz/ (BrE)
spread out over a large area and not concentrated in one place (such as light, a network, or a group)

Examples

Diffuse light
A diffuse network of volunteers coordinated aid across the country.
Her explanation was too diffuse to follow easily.
The light from the window was diffuse, creating a soft glow in the room.
Clouds act as a natural filter, creating diffuse sunlight on overcast days.
Photographers often prefer diffuse lighting to avoid harsh shadows in portraits.
The studio was flooded with diffuse light from the large skylights.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrated - focused and gathered in a small area
  • antonym: direct - (of light) coming from a single, focused source, not scattered
  • synonym: scattered - spread or dispersed over a wide area
