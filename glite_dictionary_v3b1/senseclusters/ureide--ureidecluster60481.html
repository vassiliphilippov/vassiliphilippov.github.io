ureide

noun
chemical compound derived from urea
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊr.i.aɪd / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.ri.aɪd / (BrE)
any of a class of chemical compounds derived from urea by replacement of hydrogen atoms with acyl groups.

Examples

Ureide compounds
Certain plants accumulate ureide compounds as part of their nitrogen metabolism.
Researchers studied the role of ureides in the transport and storage of nitrogen in legumes.
The synthesis of a ureide can be achieved by acylating urea with various acids.

Related Terms

  • synonym: urea - a compound serving as the main nitrogenous breakdown product of protein metabolism
