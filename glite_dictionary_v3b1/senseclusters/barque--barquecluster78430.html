barque

noun
sailing ship with three or more masts
Pronunciation: / bɑrk / (AmE), / bɑːk / (BrE)
a sailing ship with three or more masts, with the rear mast rigged fore-and-aft and the others square-rigged

Examples

A barque ship
In the harbor, a restored barque attracts visitors interested in maritime history.
Many 19th-century explorers traveled the world in sturdy barques.
The old barque sailed gracefully across the Atlantic under full sail.
