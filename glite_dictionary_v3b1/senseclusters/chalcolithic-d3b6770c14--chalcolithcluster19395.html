Chalcolithic

noun
stone and copper age
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkælkəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌkælkəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
the prehistoric period when both stone and copper tools were used, before the Bronze Age

Examples

Artifacts from the Chalcolithic
Archaeologists discovered pottery and jewelry from the Chalcolithic in the ancient settlement.
The Chalcolithic marks a transition between the Stone Age and the widespread use of metals.
Many early farming communities developed during the Chalcolithic period.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Bronze Age - the period after the Chalcolithic, when bronze tools were used
  • degree lower: Neolithic - the later part of the Stone Age, before metal tools
