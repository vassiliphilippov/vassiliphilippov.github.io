squatter

noun
person settling lawfully on government land
7.9 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtə / (BrE)
a person who settles lawfully on government land with the intention of acquiring legal ownership of it, often by meeting certain requirements

Examples

Land squatter
In some countries, laws allowed squatters to claim land if they improved and maintained it for a number of years.
Several squatters established farms on the frontier, hoping to eventually gain legal ownership of the land.
The government encouraged squatters to move west in the 19th century by offering land titles after a period of residence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homesteader - someone who acquires ownership of land by living on and cultivating it
