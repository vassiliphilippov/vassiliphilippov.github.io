rancid

adjective
having an unpleasant stale smell or taste (like old fats)
3.9 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈræn.sɪd/ (AmE), /ˈræn.sɪd/ (BrE)
having an unpleasant, stale, or sour smell or taste, especially because food, fat, or oil has gone bad

Examples

Rancid oil
He threw out the rancid oil after noticing its sharp and unpleasant odor.
I can't eat this butter; it's gone rancid and tastes sour.
The rancid smell coming from the trash can made it hard to enter the kitchen.
He opened the container and was met with a rancid smell.
The rancid odor from the butter made it clear it had gone bad.
Throw away any oil that has become rancid, as it can ruin the flavor of your food.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - recently made or obtained; not stale or spoiled
