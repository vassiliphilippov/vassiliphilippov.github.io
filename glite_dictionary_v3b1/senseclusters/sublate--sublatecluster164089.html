sublate

verb
to elevate or exalt
Pronunciation: / sʌˈbleɪt / (AmE), / sʌˈbleɪt / (BrE)
to raise up, elevate, or exalt someone or something (archaic)

Examples

To sublate a person
In ancient texts, rulers would sublate their loyal subjects to positions of honor.
The ceremony was designed to sublate the hero above all others in the kingdom.
He hoped his achievements would sublate his family's name for generations to come.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elevate - to raise to a higher position or rank
  • synonym: exalt - to raise in rank, honor, or power
  • antonym: lower - to move or cause to move to a less high position
