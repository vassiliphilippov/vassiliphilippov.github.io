usufructuary

noun
person with right to use another's property
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåjuÀêz…ôÀàfr åkt ÉuÀêÀå…õri / (AmE), / ÀåjuÀêzjuÀêÀàfr åktj ä…ôri / (BrE)
A person who has the right to use and enjoy the property of another, without owning it, under the right of usufruct.

Examples

Usufructuary of the house
As the usufructuary, she could live in the house and collect its profits, but she could not sell it.
During his lifetime, he remained the usufructuary of the estate, while his children held the title.
The law grants certain rights to a usufructuary, distinguishing them from full ownership.

  • synonym: lessee - person who leases property; tenant
