caseless

adjective
without grammatical case
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsləs / (BrE)
not having grammatical case or not distinguished by uppercase and lowercase letters

Examples

Caseless nouns
In some languages, nouns are caseless and do not change form for subject or object.
The script is caseless, so there is no difference between uppercase and lowercase letters.
Unlike English, which uses cases for pronouns, this language is almost entirely caseless.

Related Terms

  • antonym: case-marked - having grammatical case distinctions
  • synonym: uncased - not distinguished by uppercase or lowercase letters
