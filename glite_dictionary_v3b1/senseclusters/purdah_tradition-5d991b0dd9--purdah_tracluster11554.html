purdah tradition

noun
customs of female seclusion
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜːrdɑː trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdə trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the social customs and practices involving the seclusion of women from public observation, especially prevalent in some Muslim and Hindu communities

Examples

The purdah tradition practice
Many families in the region still follow the purdah tradition despite changing times.
She wrote a memoir about growing up under the strict purdah tradition.
The purdah tradition requires women to avoid being seen by men who are not close relatives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: purdah - practice of female seclusion behind a curtain or veil
