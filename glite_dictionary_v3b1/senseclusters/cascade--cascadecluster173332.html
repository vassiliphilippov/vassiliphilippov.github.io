cascade

noun
series of sequential stages or processes
3.5 4.3
Pronunciation: /kæ.ˈskeɪd/ (AmE), /kæ.ˈskeɪd/ (BrE)
a process in which something passes through a series of stages, events, or operations, each triggering the next

Examples

Cascade of events
A cascade of biochemical reactions occurs inside the cell after a signal is received.
The failure of one system can trigger a cascade of failures throughout the network.
The project went through a cascade of approvals before final implementation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chain - a connected series of events or things
  • synonym: sequence - a series of related things that follow each other in order
