unbundle

verb
to separate packaged items
4.6
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈbʌn.dəl/ /; / /ʌn.ˈbʌn.dəl/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈbʌn.dəl/ /; / /ən.ˈbʌn.dəl/ (BrE)
to separate products, services, or items that were previously combined or sold together, so they can be offered or sold individually

Examples

To unbundle services
The company decided to unbundle its streaming service from the cable package.
Some airlines now unbundle checked baggage fees from the ticket price.
Regulators required the telecom provider to unbundle its internet and phone services.
The company decided to unbundle its software suite so customers could buy only the features they needed.
Regulators required the utility provider to unbundle its services to encourage competition.
After unbundling the package, each channel was offered as a separate subscription.
The software company decided to unbundle its products to offer more choices to customers.
Many airlines now unbundle services like checked baggage and meals from the ticket price.
After the merger, the new management chose to unbundle several business units.
