second-guess

verb
evaluate or criticize with hindsight
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛk.əndˈɡɛs /; / ˌsɛkəndˈɡɛs / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.əndˈɡɛs /; / ˌsɛkəndˈɡɛs / (BrE)
to criticize, question, or doubt a decision or action after it has been made, especially with the benefit of hindsight

Examples

To second-guess a decision
It is easy to second-guess the decision once you know the outcome.
She tried not to second-guess herself during the presentation.
The media loves to second-guess the coach after a loss.
After hearing new evidence, the jury began to second-guess their earlier verdict.
It's impossible to second-guess how the market will react to the news.
She tends to second-guess every decision she makes at work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something; synonym in evaluative sense
  • synonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something; antonym in evaluative sense
  • antonym: trust - to have confidence in someone or something
