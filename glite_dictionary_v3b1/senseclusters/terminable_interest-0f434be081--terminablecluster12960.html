terminable interest

noun
property interest ending under certain conditions
7.0 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.mɪ.nə.bəl ˈɪn.trəst / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɪ.nə.bəl ˈɪn.trəst / (BrE)
A legal interest in property that ends or expires when certain conditions are met, such as at a specified time or upon the occurrence of a particular event.

Examples

A terminable interest in property
A trust may grant a terminable interest to a spouse, which ends upon remarriage or death.
His lawyer explained that a terminable interest means he won't have rights to the property forever.
Under the will, she received only a terminable interest in the estate, not outright ownership.
