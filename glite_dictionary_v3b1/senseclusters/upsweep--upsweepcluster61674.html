upsweep

verb
to increase pitch
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌswiːp / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌswiːp / (BrE)
to cause the frequency or pitch of a sound or signal to rise over time

Examples

To upsweep the siren
The siren began to upsweep as the emergency vehicle approached the intersection.
Audio engineers often upsweep test tones to check speaker performance across frequencies.
As the music played, the synthesizer would upsweep to create a dramatic effect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall - to move downward or decrease in level
  • synonym: rise - to move upward or increase in level
