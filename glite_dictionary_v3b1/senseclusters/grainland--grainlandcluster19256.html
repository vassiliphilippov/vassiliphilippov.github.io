grainland

noun
land for grain crops
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪnlænd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪnlænd / (BrE)
land that is used for growing grain crops such as wheat, corn, or barley

Examples

The grainland area
The vast grainland of the Midwest produces millions of tons of wheat each year.
Farmers rotate crops on their grainland to maintain soil health and productivity.
Much of the region's grainland is planted with wheat each spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farmland - land used for farming or agriculture
  • antonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals
