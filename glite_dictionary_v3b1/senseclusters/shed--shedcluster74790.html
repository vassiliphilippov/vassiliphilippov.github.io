shed

verb
remove or get rid of something
3.3
Pronunciation: / ʃɛd / (AmE), / ʃɛd / (BrE)
to remove or get rid of something, especially something unwanted or no longer needed

Examples

To shed old habits
After years of working in finance, he decided to shed his corporate identity and travel the world.
She quickly shed her coat as she stepped into the warm room.
You should shed your fear of making mistakes if you want to succeed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discard - to throw away or get rid of something as no longer useful
  • antonym: retain - to continue to have or keep something
