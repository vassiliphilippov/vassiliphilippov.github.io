St John's Eve

noun
evening before Midsummer Day
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈdʒɑnz iv / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒɒnz iːv / (BrE)
the evening before Midsummer Day, traditionally celebrated on June 23, associated with various customs and festivals in Christian and European folklore

Examples

St John's Eve celebrations
In many countries, St John's Eve marks the beginning of summer festivities.
Shakespeare references magical happenings on St John's Eve in his plays.
Villagers gathered to celebrate St John's Eve with bonfires and songs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Midsummer Day - the day after St John's Eve, celebrated on June 24
