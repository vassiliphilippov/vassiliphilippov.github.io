absorption

noun
uptake of nutrients after digestion
3.9 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàz…îrp É…ôn / (AmE), / …ôbÀàz…îÀêp É…ôn / (BrE)
the process by which the body takes up nutrients from digested food into the bloodstream or tissues

Examples

Nutrient absorption
After digestion, the nutrients undergo absorption in the small intestine.
Poor absorption in the intestines can lead to nutritional deficiencies.
Vitamin C enhances the absorption of iron from plant-based foods.

Synonyms

  • assimilation
    5.8 0.27/m
    assimilation is the body's process of turning digested food into usable nutrients, while absorption is the physical uptake into blood or tissues.
  • uptake
    5.1 1.44/m
    uptake refers to taking in substances in many contexts, while absorption specifically means the body's transfer of digested nutrients into blood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilation - the process of incorporating absorbed nutrients into body tissues
