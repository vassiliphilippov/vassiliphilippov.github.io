absorption

noun
uptake of nutrients after digestion
1.4
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which the body takes up nutrients from digested food into the bloodstream or tissues

Examples

Nutrient absorption
After digestion, the nutrients undergo absorption in the small intestine.
Poor absorption in the intestines can lead to nutritional deficiencies.
Vitamin C enhances the absorption of iron from plant-based foods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilation - the process of incorporating absorbed nutrients into body tissues
