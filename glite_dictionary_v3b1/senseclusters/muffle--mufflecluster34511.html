muffle

verb
suppress or conceal an emotion or reaction
6.1 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌf.əl/ /; / /mʌfl/ (AmE), /ˈmʌf.əl/ /; / /mʌfl/ (BrE)
to make a sound, emotion, or reaction quieter, less clear, or less noticeable, often by covering or suppressing it

Examples

To muffle a sound
He muffled a sigh when he heard the disappointing news.
She tried to muffle her laughter during the serious meeting.
The child managed to muffle his excitement so he wouldn't wake his parents.
He spoke through a scarf that muffled his voice.
Heavy curtains help muffle the noise from the street outside.
She tried to muffle her laughter by covering her mouth with her hand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplify - to increase the strength or volume of sound
  • synonym: dampen - to make a sound less loud or less intense
  • synonym: repress - to restrain or prevent a feeling or action
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to forcibly; subdue
