sinewy

adjective
tough and muscular; like sinews
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnju.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjuː.i / (BrE)
having strong muscles and little fat; lean and muscular, with a tough appearance

Examples

Sinewy arms
She admired the sinewy grace with which the dancer moved across the stage.
The sinewy arms of the athlete rippled as he lifted the weights.
Thick and sinewy roots pushed up through the hard-packed earth.
His sinewy frame revealed years of dedicated physical labor on the farm.
She admired the sinewy arms of the rock climber as he reached for the next hold.
The sinewy runner moved swiftly across the rugged terrain, barely breaking a sweat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flabby - soft and lacking firmness or muscle
  • synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles
  • antonym: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
