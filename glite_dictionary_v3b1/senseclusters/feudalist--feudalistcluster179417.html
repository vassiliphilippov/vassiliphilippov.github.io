feudalist

adjective
of feudalism
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːdəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdəlɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of feudalism, especially a hierarchical or decentralized system based on loyalty and patronage.

Examples

Feudalist society
The country maintained a feudalist structure long after most of Europe had modernized.
Some critics argue that the organization operates in a feudalist manner, with power concentrated at the top.
The novel describes a feudalist society where loyalty to local leaders is more important than national laws.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarian - believing in or based on the principle that all people are equal
  • synonym: feudal - relating to the social system of feudalism
