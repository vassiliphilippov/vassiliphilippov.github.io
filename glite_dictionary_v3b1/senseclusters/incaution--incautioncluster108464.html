incaution

noun
lack of caution; carelessness
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɔʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
The trait of being careless or not taking enough care to avoid danger or risk.

Examples

His incaution crossing street
His incaution while crossing the busy street nearly led to an accident.
She regretted her incaution in revealing sensitive information to a stranger.
The company suffered losses due to the incaution of its financial team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: caution - careful attention to avoid danger or risks
