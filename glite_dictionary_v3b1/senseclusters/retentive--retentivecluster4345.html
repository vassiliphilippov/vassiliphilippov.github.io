retentive

adjective
able to retain or remember easily
4.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɛntɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛntɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or tendency to hold or keep something, especially water or moisture

Examples

Retentive soil
A retentive soil keeps moisture longer and benefits certain crops.
She has a remarkably retentive memory for names and dates.
The sponge proved very retentive and held much more water than expected.
A retentive sponge can hold large amounts of liquid without dripping.
Farmers prefer retentive soils for crops that require consistent moisture.
The clay in the garden soil is highly retentive of water, making it prone to puddling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquids easily
  • antonym: forgetful - unable to remember things; tending not to retain information
  • antonym: permeable - allowing liquids or gases to pass through
