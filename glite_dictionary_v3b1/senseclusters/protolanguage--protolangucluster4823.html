protolanguage

noun
early ancestral language
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊtoʊˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəʊˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a hypothetical or reconstructed early form of a language from which later languages are believed to have developed

Examples

Indo-European protolanguage
Linguists reconstruct the protolanguage to trace the history of language families.
The Indo-European protolanguage is the common ancestor of many modern European and Asian languages.
Studying a protolanguage helps researchers understand how languages evolve over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancestor language - a language from which others have descended
  • antonym: descendant language - a language that has developed from an earlier language
