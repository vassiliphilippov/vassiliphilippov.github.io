charge

verb
formally accuse of wrongdoing
4.2 36/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
to formally accuse someone of a crime or wrongdoing

Examples

To charge someone with a crime
The environmental group charged the company with polluting the river.
The senator was charged with corruption after the investigation.
They charged her with neglecting her responsibilities at work.
His critics charged him with responsibility for the failed project.
Many people unfairly charge others for mistakes beyond their control.
The investigation charged the manager with negligence.
During the meeting, she charged her opponent with breaking the rules.
The editor charged that the article was plagiarized.
The lawyer charged that the company had covered up the evidence.

Synonyms

  • indict
    8.0 9.27/m
    indict is a formal legal accusation by authorities, often for serious crimes, while charge is a general formal accusation.
  • accuse
    2.7 59/m
    accuse is to say someone did wrong, often informally or publicly, while charge is an official legal accusation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolve - to declare someone free from blame or responsibility
  • synonym: accuse - to claim someone has done something wrong
  • antonym: acquit - to formally declare not guilty of a criminal charge
  • synonym: blame - to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong
  • antonym: defend - to support or justify someone against an accusation
