common hyacinth

noun
a fragrant cultivated flowering plant
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən haɪˈæsɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən hʌɪˈasɪnθ / (BrE)
a bulbous plant (Hyacinthus orientalis) widely grown for its fragrant, white, pink, blue, or purple flowers

Examples

The common hyacinth flower
Many florists recommend the common hyacinth for brightening up window boxes.
She planted pink and blue common hyacinths along the walkway for vibrant color.
The common hyacinth blooms early in spring, filling gardens with a sweet scent.
