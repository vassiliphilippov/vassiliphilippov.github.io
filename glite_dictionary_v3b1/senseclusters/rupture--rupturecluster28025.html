rupture

verb
break or burst apart suddenly
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌptʃər / (AmE), / ˈrʌptʃə / (BrE)
to break or cause to break suddenly and violently; to separate or burst apart abruptly

Examples

To rupture a membrane
He feared that the stress would rupture the thin membrane.
If you drop the glass bottle, it may rupture on impact.
The intense pressure caused the pipe to rupture and leak water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: snap - to break suddenly with a sharp sound
  • synonym: tear - to pull apart or destroy by force
