markedness

noun
distinctive quality
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being distinct or different from a more neutral or common form, especially in linguistics

Examples

Linguistic markedness concept
In linguistics, markedness helps explain why some word forms are more basic than others.
The concept of markedness is important when analyzing grammatical structures in different languages.
Researchers discussed how markedness affects the way people learn new vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marked - having a feature that makes it distinct from the neutral form
  • antonym: unmarked - having the more neutral or common form
