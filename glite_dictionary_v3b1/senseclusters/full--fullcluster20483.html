full

adjective
having a deep and rich sound
2.3
Pronunciation: / fʊl / (AmE), / fʊl / (BrE)
(of a sound) having deep, rich, and resonant qualities; marked by depth and body.

Examples

A full voice
His speech had a full and commanding sound that made people sit up and listen.
The full tone of the cello contrasted beautifully with the bright sound of the violin.
The singer's full voice resonated throughout the concert hall, captivating the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thin - lacking depth, resonance, or body (of sound)
