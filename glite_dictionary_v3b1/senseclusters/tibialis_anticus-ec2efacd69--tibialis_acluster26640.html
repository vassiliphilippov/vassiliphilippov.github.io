tibialis anticus

noun
muscle in the front of the lower leg
8.2 0.00/m

a muscle in the front part of the lower leg that runs from the tibia to the first metatarsal and cuneiform bones, responsible for dorsiflexing and inverting the foot.

Examples

tibialis anticus muscle
After his injury, the doctor checked the strength of his tibialis anticus.
The tibialis anticus helps lift the foot during walking.
Weakness of the tibialis anticus can cause difficulty in dorsiflexing the foot.
