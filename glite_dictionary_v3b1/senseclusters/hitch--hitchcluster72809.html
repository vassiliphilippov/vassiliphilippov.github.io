hitch

noun
a temporary, easily untied knot
Pronunciation: / hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a knot that can be quickly tied and untied, usually by pulling against the standing part; a temporary knot

Examples

A hitch knot
After undoing the hitch, the tent collapsed within seconds.
The sailor tied a quick hitch to secure the line temporarily.
With a simple hitch, the climber fixed the rope to the anchor point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knot - a fastening made by tying a piece of rope, string, or similar
