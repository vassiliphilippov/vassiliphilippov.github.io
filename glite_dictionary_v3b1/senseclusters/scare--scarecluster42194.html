scare

verb
cause fear in someone
Pronunciation: / skɛr / (AmE), / skɛə / (BrE)
to make someone feel afraid or lose confidence

Examples

To scare a child
Don't scare your little brother with ghost stories before bed.
She scares easily, so be gentle when you approach her.
The loud noise scared the children during the thunderstorm.
The difficult exam might scare a few students off, but most are determined to try.
The threat of rejection can scare some people away from applying.
They tried to scare the opposition into silence with harsh criticism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daunt - to make someone feel intimidated or discouraged
  • antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone
  • synonym: frighten - to make someone feel afraid
  • synonym: intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone, often to make them do what one wants
  • degree lower: startle - to surprise and frighten someone suddenly
  • degree higher: terrify - to cause intense fear
