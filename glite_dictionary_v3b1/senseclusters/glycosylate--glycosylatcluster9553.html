glycosylate

verb
to add a sugar molecule
Pronunciation: / ɡlaɪˈkəʊsɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈkəʊsɪleɪt / (BrE)
to attach a sugar molecule to another molecule, especially in a biological or chemical process

Examples

To glycosylate proteins
Researchers glycosylate proteins to study how sugars affect their function in the body.
The enzyme can glycosylate various molecules during the metabolic process.
Scientists observed that the drug was less effective when the compound was not glycosylated.

  • synonym: phosphorylate - to add a phosphate group to a molecule
