overdefine

verb
to define multiple times
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to give more than one definition to a name, variable, or function, often causing a conflict or error, especially in computing

Examples

To overdefine a variable
If you overdefine a variable in your code, the program may not compile correctly.
The error message appeared because the user tried to overdefine the same function twice.
It is important not to overdefine symbols in a shared library to avoid conflicts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: define - to state or describe the meaning of something
  • synonym: redefine - to define again or differently
