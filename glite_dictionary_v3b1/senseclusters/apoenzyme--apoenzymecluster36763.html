apoenzyme

noun
protein component of an enzyme
5.1 9.1
Pronunciation: /æp.oʊ.ˈen.zaɪm/ /; / /ə.poʊ.ˈen.zaɪm/ (AmE), /æp.əʊ.ˈen.zaɪm/ /; / /ə.pəʊ.ˈen.zaɪm/ (BrE)
The protein part of an enzyme, which requires combination with a coenzyme or cofactor to become catalytically active.

Examples

The apoenzyme protein
A deficiency in the coenzyme prevents the apoenzyme from catalyzing the reaction.
Researchers isolated the apoenzyme to study its structure separately from the cofactor.
The apoenzyme becomes active only when it binds to its specific coenzyme.
