extramission

noun
theory of vision rays
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstrəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a historical theory that vision is produced by rays emitted from the eyes toward objects

Examples

Extramission theory of vision
The philosopher supported extramission as the explanation for how humans see the world.
In medieval times, extramission was widely accepted before scientific advances disproved it.
Debates about extramission and intromission shaped early theories of optics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intromission - the theory that vision occurs when objects send rays into the eyes
