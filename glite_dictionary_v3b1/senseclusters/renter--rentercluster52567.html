renter

noun
person who pays to use property
1.8 2.20/m

a person who pays to use land, a building, a vehicle, or other property that belongs to someone else

Examples

A renter of apartments
Every renter in this building must follow the same set of rules.
If a renter damages the car, they are responsible for the repair costs.
The renter signed a one-year lease for the apartment.

Synonyms

  • tenant
    3.0 12/m
    tenant signs a formal rental agreement and occupies a home or flat, while renter is the common everyday word for that person.
  • lessee
    7.0 0.30/m
    lessee holds legal lease rights to a property for a set time, while renter is the general person who pays to use property.
  • leaseholder
    6.3 0.14/m
    leaseholder has long-term right under a lease and often appears in legal papers, while renter is the everyday person who pays to use property.
  • leaser
    5.3 0.01/m
    leaser is a less common term for someone who takes a lease, while renter is the usual everyday word for that person.
