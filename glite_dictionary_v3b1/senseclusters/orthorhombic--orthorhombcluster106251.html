orthorhombic

adjective
crystal system shape
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθəˈrɑmbɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈrɒmbɪk / (BrE)
having three unequal axes at right angles to each other, used to describe a type of crystal system

Examples

Orthorhombic crystal system
The mineral forms orthorhombic crystals under certain conditions.
Scientists identified the orthorhombic structure by analyzing the angles between the crystal axes.
Many common minerals, such as sulfur, can crystallize in the orthorhombic system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monoclinic - a crystal system with three unequal axes, one inclined
  • antonym: tetragonal - a crystal system with two equal axes and one different, all at right angles
