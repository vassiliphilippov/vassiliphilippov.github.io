scarceness

noun
the state of being scarce
7.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsk…õr.s…ôn…ôs / (AmE), / Ààsk…õÀê.s…ôn…™s / (BrE)
the state of being in short supply; a small or inadequate quantity

Examples

The scarceness of evidence
Due to the scarceness of evidence, the case was difficult to solve.
The scarceness of clean water is a critical problem in many regions.
They were worried by the scarceness of medical supplies after the storm.

Synonyms

    4.4 4.99/m
    scarcity is the common neutral term for low supply in many fields, while scarceness is a rarer alternative with same basic meaning.
  • shortage
    2.7 21/m
    shortage describes a situation or period with not enough of something needed, while scarceness names the general state of low supply.
  • paucity
    8.6 1.57/m
    paucity feels formal and focuses on a very small amount or number, while scarceness means a more general lack of supply.
  • dearth
    8.5 2.23/m
    dearth sounds literary and points to a serious lack of something, while scarceness is a straightforward word for low supply.
  • insufficiency
    6.5 0.36/m
    insufficiency often implies not having enough skill, quality, or quantity, while scarceness focuses specifically on low supply amounts.
  • deficiency
    4.5 15/m
    deficiency suggests a missing necessary part or weakness to be fixed, while scarceness describes a general shortness in amount available.
  • scantiness
    8.8 0.04/m
    scantiness describes small, thin, or minimal amount often with poor detail, while scarceness means low supply in quantity.
  • meagerness
    7.0 0.07/m
    meagerness highlights a very poor, small amount that feels weak or disappointing, while scarceness names simple lack of supply.
