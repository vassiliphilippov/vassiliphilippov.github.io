New World blackbird

noun
bird of the Icteridae family (Americas)
6.5 5.5
Pronunciation: /nuː wɜːrld ˈblæk.bɜːrd/ /; / /njuː wɜːrld ˈblæk.bɜːrd/ (AmE), /njuː wɜːld ˈblæk.bɜːd/ /; / /nuː wɜːld ˈblæk.bɜːd/ (BrE)
any bird of the Icteridae family native to the Americas, whose male is black or mostly black in color

Examples

Male New World blackbirds
Male New World blackbirds often have glossy black feathers that stand out during mating season.
The New World blackbird is common in wetlands across North America.
Unlike the European blackbird, the New World blackbird belongs to the Icteridae family.
