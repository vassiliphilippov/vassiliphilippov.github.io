Calapooya

noun
member of a Native American tribe in Oregon
Pronunciation: / ˌkæləˈpuːjə / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈpuːjə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people from the Willamette Basin in Oregon.

Examples

A Calapooya elder
A Calapooya elder shared stories about their history at the local museum.
Many descendants of the Calapooya are active in cultural preservation efforts today.
The Calapooya were traditionally inhabitants of the Willamette Valley in what is now Oregon.
