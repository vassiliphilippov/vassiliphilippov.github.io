i.e.

adverb
that is to say; in other words
3.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / a…™ Àài / (AmE), / a…™ ÀàiÀê / (BrE)
used to introduce a clearer or more precise explanation of something just mentioned; that is to say

Examples

That is, i.e.
Each student must submit one project, i.e., a completed research paper, by next Friday.
She prefers citrus fruits, i.e., oranges and lemons, in her smoothies.
The meeting will be held in the main hall, i.e., Room 101 on the first floor.
The report must be finished by EOD, i.e., by the end of the day.
We need to address the main issue, i.e., the lack of communication within the team.
You should list all major expenses, i.e., rent, utilities, and groceries.

Synonyms

  • that is to say
    3.5 3.44/m
    that is to say repeats a clear restatement or clarification in normal English, while i.e. is its brief written abbreviation.
  • id est
    8.2 0.00/m
    id est is the Latin full form used in formal writing, while i.e. is the common abbreviated form.
  • namely
    3.6 16/m
    namely introduces specific details or items that clarify a previous statement, while i.e. more often restates briefly.
  • viz.
    8.0 0.00/m
    viz. names specific examples or items in formal lists, while i.e. is a general brief clarification.
  • in other words
    2.7 20/m
    in other words explains something by paraphrase for clarity, while i.e. gives a short precise restatement.
  • to be precise
    3.7 0.77/m
    to be precise signals you will add exact facts or correct a detail, while i.e. introduces a concise clarification.
