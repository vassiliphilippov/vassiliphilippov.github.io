Pronunciation: / ɡruːp / (AmE), / ɡruːp / (BrE)
(Chemistry) Two or more atoms bonded together, functioning as a single unit and forming part of a molecule.

Examples

A methyl group
A methyl group consists of one carbon atom bonded to three hydrogen atoms.
Adding a phosphate group can change the molecule's function significantly.
The hydroxyl group in alcohols is responsible for their reactivity.
