intermedius

adjective
intermediate position
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmiːdiəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiəs / (BrE)
intermediate in position, form, or size, especially used in anatomy or scientific names

Examples

Intermedius muscle
The intermedius muscle lies between the vastus lateralis and vastus medialis in the thigh.
In anatomy, the term intermedius is often used to describe structures located between others.
The nerve labeled as intermedius connects two major branches in the cranial region.
The species name intermedius suggests it has features between two related animals.
Researchers identified a new plant called Rosa intermedius because of its intermediate characteristics.
In taxonomy, the term intermedius often appears in Latin names to show a middle form.
