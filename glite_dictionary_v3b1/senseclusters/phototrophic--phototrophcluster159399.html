phototrophic

adjective
using light for energy
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtəˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
describing organisms that obtain energy from light to produce food

Examples

Phototrophic organisms
Many types of algae are phototrophic and rely on sunlight for their energy needs.
Scientists study phototrophic bacteria to understand how they convert light into chemical energy.
The pond's ecosystem is supported by phototrophic organisms at the base of the food chain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterotrophic - obtaining food by consuming other organisms
  • synonym: photosynthetic - using light to synthesize food from carbon dioxide and water
