oil drying agent

noun
substance promoting the drying of oils
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl ˈdraɪɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈdraɪɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a substance added to oils, especially in paints or varnishes, to accelerate the drying process by promoting oxidation or removing moisture.

Examples

Add oil drying agent
Add a small amount of oil drying agent to linseed oil to speed up the curing process.
Cobalt compounds are a common oil drying agent used in the production of paints.
The manufacturer recommends using an oil drying agent to ensure a smooth, hard finish on the artwork.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desiccant - a substance used to absorb moisture and keep things dry
