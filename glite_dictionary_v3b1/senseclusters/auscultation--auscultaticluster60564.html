auscultation

noun
listening to body sounds for diagnosis
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɔskəlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːskəlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of listening to sounds from within the body, typically using a stethoscope, as part of a medical examination

Examples

Routine auscultation
Routine auscultation is an important part of a physical examination.
The doctor performed auscultation to check for abnormal lung sounds.
Through careful auscultation, the cardiologist detected a heart murmur.
