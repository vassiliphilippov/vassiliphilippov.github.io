dominionist

noun
religious governance supporter
9.9
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɪnjənɪst / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnjənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that a nation or society should be governed according to religious (especially Christian) principles, especially in politics and law

Examples

A dominionist leader
The political candidate was described as a dominionist because of his views on church and state.
Some activists worry about the influence of dominionist groups on national policy.
The debate centered on whether dominionist ideas should shape the country's laws.
Some dominionists believe that religious values should guide national laws and policies.
The political movement was influenced by several prominent dominionists in the community.
Critics argue that dominionist ideas challenge the separation of church and state.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secularist - a person who supports separation of religion from government
  • synonym: theocrat - a person who supports government by religious leaders or principles
