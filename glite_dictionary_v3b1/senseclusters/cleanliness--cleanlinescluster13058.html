cleanliness

noun
quality of a dataset (free of errors)
5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkliːn.lɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈkliːn.lə.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈkliːn.lɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈkliːn.lə.nəs/ (BrE)
(in data science and computing) the degree to which a dataset or collection of data is free from errors, missing values, duplicates, inconsistent formats, or other problems that would affect analysis.

Examples

Data cleanliness
Before training the model, she evaluated the dataset's cleanliness by checking missing values and duplicates.
Data engineers introduced scripts to improve cleanliness, removing inconsistent entries and fixing formatting problems across tables.
Stakeholders questioned the cleanliness of the merged records before approving the analytics dashboard rollout.
