reproduction

noun
recall by replicating original stimulus
3.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /riː.prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of recalling information by reproducing the original stimulus as it was initially perceived or learned, especially in memory research

Examples

Memory reproduction
His reproduction of the poem from memory was almost word-perfect.
In psychology, reproduction of learned material is tested by asking subjects to restate exactly what they studied.
The experiment examines whether reproduction or recognition is more effective for memorizing complex figures.
