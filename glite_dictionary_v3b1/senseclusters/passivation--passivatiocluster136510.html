passivation

noun
protective surface process
Pronunciation: / ˌpæsɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
a process that makes a material less reactive by forming a protective layer on its surface

Examples

Metal passivation
Stainless steel resists rusting due to passivation of its surface.
The passivation of aluminum helps prevent it from corroding in moist environments.
Engineers recommend regular passivation to extend the lifespan of metal equipment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrosion - the gradual destruction of materials by chemical reaction
  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction in which a material combines with oxygen
