locust

noun
hardwood from the locust tree
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.kəst / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəst / (BrE)
a type of hardwood tree (such as black locust or honey locust) or its strong, durable wood

Examples

Locust wood furniture
Carpenters prize locust for its strength and durability in outdoor furniture.
He split the locust logs for firewood, noting how dense the wood was.
The fence posts were made from sturdy locust to resist rot and insects.
A row of locust trees lines the main street, providing shade in summer.
Farmers sometimes plant locust for windbreaks and erosion control due to its hardy nature.
The wood from the locust tree is prized for its exceptional strength and resistance to rot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: black locust - a North American locust tree with fragrant white flowers
  • synonym: honey locust - a North American locust tree with long seed pods and sweet pulp
  • synonym: locust tree - a tree species such as black locust or honey locust
