emancipation

noun
act of setting someone free from authority
8.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /ɪ.mæn.sə.ˈpeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪ.mæn.sɪ.ˈpeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪ.mæn.sə.ˈpeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪ.mæn.sɪ.ˈpeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of setting someone free from control, authority, slavery, or legal or social restrictions

Examples

The emancipation of women led to greater equality in many societies.
After years of struggle, the country celebrated the emancipation of its people from colonial rule.
The emancipation of minors allows some teenagers to live independently from their parents.
