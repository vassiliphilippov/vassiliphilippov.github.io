emancipation

noun
act of setting someone free from authority
8.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmænsɪˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmansɪˈpeɪʃən /; / ɪˌmæn.sɪˈpeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of setting someone free from control, authority, slavery, or legal or social restrictions

Examples

Many activists fought for the emancipation of enslaved people in the 19th century.
The legal emancipation of a minor allows them to live independently from their parents.
The emancipation of women led to greater equality in many societies.
After years of struggle, the country celebrated the emancipation of its people from colonial rule.
The emancipation of minors allows some teenagers to live independently from their parents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enslavement - the act of making someone a slave or subjecting them to control
  • synonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free; freeing from imprisonment or oppression
  • antonym: subjugation - the act of bringing someone or something under domination or control
