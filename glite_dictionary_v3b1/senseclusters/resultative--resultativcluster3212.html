resultative

noun
grammatical outcome marker
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzʌltətɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌltətɪv / (BrE)
a grammatical construction or element that shows the resulting state or outcome produced by an action in a sentence

Examples

A resultative construction
In the sentence 'She painted the door red,' 'red' is a resultative showing the door's new state.
Linguists often analyze the use of resultatives in different languages to compare sentence structures.
The teacher explained how a resultative can change the meaning of a verb in a sentence.
