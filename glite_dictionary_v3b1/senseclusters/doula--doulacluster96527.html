doula

noun
a childbirth support person
7.2 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀêl…ô / (AmE), / ÀàduÀêl…ô / (BrE)
A person, typically trained, who provides emotional and practical support to a woman during childbirth.

Examples

Birth doula
After training, she began working as a doula at a local hospital.
Many couples hire a doula to help them navigate the birth experience.
The doula provided encouragement and comfort throughout her labor.

Synonyms

  • birthing coach
    5.4 0.00/m
    a birthing coach helps with techniques and gives emotional, practical support during childbirth, while a doula is a trained support person.
  • labor coach
    3.8 0.01/m
    a labor coach often helps the partner with breathing and comfort techniques in labor, while a doula provides trained emotional and practical support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: midwife - a person trained to assist women in childbirth
