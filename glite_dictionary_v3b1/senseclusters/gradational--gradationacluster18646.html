gradational

adjective
occurring in gradual stages or degrees
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈdeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ɡrəˈdeɪʃnəl / (BrE)
Taking place or changing by gradual stages or degrees.

Examples

A gradational change
Economic growth in the region has been gradational rather than abrupt.
Her painting style is known for its gradational blending of tones and shades.
The transition from day to night is often a gradational change in the sky's colors.
