safety

noun
American football scoring play (two points)
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti / (BrE)
(in American football) a scoring play worth two points, awarded to the opposing team when a player is tackled or commits a foul in their own end zone.

Examples

A safety score
A safety can shift the momentum in a closely contested football game.
The defense scored a safety in the second quarter to take the lead.
When the quarterback was sacked in the end zone, the team gave up a safety.
