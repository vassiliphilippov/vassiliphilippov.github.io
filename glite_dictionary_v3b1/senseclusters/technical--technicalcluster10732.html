technical

adjective
of market changes due to trading, not fundamentals
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛk.nɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛk.nɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Relating to movements in financial markets that result from short-term market factors or trading patterns, rather than underlying fundamental economic considerations.

Examples

Technical analysis
Some investors look only at technical indicators rather than company performance.
The rally was largely technical, driven by trading momentum rather than positive news.
The sharp drop in prices was considered a technical correction by most analysts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fundamental - in finance, relating to intrinsic value or core underlying factors
