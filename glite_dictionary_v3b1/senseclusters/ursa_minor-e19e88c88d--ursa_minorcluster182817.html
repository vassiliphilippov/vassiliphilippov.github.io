Ursa Minor

proper noun
constellation containing the North Star
Pronunciation: / ˌɜːrsə ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˌɜːsə ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a constellation in the northern sky, also called the Little Bear or the Little Dipper, which contains the North Star (Polaris)

Examples

Ursa Minor constellation
As a child, she learned to find the Ursa Minor constellation on clear nights.
Navigators used Ursa Minor to help guide their way across the ocean.
Polaris, the North Star, is the brightest star in Ursa Minor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Ursa Major - a constellation in the northern sky, also known as the Great Bear and containing the Big Dipper
