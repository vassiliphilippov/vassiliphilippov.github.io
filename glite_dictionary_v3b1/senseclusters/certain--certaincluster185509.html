certain

adjective
sure to happen; inevitable
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːtən /; / ˈsɝː.tən /; / ˈsɝːtn̩ /; / ˈsɜːrtən / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtən /; / ˈsɜː.tən /; / ˈsɜːtn̩ /; / ˈsɜːtn / (BrE)
sure to happen or be true; established beyond doubt; guaranteed or inevitable

Examples

Certain victory
After months of drought, crop failure seemed certain to the local farmers.
It is certain that the sun will rise tomorrow, regardless of the weather.
With their team ahead by three goals, victory appeared certain before the final whistle.
For a certain fix to the error, reset the device completely.
Their method is a certain way to increase productivity in the office.
This new vaccine is a certain protection against the disease.
If you want successful results, you must be certain to follow the instructions carefully.
Please be certain to turn off the lights before leaving the house.
She was certain to double-check all her answers on the test.
