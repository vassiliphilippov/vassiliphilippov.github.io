runaway

adjective
out of control; unchecked
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnəweɪ / (BrE)
(of a situation or process) completely out of control or happening faster than expected.

Examples

Runaway fire
A runaway fire swept through the forest, destroying hundreds of acres.
Economists are concerned about runaway inflation affecting the cost of living.
The company struggled to control runaway spending during the busy holiday season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contained - kept under control; restrained
  • synonym: unchecked - not restrained or controlled
