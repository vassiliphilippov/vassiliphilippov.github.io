Tidewater region

noun
coastal plain in the southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪdˌwɑtər ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdˌwɔːtə ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
the low-lying coastal plain in the southeastern United States, especially the eastern parts of Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Georgia

Examples

The Tidewater region coast
Many plantations were established in the Tidewater region during the colonial era.

The climate of the Tidewater region is influenced by its proximity to the Atlantic Ocean.
