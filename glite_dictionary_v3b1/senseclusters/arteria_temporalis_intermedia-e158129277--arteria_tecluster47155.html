arteria temporalis intermedia

noun
branch of temporal artery to middle temporal lobe
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈtɪəriə tɛmˌpɔːrælɪs ˌɪntərˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə tɛmˌpɔːrælɪs ˌɪntəˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
A branch of the temporal artery supplying blood to the middle part of the cerebral cortex in the temporal lobe.

Examples

The arteria temporalis intermedia branch
Damage to the arteria temporalis intermedia can affect brain function in the temporal region.
Surgeons must carefully identify the arteria temporalis intermedia during procedures near the middle temporal cortex.
The arteria temporalis intermedia plays an important role in supplying blood to the temporal lobe.
