crucible

noun
container for high-temperature chemical reactions
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪbəl / (BrE)
a container made of a material that can withstand very high temperatures, used to melt or heat substances in chemical processes

Examples

A crucible for melting
Ceramic crucibles are often used in laboratories for high-temperature experiments.
During the process, impurities separate out while the substance is in the crucible.
The chemist heated the metal in a crucible until it melted.
