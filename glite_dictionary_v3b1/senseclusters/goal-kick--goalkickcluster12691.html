goal-kick

noun
restart in football after ball crosses goal line
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊl kɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl kɪk / (BrE)
(in football) a method of restarting play, taken by the defending team from within their goal area after the ball has been sent over the goal line by the attacking team.

Examples

Football goal-kick
A well-placed goal-kick can quickly turn into a counter-attack for the defending team.
After the shot missed the target, play restarted with a goal-kick from the edge of the six-yard box.
The goalkeeper took a goal-kick after the ball was sent out of play by the striker.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corner kick - kick from the corner to restart play in football
  • synonym: free kick - kick awarded for infringement in football
