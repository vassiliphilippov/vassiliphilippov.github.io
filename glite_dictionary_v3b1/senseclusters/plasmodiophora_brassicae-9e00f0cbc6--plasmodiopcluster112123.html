Plasmodiophora brassicae

noun
parasite causing clubroot in cabbages
9.9
Pronunciation: / plæzˌmoʊdiˈɒfərə brəˈsɪkiː / (AmE), / plazˌməʊdiˈɒfərə brəˈsɪkiː / (BrE)
a pathogenic microorganism (fungus-like protist) that infects brassica plants, causing clubroot disease and root swellings

Examples

Plasmodiophora brassicae infection
Careful crop rotation can help manage the spread of Plasmodiophora brassicae in vegetable gardens.
Researchers are studying how Plasmodiophora brassicae infects cabbage roots to develop resistant cultivars.
The soil in the field was contaminated with Plasmodiophora brassicae, leading to a severe outbreak of clubroot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clubroot - disease of brassicas caused by Plasmodiophora brassicae, leading to swollen roots
