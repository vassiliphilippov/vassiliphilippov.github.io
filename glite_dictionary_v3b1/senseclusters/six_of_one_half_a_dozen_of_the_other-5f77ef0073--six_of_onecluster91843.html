six of one, half a dozen of the other

x
two things that are essentially the same
Pronunciation: / sɪks əv wʌn hæf ə dəzən əv ði ʌðər / (AmE), / sɪks əv wʌn hɑːf ə dʌzən əv ði ˈʌðə / (BrE)
used to say that two choices or possibilities are virtually the same or make no real difference

Examples

Six of one, half a dozen of the other
For most people, doing it now or later is six of one, half a dozen of the other.
To be honest, it's six of one, half a dozen of the other which restaurant we go to tonight.
Whether you travel by train or by bus, it's six of one, half a dozen of the other in terms of time.
