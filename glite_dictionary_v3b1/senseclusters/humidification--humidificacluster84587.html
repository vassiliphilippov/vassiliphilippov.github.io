humidification

noun
adding moisture
5.3
Pronunciation: /huː.mɪ.dɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /hjuː.mɪ.dɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /hjuː.mɪ.dɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /huː.mɪ.dɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of adding moisture to something, especially to the air, to increase or maintain its humidity

Examples

Proper humidification process
Proper humidification of the cigars kept them supple and prevented the wrappers from drying out.
The humidification of wood is important to prevent cracking during storage.
Many homes use devices for humidification during dry winter months to improve air quality.
Proper humidification can help prevent dry skin and respiratory problems during winter.
The hospital uses advanced humidification systems to maintain a comfortable environment for patients.
Greenhouses rely on humidification to create ideal growing conditions for tropical plants.
