Examples

Synonyms

  • buildup
    4.1 1.10/m
    a buildup is steady, growing publicity that prepares people for something, while hype is loud, often exaggerated promotion to attract attention.
  • ballyhoo
    9.9 0.15/m
    ballyhoo is noisy, exaggerated publicity that aims to grab attention, while hype is broader promotion used to create excitement.
  • hoopla
    7.6 1.21/m
    hoopla is loud public fuss and excitement around an event, while hype is planned publicity meant to promote or sell something.
  • puffery
    8.3 0.11/m
    puffery is exaggerated advertising praise often used by companies, while hype is general promotional publicity meant to draw attention.
  • puff
    7.2 0.23/m
    a puff is a short promotional article or praise piece, while hype is wider, often louder publicity to build excitement.
  • plug
    6.9 1.54/m
    a plug is a brief, direct promotion or mention in media, while hype is larger scale publicity that seeks broad attention.
  • fanfare
    5.1 0.99/m
    fanfare is a showy, often ceremonial display with music or spectacle, while hype is promotional publicity aimed at selling or exciting people.
