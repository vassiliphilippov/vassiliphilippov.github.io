expert

noun
person with special skill or knowledge
3.2 121/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õk.sp…öt / (AmE), / Ààek.sp…úÀêt / (BrE)
a person who has a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular subject or area

Examples

A expert in science
As an expert in computer programming, he quickly fixed the problem.
She consulted an expert to help her with the legal documents.
The museum invited an art expert to authenticate the rare painting.

Synonyms

  • specialist
    3.9 28/m
    specialist studies and works in one narrow area, while expert has a high level of knowledge across a subject or field.
  • master
    4.2 42/m
    master is highly skilled and often teaches or leads in an art, while expert is simply someone with deep knowledge or skill.
  • authority
    4.2 23/m
    authority is a widely trusted or official source on a topic, while expert is any person with a high level of knowledge.
  • adept
    4.7 0.56/m
    adept is skilled at doing a particular activity by practice, while expert has broad, deep knowledge or skill in a subject.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amateur - a person who engages in an activity for pleasure rather than as a profession or with expert skill
  • synonym: specialist - a person who is an expert in a specific area or field
