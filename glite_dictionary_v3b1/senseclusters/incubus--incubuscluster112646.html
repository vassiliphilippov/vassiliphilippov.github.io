incubus

noun
distressing or nightmarish situation
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋ.kjə.bəs /; / ˈɪŋkjəbəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.kjʊ.bəs /; / ˈɪŋkjʊbəs / (BrE)
a situation, feeling, or thing that causes great distress, worry, or anxiety

Examples

A heavy incubus
His guilt became an incubus that haunted him day and night.
The economic crisis proved to be an incubus for the country's leaders.
The shadow of war hung over the region like an incubus for years.
Financial worries were a constant incubus in her life.
The memory of his mistake became an incubus that haunted him for years.
Unresolved issues acted as an incubus on the team's morale.
