sapiential book

noun
biblical book of wisdom literature
8.1
Pronunciation: / sæˈpɪɛnʃəl bʊk / (AmE), / sæˈpɪɛnʃəl bʊk / (BrE)
any of the biblical books, such as Proverbs or Ecclesiastes, that are classified as wisdom literature and contain teachings on wisdom and ethics.

Examples

A sapiential book of wisdom
Ecclesiasticus is one of the lesser-known sapiential books in biblical literature.
Many scholars study sapiential books to understand ancient perspectives on wisdom and morality.
The Book of Proverbs is considered a sapiential book in the Old Testament.
