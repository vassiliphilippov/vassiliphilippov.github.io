panpsychist

adjective
believing all matter is conscious
Pronunciation: / pænˈsaɪkɪst / (AmE), / pænˈsʌɪkɪst / (BrE)
relating to the belief that consciousness or mind is a fundamental property of all matter.

Examples

Panpsychist theory
The panpsychist view suggests that even simple particles possess some form of consciousness.
Her panpsychist perspective influenced her approach to both science and philosophy.
Some scientists remain skeptical of panpsychist explanations for the origins of consciousness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animist - believing all things have a spirit or soul
  • antonym: materialist - believing only physical matter exists
