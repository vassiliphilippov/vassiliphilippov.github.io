intimidatory

adjective
threatening or frightening
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɪmɪdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪmɪdətəri / (BrE)
intended to frighten, threaten, or make someone feel less confident

Examples

Intimidatory tone
The manager's intimidatory tone made the employees uncomfortable during the meeting.
Some politicians use intimidatory language to silence their critics.
The team's intimidatory tactics were criticized by the referee after the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: menacing - suggesting the presence of danger; threatening
  • antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or afraid
  • synonym: threatening - expressing a threat or intention to harm
