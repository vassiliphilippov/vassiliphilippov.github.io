roller coaster

noun
a situation with abrupt changes
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlər ˌkoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlə ˌkəʊstə / (BrE)
a situation or experience marked by sudden or extreme changes, especially of mood, fortune, or condition

Examples

Roller coaster of emotions
His career took him on a roller coaster of successes and setbacks over the years.
Our relationship felt like an emotional roller coaster with all its sudden highs and lows.
The stock market has been a roller coaster ride lately, with prices rising and falling rapidly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and unchanging
  • synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption
