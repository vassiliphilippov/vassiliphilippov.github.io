double-decker sandwich

noun
sandwich with two filling layers
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌbəlˈdɛkɚ ˈsænwɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈdɛkə ˈsænwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A sandwich consisting of three slices of bread with two layers of fillings in between.

Examples

A double-decker sandwich with ham
At the deli, the chef prepared a classic double-decker sandwich with ham and cheese.
She ordered a huge double-decker sandwich for lunch.
The double-decker sandwich was filled with turkey, bacon, and fresh vegetables.
