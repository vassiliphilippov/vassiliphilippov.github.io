vitrified

adjective
shiny, glass-like and nonporous (in ceramics)
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪtrəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of ceramics or materials) made shiny and nonporous by being fused with a glassy or vitreous coating through intense heat.

Examples

Vitrified tiles
Many tiles are vitrified to make them more durable and water-resistant.
The vitrified surface of the mug prevents it from absorbing moisture.
The pottery was vitrified at high temperatures to achieve a glossy finish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glazed - having a glassy coating, especially on pottery
  • antonym: porous - having tiny holes that allow liquid or air to pass through
