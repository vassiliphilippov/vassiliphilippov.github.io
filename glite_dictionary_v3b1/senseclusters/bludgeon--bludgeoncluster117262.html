bludgeon

verb
coerce or force (figuratively)
9.5 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈblʌdʒ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈblʌdʒ.ən/ (BrE)
to force or coerce someone to do something by using aggressive pressure or repeated arguments, as if striking with a heavy club

Examples

To bludgeon someone into action
He tried to bludgeon his colleagues into supporting his plan at the meeting.
She felt bludgeoned by constant reminders about the deadline.
The company was accused of bludgeoning customers into buying unnecessary upgrades.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bully - to use superior strength or influence to intimidate someone
  • synonym: coerce - to persuade someone forcefully to do something they are unwilling to do
  • degree lower: persuade - to cause someone to do something through reasoning or argument
