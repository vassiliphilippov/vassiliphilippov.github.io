stripe

noun
narrow colored or textured band
1.0 5.89/m
Pronunciation: / straÉªp / (AmE), / straÉªp / (BrE)
a long, narrow band or line of color or material, often one of several, on clothing, flags, or other objects

Examples

A red stripe
She wore a shirt with red and white stripes for the festival.
The artist painted a blue stripe down the center of the canvas.
The tiger's stripes help it blend into the tall grass.
A green stripe ran across the length of the flag.
He earned a stripe on his uniform for good conduct.
The dress was decorated with a bright red stripe down the side.

Synonyms

  • band
    1.3 3.76/m
    a band is a decorative strip used to form patterns on objects, while a stripe is a long narrow repeated line.
  • bar
    2.0 2.23/m
    a bar is a solid rectangular band or mark often used in design, while a stripe is usually a long narrow ornamental line.
  • streak
    2.6 5.12/m
    a streak is a thin irregular mark or flash of color that may be accidental, while a stripe is a planned long decorative line.
