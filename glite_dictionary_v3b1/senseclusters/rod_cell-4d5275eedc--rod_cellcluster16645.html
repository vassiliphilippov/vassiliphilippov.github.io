rod cell

noun
retinal cell sensitive to dim light
5.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ‘d sÉ›l / (AmE), / rÉ’d sÉ›l / (BrE)
a type of cell in the retina that is highly sensitive to dim light and is responsible for vision in low-light conditions

Examples

Rod cells in retina
At night, our rod cells help us see even in very low levels of light.
Diseases that damage rod cells can lead to difficulty seeing at dusk or in darkness.
The retina contains both rod cells and cone cells, each serving different visual functions.

Synonyms
