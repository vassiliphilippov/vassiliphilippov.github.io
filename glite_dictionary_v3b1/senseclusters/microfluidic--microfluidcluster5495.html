microfluidic

noun
fluid control device
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊfluˈɪdɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊfluˈɪdɪk / (BrE)
a device or system designed to manipulate or control very small amounts of fluids, especially in laboratory or diagnostic applications

Examples

A microfluidic device
The laboratory purchased a new microfluidic to improve the accuracy of its blood tests.
Researchers used a microfluidic to mix chemicals in extremely small volumes for their experiment.
A portable microfluidic can quickly analyze samples in remote locations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lab-on-a-chip - a device that integrates laboratory functions on a small chip
  • synonym: microfluidics - the science and technology of systems that process small amounts of fluids
