interrelate

verb
place in mutual relationship
5.1 4.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tə.rɪ.ˈleɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.rɪ.ˈleɪt/ (BrE)
to have or show a mutual or reciprocal relationship or connection with each other

Examples

To interrelate ideas
It is important to interrelate different concepts when studying complex subjects.

Economic and social factors often interrelate, influencing policy decisions.
It is important to understand how culture and language interrelate within a society.
The two theories interrelate in several fundamental ways.
