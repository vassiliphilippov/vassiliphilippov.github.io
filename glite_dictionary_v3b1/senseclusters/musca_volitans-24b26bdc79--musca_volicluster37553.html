musca volitans

noun
spots or floaters seen before the eyes
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌskə voʊˈlaɪtænz / (AmE), / ˌmʌskə vəʊˈlɪtænz / (BrE)
Small moving spots or threads seen in the field of vision, caused by tiny cell fragments in the vitreous humor or lens of the eye.

Examples

See musca volitans spots
Many people notice musca volitans as they age, especially when looking at a bright background.
She was diagnosed with musca volitans when she complained of seeing spots in her vision.
The ophthalmologist explained that musca volitans are usually harmless but can be distracting.
