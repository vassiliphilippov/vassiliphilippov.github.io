satrapy

noun
Persian province
Pronunciation: / ˈsætrəpi / (AmE), / ˈsætrəpi / (BrE)
a province or territory governed by a satrap or subordinate ruler, especially in the ancient Persian Empire

Examples

Persian satrapy
Each satrapy was responsible for collecting taxes and maintaining order for the Persian king.
The ancient empire was divided into many satrapies to help manage its vast territory.
A satrap governed his satrapy with considerable autonomy but answered to the emperor.
The ancient Persian Empire was divided into several satrapies, each ruled by a governor.
After its conquest, the region became a satrapy and paid taxes to the central authority.
Historical records describe how the satrapy maintained some local customs under imperial rule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colony - a territory governed by a foreign power
  • synonym: protectorate - a state that is controlled and protected by another
  • synonym: province - an administrative division of a country or empire
  • antonym: sovereign state - an independent and self-governing country
