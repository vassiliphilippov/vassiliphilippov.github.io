myrrh

noun
aromatic resin used in incense and perfume
Pronunciation: / mɜr / (AmE), / mɜː / (BrE)
An aromatic resin obtained from certain trees, burned as incense and used in perfumes and traditional medicines.

Examples

Myrrh resin
Myrrh was among the gifts said to be brought by the Magi in the biblical story.
She purchased a rare perfume that contained hints of myrrh and sandalwood.
The ancient Egyptians valued myrrh for its use in embalming and religious rituals.
