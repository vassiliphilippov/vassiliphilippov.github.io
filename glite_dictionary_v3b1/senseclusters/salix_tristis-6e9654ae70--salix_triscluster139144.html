Salix tristis

noun
species of willow shrub (eastern U.S.)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪlɪks ˈtraɪstɪs / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks ˈtrɪstɪs / (BrE)
A species of willow shrub native to dry areas in the eastern United States, with long, narrow leaves that are grayish beneath.

Examples

Salix tristis shrub
Botanists studied the adaptive features of Salix tristis in drought-prone habitats.
The Salix tristis commonly grows along sandy ridges and dry upland clearings in the eastern United States.
The leaves of Salix tristis are distinctively long and narrow, with a pale, canescent underside.
