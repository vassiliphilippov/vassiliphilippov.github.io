abortion pill

noun
drug that induces abortion
3.8 4.9
Pronunciation: / əˈbɔrʃən pɪl / (AmE), / əˈbɔːʃən pɪl / (BrE)
A drug taken to induce an abortion, especially in early pregnancy, typically by blocking the action of progesterone.

Examples

The abortion pill
Healthcare providers must inform patients about the possible side effects of the abortion pill.
Some women choose the abortion pill as a non-surgical option for ending a pregnancy.
The abortion pill is most effective in the early weeks of pregnancy.
