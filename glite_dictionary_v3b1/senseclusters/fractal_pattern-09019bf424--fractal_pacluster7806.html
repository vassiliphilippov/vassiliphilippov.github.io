fractal pattern

noun
self-repeating geometric pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈfræk.təl ˈpæt.ɚn / (AmE), / ˈfræk.təl ˈpæt.ən / (BrE)
a complex geometric pattern that repeats itself at different scales, often found in mathematics and nature

Examples

A fractal pattern in nature
Many plants, such as ferns, exhibit fractal patterns in their leaves and branches.
Mathematicians use fractal patterns to model the complex shapes of coastlines and clouds.
The artist based his design on fractal patterns observed in snowflakes and crystals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-similarity - property of a shape appearing similar at different scales
