diaphysis

noun
main shaft of a long bone
6.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæfəsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈafɪsɪs / (BrE)
the shaft or central part of a long bone, situated between the ends (epiphyses)

Examples

The diaphysis shaft
Bone growth in children primarily takes place near the ends, not in the diaphysis.
The fracture occurred in the diaphysis of the femur, requiring surgical intervention.
Tumors located in the diaphysis are less common than those in the bone ends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epiphysis - the end part of a long bone, initially growing separately from the shaft
