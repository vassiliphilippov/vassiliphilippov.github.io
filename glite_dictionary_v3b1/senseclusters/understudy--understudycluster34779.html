understudy

verb
learn a role as a backup actor
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstʌdi / (BrE)
to learn another actor's role in order to be able to replace them if needed

Examples

To understudy a role
He has understudied several major roles during his career.
She was asked to understudy the lead actress in the production.
When the main actor fell ill, Jane was ready to understudy at a moment's notice.
She understudied the lead role and was ready to perform at a moment's notice.
Many actors understudy several parts before getting a main role in a production.
He understudied for the famous actor during the entire theater season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover - to act as a substitute for an actor's role
  • degree higher: perform - to act or take part in a play or show
