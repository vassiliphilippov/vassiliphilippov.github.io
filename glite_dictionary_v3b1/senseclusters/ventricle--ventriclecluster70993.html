ventricle

noun
cavity in the brain containing cerebrospinal fluid
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
one of the four connected cavities in the brain that contain cerebrospinal fluid and are continuous with the central canal of the spinal cord

Examples

Brain ventricle
Cerebrospinal fluid flows from one ventricle to another, cushioning the brain.
Obstruction of a brain ventricle can lead to increased pressure on the brain.
The third ventricle is located between the two halves of the thalamus.
