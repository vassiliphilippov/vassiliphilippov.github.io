disutility

noun
negative value or harm
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsjuːˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdɪsjuːˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the negative value, harm, or disadvantage resulting from an action, choice, or outcome

Examples

The disutility of smoking
The disutility of smoking includes both health risks and financial costs.
Economists often weigh the utility and disutility of different policy options.
Long working hours can create significant disutility for employees and their families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cost - something that must be given up or suffered
  • synonym: harm - physical or mental damage or injury
  • antonym: utility - the usefulness or positive value of something
