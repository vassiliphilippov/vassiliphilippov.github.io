secondary

adjective
of lower or second importance
1.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈse.kən.driː/ /; / /ˈse.kən.dər.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈse.kən.driː/ /; / /ˈse.kən.dər.ɪ/ (BrE)
less important, not primary, or lower in rank, order, or importance; coming after something else

Examples

Secondary role
Although the main road was closed, traffic was diverted to a secondary route.
Her interests in music are secondary to her passion for science.
In many jobs, a degree is only a secondary requirement to relevant experience.
Art was always a secondary interest for her, with her main focus devoted to science.

Environmental damage is often a secondary effect of industrial development.
His interest in the project was only secondary to his main responsibilities.
The patient developed a secondary infection after surgery.
