residua

noun
remaining estate assets
9.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪdʒuə / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjʊə / (BrE)
the parts of an estate that remain after all debts, taxes, and specific gifts have been paid or distributed

Examples

Estate residua
The lawyer explained how the residua would be divided among the heirs.
After all debts were settled, the residua of the estate was distributed.
Beneficiaries received their shares from the residua left in the will.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bequest - a gift of personal property by will
  • synonym: residue - the remainder or what is left after a part is removed
