anterior temporal artery

noun
artery supplying anterior temporal lobe
6.2
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈtɪr.iː.ər ˈtem.pər.əl ˈɑːr.tər.iː/ (AmE), /æn.ˈtɪə.ri.ə ˈtem.pə.rəl ˈɑː.tə.ri/ (BrE)
a branch of the temporal artery that supplies blood to the front part of the temporal lobe of the cerebral cortex

Examples

The anterior temporal artery
Damage to the anterior temporal artery can lead to impaired function in the temporal cortex.
Surgeons must be cautious not to injure the anterior temporal artery during temporal lobe procedures.
The anterior temporal artery is often visualized during cerebral angiography to assess blood flow to the temporal lobe.
