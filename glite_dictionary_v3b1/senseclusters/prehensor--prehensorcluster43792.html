prehensor

noun
centipede's venom-injecting forelimb
Pronunciation: / prɪˈhɛnsər / (AmE), / prɪˈhɛnsə / (BrE)
the anterior pair of legs in centipedes, specialized for grasping prey and injecting venom

Examples

Centipede prehensors
A centipede's venom is delivered through specialized structures called prehensors.
The centipede used its prehensors to immobilize the insect before eating it.
Zoologists studied the function of the prehensors in various centipede species.
