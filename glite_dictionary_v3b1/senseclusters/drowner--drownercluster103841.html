drowner

noun
cause of drowning
Pronunciation: / ˈdraʊnər / (AmE), / ˈdraʊnə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes someone or something to drown

Examples

A strong drowner
The strong current was a silent drowner for careless swimmers.
He warned that the old well could be a dangerous drowner for small animals.
The faulty pool cover became an unexpected drowner after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: killer - a person or thing that causes death
  • antonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone from danger
