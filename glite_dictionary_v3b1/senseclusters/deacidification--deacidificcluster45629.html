deacidification

noun
neutralizing acidity
8.2
Pronunciation: / diˌæsɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌæsɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing or neutralizing acidity in soil, water, or other substances, often to improve quality or meet standards

Examples

Deacidification of soil
The deacidification of the lake helped restore its natural ecosystem and support aquatic life.
Libraries use deacidification to preserve old books and prevent paper from deteriorating.
Regular deacidification of soil can improve crop yields and maintain healthy farmland.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acidification - the process of making something more acidic
  • synonym: neutralization - the process of making a substance chemically neutral
