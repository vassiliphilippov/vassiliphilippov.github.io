meson

noun
elementary particle mediating nuclear forces
9.9 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…õz…ën / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀêz…ín / (BrE)
an elementary particle in physics, with a baryon number of zero, that mediates the strong force in the atomic nucleus

Examples

A meson particle
Physicists used particle accelerators to detect various varieties of meson in high-energy experiments.
Pions are a well-known type of meson involved in mediating interactions between nucleons.
The discovery of the meson provided important evidence for the theory of nuclear forces.
