hepatic portal vein

noun
vein carrying blood to the liver
8.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /hə.ˈpæt.ɪk ˈpɔːr.təl veɪn/ /; / /hɪ.ˈpæt.ɪk ˈpɔːr.təl veɪn/ (AmE), /hɪ.ˈpæt.ɪk ˈpɔː.təl veɪn/ /; / /hə.ˈpæt.ɪk ˈpɔː.təl veɪn/ (BrE)
A short vein that carries blood from the digestive organs to the liver for processing.

Examples

The hepatic portal vein carries blood
A blockage in the hepatic portal vein can lead to serious medical conditions.
Doctors monitor the hepatic portal vein during liver surgery to ensure proper blood flow.
The hepatic portal vein transports nutrients absorbed from the intestines to the liver.
