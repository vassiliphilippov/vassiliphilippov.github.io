escape

verb
leak or be released from confinement
2.4 2.6
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈskeɪp/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈskeɪp/ (BrE)
(of a substance) to leak, flow, or be released from a confined space, typically through a small opening.

Examples

Gas escapes slowly
A faint smell of gas began to escape from the old stove.
Make sure no steam can escape while cooking under pressure.
Water can escape through even the smallest crack in a pipe.
