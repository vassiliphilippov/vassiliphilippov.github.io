hesitating

adjective
uncertain or indecisive
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛzɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛzɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
slow to act or speak because of uncertainty or indecision; lacking firmness or decisiveness.

Examples

A hesitating voice
A hesitating voice came from the back of the room, barely above a whisper.
His hesitating manner made the others wonder if he was telling the truth.
She gave a hesitating reply, unsure of what to say next.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decisive - able to make decisions quickly and firmly
  • synonym: uncertain - feeling or showing doubt; not having fixed opinions or knowledge
