objectivism

noun
Ayn Rand philosophy
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒɛktɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical system developed by Ayn Rand that emphasizes reason, individual rights, rational self-interest, and free-market capitalism

Examples

Ayn Rand's objectivism
Many of her beliefs were influenced by objectivism and its focus on rational self-interest.
The debate centered on whether objectivism offers a practical guide for modern society.
He studied objectivism to better understand Ayn Rand's views on ethics and politics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual
  • synonym: individualism - the belief in the importance of individual rights and independence
