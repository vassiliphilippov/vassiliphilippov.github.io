denarius

noun
medieval coin
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈnɛəriəs / (AmE), / dɪˈnɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
a medieval coin or unit of account in parts of Europe, originally based on the Roman denarius and used in pricing or bookkeeping

Examples

Medieval denarius value
The merchant accepted a single denarius for a loaf of bread at the market.
In medieval records, prices were often listed in denarius to standardize accounting.
A silver denarius was considered valuable in trade across several European regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: penny - a small coin or unit of currency in various countries
  • synonym: solidus - a gold coin used in the later Roman Empire and medieval Europe
