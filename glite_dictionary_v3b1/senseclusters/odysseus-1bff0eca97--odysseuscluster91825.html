Odysseus

proper noun
Greek hero of the Odyssey
7.6
Pronunciation: /əʊ.ˈdɪ.siː.əs/ /; / /oʊ.ˈdɪ.siː.əs/ /; / /ə.ˈdɪ.sjuːs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdɪ.siː.əs/ /; / /ə.ˈdɪ.sjuːs/ /; / /əʊ.ˈdɪ.siː.əs/ (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the legendary Greek hero known for his cleverness, whose long journey home after the Trojan War is told in the Odyssey.

Examples

Odysseus in Greek myth
Odysseus is renowned for devising the wooden horse that allowed the Greeks to enter Troy.
Homer's epic poem, the Odyssey, recounts the adventures of Odysseus as he strives to return home.
Many admire Odysseus for his cunning intelligence and perseverance during his long journey.
