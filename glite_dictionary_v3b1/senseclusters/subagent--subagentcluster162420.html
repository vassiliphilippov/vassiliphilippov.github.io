subagent

noun
agent's representative
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.eɪ.dʒənt/ /; / /ˈsʌb.ə.dʒənt/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.eɪ.dʒənt/ /; / /ˈsʌb.ə.dʒənt/ (BrE)
a person appointed by an agent to act or assist on behalf of the principal, usually with limited authority

Examples

Company subagent
The main agent hired a subagent to manage the company's affairs in the new region.
A subagent must follow the instructions given by both the agent and the principal.
The contract specified the duties and limits of the subagent in handling transactions.
The subagent was responsible for delivering the documents to the client on time.
When the main agent was unavailable, the subagent managed the negotiations.
A subagent must follow the instructions given by the primary agent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agent - a person authorized to act on behalf of another
  • synonym: deputy - a person appointed to assist or act for another
  • antonym: principal - the person who authorizes an agent to act on their behalf
