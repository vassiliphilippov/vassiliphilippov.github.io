pitch-dark

adjective
completely dark
3.4 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / p…™t É d…ërk / (AmE), / p…™t É d…ëÀêk / (BrE)
Completely or extremely dark; having no light at all.

Examples

A pitch-dark room
It felt pitch-dark as we walked through the tunnel without a flashlight.
The room became pitch-dark when the power went out during the storm.
Without any windows, the basement was pitch-dark at night.

Synonyms

  • pitch-black
    3.4 0.49/m
    pitch-black is intensely dark and very common in speech, while pitch-dark is the near-identical variant often used the same way.
  • lightless
    5.1 0.36/m
    lightless is used to describe places without light in a more formal or poetic tone, while pitch-dark is a common casual description.
