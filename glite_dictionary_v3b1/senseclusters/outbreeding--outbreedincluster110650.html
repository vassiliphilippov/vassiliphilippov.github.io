outbreeding

noun
mating unrelated individuals
6.8
Pronunciation: /aʊt.ˈbriː.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈaʊt.briː.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /aʊt.ˈbriː.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈaʊt.briː.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the practice of mating individuals that are not closely related to increase genetic diversity and reduce inherited problems

Examples

Outbreeding in herds
Many animal breeders use outbreeding to improve the health and vigor of their herds.
Conservationists recommend outbreeding to prevent genetic disorders in endangered species.
The success of the new crop variety was due to careful outbreeding over several generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crossbreeding - the breeding of animals or plants from different breeds or varieties
  • antonym: inbreeding - the mating of closely related individuals
