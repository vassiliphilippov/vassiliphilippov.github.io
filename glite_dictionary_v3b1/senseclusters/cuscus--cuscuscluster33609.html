cuscus

noun
arboreal marsupial of New Guinea and Australia
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkʊsˌkʊs / (AmE), / ˈkʊs.kəs / (BrE)
a woolly-haired, tree-dwelling marsupial native to New Guinea and northern Australia, resembling a monkey in appearance

Examples

A cuscus in trees
Farmers in Papua New Guinea sometimes spot a cuscus eating leaves high in the trees.
Researchers observed a cuscus climbing quietly among the branches at dusk.
The cuscus is known for its slow movements and thick, woolly fur.

Related Terms

  • synonym: possum - a tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia and nearby islands
