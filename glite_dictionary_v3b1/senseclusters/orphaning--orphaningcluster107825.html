orphaning

noun
loss of parents
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrfənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːfənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making someone an orphan, especially by the loss of parents

Examples

Orphaning of children
The war led to the orphaning of many children.
Natural disasters can result in the sudden orphaning of entire families.
The orphaning of young animals in the wild often reduces their chances of survival.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adoption - the act of legally taking another's child as one's own
  • synonym: bereavement - the state of having lost a loved one through death
