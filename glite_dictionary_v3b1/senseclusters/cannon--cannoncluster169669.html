cannon

noun
cylindrical arm armor plate (historical)
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkænən / (AmE), / ˈkanən / (BrE)
(historical) A cylindrical piece of armor plate designed to protect the arm, especially during the Middle Ages.

Examples

Medieval cannon armor
In the armorer’s workshop, a fresh cannon was being shaped from a sheet of steel.
Restorers discovered a rusted cannon among the remains of the medieval suit of armor.
The knight wore a cannon over his forearm to shield against sword blows.
