faucet

noun
device for controlling water flow
1.0 4.28/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îs…™t / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀês…™t / (BrE)
A device for controlling the flow of liquid, especially water, from a pipe or container.

Examples

Kitchen faucet
He turned on the faucet and waited for the water to heat up.
Please make sure the faucet is tightly closed after you use it.
The kitchen faucet has been leaking for days, and we need to fix it.

Synonyms

  • tap
    1.0 4.12/m
    a tap is the common British word for a water valve on sinks and pipes, while a faucet is the American term.
  • spigot
    5.5 0.63/m
    a spigot is often a small outdoor or barrel valve used for hoses or casks, while a faucet is a household water fixture.
  • turncock
    9.9 0.00/m
    a turncock is a valve operated by turning a handle, often old-fashioned or technical, while a faucet is the usual sink valve.

Related Terms
