signer

noun
creator of a digital signature
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.nər/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.nə/ (BrE)
(in computing and cryptography) an entity (a person, device, or process) that creates a digital signature to authenticate or authorize data or messages.

Examples

Digital signer
The server checked the signer's certificate before accepting the uploaded file.
In the blockchain network each transaction includes a signer to confirm authenticity.
Developers must ensure the private key used by a signer remains secure at all times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authenticator - an entity or mechanism that proves identity or authenticity, often by creating or verifying credentials
