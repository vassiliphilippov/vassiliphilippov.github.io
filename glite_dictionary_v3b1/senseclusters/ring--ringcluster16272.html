ring

noun
rigid circular band used for fastening or hanging
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
a small circular band, usually of metal, worn as jewelry on the finger or used for fastening, hanging, or identifying (such as on birds)

Examples

A gold ring
He attached the bucket to a metal ring hanging from the well.
The curtain was held back by a wooden ring fastened to the wall.
The old barn door had a heavy iron ring for pulling it open.
After several years, the information stored on the ring revealed valuable details about the bird’s life.
Each migrating stork was fitted with a ring so scientists could track its journey.
The ornithologists checked the ring on the bird’s leg to see where it had been tagged.
He gave her a gold ring as a symbol of their engagement.
She wore a sparkling diamond ring on her left hand.
The antique ring was passed down through generations of her family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: band - a thin strip of material put around something, such as for identification; also used of birds
  • synonym: bracelet - jewelry worn around the wrist
  • synonym: hoop - a circular band of metal, wood, or other material, often used for holding things together
