sika

noun
East Asian deer with forked antlers
9.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêk…ô / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêk…ô / (BrE)
A small deer native to East Asia, especially Japan, with slightly forked antlers, noted for its spotted coat.

Examples

A sika deer
Several sika were spotted grazing near the edge of the forest.
The sika is known for its distinctive white spots that remain throughout adulthood.
Unlike the red deer, the sika has slightly forked antlers and a smaller build.

Synonyms

  • Cervus sika
    9.3 0.00/m
