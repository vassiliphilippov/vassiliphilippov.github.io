metaphysical

adjective
highly abstract or overly theoretical
4.8 7.8
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈfɪ.zɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /me.tə.ˈfɪ.zɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
(of an idea, argument, or reasoning) highly abstract or overly theoretical, often lacking practical grounding.

Examples

Metaphysical ideas
Her arguments became increasingly metaphysical and detached from real-world concerns.
The discussion quickly turned metaphysical, with little connection to practical outcomes.
The professor's lecture was criticized for being too metaphysical to be useful for new students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theory or ideas
  • synonym: theoretical - concerned with or involving the theory of a subject rather than its practical application
