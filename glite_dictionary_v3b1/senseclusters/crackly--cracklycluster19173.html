crackly

adjective
dry and brittle
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrækli / (AmE), / ˈkrækli / (BrE)
making small, sharp, crisp, or popping noises, like something dry or brittle being moved or touched

Examples

Crackly radio sound
The old radio made a crackly sound whenever she tried to tune it.
He enjoyed the crackly texture of the toasted bread with his soup.
Autumn leaves became crackly underfoot as the weather grew colder.
The old vinyl record played with a crackly sound throughout the song.
We sat by the crackly fire, listening to the wood pop and hiss.
Her voice was crackly on the phone due to a bad connection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crackling - making a series of short, sharp noises
  • synonym: crispy - firm and easily broken or crunched
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface or texture
