to give, provide, supply, or formally hand over something (such as a service, payment, thanks, or property)

Examples

To render a service
She refused to render the confidential documents to the investigating authority.
The army was forced to render the captured fort to the enemy at sunrise.
Under the treaty, the government must render the escaped fugitives to their country of origin.
It may take several hours for the board to render its final decision.
The court rendered judgment in favor of the plaintiff.
The judge will render a verdict after considering all the evidence.
The land rendered enough crops to feed three families.
The new law will render benefits to all qualifying citizens.
This agreement renders substantial income for the company every year.

Synonyms

  • provide
    1.5 478/m
    provide means to give what is needed or wanted, while render often means to formally give services, payments, or property.
  • supply
    1.6 37/m
    supply is to make things available, often in quantity or regularly, while render focuses on formally giving services or payments.
  • give
    1.0 783/m
    give means to hand or offer something directly to someone, while render often means to provide or formally hand over services or payments.
  • deliver
    3.0 16/m
    deliver means to hand over or bring something to a person or authority, while render often means formally giving services or property.
  • furnish
    3.7 9.62/m
    furnish means to provide furniture or necessary items for a place, while render covers giving services, payments, or property more broadly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surrender - to give up possession or control to another
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something
