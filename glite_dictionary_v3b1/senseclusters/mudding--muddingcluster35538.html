mudding

noun
drywall finishing process
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of applying joint compound to drywall seams and screw holes to create a smooth wall surface

Examples

Drywall mudding
After hanging the drywall, the next step is mudding all the seams and screw holes.
He spent the weekend mudding the new walls to prepare them for painting.
Good mudding skills are essential for achieving a smooth, professional finish on drywall.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: painting - the act of applying paint to a surface after mudding and sanding
  • synonym: taping - the process of applying tape to drywall joints before mudding
