kick back

verb
recoil or spring backward suddenly
Pronunciation: / kɪk bæk / (AmE), / kɪk bæk / (BrE)
(of a machine or device) to suddenly recoil or spring backward, usually as a result of a forceful thrust or impact.

Examples

The gun kicks back
Be careful, because that old rifle tends to kick back pretty hard.
The chainsaw can kick back if it hits a hard knot in the wood.
When he fired the shotgun, it kicked back and nearly knocked him off his feet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recoil - to spring back upon firing or release; to suddenly move back
