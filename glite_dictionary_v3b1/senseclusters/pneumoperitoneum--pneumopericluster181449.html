pneumoperitoneum

noun
gas in abdominal cavity
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːmoʊˌpɛrɪtəˈniːəm / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməʊˌpɛrɪtəˈniːəm / (BrE)
the introduction of gas into the peritoneal cavity, usually to create space for abdominal surgery such as laparoscopy

Examples

Surgical pneumoperitoneum
The surgeon created a pneumoperitoneum to allow better access during the laparoscopic procedure.
A pneumoperitoneum is often established using carbon dioxide gas before minimally invasive abdominal surgery.
Proper monitoring is essential when a pneumoperitoneum is induced to avoid complications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laparoscopy - a surgical procedure using a camera inserted into the abdomen
