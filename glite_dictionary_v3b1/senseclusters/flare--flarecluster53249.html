flare

noun
reddened area of skin spreading from a focus
Pronunciation: / flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
a sudden reddening of the skin or a recurrence/worsening of symptoms of a disease

Examples

Skin flare after allergy
A characteristic flare developed after the patient was exposed to the allergen.
The doctor noticed a flare around the site of the insect bite.
The nurse monitored the flare to check if the redness was spreading rapidly.
Allergy flares are common in the spring due to pollen exposure.
Patients with rheumatoid arthritis may experience a flare after stopping their medication.
Stress can trigger a flare in people with chronic skin conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relapse - a return of symptoms after a period of improvement
  • antonym: remission - a period during which symptoms reduce or disappear
