huntsman's cup

noun
a bog plant with pitcher-shaped leaves and dark red flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌntsmənz kʌp / (AmE), / ˈhʌntsmənz kʌp / (BrE)
A perennial bog plant of northeastern North America, with dark red flowers and decumbent, broadly winged pitcher-shaped leaves forming a rosette; also found naturalized in parts of Europe, especially Ireland.

Examples

The huntsman's cup plant
Botanists carefully observed the huntsman's cup to study the insects trapped inside its pitcher leaves.
In Ireland, the huntsman's cup has become naturalized and is often seen in damp meadows.
The huntsman's cup thrives in peat bogs, where its striking dark red flowers stand out amongst the mosses.
