borscht circuit

noun
resort area in the Catskills for Jewish guests and entertainers
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔːrʃt ˌsɜːrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːʃt ˌsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
(informal) a group of resorts in the Catskill Mountains of New York, known for being frequented primarily by Jewish guests and for fostering a thriving entertainment scene, especially for comedians and performers.
