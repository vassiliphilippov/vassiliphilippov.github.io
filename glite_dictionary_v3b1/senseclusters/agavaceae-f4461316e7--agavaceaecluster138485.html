Agavaceae

noun
family of agave and related plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌɡævəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / əˌɡævəˈsiː.iː /; / əˌɡæv.əˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of tropical, drought-resistant plants (including agaves), sometimes classified with Amaryllidaceae or Liliaceae

Examples

Agavaceae plant family
Many species in the Agavaceae family are native to arid regions of the Americas.
Recent studies have led to the reclassification of Agavaceae into other plant families.
The blue agave, well known for tequila production, belongs to the Agavaceae.
Botanists have long debated whether certain genera belong in the Agavaceae or Liliaceae.
Many economically important plants, such as the sisal, are members of the Agavaceae.
The Agavaceae family includes many succulents adapted to arid environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Amaryllidaceae - family of flowering plants including amaryllis and others, sometimes containing Agavaceae in broader classifications
  • synonym: Liliaceae - family of lily and related plants, sometimes including Agavaceae in older classifications
