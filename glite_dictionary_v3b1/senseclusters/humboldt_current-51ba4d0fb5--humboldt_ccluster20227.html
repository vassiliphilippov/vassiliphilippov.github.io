Humboldt current

noun
cold ocean current off South America
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌmˌboʊlt ˈkʌrənt / (AmE), / ˈhʌmbəʊlt ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a cold ocean current that flows northward along the Pacific coast of South America before veering westward into the Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Humboldt current
Changes in the Humboldt current can have a significant impact on the weather patterns of western South America.
Many species of fish thrive in the Humboldt current due to its high productivity.
The Humboldt current is responsible for the cool, nutrient-rich waters off the coast of Peru.
