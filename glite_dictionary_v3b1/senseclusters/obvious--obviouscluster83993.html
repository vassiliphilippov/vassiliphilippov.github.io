obvious

adjective
easily perceived or understood
1.0 91/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêbvi…ôs / (AmE), / Àà…íbvi…ôs / (BrE)
Easily perceived or understood; clear and apparent.

Examples

An obvious fact
It was obvious that he had forgotten her birthday.
She pointed out the most obvious errors in my essay.
The solution to the puzzle is not immediately obvious.

Synonyms

  • clear
    1.0 225/m
    clear is easy to understand or see in many situations, while obvious often stresses that something is plainly noticeable.
  • evident
    2.1 27/m
    evident is shown by facts or signs and reads as proof, while obvious is a more general word for something clearly seen.
  • apparent
    2.8 33/m
    apparent seems clear from the way things look or feel, while obvious often shows itself without doubt or proof.
  • plain
    1.0 14/m
    plain looks simple and easy to notice, while obvious usually means something is easily seen or understood by anyone.
  • crystal clear
    2.5 1.75/m
    crystal clear sounds strong and vivid in speech, while obvious describes something plainly true or easy to see.
  • unmistakable
    3.9 4.43/m
    unmistakable is so clear that it cannot be confused with anything else, while obvious just means plainly noticeable.
  • self-evident
    4.8 3.10/m
    self-evident needs no proof because it seems true on its face, while obvious is clear but may need explanation.
  • clear-cut
    4.2 2.45/m
    clear-cut gives a firm, definite result or choice, while obvious describes what is simply easy to see or understand.
  • straightforward
    3.7 9.15/m
    straightforward is simple to follow or do, often about tasks, while obvious is about facts people easily notice or understand.
  • conspicuous
    5.1 5.54/m
    conspicuous attracts attention by standing out in a crowd, while obvious means easy to understand or plainly visible to people.
