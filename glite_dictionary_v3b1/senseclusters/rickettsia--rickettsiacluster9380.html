rickettsia

noun
small bacteria causing diseases like typhus
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈket.sɪ.ə/ /; / /rɪ.ˈket.sjə/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈket.sɪ.ə/ /; / /rɪ.ˈket.sjə/ (BrE)
Any of a group of very small rod-shaped bacteria that live in biting arthropods, such as ticks and mites, and cause disease in vertebrate hosts, including typhus and other febrile illnesses in humans.

Examples

Rickettsia bacteria group
Rickettsia are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected ticks or mites.
Some species of rickettsia can cause severe illnesses such as Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
The doctor suspected a rickettsia infection after the patient returned from a hiking trip with a high fever.
