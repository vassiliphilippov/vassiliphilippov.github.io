bodkin

noun
blunt needle for threading ribbon
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑd.kɪn /; / ˈbɑːdkɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒd.kɪn /; / ˈbɒdkɪn / (BrE)
a small tool, usually blunt or pointed, used in sewing for threading ribbon, tape, or cord, or for making holes in fabric or leather

Examples

Sewing bodkin
A bodkin makes it much easier to pull elastic through a narrow casing.
She used a bodkin to thread the new ribbon through the waistband of the skirt.
The sewing kit contained a bodkin along with other useful tools.
A bodkin is essential for certain sewing projects.
She used a bodkin to thread the elastic through the waistband.
The leatherworker made a clean hole with his bodkin.
