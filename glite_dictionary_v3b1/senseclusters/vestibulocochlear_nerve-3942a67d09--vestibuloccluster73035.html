vestibulocochlear nerve

noun
cranial nerve for hearing and balance
6.3
Pronunciation: / vɛˌstɪbjəloʊˈkoʊkliɚ nɜrv / (AmE), / vɛˌstɪbjʊl.əʊˈkəʊkli.ə nɜːv / (BrE)
the eighth cranial nerve, responsible for transmitting sound and balance information from the inner ear to the brain

Examples

The vestibulocochlear nerve function
Damage to the vestibulocochlear nerve can result in hearing loss and dizziness.
Doctors tested her vestibulocochlear nerve function after she reported symptoms of vertigo.
The vestibulocochlear nerve plays a crucial role in both hearing and maintaining balance.
