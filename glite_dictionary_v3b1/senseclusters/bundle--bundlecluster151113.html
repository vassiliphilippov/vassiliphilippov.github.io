bundle

noun
a collection of items tied together
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌn.dl̩ /; / ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.dl̩ /; / ˈbʌndl̩ /; / ˈbʌnd(ə)l / (BrE)
a group of things tied, wrapped, or packaged together; a collection or package (including software or other items grouped as one)

Examples

A bundle of sticks
Please leave the bundle of sticks by the door.
She carried a heavy bundle of newspapers under her arm.
The old man tied his clothes into a neat bundle before leaving the house.
Before departing, he tied his clothes into a small bundle and placed it on the cart.
She carried a bundle of newspapers under her arm as she walked down the street.
We received a bundle of documents in the mail this morning.
The developers released a bundle of tools for the project.
She downloaded a security bundle that installed several utilities at once.
App stores often sell software in bundles at a discounted price.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heap - a pile or mass of things, especially disorderly
  • synonym: package - an object or group of objects wrapped in paper or packed in a box
  • synonym: parcel - an object or collection of objects wrapped in paper, usually to be sent by mail
  • synonym: software package - a collection of computer programs or libraries distributed together
