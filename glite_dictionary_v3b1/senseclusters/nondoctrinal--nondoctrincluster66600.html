nondoctrinal

adjective
not based on doctrine
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɑkˈtraɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɒkˈtraɪ.nəl / (BrE)
not based on, related to, or influenced by official doctrine or established beliefs

Examples

A nondoctrinal viewpoint
The committee welcomed nondoctrinal viewpoints to encourage open discussion.
Her analysis was strictly nondoctrinal, focusing on practical outcomes rather than beliefs.
They sought a nondoctrinal approach to interpreting the historical events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: doctrinal - based on or relating to official doctrine or beliefs
  • synonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
