betatron

noun
type of electron particle accelerator
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛtəˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈbiːtəˌtrɒn / (BrE)
a type of particle accelerator that increases the speed of electrons using an electric field generated by a changing magnetic field

Examples

A betatron accelerator
A betatron is used in physics laboratories to accelerate electrons to nearly the speed of light.
Engineers studied the magnetic fields required for a betatron to operate efficiently.
The invention of the betatron marked a breakthrough in producing high-energy X-rays for medical imaging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclotron - a particle accelerator in which charged particles are propelled by a constant magnetic field and a rapidly varying electric field
  • synonym: synchrotron - a type of circular particle accelerator in which the magnetic field increases with particle energy
