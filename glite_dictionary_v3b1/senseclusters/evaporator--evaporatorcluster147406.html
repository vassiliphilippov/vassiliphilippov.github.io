evaporator

noun
liquid-to-vapor device
4.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvæpəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈvæpəreɪtə /; / ɪˈvæpəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a device or machine that removes liquid by turning it into vapor, used in cooling, industrial, or laboratory settings

Examples

Air conditioner evaporator
The air conditioner uses an evaporator to cool the air inside the room.
Technicians checked the evaporator for leaks during the maintenance of the cooling system.
In many refrigerators, the evaporator is hidden behind the back panel.
The evaporator in the air conditioner absorbs heat from the room.
Technicians checked the evaporator for leaks during the maintenance visit.
A faulty evaporator can cause the refrigerator to stop cooling properly.
The chemist placed the solution in the evaporator to remove excess solvent.
A rotary evaporator is commonly used in organic chemistry labs.
After using the evaporator, the sample was ready for further analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condenser - a device that turns vapor into liquid
  • synonym: distiller - a device that separates substances by heating and cooling
  • synonym: rotary evaporator - a type of evaporator that rotates samples to remove solvent efficiently
