preemergent

noun
weed-preventing chemical
8.0
Pronunciation: /priː.ɪ.ˈmɜːr.dʒənt/ /; / /priː.ə.ˈmɜːr.dʒənt/ (AmE), /priː.ɪ.ˈmɜː.dʒənt/ /; / /priː.ə.ˈmɜː.dʒənt/ (BrE)
a chemical, especially an herbicide, applied to soil before plants emerge to prevent weed seeds from germinating

Examples

Apply a preemergent
Gardeners often use a preemergent in early spring to stop weeds before they start growing.
Applying a preemergent can help keep your lawn free of unwanted plants throughout the season.
The landscaper recommended a preemergent to prevent crabgrass from taking over the yard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: herbicide - a chemical used to kill or control unwanted plants
  • antonym: postemergent - a chemical applied after plants have emerged
