round

noun
regular route for duty, esp. police or sentry
Pronunciation: / raʊnd / (AmE), / raʊnd / (BrE)
a stage, section, or set of visits or activities in a sequence, such as in a game, competition, or as part of a job

Examples

A round of interviews
Every evening, the nurse makes her round to check on each patient.
He grew familiar with the neighborhood while walking his round as a young constable.
The security guard finished his round before returning to the office.
In each round, the teams switch between offense and defense.
The fighters rested briefly between each round of the boxing match.
The match lasted twelve rounds before a winner was declared.
Each round of the competition will have different challenges for the participants.
The hiring process consists of several rounds of interviews.
The meetings are held every month in a regular round.
