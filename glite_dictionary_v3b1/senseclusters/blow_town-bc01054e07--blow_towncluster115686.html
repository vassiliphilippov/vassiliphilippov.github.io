blow town

verb
leave quickly (informal, often to escape trouble)
9.2
Pronunciation: / bloʊ taʊn / (AmE), / bləʊ taʊn / (BrE)
to leave a place quickly, especially in order to avoid trouble or escape a situation

Examples

To blow town fast
He had to blow town when the police started asking questions about him.
If things get too heated, sometimes it's best just to blow town and start over.
They planned to blow town the same night after the job was done.
