glamorize

verb
make glamorous or attractive
2.8 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈglæm.ə.raɪz/ (AmE), /ˈglæm.ə.raɪz/ (BrE)
to make something or someone seem more attractive, exciting, or appealing than it really is, often in an exaggerated or romanticized way

Examples

To glamorize a job
She tried to glamorize the job to make it more appealing to applicants.
The movie glamorizes the lives of criminals, presenting them as exciting and stylish.
The photoshoot glamorized a simple kitchen, turning it into a dreamy space.
He didn't want to glamorize war, preferring to show its tragic consequences.
Many movies tend to glamorize the lives of criminals, ignoring the harsh realities.
The article glamorized the fast-paced lifestyle but failed to mention its drawbacks.
Magazines often glamorise celebrity culture, making it seem perfect and effortless.
The film tends to glamorise the criminal lifestyle, ignoring its harsh realities.
The new advertising campaign glamorises fast food despite concerns about health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or attractive
  • synonym: embellish - to make something more attractive by adding details
  • synonym: glamorize - to make something appear glamorous or attractive (American spelling)
  • synonym: romanticize - to make something seem more attractive or ideal than it really is
