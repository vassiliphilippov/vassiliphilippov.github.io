chill

noun
sudden cold sensation (often illness-related)
1.5
Pronunciation: / tʃɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪl / (BrE)
a feeling of coldness, often from temperature or illness

Examples

A winter chill
A sharp chill signaled the onset of the flu.
She felt a sudden chill run down her spine before the fever started.
The doctor asked if he had experienced any chill or other symptoms of illness.
A sudden chill filled the room when the window was opened.
She wrapped her coat tighter to protect herself from the evening chill.
There was a noticeable chill in the air as autumn approached.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: coolness - a state or sensation of being slightly cold
  • degree higher: frost - extreme cold, often causing freezing
  • synonym: shiver - an involuntary shaking as a result of cold or fear
  • antonym: warmth - a state or sensation of being warm
