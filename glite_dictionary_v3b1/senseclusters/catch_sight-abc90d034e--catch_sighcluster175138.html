catch sight

verb
see briefly or unexpectedly
2.5
Pronunciation: / kætʃ saɪt / (AmE), / kætʃ saɪt / (BrE)
to see something briefly or unexpectedly, often only for a short moment

Examples

To catch sight of a bird
As the train left the station, we caught sight of the city skyline glowing in the sunset.
Out of the window, I caught sight of a fox darting across the field.
She caught sight of her friend in the crowd before losing her again.
