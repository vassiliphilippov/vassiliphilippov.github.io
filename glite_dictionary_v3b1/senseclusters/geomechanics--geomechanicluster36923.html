geomechanics

noun
study of soil and rock forces
8.1
Pronunciation: /dʒiː.oʊ.mə.ˈkæn.ɪks/ (AmE), /dʒiː.əʊ.mə.ˈkæn.ɪks/ (BrE)
the scientific study of how soils and rocks behave under mechanical forces, especially for engineering purposes

Examples

Modern geomechanics study
Modern geomechanics helps engineers predict how tunnels will behave during construction.
A strong understanding of geomechanics is essential for safe mining operations.
Researchers in geomechanics analyze landslides to improve disaster prevention methods.
Engineers analyzed the geomechanics of the site before starting construction on the new bridge.
Understanding geomechanics is essential for designing safe tunnels and underground structures.
The course in geomechanics covers both soil and rock behavior under different conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geotechnics - the application of scientific methods to engineering problems involving earth materials
  • synonym: rock mechanics - the study of the behavior of rock materials under force
  • synonym: soil mechanics - the study of the properties and behavior of soil under force
