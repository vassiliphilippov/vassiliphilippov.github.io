superphosphate

noun
phosphorus fertilizer
6.3
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.ˈfɑːs.feɪt/ /; / /suː.pər.ˈfɔːs.feɪt/ (AmE), /suː.pə.ˈfɒs.feɪt/ (BrE)
a fertilizer produced by treating phosphate rock with acid to make phosphorus more available to plants

Examples

Spread superphosphate fertilizer
The farmer spread superphosphate on the fields to boost the growth of his crops.
Many commercial fertilizers contain superphosphate as a key ingredient for plant nutrition.
Applying superphosphate can help improve soil fertility in areas with low phosphorus levels.
