pine leaf aphid

noun
adelgid insect that feeds on pines and spruces
9.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /paɪn liːf ˈæ.fɪd/ /; / /paɪn liːf ˈeɪ.fɪd/ (AmE), /paɪn liːf ˈeɪ.fɪd/ /; / /paɪn liːf ˈæ.fɪd/ (BrE)
a type of adelgid insect (Pineus pinifoliae) that infests the needles of pines and spruces, feeding on their sap

Examples

pine leaf aphid infestation
A heavy infestation of pine leaf aphid can cause the needles on young pines to yellow and fall.
Foresters are concerned about the spread of pine leaf aphid in commercial tree plantations.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the population of the pine leaf aphid.
