revolutionary

adjective
advocating or involved in revolution
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
involving, supporting, or characteristic of a complete and dramatic change, especially in politics or society

Examples

Revolutionary army
During the uprising, revolutionary forces gained control of the capital.
His revolutionary ideas inspired thousands to join the movement.
The revolutionary group called for the overthrow of the government.
His revolutionary ideas challenged the traditional ways of thinking in science.
The country underwent revolutionary changes after the new government took power.
The declaration marked a revolutionary moment in the nation's history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - holding to traditional attitudes; resistant to change, especially abrupt change
  • synonym: radical - favoring extreme change or reform, especially in politics
  • antonym: reactionary - opposing political or social progress or reform
