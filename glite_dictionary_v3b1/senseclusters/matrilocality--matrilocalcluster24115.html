matrilocality

noun
living with wife's family
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmætrɪloʊˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪləʊˈkælɪti / (BrE)
a social system in which a married couple lives with or near the wife's family after marriage

Examples

Marriage matrilocality custom
In some cultures, matrilocality ensures that daughters remain close to their mothers after marriage.
Anthropologists study matrilocality to understand family structures in different societies.
The tradition of matrilocality can influence inheritance and social roles within a community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neolocality - a social system in which a married couple establishes a new, independent household
  • antonym: patrilocality - a social system in which a married couple lives with or near the husband's family
