sunscald

verb
to damage by sunlight
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌnˌskɔld / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌskɔːld / (BrE)
to damage a plant or surface by exposing it to intense or prolonged direct sunlight.

Examples

To sunscald the bark
If you remove the tree's lower branches too quickly, the bark may sunscald in the summer heat.
Gardeners often use cloth to prevent young plants from being sunscalded during hot afternoons.
Leaving your car outside all day can sunscald the dashboard and fade the upholstery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scorch - to burn the surface of something with heat or sunlight
  • antonym: shade - to protect from direct sunlight
