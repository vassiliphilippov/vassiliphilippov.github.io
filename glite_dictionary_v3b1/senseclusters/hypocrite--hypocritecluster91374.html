hypocrite

noun
person pretending to hold beliefs or values
4.6 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhɪp.ə.krɪt/ (AmE), /ˈhɪp.ə.krɪt/ (BrE)
A person who pretends to have moral standards or beliefs to which their own behavior does not conform.

Examples

A hypocrite person
He accused politicians of being hypocrites for not following their own advice.
No one likes a hypocrite who says one thing and does another.
She called him a hypocrite for criticizing others while engaging in the same behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phony - a person who is not genuine
  • antonym: sincere person - someone who genuinely believes what they profess
