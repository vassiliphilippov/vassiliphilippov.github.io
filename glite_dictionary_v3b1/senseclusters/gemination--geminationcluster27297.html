gemination

noun
doubling of a word or phrase for effect
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the repetition of a word or phrase immediately, especially for rhetorical or emphatic effect

Examples

Gemination of a phrase
In poetry, gemination can emphasize important ideas by repeating key phrases.
Shakespeare often employs gemination such as 'farewell, farewell' for dramatic emphasis.
The use of gemination creates a rhythmic effect that draws the reader's attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repetition - the act of repeating something that has already been said or written
