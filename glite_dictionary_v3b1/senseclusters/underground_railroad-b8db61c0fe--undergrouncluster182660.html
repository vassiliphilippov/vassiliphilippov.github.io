Underground Railroad

noun
clandestine network helping migrants
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.graʊnd ˈreɪl.roʊd/ /; / /ˈʌn.dər.graʊnd ˈreɪ.roʊd/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.ɡraʊnd ˈreɪl.rəʊd/ (BrE)
A secret or clandestine network that helps migrants or refugees move across borders to safety; used metaphorically with reference to the historical Underground Railroad.

Examples

Modern Underground Railroad
Humanitarian groups set up an informal Underground Railroad to help asylum seekers reach safety across the border.
Journalists described the network of safe houses as a modern Underground Railroad for migrants fleeing violence.
Volunteers warned they risk prosecution by running an illegal Underground Railroad across several countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clandestine network - a secret organization or set of routes that helps people move covertly
