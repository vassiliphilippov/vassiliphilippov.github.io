storm

verb
to rain, hail, or snow hard with wind (weather)
4.0
Pronunciation: / stɔrm / (AmE), / stɔːm / (BrE)
(of weather) to rain, snow, or blow very hard with strong winds, often with thunder or lightning

Examples

It storms all night
If it storms tonight, we will postpone the outdoor concert.
It was storming so badly that the streets quickly flooded.
The forecast says it may storm for most of the afternoon.
Last night, it stormed so fiercely that we lost power for hours.
The weather forecast predicts that it will storm this weekend.
We stayed inside and played games while it stormed outside.
