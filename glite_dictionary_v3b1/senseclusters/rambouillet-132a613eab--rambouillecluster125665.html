Rambouillet

noun
breed of sheep known for wool and mutton
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌræmbuːˈjeɪ / (AmE), / ˌræmbuːˈjeɪ / (BrE)
a hardy breed of sheep descended from merino, known for producing both fine wool and quality mutton

Examples

A Rambouillet sheep
A flock of Rambouillets can supply both fine wool and tender meat.
Many ranchers raise Rambouillet for their adaptability to harsh climates.
The Rambouillet is prized among farmers for its high-quality wool.
