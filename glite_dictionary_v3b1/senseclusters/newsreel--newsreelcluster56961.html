newsreel

noun
short film of current events
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːzˌriːl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌriːl / (BrE)
a short film or video segment reporting current events, typically shown in cinemas before television became widespread

Examples

A newsreel film
Before movies, audiences would watch a newsreel to catch up on recent events.
Historians often use newsreels to study public reactions to historic events.
The old newsreel footage gave us a glimpse of life during World War II.

Related Terms

  • synonym: documentary - a film or television program presenting factual information about real events
