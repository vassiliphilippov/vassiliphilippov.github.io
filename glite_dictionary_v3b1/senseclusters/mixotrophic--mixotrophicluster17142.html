mixotrophic

adjective
both autotrophic and heterotrophic
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪksəˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəˈtrəʊfɪk / (BrE)
able to obtain energy by both photosynthesis and consuming other organisms

Examples

Mixotrophic organisms
Certain protists are mixotrophic, using both sunlight and organic material for energy.
Researchers discovered a mixotrophic species of algae in the lake.
The mixotrophic nature of these microorganisms allows them to survive in changing environments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: autotrophic - able to produce food from inorganic substances
  • degree lower: heterotrophic - obtaining food by consuming other organisms
