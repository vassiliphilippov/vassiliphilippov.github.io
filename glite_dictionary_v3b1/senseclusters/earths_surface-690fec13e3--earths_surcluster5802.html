Earth's surface

noun
outermost layer of the planet Earth
Pronunciation: / ɝːθs ˈsɝː.fɪs / (AmE), / ɜːθs ˈsɜː.fɪs / (BrE)
the outermost layer of the planet Earth, including land and sea, where terrestrial and aquatic life exists

Examples

Earth's surface layer
About 70% of the Earth's surface is covered by oceans.
Scientists monitor changes on the Earth's surface to understand climate patterns.
The Earth's surface is constantly changing due to erosion and tectonic activity.
