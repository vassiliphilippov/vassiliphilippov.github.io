resurface

verb
to appear again on the surface
4.2 1.88/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàs…úrf…™s /; / riÀàs…úrf…ôs / (AmE), / riÀàs…úÀêf…™s / (BrE)
to appear or become visible again after being hidden, absent, or inactive (literally or figuratively)

Examples

To resurface after years
After hours without a trace, the missing diver finally resurfaced near the boat.
Old rumors sometimes resurface when new evidence comes to light.
The whale will resurface several times before diving deep again.
After several years away from the music scene, the singer finally resurfaced with a new album.
He vanished for months but resurfaced unexpectedly at his sister's wedding.
Rumors about the old scandal resurfaced during the election campaign.

Synonyms

  • reemerge
    4.1 0.98/m
    reemerge suggests coming back into view or activity after a long absence, while resurface emphasizes becoming visible again.
  • reappear
    2.7 6.66/m
    reappear means appearing again in the same place or time, while resurface often stresses returning after hiding or inactivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emerge - to come out into view or become visible
  • synonym: reappear - to appear again after disappearing
  • antonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
