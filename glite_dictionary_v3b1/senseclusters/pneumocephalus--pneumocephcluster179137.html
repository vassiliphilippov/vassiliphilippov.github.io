pneumocephalus

noun
air in the skull
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːmoʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which air is present within the cranial cavity or skull

Examples

pneumocephalus after injury
The CT scan revealed pneumocephalus after the patient suffered a head injury.
Surgery was required to treat the pneumocephalus caused by the skull fracture.
Doctors monitored the pneumocephalus closely to ensure the air would be absorbed naturally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrocephalus - a condition where fluid accumulates in the brain
