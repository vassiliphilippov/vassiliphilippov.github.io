mouth

verb
form words silently with lips
Pronunciation: / maʊθ / (AmE), / maʊθ / (BrE)
to form words with the lips without making a sound, or to say something insincerely

Examples

To mouth the words
During the play, the actor mouthed the words while the real singer performed backstage.
She mouthed 'thank you' across the crowded room so her friend would know she was grateful.
When the teacher looked away, he mouthed a joke to his classmates and tried not to laugh.
During the play, the actor mouthed the final lines dramatically.
He mouthed an apology from across the room, hoping she would understand.
She silently mouthed the words, so nobody would hear.
