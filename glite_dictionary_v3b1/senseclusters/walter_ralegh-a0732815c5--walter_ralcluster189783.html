Walter Ralegh

proper noun
English courtier and explorer (1552–1618)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔltər ˈreɪli / (AmE), / ˈwɔːltə ˈrɑːli / (BrE)
(1552–1618) An English courtier, explorer, and writer, a favorite of Elizabeth I, who attempted to found English colonies in America and is credited with introducing potatoes and tobacco to England.

Examples

Walter Ralegh explorer
Walter Ralegh was a key figure in England's early attempts to colonize North America.

The adventurous life of Walter Ralegh has inspired countless stories and plays.
