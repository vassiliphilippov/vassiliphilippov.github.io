phytoalexin

noun
plant defense compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪtoʊəˈlɛksɪn / (AmE), / ˌfaɪtəʊəˈlɛksɪn / (BrE)
a substance produced by plants that helps defend against harmful microorganisms such as bacteria or fungi

Examples

Plant phytoalexin production
When attacked by fungi, the plant produces more phytoalexin to protect itself.
Scientists study phytoalexin levels to understand how plants resist disease.
The discovery of a new phytoalexin could help improve crop resistance to bacteria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antimicrobial - a substance that kills or inhibits the growth of microorganisms
  • degree lower: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living organisms
