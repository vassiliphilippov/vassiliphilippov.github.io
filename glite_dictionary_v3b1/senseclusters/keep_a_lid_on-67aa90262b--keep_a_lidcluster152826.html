keep a lid on

verb
control or suppress (something)
Pronunciation: / kip ə lɪd ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp ə lɪd ɒn / (BrE)
to control or suppress something, especially to prevent it from becoming known or out of control

Examples

To keep a lid on costs
It's important to keep a lid on costs if we want the project to succeed.
She struggled to keep a lid on her frustration during the meeting.
The government worked hard to keep a lid on the story to avoid public panic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to the activities of (a person, body of persons, etc.)
