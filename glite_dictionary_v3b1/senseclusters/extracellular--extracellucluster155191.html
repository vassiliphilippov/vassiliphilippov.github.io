extracellular

adjective
situated outside a cell (biology)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.strəˈsɛl.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈsɛl.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
Located or occurring outside a cell or cells, especially in a biological context.

Examples

Extracellular fluid
Scientists are studying extracellular vesicles for their potential in disease diagnosis.
The extracellular matrix provides structural support to surrounding cells.
White blood cells move through extracellular spaces as they fight infection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intracellular - located or occurring within a cell or cells
