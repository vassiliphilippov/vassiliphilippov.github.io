wettable

adjective
can be wetted
5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈwet.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈwet.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
able to be easily covered or soaked with water or other liquid (including surfaces, materials, or powders that can be wetted and dispersed)

Examples

A wettable surface
The new coating makes the fabric highly wettable for better cleaning.
A wettable surface allows water to spread evenly across it.
Some powders are not wettable and will float on water instead of mixing.
The wettable powder dissolved quickly when added to the water in the spray tank.
For best results, use a wettable formulation that mixes evenly without clumping.
Some pesticides are sold as wettable powders to ensure they disperse well in liquid applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquid easily
  • antonym: water-repellent - not easily penetrated by water
