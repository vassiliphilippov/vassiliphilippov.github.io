intervene

verb
become involved to change a situation
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvin / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈviːn / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation in order to alter or prevent what is happening, often by force or threat of force

Examples

To intervene in a fight
If no one intervenes, the conflict between the two groups could escalate further.
The government may intervene to stabilize the economy during a financial crisis.
When the argument got out of hand, the teacher had to intervene quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to a situation or event
  • synonym: interfere - to become involved in a situation where your involvement is not wanted or is unnecessary
  • synonym: mediate - to try to settle a dispute by intervening as a neutral party
