half-pay

noun
reduced wage for non-full-time work
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhæf ˌpeɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌpeɪ / (BrE)
a reduced wage paid to an employee, often in the military, who is not working full time but remains on the payroll

Examples

Half-pay military salary
After leaving active service, the officer was placed on half-pay until reassignment.
She managed her household carefully while her husband received only half-pay from the army.
Some workers agreed to remain on half-pay during the temporary closure of the factory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: full pay - the regular wage paid to someone working full time
