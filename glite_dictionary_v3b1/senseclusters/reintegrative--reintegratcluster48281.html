reintegrative

adjective
restoring unity
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪntɪɡrətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the process of making something whole or unified again after separation or division

Examples

A reintegrative approach
The reintegrative approach helps former prisoners adjust to life outside of jail.
Therapists use reintegrative methods to help families heal after conflict.
A reintegrative program can support students returning to school after a long absence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divisive - causing disagreement or separation
  • synonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health or well-being
