critical

adjective
decisive or turning-point (situation, moment)
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪtɪkəl /; / ˈkrɪd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪkəl /; / ˈkrɪt.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
extremely important or at a decisive or dangerous point, especially in a crisis or for survival

Examples

Critical moment
Making the right decision at this critical juncture could save the company.
The patient's condition became critical overnight, requiring immediate attention.
We are approaching a critical moment in the peace negotiations.
The patient is in a critical condition and requires constant monitoring.
The wildfire has reached a critical stage, prompting an evacuation order.
There is a critical lack of clean water after the hurricane.
Access to critical resources was the key to their survival.
It is critical that we meet the project deadline to secure funding.
The hospital is in urgent need of critical equipment for the emergency room.
