annual parallax

noun
apparent shift of celestial object due to Earth's orbit
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.ju.əl pəˈræ.læks / (AmE), / ˈæn.ju.əl pəˈræl.æks / (BrE)
The apparent shift in the position of a celestial object, measured from two points in Earth's orbit around the Sun, used to determine its distance from Earth.

Examples

The annual parallax shift
By observing the annual parallax, scientists determined the vast distance to Alpha Centauri.
Modern telescopes can measure the annual parallax of even very distant stars.
The annual parallax of a star helps astronomers measure its distance from Earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parallax - the apparent displacement of an object because of a change in the observer's point of view
