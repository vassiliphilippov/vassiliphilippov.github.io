self-preservation

noun
instinct to protect oneself from harm
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˌprɛzɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌselfˌprɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the natural instinct or act of protecting oneself from harm or danger

Examples

Self-preservation instinct
His sense of self-preservation kicked in when he saw the wild animal approaching.
In times of crisis, self-preservation often becomes our top priority.
She acted out of pure self-preservation, avoiding situations that might put her at risk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: self-destruction - behavior or actions leading to one's own harm or ruin
  • synonym: survival - the act or fact of continuing to live or exist
