wiretapper

noun
person who intercepts communications
5.6 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪr.tæp.ər/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪ.ə.tæp.ə/ (BrE)
a person who secretly listens to telephone or telegraph conversations by using a hidden listening device

Examples

A wiretapper listens secretly
A skilled wiretapper can intercept phone calls without being detected.
It is illegal for a private citizen to act as a wiretapper without a warrant.
The police arrested the wiretapper after months of secret surveillance.
