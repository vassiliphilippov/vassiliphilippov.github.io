supermolecule

noun
very large complex molecule
Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.pərˈmoʊ.lɪˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈməʊ.lɪ.kjuːl / (BrE)
a large and complex molecule composed of multiple smaller molecules, typically found in living organisms

Examples

A supermolecule in biology
DNA is often cited as a classic example of a biological supermolecule.
Proteins are one type of supermolecule essential for life.
Researchers are studying how a supermolecule can self-assemble in biological systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macromolecule - a molecule containing a very large number of atoms, such as a protein or nucleic acid
