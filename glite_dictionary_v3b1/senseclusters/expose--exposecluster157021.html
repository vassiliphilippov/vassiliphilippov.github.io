expose

verb
make visible by removing a cover
4.2 2.4
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspoʊz/ /; / /ek.ˈspoʊz/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspəʊz/ /; / /ek.ˈspəʊz/ (BrE)
to make something visible or apparent by uncovering or revealing it

Examples

To expose the floor
Lifting the old carpet exposed the polished wooden floor beneath.
The archaeologists carefully exposed the ancient mosaic by removing layers of soil.
The photographer waited for the clouds to pass and expose the mountain peak to the sunlight.
He accidentally exposed the photograph to the light, ruining the film.
The documentary will expose the harsh living conditions of the workers.
The investigation aimed to expose the truth behind the company's financial dealings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to hide something so it cannot be seen or found
  • synonym: reveal - to make something known or visible that was hidden
