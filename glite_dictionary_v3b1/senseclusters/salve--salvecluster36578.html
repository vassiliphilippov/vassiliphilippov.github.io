salve

verb
apply a healing ointment
Pronunciation: / sæv / (AmE), / sɑːv / (BrE)
to soothe, comfort, or heal pain or distress, either physically or emotionally; or to apply ointment to a wound

Examples

To salve a wound
After the rash appeared, she immediately salved her skin to soothe the irritation.
Ancient remedies often involved salving wounds with herbal ointments.
The nurse carefully salved the child's scraped knee to prevent infection.
She tried to salve his disappointment with encouraging words.
A kind gesture can sometimes salve the pain of a difficult loss.
He salved his conscience by donating to charity after making a mistake.
She tried to salve his hurt feelings with kind words.
A warm cup of tea can sometimes salve a troubled mind.
He hoped to salve his guilt by apologizing for his mistake.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravate - to make a problem or pain worse
  • synonym: anoint - to smear or rub with oil or an oily substance, especially for ritual purposes or healing
  • synonym: comfort - to make someone feel better when they are sad or worried
  • degree higher: heal - to make or become healthy or whole again
  • synonym: soothe - to make someone feel calmer or less upset
