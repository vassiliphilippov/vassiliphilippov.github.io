moralize

verb
improve someone's morals by advice
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrəˌlaɪz /; / ˈmɔːrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlaɪz /; / ˈmɒrəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to give moral advice or express judgments about what is right or wrong, often in a way that seems like lecturing

Examples

To moralize to others
It's easy to moralize when you're not facing the same challenges as others.
Some parents attempt to moralize their children's behavior through stories about right and wrong.
The teacher tried to moralize the students, encouraging them to act with honesty and integrity.
He tends to moralize whenever the conversation turns to politics.
Instead of offering practical advice, she chose to moralize about our choices.
Readers may find the author begins to moralize rather than let the story speak for itself.
If you keep moralising every discussion, people will stop listening to you.
It's annoying when people moralise about the choices of others without understanding their situation.
She couldn't help but moralise after hearing about his reckless behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condone - to accept or allow behavior that is morally wrong
  • synonym: lecture - to give a long, serious talk intended to criticize or instruct
  • synonym: preach - to give moral advice, especially in a tiresome way
  • synonym: sermonize - to deliver moral or religious lectures; to preach
