Medicare

noun
US federal health insurance for the elderly
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdɪˌkɛr / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪˌkeə / (BrE)
A federal system of health insurance in the United States for people aged 65 and older and certain younger people with disabilities.

Examples

Medicare benefits
After turning 65, she became eligible for Medicare benefits.
Enrollment in Medicare is an important step for older adults in the US.
Many Americans rely on Medicare to help cover their medical expenses after retirement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Medicaid - A US government program providing health insurance for people with low incomes
