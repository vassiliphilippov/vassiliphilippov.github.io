creed

noun
system of beliefs or principles
4.5 5.92/m
Pronunciation: / krid / (AmE), / kriÀêd / (BrE)
a set of beliefs or principles, especially religious ones, that guide a person or group

Examples

A creed of beliefs
Despite their different backgrounds, the staff all shared a common creed of respect and integrity.
He lived by a simple creed: always tell the truth, no matter what.
Many organizations have a written creed that outlines their guiding values.
Each denomination interprets its own creed in a slightly different way.
He could not accept a creed that excluded people of other faiths.
The Nicene creed is recited during the service every Sunday.

Synonyms

  • credo
    7.5 1.50/m
    a credo is a personal statement of guiding principles people follow, while a creed names the wider set of group beliefs.
  • religious doctrine
    4.5 0.57/m
    religious doctrine is an official set of faith teachings taught by a church or school, while a creed guides a group's beliefs.
  • doctrine
    6.0 32/m
    a doctrine is a taught belief system used by institutions or thinkers, while a creed is a stated set of guiding beliefs for people.
  • gospel
    5.0 6.70/m
    the gospel is the core authoritative teachings a religious group accepts and proclaims, while a creed is a formal summary of beliefs.
  • dogma
    7.7 4.72/m
    dogma is a belief declared unquestionable by leaders or authorities, while a creed is a guiding set of beliefs people accept.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held by a group, especially in a church or religion
  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • antonym: heresy - belief or opinion that goes against the established doctrine
