flysch

noun
marine sedimentary rocks
Pronunciation: / fliʃ / (AmE), / fliʃ / (BrE)
a sequence of sedimentary rocks deposited rapidly in deep marine environments, often associated with mountain building

Examples

Layers of flysch
The Alps contain thick layers of flysch that reveal the region's geological history.
Researchers collected samples from the flysch to analyze ancient marine environments.
The presence of flysch indicates a period of rapid sedimentation during mountain formation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: molasse - sedimentary rocks formed in shallow water after flysch deposition
  • synonym: turbidite - a type of sedimentary deposit formed by underwater landslides
