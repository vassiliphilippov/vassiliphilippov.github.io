redshirt

verb
to delay athletic eligibility
7.7
Pronunciation: /red.ˈʃɜːrt/ (AmE), /red.ˈʃɜːt/ (BrE)
to delay an athlete's participation in competitions for a season to extend their period of eligibility

Examples

To redshirt a player
The coach decided to redshirt the talented freshman so he could develop his skills for another year.
She chose to redshirt her first season after recovering from an injury.
Many athletes redshirt to gain an extra year of eligibility and improve their performance.
The coach decided to redshirt the freshman so he could develop his skills before competing.
Many athletes choose to redshirt their first year to adjust to college-level sports.
She was redshirted after her injury to preserve a year of eligibility.
The coach decided to redshirt the talented freshman to give him more time to develop.
Many athletes choose to redshirt their first year to extend their college careers.
After his injury, the team opted to redshirt him for the season so he could recover fully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bench - to keep a player out of competition
  • antonym: play - to participate in a game or competition
