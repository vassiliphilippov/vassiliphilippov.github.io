self-sown

adjective
growing naturally from fallen seed
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈsoʊn / (AmE), / ˌselfˈsəʊn / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing from seed that has fallen and germinated naturally, without being deliberately planted by humans.

Examples

Self-sown tomato plants
After last year’s harvest, several self-sown tomatoes sprouted unexpectedly in the compost pile.
Many wildflowers in the meadow are self-sown and appear each spring without any help.
The self-sown seedlings quickly took over the garden beds by summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: volunteer - a plant that grows without having been deliberately planted
