turn on

verb
activate a device or machine
1.0 23/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn …ën / (AmE), / t…úÀên …ín / (BrE)
to cause a machine or device to begin operating by pressing a button or flipping a switch

Examples

To turn on the lights
Please turn on the lights when you enter the room.
She forgot to turn on the oven before baking the cake.
You can turn on the radio by pressing that button.

Synonyms

  • switch on
    1.6 3.13/m
    switch on makes a device start by pressing a button or moving a switch, while turn on describes the same action.
  • switch
    1.0 9.26/m
    switch means causing a device to go on often by moving a switch, while turn on is a more general phrase.
  • start up
    3.0 1.68/m
    start up means beginning a machine or system, often by starting its engine or program, while turn on focuses on switching it on.
  • activate
    3.6 20/m
    activate means make something work or active, often in technical settings, while turn on usually means switching a device on.
  • fire up
    3.5 1.68/m
    fire up means start an engine or computer in an informal way, while turn on is the neutral everyday verb.
  • flip the switch
    3.5 0.36/m
    flip the switch means quickly move a switch to activate something, while turn on is the general action of starting a device.

Related Terms

  • antonym: turn off - to stop a machine or device from operating
