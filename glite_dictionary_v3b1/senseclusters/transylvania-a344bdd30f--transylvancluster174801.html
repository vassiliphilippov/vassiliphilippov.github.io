Transylvania

proper noun
historical region in northwestern Romania
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzəlˈveɪniə / (AmE), / ˌtrænzɪlˈveɪniə / (BrE)
a historical region in northwestern Romania, noted for its mountainous terrain and cultural history; formerly part of Hungary and incorporated into Romania after World War I.

Examples

Transylvania region
After the First World War, Transylvania became part of Romania.
The legend of Dracula is famously associated with Transylvania.
Tourists flock to Transylvania for its gothic castles and scenic landscapes.
