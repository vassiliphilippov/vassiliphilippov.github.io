landsmanshaft

noun
immigrant aid society
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlændzmənʃɑft /; / ˈlændzmənˌʃɑft / (AmE), / ˈlændzmənʃɑːft /; / ˈlændzmənˌʃɑːft / (BrE)
a mutual aid society or association formed by immigrants from the same town or region, especially among Jewish communities, often providing support such as funeral arrangements and cemetery plots

Examples

A landsmanshaft society
The landsmanshaft provided support to newcomers adjusting to life in the city.
Many immigrants joined a landsmanshaft to connect with others from their hometown.
Through the landsmanshaft, families found assistance with housing and employment.
The local landsmanshaft helped new immigrants by offering funeral arrangements and community support.
Many families joined a landsmanshaft to ensure they would have a proper burial.
The landsmanshaft organized annual memorial services for its members.
