dika

noun
African tree with edible fruit and oil-rich seed
8.5 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdiː.kə/ /; / /ˈdɪ.kə/ (AmE), /ˈdiː.kə/ /; / /ˈdɪ.kə/ (BrE)
an African tree (Irvingia gabonensis) bearing edible yellow fruit resembling mangos, valued for its oil-rich seed and termite-resistant wood

Examples

African dika tree
Farmers plant dika to harvest both the fruit and the valuable oil-rich seeds.
The dika tree produces fruit that is an important food source in many African communities.
The hard wood of the dika is often used for making furniture due to its resistance to termites.
