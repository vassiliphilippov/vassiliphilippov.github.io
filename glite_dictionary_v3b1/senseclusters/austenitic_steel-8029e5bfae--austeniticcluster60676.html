austenitic steel

noun
non-magnetic, corrosion-resistant steel type
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːstəˈnɪtɪk stil / (AmE), / ˌɔːstɪˈnɪtɪk stiːl / (BrE)
A type of steel containing enough nickel and chromium or manganese to retain austenite at atmospheric temperatures, known for its corrosion resistance and non-magnetic properties.

Examples

Austenitic steel medical instruments
Medical instruments are typically manufactured using austenitic steel for hygiene and strength.
Stainless cookware is often made from austenitic steel because of its resistance to rust and corrosion.
The welders specified austenitic steel due to its durability in high-temperature environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: martensitic steel - a type of steel that forms martensite on cooling, usually harder and more brittle
