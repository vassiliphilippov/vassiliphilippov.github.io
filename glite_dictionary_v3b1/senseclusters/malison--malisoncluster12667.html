malison

noun
archaic curse
Pronunciation: / ˈmælɪsən / (AmE), / ˈmælɪsən / (BrE)
an old-fashioned word for a curse or expression of a wish that harm befall someone

Examples

A malison cast
The old legend tells of a malison cast upon the land by a vengeful spirit.
He feared that his enemy's malison would bring misfortune to his family.
In ancient tales, a malison could only be broken by a brave hero.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blessing - a prayer or wish for good fortune and happiness
  • synonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke harm or punishment
