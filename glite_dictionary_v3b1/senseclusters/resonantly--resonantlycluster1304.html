resonantly

adverb
with deep sound
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈre.zən.ənt.liː/ (AmE), /ˈre.zən.ənt.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that produces a deep, clear, strong, or reverberating sound

Examples

Spoke resonantly on stage
The actor spoke resonantly, filling the theater with his powerful voice.
The organ played resonantly during the ceremony, creating a moving atmosphere.
Her laughter echoed resonantly through the empty hall.
The singer's voice echoed resonantly through the grand hall, captivating the entire audience.
When struck, the bell rang resonantly, filling the air with a deep, lasting tone.
The violin strings vibrated resonantly as the musician played the final note of the piece.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dully - in a way that lacks brightness or resonance
  • synonym: sonorously - in a way that is full, deep, or rich in sound
  • synonym: vibrantly - in a lively or resonant manner
