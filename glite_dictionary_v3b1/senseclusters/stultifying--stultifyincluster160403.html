stultifying

adjective
making seem foolish
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌltɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstʌltɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
causing something or someone to become dull, boring, or lacking in energy, enthusiasm, or effectiveness

The endless paperwork had a stultifying effect on the team's creativity.

The stultifying routine of his job left him feeling uninspired and bored.
A stultifying set of regulations prevented any real progress on the project.
The meeting was so stultifying that several people struggled to stay awake.
