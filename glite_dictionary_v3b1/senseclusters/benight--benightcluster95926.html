benight

verb
envelop in darkness or ignorance
9.1 9.9
Pronunciation: /biː.ˈnaɪt/ /; / /bɪ.ˈnaɪt/ (AmE), /bɪ.ˈnaɪt/ (BrE)
to envelop with intellectual, social, or moral darkness; to make ignorant or unenlightened

Examples

To benight a people
Centuries of isolation served to benight the rural communities, leaving them far behind the rest of the country.
Misleading propaganda can easily benight a population if left unchecked.
The regime sought to benight its citizens by restricting access to information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge and understanding
