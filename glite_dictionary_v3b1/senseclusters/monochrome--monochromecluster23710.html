monochrome

noun
black-and-white or single-color photograph
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnəˌkroʊm /; / ˈmɑnəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəkrəʊm / (BrE)
a picture, photograph, or artwork made using only one color or different shades of a single color

Examples

A monochrome photo
A simple monochrome can sometimes convey more emotion than a color photograph.
He prefers shooting in monochrome to capture the mood of the city streets.
The old family album is full of stunning monochromes from the early 1900s.
He prefers to work in monochrome rather than using a full spectrum of colors.
In the gallery, a large monochrome dominated the central wall.
The artist created a striking monochrome using only shades of blue.
