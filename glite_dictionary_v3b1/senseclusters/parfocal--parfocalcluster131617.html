parfocal

adjective
lenses stay focused
9.3
Pronunciation: /pɑːr.ˈfoʊ.kəl/ /; / /pər.ˈfoʊ.kəl/ (AmE), /pɑː.ˈfəʊ.kəl/ /; / /pə.ˈfəʊ.kəl/ (BrE)
describing lenses or optical devices that remain in focus when the magnification or focal length is changed

Examples

Parfocal microscope lenses
The parfocal objectives on the microscope allowed students to switch magnifications without losing focus.
Using parfocal lenses saves time during laboratory work because you do not need to refocus each time.
The camera's parfocal zoom lens kept the subject sharp as I changed the focal length.
