singular

noun
word form denoting one item
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
The form of a word used to refer to one person, thing, or concept, as opposed to more than one.

Examples

The singular of 'cats'
Nouns in English typically add an 's' to change from singular to plural.
The word 'child' is in its singular form, while 'children' is the plural.
You should write the answer using the singular, not the plural, of the noun.
