draw back

verb
hold open edges of a wound with an instrument
Pronunciation: / drɔ ˈbæk / (AmE), / drɔː ˈbak / (BrE)
to use a surgical instrument to hold open the edges of a wound or an organ during a procedure

Examples

To draw back the skin
Once the area was exposed, the assistant carefully drew back the skin to allow access to the organ.
Special forceps were used to draw back the edges of the wound for better visibility.
The surgeon instructed the nurse to draw back the tissue using the retractor.
