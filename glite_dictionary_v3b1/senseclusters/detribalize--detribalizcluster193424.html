detribalize

verb
cause loss of tribal identity/culture
9.5
Pronunciation: / diˈtraɪbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈtraɪbəlaɪz /; / diˈtraɪbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause members of a tribe or a community to lose their traditional culture, customs, or social structure, especially as a result of outside influence

Examples

To detribalize indigenous people
Colonial policies often sought to detribalize indigenous peoples by discouraging their traditional practices.
Efforts to detribalize the region had lasting effects on the community's cultural heritage.
Missionary schools played a key role in attempts to detribalize local children.
Government policies attempted to detribalise indigenous communities through education and relocation programs.
Some argue that rapid urbanization can unintentionally detribalise rural societies, leading to the loss of ancestral heritage.
The missionaries' efforts in the region aimed to detribalise the local population by replacing traditional customs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acculturate - to cause to adopt the cultural traits of another group
  • synonym: assimilate - cause to become similar to the dominant culture
