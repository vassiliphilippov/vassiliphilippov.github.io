transversality

noun

a technical condition in mathematics and economics that ensures solutions to certain problems behave properly at infinity or boundaries

Examples

Transversality condition
The economist checked the transversality condition to ensure the model's solution was valid over time.
Without transversality, the optimization problem could produce unrealistic results at infinity.
In advanced mathematics, transversality helps guarantee that solutions do not diverge at the boundaries.
