judicator

noun
automated decision-maker
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a device, system, or computer program that evaluates inputs and makes automated decisions or judgments

Examples

Automated judicator system
The bank uses a judicator to automatically approve or reject loan applications.
An advanced judicator can process thousands of data points in seconds to make a decision.
Many companies rely on a judicator to ensure fair and consistent hiring practices.
