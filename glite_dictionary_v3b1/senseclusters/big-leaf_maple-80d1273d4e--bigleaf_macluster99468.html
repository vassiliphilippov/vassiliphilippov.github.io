big-leaf maple

noun
maple tree with large leaves, western North America
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪɡ liːf ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡ liːf ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A species of maple tree native to western North America, known for its large five-lobed leaves that turn orange in autumn.

Examples

The big-leaf maple tree
In autumn, the leaves of the big-leaf maple turn vivid shades of orange and yellow.
Many animals find shelter under the broad canopy of the big-leaf maple.
The big-leaf maple is easily identified by its enormous leaves, which can be the size of dinner plates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maple - a tree or shrub with lobed leaves, used for timber or syrup
