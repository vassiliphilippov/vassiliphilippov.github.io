seclude

verb
to keep away from others
6.7 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / s…™Ààklud / (AmE), / s…™ÀàkluÀêd / (BrE)
to keep someone or oneself away from other people; to isolate

Examples

To seclude oneself
After her surgery, she chose to seclude herself from visitors until she felt better.
Monks often seclude themselves from society to focus on meditation and prayer.
The author decided to seclude himself in a mountain cabin while working on his novel.

Synonyms

  • isolate
    4.2 16/m
    isolate often means making someone completely separate for safety or study, while seclude suggests private withdrawal for peace.
  • sequester
    8.1 1.04/m
    sequester is used to set someone apart for a specific legal or formal reason, while seclude means private withdrawal.
  • shut away
    5.5 0.10/m
    shut away means putting someone in a place where they cannot leave, while seclude means keeping someone apart by choice.
  • cloister
    8.6 0.11/m
    cloister means to shut away in a quiet, religious place, while seclude is a more general private separation.
  • keep apart
    3.2 1.13/m
    keep apart means stopping people or things from meeting or mixing, while seclude means withdrawing one person for privacy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expose - to make visible or accessible to others; not keep secluded
  • synonym: isolate - to set apart or keep separate from others
  • synonym: sequester - to remove or withdraw into solitude or retirement
