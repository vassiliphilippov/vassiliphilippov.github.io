long-beard

noun
17th-century stoneware drinking jug
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌbɪəd / (BrE)
A stoneware drinking jug with a long neck, often decorated with a caricature of Cardinal Bellarmine; popular in the 17th century.

Examples

A long-beard jug
Collectors prize the long-beard for its distinctive bearded face and historical value.
