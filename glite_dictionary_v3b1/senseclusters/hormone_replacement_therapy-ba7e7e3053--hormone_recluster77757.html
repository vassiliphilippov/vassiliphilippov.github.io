hormone replacement therapy

noun
gender‑affirming hormone treatment
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhɔːr.moʊn rɪ.ˈpleɪs.mənt ˈθer.ə.piː/ /; / /ˈhɔːr.mən rɪ.ˈpleɪs.mənt ˈθer.ə.pɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhɔː.məʊn rɪ.ˈpleɪs.mənt ˈθe.rə.piː/ (BrE)
(in gender‑affirming medicine) a medical regimen of hormones given to transgender people to develop or maintain secondary sexual characteristics consistent with their gender identity.

Examples

Hormone replacement therapy for transition
Many transgender people begin hormone replacement therapy to align their bodies with their gender identity.
Her doctor discussed risks before starting hormone replacement therapy as part of transition care.
The clinic offers counselling and medical exams for those seeking hormone replacement therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gender-affirming hormone therapy - medical hormone treatment used to align a person's physical characteristics with their gender identity
  • synonym: hormone therapy - the use of hormones as a medical treatment; in context, often refers to hormone treatments for gender transition
