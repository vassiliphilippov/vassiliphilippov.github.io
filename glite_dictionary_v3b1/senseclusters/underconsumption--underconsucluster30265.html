underconsumption

noun
insufficient buying
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərkənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəkənˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
a situation in which people buy less than what is produced, resulting in surplus goods or services

Examples

Widespread underconsumption
Economic crises can sometimes be caused by widespread underconsumption in the population.
The factory faced losses due to underconsumption of its products in the market.
Some economists argue that underconsumption leads to unemployment and slow growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overconsumption - the act of consuming more than is needed or produced
  • synonym: overproduction - the production of more goods than can be sold
