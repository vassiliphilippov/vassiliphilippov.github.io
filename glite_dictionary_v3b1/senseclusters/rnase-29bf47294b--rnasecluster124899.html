RNase

noun
enzyme that breaks down RNA
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrˌɛnˌeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɑː ˌɛn ˌeɪ z / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of ribonucleic acid (RNA) into smaller components

Examples

Purified RNase enzyme
Researchers used purified RNase to degrade the RNA in their samples.
The presence of RNase activity was confirmed by measuring the breakdown of RNA substrates.
Treatment with RNase ensured that only DNA remained for analysis.
