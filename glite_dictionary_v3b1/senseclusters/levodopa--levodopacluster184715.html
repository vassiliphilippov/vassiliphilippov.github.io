levodopa

noun
drug for Parkinson's disease
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌliːvoʊˈdoʊpə / (AmE), / ˌlɛvəˈdəʊpə / (BrE)
a drug used in the treatment of Parkinson's disease, consisting of the levorotatory form of dopa

Examples

Levodopa therapy
Many patients experience an improvement in movement after starting levodopa therapy.
Research shows that levodopa remains the most effective drug for managing motor symptoms in Parkinson's disease.
The neurologist prescribed levodopa to help control the patient's tremors.
