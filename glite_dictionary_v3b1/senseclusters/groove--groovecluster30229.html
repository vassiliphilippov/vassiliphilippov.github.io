groove

verb
to dance to music
3.9 0.50/m
Pronunciation: / …°ruÀêv / (AmE), / …°ruÀêv / (BrE)
to enjoy music and move your body in time with its rhythm, often by dancing or swaying

Examples

To groove to music
Everyone was grooving to the beat at the concert.
She loves to groove when her favorite song comes on.
The crowd started grooving as soon as the band began to play.

Synonyms

  • get one's groove on
    8.6 0.10/m
    get one's groove on means dancing with strong, fun energy in a lively way, while groove means enjoying music and moving in rhythm.
  • boogie
    7.5 0.45/m
    boogie is energetic dancing to upbeat music, while groove often describes moving smoothly and enjoying the rhythm.
  • boogie on down
    8.8 0.01/m
    boogie on down urges lively, energetic dancing at a party, while groove suggests a more relaxed, rhythmic sway to music.
  • bop
    6.1 0.11/m
    bop is playful, quick dancing with sharp steps, while groove is more about feeling and moving smoothly to the beat.
  • dance
    1.0 43/m
    dance means moving your body to music in many styles, while groove implies enjoying the music and moving in time.

  • antonym: stand still - to remain without moving
