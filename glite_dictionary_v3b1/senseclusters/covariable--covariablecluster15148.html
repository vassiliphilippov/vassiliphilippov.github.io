covariable

noun
predictive variable
9.1
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ˈver.ɪ.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ˈveə.rɪ.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
a variable that may influence or predict the outcome in a statistical analysis, often controlled for in research studies

Examples

A covariable in research
The researchers controlled for each covariable in their analysis.
Age and gender were included as covariables in the statistical model.
It is important to identify every relevant covariable before drawing conclusions from the data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: covariate - a variable that is possibly predictive of the outcome in statistical analysis
  • antonym: dependent variable - the outcome variable being studied in an analysis
  • synonym: independent variable - a variable that is manipulated to observe its effect on the outcome
