Objectivist

noun
believer in objective truth
9.1
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒɛktɪvɪst / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that truth and moral values exist independently of personal feelings and emphasizes objective facts or standards

Examples

An Objectivist thinker
As an Objectivist, she insisted that journalists separate personal views from factual reporting.
The debate between the Objectivist and the subjectivist highlighted their different approaches to ethics.
Many philosophers consider themselves Objectivists when discussing universal moral principles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and deals with it accordingly
  • antonym: subjectivist - a person who believes that knowledge and values depend on personal feelings or opinions
