ground glass

noun
hazy lung opacity on imaging

In radiology, a hazy area seen on lung imaging (especially CT) that increases density but still allows underlying blood vessels or airways to be seen; often called a ground-glass opacity.

Examples

Ground glass opacity
The chest CT showed diffuse ground glass opacities consistent with viral pneumonia.
Radiologists reported a small area of ground glass in the patient's right lower lung.
Early COVID-19 often produces bilateral ground glass patterns visible on high-resolution CT scans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consolidation - a denser area of lung on imaging that usually obscures vessels and airways; contrasts with ground-glass opacity.
  • synonym: ground-glass opacity - the medical term for a hazy lung opacity on CT or X-ray (often abbreviated GGO).
  • synonym: opacity (radiology) - an area on an imaging scan that appears denser than normal lung tissue.
