Taoist Trinity

noun
three principal gods of Taoism
Pronunciation: / ˈtaʊ ɪst ˈtrɪn ɪ ti / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ ɪst ˈtrɪn ɪ ti / (BrE)
In Taoism, the three supreme deities known as the Three Pure Ones: Yuanshi Tianzun, Lingbao Tianzun, and Daode Tianzun.

Examples

The Taoist Trinity deities
Many Taoist temples are dedicated to the Taoist Trinity, who are worshipped as the highest deities.
Scholars often study the symbolism and rituals connected to the Taoist Trinity to understand Taoist beliefs.
The Taoist Trinity represents different aspects of the cosmos and the path to spiritual enlightenment in Taoist doctrine.
