mainsheet

noun
rope controlling a sail's angle
6.6 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪnˌʃit / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnʃiːt / (BrE)
(Nautical) A rope or chain used to control the angle of the mainsail in relation to the wind on a sailing vessel.

Examples

Adjusting the mainsheet
Adjusting the mainsheet will change the shape and power of the mainsail.
Be careful not to let the mainsheet get tangled during a tack.
The crew tightened the mainsheet as the wind picked up to maintain speed.
