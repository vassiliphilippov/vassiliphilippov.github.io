moralization

noun
preaching or expressing moral judgments
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔrəlɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌmɔːrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing moral judgments or expounding a moral code, often in a preachy or superficial way

Examples

Public moralization
Critics accused the film of excessive moralization, which overshadowed the story.
His speech was full of moralization about society's need for honesty.
The moralization of every discussion became tiresome to the other members of the group.
Her speech was full of moralisation that left little room for differing opinions.
The movie avoids overt moralisation, allowing viewers to draw their own conclusions.
Too much moralisation can sometimes alienate an audience instead of inspiring them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moralizing - the act of commenting on issues of right and wrong, often self-righteously
  • synonym: sermonizing - giving moral advice in a preachy manner
