tachogram

noun
graph of heart rate over time
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈtæk.ə.ɡræm/ (AmE), /ˈtæk.ə.ɡræm/ (BrE)
A record or graph showing heart rate or consecutive inter-beat (RR) intervals plotted over time, used in cardiology and physiology to analyse heart-rate variability.

Examples

Heart tachogram
The cardiologist examined the patient's tachogram to assess beat-to-beat heart rate variability after the exercise.
The researchers compared tachograms recorded during sleep and wakefulness to study autonomic nervous system activity.
During the stress test, her tachogram showed rapid fluctuations indicating impaired variability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: RR tachogram - a plot of successive RR intervals (time between heartbeats) showing beat-to-beat heart-rate changes
