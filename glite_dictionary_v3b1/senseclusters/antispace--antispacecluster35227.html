antispace

noun
area around subjects
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæntiˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈæntiˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the area around and between the main subjects in an artwork or design, often used to create balance or focus

Examples

Antispace in art
The clever use of antispace in the painting draws the viewer's eye to the main subject.
Designers often manipulate antispace to create a sense of harmony in their layouts.
By increasing the antispace between elements, the poster became much easier to read.

Related Terms

  • synonym: negative space - the empty or open space around objects in art or design
  • antonym: positive space - the area occupied by the main subjects in an artwork
