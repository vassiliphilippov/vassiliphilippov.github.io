tineid

noun
small moth whose larvae feed on wool or fur
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪniɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɪniːɪd / (BrE)
A small yellowish moth of the family Tineidae, whose larvae feed on wool, fur, and other animal products.

Examples

A tineid moth
Collections of animal furs must be protected from tineid infestations to prevent loss.
She discovered that tineids had chewed holes in her vintage sweater.
The tineid is notorious for damaging wool clothing stored in dark closets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, typically nocturnal
