closure

noun
Gestalt tendency to complete incomplete figures
5.1 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkloʊ.ʒər/ (AmE), /ˈkləʊ.ʒə/ (BrE)
the tendency, according to Gestalt psychology, to perceive incomplete figures or patterns as complete by filling in gaps or making them whole

Examples

Gestalt closure
According to Gestalt psychologists, closure helps us see a circle even when part of its outline is missing.
Logo designers often use the principle of closure to suggest shapes without drawing every part.
Our brains apply closure to connect the dots and perceive a coherent image.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gestalt - psychological term for patterns perceived as unified wholes
