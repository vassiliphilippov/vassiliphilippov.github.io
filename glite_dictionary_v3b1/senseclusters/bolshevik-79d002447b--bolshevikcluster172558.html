Bolshevik

noun
member of early Russian Communist Party
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃəvɪk / (BrE)
a member of the Bolshevik faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party that seized power in Russia in 1917; more generally, a radical revolutionary

Examples

A Bolshevik leader
After the overthrow of the provisional government, the Bolsheviks established a socialist state.
Many Bolsheviks faced opposition from both internal and external enemies during the civil war.
The Bolsheviks played a pivotal role in the Russian Revolution of 1917.
During the early 20th century, the rise of the Bolsheviks changed the course of Russian history.
He was accused of being a Bolshevik after expressing his radical views at the meeting.
Some critics called the reforms Bolshevik in their extremity and opposition to tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Communist - advocate or supporter of communism, especially in Russia
  • antonym: Menshevik - member of the minority faction opposed to the Bolsheviks in the Russian Social Democratic Party
  • antonym: moderate - a person with moderate views, not extreme
  • synonym: radical - a person with extreme political or social views
