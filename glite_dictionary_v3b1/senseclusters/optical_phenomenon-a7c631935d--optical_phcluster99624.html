optical phenomenon

noun
event involving light
Pronunciation: / ˈɑp.tɪ.kəl fəˈnɑ.məˌnɑn / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tɪ.kəl fɪˈnɒ.mɪ.nən / (BrE)
A physical event or occurrence related to the nature, behavior, or properties of light.

Examples

A optical phenomenon in light
A rainbow is a well-known optical phenomenon caused by the refraction and reflection of sunlight in droplets of water.
Scientists study every optical phenomenon to better understand the properties of light.
The mirage on the highway is an optical phenomenon resulting from temperature differences in air layers.
