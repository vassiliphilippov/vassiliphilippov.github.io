Indian beech

noun
evergreen Asian tree with fragrant flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈbiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈbiːtʃ / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to Asia with glossy, pinnate leaves and clusters of fragrant, creamy-white flowers, often grown as a shade tree.

Examples

The Indian beech tree
Farmers appreciate the Indian beech for its ability to thrive in poor soil and harsh climates.
The Indian beech is commonly planted along roadsides for its broad canopy and shade.
The perfumed blossoms of the Indian beech attract bees and butterflies in the spring.
