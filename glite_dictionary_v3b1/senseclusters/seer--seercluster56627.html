seer

noun
game role who learns players' identities
8.6
Pronunciation: /siːr/ /; / /sɪər/ (AmE), /sɪə/ /; / /siːə/ (BrE)
(in social-deduction games) a player role that, typically during a night phase, can learn another player's true alignment or identity.

Examples

Seer role revealed
In the online Werewolf game, the seer revealed the guard's role to the town.
As seer, Maya accurately identified a vampire during the night phase to help the villagers.
Multiple seers were banned in the tournament because their powers unbalanced the social-deduction format.
