justifiability

noun
ability to be justified
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒʌstəˌfaɪəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌstəˌfaɪəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be shown as reasonable, right, or defensible

Examples

The justifiability of actions
The lawyer questioned the justifiability of the new policy in court.
Many people debated the justifiability of the company's actions after the incident.
The justifiability of spending so much money on the project was challenged by the board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legitimacy - the quality of being lawful or acceptable
  • antonym: unjustifiability - the quality of not being able to be justified
