open up

verb
talk freely and honestly about feelings
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ʌp / (BrE)
to begin to talk more freely and honestly about one's thoughts and feelings

Examples

To open up emotionally
After a few sessions with her therapist, she finally started to open up.
He doesn’t usually open up, but today he shared a lot about his childhood.
It can be difficult for some people to open up about their emotions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confide - to tell someone about a secret or private matter
