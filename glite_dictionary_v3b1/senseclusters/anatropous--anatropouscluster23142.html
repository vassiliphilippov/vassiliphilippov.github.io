anatropous

adjective
inverted (of plant ovule)
Pronunciation: / əˈnætrəpəs / (AmE), / əˈnætrəpəs / (BrE)
(Of a plant ovule) completely inverted so that it is turned back 180 degrees on its stalk, with the micropyle near the funiculus.

Examples

Anatropous plant ovule
Botanists distinguish anatropous from orthotropous ovules based on orientation.
The anatropous ovule is the most common type found in flowering plants.
Under the microscope, the anatropous structure is evident in this species' reproductive system.
