Central Time

noun
time zone for central US (UTC-6/UTC-5 DST)
4.8 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.trəl taɪm/ (AmE), /ˈsen.trəl taɪm/ (BrE)
the standard time of the 6th time zone west of Greenwich, used in the central United States and parts of Canada and Mexico, corresponding to UTC-6 during standard time and UTC-5 during daylight saving time.

Examples

Central Time zone
Broadcasts usually air an hour later in Central Time compared to Eastern Time.
She lives in Texas, which observes Central Time throughout the year.
The meeting is scheduled for 3 p.m. Central Time, so make sure to adjust your clocks accordingly.
