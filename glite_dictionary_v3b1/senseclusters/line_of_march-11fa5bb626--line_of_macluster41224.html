line of march

noun
route followed by an advancing group
5.5
Pronunciation: / laɪn əv mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / laɪn əv mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the route or path along which a group, especially a column of people or troops, proceeds in an organized advance

Examples

Army line of march
During the campaign, the army changed its line of march to avoid enemy detection.
Spectators gathered along the line of march to watch the procession pass by.
The parade organizers carefully planned the line of march to ensure all groups would fit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: route - way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination
