sulfation

noun
battery sulfate buildup
8.1
Pronunciation: /sʌl.ˈfeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /səl.ˈfeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sʌl.ˈfeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /səl.ˈfeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the formation or accumulation of sulfate crystals, especially on the plates of a lead–acid battery, reducing its performance

Examples

Battery sulfation
If sulfation occurs in a car battery, it can lead to a significant loss of power.
Regular maintenance can help prevent sulfation and extend the life of your battery.
Technicians checked the battery for signs of sulfation before recommending a replacement.
If a battery is left unused for too long, sulphation can occur and damage its performance.
Technicians checked the battery for signs of sulphation before attempting to recharge it.
Regular maintenance helps prevent sulphation and extends the life of lead-acid batteries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrosion - the gradual destruction of materials by chemical reaction
  • antonym: desulfation - the process of removing sulfate buildup from a battery
  • synonym: sulfation - the process of forming sulfate compounds, especially in batteries
