mandatory injunction

noun
court order requiring a specific act
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.dəˌtɔr.i ɪnˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈmæn.dəˌtɔː.ri ɪnˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (BrE)
A court order that requires a party to perform a specific act.

Examples

A mandatory injunction order
A mandatory injunction may be granted to force a landlord to carry out repairs.
The company was subject to a mandatory injunction compelling it to remove the illegal construction.
The judge issued a mandatory injunction requiring the city to restore the park.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prohibitory injunction - an injunction forbidding a specific act
