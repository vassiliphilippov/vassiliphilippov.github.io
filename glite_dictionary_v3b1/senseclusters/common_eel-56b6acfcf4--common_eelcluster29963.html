common eel

noun
freshwater eel species that spawns at sea
3.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëm…ôn il / (AmE), / Ààk…ím…ôn iÀêl / (BrE)
A species of eel that lives in freshwater during adulthood but migrates to the sea to spawn, found in Europe and America, marketed fresh and smoked.

Examples

The common eel fish
Conservationists are concerned about the declining populations of the common eel due to overfishing and habitat loss.
Many rivers in Europe are habitats for the common eel, which migrates thousands of miles to breed.
The chef prepared smoked common eel as a local delicacy for the festival.
