clear

verb
to let goods pass customs officially
2.4
Pronunciation: / klɪr / (AmE), / klɪə / (BrE)
to pass, approve, or authorize officially

Examples

To clear customs
If you want to clear your package quickly, make sure you pay all the required duties.
Only after the goods cleared customs were they transported to the warehouse.
The shipment cannot clear customs until all the documents are in order.
She refused to clear the files for release, citing confidentiality concerns.
The committee must clear the new policy before it is announced to the public.
The document cannot be published until legal has cleared it.
All passengers must clear customs before collecting their luggage.
It took her hours to clear security before boarding the international flight.
The goods cannot be delivered until they clear inspection at the port.
