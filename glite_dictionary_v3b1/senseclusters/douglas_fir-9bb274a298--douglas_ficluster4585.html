Douglas fir

noun
timber from a Douglas fir tree
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌɡləs fɜr / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs fɜː / (BrE)
the strong, durable timber of a Douglas fir tree, widely used in construction and carpentry.

Examples

Douglas fir wood
Builders often prefer Douglas fir for framing houses because of its resilience.
The beams were made from Douglas fir to ensure strength and durability.
The new deck was constructed using Douglas fir for its appealing grain and toughness.
