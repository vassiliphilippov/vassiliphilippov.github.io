symphony

noun
orchestral musical composition
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪm.fə.ni / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.fə.ni / (BrE)
a long, elaborate musical composition, typically for a full orchestra, usually consisting of several movements

Examples

A famous symphony
Each movement of the symphony has its own distinct character and mood.
She has composed her first symphony, which will premiere this autumn.
The orchestra performed Beethoven's Fifth symphony to a packed concert hall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concerto - a musical work for solo instrument(s) and orchestra
