unison

noun
simultaneous occurrence
Pronunciation: / ˈjuː nɪ sən /; / ˈjuːnəsən / (AmE), / ˈjuː nɪs ən /; / ˈjuːnɪsən / (BrE)
the state of doing or saying something at the same time as someone else; perfect agreement or simultaneous action

Examples

Speak in unison
Both children answered the question in unison, surprising their teacher.
The choir sang the song in unison, creating a powerful effect.
The crowd cheered in unison as the team scored a goal.
The audience clapped in unison after the performance ended.
The children recited the poem in unison during the assembly.
The soldiers marched in perfect unison across the parade ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony
  • synonym: harmony - the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes
