Paeonian

noun
extinct Balkan language
Pronunciation: / piˈoʊniən / (AmE), / piˈəʊniən / (BrE)
an extinct Indo-European language once spoken in the ancient region of Paeonia in the Balkans

Examples

Paeonian language
Very little is known about the vocabulary of Paeonian due to the scarcity of surviving texts.
Scholars debate whether Paeonian was closely related to other ancient Balkan languages.
The discovery of new inscriptions has provided fresh clues about the structure of Paeonian.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Thracian - an extinct Indo-European language once spoken in the Balkans
