observe

verb
watch attentively
2.4 76/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàz…ùv / (AmE), / …ôbÀàz…úÀêv / (BrE)
to watch, notice, or pay attention to something carefully, especially to learn or discover something

Examples

To observe animals
He likes to observe people at the park and guess their stories.
Please observe how she solves the problem before you try it yourself.
Scientists often observe animal behavior in the wild to understand their habits.
He observed the suspect from a distance without being noticed.
Scientists observe the behavior of wild animals to learn about their habits.
Security guards observed the building throughout the night using surveillance cameras.
From his window, he could observe the birds building their nest.
The children excitedly observed the comet as it streaked across the night sky.
The scientist was able to observe changes in the cells under the microscope.
