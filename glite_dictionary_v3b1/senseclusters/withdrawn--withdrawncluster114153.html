withdrawn

adjective
quiet, reserved; introspective
7.2
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈdrɔn / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːn / (BrE)
quiet and preferring to be alone rather than with other people; reserved and avoiding social interaction

Examples

A withdrawn person
Although he was withdrawn at first, he eventually opened up to his classmates.
His withdrawn nature made it difficult for him to make friends.
She became increasingly withdrawn after moving to a new city.
After moving to a new city, she felt withdrawn and isolated for several months.
Since the incident, he has become noticeably more withdrawn and rarely speaks to anyone.
The withdrawn child preferred reading alone to playing with classmates.

  • synonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotions or opinions; shy or restrained

  • synonym: unsociable - not enjoying or seeking the company of others
