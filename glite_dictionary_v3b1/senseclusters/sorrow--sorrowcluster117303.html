sorrow

noun
something causing deep unhappiness
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊ / (BrE)
a feeling of deep sadness or grief, especially caused by loss, disappointment, or misfortune

Examples

Deep sorrow
After hearing the tragic news, she was overcome with sorrow.
For many years, the memory of the accident remained his greatest sorrow.
The loss of his childhood home was a great sorrow to him.
Despite his sorrow, he tried to comfort his friends.
She felt a great sorrow after hearing the tragic news.
The poem speaks of sorrow and hope in the face of loss.
Expressions of sorrow filled the room during the memorial service.
She felt a deep sorrow after the loss of her beloved pet.
Time gradually eased the sorrow he carried after the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grief - intense sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness
