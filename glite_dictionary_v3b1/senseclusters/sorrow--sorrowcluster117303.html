sorrow

noun
something causing deep unhappiness
2.1 14/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ëÀêro ä / (AmE), / Ààs…ír…ô ä / (BrE)
a feeling of deep sadness or grief, especially caused by loss, disappointment, or misfortune

Examples

Synonyms

  • grief
    2.1 26/m
    grief is very deep sadness often after losing someone, while sorrow is a broader deep sadness from loss or disappointment.
  • mournfulness
    6.5 0.02/m
    mournfulness shows a visible, quiet sadness like in mourning, while sorrow is the inner feeling of deep grief or pain.
  • sadness
    1.0 14/m
    sadness is a general feeling of unhappiness or low mood, while sorrow is deeper and often linked to real loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grief - intense sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness
