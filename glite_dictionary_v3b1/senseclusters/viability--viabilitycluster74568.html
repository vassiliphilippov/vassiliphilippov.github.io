viability

noun
ability to grow and develop (biology)
Pronunciation: / ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the ability of a living thing, such as an embryo or organism, to develop or grow normally and survive

Examples

Viability of an embryo
Premature birth can sometimes threaten the viability of newborn animals.
Scientists tested the viability of the seeds stored in the laboratory.
The viability of the fetus was monitored carefully throughout the pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonviable - not capable of normal growth or development
