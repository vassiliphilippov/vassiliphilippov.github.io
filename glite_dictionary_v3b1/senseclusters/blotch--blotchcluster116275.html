blotch

verb
mark with spots or patches of color
5.3 0.25/m
Pronunciation: / blÉ‘tÊƒ / (AmE), / blÉ’tÊƒ / (BrE)
to mark with spots or irregular patches of different colors or shades, as if stained

Examples

To blotch the paper
If you use this marker on wet paper, it may blotch the surface unpredictably.
The artist blotched the canvas with dark blue and red to create a dramatic effect.
The paint began to blotch the wall after the rain leaked in.

Synonyms

  • splotch
    6.7 0.28/m
    splotch puts irregular blobs of paint or liquid on a surface, while blotch means a stained, uneven patch.
  • mottle
    8.0 0.62/m
    mottle gives a surface many small spots or streaks of different shades, while blotch names larger, more stain-like patches.
  • dapple
    7.2 0.78/m
    dapple makes a pattern of small light or dark spots, while blotch means irregular stained patches without a regular pattern.
  • splash
    4.1 1.33/m
    splash scatters liquid or color in sudden drops or marks, while blotch refers to a shaped stained patch.
