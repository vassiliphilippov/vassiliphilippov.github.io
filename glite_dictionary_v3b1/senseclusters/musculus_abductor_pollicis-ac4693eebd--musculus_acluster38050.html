musculus abductor pollicis

noun
a muscle moving the thumb away from the palm
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs æbˈdʌk.tɚ pəˈlɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs æbˈdʌk.tər pəˈlɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
An abductor muscle of the thumb that moves the thumb away from the palm of the hand.

Examples

Musculus abductor pollicis muscle
Damage to the musculus abductor pollicis may result in difficulty grasping objects.
Surgical repair of the musculus abductor pollicis can improve thumb function after injury.
The musculus abductor pollicis is essential for enabling thumb movement away from the hand.
