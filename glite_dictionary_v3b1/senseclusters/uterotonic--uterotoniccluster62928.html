uterotonic

noun
labor-inducing drug
8.6
Pronunciation: /juː.tə.rə.ˈtɑːn.ɪk/ (AmE), /juː.tə.rə.ˈtɒn.ɪk/ (BrE)
a drug or agent that stimulates contractions of the uterus, often used to induce labor or reduce bleeding after childbirth

Examples

Uterotonic drug
The doctor administered a uterotonic to help control bleeding after the birth.
A uterotonic is often given to induce labor in certain medical situations.
Midwives are trained to use uterotonics to prevent postpartum hemorrhage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxytocic - a drug that causes the uterus to contract
  • antonym: tocolytic - a drug that suppresses uterine contractions
