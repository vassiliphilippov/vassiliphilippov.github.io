windjammer

noun
large sailing ship with several masts
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndˌdʒæmər / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌdʒæmə / (BrE)
a large sailing ship, typically with several masts and square sails, used especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

A large windjammer ship
In the late 1800s, a windjammer could transport goods across oceans faster than many steamships.
The old windjammer sailed gracefully across the harbor, its white sails billowing in the breeze.
Tourists marveled at the restored windjammer docked at the maritime museum.
