coverslip

noun
thin glass for slides
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvərˌslɪp / (AmE), / ˈkʌvəslɪp / (BrE)
a very thin, flat piece of glass placed over a specimen on a microscope slide to protect it and allow viewing under a microscope

Examples

A glass coverslip
Carefully place the coverslip over the drop of water to avoid trapping air bubbles.
The student broke a coverslip while preparing the microscope slide for observation.
A clean coverslip is essential for getting a clear image under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slide - a flat piece of glass used to hold specimens for examination under a microscope
