backcasting

noun
reconstructing past events
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of reconstructing or estimating past conditions or events using current data or models

Examples

Backcasting past data
Scientists used backcasting to estimate the levels of pollution before industrialization.
The climate model relies on backcasting to validate its predictions against historical data.
Through backcasting, researchers reconstructed the spread of the disease over the past century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forecasting - predicting future events based on current data
  • synonym: retrodiction - inferring past events from present evidence
