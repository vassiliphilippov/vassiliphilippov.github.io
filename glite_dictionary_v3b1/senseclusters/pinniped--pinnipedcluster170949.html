pinniped

noun
aquatic mammal with flippers, e.g. seal
9.1 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪn.ɪ.ped/ /; / /ˈpɪn.ə.ped/ (AmE), /ˈpɪn.ɪ.ped/ /; / /ˈpɪn.ə.ped/ (BrE)
an aquatic carnivorous mammal with flippers, such as a seal, sea lion, or walrus

Examples

A pinniped animal
All pinnipeds have flippers that allow them to move swiftly through the water.
Researchers observed a group of pinnipeds basking on the rocky shore.
The polar region is home to several pinnipeds, including seals and walruses.
Researchers study pinnipeds to learn about their adaptation to marine life.
The harbor seal is a common pinniped found along many coastlines.
The sea lion is a pinniped known for its intelligence and agility.
Scientists study the diving abilities of various pinnipeds to understand their adaptations.
The walrus is a well-known pinniped found in Arctic waters.
Unlike most mammals, a pinniped can close its nostrils completely while swimming.
