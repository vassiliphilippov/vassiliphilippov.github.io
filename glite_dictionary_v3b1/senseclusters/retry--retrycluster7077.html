retry

verb
re-attempt a failed operation (computing)
2.7
Pronunciation: / riˈtraɪ /; / ˌriːˈtraɪ / (AmE), / riˈtraɪ /; / ˌriːˈtraɪ / (BrE)
to try to do something again, especially after failing or being interrupted

Examples

To retry the task
The API uses retry logic to recover from transient network errors.
When the upload fails, the client automatically retries up to three times with exponential backoff.
After the timeout, the service retried the database transaction and completed successfully.
If the connection fails, you can retry the operation after a few minutes.
She decided to retry the exam after not passing the first time.
The software will automatically retry sending the message if it does not go through.
After failing the driving test, he decided to retry it the following month.
If the download fails, you can simply retry by clicking the button again.
She retried the puzzle several times before finally solving it.
