Prohibition era

noun
U.S. period (1920–1933) when alcohol was banned
Pronunciation: / proʊˈhɪbɪʃən ˈɛrə / (AmE), / prəˌhɪbɪˈʃən ˈɪərə / (BrE)
The period in the United States from 1920 to 1933 when the production, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages were banned by constitutional amendment.

Examples

The Prohibition era in America
Jazz music flourished in cities across America throughout the Prohibition era.
Many secret bars, known as speakeasies, operated during the Prohibition era.
The Prohibition era led to a rise in organized crime in the United States.
