carpometacarpal

noun
wrist-hand joint
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrpoʊˌmɛtəˈkɑːrpəl / (AmE), / ˌkɑːpəʊˌmɛtəˈkɑːpəl / (BrE)
the joint where a carpal bone of the wrist meets a metacarpal bone of the hand, especially at the base of the thumb

Examples

Carpometacarpal of thumb
The doctor examined the carpometacarpal for signs of arthritis in the patient's thumb.
Injuries to the carpometacarpal are common among athletes who use their hands frequently.
Pain in the carpometacarpal can make gripping objects difficult for some people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carpal - relating to the bones of the wrist
  • antonym: metacarpophalangeal - the joint between a metacarpal bone and a finger bone
