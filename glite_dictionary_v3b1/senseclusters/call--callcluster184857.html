call

verb
redeem before maturity (finance)
3.3 6.6
Pronunciation: /kɔːl/ /; / /kɑːl/ (AmE), /kɔːl/ (BrE)
to require redemption or payment before the scheduled maturity date, or to demand repayment of a loan before its normal due date

Examples

To call a loan
If the issuer calls the bond, you might have to reinvest at a lower rate.
Investors were surprised when the issuer chose to call their preferred shares ahead of maturity.
The company decided to call the bonds early due to lower interest rates.
If you miss too many payments, the bank can call your loan at any time.
The lender decided to call the mortgage after repeated late payments.
Under the agreement, the creditor has the right to call the balance due immediately.
