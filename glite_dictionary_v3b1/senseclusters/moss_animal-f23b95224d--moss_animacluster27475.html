moss animal

noun
aquatic colonial invertebrate (Bryozoa)
6.5 8.4
Pronunciation: /mɔːs ˈæn.ə.məl/ /; / /mɑːs ˈæn.ə.məl/ (AmE), /mɒs ˈæn.ɪ.məl/ (BrE)
a small sessile aquatic invertebrate that forms colonies resembling moss, with each individual having a ring of tentacles; commonly found attached to rocks, seaweed, or other surfaces in water

Examples

A moss animal colony
Each moss animal has a crown of tentacles used for feeding.
Scientists study moss animals to learn more about marine ecosystems and water quality.
The rocks were covered with colonies of moss animals that looked like underwater moss.
