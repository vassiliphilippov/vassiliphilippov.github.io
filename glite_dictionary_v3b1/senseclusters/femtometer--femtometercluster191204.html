femtometer

noun
unit of length; one quadrillionth of a meter
7.3 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfem.tə.miː.tər/ /; / /ˈfem.toʊ.miː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈfem.tə.miː.tə/ (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one quadrillionth (10^-15) of a meter, used especially in nuclear and particle physics

Examples

A femtometer unit
A femtometer is so small that it is far beyond the scale of everyday objects.
Physicists often use the femtometer to measure distances within an atomic nucleus.
The diameter of a proton is about one femtometer.
One femtometre is an extremely small distance, only useful at the subatomic level.
Physicists use the femtometre to measure atomic nuclei.
The diameter of a proton is roughly one femtometre.
