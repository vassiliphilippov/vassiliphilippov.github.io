light

adjective
not serious; trivial
1.0 1.8
Pronunciation: /laɪt/ (AmE), /laɪt/ (BrE)
cheerful, carefree, or not serious

Examples

Light conversation
He preferred a light conversation after a long, stressful day at work.
The party was filled with light banter and laughter, making everyone feel at ease.
Their jokes were so light that no one took offense or felt uncomfortable.
He preferred light reading after a long day at work.
Her new movie is a light romantic comedy designed to make audiences laugh.
The festival offered a light program of music and comedy for families.
After hearing the good news, she walked away with a light heart.
He always felt light after a good laugh with friends.
Her mood was light and carefree at the start of the summer holiday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheerful - happy and showing it; in good spirits
  • synonym: frivolous - not having any serious purpose or value
  • antonym: heavy - weighed down with sadness or trouble (figurative sense, as in 'a heavy heart')
  • antonym: serious - acting with careful thought or intention; not silly
  • synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
