careless

adjective
not showing concern for consequences

(of a person or action, usually followed by 'of') not showing sufficient thought or concern; unconcerned about possible consequences.

Examples

Careless of the rules
He was careless of the risks, acting as if nothing could go wrong.
It was terribly careless of him to forget the safety instructions.
She drove with a careless disregard for the icy roads.

Synonyms

  • uncareful
    5.7 0.01/m
    uncareful is lacking attention or thought in actions, while careless usually shows lack of concern for possible consequences.
  • inattentive
    5.3 0.70/m
    inattentive means not paying attention to details or people, while careless often implies not thinking about consequences.
  • unmindful
    7.2 0.26/m
    unmindful describes not being aware of something, while careless suggests a wider lack of thought or concern.
  • heedless
    7.3 0.71/m
    heedless is ignoring warnings or signs, while careless is more general lack of thought about possible outcomes.
  • incautious
    6.7 0.25/m
    incautious means failing to take normal care around danger, while careless can mean not thinking about consequences more broadly.
  • negligent
    5.3 3.20/m
    negligent describes failing to take proper care that may cause harm, while careless is a simpler lack of thought or concern.
  • slipshod
    8.1 0.31/m
    slipshod describes work done badly with little effort, while careless refers to a person's lack of thought or attention more generally.
