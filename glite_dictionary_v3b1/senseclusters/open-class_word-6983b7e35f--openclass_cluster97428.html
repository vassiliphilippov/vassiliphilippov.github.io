open-class word

noun
word type with independent meaning; open to new members
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən klæs wɝd / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən klɑːs wɜːd / (BrE)
A word that belongs to a category in which new words are frequently added, such as nouns, verbs, adjectives, or adverbs, and to which an independent meaning can be assigned.

Examples

New open-class words
Language constantly evolves as new open-class words are coined to describe emerging concepts.
Nouns, verbs, and adjectives are considered open-class words in English.
Unlike prepositions, open-class words regularly grow in number with technological advancements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closed-class word - word from a grammatical category with few or no new members (e.g., prepositions, conjunctions)
