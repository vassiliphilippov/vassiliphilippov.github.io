take away

verb
remove or get rid of something abstract
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: / teɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / teɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or take something or someone away from a place, situation, or state; to get rid of or diminish something (including abstract things like rights, feelings, or value)

Examples

To take away rights
New regulations take away some of the rights workers previously enjoyed.
The apology did not take away the pain she felt.
Therapy helped take away his fear of public speaking.
A chipped plate can take away from the enjoyment of a nice meal.
His lack of enthusiasm really takes away from the team's overall spirit.
The rude comments take away from her otherwise excellent presentation.
The police took away the evidence from the scene.
The surgery was meant to take away her pain.
The waiter will take away your plate once you finish your meal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: add - to put something together with something else to increase the number, amount, or size; the opposite of remove
  • antonym: bring - to take or carry someone or something to a place
  • synonym: detract - to diminish the worth or value of something
  • synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or position
