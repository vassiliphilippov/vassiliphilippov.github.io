electrical cable

noun
cable for conducting electricity
1.6
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈkeɪbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈkeɪbəl / (BrE)
A cable made of one or more conductors, designed to carry electricity for use in devices such as telephones, televisions, or power transmission.

Examples

A electrical cable wire
A short circuit was caused by a frayed electrical cable behind the television.
Always ensure that an electrical cable is rated for outdoor use before installing it in your garden.
The technician replaced the damaged electrical cable to restore power to the building.

Related Terms
