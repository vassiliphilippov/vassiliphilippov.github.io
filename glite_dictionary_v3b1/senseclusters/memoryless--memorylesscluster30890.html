memoryless

adjective
independent of past
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmem.ər.ɪ.ləs/ /; / /ˈmem.ər.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈmem.ə.rɪ.ləs/ /; / /ˈmem.ə.ləs/ (BrE)
describing a process or system that does not depend on or remember past states; stateless

Examples

Memoryless process
A memoryless system produces the same output regardless of its previous states.
In probability theory, the exponential distribution is known for being memoryless.
The process is considered memoryless because its next step does not depend on earlier steps.
A memoryless system processes each input independently, without considering previous data.
The protocol is designed to be memoryless, making it easier to scale and maintain.
Because the device is memoryless, it cannot recall any past commands or settings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Markovian - having the property that the future state depends only on the current state
  • antonym: dependent - relying on or influenced by something else, especially the past
  • antonym: stateful - retaining information or state between operations
  • synonym: stateless - not maintaining information about previous interactions
