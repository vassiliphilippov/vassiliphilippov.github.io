Cerastium arvense

noun
a perennial chickweed plant species
Pronunciation: / səˈræstiəm ɑːrˈvɛnsi / (AmE), / səˈræstiəm ɑːˈvɛnsɪ / (BrE)
A perennial chickweed plant with densely tufted growth, native to the north temperate zone.

Examples

Cerastium arvense plant
Cerastium arvense is often found growing in open meadows and along roadsides in temperate regions.
Gardeners sometimes plant Cerastium arvense as a ground cover due to its dense, tufted growth.
The delicate white flowers of Cerastium arvense bloom throughout late spring and early summer.
