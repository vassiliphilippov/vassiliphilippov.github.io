armadillo

noun
nocturnal mammal with a hard shell
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrməˈdɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˌɑːməˈdɪləʊ / (BrE)
A nocturnal, burrowing mammal native to the Americas, with a body covered in a hard, protective shell composed of bony plates.

Examples

The armadillo digs
Farmers sometimes spot an armadillo digging for insects in their fields at night.
In South America, the nine-banded armadillo is a common sight in grassland areas.
The armadillo curls into a ball when threatened by predators.
