mummy-brown

adjective
grayish-yellowish brown color
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌmi ˌbraʊn / (AmE), / ˈmʌmi ˌbraʊn / (BrE)
Of a grayish to yellowish brown color, named after the pigment once made from ground Egyptian mummies.

Examples

Mummy-brown color
She wore a coat of mummy-brown that matched the autumn leaves perfectly.
The artist used mummy-brown to create a warm, antique effect in the painting.
The old manuscript was stained with a mummy-brown hue over the years.
