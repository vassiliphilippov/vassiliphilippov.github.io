commit

verb
to carry out or perform an act
1.0
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to give, dedicate, or put yourself or your resources (such as time, money, or effort) into something or someone

Examples

To commit time
He wants to commit himself fully to the project and see it succeed.
The company was accused of having committed numerous violations of environmental laws.
They encouraged everyone to commit a random act of kindness during the holiday season.
Before you commit your savings, be sure to do thorough research.
Investors are hesitant to commit more funds given the market uncertainty.
She decided to commit a large sum to the new start-up.
After graduating, she committed herself to helping disadvantaged children in her community.
He decided to commit all his energy to training for the national championship.
They committed their entire savings to launching the new business.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstain - to deliberately avoid or refrain from performing an action
  • synonym: dedicate - to devote time, effort, or oneself to a particular task or purpose
  • synonym: devote - to give all or a large part of one's resources or time to something
  • synonym: invest - to put money or effort into something to achieve a profit or result
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or give proper attention to something
  • synonym: perpetrate - to carry out or commit (a harmful, illegal, or immoral action)
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove money that was invested
