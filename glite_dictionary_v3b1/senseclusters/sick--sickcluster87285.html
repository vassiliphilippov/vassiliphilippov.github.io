sick

adjective
deeply affected by strong emotion
Pronunciation: / sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
deeply affected by strong negative feelings such as jealousy, disgust, or being fed up

Examples

Sick with jealousy
He was sick with jealousy when he saw his friend succeed.
She felt sick with worry before hearing the results.
The news left her sick with fear for her family.
After years of criticism, she was sick of having to defend herself all the time.
He grew sick of eating the same leftovers every night.
People get sick of complaints and negativity in the workplace.
He described the sick details of the crime in a cold, unemotional tone.
The movie was so sick that many people walked out halfway through.
The news report showed a sick scene of devastation after the attack.
