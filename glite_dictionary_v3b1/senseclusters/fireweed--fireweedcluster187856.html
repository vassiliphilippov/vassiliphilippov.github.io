fireweed

noun
a North American weedy plant
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrˌwid /; / ˈfaɪərˌwid / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwiːd / (BrE)
a North American plant that quickly grows in areas that have been burned or disturbed, often with colorful flowers

Examples

Bright fireweed flowers
After the wildfire, fireweed quickly began to sprout across the charred landscape.
Gardeners often remove fireweed because it spreads rapidly and can take over flower beds.
The field was full of fireweed, its clusters of small greenish flowers standing out among the grass.
After the forest fire, fireweed quickly covered the blackened ground with its bright flowers.
Bees are attracted to fireweed because it is a rich source of nectar.
Many hikers admired the fields of blooming fireweed along the trail in late summer.
