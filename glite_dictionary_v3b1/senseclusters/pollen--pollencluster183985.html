pollen

noun
microscopic fertilizing grains in plants
1.2 1.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.lən/ (AmE), /ˈpɒ.lən/ (BrE)
A fine powder consisting of microscopic grains produced by the male part of a flower or by male cones, which can fertilize the female ovule.

Examples

Pollen grains
Bees collect pollen from flowers and help with plant pollination.
Many people suffer from allergies caused by airborne pollen in the spring.
Scientists examined the pollen under a microscope to study the plant's reproduction process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual without fusion with another cell
