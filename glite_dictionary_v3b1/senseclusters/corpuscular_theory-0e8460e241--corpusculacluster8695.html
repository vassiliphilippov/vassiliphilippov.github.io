corpuscular theory

noun
theory that light is made of particles
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrˈpʌskjələr ˈθɪri / (AmE), / ˌkɔːˈpʌskjʊlə ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the theory in physics that light consists of a stream of particles rather than a continuous wave

Examples

Corpuscular theory of light
According to the corpuscular theory, light travels in straight lines as a stream of particles.
Isaac Newton's corpuscular theory competed with the wave theory in explaining how light behaves.
The corpuscular theory was once widely accepted before experiments showed wave-like behavior of light.
