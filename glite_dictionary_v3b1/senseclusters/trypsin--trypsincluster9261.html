trypsin

noun
digestive enzyme from pancreas
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪp.sɪn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.sɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme produced in the pancreas that catalyzes the breakdown of proteins into smaller peptides during digestion

Examples

Trypsin enzyme activity
Trypsin plays a crucial role in the breakdown of dietary proteins in the small intestine.
A deficiency in trypsin can lead to digestive problems.
Researchers added trypsin to the solution to digest the sample proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pepsin - a digestive enzyme in the stomach that breaks down proteins
