mint

noun
aromatic plant of the genus Mentha
Pronunciation: / mɪnt / (AmE), / mɪnt / (BrE)
a plant with aromatic leaves, or its leaves, used in cooking, flavoring, or as a garnish

Examples

Fresh mint leaves
Many herbal teas contain dried mint for a refreshing taste.
She added fresh mint leaves to the salad for extra flavor.
The scent of mint filled the garden in early summer.
A sprig of mint was used to garnish the dessert.
Fresh mint is often added to dishes for a burst of flavor.
He grows several varieties of mint in his herb garden.
A sprig of mint makes this tea especially refreshing on a hot day.
He enjoys chewing candied mint after meals.
She garnished the dessert with fresh mint to add a burst of flavor.
