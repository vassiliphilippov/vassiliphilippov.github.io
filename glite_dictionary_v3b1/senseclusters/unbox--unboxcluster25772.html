unbox

verb
remove from a box
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbɑks / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɒks / (BrE)
to remove something from a box or package, often referring to opening a new product (sometimes on video or for review)

Examples

To unbox a phone
Please unbox all the supplies before the event starts.
She couldn't wait to unbox her birthday gifts in front of everyone.
The new phone was delivered today, and I filmed myself unboxing it.
After receiving the package, they eagerly unboxed the latest headphones during a live stream.
Millions tuned in to watch him unbox the highly anticipated gaming console.
She plans to unbox the new smartphone on her YouTube channel tonight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: box - to put (something) in a box; to pack
  • synonym: unpack - to remove from a container or box
