noddy

noun
editing shot in video
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑdi / (AmE), / ˈnɒdi / (BrE)
a short filmed shot of a person nodding, used in television or video production to cover edits or transitions

Examples

Noddy shot
The editor inserted a noddy of the host listening to smooth out the conversation.
To hide the awkward pause, they used a quick noddy of the reporter nodding.
A well-placed noddy can make an interview appear seamless on television.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cutaway - a shot that interrupts the main action to show something else
