decretive

adjective
relating to a decree
9.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈkriː.tɪv/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈkriː.tɪv/ (BrE)
having the nature of a decree; authoritative or relating to an official order

Examples

Decretive statement
The committee issued a decretive statement that all members must follow the new rules.
Her tone was decretive, leaving no room for discussion or disagreement.
The decretive nature of the policy made it clear that exceptions would not be allowed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritative - having the power or right to give orders
  • synonym: decreed - ordered officially by someone in authority
