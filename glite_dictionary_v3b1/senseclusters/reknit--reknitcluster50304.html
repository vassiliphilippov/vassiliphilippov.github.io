reknit

verb
to knit again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈnɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈnɪt / (BrE)
to knit something again, especially after undoing previous knitting to fix or change it

Examples

To reknit a sweater
After noticing a flaw, she decided to reknit the entire sleeve of the sweater.
He had to reknit the scarf because he dropped several stitches.
Sometimes it is easier to reknit a section than to try to fix small mistakes.
