gutted

verb
to remove insides
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtɪd / (BrE)
removed the internal organs of an animal or fish, or took out the inside contents of something.

Examples

To gut a building
After the storm, workers gutted the damaged building to prepare for repairs.
She gutted the fish before cooking it for dinner.
The old house was completely gutted and renovated into a modern home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cleaned - removed unwanted parts from something, especially an animal or fish
  • antonym: filled - put something inside to make full
