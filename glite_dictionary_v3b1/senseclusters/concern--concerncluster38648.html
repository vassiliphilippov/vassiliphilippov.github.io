concern

verb
be relevant or related to
2.1 45/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàs…ùn / (AmE), / k…ônÀàs…úÀên / (BrE)
to be relevant or related to someone or something; to have to do with

Examples

The rules concern safety
My responsibilities mainly concern the financial aspects of the project.
The information in the report concerns several departments within the company.
This issue does not concern you at all, so there's no need to get involved.

Synonyms

  • have to do with
    2.7 29/m
    have to do with is a plain phrase meaning something is connected or related, while concern is the formal verb for being relevant.
  • relate
    2.2 33/m
    relate means to have a connection or link in general, while concern means to be relevant or to have to do with.
  • pertain
    4.6 10/m
    pertain is a more formal verb used to show something is relevant to a subject, while concern is common in everyday speech.
  • refer
    3.0 14/m
    refer usually means to be about or to point to something directly, while concern means simply to be related or relevant.
  • bear on
    5.1 3.65/m
    bear on is a formal phrase that often suggests having an effect or influence, while concern means to be relevant or related.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrelevant - not related to the subject being considered
  • synonym: pertain - to relate to; to have to do with
  • synonym: refer - to relate to or be connected with
