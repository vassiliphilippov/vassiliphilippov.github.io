moderate

adjective
avoiding extremes; of medium intensity
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdərət /; / ˈmɑdərət / (AmE), / ˈmɒdərət / (BrE)
not extreme or excessive; average in amount, intensity, or degree

Examples

A moderate amount
He eats a moderate amount of sweets, never too many at once.
She gave a moderate response to the criticism, avoiding any harsh words.
The climate in that region is moderate, without extreme heat or cold.
She has only a moderate interest in gardening, preferring to spend her time reading.
The hotel offers moderate prices that are affordable for most travelers.
The weather was moderate throughout the week, with temperatures neither too hot nor too cold.
She enjoys a moderate level of exercise, avoiding anything too intense.
The judge imposed a moderate fine rather than the maximum penalty.
The weather this week has been pleasantly moderate after last week's heatwave.

  • synonym: moderate - act as a moderator; preside over discussion
  • synonym: restrained - characterized by restraint or moderation
