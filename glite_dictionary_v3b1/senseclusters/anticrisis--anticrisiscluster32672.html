anticrisis

adjective
preventing crisis
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
designed to prevent, counteract, or reduce the effects of a crisis, especially an economic or financial one

Examples

Anticrisis measures
The government introduced several anticrisis measures to stabilize the economy.
Banks implemented anticrisis policies to protect themselves from financial shocks.
An anticrisis plan was developed to address potential disruptions in the supply chain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preventive - intended to stop something from happening
  • synonym: proactive - taking action in advance to deal with an expected difficulty
