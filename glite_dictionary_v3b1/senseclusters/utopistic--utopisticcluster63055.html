utopistic

adjective
unrealistically idealistic
Pronunciation: / juːtoʊˈpɪstɪk / (AmE), / juːtəˈpɪstɪk /; / juːtəʊˈpɪstɪk / (BrE)
characterized by unrealistic or idealistic ideas about a perfect society or outcome

Examples

A utopistic vision
His utopistic vision for the city ignored the financial and social challenges involved.
Critics argued that the proposal was too utopistic to be implemented in the real world.
She admired his utopistic dreams, even though they seemed impossible to achieve.
The architect's utopistic designs focused on harmony and equality for all residents.
Some critics dismissed the proposal as too utopistic to be practical in the real world.
Her utopistic dreams of a world without conflict inspired her to become an activist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically
  • antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are; practical
  • synonym: utopian - having or aiming for a perfect but unrealistic society
