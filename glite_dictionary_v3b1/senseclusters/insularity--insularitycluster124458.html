insularity

noun
state of being isolated or detached
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnsjʊˈlærɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnsjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
the state of being isolated, detached, or having a narrow point of view, especially due to physical or cultural separation

Examples

Corporate insularity
Some critics attribute the company's failure to a corporate insularity that discouraged new ideas.
The insularity of the small village made it difficult for outsiders to settle there.
The island's insularity contributed to its unique culture and traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitanism - the idea of being open and connected to the wider world
  • synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
