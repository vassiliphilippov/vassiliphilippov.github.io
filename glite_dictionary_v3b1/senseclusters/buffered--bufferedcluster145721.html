buffered

adjective
treated to resist pH change
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfərd / (AmE), / ˈbʌfəd / (BrE)
treated with a chemical buffer so that a solution resists changes in pH when acids or bases are added

Examples

Buffered solution
The scientist used a buffered solution to ensure the experiment's accuracy.
Medications are sometimes buffered to reduce stomach irritation.
A buffered environment helps maintain stable conditions for sensitive reactions.

Related Terms
