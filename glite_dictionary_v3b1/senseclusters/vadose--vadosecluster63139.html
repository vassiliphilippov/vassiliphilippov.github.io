vadose

adjective
above water table
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪdoʊs / (AmE), / ˈveɪdəʊs / (BrE)
describing underground zones above the water table where spaces contain both air and water, not fully saturated with water

Examples

The vadose zone
The cave developed in the vadose zone, where water seeps through cracks above the water table.
Scientists study the vadose layer to understand how rainwater moves underground.
In the vadose region, both air and water fill the spaces between soil particles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phreatic - relating to underground zones below the water table, fully saturated with water
