noncustodial

adjective
not involving custody
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkʌˈstoʊdiəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkʌˈstəʊdiəl / (BrE)
not involving imprisonment or confinement; not requiring someone to be kept in custody

Examples

Noncustodial sentence given
The court decided on a noncustodial sentence, allowing the defendant to remain in the community.
He received a noncustodial punishment, so he did not have to go to jail.
A noncustodial parent may still have visitation rights even without physical custody.

Related Terms

  • antonym: custodial - involving imprisonment or confinement
