horsecar

noun
streetcar pulled by horses
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrsˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈhɔːs.kɑː / (BrE)
an early type of streetcar or tram that was pulled by horses and used for passenger transport before electric streetcars were introduced

Examples

A horsecar tram
Each horsecar required at least two strong horses to pull it along the tracks.
Passengers boarded the horsecar at the corner to travel across town in the 19th century.
The city museum displays a restored horsecar from the era before electric trams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: streetcar - a vehicle on rails used for public transport in cities
