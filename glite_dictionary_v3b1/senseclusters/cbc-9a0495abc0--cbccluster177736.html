CBC

noun
blood test measuring cell counts
3.7 2.29/m
Pronunciation: / si bi si / (AmE), / siÀê biÀê siÀê / (BrE)
a blood test that counts the number of white and red blood cells and platelets in a given volume of blood

Examples

A CBC blood test
A CBC is often part of a routine health examination.
The doctor ordered a CBC to check for signs of infection.
Your CBC results indicate that your platelet count is within the normal range.

Synonyms

  • complete blood count
    3.9 0.05/m
    complete blood count is the full medical test name used in reports and hospitals, while CBC is its common abbreviation.
  • blood count
    3.0 0.33/m
    blood count is a general term for measuring blood cells or the test result, while CBC specifically names the lab test.
  • hemogram
    6.8 0.00/m
    hemogram is the technical laboratory term used by specialists, while CBC is the common clinical name patients hear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: full blood count - blood test measuring cell components (primarily in British usage)
