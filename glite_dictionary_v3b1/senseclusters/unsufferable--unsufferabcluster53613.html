unsufferable

adjective
too extreme to bear
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsʌfəɹəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌf(ə)ɹəbəl / (BrE)
too extreme to bear; intolerable.

Examples

The unsufferable heat
After hours in the unsufferable heat, the group finally reached a shaded rest area.
Her unsufferable arrogance kept her from making any real friends at work.
The unsufferable noise from the construction site made it impossible to concentrate all day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bearable - able to be endured or tolerated
  • synonym: insufferable - impossible to endure; extremely unpleasant
