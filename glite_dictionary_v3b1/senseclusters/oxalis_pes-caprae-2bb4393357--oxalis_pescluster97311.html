Oxalis pes-caprae

noun
a South African yellow-flowered wood sorrel
9.9
Pronunciation: / oʊˈzælɪs pɛs kæpˈraɪ / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs pɛs kæpˈriː / (BrE)
A South African species of bulbous wood sorrel with showy yellow flowers, often found as a weed in Mediterranean climates.

Examples

Oxalis pes-caprae yellow flowers
Oxalis pes-caprae is known for its bright yellow flowers that carpet fields in spring.
Gardeners often consider Oxalis pes-caprae a weed because it spreads aggressively.
The leaves of Oxalis pes-caprae are said to resemble a goat's foot, which inspired its species name.
