mesopelagic

noun
middle ocean layer
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsoʊpəˈlædʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊpəˈlædʒɪk / (BrE)
the middle layer of the open ocean, typically between 200 and 1000 meters deep, or the animals living in this zone

Examples

The mesopelagic animals
Many unique fish species inhabit the mesopelagic zone, where sunlight barely reaches.
Scientists study the mesopelagic to learn about oceanic food webs and carbon cycling.
The mesopelagic contains animals adapted to low light and high pressure environments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bathypelagic - the deep ocean layer below the mesopelagic, typically from 1000 to 4000 meters
  • degree higher: epipelagic - the uppermost layer of the ocean, from the surface to about 200 meters deep
