Sarpanitu

proper noun
Babylonian goddess, consort of Marduk
Pronunciation: / sɑːrˈpænɪtuː / (AmE), / sɑːˈpænɪtuː / (BrE)
the chief consort of Marduk in Babylonian mythology, regarded as a goddess associated with fertility and childbirth

Examples

Sarpanitu goddess
Many ancient texts mention Sarpanitu alongside Marduk in religious rituals.
Priests sought blessings from Sarpanitu for fertility and the health of newborns.
The temple was dedicated to Sarpanitu, the revered goddess of Babylon.
