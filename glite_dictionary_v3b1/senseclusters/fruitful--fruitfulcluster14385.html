fruitful

adjective
productive; yielding results or abundance
3.9 5.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfrut.f…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàfruÀêt.f(…ô)l / (BrE)
producing good results; productive or conducive to producing in abundance.

Examples

A fruitful research
Her years of fruitful research changed the field of medicine.
The meeting was very fruitful and led to several new initiatives.
We hope the discussion will prove fruitful for both parties.

Synonyms

  • productive
    2.7 5.33/m
    productive means making large amounts of work or goods in work settings, while fruitful stresses useful, successful results and benefits.
  • prolific
    6.0 0.68/m
    prolific describes producing a large number or many works or offspring, while fruitful focuses on producing good or useful results.
  • fertile
    4.3 1.70/m
    fertile describes land or imagination that can generate many ideas or crops, while fruitful means producing helpful or successful outcomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barren - not productive or unable to produce results
  • synonym: productive - producing or able to produce large amounts or results
