node of Ranvier

noun
gap in myelin sheath of nerve
7.7
Pronunciation: /noʊd əv ræn.vi.ˈeɪ/ (AmE), /nəʊd əv ræn.vi.ˈeɪ/ (BrE)
a small gap in the myelin sheath of a nerve fiber, between adjacent Schwann cells, where nerve impulses are propagated

Examples

A node of Ranvier gap
Action potentials jump from one node of Ranvier to the next in a process called saltatory conduction.
Damage to the myelin sheath can disrupt the function of the nodes of Ranvier, affecting nerve signal transmission.
The nodes of Ranvier play a crucial role in the rapid conduction of nerve impulses along axons.
