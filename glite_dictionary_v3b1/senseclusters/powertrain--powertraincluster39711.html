powertrain

noun
machine propulsion system
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊərˌtreɪn / (AmE), / ˈpaʊəˌtreɪn / (BrE)
the system of parts in a vehicle or machine that generates power and transmits it to make it move (such as the engine, transmission, and related components)

Examples

Vehicle powertrain system
The ship's powertrain combines diesel engines and gearboxes to drive the propellers efficiently.
Engineers upgraded the aircraft's powertrain to improve fuel efficiency and performance.
Regular maintenance of the powertrain is essential for the safe operation of industrial equipment.
The mechanic inspected the powertrain to diagnose the strange noise coming from the car.
Electric vehicles have a different powertrain compared to traditional gasoline cars.
Upgrading the powertrain can improve both performance and fuel efficiency in modern vehicles.
The new electric car features a highly efficient powertrain that increases its driving range.
Engineers are working to improve the powertrain for better fuel economy and lower emissions.
A faulty powertrain can cause serious performance issues in any vehicle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drivetrain - the components that transmit power from the engine to the wheels or propellers
  • synonym: engine - the machine that converts fuel into mechanical power
