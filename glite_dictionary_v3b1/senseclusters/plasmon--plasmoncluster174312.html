plasmon

noun
hypothetical cell element
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplæzmɒn / (AmE), / ˈplæzmɒn / (BrE)
a historical term for a hypothetical part of protoplasm believed to carry hereditary or cytoplasmic traits

Examples

Hereditary plasmon
Early scientists believed the plasmon played a key role in inheritance.
The concept of the plasmon is now considered outdated in modern biology.
Some textbooks from the early 20th century still mention the plasmon as a carrier of traits.
