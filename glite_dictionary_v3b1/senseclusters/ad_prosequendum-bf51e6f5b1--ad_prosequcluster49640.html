ad prosequendum

noun
court order for prosecution
Pronunciation: / æd proʊˌsɛkwəndəm / (AmE), / ad prəˌsɛkwɛndəm / (BrE)
a writ or court order requiring that a person be brought before a court to be prosecuted

Examples

Ad prosequendum writ
The judge issued an ad prosequendum to ensure the defendant appeared in court for trial.
An ad prosequendum was necessary to transfer the accused from another jurisdiction.
Lawyers filed an ad prosequendum to bring the witness before the court for prosecution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: writ of habeas corpus - a court order requiring a person to be brought before a judge
