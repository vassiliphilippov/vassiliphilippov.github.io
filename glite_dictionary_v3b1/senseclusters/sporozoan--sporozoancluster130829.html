sporozoan

noun
parasitic spore-forming protozoan
Pronunciation: / spɔːrəˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / spɔːrəˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
A parasitic protozoan that reproduces by forming spores, typically belonging to the phylum Apicomplexa.

Examples

A sporozoan parasite
Scientists study sporozoan life cycles to better understand how to prevent parasitic infections.
Some sporozoans can cause serious diseases in animals and humans.
The malaria parasite is a well-known type of sporozoan that infects humans through mosquito bites.
