nonconvergence

noun
failure to reach a limit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈvɝrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the property of a sequence, series, or function not approaching a finite limit as its index or variable increases

Examples

Nonconvergence of a series
The nonconvergence of the series means it does not sum to a finite value.
Mathematicians often study the causes of nonconvergence in iterative algorithms.
If there is nonconvergence, the function will not settle at a single value as input increases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convergence - the property of approaching a finite limit
  • synonym: divergence - the process of moving away from a limit or becoming infinite
