scrum

noun
Agile framework for iterative team development
Pronunciation: / skrʌm / (AmE), / skrʌm / (BrE)
(in software development and product management) an Agile framework for organizing teams to deliver work in short, iterative cycles (sprints) with defined roles and ceremonies.

Examples

Scrum meeting
The team adopted scrum to organize two-week sprints and daily stand-up meetings during the project.
During the audit, the manager explained how scrum increases transparency and speeds up product delivery.
She trained as a scrum master and then coached two teams to adopt Agile practices successfully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Agile - an approach to software development emphasizing iterative delivery and collaboration; Scrum is a specific Agile framework.
  • antonym: waterfall - a traditional, sequential approach to development that contrasts with iterative frameworks like Scrum.
