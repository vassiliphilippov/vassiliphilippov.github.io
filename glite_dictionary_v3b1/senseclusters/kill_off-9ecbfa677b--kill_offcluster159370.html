Examples

Synonyms

  • wipe out
    4.2 6.01/m
    wipe out means to completely destroy so nothing remains, while kill off often refers to ending whole populations.
  • exterminate
    6.9 2.39/m
    exterminate means to destroy pests or vermin completely by deliberate action, while kill off is broader and less technical.
  • annihilate
    7.5 3.11/m
    annihilate means to destroy or defeat completely and utterly, while kill off often suggests ending a species or group.
  • eradicate
    6.6 5.77/m
    eradicate means to remove a harmful thing completely, often in public health, while kill off is more direct physical killing.
  • eliminate
    3.4 39/m
    eliminate means to remove or get rid of something, often by choice, while kill off implies complete destruction or death.
  • snuff out
    7.7 0.40/m
    snuff out means to end suddenly or kill quietly, often one life or idea, while kill off suggests mass destruction.
  • extinguish
    7.7 0.80/m
    extinguish means to put out a fire or end something slowly, while kill off usually means to destroy living populations.
