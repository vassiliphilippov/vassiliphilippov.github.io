preselector

noun
person making initial selection
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːsəˈlɛktər / (AmE), / ˌpriːsɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
a person who selects or screens candidates, entries, or items in advance of a final selection process

Examples

Job preselector
The company hired a preselector to review all the applications before the main interviews.
As a preselector, she was responsible for narrowing down hundreds of entries to a manageable shortlist.
Each preselector used specific criteria to decide which candidates would move to the next round.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finalist - a person who reaches the final stage of a selection process
  • synonym: screener - a person who examines or filters candidates or items
  • synonym: selector - a person who chooses or selects from a group
