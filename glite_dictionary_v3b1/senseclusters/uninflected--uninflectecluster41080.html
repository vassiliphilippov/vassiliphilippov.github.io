uninflected

adjective
not changed by inflection
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪnˈflɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈflɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a word, form, or language) not changed in form to show tense, number, case, or other grammatical features; staying in its basic form

Examples

Uninflected noun form
In English, most nouns are uninflected in the plural form unless they end with 's' or another marker.
Some languages have more uninflected words, while others rely heavily on inflection for grammatical meaning.
The word 'fish' is often uninflected, serving as both singular and plural.
In analytic languages, verbs often remain uninflected and use auxiliary words instead.
Some English nouns, such as 'sheep', are uninflected for plural.
The word 'fast' is uninflected regardless of its role as adjective or adverb.
