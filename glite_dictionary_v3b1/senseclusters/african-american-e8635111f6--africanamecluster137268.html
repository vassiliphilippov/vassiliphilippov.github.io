African-American

noun
an American of African descent
Pronunciation: / ˌæfrɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌæfrɪkən əˈmerɪkən / (BrE)
A person in the United States who has African ancestry, especially one whose ancestors were brought to America as slaves.

Examples

An African-American leader
Many African-American leaders have shaped the course of history in the United States.
She researches African-American literature from the 20th century.
The museum highlights the contributions of African-American artists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Black - a Black person, especially in the US, often used as a synonym for African-American
