spiritual

adjective
immaterial; lacking material form
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪr.tʃu.əl /; / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪr.ɪ.tʃu.əl /; / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl /; / ˈspɪrɪtʃʊəl / (BrE)
relating to the non-physical, immaterial, or religious aspects of life, such as the soul, spirit, or sacred matters (not material things)

Examples

Spiritual life
Philosophers have long debated the nature of spiritual existence that transcends the material world.
Some cultures have rich traditions describing spiritual beings that lack physical bodies.
The idea of a spiritual realm fascinates those interested in mysteries beyond observable reality.
Her spiritual beliefs guided her through difficult times.
Many people seek spiritual fulfillment through meditation or prayer.
The retreat offered both physical and spiritual renewal.
Her interests are more spiritual than material, focusing on religious studies and meditation.
Many traditional songs in the choir are inspired by spiritual themes and sacred texts.
The spiritual leaders of the community played an important role during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: material - having physical form or substance
  • synonym: religious - relating to religion or the belief in a higher power
  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or sacred matters
