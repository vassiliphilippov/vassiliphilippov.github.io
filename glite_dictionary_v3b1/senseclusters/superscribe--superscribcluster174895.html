superscribe

verb
write on the outside or top of something
9.9 7.7
Pronunciation: /suː.pə.ˈskraɪb/ (AmE), /suː.pə.ˈskraɪb/ (BrE)
to write or inscribe words, a name, or an address on the outside or top of something, especially an envelope or package

Examples

To superscribe an envelope
Applicants are instructed to superscribe the post applied for on the top of the application envelope.
Please superscribe your name and address on the envelope before mailing it.
The ancient tablet was superscribed with the king’s decree.
All files must be superscribed with the project code before submission.
He superscribed the envelope with the recipient's name and address clearly.
Please superscribe your full address in the space provided at the top of the form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inscribe - to write or engrave words or symbols onto something
