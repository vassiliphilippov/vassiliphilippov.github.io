resynchronization

noun
heart rhythm treatment
6.7
Pronunciation: /riː.sɪŋ.krə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.sɪŋ.krə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.sɪŋ.krə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.sɪŋ.krə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a medical procedure that restores coordinated contractions of the heart's ventricles, often using a specialized pacemaker

Examples

Cardiac resynchronization
The doctor recommended resynchronization to help the patient's heart pump more efficiently.
After resynchronization, her symptoms of fatigue and shortness of breath improved significantly.
Many patients with heart failure benefit from resynchronization therapy to restore proper heart function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cardiac resynchronization - a therapy to improve the heart's rhythm by coordinating ventricular contractions
  • antonym: defibrillation - a treatment to restore normal heart rhythm using electric shocks
