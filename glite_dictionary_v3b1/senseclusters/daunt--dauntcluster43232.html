daunt

verb
make someone feel afraid or discouraged
Pronunciation: / dɔnt / (AmE), / dɔːnt / (BrE)
to make someone feel afraid, less confident, or discouraged

Examples

To daunt a person
Don't let the possibility of failure daunt you from trying.
He was daunted by the enormity of the task ahead.
The challenge did not daunt her; she faced it bravely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give someone support, confidence, or hope
  • synonym: intimidate - to frighten or make timid
