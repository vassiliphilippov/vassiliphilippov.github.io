functionalism

noun
doctrine focusing on function or utility
5.7 8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌŋkʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that the function or purpose of something should be the focus, especially in philosophy, architecture, and the social sciences.

Examples

Architectural functionalism
Critics argue that strict functionalism sometimes ignores the emotional or cultural aspects of design.
In sociology, functionalism examines how social institutions fulfill useful roles within society.
Many modern buildings reflect functionalism by emphasizing practicality over decoration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: functionality - the quality of being suited to serve a purpose well; practicality
