counterflow

noun
opposite fluid flow
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəfləʊ /; / ˈkaʊntəˌfləʊ / (BrE)
a flow or movement in the opposite direction to another flow, such as fluids, people, or vehicles

Examples

Counterflow of traffic
The counterflow design in the heat exchanger allows for more efficient temperature transfer.
Engineers chose a counterflow system to maximize energy savings in the plant.
In a counterflow arrangement, the two fluids move in opposite directions for better heat exchange.
The bridge was designed to handle counterflow traffic during rush hour.
Emergency workers struggled to move through the counterflow of people leaving the stadium.
A sudden counterflow of vehicles caused confusion on the one-way street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congestion - overcrowding or blockage, especially in traffic
  • antonym: mainstream - the direction of the main flow
  • antonym: parallel flow - a flow where two fluids move in the same direction
  • synonym: reverse flow - movement in the opposite direction to the usual flow
