opposite

adjective
leaves/parts growing in pairs on both sides
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːpəzɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒpəzɪt / (BrE)
directly across from or facing something or someone; arranged in pairs on opposite sides

Examples

Opposite sides
In many maples, the leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern rather than alternating.
Some plants have opposite branches, making them symmetrical along the stem.
The shrub is easy to identify by its opposite leaves, which grow in pairs along the stem.
At the meeting, he sat opposite his boss across the table.
Her house is opposite mine on the other side of the street.
The two sofas were placed opposite each other in the living room.
