retorsion

noun
twisting fibers again
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɔrʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
the act of twisting or re-twisting fibers or threads together to increase the strength of yarn, rope, or similar material

Examples

Retorsion of fibers
The process of retorsion helps ensure that the rope will not unravel easily under tension.
After the initial spinning, a second retorsion was applied to make the yarn more durable.
Modern textile factories use machines to automate the retorsion of threads for consistent quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: untwisting - the act of making something straight that was twisted
