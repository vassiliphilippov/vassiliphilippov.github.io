Hadrian

proper noun
Roman Emperor, builder of Hadrian's Wall
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪdriən / (AmE), / ˈheɪdrɪən / (BrE)
Roman Emperor (AD 76–138) known for strengthening the Roman Empire and building Hadrian's Wall in Britain

Examples

Hadrian the Emperor
Hadrian is remembered for commissioning the famous wall across northern Britain to defend the Roman Empire.
During Hadrian's reign, the Roman Empire experienced relative peace and stability.
Many ancient Roman structures in Greece and Egypt were restored under Hadrian's leadership.
Hadrian is remembered for his extensive travels across the Roman Empire to oversee its administration.
Many historians admire Hadrian for his patronage of the arts and architecture.
The construction of Hadrian's Wall marked a significant moment in the history of Roman Britain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hadrian's Wall - A defensive wall in northern Britain built during Hadrian's reign
  • synonym: Trajan - Roman Emperor and adoptive father of Hadrian
