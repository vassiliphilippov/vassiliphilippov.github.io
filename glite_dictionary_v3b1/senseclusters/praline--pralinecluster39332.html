praline

noun
nut-based sweet confection
Pronunciation: / ˈpreɪˌlin / (AmE), / ˈprɑːliːn / (BrE)
A sweet confection made from nuts and sugar syrup, often with butter, typically shaped into small, cookie-sized pieces.

Examples

A praline candy
He offered me a homemade praline with my cup of coffee.
She bought a box of pecan pralines as a souvenir from New Orleans.
The praline had a rich, buttery flavor that melted in my mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: toffee - a sweet made by boiling sugar, typically with butter
