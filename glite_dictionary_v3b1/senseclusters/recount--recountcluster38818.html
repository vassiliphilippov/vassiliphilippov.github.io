recount

verb
count again, especially for accuracy
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / rɪˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
to count something again, especially in order to check the accuracy of the count

Examples

To recount the votes
After a discrepancy was found, officials decided to recount the ballots.
Due to the tight results, they had to recount the votes to confirm the outcome.
The teacher asked the student to recount the books on the shelf to ensure the number was correct.

Related Terms

  • synonym: count - to determine the total number of something
