pirogue

noun
canoe made from a hollowed log
7.2 8.0
Pronunciation: / piˈroʊg / (AmE), / pɪˈrəʊg / (BrE)
a long, narrow canoe made by hollowing out a tree trunk, traditionally used in parts of Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia

Examples

A pirogue boat
Children watched as their grandfather carved a pirogue from a massive tree trunk.
Fishermen skillfully maneuvered their pirogue through the narrow mangrove channels.
The villagers built a new pirogue to replace the one damaged in the storm.
