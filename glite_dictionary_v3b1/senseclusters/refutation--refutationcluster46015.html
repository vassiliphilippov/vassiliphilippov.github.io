Synonyms

  • disproof
    6.8 0.13/m
    a disproof presents clear evidence or a demonstration that something is false, while refutation names the act of proving it wrong.
  • confutation
    9.9 0.01/m
    a confutation uses strong evidence or argument to show an idea is wrong, while refutation names the act of proving it false.
  • falsification
    7.7 0.16/m
    falsification shows a theory is false often by test or data, while refutation means proving a claim wrong in general.
  • disconfirmation
    8.0 0.05/m
    disconfirmation is specific evidence that contradicts a hypothesis, while refutation is the broader act of proving it false.
  • refutal
    8.8 0.00/m
    refutal is a less common variant used sometimes in writing, while refutation is the standard, widely used word for proving something false.
  • rebuttal
    6.7 3.10/m
    rebuttal is a response argument that tries to disprove a claim, while refutation implies actually proving that claim false.
  • counterargument
    5.1 0.99/m
    a counterargument offers opposing reasons or evidence against an argument, while refutation aims to show that argument is false.
  • counterstatement
    7.3 0.02/m
    a counterstatement is a formal opposing statement often used in reply, while refutation is the act of proving it wrong.
  • contradiction
    4.1 2.09/m
    a contradiction states the opposite of a claim or idea, while refutation provides evidence or argument that proves it false.
  • debunking
    5.6 0.68/m
    debunking exposes myths or false claims publicly and often plainly, while refutation is the formal act of proving those claims false.
