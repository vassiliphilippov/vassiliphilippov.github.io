refutation

noun
the act of proving something false
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛfjuˈteɪʃən /; / ˌrɛfjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛfjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of proving a statement, theory, or accusation to be wrong or false, or the evidence or argument that does this

Examples

A refutation of claim
He wrote an article that serves as a clear refutation of the rumor.
Her detailed refutation of the claims silenced all her critics.
The scientist presented a strong refutation of the outdated theory.
During the debate, his calm refutation impressed the judges.
Her refutation of the allegations was detailed and convincing.
The scientist's refutation of the theory led to its eventual rejection.
Her detailed refutation of the allegations convinced the committee of her innocence.
His article offers a strong refutation of common misconceptions about climate change.
The scientist presented a refutation supported by new experimental data.
