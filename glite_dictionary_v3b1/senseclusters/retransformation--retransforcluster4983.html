retransformation

noun
repeat genetic transformation
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːtrænsfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːtrænsfɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing foreign genetic material into cells that have already been transformed, often to restore or confirm gene expression

Examples

Gene retransformation
The scientist performed a retransformation to ensure the gene was properly expressed in the bacterial cells.
After the initial experiment failed, a retransformation was necessary to confirm the results.
Researchers often use retransformation to increase the number of cells carrying the desired plasmid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transformation - the introduction of foreign DNA into a cell
