supplicant

noun
network client that initiates authentication
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌ.plɪ.kənt/ /; / /ˈsʌ.plə.kənt/ (AmE), /ˈsʌ.plɪ.kənt/ /; / /ˈsʌ.plə.kənt/ (BrE)
(in computer networking) a client device or software entity that initiates authentication to gain access to a network or service, presenting credentials to an authenticator (for example, in 802.1X).

Examples

Network supplicant
The Wi‑Fi client acts as the supplicant during 802.1X authentication to the access point.
When the company switched to certificate-based login, each laptop became a supplicant needing new credentials.
Troubleshooting showed the supplicant failed to present the correct identity, so access was denied.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authenticator - the network component that verifies credentials presented by the supplicant and grants or denies access
  • synonym: client (network) - a device or software that requests access to a network or service; often the entity that authenticates as a supplicant
