Leptoptilus crumeniferus

noun
large African stork; source of feather trim
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɛpˈtɑptɪləs ˌkruːməˈnɪfərəs / (AmE), / lɛpˈtɒptɪləs ˌkruːməˈnɪfərəs / (BrE)
A large African black-and-white stork that feeds on carrion; its soft underwing feathers are used to adorn garments.

Examples

The Leptoptilus crumeniferus stork
Feathers from the Leptoptilus crumeniferus were once prized for decorating hats and clothing.
The Leptoptilus crumeniferus is often seen scavenging near wetlands and garbage dumps in sub-Saharan Africa.
Unlike other storks, Leptoptilus crumeniferus is notable for its consumption of carrion as its primary food source.
