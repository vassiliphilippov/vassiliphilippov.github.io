burned

adjective
treated by heating (not melted)
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɝnd /; / bɝːnt / (AmE), / bɜːnd /; / bɜːnt / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) treated by heating to a high temperature but below its melting or fusing point.

Examples

Burned clay
Artists frequently use burned ochre as a pigment for earthy tones.
Some metallic finishes are created by applying a burned coating to the surface.
The ceramic was burned to achieve its distinctive color and texture.
Some natural pigments, such as burnt umber, are made by heating the raw material.
The artist preferred burnt sienna for its rich, earthy tone in his landscape paintings.
This pottery has a distinctive look due to its burnt clay surface.
