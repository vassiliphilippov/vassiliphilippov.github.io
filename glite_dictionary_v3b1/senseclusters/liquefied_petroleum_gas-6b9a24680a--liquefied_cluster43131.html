liquefied petroleum gas

noun
mixture of propane and butane gases used as fuel
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwəˌfaɪd pərˈtroʊliəm ɡæs / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfaɪd pəˈtrəʊliəm ɡas / (BrE)
a flammable mixture of hydrocarbon gases, mainly propane and butane, that is kept under pressure and used as fuel

Examples

Liquefied petroleum gas fuel
Most portable heaters use liquefied petroleum gas as an energy source.
The restaurant switched from natural gas to liquefied petroleum gas for its kitchen.
Transporting liquefied petroleum gas requires special pressurized containers for safety.
