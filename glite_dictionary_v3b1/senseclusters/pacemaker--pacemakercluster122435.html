pacemaker

noun
horse setting pace in racing
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a horse that is entered in a race to establish a fast pace for the benefit of another runner

Examples

Race pacemaker
That horse has often served as a pacemaker for more talented stablemates.
The trainer assigned a pacemaker to help the favorite maintain a steady speed.
Without a pacemaker in the race, the early pace was unusually slow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pacesetter - one that sets the pace in a race or competition
