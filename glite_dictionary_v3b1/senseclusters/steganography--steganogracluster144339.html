steganography

noun
concealing messages within other data
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛɡəˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌstiːɡəˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The practice of concealing messages or information within other non-secret text or data; the art or science of hiding information to prevent detection.

Examples

Digital steganography
Digital steganography allows sensitive information to be embedded harmlessly inside regular documents.
Researchers demonstrated steganography techniques for securely transmitting data during the conference.
The spy used steganography to hide the secret message within an image file.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cryptography - the practice of secure communication through codes and ciphers
