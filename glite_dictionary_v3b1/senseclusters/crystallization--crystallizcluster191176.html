crystallization

noun
process of forming crystals
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which crystals are formed from a solution, melt, or gas

Examples

Mineral crystallization
During the cooling of magma, crystallization produces various minerals.
The crystallization of salt from seawater is a common example of this process.
The scientist observed the crystallization occurring slowly under the microscope.
Impurities in the solution can interfere with the crystallisation of minerals.
The process of crystallisation is crucial in the manufacture of high-quality sugar.
The slow crystallisation of salt from seawater can be observed under the right conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystallization - American spelling of 'crystallisation'
  • synonym: precipitation - the process by which a substance separates from a solution as a solid
