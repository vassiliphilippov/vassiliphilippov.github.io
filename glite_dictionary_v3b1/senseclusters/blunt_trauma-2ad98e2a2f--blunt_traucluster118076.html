blunt trauma

noun
injury from non-penetrating impact
Pronunciation: / blʌnt ˈtrɑmə / (AmE), / blʌnt ˈtrɔːmə / (BrE)
An injury caused by impact with a non-penetrating object, typically resulting in damage to body tissues without an open wound.

Examples

Blunt trauma injury
Blunt trauma is often seen in car crashes or falls from a great height.
Doctors treated her for multiple injuries, including blunt trauma to the head.
The accident victim suffered severe blunt trauma to the chest and abdomen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: penetrating trauma - injury in which an object pierces the body and creates an open wound
