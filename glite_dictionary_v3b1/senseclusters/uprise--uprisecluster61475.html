uprise

verb
rise to one's feet
7.7
Pronunciation: / əˈpraɪz / (AmE), / ʌpˈraɪz / (BrE)
to get up or rise to one's feet, especially from sitting or lying down

Examples

People uprise from seats
As the king entered, everyone in the hall uprise to show their respect.
He would uprise from his chair, stretching after a long conversation.
The whole assembly uprise in silent protest at the announcement.
As the clock struck six, he uprises and prepares for the day ahead.
Each morning in the monastery, the monks would uprise before dawn to begin their prayers.
She uprose quietly, careful not to wake the children still sleeping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arise - to get up, especially from bed after sleeping
  • synonym: rise - to get up from lying, sitting, or kneeling
  • synonym: stand up - to rise to a standing position
