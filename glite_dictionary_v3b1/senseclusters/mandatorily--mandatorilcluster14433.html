mandatorily

adverb
in a required or obligatory manner
3.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmæn.də.tɔːr.ə.liː/ (AmE), /ˈmæn.də.tɔː.rə.liː/ (BrE)
in a manner required by rules, laws, or authority; in a way that is obligatory and cannot be avoided

Examples

To be mandatorily inspected
All vehicles must be mandatorily inspected before they are allowed on the road.
Safety equipment is mandatorily worn by all workers in the laboratory.
The law requires that all students are mandatorily enrolled in the vaccination program.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compulsorily - in a way that is required by law or rule
  • antonym: voluntarily - in a way that is done by choice, not requirement
