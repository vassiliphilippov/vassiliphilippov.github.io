coulter

noun
blade attached to a plow
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊltər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊltə / (BrE)
A sharp steel blade or disc attached in front of a plow to make a vertical cut in the soil.

Examples

A sharp coulter blade
A sharp coulter helps cut through tough soil before the plow blade follows.
Every spring, he checked that the coulter was properly aligned with the rest of the plow.
The farmer replaced the worn coulter to ensure his plow worked efficiently.
