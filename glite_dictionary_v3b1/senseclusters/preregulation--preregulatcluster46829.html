preregulation

adjective
before regulations
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
existing or occurring before official rules or regulations were introduced, especially referring to products or conditions from an earlier unregulated period

Examples

Preregulation cars
Many collectors prefer preregulation cars because they have simpler engines and classic styling.
The preregulation market allowed companies to operate with fewer restrictions.
Some investors study preregulation periods to understand how industries behaved before government oversight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regulated - controlled or governed by official rules or laws
  • synonym: unregulated - not controlled by official rules or laws
