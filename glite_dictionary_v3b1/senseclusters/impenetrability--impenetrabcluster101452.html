impenetrability

noun
the state of being impossible to penetrate
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpɛnɪtrəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛnɪtrəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to pass through, enter, or understand.

Examples

Impenetrability of the wall
Dense fog added to the impenetrability of the forest, making it difficult to find the path.
The impenetrability of the fortress walls deterred any would-be invaders.
The novel was criticized for the impenetrability of its plot and prose style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque; not allowing light to pass through
  • antonym: transparency - the quality of allowing light to pass through; opposite of impenetrability
