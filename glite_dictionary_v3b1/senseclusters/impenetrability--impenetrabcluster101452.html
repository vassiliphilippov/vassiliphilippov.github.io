The quality or state of being impossible to pass through, enter, or understand.

Examples

Impenetrability of the wall
Dense fog added to the impenetrability of the forest, making it difficult to find the path.
The impenetrability of the fortress walls deterred any would-be invaders.
The novel was criticized for the impenetrability of its plot and prose style.

Synonyms

  • impassability
    8.3 0.02/m
    impassability is the state of being impossible to cross or get through, while impenetrability also includes being hard to understand.
  • imperviousness
    8.7 0.06/m
    imperviousness keeps water, light, or people from entering, while impenetrability also covers being hard to understand or explain.
  • inscrutability
    9.9 0.07/m
    inscrutability means being impossible to read or interpret, while impenetrability adds a physical sense of not being entered.
  • inexplicability
    9.8 0.04/m
    inexplicability is being very hard to explain clearly, while impenetrability can mean both physical blockage and incomprehension.
