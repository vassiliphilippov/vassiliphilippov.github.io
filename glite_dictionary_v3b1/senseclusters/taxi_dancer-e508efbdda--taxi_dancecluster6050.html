taxi dancer

noun
woman who dances with patrons for payment
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtæksi ˌdænsər / (AmE), / ˈtæksi ˌdɑːnsə / (BrE)
a woman employed, especially in a dance hall, to dance with patrons for a fee per dance

Examples

Taxi dancers in halls
In the 1920s, taxi dancers were common in big-city dance halls.
Many patrons visited the club just to spend an evening dancing with the cheerful taxi dancers.
The film tells the story of a young woman who becomes a taxi dancer to make ends meet.
