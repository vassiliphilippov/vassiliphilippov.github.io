Athanasian

noun
follower of Athanasius
9.9
Pronunciation: /æθ.ə.ˈneɪ.zɪ.ən/ /; / /æθ.ə.ˈneɪ.ʒən/ (AmE), /æθ.ə.ˈneɪ.ʒən/ /; / /æθ.ə.ˈneɪ.zɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a person who follows or supports the teachings of Athanasius, especially regarding the doctrine of the Trinity in Christianity

Examples

An Athanasian believer
As an Athanasian, she defended the church's traditional doctrine of the Trinity at every meeting.
The debate between the Athanasian and Arian positions shaped early Christian theology.
He identified himself as an Athanasian because of his strong belief in the Nicene Creed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Arian - a person who follows the teachings of Arius, often opposing the doctrine of the Trinity
  • synonym: Trinitarian - a person who believes in the doctrine of the Trinity
