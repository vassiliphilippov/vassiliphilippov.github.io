ultraviolet ray

noun
type of high-energy radiation from the sun
2.8 3.9
Pronunciation: / ʌltrəˈvaɪəlɛt reɪ / (AmE), / ʌltrəˈvaɪələt reɪ / (BrE)
a type of high-energy electromagnetic radiation, especially from the sun, with a wavelength shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays

Examples

Strong ultraviolet rays
Excessive exposure to ultraviolet rays can increase the risk of skin cancer.
Special lamps emit ultraviolet rays to sterilize medical equipment.
Use sunscreen to protect your skin from ultraviolet rays when outdoors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: infrared ray - radiation with a wavelength longer than visible light
  • synonym: ultraviolet light - electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength just shorter than visible light
