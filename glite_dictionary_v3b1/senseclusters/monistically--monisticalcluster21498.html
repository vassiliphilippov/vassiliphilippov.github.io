monistically

adverb
in a monist way
Pronunciation: / moʊˈnɪstɪkli / (AmE), / məˈnɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to or supports the idea that all things are fundamentally one substance or principle

Examples

Explained monistically
The theory was explained monistically, emphasizing the unity of all existence.
She interpreted the ancient texts monistically, seeing no separation between mind and matter.
Some scientists approach the nature of consciousness monistically, rejecting dualist explanations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dualist - in a way that supports the existence of two fundamental principles
