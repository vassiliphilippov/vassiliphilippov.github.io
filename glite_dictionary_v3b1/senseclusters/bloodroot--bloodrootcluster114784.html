bloodroot

noun
a North American herb with a red root and white flower
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdˌrut / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌruːt / (BrE)
a North American perennial herb (Sanguinaria canadensis) with a red root and sap, a solitary lobed leaf, and a white spring flower; its rootstock is used in traditional medicine as a stimulant and expectorant

Examples

The bloodroot plant
After heavy rain, the red sap from a broken bloodroot stained the gardener’s hands.
In spring, the bloodroot blooms with its distinctive white flowers in forest clearings.
Native Americans traditionally used bloodroot as a remedy for respiratory ailments.
