side

noun
half of an animal carcass for food
Pronunciation: / saɪd / (AmE), / saɪd / (BrE)
A lengthwise half of an animal's carcass, especially after being dressed and prepared for use as food.

Examples

A side of beef
Each side typically weighs several hundred pounds after the animal is processed.
The butcher hung a side of beef in the cold storage room.
They ordered a full side of pork for the barbecue.

  • degree lower: quarter - one fourth of an animal's carcass
  • degree higher: whole - entire animal carcass
