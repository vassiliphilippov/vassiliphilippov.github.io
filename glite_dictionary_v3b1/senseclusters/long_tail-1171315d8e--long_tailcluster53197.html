long tail

noun
extended animal tail
3.3
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ teɪl / (AmE), / lɒŋ teɪl / (BrE)
a physically long, tapering tail of an animal or object, often used for balance or movement

Examples

A cheetah's long tail
The cheetah's long tail helps it keep balance while running at high speeds.
Some lizards use their long tail to distract predators and escape danger.
The kite's long tail fluttered in the wind, keeping it steady in the sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short tail - a tail that is short in length
