reconceptualization

noun
new way of thinking
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˌsɛptʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˌsɛptjʊəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of thinking about something in a completely new or different way; forming a new concept or understanding by rethinking an existing idea

Examples

The reconceptualization process
The reconceptualization of the project led to a more effective solution.
After much discussion, the team began a reconceptualization of their marketing strategy.
Her reconceptualization of the problem helped everyone see it from a new perspective.
The reconceptualization of the curriculum made learning more accessible for students.
After much debate, the committee agreed on a reconceptualization of the city's transportation plan.
Her reconceptualization of the problem led to a creative and effective solution.

  • synonym: revision - the process of changing or improving something
