lollipop

noun
ice cream or water ice on a stick
1.0 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêliÀåp…ëp / (AmE), / Ààl…íl…™p…íp / (BrE)
A piece of flavored ice cream or water ice on a small stick, eaten as a sweet treat.

Examples

An ice lollipop
After school, the children bought a lollipop from the ice cream cart.
In England, what Americans call a popsicle is usually called an ice lollipop.
On hot days, I love sucking on a cold lollipop in the park.

Synonyms

  • ice lolly
    2.0 0.01/m
    an ice lolly is a frozen sweet on a stick often called a popsicle, while a lollipop is usually hard candy on a stick.
  • popsicle
    1.4 1.29/m
    a popsicle is a frozen fruity treat on a stick, while a lollipop is a sweet usually made from hard candy rather than ice.
  • lolly
    6.4 0.18/m
    a lolly is a general term for a sweet or snack, while a lollipop usually means a hard candy on a stick.
