thermogenesis

noun
production of heat
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the production of heat by chemical, mechanical, or physical processes, especially in non-living systems or industrial settings

Examples

Battery thermogenesis
Unexpected thermogenesis in the battery pack raised safety concerns and triggered an automatic shutdown.
Engineers monitor thermogenesis during chemical reactions to prevent overheating in the laboratory.
The device's design minimizes thermogenesis to improve energy efficiency and safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cooling - the process of making something less hot
  • synonym: heat generation - the process of producing heat
