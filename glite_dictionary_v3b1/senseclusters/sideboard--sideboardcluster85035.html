sideboard

noun
removable board on wagon to increase capacity
1.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.bɔːrd/ /; / /ˈsaɪd.boʊrd/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.bɔːd/ (BrE)
a removable board attached to the side of a wagon or cart to increase its capacity for carrying goods

Examples

Wagon sideboard
By raising the sideboard, it was possible to transport larger loads without them spilling out.
Old wooden wagons often featured a detachable sideboard for extra hauling capacity during harvest time.
The farmer added a sideboard to his wagon to carry more hay across the fields.
