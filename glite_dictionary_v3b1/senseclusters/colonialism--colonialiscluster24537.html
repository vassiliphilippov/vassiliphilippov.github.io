colonialism

noun
policy of controlling and exploiting another country
4.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈloʊniəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the policy or practice of acquiring full or partial political control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically

Examples

Colonialism policy
Anti-colonialism movements emerged in the twentieth century across Africa and Asia.
Some historians argue that colonialism permanently altered the economies of many countries.
The legacy of colonialism can still be seen in modern political boundaries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imperialism - policy of extending a country's power through colonization or military force
