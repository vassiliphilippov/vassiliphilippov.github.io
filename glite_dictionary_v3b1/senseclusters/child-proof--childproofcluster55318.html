child-proof

verb
make safe against children
3.8 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪldˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌɪldˌpruːf / (BrE)
To make something safe so that children cannot access or be harmed by it.

Examples

To child-proof a home
Parents often child-proof their homes to prevent accidents and injuries.
She child-proofed the living room by covering the electrical outlets and moving sharp objects out of reach.
We need to child-proof all the cabinets before the baby starts crawling.
