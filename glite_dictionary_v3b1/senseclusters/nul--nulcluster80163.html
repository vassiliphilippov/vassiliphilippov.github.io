nul

noun
DOS null device
Pronunciation: / nʌl / (AmE), / nʌl / (BrE)
a special device in DOS and Windows operating systems that discards all data written to it, acting as a null device

Examples

Send output to nul
To prevent unnecessary output, the script redirected its results to nul.
If you want to ignore the command's output, just send it to nul.
The developer explained that writing to nul is like sending data into a black hole.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dev/null - the Unix equivalent of a null device
  • synonym: null device - a device that discards all data written to it
