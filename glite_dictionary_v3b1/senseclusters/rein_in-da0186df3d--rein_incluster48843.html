rein in

verb
control or limit; restrain
Pronunciation: / reɪn ɪn / (AmE), / reɪn ɪn / (BrE)
to control or limit something or someone, or to make a horse slow down or stop by pulling on the reins

Examples

To rein in a horse
If you don't rein in your excitement, you might scare the animals away.
She struggled to rein in her impatience during the long meeting.
The government has promised to rein in public spending this year.
She had to rein in her excitement during the meeting.
The boss reined in spending on unnecessary projects last year.
The government is trying to rein in inflation before it gets out of control.
He quickly reined in the horse as they approached the busy intersection.
She had to rein in her excitement before speaking to the press.
The coach told the players to rein in their aggressive behavior on the field.
