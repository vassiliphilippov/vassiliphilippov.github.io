vitriolic remark

noun
extremely harsh or bitter comment
9.7
Pronunciation: / vɪˈtriɑlɪk rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / vɪˈtrɪəlɪk rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
an extremely harsh or bitter comment, often intended to hurt or criticize someone severely

Examples

A vitriolic remark
He regretted making such a vitriolic remark to his former friend.
Her vitriolic remark during the meeting shocked everyone in the room.
Online forums are often filled with vitriolic remarks aimed at public figures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
  • synonym: insult - an offensive or rude remark meant to hurt someone
