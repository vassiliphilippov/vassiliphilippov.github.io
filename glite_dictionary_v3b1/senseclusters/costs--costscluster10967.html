costs

noun
legal expenses awarded by a court
Pronunciation: / kɑsts / (AmE), / kɒsts / (BrE)
Money that a court orders to be paid to a party in a lawsuit to cover legal expenses, usually by the losing side to the winning side.

Examples

Legal costs
Her lawyer filed a motion to recover legal costs after she won the case.
If you win the lawsuit, you may be entitled to costs as part of the judgment.
The judge ordered the losing party to pay all costs of the proceedings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damages - money awarded by a court as compensation for loss or injury
