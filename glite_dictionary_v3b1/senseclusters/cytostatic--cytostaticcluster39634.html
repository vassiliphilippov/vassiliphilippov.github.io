cytostatic

noun
cell growth inhibitor
7.6
Pronunciation: /saɪ.tə.ˈstæt.ɪk/ /; / /saɪ.toʊ.ˈstæt.ɪk/ (AmE), /saɪ.tə.ˈstæt.ɪk/ (BrE)
a drug or agent that stops or slows the growth and division of cells, especially used in cancer treatment

Examples

The doctor prescribed a cytostatic to help control the spread of the tumor.
Researchers are developing new cytostatic agents with fewer side effects for cancer patients.
A cytostatic can be used to slow the progression of certain types of cancer.
