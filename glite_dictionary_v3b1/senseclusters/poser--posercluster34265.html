poser

noun
person who pretends to be something they are not

a person who pretends to be someone they are not, often to impress others or fit in with a group

Examples

A fake poser
Many people thought he was a true artist, but in reality, he was just a poser.
She accused him of being a poser who cared more about image than substance.
Teenagers sometimes act like posers to fit in with certain social groups.

    5.2 0.63/m

  • phony
    6.2 2.39/m
    phony claims beliefs or feelings to fool people, while poser mainly changes style or behavior to seem part of a group.
  • fake
    3.7 1.97/m
    fake pretends broadly and can be an object or person meant to deceive, while poser focuses on social image and fitting in.
  • pretender
    7.4 0.30/m
    pretender claims something false, often a title or role, while poser tries to seem part of a group or fashionable.
  • impostor
    5.1 1.43/m
    impostor pretends to be a specific person to deceive or gain, while poser pretends to be fashionable or belong to a group.
  • impersonator
    6.2 0.97/m
    impersonator imitates someone else, often to entertain or trick, while poser pretends about identity mainly to fit in or impress.
