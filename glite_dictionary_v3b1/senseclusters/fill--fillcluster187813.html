fill

verb
appoint to a job or position
Pronunciation: / fɪl / (AmE), / fɪl / (BrE)
to appoint someone to a position or to occupy a position, role, or office

Examples

To fill a job
The company needs to fill the position of marketing director immediately.
They struggled to fill several vacancies in the IT department.
We are hoping to fill all open roles by the end of the month.
After the director resigned, it took months to fill the post.
She was honored when asked to fill the role of committee chair.
They hired a new manager to fill the vacant position.
