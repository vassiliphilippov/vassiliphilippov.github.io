rainband

noun
band of rain clouds
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪnˌbænd /; / ˈreɪnbænd / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbænd / (BrE)
a long, narrow area of rain and clouds, often seen with storms, hurricanes, or weather fronts

Examples

A rainband of clouds
The approaching hurricane had several rainbands that brought heavy showers to the coastline.
Meteorologists warned that a rainband could cause sudden downpours in the city this afternoon.
As the storm moved inland, a wide rainband swept across the farmland, soaking the crops.
The satellite image showed a large rainband stretching across the region.
As the rainband passed over the city, the streets quickly became wet and slippery.
Meteorologists warned that the approaching rainband could bring heavy rainfall and strong winds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear sky - a sky without clouds or precipitation
  • synonym: squall line - a line of severe thunderstorms
