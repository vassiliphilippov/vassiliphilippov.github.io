hard

adjective
very strong or forceful (especially a hit or action)
1.8 19/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërd / (AmE), / h…ëÀêd / (BrE)
very strong, forceful, or intense (such as a blow, light, or alcoholic drink)

Examples

Hard punch
He delivered a hard punch that knocked his opponent down.
She hit the ball with a hard swing, sending it over the fence.
The team suffered a hard blow when their captain was injured.
Cinematographers often avoid hard light because it accentuates imperfections and creates harsh contrasts.
In the afternoon, the sun shining through the window produced a hard beam of light across the floor.
The photographer used a flash to create hard lighting that cast dramatic shadows on the model's face.
Many people prefer hard liquor like whiskey or vodka over beer.
She poured herself a glass of hard cider at the party.
The bar serves a variety of hard spirits, including rum, gin, and tequila.

Synonyms

  • stiff

  • heavy
    2.1 14/m
    heavy describes something that is intense or demanding to deal with, while hard means forceful or very intense in impact.
  • severe
    3.5 42/m
    severe is very serious or harmful in degree and formal, while hard is about force, intensity, or a strong physical effect.
