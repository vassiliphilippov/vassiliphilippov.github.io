sempre

adverb
Italian for always
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmpreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɛmpreɪ / (BrE)
an Italian adverb meaning 'always' (used in English especially in music to mean 'throughout' or 'always in the same way')

Examples

Piano sempre
The composer wrote 'piano sempre' in the score to indicate the music should always be soft.
She ended her letter with 'ti penso sempre', expressing her constant thoughts.
In the novel, the Italian father whispers 'ti amo sempre' to show lasting devotion.
The sheet music instructs the pianist to play sempre legato for a smooth sound.
Remember to keep the melody sempre piano, even as the dynamics change elsewhere.
The conductor reminded the orchestra to maintain sempre staccato in this section.

Related Terms

  • synonym: always - at all times; on every occasion
  • synonym: continuously - without interruption; all the time
  • antonym: never - at no time; not ever
  • antonym: occasionally - from time to time; not always
