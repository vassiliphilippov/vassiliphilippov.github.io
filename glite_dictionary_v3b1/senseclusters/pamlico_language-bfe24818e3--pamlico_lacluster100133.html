Pamlico language

noun
extinct Algonquian language of the Pamlico people
Pronunciation: / ˈpæm.lɪˌkoʊ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpæm.lɪ.kəʊ ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the extinct Algonquian language formerly spoken by the Pamlico people of North Carolina.

Examples

The Pamlico language extinct
Linguists have documented several words from the Pamlico language in colonial records.
Sadly, the Pamlico language is no longer spoken today.
The Pamlico language belonged to the larger Algonquian language family.
