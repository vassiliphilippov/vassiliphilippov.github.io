overglazing

noun
decorative top glaze
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈɡleɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡleɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of applying a decorative or protective layer of glaze on top of an already-glazed and fired ceramic surface

Examples

Overglazing ceramic surface
The artist added a layer of overglazing to give the vase a glossy finish.
Restorers examined the overglazing to determine the age of the ceramic plate.
Some antique dishes feature colorful overglazing that enhances their decorative appeal.
The pottery class practiced overglazing to create unique patterns on their vases.
After the first firing, the artist used overglazing to enhance the colors of the plate.
Careful overglazing can give ceramics a glossy and vibrant finish.
