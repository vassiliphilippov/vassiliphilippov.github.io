mass production

noun
manufacturing large quantities of standardized products
5.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /mæs prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /mæs prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ /; / /mɑːs prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the production of large quantities of standardized products, often using assembly line techniques.

Examples

Mass production of goods
Critics argue that mass production can lead to a loss of craftsmanship and individuality in products.
Many household appliances became affordable once mass production techniques were adopted.
The invention of the assembly line revolutionized mass production in the automobile industry.
