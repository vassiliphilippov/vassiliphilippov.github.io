steam

verb
become very angry or agitated (informal)
4.4
Pronunciation: / stiːm / (AmE), / stiːm / (BrE)
(informal) To become very agitated or angry; to show strong irritation or annoyance.

Examples

He steams with anger
Don't steam over it all day—just let it go.
He was steaming after being blamed for something he didn't do.
She began to steam when she heard about the unfair decision.
