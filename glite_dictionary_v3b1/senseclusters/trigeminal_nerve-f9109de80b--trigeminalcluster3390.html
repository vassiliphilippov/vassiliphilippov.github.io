trigeminal nerve

noun
major facial sensory and chewing nerve
6.8 6.1
Pronunciation: / traɪˈdʒɛmɪnəl nɜrv / (AmE), / traɪˈdʒɛmɪnəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the fifth cranial nerve that is responsible for sensation in the face and motor functions such as biting and chewing

Examples

The trigeminal nerve function
After the accident, her trigeminal nerve was affected, causing difficulty in feeling sensations on one side of her face.
Damage to the trigeminal nerve can result in severe facial pain and numbness.
The dentist explained that the trigeminal nerve controls the muscles used for chewing.
