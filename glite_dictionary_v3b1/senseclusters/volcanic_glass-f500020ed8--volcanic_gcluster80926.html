volcanic glass

noun
natural glass from rapidly cooled lava
4.3 6.7
Pronunciation: / vɑlˈkænɪk ɡlæs / (AmE), / vɒlˈkænɪk ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A natural glass formed when molten lava cools and solidifies rapidly, preventing crystal formation.

Examples

Natural volcanic glass
Due to its rapid cooling, volcanic glass lacks a crystalline structure.
Obsidian is one of the most well-known forms of volcanic glass.
The archaeologist found a blade made from volcanic glass at the site.
