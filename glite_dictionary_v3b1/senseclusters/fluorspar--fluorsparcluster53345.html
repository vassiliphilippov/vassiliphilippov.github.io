fluorspar

noun
a mineral; chief source of fluorine
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈflʊrˌspɑr / (AmE), / ˈflɔːˌspɑː / (BrE)
A mineral consisting of calcium fluoride that is often fluorescent under ultraviolet light and is the chief source of fluorine.

Examples

A fluorspar mineral
China is the world's largest producer of fluorspar.
The mine is rich in fluorspar, which is processed for use in industry.
Under ultraviolet light, fluorspar emits a distinctive glow.
