incused

noun
sunken impression
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkjuːst / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjuːst / (BrE)
a sunken or stamped impression on a surface, especially on a coin or medal

Examples

A coin's incused
The ancient coin had an incused showing the outline of a lion.
Collectors value coins with a clear incused because they reveal details of the original design.
The medal's incused was created by pressing a die into the metal surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impression - a mark or design made by pressing something into a surface
  • antonym: relief - a raised design or image on a surface
