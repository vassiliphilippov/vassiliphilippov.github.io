slice

noun
a golf shot that curves to the right
Pronunciation: / slaɪs / (AmE), / slaɪs / (BrE)
a golf shot that curves sharply to the right (for a right-handed player) due to sidespin

Examples

A golf slice
After weeks of practice, her slice finally turned into a straight shot.
He struggled with his slice throughout the tournament and missed several fairways.
The coach analyzed his swing to help correct the slice in his drive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hook - a golf shot that curves to the left for a right-handed golfer
