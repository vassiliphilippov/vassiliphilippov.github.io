Dilbert principle

noun
satirical management concept about promoting incompetence
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪlbɚt ˌprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪlbət ˌprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
the humorous idea that organizations tend to promote their least competent employees to management positions

Examples

The Dilbert principle
After the restructuring, the Dilbert principle seemed to perfectly describe the new leadership.
Many employees joked that the Dilbert principle was alive and well in their company.
Some critics argue that the Dilbert principle explains the abundance of ineffective managers.
