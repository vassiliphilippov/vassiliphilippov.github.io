relisting

noun
sale or auction again
3.6
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈlɪs.tɪŋ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈlɪs.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /riː.ˈlɪs.tɪŋ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈlɪs.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of putting something up for sale or auction again after it was not sold previously

Examples

A relisting at auction
After the house did not sell, a relisting was necessary to attract new buyers.
The auction site allows for the relisting of unsold items at no extra charge.
Sellers often lower the price during a relisting to increase the chances of a sale.
