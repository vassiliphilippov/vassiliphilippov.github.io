aside

adverb
to one side for a purpose
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsaɪd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsaɪd/ (BrE)
kept or saved for a particular purpose or for future use

Examples

Put aside money
She set her book aside and listened to the music.
The judge asked the jury to put their personal feelings aside when making a decision.
They put some money aside for their vacation next year.
She always puts a little money aside for emergencies.
Some time was set aside at the end of the meeting for questions.
They kept the best pieces aside for the museum's collection.
