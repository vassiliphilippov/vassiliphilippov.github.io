transitoriness

noun
impermanence or temporariness
9.9 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtræn.zɪ.tə.rɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈtræn.zə.tə.rɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈtræn.zɪ.tə.rɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈtræn.zə.tə.rɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
The state or quality of being temporary and likely to come to an end; impermanence, especially as it suggests the inevitability of ending or dying.

Examples

The transitoriness of life
Artists often reflect on the transitoriness of life in their work.
He was struck by the transitoriness of happiness during difficult times.
The transitoriness of youth makes it all the more precious and fleeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impermanence - the state of not lasting forever; transiency
  • antonym: permanence - the quality of lasting or remaining unchanged; permanent existence
