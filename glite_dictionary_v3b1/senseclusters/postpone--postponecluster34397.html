Synonyms

  • defer
    5.5 0.7/m
    defer is more formal and often used in official contexts to delay action, while postpone is a general everyday word.
  • put off
    2.9 5/m
    put off is an informal phrasal verb used to delay events to a later time, while postpone is neutral and more formal.
  • reschedule
    3.0 5/m
    reschedule means change the planned time to a later slot, while postpone focuses on moving the event later without naming a new time.
  • delay
    1.4 30/m
    delay means make something happen later or slow its progress, while postpone is arranging a later time by plan or reason.
  • hold off
    3.8 4/m
    hold off means wait before starting or acting for a short time, while postpone means arrange the whole event for later.
  • hold over
    4.6 0.06/m
    hold over means extend or carry something into a later period, while postpone usually means delay to a new planned time.
  • table
    7.3 0.6/m
    table means set aside discussion of a proposal in meetings, while postpone covers delaying events or plans more generally.
