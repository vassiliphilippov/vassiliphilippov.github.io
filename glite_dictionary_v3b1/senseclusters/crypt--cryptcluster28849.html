crypt

verb
to entomb in crypt
9.9
Pronunciation: / krɪpt / (AmE), / krɪpt / (BrE)
to place a dead body in a crypt or entomb it, especially in a stone chamber (archaic)

Examples

To crypt a body
In the 18th century, wealthy families often crypted their loved ones beneath grand cathedrals.
The nobleman was crypted with his ancestors in the family vault after his passing.
They crypted the remains in a stone chamber beneath the old abbey.
