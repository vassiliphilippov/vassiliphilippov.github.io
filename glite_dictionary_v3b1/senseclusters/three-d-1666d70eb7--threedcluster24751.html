three-D

adjective
giving the illusion of depth; three-dimensional
2.9
Pronunciation: / θɹiː ˈdiː / (AmE), / θɹiː ˈdiː / (BrE)
(of a film, image, or object) giving the illusion of depth or relating to three dimensions; short for 'three-dimensional'.

Examples

Three-D graphics
Many modern video games feature three-D graphics for a more immersive experience.
She wore special glasses to watch the three-D film at the cinema.
The entire movie was shot in three-D, so the action scenes felt incredibly lifelike.

Related Terms

  • antonym: 2D - having or relating to two dimensions; lacking depth
  • synonym: three-dimensional - having or relating to three dimensions or aspects
