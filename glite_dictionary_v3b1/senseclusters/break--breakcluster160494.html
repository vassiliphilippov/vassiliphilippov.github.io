break

verb
(boxing) command to separate during clinch
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
(in boxing) for a referee to order boxers to separate from a clinch during a match.

Examples

Referee break in boxing
As soon as the clinch began, the official moved in to break them apart.
During the match, the referee had to break the boxers several times.
The referee shouted break when the fighters got tangled in the corner.
