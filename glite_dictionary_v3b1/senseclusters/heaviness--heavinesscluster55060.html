heaviness

noun
oppressive or ponderous quality
6.0 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈhev.ɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈhev.ɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
a quality of being laborious, solemn, or oppressive, especially in style, manner, or atmosphere, lacking grace or fluency

Examples

Heaviness of the style
Critics commented on the heaviness of the film, noting its solemn tone and slow pace.
The heaviness of the novel's prose made it challenging to read for long periods.
There was a certain heaviness in his speech that left the audience disengaged.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lightness - the quality of being airy, graceful, or not oppressive in style or manner
  • synonym: ponderousness - the quality of being heavy, dull, or lacking lightness, especially in manner or style
