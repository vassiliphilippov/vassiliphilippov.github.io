invite

verb
ask someone to do something or attend
1.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈvaɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈvaɪt/ (BrE)
to ask someone in a friendly or polite way to come, join, or take part in something (such as an event, activity, or at your home)

Examples

To invite a friend
She invited her colleagues to join her for lunch after the meeting.
They invited questions from the audience at the end of the presentation.
We would like to invite you to our wedding next month.
She invited her colleagues over after work to celebrate the promotion.
They are always happy to invite new neighbors to their annual barbecue.
We would like to invite you to our home for dinner next Friday.
After the rain started, he invited the guests inside to get dry.
She invited her friends into the living room as soon as they arrived.
They often invite neighbors over for tea on weekends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ask - to request someone to do something
  • antonym: exclude - to deliberately not include or invite
  • synonym: host - to act as the person who invites guests and entertains them
  • synonym: summon - call or order someone to be present
  • synonym: welcome - to greet someone in a friendly way when they arrive
