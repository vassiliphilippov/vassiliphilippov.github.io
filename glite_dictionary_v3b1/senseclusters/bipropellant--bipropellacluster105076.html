bipropellant

noun
two-part rocket fuel
8.2
Pronunciation: /baɪ.prə.ˈpel.ənt/ (AmE), /baɪ.prə.ˈpel.ənt/ (BrE)
a rocket fuel system that uses two separate propellants, typically a fuel and an oxidizer, stored separately until use

Examples

Bipropellant system
The engineers chose a bipropellant system for the new satellite to improve performance.
A bipropellant engine combines fuel and oxidizer just before combustion for greater efficiency.
Many modern rockets rely on bipropellant technology to achieve higher thrust.
