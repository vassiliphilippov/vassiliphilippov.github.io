magpie

noun
large, black-and-white bird with a chattering call
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.paɪ / (BrE)
a large, long-tailed bird of the crow family with black-and-white plumage and a chattering call, known for its attraction to shiny objects

Examples

A magpie bird
A magpie perched on the fence, chattering loudly at the cat below.
Several magpies swooped down to collect shiny coins from the grass.
The distinctive black-and-white feathers make it easy to spot a magpie in the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crow - a large, black bird of the corvid family
