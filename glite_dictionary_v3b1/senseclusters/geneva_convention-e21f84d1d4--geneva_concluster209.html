Geneva Convention

noun
international treaties on wartime humanitarian treatment
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnivə kənˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / dʒəˈniːvə kənˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
an international agreement, first adopted in 1864 and subsequently revised, that sets standards for the humane treatment of wounded soldiers, prisoners of war, and civilians during armed conflicts

Examples

The Geneva Convention rules
Many countries are signatories to the Geneva Convention, pledging to follow its rules in armed conflict.
Reports of violations of the Geneva Convention often spark international outrage.
The Geneva Convention prohibits the mistreatment of prisoners of war.
