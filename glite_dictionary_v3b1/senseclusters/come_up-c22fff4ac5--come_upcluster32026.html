come up

verb
move closer or approach
1.7 18/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm ÊŒp / (AmE), / kÊŒm ÊŒp / (BrE)
to move toward or approach a place or person, often from a lower position or distance

Examples

A person comes up
If a stranger comes up to you, be cautious.
Please come up to the front of the classroom.
She saw her friend come up the steps and waved hello.

Synonyms

  • come up to
    2.2 7.63/m
    come up to means approaching a person to speak or touch, while come up means moving toward someone or a place from below.
  • come near
    1.8 1.72/m
    come near means moving closer in distance or time to something, while come up means moving toward a place or person, often upward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approach - to move nearer in distance or time
  • antonym: go away - to move farther from a place or person
