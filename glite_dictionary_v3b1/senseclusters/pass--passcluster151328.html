pass

verb
enter a state or condition
1.4
Pronunciation: / pæs / (AmE), / pɑːs / (BrE)
to disappear, end, or change state

Examples

To pass into silence
After hearing the news, he passed into a deep silence.

She passed into a peaceful sleep beside the window.
After a few hours, the discomfort finally passed and she felt better.
Don't worry, this difficult period will pass with time.
The excitement of the crowd will pass as the night goes on.
