disorder

verb
make untidy or disorganized
6.8
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to disturb the order or arrangement of something; to make untidy or disorganized

Examples

To disorder a room
A sudden interruption can easily disorder your train of thought.
The puppy disordered the neatly stacked newspapers by running through them.
The strong winds disordered the carefully arranged chairs in the garden.
