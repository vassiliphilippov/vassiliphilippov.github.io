vodoun

noun
Haitian-Caribbean religion involving spirits and magic
9.5
Pronunciation: / voʊˈduːn / (AmE), / vəˈduːn / (BrE)
A religion of West African origin, practiced chiefly in Haiti and other Caribbean countries, involving beliefs in spirits, magic, and ancestor worship.

Examples

Vodoun religion
Anthropologists have studied how vodoun influences social and communal life in Caribbean countries.
In Haiti, many people practice vodoun as a blend of African traditions and Catholic beliefs.
The rituals of vodoun often include music, dance, and offerings to the spirits.
