reship

verb
place or transfer on a ship again
6.6
Pronunciation: / riˈʃɪp / (AmE), / riˈʃɪp / (BrE)
to place (cargo or goods) on a ship again, or to transfer them from one ship to another

Examples

To reship cargo
Due to an unexpected detour, the company had to reship the cargo in Lisbon.
Port officials often reship containers as part of international trade logistics.
The damaged goods were reshipped to their destination after repairs.
