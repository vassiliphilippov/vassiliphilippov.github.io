condonement

noun
forgiveness of misconduct
Pronunciation: / kənˈdoʊnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈdəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the act of forgiving a spouse's marital misconduct, which may prevent divorce based on that misconduct in family law

Examples

Condonement of adultery
The judge ruled that her condonement of his actions meant she could not use them as grounds for divorce.
Repeated condonement of marital misconduct can affect the outcome of legal proceedings.
His lawyer argued that there was no evidence of condonement after the affair.

  • synonym: forgiveness - the act of pardoning someone for a mistake or wrongdoing
