macroeconomics

noun
study of the overall economy
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊˌɛkəˈnɑmɪks / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˌiːkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of economics dealing with the overall performance, structure, and behavior of a national or regional economy as a whole

Examples

Macroeconomics studies economy
Macroeconomics examines issues such as inflation, unemployment, and gross domestic product.
He decided to specialize in macroeconomics during his postgraduate studies.
The government used concepts from macroeconomics to develop its fiscal policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microeconomics - the branch of economics that studies individuals and firms in making decisions
