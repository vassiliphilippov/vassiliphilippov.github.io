rag

verb
reproach or scold harshly
Pronunciation: / ræɡ / (AmE), / ræɡ / (BrE)
to tease, criticize, or torment someone repeatedly, either playfully or cruelly

Examples

To rag a friend
She ragged her brother after he broke the vase.
The editor ragged the journalist for missing the deadline.
The manager ragged the staff for neglecting their duties.
Please stop ragging me over such a small mistake.
She got tired of her friends ragging her for being late all the time.
The other kids would often rag him about his unusual accent.
She was tired of being ragged by her classmates for every small mistake.
Sometimes older children rag the newcomers mercilessly at summer camp.
The other students would often rag him about his accent until he was visibly upset.
