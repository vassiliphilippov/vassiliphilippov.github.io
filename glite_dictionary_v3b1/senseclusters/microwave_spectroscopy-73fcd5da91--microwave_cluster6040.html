microwave spectroscopy

noun
study of atoms/molecules using microwaves
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌweɪ spɛkˌtrɑskəpi / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌweɪ spɛkˈtrɒskəpi / (BrE)
a technique that uses spectroscopy to study atomic or molecular resonances within the microwave region of the electromagnetic spectrum

Examples

Microwave spectroscopy technique
Advances in microwave spectroscopy have improved our understanding of molecular rotation.
Her research in microwave spectroscopy helped identify the energy levels in water vapor.
Scientists used microwave spectroscopy to determine the structure of the new chemical compound.
