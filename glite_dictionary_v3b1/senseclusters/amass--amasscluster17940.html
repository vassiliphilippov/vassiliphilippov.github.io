amass

verb
collect or gather in large quantities
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈmæs / (AmE), / əˈmæs / (BrE)
to collect or gather a large amount of something over a period of time

Examples

To amass wealth
Dust and clutter amass quickly if you don’t clean regularly.
Over the years, she managed to amass a fortune through wise investments.
The company sought to amass enough evidence to support its claim.
Over the years, he managed to amass a remarkable collection of rare coins.
The researcher is amassing valuable data for her groundbreaking study.
They amassed significant wealth through wise investments and careful planning.
