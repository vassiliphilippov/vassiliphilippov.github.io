objectlessness

noun
absence of attachments
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːb.dʒekt.les.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈɒb.dʒekt.les.nəs/ (BrE)
a psychological state in which a person lacks meaningful emotional attachments or internalized relationships with others

Examples

Emotional objectlessness
The therapist noted a sense of objectlessness in the patient, making it hard for him to form close relationships.
Feelings of objectlessness can develop after long periods of emotional neglect or isolation.
In object-relations theory, objectlessness is seen as a barrier to healthy emotional development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connectedness - the state of having emotional bonds or relationships
  • synonym: detachment - a state of emotional separation or lack of involvement
