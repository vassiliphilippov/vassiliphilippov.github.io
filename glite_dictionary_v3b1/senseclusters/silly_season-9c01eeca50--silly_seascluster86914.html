silly season

noun
period of trivial news stories
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪli ˌsizən / (AmE), / ˈsɪli ˌsiːzən / (BrE)
a period, usually in late summer, when news outlets publish trivial or frivolous stories due to a lack of major news events

Examples

The silly season news
Every year, during the silly season, newspapers seem to focus on odd animal stories and celebrity gossip.
Reporters complain that the silly season makes it hard to find serious newsworthy topics to cover.
With parliament in recess, the media entered its usual silly season of exaggerated scandals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slow news day - a day with little important news, leading to coverage of minor topics
