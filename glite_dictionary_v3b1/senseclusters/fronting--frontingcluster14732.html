fronting

noun
forward sound shift
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfrʌn.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈfrʌnt.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈfrʌn.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈfrʌnt.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a phonetic or phonological change in which a sound, especially a vowel, is pronounced with the tongue further forward in the mouth

Examples

Vowel fronting
Linguists observed fronting of certain vowels in the regional dialect.
The process of fronting can affect how words are pronounced over time.
Children often display fronting when learning to speak, especially with some consonants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backing - a change in which a sound is pronounced further back in the mouth
  • synonym: palatalization - a change where a sound becomes closer to a palatal articulation
