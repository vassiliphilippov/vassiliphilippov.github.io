viola da gamba

noun
historical bowed string instrument; bass viol
Pronunciation: / viˈoʊlə də ˈɡæmbə / (AmE), / viˈəʊlə də ˈɡæmbə / (BrE)
A bass viol, a bowed string instrument from the viol family, with a range similar to the cello, commonly used during the Renaissance and Baroque periods.

Examples

A viola da gamba instrument
A collection of violas da gamba was displayed at the music museum.
He learned to play the viola da gamba after studying the cello.
The viola da gamba was a popular instrument in Baroque chamber music.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cello - modern bowed string instrument with a similar range
  • synonym: viol - any member of a family of bowed string instruments used chiefly in the Renaissance and Baroque periods
