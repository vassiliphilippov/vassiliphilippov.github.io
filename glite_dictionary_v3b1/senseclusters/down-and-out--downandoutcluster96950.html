down-and-out

adjective
destitute; lacking resources or prospects
6.7 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdaʊn ən ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌdaʊn ənd ˈaʊt / (BrE)
Lacking money, resources, or prospects; destitute, especially to the point of being homeless.

Examples

Down-and-out man
After losing his job and home, he was completely down-and-out with nowhere to turn.
She once was down-and-out, but now she runs a successful business.
Volunteers help feed and shelter down-and-out individuals during the harsh winter months.
