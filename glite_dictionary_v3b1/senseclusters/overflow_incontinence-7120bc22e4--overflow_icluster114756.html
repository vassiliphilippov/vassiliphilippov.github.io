overflow incontinence

noun
urinary leakage from an overfull bladder
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvɚˌfloʊ ɪnˈkɑn.tɪ.nəns / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌfləʊ ɪnˈkɒn.tɪ.nəns / (BrE)
a type of urinary incontinence in which the bladder becomes overly full and leaks urine continuously, often due to blockage or weak bladder muscles

Examples

Overflow incontinence symptoms
Overflow incontinence can sometimes be a sign of nerve damage in patients with diabetes.
After her surgery, she developed overflow incontinence and required medical management.
Men with enlarged prostates are more prone to overflow incontinence.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: stress incontinence - urine leakage during physical activity or exertion
  • degree lower: urge incontinence - sudden, intense urge to urinate followed by involuntary loss of urine
