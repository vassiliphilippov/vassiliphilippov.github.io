TPN

noun
intravenous nutritional therapy
7.4 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ti pi …õn / (AmE), / tiÀê piÀê …õn / (BrE)
a method of providing nutrition by delivering a nutritionally complete solution directly into the blood through a central vein, used when a person cannot eat or absorb food normally

Examples

TPN nutrition method
Complications such as infection can arise from prolonged use of TPN.
Doctors often use TPN for premature infants who cannot feed orally.
The patient required TPN after surgery because his digestive system was not functioning.

Synonyms

  • total parenteral nutrition
    4.9 0.01/m
    total parenteral nutrition is the full formal name used in medical records, while TPN is the common abbreviation clinicians use in practice.
  • hyperalimentation
    9.9 0.01/m
    hyperalimentation refers to the intravenous supply of all nutrients often used as an older medical term, while TPN is the current clinical name.
  • intravenous feeding
    4.3 0.01/m
    intravenous feeding is a general term for giving nutrients into a vein and can be partial, while TPN means complete nutrition via a central vein.
