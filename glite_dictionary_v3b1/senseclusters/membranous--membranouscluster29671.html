membranous

adjective
resembling or made of a membrane
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛm.brə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.brə.nəs / (BrE)
relating to, made of, or resembling a membrane (a thin, flexible layer or covering)

Examples

Membranous tissue
In biology, many cells are enclosed by a membranous structure that regulates what enters and leaves.
The patient was diagnosed with membranous nephropathy, a disease affecting the kidney's filtering membranes.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the membranous tissue surrounding the organ.
In certain infections, membranous substances may form in the throat.
The plant’s leaves have a thin, almost membranous texture.
The surgeon noted a membranous layer surrounding the organ.

Related Terms

  • synonym: membrane - a thin, flexible layer of tissue or material
