penitente

noun
member of penance brotherhood
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnteɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnteɪ / (BrE)
a person who performs acts of penance, especially as part of a religious practice (sometimes specifically a member of a Catholic brotherhood in the American Southwest)

During Holy Week, a penitente led the procession through the village streets.
The penitentes are known for their acts of devotion and self-discipline.

During the festival, each penitente wore a simple robe as a sign of humility.
She admired the dedication of the penitente as he completed his journey of penance.
