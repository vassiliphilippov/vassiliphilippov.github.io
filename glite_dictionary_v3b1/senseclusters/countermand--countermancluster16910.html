countermand

verb
officially cancel a command or order
9.9 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.mænd/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.tə.mænd/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.mɑːnd/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.tə.mænd/ (BrE)
to officially cancel or reverse a previous command or order

Examples

To countermand instructions
Due to new information, the CEO chose to countermand the company's announcement.
The general decided to countermand the previous orders given to the troops.
The manager had to countermand the directive after realizing it contained errors.
After realizing the mistake, he immediately countermanded the shipment instructions.
Due to the unexpected risk, the general decided to countermand the attack order.
The CEO can countermand any company policy she deems unwise.
