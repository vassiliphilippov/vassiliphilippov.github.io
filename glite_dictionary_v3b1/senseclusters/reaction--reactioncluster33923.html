Examples

Political reaction
Some politicians built their careers by appealing to the forces of reaction.

Throughout history, periods of reform have often been followed by a wave of reaction.

Synonyms

  • backlash

    backlash is a strong public negative reaction against change, while reaction means a response that rejects or criticizes something.
  • pushback
    5.9 1.78/m

  • counterblast
    8.5 0.02/m
    counterblast is a loud, forceful reply aimed at strong critics, while reaction means any rejection or negative response in society.
  • resistance
    4.0 18/m
    resistance is active refusal or effort to stop change, while reaction covers a rejecting response that may be sudden or public.
  • opposition
    3.4 19/m
    opposition is the state or act of being against something, while reaction describes a specific rejecting response to change or action.
  • dissidence
    8.2 0.15/m
    dissidence is public disagreement with official policy or authority, while reaction refers to a response that rejects change in society.
  • dissent
    6.6 6.11/m
    dissent is speaking or showing disagreement, often formally or privately, while reaction is a broader rejecting response in public or politics.
  • antagonism
    6.9 2.25/m

  • objection
