reaction

noun
opposition to political or social progress
1.5 4.7
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
strong opposition to change or a response that deliberately rejects or criticizes something, especially in politics or society

Examples

Political reaction
Some politicians built their careers by appealing to the forces of reaction.
The new policy was met with fierce reaction from traditionalists who feared change.
Throughout history, periods of reform have often been followed by a wave of reaction.
Her early novels were a reaction to the traditional roles assigned to women in society.
His outspokenness was a direct reaction to years of being ignored.
The new art movement emerged as a reaction against strict academic standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backlash - a strong adverse reaction, especially by a large group of people
  • synonym: conservatism - commitment to traditional values and ideas
  • synonym: countermovement - a movement in opposition to another movement
  • antonym: progressivism - advocacy of social reform and progressive change
