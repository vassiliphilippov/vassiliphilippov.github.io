arteria celiaca

noun
major artery branching from abdominal aorta
8.6
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ˈtɪə.rɪə siː.lɪ.ˈæk.ə/ /; / /ɑːr.ˈtɪə.rɪə sə.lɪ.ˈæk.ə/ (AmE), /ɑː.ˈtɪə.rɪə siː.lɪ.ˈæk.ə/ /; / /ɑː.ˈtɪə.rɪə sə.lɪ.ˈæk.ə/ (BrE)
the arterial trunk arising from the abdominal aorta just below the diaphragm, which branches into the left gastric, common hepatic, and splenic arteries

Examples

The arteria celiaca trunk
Blockage of the arteria celiaca can affect blood supply to several vital abdominal organs.
During surgery, the arteria celiaca must be identified to prevent unintended injury.
The radiologist detected an aneurysm in the patient's arteria celiaca on the CT scan.
