flint

noun
hard stone used for tools or fire
6.9 4.9
Pronunciation: / flɪnt / (AmE), / flɪnt / (BrE)
a hard, gray or brown stone that breaks with a conchoidal fracture and produces a spark, formerly used for tools and for striking fire

Examples

A piece of flint stone
Archaeologists found ancient tools made of flint near the riverbank.
The cliffs along the coast contain layers of flint embedded in chalk.
To start the campfire, he struck a piece of steel against a flint.
