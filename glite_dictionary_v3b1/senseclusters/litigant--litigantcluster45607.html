litigant

noun
person involved in a lawsuit
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtɪɡənt /; / ˈlɪt̬.ɪ.ɡənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtɪɡənt /; / ˈlɪt.ɪ.ɡənt / (BrE)
a person or party involved in a lawsuit, either as the one suing (plaintiff) or being sued (defendant)

Examples

A litigant in court
As a seasoned litigant, she was familiar with the procedures of civil litigation.
Both litigants agreed to settle the dispute out of court before the trial began.
The litigant appeared in court to present his case against the corporation.
As a litigant, she had to attend several hearings.
Both the plaintiff and the defendant are considered litigants in court.
The judge instructed both litigants to present their evidence clearly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - the party who is sued in a lawsuit
  • synonym: plaintiff - the party who initiates a lawsuit
