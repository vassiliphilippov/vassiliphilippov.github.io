eat

verb
use up resources or materials
2.7 5.3
Pronunciation: /iːt/ (AmE), /iːt/ (BrE)
to use up or gradually destroy something, such as resources or materials

Examples

Machines eat energy
Running the generators all night will eat through our fuel reserves quickly.
The new appliances eat a lot of electricity, so our bills went up.
This project is really starting to eat into our budget.
Acidic rain can eat through concrete structures if left unchecked.
Over time, the salty air began to eat away at the metal bridge.
Rust will eventually eat holes in an unprotected iron fence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conserve - to use sparingly or save resources
  • synonym: consume - to use up, especially resources or energy
  • synonym: corrode - to destroy or damage slowly by chemical action
  • synonym: erode - to gradually destroy or be gradually destroyed through natural forces
  • synonym: expend - to use up, as in spending materials or energy
