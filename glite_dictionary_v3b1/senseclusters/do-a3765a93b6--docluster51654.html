DO

noun
doctor's degree in osteopathy
8.0 2.05/m
Pronunciation: / diÀê o ä / (AmE), / diÀê …ô ä / (BrE)
A professional degree for physicians who practice osteopathic medicine, emphasizing a holistic approach and the body's ability to heal itself.

Examples

Earn a DO degree
After eight years of training, she earned her DO and began practicing family medicine.
In the United States, both DO and MD degrees qualify physicians for full medical licensure.
The hospital employs several DOs who emphasize patient-centered care.

Synonyms

  • Doctor of Osteopathy
    5.1 0.00/m
    Doctor of Osteopathy is the full formal degree name used in documents and certificates, while DO is the common abbreviation.
