right of offset

noun
bank's right to seize funds to cover a defaulted loan
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt əv ˈɔfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈraɪt əv ˈɒfˌset / (BrE)
(Banking, law) The legal right of a bank to seize funds from a depositor's account to cover a defaulted loan owed to the bank by that depositor.

Examples

The right of offset by bank
If you default on your loan, the bank may exercise its right of offset and withdraw funds from your account.
Many customers are unaware that banks have a right of offset against their deposit accounts.
The terms of the contract clearly explain the lender's right of offset in the event of missed payments.
