Oregon myrtle

noun
aromatic tree of the Pacific coast
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrɪgən ˈmɜr.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ˈmɜː.tl̩ / (BrE)
A Pacific coastal tree (Umbellularia californica) with aromatic leaves, small clustered flowers, and hard, tough wood.

Examples

The Oregon myrtle tree
On our hike, we noticed the aromatic leaves of the Oregon myrtle along the trail.
Some people use Oregon myrtle leaves in cooking for their baylike flavor.
The wood of the Oregon myrtle is prized for furniture and decorative items.
