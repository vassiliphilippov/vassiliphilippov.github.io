front-porch campaigning

noun
campaigning from one's home; not traveling
7.2 6.1
Pronunciation: /frʌnt pɔːrtʃ kæm.ˈpeɪn.ɪŋ/ /; / /frʌnt pɔːrtʃ kəm.ˈpeɪn.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /frʌnt pɔːtʃ kæm.ˈpeɪn.ɪŋ/ /; / /frʌnt pɔːtʃ kəm.ˈpeɪn.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
A style of political campaigning in which a candidate delivers speeches and receives supporters at their home rather than traveling widely to campaign.

Examples

Front-porch campaigning style
In the era before mass media, front-porch campaigning helped candidates appear dignified and approachable.
Instead of traveling cross-country, the senator opted for front-porch campaigning to connect with voters locally.
William McKinley was famous for his front-porch campaigning during the 1896 election.
