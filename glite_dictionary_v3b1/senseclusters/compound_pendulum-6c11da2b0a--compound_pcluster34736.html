compound pendulum

noun
rigid body pendulum in physics
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.paʊnd ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ /; / /kəm.ˈpaʊnd ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.paʊnd ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ /; / /kəm.ˈpaʊnd ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ (BrE)
a pendulum consisting of a rigid body allowed to swing freely about a horizontal axis that does not pass through its center of mass

Examples

Compound pendulum motion
Engineers measure the center of mass to calculate the oscillation of a compound pendulum.
The period of a compound pendulum depends on the distribution of its mass.
Unlike a simple pendulum, a compound pendulum is made of an extended body rather than a point mass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: simple pendulum - an idealized pendulum consisting of a point mass suspended from a string or rod
