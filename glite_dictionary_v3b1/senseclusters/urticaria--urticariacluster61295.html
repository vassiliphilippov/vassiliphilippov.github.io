urticaria

noun
itchy skin condition with red wheals
9.2 7.6
Pronunciation: /ɜːr.tɪ.ˈkeə.rɪ.ə/ (AmE), /ɜː.tɪ.ˈkeə.rɪ.ə/ (BrE)
an itchy skin condition marked by pale, raised wheals with red edges, often caused by an allergic reaction to food, drugs, or other triggers

Examples

Urticaria skin rash
After eating strawberries, Amanda developed urticaria all over her arms and legs.
Certain medications can trigger urticaria in sensitive individuals.
The doctor diagnosed the red, itchy welts as a clear case of urticaria.
