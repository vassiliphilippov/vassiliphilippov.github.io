leptomeninges

noun
the two innermost layers of the meninges
Pronunciation: / ˌleptəˌməˈnɪndʒiz / (AmE), / ˌlɛptəʊməˈnɪndʒiːz / (BrE)
the two thin innermost layers of the meninges (the arachnoid mater and pia mater) that cover the brain and spinal cord, between which cerebrospinal fluid circulates

Examples

The leptomeninges layers
Inflammation of the leptomeninges is called leptomeningitis and can lead to serious neurological symptoms.
The leptomeninges play a critical role in protecting the brain and facilitating the flow of cerebrospinal fluid.
Tumors involving the leptomeninges may result in the spread of cancer within the central nervous system.
