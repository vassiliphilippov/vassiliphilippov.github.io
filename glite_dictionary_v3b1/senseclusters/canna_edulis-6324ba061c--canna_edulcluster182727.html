Canna edulis

noun
a canna plant with edible rootstock
Pronunciation: / ˈkænə ˈɛdʒʊlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkanə ˈɛdjʊlɪs / (BrE)
A species of canna grown chiefly for its edible rootstock, which is used to produce arrowroot starch.

Examples

Canna edulis rootstock
Farmers in some regions grow Canna edulis as an alternative source of carbohydrates.
In South America, Canna edulis is commonly cultivated for its starchy rhizomes.
The rootstock of Canna edulis is processed to make arrowroot flour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrowroot - starch obtained from the rootstock of several tropical plants, including Canna edulis
