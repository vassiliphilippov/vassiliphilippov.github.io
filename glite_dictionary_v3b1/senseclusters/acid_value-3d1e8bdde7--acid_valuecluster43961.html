acid value

noun
chemistry: measure of free acid in fat or oil
5.1 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈæs.əd ˈvæl.juː/ /; / /ˈæs.ɪd ˈvæl.juː/ (AmE), /ˈæs.ɪd ˈvæl.juː/ /; / /ˈæs.əd ˈvæl.juː/ (BrE)
A measure of the amount of free acid present in fat or oil, determined by the milligrams of potassium hydroxide required to neutralize it.

Examples

The acid value test
An increase in acid value indicates the deterioration of oils or fats over time.
Regular testing of the acid value helps maintain standards in food processing.
The acid value of the sample was determined to assess its quality.
