St. Thomas

proper noun
Italian theologian and philosopher, Doctor of the Church
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈtɑːməs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈtɒməs / (BrE)
A name often referring to St. Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274), Italian Dominican friar, philosopher, and theologian, recognized as a Doctor of the Church for his influential work reconciling faith and reason.

As a Doctor of the Church, St. Thomas is still influential today.

The teachings of St. Thomas have shaped Catholic doctrine for centuries.
