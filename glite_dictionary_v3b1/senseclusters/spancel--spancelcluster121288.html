spancel

verb
to tie animal's legs
Pronunciation: / ˈspænsəl / (AmE), / ˈspænsəl / (BrE)
to tie or fasten the legs of an animal together to restrict its movement, especially to prevent escape.

Examples

To spancel a cow
The farmer had to spancel the restless goat before repairing the fence.
To keep the young horse from wandering, they carefully spanceled its legs.
She learned how to spancel sheep during her summer on the farm.

  • antonym: unbind - to remove bindings or restraints
