trabeate

adjective
having horizontal beams in architecture
9.9 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈtreɪ.biː.ət/ /; / /ˈtreɪ.bi.eɪt/ (AmE), /ˈtreɪ.biː.ət/ /; / /ˈtreɪ.bi.eɪt/ (BrE)
(of architecture) constructed with horizontal beams or lintels, rather than arches; not arcuate.

Examples

A trabeate design
In contrast with the Gothic use of arches, Classical architecture is often trabeate in design.
Many ancient structures in Egypt display a trabeate architectural system.
The temple was built in a trabeate style, using massive stone lintels instead of arches.
