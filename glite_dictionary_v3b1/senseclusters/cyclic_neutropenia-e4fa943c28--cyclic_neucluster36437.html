cyclic neutropenia

noun
periodic decrease in neutrophils
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪklɪk ˌnuːtrəˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪk ˌnjuːtrəˈpiːniə / (BrE)
A rare blood disorder characterized by periodic decreases in the number of neutrophils, a type of white blood cell, occurring at regular intervals.

Examples

Cyclic neutropenia disorder
A diagnosis of cyclic neutropenia can typically be made after observing the periodic pattern of neutrophil levels.
Patients with cyclic neutropenia may experience regular intervals of increased susceptibility to infections.
Treatment for cyclic neutropenia often involves careful monitoring of blood counts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neutropenia - a condition of abnormally low neutrophils
