frequency

noun
number of times an event occurs
2.6 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfriː.kwən.siː/ (AmE), /ˈfriː.kwən.siː/ (BrE)
the number of times something happens, especially in a period of time or in statistics

Examples

The frequency of events
In the survey, the most common response had a frequency of 45.
The frequency of car accidents tends to increase in bad weather.
The table shows the frequency of each answer given by participants.
Doctors monitor the frequency of symptoms to track the progression of the disease.
Radio stations each broadcast on a different frequency.
The frequency of safety drills increased after the accident.
Statisticians often use frequency to compare different categories in a dataset.
The frequency of red cars in the survey was higher than that of any other color.
The frequency with which an outcome appears can reveal important patterns in data.
