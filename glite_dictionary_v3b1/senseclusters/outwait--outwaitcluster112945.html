outwait

verb
to wait longer
7.0
Pronunciation: /aʊt.ˈweɪt/ /; / /ˈaʊt.weɪt/ (AmE), /aʊt.ˈweɪt/ /; / /ˈaʊt.weɪt/ (BrE)
to wait longer than someone else, usually to gain an advantage or until a situation changes

Examples

To outwait a rival
They hoped to outwait the traffic jam by staying at the cafe until the roads cleared.
The negotiators tried to outwait each other, refusing to make the first move.
Sometimes, the best strategy is simply to outwait your opponent and remain patient.
The clever negotiator managed to outwait the other side and secure a better contract.
If you can outwait your opponents, you might win the game by patience alone.
She decided to outwait the rain rather than walk home in the storm.
He decided to outwait his competitors by staying at the negotiation table longer.
Sometimes, the best strategy is simply to outwait your opponent until they lose patience.
The cat managed to outwait the dog and finally got the sunny spot by the window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endure - to continue through a difficult situation
  • antonym: give up - to stop trying or waiting
  • synonym: outlast - to last longer than something or someone else
  • antonym: yield - to give up or surrender, especially before someone else
