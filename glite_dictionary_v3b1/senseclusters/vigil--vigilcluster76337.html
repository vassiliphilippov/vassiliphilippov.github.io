vigil

noun
period of devotional wakefulness, often before a festival
7.9 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪdʒəl /; / ˈvɪdʒ.əl / (AmE), / ˈvɪdʒɪl /; / ˈvɪdʒ.ɪl / (BrE)
a period of staying awake, especially at night, to pray, keep watch, or show support for someone or something

Examples

A vigil candlelight
A candlelight vigil was organized to commemorate the victims of the tragedy.
Hundreds of people attended the vigil held at the cathedral on Christmas Eve.
They kept a silent vigil throughout the night, praying for their loved ones.
After the storm, many people observed a silent vigil for the victims.
During his illness, his family maintained a constant vigil at his bedside.
She kept a lonely vigil by the window all night.
Friends and family held a vigil at the hospital, hoping for good news.
Security cameras maintained a silent vigil over the crowded plaza.
The police kept a close vigil outside the suspect's house throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sleep - the natural condition of rest in which you are unconscious
  • synonym: wake - a watch or vigil, especially associated with a funeral or the night before
  • synonym: wakefulness - the state of being awake and alert
  • synonym: watch - a period of guarding or observing, especially at night
