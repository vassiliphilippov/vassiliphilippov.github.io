fall equinox

noun
autumnal equinox (around September 22)
7.3 4.8
Pronunciation: /fɔːl ˈe.kwɪ.nɑːks/ /; / /fɑːl ˈe.kwɪ.nɑːks/ (AmE), /fɔːl ˈe.kwɪ.nɒks/ /; / /fɔːl ˈe.kwə.nɒks/ (BrE)
The time at which the sun crosses the celestial equator, resulting in nearly equal day and night, marking the beginning of autumn in the Northern Hemisphere, usually around September 22.

Examples

The fall equinox date
After the fall equinox, the days begin to grow shorter and the nights longer in the Northern Hemisphere.
Many cultures hold festivals to celebrate the fall equinox.
The fall equinox usually occurs on September 22 or 23 each year.
