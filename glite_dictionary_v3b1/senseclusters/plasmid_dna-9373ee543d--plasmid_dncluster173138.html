plasmid DNA

noun
circular, self-replicating DNA molecule
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈplæz.mɪd ˌdiː.enˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈplaz.mɪd ˌdiː.enˈeɪ / (BrE)
a small circular DNA molecule found in cells, separate from chromosomal DNA, capable of independent replication

Examples

Plasmid DNA molecule
Antibiotic resistance can be transferred between bacteria through plasmid DNA.
Scientists often insert new genes into plasmid DNA to produce genetically modified bacteria.
The plasmid DNA was isolated using a specialized extraction method for analysis.
