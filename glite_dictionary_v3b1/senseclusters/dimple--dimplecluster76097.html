dimple

noun
slight depression or indentation
4.5 1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪmpəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪmpəl / (BrE)
a small, rounded indentation or depression on a surface

Examples

Metal dimple
A shallow dimple formed in the metal after it was lightly struck.

A dimple on the punch card might cause confusion during tallying.
Inspectors carefully examined each punch card for any dimple or hanging chad.
The election recount was delayed because some ballots had a dimple rather than a fully punched hole.
