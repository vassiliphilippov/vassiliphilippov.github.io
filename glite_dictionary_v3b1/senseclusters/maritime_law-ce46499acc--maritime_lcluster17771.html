maritime law

noun
law governing navigation and commerce at sea
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrɪˌtaɪm lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmærɪtaɪm lɔː / (BrE)
the branch of law dealing with matters relating to shipping, navigation, and commerce on seas and other navigable waters, including territorial and international issues.

Examples

Maritime law rules
A solid understanding of maritime law is essential for anyone working in the shipping industry.
Many international disputes over shipping are settled according to maritime law.
The captain consulted a lawyer who specialized in maritime law after the incident at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admiralty law - the same branch of law dealing with maritime matters
