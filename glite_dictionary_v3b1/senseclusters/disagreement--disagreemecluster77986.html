disagreement

noun
conflict of opinions or attitudes
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈɡrimənt /; / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
a situation or act in which people have different opinions and do not agree

Examples

A disagreement between friends
Despite their disagreement, they remained good friends.
The meeting ended in disagreement rather than consensus.
There was a disagreement between the two colleagues about the best way to proceed.
Even close friends can have a disagreement from time to time.
There was a heated disagreement between the two colleagues during the meeting.
They managed to resolve their disagreement before it got out of hand.
A minor disagreement between friends shouldn’t ruin a lifelong relationship.
The two scientists had a disagreement over the interpretation of the data.
There was obvious disagreement among the committee members about the final decision.
