noun

a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable, suggesting a similarity between them

Examples

A metaphor in speech
Calling a careless student a "bull in a china shop" uses metaphor to highlight the student's clumsiness.
In literature, a metaphor can create powerful imagery and make abstract concepts more relatable.
The phrase "time is a thief" is a well-known metaphor for the way time seems to take things away from us.
