lazy

adjective
moving slowly and gently

moving slowly and gently; lacking briskness or energy in movement or progression

Examples

Lazy river flow

We spent the afternoon drifting down the lazy river under the sun.

    sluggish means moving or reacting slowly with low energy, while lazy suggests a choice not to work or move much.
  • slow-moving
    2.3 1.81/m
    slow-moving goes at a low speed and describes motion, while lazy often shows a lack of effort or willingness to act.
  • lethargic
    5.3 0.99/m
    lethargic feels very low in energy and can be medical, while lazy often means a person chooses not to try.
  • slowgoing
    4.7 0.00/m
    slowgoing progresses at an unhurried pace and lacks drive, while lazy suggests unwillingness to put in effort.
  • plodding
    5.3 0.04/m
    plodding moves slowly and steadily, often through hard work, while lazy avoids effort and may do little or nothing.
  • laggardly
    8.1 0.03/m
    laggardly acts slowly or late in response and sounds formal, while lazy implies a personal unwillingness to move quickly.
