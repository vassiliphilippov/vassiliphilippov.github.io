self-indulgence

noun
excessive gratification of personal desires
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌl.dʒəns /; / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌl.dʒəns /; / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (BrE)
excessive or unrestrained gratification of one's own desires or appetites

Examples

A moment of self-indulgence
He regretted his moments of self-indulgence after realizing the negative impact on his health.
Her self-indulgence led her to spend the entire weekend binge-watching movies and eating junk food.
The novel criticizes the self-indulgence of the wealthy class in the early twentieth century.
He acknowledged that his self-indulgence was getting in the way of healthier habits.
Her self-indulgence led her to spend every weekend at luxury spas.
Too much self-indulgence can sometimes prevent people from achieving their long-term goals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hedonism - the pursuit of pleasure as the highest good
  • synonym: intemperance - lack of moderation or restraint, especially in eating or drinking
  • antonym: self-control - restraint exercised over one's own impulses, emotions, or desires
