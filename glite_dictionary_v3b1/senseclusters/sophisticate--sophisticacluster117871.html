sophisticate

verb
make less natural or innocent
Pronunciation: / səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make a person or thing more complex, refined, or less naive, often through experience or culture

Examples

To sophisticate a person
Some believe that too much education can sophisticate a child's innocence.
The city life tended to sophisticate the newcomers, changing their simple ways.
Traveling abroad can sophisticate a person's tastes and manners.
As societies evolve, they often sophisticate their political systems and institutions.
Modern technology tends to sophisticate even the simplest devices over time.
The engineer tried to sophisticate the machine so it could handle multiple tasks at once.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrupt - to make someone or something less pure or innocent
  • synonym: refine - to make someone or something more polished or cultured
  • antonym: simplify - make less complex, more simple
