furnace lining

noun
heat-resistant furnace wall layer
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝːnəs ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːnɪs ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer of heat-resistant material used to line the inner walls of a furnace to protect it from high temperatures

Examples

Heat-resistant furnace lining
Engineers selected a new furnace lining with an even higher melting point for the industrial plant.
Replacing a worn furnace lining can improve the furnace's efficiency and longevity.
The furnace lining must be inspected regularly for damage caused by extreme heat.
