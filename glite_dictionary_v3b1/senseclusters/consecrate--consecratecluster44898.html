consecrate

verb
appoint to a clerical post
7.4 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.sə.kreɪt/ /; / /ˈkɑːn.sə.krət/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.sə.kreɪt/ /; / /ˈkɒn.sə.krət/ (BrE)
to make or declare something or someone sacred, especially by a formal ceremony, or to dedicate them to a special purpose

Examples

To consecrate a church
It is traditional for the archbishop to consecrate new bishops in an elaborate ceremony.
She was consecrated as a deacon in a solemn ritual attended by the whole congregation.
The bishop will consecrate the new priest during the ceremony next Sunday.
She chose to consecrate her life to helping the poor and needy.
The church will consecrate the new altar during a special ceremony next Sunday.
They decided to consecrate the building for use as a place of worship.
She decided to consecrate her life to medical research.
The artist wanted to consecrate his latest work to his late mentor.
They chose to consecrate their new home to the service of their community.
