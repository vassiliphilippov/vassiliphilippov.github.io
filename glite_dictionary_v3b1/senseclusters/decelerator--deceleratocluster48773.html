decelerator

noun
factor that slows progress
7.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛləˌreɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛləˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or factor that causes a process, activity, or rate to slow down

Examples

A policy decelerator
Strict regulations acted as a decelerator for the company's growth.
The sudden increase in costs became a major decelerator in the project timeline.
Negative public opinion served as a decelerator to the proposed policy changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accelerator - a person or thing that speeds up a process
  • synonym: impediment - something that slows or blocks progress
