paramyxovirus

noun
virus group including mumps, measles viruses
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˌmɪksəˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌparəˌmɪksəˈvʌɪrəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of viruses, including those that cause mumps and measles, belonging to the family Paramyxoviridae.

Examples

A paramyxovirus infection
Both measles and mumps are caused by types of paramyxovirus.
Scientists are researching how the paramyxovirus family infects human cells.
The outbreak was traced to a rare paramyxovirus that spread rapidly among children.
