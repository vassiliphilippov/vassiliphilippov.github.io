demobilization

noun
transition from war to peace, disbanding troops
1.0 7.7
Pronunciation: / diˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / dɪˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / dɪˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transitioning from a state of war to peace, including the disbanding of armed forces and conversion of resources to civilian purposes

Examples

Military demobilization process
After the war ended, demobilization of military equipment began across several countries.
The government's demobilization plan aimed to reintegrate soldiers into civilian life quickly.
The rapid demobilization of factories led to a sharp rise in unemployment.
After the war ended, the country began the long process of demobilisation.
Effective demobilisation requires careful planning to reintegrate troops into civilian life.
The sudden demobilisation of thousands of soldiers caused economic challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demobilization - American spelling: the process of disbanding armed forces and transitioning to peace
  • antonym: mobilisation - the process of preparing troops or resources for war
  • antonym: mobilization - the act of preparing for war or other emergencies by organizing troops and resources
