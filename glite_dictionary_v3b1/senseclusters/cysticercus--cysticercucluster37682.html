cysticercus

noun
tapeworm larva
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈstɪ.sɜːr.kəs/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈstɪ.sɜː.kəs/ (BrE)
a small, fluid-filled larval stage of certain tapeworms, typically found in the tissues of animals

Examples

A cysticercus larva
The veterinarian found a cysticercus in the muscle tissue of the infected pig.
Proper cooking destroys cysticercus in pork, making it safe to eat.
Scientists study the life cycle of the cysticercus to understand how tapeworms infect animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - the mature, fully developed stage of an organism
  • synonym: larva - an immature form of an animal that undergoes metamorphosis
  • synonym: tapeworm - a parasitic flatworm that lives in the intestines of vertebrates
