dysplasia

noun
abnormal development of cells or organs
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪziə / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪzɪə / (BrE)
abnormal development of cells, tissues, or organs, or an abnormal structure resulting from such growth, especially as a medical condition

Examples

Hip dysplasia
Hip dysplasia is commonly diagnosed in some dog breeds and can lead to joint problems.
The biopsy confirmed the presence of cervical dysplasia, which required closer monitoring.
The doctor explained that dysplasia in the tissue could increase the risk of cancer.
