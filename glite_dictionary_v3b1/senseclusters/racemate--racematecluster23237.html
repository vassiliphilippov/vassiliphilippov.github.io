racemate

noun
mixture of enantiomers
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪsɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪˌmeɪt /; / ˈræsɪˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a chemical substance or mixture that contains equal amounts of left- and right-handed enantiomers

Examples

A racemate mixture
The laboratory produced a racemate to compare its properties with those of the pure enantiomers.
Pharmaceutical companies often test both the racemate and individual enantiomers for effectiveness.
A racemate may have different biological effects than its separate enantiomers.
The chemist separated the racemate into its individual enantiomers for further analysis.
A racemate often has different biological effects compared to its pure enantiomers.
The drug was initially produced as a racemate before researchers isolated the active form.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enantiomer - one of two mirror-image forms of a chiral molecule
  • synonym: racemic - containing equal amounts of enantiomers
  • synonym: racemic mixture - a mixture with equal amounts of two enantiomers
