preterition

noun
rhetorical omission for effect
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛtəˈrɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːtəˈrɪʃən / (BrE)
the rhetorical technique of briefly mentioning a subject while suggesting that significant details are being deliberately omitted

Examples

Preterition in speech
He employed preterition in his speech, alluding to scandals without revealing their specifics.
The attorney's use of preterition made the jury wonder what details were not being discussed.
Writers often rely on preterition to intrigue readers and hint at underlying tensions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paralipsis - rhetorical device where something is emphasized by pretending to omit it
