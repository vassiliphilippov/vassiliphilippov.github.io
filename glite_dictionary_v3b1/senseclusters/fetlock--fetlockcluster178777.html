fetlock

noun
joint area above a horse's hoof
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛt.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈfɛt.lɒk / (BrE)
the joint just above the hoof on a horse or other hoofed animal, often with a tuft of hair

Examples

Horse fetlock joint
An injury to a horse's fetlock can seriously affect its ability to walk or run.
The groom carefully brushed the horse's fetlock to remove dirt and debris.
White hair often grows around the fetlock, giving the horse a distinctive look.
A small tuft of hair often grows over the fetlock in many breeds.
The horse injured its fetlock while running in the field.
You should check your horse’s fetlock for any swelling or heat after exercise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hoof - the hard covering on the foot of horses and some other animals
