field-strip

noun
quick disassembly
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌstrɪp / (BrE)
the act or process of quickly disassembling a weapon or device for cleaning, inspection, or maintenance, usually without special tools

Examples

A quick field-strip
The soldier completed the field-strip of his rifle in under a minute during training.
Proper field-strip of equipment is essential for regular maintenance in the field.
After a long day, he performed a quick field-strip of his handgun before storing it.
