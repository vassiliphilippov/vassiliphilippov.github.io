consolidate

verb
make something stronger or more secure
1.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɑː.lɪˌdeɪt /; / kənˈsɑː.lɪ.deɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt / (BrE)
to make something stronger, more secure, or more stable

Examples

To consolidate power
She worked hard to consolidate her early gains and maintain her lead.
The company managed to consolidate its position in the market despite new competitors.
The leader hoped to consolidate his control over the party after the elections.
After weeks of rain, the dam's structure finally consolidated and became secure.
The wet soil began to consolidate as the sun dried it.
When heated, the powder will consolidate into a dense mass.
