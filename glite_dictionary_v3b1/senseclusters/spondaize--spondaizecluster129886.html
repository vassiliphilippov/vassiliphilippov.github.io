spondaize

verb
make spondaic; use spondees in verse
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈspɑːn.deɪz/ (AmE), /ˈspɒn.deɪz/ (BrE)
To make a verse or passage spondaic by the use of spondees (metrical feet with two long or stressed syllables).

Examples

To spondaize a verse
It's difficult to spondaize English verses because of the natural stress patterns of the language.
Some translators attempt to spondaize classical poetry to echo the original meter.
The poet chose to spondaize several lines to slow the rhythm of the stanza.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spondaic - relating to or consisting of spondees
