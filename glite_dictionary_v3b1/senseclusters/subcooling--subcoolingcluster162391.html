subcooling

noun
cooling below boiling point
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌkuːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌkuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or amount of cooling a liquid below its normal boiling or saturation temperature without it becoming a solid (especially in refrigeration systems)

Examples

Subcooling process
Engineers use subcooling to improve the efficiency of refrigeration systems.
The process of subcooling helps prevent vapor bubbles from forming in the liquid.
Proper subcooling is essential for maintaining safe and effective cooling in air conditioning units.
Engineers monitor subcooling to ensure the refrigeration system is working efficiently.
Proper subcooling helps prevent vapor bubbles from forming in the liquid line.
The technician adjusted the system to achieve the correct amount of subcooling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heating - raising the temperature of something
  • synonym: supercooling - cooling a liquid below its freezing point without it becoming solid
  • antonym: superheating - heating a liquid above its boiling point without it boiling
