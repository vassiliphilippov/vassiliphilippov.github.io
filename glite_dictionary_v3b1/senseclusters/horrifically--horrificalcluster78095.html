horrifically

adverb
extremely; very
Pronunciation: / həˈrɪfɪkli / (AmE), / hɒˈrɪfɪkli / (BrE)
used to emphasize a very great degree of something, not necessarily involving horror or fear

Examples

Horrifically expensive mistake
The repairs turned out to be horrifically expensive, much more than we had budgeted for.
She was horrifically late to the meeting and missed the important announcement.
The weather was horrifically hot during our entire vacation, making it hard to enjoy outdoor activities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • antonym: slightly - to a small degree
  • synonym: terribly - to a very great or severe degree
