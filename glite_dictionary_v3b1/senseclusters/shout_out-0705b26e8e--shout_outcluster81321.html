shout out

verb
utter a sudden loud cry
Pronunciation: / ʃaʊt aʊt / (AmE), / ʃaʊt aʊt / (BrE)
to say or call something loudly and suddenly, often to express emotion or attract attention

Examples

To shout out loud
He shouts out every answer in class without raising his hand.
She couldn't help but shout out when she saw the spider crawling on her desk.
The children shouted out as the roller coaster took a sharp turn.
Please don't shout out answers during the test; raise your hand instead.
She had to shout out to her friend across the busy street to get his attention.
The fans began to shout out the team's name as they entered the stadium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cry out - to make a loud vocal sound, especially in pain or fear
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly without using the vocal cords
  • synonym: yell - to shout loudly, especially when angry, in pain, or excited
