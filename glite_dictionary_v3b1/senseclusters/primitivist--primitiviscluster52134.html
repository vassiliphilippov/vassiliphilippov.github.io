primitivist

noun
advocate of simplicity
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪmɪtɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈprɪmɪtɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices a return to a simpler, more basic way of life or art

Examples

A primitivist thinker
The primitivist believed that modern technology had made life too complicated.
As a primitivist, she preferred handmade crafts over mass-produced goods.
Many artists in the early 20th century were influenced by primitivist ideas in their work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernist - a person who supports modern ideas or styles
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports traditional ideas or practices
