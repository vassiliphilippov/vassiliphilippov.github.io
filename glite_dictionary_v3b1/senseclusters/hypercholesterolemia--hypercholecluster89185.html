hypercholesterolemia

noun
high cholesterol in the blood
5.4
Pronunciation: / haɪpɚkəˌlɛstɚəˈlimiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəkɒˌlɛstərəˈliːmɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormally high levels of cholesterol in the blood, increasing the risk of heart disease and atherosclerosis.

Examples

High hypercholesterolemia levels
Medications may be prescribed to manage hypercholesterolemia when lifestyle changes are not enough.
People with hypercholesterolemia are often advised to change their diet and exercise regularly.
The doctor diagnosed him with hypercholesterolemia after a routine blood test showed very high cholesterol levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cholesterolemia - the presence of cholesterol in the blood
  • antonym: hypocholesterolemia - abnormally low cholesterol level in the blood
