Tarquin the Proud

proper noun
legendary last Etruscan king of Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːr.kwɪn ðə ˈpraʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.kwɪn ðə ˈpraʊd / (BrE)
the seventh and last Etruscan king of Rome, according to legend, known for his cruelty and expelled from the throne (reigned 534–510 BC)

Examples

Tarquin the Proud king
According to legend, Tarquin the Proud's tyranny led to the rise of Rome's republican government.
Stories of Tarquin the Proud have influenced many works of literature and history.
The Roman Republic was established after Tarquin the Proud was overthrown.
