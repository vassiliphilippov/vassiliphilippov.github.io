underplant

verb
to plant beneath
6.5
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈplænt/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈplɑːnt/ (BrE)
to plant smaller or lower-growing plants beneath or among larger plants or trees

Examples

To underplant tall trees
Many gardeners underplant tall shrubs with colorful ground cover to create a layered effect.
It is common to underplant large trees with ferns that thrive in the shade.
They decided to underplant the rose bushes with low-growing herbs for added interest.
Many gardeners underplant shade-loving flowers beneath tall trees to create a layered effect.
It is common to underplant bulbs among shrubs for added color in the spring.
You can underplant ferns under larger bushes to fill in empty spaces in your garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interplant - to plant different species together in the same area
  • antonym: overplant - to plant too many plants in a given area
