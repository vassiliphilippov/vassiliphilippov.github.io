tumbrel

noun
two-wheeled cart for farm or historical (execution) use
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌm.bɹəl /; / ˈtʌm.brəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌmb.rəl /; / ˈtʌm.brɪl / (BrE)
a two-wheeled cart, especially one used in the past on farms for carrying manure, or for transporting prisoners to execution during the French Revolution

Examples

A tumbrel cart
Farmers once used a tumbrel to carry heavy loads of manure across the fields.
Historians often mention the tumbrel in accounts of the French Revolution.
The condemned prisoner was led to the guillotine in a tumbrel during the Revolution.
During the French Revolution, prisoners were carried to their fate in a tumbril.
He studied the museum's weathered tumbril, imagining its somber journeys through Paris's streets.
The old tumbril creaked as it carried manure across the muddy field.
