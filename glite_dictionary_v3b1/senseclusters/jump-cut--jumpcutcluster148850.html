jump-cut

noun
abrupt film transition/edit
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌmpˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpˌkʌt / (BrE)
An abrupt transition from one scene or shot to another in film or video, often creating a noticeable break in continuity or indicating rapid passage of time.

Examples

A jump-cut scene
Many music videos rely on the jump-cut to create a fast-paced visual effect.
The director used a jump-cut to show the character aging instantly.
The sudden jump-cut between scenes gave the documentary a disjointed feel.
