capsulize

verb
enclose in a capsule
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.sjəˌlaɪz /; / ˈkæpsjəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkap.sjʊ.laɪz /; / ˈkæpsjʊlaɪz / (BrE)
to enclose or put something within a capsule, especially for protection or administration

Examples

To capsulize medicine
Pharmaceutical manufacturers often capsulize powdered medicines to improve dosage accuracy.
Some vitamins are specially capsulized to prevent them from dissolving in the stomach.
The researchers decided to capsulize the new supplement for easier administration.
After extraction, vitamins are often capsulised to preserve their potency.
Researchers have developed a method to capsulise the drug for targeted delivery.
The company plans to capsulise the powder for easier consumption.
