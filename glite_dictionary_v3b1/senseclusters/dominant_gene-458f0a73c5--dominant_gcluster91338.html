dominant gene

noun
gene that expresses its trait if present
3.1 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.mɪ.nənt dʒiːn/ /; / /ˈdɑː.mə.nənt dʒiːn/ (AmE), /ˈdɒm.ɪ.nənt dʒiːn/ /; / /ˈdɒm.ə.nənt dʒiːn/ (BrE)
a gene that expresses its effect in the phenotype even when only one copy is present, masking the effect of a recessive gene

Examples

A dominant gene trait
Brown eyes are often the result of a dominant gene inherited from one parent.
If a child inherits the dominant gene, they will display the associated trait regardless of the other allele.
Scientists explained how a dominant gene determines certain physical characteristics.
