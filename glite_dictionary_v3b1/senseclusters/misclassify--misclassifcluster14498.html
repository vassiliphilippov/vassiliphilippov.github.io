misclassify

verb
to assign incorrectly
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈklæsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈklɑːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to put something into the wrong group or category by mistake

Examples

To misclassify plants
The researcher accidentally misclassified several plant species in her report.
If you misclassify the data, your results may be misleading.
Some students were misclassified as advanced when they needed more support.
If you do not check the data carefully, you might misclassify some of the results.
The teacher accidentally misclassified several students into the wrong reading group.
It is important not to misclassify recyclable materials as trash.
If you misclassify the data, your results may be inaccurate.
The teacher accidentally misclassified several students' assignments in the wrong folder.
Scientists try not to misclassify new species when they are discovered.
