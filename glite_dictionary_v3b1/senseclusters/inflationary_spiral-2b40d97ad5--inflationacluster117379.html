inflationary spiral

noun
rapid, self-reinforcing inflation event
3.0 6.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃə.rɪ ˈspaɪ.rəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃə.rɪ ˈspaɪ.rəl/ (BrE)
an economic situation in which prices and wages rise rapidly and continuously, causing the value of money to fall sharply.

Examples

An inflationary spiral rises
During the 1970s, several nations struggled to escape from a persistent inflationary spiral.
Many economists warn that unchecked public spending can lead to an inflationary spiral.
The country experienced an inflationary spiral after the government printed too much money.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflation - reduction of the general level of prices
  • degree higher: hyperinflation - extremely rapid or out of control inflation
