synizesis

noun
chromatin contraction in meiosis
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɪzɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪzɪs / (BrE)
the contraction of chromatin into a dense mass at one side of the nucleus during the prophase of meiosis

Examples

Synizesis in meiosis
During meiosis, synizesis can be observed as chromatin condenses on one side of the nucleus.
Some researchers debate the significance of synizesis in plant gametogenesis.
The phenomenon of synizesis puzzled early cytologists studying cell division.
