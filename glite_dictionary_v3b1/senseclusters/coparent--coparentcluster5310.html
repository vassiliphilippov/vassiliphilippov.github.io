coparent

verb
to share child-raising
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌpɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌpɛrənt / (BrE)
to share the duties and responsibilities of raising a child with another person, especially after separation or divorce

Examples

To coparent a child
After their divorce, they agreed to coparent their son to ensure he felt supported by both parents.
It can be challenging to coparent effectively when communication breaks down between adults.
Many separated couples choose to coparent so their children maintain strong relationships with both families.
