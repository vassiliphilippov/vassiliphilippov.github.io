cargo cult

noun
Melanesian religious movement awaiting miraculous goods
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrɡoʊ kʌlt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːɡəʊ kʌlt / (BrE)
a religious movement, especially in Melanesia, where people believe that material wealth ('cargo') will arrive through spiritual or supernatural means, often imitating Western practices

Examples

Cargo cult rituals
Anthropologists have studied the rituals and beliefs of various cargo cults in the South Pacific.
The concept of a cargo cult is used metaphorically in discussions of imitating external features without understanding underlying principles.
The villagers developed a cargo cult after seeing Western supplies arrive by airplane.
Anthropologists have studied the rituals of the cargo cult to understand its origins in Melanesian culture.
The concept of the cargo cult highlights how cultural contact can result in new religious movements.
The villagers began building imitation airstrips, hoping the cargo cult prophecy would attract planes loaded with goods.
