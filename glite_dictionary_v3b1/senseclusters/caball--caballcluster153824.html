caball

verb
to plot secretly
Pronunciation: / kəˈbæl / (AmE), / kəˈbæl / (BrE)
to form a secret group in order to plot or conspire against someone or something

Examples

To caball secretly
The politicians were caballing late into the night to undermine their rival.
Rumors spread that several executives had been caballing to take control of the company.
He accused his colleagues of caballing against him to get him fired.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conspire - to secretly plan with others to do something harmful or illegal
  • antonym: cooperate - to work together for a common purpose, usually openly
  • synonym: plot - to secretly make plans to carry out an illegal or harmful act
