bombard

verb
attack repeatedly by throwing objects
2.6
Pronunciation: / bɑmˈbɑrd / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːd / (BrE)
to attack someone or something repeatedly by throwing or launching objects, such as stones, bombs, or missiles

Examples

To bombard the city
Angry protesters began to bombard the police with stones and bottles.
During the food fight, students bombarded each other with handfuls of spaghetti.
Kids love to bombard their friends with snowballs during winter.
Allied planes continued to bombard enemy positions throughout the night.
During the siege, they bombarded the fortress for several days without stopping.
The enemy forces began to bombard the city at sunrise, causing widespread panic among residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bomb - to attack with explosive devices
  • antonym: ceasefire - a temporary stoppage of fighting
  • synonym: pelt - to attack repeatedly with missiles or blows
  • synonym: shell - to fire shells at; bombard with artillery
