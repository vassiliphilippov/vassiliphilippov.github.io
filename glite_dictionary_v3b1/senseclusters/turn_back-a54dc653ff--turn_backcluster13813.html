turn back

verb
to force away or drive off
Pronunciation: / tɝn ˈbæk /; / tɝn bæk / (AmE), / tɜːn ˈbæk /; / tɜːn bæk / (BrE)
to stop or prevent someone or something from continuing, entering, or making progress

Examples

Police turn back cars
Heavy rain turned back the hikers before they reached the summit.
She tried to stay positive and turned back her doubts.
The security guards turned back anyone without a ticket.
International sanctions were imposed to turn back the spread of nuclear weapons.
The firefighters worked tirelessly to turn back the flames threatening the village.
With strong leadership, they hoped to turn back the rising tide of violence in the community.

  • antonym: encourage - to support the advance or growth of something
