influence

verb
have an effect on; shape or direct
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.flu.əns /; / ˈɪnfluəns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.flʊ.əns /; / ˈɪnfluəns / (BrE)
to affect or change how someone or something develops, behaves, or thinks

Examples

To influence a decision
Her words influenced my decision to apply for the job.
Leaders often influence public opinion through speeches.
Parental behavior can strongly influence a child's attitude and values.
He tried to influence his friends to join the club by emphasizing how fun it was.
Leaders often influence people not just through orders, but through their charisma and vision.
She managed to influence the board members with her persuasive arguments.
He was deeply influenced by his mentor in college.
Her leadership style has influenced the entire organization.
The weather can influence our travel plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affect - to have an influence on; produce a change in
  • antonym: coerce - to force someone to do something against their will
  • synonym: determine - to decisively direct or control the outcome
  • synonym: guide - to direct or have influence over someone or something
  • synonym: persuade - to cause someone to do something through reasoning or argument
