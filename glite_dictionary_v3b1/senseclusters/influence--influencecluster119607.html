to affect or change how someone or something develops, behaves, or thinks

Examples

To influence a decision
Her words influenced my decision to apply for the job.
Leaders often influence public opinion through speeches.
Parental behavior can strongly influence a child's attitude and values.
He tried to influence his friends to join the club by emphasizing how fun it was.
Leaders often influence people not just through orders, but through their charisma and vision.
She managed to influence the board members with her persuasive arguments.
He was deeply influenced by his mentor in college.
Her leadership style has influenced the entire organization.
The weather can influence our travel plans.

Synonyms

  • affect
    3.0 167/m
    affect means to cause a change in someone or something, while influence often suggests a broader or longer effect on development.
  • shape
    3.4 37/m
    shape helps form someone's development or character in a clear way, while influence covers many kinds of change over time.
  • mold
    3.6 3.72/m
    mold gives a strong, active form to behavior or ideas, while influence can be weaker and work more slowly.
  • act upon
    3.5 1.95/m
    act upon means to affect or cause change directly, while influence also includes a slow or indirect effect over time.
  • sway
    3.6 4.33/m
    sway changes someone's opinion or decision, while influence covers many kinds of change in behavior, development, or thought.
