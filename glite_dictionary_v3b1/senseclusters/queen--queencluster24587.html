queen

noun
fertile female insect in a colony
Pronunciation: / kwin / (AmE), / kwiːn / (BrE)
the main reproductive female in a colony of social insects or naked mole rats

Examples

Bee queen
In a beehive, the queen is responsible solely for reproduction.
The queen ant can lay thousands of eggs throughout her lifetime.
Without a queen, the colony cannot survive for long.
After the old queen died, several females competed to become the next reproductive individual.
In each colony, only the queen breeds while the other females are reproductively suppressed.
The queen is significantly larger than the worker mole rats and rules the colony with aggression.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drone - a male bee in a colony
  • antonym: worker - a non-reproductive member of an insect colony that performs labor
