inoperability

noun
unsuitability for surgery
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌɑːpərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌɒpərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the condition of being unsuitable or impossible to treat with surgery

Examples

Tumor inoperability
The doctor explained that the tumor's inoperability meant surgery was not an option.
Due to its location, the inoperability of the mass required the team to consider other treatments.
Advances in technology may reduce the inoperability of certain medical conditions in the future.
