bistate

noun
two-state device
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌsteɪt / (BrE)
a device or system, especially in electronics or computing, that has two stable states and is used to represent or store a binary value

Examples

A bistate circuit
The engineer checked the bistate to ensure it was correctly storing the binary value.
A bistate is essential in digital circuits for representing on and off conditions.
When the bistate failed, the system could no longer reliably store information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flip-flop - an electronic circuit with two stable states used for storing binary data
  • antonym: multistate - a device or system with more than two stable states
