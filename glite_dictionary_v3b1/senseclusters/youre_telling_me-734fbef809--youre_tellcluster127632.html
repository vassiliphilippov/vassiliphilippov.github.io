you're telling me

interjection
used to show strong agreement or empathy
Pronunciation: / jʊr ˈtɛlɪŋ mi / (AmE), / jɔː ˈtɛlɪŋ miː / (BrE)
used to express strong agreement, understanding, or empathy, often in response to something obvious or unpleasant.

Examples

You're telling me
"It's been a long week." "You're telling me."
"So we have to redo the whole project?" "You're telling me!"
"The prices just keep going up?" "You're telling me!"
