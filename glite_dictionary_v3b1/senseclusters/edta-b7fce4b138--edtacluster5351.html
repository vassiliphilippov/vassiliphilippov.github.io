EDTA

noun
chemical compound for binding metal ions
7.5
Pronunciation: /iː.diː.tiː.ˈeɪ/ (AmE), /iː.diː.tiː.ˈeɪ/ (BrE)
a synthetic chemical compound used to bind and remove metal ions from solutions, commonly in treating heavy metal poisoning and in laboratory analysis

Examples

EDTA binds metals
EDTA binds to metal ions, allowing for their removal from the bloodstream.
Laboratories often use EDTA as an anticoagulant in blood sample collection tubes.
The patient was treated with EDTA to counteract lead poisoning.
