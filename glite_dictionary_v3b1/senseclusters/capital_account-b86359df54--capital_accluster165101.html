capital account

noun
record of international financial flows (economics)
3.8 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.pɪ.təl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.pɪ.təl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (BrE)
(in economics) the part of a country's balance of payments that records the flow of financial assets, such as investments and loans, into and out of the country.

Examples

Country's capital account
Global economic changes can greatly affect a nation's capital account position.
Many developing countries struggle to keep their capital account balanced as money flows in and out.
The government's capital account showed a significant surplus due to foreign investments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: current account - part of the balance of payments recording trade in goods and services
