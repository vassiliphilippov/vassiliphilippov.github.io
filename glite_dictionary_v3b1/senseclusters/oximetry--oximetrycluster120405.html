oximetry

noun
measuring blood oxygen
Pronunciation: / ɑkˈsɪmətri / (AmE), / ɒkˈsɪmətri / (BrE)
the measurement of oxygen levels in the blood or body tissues, usually using a special device

Examples

Oximetry measurement
The doctor used oximetry to monitor the patient's oxygen saturation during surgery.
Portable oximetry devices are often used in emergency situations to check breathing.
Regular oximetry checks are important for patients with chronic lung disease.
The nurse used oximetry to monitor the patient's blood oxygen during surgery.
Advances in oximetry have improved the care of patients with respiratory illnesses.
Portable oximetry devices allow doctors to check oxygen levels quickly and easily.
The nurse used oximetry to quickly check the patient's blood oxygen level.
Portable oximetry devices are common in emergency medical kits.
Regular oximetry monitoring is important for patients with respiratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blood gas analysis - a test measuring oxygen and other gases in the blood
  • antonym: capnometry - the measurement of carbon dioxide levels in respiratory gases
  • synonym: pulse oximetry - a noninvasive method for measuring blood oxygen using a fingertip device
  • antonym: spirometry - the measurement of lung function, especially the amount and speed of air inhaled and exhaled
