lobby

noun
group seeking to influence legislation
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑː.bi /; / ˈlɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.i /; / ˈlɒbi / (BrE)
a group of people who try to influence politicians or public officials to support a particular cause or interest

Examples

A lobby group
Environmental groups formed a lobby to push for stricter pollution controls.
The tobacco lobby spent millions of dollars to oppose new regulations.
There has been criticism of the influence wielded by the tech lobby in government decision-making.
A powerful gun rights lobby poured millions into the election campaign.
Consumer advocates accused the tobacco lobby of misleading the public about health risks.
The environmental lobby is pushing for stricter emissions regulations in parliament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interest group - group organized to promote a particular interest or cause
  • synonym: pressure group - organization that tries to influence public policy
