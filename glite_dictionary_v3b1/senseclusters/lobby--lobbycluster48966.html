lobby

noun
group seeking to influence legislation
3.9 5.76/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀê.bi /; / Ààl…ëÀêbi / (AmE), / Ààl…íb.i /; / Ààl…íbi / (BrE)
a group of people who try to influence politicians or public officials to support a particular cause or interest

Examples

A lobby group
Environmental groups formed a lobby to push for stricter pollution controls.
The tobacco lobby spent millions of dollars to oppose new regulations.
There has been criticism of the influence wielded by the tech lobby in government decision-making.
A powerful gun rights lobby poured millions into the election campaign.
Consumer advocates accused the tobacco lobby of misleading the public about health risks.
The environmental lobby is pushing for stricter emissions regulations in parliament.

Synonyms

  • lobby group
    3.9 0.14/m
    lobby group works to influence lawmakers and policy directly, while lobby names the group that tries to sway officials.
  • interest group
    3.1 6.28/m
    interest group seeks to shape public policy for its members, while lobby names the group that tries to influence officials.
  • special interest group
    3.9 0.25/m
    special interest group defends a narrow set of member benefits and aims to influence policy, while lobby is the general name.
  • special-interest group
    3.7 1.41/m
    special-interest group sounds more formal or critical in politics, while lobby is a neutral label for the influencing group.
  • advocacy group
    3.9 3.30/m
    advocacy group actively campaigns and informs the public as well as lobbying, while lobby simply names the influencing group.
  • pressure group
    4.1 0.64/m
    pressure group uses public or private pressure to push decisions, while lobby is a general term for influence groups.
  • special interest
    3.6 5.34/m
    special interest often refers to a specific benefit or the group that defends it, while lobby names those who influence officials.
  • third house
    9.1 0.00/m
    third house is US political slang for powerful interest groups seen as a separate chamber, while lobby is a neutral term.
