prepublication

adjective
before publication
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
existing or occurring before the official publication of a work, such as drafts or proofs released in advance.

Examples

Prepublication draft
The author shared a prepublication version of the article with selected reviewers.
Researchers often request prepublication feedback to improve their manuscripts before release.
A prepublication proof was sent to the editor for a final check.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postpublication - occurring after the official publication of a work
  • synonym: preliminary - happening before something more important, often as a preparation
