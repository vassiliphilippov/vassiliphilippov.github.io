sensory deprivation

noun
removal of sensory stimuli, sometimes as torture
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.sə.ri ˌdɛ.prəˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.səri ˌdɛprɪˈveɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The deliberate reduction or removal of stimuli from one or more of the senses, sometimes used as a form of psychological torture or for experimental purposes.

Examples

Prolonged sensory deprivation
Prolonged sensory deprivation can have serious psychological effects on a person.
Some meditation techniques use controlled sensory deprivation to enhance self-awareness.
The prisoners were subjected to sensory deprivation as a form of punishment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from other people or things
  • antonym: stimulation - the action of arousing interest or activity in something
