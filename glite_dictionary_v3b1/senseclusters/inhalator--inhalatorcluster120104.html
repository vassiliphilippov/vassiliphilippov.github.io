inhalator

noun
device for artificial respiration
9.6 6.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈheɪ.lə.tər/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈheɪ.lə.tə/ (BrE)
a device for administering artificial respiration by supplying air or oxygen to the lungs over a period of time

Examples

Emergency inhalator
Emergency kits are often equipped with an inhalator for use in cases of smoke inhalation.
The old-style inhalator required manual operation by hospital staff.
The patient was placed on an inhalator after experiencing severe respiratory distress.
