foreseeable

adjective
able to be predicted; expected
7.5
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈsiəbl̩ / (AmE), / fɔːˈsiːəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be anticipated or predicted in advance; likely to happen in the near or immediate future.

Examples

The foreseeable future
All foreseeable costs were considered in the financial plan.
For the foreseeable future, remote work will remain a popular option.
The risks involved in the project are all foreseeable and have been addressed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predictable - able to be predicted or expected
  • antonym: unforeseeable - not able to be anticipated or predicted
