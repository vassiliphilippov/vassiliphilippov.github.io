chicot

noun
North American tree with pods used as coffee substitute
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈʃiː.koʊ/ (AmE), /ˈʃiː.kəʊ/ (BrE)
A handsome tree of central and eastern North America with large bipinnate leaves, greenish-white flowers, and woody brown pods containing seeds sometimes used as a coffee substitute.

Examples

The chicot tree
Botanists are studying the ecological role of the chicot in native woodlands.
Some settlers brewed a hot drink from chicot seeds when coffee was scarce.
The tall chicot stood out against the autumn sky with its distinctive seed pods.
