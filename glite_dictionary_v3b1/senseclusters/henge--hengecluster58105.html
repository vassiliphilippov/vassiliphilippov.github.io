henge

noun
prehistoric earthwork
Pronunciation: / hɛndʒ / (AmE), / hɛndʒ / (BrE)
a prehistoric monument consisting of a circular area enclosed by a bank and ditch, often containing standing stones or timber posts

Examples

A henge monument
Archaeologists discovered a new henge near the river, dating back thousands of years.
Stonehenge is the most famous henge in the world, attracting millions of visitors annually.
The ancient henge was believed to have been used for ceremonial purposes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barrow - a prehistoric burial mound
  • synonym: stone circle - a prehistoric monument of standing stones arranged in a circle
