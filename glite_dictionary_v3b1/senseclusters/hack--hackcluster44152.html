hack

verb
to cut roughly with a sharp tool
4.0 4.7
Pronunciation: / hæk / (AmE), / hæk / (BrE)
to cut or chop something roughly or repeatedly, often with heavy blows; also, to alter something (like a manuscript) in a rough or careless way

Examples

To hack a branch
He used a machete to hack through the tangled undergrowth.
She hacked away at the dead branches blocking the path.
The butcher hacked the meat into smaller pieces for the stew.
During the storm, crews hacked fallen branches off the road to clear a path.
He hacked his way through the dense jungle with a machete.
The sculptor hacked away at the block of stone until a rough shape emerged.
In desperation, the playwright decided to hack his script before the deadline.
She hated to hack her manuscript, but many scenes had to go.
The editor had to hack the article to fit the word limit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chop - to cut something into pieces with repeated sharp blows
  • synonym: edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting or revising it
  • antonym: polish - to improve something until it is of a very high standard
  • synonym: revise - to change or correct something, especially a piece of writing
  • synonym: slash - to cut or strike with a sweeping or vigorous blow
  • antonym: smooth - to make a surface even or free from roughness
