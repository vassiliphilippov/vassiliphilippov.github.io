glasswort

noun
a salt marsh plant with prickly leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæsˌwɜrt /; / ˈɡlæsˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːswɜːt / (BrE)
a plant found in salt marshes or coastal areas, with fleshy or prickly stems, sometimes burned to make soda ash for glass-making

Examples

Glasswort plant
A botanist walked along the estuary collecting samples of glasswort for her research.
Historically, glasswort was burned to create soda ash for glass production.
The tidal flats were covered in dense patches of glasswort, turning red in the autumn sunlight.
Early glassmakers burned glasswort to obtain soda ash for their furnaces.
The marsh along the coast was dotted with patches of glasswort thriving in the salty environment.
We spotted a cluster of glasswort during our walk in the tidal wetlands.
