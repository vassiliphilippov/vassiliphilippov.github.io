moveable feast

noun
religious holiday with varying date
4.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmuː.və.bəl fiːst/ /; / /ˈmoʊ.və.bəl fiːst/ (AmE), /ˈmuː.və.bəl fiːst/ (BrE)
A religious holiday whose date changes each year, rather than being fixed.

Examples

A moveable feast date
Easter is a classic example of a moveable feast in the Christian calendar.
Many cultures observe at least one moveable feast based on lunar or solar calendars.
Unlike Christmas, which is celebrated on December 25th every year, a moveable feast shifts its date annually.
