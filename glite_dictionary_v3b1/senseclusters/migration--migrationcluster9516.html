migration

noun
group moving from one place to another
1.4 3.1
Pronunciation: /maɪ.ˈgreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /maɪ.ˈgreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the movement of people from one place, country, or region to another, often in groups and sometimes to settle permanently or temporarily

Examples

Migration of people
Each year, the migration of farm workers brings new life to the rural towns.
That migration of people transformed the entire region over a single decade.
The museum featured an exhibit on the migration of Irish families during the 19th century.
Government policies often have a significant impact on migration flows.
Seasonal migration patterns can be influenced by changes in job opportunities.
The recent increase in migration to urban areas has transformed the region's economy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emigration - the act of leaving one's own country to settle elsewhere
  • antonym: immigration - the act of coming to live permanently in a foreign country
