skimmer

noun
seabird with a long lower bill used for skimming
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪmər / (AmE), / ˈskɪmə / (BrE)
a gull-like seabird with an elongated lower mandible, which it immerses in the water while flying to catch food

Examples

A skimmer bird
A group of skimmer chicks huddled together on the sandy shore.
Many photographers travel to coastal areas to observe skimmers in their natural habitat.
The skimmer glided just above the water, dipping its long bill to catch small fish.
