bog

verb
to become stuck or slowed down
6.1 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / bÉ‘É¡ /; / bÉ‘g / (AmE), / bÉ’É¡ / (BrE)
to become stuck or slow down, especially in a process, activity, or difficult situation

Examples

Projects bog down
Discussions may bog if everyone talks at once.
She tends to bog down in details instead of focusing on the bigger picture.
The project began to bog down once new regulations were introduced.
The discussion started to bog when they argued over details.
We shouldn't let the project bog in unnecessary bureaucracy.
Without clear leadership, the reforms may bog and lose momentum.

Synonyms

  • bog down
    5.8 0.29/m
    bog down traps progress in a complicated task and makes work slow, while bog more generally means to slow or get stuck.
  • get stuck
    2.2 5.92/m
    get stuck means becoming unable to move or continue because something blocks you, while bog often describes slowing or stalling a process.
  • mire
    7.6 2.58/m
    mire means to leave someone or something trapped in mud or a hard situation, while bog focuses on slowing or getting stuck.
  • clog up
    3.7 0.45/m
    clog up means to block a flow so movement is hard, while bog is used for processes or tasks that slow or stall.
  • clog
    3.2 1.62/m
    clog blocks or stops physical or system flow by pressure, while bog usually describes being stuck or slowed in an activity.
