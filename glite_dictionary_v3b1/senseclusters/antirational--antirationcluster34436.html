antirational

adjective
against logical thinking
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˈræʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈræʃənəl / (BrE)
opposed to or rejecting the use of reason or logical thinking

Examples

Antirational thinking
Some critics called the new policy antirational because it ignored scientific evidence.
His antirational beliefs made it difficult to have a logical discussion with him.
The debate became antirational when participants stopped considering facts and relied on emotions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rational - based on reason or logic
