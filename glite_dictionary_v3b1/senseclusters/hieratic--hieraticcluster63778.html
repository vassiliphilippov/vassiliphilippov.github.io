hieratic

noun
cursive Egyptian script used by priests
Pronunciation: / haɪ.əˈræt.ɪk / (AmE), / haɪ.əˈræt.ɪk / (BrE)
A cursive form of ancient Egyptian writing, used especially by priests for religious texts.

Examples

Ancient hieratic script
Researchers have found temple accounts written in hieratic dating back three thousand years.
The priest carefully copied the ancient texts in hieratic onto strips of papyrus.
Unlike hieroglyphics, hieratic was written more quickly and used by priests for everyday administrative work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hieroglyphics - the formal writing system of ancient Egypt using pictorial symbols
