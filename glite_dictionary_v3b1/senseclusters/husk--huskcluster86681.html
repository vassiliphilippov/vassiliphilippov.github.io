husk

verb

3.9 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / hÊŒsk / (AmE), / hÊŒsk / (BrE)
to remove the outer covering (husk) from grains, seeds, or corn

Examples

To husk corn
Farmers often husk corn before taking it to market.
It takes time to husk all the sunflower seeds by hand.
She husked the ears of corn, preparing them for dinner.

Synonyms

  • hull
    5.5 0.62/m
    hull means removing the outer covering from seeds, fruit, or vegetables, while husk refers mainly to removing the dry layer from grains.
  • shell
    2.1 2.09/m
    shell means taking off a shell or pod from nuts, peas, or seafood, while husk usually describes removing a grain's dry outer skin.
