Pennatulidae

noun
family of sea pen marine animals
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɛˌnætəˈlaɪdiː / (AmE), / pɛnˌatjʊˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of colonial marine cnidarians known as sea pens, characterized by their feather-like appearance and found in oceans worldwide.

Examples

Pennatulidae sea pens
Members of the Pennatulidae family are commonly referred to as sea pens due to their feather-like appearance.
Researchers discovered several new species of Pennatulidae off the coast of Australia.
The Pennatulidae are known for anchoring themselves in soft ocean sediments.
