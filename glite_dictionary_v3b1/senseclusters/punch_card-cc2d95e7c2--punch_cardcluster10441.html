punch card

noun
card for storing data as holes
5.0 5.9
Pronunciation: /pʌntʃ kɑːrd/ (AmE), /pʌntʃ kɑːd/ /; / /pʌnʃ kɑːd/ (BrE)
A stiff card in which data is represented by the presence or absence of holes in predefined positions, formerly used for data processing and storage.

Examples

Old punch cards
Early computers used punch cards to input and store information.
He spent hours preparing a deck of punch cards for the data analysis job.
The museum exhibits several vintage punch cards from the 1960s.
