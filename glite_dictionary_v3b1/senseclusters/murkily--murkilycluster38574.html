murkily

adverb
in a dark or dim way
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝːkɪli / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is dark, unclear, or hard to see or understand

Examples

Figures move murkily
He could just make out the murkily moving figures in the distant alley.
The hallway was murkily lit, making it difficult to see the end.
Through the foggy glass, the lamp shone murkily into the night.
His motives remained murkily defined throughout the investigation, puzzling everyone involved.
The details of the incident were murkily reported in the newspapers, leaving many questions unanswered.
The river flowed murkily after the heavy rain, making it dangerous to swim.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearly - in a clear or distinct manner
  • synonym: dimly - in a way that is not bright or clear
