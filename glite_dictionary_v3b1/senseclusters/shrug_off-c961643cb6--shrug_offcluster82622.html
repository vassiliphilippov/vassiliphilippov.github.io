shrug off

verb
dismiss as unimportant
3.9 3.9
Pronunciation: / ʃrʌɡ ɔf / (AmE), / ʃrʌɡ ɒf / (BrE)
to treat something as unimportant and not let it affect you; to ignore or dismiss something

Examples

To shrug off criticism
Despite the rumors, he managed to shrug off the negative attention.
She tried to shrug off the criticism and keep working toward her goal.
The company spokesperson shrugged off concerns about the product's safety.
Despite the negative feedback, she chose to shrug off the comments and continued her work.
He tried to shrug off his worries, pretending everything was fine.
The company quickly shrugged off reports of financial trouble in the press.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dismiss - decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
  • synonym: ignore - refuse to take notice of or acknowledge; disregard intentionally
