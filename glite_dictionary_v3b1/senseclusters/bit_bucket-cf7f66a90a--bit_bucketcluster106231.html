bit bucket

noun
imaginary place for lost data (computing)
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɪt ˈbʌkət / (AmE), / bɪt ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
a notional place where lost or discarded data is said to go, especially in computing

Examples

The bit bucket place
After the power outage, my entire project went straight to the bit bucket.
Any errors in the transmission will send the data into the bit bucket.
He joked that all his unsaved changes are now in the bit bucket.

Related Terms
