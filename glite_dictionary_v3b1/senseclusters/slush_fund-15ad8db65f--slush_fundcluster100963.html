slush fund

noun
secret fund for bribery or corruption
7.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / slʌʃ fʌnd / (AmE), / slʌʃ fʌnd / (BrE)
a reserve of money used for illicit purposes, such as bribery or political corruption, kept secret from official accounts

Examples

A slush fund account
Investigators uncovered a political slush fund used to buy votes in the election.
Laws were introduced to prevent the creation of slush funds in government departments.
The company maintained a slush fund to pay off officials and secure contracts.

  • synonym: kickback - a secret payment for facilitating a transaction
