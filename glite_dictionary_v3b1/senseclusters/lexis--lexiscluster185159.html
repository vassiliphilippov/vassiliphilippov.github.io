lexis

noun
the vocabulary of a language
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛksɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪs / (BrE)
the complete set of words and word forms in a language; the vocabulary of a language

Examples

The lexis of language
Each dialect may have its own distinct lexis that sets it apart from the standard language.
Students are encouraged to study the lexis relevant to academic writing.
The English lexis has expanded greatly over the last century thanks to technological innovations.

Related Terms
