Caesalpiniaceae

noun
former plant family in botany
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsiːsælˌpɪniˈeɪʃiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌsiːsælˌpɪniˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a former family of spiny trees, shrubs, or perennial herbs (including the genera Caesalpinia, Cassia, Ceratonia, and Bauhinia), now usually included within the broader family Fabaceae or Leguminosae

Examples

Caesalpiniaceae spiny trees
Many ornamental trees once classified under Caesalpiniaceae are now grouped in the Fabaceae family.
Research on Caesalpiniaceae has expanded our understanding of legume evolution.
The genus Cassia, formerly placed in Caesalpiniaceae, includes several species used in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Fabaceae - the larger legume family that now includes Caesalpiniaceae
  • synonym: Mimosaceae - another former family of legumes traditionally separated from Fabaceae
