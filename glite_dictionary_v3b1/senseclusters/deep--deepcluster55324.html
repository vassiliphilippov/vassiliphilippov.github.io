deep

noun
steep ocean depression
Pronunciation: / dip /; / diːp / (AmE), / diːp / (BrE)
the deepest part of the sea or ocean, or a long, steep-sided depression in the ocean floor

Examples

The ocean deep
Marine life in the deep is vastly different from that at shallower depths.
Scientists are mapping the deep to learn more about underwater earthquakes.
The Mariana Deep is the deepest known part of the Earth's oceans.
Many tales tell of monsters lurking in the deep.
Poets have often described the deep as both beautiful and fearsome.
The ship vanished into the silent deep without a trace.
Legends tell of monsters living in the deeps of the ocean.
Explorers sent a submarine into the deep to study unknown sea creatures.
Few people have ever seen the mysterious deeps beneath the polar ice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abyss - a very deep or seemingly bottomless chasm, especially in the ocean
  • synonym: ocean - the vast body of salt water covering most of the earth
  • synonym: sea - the expanse of salt water covering most of the earth's surface
  • antonym: shallows - areas of water that are not deep
  • synonym: trench - a long, narrow and deep depression in the ocean floor
