concomitant

adjective
accompanying, often as a result
8.4 2.75/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàk…ëÀêm…™t…ônt / (AmE), / k…ônÀàk…ím…™t…ônt / (BrE)
happening at the same time as something else, especially as a consequence; accompanying

Examples

Concomitant effects
Economic growth is often accompanied by a concomitant increase in energy consumption.
The patient's fever was a concomitant symptom of the underlying infection.
With rapid technological change come concomitant challenges and opportunities.

Synonyms

  • accompanying
    3.4 4.27/m
    accompanying happens at the same time or as a result of something, while concomitant is a formal word for an accompanying effect.
  • attendant
    6.2 1.38/m
    attendant occurs as a result of or with an event, while concomitant names something that happens alongside and as a consequence.
  • co-occurrent
    8.8 0.00/m
    co-occurrent occurs at the same time as something, while concomitant is more common and often refers to effects that accompany events.
  • coincident
    5.6 0.12/m
    coincident happens at exactly the same time, while concomitant often implies an accompanying result or effect rather than mere timing.
  • coexisting
    3.9 0.01/m
    coexisting exists or continues at the same time, while concomitant usually describes an event or effect that accompanies another.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attendant - occurring with or as a result of something
  • synonym: consequent - following as a result or effect
  • synonym: incidental - accompanying but not a major part
