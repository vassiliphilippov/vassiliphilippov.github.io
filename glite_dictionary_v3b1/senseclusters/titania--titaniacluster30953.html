titania

noun
white pigment, titanium dioxide
Pronunciation: / taɪˈteɪniə / (AmE), / tɪˈtɑːnɪə / (BrE)
A white, powdery form of titanium dioxide used as a pigment for its excellent coverage and durability.

Examples

White titania pigment
Painters use titania in white paint because of its strong covering power.
Sunscreens sometimes contain titania to block harmful UV rays.
The durability of titania makes it ideal for industrial coatings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: titanium dioxide - A white inorganic compound, used as a pigment and in sunscreens
