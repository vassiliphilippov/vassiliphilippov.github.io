kakke disease

noun
endemic form of beriberi
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.keɪ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈkæ.keɪ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A form of beriberi endemic to Japan and some East Asian countries, caused by a deficiency of vitamin B1 (thiamine).

Examples

kakke disease symptoms
Cases of kakke disease decreased significantly after the introduction of thiamine-rich foods.
Historically, kakke disease was a major public health issue in Japan in the late nineteenth century.
Researchers discovered that kakke disease was caused by a severe deficiency of vitamin B1.
