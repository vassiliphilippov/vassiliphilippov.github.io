margin

noun
financial profit after costs
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
(finance) The difference between the selling price of goods or services and the cost of producing them, often expressed as a percentage of sales.

Examples

Profit margin
Retailers often operate on a small margin to stay competitive.
Rising costs have squeezed the margin for many businesses.
The company's profit margin increased significantly last quarter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: profit - financial gain; the amount earned after all expenses are subtracted
