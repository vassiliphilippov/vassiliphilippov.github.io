margin

noun
financial profit after costs
5.5 8/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ërd í…™n / (AmE), / Ààm…ëÀêd í…™n / (BrE)
(finance) The difference between the selling price of goods or services and the cost of producing them, often expressed as a percentage of sales.

Examples

Profit margin
Retailers often operate on a small margin to stay competitive.
Rising costs have squeezed the margin for many businesses.
The company's profit margin increased significantly last quarter.

Synonyms

  • profit margin
    3.6 4/m
    profit margin shows how much of sales becomes profit as a percent, while margin is a more general finance term.
  • margin of profit
    3.9 0.03/m
    margin of profit names the percent by which sales exceed costs, while margin is a shorter, more general finance term.
  • gross profit margin
    4.1 0.07/m
    gross profit margin compares sales and direct cost before other expenses, while margin can mean profit percentage in general.
  • gross margin
    4.9 0.6/m
    gross margin measures sales minus direct costs at company level, while margin is a general term for profit difference or percentage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: profit - financial gain; the amount earned after all expenses are subtracted
