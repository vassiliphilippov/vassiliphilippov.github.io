building society

noun
British savings and mortgage institution
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪl.dɪŋ səˌsaɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.dɪŋ səˌsaɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
A financial institution, especially in the UK, that accepts savings deposits and lends money for the purchase of houses.

Examples

A building society account
Many people in the UK save money in a building society rather than a bank.
She took out a mortgage with her local building society.
The oldest building society in Britain was founded in the 18th century.
