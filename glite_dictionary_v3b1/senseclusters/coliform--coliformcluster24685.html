coliform

noun
indicator bacteria
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊlɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlɪˌfɔːm / (BrE)
a type of bacteria found in soil, water, or feces, used as an indicator of contamination

Examples

A coliform bacterium
The presence of coliform in the water sample indicated possible contamination.
Health inspectors regularly test for coliform to ensure public safety.
A high coliform count can signal that water is unsafe to drink.
