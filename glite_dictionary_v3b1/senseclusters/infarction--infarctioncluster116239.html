infarction

noun
tissue death from blocked blood supply
8.9 1.71/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàf…ërk É…ôn / (AmE), / …™nÀàf…ëÀêk É…ôn / (BrE)
The death of tissue in a localized area of the body resulting from the obstruction of its blood supply, typically by a clot or embolus.

Examples

Myocardial infarction
A myocardial infarction occurs when blood flow to the heart muscle is severely reduced or blocked.
Prompt treatment can reduce the extent of tissue damage caused by a infarction.
The CT scan revealed an area of infarction in the patient's brain.

Synonyms
