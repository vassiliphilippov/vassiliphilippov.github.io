debrief

verb
to question after an event to gather information
Pronunciation: / diˈbɹif / (AmE), / diˈbɹiːf / (BrE)
to question someone officially about a completed mission or experience, especially to gather information or assess outcomes

Examples

To debrief a team
After the simulation, participants were debriefed to assess their reactions and learning outcomes.
All team members were debriefed after returning from their assignment.
The investigator will debrief you about what happened during the incident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrogate - to ask someone questions thoroughly, especially formally or aggressively
