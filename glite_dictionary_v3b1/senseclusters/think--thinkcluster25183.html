think

verb
expect, believe, or suppose
1.2 1.0
Pronunciation: /θɪŋk/ (AmE), /θɪŋk/ /; / /fɪŋk/ (BrE)
to have an opinion, belief, or idea about something; to suppose or expect

Examples

I think so
Do you think it will rain this afternoon?
He didn't think to check if he had locked the door.

Many people think the new law will benefit the community.
I don’t think he will be coming to the party tonight.

She thinks it’s a good idea to invest in technology stocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: believe - accept that something is true, especially without proof
  • antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • synonym: guess - form an opinion or answer without certain knowledge
  • antonym: know - be aware of something with certainty; to have knowledge
  • synonym: suppose - assume something is true without certain proof
