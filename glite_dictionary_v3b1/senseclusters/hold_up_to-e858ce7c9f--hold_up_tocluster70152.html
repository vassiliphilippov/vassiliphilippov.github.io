hold up to

verb
meet expectations or standards
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ʌp tu / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp tuː / (BrE)
to be as good as expected or promised; to meet the required standards or expectations

Examples

To hold up to standards
Her performance did not hold up to our expectations for the leading role.
The new software doesn't hold up to the company's usual high standards.
The witness's story failed to hold up to serious scrutiny during the trial.
