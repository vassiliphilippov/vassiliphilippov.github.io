peanut

noun
edible underground seed/pod
1.0 13/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpiÀê.n åt /; / ÀàpiÀån åt /; / ÀàpiÀên åt / (AmE), / ÀàpiÀê.n åt /; / ÀàpiÀên åt / (BrE)
a small, oval, edible seed that grows in a pod underground and is commonly eaten as a snack or used to make peanut butter; also, the plant that produces these seeds

Examples

Salted peanuts

Some people are allergic to peanuts and must avoid them.
The peanut grows underground, unlike most other nuts that grow on trees.
After the rains, the peanut fields looked lush and green.
Farmers in Georgia grow thousands of acres of peanuts each year.
The peanut plant has yellow flowers that bend to the soil as the pods develop underground.
He is allergic to peanuts and must avoid them at all costs.
She packed a bag of peanuts to take with her on the hike.
The recipe calls for roasted peanut halves sprinkled on top.

Synonyms

  • groundnut
    3.0 0.07/m
    groundnut is the name for the edible pod seed often used in British English, while peanut is the more common global name.
  • earthnut
    7.7 0.00/m
    earthnut refers to the peanut pod and is a less common alternative name, while peanut is the usual everyday term.
  • monkey nut
    6.1 0.00/m
    monkey nut names the peanut in its shell in British use, while peanut is the standard word used worldwide.
  • goober pea
    9.3 0.00/m
    goober pea is a folk American name for the peanut pod, while peanut is the normal, neutral name in general speech.
  • goober
    8.9 0.14/m
    goober is an informal US word for a peanut, often used in speech, while peanut is the standard neutral term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groundnut - another name for the peanut, especially outside North America
  • synonym: monkey nut - British term for a peanut in its shell
  • antonym: tree nut - edible seeds from trees, as contrasted with peanuts which are legumes
