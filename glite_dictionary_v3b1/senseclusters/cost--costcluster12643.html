cost

noun
sacrifice or amount needed to obtain something
1.0 70/m
Pronunciation: / kÉ‘st / (AmE), / kÉ’st / (BrE)
The value or sacrifice required to obtain, achieve, or produce something, often measured in effort, loss, or resources.

Examples

At great cost
Achieving peace may come at a great cost to both sides involved.
He wondered if the cost of fame was worth the sacrifices he had to make.
The cost of saving the rainforest can be measured in both money and human effort.

Synonyms

  • price
    1.0 17/m
    price names the amount asked in money for something, while cost covers money and other sacrifices needed to get it.
  • expense
    3.5 19/m
    expense refers to money spent or loss for a purpose, while cost means the overall sacrifice including money, time, or effort.
