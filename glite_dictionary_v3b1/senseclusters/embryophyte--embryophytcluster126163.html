embryophyte

noun
land plant group
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmbrioʊˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛmbrɪəʊˌfaɪt / (BrE)
a plant that lives on land and produces embryos, such as mosses, ferns, conifers, and flowering plants

Examples

A embryophyte plant
A moss is an example of an embryophyte that thrives in damp forests.
The evolution of the embryophyte was a key step in the colonization of land by plants.
Scientists compare embryophytes with algae to understand how plants adapted to life on land.
A moss is a simple embryophyte that thrives in damp environments.
Ferns are considered embryophytes because they reproduce using embryos protected by tissues.
The evolution of embryophytes allowed plants to colonize land millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alga - a simple plant-like organism, usually living in water
  • synonym: bryophyte - a non-vascular land plant such as mosses and liverworts
  • synonym: land plant - a plant that grows on land rather than in water
  • synonym: tracheophyte - a vascular land plant such as ferns and seed plants
