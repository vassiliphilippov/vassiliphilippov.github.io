dissembler

noun
person who hides true feelings or motives
4.2 7.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsem.bər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsem.bə/ (BrE)
a person who conceals their true feelings or motives by pretending to hold different beliefs or opinions

Examples

A dissembler hides feelings
He proved to be a skilled dissembler, hiding his ambitions behind a charming smile.
In the novel, the main villain is a master dissembler, deceiving everyone around him.
The senator was accused of being a dissembler who said one thing in public and another in private.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: hypocrite - a person who pretends to have virtues, moral or religious beliefs, etc., that he or she does not actually possess
