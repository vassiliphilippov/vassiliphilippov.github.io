reopener

noun
contract renegotiation clause
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈoʊpənər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈəʊpənə / (BrE)
a clause or provision in a contract that allows negotiations or terms to be reconsidered at a later date

Examples

Contract reopener clause
The union insisted on including a reopener in the contract to revisit salary terms after one year.
A reopener provision can help both parties address unforeseen economic changes during a long-term agreement.
Without a reopener, the company would have been unable to renegotiate health benefits mid-contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: final clause - a contract term that cannot be changed or reopened
  • synonym: renegotiation - the process of discussing and changing the terms of an agreement
