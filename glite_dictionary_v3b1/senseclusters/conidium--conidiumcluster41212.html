conidium

noun
asexual fungal spore
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɪdiəm / (AmE), / kəˈnɪdɪəm / (BrE)
An asexual spore produced by a fungus, typically formed at the tip of a conidiophore.

Examples

A conidium spore
Scientists studied the germination rates of conidia under various conditions.
The fungus releases conidia to reproduce and spread in its environment.
When observed under a microscope, you can see the conidia forming on the tips of the hyphae.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual
