knotty

adjective
covered with knobs or knots
7.0 0.40/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆnÉ‘ti / (AmE), / ËˆnÉ’ti / (BrE)
full of knots, lumps, or tangles (used for wood, hair, or body parts)

Examples

Knotty wood
Her knotty fingers showed years of hard labor in the fields.
The knotty trunk of the ancient oak was rough to the touch.
They sat on a knotty wooden bench carved from an old tree.
A knotty mass of hair formed after several days without brushing.
She spent hours working out the knotty mess in her shoelaces.
The knotty string was impossible to unravel without scissors.

Synonyms

  • snarly
    6.8 0.12/m
    snarly is tangled and forms messy knots in hair or rope, while knotty can describe knots or bumps in wood and skin.
  • tangled
    3.3 0.82/m
    tangled means twisted together into a messy mass that is hard to separate, while knotty often refers to knots or bumps in wood.
  • tangly
    5.1 0.01/m
    tangly tends to form loose, small tangles that catch and knot easily, while knotty describes actual knots or lumps in material.
  • gnarled
    7.1 1.72/m
    gnarled looks twisted and rough with deep knots on old wood or hands, while knotty simply means full of knots or lumps.
  • knobby
    5.8 0.29/m
    knobby has rounded bumps or knobs on a surface, while knotty implies actual knots, tangles, or lumps in wood, hair, or skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knobby - having or covered with small knobs
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not tangled
  • synonym: tangled - twisted together untidily; matted
