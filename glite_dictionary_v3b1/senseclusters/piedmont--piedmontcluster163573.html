piedmont

noun
slope at base of mountain
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpiːd.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈpiːd.mɒnt/ (BrE)
A gentle slope at the base of a mountain, leading to an area of flat land.

Examples

The piedmont slope
Grapes grow particularly well in the piedmont regions due to the rich soil and mild climate.
The piedmont stretches for miles before merging with the broad plain.
They built their new house on the piedmont to enjoy views of both the mountain and the valley.
