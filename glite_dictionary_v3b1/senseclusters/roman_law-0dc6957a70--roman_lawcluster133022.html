Roman law

noun
legal system of ancient Rome
8.1 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / Ààro ä.m…ôn l…î / (AmE), / Ààr…ô ä.m…ôn l…îÀê / (BrE)
the legal system of ancient Rome, especially as codified under Emperor Justinian, forming the foundation of many modern civil law systems

Examples

Roman law system
Many European countries based their legal systems on Roman law after the fall of the Roman Empire.
Scholars study Roman law to understand the origins of modern civil codes.
The principles of Roman law influenced legal thinking throughout the Western world.
