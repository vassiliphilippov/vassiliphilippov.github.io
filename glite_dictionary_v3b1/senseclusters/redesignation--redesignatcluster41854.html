redesignation

noun
giving new name or status
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən /; / ˌriːdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən /; / ˌriːdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of officially giving something a new name, title, classification, or status

Examples

The redesignation of a building
The redesignation of the old library as a community center was welcomed by local residents.
After the redesignation of the land, new regulations were put in place to protect wildlife.
The company announced the redesignation of several job titles to better reflect employees' roles.
The redesignation of the area as a national park brought new protections for wildlife.
After the company's merger, the redesignation of several departments was necessary.
The redesignation of the road changed it from a local street to a state highway.
