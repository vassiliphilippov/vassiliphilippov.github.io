boundary layer

noun
thin layer of slower fluid flow near a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊndəri ˌleɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊndəri ˌleɪə / (BrE)
the thin region adjacent to the surface of an object where a fluid (such as air or water) flows more slowly compared to the fluid farther away, due to friction along the surface

Examples

The boundary layer region
Dust accumulation is often limited to the boundary layer close to the ground.
Engineers study the boundary layer to improve the aerodynamics of airplane wings.
The thickness of the boundary layer can significantly affect heat transfer rates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laminar flow - smooth, constant fluid motion where layers slide past one another
  • antonym: turbulent flow - irregular fluid motion with mixing and eddies
