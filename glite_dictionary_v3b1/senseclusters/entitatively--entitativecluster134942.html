entitatively

adverb
as a single entity
9.9
Pronunciation: /en.ˈtɪt.ə.tɪv.liː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtɪt.ə.tɪv.liː/ (AmE), /en.ˈtɪt.ə.tɪv.lɪ/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtɪt.ə.tɪv.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that treats or considers something as a single, distinct entity or whole

Examples

To analyze entitatively
The research team analyzed the data entitatively, focusing on the group as a single unit.
She described the committee entitatively, saying it acted like a single decision-making body.
When viewed entitatively, the organization appears more unified than its individual departments suggest.
The concept was analyzed entitatively to understand its independent existence.
She approached the issue entitatively, focusing on what the object is rather than what it does.
In his lecture, the professor discussed reality entitatively rather than in terms of relationships.

Related Terms

  • antonym: functionally - in a way that relates to the function or purpose of something
  • synonym: holistically - in a way that considers something as a whole
  • antonym: individually - in a way that considers each part separately
