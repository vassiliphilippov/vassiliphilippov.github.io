bucolic

adjective
idyllically rustic or pastoral
8.6 0.93/m
Pronunciation: / bjuÀàk…ël…™k / (AmE), / bjuÀàk…íl…™k /; / bjuÀêÀàk…íl…™k / (BrE)
relating to the pleasant, peaceful, or idyllic aspects of country life; rustic or pastoral

Examples

Bucolic countryside
She longed for the bucolic calm of village life after years in the city.
The artist painted a bucolic landscape filled with green hills and grazing sheep.
They enjoyed a bucolic picnic under the old oak trees on a summer afternoon.
Her dream was to retire to a bucolic village far from the city's noise.
The artist specialized in bucolic landscapes, full of country charm and rustic barns.
They loved the bucolic scenery of rolling fields and grazing sheep.

Synonyms

  • pastoral
    6.8 2.39/m
    pastoral often paints calm, ideal country scenes with shepherds and fields, while bucolic means peaceful, rustic country life.
  • Arcadian
    9.9 0.18/m
    Arcadian evokes an ideal, untouched rural paradise in art or stories, while bucolic means pleasant, simple country life.
  • rustic
    5.2 4.13/m
    rustic shows simple, rough country style in houses or food, while bucolic highlights peaceful, idyllic country life.
  • idyllic
    6.6 2.22/m
    idyllic describes a very peaceful, perfect scene often by the sea or park, while bucolic refers to peaceful rural country life.
  • agrestic
    9.9 0.00/m
    agrestic names things of the fields or countryside in a formal or rare way, while bucolic feels pleasantly rural and poetic.
  • rural
    2.5 52/m
    rural simply means related to the countryside or towns near farms, while bucolic stresses pleasant, idyllic country charm.
