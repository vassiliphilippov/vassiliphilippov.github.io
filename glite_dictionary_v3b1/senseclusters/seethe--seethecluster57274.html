seethe

verb
be noisy or turbulent with activity
Pronunciation: / sið / (AmE), / siːð / (BrE)
(of a place) to be filled with intense, often noisy activity or movement; to be in a state of turmoil.

Examples

Seething crowd
On election night, the campaign headquarters seethed with anticipation and movement.
The city streets seethe with energy during the morning rush hour.
The market seethed with shoppers searching for deals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buzz - to be full of busy movement or excitement
  • antonym: calm - absence of noise or movement; peaceful
  • synonym: hustle - to be bustling with energetic activity
