Dolichos biflorus

noun
edible tropical twining herb cultivated in India
9.9
Pronunciation: / doʊˈlaɪkəs baɪˈflɔːrəs / (AmE), / dəˈlɪkɒs baɪˈflɔːrəs / (BrE)
A twining herb native to the Old World tropics, cultivated in India for its edible seeds and as fodder; sometimes classified within the genus Dolichos.

Examples

Dolichos biflorus plant
Dolichos biflorus is commonly grown in India for its protein-rich seeds.
Farmers use Dolichos biflorus not only as human food but also as nutritious animal fodder.
The seeds of Dolichos biflorus are also known as horse gram in many markets.
