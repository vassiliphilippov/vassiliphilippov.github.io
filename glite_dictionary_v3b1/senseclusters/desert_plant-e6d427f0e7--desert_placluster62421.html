desert plant

noun
plant adapted to dry, desert environments
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛz.ɚt plænt / (AmE), / ˈdez.ət plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that is adapted to survive in environments with limited water, such as deserts

Examples

A desert plant survives
Some desert plants have developed thick leaves to reduce water loss.
The botanical garden features a variety of rare desert plants from around the world.
The cactus is a well-known example of a desert plant that stores water in its tissues.

  • antonym: mesophyte - plant adapted to moderate water conditions
