divertor

noun
flow-directing valve
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɜːrtər / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
a valve or device that directs the flow of a liquid or gas into different pipes or outlets

Examples

Shower divertor valve
The plumber installed a new divertor to allow water to flow to either the shower or the bathtub.
A faulty divertor can cause water to leak from both the faucet and the showerhead at the same time.
Modern kitchen sinks often include a divertor to switch between the main tap and a spray nozzle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit or system
  • synonym: valve - a device that controls the flow of liquids or gases
