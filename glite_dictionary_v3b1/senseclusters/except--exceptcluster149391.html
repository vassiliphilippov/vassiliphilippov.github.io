except

verb
to object
6.2
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to object to or express disagreement with something, especially formally or officially

Examples

Except to a decision
Several members excepted to the new proposal during the meeting.
The lawyer excepted to the judge's ruling, requesting a formal review.
If anyone excepts to this plan, please speak up now.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or accept a suggestion
  • synonym: object - to express or feel disapproval or opposition
