electroluminescence

noun
light from electricity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˌluːməˈnɛsəns /; / ɪˌlɛktroʊluːməˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌluːmɪˈnɛsəns /; / ɪˌlɛktrəʊluːmɪˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
the emission of light from a material when electricity (such as a current or electric field) is applied

Related Terms

  • antonym: incandescence - light produced by a material due to high temperature
  • synonym: phosphorescence - light emitted by a substance without combustion or heat
