chrisom

noun
consecrated ointment for baptism or confirmation
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪzəm / (BrE)
a consecrated ointment made of a mixture of oil and balsam, used in Christian ceremonies such as baptism and confirmation

Examples

The chrisom oil
Ancient churches often kept a special vessel for storing the chrisom.
The priest anointed the infant with the chrisom during the baptism ceremony.
The recipe for making the sacred chrisom was carefully guarded by church officials.
