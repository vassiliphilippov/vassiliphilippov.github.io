well-grounded

adjective
based on good reasons or evidence
1.9
Pronunciation: / wɛl ˈɡraʊndɪd / (AmE), / wɛl ˈɡraʊndɪd / (BrE)
Based on good reasons or evidence; logically valid or justified.

Examples

Well-grounded opinion
Before making his decision, he ensured his opinion was well-grounded in fact.
Her conclusions are well-grounded in years of research and careful analysis.
The lawyer presented a well-grounded argument that swayed the jury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illogical - not based on clear or sound reasoning
  • synonym: sound - (of an argument or reasoning) based on logic or good evidence
