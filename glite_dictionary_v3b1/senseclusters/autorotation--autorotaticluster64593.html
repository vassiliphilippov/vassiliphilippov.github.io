autorotation

noun
helicopter rotor movement
Pronunciation: / ˌɔtoʊroʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊrəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the spinning of a rotor, such as in a helicopter or turbine, caused by air or fluid movement rather than by engine power (often used in emergencies)

Examples

Helicopter autorotation landing
The pilot practiced autorotation to prepare for engine failure emergencies.
During training, students learn how to perform a safe autorotation landing.
In the event of engine loss, autorotation allows the helicopter to descend safely.
In helicopters, autorotation allows the pilot to land safely if the engine fails.
The wind caused the blades to enter autorotation even though the motor was off.
Engineers studied autorotation to improve the efficiency of wind turbines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously through the air without engine power
  • synonym: rotation - the act of turning around a central point
