follow

verb
understand; grasp the meaning
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ / (BrE)
to understand or keep track of what someone is saying or explaining; to follow logic or reasoning

Examples

To follow the story
I'm not sure I follow what you mean—could you explain that again?
If you follow the reasoning, you will see why the answer is correct.
The textbook was so confusing that few students could follow it.
From these data, it follows that the hypothesis is incorrect.
If everyone arrived late, it would follow that the meeting could not start on time.
If the premises are true, then the conclusion must follow.
