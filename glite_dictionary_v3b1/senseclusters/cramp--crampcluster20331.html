cramp

verb
to suffer a sudden muscle contraction
2.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /kræmp/ (AmE), /kræmp/ (BrE)
to experience or cause a sudden, painful tightening of a muscle

Examples

Muscles cramp suddenly
After sitting for too long, his back muscles started to cramp painfully.
My leg suddenly began to cramp during the marathon and I had to stop running.
Swimmers often cramp if they don't stretch properly before getting into the pool.
After swimming in the cold water, my legs began to cramp painfully.
Her fingers would cramp whenever she wrote for more than an hour.
If you take on too much work at once, it may cramp your creativity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relax - to make or become less tense or tight
  • synonym: restrict - to limit the movement or actions of someone or something
  • synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary contraction of a muscle or group of muscles
