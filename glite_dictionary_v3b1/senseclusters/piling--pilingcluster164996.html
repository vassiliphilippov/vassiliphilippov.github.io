piling

noun
deep foundation support post
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a heavy post, usually of wood, steel, or concrete, that is driven deep into the ground to support a structure such as a bridge or building

Examples

Steel pilings
Steel pilings are often used to stabilize buildings on soft or wet ground.
The contractor inspected each piling before construction began on the new bridge.
The waterfront deck rests on dozens of wooden pilings driven into the riverbed.
