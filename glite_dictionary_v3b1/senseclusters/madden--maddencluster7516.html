madden

verb
make someone very angry or annoyed
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmæd.ən /; / ˈmædən / (AmE), / ˈmæd.ən /; / ˈmædn̩ /; / ˈmad.ən / (BrE)
to make someone extremely angry, annoyed, or drive them insane

Examples

To madden a team
His arrogant behaviour during the meeting maddened the entire team.
It maddens me when people chew loudly in a quiet room.
The constant noise from the construction site would madden anyone trying to work from home.
His stubborn refusal to listen only served to madden her.
It maddened him that he could not solve the puzzle.
The constant noise would madden anyone after a few hours.
Her constant interruptions began to madden everyone in the room.
His refusal to listen could madden his colleagues beyond endurance.
The relentless noise would madden even the calmest person.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make or become calm
  • synonym: enrage - to make extremely angry
  • synonym: infuriate - to make very angry
  • antonym: soothe - to calm someone down, to make less angry
