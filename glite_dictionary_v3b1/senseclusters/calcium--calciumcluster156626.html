calcium

noun
a metallic chemical element; essential for bones and teeth
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.si.əm / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metallic chemical element, essential in the formation of bones and teeth in animals and found widely in the earth's crust

Examples

Calcium carbonate
Chalk is mainly composed of calcium carbonate.
During the experiment, calcium produced a bright orange-red flame.
The doctor said I need more calcium in my diet to keep my bones healthy.
