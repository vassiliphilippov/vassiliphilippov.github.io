rough-legged hawk

noun
a large rodent-eating hawk of northern regions
Pronunciation: / ˌrʌfˌlɛɡd ˈhɑk / (AmE), / ˌrʌfˌlɛɡd ˈhɔːk / (BrE)
a large hawk of the Northern Hemisphere, noted for its feathered legs, that preys mainly on small rodents and benefits farmers by controlling pests

Examples

The rough-legged hawk bird
A distinctive feature of the rough-legged hawk is its feathered legs, adapted to cold climates.
Farmers appreciate the rough-legged hawk because it helps control vole and mouse populations.
The rough-legged hawk can often be spotted hovering over open fields in winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buzzard - a large bird of prey; common British term for certain hawks
