sweeper

noun
small nocturnal marine fish
7.7 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈswiː.pər/ (AmE), /ˈswiː.pə/ (BrE)
A small nocturnal fish found in warm shallow seas, with an oblong, laterally compressed body.

Examples

Nocturnal sweeper fish
Sweepers are rarely seen during the day as they shelter in rocks and crevices.
Divers often spot sweepers because of their bright, reflective eyes in the dark.
The reef was teeming with sweepers hiding among the corals at night.
