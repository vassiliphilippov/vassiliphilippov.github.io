Synonyms

  • need
    1.0 1900/m
    need shows a simple, everyday lack or want that people feel, while require is more formal and often used in rules.
  • necessitate
    6.3 1/m
    necessitate means cause something to become necessary as a result, while require simply states that something is needed.
  • oblige
    4.5 3/m
    oblige forces or compels someone often by duty or law, while require can describe a rule or general need without strong compulsion.
  • mandate
    4.9 8/m
    mandate officially orders or requires something by authority, while require can be general and not always an official order.
  • demand
    2.1 14/m
    demand asks for something strongly or urgently, often with pressure, while require is less forceful and can state a rule.
  • call for
    3.2 17/m
    call for asks that something be done for a situation or purpose, while require more often describes a rule or need.
  • involve
    1.8 110/m
    involve usually means include someone or something as a necessary part, while require focuses on saying that something is needed.
  • take
    1.0 200/m
    take often appears in casual talk to say how much time or effort is needed, while require is more formal or rule-based.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit or make possible

  • synonym: need - to require something because it is essential or important
  • antonym: permit - to allow someone to do something, especially officially
  • degree lower: request - ask for something politely or formally
