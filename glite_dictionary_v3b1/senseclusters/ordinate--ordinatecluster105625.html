ordinate

noun
vertical axis coordinate value
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɪnət / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
(in mathematics) the value of a point measured along the vertical or y-axis in a coordinate system.

Examples

Ordinate value on a graph
Every point is defined by its abscissa and ordinate in the Cartesian plane.
If the ordinate is negative, the point lies below the x-axis.
On the graph, the ordinate corresponds to the height of the plotted point above the x-axis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abscissa - the value of a coordinate on the horizontal axis
