windfall

noun
unexpected gain or good fortune
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌfɔːl /; / ˈwɪnd.fɔːl / (BrE)
an unexpected gain, advantage, or good fortune, especially a sudden amount of money

Examples

A windfall profit
A surprise inheritance proved to be a welcome windfall for the struggling family.
The company received a financial windfall after selling part of its business.
Winning the lottery was an incredible windfall for her, allowing her to retire early.
After paying off her debts, she used the windfall to travel the world.
Taxpayers can expect a small windfall from the government surplus this year.
The company received a windfall after selling its old headquarters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bonanza - a situation that creates a sudden increase in wealth, good fortune, or profits
  • synonym: boon - a thing that is helpful or beneficial
  • antonym: loss - the fact or process of losing something
