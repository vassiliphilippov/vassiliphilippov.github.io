delay

verb
make happen later; slow down
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈleɪ/ /; / /də.ˈleɪ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈleɪ/ /; / /də.ˈleɪ/ (BrE)
to make something happen later than planned, to slow down or postpone

Examples

To delay a meeting
Construction on the new bridge was delayed due to unexpected issues.
She asked if we could delay the meeting until next week.
The heavy snow delayed our flight by several hours.
Exposure to certain chemicals could delay plant germination.
Lack of proper nutrition may delay growth in young animals.
The illness can delay a child’s physical development.
Please delay the meeting until all participants have arrived.
The train was delayed due to heavy snowfall.
You should not delay submitting your tax return this year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expedite - to make something happen sooner or more quickly
  • antonym: hasten - to make something happen sooner or accelerate
  • synonym: postpone - to arrange for something to take place at a later time
  • synonym: retard - to slow down the development or progress of something
