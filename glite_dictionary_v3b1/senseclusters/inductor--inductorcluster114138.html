inductor

noun
electrical component adding inductance
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌktər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌktə / (BrE)
An electrical component, usually a coil of wire, that introduces inductance into a circuit by opposing changes in electric current.

Examples

An inductor coil
A transformer relies on the principle of mutual induction between two inductors.
The engineer added an inductor to the circuit to smooth out voltage fluctuations.
When current flows through an inductor, it generates a magnetic field around it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capacitor - an electrical component that stores energy in an electric field
  • antonym: resistor - an electrical component that resists the flow of electric current
