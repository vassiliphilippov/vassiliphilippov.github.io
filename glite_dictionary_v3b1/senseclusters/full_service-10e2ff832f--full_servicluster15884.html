full service

noun
comprehensive service offered to guests or clients
1.0
Pronunciation: / fʊl ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / fʊl ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
A level of customer service in which all possible needs or amenities are provided to guests or clients, often by dedicated staff.

Examples

A full service station
At our full service gas station, attendants will pump your gas and check your oil.
Many travelers prefer full service restaurants over fast-food chains for the extra attention and comfort.
The hotel prides itself on offering full service to all its guests, including a spa and concierge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: self-service - service where customers serve themselves
