per curiam

adjective, adverb
court-issued, unsigned
9.9
Pronunciation: / pər ˈkjʊriˌæm /; / pər ˈkjʊriəm / (AmE), / pə ˈkjʊəriˌam /; / pə ˈkjʊəriam / (BrE)
issued or done by a court as a whole, without naming any specific judge

Examples

Per curiam decision
The Supreme Court issued a per curiam decision that was brief and unsigned.
In some cases, a per curiam opinion is used to show the court's unanimous agreement.
The appellate court resolved the matter quickly with a per curiam ruling.
The appellate court issued its decision per curiam, without naming any individual judge.
A per curiam opinion is often brief and does not list the author.
The ruling was delivered per curiam, reflecting the consensus of the entire court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: en banc - heard or decided by all the judges of a court
  • antonym: signed opinion - a court opinion authored and signed by a specific judge
