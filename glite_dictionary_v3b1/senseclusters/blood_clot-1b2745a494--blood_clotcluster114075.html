blood clot

noun
a mass formed by coagulated blood
3.8 1.59/m
Pronunciation: / blÊŒd klÉ‘t / (AmE), / blÊŒd klÉ’t / (BrE)
A thickened mass in the blood formed when blood cells and other components stick together during the process of coagulation.

Examples

A blood clot forms
A blood clot can be dangerous if it travels to the lungs or brain.
The medication helps to prevent the formation of a blood clot after surgery.
The surgeon removed a blood clot that was blocking the artery.

Synonyms

  • clot
    5.0 3.63/m
    a clot is a general word for a lump from coagulated liquid, while a blood clot means that lump formed in blood.
  • coagulum
    9.9 0.01/m
    a coagulum is a formal medical term for a semi-solid mass from coagulation, while a blood clot is the everyday name for clotted blood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thrombus - a blood clot formed within a blood vessel or the heart
