pyoderma

noun
pus-producing skin infection
9.8
Pronunciation: /paɪ.ə.ˈdɜːr.mə/ /; / /paɪ.oʊ.ˈdɜːr.mə/ (AmE), /paɪ.ə.ˈdɜː.mə/ /; / /paɪ.əʊ.ˈdɜː.mə/ (BrE)
a skin disease that causes pus-filled sores or lesions, often caused by bacteria and common in animals such as dogs

Examples

Pyoderma in dogs
The veterinarian diagnosed the dog with pyoderma after noticing several pus-filled sores on its skin.
Treatment for pyoderma often includes antibiotics and regular cleaning of the affected area.
Cats can also develop pyoderma, though it is more common in dogs.
The veterinarian treated the dog's pyoderma with a special medicated shampoo.
Children with pyoderma may develop red, pus-filled sores on their skin.
After swimming in the lake, he noticed signs of pyoderma on his arms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dermatitis - inflammation of the skin
  • synonym: impetigo - a contagious bacterial skin infection causing blisters and sores
