acre-foot

noun
unit of volume for water (acre × foot deep)
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.kər.fʊt/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.kə.fʊt/ (BrE)
A unit of volume used in the United States to measure large quantities of water, equal to the amount needed to cover one acre of surface area to a depth of one foot (43,560 cubic feet or about 1,233.5 cubic meters).

Examples

One acre-foot of water
Farmers often use thousands of acre-feet of water each year for irrigation.
One acre-foot is enough water to supply a family of four for about a year.
The reservoir can hold up to 100,000 acre-feet of water.
