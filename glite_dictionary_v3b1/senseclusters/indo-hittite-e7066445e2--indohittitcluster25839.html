Indo-Hittite

noun
hypothesized ancient language family (Indo-European + Anatolian)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndoʊˈhɪtaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊˈhɪtaɪt / (BrE)
A hypothesized family of languages thought to comprise the Indo-European languages and the extinct Anatolian languages, spoken in Europe and parts of Asia by 1000 BC.

Examples

The Indo-Hittite family
Debates about the classification of Indo-Hittite continue in the field of historical linguistics.
Some linguists support the theory that Indo-Hittite is the ancestor language group for both Indo-European and Anatolian tongues.
The Indo-Hittite hypothesis suggests a broader family than traditionally defined Indo-European languages.
