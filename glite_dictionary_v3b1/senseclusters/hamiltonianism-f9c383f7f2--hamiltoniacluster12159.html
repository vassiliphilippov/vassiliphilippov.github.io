Hamiltonianism

noun
Hamilton's philosophy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhæmɪlˈtoʊniənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhæmɪlˈtəʊniənɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical doctrines of Sir William Hamilton, especially concerning human knowledge and its limitations

Examples

Hamiltonianism in philosophy
The professor gave a lecture on Hamiltonianism and its impact on nineteenth-century philosophy.
Many students found Hamiltonianism challenging because of its complex ideas about human knowledge.
Scholars still debate the influence of Hamiltonianism on later philosophical movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empiricism - the theory that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
  • antonym: rationalism - the theory that reason is the chief source of knowledge
