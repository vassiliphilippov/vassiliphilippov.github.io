indirect

adjective
not as a direct effect or result
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.də.ˈrekt/ /; / /ɪn.dɪ.ˈrekt/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.ˈrekt/ /; / /ɪn.də.ˈrekt/ (BrE)
not happening, received, or caused directly, but through an intermediate step, person, or thing

Examples

Indirect effect
Her influence on the project was mostly indirect, through her colleagues.
Many people experienced indirect effects from the economic downturn.
The new policy provides indirect benefits to rural communities.
Soft indirect lighting made the room feel more comfortable.
The interview was conducted in an indirect manner to avoid leading the witness.
We found indirect evidence that pointed to a larger problem within the organization.
