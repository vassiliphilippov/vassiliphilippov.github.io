dom

noun
monastic title
9.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / dÉ‘m / (AmE), / dÉ’m / (BrE)
a title used before the names of certain Benedictine or Carthusian monks, especially in Roman Catholic tradition

Examples

Dom Perignon
The famous monk Dom Perignon is often credited with contributions to the development of champagne.
At the monastery, Dom Bernard led the evening prayers with a calm and steady voice.
Visitors were greeted by Dom Hugo, who explained the history of the abbey.
