cosmopolitanism

noun
global citizenship belief
8.2
Pronunciation: /kɑːz.mə.ˈpɑːl.ɪ.tə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /kɒz.mə.ˈpɒl.ɪ.tə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that all people belong to a single global community and should be open to and accepting of different cultures

Examples

Cosmopolitanism promotes unity
Many philosophers argue that cosmopolitanism encourages people to care about global issues like poverty and climate change.
Her commitment to cosmopolitanism led her to volunteer in several countries around the world.
Debates about immigration often include discussions of cosmopolitanism versus national identity.
Her travels inspired a strong sense of cosmopolitanism and appreciation for different cultures.
The university promotes cosmopolitanism by encouraging students from all over the world to study together.
Many believe that cosmopolitanism can help reduce prejudice and foster global cooperation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internationalism - the principle of cooperation among nations for common good
  • synonym: multiculturalism - the presence and support of multiple cultures in a society
  • antonym: nationalism - the belief in the interests and culture of one's own nation above others
