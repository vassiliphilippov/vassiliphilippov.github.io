alopecia areata

noun
patchy hair loss disorder
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈpiʃiə ɛriˈætə / (AmE), / ˌaləˈpiːʃɪə ˌɛːrɪˈeɪtə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which hair falls out in small, round patches, leading to areas of baldness on the scalp or other parts of the body.

Examples

alopecia areata patches
People with alopecia areata may regrow hair spontaneously, but the condition can recur.
She wore a scarf to cover the effects of alopecia areata during her treatment.
The dermatologist diagnosed the sudden hair loss as alopecia areata.
