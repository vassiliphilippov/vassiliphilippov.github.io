long-spurred

adjective
having a long spur at the base (of a flower)
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋ spɝrd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ spɜːd / (BrE)
(of a flower) having a long extension or projection at the base of the corolla.

Examples

Long-spurred flower
Gardeners prize long-spurred violets for their striking floral appearance.
Many orchids display long-spurred blossoms to facilitate specialized pollination.
The long-spurred columbines attract certain pollinators with their unique shape.
