Examples

Hemizygous gene copy
Males are hemizygous for most genes on the X chromosome because they have only one X.
A mutation in a hemizygous gene can have a direct effect since there is no second copy.
In genetic studies, researchers often examine hemizygous individuals to understand gene function.
