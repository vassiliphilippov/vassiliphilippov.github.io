hemizygous

adjective
one gene copy
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmiˈzaɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmiˈzɪɡəs / (BrE)
having only one copy of a gene in a cell or organism, instead of the usual two

Examples

Hemizygous gene copy
Males are hemizygous for most genes on the X chromosome because they have only one X.
A mutation in a hemizygous gene can have a direct effect since there is no second copy.
In genetic studies, researchers often examine hemizygous individuals to understand gene function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterozygous - having two different copies of a gene
  • antonym: homozygous - having two identical copies of a gene
