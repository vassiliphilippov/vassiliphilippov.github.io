inchoative

noun
grammatical aspect indicating beginning of action
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkoʊətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊətɪv / (BrE)
(Grammar) Referring to or denoting an aspect of a verb that expresses the beginning of an action or state.

Examples

Inchoative verb
Linguists analyze inchoative verbs to study how actions are conceptualized as beginning.
Some languages mark the inchoative explicitly in their verb forms.
The verb 'to bloom' can have an inchoative sense when it means 'to begin to bloom'.
