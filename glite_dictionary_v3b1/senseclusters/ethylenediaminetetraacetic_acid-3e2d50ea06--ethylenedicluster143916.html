ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid

noun
chemical used to remove heavy metals
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθəˌliːnˌdaɪəˌmɪnəˌtɛtrəəˈsɪtɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌiːθɪliːndaɪəˌmɪnəˌtɛtrəəˈsɪtɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic compound used to bind and remove metal ions from the body, especially in cases of lead or heavy metal poisoning

Examples

ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid chelation therapy
Industrial cleaners sometimes contain ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid to neutralize metal deposits.
Researchers used ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid to chelate excess metal ions in the laboratory solution.
The doctor prescribed ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid to treat the patient's lead poisoning.
