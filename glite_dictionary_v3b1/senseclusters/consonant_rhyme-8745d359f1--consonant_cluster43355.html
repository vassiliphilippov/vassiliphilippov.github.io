consonant rhyme

noun
repetition of consonant sounds in poetry
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.sə.nənt raɪm / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt raɪm / (BrE)
the repetition of consonant sounds, especially at the ends of words, used as a poetic device

Examples

Consonant rhyme sounds
In 'blank' and 'think,' the repeated 'nk' sound is an example of consonant rhyme.
The poem uses consonant rhyme to create a pleasing echo at the end of each line.
Writers often employ consonant rhyme for subtle sound effects in their verses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assonance - repetition of vowel sounds in nearby words
  • synonym: rhyme - the correspondence of sounds between words, especially at the ends
