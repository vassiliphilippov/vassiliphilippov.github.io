hay bacillus

noun
soil-dwelling bacterium; Bacillus subtilis
Pronunciation: / heɪ bəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / heɪ bəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium (Bacillus subtilis) commonly found in soil and decaying organic matter, some strains of which produce antibiotics

Examples

Hay bacillus bacteria
Hay bacillus plays a significant role in breaking down organic material in soil.
Some antibiotics are derived from certain strains of hay bacillus.
The scientist isolated the hay bacillus from a sample of decomposing grass.
