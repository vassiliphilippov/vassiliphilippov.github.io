need

noun
something necessary or essential
1.0 243/m
Pronunciation: / nid / (AmE), / niÀêd / (BrE)
something that is necessary or required; a situation where something is needed

Examples

A basic need
Basic healthcare is a fundamental need for every human being.
Food, water, and shelter are the most urgent needs after a natural disaster.
The shelter tries to address the needs of the homeless community.
His need for companionship led him to adopt a pet.
The organization tries to help those in need during the winter months.
There is a growing need for affordable housing in urban areas.

Synonyms

  • necessity
    2.0 30/m
    necessity is something essential or unavoidable in a situation, while need is a general requirement someone must meet.
  • requirement
    2.0 93/m
    requirement is a specific condition or rule for a purpose, while need is a general thing that is necessary.
  • must
    1.0 0.56/m
    must is something seen as absolutely required or urgent, while need is a broader, normal necessity.
  • essential
    2.4 4.56/m
    essential is something absolutely necessary and often described as vital, while need is any thing that is required or lacking.
  • demand
    2.3 55/m
    demand is a strong request or need that must be met, often with pressure, while need is a general necessity.
  • want
    4.3 2.11/m
    want is a lack or desire often tied to shortage or wish, while need is something necessary someone must have.

Related Terms

  • antonym: luxury - something desirable but not essential
  • synonym: want - something desired but not necessarily required
