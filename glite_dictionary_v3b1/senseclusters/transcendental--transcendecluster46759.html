transcendental

adjective
existing beyond nature or reality
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌtræn.sənˈdɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌtræn.senˈden.tl̩ / (BrE)
existing outside of or beyond the limits of the natural world; supernatural or abstract in nature

Examples

Transcendental experience
Her explanation took on a transcendental tone, suggesting realities beyond ordinary perception.
Some philosophies seek a transcendental truth that lies beyond the physical world.
The poet's work often explored transcendental ideas unrelated to human experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humankind
  • synonym: supernatural - attributed to a force beyond scientific understanding or the laws of nature
