local anesthetic

noun
drug causing numbness in one area
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəl ænəsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˈloʊkəl ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl anɪsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˈləʊkəl ˌanəsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
A drug or agent that causes loss of sensation in a specific area of the body, usually to prevent pain during a medical procedure.

Examples

A local anesthetic injection
After receiving a local anesthetic, I didn't feel any pain during the minor surgery.
Some people are allergic to a particular type of local anesthetic.
The dentist applied a local anesthetic before filling my cavity.
A local anaesthetic is often used for minor skin surgery.
The dentist injected a local anaesthetic before filling the cavity.
You may feel some pressure, but the local anaesthetic will prevent any pain during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general anesthetic - anesthetic that produces loss of consciousness
  • synonym: local anesthetic - American spelling: drug that numbs a specific area of the body
