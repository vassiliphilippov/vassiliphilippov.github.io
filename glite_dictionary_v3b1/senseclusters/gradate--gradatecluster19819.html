gradate

verb
arrange or form in grades or stages
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪ.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.deɪt / (BrE)
to arrange or sort into grades, levels, or stages; to form with a gradual transition from one degree to another

Examples

To gradate levels
In geography class, students learned how to gradate the slopes of different hills for their mapping project.
The artist chose to gradate the colors, creating a smooth transition from blue to green across the canvas.
The stones were carefully gradated in size to create a visually pleasing effect along the pathway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grade - to arrange in order according to quality, rank, or degree
