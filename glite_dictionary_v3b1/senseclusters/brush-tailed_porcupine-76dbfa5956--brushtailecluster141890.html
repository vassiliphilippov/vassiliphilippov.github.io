brush-tailed porcupine

noun
porcupine with bristled tail tuft
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbrʌʃ teɪld ˈpɔːrkjəˌpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃ teɪld ˈpɔːkjʊˌpaɪn / (BrE)
a porcupine species characterized by a tuft of large, beaded bristles at the end of its tail

Examples

A brush-tailed porcupine species
Researchers observed several brush-tailed porcupines searching for food at night in the rainforest.
The brush-tailed porcupine is known for its distinctive tail bristles which create a rattling noise when shaken.
Unlike other porcupines, the brush-tailed porcupine has unique beaded bristles on its tail.
