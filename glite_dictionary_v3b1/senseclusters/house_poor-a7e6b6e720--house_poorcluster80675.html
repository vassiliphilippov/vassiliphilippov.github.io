house poor

adjective
having little money left after housing costs
5.7 6.1
Pronunciation: /haʊs pʊr/ /; / /haʊz pʊr/ (AmE), /haʊs pɔː/ /; / /haʊs ʊə/ (BrE)
Spending so much of one's income on housing costs that little money is left for other expenses or savings.

Examples

Feeling house poor
Feeling house poor, she had to cut back on dining out and entertainment.
Many young couples end up house poor because they underestimate ongoing homeownership costs.
They became house poor after buying an expensive home and couldn't afford vacations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cash-strapped - having little available money
