zag

verb
to turn sharply
Pronunciation: / zæɡ / (AmE), / zæɡ / (BrE)
to make a sharp, sudden turn or change in direction, especially as part of a zigzag movement

Examples

To zag suddenly
The runner had to zag quickly to avoid the obstacle on the track.
When the path suddenly turned, we all zagged to stay on course.
During the game, she would zag left and right to confuse her opponents.
The runner zagged to avoid the puddle on the track.
During the chase, the rabbit zags quickly to escape the fox.
She zagged left and then right to weave through the crowded street.
The runner had to zag quickly to avoid the fallen branch on the path.
During the chase, the rabbit would zag to escape its predator.
If you zag at the last second, you might surprise your opponent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to keep going in the same direction
  • synonym: swerve - to turn suddenly aside from a straight course
  • synonym: zigzag - to move in sharp angles from side to side
