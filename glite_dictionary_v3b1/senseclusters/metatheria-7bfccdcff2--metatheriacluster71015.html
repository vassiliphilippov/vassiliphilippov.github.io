Metatheria

noun
group of mammals including marsupials
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈθɪriə / (AmE), / ˌmiːtəˈθɪəriə /; / ˌmɛtəˈθɪərɪə / (BrE)
a group of mammals that includes all marsupials, whose young are born undeveloped and are often carried in a pouch

Examples

The Metatheria group
Ancestors of modern Metatheria are thought to have originated in South America.
Scientists continue to study how the reproductive systems of Metatheria differ from those of placental mammals.
The kangaroo is a well-known member of the Metatheria.
Paleontologists have found fossil evidence of early Metatheria in South America.
The subclass Metatheria includes all living marsupials such as kangaroos and koalas.
Unlike placental mammals, members of Metatheria give birth to relatively undeveloped young.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marsupial - a mammal carried and suckled in a pouch after birth
