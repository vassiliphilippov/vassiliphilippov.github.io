humanist

adjective
relating to the humanities
3.5 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (BrE)
relating to humanism, the humanities, or humanistic values (such as human dignity, rationality, and welfare, often secular)

Examples

Humanist values
Many humanist disciplines encourage students to think critically about culture and society.
She has a humanist approach to education, emphasizing literature and philosophy over pure science.
The university's humanist tradition can be seen in its broad curriculum.
Her humanist approach to history focused on the achievements of individuals rather than religious themes.
Several humanist scholars studied ancient Greek and Roman texts to inspire new thinking.
The humanist ideals of the Renaissance influenced art, philosophy, and education.
Her humanist approach to education inspires respect for every student.
The award honored organizations for their humanist contributions to society.
They believe that humanist principles should guide social policy and government.
