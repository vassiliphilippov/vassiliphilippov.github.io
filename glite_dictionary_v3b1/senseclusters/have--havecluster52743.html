have

verb
organize or be responsible for an event
2.4
Pronunciation: / hæv / (AmE), / hæv / (BrE)
to arrange, organize, cause, or make something happen; to arrange for someone to do something

Examples

Have a meeting
She decided to have a small dinner party for her birthday.
The company will have a special event to celebrate its 50th anniversary.
We're going to have a meeting tomorrow to discuss the project.
Her parents had her stay late to finish the project.
I'll have the car ready for you by noon.
The teacher had the students stand in a circle for the activity.
I'll have the plumber fix the leak tomorrow.
She had her assistant call the client to confirm the meeting.
The manager had the staff stay late to finish the project.
