hyperparathyroidism

noun
excess of parathyroid hormone; high blood calcium
4.5 8.2
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.pær.ə.ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.pær.ə.ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which the parathyroid glands produce too much parathyroid hormone, causing high calcium levels in the blood and potentially affecting bones and other organs.

Examples

Hyperparathyroidism disease
Surgery may be required to treat severe cases of hyperparathyroidism.
Symptoms of hyperparathyroidism include fatigue, bone pain, and kidney stones.
The patient was diagnosed with hyperparathyroidism after blood tests revealed elevated calcium levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypoparathyroidism - Deficiency of parathyroid hormone; low blood calcium
