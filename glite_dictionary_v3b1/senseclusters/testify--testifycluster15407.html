testify

verb
serve as evidence; indicate or show
4.9 7.34/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtÉ›stÉªËŒfaÉª / (AmE), / ËˆtÉ›stÉªfaÉª / (BrE)
to serve as evidence for something; to show or indicate the truth or existence of something

Examples

Actions testify to character
Her generous acts testify to her compassion and kindness.
The endurance of ancient monuments testifies to the skill of past civilizations.
The sudden drop in temperature testifies to the arrival of winter.

Synonyms

  • bear witness
    4.6 1.84/m
    bear witness gives direct evidence or confirms truth, while testify often means speaking under oath or giving spoken evidence in court.
  • attest
    6.6 6.89/m
    attest officially confirms something, often in writing or by signature, while testify usually means giving spoken evidence or a witness account.
  • corroborate
    9.0 4.84/m
    corroborate provides extra evidence to support a claim, while testify usually means offering evidence or a witness account about that claim.
  • substantiate
    7.2 3.59/m
    substantiate supplies proof that supports a claim, while testify usually refers to giving evidence or a spoken witness statement.
  • bear out
    4.7 0.55/m
    bear out supports or confirms a report with facts, while testify usually means giving spoken evidence about those facts.
  • evidence
    6.5 7.09/m
    evidence provides facts or signs that support a claim, while testify usually means giving spoken evidence or acting as a witness.
  • prove
    3.1 156/m
    prove shows something is true by strong evidence or logic, while testify often means providing evidence or an eyewitness account.
  • demonstrate
    3.0 82/m
    demonstrate shows or proves something by example or test, while testify usually means serving as evidence or speaking about the matter.
  • show
    1.0 329/m
    show makes something visible or clear to others, while testify usually means serving as evidence or giving a witness account.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disprove - to show that something is not true or incorrect
  • synonym: prove - to demonstrate the truth of something by evidence or argument
