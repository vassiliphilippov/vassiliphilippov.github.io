segregant

noun
agent of separation
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛɡrɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡrɪɡənt / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes, enforces, or promotes the separation of people, groups, or elements

Examples

A segregant leader
The new law was criticized for being a segregant that divided neighborhoods along economic lines.
As a segregant, the policy prevented different groups from interacting in public spaces.
Historians studied the role of each segregant in shaping the city's divided communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrator - a person or thing that brings together or unites
  • synonym: separator - a person or thing that separates or divides
