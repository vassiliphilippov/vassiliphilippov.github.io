unabsorbed

adjective
not allocated (finance)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəbˈzɔrbd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəbˈzɔːbd / (BrE)
not assigned, allocated, or written off in accounting or financial records; remaining unallocated or outstanding.

Examples

Unabsorbed costs
The company reported several unabsorbed costs at the end of the fiscal year.
Unresolved or unabsorbed losses can affect the financial statements of a business.
The audit revealed a significant amount of unabsorbed overhead in the factory's accounts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorbed - assigned or taken up, especially in accounting
  • synonym: unallocated - not assigned or distributed to a particular use or account
