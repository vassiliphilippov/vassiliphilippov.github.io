mindful

adjective
attentive; conscious of something
3.8 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnd.fəl /; / ˈmaɪndfəl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd.fəl /; / ˈmaɪndfʊl / (BrE)
conscious or aware of something; paying careful attention

Examples

Be mindful of safety
He is always mindful to lock the door when he leaves the house.
Please be mindful of other people's feelings during the meeting.
She remained mindful of the risks involved in the project.
Drivers must remain mindful of pedestrians, especially in busy city areas.
He tried to be mindful of his words when speaking in front of the audience.
She was mindful of the time and hurried to finish her work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attentive - paying close attention to something
  • synonym: aware - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • antonym: heedless - not paying attention; careless
  • antonym: unmindful - not conscious or aware of something
