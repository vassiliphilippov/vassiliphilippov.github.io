multitrack

verb
to record on separate tracks
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltiˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈmʌltiˌtræk / (BrE)
to record different instruments or voices separately on individual tracks so they can be mixed together later

Examples

To multitrack vocals
The band decided to multitrack each instrument to achieve a cleaner sound in the final mix.
Modern studios often multitrack vocals so producers can adjust each part individually.
She learned how to multitrack her guitar and piano parts for her new album.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mix - to combine separate audio tracks into a final version
  • synonym: overdub - to record new sounds over an existing recording
