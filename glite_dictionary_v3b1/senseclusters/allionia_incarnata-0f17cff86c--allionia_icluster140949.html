Allionia incarnata

noun
species of trailing plant with pink flowers
Pronunciation: / əˌloʊni.ə ɪnˌkɑːrˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ælɪˌəʊniə ɪnˌkɑːˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A trailing plant found in the southwestern United States and Mexico, noted for its clusters of three deep pink flowers that appear as a single bloom, typically growing in dry gravelly or sandy soils.
