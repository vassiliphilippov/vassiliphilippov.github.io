ghazi

noun
Muslim warrior
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːzi / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːzi / (BrE)
a Muslim warrior or an honorific title for a Muslim warrior or ruler, especially one who fought against non-Muslims or achieved military victory (historical Islamic context)

Examples

Ottoman ghazi leader
The Ottoman sultans were often given the title ghazi to honor their military achievements.
As a respected ghazi, he led many campaigns on the empire's frontier.
Stories of the brave ghazi inspired young men to join the army.
The title of ghazi was given to warriors who expanded the frontiers of the Ottoman Empire.
Many stories celebrate the bravery of a ghazi in defending his faith.
Several ghazis were honored for their victories in battle during the early Islamic period.
The victorious ghazi was celebrated throughout the region for his bravery in battle.
Many rulers in history adopted the title ghazi after leading successful military campaigns.
The people honored the ghazi for defending their land against invaders.
