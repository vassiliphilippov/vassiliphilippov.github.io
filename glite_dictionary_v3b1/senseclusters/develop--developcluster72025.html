develop

verb
expand or elaborate on an idea or theory
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɛləp / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləp / (BrE)
to create, formulate, or produce something new, such as a plan, idea, theory, product, or artistic work

Examples

To develop a plan
Could you develop your arguments further so we can understand your reasoning?
He spent several pages trying to develop his theory of social behavior.
The professor asked the student to develop her hypothesis in more detail during the presentation.
She plans to develop a business strategy that targets younger consumers.
The research team worked for years to develop an effective treatment for the disease.
They collaborated to develop a comprehensive model explaining the observed phenomena.
She hopes to develop a more user-friendly version of the software.
The company has recently developed a technology that improves battery life.
The research team worked for years to develop an effective vaccine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elaborate - to develop or present in detail
  • synonym: formulate - to create or devise methodically (a strategy or proposal)
  • synonym: invent - to create something for the first time
  • synonym: produce - to make or manufacture something
