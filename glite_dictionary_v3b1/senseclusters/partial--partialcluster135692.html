partial

noun
mathematical derivative with respect to one variable
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl / (BrE)
A derivative of a function with respect to one of several variables, with the others held constant; short for partial derivative.

Examples

A partial derivative
Let’s compute the partial with respect to y to find the slope in that direction.
The equation requires taking the partial of the function with respect to x.
The physicist evaluated the partial of temperature as pressure was held constant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: partial derivative - the derivative of a multivariable function with respect to one variable
