vizcacha

noun
South American rodent
9.9
Pronunciation: / vɪsˈkɑːtʃə / (AmE), / vɪsˈkætʃə / (BrE)
a large burrowing rodent native to South America, resembling a chinchilla and known for its long whiskers and bushy tail

Examples

The vizcacha rodent
The vizcacha lives in colonies and digs complex burrows in the dry plains of Argentina.
A vizcacha can often be seen sitting upright on rocks, scanning for predators.
Researchers observed the vizcacha using its strong hind legs to leap between boulders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chinchilla - a small South American rodent with soft fur
  • synonym: guinea pig - a small, tailless South American rodent often kept as a pet
