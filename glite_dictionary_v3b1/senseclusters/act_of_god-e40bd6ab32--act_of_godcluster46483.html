act of God

noun
uncontrollable natural event disrupting events
4.3
Pronunciation: / ækt əv ɡɑd / (AmE), / ækt əv ɡɒd / (BrE)
an event caused by natural forces, such as a flood or earthquake, that cannot be prevented by human effort and is often referred to in legal or insurance contexts as being outside of human control

Examples

An act of God event
Many contracts include clauses that excuse performance in the event of an act of God.
The flood was declared an act of God by local authorities, absolving the company of liability.
The insurance policy did not cover damage caused by an act of God such as a tornado.
