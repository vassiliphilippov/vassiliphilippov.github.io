two-toed

adjective
having two toes
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːˌtoʊd /; / ˈtuˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌtəʊd /; / ˈtuːtəʊd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having two toes on each foot or leg

Examples

The two-toed sloth moves slowly through the rainforest canopy.
Unlike its three-toed cousin, the ostrich is a two-toed bird.

The two-toed sloth is known for its slow movements and tree-dwelling lifestyle.
Unlike their three-toed relatives, two-toed sloths have a different style of climbing.
