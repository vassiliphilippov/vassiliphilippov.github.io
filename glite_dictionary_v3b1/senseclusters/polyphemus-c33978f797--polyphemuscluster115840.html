Polyphemus

proper noun
one-eyed cyclops
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪfəməs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈfiːməs / (BrE)
a giant one-eyed cyclops from Greek mythology, famously encountered by Odysseus in the Odyssey

Examples

Polyphemus the cyclops
In Greek mythology, Polyphemus is the cyclops who traps Odysseus and his men in a cave.
The story of Polyphemus is one of the most memorable episodes in the Odyssey.
Artists have often depicted Polyphemus as a towering figure with a single eye in the center of his forehead.
