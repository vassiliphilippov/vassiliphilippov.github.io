then again

adverb
introduces a different perspective
3.1
Pronunciation: / ðɛn əˈɡɛn / (AmE), / ðɛn əˈɡɛn /; / ðen əˈɡen / (BrE)
used to introduce a different or opposite idea, possibility, or point of view

Examples

Then again maybe
I could buy the blue shirt; then again, the red one suits me better.
It looks like rain today; then again, the weather forecast could be wrong.
She was planning to come to the party; then again, she might have changed her mind.
I thought she was upset; then again, maybe she was just tired.
It seemed like a good idea at first; then again, it could be risky.
The restaurant is expensive; then again, the food is excellent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: on the other hand - used to introduce an alternative or opposite point of view
