upscale

adjective
suitable for people with higher incomes
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌskeɪl / (BrE)
suitable for or appealing to people with higher incomes; relatively expensive and of high quality

Examples

An upscale restaurant
The hotel was renovated into an upscale boutique property attracting business travelers.
They decided to move to an upscale neighborhood with better schools.
This upscale restaurant is known for its gourmet menu and elegant atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downscale - relatively inexpensive and of lower quality
  • synonym: exclusive - restricted to a select group, often wealthy people
  • synonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, especially in a way that involves great expense
