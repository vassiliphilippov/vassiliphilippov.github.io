gleaning

noun
gradual collection
Pronunciation: / ˈɡliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of collecting leftover items, especially crops or information, gradually or bit by bit

Examples

Gleaning after harvest
After the harvest, volunteers participated in gleaning to collect leftover vegetables for local food banks.
Her report was based on the careful gleaning of facts from many different sources.
The tradition of gleaning helps reduce food waste and supports families in need.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discarding - the act of throwing something away
  • synonym: gathering - the act of collecting things together
  • synonym: harvesting - the process of collecting crops from the fields
