abdicable

adjective
capable of being abdicated or relinquished
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.dɪ.kə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈabdɪkəbl / (BrE)
Able to be abdicated, renounced, or relinquished.

Examples

An abdicable right
Not all authority is abdicable; some obligations must be fulfilled in person.
Some duties are personal and not abdicable to others.
The CEO retained certain non-abdicable responsibilities despite delegating most tasks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrevocable - unable to be revoked or changed
