rulemaking

noun
creation of regulations
Pronunciation: / ˈruːlˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈruːlˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of creating, changing, or removing official rules or regulations, especially by a government agency

Examples

The rulemaking process
The agency held a public hearing as part of the rulemaking process.
Effective rulemaking requires input from experts and stakeholders.
Citizens can submit comments during the rulemaking period to influence new regulations.

New environmental standards were established through federal rulemaking last year.
Public comments are often invited during rulemaking to ensure transparency and fairness.
