centeredness

noun
statistical adjustment
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.tərd.nəs/ /; / /ˈsen.tər.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈsen.təd.nəs/ /; / /ˈsen.tə.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or state of data being adjusted so that values are measured relative to a central point, such as the mean.

Examples

Data centeredness
The centeredness of the variables allowed for a more accurate comparison between groups.
Researchers often check the centeredness of data before running statistical models.
Improper centeredness can lead to misleading results in regression analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: normalization - the process of adjusting values to a common scale
  • antonym: raw data - data in its original, unadjusted form
