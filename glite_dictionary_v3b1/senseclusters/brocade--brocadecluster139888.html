brocade

verb
weave a decorative design into fabric
Pronunciation: / broʊˈkeɪd / (AmE), / brəʊˈkeɪd / (BrE)
to weave a raised design into fabric, often with gold or silver threads

Examples

To brocade silk
She spent hours brocading the velvet with delicate geometric designs.
The craftsman brocaded an intricate floral pattern into the silk.
Traditional artisans brocade luxurious fabrics for royal garments using gold and silver threads.
