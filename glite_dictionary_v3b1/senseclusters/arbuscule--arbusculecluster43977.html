arbuscule

noun
fungal root structure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbəˌskjuːl / (AmE), / ˈɑːbjuːskjuːl / (BrE)
a tiny, branched structure formed by certain fungi inside plant root cells to help exchange nutrients

Examples

Fungal arbuscule structure
The scientist observed an arbuscule under the microscope in the plant root.
Many plants rely on arbuscules to absorb nutrients from the soil more efficiently.
The presence of an arbuscule indicates a healthy symbiotic relationship between the fungus and the plant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypha - a long, branching filament of a fungus
  • synonym: mycorrhiza - a symbiotic association between a fungus and plant roots
