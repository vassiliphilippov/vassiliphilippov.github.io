refractometer

noun
instrument for measuring refractive index
Pronunciation: / rɪˌfrækˈtɑmɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˌfrækˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the refractive index of a substance, often to determine concentration or purity

Examples

A refractometer device
A digital refractometer provides quick and precise measurements in both laboratory and field settings.
Modern wineries often rely on a refractometer to monitor grape ripeness before harvesting.
The scientist used a refractometer to determine the sugar content of the juice sample.
