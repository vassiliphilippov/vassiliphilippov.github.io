pondweed

noun
a submerged freshwater plant
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑndˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpɒndˌwiːd / (BrE)
a freshwater aquatic plant, usually with narrow or floating leaves, commonly found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers or streams

Examples

Pondweed plants
Scientists found several species of pondweed thriving in the clear lake waters.
She carefully removed the pondweed from the garden pond to keep it from choking the other plants.
The surface of the pond was thick with pondweed, making it difficult to see the fish below.
Conservationists are studying the growth of pondweed to assess the health of freshwater habitats.
Ducks and fish often feed among the pondweed in shallow water.
The lake was covered with dense mats of pondweed near the shoreline.
