slip

verb
be unintentionally forgotten
3.9 2.6
Pronunciation: /slɪp/ (AmE), /slɪp/ (BrE)
to make a mistake or become incorrect through carelessness or accident

Examples

To slip up
I'm sorry—your birthday completely slipped my mind this year.
It had slipped her mind that the meeting was scheduled for Monday.
The answer slipped my memory just when I needed it most.
Even the best editors sometimes slip and let an error pass.
He slipped and said something he shouldn't have in the meeting.
I hope I didn't slip and give you the wrong address.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blunder - to make a careless or serious mistake
  • antonym: correct - free from mistake or error
  • synonym: err - to make a mistake or be incorrect
  • synonym: forget - to fail to remember something
  • antonym: remember - to keep something in one's mind; not forget
