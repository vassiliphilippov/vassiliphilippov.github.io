Orwellian

adjective
resembling Orwell's dystopian themes
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.ˈwel.ɪ.ə.n/ (AmE), /ɔː.ˈwel.ɪ.ə.n/ (BrE)
Characteristic of, or relating to, the dystopian ideas or totalitarian regime depicted in the works of George Orwell, especially a society marked by surveillance, propaganda, and lack of individual freedoms.

Examples

An Orwellian society
Critics warned that constant surveillance cameras on every corner could create an Orwellian society.
Some fear that the rise of facial recognition technology is leading us toward an Orwellian future.
The new law has been criticized as Orwellian because it allows the government to monitor citizens' private communications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dystopian - relating to an imagined state or society where there is great suffering or injustice
  • synonym: totalitarian - characteristic of a system of government that is centralized and dictatorial
  • antonym: utopian - relating to an imagined perfect society, contrasted with dystopian
