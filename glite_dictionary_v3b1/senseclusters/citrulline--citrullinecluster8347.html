citrulline

noun
non-protein amino acid; urea cycle intermediate
8.5
Pronunciation: / sɪˈtruːliːn / (AmE), / sɪˈtruːliːn / (BrE)
an amino acid that does not occur in proteins but is an intermediate in the conversion of ornithine to arginine, especially in the urea cycle

Examples

Citrulline amino acid
Citrulline is produced in the body as part of the urea cycle to help remove ammonia.
Researchers detected elevated citrulline levels in the blood of patients with certain metabolic disorders.
Some supplements contain citrulline to support exercise performance and heart health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arginine - an amino acid produced from citrulline in the urea cycle
  • synonym: ornithine - an amino acid involved in the urea cycle
