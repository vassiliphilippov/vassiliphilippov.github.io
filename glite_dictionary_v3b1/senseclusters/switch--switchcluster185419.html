switch

verb
turn something on or activate it
Pronunciation: / swɪtʃ / (AmE), / swɪtʃ / (BrE)
to cause a device or system to go on or to be set in operation, especially by moving a switch

Examples

To switch on lights
He switched on the computer to check his emails.
Please switch the lights when you leave the office.
She switched the radio on as soon as she entered the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: switch off - to cause a device to stop operating
  • synonym: turn on - to activate or start a machine or device
