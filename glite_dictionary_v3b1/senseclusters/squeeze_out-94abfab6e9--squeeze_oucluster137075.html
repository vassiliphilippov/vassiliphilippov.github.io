squeeze out

verb
form by forcing through an opening
1.0
Pronunciation: / skwiz aʊt / (AmE), / skwiːz aʊt / (BrE)
to shape or form a material by forcing it through an opening; to extrude

Examples

To squeeze out clay
By squeezing out the clay, the machine produced uniform tubes.
Engineers squeezed out the metal to create long, thin rods.
The factory squeezes out plastic pipes for construction use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extrude - force or push out (material) to shape it
