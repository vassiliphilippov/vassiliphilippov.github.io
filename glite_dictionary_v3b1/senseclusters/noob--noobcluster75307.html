noob

noun
inexperienced person
Pronunciation: / nuːb / (AmE), / nuːb / (BrE)
an inexperienced or new person, especially in gaming or online communities (often used informally or insultingly)

Examples

A total noob
When I first started playing chess online, I was a total noob and lost every game.
The experienced players offered advice to the noob who had just joined their team.
Don't worry if you make mistakes at first—everyone is a noob at some point.
He was called a noob after making several mistakes in his first online match.
Some players enjoy helping a noob learn the basics of the game.
If you keep acting like a noob, you will never improve your skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beginner - someone who has just started to do or learn something
  • antonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill
  • synonym: newbie - a person who is new to a particular activity or field
  • synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
