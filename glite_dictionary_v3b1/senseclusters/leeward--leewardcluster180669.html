leeward

noun
side sheltered from the wind
6.9 0.35/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliÀêw…ôrd, ÀàluÀê…ôrd /; / ÀàliÀåw…ùd / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêw…ôd, ÀàluÀê…ôd /; / ÀàliÀêw…ôd / (BrE)
the side or direction away from the wind; the sheltered side

Examples

The leeward side
Plants tend to grow more on the leeward side of the dunes where the wind is blocked.
The tents were pitched on the leeward side of the hill for better shelter.
They took cover on the leeward of the ship during the storm.
Clouds gathered on the leeward side of the mountain, away from the prevailing wind.
The captain ordered the crew to steer toward the leeward so they could move with the wind.
We anchored on the leeward of the island for protection from the strong breeze.

Synonyms

  • leeward side
    5.9 0.13/m
    leeward side is the part of something sheltered from the wind, while leeward names the direction or sheltered side.
  • lee side
    6.3 0.13/m
    lee side is a common phrase for the sheltered side of a ship or hill, while leeward is the single-word term.
  • lee
    7.4 0.40/m
    lee is the sheltered area beside a boat or building, while leeward emphasizes the direction away from the wind.
  • downwind
    2.8 0.63/m
    downwind describes moving or located in the direction the wind blows, while leeward stresses the sheltered side.

Related Terms

  • antonym: windward - the side or direction from which the wind blows
