lease

noun
property that is rented out
3.8
Pronunciation: / lis /; / liːs / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
a legal agreement to rent property, or the property or period of time covered by such an agreement

Examples

A lease agreement
After inheriting the estate, she decided to sell the lease on the apartment building.
The farmland was available only as a lease from the local council.
They inspected several commercial properties before signing a lease for the warehouse.
Our apartment lease expires at the end of July, so we need to decide whether to renew.
She signed a five-year lease for the new office space downtown.
The company terminated its lease early due to relocation plans.
Before moving in, you must agree to the terms of the lease.
The company signed a lease on the new office building for five years.
Their lease expires at the end of the month, so they are looking for a new apartment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ownership - the state or fact of owning something, often contrasting with leasehold
  • synonym: rent - payment made periodically by a tenant to a landlord for use of land or property
  • synonym: rental - an amount paid or property rented for use
  • synonym: rental agreement - a document forming the terms for renting property
  • antonym: sell - to transfer ownership of property in exchange for money
  • synonym: tenancy - the period during which a tenant has possession of a property
