depositional

adjective
relating to deposition
7.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˌpɑːzɪˈʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˌpɒzɪˈʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the process by which material is laid down or deposited, especially by natural forces

Examples

Depositional layers
Scientists study depositional patterns to understand how river deltas are formed over time.
The depositional layers in the canyon reveal the history of ancient environments.
A depositional environment can include beaches, riverbeds, or the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erosional - relating to the process of wearing away material
  • synonym: sedimentary - relating to material formed by deposition of particles
