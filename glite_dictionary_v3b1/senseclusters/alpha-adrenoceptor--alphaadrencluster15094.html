alpha-adrenoceptor

noun
receptor in sympathetic nervous system
9.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àå√¶lf…ô …ôÀàdrino äÀås…õpt…ôr / (AmE), / Àå√¶lf…ô …ôÀàdriÀên…ô äÀås…õpt…ô / (BrE)
A type of receptor on cell membranes in the sympathetic nervous system that responds specifically to certain agents, leading to effects such as vasoconstriction, relaxation of intestinal muscles, and contraction of smooth muscles.

Examples

Alpha-adrenoceptor blocker
Certain medications work by blocking the alpha-adrenoceptor to lower blood pressure.
Researchers are studying how the alpha-adrenoceptor influences the contraction of smooth muscle cells.
Stimulation of the alpha-adrenoceptor leads to constriction of blood vessels in peripheral tissues.

Synonyms

  • alpha-adrenergic receptor
    7.4 0.00/m

  • alpha receptor
    7.8 0.01/m
    alpha receptor is a shorter, more general name used in speech and notes, while alpha-adrenoceptor is the precise medical term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beta-adrenoceptor - a receptor responding to adrenaline but with different physiological effects (e.g., heart rate increase)
