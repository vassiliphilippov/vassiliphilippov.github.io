extracurricular

adjective
outside regular duties or curriculum
3.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /ek.strə.kə.ˈrɪk.jə.lər/ /; / /ek.strə.kə.ˈrɪk.jʊ.lər/ (AmE), /ek.strə.kə.ˈrɪk.jə.lə/ /; / /ek.strə.kə.ˈrɪk.jʊ.lə/ (BrE)
not part of the regular curriculum or normal duties; happening outside the usual school or work activities

Examples

Extracurricular interests
He enjoys pursuing extracurricular interests unrelated to his profession, such as mountain climbing.
She participates in several extracurricular activities outside of work, including volunteering and painting classes.
The company encourages extracurricular involvement that benefits the local community.
Joining extracurricular activities can help students develop leadership and teamwork skills.
Many colleges look for evidence of extracurricular involvement on student applications.
She participated in several extracurricular clubs during her time at university.
