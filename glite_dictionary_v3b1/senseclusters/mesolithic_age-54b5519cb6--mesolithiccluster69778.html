Mesolithic Age

noun
middle part of the Stone Age
9.9
Pronunciation: /me.zə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk eɪdʒ/ /; / /mes.ə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk eɪdʒ/ (AmE), /me.zə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk eɪdʒ/ /; / /mes.ə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk eɪdʒ/ (BrE)
the middle part of the Stone Age, lasting from about 15,000 to 5,000 years ago, characterized by the development of small stone tools and the beginning of settled life in some areas

Examples

The Mesolithic Age period
Archaeologists have found tools from the Mesolithic Age in various parts of Europe.
During the Mesolithic Age, humans developed advanced techniques for fishing and hunting small game.
The Mesolithic Age marked a transition from hunter-gatherer societies to more settled ways of life.
