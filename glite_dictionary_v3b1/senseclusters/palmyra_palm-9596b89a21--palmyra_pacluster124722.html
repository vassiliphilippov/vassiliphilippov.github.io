palmyra palm

noun
a tall tropical palm tree with useful sap and leaves
Pronunciation: / pælˈmaɪrə pɑm / (AmE), / pælˈmaɪrə pɑːm / (BrE)
a tall fan palm (Borassus flabellifer) of Africa, India, and Southeast Asia, with hard wood, edible sweet sap, and leaves used for thatching and weaving

Examples

The palmyra palm tree
Carpenters highly value the hard, durable wood of the palmyra palm for construction.
Many rural homes are roofed with leaves from the palmyra palm.
The palmyra palm is widely cultivated for its sweet sap, which is harvested to make palm wine.
