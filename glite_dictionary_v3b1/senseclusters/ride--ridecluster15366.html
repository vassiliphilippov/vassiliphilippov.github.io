ride

verb
(of a vessel) be moored or anchored
Pronunciation: / raɪd / (AmE), / raɪd / (BrE)
to be supported, carried, or float on something, either physically or figuratively

Examples

A ship rides at anchor
A large cruise ship rode at anchor just outside the harbor.
Several yachts ride at their moorings during the storm, barely moving.
The old fishing boat rides peacefully at anchor in the bay.
Her reputation rides on the success of this project.
The cat's collar rode high on its neck as it grew.
The trophy rode proudly on the shelf, gleaming in the sunlight.
A lone gull rode the wind high above the crashing waves.
Clouds ride across the summer sky on a gentle breeze.
The full moon rode majestically above the silent hills.
