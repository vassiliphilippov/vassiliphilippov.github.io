silents

noun
early films without sound
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪlənts / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənts / (BrE)
Silent films made before the introduction of sound in cinema, often characterized by the absence of a synchronized soundtrack.

Examples

Old silents movies
He has a large collection of rare silents from the early 1900s.
Many classic silents have been restored and are now available on DVD.
Unlike today's films, silents relied heavily on expressive acting and intertitles to tell their stories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: silent film - a movie made without synchronized recorded sound
  • antonym: talkie - a motion picture with a synchronized soundtrack
