prior

noun
previous experience
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪər / (AmE), / ˈpraɪə / (BrE)
a previous experience, belief, or conviction that influences current decisions or opinions

Examples

A prior belief
The judge asked if any juror had a prior that might affect their judgment.
Her priors made her cautious about trusting new people.
He tried to set aside his prior when considering the evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - a tendency to favor one thing over another, often unfairly
  • synonym: preconception - an idea or opinion formed before having evidence
