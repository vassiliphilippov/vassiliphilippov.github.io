plane

verb
to glide in air
3.6
Pronunciation: / pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
to glide or move smoothly through the air, especially while descending or maintaining lift, as an aircraft or glider does.

Examples

To plane through air
The glider was planing gently down toward the airfield with little engine power.
Birds are often seen planing on air currents above the cliffs.
After shutting off the engine, the pilot managed to keep the aircraft planing safely to the ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descend - to move downward, especially from a higher to a lower place
  • synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously through the air without engine power
  • antonym: stall - to lose lift and begin to fall while flying
