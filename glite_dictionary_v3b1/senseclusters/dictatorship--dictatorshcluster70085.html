dictatorship

noun

A form of government in which absolute power is concentrated in the hands of a single ruler or small group, not restricted by laws, constitutions, or opposition.

Examples

A dictatorship government
After the coup, the country fell under a brutal dictatorship that suppressed political opposition.
Many citizens fled the nation to escape the oppressive dictatorship in search of freedom.
The history book analyzed how dictatorship can emerge when democratic institutions are weak.

Synonyms

  • authoritarianism
    8.7 2.36/m
    authoritarianism is a political system where one authority holds strong power over politics, while dictatorship names absolute rule by one person or small group.
  • authoritarian regime
    7.4 1.80/m
    authoritarian regime describes a government run by one leader or elite with limited freedoms, while dictatorship stresses absolute unchecked power.
  • absolutism
    8.1 0.61/m
    absolutism is a system where a ruler has complete legal power, while dictatorship focuses on political control without legal limits.
  • despotism
    9.6 1.33/m
    despotism is rule by a single leader who often governs harshly, while dictatorship simply means concentrated, unchecked power.
  • tyranny
    6.7 7.83/m
    tyranny is cruel and oppressive rule that harms people, while dictatorship is the broader term for concentrated, unchecked political power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autocracy - a system of government by one person with absolute power
  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population or all eligible members, typically through elected representatives
