family Caesalpiniaceae

noun
family of spiny trees, shrubs, and perennial herbs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsiːsælˌpɪniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌsiːzælˌpɪniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of spiny trees, shrubs, or perennial herbs, including genera such as Caesalpinia, Cassia, Ceratonia, and Bauhinia, often combined in the family Leguminosae

Examples

Family Caesalpiniaceae plants
Botanists often debate whether to classify certain genera within the family Caesalpiniaceae or more broadly under Leguminosae.
Many species in the family Caesalpiniaceae are cultivated for their beautiful flowers and ornamental value.
The carob tree, part of the family Caesalpiniaceae, is valued for its edible pods.
