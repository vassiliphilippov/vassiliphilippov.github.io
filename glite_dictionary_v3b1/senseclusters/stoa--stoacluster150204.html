stoa

noun
Stoic philosophy school
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊə / (AmE), / ˈstəʊə / (BrE)
the school of Stoic philosophy founded in ancient Athens, or Stoic philosophy in general

Examples

The stoa philosophy
She studied ethics influenced by the ancient stoa and tried to live with greater calm.
Many philosophers gathered at the stoa to discuss ideas about virtue and reason.
The teachings of the stoa continue to inspire people seeking wisdom today.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Epicureanism - an ancient Greek philosophy focused on pleasure and avoiding pain
  • synonym: Stoicism - the philosophy founded by Zeno of Citium in ancient Greece
