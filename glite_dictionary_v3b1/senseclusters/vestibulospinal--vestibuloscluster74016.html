vestibulospinal

noun
nerve tract for balance
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛstɪbjuloʊˈspaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌvɛstɪbjʊləʊˈspaɪnəl / (BrE)
a nerve tract that carries signals from the vestibular nuclei to the spinal cord to help control posture and balance

Examples

Vestibulospinal tract
The vestibulospinal tract plays a crucial role in maintaining upright posture.
Damage to the vestibulospinal pathway can result in difficulties with balance and coordination.
Researchers studied how the vestibulospinal system responds to sudden changes in head position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corticospinal - a nerve tract from the cerebral cortex to the spinal cord
  • synonym: vestibular - relating to the inner ear or sense of balance
