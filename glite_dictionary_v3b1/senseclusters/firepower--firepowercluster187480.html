firepower

noun
available financial resources
4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər.paʊ.ər/ /; / /ˈfaɪr.paʊər/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə.paʊ.ə/ (BrE)
(in business, finance) the amount of financial resources (cash, credit, or capital) that an individual or organization can deploy to pursue major actions such as acquisitions, investments, or rapid expansion.

Examples

Financial firepower
The startup's growing cash reserves boosted its firepower to acquire competitors.
After the funding round, the company had enough firepower to expand into three new markets.
Investors were impressed by the firm's financial firepower and its ability to outbid rivals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capital - financial assets or resources available to a business for use in production or investment
  • synonym: financial resources - money or assets that an organization can use to fund operations or strategic moves
