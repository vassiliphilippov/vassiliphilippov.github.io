improvability

noun
ability to improvise
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌprɑːvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒvəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be improvised or adapted spontaneously, especially in performance or creation

Examples

Improvability in acting
The director appreciated the improvability of the script, allowing actors to add their own lines.
Her improvability made her a valuable member of the comedy troupe.
The game's improvability encourages players to create new rules and scenarios on the spot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being inflexible or unable to change
  • synonym: spontaneity - the quality of being spontaneous or acting without planning
