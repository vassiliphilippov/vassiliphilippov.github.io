Examples

Synonyms

  • generosity
    2.1 7.65/m
    generosity means freely giving money, time, or help to others, while liberality is a general willingness to give openly and generously.
  • openhandedness
    7.2 0.01/m
    openhandedness shows a clear habit of giving freely in daily acts, while liberality stresses a broad generous attitude.
  • bounteousness
    9.9 0.00/m
    bounteousness appears in literary or old texts as abundant giving, while liberality is the common idea of being generous in life.
  • munificence
    9.9 0.10/m
    munificence describes very large, often formal gifts, while liberality means general openness to give without that high scale or formality.
  • beneficence
    8.4 0.40/m
    beneficence means doing good through acts or help, often in formal settings, while liberality focuses on willingness to give freely.
  • charitableness
    5.3 0.01/m
    charitableness refers to giving help or money to those in need, while liberality covers giving freely in many situations.
  • benevolence
    6.1 1.88/m
    benevolence means a kind wish or act toward others, often feeling-based, while liberality emphasizes actual giving and open-handed action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generosity - willingness to give more than is necessary or expected
  • antonym: stinginess - lack of generosity; unwillingness to give or spend
