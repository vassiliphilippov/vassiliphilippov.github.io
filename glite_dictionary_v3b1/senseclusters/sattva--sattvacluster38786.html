sattva

noun
purity and harmony
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌt.və / (AmE), / ˈsʌt.və / (BrE)
in Indian philosophy, the quality of purity, harmony, and balance in nature or the mind; the true essence of something

Examples

Sattva quality balance
Practicing yoga is believed to increase sattva and bring inner peace.
A diet rich in fresh fruits and vegetables is said to promote sattva in the body.
Many spiritual traditions value sattva as a path to clarity and balance.
Meditation is believed to increase sattva and bring inner peace.
The teacher explained that sattva leads to clarity and understanding.
Many spiritual practices aim to cultivate sattva in daily life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rajas - the quality of activity, passion, and restlessness in Indian philosophy
  • antonym: tamas - the quality of darkness, inertia, and ignorance in Indian philosophy
