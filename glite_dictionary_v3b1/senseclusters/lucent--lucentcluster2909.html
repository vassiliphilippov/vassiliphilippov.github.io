lucent

adjective
softly bright or radiant
8.8 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀê.s…ônt /; / ÀàluÀês…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàluÀê.s…ônt /; / ÀàluÀês…ônt / (BrE)
shining softly, glowing, or clear because light passes through

Examples

A lucent crystal
A lucent crystal hung from the necklace, sparkling subtly in the sunlight.
Her lucent eyes reflected the light from the window.
The lucent moon cast a gentle glow over the quiet landscape.
The lucent moonlight made the path easy to follow at night.
She admired the lucent quality of the crystal vase on the table.
The artist used lucent colors to create a glowing effect in the painting.

Synonyms

  • luminous
    4.8 4.77/m
    luminous emits or reflects a steady soft light, while lucent also suggests being clear or letting light pass through.
  • lambent
    9.6 0.03/m
    lambent glows with a soft, often flickering light on surfaces, while lucent usually means softly shining or somewhat transparent.
  • aglow
    5.5 0.56/m
    aglow shines brightly or warmly as if lit up, while lucent emphasizes a soft glow or light passing through.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dim - not bright; lacking in light or brightness
  • synonym: luminous - giving off light; bright or shining
  • antonym: opaque - not letting light through; not transparent
  • synonym: radiant - sending out light; shining or glowing brightly
  • degree lower: translucent - allowing some light to pass through but not clear
