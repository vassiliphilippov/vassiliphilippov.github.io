concomitantly

adverb
simultaneously
8.6
Pronunciation: / kənˈkɑːmətəntli / (AmE), / kənˈkɒmɪtəntli /; / kɒnˈkɒmɪtəntli / (BrE)
at the same time as something else; simultaneously or together with something else

Examples

As the temperature dropped, energy consumption concomitantly increased across the city.
The artist developed her style concomitantly with the rise of modern art movements.
