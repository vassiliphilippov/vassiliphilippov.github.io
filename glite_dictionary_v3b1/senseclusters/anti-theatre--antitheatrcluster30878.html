anti-theatre

noun
drama opposing theatrical conventions
8.0 8.3
Pronunciation: /æn.ti.ˈθiː.ə.tər/ /; / /æn.taɪ.ˈθiː.ə.tər/ (AmE), /æn.ti.ˈθiː.ə.tə/ /; / /æn.taɪ.ˈθiː.ə.tə/ (BrE)
A form of drama that deliberately rejects or subverts traditional, naturalistic, or conventional theatrical forms and rules.

Examples

Anti-theatre style
Many playwrights in the 20th century experimented with anti-theatre to challenge audience expectations.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of anti-theatre in modern European drama.
The production embraced anti-theatre techniques, abandoning traditional plot and character.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avant-garde - experimental and innovative works, especially in the arts
  • synonym: theatre of the absurd - a style of drama that emphasizes the absurdity of human existence
