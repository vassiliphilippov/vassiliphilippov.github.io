lead

verb
to direct a musical performance
Pronunciation: / lid /; / liːd / (AmE), / liːd / (BrE)
to guide, direct, or be in charge of a group, activity, or performance; to go first or be ahead

Examples

To lead a team
Barenboim used to lead the symphony with great passion and skill.
He will lead the choir during the Christmas performance this year.
She was chosen to lead the orchestra at the annual concert.
She led the thread through the eye of the needle with steady hands.
The technician led the cable through the wall to the outlet.
You should lead the hose under the fence to reach the garden.
A police car leads the motorcade down the main street.
At the front of the hiking group, Sarah led the way through the forest.
The parade was led by the mayor on horseback.
