precipitate

verb
(of rain, snow) fall from clouds
4.6
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪpɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of rain, snow, etc.) to fall from clouds, usually as a result of atmospheric processes.

Examples

Clouds precipitate rain
Heavy clouds began to precipitate snow across the region by midday.
The storm will precipitate several inches of rain before moving east.
Water vapor in the air can precipitate as sleet under certain conditions.
