cultch

noun
oyster bed material
Pronunciation: / kʌltʃ / (AmE), / kʌltʃ / (BrE)
broken shells, stones, or similar material spread on the sea bottom to provide a surface for oyster larvae to attach and grow

Examples

Oyster cultch bed
The workers spread cultch across the bay to help new oysters grow.
Without enough cultch, oyster larvae have nowhere to attach and develop.
Marine biologists studied the effects of different types of cultch on oyster populations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reef - a ridge of material, such as rock or coral, in the sea
  • synonym: substrate - a surface on which an organism grows or is attached
