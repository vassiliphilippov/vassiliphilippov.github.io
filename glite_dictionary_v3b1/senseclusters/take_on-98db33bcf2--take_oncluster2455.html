Synonyms

  • undertake
    4.2 24/m
    undertake means agreeing to do a formal task or project in writing or business, while take on is more general and casual.
  • assume
    3.7 10/m
    assume describes someone beginning a role or duty officially, while take on focuses on agreeing to handle tasks or challenges.
  • take responsibility
    3.3 8.24/m
    take responsibility is accepting blame or duty for something, while take on means agreeing to handle a task or role.
  • tackle
    3.4 16/m
    tackle means making a determined effort to deal with a difficult problem, while take on means agreeing to accept or handle it.
  • adopt
    3.2 13/m
    adopt means beginning to have a new role, quality, or attitude, while take on usually means agreeing to handle a specific task.
