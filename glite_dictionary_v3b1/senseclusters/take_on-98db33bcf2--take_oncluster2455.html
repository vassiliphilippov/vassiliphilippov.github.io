take on

verb
accept a challenge or responsibility
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ɒn / (BrE)
to accept or agree to handle a task, responsibility, role, or challenge

Examples

To take on a project
He loves to take on challenging assignments at work.
She decided to take on the new project despite its difficulties.
Will you take on the responsibility of leading the team?
After the resignation, John had to take on the role of project manager.
She was reluctant to take on additional duties at work.
The company will take on new responsibilities as it expands internationally.
After her promotion, she agreed to take on additional managerial duties.
He's always willing to take on new challenges, no matter how difficult they seem.
The football team was ready to take on their biggest rivals in the championship match.
