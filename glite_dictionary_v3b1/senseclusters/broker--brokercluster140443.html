broker

noun
person who buys/sells for others for commission
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkə / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells goods or assets for others in exchange for a commission

Examples

A broker sells stocks
He hired a broker to help him purchase shares in the company.
Real estate brokers often charge a commission on successful sales.
The insurance broker found the best policy for our needs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another
  • antonym: customer - a person who purchases goods or services
  • synonym: dealer - a person who buys and sells a particular product
