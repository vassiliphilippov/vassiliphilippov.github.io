second-guess oneself

verb
doubt one's own decisions or actions
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛk.ənd ˈɡɛs wʌnˌsɛlf / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.ənd ˈɡɛs wʌnˌsɛlf / (BrE)
to doubt or question one's own decisions or actions, often repeatedly or unnecessarily

Examples

To second-guess oneself often
After submitting his application, he couldn't help but second-guess himself about his answers.
It's common to second-guess oneself after making a tough choice.
She tends to second-guess herself whenever an important decision needs to be made.
