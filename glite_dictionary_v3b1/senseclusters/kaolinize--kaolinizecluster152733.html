kaolinize

verb
to turn into kaolin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪəlɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkeɪəlɪˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to change a mineral or rock into kaolin (a soft, white clay), usually by chemical weathering

Examples

To kaolinize minerals
Certain minerals kaolinize when exposed to acidic groundwater over long periods.
Geologists observed that the granite had begun to kaolinize due to weathering.
The process to kaolinize feldspar is important in the formation of clay deposits.
Over millions of years, granite can kaolinize into a soft, white clay used in ceramics.
Geologists study how volcanic rocks kaolinize in tropical climates due to intense weathering.
If the soil is acidic and wet, minerals may gradually kaolinize and change the landscape.
