diacope

noun
repetition with interruption
Pronunciation: / daɪˈækəpi / (AmE), / daɪˈækəpi / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which a word or phrase is repeated with one or more words in between

Examples

Diacope in speech
The famous line 'to be, or not to be' is a classic example of diacope.
Writers often use diacope to create emphasis and rhythm in their sentences.
In the speech, the repeated phrase separated by other words was a clear use of diacope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anaphora - repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses
  • synonym: epistrophe - repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses
