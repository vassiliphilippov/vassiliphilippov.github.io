indwell

verb
exist or reside within as a spirit or force
9.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdwɛl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdwɛl / (BrE)
(of a spirit, force, or principle) to exist or reside within something or someone as an inner presence.

Examples

To indwell a spirit
In certain philosophies, divine energy is said to indwell all living things.
Many believe that the Holy Spirit can indwell a person's heart and guide their actions.
The sense of purpose seemed to indwell her, shaping every decision she made.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inhabit - to live or reside in a place
  • synonym: possess - to take control of, especially supernaturally
