ventral fin

noun
pair of fins on a fish's underside
7.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈven.trəl fɪn/ (AmE), /ˈven.trəl fɪn/ (BrE)
either of a pair of fins attached to the underside (pelvic region) of a fish, aiding in the control of movement direction and corresponding to the hind limbs of land vertebrates

Examples

Fish ventral fin
If a fish injures its ventral fin, it may have trouble keeping its balance.
In some species, the ventral fins are larger and play a role in mating displays.
The ventral fin helps the fish stabilize itself as it swims through the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pelvic fin - one of the paired fins on the underside of a fish's body
