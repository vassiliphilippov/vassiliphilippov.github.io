Intropin

noun
trade name for the drug dopamine
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.troʊ.pɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪn.trəʊ.pɪn / (BrE)
A trade name for dopamine, a drug used to treat shock and low blood pressure by improving blood flow.

Examples

Intropin is commonly used in emergency medicine to treat shock.
Due to its powerful effects, Intropin must be dosed carefully by medical professionals.
The doctor administered Intropin to stabilize the patient's blood pressure.
