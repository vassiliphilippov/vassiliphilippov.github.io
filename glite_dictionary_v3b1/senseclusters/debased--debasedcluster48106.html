debased

adjective
reduced in quality or value
5.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈbeɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪst / (BrE)
reduced in quality, value, or moral character; corrupted or degraded

Examples

A debased value

He regretted the debased form his art had taken over the years.
The country suffered under a debased currency, making daily life more difficult for everyone.
Some argue that modern art is debased by commercial interests.
The coin was made of debased metal, no longer pure silver.
The wine tasted strange, clearly debased with water or other cheap additives.
After years of inflation, the debased coins were almost worthless.
The once noble traditions became debased by greed and selfishness.
Their language had become so debased that it was hard to understand their meaning.
