noncompensatory

adjective
no offsetting allowed
9.8
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.kəm.ˈpen.sə.tɔːr.i/ /; / /nən.kəm.ˈpen.sə.tɔːr.i/ (AmE), /nɒn.kəm.ˈpen.sə.tə.ri/ /; / /nɒn.kəm.ˈpen.sə.tɔː.ri/ (BrE)
describing a process or system in which a weakness in one area cannot be offset by strength in another

Examples

Noncompensatory rule
The hiring committee used a noncompensatory method, so each requirement had to be met individually.
In a noncompensatory grading system, failing one section means you cannot pass the course.
Their evaluation process is strictly noncompensatory, so no single skill can outweigh a deficiency in another.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compensatory - allowing one factor to make up for another
