Salerno

proper noun
site of a WWII battle in Italy
Pronunciation: / səˈlɜrnoʊ / (AmE), / səˈlɜːnəʊ / (BrE)
the site of a major World War II battle in southern Italy, where Allied forces captured the port from German troops in September 1943.

Examples

Battle of Salerno
Many soldiers lost their lives during the fierce fighting near Salerno in 1943.
The Allied landing at Salerno marked the beginning of the Italian Campaign in World War II.
The port of Salerno was strategically important for supplying Allied troops after the invasion.
