psychological warfare

noun
use of psychological tactics in conflict
4.1 5.6
Pronunciation: / saɪˌka.ləˈdʒɪ.kəl ˈwɔːr.fɛr /; / ˌsaɪkəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / saɪˌkɒ.ləˈdʒɪ.kəl ˈwɔː.feə /; / ˌsaɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
the use of psychological tactics, such as propaganda or intimidation, to influence or weaken an opponent, especially in military or political situations
