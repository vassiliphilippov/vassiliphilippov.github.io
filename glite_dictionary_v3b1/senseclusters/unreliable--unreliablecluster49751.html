unreliable

adjective
not able to be trusted or depended on
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl /; / ˌʌnrɪˈlaɪəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl /; / ˌʌnrɪˈlaɪəbl̩ / (BrE)
not able to be trusted or depended on; likely to be wrong, misleading, or not do what is expected

Examples

Unreliable friend
His unreliable memory made it difficult to confirm the story.
The company lost clients due to unreliable delivery schedules.
We had to replace the old car because it had become too unreliable.
His memory became increasingly unreliable as he got older.
The train service here is notoriously unreliable during the winter months.
We lost the data because the software was unreliable.
Because Mark was so unreliable, no one wanted to assign him important tasks.
She found her old car unreliable and finally decided to buy a new one.
The weather in April is notoriously unreliable; sunshine can turn to rain in minutes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trustworthy - deserving of trust or confidence
