doubletree

noun
crossbar for harnessing two horses abreast
6.7 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌb.əl.triː/ (AmE), /ˈdʌb.əl.triː/ (BrE)
A horizontal crossbar on a wagon or carriage, to which two whiffletrees are attached, used to harness a pair of horses side by side.

Examples

The doubletree crossbar
Harnessing the horses to the doubletree took only a few minutes before they were ready to work.
The old wagon had a sturdy doubletree that easily supported two strong draft horses.
When repairing the harness, he noticed the doubletree was beginning to crack with age.
