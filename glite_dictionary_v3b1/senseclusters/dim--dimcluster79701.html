dim

adjective
not clearly perceived or remembered
4.0 3.5
Pronunciation: / dɪm / (AmE), / dɪm / (BrE)
not clear, distinct, or easy to understand; vague or uncertain (about things, memories, or hopes)

Examples

Dim memories
He could only make out a dim silhouette through the thick fog.
Her memories of that summer have grown dim over the years.
The photograph was too dim to recognize any faces clearly.
After the disappointing results, our chances of winning the contract seemed dim.
His hopes for recovery grew dim as the days passed without improvement.
Many people have a dim view of the country's economic future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bleak - cold, discouraging, or offering little hope
  • antonym: distinct - easily seen, heard, or recognized; clear
  • synonym: faint - not clear, strong, or definite
  • antonym: hopeful - full of hope; likely to succeed
  • synonym: vague - not clearly or sharply defined or stated
