paraphragm

noun
membrane in speakers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌfræm / (BrE)
a thin, flexible membrane or cone in a microphone or loudspeaker that vibrates to produce or detect sound

Examples

Microphone paraphragm
The paraphragm inside the microphone was damaged, causing poor sound quality during the recording.
Engineers designed a new paraphragm to improve the loudspeaker's performance at high frequencies.
If the paraphragm becomes stiff or torn, the device may not function properly.
