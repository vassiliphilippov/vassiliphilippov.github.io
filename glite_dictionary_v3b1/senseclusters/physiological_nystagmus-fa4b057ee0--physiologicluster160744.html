physiological nystagmus

noun
normal small involuntary eye movements
8.4 6.9
Pronunciation: /fɪ.zɪ.ə.ˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl nɪ.ˈstæɡ.məs/ /; / /fɪ.zɪ.ə.ˈlɑː.dʒə.kəl nɪ.ˈstæɡ.məs/ (AmE), /fɪ.zɪ.ə.ˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl nɪ.ˈstæɡ.məs/ /; / /fɪ.zɪ.ə.ˈlɒ.dʒə.kəl nɪ.ˈstæɡ.məs/ (BrE)
small involuntary, rapid movements of the eyeballs that occur normally and help maintain visual stability; when these are suppressed, vision fades quickly due to fatigue of the retinal receptors

Examples

Physiological nystagmus eye movements
Doctors often observe physiological nystagmus during routine eye examinations.
If physiological nystagmus is artificially suppressed, the patient's vision may rapidly fade.
The presence of physiological nystagmus helps prevent sensory adaptation and keeps visual perception stable.
