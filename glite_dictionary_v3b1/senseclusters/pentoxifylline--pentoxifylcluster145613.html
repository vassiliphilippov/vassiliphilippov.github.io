pentoxifylline

noun
drug for improving blood flow in peripheral artery disease
Pronunciation: / pɛnˌtɑksəˈfaɪlin / (AmE), / pɛnˌtɒksɪˈfɪliːn / (BrE)
A drug used to improve blood flow in patients with peripheral artery disease by increasing the flexibility of red blood cells; commonly prescribed to treat intermittent claudication.

Examples

pentoxifylline improves blood flow
After several weeks of taking pentoxifylline, she noticed less pain when walking.
Patients with intermittent claudication may benefit from treatment with pentoxifylline.
The doctor prescribed pentoxifylline to help improve circulation in his legs.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: claudication - Pain caused by too little blood flow, often during exercise
