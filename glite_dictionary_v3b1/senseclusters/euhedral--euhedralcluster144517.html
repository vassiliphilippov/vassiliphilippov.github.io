euhedral

adjective
well-formed crystals
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈhiːdrəl / (AmE), / juːˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
having well-formed, clearly defined crystal faces, especially in minerals or crystals

Examples

A euhedral crystal
The mineral sample contained several euhedral crystals that sparkled under the microscope.
Geologists often look for euhedral forms to study the natural growth of minerals.
A perfectly euhedral crystal is rare in nature due to space limitations during formation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anhedral - lacking well-formed crystal faces
  • degree lower: subhedral - having partially developed crystal faces
