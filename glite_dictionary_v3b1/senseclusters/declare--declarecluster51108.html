declare

verb
formally announce a payment (e.g., dividend)
2.3 3.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈkleər/ /; / /də.ˈkleər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈkleə/ /; / /də.ˈkleə/ (BrE)
to state or announce something officially, formally, or publicly (such as an opinion, decision, payment, or to authorities)

Examples

To declare a decision
After a profitable year, the management decided to declare a bonus for employees.
The board voted to declare a dividend at the end of the quarter.
The company may declare special payments to reward loyal shareholders.
She honestly declared the value of her purchases to the border official.
Travelers must declare all items purchased abroad when they arrive at customs.
You are required to declare any cash or goods exceeding the limit when entering the country.
He declared in favor of relocating the company headquarters to another city.
She bravely declared her support for the new initiative at the meeting.
The senator publicly declared against the proposed changes to the law.
