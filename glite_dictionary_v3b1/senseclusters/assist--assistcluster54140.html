assist

verb
to serve or wait on someone
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪst / (AmE), / əˈsɪst / (BrE)
to help or support someone, especially by making a task easier or by serving them

Examples

Do you need someone to assist you with your shopping today?
The nurse will assist you during your stay at the hospital.

Several volunteers assist the elderly residents at the community center.
The nurse will assist the doctor during the operation.
He offered to assist me with my luggage at the airport.
Nurses often assist doctors during medical procedures.
She will assist the manager in organizing the event next week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attend - to give practical help or care to someone
  • synonym: help - to make it easier or possible for someone to do something by offering assistance
  • antonym: hinder - to make it more difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen
  • synonym: serve - to perform duties or services for someone
