inferior rectus muscle

noun
eye muscle moving eyeball down and in
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˈrɛktəs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˈrɛktəs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a muscle in the eye that controls downward and inward movement of the eyeball

Examples

The inferior rectus muscle
An injury to the inferior rectus muscle may result in double vision when attempting to look down.
The surgeon carefully repositioned the inferior rectus muscle during the corrective eye procedure.
Weakness in the inferior rectus muscle can cause difficulty looking downward.
