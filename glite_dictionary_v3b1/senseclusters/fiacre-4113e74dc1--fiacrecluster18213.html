Fiacre

proper noun
Irish patron saint
9.9
Pronunciation: / fiˈækər / (AmE), / fiˈɑːkə / (BrE)
a 7th-century Irish hermit and saint, traditionally regarded as the patron of gardeners

Examples

Fiacre the saint
Many gardeners still plant marigolds on the feast day of Fiacre to honor his memory.
The church dedicated to Fiacre attracts visitors from all over Europe.
Stories about Fiacre often mention his kindness to travelers and the sick.
