Ranunculus ficaria

noun
European perennial herb with yellow flowers; lesser celandine
9.9
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈnʌŋ.kjʊ.ləs fɪ.ˈker.iː.ə/ /; / /rə.ˈnʌŋ.kjə.ləs fɪ.ˈker.iː.ə/ (AmE), /rə.ˈnʌŋ.kjʊ.ləs fɪ.ˈkeə.riː.ə/ /; / /rə.ˈnʌŋ.kjə.ləs fɪ.ˈkeə.riː.ə/ (BrE)
a perennial herb native to Europe, with heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers, sometimes called lesser celandine; its tuberous roots have been used medicinally to relieve piles

Examples

Ranunculus ficaria typically blooms in early spring with a striking carpet of yellow flowers.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control the rapid spread of Ranunculus ficaria in moist lawns.
The roots of Ranunculus ficaria were traditionally used in folk medicine to treat piles.
