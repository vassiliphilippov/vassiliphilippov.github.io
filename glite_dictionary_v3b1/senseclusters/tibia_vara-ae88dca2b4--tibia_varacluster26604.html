tibia vara

noun
bowed leg condition (medical)
8.8 8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪb.i.ə ˈvɑː.rə/ /; / /ˈtɪb.jə ˈvɑː.rə/ (AmE), /ˈtɪb.i.ə ˈvɑː.rə/ /; / /ˈtɪb.jə ˈvɑː.rə/ (BrE)
A condition in which the leg bows outward at or below the knee, also known as Blount's disease.

Examples

tibia vara condition
Children with tibia vara may require bracing or surgery to correct the deformity.
Early treatment of tibia vara can help prevent long-term joint problems.
The specialist diagnosed him with tibia vara after noticing significant bowing of his lower legs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genu valgum - knock-knee (knees angle in and touch each other when standing)
  • synonym: genu varum - bow-leggedness (general term for outward bowing of the legs)
