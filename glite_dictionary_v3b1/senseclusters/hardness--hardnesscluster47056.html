hardness

noun
difficulty or challenging nature
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being difficult to do, understand, or solve

Examples

Hardness of a task
Her persistence overcame the hardness of learning a new language.
The hardness of the exam surprised all the students.
We underestimated the hardness of the task ahead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ease - the state of being easy, not difficult
