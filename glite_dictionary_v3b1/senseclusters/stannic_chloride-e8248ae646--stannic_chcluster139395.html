stannic chloride

noun
a caustic liquid chemical compound (SnCl₄)
8.9
Pronunciation: / stæˌnɪk kləˈraɪd / (AmE), / stæˌnɪk klɪəˈraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, caustic liquid compound (SnCl₄) formed by treating tin with chlorine, used in dyeing and as a reagent

Examples

Stannic chloride solution
Accidental spillage of stannic chloride can release fumes that irritate the respiratory tract.
Industrial processes often use stannic chloride as a reagent in dye manufacturing.
The chemist wore gloves while handling stannic chloride due to its caustic nature.
