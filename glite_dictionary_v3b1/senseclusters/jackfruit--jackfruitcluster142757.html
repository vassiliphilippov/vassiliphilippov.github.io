jackfruit

noun
large tropical fruit with edible flesh and seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒækˌfruːt /; / ˈdʒækˌfrut / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.fruːt / (BrE)
a large tropical fruit with rough green or yellow-green skin and sweet, edible flesh, or the tree that produces this fruit, native to South and Southeast Asia

Examples

A jackfruit tree
Large pieces of jackfruit were stacked in the market, their scent filling the air.
She tried roasted jackfruit seeds for the first time and loved their nutty flavor.
The ripe jackfruit is often used in desserts and sweet dishes across Southeast Asia.
Dried jackfruit seeds are often roasted and eaten as a snack.
The ripe jackfruit is prized for its sweet, aromatic flesh in many Asian cuisines.
We saw a massive jackfruit hanging from a tree during our trip to Kerala.
