orbital motion

noun
movement of an object in an orbit
5.5 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.bə.təl ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈɔːr.bɪ.təl ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.bɪ.təl ˈməʊ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the movement of an object along a curved path around a fixed point, especially in space, such as a planet or satellite around a star or planet

Examples

Orbital motion of planets
Precise calculations are required to keep a satellite in stable orbital motion around the earth.
Scientists study the orbital motion of planets to understand their behavior in the solar system.
The orbital motion of the moon around the earth affects the tides.
