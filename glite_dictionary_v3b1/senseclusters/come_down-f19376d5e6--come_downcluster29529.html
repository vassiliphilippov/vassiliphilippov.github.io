come down

verb
fall from the sky (rain, snow, etc.)
1.3 6.52/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm ËˆdaÊŠn / (AmE), / kÊŒm ËˆdaÊŠn / (BrE)
(of rain, snow, or other precipitation) to fall from the clouds or sky to the ground.

Examples

Rain comes down
Ash from the volcano started to come down on the surrounding villages.
Heavy rain began to come down just as we were leaving the house.
Snow will come down throughout the night, making travel difficult in the morning.

Synonyms

  • precipitate
    5.5 0.17/m
    precipitate is a formal scientific verb for rain or snow falling from clouds, while come down is everyday speech.
  • rain down
    3.4 1.83/m
    rain down means fall or be sent in large amounts like rain, while come down refers simply to precipitation falling.
  • shower down
    3.5 0.09/m
    shower down means pour or fall in heavy amounts from above, while come down is the neutral term for precipitation.
  • fall
    1.0 164/m
    fall is a general verb for moving downward or dropping, while come down specifically describes precipitation falling from the sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall - to drop or descend from a higher to a lower place
  • synonym: precipitate - to cause (something) to fall, especially from the atmosphere as rain, snow, etc.
