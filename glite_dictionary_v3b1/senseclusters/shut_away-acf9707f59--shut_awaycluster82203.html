shut away

verb
keep isolated or confined
Pronunciation: / ʃʌt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ʃʌt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a place where they cannot leave, be seen, or be accessed; to keep isolated or confined

Examples

To shut away in room
After the incident, he was shut away in his room for several days.
She shut away her old letters in a locked drawer, never to be read again.
The valuable paintings were shut away in a secure vault for safekeeping.

  • synonym: lock up - to put someone or something in a place where they cannot leave or escape
