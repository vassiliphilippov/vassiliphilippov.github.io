blandish

verb
coax by gentle flattery
6.4 8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈblæn.dɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈblæn.dɪʃ/ (BrE)
to coax or influence someone by gentle flattery or insincere praise

Examples

To blandish with praise
He spent hours blandishing her with praise and empty promises.
She tried to blandish the manager into giving her a refund with charming compliments.
The salesman blandished his customers, hoping to secure another sale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cajole - to persuade with flattery or repeated appeals
  • synonym: flatter - to praise excessively or insincerely
  • antonym: scold - to rebuke or criticize angrily
