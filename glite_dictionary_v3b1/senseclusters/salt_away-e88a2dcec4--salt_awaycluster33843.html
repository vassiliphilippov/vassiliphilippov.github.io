salt away

verb
store or save for future use
8.5
Pronunciation: / sɔlt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sɒlt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to save or store something, especially money, for future use (sometimes secretly or gradually)

Examples

To salt away money
He quietly salted away enough money to take a long vacation after retirement.
Investors often salt away assets in offshore accounts for tax purposes.
She managed to salt away a considerable sum from her modest salary over the years.
It's always smart to salt away some cash for unexpected expenses.
Many people try to salt away a portion of their income each month.
Over the years, she managed to salt away quite a bit for her retirement.

  • synonym: save up - to keep money aside for future needs
