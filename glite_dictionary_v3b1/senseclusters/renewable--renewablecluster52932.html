renewable

noun
sustainable energy source
3.7
Pronunciation: /riːn.ˈuː.ə.bəl/ /; / /rɪ.ˈnjuː.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈnjuː.ə.bəl/ /; / /riːn.ˈuː.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
a natural energy source, such as solar or wind power, that can be replenished and does not run out

Examples

Solar renewables
Many governments are increasing their investment in renewables to combat climate change.
Solar and wind power are two of the most common renewables used worldwide.
Switching to renewables can help reduce our dependence on fossil fuels.
