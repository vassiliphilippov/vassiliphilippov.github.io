hold

verb
consider or believe; have an opinion
3.8 1.0
Pronunciation: / hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
to have, maintain, or express a belief, opinion, or thought

Examples

To hold an opinion
Do you hold him responsible for what happened?
Many experts hold that exercise is essential for good health.
She held the belief that education could change society.
Despite the criticism, he held onto his original theory.
Many people hold resentments for years without realizing it.
She continues to hold the belief that honesty is always the best policy.
It is commonly held that exercise improves mental health.
Many scientists hold that climate change is caused by human activity.
She holds the view that education should be free for everyone.
