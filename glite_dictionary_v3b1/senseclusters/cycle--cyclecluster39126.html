cycle

verb
cause to go through a recurring sequence
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkəl /; / ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəl /; / ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
to go through or cause something to go through a series of repeating stages or events

Examples

To cycle the power
Every 30 minutes, the program automatically cycles the images on the screen.
Please cycle the dishwasher once before putting in new dishes.
The machine will cycle the air through a filter to keep it clean.
Economic booms and busts tend to cycle in a predictable pattern over time.
The seasons cycle from spring to winter each year without fail.
Trends in fashion often cycle every few decades, coming back into style.
Many computer systems automatically cycle between different power modes to save energy.
The washing machine will cycle through several stages during the cleaning process.
To maintain its effectiveness, the pump is designed to cycle on and off throughout the day.
