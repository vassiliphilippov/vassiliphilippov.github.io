S.S.

proper noun
steamship prefix
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˈɛs / (BrE)
an abbreviation used before the name of a steam-powered ship, meaning 'steamship'

Examples

S.S. Titanic
The S.S. Great Eastern was one of the largest ships of its time.
Passengers boarded the S.S. United States for a transatlantic voyage.
The S.S. Minnow set sail despite the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: H.M.S. - an abbreviation for 'Her Majesty's Ship', used before the name of a British naval ship
  • antonym: M.V. - an abbreviation for 'motor vessel', used before the name of a motor-powered ship
