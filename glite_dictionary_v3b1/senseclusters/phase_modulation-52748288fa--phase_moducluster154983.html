phase modulation

noun
variation of a wave's phase to transmit signals
6.0 5.3
Pronunciation: /feɪz mɑː.dʒə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /feɪz mɒd.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a method of sending information by varying the phase of a carrier wave, often used in radio transmission and telecommunications

Examples

Phase modulation signal
Digital signals are commonly transmitted using phase modulation techniques.
Engineers often compare phase modulation and frequency modulation when designing radio transmitters.
The new communication system relies on phase modulation to encode information efficiently.
