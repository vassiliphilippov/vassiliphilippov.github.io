particulate radiation

noun
radiation made of atomic or subatomic particles
Pronunciation: / pɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt ɹeɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪkjʊlət reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
radiation consisting of a stream of atomic or subatomic particles, which may be positively charged, negatively charged, or neutral

Examples

Particulate radiation particles
Certain types of particulate radiation can penetrate human tissue and pose health risks.
Researchers measured the level of particulate radiation emitted by the radioactive material.
The spacecraft was built to withstand exposure to both cosmic rays and particulate radiation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electromagnetic radiation - radiation in the form of electromagnetic waves, such as light or radio waves
