housekeep

verb
perform digital maintenance
6.8
Pronunciation: / c8ha8ascckid0p / (AmE), / c8ha8ascckid0p / (BrE)
(in computing and data management) to perform routine maintenance on digital systems, files, or databases; to prune, archive, reorganize, or remove obsolete data to keep storage and systems tidy and efficient.

Examples

To housekeep files
Each weekend the sysadmin would housekeep old log files to free up disk space for new backups.
Automated scripts now housekeep daily records by compressing, archiving, and deleting obsolete entries.
Before the audit, the team housekept the database, removing duplicates and reorganizing tables for clarity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archive - to store data in a way that preserves it for long-term access while freeing active storage
  • synonym: maintain - to keep something in good condition by doing necessary work or updates
