nonmanual

adjective
not using hands
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈmænjuəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈmænjuəl / (BrE)
relating to facial expressions, head or body movements, or other signals not made with the hands, especially in sign languages.

Examples

Nonmanual facial signal
In sign language, nonmanual signals like raised eyebrows can change the meaning of a sentence.
Researchers study both manual and nonmanual features to understand how sign languages convey emotion.
The teacher emphasized the importance of nonmanual cues when learning new signs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manual - relating to or done with the hands
