some

adjective
remarkable or impressive (emphatic)
2.2
Pronunciation: / sʌm / (AmE), / sʌm / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize that someone or something is remarkable or impressive in some way.

Examples

Some performance tonight
He is some drummer; I've never seen anyone play like that before!
That was some party—they really went all out with the decorations and entertainment.
The storm last night was some storm, with thunder shaking the whole house.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ordinary - not distinctive or remarkable; commonplace
  • synonym: remarkable - worthy of attention; striking
