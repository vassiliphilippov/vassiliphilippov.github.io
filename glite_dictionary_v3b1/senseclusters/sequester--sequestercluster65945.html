sequester

verb
capture and store atmospheric carbon
7.0
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈkwes.tər/ /; / /sə.ˈkwes.tər/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈkwes.tə/ /; / /sə.ˈkwes.tə/ (BrE)
(in environmental science) to capture and store carbon dioxide or other greenhouse gases from the atmosphere, typically in vegetation, soils, or geological formations.

Examples

To sequester carbon
Forests play a vital role because they sequester carbon dioxide in biomass and soil over decades.
The new project will sequester CO2 underground using pipelines and deep geological storage sites.
Researchers study whether certain soils can sequester carbon more effectively under different farming practices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capture - to take or trap a gas or substance so it can be stored or prevented from entering the atmosphere
  • synonym: carbon sequestration - the process of long-term removal and storage of atmospheric carbon dioxide
  • synonym: store - to keep something in a place or system for future use or long-term retention
