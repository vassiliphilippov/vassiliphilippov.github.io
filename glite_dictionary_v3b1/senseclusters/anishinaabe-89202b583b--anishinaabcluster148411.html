Anishinaabe

proper noun
Indigenous North American group
Pronunciation: / əˌnɪʃɪˈnɑːbeɪ / (AmE), / əˌnɪʃɪˈnɑːbeɪ / (BrE)
a member of a group of Indigenous peoples native to Canada and the United States, including the Ojibwe, Odawa, and Algonquin peoples

Examples

An Anishinaabe person
The Anishinaabe have a long history of storytelling and oral tradition.
Many Anishinaabe communities celebrate their heritage through annual powwows.
The language of the Anishinaabe is still spoken by many people today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Algonquin - an Indigenous people of Canada, part of the Anishinaabe group
  • synonym: Ojibwe - an Indigenous people of North America, part of the Anishinaabe group
