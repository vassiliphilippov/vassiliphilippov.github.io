mezzanine floor

noun
intermediate floor above ground level
6.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…õz.…ôÀånin fl…îr / (AmE), / Ààm…õz.…ôniÀên fl…îÀê / (BrE)
an intermediate floor between the main floors of a building, especially just above the ground floor

Examples

The mezzanine floor level
From the mezzanine floor, you get a perfect view of the main hall below.
The library has a mezzanine floor with extra seating and computers for visitors.
We decided to convert the mezzanine floor into a small art gallery.

Synonyms

  • mezzanine
    6.7 0.45/m
    mezzanine is the common short term for a low upper level in buildings, while mezzanine floor is the fuller, slightly more formal phrase.
  • entresol
    9.9 0.00/m
    entresol is an architectural term for a low intermediate level often projecting inside buildings, while mezzanine floor is the general everyday term.
