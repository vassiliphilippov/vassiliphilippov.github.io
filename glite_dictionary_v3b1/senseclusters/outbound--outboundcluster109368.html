outbound

adjective
going out or leaving
3.5 0.47/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆaÊŠtËŒbaÊŠnd / (AmE), / ËˆaÊŠtbaÊŠnd / (BrE)
traveling or directed away from a place, especially from the point of origin or center.

Examples

All outbound trains
Heavy traffic delayed all outbound trains this morning.
Our flight is outbound at 3 p.m. from Terminal 2.
The outbound road was closed due to flooding.

Synonyms

  • outward
    2.4 1.75/m
    outward describes movement away from a place at the start of a journey, while outbound labels travel directed from a center or origin.
  • outward-bound
    5.2 0.01/m
    outward-bound describes a ship or train leaving on its first leg, while outbound describes travel directed away from the point of origin.
  • departing
    1.3 0.00/m
    departing refers to a person or vehicle that is about to leave or has just left, while outbound means moving away from the origin.
