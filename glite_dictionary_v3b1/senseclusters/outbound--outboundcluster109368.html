outbound

adjective
going out or leaving
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtbaʊnd / (BrE)
traveling or directed away from a place, especially from the point of origin or center.

Examples

All outbound trains
Heavy traffic delayed all outbound trains this morning.
Our flight is outbound at 3 p.m. from Terminal 2.
The outbound road was closed due to flooding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inbound - coming in or arriving, especially from elsewhere
