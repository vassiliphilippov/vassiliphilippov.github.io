ill-fated

adjective
marked by bad fortune
7.6
Pronunciation: / ɪlˈfeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪlˈfeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having or likely to have very bad luck; destined to end in failure or disaster.

Examples

An ill-fated plan
Many believe the company was ill-fated from the beginning due to poor planning.
The ill-fated voyage ended in tragedy when the ship struck an iceberg.
They invested all their savings in an ill-fated scheme that failed within months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doomed - certain to suffer misfortune or failure
  • antonym: fortunate - having good luck
  • synonym: unlucky - having or marked by bad luck
