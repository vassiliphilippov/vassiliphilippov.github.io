trade acceptance

noun
buyer-accepted bill of exchange for a purchase
Pronunciation: / treɪd əkˈsɛptəns / (AmE), / treɪd əkˈsɛptəns / (BrE)
a bill of exchange drawn by a seller on the buyer to pay for specific goods or services, which the buyer accepts and agrees to pay by signing it

Examples

A trade acceptance bill
By signing the trade acceptance, the buyer confirmed their obligation to pay the agreed amount on the specified date.
In international transactions, a trade acceptance helps assure sellers they will receive payment from the buyer.
The supplier requested a trade acceptance from the buyer to secure payment for the delivered goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banker's acceptance - a bill of exchange accepted by a bank
  • synonym: bill of exchange - a written order for payment, used in commerce
