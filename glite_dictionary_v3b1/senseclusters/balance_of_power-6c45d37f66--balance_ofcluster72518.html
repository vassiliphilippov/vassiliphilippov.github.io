balance of power

noun
equilibrium between rival powers or nations
Pronunciation: / ˈbæl.əns əv ˈpaʊ.ɚ /; / ˈbæləns əv ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əns əv ˈpaʊ.ə /; / ˈbæləns əv ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
a situation where power is shared among groups or nations so that no single group can dominate the others, especially in politics or international relations

Examples

The balance of power
Diplomats work tirelessly to preserve the balance of power in unstable regions.
During the Cold War, the arms race was partly driven by the desire to maintain a balance of power.
The treaty established a balance of power that prevented any one country from becoming too dominant.
A good constitution maintains a balance of power between different branches of government.
Diplomats worked to preserve the balance of power and prevent any country from becoming too strong.
The new alliance shifted the balance of power in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equilibrium - a state in which opposing forces or influences are balanced
  • antonym: hegemony - leadership or dominance by one state or group
