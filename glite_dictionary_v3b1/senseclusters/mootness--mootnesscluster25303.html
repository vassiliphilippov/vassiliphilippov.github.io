mootness

noun
irrelevance in law
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːtnəs / (AmE), / ˈmuːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being debatable or no longer relevant, especially in a legal context where an issue is no longer subject to a court ruling

Examples

The mootness of issue
The court questioned the mootness of the dispute after the law had changed.
Because the contract was already fulfilled, the mootness of the claim was discussed.
The judge explained that the mootness doctrine prevents courts from ruling on hypothetical issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrelevance - the state of not being related or important to the matter at hand
  • antonym: relevance - the quality of being closely connected or appropriate
