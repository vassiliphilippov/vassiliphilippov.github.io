ward

noun
district for administration or elections
3.4 3.64/m
Pronunciation: / w…îrd / (AmE), / w…îÀêd / (BrE)
a district into which a city or town is divided for administrative or electoral purposes

Examples

City ward
Each city council member represents a ward in the municipal government.
New boundaries for the third ward were approved by the committee.
Voters in the southern ward turned out in record numbers for the election.

Synonyms

  • precinct
    6.2 8.18/m
    precinct is an area used for police or voting work in a city, while ward is a local district for administration or elections.
  • election district
    3.5 0.09/m
    election district is an area set for voting with its own polling place, while ward also serves wider local administrative functions.
  • voting precinct
    5.2 0.16/m
    voting precinct is a small district with a specific polling place for elections, while ward can be a larger administrative or electoral area.
  • city district
    2.8 0.10/m
    city district is an area defined for city planning or local services, while ward is an official division often used for elections.
