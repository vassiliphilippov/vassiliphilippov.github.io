reemit

verb
to send out again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈmɪt / (BrE)
to send out or give off again something that was previously absorbed, especially light, heat, or energy

Examples

Some gases in the atmosphere reemit infrared radiation back toward the Earth's surface.

The sensor detects how much energy the material will reemit after exposure to light.

Some surfaces reemit infrared radiation after being exposed to sunlight.
