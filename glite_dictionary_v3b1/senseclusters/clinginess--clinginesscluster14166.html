clinginess

noun
tendency to stick
4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈklɪŋ.iː.nəs/ /; / /ˈklɪŋ.ɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈklɪŋ.iː.nəs/ /; / /ˈklɪŋ.ɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of tending to stick closely to surfaces, especially referring to fabrics or materials that adhere to something.

Examples

Clinginess of fabric
The clinginess of the wet fabric made it uncomfortable to wear after swimming.
Some people dislike the clinginess of certain synthetic materials in hot weather.
The clinginess of the plastic wrap kept the food fresh for several days.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slipperiness - the quality of being smooth and difficult to hold or stay on
  • synonym: stickiness - the quality of being sticky or adhesive
