excitation

noun
neural or electrical stimulation of an organ or tissue
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksaɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksaɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a nerve, muscle, or gland is stimulated by neural or electrical activity, leading to increased activity or response

Examples

Nerve excitation
During a heartbeat, electrical excitation spreads throughout the cardiac muscle.
Recent studies measure the excitation of glands in response to hormonal signals.
The excitation of the nerves caused the muscle to contract rapidly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stimulation - the act of rousing to activity or increased action
