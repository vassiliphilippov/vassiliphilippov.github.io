anesthesia

noun
loss of sensation (medical)
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
Loss of sensation in all or part of the body, often with loss of consciousness, typically induced by medical drugs or other medical means so surgical or medical procedures can be performed without pain.

Examples

General anesthesia
After the dental procedure, the anesthesia wore off slowly, leaving her mouth numb for hours.
Different types of anesthesia are used depending on the operation and the patient's health.
The patient was given anesthesia before the surgery began.
General anaesthesia can sometimes cause side effects such as nausea or dizziness.
Local anaesthesia was used during the dental procedure to numb the area.
The patient remained under anaesthesia for the entire duration of the surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analgesia - the absence of pain in response to stimulation that would normally be painful
  • synonym: anesthesia - American spelling of 'anaesthesia'
