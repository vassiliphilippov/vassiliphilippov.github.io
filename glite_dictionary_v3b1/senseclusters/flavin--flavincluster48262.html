flavin

noun
yellow pigment compound nucleus
7.3 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈfleɪ.vɪn/ (AmE), /ˈfleɪ.vɪn/ (BrE)
a group of naturally occurring yellow pigments, whose chemical nucleus is a ketone structure, particularly found in compounds such as riboflavin

Examples

Yellow flavin pigments
Researchers studied the role of flavin derivatives in cellular respiration.
Riboflavin, a type of vitamin B, contains the flavin nucleus in its structure.
The distinctive yellow color of some enzymes is due to their flavin components.
