dermis

noun
inner layer of the skin
Pronunciation: / ˈdɝː.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɜː.mɪs / (BrE)
the thick inner layer of the skin beneath the epidermis, containing blood vessels, nerves, and glands

Examples

The dermis layer
An injury that reaches the dermis can result in significant scarring.
Collagen fibers in the dermis provide skin with strength and elasticity.
The dermis contains sweat glands and hair follicles that help regulate body temperature.
