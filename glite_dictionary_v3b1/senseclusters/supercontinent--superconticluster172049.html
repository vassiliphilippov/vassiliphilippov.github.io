supercontinent

noun
large joined landmass
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌkɑːntɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəkɒntɪnənt / (BrE)
a very large landmass formed by the joining of several continents into one unit

Examples

The supercontinent Pangaea
Scientists believe that the ancient supercontinent Pangaea broke apart about 175 million years ago.
The theory suggests that Earth's continents will merge again into a new supercontinent in the distant future.
Gondwana was another supercontinent that existed before the continents separated into their current positions.
