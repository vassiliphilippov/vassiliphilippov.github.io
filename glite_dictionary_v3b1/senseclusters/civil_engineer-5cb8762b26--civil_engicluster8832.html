civil engineer

noun
engineer of public works (roads, bridges, etc.)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪvəl ɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
an engineer who is professionally trained to design, construct, and maintain public works such as roads, bridges, and harbors

Examples

Civil engineer designs bridges
After graduating, she became a civil engineer and worked on the city's new bridge project.
Many civil engineers work for government agencies overseeing highway construction.
The company is seeking an experienced civil engineer to design sustainable transportation systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: structural engineer - an engineer specializing in the structure and framework of buildings and other large structures
