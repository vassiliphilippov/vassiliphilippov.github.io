Knight Templar

noun
member of medieval Christian military order
9.7
Pronunciation: / naɪt tɛmˈplɑr / (AmE), / naɪt ˈtɛmplə / (BrE)
a member of a medieval Christian religious military order founded in 1118 to protect pilgrims and the Holy Sepulcher in Jerusalem

Examples

Medieval Knight Templar
As part of the crusades, a Knight Templar would take holy vows and defend sacred sites.
Many legends surround the Knight Templar and their mysterious treasures.
The Knight Templar rode across Europe to aid Christian pilgrims on their journey to Jerusalem.
