koine

noun
common language between language groups
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɔɪˈneɪ / (AmE), / kɔɪˈneɪ / (BrE)
A language or dialect that serves as a common means of communication between speakers of different native languages, especially referring to the common Greek dialect used across the eastern Mediterranean in Hellenistic and Roman times.

Examples

The koine Greek
Classical scholars often study the koine to better understand early Christian texts.
In ancient times, the use of a koine helped traders from different regions communicate effectively.
Some sociolinguists compare modern global English to the role of the koine in the ancient Mediterranean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lingua franca - a language systematically used to make communication possible between people not sharing a first language
