phase IV

noun
post-approval drug trial stage
6.0
Pronunciation: / feɪz fɔr / (AmE), / feɪz fɔː / (BrE)
the stage of a clinical drug trial conducted after a drug has been approved for general use, during which its long-term effects and effectiveness are monitored in a broader patient population

Examples

Phase IV clinical trial
After FDA approval, phase IV studies help ensure the treatment's safety in the general population.
Doctors discovered rare side effects of the drug during the phase IV monitoring.
The medication is now being tested in phase IV trials to monitor its long-term effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clinical trial - a research study to test how well a medical treatment works in humans
  • degree lower: phase III - an earlier stage of clinical drug trials to assess effectiveness and safety
  • synonym: post-marketing surveillance - monitoring the safety of a drug after it has been released on the market
