telegraph plant

noun
tropical Asian plant with moving leaves
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛl.ɪˌɡræf plænt / (AmE), / ˈtɛl.ɪˌɡrɑːf plɑːnt / (BrE)
a tropical Asian shrub (Desmodium gyrans) whose small lateral leaflets rotate and move up and down, particularly in sunlight

Examples

A telegraph plant moves
A telegraph plant stood in the corner of the greenhouse, attracting attention with its jerky motions.
Scientists studied the movements of the telegraph plant to understand rapid plant reactions.
The telegraph plant is famous for its leaves that appear to dance in response to light.
