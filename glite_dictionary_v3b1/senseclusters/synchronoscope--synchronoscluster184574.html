synchronoscope

noun
instrument for checking synchronization of motions
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪŋ.krə.noʊˈskoʊp / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋ.krə.nəˈskəʊp / (BrE)
an instrument used to determine whether two periodic motions are synchronized, especially for synchronizing the propellers of multi-engine aircraft

Examples

The synchronoscope instrument
A pilot monitors the synchronoscope to ensure both propellers are operating in unison.
Maintenance crews regularly check the synchronoscope when servicing the aircraft's engines.
The synchronoscope indicated that the generators were out of phase and needed adjustment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oscilloscope - electronic instrument for displaying varying signal voltages
