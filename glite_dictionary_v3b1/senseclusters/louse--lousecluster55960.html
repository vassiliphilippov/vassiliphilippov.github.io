louse

verb
to spoil or ruin
Pronunciation: / laʊs / (AmE), / laʊs / (BrE)
to spoil, ruin, or mess up something, especially by making careless mistakes

Examples

To louse up work
He completely loused up the report by submitting it late.
If you are not careful, you might louse up the whole project.
She realized she had loused her chances by missing the interview.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fix - to repair or correct something
  • synonym: mess up - to make a mistake or spoil something
  • synonym: ruin - to destroy or spoil something completely
