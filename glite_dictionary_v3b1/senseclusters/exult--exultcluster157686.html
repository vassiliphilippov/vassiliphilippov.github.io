exult

verb
show or feel triumphant joy
7.4
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzʌlt /; / ɪgˈzʌlt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzʌlt /; / ɪgˈzʌlt / (BrE)
to show or feel great joy or triumph, especially after a success or victory

Examples

To exult in victory
Crowds exulted in the streets as the final results were announced.
She exulted in her success, unable to hide her excitement from her friends.
The team exulted after winning the championship for the first time in a decade.
Crowds gathered to exult in the nation's historic achievement.
She couldn't help but exult when she received the good news.
The players exulted after their unexpected victory in the finals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret, often for something lost
  • antonym: mourn - to feel or express sorrow or grief
  • synonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
