nothosaur

noun
extinct marine reptile of the Triassic
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊθəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈnɒθəˌsɔː / (BrE)
an extinct marine reptile of the Triassic period, with longer and more slender limbs than plesiosaurs and less fully adapted for swimming

Examples

The nothosaur fossil
Fossils of the nothosaur have been found in Europe and Asia.
The nothosaur had elongated jaws filled with sharp teeth for catching fish.
The scientist explained how the limbs of a nothosaur were less adapted for swimming than those of a plesiosaur.
