methylprednisolone

noun
corticosteroid medication
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛθəlprɛdˈnɪsəˌloʊn / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəlprɛdˈnɪsələʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic corticosteroid drug used to reduce inflammation and treat immune-related conditions (such as allergies)

Examples

methylprednisolone medicine
The doctor prescribed methylprednisolone to help control the patient's severe allergic reaction.
After her organ transplant, she was given methylprednisolone to prevent her body from rejecting the new kidney.
High doses of methylprednisolone are sometimes used to treat flare-ups in autoimmune diseases like lupus.
The doctor prescribed methylprednisolone to help with her symptoms.
After taking methylprednisolone, his swelling and pain decreased significantly.
Patients with severe allergies may be treated with methylprednisolone to control their reactions.
