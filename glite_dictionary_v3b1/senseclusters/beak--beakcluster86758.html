beak

noun
hard pointed mouthpart of a bird
3.1
Pronunciation: / bik / (AmE), / biːk / (BrE)
the hard, pointed, projecting mouthpart of a bird, used for eating and other functions

Examples

A bird's beak
Parrots have a curved beak which helps them crack nuts and seeds.
The eagle used its sharp beak to tear the meat from its prey.
The robin wiped its beak on a branch after catching an insect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bill - the beak of a bird, especially a web-footed one
