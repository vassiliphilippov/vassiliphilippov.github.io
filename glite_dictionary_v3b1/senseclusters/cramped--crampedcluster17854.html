cramped

adjective
restricted or uncomfortably small space
3.2 2.59/m
Pronunciation: / krÃ¦mpt / (AmE), / krÃ¦mpt / (BrE)
(of a space) uncomfortably small or restricted; having little room to move.

Examples

A cramped space
He felt uncomfortable in the cramped elevator filled with people.
The family lived in a cramped apartment with barely enough space for their belongings.
The office was so cramped that employees could hardly move around their desks.

Synonyms

  • incommodious
    9.9 0.01/m
    incommodious is a formal word for an awkward, inconveniently small space often used in writing, while cramped is common and physical.
  • confining
    6.2 0.03/m
    confining feels restrictive and trapping in both space and situation, while cramped simply means physically small with little room to move.
  • claustrophobic
    6.7 0.94/m
    claustrophobic makes someone feel anxious or panicky in tight spaces, while cramped only describes a small, uncomfortable area.
  • boxed-in
    6.3 0.13/m
    boxed-in describes a feeling of being surrounded by barriers or limits, while cramped focuses on a small physical space.
  • crowded
    1.0 14/m
    crowded means filled with too many people or things, while cramped means the actual space is small and gives little room.
  • tight
    1.0 46/m
    tight often describes things fitting closely, like clothes or schedules, while cramped describes a small space that feels uncomfortable.
