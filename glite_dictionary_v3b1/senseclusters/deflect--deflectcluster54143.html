deflect

verb
divert attention or focus
4.6 6.04/m
Pronunciation: / dÉªËˆflÉ›kt / (AmE), / dÉªËˆflÉ›kt / (BrE)
to cause something (such as an object, attention, or criticism) to change direction or be diverted from its original course, often to avoid, block, or distract

Examples

To deflect criticism
His sudden outburst was meant to deflect the teacher's criticism away from his mistakes.
She deflected questions about her personal life with a joke.
The politician tried to deflect attention from the scandal by focusing on economic issues.
She skillfully deflected the opponent's attack during the match.
The defender managed to deflect the pass before it reached the striker.
The goalkeeper deflected the ball with his fingertips to prevent a goal.
Good communication can often deflect unnecessary arguments before they escalate.
She tried to deflect criticism by explaining her decision in detail.
The company introduced new policies to deflect potential lawsuits.

Synonyms

  • divert
    4.2 5.07/m
    divert causes someone or something to change route or direction, while deflect often changes direction to avoid or block.
  • parry
    8.1 0.23/m
    parry avoids answering questions cleverly to escape, while deflect means turning aside attention or criticism to avoid dealing with it.
  • parry
    6.9 0.41/m
    parry wards off an opponent's blow or object in sport or combat, while deflect simply changes the object's direction.
  • misdirect
    6.0 0.26/m
    misdirect sends someone or something the wrong way or gives false guidance deliberately, while deflect is a broader change of direction.
  • distract
    2.1 25/m
    distract takes a person's attention away from a task or thought, while deflect can change direction of attention or objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to let through, not block or impede
  • synonym: avert - to prevent something bad from happening
  • synonym: avoid - to keep away from or stop something from happening
  • synonym: block - to stop or prevent movement, especially in games or sports
  • synonym: deviate - to turn aside from a route, way, course, etc.
  • antonym: direct - to cause to move in a straight line or toward a point
  • synonym: distract - to prevent someone from concentrating
  • synonym: divert - to cause something or someone to change direction
  • antonym: invite - to bring on or make more likely (in context of danger or risk)
  • synonym: redirect - to send something in a different direction
  • synonym: reflect - cause to bounce back or turn aside, especially light or sound
  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight; not to turn aside
