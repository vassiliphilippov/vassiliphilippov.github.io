shlep

noun
tedious or difficult journey
Pronunciation: / ʃlɛp / (AmE), / ʃlɛp / (BrE)
An arduous, tedious, or difficult journey or trip, often implying inconvenience.

Examples

A long shlep
After that long shlep up the mountain, we were completely exhausted.
It was a real shlep getting across town during rush hour.
Visiting her means a two-hour shlep from the suburbs into the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: jaunt - a short excursion or journey for pleasure
  • synonym: trek - a long arduous journey, especially on foot
