clot buster

noun
drug that dissolves blood clots
Pronunciation: / klɑt ˈbʌstɚ / (AmE), / klɒt ˈbʌstə / (BrE)
a drug used to dissolve blood clots that block blood flow, especially to the heart

Examples

A clot buster drug
Doctors say that giving a clot buster quickly can significantly improve survival rates after a heart attack.
Paramedics administered a clot buster to the patient en route to the hospital.
She credited the clot buster for her speedy recovery from the stroke.
