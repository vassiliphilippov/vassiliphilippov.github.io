buffalo gourd

noun
perennial vine with inedible fruit
8.8 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌf.ə.loʊ gɔːrd/ /; / /ˈbʌf.ə.loʊ gʊərd/ (AmE), /ˈbʌf.ə.ləʊ gɔːd/ /; / /ˈbʌf.ə.ləʊ gʊəd/ (BrE)
A perennial vine native to dry regions of central and southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its small, hard, mottled green inedible fruit.

Examples

The buffalo gourd fruit
Although the fruit of the buffalo gourd is not edible, its roots have traditional uses.
Native peoples once used the seeds of the buffalo gourd for food and oil.
The buffalo gourd is commonly found in arid regions where few other plants thrive.
