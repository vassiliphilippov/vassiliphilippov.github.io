claim

noun
a demand for money, property, or rights
Pronunciation: / kleɪm / (AmE), / kleɪm / (BrE)
a demand, request, or right to something, especially money, property, or compensation

Examples

An insurance claim
She filed a claim for compensation after her luggage was lost during the flight.
The court rejected the claim because it lacked supporting evidence.
The insurance company's response to his claim was delayed for several weeks.
He filed a claim for unpaid wages through his attorney.
She made a claim for the lost luggage at the airport.
The company rejected his claim for compensation, stating it was not covered by the policy.
He filed a claim for damages after the accident.
The employees presented a claim for fair compensation during the contract negotiations.
Their claim for a refund was supported by clear evidence of the defect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claimant - a person making a claim, especially in a lawsuit
  • synonym: demand - an insistent request for something due
  • synonym: entitlement - the right to have something; a justified claim
  • degree lower: request - the act of asking for something
  • synonym: right - a legal, moral, or just claim
  • synonym: title - a legal right to own something, especially property
