circumscribe

verb
to draw a shape around another in geometry
8.7 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrkəmˌskraɪb /; / ˌsɜrkəmˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmskraɪb /; / ˌsɜːkəmˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to draw a shape or line around another shape so that it touches at all possible points, especially in geometry

Examples

To circumscribe a triangle
In geometry class, the teacher showed us how to circumscribe a circle around a triangle.
The engineer needed to circumscribe the square with a circle of precisely calculated radius.
To find the minimum area rectangle that can circumscribe a given polygon, you need to follow a specific method.
A triangle can be circumscribed by a circle so that all its vertices touch the circle.
The artist carefully circumscribed the area for the mural.
They used chalk to circumscribe a section of the playground for the new game.
