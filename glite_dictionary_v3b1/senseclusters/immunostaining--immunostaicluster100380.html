immunostaining

noun
protein detection technique
7.8
Pronunciation: /ɪ.mjuː.noʊ.ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ɪ.mjuː.nə.ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ɪ.mjuː.nəʊ.ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ɪ.mjuː.nə.ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
a laboratory technique that uses antibodies to detect specific proteins in cells or tissues, often producing a visible colored pattern

Examples

Immunostaining technique
The scientist performed immunostaining to visualize the distribution of the protein in the tissue.
Accurate immunostaining requires careful preparation of both the antibodies and the samples.
Using immunostaining, researchers can identify specific cell types in complex tissues.
The immunostaining revealed the presence of the virus in the tissue sample.
Researchers used immunostaining to identify which cells were producing the protein.
Clear immunostaining patterns helped the doctors diagnose the disease accurately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunohistochemistry - a method for detecting proteins in tissue sections using antibodies
  • synonym: staining - the process of coloring cells or tissues to observe them under a microscope
  • synonym: western blot - a technique for detecting specific proteins in a sample
