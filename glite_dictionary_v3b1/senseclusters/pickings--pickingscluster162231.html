pickings

noun
things acquired, often easily or with little competition
6.2 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪk.ɪŋz/ (AmE), /ˈpɪk.ɪŋz/ (BrE)
things that are collected, taken, or acquired, especially when they are easy to obtain or there is little competition for them.

Examples

The pickings were slim
At the end of the sale, the pickings were slim and most good items were gone.
During the famine, the pickings from the fields were meager.
The store was closed and the pickings for the thieves were easy.
