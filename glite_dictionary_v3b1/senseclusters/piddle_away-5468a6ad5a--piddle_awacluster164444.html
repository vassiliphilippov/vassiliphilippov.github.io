piddle away

verb
waste time idly
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪdəl əˈweɪ /; / ˈpɪd.əl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɪdəl əˈweɪ /; / ˈpɪd.əl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to waste time or resources by being idle, inefficient, or unproductive

Examples

To piddle away time
Don’t piddle away your weekend—you should finish your project instead.
He tends to piddle away his afternoons scrolling through social media without getting much done.
She realized she had piddled away two hours just chatting by the water cooler.
Many students piddle away their study time by chatting with friends.
On rainy days, he tends to piddle away the hours doing nothing in particular.
She realized she had piddled away the morning scrolling through social media.
