prytany

noun
term of office in Greece
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪ.tə.niː/ (AmE), /ˈprɪ.tə.niː/ (BrE)
a fixed period during which a group of officials held power in an ancient Greek city-state, especially in Athens

Examples

Athenian prytany period
Each prytany in ancient Athens lasted about one-tenth of the year.
The council's decisions were reviewed at the end of every prytany.
Historians study the records from each prytany to understand Athenian democracy.
