schoolmasterly

adjective
strictly teacher-like
Pronunciation: / ˈskuːlˌmæstərli / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɑːstəli / (BrE)
having the qualities or manner of a strict, formal, or authoritative schoolteacher

Examples

Schoolmasterly tone
His schoolmasterly tone made the meeting feel more like a classroom than a discussion.
She gave schoolmasterly instructions, ensuring everyone understood each step clearly.
The manager's schoolmasterly approach was effective but sometimes felt overly strict to the team.
Her schoolmasterly approach to meetings made everyone feel like they were back in class.
He gave a schoolmasterly lecture on the importance of punctuality.
The manager's schoolmasterly tone discouraged open discussion among the team.
Her schoolmasterly approach to teaching made the students work hard and respect her authority.
He corrected our essays in a schoolmasterly manner, pointing out every small mistake.
The professor's schoolmasterly tone reminded the class of a strict headteacher.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritative - commanding and self-confident, often in teaching
  • antonym: lenient - not strict or harsh in discipline
  • synonym: pedagogical - relating to teaching or education
  • synonym: pedantic - overly concerned with formal rules or details
  • synonym: scholarly - having or showing knowledge, learning, or devotion to academic study
