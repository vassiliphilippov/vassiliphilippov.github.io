mesh

verb
to work well together
6.5 3.4
Pronunciation: /meʃ/ (AmE), /meʃ/ (BrE)
to fit, coordinate, or work well together; to function smoothly with others or as parts of a machine

Examples

To mesh ideas
Her ideas simply didn't mesh with the direction the company was taking.
The new software does not mesh with the old system as smoothly as we hoped.
Their personalities mesh well, making them a strong team.
If the cogs don't mesh properly, the machine could be damaged.
The technician made sure the two gears meshed before starting the engine.
Watch how the pinions mesh with the main gear as the wheel turns.
Our schedules finally meshed, so we were able to meet for lunch.
The new manager and her team mesh perfectly, leading to great results.
Their personalities don't always mesh, but their skills complement each other well.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conflict - to be incompatible or in disagreement
  • synonym: engage - (of gears or parts) to interlock or fit together
  • synonym: integrate - to combine with others so as to become a whole
