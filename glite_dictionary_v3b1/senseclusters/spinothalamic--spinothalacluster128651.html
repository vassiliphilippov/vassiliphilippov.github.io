spinothalamic

noun
nerve tract to thalamus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌspaɪnoʊθəˈlæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌspaɪnəʊθəˈlæmɪk / (BrE)
a nerve tract that transmits pain and temperature signals from the spinal cord to the thalamus in the brain

Examples

Spinothalamic tract
Damage to the spinothalamic can result in loss of pain and temperature sensation.
The neurologist explained how the spinothalamic is essential for transmitting sensory information.
An MRI revealed a lesion affecting the patient's spinothalamic after the accident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corticospinal tract - a nerve pathway from the cerebral cortex to the spinal cord
  • synonym: spinothalamic tract - the nerve pathway carrying pain and temperature signals to the thalamus
