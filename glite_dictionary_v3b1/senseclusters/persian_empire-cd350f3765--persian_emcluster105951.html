Persian Empire

noun
ancient empire in southwest Asia
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrʒən ˈɛmpaɪr / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
a major ancient empire in southwest Asia, established by Cyrus the Great in the 6th century BC and conquered by Alexander the Great in the 4th century BC

Examples

The Persian Empire history
Historians consider the Persian Empire one of the greatest powers of the ancient world.
The Persian Empire fell to Alexander the Great in 330 BC after years of warfare.
The administration of the Persian Empire was known for its efficiency and tolerance of different cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Achaemenid Empire - the first Persian Empire founded by Cyrus the Great
  • antonym: Macedonian Empire - empire established by Alexander the Great who conquered the Persian Empire
