little avail

noun
minimal benefit or effectiveness
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬l əˈveɪl / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl əˈveɪl / (BrE)
Very little benefit or effectiveness; used to express that efforts produce minimal or no useful results.

Examples

Of little avail
Despite all their plans and precautions, it was of little avail against the sudden storm.
She pleaded with him to reconsider, but her arguments were of little avail.
Their efforts to stop the leak were to little avail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: to no avail - with no result; ineffective
