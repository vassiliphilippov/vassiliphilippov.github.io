prebendal

adjective
relating to patronage
9.9
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈben.dəl/ /; / /pre.ˈben.dəl/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈben.dəl/ /; / /pre.ˈben.dəl/ (BrE)
relating to the use of public office to distribute state resources to supporters, especially through patronage or clientelism

Examples

Prebendal politics
The country struggled with prebendal politics, where government jobs were given to loyal supporters.
Analysts warned that prebendal systems can undermine trust in public institutions.
Many citizens were frustrated by the prebendal distribution of state contracts and resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clientelist - relating to a system of exchanging goods or services for political support
  • antonym: meritocratic - based on ability or merit rather than favoritism
  • synonym: patronage - the power to give jobs or favors to supporters
