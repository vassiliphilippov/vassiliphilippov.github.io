indecently

adverb
in an improper or offensive manner
6.7 0.09/m

in a way that does not conform to accepted standards of morality or decency; in an improper or offensive manner

Examples

Behave indecently in public
He behaved indecently in public, shocking the other bystanders.
She was indecently dressed at the party, attracting a lot of attention.
The comedian's jokes were considered indecently vulgar for a family show.

Synonyms

  • obscenely
    7.6 0.11/m
    obscenely is strongly offensive often in a sexual way, while indecently covers a wider range of improper or offensive acts.
  • lewdly
    8.5 0.05/m
    lewdly describes sexual or crude behavior meant to shock, while indecently can mean offensive in many nonsexual situations too.
  • vulgarly
    8.3 0.07/m
    vulgarly uses crude language or acts in public, while indecently covers broader moral or offensive behavior beyond mere rudeness.
  • indecorously
    8.7 0.01/m
    indecorously breaks social rules or good manners in a mild way, while indecently often implies a stronger moral or sexual offense.
