Franco-Prussian War

noun
war between France and Prussia, 1870–71
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfræŋkoʊ prʌʃən ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌfræŋkəʊ ˈprʌʃən ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a war fought between France and Prussia from 1870 to 1871, resulting in the fall of the French Second Empire and the unification of Germany

Examples

The Franco-Prussian War
After the Franco-Prussian War, the German Empire was officially proclaimed in 1871.
Historians often cite the Franco-Prussian War as a key moment leading to the rise of modern Germany.
The Franco-Prussian War dramatically changed the political landscape of Europe.
