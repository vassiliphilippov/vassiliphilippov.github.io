ungroundedness

noun
lack of foundation
9.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡraʊndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡraʊndɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state of lacking a firm basis, justification, or connection to reality

Examples

Ungroundedness of ideas
The ungroundedness of his claims made them difficult to believe.
She worried that her ideas would be dismissed for their apparent ungroundedness.
Critics pointed out the ungroundedness in the theory's assumptions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baselessness - the quality of having no foundation or justification
  • antonym: groundedness - the quality of being based on facts or reality
