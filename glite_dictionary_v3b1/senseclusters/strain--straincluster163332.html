strain

noun
deformation of a body under force (physics)
7.5 1.82/m
Pronunciation: / streÉªn / (AmE), / streÉªn / (BrE)
(Physics) The deformation or change in shape of a physical body as a result of applied stress or forces.

Examples

Physical strain
Engineers must calculate how much strain a bridge can tolerate before it distorts.
Excessive strain can permanently deform certain materials.
The metal rod experienced strain when pressure was applied to its ends.

Synonyms

  • deformation
    5.9 1.85/m
    deformation is the general process or result of a shape changing and often implies damage, while strain is the change caused by applied stress.
