chitosan

noun
water treatment flocculant
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkaɪ.tə.sæn/ (AmE), /ˈkaɪ.tə.sæn/ (BrE)
a substance derived from chitin, used as a flocculant in water treatment to bind and remove suspended particles

Examples

Chitosan water treatment
The engineers used chitosan to improve the clarity of the treated water.
Adding chitosan to the process helped remove more impurities from the wastewater.
Researchers are studying chitosan for its effectiveness in different water treatment systems.
