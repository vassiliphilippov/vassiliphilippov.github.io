hardstem bulrush

noun
North American sedge with rigid green stems
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrd.stɛm ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd.stɛm ˈbʊl.rʌʃ / (BrE)
A widely distributed North American sedge with rigid olive-green stems, found in wetlands; a species of bulrush (Schoenoplectus acutus).

Examples

The hardstem bulrush plant
Scientists collected samples of hardstem bulrush to study its ecological role in the ecosystem.
The marsh was full of hardstem bulrush, providing cover for nesting waterfowl.
Wetland restoration projects often include planting hardstem bulrush to stabilize the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulrush - A tall marsh plant of the sedge family, typically with a rounded stem and small, brown, wind-pollinated flowers
