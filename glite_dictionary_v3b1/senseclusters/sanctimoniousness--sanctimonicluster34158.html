sanctimoniousness

noun
hypocritical show of moral superiority
7.9 8.1
Pronunciation: /sæŋk.tɪ.ˈmoʊ.nɪ.əs.nəs/ /; / /sæŋk.tə.ˈmoʊ.nɪ.əs.nəs/ (AmE), /sæŋk.tɪ.ˈməʊ.nɪ.əs.nəs/ /; / /sæŋk.tə.ˈməʊ.nɪ.əs.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality or state of making a show of being morally superior to others, especially in a hypocritical or insincere way.

Examples

Sanctimoniousness in speech
Her sanctimoniousness was apparent when she criticized others for actions she herself had done.
His speech was full of sanctimoniousness, but his private life told a different story.
People grow weary of the sanctimoniousness displayed by some politicians during campaigns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypocrisy - the practice of claiming to have moral standards to which one's behavior does not conform
  • synonym: sanctimony - the action or practice of affecting sanctity; hypocritical devoutness
