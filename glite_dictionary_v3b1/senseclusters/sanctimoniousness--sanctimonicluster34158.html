The quality or state of making a show of being morally superior to others, especially in a hypocritical or insincere way.

Examples

Sanctimoniousness in speech
Her sanctimoniousness was apparent when she criticized others for actions she herself had done.
His speech was full of sanctimoniousness, but his private life told a different story.
People grow weary of the sanctimoniousness displayed by some politicians during campaigns.

Synonyms

  • sanctimony
    9.9 0.26/m
    sanctimony is a show of false piety and outward goodness, while sanctimoniousness is openly acting morally superior.
  • hypocrisy
    6.4 9.16/m
    hypocrisy means saying or showing moral rules you do not follow, while sanctimoniousness focuses on looking morally superior.
  • priggishness
    9.9 0.01/m
    priggishness describes being overly proper and self-righteous about manners, while sanctimoniousness stresses moral superiority and insincerity.
