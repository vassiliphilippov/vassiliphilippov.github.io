double entry

noun
bookkeeping system recording debits and credits
4.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊŒbÉ™l ËˆÉ›ntri / (AmE), / ËˆdÊŒbÉ™l ËˆÉ›ntri / (BrE)
A system of bookkeeping in which every transaction is entered as both a debit in one account and a credit in another, ensuring that the accounting equation remains balanced.

Examples

Double entry accounting
The company switched to double entry accounting to improve financial accuracy.
Understanding double entry bookkeeping is essential for every accountant.
With double entry, each transaction affects at least two accounts in the ledger.
