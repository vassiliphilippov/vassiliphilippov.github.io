deprogrammer

noun
device to erase data
Pronunciation: / diːˈproʊɡræmər / (AmE), / diːˈprəʊɡræmə / (BrE)
a device or tool used to erase or remove programmed data, settings, or firmware from electronic memory or devices

Examples

Electronic deprogrammer
The engineer used a deprogrammer to wipe the old software from the circuit board.
Before recycling the phones, each one was connected to a deprogrammer to remove sensitive information.
A deprogrammer is essential for technicians who need to clear devices before installing new firmware.

Related Terms

  • antonym: programmer - a device or person that installs or writes programs for electronic devices
  • synonym: resetter - a device that restores electronic equipment to its original settings
