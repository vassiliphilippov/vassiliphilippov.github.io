gullibly

adverb
naively, easily fooled
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌləbli / (AmE), / ˈɡʌləbli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a tendency to believe things too easily or without questioning

Examples

To gullibly believe stories
She gullibly accepted the fake invitation without checking if it was real.
Tourists sometimes gullibly fall for scams when visiting unfamiliar places.
He gullibly believed the advertisement and bought the useless product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: naively - in a way that shows a lack of experience or wisdom
  • antonym: skeptically - in a way that shows doubt or disbelief
