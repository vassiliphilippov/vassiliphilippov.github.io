Crataegus oxycantha

noun
a Eurasian hawthorn shrub or small tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / krəˈtiːɡəs ɒksɪˈkænθə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ɒksɪˈkanθə / (BrE)
A thorny Eurasian shrub or small tree of the hawthorn genus, bearing dense clusters of white to scarlet flowers and deep red berries, established in parts of eastern North America.

The garden in spring was alive with the blossoms of Crataegus oxycantha.
