baroclinic

adjective
atmospheric instability
Pronunciation: / ˌbæroʊˈklɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌbærəʊˈklɪnɪk / (BrE)
describing a state in the atmosphere or ocean where surfaces of constant pressure and constant density are not parallel, often leading to weather changes, storms, or currents

Examples

A baroclinic atmosphere
A baroclinic atmosphere is often associated with the formation of cyclones and storms.
Meteorologists study baroclinic zones to predict severe weather events.
Rapid temperature changes can indicate a strong baroclinic environment.
Meteorologists study baroclinic zones to understand the development of storms and weather patterns.
The baroclinic instability in the ocean can lead to the formation of powerful currents.
A baroclinic atmosphere often results in the creation of cyclones and other dynamic weather systems.
