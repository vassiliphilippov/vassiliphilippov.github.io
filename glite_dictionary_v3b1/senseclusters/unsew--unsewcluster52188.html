unsew

verb
to undo unity
9.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈsoʊ/ /; / /ən.ˈsoʊ/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈsəʊ/ /; / /ən.ˈsəʊ/ (BrE)
to weaken or destroy the structure, unity, or coherence of something, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

To unsew a group
The constant disagreements threatened to unsew the team's fragile unity before the project ended.
Rumors and mistrust began to unsew the close-knit community.
A single mistake could unsew years of careful planning and cooperation.
