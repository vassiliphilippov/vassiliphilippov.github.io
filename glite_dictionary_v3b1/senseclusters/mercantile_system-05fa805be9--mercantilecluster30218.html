mercantile system

noun
economic system focused on government regulation and national wealth
4.2 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜr.kən.taɪl ˌsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.kən.taɪl ˌsɪs.təm / (BrE)
an economic theory and policy, especially prevalent in Europe during the 16th to 18th centuries, aimed at increasing a nation's wealth through government regulation of trade and accumulation of precious metals.

Examples

The mercantile system theory
Critics of the mercantile system argued that it stifled innovation and favored large monopolies.
Historians often debate the long-term impacts of the mercantile system on modern economies.
The mercantile system encouraged colonial powers to establish strict controls over their overseas territories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mercantilism - an economic policy focused on maximizing exports and accumulating wealth through trade regulation
