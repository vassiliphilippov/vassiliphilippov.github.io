wobbliness

noun
unsteady quality

the quality of being unsteady, shaky, or likely to move from side to side (in movement, sound, appearance, or reliability)

Examples

Noticeable wobbliness in voice
There was a noticeable wobbliness in her voice as she spoke to the large audience.
The table's wobbliness made it difficult to write without the paper moving.
He tried to hide the wobbliness in his hands during the presentation.
The wobbliness of the old chair made it unsafe to sit on.
He noticed a slight wobbliness in his legs after the long hike.
The bridge's wobbliness worried the pedestrians crossing it during the storm.
The wobbliness of the table made it difficult to write without shaking the paper.
Her wobbliness on important issues made voters question her leadership abilities.
You could sense a certain wobbliness in his voice as he announced the decision.

Synonyms

  • unsteadiness
    5.1 0.11/m
    unsteadiness is a general lack of balance or firmness, while wobbliness often suggests a side-to-side, shaky movement.
  • shakiness
    4.1 0.14/m
    shakiness is quick trembling or nervous shaking, while wobbliness suggests slow side-to-side instability you can see or feel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stability - the quality of being steady and not changing
  • antonym: steadiness - the quality of being stable and not likely to move or change
  • synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not confident
  • synonym: unsteadiness - the state of being not firm or stable
