PPI

noun
acid-reducing medicine
4.1
Pronunciation: /piː piː aɪ/ (AmE), /piː piː aɪ/ (BrE)
a type of medicine (proton pump inhibitor) that reduces stomach acid, used to treat ulcers and acid reflux

Examples

PPI medicine
The doctor recommended a PPI to help manage her acid reflux symptoms.
Many patients with stomach ulcers are prescribed a PPI for long-term treatment.
He asked his pharmacist about possible side effects of taking a PPI daily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: H2 blocker - a medicine that reduces stomach acid by blocking histamine receptors
  • synonym: antacid - a medicine that neutralizes stomach acid
