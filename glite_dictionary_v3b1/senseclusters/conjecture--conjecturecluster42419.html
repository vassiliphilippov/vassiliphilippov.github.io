conjecture

noun
speculation based on incomplete evidence
5.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛktʃər /; / kənˈdʒɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛktʃə / (BrE)
an opinion or conclusion formed without enough evidence; a guess or hypothesis

Examples

A wild conjecture
Her theory was dismissed as mere conjecture without supporting data.
Much of the initial report is conjecture, awaiting confirmation from further investigation.
The historian's argument relies heavily on conjecture rather than solid facts.
Her statement was just a conjecture based on a few uncertain facts.
There has been much conjecture about the reasons for his sudden departure.
Without proof, all their theories remain mere conjecture.
Much of what was written about the event was pure conjecture and not based on evidence.
The police have dismissed the rumors as mere conjecture without any factual basis.
The scientist admitted that her theory was, at this stage, only a conjecture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - something that is known for sure
  • antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true
  • synonym: hypothesis - a supposition or proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence
  • synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
