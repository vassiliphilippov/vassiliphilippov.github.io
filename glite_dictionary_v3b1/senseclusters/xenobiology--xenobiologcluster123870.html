xenobiology

noun
study of synthetic life
Pronunciation: / ˌzɛnəbaɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌziːnəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of life forms or biological systems that are not based on standard Earth biology, including artificial or extraterrestrial life

Examples

Study of xenobiology
Advances in xenobiology could lead to the creation of organisms with entirely new genetic codes.
The university offers a course in xenobiology for students interested in synthetic life forms.
Scientists in xenobiology are exploring how alternative amino acids can expand the possibilities of life.
The university introduced a new course in xenobiology to explore the possibility of extraterrestrial life.
Her research in xenobiology focuses on how alien organisms might adapt to different planetary environments.
Advances in xenobiology could help scientists design life forms for space exploration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: astrobiology - the study of life in the universe, including extraterrestrial life
  • degree lower: biology - the scientific study of living organisms
  • synonym: synthetic biology - the design and construction of new biological parts or systems
