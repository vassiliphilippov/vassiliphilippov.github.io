mandarin

noun
influential intellectual or cultural elite member
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.də.rɪn /; / ˈmændəˌrɪn / (AmE), / ˈmæn.dər.ɪn /; / ˈmandərɪn / (BrE)
an influential or powerful person, especially in government or culture

Examples

A government mandarin
Many critics argue that political mandarins are out of touch with everyday concerns.
She was considered a cultural mandarin because of her influence in artistic circles.
The academic mandarins held enormous sway over national educational policy.
He was accused of being a distant mandarin out of touch with ordinary people.
Several retired mandarins offered their advice on the restructuring of the department.
The government mandarins met behind closed doors to decide the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elite - a group of people considered to be the best in a society or category
  • synonym: official - a person holding public office or having official duties
