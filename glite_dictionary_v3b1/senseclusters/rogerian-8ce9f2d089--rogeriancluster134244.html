Rogerian

adjective
emphasizing common ground
8.8
Pronunciation: /rɑː.ˈdʒɪr.iː.ən/ /; / /rɑː.ˈdʒɪə.riː.ən/ (AmE), /rɒ.ˈdʒɪə.ən/ /; / /rə.ˈdʒɪə.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the theories or methods of Carl Rogers, especially those emphasizing empathy, understanding, and client-centeredness (in therapy, counseling, or argument)

Examples

Rogerian therapy method
The professor encouraged a Rogerian approach to debate, focusing on understanding both sides.
Her Rogerian counseling style helped clients feel heard and respected.
A Rogerian argument seeks to reduce conflict by finding shared values.
The counselor adopted a Rogerian style, listening carefully and showing empathy to the client.
Many modern therapists incorporate Rogerian principles into their practice to build trust with patients.
A Rogerian approach encourages open dialogue and values the client's own experiences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adversarial - involving conflict or opposition
  • synonym: client-centered - focused on the client's perspective and needs in therapy
  • synonym: collaborative - working together to achieve a common goal
