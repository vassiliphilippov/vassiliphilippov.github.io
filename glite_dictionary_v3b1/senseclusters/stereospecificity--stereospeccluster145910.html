stereospecificity

noun
specific spatial outcome
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛrioʊˌspɪsɪˈfɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəʊˌspɪsɪˈfɪsɪti / (BrE)
the property of a chemical reaction or process that results in a specific spatial arrangement of atoms in the product

Examples

The stereospecificity of enzyme
The stereospecificity of the enzyme allows it to produce only one form of the molecule.
Researchers studied the stereospecificity of the reaction to understand how the product's shape was determined.
High stereospecificity is important in drug development to ensure the correct biological activity.
