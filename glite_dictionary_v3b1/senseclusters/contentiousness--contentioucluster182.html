contentiousness

noun
tendency to argue or provoke disputes
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛn(t)ʃəsnəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being likely to argue or provoke disagreement; inclination to be quarrelsome or disputatious

Examples

His contentiousness behavior
His contentiousness often alienated his coworkers, who preferred a more harmonious environment.
The contentiousness of the debate made it difficult to reach any agreement.
The teacher was frustrated by the student's persistent contentiousness during classroom discussions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreeableness - the quality of being pleasant and accommodating
  • synonym: argumentativeness - the quality of being prone to argue
