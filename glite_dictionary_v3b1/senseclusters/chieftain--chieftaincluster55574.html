chieftain

noun
leader of a tribe or clan
7.9 1.34/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt ÉiÀêf.t…ôn / (AmE), / Ààt ÉiÀêf.t…™n / (BrE)
the leader or head of a tribe, clan, or group of people, especially in traditional societies

Examples

The chieftain of clan
Each clan had its own chieftain who was responsible for maintaining peace and order.
The chieftain addressed his people with pride and authority during the ceremony.
When the old chieftain died, his eldest son was chosen to succeed him.
After the war, the chieftain worked to unite the rival clans under his rule.
Legend has it that the ancient chieftain still watches over the valley.
The chieftain welcomed the visitors into the village with a grand ceremony.

Synonyms

  • tribal chief
    4.7 0.21/m
    tribal chief is the leader of a tribe in modern usage, while chieftain sounds more old-fashioned and traditional.
  • headman
    7.3 0.62/m
    headman is a male leader chosen by a village or clan, while chieftain is the general, gender-neutral term for the group's leader.
  • chief
    1.9 90/m
    chief is the leader of any group, company, or community in formal settings, while chieftain refers to a tribal leader in traditional societies.
