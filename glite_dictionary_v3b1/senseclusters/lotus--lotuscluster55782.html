lotus

noun
a plant of the genus Lotus
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.təs /; / ˈloʊ.t̬əs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
a water plant with large, showy flowers, often found in ponds and associated with spiritual or cultural symbolism

Examples

A lotus flower
She planted several lotuses in the garden pond.
The lotus is revered in many cultures for its beauty and symbolism.
The leaves of the lotus are often broad and water-repellent.
A lotus is often used as a symbol of purity and spiritual enlightenment in many Asian cultures.
She photographed the delicate pink lotus rising above the still water.
The garden pond was filled with blooming lotus flowers in the summer.
A single lotus flower floated gracefully on the surface of the pond.
Artists often depict the goddess Isis holding a white lotus in Egyptian art.
The ancient Egyptians considered the lotus to be a symbol of creation and rebirth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large floating leaves and flowers, sometimes confused with lotus
