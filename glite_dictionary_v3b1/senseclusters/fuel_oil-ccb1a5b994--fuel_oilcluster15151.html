fuel oil

noun
heavy petroleum fuel
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːəl ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəl ɔɪl / (BrE)
A heavy petroleum product used as fuel in engines, furnaces, or boilers.

Examples

Fuel oil price
Rising prices for fuel oil led to increased heating costs during the winter.
The factory switched from natural gas to fuel oil as its primary energy source.
The ship was powered by fuel oil rather than coal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diesel oil - type of fuel oil used in diesel engines
  • antonym: gasoline - light petroleum-based fuel for engines
