get one's fingers burnt

verb
suffer loss from a risky action
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡɚz bɝːnt /; / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡərz bɜrnt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡəz bɜːnt / (BrE)
to suffer negative or unpleasant consequences as a result of taking a risk or making a bad decision

Examples

To get one's fingers burnt once
After getting his fingers burnt once, he now avoids risky investments.
Many first-time entrepreneurs get their fingers burnt by underestimating the challenges of running a business.
She invested heavily in real estate last year and got her fingers burnt when the market collapsed.
After her last business failed, she became cautious about new ventures, not wanting to get her fingers burnt again.
He warned me not to try it or I'd get my fingers burnt.
Many investors get their fingers burnt when the stock market crashes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn one's fingers - to suffer because of one's actions, often by taking risks; same idiom with different form
  • synonym: burn oneself - to experience harm or damage oneself, literally or figuratively
