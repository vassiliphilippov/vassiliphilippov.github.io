prelims

noun
preliminary exams
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːlɪmz / (AmE), / ˈpriːlɪmz / (BrE)
preliminary examinations taken before the main or final exams, often to assess readiness

Examples

University prelims
She was nervous about her prelims next week.
Passing the prelims is required before you can take the final exam.
Many students study hard for their prelims to ensure they are prepared for the finals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finals - the main or last set of exams in a course
  • synonym: midterms - exams taken in the middle of an academic term
