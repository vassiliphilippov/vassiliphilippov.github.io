orbital plane

noun
plane defined by an orbiting body's path
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrbɪtl̩ pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪt(ə)l pleɪn / (BrE)
(astronomy) the flat, two-dimensional surface defined by the path along which an object travels as it orbits around another body.

Examples

The orbital plane path
Satellites must be placed in the correct orbital plane to function properly.
The Earth's orbital plane is tilted relative to its equator, causing the seasons.
The asteroid crossed the orbital plane of Mars during its journey around the Sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orbit - the curved path of a celestial object or spacecraft around a star, planet, or moon
