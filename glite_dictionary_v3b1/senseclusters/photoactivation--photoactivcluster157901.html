photoactivation

noun
activation by light
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of activating a substance or reaction by exposure to light

Examples

The photoactivation process
The photoactivation of the drug allows it to target cancer cells only when exposed to light.
Researchers measured the photoactivation of enzymes under different wavelengths.
Modern therapies often rely on photoactivation to trigger specific biological responses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inactivation - the process of making something inactive
  • synonym: photoreaction - a chemical reaction caused by light
