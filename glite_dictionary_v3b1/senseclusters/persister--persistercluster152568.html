persister

noun
surviving microorganism
Pronunciation: / pərˈsɪstər / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstə / (BrE)
a microorganism, cell, organism, or program that survives or continues to function in difficult or harsh conditions, especially by resisting threats or treatment

Examples

Antibiotic persister
Some bacteria become persisters and survive antibiotic treatment.
Researchers are studying how persisters evade the effects of common drugs.
The presence of persisters can make infections difficult to eliminate completely.
Certain bacteria become persisters and survive even after antibiotic treatment.
The software uses a persister to save user data during unexpected shutdowns.
In harsh environments, a persister can outlast other cells by entering a dormant state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resistant - able to withstand or not be affected by something
  • synonym: survivor - an organism that continues to live despite difficult conditions
