coil

verb
make pottery by spiraling clay
Pronunciation: / kɔɪl / (AmE), / kɔɪl / (BrE)
to wind or twist into a spiral or a series of loops, or to move in a spiral way

Examples

To coil a hose
Many traditional cultures coil large water jars as part of their craftwork.
She learned how to coil clay to create beautiful vases.
The artist prefers to coil her pots by hand instead of using a wheel.
Dark smoke coiled upwards from the chimney after they lit the fire.
Her hair coiled elegantly down her back in loose ringlets.
The snake began to coil itself around the branch for warmth.
He coiled the extension cord and hung it on the wall after finishing the repairs.
She carefully coiled the rope around the post to secure the boat.
The snake coiled itself tightly before striking at its prey.

  • antonym: unwind - to undo or loosen a coiled shape
