unfixity

noun
lack of stability
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfɪksɪti / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪksɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being fixed, stable, or settled (including in technical or general contexts)

Examples

Material unfixity
The unfixity of the photographic image meant it faded quickly when exposed to light.
Scientists studied the unfixity of certain dyes to improve their resistance to washing.
Because of its unfixity, the color on the fabric changed after every cleaning.
The unfixity of the schedule caused confusion among the participants.
Political unfixity can lead to uncertainty in economic planning.
She was frustrated by the unfixity of the rules, which seemed to change every week.
