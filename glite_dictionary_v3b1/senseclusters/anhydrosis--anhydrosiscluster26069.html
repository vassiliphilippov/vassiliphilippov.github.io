anhydrosis

noun
inability to sweat (medical)
Pronunciation: / æn.haɪˈdroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / æn.hɪˈdrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the inability of the sweat glands to produce sweat.

Examples

Anhydrosis condition
Because anhydrosis prevents sweating, it increases the risk of overheating in hot weather.
Inherited forms of anhydrosis can lead to serious health problems if not managed properly.
The patient was diagnosed with anhydrosis after complaining of heat intolerance during exercise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperhidrosis - excessive sweating (medical condition)
  • degree lower: hypohidrosis - reduced ability to sweat (medical condition)
