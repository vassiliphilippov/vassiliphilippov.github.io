pinnated

adjective
botanical: feather-like, divided leaf
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪ.neɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf or similar structure) divided into leaflets or segments arranged on either side of a common axis, resembling a feather.

Examples

Pinnated leaf structure
Many acacia species have pinnated foliage, giving them a delicate, featherlike appearance.
The botanist examined the pinnated structure of the branch to classify the plant.
The fern's pinnated leaves are arranged symmetrically along the central stem.
