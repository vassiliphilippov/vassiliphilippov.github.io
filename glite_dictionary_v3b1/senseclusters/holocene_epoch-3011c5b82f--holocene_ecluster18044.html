Holocene epoch

noun
current geological epoch since last ice age
7.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààho ä.l…ôÀåsin Àà…õp.…ôk / (AmE), / Ààh…íl.…ôÀåsiÀên Àà…õp.…ík / (BrE)
The current geological epoch, beginning around 11,700 years ago after the last major ice age and continuing to the present.

Examples

The Holocene epoch
Many modern species of plants and animals have evolved during the Holocene epoch.
Scientists study ice cores to understand the climate changes during the Holocene epoch.
The Holocene epoch has seen the development of human civilization and agriculture.

Synonyms

  • Holocene
    9.1 0.11/m
    Holocene is the standard single-word name used in science and media, while Holocene epoch is the full formal term used in geology.
  • Recent epoch
    7.3 0.00/m
    Recent epoch is a general, less precise name for the latest geological period, while Holocene epoch is the formal geological term.
