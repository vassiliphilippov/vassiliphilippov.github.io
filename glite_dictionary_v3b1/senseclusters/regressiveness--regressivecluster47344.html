regressiveness

noun
tendency to move backward
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of tending to move backward or become less advanced

Examples

Regressiveness of a policy
The regressiveness of the new tax system was criticized by many economists.
Some argued that the law's regressiveness would harm social progress.
Activists protested against the regressiveness of the proposed education reforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressiveness - the quality of moving forward or becoming more advanced
  • synonym: retrogression - a return to a former or less developed state
