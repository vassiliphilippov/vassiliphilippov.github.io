nude

adjective
having no clothes on
3.7 3.41/m
Pronunciation: / nud / (AmE), / njuÀêd / (BrE)
having no clothes on; naked.

Examples

Completely nude
He ran into the street completely nude after the fire alarm went off.
Many statues in the museum depict the human body nude.
The artist painted a nude figure reclining on the chair.

Synonyms

  • naked
    1.0 33/m
    naked means not wearing any clothes and can sound plain, while nude is often used in art or formal talk.
  • stark naked
    4.6 0.34/m
    stark naked is completely without clothes and sounds stronger or shocking, while nude is more neutral or artistic.
  • clothesless
    8.4 0.01/m
    clothesless simply means not wearing clothes and sounds informal or blunt, while nude often names an artistic or formal state.
  • unclothed
    3.7 0.41/m
    unclothed is a formal word for having no clothes, while nude is commonly used in art or fashion contexts.
  • unclad
    7.5 0.03/m
    unclad feels old or literary for being without clothes, while nude is modern and used in art, fashion, and speech.
  • undressed
    1.9 0.01/m
    undressed often shows someone has removed clothes or is partly without clothes, while nude stresses complete absence of clothing.
  • au naturel
    9.6 0.12/m
    au naturel is a French phrase for natural, unadorned appearance and can mean nude, while nude is plain and direct.

  • antonym: clothed - wearing clothes
  • synonym: naked - having no clothes covering the body
