law

noun
the legal profession
5.5 89/m
Pronunciation: / l…ë / (AmE), / l…îÀê / (BrE)
the profession concerned with the study and practice of legal principles, requiring postgraduate education and responsibility for the judicial system

Examples

Study law
A career in law often requires years of intensive education and training.
After graduating from Harvard, she decided to practice law in New York.
He studied law because he wanted to become a judge one day.

Synonyms

  • legal profession
    3.3 1.38/m
    legal profession includes the licensed people who work as lawyers in a country, while law is the profession and system they follow.
  • bar
    6.3 7.70/m
    bar is the body of qualified lawyers in a region, while law is the profession and system they study and practice.
  • practice of law
    4.9 0.64/m
    practice of law means the actual work lawyers do for clients, while law names the whole profession and its training.
