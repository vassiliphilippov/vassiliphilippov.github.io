jail

verb
to put in prison; confine
2.1 4.63/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’eÉªl / (AmE), / dÊ’eÉªl / (BrE)
to put someone in prison or to confine someone as if in prison

Examples

To jail a criminal
Many activists were jailed after the peaceful protest.
She fears that speaking out could result in her being jailed for dissent.
The judge decided to jail the man for violating his probation.

Synonyms

  • imprison
    4.2 9.33/m
    imprison means to put someone in prison as punishment, while jail is a more common, everyday word for the same action.
  • incarcerate
    8.4 0.68/m
    incarcerate is a formal legal word used in reports and law, while jail is the normal everyday verb people use.
  • put behind bars
    4.3 0.64/m
    put behind bars is an informal idiom that suggests sending someone to prison, while jail is a shorter everyday verb.
  • put away
    5.8 1.85/m
    put away is a casual, often euphemistic phrase for locking someone in prison, while jail is a direct everyday verb.
  • lock up
    4.0 7.01/m
    lock up can mean to secure someone temporarily or keep them safe, while jail specifically means to put someone in prison.
