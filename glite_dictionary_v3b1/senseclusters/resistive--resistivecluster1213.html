resistive

adjective
opposing authority; defiant
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstɪv / (BrE)
showing a tendency to oppose or defy authority or control; resistant or opposed to established rules or leadership

Examples

Resistive student
Her resistive attitude concerned the teachers during the new policy discussion.
Some employees are naturally resistive to changes in management style.
The crowd became increasingly resistive as the police tried to disperse them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliant - willing to obey or conform to authority
  • synonym: defiant - openly resisting or challenging authority
