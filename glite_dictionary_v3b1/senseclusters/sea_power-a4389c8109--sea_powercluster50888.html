sea power

noun
nation with formidable naval strength
Pronunciation: / siː ˈpaʊər /; / siː ˈpaʊ.ər / (AmE), / siː ˈpaʊə /; / siː ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the strength of a country's navy, or a country that has a powerful navy

Examples

A sea power nation
Britain was considered the leading sea power of the nineteenth century.
Control of the oceans often determines which sea power prevails in times of conflict.
Emerging as a major sea power, the nation expanded its influence overseas.
Britain's dominance during the 19th century was largely due to its unrivaled sea power.
Recent tensions have led to debates about increasing our sea power.
The nation invested heavily in its sea power to protect its overseas interests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: land power - a country with formidable army forces rather than navy
  • synonym: naval power - a country with strong military naval forces
