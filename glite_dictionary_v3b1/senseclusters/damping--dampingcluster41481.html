damping

noun
reduction in intensity
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdæm.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdæm.pɪŋ/ (BrE)
a reduction in the intensity, strength, or activity of something, such as enthusiasm, noise, or effect

Examples

Damping of noise
The engineer adjusted the damping to reduce vibrations in the machine.
There was a clear damping of enthusiasm after the disappointing announcement.
Proper damping is essential to prevent loud echoes in the concert hall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplification - an increase in the strength or intensity of something
  • synonym: diminishing - the act of making something smaller or less
