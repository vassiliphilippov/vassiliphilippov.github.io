mutableness

noun
quality of being able to change
Pronunciation: / mjuˈteɪblnəs / (AmE), / mjuˈtɛːblnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being capable of change or mutation

Examples

The mutableness of nature
Many philosophers have debated the mutableness of human nature throughout history.
The mutableness of fashion trends makes it difficult to keep up with current styles.
The mutableness of species is a central idea in evolutionary theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable
  • synonym: mutability - the quality of being changeable or able to be changed
