law of parsimony

noun
principle favoring simplest explanation
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ˈpɑrsəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈpɑːsɪməni / (BrE)
The principle that the simplest explanation or theory, with the fewest assumptions, should be preferred when comparing competing hypotheses.

Examples

The law of parsimony principle
In evaluating the data, the law of parsimony suggests we should avoid unnecessary complexity.
Scientists often invoke the law of parsimony when choosing between competing models.
The law of parsimony is a cornerstone in modern scientific reasoning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Occam's razor - the principle favoring the simplest theory
