decolonize

verb
grant independence to a former colony
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkɑlɪˌnaɪz /; / dɪˈkɑː.ləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diˈkɒlɪnaɪz /; / dɪˈkɒl.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
To grant independence to a country or region that was a colony.

Examples

To decolonize a country
Movements to decolonize territories gained momentum after World War II.
Several African nations were decolonized in the twentieth century.
The government worked with international organizations to decolonize the island and transfer power to local leaders.
Historians have studied the movement to decolonise India in great depth.
Many African countries were decolonised in the twentieth century after years of struggle.
The process to decolonise territories often involved complex negotiations with colonial powers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colonize - to establish control over a foreign territory and settle it with people from the colonizing country
