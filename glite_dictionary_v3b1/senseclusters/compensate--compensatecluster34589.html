compensate

verb
pay money or make amends
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːm.pɛnˌseɪt /; / ˈkɑːm.pən.seɪt /; / ˈkɑmpənˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pən.seɪt /; / ˈkɒmpənseɪt / (BrE)
to pay or give something to someone to make up for loss, injury, suffering, or work done

Examples

To compensate a worker
Employers are legally required to compensate workers who are injured on the job.
She was compensated for the damage caused to her car.
The company agreed to compensate him for the overtime hours he worked.
She tried to compensate him for his hard work with a generous bonus.
The company will compensate you for any travel expenses incurred.
Volunteers are not usually compensated for their time.
After the error was discovered, the airline agreed to compensate passengers with travel vouchers.
The company was ordered to compensate the victims for the environmental damage it caused.
The government passed a law to compensate those who lost property during the conflict.
