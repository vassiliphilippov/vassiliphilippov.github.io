alfilaria

noun
a weed with reddish creeping stems and fernlike leaves
Pronunciation: / ˌælfɪˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌælfɪˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A European weed, naturalized in the southwestern United States and Mexico, with reddish creeping stems, fernlike leaves, and small deep reddish-lavender flowers, often grown for forage.

Examples

The alfilaria plant
Cattle often graze on alfilaria because it provides good forage in dry areas.
She studied the spread of alfilaria across the southwestern United States.
The fields were covered with patches of alfilaria after the spring rains.
