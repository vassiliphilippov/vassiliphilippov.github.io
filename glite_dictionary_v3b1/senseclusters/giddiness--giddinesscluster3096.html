giddiness

noun
impulsive, scatterbrained behavior
6.9 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪdi nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪdɪ nɪs / (BrE)
a tendency to behave in an impulsive or scatterbrained manner, often as a result of excitement or enthusiasm

Examples

Childish giddiness
Her giddiness made it hard for her to focus on the serious discussion.
His giddiness sometimes led him to make rash decisions without thinking.
There was a sense of giddiness in the room after hearing the exciting news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flightiness - tendency to be irresponsible or frivolous
  • antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious, not impulsive or scatterbrained
