Haematobia irritans

noun
species of bloodsucking fly affecting cattle
Pronunciation: / hiːməˈtoʊbiə ɪˈrɪtænz / (AmE), / hiːməˈtəʊbɪə ɪˈrɪtanz / (BrE)
A small black European fly, commonly known as the horn fly, that was introduced to North America and feeds on the blood of cattle, especially around the base of the horns.

Examples

Haematobia irritans fly
Haematobia irritans infestations can lead to significant weight loss in cattle herds.
Reducing the population of Haematobia irritans is a key concern for livestock farmers during the summer months.
Veterinarians recommend monitoring for Haematobia irritans as part of herd health management.
