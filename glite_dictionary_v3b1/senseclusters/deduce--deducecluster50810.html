deduce

verb
reach a conclusion by reasoning
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdus / (AmE), / dɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to reach a conclusion by reasoning from the available facts or evidence

Examples

To deduce a fact
By examining the evidence, scientists deduced the cause of the phenomenon.
From the clues, the detective was able to deduce who committed the crime.
We can deduce from her accent that she grew up in Scotland.
Based on the evidence, the detective managed to deduce who the culprit was.
Scientists can deduce the age of a fossil through carbon dating.
She tried to deduce the meaning of the word from the context.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: guess - to estimate or suppose without strong evidence
  • antonym: induce - To derive a general principle from specific instances
  • synonym: infer - to reach a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning
