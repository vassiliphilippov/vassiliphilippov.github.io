underdo

verb
to not do enough
6.8
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈduː/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈduː/ (BrE)
to cook, heat, or perform something for too short a time, especially so it is not fully done or finished

Examples

To underdo the cooking
If you underdo the chicken, it will be unsafe to eat.
Be careful not to underdo your workout, or you may not see results.
She tends to underdo her makeup for formal events, preferring a very natural look.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overdo - to do something to an excessive degree
  • synonym: undercook - to cook food for too short a time so it is not fully cooked
