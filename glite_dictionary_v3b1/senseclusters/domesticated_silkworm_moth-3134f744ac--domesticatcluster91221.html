domesticated silkworm moth

noun
moth that produces commercial silk
6.3 8.1
Pronunciation: / dəˌmɛstɪˌkeɪtɪd ˈsɪlkwɜrm mɑθ / (AmE), / dəˌmɛstɪˌkeɪtɪd ˈsɪlkwɜːm mɒθ / (BrE)
A creamy-white Asian moth (Bombyx mori) bred in captivity, whose larvae are the primary source of commercial silk.

Examples

The domesticated silkworm moth larvae
After pupating, the domesticated silkworm moth emerges but usually cannot fly due to generations of selective breeding.
China has a long tradition of breeding the domesticated silkworm moth for silk production.
The domesticated silkworm moth is no longer found in the wild and relies entirely on humans to survive.
