educated

adjective
having received an education
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdʒəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛdjʊkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having received a good education; possessing a high level of knowledge, especially more than average.

Examples

Educated person
He comes from a highly educated family with a tradition in academia.
Many educated people support lifelong learning and skill development.
She is an educated woman who speaks four languages fluently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: learned - having much knowledge acquired by study
  • antonym: uneducated - lacking an education or schooling
