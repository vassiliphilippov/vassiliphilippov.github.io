cattalo

noun
hybrid of cattle and American buffalo
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəloʊ / (AmE), / ˈkatələʊ / (BrE)
A hardy hybrid animal produced by crossing domestic cattle with the American buffalo, valued for its leaner beef.

Examples

The cattalo beef
Beef from cattalo is becoming popular among consumers seeking leaner meat options.
Researchers studied how cattalo adapt compared to traditional cattle breeds.
The rancher decided to raise cattalo for their disease resistance and ability to thrive in harsh climates.
