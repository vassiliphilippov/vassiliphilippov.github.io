waterfinder

noun
tool used to locate underground water
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
A forked stick or similar tool used in dowsing, believed to indicate the presence of underground water or oil by dipping downward.

Examples

A waterfinder stick
Before drilling, they hired a dowser who brought a traditional waterfinder to survey the land.
Some people believe that a waterfinder can locate underground water sources with remarkable accuracy.
The farmer used a waterfinder to search for a new well site on his property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dowsing rod - a rod or stick used in dowsing to locate underground water, metal, etc.
