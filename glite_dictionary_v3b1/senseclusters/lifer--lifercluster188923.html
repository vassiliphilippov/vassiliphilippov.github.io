lifer

noun
long-term member
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪfər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfə / (BrE)
a person who remains in a job, place, or institution for a very long time, often for their entire career or life

Examples

Company lifer
After thirty years at the same school, she was known as a lifer among the staff.
Many people admired the lifer who dedicated his entire career to the company.
He never considered leaving; he was a true lifer in the organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: newcomer - a person who has recently joined a place or organization
  • synonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field
