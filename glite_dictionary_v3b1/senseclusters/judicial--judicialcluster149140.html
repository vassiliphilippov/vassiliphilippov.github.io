judicial

adjective
careful and impartial in judging
Pronunciation: / dʒuˈdɪʃəl / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl / (BrE)
showing or characterized by careful and impartial judgment, as if by a judge.

Examples

Judicial manner
He listened with a judicial calmness before giving his opinion.
Her judicial approach to the debate helped the team find a fair solution.
The judicial tone of the review added credibility to her criticism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something
  • synonym: judicious - having or showing good judgment or sense
