corpuscular theory of light

noun
theory that light consists of particles
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔr.pəˈskju.lɚ ˈθɪə.ri əv laɪt / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.pəˈskjuː.lə ˈθɪə.ri ɒv laɪt / (BrE)
the theory that light is transmitted as a stream of particles rather than as a wave

Examples

The corpuscular theory of light
Newton initially supported the corpuscular theory of light over the wave theory.
Some early physicists believed the corpuscular theory of light could explain reflection and refraction.
The corpuscular theory of light was eventually challenged by new discoveries in optics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: wave theory of light - the theory that light is transmitted as waves
