cranny

noun
small narrow opening or crack
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkræni / (AmE), / ˈkræni / (BrE)
a small, narrow opening or crack, especially in a wall, rock, or surface

Examples

A cranny in wall
Dust had settled into every cranny of the wooden floor.
She hid the key in a cranny behind the bookshelf.
Tiny plants were growing in every cranny of the old stone wall.
He searched every cranny of the attic for the missing letter.
She hid the key in a cranny in the old stone wall.
Tiny plants grew in every cranny between the bricks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chink - a narrow opening, such as a crack or slit
  • synonym: crevice - a narrow opening or fissure, especially in a rock or wall
  • synonym: gap - a break or hole in an object or between two objects
