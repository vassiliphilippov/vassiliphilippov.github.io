conacre

noun
seasonal rented land
Pronunciation: / kənˈeɪkər /; / kəˈneɪkər / (AmE), / kənˈeɪkə /; / kəˈneɪkə / (BrE)
a small plot of land, or the system of renting land, for a single season to grow crops

Examples

A conacre plot

The conacre system allowed tenants to rent land just for the summer.
The farmer secured a conacre to plant potatoes for the season.
Many smallholders relied on conacre arrangements to grow their crops each year.
After losing his own land, he depended on conacre to continue farming.
