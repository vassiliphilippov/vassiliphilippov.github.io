smolder

verb
burn slowly and without a flame
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsmoʊldɚ /; / ˈsmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
to burn slowly with smoke but without a flame

Examples

To smolder in the fireplace
An old log in the fireplace was still smoldering this morning.
The campfire continued to smolder long after the campers left.
Wet leaves often smolder for hours before finally extinguishing.
A pile of leaves smouldered in the corner of the garden, releasing a steady stream of smoke.
The campfire was left to smoulder overnight, filling the air with the scent of burning wood.
The logs continued to smoulder long after the flames had died down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn - to be on fire
  • antonym: extinguish - to put out a fire or light
  • degree higher: ignite - to set on fire; to start burning
  • synonym: smolder - American English spelling of 'smoulder'
