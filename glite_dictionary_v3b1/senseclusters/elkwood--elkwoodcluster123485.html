elkwood

noun
small deciduous tree of eastern North America
Pronunciation: / ˈɛlkˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɛlkˌwʊd / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its creamy white flowers and large, umbrella-like leaves at branch ends.

Examples

The elkwood tree
Many animals find shelter beneath the broad leaves of the elkwood.
The elkwood blooms in late spring, covering its branches with clusters of creamy white flowers.
We identified the tree as an elkwood by its unique umbrella-shaped leaves.
