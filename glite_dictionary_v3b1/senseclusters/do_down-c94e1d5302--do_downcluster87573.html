do down

verb
to belittle or criticize someone
8.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /duː daʊn/ (AmE), /duː daʊn/ (BrE)
to criticize or belittle someone, often unfairly; to speak negatively about someone or something so as to reduce their value or reputation

Examples

To do down a person
Despite all the praise, some critics tried doing down the artist's latest work.
It's not fair to do down your coworkers just to make yourself look good.
The media often does down the team's achievements when things go wrong.
