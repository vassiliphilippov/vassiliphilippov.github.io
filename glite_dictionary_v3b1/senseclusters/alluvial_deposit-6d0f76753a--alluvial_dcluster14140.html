alluvial deposit

noun
sediment left by flowing water
Pronunciation: / əˈluː.vi.əl dɪˈpɑː.zɪt / (AmE), / əˈluː.vi.əl dɪˈpɒz.ɪt / (BrE)
Material such as clay, silt, sand, or gravel carried by running water and deposited when the flow slows, typically in riverbeds or floodplains.

Examples

Alluvial deposit in river
Centuries of alluvial deposit have shaped the delta region.
Engineers studied the composition of the alluvial deposit before beginning construction.
The river's alluvial deposit created a fertile plain perfect for agriculture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sediment - solid material that settles to the bottom of a liquid
