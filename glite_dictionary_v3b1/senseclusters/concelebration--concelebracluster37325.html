concelebration

noun
joint religious ceremony
9.0
Pronunciation: /kən.se.lə.ˈbreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kɑːn.se.lə.ˈbreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒn.se.lə.ˈbreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.se.lə.ˈbreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a religious ceremony, especially a Mass, performed together by two or more officiating clergy

Examples

Easter concelebration
The concelebration of the Easter Mass brought together priests from several parishes.
During the concelebration, each officiant played a special role in the ceremony.
The bishop invited local clergy to join him in the concelebration of the service.
