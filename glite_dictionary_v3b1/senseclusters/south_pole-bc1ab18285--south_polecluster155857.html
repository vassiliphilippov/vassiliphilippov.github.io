South Pole

noun
the southernmost point on Earth
Pronunciation: / saʊθ poʊl / (AmE), / saʊθ pəʊl / (BrE)
the southernmost point on the Earth's surface, situated at the axis of the planet's rotation

Examples

The South Pole station
Scientists study climate change at research stations near the South Pole.
Temperatures at the South Pole can drop to minus 60 degrees Celsius during winter.
The first expedition to the South Pole was led by Roald Amundsen in 1911.
