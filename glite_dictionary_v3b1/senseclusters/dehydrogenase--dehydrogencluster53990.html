dehydrogenase

noun
hydrogen-removing enzyme
Pronunciation: / diˌhaɪˈdrɑdʒəˌneɪs / (AmE), / diˌhaɪˈdrɒdʒəˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that removes hydrogen atoms from other molecules during chemical reactions

Examples

Alcohol dehydrogenase
Alcohol dehydrogenase is important in the breakdown of alcohol in the liver.
Scientists studied the activity of dehydrogenase in different metabolic pathways.
A deficiency in a certain dehydrogenase can lead to metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxidase - an enzyme that catalyzes oxidation reactions
