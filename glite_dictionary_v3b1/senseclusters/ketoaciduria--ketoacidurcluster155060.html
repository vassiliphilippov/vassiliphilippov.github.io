ketoaciduria

noun
excess ketone bodies in urine
9.9
Pronunciation: /kiː.toʊ.ə.ˈsɪd.jʊr.iː.ə/ /; / /kiː.toʊ.ə.ˈsɪd.juːr.iː.ə/ (AmE), /kiː.təʊ.ə.ˈsɪd.jʊə.riː.ə/ /; / /kiː.təʊ.ə.ˈsɪd.juː.riː.ə/ (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an excessive amount of ketone bodies in the urine, often seen in diabetes mellitus or starvation.

Examples

Ketoaciduria in urine

Doctors monitor ketoaciduria to assess the severity of certain metabolic disorders.
The presence of ketoaciduria is an important indicator in diagnosing diabetic complications.
