Alpha Crucis

noun
brightest star in the Southern Cross
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌælfə ˈkruːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌælfə ˈkruːsɪs / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Crux (the Southern Cross)

Examples

Bright Alpha Crucis
Many stargazers can easily spot Alpha Crucis due to its brightness at the foot of Crux.
Sailors have long used Alpha Crucis for navigation in the southern oceans.
The Southern Hemisphere sky is dominated by Alpha Crucis, marking the base of the Southern Cross.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Crux - the constellation also known as the Southern Cross
