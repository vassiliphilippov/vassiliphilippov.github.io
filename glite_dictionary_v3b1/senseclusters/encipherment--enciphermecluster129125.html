encipherment

noun
coding information
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsaɪfərmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪfəmənt / (BrE)
the process of converting information or a message into a coded form to prevent unauthorized access

Examples

The encipherment process
The encipherment of sensitive data is a key part of cybersecurity.
Modern banking systems rely on encipherment to protect customer information.
Without proper encipherment, private messages could be easily intercepted.
The encipherment of the document ensured that only authorized personnel could read its contents.
After the encipherment, the message appeared as a string of random letters and numbers.
Modern technology has made encipherment more secure and difficult to break.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decipherment - the act of converting coded information back into its original form
  • synonym: encryption - the act of converting data into a secret code
