Lancastrian

noun
supporter of Lancaster house (historical)
Pronunciation: / lænˈkæstriən / (AmE), / laŋˈkæstriən / (BrE)
a member or supporter of the house of Lancaster, especially during the Wars of the Roses in England

Examples

Lancastrian supporter
During the Wars of the Roses, every Lancastrian fought fiercely to restore their house to the throne.
He identified as a proud Lancastrian and believed in the cause of Henry VI.
The castle was a stronghold for Lancastrian forces in the fifteenth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Yorkist - a member or supporter of the house of York
