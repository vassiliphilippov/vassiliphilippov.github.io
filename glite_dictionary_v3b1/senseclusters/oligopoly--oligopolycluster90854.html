oligopoly

noun
market dominated by a few producers
8.6 7.7
Pronunciation: /ɑː.lɪ.ˈgɑː.pə.liː/ /; / /ə.lɪ.ˈgɑː.pə.liː/ (AmE), /ɒ.lɪ.ˈgɒp.ə.liː/ /; / /ɒ.lɪ.ˈgɒp.liː/ (BrE)
(Economics) a market situation in which a small number of producers or sellers control the supply of a commodity, allowing each to influence prices and affect competitors.

Examples

An oligopoly market
Government regulations can affect the behavior of companies within an oligopoly.
The telecommunications industry in many countries operates as an oligopoly, with just a few firms dominating the market.
When airlines form an oligopoly, they are able to set similar ticket prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: competition - a market with many sellers and buyers; opposite of monopoly or oligopoly
  • degree lower: duopoly - a market dominated by two producers or sellers
  • degree lower: monopoly - a market with only one producer or seller
