patronus

noun
Roman protector
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈtroʊnəs / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊnəs / (BrE)
a patron or protector, especially a man who supported and defended clients in ancient Rome

Examples

Roman patronus
In ancient Rome, a patronus was responsible for the welfare of his clients.
The freedman showed loyalty to his patronus by supporting him in public matters.
A patronus often provided legal and financial help to those under his protection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: client - a person under the protection of a patron in ancient Rome
  • synonym: patron - a person who gives support or protection
