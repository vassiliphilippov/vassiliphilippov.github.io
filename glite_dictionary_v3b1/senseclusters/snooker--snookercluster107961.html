snooker

verb
to deceive or trick (informal)
8.8 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsn äk…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàsnuÀêk…ô / (BrE)
to trick, deceive, or dupe someone, especially so they are put at a disadvantage or prevented from succeeding

Examples

To snooker someone
I can't believe I was snookered into buying such an expensive ticket.
She snookered her rivals by leaking false information about the project.
The team felt snookered by the referee's unexpected call.

Synonyms

  • trick
    3.5 9.77/m
    trick means deceiving someone by clever or dishonest means to gain advantage, while snooker means tricking someone and stopping their success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick someone, often so that they do something for you
  • synonym: fool - to trick or deceive someone
