barye

noun
unit of pressure in cgs system
Pronunciation: / ˈbæri / (AmE), / ˈbæri / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to one dyne per square centimeter, formerly used in the cgs system

Examples

One barye unit
One barye is a very small unit compared to the pascal, the standard unit of pressure today.
Scientists no longer use the barye in most modern research, preferring SI units instead.
The pressure was measured at 500 baryes in the experiment.
