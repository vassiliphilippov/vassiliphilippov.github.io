hinge

verb
attach with a hinge
3.9 2.6
Pronunciation: / hɪndʒ / (AmE), / hɪndʒ / (BrE)
to attach, join, or allow movement with a hinge

Examples

To hinge a door
The cabinet doors were carefully hinged to ensure they swung open smoothly.
We watched the carpenter hinging each panel onto the folding screen.
You need to hinge the gate so that it will open and close properly.
The heavy gate will hinge open smoothly if you oil it regularly.
Some windows are designed to hinge outward for easy cleaning.
The lid can hinge back to reveal a hidden compartment inside the box.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swing - to move back and forth or from side to side while suspended or as if suspended
  • antonym: unhinge - to remove from hinges or make unstable
