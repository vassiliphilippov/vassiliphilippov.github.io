conserver

noun
resource saver
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɜːrvər / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvə / (BrE)
a person or device that saves or protects resources or things from waste, loss, or harm

Examples

An energy conserver
As a dedicated conserver of energy, she always turns off lights when leaving a room.
Modern appliances are designed to be effective conservers of water and electricity.
The school recognized him as a leading conserver for his efforts to reduce waste.
As a dedicated conserver of wildlife, she volunteers at the local animal sanctuary.
A true conserver always looks for ways to reduce energy use at home.
The organization recognized him as a leading conserver of historical buildings in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: destroyer - a person who causes damage or ruin
  • synonym: preserver - a person or thing that keeps something in its original state
  • synonym: protector - a person who defends or guards something from harm
  • antonym: spender - a person who uses resources freely or wastefully
