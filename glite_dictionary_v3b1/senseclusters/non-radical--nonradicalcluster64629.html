non-radical

adjective
not a radical species
Pronunciation: / nɑn ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒn ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
in chemistry, not having an unpaired electron and not behaving as a radical species

Examples

Non-radical molecule
A non-radical compound is generally more stable than a radical compound.
Scientists studied the reaction rates of non-radical molecules under different conditions.
The antioxidant acts as a non-radical species, preventing chain reactions in the cell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radical - a molecule or atom with an unpaired electron
