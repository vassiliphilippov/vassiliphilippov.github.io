interfacial surface tension

noun
surface tension between two liquids
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈfeɪʃəl ˈsɝːfɪs ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfeɪʃl ˈsɜːfɪs ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
the surface tension at the boundary between two non-miscible liquids

Examples

Interfacial surface tension between liquids
Adding a surfactant can reduce the interfacial surface tension and promote mixing of the liquids.
Researchers measured the interfacial surface tension between oil and water under various conditions.
The stability of an emulsion is influenced by its interfacial surface tension.
