Julius Caesar

proper noun
Roman general and dictator (100–44 BC)
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːliəs ˈsiːzər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːliəs ˈsiːzə / (BrE)
(100–44 BC) Roman general, statesman, and dictator, known for his conquest of Gaul and pivotal role in the fall of the Roman Republic.

Examples

Julius Caesar the general
