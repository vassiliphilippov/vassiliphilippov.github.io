carinate

adjective
having a ridge like a keel
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrɪneɪt / (BrE)
having a ridge or shaped like a keel; resembling the keel of a ship, especially used to describe anatomical or botanical structures

Examples

Carinate sternum
Some bird species are distinguished by having a carinate sternum.
The leaf margins are carinate, forming a distinct ridge along the edge.
The orchid's carinate sepal helps identify its species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keeled - having a ridge or projecting line like a keel
