throw stones

verb
criticize others while flawed oneself
Pronunciation: / θroʊ stoʊnz / (AmE), / θrəʊ stəʊnz / (BrE)
to criticize others for faults while ignoring one's own flaws or shortcomings

Examples

To throw stones at others
Before you throw stones, remember your own errors.
He shouldn't throw stones, considering his own mistakes.
It's easy to throw stones when you're not involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cast the first stone - to be the first to criticize or condemn someone
  • synonym: people who live in glass houses shouldn't throw stones - idiom warning against criticizing others when oneself is vulnerable
