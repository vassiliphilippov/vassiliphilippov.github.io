U.S. waters

noun
sea area under U.S. jurisdiction
2.7
Pronunciation: / juː ˈɛs ˈwɑːtərz / (AmE), / juː ˈɛs ˈwɔːtəz / (BrE)
the area of sea extending 12 nautical miles from the coasts of the United States and its territories, subject to U.S. jurisdiction

Examples

U.S. waters jurisdiction
All foreign vessels entering U.S. waters are required to report to the Coast Guard.
Fishing in U.S. waters is regulated to protect marine life and local industries.
The environmental regulations are stricter within U.S. waters than in international areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: territorial waters - the waters adjacent to a state's coast, under its jurisdiction
