underly

verb
form the basis; be the cause of
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈlaɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to form the basis or foundation of something; to be the cause or underlying reason for a change or situation

Examples

Changes underly reasons
Changes in legislation frequently underly the transformation of entire industries.
Deep cultural factors often underly social shifts and movements.
Recent scientific discoveries underly our new understanding of the disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cause - to make something happen; to be the reason for
  • synonym: underpin - to support or form the basis for something
