bad temper

noun
persistent tendency to anger
Pronunciation: / bæd ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / bad ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
a tendency to become angry easily or remain in an irritable mood

Examples

A bad temper
Despite his bad temper, he is respected for his honesty.
His bad temper often causes arguments within the team.
She is known for her bad temper, especially when things don't go her way.

Related Terms

  • antonym: good temper - a consistently calm or cheerful mood
  • synonym: irascibility - the quality of being easily angered
