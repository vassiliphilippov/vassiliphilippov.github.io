simulate

verb
imitate appearance or behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to imitate the appearance or behavior of someone or something, often to make it seem real or genuine

Examples

To simulate a voice
Some children like to simulate their teachers’ voices when playing school at home.
The actor was able to simulate the famous politician’s gestures perfectly on stage.
With lots of practice, you can simulate a convincing British accent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - make a reproduction of something; do as someone else does
  • synonym: imitate - copy the behavior or appearance of someone or something
  • synonym: mimic - imitate someone, especially to entertain or ridicule
