hyphenism

noun
hyphenated identity labels
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪfənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪfənɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of describing or identifying people using hyphenated national, ethnic, or cultural labels, such as 'Mexican-American'

Examples

Cultural hyphenism
The debate about hyphenism reflects changing attitudes toward multicultural identities in society.
Some people reject hyphenism because they feel it divides rather than unites communities.
The rise of hyphenism in the media highlights the complexity of modern cultural identities.

Related Terms
