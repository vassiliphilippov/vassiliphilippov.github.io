on the off chance

adverb
with a slight possibility
8.4 6.7
Pronunciation: / ɑn ði ɔf tʃæns / (AmE), / ɒn ði ɒf tʃɑːns / (BrE)
If you do something on the off chance, you do it because there is a slight possibility that it will succeed or that something will happen.

Examples

Do on the off chance
He emailed the professor on the off chance there was still space in the course.
I took an umbrella on the off chance it might rain later.
She stopped by the office on the off chance she would catch her manager before he left.
