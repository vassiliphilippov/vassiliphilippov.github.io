shuttlepod

noun
small spacecraft
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʌtəlˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtəlˌpɒd / (BrE)
a small spacecraft or capsule used for short trips, such as moving people or cargo between a larger ship and a planet, station, or for emergency escape

Examples

The shuttlepod capsule
The crew entered the shuttlepod to begin their descent to the planet.
After docking with the station, the shuttlepod was quickly refueled for its next trip.
A malfunction in the shuttlepod delayed the team's return to the main ship.
The automated shuttlepod delivered supplies from the storage area to the assembly line every hour.
Engineers programmed the shuttlepod to navigate safely around obstacles in the warehouse.
A fleet of shuttlepods efficiently moved equipment between different sections of the facility.
The crew quickly boarded the shuttlepod when the alarm signaled a critical system failure.
In case of emergency, each astronaut is trained to operate the shuttlepod for rapid evacuation.
The damaged station was abandoned after everyone escaped in a shuttlepod.
