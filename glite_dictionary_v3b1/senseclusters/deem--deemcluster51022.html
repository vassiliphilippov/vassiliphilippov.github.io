deem

verb
to consider or judge
4.6 21/m
Pronunciation: / diÀêm / (AmE), / diÀêm / (BrE)
to consider, judge, or regard something in a particular way

Examples

To deem it necessary
She deemed it necessary to apologize for the mistake.
The law deems him innocent until proven guilty.
They deem these changes essential for the company's growth.
His actions were deemed inappropriate by the principal.
Many parents deem it important to read to their children every night.
The committee deemed the plan unacceptable and asked for revisions.

Synonyms

  • consider
    1.8 164/m
    consider is the everyday verb people use when thinking something through, while deem is more formal and used in writing.
  • regard as
    3.3 21/m
    regard as is a phrase used to label something clearly, while deem is more formal and often appears in official statements.
  • view
    1.8 54/m
    view often means to think about something from a certain angle, while deem sounds more formal and official.
  • view as
    3.1 33/m
    view as is used to describe how people see someone in context, while deem is used more in formal writing.
  • hold
    1.1 42/m
    hold expresses a lasting belief or opinion someone keeps, while deem is a formal judgment often used by institutions.
  • judge
    2.5 48/m
    judge means to form a decision after weighing facts and evidence, while deem mainly means to consider or regard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consider - to think about something carefully, especially in making decisions
  • synonym: judge - to form an opinion or conclusion about
  • synonym: regard - to think of someone or something in a particular way
