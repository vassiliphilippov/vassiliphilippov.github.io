reanalysis

noun
new analysis
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the act of analyzing something again or in a new way to gain better understanding or correct errors

Examples

A reanalysis of results
After discovering an error, the scientist performed a reanalysis of the experiment's data.
The weather forecast improved after a reanalysis of satellite information.
The team requested a reanalysis to ensure the results were accurate and reliable.
After discovering a mistake, the team conducted a reanalysis of the experiment's results.
The reanalysis of climate data led to new conclusions about global warming trends.
A reanalysis can sometimes reveal patterns that were missed in the original study.
