commitment

noun
official consignment to institution or prison
3.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
the official act of sending someone to prison or to a mental hospital, especially by a court order

Examples

Court commitment
After a brief hearing, the court ruled for his commitment to prison.
Her lawyer appealed the commitment, claiming the confinement was unjustified.
The judge ordered the commitment of the defendant to the state mental hospital.
