cropland

noun
land for crops
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑːpˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒpˌlænd / (BrE)
land that is used for growing crops such as grains, vegetables, or fruits

Examples

Fertile cropland
The government provided subsidies to help farmers maintain their cropland during the drought.
Much of the region's cropland is dedicated to growing corn and soybeans.
After years of neglect, the old cropland was finally restored and planted again.
The region's rich cropland produces large amounts of wheat every year.
Farmers are concerned about the effects of drought on their cropland.
Much of the cropland was flooded after the heavy rains last spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farmland - land used for farming, including crops and livestock
  • antonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals
