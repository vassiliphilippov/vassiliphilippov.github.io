single-furrow plow

noun
plow with one furrow blade
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈfɜroʊ plaʊ /; / ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈfɜːroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈfʌrəʊ plaʊ /; / ˈsɪŋɡl ˈfʌrə plʌʊ / (BrE)
a plow designed with a single blade or moldboard for cutting one furrow at a time or turning over the soil, typically operated by one animal or person and guided by handles at the rear, sometimes supported by a front wheel.

Examples

A single-furrow plough is ideal for small plots and lighter soils.
The farmer used a single-furrow plough to prepare the field for planting.
Traditionally, a single-furrow plough would be pulled by one horse to turn the earth.

  • synonym: plow - a farm tool used for cutting, lifting, and turning over soil
