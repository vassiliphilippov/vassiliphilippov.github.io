Ghibelline

adjective
swallow-tailed battlements
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪbəliːn / (AmE), / ˈɡɪbəliːn / (BrE)
describing a style of battlement with swallow-tailed (V-shaped) merlons, historically linked to the Ghibelline faction in medieval Italy

Examples

Ghibelline battlements
The castle's Ghibelline battlements made it stand out among other medieval fortresses.
Architects restored the tower with its original Ghibelline merlons to preserve its historical character.
You can identify a Ghibelline wall by the distinctive V-shaped notches along the top.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Guelf - relating to the rival medieval Italian faction, often with square-topped battlements
