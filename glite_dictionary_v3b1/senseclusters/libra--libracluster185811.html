libra

noun
Roman weight unit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈliːbrə / (AmE), / ˈliːbrə / (BrE)
an ancient Roman unit of weight, approximately equal to a modern pound

Examples

Ancient libra weight
In ancient Rome, a libra was used to measure goods in the marketplace.
The recipe called for one libra of flour, which is about a pound.
Historians often convert the value of a libra to modern units for clarity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kilogram - the base unit of mass in the metric system
  • synonym: pound - a unit of weight equal to 16 ounces
