frothy

adjective
full of bubbles or foam
3.2 0.80/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfr…ëÀêŒ∏i / (AmE), / Ààfr…íŒ∏i / (BrE)
(of a liquid) containing many small bubbles; emitting or covered with foam or froth as from carbonation or fermentation.

Examples

Frothy coffee
After shaking the bottle, the soda became extremely frothy when opened.
She enjoyed a frothy cappuccino while reading her morning paper.
The waiter poured a glass of frothy beer for each guest at the table.

Synonyms

  • foamy
    2.2 0.49/m
    foamy describes a liquid covered in thick, soft foam, while frothy usually points to many small bubbles from fermentation or agitation.
  • spumy
    9.9 0.01/m
    spumy means full of rough foam and bubbles, while frothy usually refers to a light foam on drinks from carbonation.
  • bubbly
    3.2 1.48/m
    bubbly describes a liquid with many visible bubbles, often from carbonation, while frothy stresses foam or froth on top.
  • fizzy
    1.6 0.48/m
    fizzy describes a drink with lively gas bubbles and sparkle, while frothy focuses more on a layer of foam.
  • effervescent
    7.3 0.12/m
    effervescent is a formal word for a sparkling, bubbling liquid in writing, while frothy highlights foam or froth on the surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubbly - full of bubbles; effervescent
  • synonym: foamy - full of or covered with foam
