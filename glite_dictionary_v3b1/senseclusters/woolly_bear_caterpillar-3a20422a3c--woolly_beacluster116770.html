woolly bear caterpillar

noun
bristly, hairy caterpillar of various moths
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli bɛr ˈkæt̬əˌpɪlər / (AmE), / ˈwʊli beə ˈkatəpɪlə / (BrE)
a caterpillar of several moth species, distinguished by a dense covering of bristly hairs; some species feed on plants and can be harmful to crops

Examples

A woolly bear caterpillar larva
A woolly bear caterpillar eventually transforms into the Isabella tiger moth.
Some gardeners worry when they see woolly bear caterpillars eating their plants, but most species cause little damage.
The woolly bear caterpillar is often seen crossing roads in autumn, searching for a place to hibernate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caterpillar - larval stage of butterflies and moths
