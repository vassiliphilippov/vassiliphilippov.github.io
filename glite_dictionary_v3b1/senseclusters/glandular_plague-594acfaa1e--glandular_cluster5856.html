glandular plague

noun
form of plague with buboes, not person-to-person
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæn.dʒələr pleɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡlæn.djʊ.lə pleɪɡ / (BrE)
the most common form of plague in humans, marked by chills, prostration, delirium, and the appearance of buboes in the armpits and groin; not transmitted from person to person

Examples

Painful glandular plague
Early symptoms of glandular plague include fever and painfully swollen lymph nodes.
The glandular plague is usually contracted after being bitten by an infected flea.
Treatment of glandular plague with antibiotics greatly improves the chances of recovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubonic plague - form of plague characterized by buboes, commonly spread by fleas
