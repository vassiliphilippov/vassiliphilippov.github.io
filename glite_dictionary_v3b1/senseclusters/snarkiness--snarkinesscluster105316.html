snarkiness

noun
sarcastic attitude

the quality of being sarcastic, mocking, or sharply critical in tone or manner

Examples

Snarkiness in comments
His snarkiness often made people laugh, but sometimes it hurt their feelings.

Synonyms

  • snideness
    8.1 0.05/m
    snideness is quietly sneering and mean in speech, while snarkiness is more playful, sarcastic, and sharply critical.
  • sarcasm
    3.9 5.54/m
    sarcasm uses ironic words to mock or insult, while snarkiness adds slyness and a sharper, often playful bite.
  • sardonicism
    9.3 0.01/m
    sardonicism is grim, bitter mockery that sounds cold, while snarkiness is often sharper, witty, and more playful.
  • causticity
    9.7 0.00/m
    causticity is biting and harsh in speech or writing, while snarkiness is more casual, sarcastic, and often humorous.
  • mordancy
    9.8 0.01/m
    mordancy is sharp, literary biting criticism in style, while snarkiness is more informal, witty, and mocking in tone.
