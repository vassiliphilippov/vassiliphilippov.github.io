vassalage

noun
the state of being a vassal or serf
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvæsəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvasəlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the condition of being a vassal or serf, especially under a feudal system; subjection or servitude to a lord or superior.

Examples

In vassalage to empire
For centuries, the region remained in vassalage to a distant empire.
The end of vassalage brought new hopes of independence and equality for the former serfs.
Under the terms of the feudal system, peasants lived in vassalage to powerful landowners.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the state of being free rather than under control or subjection
  • synonym: serfdom - the state of being a serf, especially in feudal Europe
