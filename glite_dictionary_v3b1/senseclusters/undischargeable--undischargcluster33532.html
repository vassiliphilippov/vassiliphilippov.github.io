undischargeable

adjective
cannot be dismissed
9.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dɪs.ˈtʃɑːr.dʒə.bəl/ /; / /ə.dɪs.ˈtʃɑːr.dʒə.bəl/ (AmE), /ʌn.dɪs.ˈtʃɑː.dʒə.bəl/ /; / /ə.dɪs.ˈtʃɑː.dʒə.bəl/ (BrE)
not able to be dismissed or removed from a position, job, or service; not subject to discharge.

Examples

Undischargeable position
Due to the terms of his contract, the director was undischargeable except in cases of serious misconduct.
Some government officials hold undischargeable positions to ensure independence from political pressure.
The judge's role was considered undischargeable under the constitution, providing job security.
