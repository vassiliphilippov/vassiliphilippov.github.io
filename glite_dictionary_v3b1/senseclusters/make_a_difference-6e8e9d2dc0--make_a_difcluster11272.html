make a difference

verb
have a significant impact
Pronunciation: / meɪk ə ˈdɪfərəns /; / meɪk ə ˈdɪfəɹəns / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈdɪf(ə)rəns /; / meɪk ə ˈdɪfərəns / (BrE)
to have a significant effect or influence, especially to improve a situation or change the outcome

Examples

Teachers can truly make a difference in their students' lives.

Her encouragement made a difference in his confidence and performance.
Their quick response made a difference during the emergency.
