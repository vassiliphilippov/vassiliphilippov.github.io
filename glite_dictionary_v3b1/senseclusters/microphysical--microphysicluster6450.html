microphysical

adjective
subatomic properties
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the physical properties or processes of matter at a very small (microscopic or subatomic) scale

Examples

Microphysical particle processes
The scientist studied the microphysical interactions between particles in the laboratory.
Some theories in physics focus on the microphysical laws that govern the universe.
Understanding microphysical processes is essential for advances in quantum computing.
Researchers analyze microphysical changes in materials to improve electronic devices.
The microphysical structure of clouds affects how they reflect sunlight.
Understanding microphysical interactions is essential in the study of nanotechnology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macrophysical - relating to large-scale physical properties
  • synonym: microscopic - so small as to be visible only with a microscope
  • synonym: quantum - relating to the smallest possible units in physics
