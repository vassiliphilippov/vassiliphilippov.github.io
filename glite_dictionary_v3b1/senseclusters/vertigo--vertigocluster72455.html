vertigo

noun
sensation of spinning or dizziness

a sensation of spinning or dizziness, often causing a feeling that you or your surroundings are moving or about to fall

Examples

Feeling vertigo suddenly
After standing up too quickly, a wave of vertigo washed over her and she had to sit down.
People with inner ear problems may experience severe vertigo and nausea.
The doctor diagnosed his recurring dizziness as chronic vertigo.

Synonyms

    2.9 5.99/m

  • giddiness
    4.6 0.16/m
    giddiness is a light, dizzy feeling that can include excitement or joy, while vertigo is a stronger spinning sensation.
  • wooziness
    5.4 0.04/m
    wooziness is a vague, faint unsteadiness and slight confusion, while vertigo is a clear spinning feeling that the world is moving.
  • lightheadedness
    5.6 0.23/m
    lightheadedness feels faint and as if you might pass out, while vertigo is a distinct spinning or moving sensation.
  • headedness
    7.8 0.26/m
    headedness is an uncommon term for feeling dizzy or faint, while vertigo names a clear spinning sensation that affects balance.
