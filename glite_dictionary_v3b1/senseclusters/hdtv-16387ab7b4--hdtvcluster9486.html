HDTV

noun
high-definition television system
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ diː tiː ˈviː / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ diː tiː ˈviː / (BrE)
(abbr.) A television system with a higher resolution than standard-definition television, offering more detailed and clearer images.

Examples

A HDTV screen
All new movies look fantastic when displayed on a large HDTV.
She upgraded her living room by purchasing a new 55-inch HDTV.
Whenever I watch sports on my HDTV, the sharpness and color are incredible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: SDTV - standard-definition television, lower-resolution format
  • synonym: high definition - high level of detail in an image or sound
