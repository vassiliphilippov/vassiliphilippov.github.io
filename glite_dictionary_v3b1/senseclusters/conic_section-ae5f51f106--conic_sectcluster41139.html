conic section

noun
curve formed by intersecting a cone and a plane
8.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.nɪk ˈsek.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkoʊ.nɪk ˈsek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.nɪk ˈsek.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkəʊ.nɪk ˈsek.ʃən/ (BrE)
A curve formed by the intersection of a plane with a circular cone, such as an ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola.

Examples

A conic section curve
Astronomers often describe planetary orbits as conic sections depending on their energy.
Each conic section can be defined by its unique geometric properties.
The parabola is a classic example of a conic section in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circle - a conic section in which the plane cuts perpendicular to the cone axis
  • synonym: ellipse - a conic section shaped like an oval
  • synonym: hyperbola - an open curve with two branches, a type of conic section
  • synonym: parabola - a symmetrical open plane curve formed as a conic section
