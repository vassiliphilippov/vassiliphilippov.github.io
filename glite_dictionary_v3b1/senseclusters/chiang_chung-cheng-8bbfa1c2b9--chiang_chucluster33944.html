Chiang Chung-cheng

proper noun
Chinese military and political leader, president of Taiwan
9.9
Pronunciation: /tʃæŋ ˈtʃʌŋ.tʃeŋ/ (AmE), /tʃæŋ ˈtʃʌŋ.tʃeŋ/ (BrE)
(1897–1975) A Chinese military and political leader who was defeated by the Chinese communists in the civil war and, after withdrawing to Taiwan in 1949, served as president of the Republic of China until his death.

Examples

Chiang Chung-cheng leader
Chiang Chung-cheng led the Nationalist government during a tumultuous period of Chinese history.
After the civil war, Chiang Chung-cheng relocated the government to Taiwan.
Many history books refer to Chiang Chung-cheng when discussing 20th century Chinese politics.
