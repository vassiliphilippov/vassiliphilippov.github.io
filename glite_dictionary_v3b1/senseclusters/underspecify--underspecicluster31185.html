underspecify

verb
to give incomplete details
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈspɛsɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈspɛsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to provide an incomplete or insufficient specification, leaving important details undefined or ambiguous

Examples

To underspecify requirements
If you underspecify the project requirements, the team may not know what to deliver.
The instructions were underspecified, so the students were confused about the assignment.
It is risky to underspecify technical details when designing complex systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overdefine - to provide too many details or constraints
  • antonym: specify - to state or describe something clearly and exactly
