Myxococcus

proper noun
bacterial genus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪksəˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a genus of bacteria known for forming fruiting bodies and moving by gliding, often studied for their social behavior

Examples

Myxococcus bacteria
Scientists observed Myxococcus forming complex fruiting bodies under the microscope.
The unique gliding movement of Myxococcus helps it survive in challenging environments.
Myxococcus is often used as a model organism in studies of bacterial cooperation.
