whack-a-mole

noun
situation with recurring problems
Pronunciation: / wæk ə ˈmoʊl / (AmE), / wæk ə ˈməʊl / (BrE)
a situation in which, as soon as one problem is dealt with, another appears, often in rapid or unpredictable succession

Examples

A whack-a-mole problem
Dealing with customer complaints has become a constant whack-a-mole for the support team.
The regulators described fighting online scams as a never-ending game of whack-a-mole.
Trying to clean the house with children around is like playing whack-a-mole—every mess you fix, another appears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: never-ending - describing something that does not stop or seems endless
