upheaval

noun
geological rising of land
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈhivəl / (AmE), / ʌpˈhiːvəl / (BrE)
(Geology) A movement in which part of the earth's crust is raised to a higher elevation, especially during the formation of mountains.

Examples

Geological upheaval
Early geologists studied the upheaval of land in order to understand the origins of mountain ranges.
Seismic activity sometimes results in the sudden upheaval of landforms in the region.
The Himalayan mountains were formed by a massive upheaval of the earth's crust millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uplift - the process by which regions of the Earth's crust are raised to higher elevations
