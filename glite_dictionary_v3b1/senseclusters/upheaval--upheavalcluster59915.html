upheaval

noun
geological rising of land
7.3 0.22/m
Pronunciation: /  åpÀàhiv…ôl / (AmE), /  åpÀàhiÀêv…ôl / (BrE)
(Geology) A movement in which part of the earth's crust is raised to a higher elevation, especially during the formation of mountains.

Examples

Geological upheaval
Early geologists studied the upheaval of land in order to understand the origins of mountain ranges.
Seismic activity sometimes results in the sudden upheaval of landforms in the region.
The Himalayan mountains were formed by a massive upheaval of the earth's crust millions of years ago.

Synonyms

  • uplift
    7.0 0.41/m
    uplift is a general rise of land used in geology, while upheaval names a sudden crustal raising linked to mountain building.
  • upthrust
    8.6 0.02/m
    upthrust is a rise of land caused by tectonic forces during mountain building, while upheaval names a crustal raising event.
  • upthrow
    9.9 0.00/m
    upthrow is an uplift of land often due to fault movement, while upheaval describes a crustal raising linked to broader mountain formation.
  • orogeny
    9.9 0.01/m
    orogeny is the long process of mountain formation, while upheaval names the actual crustal raising that happens during that process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uplift - the process by which regions of the Earth's crust are raised to higher elevations
