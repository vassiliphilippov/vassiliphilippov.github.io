exchequer

noun
government finance department
Pronunciation: / ɛksˈtʃɛkər / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
the government department responsible for managing a country's finances and public revenue (especially in the UK)

Examples

British exchequer
The British exchequer announced new tax policies this year.
Funds from the exchequer were allocated to improve public transportation.
The annual report detailed how the exchequer managed the country's revenue and spending.
The exchequer announced new tax policies this year.
Funds from the exchequer will be used to improve public transportation.
The annual report showed a surplus in the exchequer after careful budgeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: finance ministry - a government department managing national finances
  • synonym: treasury - the government department in charge of public money
