stretch forth

verb
extend or thrust out (something)
6.1 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / str…õt É f…îrŒ∏ / (AmE), / str…õt É f…îÀêŒ∏ / (BrE)
to extend or thrust out (a part of the body or something held) towards something or in a particular direction

Examples

To stretch forth an arm
He stretched forth his arm to point at the distant mountain.
She stretched forth her hand to help the child up.
The priest stretched forth his hands in blessing over the congregation.

Synonyms

    3.9 1.45/m
    outstretch extends something straight outward to full length, while stretch forth means to thrust a part outward toward a target.
  • stretch out
    3.4 2.36/m
    stretch out is to extend a body part to reach or relax, while stretch forth implies thrusting something outward toward a point.
  • reach out
    2.7 8.61/m
    reach out means to extend or stretch outward in space, while stretch forth suggests thrusting a part toward something in a direction.
  • hold out
    3.5 12/m
    hold out is to extend something, often to offer it forward, while stretch forth stresses thrusting a part toward a target.
  • put out
    3.4 1.35/m
    put out is to hold something away from your body, while stretch forth focuses on thrusting a part toward something or direction.
  • extend
    2.7 27/m
    extend means to make something longer or reach farther, while stretch forth suggests a directed thrust of a body part or object.
  • reach
    1.0 113/m
    reach is to extend in order to touch or grasp something, while stretch forth describes thrusting a part outward toward somewhere.
