harrumph

noun
gruff throat sound
9.2
Pronunciation: / həˈrʌmf / (AmE), / həˈrʌmf / (BrE)
a short, gruff sound made by clearing the throat, often to show annoyance, disapproval, or doubt

Examples

A loud harrumph
With a loud harrumph, the professor signaled his displeasure at the late assignment.
He gave a quick harrumph before answering the difficult question.
The meeting was interrupted by a sudden harrumph from the back of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grunt - a low, short sound made in the throat
  • synonym: sigh - a long, deep breath expressing sadness or relief
