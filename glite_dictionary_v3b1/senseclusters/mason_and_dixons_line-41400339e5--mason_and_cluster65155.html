Mason and Dixon's line

noun
symbolic boundary between North and South U.S.
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪsən ənd ˈdɪksənz laɪn / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsən ənd ˈdɪksənz laɪn / (BrE)
The boundary between Maryland and Pennsylvania, regarded as a symbolic dividing line between the Northern and Southern United States, especially before the American Civil War.

Examples

The Mason and Dixon's line
Many people still refer to the Mason and Dixon's line when discussing cultural differences between North and South.
Slavery was largely prohibited north of the Mason and Dixon's line before the Civil War.
The survey work that established the Mason and Dixon's line began in the 1760s.
