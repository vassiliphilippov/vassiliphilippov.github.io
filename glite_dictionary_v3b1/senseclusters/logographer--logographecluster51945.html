logographer

noun
speech writer
9.9
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈɡɑː.grə.fər/ (AmE), /lə.ˈɡɒg.rə.fə/ (BrE)
a person who writes speeches for others, especially for public speakers or legal clients

Examples

Logographer for speeches
The politician relied on a skilled logographer to prepare her campaign speeches.
In ancient Greece, a logographer would compose legal arguments for clients to present in court.
He became a respected logographer after years of writing for prominent public figures.
