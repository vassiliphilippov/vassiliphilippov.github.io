free

adjective
not occupied; available for use
1.0
Pronunciation: / friː / (AmE), / friː / (BrE)
not occupied, taken, or in use; available (of space, seat, time, or person)

Examples

Free parking space
He found a free parking space right outside the restaurant.
Is this seat free, or is someone sitting here?
The conference room will be free after 3 p.m.
Are you free this afternoon to meet for coffee?
If you’re free later, let me know and we can talk.
She usually has a free period between her classes on Tuesday.

Related Terms

  • antonym: busy - having a great deal to do; not free
  • antonym: occupied - being used, taken, or filled by someone or something
