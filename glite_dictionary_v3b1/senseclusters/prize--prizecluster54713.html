prize

verb
value or regard highly
Pronunciation: / praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)
to value something or someone highly; to consider important or precious

Examples

To prize friendship
He prizes the old watch his grandfather gave him.
She has always prized honesty in her friends above all else.
They prized her advice and often asked for her opinion.
He prizes honesty above all other virtues.
I prize these old photographs because they remind me of my childhood.
She really prizes the friendship they have shared for years.
She prizes her grandmother's ring above all her other jewelry.
Many cultures prize honesty and loyalty in their leaders.
He prized his friendship with her and never took it for granted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cherish - to hold something dear; to care for deeply
  • antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; ignore
  • synonym: treasure - to value something greatly and keep it safe
  • synonym: value - to consider something important or beneficial; have a high opinion of
