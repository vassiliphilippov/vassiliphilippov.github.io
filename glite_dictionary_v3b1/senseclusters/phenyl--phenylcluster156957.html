phenyl

noun
benzene-derived group
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːnɪl / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group derived from benzene, consisting of a ring of six carbon atoms bonded to a molecule

Examples

Phenyl group
The scientist explained that the phenyl group is important in many pharmaceutical compounds.
Adding a phenyl ring can change the properties of a chemical substance.
The structure of aspirin includes a phenyl group attached to its core.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benzene - an aromatic hydrocarbon with a ring of six carbon atoms
  • antonym: methyl - a chemical group derived from methane, consisting of one carbon atom and three hydrogen atoms
