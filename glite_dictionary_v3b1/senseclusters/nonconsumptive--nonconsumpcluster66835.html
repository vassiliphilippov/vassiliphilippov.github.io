nonconsumptive

adjective
not using resources
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈsʌmptɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈsʌmptɪv / (BrE)
not involving the removal, depletion, or use of a resource, especially in environmental contexts

Examples

Nonconsumptive use of wildlife
Wildlife photography is considered a nonconsumptive activity because it does not harm the animals or their habitat.
Many parks encourage nonconsumptive recreation such as hiking and birdwatching.
The study focused on the benefits of nonconsumptive uses of natural resources for local communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consumptive - involving the use or depletion of resources
  • synonym: sustainable - able to be maintained without depleting resources
