bisyllabic

adjective
two syllables
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪsɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪsɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
having or consisting of two syllables

Examples

Bisyllabic word
The word 'window' is bisyllabic because it has two syllables.
In poetry, a bisyllabic word can help maintain the rhythm of a line.
Teachers often ask students to clap for each syllable in a bisyllabic word.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monosyllabic - having only one syllable
  • degree higher: trisyllabic - having three syllables
