wildcard

noun
special tournament entry
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪldˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldˌkɑːd / (BrE)
a competitor allowed to enter a contest or tournament without qualifying through the normal process

Examples

Wildcard entry
The tennis player received a wildcard to compete in the international tournament.
She was thrilled when the organizers offered her a wildcard spot in the finals.
Because of his impressive record, he was granted a wildcard entry into the championship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invitee - a person invited to participate in an event
  • antonym: qualifier - a competitor who earns a place by meeting standard requirements
