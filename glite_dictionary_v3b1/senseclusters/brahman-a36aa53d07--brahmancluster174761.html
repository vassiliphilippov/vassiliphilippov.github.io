Brahman

noun
a heat- and tick-resistant cattle breed
7.8 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbrɑː.mən/ /; / /brə.ˈmɑːn/ (AmE), /ˈbrɑː.mən/ /; / /brə.ˈmɑːn/ (BrE)
a breed of large, usually greyish, humped cattle originally developed in the United States from Indian breeds, valued for its resistance to heat and ticks and widely used for crossbreeding

Examples

Brahman cattle breed
Brahman cattle are well suited to hot, humid climates thanks to their distinctive hump and tough skin.
By crossbreeding Brahman bulls with local cows, farmers have produced livestock that thrive in the Southern US.
The rancher imported several Brahman calves to improve his herd's resistance to local parasites.
