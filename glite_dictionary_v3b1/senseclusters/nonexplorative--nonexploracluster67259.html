nonexplorative

adjective
not exploratory; not inclined to explore
Pronunciation: / nɑn.ɪkˈsplɔr.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / nɒn.ɪkˈsplɒr.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
Not inclined or intended to explore; lacking a tendency to investigate or inquire.

Examples

A nonexplorative approach
His nonexplorative approach to research meant he rarely ventured beyond his area of expertise.
In contrast to exploratory learning, the nonexplorative method relies on direct instruction.
The novel adopts a nonexplorative narrative, sticking to well-established storylines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exploratory - involving or inclined to exploration
