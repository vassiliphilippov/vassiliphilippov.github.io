hydroclimate

noun
regional water climate
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdroʊˌklaɪmət / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəʊˌklaɪmət / (BrE)
the overall water-related climate conditions of a region, including rainfall, evaporation, and runoff

Examples

The hydroclimate system
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the hydroclimate of river basins worldwide.
The hydroclimate in this area is marked by heavy rainfall during the summer months.
Changes in the hydroclimate can impact agriculture and water resources in the region.
Scientists monitor the hydroclimate to understand how rainfall patterns affect river systems.
Changes in the hydroclimate can lead to more frequent droughts or floods in some regions.
The hydroclimate of the area influences both agriculture and water supply planning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: climate - the general weather conditions of a region over a long period
  • synonym: climatology - the scientific study of climate
  • synonym: hydrology - the study of water movement, distribution, and quality
