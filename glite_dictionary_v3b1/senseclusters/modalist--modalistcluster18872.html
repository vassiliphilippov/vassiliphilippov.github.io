modalist

noun
believer in modalism
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.dəl.ɪst/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.dəl.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes in or studies modalism, especially in theology or logic

Examples

Modalist theologian
The modalist view of the Trinity was declared heretical by many early church councils.
As a modalist, he argued that the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are not separate persons.
In logic, a modalist might focus on the different modes of truth and necessity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modalism - the doctrine that the persons of the Trinity are modes or aspects of one God
  • antonym: trinitarian - a person who believes in the doctrine of the Trinity as three distinct persons
