reinstall

verb
install again
Pronunciation: / riˈɪnˌstɔl / (AmE), / riːɪnˈstɔːl / (BrE)
to install something again, especially after it has been removed or repaired

Examples

To reinstall the sink
After the repairs, the plumber will reinstall the sink in your kitchen.
If the software stops working, you may need to reinstall it from the original disk.
She had to reinstall the app because it kept crashing on her phone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: install - to put something in place and make it ready for use
  • antonym: uninstall - to remove a program or device from a system
