worship

verb
attend religious services
4.2 8.81/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…úÀêr É…™p / (AmE), / Ààw…úÀê É…™p / (BrE)
to show devotion or reverence to a god or religious figure, especially by taking part in religious ceremonies

Examples

To worship at church
Every Sunday, the family worships at their local church.
Many people worship in different ways, some alone and others in large congregations.
They worship together during special religious holidays and festivals.
Many communities worship the sun in religious ceremonies.
She worships at the temple every weekend to honor her faith.
They worshipped together during the festival, singing hymns and offering prayers.

Synonyms

  • reverence
    7.1 0.29/m
    reverence is quiet, deep respect often shown for sacred people or things, while worship is open religious devotion with ceremonies.
  • deify
    8.4 0.60/m
    deify means to treat someone as a god, often in praise or excess, while worship means regular devotion and religious ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adore - to love and respect (someone or something) deeply
  • antonym: blaspheme - to speak irreverently about a deity or sacred things
  • synonym: pray - to speak to God or a deity, especially in order to give thanks or ask for help
