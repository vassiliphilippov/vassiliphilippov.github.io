contraband

noun
illegally imported or prohibited goods
3.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.trə.bænd/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.trə.bænd/ (BrE)
goods that are imported, exported, or possessed illegally, especially because they are prohibited by law

Examples

Contraband goods seized
During the inspection, several boxes of contraband cigarettes were seized.
Possession of contraband can lead to heavy fines and imprisonment.
The customs officers discovered contraband hidden in the cargo hold of the ship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legal goods - goods that are imported or exported lawfully
  • synonym: smuggled goods - goods brought into a country illegally
