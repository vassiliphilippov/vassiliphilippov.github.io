diff

verb
to compare files
8.0
Pronunciation: /dɪf/ (AmE), /dɪf/ (BrE)
to compare two files or versions of text and show the differences between them, usually with a software tool

Examples

To diff two files
You should diff the old and new documents to see what has changed.
Before merging the code, the developer decided to diff the two versions for conflicts.
It is helpful to diff your files regularly to track any unexpected changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compare - to examine two or more things to see how they are similar or different
  • antonym: merge - to combine two or more files or sets of information into one
