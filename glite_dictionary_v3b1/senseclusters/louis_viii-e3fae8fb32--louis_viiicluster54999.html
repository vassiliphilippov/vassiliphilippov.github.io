Louis VIII

proper noun
King of France, 1223–1226
7.9
Pronunciation: / luˈi eɪtθ / (AmE), / luˈi ˈeɪtθ / (BrE)
(1187–1226) King of France from 1223 to 1226, known for strengthening royal authority over feudal lords.

Examples

Louis VIII of France
Louis VIII ascended to the French throne in 1223 after the death of his father, Philip II.
During his short reign, Louis VIII worked to diminish the power of the great lords and strengthen the monarchy.
The policies of Louis VIII laid the groundwork for his son, Louis IX, to become a powerful ruler.
