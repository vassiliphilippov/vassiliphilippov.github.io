agrarianism

noun
support for land reform
8.3
Pronunciation: / əˈɡrɛriənɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈɡreəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
a political and social belief or philosophy that values rural society, agriculture, and small farmers, often supporting land reform

Examples

Agrarianism movement
The rise of agrarianism in the 1800s led to major land reforms in several countries.
Many political parties in rural areas promote agrarianism to protect small farmers' interests.
Debates about agrarianism often focus on fair land distribution and rural development.
The political party promoted agrarianism as a way to support small farmers and rural communities.
Many 19th-century thinkers believed that agrarianism would lead to a more equal and stable society.
Debates about agrarianism often focus on the balance between agriculture and industrial development.
Agrarianism played a significant role in shaping early American political thought.
The movement was inspired by agrarianism and the desire to preserve traditional farming communities.
Many writers have explored the values of agrarianism in their works about rural life.

  • synonym: ruralism - preference for rural life and values
