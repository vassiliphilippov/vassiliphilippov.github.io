fall

verb
(of a face) become sad or disappointed
3.7
Pronunciation: / fɔl / (AmE), / fɔːl / (BrE)
to show or express a sudden change in emotion, especially disappointment or sadness (of face, eyes, mood, silence, etc.)

Examples

Her face fell suddenly
Her smile disappeared and her face fell when she heard the news.
When he realized he had lost the game, his expression fell instantly.
You could see their faces fall as the reality of the situation set in.
After resolving their differences, suspicion fell from his heart.
As the announcement sank in, a wave of relief fell from their minds.
With time, the bitterness she once felt gradually fell away.
A heavy mood fell upon the room after the sad news was announced.
As dusk approached, a peaceful silence fell over the busy city.
When darkness fell, the hikers quickly set up their tents for the night.
