stifled

adjective
muted or held back with difficulty
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪfəld / (AmE), / ˈstaɪfəld / (BrE)
restrained, muted, or held back with difficulty; not expressed openly or fully

Examples

A stifled yawn
He spoke through a stifled yawn, unable to hide his sleepiness.
She let out a stifled laugh, trying not to distract the speaker.
Their stifled anger soon gave way to open protest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expressed - communicated or made known; not restrained
  • synonym: smothered - completely covered or suppressed; prevented from being expressed
  • synonym: suppressed - kept from being revealed, published, or recognized; subdued
