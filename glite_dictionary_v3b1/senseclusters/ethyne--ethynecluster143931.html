ethyne

noun
a colorless flammable gas (acetylene)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθ.aɪn / (AmE), / ˈiːθ.aɪn / (BrE)
A colorless, flammable gas (C2H2), used chiefly in welding and in organic synthesis; also known as acetylene.

Examples

Ethyne gas
Because of its triple bond, ethyne is reactive in many chemical syntheses.
The laboratory stored ethyne cylinders for use in organic reactions.
Welding with ethyne produces a flame hot enough to melt steel.
