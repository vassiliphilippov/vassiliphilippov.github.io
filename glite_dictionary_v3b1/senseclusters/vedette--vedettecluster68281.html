vedette

noun
military lookout
Pronunciation: / vəˈdɛt / (AmE), / vəˈdɛt / (BrE)
a mounted sentry or lookout, especially one stationed ahead of a military force to observe and report on enemy movements

Examples

Military vedette on patrol
A vedette was posted at the edge of the camp to watch for movement.
The army relied on a vedette to warn them of any approaching danger.
Before dawn, the vedette spotted enemy troops moving through the valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scout - a person sent ahead to gather information, especially in the military
  • synonym: sentry - a soldier stationed to keep guard or control access
