bright

adjective
full of light; well-lit

Examples

Bright light
Her office feels cheerful and bright, even on cloudy days.
The hall was bright after they switched on all the chandeliers.
We chose the room with the bright windows facing the garden.
After days of rain, it was a relief to wake up to a bright morning.
He wore a bright yellow shirt that made him stand out in the crowd.

She admired the bright surface of the newly waxed car.

The jeweler carefully polished the ring until the gold was bright and reflective.

Synonyms

  • brilliant
    3.0 4.15/m
    brilliant shines very intensely and feels dazzling, while bright is a general word for strong light.
  • shining
    1.0 0.42/m
    shining reflects or sends back light on a surface, while bright describes something full of strong light.
  • radiant
    4.1 4.39/m
    radiant glows outward and looks warm or glowing, while bright simply means full of light or shining strongly.
  • blazing
    3.5 0.04/m
    blazing burns or shines with very strong, fierce light, while bright covers any object that gives off or reflects light.
  • shiny
    1.0 18/m
    shiny is smooth and glossy, reflecting surface light clearly, while bright means giving off or reflecting a lot of light.
  • gleaming
    3.2 0.06/m
    gleaming shows a neat, reflected shine like polished metal, while bright is a general word for strong or obvious light.
  • lustrous
    5.3 0.85/m
    lustrous has a soft, smooth glow on a surface and sounds a bit formal, while bright is plain and widely used.
  • luminous
    4.8 4.77/m
    luminous gives a gentle, steady light that can seem soft, while bright usually refers to strong or intense light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brilliant - very bright; giving off intense light
  • degree higher: dazzling - extremely bright; blinding
  • antonym: dim - not bright; with little light
  • antonym: dull - lacking brightness, gloss, or sheen; matte
  • synonym: lustrous - having a gentle sheen or soft glow, especially from a surface
  • synonym: shiny - having a smooth, glossy, or polished surface that reflects light
