kinesthesia

noun
sense of body movement and position
9.4
Pronunciation: / kɪnəsˈθiʒə /; / ˌkɪnəˈsθiʒə /; / ˌkɪn.əsˈθiː.ʒə /; / kɪn.iˈθiː.ʒə / (AmE), / kɪnɛsˈθiːziə /; / ˌkɪnɪsˈθiːziə /; / ˌkɪn.ɪsˈθiː.zi.ə /; / kɪn.iˈθiːz.i.ə / (BrE)
the sense that allows you to feel the position, movement, and tension of your own muscles and joints

Examples

Kinesthesia sense
After his injury, his kinesthesia was impaired, making it difficult for him to sense the position of his arm.
Athletes often have highly developed kinesthesia, allowing them to coordinate complex movements.

After his injury, he lost his kinesthesia and could not sense where his limbs were.
Dancers and athletes often have highly developed kinesthesia to control their precise movements.

After his injury, his kinaesthesia was affected, making it difficult for him to sense limb movements.
Children with certain neurological conditions may have reduced kinaesthesia compared to their peers.
Yoga exercises are designed to improve balance and kinaesthesia.
