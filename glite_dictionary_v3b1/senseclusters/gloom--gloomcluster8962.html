gloom

noun
a feeling of sadness or melancholy
Pronunciation: / ɡlum / (AmE), / ɡluːm / (BrE)
a feeling or atmosphere of sadness, depression, or melancholy

Examples

A feeling of gloom
A sense of gloom settled over the room after they heard the disappointing news.
Despite the gloom in her eyes, she managed a small smile.
He tried to shake off the gloom that had followed him all week.
A sense of gloom settled over the town during the long winter nights.
He tried to shake off the gloom that had lingered since his failure.
There was a palpable gloom in the room after they heard the news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheer - a feeling or atmosphere of happiness and encouragement
  • antonym: cheerfulness - a state of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: depression - a state of severe sadness or hopelessness
  • synonym: melancholy - a deep feeling of sadness or sorrow
