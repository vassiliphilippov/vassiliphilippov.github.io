spur

noun
encouraging remark or stimulus
4.1 0.94/m
Pronunciation: / sp…ùÀê / (AmE), / sp…úÀê / (BrE)
a remark or comment that encourages someone to try or do something, or motivates action

Examples

A spur to effort
Her praise acted as a spur to his efforts in the project.
Sometimes a gentle spur is all someone needs to take the first step.
The threat of failure was the main spur for him to study harder.

Synonyms

  • prod
    4.4 0.13/m

  • exhortation
    8.4 1.85/m

  • urging
    3.9 2.01/m

  • prompting
    3.8 0.03/m
    prompting is a gentle suggestion or reminder to take action, while spur is a quick remark meant to encourage doing something.
  • motivating
    3.4 0.01/m
    motivating is giving reasons or rewards to cause action, while spur is often a short spoken remark that encourages someone.
