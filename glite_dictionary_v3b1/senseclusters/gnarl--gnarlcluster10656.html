gnarl

verb
twist into a deformed shape
7.8 6.8
Pronunciation: /nɑːrl/ (AmE), /nɑːl/ (BrE)
to twist or contort into a knotted or deformed shape, especially referring to wood or trees

Examples

To gnarl a branch
Arthritis can gnarl a person’s fingers over time.
Centuries of harsh weather have gnarled the branches of the ancient oak.
The fierce winds gnarled the saplings along the coast, giving them a striking, twisted appearance.
