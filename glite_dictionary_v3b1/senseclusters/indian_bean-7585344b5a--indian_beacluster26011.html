Indian bean

noun
climbing vine with edible beans, grown in South Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən biːn / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən biːn / (BrE)
a perennial climbing vine (Lablab purpureus) of the Old World tropics with purple flowers and maroon pods containing edible seeds, grown as an ornamental and food plant, especially in South Asia

Examples

Indian bean plant
Farmers in India cultivate Indian beans for both local consumption and export.
She added fresh Indian beans to the curry, giving it a distinctive flavor and texture.
The Indian bean is often grown in gardens for its beautiful purple flowers and edible pods.
