apatheia

noun
emotional calmness
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈθiːə / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈθiːə / (BrE)
a state of being free from passion or emotional disturbance, especially as an ideal in Stoic philosophy

Examples

Stoic apatheia
The Stoic sage strives for apatheia to remain unaffected by external events.
Through meditation, she hoped to achieve a sense of apatheia in her daily life.
Many philosophers regard apatheia as essential for true inner peace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equanimity - calmness and composure, especially in difficult situations
  • antonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion
