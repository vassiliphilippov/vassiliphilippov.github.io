hitch

verb
travel by getting free rides from drivers
3.9 1.66/m
Pronunciation: / hÉªtÊƒ / (AmE), / hÉªtÊƒ / (BrE)
to travel by getting free rides from passing vehicles, especially by standing at the side of the road and signalling to drivers

Examples

To hitch a ride
In college, he would often hitch to music festivals to save money on travel.
She managed to hitch a ride to the next town when her car broke down.
We decided to hitch all the way across the country during our summer vacation.

Synonyms

  • hitchhike
    3.9 1.57/m
    hitchhike means to travel by getting free roadside rides, while hitch is the shorter informal verb for the same action.
  • thumb a ride
    8.2 0.04/m
    thumb a ride is to signal with your thumb for a free roadside lift, while hitch names that general action.
  • thumb
    6.2 0.30/m
    thumb means signal with an outstretched thumb for a free ride, while hitch is any roadside way of getting lifts.
  • catch a ride
    3.2 0.40/m
    catch a ride means get a lift offered by someone, while hitch focuses on asking strangers for free roadside rides.
