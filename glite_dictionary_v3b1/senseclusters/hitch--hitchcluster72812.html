hitch

verb
travel by getting free rides from drivers
Pronunciation: / hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
to travel by getting free rides from passing vehicles, especially by standing at the side of the road and signalling to drivers

Examples

To hitch a ride
In college, he would often hitch to music festivals to save money on travel.
She managed to hitch a ride to the next town when her car broke down.
We decided to hitch all the way across the country during our summer vacation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hitchhike - to travel by soliciting free rides from passing vehicles
