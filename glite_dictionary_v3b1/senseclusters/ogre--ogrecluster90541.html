ogre

noun
man-eating giant from folklore
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈəʊɡə / (BrE)
(in folklore) a large, hideous, man-eating giant, often found in fairy tales and legends.

Examples

A fairy tale ogre
Children were warned not to stray into the woods for fear of being caught by an ogre.
The fairytale described an ogre who lived under the bridge and terrified travelers.
The movie's main villain is a towering ogre who eats anyone who comes near his lair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: giant - a being of great size and strength in legends and folklore
  • synonym: troll - a mythical, cave-dwelling being often depicted as large and ugly
