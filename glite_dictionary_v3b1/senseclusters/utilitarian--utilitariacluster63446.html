utilitarian

noun
believer in utilitarianism
4.2
Pronunciation: / juˌtɪlɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / juːˌtɪlɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who believes that the value of something depends on its usefulness or utility, especially in reference to the philosophy of utilitarianism

Examples

A utilitarian thinker
As a committed utilitarian, she tries to maximize happiness for the greatest number.
Jeremy Bentham is often cited as a pioneering utilitarian.
Many philosophers debate whether a true utilitarian could endorse such actions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pragmatist - a person who is guided more by practical considerations than by ideals
