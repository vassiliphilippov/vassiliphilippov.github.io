Lucretius

proper noun
Roman philosopher and poet (c. 96–c. 55 BC)
9.9
Pronunciation: /luː.ˈkriː.ʃəs/ /; / /luː.ˈkreɪ.ʃəs/ (AmE), /luː.ˈkriː.ʃəs/ /; / /luː.ˈkreɪ.ʃəs/ (BrE)
(c. 96–c. 55 BC) A Roman philosopher and poet known for his didactic poem 'De Rerum Natura', offering a scientific explanation of the universe.

Examples

Lucretius the poet
Lucretius argued that everything in the world is made up of tiny, indestructible atoms.
Many scholars study Lucretius to better understand ancient Roman views on nature and the cosmos.
The poetry of Lucretius continues to influence both scientific and philosophical thought today.
