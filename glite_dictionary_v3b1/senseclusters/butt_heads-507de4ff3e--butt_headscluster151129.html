butt heads

verb
to argue or clash with someone
6.0 5.1
Pronunciation: /bʌt hedz/ (AmE), /bʌt hedz/ (BrE)
(of two or more people) to disagree strongly or clash, especially in an argumentative or confrontational way.

Examples

To butt heads strongly
The coach warned the team not to butt heads with the referee during the match.
The new manager and his assistant often butt heads about the best strategies for the company.
Whenever politics comes up, they butt heads and neither of them backs down.
