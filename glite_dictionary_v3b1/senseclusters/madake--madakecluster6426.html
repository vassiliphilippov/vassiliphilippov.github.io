madake

noun
a species of large bamboo
9.3
Pronunciation: / mɑːˈdɑːkeɪ / (AmE), / məˈdɑːkeɪ / (BrE)
A large species of bamboo with thick-walled stems, native to China and possibly Japan, and widely cultivated elsewhere.

Examples

The madake bamboo
In botanical gardens, madake is often displayed alongside other Asian bamboo species.
Many traditional Japanese fences are constructed using strong madake culms.
The craftsman made the flute from madake because of its excellent tonal quality.
