brittleness

noun
quality of breaking easily
8.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbrɪt.əl.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈbrɪt.əl.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality of being hard but easily broken or cracked.

Examples

Glass brittleness
Cold temperatures can increase the brittleness of certain metals, causing them to break more easily.
The brittleness of the glass made it unsuitable for use in a children's playroom.
The scientist measured the brittleness of the polymer to determine its potential applications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ductility - the ability to be drawn out into a thin wire; opposite of brittleness in materials science
  • synonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
