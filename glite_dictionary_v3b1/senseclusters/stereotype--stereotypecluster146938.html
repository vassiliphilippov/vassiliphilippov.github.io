stereotype

verb
to judge or classify by stereotypes
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəˌtaɪp / (BrE)
to have a fixed idea of what a particular type of person or thing is like, often unfairly, and to treat or categorize them accordingly

Examples

To stereotype a group
It's wrong to stereotype people based on their appearance alone.
She felt frustrated that she had been stereotyped because of her accent.
The media often stereotypes teenagers as rebellious and uninterested in school.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individualize - consider or treat as an individual, not as part of a group or category
  • synonym: prejudge - form a judgment on an issue or person before having adequate information
