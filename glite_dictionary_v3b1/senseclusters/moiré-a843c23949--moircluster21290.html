moiré

verb
to create wavy pattern
Pronunciation: / mwɑːˈreɪ / (AmE), / mwɑːˈreɪ / (BrE)
to produce a moiré pattern, creating a rippled or wavy interference effect on fabric or an image

Examples

To moire an image
The designer decided to moiré the fabric to give the dress a shimmering effect.
Photographers sometimes accidentally moiré digital images when patterns overlap.
Special rollers are used to moiré silk, creating a unique wavy appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth - to make a surface even and without patterns
  • synonym: watermark - to mark with a faint design, often for security or decoration
