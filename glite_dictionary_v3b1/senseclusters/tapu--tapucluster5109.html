tapu

noun
sacred or forbidden thing
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːpuː / (AmE), / ˈtɑːpuː / (BrE)
a Polynesian concept referring to something sacred or forbidden by social or religious custom

Examples

A tapu object
The ancient tree was considered tapu and no one was allowed to touch it.
Breaking a tapu could bring misfortune according to traditional beliefs.
Certain foods were tapu during religious ceremonies in the village.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permitted - allowed by rules or customs
  • synonym: sacred - regarded with great respect and reverence
  • synonym: taboo - a social or religious custom prohibiting a particular practice
