yoicks

interjection
hunting exclamation
Pronunciation: / jɔɪks / (AmE), / jɔɪks / (BrE)
an old exclamation used by hunters to encourage hounds during a hunt

Examples

Yoicks to the hounds
The huntsman raised his horn and shouted yoicks to urge the hounds forward.
With a loud yoicks, the riders spurred their horses after the pack.
In old English hunting scenes, you often hear the call yoicks echoing through the woods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tally-ho - a traditional hunting cry to signal the sighting of a fox
