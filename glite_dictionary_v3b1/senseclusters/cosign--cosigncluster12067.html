cosign

verb
sign jointly (often to guarantee a loan)
1.0 6.8
Pronunciation: / koʊˈsaɪn /; / ˈkoʊˌsaɪn / (AmE), / kəʊˈsaɪn /; / ˈkəʊˌsaɪn / (BrE)
to sign a document together with another person, especially to guarantee a loan or agreement

Examples

To cosign a loan
Both parents were required to cosign the loan application for their son.
If you cosign this contract, you share responsibility for the debt.
She agreed to cosign the apartment lease with her friend.
Before agreeing to cosign a friend's lease, consider the potential financial risks.
If you cosign on a mortgage, you are equally responsible for the debt.
Sarah asked her father to cosign the loan application so she could buy her first car.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endorse - to sign the back of a financial document to make it payable to someone else
  • synonym: sign - to write your name on a document to indicate approval or agreement
