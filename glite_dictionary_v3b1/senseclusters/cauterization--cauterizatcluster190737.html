cauterization

noun
burning tissue to stop bleeding or infection
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːtərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of burning a part of the body to stop bleeding or prevent infection, typically using a hot iron, caustic agent, or extreme cold

Examples

Cauterization of wound
After the surgery, the wound was sealed using cauterization to prevent excessive bleeding.
In ancient medicine, cauterization was often employed to treat wounds and infections.
Modern techniques of cauterization include the use of lasers and electrical currents.
Cauterisation of the wound helped reduce the risk of postoperative complications.
Early medical practices often relied on cauterisation to treat wounds and infections.
The surgeon performed cauterisation to prevent further bleeding during the operation.
