iodine-125

noun
a radioactive isotope of iodine (used in medicine)
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.oʊˌdiːn wʌn ˈtwɛnti faɪv / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əˌdiːn wʌn ˈtwen.ti faɪv / (BrE)
a light radioactive isotope of iodine (with a mass number of 125), used as a tracer in thyroid studies and in the treatment of hyperthyroidism

Examples

iodine-125 radioactive isotope
Proper handling procedures are essential when working with iodine-125 in the laboratory.
Radioactive tracers such as iodine-125 help in diagnosing thyroid disorders.
The doctor recommended using iodine-125 for targeted treatment of the patient's thyroid cancer.
