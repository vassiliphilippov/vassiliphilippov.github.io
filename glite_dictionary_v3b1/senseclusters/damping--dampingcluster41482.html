damping

noun
reduction of vibrations
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdæm.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdæm.pɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of reducing vibrations, oscillations, or movement energy in a system

Examples

Damping of vibrations
The damping in the suspension system helps make the ride smoother.
Engineers improved the damping to prevent the bridge from swaying in strong winds.
Proper damping is essential in musical instruments to control unwanted vibrations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplification - the process of increasing the strength or magnitude of something
  • synonym: attenuation - the reduction of the force, effect, or value of something
