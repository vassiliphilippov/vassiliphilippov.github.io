snuff out

verb
extinguish a flame or light
6.5
Pronunciation: / snʌf aʊt / (AmE), / snʌf aʊt / (BrE)
to extinguish a flame, fire, or light; to cause something burning to stop burning.

Examples

To snuff out a candle
Firefighters managed to snuff out the blaze after several hours of hard work.
He used his fingers to snuff out the match before tossing it away.
She carefully snuffed out the candle before leaving the room.
