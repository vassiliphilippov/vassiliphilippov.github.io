incompressibility

noun
resistance to being compressed
8.2 7.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kəm.prə.sɪ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.kəm.prə.sɪ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
The quality or state of being unable to be compressed; resistance to reduction in volume under pressure.

Examples

High incompressibility of water
Due to its high incompressibility, this liquid cannot easily be compacted further.
Scientists study the incompressibility of materials to understand their behavior under extreme pressure.
The incompressibility of water makes it useful in hydraulic systems.
