gaslighting

noun
psychological manipulation
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈgæs.laɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈgæs.laɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of manipulating someone into doubting their own perceptions, memory, or sanity

Examples

Emotional gaslighting
After months of gaslighting, he began to question his own memory and judgment.
The therapist explained how gaslighting can damage a person's confidence and sense of reality.
She recognized the signs of gaslighting in her relationship and decided to seek help.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deception - the act of making someone believe something that is not true
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere
  • synonym: manipulation - controlling or influencing someone in a clever or unfair way
