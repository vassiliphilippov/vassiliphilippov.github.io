perforant

noun
hole-making tool
9.5 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úÀêrf…ôr…ônt / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêf…ôr…ônt / (BrE)
a tool or instrument used to make holes or perforations in materials such as paper, leather, or fabric

Examples

A perforant tool
The artisan used a perforant to create evenly spaced holes in the leather belt.
Before binding the pages, she made holes with a perforant to thread the string through.
A perforant is essential for crafts that require precise holes in fabric or paper.

Synonyms

  • perforator
    7.1 0.01/m
    a perforator makes neat, regular rows of holes for tearing or binding, while a perforant is any tool that makes holes.
  • puncher
    4.0 0.04/m
    a puncher cuts or punches individual holes by force, while a perforant is a more general tool for making holes in materials.
  • piercer
    5.6 0.00/m
    a piercer has a sharp point to stab or puncture materials, while a perforant is a tool made to create holes cleanly.
