swamp fever

noun
infectious disease (leptospirosis) with fever and jaundice
8.1 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / sw…ëmp ÀàfiÀêv…ö / (AmE), / sw…ímp ÀàfiÀêv…ô / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by Leptospira bacteria, transmitted from animals to humans and characterized by fever and jaundice; also known as leptospirosis

Examples

Swamp fever disease
Symptoms of swamp fever may include severe headaches, muscle pain, and jaundice.
The farmer was hospitalized after contracting swamp fever from contaminated water.
Veterinarians warn that swamp fever can spread to humans through animal contact.
