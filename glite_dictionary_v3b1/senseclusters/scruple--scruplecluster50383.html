scruple

noun
moral doubt; ethical hesitation
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈskruːpəl / (AmE), / ˈskruːpəl / (BrE)
a feeling of doubt or hesitation about whether something is morally right or proper

Examples

Moral scruples
His scruples prevented him from accepting the bribe.
She had no scruple about telling the truth, even if it hurt.
Without a scruple, he manipulated the data to fit his theory.
Despite his financial troubles, he had a scruple about taking money that was not his.
His scruples prevented him from lying on the application form.
She acted without a scruple, ignoring the consequences of her actions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compunction - a feeling of guilt or moral scruple that prevents or follows wrongdoing
  • synonym: qualm - an uneasy feeling of doubt or worry, especially about one's own conduct
  • degree higher: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
