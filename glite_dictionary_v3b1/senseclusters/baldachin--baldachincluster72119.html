baldachin

noun
ornamental canopy over an altar or similar place
Pronunciation: / ˈbæl.də.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈbæld.ə.kɪn / (BrE)
An ornamental canopy of stone, metal, or fabric, often supported on columns, typically erected over an altar, throne, or doorway.

Examples

A baldachin over altar
A gilded baldachin hung over the king's throne, symbolizing his authority.
Restorers were careful to preserve the original details of the eighteenth-century baldachin.
The cathedral's altar was crowned by a magnificent baldachin supported by marble columns.
