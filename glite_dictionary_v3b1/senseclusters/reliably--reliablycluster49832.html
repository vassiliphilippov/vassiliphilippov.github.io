reliably

adverb
in a dependable or consistent manner
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlaɪəbli / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪəbli / (BrE)
In a dependable or consistent manner; in such a way that something can be trusted to happen or work as expected.

Examples

reliably pays bills
He always pays his bills reliably and on time.
The new software performed reliably during the entire test period.
The train arrived reliably every morning at 8 o'clock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faithfully - in a loyal or dependable manner
  • antonym: unreliably - in a way that cannot be depended on
