In a dependable or consistent manner; in such a way that something can be trusted to happen or work as expected.

Examples

reliably pays bills
He always pays his bills reliably and on time.
The new software performed reliably during the entire test period.
The train arrived reliably every morning at 8 o'clock.

Synonyms

    3.0 0.18/m
    dependably means in a trustworthy, steady way you can count on, while reliably stresses that something will work as expected.
  • consistently
    2.8 29/m
    consistently means doing something the same way over time, while reliably means being trusted to work when needed.
  • unfailingly
    5.6 0.54/m
    unfailingly means happening without fail every time, often with more force, while reliably means it can be trusted in normal use.
