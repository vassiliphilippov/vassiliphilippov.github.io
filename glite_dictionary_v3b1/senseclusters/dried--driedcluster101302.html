dried

adjective
preserved by moisture removal
1.0 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / draÉªd / (AmE), / draÉªd / (BrE)
(of food or other substances) preserved by removing natural moisture, often to increase shelf life or for culinary purposes.

Examples

Dried fruit
She added a handful of dried herbs to the sauce for extra flavor.
The hikers packed dried beef as a lightweight source of protein for their trip.
We always keep some dried fruit in the pantry for baking and snacking.

Synonyms

  • dehydrated
    3.6 0.18/m
    dehydrated is made by removing moisture to preserve food or concentrate flavor, while dried is the general term for removing water.
  • desiccated
    7.2 0.02/m
    desiccated means nearly all moisture is removed and the item is very dry, while dried is a broader term for removing water to preserve substances.

Related Terms
