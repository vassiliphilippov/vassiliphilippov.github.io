chondrin

noun
gelatinous material from boiled cartilage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.drɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.drɪn / (BrE)
a gelatinous substance obtained by boiling cartilage in water, formerly thought to be a chief constituent of cartilage

Examples

Boiled chondrin substance
Boiling cartilage produces chondrin, which was once believed to give cartilage its firmness.
During the experiment, the researcher extracted chondrin from the cartilage samples.
The study detailed the properties of chondrin in connective tissue.
