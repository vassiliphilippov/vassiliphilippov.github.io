Florence Nightingale

proper noun
pioneering English nurse (1820–1910)
Pronunciation: / ˈflɔːrəns ˈnaɪtənˌɡeɪl /; / ˈflɔːrəns ˈnaɪtɪnˌɡeɪl / (AmE), / ˈflɒrəns ˈnaɪtɪnɡeɪl / (BrE)
an English nurse (1820–1910) known for her pioneering work in nursing and as the founder of modern nursing; also used figuratively to mean a compassionate caregiver
