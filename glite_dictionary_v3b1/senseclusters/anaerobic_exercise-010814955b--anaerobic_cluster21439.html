anaerobic exercise

noun
short, intense exercise that builds muscle
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈroʊbɪk ˈɛksərˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˌænəˈrəʊbɪk ˈɛksəsaɪz / (BrE)
physical exercise that involves short bursts of high-intensity activity, building muscle strength and mass without relying primarily on oxygen intake

Examples

High-intensity anaerobic exercise
Short sprints and high-intensity interval training are both considered types of anaerobic exercise.
Unlike running a marathon, anaerobic exercise does not rely heavily on sustained oxygen consumption.
Weightlifting is a popular form of anaerobic exercise that helps build muscle strength.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aerobic exercise - exercise that requires oxygen and is sustained over longer periods
