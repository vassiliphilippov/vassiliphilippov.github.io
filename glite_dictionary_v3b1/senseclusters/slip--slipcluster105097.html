slip

verb
decline to a lower standard
1.0
Pronunciation: / slɪp / (AmE), / slɪp / (BrE)
to decline or fall to a lower standard, level, or condition

Examples

To slip in quality
After a strong start to the season, the team's performance began to slip in the final games.
Her grades started to slip after she missed several classes.
If you let your focus slip, your work may suffer as a result.
