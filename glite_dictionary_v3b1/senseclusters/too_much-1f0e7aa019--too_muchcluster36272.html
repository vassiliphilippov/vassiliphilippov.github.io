too much

adverb
excessively; more than necessary
1.2 101/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀê m åt É / (AmE), / tuÀê m åt É / (BrE)
to a greater degree or amount than is necessary, desirable, or acceptable; excessively

Examples

Too much noise
He apologized for talking too much during the meeting.
If you eat too much sugar, it can affect your health.
She always worries too much about what others think.

You added too much sugar to the cake and made it overly sweet.

Synonyms

  • excessively
    3.0 3.81/m
    excessively describes actions done to a more than acceptable degree, while too much is the everyday way to say that.
  • overly
    3.5 14/m
    overly often modifies adjectives in speech or writing, while too much is more flexible and used in many contexts.
  • inordinately
    7.5 0.75/m
    inordinately sounds formal and describes an unusually large amount, while too much is common and more neutral.
  • overmuch
    7.9 0.12/m
    overmuch appears in older or literary English and feels rare, while too much is modern and used every day.
  • unduly
    6.9 2.35/m
    unduly often sounds formal or legal and means more than justified, while too much is more general everyday language.
  • exorbitantly
    7.3 0.16/m
    exorbitantly describes very high, often unfair prices or costs, while too much can describe any excessive amount.
  • all too
    4.7 14/m
    all too usually comes before an adjective to stress a negative fact, while too much can stand alone after verbs or with nouns.
