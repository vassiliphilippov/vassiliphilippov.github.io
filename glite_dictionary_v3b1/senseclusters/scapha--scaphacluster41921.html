scapha

noun
small shallow boat
Pronunciation: / ˈskæfə / (AmE), / ˈskɑːfə / (BrE)
an archaic term for a small, shallow boat or skiff

Examples

Ancient scapha boat
The old manuscript described a scapha used to cross the river in ancient times.
Sailors would sometimes use a scapha to reach the shore from their anchored ship.
He admired the craftsmanship of the wooden scapha displayed in the maritime museum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dinghy - a small open boat, often carried on a ship
  • synonym: skiff - a small, light boat for rowing or sailing
