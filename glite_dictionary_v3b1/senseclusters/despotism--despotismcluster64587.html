despotism

noun
absolute rule by a dictator
3.5 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛspəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɛspətɪz(ə)m /; / ˈdɛspətɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of government where one person or group has absolute power, often ruling in a cruel or oppressive way

Examples

Absolute despotism
Many intellectuals fled the nation to escape the ruler's despotism.
The country suffered under a long period of despotism that suppressed all opposition.
Throughout history, despotism has often led to widespread social unrest.
Many revolutionaries rose up to end the despotism that plagued their nation.
The country suffered under the despotism of a single ruler for decades.
Writers often spoke against despotism, calling for liberty and justice instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autocracy - government by one person with absolute power
  • antonym: democracy - government by the people, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: dictatorship - government by a dictator with absolute power
  • synonym: tyranny - cruel and oppressive government or rule
