plead

verb
ask or beg earnestly
3.9 14/m
Pronunciation: / plid / (AmE), / pliÀêd / (BrE)
to ask for something in an earnest or emotional way; to appeal or beg seriously

Examples

To plead for help
He pleaded with his parents to let him go to the concert.
She pleaded for a second chance after making a mistake.
The witness pleaded earnestly with the judge for leniency.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • implore
    5.3 2/m
    implore asks for something in a desperate, urgent way, while plead can mean a general earnest or legal appeal.
  • beseech
    8.2 2/m
    beseech asks someone with strong feeling or formality, while plead is more neutral and used both emotionally and legally.
  • entreat
    8.1 0.7/m
    entreat asks earnestly and politely, often in formal speech, while plead is broader and appears in everyday and legal contexts.
  • beg
    1.0 9/m
    beg asks directly and plainly, often in desperate or informal speech, while plead can be more formal and less blunt.
  • supplicate
    9.6 0.2/m
    supplicate asks humbly, often like a prayer to someone in power, while plead is more general and not always humble.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • synonym: implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
