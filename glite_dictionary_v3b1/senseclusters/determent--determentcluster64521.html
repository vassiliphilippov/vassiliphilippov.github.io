determent

noun
something that discourages action
2.1 6.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈtɜːr.mənt/ /; / /də.ˈtɜːr.mənt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈtɜː.mənt/ /; / /də.ˈtɜː.mənt/ (BrE)
a communication or action that discourages someone from attempting something, often by warning of possible bad consequences

Examples

A determent to crime
Fear of punishment can serve as a determent to would-be offenders.
Government warnings acted as a determent to people considering illegal downloads.
The threatening letter served as a powerful determent against further protests.
