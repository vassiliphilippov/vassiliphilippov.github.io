Albers-Schonberg disease

noun
an inherited bone density disorder
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɑlbərz ˈʃoʊn.bɜrɡ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈal.bəz ˈʃɒn.bɜːɡ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
an inherited disorder marked by increased bone density, which in severe cases can result in the obliteration of the bone marrow cavity

Examples

Albers-Schonberg disease bone density
Children affected by Albers-Schonberg disease may experience bone pain and frequent fractures.
Research into treatments for Albers-Schonberg disease is ongoing due to the severity of its skeletal effects.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with Albers-Schonberg disease after observing abnormally dense bones on the X-ray.
