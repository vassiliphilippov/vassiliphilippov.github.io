entirety

noun
the state of being whole or complete
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtaɪrəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪərɪti / (BrE)
the state of being whole, complete, or undivided; the quality of including everything or lacking nothing

Examples

The entirety of something
He was fascinated by the entirety of the ancient manuscript.
She watched the movie in its entirety, not missing a single scene.
The proposal must be read in entirety to fully understand its impact.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragment - a small part broken off or detached
  • synonym: totality - the state of being complete or whole
