iron carbide

noun
hard, brittle steel and cast-iron compound
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪərn ˈkɑrˌbaɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪən ˈkɑːbaɪd / (BrE)
a hard, brittle chemical compound (Fe₃C) that is a constituent of steel and cast iron

Examples

Iron carbide compound
During cooling, iron carbide separates out as a hard phase.
Modern steels contain controlled amounts of iron carbide to achieve desired properties.
Researchers studied the formation of iron carbide in tempered steel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cementite - another name for iron carbide, especially in metallurgy
