common beech

noun
European beech tree species, Fagus sylvatica
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈbitʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbiːtʃ / (BrE)
A large European beech tree (Fagus sylvatica) with slightly toothed leaves, widely grown as an ornamental tree in North America and elsewhere.

Examples

The common beech tree
Many North American landscapes feature the common beech as a shade tree.
The common beech is prized for its smooth grey bark and stately appearance in parks.
We planted a common beech in our garden for its beautiful autumn foliage.
