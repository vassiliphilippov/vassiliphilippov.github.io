combustor

noun
fuel-burning chamber
7.5
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbʌstər / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌstə / (BrE)
a chamber or device in which fuel is burned to produce energy, especially in engines or turbines

Examples

Jet engine combustor
The combustor in a jet engine mixes fuel and air for efficient burning.
Engineers inspected the combustor for signs of overheating after the test run.
Modern gas turbines use an advanced combustor to reduce emissions and increase efficiency.
