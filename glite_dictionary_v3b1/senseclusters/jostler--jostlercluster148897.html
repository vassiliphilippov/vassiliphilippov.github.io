jostler

noun
aggressive competitor
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɑslər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒslə / (BrE)
a person who pushes or competes forcefully with others, especially in a crowd

Examples

A jostler in a crowd
In the busy marketplace, every jostler tried to get the best spot for their stall.
As a natural jostler, she always found a way to stand out in group discussions.
The conference was full of jostlers hoping to catch the attention of potential employers.
The jostler made his way through the crowd at the concert, barely apologizing as he passed.
Security watched the jostler closely as he moved quickly through the busy subway station.
Tourists were warned to be careful of jostlers in the crowded marketplace.
