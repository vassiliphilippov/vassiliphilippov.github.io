inebriate

verb
make drunk; intoxicate with alcohol
Pronunciation: / ɪˈniː.bri.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈniː.bri.eɪt /; / ɪˈniː.brɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to make someone drunk or to become drunk from drinking alcohol

Examples

To inebriate the mind
Celebrations that inebriate the senses are common during the festival.
It was considered irresponsible to inebriate oneself before an important meeting.
The bartender refused to inebriate the already drunken guest any further.
He managed to inebriate himself after only two drinks.
She warned him not to inebriate before the important meeting.
The celebration was meant to inebriate the participants with both joy and wine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intoxicate - to make someone drunk or affected by alcohol
  • antonym: sober - to make or become not drunk
