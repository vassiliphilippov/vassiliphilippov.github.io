pipe clamp

noun
device for holding pipes during work
Pronunciation: / paɪp klæmp / (AmE), / paɪp klɑːmp / (BrE)
a device used to securely hold a pipe in place, especially while it is being cut or threaded

Examples

A pipe clamp tool
He used a pipe clamp to hold the pipe steady while cutting it.
The workshop has several sizes of pipe clamps for different projects.
When threading new plumbing, a pipe clamp makes the job much easier.
