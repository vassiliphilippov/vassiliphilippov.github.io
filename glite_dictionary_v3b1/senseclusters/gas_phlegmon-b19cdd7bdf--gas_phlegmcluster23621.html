gas phlegmon

noun
severe bacterial gangrene with gas in tissues
Pronunciation: / ɡæs ˈflɛɡˌmɑn / (AmE), / ɡas ˈflɛɡ.mən / (BrE)
A severe, rapidly spreading and often fatal bacterial infection causing gas formation in tissues, usually caused by Clostridium bacteria and characterized by tissue death; can be used as a biological weapon.

Examples

Gas phlegmon infection
Bioterrorism experts are concerned about the potential use of gas phlegmon as a weapon.
Surgeons discovered extensive tissue damage due to gas phlegmon in the patient's leg.
Without prompt treatment, gas phlegmon can lead to widespread organ failure and death.
