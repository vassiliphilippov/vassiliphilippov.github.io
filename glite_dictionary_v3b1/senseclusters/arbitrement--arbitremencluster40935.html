arbitrement

noun
authoritative judgment or decision
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbətrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrəmənt / (BrE)
the act of deciding as an arbiter; authoritative judgment or decision, especially as rendered by an arbitrator

Examples

An arbitrement decision
Medieval contracts sometimes called for arbitrement in case of disagreement.
The arbitrement of the council resolved the dispute between the two merchants.
They accepted the arbitrement of the chosen neutral party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arbitration - the process of settling a dispute by an impartial third party
