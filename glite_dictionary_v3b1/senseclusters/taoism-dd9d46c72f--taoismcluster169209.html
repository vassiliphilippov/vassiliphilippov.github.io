Taoism

noun
Chinese philosophical and religious tradition
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊˌɪzəm /; / ˈdaʊ.ɪzəm, ˈtaʊ.ɪzəm /; / ˈdaʊˌɪzəm, ˈtaʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtaʊˌɪzəm /; / ˈtaʊ.ɪzəm, ˈdaʊ.ɪzəm /; / ˈtaʊ.ɪzəm / (BrE)
a Chinese philosophical and religious tradition based on the teachings of Lao-tzu, emphasizing living in harmony with the Tao (the Way), and sometimes including practices like meditation, alchemy, and divination

Taoism philosophy
In Taoism, practitioners seek to live in accordance with the Tao, or the natural order of the universe.

The principles of Taoism have influenced Chinese art, medicine, and literature for centuries.
Historically, Taoism has influenced Chinese art, medicine, and politics.
Many people find that Taoism encourages a peaceful and mindful approach to daily life.

Taoism teaches that a simple life in harmony with nature is the key to happiness.
Many ancient texts on Taoism emphasize the importance of flexibility and effortless action.
She studied Taoism to better understand Eastern philosophies and meditation techniques.
