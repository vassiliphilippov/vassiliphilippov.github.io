harrow

verb
to break up soil with a harrow
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛroʊ /; / ˈhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhærəʊ / (BrE)
to break up and smooth the surface of soil with a harrow, especially after plowing

Examples

To harrow the field
After plowing, they carefully harrowed the land to break up large clods of soil.
It is essential to harrow the ground evenly for a successful crop.
The farmer will harrow the field to prepare it for planting.
After planting, they harrowed the field to break clods and level the soil before sowing.
Farmers often harrow the land to prepare a smooth seedbed for crops.
The soil was harrowed thoroughly to ensure even growth of the new plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plow - to turn over the earth with a plow
  • synonym: till - to prepare land for growing crops by turning and breaking up the soil
