medlar tree

noun
deciduous Eurasian tree with edible fruit
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛd.lɚ triː / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lə ˌtriː / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Eurasia, cultivated for its apple-like edible fruit.

Examples

A medlar tree fruit
A centuries-old medlar tree still blossoms in the village square every spring.
Farmers planted a row of medlar trees to harvest the unusual fruit each autumn.
The medlar tree thrives best in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
