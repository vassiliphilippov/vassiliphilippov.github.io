skald

noun
Norse poet
9.9
Pronunciation: / skɑld / (AmE), / skɑːld / (BrE)
a poet and storyteller in ancient or medieval Scandinavian culture, especially one who composed and recited heroic poems

Examples

The skald told stories
The skald entertained the Viking chieftain with tales of ancient battles.
In the great hall, a skald recited poetry about the gods and heroes.
Many legends from Norse mythology were preserved by the words of a skald.
The skald entertained the Viking chieftain's guests with stories of legendary battles.
In old Norse courts, a skald was highly respected for his poetic skill.
The saga was preserved thanks to a talented skald who memorized every verse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bard - a poet and singer, especially in ancient times
  • synonym: minstrel - a medieval singer or musician who performed poetry or music
  • synonym: storyteller - a person who tells stories
