possessive pronoun

noun
pronoun indicating possession
4.6 1.8
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈze.sɪv ˈproʊ.naʊn/ (AmE), /pə.ˈze.sɪv ˈprəʊ.naʊn/ (BrE)
a pronoun that indicates possession or ownership, such as mine, yours, hers, theirs, etc.

Examples

English possessive pronouns
English possessive pronouns include mine, yours, his, hers, ours, and theirs.
In the sentence 'This book is mine,' the word 'mine' is a possessive pronoun.
She forgot her umbrella, so I lent her mine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: personal pronoun - a pronoun referring to specific persons or things (I, you, he, she, etc.)
  • synonym: possessive determiner - a word like 'my', 'your', 'her', etc., that comes before a noun to show possession
