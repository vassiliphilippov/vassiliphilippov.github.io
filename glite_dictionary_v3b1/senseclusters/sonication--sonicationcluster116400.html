sonication

noun
sound-based agitation
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɒnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of using sound energy (such as ultrasound) to agitate, disrupt, or break up particles in a sample or liquid, often for scientific or cleaning purposes

Examples

Lab sonication process
The scientist used sonication to break apart the cell walls in the sample.
Proper sonication can help dissolve stubborn particles in laboratory solutions.
After sonication, the mixture became much more uniform and easier to analyze.
The laboratory used sonication to disperse the particles evenly in the solution.
After sonication, the glassware was free of any remaining contaminants.
Researchers rely on sonication to break up cell membranes during sample preparation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centrifugation - the process of separating substances using centrifugal force
  • synonym: cleaning - the act of making something free from dirt or impurities
  • synonym: ultrasonication - the use of ultrasound waves to agitate particles in a sample
