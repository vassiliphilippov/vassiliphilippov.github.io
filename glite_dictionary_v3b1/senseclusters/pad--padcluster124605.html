pad

verb
walk heavily and steadily
Pronunciation: / pæd / (AmE), / pæd / (BrE)
to walk heavily and steadily, especially when tired or moving through something like mud

Examples

To pad through mud
She padded slowly up the steep path, her steps sinking into the soft earth.
The tired workers padded home in their heavy boots after a long day.
We padded through the muddy field after the rainstorm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plod - to walk doggedly and slowly with heavy steps
  • synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically because of exhaustion or harsh conditions
