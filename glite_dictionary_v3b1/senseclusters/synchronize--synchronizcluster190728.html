synchronize

verb
arrange events to coincide in time
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪz /; / ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make two or more things happen at the same time or rate, or to coordinate their timing

Examples

To synchronize clocks
Historians often synchronize events from various cultures to understand their global context.
Scholars synchronize different historical timelines to compare significant milestones.
The teacher tried to synchronize the class clocks to ensure everyone finished at the same time.
The dancers synchronized their movements perfectly during the performance.
The two computers synchronize automatically every night to back up data.
We need to synchronize our watches before starting the mission.
The clocks in the train station synchronize every morning to ensure accuracy.
The two events synchronized perfectly, making the celebration even more special.
They tried to synchronize their movements during the dance performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: align - arrange things in a straight line or in correct relative positions
  • synonym: coincide - to happen at the same time
  • synonym: coordinate - organize different elements to work together effectively
  • antonym: desynchronise - cause to become unsynchronized or out of time with each other
  • antonym: desynchronize - to cause to be out of sync or not occur at the same time
