unveil

verb
remove a veil from someone
3.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈveɪl / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪl / (BrE)
to remove a veil or covering from a person or object, especially in a ceremony, to show it for the first time

Examples

To unveil the bride
In some traditions, brides unveil themselves at the end of the wedding ceremony.
Many women chose not to unveil themselves in front of strangers.
The actress was reluctant to unveil herself to the public after years of seclusion.
At the ceremony, the mayor helped unveil the commemorative plaque.
She was nervous as they prepared to unveil her latest sculpture to the public.
The museum will unveil the newly restored painting next week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reveal - to make known or show something previously hidden
  • antonym: veil - cover with or as if with a veil
