anachronistic

adjective
chronologically out of place
8.1 9.6
Pronunciation: / əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists; chronologically misplaced

Examples

An anachronistic idea
His views on gender roles are considered anachronistic by many people today.
That ancient typewriter looks positively anachronistic in a modern workspace.
The use of candles for lighting in the office seemed anachronistic in the age of electricity.
