woodenness

noun
stiff or awkward quality
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdənnəs / (AmE), / ˈwʊdənnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being stiff, awkward, or lacking naturalness, especially in speech, behavior, or performance

Examples

The woodenness of speech
Critics noted the woodenness of the dialogue, which made the film feel less engaging.
Despite his charming smile, there was a woodenness in his voice that betrayed his nervousness.
The woodenness of her performance made the emotional scenes unconvincing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: awkwardness - the quality of being awkward; lack of grace or smoothness
  • antonym: naturalness - the quality of being natural; absence of artificiality or stiffness
