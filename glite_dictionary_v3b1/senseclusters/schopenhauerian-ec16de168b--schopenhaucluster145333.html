Schopenhauerian

adjective
of Schopenhauer's philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˌʃoʊpənˈhaʊəriən / (AmE), / ˌʃəʊpənˈhaʊəriən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas, philosophy, or style of Arthur Schopenhauer, especially his views on pessimism and human desire

Examples

Schopenhauerian philosophy
The novel presents a Schopenhauerian outlook, emphasizing the futility of human striving.
Her argument was deeply Schopenhauerian, focusing on the role of suffering in life.
Many critics noted the Schopenhauerian themes of pessimism and desire in his latest work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kantian - relating to the philosophy of Immanuel Kant
  • synonym: Nietzschean - relating to the philosophy of Friedrich Nietzsche
