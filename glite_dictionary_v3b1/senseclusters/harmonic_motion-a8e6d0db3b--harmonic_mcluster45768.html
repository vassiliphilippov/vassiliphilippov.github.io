harmonic motion

noun
periodic movement around an equilibrium point
1.0
Pronunciation: / hɑːrˈmɑːnɪk ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒnɪk ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
a type of periodic motion in which an object moves back and forth symmetrically around an equilibrium point, or as the sum of such motions

Examples

Harmonic motion pattern
Engineers study harmonic motion to design safer buildings in earthquake-prone areas.
Simple harmonic motion is a fundamental concept in introductory physics.
The pendulum of a clock exhibits harmonic motion under ideal conditions.
