Germanize

verb
to make German
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɜːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone more German in language, culture, or style (for example, by changing names, translating, or adopting German customs)

Examples

To Germanize names
The town decided to Germanize its street names after the new settlers arrived.
Many immigrants Germanized their surnames to better fit into local society.
The company chose to Germanize its product packaging for the German market.
The government attempted to Germanize the region by changing street names and teaching only in German.
Policies were introduced to Germanize local customs and traditions over several decades.
Some families resisted efforts to Germanize their language and continued to speak their native tongue at home.
The government tried to Germanize the local schools by changing the language of instruction.
During the occupation, many street names were Germanized to reflect the new rulers.
Some families resisted efforts to Germanize their traditions and continued to celebrate their own holidays.
