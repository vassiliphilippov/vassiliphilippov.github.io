stakeout

noun
police surveillance of a place or person
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪkˌaʊt / (BrE)
a period of secret surveillance by the police or authorities, usually of a place, in order to observe illegal activity or catch a criminal

Examples

Police stakeout
After hours of stakeout, the police finally caught the burglar in the act.
The detectives set up a stakeout across the street from the suspect's house.
We watched a crime show featuring a tense stakeout that lasted through the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surveillance - close observation, especially of a suspected person
