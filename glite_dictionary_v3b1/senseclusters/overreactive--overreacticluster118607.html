overreactive

adjective
excessively responsive
6.3
Pronunciation: /oʊ.və.riː.ˈæk.tɪv/ /; / /oʊ.və.rɪ.ˈæk.tɪv/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.riː.ˈæk.tɪv/ /; / /əʊ.və.rɪ.ˈæk.tɪv/ (BrE)
responding too strongly or with excessive intensity to something (emotionally, physically, or in a system)

Examples

An overreactive response
Her overreactive response surprised everyone at the meeting.
Parents can sometimes be overreactive when their children make mistakes.
Try not to be overreactive to minor setbacks at work.
The overreactive smoke detector went off every time someone cooked in the kitchen.
Her overreactive immune system caused allergies to even mild substances.
The security system was so overreactive that it sent alerts for harmless movements.
An overreactive immune system can lead to allergies and other health problems.
Doctors monitored the patient for any overreactive responses after the new medication was administered.
Some people have an overreactive skin reaction to certain soaps or detergents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing strong emotions or excitement
  • synonym: hypersensitive - extremely sensitive to stimuli or criticism
  • synonym: overreact - to respond more strongly than is appropriate
  • synonym: overreacting - responding more strongly than necessary
  • antonym: underreactive - showing less response than normal
  • antonym: unresponsive - not reacting or responding to stimuli
