hyperimmune

adjective
excessive immune response
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərɪˈmjun / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərɪˈmjuːn / (BrE)
having an abnormally strong or exaggerated immune response that may cause tissue damage or illness

Examples

Hyperimmune reaction
The doctor explained that a hyperimmune reaction could lead to serious complications after the infection.
Researchers are studying why some patients develop a hyperimmune response to certain medications.
A hyperimmune system can sometimes attack healthy tissues, causing autoimmune diseases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypoimmune - having a weak or deficient immune response
  • antonym: immunocompromised - having a weakened or impaired immune system
