Gaspar

proper noun
one of the Magi who visited Jesus
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæspɑr / (AmE), / ˈɡæspɑː / (BrE)
In Christian tradition, one of the three Magi or wise men who visited the infant Jesus after his birth, typically identified as the bearer of gold.

Examples

Gaspar the Magi
A stained-glass window in the chapel depicted Gaspar alongside the other two wise men.
Every Christmas pageant at the church, I was assigned to play Gaspar, carrying the box of gold.
The legend says that Gaspar traveled from India to honor the newborn king with his gift.
