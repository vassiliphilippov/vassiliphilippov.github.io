British English

noun
English as used in the United Kingdom
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
The form of English language spoken and written in the United Kingdom, including its distinctive vocabulary, spelling, and usage.

Examples

British English spelling
Many language learners study British English because they plan to move to the UK.
She grew up speaking British English, so she uses words like "lorry" instead of "truck".
The spelling of "colour" in British English includes a 'u', unlike American usage.
