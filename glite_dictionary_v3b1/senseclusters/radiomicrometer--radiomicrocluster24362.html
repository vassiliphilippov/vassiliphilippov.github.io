radiomicrometer

noun
very sensitive radiometer
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˌoʊmaɪˈkɹɑːmɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊmaɪˈkrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An extremely sensitive radiometer used to detect and measure very small amounts of radiant energy.

Examples

A radiomicrometer device
Because of its high sensitivity, a radiomicrometer is ideal for measuring very weak sources of light.
Early astronomical observations relied on the radiomicrometer to register minimal heat from distant stars.
The scientist used a radiomicrometer to detect the faint energy emitted by the sample.
