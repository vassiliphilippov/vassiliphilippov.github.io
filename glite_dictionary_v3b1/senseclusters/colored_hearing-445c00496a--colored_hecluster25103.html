colored hearing

noun
perceiving colors in response to sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚd ˈhɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd ˈhɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
A form of synesthesia in which the perception of sounds is accompanied by experiences of color.

Examples

Colored hearing experience
Her colored hearing allows her to associate each musical note with a specific hue.
Researchers study colored hearing to understand links between the senses in the human brain.
Some people with synesthesia experience colored hearing, seeing flashes of color when they hear music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromesthesia - synesthesia where sounds evoke colors
