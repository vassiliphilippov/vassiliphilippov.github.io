blowdown

noun
controlled fluid release
Pronunciation: / ˈbloʊˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌdaʊn / (BrE)
the controlled release or removal of water, gas, or other fluid from a boiler, pipeline, or pressure vessel to reduce pressure or remove impurities

Examples

Boiler blowdown
The technician performed a blowdown to remove built-up impurities from the boiler.
Regular blowdown is necessary to maintain the efficiency of industrial steam systems.
After the blowdown, the pressure inside the vessel returned to safe levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drainage - the process of removing liquid from a system
  • synonym: venting - the release of gas or vapor from a system
