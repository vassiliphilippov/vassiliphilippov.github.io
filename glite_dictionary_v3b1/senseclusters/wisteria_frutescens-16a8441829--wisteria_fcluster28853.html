Wisteria frutescens

noun
North American wisteria species
Pronunciation: / wɪˈstɪəriə fruˈtɛsəns / (AmE), / wɪˈstɪəriə fruˈtesəns / (BrE)
A species of wisteria native to the eastern United States, known for its pale purple-lilac flowers.

Examples

Botanists recommend Wisteria frutescens for native plant gardens because of its resilience and beauty.

Unlike the Asian wisteria, Wisteria frutescens is less invasive and better suited to North American climates.
