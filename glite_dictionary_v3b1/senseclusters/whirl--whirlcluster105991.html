whirl

verb
cause to spin or rotate
2.1 2.4
Pronunciation: /wɜːrl/ /; / /hwɜːrl/ (AmE), /wɜːl/ /; / /hwɜːl/ (BrE)
to spin or move rapidly in a circular motion, or to make something do this

Examples

To whirl a pencil
She used her fingers to whirl the pencil on the table.
The magician whirled the coins in his palm before making them disappear.
You can whirl the spinner to start the game.
Leaves whirled through the air as the autumn wind picked up.
She felt dizzy after whirling so quickly on the dance floor.
The children began to whirl around the playground, laughing with delight.
Children laughed as they whirled around on the merry-go-round.
Smoke whirled upwards, disappearing into the blue sky.
The wind caused the leaves to whirl through the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spin - to turn around and around quickly
  • antonym: stand still - to stop moving, remain motionless
  • synonym: swirl - to move in a twisting or spiraling pattern
  • synonym: twirl - to turn something repeatedly around or quickly in a circle
