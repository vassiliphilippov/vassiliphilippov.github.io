lahar

noun
volcanic mudflow or debris flow
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɑˈhɑr / (AmE), / lɑːˈhɑː / (BrE)
A destructive flow of volcanic water and mud down the slopes of a volcano, often triggered by heavy rainfall or the melting of snow and ice during an eruption.

Examples

A lahar flow
Heavy rains after the eruption increased the risk of a lahar sweeping down the mountain.
Scientists monitored the volcano closely for signs of another lahar following the sudden snowmelt.
The eruption triggered a deadly lahar that destroyed several villages along the river valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mudflow - A flow of water-saturated earth material, not necessarily volcanic in origin
