woolly indris

noun
nocturnal lemur with thick fur and long tail
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli ˈɪndrɪs / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈɪndrɪs / (BrE)
A type of nocturnal lemur with thick grey-brown fur and a long tail, native to Madagascar.

Examples

Woolly indris lemurs
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitats of woolly indris from deforestation.
The woolly indris are known for their haunting calls at night in the rainforests of Madagascar.
Unlike some other lemurs, woolly indris have particularly thick fur to keep them warm in the cool nights.
