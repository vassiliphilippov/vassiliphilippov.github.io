skim off

verb
take the best from a group
Pronunciation: / skɪm ɑf / (AmE), / skɪm ɒf / (BrE)
to select and take the best people or things from a group, leaving others behind

Examples

To skim off the best
Critics say the policy helps banks skim off the wealthiest clients, leaving poorer customers with fewer options.
Elite universities often skim off the top students from high schools across the country.
The new company managed to skim off the best engineers from its competitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cherry-pick - to selectively choose the best or most desirable items, people, or ideas
