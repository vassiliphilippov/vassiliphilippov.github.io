ethosuximide

noun
anticonvulsant drug for epilepsy
Pronunciation: / ˌiːθoʊˈsʌksɪˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌiːθəʊˈsʌksɪˌmaɪd / (BrE)
an anticonvulsant medication used to treat absence (petit mal) seizures in epilepsy

Examples

Ethosuximide medication
Many patients with childhood epilepsy respond well to ethosuximide.
Researchers are studying the long-term effects of ethosuximide therapy in children.
The doctor prescribed ethosuximide to control her absence seizures.
