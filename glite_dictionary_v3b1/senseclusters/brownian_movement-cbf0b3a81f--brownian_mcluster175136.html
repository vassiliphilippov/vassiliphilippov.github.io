Brownian movement

noun
random motion of particles in a fluid
3.6 6.1
Pronunciation: /braʊ.ˈniː.ən ˈmuːv.mənt/ (AmE), /braʊ.ˈniː.ən ˈmuːv.mənt/ (BrE)
the random movement of microscopic particles suspended in a fluid, caused by collisions with molecules of the fluid

Examples

Brownian movement of particles
Physicists study Brownian movement to better understand the behavior of particles at a microscopic level.
The Brownian movement of pollen grains in water can be observed under a microscope.
Tiny dust particles displayed Brownian movement when illuminated by a beam of sunlight.
