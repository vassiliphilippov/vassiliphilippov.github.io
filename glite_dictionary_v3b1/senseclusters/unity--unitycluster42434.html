unity

noun
state of being unified or whole
2.8 24/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàjuÀên…ôti / (AmE), / ÀàjuÀên…ôti /; / ÀàjuÀên…™ti / (BrE)
the state of being joined together or forming a whole, especially in agreement, harmony, or being undivided

Examples

National unity
After the merger, there was a strong sense of unity among the employees.
National unity is crucial during times of crisis.
The leaders emphasized the importance of unity in overcoming national challenges.
The country's unity was threatened by internal conflict.
The piece achieves a sense of unity through its consistent style.
We must strive for unity in our team to succeed.

Synonyms

  • oneness
    6.5 2.28/m
    oneness means being a single, undivided whole, while unity refers to being joined together or agreeing as a group.
  • unicity
    9.6 0.03/m
    unicity names the abstract idea of being one and undivided, while unity is the common word for joined agreement or wholeness.
  • union
    2.7 11/m
    union is a joining of parts or groups into one organized whole, while unity stresses agreement and harmony among them.
  • cohesion
    4.8 3.87/m
    cohesion is the force or quality that keeps parts stuck together, while unity is a broader state of being joined and agreed.
  • wholeness
    3.9 2.77/m
    wholeness is the feeling or fact of being complete and undivided, while unity highlights being joined or in agreement.
  • integrity
    4.4 8.25/m
    integrity means being whole and sound or honest in character, while unity focuses on parts joining and shared agreement.
  • harmony
    2.5 11/m
    harmony is a friendly, working agreement where things fit well, while unity describes being joined together as a single whole.
  • solidarity
    5.2 10/m
    solidarity is active support and shared purpose among group members, while unity means general joining or agreed togetherness.
