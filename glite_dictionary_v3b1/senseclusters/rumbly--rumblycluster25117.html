rumbly

adjective
low, continuous sound
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌmbli / (AmE), / ˈrʌmbli / (BrE)
making a low, deep, and often continuous sound, like thunder or a hungry stomach

Examples

A rumbly sound
The distant thunder sounded rumbly as the storm approached the city.
After skipping lunch, his stomach became quite rumbly during the meeting.
We heard a rumbly noise coming from the old washing machine in the basement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
  • synonym: rumbling - making a deep, resonant, and continuous sound
