primordialism

noun
belief in origins
9.9
Pronunciation: / praɪˈmɔːrdiəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / praɪˈmɔːdiəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that something (especially social bonds like ethnicity) is ancient, original, and rooted in the earliest stages of history

Examples

Primordialism theory
The theory of primordialism suggests that certain social bonds are rooted in ancient origins.
Some anthropologists debate whether primordialism or social construction better explains ethnic identity.
In literature, primordialism often appears in stories about the dawn of civilization.
Many scholars debate whether primordialism or social construction better explains the origins of ethnic groups.
Critics argue that primordialism overlooks the ways in which identities can change over time.
The idea of primordialism has influenced discussions about nationalism and cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructivism - the theory that social bonds like ethnicity are created by social processes
  • antonym: modernism - belief in or emphasis on modern ideas and methods
  • synonym: primitivism - belief in or emphasis on the earliest or simplest forms
