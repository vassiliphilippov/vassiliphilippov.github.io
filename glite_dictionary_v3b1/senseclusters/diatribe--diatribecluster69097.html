diatribe

noun
forceful verbal attack
8.7 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌtraɪb / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ətraɪb / (BrE)
a forceful and bitter verbal attack against someone or something

Examples

A diatribe against injustice
After listening to his diatribe, she decided not to respond and left the room quietly.
Her angry diatribe shocked everyone at the meeting.
The politician launched into a diatribe against his opponents during the debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: panegyric - a public speech or written text in praise of someone or something
