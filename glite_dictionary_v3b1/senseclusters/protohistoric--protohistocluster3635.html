protohistoric

adjective
early written period
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtoʊhɪˈstɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊhɪˈstɒrɪk / (BrE)
relating to a period in a culture's development when some written records exist but a complete historical record is lacking

Examples

Protohistoric period
The region's protohistoric artifacts reveal the gradual adoption of writing systems.
Researchers found protohistoric inscriptions that helped bridge the gap between prehistory and history.
Many protohistoric sites show evidence of both oral traditions and early written records.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: historic - relating to recorded history
  • degree lower: prehistoric - relating to the time before written records
