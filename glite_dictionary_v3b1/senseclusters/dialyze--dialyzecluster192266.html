dialyze

verb
separate by dialysis
8.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.ə.laɪz/ /; / /ˈdaɪ.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.ə.laɪz/ /; / /ˈdaɪ.laɪz/ (BrE)
to separate substances in a solution by means of dialysis, often to remove unwanted materials in medical procedures or laboratory processes, especially using a semipermeable membrane

Examples

To dialyze blood
Doctors decided to dialyze the patient’s blood to eliminate toxins.
Researchers dialyzed the sample and then tested its purity.
The laboratory technician will dialyze the protein solution overnight to remove small molecules.
Many patients with kidney failure need to dialyse regularly to remove toxins from their blood.
Researchers often dialyse cell extracts before further biochemical analysis.
The laboratory technician used a membrane to dialyse the solution, separating small molecules from proteins.
