rhema

noun
word or utterance
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.mə/ /; / /ˈreɪ.mə/ (AmE), /ˈriː.mə/ /; / /ˈreɪ.mə/ (BrE)
the part of a sentence that gives new information about the subject, usually the predicate, in grammar or linguistics

Examples

Sentence rhema
In linguistics, a rhema is the part of a sentence that provides new information.
The pastor spoke about receiving a personal rhema during prayer.
Scholars debate the distinction between rhema and other units of meaning in language.
In the sentence 'The sun rises', 'rises' is the rhema that tells us what the sun does.
Linguists often analyze the rhema to understand how new information is presented in a sentence.
The rhema usually follows the theme and provides the main message of the statement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: logos - in theology, the Word of God or principle of divine reason
  • synonym: predicate - the part of a sentence that tells something about the subject
  • antonym: theme - the part of a sentence that indicates what is being talked about, usually the subject
  • synonym: utterance - a spoken word, statement, or vocal sound
