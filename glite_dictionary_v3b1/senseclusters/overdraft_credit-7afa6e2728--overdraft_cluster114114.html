overdraft credit

noun
credit covering account overdrafts
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərdræft ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvədrɑːft ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
credit provided by a bank to cover withdrawals that exceed a customer's account balance

Examples

Use overdraft credit
Businesses often rely on overdraft credit to manage temporary cash shortages.
She arranged an overdraft credit with her bank to avoid rejected payments.
The bank charges interest on your overdraft credit if you exceed your account balance.
