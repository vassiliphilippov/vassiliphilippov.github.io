robbery

noun
crime of theft by force or threat
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːbəri / (AmE), / ˈrɒbəri / (BrE)
the crime of stealing property from a person or place using force or the threat of violence

Examples

Armed robbery
He was charged with robbery after threatening the shop owner with a weapon.
Reports of robbery in the city have increased significantly this year.
The bank robbery was carefully planned by the criminals.
Robbery was rampant as soldiers moved through the conquered villages.
During the chaos of the uprising, widespread robbery broke out in the city.
Many historical accounts detail the robbery committed by invading armies during wartime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burglary - the unlawful entry into a building, typically to commit theft
  • synonym: larceny - theft of personal property
  • synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
  • synonym: theft - the act of stealing property
