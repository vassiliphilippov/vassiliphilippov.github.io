propensity

noun
natural tendency or inclination
9.5
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɛnsəti / (AmE), / prəˈpɛnsəti /; / prəˈpɛnsɪti / (BrE)
a natural tendency or inclination to behave in a particular way

Examples

Strong propensity for mistakes
He has a strong propensity for forgetting people’s names.
Scientists are studying the propensity for certain diseases to run in families.
Some people have a propensity to eat when they’re stressed.
His propensity for procrastination often led to last-minute stress.
She has a strong propensity for saving money, even on small purchases.
Some children have a propensity to ask a lot of questions.
Due to his propensity toward risk-taking, he often found himself in unpredictable situations.
She has a propensity for staying up late despite her early work schedule.
The company's propensity to innovate set it apart from its competitors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • synonym: inclination - a tendency to do something
  • synonym: proclivity - a tendency to choose or do something regularly; an inclination
