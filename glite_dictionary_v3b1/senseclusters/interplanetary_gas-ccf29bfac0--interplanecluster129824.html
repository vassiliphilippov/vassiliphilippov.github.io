interplanetary gas

noun
gas and plasma in space between planets
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈplænəˌtɛri ɡæs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈplænɪtəri ɡæs / (BrE)
the thin stream of gas and charged particles, mainly plasma, that flows through space between planets, especially as part of the solar wind

Examples

Interplanetary gas flow
Interplanetary gas plays a key role in shaping the behavior of the solar wind.
Scientists study interplanetary gas to better understand the dynamics of the solar system.
The spacecraft detected changes in the interplanetary gas as it traveled beyond Earth's orbit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solar wind - a stream of charged particles released from the sun's atmosphere
