frame of reference

noun
system of assumptions or context for interpretation
3.9 4.7
Pronunciation: /freɪm əv ˈrɛf.ər.əns/ (AmE), /freɪm əv ˈrɛf.ər.əns/ (BrE)
a set of ideas, assumptions, or criteria by which things are judged or understood, providing context for interpreting information or behavior

Examples

Cultural frame of reference
Cultural background often shapes a person's frame of reference when interpreting new experiences.
Her frame of reference made it difficult to appreciate perspectives different from her own.
In physics, understanding motion requires specifying the frame of reference from which measurements are made.

Related Terms

  • synonym: point of view - a particular way of thinking about or considering something
