gap

noun
low pass between hills or mountains
Pronunciation: / ɡæp / (AmE), / ɡæp /; / ɡap / (BrE)
an opening or empty space between objects, areas, or in nature (such as a pass between hills or a space in a window)

Examples

A gap between walls
A cool breeze blew through the narrow gap in the hills.
Settlers used the gap as a route through the Appalachian Mountains.
The hikers crossed the mountain gap just before noon.
A sudden gap in the clouds let sunlight stream onto the field.
Please mind the gap between the train and the platform.
The children crawled through the gap in the fence to reach the playground.
A cold draft entered the room through a gap in the window frame.
Be careful not to step in the gap between the train and the platform.
Sunlight streamed through a gap in the curtains, waking her up.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barrier - a structure or object that blocks passage
  • synonym: crack - a very narrow opening, usually a long, thin one
  • synonym: hole - an opening through something; a gap or break in a surface
  • synonym: opening - an open space that allows passage or access
  • synonym: pass - a way through a range of mountains
