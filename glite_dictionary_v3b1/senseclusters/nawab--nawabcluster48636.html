nawab

noun
Mughal-era Indian governor or nobleman
5.5
Pronunciation: / nəˈwɑb / (AmE), / nəˈwɑːb / (BrE)
a Muslim governor or nobleman in India during the Mughal empire, often ruling a province or territory

Examples

The nawab of Bengal
Stories of the extravagant lifestyle of the nawab are still told in the region.
The British East India Company negotiated treaties with various nawabs across the subcontinent.
The palace once belonged to a prominent nawab during the height of the Mughal empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nabob - a wealthy and influential person, originally one who returned to Europe from India with a fortune; sometimes used synonymously with nawab
