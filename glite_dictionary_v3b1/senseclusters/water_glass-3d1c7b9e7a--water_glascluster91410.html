water glass

noun
water-level gauge for tanks or boilers
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a gauge used to indicate the level of water in a tank, boiler, or reservoir

Examples

Boiler water glass
A clogged water glass can give misleading readings and should be cleaned regularly.
The water glass showed that more water needed to be added to the tank.
The engineer checked the water glass to ensure the boiler was at a safe level.
