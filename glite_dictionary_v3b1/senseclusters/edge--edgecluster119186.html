edge

noun
sharp border of a surface
Pronunciation: / ɛdʒ / (AmE), / ɛdʒ / (BrE)
the outer line, border, or limit of an object, area, or surface, often sharp or distinct

Examples

Sharp edge
Be careful not to cut yourself on the edge of the metal sheet.

A fence runs along the edge of the park to mark its boundary.
She stood at the edge of the cliff, looking down at the sea below.
The dog chased the ball to the edge of the garden.
She carefully trimmed the edge of the paper to make it perfectly straight.
The children played near the edge of the playground, away from the swings.
They admired the view from the edge of the cliff.
