motion-picture camera

noun
camera for filming sequences; makes movies
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.ʃən ˈpɪk.tʃər ˈkæm.rə/ /; / /ˈmoʊ.ʃən ˈpɪk.tʃər ˈkæm.ə.rə/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.ʃən ˈpɪk.tʃə ˈkæm.rə/ /; / /ˈməʊ.ʃən ˈpɪk.tʃə ˈkæm.ə.rə/ (BrE)
A camera designed to take a rapid sequence of photographs for producing the illusion of motion when projected as a film.

Examples

A motion-picture camera device
Advances in technology have made the motion-picture camera lighter and more portable.
Early filmmakers used hand-cranked motion-picture cameras to record silent movies.
The director positioned the motion-picture camera carefully to capture the perfect shot.
