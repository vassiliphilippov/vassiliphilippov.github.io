imperishability

noun
resistance to decay or destruction
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpɛrɪʃəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛrɪʃəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of not decaying or being destroyed; resistance to deterioration

Examples

The imperishability of goods
Marketing campaigns often stress the imperishability of their products to attract customers.
Some people believe in the imperishability of the human soul.
The imperishability of ancient monuments has fascinated historians for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: durability - ability to withstand wear or decay over time
  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
