thrust

verb
push or force suddenly
Pronunciation: / θrʌst / (AmE), / θrʌst / (BrE)
to push or move something or someone suddenly and forcefully, often forward or into something

Examples

To thrust a sword
He thrust his hand forward to greet the visitor.
She thrust her keys into her bag and hurried out the door.
The nurse thrust the thermometer under the patient's arm.
He thrust his hand into his pocket and pulled out a coin.
She thrust open the door and ran outside.
The soldier thrust his sword forward with great force.
He thrust the knife deep into the loaf of bread to cut it.
She thrust the scissors into the thick package to open it.
The soldier thrust his bayonet through the enemy's defenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insert - to put something into something else
  • synonym: pierce - to go into or through something, especially with a sharp object
  • antonym: pull - to draw or haul toward oneself
  • synonym: push - to move something away from oneself, typically with force
  • synonym: shove - push someone or something roughly
  • synonym: stab - to push a pointed object into something or someone, often to injure
  • antonym: withdraw - remove or take something back or away
