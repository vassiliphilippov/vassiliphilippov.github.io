obliquus

noun
slanting muscle
9.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈblɪ.kwəs/ /; / /ə.ˈbliː.kwəs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈblɪ.kwəs/ /; / /ɒ.ˈblɪ.kwəs/ (BrE)
a muscle with slanting fibers, especially referring to certain muscles in the body such as the oblique muscles of the abdomen

Examples

Obliquus muscle
The obliquus externus abdominis is important for twisting movements of the torso.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the obliquus muscles in detail.
Injury to the obliquus can affect your ability to bend sideways.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rectus - a muscle with straight fibers
