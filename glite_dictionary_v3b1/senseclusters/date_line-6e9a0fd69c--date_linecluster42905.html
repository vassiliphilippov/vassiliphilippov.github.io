date line

noun
imaginary line where calendar date changes
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪt ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪt ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
an imaginary line on the Earth's surface, running from the North to the South Pole near the 180th meridian, where the date changes by one day when crossed.

Examples

The date line
Flights crossing the date line often arrive before they depart, due to time zone changes.
The date line is significant for coordinating global time and international travel.
When the ship crossed the date line, the crew had to adjust their clocks and calendars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: International Date Line - the specific term for the internationally recognized date-changing line
