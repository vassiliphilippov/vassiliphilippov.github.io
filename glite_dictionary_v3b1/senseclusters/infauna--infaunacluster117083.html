infauna

noun
sediment-dwelling animals
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔnə / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːnə / (BrE)
animals or small organisms that live within the sediments at the bottom of a sea, river, lake, or other body of water

Examples

Infauna in sediments
Many types of infauna play a crucial role in recycling nutrients in marine ecosystems.
Researchers collected samples of infauna from the muddy riverbed to study their diversity.
Clams and certain worms are common examples of infauna found beneath the ocean floor.
Scientists collected samples of infauna to monitor the health of the lake ecosystem.
The diversity of infauna can indicate the level of pollution in river sediments.
Many types of infauna play a crucial role in breaking down organic matter underwater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthos - organisms living on or in the bottom of a water body
  • antonym: epifauna - animals that live on the surface of the sea floor or river beds
