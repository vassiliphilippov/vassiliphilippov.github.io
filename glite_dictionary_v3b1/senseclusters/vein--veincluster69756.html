vein

noun
vascular bundle in a plant leaf or organ
7.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /veɪn/ (AmE), /veɪn/ (BrE)
any of the vascular bundles or ribs that form the branching framework of conducting and supporting tissues in a leaf or other plant organ

Examples

Leaf vein
Some insects feed on the sap flowing inside the veins of leaves.
The maple leaf's prominent veins are clearly visible against the sunlight.
Water moves through the veins of the plant, nourishing every part of the leaf.
