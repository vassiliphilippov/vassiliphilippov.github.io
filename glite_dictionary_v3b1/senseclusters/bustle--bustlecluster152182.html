bustle

noun
support used to puff out a woman's skirt
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌsəl / (BrE)
A framework worn at the back below the waist to give fullness to a woman's skirt, especially in late 19th-century fashion.

Examples

A bustle skirt
Fashion in the 1880s was characterized by the prominence of the bustle beneath women's skirts.
She wore a replica gown with a padded bustle to achieve the period look.
The museum exhibits included a Victorian dress complete with a bustle.
