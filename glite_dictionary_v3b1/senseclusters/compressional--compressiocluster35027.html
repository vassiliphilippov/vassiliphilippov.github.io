compressional

adjective
caused by compression
Pronunciation: / kəmˈprɛʃənəl / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by compression, especially in the context of physical forces or waves

Examples

Compressional waves
Seismic waves can be classified as compressional or shear waves depending on their movement.
The compressional strength of the material determines how much force it can withstand before deforming.
Engineers studied the compressional properties of the soil before building the foundation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressive - relating to or involving compression
  • antonym: tensional - relating to or caused by tension
