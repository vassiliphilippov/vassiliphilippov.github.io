tolerability

noun
ability to withstand stress
6.7
Pronunciation: /tə.lə.rə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /tɒ.lə.rə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the degree to which something (such as a treatment or medication) can be accepted or endured without causing unacceptable problems or side effects

Improving the tolerability of chemotherapy can help patients complete their courses.
