tolerability

noun
ability to withstand stress
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑːlərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌtɒlərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the degree to which something (such as a treatment or medication) can be accepted or endured without causing unacceptable problems or side effects

The tolerability of the new medication was evaluated during clinical trials.
Public tolerability of noise from construction sites varies from city to city.

The tolerability of the new drug was evaluated during clinical trials.
Doctors are concerned about the tolerability of this treatment in elderly patients.
Improving the tolerability of chemotherapy can help patients complete their courses.
