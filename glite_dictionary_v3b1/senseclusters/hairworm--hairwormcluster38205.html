hairworm

noun
parasitic thin worm
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈheəˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a long, thin parasitic worm, often found infecting insects and resembling a strand of hair

Examples

A hairworm parasite
The scientist discovered a hairworm living inside the body of a grasshopper.
After heavy rain, several hairworms were seen wriggling in the puddles.
A hairworm can control the behavior of its insect host to reach water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nematode - a type of roundworm, many of which are parasitic
  • antonym: tapeworm - a flat, parasitic worm that lives in the intestines of animals
