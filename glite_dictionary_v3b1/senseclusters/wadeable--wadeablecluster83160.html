wadeable

adjective
shallow enough to walk
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪdəbəl / (BrE)
shallow enough to be crossed on foot by walking through the water

Examples

A wadeable stream
The stream is wadeable in summer when the water level is low.
We looked for a wadeable section of the river to cross safely.
During the dry season, the lake becomes wadeable for hikers and animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deep - having a large distance from the top to the bottom; not shallow
  • synonym: fordable - shallow enough to be crossed by walking or driving
