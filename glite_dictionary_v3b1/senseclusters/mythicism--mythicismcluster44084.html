mythicism

noun
belief in myth, not history
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪθɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that certain figures or events are mythical and did not exist or happen historically

Examples

Mythicism about Jesus
The theory of mythicism challenges the historical existence of certain religious leaders.
Debates about mythicism often arise in discussions of ancient history and literature.
Some authors defend mythicism by analyzing similarities between myths from different cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: historicist - someone who believes events or figures are historical
  • synonym: skepticism - doubt or disbelief about accepted ideas or claims
