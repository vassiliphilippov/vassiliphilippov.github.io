colonel

noun
military officer rank above lieutenant colonel
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.nəl / (BrE)
A commissioned military officer ranking above a lieutenant colonel and below a brigadier general, especially in the army, air force, or marines.

Examples

Army colonel
After twenty years of service, he was promoted to colonel in the Army.
She consulted with the Marine colonel about the upcoming training exercises.
The colonel addressed his troops before the mission began.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: brigadier general - military officer rank just above colonel
  • degree lower: lieutenant colonel - military officer rank just below colonel
