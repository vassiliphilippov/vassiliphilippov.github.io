torment

verb
inflict severe suffering
7.2 5.75/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îrÀåm…õnt /; / t…îrÀàm…õnt / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀêÀåm…õnt /; / t…îÀêÀàm…õnt / (BrE)
to cause someone to suffer severe physical or mental pain or distress

Examples

To torment a person
She was tormented by memories of her past mistakes for years.
The captors tormented their prisoners with loud noises and sleepless nights.
The myth says that the gods torment the wicked in the afterlife.
He tormented the stray cat until someone told him to stop.
She felt guilty for tormenting her brother with constant pranks.
The older boys would often torment their younger classmates at recess.
He allowed his past mistakes to torment him every night.
He was tormented by doubts about his decision.
Her cruel words seemed designed to torment him emotionally.

Synonyms

  • torture
    5.7 21/m
    torture means causing extreme, often deliberate physical pain, while torment covers severe physical or mental suffering.
  • excruciate
    9.9 0.46/m
    excruciate means causing very intense physical or mental pain, while torment is a broad word for severe suffering.
  • rack
    7.5 2.12/m
    rack means to cause great mental or physical anguish, often by strong pressure, while torment means severe suffering.
  • afflict
    6.0 6.93/m
    afflict means to cause pain or trouble to someone or something, while torment implies long or intense suffering.
  • harrow
    7.7 0.04/m
    harrow means to deeply distress someone emotionally, often in a shock, while torment can be physical or mental suffering.
  • bedevil
    8.2 0.01/m
    bedevil means to trouble or torment someone persistently, often in small ways, while torment is severe, direct suffering.
