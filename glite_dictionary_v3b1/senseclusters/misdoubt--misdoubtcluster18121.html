misdoubt

verb
to suspect or distrust
9.9
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈdaʊt/ /; / /ˈmɪs.daʊt/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈdaʊt/ /; / /ˈmɪs.daʊt/ (BrE)
to doubt or suspect something, especially in an uncertain or mistrustful way

Examples

To misdoubt a story
She began to misdoubt his explanation after hearing conflicting stories.
If you misdoubt the results, you should double-check the calculations.
They misdoubted the sincerity of the offer because it seemed too generous.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • antonym: trust - to believe that someone or something is reliable
