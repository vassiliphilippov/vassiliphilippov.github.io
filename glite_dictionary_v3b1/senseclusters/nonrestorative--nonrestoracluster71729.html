nonrestorative

adjective
not refreshing
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnrɪˈstɔrətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnrɪˈstɔːrətɪv / (BrE)
not having the effect of restoring health, energy, or a sense of well-being

Examples

Nonrestorative sleep
After several hours of nonrestorative sleep, he still felt exhausted in the morning.
Many people with insomnia complain of nonrestorative nights despite spending enough time in bed.
The treatment was discontinued because it was found to be nonrestorative for most patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: refreshing - making you feel more energetic or less tired
  • antonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health or energy
