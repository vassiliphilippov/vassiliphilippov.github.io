regressive

adjective
(of tax) decreasing rate as income rises
5.2 7.1
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈgre.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈgre.sɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈgre.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈgre.sɪv/ (BrE)
(of taxes or taxation) decreasing in rate as the amount subject to taxation increases, so that those with lower incomes pay a higher proportion of their income than those with higher incomes.

Examples

Regressive tax
Many economists criticize regressive tax systems for placing a heavier burden on low-income individuals.
Sales taxes are often considered regressive because they take a larger percentage of income from the poor than from the rich.
The government debated whether a regressive tax was fair to all citizens.

Related Terms
