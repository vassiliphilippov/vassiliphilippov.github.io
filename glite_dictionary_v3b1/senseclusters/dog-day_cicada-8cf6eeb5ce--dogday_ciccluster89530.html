dog-day cicada

noun
a cicada species heard in midsummer
Pronunciation: / dɔɡ deɪ sɪˈkeɪdə / (AmE), / dɒɡ deɪ sɪˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
a North American cicada (Tibicen canicularis) known for its distinctive song during the hot summer months of July and August

Examples

The dog-day cicada song
I spotted a dog-day cicada clinging to the bark of an oak tree.
Many children recognize summer by the sound of the dog-day cicada in the trees.
The dog-day cicada fills the air with its buzzing song every July and August.
