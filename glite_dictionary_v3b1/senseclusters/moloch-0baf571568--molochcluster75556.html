Moloch

noun
force demanding sacrifice or obedience
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊ.lɑk /; / ˈmoʊ.lɑːk / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.ək /; / ˈmɒl.ɒk / (BrE)
a powerful force, institution, or thing that demands extreme sacrifice or obedience, often at great human cost (used metaphorically)

Examples

Moloch of power
He refused to sacrifice his principles to the Moloch of political ambition.
Some view the relentless pursuit of profit as a modern Moloch demanding people's well-being.
The dictatorship became a Moloch that consumed the hopes of its citizens.
Many believe that unbridled ambition can turn a career into a Moloch demanding all of one's time and energy.
She refused to offer her happiness as a sacrifice to the Moloch of corporate success.
The city's relentless growth became a Moloch consuming neighborhoods and histories in its path.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idol - an image or representation of a god used as an object of worship
  • synonym: juggernaut - a massive, inexorable force, campaign, or object that crushes whatever is in its path
  • synonym: sacrifice - the act of giving up something valued for the sake of something else
  • synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler
