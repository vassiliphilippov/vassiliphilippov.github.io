biomaterial

noun
material from living sources
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊməˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊməˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a material made from biological sources, often used in industry or products for its renewable or biodegradable properties

Examples

Biomaterial packaging
Scientists are developing new biomaterials to replace traditional plastics in packaging.
The medical device is made from a biomaterial that safely dissolves in the body.
Using biomaterial from algae, the company created an eco-friendly alternative to foam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioplastic - a type of plastic made from renewable biological sources
  • antonym: synthetic material - a material made from non-biological, often chemical, sources
