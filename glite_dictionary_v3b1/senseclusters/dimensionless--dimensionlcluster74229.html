Examples

A dimensionless number
In mathematics, a dimensionless quantity has no units and is represented by a pure number.
The model assumes the particles are dimensionless to simplify the calculations.
A point is considered dimensionless because it has no length, width, or height.
The constant pi is a dimensionless number used in mathematics and physics.
A ratio of two lengths is dimensionless because the units cancel out.
Scientists often use dimensionless quantities to compare results from different experiments.
