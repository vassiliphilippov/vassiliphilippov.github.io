Ptolemaic dynasty

noun
royal dynasty ruling Egypt, 323–30 BC
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtoʊ.ləˈmeɪ.ɪk ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˌtɒl.ɪˈmeɪ.ɪk ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
an ancient Macedonian royal house that ruled Egypt from 323 BC to 30 BC, founded by Ptolemy I and ending with Cleopatra VII

Examples

The Ptolemaic dynasty rulers
Cleopatra was the last ruler of the Ptolemaic dynasty before Egypt became a Roman province.
Historians study the achievements and downfall of the Ptolemaic dynasty in ancient Egypt.
The Ptolemaic dynasty was marked by a fusion of Greek and Egyptian cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cleopatra - the last active ruler of the Ptolemaic dynasty of Egypt
  • synonym: Ptolemy I - founder of the Ptolemaic dynasty in Egypt
