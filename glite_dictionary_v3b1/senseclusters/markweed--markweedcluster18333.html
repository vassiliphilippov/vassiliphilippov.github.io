markweed

noun
a North American climbing plant with irritating oil
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkˌwiːd / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to eastern and central United States, with three-part leaves, greenish flowers, and white berries, whose oil can cause skin irritation.

Examples

Markweed plant leaves
He learned to identify markweed on sight after suffering a skin reaction during his first camping trip.
Many hikers avoid markweed because contact with its leaves can lead to an itchy rash.
The white berries of markweed are easily spotted along forest edges in late summer.
