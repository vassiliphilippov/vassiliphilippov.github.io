lukewarm

adjective
unenthusiastic or indifferent
4.4 0.90/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…îkÀåw…îrm / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêkÀåw…îÀêm / (BrE)
showing little enthusiasm, interest, or conviction; unenthusiastic or indifferent

Examples

Lukewarm response
He gave a lukewarm endorsement of the candidate, failing to show real support.
Her performance received only lukewarm applause from the audience.
The proposal was met with a lukewarm response from the committee.

Synonyms

  • tepid
    6.2 0.99/m
    tepid is used for a weak or mild reaction and often sounds slightly formal, while lukewarm feels more common and casual.
  • unenthusiastic
    3.9 0.33/m
    unenthusiastic describes a person showing little interest or excitement, while lukewarm also implies indifference or low energy.
  • unenthused
    5.3 0.03/m
    unenthused is an informal way to say someone shows no excitement, while lukewarm is more common in neutral speech.
  • halfhearted
    4.3 0.46/m
    halfhearted describes doing something with little effort or commitment, while lukewarm focuses on a mild or indifferent feeling.
  • lackluster
    6.1 2.29/m
    lackluster describes dull or uninspired performance or appearance, while lukewarm refers to weak feeling or interest.
