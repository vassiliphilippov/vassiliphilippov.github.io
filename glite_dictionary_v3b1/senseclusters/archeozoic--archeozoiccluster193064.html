archeozoic

adjective
earliest Precambrian geological era
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrkioʊˈzoʊɪk /; / ˌɑr.ki.oʊˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪəʊˈzəʊɪk /; / ˌɑː.ki.əʊˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the earlier of two divisions of the Precambrian era, marked by the formation of the Earth's crust and the appearance of primitive life.

Examples

The archeozoic period
Fossils from the archeozoic period are extremely rare due to their great age.
Scientists study archeozoic rocks to learn about Earth’s early history.
The archeozoic era is known for the first simple life forms.
Geologists have discovered fossils of archaeozoic bacteria in ancient rock layers.
Some of the oldest rocks on Earth are classified as archaeozoic in origin.
The archaeozoic eon predates the proterozoic and includes Earth's earliest known life.
