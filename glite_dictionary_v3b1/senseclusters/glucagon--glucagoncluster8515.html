glucagon

noun
hormone raising blood sugar
Pronunciation: / ɡluˈkɑːɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈɡluːkəɡɒn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the pancreas that raises the level of glucose in the blood, opposing the action of insulin

Examples

Glucagon hormone
The doctor administered glucagon to quickly raise the patient's blood sugar during an episode of hypoglycemia.
Unlike insulin, glucagon works to increase blood sugar by promoting the breakdown of glycogen.
When blood glucose levels drop, the pancreas releases glucagon into the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insulin - a hormone lowering blood sugar levels, produced by the pancreas
