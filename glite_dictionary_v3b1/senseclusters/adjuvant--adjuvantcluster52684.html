adjuvant

adjective
enhancing a medical treatment
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈædʒ.ə.vənt/ (AmE), /ˈædʒ.ə.vənt/ (BrE)
helping to make a treatment, drug, or process more effective; giving added support or assistance

Examples

Adjuvant therapy
Chemotherapy may be given as an adjuvant treatment to surgery for cancer.
The doctor discussed the use of adjuvant therapies to increase treatment effectiveness.
Vaccines sometimes include adjuvant ingredients to improve immune response.
In this therapy, certain drugs act in an adjuvant role to enhance the effect of the primary medication.
Physical exercise can be adjuvant to dietary changes for better health.
The seminar was intended as an adjuvant course to the main curriculum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancillary - providing necessary support to the primary activities
  • synonym: auxiliary - providing supplementary or additional help and support
  • synonym: supportive - providing additional help or reinforcement, especially in medical contexts
