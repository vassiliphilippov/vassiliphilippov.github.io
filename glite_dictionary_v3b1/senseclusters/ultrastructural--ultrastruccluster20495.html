ultrastructural

adjective
cell fine structure
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəˈstrʌktʃərəl / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈstrʌktʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to the fine details of cells, tissues, or materials that can only be seen with very high magnification, such as with an electron microscope

Examples

Ultrastructural cell details
Researchers studied the ultrastructural features of the virus to understand how it infects cells.
The ultrastructural analysis revealed damage to the cell membranes after treatment.
Advances in technology have improved our ability to observe ultrastructural changes in tissues.
The ultrastructural analysis revealed tiny changes in the cell membrane after treatment.
Researchers used electron microscopy to study the ultrastructural organization of the virus.
Damage at the ultrastructural level can affect how tissues function even if they look normal under a light microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macroscopic - large enough to be seen with the naked eye
  • synonym: microscopic - too small to be seen without a microscope
