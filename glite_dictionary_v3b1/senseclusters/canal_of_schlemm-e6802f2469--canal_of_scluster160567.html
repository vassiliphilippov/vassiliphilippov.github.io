canal of Schlemm

noun
an eye canal for draining aqueous humor
7.8
Pronunciation: / kəˌnæl əv ˈʃlɛm / (AmE), / kəˌnæl əv ˈʃlɛm / (BrE)
a circular channel in the eye that drains aqueous humor from the anterior chamber into the veins, helping to regulate intraocular pressure

Examples

Canal of Schlemm in eye
Damage to the canal of Schlemm can contribute to the development of glaucoma.
Elevated eye pressure can occur when the canal of Schlemm becomes blocked or does not function properly.
Fluid drains from the anterior chamber of the eye through the canal of Schlemm into the surrounding veins.
