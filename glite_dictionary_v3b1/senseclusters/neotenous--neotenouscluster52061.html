neotenous

adjective
retaining juvenile traits into adulthood
Pronunciation: / niˈɑːtənəs / (AmE), / niˈɒtɪnəs / (BrE)
having or relating to the retention of juvenile characteristics into adulthood, especially in animals or humans; characterized by neoteny.

Examples

A neotenous face
Her neotenous face, with its large eyes and round cheeks, gave her a youthful appearance well into adulthood.
Researchers studied the neotenous traits of axolotls, which remain in a larval state even when sexually mature.
Some amphibians display a neotenous form, retaining gills throughout their lives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neoteny - the retention of juvenile features in the adult animal
