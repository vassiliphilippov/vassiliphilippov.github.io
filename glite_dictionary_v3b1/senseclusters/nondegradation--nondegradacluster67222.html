nondegradation

noun
prevention of decline
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɛɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or policy of preventing decline in quality or condition, especially of the environment or resources

Examples

Nondegradation of air quality
The new regulations ensure the nondegradation of air quality in urban areas.
Environmental groups advocate for nondegradation policies to protect natural habitats.
Strict monitoring is required to maintain nondegradation of the region's water sources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: degradation - the process of something becoming worse or being damaged
  • synonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state
