confine

verb
restrict or keep within limits
2.6 3.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈfaɪn / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits, boundaries, or a place; to limit freedom or movement

Examples

To confine to a room
He felt confined in the cramped office space with no windows.
The disease was successfully confined to one region of the country.
The prisoners are confined to their cells for 23 hours a day.
Authorities confined the protesters to a designated area during the event.
She felt confined by the strict rules of her new workplace.
The patient was confined to his room for a week due to illness.
During the quarantine, patients were confined to their rooms for safety reasons.
The criminal was confined in a cell until his trial.
The dog was confined to the backyard while the guests were inside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expand - to increase in size, range, or amount
  • antonym: free - to set at liberty; release from restriction
  • synonym: imprison - to confine in prison; deprive of liberty
  • synonym: limit - to set a limit or restriction on
  • antonym: release - to set free from confinement or restraint
  • synonym: restrain - to prevent someone or something from moving freely
  • synonym: restrict - to put a limit on; keep under control
