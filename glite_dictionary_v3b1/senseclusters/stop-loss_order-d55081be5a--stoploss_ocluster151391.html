stop-loss order

noun
brokerage order to limit loss
7.0 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑp lɔs ˌɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈstɒp lɒs ˌɔːdə / (BrE)
An order given to a broker to sell or buy a security when its price reaches a specified level, intended to limit an investor's loss or lock in a profit.

Examples

A stop-loss order
A stop-loss order can help you stick to your investment plan during volatile market swings.
Investors use a stop-loss order as a risk management strategy to prevent significant losses.
The trader placed a stop-loss order to automatically sell his shares if the price dropped below $100.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limit order - an order to buy or sell a security at a specified price or better
