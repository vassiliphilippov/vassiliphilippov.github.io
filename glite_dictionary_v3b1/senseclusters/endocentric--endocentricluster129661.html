endocentric

adjective
describing a construction with the same function as one of its parts
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /en.doʊ.ˈsen.trɪk/ /; / /en.də.ˈsen.trɪk/ (AmE), /en.dəʊ.ˈsen.trɪk/ /; / /en.də.ˈsen.trɪk/ (BrE)
(of a phrase or construction) having the same grammatical function as one of its components; forming a unit with the same function as one of its elements.

Examples

Endocentric phrase structure
In the phrase 'large house', the construction is endocentric because it functions as a noun, like 'house'.
Linguists often discuss whether a phrase is endocentric or exocentric when analyzing sentence structure.
Some compound nouns are endocentric, where the whole compound shares the grammatical category of one element.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exocentric - describing a construction whose function is not the same as any of its parts
