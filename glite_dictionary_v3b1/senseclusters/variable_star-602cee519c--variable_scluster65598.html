variable star

noun
star with varying brightness
1.6 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈve.rɪ.ə.bəl stɑːr/ /; / /ˈvæ.rɪ.ə.bəl stɑːr/ (AmE), /ˈveə.rɪ.ə.bəl stɑː/ /; / /ˈve.rɪ.ə.bəl stɑː/ (BrE)
a star whose brightness, as seen from Earth, fluctuates over time due to physical changes or eclipsing effects

Examples

A variable star
Some variable stars are used as standard candles to measure cosmic distances.
The astronomer discovered a variable star that dimmed and brightened over a period of days.
The sudden change in brightness indicated that the object was a variable star rather than a planet.
