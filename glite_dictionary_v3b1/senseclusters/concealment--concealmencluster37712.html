concealment

noun
the state of being hidden
5.0 0.79/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsiÀêlm…ônt / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsiÀêlm…ônt / (BrE)
the act or state of hiding something or keeping it secret

Examples

Secret concealment
Effective concealment of the evidence made the investigation difficult.
She revealed her secret after years of strict concealment.
The burglar relied on the concealment of darkness to avoid detection.
Her concealment of the truth made it hard to trust her again.
The company's concealment of important safety data led to a major scandal.
The spy was skilled in the art of concealment and evaded capture for years.

Synonyms

  • hiding
    2.7 6.82/m
    hiding is an active, often physical act of staying out of sight, while concealment is a broader, more formal act of keeping something secret.
  • secrecy
    4.4 8.18/m
    secrecy is the state of keeping information private and not telling others, while concealment covers hiding things or facts.
  • masking
    4.8 0.99/m
    masking hides something from view by covering or camouflaging it, while concealment names the general act or state of keeping things secret.
