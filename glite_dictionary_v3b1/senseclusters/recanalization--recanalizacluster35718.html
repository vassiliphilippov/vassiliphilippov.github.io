recanalization

noun
restoring vessel flow
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkænəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkænəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of restoring the normal flow in a blocked blood vessel or duct by reopening it

Examples

Artery recanalization
The doctor recommended recanalization to restore blood flow after the artery became blocked.
Successful recanalization can greatly improve outcomes for patients with certain types of strokes.
Advances in medical technology have made recanalization procedures safer and more effective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: occlusion - the blockage or closing of a blood vessel or duct
  • synonym: reopening - the act of opening something again
