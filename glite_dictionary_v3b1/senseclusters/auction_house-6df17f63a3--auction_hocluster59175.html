auction house

noun
firm that runs public auctions
Pronunciation: / ˈɔkʃən ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkʃən ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a company or firm that organizes and conducts public auctions for the sale of goods or property

Examples

An auction house sale
Many antiques are brought to an auction house to be appraised and sold.
Sotheby's is a famous auction house based in London.
The painting was sold at an auction house for a record price.
