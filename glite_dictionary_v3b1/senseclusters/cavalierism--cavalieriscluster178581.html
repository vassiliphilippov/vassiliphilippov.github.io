cavalierism

noun
support for Royalists
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkævəˈlɪrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌkævəˈlɪərɪzəm / (BrE)
political support for the Cavaliers or loyalty to the Royalist cause, especially during the English Civil War

Examples

Royalist cavalierism
During the 17th century, cavalierism was a defining feature of Royalist strongholds in England.
The novel explores themes of cavalierism and loyalty to the monarchy during turbulent times.
Historians debate how much cavalierism influenced the outcome of the English Civil War.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parliamentarianism - support for parliamentary government, especially against royal authority
  • synonym: royalism - support for a monarchy or royal family
