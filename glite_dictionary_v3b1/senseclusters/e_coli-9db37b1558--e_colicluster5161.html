E. coli

noun
intestinal bacterium; sometimes pathogenic
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌi ˈkoʊ.laɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː ˈkəʊ.laɪ / (BrE)
a bacterium commonly found in the intestines of humans and animals, sometimes causing illness or food poisoning

Examples

E. coli bacteria
Food contaminated with E. coli can cause serious illness if consumed without proper cooking.
Scientists isolated the E. coli strain to study its effects on the digestive system.
Several outbreaks of E. coli infections have led to recalls of fresh produce.
