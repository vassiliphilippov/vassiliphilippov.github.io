macula

noun
flat, discolored skin patch
8.8 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmæk.jə.lə/ /; / /ˈmæk.jʊ.lə/ (AmE), /ˈmæk.jə.lə/ /; / /ˈmæk.jʊ.lə/ (BrE)
A patch of skin that is discolored but not usually raised, often occurring as a result of disease.

Examples

Skin macula
A flat red macula appeared on his cheek after he developed the rash.
Multiple maculae across the patient's back indicated a possible skin infection.
The doctor examined the macula on her arm to determine the cause of discoloration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: papule - a small, raised, solid pimple or swelling on the skin
