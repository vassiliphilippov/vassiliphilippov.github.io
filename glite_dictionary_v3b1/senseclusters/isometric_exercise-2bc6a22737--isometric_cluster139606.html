isometric exercise

noun
muscle-building with static contractions
6.1 3.5
Pronunciation: /aɪ.sə.ˈme.trɪk ˈek.sər.saɪz/ /; / /aɪ.soʊ.ˈme.trɪk ˈek.sər.saɪz/ (AmE), /aɪ.səʊ.ˈme.trɪk ˈek.sə.saɪz/ /; / /aɪ.sə.ˈme.trɪk ˈek.sə.saɪz/ (BrE)
A system of muscle-building exercises involving muscular contractions against resistance without movement, so the muscle length does not change.

Examples

Isometric exercise muscle hold
He included isometric exercise in his daily fitness routine to build strength without stressing his joints.
Holding a plank is a classic example of isometric exercise.
Physiotherapists often recommend isometric exercise for patients recovering from injury.
