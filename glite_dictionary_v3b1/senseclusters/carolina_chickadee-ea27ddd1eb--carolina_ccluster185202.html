Carolina chickadee

noun
small southern U.S. songbird, tit family
8.5
Pronunciation: / kəˈraɪlənə ˈtʃɪkəˌdi / (AmE), / ˌkarəˈlʌɪnə ˈtʃɪkədi / (BrE)
A small North American songbird of the tit family, found in the southern United States, similar to the black-capped chickadee but smaller.

Examples

The Carolina chickadee bird
The Carolina chickadee often visits backyard feeders throughout the southern United States.
Unlike its northern cousin, the Carolina chickadee is slightly smaller and found further south.
We spotted a Carolina chickadee singing in the oak tree early this morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: black-capped chickadee - a related larger chickadee species found in northern United States and Canada
