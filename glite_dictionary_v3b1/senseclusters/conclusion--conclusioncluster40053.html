conclusion

noun
the act of deciding or judging
1.6 46/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàkluÀê í…ôn /; / k…ônÀàklu í…ôn / (AmE), / k…ônÀàkluÀê í…ôn / (BrE)
a decision, judgment, or opinion formed after thinking about the facts or arguments

Examples

Reach a conclusion
After listening to all the arguments, she finally reached her conclusion.
The evidence led the detective to an unexpected conclusion.
We must not jump to a conclusion before knowing all the facts.
After weeks of debate, the committee finally reached a conclusion about the proposal.
Based on the evidence presented, the detective drew a logical conclusion.
Her conclusion was that more research was needed before making a decision.

Synonyms

  • judgment
    2.4 21/m
    judgment is an opinion formed after careful thought about facts and evidence, while conclusion is the final view reached from arguments.
  • decision
    1.0 253/m
    decision is choosing between options after weighing pros and cons, while conclusion is a reasoned opinion reached from evidence.
  • determination
    4.0 10/m
    determination is an official, often formal decision made after review, while conclusion is a general opinion formed from arguments.
