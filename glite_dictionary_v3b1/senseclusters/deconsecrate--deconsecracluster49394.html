deconsecrate

verb
remove sacred status ceremonially
9.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀådiÀêÀàk…ëÀêns…ôÀåkre…™t / (AmE), / ÀådiÀêÀàk…íns…™Àåkre…™t / (BrE)
to officially remove the sacred or consecrated status of a person, place, or object, usually through a formal ceremony

Examples

To deconsecrate a church
After centuries of use, the altar was formally deconsecrated during a special ceremony.
The church was deconsecrated before being converted into a community center.
They plan to deconsecrate the old burial ground before redevelopment begins.

Synonyms

  • unsanctify
    9.5 0.00/m
    unsanctify removes holy status in general, while deconsecrate means formally take sacred status away, often in a ceremony.
  • unhallow
    9.9 0.00/m
    unhallow strips a place or object of its consecration in speech or text, while deconsecrate implies an official ceremony.
  • desacralize
    9.9 0.05/m
    desacralize moves something from church control into secular use or study, while deconsecrate focuses on an official ritual change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consecrate - to make or declare something sacred
  • synonym: desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect
