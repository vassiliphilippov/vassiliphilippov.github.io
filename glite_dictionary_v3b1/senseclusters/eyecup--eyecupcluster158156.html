eyecup

noun
embryonic structure forming retina layers
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌkʌp / (BrE)
(embryology) A two-layered cuplike structure in the developing embryo that forms the pigmented and sensory layers of the retina.

Examples

Embryonic eyecup
During embryonic development, the eyecup gives rise to the retina's important layers.
Scientists observed the formation of the eyecup under a microscope in the early stages of eye development.
The inner and outer layers of the eyecup differentiate into the sensory and pigmented tissues of the retina.
