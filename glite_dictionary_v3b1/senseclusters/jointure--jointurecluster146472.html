jointure

noun
estate settled on a wife in lieu of dower
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔɪntʃər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪntjə / (BrE)
(law) an estate settled on a woman in lieu of a dower, to take effect upon her husband's death.

Examples

A jointure estate
Debate arose in the family over the provisions of Lady Whitmore's jointure.
The wealthy landowner arranged a jointure for his daughter before her marriage.
Upon her husband's death, she received her jointure as stipulated in the marriage settlement.
