Tetraclinis articulata

noun
a North African and Spanish evergreen tree species
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈklaɪnɪs ˌɑːrtɪˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈklaɪnɪs ˌɑːtɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
a large coniferous evergreen tree native to North Africa and Spain, with flattened branches and scalelike leaves, yielding hard, fragrant wood and a resinous bark used in varnishes

Examples

The Tetraclinis articulata tree
In North Africa, the Tetraclinis articulata is often planted for both ornamental and practical purposes.
Resin extracted from Tetraclinis articulata bark is commonly used in high-quality varnishes.
The wood of the Tetraclinis articulata is prized for its durability and fragrance.
