feminine

adjective
of female grammatical gender
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛmənɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɛmɪnɪn / (BrE)
(of a grammatical gender) belonging to the class of words or forms denoting female beings or grammatical gender typically marked as female.

Examples

Feminine noun
German nouns can be masculine, feminine, or neuter.
In French, the word for table is feminine, so it uses the article 'la'.
Many languages assign either masculine or feminine gender to inanimate objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: masculine - of grammatical gender typically associated with male beings; opposite of feminine gender
  • antonym: neuter - of grammatical gender neither masculine nor feminine
