haunt

verb
to trouble persistently; to appear as a ghost
1.3 5.9
Pronunciation: / hɔnt /; / hɑnt / (AmE), / hɔːnt / (BrE)
(of a ghost or supernatural being) to regularly appear or manifest in a place

Examples

Ghosts haunt a house
Many believe the old mansion is haunted by the ghost of its former owner.
The fear of failure can haunt even the most successful people.
The painful memory continued to haunt him for years after the accident.
Legends claim that a headless rider haunts the country roads at night.
People said the ghost continued to haunt the old library long after it was abandoned.
She was convinced the empty cottage had been haunted for years.

  • antonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly, often in a supernatural way
