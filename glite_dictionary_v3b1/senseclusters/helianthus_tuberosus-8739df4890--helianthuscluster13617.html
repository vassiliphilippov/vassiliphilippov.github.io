Helianthus tuberosus

noun
species of sunflower with edible tubers
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːliˈænθəs tuːbəˈroʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈænθəs tjuːbəˈrəʊsəs / (BrE)
a tall North American perennial plant with hairy stems and leaves, widely cultivated for its large, irregular, edible tubers, commonly known as Jerusalem artichoke

Examples

Helianthus tuberosus plant
Farmers often grow Helianthus tuberosus for its nutritious, knobby tubers known as Jerusalem artichokes.
Researchers study Helianthus tuberosus because its tubers are resistant to frost and can be harvested in winter.
The leaves of Helianthus tuberosus are rough and hairy, similar to those of other sunflowers.
