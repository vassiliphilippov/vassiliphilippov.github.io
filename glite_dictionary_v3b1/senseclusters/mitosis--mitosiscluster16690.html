mitosis

noun
type of cell division
1.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /maɪ.ˈtoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /maɪ.ˈtəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A process of cell division in which the nucleus divides into two nuclei, each containing the same number of chromosomes as the original cell.

Examples

Mitosis process
During mitosis, each daughter cell receives an identical set of chromosomes.
Errors in mitosis can lead to the development of cancer.
Scientists observed the stages of mitosis through a microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: meiosis - type of cell division that reduces the chromosome number by half
