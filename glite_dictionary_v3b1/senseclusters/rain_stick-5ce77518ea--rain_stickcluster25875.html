rain stick

noun
percussion instrument imitating rain sound
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪn stɪk / (AmE), / ˈreɪn stɪk / (BrE)
A percussion instrument, usually made from a hollowed cactus branch filled with small pebbles and capped at both ends, which produces a sound like falling rain when tilted; traditionally used in Chilean ceremonies to invoke rain.

Examples

A rain stick instrument
She bought a decorative rain stick while traveling in South America.
The musician created a soothing atmosphere by playing the rain stick during the performance.
The sound of the rain stick reminded him of a gentle summer shower.
