waive

verb
to give up a right or claim voluntarily
6.6 4.29/m
Pronunciation: / weÉªv / (AmE), / weÉªv / (BrE)
to choose not to demand or use (a right or claim), or to intentionally give something up

Examples

To waive a right
By checking this box, you waive any claim to a refund.
She chose to waive her right to an attorney.
The team agreed to waive the fee for late registration this year.

Synonyms

  • relinquish
    7.2 5.71/m
    relinquish is to give up something formally and often permanently, while waive means choosing not to use or demand a right or claim.
  • forgo
    6.3 2.94/m
    forgo means deciding not to have or enjoy something desirable, while waive refers to choosing not to demand or use a legal right.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, as a right
  • synonym: forgo - to decide not to do or have something
  • synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily give up or let go of something
