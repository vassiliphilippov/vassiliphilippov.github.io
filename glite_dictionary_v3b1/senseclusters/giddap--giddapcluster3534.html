giddap

verb
to urge forward
Pronunciation: / ɡɪˈdæp / (AmE), / ɡɪˈdæp / (BrE)
to urge a horse or other animal to move faster, especially by calling out or spurring on

Examples

To giddap a mule
The farmer shouted giddap to get the mule moving again.
Whenever the wagon slowed down, she would call out giddap to the horses.
He laughed as the children pretended to giddap their imaginary ponies around the yard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: giddy-up - a command to a horse to go faster
  • antonym: whoa - a command to a horse to stop
