gothic

adjective
medieval or old-fashioned in outlook
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
Having characteristics of the Middle Ages; old-fashioned and unenlightened in outlook.

Examples

Gothic ideas
His gothic views on education are out of step with the 21st century.
Some people think the tradition is charming, but others find it rather gothic.
The company abandoned its gothic approach to customer service in favor of modern methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: medieval - of, relating to, or characteristic of the Middle Ages
  • antonym: modern - characteristic of the present or recent times
