burial chamber

noun
room or space used as a grave
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛriəl ˌtʃeɪmbər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːriəl ˌtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
an enclosed room or space, typically underground, where a body is placed for burial

Examples

Royal burial chamber
Each royal burial chamber was carefully sealed to protect the king's body from grave robbers.
The archaeologists discovered a well-preserved burial chamber containing ancient artifacts and remains.
The pharaoh was laid to rest in a lavish burial chamber deep inside the pyramid.
