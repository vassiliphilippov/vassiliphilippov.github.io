abnormality

noun
medical condition of abnormal development
2.2 4.0
Pronunciation: /æb.nɔːr.ˈmæl.ə.tiː/ /; / /æb.nɔːr.ˈmæl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /æb.nɔː.ˈmæl.ə.tiː/ /; / /æb.nɔː.ˈmæl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which part of the body does not develop normally, often due to genetic defects or developmental problems.

Examples

A heart abnormality
Doctors detected an abnormality in the baby's chromosome structure.
Genetic testing can help identify an abnormality before symptoms develop.
She was born with a heart abnormality that required surgery.
