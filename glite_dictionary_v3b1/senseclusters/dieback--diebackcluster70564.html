dieback

noun
plant disease causing shoot death
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈdaɪˌbæk / (BrE)
A disease of plants in which new shoots gradually die from the tips downward, eventually affecting larger branches.

Examples

Plant dieback disease
Gardeners noticed a progressive dieback starting at the tips of the rose bushes.
Many species of eucalyptus have suffered severe dieback due to fungal infections in recent years.
To prevent dieback, it is important to prune away affected branches promptly.
