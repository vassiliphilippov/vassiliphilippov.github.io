hoard

verb
collect and store for future use
4.8 2.92/m
Pronunciation: / h…îrd / (AmE), / h…îÀêd / (BrE)
to collect and store large amounts of something, often secretly, for future use

Examples

To hoard supplies
During the crisis, many people began to hoard food and essential supplies.
He has a tendency to hoard money rather than spend it.
The squirrels hoard nuts in preparation for winter.
During times of crisis, people often hoard essential supplies like food and water.
She began to hoard newspapers in her attic, fearing she might need them one day.
Some animals hoard nuts and seeds to survive the winter months.
