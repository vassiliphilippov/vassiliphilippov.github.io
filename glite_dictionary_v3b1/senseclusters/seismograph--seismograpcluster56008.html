seismograph

noun
instrument for measuring earthquakes
3.7 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪz.mə.græf/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪz.mə.grɑːf/ (BrE)
an instrument that detects and records the intensity, direction, and duration of ground movements, especially during earthquakes

Examples

Modern seismographs
Modern seismographs are sensitive enough to detect even the smallest ground vibrations.
Scientists analyzed the seismograph data to determine the earthquake's magnitude.
The seismograph recorded significant tremors just before the main earthquake struck the city.
