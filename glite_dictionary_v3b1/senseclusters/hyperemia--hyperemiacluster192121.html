hyperemia

noun
excess blood in a body part
5.7 8.5
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.ɪ.ˈmiː.ə/ /; / /haɪ.pər.iː.ˈmiː.ə/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.rɪ.ˈmiː.ə/ /; / /haɪ.pə.riː.ˈmiː.ə/ (BrE)
an excess of blood in a particular organ or body part, often due to increased blood flow

Examples

Chronic hyperemia
Chronic hyperemia can be a sign of underlying circulatory disorders.
Doctors noted hyperemia in the affected limb after the operation.
The redness of the skin was a result of hyperemia following the injury.
Allergic reactions can cause nasal hyperaemia, leading to congestion and discomfort.
Sustained physical activity may result in temporary hyperaemia of the muscle tissue.
The doctor noted increased hyperaemia in the affected limb during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congestion - an excessive accumulation of blood or other fluid in a body part
  • antonym: ischemia - an inadequate blood supply to a part of the body
