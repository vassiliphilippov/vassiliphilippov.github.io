hyomandibular

noun
jaw-connecting bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪoʊmænˈdɪbjələr / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəʊmænˈdɪbjʊlə / (BrE)
a bone or cartilage in fishes and some other vertebrates that connects the jaw to the skull

Examples

A fish hyomandibular
The paleontologist carefully examined the fossil's hyomandibular to determine how the fish bit its prey.
Damage to the hyomandibular can affect how a fish opens and closes its mouth.
Researchers compared the hyomandibular structures of different fish species to study their evolutionary relationships.
