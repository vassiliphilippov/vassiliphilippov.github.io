exciter

noun
source of excitement
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes excitement or stimulation in others or in a system

Examples

A real exciter
The energetic coach was a real exciter for the team before the big game.
In electronics, an exciter is used to generate the initial signal for amplification.
The festival's opening act served as an exciter for the enthusiastic audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibitor - something that restrains or slows down a process
  • synonym: stimulus - something that provokes a response or activity
