dhamma

noun
Buddhist moral conduct
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈdʌmə / (BrE)
moral conduct or behavior in accordance with Buddhist teachings and ethical principles

Examples

Right dhamma practice
He devoted his life to practicing dhamma and helping others follow the Buddhist path.
The teacher explained that dhamma guides people to act with compassion and honesty.
Many monks gather to discuss the meaning of dhamma in their daily lives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dharma - the principle of cosmic order or moral law in Indian religions
  • synonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
