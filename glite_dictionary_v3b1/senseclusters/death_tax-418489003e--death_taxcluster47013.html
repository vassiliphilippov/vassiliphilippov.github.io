death tax

noun
tax on the estate of a deceased person
6.3 4.8
Pronunciation: /deθ tæks/ (AmE), /deθ tæks/ (BrE)
a tax imposed on the transfer of property or estate to heirs after someone's death (inheritance or estate tax)

Examples

The death tax law
Many small business owners express concern about how the death tax could affect their heirs.
Some politicians have campaigned to eliminate the death tax because they believe it unfairly burdens families.
The estate was significantly reduced after paying the required death tax.
He believes eliminating the death tax would benefit small business owners.
Some families are forced to sell property in order to pay the death tax.
The debate over the death tax is a recurring topic during election season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: estate tax - a tax levied on the net value of the estate of a deceased person before distribution to the heirs
  • synonym: inheritance tax - a tax paid by a person who inherits money or property from someone who has died
