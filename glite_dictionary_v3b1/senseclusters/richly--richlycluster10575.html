To a great or plentiful degree; in an abundant or ample manner.

Examples

Richly deserves praise
He richly deserves the recognition he is finally receiving.
The newly decorated hall was richly adorned with gold leaf and velvet curtains.
The play's atmosphere is richly evocative of the Renaissance period.

Synonyms

  • abundantly
    3.5 2.68/m
    abundantly means in very large amounts, while richly often adds a sense of fullness or richness to something.
  • plentifully
    2.5 0.15/m
    plentifully describes things available in large amounts, while richly suggests an abundant, full quality that is often vivid.
  • plenteously
    6.3 0.00/m
    plenteously means with great plenty and often sounds old-fashioned, while richly feels more modern and vivid in use.
  • profusely
    4.8 1.88/m
    profusely means in very large or even excessive amounts, while richly suggests ample abundance without necessarily implying excess.
  • amply
    3.2 1.65/m
    amply means more than enough or sufficient for a purpose, while richly emphasizes abundance and a full, often luxurious, feel.
  • bountifully
    4.6 0.08/m
    bountifully means in a generous, abundant way often tied to giving or harvests, while richly focuses on lavish presence.
  • bounteously
    8.1 0.01/m
    bounteously suggests generous giving or gifts to others, while richly describes general abundance or luxurious quality.
  • liberally
    3.9 1.18/m
    liberally means freely or in generous amounts, often applied to people or actions, while richly denotes abundant substance or style.
