historicist

noun
believer in historical context
Pronunciation: / hɪˈstɔrɪsɪst / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪsɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that historical context is essential for understanding ideas, events, or works

Examples

A historicist scholar
The historicist approach to literature considers the time and place in which a work was written.
As a historicist, she argued that political theories must be understood in their historical context.
Many historicists believe that ignoring history leads to misunderstandings of important events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ahistoricist - a person who ignores or downplays historical context
  • synonym: contextualist - a person who emphasizes the importance of context in interpretation
