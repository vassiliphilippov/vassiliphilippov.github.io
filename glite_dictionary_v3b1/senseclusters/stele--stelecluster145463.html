stele

noun
ancient inscribed stone slab
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈstiːli / (AmE), / ˈstiːli / (BrE)
An upright stone slab or pillar, often inscribed or carved, erected as a monument or marker, especially in ancient times.

Examples

Ancient stele
Archaeologists discovered a stele dating back to the ancient Egyptian civilization.
The Rosetta Stone is a famous stele inscribed with three scripts.
The museum displayed a Mayan stele depicting a royal figure and hieroglyphs.
