power broker

noun
an influential person in politics or business
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˌbroʊ.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˌbrəʊ.kə / (BrE)
a person who has significant influence in politics or business by controlling votes or the support of other influential people

Examples

Power broker in politics
As a power broker in the city, she could influence many key decisions.
Critics accused him of acting as a behind-the-scenes power broker in local politics.
The mayor relied on a power broker to secure the necessary votes for the bill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kingmaker - a person who has great influence in making decisions or choosing leaders
