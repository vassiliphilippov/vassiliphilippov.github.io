then

adverb
as a result; in that case
Pronunciation: / ðɛn / (AmE), / ðɛn / (BrE)
used to introduce the result or consequence of something or to indicate what happens next in a sequence of events.

Examples

Then comes dessert
Finish your homework first, then you can go outside.
If you don't want the job, then just say so.
She forgot her keys, and then she missed the bus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: so - used to introduce a result or conclusion
  • synonym: therefore - for that reason; consequently
