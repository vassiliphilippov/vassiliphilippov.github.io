do

verb
cause to happen or occur
1.0 250/m
Pronunciation: / du / (AmE), / duÀê / (BrE)
to cause something to happen or occur, whether intentionally or unintentionally

Examples

To do damage
He accidentally did harm to the machine while trying to fix it.
Her comments did more harm than good during the meeting.
The storm did a lot of damage to the old bridge last night.

Synonyms

  • cause
    1.0 262/m
    cause is a direct, often formal verb for producing an effect, while do covers any action that makes something happen.
  • make
    1.0 877/m
    make often means to create or change something into a new state, while do means to perform actions or cause events more generally.
  • bring about
    3.0 17/m
    bring about is a phrase for causing a change or result over time, while do means to cause something to happen in general.
