nucleoplasm

noun
fluid substance inside a cell nucleus
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.əˌplæz.əm / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əˌplaz.əm / (BrE)
the semi-fluid substance within the nucleus of a cell, containing the chromosomes and nucleolus

Examples

The nucleoplasm inside nucleus
During cell division, parts of the nucleoplasm reorganize to help form the new nuclei.
Proteins and enzymes move freely within the nucleoplasm to carry out vital cell functions.
The nucleoplasm supports the chromosomes and other nuclear components.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cytoplasm - the material within a cell, excluding the nucleus
