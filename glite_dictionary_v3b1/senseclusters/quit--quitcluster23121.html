Synonyms

  • give up
    1.2 80/m
    give up means stop trying to continue an activity after effort, while quit means to stop doing something or abandon it.
  • call it quits
    3.9 2/m
    call it quits means decide to stop after enough or failure, while quit is the general verb for stopping an activity.
  • stop
    1.0 700/m
    stop means cause something to end or not continue, while quit means to stop doing or give up something.
  • cease
    3.6 15/m
    cease is a more formal word for bringing something to an end, while quit is common talk for stopping activities or habits.
  • discontinue
    4.2 3/m
    discontinue is formal and often used for ending products or services, while quit is used for people stopping activities.
  • abandon
    3.8 19/m
    abandon means leave or stop supporting a plan or idea completely, while quit usually means stop taking part or a habit.
  • withdraw
    2.8 20/m
    withdraw means remove yourself from a group or situation, while quit means stop doing or giving up an activity.
  • relinquish
    7.2 3/m
    relinquish means formally give up a right, claim, or possession, while quit refers to stopping an activity or habit.
