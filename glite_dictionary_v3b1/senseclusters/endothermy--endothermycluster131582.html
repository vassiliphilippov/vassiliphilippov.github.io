endothermy

noun
absorption of heat
9.7
Pronunciation: /en.ˈdɑː.θər.miː/ (AmE), /en.ˈdɒθ.ə.miː/ (BrE)
the property of absorbing heat from the surroundings during a chemical reaction or physical process

Examples

Chemical endothermy
The process of melting ice is an example of endothermy in everyday life.
Scientists observed endothermy when the chemical reaction caused the container to feel colder.
Understanding endothermy is important for explaining why some reactions require constant heating.
