spook

noun
ghost or spirit
Pronunciation: / spuːk / (AmE), / spuːk / (BrE)
a ghost or spirit that is believed to frighten people

Examples

A spook in the attic
The children believed a spook lived in the attic of the abandoned house.
On Halloween night, he dressed up as a spook to scare his friends.
Some people claim to have seen a spook wandering the old cemetery at midnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person that appears to the living
  • synonym: phantom - a ghostly figure or apparition
  • synonym: spirit - a supernatural being, often the soul of a dead person
