nitroglycerin

noun
explosive compound; also used in medicine
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnaɪtroʊˈɡlɪsərɪn /; / ˌnaɪtroʊˈɡlɪsəriːn / (AmE), / ˌnaɪtrəʊˈɡlɪsərɪn /; / ˌnaɪtrəʊˈɡlɪsəriːn / (BrE)
a heavy, yellow, oily, explosive liquid used in making explosives and, medically, as a vasodilator to widen blood vessels and treat heart conditions; it is poisonous.

Examples

Nitroglycerin explosive liquid
Dynamite is made by absorbing nitroglycerin into an inert porous material.
Strict safety protocols are required when transporting nitroglycerin due to its highly explosive nature.
The doctor administered nitroglycerin to relieve the patient's severe chest pain.
Doctors sometimes prescribe nitroglycerine to patients with angina to relieve chest pain.
Due to its explosive nature, nitroglycerine must be handled with extreme care in laboratories.
The dynamite used in mining operations often contains nitroglycerine as a key component.
