white-seeded

adjective
having white seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt ˌsidɪd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant or variety) having seeds that are white in color.

Examples

White-seeded plants
Researchers compared the growth rates of white-seeded and black-seeded wheat strains.
She planted both the white-seeded and red-seeded types to observe their differences.
The white-seeded variety of bean is popular among gardeners for its mild flavor.
