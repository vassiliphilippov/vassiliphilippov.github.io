blastodisc

noun
layer of cells in early embryo
9.3 8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈblæs.tə.dɪsk/ (AmE), /ˈblæs.tə.dɪsk/ (BrE)
a thin layer of cells formed on the surface of the yolk in the early embryo of some animals, such as birds and reptiles, where development begins

Examples

The blastodisc layer
During early embryonic development, the blastodisc contains the cells from which the embryo will form.
Scientists observed the changes in the blastodisc under the microscope after fertilization.
The fertilized egg of a chicken develops a blastodisc on the surface of its yolk.
