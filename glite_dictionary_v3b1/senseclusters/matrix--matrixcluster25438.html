matrix

noun
environment where something develops
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (BrE)
A place or environment in which something develops or is formed; an enclosure within which something originates or takes shape.

Examples

Cultural matrix
The city served as a matrix for new forms of art and technology.
The university was a matrix for social reform movements during the early 20th century.
Within the rich soil's matrix, the seeds sprouted and grew rapidly.
