projectively

adverb
relating to projection
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛktɪvli / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to projection or projecting, especially in mathematics or psychology

Examples

To projectively interpret data
The psychologist interpreted the test results projectively to uncover hidden emotions.
In geometry, the figures were compared projectively to find similarities.
The data was analyzed projectively to reveal patterns not seen directly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analytically - in a way that involves analysis or logical reasoning
  • synonym: geometrically - in a way that relates to geometry
