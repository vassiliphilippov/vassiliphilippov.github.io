roadshow

verb
to promote by touring
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊdˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
to travel to different locations to promote a product, service, or company, often to attract customers or investors

Examples

To roadshow a product
The company plans to roadshow its latest software in several major cities this summer.
They will roadshow the new electric car model to generate excitement among potential buyers.
After developing the prototype, the team began to roadshow it at industry conferences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: present - to show or display something to an audience
  • synonym: tour - to travel from place to place for promotion or performance
