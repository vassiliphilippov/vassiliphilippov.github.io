hawkishly

adverb
aggressively, warlike manner
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑː.kɪʃ.liː/ /; / /ˈhɔː.kɪʃ.liː/ (AmE), /ˈhɔː.kɪʃ.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that supports aggressive or warlike policies, especially in politics or foreign affairs

Examples

To speak hawkishly on war
The senator argued hawkishly for a stronger military response to the crisis.
Some commentators spoke hawkishly about the need for increased defense spending.
He responded hawkishly to any suggestion of negotiating with the opposition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressively - in a forceful or combative way
  • antonym: dovishly - in a way that favors peace or compromise
