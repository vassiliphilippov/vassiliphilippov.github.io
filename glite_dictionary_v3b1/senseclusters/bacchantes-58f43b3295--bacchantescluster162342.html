Bacchantes

proper noun
followers of Bacchus
Pronunciation: / bækˈkæntiːz / (AmE), / bakˈkæntiːz / (BrE)
female followers of Bacchus in Roman mythology, famous for their wild and ecstatic celebrations

Examples

Mythical Bacchantes dance
The ancient sculpture shows a group of Bacchantes dancing with garlands in their hair.
In Roman stories, the Bacchantes were known for their wild rituals in the woods.
The festival was inspired by the legendary celebrations of the Bacchantes.
