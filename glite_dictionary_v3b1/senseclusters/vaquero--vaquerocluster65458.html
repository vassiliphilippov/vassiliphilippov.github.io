vaquero

noun
Southwestern cowboy or cattle herder
Pronunciation: / vɑˈkɛroʊ / (AmE), / vaˈkɛːrəʊ / (BrE)
A cowboy, especially one in the southwestern United States, originally referring to Spanish-speaking cattle herders.

Examples

A vaquero on ranches
As a young man, he worked as a vaquero on ranches throughout Texas.
Many traditions of the American cowboy were adopted from the vaquero culture.
The skilled vaquero demonstrated his roping techniques at the rodeo.
