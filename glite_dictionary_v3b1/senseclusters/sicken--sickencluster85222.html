sicken

verb
to become sick or ill
3.0 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÉªkÉ™n / (AmE), / ËˆsÉªkÉ™n / (BrE)
to become sick or ill; to fall ill

Examples

He sickened after dinner
After eating the spoiled food, he began to sicken within hours.
Several people may sicken if the flu continues to spread through the community.
She seemed fine all day, but suddenly started to sicken that evening.

Synonyms

  • get sick
    1.0 12/m
    get sick is a common everyday phrase for becoming ill, while sicken sounds more formal and appears less in speech.
  • take ill
    5.6 0.34/m
    take ill is a somewhat old-fashioned phrase meaning to become unwell suddenly, while sicken is neutral and simply means to fall ill.
  • come down
    4.3 2.86/m
    come down is used with 'with' to mean catching a specific illness, while sicken means becoming ill in general.
