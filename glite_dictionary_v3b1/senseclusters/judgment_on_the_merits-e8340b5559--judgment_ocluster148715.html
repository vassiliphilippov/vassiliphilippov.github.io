judgment on the merits

noun
legal court decision based on facts and law
6.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɑːn ðə ˈmer.ɪts/ /; / /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ən ðə ˈmer.ɪts/ (AmE), /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɒn ðə ˈmer.ɪts/ /; / /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ən ðə ˈmer.ɪts/ (BrE)
a formal court decision made after considering the actual facts and legal issues of a case, not just procedural or technical matters

Examples

A judgment on the merits
In most cases, parties prefer to win a judgment on the merits rather than on a technicality.
The appeal was dismissed because there had already been a judgment on the merits in the lower court.
The judge issued a judgment on the merits after carefully reviewing all the evidence.
A dismissal due to lack of jurisdiction does not constitute a judgment on the merits.
The court issued a judgment on the merits after considering all of the evidence presented at trial.
The plaintiff appealed because the lower court never reached a judgment on the merits of her claims.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dismissal without prejudice - a case dismissal that allows for refiling, often not a judgment on the merits
  • antonym: summary judgment - a court judgment without a full trial, often based on procedural or undisputed facts
