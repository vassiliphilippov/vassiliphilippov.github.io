Lake Leman

proper noun
lake between Switzerland and France
Pronunciation: / leɪk ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / leɪk ˈliːmən / (BrE)
a large lake situated between southwestern Switzerland and France, traversed by the Rhône River; also known as Lake Geneva.

Examples

Lake Leman in Switzerland
Several international conferences have been held on the banks of Lake Leman in Switzerland.
The Rhône River flows through Lake Leman from east to west before continuing on to France.
The shores of Lake Leman offer stunning views of the Alps and picturesque villages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lake Geneva - the more commonly used English name for Lake Leman
