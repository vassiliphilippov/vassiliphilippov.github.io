western white pine

noun
tall pine tree native to western North America
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn waɪt paɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən waɪt paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree of western North America with stout blue-green needles and grey-brown bark that develops rectangular plates when mature

Examples

The western white pine tree
The western white pine is known for its valuable timber and resistance to cold climates.
The needles of the western white pine have a distinctive bluish tint.
We saw several towering western white pines while hiking in the mountains of Idaho.
