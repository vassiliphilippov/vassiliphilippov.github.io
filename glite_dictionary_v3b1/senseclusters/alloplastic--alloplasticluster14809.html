alloplastic

adjective
changing environment
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈplastɪk / (BrE)
describing behavior or coping strategies that involve changing the external environment rather than oneself to deal with stress or problems

Examples

Alloplastic coping style
The therapist noted his alloplastic approach to stress, as he often changed his surroundings to feel better.
Adopting an alloplastic strategy, she rearranged her office to minimize distractions and improve productivity.
Some people prefer alloplastic solutions, focusing on altering their environment instead of their own reactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autoplastic - describing coping by changing oneself rather than the environment
