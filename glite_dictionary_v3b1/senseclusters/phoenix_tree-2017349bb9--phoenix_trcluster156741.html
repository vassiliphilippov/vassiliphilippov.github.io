phoenix tree

noun
ornamental deciduous tree with maple-like leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːnɪks triː / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɪks triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree widely cultivated in the southern United States for its attractive, maple-like leaves and long clusters of yellow-green flowers, followed by distinctive, leaflike pods.

Examples

The phoenix tree leaves
Many parks in the southern United States feature the phoenix tree for its striking foliage.
The phoenix tree in our yard produces clusters of yellow-green flowers every spring.
The leaflike pods hanging from the phoenix tree are a unique sight after the flowering season.
