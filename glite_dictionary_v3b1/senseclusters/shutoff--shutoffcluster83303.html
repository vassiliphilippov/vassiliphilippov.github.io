shutoff

noun
device to stop flow
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈʃʌt.ɔːf/ /; / /ˈʃʌt.ɑːf/ (AmE), /ˈʃʌt.ɒf/ (BrE)
a device or action that stops the flow or operation of something, such as water, gas, electricity, or a machine, especially for safety or control

Examples

Main shutoff valve
The plumber located the main water shutoff in the basement.
In case of emergency, use the gas shutoff to stop the supply immediately.
The power shutoff prevented further damage during the storm.
The plumber located the main shutoff for the water supply under the kitchen sink.
In case of emergency, you should know where the gas shutoff is in your house.
The technician used the electrical shutoff to safely repair the circuit.
The water heater has a built-in shutoff to prevent overheating.
In case of emergency, press the shutoff button to stop the machine immediately.
The automatic shutoff engaged when the engine reached a dangerous temperature.
