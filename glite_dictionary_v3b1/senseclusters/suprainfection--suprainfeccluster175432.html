suprainfection

noun
secondary infection by an opportunistic organism
Pronunciation: / suˌpraɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / suːˌprɑːɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
a secondary infection caused by the growth of a resistant or opportunistic microorganism after the normal flora has been disturbed, often following antibiotic treatment

Examples

A suprainfection after antibiotics
A suprainfection can complicate the treatment of the original disease, making management more challenging.
Patients receiving broad-spectrum antibiotics are at risk of developing a suprainfection with resistant bacteria.
Physicians monitor for signs of suprainfection during prolonged antibiotic therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superinfection - a second infection superimposed on an earlier one, especially by a different agent
