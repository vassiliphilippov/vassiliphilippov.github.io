hard work

noun
persistent serious effort or labor
1.6 18/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërd w…ùk / (AmE), / h…ëÀêd w…úÀêk / (BrE)
persistent and serious effort or labor required to achieve something valuable or difficult

Examples

Her hard work pays
Her hard work on the project earned her a promotion at the company.
Success is rarely achieved without a lot of hard work and determination.
They recognized the hard work of the volunteers at the award ceremony.

Synonyms

  • industry
    3.0 2.70/m
    industry means steady, hardworking effort in carrying out duties, while hard work is the sustained labor to reach difficult goals.
  • application
    3.4 2.52/m
    application is serious, focused effort put into a task, while hard work covers any long, difficult labor to achieve results.
  • diligence
    4.1 3.76/m
    diligence is careful, steady attention to tasks, while hard work emphasizes the large amount of effort over time.
  • effort
    1.1 31/m
    effort is a single act of trying or exertion, while hard work is ongoing, serious labor to reach a hard goal.
  • sweat of one's brow
    5.8 0.09/m
    sweat of one's brow describes physical, often paid toil, while hard work is a general term for sustained serious effort.
