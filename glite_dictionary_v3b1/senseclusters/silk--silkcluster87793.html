silk

noun
fine fabric from insect larvae
Pronunciation: / sɪlk / (AmE), / sɪlk / (BrE)
a fine, soft, and shiny material or fiber produced by silkworms (and some other insects), used to make fabric and other products

Examples

A silk dress
She wore a beautiful dress made of silk to the wedding.
The scarf felt smooth and cool, as only real silk can.
Traditional kimonos are often crafted from brightly colored silk.
A spider spun a delicate web with threads of silk glistening in the morning dew.
They exported raw silk to Europe where it was woven into luxurious fabrics.
Traditional Chinese garments are often made of silk because of its natural shine and softness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cotton - a soft, white fibrous substance that surrounds the seeds of the cotton plant, used as a fabric
  • synonym: satin - a smooth, glossy fabric often made from silk or synthetic fibers
  • antonym: wool - fibers from the fleece of sheep, used in textiles
