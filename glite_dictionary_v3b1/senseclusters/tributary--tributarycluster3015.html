tributary

adjective
paying tribute to another power
8.7 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɪb.jə.tər.iː/ /; / /ˈtrɪb.jʊ.tər.iː/ (AmE), /ˈtrɪb.jʊ.tə.riː/ /; / /ˈtrɪb.jə.tə.riː/ (BrE)
(of a country, state, or people) paying tribute to another, especially as a sign of dependence or submission.

Examples

Tributary state
Several ancient empires maintained control over tributary colonies across vast regions.
The city remained tributary to its powerful neighbor for centuries.
The smaller kingdom became a tributary state after its defeat in battle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independent - not subject to another's rule or not paying tribute
  • synonym: subject - under the control of another as a subject nation
