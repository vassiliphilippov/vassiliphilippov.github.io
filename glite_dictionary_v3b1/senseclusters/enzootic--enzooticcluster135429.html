enzootic

adjective
persistently present animal disease
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnzoʊˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛnzuːˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease) constantly present in a particular animal population but typically affecting only a small number of cases at any time.

Examples

Enzootic disease in animals
Rabies is considered enzootic in certain wildlife populations across North America.
Scientists are studying how an enzootic disease persists at low levels within bat colonies.
The veterinarian reported that the infection was enzootic in several herds but not causing widespread outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endemic - (of a disease) regularly found among particular people or in a certain area
  • antonym: epizootic - (of a disease) affecting many animals in a population at the same time
