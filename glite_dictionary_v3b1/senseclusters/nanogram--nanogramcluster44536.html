nanogram

noun
one billionth of a gram (mass unit)
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈnænəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈnanəɡram / (BrE)
A unit of mass equal to one billionth (10⁻⁹) of a gram.

Examples

One nanogram
A single nanogram of the toxin can cause severe symptoms in humans.
Strict regulations require that no more than five nanograms per milliliter be present.
The laboratory detected only a nanogram of the substance in the blood sample.
