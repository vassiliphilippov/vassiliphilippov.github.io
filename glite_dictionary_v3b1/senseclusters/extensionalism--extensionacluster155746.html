extensionalism

noun
philosophy of sets
9.9
Pronunciation: /ek.sten.ʃə.ˈnæl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ɪk.sten.ʃə.ˈnæl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ek.sten.ʃə.ˈnæl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ɪk.sten.ʃə.ˈnæl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the view in logic or mathematics that objects are defined only by their members or outputs, not by how they are described

Examples

Extensionalism in set theory
In set theory, extensionalism means two sets are equal if they have the same elements.
The debate between intensionalism and extensionalism is important in the philosophy of language.
Many mathematicians adopt extensionalism when defining functions by their outputs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intensionalism - the view that objects are defined by their properties or descriptions, not just their members
