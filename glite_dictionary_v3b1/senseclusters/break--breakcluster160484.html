break

verb
(news) become publicly known
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
to make information, news, or events become known or start to happen, especially suddenly

Examples

To break the news
Rumors of the merger broke early Monday morning.
The scandal broke just days before the election.
When the story broke, it quickly spread across the internet.
It took weeks before investigators were ready to break the details of the case to the public.
She didn't want to break the news to her family until she was sure.
The newspaper was the first to break the story about the scandal.
As soon as the results broke, social media was flooded with reactions.
News of the scandal began to break late last night.
When the story broke, reporters rushed to the scene.
