publish

verb
issue work for public distribution
2.6 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpʌb.lɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈpʌb.lɪʃ/ (BrE)
to prepare and make a book, article, or other material available to the public, especially by printing or online

Examples

To publish a book
After years of research, she finally published her findings in a leading scientific journal.
Some musicians choose to publish their songs online instead of through traditional record labels.
The famous novelist published his latest book last month to critical acclaim.
Authors often dream of seeing their novels published by a major company.
Every scientific journal carefully reviews articles before they publish them.
The university plans to publish a new history textbook next year.
Several scientific journals publish the results of groundbreaking research every month.
She hopes to publish her first novel before turning thirty.
The company decided to publish a new edition of the textbook this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: print - to produce text or images on paper with a machine

  • antonym: suppress - to prevent information from being published or made public
  • antonym: withhold - refuse to publish or make public
