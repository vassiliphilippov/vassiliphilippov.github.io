copyist

noun
person who makes written copies
7.7 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀê.pi.…™st / (AmE), / Ààk…íp.i.…™st / (BrE)
a person who makes written copies of documents or manuscripts, especially by hand

Examples

A copyist of manuscripts
Before the invention of the printing press, every book was painstakingly reproduced by a copyist.
The accuracy of the document depended on the attention to detail of the copyist.
The museum displayed several ancient manuscripts preserved by a skilled copyist.

Synonyms

  • amanuensis
    9.9 0.09/m
    an amanuensis writes dictated or copied texts for another person, while a copyist makes handwritten copies of documents, especially manuscripts.
  • scrivener
    9.9 0.23/m
    a scrivener writes legal or official documents by hand and often appears in older texts, while a copyist makes handwritten copies of manuscripts.
  • scribe
    6.2 2.69/m
    a scribe records information in many roles, from clerks to medical assistants, while a copyist specifically makes handwritten copies of documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scribe - a person who copies out documents, especially in historical contexts
  • synonym: transcriber - a person who puts speech or data into written or printed form
