foreknow

verb
to know in advance
9.7 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrÀàno ä / (AmE), / f…îÀêÀàn…ô ä / (BrE)
to know something in advance; to realize or be aware of something before it happens

Examples

To foreknow the future
Historians wonder whether leaders could have foreknown the consequences of their decisions.
It is impossible to foreknow what challenges the new year will bring.
No one can foreknow the outcome with absolute certainty.

Synonyms

  • foresee
    3.9 5.45/m
    foresee predicts or imagines future events, while foreknow means being already aware of something before it happens.
  • anticipate
    3.2 8.77/m
    anticipate often means expect and prepare for something, while foreknow means simply to know it before it happens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something; to realize in advance
  • synonym: predict - to declare or indicate in advance, especially on the basis of observation, experience, or scientific reason
