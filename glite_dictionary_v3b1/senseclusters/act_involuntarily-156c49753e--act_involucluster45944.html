act involuntarily

verb
do something without conscious control
Pronunciation: / ækt ɪnˌvɑːlənˈterəli / (AmE), / ækt ɪnˌvɒlənˈteərəli / (BrE)
to behave or perform an action without conscious control or intention

Examples

To act involuntarily during sneeze
During a sneeze, your body acts involuntarily to expel irritants from the nose.
The patient may act involuntarily due to a neurological disorder.
When startled, people sometimes act involuntarily and drop what they are holding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: act voluntarily - to act as a result of one's own free will or choice
