burn

verb
spend a lot of money quickly
3.2 10/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùn / (AmE), / b…úÀên / (BrE)
to use up or spend something, especially money, energy, or calories

Examples

To burn money
The company can't afford to burn cash at this rate for much longer.
They managed to burn through their entire budget in just two months.
When he gets paid, he tends to burn his money on expensive gadgets.
She wanted to burn off the dessert she ate last night.
The body continues to burn calories even after you stop exercising.
You can burn a lot of energy by swimming for an hour.

Synonyms

  • use up
    2.7 4.16/m
    use up is a casual phrase for finishing all of something, while burn often means using or spending resources like money or energy.
  • consume
    3.2 16/m
    consume is a formal verb for using up a resource completely, while burn is more common in speech for spending or using energy.
  • expend
    5.0 5.70/m
    expend is a formal word for spending resources for a purpose, while burn is more general and also used in everyday talk.
  • spend
    1.0 142/m
    spend is mainly used when paying money or time, while burn can mean using money or energy more broadly.
  • burn up
    3.4 0.76/m
    burn up describes using energy or fuel quickly, while burn covers using or spending many things including money and calories.
  • burn off
    3.4 0.46/m
    burn off means using up calories by exercise, while burn is a wider verb that can mean spending money or using energy.
  • run through
    4.4 0.78/m
    run through means using or spending something quickly and often wastefully, while burn is neutral and can simply mean use up.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conserve - to prevent waste or use less, especially with energy or resources
  • synonym: expend - to use up (a resource, such as energy, time, or money)
  • antonym: save - to keep or accumulate money rather than spending it
  • synonym: squander - to spend money wastefully
