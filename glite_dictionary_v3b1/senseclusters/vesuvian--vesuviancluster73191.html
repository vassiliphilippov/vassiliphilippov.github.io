vesuvian

noun
a mineral gemstone, also called idocrase
9.9
Pronunciation: / vəˈzuːviən / (AmE), / vɪˈsjuːviən / (BrE)
a mineral consisting of a hydrated silicate, occurring in green, yellow, or brown crystals, often found in limestone and used as a gemstone

Examples

A vesuvian mineral
Gem collectors prize vesuvian for its unique color and clarity.

The museum display included several striking vesuvian crystals from Italy.
