indolebutyric acid

noun
synthetic plant hormone for root and stem growth
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndoʊlˌbjuːˈtɪrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊlˌbjuːˈtɪərɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic plant hormone used to promote the elongation of stems and roots in plants

Examples

Indolebutyric acid hormone
Researchers found that seedlings exposed to indolebutyric acid grew longer roots than the control group.
Some commercial root powders contain indolebutyric acid as an active ingredient.
To encourage strong root development, the gardener treated the cuttings with indolebutyric acid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auxin - a class of plant hormones promoting cell elongation
