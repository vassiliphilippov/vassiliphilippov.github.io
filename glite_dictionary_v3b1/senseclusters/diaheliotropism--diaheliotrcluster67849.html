diaheliotropism

noun
plant movement toward sun
9.9
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ə.hiː.li.ə.ˈtroʊ.pɪ.zəm/ /; / /diː.ə.hiː.li.ə.ˈtroʊ.pɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /daɪ.ə.hiː.li.ə.ˈtrəʊ.pɪ.zəm/ /; / /diː.ə.hiː.li.ə.ˈtrəʊ.pɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the movement of plant parts, especially leaves or flowers, to face toward the direction of sunlight

Examples

Diaheliotropism in plants
Sunflowers are well known for their diaheliotropism, turning to follow the sun across the sky.
The diaheliotropism of certain leaves helps maximize their exposure to sunlight for photosynthesis.
Researchers studied diaheliotropism in alpine plants to understand how they adapt to high-altitude sunlight.

  • synonym: phototropism - the growth of plants in response to light
