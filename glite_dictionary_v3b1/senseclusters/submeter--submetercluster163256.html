submeter

verb
to measure individual usage
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈmiːtə / (BrE)
to measure or record the utility consumption of individual units or customers using separate meters within a larger property or system

Examples

To submeter water usage
The building owner decided to submeter each apartment to track water usage separately.
Many landlords are now submetering electricity to encourage tenants to save energy.
After they submetered the offices, each company received its own utility bill.
