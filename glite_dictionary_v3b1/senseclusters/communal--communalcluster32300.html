communal

adjective
shared by a group or community
3.9 6.46/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàmjun…ôl / (AmE), / k…ôÀàmjuÀên…ôl / (BrE)
shared or used by all members of a group; relating to ownership, use, or action by a community collectively rather than by individuals.

Examples

Communal kitchen use
The team achieved their success through communal effort and cooperation.
The villagers maintained a communal garden that provided fresh vegetables for everyone.
There was a communal sense of responsibility during the festival preparations.

Synonyms

  • collective
    3.9 26/m
    collective is used for people acting together on a shared plan or decision, while communal stresses shared ownership and use by a community.
  • common
    1.0 119/m
    common means belonging to or found among many people, while communal emphasizes shared ownership and use by a community.
  • shared
    1.0 0.14/m
    shared describes something used or experienced by several people, while communal focuses on community ownership and joint use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collective - done by people acting as a group
  • antonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing, not shared
