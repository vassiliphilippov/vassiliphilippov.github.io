Chilopoda

noun
class of arthropods: centipedes
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɪˈlɑpədə / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊpədə / (BrE)
A class of arthropods comprising centipedes, characterized by elongated bodies with numerous segments, each bearing one pair of legs.

Examples

Chilopoda anatomy
Biology students studied the anatomy of Chilopoda to understand arthropod diversity.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Chilopoda class in the tropical rainforest.
The Chilopoda are easily distinguished from millipedes by their single pair of legs per segment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centipede - a predatory arthropod with many body segments and legs
