penitential

noun
book of penances
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a book or set of rules prescribing penances for sins, especially used in the early Christian church

Examples

A penitential book
In medieval times, priests often referred to a penitential to assign penances for various sins.
The ancient penitentials listed specific punishments for each type of wrongdoing.
Scholars study penitentials to understand early church practices regarding confession and penance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confessional - a place or act of confessing sins
  • synonym: penance - a punishment or act to show sorrow for sin
