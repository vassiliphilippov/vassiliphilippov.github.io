outmeasure

verb
to exceed in measurement
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈmɛʒər / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
to measure more than someone or something else in size, length, or amount

Examples

To outmeasure the old stadium
The new stadium outmeasures the old one by nearly a hundred seats.
In terms of height, the skyscraper easily outmeasures every other building in the city.
Her patience outmeasured everyone else's during the long wait.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall short - to not reach the expected or required amount
  • synonym: outweigh - to be heavier than something else
  • synonym: surpass - to be greater than or go beyond
