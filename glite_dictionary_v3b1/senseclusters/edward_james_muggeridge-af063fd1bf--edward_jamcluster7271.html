Edward James Muggeridge

proper noun
pioneer in motion photography, studied running horses
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛd.wɚd dʒeɪmz ˈmʌɡ.ə.rɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛd.wəd dʒeɪmz ˈmʌɡ.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
(1830–1904) United States motion-picture pioneer, remembered for his serial photographs of running horses that advanced the study of animal locomotion

Examples

Edward James Muggeridge motion-picture pioneer
Edward James Muggeridge revolutionized the field of photography with his studies of animal movement.
Many consider Edward James Muggeridge a foundational figure in the history of cinema.
The innovative techniques developed by Edward James Muggeridge paved the way for modern motion pictures.
