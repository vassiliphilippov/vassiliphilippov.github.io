nonheritable

adjective
not capable of being inherited
6.4
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈhɛrɪtəbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈhɛrɪtəbəl / (BrE)
(of a trait, disease, or characteristic) not able to be passed from parents to offspring through genetic inheritance.

Examples

Nonheritable disease
Some diseases are purely environmental and therefore nonheritable.
The condition was determined to be nonheritable after extensive genetic testing of the family.
Unlike hemophilia, which is genetic, this disorder is entirely nonheritable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heritable - capable of being inherited genetically
  • antonym: inheritable - able to be inherited
