gambier

noun
tropical shrub for tannin
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈgæm.bɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈgæm.bɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a tropical climbing shrub (Uncaria gambir) whose leaves are used to produce a tannin-rich extract for tanning and dyeing

Examples

Gambier plant leaves
The gambier plant is cultivated in Southeast Asia for its valuable extract.
Traditional leather tanning in Indonesia often uses gambier as a natural source of tannin.
Workers collected fresh leaves from the gambier vines early in the morning.
