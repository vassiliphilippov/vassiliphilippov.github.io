rutted

adjective
full of ruts (grooves in a surface)
6.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÊŒtÉªd / (AmE), / ËˆrÊŒtÉªd / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially a road or track) full of long, deep grooves made by the repeated passage of wheels.

Examples

A rutted road
Heavy farm equipment had left the field rutted and uneven.
The old country lane was so rutted that driving became difficult after rain.
We cycled along a rutted path through the forest.

Synonyms

  • rutty
    7.0 0.06/m
    rutty describes a surface full of deep wheel-made tracks that make driving rough, while rutted shows grooves formed by repeated wheels.
  • furrowed
    4.9 0.09/m
    furrowed means marked with long narrow grooves often in soil or on skin, while rutted refers to wheel-made deep tracks on roads.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth - having an even, regular surface; free from ruts or bumps
