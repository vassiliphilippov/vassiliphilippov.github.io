old-world

adjective
characteristic of earlier European times
Pronunciation: / oʊld ˈwɜrld / (AmE), / əʊld ˈwɜːld / (BrE)
Characteristic of former times, especially with reference to traditional European styles or manners.

Examples

Old-world manners
Her manners were so old-world that she seemed out of place in the bustling city.
The little inn had an old-world charm that appealed to all its visitors.
They decorated the house in an old-world style, with antique furniture and embroidered curtains.

  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
