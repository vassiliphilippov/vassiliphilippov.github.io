epibenthic

noun
bottom-dwelling organism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈbɛnθɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈbɛnθɪk / (BrE)
an organism that lives on or just above the bottom surface of a sea or lake

Examples

An epibenthic creature
The researchers observed several epibenthic species thriving on the rocky seabed.
Changes in water quality can greatly affect epibenthic communities in freshwater lakes.
A variety of epibenthic organisms contribute to the overall health of marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthic - organism living at the bottom of a body of water
  • antonym: pelagic - organism living in the open water away from the bottom
