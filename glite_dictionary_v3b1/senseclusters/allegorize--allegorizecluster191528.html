allegorize

verb
interpret as allegory
9.0 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.ə.gə.raɪz/ /; / /ˈæl.ɪ.gə.raɪz/ (AmE), /ˈæl.ə.gə.raɪz/ /; / /ˈæl.ɪ.gə.raɪz/ (BrE)
to interpret or present something as an allegory (a story or image with a hidden meaning); to explain or make something into an allegory

Examples

To allegorize a story
In her essay, she allegorized the historical event as a struggle between light and darkness.
Some critics allegorize fairy tales to reveal their underlying moral messages.
The professor encouraged us to allegorize the poem to uncover its deeper meanings.
Over time, the historical events were increasingly allegorized in literature.
Some scholars tend to allegorize ancient myths to uncover hidden philosophical meanings.
The director chose to allegorize political conflict through his film's use of animal characters.
Many critics allegorise this poem to find a deeper political meaning.
Over centuries, scholars have allegorised these myths to reflect changing values.
The author chose to allegorise the events of her childhood in the novel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegorize - to interpret or treat as an allegory; American spelling variant
  • synonym: symbolize - to represent something by means of symbols
