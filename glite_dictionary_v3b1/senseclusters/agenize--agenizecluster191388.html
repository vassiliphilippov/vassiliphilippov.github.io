agenize

verb
treat flour with Agene
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪnaɪz /; / ˈeɪdʒənaɪz / (BrE)
to treat flour with Agene (nitrogen trichloride) to age or bleach it

Examples

To agenize flour
Many mills used to agenize flour to improve its baking qualities.
Some bakers noticed changes in taste when flour began to be agenized.
The company decided to agenize their wheat flour before the process was banned.
Early in the twentieth century, many mills would agenise flour to improve its appearance.
Regulations soon prohibited companies from continuing to agenise flour due to health risks.
The process used to agenise flour has been largely abandoned in modern baking.
