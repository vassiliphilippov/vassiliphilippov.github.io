bayadere

noun
Indian temple dancer
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪəˈdɛr / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈdɛə / (BrE)
a female temple dancer in India, especially as portrayed in European art and literature

Examples

A bayadere dancer
The artist was inspired by the graceful movements of the bayadere in the performance.
In 19th-century paintings, a bayadere is often shown wearing elaborate jewelry and silk garments.
The novel describes a bayadere who captivates audiences with her expressive dance.

Related Terms
