enfilade

verb
fire along the length of a target (military)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.fəˈleɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛn.fɪˈlɑːd / (BrE)
to direct gunfire along the length of a target, especially a line of troops or a fortification

Examples

To enfilade a line
As the soldiers crossed the bridge, they were enfiladed by fire from both sides.
The commander ordered his artillery to enfilade the defensive trench lines.
The machine guns were positioned to enfilade any advancing enemy troops.
