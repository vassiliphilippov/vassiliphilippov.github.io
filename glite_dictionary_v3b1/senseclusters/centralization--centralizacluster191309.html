centralization

noun
the act of consolidating control centrally
1.2 5.1
Pronunciation: /se.n.trə.lɪz.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /se.n.trə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /se.n.trə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of bringing control, power, or activities under a single central authority or organization

Examples

Many believe the centralization of government power threatens local autonomy and democracy.
The centralization of decision-making often leads to greater efficiency in large organizations.

Some critics argue that too much centralisation can stifle local innovation and autonomy.
The centralisation of power in the capital led to more efficient decision-making.
