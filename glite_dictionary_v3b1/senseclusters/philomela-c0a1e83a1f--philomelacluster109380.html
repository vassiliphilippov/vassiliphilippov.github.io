Philomela

proper noun
mythical nightingale figure
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪləˈmiːlə / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈmiːlə / (BrE)
a figure in Greek mythology who was transformed into a nightingale and is often associated with sorrow and poetic lament

Examples

Greek myth Philomela
The poet compared her sorrow to that of Philomela in his latest work.
In Greek myths, Philomela was transformed into a nightingale after her tragic story.
The song of the nightingale is often linked to the legend of Philomela.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Procne - Philomela's sister in Greek mythology, also transformed into a bird
  • synonym: nightingale - a small songbird often symbolizing sorrow or poetry
