burn

verb
to undergo combustion
1.0 76/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùÀên /; / b…ùn / (AmE), / b…úÀên / (BrE)
to be on fire, to undergo or cause combustion, or to destroy or damage something by fire or heat

Examples

To burn wood
After hours, the fire was still burning in the fireplace.
Some woods burn hotter and longer than others.
The dry leaves burn easily when exposed to a spark.
Please do not burn any waste materials in this area.
The chef accidentally burned the bread in the oven.
The rebels threatened to burn the entire village if their demands were not met.
Lightning can burn an entire forest if it strikes the right spot.
They burn wood to keep the house warm during the winter.
To dispose of the waste safely, the facility will burn it at high temperatures.

Synonyms

  • set on fire
    3.0 3.38/m
    set on fire is a common phrase for causing something to burn, while burn covers both being on fire and causing it.
  • set afire
    4.1 0.11/m
    set afire is a slightly formal way to cause something to burn, while burn is the general verb used in everyday speech.
  • set alight
    4.2 0.08/m
    set alight is common in British English for making something catch fire, while burn is the neutral, global verb for fire.
  • set ablaze
    4.8 0.43/m
    set ablaze is a vivid phrase for starting a fire, while burn is broader and can mean being on fire or causing damage.
  • set aflame
    5.1 0.05/m
    set aflame is a less common way to say light something on fire, while burn is the usual, general verb for fire.
  • ignite
    4.0 3.82/m
    ignite is a slightly technical verb for starting a fire or spark, while burn also includes being on fire and causing heat damage.
  • torch
    6.5 2.51/m
    torch means to set fire to something deliberately, often as arson, while burn is neutral and does not imply intent.
  • combust
    7.4 0.36/m
    combust is a formal or scientific term for starting to burn, while burn is simple everyday language for fire or damage.
  • burn down
    3.0 4.14/m
    burn down means to be destroyed completely by fire, while burn can mean any burning or damaging by heat, big or small.
  • burn up
    3.7 0.97/m
    burn up means to be consumed entirely by fire or heat, while burn can refer to any burning, from small to total destruction.
