burn

verb
to undergo combustion
Pronunciation: / bɝːn /; / bɝn / (AmE), / bɜːn / (BrE)
to be on fire, to undergo or cause combustion, or to destroy or damage something by fire or heat

Examples

To burn wood
After hours, the fire was still burning in the fireplace.
Some woods burn hotter and longer than others.
The dry leaves burn easily when exposed to a spark.
Please do not burn any waste materials in this area.
The chef accidentally burned the bread in the oven.
The rebels threatened to burn the entire village if their demands were not met.
Lightning can burn an entire forest if it strikes the right spot.
They burn wood to keep the house warm during the winter.
To dispose of the waste safely, the facility will burn it at high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: char - to burn until blackened
  • antonym: extinguish - to put out a fire
  • antonym: freeze - to damage with extreme cold (opposite type of damage)
  • synonym: ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
  • synonym: scorch - to damage the surface of something by burning
