Ninurta

proper noun
Mesopotamian god of war, hunting, and agriculture
9.9
Pronunciation: /nɪ.ˈnʊr.tə/ (AmE), /nɪ.ˈnʊə.tə/ (BrE)
An ancient Mesopotamian solar deity, firstborn of Bel, regarded as the god of war, hunting, and agriculture, sometimes identified with the biblical Nimrod.

Examples

Ninurta god of war
Ancient texts often describe Ninurta as a heroic figure battling the forces of chaos.
Artifacts depicting Ninurta have been discovered in many Mesopotamian archaeological sites.
In some myths, Ninurta is credited with teaching humanity the secrets of agriculture.
