oddly

adverb
in a strange or unusual manner
3.4 12/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆÉ‘dli / (AmE), / ËˆÉ’dli / (BrE)
in a strange, unusual, or unexpected way

Examples

Behave oddly
He behaved oddly at the meeting, making everyone feel uncomfortable.
I found the painting oddly beautiful, despite its unusual colors and shapes.
The clock was oddly silent, though everything else in the house seemed normal.
I found his behavior oddly familiar, though we had never met before.
She answered oddly, leaving everyone puzzled by her response.
The room was oddly quiet despite the crowded event.

Synonyms

  • strangely
    3.2 13/m
    strangely is used to describe something unusual or unexpected, while oddly often sounds slightly more informal in speech.
  • weirdly
    3.5 1.58/m
    weirdly is a blunt, informal way to say something is odd, while oddly is more neutral and common.
  • peculiarly
    6.2 0.73/m
    peculiarly sounds slightly formal and points to a strange, not typical feature, while oddly is more everyday.
  • curiously
    4.1 3.94/m
    curiously often suggests surprise or interest as well as oddness, while oddly focuses only on strangeness.
  • bizarrely
    4.4 0.97/m
    bizarrely describes something very strange or extreme, while oddly describes something simply unusual or unexpected.
  • funnily
    5.3 0.41/m
    funnily can mean oddly or amusingly depending on context, while oddly usually means only strange or unexpected.
