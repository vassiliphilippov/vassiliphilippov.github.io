comfort zone

noun
situation where one feels safe or at ease
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌmfərt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət zəʊn / (BrE)
a situation or environment in which a person feels at ease and in control, experiencing little stress or risk

Examples

Step outside your comfort zone
He found himself far outside his comfort zone during the team's challenging expedition.
Trying new activities often means leaving your comfort zone behind.
You won't grow if you stay in your comfort zone all the time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: risk zone - a situation involving uncertainty or difficulty
