oculomotor

noun
third cranial nerve controlling eye movement
7.6 8.2
Pronunciation: /ɑːk.jə.lə.ˈmoʊ.tər/ /; / /ɑːk.jʊ.lə.ˈmoʊ.tər/ (AmE), /ɒk.jʊ.lə.ˈməʊ.tə/ /; / /ɒk.jə.lə.ˈməʊ.tə/ (BrE)
the third cranial nerve, responsible for controlling most of the muscles that move the eyeball

Examples

The oculomotor nerve
A neurologist tested the patient’s oculomotor function after the head injury.
Damage to the oculomotor nerve can result in double vision and drooping eyelids.
The oculomotor is essential for normal eye movement and pupil constriction.
