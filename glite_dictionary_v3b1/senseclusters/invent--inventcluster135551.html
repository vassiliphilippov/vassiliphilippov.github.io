invent

verb
to fabricate; make up (a lie, story, etc.)
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈvent/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈvent/ (BrE)
to make up or create something in your mind, such as an excuse, story, or something untrue

Examples

To invent a story
He was accused of trying to invent an alibi that never existed.
Some children like to invent stories about imaginary friends.
The author decided to invent an entirely fictional town for her latest novel.
He had to invent an excuse for being late to the meeting.
Scientists often invent new theories to explain unusual phenomena.
She managed to invent a clever solution to the problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceive - to form or devise (a plan or idea) in the mind
  • synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • antonym: discover - to find something that already exists rather than create it
  • synonym: fabricate - to make up (a story, information, etc.) with intent to deceive
  • synonym: lie - to say or write something untrue or misleading
