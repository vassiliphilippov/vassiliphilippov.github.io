Saul

proper noun
original name of Paul the Apostle
6.1 1.11/m
Pronunciation: / s…îl / (AmE), / s…îÀêl / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the original name of Paul the Apostle, a Christian missionary and author of several Epistles.

Examples

Saul becomes Paul
Saul became a prominent figure in the early Christian church after his dramatic encounter on the road to Damascus.
Before his conversion, the Apostle Paul was known as Saul.
Many biblical scholars study the transformation of Saul into Paul as a pivotal moment in Christian history.
