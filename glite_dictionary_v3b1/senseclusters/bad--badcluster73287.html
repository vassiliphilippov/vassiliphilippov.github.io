bad

adjective
not working properly; defective
4.7 1.0
Pronunciation: /bæd/ (AmE), /bæd/ (BrE)
not working properly, defective, physically unsound, or diseased; not healthy or in good condition

Examples

A bad battery
A bad battery can prevent your car from starting in the morning.
The bad connection made it impossible to hear the other person clearly.
We had to return the toaster because it was bad and wouldn't heat up.
A bad heart prevented her from climbing the stairs.
He had to go to the dentist because of his bad teeth.
My father can't play tennis anymore because of his bad back.
His headaches made him feel bad most mornings.
I felt bad after eating too much dessert last night.
She stayed home from work because she was feeling bad with a cold.
