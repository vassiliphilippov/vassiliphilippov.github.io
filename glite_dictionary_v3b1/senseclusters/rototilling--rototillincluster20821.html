rototilling

noun
soil preparation method
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.tə.tɪl.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈroʊ.toʊ.tɪl.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.tə.tɪl.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈrəʊ.təʊ.tɪl.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of breaking up and turning over soil using a powered rotary tiller

Examples

Rototilling the soil
Many gardeners recommend rototilling the soil before planting vegetables in the spring.
After rototilling, the ground was much easier to work with and free of weeds.
Proper rototilling can help improve soil aeration and drainage in your garden beds.
