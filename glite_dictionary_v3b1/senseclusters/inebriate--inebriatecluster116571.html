inebriate

verb
to fill with strong emotion or exhilaration
Pronunciation: / ɪˈniː.brieɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈniː.brɪeɪt / (BrE)
(literary) To fill (someone) with strong emotion or exhilaration.

Examples

Inebriated with happiness
She felt inebriated with joy after hearing the good news.
Success seemed to inebriate him, filling him with boundless energy and optimism.
The audience was inebriated by the soaring music of the orchestra.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excite - to stir up feelings of enthusiasm or eagerness
  • synonym: thrill - to cause someone to feel intense excitement
