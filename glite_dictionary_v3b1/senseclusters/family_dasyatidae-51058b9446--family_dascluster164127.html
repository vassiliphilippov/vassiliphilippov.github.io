family Dasyatidae

noun
family of sting rays
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli ˌdæsiəˈtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌdæsɪəˈtaɪdi / (BrE)
A family of cartilaginous fish commonly known as sting rays, characterized by their flattened bodies and long, whiplike tails, found in coastal tropical and subtropical marine waters.

Examples

family Dasyatidae sting rays
Members of the family Dasyatidae typically have venomous spines in their tails for defense.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the family Dasyatidae to understand how these rays adapted to different environments.
The family Dasyatidae includes many species of sting rays found throughout the world's oceans.
