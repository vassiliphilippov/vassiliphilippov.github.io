expatriate

verb
expel from one's native country
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈspeɪtriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɛksˈpætrɪeɪt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave their native country; to banish or exile.

Examples

To expatriate a leader
Following the coup, several leaders were expatriated and stripped of their citizenship.
The government decided to expatriate the dissident artists for their political activities.
The regime threatened to expatriate anyone who spoke out against its policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exile - to force someone to leave and live outside their native country
