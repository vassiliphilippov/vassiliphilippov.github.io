therm

noun
unit of heat (100,000 BTU)
9.9
Pronunciation: / θɜrm / (AmE), / θɜːm / (BrE)
a unit of heat equal to 100,000 British thermal units, commonly used to measure natural gas.

Examples

A therm of gas
Industrial customers often buy natural gas by the therm.
One therm is approximately equivalent to 29.3 kilowatt-hours of energy.
The gas bill was calculated based on the number of therms used during the month.
