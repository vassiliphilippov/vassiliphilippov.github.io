Lamarckian

adjective
relating to Lamarckism
Pronunciation: / ləˈmɑrkiən / (AmE), / ləˈmɑːkiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Lamarckism, the theory that characteristics acquired or lost during an organism's lifetime can be inherited by its offspring.

Examples

Lamarckian theory
He gave a lecture on Lamarckian inheritance and its historical significance.
Some early nineteenth century scientists promoted Lamarckian ideas about evolution.
The debate between Lamarckian and Darwinian evolution persists in academic circles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Darwinian - relating to Charles Darwin's theories of evolution by natural selection
