common shares

noun
ordinary shares with voting rights
2.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.mən ʃerz/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.ən ʃeəz/ (BrE)
shares that represent ownership in a corporation, giving holders the right to a share of profits and voting rights, as opposed to preferred shares.

Examples

Buy common shares
Most retail investors purchase common shares rather than preferred shares.
She decided to invest in common shares to gain a voice in the company's decisions.
The value of his common shares increased as the company reported strong earnings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: preferred shares - shares given preference in dividends and asset distribution
  • synonym: stock - general term for ownership shares in a corporation
