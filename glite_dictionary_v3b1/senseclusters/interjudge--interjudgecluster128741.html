interjudge

adjective
between judges
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈdʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
relating to comparison, agreement, or consistency between two or more judges or evaluators

Examples

Interjudge agreement
Researchers calculated interjudge reliability to ensure the scoring was consistent across all evaluators.
The study found high interjudge agreement when assessing the quality of the essays.
Training sessions were held to improve interjudge consistency among the panel members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrater - relating to agreement or consistency between different raters
  • antonym: intrajudge - relating to consistency within the same judge
