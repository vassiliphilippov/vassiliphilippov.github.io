lungful

noun
amount of air inhaled
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌŋfʊl / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋfʊl / (BrE)
the amount of air that fills your lungs in one breath

Examples

A lungful of air
He took a deep lungful of fresh mountain air before starting the hike.
After swimming underwater, she surfaced and gasped for a lungful of oxygen.
The firefighter emerged from the smoke, grateful for every clean lungful he could get.
He took a huge lungful of salty sea air before diving underwater.
After running up the hill, she paused to catch her breath and drew in a deep lungful.
The smoke was so thick that just one lungful made him cough.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breath - the air taken into or expelled from the lungs in a single act
  • synonym: gulp - a large and hurried swallow of air or liquid
