soft

adjective
tolerant or lenient
2.0 7.63/m
Pronunciation: / sÉ”ft / (AmE), / sÉ’ft / (BrE)
not strict or severe; lenient, gentle, or willing to compromise in attitude or approach

Examples

Soft on crime
Many people criticized the new law for being too soft on crime.
Some argue that judges are too soft when sentencing first-time offenders.
The teacher was often soft with students who turned in late assignments.
She took a soft approach during the negotiations, seeking compromise rather than conflict.
Some voters felt the candidate's stance on crime was too soft to be effective.
The president was criticized for being soft on immigration policy.
His parents are too soft on him, letting him do whatever he wants.
She has a soft heart and always forgives quickly.
The teacher took a soft approach with the troubled student.

Synonyms

  • lenient
    4.4 1.58/m
    lenient is gentle in punishment and allows rule-breaking, while soft is a broader word for being not strict or severe.
  • gentle
    1.0 14/m
    gentle is kind and mild in manner or touch, while soft often means not strict and ready to compromise.
  • indulgent
    6.1 3.02/m
    indulgent lets people do what they want even if harmful, while soft simply means not strict or willing to compromise.
