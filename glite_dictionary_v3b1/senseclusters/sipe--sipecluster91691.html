sipe

verb
to cut slits for grip
9.6
Pronunciation: / saɪp / (AmE), / saɪp / (BrE)
to cut thin slits or grooves in the surface of a tire, shoe sole, or similar material to improve traction or water drainage

Examples

To sipe a tire
The mechanic will sipe your tires to help prevent slipping on wet roads.
Some hikers sipe the soles of their boots for better traction on icy trails.
Before the race, the team decided to sipe the tires for improved performance in the rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groove - to cut a long, narrow cut or indentation
  • synonym: score - to make shallow cuts or lines on a surface
