turbulent

adjective
marked by disorder or unrest
6.6 2.91/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…úrbj…ôl…ônt / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀêbj äl…ônt / (BrE)
characterized by confusion, disorder, or unrest; marked by disturbance or turbulence

Examples

Turbulent times
Economic conditions have been particularly turbulent this year, causing uncertainty for many businesses.
She had a turbulent relationship with her parents during her teenage years.
The country went through a turbulent period following the revolution.

Synonyms

  • tumultuous
    7.5 2.73/m
    tumultuous is full of loud, excited activity and strong feeling, while turbulent describes unrest and disturbance in situations or places.
  • chaotic
    4.7 7.70/m
    chaotic means completely disordered and unpredictable, while turbulent suggests disturbance, unrest, or violent change rather than total breakdown.
