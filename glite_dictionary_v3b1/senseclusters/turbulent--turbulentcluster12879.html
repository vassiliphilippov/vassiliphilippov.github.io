turbulent

adjective
marked by disorder or unrest
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrbjələnt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊlənt / (BrE)
characterized by confusion, disorder, or unrest; marked by disturbance or turbulence

Examples

Turbulent times
Economic conditions have been particularly turbulent this year, causing uncertainty for many businesses.
She had a turbulent relationship with her parents during her teenage years.
The country went through a turbulent period following the revolution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - free from disturbance or turmoil
  • synonym: tumultuous - involving a lot of noise, confusion, or violent change
