Teton Sioux

noun
western branch of Sioux people; Native American group
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːtɒn suː / (AmE), / ˈtiːtɒn suː / (BrE)
a member of the western branch of the Sioux people, consisting of several groups who traditionally lived on the plains

Examples

Teton Sioux people
Anthropologists have studied the traditions and language of the Teton Sioux.
Many Teton Sioux moved westward in search of bison on the Great Plains.
The Teton Sioux played a significant role in the history of the American West.
