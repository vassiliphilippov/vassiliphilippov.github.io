incipit

noun
opening words of text
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnsɪpɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɪpɪt / (BrE)
the opening words or beginning section of a text, manuscript, or musical piece, sometimes used as its title

Examples

The incipit of a text
The ancient poem is known by its incipit because it has no official title.
Scholars often refer to medieval texts by their incipit when cataloging manuscripts.
The incipit of the letter set the tone for the entire message.
The incipit of the ancient manuscript was beautifully decorated with gold leaf.
Scholars often refer to the incipit when identifying anonymous medieval texts.
The incipit of the symphony immediately captures the listener's attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epilogue - a concluding section of a literary or musical work
  • synonym: prologue - an introduction to a literary or musical work
  • synonym: title - the name of a book, composition, or other work
