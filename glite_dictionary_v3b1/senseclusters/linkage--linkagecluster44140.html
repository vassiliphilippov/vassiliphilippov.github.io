linkage

noun
tendency of genes to be inherited together
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
(Genetics) The tendency of genes located close together on the same chromosome to be inherited together.

Examples

Genetic linkage
Because of linkage, genes for eye color and hair color may be passed on as a group.
Researchers mapped the gene linkage on chromosome 12 to study inherited diseases.
The discovery of genetic linkage helped scientists understand how certain traits are inherited together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recombination - (Genetics) the process by which genetic material is rearranged during meiosis
