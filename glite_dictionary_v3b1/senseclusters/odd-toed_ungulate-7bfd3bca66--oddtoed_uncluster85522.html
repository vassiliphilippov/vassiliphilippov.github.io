odd-toed ungulate

noun
hoofed mammal with an odd number of toes
7.7 8.0
Pronunciation: /ɑːd toʊd ˈʌŋ.gjə.leɪt/ /; / /ɑːd toʊd ˈʌŋ.gjə.lət/ /; / /ɑːd təʊd ˈʌŋ.gjə.leɪt/ (AmE), /ɒd təʊd ˈʌŋ.gjə.lət/ /; / /ɒd təʊd ˈʌŋ.gjə.leɪt/ (BrE)
a hoofed mammal of the order Perissodactyla, having an odd number of toes on each foot, such as horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs

Examples

An odd-toed ungulate animal
Rhinoceroses, tapirs, and zebras all belong to the group known as odd-toed ungulates.
The horse is a classic example of an odd-toed ungulate in the animal kingdom.
Unlike cows and deer, which have two or four toes, an odd-toed ungulate typically has one or three toes on each foot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: even-toed ungulate - a hoofed mammal with an even number of toes, such as cattle or pigs
