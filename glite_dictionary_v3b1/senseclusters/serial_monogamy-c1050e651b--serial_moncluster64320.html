serial monogamy

noun
succession of exclusive relationships
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriəl məˈnɑɡəmi / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl məˈnɒɡəmi / (BrE)
the practice of engaging in a succession of monogamous relationships, typically one after another, often involving repeated divorces or breakups

Examples

Serial monogamy practice
He has been married four times, which is a classic example of serial monogamy.
Modern dating culture often leads to serial monogamy instead of traditional marriage.
Some people practice serial monogamy rather than staying in a lifelong partnership.
