Plough

noun
asterism of seven stars in Ursa Major
5.1
Pronunciation: / plaʊ / (AmE), / plaʊ / (BrE)
a group of seven bright stars forming a prominent pattern in the constellation Ursa Major, known as an asterism and commonly called the Big Dipper in American English

Examples

The Plough stars
British stargazers often refer to the Plough, while Americans call it the Big Dipper.
Many people use the Plough to find the North Star in the night sky.
The Plough is a prominent feature of the constellation Ursa Major, easily visible in the Northern Hemisphere.
