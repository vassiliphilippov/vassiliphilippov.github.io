twine

verb
form by twisting together strands
5.7 3.4
Pronunciation: / twaɪn / (AmE), / twaɪn / (BrE)
to twist or wind strands, threads, or things together, or to twist something around something else

Examples

To twine threads together
He twined together the strips of leather to make a simple rope.
She twined several strands of thread to create a strong cord.
The vines quickly twine around the metal fence as they grow.
He twined his fingers together nervously as he waited for the results.
She expertly twined the ribbon around the box to make a bow.
The vine twined itself around the old wooden fence.
He likes to twine the threads before weaving them into fabric.
She carefully twined the yellow ribbons around the gift box.
The vines twined themselves around the old wooden fence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coil - to arrange or wind in a spiral or sequence of loops
  • synonym: intertwine - to twist or twine together, intricately
  • synonym: twist - to form into a bent, curling, or spiral shape
  • antonym: untwist - to reverse the action of twisting; to straighten after being twisted
  • synonym: wind - to wrap or twist something around a central object
