brassbound

adjective
rigidly fixed or inflexible (especially of traditions)
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbræsˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of attitudes, rules, or traditions) rigidly fixed and unlikely to change; inflexibly entrenched.

Examples

Brassbound rules
He found it difficult to fit in with the brassbound traditions of his new workplace.
Many critics argue that brassbound regulations prevent meaningful reform in the education system.
The company followed a brassbound set of procedures that left no room for innovation.
