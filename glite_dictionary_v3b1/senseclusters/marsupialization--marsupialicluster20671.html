marsupialization

noun
surgical pouch creation
Pronunciation: / mɑrˌsuːpiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɑːˌsuːpiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / mɑːˌsjuːpiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a pouch or open cavity is created to allow continuous drainage, usually to treat a cyst or abscess

Examples

Marsupialization surgery
The surgeon performed marsupialization to treat the patient's recurring Bartholin's cyst.
After marsupialization, the wound was able to drain properly and heal over time.
Doctors may recommend marsupialization for certain types of cysts that do not respond to other treatments.
The doctor recommended marsupialization to treat the recurring cyst and prevent future infections.
After the marsupialization, the patient needed to keep the area clean to promote healing.
Successful marsupialization can reduce the risk of a cyst returning by allowing continuous drainage.
