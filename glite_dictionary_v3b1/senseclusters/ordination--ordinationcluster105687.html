ordination

noun
religious appointment ceremony
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrdnˈeɪʃən /; / ˌɔːr.dəˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌɔː.dɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or ceremony of officially making someone a priest, minister, or other religious leader

Examples

Priest ordination
After his ordination, he began serving as a parish priest in the countryside.
He spent years studying theology before finally receiving ordination into the clergy.
The cathedral was filled with friends and family for her ordination as a minister.
After his ordination, he began serving the local parish as a priest.
He spent years in theological study before finally receiving ordination.
Her family attended her ordination ceremony at the cathedral.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consecration - the act of making something sacred, often in a religious rite
  • antonym: deposition - removal from a religious office
  • synonym: installation - the act of formally placing someone in an office or position
