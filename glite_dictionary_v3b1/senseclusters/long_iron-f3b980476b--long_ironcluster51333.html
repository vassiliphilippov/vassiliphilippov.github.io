long iron

noun
golf club for long, low shots
5.2
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈaɪən / (BrE)
A type of golf club with a long shaft and a steeply angled face, used for hitting long, low shots.

Examples

A long iron club
His shot with the long iron landed just short of the green.
Many beginners struggle to use a long iron effectively.
She switched from a long iron to a hybrid for her second shot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short iron - a golf club with a shorter shaft for high, short shots
