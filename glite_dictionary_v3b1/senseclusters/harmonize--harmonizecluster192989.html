harmonize

verb
compose harmony for music
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange, sing, or play musical notes that combine to produce harmony

Examples

To harmonize a song
She loves to harmonize simple melodies to make them richer and more engaging.
The composer was asked to harmonize the folk tune for a four-part choir.
The team worked together to harmonize the song for the concert performance.
She loves to harmonize with her friends at karaoke night.
The choir members harmonize beautifully during the chorus.
The two guitars harmonize to create a rich, layered sound.
As the band played, the guitarist began to harmonize with the lead singer.
She learned to harmonize with the melody after years of practice.
The choir members harmonized beautifully during the final chorus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to adapt a piece of music for performance by particular voices or instruments
  • synonym: blend - to mix different voices or instruments smoothly together
  • synonym: compose - to write music, especially a piece of music
  • antonym: discord - lack of harmony, especially in music
  • synonym: harmonize - American spelling of 'harmonise', meaning to bring musical sounds into harmony
