reseller

noun
person who resells
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌsɛlɚ /; / ˌriːˈsɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈriːˌsɛlə /; / ˌriːˈsɛlə / (BrE)
a person or business that buys goods or services to sell them to others, usually for profit (sometimes adding extra services or support)

Examples

A reseller of goods
The reseller bought dozens of concert tickets to sell them at a profit online.
Many electronics are purchased by a reseller and then offered to customers at higher prices.
Fans were frustrated when a reseller made it difficult to buy tickets at face value.
The reseller offered technical support and training for the new software package.
Many small businesses buy products from a reseller instead of directly from the manufacturer.
Our company became an authorized reseller of computer hardware last year.
The reseller purchased smartphones in bulk to offer them at lower prices online.
Many small businesses act as a reseller for popular clothing brands.
After buying limited-edition sneakers, he became a successful reseller at local markets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buyer - a person who purchases goods or services
  • antonym: consumer - a person who buys goods or services for personal use
  • synonym: distributor - a company that supplies goods to stores or other businesses
  • antonym: manufacturer - a company that makes products, usually in large quantities
  • synonym: retailer - a person or business that sells goods to the public
  • synonym: scalper - a person who resells tickets at a higher price
  • synonym: wholesaler - a person or company that sells goods in large quantities to businesses
