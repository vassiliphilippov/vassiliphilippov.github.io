Synonyms

  • awkwardness
    4.5 2/m
    awkwardness is a clumsy, uncomfortable feeling in social situations, while embarrassment is a painful sense of shame or self-consciousness.
  • self-consciousness
    6.5 2/m
    self-consciousness is a feeling of being watched or judged, while embarrassment is a wider mix of shame and awkwardness.
  • sheepishness
    5.6 0.05/m
    sheepishness is a shy, guilty look after a small mistake, while embarrassment covers many awkward or shameful moments.
  • abashment
    8.8 0.01/m
    abashment is a modest, inward discomfort often from shame, while embarrassment can be public and more visible.
  • shamefacedness
    9.3 0.01/m
    shamefacedness is a shy, ashamed manner often shown in formal settings, while embarrassment can be sudden and everyday.
  • mortification
    8.6 0.3/m
    mortification is a deep, intense shame that wounds pride, while embarrassment is usually milder and less lasting.
  • humiliation
    4.2 8/m
    humiliation is a public, often forced loss of self-respect, while embarrassment is often personal and less severe.
