compromise verdict

noun
verdict reached by improper juror compromise
7.9 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.prə.maɪz ˈvɜːr.dɪkt/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.prə.maɪz ˈvɜː.dɪkt/ (BrE)
A verdict in a trial that results from improper agreement or concessions among jurors on important issues, rather than from unbiased deliberation on the evidence.

Examples

A compromise verdict
A compromise verdict can undermine the fairness of a court proceeding.
Attorneys argued that the jury's compromise verdict invalidated the integrity of the entire trial.
The judge declared a mistrial upon discovering that the jury had reached a compromise verdict rather than a unanimous one.
