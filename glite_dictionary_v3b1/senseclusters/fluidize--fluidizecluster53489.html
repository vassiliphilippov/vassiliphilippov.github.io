fluidize

verb
to make more flexible
Pronunciation: / ˈfluːɪˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪdaɪz / (BrE)
to make a process, system, or situation more flexible, smooth, or adaptable, often by reducing rigidity

Examples

To fluidize a system
The new software helped fluidize communication between different departments.
Leaders must fluidize their strategies to adapt to changing market conditions.
The company tried to fluidize its workflow to improve productivity and morale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rigidify - to make something more rigid or inflexible
  • synonym: streamline - to make a process simpler and more efficient
