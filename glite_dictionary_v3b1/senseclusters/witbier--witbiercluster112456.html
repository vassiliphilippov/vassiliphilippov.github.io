witbier

noun
Belgian-style wheat beer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪt.bɪr / (AmE), / ˈvɪt.bɪə / (BrE)
A pale, cloudy wheat beer of Belgian origin, typically brewed with barley, wheat, and spices such as coriander and orange peel.

Examples

A witbier beer
Many local breweries have started making their own witbier infused with citrus flavors.
She prefers a witbier over darker ales, especially in the summer.
The menu recommended pairing the seafood dish with a light witbier.

Related Terms
