mandrake

noun
a plant with forked root and mythic powers
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmænˌdreɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæn.dreɪk / (BrE)
a plant of the nightshade family, native to southern Europe and North Africa, having purple or white flowers, yellow berries, and a forked root, traditionally thought to have magical powers

Examples

Wild mandrake plant
Botanists have studied the properties of the Mediterranean mandrake for centuries.
In ancient folklore, the mandrake was believed to scream when uprooted from the ground.
The wizard used a dried mandrake root in his potion.
