geometric isomer

noun
compound differing in atomic arrangement
9.3
Pronunciation: / dʒiˌɑːmɛtrɪk ˈaɪsəmər / (AmE), / dʒɪˌɒmɪtrɪk ˈaɪsəmə / (BrE)
a chemical compound with the same molecular formula as another but a different spatial arrangement of atoms, resulting in distinct chemical properties

Examples

Geometric isomers in chemistry
Cis and trans forms of butene are classic examples of geometric isomers in organic chemistry.
Light absorption differs between the two geometric isomers of a particular compound.
Researchers often study the properties of geometric isomers to understand their different biological effects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: structural isomer - a compound with the same molecular formula but a different structural formula
