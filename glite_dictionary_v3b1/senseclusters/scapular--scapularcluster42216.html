scapular

noun
feather covering shoulder of a bird
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈskæpjələr / (AmE), / ˈskapjʊlə / (BrE)
a feather or group of feathers covering the shoulder area of a bird, often aiding in streamlining and protection

Examples

Scapular feathers
During molting season, some birds lose their scapular feathers first.
The distinctive white scapular makes the heron easy to identify from a distance.
The eagle ruffled its scapular feathers as it perched on the branch.
