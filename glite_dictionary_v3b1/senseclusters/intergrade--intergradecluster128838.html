intergrade

verb
to blend gradually
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈɡreɪd / (BrE)
to blend or merge gradually between two forms, species, or groups, creating intermediate types

Examples

Species intergrade naturally

The colors on the artist's canvas intergrade smoothly from blue to green.
Where the habitats overlap, the two animal populations intergrade rather than remain distinct.
Some bird species intergrade where their habitats overlap, creating populations with mixed features.
The colors of the two flowers intergrade in the field, making it hard to tell them apart.
In certain regions, the two subspecies intergrade and produce offspring with intermediate characteristics.
