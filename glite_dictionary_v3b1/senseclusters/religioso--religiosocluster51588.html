religioso

adjective
solemn musical style
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛlɪdʒiˈoʊsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌrɛlɪdʒiˈəʊsəʊ / (BrE)
used as a musical direction to indicate that a passage should be played in a solemn, devout, or religious manner

Examples

Religioso passage
The choir sang the hymn in a religioso style, filling the hall with reverence.
The conductor asked the orchestra to perform the passage religioso to capture its spiritual mood.
Her interpretation of the piece was deeply religioso, moving the audience to silence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devout - showing deep religious feeling or commitment
  • antonym: playful - lighthearted or cheerful in style
  • synonym: solemn - serious and dignified in manner or appearance
