bicolor

noun
two-colored object
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌkʌlə / (BrE)
an object, organism, or design that has two different colors

Examples

A bicolor flag
The national flag is a bicolor of blue and yellow.
Many flowers in the garden are bicolors, displaying two distinct shades on their petals.
The artist painted a striking bicolor pattern on the wall for visual impact.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monochrome - an object or image in only one color
  • degree higher: tricolor - an object or design with three colors
