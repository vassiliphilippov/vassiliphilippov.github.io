ciliate

noun
single-celled protozoan with cilia
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪeɪt / (BrE)
a single-celled protozoan organism that has hair-like organelles (cilia) on its surface, used for movement or feeding

Examples

A ciliate organism
Scientists study a ciliate to better understand how single-celled organisms feed and reproduce.
The pond contains several types of ciliate that are important for aquatic ecosystems.
Under the microscope, the ciliate moved gracefully using the tiny hairs covering its surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protozoan - a single-celled microscopic organism, such as an amoeba
