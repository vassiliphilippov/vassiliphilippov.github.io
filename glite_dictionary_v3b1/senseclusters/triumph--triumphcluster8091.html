triumph

verb
achieve victory or success
1.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtraɪ.əmf/ (AmE), /ˈtraɪ.əmf/ /; / /ˈtraɪ.ʌmf/ (BrE)
to achieve victory or success, or to feel or show great joy after a success

Examples

To triumph in competition
After years of hard work, she finally triumphed in her professional goals.
Despite the odds, the small nation managed to triumph over its enemies.
Justice will triumph in the end, no matter how long it takes.
She could not help but triumph when her hard work paid off.
The fans triumphed loudly after their team won the championship.
We watched them triumph over adversity with smiles and laughter.
He still triumphs in telling the story of his achievement years later.
She could not help but triumph when she heard the great news.
The team triumphed after months of hard work and dedication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exult - to feel or show triumphant elation or jubilation
  • antonym: fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • synonym: prevail - to prove more powerful or superior; to win in the end
  • synonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
