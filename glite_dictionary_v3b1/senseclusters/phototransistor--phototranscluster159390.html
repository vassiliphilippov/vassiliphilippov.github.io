phototransistor

noun
light-sensitive transistor
7.4
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.træn.ˈzɪs.tər/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.træn.ˈzɪs.tə/ (BrE)
an electronic component that changes its electrical output when exposed to light, used to detect or measure light levels

Examples

A phototransistor sensor
The phototransistor in the circuit detects changes in light and triggers the alarm.
Engineers use a phototransistor to measure the intensity of light in the laboratory.
When the room becomes dark, the phototransistor automatically turns on the night light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photodiode - a semiconductor device that converts light into an electrical current
  • synonym: transistor - a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals
