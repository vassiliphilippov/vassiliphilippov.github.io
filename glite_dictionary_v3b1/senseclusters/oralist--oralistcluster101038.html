oralist

noun
advocate of speech teaching
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports teaching deaf people to speak and lip-read instead of using sign language

Examples

Oralist teaching speech
The oralist believed that speech and lip-reading would help deaf students integrate into society.
Many schools once followed the oralist method, focusing on spoken language rather than sign language.
As an oralist, she encouraged families to practice speaking with their deaf children every day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manualist - a person who supports teaching deaf people through sign language
