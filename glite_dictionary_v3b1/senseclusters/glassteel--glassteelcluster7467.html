glassteel

noun
glass fiber material
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæsˌstiːl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːsˌstiːl / (BrE)
a strong, lightweight material made from glass fibers embedded in plastic, used for construction and manufacturing

Examples

The new car model features a body made of glassteel to reduce weight and improve safety.
Engineers chose glassteel for the bridge panels because it resists corrosion and is easy to install.
Many modern boats use glassteel for their hulls due to its strength and durability.
