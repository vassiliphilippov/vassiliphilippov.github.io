preventionist

adjective
favoring prevention
Pronunciation: / prɪˈvɛnʃənɪst / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛnʃənɪst / (BrE)
favoring or relating to policies or actions intended to prevent problems such as disease or crime before they occur

Examples

Preventionist approach
The government introduced preventionist policies to reduce the spread of infectious diseases.
Many experts support a preventionist approach to tackling social issues like drug abuse.
A preventionist strategy can save resources by addressing problems before they escalate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preventive - intended to stop something from happening
  • antonym: reactive - responding to problems after they occur
