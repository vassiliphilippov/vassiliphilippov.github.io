judgment in personam

noun
legal judgment against a person or entity
7.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɪn pɜːr.ˈsoʊ.nəm/ /; / /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ən pɜːr.ˈsoʊ.nəm/ (AmE), /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɪn pə.ˈsəʊ.nəm/ /; / /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ən pə.ˈsəʊ.nəm/ (BrE)
a court judgment rendered against a specific individual or entity, obligating them to pay money damages or perform a specific act

Examples

A judgment in personam order
A judgment in personam is enforceable against the individual wherever they may be found.
The court issued a judgment in personam requiring the defendant to pay damages to the plaintiff.
Unlike a judgment affecting property, a judgment in personam creates a personal obligation.
A judgement in personam binds only the parties named in the lawsuit, not the general public.
The court issued a judgement in personam requiring the defendant to compensate the plaintiff for damages.
Unlike a judgment affecting property, a judgement in personam enforces obligations against an individual or corporation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: judgement in rem - a legal judgement affecting the status of a property rather than a particular person
  • antonym: judgment in rem - a judgment affecting the interests of all persons in a particular property
