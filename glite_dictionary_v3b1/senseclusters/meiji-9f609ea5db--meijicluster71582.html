Meiji

proper noun
Japanese emperor 1867–1912
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒi / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒi / (BrE)
the Japanese emperor who reigned from 1867 to 1912 and led Japan's modernization during the Meiji Restoration

Examples

Emperor Meiji reign
During the Meiji era, Japan rapidly adopted Western technology and institutions.
The reforms introduced by Meiji changed Japanese society in many fundamental ways.
Historians often credit Meiji with opening Japan to the modern world.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Emperor Komei - the Japanese emperor who reigned before Meiji, from 1846 to 1867
  • degree higher: Emperor Taisho - the Japanese emperor who succeeded Meiji, reigning from 1912 to 1926
