oversow

verb
to sow too many seeds
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsəʊ / (BrE)
to sow seeds in a place too thickly or in excessive quantity, leading to overcrowding of plants

Examples

To oversow a garden
If you oversow the garden bed, the plants will compete for sunlight and nutrients.
Farmers are careful not to oversow their fields to avoid poor crop yields.
He accidentally oversowed the lawn, resulting in patchy and crowded grass growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overplant - to plant too many seeds or plants in an area
  • antonym: undersow - to sow too few seeds in an area
