pocket gopher

noun
burrowing rodent with cheek pouches
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkɪt ˈɡoʊfər / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ˈɡəʊfə / (BrE)
a burrowing rodent of the family Geomyidae, native to Central America and southwestern North America, characterized by large external cheek pouches for carrying food.

Examples

A pocket gopher burrow
A pocket gopher uses its large cheek pouches to transport food back to its burrow.
Farmers often have to deal with the damage caused by pocket gophers to their crops.
The pocket gopher is known for the tunnels it digs in gardens and fields.
