nae

determiner
Scots for 'no'
Pronunciation: / neɪ / (AmE), / neɪ / (BrE)
used before a noun to mean 'no' or 'not any', especially in Scottish English.

Examples

nae bread
There was nae bread left in the kitchen when she opened the cupboard this morning.
He has nae idea what happened after the party last night.
We found nae evidence to support the rumor in the village.

Related Terms

  • antonym: any - some or even one (used in negative statements)
  • synonym: no - not any; none
