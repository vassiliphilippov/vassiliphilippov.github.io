gravy

noun
meat-based sauce
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪ.vi /; / ˈɡreɪvi / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.vi /; / ˈɡreɪvi / (BrE)
a sauce or the juices from cooked meat, often served with meat dishes

Examples

Beef with gravy
After roasting the beef, he made a delicious gravy from the pan juices.
She poured rich gravy over her mashed potatoes.
The turkey tasted dry without any gravy on it.
She collected the flavorful gravy from the pan and served it with mashed potatoes.
Sometimes, a little water is added to the gravy to stretch it further.
The chef served the roast beef with plenty of gravy poured over the top.
