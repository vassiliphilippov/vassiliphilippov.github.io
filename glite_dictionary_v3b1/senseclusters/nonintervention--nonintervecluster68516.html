nonintervention

noun
policy of not interfering in others' affairs
7.5
Pronunciation: / nɑnˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / nɒnˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
The policy or practice of not becoming involved in the affairs or conflicts of other countries, especially in international relations.

Examples

Policy of nonintervention
International law often respects the principle of nonintervention in sovereign states.
Many argued that nonintervention would prevent unnecessary escalation of the conflict.
The government adopted a policy of nonintervention in the neighboring country's civil war.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intervention - the action of becoming intentionally involved in a difficult situation
  • synonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict
