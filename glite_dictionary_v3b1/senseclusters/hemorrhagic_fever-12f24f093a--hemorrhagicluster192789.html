hemorrhagic fever

noun
viral disease causing fever and bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.vɚ /; / ˌhɛmərˈædʒɪk ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və /; / ˌhiːmɒˈrædʒɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
Any of a group of severe infectious diseases caused by certain viruses, characterized by fever and internal bleeding, often occurring in specific geographic regions.
