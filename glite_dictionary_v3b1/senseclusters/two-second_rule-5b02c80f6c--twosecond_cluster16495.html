two-second rule

noun
informal food safety guideline (dropped food)
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː ˌsɛkənd ruːl / (AmE), / ˈtuː ˌsɛkənd ruːl / (BrE)
an informal guideline suggesting that food dropped on the floor for less than two seconds is still safe to eat

Examples

The two-second rule for food
After dropping her cookie, she picked it up quickly, citing the two-second rule.
My brother insists the two-second rule makes it okay to eat dropped pizza.
Some scientists say there’s no scientific truth to the two-second rule at all.

Related Terms
