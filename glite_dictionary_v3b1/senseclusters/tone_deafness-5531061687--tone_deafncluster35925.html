tone deafness

noun
inability to distinguish musical pitch
Pronunciation: / toʊn ˈdɛfnəs / (AmE), / təʊn ˈdɛfnəs / (BrE)
An inability to distinguish differences in musical pitch, making it difficult to recognize or reproduce musical notes accurately.

Examples

Tone deafness in music
Despite his tone deafness, he enjoyed listening to music and attending concerts.
His tone deafness made it impossible for him to sing in tune with the choir.
She discovered her tone deafness when she couldn't tell one note from another during music class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amusia - a musical disorder marked by the inability to recognize musical tones or reproduce them
