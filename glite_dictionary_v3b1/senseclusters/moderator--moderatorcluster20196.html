(in statistics and research) a variable that affects the strength or direction of the relationship between two other variables; often tested as an interaction effect.

Examples

Moderator variable in research
Age often acts as a moderator in studies linking stress levels to physical health outcomes.
The researchers found that socioeconomic status was a significant moderator between education and job satisfaction.
When a variable is a moderator, effects differ at high and low levels of that variable.
