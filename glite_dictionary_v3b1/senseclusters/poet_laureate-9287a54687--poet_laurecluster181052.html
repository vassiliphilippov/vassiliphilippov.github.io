poet laureate

noun
honorary poet for a group or region
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊət lɔˈreɪət /; / ˈpoʊət lɔˈrɪət / (AmE), / ˌpəʊɪt lɒˈrɪət /; / ˈpəʊɪt lɒˈrɪət / (BrE)
a poet who is officially or honorarily recognized as representing a group, region, or nation, often expected to write poems for special occasions

Examples

The poet laureate title
He became the unofficial poet laureate of his neighborhood, reciting verses at every local event.
In their hearts, she will always be the poet laureate of our small town.
Maya Angelou was often called the poet laureate of the African American community.
As poet laureate, he was expected to celebrate national milestones through his poetry.
She was named poet laureate of the United Kingdom in 2009.
The poet laureate wrote a moving ode for the Queen's birthday.
