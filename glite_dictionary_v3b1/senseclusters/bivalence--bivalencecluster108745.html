bivalence

noun
valence of two
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.və.ləns/ /; / /baɪ.ˈveɪ.ləns/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.və.ləns/ /; / /baɪ.ˈveɪ.ləns/ (BrE)
the state of having a valence of two, especially in chemistry where an atom or ion can form two bonds

Examples

Bivalence of oxygen
The bivalence of oxygen allows it to form two chemical bonds in water molecules.
Copper's bivalence is important in many biological and industrial processes.
Understanding bivalence helps chemists predict how elements will react with each other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divalence - the quality of having a valence of two
  • antonym: monovalence - the quality of having a valence of one
  • degree higher: trivalence - the quality of having a valence of three
