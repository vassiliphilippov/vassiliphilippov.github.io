St. Thomas

proper noun
the Apostle who doubted Jesus's resurrection
7.0
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈtɑːməs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈtɒməs / (BrE)
(in Christianity) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, known for doubting the resurrection until he saw Jesus himself.

Examples

St. Thomas the Apostle
In Christian tradition, St. Thomas is often remembered for doubting until he saw proof.
Many paintings depict St. Thomas touching the wounds of the resurrected Christ.
The story of St. Thomas teaches the value of faith and the struggle with doubt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Doubting Thomas - a person who refuses to believe without direct evidence
