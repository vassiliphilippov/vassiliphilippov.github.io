draw a line

verb
set a limit or boundary
1.1
Pronunciation: / drɔ ə laɪn / (AmE), / drɔː ə laɪn / (BrE)
to set or establish a clear boundary or limit, especially about what is acceptable

Examples

To draw a line clearly
It's important for teachers to draw a line between being friendly and being too informal with students.
She had to draw a line somewhere, so she refused to work overtime on weekends.
When it comes to personal privacy, I draw a line that nobody is allowed to cross.
She decided to draw a line regarding how late her children could stay out.
Sometimes it's necessary to draw a line and stick to your principles.
We have to draw a line between friendship and professional relationships.
