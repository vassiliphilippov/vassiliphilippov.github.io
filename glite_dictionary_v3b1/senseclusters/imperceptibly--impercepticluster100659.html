imperceptibly

adverb
in an almost unnoticeable way
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmpərˈsɛptəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪmpəˈsɛptəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is so slight, gradual, or subtle as to be almost unnoticeable.

Examples

Shift imperceptibly slowly
Over the years, his attitude toward work shifted imperceptibly.
She moved imperceptibly, so no one noticed her slipping into the room.
The colors of the sky changed imperceptibly as the sun began to rise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imperceptible - unable to be perceived
  • antonym: noticeably - in a way that is easy to notice or perceive
