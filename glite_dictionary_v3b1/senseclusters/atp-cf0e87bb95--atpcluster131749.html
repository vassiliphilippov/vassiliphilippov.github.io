ATP

noun
energy-carrying molecule in cells
4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.tiː.piː/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.tiː.piː/ (BrE)
adenosine triphosphate; a compound present in all living cells that provides energy for many metabolic processes

Examples

ATP energy molecule
A sudden drop in ATP levels can cause fatigue and impair cellular function.
Cells constantly produce and consume ATP to fuel their biochemical reactions.
During exercise, muscles use ATP for energy to contract and relax efficiently.
