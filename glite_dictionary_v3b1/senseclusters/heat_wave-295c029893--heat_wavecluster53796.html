heat wave

noun
period of unusually hot weather
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːt weɪv /; / ˈhit ˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈhiːt weɪv /; / ˈhiːt ˌweɪv / (BrE)
a period of unusually hot weather that lasts for several days

Examples

A heat wave period
A severe heat wave swept across Europe, breaking temperature records in several countries.
During the heat wave, many people were advised to stay indoors and drink plenty of water.
The crops suffered extensive damage due to an unexpected heat wave last summer.
Last summer's heat wave broke several temperature records in the city.
Residents were urged to conserve water during the unexpected heat wave.
The heat wave caused power outages as people relied on air conditioning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cold snap - a brief period of very cold weather
