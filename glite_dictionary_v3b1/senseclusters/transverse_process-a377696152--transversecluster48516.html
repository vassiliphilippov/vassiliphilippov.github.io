transverse process

noun
bony projection on a vertebra
8.1 6.9
Pronunciation: /trænz.ˈvɜːrs ˈprɑː.ses/ /; / /træns.ˈvɜːrs ˈprɑː.ses/ (AmE), /trænz.ˈvɜːs ˈprɒ.ses/ /; / /træns.ˈvɜːs ˈprɒ.ses/ (BrE)
either of the two bony projections on each side of a vertebra that serve as points of attachment for muscles and, in the thoracic region, ribs

Examples

The transverse process bone
Muscles and ligaments attach to the transverse processes, supporting movement and stability of the spine.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the transverse process during the spinal procedure.
X-rays revealed a fracture in the transverse process of the lumbar vertebra.
