hepatojugular reflux

noun
clinical sign in heart failure, jugular vein response
9.1
Pronunciation: / hɛˌpætoʊˈdʒuɡjələr ˈriːflʌks / (AmE), / hɛˌpætəʊˈdʒuːɡjʊlə ˈriːflʌks / (BrE)
a clinical sign observed in heart failure, where applying pressure over the liver causes increased jugular vein distention due to venous reflux

Examples

Positive hepatojugular reflux
A positive hepatojugular reflux can help differentiate cardiac from respiratory causes of shortness of breath.
His jugular vein became distended when pressure was applied to the abdomen, indicating hepatojugular reflux.
The doctor detected hepatojugular reflux during the patient's examination, suggesting fluid overload.
