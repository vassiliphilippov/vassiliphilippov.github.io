sedative

noun

A drug or substance that calms or soothes a person by reducing excitement or anxiety, often used to aid sleep or relaxation.

Examples

A sedative drug
Because he was anxious about flying, his physician prescribed a mild sedative.
The doctor administered a sedative to help the patient relax before surgery.
The strong sedative quickly eased her restlessness after the accident.

Synonyms

  • sedative drug
    3.9 0.01/m
    sedative drug calms the central nervous system and helps people sleep, while sedative is a broad calming medicine for relaxation.
  • tranquilizer
    6.1 1.81/m
    tranquilizer reduces anxiety and keeps mental clarity, while sedative often causes stronger drowsiness and aims to help sleep and calm.
  • depressant
    5.5 0.27/m
    depressant lowers overall nervous system activity and affects many functions, while sedative specifically calms or helps people relax or sleep.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases alertness and energy
  • synonym: tranquilizer - a drug used to reduce tension or anxiety
