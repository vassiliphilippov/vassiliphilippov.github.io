upwelling

noun
rising ocean water
6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈʌp.wel.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʌp.wəl.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʌp.wel.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʌp.wəl.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the upward movement of deep, cold, and nutrient-rich water to the surface of the ocean or a lake

Examples

Ocean upwelling
The strong upwelling off the coast brings nutrients that support large fish populations.
Scientists study upwelling to understand changes in marine ecosystems and climate.
Seasonal upwelling events can lead to increased growth of plankton near the ocean surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downwelling - the downward movement of surface water to deeper parts of the ocean
