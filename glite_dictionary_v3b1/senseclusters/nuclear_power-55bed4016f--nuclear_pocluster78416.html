nuclear power

noun
electricity from nuclear energy
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliɚ ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
Electricity generated by means of nuclear energy, usually supplied to the power grid for civilian use.

Examples

Nuclear power plant
France generates a large portion of its electricity from nuclear power.
Many countries are investing in nuclear power to reduce carbon emissions.
The debate over nuclear power safety continues among environmental activists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fossil fuel - energy source such as coal, oil, or natural gas
  • synonym: nuclear energy - energy released during nuclear fission or fusion
