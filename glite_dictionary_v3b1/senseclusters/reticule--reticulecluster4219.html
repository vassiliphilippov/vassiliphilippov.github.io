reticule

noun
crosshairs or fine lines in an optical instrument
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtɪˌkjul / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪkjuːl / (BrE)
A network of fine lines, crosshairs, or dots in the focal plane of an eyepiece of an optical instrument, used for measurement or alignment.

Examples

Optical reticule
A reticule is essential for accurate aiming in surveying instruments.
He carefully adjusted the reticule to align the telescope with the target star.
The microscope's reticule allowed the scientist to make precise measurements of the specimen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crosshair - fine lines in the shape of a cross in the eyepiece of an optical instrument
