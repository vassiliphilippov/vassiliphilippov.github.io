affirmation

noun
a higher court upholding a lower court's judgment
2.6 5.6
Pronunciation: /æ.fər.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.fər.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /æ.fə.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.fə.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(Law) A judgment by a higher court that confirms the decision of a lower court as correct and allows it to stand.

Examples

Court affirmation of verdict
After a thorough review, the Supreme Court issued an affirmation of the original verdict.
The appellate court's affirmation of the lower court's ruling brought relief to the defendant.
The judge explained the significance of the affirmation in upholding judicial consistency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reversal - a court's judgment overturning a lower court's decision
