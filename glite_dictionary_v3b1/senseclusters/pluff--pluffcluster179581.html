pluff

verb
to fall softly
9.9
Pronunciation: / plʌf / (AmE), / plʌf / (BrE)
to fall or drop with a soft, muffled sound, especially gently or lightly

Examples

To pluff onto carpet
The old book slipped from the shelf and pluffed onto the carpet below.
A handful of feathers pluffed down from the pillow as it tore open.
Snowflakes pluff to the ground, barely making a sound in the quiet night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plop - to fall or drop with a soft, often wet sound
  • degree higher: thud - to fall or hit with a dull, heavy sound
