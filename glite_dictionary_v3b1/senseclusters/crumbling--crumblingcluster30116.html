crumbling

adjective
falling apart
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmblɪŋ / (BrE)
breaking into small pieces or gradually falling apart, often due to age or neglect

Examples

Crumbling walls
The crumbling walls of the old castle revealed its long history.
After years without repairs, the school building was visibly crumbling.
We walked carefully across the crumbling steps leading to the abandoned house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decaying - gradually breaking down or rotting
  • synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin
  • antonym: intact - not damaged or broken; whole
