physical distancing

noun
maintaining space to prevent infection spread
1.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪz.ɪ.kəl ˈdɪ.stəns.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈfɪz.kəl ˈdɪ.stəns.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈfɪz.ɪ.kəl ˈdɪ.stəns.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈfɪz.kəl ˈdɪ.stəns.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
The practice of maintaining a safe physical space between individuals, especially to prevent the spread of infectious diseases by avoiding close contact and crowded places.

Examples

Physical distancing rules
Many public places required physical distancing during the pandemic to keep everyone safe.
The company encouraged physical distancing by rearranging office desks and limiting the number of people in meeting rooms.
Through physical distancing, communities have slowed the transmission of contagious illnesses.
