sand-lime brick

noun
brick made of sand and lime
Pronunciation: / ˈsænd laɪm brɪk / (AmE), / ˈsænd laɪm brɪk / (BrE)
a type of brick made from a mixture of sand and slaked lime, typically light gray or off-white, used as a building material

Examples

A sand-lime brick wall
Compared to ordinary clay bricks, sand-lime bricks have a smoother surface and lighter color.
The architect specified sand-lime brick because of its durability and aesthetic qualities.
The new building was constructed entirely from sand-lime bricks for strength and uniform appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clay brick - brick made chiefly from clay and fired in a kiln
