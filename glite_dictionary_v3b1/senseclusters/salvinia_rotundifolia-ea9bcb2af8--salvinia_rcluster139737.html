Salvinia rotundifolia

noun
a species of floating aquatic fern
Pronunciation: / sælˈvɪniə roʊˌtʌndɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / sælˈvɪniə rəʊˌtʊndɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of free-floating aquatic fern found in still or slow-moving freshwater; known for its oval-shaped leaves forming dense mats.

Examples

Dense Salvinia rotundifolia mats
Dense mats of Salvinia rotundifolia can block sunlight and hinder the growth of other aquatic plants.
Researchers found that Salvinia rotundifolia thrives in nutrient-rich, calm waters.
The rapid spread of Salvinia rotundifolia poses challenges for managing freshwater ecosystems.
