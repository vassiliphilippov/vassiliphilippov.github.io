Middle Low German

noun
Low German from 1100–1500
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪdəl loʊ ˈdʒɜrmən /; / ˈmɪdəl loʊ dʒɜrmən / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl ləʊ ˈdʒɜːmən /; / ˈmɪdəl ləʊ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the form of the Low German language spoken and written from about 1100 to 1500, especially in northern Germany and the Hanseatic League

Examples

Middle Low German language
Linguists study the vocabulary of Middle Low German to trace the evolution of modern northern German dialects.
Many manuscripts from the 14th century are written in Middle Low German.
The Middle Low German language played a crucial role in the trade of the Hanseatic League.
Many historical documents from northern Germany are written in Middle Low German.
The Hanseatic League conducted much of its trade negotiations in Middle Low German.
The grammar of Middle Low German differs significantly from that of modern Standard German.
