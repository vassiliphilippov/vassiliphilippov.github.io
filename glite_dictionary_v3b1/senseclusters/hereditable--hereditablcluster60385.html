hereditable

adjective
inheritable by genetics
Pronunciation: / həˈrɛdɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / həˈrɛdɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of being passed from parent to offspring through genetic inheritance

Examples

Hereditable genetic traits
Some diseases are hereditable and can be traced through several generations of a family.
Eye color is a classic example of a hereditable trait in humans.
Doctors explained that the condition was hereditable, so genetic testing was recommended.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired - developed after birth, not inherited
  • synonym: heritable - able to be inherited genetically
