rigorist

noun
strict rule follower
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪɡərɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡərɪst / (BrE)
a person who strictly adheres to rules or principles, especially in matters of religion or ethics

Examples

A rigorist leader
As a rigorist, she never allowed exceptions to the organization's code of conduct.
The committee was divided between rigorists and those who favored a more flexible approach.
His rigorist attitude made it difficult for him to accept any compromise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lenient - a person who is permissive or not strict in enforcing rules
  • synonym: purist - a person who insists on absolute adherence to traditional rules or structures
