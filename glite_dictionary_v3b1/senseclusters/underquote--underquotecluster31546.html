underquote

verb
quote a lower price than another seller
8.4 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.kwoʊt/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.kəʊt/ (BrE)
to offer or quote a price lower than that of competitors or the market

Examples

To underquote a price
She was disappointed to learn her bid had been underquoted by another firm.
The supplier managed to underquote all of its competitors in the bidding process.
When two companies compete, one may underquote the other to win the contract.
Due to intense competition, the company decided to underquote its rivals for the government contract.
She realized they had underquoted the price, leading to a loss on the project.
Some suppliers will underquote to win the deal, hoping to make up profits elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overquote - to quote a higher price than another seller
  • synonym: undercut - to offer goods or services at a lower price than a competitor
