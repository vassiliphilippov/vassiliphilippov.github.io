locoweed

noun
legume plant causing poisoning in livestock
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkoʊˌwid / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəʊˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several leguminous plants native to western North America that cause poisoning (locoism) in livestock when eaten.

Examples

Cattle eat locoweed
Many cattle died after grazing on locoweed in the open pastures.
Ranchers must watch out for locoweed because of the danger it poses to their herds.
The spread of locoweed has become a serious problem for livestock owners in the western states.

Related Terms

  • synonym: astragalus - a large genus of leguminous plants, many species of which are called locoweed
  • synonym: locoism - poisoning in livestock caused by eating locoweed
