humanistically

adverb
in a humanistic way
Pronunciation: / hjuːˌmænɪˈstɪkli / (AmE), / hjuːˌmænɪˈstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to humanism, human values, or humanistic ideas, especially focusing on people, culture, or society

Examples

Humanistically focused teaching
The counselor listened humanistically, making each student feel valued and understood.
Policies were developed humanistically to ensure the well-being of all employees.
She always tries to solve problems humanistically, considering the needs of everyone involved.
The professor approached the subject humanistically, emphasizing its impact on society and culture.
She analyzed the painting humanistically, considering the artist's background and historical context.
The debate was conducted humanistically, with respect for different viewpoints and cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compassionately - in a way that shows sympathy and concern for others
  • synonym: culturally - in a way that relates to culture or cultural aspects
  • antonym: mechanistically - in a way that treats people as machines or without regard for human values
  • antonym: scientifically - in a way that relates to science or scientific methods
