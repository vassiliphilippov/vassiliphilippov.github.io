Galatea

proper noun
mythical statue brought to life by Aphrodite
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæləˈtiːə / (AmE), / ˌɡaləˈtiːə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a maiden created as a statue by Pygmalion and brought to life by Aphrodite in answer to his prayers.

Examples

Galatea statue comes alive
In some works of art, Galatea is depicted emerging gracefully from marble.
She named her sculpture Galatea, inspired by the legendary figure who came to life.
The story of Galatea and Pygmalion is a classic myth about love and transformation.
