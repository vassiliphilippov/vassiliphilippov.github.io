incommunicability

noun
not transmissible
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kə.mjuː.nɪ.kə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.kə.mjuː.nɪ.kə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality or state of not being able to be transmitted or communicated from one person to another, especially of a disease

Examples

Incommunicability of disease
The doctor assured the public of the incommunicability of the rare illness.
Because of its incommunicability, the disease did not require quarantine measures.
The report highlighted the incommunicability of the condition to ease community concerns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: communicability - the ability to be transmitted from one person to another
  • synonym: noncontagiousness - the state of not being able to spread from person to person
