graduate

verb
mark with measurement intervals
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡrædjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to divide or mark (an instrument or container) with lines indicating intervals of measurement; to calibrate for accurate reading

Examples

To graduate a cylinder
Carefully graduate the measuring cylinder to ensure accurate readings.
Manufacturers must graduate each beaker so users can pour precise amounts of liquid.
The technician spent the morning graduating the new set of pipettes.
