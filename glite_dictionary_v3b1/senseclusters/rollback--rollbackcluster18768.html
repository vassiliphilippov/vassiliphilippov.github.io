rollback

noun
forcing an enemy to withdraw
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌbæk / (BrE)
the act of driving back an enemy or causing them to withdraw, especially in military or strategic contexts

Examples

Military rollback
Officials described the operation as a successful rollback of the insurgents' advances.
The general planned a strategic rollback to reclaim lost territory.
The military launched a massive rollback of enemy forces along the border.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing forces from enemy contact
