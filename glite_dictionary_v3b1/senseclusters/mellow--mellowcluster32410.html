mellow

verb
become or make softer or more pleasant
5.1 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmel.oʊ/ /; / /ˈmel.əʊ/ (AmE), /ˈmel.əʊ/ /; / /ˈmel.oʊ/ (BrE)
to become or make something softer, gentler, or more pleasant in taste, character, or mood

Examples

To mellow with age
If you let the cheese mellow, its flavor becomes much richer.
The apples need to mellow for another week before they are sweet enough to eat.
Time had mellowed his once sharp temper.
Parenthood tends to mellow even the most intense personalities.
She has mellowed with age and is no longer as strict as she once was.
Time and distance have mellowed his anger towards his old friends.
As she got older, Maria began to mellow and became easier to talk to.
Over time, the angry leader mellowed and listened to others' opinions more.
Some people mellow with experience and learn to let small issues pass.
