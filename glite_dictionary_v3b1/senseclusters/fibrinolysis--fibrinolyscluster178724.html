fibrinolysis

noun
dissolution of blood clots
7.4
Pronunciation: / faɪbrəˈnaɪlɪsɪs / (AmE), / fɪbrɪnəʊˈlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process in which fibrin in blood clots is broken down and removed from the bloodstream

Examples

Fibrinolysis process
A defect in fibrinolysis can lead to an increased risk of stroke or heart attack.
Certain medications can enhance fibrinolysis to treat patients with thrombosis.
The doctor explained that fibrinolysis helps prevent dangerous blood clots from forming in the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thrombosis - the formation of blood clots in blood vessels
