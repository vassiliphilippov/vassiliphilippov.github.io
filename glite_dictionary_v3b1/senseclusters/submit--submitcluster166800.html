submit

verb
accept or undergo unwillingly
Pronunciation: / səbˈmɪt / (AmE), / səbˈmɪt / (BrE)
to accept or yield to authority, control, or an outcome, often unwillingly

Examples

To submit to authority
After months of treatment, she submitted to surgery as a last resort.
He finally had to submit to the board's decision, even though he disagreed.
They were forced to submit to the new regulations despite their objections.
After hours of debate, she finally chose to submit to her colleagues' decision.
Sometimes, it is better to submit rather than escalate an argument unnecessarily.
The rebels refused to submit to the king's demands, prolonging the conflict.
After months of struggle, he finally submitted to the doctor's advice and took a break.
She knew she couldn't change the situation, so she submitted to her fate gracefully.
They submitted without protest, realizing there was nothing more they could do.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of something
  • synonym: succumb - to fail to resist pressure, temptation, or some other negative force
  • synonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to authority
  • synonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
