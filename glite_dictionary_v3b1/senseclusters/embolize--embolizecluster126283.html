embolize

verb
to block a blood vessel
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛmbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to block a blood vessel by introducing or forming an embolus, often as a medical procedure

Examples

Doctors embolize an artery
Surgeons decided to embolize the artery to prevent further bleeding during the operation.
Doctors may embolize a blood vessel to stop internal bleeding.
The radiologist used a special technique to embolize the tumor's blood supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occlude - to close or block up a passage or vessel
  • antonym: unblock - to remove an obstruction from something
