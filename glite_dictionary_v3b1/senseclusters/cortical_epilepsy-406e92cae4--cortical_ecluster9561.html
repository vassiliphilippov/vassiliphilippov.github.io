cortical epilepsy

noun
epilepsy with seizures starting in the cerebral cortex
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtɪkəl ɛˈpɪlɛpˌsi / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪkəl ɛˈpɪlɛpsi / (BrE)
A type of epilepsy in which seizures begin with localized disturbances of cerebral function, such as twitching of a limb, unusual sensations, or mental disturbances.

Examples

Cortical epilepsy seizures
Advances in imaging techniques have improved the detection and treatment of cortical epilepsy.
Early symptoms of cortical epilepsy can include sensory illusions or brief mental disturbances before a seizure progresses.
The neurologist diagnosed the patient with cortical epilepsy after observing the isolated twitching in his right arm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epilepsy - a neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent episodes of sensory disturbance or convulsions
