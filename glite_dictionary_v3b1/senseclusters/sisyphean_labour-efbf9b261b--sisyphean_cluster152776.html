Sisyphean labour

noun
endless and futile task
8.6
Pronunciation: / sɪˈsɪfiən ˈleɪbər / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪfiən ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
An endless and futile task that can never be completed, likened to the labor of Sisyphus in Greek mythology.

Examples

A Sisyphean labour task
After hours of trying to fix an unsolvable software bug, he described it as a Sisyphean labour.
Maintaining perfect order in the busy household became her Sisyphean labour.
The volunteers soon found cleaning the entire coastline to be a Sisyphean labour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: futile effort - work that achieves nothing
  • antonym: meaningful work - labor that has purpose and results
