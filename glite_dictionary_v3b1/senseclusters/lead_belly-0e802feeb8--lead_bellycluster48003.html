Lead Belly

proper noun
American folk singer and composer (1885–1949)
Pronunciation: / lɛd ˈbɛli / (AmE), / lɛd ˈbɛli / (BrE)
(1885–1949), American folk singer and composer known for his strong vocals, twelve-string guitar playing, and influential repertoire of folk and blues songs.

Examples

Lead Belly singer
Bob Dylan cited Lead Belly as a major influence on his early songwriting.
Many consider Lead Belly one of the most influential figures in American folk music history.
Several of Lead Belly's songs, such as "Goodnight, Irene," have become standards in folk and blues music.
