unscrupulous

adjective
lacking moral principles; dishonest
7.5 1.56/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàskruÀêpj…ôl…ôs / (AmE), /  ånÀàskruÀêpj äl…ôs / (BrE)
Having or showing no moral principles; not honest or fair.

Examples

Unscrupulous people
He warned her about unscrupulous people who try to take advantage of tourists.
Only an unscrupulous landlord would raise the rent without making necessary repairs.
The company was accused of unscrupulous business practices by its competitors.
