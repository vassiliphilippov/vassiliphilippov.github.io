battling a hydra

verb
facing many recurring challenges
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt.əlɪŋ ə ˈhaɪ.drə / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əlɪŋ ə ˈhaɪ.drə / (BrE)
Facing a situation in which solving one problem causes others to arise, so that one is confronted with numerous, simultaneous challenges.

Examples

To battle a hydra problem
Dealing with the unexpected costs was like battling a hydra—every solution created more issues.
For entrepreneurs, starting a business often means battling a hydra of regulations and market changes.
She described parenting three toddlers as battling a hydra, with new challenges popping up every day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cutting off one head and two grow back - solving a problem only for more to arise; also refers to the myth of the Hydra
  • synonym: fighting an uphill battle - struggling with a difficult or seemingly impossible task
