short

adverb
in finance, selling something not owned
8.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: /  É…îrt / (AmE), /  É…îÀêt / (BrE)
(Finance) In a way that involves selling an asset that one does not possess at the time, typically expecting to buy it back later at a lower price.

Examples

Trade short in markets
During the crisis, many traders were positioned short and profited as prices fell.
He made millions by betting short on tech stocks before the market crashed.
Investors who went short too early risked significant losses if the value went up instead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: long - (Finance) Buying an asset to profit from a price increase; the opposite of short
