Balthazar

proper noun
one of the three Magi in the Nativity story
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbæl.θə.zər/ /; / /ˈbæl.θeɪ.zər/ /; / /ˈbæl.θə.zɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈbæl.θeɪ.zə/ /; / /ˈbæl.θə.zə/ (BrE)
(in the Bible) one of the three Magi or Wise Men who visited the infant Jesus, traditionally said to be an Arabian scholar who brought the gift of myrrh.

Examples

Balthazar the wise man
At the nativity play, Tom was chosen to portray Balthazar, the wise man bringing myrrh.
Legends often describe Balthazar as an Arabian scholar who traveled far to see the newborn Christ.
Many paintings depict Balthazar wearing richly colored robes as he presents his gift to Jesus.
