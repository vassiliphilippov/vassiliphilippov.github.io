devisor

noun
person who leaves real property in a will
7.8 8.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈvaɪ.zər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈvaɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a person who bequeaths real property to someone else in a will

Examples

A devisor in a will
A devisor can only leave real estate to a beneficiary, not personal property.
Disputes often arise when the intentions of the devisor are ambiguous in the will.
The devisor clearly stated in her will that the property should go to her nephew.

Related Terms

  • antonym: devisee - a person to whom property is given in a will
  • synonym: testator - a person who makes a will
