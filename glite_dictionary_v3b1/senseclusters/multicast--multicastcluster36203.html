multicast

noun
network data transmission
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌl.tɪ.kæst/ /; / /ˈmʌl.ti.kæst/ (AmE), /ˈmʌl.tɪ.kɑːst/ /; / /ˈmʌl.ti.kɑːst/ (BrE)
a method of transmitting data from one sender to multiple receivers simultaneously over a network

Examples

A multicast stream
The company used multicast to stream the event to all employees at once.
With multicast, the video conference reached every office without overloading the network.
Engineers set up a multicast to distribute software updates to multiple devices efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broadcast - sending data to all devices on a network
  • antonym: unicast - sending data from one sender to one receiver
