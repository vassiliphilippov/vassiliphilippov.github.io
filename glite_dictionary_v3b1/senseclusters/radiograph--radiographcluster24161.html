radiograph

noun
image made using X-rays or gamma rays
1.7 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.di.ə.græf/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.dɪ.ə.ɡrɑːf/ (BrE)
an image produced on a sensitive surface by exposure to radiation other than visible light, especially X-rays or gamma rays, commonly used in medical imaging

Examples

A radiograph image
A chest radiograph can reveal lung infections or heart problems.
She carefully labeled each radiograph before filing them in the patient's folder.
The dentist examined the radiograph to look for any cavities or hidden damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: X-ray - an image produced by X-rays, often a synonym for radiograph in medicine
  • degree lower: scan - a medical image produced by non-invasive methods
