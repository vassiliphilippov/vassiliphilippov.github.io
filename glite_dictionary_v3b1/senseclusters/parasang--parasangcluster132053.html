parasang

noun
ancient Persian distance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌsæŋ / (AmE), / ˈpærəsæŋ / (BrE)
an ancient Persian unit of distance, approximately 3.5 to 4 miles (about 5.5 to 6.5 kilometers)

Examples

A parasang distance
The travelers covered ten parasangs before reaching the next village.
In ancient texts, distances are often measured in parasangs rather than miles or kilometers.
The messenger rode for three parasangs to deliver the urgent news.
The ancient army marched a full parasang before making camp for the night.
Historians estimate the city was about two parasangs from the river.
Travelers in Persia often measured their journeys in parasangs rather than miles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kilometer - a unit of distance equal to 1,000 meters
  • synonym: league - an old unit of distance, usually about three miles
  • synonym: mile - a unit of distance equal to 1,609 meters
