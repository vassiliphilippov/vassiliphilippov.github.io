leger line

noun
short line in music notation for notes outside the staff
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒər laɪn / (AmE), / ˈliːdʒə laɪn / (BrE)
in music notation, a short line written above or below the staff to indicate notes that extend beyond its range

Examples

A leger line in music
If you see many leger lines, playing the passage will require special attention.
The composer used a leger line to show the note above the staff.
The high C in this piece is written above a leger line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: staff - the five lines and four spaces used in music notation
