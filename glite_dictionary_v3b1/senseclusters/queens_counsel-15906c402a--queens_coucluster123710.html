Queen's Counsel

noun
senior barrister appointed during a queen's reign

(in the UK and some Commonwealth countries) a senior barrister appointed by the Crown to be one of 'Her Majesty's Counsel learned in the law' when the reigning monarch is a queen.

Queen's Counsel appointment
After decades of dedicated service, she was appointed Queen's Counsel by the Crown.
Many aspiring lawyers dream of one day becoming a Queen's Counsel or King's Counsel.
The title of Queen's Counsel is used only when the monarch is a queen, otherwise it becomes King's Counsel.
