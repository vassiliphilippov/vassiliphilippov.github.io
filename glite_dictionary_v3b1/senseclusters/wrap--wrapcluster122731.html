wrap

verb
crash so as to coil around something
Pronunciation: / ræp / (AmE), / ræp / (BrE)
to crash into something so forcefully that an object, especially a vehicle, becomes bent or coiled around it

Examples

To wrap a car around a pole
If you don't slow down, you might wrap the car around a telephone pole.
The icy roads caused her to lose control and wrap her vehicle around a street sign.
The reckless driver wrapped his sports car around a tree last night.
