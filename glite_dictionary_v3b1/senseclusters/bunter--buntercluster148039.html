bunter

noun
close cricket fielder
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌntə / (BrE)
a cricket fielder positioned close to the batsman, ready to catch short balls near the wicket

Examples

A close bunter
The bunter moved quickly to catch the ball that popped up off the bat.
During the match, the captain placed a bunter close to the batsman for extra pressure.
A skilled bunter can make a big difference in tight cricket games.

Related Terms

  • synonym: short leg - a fielding position close to the batsman on the leg side in cricket
  • synonym: slip - a fielding position next to the wicketkeeper in cricket
