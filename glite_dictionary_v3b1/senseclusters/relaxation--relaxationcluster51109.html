relaxation

noun
making rules less strict
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌlækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌlækˈseɪʃən /; / ˌriːˌlækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making a rule, restriction, or control less strict or severe, or the weakening of strength or tension

Examples

Relaxation of rules
After the relaxation of government regulations, more small businesses were able to open.
The relaxation of the company’s dress code was welcomed by many employees.
The new policy led to a relaxation of strict security procedures.
A sudden relaxation of his grip caused the rope to slip.
The new law led to the relaxation of previous safety regulations.
There has been a relaxation in immigration controls in recent years.

  • synonym: loosening - the act of making something less tight or strict
  • antonym: tightening - the act of making rules or restrictions more strict
