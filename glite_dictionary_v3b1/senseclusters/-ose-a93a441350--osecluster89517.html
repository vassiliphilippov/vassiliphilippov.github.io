-ose

sugar suffix
7.9
Pronunciation: / oʊs / (AmE), / əʊz / (BrE)
a suffix used in the names of sugars, especially simple carbohydrates like glucose or fructose

Examples

Glucose sugar
The sugar in fruit is called glucose, which ends with the suffix -ose.
Many carbohydrates, such as lactose and sucrose, have names ending in -ose.
When you see the suffix -ose, it usually means the word refers to a type of sugar.
