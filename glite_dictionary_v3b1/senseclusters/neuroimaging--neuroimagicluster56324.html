neuroimaging

noun
brain imaging techniques
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of technology to create images of the brain or nervous system, especially for research, analysis, or medical diagnosis

Examples

Neuroimaging of the brain
Advances in neuroimaging have helped scientists better understand how the brain functions.
Doctors often use neuroimaging to detect tumors or other abnormalities in the brain.
The research team analyzed neuroimaging data to study the effects of the new medication.
Advances in neuroimaging have helped scientists understand how different parts of the brain function.
Doctors often use neuroimaging to detect tumors or injuries in the brain.
Modern neuroimaging techniques can reveal changes in brain activity during various tasks.
