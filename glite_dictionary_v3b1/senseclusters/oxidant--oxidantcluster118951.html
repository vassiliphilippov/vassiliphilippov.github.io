oxidant

noun
substance that causes oxidation
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdənt / (BrE)
A substance that causes oxidation by accepting electrons from another substance.

Examples

Strong oxidants
Exposure to strong oxidants can damage living tissues.
In this reaction, hydrogen peroxide acts as the oxidant and oxidizes the iron.
Ozone is a powerful oxidant used in water purification systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxidizer - another term for oxidant; a substance that causes oxidation
  • antonym: reductant - a substance that reduces another substance; the opposite of oxidant
