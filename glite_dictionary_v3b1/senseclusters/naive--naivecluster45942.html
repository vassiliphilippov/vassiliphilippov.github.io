naive

adjective
lacking experience or judgment
8.4 4.1
Pronunciation: / naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)
lacking experience, wisdom, or sophistication; innocent, simple, or too trusting

Examples

A naive person
As a naive newcomer, she made many honest mistakes at her first job.
His naive optimism led him to believe success would be easy.
Many people are naive about the risks involved in starting a new business.
As a naive newcomer, he was easily misled by others.
It's naive to believe every advertisement you see on the internet.
She was naive about the complexities of the legal system.
Being naive can sometimes lead to disappointment in business.
His naive approach to the world gradually changed as he gained experience.
She was so naive that she believed everything she was told.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something; credulous
  • synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • antonym: informed - having knowledge or information
  • synonym: innocent - lacking experience or sophistication
  • antonym: sophisticated - having much worldly experience and knowledge
