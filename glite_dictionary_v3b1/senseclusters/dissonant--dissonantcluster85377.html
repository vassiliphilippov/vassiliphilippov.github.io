dissonant

adjective
lacking harmony; disagreeing
5.8 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪs.ə.nənt/ (AmE), /ˈdɪs.ə.nənt/ (BrE)
not in agreement or harmony; conflicting or clashing (used for things, opinions, colors, etc.)

Examples

Dissonant opinions
Bright colors can appear dissonant if they do not complement each other well.
Her dissonant views often clashed with the consensus of the group.
The dissonant chords in the music created a sense of tension and unrest.
Her views were dissonant with those of the rest of the team.
The committee was plagued by dissonant opinions that made consensus difficult.
Their dissonant statements confused the public.
