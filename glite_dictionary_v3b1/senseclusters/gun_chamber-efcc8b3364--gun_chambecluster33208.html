gun chamber

noun
part of a gun holding the charge
1.3
Pronunciation: / ɡʌn ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ɡʌn ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
the part of a gun where the ammunition or charge is loaded before firing

Examples

The gun chamber is empty
Always make sure the gun chamber is empty before cleaning your firearm.
The spent cartridge remained stuck in the gun chamber after firing.
When loading a revolver, each gun chamber holds one round of ammunition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breech - rear part of a firearm's barrel where the cartridge is loaded
