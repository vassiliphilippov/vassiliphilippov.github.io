backseat

noun
secondary or inferior position
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌsit / (AmE), / ˈbækˌsiːt / (BrE)
a secondary or less important position or status within an organization or activity

Examples

Take a backseat
After her promotion, his ambitions had to take a backseat for a while.
During the crisis, normal procedures were given a backseat in favor of urgent action.
In the project, marketing played a backseat role to product development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: front seat - a leading or important position
