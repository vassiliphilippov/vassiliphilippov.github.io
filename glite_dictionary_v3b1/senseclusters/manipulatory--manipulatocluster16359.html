manipulatory

adjective
controlling or influencing
8.0
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪpjəˌleɪtɔri / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊlətəri / (BrE)
intending or designed to influence or control people or situations, often in a clever or unfair way

Examples

Manipulatory behavior
The manager's manipulatory approach made employees feel uneasy about sharing their opinions.
She used manipulatory methods to get what she wanted from the group.
Critics accused the politician of relying on manipulatory strategies to win support.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: manipulative - good at influencing or controlling others, often unfairly
