overscrupulous

adjective
excessively precise
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈskruːpjʊləs / (BrE)
extremely or unnecessarily careful to follow rules or avoid mistakes

Examples

An overscrupulous person
Her overscrupulous approach to organizing the files slowed down the entire project.
Some teachers can be overscrupulous about grammar, correcting every tiny mistake.
He was so overscrupulous in following the instructions that he missed the bigger picture.
Her overscrupulous approach to editing meant every document took twice as long to finish.
Some people find him overscrupulous because he insists on following every rule exactly.
Being overscrupulous can sometimes prevent you from making quick decisions when needed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: careless - not giving enough attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • synonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail
  • degree lower: scrupulous - very careful to do what is right or proper
