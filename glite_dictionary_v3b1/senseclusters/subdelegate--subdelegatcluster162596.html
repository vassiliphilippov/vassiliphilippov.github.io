subdelegate

verb
to pass on delegated duties
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈdɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈdɛlɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to transfer or assign authority, duties, or powers that have been delegated to you to another person, especially a subordinate or another delegate

Examples

To subdelegate tasks
The manager decided to subdelegate the project tasks to her assistant to save time.
If you are overwhelmed, you can subdelegate some of your responsibilities to trusted team members.
He realized he needed to subdelegate certain duties to ensure the work was completed on schedule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delegate - to give responsibility or authority to another person
  • antonym: retain - to keep responsibility or authority instead of passing it on
