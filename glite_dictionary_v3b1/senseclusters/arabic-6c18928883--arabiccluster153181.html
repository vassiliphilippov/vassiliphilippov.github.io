Arabic

noun
Semitic language spoken in Arab countries
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈærəbɪk / (AmE), / ˈærəbɪk / (BrE)
the Semitic language spoken by Arabs, used in many countries and as the language of the Qur'an

Examples

Speak Arabic
Many people study Arabic to read the Qur'an in its original language.
She speaks fluent Arabic after living in Cairo for five years.
There are different dialects of Arabic spoken across North Africa and the Middle East.
Many people around the world study Arabic to better understand Middle Eastern culture and history.
She speaks fluent Arabic as well as English and French.
The holy book of Islam, the Quran, is written in Arabic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arabic script - the script used for writing Arabic and several other languages
  • synonym: Aramaic - another Semitic language related to Arabic
  • antonym: English - the Germanic language spoken in England and widely worldwide
  • synonym: Hebrew - another major Semitic language
