darkling ground beetle

noun
a black terrestrial beetle; larvae feed on decaying matter
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑrklɪŋ ˈɡraʊnd ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈdɑːklɪŋ ˈɡraʊnd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a sluggish, hard-bodied black terrestrial beetle whose larvae feed on decaying plant material or grain

Examples

A darkling ground beetle
A darkling ground beetle moves slowly in the shadows, blending in with the rich soil.
Farmers sometimes find darkling ground beetles around stored grain, where their larvae feed.
The scientist discovered a darkling ground beetle under a rotting log in the forest.
