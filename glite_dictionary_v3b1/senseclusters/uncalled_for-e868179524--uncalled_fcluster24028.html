uncalled for

adjective
inappropriate or unnecessary
5.1 0.72/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàk…îld f…îr / (AmE), /  ånÀàk…îÀêld f…îÀê / (BrE)
not appropriate or necessary; unwarranted or unjustified

Examples

Uncalled for rude comment
Her rude comment was uncalled for and took everyone by surprise.
His aggressive criticism was completely uncalled for during the meeting.
The manager apologized for the uncalled for remarks made by his staff.

Synonyms

  • unwarranted
    7.0 2.62/m
    unwarranted describes actions done without any good reason or permission, while uncalled for criticizes something unnecessary or inappropriate.
  • unjustified
    4.8 1.61/m
    unjustified refers to something lacking a good reason or excuse, while uncalled for highlights it as unnecessary or improper.
  • gratuitous
    7.7 2.09/m
    gratuitous means unnecessary and often excessive, while uncalled for is a plain complaint about something unjustified or inappropriate.
  • inappropriate
    3.7 21/m
    inappropriate describes something not suitable for the situation or people, while uncalled for stresses it is unnecessary or unwarranted.
  • unbefitting
    7.2 0.00/m
    unbefitting sounds formal or old-fashioned for something not proper, while uncalled for is common and criticizes unnecessary behavior.
