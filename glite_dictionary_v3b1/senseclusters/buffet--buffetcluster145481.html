buffet

verb
strike repeatedly and forcefully
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌf.ɪt /; / ˈbʌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌf.ɪt /; / ˈbʌfɪt / (BrE)
to strike or hit something repeatedly and forcefully, especially by wind or waves

Examples

Waves buffet the shore
Strong winds buffet the coastline every winter, eroding the cliffs.
The airplane was buffeted by turbulence during the flight.
The small boat was buffeted by the storm all night.
For hours, the hikers were buffeted by fierce winds on the exposed ridge.
The city was buffeted by a series of violent storms last winter.
The small boat was buffeted by the powerful waves throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: batter - to strike repeatedly with hard blows
