bayman

noun
bay fisherman
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪmən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪmən / (BrE)
a person who lives or works near a bay, especially someone who fishes or gathers shellfish there

Examples

The bayman fishing
The bayman set out early to check his crab traps before sunrise.
Generations of baymen have worked these waters, relying on the bay for their livelihood.
As a skilled bayman, he knew every channel and sandbar in the area.
The bayman rose before dawn to prepare his boat for a day on the water.
Local baymen have passed down their fishing traditions for generations.
After a long day, the bayman returned home with baskets full of clams.
