elision

noun
omission of a sound in speech
9.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlɪ.ʒən/ /; / /ɪ.ˈlɪ.zjən/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlɪ.ʒən/ /; / /ɪ.ˈlɪ.zjən/ (BrE)
the omission of a sound or syllable when speaking, especially the omission of a vowel at the end of one word when the next word begins with a vowel

Examples

Elision in speech
French uses elision frequently when a word ending in a vowel is followed by another starting with a vowel.
In casual speech, elision often leads to sounds being dropped from words.
The phrase 'I'm' is an example of elision because 'am' loses its initial vowel.
