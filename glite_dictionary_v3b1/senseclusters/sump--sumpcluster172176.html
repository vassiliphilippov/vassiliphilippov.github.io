sump

noun
pit for liquid collection
8.2 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / sÊŒmp / (AmE), / sÊŒmp / (BrE)
a pit or reservoir, usually underground, where liquid such as water or sewage collects, especially as part of a drainage or sewage system

Examples

Basement sump
Heavy rains caused water to accumulate in the basement sump, which had to be pumped out.
Oil collected at the bottom of the sump in the engine needs to be drained during servicing.
The maintenance team checked the sump to ensure it was not clogged with debris.
The basement sump needs to be checked regularly to prevent flooding.
The plumber inspected the sump for blockages caused by debris.
When it rains heavily, water collects in the sump before being pumped out.

Synonyms
