triarius

noun
veteran Roman soldier
Pronunciation: / traɪˈɛəriəs / (AmE), / trɪˈɑːrɪəs / (BrE)
a member of the third and most experienced line of soldiers in the Roman army

Examples

Roman triarius soldier
When the front lines broke, the triarius stepped forward to defend the Roman position.
A triarius was respected for his experience and bravery in battle.
The commander relied on the triarius to turn the tide during desperate moments.
