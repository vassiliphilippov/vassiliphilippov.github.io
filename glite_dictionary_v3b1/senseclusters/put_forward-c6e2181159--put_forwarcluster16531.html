put forward

verb
suggest, propose, or present (an idea, etc.)
3.2 4.21/m
Pronunciation: / p ät Ààf…îrw…ôrd / (AmE), / p ät Ààf…îÀêw…ôd / (BrE)
to suggest, propose, or present an idea, plan, or argument for consideration or discussion

Examples

To put forward an idea
He is always willing to put forward fresh ideas to solve the problem.
Several new theories were put forward during the seminar.
She decided to put forward her own proposal at the meeting.
I appreciate everyone who put forward their ideas in today's discussion.
She decided to put forward her proposal at the next team meeting.
The committee has put forward several solutions to the problem.

Synonyms

  • propose
    3.0 84/m
    propose is to suggest a plan or idea clearly and often formally, while put forward means to present an idea for discussion.
  • suggest
    1.6 151/m
    suggest offers an idea gently or as an option, while put forward covers offering ideas for wider discussion or decision.
  • advance
    3.9 3.52/m
    advance puts an idea or reason forward, often in argument or theory, while put forward is a more general suggestion.
  • float
    5.3 6.49/m
    float is to suggest an idea tentatively to see reaction, while put forward may simply present an idea for discussion.
  • bring forward
    3.9 0.19/m
    bring forward introduces an item or idea, often in a formal meeting, while put forward is used more generally.
  • put up an argument
    3.7 0.00/m
    put up an argument presents reasons or a defence in debate, while put forward can mean suggesting any idea for general consideration.
  • submit
    3.7 27/m
    submit formally hands in a document or request for approval, while put forward means to present an idea for discussion, not always formal.
  • set forth
    4.2 5.17/m
    set forth states an idea clearly and often in writing, while put forward can mean casually offering an idea for others to consider.
  • propound
    7.9 0.71/m
    propound puts forward a theory or idea for careful study, while put forward is more general and common in everyday speech.
