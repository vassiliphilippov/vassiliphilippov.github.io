plant

noun
living organism lacking locomotion
4.6 1.0
Pronunciation: /plænt/ (AmE), /plɑːnt/ (BrE)
A living organism that grows in the earth or in water, typically with green leaves, and lacks the ability to move actively, such as a tree, shrub, grass, or fern.

Examples

A green plant
A cactus is a type of plant adapted to dry conditions.
Photosynthesis allows a plant to produce its own food from sunlight.
The plant in the corner needs to be watered every week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: animal - a living organism that can move and respond to its environment
  • degree lower: fungus - a group of living organisms distinct from plants and animals, including molds and mushrooms
