common European jay

noun
a species of jay bird found in Europe
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən jəˈɹəʊpiən dʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən jʊəˈrəʊpiən dʒeɪ / (BrE)
A Eurasian jay (Garrulus glandarius), a fawn-colored bird with a black-and-white crest and blue-and-black wings, found in Europe and parts of Asia.

Examples

The common European jay bird
A common European jay collected acorns and hid them among the roots.

We spotted a common European jay perched in the oak tree, displaying its colorful feathers.
