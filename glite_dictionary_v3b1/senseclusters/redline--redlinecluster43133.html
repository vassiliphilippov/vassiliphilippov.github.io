redline

verb
mark a document to show edits
5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈre.d.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈre.d.laɪn/ (BrE)
to mark a document to show changes, corrections, or revisions, usually using red ink or tracked changes

Examples

To redline a document
Before sending the contract, the editor will redline the document to indicate revisions.
She redlined the agreement, highlighting clauses that needed clearer deadlines.
The team spent the afternoon redlining the proposal before the client meeting.
The legal team spent hours redlining the contract before sending it back for review.
Please redline any sections of the report that need further discussion.
After redlining the document, she shared the revised version with her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annotate - to add notes or comments to a text to explain or suggest changes
  • antonym: approve - to accept or agree to something as it is
  • synonym: edit - to make changes or corrections to a text
  • synonym: mark up - to add editorial comments or show changes to a document
