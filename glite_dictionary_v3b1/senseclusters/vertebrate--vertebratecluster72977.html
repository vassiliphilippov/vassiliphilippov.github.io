vertebrate

adjective
having a backbone or spinal column
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrtəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪbrət / (BrE)
(of an animal) having a backbone or spinal column.

Examples

Vertebrate skeleton
A bird's skeleton is typical of a vertebrate structure, with a well-defined spine.
Frogs are classified as vertebrate species because they possess a backbone.
Unlike insects, all mammals are vertebrate animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: invertebrate - lacking a backbone or spinal column
