underbidder

noun
lower-price bidder
6.7
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈbɪd.ər/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈbɪd.ə/ (BrE)
a person or company that offers a lower price than others but does not win, especially in a competition for a contract, job, or at an auction

Examples

The underbidder company
The company became the successful underbidder and won the contract for the new bridge.
As the only underbidder, she secured the job at a much lower price than expected.
Several firms competed, but the lowest underbidder was awarded the project.
The underbidder was disappointed when his offer was not accepted at the auction.
At the charity event, the underbidder congratulated the winner on securing the painting.
She was the underbidder for the antique vase, missing out by just a small amount.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bidder - a person or company making an offer in an auction or tender
  • synonym: contractor - a person or company hired to do work or provide services
  • antonym: overbidder - a person or company that offers a higher price than others when bidding
  • antonym: winner - the person whose bid is accepted at an auction
