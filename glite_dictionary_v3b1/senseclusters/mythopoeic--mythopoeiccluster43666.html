mythopoeic

adjective
like a myth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪθəˈpiːɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪθəˈpiːɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities of myth or relating to the creation of myths or mythologies

Examples

Mythopoeic stories
The author created a mythopoeic world filled with ancient gods and epic heroes.
Her paintings have a mythopoeic quality, evoking stories from forgotten times.
The festival's rituals are deeply mythopoeic, drawing on centuries-old legends.
The author’s mythopoeic imagination brought ancient legends to life in her stories.
Many cultures have a mythopoeic tradition that explains the origins of the world.
His paintings have a mythopoeic quality, blending fantasy with historical themes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: factual - based on facts rather than myths or imagination
  • synonym: legendary - remarkable enough to be famous or very well known, like a legend
  • synonym: mythical - relating to or resembling a myth
  • synonym: mythological - relating to myths or mythology
  • antonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is accurate and true to life
