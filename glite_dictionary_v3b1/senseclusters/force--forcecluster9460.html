force

noun
legal validity or effect of a law
6.0 8.21/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs / (AmE), / f…îÀês / (BrE)
(of a law or rule) the state of being valid or operative legally; the capacity to have legal effect.

Examples

In force legally
Several outdated rules remain technically in force despite being ignored.
That law is no longer in force after the changes.
The new regulations will come into force next month.

Synonyms

  • in force
    3.9 1.71/m
    in force is used when a law is currently operative and valid, while force names the law's legal capacity to have effect.
  • in effect
    3.7 4.49/m
    in effect describes a rule that is currently valid or being applied, while force refers to the rule's legal power to work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: effect - the state of being in operation or having legal validity
