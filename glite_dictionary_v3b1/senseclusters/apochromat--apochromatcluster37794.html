apochromat

adjective
optically corrected lens
Pronunciation: / ˌæpoʊˈkroʊmæt / (AmE), / ˌapəˈkrəʊmat / (BrE)
having optical correction so that three wavelengths of light are brought into focus at the same point, reducing chromatic aberration.

Examples

Apochromat lens design
The scientist used an apochromat lens to achieve sharper images in his microscope.
Photographers often prefer apochromat objectives to minimize color fringing in their pictures.
An apochromat design is essential for high-precision optical instruments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: achromat - a lens corrected to bring two wavelengths of light into focus together
  • antonym: chromatic - relating to color, often causing color distortion in optics
