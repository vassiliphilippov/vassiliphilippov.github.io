one iron

noun
golf club with least loft
Pronunciation: / wʌn ˈaɪ.ɚn / (AmE), / wʌn ˈaɪ.ən / (BrE)
in golf, a long iron club with the least loft, used for long, low shots

Examples

A one iron club
He hit a low, piercing shot with his one iron that traveled over 200 yards.
Many players now prefer hybrids instead of the traditional one iron.
The golfer pulled out his one iron for the challenging shot across the fairway.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: driver - golf club with a very low loft, used for long-distance shots from the tee
  • degree higher: two iron - golf club with slightly more loft than a one iron
