delegitimization

noun
making seem illegitimate
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːlɪˌdʒɪtɪməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːlɪˌdʒɪtɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something or someone lose legitimacy, authority, or public support

Examples

The media played a role in the delegitimization of the new government.
International criticism led to the delegitimization of the election results.
Activists warned against the delegitimization of peaceful protest movements.
The international community condemned the delegitimization of the newly elected government.
Activists argued that the law led to the delegitimization of several minority organizations.
The process of delegitimization can undermine public trust in official institutions.
The delegitimization of the government occurred after several corruption scandals were revealed.
Social media campaigns contributed to the delegitimization of the company's public image.
Rapid delegitimization of the old system led to widespread calls for reform.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discrediting - the act of causing something to lose credibility or respect
  • antonym: legitimation - the act of making something legitimate or accepted
  • antonym: legitimization - the act of making something legal or officially recognized
