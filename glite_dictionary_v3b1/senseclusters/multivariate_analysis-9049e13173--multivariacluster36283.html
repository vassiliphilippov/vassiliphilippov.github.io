multivariate analysis

noun
statistical method for two or more variables
7.6 5.0
Pronunciation: /mʌl.tɪ.ˈver.ɪ.ət ə.ˈnæl.ə.sɪs/ /; / /mʌl.tə.ˈver.ɪ.ət ə.ˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /mʌl.tɪ.ˈveə.rɪ.ət ə.ˈnæl.ə.sɪs/ /; / /mʌl.tɪ.ˈveə.rɪ.ət ə.ˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
any statistical method used to examine data involving two or more variables, especially to understand relationships among them

Examples

A multivariate analysis method
A multivariate analysis can help control for confounding variables in complex data sets.
Researchers used multivariate analysis to assess the impact of several socioeconomic factors on education outcomes.
The multivariate analysis revealed a significant relationship between age, diet, and blood pressure.
