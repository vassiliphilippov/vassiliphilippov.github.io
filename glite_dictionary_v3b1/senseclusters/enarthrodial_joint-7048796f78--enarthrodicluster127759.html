enarthrodial joint

noun
type of ball-and-socket joint in anatomy
Pronunciation: / ɛnˌɑːrˈθroʊdiəl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / enˌɑːˈθrəʊdiəl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a freely movable joint in which the rounded head of one bone fits into a cup-like cavity of another, allowing movement in multiple directions

Examples

An enarthrodial joint movement
Damage to an enarthrodial joint may result in limited flexibility and pain.
The hip is classified as an enarthrodial joint because it permits motion in multiple axes.
The shoulder is an example of an enarthrodial joint, allowing for a wide range of movement.
