winterization

noun
preparation for winter
Pronunciation: / ˌwɪntərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌwɪntəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the physiological or behavioral changes that organisms undergo to survive winter conditions, such as storing fat or entering dormancy

Examples

Winterization in animals
The winterization of certain mammals includes growing thicker fur to retain body heat.
Farmers study the winterization process in plants to improve crop survival during cold months.
Some birds undergo winterization by migrating to warmer regions before the temperature drops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - a change by which an organism becomes better suited to its environment
  • synonym: hibernation - a state of inactivity and metabolic depression during winter
