protasis

noun
introductory section
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊtəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəsɪs / (BrE)
the introductory part of a play, narrative, or speech that sets the scene or situation

Examples

Protasis of a play
The protasis of the novel carefully introduced the main characters and their relationships.
In classical drama, the protasis was essential for establishing the background of the story.
The audience listened closely as the protasis set the stage for the unfolding events.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: epitasis - the part of a play where the main action develops
  • synonym: introduction - the beginning section that sets the stage for what follows
