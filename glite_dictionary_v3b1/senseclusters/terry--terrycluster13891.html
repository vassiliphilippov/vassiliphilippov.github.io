terry

noun
fabric with loops, used for towels
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.i / (BrE)
A strong cotton or linen fabric with uncut loops on both sides, commonly used for towels and bathrobes.

Examples

Terry cloth fabric
Bath towels made from terry are soft and highly absorbent.
She bought a luxurious robe made of white terry for the spa.
The hotel provided slippers with matching terry lining for added comfort.
