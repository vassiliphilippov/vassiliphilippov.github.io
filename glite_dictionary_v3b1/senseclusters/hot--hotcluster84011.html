hot

adjective
exceptionally skillful or energetic; impressive
3.1 3.38/m
Pronunciation: / hÉ‘t / (AmE), / hÉ’t / (BrE)
very skillful, lucky, or performing exceptionally well, especially in games or competitions

Examples

Hot streak
She's been hot lately, winning every match she plays.
The guitarist was absolutely hot during the solo of the final song.
The whole team was hot tonight, scoring twice as many points as usual.
He's hot at chess but not so hot at basketball.
Jenny's really hot when it comes to solving puzzles quickly.
Unfortunately, my cooking skills are not hot by any stretch of the imagination.
Police were in hot pursuit of the suspect after the robbery.
That new laptop is so hot that it can load complex programs almost instantly.
The team got off to a hot start in the first quarter, scoring three goals quickly.
