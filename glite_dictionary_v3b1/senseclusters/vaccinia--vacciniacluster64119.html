vaccinia

noun
virus used in smallpox vaccination
9.8 7.7
Pronunciation: /væk.ˈsɪn.ɪ.ə/ (AmE), /væk.ˈsɪn.ɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a viral disease of cattle that causes mild skin lesions, and the virus used in the vaccine for smallpox in humans

Examples

Vaccinia virus infection
Vaccinia virus was historically instrumental in the global eradication of smallpox.
A mild skin reaction can sometimes occur at the site of vaccinia inoculation.
Researchers are investigating new uses for vaccinia virus in developing other vaccines.
