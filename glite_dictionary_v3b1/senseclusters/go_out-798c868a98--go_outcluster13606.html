go out

verb
cease burning or shining
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
(of a light, fire, etc.) to stop burning or shining; to become extinguished.

Examples

The lights go out
Make sure the campfire goes out before you leave the site.
Suddenly, all the lights in the house went out during the storm.
The candles went out when the wind blew through the window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: go off - to stop functioning (especially of devices, machines)
  • antonym: ignite - to catch fire or begin to burn
