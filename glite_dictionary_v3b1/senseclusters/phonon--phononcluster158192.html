phonon

noun
quantum of vibration
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊnɑn / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnɒn / (BrE)
a unit of vibrational energy that carries sound or heat through a solid material

Examples

Phonons in crystals
Scientists study how phonons move through crystals to understand heat transfer.
The interaction between electrons and phonons affects the electrical properties of materials.
A phonon can be thought of as a particle representing a unit of vibrational energy.
Researchers measured the movement of a phonon in a crystal lattice to study heat transfer.
The concept of the phonon helps explain how sound travels through solid materials.
Advanced materials can control the flow of phonons to improve thermal insulation.

  • synonym: photon - a quantum of electromagnetic energy, or light particle
