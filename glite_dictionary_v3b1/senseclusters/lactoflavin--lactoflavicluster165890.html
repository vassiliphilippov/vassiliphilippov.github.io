lactoflavin

noun
a B vitamin (riboflavin); nutrient for health
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌlæktoʊˈfleɪvɪn / (AmE), / ˌlaktəʊˈfleɪvɪn / (BrE)
a B vitamin, also known as riboflavin, important for body growth, red cell production, and preventing skin disorders and weight loss

Examples

The lactoflavin vitamin
A deficiency of lactoflavin can lead to symptoms like skin lesions and fatigue.
Milk is a rich source of lactoflavin, which helps maintain healthy skin.
Some cereals are fortified with lactoflavin for added nutritional value.
