ahead

adverb
to a more advanced or advantageous position
Pronunciation: / əˈhɛd / (AmE), / əˈhɛd / (BrE)
at or to a position in front or further forward; moving or facing forward

Examples

Move ahead in life
If you want to get ahead in your career, you need to keep learning new skills.
Thanks to the early investment, the company stayed ahead of its competitors.
With his experience, he moved ahead of the other candidates in the interview process.
Please go ahead and open the door for our guests.
The hikers moved ahead along the narrow trail as the sun began to set.
The ship sailed ahead through the thick fog, barely visible from the shore.
He looked straight ahead and continued walking without hesitation.
Please keep your eyes ahead while driving.
There was a long stretch of empty road ahead of us.

Related Terms

  • antonym: back - in the opposite direction; to a previous position
  • antonym: behind - at a less advanced position; less developed or progressed
  • synonym: forward - toward the front or a position in advance
  • synonym: in front - in a forward position; ahead
