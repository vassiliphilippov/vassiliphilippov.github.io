in an enfilade

preposition
arranged in a straight line or alignment
8.3
Pronunciation: / ɪn æn ˈɛn.fəˌleɪd / (AmE), / ɪn ən ˈɛn.fɪˌleɪd / (BrE)
Arranged in a straight or aligned formation, especially to maximize visibility or the effectiveness of fire, as in military strategy or design.

Examples

Troops in an enfilade
Officers lined up the troops in an enfilade to cover the width of the field.
The artwork was displayed in an enfilade, drawing visitors down the long hallway.
The chairs were placed in an enfilade so everyone had a clear view of the presentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enfilade - a position or formation providing a clear, straight line of fire
