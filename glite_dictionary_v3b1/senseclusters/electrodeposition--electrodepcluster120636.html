electrodeposition

noun
deposition on an electrode using electricity
9.1 7.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.troʊ.dɪ.pə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trəʊ.dɪ.pə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of depositing a substance onto an electrode using electrical current, especially by electrolysis

Examples

Electrodeposition of metal
Nickel electrodeposition is widely used in manufacturing electronic components.
Recent advances in electrodeposition techniques have improved battery performance.
The metal coating was achieved by electrodeposition from a salt solution.
