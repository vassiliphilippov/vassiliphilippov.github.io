absorptiometry

noun
measurement of absorption
9.8
Pronunciation: / əbˌsɔrpʃiˈɑmətri /; / əbˌsɔːrpʃiˈɑːmɪtri / (AmE), / əbˌsɔːpʃiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
a scientific technique for measuring how much a substance or material absorbs, especially used in medical testing to measure things like bone density

Examples

Absorptiometry test
The researchers used absorptiometry to determine the mineral content in the bone samples.
Modern laboratories rely on absorptiometry for accurate measurement of chemical concentrations.
A technician performed absorptiometry to analyze how much dye the fabric absorbed.
Doctors use absorptiometry to assess bone density in patients at risk of osteoporosis.
Modern hospitals rely on absorptiometry for accurate measurement of mineral content in bones.
The technician explained how absorptiometry helps detect early signs of bone loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: densitometry - the measurement of density, especially in medical imaging
  • synonym: spectrophotometry - a technique for measuring how much light a substance absorbs
