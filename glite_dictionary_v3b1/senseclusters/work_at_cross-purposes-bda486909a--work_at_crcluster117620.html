work at cross-purposes

verb
act with conflicting intentions
6.5
Pronunciation: / wɜrk ət krɔs ˈpɜrpəsɪz / (AmE), / wɜːk ət krɒs ˈpɜːpəsɪz / (BrE)
to act with conflicting goals or intentions, often causing misunderstanding or ineffectiveness

Examples

To work at cross-purposes
During the meeting, the managers seemed to work at cross-purposes, so little was accomplished.
If departments work at cross-purposes, progress on company goals will be slow.
They realized halfway through the project that they were working at cross-purposes and needed to coordinate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pull in opposite directions - to try to achieve different aims, making progress difficult
