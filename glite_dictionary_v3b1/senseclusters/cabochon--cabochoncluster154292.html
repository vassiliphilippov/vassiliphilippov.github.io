cabochon

noun
smooth, faceted-less gemstone cut
Pronunciation: / ˈkæbəˌʃɑn / (AmE), / ˈkab.ə.ʃɒ̃ / (BrE)
A gemstone that has been polished into a smooth, rounded, convex shape without facets.

Examples

A cabochon stone
Jewelry designers often use cabochon stones to highlight a gem's natural color and clarity.
The museum display included an exquisite emerald cabochon from the 19th century.
The ruby in her ring was cut as a cabochon, giving it a distinctive, smooth look.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brilliant cut - a gem cut with many facets, especially for diamonds
  • antonym: facet - a flat polished surface on a cut gemstone
