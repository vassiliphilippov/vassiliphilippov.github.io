bill of particulars

noun
detailed statement of charges in a court case
2.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / bɪl əv pərˈtɪkjələrz / (AmE), / bɪl əv pəˈtɪkjʊləz / (BrE)
a detailed written statement of the specific facts and charges presented by the prosecution in a criminal case to inform the defendant of the claims to be answered

Examples

A bill of particulars request
The defense attorney requested a bill of particulars to clarify the charges against her client.
The judge ordered the prosecution to file a bill of particulars within ten days.
Without a bill of particulars, the defendant may not be fully aware of the allegations they face.
