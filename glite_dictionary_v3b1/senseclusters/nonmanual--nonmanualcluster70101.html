nonmanual

noun
non-hand sign language signal
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈmænjuəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈmænjuəl / (BrE)
a facial expression, head movement, or other non-hand signal in sign language that conveys grammatical or emotional information

Examples

A nonmanual in ASL
In American Sign Language, a raised eyebrow is a common nonmanual used to indicate a question.
The meaning of the sign changes depending on the nonmanual signals that accompany it.
Teachers emphasize the importance of using correct nonmanual markers when learning sign language.
