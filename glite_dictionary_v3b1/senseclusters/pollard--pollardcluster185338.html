pollard

noun
animal with removed or shed horns
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlɚd / (AmE), / ˈpɒləd / (BrE)
an animal, typically one with horns, that has had its horns removed or has shed them

Examples

A hornless pollard
In the herd, one pollard stood out, lacking the horns of its peers.
Removing horns turns the animal into a pollard, which can reduce injuries among livestock.
The farmer separated the pollard from the rest of the cattle for health reasons.
