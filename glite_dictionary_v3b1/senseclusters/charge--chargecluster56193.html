charge

noun
amount of unbalanced electrical quantity
6.7 8.40/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
the amount of unbalanced electrical quantity in a body, either positive or negative, viewed as an excess or deficiency of electrons

Examples

An electric charge
An object with a negative charge contains more electrons than protons.
Static charge can build up when you rub a balloon against your hair.
The battery needed a fresh charge before it would power the device.
