pseudorubella

noun
viral disease with brief rash in children
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåsuÀêdo äruÀàb…õl…ô / (AmE), / ÀåsjuÀêd…ô äruÀêÀàb…õl…ô / (BrE)
A viral disease of infants and young children, marked by a sudden high fever, mild sore throat, and a brief pinkish rash.

Examples

Mild pseudorubella rash
Cases of pseudorubella are often mistaken for more serious illnesses like measles or rubella.
Parents should be aware that pseudorubella usually resolves on its own without complications.
The doctor diagnosed the child with pseudorubella after noticing the brief rash and high fever.

Synonyms

  • roseola infantum
    8.8 0.00/m
    roseola infantum is the common medical name for the sudden fever and brief pinkish rash in babies, while pseudorubella is a less used term.
  • roseola infantilis
    6.5 0.00/m
    roseola infantilis is a variant name used in some textbooks for the same sudden fever and rash in infants, while pseudorubella is less common.
  • exanthema subitum
    8.6 0.00/m
    exanthema subitum is the formal Latin medical name seen in clinical reports, while pseudorubella is a more descriptive English term.
