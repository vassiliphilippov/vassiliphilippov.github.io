Toxicodendron radicans

noun
plant causing skin irritation (poison ivy)
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑksɪoʊˈdɛndrən ˈrædɪkænz / (AmE), / ˌtɒksɪəʊˈdɛndrən ˈrædɪkænz / (BrE)
A climbing or spreading plant native to eastern and central North America, known for its three-part leaves and an oil that causes skin irritation upon contact.

Examples

Toxicodendron radicans plant
Hikers are warned to avoid touching Toxicodendron radicans because it can cause a severe rash.

The white berries of Toxicodendron radicans are eaten by birds even though the plant is toxic to humans.
