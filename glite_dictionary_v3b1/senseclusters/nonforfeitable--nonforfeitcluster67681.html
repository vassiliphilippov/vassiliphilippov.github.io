nonforfeitable

adjective
cannot be taken away
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈfɔrfɪtəbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈfɔːfɪtəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be lost, given up, or taken away, especially by legal or financial action

Examples

Nonforfeitable pension benefits
After ten years of service, her pension benefits became nonforfeitable under the company's policy.
The contract guarantees nonforfeitable rights to the property for all heirs.
Once the conditions are met, the employee's stock options are nonforfeitable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forfeitable - able to be lost or taken away as a penalty
  • synonym: irrevocable - impossible to change or reverse
