candescent

adjective
glowing with heat
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: / kænˈdɛsənt / (AmE), / kænˈdɛsənt / (BrE)
glowing or shining as a result of being heated to a high temperature; emitting light due to great heat.

Examples

A candescent light
Molten lava poured down the hillside, its surface candescent in the night.
She shielded her eyes from the candescent coals inside the furnace.
The candescent metal glowed brightly in the forge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dark - having little or no light
  • synonym: incandescent - emitting light as a result of being heated
  • synonym: luminous - emitting or reflecting light, especially in the dark
