neptunium

noun
radioactive metallic chemical element, Np
6.8
Pronunciation: / nɛpˈtuniəm / (AmE), / nɛpˈtjuːniəm / (BrE)
a radioactive metallic chemical element with the symbol Np and atomic number 93, occurring in trace amounts in uranium ores and produced artificially as a by-product of plutonium production.

Examples

Neptunium element
Handling neptunium requires specialized safety procedures due to its radioactivity.
Scientists detected trace amounts of neptunium in the uranium ore sample.
The nuclear reactor generated neptunium as a by-product during plutonium production.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: plutonium - a radioactive chemical element produced from neptunium in nuclear reactors
  • degree lower: uranium - a heavy radioactive metal from which neptunium is derived
