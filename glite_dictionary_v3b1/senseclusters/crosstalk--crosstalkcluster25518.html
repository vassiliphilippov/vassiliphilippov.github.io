crosstalk

noun
unwanted signal interference
3.9 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɔː.stɔːk/ /; / /ˈkrɑː.stɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒ.stɔːk/ (BrE)
undesired interference caused by signals from one circuit or channel being unintentionally transferred to another

Examples

High levels of crosstalk in network cables can seriously affect data transmission quality.
The telephone lines suffered from noticeable crosstalk, making conversations unclear.
