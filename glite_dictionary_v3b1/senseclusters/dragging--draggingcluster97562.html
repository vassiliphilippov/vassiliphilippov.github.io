dragging

adjective
painfully slow and effortful; tedious
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdræɡɪŋ / (BrE)
moving or continuing in a painfully slow and effortful way; prolonged and tedious.

Examples

A dragging negotiation
After months of dragging negotiations, they finally reached an agreement.
The meeting turned into a dragging affair that tested everyone's patience.
The movie felt slow and dragging despite the interesting premise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brisk - quick and energetic
  • synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome
