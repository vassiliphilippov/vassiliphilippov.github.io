shoulder-to-shoulder

adverb
side by side, close together
Pronunciation: / ˌʃoʊldɚ tə ˈʃoʊldɚ / (AmE), / ˌʃəʊldə tə ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
side by side and very close together, especially in a row or group

Examples

Shoulder-to-shoulder together
During the protest, students marched shoulder-to-shoulder down the main street.
In the kitchen, they worked shoulder-to-shoulder to prepare the meal.
The crowd stood shoulder-to-shoulder waiting for the parade to begin.
