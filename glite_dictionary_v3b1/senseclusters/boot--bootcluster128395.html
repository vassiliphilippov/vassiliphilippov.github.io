boot

noun
car's luggage compartment (British)
3.4 3.37/m
Pronunciation: / buÀêt / (AmE), / buÀêt / (BrE)
(British) the enclosed space at the back or front of a car used for carrying luggage or other items; trunk.

Examples

Car boot space
He found a spare tire in the boot of his car.
She opened the boot to load her groceries after shopping.
The suitcase wouldn't fit in the boot, so they had to rearrange everything.

Synonyms

  • trunk
    1.0 9.16/m
    trunk is the American name for a car's rear storage compartment, while boot is the British word used in everyday speech.
  • luggage compartment
    3.1 0.05/m
    luggage compartment refers to any vehicle area for bags and tools, while boot is the British term for the car's trunk.
