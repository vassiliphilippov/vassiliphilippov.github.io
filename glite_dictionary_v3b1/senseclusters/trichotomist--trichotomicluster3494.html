trichotomist

noun
proponent of three-part division
9.9
Pronunciation: / traɪˈkɑːtəˌmɪst / (AmE), / traɪˈkɒtəmɪst / (BrE)
a person who divides something into three parts or supports a theory based on a three-part division

Examples

A famous trichotomist
The ancient scholar was a well-known trichotomist who believed in the division of reality into three elements.
As a trichotomist, she argued that human nature consists of body, mind, and spirit.
The debate between the dichotomist and the trichotomist revealed different approaches to philosophy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dichotomist - a person who divides something into two parts
