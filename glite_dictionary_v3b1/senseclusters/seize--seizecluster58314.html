seize

verb
take legal possession temporarily
Pronunciation: / siːz / (AmE), / siːz / (BrE)
to take possession of something by legal authority, especially temporarily or as security

Examples

Police seize drugs
Customs officers may seize goods if they suspect illegal activity.
The government can seize property during an investigation under certain laws.
The police decided to seize all computer equipment related to the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confiscate - to take or seize someone's property with authority, often as a penalty
  • synonym: impound - to seize and take legal custody of something, especially by officials
