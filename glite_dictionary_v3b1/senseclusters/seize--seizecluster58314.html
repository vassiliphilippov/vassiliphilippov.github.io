seize

verb
take legal possession temporarily
4.6 8.02/m
Pronunciation: / siÀêz / (AmE), / siÀêz / (BrE)
to take possession of something by legal authority, especially temporarily or as security

Examples

Police seize drugs
Customs officers may seize goods if they suspect illegal activity.
The government can seize property during an investigation under certain laws.
The police decided to seize all computer equipment related to the case.

Synonyms

  • attach
    7.1 3.14/m
    attach means taking property by legal authority as security for a claim, while seize means taking possession more generally, often temporarily.
  • sequester
    8.6 0.17/m
    sequester means temporarily taking property by court order to hold it, while seize covers broader lawful taking, sometimes permanently.
  • distrain
    9.9 0.00/m
    distrain means legally seizing someone's goods to force payment of debt, while seize is a general lawful taking often for security.
  • impound
    7.2 1.21/m
    impound means taking items into legal custody for evidence or safekeeping, while seize is a broader legal taking.
  • confiscate
    5.5 6.59/m
    confiscate means officially taking something away as a penalty, while seize means taking possession by legal authority.
  • commandeer
    8.5 1.39/m
    commandeer means taking control for military or official use often without permission, while seize means taking possession by legal authority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confiscate - to take or seize someone's property with authority, often as a penalty
  • synonym: impound - to seize and take legal custody of something, especially by officials
