drawn-out

adjective
tediously long in duration
4.6 0.80/m
Pronunciation: / dr…în a ät / (AmE), / dr…îÀên a ät / (BrE)
lasting longer than necessary or expected, often in a tedious or tiresome way.

Examples

Drawn-out process
She found the process of refunding her ticket to be unnecessarily drawn-out.
The meeting turned into a drawn-out debate that lasted well into the evening.
Their drawn-out farewell made everyone in the room feel uncomfortable.
