levee

noun
embankment to prevent river flooding
7.2 4.74/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆlÉ›vi / (AmE), / ËˆlÉ›vi / (BrE)
an embankment or landing place built along a river, often to prevent flooding or to serve as a place for boats to dock

Examples

River levee
After heavy rains, engineers inspected the levee for any signs of weakness.
During the storm, water threatened to breach the levee along the riverbank.
The city built a new levee to protect neighborhoods from seasonal flooding.
A steamboat was moored at the levee, waiting for passengers to board.
Fishermen gathered at the wooden levee to unload their catch at sunrise.
The old city built a stone levee to allow ferries to dock along the riverbank.

Synonyms

  • embankment
    5.9 2.40/m
    embankment is a raised earth or stone bank that holds back water or supports roads, while levee protects rivers and serves as a landing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dam - a barrier constructed to hold back water and raise its level
  • synonym: dock - an enclosed area of water for loading or unloading ships
  • synonym: dyke - an embankment built to prevent flooding, especially in the Netherlands
  • synonym: embankment - a wall or bank of earth or stone built to prevent a river flooding an area
  • synonym: quay - a platform lying alongside or projecting into water for loading and unloading ships
