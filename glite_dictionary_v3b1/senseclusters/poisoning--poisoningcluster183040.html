poisoning

noun
harmful condition from poison or toxin
2.1 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
A harmful physiological condition caused by the absorption of a poison or toxic substance into the body.

Examples

Food poisoning incident
Carbon monoxide poisoning can be fatal if not treated quickly and effectively.
The doctor diagnosed her condition as lead poisoning caused by exposure at work.
The patient was admitted to the hospital with acute poisoning after ingesting contaminated food.
