corruptor

noun
agent causing decay
7.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌptər / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a substance or agent that causes decay, spoilage, or decomposition of materials or organic matter

Examples

A chemical corruptor
The chemical spill proved to be a serious corruptor of the farmland, ruining crops for months.
Exposure to moisture can act as a corruptor of stored grains, leading to mold and spoilage.
Scientists are searching for ways to neutralize the corruptor before it damages the water supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contaminant - a substance that makes something impure or harmful
  • antonym: preservative - a substance used to prevent decay or spoilage
