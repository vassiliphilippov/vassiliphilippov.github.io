jerk

verb
move sharply and suddenly
2.0 15/m
Pronunciation: / d í…ùk / (AmE), / d í…úÀêk / (BrE)
to move or make something move suddenly and sharply

Examples

To jerk your head
He jerked his head around to see what had made the noise.
Her arm suddenly jerked in her sleep, waking her up.
The train jerked forward as it left the station.
He jerked his head up when he heard the noise.
She jerked her thumb over her shoulder to indicate the direction.
The train jerked forward suddenly, catching the passengers off guard.
He jerked the drawer open with one strong pull.
She jerked her hand away from the hot pan.
Suddenly, the train jerked forward and caught everyone by surprise.

Synonyms

  • jolt
    3.7 1.70/m
    jolt moves something in a sudden, rough motion, while jerk means a quick, sharp movement that is often briefer or less rough.
  • jar
    5.3 0.08/m
    jar moves or shakes something suddenly and roughly, while jerk highlights a single sharp, quick motion rather than ongoing shaking.
  • yank
    4.1 13/m
    yank pulls something with a sudden, strong force, while jerk describes any quick, sharp movement not only a forceful pull.
  • snap
    1.6 5.49/m
    snap makes a sudden sharp sound or quick movement, while jerk focuses on a short abrupt physical motion without sound.
