woodlander

noun
forest inhabitant
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌlændər / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌlændə / (BrE)
a person or animal that lives in or is closely connected to woodlands

Examples

An experienced woodlander
The experienced woodlander could identify every plant and animal in the forest.
Many stories feature a wise woodlander who helps lost travelers find their way.
Some animals, like the fox, are true woodlanders adapted to life among the trees.
The experienced woodlander knew every path and animal track in the forest.
As a woodlander, she spent her days caring for the health of the woodland.
Many woodlanders rely on traditional knowledge to manage the trees sustainably.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forest dweller - a person or animal living in a forest
  • synonym: forester - a person in charge of managing forests
  • antonym: urbanite - a person who lives in a city
  • synonym: woodcutter - a person who cuts down trees or chops wood
