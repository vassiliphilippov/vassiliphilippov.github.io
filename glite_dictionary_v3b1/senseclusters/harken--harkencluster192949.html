harken

verb
to recall the past
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːr.kən/ (AmE), /ˈhɑː.kən/ (BrE)
to recall or be reminiscent of something from the past, often used in the phrase 'harken back'.

Examples

Harken back to childhood
The new film harkens back to the golden age of cinema.
Her vintage dress harkens to styles popular in the 1950s.
This tradition harkens to ancient rituals practiced centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember
  • synonym: reminisce - to think or talk about past experiences
