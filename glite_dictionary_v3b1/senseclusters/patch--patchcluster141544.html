patch

noun
soft material for covering injuries
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /pætʃ/ (AmE), /pætʃ/ (BrE)
a piece of material used to cover, protect, or repair something, such as skin, an eye, or clothing

Examples

An eye patch
A small patch kept the wound clean and helped it to heal.
He wore an eye patch after the surgery to protect his eye.
The nurse applied a sterile patch over the cut on his arm.
After tearing her shirt, she covered the rip with a small blue patch.
He sewed a patch onto the knee of his jeans to cover a hole.
The jacket was decorated with a colorful patch on the sleeve.
After his surgery, he wore a patch over his right eye for several weeks.
She replaced the old patch with a fresh, clean one to help the wound heal.
The pirate is often depicted with a patch covering one eye.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandage - a strip of fabric used to bind a wound or protect an injured part
  • synonym: eyepatch - a piece of cloth or material worn over an eye, especially to protect an injured one
  • synonym: mend - a repair made to something that is damaged or worn, especially by sewing
  • antonym: tear - a hole caused by being torn
