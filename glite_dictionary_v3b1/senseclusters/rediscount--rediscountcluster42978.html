rediscount

noun
rate for re-discounting
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (BrE)
the rate or charge applied when a bill or financial instrument is discounted again, usually by a central bank.

Examples

Rediscount rate
The government announced a reduction in the rediscount to stimulate economic growth.
Banks often monitor the rediscount set by the central bank to adjust their lending rates.
A lower rediscount can encourage financial institutions to provide more loans to businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discount rate - the interest rate charged by a central bank on loans to commercial banks
