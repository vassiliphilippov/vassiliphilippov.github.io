form

verb
compose or make up
Pronunciation: / fɔrm / (AmE), / fɔːm / (BrE)
to serve as, make up, or constitute a particular part or feature of something; to compose or represent

Examples

Rivers form borders
The river forms a natural boundary between the two countries.
The statue in the garden forms the focal point of the entire landscape.
These facts form the basis of our conclusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose - to make up or constitute a whole
  • antonym: dissolve - to disintegrate or break apart; to cease to form a whole
  • synonym: make up - to constitute; to be parts of a whole
