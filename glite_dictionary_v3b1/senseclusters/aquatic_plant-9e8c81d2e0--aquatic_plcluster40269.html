aquatic plant

noun
plant that grows in water
3.1
Pronunciation: / əˈkwɑːtɪk plænt / (AmE), / əˈkwɒtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that grows partly or entirely in water, such as a water lily or pondweed

Examples

Aquatic plants in water
Many aquatic plants provide shelter for fish and other wildlife.
Removing invasive aquatic plants helps maintain the balance of the ecosystem.
The pond was covered with aquatic plants like lilies and duckweed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrophyte - a plant adapted to living in aquatic environments
