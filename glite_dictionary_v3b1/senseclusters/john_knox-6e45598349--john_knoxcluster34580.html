John Knox

proper noun
Scottish theologian and Presbyterianism founder
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn nɑks / (AmE), / dʒɒn nɒks / (BrE)
(1514–1572) A Scottish theologian and reformer who founded Presbyterianism in Scotland and wrote a history of the Scottish Reformation.

Examples

John Knox Scottish reformer
John Knox played a central role in the Scottish Reformation.
Many regard John Knox as the founder of Presbyterianism.
The teachings of John Knox influenced religious practices across Scotland.
