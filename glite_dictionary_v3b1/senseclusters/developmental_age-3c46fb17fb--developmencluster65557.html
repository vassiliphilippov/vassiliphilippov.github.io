developmental age

noun
a child's development measured by age norms
3.6 4.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt.əl eɪdʒ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt.əl eɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a measure of a child's development, expressed in terms of the age at which typical children reach a given physical, motor, or psychological ability

Examples

Developmental age level
Some children with learning disabilities may have a lower developmental age than their peers.
The psychologist assessed the child's developmental age to identify any delays in cognitive abilities.
Therapists design activities appropriate for the developmental age rather than just the chronological age of the child.
