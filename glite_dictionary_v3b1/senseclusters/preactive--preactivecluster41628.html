preactive

adjective
before something happens
7.7
Pronunciation: / priˈæktɪv / (AmE), / priˈæktɪv / (BrE)
describing actions or planning that take place before an event or situation occurs

Examples

Preactive planning
A preactive strategy can help companies avoid potential problems before they arise.
The team held a preactive meeting to discuss possible risks for the project.
By being preactive, she was able to prepare for changes in the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proactive - taking action in advance to deal with expected difficulties
  • antonym: reactive - responding to events after they have happened
