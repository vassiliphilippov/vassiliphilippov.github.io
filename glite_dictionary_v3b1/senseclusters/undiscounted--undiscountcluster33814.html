undiscounted

adjective
before discounting
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪsˈkaʊntɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪsˈkaʊntɪd / (BrE)
not adjusted to reflect the time value of money; stated before any discounting to present value, especially in finance

Examples

Undiscounted cash flow
The financial report included both undiscounted and discounted cash flow projections for the next ten years.
Investors should not rely solely on undiscounted figures when evaluating long-term projects.
The undiscounted value of the future payments was much higher than their present value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discounted - adjusted to present value by accounting for the time value of money
