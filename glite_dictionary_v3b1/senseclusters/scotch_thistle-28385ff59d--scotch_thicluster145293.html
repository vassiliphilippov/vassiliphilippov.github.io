Scotch thistle

noun
a spiny Eurasian biennial thistle plant
Pronunciation: / skɑtʃ ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A tall, spiny biennial plant native to Eurasia, with white hairy stems and leaves and pale purple flowers; often naturalized in North America.

Examples

The Scotch thistle plant
Gardeners often struggle to remove Scotch thistle because of its sharp spines and rapid spread.
The Scotch thistle can grow up to three meters tall in ideal conditions.
The national emblem of Scotland is the Scotch thistle, which appears on many traditional crests.
