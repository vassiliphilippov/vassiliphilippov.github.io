orienteering

noun
adjusting to new situations
Pronunciation: / ˌɔriənˈtɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɒriənˈtɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of learning how to navigate or find one's way in a new place, job, or situation, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Job orienteering
Starting the new job required a lot of orienteering to understand procedures and meet colleagues.
After moving to a new city, she spent weeks orienteering and learning her way around.
University life involves plenty of orienteering as students adjust to new routines and expectations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acclimatization - the process of becoming accustomed to a new environment
  • synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to new conditions
