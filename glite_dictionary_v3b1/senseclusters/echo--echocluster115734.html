echo

noun
imitation or repetition
3.6 2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ /; / ˈekəʊ / (BrE)
an imitation or repetition of something, such as an idea, style, or statement

Examples

An echo of style
Her painting is an echo of the master’s famous landscape.
His speech offered an echo of ideas from earlier debates.
The new building is an echo of the classical style.
Her joke was so funny that it got an echo from the entire group.
His angry words found an echo in her own frustrations.
When she called his name, his echo came back faintly from the other end of the tunnel.
Her speech was an echo of the leader's earlier remarks.
His new novel contains more than an echo of Hemingway's style.
The band's latest album is an echo of 1990s grunge music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - something made to be similar or identical to another
  • synonym: imitation - the action of using someone or something as a model
  • antonym: original - the first or earliest form of something, not an imitation
  • synonym: repetition - the action of repeating something that has already been said or written
  • synonym: response - an answer or reply to something said
