erase

verb
remove digitally or magnetically recorded information
Pronunciation: / ɪˈreɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪs /; / ɪˈreɪz / (BrE)
to remove or delete something so that it is no longer visible or accessible (such as marks, writing, or digital information)

Examples

To erase a file
He accidentally erased all the photos from his camera's memory card.
Make sure to back up your work before you erase the hard drive.
The technician erased the confidential data from the computer before recycling it.
She tried to erase the pencil marks from the page.
The technician will erase all data from the old computer before recycling it.
You should erase any incorrect answers before submitting your test.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delete - remove or eliminate data, usually from a digital medium
  • synonym: remove - to take away or get rid of something
  • antonym: restore - to return deleted information to its original state
  • antonym: write - to create or put down marks or letters on a surface
