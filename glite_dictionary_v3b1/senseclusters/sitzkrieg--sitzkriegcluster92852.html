sitzkrieg

noun
period of inaction
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪts.kriːg/ /; / /ˈzɪts.kriːg/ (AmE), /ˈsɪts.kriːg/ /; / /ˈzɪts.kriːg/ (BrE)
a period of little or no active progress, especially during a conflict, negotiation, or competition, marked by waiting or inaction

Examples

Negotiation sitzkrieg
Negotiations between the two countries entered a sitzkrieg as neither side was willing to compromise.
The project was stuck in a sitzkrieg for months while management waited for new funding.
After weeks of heated debate, the committee settled into a sitzkrieg with no decisions being made.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deadlock - a situation where neither side can win or make progress
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation in which no progress can be made
