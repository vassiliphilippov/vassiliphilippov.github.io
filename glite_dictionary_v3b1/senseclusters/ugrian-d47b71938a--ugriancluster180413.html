Ugrian

noun
branch of Finno-Ugric languages
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːɡriən / (AmE), / ˈjuːɡriən / (BrE)
A branch of the Finno-Ugric family of languages, spoken in Hungary and northwestern Siberia.

Examples

Ugrian languages
Linguists have long debated the origins of the Ugrian branch within the Finno-Ugric family.
Some Ugrian languages are endangered as a result of cultural and societal changes.
The Ugrian languages include Hungarian and several languages spoken in western Siberia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Finno-Ugric - a family of languages including Finnish, Hungarian, and others
