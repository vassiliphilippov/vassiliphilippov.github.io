skeleton shrimp

noun
small amphipod marine crustacean
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛlətən ʃrɪmp / (AmE), / ˈskɛlɪtən ʃrɪmp / (BrE)
a small, slender marine crustacean of the family Caprellidae, noted for its elongated body and resemblance to a praying mantis, found chiefly on seaweed

Examples

Small skeleton shrimp
Divers often spot skeleton shrimp swaying gently in shallow coastal waters.
Due to its bizarre shape, the skeleton shrimp is sometimes mistaken for a piece of debris.
The skeleton shrimp clung to the seaweed, perfectly camouflaged among the green strands.
