immediate

adjective
very close in space or time
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
very close or connected in space, time, or relationship; direct, with nothing or no one in between; existing or occurring in the present moment or place

Examples

The substance produces an immediate reaction when it comes into contact with air.
An immediate cause of stress in the workplace is unclear communication.
The immediate result of the policy was increased funding for public schools.
The doctor focused on treating the immediate effects of the injury before addressing long-term concerns.
