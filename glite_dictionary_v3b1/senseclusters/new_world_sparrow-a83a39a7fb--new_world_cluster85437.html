New World sparrow

noun
American songbird, family Passerellidae
9.9
Pronunciation: / nuː ˈwɝld ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
a small songbird of the Americas, belonging to the family Passerellidae, resembling Old World sparrows but not closely related

Examples

A New World sparrow bird
Conservationists are concerned about declining New World sparrow populations due to habitat loss.
The white-throated New World sparrow is often heard singing in North American woodlands.
Unlike the house sparrow, a true Old World species, the New World sparrow belongs to a different family.
