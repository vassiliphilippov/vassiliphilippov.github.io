ersatz

adjective
artificial and inferior substitute
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrˌzɑts / (AmE), / ˈɜːzæts / (BrE)
(of a product) made or used as a substitute, typically of inferior quality to the original.

Examples

Ersatz coffee
During the war, people drank ersatz coffee made from roasted barley.
His apology sounded so ersatz that no one believed he was sincere.
Many travelers were disappointed by the ersatz charm of the reconstructed village.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genuine - authentic, not artificial or inferior
  • synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another
