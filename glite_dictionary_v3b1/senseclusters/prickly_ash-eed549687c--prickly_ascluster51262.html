prickly ash

noun
spiny tree/shrub (Zanthoxylum genus)
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪkli ˌæʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli ˌaʃ / (BrE)
Any of several trees or shrubs of the genus Zanthoxylum, characterized by spiny branches, found in temperate and subtropical regions.

Examples

Spiny prickly ash shrub
Several species of prickly ash are cultivated for their distinctive aromatic qualities.
The bark of the prickly ash has long been used in traditional medicine.
The spiny branches of prickly ash trees can make them difficult to handle without gloves.
