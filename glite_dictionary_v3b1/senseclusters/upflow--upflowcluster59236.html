upflow

adjective
moving upward (fluid)
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈʌp.fləʊ / (BrE)
designed to move air or another fluid upward, especially in equipment or systems like furnaces or ventilation.

Examples

Upflow furnace system
The technician recommended installing an upflow furnace for better heating on the upper floors.
This upflow ventilation system helps remove warm air from the basement.
Many modern HVAC units use an upflow design to improve energy efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downflow - designed to move air or fluid downward
