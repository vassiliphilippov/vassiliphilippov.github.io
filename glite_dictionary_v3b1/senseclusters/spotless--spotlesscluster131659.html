Synonyms

  • immaculate
    5.9 2.42/m
    immaculate is perfectly clean with no tiny marks and sounds formal, while spotless means extremely clean with no stains.
  • spic-and-span
    4.9 0.07/m
    spic-and-span is an idiom for very tidy and well-cleaned homes used in speech, while spotless is simple and direct.
  • pristine
    5.1 5.77/m
    pristine is fresh and like new, often unused or untouched, while spotless only stresses being very clean.
  • unsoiled
    3.0 0.01/m
    unsoiled means not dirty and free of soil, often used for clothes or surfaces, while spotless is fully clean.
  • unstained
    3.2 0.01/m
    unstained describes items without marks or discoloration, often on fabric, while spotless means perfectly clean overall.
  • unspotted
    6.3 0.04/m
    unspotted says something has no spots or blemishes, often on skin or cloth, while spotless focuses on complete cleanliness.
  • unsmudged
    4.2 0.03/m
    unsmudged refers to surfaces without smears or smudges, often small marks, while spotless means entirely clean.
  • unblemished
    5.1 0.68/m
    unblemished means without physical flaws or moral faults and can be figurative, while spotless focuses on literal cleanliness.
