separate

adjective
not attached; standing apart
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛpəˌreɪt /; / ˈsɛpɚət / (AmE), / ˈsɛpərət / (BrE)
not joined, connected, or united; standing apart or distinct from others

Examples

A separate room
Each laboratory operates in a separate building on the university campus.
She prefers a separate workspace to avoid distractions from her colleagues.
The guest house has a separate entrance from the main building.
After the disagreement, the partners decided to go their separate ways.
Please keep the utensils separate from the rest of the dishes to avoid confusion.
The two islands remained separate after the land bridge disappeared.
The issue will be discussed during a separate meeting next week.
The two companies operate as separate entities within the same market.
We decided to go our separate ways after graduation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connected - joined together; attached
  • synonym: detached - not joined or connected; separate or disconnected
  • synonym: distinct - recognizably different or separate from something else
  • synonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others; free-standing
  • antonym: united - joined together as a single unit or entity
