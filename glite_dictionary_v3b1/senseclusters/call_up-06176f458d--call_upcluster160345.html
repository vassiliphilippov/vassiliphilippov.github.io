call up

verb
summon to active military service
4.4 2/m
Pronunciation: / k…îl  åp / (AmE), / k…îÀêl  åp / (BrE)
to order someone, especially military personnel, to begin active service; to summon to duty or arms

Examples

To call up soldiers
He received a letter telling him he would be called up for military duty.
Many young men were called up during the conflict to serve in the army.
The government decided to call up reservists in response to the crisis.

Synonyms

  • draft
    5.3 8/m
    draft selects people to join the military, while call up orders existing personnel to begin active duty, often temporarily.
  • conscript
    7.2 0.8/m
    conscript forces people by law to serve in the military, whereas call up usually summons trained personnel to start active duty.
  • muster in
    8.4 0.02/m
    muster in formally enrolls recruits into the armed forces, while call up summons personnel to start active service during a crisis.
