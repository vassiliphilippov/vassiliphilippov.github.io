distort

verb
twist out of shape
6.5 3.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstɔːrt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstɔːt/ (BrE)
to change the shape or form of something so that it is no longer normal or correct

Examples

To distort a shape
Heat can distort plastic containers, making them unusable.
The funhouse mirrors distort your reflection in hilarious ways.
When pressed too tightly, the soft metal will distort and lose its original shape.
Prolonged pressure can gradually distort the shape of a soft object.
She accidentally distorted the plastic lid when she pressed down too hard.
The heat began to distort the metal frame, causing it to bend unnaturally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deform - to change the shape of something, especially in a damaging way
  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight or normal in shape
  • synonym: warp - to bend or twist out of shape, typically by heat or damp
