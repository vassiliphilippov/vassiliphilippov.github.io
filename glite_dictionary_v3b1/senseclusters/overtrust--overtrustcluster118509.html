overtrust

verb
to trust too much
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtrʌst / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtrʌst / (BrE)
to trust someone or something more than is safe or reasonable

Examples

To overtrust a stranger
It is risky to overtrust unfamiliar online sources.
You should not overtrust people you have just met, no matter how friendly they seem.
Many investors overtrusted the company's promises and lost their savings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distrust - to doubt the honesty or reliability of someone or something
  • antonym: mistrust - to have little or no trust in someone or something
  • degree lower: trust - to believe that someone or something is reliable or honest
