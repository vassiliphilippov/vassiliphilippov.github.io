consume

verb
use up resources or materials
3.2 16/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsum / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsjuÀêm / (BrE)
to use up a resource, material, or supply; to expend until nothing is left

Examples

To consume energy
If we consume all of our natural resources at this rate, we may run out soon.
The factory consumes vast amounts of electricity during peak hours.
This old heating system consumes more fuel than modern, energy-efficient models.

Synonyms

  • use up
    2.7 4.16/m
    use up means to finish a supply completely by using it, while consume is a general word for using resources.
  • exhaust
    3.7 4.43/m
    exhaust uses all of a resource until nothing remains, while consume is a broader term for using supplies.
  • deplete
    6.3 1.51/m
    deplete reduces a supply until little or none is left, while consume simply means to use resources.
  • run through
    4.4 0.78/m
    run through uses up resources quickly, often in a wasteful way, while consume does not imply speed.
  • drain resources
    4.6 0.09/m
    drain resources means steadily reduce money or assets over time, while consume is a general word for using supplies.
  • drain
    3.4 9.92/m
    drain gradually uses up energy, strength, or money, while consume focuses simply on using or expending resources.
  • eat up
    3.1 1.42/m
    eat up quickly uses or wastes a supply, often completely, while consume is a neutral, general term for using resources.
  • eat
    6.6 20/m
    eat often describes literal food use and sometimes means to reduce resources, while consume is the standard formal word for using supplies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conserve - protect and prevent the waste or overuse of a resource
  • synonym: deplete - reduce the quantity or amount of something, especially resources
  • synonym: expend - use up or spend a resource or material
