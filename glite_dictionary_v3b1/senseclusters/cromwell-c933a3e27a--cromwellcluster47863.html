Cromwell

proper noun
English general and ruler, 1599–1658
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑmˌwɛl / (AmE), / ˈkrɒmwəl / (BrE)
Oliver Cromwell (1599–1658), English general and statesman who led the parliamentary forces during the English Civil War and later ruled as Lord Protector.

Examples

Cromwell as Lord Protector
Cromwell became Lord Protector of England following the execution of King Charles I.
During the 1640s, Cromwell played a decisive role in the outcome of the English Civil War.
Some historians view Cromwell as a defender of parliamentary democracy, while others criticize his rule.
