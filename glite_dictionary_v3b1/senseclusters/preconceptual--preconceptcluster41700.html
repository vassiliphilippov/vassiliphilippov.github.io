preconceptual

adjective
before concepts form
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkənˈsɛptʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsɛptjʊəl / (BrE)
existing or occurring before the development of concepts or ideas in the mind

Examples

Preconceptual thinking
In early childhood, preconceptual thinking often relies on imagination rather than logic.
The study focused on the preconceptual stage of language development in toddlers.
Some animals display preconceptual behaviors before they learn to categorize objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceptual - relating to or based on mental concepts
  • synonym: prelogical - relating to a stage before logical thinking develops
