reed

noun
vibrating strip in wind instruments
6.0 0.6/m
Pronunciation: / rid / (AmE), / riÀêd / (BrE)
a thin strip of stiff material that vibrates to produce a tone in some wind instruments when air is blown across it

Examples

A reed for saxophone
Many saxophonists carry spare reeds to replace broken ones during performances.
She soaked the reed in water to make it more flexible for her oboe.
The clarinetist fitted a new reed onto his mouthpiece before the concert.
