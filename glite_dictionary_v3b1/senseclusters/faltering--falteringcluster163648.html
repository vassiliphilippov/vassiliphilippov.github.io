faltering

noun
pause or uncertainty, esp. in speech
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔltɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltərɪŋ / (BrE)
A pause or moment of uncertainty, especially in speech or action.

Examples

A faltering in speech
A brief faltering interrupted the otherwise smooth speech.
His faltering during the presentation revealed his nervousness.
There was a noticeable faltering in her voice as she shared the news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confidence - the quality of being certain or assured
  • synonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or delaying due to uncertainty or indecision
