finger hole

noun
hole on a woodwind instrument for pitch control
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪŋɡɚ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə həʊl / (BrE)
A hole in a woodwind instrument that is covered or uncovered by the player's finger to change the pitch.

Examples

Finger hole on a flute
Each finger hole on the flute changes the sound when it is opened or closed.
He learned to cover the finger holes properly to produce a clear melody on his recorder.
The clarinet has several finger holes that must be covered to play different notes.
