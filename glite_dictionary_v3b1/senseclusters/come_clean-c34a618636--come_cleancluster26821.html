come clean

verb
confess or admit the truth
4.1 2.46/m
Pronunciation: / k åm klin / (AmE), / k åm kliÀên / (BrE)
to confess or tell the truth about something previously hidden or kept secret

Examples

To come clean about mistakes
After weeks of denial, he finally decided to come clean about the incident.
It's always better to come clean than to keep lying and get into trouble.
She knew she had to come clean with her parents about breaking the vase.

Synonyms

  • confess
    3.0 21/m
    confess means formally admit a wrong or crime, while come clean is a more informal, general way to admit a secret.
  • make a clean breast of
    7.5 0.04/m
    make a clean breast of means fully reveal an embarrassing fault in speech, while come clean is more common in casual talk.
  • own up
    4.4 1.66/m
    own up means admit you did something wrong, often to someone affected, while come clean suggests a fuller, honest confession.
  • fess up
    8.0 0.91/m
    fess up is a very informal request to admit a fault or secret, while come clean is more neutral everyday language.
  • tell the truth
    2.7 20/m
    tell the truth means speak honestly about facts in general, while come clean means confess a previously hidden secret or fault.

Related Terms

  • synonym: come out - to reveal the truth or make something known
  • synonym: confess - to admit to something, especially a wrongdoing or secret
  • antonym: cover up - to hide the truth or keep something secret
