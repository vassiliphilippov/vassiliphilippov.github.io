coelom

noun
body cavity in animals (biology)
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.ləm/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.ləm/ (BrE)
the main body cavity in most multicellular animals, located within the mesoderm; in embryos it develops and later divides into major cavities such as the pleural, pericardial, and peritoneal cavities in humans

Examples

A coelom cavity
A true coelom distinguishes coelomates from other groups of animals.
In embryonic development, the human coelom forms several separate cavities.
The coelom allows internal organs to develop independently from the body wall.
During embryonic development, the celom forms within the mesoderm and later divides into several cavities.
Scientists study the formation of the celom to understand congenital disorders affecting body cavities.
The peritoneal cavity in adults can be traced back to the original celom of the embryo.
