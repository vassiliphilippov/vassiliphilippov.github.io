Ptolemy I

proper noun
founder of Macedonian dynasty in Egypt
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːlɪmi ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ˈtɒləmi ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
King of Egypt (circa 367–285 BC) who founded the Macedonian dynasty and was a general and friend of Alexander the Great.

Examples

Ptolemy I king Egypt
Ptolemy I seized control of Egypt following the death of Alexander the Great.
The dynasty established by Ptolemy I lasted nearly three centuries.
Under Ptolemy I, Alexandria became a prominent center of culture and learning.
