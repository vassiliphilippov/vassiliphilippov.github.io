Ayyubid

proper noun
Muslim dynasty, 12th century
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈjuː.bɪd/ /; / /aɪ.ˈjuː.bɪd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈjuː.bɪd/ /; / /aɪ.ˈjuː.bɪd/ (BrE)
the Muslim dynasty founded by Saladin that ruled Egypt, Syria, and parts of the Levant in the 12th and 13th centuries

Examples

The Ayyubid dynasty
The Ayyubid dynasty was established by Saladin after the fall of the Fatimids.
Many impressive fortresses in Syria were built during the Ayyubid period.
Historians study the Ayyubid rule to understand the politics of medieval Egypt and the Levant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Fatimid - the Muslim dynasty that ruled Egypt before the Ayyubids
  • antonym: Mamluk - the dynasty that succeeded the Ayyubids in Egypt
