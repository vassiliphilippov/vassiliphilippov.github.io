sordid

adjective
meanly avaricious; greedy for money
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrdɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɔːdɪd / (BrE)
Showing an excessive and selfish desire for money or possessions; meanly avaricious or mercenary.

Examples

Sordid greed
His sordid obsession with wealth alienated him from his family and friends.
The charity was meant to help, but some had sordid motives and misused the funds.
They were caught up in a sordid scheme to exploit the city's poorest residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avaricious - having or showing an extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • antonym: generous - showing kindness and willingness to give more than is usual or expected
  • synonym: mercenary - primarily concerned with making money at the expense of ethics
