tutorship

noun
private individual teaching; position of tutor
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːtərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtəʃɪp / (BrE)
the practice or position of teaching pupils individually, especially by a tutor hired privately

Examples

Private tutorship
After a year of tutorship, the student’s grades improved dramatically.
His career began with a tutorship at a private language school.
She accepted the tutorship of several children preparing for university entrance exams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tutoring - the act of instructing or teaching one-on-one
