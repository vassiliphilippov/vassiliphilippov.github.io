sorghum

noun
sweet syrup from sorghum juice
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrɡəm / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəm / (BrE)
A sweet syrup obtained from the juice of the sweet sorghum plant, used as a sweetener or in cooking.

Examples

Sorghum syrup bottle
Farmers harvested the sweet sorghum to make syrup for local markets.
Many southern recipes use sorghum as a natural sweetener instead of refined sugar.
The chef drizzled sorghum over the pancakes for an earthy, rich flavor.
