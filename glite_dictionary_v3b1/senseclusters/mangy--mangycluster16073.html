mangy

adjective
shabby or threadbare (object)
6.4 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈmeɪndʒi / (BrE)
dirty, shabby, or in poor condition, especially in appearance (like something worn out or an animal with mange)

Examples

A mangy old blanket
He wrapped himself in a mangy blanket that barely kept him warm.
The old sofa looked mangy after years of use and neglect.
They replaced the mangy rug in the hallway with something new and clean.
He wore a mangy coat that barely kept out the cold.
The once-beautiful rug now looks mangy after years in the hallway.
We replaced the mangy armchair with something much newer.
The stray dog looked mangy and desperately needed a bath.
He wore a mangy coat that was full of holes and stains.
After months without care, the old sofa became mangy and uninviting.
