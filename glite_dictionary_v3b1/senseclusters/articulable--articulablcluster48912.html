articulable

adjective
able to be expressed
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪkjələbəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊləbəl / (BrE)
able to be clearly expressed or put into words

Examples

Articulable reason
The judge asked if the lawyer had an articulable reason for the objection.
His feelings about the situation were not easily articulable in a single sentence.
Only concerns that are articulable can be addressed by the committee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expressible - able to be expressed in words or shown
  • antonym: inexpressible - impossible to express in words
