chromaticism

noun
use of non-key notes
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmætɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / krəʊˈmætɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the use of notes outside the main key in music to create color, tension, or expressive effect

Examples

Musical chromaticism
The pianist's use of chromaticism added emotional depth to the performance.
Modern composers often experiment with chromaticism to create new musical textures.
Students studied how chromaticism can increase tension in a classical piece.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: atonality - music that lacks a key or tonal center
  • degree lower: tonality - music based on a key or tonal center
