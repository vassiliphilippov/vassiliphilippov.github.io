sounding board

noun
person for testing ideas or reactions
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a person whose opinions or reactions are used to test or develop ideas before taking further action

Examples

A sounding board for ideas
It's helpful to have a sounding board when making difficult decisions.
She acted as a sounding board for his proposals before he presented them to the team.
Whenever I have a new business idea, I use my brother as a sounding board.
