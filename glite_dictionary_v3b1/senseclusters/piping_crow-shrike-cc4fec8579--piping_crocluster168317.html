piping crow-shrike

noun
Australian black-and-white bird, a good mimic
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪpɪŋ kroʊ ʃraɪk / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ krəʊ ʃraɪk / (BrE)
a black-and-white bird of Australia, about the size of a crow, known for its melodious call and ability to mimic sounds

Examples

A piping crow-shrike bird
A piping crow-shrike was spotted near the edge of the forest, singing beautifully early in the morning.
Many bird enthusiasts keep a piping crow-shrike in captivity because of its pleasant song.
The piping crow-shrike is often admired for its remarkable ability to imitate other birds' calls.

Related Terms
