bioelectricity

noun
electrical phenomena in living organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˌɪlɛkˈtrɪs.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.ɪlɛkˈtrɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the electrical phenomena or processes that occur in living organisms, such as animals or plants

Examples

Bioelectricity in muscles
Muscles generate bioelectricity that allows movement.
Scientists studied the bioelectricity present in a frog's nervous system.
The field of bioelectricity explores how electric signals control animal and plant functions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electricity - a form of energy resulting from the existence of charged particles
