lambkill

noun
a poisonous North American shrub (Kalmia angustifolia)
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlæmˌkɪl / (AmE), / ˈlæmˌkɪl / (BrE)
A low, evergreen shrub of North America (Kalmia angustifolia) with narrow leaves and small red flowers, poisonous to young animals such as sheep.

Examples

The lambkill shrub
Hikers admired the bright red blossoms of lambkill along the woodland trail.
Livestock losses increased until the owners discovered lambkill growing among the grazing areas.
The farmer warned us that lambkill grows near the edge of the pasture and can harm young sheep.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sheep laurel - alternative name for lambkill, also Kalmia angustifolia
