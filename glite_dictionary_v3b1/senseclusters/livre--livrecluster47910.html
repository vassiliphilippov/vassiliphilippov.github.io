livre

noun
old French currency; French 'book'
9.9 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliÀêvr…ô / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêvr…ô / (BrE)
a former French unit of currency used before the franc

Examples

Three livres for a book
He paid three livres for the rare manuscript at the auction.
The livre was replaced by the franc as France's official currency in the 18th century.
In French, the word livre means 'book', which can be confusing for English speakers.
In 1780, a loaf of bread cost just a few livres in Paris.
The merchant counted out fifty livres to pay for the shipment.
Many historical documents mention prices in livres rather than francs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: book - a set of written or printed pages bound together
  • antonym: franc - the currency that replaced the livre in France
  • synonym: pound - a unit of currency in the UK, similar in origin to the livre
