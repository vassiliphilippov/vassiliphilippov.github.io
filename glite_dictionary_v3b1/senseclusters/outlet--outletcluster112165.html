outlet

noun
electrical socket or receptacle
2.7 5.19/m

A socket or receptacle in a wiring system that provides a place where electrical devices can be connected to receive current.

Examples

Electrical outlet
Every hotel room should have at least one electrical outlet near the bed.
The lamp won't turn on unless you plug it into an outlet.
We installed a new outlet in the kitchen for our coffee maker.

Synonyms

  • electrical outlet
    3.0 0.51/m
    electrical outlet is the common wall point where you plug devices, while outlet refers to any wiring receptacle providing electric current.
  • receptacle
    7.1 0.38/m
    receptacle is the technical term used in manuals and wiring, while outlet is the everyday word people use for a power socket.
  • wall socket
    2.9 0.20/m
    wall socket sits built into a wall and holds a plug securely, while outlet is the broader term for any power receptacle in wiring.
  • socket
    2.8 1.70/m
    socket can mean a fitting for electronic parts or a plug, while outlet means the specific power point for household devices.
