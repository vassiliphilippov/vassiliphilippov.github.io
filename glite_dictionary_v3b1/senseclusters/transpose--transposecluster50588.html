transpose

verb
transfer to another context or time
9.5
Pronunciation: / trænˈspoʊz / (AmE), / trænˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to transfer something to a different place, context, or period

Examples

To transpose an idea
Historians often transpose past events to present situations to draw parallels.
The director transposed Shakespeare's play to 1920s Chicago.
The novel skillfully transposes a medieval tale to a modern urban setting.
