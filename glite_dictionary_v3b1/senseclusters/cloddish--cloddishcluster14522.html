cloddish

adjective
awkward or stupid in manner
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈklɒdɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) awkward, stupid, or lacking in intelligence or refinement.

Examples

A cloddish person
Despite his cloddish demeanor, he was surprisingly kind-hearted.
His cloddish behavior embarrassed everyone at the dinner party.
She found his cloddish jokes more irritating than amusing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loutish - uncouth and aggressive in manner
  • synonym: oafish - clumsy, stupid, or uncultured in manner
  • antonym: refined - showing or having good taste and manners; elegant
