trollop

noun
disparaging term for an untidy or promiscuous woman
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɑː.ləp/ (AmE), /ˈtrɒ.ləp/ (BrE)
a woman who is insulted as being dirty, untidy, or sexually promiscuous (derogatory)

Examples

A trollop woman
He dismissed her as a trollop because of her unkempt appearance.
She was unfairly labeled a trollop by the gossips in her village.
The novel's villain referred to the servant girl as a mere trollop.
He unfairly called her a trollop for simply enjoying herself at the party.
In Victorian literature, the word trollop was often used to shame independent women.
The novel depicts her as a trollop who scandalizes the conservative town.
In old literature, the term trollop often describes women rejected by society for their relationships.
She was cruelly called a trollop by the townsfolk after the scandal.
The tabloid unfairly portrayed her as a trollop, causing a public outcry.
