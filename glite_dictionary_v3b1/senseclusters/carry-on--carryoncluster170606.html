carry-on

noun
bag or luggage taken onto an airplane
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæri ɒn / (BrE)
A small piece of luggage that a passenger is allowed to take onto an airplane, rather than checking it in.

Examples

A carry-on bag
He forgot his laptop in his carry-on during security screening.
She placed her carry-on in the overhead compartment before sitting down.
The airline limits the size and weight of each carry-on.

Related Terms

  • antonym: checked bag - luggage that is stored in the aircraft's hold
