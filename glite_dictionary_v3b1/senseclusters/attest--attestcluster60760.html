attest

verb
provide cryptographic proof of system state
7.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtest/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtest/ (BrE)
(in computing and information security) to produce or provide cryptographic evidence—such as a signed measurement, signature, or proof—that a device's state, identity, or a claim about a system is true, for the purpose of remote verification.

Examples

To attest device security
The TPM chip can attest that the boot process loaded only trusted software.
Security researchers attested the device's firmware integrity by verifying its signed measurements.
The protocol is attesting the server's configuration with a cryptographic signature for remote verification.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authenticate - to verify the identity or validity of something, often using cryptographic methods
  • synonym: verify - to check or confirm the truth or accuracy of information or a claim
