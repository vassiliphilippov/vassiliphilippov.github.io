Synonyms

  • eternally
    5.2 3.06/m
    eternally describes lasting without end in a formal or poetic way, while forever is the common everyday word for all time.
  • forevermore
    6.8 0.29/m
    forevermore sounds old-fashioned or poetic and often points from now on, while forever is neutral everyday speech for all time.
  • in perpetuity
    7.8 1.11/m
    in perpetuity appears in formal or legal writing for an unlimited period, while forever is used in general speech.
  • endlessly
    2.3 6.37/m
    endlessly suggests continuous action with no stop and often a vivid image, while forever is a broader time idea.
  • perpetually
    4.8 3.49/m
    perpetually describes repeated or constant action, often annoying or temporary, while forever means for all time without end.
