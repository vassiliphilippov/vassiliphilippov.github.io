forever

adverb
for a limitless time; always
1.0
Pronunciation: / fərˈɛvər /; / fəˈrɛvɚ / (AmE), / fəˈrɛvə / (BrE)
for all time or for a very long (seemingly endless) time

Examples

Love you forever
I will love you forever, no matter what happens.
Some people believe that diamonds last forever.
The mountain seemed as if it had stood there forever.
If you leave metal outside, it won't last forever without rusting.
It felt like we waited forever for our food to arrive at the restaurant.
The movie seemed to last forever, and I was glad when it finally ended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eternally - In a manner that lasts forever; for all time.
  • antonym: never - At no time; not ever (opposite of forever in some contexts).
