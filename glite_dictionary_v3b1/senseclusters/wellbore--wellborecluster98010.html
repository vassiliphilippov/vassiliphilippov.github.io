wellbore

noun
drilled extraction hole
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛlˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌbɔː / (BrE)
a drilled hole in the ground used to extract oil, gas, or water from underground

Examples

Oil wellbore
The drilling team inspected the wellbore to ensure it was stable before continuing operations.
Mud was circulated through the wellbore to control pressure during the extraction process.
Accurate measurements of the wellbore are essential for safe oil and gas production.
The engineers measured the diameter of the wellbore before installing the casing.
Mud circulation is important to keep the wellbore stable during drilling operations.
A collapsed wellbore can cause serious delays in oil and gas production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: borehole - a deep, narrow hole drilled into the ground
  • antonym: casing - a metal pipe used to line the inside of a wellbore
  • synonym: well - a shaft dug or drilled to obtain water, oil, or gas
