indorser

noun
person who transfers interest by signing a negotiable instrument
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsə / (BrE)
a person who signs a check or negotiable instrument to transfer their ownership interest to another.

Examples

Indorser of a check
As an indorser, she transferred the title of the bond to the new owner.
Banks often require the indorser to provide identification to process the transfer.
The indorser of the check must sign the back before it can be deposited.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endorser - a person who endorses a check or document
