tug

verb
carry or drag with difficulty
3.7 1.0
Pronunciation: / tʌɡ / (AmE), / tʌɡ / (BrE)
to pull something hard, suddenly, or repeatedly, often with effort

Examples

To tug a rope
After shopping, he tugged the heavy bags all the way home.
She had to tug her suitcase up the steep stairs.
They tugged the broken bicycle through the mud.
He tugged his coat closed against the cold wind.
She had to tug the drawer several times before it opened.
The fisherman tugged on the line when he felt a bite.
He kept tugging at his sleeve, trying to get her attention.
She tugged the suitcase up the stairs, step by step.
The little boy had to tug hard at the door to open it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haul - to pull or drag something with effort

  • antonym: push - to exert force on something to move it away from oneself
  • synonym: struggle - to make forceful or violent efforts to get free or achieve something
  • synonym: wrestle - to struggle with an opponent by grappling
