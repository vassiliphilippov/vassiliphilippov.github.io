stoop

verb
lower oneself morally or act dishonorably
8.1 0.70/m
Pronunciation: / stuÀêp / (AmE), / stuÀêp / (BrE)
to lower oneself by doing something considered morally wrong or undignified; to act beneath one's own standards of honor or dignity

Examples

To stoop to cheating
I refuse to stoop to their level, even if they insult me.
She wouldn't stoop to cheating just to win the contest.
They expected him to stoop so low, but he maintained his integrity.

Synonyms

  • stoop to
    7.7 0.81/m
    stoop to is used when someone lowers themselves to do something shameful or desperate, while stoop means acting beneath one's dignity.
  • lower oneself
    6.5 0.14/m
    lower oneself is a direct phrase about losing respect by bad actions, while stoop describes acting beneath one's dignity.
  • deign
    9.5 0.41/m
    deign often shows reluctant action from a proud person, while stoop focuses on lowering oneself by shameful behavior.
  • condescend
    8.1 0.16/m
    condescend implies a patronizing, superior attitude when helping, while stoop means simply acting beneath one's moral standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debase oneself - to lower one's moral standards or dignity
  • antonym: rise above - to refuse to be affected by or lower oneself
  • synonym: sink - to lower oneself in conduct or quality
