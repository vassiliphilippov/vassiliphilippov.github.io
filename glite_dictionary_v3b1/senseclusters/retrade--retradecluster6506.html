retrade

verb
to renegotiate terms
8.7
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtreɪd/ /; / /riː.ˈtreɪd/ /; / /rə.ˈtreɪd/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtreɪd/ /; / /riː.ˈtreɪd/ /; / /rə.ˈtreɪd/ (BrE)
to attempt to change the terms or price of an agreement after it has already been accepted, usually to gain a better deal

Examples

To retrade a contract
The buyer tried to retrade the deal after discovering a cheaper supplier.
It is considered unprofessional to retrade once both parties have agreed to the contract.
After months of negotiation, the company refused to retrade the agreed terms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honor - to keep or fulfill an agreement
  • synonym: negotiate - to discuss in order to reach an agreement
  • synonym: renege - to go back on a promise or agreement
