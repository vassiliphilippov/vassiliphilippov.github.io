fusariosis

noun
Fusarium fungal infection
Pronunciation: / ˌfjuːzəriˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfjuːzəriˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease or infection in humans or animals caused by fungi of the genus Fusarium, often affecting the skin, eyes, or nails

Examples

Human fusariosis
The doctor diagnosed the patient with fusariosis after noticing lesions on his skin.
People with weakened immune systems are at higher risk of developing fusariosis.
Treatment for fusariosis often requires strong antifungal medications and careful monitoring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: candidiasis - a fungal infection caused by Candida species
  • synonym: mycosis - a disease caused by a fungal infection
