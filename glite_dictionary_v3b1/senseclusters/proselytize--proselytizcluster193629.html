proselytize

verb
attempt to convert someone's religion
9.1 1.41/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀês…ôl…ôÀåta…™z /; / Ààpr…ëÀês.…ô.l…™.ta…™z / (AmE), / Ààpr…ís…™l…™Àåta…™z /; / Ààpr…ís.…™.l…™.ta…™z / (BrE)
to try to persuade someone to change their religious beliefs or to join another religion

Examples

To proselytize new members
He openly admitted that he wanted to proselytize those who had never attended church.
Some missionaries travel overseas to proselytize and share their religious beliefs with others.
The group was accused of trying to proselytize students on campus.
He was criticized for trying to proselytise his colleagues at work.
Some missionaries travel to remote areas specifically to proselytise among indigenous communities.
The organization does not proselytise, but offers help to anyone in need.

Synonyms

  • evangelize
    6.9 0.29/m
    evangelize means trying to spread religious beliefs warmly to others, while proselytize stresses active effort to change someone's faith.
  • convert
    4.5 9.13/m
    convert can mean changing someone's beliefs or becoming a believer, while proselytize focuses on trying to persuade people into a religion.
  • missionize
    8.6 0.02/m
    missionize means trying to gain support for an idea or cause, often political or secular, while proselytize refers to religious conversion attempts.
