overstrike

verb
to type over characters
7.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈstraɪk/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈstraɪk/ (BrE)
to print, type, or stamp a new character or design directly on top of something that has already been printed or stamped

Examples

To overstrike a letter
On early computers, you could overstrike a hyphen and a vertical bar to make a plus sign.
The typist had to overstrike certain letters to create accented characters.
Some old printers allowed users to overstrike text for special formatting.
The mint decided to overstrike the old coins with a new design.
To correct the error, the printer had to overstrike the original text with a new line.
Collectors value coins that have been overstruck because of their unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erase - to remove marks or writing
  • synonym: overwrite - to write or type over existing text, replacing it
  • synonym: reprint - to print again or with changes
