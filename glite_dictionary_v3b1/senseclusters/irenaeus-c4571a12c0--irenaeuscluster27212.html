Irenaeus

proper noun
Greek theologian and bishop, c. 130–200
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌriːneɪˈəs / (AmE), / ɪˌriːniˈeɪəs / (BrE)
(circa 130–200) A Greek theologian, bishop of Lyons, known for his writings against heresies, recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.

Examples

Irenaeus the theologian
Irenaeus played a crucial role in shaping early Christian theology through his arguments against heresies.
Many scholars consider Irenaeus a key figure in the development of Christian doctrine.
The writings of Irenaeus provide valuable insights into the beliefs of the early Church.
