self-sealing

adjective
able to seal itself automatically
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an object or material) able to seal itself automatically, especially after being pierced or opened

Examples

Self-sealing containers
Our lab uses self-sealing containers to prevent chemical leaks.
The self-sealing bags kept the snacks fresh during the long hike.
These self-sealing envelopes make mailing quicker and easier.
If you drive often on rough roads, consider installing self-sealing tires on your car.
Modern self-sealing fuel tanks greatly reduce the risk of leaks after damage.
This self-sealing envelope saves time by eliminating the need for glue or tape.
