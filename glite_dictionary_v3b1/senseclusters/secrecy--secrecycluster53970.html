secrecy

noun
state of being hidden or confidential
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.krə.siː/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.krə.siː/ (BrE)
the state or quality of keeping something hidden or private; the act of not telling or revealing something

Examples

Maintain secrecy
He valued his privacy and maintained secrecy about his personal life.
The negotiations were conducted in complete secrecy to avoid public scrutiny.
The organization's work is shrouded in secrecy, making it difficult to obtain information.
Companies often operate with a certain level of secrecy about new products.
He admired her ability to maintain secrecy even under pressure.
The negotiations were conducted in complete secrecy to avoid media attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: openness - the quality of not hiding secrets, being transparent
