inertial mass

noun
property of a body determining acceleration under force
7.5 6.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈɜːr.ʃəl mæs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈɜː.ʃəl mæs/ (BrE)
(physics) The property of a body that determines the acceleration it experiences when subjected to a force, excluding gravitational force; measured by the ratio of applied force to acceleration.

Examples

Inertial mass property
Her research focused on comparing inertial mass and gravitational mass in different materials.
In physics experiments, scientists measure the inertial mass of objects to study how they respond to forces.
The concept of inertial mass is fundamental to Newton’s second law of motion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gravitational mass - the mass of a body as measured by its gravitational interaction
