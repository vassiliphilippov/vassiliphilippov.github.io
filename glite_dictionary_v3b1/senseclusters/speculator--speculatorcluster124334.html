speculator

noun
one who guesses without sufficient evidence
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a person who forms theories or conjectures without sufficient evidence or knowledge of the facts

Examples

An armchair speculator
As a speculator, she often drew conclusions that later proved completely wrong.
The speculator offered a wild theory about the missing painting without any real evidence.
You can't trust every speculator you hear on the radio; some just love to stir up rumors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analyst - a person who examines or studies something in detail
  • synonym: guesser - a person who makes guesses
