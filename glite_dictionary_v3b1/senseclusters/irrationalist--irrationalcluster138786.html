irrationalist

noun
person acting without reason
Pronunciation: / ɪˈræʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who does not prioritize reason or logic, either in belief or behavior

Examples

An irrationalist person
Despite all the evidence, he remained an irrationalist and refused to change his mind.
The debate was difficult because one participant was an outspoken irrationalist.
Her friends worried that she was becoming an irrationalist after making several impulsive decisions.
The philosopher was labeled an irrationalist because he valued emotion over logic.
Some critics argue that the artist's work reflects an irrationalist approach to understanding the world.
In debates, an irrationalist might question the limits of logical reasoning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illogical - not based on clear reasoning or logic
  • antonym: rationalist - a person who bases actions or beliefs on reason and logic
