isobath

noun
line of equal depth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌbæθ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəˌbɑːθ /; / ˈaɪsəʊˌbæθ / (BrE)
a line on a map or chart that connects points of equal depth below the surface of a body of water

Examples

The isobath line
The isobath on the nautical chart helps sailors avoid shallow areas.
Scientists used the 200-meter isobath to study changes in the ocean floor.
Each isobath marks a different depth, making the map easier to interpret.
The marine chart uses isobaths to show the varying depths of the ocean floor.
Divers often follow isobaths to maintain a constant depth during underwater exploration.
The scientist drew several isobaths to illustrate the shape of the lakebed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contour line - a line on a map joining points of equal elevation
  • synonym: isobar - a line on a map connecting points of equal atmospheric pressure
  • synonym: isotherm - a line on a map connecting points of equal temperature
