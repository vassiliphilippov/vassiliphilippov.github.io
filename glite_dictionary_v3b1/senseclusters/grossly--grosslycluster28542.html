In a way that is extreme, flagrant, or obviously wrong; to a very great or serious degree.

Examples

To grossly exaggerate
Her behavior was grossly inappropriate at the meeting.
That estimate is grossly exaggerated and not supported by the evidence.
The judge found the company's actions to be grossly negligent.

Synonyms

  • egregiously
    8.2 0.47/m
    egregiously describes acts that are very bad and shocking, while grossly emphasizes extreme or seriously wrong degree.
  • flagrantly
    7.4 0.39/m
    flagrantly shows open, immoral or offensive behavior, while grossly focuses on something extreme or seriously wrong in degree.
  • blatantly
    4.8 3.57/m
    blatantly means very obvious and unashamed, often about behavior, while grossly stresses great or severe wrongness.
  • outrageously
    5.8 1.20/m
    outrageously is used for shocking or extreme acts that anger people, while grossly highlights being very wrong or serious.
