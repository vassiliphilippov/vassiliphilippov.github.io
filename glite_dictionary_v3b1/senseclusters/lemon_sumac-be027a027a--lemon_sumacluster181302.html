lemon sumac

noun
a sweet-scented sumac shrub of eastern North America
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛmən ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
A species of sumac native to eastern North America, noted for its sweet scent, ternate leaves, yellowish-green catkin-like flowers, and clusters of red hairy fruit.

Examples

lemon sumac plant
Botanists studied the unique catkin-like flowers of the lemon sumac.
Clusters of red hairy fruits on the lemon sumac attract birds throughout the autumn.
The lemon sumac in our garden filled the air with its sweet fragrance every spring.
