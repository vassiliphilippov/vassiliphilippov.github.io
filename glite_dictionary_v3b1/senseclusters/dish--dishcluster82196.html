dish

noun
parabolic antenna for signals
7.1 5.3
Pronunciation: /dɪʃ/ (AmE), /dɪʃ/ (BrE)
A parabolic antenna, typically used for receiving or transmitting signals such as satellite television or radio, consisting of a curved reflector that focuses incoming or outgoing waves.

Examples

A satellite dish
A large dish can pick up weak signals from distant satellites.
Many rural homes use a dish to access television channels that aren't available by cable.
The technician climbed onto the roof to adjust the dish for better reception.
