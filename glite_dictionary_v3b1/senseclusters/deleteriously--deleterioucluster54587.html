Examples

Synonyms

  • detrimentally
    7.2 0.08/m
    detrimentally is used in formal contexts to say something causes harm or loss, while deleteriously means causing harm or damage.
  • harmfully
    4.3 0.06/m
    harmfully is a common, simple adverb for causing harm, while deleteriously is more formal and often used in writing.
  • damagingly
    4.4 0.01/m
    damagingly describes clear physical or reputational damage, while deleteriously covers harm or damage in a broader, formal way.
  • adversely
    4.9 3.61/m
    adversely often describes a negative effect on results or health, while deleteriously focuses on causing actual harm or damage.
  • injuriously
    7.6 0.02/m
    injuriously sounds formal and legal and means causing injury or harm, while deleteriously is more common in scientific writing.
  • perniciously
    8.9 0.11/m
    perniciously means causing harm slowly or secretly over time, while deleteriously means causing harm or damage more generally.
  • destructively
    4.2 0.24/m
    destructively often shows violent or total ruin, while deleteriously means causing harm or damage in a less violent, general way.
