hustle

verb
obtain by energetic effort or cleverness
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
to get or achieve something by energetic effort, persuasion, or sometimes dishonest or forceful means

Examples

To hustle tickets
He hustled tickets to the concert by convincing people they were about to sell out.
She managed to hustle a free lunch out of the manager by chatting with him.
You have to hustle if you want to get by in this city.
She knows how to hustle her friends into joining her at events.
The salesman tried to hustle me into buying an extended warranty.
You can't hustle me into making a quick decision.
He used to hustle counterfeit tickets outside the stadium.
She tried to hustle tourists into buying fake designer bags.
The con artist hustled money from unsuspecting strangers.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bully - to use intimidation or abuse to force someone to act
  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • degree lower: coax - to gently persuade someone to do something
  • synonym: deal - to sell or trade something, often used for illicit goods
  • synonym: scrounge - obtain something by seeking or begging, often informally
  • synonym: swindle - to obtain money or goods by deception
