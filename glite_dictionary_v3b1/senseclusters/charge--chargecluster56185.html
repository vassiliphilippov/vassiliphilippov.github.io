charge

noun
official accusation of a crime
6.0 52/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
an official accusation or statement of blame

Examples

A charge of theft
He was arrested on a charge of burglary after the investigation was completed.
Serious charges were brought against the company for environmental violations.
The defendant denied the charge and pleaded not guilty in court.
She vehemently denied the charge that she had cheated in the exam.
The mayor resigned following charges of corruption and mismanagement.
The police filed a charge of theft against the suspect.

Synonyms

  • accusation
    3.1 20/m
    an accusation is a general statement that someone did wrong, while a charge is an official legal claim of blame.
  • indictment
    7.8 10/m
    an indictment is a formal criminal accusation often issued by authorities, while a charge is any official statement of blame.
  • complaint
    5.3 21/m
    a complaint is a formal court filing that starts a case, while a charge is an official accusation of wrongdoing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accusation - a statement saying someone has done something wrong or illegal
  • antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they were charged
