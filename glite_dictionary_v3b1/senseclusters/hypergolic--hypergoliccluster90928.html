hypergolic

adjective
spontaneously igniting
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈɡɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈɡɒlɪk / (BrE)
describing fuels or propellants that ignite spontaneously when they come into contact with each other

Examples

Hypergolic fuel mixture
The engineers chose hypergolic fuels for the spacecraft to ensure reliable ignition in space.
Because hypergolic propellants ignite on contact, they are often used in rocket engines.
Handling hypergolic substances requires extreme caution due to their instant reactivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonhypergolic - not igniting spontaneously on contact
  • synonym: spontaneous - happening without external cause or planning
