epideictical

adjective
of rhetorical display or ceremony
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪkəl / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) intended primarily for rhetorical display or ceremonial purposes, rather than to inform or persuade.

Examples

Epideictical speech
Awards ceremonies often include epideictical addresses that celebrate achievement and community.
He delivered an epideictical oration praising the virtues of the retiring principal.
The professor analyzed several epideictical speeches from ancient Athens in her lecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epideictic - of or relating to rhetorical display or ceremonial speeches
