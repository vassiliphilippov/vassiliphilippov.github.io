shark

noun
an expert or aggressive, skillful person
3.6
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a person who is very skilled, aggressive, or dishonest, especially in business or gambling

Examples

A shark at cards
At the poker table, he was known as a real shark.
She quickly proved herself a shark at negotiating business deals.
You have to watch out for sharks in the real estate industry.
Everyone in the office knows he's a shark who will step on anyone to get ahead.
The world of high-stakes poker is full of sharks looking to take advantage of newcomers.
Watch out for business sharks who promise big profits with little risk.

  • synonym: fraudster - someone who commits fraud, especially for personal gain

  • antonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • synonym: swindler - a person who cheats others out of money or assets
