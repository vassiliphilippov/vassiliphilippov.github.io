untoward

adjective
improper; socially inappropriate
6.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɔːd / (BrE)
improper, inappropriate, or unfavorable; not as expected or causing trouble

Examples

An untoward event
Any untoward conduct at the formal dinner will not be tolerated.
His comments at the meeting were considered untoward and offended several participants.
The principal addressed the student's untoward behavior during the assembly.
Nothing untoward happened during their journey, despite the stormy weather.
She apologized if her remarks seemed untoward during the meeting.
The company assured customers that no untoward activity had been detected on their accounts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adverse - having a negative or unfavorable effect
  • antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards; proper
  • antonym: favorable - beneficial or advantageous
  • synonym: improper - not in keeping with accepted standards of behavior
