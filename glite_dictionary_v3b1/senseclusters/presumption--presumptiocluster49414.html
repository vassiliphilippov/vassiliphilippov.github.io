presumption

noun
arrogant or bold behavior
4.1 7.2
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (BrE)
audacious, arrogant, or disrespectful behavior; going beyond what is proper

Examples

His presumption shocked everyone
He was shocked by the presumption of the newcomer, who spoke as if he owned the place.
She had the presumption to ask for a raise after only a week on the job.
Their presumption in making such demands was met with disapproval from the group.
Her presumption in interrupting the meeting annoyed everyone in the room.
I was shocked by the presumption of his request, considering how little we knew each other.
There is a difference between confidence and presumption when dealing with authority figures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assumption - something taken for granted; also, presumptuousness
  • antonym: humility - modest or low view of one's importance
  • synonym: impertinence - lack of respect; rudeness
  • synonym: impudence - rude or disrespectful behavior
  • antonym: politeness - respectful or considerate behaviour
