induction

noun
cooking using electromagnetic induction
4.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (BrE)
a method of cooking that heats pots and pans directly by electromagnetic induction, often used to refer to induction cooktops or the cooking technique.

Examples

Induction cooktop
The chef switched to induction cooking for faster, cleaner heating.
Our new induction hob boils water almost instantly compared with the old gas stove.
They prefer induction because it cools the kitchen more and is easier to clean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic induction - the physical phenomenon (changing magnetic field producing electric current) used to generate heat in cookware
  • synonym: induction cooktop - a kitchen appliance that uses induction to heat cookware directly
