esophageal veins

noun
veins draining blood from the esophagus
5.6 6.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˌsɑfəˈdʒiəl veɪnz /; / iˌsɑfəˈdʒiəl veɪnz / (AmE), / ɪˌsɒfəˈdʒiːəl veɪnz /; / iˌsɒfəˈdʒiːəl veɪnz / (BrE)
the small veins that drain blood from the esophagus into the brachiocephalic vein or the azygos veins

Examples

Esophageal veins drain blood
Rupture of the esophageal veins may lead to severe bleeding.
Surgeons carefully avoided damaging the esophageal veins during the procedure.
The esophageal veins can become enlarged in patients with liver disease.
Surgeons must take care to avoid damaging the oesophageal veins during thoracic operations.
The oesophageal veins are important pathways for blood to return to the systemic circulation.
The oesophageal veins can become enlarged in certain liver diseases, leading to varices.
