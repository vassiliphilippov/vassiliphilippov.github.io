foreordination

noun
theological doctrine of predetermination
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåf…îÀêrÀå…îrd…ôÀàne…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåf…îÀêrÀå…îÀêd…™Ààne…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
(in theology) the act or state of being determined in advance, especially the doctrine that God has predetermined every event and outcome, including the salvation of souls.

Examples

The foreordination doctrine
Debates about foreordination have shaped much of Protestant theology.
She struggled to reconcile divine foreordination with the idea of free will.
The Calvinist doctrine of foreordination holds that every event is predestined by God.

Synonyms

  • predestination
    9.9 0.36/m
    predestination states that God already decides people's fates and salvation, while foreordination names that same theological idea.
  • preordination
    9.9 0.01/m
    preordination says God decides events before they happen for all time, while foreordination refers to this same theological decision.
  • predetermination
    9.7 0.09/m
    predetermination claims that events are fixed by divine will ahead of time, while foreordination focuses on God's predetermined outcomes.
  • predestinarianism
    9.9 0.04/m
    predestinarianism is the belief or movement that accepts divine choice over salvation, while foreordination names that doctrine more neutrally.
  • predestiny
    9.9 0.01/m
    predestiny means a fate set by a higher power or fate, while foreordination is the theological claim of God's specific control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events have been willed by God, often used interchangeably with foreordination
