guttural

adjective
produced in the throat; harsh, throaty sound
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌt.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a sound) harsh and produced in the throat, resembling the croak of a frog or the call of a crow.

Examples

Guttural voice
As night fell, we heard the guttural calls of frogs drifting from the pond.
Her laughter had a distinctly guttural quality, deep and raspy.
The actor's guttural shout echoed through the empty hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harsh - (of a sound) unpleasantly rough or jarring
  • synonym: throaty - (of a voice or sound) produced in or involving the throat
