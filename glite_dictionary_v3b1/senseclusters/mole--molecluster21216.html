mole

noun
breakwater structure extending into water
9.9
Pronunciation: / moʊl / (AmE), / məʊl / (BrE)
a massive structure of stone or concrete built out from the shore into the water to protect a coast, harbor, or beach from the force of waves

Examples

Harbor mole
A concrete mole was constructed to prevent further erosion of the coastline.
Fishing boats were moored safely behind the long harbor mole.
Workers reinforced the eastern mole after last winter's storms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breakwater - a structure built to protect a shore or harbor from the force of waves
