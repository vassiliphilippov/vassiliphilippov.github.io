paddle

verb
play or splash in shallow water
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpæd.əl /; / ˈpædəl / (AmE), / ˈpæd.əl /; / ˈpædəl / (BrE)
to play, walk, or move in shallow water, especially by splashing with hands or feet

Examples

To paddle in water
After lunch, the toddlers spent an hour paddling in the warm sea.
She took off her shoes and paddled along the edge of the lake.
The children loved to paddle in the shallow stream on hot summer days.
She paddled across the stream without getting completely wet.
The children paddled in the shallow end of the pool, laughing as they moved their arms and legs.
The puppy fell into the pond and began to paddle instinctively to stay afloat.
The children enjoyed paddling in the cool stream on a hot day.
We spent the afternoon paddling along the edge of the lake, laughing and splashing.
She took off her shoes and started paddling in the shallow water at the beach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dog-paddle - to swim by moving arms and legs like a dog
  • synonym: splash - to move or play energetically in water so as to send drops flying
  • degree higher: swim - to move through water by moving your body
  • synonym: wade - to walk with effort through water or another substance
