Houston, we have a problem

Pronunciation: / ˈhuːstən ˌwiː hæv ə ˈprɑːbləm / (AmE), / ˈhjuːstən ˌwiː hav ə ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
An expression used to indicate that a significant or unexpected problem has arisen.

Examples

Houston, we have a problem phrase
Houston, we have a problem; the servers are all down this morning.
Everyone was enjoying the event until someone spilled wine on the mayor—Houston, we have a problem.
When the project manager didn't show up, it was clear: Houston, we have a problem.
