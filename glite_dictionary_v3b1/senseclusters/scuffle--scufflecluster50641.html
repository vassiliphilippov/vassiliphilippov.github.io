scuffle

verb
to engage in a confused close fight
3.9 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈskʌf.əl/ (AmE), /ˈskʌf.əl/ (BrE)
to fight or struggle in a confused or disorderly way, usually at close quarters and without weapons

Examples

To scuffle over toys
Children sometimes scuffle on the playground over toys or games.
Security guards had to separate fans who scuffled after the match.
Two students began to scuffle in the hallway before a teacher intervened.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brawl - a noisy, rough fight, especially in a crowd (verb)
  • antonym: peace - a state of calm or lack of conflict
  • synonym: skirmish - to engage in a brief or minor fight (verb)
