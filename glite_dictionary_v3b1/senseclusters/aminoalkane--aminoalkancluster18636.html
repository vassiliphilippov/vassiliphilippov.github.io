aminoalkane

noun
organic compound; ammonia derivative with alkyl group(s)
8.2
Pronunciation: / əˌminoʊˈælˌkeɪn / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˈælkeɪn / (BrE)
A compound derived from ammonia by replacing one or more hydrogen atoms with univalent hydrocarbon radicals (alkyl groups).

Examples

A aminoalkane compound
Butylamine is a type of aminoalkane commonly used in organic synthesis.
Many aminoalkane compounds serve as precursors for pharmaceuticals and agrochemicals.
The simplest aminoalkane is methylamine, which has only one carbon atom.
