downcourt

noun
opponent's end of court
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌkɔːt / (BrE)
the area of a sports court toward the opponent's end, especially near the opponent's basket in basketball.

Examples

A downcourt pass
After grabbing the rebound, she sprinted downcourt to set up a fast break.
The team quickly moved the ball downcourt to catch the defense off guard.
He signaled his teammates to run downcourt as soon as they gained possession.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backcourt - the part of the court near a team's own basket in basketball
  • synonym: frontcourt - the part of the court near the opponent's basket in basketball
