mutualist

noun
supporter of mutualism
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtʃuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates mutualism, an economic or social theory promoting cooperative ownership and mutual aid

Examples

Mutualist thinker
The mutualist believed that communities should own resources together for everyone's benefit.
As a mutualist, he joined organizations that promoted cooperative businesses and shared profits.
Many mutualists advocate for systems where people help each other through reciprocal exchange.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cooperator - a person who works together with others for a common purpose
  • antonym: individualist - a person who values independence and self-reliance over group cooperation
