reflow

noun
soldering process
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.floʊ/ /; / /rə.ˈfloʊ/ (AmE), /ˈriː.fləʊ/ /; / /rə.ˈfləʊ/ (BrE)
the process of heating solder to melt it and create electrical connections between components and a circuit board

Examples

Reflow process
The technician monitored the reflow to ensure all connections were properly made on the circuit board.
A precise temperature profile is crucial during reflow to avoid damaging sensitive electronic components.
Modern electronics manufacturing relies heavily on automated reflow ovens for surface-mount assembly.
