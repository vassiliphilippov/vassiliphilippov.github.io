Sterculiaceae

noun
a former plant family in Malvales
9.9
Pronunciation: /stɜːr.kjuː.liː.ˈeɪ.siː/ /; / /stɜːr.kjʊl.ɪ.ˈeɪ.siː/ (AmE), /stɜː.kjuː.liː.ˈeɪ.siː/ /; / /stɜː.kjʊl.ɪ.ˈeɪ.siː/ (BrE)
a former family of flowering plants within the order Malvales, including trees and shrubs such as the cacao tree; now largely classified under Malvaceae.

Examples

The Sterculiaceae family
Many tropical trees were historically grouped under Sterculiaceae.
Recent taxonomic changes have reassigned most members of Sterculiaceae to the Malvaceae family.
The Sterculiaceae family once included the cacao tree before botanical classifications were revised.
