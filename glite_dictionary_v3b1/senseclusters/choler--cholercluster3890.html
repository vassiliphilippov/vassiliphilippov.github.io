choler

noun
anger or irritability
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
anger or a feeling of irritability; wrath (sometimes used in reference to old beliefs about bodily humors)

Examples

Sudden choler
A fit of choler overtook her, causing her to lash out unexpectedly.
He struggled to control his choler when confronted with the unfair decision.
His choler was evident in his sharp words and flushed face.
He could barely control his choler when confronted with the accusation.
Her choler rose quickly during the heated argument.
Literature from that era often describes heroes with uncontrollable choler.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness or tranquility, not angry
