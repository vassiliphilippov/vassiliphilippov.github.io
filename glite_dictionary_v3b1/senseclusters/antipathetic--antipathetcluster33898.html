antipathetic

adjective
strongly opposed or averse to
7.5 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛt.ɪk /; / ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛt.ɪk /; / ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
having or showing a strong dislike or opposition to someone or something

Examples

Antipathetic to change
He was antipathetic to any changes in the company’s traditional policies.
Her antipathetic attitude toward authority figures often led to conflict.
Many people remain antipathetic to genetically modified foods despite scientific assurances.
He always seemed antipathetic toward his colleague’s ideas, rarely offering support.
Many voters were antipathetic to the new tax proposal, fearing it would hurt the economy.
The two families remained antipathetic to each other for generations after the dispute.
