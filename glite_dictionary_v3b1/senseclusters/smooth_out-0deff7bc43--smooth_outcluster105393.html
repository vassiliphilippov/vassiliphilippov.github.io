smooth out

verb
reduce noise in data
4.2
Pronunciation: /smuːð aʊt/ (AmE), /smuːð aʊt/ (BrE)
to reduce random fluctuations, noise, or irregularities in data, a signal, or a graph by applying smoothing or filtering techniques so that the underlying trend becomes clearer.

Examples

To smooth out data
The analyst will smooth out the noise in the quarterly data to reveal the underlying trend.
During preprocessing she is smoothing out rapid fluctuations to make the graph easier to read.
They smoothed out the sales figures before presenting, removing daily spikes and random errors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denoise - to remove noise from data or a signal, typically using statistical or signal-processing methods.
  • synonym: filter - to remove unwanted parts (such as noise) from a signal or dataset, often using a mathematical process.
