polestar

noun
the North Star; brightest in Ursa Minor
6.8 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpo älÀåst…ër / (AmE), / Ààp…ô älÀåst…ëÀê / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor, known as the North Star, toward which the Earth's northern axis points

Examples

The polestar in sky
For centuries, sailors have navigated by the polestar to determine their course at night.
Many cultures have myths and legends about the significance of the polestar.
The polestar appears almost stationary in the sky while other stars rotate around it.

Synonyms

  • Polaris
    5.6 0.78/m
    Polaris is the specific proper name of the northern guiding star, while polestar is the general term for that star.
  • North Star
    3.5 0.51/m
    North Star is the common everyday name for the same guiding star, while polestar is a more formal descriptive term.
  • pole star
    5.6 0.03/m
    pole star is a descriptive astronomy name for the star near Earth's axis, while polestar refers to the same guiding star.
  • polar star
    4.6 0.02/m
    polar star is a less common variant name used in some texts for Polaris, while polestar is the general navigational term.
  • lodestar
    9.7 0.05/m
    lodestar means a guiding or metaphorical star used in speech, while polestar names the actual astronomical North Star.
