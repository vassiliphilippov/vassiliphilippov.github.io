Joshua

proper noun
successor of Moses in the Old Testament
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɑːʃuə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒʃuə / (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) The leader of the Israelites after Moses, who led them into the Promised Land and is known for the destruction of Jericho.

Examples

Leader Joshua of Israel
According to the Bible, Joshua led the Israelites after Moses' death.
Some parents name their sons Joshua after the biblical leader.
The story of Joshua and the battle of Jericho is taught in many Sunday schools.
