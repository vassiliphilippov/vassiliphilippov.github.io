allegorism

noun
allegorical interpretation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌɡɔːrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌɡɒrɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or doctrine of interpreting stories, events, or texts as allegories rather than as literal accounts

Examples

Allegorism in interpretation
The professor explained how allegorism influenced medieval interpretations of ancient texts.
Some readers prefer allegorism to find deeper meanings in classic literature.
Religious scholars often debate the merits of allegorism versus literal readings of sacred texts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegory - a story or image with a hidden meaning
  • antonym: literalism - the interpretation of words in their most basic sense
