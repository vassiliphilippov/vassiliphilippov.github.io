inconsistent

adjective
not able to be consistent or harmonious
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkənˈsɪstənt /; / ˌɪn.kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈsɪstənt /; / ˌɪn.kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
not staying the same, not in agreement, or lacking regularity, harmony, or logic

Examples

Inconsistent testimony
The witness gave inconsistent testimony throughout the trial.
Their plans were inconsistent with each other, leading to confusion.
You cannot base your argument on inconsistent data.
Her explanations for the mistake were inconsistent and raised many questions.
His inconsistent attendance at meetings made it difficult to rely on him.
The data collected from the experiment were inconsistent with previous research findings.
Her statements about the project were inconsistent, causing confusion among the team.
The results of the experiment were inconsistent with previous findings.
The witness gave inconsistent answers throughout the trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • antonym: consistent - acting or done in the same way over time; not self-contradictory
  • synonym: contradictory - mutually opposed or inconsistent
