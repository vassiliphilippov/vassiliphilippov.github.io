coagulate

verb
change a liquid to a thickened or solid state
Pronunciation: / koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to become thick or solid, or to become thick or solid, usually by a chemical process

Examples

To coagulate milk
Adding vinegar will coagulate the proteins in the cheese-making process.
Blood proteins coagulate to form a clot and stop the bleeding.
The milk began to coagulate within a few hours at room temperature.
After the injury, his blood began to coagulate and form a clot.
Proteins in the egg white coagulate as it cooks, turning the liquid clear to opaque.
The milk will coagulate if you add lemon juice to it.
