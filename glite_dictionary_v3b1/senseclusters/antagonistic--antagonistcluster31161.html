antagonistic

adjective
acting in opposition (esp. drugs or muscles)
3.2 6.1
Pronunciation: /æn.tæ.gə.ˈnɪ.stɪk/ (AmE), /æn.tæ.gə.ˈnɪ.stɪk/ (BrE)
(of drugs or muscles) counteracting or neutralizing each other's effects; acting in opposition to each other.

Examples

Antagonistic muscles
Flexor and extensor muscles can be antagonistic in their actions on a joint.
The combination of these drugs produces an antagonistic rather than a synergistic effect.
The two antibiotics are antagonistic when used together, reducing each other's effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synergistic - (of drugs or muscles) working together to produce a combined effect
