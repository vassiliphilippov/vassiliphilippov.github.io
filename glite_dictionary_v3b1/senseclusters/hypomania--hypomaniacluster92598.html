hypomania

noun
mild elevated mood
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
a mild state or period of unusually high energy, mood, and activity that is less severe than mania

Examples

Mild hypomania episode
People experiencing hypomania may feel unusually energetic and confident for several days.
Her doctor explained that hypomania can be a symptom of bipolar disorder.
During periods of hypomania, he often started new projects and talked rapidly.
After finishing the book, she entered a brief hypomania and painted murals all week.
His friends noticed his sudden hypomania as he enthusiastically started several new projects.
Periods of hypomania can sometimes lead to bursts of creativity and productivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depression - a state of low mood and loss of interest or pleasure
  • synonym: euphoria - a feeling or state of intense excitement and happiness
  • degree lower: euthymia - a normal, stable mood state
  • degree higher: mania - an abnormally elevated and intense mood state
