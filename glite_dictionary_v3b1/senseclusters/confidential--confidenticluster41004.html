confidential

adjective
entrusted with private information
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.fəˈdɛn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɪˈdɛn.ʃəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their job) entrusted with private or secret information from others; able to be trusted with confidential matters.

Examples

Confidential employee
All confidential employees signed a non-disclosure agreement before starting their roles.
As a confidential secretary, she handled many sensitive documents every day.
The company relied on him as a confidential advisor to the board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: private - not intended for or shared with others; secret
  • antonym: public - open to all or shared with others; not secret
