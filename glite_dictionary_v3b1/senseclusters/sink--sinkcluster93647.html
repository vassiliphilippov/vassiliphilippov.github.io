sink

verb
fall or drop heavily
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɪŋk / (AmE), / sɪŋk / (BrE)
to move, fall, or descend to a lower position, level, or state, either physically or emotionally

Examples

To sink into a chair
After hearing the bad news, I sink into my chair, feeling overwhelmed.
At the end of a long day, she would often sink into bed, utterly exhausted.
His heart sank when he realized he had lost his wallet.
After a long hike, she sank into her chair, exhausted.
He accidentally sank to his knees in the muddy field.
The sun slowly sinks below the horizon each evening.
As evening approached, the sun began to sink below the horizon.
She watched the distant mountain slowly sink into a sea of clouds.
The full moon seemed to sink behind the hills as dawn broke.
