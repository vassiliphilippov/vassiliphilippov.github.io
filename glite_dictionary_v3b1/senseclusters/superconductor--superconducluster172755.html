superconductor

noun
zero-resistance material
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərkənˈdʌktər / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəkənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a material that can conduct electricity with zero resistance when cooled below a certain temperature

Examples

A superconductor material
A superconductor can carry electric current without losing any energy as heat.
Scientists cooled the material to turn it into a superconductor for their experiment.
The discovery of a new superconductor could revolutionize power transmission technology.
