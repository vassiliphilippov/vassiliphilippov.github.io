mistletoe

noun
parasitic plant with white berries, Chrismas tradition
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsl̩təʊ /; / ˈmɪsəlˌtəʊ /; / ˈmɪs.əl.təʊ / (BrE)
a plant with white berries that grows on trees and is traditionally used as a Christmas decoration, often hung in doorways for the custom of kissing underneath

Examples

According to tradition, you are supposed to kiss beneath the mistletoe.
He hung a sprig of mistletoe over the doorway before the Christmas party.
The waxy white berries of mistletoe are poisonous if eaten.
During the holidays, people often hang mistletoe in their doorways as part of the tradition.
Legend says couples who kiss under the mistletoe will have good luck.
The mistletoe grew high up in the branches of the old oak tree.
