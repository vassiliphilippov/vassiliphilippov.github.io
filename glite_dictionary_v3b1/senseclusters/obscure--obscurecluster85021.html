obscure

verb
make unclear or conceal
5.1 5.6
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈskjʊr/ /; / /əb.ˈskjɜːr/ (AmE), /əb.ˈskjʊə/ /; / /əb.ˈskjɔː/ (BrE)
to make something unclear, difficult to see, or hard to understand; to conceal or make indistinct

Examples

To obscure the truth
He tried to obscure the truth with vague and confusing statements.
The details were obscured by shadow.
Thick fog can obscure the view of the mountains completely.
Technical jargon can obscure the main points of a scientific paper.
The recent changes to the law only obscured the original intentions of the legislation.
Thick fog obscured the sign, making it impossible to read.

The true meaning was obscured by complicated language and confusing analogies.
Thick fog can obscure the road, making driving dangerous.
