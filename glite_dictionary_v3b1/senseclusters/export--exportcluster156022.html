export

verb
transfer data out in a usable format
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɔrt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːt / (BrE)
to transfer electronic data out of a database or document, typically into a format that can be used by other programs

Examples

To export a file
Before upgrading the system, remember to export all your important documents.
The data was exported from the database for analysis in another program.
You can export your contacts from this app as a CSV file.

Related Terms

  • antonym: import - to bring data into a database or program from another format
