horn

noun
noisemaker for parties and games
6.3
Pronunciation: / hɔrn / (AmE), / hɔːn / (BrE)
a device that makes a loud sound as a warning or signal, such as in vehicles or at parties

Examples

Car horn
At midnight on New Year's Eve, everyone started making noise with a horn.
He bought a colorful horn to celebrate his team's victory.
The children took turns blowing the horn at the birthday party.
He pressed the horn to alert the pedestrian crossing the road.
The drivers exchanged angry blasts of the horn during the traffic jam.
You should use your car’s horn only when absolutely necessary.
He pressed the horn to alert the cyclist in front of him.
Please don't use the horn late at night unless it's an emergency.
The loud sound of the horn startled the pedestrians at the crosswalk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bell - a device that rings to signal or warn
  • synonym: hooter - a device that makes a loud noise, similar to a horn
  • synonym: siren - a device that makes a loud warning sound, especially in emergency vehicles
  • synonym: whistle - a device that emits a sound when blown, often used for signaling
