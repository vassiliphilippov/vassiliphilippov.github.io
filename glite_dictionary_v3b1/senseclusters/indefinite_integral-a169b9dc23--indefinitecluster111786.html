indefinite integral

noun
general form of an integral with constant of integration
6.3 7.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdef.ɪ.nət ˈɪn.tə.grəl/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdef.ɪ.nət ˈɪn.tɪ.grəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdef.ɪ.nət ˈɪn.tɪ.grəl/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdef.ə.nət ˈɪn.tə.grəl/ (BrE)
an integral that represents a general form of antiderivatives of a function, expressed as F(x) + C, where C is a constant

Examples

An indefinite integral function
Calculating an indefinite integral involves finding the general antiderivative of a function.
Students learned how to solve indefinite integrals using substitution methods in calculus class.
The indefinite integral of sin(x) is -cos(x) plus a constant of integration.
