bodiliness

noun
physical existence
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːdilɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊdilɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having or relating to a physical body rather than being purely spiritual or abstract

Examples

The bodiliness of form
The artist explored the bodiliness of the human form in her sculptures.
Some philosophies emphasize the importance of bodiliness in understanding consciousness.
He questioned whether dreams have any sense of bodiliness or are purely mental experiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corporeality - the state of having a physical body
  • antonym: spirituality - the quality of being concerned with the spirit rather than the body
