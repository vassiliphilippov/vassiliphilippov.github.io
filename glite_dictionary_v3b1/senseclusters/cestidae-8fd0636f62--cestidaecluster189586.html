Cestidae

noun
a family of elongated, flattened ctenophores
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛstaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈsɛstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of ctenophores characterized by a greatly flattened and elongated body, coextensive with the order Cestida.

Examples

Cestidae family
Marine biologists are studying members of the Cestidae family to understand their unique swimming mechanisms.
Textbooks often illustrate Cestidae alongside other ctenophore families to show anatomical differences.
The elongated body of Cestidae species aids them in gliding through ocean currents.
