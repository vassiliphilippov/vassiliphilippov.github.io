equant

noun
astronomical reference point
Pronunciation: / ˈiːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈiːkwənt / (BrE)
a point in ancient astronomy from which a planet's motion appears uniform, used in Ptolemaic models

Examples

The equant point
In Ptolemaic astronomy, the equant was used to explain the irregular speed of planets.
The concept of the equant helped ancient astronomers model planetary motion more accurately.
Modern astronomy no longer uses the equant, but it was essential in historical models.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deferent - the large circle in ancient astronomy along which a planet or the center of an epicycle moves
  • synonym: epicycle - a small circle whose center moves around the circumference of a larger one in ancient astronomy
