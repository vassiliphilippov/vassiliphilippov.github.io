continence

noun
control over urination and defecation
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑntənəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɪnəns / (BrE)
the ability to control the discharge of urine and feces voluntarily

Examples

Bladder continence
Children usually achieve continence by the age of three.
Loss of continence can greatly affect a person's quality of life.
The patient regained continence after several weeks of physical therapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incontinence - lack of control over urination or defecation
