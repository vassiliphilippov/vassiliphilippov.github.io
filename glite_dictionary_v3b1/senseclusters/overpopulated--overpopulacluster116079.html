overpopulated

adjective
too many inhabitants
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpɑpjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpɒpjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
having more people or animals in a place than it can support comfortably or safely

Examples

An overpopulated city
The small island became overpopulated after years of rapid growth.
Many cities are facing problems because they are overpopulated and lack enough resources.
Wildlife reserves can become overpopulated if animals have no natural predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crowded - filled with too many people or things
  • antonym: underpopulated - having fewer people than is normal or needed
