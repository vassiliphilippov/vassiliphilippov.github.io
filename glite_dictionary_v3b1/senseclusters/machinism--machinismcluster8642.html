machinism

noun
mechanical explanation doctrine
Pronunciation: / məˈʃiːnɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈʃiːnɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine that all natural phenomena, including living processes, can be explained entirely by mechanical laws

Examples

Scientific machinism
The scientist's belief in machinism led him to search for mechanical explanations for all biological functions.
Some philosophers reject machinism, arguing that life cannot be fully explained by physical laws alone.
In the nineteenth century, machinism influenced many debates about the nature of living organisms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mechanism - the theory that natural processes are mechanically determined
  • antonym: vitalism - the doctrine that living organisms are fundamentally different from non-living entities
