whereon

adverb
on which
Pronunciation: / wɛrˈɑn / (AmE), / weərˈɒn / (BrE)
on which; used to refer to something previously mentioned as the place or surface for an action or event.

Examples

The table whereon books rest
He built a platform whereon the statue would stand.
The table whereon the documents were placed was made of oak.
They reached the hilltop whereon the old castle ruins remained.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whereupon - immediately after which
