Synonyms

  • set out
    3.2 8.05/m
    set out is used when someone begins a project with a clear aim or plan, while embark implies starting an important, often challenging, undertaking.
  • launch
    3.7 41/m
    launch is used for starting something new or public, like a product or campaign, while embark describes a personal, challenging start.
  • commence
    4.4 7.85/m
    commence sounds formal and fits official or planned starts, while embark suggests beginning an important or daring new project or journey.
  • begin
    1.0 680/m
    begin is the general verb for starting any action, while embark emphasizes starting something new, important, or especially challenging.
