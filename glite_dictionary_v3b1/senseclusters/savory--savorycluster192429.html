savory

noun
aromatic or spicy dish at meal's end
4.2 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪˌvəri /; / ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
A small aromatic or spicy dish served at the end of a meal or as an hors d'oeuvre, especially in British cuisine.

Examples

A British savory dish
After dessert, the waiter brought a savory and a selection of cheeses to the table.
In traditional British dinners, a small savory is sometimes served before the port.
She prepared a mushroom savory wrapped in puff pastry for the guests.
At formal dinners, a savoury is sometimes served after dessert.
She prepared a delicious savoury of mushrooms on toast as a starter.
The restaurant's signature savoury features smoked salmon and dill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appetizer - a small dish served before the main meal to stimulate the appetite
  • antonym: sweet - a dish or course having a sugary taste, often served as dessert
