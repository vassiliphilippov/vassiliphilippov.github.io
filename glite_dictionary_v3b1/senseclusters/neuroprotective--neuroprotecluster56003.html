neuroprotective

noun
nerve cell protector
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊprəˈtɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊprəˈtɛktɪv / (BrE)
a substance or treatment that helps protect nerve cells from injury, damage, or degeneration

Examples

Neuroprotective drug
Researchers are developing new neuroprotectives to help treat patients with Parkinson's disease.
The doctor explained how a neuroprotective might slow the progression of nerve damage.
Clinical trials are testing whether this neuroprotective can prevent memory loss in Alzheimer's patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antioxidant - a substance that prevents or slows cell damage
  • antonym: neurotoxin - a substance that damages nerve cells
