unquietness

noun
state of unrest
7.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkwaɪətnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwaɪətnəs / (BrE)
a state of disturbance, unrest, or lack of calm or peace

Examples

Growing unquietness
The growing unquietness in the region led to increased police presence on the streets.
After the controversial decision, unquietness spread among the citizens, resulting in protests.
Periods of unquietness often follow major political changes in a country.
The unquietness in the room grew as the storm approached.
He could not sleep because of the unquietness in his mind.
Political changes brought a sense of unquietness to the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or excitement
  • antonym: calmness - a state of being peaceful and free from disturbance
  • synonym: disorder - a lack of order or peace, often in society
  • antonym: peace - a state of calm and order in a community
  • synonym: restlessness - an inability to relax or be still
  • synonym: unrest - a state of dissatisfaction or disturbance in a community
