Rocky Mountain bristlecone pine

noun
long-lived pine tree of the Rocky Mountains
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑki ˌmaʊntən ˈbrɪsəlkoʊn paɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɒki ˌmaʊntɪn ˈbrɪsəlkəʊn paɪn / (BrE)
a small, slow-growing pine tree (Pinus aristata) native to the Rocky Mountains of the western United States, known for its extremely long lifespan and dense branches with short needles and thorn-tipped cone scales

Examples

A Rocky Mountain bristlecone pine tree
A Rocky Mountain bristlecone pine in Colorado was estimated to be more than 2,000 years old.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Rocky Mountain bristlecone pines to understand climate changes over millennia.
The Rocky Mountain bristlecone pine is renowned for being one of the oldest living tree species on Earth.
