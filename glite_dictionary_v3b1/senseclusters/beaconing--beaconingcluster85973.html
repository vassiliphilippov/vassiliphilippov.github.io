beaconing

noun
signal emission
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of sending out a light or radio signal from a beacon for navigation, warning, or identification purposes

Examples

Lighthouse beaconing
The ship relied on beaconing from the lighthouse to safely approach the shore at night.
Modern airports use beaconing systems to help pilots land in poor visibility.
Emergency services tracked the lost hiker by following the beaconing from his radio transmitter.
