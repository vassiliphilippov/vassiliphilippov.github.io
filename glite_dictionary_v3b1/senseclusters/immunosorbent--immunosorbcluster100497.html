immunosorbent

noun
antibody-binding material
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˈsɔrbənt / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˈsɔːbənt / (BrE)
a material or device that specifically binds antibodies or antigens, used in laboratory tests to detect immune responses

Examples

An immunosorbent device
The laboratory uses an immunosorbent to capture specific antibodies during diagnostic tests.
After preparing the immunosorbent, the scientist added the patient samples for analysis.
Proper storage of the immunosorbent is essential to maintain its effectiveness in experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adsorbent - a substance that collects molecules on its surface
