ecumenical

adjective
promoting unity among churches or religions
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.jəˈmɛn.ɪ.kəl /; / ˌiːkjəˈmɛnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiː.kjuːˈmɛn.ɪ.kəl /; / ˌiːkjʊˈmɛnɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or promoting unity among the world's Christian churches or, more broadly, among religions.

Examples

Ecumenical church unity
Her approach to theology is highly ecumenical, embracing diverse traditions.
Leaders from different denominations gathered for an ecumenical service celebrating Christian unity.
The ecumenical movement seeks to foster dialogue and cooperation between churches.
An oecumenical approach can help bridge differences between religious communities.
She has a strong interest in oecumenical dialogue and interfaith activities.
The oecumenical council was attended by leaders from many different Christian denominations.
