gut

verb
remove internal parts completely
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɡʌt / (AmE), / ɡʌt / (BrE)
to remove the internal parts or organs from something, such as an animal or the inside of a building

Examples

To gut a fish
Firefighters managed to save the walls, but the fire completely gutted the inside of the building.
Renovators had to gut the house before rebuilding the interior.
The company plans to gut the old warehouse and turn it into apartments.
Anna watched as the chef expertly gutted the chicken in preparation for lunch.
It's important to gut the fish before grilling it.
The hunter quickly gutted the deer after the chase.
