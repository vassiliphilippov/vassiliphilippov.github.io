lignified

adjective
woody plant tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪɡnɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡnɪfaɪd / (BrE)
describing plant tissue that has become woody or hardened due to the presence of lignin

Examples

Lignified stem
The stem became lignified as the plant matured, providing extra support.
Botanists study how lignified tissues help trees withstand strong winds.
Some roots develop a lignified layer to protect against soil pathogens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: herbaceous - having the characteristics of non-woody plants
  • synonym: woody - having the characteristics of wood
