stylize

verb
represent in a conventional or artistic style
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstaɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or design something in a particular, conventional, or non-naturalistic style, often deliberately rather than realistically

Examples

To stylize a design
Fashion designers often stylize classic outfits to fit current trends.
The artist decided to stylize the trees in the painting to create a unique visual effect.
The film stylized historical events, making them more dramatic and visually striking.
Many video games stylise their graphics to achieve a unique look rather than realism.
The artist chose to stylise the portrait, giving it exaggerated features for dramatic effect.
The director decided to stylise the setting to evoke the mood of 1950s noir films.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naturalize - to represent or render in a naturalistic or realistic way
  • antonym: realistic - depicted or represented in a realistic manner
  • synonym: stylize - to represent or design something according to a particular style (US spelling)
