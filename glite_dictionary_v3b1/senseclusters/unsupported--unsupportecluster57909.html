unsupported

adjective
not held up; lacking support
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.səˈpɔr.tɪd /; / ˌʌnsəˈpɔrtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈpɔː.tɪd /; / ˌʌnsəˈpɔːtɪd / (BrE)
not held up, helped, or approved; lacking physical, structural, or other support

Examples

Unsupported roof
Heavy snow caused the roof to collapse when it was left unsupported by beams.
The shelf remained unsupported after the bracket broke.
The structure is dangerous because many floors are unsupported.
The bridge collapsed because it was unsupported in the middle.
She felt unsupported by her friends during a difficult time.
The theory remains unsupported by any scientific evidence.
