conservatorship

noun
court-appointed management
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɝːvətɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvətəʃɪp / (BrE)
a legal arrangement in which a court appoints a person or organization to manage the personal or financial affairs of someone who cannot do so themselves

Examples

Court conservatorship
After the court hearing, her finances were placed under conservatorship to protect her interests.
The museum's rare collection is under conservatorship to ensure its preservation for future generations.
A judge may grant conservatorship if someone is unable to manage their own affairs due to illness.
The court established a conservatorship to protect the elderly man's assets.
After her accident, her family applied for conservatorship to help manage her medical decisions.
A judge reviews each conservatorship case to ensure the person's best interests are served.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guardianship - legal responsibility for another person's care or property
  • synonym: trusteeship - the position of managing assets or affairs for someone else
  • antonym: ward - a person under the protection of a legal guardian
