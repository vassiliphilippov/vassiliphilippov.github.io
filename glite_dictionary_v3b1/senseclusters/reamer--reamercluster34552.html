reamer

noun
tool for enlarging or finishing holes
Pronunciation: / ˈriːmər / (AmE), / ˈriːmə / (BrE)
a tool with a cylindrical, often fluted, cutting edge used to enlarge or finish holes in metal, wood, or other materials

Examples

Metal reamer
A reamer is essential in precision engineering for finishing drilled holes.
After drilling, he ran a reamer through the metal to smooth the edges.
The mechanic used a reamer to enlarge the hole for the new bolt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drill - a tool or machine with a rotating cutting tip used for making holes
