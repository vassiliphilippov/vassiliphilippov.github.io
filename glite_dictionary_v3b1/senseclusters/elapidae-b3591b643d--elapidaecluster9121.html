Elapidae

noun
a family of venomous snakes
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæpɪˌdi /; / ɪˈlæpəˌdi / (AmE), / ɪˈlæpɪdiː /; / ɪˈlapɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of venomous snakes that includes cobras, kraits, mambas, coral snakes, and Australian snakes such as taipans and tiger snakes

Examples

The Elapidae family
Members of the Elapidae are recognizable by their fixed front fangs and slender bodies.
Researchers studied the venom composition of several Elapidae species to better understand their toxicity.
The Elapidae family includes some of the world's most dangerous snakes, such as cobras and mambas.
Many members of the Elapidae family, such as cobras and mambas, are highly dangerous to humans.
The Elapidae are distinguished by their fixed, front-fanged venom delivery system.
The taipan, one of the world's most venomous snakes, belongs to the Elapidae.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cobra - large venomous snake belonging to Elapidae, known for its hood
  • antonym: viper - member of the Viperidae family; another major group of venomous snakes
