snarly

adjective
tangled and knotted
Pronunciation: / ˈsnɑːrli / (AmE), / ˈsnɑːli / (BrE)
(of hair, thread, etc.) tangled or full of knots; forming snarls.

Examples

A snarly hair
After the hike, her hair was so snarly that she needed extra conditioner to comb it out.
He spent hours trying to untangle the snarly fishing line.
The box was filled with snarly bits of thread that were impossible to separate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knotted - full of knots or tied in knots
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, not rough or tangled
  • synonym: tangled - twisted together in a confusing mass
