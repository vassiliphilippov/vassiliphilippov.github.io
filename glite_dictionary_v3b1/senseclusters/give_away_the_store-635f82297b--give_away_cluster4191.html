give away the store

verb
concede too much; be overly generous
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv əˈweɪ ðə stɔr / (AmE), / ɡɪv əˈweɪ ðə stɔː / (BrE)
to be excessively generous or concede too much, especially in a negotiation, often resulting in disadvantage or loss

Examples

To give away the store in talks
During the merger, she almost gave away the store by agreeing to all their terms.
The manager warned not to give away the store when discussing salary increases.
We need to make a profit, so let's avoid giving away the store in this deal.
