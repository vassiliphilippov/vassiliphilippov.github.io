stiffness

noun
difficulty or pain in movement
3.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈstɪf.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈstɪf.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or condition of being inflexible, rigid, or difficult to move (in the body or in objects), often causing discomfort or pain

Examples

Muscle stiffness
After spending hours gardening, she felt a strong stiffness in her lower back.
Regular stretching can help reduce morning stiffness in your joints.
The stiffness in his legs made it hard for him to climb the stairs.
After sleeping in an awkward position, he noticed some stiffness in his neck.
The stiffness of the metal makes it ideal for construction purposes.
The engineer measured the stiffness of the beam to ensure it could support the load.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking
  • synonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and inflexible
