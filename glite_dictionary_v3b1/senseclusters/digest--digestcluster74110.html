digest

verb
summarize or condense information
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / daɪˈdʒɛst /; / dɪˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
to make information more concise by summarizing or condensing it

Examples

To digest a report
He managed to digest the long report into a two-page summary.
The teacher asked the students to digest the chapter for the class discussion.
This article digests years of scientific research into an accessible overview.
Analysts often digest large data sets to highlight key trends.
He digested the complex regulations into a handy guide for new employees.
The committee will digest all the feedback and present a concise report.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condense - to make something more concise or compact
  • synonym: organize - to arrange systematically
  • synonym: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
