opprobrium

noun
public disgrace or harsh criticism
Pronunciation: / əˈproʊbriəm / (AmE), / əˈprəʊbrɪəm /; / əˈprəʊbriəm / (BrE)
severe public disgrace or harsh criticism because of shameful or improper actions

Examples

Widespread opprobrium
After the scandal, the politician became the target of widespread opprobrium in the media.
His dishonest actions brought opprobrium upon the entire organization.
The company worked hard to recover from the opprobrium following the safety violations.
After the incident, the company was subject to public opprobrium and widespread calls for reform.
He sought to atone for the opprobrium caused by his irresponsible behavior.
The scandal brought lasting opprobrium to the politician's career.
