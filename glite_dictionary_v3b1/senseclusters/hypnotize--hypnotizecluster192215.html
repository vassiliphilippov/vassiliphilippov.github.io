hypnotize

verb
to control someone's mind
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪpnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪpnətaɪz / (BrE)
to influence or control someone's thoughts, feelings, or actions in a powerful or gradual way, often without their full awareness.

Examples

To hypnotize the crowd
The magician seemed to hypnotize the entire audience with his mysterious performance.
She tried to hypnotize her friend into forgetting the embarrassing moment.
The repetitive music was so soothing it almost hypnotized everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enchant - to attract and hold someone's attention as if by magic
  • antonym: free - to release someone from control or influence
  • synonym: mesmerize - to capture someone's complete attention
