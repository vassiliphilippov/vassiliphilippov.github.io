electrocatalysis

noun
catalysis at electrodes
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊkəˈtæləsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊkəˈtæləsɪs / (BrE)
the acceleration of a chemical reaction at an electrode surface by a catalyst during an electrochemical process

Examples

Electrocatalysis at electrode
Advances in electrocatalysis have made fuel cells more efficient and environmentally friendly.
Researchers study electrocatalysis to develop better batteries and energy storage systems.
The process of electrocatalysis is essential for converting chemical energy into electrical energy in many devices.
