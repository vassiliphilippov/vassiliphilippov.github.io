polygenesis

noun
trait from many genes
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɑːl.ɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /pɒl.ɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the genetic condition in which a trait or characteristic is produced by the combined action of several genes

Examples

Polygenesis of traits
Some diseases are caused by polygenesis, making them more complex to study and treat.
Eye color in humans is often determined by polygenesis rather than a single gene.
The concept of polygenesis helps explain why certain traits show a wide range of variation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monogenesis - the origin of a trait from a single source or gene
  • synonym: polygenic - involving or controlled by many genes
