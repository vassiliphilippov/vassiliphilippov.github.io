faerie

noun
mythical small magical being
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri / (BrE)
A small, magical being in folklore, typically human in form and often depicted as playful or mischievous.

Examples

A mischievous faerie
In the story, a mischievous faerie led the children through the mysterious forest.
Legends say that faeries dance in circles on midsummer’s eve.
The delicate wings of the faerie sparkled in the moonlight.
