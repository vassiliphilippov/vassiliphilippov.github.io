Erlenmeyer flask

noun
conical laboratory flask
Pronunciation: / ˈɜr.lənˌmaɪ.ər flæsk / (AmE), / ˈɜː.lənˌmaɪ.ə flɑːsk / (BrE)
A conical glass laboratory flask with a wide, flat base and a narrow cylindrical neck, used for mixing and heating liquids.

Examples

The Erlenmeyer flask neck
Always hold the Erlenmeyer flask by the neck when swirling liquids to prevent spills.
Several Erlenmeyer flasks were lined up on the lab bench, each containing a different reagent.
The scientist poured the solution into the Erlenmeyer flask before starting the experiment.
