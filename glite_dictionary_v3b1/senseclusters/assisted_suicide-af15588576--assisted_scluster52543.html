assisted suicide

noun
suicide carried out with assistance, often in terminal illness
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪstəd ˈsuːɪsaɪd / (AmE), / əˈsɪstɪd ˈsuːɪsaɪd / (BrE)
The act of intentionally ending a person's life, typically that of someone with a terminal illness, with the help of another, often to relieve suffering.

Examples

Assisted suicide laws
Laws regarding assisted suicide vary greatly from one state to another.
Some terminally ill patients seek assisted suicide to avoid prolonged suffering.
The debate over assisted suicide raises complex ethical and legal questions in many countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: euthanasia - the painless killing of a patient suffering from an incurable disease
