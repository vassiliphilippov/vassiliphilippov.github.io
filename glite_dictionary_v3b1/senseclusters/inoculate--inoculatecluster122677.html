inoculate

verb
introduce a virus/germ to produce immunity
4.8 5.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɑk.jə.leɪt /; / ɪˈnɑkjəˌleɪt /; / ɪˈnɑːkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒk.jʊ.leɪt /; / ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to introduce a vaccine, antigen, or microorganism into a person or animal to produce immunity or for study

Examples

To inoculate a patient
Before traveling abroad, she decided to inoculate herself against hepatitis A.
Doctors now inoculate children against common childhood diseases such as measles and mumps.
The campaign aims to inoculate thousands of people in remote areas where medical access is limited.
It is important to inoculate livestock to prevent the spread of foot-and-mouth disease.
Researchers worked hard to inoculate volunteers during the clinical trial of the new vaccine.
The doctor will inoculate the children against measles next week.
Doctors often inoculate children to protect them against common diseases.
Researchers inoculated the test samples with a strain of harmless virus.
The scientist will inoculate the mice with the bacteria to study the effects of the infection.
