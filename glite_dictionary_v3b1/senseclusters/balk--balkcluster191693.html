balk

noun
sloping roof support beam
Pronunciation: / bɔk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
one of several parallel sloping beams that support a roof, especially in a timber-framed building

Examples

Wooden balk beam
A beam broke during the storm, but the remaining balks kept the roof standing.
Carpenters carefully adjusted each balk to ensure the rafters would align correctly.
The ancient barn still had its original balks supporting the heavy thatched roof.
Each baulk was carefully placed to ensure the roof's stability.
The carpenters repaired the damaged baulk to prevent the roof from sagging.
The old barn's roof was supported by massive baulks of oak timber.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rafter - a sloping beam supporting a roof, similar function to baulk
