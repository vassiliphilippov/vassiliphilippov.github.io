intreat

verb
to negotiate terms
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtriːt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːt / (BrE)
to negotiate, discuss terms, or deal with a matter, especially in a formal or serious way (archaic).

Examples

To intreat for peace
The council sent representatives to intreat with the rebels for peace.
He was chosen to intreat with the foreign merchants about the new trade agreement.
In ancient times, leaders would often intreat with their rivals before battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bargain - to discuss the terms of a deal or agreement
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to or refuse to deal with
  • synonym: negotiate - to discuss something in order to reach an agreement
