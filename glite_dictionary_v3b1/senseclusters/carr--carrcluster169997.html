carr

noun
shrubby wetland
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑr / (AmE), / kɑː / (BrE)
a wetland area with waterlogged soil, typically dominated by shrubs or small trees such as willows or alders

Examples

A carr wetland
The rare carr in this region supports many species of birds and insects.
A dense carr of willows and alders lined the edge of the lake.
Scientists studied the waterlogged soil of the carr to understand wetland ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - a wetland with acidic, spongy ground and mosses
  • antonym: desert - a dry, barren area with little rainfall
  • synonym: swamp - a wetland area with standing water and trees
