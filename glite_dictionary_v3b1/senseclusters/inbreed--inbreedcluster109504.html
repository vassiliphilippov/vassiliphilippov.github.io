inbreed

verb
to breed close relatives
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.briːd/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.briːd/ (BrE)
to breed animals or plants from closely related individuals, often resulting in reduced genetic diversity or health problems

Examples

To inbreed animals
Scientists warn that it is dangerous to inbreed animals because it can cause health problems.
Some rare plant species inbreed naturally due to their isolated habitats.
The breeder avoided the risk to inbreed by introducing new genetic lines.
Scientists warn that if animals inbreed for many generations, genetic disorders may become more common.
Some dog breeders avoid letting their animals inbreed to maintain healthy bloodlines.
Wild populations can inbreed when their numbers are very low and there are few mates available.
If animals inbreed for many generations, genetic disorders can become more common.
Conservationists warn that small populations may inbreed and lose genetic diversity.
The isolated community tried to avoid inbreeding by encouraging marriages with outsiders.
