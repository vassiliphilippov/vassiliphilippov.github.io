wait on hand and foot

verb
serve someone excessively
4.3
Pronunciation: / weɪt ɑn hænd ənd fʊt / (AmE), / weɪt ɒn hænd ənd fʊt / (BrE)
to serve someone attentively and excessively, attending to all their needs or wishes

Examples

To wait on hand and foot someone
It isn't healthy to wait on hand and foot for your children, or they may become spoiled.
She felt uncomfortable when her relatives tried to wait on hand and foot during her visit.
The wealthy businessman expected his staff to wait on hand and foot every day.
