calling card

noun
greeting card left to indicate a visit
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔlɪŋ kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlɪŋ kɑːd / (BrE)
a small card bearing a person's name, left as a greeting or proof of visit, especially in social or formal contexts

Examples

A visitor's calling card
After visiting her neighbor, she left a calling card to let them know she had stopped by.
In Victorian times, a gentleman would always leave his calling card at a friend's home if they were not in.
She found a calling card on her doorstep, a sign that someone had visited.
