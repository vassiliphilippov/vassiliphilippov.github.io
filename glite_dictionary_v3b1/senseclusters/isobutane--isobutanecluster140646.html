isobutane

noun
branched alkane gas
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈbjuːteɪn /; / ˌaɪsoʊˈbjuːteɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈbjuːteɪn /; / ˌaɪsəʊˈbjuːteɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable gas (a type of butane) used as a fuel and in refrigerants and aerosol propellants

Examples

Isobutane gas
The laboratory stored isobutane in pressurized tanks for use in chemical experiments.

Many portable stoves use isobutane as a fuel source.
The new aerosol cans are filled with isobutane to help spray the contents evenly.
Because isobutane is highly flammable, it must be stored carefully.
