thermostability

noun
heat resistance
7.7
Pronunciation: /θɜːr.moʊ.stə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /θɜː.məʊ.stə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the ability of a substance to remain stable and not change when exposed to high temperatures

Examples

The thermostability of this protein allows it to function in extremely hot environments.
Researchers tested the thermostability of the compound to ensure it would not degrade during processing.
Improving the thermostability of vaccines can help them remain effective without refrigeration.
