ultimatum

noun
final demand or threat
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltəˈmeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌʌltɪˈmeɪtəm / (BrE)
a final demand or statement of terms, the rejection of which will result in retaliation or a breakdown in relations

Examples

A ultimatum demand
He delivered an ultimatum to the company: improve working conditions or face a strike.
She gave him an ultimatum: change your ways or the relationship is over.
The two nations exchanged threats and ultimatums as tensions escalated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demand - an insistent request or requirement
  • antonym: offer - an act of presenting something for acceptance or rejection
