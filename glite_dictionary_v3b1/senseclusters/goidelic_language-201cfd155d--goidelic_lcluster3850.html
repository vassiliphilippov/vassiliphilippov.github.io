Goidelic language

any of the group of Celtic languages that includes Irish, Scottish Gaelic, and Manx, spoken mainly in Ireland, Scotland, and the Isle of Man.

Examples

Goidelic language group
Linguists have traced the history of the Goidelic language group across several centuries.
Manx, once nearly extinct, is a revived Goidelic language spoken on the Isle of Man.
The Irish and Scottish Gaelic are both considered Goidelic languages.
