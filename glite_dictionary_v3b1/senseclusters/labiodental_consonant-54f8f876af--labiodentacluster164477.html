labiodental consonant

noun
consonant produced with lips and teeth
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪbioʊˈdɛntl ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / ˌleɪbiəʊˈdɛntəl ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by touching the lower lip to the upper teeth, as in the English 'f' or 'v'

Examples

Labiodental consonant sound
Children sometimes have difficulty pronouncing labiodental consonants when they are learning to speak.
Phonetic studies often discuss how labiodental consonants are produced in different languages.
The sounds represented by 'f' and 'v' in English are both labiodental consonants.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dental consonant - a consonant articulated with the tongue against the teeth
