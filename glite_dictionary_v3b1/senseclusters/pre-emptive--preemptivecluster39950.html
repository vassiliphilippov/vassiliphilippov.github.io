Intended to deter or prevent an expected situation or occurrence, especially by taking action in advance.

Examples

A pre-emptive apology
He delivered a pre-emptive apology, anticipating criticism for his forthcoming remarks.
Health officials took pre-emptive measures to prevent the spread of the virus.
The company made a pre-emptive offer to acquire its competitor before others could bid.

Synonyms

  • preemptive
    7.0 2.59/m
    preemptive is used without a hyphen and appears in modern writing, while pre-emptive is the hyphenated form of the same idea.
  • preemptory
    8.0 0.01/m
    preemptory is done to forestall someone else's action, while pre-emptive is aimed at preventing an expected event by acting early.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reactive - acting in response to a situation rather than in anticipation
