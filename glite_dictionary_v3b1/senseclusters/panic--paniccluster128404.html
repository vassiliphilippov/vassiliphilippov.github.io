panic

verb
to become or cause to become suddenly afraid
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpænɪk / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk / (BrE)
to feel or cause someone to feel sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety; to lose the ability to think or act calmly because of fear

Examples

To panic during trouble
Don't panic if you forget your lines during the play.
The loud crash outside panicked everyone in the classroom.
The sudden smoke alarm panicked the shoppers, causing them to rush to the exits.
He panicked during the interview and forgot everything he wanted to say.
Try not to panic if you can't find your passport before the flight.
When the fire alarm sounded, several people began to panic and rushed to the exits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarm - to cause to feel sudden fear or anxiety
  • antonym: calm - to make someone free from fear or agitation
  • synonym: freak out - lose control due to fear or anxiety
  • degree lower: worry - feel anxious or uneasy about something
