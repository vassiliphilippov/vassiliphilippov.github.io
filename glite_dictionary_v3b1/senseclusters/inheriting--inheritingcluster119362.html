inheriting

adjective
having legal right to inherit
2.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Having the legal right to receive property, a title, or money from someone after their death.

Examples

The inheriting son
As the inheriting son, he was entitled to the family estate.
In some cultures, the inheriting daughter takes on significant responsibilities.
Only the inheriting siblings gathered to hear the lawyer's announcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heir - a person legally entitled to the property or rank of another on that person's death
