generalizable

adjective
applicable in many cases
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlʌɪzəbəl / (BrE)
able to be applied to or valid for many different situations, groups, or cases

Examples

Generalizable results
The findings from this experiment are not generalizable to the entire population.
Researchers want their results to be as generalizable as possible across different groups.
A small sample size can make a study less generalizable to real-world situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: specific - limited to a particular situation or group
  • synonym: universal - existing or true in all cases or situations
