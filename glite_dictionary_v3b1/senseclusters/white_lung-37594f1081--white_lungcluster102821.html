white lung

noun
acute lung injury from inhaled irritants
6.6
Pronunciation: / waɪt lʌŋ / (AmE), / waɪt lʌŋ / (BrE)
A severe form of acute lung injury resulting in stiff, fibrous lungs with impaired oxygen exchange, often caused by inhaling irritant vapors such as ammonia or chlorine.

Examples

White lung disease
Doctors cautioned that early detection of white lung can improve the chances of recovery.
Persistent coughing and shortness of breath are common symptoms of white lung in patients exposed to chlorine gas.
Workers exposed to hazardous chemicals developed white lung after a major spill at the plant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acute respiratory distress syndrome - a severe lung condition characterized by inflammation and reduced oxygen exchange
