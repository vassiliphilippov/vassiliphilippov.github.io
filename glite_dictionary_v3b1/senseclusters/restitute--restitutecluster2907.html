restitute

verb
return or restore to original state or owner
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛstɪˌtut / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪˌtjuːt / (BrE)
to restore something lost or stolen to its proper owner; to return something to its original state or position

Examples

To restitute stolen property
After the investigation, the museum agreed to restitute the ancient artifact to its country of origin.
He promised to restitute the borrowed books as soon as possible.
The court ordered the company to restitute the funds to the victims.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forfeit - to lose or give up something as a penalty
  • synonym: restore - to return something to a former place or condition
