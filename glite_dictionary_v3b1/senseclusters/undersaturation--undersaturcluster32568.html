undersaturation

noun
insufficient supply
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a state in which the presence or supply of something, especially in a market, is below its potential or demand.

Examples

Undersaturation of the market
The undersaturation of electric vehicles in rural areas presents opportunities for new businesses.
Analysts noted undersaturation in the local housing market, suggesting more homes could be built.
Because of undersaturation in the tech sector, startups found it easier to attract customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oversaturation - a state where supply or presence exceeds demand or capacity
  • synonym: scarcity - a situation in which something is in short supply
