subspace

noun
part of a larger space
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌspeɪs / (BrE)
a space that forms part of a larger space, especially in mathematics or physics

Examples

A subspace in math
Each solution corresponds to a different subspace of the overall system's state space.
In linear algebra, a subspace is a subset of a vector space that is itself a vector space.
The scientist analyzed the properties of the physical subspace within the laboratory environment.
