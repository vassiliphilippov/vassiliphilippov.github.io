(in 3D imaging and manufacturing) the process of capturing an object's three-dimensional shape (and often its color or texture) using devices such as laser or structured-light scanners, producing a digital 3D model, point cloud, or mesh.

Examples

3D scanning
Manufacturers now rely on scanning to produce high-precision 3D models of components.
The museum used handheld scanning to create detailed replicas of fragile artifacts for study.
After reverse-engineering the part, engineers compared the CNC output with the original scanning data.
