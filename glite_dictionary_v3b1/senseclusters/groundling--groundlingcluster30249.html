groundling

noun
common person
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndlɪŋ / (BrE)
a common person, especially one with little social status or influence

Examples

A poor groundling
In Shakespeare's time, the groundlings stood closest to the stage during performances.
The politician promised to listen to the concerns of ordinary groundlings in the community.
Despite being a groundling, she dreamed of making a difference in society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aristocrat - a member of the highest social class
  • synonym: commoner - a person who is not of noble rank
