ad valorem

adjective, adverb
proportional to value (esp. taxes)
8.0
Pronunciation: / æd vəˈlɔrəm /; / æd vəˈlɔːrəm / (AmE), / ad vəˈlɔːrəm / (BrE)
calculated according to the value of something, especially for taxes or duties

Examples

Ad valorem tax
Some countries prefer ad valorem duties over flat-rate tariffs to adjust with market prices.
The bill proposed shifting from fixed taxes to an ad valorem taxation system.
The imported cars were taxed ad valorem by customs authorities.
The government imposed an ad valorem tax on all imported vehicles.
Property taxes are often assessed on an ad valorem basis, depending on the value of the property.
An ad valorem duty means the tax increases as the value of the goods increases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fixed-rate - not changing with value; set at a constant amount
