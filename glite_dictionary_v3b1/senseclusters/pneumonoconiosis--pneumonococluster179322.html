pneumonoconiosis

noun
chronic lung disease from mineral dust
6.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / nuˌmoʊnəˌkoʊniˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / njuːˌməʊnəˌkəʊnɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic lung disease caused by the inhalation of metallic or mineral dust particles, leading to inflammation and scarring of lung tissue.

Examples

pneumonoconiosis lung disease
Doctors diagnosed several workers with pneumonoconiosis after years of unprotected dust exposure.
Long-term exposure to coal dust can lead to pneumonoconiosis in miners.
The prevalence of pneumonoconiosis has declined with stricter workplace safety regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asbestosis - lung disease caused by inhaling asbestos fibers
  • synonym: silicosis - lung disease caused by inhaling silica dust
