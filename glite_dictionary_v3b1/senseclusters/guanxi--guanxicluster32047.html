guanxi

noun
personal connections
Pronunciation: / ˈɡwɑːnˌʃi / (AmE), / ˈɡwænˌʃi / (BrE)
the system of personal relationships and social networks in Chinese society used to exchange favors, gain influence, or advantages, especially in business or social situations

Examples

Strong guanxi network
In Chinese business culture, having strong guanxi can open many doors.
He relied on his guanxi to secure the contract with the local government.
Without guanxi, it can be difficult to succeed in certain industries in China.
In Chinese business culture, guanxi is often more important than written contracts.
She relied on her strong guanxi to secure a new job opportunity.
Understanding guanxi can help foreigners build better relationships in China.

Related Terms

  • antonym: favoritism - giving unfair advantages to friends or associates
  • antonym: nepotism - favoritism shown to relatives or friends, especially in jobs
  • synonym: networking - the act of making and using contacts to gain opportunities
