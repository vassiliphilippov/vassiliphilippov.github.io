Astropogon stellatus

noun
a species of cardinalfish of the West Indies
Pronunciation: / ˌæstrəˈpoʊɡɑn stəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈpəʊɡɒn stəˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of cardinalfish found in the West Indies, known for inhabiting the mantle cavity of living conchs.

Examples

Astropogon stellatus fish
Marine biologists studied the relationship between Astropogon stellatus and its host conchs in the Caribbean.
The Astropogon stellatus can often be found sheltering inside the shells of living conchs.
Unlike many other cardinalfish, Astropogon stellatus has a unique symbiotic habitat.
