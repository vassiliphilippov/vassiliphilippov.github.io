fracture

verb
break into pieces
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfræk.tʃɚ /; / ˈfræktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfræk.tʃə /; / ˈfræktʃə / (BrE)
to break or crack something hard, such as a bone or a physical object

Examples

To fracture a bone
Repeated stress can fracture metal over time.
She accidentally fractured her phone screen when she dropped it on the ground.
The force of the impact could easily fracture the windshield.
Doctors worry a fall could fracture his already fragile hip bone.
She fractured her ankle slipping on the stairs last winter.
The skier managed to fracture his wrist during the race.
His collarbone fractured when he was tackled during the game.
If too much pressure is applied, the glass may fracture.
The doctor warned that a fall could fracture her hip.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mend - to repair or heal after being broken
  • synonym: shatter - to break into many pieces; to smash
