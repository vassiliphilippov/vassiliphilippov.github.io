homologize

verb
make or demonstrate as homologous
5.9
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑːləˌdʒaɪz /; / həˈmɑːləˌdʒaɪz /; / həˈmɑːləˌɡaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒl.ə.dʒaɪz /; / həˈmɒlədʒaɪz /; / həˈmɒləˌɡaɪz / (BrE)
To show or make things have the same origin, relation, or structure, especially in science (such as biology or chemistry).

Examples

To homologize chemical structures
Chemists can homologize hydrocarbons by adding repeating units to their structure.
Researchers often homologize genes across species to understand evolutionary relationships.
The scientist attempted to homologize the bones of the whale with those of land mammals.
Geneticists can homologize genes between different species to study their functions.
In comparative anatomy, experts often homologize structures to trace evolutionary relationships.
Scientists homologize the bones of a whale's flipper with those of the human arm.
It can be challenging to homologise structures from different organisms due to evolutionary divergence.
Researchers aim to homologise the gene sequences to study evolutionary relationships.
Scientists attempted to homologise the anatomical features between the two species.
