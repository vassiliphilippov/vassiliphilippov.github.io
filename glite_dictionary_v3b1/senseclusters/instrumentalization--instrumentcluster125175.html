instrumentalization

noun
use as a tool
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstrəˌmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrəˌmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of using something, especially an instrument or variable, as a tool to achieve a specific purpose, often in research or analysis

Examples

Instrumentalization of data
The instrumentalization of statistical methods allowed the researchers to identify causal relationships in the data.
Critics argue that the instrumentalization of art reduces its value to mere utility.
In economics, the instrumentalization of variables is crucial for establishing cause and effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: application - the act of putting something to use
  • synonym: utilization - the act of using something for a particular purpose
