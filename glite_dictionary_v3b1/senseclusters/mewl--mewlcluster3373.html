mewl

verb
to cry or whimper weakly
9.9 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / mjuÀêl / (AmE), / mjuÀêl / (BrE)
to cry or whimper softly in a weak, plaintive way

Examples

To mewl softly
Children sometimes mewl when they are tired or uncomfortable.
He couldn't help but mewl softly as the nurse cleaned his wound.
The tiny kitten began to mewl for its mother in the early morning.

Synonyms

  • pule
    9.9 0.01/m
    pule is a weak, high-pitched cry often used to sound pitiful, while mewl is a soft, plaintive whine.
  • whimper
    3.3 3.87/m
    whimper means making low, soft sounds from pain or fear, while mewl is a quieter, more plaintive little cry.
  • mew
    7.9 0.15/m
    mew is a soft, high animal cry like a kitten, while mewl is a weak, plaintive cry by a person.

Related Terms
