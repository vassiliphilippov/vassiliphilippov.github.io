Anglo-Catholic

adjective
High Church Anglican tradition supporter
Pronunciation: / ˌæŋɡloʊˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊˈkæθəlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the High Church tradition within Anglicanism, emphasizing its Catholic heritage and practices

Examples

Anglo-Catholic priest
As an Anglo-Catholic priest, he upheld many ancient ceremonies in his church.
She was raised in an Anglo-Catholic parish that valued traditional rituals.
The Anglo-Catholic movement stressed the importance of liturgy and sacraments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: High Church - Relating to the branch of Anglicanism emphasizing ritual and Catholic practices
  • antonym: low church - Relating to the branch of Anglicanism that emphasizes simpler worship and Protestant doctrine
