noise pollution

noun
harmful or unwanted environmental noise
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔɪz pəˌluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪz pəˌluːʃən / (BrE)
Unwanted or harmful sounds in the environment, especially those caused by human activity, that disturb or endanger health and well-being.

Examples

Noise pollution levels
Living near a busy highway exposes residents to high levels of noise pollution.
Prolonged noise pollution can lead to health issues like stress and hearing loss.
The city council is introducing measures to reduce noise pollution from construction sites.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pollution - the presence or introduction of harmful substances into an environment
