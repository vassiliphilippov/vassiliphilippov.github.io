decryption

noun
converting encrypted data to readable form
Pronunciation: / diˈkrɪpʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting encoded or encrypted data back into its original, readable form

Examples

Decryption process
After successful decryption, the confidential files were accessible to authorized users.
Modern cybersecurity relies heavily on robust encryption and decryption techniques.
Without the correct password, decryption of the message was impossible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encryption - the process of converting information or data into a code to prevent unauthorized access
