show-stopper

noun
critical problem that halts progress
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈʃoʊ.stɑːp.ər/ (AmE), /ˈʃəʊ.stɒp.ə/ (BrE)
a serious problem, defect, or obstacle that prevents a plan, product, event, or process from proceeding or being completed.

Examples

Show-stopper problem
A critical security flaw became a show-stopper for the software release this week.
Negotiators walked away when the licensing issue turned into an unexpected show-stopper for the deal.
They postponed the product launch after discovering a performance bug that was a real show-stopper.
