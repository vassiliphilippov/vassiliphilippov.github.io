horizontal combination

noun
merger of similar firms at the same production level
Pronunciation: / hɔːrɪˌzɑːntəl ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˌzɒntəl ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the merging of several firms that operate at the same stage in the production process, often to increase market power or efficiency

Examples

A horizontal combination of firms
A horizontal combination allowed the companies to pool their resources and reduce costs at the manufacturing level.
Antitrust laws are designed to regulate horizontal combination to prevent the creation of monopolies.
The proposed horizontal combination of the two banks raised concerns over reduced competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: horizontal integration - process by which a company acquires or merges with similar companies
  • antonym: vertical combination - merging of firms at different stages of production
