accent

noun
emphasis on a syllable or note
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.sɛnt / (AmE), / ˈæk.sənt / (BrE)
greater force or emphasis placed on a particular syllable in a word or a note in music, especially through stress or pitch.

Examples

Word accent
In the word 'banana,' the accent falls on the second syllable.
The pianist placed a clear accent on each note in the melody.
To pronounce this word correctly, remember which syllable takes the accent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice in speaking, especially as relating to pitch
  • synonym: stress - emphasis placed on a syllable or word when speaking
