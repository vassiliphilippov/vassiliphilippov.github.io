compadre

noun
male godparent
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɑːdreɪ / (AmE), / kəmˈpɑːdreɪ / (BrE)
a man who is a child's godfather, especially one who has a special social or familial role after a baptism

Examples

Child's compadre
After the ceremony, he was honored to become the child's compadre and take on new responsibilities.
In many cultures, a compadre is considered part of the extended family.
He introduced his lifelong friend as his son's compadre at the party.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comadre - a woman who is a child's godmother
  • synonym: godfather - a man who sponsors a child at baptism
