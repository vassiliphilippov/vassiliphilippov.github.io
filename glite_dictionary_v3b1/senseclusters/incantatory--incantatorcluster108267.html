incantatory

adjective
chant-like; hypnotic
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkæntəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkæntətəri / (BrE)
having a quality that is like a chant or spell, often enchanting or hypnotic

Examples

Incantatory music
The incantatory music lulled the children to sleep during the ceremony.
Her voice had an incantatory effect, drawing everyone into a calm silence.
The novel's incantatory prose created a dreamlike atmosphere throughout the story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypnotic - having a sleep-inducing or trance-like effect
  • degree lower: monotonous - dull and repetitive, lacking in variety
