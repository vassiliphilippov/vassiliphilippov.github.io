soundbite

noun
short media quote
4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsaʊnd.baɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsaʊnd.baɪt/ (BrE)
a short, memorable part of a speech or interview, often used in news or media

Examples

A famous soundbite
The politician's soundbite was played repeatedly on the evening news.
Journalists often look for a catchy soundbite to summarize a long interview.
Her speech was full of memorable soundbites that quickly spread online.
The news program played a soundbite from the president's speech to highlight his main message.
Journalists often look for a catchy soundbite to include in their reports.
Her clever soundbite quickly spread across social media after the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clip - a short segment taken from a longer audio or video recording
  • synonym: excerpt - a short part taken from a speech, book, or film
  • synonym: quote - a repetition of someone else's statement or thoughts
