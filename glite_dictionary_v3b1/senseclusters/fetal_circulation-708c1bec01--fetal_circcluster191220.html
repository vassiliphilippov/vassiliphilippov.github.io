fetal circulation

noun
blood flow system in a fetus
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːtəl sɜrkjəˈleɪʃən /; / ˈfiːtəl sɝːˌkjuleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəl sɜːkjʊˈleɪʃən /; / ˈfiːtəl sɜː.kjʊˌleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the system of blood vessels and structures that direct blood flow in a developing fetus, differing from postnatal circulation

Examples

Fetal circulation system
Doctors can assess fetal circulation using advanced ultrasound techniques to check for abnormalities.
During pregnancy, fetal circulation ensures that oxygen and nutrients reach the developing organs.
Unlike adults, fetal circulation bypasses the lungs via the ductus arteriosus and foramen ovale.
Abnormalities in foetal circulation can lead to congenital heart defects after birth.
Doctors study foetal circulation to understand how oxygen and nutrients reach the developing baby.
The placenta plays a crucial role in foetal circulation by allowing the exchange of gases and nutrients.
