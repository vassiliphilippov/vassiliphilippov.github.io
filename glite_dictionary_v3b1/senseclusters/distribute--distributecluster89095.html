distribute

verb
give or hand out to several people
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɪbjut / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to give out, share, or spread something among a number of people, places, or over an area

Examples

To distribute food
Please distribute these forms to everyone in the meeting.
The company plans to distribute free samples at the event.
Volunteers will distribute food and water to the affected families.
Please distribute these handouts before the meeting starts.
The charity will distribute food and water to those in need after the hurricane.
The festival committee distributed tickets evenly among all volunteers.
She helped distribute flyers throughout the neighborhood before the event.
The organization will distribute food and blankets to those in need.
The seeds are distributed by the wind over a wide area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocate - to assign or set apart for a particular purpose
  • synonym: circulate - to pass something from person to person or place to place
  • antonym: collect - to gather things together, often from several people; opposite of distribute
  • synonym: dispense - to give out something, especially in fixed amounts
  • antonym: gather - to bring things together into a group
  • synonym: hand out - to give something to each member of a group
  • synonym: scatter - spread or throw loosely in different directions
  • synonym: spread - to make something widely known or distributed
