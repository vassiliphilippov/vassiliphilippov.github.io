co-vary

verb
change together (of variables)
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: / koʊˈvɛri / (AmE), / kəʊˈveəri / (BrE)
(of two or more variables) to change or vary together in a related way, especially over the same time period.

Examples

Variables co-vary together
Economic output tends to co-vary with employment rates in this region.
Researchers found that temperature and humidity often co-vary throughout the year.
The two variables co-varied significantly during the experiment, suggesting a possible connection.
