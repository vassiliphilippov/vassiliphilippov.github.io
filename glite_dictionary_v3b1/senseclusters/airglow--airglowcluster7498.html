airglow

noun
faint night sky light
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈeəˌɡləʊ / (BrE)
a faint natural light in the night sky caused by chemical reactions in the Earth's upper atmosphere

Examples

The airglow light
The airglow was visible as a soft greenish light above the horizon.
Scientists study airglow to learn more about the Earth's upper atmosphere.
On clear nights, photographers sometimes capture the subtle airglow in their images of the stars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nightglow - faint light in the night sky from atmospheric processes
