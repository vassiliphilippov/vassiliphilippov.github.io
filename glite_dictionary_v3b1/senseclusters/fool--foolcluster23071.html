fool

verb
act playfully or waste time
1.8 6.25/m
Pronunciation: / ful / (AmE), / fuÀêl / (BrE)
to behave in a playful, silly, or mischievous way, especially by indulging in horseplay or wasting time

Examples

To fool around
He told his friends to stop fooling and pay attention to the instructions.
Sometimes it's nice just to fool about and not take life too seriously.
The students began to fool around after the teacher left the room.

    3.6 0.99/m

  • horse around
    6.3 0.23/m
    horse around means play roughly and loudly, often as physical horseplay, while fool describes more general playful or time-wasting silliness.
  • clown around
    4.8 0.28/m
    clown around means joke and act silly to make others laugh, while fool covers broader playful or mischievous behavior.
  • play the fool
    4.6 0.23/m
    play the fool means act deliberately silly to amuse others or avoid seriousness, while fool is general playful, silly behavior.
  • act a fool
    8.0 0.04/m

  • fool away
    8.1 0.00/m
