intermediary

noun
person who mediates between parties
6.8 4.14/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™n.tÃ¨…öÀàmiÀê.diÀå…õr.i / (AmE), / Àå…™n.t…ôÀàmiÀê.di.…ôri / (BrE)
a person or organization that acts as a mediator or link between two or more parties in order to help them reach an agreement or understand each other

Examples

An intermediary helps
An experienced intermediary can help resolve disputes before they escalate into serious conflicts.
She acted as an intermediary between the management and the employees during talks.
The company hired an intermediary to negotiate the terms of the merger.

Synonyms

  • mediator
    4.0 4.83/m
    mediator helps people find agreement through guided discussion and neutral facilitation, while intermediary is a broader link or organization between parties.
  • intermediator
    7.7 0.00/m
    intermediator negotiates or links parties in talks, while intermediary is the standard, more common term for that role.
  • middleman
    3.9 0.87/m
    middleman arranges deals and connects buyers and sellers in business, while intermediary can mean any neutral link or organization between parties.
  • go-between
    4.9 0.63/m
    go-between carries messages or negotiates quietly between people in personal matters, while intermediary covers formal or organizational links too.
  • interposer
    8.4 0.00/m
    interposer steps into a dispute to interrupt or change events, while intermediary usually helps parties reach agreement as a neutral link.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mediator - person who helps two sides come to an agreement
  • antonym: participant - person taking part directly, not as an intermediary
