Geminorum

proper noun
Latin genitive plural
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛmɪˈnɔːrəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛmɪˈnɔːrəm / (BrE)
a Latin genitive plural form meaning 'of the twins', used as a specific epithet in scientific names to indicate association with twins or twin-related features

Examples

Geminorum species name
The species name Geminorum was chosen to honor the twin peaks near its habitat.
In taxonomy, Geminorum indicates that the organism is associated with twins or a pair.
The astronomer referred to the constellation as Castor et Pollux Geminorum in his notes.
