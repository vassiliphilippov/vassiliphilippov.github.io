Heteroscelus

noun
genus of wading birds (tattlers)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈsiːləs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈsiːləs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, commonly known as tattlers, found in coastal regions of Asia, Australia, and North America.

Examples

Heteroscelus waders
Researchers observed the migratory routes of Heteroscelus along the Pacific coast.
The Heteroscelus is characterized by its distinct calls and migratory habits.
Two species within the genus Heteroscelus are commonly referred to as tattlers.
