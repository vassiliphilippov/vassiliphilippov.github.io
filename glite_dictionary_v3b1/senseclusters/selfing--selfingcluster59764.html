selfing

noun
self-fertilization process
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛlfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlfɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which an organism fertilizes itself, especially in plants or hermaphroditic animals

Examples

Selfing in plants
Many flowering plants rely on selfing to reproduce when pollinators are scarce.
Genetic diversity can decrease in populations where selfing is common.
Researchers studied the effects of selfing on seed production in isolated environments.
The first generation of selfing in the experiment showed little genetic diversity.
Scientists observed that repeated selfing led to more uniform traits in the plant population.
In some species, selfing is a common reproductive strategy when mates are scarce.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross-fertilization - fertilization involving two different individuals
  • antonym: cross-pollination - fertilization involving two different plants
  • synonym: self-fertilization - fertilization of an organism by its own pollen or gametes
