truth drug

noun
drug thought to induce people to talk freely
Pronunciation: / truθ drʌɡ / (AmE), / truːθ drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug, typically an intravenous anesthetic, believed to induce people to talk freely by reducing inhibitions.

Examples

A truth drug effect
Because truth drugs can impair judgment, anything said under their influence may not be entirely reliable.
Some believe that administering a truth drug can help uncover hidden information, although its effectiveness is disputed.
The suspect was given a truth drug during the police investigation, but his statements were later questioned.
