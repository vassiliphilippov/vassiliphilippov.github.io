amyloid

noun
abnormal protein deposit in tissues
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.i.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈæm.ɪ.lɔɪd / (BrE)
a waxy, translucent protein substance that accumulates in tissues as the result of certain diseases, often causing organ dysfunction

Examples

Amyloid deposits in tissue
Doctors detected amyloid deposits in her liver tissue during the biopsy.
Scientists are researching new methods to prevent amyloid buildup in patients with neurodegenerative disorders.
The accumulation of amyloid in the brain is a hallmark of Alzheimer's disease.
