baccalauréat

noun
historical university degree
Pronunciation: / ˌbækəˌlɔːriˈɑː / (AmE), / ˌbakalɔːˈrea / (BrE)
a university degree awarded by medieval and early modern universities, similar to a bachelor's degree

Examples

Medieval baccalauréat degree
In medieval times, students often graduated with a baccalauréat before continuing to study law or theology.
The baccalauréat was an important step for scholars in early European universities.
Earning a baccalauréat allowed students to pursue advanced degrees in various fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bachelor's degree - an undergraduate academic degree awarded by colleges and universities
