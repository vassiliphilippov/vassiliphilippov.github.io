luck

verb
to succeed by chance
6.6
Pronunciation: / lʌk / (AmE), / lʌk / (BrE)
to achieve or obtain something by chance or good fortune rather than by effort or planning

Examples

To luck into a job
He lucked into a free ticket to the concert when someone canceled at the last minute.
They lucked out and found the last available table at the busy restaurant.
Sometimes you just luck into the right answer without really knowing it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chance - to do something by accident or luck
  • antonym: earn - to get something through effort or work
