monosemy

noun
having a single meaning
9.8
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑː.sə.mi / (AmE), / məˈnɒ.sə.mi / (BrE)
The characteristic of having only one meaning; absence of ambiguity in a word or phrase.

Examples

The monosemy concept
As a dictionary compiler, she preferred terms with monosemy to avoid confusion.
Linguists study both monosemy and polysemy to understand language development.
The word 'oxygen' is an example of monosemy, as it refers to only one concept.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polysemy - the phenomenon of a word having multiple meanings
