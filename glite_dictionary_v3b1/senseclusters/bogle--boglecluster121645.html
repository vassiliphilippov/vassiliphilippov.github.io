bogle

verb
to frighten suddenly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɡl / (BrE)
to frighten or startle someone, causing them to hesitate or recoil suddenly

Examples

To bogle the children
The loud crash in the night bogled the campers, making them sit up in fear.
A sudden shadow bogled her as she walked through the empty hallway.
The unexpected bark from the dog bogled the children playing in the yard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reassure - to make someone feel less afraid or worried
  • synonym: scare - to cause someone to feel afraid
  • synonym: startle - to surprise or frighten someone suddenly
