mainliner

noun
mainline church member
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪnˌlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌlaɪnə / (BrE)
a member or supporter of a mainline Protestant church, such as Methodist or Episcopal.

Examples

Mainliner church member
As a lifelong mainliner, she attended Methodist services every Sunday.
Many mainliners support social justice initiatives through their churches.
He grew up as a mainliner in a Presbyterian family in the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Protestant - a member of a Christian church separate from the Roman Catholic Church
  • antonym: evangelical - a member of a Protestant group emphasizing the authority of the Bible and personal conversion
