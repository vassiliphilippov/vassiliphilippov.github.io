crackly

adjective
with fine cracks
Pronunciation: / ˈkrækli / (AmE), / ˈkrækli / (BrE)
having a surface covered with many fine cracks or appearing as if cracked.

Examples

Crackly paint surface
The old painting had a crackly finish that revealed its age.
She admired the crackly glaze on the handmade pottery.
The photograph showed a crackly texture on the surface of the dried mud.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crazed - covered with a network of fine cracks
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough or cracked
