stander

noun
person who stands
Pronunciation: / ˈstændər / (AmE), / ˈstændə / (BrE)
a person who is standing, especially as opposed to someone who is sitting

Examples

A stander at the park
The park was filled with standers watching the parade.
At the concert, most people were standers near the stage, while others sat on the grass.
The teacher asked the standers at the back of the room to find seats.
During the concert, most people were standers because there were not enough seats.
The train was so full that only a few standers could fit in the aisle.
At the meeting, the standers gathered near the back of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bystander - a person who is present but not involved
  • antonym: sitter - a person who is sitting
