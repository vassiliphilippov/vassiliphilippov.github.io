imagine

verb
form a mental image or concept
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmædʒɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪn / (BrE)
to form a mental image or idea of something that is not present or real

Examples

To imagine a new world
Can you imagine living in a world without electricity?
It's hard to imagine him as anything other than a teacher.
She tried to imagine what life would have been like a hundred years ago.
She spent the afternoon imagining what her life would be like in a new city.
The children were imagining that they were pirates sailing the high seas.
He is imagining how the new policy will affect employees next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceive - to form an idea or notion in the mind
  • synonym: envision - to picture something in your mind
  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember or be unable to bring to mind
  • synonym: visualize - to form a clear mental image of something
