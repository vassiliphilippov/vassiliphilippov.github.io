flue pipe

noun
organ pipe sounded by air over a lip
9.9
Pronunciation: / fluː paɪp / (AmE), / fluː paɪp / (BrE)
an organ pipe that produces sound by air passing across the sharp edge of a lip or fissure, rather than by a vibrating reed

Examples

Flue pipe sound
Each flue pipe must be carefully voiced to produce the desired tonal quality.
The cathedral organ's deepest notes are created by its largest flue pipe.
Unlike a reed pipe, the flue pipe relies purely on air and its shape to make sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reed pipe - organ pipe with a vibrating reed to produce sound
