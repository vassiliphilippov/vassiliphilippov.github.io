stock option

noun
right to buy or sell stock at set price
4.0 3.9
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / stɒk ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
a contract that gives someone the right, but not the obligation, to buy company stock at a fixed price, often given to employees as a benefit

Examples

To exercise stock option
By exercising his stock option, he was able to purchase shares at a much lower price.
Many executives benefit financially from stock options when the value of company shares rises.
The company offered its employees stock options as part of their compensation package.
If the company's share price rises, stock options can become highly valuable to employees.
Many technology companies use stock options to attract top talent and incentivize employees to stay.
She exercised her stock options when the market price exceeded the option price.

Related Terms

  • synonym: option - a contract giving the right but not the obligation to buy or sell assets
  • synonym: share option - option to buy shares, especially as employee incentive (UK equivalent)
