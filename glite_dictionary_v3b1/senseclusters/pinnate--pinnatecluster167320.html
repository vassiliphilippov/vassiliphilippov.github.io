pinnate

adjective
featherlike, leaflets on both sides of a stem
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having leaflets arranged on either side of a common axis, resembling a feather.

Examples

Pinnate leaflets
Acacia trees are known for their delicate, pinnate foliage.
Many ferns display a clearly pinnate pattern along their fronds.
The rose has pinnate leaves, each consisting of several small leaflets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: palmate - (of a leaf) having lobes or leaflets radiating from a single point
