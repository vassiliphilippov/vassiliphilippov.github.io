introduction

noun
opening section of a communication
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the first section of a book, speech, essay, or other communication, which sets the stage for what follows

Examples

Introduction of a book
Her speech began with a brief introduction to the topic.
Please read the introduction before starting the first chapter.
The book’s introduction explains the author’s main arguments and objectives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conclusion - the last part of a book or speech, summarizing or ending it
  • synonym: preface - an introduction written by the author to a book
