deeds

noun
moral or religious actions
1.0
Pronunciation: / didz / (AmE), / diːdz / (BrE)
actions that are considered morally or religiously significant, often viewed as deserving merit or reward.

Examples

Good deeds
His deeds were remembered long after his words were forgotten.
Many religions emphasize the importance of performing deeds that help others.
She believed that only through good deeds could one achieve salvation.

  • antonym: sins - immoral acts considered wrong by religious or moral laws
