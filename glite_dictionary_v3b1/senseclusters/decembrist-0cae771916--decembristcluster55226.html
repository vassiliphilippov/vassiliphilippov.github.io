Decembrist

noun
Russian revolutionary
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛmbrɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmbrɪst / (BrE)
a member of a group of Russian revolutionaries who led an unsuccessful uprising in December 1825

Examples

Russian Decembrist rebel
The Decembrist movement inspired later generations of Russian reformers.
Many Decembrists were exiled to Siberia after the failed uprising.
Historians still debate the impact of the Decembrist revolt on Russian society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to the established government
  • synonym: revolutionary - a person who works for or participates in a revolution
