mournful

adjective
expressing sadness or sorrow
3.9 1.97/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…îrn.f…ôl / (AmE), / Ààm…îÀên.f…ôl / (BrE)
feeling, showing, or expressing sadness or sorrow

Examples

Mournful expression
He looked at the empty chair with a mournful expression on his face.
The music had a mournful tone that brought tears to many listeners.
There was a mournful silence after the announcement of her passing.
His mournful expression spoke louder than words.
She gave a mournful sigh as she looked at the empty playground.
The mournful music made everyone feel the depth of his sorrow.

Synonyms

  • doleful
    8.2 0.30/m
    doleful is a formal, somewhat old-fashioned word that shows clear sadness, while mournful is a general word for expressing sorrow.
  • sorrowful
    3.6 1.25/m
    sorrowful shows deep sadness often because of loss, while mournful describes showing sorrow in many situations.
  • melancholy
    5.9 3.11/m
    melancholy describes a deep, quiet gloom or mood, while mournful focuses on showing or expressing sadness.
  • grieving
    3.4 0.00/m
    grieving is the strong sorrow felt after a death or loss, while mournful can mean any visible sadness.
  • plaintive
    7.9 1.24/m
    plaintive sounds sad and often refers to a voice or tone, while mournful means showing sorrow more generally.
  • sad
    1.0 40/m
    sad is a simple everyday word for feeling unhappy, while mournful is more formal and often sounds serious or solemn.
