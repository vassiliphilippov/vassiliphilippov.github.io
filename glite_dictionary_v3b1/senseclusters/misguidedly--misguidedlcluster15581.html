misguidedly

adverb
with poor judgment
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈɡaɪdɪdli / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡaɪdɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that shows poor judgment or is based on incorrect beliefs or information

Examples

To misguidedly trust advice
She misguidedly trusted the advice of someone she barely knew.
They misguidedly thought skipping breakfast would help them lose weight.
The company misguidedly invested in a failing market despite warnings.
He misguidedly trusted the rumors and made a poor decision.
The team misguidedly believed that the shortcut would save time, but it caused delays.
She misguidedly thought her actions would help, but they only made things worse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: correctly - in a way that is right or accurate
  • synonym: foolishly - in a way that lacks good sense or judgment
  • antonym: wisely - in a way that shows good judgment or sense
  • synonym: wrongly - in an incorrect or mistaken way
