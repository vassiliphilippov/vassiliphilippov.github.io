tartar

noun
hard deposit on teeth and gums
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
a hard, yellowish deposit that forms on the teeth and gums, often leading to dental problems if not removed

Examples

Dental tartar buildup
A dental hygienist can remove stubborn tartar during a routine cleaning.
Brushing twice a day helps prevent tartar buildup on your teeth.
If tartar is not removed, it can lead to gum disease and cavities.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: plaque - a soft, sticky film that accumulates on the teeth
