maltose

noun
a sugar formed from starch digestion
5.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔːl.toʊs/ (AmE), /ˈmɔːl.təʊs/ (BrE)
a white, crystalline sugar produced by the breakdown of starch; a disaccharide consisting of two glucose units

Examples

White maltose sugar
Biologists studied how maltose behaves when exposed to heat and different enzymes.
During the fermentation process, starch is broken down into maltose and then converted to alcohol by yeast.
Saliva contains an enzyme that helps convert starch into maltose during digestion.
