factor

noun
commission merchant or agent
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
a person or company that acts as an agent in buying or selling goods for others, usually for a commission

Examples

A factor for trade
As a factor, she negotiated prices and ensured delivery for several small producers.
In colonial times, a factor managed trade between remote settlements and large companies.
The merchant hired a factor to handle the export of his goods to foreign markets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another
  • antonym: principal - the person whom an agent represents
