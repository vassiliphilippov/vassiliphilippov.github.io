pre-exposure

adjective
before exposure
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː ɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˌpriː ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
happening or provided before exposure to a substance, disease, or other risk

Examples

Pre-exposure vaccine
The doctor recommended a pre-exposure vaccine for travelers visiting high-risk areas.
Researchers collected pre-exposure blood samples to compare with results after the experiment.
A pre-exposure briefing was held to inform workers about potential chemical hazards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: post-exposure - occurring after exposure to a substance or risk
