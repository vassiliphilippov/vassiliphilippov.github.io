nondistinctive

adjective
not distinguishing meaning
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˈstɪŋktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈstɪŋktɪv / (BrE)
not serving to distinguish meaning or create contrasts between words, sounds, or grammatical forms, especially in linguistics

Examples

Nondistinctive word stress
In some languages, stress is nondistinctive and does not affect the meaning of words.
The sound was considered nondistinctive because it did not change the word's meaning.
Vowel length is nondistinctive in this dialect, so both forms are understood the same way.
