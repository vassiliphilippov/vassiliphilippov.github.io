phenomenalism

noun
philosophy of appearances
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑːmənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / fəˈnɒmɪnəˌlɪzəm /; / fɪˈnɒmɪnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical theory that we can only know things through our sensory experiences, and that physical objects exist only as perceptions

Examples

Phenomenalism theory
In her philosophy class, they debated whether phenomenalism offers a complete account of human knowledge.
Some philosophers argue that phenomenalism cannot explain how we talk about things we cannot perceive.
The theory of phenomenalism suggests that reality is only what we can experience through our senses.
In philosophy class, we discussed how phenomenalism challenges the idea of an objective physical world.
Some thinkers argue that phenomenalism cannot fully explain the persistence of objects when unobserved.
According to phenomenalism, a tree exists only as a set of possible perceptions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empiricism - the theory that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
  • synonym: idealism - the philosophical theory that reality is fundamentally mental or immaterial
  • antonym: realism - the belief that objects exist independently of perception
