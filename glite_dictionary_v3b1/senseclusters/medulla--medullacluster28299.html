medulla

noun
fatty sheath of nerve fibers
Pronunciation: / məˈdʌlə / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌlə / (BrE)
a white fatty substance that forms a protective sheath (medullary sheath) around the central part of some nerve fibers

Examples

Nerve medulla sheath
Damage to the medulla can disrupt nerve signal transmission in the body.
Scientists studied the role of the medulla in neurological disorders such as multiple sclerosis.
The medulla is essential for the rapid conduction of electrical impulses along nerve cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: myelin - the insulating layer, or sheath, that forms around nerves
