dial-up

noun
slow internet connection
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəl ʌp / (BrE)
an internet connection that uses a telephone line and is typically much slower than broadband

Examples

A dial-up modem
In the 1990s, most people accessed the internet using dial-up at home.
It can take several minutes to download a single photo with dial-up.
Many rural areas still rely on dial-up because broadband is unavailable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broadband - a high-speed internet connection
  • synonym: modem - a device that connects a computer to the internet over a telephone line
