Old World warbler

noun
a type of small Old World songbird
Pronunciation: / oʊld wɜrld ˈwɔrbɚ / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
a small, active bird native to the Old World, typically brownish or greyish, belonging to several related families of songbirds

Examples

The Old World warbler species
During their migration, several species of Old World warbler pass through the wetlands every spring.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of the Old World warbler in southern Europe.
The garden was alive with the song of an Old World warbler perched in the hedgerow.
