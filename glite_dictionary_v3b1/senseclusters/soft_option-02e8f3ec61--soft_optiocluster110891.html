soft option

noun
an easier alternative
5.8
Pronunciation: / sɑft ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / sɒft ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
an easier course of action or alternative compared to others, often chosen to avoid difficulty or effort

Examples

The soft option
Choosing to apologize was certainly the soft option, but it avoided further conflict.
He was accused of taking the soft option instead of facing up to his responsibilities.
Many students prefer the soft option of taking an easier subject rather than a challenging one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hard option - a more difficult alternative or course of action
