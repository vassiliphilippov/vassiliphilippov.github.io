forcible

adjective
done using physical force
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsɪb(ə)l / (BrE)
done using physical force, especially against resistance or without consent.

Examples

A forcible eviction
A forcible eviction took place after the tenants refused to leave the property.
During the protest, there were several reports of forcible removal of demonstrators.
The police made a forcible entry into the apartment after hearing screams inside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coercive - using force or threats to make someone do something
  • antonym: voluntary - done by choice, without force
