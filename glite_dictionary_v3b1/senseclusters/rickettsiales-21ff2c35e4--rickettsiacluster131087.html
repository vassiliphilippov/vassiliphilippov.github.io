Rickettsiales

noun
order of pathogenic Gram-negative bacteria
9.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈkets.iː.eɪ.liːz/ /; / /rɪ.ˈkets.iː.eɪlz/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈkets.iː.eɪ.liːz/ /; / /rɪ.ˈkets.iː.eɪlz/ (BrE)
an order of small, pleomorphic Gram-negative bacteria that are typically pathogenic to humans and other animals

Examples

Rickettsiales bacteria group
Rickettsiales are responsible for several serious diseases, including typhus and Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
Because many Rickettsiales are obligate intracellular parasites, they are difficult to culture in the lab.
The laboratory specializes in the identification and study of Rickettsiales bacteria.

  • synonym: Rickettsia - genus of bacteria within Rickettsiales, many species pathogenic to humans
