northern porgy

noun
North American marine food fish
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrðərn ˈpɔrɡi / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈpɔːɡi / (BrE)
A North American marine fish (Stenotomus chrysops) found in Atlantic coastal waters from South Carolina to Maine, valued as a food fish.

Examples

The northern porgy fish
Anglers appreciate the fighting spirit of the northern porgy when hooked on light tackle.
Fishermen often catch northern porgy along the coasts of New England in the summer.
The restaurant specials included grilled northern porgy served with lemon and herbs.
