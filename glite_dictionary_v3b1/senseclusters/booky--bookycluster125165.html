booky

adjective
fond of reading
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊki / (AmE), / ˈbʊki / (BrE)
describing someone who enjoys reading or is studious

Examples

A booky child
As a child, she was very booky and loved spending hours in the library.
His booky nature made him a favorite among teachers.
People often called him booky because he always had a novel in his bag.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bookish - enjoying reading or studying; studious
  • synonym: studious - spending a lot of time studying or reading
