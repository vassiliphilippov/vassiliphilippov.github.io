indeed

adverb
used to emphasize or confirm a statement
1.0 148/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàdid / (AmE), / …™nÀàdiÀêd / (BrE)
used to emphasize a statement or response, or to confirm and stress the truth of a preceding statement

Examples

Very cold indeed
It was cold indeed when we arrived at the mountain cabin.
She is indeed one of the most talented artists of her generation.
The new policy is, indeed, a significant improvement over the old one.

Synonyms

  • in truth
    3.8 7.08/m
    in truth says something is really true, often in formal speech, while indeed is a common word that confirms and stresses a point.
  • in fact
    1.0 84/m
    in fact introduces a true or extra detail, often to add or correct, while indeed more often simply confirms what was said.
  • actually
    1.0 524/m
    actually points out what is real or surprising, often to correct a wrong idea, while indeed mainly confirms a previous statement.
  • certainly
    1.0 226/m
    certainly shows strong surety or agreement, often as a direct answer, while indeed is mainly used to emphasize or confirm a point.
  • as a matter of fact
    3.0 10/m
    as a matter of fact is a conversational phrase that often corrects or adds detail, while indeed is shorter and more neutral.
  • in point of fact
    5.6 0.24/m
    in point of fact is a formal or old-fashioned phrase for adding a factual correction, while indeed is more common and concise.
