nephropore

noun
embryonic kidney opening
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛfrəˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈnɛfrəˌpɔː / (BrE)
an opening in the body of some animals (such as embryos or invertebrates) through which waste is excreted

Examples

The nephropore opening
The nephropore is essential for removing waste from the developing embryo.
Scientists observed the flow of fluid through the nephropore during early development.
A blocked nephropore can lead to serious health issues in some aquatic animals.
The earthworm expels waste through its nephropore.
Scientists observed the movement of fluids out of the nephropore during the experiment.
Each segment of the worm contains a nephropore for excreting waste products.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anus - the opening at the end of the digestive tract for waste elimination
  • synonym: nephridium - an excretory organ in some invertebrates
