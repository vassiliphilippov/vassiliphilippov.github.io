chemical equilibrium

noun
state where forward and reverse chemical reactions balance
5.1 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…õm…™k…ôl Àåikw…ôÀàl…™bri…ôm / (AmE), / Àåk…õm…™k…ôl ÀåiÀêkw…™Ààl…™br…™…ôm / (BrE)
a state in a chemical reaction in which the forward and reverse reactions occur at the same rate, so the concentrations of reactants and products remain constant

Examples

Chemical equilibrium state
Adding more reactant can disturb the chemical equilibrium and cause the reaction to shift.
At chemical equilibrium, the rate of the forward reaction equals that of the reverse reaction.
The reaction eventually reaches chemical equilibrium, at which point the concentrations of all substances remain constant.
