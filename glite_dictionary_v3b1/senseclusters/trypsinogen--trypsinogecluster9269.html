trypsinogen

noun
inactive precursor of trypsin enzyme
Pronunciation: / trɪpˈsɪnədʒən / (AmE), / trɪpˈsɪnədʒən / (BrE)
An inactive precursor of the enzyme trypsin, secreted by the pancreas and converted to active trypsin in the small intestine.

Examples

Inactive trypsinogen enzyme
Low levels of trypsinogen in the blood can indicate pancreatic disease.
Researchers studied the genetic mutations affecting trypsinogen production in hereditary pancreatitis.
The pancreas releases trypsinogen into the digestive tract, where it is activated to trypsin.
