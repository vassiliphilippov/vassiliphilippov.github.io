vitiate

verb
to corrupt morally
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to harm or corrupt morally, especially by introducing moral weakness or sensual indulgence

Examples

To vitiate morals
Critics argued that the new policy might vitiate students' sense of discipline.
Excess luxury and indulgence can vitiate one's character over time.
She worried that the city's nightlife would vitiate the morals of young people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupt - to make someone or something morally depraved
  • synonym: debauch - to destroy or debase the moral purity of; corrupt
