wireworm

noun
larva of click beetles; crop pest
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪərˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the slender, tough larva of various click beetles (family Elateridae), which feeds on the roots of plants and can be a serious crop pest

Examples

The wireworm larva
Effective control measures are needed to prevent wireworm infestations in newly planted fields.
Gardeners often check the soil for wireworms before planting sensitive vegetables like carrots.
The farmer discovered that wireworms were damaging the potato crop by eating their roots.
