Yellowhammer

noun
UK government no-deal Brexit contingency plan (codename)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊˌhæmər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌhæmə / (BrE)
(UK, politics) the codename for a UK government contingency plan prepared for the possibility of a no-deal Brexit; used to refer to the planning document or its findings.

Examples

Yellowhammer report
The government released its Yellowhammer report outlining expected disruptions in a no-deal Brexit scenario.
Opposition MPs criticised the secrecy around Yellowhammer, demanding full publication of contingency planning documents.
Journalists cited the leaked Yellowhammer papers when reporting possible fuel shortages and supply chain delays.
