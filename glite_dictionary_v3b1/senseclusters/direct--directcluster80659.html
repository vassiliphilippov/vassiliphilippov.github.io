direct

verb
aim or point something at a target
1.3
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt, daɪˈrɛkt /; / dɪˈrɛkt, daɪ- / (AmE), / dəˈrɛkt, daɪˈrɛkt /; / dɪˈrɛkt, daɪ- / (BrE)
to aim, point, or focus something (such as an object, attention, or emotion) toward a particular target or person

Examples

To direct attention
He directed the spotlight onto the stage to highlight the main actor.
Please direct your attention to the left side of the display.
She directed her camera at the unusual bird in the tree.
Please direct any comments or questions to the manager after the presentation.
She tried to direct her energy towards finishing the project rather than complaining.
The coach directed his anger at the team's poor performance during the game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aim - to point a weapon or device at a target
  • antonym: misdirect - to aim or focus something toward the wrong target
  • synonym: train - to point or aim something, especially a device, at a target
