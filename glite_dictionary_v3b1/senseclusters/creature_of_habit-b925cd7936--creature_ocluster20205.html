creature of habit

noun
person who sticks to the same routine
Pronunciation: / ˈkritʃɚ əv ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈkriːtʃər əv ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
a person who tends to follow the same routine or habits consistently, often resisting change

Examples

A creature of habit
Although she travels often, she remains a creature of habit, always unpacking her suitcase in the same way.
As a creature of habit, Mr. Evans parks in the same spot at work every day.
Ever since college, he has been a creature of habit who eats breakfast at exactly 7 a.m. every morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creature of impulse - a person who acts on sudden desires rather than habits
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports tradition and refuses to change
