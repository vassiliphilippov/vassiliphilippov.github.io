summon

verb
gather or bring together (feelings, qualities, etc.)
6.2 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌm.ən/ (AmE), /ˈsʌm.ən/ (BrE)
to gather or bring together qualities, strength, or resources, often with effort

Examples

To summon courage
Despite her doubts, she summoned the determination to continue her studies.
He had to summon all his strength to finish the marathon.
She tried to summon the courage to speak in front of the large audience.
In times of crisis, communities often summon remarkable resilience.
She tried to summon the courage to speak in front of the large audience.
The commander summoned all available resources to deal with the emergency.
In times of crisis, people often summon their inner strength to cope with challenges.
She tried to summon up the courage to speak in front of the audience.
The old photograph summoned memories of his childhood summers by the lake.
