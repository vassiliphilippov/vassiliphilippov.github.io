bishop's pine

noun
a pine tree native to California coast
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪʃəps paɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəps paɪn / (BrE)
a species of pine tree (Pinus muricata) native to the northwestern coast of California, typically with two or three needles per bundle and known for its use in landscaping and reforestation.

Examples

Bishop's pine tree
Many conservationists are working to protect the last natural stands of bishop's pine.
Rare birds sometimes nest in the branches of the bishop's pine.
The coastal fog provides an ideal environment for the growth of the bishop's pine.
