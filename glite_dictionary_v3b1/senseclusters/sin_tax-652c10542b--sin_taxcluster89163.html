sin tax

noun
tax on harmful or immoral products
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪn tæks / (AmE), / sɪn tæks / (BrE)
A government tax imposed on products or activities considered harmful or immoral, such as alcohol, tobacco, or sugary drinks.

Examples

A sin tax on alcohol
Alcohol and tobacco are often subject to a sin tax to offset their public health costs.
Debates continue over whether a sin tax is effective in reducing smoking rates.
The government imposed a sin tax on sugary drinks to discourage excessive consumption.
