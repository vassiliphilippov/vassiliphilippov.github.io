fiber

noun
indigestible plant material in food
1.9 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.bər/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.bə/ (BrE)
Plant material that cannot be digested but helps move food through the digestive system by adding bulk.

Examples

Dietary fiber
Foods high in fiber can help prevent constipation and maintain healthy digestion.
Nutritionists recommend eating plenty of vegetables to boost your daily fiber intake.
Whole grains are a good source of dietary fiber and promote a feeling of fullness.
A high-fibre diet can help prevent constipation and other digestive issues.
Whole grains, fruits, and vegetables are good sources of dietary fibre.
You should eat plenty of fibre to maintain a healthy digestive system.
