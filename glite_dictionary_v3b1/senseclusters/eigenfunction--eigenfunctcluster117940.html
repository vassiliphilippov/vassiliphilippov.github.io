eigenfunction

noun
function unchanged by operator
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪɡənˌfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈaɪɡənˌfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a function that, when acted on by a specific operator, is only multiplied by a constant and not otherwise changed

Examples

An eigenfunction of operator
In quantum mechanics, the wave function of a particle can be an eigenfunction of the energy operator.
Solving the equation, we found that the sine function is an eigenfunction of the differential operator.
Each eigenfunction corresponds to a specific energy level in the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eigenvalue - the constant by which an eigenfunction or eigenvector is multiplied
  • synonym: eigenvector - a vector that is only scaled, not changed in direction, by a given transformation
