overglaze

verb
to add enamel layer
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.ɡleɪz/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.ɡleɪz/ (BrE)
to apply a decorative or protective layer of enamel or pigment over an existing glaze on ceramics, usually followed by another firing

Examples

To overglaze a plate
The artist chose to overglaze the vase with a bright red pigment for a glossy finish.
After the first firing, you should overglaze the plate to add decorative patterns.
Ceramic students learned how to overglaze their pieces to achieve different textures and effects.
