overburden

noun
soil/rock layer above mineral deposits
7.1 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.bɜːr.dən/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.bɜː.dən/ (BrE)
the layer of soil and rock covering a mineral deposit, especially one that must be removed before mining can proceed

Examples

Thick overburden layer
Before mining could begin, the crew had to remove the thick overburden from the site.
Excavators worked for weeks to clear the overburden and expose the mineral deposit beneath.
The cost of stripping the overburden often determines whether a coal seam is economical to extract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subsoil - the layer of soil beneath the topsoil
