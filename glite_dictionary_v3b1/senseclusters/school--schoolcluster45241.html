school

noun
period of instruction at an educational institution
1.3
Pronunciation: / skuːl / (AmE), / skuːl / (BrE)
a place where children or young people receive education, or the people, time, or process involved in education

Examples

At school every day
After school, the students played soccer on the field.
He forgot his lunchbox at school and had to go back for it.
I usually feel tired at the end of the school day.
Our local school is known for its excellent music program.
She started a new job teaching at a private school.
The children walk to school every morning.
The school celebrated its victory at the regional sports competition.
The entire school gathered in the auditorium for the assembly.
When the school voted, the majority supported extending lunch hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: academy - an institution of learning or higher study
  • synonym: college - an educational institution for higher learning
  • synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction
  • degree lower: kindergarten - a school for young children before they enter primary school
  • degree higher: university - an institution for higher education and research
  • antonym: vacation - a break or period when school is not in session
  • antonym: work - employment, especially as the next phase after school
