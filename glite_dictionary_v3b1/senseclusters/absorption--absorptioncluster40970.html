absorption

noun
state of being engrossed
7.2
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔɹpʃən / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the state of being deeply engrossed or giving complete attention to something

Examples

Absorption in work
His complete absorption in his work often causes him to lose track of time.
Julia’s absorption in her book was so intense that she did not hear the doorbell.
The children watched the magician with total absorption.
His absorption in the book made him forget about the time.
She listened to the story with total absorption, barely noticing anything around her.
The scientist worked in complete absorption, unaware of the noisy lab.
