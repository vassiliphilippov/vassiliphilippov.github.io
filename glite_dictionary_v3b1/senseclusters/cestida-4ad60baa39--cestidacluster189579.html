Cestida

noun
order of ribbon-like ctenophores
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɛsˈtaɪdə / (AmE), / sɛsˈtiːdə / (BrE)
A small order of ctenophores (marine invertebrates), characterized by ribbon-like bodies and short tentacles; includes one family.

Examples

A Cestida specimen
Scientists observed a Cestida specimen displaying bioluminescence under laboratory conditions.
The Cestida are known for their delicate, ribbon-shaped bodies gliding gracefully through the water.
Unlike other ctenophores, the members of the Cestida have considerably shorter tentacles.
