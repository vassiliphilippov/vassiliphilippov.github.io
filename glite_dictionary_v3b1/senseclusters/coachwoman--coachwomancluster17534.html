coachwoman

noun
female coach or carriage driver
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊtʃˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtʃˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman whose job is to drive a coach or carriage.

Examples

The coachwoman drives horses
Dressed smartly in a top hat, the coachwoman expertly guided the horses along the winding road.
In Victorian England, it was rare to see a coachwoman steering a carriage through busy city streets.
The wealthy family hired a skilled coachwoman to transport guests between country estates.
