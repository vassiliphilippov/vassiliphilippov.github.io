Mohammed Ali

proper noun
viceroy of Egypt, founder of modern Egypt
8.6
Pronunciation: / moʊˈhæməd ˈɑːli / (AmE), / məˈhæmɪd ˈɑːli / (BrE)
(1769–1849) Albanian soldier and statesman who became viceroy of Egypt, wrested control from the Ottoman Empire, and established the foundations of modern Egypt.

Examples

Mohammed Ali of Egypt
Mohammed Ali is often regarded as the founder of modern Egypt for his extensive economic and military reforms.
Historians credit Mohammed Ali with weakening Ottoman control over Egypt and introducing significant modernization.
The dynasty established by Mohammed Ali ruled Egypt until the mid-20th century.
