Synonyms

  • absolute
    3.9 11/m
    absolute is fully complete and often used in formal statements, while utter is a common emphatic adjective in speech.
  • total
    1.0 136/m
    total means complete and including everything, often about amounts or sums, while utter stresses absolute extent or degree.
  • complete
    2.8 13/m
    complete describes something with no parts missing or full in itself, while utter is a stronger emphatic before nouns.
  • outright
    4.2 2.41/m
    outright means completely or immediately, often about actions or results, while utter is a general intensifier for qualities.
  • unadulterated
    6.9 0.40/m
    unadulterated means pure and not mixed, used to describe a thing's quality, while utter is broader and means complete.
  • pure
    3.9 14/m
    pure stresses lack of mixture or moral clarity, often positive or negative, while utter simply stresses total degree without purity.
  • arrant
    9.9 0.10/m
    arrant is used angrily for very bad things and feels formal or old-fashioned, while utter is more general and common.
