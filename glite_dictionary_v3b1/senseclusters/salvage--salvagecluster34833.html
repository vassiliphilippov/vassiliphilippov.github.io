salvage

verb
save from ruin or destruction
Pronunciation: / ˈsæl.vɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsæl.vɪdʒ / (BrE)
to save, recover, or collect something from danger, destruction, or from discarded or damaged sources

Examples

To salvage data
Engineers worked overnight to salvage critical data from the damaged hard drive.
She tried to salvage her reputation after the scandal broke out.
The firefighters managed to salvage a few family photos from the burning house.
After the storm, volunteers helped salvage wood and supplies from damaged buildings.
She managed to salvage several old chairs from the curbside trash.
Workers were able to salvage usable metal parts from the abandoned factory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to fail to keep or maintain; to no longer have possession
  • synonym: rescue - to save from danger or distress
  • synonym: scavenge - to search through waste for usable items
