salvage

verb
save from ruin or destruction
5.1 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsæl.vɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈsæl.vədʒ/ (AmE), /ˈsæl.vɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈsæl.vədʒ/ (BrE)
to save, recover, or collect something from danger, destruction, or from discarded or damaged sources

Examples

To salvage data
Engineers worked overnight to salvage critical data from the damaged hard drive.

She managed to salvage several old chairs from the curbside trash.
Workers were able to salvage usable metal parts from the abandoned factory.
