precontest

noun
event before contest
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌkɑntɛst / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌkɒntɛst / (BrE)
an event, activity, or period that takes place before a main contest, often to prepare or select participants

Examples

A precontest event
The organizers held a precontest to determine which teams would advance to the main event.
All participants must register during the precontest period to be eligible for the finals.
The precontest activities helped the students prepare for the big competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: final - the last and most important round of a contest
  • synonym: preliminary - an event or round that comes before the main one
