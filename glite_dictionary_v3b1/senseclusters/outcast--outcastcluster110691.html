outcast

verb
to exclude from group
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to expel or exclude someone from a group or community, making them an outcast

Examples

To outcast a member
After the incident, the community began outcasting him, leaving him completely isolated.
She felt the pain of being outcast by her former friends at school.
The group was criticized for outcasting anyone who disagreed with their views.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to include or welcome someone into a group
  • synonym: ostracize - to exclude someone from a group or society
