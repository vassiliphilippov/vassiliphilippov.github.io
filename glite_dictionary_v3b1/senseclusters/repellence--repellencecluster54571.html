repellence

noun
force pushing apart
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛləns / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛl.əns / (BrE)
the force or quality that causes objects or particles to push away from each other, especially in physics or chemistry.

Examples

Magnetic repellence
The repellence between the two magnets made it impossible to push them together.
Scientists studied the repellence of charged particles in the laboratory experiment.
Increasing the voltage increased the repellence observed between the objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attraction - the force that pulls objects or particles together
  • synonym: repulsion - the act of pushing away or the force that drives things apart
