inexplicably

adverb
without explanation
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪkˈsplɪkəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈsplɪkəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be explained or understood

Examples

The train inexplicably stopped
The train inexplicably stopped in the middle of the tunnel for several minutes.
She felt inexplicably nervous before the interview, even though she was well prepared.
The painting was inexplicably missing from the gallery when the doors opened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to understand or explain
  • synonym: mysteriously - in a way that is difficult to understand or explain
