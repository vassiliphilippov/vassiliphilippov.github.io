degradative

adjective
causing deterioration
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡreɪdəˌtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdətɪv / (BrE)
causing deterioration or a reduction in quality, performance, or effectiveness of something

Examples

Degradative effect
The chemical had a degradative effect on the metal, causing it to weaken over time.
Researchers studied the degradative processes that break down plastics in the environment.
Continuous exposure to moisture can have a degradative impact on wooden structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destructive - causing damage or harm
  • antonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health or strength
