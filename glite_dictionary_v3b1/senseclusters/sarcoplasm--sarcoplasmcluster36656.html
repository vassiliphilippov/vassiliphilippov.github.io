sarcoplasm

noun
cytoplasm of muscle fiber
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑr.kəˌplæz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.kəʊˌplæz.əm / (BrE)
the cytoplasm of a striated muscle fiber, containing organelles and dissolved substances essential for muscle contraction

Examples

The sarcoplasm of muscle
Compared to other cells, the sarcoplasm is rich in glycogen and myoglobin.
During muscle contraction, calcium ions move within the sarcoplasm.
The sarcoplasm contains many enzymes needed for muscle metabolism.
