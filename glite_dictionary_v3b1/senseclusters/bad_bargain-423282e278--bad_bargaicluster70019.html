bad bargain

noun
a poor or disadvantageous deal
Pronunciation: / bæd ˈbɑrɡən / (AmE), / bæd ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
a deal or purchase that results in an unfavorable or disadvantageous outcome

Examples

A bad bargain
Don't let flashy advertising trick you into making a bad bargain.
She realized too late that the used laptop was a bad bargain.
Some say the peace treaty was a bad bargain for the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: good bargain - a deal or purchase that is advantageous or favorable
