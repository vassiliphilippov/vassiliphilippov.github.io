overharvest

noun
excessive harvesting
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈhɑrvəst / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhɑːvɪst / (BrE)
the act or amount of harvesting a resource beyond sustainable levels, leading to depletion or long-term harm.

Examples

Overharvest of fish
The overharvest of fish in the lake has led to a dramatic decline in their population.
Strict regulations were introduced to prevent the overharvest of valuable timber species.
Scientists warned that continued overharvest could cause the extinction of several local plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overexploitation - the excessive use of a resource to the point of depletion
  • antonym: sustainable harvest - harvesting at a rate that does not harm future availability
