Dixiecrats

proper noun
U.S. Southern Democratic political party (1948)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪk.siː.kræts/ /; / /ˈdɪk.sɪ.kræts/ (AmE), /ˈdɪk.siː.kræts/ /; / /ˈdɪk.sɪ.kræts/ (BrE)
A former political party in the United States, formed in 1948 by Southern Democrats who opposed the civil rights policies of President Harry S. Truman.

Examples

The Dixiecrats party
Many former Dixiecrats later joined the Republican Party as political alignments shifted in the South.
The Dixiecrats nominated Strom Thurmond as their presidential candidate in 1948.
The rise of the Dixiecrats marked a major split within the Democratic Party during the mid-20th century.
