navigation

noun
movement of ships or boats
1.1 3.0
Pronunciation: /næ.və.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /næ.vɪ.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /næ.vɪ.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /næ.və.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of planning and controlling the movement of vehicles (especially ships, but also aircraft or cars) from one place to another

Examples

Ship navigation
Due to the dense fog, navigation through the strait was temporarily suspended.
The canal was cleared of debris to allow navigation by larger vessels.
Winter storms often disrupt navigation along the northern coastline.
He studied navigation before joining the merchant navy.
Modern navigation requires not only skill but also technological knowledge.
The art of navigation has evolved greatly since the age of sail.
Modern cars often include satellite navigation systems to assist drivers.
Precise navigation is essential when flying through poor weather conditions.
The crew relied on advanced technology to improve their navigation at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pilotage - the act of navigating, especially by visual reference to landmarks
  • synonym: seamanship - skill or work involved in operating a ship
  • synonym: shipping - the transport of goods or people by sea
  • synonym: steering - control of the direction in which a vehicle, ship, or aircraft moves
