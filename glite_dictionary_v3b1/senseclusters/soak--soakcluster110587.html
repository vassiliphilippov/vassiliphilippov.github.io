Synonyms

  • immerse
    4.2 1/m
    immerse is to put something fully under liquid, while soak also covers pouring or slow absorption over time.
  • saturate
    5.2 8/m
    saturate means to fill something so fully that it can take no more liquid, while soak can simply make something very wet.
  • drench
    3.0 3/m
    drench makes something completely wet quickly by pouring or heavy rain, while soak can also mean slow absorption.
  • douse
    4.4 1/m
    douse means to pour liquid over something suddenly, while soak also includes leaving something in liquid to absorb.
  • souse
    8.8 0.01/m
    souse is an informal older word for drenching or immersing, while soak is common modern everyday language.
  • steep
    3.5 0.9/m
    steep means to soak in liquid to extract flavor or cleanse, while soak has broader use for wetting things.
  • sop
    8.3 0.08/m
    sop soaks or wets using a small piece of bread or cloth, while soak means making an object very wet in general.
