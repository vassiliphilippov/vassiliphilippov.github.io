soak

verb
make thoroughly wet or saturated
2.6 1.0
Pronunciation: / soʊk / (AmE), / səʊk / (BrE)
to make something very wet by immersing it in liquid, pouring liquid over it, or letting it absorb liquid

Examples

To soak a sponge
Be sure to soak the bandage with enough disinfectant before applying it to the wound.
The team needed to soak the filter in cleaning solution overnight for best results.
To remove the stain, you should soak the cloth completely with vinegar.
After a long run, I soaked my feet in warm water to relax.
She soaked the cloth in cold water before placing it on his forehead.
The rain soaked everyone who was standing outside without an umbrella.

Related Terms

  • synonym: saturate - to cause something to become thoroughly soaked with liquid
