stratigraphical

adjective
rock layer analysis
Pronunciation: / ˌstrætɪˈɡræfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌstrætɪˈɡræfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the layers of rock or soil and their arrangement or study

Examples

A stratigraphical study
The stratigraphical study helped scientists understand the sequence of ancient volcanic eruptions.
Archaeologists used stratigraphical methods to date the artifacts found at the site.
A stratigraphical map shows the different layers of rock beneath the surface.
The stratigraphical analysis revealed several distinct layers of volcanic ash in the region.
Researchers used stratigraphical methods to date the fossils found at the site.
A stratigraphical approach helps archaeologists understand the sequence of human occupation in ancient settlements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronological - relating to the arrangement of events in time
  • synonym: geological - relating to the science of the Earth's physical structure
  • synonym: sedimentary - relating to material that settles at the bottom of a liquid, especially rock layers
