fixed-point part

noun
fractional component of fixed-point number
8.4
Pronunciation: /fɪkst.pɔɪnt pɑːrt/ (AmE), /fɪkst.pɔɪnt pɑːt/ (BrE)
In computing, the fractional component of a fixed-point number — the digits or bits that appear after the radix (decimal) point and determine fractional precision.

Examples

Fixed-point part after the decimal
When using Q15.16 format, the fixed-point part determines fractional precision for arithmetic operations.
To avoid overflow, adjust the scale so the fixed-point part fits within the available fractional bits.
During testing they compared results against floating-point and found the fixed-point part rounded differently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fractional part - the part of a number after the decimal or radix point
  • antonym: integer part - the part of a number before the decimal or radix point; the complement of the fractional component
