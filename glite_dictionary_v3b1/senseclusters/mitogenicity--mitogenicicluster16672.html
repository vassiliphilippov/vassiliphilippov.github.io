mitogenicity

noun
ability to trigger mitosis
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪtəˌdʒəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌmaɪtəʊdʒəˈnɪsɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance to stimulate cell division by mitosis

Examples

The mitogenicity of factor
The mitogenicity of the growth factor was tested on cultured human cells.
Scientists are studying the mitogenicity of various proteins to understand cancer development.
High mitogenicity can indicate that a compound promotes rapid cell division.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cytotoxicity - the quality of being toxic to cells
  • synonym: proliferative - having the ability to multiply or increase in number
