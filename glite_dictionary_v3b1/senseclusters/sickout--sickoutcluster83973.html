sickout

noun
mass illness absence
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪkˌaʊt / (BrE)
a situation where many people are absent from work or school due to genuine illness, not as a form of protest

Examples

Flu sickout
A severe flu season led to a sickout that closed several offices for days.
The hospital struggled to operate normally during the unexpected sickout among its staff.
After a sickout swept through the school, classes were canceled for the rest of the week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absenteeism - frequent absence from work or school
  • antonym: walkout - a protest in which workers leave their workplace as a group
