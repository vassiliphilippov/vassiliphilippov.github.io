Ophrys insectifera

noun
European orchid with fly-shaped flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈɑfrɪs ɪnˌsɛktɪˈfɪrə / (AmE), / ˈɒfrɪs ɪnˌsɛktɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A European orchid species whose flowers closely resemble flies, aiding in pollination.

Examples

Ophrys insectifera flower
Botanists are fascinated by the unique pollination strategy of Ophrys insectifera.
The Ophrys insectifera attracts pollinators by mimicking the appearance of female flies.
We saw several Ophrys insectifera growing wild in the damp meadows of southern England.
