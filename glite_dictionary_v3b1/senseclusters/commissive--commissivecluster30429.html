commissive

adjective
committing speech act
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
describing a speech act in which the speaker commits to doing something in the future

Examples

Commissive speech act
A promise is a classic example of a commissive speech act.
When you say 'I will help you,' you are making a commissive statement.
In linguistics, offers and vows are considered types of commissive acts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative - describing a speech act that states or declares something
  • antonym: directive - describing a speech act that aims to get someone to do something
