quaggy

adjective
soft, watery, spongy (soil or ground)
Pronunciation: / ˈkwæɡi / (AmE), / ˈkwæɡi / (BrE)
(of soil or ground) soft, spongy, and wet; resembling a quagmire.

Examples

A quaggy patch
A quaggy patch behind the barn made it impossible to park vehicles there in spring.
After the rainstorm, the field became so quaggy that walking across it was a challenge.
The explorers avoided the quaggy banks of the river, wary of sinking into the mud.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boggy - (of ground) wet and muddy
  • synonym: marshy - (of land) resembling a marsh; wet and soft
