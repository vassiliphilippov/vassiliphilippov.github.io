pull at

verb
to pluck or tug lightly with fingers
3.2
Pronunciation: / pʊl æt / (AmE), / pʊl æt / (BrE)
to pluck or repeatedly tug at something lightly with the fingers, especially in a nervous or distracted manner

Examples

To pull at a sleeve
During the interview, she nervously pulled at her sleeve.
He absentmindedly pulled at his beard while thinking.
The child began to pull at the loose thread on his sweater.
