recidivist

noun
person who repeatedly offends criminally
8.0
Pronunciation: / rəˈsɪdəˌvɪst /; / rɪˈsɪdəvɪst / (AmE), / rəˈsɪdəvɪst /; / rɪˈsɪdəvɪst / (BrE)
a person who repeatedly commits crimes or bad behavior, especially after being punished

Examples

Known recidivist criminal
As a known recidivist, he faced a harsher sentence for his latest conviction.
Efforts to rehabilitate recidivists often focus on addressing the root causes of their behavior.
The judge expressed concern that the defendant was a recidivist with a long criminal record.
Because she was a known recidivist, the sentence was more severe than usual.
Many recidivists struggle to adjust to life outside prison.
The judge referred to him as a recidivist who had shown no signs of changing his ways.

Related Terms

  • antonym: first offender - a person convicted of a crime for the first time
  • synonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act
  • synonym: repeat offender - a person who has committed the same offense multiple times
