balm of Gilead

noun
a North American fir tree with balsam-scented leaves
8.8
Pronunciation: / bɑm əv ˈɡɪliæd / (AmE), / bɑːm əv ˈɡɪliæd / (BrE)
a medium-sized fir tree of northeastern North America, whose crushed leaves emit a balsam scent; widely used for pulpwood and as Christmas trees.

Examples

Balm of Gilead tree
Many paper mills rely on balm of Gilead trees for high-quality pulpwood.
The balm of Gilead is prized for its fragrant needles and use as Christmas trees.
When you crush the needles of balm of Gilead, they release a distinctive balsam aroma.
