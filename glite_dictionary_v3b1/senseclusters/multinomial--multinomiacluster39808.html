multinomial

noun
mathematical expression with multiple terms
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌl.təˈnoʊ.mi.əl / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈnəʊ.mi.əl / (BrE)
a mathematical expression consisting of the sum of several terms, each a product of constants and variables raised to non-negative integer powers

Examples

A multinomial expression
A multinomial can generalize a binomial by allowing more than two terms.
Factoring a multinomial can be challenging, especially when there are more than three terms.
The equation contains a multinomial with four terms, each representing a different process.
