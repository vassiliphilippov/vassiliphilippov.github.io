telemetry intelligence

noun
actionable insights from telemetry data
7.2
Pronunciation: /təl.ˈem.ə.triː ɪn.ˈtel.ə.dʒəns/ /; / /tə.ˈlem.ə.triː ɪn.ˈtel.ɪ.dʒəns/ (AmE), /tɪ.ˈlem.ɪ.triː ɪn.ˈtel.ɪ.dʒəns/ /; / /tə.ˈlem.ə.triː ɪn.ˈtel.ə.dʒəns/ (BrE)
The collection, processing, and analysis of telemetry (automatic measurements and logs) from devices, systems, or networks to produce actionable insights for monitoring, maintenance, security, or decision-making.

Examples

Device telemetry intelligence
Many companies rely on telemetry intelligence from their devices to predict failures before they occur.
The security team used telemetry intelligence to detect unusual network activity within minutes.
Our monitoring dashboard displays telemetry intelligence that highlights trends and potential system anomalies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: observability - the ability to understand a system's internal state by collecting and analysing telemetry and logs
  • synonym: operational intelligence - real-time analytics and insights used to improve operations and respond to incidents
