revarnish

verb
to apply varnish again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈvɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈvɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
to apply a new layer of varnish to a surface, especially wood, to restore or improve its appearance

Examples

To revarnish a table
We decided to revarnish the old wooden table to restore its shine.
The museum staff will revarnish the antique frames to protect them from damage.
After years of use, it was time to revarnish the front door to keep it looking fresh.
After years of use, they decided to revarnish the dining room chairs to make them look new again.
The artist carefully revarnished the painting to protect its colors from fading.
You should revarnish the deck every few years to keep the wood in good condition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strip - to remove a layer of paint or varnish from a surface
  • synonym: varnish - to apply a protective or decorative finish to a surface
