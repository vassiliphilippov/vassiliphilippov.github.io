Williamite

noun
supporter of William III
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəmaɪt / (BrE)
a supporter of William III of England, especially during the late 17th century conflicts against the Jacobites

Examples

A loyal Williamite
The Williamites played a key role in the Battle of the Boyne in 1690.
Many Irish Protestants identified as Williamites during the conflict with the Jacobites.
The victory of the Williamites changed the course of British and Irish history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Jacobite - a supporter of James II and his descendants in the late 17th and 18th centuries
