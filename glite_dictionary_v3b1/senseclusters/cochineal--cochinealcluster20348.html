cochineal

noun
red dye-producing Mexican insect
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààko ä.t É…™.niÀêl / (AmE), / Ààk…í.t É…™.niÀêl / (BrE)
A Mexican scale insect that feeds on cacti and is used as the source of a natural red dye.

Examples

Cochineal insect
Insects called cochineal were once harvested by hand from cactus plants in Mexico.
Some food products are colored with a natural extract made from cochineal.
The bright red color in the fabric was achieved using cochineal dye.

Synonyms

  • cochineal insect
    9.8 0.01/m
    cochineal insect is the everyday name for the red cactus scale insect, while cochineal is the shorter English name also used for its dye.
  • Dactylopius coccus
    9.9 0.00/m
    Dactylopius coccus is the formal scientific name used by scientists, while cochineal is the common English name used by sellers and speakers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carmine - a vivid red pigment made from cochineal insects
