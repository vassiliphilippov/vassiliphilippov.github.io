alpha fetoprotein

noun
fetal protein linked to neural tube defects
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.fə ˌfiː.toʊˈproʊ.tiːn /; / ˈælfə ˌfoʊtəˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˈal.fə ˌfiː.təʊˈprəʊ.tiːn /; / ˈælfə ˌfəʊtəˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a protein produced mainly by the fetal liver and yolk sac and present in fetal serum and amniotic fluid; abnormal or elevated levels in a fetus may indicate developmental abnormalities such as neural tube defects (for example spina bifida or anencephaly), and in other contexts may be associated with some cancers.
