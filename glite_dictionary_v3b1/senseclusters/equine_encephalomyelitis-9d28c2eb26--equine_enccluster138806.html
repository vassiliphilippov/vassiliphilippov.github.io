equine encephalomyelitis

noun
viral brain disease in horses
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈiː.kwaɪn ɪnˌsɛfəloʊˌmaɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈiː.kwʌɪn ɪnˌsɛfələʊˌmʌɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A viral inflammation of the brain and spinal cord in horses, transmitted by mosquitoes and sometimes affecting humans.

Examples

Equine encephalomyelitis virus
Public health officials warned the community about the risks of equine encephalomyelitis spreading to humans.
There was an outbreak of equine encephalomyelitis in the region, leading to concern among veterinarians.
Vaccination is crucial for preventing equine encephalomyelitis in horses during mosquito season.
