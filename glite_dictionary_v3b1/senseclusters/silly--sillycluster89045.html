silly

adjective
foolish or absurd
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪli / (AmE), / ˈsɪli / (BrE)
showing a lack of good sense or judgment; foolish, trivial, or deserving of pity

Examples

A silly mistake
He looked silly wearing that giant clown wig to the party.
It was a silly mistake, but fortunately no one was hurt.
The idea sounded silly at first, but it actually worked.
She wore a silly hat that made everyone laugh.
Stop acting so silly during the meeting and try to focus.
The children exchanged silly jokes and giggled all afternoon.
His silly dreams of fame earned him nothing but quiet pity from his friends.
She felt silly standing in the rain, waiting for someone who would never come.
The old man's desperate attempts to impress the crowd seemed more silly than amusing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dignified - having or showing a composed or serious manner worthy of respect
  • synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • synonym: frivolous - not serious or sensible; carefree
  • synonym: pitiful - arousing pity, often mingled with scorn
  • antonym: sensible - having or showing good sense or judgment
  • antonym: serious - acting or speaking sincerely and in earnest
