red pepper

noun
pungent ground Capsicum spice
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A pungent powder made from the dried, ground pods and seeds of Capsicum peppers, used as a spice in cooking.

Examples

Ground red pepper
I prefer using red pepper instead of black pepper in spicy dishes.
She sprinkled a pinch of red pepper onto her pizza for extra heat.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of ground red pepper to enhance the flavor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: black pepper - pungent spice made from Piper nigrum berries
  • synonym: cayenne pepper - hot red powder made from cayenne chilies
