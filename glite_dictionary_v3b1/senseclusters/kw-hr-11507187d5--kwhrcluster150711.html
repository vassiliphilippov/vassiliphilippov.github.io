kW-hr

noun
unit of energy (kilowatt-hour)
3.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / kiÀê Ààd åb…ôl.juÀê a…™t É …ëÀêr / (AmE), / kiÀê Ààd åb…ôl.juÀê e…™t É …ëÀê / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to the work done by a power of 1,000 watts operating for one hour; commonly used to measure electricity consumption

Examples

One kW-hr unit
One kW-hr is the amount of energy used by a 1,000-watt device running for an hour.
Solar panels on the roof generated over 5 kW-hr today.
The monthly electricity bill showed a total consumption of 400 kW-hr.

Synonyms

  • kilowatt hour
    3.2 1.22/m
    kilowatt hour is the standard term used in most places for this energy unit, while kW-hr is its abbreviated form used on bills.
  • Board of Trade unit
    7.1 0.00/m
    Board of Trade unit is a British official name for the same energy amount, while kW-hr is the common modern abbreviation on meters.
