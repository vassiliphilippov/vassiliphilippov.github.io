non-breakable

adjective
cannot be broken
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈbreɪkəbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈbreɪkəbl / (BrE)
not able to be broken; resistant to breaking or shattering

Examples

Non-breakable glasses
The manufacturer guarantees that these non-breakable glasses will survive accidental drops.
Parents often choose non-breakable plates for their young children to prevent accidents.
The new phone case is advertised as non-breakable and highly durable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: unbreakable - impossible to break or shatter
