secreta

noun
bodily secretions
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkriːtə / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtə / (BrE)
substances produced and released by glands or cells, especially in a medical context

Examples

Test secreta samples
The laboratory tested the patient's secreta to identify the cause of the infection.
Doctors often examine secreta from wounds to monitor healing progress.
Certain diseases can change the appearance or amount of secreta produced by the body.
