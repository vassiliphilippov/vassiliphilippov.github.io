dry

adjective

(of humor, writing, or manner) subtle, unemotional, plain, or dull

Examples

Dry humor
He made a dry remark about the weather that had everyone chuckling quietly.
Her dry humor often goes unnoticed by people who take things literally.
The novel is full of dry wit that rewards careful readers.
Although the subject is important, the textbook makes it seem incredibly dry.

The experiment was a dry run, giving us no useful data.

Synonyms

  • deadpan
    7.5 1.13/m
    deadpan is deliberately expressionless and used for comic effect, while dry is quiet, unemotional humor or a plain, dull manner.
  • wry
    7.5 2.84/m
    wry is ironic and mildly mocking in tone, while dry is more plain, unemotional humor or a dull manner.
