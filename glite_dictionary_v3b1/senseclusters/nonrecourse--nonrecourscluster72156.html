nonrecourse

adjective
loan limited to collateral
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.rɪˈkɔrs / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈkɔːs / (BrE)
describing a loan where the lender can only claim the collateral if the borrower defaults, not other assets

Examples

Nonrecourse loan agreement
The bank offered a nonrecourse loan, so the borrower's other assets were protected.
Investors often prefer nonrecourse financing to limit their personal financial risk.
With a nonrecourse mortgage, the lender can only take the property if the loan is not repaid.
The company obtained a nonrecourse loan, so the lender could only claim the property if they defaulted.
A nonrecourse agreement protects the borrower's other assets from being seized.
Investors preferred the nonrecourse structure because it limited their financial risk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recourse - a loan where the lender can pursue the borrower's other assets if the collateral is insufficient
