plutonium-239

noun
radioactive isotope of plutonium (nuclear fuel/weapons)
8.1
Pronunciation: / pluːˌtoʊniəm tuː ˈθɜːrtiː naɪn / (AmE), / pluːˌtəʊniəm tuː ˈθɜːti naɪn / (BrE)
a radioactive isotope of plutonium used as a fuel in nuclear reactors and in the manufacture of nuclear weapons

Examples

Plutonium-239 fuel
Developing atomic weapons often involves the use of plutonium-239 as a core material.
Scientists carefully monitored the storage of plutonium-239 due to its radioactive properties.
The nuclear reactor was powered by plutonium-239 to sustain the chain reaction.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: plutonium-238 - a different isotope of plutonium used mainly as a heat source in radioisotope thermoelectric generators
  • degree higher: plutonium-240 - an isotope of plutonium, less suitable for weapons than plutonium-239
  • synonym: uranium-235 - a fissile isotope of uranium also used as nuclear fuel or in weapons
