sink

verb
go below the surface (of water or liquid)
Pronunciation: / sɪŋk / (AmE), / sɪŋk / (BrE)
to go down below the surface of water or another liquid; to become submerged

Examples

To sink in water
If you fill the container with too many stones, it will sink to the bottom.
She watched her childhood toy sink slowly beneath the surface of the lake.
The boat began to sink after hitting an iceberg.

Related Terms

  • antonym: float - to stay on the surface of a liquid and not sink
