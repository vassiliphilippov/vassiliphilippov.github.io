buckeye

noun
tree with palmate leaves and shiny brown seeds
9.1 0.09/m

A tree of the genus Aesculus, having palmate leaves and large clusters of white to red flowers, followed by shiny brown inedible seeds.

Examples

Buckeye tree
Clusters of red flowers make the buckeye especially attractive in the spring.
Gardeners often plant buckeye trees for their ornamental beauty, despite the seeds being inedible.
The buckeye produces shiny brown seeds that resemble the eye of a deer.
