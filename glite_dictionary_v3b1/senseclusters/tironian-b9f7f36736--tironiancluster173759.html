Tironian

noun
shorthand writer
Pronunciation: / taɪˈroʊniən / (AmE), / tɪˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
a person who wrote or used Tiro's shorthand system for rapid writing, especially in ancient Rome

Examples

A Tironian writer
In ancient Rome, a skilled Tironian could record entire speeches as they were delivered.
The historian studied manuscripts written by a Tironian to understand early shorthand techniques.
He aspired to become a Tironian and master the art of rapid note-taking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scribe - a person who copies or writes documents by hand
  • synonym: stenographer - a person who writes in shorthand
