parliamentary democracy

noun
system of government with a parliament
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrləˈmɛn.tə.ri dɪˈmɑː.krə.si / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.lɪˈmɛn.təri dɪˈmɒk.rə.si / (BrE)
A system of government in which the highest legislature is a parliament, and the executive is drawn from and accountable to it.

Examples

A parliamentary democracy system
In a parliamentary democracy, the head of government is usually the leader of the majority party in parliament.
Many countries adopted a parliamentary democracy after gaining independence from colonial rule.
The United Kingdom is an example of a parliamentary democracy with a long history of stable government.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: democracy - a system of government by the people, typically through elected representatives
  • antonym: presidential democracy - a democracy where the executive is separate from the legislature and elected directly
  • synonym: representative democracy - a democracy where citizens elect representatives to make decisions on their behalf
