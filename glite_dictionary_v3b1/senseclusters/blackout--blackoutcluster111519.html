blackout

noun
temporary loss of consciousness
Pronunciation: / ˈblækaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblækaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary loss of consciousness or memory, often caused by a medical condition, shock, or intoxication

Examples

Memory blackout
After standing up too quickly, he experienced a brief blackout and had to sit down immediately.
She suffered a blackout right after the accident and didn’t remember what happened.
The stress caused several blackouts during his college exams.
After the accident, he suffered a blackout and couldn't remember what happened.
She often experienced a brief blackout when she stood up too quickly.
The witness described a blackout that lasted for several hours after the traumatic event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amnesia - loss of memory, especially due to brain injury, illness, or psychological trauma
  • synonym: fainting - loss of consciousness for a short time because of temporarily insufficient blood flow to the brain
