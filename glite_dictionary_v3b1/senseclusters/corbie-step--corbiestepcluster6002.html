corbie-step

noun
step-like projection on a gable wall
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.bi ˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.bi ˌstɛp / (BrE)
A step-like projection on the top of a gable wall, forming a stepped outline; commonly found in traditional Scottish and northern European architecture.

Examples

Corbie-step gable
Many traditional Scottish buildings feature corbie-step gables as a characteristic architectural detail.
Restorers carefully repaired each corbie-step to preserve the building's historic appearance.
The old castle was famous for its distinctive corbie-steps along the roofline.
