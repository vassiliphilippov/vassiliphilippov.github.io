precipice

noun
a very steep cliff
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsəpɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪpɪs / (BrE)
a very steep or overhanging cliff; a high, sheer rock face

Examples

A steep precipice
A single wrong step could send him tumbling over the precipice.
Clouds drifted near the top of the precipice, obscuring its dizzying height.
The hikers stopped at the edge of the precipice to admire the breathtaking view below.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cliff - a steep rock face, typically at the edge of the sea
