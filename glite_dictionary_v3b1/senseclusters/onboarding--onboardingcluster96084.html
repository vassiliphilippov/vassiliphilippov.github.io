onboarding

noun
introduction process
Pronunciation: / ˈɑnˌbɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌbɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of introducing and integrating new people (such as employees or users) or things (such as devices or software) into a company, organization, or system

Examples

Employee onboarding process
The company improved its onboarding process to help new employees feel welcome and prepared.
Effective onboarding ensures that users understand how to get the most out of the software.
During onboarding, new hires receive important information about company policies and procedures.
The company has a detailed onboarding process to help new employees settle in quickly.
Effective onboarding can improve job satisfaction and reduce staff turnover.
During onboarding, new hires learn about the company's culture and expectations.
The IT department managed the onboarding of hundreds of new laptops for the staff.
Proper onboarding ensures that all devices are secure and ready for use.
Automated tools can speed up the onboarding process for new software systems.

  • synonym: installation - the act of setting up software or hardware for use
  • synonym: orientation - the process of familiarizing someone with a new environment or role
  • synonym: training - the process of teaching or learning skills for a job or activity
