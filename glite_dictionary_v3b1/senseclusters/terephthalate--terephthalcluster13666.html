terephthalate

noun
plastic chemical compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrəfˈθæleɪt / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəfˈθaleɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from terephthalic acid, commonly used in the production of plastics

Examples

Polyethylene terephthalate
Polyethylene terephthalate is widely used to make water and soda bottles.
The recycling code '1' refers to plastics made from terephthalate compounds.
Scientists are developing new ways to break down terephthalate for more efficient recycling.
