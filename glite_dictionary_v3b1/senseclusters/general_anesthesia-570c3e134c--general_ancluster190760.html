general anesthesia

noun
medically induced unconsciousness for surgery
4.3 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒen.ər.əl æn.əs.ˈθiː.ʒə/ (AmE), /ˈdʒen.ər.əl æn.əs.ˈθiː.zi.ə/ (BrE)
A medically induced state of complete or total unconsciousness caused by anesthetic drugs, used to prevent pain during surgery or other medical procedures, typically for major surgery.

Examples

General anesthesia during surgery
Complications from general anesthesia are rare but can occur during surgery.
Some procedures require only local anesthesia, while others need general anesthesia.

Advances in general anaesthesia have made complex surgeries much safer.
Children may feel groggy after waking up from general anaesthesia.
