general anesthesia

noun
medically induced unconsciousness for surgery
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɚl ˌænəsˈθiʒə /; / ˈdʒɛnərəl ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˌænɪsˈθiːziə /; / ˈdʒɛnərəl ˌænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
A medically induced state of complete or total unconsciousness caused by anesthetic drugs, used to prevent pain during surgery or other medical procedures, typically for major surgery.
