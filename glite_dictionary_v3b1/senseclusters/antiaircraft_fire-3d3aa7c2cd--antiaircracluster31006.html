antiaircraft fire

noun
gunfire against enemy aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈɛr.kræft ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈeə.krɑːft ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
gunfire directed at enemy aircraft to defend against aerial attack.

Examples

Heavy antiaircraft fire
Heavy antiaircraft fire forced the attacking planes to change course.
The pilot skillfully evaded the intense antiaircraft fire during the mission.
The soldiers braced themselves as antiaircraft fire erupted around the low-flying bombers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flak - antiaircraft fire, especially from German forces in WWII
