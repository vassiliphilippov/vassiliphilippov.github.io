stratification

noun
placing seeds in damp material to aid germination
Pronunciation: / ˌstrætəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of placing seeds in moist material, such as sand, sawdust, or peat moss, to preserve them or encourage germination

Examples

Seed stratification
He read about stratification in a horticultural manual before starting his seed project.
Successful germination of some tree species depends on proper stratification beforehand.
The gardener used stratification to ensure the rare seeds would sprout in spring.
