magma

noun
molten rock beneath Earth's surface
6.0 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmæɡ.mə/ (AmE), /ˈmæɡ.mə/ (BrE)
Molten rock beneath the Earth's surface, which can solidify to form igneous rock when it cools.

Examples

Hot magma underground
Geologists study magma to understand volcanic eruptions and the formation of igneous rocks.
The movement of magma can cause earthquakes and the creation of new landforms.
When magma reaches the Earth's surface, it is known as lava.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lava - molten rock expelled by a volcano during an eruption
