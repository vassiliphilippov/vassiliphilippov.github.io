alternate

verb
act as a substitute or understudy
Pronunciation: / ˈɔl.tɚˌneɪt /; / ˈɔl.tərˌneɪt /; / ˈɔl.tɚ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.neɪt /; / ˈɔːl.təˌneɪt / (BrE)
to take turns, go back and forth, or cause people to take turns in doing something

Examples

To alternate turns
During the championship, two goalkeepers would alternate in starting matches.
Sarah was chosen to alternate for the lead actress in case of illness.
They asked Jim to alternate in the choir when the regular tenor was absent.
Children often alternate between periods of quiet concentration and bursts of energy.
During the meeting, his mood would alternate between enthusiasm and frustration.
The weather seems to alternate from sunny to rainy every other day this week.
During the meeting, the speakers alternated between presenting and answering questions.
The children alternate turns on the swing to make sure everyone gets a chance.
We alternate cooking dinner every night, so neither of us gets too tired.
