Attic

noun
Ancient Greek dialect of Attica and Athens
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˈætɪk / (BrE)
the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken and written in Attica, Athens, and parts of Ionia, noted for its influence on classical Greek literature and philosophy

Examples

Attic Greek
Many classical Greek plays were originally written in Attic Greek.
She studied Attic to better understand the writings of Plato and Thucydides.
The Attic dialect significantly shaped the development of later Greek language forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ionic - the Ancient Greek dialect spoken in Ionia and the Aegean islands
  • degree higher: Koine - the common Greek dialect that developed from Attic and Ionic Greek
