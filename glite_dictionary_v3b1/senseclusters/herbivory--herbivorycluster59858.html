herbivory

noun
plant-eating behavior
7.0
Pronunciation: / hɜrˈbɪvəri / (AmE), / hɜːˈbɪvəri / (BrE)
the act or process of animals feeding on plants (sometimes causing damage to the plants)

Examples

Herbivory by animals
Giraffes are well known for their herbivory in African savannas.
The study focused on the impact of herbivory on local plant populations.
Many insects display herbivory by feeding exclusively on leaves and stems.
In some forests, herbivory by deer can prevent young trees from growing.
Scientists study herbivory to understand how animal feeding affects plant populations.
Increased herbivory may lead to lower crop yields for farmers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carnivory - the act of animals feeding mainly on other animals
  • synonym: grazing - the act of animals feeding on grass or other low vegetation
  • antonym: omnivory - the act of animals eating both plants and animals
  • antonym: predation - the act of one animal hunting and eating another animal
