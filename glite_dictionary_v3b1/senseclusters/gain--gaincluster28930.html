gain

verb
obtain or secure (something)
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: /geɪn/ (AmE), /geɪn/ (BrE)
to obtain, achieve, or get something (such as an advantage, knowledge, experience, or reaching a place), often through effort

Examples

To gain experience
By working overtime, he managed to gain extra income for his family.
Maria hoped to gain new skills by attending the workshop.
The company expects to gain significant market share this year.

He hopes to gain enough through investments to retire early.
The merger helped the business gain millions in extra revenue last year.
After months of study, he finally gained a good understanding of the subject.
It can take years to gain someone's trust.
Through hard work, she gained the respect of her colleagues.
