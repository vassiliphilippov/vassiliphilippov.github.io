Olympic god

noun
major Greek deity of Mount Olympus
Pronunciation: / oʊˈlɪmpɪk ɡɑd / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪk ɡɒd / (BrE)
One of the principal gods of ancient Greek mythology, said to dwell on Mount Olympus after the defeat of the Titans.

Examples

An Olympic god of Greece
Hera, an Olympic god, was known as the queen of the gods.
The ancient Greeks believed each Olympic god had unique powers and responsibilities.
Zeus is perhaps the most famous Olympic god in Greek mythology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Olympian - a Greek god who lives on Mount Olympus
  • antonym: Titan - one of the earlier race of gods in Greek mythology
