permanent-press

adjective
fabric treated to resist wrinkling
7.7 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpɜːr.mə.nənt.pres/ /; / /ˈpɜːr.mə.nənt ˈpres/ (AmE), /ˈpɜː.mən.ənt.pres/ /; / /ˈpɜː.mən.ənt ˈpres/ (BrE)
(of fabric or clothing) treated to remain smooth and resist wrinkling, so as to require little or no ironing after washing.

Examples

Permanent-press fabric
After laundering, the permanent-press dress came out of the dryer with hardly a wrinkle.
I prefer permanent-press shirts because they're easy to care for and rarely need ironing.
Most modern work uniforms are made from permanent-press fabrics for convenience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drip-dry - fabric that dries quickly and does not need ironing
