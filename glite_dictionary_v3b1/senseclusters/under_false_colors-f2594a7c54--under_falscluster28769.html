under false colors

adverb
deceitful pretense or misrepresentation
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚ fɔls ˈkʌlɚz / (AmE), / ˈʌndə fɔːls ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
pretending to be something one is not, especially to deceive others or conceal one's true intentions

Examples

Under false colors to deceive
She joined the group under false colors to uncover their secrets.
The politician campaigned under false colors, hiding his real agenda.
The spy entered the building under false colors, posing as a maintenance worker.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to mislead by false appearance or statement
  • synonym: disguise - to give a false appearance or mislead by appearance
  • antonym: openly - without concealment or pretense
