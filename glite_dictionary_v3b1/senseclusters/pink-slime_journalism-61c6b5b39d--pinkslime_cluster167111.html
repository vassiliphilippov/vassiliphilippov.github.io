pink-slime journalism

noun
misleading, low-quality journalism
Pronunciation: / pɪŋk slaɪm ˈdʒɜrnəlɪzəm / (AmE), / pɪŋk slaɪm ˈdʒɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A type of journalism that is misleading, sensationalized, and often seen as low-quality or unethical, typically involving the creation of deceptive news sites or articles resembling authentic reporting.

Examples

Pink-slime journalism sites
Lawmakers proposed new regulations to combat the spread of pink-slime journalism online.
Many news sites have been criticized for publishing pink-slime journalism that confuses readers.
The rise of pink-slime journalism has made it harder to distinguish between real and fake news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: yellow journalism - journalism that is sensationalized or poorly researched
