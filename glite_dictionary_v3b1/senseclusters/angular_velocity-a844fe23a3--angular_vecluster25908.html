angular velocity

noun
rate of change of angular position (physics)
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡjələr vəˈlɑsəti / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡjʊlə vəˈlɒsɪti / (BrE)
the rate at which the angular position of a rotating object changes, usually measured in radians per second

Examples

Angular velocity of rotation
A spinning figure skater increases her angular velocity by pulling in her arms.
In physics class, we learned how to calculate angular velocity for rotating objects.
The angular velocity of the wheel is measured in radians per second.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: angular acceleration - the rate of change of angular velocity
  • synonym: rotational speed - rate at which an object rotates, not necessarily in radians
