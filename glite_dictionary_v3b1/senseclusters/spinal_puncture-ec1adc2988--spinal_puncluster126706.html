spinal puncture

noun
medical procedure to remove spinal fluid
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪnəl ˈpʌŋktʃər / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl ˈpʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
a medical procedure involving the removal of fluid from the subarachnoid space of the lower spinal cord, usually for diagnosis or treatment

Examples

A spinal puncture procedure
A spinal puncture can help diagnose infections or bleeding around the brain and spinal cord.
Some patients may experience headaches as a side effect of a spinal puncture.
The doctor performed a spinal puncture to collect cerebrospinal fluid for analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lumbar puncture - removal of spinal fluid specifically from the lumbar region
