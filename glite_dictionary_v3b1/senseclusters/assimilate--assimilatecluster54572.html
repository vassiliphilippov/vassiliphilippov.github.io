assimilate

verb
(linguistics) become similar in sound
9.1 5.9
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a sound) to change so as to become similar to a neighboring sound, especially in pronunciation.

Examples

Sounds assimilate in speech
In rapid speech, the final nasal sound often assimilates to the place of articulation of the next consonant.
Phonemes may assimilate to their neighbors during connected speech.
The 'n' in 'input' assimilates to an 'm' sound because of the following 'p'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissimilation - (linguistics) process by which similar sounds become less similar
