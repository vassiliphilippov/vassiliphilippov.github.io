Zechariah

noun
Old Testament book of prophecies
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (AmE), / ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament, consisting of the prophecies attributed to the prophet Zechariah, mainly concerning the renewal of Israel after the Babylonian Captivity

Examples

The Book of Zechariah
Among the Minor Prophets, Zechariah stands out for its visionary passages.
Many scholars have studied the symbolism in Zechariah to understand its references to the coming Messiah.
The Book of Zechariah is often quoted in discussions about the restoration of Israel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Malachi - last book of the Old Testament with similar prophetic content
