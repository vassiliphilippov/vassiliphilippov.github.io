échelle

noun
spectrograph for fine spectra
Pronunciation: / eɪˈʃɛl / (AmE), / eɪˈʃɛl / (BrE)
a type of diffraction grating or spectrograph used to separate light into fine spectral lines, especially in astronomy and laboratory spectroscopy

Examples

Astronomy échelle
The astronomers used an échelle to analyze the starlight with high precision.
Upgrading the échelle allowed the laboratory to detect much finer spectral details.
A new échelle was installed to improve the telescope's ability to separate close spectral lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diffraction grating - an optical device that splits light into several beams
  • synonym: spectrograph - an instrument for recording spectra
