keratodermia

noun
skin disorder with horn-like thickening
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.rə.tə.ˈdɜːr.miː.ə/ /; / /kə.rə.toʊ.ˈdɜːr.miː.ə/ (AmE), /kə.rə.tə.ˈdɜː.miː.ə/ /; / /kə.rə.təʊ.ˈdɜː.miː.ə/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which the skin develops areas of thickened, horn-like growth.

Examples

Keratodermia skin thickening
Certain genetic conditions can lead to the development of keratodermia in childhood.
The patient was diagnosed with keratodermia following the appearance of thick, scaly patches on her hands.
Treatment for keratodermia often involves softening the hardened areas and managing symptoms.
