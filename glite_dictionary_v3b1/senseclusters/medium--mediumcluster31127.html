medium

noun
means or channel for communicating information
4.1 14/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmiÀêdi…ôm / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀêdi…ôm / (BrE)
a means or channel for storing, transmitting, or communicating information, signals, or messages (including mass media like TV, radio, or newspapers)

Examples

A digital medium
Digital storage is an efficient medium for preserving important documents and records.
Radio remains a popular medium for news and entertainment in many regions.
The company chose television as its main medium for advertising the new product.
Fiber optics use glass fibers as the medium for transmitting digital data.
Light requires no physical medium to move through space, unlike sound.
Sound waves travel through air, which acts as a medium for their transmission.
Social media has emerged as a powerful medium for sharing information and opinions.
Television became the dominant medium of mass communication in the 20th century.
The medium of radio allowed the news to reach millions quickly.

Synonyms

  • channel
    3.6 12/m
    channel is a general route or system used to send messages or requests, while medium names any means for storing or communicating information.
  • mass medium
    3.8 0.07/m
    mass medium reaches a large audience through TV, radio, or newspapers, while medium covers any means of communicating information.
  • broadcast medium
    3.4 0.27/m
    broadcast medium sends information by radio or TV waves to many people, while medium covers all types of channels for information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: channel - a medium of communication or the route through which information is transmitted
  • synonym: media - plural form commonly used to refer to mass communication channels collectively
  • antonym: mediums - persons claiming to communicate with spirits (contrasting sense)
  • synonym: method - a particular form or procedure for accomplishing something
  • synonym: vehicle - a thing used as a means of transmission or communication
