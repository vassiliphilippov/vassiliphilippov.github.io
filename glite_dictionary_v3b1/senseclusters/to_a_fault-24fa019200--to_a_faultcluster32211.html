to a fault

adverb
excessively; beyond what is proper
7.4
Pronunciation: / tə ə fɔlt / (AmE), / tə ə fɔːlt / (BrE)
to an excessive or extreme degree (especially of a usually positive quality)

Examples

Loyal to a fault
He is loyal to a fault, even when it is not in his best interest.
His honesty is to a fault, sometimes hurting people's feelings with his bluntness.
She is generous to a fault, always giving more than she should.
He is generous to a fault, always giving more than necessary.
She is honest to a fault, sometimes offending people without meaning to.
Their loyalty is admirable to a fault, never wavering regardless of the situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excessively - to a degree that is more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • antonym: moderately - to a limited degree, not excessive
