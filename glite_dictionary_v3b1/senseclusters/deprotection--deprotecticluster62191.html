deprotection

noun
removal of protection
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːprəˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːprəˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
the state of no longer being protected, especially after a previous protection has been removed or ended

Examples

Deprotection of land
The deprotection of the forest area led to increased illegal logging activities.
After the treaty ended, the region was left in a state of deprotection.
Environmentalists warned that deprotection could threaten the survival of rare species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exposure - the state of being unprotected or open to harm
  • antonym: protection - the act of keeping someone or something safe
