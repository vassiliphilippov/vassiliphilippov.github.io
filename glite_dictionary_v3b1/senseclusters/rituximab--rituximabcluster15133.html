rituximab

noun
monoclonal antibody drug
8.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtʌksɪmæb / (AmE), / rɪˈtʌksɪmæb / (BrE)
a medicine (antibody) used to treat certain cancers and autoimmune diseases by targeting specific cells in the immune system

Examples

Some patients with lymphoma respond well to treatment with rituximab.

Patients with rheumatoid arthritis may receive rituximab to help control their symptoms.
After several infusions of rituximab, his condition began to improve noticeably.
