Edward III of England

proper noun
King of England, 1327–1377
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈed.wərd ðə θɜːrd əv ˈɪŋ.glənd/ (AmE), /ˈed.wəd ðə θɜːd əv ˈɪŋ.glənd/ (BrE)
(1312–1377) The King of England from 1327 to 1377, known for initiating the Hundred Years' War, presiding during the Black Death, and witnessing the rise of the House of Commons.

Examples

Edward III of England reign
Edward III of England began his reign as a teenager after the deposition of his father, Edward II.
During the reign of Edward III of England, the Black Plague swept through much of Europe.
The claim of Edward III of England to the French throne led to the outbreak of the Hundred Years' War.
