Saint James the Apostle

proper noun
apostle of Jesus in the New Testament
6.7
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz ði əˈpɑstl̩ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz ði əˈpɒstl̩ / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, brother of John, and traditionally considered the author of the Epistle of James.

Examples

Saint James the Apostle in the New Testament
Saint James the Apostle is said to have written the Epistle of James in the New Testament.
Many pilgrims travel to Spain to visit the shrine of Saint James the Apostle in Santiago de Compostela.
The painting depicts Saint James the Apostle standing beside his brother John.
