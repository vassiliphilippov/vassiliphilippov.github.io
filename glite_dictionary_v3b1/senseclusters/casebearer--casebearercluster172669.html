casebearer

noun
moth with portable case
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsˌbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsˌbɛərə / (BrE)
a small moth or its larva that builds and lives in a portable case made from silk and plant material

Examples

The casebearer larva
The casebearer larva carried its tiny case as it moved along the branch.
Gardeners often spot a casebearer attached to the underside of leaves.
A casebearer can be identified by the portable case its larva builds for protection.
The casebearer carefully added tiny leaves to its portable home as it moved along the branch.
Scientists observed a casebearer larva using grains of sand to strengthen its case.
A casebearer can be difficult to spot because its case blends in with its surroundings.

Related Terms
