melanosis

noun
abnormal melanin deposits; medical condition
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛləˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormal deposits of melanin, often resulting in dark patches especially on the skin.

Examples

Dark patch melanosis
Chronic exposure to certain chemicals can lead to melanosis in affected individuals.
Intestinal melanosis is rare and often discovered incidentally during examinations.
The dermatologist diagnosed the patient with melanosis after noticing dark patches on her skin.
