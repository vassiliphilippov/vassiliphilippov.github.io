animate

verb
make lively or energetic
4.7 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.ə.meɪt/ /; / /ˈæn.ə.mət/ (AmE), /ˈæn.ɪ.meɪt/ /; / /ˈæn.ɪ.mət/ (BrE)
to make something more lively, energetic, or vivid; to give new life or energy to

Examples

To animate a crowd
A good leader can animate a team and inspire creativity.
Her presence helped to animate the party, making it much more enjoyable for everyone.
The director’s vision really animated the otherwise dull script.
Bright colors animate the mural, turning an ordinary wall into a vibrant work of art.
Her lively stories always animate our conversations, making them much more entertaining.
The speaker's passion seemed to animate the entire audience during the lecture.
A walk in the fresh spring air would animate anyone after a long winter.
Nothing can animate him more than the prospect of an adventure.
The speaker managed to animate the crowd with her passionate words.
