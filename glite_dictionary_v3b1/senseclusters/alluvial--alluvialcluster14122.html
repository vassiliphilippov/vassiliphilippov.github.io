alluvial

adjective
relating to river-deposited soil or sediment
Pronunciation: / əˈlu.vi.əl / (AmE), / əˈluː.vi.əl / (BrE)
relating to or made of soil or sediment deposited by rivers or flowing water.

Examples

Alluvial soil
Gold was often found in alluvial soil during the gold rush era.
Many cities are built on alluvial plains stretching out on either side of major rivers.
The alluvial deposits created fertile farmland along the riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - clay, silt, sand, or gravel deposited by running water
