gasoline tax

noun
tax on gasoline sales
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæsəliːn tæks / (AmE), / ˈɡæsəliːn tæks / (BrE)
A government tax imposed on each gallon of gasoline sold, usually intended to raise revenue for transportation funding or environmental purposes.

Examples

The gasoline tax increase
Many drivers are concerned about how the gasoline tax affects fuel prices.
Revenue from the gasoline tax is often used to improve highways and bridges.
The state increased the gasoline tax to fund road repairs.
