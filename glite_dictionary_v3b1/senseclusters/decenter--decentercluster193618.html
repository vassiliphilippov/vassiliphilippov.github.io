decenter

verb
to shift focus
7.5
Pronunciation: / diːˈsɛntər / (AmE), / diːˈsɛntə / (BrE)
to shift focus or attention away from a central or dominant point, perspective, or authority

Examples

To decenter a viewpoint
The new curriculum aims to decenter traditional Western viewpoints in history classes.
In group therapy, participants are encouraged to decenter their personal experiences and listen to others.
The artist tried to decenter the main subject in her painting to draw attention to the background.
The new curriculum aims to decenter the teacher's authority and encourage student participation.
Modern art often tries to decenter traditional ideas about beauty and meaning.
Social movements can decenter power by giving a voice to marginalized groups.
Therapy helped him decentre from his own feelings and see his partner's point of view.
Teachers encourage students to decentre and think about how their actions affect others.
Learning to decentre can improve communication and reduce misunderstandings in relationships.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralize - to bring to a central point or under single control
  • synonym: disperse - to spread out or scatter in different directions
  • synonym: diversify - to make more varied or diverse
  • synonym: empathize - to understand and share another person's feelings
  • antonym: self-center - to focus only on oneself
