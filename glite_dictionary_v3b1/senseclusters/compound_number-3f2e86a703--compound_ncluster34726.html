compound number

noun
quantity expressed using multiple units
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmˌpaʊnd ˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.paʊnd ˈnʌm.bə / (BrE)
a number or quantity expressed using two or more different units, such as 'one hour and ten minutes'

Examples

A compound number example
Ancient measurements often used a compound number, like two yards and one foot.
The recipe called for a compound number of one pound and eight ounces of flour.
Tim calculated his commute as a compound number—forty minutes and fifteen seconds.
