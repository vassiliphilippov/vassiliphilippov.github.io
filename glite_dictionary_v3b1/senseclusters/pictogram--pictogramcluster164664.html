pictogram

noun
symbolic picture
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪktəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈpɪktəɡræm / (BrE)
a simple picture or symbol that represents a word, idea, or object, used in writing, signs, or maps

Examples

Ancient pictogram writing
The ancient cave walls were covered with pictograms that told stories of daily life.
A restroom sign is a modern example of a pictogram used for communication.
Researchers studied the pictogram to understand how early people recorded information.
The museum displayed ancient pictograms carved into stone tablets.
Many public signs use pictograms to communicate information without words.
Children quickly understood the safety instructions thanks to the clear pictograms.
The map used a pictogram of a tree to show the location of the park.
Children quickly understood the safety instructions thanks to the clear pictogram on the sign.
A pictogram of a phone indicated where to find public telephones in the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: icon - a graphic symbol representing an object or function
  • synonym: ideogram - a symbol representing an idea or concept
  • synonym: logogram - a written character representing a word or phrase
  • antonym: word - a unit of language that carries meaning and can be spoken or written
