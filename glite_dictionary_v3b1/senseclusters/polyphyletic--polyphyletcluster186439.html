polyphyletic

adjective
multiple ancestral origins
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɑː.lɪ.faɪ.ˈlet.ɪk/ (AmE), /pɒl.ɪ.faɪ.ˈlet.ɪk/ (BrE)
describing a group of organisms that have more than one common ancestor and do not share a single evolutionary origin

Examples

Polyphyletic animal group
Scientists determined that the group of reptiles was actually polyphyletic and not closely related.
A polyphyletic classification can mislead researchers about evolutionary relationships.
The study revealed that the plants in question formed a polyphyletic group with different ancestral lines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monophyletic - descended from a single common ancestor
  • synonym: paraphyletic - descended from a common ancestor but not including all descendants
