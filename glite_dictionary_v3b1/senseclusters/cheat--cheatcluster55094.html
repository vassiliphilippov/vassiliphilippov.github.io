cheat

noun
weedy grass found in cultivated fields
Pronunciation: / tʃit / (AmE), / tʃiːt / (BrE)
a weedy grass (Bromus secalinus) that grows in grainfields and is considered a weed

Examples

Wild cheat grass
Some fields had so much cheat that harvesting the grain became difficult.
The farmer was concerned about cheat growing among his wheat crop.
The seeds of cheat can sometimes be harmful to livestock if ingested.
Farmers struggled to remove cheat from their wheat crops each year.
Some botanists identify cheat by its slender, nodding seed heads.
The spread of cheat can reduce wheat yields significantly if not controlled.
