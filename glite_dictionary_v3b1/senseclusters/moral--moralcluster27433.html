moral

adjective
psychological rather than physical in effect
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
(of a victory, support, etc.) achieved or given in a way that affects the mind or feelings rather than having a direct physical or tangible effect.

Examples

Moral support
Although his team lost the final, they still claimed a moral victory.
She appreciated the moral support from her friends during the difficult time.
The protestors offered only moral encouragement, avoiding any physical involvement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: physical - relating to the body or tangible reality rather than the mind
  • synonym: psychological - relating to the mind or emotional state
