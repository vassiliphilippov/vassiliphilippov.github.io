poundal

noun
unit of force in the FPS system
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpaʊn.dəl/ /; / /ˈpaʊn.dæl/ (AmE), /ˈpaʊn.dəl/ /; / /ˈpaʊn.dæl/ (BrE)
a unit of force equal to the force that imparts an acceleration of 1 foot per second squared to a mass of 1 pound (equivalent to approximately 0.1382 newtons)

Examples

One poundal force
A force of 10 poundals acts on the block, causing it to accelerate across the floor.
In older physics texts, you may encounter the poundal as a unit of force.
The poundal is now largely replaced by the newton in most scientific applications.
