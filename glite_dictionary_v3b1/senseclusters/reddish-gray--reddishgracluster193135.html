reddish-gray

adjective
grey with a hint of red
1.7
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdɪʃˌɡreɪ /; / ˈrɛd.ɪʃ ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdɪʃˌɡreɪ /; / ˈrɛd.ɪʃ ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
Of a grey color tinged with red; having a slight red hue in a grey shade.

Examples

A reddish-gray color
She wore a reddish-gray scarf that complemented her autumn outfit perfectly.
The sky took on a reddish-gray hue at dawn, hinting at a warm day ahead.
The stones along the riverbank were mostly reddish-gray, worn smooth by years of water flow.
A thick mist gave the early morning sky a reddish-grey appearance.
She painted the living room walls a reddish-grey to make the space feel warmer.
The old castle's stones had weathered to a reddish-grey over the centuries.
