red marrow

noun
bone marrow producing blood cells
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / red ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
The bone marrow found in children and certain adult bones, responsible for the production of red blood cells.

Examples

Red marrow in bones
As we age, much of our red marrow is gradually replaced by yellow marrow.
Diseases like leukemia often affect the red marrow within our bones.
The doctor explained that red marrow is responsible for creating new blood cells in the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: yellow marrow - the fatty bone marrow that replaces red marrow in adult bones
