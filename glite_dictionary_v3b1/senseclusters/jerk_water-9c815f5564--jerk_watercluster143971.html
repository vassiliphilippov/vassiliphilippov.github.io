jerk water

noun
poor-quality water; spicy sauce for cooking
8.5
Pronunciation: / dʒɝk ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / dʒɜːk ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
1. Humorous term for unappealing or poor-quality water. 2. A spicy sauce, typically used in cooking, especially for marinating or flavoring meats.

Examples

Jerk water sauce
He slathered his chicken in jerk water before roasting it over the fire.
No one wanted to drink the jerk water after seeing its cloudy appearance.
The tap in the old building only dispensed jerk water that tasted terrible.
