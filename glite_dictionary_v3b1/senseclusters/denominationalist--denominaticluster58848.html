denominationalist

noun
religious supporter
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˌnɑːməˈneɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports or identifies with a particular religious denomination

Examples

A denominationalist leader
As a proud denominationalist, she attended every event organized by her church.
The denominationalist opposed merging with other religious groups to preserve tradition.
Many denominationalists value the unique beliefs and practices of their faith community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ecumenist - a person who promotes unity among different Christian churches
  • synonym: sectarian - a person strongly supporting a particular religious group
