rephotograph

verb
to photograph again
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfoʊtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfəʊtəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to take a photograph of an existing photograph, print, or image to reproduce or digitize it

Examples

To rephotograph a picture
The museum staff will rephotograph the old paintings to preserve their details digitally.
She decided to rephotograph her childhood pictures to share them online with her family.
Before the originals faded, the historian rephotographed the documents for the archive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - to make a duplicate of something
  • synonym: scan - to create a digital image of a document or photo
