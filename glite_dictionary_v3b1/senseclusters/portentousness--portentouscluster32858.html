portentousness

noun
pompous seriousness
9.8
Pronunciation: /pɑːr.ˈten.təs.nəs/ /; / /pɔːr.ˈten.təs.nəs/ (AmE), /pɔː.ˈten.təs.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being overly serious, pompous, or giving an impression of great importance

Examples

Portentousness of a speech
The portentousness of his speech made the audience feel uncomfortable rather than inspired.
Critics often point out the portentousness in her writing style, calling it unnecessarily grand.
He delivered the news with such portentousness that everyone expected something far more serious.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humility - the quality of being humble and not thinking you are better than others
  • synonym: pomposity - the quality of being self-important and grand
  • synonym: pretentiousness - the quality of trying to appear more important or impressive than is actually the case
