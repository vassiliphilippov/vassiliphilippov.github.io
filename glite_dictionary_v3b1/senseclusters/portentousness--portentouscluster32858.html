portentousness

noun
pompous seriousness
9.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / p…îrÀàt…õnt…ôsn…ôs / (AmE), / p…îÀêÀàt…õnt…ôsn…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being overly serious, pompous, or giving an impression of great importance

Examples

Portentousness of a speech
The portentousness of his speech made the audience feel uncomfortable rather than inspired.
Critics often point out the portentousness in her writing style, calling it unnecessarily grand.
He delivered the news with such portentousness that everyone expected something far more serious.

Synonyms

    7.4 0.02/m

  • pomposity
    7.7 0.33/m
    pomposity is a formal, exaggerated style of dignity, while portentousness is solemn and tries to sound weighty.
  • pretentiousness
    7.7 0.26/m
    pretentiousness tries to impress others by seeming cultured or important, while portentousness feels serious and solemnly important.
