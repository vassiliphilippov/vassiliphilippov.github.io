work up

verb
develop or produce something by effort
3.9 2.20/m
Pronunciation: / w…ùk  åp / (AmE), / w…úÀêk  åp / (BrE)
to develop or produce something, such as an idea, plan, or design, with effort

Examples

To work up a plan
After several meetings, they finally worked up a proposal for the project.
She managed to work up a new marketing strategy in just a few days.
The artists will work up your sketches into a finished logo.

Synonyms

  • work out
    2.8 25/m
    work out means finding a solution or plan by careful thought, while work up means developing something step by step with effort.
  • come up with
    2.7 59/m
    come up with means thinking of or producing an idea quickly, while work up means developing that idea over time with effort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceive - to form or devise in the mind (an idea or plan)
  • synonym: develop - to grow or cause to grow and become more mature, advanced, or elaborate
