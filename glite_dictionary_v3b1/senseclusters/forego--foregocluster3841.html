forego

verb
voluntarily go without
6.3 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrÀà…°o ä / (AmE), / f…îÀêÀà…°…ô ä / (BrE)
to decide not to have or do something; to give up or go without something voluntarily

Examples

To forego dessert
He will forego his right to speak at the meeting in favor of a written statement.
Many people chose to forego their morning coffee during the fasting period.
She decided to forego dessert after dinner to stick to her diet.

Synonyms

  • forgo
    6.3 2.94/m
    forgo means choosing not to enjoy something you want, while forego describes giving up or going without it by choice.
  • dispense with
    6.3 1.89/m
    dispense with means stop using something you do not need, while forego means choose to give up something by your own decision.
  • waive
    6.6 4.29/m
    waive means choose not to use or demand a legal right in a formal setting, while forego means give up something voluntarily.
  • relinquish
    7.2 5.71/m
    relinquish means voluntarily give up a possession, right, or position, often permanently, while forego means choose not to have or do something.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forfeit - to lose or give up as a penalty or choice
  • synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim; give up
