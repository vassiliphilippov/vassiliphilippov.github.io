celestial equator

noun
imaginary circle dividing sky's hemispheres
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛstʃəl ɪˈkweɪtər / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstʃl ɪˈkweɪtə / (BrE)
the imaginary great circle on the celestial sphere that is equidistant from the celestial poles and divides the sky into northern and southern hemispheres

Examples

The celestial equator line
Because of Earth's tilt, the celestial equator is different from the ecliptic.
The celestial equator is used by astronomers to map the positions of stars in the night sky.
When the Sun crosses the celestial equator, we experience either the spring or autumn equinox.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equator - the imaginary great circle around the Earth equidistant from the poles
