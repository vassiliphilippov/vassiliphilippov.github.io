cornerstone

noun
inscribed stone marking start of building
3.5 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔːr.nər.stoʊ/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.nə.stəʊ/ (BrE)
a stone placed at the corner of a building, often during a ceremony, marking the start or foundation of the construction

Examples

Building cornerstone
An inscription on the cornerstone marks the year the school was established.
Architects pointed out the original cornerstone at the base of the historic courthouse.
The new museum's cornerstone was laid in a grand ceremony attended by city officials.
A time capsule was placed inside the cornerstone of the new library.
Architects inspected the ancient cornerstone for signs of structural damage.
The building's cornerstone was laid in 1892 during a public ceremony.
