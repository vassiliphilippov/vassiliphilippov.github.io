fertilize

verb
to make (a female) conceive by introducing semen
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause an egg or plant to start developing by joining with a male cell (such as sperm or pollen)

Examples

To fertilize an egg
In mammals, only one sperm is required to fertilize the female's egg.
Modern breeding techniques allow scientists to fertilize eggs outside the body.
The veterinarian explained how to artificially fertilize the cows to improve the herd's genetics.
Farmers fertilize their fields every spring to ensure a healthy crop.
The scientist explained how only one sperm can fertilize an egg during conception.
Gardeners often fertilize their plants to help them grow stronger and produce more flowers.
Artificial methods are sometimes used to fertilise cows in modern agriculture.
Many animals fertilise the female internally, increasing the chances of successful reproduction.
The doctor used a special technique to fertilise the eggs during treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impregnate - to make pregnant by introducing semen
  • synonym: inseminate - to introduce semen into a female; especially artificially
  • synonym: pollinate - to transfer pollen to a plant to allow fertilization
  • antonym: sterilize - to make unable to reproduce or develop
