still-hunt

verb
hunt by stealth and ambush
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪlˌhʌnt / (AmE), / ˈstɪlˌhʌnt / (BrE)
to hunt game by moving quietly and stealthily to stalk and ambush it, rather than by driving or flushing.

Examples

To still-hunt quietly
Many experienced hunters believe that to still-hunt requires greater skill than driving game.
The hunter chose to still-hunt deer at sunrise, moving quietly through the woods.
They successfully still-hunted a wild turkey by remaining perfectly silent and hidden.
