pooka

noun
Irish folklore spirit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpuːkə / (AmE), / ˈpuːkə / (BrE)
a mischievous spirit or creature from Irish folklore, often taking the form of an animal

Examples

A pooka in folklore
The villagers believed a pooka haunted the nearby hills.
In the story, a pooka appeared as a black horse to lead travelers astray.
Some say a pooka can bring both good and bad fortune, depending on its mood.
