diocese

noun
district under a bishop's care
7.6 8.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààda…™.…ô.s…™s / (AmE), / Ààda…™.…ô.siÀês / (BrE)
A district under the pastoral care and jurisdiction of a bishop in the Christian Church.

Examples

A diocese district
He was appointed bishop of a large diocese in the south of the country.
New parishes have been established within the diocese to serve the growing population.
The diocese will hold a charity event to support local families next month.

Synonyms

  • bishopric
    9.7 0.15/m
    bishopric names the district a bishop governs in many churches, while diocese is the widely used term for that same district.
  • episcopate
    9.9 0.00/m
    episcopate refers to the area under a bishop or the bishop's office in formal church language, while diocese names the local district.
  • eparchy
    9.9 0.01/m
    eparchy is the district term used in the Eastern Orthodox Church, while diocese is the general term used in many other Christian traditions.
  • bishopry
    9.9 0.00/m
    bishopry often refers to the office, rank, or authority of a bishop in older language, while diocese names the bishop's district.
