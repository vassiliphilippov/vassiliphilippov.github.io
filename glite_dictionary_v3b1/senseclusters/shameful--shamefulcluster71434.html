shameful

adjective
deserving disgrace or shame
2.5 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈʃeɪm.fəl/ (AmE), /ˈʃeɪm.fəl/ (BrE)
deserving or causing shame or disgrace; morally wrong or offensive

Examples

A shameful mistake
He made a shameful mistake that cost him his reputation.
It is shameful to cheat during an exam.
The company’s shameful treatment of its workers was revealed in the investigation.
His shameful actions ruined the company's reputation.
She apologized for her shameful behavior at the party.
The treatment of the prisoners was truly shameful and inhumane.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disgraceful - causing shame or loss of respect
  • antonym: honorable - deserving honor and respect; honest and fair
  • synonym: ignominious - deserving or causing public disgrace or shame
  • synonym: scandalous - causing general public outrage by a perceived offense against morality
  • antonym: shameless - having no shame; not showing guilt or embarrassment
