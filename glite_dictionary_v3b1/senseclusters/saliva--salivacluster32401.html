saliva

noun
the clear liquid in the mouth
4.7
Pronunciation: / səˈlaɪ.və / (AmE), / səˈlaɪ.və / (BrE)
the clear, watery liquid secreted in the mouth by salivary glands, helping to moisten food and begin the digestion of starches

Examples

Saliva in mouth
Animals use saliva not only for eating but also for grooming.
The dentist explained how saliva helps protect your teeth from decay.
When you smell delicious food, your mouth produces more saliva.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spit - saliva, especially when ejected from the mouth
