pile

verb
arrange in a stack or heap
1.2 18/m
Pronunciation: / paÉªl / (AmE), / paÉªl / (BrE)
to put things in a heap or stack; to arrange or cram things or people together in a pile

Examples

To pile clothes
He piled his clothes on the chair before going to bed.
Please pile the dirty dishes on the counter so I can wash them.
The workers piled sandbags along the riverbank to prevent flooding.
After dinner, the kids piled their dirty dishes in the sink without washing them.
She piled books onto the table until there was barely any space left.
The teacher piled work on the students until they were overwhelmed.
Fans piled onto the train to catch the concert in the city.
The children piled into the car, eager to leave for the trip.
We piled the boxes in the corner to make more space in the room.
