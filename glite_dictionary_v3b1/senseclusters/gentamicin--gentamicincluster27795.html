gentamicin

noun
an antibiotic for bacterial infections
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛntəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛntəˈmʌɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections, especially of the urinary tract, obtained from certain species of actinomycete bacteria

Examples

Gentamicin antibiotic treatment
Gentamicin can cause kidney toxicity if not used carefully.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to gentamicin over time.
The doctor prescribed gentamicin to treat the stubborn urinary tract infection.

  • synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms
