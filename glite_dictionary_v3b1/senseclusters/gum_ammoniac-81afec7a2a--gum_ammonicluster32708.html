gum ammoniac

noun
aromatic gum from the ammoniac plant
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm əˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / ɡʌm əˈmɒniˌæk / (BrE)
a fragrant and resinous gum obtained from the ammoniac plant, used in medicine and industry

Examples

Gum ammoniac resin
Historically, gum ammoniac was valued for its expectorant properties in traditional medicines.
The doctor prescribed a paste containing gum ammoniac for the skin condition.
Traders imported gum ammoniac from the Middle East for use in varnishes and plasters.
