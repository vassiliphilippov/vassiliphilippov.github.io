bell gable

noun
gable extension holding bells
5.7
Pronunciation: / bɛl ˈɡeɪbəl / (AmE), / bɛl ˈɡeɪbəl / (BrE)
An extension of a gable wall that supports and houses one or more bells, typically serving as a bell cote.

Examples

A bell gable wall
A weathered bell gable still stands at the west end of the building.
Many rural churches were built with a bell gable rather than a tower.
The old chapel featured a simple bell gable above its entrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bell cote - a small structure for hanging bells, usually on a roof or gable
