detuning

noun
reducing performance
Pronunciation: / diːˈtuːnɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈtjuːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate adjustment of a machine or device to reduce its performance or power, often for regulatory or reliability reasons

Examples

Engine detuning
The detuning of the car's engine helped it comply with new environmental standards.
Engineers performed a detuning to increase the reliability of the generator under heavy use.
Some manufacturers use detuning to ensure their products last longer and require less maintenance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modification - the process of changing something to improve or alter its function
  • antonym: tuning - the adjustment of a machine or device for optimal performance
