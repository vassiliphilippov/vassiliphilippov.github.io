cumulonimbus cloud

noun
towering thunderstorm cloud
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkjuːmjəloʊˈnɪmbəs klaʊd / (AmE), / ˌkjuːmjʊləʊˈnɪmbəs klaʊd / (BrE)
A dense, towering cloud with great vertical development, often dark and charged with electricity, associated with thunderstorms and severe weather.

Examples

A cumulonimbus cloud
A single cumulonimbus cloud stretched across the sky, warning of a coming storm.
Lightning flashed within the towering cumulonimbus cloud above the city.
Pilots carefully avoid flying near a cumulonimbus cloud due to severe turbulence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stratus cloud - a low, gray, uniform cloud layer
  • synonym: thundercloud - cloud that produces thunder and lightning
