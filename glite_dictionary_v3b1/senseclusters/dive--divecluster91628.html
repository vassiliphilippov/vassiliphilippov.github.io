dive

noun
steep nose-down aircraft descent
4.2
Pronunciation: / daɪv / (AmE), / daɪv / (BrE)
a steep, nose-down descent by an aircraft, often under control as part of a maneuver or attack

Examples

A plane dive
A sudden dive can result in a rapid loss of altitude if not carefully managed.
During the air show, the stunt plane performed a dramatic dive towards the crowd.
The pilot initiated a dive to avoid enemy fire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: climb - an aircraft's upward ascent; opposite of dive
