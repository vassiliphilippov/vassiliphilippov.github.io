smudge

noun
dirty mark or stain
1.9 1.81/m
Pronunciation: / smÊŒdÊ’ / (AmE), / smÊŒdÊ’ / (BrE)
a dirty mark or stain that makes a surface look untidy

Examples

A smudge on glass
She wiped the smudge of chocolate from the boy's face.
The white shirt had a noticeable smudge on the sleeve.
There was a dark smudge on the page where the ink had spread.

Synonyms

  • smear
    4.1 1.45/m
    smear is a mark made by spreading a substance across a surface, while smudge is a softer untidy mark.
  • smirch
    9.8 0.00/m
    smirch is a mark that spoils an object's look and often appears in writing, while smudge is common everyday dirt.
  • blot
    2.7 0.63/m
    blot is a small spot, often from ink or liquid, while smudge is a blurred dirty mark that looks untidy.
  • stain
    1.4 8.65/m
    stain is a patch of discoloration that can be hard to remove, while smudge is often a softer, removable dirty mark.
  • daub
    7.7 0.02/m
    daub is a thick, roughly applied smear of sticky material, while smudge is a light untidy mark left by dirt.
