decontextualize

verb
to remove from context
9.0 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / diÀêk…ônÀàt…õkst Éu…ôÀåla…™z / (AmE), / diÀêk…ônÀàt…õkstj ä…ôla…™z / (BrE)
to remove something from its original context so it can be understood, used, or considered separately

Examples

To decontextualize ideas
Teachers often decontextualize concepts so students can apply them in new situations.
It is important not to decontextualize quotes when analyzing literature.
The researcher tried to decontextualize the data to find universal patterns.
When you decontextualize historical events, their true meaning can be lost.
The teacher warned not to decontextualize the poem's lines when analyzing its message.
News articles sometimes decontextualize quotes to create a misleading impression.

Synonyms

Related Terms
