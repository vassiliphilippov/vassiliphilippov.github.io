unsuspended

adjective
not delayed (legal)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnsəˈspɛndɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnsəˈspɛndɪd / (BrE)
not suspended; to be served or enforced immediately, especially of a legal punishment or sentence.

Examples

Unsuspended sentence
The court handed down an unsuspended sentence, requiring the defendant to go to prison immediately.
Unlike his previous conviction, this time he received an unsuspended fine that had to be paid at once.
The judge explained that an unsuspended punishment meant there would be no delay in serving the sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: suspended - delayed or temporarily not enforced, especially of a sentence
