extreme

adjective
farthest or most distant
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstrim / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːm / (BrE)
at the farthest point or at a very great degree, far beyond what is usual or expected

Examples

Extreme weather conditions
She placed the fence at the extreme boundary of her property.
The marathon ended at the extreme southern tip of the city.
They built a house on the extreme edge of the forest, far from neighbors.
She showed extreme caution when handling the fragile artifact.
The hikers faced extreme cold as they climbed higher up the mountain.
The patient was suffering from extreme pain after the accident.
An extreme response to the situation would only escalate the conflict.
It's important to avoid extreme opinions and consider a balanced perspective.
She was known for her extreme views on education reform and often sparked controversy.

  • synonym: utmost - to the greatest or highest degree
