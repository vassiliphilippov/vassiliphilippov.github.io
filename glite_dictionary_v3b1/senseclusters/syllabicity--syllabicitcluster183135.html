syllabicity

noun
property or pattern of syllable formation
Pronunciation: / sɪˌlæbɪˈsɪti / (AmE), / sɪˌlæbɪˈsɪti / (BrE)
the characteristic or property of having or forming syllables, or the pattern of syllable structure in a particular language

Examples

Syllabicity in language
Changes in syllabicity can affect a language's rhythm and poetic meter.
The syllabicity of certain sounds varies between languages and dialects.
Understanding syllabicity helps linguists analyze how words are structured and pronounced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: syllabic - relating to or forming syllables
