snowdrift

noun
a heap of snow shaped by wind
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊˌdrɪft / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌdrɪft / (BrE)
A deep pile or bank of snow formed by the wind blowing and accumulating it in one area.

Examples

A snowdrift bank
A single snowdrift lay along the fence, shimmering in the morning sun.
Children played on the snowdrift behind the house, turning it into a fort.
The road was blocked by a large snowdrift after last night's storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drift - a mass of matter, such as sand or snow, accumulated by wind or water
