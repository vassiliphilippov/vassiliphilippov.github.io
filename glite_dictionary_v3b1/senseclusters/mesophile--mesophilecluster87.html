mesophile

noun
organism preferring moderate heat
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛsəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈmiːsəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
an organism, especially a microorganism, that grows best at moderate temperatures, typically between 20°C and 45°C

Examples

A mesophile organism
Many soil bacteria are classified as mesophiles because they prefer moderate temperatures.
A mesophile will not survive in extremely hot or cold environments.
Yogurt production relies on mesophile cultures that grow best at room temperature.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: psychrophile - an organism that thrives at low temperatures
  • degree higher: thermophile - an organism that thrives at high temperatures
