Pitot-static tube

noun
instrument to measure airspeed in aircraft
9.1
Pronunciation: /pɪ.ˈtoʊ ˈstæt.ɪk tuːb/ /; / /ˈpiː.toʊ ˈstæt.ɪk tuːb/ /; / /pɪ.ˈtoʊ ˈstæt.ɪk tjuːb/ (AmE), /ˈpiː.tə ˈstæt.ɪk tjuːb/ /; / /pɪ.ˈtə ˈstæt.ɪk tjuːb/ (BrE)
a measuring instrument consisting of a combined Pitot tube and static tube, used in aircraft to measure total and static air pressure and thus determine airspeed

Examples

Pitot-static tube instrument
A blocked Pitot-static tube can cause serious errors in an aircraft's instrument readings.
Technicians inspected the Pitot-static tube for any signs of icing during the routine maintenance check.
The pilot checked the Pitot-static tube to ensure accurate airspeed readings before takeoff.
