centum

noun
Indo-European language group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛntəm / (AmE), / ˈkɛntəm / (BrE)
a group of Indo-European languages distinguished by a particular historical sound change, including Latin and most Western branches

Examples

Centum languages group
Latin is considered a classic example of a centum language in historical linguistics.
The division between centum and satem languages is important in Indo-European studies.
Ancient Greek and Celtic are also classified as centum languages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: satem - a group of Indo-European languages with a different historical sound change
