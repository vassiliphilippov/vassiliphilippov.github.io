mature-onset diabetes

noun
adult-onset form of diabetes mellitus
7.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈtʃʊr ˈɑːn.set daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz/ /; / /mə.ˈtʃʊr ˈɔːn.set daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs/ (AmE), /mə.ˈtʃʊə ˈɒn.set daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz/ /; / /mə.ˈtʃɔː ˈɒn.set daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs/ (BrE)
A mild form of diabetes mellitus that develops gradually in adults, often managed with diet and oral medication rather than insulin injections.

Examples

Mature-onset diabetes in adults
Many adults are diagnosed with mature-onset diabetes in their forties or fifties.
Obesity is a major risk factor for developing mature-onset diabetes.
With proper diet and exercise, most cases of mature-onset diabetes can be managed without insulin injections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: type 2 diabetes - chronic condition affecting glucose metabolism in adults, formerly called mature-onset diabetes
