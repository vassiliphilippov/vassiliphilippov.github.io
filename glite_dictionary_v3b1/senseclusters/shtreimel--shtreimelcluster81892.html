shtreimel

noun
Hasidic fur hat
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃtraɪməl / (AmE), / ˈʃtraɪməl / (BrE)
a round fur hat traditionally worn by some married Hasidic Jewish men on special religious or festive occasions

Examples

A shtreimel hat
At the Sabbath dinner, he proudly wore his shtreimel as part of his traditional attire.
The shtreimel is often passed down from father to son in some Hasidic families.
Tourists were fascinated by the elegant shtreimel seen during the festival in Jerusalem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fedora - a soft felt hat with a brim and indented crown
  • synonym: kippah - a small cap worn by Jewish men
