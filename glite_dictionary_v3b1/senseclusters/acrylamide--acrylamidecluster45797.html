acrylamide

noun
a potentially harmful chemical compound
7.3
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɪləˌmaɪd / (AmE), / əˈkrɪləmaɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline chemical compound (C3H5NO) used in industry and known to be potentially carcinogenic, especially when formed during high-temperature cooking of foods.

Examples

Acrylamide in food
Acrylamide can form in starchy foods cooked at high temperatures, such as baking or frying.
Manufacturers monitor acrylamide content in foods to ensure consumer safety.
Researchers found high levels of acrylamide in some fried potato products.
