Primula vulgaris

noun
a species of primrose with pale yellow flowers
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪmjələ vʌlˈɡɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɪmjʊlə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a plant native to western and southern Europe, widely cultivated for its pale yellow flowers; also known as the common primrose

Examples

Primula vulgaris pale flowers
Gardeners often cultivate Primula vulgaris for its delicate, pale yellow flowers.
In some regions, Primula vulgaris is a protected species due to its dwindling wild populations.
The Primula vulgaris blooms early in the spring, brightening woodlands across Europe.
