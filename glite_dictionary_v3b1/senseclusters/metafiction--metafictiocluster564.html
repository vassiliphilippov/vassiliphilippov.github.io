metafiction

noun
self-referential fiction
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
fiction that deliberately draws attention to its own nature as a constructed story or to the act of storytelling itself

Examples

Metafiction in novels
The author uses metafiction to make readers aware that they are reading a novel.
Many modern novels employ metafiction to blur the line between reality and fiction.
By breaking the fourth wall, the story becomes a clear example of metafiction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: postmodernism - a style of art and literature characterized by self-reference and irony
  • antonym: realism - a style of fiction that aims to represent life accurately without self-reference
