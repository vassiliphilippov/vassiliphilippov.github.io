intellectually

adverb
in a way relating to the intellect
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.təˈlɛk.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təlˈɛk.tʃu.əli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the intellect, reasoning, or mental capacity.

Examples

To be intellectually active
He does not find the work intellectually stimulating anymore.
She is intellectually curious and loves engaging in deep discussions.
The task was intellectually challenging for even the brightest students.
