ball

noun
baseball: pitch outside the strike zone
4.1 4.38/m
Pronunciation: / b…îl / (AmE), / b…îÀêl / (BrE)
(in baseball) a pitch that is outside the strike zone and not struck at by the batter, counted against the pitcher.

Examples

Four balls in baseball
After four balls, the batter was allowed to walk to first base.
The pitcher tried to throw a strike but ended up with another ball.
The umpire called the pitch a ball because it was outside the strike zone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strike - a legal pitch in baseball that is either swung at and missed, or passes through the strike zone
