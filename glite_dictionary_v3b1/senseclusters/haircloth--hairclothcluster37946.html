haircloth

noun
coarse fabric made from animal hair
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈheəˌklɒθ / (BrE)
A stiff, coarse fabric woven from horsehair or camelhair, used especially for upholstery or as stiffening in garments.

Examples

A haircloth fabric
She examined the coarse texture of the haircloth lining inside the antique garment.
The tailor used haircloth to give the jacket its structured shape.
Victorian furniture often contained haircloth under the upholstery for extra support.
