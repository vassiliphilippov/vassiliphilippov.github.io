sodium

noun
a soft, reactive metallic chemical element
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive metallic chemical element, symbol Na, that belongs to the alkali metal group and is found naturally in salt and seawater

Examples

Pure sodium metal
Pure sodium reacts explosively when it comes into contact with water.
Table salt is composed of sodium and chlorine atoms.
The doctor advised him to reduce his sodium intake to control his blood pressure.
