Tudor

noun
member of English ruling dynasty (1485–1603)
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːdər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːdə / (BrE)
a member of the English royal dynasty that ruled from 1485 to 1603

Examples

A Tudor king
As a Tudor, Elizabeth I presided over a golden age of English culture.
Henry VIII was a famous Tudor known for his six marriages.
The Tudors played a vital role in establishing the English Reformation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Stuart - member of the royal dynasty that succeeded the Tudors in England
