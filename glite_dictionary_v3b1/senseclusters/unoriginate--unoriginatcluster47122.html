unoriginate

verb
to remove origin
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəˈrɪdʒəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈrɪdʒɪneɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to lose its original source or to make it seem as if it did not originate from anywhere

Examples

To unoriginate a story
The historian tried to unoriginate the legend by questioning its earliest sources.
As new facts emerged, the story seemed to unoriginate and lose its foundation.
Some myths unoriginate over time as their origins are forgotten or disproven.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erase - to remove or wipe out completely
  • antonym: originate - to begin or start from a source
