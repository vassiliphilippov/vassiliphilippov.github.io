Hasidism

noun
sect of Orthodox Judaism; mystical Jewish movement
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhæ.sɪ.dɪ.zəm/ /; / /hə.ˈsiː.dɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈhæ.sɪ.dɪ.zəm/ /; / /hə.ˈsiː.dɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a Jewish religious movement that began in Eastern Europe in the 18th century, known for mysticism, strict religious observance, strong community life, and leadership by a Rebbe

Examples

Hasidism religious movement
Hasidism emphasizes joyful worship and spiritual connection with God.
Many followers of Hasidism dress in distinctive traditional clothing.
The history of Hasidism traces back to 18th-century Eastern Europe.
Many Jewish communities in New York include members of Hasidism.
Modern Hasidism is known for its emphasis on the leadership of a Rebbe.
The spiritual teachings of Hasidism focus on joy and devotion in worship.
