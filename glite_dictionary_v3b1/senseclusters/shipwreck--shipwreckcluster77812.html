shipwreck

verb
to suffer complete failure
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪpˌrɛk / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpˌrɛk /; / ˈʃɪp.rɛk / (BrE)
to completely ruin or cause to fail, especially in a figurative sense (such as a plan, career, or reputation)

Examples

To shipwreck a career
Many promising careers have shipwrecked on the rocks of unrealistic ambition.
The project shipwrecked due to poor planning and lack of communication.
Their business shipwrecked after the sudden loss of funding.
A single mistake could shipwreck his entire reputation.
Careless financial decisions eventually shipwrecked the company.
Her betrayal nearly shipwrecked our friendship beyond repair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • synonym: fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • synonym: ruin - to spoil or destroy something completely
  • antonym: save - to rescue or preserve from harm or ruin
