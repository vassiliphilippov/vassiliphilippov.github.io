isocryme

noun
line connecting equal coldest-month temperatures
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌkraɪm / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəˌkraɪm / (BrE)
A line on a map connecting places that have the same average temperature in the coldest month of the year.

Examples

An isocryme line
On the climate map, each isocryme showed regions that shared the same average temperature in January.
Researchers compared the positions of isocrymes over several decades to study effects of climate change.
The meteorologist drew an isocryme to illustrate areas experiencing similar winter temperatures.
