linguistic competence

noun
implicit knowledge of language rules
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk kəmˈpɛtəns / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk kəmˈpɛtəns / (BrE)
in linguistics, a speaker's implicit knowledge of the grammatical rules and structure of their language, distinct from actual language use

Examples

Linguistic competence knowledge
A child's linguistic competence develops rapidly as they grow and are exposed to more language.
Chomsky introduced the distinction between linguistic competence and performance in his work on generative grammar.
Language tests often aim to evaluate both linguistic competence and communicative abilities.

Related Terms
