nice

adjective
courteous and polite
Pronunciation: / naɪs / (AmE), / naɪs / (BrE)
behaving in a kind, polite, or morally good way

Examples

A nice person
He always makes a nice gesture by holding the door open for others.
It was nice of her to send a thank-you note after the interview.
The staff were extremely nice and made us feel welcome throughout our stay.
Her parents wanted her to marry a nice young man from their community.
In those days, a nice girl was expected to be home before dark.
She comes from a very nice family and always behaves politely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: improper - not in keeping with accepted standards of morality or propriety
  • synonym: polite - having or showing good manners and respect for others
  • antonym: rude - offensive or impolite in manner or speech
  • synonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
