canker

verb
to infect or corrupt with a canker
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkæŋkɚ /; / ˈkæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈkaŋkə / (BrE)
to infect, corrupt, or cause to decay, either physically or morally

Examples

To canker a wound
If wounds are not properly cleaned, they can quickly canker and spread infection.
The old tree had been cankered by disease over many seasons.
Unchecked malice can canker even the noblest of hearts.
If the wound is left untreated, it may canker and become difficult to heal.
Old trees sometimes canker at the base, threatening their stability.
The rose bushes began to canker after the long period of damp weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupt - to make morally corrupt or rotten
  • synonym: decay - to become decomposed or corrupted
