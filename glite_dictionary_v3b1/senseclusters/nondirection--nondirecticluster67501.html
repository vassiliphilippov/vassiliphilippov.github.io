nondirection

noun
no predicted effect direction
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dəˈrɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈrɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the condition in research or statistics of not specifying a predicted direction for an effect or outcome

Examples

Nondirection in research
The hypothesis was based on nondirection, allowing for any possible outcome in the experiment.
In statistical testing, nondirection means the researchers do not predict whether the effect will be positive or negative.
Choosing nondirection can make a study more open to unexpected results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directionality - the quality of having a specific direction or orientation
  • synonym: non-directional - not specifying a direction for an expected effect
