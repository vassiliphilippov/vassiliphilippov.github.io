trespass

verb
go beyond limits or boundaries
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛsˌpæs /; / ˈtrɛspæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəs / (BrE)
to go beyond the limits of a place, law, or someone's rights, especially without permission

Examples

To trespass limits
Hikers are warned not to trespass on private land.
If you continue to trespass, you may face a fine.
She realized she had trespassed over the boundaries of good taste with her comment.
He apologized for trespassing upon her kindness.
I hope I do not trespass on your patience with this long explanation.
She felt that the reporter had trespassed upon her privacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encroach - to gradually take or use more of something than is rightfully yours
  • synonym: intrude - to enter a place or situation where one is unwelcome or uninvited
  • synonym: invade - to intrude or encroach upon
