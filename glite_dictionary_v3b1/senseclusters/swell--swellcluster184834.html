swell

noun
long wave on the sea's surface
5.7 3.22/m
Pronunciation: / swÉ›l / (AmE), / swÉ›l / (BrE)
A long, smooth, regular movement or series of waves on the surface of the open sea, often caused by distant winds or storms.

Examples

A sea swell
After the storm had passed, a steady swell continued to roll toward the shore.
Surfers waited patiently for the next big swell to arrive.
The boat rocked gently over the swell of the open ocean.

Synonyms
