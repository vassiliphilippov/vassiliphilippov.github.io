outer planet

noun
major planet beyond asteroid belt
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtər ˈplænɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə ˈplænɪt / (BrE)
(astronomy) Any major planet in the Solar System whose orbit lies outside the asteroid belt, such as Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, or Pluto.

Examples

The outer planets
Astronomers use powerful telescopes to study the outer planets and their rings.
Jupiter is the largest outer planet in our Solar System.
The outer planets are mostly composed of gas and have many moons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inner planet - one of the planets whose orbit is inside the asteroid belt
