underpass

noun
tunnel beneath road or railway
6.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.pæs/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.pɑːs/ (BrE)
A passage or tunnel running beneath a road or railway, allowing pedestrians or vehicles to cross safely.

Examples

A underpass tunnel
After the heavy rain, the underpass was flooded and closed to the public.
The city built a new underpass so pedestrians could avoid the busy traffic above.
We walked through the underpass to get to the other side of the train tracks.
