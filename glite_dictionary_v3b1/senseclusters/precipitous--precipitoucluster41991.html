Done suddenly or with very great haste and without careful consideration; rash or impulsive.

Examples

Precipitous decision
His precipitous resignation left the team scrambling for leadership.
She regretted her precipitous move to another city, feeling she hadn't thought it through.
The company made a precipitous decision to close the branch without consulting the staff.

Synonyms

    7.6 0.03/m
    precipitate describes an action done with excessive haste, while precipitous means sudden and rash without careful thought.
  • overhasty
    7.2 0.01/m
    overhasty means done too quickly with little thought, while precipitous suggests sudden, reckless action that lacks careful consideration.
  • hasty
    3.9 3.71/m
    hasty describes something done quickly and without enough thought, while precipitous suggests a more sudden, extreme, or rash act.
