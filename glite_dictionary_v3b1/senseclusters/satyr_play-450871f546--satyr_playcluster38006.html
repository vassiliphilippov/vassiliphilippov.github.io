satyr play

noun
ancient Greek comic play with satyrs
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.tɚ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.tə pleɪ / (BrE)
an ancient Greek play that combines elements of tragedy with burlesque comedy, featuring a chorus of satyrs

Examples

An ancient satyr play
In ancient Athens, every set of tragedies was followed by a satyr play for comic relief.
The chorus in a satyr play was traditionally made up of actors dressed as satyrs.
The festival concluded with a satyr play to lighten the mood after the tragedies.
