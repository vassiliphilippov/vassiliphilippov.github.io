sharif

noun
Muslim noble title
8.9
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈriːf / (AmE), / ʃəˈriːf / (BrE)
a Muslim nobleman, especially one who claims descent from the Prophet Muhammad, used as a title of honor

Examples

The sharif of Mecca
The sharif of Mecca played an important role in regional history.
He was honored with the title of sharif for his noble lineage.
Many people respected the sharif for his leadership and wisdom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caliph - the chief Muslim civil and religious ruler
  • synonym: emir - a Muslim ruler or prince
