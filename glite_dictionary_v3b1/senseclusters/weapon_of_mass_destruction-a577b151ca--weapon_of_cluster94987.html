weapon of mass destruction

noun
a destructive weapon affecting large populations
3.6 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛpən əv ˌmæs dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɛpən əv ˌmæs dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
a weapon that is capable of causing large-scale death or injury to civilians as well as military personnel, especially nuclear, chemical, or biological weapons

Examples

Nuclear weapon of mass destruction
Concerns about rogue states developing weapons of mass destruction have led to increased global security measures.
The international community strongly condemns the use of weapons of mass destruction in conflict zones.
The treaty aims to prevent the spread of weapons of mass destruction around the world.

  • synonym: chemical weapon - a weapon that uses chemicals formulated to inflict death or harm
  • synonym: nuclear weapon - a bomb or missile that uses nuclear energy to cause an explosion
