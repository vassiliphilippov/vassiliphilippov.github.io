eyedropper

noun
liquid measuring tool
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌdrɑpər / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌdrɒpə / (BrE)
a small tool used to draw up and release drops of liquid in a controlled way, often for medicine or laboratory use

Examples

A eyedropper bottle
She used an eyedropper to add medicine to the solution.
The artist carefully applied ink to the paper with an eyedropper.
You can use an eyedropper to measure out small amounts of liquid for your experiment.
She used an eyedropper to add medicine to the water.
The scientist carefully transferred the solution with an eyedropper to the test tube.
You can use an eyedropper to apply just a few drops of essential oil.
