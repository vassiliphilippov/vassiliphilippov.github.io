shade

verb
to vary something slightly
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪd / (AmE), / ʃeɪd / (BrE)
to change or adjust something slightly, especially in a subtle or gradual way (such as tone, color, or meaning)

Examples

To shade the meaning
By shading her tone, she communicated disapproval without saying it outright.
He tried to shade his statement to avoid offending anyone.
The author decided to shade the argument to suggest a softer position.
Her hair shaded gradually from dark brown to blonde at the tips.
In the painting, the colors shade seamlessly into each other, creating a soft gradient effect.
The blue of the sky shades to a lighter hue near the horizon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nuance - to give subtle differences to
