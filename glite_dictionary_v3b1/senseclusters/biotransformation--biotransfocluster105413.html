biotransformation

noun
chemical change by organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˌtrænsfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˌtrænsfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the chemical modification of a substance by living organisms or cells, often to make it easier to eliminate

Examples

Liver biotransformation
Many drugs are removed from the body through biotransformation in the liver.
Scientists study biotransformation to understand how pollutants are broken down by bacteria.
The process of biotransformation can change a toxic compound into a harmless one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degradation - the breakdown of substances into simpler components
  • synonym: metabolism - the chemical processes that occur within a living organism
