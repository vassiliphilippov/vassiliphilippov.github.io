latency

noun
state of being inactive or hidden
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.tən.si / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tən.si / (BrE)
The state or period in which something exists but is not yet visible, active, or evident.

Examples

A period of latency
Innovation often undergoes a phase of latency before becoming widespread.
The virus can remain in latency for years before symptoms appear.
There was a long period of latency before the volcano erupted.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manifestation - the process of becoming evident or active
