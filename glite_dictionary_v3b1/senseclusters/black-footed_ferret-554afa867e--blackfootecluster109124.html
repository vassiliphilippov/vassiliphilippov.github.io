black-footed ferret

noun
rare prairie mammal of North America
Pronunciation: / ˌblæk ˈfʊt̬ɪd ˈfɛrɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ˈfʊtɪd ˈfɛrɪt / (BrE)
a rare mustelid mammal (Mustela nigripes) native to the prairie regions of the United States, distinguished by its black feet and mask

Examples

The black-footed ferret species
Researchers are tracking black-footed ferrets to monitor the success of breeding programs.
The black-footed ferret relies on prairie dogs for both food and burrows.
The black-footed ferret was once thought to be extinct but has been reintroduced to several sites in the wild.
