cadaverous

adjective
corpse-like in appearance
5.0 7.2
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈdæv.ər.əs/ /; / /kə.ˈdæv.rəs/ (AmE), /kə.ˈdæv.ər.əs/ /; / /kə.ˈdæv.rəs/ (BrE)
very pale, thin, or gaunt; looking like a corpse, especially because of illness, hunger, or cold

Examples

A cadaverous face
After weeks of illness, his cadaverous face alarmed his friends.
She wore makeup to conceal her cadaverous pallor at the party.
The refugees looked cadaverous after days without food.
After weeks at sea with little food, the sailors' faces looked cadaverous.
Her cadaverous appearance shocked everyone at the reunion.
The patient was so cadaverous that the doctors feared for his life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corpse-like - resembling a dead body
  • synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food
  • synonym: gaunt - very thin and bony, especially because of illness or hunger
  • antonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • antonym: plump - having a full and rounded shape
