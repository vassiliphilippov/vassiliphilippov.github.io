form

noun
biological subgroup within a species
3.4 6.7
Pronunciation: / fɔrm / (AmE), / fɔːm / (BrE)
(biology) a group of organisms within a species that differs insignificantly from similar groups, often in minor physical or genetic traits.

Examples

Researchers are studying a new form of bacteria resistant to antibiotics.

The scientist identified a distinct form of the butterfly that only inhabits high-altitude meadows.
