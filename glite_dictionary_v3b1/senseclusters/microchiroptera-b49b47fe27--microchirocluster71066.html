Microchiroptera

noun
group of insect-eating bats (excluding fruit bats)
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˌkaɪˈrɒptərə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊkaɪˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
a former suborder of bats comprising most bat species except fruit bats, typically insectivorous and having a complex echolocation system

Examples

Microchiroptera bat species
Many caves across Asia and Africa are inhabited by Microchiroptera colonies.
The majority of bat species belong to the Microchiroptera, which are primarily insectivorous.
Unlike fruit bats, Microchiroptera use echolocation to navigate and hunt for food.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Megachiroptera - group of large, fruit-eating bats
