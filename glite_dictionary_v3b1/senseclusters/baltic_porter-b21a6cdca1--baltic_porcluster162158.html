Baltic porter

noun
a dark, strong lager beer from the Baltic region
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔltɪk ˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈbɔːltɪk ˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a dark, strong lager-style beer originating from countries around the Baltic Sea, typically fermented at cool temperatures

Examples

A Baltic porter beer
After visiting Poland, she became a fan of locally brewed Baltic porters.
He ordered a Baltic porter to pair with his hearty stew at dinner.
The brewery's award-winning Baltic porter is famous for its rich, complex flavor.
