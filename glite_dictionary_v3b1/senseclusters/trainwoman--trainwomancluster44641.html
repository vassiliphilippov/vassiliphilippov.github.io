trainwoman

noun
female railroad employee
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪnwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnwʊmən / (BrE)
a female employee who works for a railroad, especially in train operations or service

Examples

A trainwoman on duty
As a trainwoman, she was responsible for assisting passengers and ensuring safety on board.
Many young women now aspire to become a trainwoman and work on the railways.
The first trainwoman in the company was hired in 1978.
