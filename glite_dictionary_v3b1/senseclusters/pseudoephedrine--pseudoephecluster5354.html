pseudoephedrine

noun
stimulant alkaloid and decongestant
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊɪˈfɛdrɪn / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊɪˈfiːdrɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous crystalline alkaloid found with ephedrine in certain plants, used as a decongestant in medicine

Examples

Pseudoephedrine medicine
Pseudoephedrine is commonly found in over-the-counter cold and allergy medications.
Patients with high blood pressure should consult a doctor before taking pseudoephedrine.
Some states restrict the sale of pseudoephedrine due to its potential for misuse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ephedrine - an alkaloid similar in structure and function, used as a stimulant and decongestant
