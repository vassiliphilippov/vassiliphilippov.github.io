mesocosm

noun
experimental environment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛsəˌkɑzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛsəˌkɒzəm / (BrE)
a controlled, medium-scale environment or system used for scientific experiments to study natural or ecological processes under semi-natural conditions

Examples

A mesocosm experiment
Scientists built a mesocosm to observe how pollution affects freshwater ecosystems.
The research team monitored temperature changes inside the mesocosm over several months.
A mesocosm allows researchers to control variables while studying natural communities.
The scientists set up a mesocosm to simulate the effects of pollution on aquatic plants.
Each mesocosm was carefully monitored to track changes in water quality over time.
By using a mesocosm, researchers can control variables that are difficult to manage in the wild.
Scientists set up a mesocosm in the laboratory to observe the effects of pollution on aquatic life.
The research team used a coastal lagoon as a mesocosm to study interactions among birds, fish, and plants.
By controlling variables in the mesocosm, ecologists could better understand ecosystem responses to climate change.
