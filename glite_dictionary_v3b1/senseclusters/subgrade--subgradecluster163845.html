subgrade

verb
to prepare ground base
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
to prepare or shape the soil or ground to form a stable base for a road, railway, or pavement

Examples

To subgrade the ground
Before construction begins, workers must subgrade the area to ensure a solid foundation.
The team will subgrade the site to meet the engineering specifications for the new road.
Properly subgraded ground helps prevent future pavement cracks and uneven surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excavate - to dig out and remove earth
  • synonym: grade - to level or smooth the ground for construction
