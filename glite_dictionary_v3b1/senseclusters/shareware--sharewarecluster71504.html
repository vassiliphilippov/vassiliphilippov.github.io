shareware

noun
software distributed for free trial or evaluation
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛrˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈʃeəˌweə / (BrE)
software that is initially distributed free of charge, often for evaluation, with payment requested for continued use or to access additional features

Examples

The shareware program
If you like the shareware, you can pay to unlock all the features and support the creator.
Many small developers use shareware to distribute their programs before charging for the full version.
The internet made shareware popular by making it easy for users to try new software before buying.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: commercial software - software sold for profit, requiring full payment before use
  • degree lower: freeware - software distributed at no cost with no payment required
