impose

verb
force or compel (behavior, obligation)
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to force someone to accept something, especially something unwelcome or by using authority or pressure

Examples

To impose ideas
Parents sometimes impose their own values on their children.
The government cannot impose change without public support.
The new manager tried to impose stricter rules on the staff.
I don’t want to impose my beliefs on anyone else.
The teacher decided to impose a strict new rule after the incident.
These taxes impose an unfair burden on small businesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - permit, not prevent or compel
  • synonym: compel - force or oblige someone to do something
  • synonym: enforce - make people obey something, such as a rule or law
  • synonym: inflict - to cause something unpleasant to be suffered by someone or something
