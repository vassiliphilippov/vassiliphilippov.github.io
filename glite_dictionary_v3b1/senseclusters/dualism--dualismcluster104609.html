dualism

noun
philosophical theory of two basic opposing principles
Pronunciation: / ˈduːəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that reality is fundamentally composed of two opposing or contrasted principles, such as mind and matter, or good and evil.

Examples

Mind-body dualism
Her essay critiques dualism by arguing for a more integrated view of reality.
Religious traditions often grapple with dualism between good and evil.
The philosopher explained dualism as the view that mind and body are distinct kinds of substance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monism - the doctrine that only one supreme being or principle exists
