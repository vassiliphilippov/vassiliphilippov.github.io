arterial blood gases

noun
test measuring blood gases and pH in arteries
9.0
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈtɪriəl blʌd ˈɡæsɪz / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriəl blʌd ˈɡæsɪz / (BrE)
a test that measures the levels of oxygen, carbon dioxide, and pH in arterial blood, used to assess lung function and detect respiratory diseases

Examples

Test of arterial blood gases
A sudden drop in arterial blood gases can indicate a serious problem with the patient's breathing.
Nurses are trained to draw samples for arterial blood gases in critical care settings.
The doctor ordered arterial blood gases to evaluate her respiratory status after the surgery.
