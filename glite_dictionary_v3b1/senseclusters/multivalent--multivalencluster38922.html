multivalent

adjective
having multiple values or meanings
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
having many values, meanings, or appeals; capable of being interpreted or applied in several ways

Examples

Multivalent meaning
Critics praised the movie for its multivalent symbolism and rich layers of meaning.
Her sculptures are intentionally multivalent, conveying different emotions to each viewer.
The novel offers a multivalent narrative open to diverse interpretations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: univalent - having only one value or meaning
