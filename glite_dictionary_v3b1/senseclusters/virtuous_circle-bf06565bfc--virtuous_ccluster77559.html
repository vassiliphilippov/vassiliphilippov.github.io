virtuous circle

noun
beneficial cycle of positive events
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝːtʃuəs ˈsɝːkəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtjuəs ˈsɜːkəl / (BrE)
a situation in which one good action or event causes another that supports or reinforces the first, resulting in a beneficial cycle

Examples

A virtuous circle of trust
Community involvement can create a virtuous circle of trust, cooperation, and shared progress.
The scholarship program set off a virtuous circle by helping students who then inspired others to succeed.
When companies prioritize employee wellbeing, it often leads to a virtuous circle of higher morale and greater productivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vicious circle - a sequence of events in which one problem causes another and worsens the original problem
