priestess

noun
female priest or religious leader
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːstɪs / (AmE), / ˈpriːstɪs / (BrE)
a woman who performs religious duties and ceremonies as a priest, especially in certain religions or historical contexts

Examples

A priestess in temple
In ancient Greece, a priestess would often serve at the temple of Athena.
She was ordained as a priestess in her religious community.
The village priestess led the ceremony to honor the harvest gods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy
  • synonym: priest - a person authorized to perform religious rites, especially in Christian churches
