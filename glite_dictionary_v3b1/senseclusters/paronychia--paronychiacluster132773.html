paronychia

noun
infection near a nail
Pronunciation: / ˌpæroʊˈnɪkiə / (AmE), / ˌparəˈnɪkɪə / (BrE)
An infection of the tissue surrounding a fingernail or toenail, typically causing redness, swelling, and pain.

Examples

Nail paronychia infection
He treated the mild paronychia at home, but severe cases may require antibiotics.
Improper nail trimming can sometimes lead to paronychia in both fingers and toes.
The patient was diagnosed with paronychia after developing swelling and redness around her fingernail.
