Robert the Bruce

proper noun
King of Scotland, 1306–1329
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːbərt ðə bruːs / (AmE), / ˈrɒbət ðə bruːs / (BrE)
(1274–1329) King of Scotland from 1306 to 1329, who led the Scots to victory over the English at the Battle of Bannockburn and secured Scottish independence.

Examples

Robert the Bruce king
Robert the Bruce is remembered as a national hero for leading Scotland to independence.
Many Scottish legends and tales celebrate the bravery of Robert the Bruce.
The Battle of Bannockburn was a turning point in history, thanks to Robert the Bruce's leadership.
