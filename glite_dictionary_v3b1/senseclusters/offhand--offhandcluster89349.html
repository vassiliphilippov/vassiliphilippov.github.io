offhand

adjective, adverb
without preparation; impromptu
8.5 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈhænd /; / ˈɔfˌhænd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhænd / (BrE)
without previous thought or preparation; done or said immediately and without careful consideration (adverb or adjective)

Examples

Offhand answer
He didn't know the exact number offhand, but he thought it was around fifty.
I can't answer that question offhand, but I'll check and get back to you.
She made a suggestion offhand during the meeting that everyone liked.
His offhand remarks during the meeting surprised everyone with their honesty.
I can't recall the details offhand, but I can look them up for you.
She gave an offhand response to the question, without thinking it through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extemporaneous - spoken or done without preparation
  • synonym: impromptu - done without being planned or rehearsed
  • antonym: prepared - done with prior thought or planning
  • synonym: spontaneously - in a way that is not planned, but done freely
