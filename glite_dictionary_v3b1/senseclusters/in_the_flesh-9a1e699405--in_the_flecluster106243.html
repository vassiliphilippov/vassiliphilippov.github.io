in the flesh

adverb
in person; physically present
5.1
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə flɛʃ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə fleʃ / (BrE)
used to refer to seeing or meeting someone in person, as opposed to seeing them in a photograph, on television, etc.

Examples

Meet in the flesh
Although I'd chatted with her online for years, it was amazing to finally see her in the flesh.
I couldn't believe I was meeting the famous author in the flesh at the signing event.
Many fans hope to see their favorite celebrities in the flesh at concerts or public events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in person - physically present, not represented by a photograph or video
