nipa palm

noun
tropical palm found in mangrove swamps
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàniÀêp…ô p…ëm / (AmE), / ÀàniÀêp…ô p…ëÀêm / (BrE)
a creeping semi-aquatic palm of the genus Nipa found in mangrove swamps and tidal estuaries, valued for its sap (used to make liquor), thatching leaves, and edible seeds

Examples

The nipa palm tree
Farmers collect the sweet sap from the nipa palm to produce a local alcoholic beverage.
The fruit of the nipa palm contains edible seeds enjoyed as a snack in many countries.
The leaves of the nipa palm are traditionally used for thatching roofs in Southeast Asia.

Synonyms

  • Nipa fruticans
    9.9 0.00/m
    Nipa fruticans is the specific species with edible seeds, sap for liquor, and thatching leaves, while nipa palm is the common name.
  • Nypa
    9.9 0.03/m
    Nypa is the formal scientific genus name used in botanical texts, while nipa palm is the common name used in everyday speech.
  • Nipa
    9.9 0.00/m
    Nipa refers to the group of tropical coastal palms, while nipa palm names the common species used for sap, thatch, and food.
