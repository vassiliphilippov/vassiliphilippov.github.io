spectrography

noun
analysis of light spectrum
7.9
Pronunciation: /spek.trə.ˈgræf.i/ (AmE), /spek.trə.ˈgræf.i/ (BrE)
a scientific technique for recording and analyzing the spectrum of light (or other electromagnetic radiation) to study the properties or composition of materials

Examples

Spectrography of light
Scientists use spectrography to determine the chemical composition of unknown substances.
Advances in spectrography have improved our understanding of distant galaxies.
The laboratory relies on spectrography to analyze samples from environmental studies.
The laboratory relied on spectrography to analyze the chemical composition of the samples.
Advances in spectrography have improved our understanding of distant stars and galaxies.
Medical researchers use spectrography to detect subtle changes in tissue samples.
