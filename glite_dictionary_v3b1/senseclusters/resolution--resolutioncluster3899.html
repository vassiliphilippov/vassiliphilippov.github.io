resolution

noun
musical progression from dissonance to consonance
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛzəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
(Music) The progression of a dissonant chord to a consonant chord, creating a sense of release or completion.

Examples

Chord resolution
In classical music, composers often use resolution to create and then release musical tension.
The resolution of the dissonant interval gave a sense of closure to the phrase.
The piece ended with the resolution of the final chord, leaving listeners satisfied.
