Grinch

proper noun
Dr. Seuss character
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪntʃ / (AmE), / ɡrɪntʃ / (BrE)
the main character in Dr. Seuss's story 'How the Grinch Stole Christmas!', known for disliking Christmas but eventually embracing its spirit

Examples

The Grinch character
The Grinch tried to steal Christmas from the people of Whoville but learned the true meaning of the holiday.
Many children enjoy reading about the Grinch and his change of heart during Christmas.
In the movie adaptation, the Grinch is portrayed as both grumpy and lovable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Scrooge - a character from Dickens's 'A Christmas Carol' who dislikes Christmas
