trepanation

noun
circular hole boring
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɛpəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrɛpəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of boring a circular hole or removing a core from a material such as metal, wood, stone, or concrete using a trepan or similar tool

Examples

Stone trepanation
The construction crew performed trepanation to extract a core sample from the stone pillar.
Modern mining techniques often use trepanation to create precise openings in rock formations.
Engineers rely on trepanation when they need to remove a circular section from a metal plate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cutting - the act of dividing or opening something with a sharp tool
  • synonym: drilling - the act of making a hole in a material using a drill
