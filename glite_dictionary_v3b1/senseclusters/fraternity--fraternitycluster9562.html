fraternity

noun
group of people in the same profession
5.5 0.91/m
Pronunciation: / fr…ôÀàt…úrn…ôti / (AmE), / fr…ôÀàt…úÀên…ôti / (BrE)
all the people who share a particular profession or occupation, regarded as a group; a professional or occupational brotherhood

Examples

Medical fraternity
She was highly respected by the legal fraternity for her integrity and expertise.
The fraternity of scientists welcomed new research in their field.
The entire journalism fraternity mourned the loss of their colleague.

Synonyms

  • brotherhood
    3.7 3.10/m
    brotherhood describes a close, loyal group of people united by shared goals and support, while fraternity focuses on professional or occupational ties.
  • sodality
    9.9 0.16/m
    sodality is a formal or older word for an association of people with a shared purpose, while fraternity stresses an occupational brotherhood.
  • fellowship
    3.9 3.01/m
    fellowship often means a friendly, interest-based association and shared time, while fraternity usually refers to a professional brotherhood.
