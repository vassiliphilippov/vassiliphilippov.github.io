chemoresistance

noun
resistance to chemicals
8.2
Pronunciation: /ke.moʊ.rɪ.ˈzɪs.təns/ /; / /kə.moʊ.rɪ.ˈzɪs.təns/ (AmE), /ke.məʊ.rɪ.ˈzɪs.təns/ /; / /kə.məʊ.rɪ.ˈzɪs.təns/ (BrE)
the ability of microorganisms, parasites, or pests to survive and continue growing despite treatment with chemical agents such as drugs or pesticides

Examples

Bacterial chemoresistance
The spread of chemoresistance among bacteria makes infections harder to treat.
Researchers are developing new drugs to overcome chemoresistance in cancer cells.
Farmers are concerned about chemoresistance in pests affecting their crops.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chemosensitivity - the susceptibility of organisms to chemical agents
  • synonym: drug resistance - the ability of organisms to withstand the effects of a drug
