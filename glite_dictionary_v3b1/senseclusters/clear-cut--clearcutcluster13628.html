clear-cut

verb
remove all trees from an area at once
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌkʌt / (BrE)
to remove all the trees from an area of land at one time, especially as part of logging operations.

Examples

To clear-cut a hillside
After they clear-cut the land, the landscape changed dramatically.
Environmentalists are protesting the decision to clear-cut the entire valley.
The company plans to clear-cut 200 acres of forest this year.
