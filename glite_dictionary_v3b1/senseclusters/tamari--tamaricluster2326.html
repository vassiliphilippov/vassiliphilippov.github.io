tamari

noun
Japanese soy sauce
6.6
Pronunciation: / təˈmɑːri / (AmE), / təˈmɑːri / (BrE)
a type of dark, rich soy sauce used in Japanese cooking, often wheat-free and with a strong flavor

Examples

Dark tamari sauce
She prefers to use tamari instead of regular soy sauce in her recipes.
The chef recommended adding a splash of tamari to enhance the umami flavor.
For a gluten-free option, many people choose tamari when cooking Asian dishes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shoyu - a Japanese soy sauce made with both soybeans and wheat
  • synonym: soy sauce - a salty, dark sauce made from fermented soybeans
