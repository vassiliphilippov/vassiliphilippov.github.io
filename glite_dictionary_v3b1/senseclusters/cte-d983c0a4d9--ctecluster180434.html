CTE

noun
thermal expansion property
8.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / si ti i / (AmE), / siÀê tiÀê iÀê / (BrE)
the coefficient of thermal expansion, a property that measures how much a material expands as its temperature increases

Examples

Metal CTE value
The CTE of metals is important when designing parts that will experience temperature changes.
Different materials have different CTE values, which engineers must consider in construction.
If the CTE is too high, the material may crack when exposed to heat.

Synonyms

  • coefficient of expansion
    7.6 0.01/m
    coefficient of expansion is a ratio showing fractional length or volume change per degree, while CTE names the thermal expansion property.
  • expansivity
    9.8 0.00/m
    expansivity is the measured rate of size change with temperature, while CTE names the standard coefficient used to quantify thermal expansion.

Related Terms
