CTE

noun
thermal expansion property
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.tiː.iː/ /; / /ˈsiː.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.tiː.iː/ /; / /ˈsiː.tiː/ (BrE)
the coefficient of thermal expansion, a property that measures how much a material expands as its temperature increases

Examples

Metal CTE value
The CTE of metals is important when designing parts that will experience temperature changes.
Different materials have different CTE values, which engineers must consider in construction.
If the CTE is too high, the material may crack when exposed to heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermal expansion - the increase in size of a material as its temperature rises
