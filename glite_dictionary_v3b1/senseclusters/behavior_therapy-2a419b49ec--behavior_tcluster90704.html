behavior therapy

noun
psychotherapy focused on changing behavior patterns
3.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /bɪ.ˈheɪ.vər ˈθɛr.ə.pi/ /; / /bɪ.ˈheɪ.vjər ˈθɛr.ə.pi/ (AmE), /bɪ.ˈheɪ.və ˈθɛr.ə.pi/ /; / /bɪ.ˈheɪ.vjə ˈθɛr.ə.pi/ (BrE)
A form of psychotherapy that aims to change maladaptive or unwanted behaviors by reinforcing desired behaviors and reducing undesired ones.

Examples

Behavior therapy techniques
Behavior therapy focuses on changing specific actions rather than exploring past experiences.
Her doctor recommended behavior therapy to help manage her anxiety.
Many children benefit from behavior therapy to address disruptive behaviors in school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cognitive-behavioral therapy - a related approach combining behavioral and cognitive techniques
