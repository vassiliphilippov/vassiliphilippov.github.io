ostracod

noun
tiny shell-encased crustacean
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɑː.strə.kɑd / (AmE), / ˈɒs.trə.kɒd / (BrE)
A tiny marine or freshwater crustacean with a shrimp-like body enclosed in a bivalve shell.

Examples

Tiny ostracod shell
Fossilized ostracods can provide important information about ancient aquatic environments.
Some species of ostracod emit light through bioluminescence.
The scientist collected several ostracods from the pond for microscopic examination.
