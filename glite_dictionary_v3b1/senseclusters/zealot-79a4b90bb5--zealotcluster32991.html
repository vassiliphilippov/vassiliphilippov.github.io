Zealot

noun
member of an ancient Jewish sect opposing Roman rule
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈzɛlət / (AmE), / ˈzɛlət / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Jewish sect in Judea during the first century, known for their uncompromising opposition to Roman rule and for using violence against Jews who collaborated with the Romans

Examples

The Zealots in Judea
Historians often debate the methods and motivations of the Zealots in first-century Judea.
Some members of the Zealots believed in resisting Roman authority at any cost.
The Zealot movement played a significant role in sparking the First Jewish-Roman War.
