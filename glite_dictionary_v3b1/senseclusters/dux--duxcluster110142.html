dux

noun
medieval noble title
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / dÊŒks / (AmE), / dÊŒks / (BrE)
a noble title equivalent to 'duke', used in medieval or Latin contexts for a hereditary or territorial ruler

Examples

Medieval dux
In medieval documents, the ruler was often referred to as the dux of the province.
The title of dux signified great authority and responsibility in the region.
Historians studied the role of the dux in shaping early European politics.

Synonyms
