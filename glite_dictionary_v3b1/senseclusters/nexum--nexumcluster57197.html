nexum

noun
Roman debt contract
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛksəm / (AmE), / ˈnɛksəm / (BrE)
an ancient Roman contract in which a debtor pledged personal service or bondage as security for a loan

Examples

Ancient nexum contract
In ancient Rome, a nexum could result in a debtor becoming a bondservant until the debt was paid.
The abolition of nexum marked a significant change in Roman legal history.
Scholars debate the exact terms and social impact of the nexum contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bondage - the state of being bound or enslaved
  • synonym: debt slavery - a condition where a person is forced to work to pay off a debt
