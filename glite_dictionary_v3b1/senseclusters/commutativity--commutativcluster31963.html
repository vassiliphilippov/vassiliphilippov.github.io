commutativity

noun
order-independent property
Pronunciation: / kəˌmjuːtəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːtəˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the property of an operation in which changing the order of the elements does not change the result

Examples

Commutativity of addition
Multiplication of real numbers shows commutativity because 3 × 4 equals 4 × 3.
In mathematics, commutativity is an important concept for understanding different operations.
Matrix multiplication does not have commutativity, so the order of multiplication matters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: associativity - the property of grouping elements without changing the result
  • antonym: noncommutativity - the property of an operation where order affects the result
