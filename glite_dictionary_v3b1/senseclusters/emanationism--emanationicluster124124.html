emanationism

noun
philosophical origin theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /em.ə.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /em.ə.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the philosophical theory that all things originate by flowing out from a single source or principle

Examples

Emanationism theory
Many ancient philosophers discussed emanationism as an explanation for the origin of the universe.
The doctrine of emanationism suggests that everything flows from a single, ultimate reality.
Some modern thinkers have revisited emanationism in discussions about the nature of existence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creationism - the belief that the universe and life originate from specific acts of divine creation
  • synonym: neoplatonism - a philosophical system that emphasizes the emanation of all things from a single principle
