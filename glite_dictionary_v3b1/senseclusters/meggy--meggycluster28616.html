meggy

noun
sour cherry tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡi / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡi / (BrE)
a sour cherry tree (Prunus cerasus) or its small, tart cherries, often used in cooking and preserves, especially in Hungarian cuisine

Examples

Meggy jam
They made delicious jam from the meggy growing in their backyard.
A mature meggy can provide both shade and plenty of tart fruit in summer.
The recipe calls for a handful of fresh meggy to add a sour flavor.
The traditional Hungarian pie was filled with meggy to give it a tangy taste.
She bought a jar of meggy jam from the local market.
In summer, many desserts in Hungary feature fresh meggy as a main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sour cherry - a small, tart cherry from the Prunus cerasus tree
  • antonym: sweet cherry - a cherry from the Prunus avium tree, sweeter than the sour cherry
