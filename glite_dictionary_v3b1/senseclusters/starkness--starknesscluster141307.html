starkness

noun
extreme plainness; lack of decoration
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑrknɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɑːknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having an extreme lack of furnishings, decoration, or ornamentation; severe plainness

Examples

Starkness in design
Many modern buildings embrace starkness in their interior design to create a minimalist atmosphere.
She was impressed by the starkness of the mountain landscape, with its bare rocks and sparse vegetation.
The starkness of the empty room made it feel cold and unwelcoming.

Related Terms

  • synonym: austerity - plainness and simplicity, especially relating to style and decoration
  • antonym: ornateness - the quality of being highly decorated or ornamented
