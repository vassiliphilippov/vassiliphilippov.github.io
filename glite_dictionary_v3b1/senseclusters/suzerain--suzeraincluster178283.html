suzerain

noun
a state with sovereignty over a dependent state
9.9 9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːzəˌreɪn / (AmE), / ˈsjuːzəreɪn / (BrE)
A state that has control over the foreign affairs of another, dependent state, often without direct internal control.

Examples

The suzerain state
During the 19th century, the Ottoman Empire served as the suzerain of several Balkan territories.
The smaller kingdom became a vassal under the protection of its suzerain.
Treaties often clarified the rights and duties of the suzerain over its dependent states.
