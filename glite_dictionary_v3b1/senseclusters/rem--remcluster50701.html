rem

noun
unit of radiation dosage (biological effect)
9.4
Pronunciation: /rem/ (AmE), /rem/ (BrE)
a unit of measurement for ionizing radiation, representing the dosage that causes the same biological effect as one roentgen of X-rays in human tissue

Examples

One rem dose
Acute exposure to several hundred rem can cause severe health effects.
Laboratory signs warned of areas where rem levels were dangerously high.
The technician measured the cumulative exposure in rem to ensure safety.
