unexecuted

adjective
not carried out
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈɛksɪˌkjuːtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɛksɪˌkjuːtɪd / (BrE)
not carried out, completed, or made legally effective (especially about contracts or documents)

Examples

An unexecuted contract
The contract remained unexecuted because both parties failed to sign it.
Several of the architect's designs were left unexecuted after his death.
Due to budget cuts, many proposed projects stayed unexecuted for years.
The contract remained unexecuted because both parties had not signed it.
An unexecuted will has no legal effect until it is properly signed and witnessed.
The agreement was still unexecuted at the end of the negotiation process.
