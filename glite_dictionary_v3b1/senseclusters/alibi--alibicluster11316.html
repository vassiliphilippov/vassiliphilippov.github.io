alibi

noun
excuse or pretext to justify failings
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælɪbaɪ / (BrE)
an excuse or explanation offered to avoid blame or justify failing to do something

Examples

A clever alibi
Her alibi for not finishing the assignment was that she had computer issues.
The manager grew tired of hearing the same alibi every time a deadline was missed.
Whenever he missed a meeting, he always had a new alibi to explain his absence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation given to justify a fault or offense
  • synonym: justification - the act of showing something to be right or reasonable
