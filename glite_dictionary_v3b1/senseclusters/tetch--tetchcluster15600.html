tetch

verb
to irritate
Pronunciation: / tɛtʃ / (AmE), / tɛtʃ / (BrE)
to irritate or make someone annoyed; now rare or obsolete

Examples

To tetch someone
In old novels, a rude remark might tetch a character into anger.
The constant noise would easily tetch even the calmest person in those days.
Writers once used the word tetch to describe provoking someone's temper.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • synonym: irritate - to make someone feel annoyed or impatient
  • antonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied
