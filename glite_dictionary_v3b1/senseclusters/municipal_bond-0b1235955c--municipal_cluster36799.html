municipal bond

noun
bond issued by local government
7.5 3.6
Pronunciation: /mjuː.ˈnɪs.ə.pəl bɑːnd/ (AmE), /mjuː.ˈnɪs.ɪ.pəl bɒnd/ /; / /mjuː.ˈnɪs.ə.pəl bɒnd/ (BrE)
A bond issued by a city, state, or local government to finance public projects, typically exempt from federal taxes.

Examples

A municipal bond issue
Interest earned from a municipal bond is often free from federal income tax.
Many investors purchase municipal bonds to benefit from tax-exempt interest income.
The city issued a municipal bond to fund the construction of the new library.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corporate bond - bond issued by a corporation, not a government
  • synonym: government bond - bond issued by national or local government
