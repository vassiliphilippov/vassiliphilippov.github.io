pancreatic vein

noun
vein draining blood from the pancreas
6.8 5.0
Pronunciation: / pæn.kriˈæt.ɪk veɪn / (AmE), / pan.krɪˈat.ɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the pancreas and empties into the superior mesenteric and splenic veins

Examples

The pancreatic vein drains
Blockage of a pancreatic vein can lead to serious complications like pancreatitis.
Surgeons must identify the pancreatic vein during certain procedures involving the pancreas.
The pancreatic vein plays a crucial role in returning blood from the pancreas to the portal system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superior mesenteric vein - vein draining blood from the small intestine and part of the large intestine
