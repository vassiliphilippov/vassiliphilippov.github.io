large-leafed

adjective
having large leaves
Pronunciation: / lɑrdʒˈliːft / (AmE), / lɑːdʒˈliːft / (BrE)
(of a plant) having relatively large leaves.

Examples

A large-leafed plant
Among the ferns, the large-leafed varieties offer dramatic foliage for decorative pots.
Many tropical plants are large-leafed, making them popular choices for creating a jungle-like atmosphere indoors.
The large-leafed hosta thrives in shady gardens and adds lush texture to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broad-leaved - having broad leaves
  • antonym: small-leafed - having relatively small leaves
