squirm

verb
wriggle or twist, often from discomfort
3.9 5.70/m
Pronunciation: / skw…ùm / (AmE), / skw…úÀêm / (BrE)
to twist or wriggle one's body with small movements, especially as a result of discomfort, embarrassment, or struggle

Examples

To squirm in a chair
She tried not to squirm while the dentist examined her tooth.
The captured fish continued to squirm in the net.
The little boy began to squirm in his seat during the long ceremony.
