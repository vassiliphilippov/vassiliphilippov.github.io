mesne

noun
intermediate landlord
Pronunciation: / miːn / (AmE), / miːn / (BrE)
an intermediate person or party, especially a feudal lord who holds land from a superior and grants it to a tenant

Examples

Mesne lord
In medieval England, a mesne lord could both receive and grant land rights.
The mesne collected rents from tenants and paid dues to the overlord.
Legal disputes sometimes arose between the mesne and those above or below in the feudal hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overlord - a person of superior rank who grants land to a vassal
  • antonym: tenant - a person who holds or rents land from a landlord
