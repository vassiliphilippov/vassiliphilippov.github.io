undercut

noun
notch in a tree trunk to guide its fall
4.7 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.kʌt/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.kʌt/ (BrE)
A notch cut into the trunk of a tree to control the direction in which it will fall when being felled.

Examples

Tree undercut
A proper undercut ensures the tree falls safely in the intended direction.
Before felling the oak, they checked the depth and angle of the undercut.
The lumberjack carefully made an undercut before finishing the back cut on the old pine tree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: notch - a small cut or indentation on an edge or surface
