recapitalize

verb
to add new capital
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkæpɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to provide new capital or funds to a business or organization, or to reorganize its finances to improve financial stability

Examples

To recapitalize a bank
The government decided to recapitalize the national bank to prevent a financial crisis.
After suffering heavy losses, the company was recapitalized by new investors.
The board approved a plan to recapitalize the business and restore its stability.
The government decided to recapitalize the struggling bank to prevent its collapse.
After suffering heavy losses, the company was recapitalized by a group of investors.
The board voted to recapitalize the organization to support its expansion plans.
The board decided to recapitalize the company after several years of declining profits.
To avoid bankruptcy, the bank was forced to recapitalize with help from investors.
The government offered to recapitalize struggling industries during the economic crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liquidate - to close a business and sell its assets
  • synonym: refinance - to provide new financing or restructure existing debt
  • synonym: restructure - to organize something in a new way, especially a business or system
