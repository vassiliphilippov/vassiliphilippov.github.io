heretical

adjective
departing from accepted beliefs
Pronunciation: / həˈrɛt̬ɪkəl / (AmE), / həˈrɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Holding or expressing views that depart from accepted beliefs or standards, especially in religion or ideology.

Examples

A heretical idea
His heretical ideas challenged the established doctrines of the church.
She was labeled heretical for questioning the traditional practices of her community.
The scientist faced criticism for proposing a heretical theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orthodox - following or conforming to traditional beliefs or standards
