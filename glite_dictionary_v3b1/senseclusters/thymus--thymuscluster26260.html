thymus

noun
immune system gland in the neck
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.məs / (BrE)
A glandular organ at the base of the neck that produces lymphocytes and plays a role in immune system development, especially during childhood.

Examples

The thymus gland
A healthy thymus is important for the development of the immune system.
Doctors monitor the thymus in patients with certain immune disorders.
The thymus is most active during childhood and gradually shrinks in adults.
