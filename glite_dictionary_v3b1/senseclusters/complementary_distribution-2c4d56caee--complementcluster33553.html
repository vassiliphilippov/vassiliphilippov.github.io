complementary distribution

noun
linguistic phenomenon where related forms never appear in the same context
7.5 8.0
Pronunciation: /kɑːm.plə.ˈmen.tər.i dɪ.strɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ /; / /kɑːm.plə.ˈmen.triː dɪ.strɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒm.plɪ.ˈmen.tə.ri dɪ.strɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ /; / /kɒm.plɪ.ˈmen.triː dɪ.strɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
(Linguistics) The distribution of two or more linguistically related sounds or forms such that each occurs in different, mutually exclusive contexts and not in the same environment.

Examples

complementary distribution of sounds
English [p] and [ph] are in complementary distribution, as they never appear in the same phonetic environment.
If two sounds are in complementary distribution, they are considered allophones of the same phoneme.
The linguistics professor explained complementary distribution using examples from several different languages.
