shortleaf pine

noun
pine tree native to southern US
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrtˌlif paɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtˌliːf paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree native to the southern United States, with short needles in bundles of two or three and reddish-brown mature bark

Examples

A shortleaf pine tree
A mature shortleaf pine can reach a height of over 30 meters.
The shortleaf pine is valued for its timber and resistance to disease.
The dense forests of Arkansas are home to many shortleaf pines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loblolly pine - a common pine of the Southeast US with long needles and large cones
