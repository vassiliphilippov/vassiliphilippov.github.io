tramp down

verb
flatten by walking on repeatedly
Pronunciation: / træmp daʊn / (AmE), / tramp daʊn / (BrE)
to flatten something by walking heavily on it repeatedly

Examples

To tramp down grass
The children tramped down the long grass along the path.
We need to tramp down the soil before planting the seeds.
Workers tramp down loose gravel to create a firm surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trample - to tread heavily so as to crush or injure
