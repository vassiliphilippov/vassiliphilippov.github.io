drachm

noun
British unit of capacity (3.55 ml)
Pronunciation: / dræm / (AmE), / dræm / (BrE)
a small unit of capacity (volume), used in the British imperial or apothecary systems

Examples

A drachm of liquid
A recipe from the 19th century called for half a drachm of saffron.
In historical texts, measurements such as the drachm are commonly found.
The old apothecary carefully measured one drachm of the tincture for the prescription.
A drachm was once a common unit in apothecary prescriptions.
The pharmacist measured out three drachms of the liquid medicine.
Traditional recipes sometimes call for quantities listed in drachms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dram - variant spelling of drachm, also a unit of mass or capacity
  • degree higher: fluid ounce - a unit of liquid capacity equal to 1/16 of a pint
