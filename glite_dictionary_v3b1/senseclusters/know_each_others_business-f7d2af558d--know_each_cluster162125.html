know each other's business

verb
be aware of others' private matters
2.3
Pronunciation: / noʊ iːtʃ ˈʌðərz ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / nəʊ iːtʃ ˈʌðəz ˈbɪznəs / (BrE)
to be aware of the private or personal matters of other people, often used in reference to small communities or groups where little is kept secret

Examples

To know each other's business well
In a close-knit community, it's hard to keep secrets because everyone quickly comes to know each other's business.
In small towns, people often know each other's business whether they like it or not.
She hated how her neighbors always seemed to know each other's business.
