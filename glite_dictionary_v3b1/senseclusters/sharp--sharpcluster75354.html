sharp

adjective
raised by one semitone (music)
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrp / (AmE), / ʃɑːp / (BrE)
(of a musical note) higher in pitch by one chromatic semitone.

Examples

F sharp
Guitarists often find F sharp easier to play than F natural.
In this piece, all C notes should be played sharp unless otherwise marked.
The last note sounded slightly sharp, as if it had been raised accidentally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flat - (of a musical note) lowered in pitch by one chromatic semitone
