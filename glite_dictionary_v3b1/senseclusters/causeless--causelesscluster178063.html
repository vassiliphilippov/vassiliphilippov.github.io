causeless

adjective
without cause or justification
2.6 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːz.ləs/ /; / /ˈkɔːz.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈkɔːz.ləs/ (BrE)
without a cause or reason; happening or existing for no identifiable reason

Examples

A causeless event
His sudden departure seemed completely causeless to his friends.
The judge described the attack as a causeless act of violence.
They entered into a causeless argument that left everyone confused.
He felt a causeless sense of dread as he walked into the room.
Some friendships end not because of conflict, but for seemingly causeless reasons.
The rumors spread by the press created a causeless panic among the public.
