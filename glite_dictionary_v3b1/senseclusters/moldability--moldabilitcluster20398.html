Examples

Synonyms

  • formability
    6.0 0.01/m
    formability describes how a material can be shaped without cracking under normal processes, while moldability means easy shaping for many uses.
  • plasticity
    6.1 0.29/m
    plasticity describes a material's ability to be molded and hold a new shape under force, while moldability focuses on ease of shaping.
  • pliability
    6.0 0.03/m
    pliability means a material bends or folds easily without breaking, while moldability means it can be shaped and formed into parts.
  • malleability
    7.4 0.29/m
    malleability means a material can be beaten or pressed into shape without cracking, whereas moldability covers easy shaping by many methods.
  • deformability
    7.3 0.00/m
    deformability means a material can change shape under force, but moldability implies easy, controlled shaping without damage for intended use.
