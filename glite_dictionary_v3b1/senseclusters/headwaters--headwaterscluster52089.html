headwaters

noun
origin or source (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌwɔtərz / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌwɔːtəz / (BrE)
the origin or early stage of a movement, idea, or activity from which something develops, used figuratively.

Examples

Headwaters of change
The new philosophy became the headwaters of a major cultural movement.
Many believe that education is the headwaters of social progress.
Innovative ideas often serve as the headwaters for technological change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: origin - the beginning or cause of something
  • synonym: source - the place or point where something begins
