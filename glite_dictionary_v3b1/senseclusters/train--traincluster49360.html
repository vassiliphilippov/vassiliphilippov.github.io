train

verb
cause a plant to grow in a controlled way
9.6
Pronunciation: / treɪn / (AmE), / treɪn / (BrE)
to guide the growth or movement of something (such as a plant) or to allow something to trail along the ground

Examples

To train a plant
Gardeners often train climbing roses along fences to create beautiful displays.
It takes patience to train grapevines to grow neatly along the trellis.
You should train the young tree early to ensure it develops a strong shape.
During the parade, the dancer's ribbon trained along the pavement in colorful swirls.
His long cloak trained in the dust as he entered the old mansion.
The bride's gown trained elegantly behind her as she walked down the aisle.

  • synonym: trail - to drag or let something drag along the ground
