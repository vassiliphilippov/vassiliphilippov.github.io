thoracoplasty

noun
rib-removal surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: /θɑːr.ə.ˈplæs.tɪ/ /; / /θɔːr.ə.ˈplæs.tɪ/ (AmE), /θɒr.ə.ˈplæs.tɪ/ /; / /θɔːr.ə.ˈplæs.tɪ/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which parts of the ribs are removed or reshaped to collapse part of the lung, often to treat lung disease

Examples

Thoracoplasty surgery
The surgeon recommended thoracoplasty to help control the patient's chronic lung infection.
After undergoing thoracoplasty, the patient needed several weeks to recover from the operation.
Advances in medicine have made thoracoplasty less common than it once was.
