crime

noun
act punishable by law
3.0 1.3
Pronunciation: / kraɪm / (AmE), / kraɪm / (BrE)
An act that is forbidden by law and punishable as a serious offense; typically considered morally wrong or harmful to society.

Examples

Serious crime

The documentary explores the impact of organized crime on society.
The police are working hard to reduce crime in the city.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: misdemeanor - a minor wrongdoing, less serious than a crime
