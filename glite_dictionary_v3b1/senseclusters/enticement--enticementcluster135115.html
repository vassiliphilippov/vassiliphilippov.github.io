enticement

noun

Examples

Special enticements offered
Holiday packages often include special enticements such as spa treatments or free meals.
No advertising enticement could persuade her to buy the product.
The enticement of higher salaries attracted many skilled workers to the city.
Advertising often relies on the enticement of glamour and excitement to attract customers.
He couldn't resist the enticement of freshly baked bread wafting from the kitchen.
The promise of higher pay was a strong enticement for workers to switch companies.
The enticement of a high salary convinced her to take the job abroad.
The advertisement used bright colors as an enticement to attract new customers.
There was little enticement for the children to eat their vegetables without any sauce.

Synonyms

  • lure
    4.3 2.44/m
    lure is an attractive feature that promises reward, while enticement is a general offer that tempts someone to act.
  • bait
    3.4 6.11/m
    bait is something used to attract people, often as a trap, while enticement offers reward or pleasure to tempt action.
  • inducement
    6.3 1.02/m
    inducement is a formal incentive that encourages someone to act, while enticement is a more general temptation by reward.
  • allurement
    7.7 0.11/m
    allurement is a charming appeal that draws attention, while enticement more directly offers a reward or advantage to tempt.
  • temptation
    3.8 13/m
    temptation is a strong inner desire to do something, often unwise, while enticement is an external offer that tries to draw someone.
  • seduction
    5.8 1.82/m
    seduction is a romantic or sexual act to win someone, while enticement can mean any offer that tempts by reward or pleasure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: temptation - the desire to do something, especially something wrong or unwise
