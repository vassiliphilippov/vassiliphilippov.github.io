Japanese deer

noun
small deer species native to Japan
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz dɪr / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz dɪə / (BrE)
a small species of deer native to Japan, known for slightly forked antlers and a reddish-brown coat

Examples

The Japanese deer species
Researchers have been studying the migration patterns of the Japanese deer for decades.
The Japanese deer roams freely in many of Nara's parks, where it is considered sacred.
With its elegant antlers, the Japanese deer is a popular subject in traditional Japanese art.
