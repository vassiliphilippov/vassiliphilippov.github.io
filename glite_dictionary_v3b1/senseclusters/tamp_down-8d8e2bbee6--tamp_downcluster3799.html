tamp down

verb
reduce or suppress (intensity, emotions, rumors, etc.)
Pronunciation: / tæmp daʊn / (AmE), / tamp daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the intensity of something or suppress it, especially emotions, rumors, or unrest

Examples

To tamp down rumors
News outlets worked to tamp down the speculation by reporting verified facts only.
She tried her best to tamp down her excitement before entering the meeting.
The government acted swiftly to tamp down public unrest after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplify - to make something larger, greater, or stronger
  • synonym: quell - to put an end to (a rebellion or disorder)
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to the activities of; to subdue
