ordnance

noun
military weapons and ammunition
8.7 1.10/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…îrd.n…ôns /; / Àà…îÀêrd.n…ôns / (AmE), / Àà…îÀêd.n…ôns / (BrE)
military weapons, ammunition, and equipment (especially large guns and artillery)

Examples

Unexploded ordnance
Experts were called in to safely remove unexploded ordnance from the battlefield.
Large amounts of ordnance were stored in underground bunkers during the war.
The army's supply officers are responsible for distributing ordnance to the front lines.
Modern warships are equipped with advanced ordnance for defense and attack.
Old military ordnance was discovered and safely detonated by experts.
The army transported heavy ordnance to the front lines during the conflict.

Synonyms

    7.6 2.35/m
    munition refers to ammunition and battle supplies often in general, while ordnance highlights large guns, shells, and artillery equipment.
  • armament
    7.0 1.66/m
    armament refers to a country's or army's weapons and defence systems, while ordnance focuses more on guns, shells, and heavy artillery.
  • arms
    2.8 0.06/m
    arms means weapons and military gear used broadly by people or units, while ordnance often refers to artillery, shells, and related heavy equipment.
  • weaponry
    4.2 4.23/m
    weaponry means the set of weapons a group uses, while ordnance usually points to large weapons like artillery and ammunition.
  • materiel
    8.8 0.64/m
    materiel covers a military force's tools, vehicles, and supplies, while ordnance specifically names weapons, shells, and artillery equipment.
  • implements of war
    7.0 0.04/m
    implements of war are objects used for fighting across history, while ordnance is modern military weapons, ammunition, and heavy artillery.
