St. Thomas a Becket

proper noun
Archbishop of Canterbury, martyred saint (12th century)
9.8
Pronunciation: /seɪnt ˈtɑː.məs ə ˈbek.ət/ (AmE), /seɪnt ˈtɒm.əs ə ˈbek.ɪt/ /; / /seɪnt ˈtɒm.əs ə ˈbek.ət/ (BrE)
(c. 1118–1170) Archbishop of Canterbury who was murdered after opposing King Henry II's attempts to constrain ecclesiastical authority; venerated as a saint and martyr in the Roman Catholic Church.
