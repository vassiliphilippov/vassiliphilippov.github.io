unsettlement

noun
displacement of people
7.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈset.əl.mənt/ /; / /ən.ˈset.əl.mənt/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈset.əl.mənt/ /; / /ən.ˈset.əl.mənt/ (BrE)
the process or state of people being displaced from their homes or communities, often causing instability or insecurity in an area

Examples

Unsettlement of families
The civil war brought about the unsettlement of many coastal communities along the river.
Years of conflict led to the unsettlement of entire villages, leaving them abandoned.
Rapid unsettlement can create long-term challenges for both displaced people and the regions they leave behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: displacement - the forced movement of people from their homes
  • antonym: resettlement - the process of moving people to a new place to live
