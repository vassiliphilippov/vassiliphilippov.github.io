iridophore

noun
light-reflecting cell
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɪdəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪdəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a type of cell in animals that reflects light and produces iridescent colors, especially in fish and reptiles

Examples

Iridophores in skin
The shimmering scales of the fish are caused by specialized iridophores in its skin.
Scientists studied how iridophores contribute to the bright colors of certain reptiles.
Under the microscope, the iridophore cells reflected light in a rainbow of hues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromatophore - a pigment-containing cell that changes color in animals
  • degree lower: melanophore - a cell containing dark pigment in animals
