sweet wormwood

noun
a species of wormwood plant
Pronunciation: / swit ˈwɜːrm.wʊd / (AmE), / swiːt ˈwɜː.mwʊd / (BrE)
A species of wormwood (Artemisia annua), native to southeastern Europe and Iran, known for its use in herbal medicine.

Examples

Sweet wormwood plant
Many traditional remedies use sweet wormwood for its medicinal properties.
Researchers discovered artemisinin in sweet wormwood as an effective treatment for malaria.
The fragrant leaves of sweet wormwood are sometimes brewed into herbal teas.
