kosher

adjective
conforming to Jewish dietary laws
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊʃər /; / ˈkoʊʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə / (BrE)
(of food, places, or preparation) prepared or kept according to Jewish dietary laws

Examples

Kosher kitchen
Many Jewish families only eat kosher food in their homes.
The restaurant is certified kosher and follows all dietary regulations.
They keep a strictly kosher kitchen for religious reasons.
After switching to a kosher diet, she carefully checked every food label for compliance.
Is this chicken kosher, or was it prepared in a regular kitchen?
The restaurant is certified as kosher and serves only food prepared according to Jewish law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: halal - permissible or lawful according to Islamic law, especially regarding food
  • antonym: non-kosher - not conforming to Jewish dietary laws
