Battle of the Chemin-des-Dames

noun
World War I battle in France, 1918
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬.əl əv ðə ʃəˈmæ̃ deɪˈdɑm / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ðə ʃəˈmæ̃ deɪˈdam / (BrE)
a major World War I battle fought in May 1918 along the Chemin des Dames ridge in France, where a German offensive achieved initial success but ultimately failed strategically.

Examples

The Battle of the Chemin-des-Dames 1918
Historians often analyze the Battle of the Chemin-des-Dames for its strategic implications in the war.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the Battle of the Chemin-des-Dames in May 1918.
The Battle of the Chemin-des-Dames marked a turning point in World War I battle tactics.
