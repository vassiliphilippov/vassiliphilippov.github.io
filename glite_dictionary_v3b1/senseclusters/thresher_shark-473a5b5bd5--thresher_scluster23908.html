thresher shark

noun
a large shark with a long, whiplike tail
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɛʃər ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃə ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large shark found in warm seas, known for its long, whiplike tail which it uses to herd small fish

Examples

Large thresher shark
Conservationists are concerned about declining thresher shark populations due to overfishing.
The thresher shark's long tail is used to stun schools of fish before feeding.
While diving off the coast, we were lucky enough to spot a thresher shark.
