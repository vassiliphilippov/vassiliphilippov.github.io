reaccredit

verb
to grant approval again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to grant official recognition or approval to something again, usually after a review or inspection

Examples

To reaccredit a hospital
The hospital was reaccredited after passing a thorough inspection by the health authorities.
Every five years, the school must be reaccredited to maintain its official status.
The training program was reaccredited following updates to its curriculum and teaching methods.
