black bear

noun
North American bear species, usually black
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk bɛr / (AmE), / ˈblæk beə / (BrE)
A medium-sized North American bear (Ursus americanus) with fur that is usually black or dark brown; less aggressive and smaller than the brown bear.

Examples

North American black bear
On our camping trip, we spotted a black bear rummaging near the edge of the forest.
Park rangers warn visitors to secure their food to avoid attracting black bears.
The black bear is often found in wooded areas across North America.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: brown bear - a large bear species (Ursus arctos) found in North America and Eurasia
