nonpossession

noun
renunciation of ownership
8.8
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.pə.ˈzeʃ.ən/ (AmE), /nɒn.pə.ˈzeʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the deliberate practice or principle of not owning personal property, often for religious or philosophical reasons

Examples

Religious nonpossession
After her spiritual awakening, she adopted nonpossession and gave away all her belongings.
Many monks practice nonpossession as part of their religious vows.
The philosophy of nonpossession encourages people to find happiness without material goods.

  • antonym: materialism - the tendency to consider material possessions as important
  • synonym: minimalism - a lifestyle characterized by owning as few possessions as possible
