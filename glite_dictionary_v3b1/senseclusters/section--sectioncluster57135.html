section

verb
to detain for treatment
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
to detain a person under a legal mental health section for compulsory psychiatric assessment or treatment.

Examples

To section a patient
The doctors decided to section her after assessing her mental health condition.
Family members were concerned when the hospital discussed sectioning their relative for his own safety.
In the UK, authorities can section someone if they pose a risk to themselves or others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commit - to officially send someone to a psychiatric hospital
  • antonym: release - to allow someone to leave a place where they were detained
