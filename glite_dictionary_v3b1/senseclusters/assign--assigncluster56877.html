assign

verb
attribute or credit something
Pronunciation: / əˈsaɪn / (AmE), / əˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to attribute or credit something (such as a work, meaning, or quality) to a person, cause, or source

Examples

To assign meaning
Many historians assign this painting to the early works of Picasso.
Some people assign too much importance to luck in achieving success.
The critics assign the novel's popularity to its relatable characters.
Many critics assign symbolic meaning to the colors used in the painting.
People often assign blame without knowing all the facts.
The teacher assigned different roles to each student in the play.
He tried to assign wisdom to every action of the leader, even when it was clearly accidental.
It is presumptuous to assign such motives to her without evidence.
The critics assigned great importance to a minor remark, which was unwarranted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocate - to distribute for a particular purpose
  • synonym: attribute - to regard something as being caused by
  • synonym: impute - to attribute to a source or cause
  • antonym: remove - to take something away from someone or something
  • synonym: usurp - to take a position or right wrongfully
