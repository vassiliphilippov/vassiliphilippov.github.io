barratry

noun
buying or selling church offices
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbæˌrætɹi / (AmE), / ˈbær.ə.tɹi / (BrE)
the buying or selling of ecclesiastical offices or church appointments; the traffic in church preferments

Examples

Church barratry
He was accused of barratry after attempting to purchase a prominent ecclesiastical position.
The medieval church condemned barratry as a grave sin against its integrity.
The practice of barratry undermined trust in the church during the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simony - the buying or selling of church offices or sacred things
