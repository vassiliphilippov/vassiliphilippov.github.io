reach

verb
extend to a point or distance
1.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /riːtʃ/ (AmE), /riːtʃ/ (BrE)
to extend or stretch out in order to touch, grasp, or make contact with something (physically or metaphorically)

Examples

To reach the shelf
If the rope doesn't reach the window, you'll have to find another way.
The river reaches the ocean after winding through the countryside.
The tree branches reach all the way up to the second floor.
He stretched out his hand to reach for the light switch.
She tried to reach the top shelf, but it was still too high.
The organization wants to reach more students in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extend - to stretch out in space or time
  • synonym: stretch - to extend one's body or limb
  • antonym: withdraw - to move back or away from a position (opposite of reach)
