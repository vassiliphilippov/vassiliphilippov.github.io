Pope Pius XII

proper noun
pope (1939–1958); head of Catholic Church during WWII
7.2
Pronunciation: / poʊp paɪ.əs ˌtwɛlv / (AmE), / pəʊp ˈpaɪ.əs ˌtwɛlv / (BrE)
the head of the Roman Catholic Church from 1939 to 1958, known for maintaining neutrality during World War II and later criticized for his response to the persecution of Jews.

Examples

Pope Pius XII during war
Pope Pius XII led the Catholic Church through the challenging years of World War II.
Many historians debate the actions and responsibilities of Pope Pius XII during the Holocaust.
The Vatican archives from the time of Pope Pius XII have been a source of significant scholarly interest.
