catchment basin

noun
area drained by a river system
9.9 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃmənt ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈkætʃmənt ˈbeɪsɪn / (BrE)
an area of land where all rainfall and other runoff drains into a common outlet, such as a river, lake, or reservoir

Examples

A catchment basin area
Farmers in the catchment basin depend on the river for irrigation throughout the year.
Heavy rainfall in the catchment basin led to flooding in nearby towns.
The government implemented new policies to protect the catchment basin from industrial pollution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drainage basin - an area drained by a river and its tributaries
  • synonym: watershed - an area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers, basins, or seas
