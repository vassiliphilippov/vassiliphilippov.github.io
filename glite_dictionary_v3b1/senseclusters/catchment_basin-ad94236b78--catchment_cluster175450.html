noun

an area of land where all rainfall and other runoff drains into a common outlet, such as a river, lake, or reservoir

Examples

A catchment basin area
Farmers in the catchment basin depend on the river for irrigation throughout the year.
Heavy rainfall in the catchment basin led to flooding in nearby towns.
The government implemented new policies to protect the catchment basin from industrial pollution.

Synonyms

  • catchment area
    5.6 0.2/m
    catchment area describes the land that collects rainfall and runoff for one outlet, while catchment basin is a close, slightly more technical term.
  • drainage basin
    4.0 0.1/m
    drainage basin covers the whole land area where rainfall drains to one river or lake, while catchment basin names the same drained region.
  • drainage area
    3.7 0.05/m
    drainage area means the full land that sheds water to a single outlet, while catchment basin means that drained area.
  • river basin
    3.5 0.3/m
    river basin is the land drained by a river and its tributaries, while catchment basin can mean drained land for any outlet.
  • watershed
    6.1 3/m
    watershed refers to the land draining to one river system or the ridge dividing rivers, while catchment basin always means the drained area.
  • catchment
    6.4 0.9/m
    catchment describes land that collects water or a structure that collects water, while catchment basin specifically names the drained area.
  • basin
    3.1 10/m
    basin can mean a land hollow or a drainage area that holds water, while catchment basin specifically means drained land to one outlet.
