Odoacer

proper noun
Germanic ruler who ended Western Roman Empire
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊdoʊˈeɪsər / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəʊˈeɪsə / (BrE)
(c. 434–493) A Germanic chieftain who deposed the last Western Roman Emperor in 476 and became the first barbarian ruler of Italy.

Examples

Odoacer deposed emperor
After Odoacer deposed Romulus Augustulus, the Western Roman Empire came to an end.
Historians regard Odoacer as the first non-Roman king of Italy.
The reign of Odoacer marked a pivotal moment in European history.
