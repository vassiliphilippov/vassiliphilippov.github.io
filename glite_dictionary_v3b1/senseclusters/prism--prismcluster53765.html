prism

verb
to interpret through a lens
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
to present or interpret something through a particular viewpoint that alters or colors its appearance, often in a figurative sense

Examples

To prism a story
The story was prismed through the author's unique cultural background, giving it a fresh perspective.
Her memories of childhood are often prismed by nostalgia, making them seem happier than they were.
News reports can be prismed through political viewpoints, affecting how events are understood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distort - to change the appearance or meaning of something so it is no longer accurate
  • synonym: filter - to present or interpret something through a particular perspective
