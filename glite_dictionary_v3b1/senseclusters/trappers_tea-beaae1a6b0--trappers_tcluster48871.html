trapper's tea

noun
Rocky Mountain shrub, used for herbal tea
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtræpərz ti / (AmE), / ˈtræpəz tiː / (BrE)
a shrub native to the Rocky Mountains, similar to Labrador tea (Ledum groenlandicum), traditionally used for making herbal tea.

Examples

Trapper's tea shrub
Botanists in the Rocky Mountains frequently study trapper's tea for its medicinal properties.
Early explorers often brewed trapper's tea from local shrubs to warm themselves during cold nights.
The leaves of trapper's tea were prized for their aromatic qualities and soothing effects.
