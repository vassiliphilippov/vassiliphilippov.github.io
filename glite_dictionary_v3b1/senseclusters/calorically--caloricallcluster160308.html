calorically

adverb
by caloric theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈlɑːr.ɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /kə.ˈlɔːr.ɪ.kəl.iː/ (AmE), /kə.ˈlɒr.ɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /kə.ˈlɔːr.ɪ.kəl.iː/ (BrE)
in a manner relating to the caloric theory of heat, treating heat as a fluid substance (now obsolete)

Examples

Calorically explained heat
In early scientific literature, heat was often described as moving calorically from one object to another.
The experiment was explained calorically, following the now outdated theory of heat as a fluid.
Some nineteenth-century textbooks discuss energy transfer calorically rather than using modern thermodynamic concepts.
