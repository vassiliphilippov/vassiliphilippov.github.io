overstocked

adjective
excess goods
3.9 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàst…ëÀêkt / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàst…íkt / (BrE)
having more goods or products than are needed or can be sold

Examples

An overstocked warehouse
The warehouse became overstocked after the company overestimated demand for the new product.
Many stores were overstocked with winter clothing after an unusually warm season.
If a shop is overstocked, it may offer discounts to clear out excess inventory.

Synonyms

  • glutted
    6.3 0.02/m
    glutted describes a market with too many goods that cannot be sold, while overstocked refers to excess store inventory.
  • overabundant
    5.8 0.05/m
    overabundant describes large, often natural or general extra amounts, while overstocked refers specifically to excess goods a shop cannot sell.
  • stocked with
    3.7 1.37/m
    stocked with describes being supplied or filled with items, while overstocked means having more goods than can be sold.
  • overfull
    5.5 0.09/m
    overfull means physically filled past capacity, while overstocked means a shop has more products than it can sell.
  • overflowing with
    2.9 1.56/m
    overflowing with gives a visual image of things spilling over, while overstocked refers to unsold excess goods in stores.
