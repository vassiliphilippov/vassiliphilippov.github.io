jumper stay

noun
extra mast stay on a sailboat
9.9 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒʌm.pər steɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdʒʌm.pə steɪ/ (BrE)
A rigging component on a sailing vessel, consisting of an additional stay used to limit the bend of the upper part of the mast and efficiently transfer tension from the backstay to the forestay.

Examples

A jumper stay rigging
Older sailing yachts often featured a jumper stay as part of their rigging.
The jumper stay helps maintain the mast's alignment during high winds.
Without a jumper stay, the upper mast can flex excessively under heavy load.
