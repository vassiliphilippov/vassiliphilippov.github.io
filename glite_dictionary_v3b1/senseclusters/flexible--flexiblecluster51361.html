flexible

adjective
capable of being changed or adapted
1.0 2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflɛksəbəl / (AmE), / ˈflɛksɪbl /; / ˈflɛksɪbəl / (BrE)
able or willing to change or adapt easily to different situations, needs, or ideas

Examples

Flexible working hours
Our company offers flexible working hours to accommodate employees' personal schedules.
Thanks to a flexible approach, the plan could be adjusted to meet new requirements.
This ticket allows for flexible travel dates within a three-month period.
I'm flexible with the schedule, so let me know if you need to change the time.
Negotiating with someone who is flexible makes reaching an agreement much easier.
Our manager is very flexible and always listens to different opinions during meetings.
Children are often more flexible and open to change than adults.
My manager is very flexible when it comes to working hours.
The company needs someone flexible enough to handle different tasks.

  • antonym: rigid - unable or unwilling to change or adapt
  • antonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's mind or attitude
