monad

noun
chemistry: atom with valence of one
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊnæd / (AmE), / ˈməʊnæd / (BrE)
(Chemistry) An atom or radical with a valence of one; a monovalent atom or group.

Examples

A chemical monad
Hydrogen is the simplest example of a chemical monad in many compounds.
In sodium chloride, the sodium atom acts as a monad, forming a single bond with the chloride ion.
The chemist explained that a monad has only one combining site in reactions.
