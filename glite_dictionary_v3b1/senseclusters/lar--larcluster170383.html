lar

noun
Roman household spirit
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɑr / (AmE), / lɑː / (BrE)
a protective household spirit in ancient Roman religion, believed to guard the home and family

Examples

Ancient lar spirit
In ancient Rome, families would honor their lar with daily offerings at the household altar.
The statue of the lar stood in a special place in the kitchen, watching over the family.
During festivals, Romans believed their lar brought good fortune and protection to the home.
