circumstantial

adjective
based on circumstances
7.2
Pronunciation: /sɜːr.kəm.ˈstæn.ʃəl/ /; / /sər.kəm.ˈstæn.ʃəl/ (AmE), /sɜː.kəm.ˈstæn.ʃəl/ /; / /sə.kəm.ˈstæn.ʃəl/ (BrE)
depending on or influenced by particular situations, conditions, or circumstances rather than being direct or absolute

Examples

Circumstantial evidence
The evidence against the suspect was entirely circumstantial and did not prove his guilt.
Her promotion was circumstantial, resulting from a colleague's sudden resignation.
Success in this field is often circumstantial, depending on timing and opportunity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolute - not dependent on any conditions or circumstances
  • synonym: conditional - depending on something else to happen
