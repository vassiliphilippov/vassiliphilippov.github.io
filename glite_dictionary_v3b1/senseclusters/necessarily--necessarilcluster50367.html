necessarily

adverb
inevitably; as a necessary result
4.9 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈnes.ə.sər.ɪ.liː/ /; / /ˈnes.ə.sə.rɪ.liː/ (AmE), /ˈnes.ə.sə.rɪ.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that is unavoidable, inevitable, or required; as a necessary result

Examples

Not necessarily true
Being the oldest does not necessarily make you the wisest.
If we increase production, it does not necessarily mean that profits will rise.
Not all rich people are necessarily happy or satisfied with their lives.
A higher price does not necessarily mean better quality.
Being honest does not necessarily lead to success.
Not every minor delay will necessarily cause major problems.
Just because it is expensive does not necessarily mean it is better.
Success does not necessarily come from working the longest hours.
The changes will necessarily affect all employees in the company.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accidentally - by chance, not planned or intended
  • synonym: inevitably - as is certain to happen; unavoidably
  • antonym: optionally - in a way that is not required; by choice
  • antonym: possibly - in a way that might happen or be true, but is not certain
