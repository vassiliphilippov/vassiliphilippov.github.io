right of way

noun
legal right to pass over another's property
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪt əv ˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌraɪt əv ˈweɪ / (BrE)
the legal right to pass along a specific route through someone else's property

Examples

Right of way through land
Disputes over the right of way can arise when building fences or barriers.
The farmer granted his neighbor a right of way across the field for access to the main road.
You must not block the right of way that allows walkers to pass through the private estate.
Hikers may use the right of way that cuts across these fields.
The map clearly marks the right of way for walkers and cyclists.
The property owner tried to block the right of way with a fence, leading to a legal dispute.
