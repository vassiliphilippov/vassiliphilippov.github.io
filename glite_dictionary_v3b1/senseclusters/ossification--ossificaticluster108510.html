ossification

noun
rigid adherence to ideas or behaviors
8.3 7.5
Pronunciation: /ɑː.sə.fə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɑː.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɒ.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɒ.sə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of becoming rigid or inflexible, especially in attitudes, ideas, or systems

Examples

Ossification of ideas
Many critics argue that educational systems suffer from an ossification of outdated teaching methods.
Over time, political parties risk ossification if they fail to respond to new ideas.
The ossification of corporate culture can hinder innovation and adaptability.
Social progress often stalls due to the ossification of traditional beliefs.
The ossification of management structures makes it difficult for the company to adapt to new challenges.
The historian warned against the ossification of cultural practices that once encouraged innovation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and unyielding in opinion or behavior
