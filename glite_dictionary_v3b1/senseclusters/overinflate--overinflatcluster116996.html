overinflate

verb
to inflate excessively
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪnˈfleɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪnˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to exaggerate or make something seem bigger, more important, or more valuable than it really is

Examples

To overinflate numbers
The company was accused of having overinflated its profits to attract investors.
If you overinflate the tires, they may burst while driving.
Some sellers overinflate the value of their products to make them seem more appealing.
The media tends to overinflate the achievements of celebrities, making them seem larger than life.
If you overinflate your skills on your resume, you might face problems during the interview.
Some companies overinflate prices during the holiday season to increase profits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it really is
  • synonym: inflate - to increase something, especially prices or numbers, often beyond what is justified
  • antonym: understate - to describe something as being smaller or less important than it really is
