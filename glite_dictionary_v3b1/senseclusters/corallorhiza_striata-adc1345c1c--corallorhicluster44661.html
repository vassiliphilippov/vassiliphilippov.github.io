Corallorhiza striata

noun
nearly leafless wildflower; western North America orchid
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɔːˌræləˈraɪzə straɪˈeɪtə / (AmE), / kɒˌraləˈraɪzə straɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A nearly leafless orchid native to western North America, with erect reddish-purple stems and racemes of pale, pinkish, and brownish-striped flowers.

Examples

Corallorhiza striata orchid

Unlike many orchids, Corallorhiza striata lacks green leaves and depends on fungi for nourishment.
