ACE

noun
adverse childhood experience (trauma)
8.1
Pronunciation: / \u02cce\u026a si \u02c8i / (AmE), / \u02cce\u026a si \u02c8i / (BrE)
(in public health and psychology) an event or experience in childhood that is traumatic or highly stressful and is linked to increased risk of poor health or social outcomes later in life.

Examples

High ACE score
A high ACE score is associated with greater health risks in adulthood.
After the study, clinicians screened children for multiple ACEs to identify early support needs.
Communities that reduce poverty and violence can lower young people's overall ACE burdens.
