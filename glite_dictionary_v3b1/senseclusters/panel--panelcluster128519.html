panel

noun
piece of cloth in garments or sails
Pronunciation: / ˈpænəl / (AmE), / ˈpanəl /; / ˈpænəl / (BrE)
a flat or shaped piece that forms part of a larger surface or object, such as in clothing, machines, or as a pad under a saddle

Examples

A door panel
Each section of the umbrella is made from a different colored panel.
The dress was sewn with several triangular panels to give it more shape.
They patched the sail by replacing the torn panel with a new piece of canvas.
A technician opened the metal panel to rewire some of the connections.
He stared at the glowing lights on the panel, searching for the source of the problem.
The pilot adjusted several switches on the panel before takeoff.
A worn-out panel can cause sores on a horse's back during long rides.
She made sure the panel was securely in place before mounting her horse.
The horse's comfort improved after we replaced the old panel under the saddle.
