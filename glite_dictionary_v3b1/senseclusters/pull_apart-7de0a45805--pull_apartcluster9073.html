pull apart

verb
take something into pieces by pulling
3.2 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / p äl …ôÀàp…ërt / (AmE), / p äl …ôÀàp…ëÀêt / (BrE)
to separate something into pieces by pulling or to take something to pieces

Examples

To pull apart a chair
After years of use, the chair was so weak that it easily pulled apart.
He watched her patiently pull apart the sandwich to remove the tomatoes.
The children tried to pull apart the robot to see how it worked.

Synonyms

    3.0 1.51/m
    take apart is to dismantle an object into neat parts, while pull apart suggests separating it by force or pulling.
  • disassemble
    3.8 1.46/m
    disassemble is a technical word for taking a machine into parts carefully, while pull apart suggests pulling pieces apart.
  • dismantle
    4.2 3.06/m
    dismantle often means removing parts or equipment from a structure, while pull apart focuses on separating by pulling pieces.
  • break apart
    3.0 1.62/m
    break apart describes coming apart into pieces suddenly or gradually, while pull apart implies physically separating by pulling force.
  • break down
    2.8 8.92/m
    break down can mean separating into parts for study or repair, while pull apart usually means physically pulling pieces apart.
  • tear
    1.0 41/m
    tear means ripping something with force along a line, while pull apart implies separating pieces by pulling them away.
  • rip up
    2.8 0.17/m
    rip up means tearing into small shreds or pieces quickly, while pull apart suggests pulling larger pieces apart rather than shredding.
  • split
    1.3 25/m
    split means dividing something into parts often by cutting or force, while pull apart emphasises separating by pulling or tearing.
  • split up
    2.6 0.81/m
    split up means dividing people or groups or things into smaller parts, while pull apart usually refers to physically pulling items into pieces.
