dismissively

adverb
showing lack of respect
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmɪsɪvli / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that shows something or someone is not considered important or worthy of attention

Examples

To speak dismissively
She replied dismissively to his concerns, barely looking up from her phone.
The manager waved dismissively at the idea, refusing to discuss it further.
He shrugged dismissively when asked about the team's chances of winning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contemptuously - in a way that shows strong dislike or lack of respect
  • antonym: respectfully - in a way that shows respect or admiration
