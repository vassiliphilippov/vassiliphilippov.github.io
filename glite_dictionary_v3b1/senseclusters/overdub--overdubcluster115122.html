overdub

verb
to add new recording
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌdʌb / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌdʌb / (BrE)
to record a new sound or musical part over an existing recording without removing the original

Examples

To overdub vocals
The singer decided to overdub her vocals to create a richer harmony.
After recording the drums, the band went back to overdub the guitar parts.
Producers often overdub instruments to enhance the final mix of a song.
The producer decided to overdub the guitar solo to improve the song's quality.
They will overdub the actor's lines with a different voice to match the translation.
Sometimes artists overdub vocals to fix mistakes made during the original recording session.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dub - to add or replace sound in a film or recording
  • antonym: erase - to remove recorded sound from a track
  • synonym: layer - to add one sound or track on top of another
