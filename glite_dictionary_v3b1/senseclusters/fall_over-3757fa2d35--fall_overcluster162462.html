fall over

verb
lose balance and drop to the ground
1.0 3.06/m
Pronunciation: / f…îl Àào äv…ö / (AmE), / f…îÀêl Àà…ô äv…ô / (BrE)
to lose your balance and fall to the ground, usually by slipping, tripping, or stumbling

Examples

To fall over suddenly
He slipped on the ice and fell over in front of everyone.
Please pick up those chairs before they fall over.
The baby laughed when the blocks fell over on the carpet.
He tripped on the carpet and fell over in front of everyone.
The toddler giggled every time she would fall over while learning to walk.
Watch your step on the icy path or you might fall over.

Synonyms

  • fall down
    1.0 7.45/m
    fall down is when someone suddenly loses an upright position and drops to the ground, while fall over stresses losing balance.
  • go over
    3.5 2.97/m
    go over means to fall forward and down, often suddenly in one movement, while fall over means to lose balance and topple.
  • tumble
    2.9 9.65/m
    tumble means to fall and roll over in an uncontrolled way, while fall over means to lose balance and drop to the ground.
  • overbalance
    5.6 0.04/m
    overbalance means to lean or tip too far and nearly fall, while fall over means to lose balance and fall fully to the ground.
  • tip over
    3.0 1.40/m
    tip over means to fall or be made to fall from an upright position, while fall over focuses on a person losing balance.
  • topple
    4.1 7.56/m
    topple means to fall over or make something fall, often suddenly or by force, while fall over usually describes a person losing balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall down - to drop to the ground, often after losing balance
  • antonym: stand up - to rise to a standing position
  • synonym: topple - to fall forward, especially because of being unbalanced
  • synonym: trip - to catch one's foot and stumble or fall
