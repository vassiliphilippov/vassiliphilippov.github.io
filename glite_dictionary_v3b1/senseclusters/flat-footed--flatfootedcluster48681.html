flat-footed

adjective
unprepared and slow to react
4.4
Pronunciation: / flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
not prepared for something, and therefore unable to react quickly or effectively

Examples

Caught flat-footed
He was flat-footed when asked to explain the missing documents.
The storm caught the entire town flat-footed, causing significant unpreparedness.
The surprise question in the interview left her flat-footed and unsure how to answer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ready - prepared and able to respond quickly
  • synonym: unprepared - not ready for something; not equipped or forewarned
