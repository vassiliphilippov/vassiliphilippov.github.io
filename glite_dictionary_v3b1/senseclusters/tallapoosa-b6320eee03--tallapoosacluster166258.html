Tallapoosa

proper noun
river in Georgia and Alabama, USA
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌtæləˈpuːsə / (AmE), / ˌtæləˈpuːsə / (BrE)
a river in the southeastern United States that rises in northwestern Georgia and flows southwest through central Alabama, joining the Coosa River near Montgomery to form the Alabama River.

Examples

Tallapoosa River
A dam was built on the Tallapoosa to create Lake Martin, one of the largest man-made lakes in the region.
Flooding along the Tallapoosa caused several road closures in central Alabama last year.
The Tallapoosa River is popular for canoeing, especially during the spring and summer months.
