bedesman

noun
person paid to pray for another's soul
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːdz.mən / (AmE), / ˈbiːdz.mən / (BrE)
A man who was paid or endowed to pray for the soul of another, especially in former times.

Examples

A bedesman prays
In medieval times, a bedesman would recite prayers for the deceased benefactor.
The historian discovered records of a bedesman employed by the monastery in the 15th century.
The wealthy noble left funds to support a bedesman in his local church.
