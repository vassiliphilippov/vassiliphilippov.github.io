discardable

adjective
able to be rejected
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkɑːrdəbəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːdəbəl / (BrE)
able to be rejected, ignored, or removed because it is unimportant or unnecessary

Examples

Discardable information
The report contained several discardable details that did not affect the final decision.
He sorted through his belongings, setting aside the discardable items for donation.
Only the most relevant data was kept; the rest was considered discardable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: essential - absolutely necessary or extremely important
