folklore

noun
traditional stories and customs of a culture
4.9 4.83/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfo äkÀål…îr / (AmE), / Ààf…ô äk.l…îÀê / (BrE)
The traditional stories, customs, and beliefs of a community passed through generations, especially by word of mouth.

Examples

Local folklore stories
According to local folklore, the forest is haunted by the spirit of an old king.
Much of Native American folklore has been handed down orally through generations.
The region's folklore includes tales of supernatural creatures and mysterious events.

Synonyms

  • lore
    6.1 3.84/m
    lore is traditional knowledge passed down orally in a community, while folklore includes the full set of stories, customs, and beliefs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: history - the study of past events, particularly in human affairs
  • synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated
  • synonym: mythology - a collection of myths, especially those belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition
