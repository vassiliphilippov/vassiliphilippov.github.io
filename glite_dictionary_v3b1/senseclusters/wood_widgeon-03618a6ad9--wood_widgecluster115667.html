wood widgeon

noun
North American duck nesting in trees
Pronunciation: / wʊd ˈwɪdʒən / (AmE), / wʊd ˈwɪdʒən / (BrE)
A showy North American duck, also known as the wood duck, that nests in hollow trees.

Examples

A wood widgeon duck
A flock of wood widgeon was spotted nesting near the lake.
In the spring, wood widgeon often return to the same nesting sites in hollow trees.
The male wood widgeon is known for its striking plumage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wood duck - North American duck with iridescent plumage, also known as Aix sponsa
