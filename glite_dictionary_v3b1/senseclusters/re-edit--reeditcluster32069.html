re-edit

verb
to edit again
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛdɪt / (BrE)
to edit something again, usually to improve, correct, or update it

Examples

To re-edit a report
After receiving new information, the journalist had to re-edit the report before publishing.
The director decided to re-edit the film to improve its pacing and clarity.
You may need to re-edit your essay if you find any mistakes after reviewing it.
