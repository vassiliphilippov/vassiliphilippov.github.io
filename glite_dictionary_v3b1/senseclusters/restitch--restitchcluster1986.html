restitch

verb
to sew again
4.1 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàst…™t É / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàst…™t É / (BrE)
to sew something again, especially to repair a seam or join that has come undone

Examples

To restitch a seam
After noticing a tear, she decided to restitch the hem of her dress before the party.
The tailor had to restitch the pocket because the original thread had come loose.
If the seam splits, you can easily restitch it with a needle and some matching thread.

Synonyms

  • resew
    6.0 0.02/m
    resew means to sew a torn area again to strengthen or fix it, while restitch describes repairing a seam or join that came undone.
  • stitch up
    3.9 0.19/m
    stitch up mends and finishes a hem or seam by sewing it together, while restitch focuses on sewing again to repair an undone join.

Related Terms
