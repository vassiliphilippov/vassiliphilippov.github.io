winnow

verb
to blow lightly (wind, poetic/literary)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊ / (BrE)
(of wind) to blow lightly across or through something, causing movement or separation.

Examples

Winds winnow the grass
A gentle breeze began to winnow the tall grass in the field.
Her long hair was winnowed by the wind as she walked along the shore.
The open window allowed the cool air to winnow the curtains softly.
