glacial boulder

noun
boulder moved by a glacier
7.1 7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃəl ˈboʊldər / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪsɪəl ˈbəʊldə / (BrE)
a large rock that has been transported and deposited by a glacier far from its original location

Examples

A glacial boulder
A glacial boulder can be found hundreds of miles from the rock formation it once belonged to.
Geologists studied the glacial boulder to determine where the glacier originated.
The massive glacial boulder in the field is evidence of the area's glacial past.
