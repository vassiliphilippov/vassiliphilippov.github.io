hair-tailed mole

noun
North American mole species
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛɹ teɪld moʊl / (AmE), / ˈheə teɪld məʊl / (BrE)
A species of mole found in eastern North America, distinguished by a tail covered with short hairs.

Examples

The hair-tailed mole species
Scientists are studying the habitat preferences of the hair-tailed mole in forested areas.
The hair-tailed mole is rarely seen above ground due to its subterranean lifestyle.
Unlike other moles, the hair-tailed mole has a distinctive furry tail.
