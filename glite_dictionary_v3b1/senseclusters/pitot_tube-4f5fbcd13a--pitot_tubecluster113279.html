Pitot tube

noun
instrument for measuring fluid or airspeed
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːtoʊ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəʊ tjuːb / (BrE)
an instrument (a small tube) used to measure the speed or pressure of a moving fluid, especially to determine airspeed in aircraft or in experiments

Examples

The Pitot tube instrument
A blockage in the Pitot tube can lead to incorrect cockpit readings.
Engineers calibrated the Pitot tube before conducting the wind tunnel experiment.
The Pitot tube on the wing measures the airplane's airspeed accurately.
A Pitot tube is essential for accurate flow measurement in many wind tunnel experiments.
Technicians regularly inspect the Pitot tube to ensure it is not blocked by debris.
The airspeed indicator on the aircraft relies on a Pitot tube to measure velocity.

  • synonym: anemometer - device for measuring wind speed
