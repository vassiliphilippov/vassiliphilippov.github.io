neotony

noun
retention of juvenile traits in adults
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /niː.ˈɑː.tə.niː/ (AmE), /niː.ˈɒ.tə.niː/ (BrE)
The retention of juvenile characteristics or traits into adulthood by members of a species.

Examples

Neotony in animals
Axolotls are well-known for exhibiting neotony, retaining their gills even as adults.
Human beings show some degree of neotony compared to other primates, such as flatter faces and larger brains.
The study explained how environmental factors could promote neotony in certain amphibians.
