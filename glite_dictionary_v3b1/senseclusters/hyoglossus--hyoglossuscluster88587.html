hyoglossus

noun
tongue muscle
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪoʊˈɡlɑsəs / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəʊˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
a muscle of the tongue that helps depress and retract the tongue during movement

Examples

The hyoglossus muscle
The hyoglossus muscle assists in lowering the sides of the tongue during speech.
Damage to the hyoglossus can affect a person's ability to swallow properly.
Surgeons must be careful not to injure the hyoglossus during procedures involving the mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genioglossus - a muscle that helps move the tongue forward
  • synonym: styloglossus - a muscle that helps retract and elevate the tongue
