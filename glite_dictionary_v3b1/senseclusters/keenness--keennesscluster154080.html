keenness

noun
enthusiasm; eagerness
3.6 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkin.n…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàkiÀên.n…ôs / (BrE)
great enthusiasm or eagerness to do something; strong desire or interest

Examples

Keenness to learn
Despite his keenness, he waited patiently for the right opportunity.
Her keenness to start the project impressed everyone in the team.
The children showed great keenness for learning new things during the workshop.

Synonyms

  • eagerness
    2.5 3.20/m
    eagerness is a strong, ready desire to act, while keenness means a general strong interest or desire to do something.
  • enthusiasm
    2.2 24/m
    enthusiasm is a lively feeling of interest and approval, while keenness is more about eager desire to take part or try.
  • alacrity
    8.4 0.40/m
    alacrity is quick, cheerful readiness to do something, while keenness is a strong interest or desire without speed.
  • avidness
    6.6 0.00/m
    avidness is keen, often intense eagerness toward a pursuit, while keenness is general strong interest or desire.
  • avidity
    8.0 0.30/m
    avidity is a formal word for eager desire to obtain something, while keenness is a plain term for strong interest.
  • fervor
    6.3 3.26/m
    fervor is intense, often emotional passion, while keenness is a strong eager interest without the same heat or emotion.
  • ardor
    7.5 1.05/m
    ardor is deep passionate feeling often for people or causes, while keenness is eager interest or desire in general tasks.
  • excitement
    1.0 32/m
    excitement is a high, often joyful feeling caused by events, while keenness is steady eager interest or desire to do something.
  • zestfulness
    5.9 0.00/m
    zestfulness is lively energy and enjoyment in actions, while keenness is focused strong desire or interest without that playful energy.
