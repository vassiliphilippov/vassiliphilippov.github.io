deep-seated anger

noun
long-lasting, deeply held anger
3.8
Pronunciation: / diːp ˈsiːtɪd ˈæŋɡər / (AmE), / diːp ˈsiːtɪd ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
anger that is firmly established and has persisted for a long time

Examples

Deep-seated anger from childhood
He carried a deep-seated anger from childhood that influenced his decisions as an adult.
The therapist helped her address her deep-seated anger towards her parents.
Years of betrayal fueled a deep-seated anger that he struggled to express.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resentment - persistent feeling of bitterness or anger
