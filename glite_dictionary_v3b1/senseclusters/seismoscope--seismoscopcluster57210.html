seismoscope

noun
earthquake direction detector
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪzməˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzməskəʊp / (BrE)
an instrument that detects the occurrence of earthquakes, sometimes also showing the direction they come from

Examples

Ancient seismoscope device
The ancient Chinese seismoscope could show the direction of an earthquake long before modern technology.
Scientists studied a restored seismoscope to understand how early civilizations detected earthquakes.
A seismoscope in the museum demonstrates how people once identified the source of seismic activity.
The ancient Chinese invented the first seismoscope to detect distant earthquakes.
A modern seismoscope can sense even the slightest ground movements.
Scientists checked the seismoscope after the tremor was felt in the city.
