bargain

noun
agreement after negotiation
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
an agreement between two or more parties, especially after negotiation, fixing what each party must do or provide

Examples

A bargain agreement
After hours of discussion, they finally reached a bargain that satisfied both sides.
Breaking the bargain could result in serious consequences for both companies.
He felt that the bargain they struck was fair and mutually beneficial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties
  • synonym: contract - a formal or legally binding agreement
