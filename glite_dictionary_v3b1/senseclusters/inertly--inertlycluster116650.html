inertly

adverb
without movement
6.7
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈnɜːrt.liː/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈnɜːt.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that shows no movement, energy, or response; passively or without reacting

Examples

Inertly lying still
The cat lay inertly in the sun, barely twitching its tail.
After the long hike, he collapsed inertly onto the couch.
The leaves floated inertly on the surface of the pond.
The gas remained inertly in the container, showing no signs of reaction.
He sat inertly on the bench, not responding to the commotion around him.
The chemicals mixed together, but one substance floated inertly on the surface.
He sat inertly on the couch, staring at the wall for hours.
The students listened inertly to the long lecture, barely reacting to the teacher's questions.
After the shocking news, she responded inertly, unable to express any emotion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actively - in a way that involves action or movement
  • synonym: motionlessly - without moving; still
  • synonym: passively - in a way that does not take action or respond
