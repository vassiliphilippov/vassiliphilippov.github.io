regeneratively

adverb
in a restorative way
7.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtɪvli /; / rɪˈdʒɛnərətɪvli / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛnərətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that restores, renews, or recovers something, especially resources, energy, or health

Examples

Works regeneratively
The factory operates regeneratively by capturing waste heat and using it to power other processes.
Modern vehicles often brake regeneratively to convert kinetic energy back into electricity.
The building was designed regeneratively to minimize environmental impact and reuse water efficiently.
The farm was managed regeneratively to restore the health of the soil.
She approached her recovery regeneratively, focusing on healing and renewal.
The company invested in regeneratively designed buildings to benefit the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: destructively - in a way that causes damage or harm
  • synonym: restoratively - in a way that brings back health or strength
  • synonym: sustainably - in a way that avoids depletion of resources
  • antonym: wastefully - in a way that uses resources carelessly or inefficiently
