stable

adjective
not likely to change; maintaining equilibrium
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪ.bəl /; / ˈsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.bəl /; / ˈsteɪbl /; / ˈsteɪbəl / (BrE)
not likely to change, move, or fail; steady and secure

Examples

Stable condition
After treatment, her medical condition became much more stable.
During the experiment, the temperature remained stable despite external changes.
It's important for a government to maintain a stable economy.
Thanks to careful policies, inflation remained stable throughout the year.
The new medicine has made his condition more stable.
We need a stable base to prevent the shelves from toppling over.
Prices have remained stable over the past few months.
The country seeks a stable government to promote economic growth.
The patient is in a stable condition and expected to recover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: secure - fixed, not likely to give way or be lost
  • synonym: static - showing little or no change, especially in a way that is dull or uninteresting
  • synonym: steady - firmly fixed, constant, or unchanging
  • antonym: unstable - likely to change or fail; not firm or fixed
  • antonym: unsteady - not firm, likely to change or move
