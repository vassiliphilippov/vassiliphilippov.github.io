Nisei

noun
second-generation Japanese immigrant (US/Canada)
Pronunciation: / ˈniːseɪ / (AmE), / ˈniːseɪ / (BrE)
A person born in the United States or Canada to Japanese immigrant parents, especially those who were the second generation after emigration.

Examples

A Nisei person
Her grandfather was an Issei, but she proudly identified as a Nisei.
Many Nisei grew up bilingual, balancing Japanese traditions with American culture.
The experiences of Nisei during World War II have been widely documented by historians.
