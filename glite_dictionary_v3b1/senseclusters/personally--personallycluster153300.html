personally

adverb
in one's own opinion or capacity
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpɜːr.sən.ə.liː/ /; / /ˈpɜːr.snə.liː/ (AmE), /ˈpɜː.sən.ə.liː/ /; / /ˈpɜː.snə.liː/ (BrE)
used to show that you are giving your own opinion or experience, not someone else's

Examples

Personally, I agree
Personally, I think this is the best solution available.
I don't personally know the manager, but I've heard good things about him.
She said she would handle the issue personally to ensure it was resolved quickly.
Personally, I think the new policy will benefit everyone involved.
Personally, I've never been a fan of horror movies.
If you ask me personally, I would choose the second option.
