ton

noun
US unit of weight (2,000 pounds)
Pronunciation: / tʌn / (AmE), / tʌn / (BrE)
A unit of weight in the United States equal to 2,000 pounds (about 907 kilograms).

Examples

An American ton
Each shipment weighed over five tons, making logistics more complex.
In the US, a ton is commonly used for measuring large weights.
The truck was capable of hauling one ton of material at a time.
