photocoagulation

noun
laser surgery treating retinal disorders
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊkoʊˌæɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊkəʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure that uses an intense laser beam to destroy diseased retinal tissue or create a scar to secure the retina, especially to treat retinal disorders such as a detached retina

Examples

Laser photocoagulation treatment
Advances in photocoagulation have greatly improved outcomes for patients with diabetic retinopathy.
After the photocoagulation procedure, the patient noticed significant improvement in his vision.
The ophthalmologist recommended photocoagulation to treat the damaged blood vessels in her retina.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cryocoagulation - a surgical technique using freezing to coagulate tissue
  • synonym: laser surgery - surgical procedures performed using a laser
