physoclistous

adjective
fish without swim bladder duct
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪsoʊˈklɪstəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪsəˈklɪstəs / (BrE)
describing fish whose swim bladder is not connected to the gut by a duct

Examples

Physoclistous fish species
Many deep-sea species are physoclistous, allowing them to control buoyancy without surfacing.
The goldfish is not physoclistous, but some aquarium fish are.
Researchers studied how physoclistous fish adjust to rapid changes in water pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: physostomous - describing fish with a duct connecting the swim bladder to the gut
