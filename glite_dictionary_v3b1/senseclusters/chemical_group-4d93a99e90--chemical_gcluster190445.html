chemical group

noun
unit of atoms within a molecule
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmɪkəl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ɡruːp / (BrE)
(Chemistry) Two or more atoms bonded together as a unit and forming part of a molecule.

Examples

A chemical group in molecule
Each chemical group in the compound affects how it reacts with other substances.
Researchers identified a new chemical group attached to the polymer backbone.
The carboxyl chemical group is found in all amino acids.
