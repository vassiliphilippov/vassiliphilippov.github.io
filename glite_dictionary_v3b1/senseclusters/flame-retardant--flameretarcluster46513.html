flame-retardant

adjective
not easily catching fire
Pronunciation: / fleɪm rɪˈtɑrdənt / (AmE), / fleɪm rɪˈtɑːdənt / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) resistant to catching fire and designed to slow or prevent the spread of flames.

Examples

Flame-retardant material
All children's pajamas sold here must be flame-retardant according to new regulations.
The curtains are made from flame-retardant material for added safety.
When building a campfire, keep flame-retardant blankets nearby just in case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fire-resistant - able to withstand fire or heat
  • antonym: flammable - easily set on fire
