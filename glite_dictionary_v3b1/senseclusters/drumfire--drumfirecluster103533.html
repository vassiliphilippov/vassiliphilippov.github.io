drumfire

noun
continuous heavy artillery bombardment
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌmˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌfaɪə / (BrE)
intense and continuous artillery bombardment, especially characterized by rapid and sustained firing.

Examples

Continuous drumfire attack
During the night, the relentless drumfire shattered any hope of sleep in the trenches.
The general ordered a drumfire to weaken the enemy's defenses before the assault.
The soldiers endured hours of drumfire before advancing toward the enemy lines.
