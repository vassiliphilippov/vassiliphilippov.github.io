impersonalism

noun
emphasis on impersonality
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɜrsənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or practice of emphasizing impersonality or avoiding personal involvement or emotion

Examples

Corporate impersonalism
The company's strict impersonalism made employees feel disconnected from management.
Some schools of thought promote impersonalism to ensure fairness and equality.
Critics argue that excessive impersonalism can lead to a lack of empathy in society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: objectivity - the quality of being unbiased and not influenced by personal feelings
  • antonym: personalism - the belief in the importance of personal relationships or involvement
