  • unfavorable judgment

  • reproof
    7.1 0.09/m
    reproof is usually mild and aims to correct behavior, while criticism can be stronger and point out many faults.
  • rebuke
    6.8 1/m
    rebuke is a sharp, forceful scolding aimed at someone, while criticism can be neutral or simply point out faults.
  • reprimand
    6.1 1/m
    reprimand is a formal scolding by someone in authority, while criticism can be informal and not from a person in power.
  • chastisement
    7.2 0.1/m
    chastisement is a scolding or punishment that may be physical or verbal, while criticism usually means pointing out faults without punishment.
  • censure
    7.4 7/m
    censure is an official, serious expression of disapproval, while criticism can be casual or private rather than formal.
  • condemnation
    5.5 3/m

  • denunciation
    7.4 0.5/m

  • disparagement
