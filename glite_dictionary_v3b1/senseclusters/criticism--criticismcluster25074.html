Synonyms

  • reproval
    7.5 0.01/m
    reproval is a direct expression of disapproval in speech or writing, while criticism points out faults or shortcomings.
  • unfavorable judgment
    6.8 0.01/m
    unfavorable judgment is an opinion that points out faults and disapproval, while criticism clearly explains problems or failures.
  • reproof
    7.1 0.33/m
    reproof is usually mild and aims to correct behavior, while criticism can be stronger and point out many faults.
  • rebuke
    6.8 1.79/m
    rebuke is a sharp, forceful scolding aimed at someone, while criticism can be neutral or simply point out faults.
  • reprimand
    6.1 1.59/m
    reprimand is a formal scolding by someone in authority, while criticism can be informal and not from a person in power.
  • chastisement
    7.2 0.47/m
    chastisement is a scolding or punishment that may be physical or verbal, while criticism usually means pointing out faults without punishment.
  • censure
    7.4 5.34/m
    censure is an official, serious expression of disapproval, while criticism can be casual or private rather than formal.
  • condemnation
    5.5 6.16/m
    condemnation is a strong judgment that something is wrong and blameworthy, while criticism may simply note faults.
  • denunciation
    7.4 1.86/m
    denunciation is a public, forceful calling out of wrongdoing, while criticism can be private or less public in tone.
  • disparagement
    8.1 0.43/m
    disparagement is speech that belittles or attacks someone's value, while criticism aims to point out faults without insulting.
  • denigration
    8.4 0.61/m
    denigration is unfair talk that aims to harm someone's reputation, while criticism can be fair and focused on improvement.
