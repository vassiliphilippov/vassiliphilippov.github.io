polarizability

noun
ability to be distorted
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊləraɪzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌpəʊləraɪzəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of a molecule or atom to have its electron cloud distorted by an electric field

Examples

Molecular polarizability
The polarizability of a molecule affects how it interacts with electric fields.
Scientists measure polarizability to understand the behavior of different substances.
A higher polarizability often means a molecule is more easily influenced by external charges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polarization - the process of causing something to have a positive and negative charge
