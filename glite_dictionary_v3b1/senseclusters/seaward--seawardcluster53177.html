seaward

noun
direction toward the sea
4.7 0.25/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêw…ôrd / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêw…ôd / (BrE)
the direction that is toward the sea, especially from land

Examples

The seaward side
The cliff slopes gently seaward, giving a clear view of the open water.
The fishermen gazed seaward as they prepared to launch their boat.
We set up camp facing seaward to catch the morning breeze coming off the ocean.

Synonyms

  • asea
    9.9 0.00/m
    asea describes being out on or toward the open sea, while seaward describes movement from land toward the sea.
  • coastward
    4.6 0.00/m
    coastward means moving toward the land's edge or coast, while seaward means moving away from land toward the sea.
