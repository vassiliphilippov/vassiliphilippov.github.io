turkey vulture

noun
a large New World vulture species
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝki ˌvʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki ˌvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a large bird of prey (Cathartes aura) found in the Americas, known for its dark plumage and habit of feeding on carrion

Examples

A turkey vulture soars
A turkey vulture soared high above the fields, searching for food.
The turkey vulture is often identified by its red head and broad wingspan.
We spotted several turkey vultures circling near the highway.
