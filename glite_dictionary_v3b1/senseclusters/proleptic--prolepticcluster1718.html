proleptic

adjective
anticipating the future
9.9
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈlep.tɪk/ /; / /proʊ.ˈlep.tɪk/ (AmE), /prə.ˈlep.tɪk/ /; / /prəʊ.ˈlep.tɪk/ (BrE)
anticipating, foreshadowing, or referring to something before it happens, especially in writing, speech, or argument

Examples

Proleptic hint
The author used proleptic narration to hint at the tragedy that would unfold later.
In the speech, there was a proleptic reference to reforms that had not yet been announced.
The film's opening scene is proleptic, revealing the ending before the story begins.
The lawyer's proleptic argument addressed every possible counterpoint before the opposition could respond.
Her proleptic comments in the meeting prevented misunderstandings later on.
Writers often use proleptic strategies to strengthen their persuasive essays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preemptive - acting before something happens to prevent it
  • synonym: prophetic - predicting or foreshadowing future events
  • antonym: reactive - responding to something after it happens
  • antonym: retrospective - looking back on or dealing with past events
