infield

noun
area within baseball bases
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fiːld / (BrE)
(in baseball) the area of the field enclosed by first base, second base, third base, and home plate.

Examples

Baseball infield
The infield was wet after last night's rain, causing a delay in the game.
The shortstop made a diving catch in the infield to stop the runner.
There are four main positions on the infield: first base, second base, third base, and shortstop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outfield - the part of a baseball field beyond the diamond and the bases
