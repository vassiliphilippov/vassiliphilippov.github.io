yield

noun
income or profit from investment/property
4.1 25/m
Pronunciation: / jiÀêld / (AmE), / jiÀêld / (BrE)
the amount produced or profit gained, especially from crops, investments, or a process

Examples

High yield crops
Many investors choose bonds for their reliable annual yield.
The company's yield increased after improvements in efficiency.
The real estate investment produced a steady yield over several years.
A higher fertilizer rate can lead to greater crop yield per acre.

Last year's yield of apples was the highest in a decade.
The bond has a yield of 5% per annum.

Synonyms

  • return
    3.7 32/m
    return is the profit or income from an investment, while yield can mean that profit or a physical crop or process amount.
  • production
    2.2 66/m
    production is the total made in a factory or farm over time, while yield focuses on amount or profit per unit or area.
  • proceeds
