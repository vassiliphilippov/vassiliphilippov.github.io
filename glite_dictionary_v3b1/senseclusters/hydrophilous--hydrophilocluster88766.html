hydrophilous

adjective
pollinated by water
9.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.drə.ˈfɪl.əs/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɑː.fə.ləs/ (AmE), /haɪ.drə.ˈfɪl.əs/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɒ.fə.ləs/ (BrE)
adapted for pollination by water, with pollen transported by water currents.

Examples

Hydrophilous pollination
Some aquatic plants are hydrophilous, allowing their pollen to travel through water to reach other flowers.
The hydrophilous nature of these species helps them reproduce efficiently in underwater environments.
Researchers studied how hydrophilous pollination affects the genetic diversity of seagrass populations.
