peritonitis

noun
inflammation of the peritoneum
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrətəˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪtəˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the peritoneum, the membrane lining the abdominal cavity and covering the abdominal organs, often caused by infection or injury.

Examples

Peritonitis infection
Complications from a ruptured appendix can include peritonitis and sepsis.
Early detection of peritonitis is crucial for successful treatment.
The patient was diagnosed with peritonitis after complaining of severe abdominal pain.
