ill-judged

adjective
not showing good judgment
6.3 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / Éªl ËˆdÊ’ÊŒdÊ’d / (AmE), / Éªl ËˆdÊ’ÊŒdÊ’d / (BrE)
(of an action or decision) not showing good judgment; made without careful consideration of the likely consequences.

Examples

An ill-judged decision
He made an ill-judged investment and lost most of his savings.
Her ill-judged remarks created tension during the meeting.
The company suffered from an ill-judged decision to expand too quickly.
