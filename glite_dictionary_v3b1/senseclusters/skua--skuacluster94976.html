skua

noun
predatory seabird of northern seas
Pronunciation: / ˈskuːə / (AmE), / ˈskjuːə / (BrE)
a large, aggressive seabird of the northern hemisphere, related to gulls, known for piratical behavior and dark plumage

Examples

A skua bird
A skua swooped down and stole a fish from the other bird.
Many Arctic explorers have written about encounters with the aggressive skua.
The researchers studied the nesting habits of the great skua on remote islands.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gull - a related seabird, generally less predatory
  • synonym: jaeger - another name for certain skua species
