absorptivity

noun
ability of a material to absorb radiation or sound
8.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀås…îrpÀàt…™v…ôti / (AmE), / …ôbÀås…îÀêpÀàt…™v…™ti / (BrE)
The property of a material or body that measures the fraction of incident radiation or sound absorbed by it.

Examples

Absorptivity of material
By measuring the absorptivity at different wavelengths, scientists can study the composition of an object.
Glass generally has a low absorptivity for infrared radiation.
The absorptivity of the material determines how much solar energy is converted to heat.

Synonyms

  • absorptance
    9.9 0.01/m
    absorptance is the fraction of incoming radiant energy a material takes in, while absorptivity is the material property measuring that absorbed portion.
  • absorption factor
    7.4 0.01/m
    absorption factor is a physics measure of how much incident radiation or sound a body absorbs, while absorptivity is the material property that quantifies this.
