absorptivity

noun
ability of a material to absorb radiation or sound
9.1
Pronunciation: / əbˌsɔrpˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / əbˌsɔːpˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
The property of a material or body that measures the fraction of incident radiation or sound absorbed by it.

Examples

Absorptivity of material
By measuring the absorptivity at different wavelengths, scientists can study the composition of an object.
Glass generally has a low absorptivity for infrared radiation.
The absorptivity of the material determines how much solar energy is converted to heat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reflectivity - the property of reflecting radiation
  • antonym: transmissivity - the property of transmitting radiation through a material
