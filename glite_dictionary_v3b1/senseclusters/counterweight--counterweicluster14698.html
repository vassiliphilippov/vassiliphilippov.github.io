counterweight

verb
to act as a counterbalance
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌweɪt /; / ˈkaʊntərˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌweɪt / (BrE)
to balance or offset something, either physically by adding weight or by neutralizing its effect

Examples

To counterweight a loss
Rising exports may counterweight the negative impact of higher fuel prices.
The company's efforts to expand abroad helped to counterweight losses in the domestic market.
We need new policies to counterweight the growing influence of large corporations.
Engineers counterweight the elevator to ensure smooth operation.
The crane was carefully counterweighted to prevent it from tipping over during use.
To keep the sign upright in the wind, they counterweighted its base with heavy sandbags.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balance - to keep something steady or equal in weight
  • synonym: counterbalance - to offset or balance the weight of something
  • antonym: unbalance - to make something unstable or uneven in weight
