backhaul

noun
return cargo trip
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌhɔːl / (BrE)
the return trip of a vehicle, especially when carrying goods back to its point of origin

Examples

Truck backhaul trip
The trucking company scheduled a backhaul to maximize efficiency on the return journey.
By arranging a backhaul, the driver avoided traveling with an empty trailer.
Many logistics firms rely on backhauls to reduce transportation costs.
