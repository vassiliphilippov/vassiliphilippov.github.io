overgeneral

adjective
too broad
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
too broad or lacking in necessary detail or specificity

Examples

An overgeneral conclusion
The report was criticized for its overgeneral conclusions that ignored important exceptions.
Her overgeneral comments did not address the unique challenges faced by each department.
Avoid making overgeneral statements when discussing complex social issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: specific - clearly defined or identified
  • synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or lacking detail
