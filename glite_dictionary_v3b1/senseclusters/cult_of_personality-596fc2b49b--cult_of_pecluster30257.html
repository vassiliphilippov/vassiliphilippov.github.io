cult of personality

noun
intense admiration for a public figure
Pronunciation: / kʌlt əv pɚˌsɑːnəˈlɪɾi / (AmE), / kʌlt əv pɜː.səˈnæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
a situation in which a public figure, such as a political leader, is deliberately presented to the public as an idealized, heroic figure, and is the object of intense or even unquestioning public admiration and devotion

Examples

A cult of personality
Some historians argue that a cult of personality can undermine democratic institutions.
The dictator used propaganda to create a cult of personality around himself.
The leader's speeches and images fueled a growing cult of personality in the country.
