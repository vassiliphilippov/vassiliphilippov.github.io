populace

noun
the general public or people collectively
5.7 5.52/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêpj…ôl…ôs / (AmE), / Ààp…ípj äl…ôs / (BrE)
the people living in a particular country or area, considered as a whole

Examples

The populace of a country
The country's leaders must listen to the concerns of the populace.
The mayor made promises to improve the lives of the entire populace.
There was widespread discontent among the populace after the new law was introduced.

Synonyms

  • population
    2.3 168/m
    population means all people who live in a place counted together, while populace is a more formal word for that whole group.
  • people
    1.0 12/m
    people means human beings or citizens in general, while populace sounds more formal and groups them as a single body.
  • public
    1.0 124/m
    public means ordinary people or an audience for something, while populace refers to all residents of an area considered together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: population - all the inhabitants of a particular area
  • synonym: public - the people in general considered collectively
