lyreflower

noun
garden plant with heart-shaped pink flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪərˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪəˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A garden plant with deep-pink, drooping, heart-shaped flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental; also called bleeding heart.

Examples

The lyreflower plant
Gardeners often plant lyreflowers beneath trees for their attractive, heart-shaped petals.
The lyreflower blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to shady gardens.
The delicate pink blossoms of the lyreflower are a favorite among pollinators.
