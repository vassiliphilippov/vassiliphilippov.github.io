long dozen

noun
group or set of thirteen
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈdʌzən / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈdʌzən / (BrE)
A group or set of thirteen, especially as a term for thirteen instead of the usual twelve in a dozen.

Examples

A long dozen eggs
He ordered a long dozen eggs, which meant he received thirteen instead of twelve.
The baker added an extra loaf to make it a long dozen.
Traditionally, a long dozen was used to avoid shortchanging customers.
