deacon

noun
cleric below a priest in Christian churches
7.4 2.40/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdiÀêk…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀêk…ôn / (BrE)
A member of the clerical order in some Christian churches who ranks just below a priest and is often responsible for certain services or functions.

Examples

Catholic deacon
She was ordained as a deacon after years of theological study.
The deacon assisted the priest during the Sunday service.
The deacon organized charity activities for the local community.
