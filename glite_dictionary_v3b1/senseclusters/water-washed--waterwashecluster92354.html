water-washed

adjective
washed by water or waves
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər ˌwɑʃt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌwɒʃt / (BrE)
washed or cleaned by water, either naturally (by rain, waves, etc.) or by being washed

Examples

Water-washed stones
Ancient, water-washed stones littered the riverbed after the flood.
The water-washed pebbles gleamed in the sunlight along the shore.
The cliff face was smooth and water-washed from centuries of crashing waves.
After the rain, the stones looked as if they had been water-washed and polished.
All drinking glasses must be water-washed before use in the laboratory.
The freshly water-washed vegetables gleamed in the morning sunlight.
