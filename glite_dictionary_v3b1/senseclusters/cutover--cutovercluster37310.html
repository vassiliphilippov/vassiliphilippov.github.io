cutover

adjective
cleared of trees
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌəʊvə / (BrE)
cleared of trees by logging, especially referring to land from which timber has been removed

Examples

Cutover land
The company plans to replant the cutover land with native species next spring.
Wildlife populations declined after the forest became cutover and lost much of its habitat.
Researchers studied how quickly vegetation returned to the cutover area after logging.

  • antonym: forested - covered with trees
