pretax

adverb
before taxes
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtæks / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtæks / (BrE)
before taxes are applied or deducted, especially when describing an amount or change.

Examples

Pretax earnings reported
The company reported a pretax profit of two million dollars this quarter.
Her pretax income was higher than expected, but she still owed a lot in taxes.
Investors often compare pretax earnings to evaluate a business's performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gross - total amount before any deductions
  • antonym: net - after all deductions, including taxes
