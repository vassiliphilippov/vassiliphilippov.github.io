chemokine

noun
immune signaling protein
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkem.oʊ.kaɪn/ /; / /ˈkiː.moʊ.kaɪn/ (AmE), /ˈkem.əʊ.kaɪn/ /; / /ˈkiː.məʊ.kaɪn/ (BrE)
a type of protein that attracts or guides immune cells (especially white blood cells) to specific areas in the body, such as sites of infection or injury

Examples

A chemokine protein
The body produces chemokines to guide white blood cells to sites of infection.
Researchers study how chemokines influence inflammation in autoimmune diseases.
Blocking certain chemokines can help reduce tissue damage during chronic illness.
A chemokine can attract white blood cells to the site of an infection.
Researchers studied how each chemokine affected the movement of immune cells in the tissue.
The release of a chemokine is a key step in the body's response to injury.
