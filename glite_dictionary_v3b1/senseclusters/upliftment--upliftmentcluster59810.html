upliftment

noun
raising of land

the geological process by which the Earth's crust or land is raised to a higher elevation

Examples

Mountain upliftment
The Himalayas are a result of the continuous upliftment of the Earth's crust.
Scientists study upliftment to understand how mountain ranges are formed over time.
Tectonic forces can cause significant upliftment in certain regions, changing the landscape dramatically.

Synonyms

  • uplift
    7.0 0.41/m
    uplift is the standard geology term for a rise of land, while upliftment names the process of that raising.
  • upheaval
    7.3 0.22/m
    upheaval is a sudden geological lifting that often changes landscapes, while upliftment is the general process of land being raised.
  • upthrust
    8.6 0.02/m
    upthrust is a strong tectonic push that lifts land during mountain building, while upliftment refers to the whole raising process.
  • upthrow
    9.9 0.00/m
    upthrow is the upward movement of one fault block, while upliftment describes the broader process of the Earth's crust rising.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subsidence - the gradual sinking or lowering of land
  • synonym: uplift - the process of raising something to a higher position, especially in geology
