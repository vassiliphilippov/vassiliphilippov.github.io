co-teaching

adjective
joint teaching arrangement
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
involving two teachers working together to instruct a group of students in the same classroom or program

Examples

Co-teaching approach
The school implemented a co-teaching model to better support students with diverse learning needs.
A co-teaching classroom allows both teachers to share responsibility for planning and instruction.
Many educators find the co-teaching approach helpful for integrating students with special needs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: solo teaching - instruction delivered by a single teacher
  • synonym: team-teaching - a method where two or more teachers jointly deliver instruction
