variation

noun
a thing differing from the usual type
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛriˈeɪʃən /; / ˌveɹ.iˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌveərɪˈeɪʃən /; / ˌveə.ɹɪˈeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌvɛəriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a version or form that is different from others of the same type, including an organism with different characteristics

Examples

A new variation
Each artist creates a unique variation based on the original concept.
The designer introduced a bold variation on the classic handbag.
There are several variations of this traditional recipe available in the market.
Genetic variation in the population leads to differences in appearance and ability.
The chef created a delicious variation of the classic recipe using local ingredients.
This new song is just a slight variation on their previous hit.
Biologists documented a new variation of the butterfly that differed in wing pattern due to chromosomal alteration.
The scientist studied each variation to determine how chromosomal changes affected development.
This rare variation displays a genetic mutation not found in the parent population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutant - an organism genetically different from its parent due to mutation
  • synonym: variant - something that differs in some respect from others of the same kind
