Bacillus thuringiensis

noun
bacterial pesticide
Pronunciation: / bəˈsɪləs θʊˌrɪndʒiˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / bəˈsɪləs θjʊˌrɪndʒiˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a bacterium used as a natural pesticide to control insect pests, especially in agriculture

Examples

Bacillus thuringiensis pesticide

Scientists study Bacillus thuringiensis to develop safer pest control methods for agriculture.
Farmers often use Bacillus thuringiensis as a natural way to control insect pests on crops.
The laboratory tested the effectiveness of Bacillus thuringiensis against different species of caterpillars.
Because Bacillus thuringiensis targets specific insects, it is considered safer for the environment than chemical pesticides.
