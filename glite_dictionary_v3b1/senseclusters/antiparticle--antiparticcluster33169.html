antiparticle

noun
subatomic particle with opposite properties to its counterpart
5.5 7.4
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈpɑːr.tɪ.kəl/ /; / /æn.tə.ˈpɑːr.tɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈpɑː.tɪ.kəl/ /; / /æn.tə.ˈpɑː.tɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
a subatomic particle that has the same mass as another type of particle but opposite electric charge or other properties, so that the particle and its antiparticle annihilate each other when they meet

Examples

The antiparticle of electron
A positron is the antiparticle of the electron, sharing its mass but possessing a positive charge.
Scientists continue to study the properties and behavior of each antiparticle in high-energy experiments.
When a antiparticle meets its counterpart, both are destroyed and energy is released.
