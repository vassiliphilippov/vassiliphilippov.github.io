thermionic tube

noun
vacuum electronic device with electrodes
8.2
Pronunciation: / θɜːrˈmaɪˌɑːnɪk tuːb / (AmE), / θəːˈmaɪˌɒnɪk tjuːb / (BrE)
an electronic device with electrodes in a vacuum-sealed glass or metal enclosure, used to control the flow of electric current

Examples

A thermionic tube amplifier
Early radios often relied on a thermionic tube to amplify weak signals.
The thermionic tube was a key component in twentieth-century electronic equipment.
With the advent of transistors, the use of thermionic tubes declined significantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vacuum tube - an electronic device consisting of a sealed glass or metal enclosure from which air has been removed, used to control electric current
