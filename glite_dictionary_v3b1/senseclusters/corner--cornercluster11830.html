corner

verb
trap or force into an inescapable position
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
to force a person or animal into a place or situation from which they cannot escape

Examples

To corner a suspect
The police managed to corner the suspect in the alley.
They finally cornered the thief after a long chase.
When frightened, the cat might scratch if you corner it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trap - to catch or force into a situation from which there is no escape
