ideologize

verb
to promote ideology
8.9
Pronunciation: /aɪ.dɪ.ˈlɑː.lə.dʒaɪz/ /; / /aɪ.dɪ.ˈlɔː.lə.dʒaɪz/ (AmE), /aɪ.dɪ.ˈlɒ.lə.dʒaɪz/ (BrE)
to make someone or something influenced by, shaped by, or interpreted according to a particular ideology or set of beliefs

Examples

To ideologize a group
The government tried to ideologize the youth through carefully designed educational programs.
Some media outlets have been accused of attempting to ideologize their audiences.
It is important to teach critical thinking rather than ideologize students in the classroom.
Some commentators tend to ideologize every political issue.
It is important not to ideologize scientific research with personal beliefs.
The debate became less productive when both sides began to ideologize the discussion.
Some commentators tend to ideologize every issue, even when practical solutions are needed.
The teacher warned against ideologizing the classroom discussion about history.
It is important not to ideologize scientific research, as it should remain objective.

  • antonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject

  • synonym: systematize - to arrange according to a system or plan
