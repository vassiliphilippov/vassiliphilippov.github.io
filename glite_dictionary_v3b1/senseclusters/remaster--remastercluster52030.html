remaster

noun
improved new version
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈmæstər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmɑːstə / (BrE)
a new version of a recording, film, or game created from the original master with improved sound or image quality

Examples

A remaster album
The band released a remaster of their classic album with enhanced audio quality.
Collectors often seek out the remaster for its improved visuals and bonus content.
The movie's remaster brought new life to the old footage, making it clearer than ever.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or earliest version of a recording, film, or game
  • synonym: reissue - a new release of a previously issued recording or film
