fly-by-night

adjective
unreliable or untrustworthy, often disappearing quickly
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt /; / ˈflaɪ baɪ ˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt /; / ˈflaɪ baɪ ˌnaɪt / (BrE)
not reliable or trustworthy; likely to disappear or fail quickly, especially for dishonest reasons

Examples

Fly-by-night business
Be wary of fly-by-night companies offering deals that seem too good to be true.
Many people were scammed by a fly-by-night operation that promised instant profits.
The city cracked down on fly-by-night contractors who took deposits and vanished.
Her promise seemed fly-by-night, with no guarantee of lasting commitment.
The company turned out to be a fly-by-night operation that folded within months.
We need to avoid fly-by-night contractors who may not finish the work.
Consumers are often warned about fly-by-night operations that offer deals too good to be true.
She lost her savings to a fly-by-night investment firm.
The fly-by-night builder vanished with their deposit before any work was started.
