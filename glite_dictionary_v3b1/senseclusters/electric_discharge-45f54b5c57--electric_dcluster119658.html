electric discharge

noun
flow of electricity through a gas
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the flow of electricity through a gas caused by an applied electric field, often resulting in light or other visible effects

Examples

An electric discharge spark
A sudden electric discharge can be seen as a spark when plugging in a device.
Lightning is a natural example of a massive electric discharge in the atmosphere.
The neon lamp glowed when an electric discharge passed through the gas inside.
