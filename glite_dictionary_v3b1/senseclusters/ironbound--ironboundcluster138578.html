ironbound

adjective
rigid or unchangeable
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪərnˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
firmly fixed, rigid, or not open to change, especially in rules or agreements

Examples

Ironbound rules
The committee followed an ironbound set of rules that could not be questioned.
Negotiations failed because both sides held ironbound positions on the main issues.
The school has an ironbound policy against cheating, with no exceptions allowed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inflexible - not able or willing to change or adapt
  • synonym: rigid - not able to be changed or adapted
