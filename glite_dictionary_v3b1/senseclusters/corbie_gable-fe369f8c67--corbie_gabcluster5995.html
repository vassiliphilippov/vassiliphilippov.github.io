corbie gable

noun
gable with stepped outline or corbie-steps
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.bi ˈɡeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.bi ˈɡeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
A gable with a stepped outline formed by corbie-steps (also called corbel steps) along its sides, commonly seen in some medieval and Scottish architecture.

Examples

A corbie gable design
Architectural historians admired the finely carved stonework on the corbie gable of the manor.
The old church featured a beautiful corbie gable that highlighted its medieval origins.
Traditional Scottish houses sometimes have a corbie gable for both style and structural reasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corbie-step - a step-like feature on the side of a gable
