spiderweb

noun
tangled network
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪdərˌwɛb / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdəˌwɛb / (BrE)
a complex or tangled network or pattern that resembles a spider's web, either physically (like cracks or lines) or figuratively (like relationships or influences)

Examples

A spiderweb of lies
The investigation uncovered a spiderweb of secret deals and hidden motives.
She felt trapped in a spiderweb of conflicting loyalties and obligations.
The company’s finances were entangled in a spiderweb of offshore accounts.
A fine spiderweb of cracks spread across the windscreen after the accident.
The artist painted a delicate spiderweb pattern on the vase.
Morning dew made the spiderweb on the fence sparkle in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cobweb - a web spun by a spider, especially when old and covered with dust
  • synonym: maze - a complicated and confusing arrangement of passages or paths
  • synonym: mesh - a network of wire or thread with spaces between the strands
  • synonym: network - a system of interconnected people or things
  • synonym: web - a network of fine threads or a complex system of connections
