bascule

noun
counterbalanced structure or device
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbæsˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskjuːl / (BrE)
A structure or device with a pivoted arm balanced by a counterweight, operating on the principle of a seesaw, such as those used in some bridges.

Examples

A bascule bridge
A modern bascule bridge often uses electric motors to control its movement.
Engineers inspected the bascule mechanism for signs of wear and tear.
The bascule of the drawbridge allowed ships to pass through the canal by lifting easily.
