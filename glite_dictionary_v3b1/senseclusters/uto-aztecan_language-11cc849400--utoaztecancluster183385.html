Uto-Aztecan language

noun
language family of western US and Mexico
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːtoʊ æzˈtɛkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌjuːtəʊ æzˈtɛkən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of American Indian languages spoken in the western United States and Mexico, including languages such as Ute, Hopi, and Nahuatl

Examples

The Uto-Aztecan language family
Nahuatl is perhaps the best-known Uto-Aztecan language due to its historical use by the Aztecs.
The Hopi people speak a Uto-Aztecan language native to the American Southwest.
There are over thirty distinct Uto-Aztecan languages still spoken today.
