atmospheric static

noun
noise from atmospheric electrical interference
Pronunciation: / ˌætməsˈfɪrɪk ˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌatməsˈfɛrɪk ˈstatɪk / (BrE)
a crackling or hissing noise heard in radio receivers, caused by electrical disturbances in the atmosphere

Examples

Atmospheric static noise
Scientists study atmospheric static to better understand lightning and other weather phenomena.
The pilot reported significant atmospheric static disrupting radio communications during the storm.
We couldn't hear the broadcast clearly because of atmospheric static.

Related Terms

  • synonym: static - crackling or hissing noise in radio or television reception
