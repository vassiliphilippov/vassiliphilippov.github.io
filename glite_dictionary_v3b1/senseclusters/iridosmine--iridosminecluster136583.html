iridosmine

noun
hard mineral alloy of osmium and iridium
6.1 9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈrɪd.əz.maɪn/ /; / /ɪ.ˈrɪd.əz.miːn/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈrɪd.ɒs.miːn/ /; / /ɪ.ˈrɪd.ɒs.maɪn/ (BrE)
A hard, corrosion-resistant mineral that is a natural alloy of osmium and iridium, often containing small amounts of rhodium and platinum, used in making needles and pen nibs.

Examples

Hard iridosmine mineral
Because of its hardness, iridosmine is valued in the production of scientific instruments.
Mining for iridosmine was once common in certain riverbeds due to its industrial applications.
The pen's nib was made of iridosmine for extra durability.
