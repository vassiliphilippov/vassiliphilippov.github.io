reducing agent

noun
substance that induces reduction (chemistry)
4.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdusɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːsɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A substance that causes reduction in another substance by being oxidized itself; often used in chemical reactions and photography.

Examples

A reducing agent donates electrons
During the redox process, magnesium serves as the reducing agent by donating electrons.
Hydrogen peroxide can act as a reducing agent in certain chemical reactions.
In photography, a reducing agent is used to lighten areas of an overexposed negative.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oxidizing agent - a substance that brings about oxidation by being reduced
