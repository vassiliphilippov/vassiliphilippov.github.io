capacitor

noun
electrical device for storing charge
Pronunciation: / kəˈpæsɪtər / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪtə / (BrE)
an electrical component that stores and releases electrical energy in a circuit by accumulating electric charge

Examples

An electrical capacitor
A capacitor is often used in circuits to smooth out fluctuations in voltage.
Before handling the circuit, make sure the capacitor is fully discharged for safety.
The engineer replaced the faulty capacitor on the motherboard to restore the computer's power supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condenser - another term for an electrical device that stores charge
