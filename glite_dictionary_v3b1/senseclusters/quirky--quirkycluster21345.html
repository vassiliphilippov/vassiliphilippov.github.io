quirky

adjective
unconventional or eccentric in an appealing way
4.4 4.76/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkw…ùÀêki / (AmE), / Ààkw…úÀêki / (BrE)
Unusual in an interesting or appealing way; characterized by unexpected or unconventional traits, especially in behavior or style.

Examples

A quirky style
He wore a quirky hat that immediately caught everyone's attention.
Her quirky sense of humor always makes everyone laugh at parties.
The film is filled with quirky characters who are both odd and endearing.

Synonyms

  • offbeat
    6.6 1.27/m
    offbeat is unusually unconventional and often used for creative style, while quirky describes charming, odd personal traits.
  • kooky
    8.0 0.74/m
    kooky feels slightly crazy and laughably odd in speech, while quirky suggests a gentle, attractive oddness in behavior or style.
  • idiosyncratic
    8.1 2.81/m
    idiosyncratic means highly specific to one person or thing, while quirky focuses on small, charming odd traits people notice.
  • peculiar
    4.6 11/m
    peculiar simply means strange or unusual in many contexts, while quirky implies a pleasing, unexpected charm in behavior or style.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - typical or normal according to accepted standards
  • synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange
