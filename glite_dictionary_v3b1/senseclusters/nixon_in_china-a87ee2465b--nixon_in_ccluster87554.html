Nixon in China

noun
unexpected dramatic or reconciliatory action
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪksən ɪn tʃaɪˈnə / (AmE), / ˈnɪksən ɪn ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
An action, especially by a leader, that is unexpected and bold—typically one involving reconciliation or a dramatic policy shift, especially by someone previously seen as an opponent.

Examples

A Nixon in China moment
Historians now view the peace agreement as the administration's Nixon in China move.
The prime minister's decision to meet with her long-time rivals was a real Nixon in China moment.
When the CEO announced cooperation with a competing firm, staff called it a Nixon in China strategy.
