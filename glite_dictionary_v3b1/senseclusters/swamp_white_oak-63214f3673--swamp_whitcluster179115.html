swamp white oak

noun
oak tree native to wet eastern North America
2.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /swɑmp waɪt oʊk/ /; / /swɔːmp waɪt oʊk/ (AmE), /swɒmp waɪt əʊk/ (BrE)
a large deciduous oak (Quercus bicolor) native to eastern North America, having flaky bark, leaves with shallow lobes, and strong wood used in construction; it typically grows in wet or swampy soils.

Examples

The swamp white oak tree
Many parks have planted swamp white oak trees to help restore natural wetland habitats.
The swamp white oak thrives in low-lying areas where the soil remains moist for much of the year.
Wood from the swamp white oak is valued for its strength and durability in construction projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: white oak - a type of oak tree with pale wood and rounded-lobed leaves
