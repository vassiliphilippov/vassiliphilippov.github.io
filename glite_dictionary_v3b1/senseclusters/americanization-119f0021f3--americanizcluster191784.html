Americanization

noun
assimilation into American culture
Pronunciation: / əˌmɛrɪkənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / əˌmɛr.ɪ.kə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪkənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / əˌmɛr.ɪ.kə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person or group adopts the customs, values, and behaviors of American culture

Examples

Americanization of culture
Critics argue that Americanization can erode local customs and languages.
Many immigrants underwent Americanization programs upon their arrival in the early twentieth century.
The Americanization of traditional foods has changed how many cuisines are prepared in the United States.
Language is often influenced by the Americanisation of media and technology.
Some critics worry about the Americanisation of popular culture in Europe.
The Americanisation of local businesses has changed the landscape of the high street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Americanization - assimilation into American culture; standard American spelling
  • synonym: Anglicization - assimilation into English or British culture
