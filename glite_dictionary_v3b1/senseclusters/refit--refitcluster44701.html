refit

verb
to equip or restore again
3.9 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàf…™t / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàf…™t / (BrE)
to provide with new equipment or repair and restore for further use, especially by replacing or upgrading parts

Examples

To refit a ship
After years at sea, the navy ship was refitted with new technology.
The company decided to refit the old warehouse into a modern office space.
We plan to refit all the classrooms with better lighting and projectors next year.

Synonyms

  • retrofit
    6.5 0.87/m
    retrofit adds new or modern parts to an older item to update it, while refit may mean repairing or reequipping for further use.
  • overhaul
    5.1 4.04/m
    overhaul is a thorough inspection and repair of a machine, while refit often focuses on replacing or upgrading parts and equipment.
  • recondition
    5.4 0.32/m
    recondition restores something to good, usable condition through repair, while refit often includes fitting new equipment or major part upgrades.
  • reequip
    4.9 0.01/m
    reequip gives something replacement equipment after loss or use, while refit also involves repair and upgrading of parts for reuse.
