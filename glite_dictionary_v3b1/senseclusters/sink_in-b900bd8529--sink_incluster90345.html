sink in

verb
become fully understood or realized
2.8
Pronunciation: / sɪŋk ɪn / (AmE), / sɪŋk ɪn / (BrE)
to become fully understood or appreciated; to be realized or accepted gradually by someone

Examples

To sink in slowly
As the reality of their victory began to sink in, the team erupted in celebration.
Her words didn't sink in until I was alone and had time to think.
It took several days for the news of his promotion to really sink in.
