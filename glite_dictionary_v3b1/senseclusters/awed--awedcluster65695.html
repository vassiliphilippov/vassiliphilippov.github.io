awed

adjective
filled with reverential wonder or fear

filled with a feeling of awe (a mix of wonder, reverence, respect, or sometimes fear)

Examples

An awed expression
His awed expression showed just how deeply he was moved by the ancient ruins.
She listened in awed silence as the astronaut described walking on the Moon.
The children stood awed by the towering cathedral walls and hushed silence.

Synonyms

  • awestruck
    6.2 0.94/m
    awestruck is filled with deep reverence and wonder, while awed is a general feeling that may include fear.
  • awestricken
    8.6 0.00/m
    awestricken is an older form often found in writing, while awed is the common modern word for feeling wonder.
  • wonderstruck
    6.0 0.01/m
    wonderstruck is full of surprise and admiration at beauty, while awed covers a wider mix of reverence or fear.
  • transfixed
    6.7 0.00/m
    transfixed is held motionless by shock or amazement, while awed is the general feeling of wonder, respect, or fear.
  • overawed
    7.2 0.00/m
    overawed is deeply impressed and often intimidated by someone's power, while awed can be neutral or include gentle respect or fear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amazed - greatly surprised or impressed
  • synonym: awestruck - filled with awe; amazed by something remarkable or powerful
  • degree lower: fearful - feeling fear or anxiety; frightened
  • degree higher: overawed - extremely impressed or intimidated by awe
  • antonym: unimpressed - not affected by awe; lacking admiration or respect
