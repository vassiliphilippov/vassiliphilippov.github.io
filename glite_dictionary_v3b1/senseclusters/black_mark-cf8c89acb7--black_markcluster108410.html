black mark

noun
lasting sign of disapproval
7.0
Pronunciation: / blæk mɑrk / (AmE), / blæk mɑːk / (BrE)
An event or characteristic that causes someone's reputation to be viewed unfavourably; a lasting sign of disapproval.

Examples

A black mark against
A missed deadline was a black mark against her professional record.
Despite his achievements, that one mistake remains a black mark in his file.
The scandal left a black mark on his previously unblemished career.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blemish - a mark or flaw that spoils the appearance or reputation
  • antonym: credit - something that adds to reputation or honour
  • synonym: stigma - a mark of disgrace associated with a circumstance or person
