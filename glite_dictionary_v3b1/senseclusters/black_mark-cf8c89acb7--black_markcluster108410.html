black mark

noun
lasting sign of disapproval
7.0 4.7
Pronunciation: /blæk mɑːrk/ (AmE), /blæk mɑːk/ (BrE)
An event or characteristic that causes someone's reputation to be viewed unfavourably; a lasting sign of disapproval.

Examples

The scandal left a black mark on his previously unblemished career.
