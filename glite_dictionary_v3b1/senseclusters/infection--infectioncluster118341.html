infection

noun
moral corruption or contamination
6.0 6.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃən/ (BrE)
the gradual process or state of moral corruption or contamination, especially through the influence of others

Examples

Moral infection
He resisted the infection of hatred, even when those around him succumbed to it.
She worried that the infection of greed might spread through the organization.
The novel explores the slow infection of society's values by cynicism and dishonesty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior; the act of making someone or something morally depraved
  • antonym: purity - the state of being morally clean or free from corruption
