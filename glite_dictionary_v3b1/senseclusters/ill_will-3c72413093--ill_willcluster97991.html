ill will

noun
hostile or unfriendly feeling
5.3 1.05/m
Pronunciation: / Éªl wÉªl / (AmE), / Éªl wÉªl / (BrE)
a feeling of hostility, animosity, or strong dislike towards someone; unfriendliness or enmity

Examples

Ill will between neighbours
Decades of conflict have left a legacy of ill will between the two communities.
Despite their history of ill will, they managed to work together on the project.
She harbored no ill will towards her former classmates after the argument.
She bore no ill will towards her former colleagues despite their disagreements.
The manager worked hard to resolve any ill will within the team.
There was a lot of ill will between the two rivals after the fierce competition.

Synonyms

  • animosity

  • enmity
    8.0 1.68/m
    enmity is long-lasting hostile feeling between people or groups, while ill will often means a simpler personal dislike.
  • animus
    9.0 1.30/m
    animus is a formal word for a strong hostile feeling with a clear target, while ill will is more general dislike.
  • hatred
    1.9 23/m
    hatred is very intense dislike that can push people to act, while ill will is a less extreme, quieter hostility.
  • malevolence
    8.8 0.48/m
    malevolence is an active wish to harm or hurt others, while ill will is simply a feeling of dislike or animosity.
  • loathing
    5.2 3.13/m

  • antipathy
