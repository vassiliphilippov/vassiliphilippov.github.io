auxiliary engine

noun
secondary engine on a vessel
Pronunciation: / ɔgˈzɪljəri ˈɛnʤɪn / (AmE), / ɔːgˈzɪliəri ˈɛnʤɪn / (BrE)
a small engine on a ship or boat used to operate equipment such as a windlass or provide additional power

Examples

The auxiliary engine on ship
During the power failure, the auxiliary engine kept essential systems running smoothly.
Many older sailboats have an auxiliary engine for use when wind conditions are poor.
The ship relied on its auxiliary engine to power the anchor winch during docking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main engine - the principal engine responsible for propulsion on a ship
