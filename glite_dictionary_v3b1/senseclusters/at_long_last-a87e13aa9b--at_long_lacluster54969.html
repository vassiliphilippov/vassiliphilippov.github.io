at long last

adverb
finally, after a long wait
2.3
Pronunciation: / ət lɔŋ læst / (AmE), / ət lɒŋ lɑːst / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something has finally happened after a long delay or wait.

Examples

At long last
At long last, the committee approved the new policy after months of debate.
She completed her novel at long last, having worked on it for over a decade.
The train arrived at long last, much to the relief of the tired passengers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a lot of delays
  • synonym: finally - after a long time, typically after delay or difficulty
