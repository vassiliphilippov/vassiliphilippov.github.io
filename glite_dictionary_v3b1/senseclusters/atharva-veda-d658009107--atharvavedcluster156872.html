Atharva-Veda

noun
a Vedic collection of mantras and formulas
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌθɑrvəˈveɪdə / (AmE), / əˌθɑːvəˈveɪdə / (BrE)
one of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of mantras, spells, and incantations used in Vedic rituals

Examples

The Atharva-Veda text
Scholars often compare the Atharva-Veda with the other Vedas to study ancient Indian rituals.
She referenced the Atharva-Veda in her research on early medicinal practices.
The Atharva-Veda contains many hymns and magical incantations used for healing and protection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Rig-Veda - the oldest of the four Vedas, consisting of hymns
  • synonym: Sama-Veda - a Veda consisting largely of hymns for chanting
  • synonym: Yajur-Veda - a Veda made up primarily of prose mantras for ritual
