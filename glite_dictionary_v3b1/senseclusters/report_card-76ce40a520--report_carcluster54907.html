report card

noun
school record of academic performance
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɔrt ˌkɑrd /; / rɪˈpɔrt kɑrd / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːt ˌkɑːd /; / rɪˈpɔːt kɑːd / (BrE)
an official document from a school that shows a student's academic performance or grades

Examples

A report card from school
At the end of each term, students receive a report card from their teachers.
Her report card reflected a marked improvement in mathematics and science this semester.
Parents are often required to sign and return their child's report card to the school.
At the end of each term, every student receives a report card from the school.
Parents usually review their child's report card to discuss progress and set goals.
Sara was delighted when her report card arrived with straight A's.
