birefringent

adjective
having double refraction (splitting light into two rays)
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /baɪ.rɪ.ˈfrɪn.dʒənt/ /; / /baɪ.ə.ˈfrɪn.dʒənt/ (AmE), /baɪ.rɪ.ˈfrɪn.dʒənt/ /; / /baɪ.ə.ˈfrɪn.dʒənt/ (BrE)
(of a material) having the property of splitting a ray of light into two rays, each traveling at a different velocity and in a different direction.

Examples

Birefringent material
Calcite crystals are birefringent and cause incoming light to split into two rays.
Polarized light microscopy is used to identify birefringent substances in biological samples.
The optic fiber was found to be slightly birefringent, affecting the transmission of light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: double refraction - the physical phenomenon whereby a ray of light is split into two rays in a material
