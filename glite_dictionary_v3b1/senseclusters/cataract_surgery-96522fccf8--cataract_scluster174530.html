cataract surgery

noun
surgical removal and replacement of the eye's lens
4.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkæt.ə.rækt ˈsɜːr.dʒər.iː/ /; / /ˈkæt.ə.rækt ˈsɜːr.dʒər.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkæt.ə.rækt ˈsɜː.dʒə.rɪ/ /; / /ˈkæt.ə.rækt ˈsɜː.dʒə.iː/ (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which the cloudy lens of the eye is removed and usually replaced with an artificial intraocular lens to restore vision.

Examples

After her cataract surgery, she was amazed by how clear her vision became.
