Dewar

noun
vacuum flask for very cold liquids
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈduːər, ˈdjuːər / (AmE), / ˈdjuːə / (BrE)
A vacuum flask used to store extremely cold liquids, such as liquid air or helium, commonly used in scientific experiments.

Examples

A Dewar flask
A broken Dewar can cause the liquid helium inside to evaporate rapidly.
The laboratory used a Dewar to safely store the liquid nitrogen overnight.
To maintain the required temperature, the samples were transported in a special Dewar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vacuum flask - a container that reduces heat transfer to keep substances hot or cold
