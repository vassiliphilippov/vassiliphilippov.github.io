adipose tissue

noun
body tissue that stores fat
4.1 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈædɪˌpoʊs ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈadɪpəʊs ˈtɪʃuː / (BrE)
A type of body tissue made up of cells that store fat, serving as an energy reserve and providing cushioning and insulation to vital organs.

Examples

Adipose tissue cells
Adipose tissue accumulates under the skin and around internal organs, helping the body to retain heat.
Obesity is characterized by an excessive amount of adipose tissue in the body.
The surgeon carefully avoided cutting through the adipose tissue during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fatty tissue - another term for adipose tissue
  • antonym: muscle tissue - tissue made up of muscle fibers
