alibi

noun
legal defense of being elsewhere
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælɪbaɪ / (BrE)
(Law) evidence that a person was elsewhere when an alleged act, especially a crime, took place, used as a defense in a court of law.

Examples

A strong alibi
He claimed he had an alibi for the night of the robbery.
The defendant's alibi was corroborated by multiple witnesses.
Without a solid alibi, she became the main suspect in the investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation put forward to defend or justify a fault or offense
