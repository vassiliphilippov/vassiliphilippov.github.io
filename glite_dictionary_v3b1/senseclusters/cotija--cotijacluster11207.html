cotija

noun
Mexican crumbly cheese
6.4
Pronunciation: / koʊˈtiːhə / (AmE), / kəʊˈtiːhə / (BrE)
a firm, crumbly Mexican cheese made from cow's milk, often used as a topping for dishes

Examples

Sprinkle cotija cheese
She sprinkled cotija on her tacos for extra flavor.
The salad was topped with crumbled cotija and fresh cilantro.
Many Mexican dishes use cotija to add a salty, tangy taste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: queso fresco - a fresh, mild Mexican cheese with a crumbly texture
