progenitive

noun
ancestor or parent
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈdʒɛnətɪv / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛnətɪv / (BrE)
an ancestor or parent from whom a person is descended, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

An ancient progenitive
The family honored their ancient progenitive during the annual reunion.
Legends spoke of a noble progenitive who founded the village centuries ago.
They traced their heritage to a progenitive who crossed the sea long ago.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descendant - a person who is descended from a particular ancestor
  • synonym: progenitor - a direct ancestor or originator
