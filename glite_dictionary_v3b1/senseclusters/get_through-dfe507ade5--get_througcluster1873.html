get through

verb
succeed in reaching after obstacles
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ˈθru / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈθruː / (BrE)
to succeed in reaching a place, person, or goal, especially after overcoming difficulties or obstacles

Examples

To get through the crowd
Despite many setbacks, we managed to get through to the final round of the competition.
It took several attempts to get through to customer service on the busy phone line.
She was relieved when her application finally got through the approval process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reach - to arrive at a place or goal; to successfully get to someone or something
