insula

noun
Roman apartment building
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnsjələ / (AmE), / ˈɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
an ancient Roman apartment building, typically several stories high and housing many families

Examples

Roman insula building
Many working-class Romans lived in a crowded insula with shared facilities.
The archaeologists discovered the remains of an insula near the ancient city center.
Fires were common in an insula because of the wooden upper floors and close quarters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: domus - a private house in ancient Rome, usually for the wealthy
  • synonym: tenement - a large building divided into apartments, often for poorer residents
