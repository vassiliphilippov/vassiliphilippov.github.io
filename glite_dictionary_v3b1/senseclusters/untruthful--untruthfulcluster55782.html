untruthful

adjective
not truthful; dishonest
2.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtruθfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtruːθfəl / (BrE)
not telling or tending to tell the truth; not honest or accurate.

Examples

Untruthful answer
It is difficult to trust someone with an untruthful reputation.
She realized her untruthful answer had hurt her friend deeply.
The witness gave an untruthful statement during the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dishonest - not honest; prone to lying, cheating, or stealing
  • antonym: truthful - telling or expressing the truth; honest
