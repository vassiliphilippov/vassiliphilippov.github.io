wickedness

noun
the quality of being evil or morally wrong
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪkɪdnəs /; / ˈwɪkɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally wrong, evil, or sinful

Examples

The wickedness of evil
He tried to hide his wickedness behind a charming smile.
She was drawn to tales of wickedness and redemption.
The villagers were shocked by the wickedness of the crime committed in their midst.
Many legends speak of the wickedness lurking in the shadows of ancient castles.
She was shocked by the wickedness of his actions.
Throughout history, people have struggled to overcome wickedness with goodness and compassion.
He prayed for forgiveness and redemption from his life of wickedness.
Many sermons warn against wickedness as a path leading away from God.
They believed that wickedness brought spiritual separation from the divine.
