letter of mark and reprisal

noun
government license to seize foreign vessels
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtər əv mɑrk ənd rɪˈpraɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə əv mɑːk ənd rɪˈprʌɪzəl / (BrE)
an official license granted by a government to a private citizen, authorizing them to seize vessels and property of a foreign nation, typically in times of war

Examples

A letter of mark and reprisal
During wartime, several captains applied for a letter of mark and reprisal to lawfully target enemy vessels.
The private ship sailed under a letter of mark and reprisal issued by the crown.
With a letter of mark and reprisal, merchants could act as privateers against foreign ships.

Related Terms

  • synonym: letter of marque - license to privateers to capture enemy vessels
