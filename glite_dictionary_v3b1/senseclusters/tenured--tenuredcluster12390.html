tenured

adjective
having permanent employment, especially academic
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛn.jɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɛnjəd / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a teacher or professor) holding a permanent post, especially one from which they cannot be dismissed without cause.

Examples

A tenured professor
He became a tenured professor after years of research and teaching.
Only tenured faculty members can vote in departmental elections.
The university decided not to renew contracts for non-tenured staff.

Related Terms

  • antonym: untenured - not having permanent employment, especially in an academic position
