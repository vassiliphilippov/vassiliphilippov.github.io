hyperinflation

noun
lung overexpansion
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
an abnormal overexpansion of the lungs in which air becomes trapped, making normal breathing less efficient

Examples

Hyperinflation of lungs
The doctor explained that hyperinflation of the lungs can occur in patients with chronic asthma.
Chest X-rays revealed significant hyperinflation after the patient experienced difficulty breathing.
Treatment focused on reducing the hyperinflation to help the patient breathe more easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emphysema - a lung condition involving damaged air sacs and overinflation
