de Gaulle

proper noun
French general and president (1890–1970)
Pronunciation: / də ˈɡoʊl / (AmE), / də ˈɡɔːl / (BrE)
Charles de Gaulle (1890–1970), French general and statesman who led the Free French forces during World War II and later served as President of France.

Examples

De Gaulle leadership
De Gaulle's leadership was crucial in the restoration of French national pride.
During the Second World War, de Gaulle became a symbol of French resistance to Nazi Germany.
Many streets in France are named after de Gaulle to honor his legacy.
