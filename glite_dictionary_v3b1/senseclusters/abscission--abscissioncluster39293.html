abscission

noun
shedding of plant parts
6.9
Pronunciation: / æbˈsɪʃən / (AmE), / æbˈsɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the natural process by which a plant sheds its leaves, flowers, or fruit, usually after forming a scar at the detachment point

Examples

Leaf abscission
After pollination, abscission occurs to separate the mature fruit from the plant.
Researchers studied the chemical signals that trigger abscission in grapevines.
The abscission of leaves is a key part of a tree's annual cycle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shedding - the process of discarding something, especially parts of a plant or an animal's skin
