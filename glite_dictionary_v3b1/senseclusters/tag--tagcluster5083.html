tag

noun
label attached to show information
Pronunciation: / tæɡ / (AmE), / tæɡ / (BrE)
a small piece of paper, card, cloth, metal, or plastic attached to something to give information or identify it

Examples

A tag on luggage
Please write your name on the tag before attaching it to your suitcase.
She forgot to remove the tag from her new shirt.
The price tag showed that the book cost $20.
Every new shirt had a price tag attached to the sleeve.
Make sure to remove the washing instruction tag before wearing the dress.
The luggage tag fell off during the flight.
Each box had a tag with the recipient's name on it.
Please remove the price tag before you gift it.
The luggage tag was missing when my bag arrived at the airport.
