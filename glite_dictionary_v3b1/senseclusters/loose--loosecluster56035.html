loose

adjective
not compact or densely arranged
Pronunciation: / luːs /; / lus / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
not compact or densely packed; having many gaps or spaces between parts

Examples

Loose gravel
Be careful driving on the loose gravel at the edge of the road.
She brushed aside the loose dirt to find the seeds beneath.
The ground was covered in loose sand, making it difficult to walk.
Sand has a loose texture, making it easy for water to pass through.
She prefers towels with a loose weave because they dry quickly.
The sweater is made from a loose knit that allows air to circulate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dense - closely compacted in substance
  • synonym: open - (of texture or weave) having gaps or spaces between parts
  • antonym: tight - (of texture or weave) packed closely together without gaps
