backflip

verb
to reverse decision
6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.flɪp/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.flɪp/ (BrE)
to quickly and publicly reverse a previous decision, opinion, or policy, especially under pressure

Examples

To backflip on policy
After public criticism, the company backflipped on its plan to increase prices.
The politician was accused of having backflipped on several key campaign promises.
Facing strong opposition, the board decided to backflip and restore the original policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: U-turn - a complete change of opinion or policy
  • synonym: reverse - to change to the opposite decision or direction
  • antonym: stand firm - to refuse to change a decision or opinion
