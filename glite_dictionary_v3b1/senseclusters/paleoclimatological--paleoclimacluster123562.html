paleoclimatological

adjective
ancient climate study
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˌoʊklaɪməˈtɑlədʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliəʊˌklaɪməˈtɒlədʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the scientific study of ancient climates and climate changes throughout Earth's history

Examples

Paleoclimatological study
The paleoclimatological evidence suggests that the region was much wetter thousands of years ago.
Scientists use paleoclimatological methods to reconstruct temperature changes over millions of years.
A paleoclimatological approach helps us understand how ecosystems responded to past climate shifts.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: climatological - relating to the study of climate
  • synonym: paleoclimatic - relating to the climate of a past geological age
