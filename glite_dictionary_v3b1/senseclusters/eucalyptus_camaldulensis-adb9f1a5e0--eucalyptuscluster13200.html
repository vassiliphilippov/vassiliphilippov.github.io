Eucalyptus camaldulensis

noun
Australian river red gum tree; source of reddish timber
Pronunciation: / juːˌkɑːlɪptəs kəˌmældəˈlɛnsɪs / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪptəs kəˌmældəˈlɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of red gum tree native to Australia, chiefly growing along rivers, known for its somewhat crooked shape and durable reddish timber used in heavy construction.

Examples

The reddish timber of the Eucalyptus camaldulensis is valued for its strength and durability.
