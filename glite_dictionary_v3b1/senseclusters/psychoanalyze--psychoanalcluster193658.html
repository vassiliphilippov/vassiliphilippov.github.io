psychoanalyze

verb

3.7 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊˈænəˌlaɪz /; / ˌsaɪkoʊəˈnælaɪz / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈænəlaɪz /; / ˌsaɪkəʊəˈnælaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or examine someone using the methods of psychoanalysis, a type of psychological therapy developed by Sigmund Freud

Examples

To psychoanalyze a patient
It can be difficult to psychoanalyze someone without their willingness to participate.
Many novels feature characters who try to psychoanalyze themselves after major events.
The therapist decided to psychoanalyze her patient to uncover hidden traumas.
After her traumatic experience, she decided to psychoanalyse herself with the help of a therapist.
He wondered if being psychoanalysed would help him understand his dreams better.
The doctor had psychoanalysed dozens of patients over his long career.
