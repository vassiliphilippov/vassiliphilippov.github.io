incomplete

noun
unfinished course grade
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəmˈplit / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈpliːt / (BrE)
a grade or status given when required work for a course or assignment has not been finished by the deadline

Examples

A course incomplete
The professor agreed to give her an incomplete so she could finish the final project later.
He was worried about having too many incompletes on his academic record.
If you do not submit your assignments on time, you may receive an incomplete for the course.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fail - a grade indicating that requirements were not met
  • antonym: pass - a grade indicating successful completion of requirements
