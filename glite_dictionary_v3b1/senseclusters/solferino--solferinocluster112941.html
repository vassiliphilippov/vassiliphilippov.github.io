solferino

noun
purplish-pink dye or color
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑlfəˈrinoʊ / (AmE), / ˌsɒlfəˈriːnəʊ / (BrE)
a purplish-pink dye originally discovered in 1859, named after the Battle of Solferino; also, a color resembling this dye

Examples

The color solferino
During the 19th century, dresses in shades of solferino became quite fashionable.
The artist chose a vibrant solferino to add a rich accent to the painting.
The fabric was dyed with solferino, giving it a distinctive pinkish hue.
