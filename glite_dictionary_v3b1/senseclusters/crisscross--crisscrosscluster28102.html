crisscross

verb
to mark with crossing lines
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪsˌkrɔs /; / ˈkrɪs.krɔs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.krɒs / (BrE)
to mark or cover with a pattern of intersecting lines or paths

Examples

To crisscross the page
He likes to crisscross his notebooks with doodles during long meetings.
She crisscrossed the fabric with colorful threads for decoration.
The roads crisscross the countryside, forming a complex network.
Lightning bolts crisscrossed the night sky during the storm.
The old map was crisscrossed with faded travel routes and rivers.
Trails crisscross the park, making it easy to explore every corner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crosshatch - to shade with intersecting sets of parallel lines
