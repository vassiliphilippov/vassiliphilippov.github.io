Araguaia

proper noun
river in central Brazil
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrəˈɡwaɪə / (AmE), / ˌɑːrəˈɡwaɪə / (BrE)
A river in central Brazil that flows generally northward, passing through several states, and joins the Tocantins River.

Examples

Araguaia River
Many tourists enjoy fishing along the Araguaia during the dry season.
The Araguaia River supports diverse wildlife and is important for regional agriculture.
The Araguaia eventually merges with the Tocantins River, forming a major waterway.
