tetrasaccharide

noun
carbohydrate yielding four monosaccharides
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈsækəraɪd / (BrE)
a carbohydrate that yields four monosaccharide units when completely hydrolysed

Examples

A tetrasaccharide sugar
Complete hydrolysis of this tetrasaccharide produced four separate monosaccharides.
Researchers are studying the role of tetrasaccharides in cellular communication.
The scientist identified a new tetrasaccharide in the plant extract.
