ground

verb
fix firmly in place
8.7 2.8
Pronunciation: /graʊnd/ (AmE), /graʊnd/ (BrE)
to place, fix, or make contact with the ground or a surface; to make something stable by putting it on the ground

Examples

To ground heavy equipment
Heavy machinery must be grounded securely to prevent it from tipping over during use.
The metal pole was carefully grounded in the concrete to ensure its stability.
The monument is grounded on a deep foundation to withstand strong winds.
After the long trip, she grounded her heavy suitcase with relief.
The pilot grounded the plane safely after the storm.
We grounded the equipment to prevent it from falling over.
After striking the reef, the fierce waves grounded the ship.
Heavy fog may ground flights until visibility improves.
The captain was forced to ground the plane due to engine trouble.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anchor - to fix something firmly in position
  • synonym: land - to bring an aircraft down to the ground safely
  • antonym: take off - to become airborne; to leave the ground
