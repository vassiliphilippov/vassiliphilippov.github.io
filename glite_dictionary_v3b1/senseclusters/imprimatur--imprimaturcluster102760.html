imprimatur

noun
official approval or authorization
Pronunciation: / ˌɪm.prɪˈmɑː.tər / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prɪˈmɑː.tər / (BrE)
formal and explicit approval, especially an official license to publish or a mark of authority

Examples

The official imprimatur
His work finally received the official imprimatur of the board after several revisions.
Major funding was delayed while waiting for the committee's formal imprimatur.
The new textbook could not be published without the university's imprimatur.
