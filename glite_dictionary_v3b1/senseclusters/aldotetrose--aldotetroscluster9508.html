aldotetrose

noun
four-carbon aldehyde sugar
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌældoʊˈtɛtˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˌaldəʊˈtɛtrəʊz / (BrE)
A monosaccharide (simple sugar) containing four carbon atoms and an aldehyde group, such as threose or erythrose.
