dika bread

noun
paste made from African wild mango seeds
6.5 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdiː.kə bred/ /; / /ˈdɪ.kə bred/ (AmE), /ˈdiː.kə bred/ /; / /ˈdɪ.kə bred/ (BrE)
A somewhat astringent paste made by grinding and heating seeds of the African wild mango, commonly used as a staple food by some African peoples.

Examples

dika bread in soup
In parts of West Africa, dika bread is added to soups as a thickener and flavoring.
She brought home some dika bread after her trip to Cameroon to share with her family.
Villagers prepared dika bread by roasting, grinding, and shaping the mango seeds into a paste.
