afield

adverb
off topic; digressing from the main point
Pronunciation: / əˈfiːld / (AmE), / əˈfiːld / (BrE)
Further from the topic or issue under consideration; off the subject.

Examples

Afield from the topic
Her comments were interesting but somewhat afield from the topic at hand.
If we go too far afield, we might lose track of the main question.
The discussion began to wander afield as speakers raised unrelated issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aside - away from the topic being discussed
  • synonym: digress - to leave the main subject temporarily in speech or writing
