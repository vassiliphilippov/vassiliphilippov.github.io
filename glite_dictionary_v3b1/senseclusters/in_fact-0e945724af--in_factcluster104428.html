in fact

adverb
actually; in reality
1.0 84/m
Pronunciation: / Éªn fÃ¦kt / (AmE), / Éªn fakt / (BrE)
used to emphasize the truth of a statement, especially when it contrasts with or adds to what was previously said

Examples

Used in fact
He looks angry, but in fact he's just tired.
Many thought the plan was impossible, but in fact it succeeded beyond expectations.
She said she was busy; in fact, she had forgotten about the meeting.

Synonyms

  • actually
    1.0 524/m
    actually states that something is really true or surprising, while in fact stresses truth that contrasts or adds information.
  • as a matter of fact
    3.0 10/m
    as a matter of fact introduces a point often to correct or surprise, while in fact emphasizes truth adding or contrasting.
  • in reality
    3.3 17/m
    in reality describes the true situation opposite to appearance, while in fact highlights truth that contrasts or adds information.
  • in point of fact
    5.6 0.24/m
    in point of fact states a factual correction in formal speech, while in fact is more common and can be spoken casually.
  • in truth
    3.8 7.08/m
    in truth states plain accuracy and sounds slightly formal, while in fact appears in everyday speech to add contrast.
  • indeed
    1.0 148/m
    indeed confirms or strengthens a point and often follows or agrees with it, while in fact introduces contrasting or added truth.
  • the fact is
    3.3 7.46/m
    the fact is introduces a statement or correction as a main point, while in fact is a shorter adverbial phrase used within clauses.
