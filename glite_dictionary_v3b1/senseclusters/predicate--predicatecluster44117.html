predicate

verb
state as a condition or basis
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪt / (BrE)
(formal) to state or affirm something as a basis or condition for something else; to assert something as necessary for a consequence.

Examples

To predicate on effort
Success in this field is often predicated on years of hard work and dedication.
The judge’s decision is predicated upon established legal precedent.
Their plan was predicated on the cooperation of all team members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assume - to suppose to be true, especially as a basis for reasoning or action
  • synonym: base - to use something as a foundation or support for something else
