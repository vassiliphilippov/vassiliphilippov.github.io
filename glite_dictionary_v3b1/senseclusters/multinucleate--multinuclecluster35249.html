multinucleate

adjective
having two or more nuclei (biology)
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌl.tiˈnu.kli.ət / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈnjuː.kli.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having two or more nuclei.

Examples

Multinucleate cell
Researchers observed that the amoeba became multinucleate after repeated nuclear divisions without cell division.
Some fungi produce large, multinucleate cells called coenocytes.
The muscle fibers in skeletal tissue are multinucleate due to the fusion of precursor cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uninucleate - having a single nucleus (biology)
