interiorize

verb
incorporate within oneself
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɪriəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriəˌraɪz /; / ɪnˈtɪəriəraɪz / (BrE)
to incorporate (an idea, value, or belief) within oneself and make it subjective or part of one's inner life

Examples

To interiorize values
Children often interiorize the values taught by their parents.
She tried to interiorize the lessons learned from her mistakes.
Through meditation, he gradually interiorized a feeling of inner peace.
As children grow, they often interiorise the values taught by their parents.
She gradually interiorised the principles of the new culture after living abroad for several years.
Therapists help clients interiorise positive self-beliefs to support mental health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internalize - to make something (such as an idea or attitude) part of your own thinking or behavior
