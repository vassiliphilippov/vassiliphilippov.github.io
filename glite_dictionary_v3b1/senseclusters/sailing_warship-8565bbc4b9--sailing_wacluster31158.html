sailing warship

noun
historical warship powered by sails
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪlɪŋ ˈwɔrˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ ˈwɔːʃɪp / (BrE)
A warship powered by sails and equipped with heavy guns, used before the mid-19th century.

Examples

An old sailing warship
He researched the construction of a typical sailing warship of the eighteenth century.
Naval battles were often fought with sailing warships maneuvering for the best position.
The museum displays a restored sailing warship from the Napoleonic era.
