establish

verb
to set up something, especially an organization
Pronunciation: / əˈstæblɪʃ /; / ɪˈstæblɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃ / (BrE)
to start or create something, such as an organization, system, or rule, that is meant to last

Examples

To establish a company
Many universities were established in the nineteenth century.
The committee decided to establish a new set of guidelines for the project.
The company established its headquarters in London in 1998.
The company was established in 1985 and has grown steadily since then.
The treaty helped establish rules for peaceful cooperation between the countries.
They plan to establish a new research center in the city.
Several countries chose to establish a unified currency system.
The company was quick to establish clear guidelines for remote work.
The government plans to establish new regulations to improve public safety.

  • antonym: dissolve - to formally end or break up an organization or institution
  • synonym: found - to establish or originate an institution or organization
  • synonym: institute - to set up, establish, or initiate something, especially a system or process
