pejoratively

adverb
in a derogatory or disapproving manner
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɪˈdʒɔr.ə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / pɪˈdʒɒr.ə.tɪv.li / (BrE)
In a manner that expresses disapproval or criticism; using a derogatory or negative sense.

Examples

To speak pejoratively
He spoke pejoratively about his former colleagues, suggesting they lacked integrity.
She did not intend the word pejoratively, but some listeners took offense.
Using the term 'bureaucrat' pejoratively can undermine respect for public servants.
