state trooper

noun
state police officer (U.S.)
Pronunciation: / steɪt ˈtruːpər / (AmE), / steɪt ˈtruːpə / (BrE)
A police officer who works for a state-level law enforcement agency, especially in the United States.

Examples

State trooper car
A state trooper pulled her over for speeding on the highway.
After the accident, a state trooper arrived to direct traffic.
My cousin has been a state trooper in Texas for over a decade.
