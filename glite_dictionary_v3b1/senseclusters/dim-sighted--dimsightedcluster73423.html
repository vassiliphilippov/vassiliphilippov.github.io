dim-sighted

adjective
having impaired vision
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪmˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪmˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
having greatly reduced or impaired vision; unable to see well or clearly

Examples

A dim-sighted person
After the accident, he became dim-sighted and struggled to recognize faces.
Being dim-sighted, she often relies on a magnifying glass for her work.
The elderly man is dim-sighted and needs help reading the newspaper.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blind - unable to see; completely lacking vision
  • antonym: clear-sighted - having good and clear vision
