census

noun
official population count or survey
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
an official count or survey of a population, typically recording various details of individuals

Examples

A national census
Many policy decisions are based on the most recent census results.
The census showed a significant increase in the city's population over the last decade.
The government conducts a census every ten years to gather up-to-date demographic data.
