deflagration

noun
rapid combustion by heat transfer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛfləˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛfləˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
A rapid combustion process that spreads through a gas or along the surface of an explosive by the transfer of heat.

Examples

Chemical deflagration
A chemical deflagration can cause significant damage if not properly controlled.
Firefighters studied the process of deflagration to improve safety procedures.
The explosion was classified as a deflagration rather than a detonation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: detonation - an even more rapid, shock-driven combustion process
