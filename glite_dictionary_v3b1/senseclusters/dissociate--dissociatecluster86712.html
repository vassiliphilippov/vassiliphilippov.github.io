dissociate

verb
break down chemically into simpler parts
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsoʊʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊʃieɪt / (BrE)
(of a molecule) to break down temporarily or reversibly into simpler molecules, ions, or atoms, often as part of a chemical process.

Examples

To dissociate molecules
At high temperatures, water molecules can dissociate into hydrogen and oxygen.
Many acids dissociate in water to produce positive hydrogen ions.
The compound does not fully dissociate at room temperature, resulting in a weak electrolyte.

Related Terms

  • antonym: associate - to join together or connect in a chemical process
