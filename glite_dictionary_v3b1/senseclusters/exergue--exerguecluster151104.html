exergue

noun
introductory quotation
Pronunciation: / ˈɛɡzɜːrɡ / (AmE), / ˈɛɡzɜːɡ / (BrE)
a short quotation or epigraph placed at the beginning of a book, chapter, or section to suggest its theme

Examples

Exergue of a novel
The author selected a famous line from Shakespeare as the exergue for her new novel.
Each chapter began with a carefully chosen exergue that hinted at the upcoming events.
He spent hours searching for the perfect exergue to set the tone for his essay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epigraph - a short quotation at the beginning of a book or chapter
  • synonym: motto - a short sentence or phrase expressing a guiding principle
