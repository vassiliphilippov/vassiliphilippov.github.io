sour orange

noun
orange tree with bitter or sour fruit
6.5
Pronunciation: / saʊɚ ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / saʊə ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
an orange tree that produces bitter or acidic fruit, commonly used as rootstock in grafting or for its fruit in cooking and marmalade

Examples

Sour orange tree rootstock
Many gardeners use sour orange trees as rootstock for grafting sweet orange varieties.
The marmalade was made from sour orange peel, giving it a tangy taste.
The tart flavor of sour orange juice is often used in marinades for meat dishes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sweet orange - a type of orange tree producing sweet fruit
