grounder

noun
baseball hit along the ground
6.8 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈgraʊn.dər/ (AmE), /ˈgraʊn.də/ (BrE)
In baseball, a hit in which the ball travels along the ground.

Examples

A grounder hit
He hit a slow grounder to third base during the seventh inning.
The batter was disappointed when his powerful swing resulted in just a grounder to the infield.
The shortstop fielded the grounder cleanly and threw to first for the out.
