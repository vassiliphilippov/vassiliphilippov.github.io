anterior fontanelle

noun
soft spot in infant's skull
8.2
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪriər fɑn.təˈnɛl / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə fɒn.təˈnel / (BrE)
The soft membranous gap at the front part of an infant's skull, which later closes as the bones fuse.

Examples

The anterior fontanelle gap
A sunken anterior fontanelle may indicate dehydration in infants.
The anterior fontanelle usually closes by the age of 18 to 24 months.
The pediatrician gently examined the baby's anterior fontanelle during the check-up.
