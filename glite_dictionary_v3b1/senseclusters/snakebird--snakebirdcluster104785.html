snakebird

noun
large fish-eating bird with a long neck
Pronunciation: / ˈsneɪkˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a large fish-eating bird found in warm inland waters, with a long flexible neck and a slender sharp-pointed bill, also known as an anhinga.

Examples

A snakebird diving
During our boat trip, we watched a snakebird dive beneath the surface to catch a fish.
The snakebird glided smoothly across the lake, its long neck creating an elegant silhouette against the water.
With its slender neck raised high, the snakebird looked remarkably like a snake emerging from the water.
