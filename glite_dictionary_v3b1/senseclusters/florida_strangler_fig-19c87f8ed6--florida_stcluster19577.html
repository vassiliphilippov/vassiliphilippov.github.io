Florida strangler fig

noun
tree native to Florida and West Indies; develops thick aerial roots
Pronunciation: / fləˈrɪdə ˈstræŋɡlər fɪɡ / (AmE), / fləˈrɪdə ˈstraŋɡlə fɪɡ / (BrE)
a large tree native to southern Florida and the West Indies, known for starting life as an epiphyte and eventually developing thick aerial roots that can envelop other trees

Examples

A Florida strangler fig tree
A Florida strangler fig can become a dominant presence in the local ecosystem.
Many animals in the Everglades rely on the fruit of the Florida strangler fig for food.
The Florida strangler fig can completely envelop a host tree over many years.

  • synonym: strangler fig - a tree that begins as an epiphyte and envelops other trees with roots
