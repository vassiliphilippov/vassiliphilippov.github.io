luxuriate

verb
to grow or thrive abundantly
7.7 6.1
Pronunciation: / lʌgˈʒʊriˌeɪt / (AmE), / lʌgˈzjʊərɪeɪt / (BrE)
(of plants or living things) to thrive or flourish abundantly; to grow or develop vigorously, often with rich profusion.

Examples

Plants luxuriate in sunlight
Grapevines luxuriate in the fertile soil of the valley each spring.
The city gardens luxuriated after a season of abundant rain and sunshine.
The tropical rainforest allowed rare orchids to luxuriate in the moist, warm air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful; flourish
  • antonym: wither - to shrivel or decline, often due to lack of vitality
