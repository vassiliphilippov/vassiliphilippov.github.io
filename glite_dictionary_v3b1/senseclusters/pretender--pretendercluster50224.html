pretender

noun
someone who feigns beliefs or feelings
1.0 7.1
Pronunciation: / prɪˈtɛndər / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛndə / (BrE)
a person who claims to have beliefs, feelings, or motives that they do not genuinely possess, often to conceal their true intentions

Examples

A pretender friend
Everyone called him a pretender because he never revealed his true intentions.
Politicians are sometimes accused of being pretenders who say one thing and do another.
Though she acted supportive, her colleagues saw through her facade as a pretender.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypocrite - a person who pretends to have moral standards or opinions that they do not actually possess
