heat capacity ratio

noun
ratio of specific heats (C_P/C_V) for a substance
Pronunciation: / hiːt kəˈpæsəti ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ / (AmE), / hiːt kəˈpæsəti ˈreɪʃiəʊ / (BrE)
the ratio of the heat capacity of a substance at constant pressure to its heat capacity at constant volume

Examples

The heat capacity ratio value
Engineers use the heat capacity ratio when analyzing thermodynamic cycles.
Knowing the heat capacity ratio is essential for calculating the speed of sound in a gas.
The heat capacity ratio for an ideal diatomic gas is typically 1.4.
