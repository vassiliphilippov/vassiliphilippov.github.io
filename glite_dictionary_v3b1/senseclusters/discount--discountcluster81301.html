discount

verb
reduce the price of something
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
to reduce the usual price of something; to offer something for sale at a lower price than usual

Examples

To discount old products
Many retailers discount older electronics to clear out inventory before new models arrive.
The store decided to discount its winter coats to attract more customers.
We will discount your meal if you show your student ID.

Related Terms

  • antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, intensity, or degree
  • synonym: reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
