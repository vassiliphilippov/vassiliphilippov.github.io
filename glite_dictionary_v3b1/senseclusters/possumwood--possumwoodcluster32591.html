possumwood

noun
North American tree with astringent fruit
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀês…ômÀåw äd / (AmE), / Ààp…ís…ômÀåw äd / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree of dry woodlands in the southern and eastern United States, bearing yellow or orange astringent fruit that is edible when fully ripe.

Examples

The possumwood tree
Botanists are studying the adaptation strategies of possumwood in dry woodland environments.
Many animals in the region feed on the fallen possumwood during the autumn months.
The fruit of the possumwood is too astringent to eat until it is fully ripe.
