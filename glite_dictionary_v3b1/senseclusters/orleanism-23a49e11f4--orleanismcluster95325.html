Orleanism

noun
the philosophy of the Orleanists
6.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ˈliː.ə.nɪz.əm/ /; / /ɔːr.ˈliː.ə.nɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ɔː.ˈliː.ə.nɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the political philosophy or ideology of the Orleanists, who supported a constitutional monarchy under the Orleans branch of the French royal family in the 19th century.

Examples

Orleanism political ideology
During the July Monarchy, Orleanism became the dominant ideology among moderates.
Many historians debate the influence of Orleanism on European political thought.
The rise of Orleanism in 19th-century France reflected a desire for constitutional monarchy.
