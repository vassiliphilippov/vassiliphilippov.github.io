Synonyms

  • rebellion
    4.1 6/m
    rebellion is large, organized armed resistance aiming to overthrow a government, while revolt often names a single, smaller attempt.
  • insurrection
    9.1 1/m
    insurrection is a sudden violent attempt to overthrow authorities, while revolt often means a specific, possibly smaller act of opposition.
  • uprising
    5.1 7/m
    uprising is a popular, often short-lived rebellion by people against rulers, while revolt can mean one organized attempt.
  • rising
    6.8 0.3/m
    rising is an organized attempt to overthrow authority, often used in history, while revolt is a general act of opposition.
  • revolution
    4.1 40/m
    revolution is a broad, often lasting change in society or government, while revolt refers to an attempt to oppose authority.
  • insurgency
    8.4 3/m
    insurgency is a prolonged armed campaign against a state, often using guerrilla tactics, while revolt is usually a single organized attempt.
  • insurgence
    8.7 0.06/m
    insurgence is an organized rebellion term used less often and sounds formal, while revolt names a general attempt to remove authority.
