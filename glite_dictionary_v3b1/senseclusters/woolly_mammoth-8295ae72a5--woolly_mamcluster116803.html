woolly mammoth

noun
extinct hairy mammal of the Ice Age
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli ˈmæməθ / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈmæməθ / (BrE)
an extinct species of mammoth with a thick coat of hair, common in the cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere during the Ice Age

Examples

The woolly mammoth skeleton
Many paintings in ancient caves depict the woolly mammoth as an important part of early human life.
Scientists have extracted DNA from the remains of a woolly mammoth found frozen in permafrost.
The woolly mammoth once roamed the vast plains of Siberia during the last Ice Age.
