commoditization

noun
becoming interchangeable
8.1 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀåm…ëÀêd…ôta…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / k…ôÀåm…íd…™ta…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the process by which goods, services, or ideas become widely available, interchangeable, and mainly compete on price, often losing unique features or brand distinction

Examples

The commoditization of goods
The commoditization of smartphones has led to lower prices and fewer unique features among brands.
As streaming services increase, the commoditization of digital music has changed how artists earn money.
Many industries fear commoditization because it often results in reduced profit margins.
The commoditization of smartphones has made it difficult for companies to maintain high profit margins.
As streaming services increase, the commoditization of digital music has changed how artists earn revenue.
Rapid commoditization in the software market forced companies to cut prices and reduce profit margins.
The commoditization of healthcare has sparked debates about access and quality.
Rapid commoditization of technology products often leads to lower prices for consumers.
Some critics worry that the commoditization of art reduces its cultural value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercialization - the process of bringing something to market for profit
  • antonym: decommodification - the process of removing something from the market as a commodity
  • antonym: differentiation - the process of making a product distinct from others
  • synonym: standardization - the process of making things conform to a standard
