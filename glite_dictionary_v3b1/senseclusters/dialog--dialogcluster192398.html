dialog

verb
to discuss issues
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəlɒg / (BrE)
to have a conversation or discussion, especially to resolve a problem or reach an agreement

Examples

To dialog for peace
The two companies agreed to dialogue in order to settle their differences.
Community leaders dialogued with residents about the proposed changes to the park.
After months of tension, the countries finally began to dialogue to find a peaceful solution.
The two companies began to dialogue in hopes of finding a solution to their dispute.
Community leaders are dialoguing with residents to address their concerns about safety.
It is important to dialogue openly when trying to resolve misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: argue - to express opposite views in a heated way
  • synonym: converse - to talk with someone in an informal way
  • synonym: debate - to discuss opposing viewpoints formally
  • synonym: negotiate - to discuss something formally to reach an agreement
