myiasis

noun
infestation by fly larvae
Pronunciation: / maɪˈaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
infestation of the body by the larvae of flies, typically through a wound or natural opening, sometimes causing disease

Examples

Myiasis infestation
Certain fly species are responsible for causing myiasis in both humans and animals.
The patient was diagnosed with myiasis after maggots were discovered in his wound.
Veterinarians often monitor livestock for signs of myiasis in warm, humid climates.
