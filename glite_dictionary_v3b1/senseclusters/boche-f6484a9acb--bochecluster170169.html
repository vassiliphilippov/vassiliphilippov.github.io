Boche

noun
offensive term for a German, esp. soldier in WWI or WWII
Pronunciation: / boʊʃ / (AmE), / bɒʃ / (BrE)
An offensive, now archaic term for a person of German descent, especially a German soldier during the First or Second World War.

Examples

The Boche soldier
During the war, propaganda posters frequently depicted the Boche as an inhuman adversary.
The old soldier still spoke bitterly of fighting the Boche in the trenches of France.
Today, using the term Boche is considered both offensive and outdated.
