fuffle

verb
to make untidy
9.9 0.00/m
to disarrange or rumple something, such as hair, clothing, or papers, making it untidy

Examples

To fuffle papers
A sudden breeze fuffled the papers on the desk, scattering them across the floor.
She fuffled her hair with her hands to give it a more relaxed look.
The puppy fuffled the blanket while trying to make a comfortable spot to sleep.

Synonyms

  • dishevel
    8.1 0.15/m
    dishevel makes hair, clothes, or appearance untidy and messy, while fuffle means to rumple or disarrange items.
  • muss
    7.9 0.45/m
    muss makes things broadly messy or untidy by rough handling, while fuffle often means to rumple or disarrange objects.
  • disarrange
    6.4 0.03/m
    disarrange disturbs the order of objects or papers in a clear way, while fuffle suggests rumpling or making things untidy.
  • tousle
    7.6 0.13/m
    tousle makes hair look untidy by rough handling and play, while fuffle can apply to hair, clothes, or papers more generally.
  • ruffle
    4.2 1.03/m
    ruffle disturbs a surface, causing small waves or folds and flutter, while fuffle focuses on rumpling and making things untidy.
