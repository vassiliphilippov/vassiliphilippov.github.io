recessive

noun
genetics: allele expressed only when paired identically
5.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
An allele that only affects the organism's traits if it is paired with an identical allele, not with a dominant allele.

Examples

A recessive allele
A person with one dominant and one recessive for a trait will express the dominant.
Cystic fibrosis is caused by inheriting two recessives, one from each parent.
Some genetic disorders appear only when the recessive is inherited from both parents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dominant - an allele that expresses its trait regardless of the other allele
