underpayment

noun
payment less than required or expected
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the act or amount of paying less money than is required, expected, or owed

Examples

An underpayment of taxes
Any underpayment of taxes can lead to penalties and interest charges.
She was upset to notice the underpayment after checking her pay slip.
The company discovered an underpayment in several employees' salaries.
An underpayment on your tax return may result in a penalty.
She discovered an underpayment in her last month's paycheck and reported it to HR.
The company was fined for the underpayment of wages to its employees.
