active power

noun
net electrical power transferred over time
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪv ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
the portion of electrical power, averaged over a complete cycle of alternating current, that results in a net transfer of energy in one direction; also known as real power.

Examples

High active power
High active power indicates efficient conversion of electrical energy to useful work in a device.
The engineer calculated the active power supplied to the motor to optimize its efficiency.
Utilities often bill customers based on the amount of active power they consume each month.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: apparent power - the total power supplied to a circuit, combining both active and reactive components
  • antonym: reactive power - the portion of power that oscillates between source and load in AC circuits but does not result in net energy transfer
