echinocactus

noun
genus of ribbed, spiny cacti
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɛˌkaɪnoʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌiːkɪnəʊˈkaktəs / (BrE)
A genus of cacti with strongly ribbed, very spiny stems, native to the southwestern United States to Brazil.

Examples

The echinocactus plant
Botanists studied the spines of echinocactus to understand how they protect the plant from herbivores.
She keeps an echinocactus on her windowsill because it requires little care.
The echinocactus is often called a barrel cactus due to its round shape.
