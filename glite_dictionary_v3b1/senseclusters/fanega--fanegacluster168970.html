fanega

noun
historical land measure
Pronunciation: / fəˈneɪɡə / (AmE), / fəˈneɪɡə / (BrE)
a historical unit of land area, especially in Spain and Latin America, equal to the amount that can be sown with a fanega of seed

Examples

A fanega of land
The farmer inherited three fanegas of fertile land from his grandfather.
In colonial times, taxes were often calculated based on the number of fanegas a person owned.
The contract specified that the tenant would cultivate one fanega of wheat each season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acre - a unit of land area used in English-speaking countries
  • synonym: hectare - a metric unit of land area equal to 10,000 square meters
