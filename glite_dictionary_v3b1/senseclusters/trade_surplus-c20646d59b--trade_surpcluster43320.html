trade surplus

noun
excess of exports over imports
4.6 2.9
Pronunciation: /treɪd ˈsɜːr.pləs/ /; / /treɪd ˈsɜːr.plʌs/ (AmE), /treɪd ˈsɜː.pləs/ (BrE)
The amount by which the value of a country's exports exceeds the value of its imports over a specified period.

Examples

A trade surplus grows
A growing trade surplus can signal strong demand for a nation's goods abroad.
The country posted a record trade surplus last quarter, strengthening its currency.
When imports fall and exports rise, a trade surplus is likely to occur.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trade deficit - the amount by which a country's imports exceed its exports
