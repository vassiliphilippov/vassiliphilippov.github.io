factional

adjective
group conflict
Pronunciation: / ˈfækʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈfækʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to conflict, division, or activity among small organized groups within a larger group

Examples

Factional conflict
The organization was weakened by factional disagreements among its leaders.
Years of factional rivalry made it difficult for the club to make decisions.
The country struggled with factional violence after the election.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divisive - causing disagreement or hostility between people
  • antonym: unifying - bringing people together as a single group
