reestimation

noun
revised estimate
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɛstəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɛstəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of estimating something again or making a new or revised estimate

Examples

A reestimation of costs
The budget required a reestimation after unexpected costs were discovered.
Following the new survey, a reestimation of the population was necessary.
The project's timeline changed after a careful reestimation of the required resources.
After receiving new data, the team performed a reestimation of the project's timeline.
A sudden increase in material costs required a reestimation of the budget.
The reestimation showed that the original figures were too optimistic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: estimate - an approximate calculation or judgment
  • degree lower: estimation - the process of forming an approximate judgment or calculation
  • synonym: reevaluation - the act of assessing something again
  • synonym: revision - a change or update to something previously decided
