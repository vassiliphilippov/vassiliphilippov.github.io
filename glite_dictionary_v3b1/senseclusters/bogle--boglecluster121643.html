bogle

noun
folklore ghost or goblin
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɡl / (BrE)
a ghost or goblin, especially in Scottish or northern English folklore

Examples

A scary bogle
The children were afraid to enter the woods at night because of the bogle stories.
Legend says a bogle haunts the old stone bridge near the village.
In Scottish tales, a bogle often appears to travelers lost on lonely roads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bogey - a ghost or evil spirit in folklore
  • synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, often believed to appear to the living
  • synonym: goblin - a mischievous, ugly creature in folklore
