keel

verb
to tip over
5.7 1.31/m
Pronunciation: / kiÀêl / (AmE), / kiÀêl / (BrE)
to tip or fall over, especially suddenly or accidentally, often used of boats or people

Examples

To keel over
The canoe began to keel as the waves grew stronger.
He suddenly keeled over during the long hike in the heat.
If you lean too far, the chair might keel over.

Synonyms

  • keel over
    5.7 0.86/m
    keel over is to fall or collapse suddenly from illness or shock, while keel means to tip or fall, often of boats.
  • tip over
    3.0 1.40/m
    tip over means to be pushed or to fall from an upright position, while keel means to tip or fall suddenly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capsize - to overturn in the water
  • antonym: right - to return to an upright position
