roll

verb
move about aimlessly from place to place
2.6 1.8
Pronunciation: /roʊl/ (AmE), /rəʊl/ (BrE)
to move about aimlessly or from place to place, often in search of food, work, or adventure

Examples

To roll from town

The old musician used to roll along the coast, playing wherever he could find an audience.
With no fixed address, they would roll through small towns, taking any work they could find.
