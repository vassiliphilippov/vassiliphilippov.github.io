blackamoor

noun
archaic term for a person of African descent
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk.əˌmʊr / (AmE), / ˈblæk.əmɔː / (BrE)
(archaic, offensive) A person with dark skin, especially of African descent, formerly used to refer to Black people from Africa or their descendants.

Examples

A blackamoor figure
In old paintings, a blackamoor might be depicted as an attendant in a noble household.
Literary works from the Renaissance sometimes mention a blackamoor as an exotic figure.
The statue featured a blackamoor carrying a tray, reflecting outdated stereotypes.
