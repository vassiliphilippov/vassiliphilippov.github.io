intend

verb
to design or destine for a purpose or future
2.8 34/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆtÉ›nd / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆtÉ›nd / (BrE)
to design or destine someone or something for a particular purpose or future; to have in mind as someone's or something's fate or role

Examples

Intend someone for a job
She was intended to become the director from an early age.
The funds are intended for educational purposes only.
The room was intended for guests but was rarely used.

Synonyms

  • design
    2.8 40/m
    design means to plan something with a clear aim and shape, while intend means to have a purpose in mind.
  • specify
    3.9 12/m
    specify means to state exact details or requirements for a role or thing, while intend means to have a general purpose in mind.
  • destine
    5.7 9.62/m
    destine means to fix someone's future as if by fate or plan, while intend means to plan or hope for someone's role.
  • designate
    5.0 13/m
    designate means to officially choose someone for a job or role, while intend means to have them in mind for that purpose.
