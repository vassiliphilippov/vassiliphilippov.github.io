Laodicean

adjective
indifferent or lukewarm
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪəˈdɪsiən / (AmE), / ˌleɪəˈdɪsiən / (BrE)
showing a lack of enthusiasm or interest, especially in matters of religion or politics

Examples

Laodicean attitude
Her Laodicean response to the debate disappointed those hoping for a strong opinion.
The voters' Laodicean attitude led to low turnout at the election.
He remained Laodicean about the changes, neither supporting nor opposing them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy
  • synonym: lukewarm - not showing much enthusiasm or interest
  • antonym: passionate - showing strong feelings or enthusiasm
