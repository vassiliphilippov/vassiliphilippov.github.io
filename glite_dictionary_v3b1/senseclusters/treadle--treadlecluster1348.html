treadle

verb
press or step repeatedly, especially by foot
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛd.l̩ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛd.l̩ / (BrE)
to press or step repeatedly on something, especially as part of a mechanical process or to shape material by foot

Examples

To treadle the mixture
As part of the process, the worker must treadle over the mixture to remove lumps.
Each morning, she treadled the damp earth, readying it for the bricks to be molded.
The traditional potter treadles the clay to prepare it for shaping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tread - to step or walk on or over something
