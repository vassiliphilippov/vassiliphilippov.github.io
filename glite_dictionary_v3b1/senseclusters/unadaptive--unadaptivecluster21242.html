unadaptive

adjective
unable to adjust
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəˈdæptɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈdæptɪv / (BrE)
not able or not tending to adjust or respond to new conditions or environments

Examples

An unadaptive system
Some traditional business models are unadaptive in the face of rapid technological change.
The animal's unadaptive behavior made it difficult for it to survive in the new habitat.
An unadaptive approach to learning can hinder a student's progress in a changing classroom environment.
The species developed an unadaptive trait that reduced its chances of survival in the new climate.
Some behaviors may be unadaptive when the environment changes rapidly.
Wearing bright colors in the wild can be unadaptive for animals that need to avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adaptive - able to adjust to new conditions or environments
  • synonym: maladaptive - not adjusting well to the environment or situation
