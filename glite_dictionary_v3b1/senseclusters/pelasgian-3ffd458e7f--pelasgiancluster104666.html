Pelasgian

adjective
ancient pre-Greek
Pronunciation: / pəˈlæsdʒiən / (AmE), / pəˈlazdʒiən / (BrE)
relating to the ancient people believed to have lived in Greece before the Greeks

Examples

Pelasgian culture
The Pelasgian language remains a mystery to historians and linguists.
Some ancient ruins are believed to be of Pelasgian origin, predating Greek civilization.
Artifacts with Pelasgian designs have been discovered throughout the Aegean region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Hellenic - relating to ancient Greek culture or people
  • synonym: pre-Greek - existing in Greece before the arrival of the Greeks
