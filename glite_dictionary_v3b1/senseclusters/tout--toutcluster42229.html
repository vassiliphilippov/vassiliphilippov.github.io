tout

verb
promote or show off enthusiastically
4.5 5.0
Pronunciation: /taʊt/ (AmE), /taʊt/ (BrE)
to promote, publicize, or praise something or oneself in a forceful or enthusiastic way

Examples

To tout new products
He loved to tout his recent achievements at every opportunity.
Politicians often tout their accomplishments during election campaigns.
The company continued to tout its new product in every advertisement.
Marketers tout the supplement's health benefits despite limited scientific evidence.
The new book is being touted as a must-read for entrepreneurs.
The startup was touted as the next big thing in the tech industry.
Critics accused the athlete of trying to tout his achievements too much in interviews.
He likes to tout his hometown as a great place to live.
The company hired an agency to tout its innovative new service to consumers.

  • synonym: promote - to support or actively encourage a person or cause
  • antonym: understate - describe or represent as being smaller or less important than it actually is
