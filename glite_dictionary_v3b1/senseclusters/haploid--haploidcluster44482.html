haploid

noun
having a single set of chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˈhæplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhæplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having a single complete set of chromosomes, as found in gametes.

Examples

A haploid gamete
In humans, gametes such as sperm and egg cells are haploid and contain 23 chromosomes each.
Researchers studied the effects of radiation on haploid yeast cells.
The fungus alternates between haploid and diploid stages during its life cycle.

  • antonym: diploid - having two complete sets of chromosomes
