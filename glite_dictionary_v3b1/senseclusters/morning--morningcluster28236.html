The earliest period or beginning of something, especially in a literary or poetic context.

Examples

Morning of a new era
During the morning of the digital age, few could predict its impact.
Legends were born in the morning of the kingdom's history.
The poet spoke of the morning of humanity when hope was fresh.

Synonyms

    9.2 0.58/m
    morn is the poetic word for early hours, while morning is a general term for the day's first period.
  • dawn
    2.7 4.61/m
    dawn often means the start of something new or an era, while morning names the early part of the day.
  • dawn
    1.0 19/m
    dawn is the first light in the sky before sunrise, while morning covers hours after that first light.
  • break of the day
    1.9 0.00/m
    break of the day is a poetic phrase for first light and sunrise, while morning is a broader everyday term for early hours.
  • peep of day
    9.5 0.00/m
    peep of day describes the very first pale light at dawn, while morning refers to the whole early period of daylight.
  • crack of dawn
    3.2 0.64/m
    crack of dawn means a very early moment when light appears, while morning can mean any time before noon.
  • start of the day
    1.4 0.11/m
    start of the day focuses on when daily activity begins for people, while morning names the early time of the day.
