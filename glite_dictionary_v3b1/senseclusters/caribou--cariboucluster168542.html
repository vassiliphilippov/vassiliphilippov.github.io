caribou

noun
North American Arctic deer with large antlers
Pronunciation: / ˈkær.ɪˌbuː / (AmE), / ˈkær.ɪˌbuː / (BrE)
A large Arctic deer of North America, both sexes having large, branching antlers; called 'reindeer' in Eurasia.

Examples

A caribou herd
A herd of caribou migrated across the tundra in search of food.
She spotted a majestic caribou standing near the snowy riverbank.
The indigenous people relied on the caribou for clothing and sustenance.
