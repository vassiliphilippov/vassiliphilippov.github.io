guar

noun
drought-tolerant leguminous plant
9.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / …°w…ëÀêr / (AmE), / …°w…ëÀê / (BrE)
a drought-tolerant leguminous plant grown for its seeds, which yield a gum used as a thickening agent or sizing material

Examples

Guar plant seeds
Guar is cultivated in India and Pakistan for its seeds, which are processed to produce guar gum.
Food manufacturers often use guar gum as a thickener in dairy products and sauces.
The guar plant thrives in arid conditions and is valued for its versatility.

Synonyms
