chorea

noun
nervous disorder with involuntary movements
7.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈriːə /; / kɔˈriə / (AmE), / kɔːˈriːə /; / kɔˈriːə / (BrE)
a medical condition that causes involuntary, rapid, and jerky movements of the body

Examples

Chorea movements
Her sudden, dance-like movements led the neurologist to suspect chorea as the underlying condition.
Huntington's disease is a hereditary form of chorea that often appears in midlife.
The patient was diagnosed with chorea after exhibiting uncontrolled jerky movements of his arms and legs.
Children suffering from rheumatic fever may develop chorea as a complication.
The dog was diagnosed with chorea after displaying persistent jerky movements.
Veterinarians are trained to recognize symptoms of chorea in various animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: athetosis - involuntary writhing movements (a related movement disorder)
  • synonym: ballism - violent, flinging limb movements (a related movement disorder)
