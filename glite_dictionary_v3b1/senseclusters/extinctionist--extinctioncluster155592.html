extinctionist

noun
advocate of extinction
9.8
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪŋkʃənɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates the extinction of a species, group, or population, especially for ecological or ideological reasons

Examples

An extinctionist thinker
The extinctionist movement gained attention after several invasive species threatened local ecosystems.
Some environmental debates include both extinctionist and conservationist viewpoints.
The extinctionist argued that eradicating certain pests would benefit native wildlife.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservationist - a person who works to protect and preserve the environment and wildlife
  • synonym: eradicationist - a person who supports the complete destruction or removal of something
