debouch

verb
emerge from a narrow place into open ground
8.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈbaʊtʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈbaʊtʃ / (BrE)
to come out or emerge from a narrow or confined place into a wider, open area (used for people, troops, rivers, roads, etc.)

Examples

Troops debouch onto plain
As they reached the end of the pass, the troops debouched onto a wide plain.
The hikers debouched from the narrow alley into the bustling square.
The infantry will debouch from the forest just after sunrise.
At the end of the canyon, the trail debouches suddenly onto an open plain.
Several mountain passes debouch into the fertile valley below.
The narrow stream debouches into a wide lagoon just beyond the town.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emerge - to move out of or away from something and become visible
