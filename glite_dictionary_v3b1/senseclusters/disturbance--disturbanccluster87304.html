disturbance

noun
a noisy or disorderly outburst
3.2
Pronunciation: / dəˈstɝbəns /; / dɪˈstɝbəns / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbəns / (BrE)
a noisy or disorderly event, such as a commotion, outburst, or fight, especially in a public place

Examples

A public disturbance
A loud disturbance in the hallway interrupted the meeting.
Neighbors complained about the late-night disturbance caused by the party.
The police arrived quickly to stop the disturbance outside the theater.
A minor disturbance erupted during the concert, but security handled it quickly.
The police were called to break up a disturbance in the city square.
There was a disturbance outside the club that drew a large crowd.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altercation - a heated or angry dispute; a noisy argument
  • synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • synonym: melee - a confused fight or scuffle involving many people
  • antonym: peace - a state of quiet and calm
  • synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or excitement
