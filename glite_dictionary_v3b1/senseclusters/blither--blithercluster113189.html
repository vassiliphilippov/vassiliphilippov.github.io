blither

verb
talk foolishly or nonsensically
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪðər / (AmE), / ˈblɪðə / (BrE)
to talk in a foolish, nonsensical, or meaningless way

Examples

To blither on nonsense
He tends to blither on about trivial matters when he's nervous.
Instead of answering the question, she just blithered for several minutes.
Please stop blithering and focus on the main issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blather - to talk long-windedly without making much sense
