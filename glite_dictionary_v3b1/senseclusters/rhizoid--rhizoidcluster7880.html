rhizoid

noun
root-like filament in lower plants or fungi
8.5 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.zɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.zɔɪd/ (BrE)
A slender, root-like filament found in mosses, ferns, or fungi that anchors the organism and absorbs water and nutrients.

Examples

A rhizoid filament
Each rhizoid serves a similar function to roots in higher plants, but is simpler in structure.
In some fungi, a rhizoid absorbs nutrients from decaying matter.
The rhizoid structures help mosses attach firmly to rocks and soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: root hair - A hair-like outgrowth from a plant root that absorbs water and nutrients
