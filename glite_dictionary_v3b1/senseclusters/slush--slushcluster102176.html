slush

verb
to move in slush
4.4
Pronunciation: / slʌʃ / (AmE), / slʌʃ / (BrE)
to move with difficulty through wet, slushy, or muddy conditions, often making a splashing sound

Examples

To slush through mud
After the storm, we had to slush through ankle-deep puddles of melting snow.
Children slushed across the playground, their boots soaked from the icy mess.
He slushed his way home, leaving wet footprints behind him on the floor.
We had to slush through puddles to reach the car.
After the snowstorm, children slushed across the playground in their boots.
She slushed her way home, her shoes soaked from the melting snow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, often through something difficult
  • synonym: wade - to walk with effort through water or another substance
