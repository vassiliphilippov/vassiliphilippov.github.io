decouple

verb
to disconnect or separate
9.8
Pronunciation: / diˈkʌp.əl / (AmE), / diˈkʌp.əl / (BrE)
to disconnect or separate two or more things that have been joined or linked

Examples

To decouple growth
Efforts are being made to decouple economic growth from environmental degradation.
Engineers need to decouple the modules before performing maintenance.
The company decided to decouple its software from the hardware platform for greater flexibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disconnect - to break the connection of or between things
  • antonym: link - to make a connection or association
