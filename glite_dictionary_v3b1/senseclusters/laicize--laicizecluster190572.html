laicize

verb
to remove clerical or religious status
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.ɪˌsaɪz /; / ˈleɪɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.ɪ.saɪz /; / ˈleɪɪˌsaɪz / (BrE)
to reduce (a person, institution, or practice) to lay or secular status, especially by removing clerical status or religious character

Examples

To laicize a priest
After years in the priesthood, he was officially laicized by the Vatican.
Some governments have chosen to laicize institutions as part of secular reforms.
The church decided to laicize several schools, transferring their administration to secular authorities.
After the reforms, many religious schools were laicised and became state-run institutions.
The bishop explained the process required to laicise a clergyman.
The church decided to laicise several priests following the scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defrock - to remove the rights and status of a priest or minister
  • synonym: laicize - alternate spelling, especially American English
  • synonym: secularize - to make secular or non-religious
