universalism

noun
belief in universal values
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜrsəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that certain ideas, values, or principles are true or applicable to all people everywhere

Examples

Universalism in rights
Many educators support universalism in access to education.
The philosophy of universalism argues that human rights should apply to everyone equally.
Debates about universalism often arise when discussing cultural traditions and global ethics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cosmopolitanism - the idea that all humans belong to a single community
  • antonym: relativism - the belief that truth and values depend on individual or cultural perspectives
