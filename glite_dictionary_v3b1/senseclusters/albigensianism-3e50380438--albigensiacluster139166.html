Albigensianism

noun
medieval Christian dualist movement in France
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.bəˈdʒɛn.si.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌal.bɪˈdʒɛn.sɪ.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
A medieval Christian religious movement, prominent in southern France in the 12th and 13th centuries, which taught a dualistic worldview and was condemned as heretical by the Catholic Church.

Examples

Albigensianism religious movement
Historians study Albigensianism to understand the dynamics of religious conflict in medieval Europe.
Medieval chroniclers wrote extensively about the influence of Albigensianism and its teachings.
The Catholic Church launched a crusade to eliminate Albigensianism from southern France.
