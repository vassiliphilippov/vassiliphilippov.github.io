exceed

verb
be greater or better than a standard
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsid /; / ɪkˈsiːd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to be greater than a particular amount, limit, or standard; to go beyond what is expected or allowed

Examples

To exceed the limit
Sales have exceeded all initial projections this quarter.
The athlete managed to exceed last season's record.
Your dedication and hard work exceed what we typically expect from employees.
Her performance consistently exceeds expectations at work.
The cost of the repairs may exceed your initial budget.
The river levels exceeded the flood warning threshold after heavy rainfall.
Do not exceed the recommended dosage on the label.
She managed to exceed everyone's expectations with her outstanding performance.
The cost of repairs will exceed the original estimate.

  • synonym: outdo - to do better than someone
  • synonym: surpass - to do or be better than someone or something
