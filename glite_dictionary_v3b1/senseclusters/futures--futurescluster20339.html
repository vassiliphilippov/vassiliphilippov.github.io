futures

noun
financial contracts
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfjuː.tʃərz/ (AmE), /ˈfjuː.tʃəz/ (BrE)
contracts to buy or sell assets at a predetermined price on a specified future date, often used in financial markets

Examples

Futures contracts trading
Many investors use futures to hedge against price changes in commodities.
The price of oil futures rose sharply after the announcement of new regulations.
She studied how futures contracts are traded on the stock exchange.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forwards - contracts to buy or sell assets at a future date at an agreed price
  • synonym: options - contracts giving the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell assets at a set price
