receiver-creditor relation

noun
legal duty of receiver toward creditor in bankruptcy
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsiːvər ˈkrɛdɪtər rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə ˈkrɛdɪtə rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the fiduciary relationship in which a receiver or trustee in bankruptcy is responsible for acting in the best interests of the creditor

Examples

The receiver-creditor relation breach
A breach of the receiver-creditor relation can result in legal action against the receiver or trustee.
In court, questions arose regarding the scope of the receiver-creditor relation in this particular insolvency case.
The receiver-creditor relation requires the trustee to prioritize the claims of creditors during bankruptcy proceedings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiduciary duty - legal obligation to act in another's best interest
