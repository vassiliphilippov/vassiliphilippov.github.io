nonequilibrium

noun
unbalanced state
7.9
Pronunciation: /nʌn.ɪ.kwɪ.ˈlɪb.riː.əm/ /; / /nən.ɪ.kwɪ.ˈlɪb.riː.əm/ (AmE), /nʌn.ɪ.kwɪ.ˈlɪb.riː.əm/ /; / /nən.ɪ.kwɪ.ˈlɪb.riː.əm/ (BrE)
a state in which a system is not in balance or stable, often used in science to describe physical or chemical processes

Examples

A nonequilibrium state
The chemical reaction remained in nonequilibrium for several minutes before reaching a stable state.
Scientists study nonequilibrium conditions to understand how systems change over time.
Rapid temperature changes can push a system into nonequilibrium and cause unexpected results.
