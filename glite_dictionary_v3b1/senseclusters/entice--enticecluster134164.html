entice

verb
tempt or persuade someone, often with promises
7.6 4.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtaɪs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtaɪs/ (BrE)
to attract or persuade someone to do something, especially by offering something appealing, often in a deceptive way

Examples

To entice with gifts
She used her charm to entice him into joining the project.
The advertisement tried to entice customers with promises of huge discounts.
The bakery’s smell was enough to entice anyone passing by to stop for a pastry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: repel - drive or force back; discourage someone from taking action
  • synonym: tempt - try to attract or persuade someone to do something wrong or unwise
