tusser

noun
oriental moth producing brown silk
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌsər / (AmE), / ˈtʌsə / (BrE)
An oriental moth (Antheraea mylitta) that produces a brownish variety of silk, commonly known as tussar or tussah silk.

Examples

Tusser moth
Some regions in India rear tusser moths for their unique wild silk.
The tusser is valued for producing strong, brown silk used in traditional fabrics.
Unlike domesticated silkworms, the tusser thrives on natural forest leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tussah - silk produced by wild moths, especially Antheraea species
