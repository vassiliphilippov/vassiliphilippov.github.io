emplace

verb
put or position in place
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈpleɪs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpleɪs /; / ɛmˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put or position something firmly in a particular place (sometimes used for military equipment or artillery)

Examples

To emplace equipment
A memorial stone was emplaced at the entrance to honor the founders.
The engineers emplaced the equipment at the construction site with great care.
They will emplace the new sculpture in the central garden next week.
Engineers worked overnight to properly emplace the new missile launchers.
The artillery unit moved quickly to emplace its guns on the hilltop.
They needed several hours to emplace the heavy cannons before the battle began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deploy - to move troops or equipment into position for action
  • synonym: place - to put something in a particular position
  • antonym: remove - to take something away from its place or position
