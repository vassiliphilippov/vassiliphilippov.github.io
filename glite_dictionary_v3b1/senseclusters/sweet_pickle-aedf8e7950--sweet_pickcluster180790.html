sweet pickle

noun
pickle preserved with sugar and vinegar
Pronunciation: / swit ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / swiːt ˈpɪkəl / (BrE)
a pickle that has been preserved in a mixture of sugar and vinegar, resulting in a sweet and tangy flavor

Examples

A jar of sweet pickle
I bought a jar of sweet pickle relish to serve at the barbecue.
She prefers a sweet pickle to go with her sandwich instead of a sour one.
The recipe calls for chopped sweet pickles to add crunch and flavor to the salad.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dill pickle - a pickle flavored with dill and usually not sweetened
  • degree lower: pickle - a food, usually vegetables, preserved in a solution such as brine or vinegar
