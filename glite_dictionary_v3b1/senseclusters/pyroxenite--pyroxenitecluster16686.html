pyroxenite

noun
igneous rock type
Pronunciation: / paɪˈrɑksəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒksəˌnaɪt / (BrE)
a dark, coarse-grained igneous rock composed mainly of pyroxene minerals

Examples

Dark pyroxenite rock
The geologist collected a sample of pyroxenite from the outcrop.
Layers of pyroxenite can be found in some ancient volcanic regions.
The mining team discovered a large deposit of pyroxenite beneath the surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gabbro - a dark, coarse-grained igneous rock composed mainly of plagioclase and pyroxene
  • synonym: peridotite - a dense, coarse-grained igneous rock containing olivine and pyroxene
