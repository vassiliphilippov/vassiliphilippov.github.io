unsustainability

noun
inability to continue
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnsəˌsteɪnəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnsəˌsteɪnəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being able to be continued or maintained over time

Examples

The unsustainability of practices
The unsustainability of the current fishing practices threatens the future of the industry.
Experts warned about the unsustainability of rapid economic growth without environmental protection.
The project's unsustainability became clear when costs continued to rise each year.
The unsustainability of rapid economic growth became clear after several years of resource depletion.
Experts warn about the unsustainability of relying solely on fossil fuels for energy.
Many environmental problems stem from the unsustainability of current agricultural practices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instability - the state of being unstable or likely to change suddenly
  • antonym: sustainability - the ability to be maintained or continued over time
