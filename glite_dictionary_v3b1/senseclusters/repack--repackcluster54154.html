repack

verb
to package again
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈpæk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpæk / (BrE)
to package digital files, software, or data again, often by bundling, compressing, or converting them into a new format.

Examples

To repack software files
The developer needed to repack the software before distributing it to users.
After making changes to the files, she had to repack them into a new archive.
You should repack the data to ensure it is compatible with the latest system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compress - to reduce the size of files or data
  • antonym: unpack - to extract files from a package or archive
