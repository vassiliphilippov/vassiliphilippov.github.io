bunch

verb
gather into a cluster or group
1.2 2.52/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒntÊƒ / (AmE), / bÊŒntÊƒ / (BrE)
to gather or form into a group or cluster

Examples

To bunch the papers
He bunches the papers neatly before handing them out.
She bunched her scarf around her neck to keep warm.
The children bunched together in the doorway to avoid the rain.
His shirt bunches up around his waist when he sits down.
The hikers bunched together as the fog grew thicker.
The sheep bunched in the corner of the field when the dog approached.
