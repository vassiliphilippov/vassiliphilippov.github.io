pray

verb
to ask or beg earnestly
1.6 8.35/m
Pronunciation: / preÉª / (AmE), / preÉª / (BrE)
to ask someone for something earnestly or humbly; to entreat or beg

Examples

To pray for mercy
I pray you, listen to my explanation before deciding.
She prayed him not to leave at midnight.
You must pray their forgiveness if you hope to make amends.

Synonyms

  • entreat
    8.1 0.73/m
    entreat is to ask someone earnestly and politely in speech or writing, while pray often implies a humble or religious appeal.
  • supplicate
    9.6 0.14/m
    supplicate is to beg humbly, often toward God or an authority, while pray can be everyday asking or a religious plea.
  • beseech
    8.2 1.62/m
    beseech is to ask with strong feeling and urgency, while pray can mean asking earnestly but also private religious speech.
  • implore
    5.3 2.17/m
    implore is to beg desperately for action or help, while pray can be hopeful asking and not always urgent or desperate.
  • beg
    1.0 10/m
    beg is to ask urgently and plainly, often in fear or need, while pray suggests humble asking or a religious act.
  • plead
    3.9 13/m
    plead is to ask earnestly, often in court or emotional appeals, while pray is more humble and can be religious or personal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, not humbly
  • synonym: implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
