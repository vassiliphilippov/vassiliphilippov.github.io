reactionist

adjective
opposed to liberalism or reform
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: / riˈækʃənɪst / (AmE), / riˈækʃənɪst / (BrE)
Opposed to political or social liberalism or reform; favoring a return to a previous state or condition, especially in politics.

Examples

A reactionist view
Her reactionist stance often put her at odds with more progressive members of the council.
Many viewed the policy as reactionist, aiming to reverse years of social reform.
The senator was labeled a reactionist for his opposition to all new civil rights legislation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressive - favoring reform and progress, especially in politics
  • synonym: reactionary - strongly opposed to political or social change
