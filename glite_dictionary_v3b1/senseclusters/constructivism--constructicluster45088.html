constructivism

noun
learning theory
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌktɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
a theory of learning that suggests people build their own understanding and knowledge through experiences and reflection

Examples

Constructivism in education
Many modern classrooms use constructivism to help students learn by doing and reflecting.
The teacher explained that constructivism encourages students to build their own understanding of new topics.
Educational researchers often compare constructivism with other learning theories like behaviorism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behaviorism - a theory that learning is based on observable changes in behavior
  • synonym: experiential learning - learning through direct experience and reflection
