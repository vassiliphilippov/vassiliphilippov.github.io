Balthasar

proper noun
one of the three Magi in Christian tradition
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbælθəˌzɑr / (AmE), / ˈbælθəzɑː / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the three Magi, or wise men from the East, who visited the infant Jesus, traditionally said to be an Arabian scholar who brought the gift of myrrh.

Examples

Balthasar the Magus
Balthasar is traditionally said to have presented myrrh to the infant Jesus.
During the Christmas pageant, Thomas played Balthasar, one of the three wise men.
Many artists have depicted Balthasar in Nativity scenes, sometimes portraying him as an Arabian scholar.

  • synonym: Magi - the three wise men who visited Jesus after his birth
  • synonym: Melchior - another of the three Magi who visited the infant Jesus
