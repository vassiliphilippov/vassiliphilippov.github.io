soundbite

verb
to shorten speech
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊndˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndˌbaɪt / (BrE)
to edit or present speech or an interview as a short, attention-grabbing excerpt, often oversimplifying the original content

Examples

To soundbite a speech
The news channel soundbited the politician's speech, leaving out important context.
Producers often soundbite interviews to fit them into short television segments.
Critics argue that media outlets soundbite complex issues, making them seem simpler than they are.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expand - to make something longer or more detailed
  • synonym: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
