Artemisia maritima

noun
a coastal European aromatic plant (sea wormwood)
Pronunciation: / ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə ˌmær.ɪˈtiː.mə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə ˌmær.ɪˈtiː.mə / (BrE)
a species of aromatic plant, commonly known as sea wormwood, found along western and northern European coasts, with silvery leaves and used historically in medicine and as a flavoring

Along the sandy dunes, patches of Artemisia maritima could be seen thriving in the salty air.

The silvery foliage of Artemisia maritima helps it withstand harsh coastal winds.
