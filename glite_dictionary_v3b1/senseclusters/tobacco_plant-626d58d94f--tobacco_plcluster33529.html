tobacco plant

An annual or perennial plant of the genus Nicotiana, especially Nicotiana tabacum, grown for its aromatic leaves which are dried and processed to make tobacco products.

Examples

A field of tobacco plants
Farmers in this region often grow tobacco plants alongside other crops like corn and soybeans.
Nicotine is a natural chemical found in the leaves of the tobacco plant.
The tobacco plant produces leaves that are harvested, dried, and later processed into cigarettes and cigars.

Synonyms
