callus

verb
to form a hard, thickened area of skin
7.8 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.əs /; / ˈkæləs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əs /; / ˈkæləs / (BrE)
to form or cause to form a thickened and hardened area of skin or tissue due to repeated friction or pressure

Examples

Fingers callus
After weeks of playing guitar, her fingers began to callus.
The gardener’s hands callused after years of outdoor work.
When exposed to constant friction, the heel may callus over time.
Repeated use of hand tools can gradually callus the skin on your palms.
The walking tour callused my heels after only two days.
Years of practicing the guitar have callused his fingertips.
