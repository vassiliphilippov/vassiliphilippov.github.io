induction accelerator

noun
particle accelerator using changing magnetic flux
9.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdʌk.ʃən æks.ˈel.ə.reɪ.tər/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʌk.ʃən ək.ˈsel.ə.reɪ.tər/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʌk.ʃən ɪks.ˈel.ə.reɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdʌk.ʃən ək.ˈsel.ə.reɪ.tə/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʌk.ʃən æks.ˈel.ə.reɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a type of particle accelerator that increases the speed of a continuous beam of electrons using the electric field generated by changing magnetic flux

Examples

Researchers use an induction accelerator to study the properties of plasma under extreme conditions.
The induction accelerator was essential for achieving the high electron energies needed in the experiment.
