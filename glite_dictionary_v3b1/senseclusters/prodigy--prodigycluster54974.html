prodigy

noun
exceptionally gifted young person
6.7 1.98/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêd…ôd íi / (AmE), / Ààpr…íd…™d íi / (BrE)
a young person who has exceptional talent or ability, often in a specific field, and whose achievements arouse admiration

Examples

Child prodigy
Her reputation as a chess prodigy grew quickly after she won several national tournaments.
Mozart was considered a musical prodigy as a child, composing his first pieces at an early age.
The young math prodigy solved problems that left even the professors amazed.

Synonyms

  • wunderkind
    8.9 0.42/m
    a wunderkind is a young person praised for early success in one field, while a prodigy is admired for rare natural talent.
  • phenom
    8.2 0.60/m
    a phenom is a casual word for an outstanding young talent often in sport or music, while a prodigy is more formal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual or creative power
  • antonym: ordinary - a person with abilities or qualities that are not remarkable
  • synonym: wonder - a person who is admired for their talent or skill, especially at a young age
