kinescope

noun
early TV recording
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪnəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈkɪnɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
a recording of a television program made by filming the image from a video monitor, especially before videotape was common

Examples

Kinescope recording
Many classic television shows exist today only as kinescope recordings.
The museum displayed a rare kinescope of a 1950s live broadcast.
Before videotape, networks used kinescopes to preserve their programs for later viewing.
