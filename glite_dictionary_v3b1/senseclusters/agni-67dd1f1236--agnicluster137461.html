Agni

proper noun
Hindu god of fire
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːg.niː/ /; / /ˈæg.niː/ (AmE), /ˈɑːg.niː/ /; / /ˈæg.niː/ (BrE)
In Hinduism, the god of fire and one of the principal deities in the Vedic tradition.

Examples

Agni god of fire
In the Vedic hymns, Agni is often invoked as the messenger between gods and humans.
Many Hindu rituals include offerings to Agni as part of the ceremony.
The importance of Agni is evident in ancient Sanskrit texts and modern Hindu worship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Indra - Hindu god of rain and thunderstorms, chief of the gods in the Veda
  • synonym: Vishnu - A principal Hindu deity, preserver in the Trimurti
