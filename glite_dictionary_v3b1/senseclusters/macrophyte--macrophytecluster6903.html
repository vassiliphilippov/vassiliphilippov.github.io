macrophyte

noun
large aquatic plant
Pronunciation: / ˈmæk.rəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæk.rəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
a large aquatic plant that can be seen without a microscope, commonly found in lakes, rivers, or wetlands

Examples

Large macrophyte plants
The scientist collected samples of macrophyte species from the river for her research.
Healthy macrophyte populations can improve water quality in lakes and ponds.
Many fish use macrophyte beds as shelter and breeding grounds.
A dense growth of macrophytes can provide shelter for fish in the lake.
Scientists studied the impact of macrophyte removal on water quality in the wetland.
The riverbank was lined with tall macrophytes swaying gently in the current.

Related Terms

  • antonym: algae - simple aquatic plants, often microscopic
  • synonym: aquatic plant - a plant that grows in water
  • synonym: hydrophyte - a plant adapted to living in water
