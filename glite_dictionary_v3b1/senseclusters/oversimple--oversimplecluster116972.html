oversimple

adjective
too simplistic
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsɪmpəl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɪmpəl / (BrE)
too simple, lacking the necessary detail or complexity to be accurate or complete

Examples

An oversimple explanation
The report was criticized for its oversimple analysis of a very complicated issue.
Her oversimple answer ignored the many factors involved in the decision.
Relying on an oversimple solution can lead to unexpected problems later.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detailed - including many details or facts
  • synonym: simplistic - treating complex issues as if they are much simpler than they really are
