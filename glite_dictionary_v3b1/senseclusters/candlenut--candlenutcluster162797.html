candlenut

noun
tree with oily nuts used for candles and varnish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkændlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌnʌt / (BrE)
A large tree native to Southeast Asia, whose nuts yield oil used in varnishes, and whose nut kernels are sometimes strung together and used as candles.

Examples

Tall candlenut tree
Locals would string candlenuts together and burn them like a primitive candle.
The candlenut tree is cultivated in many tropical regions for its useful oil.
The varnish was made from the oil extracted from the candlenut.
