hard

adjective
(phonetics) velarized speech sound
Pronunciation: / hɑrd / (AmE), / hɑːd / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with the back of the tongue raised toward or touching the velum, as in certain languages' hard consonants.

Examples

Hard consonant
Children learning Russian must master both hard and soft variants of some consonants.
In phonetics, a hard consonant is often velarized or non-palatalized.
Russian distinguishes between hard and soft consonants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft - (in phonetics) produced with the tongue closer to the hard palate; palatalized
