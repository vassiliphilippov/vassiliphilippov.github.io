contrary

adjective
opposed; completely different
4.5 17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀênÀåtr…õri / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.tr…ôri / (BrE)
opposed in nature, character, or purpose; completely different or inconsistent with something else

Examples

Contrary opinion
Despite the evidence, she made a decision contrary to the facts.
His beliefs are contrary to accepted scientific understanding.
The new policy is contrary to our company's core values.

Synonyms

  • opposite
    1.0 20/m
    opposite shows a direct and exact difference in quality or direction, while contrary means inconsistent or opposed in nature or purpose.
  • antithetical
    8.4 1.91/m
    antithetical presents a strong, formal opposition of ideas or goals, while contrary describes things that simply do not match.
  • opposed
    3.5 0.00/m
    opposed is used for people or groups actively against something, while contrary often describes ideas or characteristics that differ.
  • contradictory
    6.1 9.31/m
    contradictory shows two statements or facts that cannot both be true, while contrary covers wider differences in nature or purpose.
  • conflicting
    4.2 0.85/m
    conflicting describes active disagreement or clash between facts or interests, while contrary describes things that are simply different or inconsistent.
  • reverse
    4.2 5.55/m
    reverse means a complete turn or the exact opposite outcome, while contrary refers to being inconsistent in character or purpose.
