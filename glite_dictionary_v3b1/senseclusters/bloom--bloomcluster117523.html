bloom

verb
produce or yield flowers
2.3 7.47/m
Pronunciation: / bluÀêm / (AmE), / bluÀêm / (BrE)
to produce flowers, or to thrive and flourish (for plants, people, or things)

Examples

To bloom in spring
Each spring, the apple orchards bloom in a spectacular display of white and pink.
The lotus flowers in the pond typically bloom after the rainy season.
When the magnolias bloom, the whole park fills with a sweet fragrance.
After months of hard work, her business finally began to bloom.
His confidence seemed to bloom after he received the award.
The roses in the garden bloom every spring and fill the air with sweet fragrance.

Synonyms

  • flower
    1.0 5.34/m
    flower means to produce or be covered in flowers for a plant, while bloom means to produce flowers or to thrive.
  • blossom
    1.4 0.99/m
    blossom means a plant produces visible flowers, while bloom can mean both producing flowers and growing or thriving.
  • blossom forth
    4.6 0.02/m
    blossom forth means to emerge or grow in a clear, positive way, while bloom is used for producing flowers or thriving.
  • effloresce
    9.9 0.00/m
    effloresce is a rare, formal word for coming into flower or developing, while bloom is common and used for plants or people.
  • flourish
    4.1 11/m
    flourish means to grow strong and successful in health or work, while bloom focuses more on producing flowers or simply thriving.
  • thrive
    3.2 17/m
    thrive means to grow, develop, and succeed well in life or work, while bloom usually means to produce flowers or to flourish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blossom - to produce flowers; another word describing the act of flowering
  • antonym: wither - (of a plant) to become dry and shriveled; lose freshness or vitality
