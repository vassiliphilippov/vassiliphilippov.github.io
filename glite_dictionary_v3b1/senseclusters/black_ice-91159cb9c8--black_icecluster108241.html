black ice

noun
thin, nearly invisible hazardous ice on pavement
6.9
Pronunciation: / blæk aɪs / (AmE), / blæk aɪs / (BrE)
A thin, nearly invisible layer of ice on roads or sidewalks, formed by freezing moisture, and posing a dangerous hazard for vehicles and pedestrians.

Examples

Black ice on roads
Driving became perilous when black ice formed on the highway overnight.
Pedestrians should be careful, as black ice can be impossible to see on the sidewalk.
The weather report warned of black ice during the early morning hours.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sheet ice - a continuous flat expanse of ice
