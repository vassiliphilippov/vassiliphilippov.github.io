overexpression

noun
excessive gene production
7.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪk.ˈspreʃ.ən/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪk.ˈspreʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the production of an abnormally high amount of a specific protein or gene product in a cell or organism

Examples

Gene overexpression
The overexpression of certain genes is linked to the development of some cancers.
Researchers observed overexpression of the protein in the treated cells compared to the control group.
Genetic engineering can result in the overexpression of beneficial enzymes in crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: upregulation - an increase in the production or activity of a gene or protein
