cholecystokinin

noun
hormone aiding digestion
7.3
Pronunciation: / koʊlɪˌsɪstəˈkaɪnɪn / (AmE), / kəʊlɪˌsɪstəˈkaɪnɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced in the small intestine that stimulates the release of digestive enzymes from the pancreas and the contraction of the gall bladder

Examples

Cholecystokinin hormone release
High levels of cholecystokinin stimulate the gall bladder to contract and release bile.
Researchers are studying how cholecystokinin affects appetite and digestion.
The release of cholecystokinin is triggered when fats enter the small intestine.
