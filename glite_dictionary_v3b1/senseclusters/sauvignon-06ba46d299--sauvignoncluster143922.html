Sauvignon

noun
white wine grape
6.8
Pronunciation: / soʊˈviːnjən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊviːnjɒ̃ / (BrE)
a variety of grape, especially Sauvignon Blanc, used in viticulture to produce white wine

Examples

Sauvignon grapes
The vineyard planted rows of Sauvignon to produce crisp, aromatic white wines.
Experts say that Sauvignon grapes thrive in cooler climates with well-drained soil.
She prefers wines made from Sauvignon because of their fresh and fruity flavors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Chardonnay - a popular white grape variety used in winemaking
  • synonym: Sauvignon Blanc - a white grape variety and the wine made from it
