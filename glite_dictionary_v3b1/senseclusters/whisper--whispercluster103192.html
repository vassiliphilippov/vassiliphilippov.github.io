whisper

verb
speak very softly
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪspɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪspə / (BrE)
to speak very softly, using breath rather than the vocal cords, especially so that only one person can hear you

Examples

To whisper a secret
He whispered his answer so quietly that only his friend could hear.
If you whisper during the movie, others may not notice.
She leaned over to whisper a secret in my ear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: murmur - to speak softly and indistinctly
  • antonym: shout - to speak very loudly
