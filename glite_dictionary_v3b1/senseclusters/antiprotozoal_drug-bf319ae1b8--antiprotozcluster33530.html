antiprotozoal drug

noun
drug treating protozoal diseases
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌproʊtəˈzoʊəl drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌprəʊtəˈzəʊəl drʌɡ / (BrE)
A medicinal drug used to treat or prevent diseases caused by protozoa, such as malaria.

Examples

An antiprotozoal drug treatment
Certain side effects are common when taking a powerful antiprotozoal drug.
Doctors may prescribe an antiprotozoal drug to treat intestinal infections caused by protozoa.
The development of a new antiprotozoal drug offers hope in the fight against malaria.
