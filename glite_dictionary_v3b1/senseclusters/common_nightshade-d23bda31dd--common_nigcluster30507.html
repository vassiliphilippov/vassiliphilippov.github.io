common nightshade

noun
a Eurasian plant with poisonous leaves and black berries
7.0 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.mən ˈnaɪt.ʃeɪd/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.mən ˈnaɪt.ʃeɪd/ (BrE)
A Eurasian plant (Solanum nigrum) with white flowers, hairy poisonous leaves, and black berries that can be poisonous or edible.

Examples

Common nightshade plant
Common nightshade often grows as a weed in fields and gardens.
Children should be warned not to eat the berries of the common nightshade plant.
Some varieties of common nightshade have berries that are safe to eat when fully ripe, while others are toxic.
