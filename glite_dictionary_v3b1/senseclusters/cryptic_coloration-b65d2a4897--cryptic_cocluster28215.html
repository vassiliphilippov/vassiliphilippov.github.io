cryptic coloration

noun
camouflage coloring in animals
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪptɪk ˌkʌlɚˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptɪk ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the natural coloring or pattern of an animal that helps it to blend in with its surroundings and avoid detection

Examples

Cryptic coloration in animals
Many insects rely on cryptic coloration to remain unnoticed on tree bark or leaves.
The arctic hare has white cryptic coloration during winter to match the snow.
The chameleon's cryptic coloration helps it blend into leafy branches to avoid predators.
