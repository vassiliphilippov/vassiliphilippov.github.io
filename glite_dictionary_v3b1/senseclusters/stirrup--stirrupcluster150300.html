stirrup

noun
metal foot support on a saddle
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪr.əp / (AmE), / ˈstɪr.əp / (BrE)
a pair of metal loops attached to the sides of a saddle, supporting the rider's feet and aiding in mounting and balance while riding a horse

Examples

Horse stirrup
A broken stirrup can make riding dangerous for even the most experienced riders.
She placed her feet firmly in the stirrups before guiding the horse forward.
The blacksmith was asked to repair the damaged stirrup after the long journey.
