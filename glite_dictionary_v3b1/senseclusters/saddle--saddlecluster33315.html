saddle

noun
a cut of meat from the back and loins of an animal
Pronunciation: / ˈsæd(ə)l / (AmE), / ˈsæd(ə)l / (BrE)
A cut of meat, especially from mutton or lamb, that includes part of the backbone and both loins, typically roasted as a joint.

Examples

Saddle of lamb
For the holiday feast, she cooked a saddle of venison seasoned with rosemary and thyme.
He ordered the saddle of mutton, which was presented as a large, juicy cut on the plate.
The chef prepared a saddle of lamb for the main course, perfectly roasted and tender.
