warble fly

noun
insect fly whose larvae infect cattle skin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrbəl flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːbəl flaɪ / (BrE)
a hairy, bee-like fly whose larvae form lumpy swellings (warbles) beneath the skin of cattle

Examples

Warble fly larvae
Warble fly outbreaks can cause significant distress and weight loss in livestock.
Farmers took measures to control warble fly infestations in their cattle.
The veterinarian diagnosed the bumps on the cow as being caused by warble fly larvae.

Related Terms

  • synonym: botfly - fly whose larvae are internal parasites of mammals
