obstruction of justice

noun
crime of hindering the legal process
5.7 6.8
Pronunciation: / əbˈstrʌkʃən əv ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkʃən əv ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
The crime of deliberately interfering with the administration of law by impeding prosecutors, witnesses, or investigations, often by influencing or intimidating jurors, witnesses, or officers of the court.

Examples

Obstruction of justice charge
He was charged with obstruction of justice for attempting to destroy evidence.
Tampering with a witness can constitute obstruction of justice under federal law.
The jury found her guilty of obstruction of justice after she lied to investigators.
