nestful

noun
eggs or chicks in nest
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛstfʊl / (AmE), / ˈnɛstfʊl / (BrE)
the number of eggs or young birds contained in a single nest

Examples

A nestful of eggs
The bluebird returned each morning to feed her nestful of hungry chicks.
We watched as a nestful of eggs slowly hatched over several days.
A single nestful can contain up to six baby robins in the spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brood - a group of young birds hatched at the same time by the same mother
  • synonym: clutch - a group of eggs laid by birds at one time
