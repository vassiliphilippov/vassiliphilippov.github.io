cryptomonad

noun
microscopic algae found in water
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪptoʊˈmoʊnæd / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈməʊnæd / (BrE)
a microscopic, single-celled algae of the phylum Cryptophyta, commonly found in fresh and salt water, often appearing as algal blooms

Examples

A cryptomonad algae
Researchers observed an increase in cryptomonads during the summer algal bloom in the lake.
Some species of cryptomonad can serve as an important food source for microscopic animals in aquatic environments.
The shoreline was tinged green due to a proliferation of cryptomonads after the storm.
