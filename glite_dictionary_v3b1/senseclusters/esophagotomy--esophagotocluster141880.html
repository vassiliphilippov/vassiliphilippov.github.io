esophagotomy

noun
surgical esophagus incision
Pronunciation: / ɪˌsɑfəˈɡɑtəmi / (AmE), / ɪˌsɒfəˈɡɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an incision is made into the esophagus

Examples

An esophagotomy surgery
The surgeon performed an esophagotomy to access the blocked area.
After the esophagotomy, the patient was monitored closely for complications.
An esophagotomy may be necessary if a foreign object is lodged in the esophagus.
The surgeon performed an esophagotomy to remove the foreign object lodged in the patient's throat.
After the esophagotomy, the medical team carefully monitored the patient for signs of infection.
An esophagotomy may be necessary when less invasive procedures cannot resolve the blockage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: esophagectomy - surgical removal of all or part of the esophagus
  • synonym: gastrotomy - surgical incision into the stomach
