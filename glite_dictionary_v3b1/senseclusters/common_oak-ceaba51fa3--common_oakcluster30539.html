common oak

noun
deciduous European oak tree
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən oʊk / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən əʊk / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous European oak tree (Quercus robur) with smooth leaves having rounded lobes, valued for its strong, light-colored wood.

Examples

The common oak tree
Many historic buildings in Europe are built with timber from the common oak.
The ancient common oak in the park is thought to be over 400 years old.
The leaves of the common oak have distinct rounded lobes, making it easy to recognize.

Related Terms

  • synonym: English oak - another common name for Quercus robur
  • synonym: oak - a tree or shrub in the genus Quercus
  • degree lower: sessile oak - a related European species, Quercus petraea
