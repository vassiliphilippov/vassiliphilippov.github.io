trend extrapolation

noun
forecasting using statistical projection of trends
Pronunciation: / trɛnd ˌɛkstrəˌpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / trɛnd ˌɛkstrəˌpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A forecasting technique that uses statistical methods to predict future values based on patterns in existing time series data.

Examples

Trend extrapolation method
Economists often rely on trend extrapolation when historical data indicate a consistent growth pattern.
Reliable trend extrapolation can help organizations make informed strategic decisions.
The company used trend extrapolation to forecast next year's sales figures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interpolation - estimating values within the range of data
  • synonym: trend analysis - process of identifying patterns in data over time
