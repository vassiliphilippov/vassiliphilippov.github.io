relist

verb
to list again
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈlɪst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlɪst / (BrE)
to place a company or its shares back on a stock exchange after they were removed or delisted

Examples

To relist shares
After meeting all regulatory requirements, the company decided to relist its shares on the main stock exchange.
The board approved the plan to relist following a period of restructuring.
Investors welcomed the news that the firm would relist after several years of absence from the market.
After the merger, the company plans to relist its shares on the main stock exchange.
The asset was relisted following a period of suspension from trading.
Investors were optimistic when the firm decided to relist after restructuring its finances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delist - to remove a company or its shares from a stock exchange
  • synonym: list - to place a company or its shares on a stock exchange for trading
