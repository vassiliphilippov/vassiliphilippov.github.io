phenomenalist

noun
believer in phenomena
9.9
Pronunciation: /fə.ˈnɑː.mə.nɪst/ /; / /fə.ˈnɑː.mə.nəl.ɪst/ (AmE), /fə.ˈnɒm.ə.nɪst/ /; / /fə.ˈnɒm.ə.nəl.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes that only what can be perceived or experienced is real or knowable

Examples

The phenomenalist philosopher
The phenomenalist maintained that we cannot know anything beyond our own experiences.
In the debate, the phenomenalist challenged the idea of an objective reality.
Many philosophers have discussed the arguments put forward by a phenomenalist.
The phenomenalist maintained that objects have no existence outside of our perception.
In philosophy class, we debated the arguments of a phenomenalist versus those of a realist.
A committed phenomenalist questions whether the world exists beyond our senses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empiricist - a person who believes that knowledge comes from experience
  • synonym: idealist - a person who believes reality is fundamentally mental or spiritual
  • antonym: realist - a person who believes that reality exists independently of perception
