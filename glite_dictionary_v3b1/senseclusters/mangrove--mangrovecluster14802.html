mangrove

noun
tropical tree or shrub in coastal areas
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋˌɡroʊv / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡrəʊv / (BrE)
A tropical tree or shrub with prop roots that grows in coastal saline or brackish water, and is important in land building and shoreline protection.

Examples

A mangrove tree
Researchers are studying how mangroves help protect coastlines from erosion.
The mangrove forests provide a crucial habitat for many marine animals.
We spotted a heron perched among the tangled roots of a mangrove.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swamp - an area of low-lying, uncultivated ground where water collects; a marsh or bog
