serous membrane

noun
membrane lining body cavities
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪr.əs ˈmɛm.breɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.rəs ˈmem.breɪn / (BrE)
a thin membrane that lines certain closed cavities of the body, consisting of two layers with a fluid-filled space between them

Examples

The serous membrane layers
Each lung is enclosed by a serous membrane called the pleura.
Inflammation of a serous membrane can lead to painful conditions such as peritonitis.
The serous membrane secretes a lubricating fluid that reduces friction between organs.
