fund

noun
supply kept for future use
1.6 16/m
Pronunciation: / fÊŒnd / (AmE), / fÊŒnd / (BrE)
a supply or reserve of something available for future use

Examples

A fund of knowledge
She kept a fund of patience for dealing with difficult customers.
The library has a fund of classic novels in its archives.
We should always maintain a fund of emergency supplies just in case.

Synonyms

  • reserve
    3.5 18/m
    a reserve is an amount kept back for a special purpose, while a fund is a general supply saved for future use.
  • store
    1.0 7.33/m
    a store is a kept supply ready for later use, while a fund is often a more general reserve for many needs.
  • stockpile
    4.2 2.91/m
    a stockpile is a very large kept supply for emergencies or war, while a fund is any reserve for future use.
  • stock
    2.2 19/m
    stock refers to goods kept for sale or later use, while a fund is a general supply or reserve for future needs.
  • supply
    1.0 25/m
    a supply is an amount available to use now or soon, while a fund is a reserve kept for future use.
  • reservoir
    4.7 1.59/m
    a reservoir is a large source often pictured as a store of water or ideas, while a fund is a kept reserve.
  • resource
    2.0 176/m
    a resource is something people can use when needed, while a fund is a specific reserve kept for future use.
