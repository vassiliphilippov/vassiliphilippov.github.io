archeological remains

noun
excavated relics from the past
2.1 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrkɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl rɪˈmeɪnz / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl rɪˈmeɪnz / (BrE)
Objects or relics from past human activity that have been excavated from the ground, typically studied by archaeologists.

Examples

Ancient archeological remains

Researchers studied the archeological remains to learn more about ancient settlement patterns.
The site yielded several important archeological remains dating back to the Bronze Age.
