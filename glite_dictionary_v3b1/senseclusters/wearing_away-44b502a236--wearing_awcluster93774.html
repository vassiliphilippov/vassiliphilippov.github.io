wearing away

noun
gradual erosion or grinding down
5.7 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈwer.ɪŋ ə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /ˈweə.rɪŋ ə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
The process of gradually eroding or grinding down a surface, especially by the continuous action of physical forces such as water, wind, or friction.

Examples

The wearing away of rocks
The wearing away of the coastline has been accelerated by strong ocean currents.
Years of use caused the wearing away of the marble steps.
You could see the wearing away of her patience as the day dragged on.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erosion - the process by which the surface of something is gradually destroyed through wind, water, or other natural agents
