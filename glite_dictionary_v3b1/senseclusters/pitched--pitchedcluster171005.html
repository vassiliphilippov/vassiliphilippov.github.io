pitched

adjective
set at a slant or angle
4.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / pÉªtÊƒt / (AmE), / pÉªtÊƒt / (BrE)
(of a roof or surface) set at a slant or angle rather than flat or level.

Examples

Pitched roof
A pitched driveway allows rainwater to drain easily.
The house had a traditional pitched roof covered with red tiles.
You must install special gutters for a pitched roof.

Synonyms

  • sloping
    3.1 0.01/m
    sloping describes a surface that rises or falls gently over length, while pitched refers to a roof set at a designed angle.
  • slanted
    3.9 0.01/m
    slanted means leaning at an oblique angle and often looks uneven, while pitched describes a roof built with a steady slope.
  • inclined
    3.9 0.72/m
    inclined describes something that forms an angle and is a bit formal, while pitched is common for roofs in everyday speech.
  • tilted
    2.5 0.03/m
    tilted means moved or leaning away from upright, often temporarily, while pitched describes a fixed sloping roof or surface.
