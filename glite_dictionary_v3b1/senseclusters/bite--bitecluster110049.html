bite

verb
(of wind or cold) cause stinging pain
3.9 2.6
Pronunciation: /baɪt/ (AmE), /baɪt/ (BrE)
to cause a sharp or stinging pain or discomfort (as with cold, wind, or a sharp object)

Examples

Cold bites skin
She shivered as the chill morning air began to bite.
The icy wind seemed to bite into his cheeks as he walked.
The sun can bite your skin if you stay outside too long.
As the ice bit into his skin, he pulled his hand away quickly.
The sculptor watched as the chisel bit deeper into the marble.
The sharp blade bit into the wood, leaving a deep groove.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn - to cause a stinging or intense pain (as with sun or wind)
  • synonym: cut - to penetrate or divide with a sharp instrument
  • synonym: pierce - to penetrate into or through something sharply
  • synonym: sting - to cause a sharp, tingling pain
