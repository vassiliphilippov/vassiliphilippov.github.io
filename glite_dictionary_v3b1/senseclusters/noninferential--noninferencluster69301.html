noninferential

adjective
not based on inference
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
not involving inference or reasoning; accepted or described directly, without drawing conclusions

Examples

Noninferential belief
Some philosophers argue that certain beliefs are noninferential and do not require justification from other beliefs.
Her trust in her friend was noninferential, coming naturally without any need for proof.
The report distinguishes between noninferential knowledge and knowledge gained through reasoning.
The study used noninferential methods to summarize the survey results without making predictions.
In the first section, only noninferential statistics were reported to describe the sample characteristics.
A noninferential approach focuses on what the data show, not on what might be true for a larger group.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descriptive - providing details or information about what is observed
  • synonym: immediate - direct, without any intervening process
  • antonym: inferential - based on inference or reasoning from evidence
