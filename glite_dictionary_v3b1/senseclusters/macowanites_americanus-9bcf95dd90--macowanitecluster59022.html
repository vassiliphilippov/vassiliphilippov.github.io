Macowanites americanus

noun
a small fungus species with a fragile, cracking cap
Pronunciation: / ˌmæk.əˈwaʊ.naɪtiz əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌmak.əˈwaʊ.nɪ.tiːz əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A small species of fungus with a fragile, cracking cap revealing white tissue beneath, and a white stem largely enclosed by the cap.

Examples

Macowanites americanus fungus
Field guides recommend searching for Macowanites americanus in wooded areas during late summer.
Mycologists carefully studied the almost enclosed stem of Macowanites americanus to distinguish it from similar fungi.
The fragile cap of Macowanites americanus often cracks, revealing the white tissue beneath.
