Black Sea

proper noun
large inland sea between Europe and Asia
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk si / (AmE), / ˈblæk siː / (BrE)
an inland sea between southeastern Europe and western Asia, bordered by countries including Turkey, Bulgaria, Romania, Ukraine, Russia, and Georgia

Examples

The Black Sea coast
Many tourists visit the Black Sea coast each summer for its beaches and resorts.
Several important rivers, including the Danube, flow into the Black Sea.
The Black Sea connects to the Mediterranean through the Bosporus Strait.
