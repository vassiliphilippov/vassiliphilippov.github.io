aquiculture

noun
plant cultivation in water without soil
Pronunciation: / ˈækwɪˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈakwɪˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
The cultivation of plants in water containing dissolved nutrients, without the use of soil.

Examples

Aquiculture of plants
Advances in aquiculture have made urban farming more efficient.
She studied aquiculture techniques for her environmental science degree.
The greenhouse specializes in aquiculture to produce fresh vegetables year-round.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydroponics - the process of growing plants in nutrient-rich water without soil
