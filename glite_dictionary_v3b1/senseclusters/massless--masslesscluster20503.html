massless

adjective
without mass
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmæs.ləs / (AmE), / ˈmæs.ləs / (BrE)
having no mass or measurable weight

Examples

Massless particles
In physics, photons are considered massless particles that travel at the speed of light.
The theory describes a massless field that permeates all of space.
Scientists debated whether the newly discovered particle was truly massless or had a tiny mass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: massive - having a large mass; very heavy
  • synonym: weightless - having no weight or apparent weight
