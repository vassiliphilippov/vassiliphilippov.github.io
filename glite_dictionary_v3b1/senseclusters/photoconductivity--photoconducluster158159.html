photoconductivity

noun
increase in conductivity due to light exposure
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊ.toʊ.kənˌdʌk.tɪˈvɪɾ.i / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.təʊ.kənˌdʌk.tɪˈvɪt.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the property of a material by which its electrical conductivity increases as a result of exposure to electromagnetic radiation, especially light

Examples

Photoconductivity in materials
Researchers measured a sharp increase in photoconductivity when the crystal was illuminated with ultraviolet light.
The photoconductivity of silicon makes it a key material in solar cell technology.
Understanding photoconductivity is important for developing new types of photodetectors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conductivity - the ability of a material to conduct electricity
