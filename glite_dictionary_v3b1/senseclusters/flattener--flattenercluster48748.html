flattener

noun
yield curve strategy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflætənər / (AmE), / ˈflætənə / (BrE)
a financial trade or strategy designed to profit from a decrease in the difference between short-term and long-term interest rates

Examples

Bond flattener trade
The investor added a flattener to his portfolio to benefit from the narrowing yield curve.
Many traders use a flattener when they expect long-term rates to fall closer to short-term rates.
A sudden change in central bank policy can make a flattener position more profitable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steepener - a financial trade that profits from an increasing difference between short- and long-term interest rates
