anemophilous

adjective
pollinated by wind (plants)
Pronunciation: / əˈnɛməfɪləs / (AmE), / əˈniːməfɪləs / (BrE)
(of plants) pollinated by wind rather than by animals or insects.

Examples

Anemophilous plants
Many grass species are anemophilous, relying on the wind to transfer pollen between plants.
The evolutionary adaptations of anemophilous flowers help maximize the chances of wind pollination.
Unlike insect-pollinated flowers, anemophilous plants typically have small, inconspicuous blooms.
