Pterospermum acerifolium

noun
Indian ornamental and shade tree
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛroʊˌspɜrməm ˌæsəˈrɪfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛərəʊˌspɜːməm ˌæsəˈrɪfəliəm / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to India, with fragrant nocturnal white flowers and reddish wood used for planking; often cultivated as an ornamental or shade tree.

Examples

Pterospermum acerifolium tree
Artisans in India sometimes use wood from Pterospermum acerifolium for furniture and planking.

The large Pterospermum acerifolium in the garden provides ample shade during the summer afternoons.
