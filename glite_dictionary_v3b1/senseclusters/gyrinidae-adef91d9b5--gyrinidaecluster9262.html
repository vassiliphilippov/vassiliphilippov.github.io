Gyrinidae

noun
family of aquatic whirligig beetles
Pronunciation: / dʒaɪˈrɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / dʒɪˈrɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic beetles commonly known as whirligig beetles, noted for swimming rapidly in circles on the surfaces of ponds and streams.

Examples

The Gyrinidae family
Members of the Gyrinidae family are often seen skimming in circles on the surface of ponds.
Scientists collected several Gyrinidae specimens to study their unique aquatic adaptations.
The Gyrinidae are easily identified by their divided eyes and rapid movement on water.
