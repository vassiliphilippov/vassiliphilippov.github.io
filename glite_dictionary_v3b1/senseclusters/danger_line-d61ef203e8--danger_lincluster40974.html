danger line

noun
boundary marking a dangerous area
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪndʒər laɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒə laɪn / (BrE)
an imaginary or marked boundary beyond which there is danger or risk

Examples

Cross the danger line
Crossing the danger line could result in serious injury or worse.
Swimmers are advised to stay inside the danger line painted on the pool floor.
The ship was forbidden to pass the danger line during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limit - point or boundary beyond which something does not or may not pass
  • antonym: safe zone - area considered to be free from danger
