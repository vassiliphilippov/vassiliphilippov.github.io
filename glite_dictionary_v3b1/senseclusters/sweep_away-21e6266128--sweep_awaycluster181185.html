sweep away

verb
remove or eliminate completely
1.0
Pronunciation: / swip əˈweɪ / (AmE), / swiːp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or destroy completely, so that nothing remains

Examples

Relief sweeps away fear
A sense of relief swept away her anxiety after hearing the good news.
Many traditions were swept away by the rapid modernization of the city.
The heavy rains threatened to sweep away the bridge during the flood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erase - to remove completely, especially from memory or existence
  • synonym: obliterate - to destroy completely, leaving no trace
