Brahmana

noun
ancient Hindu ritual text
9.9
Pronunciation: /brə.ˈmɑː.nə/ /; / /brə.ˈhɑː.mə.nə/ (AmE), /brə.ˈmɑː.nə/ /; / /brə.ˈhɑː.mə.nə/ (BrE)
Any of a class of ancient Hindu prose texts attached to the Vedas, giving instructions for priests on how to perform elaborate sacrificial rituals.

Examples

The Brahmana texts
Scholars often study the Brahmana to understand the origins of Hindu sacrificial traditions.
The Brahmana texts provide detailed guidance on ancient Vedic rituals.
The Brahmanas are considered essential for interpreting the ritual practices described in the Vedas.
