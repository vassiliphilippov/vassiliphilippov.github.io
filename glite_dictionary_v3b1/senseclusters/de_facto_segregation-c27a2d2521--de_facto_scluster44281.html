de facto segregation

noun
segregation in practice, not by law
9.9 6.4
Pronunciation: /deɪ ˈfæk.toʊ seɡ.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də ˈfæk.toʊ seɡ.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /deɪ ˈfæk.təʊ seɡ.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də ˈfæk.təʊ seɡ.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
Segregation, especially in schools, that occurs in practice even though it is not legally enforced.

Examples

De facto segregation in schools
De facto segregation often results from residential patterns rather than explicit legal requirements.
Community leaders are working to address de facto segregation in urban neighborhoods.
Despite the end of legal segregation, de facto segregation persisted in many school districts.
