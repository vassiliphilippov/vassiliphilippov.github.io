side by side

adjective
closely associated; existing together
Pronunciation: / saɪd baɪ saɪd / (AmE), / saɪd baɪ saɪd / (BrE)
closely related or associated with each other; existing together or in proximity.

Examples

Cultures side by side
For many years, the different cultures existed side by side in the same city.
Modern technology and ancient traditions often continue side by side in Japan.
The two companies worked side by side on the ambitious project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apart - separated by distance or time, not together
  • synonym: together - in or into one place, group, or mass; in company
