sideropenia

noun
deficiency of iron in the body
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪdərəˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ˌsɪdərəˈpiːnɪə / (BrE)
a deficiency of iron in the body, typically due to insufficient dietary intake or blood loss

Examples

Sideropenia diagnosis
A blood test confirmed that she was suffering from sideropenia, explaining her persistent fatigue.
Chronic sideropenia can lead to anemia if left untreated.
Doctors often check for sideropenia in patients with a history of heavy menstrual bleeding.
