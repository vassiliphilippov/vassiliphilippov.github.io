articled

adjective
bound by contract as an apprentice or trainee
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrtɪkəld / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪk(ə)ld / (BrE)
Bound by a formal contract, especially as an apprentice or trainee, typically in a legal or accounting profession.

Examples

An articled clerk
After university, she became an articled clerk at a prominent law firm.
He spent three years as an articled accountant before qualifying.
In the UK, trainees in some professions are known as articled students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apprenticed - bound by contract to work as an apprentice
