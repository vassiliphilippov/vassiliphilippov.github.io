harsh roast

noun
severe criticism or mocking evaluation
Pronunciation: / hɑrʃ roʊst / (AmE), / hɑːʃ rəʊst / (BrE)
A severe or strongly critical evaluation or commentary, especially delivered in a humorous or mocking way.

Examples

A harsh roast review
After the performance, the panel delivered a harsh roast of the contestants' efforts.
Her article was a harsh roast of current political policies.
The comedian's harsh roast left the audience both shocked and amused.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval
  • degree lower: roast - an act of criticizing or mocking someone, often humorously
