eudemon

noun
benevolent or good spirit
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈdiːmən /; / juːˈdiː.mən / (AmE), / juːˈdiːmən /; / juːˈdiː.mən / (BrE)
A benevolent or good spirit, especially in ancient Greek philosophy and mythology.

Examples

A eudemon spirit
In ancient Greece, a eudemon was thought to guide individuals with kindness.
She hoped a eudemon would protect her on her journey.
The philosopher believed that a eudemon watched over every virtuous soul.
In ancient Greek tales, a eudaemon would often guide heroes toward virtuous acts.
Philosophers sometimes describe conscience as a kind of inner eudaemon.
The villagers believed that a eudaemon protected their fields from harm.
