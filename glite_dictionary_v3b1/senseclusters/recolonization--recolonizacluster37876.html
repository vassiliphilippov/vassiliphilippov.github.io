recolonization

noun
return of outside control
7.5
Pronunciation: /riː.kɑː.lə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.kɑː.lə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.kɒ.lə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.kɒ.lə.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which outside influence or control returns to a country or people, often through economic or cultural means rather than formal political rule

Examples

Economic recolonization
Some historians argue that economic policies can lead to a new form of recolonization without direct political control.
The spread of foreign media was seen by some as a subtle kind of recolonization of local culture.
Activists warned that the investment deal might result in recolonization through economic dependence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act of establishing control over another country or people
  • antonym: decolonization - the process of a country or people gaining independence from outside control
