noncoding

noun
DNA or RNA not coding
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkoʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / nɒnˈkəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
a sequence of DNA or RNA that does not code for a protein, such as a noncoding region or molecule

Examples

Noncoding region
Scientists are studying noncoding regions to understand their role in gene regulation.
The mutation was found in a noncoding part of the genome, not affecting any protein.
Many noncoding RNAs have important functions in cellular processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coding - a DNA or RNA sequence that codes for a protein
