hermit

noun
person living in seclusion, often for religion
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrmɪt /; / ˈhɝː.mɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɜːmɪt /; / ˈhɜː.mɪt / (BrE)
a person who lives alone and away from society, often for religious reasons or personal reflection

Examples

A hermit in mountains
After years as a successful businessman, he became a hermit and moved to a remote mountain cabin.
The medieval hermit devoted his life to prayer and contemplation in solitude.
The village considered the old hermit to be wise but mysterious.
After years in the city, he became a hermit in the mountains, seeking peace and quiet.
She lived as a hermit, avoiding contact with the outside world.
The old hermit was rarely seen by the villagers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monk - a member of a religious community of men typically living under vows of poverty and obedience
  • synonym: recluse - a person who lives a solitary life and tends to avoid others
