come back to bite

verb
to have negative consequences later
1.3
Pronunciation: / kʌm bæk tə baɪt / (AmE), / kʌm bæk tə baɪt / (BrE)
to have negative consequences for someone, often as a result of their earlier actions or decisions

Examples

To come back to bite someone
His habit of procrastinating always seems to come back to bite him around deadlines.
If you lie on your résumé, it might come back to bite you when they check your references.
Ignoring the warning signs could come back to bite the company in the future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backfire - to have the opposite effect to what was intended, causing harm
