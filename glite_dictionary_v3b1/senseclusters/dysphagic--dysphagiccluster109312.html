dysphagic

adjective
difficulty swallowing
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfeɪdʒɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈfeɪdʒɪk / (BrE)
having difficulty swallowing or relating to difficulty in swallowing.

Examples

Dysphagic patient
The nurse prepared a special meal for the dysphagic patient to make swallowing easier.
Speech therapists often work with dysphagic individuals to improve their ability to eat safely.
After his stroke, he became dysphagic and required assistance during meals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dysphagia - difficulty or discomfort in swallowing
