conceptualize

verb
form or create a concept or idea
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛptʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈsɛptjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to form an idea or concept of something in the mind; to imagine or devise the idea for something

Examples

To conceptualize an idea
It is often difficult to conceptualize abstract scientific theories.
Students were asked to conceptualize their own solutions to the environmental problem.
The architect began to conceptualize a modern, eco-friendly building for the new museum.
It can be difficult to conceptualise the scale of the universe.
Researchers must conceptualise new theories before testing them.
The artist managed to conceptualise complex emotions in her paintings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceive - to form or devise (a plan or idea) in the mind
  • synonym: envision - to imagine as a future possibility; visualize
  • synonym: imagine - form a picture or idea in the mind
  • antonym: realise - become fully aware of something as a fact
