lapidate

verb
harshly criticize; stone
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlæpɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlapɪdeɪt / (BrE)
To attack or criticize harshly, especially in writing or speech; to stone (figuratively or literally).

Examples

To lapidate a book
She feared her novel would be lapidated by harsh reviewers.
The critics began to lapidate the poorly received film.
When his decision was announced, the public wasted no time in lapidating him in the press.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something
  • synonym: stone - to throw stones at someone as a form of punishment or killing
