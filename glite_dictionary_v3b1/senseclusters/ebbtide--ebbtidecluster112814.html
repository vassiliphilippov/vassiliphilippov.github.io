ebbtide

noun
outgoing phase of the tide
Pronunciation: / ˈɛbˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛb.taɪd / (BrE)
the period during which the tide is receding or water is flowing out from the shore

Examples

The ebbtide period
During the ebbtide, the fishing boats had to anchor farther from the shore.
The rocks become slippery and visible at ebbtide, so be careful while exploring.
We went for a walk along the beach at ebbtide and found many exposed shells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: floodtide - the tide while water is rising or flowing in
