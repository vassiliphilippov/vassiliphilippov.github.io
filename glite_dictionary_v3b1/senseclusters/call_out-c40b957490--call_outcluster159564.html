call out

verb
publicly criticize or accuse
4.6 3.90/m
Pronunciation: / k…îl a…ôt / (AmE), / k…îÀêl a…ôt / (BrE)
to publicly criticize, accuse, or demand accountability from someone, especially in public discourse or on social media.

Examples

To call out bad behavior
Activists often call out corporations for greenwashing on social platforms to demand accountability.
She publicly called out her manager at the meeting for making discriminatory remarks.
Journalists are increasingly calling out public figures who spread false information online.

Synonyms

  • call (someone) out
    5.8 0.01/m
    call (someone) out names and criticizes a person publicly for a specific wrong, while call out demands public accountability.
  • hit out at
    6.7 0.07/m
    hit out at criticizes someone strongly and publicly, often angrily in speech or press, while call out also involves public demands for accountability.
  • speak out against
    4.1 2.89/m
    speak out against publicly opposes an idea or person, often to protest, while call out is more about accusing and seeking accountability.
  • call to account
    6.0 0.15/m
    call to account asks someone to explain or accept responsibility, usually in a formal setting, while call out is public criticism or social-media accusation.
