internalization

noun
adoption of values or beliefs as one's own
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɜrnələˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪnˌtɝːnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making attitudes, values, or behaviors part of one's own beliefs or character, so that they guide thinking and actions without external influence.

Examples

The internalization of values
The internalization of societal norms begins in early childhood.
Therapy can sometimes facilitate the internalization of healthier attitudes and coping strategies.
Through internalization, she adopted her mentor's values as her own.
His internalisation of company values made him a trusted leader among his peers.
The internalisation of social norms begins at an early age through family and education.
Therapy often focuses on the internalisation of positive self-beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adoption - the act of taking up or accepting something as one's own
  • synonym: assimilation - the process of absorbing and integrating ideas or culture
  • antonym: externalization - the act of attributing one's internal thoughts or feelings to the outside world
