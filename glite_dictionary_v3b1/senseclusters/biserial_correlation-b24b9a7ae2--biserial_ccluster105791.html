biserial correlation

noun
statistical measure between a continuous and a dichotomous variable
9.9
Pronunciation: / baɪˈsɪri.əl ˌkɔr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / baɪˈsɪəri.əl ˌkɒr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A statistical correlation coefficient measuring the relationship between a continuous variable and a dichotomous variable.

Examples

A biserial correlation coefficient
A strong biserial correlation indicates a significant association between gender and exam performance.
Researchers often use biserial correlation when dealing with one continuous and one binary variable.
The biserial correlation was calculated to evaluate the relationship between test scores and pass/fail outcomes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: point-biserial correlation - A similar correlation coefficient used when one variable is continuous and the other is truly dichotomous
