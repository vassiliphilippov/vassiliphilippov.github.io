interposition

noun
the act of inserting or intervening
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of inserting something, such as a remark or action, between others, especially so as to interrupt or intervene

Examples

Quick interposition in a dispute
Her swift interposition prevented the argument from escalating into a heated quarrel.
The meeting was flowing smoothly until his interposition interrupted the discussion unexpectedly.
We welcomed the manager's interposition when the debate became too one-sided.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interjection - an abrupt remark, especially as an interruption
  • synonym: intervene - to come between so as to prevent or alter a result or course of events
