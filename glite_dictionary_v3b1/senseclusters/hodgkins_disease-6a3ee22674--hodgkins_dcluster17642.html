Hodgkin's disease

noun
malignant disease of the lymphatic system
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːdʒ.kɪnz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈhɒdʒ.kɪnz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A malignant disorder characterized by progressive, painless enlargement of lymph nodes, often followed by enlargement of the spleen and liver; also known as Hodgkin lymphoma.

Examples

Hodgkin's disease symptoms
Swelling in the neck and persistent fatigue led to tests that revealed Hodgkin's disease.
The patient was diagnosed with Hodgkin's disease after a biopsy of the enlarged lymph nodes.
Treatments for Hodgkin's disease have improved greatly over the past few decades.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lymphoma - a cancer of the lymphatic system
