kerogen

noun
organic rock material
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrəˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈkɛrəʊdʒən / (BrE)
a mixture of organic matter in sedimentary rocks that can produce oil or gas when heated

Examples

Oil kerogen mixture
Oil shale contains large amounts of kerogen that can be converted into fuel.
Scientists study kerogen to understand how oil and gas are formed in the earth.
The process of heating kerogen in rocks releases hydrocarbons used for energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bitumen - a black, sticky substance found in natural deposits or made from petroleum
  • degree higher: petroleum - a liquid mixture of hydrocarbons present in certain rock strata
