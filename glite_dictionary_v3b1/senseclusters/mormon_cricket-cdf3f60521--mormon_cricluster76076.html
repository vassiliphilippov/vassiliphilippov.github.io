Mormon cricket

noun
large wingless katydid of western US
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrmən ˌkrɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɔːmən ˌkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
a large, dark, wingless insect resembling a cricket, found in arid regions of the western United States and known for forming migratory swarms

Examples

A Mormon cricket swarm
Every summer, the fields were overrun by migrating Mormon crickets, threatening the region's crops.
Researchers studied the behavior of the Mormon cricket to understand its swarming patterns.
The sudden appearance of a Mormon cricket swarm forced farmers to protect their harvests.
