foppishly

adverb
in a showy manner
9.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ëÀêp…™ Éli / (AmE), / Ààf…íp…™ Éli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows excessive concern with appearance or fashion, often in a showy or affected way

Examples

Dressed foppishly
He always arrived at work foppishly dressed, attracting attention with his colorful ties.
The actor walked foppishly onto the stage, drawing laughter from the audience.
She criticized him for behaving foppishly at the formal dinner.

Synonyms

  • ostentatiously
    7.9 0.54/m
    ostentatiously is meant to attract notice with loud, showy displays to impress others, while foppishly is about vain, fashion-focused affectation.
  • flamboyantly
    6.8 0.23/m
    flamboyantly is bright and confident, using bold colors or gestures to attract attention, while foppishly is more about affected, vain fashion.

  • antonym: plainly - in a simple or unadorned manner
