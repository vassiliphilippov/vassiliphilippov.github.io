untax

verb
to remove tax
8.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtæks / (AmE), / ʌnˈtæks / (BrE)
to remove a tax from something or stop requiring tax on it

Examples

To untax goods
The government decided to untax certain essential goods to help low-income families.
If they untax renewable energy sources, more people might invest in solar panels.
The proposal to untax books was welcomed by educators and students alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exempt - to free from an obligation, especially from paying tax
  • antonym: tax - to impose a tax on something
