deliberate defense

noun
planned military defense with fortifications
1.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈlɪb.ər.ət dɪ.ˈfens/ /; / /dɪ.ˈlɪb.ə.reɪt dɪ.ˈfens/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈlɪb.ə.rət dɪ.ˈfens/ /; / /dɪ.ˈlɪb.ə.reɪt dɪ.ˈfens/ (BrE)
In military operations, a defense strategy that is carefully prepared in advance, often involving fortified positions and organized communication systems, before engagement with the enemy.

Examples

A deliberate defense plan
The commander ordered a deliberate defense of the position, reinforcing bunkers and establishing extensive communication lines.
Unlike a hasty retreat, a deliberate defense gives troops time to fortify and organize their response.
Years of planning went into the construction of the deliberate defense along the border.
A deliberate defence often incorporates bunkers, barbed wire, and well-planned communication lines.
The general ordered the troops to establish a deliberate defence along the eastern ridge before the enemy arrived.
Unlike a hasty response, a deliberate defence gives soldiers time to construct strong protective positions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hasty defence - a quickly organized military defense with little time for preparation
  • antonym: hasty defense - a defense established quickly without prior preparation
