recultivate

verb
to farm again
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkʌltɪveɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkʌltɪveɪt / (BrE)
to prepare and use land or grow crops on it again after a period of not doing so

Examples

To recultivate farmland
After years of neglect, the villagers decided to recultivate the old farmland.
Government programs encouraged farmers to recultivate areas that had been left fallow.
It takes time and effort to recultivate soil that has not been used for many seasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to leave something, such as land, unused or uncared for
  • synonym: cultivate - to prepare and use land for growing crops
