Synonyms

  • disassemble
    3.8 1/m
    disassemble means taking a machine into its parts carefully, while dismantle often includes removing equipment and making it unusable.
  • take apart
    3.0 2/m
    take apart is a plain, everyday phrase for separating things into pieces, while dismantle often implies removing parts to stop its use.
  • take down
    3.0 2/m
    take down means removing something from where it stands or hangs, while dismantle focuses on taking parts away to stop it working.
  • break apart
    3.0 2/m
    break apart often means something splits suddenly by force or stress, while dismantle is a careful, planned process of removing parts.
  • break up
    2.7 4/m
    break up can mean splitting into smaller pieces or ending a group or relationship, while dismantle means taking parts off to stop use.
