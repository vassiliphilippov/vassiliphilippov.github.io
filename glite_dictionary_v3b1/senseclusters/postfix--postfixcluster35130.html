postfix

noun
notation with operators after operands
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊstˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌfɪks / (BrE)
a mathematical or computing notation in which operators are placed after their operands

Examples

Postfix notation math
In postfix notation, the expression '5 6 *' means 5 times 6.
Many calculators use postfix to simplify the order of operations.
Learning postfix can help you understand how some computer algorithms work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: infix - a notation where operators are placed between operands
  • antonym: prefix - a notation where operators precede their operands
