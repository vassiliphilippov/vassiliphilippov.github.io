Hanukkah

noun
Jewish festival of lights
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑː.nə.kə /; / ˈhɑːnəkə /; / xɑːˈnukə /; / ˈhɑːnʊkə / (AmE), / ˈhæn.ʊ.kə /; / ˈhanəkə /; / ˈhænəkə /; / xəˈnʊkə /; / ˈxanʊkə / (BrE)
An eight-day Jewish festival commemorating the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC, celebrated with the lighting of the menorah (candles) each night and traditional foods.
