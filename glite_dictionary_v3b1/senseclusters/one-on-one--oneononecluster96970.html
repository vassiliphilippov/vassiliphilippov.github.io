one-on-one

noun
two-person meeting or matchup

A meeting, session, or direct matchup involving exactly two people, often for discussion, coaching, or competition.

Examples

A one-on-one meeting
The coach scheduled a one-on-one with the player to review technique.
After the presentation, she asked for a private one-on-one to discuss career goals.
They arranged a one-on-one matchup between veterans to decide who would lead the project.
