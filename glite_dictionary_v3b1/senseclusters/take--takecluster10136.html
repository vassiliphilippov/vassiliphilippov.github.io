take

verb
admit into a group or community
1.9
Pronunciation: / teɪk / (AmE), / teɪk / (BrE)
to receive, accept, or acquire something (such as an offer, opportunity, or membership)

Examples

To take an offer
After much discussion, they decided to take her into their group.
The club will take five new members this year.
We only take students who have passed the entrance exam.
He gratefully took the opportunity to join their project.
If you offer help, please be ready for someone to take it.
She smiled and took the gift from his hands.
He decided to take the job offer in New York.
I'll take a coffee to go, please.
She forgot to take her umbrella before leaving the house.
