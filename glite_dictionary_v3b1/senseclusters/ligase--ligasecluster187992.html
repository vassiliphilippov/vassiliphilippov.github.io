ligase

noun
enzyme joining molecules
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪˌɡeɪz / (AmE), / ˈlaɪˌɡeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that joins two molecules together, especially by forming a new chemical bond in DNA or other biological processes

Examples

DNA ligase
DNA ligase is essential for repairing breaks in the DNA backbone during replication.
Scientists used a ligase to join two fragments of DNA in the laboratory experiment.
A mutation in the ligase gene can lead to problems with cell repair mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nuclease - an enzyme that cuts nucleic acids by breaking bonds
  • synonym: polymerase - an enzyme that synthesizes long chains of molecules, especially nucleic acids
