urbanization

noun
the growth of cities and urban life
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɝː.bən.əˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɜrbənəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌɝː.bən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɝː.bə.nɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɜː.bən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɜːbənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an area becomes more like a city, with more people living and working in towns and cities

Examples

Rapid urbanization growth
Experts debate the environmental impact of large-scale urbanization.
Rapid urbanization has transformed many rural villages into bustling cities.
The government is preparing for the challenges brought by increased urbanization.
Many countries are facing challenges due to the effects of urbanization on housing and infrastructure.
Rapid urbanization has transformed the rural landscape into a bustling metropolis.
The urbanization of coastal regions often leads to environmental concerns such as pollution.
As urbanisation increases, so does the demand for affordable housing in big cities.
Environmental issues often arise as a consequence of extensive urbanisation.
The rapid urbanisation of developing countries has strained infrastructure and public services.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ruralisation - the process of making an area more rural or less urban
  • antonym: suburbanization - the process of population movement from cities to surrounding suburbs
  • synonym: urbanization - alternative American spelling of 'urbanisation'
