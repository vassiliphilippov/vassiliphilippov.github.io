alga

noun
aquatic, chlorophyll-containing organism
5.9 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.gə/ (AmE), /ˈæl.gə/ (BrE)
A simple, mainly aquatic organism that contains chlorophyll but lacks true stems, roots, and leaves, typically found in water or moist environments.

Examples

Green alga
Scientists study alga to understand their role in oxygen production and aquatic ecosystems.
Some types of alga are harvested and used as food supplements due to their high nutrient content.
The pond was covered in a thick layer of alga that turned the water green.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: plant - a living organism of the kind exemplified by trees, shrubs, herbs, grasses, ferns, and mosses, but not including algae
  • synonym: seaweed - large marine algae, especially used for edible species
