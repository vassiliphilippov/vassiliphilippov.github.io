allonge

noun
long historical wig
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈlɑːnʒ / (AmE), / əˈlɒ̃ʒ / (BrE)
a long, full wig worn by men, especially in the 17th and 18th centuries as a symbol of status

Examples

A judge's allonge
In portraits of French nobility, the men often wore a grand allonge to display their status.
The museum's costume exhibit features an authentic allonge from the 18th century.
He completed his historical costume with a white allonge and a velvet coat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: periwig - a wig, especially one worn by men in the 17th and 18th centuries
  • degree lower: toupee - a small wig worn to cover a bald spot
