detrusor

noun
bladder muscle
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtruːsər / (AmE), / dɪˈtruːsə / (BrE)
the smooth muscle in the wall of the bladder that contracts to expel urine

Examples

The detrusor muscle
The detrusor contracts to empty the bladder during urination.
Weakness in the detrusor can lead to problems with bladder control.
Doctors may test the function of the detrusor in patients with urinary issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bladder - the organ that stores urine in the body
  • antonym: sphincter - a ring of muscle that controls the passage of substances
