inwardness

noun
the quality of being inward or internal
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnwɚdnɪs /; / ˈɪnwərdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪnwədnɪs / (BrE)
the inner nature, essence, or most important quality of something

Examples

True inwardness
Her poetry explores the inwardness of human experience and emotion.
Scientists study the inwardness of cells to better understand how the body functions.
The inwardness of the body's organs protects them from external harm.
Debates about policy often miss the inwardness of the underlying issues.
She felt she finally understood the inwardness of his message after much reflection.
The philosopher's essay sought to reveal the inwardness of human virtue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: core - the central or most important part of something
  • synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • synonym: internality - the condition of being internal
  • antonym: outwardness - the quality of being outward or external
  • antonym: surface - the outside part or uppermost layer of something, often contrasted with core
