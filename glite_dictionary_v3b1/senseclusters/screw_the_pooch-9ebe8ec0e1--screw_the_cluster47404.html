screw the pooch

verb
make a serious mistake (informal idiom)
8.3
Pronunciation: / skru ðə puːtʃ / (AmE), / skruː ðə puːtʃ / (BrE)
to make a serious or costly mistake, often due to carelessness or poor judgment

Examples

To screw the pooch badly
He knew he’d screwed the pooch as soon as he hit send on the wrong email.
If you forget the tickets again, you’ll really screw the pooch this time.
The team screwed the pooch by missing that crucial play in the final minutes.
