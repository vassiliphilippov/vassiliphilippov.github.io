relief

noun
court-ordered redress or remedy
7.7 4.71/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààlif / (AmE), / r…™ÀàliÀêf / (BrE)
(law) redress or remedy provided by a court, such as compensation or protection from harm.

Examples

Court grants relief
After months of litigation, the court finally granted relief to the victims.
The judge considered what type of relief would be appropriate for the circumstances.
The plaintiff sought relief in the form of an injunction against the company.

Synonyms

  • redress
    7.6 1.60/m
    redress means giving compensation or fixing an injustice in a formal way, while relief is court-ordered redress or protection.
  • remedy
    3.8 9.18/m
    remedy is a legal action that fixes a wrong or problem, while relief is the court's specific solution or protection.
  • satisfaction
    3.9 1.40/m
    satisfaction is compensation paid to settle a complaint or injury, while relief can be many court orders beyond payment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remedy - legal means for enforcing a right or redressing a wrong
