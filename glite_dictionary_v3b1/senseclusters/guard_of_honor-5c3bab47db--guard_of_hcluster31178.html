guard of honor

noun
ceremonial escort for dignitaries or funerals
Pronunciation: / ɡɑrd əv ˈɑnɚ / (AmE), / ɡɑːd əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
a ceremonial escort, especially for a distinguished guest or for the casket at a military funeral

Examples

A guard of honor welcomes
A guard of honor welcomed the visiting president at the airport.
As the queen arrived, a guard of honor stood at attention along the red carpet.
The fallen soldier was accompanied by a guard of honor during the procession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: honor guard - a group of people, typically from the military or police, appointed to accompany a casket or dignitary
