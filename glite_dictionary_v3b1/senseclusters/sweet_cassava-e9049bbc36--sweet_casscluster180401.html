sweet cassava

noun
variety of cassava with edible roots
Pronunciation: / swit kəˈsɑvə / (AmE), / swiːt kəˈsɑːvə / (BrE)
A variety of cassava with less toxic roots that are used as a vegetable, and foliage used for animal feed.

Examples

Sweet cassava roots
Farmers prefer sweet cassava because it requires less processing before cooking.
Livestock can safely eat the leaves of sweet cassava as forage.
The roots of sweet cassava are commonly boiled or fried in many dishes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bitter cassava - variety of cassava containing more toxins
  • synonym: cassava - tropical plant with starchy edible roots
