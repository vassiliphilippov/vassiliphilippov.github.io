neonatal intensive care unit

noun
specialized hospital unit for newborns
4.6 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊˌneɪt̬l ɪnˈtɛnsɪv kɛr ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˌneɪtl ɪnˈtɛnsɪv keə ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A hospital unit equipped and staffed to provide intensive medical care to premature or critically ill newborn infants.

Examples

The neonatal intensive care unit ward
After complications during birth, their daughter spent two weeks in the neonatal intensive care unit.
Medical staff in the neonatal intensive care unit work around the clock to support fragile infants.
The hospital’s neonatal intensive care unit is equipped to care for babies born as early as 24 weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ICU - intensive care unit for critically ill patients
  • degree lower: nursery - hospital room for newborn babies, usually healthy ones
