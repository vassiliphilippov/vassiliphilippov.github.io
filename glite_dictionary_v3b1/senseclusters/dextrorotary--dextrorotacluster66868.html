dextrorotary

adjective
rotating polarized light to the right
8.9 9.2
Pronunciation: /dek.strə.ˈroʊ.tər.iː/ (AmE), /dek.strə.ˈrəʊ.tə.riː/ (BrE)
(of a compound or its solution) rotating the plane of polarized light to the right, that is, clockwise when viewed along the direction of the beam.

Examples

A dextrorotary compound
A dextrorotary compound is often denoted by a plus sign (+) in chemical notation.
Many natural sugars, such as glucose, are dextrorotary in solution.
The sugar solution is dextrorotary and will rotate polarized light to the right.

Related Terms

  • antonym: levorotary - rotating the plane of polarized light to the left
