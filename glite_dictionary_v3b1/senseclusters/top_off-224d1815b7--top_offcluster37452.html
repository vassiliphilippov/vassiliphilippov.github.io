top off

verb
fill to the top; almost overflowing
3.6 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / tÉ‘p É”f / (AmE), / tÉ’p É’f / (BrE)
to fill something to the top or to the point of almost overflowing

Examples

To top off a glass
After pouring the wine, he topped off each glass with a splash of soda.
She quickly topped off the tank so they wouldn't run out of gas.
Would you like me to top off your coffee before you leave?

Synonyms

  • fill to the brim
    2.7 0.36/m
    fill to the brim makes something completely full with no room left, while top off means adding an extra amount to reach the top.
  • top up
    3.2 0.15/m
    top up means refilling until something reaches the desired level, while top off means adding to the top until it nearly overflows.
  • fill
    1.0 145/m
    fill makes something full or occupies space in many ways, while top off means adding a little more to reach the top.

Related Terms
