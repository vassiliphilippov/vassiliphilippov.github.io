Lake Tanganyika

proper noun
large lake in central Africa
Pronunciation: / leɪk tæŋɡəˈniːkə / (AmE), / leɪk taŋɡaˈniːkə / (BrE)
a large lake in central Africa, situated on the border between Tanzania and the Democratic Republic of Congo, and known for being the longest freshwater lake in the world

Examples

Lake Tanganyika in Africa
Many unique species of fish are found only in Lake Tanganyika.
The explorers sailed across Lake Tanganyika during their expedition in Africa.
Tourists often visit Lake Tanganyika for its scenic beauty and biodiversity.
