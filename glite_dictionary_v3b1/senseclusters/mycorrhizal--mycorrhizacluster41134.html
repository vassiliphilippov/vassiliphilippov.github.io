mycorrhizal

adjective
fungus-root association
9.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kə.ˈraɪ.zəl/ (AmE), /maɪ.kə.ˈraɪ.zəl/ (BrE)
relating to or involving the symbiotic association between a fungus and the roots of a plant

Examples

Mycorrhizal fungi
Many forest plants rely on mycorrhizal fungi to absorb nutrients from the soil.
The mycorrhizal relationship benefits both the fungus and the plant roots.
Researchers study mycorrhizal networks to understand how trees communicate underground.

Related Terms
