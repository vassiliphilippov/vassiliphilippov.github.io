bichloride

noun
compound with two chlorine atoms
Pronunciation: / baɪˈklɔːr.aɪd / (AmE), / baɪˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a compound that contains two chlorine atoms per molecule, especially used in chemical contexts

Examples

A bichloride compound
Proper safety precautions are required when handling any bichloride due to its potential toxicity.
Scientists discovered a new bichloride compound that showed promising antibacterial properties.
The most famous bichloride is mercuric bichloride, commonly known as corrosive sublimate.
