cyclic

adjective
arranged in whorls (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪklɪk, ˈsɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪk, ˈsaɪklɪk / (BrE)
(of plant parts) arranged in whorls, or forming a whorl, especially of petals or flowers.

Examples

Cyclic flower arrangement
Botanists classified the species as cyclic because its floral organs are arranged in concentric circles.
Cyclic flowers often exhibit greater symmetry than those with spiral arrangements.
The rose has cyclic petals, typical of many ornamental flowers.
