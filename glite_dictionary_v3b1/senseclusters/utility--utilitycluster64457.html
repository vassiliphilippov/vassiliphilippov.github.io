utility

noun
measure of satisfaction or value (economics)
2.3
Pronunciation: / juːˈtɪləti / (AmE), / juːˈtɪləti / (BrE)
(Economics) A measure of satisfaction or value that an individual derives from goods or services, used to explain choices and preferences.

Examples

Utility in economics
Different individuals may assign different levels of utility to the same product.
In economics, utility is used to quantify the satisfaction people get from consuming goods.
Maximizing utility is a fundamental principle in consumer choice theory.
