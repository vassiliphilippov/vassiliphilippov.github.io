acute kidney failure

noun
sudden loss of kidney function
3.5 3.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkjuːt ˈkɪd.niː ˈfeɪ.ljər/ /; / /ˈæk.juːt ˈkɪd.niː ˈfeɪ.ljər/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkjuːt ˈkɪd.niː ˈfeɪ.lə/ /; / /ˈæk.juːt ˈkɪd.niː ˈfeɪ.lə/ (BrE)
A sudden loss of kidney function, often due to injury, infection, or obstruction, resulting in the kidneys' inability to filter waste products from the blood.

Examples

Sudden acute kidney failure
Burn victims can be at high risk of acute kidney failure if not properly managed.
Doctors treated her acute kidney failure promptly to prevent permanent organ damage.
The patient was rushed to the ICU after developing acute kidney failure following severe dehydration.
