misjudgment

noun
incorrect legal decision
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
an incorrect ruling, verdict, or legal decision made by a court or judge

Examples

Court misjudgment
The defense attorney argued that the conviction was based on a misjudgment by the trial judge.
After reviewing the evidence, the higher court found a clear misjudgment in the original case.
The new law aims to reduce the risk of misjudgment in complex legal proceedings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: correct judgment - a decision that is accurate and fair
  • synonym: error - a mistake or incorrect decision
