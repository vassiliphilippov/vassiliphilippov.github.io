cognitivist

noun
theory of mental processes
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːɡnətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪtɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or supports the theory that mental processes can be understood scientifically

Examples

A scientific cognitivist
The cognitivist explained how memory and perception are central to understanding learning.
Many cognitivists believe that thinking can be studied using scientific methods.
As a cognitivist, she focused her research on how people solve problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behaviorist - a person who studies or supports the theory that behavior is learned through interaction with the environment
  • synonym: cognitive scientist - a person who studies the mind and its processes scientifically
