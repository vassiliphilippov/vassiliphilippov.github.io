rusticate

verb
send to the countryside
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to send someone to the countryside, especially as a punishment or to remove them from a particular environment

Examples

To rusticate a troublemaker
After causing trouble at school, he was rusticated to his aunt’s farm for the summer.
In Victorian times, families would sometimes rusticate their children to the countryside for health reasons.
The university decided to rusticate the student for repeated violations.
