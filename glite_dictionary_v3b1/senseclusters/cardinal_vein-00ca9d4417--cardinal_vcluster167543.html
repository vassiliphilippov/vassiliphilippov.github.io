cardinal vein

noun
major embryonic vein in vertebrates
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrdɪnəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl veɪn / (BrE)
any of the major embryonic venous channels in vertebrates that return blood to the heart, especially prominent in early development.

Examples

The cardinal vein development
Disruption of a cardinal vein during development can lead to severe vascular anomalies.
In embryology, researchers often study the formation of the cardinal vein to understand blood vessel development.
The cardinal vein is crucial in the embryonic circulatory system of vertebrates.
