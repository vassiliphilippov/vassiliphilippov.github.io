kick-out

noun
removal or expulsion
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪk aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɪk aʊt / (BrE)
an act of being removed or forced to leave a place, group, or organization

Examples

A kick-out from school
After repeated warnings, the student faced a kick-out from the club.
The company announced the kick-out of employees who violated the code of conduct.
His behavior at the event led to an immediate kick-out by the organizers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admission - the act of allowing someone to enter or join
  • synonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from a position or place
  • synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave
