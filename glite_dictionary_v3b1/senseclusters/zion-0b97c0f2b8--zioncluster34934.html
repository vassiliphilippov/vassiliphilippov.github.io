Zion

proper noun
biblical name for a hill, later Jerusalem
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈzaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ən / (BrE)
an ancient hill in Jerusalem, originally a stronghold captured by King David, later used as a poetic or symbolic name for Jerusalem, especially in religious texts

Examples

Ancient Zion hill
In many hymns, Zion represents a place of peace and spiritual renewal.
Pilgrims from many countries visit Zion each year as part of their religious journey.
The ancient prophets lamented the destruction of Zion in their writings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Jerusalem - the ancient city central to several major religions
