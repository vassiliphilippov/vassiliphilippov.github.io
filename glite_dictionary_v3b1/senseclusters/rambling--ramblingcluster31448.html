(of speech or writing) lengthy and confused or inconsequential, often digressing from the main point or covering a wide range of topics.

Examples

Rambling speech
He tends to give rambling answers that drift away from the original question.
Her rambling speech made it difficult to understand her main argument.
The book is fascinating but becomes quite rambling in the later chapters.

Synonyms

  • discursive
    8.3 0.69/m
    discursive moves from topic to topic in a thoughtful, exploratory way, while rambling sounds confused and drifts from the main point.
  • digressive
    8.1 0.08/m
    digressive departs from the main subject to explore side points, while rambling is longer, confused, and often wanders off aimlessly.
  • excursive
    9.7 0.00/m
    excursive strays across many topics in a free, wide-ranging way, while rambling feels muddled and often loses the main thread.
  • diffuse
    7.1 0.43/m
    diffuse uses many words and spreads ideas thinly, while rambling not only is long but also digresses and feels confused.
  • long-winded
    5.8 0.91/m
    long-winded goes on at length with many words, while rambling also wanders off topic and seems disorganized or unclear.
