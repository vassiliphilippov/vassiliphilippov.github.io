tongue

noun
metal striker inside a bell
Pronunciation: / tʌŋ / (AmE), / tʌŋ / (BrE)
A metal striker suspended inside a bell that produces sound by striking the sides when the bell is rung.

Examples

Bell tongue
After years of use, the tongue had worn a groove inside the old church bell.
The tongue of the bell struck loudly with each swing.
The blacksmith repaired the broken tongue so the bell could sound again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clapper - the metal piece inside a bell that strikes the sides to make sound
