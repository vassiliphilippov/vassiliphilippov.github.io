redistributor

noun
one who reallocates wealth
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːdɪˈstrɪbjətər / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈstrɪbjʊtə / (BrE)
a person, group, or government that transfers income or wealth from richer to poorer groups, often through taxes or public spending

Examples

A redistributor of wealth
The new president was seen as a redistributor who aimed to reduce economic inequality.
Critics argued that the government's role as a redistributor discouraged investment.
Many social programs rely on a redistributor approach to support low-income families.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - a person who resists change and prefers traditional policies
  • synonym: reformer - a person who works to change and improve a system
