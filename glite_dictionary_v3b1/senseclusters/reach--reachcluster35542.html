reach

verb
arrive at a place or goal
1.1 162/m
Pronunciation: / riÀêt É /; / rit É / (AmE), / riÀêt É / (BrE)
to arrive at or achieve a place, state, level, or goal, especially after effort

Examples

To reach a goal
After years of work, she finally reached her goal.
It took weeks for the package to reach its destination.
We hope to reach the summit before sunset.
She hopes to reach the advanced level in her language course before the end of the year.
Temperatures may reach record highs during the summer months.
The city is expected to reach a population of one million by next year.
After years of hard work, she finally reached her dream of becoming a doctor.
It is not easy to reach the top level in this organization.
Only a few athletes reach the Olympics, and even fewer win medals.

Synonyms

  • attain
    3.9 17/m
    attain implies formal success in reaching a high level after effort, while reach covers arrival or achieving more broadly.
  • get to
    2.4 93/m
    get to expresses everyday reaching of a place or goal after effort, while reach covers both physical arrival and achieving levels.
  • achieve
    2.3 121/m
    achieve focuses on successfully completing a goal by skill or effort, while reach can mean arriving or getting to a level.
  • accomplish
    2.6 64/m
    accomplish means finish a task successfully through effort, while reach can mean arriving somewhere or achieving a level.
  • arrive at
    2.1 25/m
    arrive at describes reaching a place or conclusion after travel or thought, while reach also includes achieving goals or levels.
  • arrive
    1.0 137/m
    arrive focuses on reaching a physical place, often at journey end, while reach also covers achieving goals or states.
  • make
    1.0 399/m
    make often appears in casual phrases to mean managing to reach something, while reach is more neutral for arrival or success.
  • gain
    1.4 83/m
    gain means obtain something such as advantage, skill, or experience through effort, while reach focuses on arriving or achieving.
  • hit
    2.5 38/m
    hit is informal and often means reach a number or target suddenly, while reach covers arriving or achieving more generally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depart - to leave a place, especially to begin a journey
  • antonym: fail - to not succeed in achieving a goal
  • antonym: fall short - to fail to reach a desired level or standard
