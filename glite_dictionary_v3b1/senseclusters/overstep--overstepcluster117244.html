overstep

verb
go beyond a limit or boundary
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstɛp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstɛp / (BrE)
To go beyond what is allowed or acceptable; to exceed a limit or boundary.

Examples

To overstep the mark
He apologized for overstepping the mark with his comments.
The journalist was accused of overstepping ethical boundaries in her reporting.
You must not overstep your authority when managing the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exceed - to go beyond a limit or amount
  • antonym: obey - to follow rules or limits; to not go beyond what is allowed
  • synonym: transgress - to do something that is not allowed, especially by law or moral rule
