Panglossian

noun
naive optimist
Pronunciation: / pænˈɡlɑːsiən / (AmE), / pænˈɡlɒsiən / (BrE)
a person who is excessively or unrealistically optimistic, especially one who ignores real problems or difficulties

Examples

Panglossian thinker
Even when the company faced bankruptcy, the Panglossian insisted everything would work out perfectly.
Her Panglossian attitude made it hard for her to recognize the seriousness of the situation.
Some critics argue that being a Panglossian can prevent people from preparing for real challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: optimist - a person who tends to expect the best in all things
  • antonym: pessimist - a person who tends to see the worst aspect of things
  • antonym: realist - a person who accepts situations as they are and deals with them practically
