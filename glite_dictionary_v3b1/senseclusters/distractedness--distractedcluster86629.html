distractedness

noun
inability to focus
6.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪd.nəs/ /; / /dɪ.ˈstræk.təd.nəs/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪd.nəs/ /; / /dɪ.ˈstræk.təd.nəs/ (BrE)
the state of being unable to concentrate because your attention is pulled away by worries, thoughts, or other distractions

Examples

Distractedness during meeting
His distractedness during the meeting was obvious to everyone in the room.
She apologized for her distractedness and promised to pay more attention next time.
Periods of distractedness can make it difficult to complete important tasks.
His distractedness made it difficult for him to finish the assignment on time.
The teacher noticed the students' distractedness as loud noises came from the hallway.
Meditation can help reduce distractedness and improve your ability to focus.
Her constant distractedness made it difficult for her to finish reading a book.
The teacher noticed the student's distractedness during the important lesson.
His distractedness at work led to several small mistakes throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distraction - something that prevents concentration
  • antonym: focus - the ability to concentrate on one thing
  • synonym: inattention - lack of attention or focus
  • synonym: inattentiveness - lack of attention or focus
