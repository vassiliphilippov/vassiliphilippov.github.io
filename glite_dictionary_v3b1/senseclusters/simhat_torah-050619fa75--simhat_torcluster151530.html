Simhat Torah

noun
Jewish festival marking completion of Torah readings
9.6
Pronunciation: /sɪm.ˈhɑːt ˈtɔː.rə/ /; / /siːm.ˈhɑːt ˈtɔː.rə/ (AmE), /sɪm.ˈhɑːt ˈtɔː.rə/ /; / /siːm.ˈhɑːt ˈtɔː.rə/ (BrE)
A Jewish holy day celebrated at the end of Sukkot to mark the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings.

Examples

The Simhat Torah holiday
During Simhat Torah, the Torah scrolls are carried in joyous processions around the sanctuary.
Many families attend synagogue to celebrate Simhat Torah with singing and dancing.
On Simhat Torah, children often receive sweets and small flags as part of the festivities.
