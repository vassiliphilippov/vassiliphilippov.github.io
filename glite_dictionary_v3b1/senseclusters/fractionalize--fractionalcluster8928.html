fractionalize

verb
to divide into parts
Pronunciation: / ˈfrækʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to divide something into smaller parts or shares, especially to make it easier to manage or for multiple people to own

Examples

To fractionalize assets
The company decided to fractionalize its assets for easier management.
To attract more investors, the real estate was fractionalized into smaller ownership shares.
Some organizations fractionalize their services to better serve different customer groups.
The company decided to fractionalize its real estate holdings to attract more investors.
By fractionalizing the property, several people were able to own a share of the building.
New technology platforms make it easier to fractionalize valuable assets like art and collectibles.

  • synonym: divide - to separate something into parts or groups
  • synonym: split - to break or divide into pieces
  • antonym: unify - to bring together or combine into a single unit
