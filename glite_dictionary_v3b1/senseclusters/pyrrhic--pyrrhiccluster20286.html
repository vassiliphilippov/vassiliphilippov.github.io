pyrrhic

noun
metrical foot of two unstressed syllables
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪrɪk / (BrE)
(in prosody) A metrical foot consisting of two short or unstressed syllables.

Examples

A pyrrhic foot
In the line of poetry, the pyrrhic foot contributed a subtle, gentle rhythm.
Some literary scholars debate whether the pyrrhic truly exists in English verse.
The teacher explained the pyrrhic as two consecutive unstressed syllables in a word or line.
