been around the block

adjective
having experienced or knowledgeable
Pronunciation: / bɪn əˈraʊnd ðə blɑk / (AmE), / bɪn əˈraʊnd ðə blɒk / (BrE)
having considerable experience or knowledge, especially from dealing with various situations or challenges in life or a particular field

Examples

Having been around the block, she could spot a scam from a mile away.
He may seem young, but he's been around the block in the industry.
You need someone who's been around the block to handle these negotiations.
