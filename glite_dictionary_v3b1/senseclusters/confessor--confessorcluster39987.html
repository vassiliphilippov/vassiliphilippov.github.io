confessor

noun
person who confesses to something
3.1
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛsər / (AmE), / kənˈfɛsə / (BrE)
a person who admits or reveals information, especially one disclosing facts that may be damaging to themselves

Examples

A confessor in court
After hours of questioning, the police finally got a confessor to admit to the crime.
Sometimes a confessor seeks forgiveness after coming forward with the truth.
The confessor revealed details that only the perpetrator could have known.

Related Terms

  • synonym: witness - a person who gives testimony, often in legal settings
