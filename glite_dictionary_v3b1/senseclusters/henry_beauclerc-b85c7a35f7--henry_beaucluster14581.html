Henry Beauclerc

proper noun
King of England, 1100–1135
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛnri boʊˈklɛrk / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri bəʊˈklɛək / (BrE)
(1068–1135) King of England from 1100 to 1135, youngest son of William the Conqueror, who conquered Normandy in 1106.

Examples

Henry Beauclerc King England
Henry Beauclerc became King of England after the death of his brother William II.
In 1106, Henry Beauclerc defeated his brother Robert to gain control of Normandy.
Many historians credit Henry Beauclerc with establishing efficient government in medieval England.
