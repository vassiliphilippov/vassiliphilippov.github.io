bluster

noun
boastful or aggressive talk with little effect
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
loud, aggressive, or boastful talk that is intended to impress or intimidate, but often lacks real substance

Examples

Empty bluster
Despite all her bluster, she failed to impress the audience.
His bluster could not hide his lack of experience.
The manager's bluster faded when tough questions were asked.
Despite all his bluster, he quietly backed down from the challenge.
Many see the politician's speeches as just empty bluster without real conviction.
The general's bluster could not hide his uncertainty before the battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bragging - boasting or expressing excessive pride
  • synonym: bravado - a show of boldness intended to impress or intimidate
  • antonym: humility - the quality of being humble
  • antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence
