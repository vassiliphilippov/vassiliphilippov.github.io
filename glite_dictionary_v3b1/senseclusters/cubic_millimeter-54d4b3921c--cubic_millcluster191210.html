cubic millimeter

noun
metric unit of volume (mm³)
1.9 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkjuː.bɪk mɪ.ˈlɪ.mɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkjuː.bɪk mɪ.ˈlɪ.mɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a unit of volume in the metric system equal to the volume of a cube with sides of 1 millimeter; equal to one millionth of a liter

Examples

One cubic millimeter volume
A single drop of water contains thousands of cubic millimeters of liquid.
Scientists count the number of bacteria in a cubic millimeter of soil.
The tumor measured less than one cubic millimeter on the scan.
A single drop of water is roughly fifty cubic millimetres in volume.
Scientists measured the tumour and found it was just three cubic millimetres in size.
The required dosage is less than one cubic millimetre per injection.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: liter - a metric unit of volume, the base unit in the metric system
  • degree higher: litre - a metric unit of capacity, equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
  • synonym: millilitre - a metric unit of volume equal to one-thousandth of a liter
