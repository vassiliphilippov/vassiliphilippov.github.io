warning of war

noun
notice of possible or impending war
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrnɪŋ əv wɔr / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ əv wɔː / (BrE)
a formal notice or information given to policymakers that a hostile nation intends, prepares for, or may increase the risk of war

Examples

A warning of war
Diplomatic channels remained open even as leaders received a clear warning of war from their advisers.
Ignoring the early warning of war proved disastrous for the nation's defense strategy.
The intelligence agency issued a warning of war after detecting unusual troop movements near the border.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: declaration of war - an official announcement of the start of war
  • synonym: threat of war - the possibility or danger that war will occur
