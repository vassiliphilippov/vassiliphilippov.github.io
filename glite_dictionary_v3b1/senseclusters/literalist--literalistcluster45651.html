literalist

adjective
strictly factual
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬ərəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəlɪst / (BrE)
treating words, statements, or representations in their exact, factual sense without interpretation or metaphor

Examples

Literalist approach
His literalist reading of the poem ignored its deeper symbolic meaning.
A literalist approach to the contract can sometimes lead to unfair results.
The teacher explained that a literalist answer was not what she was looking for in the assignment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: figurative - not literal; using metaphor or symbolism
  • synonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
