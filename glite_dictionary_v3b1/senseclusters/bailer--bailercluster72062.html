bailer

noun
person posting bail
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.lər/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.lə/ (BrE)
a person who pays or provides bail to secure someone else's release from custody

Examples

A bailer pays bail
The bailer must ensure the defendant appears in court as required.
Without a bailer, the accused would have remained in jail until the trial.
Her uncle agreed to act as bailer and paid the necessary amount to the court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - a person accused or sued in a court of law
  • synonym: surety - a person who takes responsibility for another's performance of an undertaking, such as appearing in court
