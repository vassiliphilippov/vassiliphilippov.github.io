favorable

adjective
giving an advantage; beneficial
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪvərəbl /; / ˈfeɪvərəbəl /; / ˈfeɪv(ə)rəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəbl /; / ˈfeɪv(ə)rəb(ə)l / (BrE)
giving an advantage, benefit, or making success more likely; helpful or beneficial (especially of conditions or situations)

Examples

Favorable conditions
A favorable climate helped the crops grow earlier this year.
She received a favorable review, which boosted her confidence.
The terms of the deal were especially favorable for small businesses.
She chose a favorable moment to bring up her proposal.
The company launched its product during a favorable economic climate.
The weather conditions are favorable for our picnic this weekend.
The launch was postponed until the forecast showed favorable skies.
The team waited for favorable weather conditions before attempting the ascent.
We decided to sail in the morning, hoping for favorable winds.
