Bakke decision

noun
1978 Supreme Court decision on affirmative action
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːki dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈbæki dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
A landmark 1978 U.S. Supreme Court ruling that upheld the use of race as one of several factors in medical school admissions.

Examples

The Bakke decision ruling
After the Bakke decision, universities across the country revised their admissions policies to comply with the ruling.
Many law students study the Bakke decision as a key precedent in constitutional law courses.
The Bakke decision was a turning point in the debate over affirmative action in the United States.
