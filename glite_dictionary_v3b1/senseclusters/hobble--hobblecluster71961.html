hobble

verb
walk with difficulty due to injury or pain
5.6 2.32/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ‘bÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ’bl / (BrE)
to walk with difficulty, typically because of pain, injury, or physical limitation

Examples

To hobble across the room
After spraining his ankle, he could only hobble slowly across the room.
She watched her grandfather hobble down the garden path, leaning on his cane.
The injured dog managed to hobble back to its owner.
