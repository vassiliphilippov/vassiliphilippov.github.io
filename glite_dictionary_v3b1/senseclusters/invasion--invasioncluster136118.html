invasion

noun
spread of disease or cancer in the body
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
(medicine) the spread of pathogenic microorganisms or malignant cells into new areas of the body.

Examples

Tumor invasion
Doctors were concerned about the tumor's invasion into nearby blood vessels.
Early detection reduces the risk of widespread invasion by cancer cells.
The rapid invasion of healthy tissue by the bacteria made the infection difficult to treat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metastasis - the spread of cancer cells to new areas of the body
