response

noun
bodily reaction to a stimulus or agent
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a bodily process that occurs as a result of some external stimulus or agent, such as a reaction to a drug or environmental factor

Examples

Body response to cold
His rapid response to the cold surprised everyone in the room.
Older adults may have a slower physical response to certain medications.
The doctor observed her allergic response after the injection.
