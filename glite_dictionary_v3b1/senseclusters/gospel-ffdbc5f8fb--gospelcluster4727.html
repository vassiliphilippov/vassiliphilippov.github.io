Gospel

noun
the four books on Christ's life in the New Testament
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑspəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl / (BrE)
the four books in the New Testament (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John) that recount the life and teachings of Jesus Christ

Examples

The Gospel of Matthew
Many Christians read the Gospel of Matthew during Advent.
Scholars study the differences among the Gospel accounts to better understand early Christianity.
The four Gospel writers each present a unique perspective on Jesus.
