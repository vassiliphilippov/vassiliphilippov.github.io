flake

verb
cover with flakes, or make flaky
6.7 2.2
Pronunciation: /fleɪk/ (AmE), /fleɪk/ (BrE)
to break off or come off in thin, flat pieces, or to cause something to form flakes

Examples

Paint flakes off
Painters warned that the old paint would start to flake the walls if not removed.
Snow began to flake the roofs as the storm intensified.
The baker decided to flake the top of the pastry with almonds for extra crunch.
As the pastry bakes, it will flake and become beautifully crisp.
Paint on old houses often begins to flake after years of exposure to the weather.
The dry skin on his hands started to flake during the winter months.
After a few years, the paint will start to flake due to weathering.
Be careful not to scratch the table, or the varnish might flake off.
The old wallpaper began to flake from the damp walls.
