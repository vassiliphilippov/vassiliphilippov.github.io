whereat

adverb
at which place
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɛrˈæt / (AmE), / wɛərˈæt / (BrE)
at which place or point; used to refer to the location or situation just mentioned, mainly in older or formal English

Examples

The place whereat it happened
He made a bold statement, whereat the audience gasped in surprise.
A disagreement arose, whereat the meeting was abruptly ended.
She pointed to the old house, whereat everyone turned to look.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wherein - in which place or situation
  • synonym: whereupon - immediately after which
