undue

adjective
not justified; excessive or unwarranted
6.0 4.32/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàdu /; /  ånÀàduÀê / (AmE), /  ånÀàdjuÀê / (BrE)
more than is reasonable, necessary, or proper; excessive or unwarranted

Examples

An undue advantage
He was accused of taking undue advantage of his position for personal gain.
Parents should avoid showing undue concern over minor mistakes their children make.
The committee found that there was undue pressure placed on the employees to finish the project early.
She was under undue stress during the final weeks of the project.
The law protects employees from undue pressure to resign.
The manager faced criticism for his undue interference in staff decisions.
He left the meeting early to avoid undue stress from a heated debate.
Please do not give undue importance to minor errors in the report.
The judge cautioned against placing undue emphasis on one piece of evidence.

Synonyms

  • unwarranted
    7.0 2.62/m
    unwarranted is done without a good reason and seems unnecessary, while undue describes something excessive or not properly needed.
  • unjustified
    4.8 1.61/m
    unjustified lacks a valid reason or proof and feels unsupported, while undue means more than is reasonable or proper.
  • excessive
    3.6 20/m
    excessive is much too large in amount or action and feels over the limit, while undue stresses lack of need.
  • inordinate
    7.7 1.93/m
    inordinate describes something far beyond normal size or degree, while undue focuses on being unnecessary or unwarranted.
  • unreasonable
    3.0 8.97/m
    unreasonable means not fair or sensible in attitude or demand, while undue emphasizes excess or lack of proper reason.
  • disproportionate
    5.9 5.30/m
    disproportionate is too large or small compared with something else, while undue highlights being improperly excessive or needless.
  • exorbitant
    7.9 1.99/m
    exorbitant describes very high price or demand beyond common sense, while undue applies more widely to any excessive or improper thing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • synonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • antonym: justified - having a good reason; warranted
  • antonym: reasonable - fair and sensible; not excessive
  • synonym: unwarranted - not justified or authorized
