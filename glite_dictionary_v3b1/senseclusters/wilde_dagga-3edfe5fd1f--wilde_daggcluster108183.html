wilde dagga

noun
South African herb smoked like tobacco
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪldə ˈdɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈwɪldə ˈdæɡə / (BrE)
A South African herb (Leonotis leonurus) with orange flowers, often smoked as a tobacco substitute and believed to have mild psychoactive effects.

Examples

wilde dagga plant
Gardeners are attracted to wilde dagga because of its showy orange flowers and medicinal history.
In some regions, traditional healers use wilde dagga for its calming effects.
The leaves of wilde dagga are often dried and smoked as an herbal alternative to tobacco.
