Geiger-Muller counter

noun
instrument for detecting ionizing radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪər ˈmʊlər ˈkaʊntər / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪɡə ˈmʌlə ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
an instrument used to detect and measure ionizing radiation, consisting of a gas-filled tube that discharges electrically when radiation passes through it

Examples

A Geiger-Muller counter device
A Geiger-Muller counter will emit clicks when it detects ionizing particles passing through its tube.
Carrying a Geiger-Muller counter, the technician checked the contaminated area for radioactive particles.
The scientist used a Geiger-Muller counter to monitor radiation levels in the laboratory.
