bottom-feeder

noun
fish that feeds on the bottom
5.1 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑː.təm ˈfiː.dər/ (AmE), /ˈbɒt.əm ˈfiː.də/ (BrE)
a fish or animal that lives and eats food found at the bottom of a body of water, usually by scavenging

Examples

A bottom-feeder fish
Catfish are well-known bottom-feeders that scavenge food from the riverbed.
Many bottom-feeders play an important role in keeping aquatic ecosystems clean.
The aquarium contains several species of bottom-feeder fish.
Catfish are classic bottom-feeders that consume food found on riverbeds.
Some marine bottom-feeders help keep aquatic ecosystems clean by eating dead matter.
The lake is home to several species of bottom-feeders, including carp and catfish.
