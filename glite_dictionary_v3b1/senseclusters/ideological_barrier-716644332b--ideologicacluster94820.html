ideological barrier

noun
obstacle formed by conflicting ideologies
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiəˈlɑdʒɪkəl bəˈrɪr / (AmE), / ˌaɪdɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl bəˈrɪə / (BrE)
A hindrance to cooperation or communication caused by differences in beliefs, values, or ideologies.

Examples

An ideological barrier to cooperation
Cultural exchange programs can help overcome the ideological barrier that exists between different societies.
Negotiations broke down due to the ideological barrier between the two countries.
The committee struggled to find consensus because an ideological barrier divided its members.
