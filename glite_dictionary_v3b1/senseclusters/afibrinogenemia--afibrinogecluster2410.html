afibrinogenemia

noun
absence of fibrinogen in blood
Pronunciation: / eɪˌfaɪbrɪˌnoʊdʒəˈniːmiə / (AmE), / eɪˌfɪbrɪnəʊdʒəˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A rare medical condition characterized by the absence of fibrinogen in the blood plasma, resulting in excessive or prolonged bleeding.

Examples

Afibrinogenemia bleeding disorder
Because afibrinogenemia is inherited, genetic counseling was recommended for the patient's family.
People with afibrinogenemia often require immediate medical treatment after even minor injuries.
The diagnosis of afibrinogenemia was confirmed by blood tests showing no detectable fibrinogen.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hypofibrinogenemia - decreased levels of fibrinogen in the blood
