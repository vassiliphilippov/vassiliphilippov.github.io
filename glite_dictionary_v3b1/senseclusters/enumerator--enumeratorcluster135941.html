enumerator

noun
person who collects census or survey data
7.1 5.6
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈnuː.mə.reɪ.tər/ /; / /ɪ.ˈnjuː.mə.reɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈnjuː.mə.reɪ.tə/ /; / /ɪ.ˈnuː.mə.reɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a person (or sometimes a device) who collects or counts data, especially for surveys or official records like a census

Examples

A enumerator collects data
During the national census, an enumerator knocked on our door to collect data.
Each enumerator received specialized training before starting the survey work.
The enumerator visited every house on the street to record the number of residents.
The enumerator visited every house in the neighborhood to collect census information.
Each enumerator was responsible for counting the number of people in their assigned area.
A digital enumerator can quickly tally large amounts of data in a survey.
