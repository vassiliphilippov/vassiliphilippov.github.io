observe

verb
show respect or commemorate by ceremony
3.4 7.98/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàz…ùv / (AmE), / …ôbÀàz…úÀêv / (BrE)
to follow, respect, or celebrate a rule, law, custom, or tradition by doing what is required

Examples

To observe a rule
He insisted that everyone observe the tradition by showing respect to their elders.
Many families gather together to observe religious holidays with ceremonies and rituals.
The school will observe a moment of silence to honor those who lost their lives.
It is important to observe cultural customs during international business meetings.
The company failed to observe the terms of the contract and was fined as a result.
You must always observe the safety regulations while operating this machinery.
All employees are required to observe the safety regulations at all times.
Many people still observe this tradition during the holiday season.
You must observe the rules if you want to participate in the competition.
