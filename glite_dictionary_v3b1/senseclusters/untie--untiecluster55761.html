untie

verb
make loose by undoing
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtaɪ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪ / (BrE)
to make something loose or free by undoing a knot, string, or fastening

Examples

To untie a knot
Can you help me untie this package?
It took him several minutes to untie the stubborn knot in the rope.
She stopped to untie her shoelaces before entering the house.
After sailing, we untied the ropes that secured the boat.
He struggled for a moment but finally untied the knot in his shoelace.
She carefully untied the ribbon on the gift box.
