reexpression

noun
renewed gene production
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪkˈsprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪkˈsprɛʃən / (BrE)
the renewed or restored production of a gene, protein, or other biological product after it was previously reduced or silenced

Examples

Reexpression of a gene
The scientist observed the reexpression of the protein after the cells were treated with the new drug.
Successful reexpression of the gene restored normal function in the experimental mice.
Researchers are studying how reexpression of certain genes can help reverse disease symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expression - the process by which a gene produces its product
  • antonym: silencing - the process of reducing or stopping gene expression
