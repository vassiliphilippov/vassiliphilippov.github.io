Nox

proper noun
Roman goddess of night
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑks / (AmE), / nɒks / (BrE)
The Roman goddess of night, daughter of Erebus, and counterpart of the Greek goddess Nyx.

Examples

Nox goddess
According to Roman mythology, Nox governed all the mysteries of the night.
In ancient art, Nox was often depicted with a dark veil and surrounded by stars.
The festival honored Nox as the bringer of dreams and shadows.
