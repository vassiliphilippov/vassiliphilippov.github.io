bleeding heart

noun
overly sympathetic or compassionate person
5.1 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbliːdɪŋ hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈbliːdɪŋ hɑːt / (BrE)
a person who shows excessive sympathy for people who are viewed as exploited or underprivileged, often with a tone of criticism or disapproval

Examples

A bleeding heart liberal
He was called a bleeding heart by critics who thought he cared too much about social issues.
Some politicians use the term bleeding heart to criticize those who support welfare programs.
The columnist dismissed the activists as just another bleeding heart trying to save the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hard-hearted - lacking sympathy or compassion
  • synonym: sentimentalist - a person who is excessively prone to feelings of tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
