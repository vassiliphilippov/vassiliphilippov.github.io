babytalk

noun
speech of very young children
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪbiˌtɔk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbiˌtɔːk / (BrE)
the early and simplified form of speech used by very young children as they begin to learn language

Examples

A child's babytalk
As children grow older, their babytalk gradually evolves into more complex sentences.
At eighteen months, her babytalk consisted mainly of single words and simple sounds.
Parents often find their child's babytalk both amusing and endearing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: childish speech - speech characteristic of children, especially in early development
