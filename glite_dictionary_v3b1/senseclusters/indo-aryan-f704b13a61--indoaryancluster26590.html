Indo-Aryan

noun
branch of Indo-Iranian languages
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndoʊˈɛriən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊˈɛəriən / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-Iranian family of languages, including Hindi, Bengali, Punjabi, and others spoken mainly in South Asia

Examples

Indo-Aryan languages
Hindi and Bengali are both Indo-Aryan languages spoken by millions of people.
Many Indo-Aryan tongues share common grammatical structures and vocabulary.
The Indo-Aryan branch is one of the largest subgroups of the Indo-European language family.
