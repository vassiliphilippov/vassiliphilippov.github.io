sylph

noun
mythical air spirit
8.1
Pronunciation: / sɪlf / (AmE), / sɪlf / (BrE)
A mythological spirit of the air, often depicted as a slender, graceful woman.

Examples

Air sylph spirit
In the ancient legend, a sylph glided through the morning sky, unseen by human eyes.
The dancer moved with the grace of a sylph, captivating the entire audience.
Victorian poets often described a sylph as a symbol of ethereal beauty and delicacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nymph - a mythological spirit of nature imagined as a beautiful maiden
  • synonym: sprite - a small or elusive supernatural being; a fairy
