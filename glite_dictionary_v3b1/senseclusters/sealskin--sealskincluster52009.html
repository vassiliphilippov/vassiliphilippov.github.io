sealskin

noun
the pelt or fur of a seal
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːlˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːlˌskɪn / (BrE)
the pelt or fur of a seal, especially the soft underfur, used as material for making garments and other items

Examples

Sealskin material
Her coat was made from luxurious sealskin and felt incredibly warm.
The trader displayed high-quality sealskin at the coastal market.
Traditional Inuit boots are often crafted with sealskin for insulation.
