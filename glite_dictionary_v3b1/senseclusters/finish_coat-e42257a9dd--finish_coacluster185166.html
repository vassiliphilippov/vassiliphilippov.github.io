finish coat

noun
final layer of coating material
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪnɪʃ koʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ kəʊt / (BrE)
the final layer of paint, plaster, varnish, or similar material applied to a surface to complete its covering or appearance

Examples

A finish coat layer
A high-quality finish coat gives the woodwork a polished appearance.
The painter carefully applied the finish coat to ensure a smooth, even surface.
This wall will be ready for the finish coat after the primer dries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: primer - a preparatory coating put on materials before painting
  • antonym: undercoat - a preparatory or base layer of paint or coating
