bogart

verb
to keep without sharing
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊɡɑrt / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɡɑːt / (BrE)
to selfishly keep something (especially something meant to be shared) and not let others use it

Examples

To bogart the remote
Don't bogart the popcorn—pass it around so everyone can have some.
He was known to bogart the remote control during movie nights with friends.
At the party, she accidentally bogarted the joint, forgetting to pass it to the next person.
Don't bogart the remote control—let someone else choose the channel for a change.
He tends to bogart the conversation and rarely lets others speak.
At the picnic, she bogarted all the best snacks without offering to share.

  • antonym: share - to give a portion to others
