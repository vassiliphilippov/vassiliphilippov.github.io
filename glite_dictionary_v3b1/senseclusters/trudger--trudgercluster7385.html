trudger

noun
person who walks laboriously
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌdʒər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌdʒə / (BrE)
A person who walks in a slow, heavy, and laborious way, typically because of tiredness or difficult conditions.

Examples

A trudger in snow
A line of trudgers moved silently along the snowy path, heads bent against the wind.
After hours on the trail, she felt like a weary trudger with aching feet.
He watched the lone trudger make his way up the muddy hill.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: marcher - person who walks in a march or protest
  • synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically because of exhaustion or harsh conditions
