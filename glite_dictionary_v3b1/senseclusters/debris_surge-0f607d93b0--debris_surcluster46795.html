debris surge

noun
sudden spread of dust and debris
Pronunciation: / dəˈbri sɝːdʒ / (AmE), / dəˈbriː sɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a sudden and powerful spread of dust and debris, especially following the collapse or demolition of a structure

Examples

Thick debris surge
Emergency crews struggled to see through the thick debris surge after the tower came down.
Residents described the debris surge as a wall of dust and rubble rushing toward them.
The documentary showed dramatic footage of a debris surge sweeping through the city streets.
