interstate highway

noun
U.S. national highway connecting major cities
3.5 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.tər.steɪt ˈhaɪ.weɪ/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.tə.steɪt ˈhaɪ.weɪ/ (BrE)
A major highway that is part of the United States national network linking major cities, typically crossing state boundaries.

Examples

The interstate highway system
Driving from Chicago to Denver, they spent most of the trip on the interstate highway.
The construction of the interstate highway system transformed American travel and commerce.
There was heavy traffic on the interstate highway during the holiday weekend.
