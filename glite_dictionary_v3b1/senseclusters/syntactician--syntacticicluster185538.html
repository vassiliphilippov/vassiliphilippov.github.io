syntactician

noun
expert in grammar and syntax
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪn.tækˈtɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsɪn.tækˈtɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a linguist who specializes in the study of syntax and the structure of sentences

Examples

A syntactician studies sentences
As a syntactician, she spends much of her time analyzing sentence structure in different languages.
He turned to a syntactician to clarify the grammatical rules involved in that construction.
The conference brought together renowned syntacticians from around the world to discuss recent developments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: linguist - a person skilled in or studying languages; language scientist
  • degree higher: semanticist - a linguist specializing in meaning and semantics
