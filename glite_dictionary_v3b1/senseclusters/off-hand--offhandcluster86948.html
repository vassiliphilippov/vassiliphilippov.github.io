off-hand

adverb
without prior thought or preparation
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhænd / (BrE)
without previous thought or preparation; immediately, without checking or planning

Examples

Answer off-hand
Do you know, off-hand, how much the project will cost?
I can't give you an answer off-hand, but I'll check and get back to you.
She recited the poem off-hand as if she'd memorized it years ago.
