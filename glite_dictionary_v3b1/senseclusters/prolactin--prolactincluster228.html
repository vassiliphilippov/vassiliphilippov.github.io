prolactin

noun
hormone stimulating milk production
6.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /proʊ.ˈlæk.tən/ /; / /proʊ.ˈlæk.tɪn/ (AmE), /prəʊ.ˈlæk.tɪn/ /; / /prəʊ.ˈlæk.tən/ (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates breast development and milk production after childbirth

Examples

Prolactin hormone levels
After childbirth, levels of prolactin rise to stimulate milk production.
Doctors may test prolactin levels to diagnose certain endocrine disorders.
High prolactin can cause symptoms such as irregular periods and infertility in women.
