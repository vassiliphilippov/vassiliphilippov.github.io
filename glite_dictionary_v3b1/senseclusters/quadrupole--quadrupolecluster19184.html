quadrupole

noun
second-order field effect
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːdrəˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒdrʊˌpəʊl / (BrE)
an arrangement of four electric charges or magnetic poles with alternating polarity, or the effect this arrangement produces in a field (quadrupole moment)

Examples

Quadrupole moment
The quadrupole moment of the nucleus affects the energy levels in certain atoms.
Scientists use quadrupole mass spectrometers to separate ions by their mass-to-charge ratio.
A quadrupole field can be created by arranging four magnets in a specific pattern.
The quadrupole moment is important in understanding the structure of certain molecules.
Physicists use a quadrupole magnet to focus particle beams in accelerators.
The device measures the quadrupole field generated by the arrangement of charges.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dipole - a pair of equal and opposite charges or magnetic poles separated by a distance
  • degree higher: octupole - an arrangement producing a third-order effect in a field or potential
