baler

noun
machine for making bales
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪlər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪlə / (BrE)
a machine (or person) that compresses and binds hay, straw, or similar materials into compact bales

Examples

The farmer operated the baler to collect and package the hay efficiently.
Modern recycling centers often use a baler to compress cardboard and paper for transport.

Modern farms often use a baler to quickly prepare hay for storage.
After cutting the straw, the workers loaded it into the baler for bundling.

A skilled baler can prepare dozens of bales in a single afternoon.
Modern farms often use an automatic baler to save time and labor.
