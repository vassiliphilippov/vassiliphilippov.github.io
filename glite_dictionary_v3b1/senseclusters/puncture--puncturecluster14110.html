to pierce or make a small hole in something with a sharp object, especially causing air or fluid to escape

Examples

To puncture a balloon
A stray branch punctured the inflatable kayak during our trip down the river.
He accidentally punctured the tire with a nail and had to replace it.
The doctor used a sharp instrument to puncture the abscess and release the fluid.
A sharp rock on the road can easily puncture a bicycle tire.
Be careful not to puncture the balloon with that needle.

If the pipe punctures, water will leak out onto the floor.
The balloon punctured when it touched the thorny bush.
While driving over the sharp rocks, the tire suddenly punctured and lost air.

Synonyms

  • prick
    3.9 1.01/m
    prick is a quick small hole from a sharp point, often causing a tiny wound, while puncture makes a hole that lets air or fluid escape.
  • pierce
    3.5 7.11/m
    pierce means to go through or make a hole with force, often passing fully through, while puncture refers to a smaller hole that causes leaking.
  • perforate
    6.1 0.59/m
    perforate means to make one or many holes, often for a clear line or pattern, while puncture is a single hole causing escape of air or fluid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burst - to break open suddenly and violently
  • synonym: pierce - to make a hole in or through something
  • antonym: seal - to close or make something airtight, preventing holes or leaks
