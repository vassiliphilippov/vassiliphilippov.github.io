cyanobacteria

noun
photosynthetic bacteria group (blue-green algae)
1.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /saɪ.ə.noʊ.bæk.ˈtɪr.ɪ.ə/ (AmE), /saɪ.ə.nəʊ.bæk.ˈtɪə.rɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a group of photosynthetic bacteria, also called blue-green algae, found in water and important for producing oxygen

Examples

Cyanobacteria produce oxygen
Cyanobacteria play a vital role in producing oxygen through photosynthesis in aquatic ecosystems.
Researchers have found evidence of ancient cyanobacteria in fossilized stromatolites.
Some cyanobacteria can form harmful blooms that affect water quality in lakes and reservoirs.
Cyanobacteria are believed to be among the oldest living organisms on Earth.
Blooms of cyanobacteria can produce toxins that threaten water quality.
Scientists study cyanobacteria to better understand the origins of photosynthesis.
