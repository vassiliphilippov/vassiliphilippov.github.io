plain sailing

noun
easy progress; no difficulties
5.0
Pronunciation: / pleɪn ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / pleɪn ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
used to say that something is easy to do and free from difficulties once started

Examples

Plain sailing after start
After months of preparation, the actual event turned out to be plain sailing.
Once the new software was installed, setting up the system was plain sailing.
The exam was difficult, but once I finished the first section, the rest was plain sailing.
