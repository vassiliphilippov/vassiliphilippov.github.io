wheat eel

noun
parasitic roundworm of wheat
Pronunciation: / wiːt iːl / (AmE), / wiːt iːl / (BrE)
a small roundworm (Anguina tritici) that is parasitic on wheat, causing damage to the crop

Examples

The wheat eel parasite
A microscope is needed to identify the presence of wheat eel in infected grains.
Farmers use crop rotation to prevent wheat eel from spreading in their wheat crops.
The infestation of wheat eel in the fields devastated the entire season's harvest.
