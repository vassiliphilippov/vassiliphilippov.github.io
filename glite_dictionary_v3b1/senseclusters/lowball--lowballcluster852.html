lowball

verb
offer or estimate a low figure
7.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈloʊ.bɑːl/ /; / /ˈloʊ.bɔːl/ (AmE), /ˈləʊ.bɔːl/ (BrE)
to deliberately offer or estimate a figure that is much lower than what is fair or expected, especially in order to gain an advantage in negotiations

Examples

To lowball an offer
She suspected the buyer was lowballing her offer just to see if she would accept less.
The company tried to lowball the repair estimate to win the customer's business.
They lowballed the construction bid in hopes of securing the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overbid - to offer too high a price or estimate
  • synonym: underbid - to offer to do something for a lower price than others
