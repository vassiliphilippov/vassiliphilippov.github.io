plot

verb
plan secretly (often illegal)
Pronunciation: / plɑt / (AmE), / plɒt / (BrE)
to plan something secretly, especially something illegal or harmful

Examples

To plot a crime
Several members of the group had plotted the robbery months in advance.
She was accused of plotting against her boss to get his job.
The generals plotted to overthrow the president and seize power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conspire - to make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful or harmful act
