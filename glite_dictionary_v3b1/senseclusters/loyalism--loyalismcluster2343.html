loyalism

noun
support for authority
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔɪəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪəlɪzəm / (BrE)
support for or allegiance to an established government, ruler, or authority, especially in times of conflict

Examples

Loyalism during war
During the revolution, loyalism divided communities and families across the country.
The rise of loyalism made it difficult for the rebels to gain widespread support.
His strong sense of loyalism kept him faithful to the monarchy despite growing unrest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loyalty - faithful support or allegiance to someone or something
  • synonym: patriotism - love for and devotion to one's country
  • antonym: rebellion - opposition to or uprising against authority
