hepatitis

noun
inflammation of the liver
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A serious disease in which the liver becomes inflamed, typically caused by a viral infection or exposure to a toxin.

Examples

Hepatitis virus infection
People with hepatitis may experience fatigue, nausea, and jaundice.
The doctor diagnosed him with hepatitis after observing his symptoms and reviewing his blood tests.
Vaccination can help protect you from viral forms of hepatitis.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cirrhosis - chronic liver disease with scarring
  • synonym: jaundice - yellowing of the skin due to liver disorder
