Mongol

proper noun
Central Asian ethnic group
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒŋɡɒl / (BrE)
a member of a Central Asian ethnic group known for establishing a vast empire under Genghis Khan in the 13th century

Examples

Mongol Empire
The Mongol Empire once stretched from Eastern Europe to the Sea of Japan.
Many historians study the military strategies of the Mongol armies.
Genghis Khan united the Mongol tribes into a powerful force.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Turkic - relating to the Turkic peoples of Central Asia
