clayeyness

noun
quality of resembling clay
Pronunciation: / ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.i.nɪs /; / ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being like or containing clay, especially in texture or composition

Examples

The clayeyness of soil
Artists appreciate the clayeyness of some modeling compounds for sculpting.
The clayeyness of the soil made it difficult for water to drain away.
The wine's flavor was influenced by the clayeyness of the vineyard's terrain.
Excessive clayeyness can lead to soil compaction and poor root growth in crops.
Gardeners must consider the clayeyness of their land before planting certain types of flowers.
The clayeyness of the soil makes it difficult for water to drain properly after heavy rain.
