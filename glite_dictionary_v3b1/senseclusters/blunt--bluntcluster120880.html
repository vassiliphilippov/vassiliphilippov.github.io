blunt

verb
make less intense or sharp
4.2 1.94/m
Pronunciation: / blÊŒnt / (AmE), / blÊŒnt / (BrE)
to make something less sharp, less intense, or less effective; to dull or weaken the force, feeling, or effect of something

Examples

To blunt the impact
His criticism was meant to blunt the enthusiasm of the supporters.
Repeated exposure to tragedy can blunt a person's emotional response.
The medication helped to blunt the pain after surgery.
Frequent use can blunt the edge of a good knife.
Over time, the weather blunted the sharp corners of the stone.
The critique was intended to blunt the impact of his opponent's argument.
His criticism was meant to blunt her enthusiasm, but she remained optimistic.
Repeated impacts can blunt the edge of an axe over time.
The constant chopping blunted the chef's knife.
