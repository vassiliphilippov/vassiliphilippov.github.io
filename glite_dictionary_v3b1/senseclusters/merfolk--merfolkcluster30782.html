merfolk

noun
mythical sea people
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜrˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈmɜːˌfəʊk / (BrE)
mythical beings with the upper body of a human and the tail of a fish, often said to live in the sea

Examples

Merfolk in stories
Ancient sailors believed that merfolk could control the weather at sea.
Stories about merfolk appear in the folklore of many coastal cultures.
The painting depicted a group of colorful merfolk swimming near a coral reef.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mermaid - a mythical female with a human upper body and a fish tail
  • synonym: merman - a mythical male with a human upper body and a fish tail
