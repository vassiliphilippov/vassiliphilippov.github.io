to crush, press, or bend something so that it becomes wrinkled or creased; to become wrinkled or creased

Examples

To crumple a shirt
If you throw your shirt on the floor, it will crumple and look messy.
Leaves often crumple during a drought as they lose moisture.
The letter began to crumple after being stored carelessly in his pocket.
He crumpled the paper into a ball and tossed it in the bin.
She accidentally crumpled the letter before throwing it away.
The fabric crumples easily if you don't hang it up right after washing.

Synonyms

  • rumple
    5.4 0.80/m
    rumple means to make fabric or hair messy with soft folds, while crumple implies stronger crushing into tight creases.
  • crease
    2.3 2.43/m
    crease makes a single straight fold or line by pressing, while crumple means to crush something into many uneven folds.
  • scrunch
    5.5 0.87/m
    scrunch is an informal squeeze that crushes small areas, while crumple often means folding or crushing a whole object roughly.
  • crinkle
    3.7 1.46/m
    crinkle makes many small fine lines or textures, while crumple means to crush into deeper, larger wrinkles or folds.
  • pucker
    5.2 1.73/m
    pucker gathers a surface into tight small folds, usually at one spot, while crumple means to crush the whole thing more loosely.
  • cockle
    9.5 0.00/m
    cockle makes a surface wavy or rippled, often from moisture, while crumple means to crush and crease by pressure.
