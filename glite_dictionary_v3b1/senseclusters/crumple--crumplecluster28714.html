crumple

verb
become wrinkled or creased
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to crush, press, or bend something so that it becomes wrinkled or creased; to become wrinkled or creased

Examples

To crumple a shirt
If you throw your shirt on the floor, it will crumple and look messy.
Leaves often crumple during a drought as they lose moisture.
The letter began to crumple after being stored carelessly in his pocket.
He crumpled the paper into a ball and tossed it in the bin.
She accidentally crumpled the letter before throwing it away.
The fabric crumples easily if you don't hang it up right after washing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crease - to become wrinkled or marked by lines; to make a line or fold in something, especially paper or fabric
  • antonym: smooth - to make or become even, flat, or free of wrinkles
