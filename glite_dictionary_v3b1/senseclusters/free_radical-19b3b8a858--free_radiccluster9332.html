free radical

noun
highly reactive molecule with unpaired electron
Pronunciation: / fri ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / fri ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
an atom or group of atoms with at least one unpaired electron, making it highly reactive; commonly refers to such molecules in the body that can damage cells

Examples

A free radical molecule
Exposure to pollution can increase the number of free radicals in your system.
Scientists study how free radicals contribute to aging and disease.
Vitamin C is thought to protect the body by neutralizing free radicals before they cause cell damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radical - an atom or group of atoms behaving as a unit in a chemical reaction
