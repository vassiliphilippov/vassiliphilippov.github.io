Oregon maple

noun
maple tree native to western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrɪɡən ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A maple tree native to western North America, known for its large five-lobed leaves that turn orange in autumn.

Examples

A large Oregon maple
A large Oregon maple shaded the park bench where we sat.
Many birds build their nests in the branches of the Oregon maple.
The Oregon maple is admired for its vibrant foliage during the autumn months.
