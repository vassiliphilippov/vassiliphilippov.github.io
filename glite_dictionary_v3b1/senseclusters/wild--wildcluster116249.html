wild

adjective
not tamed, domesticated, or cultivated
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /waɪld/ (AmE), /waɪld/ (BrE)
(of animals, plants, people, or places) living or existing in a natural state; not tamed, domesticated, or influenced by humans; untamed or remote

Examples

Wild animals
Many hikers enjoy picking wild berries along the trail.
The hen kept her wild instincts despite being bred in captivity.
The sanctuary protects several species of wild flowers.
Historians described the early settlers as trying to bring order to wild regions.
Many explorers wrote about encountering wild tribes living in remote areas.
The young man grew up in a wild environment, far from the reach of modern society.
The expedition took them through a wild region where no one had lived for centuries.
Travelers are warned to be cautious in the wild parts of the country, where help is far away.
We spent the night camping on a wild hillside overlooking the valley.
