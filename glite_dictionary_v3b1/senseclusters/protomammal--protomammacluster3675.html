protomammal

noun
extinct ancestor group of mammals
8.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtoʊˈmæməl / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈmaməl / (BrE)
an extinct animal from a group believed to be ancestral to modern mammals, typically warm-blooded and having some mammalian characteristics

Examples

Ancient protomammals
Many paleontologists believe that protomammals had fur and laid eggs.
Recent discoveries have shed light on how protomammals adapted to changing environments millions of years ago.
The fossil record provides evidence that protomammals bridged the evolutionary gap between reptiles and mammals.
