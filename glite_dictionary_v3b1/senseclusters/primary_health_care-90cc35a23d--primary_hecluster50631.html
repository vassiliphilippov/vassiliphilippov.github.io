primary health care

noun
first-contact basic health care
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri hɛlθ kɛr / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri hɛlθ kɛː / (BrE)
basic health care provided as the first point of contact between a patient and the health care system, usually by general practitioners or community clinics.

Examples

Primary health care services
Primary health care is essential for maintaining overall community well-being.
Access to quality primary health care can prevent more serious health problems later.
Most families rely on primary health care providers for their routine medical needs.
