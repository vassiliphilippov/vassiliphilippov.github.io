pull

noun
act of exerting force to move something
1.3
Pronunciation: / pʊl / (AmE), / pʊl / (BrE)
the act of moving something toward yourself or in a direction by force; a sustained effort to achieve something

Examples

A strong pull
It took a strong pull to open the heavy door.
She gave the rope a sharp pull and the bucket rose from the well.
The car needed an extra pull to get up the steep driveway.
After a long pull, the team finally reached the summit.
Completing the marathon was a real pull for everyone involved.
We still have a tough pull ahead if we want to finish the project on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • antonym: push - the act of exerting force to move something away from oneself
  • synonym: struggle - a determined effort to achieve or resist something
