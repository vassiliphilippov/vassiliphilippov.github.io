GHB

noun
prescription sodium oxybate for narcolepsy
7.9
Pronunciation: /dʒiː eɪtʃ biː/ (AmE), /dʒiː eɪtʃ biː/ (BrE)
(medicine) a prescription form of gamma-hydroxybutyrate (the sodium salt, sodium oxybate) used under medical supervision to treat narcolepsy and cataplexy.

Examples

Prescription GHB treatment
The neurologist prescribed GHB to help control his cataplexy and daytime sleepiness.
During the trial, participants received GHB (sodium oxybate) under close medical supervision at the clinic.
Because GHB is a controlled medicine, pharmacists must verify prescriptions before dispensing it.
