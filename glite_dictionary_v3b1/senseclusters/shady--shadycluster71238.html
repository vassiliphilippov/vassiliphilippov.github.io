shady

adjective
full of or sheltered by shade
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪdi / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdi / (BrE)
(of a place) sheltered from sunlight or covered with shade; pleasantly dark or cool.

Examples

Shady spot
She preferred the shady side of the street to avoid direct sunlight.
The garden is cool and shady, making it perfect for summer picnics.
We chose a shady bench under the trees to escape the afternoon heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shadowy - full of or resembling shadows; dim or faintly lit
  • antonym: sunny - bright with sunlight; exposed to sunlight
