swine influenza

noun
contagious respiratory disease in pigs
Pronunciation: / swaɪn ɪnˈfluːənzə / (AmE), / swaɪn ɪnˈfluːənt͡sə / (BrE)
an acute, highly contagious respiratory disease of pigs caused by an orthomyxovirus, sometimes transmissible to humans

Examples

Swine influenza in pigs
Farm workers were vaccinated to prevent the spread of swine influenza among the herd.
Scientists studied the genetic similarity between swine influenza and human flu viruses.
The outbreak of swine influenza led to new biosecurity measures on the farm.
