chatter

verb
talk rapidly about trivial matters
3.7 2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃætər /; / ˈtʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
to talk quickly and continuously about unimportant or trivial things

Examples

To chatter on phone
She tends to chatter on the phone about nothing in particular for hours.
The audience began to chatter noisily before the show started.
The children chattered excitedly during the whole bus ride.
Despite the serious topic, the group couldn't help but chatter nervously to break the tension.
The students chattered excitedly before class began, barely noticing the teacher's arrival.
Whenever the team meets for coffee, they chatter about their weekend plans and family news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk quickly and in a confused or silly way
  • antonym: be silent - to not speak; to refrain from talking
  • synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly, informal way
  • synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others
  • synonym: prattle - to talk in a foolish or simple-minded way
