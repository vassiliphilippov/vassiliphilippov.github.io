squander

verb
waste recklessly, especially money or time
6.5 3.56/m
Pronunciation: / Ààskw…ëÀênd…ö /; / Ààskw…ënd…ö / (AmE), / Ààskw…índ…ô / (BrE)
to waste or use something, such as money, time, or opportunities, in a reckless or foolish way

Examples

To squander money
Don't squander your opportunities by being careless.
He managed to squander his entire inheritance in less than two years.
She regretted the way she had squandered her money during her youth.
Despite his talent, he squanders every opportunity that comes his way.
If you squander your money on unnecessary things, you might regret it later.
She squandered her chance to study abroad by missing the application deadline.
