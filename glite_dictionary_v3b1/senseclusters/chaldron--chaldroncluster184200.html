chaldron

noun
old British measure of capacity (36 bushels)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.drən / (AmE), / ˈkæl.drən / (BrE)
a former British measure of capacity for coal and other dry goods, equal to 36 bushels.

Examples

A chaldron of coal
A shipment of three chaldrons of coal arrived at the port.
Coal was once measured in chaldrons throughout much of England.
The old records list the produce by the chaldron rather than by the ton.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bushel - a unit of dry capacity equal to 8 gallons or 4 pecks
