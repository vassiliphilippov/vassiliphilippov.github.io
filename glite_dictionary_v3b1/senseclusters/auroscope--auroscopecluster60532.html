auroscope

noun
instrument for examining the ear
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəsəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument with a magnifying lens and light, used for examining the external ear, particularly the ear canal and eardrum.

Examples

A auroscope device
Medical students practiced handling the auroscope during their clinical training.
Proper use of an auroscope allows for a clear view of the tympanic membrane.
The doctor used an auroscope to check the child's ear for signs of infection.
