furunculosis

noun
disease with multiple boils
6.3 9.8
Pronunciation: /fjuː.rən.kjə.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ /; / /fɜːr.ən.kjə.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /fjuː.rən.kjə.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ /; / /fjuː.rən.kjʊ.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
An acute skin disease characterized by the presence of multiple furuncles (boils) on the body.

Examples

Chronic furunculosis
Chronic furunculosis can be caused by recurring bacterial infections of the skin.
Patients with furunculosis often need antibiotics to treat the multiple boils.
The infection quickly developed into furunculosis, requiring medical intervention.
