repurchase

verb
buy something again, especially after selling it
5.3 0.25/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàp…úrt É…ôs / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàp…úÀêt É…ôs / (BrE)
to buy back something that was previously sold, lost, or given away

Examples

To repurchase a house
After years abroad, she managed to repurchase her family home.
He regretted selling his guitar and wanted to repurchase it from its new owner.
The company decided to repurchase its shares from investors.

Synonyms

  • buy back
    3.4 1.04/m
    buy back is the everyday phrasal verb used when someone purchases an item again, while repurchase is more formal.
  • rebuy
    4.3 0.00/m
    rebuy means to purchase something again in everyday speech, while repurchase sounds more formal and appears in writing.
  • reacquire
    5.5 0.24/m
    reacquire often means getting something back by any method, while repurchase specifically means buying it back.
