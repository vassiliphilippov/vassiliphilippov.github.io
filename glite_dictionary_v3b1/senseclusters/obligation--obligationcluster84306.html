obligation

noun
sense of duty due to being indebted
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːbləˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌɑː.blɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌɒb.lɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a duty, responsibility, or feeling that you must do something, often because of law, morality, or because someone has helped you

Examples

A moral obligation
He felt a strong obligation to help her after all she had done for him.
Their kindness created an obligation in her to support them in times of need.
You shouldn't feel any obligation to return the favor, but it's appreciated if you do.
She finally paid off her debt and was free from her financial obligations.
Under the contract, he is under an obligation to deliver the goods on time.
You have a legal obligation to report any changes in your circumstances.
As a citizen, you have an obligation to obey the law.
He felt a strong obligation to help his neighbors during the crisis.
Social obligation often guides people's behavior even when there is no legal requirement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debt - a feeling of owing something, especially gratitude or a favor, to someone
  • synonym: duty - something that one is required to do by moral or legal obligation
  • antonym: freedom - the state of not being constrained or obligated
  • synonym: gratitude - the feeling of being thankful for a service or favor
  • synonym: liability - legal responsibility for something, especially a debt
  • antonym: privilege - a special right or advantage
  • synonym: responsibility - the state or fact of having a duty to deal with something or of having control over someone
