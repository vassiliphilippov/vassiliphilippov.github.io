alginate

noun
thickening agent
Pronunciation: / ˈældʒɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈælɡɪneɪt / (BrE)
a substance (a salt of alginic acid) derived from seaweed, used as a thickener or gelling agent in food, medicine, and industry

Examples

Alginate thickener
Many processed foods contain alginate to improve their texture and stability.
Dentists often use alginate to create molds of patients' teeth for dental impressions.
Wound dressings made with alginate help absorb fluids and promote healing.

Many vegan desserts use alginate as a substitute for animal-based gelatin.
