The act of blaming or criticizing oneself, especially for failing to meet one's own standards or expectations.

Examples

Self-condemnation after failure
After failing the exam, he was overwhelmed by self-condemnation and regret.
Her constant self-condemnation made it difficult for her to move past her mistakes.
Rather than practicing self-condemnation, try to learn from your errors and grow.

Synonyms

  • self-reproach
    7.1 0.11/m
    self-reproach is a feeling of guilt for a specific wrong, while self-condemnation is a harsher moral judgment of oneself.
  • self-reproof
    8.9 0.00/m
    self-reproof is a milder, inward blaming for a mistake, while self-condemnation is harsher moral judgment of oneself.
  • self-criticism
    5.1 0.58/m
    self-criticism is judging one's actions to improve or learn, while self-condemnation is harsh blaming that attacks a person's worth.
  • self-accusation
    7.6 0.02/m
    self-accusation is openly admitting fault or blaming oneself, while self-condemnation is a deeper, harsher moral judgment.
