microprobe

noun
instrument for tiny samples
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌproʊb / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌprəʊb / (BrE)
a scientific instrument used to analyze the composition of very small samples of material

Examples

Electron microprobe
The scientist used a microprobe to determine the chemical makeup of the tiny crystal.
A microprobe can analyze samples that are too small for traditional laboratory equipment.
With the help of a microprobe, researchers identified trace elements in the meteorite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microscope - an instrument used to view very small objects
  • synonym: spectrometer - an instrument used to measure properties of light or other radiation
