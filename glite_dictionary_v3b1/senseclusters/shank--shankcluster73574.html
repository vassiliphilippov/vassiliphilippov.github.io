shank

noun
poor golf shot hit with heel of club
5.2
Pronunciation: / ʃæŋk / (AmE), / ʃæŋk / (BrE)
(golf) A poor stroke in which the heel of the club strikes the ball, causing it to veer off course.

Examples

Golf shank mistake
A sudden shank can ruin an otherwise perfect round of golf.
Even professional players sometimes hit a shank under pressure.
He was frustrated after hitting a shank on the final hole.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slice - A golf shot that curves dramatically to the right (for a right-handed player)
