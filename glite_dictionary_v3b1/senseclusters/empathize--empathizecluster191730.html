empathize

verb
understand another's feelings
2.6 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈem.pə.θaɪz/ (AmE), /ˈem.pə.θaɪz/ (BrE)
to understand and share another person's feelings or experiences

Examples

To empathize with others
As a counselor, she often empathizes with students facing academic challenges.
He tried to empathize by imagining what it must feel like to move to a new country.
It's important to empathize with people going through difficult times.
If you want to help effectively, you must first empathise with their situation.
It's important for managers to empathise with their employees during difficult times.
She found it easy to empathise with the characters in the novel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antipathy - a strong feeling of dislike
  • synonym: empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another person (American spelling)
  • synonym: sympathise - to feel or express sympathy or compassion for someone
  • synonym: sympathize - to share in someone else's feelings, especially sorrow or trouble
