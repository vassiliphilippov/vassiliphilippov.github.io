snarl

verb
make complicated or tangled
Pronunciation: / snɑrl / (AmE), / snɑːl / (BrE)
to make something more complicated, confused, or tangled, especially by entangling things together

Examples

To snarl up traffic
Careless wiring can snarl up even the most organized workspace.
Negotiations were snarled by conflicting demands from the parties involved.
Rush hour always tends to snarl traffic in the city center.
It was frustrating to find the fishing lines snarled together after storing them carelessly.
She accidentally snarled the threads while sewing the patch onto her jeans.
The headphones snarled themselves in my backpack and took ages to untangle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entangle - to twist together or entwine so as to be difficult to separate
  • synonym: tangle - to twist together in a confused mass
  • antonym: untangle - to free from tangles or complications
