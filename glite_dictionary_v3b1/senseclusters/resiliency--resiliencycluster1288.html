resiliency

noun
ability of material to return to original shape
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪliənsi / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪliənsi / (BrE)
the ability of a material to return to its original shape or position after being bent, stretched, or compressed, provided the limits of elasticity are not exceeded

Examples

Resiliency of rubber
Engineers tested the resiliency of the alloy under repeated stress.
Foam mattresses are valued for their resiliency and comfort.
The resiliency of rubber allows it to bounce back after being stretched.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brittleness - the quality of breaking easily without significant deformation
  • synonym: elasticity - the quality of a material to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed
