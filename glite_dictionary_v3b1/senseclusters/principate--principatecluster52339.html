principate

noun
rule of a prince
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪnsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
the period, office, or system of government in ancient Rome when emperors held power, starting with Augustus

Examples

Roman principate
Augustus established the principate as the first emperor of Rome.
Historians often debate the true nature of the Roman principate and its impact on governance.
During the principate, the emperor held ultimate authority while maintaining republican traditions.
The principate began with Augustus and marked a new era in Roman history.
Historians often compare the principate to the later dominate period of the Roman Empire.
Under the principate, emperors maintained the appearance of republican government.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dominate - the later period of Roman imperial rule with more autocratic power
  • synonym: empire - a group of states or territories controlled by one ruler
  • synonym: monarchy - a system of government with a monarch at the head
  • synonym: principality - the territory or jurisdiction ruled by a prince
  • antonym: republic - a state in which supreme power is held by the people and their elected representatives
