lame

adjective
weak or unconvincing (excuse, argument, etc.)
1.5 5.5
Pronunciation: /leɪm/ (AmE), /leɪm/ (BrE)
pathetically lacking in force, effectiveness, or conviction; weak, unconvincing, or disappointing (especially of excuses, arguments, or things)

Examples

Lame excuse
Her lame argument failed to persuade the committee.
That was a lame excuse for being late and no one believed him.
They gave a lame explanation that didn't make any sense.
The movie was so lame that we left before it ended.
His excuse for being late was really lame and nobody believed him.
I thought the party would be fun, but it turned out to be pretty lame.
