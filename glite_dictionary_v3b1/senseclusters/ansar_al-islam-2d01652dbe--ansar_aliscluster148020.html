Ansar al-Islam

proper noun
radical Islamist group in northern Iraq
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑnˈsɑr æl ɪsˈlɑm / (AmE), / ænˈsɑːr æl ɪsˈlɑːm / (BrE)
A militant Islamist group active in northern Iraq (primarily Kurdistan), known for opposing the establishment of an independent secular state and for links to Taliban and al-Qaeda networks.

Examples

Ansar al-Islam group
Intelligence reports claimed that Ansar al-Islam received funding from Saddam Hussein's regime.
Several members of Ansar al-Islam reportedly had connections with al-Qaeda operatives.
The U.S. military linked an attack to Ansar al-Islam militants in the region.
