pressure

noun
influence or force to persuade

the use of influence or force to persuade or compel someone to act in a certain way

Examples

Political pressure
Political pressure forced the mayor to resign from office.
She finally gave in to the pressure from her peers and agreed to join the club.
The employees felt enormous pressure to meet the project's deadline.

Synonyms

  • coercion
    6.9 5.94/m
    coercion uses force or threats to make someone obey, while pressure means general influence or force used to make someone act.
  • arm-twisting
    6.3 0.18/m
    arm-twisting is an informal phrase for using strong personal pressure on someone, while pressure is the general act of pushing someone to act.
  • suasion
    9.9 0.17/m
    suasion is formal persuasive talk that aims to change minds, while pressure covers both gentle persuasion and force to compel action.
  • urging
    3.9 2.01/m
    urging is a strong plea or encouragement to act quickly, while pressure is a broader use of influence or force to make someone act.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats
  • synonym: persuasion - the action or process of convincing someone to do or believe something
