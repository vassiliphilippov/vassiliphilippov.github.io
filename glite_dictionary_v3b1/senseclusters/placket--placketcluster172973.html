placket

noun
cloth piece sewn under an opening in clothing
6.1 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplækɪt / (AmE), / ˈplækɪt / (BrE)
a part of a garment (usually a piece of fabric or a finished opening) that covers, strengthens, or decorates an opening, often where buttons or fasteners are placed

Examples

A placket opening
She chose a blouse with a decorative placket covering the buttons.
The designer added a contrasting placket to give the dress a unique look.
The shirt's placket was reinforced to prevent it from tearing.
The tailor added a hidden placket to the dress for a cleaner look.
Her blouse features a decorative placket with small pearl buttons.
He struggled to fasten the placket on his new shirt before the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vent - a slit in a garment to allow movement or ventilation
