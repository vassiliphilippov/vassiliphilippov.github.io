biosafety level 4

noun
highest biosafety risk laboratory category
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˌseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl fɔr / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˌseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl fɔː / (BrE)
The highest level of biosafety precautions, used for work with highly dangerous and exotic infectious agents that pose a high risk of life-threatening disease and for which there is no available vaccine or therapy.

Examples

Biosafety level 4 protocols
Strict protocols are enforced at all times in facilities designated as biosafety level 4.
The research on Ebola virus can only be conducted in a biosafety level 4 laboratory.
Very few countries have operational biosafety level 4 labs due to the extreme risks involved.
