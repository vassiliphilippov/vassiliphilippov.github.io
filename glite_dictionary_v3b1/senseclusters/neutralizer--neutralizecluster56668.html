neutralizer

noun
counteracting agent
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance or device that cancels, counteracts, or makes something neutral or less harmful

Examples

Chemical neutralizer
The scientist used a neutralizer to stop the chemical reaction.
A water filter often contains a neutralizer to balance acidity.
The technician added a neutralizer to the mixture to prevent damage.
The laboratory added a neutralizer to the solution to make it safe for disposal.
Many cleaning products contain a neutralizer to reduce strong odors.
A neutralizer is used in water treatment plants to balance pH levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts a poison
  • antonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction
