Pocahontas

proper noun
ship name
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊkəˈhɑːntəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒkəˈhɒntəs / (BrE)
the name given to various ships, especially naval and civilian vessels, often in honor of the historical figure Pocahontas

Examples

USS Pocahontas
The navy commissioned a new vessel called the Pocahontas in honor of the famous historical figure.
During the war, the crew boarded the transport named Pocahontas for the long sea voyage.
The old photograph showed the Pocahontas docked at the busy harbor in 1918.
