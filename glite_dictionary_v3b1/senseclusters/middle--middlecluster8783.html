middle

adjective
of an intermediate historical linguistic stage
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl / (BrE)
(of a stage in the development of a language or literature) occurring between an earlier and a later stage.

Examples

Middle English period
Researchers are currently examining texts from the Middle Gaelic era for evidence of syntactic shifts.
Scholars often study Middle English to understand the transition from Old to Modern English.
The epic was written during the Middle Dutch period, reflecting linguistic changes of the time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: early - of or in the beginning part of a period, process, or series
  • antonym: late - of or in the final period or stage
