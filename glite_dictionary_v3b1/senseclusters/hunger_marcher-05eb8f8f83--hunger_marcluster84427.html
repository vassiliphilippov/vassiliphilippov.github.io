hunger marcher

noun
protester against hunger or unemployment
5.1 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhʌŋ.gər ˈmɑːr.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈhʌŋ.gə ˈmɑː.tʃə/ (BrE)
a person, typically unemployed, who takes part in a protest march against hunger or unemployment, especially during times of economic hardship.

Examples

Hunger marchers protest
During the Great Depression, many hunger marchers demanded food and jobs from the government.
Several hunger marchers spoke passionately about their struggles at the rally.
The arrival of the hunger marchers in the capital drew national attention to the crisis.
