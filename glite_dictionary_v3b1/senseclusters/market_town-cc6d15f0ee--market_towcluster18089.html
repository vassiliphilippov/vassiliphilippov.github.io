market town

noun
a town authorized to hold markets
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkət taʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt taʊn / (BrE)
A town, typically smaller in size, that has the legal right or custom to hold a public market at regular intervals.

Examples

A market town
Many English market towns still host weekly markets in their town squares.
She grew up in a quiet market town surrounded by countryside.
The historic market town attracts visitors with its lively Saturday traditions.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: city - a large town, often with more extensive privileges and population
  • degree lower: village - a small settlement, usually without the right to hold markets
