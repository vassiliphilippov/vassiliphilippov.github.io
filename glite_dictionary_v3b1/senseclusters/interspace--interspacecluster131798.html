interspace

noun
gap between objects
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪntəspeɪs /; / ˈɪntəˌspeɪs / (BrE)
a space or gap between things (such as objects, parts, events, or periods)

Examples

An interspace between walls
The interspace between the two buildings was filled with plants.
Dust collected in the interspace between the shelves and the wall.
A narrow interspace allowed light to pass through the closed curtains.
The surgeon carefully inserted the needle into the interspace between the patient's ribs.
She used the short interspace between rehearsals to warm up her voice and stretch.
Plants were arranged with enough interspace to allow sunlight to reach each one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gap - an empty space or opening between two things
  • synonym: interval - a space between two points or objects
