profound

adjective
showing great intellectual or emotional depth
Pronunciation: / prəˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / prəˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a feeling, statement, or quality) showing great knowledge, insight, or emotional depth.

Examples

Profound thought
His speech expressed a profound sense of loss and hope.
She was moved by his profound understanding of human nature.
The book made a profound impact on me.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deep - extending far down from the top or surface; also used to mean intellectually or emotionally profound
  • antonym: superficial - lacking depth or thoroughness, opposite of profound
