divine right of kings

noun
belief that monarchs rule by God's will
6.1 8.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈvaɪn raɪt əv kɪŋz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈvaɪn raɪt ə kɪŋz/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈvaɪn raɪt əv kɪŋz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈvaɪn raɪt ə kɪŋz/ (BrE)
the political doctrine that monarchs derive their authority to rule directly from God and are not answerable to their subjects

Examples

The divine right of kings doctrine
In British history, the Stuarts strongly advocated the divine right of kings in the 17th century.
Many monarchs in Europe once claimed the divine right of kings to justify their rule.
The divine right of kings was challenged during the Enlightenment as ideas about democracy spread.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolute monarchy - a form of monarchy in which the ruler has absolute power
  • antonym: constitutional monarchy - a monarchy limited by laws or a constitution
