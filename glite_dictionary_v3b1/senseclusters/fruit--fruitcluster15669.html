fruit

verb
to produce or cause to bear fruit
4.4 0.53/m
Pronunciation: / fruÀêt / (AmE), / fruÀêt / (BrE)
(of a plant or tree) to produce fruit

Examples

Apple trees fruit yearly
If apple trees are pruned correctly, they will fruit more abundantly each year.
The greenhouse conditions encouraged the plants to fruit earlier than usual.
With proper care, even young vines can be made to fruit in their second season.
If the conditions are right, the plant will fruit abundantly in late summer.
The apple trees finally fruited after several years of careful cultivation.
This species of cactus only fruits once every five years.
