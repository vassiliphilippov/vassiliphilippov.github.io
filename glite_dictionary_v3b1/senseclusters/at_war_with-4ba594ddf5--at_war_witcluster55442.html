at war with

preposition
in conflict with
Pronunciation: / ət wɔɹ wɪθ / (AmE), / ət wɔː wɪð / (BrE)
in a state of conflict or open warfare with someone or something

Examples

At war with feelings
She often feels at war with her own emotions.
The company was at war with its main competitor throughout the year.
The two countries have been at war with each other for over a decade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: at peace with - not in conflict with; in a harmonious relationship with
