wring out

verb
extract liquid by twisting or squeezing
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ aʊt /; / ɹɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / rɪŋ aʊt /; / ɹɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove liquid from something (especially cloth) by twisting or squeezing it tightly

Examples

To wring out a towel
After washing the towel, make sure to wring out all the excess water.
He wrung out his wet socks before hanging them up to dry.
She wrung out the dishcloth and wiped down the counter.
After washing the shirt, make sure you wring out all the excess water before hanging it.
He wrung out the cleaning cloth and started wiping the table.
She tried to wring out the wet towel quickly.
