GFLOPS

noun
billion floating point operations per second (computer speed)
8.0
Pronunciation: / dʒiː ˈflɑps / (AmE), / dʒiː ˈflɒps / (BrE)
A unit for measuring computer processing speed, equal to one billion floating point operations per second.

Examples

High GFLOPS speed
Modern graphics cards can easily achieve several GFLOPS during complex rendering operations.
Researchers measured the simulation's peak speed at 1,200 GFLOPS.
The new processor is capable of performing 600 GFLOPS, making it suitable for high-performance tasks.
