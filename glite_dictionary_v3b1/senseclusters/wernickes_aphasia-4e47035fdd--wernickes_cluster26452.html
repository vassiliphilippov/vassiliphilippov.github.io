Wernicke's aphasia

noun
type of aphasia with impaired comprehension
9.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…úrn…™k…ôz …ôÀàfe…™zi…ô / (AmE), / Ààv…úÀên…™k…ôz …ôÀàfe…™zi…ô / (BrE)
A type of aphasia characterized by fluent but unintelligible speech and severe difficulties in understanding spoken or written language.

Examples

Wernicke's aphasia symptoms
Patients with Wernicke's aphasia often produce fluent speech that lacks meaning.
The neurologist diagnosed her with Wernicke's aphasia after noticing her severe comprehension difficulties.
Treatment for Wernicke's aphasia focuses on improving language understanding and communication skills.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.00/m

  • fluent aphasia
    8.6 0.01/m
    fluent aphasia is used for cases with smooth but nonsensical speech and severe comprehension loss, while Wernicke's aphasia is the specific diagnosis.
  • sensory aphasia
    9.2 0.01/m
    sensory aphasia is an older medical term for fluent, meaningless speech with major comprehension problems, while Wernicke's aphasia is the modern clinical name.
  • impressive aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    impressive aphasia is a rare, older label for fluent but meaningless speech with poor understanding, while Wernicke's aphasia is the standard term.
