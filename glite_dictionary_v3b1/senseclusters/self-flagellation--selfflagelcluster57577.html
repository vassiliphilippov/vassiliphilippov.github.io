self-flagellation

noun
self-punishment by whipping
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf flædʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf fladʒɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of punishing oneself by whipping, often as a form of religious discipline or self-punishment

Examples

Self-flagellation practice
Discussions of self-flagellation often arise in the context of extreme religious rituals.
Some medieval monks practiced self-flagellation as an expression of repentance and devotion.
The character’s self-flagellation revealed his deep sense of guilt and desire for atonement.
