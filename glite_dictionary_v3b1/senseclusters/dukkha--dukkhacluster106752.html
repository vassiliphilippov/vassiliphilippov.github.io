dukkha

noun
Buddhist suffering
Pronunciation: / ˈduːkə / (AmE), / ˈdʊkə / (BrE)
in Buddhism, the idea that life is full of suffering, dissatisfaction, or unease

Examples

Dukkha in Buddhism
Buddhism teaches that dukkha is an unavoidable part of human existence.
He reflected on the dukkha he experienced during difficult times in his life.
Understanding dukkha can help people find peace and acceptance.
The Buddha taught that dukkha is an unavoidable part of human existence.
Understanding dukkha is essential for anyone studying Buddhist philosophy.
Meditation can help people cope with the dukkha they experience in daily life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction
  • antonym: nirvana - the ultimate state of liberation from suffering in Buddhism
  • synonym: suffering - the state of undergoing pain, distress, or hardship
