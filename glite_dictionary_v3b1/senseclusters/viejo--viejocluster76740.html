viejo

noun
affectionate old man
Pronunciation: / ˈbjexoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjehəʊ / (BrE)
a Spanish term used in English to refer affectionately to an old man, especially a grandfather or elder male relative

Examples

His viejo smiles
He always greeted his viejo with a warm hug and a smile.
In their family, everyone respected the viejo for his wisdom and experience.
She called her grandfather viejo as a sign of affection and closeness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elder - an older person, often respected in a family or community
  • synonym: grandpa - an informal term for grandfather
