turret

noun
small tower on a building
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜːrət / (AmE), / ˈtʌrɪt / (BrE)
a small tower that projects vertically from the wall of a building, typically at a corner or angle, often seen in castles and fortifications

Examples

Castle turret
A narrow staircase led up to the turret where the guard kept watch.
Many Victorian houses feature a round turret at the corner, adding character to the building.
The castle's stone turret offered a panoramic view of the surrounding countryside.
