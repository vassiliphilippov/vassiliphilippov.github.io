Synonyms

  • simultaneously
    4.2 30/m
    simultaneously is a formal adverb for events happening exactly at the same instant, while at once is more general.
  • at the same time
    1.0 80/m
    at the same time describes two actions happening in the same moment, while at once is a shorter, common phrase.
  • concurrently
    5.8 2/m
    concurrently is a formal term for actions that overlap in time, while at once suggests exact simultaneous moments.
  • all at once
    2.1 2/m
    all at once often suggests many things happen together suddenly, while at once simply means at the same time.
  • together
    1.0 20/m
    together means people or items are with each other or act as one, while at once focuses on time.
  • at one time
    2.5 2/m
    at one time can mean during the same period or formerly in older use, while at once means now or simultaneously.
