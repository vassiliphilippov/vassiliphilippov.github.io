obedient

adjective
dutiful; willing to obey authority
1.0 3/m
Pronunciation: / o äÀàbiÀêdi…ônt / (AmE), / …ô äÀàbiÀêdi…ônt / (BrE)
willing to do what one is told by someone in authority; dutifully following commands or instructions.

Examples

Obedient child
She was always obedient to the rules set by her parents.
Soldiers are expected to be obedient to their commanding officers at all times.
The obedient child quickly cleaned up his toys when asked.

Synonyms

  • compliant
    5.1 3/m
    compliant follows rules or requests willingly, often in official situations, while obedient follows commands from an authority.
  • dutiful
    3.9 1/m
    dutiful acts from a sense of duty and respect, while obedient does what someone in authority tells you to do.
  • submissive
    5.1 2/m
    submissive yields easily and appears humble or passive, while obedient means following commands without necessarily being humble.
  • docile
    5.5 2/m
    docile is quiet and easy to teach or control, while obedient focuses on following orders from someone in authority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compliant - willing to comply with orders or requests
  • antonym: disobedient - unwilling to do what one is told; refusing to obey
