mammotomy

noun
breast tissue biopsy
Pronunciation: / məˈmɑːtəmi / (AmE), / məˈmɒtəmi / (BrE)
a medical procedure to remove breast tissue, either for examination or treatment

Examples

Mammotomy procedure
The radiologist performed a mammotomy to collect tissue for further analysis.
After her abnormal scan, she scheduled a mammotomy to check for any signs of disease.
A mammotomy is often recommended when a suspicious area is found in a mammogram.
The patient underwent a mammotomy to remove the infected tissue from her breast.
A mammotomy is sometimes necessary when antibiotics alone cannot treat a breast abscess.
After the procedure, the doctor explained that the mammotomy had successfully drained the fluid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biopsy - the removal of tissue for diagnostic examination
  • degree higher: lumpectomy - surgical removal of a lump from the breast
  • synonym: mastectomy - surgical removal of all or part of the breast
