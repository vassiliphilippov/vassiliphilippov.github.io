sodbuster

noun
farmer who breaks prairie sod
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑdˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈsɒdˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a farmer, especially one who cultivates land that was previously unbroken prairie, particularly in the U.S. Great Plains during the late 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

A sodbuster farmer
Many sodbusters faced difficult conditions on the Great Plains.
She grew up hearing stories about her grandfather, who was a proud sodbuster.
The sodbuster worked long hours to prepare the prairie for planting.
