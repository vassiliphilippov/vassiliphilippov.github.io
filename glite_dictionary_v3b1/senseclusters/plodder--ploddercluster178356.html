plodder

noun
person who moves or works slowly
9.6 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈplɑːdər / (AmE), / ˈplɒdə / (BrE)
a person who works slowly and steadily, often without excitement or imagination

Examples

A careful plodder
Despite his lack of speed, the plodder finished the marathon with determination.
She was a plodder at work, always reliable, if never especially quick or innovative.
The teacher encouraged the plodder in her class to keep going and not get discouraged.
Although he was a plodder, his reliability made him an asset to the company.
Even though she's a plodder, she always gets the job done in the end.
The novelist was often described as a plodder rather than a creative genius.
