John Wyclif

proper noun
English theologian and church reformer (1328–1384)
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn ˈwɪklɪf / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈwɪklɪf / (BrE)
(c.1328–1384) English theologian and reformer who criticized the doctrines and practices of the Roman Catholic Church, laying groundwork for the Protestant Reformation.

Examples

John Wyclif theologian
John Wyclif is often regarded as a forerunner of the Protestant Reformation.
Many historians consider John Wyclif one of the most important English theologians of the fourteenth century.
The teachings of John Wyclif challenged the authority of the medieval Church.
