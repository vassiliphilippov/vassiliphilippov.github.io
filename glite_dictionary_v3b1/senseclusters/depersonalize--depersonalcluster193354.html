depersonalize

verb
remove personal identifiers from data; anonymise
7.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / dic8p5dd0s59n59ccla6az / (AmE), / did0c8p5cd0s59n59la6az / (BrE)
(in data management, computing, or research) to remove or redact personal identifiers from data or records so that individuals cannot be identified; anonymise.

Examples

To depersonalize data
Before publishing the report, the team depersonalised all records to protect patients' identities.
Researchers must depersonalise datasets by removing names and ID numbers before sharing them.
The company is depersonalising customer logs to comply with new privacy regulations across Europe.

Synonyms

  • anonymize
    6.3 0.04/m
    anonymize is a general term for hiding a person's identity in data or text, while depersonalize is a technical data process that redacts identifiers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anonymise - to make data anonymous by removing identifying details
  • synonym: de-identify - to remove or mask personal identifiers from data to prevent identification
  • antonym: personalise - to make something personal or add identifying details; opposite of depersonalise (in the data sense)
