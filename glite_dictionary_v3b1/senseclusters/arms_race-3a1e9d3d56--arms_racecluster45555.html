arms race

noun
competition to build powerful military weapons
8.5 3.5
Pronunciation: /ɑːrmz reɪs/ (AmE), /ɑːmz reɪs/ (BrE)
a competition between countries or groups to develop or acquire the most powerful or advanced military weapons and technology

Examples

A arms race between countries
Many experts warn that a new arms race could destabilize the region.
The Cold War was marked by a dangerous arms race between the United States and the Soviet Union.
The leaders met to discuss ways to prevent an arms race in cyber weapons.
During the Cold War, the arms race between the United States and the Soviet Union intensified globally.
Many experts warn that a technological arms race in artificial intelligence could destabilize international relations.
The fear of falling behind sparked a costly arms race in the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detente - the easing of hostility or strained relations, especially between countries (opposite trend to arms race)
  • antonym: disarmament - the reduction or withdrawal of military forces and weapons
  • synonym: military buildup - increase in military strength and weapons
