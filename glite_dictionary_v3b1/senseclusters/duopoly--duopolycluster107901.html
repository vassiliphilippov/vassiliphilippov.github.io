duopoly

noun
market with two main suppliers
8.2
Pronunciation: /duː.ˈɑː.pə.liː/ /; / /djuː.ˈɑː.pə.liː/ (AmE), /djuː.ˈɒp.ə.liː/ /; / /duː.ˈɒp.ə.liː/ (BrE)
a situation where two companies, groups, or parties dominate or control a market or field

Examples

A duopoly market
The airline industry in some countries is a classic duopoly with only two major carriers.
A duopoly can limit consumer choices and keep prices high.
Regulators are concerned about the effects of a duopoly on competition and innovation.
The airline industry in that country is a duopoly controlled by just two major carriers.
Critics argue that a duopoly limits consumer choice and keeps prices high.
For years, the television market was a duopoly with only two networks competing.
