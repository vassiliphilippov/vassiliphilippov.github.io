outfly

verb
to fly farther or faster
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈflaɪ / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈflaɪ / (BrE)
to fly farther or faster than someone or something else; to surpass or outdo others, especially in flight or performance

Examples

The new drone has outflown its predecessor in both speed and distance.

No matter how hard the smaller plane tried, it could not outfly the jet.
