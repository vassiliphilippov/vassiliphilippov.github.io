form

verb
create or establish something
1.0 48/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrm / (AmE), / f…îÀêm / (BrE)
to create or bring into existence something, such as an organization, group, or entity

Examples

To form a club
It took only an hour to form a club for amateur painters.
Several students formed a study group before the final exams.
The committee decided to form a new sub-group to tackle the issue.

Synonyms

  • constitute

  • found
    3.9 30/m
    found means to set up an institution or group, often by a person or team, while form means to create a group.
  • establish
    4.1 95/m
    establish means to start something such as an organization that will last, while form means to create a group.
  • create
    1.0 400/m

  • originate
    3.8 16/m
