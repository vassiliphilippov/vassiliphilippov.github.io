pin tumbler

noun
cylindrical lock mechanism component
9.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /pɪn ˈtʌm.blər/ (AmE), /pɪn ˈtʌm.blə/ (BrE)
a component in a lock mechanism consisting of a series of small cylindrical pins that prevent the lock from opening unless the correct key aligns them

Examples

A pin tumbler lock
If dirt gets into the pin tumbler mechanism, it can make the key difficult to turn.
Many house keys are designed to align the pin tumblers and allow the door to unlock.
The locksmith explained how the pin tumbler inside the lock prevented unauthorized access.

Related Terms

  • antonym: wafer tumbler - flat, thin piece used in place of pins in some locks
