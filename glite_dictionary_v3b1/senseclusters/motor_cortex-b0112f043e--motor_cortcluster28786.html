motor cortex

noun
area of brain controlling voluntary movement
5.5 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / Ààmo ät…ôr Ààk…îrÀåt…õks / (AmE), / Ààm…ô ät…ô Ààk…îÀêt…õks / (BrE)
the part of the brain's cerebral cortex responsible for planning, controlling, and executing voluntary movements

Examples

The motor cortex area
Damage to the motor cortex can result in loss of voluntary muscle control.
During brain surgery, doctors carefully avoid the motor cortex to prevent movement problems.
The motor cortex is activated when you intend to move a part of your body.

Synonyms

  • motor area
    5.1 0.07/m
    motor area refers to specific spots that control movements, while motor cortex names the larger cortical region planning and executing voluntary actions.
  • motor region
    5.1 0.00/m
    motor region is a general cortical zone for movement control, while motor cortex is the specific brain area that plans and executes voluntary movements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sensory cortex - the area of the cerebral cortex that receives sensory input
