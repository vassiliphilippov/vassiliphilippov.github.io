quaich

noun
Scottish drinking cup
Pronunciation: / kweɪx / (AmE), / kweɪx / (BrE)
a shallow, two-handled drinking cup traditionally used in Scotland for sharing drinks

Examples

A quaich cup
At the ceremony, the host offered a quaich filled with whisky to each guest.
The antique quaich was displayed in the museum's Scottish heritage section.
He received a silver quaich as a gift to mark the special occasion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: goblet - a drinking glass with a stem and base
  • synonym: tankard - a large drinking cup with a handle, often made of metal
