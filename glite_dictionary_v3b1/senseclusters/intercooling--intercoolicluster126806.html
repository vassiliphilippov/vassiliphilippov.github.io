intercooling

noun
cooling between compression
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkuːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkˈuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of cooling a gas between stages of compression to improve efficiency

Examples

Intercooling process
Intercooling is commonly used in turbocharged engines to increase power and efficiency.
By adding intercooling, the engineers reduced the temperature of the compressed air.
Modern aircraft engines often rely on intercooling to prevent overheating during flight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aftercooling - cooling a gas after the final stage of compression
  • antonym: compression - the act of pressing or squeezing something to make it smaller
