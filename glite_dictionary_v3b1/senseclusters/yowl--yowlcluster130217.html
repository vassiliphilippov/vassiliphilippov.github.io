yowl

verb
make a loud, mournful cry
9.9 5.1
Pronunciation: /jaʊl/ (AmE), /jaʊl/ (BrE)
to make a loud, long, and mournful cry or wail, like an animal or person in pain

Examples

To yowl in pain
Coyotes sometimes yowl when they search for their pack.
The baby woke up and started to yowl until its mother comforted it.
The stray cat began to yowl outside my window in the middle of the night.
Coyotes often yowl when communicating with others in their pack.
The injured cat began to yowl in pain, waking everyone in the house.
The wind was so strong it seemed to yowl through the trees at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: howl - to make a long loud wailing cry
  • synonym: wail - make a prolonged high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger
  • degree lower: whimper - make a series of low, feeble sounds expressing fear or pain
