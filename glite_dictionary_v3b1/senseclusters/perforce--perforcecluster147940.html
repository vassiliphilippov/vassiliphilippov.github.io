perforce

adverb
by necessity; necessarily
Pronunciation: / pərˈfɔrs / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːs / (BrE)
necessarily; by force of circumstances.

Examples

Used perforce in need
After the factory closed, many workers were perforce forced to find jobs in other towns.
The old traditions perforce gave way to modern practices over time.
With the bridge out, we were perforce obliged to take the longer route.

Related Terms

  • synonym: necessarily - in a way that cannot be avoided; inevitably
