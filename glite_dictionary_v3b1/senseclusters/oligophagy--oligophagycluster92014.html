oligophagy

noun
extreme picky eating
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪɡəˌfeɪdʒi /; / ˌoʊlɪˈɡɑfədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪˈɡɒfəɡi /; / ˌɒlɪˈɡɒfədʒi / (BrE)
a condition or behavior in which a person eats only a very limited variety of foods

Examples

Child's oligophagy
The child's oligophagy meant she would only eat plain pasta and bread at every meal.
Doctors became concerned about his health due to his persistent oligophagy and refusal to try new foods.
Family gatherings were challenging because of her oligophagy and strict food preferences.
The doctor diagnosed her with oligophagy after she reported eating very little for weeks.
Patients suffering from oligophagy may require nutritional support to prevent further weight loss.
After his surgery, he experienced oligophagy and struggled to maintain his strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anorexia - loss of appetite or inability to eat
  • antonym: omnivory - eating a wide variety of foods, both plant and animal
  • synonym: picky eating - a tendency to refuse many foods and eat only a few preferred ones
  • antonym: polyphagy - excessive or abnormal eating
