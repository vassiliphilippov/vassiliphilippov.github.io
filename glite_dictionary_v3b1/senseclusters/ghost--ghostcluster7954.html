ghost

noun
a writer for someone else (ghostwriter)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
a person who writes for another, giving the credit of authorship to someone else; a ghostwriter

Examples

A book ghost
As a ghost, she never saw her name appear on any book covers.
He worked as a ghost for many celebrities, crafting their autobiographies in secret.
The famous athlete hired a ghost to help write his memoir.

Related Terms

  • antonym: author - the writer of a book, article, or other text
  • synonym: ghostwriter - a person whose job is to write material for someone else who is named as the author
