rotate

verb
exchange roles or positions in turns
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊteɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊteɪt / (BrE)
to take turns or regularly exchange roles, positions, or duties with others

Examples

To rotate jobs
Each team member will rotate as leader throughout the project.
The committee decided to rotate the chairperson every six months.
We rotate the night shift duties so no one has to work late all week.
Each member of the team will rotate through the on-call shift over the weekend.
The company requires new employees to rotate in various departments during their training period.
The residents rotate between different hospital departments every few months.
