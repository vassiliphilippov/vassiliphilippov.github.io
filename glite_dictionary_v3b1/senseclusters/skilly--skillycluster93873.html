skilly

noun
thin porridge or soup, often institutional
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪli / (AmE), / ˈskɪli / (BrE)
A thin porridge or soup, usually made from oatmeal and water and sometimes flavored with meat, especially as served in institutions such as workhouses, prisons, or the military.

Examples

A bowl of skilly
During the famine, many poor families survived on little more than skilly each day.
In Victorian workhouses, skilly was a common part of breakfast.
The prisoners complained about being given only skilly for their meals.
