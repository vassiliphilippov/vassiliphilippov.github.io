block up

verb
obstruct completely; make impassable
2.3
Pronunciation: / blɑk ʌp / (AmE), / blɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to make something completely obstructed or unsuitable for passage; to prevent movement through or along a space by placing something in the way

Examples

To block up the alley
Debris from the storm would often block up the narrow alleyways overnight.
If you block up the fireplace, you'll have to find another way to heat the room.
The fallen tree blocked up the only road out of the village for hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - to remove an obstruction; to make passable again
  • synonym: obstruct - to block a passage or opening so that movement is prevented
