chrismation

noun
Christian anointing sacrament
9.9
Pronunciation: /krɪz.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /krɪz.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a Christian sacrament in which a person is anointed with holy oil, especially in Eastern Orthodox and Eastern Catholic churches

Examples

Chrismation with oil
In the Orthodox tradition, chrismation is performed immediately after baptism.
The bishop led the chrismation ceremony for the new members of the church.
After his chrismation, he was considered a full member of the faith community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confirmation - a Christian rite of initiation, especially in Western churches
