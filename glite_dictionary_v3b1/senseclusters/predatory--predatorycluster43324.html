predatory

adjective

(Of an animal) living by hunting and consuming other animals for food.

Examples

Predatory animal
A predatory fish stalked the smaller fish hiding among the rocks.
Many predatory animals have sharp claws and teeth for catching their prey.
Owls are predatory birds that hunt mainly at night.

Synonyms

  • predaceous
    8.5 0.03/m
    predaceous describes animals that hunt and kill other animals for food, while predatory is a more common, general adjective.
  • predacious
    8.4 0.03/m
    predacious is a slightly rarer form often seen in older texts for hunting animals, while predatory is the usual modern word.
  • rapacious
    8.4 0.07/m
    rapacious describes animals or people that take greedily and violently, while predatory focuses specifically on hunting for food.
  • carnivorous
    5.5 1.20/m
    carnivorous refers to feeding on meat and can apply to animals or plants, while predatory specifically means hunting animals.
  • meat-eating
    2.1 0.20/m
    meat-eating describes animals that eat flesh as part of their diet, while predatory implies they actively hunt other animals.
  • flesh-eating
    6.1 0.01/m
    flesh-eating sounds more graphic and often appears in headlines, while predatory is a neutral biological term for hunting animals.
