gimble

verb
to bore holes
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmbəl / (BrE)
to make holes or bore, especially using a tool; now rare or dialectal

Examples

To gimble wood
The old craftsman would gimble small holes into the wooden beams for pegs.
In some rural areas, people still gimble holes by hand when building fences.
Before power tools, workers had to gimble each opening with simple hand tools.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bore - to make a hole in something with a tool
  • synonym: drill - to make a hole in something using a drill
