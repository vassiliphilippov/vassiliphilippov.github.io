mononeuropathy

noun
disease affecting one nerve
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.noʊ.njʊˈrɑː.pə.θi / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əʊ.njʊˈrɒ.pə.θi / (BrE)
A disease or dysfunction affecting a single peripheral nerve.

Examples

A mononeuropathy disease
Carpal tunnel syndrome is a common type of mononeuropathy involving the median nerve.
Early treatment of mononeuropathy can help prevent long-term nerve damage.
The patient was diagnosed with mononeuropathy affecting her right wrist after the injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polyneuropathy - disease affecting multiple nerves
