ketosis-prone diabetes

noun
form of diabetes with frequent ketoacidosis
Pronunciation: / kɪˌtoʊsɪs proʊn daɪəˈbiːtiz / (AmE), / kɪˌtəʊsɪs prəʊn daɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
a form of diabetes mellitus with early onset, marked by frequent episodes of ketoacidosis and requiring both diet and insulin for control

Examples

Early ketosis-prone diabetes
Early detection of ketosis-prone diabetes is critical to prevent severe metabolic complications.
People with ketosis-prone diabetes often require both diet and insulin injections to manage their condition.
The doctor diagnosed her with ketosis-prone diabetes after she experienced repeated episodes of ketoacidosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diabetes mellitus - a group of diseases that result in high blood sugar
  • degree higher: type 1 diabetes - insulin-dependent diabetes, typically of early onset
