omen

verb
to signal future event
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.mən/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.mən/ (BrE)
to serve as a sign or warning of a future event, especially something bad.

Examples

Omen bad news
The dark clouds omened a coming storm that afternoon.
Some believed the broken mirror omened years of bad luck.
The sudden silence omened trouble for the sailors as the wind died completely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreshadow - to indicate or suggest something will happen in the future
  • synonym: predict - to say what will happen before it occurs
