Thraco-Phrygian

noun
ancient Indo-European language branch
Pronunciation: / ˌθreɪkoʊˈfrɪdʒiən / (AmE), / ˌθreɪkəʊˈfrɪdʒɪən / (BrE)
an extinct branch of the Indo-European language family, possibly related to Armenian, formerly spoken in areas of Thrace and Phrygia

Examples

Thraco-Phrygian language family
Few written records of the Thraco-Phrygian languages have survived to the present day.
Linguists debate whether Thraco-Phrygian is related more closely to Greek or Armenian.
The study of Thraco-Phrygian provides insight into the history of Indo-European languages.
