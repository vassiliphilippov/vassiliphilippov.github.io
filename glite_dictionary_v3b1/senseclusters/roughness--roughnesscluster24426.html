roughness

noun
irregularity or unevenness of a surface
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌfnəs /; / c8r8cfn59s / (AmE), / ˈrʌfnəs /; / c8r8cfn59s / (BrE)
the quality of being rough, uneven, or irregular, especially on a surface or in physical conditions (including in computer graphics or the sea)

Examples

Roughness of the surface
Her hands showed the roughness from years of hard work.
The roughness of the road made the car vibrate.
You can feel the roughness of the sandpaper when you run your hand over it.
Corrosive chemicals can cause significant roughness on steel surfaces, affecting performance.
Engineers measured the roughness of the pipe's inner surface to assess damage.
The roughness of the metal increased as corrosion continued over the years.
Fishermen often cancel trips due to the roughness brought by storms.
The roughness of the sea made our journey across the channel treacherous.
The captain checked reports on the roughness before deciding to set sail.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - the state of the sea being smooth and free from waves
  • antonym: glossiness - the degree to which a surface reflects light sharply; often treated as the opposite of roughness in rendering
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of being even and without roughness or bumps
  • synonym: surface texture - the small-scale geometric detail of a surface; related concept describing micro-structure
