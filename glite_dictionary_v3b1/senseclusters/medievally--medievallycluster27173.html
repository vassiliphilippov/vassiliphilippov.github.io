medievally

adverb
cruel or outdated manner
9.6
Pronunciation: / mɪˈdiːvəli / (AmE), / mɪˈdiːvəli / (BrE)
in a way that is extremely cruel, harsh, or old-fashioned, especially when criticizing outdated or brutal practices.

Examples

Medievally harsh rules
The manager treated the team medievally, enforcing strict rules and harsh punishments for small errors.
Some critics argue that the law is enforced medievally, with little regard for fairness or modern standards.
The teacher disciplined students medievally, using outdated and severe methods that shocked parents.
