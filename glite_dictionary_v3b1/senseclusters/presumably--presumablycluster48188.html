presumably

adverb
likely assumed to be true
4.3 23/m
Pronunciation: / pr…™Ààzjum…ôbli / (AmE), / pr…™ÀàzjuÀêm…ôbli / (BrE)
Used to say that you think something is likely or reasonably assumed to be true, although you are not certain.

Examples

Used presumably to guess
Presumably, the meeting has been postponed until next week.
He had gone out, presumably to get something to eat.
Their flight was delayed, presumably due to bad weather.

Synonyms

  • presumptively
    7.4 0.40/m
    presumptively is more formal and often used in law or reports, while presumably is common in everyday speech.
  • assumedly
    6.7 0.02/m
    assumedly suggests belief without firm proof, while presumably shows a reasoned guess based on evidence or logic.
  • probably
    1.0 436/m
    probably expresses a higher chance or expectation, while presumably frames the idea as a reasonable assumption in speech.
  • supposedly
    3.4 24/m
    supposedly reports what people generally claim or believe, while presumably shows what the speaker thinks is likely.
  • apparently
    2.8 99/m
    apparently describes what seems true from what is seen or heard, while presumably expresses a logical guess without direct evidence.
  • in all likelihood
    3.2 1.81/m
    in all likelihood says something is almost certain in everyday talk by many speakers, while presumably suggests a smaller, reasonable assumption.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainly - without any doubt
  • synonym: probably - likely to happen or be the case
