hypermethylation

noun
increased DNA methylation
9.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pɜːr.meθ.ə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.meθ.ə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of adding an unusually large number of methyl groups to DNA or a molecule, often changing gene activity or properties

Examples

Hypermethylation of DNA
The scientists observed hypermethylation in the promoter regions of several tumor suppressor genes.
Abnormal hypermethylation can silence important genes involved in cell growth regulation.
Recent studies link hypermethylation to the development of certain types of cancer.
Researchers observed hypermethylation in the DNA samples, which affected gene expression.
The drug was designed to prevent hypermethylation of certain proteins in the body.
Excessive hypermethylation can lead to changes in how a molecule behaves chemically.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demethylation - the removal of a methyl group from a molecule
  • antonym: hypomethylation - a decrease in the addition of methyl groups to DNA
  • synonym: methylation - the process of adding methyl groups to DNA
