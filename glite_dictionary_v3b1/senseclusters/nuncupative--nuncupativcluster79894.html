nuncupative

adjective
spoken, not written
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌŋkjəˌpeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈnʌŋkjʊpətɪv / (BrE)
spoken rather than written, especially referring to a will declared orally

Examples

A nuncupative will
A nuncupative will is only valid under certain circumstances.
The court accepted the nuncupative statement as evidence of his last wishes.
In some jurisdictions, a nuncupative will must be witnessed by several people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oral - spoken rather than written
  • antonym: written - expressed in writing
