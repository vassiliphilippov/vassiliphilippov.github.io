volatility

noun
tendency to change rapidly and unpredictably
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌvɑːləˈtɪlɪti /; / ˌvɑːləˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɒləˈtɪlɪti /; / ˌvɒləˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being likely to change quickly and unpredictably, especially for the worse (such as in emotions, behavior, or markets)

Examples

Market volatility
His mood volatility made it difficult to predict how he would react.
Political volatility can create uncertainty for businesses and citizens.
The volatility of the stock market makes investing risky for some people.
Financial markets are known for their volatility, which can unsettle even the most experienced investors.
Her volatility made it difficult for her colleagues to predict how she would react to criticism.
The volatility of his temper meant he was easily upset by small things.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmness - the quality of being free from agitation or strong emotion
  • synonym: instability - the state of being unstable or prone to change
  • antonym: stability - the quality of being stable or unchanging
