groggy

adjective
dazed and unsteady, confused
5.6 1.37/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°r…ëÀê…°i / (AmE), / Àà…°r…í…°i / (BrE)
weak, unsteady, or confused, especially because of tiredness, illness, or the effects of alcohol or a blow to the head.

Examples

Feel groggy after sleep
After sleeping only two hours, I felt incredibly groggy when my alarm went off.
He was still groggy from the anesthesia hours after his surgery.
The boxer looked groggy after taking several hard punches to the head.

Synonyms

  • woozy
    5.7 1.02/m
    woozy is dizzy and shaky, often from a sudden bout of illness or drink, while groggy is weak and confused.
  • foggy
    3.4 0.88/m
    foggy is slow to think and unclear, often after little sleep or drink, while groggy also adds weakness and unsteadiness.
  • dazed
    4.8 4.17/m
    dazed is stunned and slow to react after a shock or blow, while groggy often includes weakness and unsteady movement.
  • logy
    9.8 0.04/m
    logy is dull and slow in thought or movement from tiredness or illness, while groggy adds dizziness or balance problems.
  • drowsy
    2.7 1.74/m
    drowsy is sleepy and heavy-eyed, usually from lack of sleep, while groggy is weak, confused, or physically unsteady.
