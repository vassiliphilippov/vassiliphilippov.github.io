overgraze

verb
to damage land by grazing
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡreɪz / (BrE)
to allow animals to eat so much grass in an area that the land is damaged or cannot recover easily

Examples

To overgraze the land
If too many sheep overgraze the hillside, the grass may not grow back.
The land was overgrazed for years, leading to serious soil erosion.
Farmers are careful not to let their cattle overgraze the same field every season.
If too many sheep overgraze the hillside, the grass may not grow back.
Farmers must rotate their cattle to avoid having them overgraze the same pasture.
The land became barren after years of overgrazing by livestock.
