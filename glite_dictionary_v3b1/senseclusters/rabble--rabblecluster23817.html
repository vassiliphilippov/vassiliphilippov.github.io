rabble

verb
to mob noisily
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈræbəl / (AmE), / ˈræbəl / (BrE)
to gather or move as a noisy, disorderly crowd, often causing disturbance

Examples

Rabble in the streets
Protesters began to rabble in front of the government building, demanding change.
The fans rabbled outside the stadium after the big win, cheering loudly.
Security was called when a group started to rabble near the entrance, blocking the way.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disperse - to break up and move in different directions
  • synonym: mob - to crowd around in a disorderly or unruly way
