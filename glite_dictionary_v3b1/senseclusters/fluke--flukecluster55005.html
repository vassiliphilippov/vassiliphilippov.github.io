fluke

noun
a parasitic flatworm
6.8 7.9
Pronunciation: /fluːk/ (AmE), /fluːk/ (BrE)
a parasitic flatworm with external suckers used for attaching to a host, typically found in the liver or blood of vertebrates

Examples

Liver fluke
Scientists studied the life cycle of the blood fluke in the laboratory.
Some flukes are serious parasites in both humans and animals.
The sheep was infected by a liver fluke that caused health problems.
