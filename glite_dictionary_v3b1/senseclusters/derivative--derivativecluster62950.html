derivative

adjective
lacking originality; imitative
8.0 5.7
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɪvətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪvətɪv / (BrE)
(of something, especially a work of art or literature) imitative of the work of another person, and lacking originality.

Examples

Derivative art style
Her painting style was considered too derivative to stand out in the competition.
Many pop songs today are accused of being derivative rather than innovative.
The novel was criticized as a derivative work that offered little new to readers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - new or inventive, not copied or imitative
