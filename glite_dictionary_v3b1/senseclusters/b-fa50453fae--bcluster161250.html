B

noun
original vitamin B; now a group of B vitamins
Pronunciation: / bi / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)
the vitamin originally believed to be a single substance but now known to comprise several different B vitamins, such as B1, B2, and B12.

Examples

Vitamin B complex
Researchers later discovered that B was actually several different vitamins, not one.
The original concept of vitamin B led to the naming of the various B vitamins.
Vitamin B was once considered a single nutrient before its components were identified.
