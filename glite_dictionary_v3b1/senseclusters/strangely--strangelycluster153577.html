strangely

adverb
in an unusual or unexpected way
3.2 13/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstreÉªndÊ’li / (AmE), / ËˆstreÉªndÊ’li / (BrE)
In a way that is unusual or unexpected.

Examples

To strangely behave
Strangely, the house was completely silent despite being full of people.
She felt strangely calm even though the situation was very tense.
The painting was strangely familiar to me, though I had never seen it before.

Synonyms

  • oddly
    3.4 12/m
    oddly highlights a small, surprising detail or feeling, while strangely describes something generally unusual or unexpected.
  • weirdly
    3.5 1.58/m
    weirdly sounds strong and informal for odd or creepy events, while strangely is a general word for something unexpected.
  • peculiarly
    6.2 0.73/m
    peculiarly feels more formal and slightly old-fashioned in writing, while strangely is common in speech and everyday description.
  • curiously
    4.1 3.94/m
    curiously often suggests interest or surprise at something, while strangely focuses only on how unusual or unexpected it is.
  • unusually
    3.3 9.56/m
    unusually means to a greater degree than normal, while strangely means simply in an odd or unexpected way.
