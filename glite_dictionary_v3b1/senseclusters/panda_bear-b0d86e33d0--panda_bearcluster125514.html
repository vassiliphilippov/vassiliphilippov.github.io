panda bear

noun
large black-and-white mammal of China
2.7 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpæn.də beər/ (AmE), /ˈpæn.də beə/ /; / /ˈpʌn.də beə/ (BrE)
A large black-and-white mammal native to bamboo forests in China and Tibet, known for its distinctive coloring and primarily herbivorous diet; also called the giant panda.

Examples

The panda bear eats bamboo
Children at the zoo were excited to see the adorable panda bear sleeping under a tree.
Scientists have studied the panda bear to learn more about its unique diet and behavior.
The panda bear spends most of its day eating bamboo in the mountains of China.

Related Terms

  • antonym: red panda - a smaller, reddish-brown mammal also native to Asia
