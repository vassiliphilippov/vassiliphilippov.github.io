Jude

proper noun
New Testament Apostle, brother of St. James
7.1 0.56/m
Pronunciation: / d íud / (AmE), / d íuÀêd / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the Apostles, traditionally regarded as the brother of St. James and invoked by many as the patron saint of desperate cases.

Examples

Saint Jude
He is known as Jude, the patron saint of lost causes.
Many Christians pray to Jude when facing seemingly impossible difficulties.
The feast day of Jude is celebrated on October 28th in the Western church.

Synonyms

  • Jude the Apostle
    7.6 0.00/m
    Jude the Apostle is the full formal title used in church histories, while Jude is the simpler personal name people use.
  • Saint Jude
    6.8 0.00/m
    Saint Jude is the devotional name people use in prayer and shrines, while Jude is the apostle's ordinary personal name.
  • St. Jude
    6.7 0.00/m
    St. Jude is the common short form seen on churches and badges, while Jude is the given name used in scriptures.
  • Thaddaeus
    9.9 0.03/m
    Thaddaeus is an alternate ancient name used in some traditions, while Jude is the name used in many English Bibles.
  • Judas
    8.5 1.62/m
    Judas is often associated with the betrayer in common use, while Jude refers to the apostle invoked for desperate cases.
