Majorana hortensis

noun
aromatic Mediterranean herb (sweet marjoram)
Pronunciation: / meɪˌdʒɔːrəˈneɪə hɔːrˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / maɪˌdʒɔːrəˈneɪə hɔːˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
An aromatic European herb of the mint family, native to the Mediterranean and Turkey, commonly used in cooking; also known as sweet marjoram.

Examples

Majorana hortensis herb
Majorana hortensis is often used in Mediterranean cuisine to add a subtle, sweet flavor to dishes.
Gardeners sometimes grow Majorana hortensis for both its culinary uses and fragrant aroma.
The leaves of Majorana hortensis are frequently dried and added to spice blends.
