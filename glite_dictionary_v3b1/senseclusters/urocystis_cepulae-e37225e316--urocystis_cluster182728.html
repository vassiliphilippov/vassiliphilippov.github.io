Urocystis cepulae

noun
fungus causing black blisters on onions
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊsɪstɪs səˈpjuːliː / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɒsɪstɪs sɪˈpjuːliː / (BrE)
a species of smut fungus that causes blackish blisters on onion scales and leaves, especially harmful to seedlings

Examples

Urocystis cepulae fungus
Urocystis cepulae infections can significantly reduce onion crop yields if left untreated.
Farmers are advised to monitor their onion fields for symptoms of Urocystis cepulae.
Seed treatments are one method to protect young plants from Urocystis cepulae.
