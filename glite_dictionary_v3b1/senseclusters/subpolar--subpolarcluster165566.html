subpolar

adjective
near polar regions
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / sʌbˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
located just outside the polar regions, especially describing climates or areas near the Arctic or Antarctic circles

Examples

Subpolar climate zone
The subpolar regions experience long, cold winters and short, cool summers.
Many hardy plants are able to survive in the subpolar climate.
Researchers studied the unique wildlife found in subpolar areas near the Arctic Circle.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: polar - relating to the regions around the North or South Pole
  • degree lower: temperate - relating to regions with mild or moderate climate
