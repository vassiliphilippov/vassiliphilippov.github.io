out on a limb

adverb
in a risky or unsupported position
6.2
Pronunciation: / aʊt ɑn ə lɪm / (AmE), / aʊt ɒn ə lɪm / (BrE)
in a risky or vulnerable position, especially without support from others

Examples

Out on a limb alone
He felt out on a limb after quitting his job to start a business.
She knew she was out on a limb when she challenged the committee's decision.
The scientist went out on a limb with a controversial new theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: play it safe - to avoid risks or dangerous situations
  • synonym: take a risk - to do something that might be dangerous or have negative consequences
