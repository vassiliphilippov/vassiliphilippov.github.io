loop

noun
complete electrical circuit
Pronunciation: / luːp / (AmE), / luːp / (BrE)
a closed path or circuit in which current or signals circulate continuously, or a network arranged in a closed path

Examples

An electric loop
An alarm system often uses a loop that triggers when the circuit is broken.
In engineering, a feedback loop helps regulate system output.
The technician repaired the broken loop to restore power to the device.
A loop topology can make troubleshooting more complex than a simple line topology.
In older computer networks, data often traveled in a loop from one device to the next.
The devices were arranged in a loop to ensure reliable communication even if one link failed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circuit - a closed path through which electricity can flow
  • synonym: ring - a network topology where each node connects to exactly two other nodes, forming a single continuous pathway
