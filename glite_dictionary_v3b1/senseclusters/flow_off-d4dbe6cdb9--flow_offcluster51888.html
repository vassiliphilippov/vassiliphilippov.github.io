flow off

verb
(liquid) move away gradually
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: / floʊ ɑf / (AmE), / fləʊ ɒf / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to move away from a place gradually, typically in a steady stream.

Examples

Water flows off roof
After the rain, the water began to flow off the roof and onto the ground.
As the tide receded, you could see the seawater flow off the rocky shore.
Make sure the oil can flow off easily to prevent clogging the machinery.
