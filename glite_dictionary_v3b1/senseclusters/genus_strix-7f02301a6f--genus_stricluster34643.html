genus Strix

noun
a genus of earless owls
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstrɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of owls lacking ear tufts, known for their rounded heads and nocturnal habits.

Examples

Genus Strix owls
Members of the genus Strix are found in forests across much of the world.
The genus Strix comprises several species, including the well-known tawny owl.
Unlike some other owls, those in the genus Strix lack prominent ear tufts.
