Nicotiana tabacum

noun
main plant species used for tobacco
9.3
Pronunciation: / nɪˌkoʊʃiˈænə təˈbeɪkəm / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊtɪˈɑːnə təˈbækəm / (BrE)
A tall South American plant with large oval leaves and clusters of tubular white or pink flowers, widely cultivated for its leaves used in making tobacco products.

Examples

Nicotiana tabacum plant leaves
Most commercial tobacco products are made from Nicotiana tabacum, a species native to South America.
Scientists have studied Nicotiana tabacum to understand how nicotine is synthesized in plants.
The leaves of Nicotiana tabacum are harvested and processed to produce cigarettes and cigars.

Related Terms
