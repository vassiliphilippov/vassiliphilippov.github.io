hold sway

verb
have influence or control
6.9
Pronunciation: / hoʊld sweɪ / (AmE), / həʊld sweɪ / (BrE)
to have power, authority, or strong influence over people or situations

Examples

To hold sway over people
Charismatic leaders often hold sway over their followers for years.
Despite opposition, the old traditions still hold sway in many rural communities.
In the 18th century, the monarch held sway over the entire nation.
Despite public protests, the old policies still hold sway in the company.
For centuries, superstition held sway over the villagers' beliefs.
It is clear that fashion trends hold sway over many young people today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominate - to have a commanding influence on; to control
  • synonym: reign - to hold royal office; to rule as monarch
  • synonym: rule - to exercise authority or influence over; to govern
  • antonym: submit - to yield to the control or authority of another
  • antonym: yield - to relinquish influence or control
