bath

noun
ancient Hebrew liquid measure (~10 gallons)
9.9
Pronunciation: / bæθ / (AmE), / bɑːθ / (BrE)
an ancient Hebrew unit of liquid capacity, approximately equal to 10 gallons or 37 liters

Examples

A bath of wine
According to the Old Testament, a bath was used to measure large quantities of wine.
The priest poured a bath of oil as required by the ancient law.
The vessel could hold about one bath of water, suitable for ritual washing.
