inured

adjective
accustomed or toughened by experience
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnjʊrd / (AmE), / ɪˈnjʊəd / (BrE)
accustomed to something, especially something unpleasant, so that it no longer affects one strongly; made tough by habitual exposure

Examples

To be inured to pain
After years of harsh winters, the villagers were inured to the bitter cold.
She had become inured to criticism after so many years in the spotlight.
The soldiers were inured to hardship by their daily training exercises.
After years of harsh winters, the villagers had become enured to the cold.
She was enured to criticism after decades in the public eye.
Soldiers quickly grow enured to the hardships of military life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accustomed - familiar with something through repeated experience
  • synonym: hardened - made tough or unfeeling by experience
  • antonym: sensitive - easily affected or responsive, not toughened by experience
