stick one's neck out

verb
take a risk; make oneself vulnerable
Pronunciation: / stɪk wʌnz nɛk aʊt / (AmE), / stɪk wʌnz nɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to take a risk or put oneself in a vulnerable position, especially by supporting or saying something that could lead to criticism or harm

Examples

To stick one's neck out for others
I'm not going to stick my neck out unless I know you'll support me.
She stuck her neck out for the team by publicly defending their decision.
Whenever you stick your neck out like that, you risk upsetting people in power.

Related Terms

  • antonym: play it safe - avoid risk
  • synonym: take a risk - do something that may have negative outcomes
