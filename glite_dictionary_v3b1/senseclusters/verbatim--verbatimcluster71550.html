verbatim

adverb
word-for-word; exactly as said or written
Pronunciation: / vɚˈbeɪtəm / (AmE), / vəˈbeɪtɪm / (BrE)
using exactly the same words as were originally used; word for word

Examples

Repeat verbatim
He repeated her remarks verbatim to the entire audience.
Please type out the instructions verbatim, without any changes.
The witness recited the conversation verbatim from memory.
He copied the passage verbatim from the original text.
She asked him to repeat her instructions verbatim to ensure accuracy.
The transcript was recorded verbatim, capturing every word.

Related Terms

  • synonym: literally - in a literal manner; word for word
  • antonym: paraphrased - expressed in different words
