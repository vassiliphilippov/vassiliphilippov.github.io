vibriosis

noun
bacterial fish disease
Pronunciation: / ˌvɪbriˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌvɪbrɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease in fish and other aquatic animals caused by bacteria of the genus Vibrio, often resulting in ulcers and high mortality

Examples

Fish vibriosis outbreak
The farm lost half its stock last summer when vibriosis spread through the young salmon.
Aquaculture facilities monitor water quality closely to prevent outbreaks of vibriosis among their fish.
Symptoms of vibriosis include skin ulcers and bleeding in affected aquatic animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furunculosis - a bacterial disease in fish causing boils and ulcers
  • antonym: ichthyophthiriasis - a parasitic disease in fish known as 'white spot disease'
