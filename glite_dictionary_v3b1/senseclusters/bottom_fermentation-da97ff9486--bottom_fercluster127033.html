bottom fermentation

noun
beer fermentation process where yeast sinks
8.2 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːt.əm fɜːr.mən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈbɒt.əm fɜː.mən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a slow process of alcoholic fermentation at low temperatures, during which yeast settles at the bottom of the fermenting liquid, commonly used in brewing lager beers.

Examples

Bottom fermentation process
Lager production relies on bottom fermentation to achieve its clean, crisp flavor profile.
The brewery uses bottom fermentation for a longer, colder brewing process.
Unlike ales, lagers are made using a specialized bottom fermentation method.

Related Terms
