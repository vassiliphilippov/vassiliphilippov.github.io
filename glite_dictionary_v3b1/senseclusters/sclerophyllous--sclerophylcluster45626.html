sclerophyllous

adjective
hard-leaved (plants)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsklɛrəˈfɪləs / (AmE), / ˌsklɪərəˈfɪləs / (BrE)
having hard, thick, and leathery leaves that help plants survive in dry or nutrient-poor environments

Examples

Sclerophyllous leaves
Eucalyptus trees are well known for their sclerophyllous leaves, which help them survive in dry climates.
Many Mediterranean plants are sclerophyllous, allowing them to conserve water during hot summers.
The sclerophyllous nature of these shrubs makes them resistant to drought and grazing.
Many Australian forests are dominated by sclerophyllous trees that can survive long dry periods.
The sclerophyllous shrubs in the Mediterranean region are well adapted to hot, dry summers.
Researchers studied the sclerophyllous vegetation to understand how plants cope with water scarcity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mesophytic - adapted to moderate water conditions
  • synonym: xerophytic - adapted to dry environments
