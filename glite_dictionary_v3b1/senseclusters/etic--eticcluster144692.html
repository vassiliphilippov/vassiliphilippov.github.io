etic

adjective
external perspective
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
describing an analysis or perspective that comes from outside a cultural group, using external or objective criteria

Examples

An etic approach
Anthropologists often use an etic approach to compare cultural practices across societies.
The study provided an etic analysis of language use in different regions.
Some researchers prefer etic methods to ensure their findings are more universally applicable.
The researcher used an etic approach to compare cultural rituals across different societies.
In linguistics, an etic description focuses on the physical properties of speech sounds.
Anthropologists often debate whether to use etic or emic perspectives in their fieldwork.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emic - relating to analysis from within a culture, using internal concepts
  • synonym: objective - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
