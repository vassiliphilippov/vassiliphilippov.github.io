cracky

adjective
full of cracks
Pronunciation: / ˈkræki / (AmE), / ˈkræki / (BrE)
having many cracks or likely to develop cracks; sometimes used to describe someone as eccentric or odd.

Examples

Cracky old vase
The old vase looked cracky after years of sitting in the sun.
His explanation sounded a bit cracky, but everyone listened politely.
The dry ground became cracky during the long summer drought.
