view

noun
opinion or perspective
Pronunciation: / vjuː / (AmE), / vjuː / (BrE)
a personal opinion, belief, or way of thinking about something

Examples

Her view on life
From her view, the proposal simply did not make sense.
In my view, honesty is the most important quality in a friend.
The book presents a critical view of the economic system.
It is important to respect each other's view even if you disagree.
My view is that education should be accessible to everyone.
She expressed her view on the topic during the meeting.
From my view, the most important thing is transparency.
She shared her view on the proposed changes during the meeting.
The committee welcomed different views from the public before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: belief - an acceptance that something exists or is true, especially without proof
  • antonym: bias - a tendency to favor one way of thinking, often unfairly
  • synonym: conviction - a firmly held belief or opinion
  • antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proven to be true
  • synonym: opinion - a belief or judgment that is not founded on proof or certainty
  • synonym: perspective - a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something
