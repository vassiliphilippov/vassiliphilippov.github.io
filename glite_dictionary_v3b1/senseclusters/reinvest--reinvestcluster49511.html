reinvest

verb
to restore authority
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈvɛst / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvɛst / (BrE)
to formally give back or restore power, authority, a title, or an office to someone.

Examples

To reinvest someone with power
After the investigation cleared her, the committee decided to reinvest her with her former responsibilities.
The king chose to reinvest the nobleman with his lost title after years of exile.
Following the successful appeal, the board will reinvest him with the authority he previously held.

Related Terms

  • antonym: remove - to take away a position or authority from someone
  • synonym: restore - to bring back a previous right, practice, or situation
