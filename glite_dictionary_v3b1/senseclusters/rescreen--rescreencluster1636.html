rescreen

verb
to show again or replace screen
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈskriːn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈskriːn / (BrE)
to show a film or program again, or to replace or repair a screen such as a window screen.

Examples

To rescreen a movie
The theater decided to rescreen the popular documentary due to high demand.
After the storm damaged the window, we had to rescreen it to keep out insects.
The festival will rescreen several classic films for new audiences this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replay - to play something again, such as a video or recording
