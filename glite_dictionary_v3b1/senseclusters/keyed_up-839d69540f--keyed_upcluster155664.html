keyed up

adjective
in a state of readiness or excitement
6.9 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / Àåkid Àà åp / (AmE), / ÀåkiÀêd Àà åp / (BrE)
in a state of readiness or excitement, often in preparation for action

Examples

To be keyed up
I'm usually keyed up on the first day of a new project.
She felt keyed up as she waited for the job interview to begin.
The whole team was keyed up before the big championship game.

Synonyms

  • psyched
    7.7 0.24/m
    psyched is very excited and eager for action, while keyed up also suggests being ready and slightly tense before doing something.
  • pumped up
    5.1 0.30/m
    pumped up is full of strong energetic excitement before activity, while keyed up mixes readiness with nervous or keen energy.
  • primed
    4.6 0.02/m
    primed means ready and prepared for action, while keyed up adds an emotional edge of excitement or tension before starting.
  • wound up
    5.2 0.54/m
    wound up is tense and nervous with high stress, while keyed up more often means ready and excited for an activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • synonym: excited - feeling enthusiasm or eagerness
