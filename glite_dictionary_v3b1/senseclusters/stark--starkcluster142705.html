stark

adjective
severely plain or simple
4.8 2.43/m
Pronunciation: / st…ërk / (AmE), / st…ëÀêk / (BrE)
very plain, simple, or bare; lacking decoration, comfort, or life; desolate

Examples

Stark room
Her stark office felt cold and uninviting.
The stark contrast of black and white furniture highlighted the simplicity of the apartment.
The room had a stark design, with white walls and no decorations.
After the wildfire, the hillside was left a stark expanse of blackened soil and ash.
Standing alone in the stark landscape, the old barn looked abandoned and forlorn.
The stark beauty of the desert lay in its emptiness and silence.

Synonyms

  • desolate
    6.8 2.73/m
    desolate is empty and lonely, often describing a place with no life, while stark means very plain or bare.
  • bare
    2.2 22/m
    bare is uncovered or simple and shows nothing added, while stark is stronger and suggests harsh emptiness or lack of comfort.
  • austere
    7.2 1.05/m
    austere is plain and formal in style, often without luxury, while stark is more forceful and can mean harsh or desolate.
  • bleak
    4.9 1.62/m
    bleak is cold, exposed, and gives little shelter or hope, while stark focuses on plainness or severe emptiness.
  • barren
    4.6 4.42/m
    barren is land that cannot support life or crops, while stark is a broader word for plainness or lifelessness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: austere - simple or plain; without luxury or excess
  • synonym: barren - too poor to produce much or any vegetation; bleak and lifeless
  • synonym: bleak - cold and inhospitable; bare and exposed (especially of places)
  • antonym: lush - rich and abundant in vegetation, the opposite of stark
  • antonym: ornate - elaborately decorated
