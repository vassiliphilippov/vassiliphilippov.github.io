protogalaxy

noun
forming galaxy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtoʊˈɡæləksi / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈɡæləksi / (BrE)
a large cloud of gas and dust in space that is in the process of forming into a galaxy

Examples

A protogalaxy cloud
Scientists believe the protogalaxy will eventually develop into a spiral galaxy like the Milky Way.
The telescope captured an image of a distant protogalaxy forming billions of years ago.
Studying a protogalaxy helps astronomers understand how galaxies are born and evolve.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: galaxy - a large system of stars, gas, and dust held together by gravity
  • synonym: nebula - a cloud of gas and dust in space, sometimes forming stars
