ideal

noun
a perfect standard or goal
1.0
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdiəl / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəl / (BrE)
a person or thing that is considered perfect or the best possible example; a standard of excellence to aim for

Examples

A personal ideal
For many athletes, winning an Olympic gold medal is the ideal they strive for.
In his essay, he discussed the gap between reality and the ideal in modern society.
The company’s new product is far from the ideal envisioned by its founders.
For centuries, the Renaissance artist was considered the ideal of creative genius.
He has always been my ideal of what a true leader should be.
The company strives to be the ideal in customer service and innovation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exemplar - a typical or perfect example
  • antonym: imperfect - lacking completeness or perfection
  • synonym: paragon - a person or thing viewed as a model of excellence
  • synonym: perfection - the condition, state, or quality of being free from all flaws or defects
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist, as opposed to an ideal
