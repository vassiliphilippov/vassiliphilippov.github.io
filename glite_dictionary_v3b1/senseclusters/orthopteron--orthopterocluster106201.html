orthopteron

noun
insect of the order Orthoptera
Pronunciation: / ɔːrˈθɑːptərɑn / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒptərɒn / (BrE)
any insect of the order Orthoptera, such as grasshoppers, crickets, and locusts, typically having leathery forewings, membranous hind wings, and chewing mouthparts.

Examples

A orthopteron insect
Locusts, being a type of orthopteron, can cause significant crop damage during swarms.

The grasshopper is a common orthopteron found in fields across the world.
