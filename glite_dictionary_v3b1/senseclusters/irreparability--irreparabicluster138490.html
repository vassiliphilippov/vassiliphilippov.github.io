irreparability

noun
impossibility of repair
8.7
Pronunciation: /ɪ.rə.pə.rə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɪ.rə.pə.rə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to repair, restore, or fix

Examples

The irreparability of damage
The court considered the irreparability of the environmental damage before issuing its decision.
Lawyers argued that the irreparability of the harm justified an immediate injunction.
Proving the irreparability of the loss was crucial to the plaintiff's case.
The irreparability of the ancient vase made its loss even more tragic for the museum.
After the flood, the irreparability of the house's foundation was confirmed by the engineers.
Some mistakes have a sense of irreparability that makes them especially hard to accept.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irreversibility - the quality of being impossible to reverse or undo
