waterwheel plant

noun
floating aquatic carnivorous plant (Aldrovanda vesiculosa)
2.1 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər.wiːl plænt/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər.wiːl plænt/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə.wiːl plɑːnt/ (BrE)
a floating aquatic carnivorous plant (Aldrovanda vesiculosa) native to central and southern Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia, characterized by whorls of leaves ending in hinged lobes for trapping small aquatic prey

Examples

The waterwheel plant traps insects
Botanists are working to conserve the rare waterwheel plant in European wetlands.
The waterwheel plant captures water fleas with its snap-trap leaves.
The waterwheel plant is sometimes called a freshwater Venus flytrap due to its feeding mechanism.
