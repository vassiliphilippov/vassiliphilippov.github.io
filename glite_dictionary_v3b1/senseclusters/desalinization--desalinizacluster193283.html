desalinization

noun
removal of salt from water
Pronunciation: / dɪˌseɪlənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌsælɪnɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌsælɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌsælɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing salt, especially from sea water, to make it suitable for drinking or irrigation

Examples

Desalinization of water
As water scarcity increases, desalinization has become an important technology worldwide.
Modern desalinization plants can supply drinking water to thousands of people daily.
The city invested heavily in desalinization to address its fresh water shortage.
Advancements in desalinisation have made clean drinking water more accessible in arid regions.
Building a new desalinisation plant is expected to reduce reliance on imported water.
The country invested heavily in desalinisation to address its chronic water shortages.

Related Terms
