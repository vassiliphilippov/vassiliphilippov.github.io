colubrid

noun
snake of the family Colubridae
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈluː.brɪd / (AmE), / kəˈluː.brɪd / (BrE)
A snake belonging to the large family Colubridae, which includes mainly non-venomous, temperate to tropical terrestrial, arboreal, or aquatic species.

Examples

A colubrid snake
Many colubrids are harmless and make popular pets among reptile enthusiasts.
Scientists discovered a new species of aquatic colubrid in the wetlands.
The grass snake is a common colubrid found throughout Europe.
