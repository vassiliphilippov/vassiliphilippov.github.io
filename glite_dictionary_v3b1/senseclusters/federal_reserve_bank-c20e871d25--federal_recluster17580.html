Federal Reserve Bank

noun
regional central bank in the U.S. Federal Reserve System
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜrv bæŋk / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜːv bæŋk / (BrE)
one of the twelve regional banks in the United States that form part of the Federal Reserve System, serving as a central bank for their district and acting as a depository for local banks

Examples

The Federal Reserve Bank district
Each district has its own Federal Reserve Bank responsible for regulating local member banks.
Local commercial banks often deposit excess funds with their regional Federal Reserve Bank.
The Federal Reserve Bank of New York is one of the most influential regional banks in the system.
