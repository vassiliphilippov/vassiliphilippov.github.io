dehorn

verb
remove or prevent animal horns
9.9 6.4
Pronunciation: / diˈhɔrn / (AmE), / diˈhɔːn / (BrE)
to remove or prevent the growth of horns on an animal, especially livestock

Examples

To dehorn cattle
It is standard practice to dehorn cattle on many ranches for safety reasons.
Special tools are used to dehorn goats when they are young.
The farmer decided to dehorn the calves to make them easier to manage.
Farmers sometimes dehorn livestock for safety and management reasons.
It is common practice to dehorn young cattle to prevent injuries to other animals.
The veterinarian was called to dehorn several goats on the farm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disbud - remove the horn buds from a young animal to prevent horn growth
