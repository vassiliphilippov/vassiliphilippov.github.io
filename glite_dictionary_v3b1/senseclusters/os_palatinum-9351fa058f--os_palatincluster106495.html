os palatinum

noun
bone forming back of hard palate
9.9
Pronunciation: / oʊs pəˈlætəˌnəm / (AmE), / əʊs pəˈlætɪnəm / (BrE)
either of two irregularly shaped bones that form the back part of the hard palate, contributing to the structure of the nasal cavity and the floor of the orbits

Examples

The os palatinum bone
A tumor invading the os palatinum may affect both the palate and the eye socket.
During surgery, the doctor identified the os palatinum as the site of the fracture.
The os palatinum plays a crucial role in forming the structure of the nasal cavity.
