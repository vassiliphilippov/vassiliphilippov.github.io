timeworn

adjective
old and worn
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪmwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmwɔːn / (BrE)
old and showing signs of long use or age

Examples

Timeworn furniture
The timeworn book had yellowed pages and a cracked spine.
They admired the timeworn statue in the center of the old town square.
Her timeworn suitcase had traveled to many countries over the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antique - very old and often valuable
  • antonym: new - recently made or acquired; not old
  • synonym: worn - damaged or thin from continuous use
