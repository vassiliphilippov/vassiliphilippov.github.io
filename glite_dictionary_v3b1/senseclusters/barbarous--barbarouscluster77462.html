barbarous

adjective

(of a person or action) extremely cruel or brutal; able or willing to inflict pain or suffering.

Examples

Barbarous acts
Historical records detail the barbarous punishments once inflicted on criminals.
She described the attack as a barbarous act that shocked the entire nation.

Synonyms

    4.8 11/m
    brutal is violently harsh and causes physical pain, while barbarous stresses extreme cruelty and lack of humane restraint.
  • barbaric
    6.3 3/m
    barbaric feels uncivilized and primitive, often while barbarous focuses on extreme cruelty and harming others.
  • atrocious
    7.6 0.8/m
    atrocious describes shocking, horrible acts that shock people, while barbarous describes very cruel behavior that hurts others.
  • cruel
    2.7 20/m
    cruel means willing to cause pain to others, while barbarous implies an extreme, brutal readiness to injure or punish.
  • vicious
    5.2 9/m
    vicious is deliberately mean or aggressive and often personal, while barbarous is more broadly extremely cruel or brutal.
  • savage
    4.5 2/m
    savage is wild, fierce, and without control, while barbarous highlights deliberate extreme cruelty and lack of civil restraint.
