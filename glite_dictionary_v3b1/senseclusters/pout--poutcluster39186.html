pout

noun
a small bottom-dwelling fish of northern seas
Pronunciation: / paʊt / (AmE), / paʊt / (BrE)
A small marine fish of northern seas, related to the cod, with a barbel on the chin; especially of the genus Trisopterus.

Examples

A sea pout
Fishermen often catch pout while trawling in the North Sea.
Several pout were swimming near the rocky seabed.
The diet of the grey seal includes pout along with other bottom-dwellers.
