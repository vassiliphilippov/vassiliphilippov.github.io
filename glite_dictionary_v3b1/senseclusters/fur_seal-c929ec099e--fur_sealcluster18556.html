fur seal

noun
large eared seal with thick fur
7.2 3.3
Pronunciation: /fɜːr siːl/ (AmE), /fɜː siːl/ (BrE)
a large eared seal with thick fur, found in cold seas and valued for its fur

Examples

The fur seal population
The fur seal population has declined drastically due to overhunting in the past.
Unlike true seals, the fur seal has visible external ears and thick, luxurious fur.
We spotted a young fur seal basking on the rocks near the shore.
A fur seal can be distinguished from a true seal by its external ears and long flippers.
The fur seal population has declined due to overhunting in the past century.
The thick underfur of the fur seal was once highly prized for making coats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sea lion - a type of eared seal distinct from fur seals
  • synonym: seal - a marine mammal of the family Phocidae or Otariidae
  • antonym: true seal - a seal lacking external ear flaps, unlike fur seals
