Ovidian

noun
follower of Ovid
Pronunciation: / oʊˈvɪdiən / (AmE), / əʊˈvɪdiən / (BrE)
a person who imitates or is strongly influenced by the Roman poet Ovid, especially in poetic style or themes

Examples

An Ovidian poet
The literary critic described her as an Ovidian because of her fascination with mythological transformation.
Many Renaissance poets were considered Ovidians due to their use of classical themes.
He proudly called himself an Ovidian, drawing inspiration from ancient Roman poetry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classicist - a person who studies or admires classical literature
  • antonym: modernist - a person who supports modern ideas in art or literature
