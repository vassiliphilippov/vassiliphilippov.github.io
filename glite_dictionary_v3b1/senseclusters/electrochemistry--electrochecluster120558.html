electrochemistry

noun
study of chemical and electrical interactions
2.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
The branch of chemistry concerned with the chemical effects of electricity and the generation of electricity by chemical reactions.

Examples

Branch of electrochemistry
Advances in electrochemistry have led to improved methods for water purification and energy storage.
His research in electrochemistry focuses on reactions occurring at the surface of electrodes.
She studied electrochemistry to understand how batteries generate power in electronic devices.
