billow

noun
large sea wave
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪləʊ / (BrE)
a large sea wave, often used poetically or in reference to a rolling mass of water

Examples

Ocean billow
After the storm, gigantic billows rolled across the surface of the ocean.
From the cliff, they watched billows crashing onto the jagged rocks below.
The poet described the billows as mountains moving restlessly beneath the moon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wave - a long body of water curling into an arched form and breaking on the shore
