horchata

noun
sweet nut- or grain-based beverage
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈtʃɑtə / (AmE), / ɔːˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
A traditional Spanish or Latin American beverage made from ground nuts, seeds, or grains, often sweetened and served cold.

Examples

A glass of horchata
Many Mexican restaurants serve horchata alongside their traditional dishes.
On a hot day, nothing is more refreshing than a cold glass of horchata.
She learned to make homemade horchata using rice and cinnamon.
