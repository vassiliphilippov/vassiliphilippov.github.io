canulization

noun
insertion of a cannula into an organ
Pronunciation: / ˌkæn.jə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæn.juː.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the insertion of a cannula or tube into a hollow body organ, typically to allow for fluid drainage or medication administration

Examples

Canulization procedure
Proper technique during canulization reduces the risk of infection.
Repeated canulization of the vein may cause irritation or phlebitis.
The nurse performed canulization quickly to administer the medication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catheterization - insertion of a catheter into a body cavity or vessel
