depersonalization

noun
feeling of detachment from one's self
Pronunciation: / diˌpɜrsənələˈzeɪʃən /; / diːˌpɝːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌpɜrsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diːˌpɜːsənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diːˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a psychological state or condition in which a person feels detached from themselves or loses their sense of personal identity

Examples

Depersonalization after trauma
After a traumatic experience, John often struggled with depersonalization and felt disconnected from his emotions.
People experiencing depersonalization may describe feeling as if they are outside their own bodies.
Prolonged stress and anxiety can sometimes trigger episodes of depersonalization.
After months in the corporate world, he began to experience depersonalization and felt like just another number.
Feelings of depersonalization can be common during times of extreme stress or trauma.
The novel explores themes of depersonalization in a society obsessed with conformity.
During periods of intense stress, he suffered episodes of depersonalisation that made daily life difficult.
People experiencing depersonalisation often report feeling like they are observing themselves from outside their body.
Therapists use specific techniques to help patients cope with severe depersonalisation symptoms.
