fibromatosis

noun
fibrous tissue growth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbroʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrəʊməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a condition marked by the formation of nodular thickening of fibrous tissue in the skin or fascia, often causing lumps or contractures

Examples

Fibromatosis skin lumps
The doctor diagnosed the lumps in her palm as fibromatosis after a physical examination.
Surgery may be required if fibromatosis causes severe contractures in the hands or feet.
Early treatment of fibromatosis can help prevent loss of movement in affected areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contracture - a permanent tightening of muscles, tendons, or skin
  • synonym: fibroma - a benign tumor made of fibrous or connective tissue
