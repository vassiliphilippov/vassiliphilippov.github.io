diaconate

noun
body of deacons
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈækənət / (AmE), / daɪˈækənət / (BrE)
the collective body of deacons serving in a particular church, diocese, or denomination

Examples

The diaconate leads services
The diaconate played a key role in organizing the church's outreach programs.
After years of service, she was appointed to the diaconate by the bishop.
The annual meeting of the diaconate addressed important issues facing the congregation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clergy - the body of people ordained for religious duties
  • antonym: laity - members of a religious community who are not clergy
