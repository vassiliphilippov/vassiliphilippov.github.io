wadmal

noun
coarse woolen cloth
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːdməl / (AmE), / ˈwɒdmæl / (BrE)
a coarse woolen cloth used as a unit of value or payment, especially in medieval Iceland and Scandinavia

Examples

Wadmal payment
In medieval Iceland, farmers paid their taxes with wadmal instead of coins.
A length of wadmal was often used to settle debts in Scandinavian markets.
The value of wadmal depended on its quality and the skill of the weaver.

Related Terms

  • synonym: currency - something used as a medium of exchange
  • synonym: homespun - cloth made at home, often coarse and simple
