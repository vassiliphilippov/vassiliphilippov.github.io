chip away

verb
remove or reduce gradually
Pronunciation: / tʃɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tʃɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually remove or reduce something, especially by breaking off small pieces or weakening it bit by bit

Examples

To chip away rock
Over many years, water can chip away at even the hardest rock.
She slowly chipped away at her debt until it was finally paid off.
The opposition tried to chip away at the government's credibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: build up - to increase or enhance gradually
  • synonym: dismantle - gradually take apart or break down
  • synonym: erode - to gradually wear away or be worn away
