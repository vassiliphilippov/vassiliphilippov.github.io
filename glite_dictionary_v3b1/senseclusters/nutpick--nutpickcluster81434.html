nutpick

verb
to highlight extreme argument
9.9
Pronunciation: /nʌt.ˈpɪk/ (AmE), /nʌt.ˈpɪk/ (BrE)
to focus on and criticize the most extreme or foolish argument made by someone on the opposing side, ignoring more reasonable points.

Examples

To nutpick a comment
It's unfair to nutpick one comment and ignore the main argument.
Some journalists nutpick social media posts to make a group look unreasonable.
Instead of discussing the real issues, he chose to nutpick the most ridiculous statement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: straw man - to misrepresent someone's argument to make it easier to attack
