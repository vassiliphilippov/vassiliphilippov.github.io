ottava

noun
eight-line stanza
Pronunciation: / oʊˈtɑːvə / (AmE), / ɒˈtɑːvə / (BrE)
a stanza of eight lines, especially used in Italian poetry or music

Examples

An ottava stanza
The poem was structured in the traditional ottava form, giving it a rhythmic flow.
In Italian literature, the ottava is a common stanza used for epic poetry.
The composer set the lyrics to music using an ottava to match the poetic style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: octave - a group of eight lines in poetry or a musical interval of eight notes
