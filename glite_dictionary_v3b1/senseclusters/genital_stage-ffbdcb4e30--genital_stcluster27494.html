genital stage

noun
psychoanalytic stage of adolescent sexual development
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl steɪdʒ / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) the fifth stage in Freud's theory of psychosexual development, occurring during adolescence, when sexual interests mature and focus on adult relationships.

Examples

The genital stage in adolescence
According to Freud, the genital stage begins at puberty and continues into adulthood.
During the genital stage, individuals are capable of mature sexual intimacy.
Psychologists debate the relevance of the genital stage in modern developmental theory.
