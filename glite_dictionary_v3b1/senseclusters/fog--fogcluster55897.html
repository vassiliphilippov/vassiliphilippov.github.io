fog

noun
mental confusion; lack of clarity
6.0 3.16/m
Pronunciation: / fÉ‘g / (AmE), / fÉ’g / (BrE)
A state of mental confusion or lack of clarity that makes it difficult to think or understand clearly.

Examples

Mind in a fog
After the accident, she was in a fog and couldn't remember what happened.
The medication left him in a mental fog for several hours.
Trying to work through my fog of exhaustion, I struggled to concentrate on the task.

Synonyms

  • mental confusion
    4.9 0.05/m
    mental confusion is a strong, often lasting state of unclear thought and disorientation, while fog is a short, vague loss of clarity.
  • confusion
    1.8 30/m
    confusion is a broad state of not understanding or deciding clearly, while fog implies a mild, misty difficulty thinking.
  • haze
    6.2 1.48/m
    haze is a vague, dreamy lack of clarity that makes things hard to see in the mind, while fog is more sudden and clouding.
  • muddiness
    7.4 0.00/m
    muddiness is a messy, unclear state of thought or writing with mixed ideas, while fog is a softer, more mental clouding.
  • incoherence
    7.3 0.79/m
    incoherence is speech or writing that lacks logical order and is hard to follow, while fog names a general, unclear mental state.
