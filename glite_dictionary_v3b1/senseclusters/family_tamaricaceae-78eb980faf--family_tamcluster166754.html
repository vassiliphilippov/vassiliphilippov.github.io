family Tamaricaceae

noun
family of desert shrubs and trees (Tamaricaceae)
Pronunciation: / ˌfæməli təˌmærɪˈkeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ˌtæmərɪˈkeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of desert shrubs and trees, mostly salt-tolerant and drought-resistant (halophytes and xerophytes), including tamarisks.

Examples

family Tamaricaceae plants
Desert landscapes are often populated with members of the family Tamaricaceae, such as tamarisks.
Researchers study the family Tamaricaceae to understand plant survival in harsh climates.
The family Tamaricaceae includes plants well-adapted to arid and saline environments.

Related Terms
