South Sea

noun
sea south of the equator, esp. South Pacific
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊθ ˌsiː / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθ ˌsiː / (BrE)
any sea located south of the equator, especially referring to the South Pacific Ocean, often used historically and literarily.

Examples

The South Sea islands
Historically, European traders sought new routes across the South Sea to reach the spice islands.
In literature, the South Sea often evokes images of adventure and mystery.
The explorers set sail for the South Sea, dreaming of uncharted islands and endless horizons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: South Pacific - the part of the Pacific Ocean south of the equator
