bushwhack

verb
live in the bush as a fugitive or guerrilla
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃˌwæk / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌwæk / (BrE)
to live in the bush as a fugitive or as a guerrilla, often to avoid authorities or conduct surprise attacks

Examples

To bushwhack for safety
After escaping the prison, the outlaws had to bushwhack for weeks to avoid capture.
The rebels bushwhacked in remote mountain regions until the conflict ended.
To survive, the fugitives bushwhacked in the dense forests along the border.
