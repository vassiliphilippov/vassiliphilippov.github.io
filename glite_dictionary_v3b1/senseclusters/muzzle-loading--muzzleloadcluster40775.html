muzzle-loading

adjective
loaded through the muzzle (of firearms)
6.3 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌz.ə.loʊ.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈmʌz.əl.loʊ.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈmʌz.ə.ləʊ.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈmʌz.əl.ləʊ.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
(of a firearm) designed to be loaded with ammunition through the muzzle rather than the breech.

Examples

Muzzle-loading rifle
Early soldiers often used muzzle-loading rifles, which required loading gunpowder and shot from the open end of the barrel.
He demonstrated how to fire a muzzle-loading cannon at the historical reenactment.
The museum displays several muzzle-loading muskets from the 18th century.
