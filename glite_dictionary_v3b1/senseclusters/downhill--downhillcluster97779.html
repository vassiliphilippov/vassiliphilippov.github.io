downhill

noun
the downward slope of a hill
1.4 1.91/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdaÊŠnËŒhÉªl / (AmE), / ËˆdaÊŠnËŒhÉªl / (BrE)
a downward slope of a hill or road; the part of a hill that descends.

Examples

A steep downhill
After a long climb, the cyclists enjoyed coasting down the downhill.
Loose rocks made the downhill treacherous for hikers.
The skiers gathered at the top of the downhill before racing each other to the bottom.

Synonyms

  • downslope
    3.7 0.57/m
    downslope is the downward side of land used in maps or technical speech, while downhill is the everyday word for that slope.
  • declivity
    9.6 0.11/m
    declivity is a formal, literary word for a steep hill slope, while downhill is the common, everyday name for that descending part.
  • descent
    3.7 8.23/m
    descent is often the act of moving down or a slope in travel contexts, while downhill refers specifically to the sloping side itself.
  • decline
    3.5 0.95/m
    decline is commonly used to mean a fall or decrease and can mean a slope, while downhill specifically names the sloping side of a hill.

  • antonym: ascent - an upward slope or climb
