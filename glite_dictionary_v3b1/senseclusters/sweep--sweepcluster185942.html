sweep

verb
to force into a situation or condition
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiːp / (BrE)
to force someone into a particular situation, condition, or course of action, often suddenly or compellingly

Examples

To sweep into action
Many voters found themselves swept up by the campaign's momentum.
She was swept into the debate before she knew it.
The nation was swept into a wave of excitement after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drag - to pull someone into a situation, often unwillingly
