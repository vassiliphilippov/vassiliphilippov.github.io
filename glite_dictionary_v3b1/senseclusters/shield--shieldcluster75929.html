shield

noun
hard protective animal covering
3.5 4.42/m
Pronunciation: /  Éild / (AmE), /  ÉiÀêld / (BrE)
the hard protective outer covering of certain animals, such as arthropods and turtles

Examples

Turtle shield
Biologists examined the shield of the arthropod under a microscope for structural differences.
Some insects have a tough shield called an exoskeleton that helps them survive harsh environments.
The turtle's shield protects it from most predators in the wild.

Synonyms

  • carapace
    8.7 0.79/m
    carapace is the hard curved outer shell of some animals like crabs and turtles, while shield is a general protective outer covering.
  • cuticle
    6.3 0.30/m
    cuticle is a thin tough skin layer on insects and plants, while shield refers to a thicker hard outer covering in animals.
  • shell
    1.0 22/m
    shell is a common word for hard cases on animals, eggs, or nuts, while shield means the animal's protective outer covering.
