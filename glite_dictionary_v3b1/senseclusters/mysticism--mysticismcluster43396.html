mysticism

noun
obscure or irrational thought
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Obscure or irrational thinking, especially beliefs or ideas that are difficult to explain in rational terms.

Examples

Philosophical mysticism
His lecture was criticized for descending into mysticism rather than offering clear arguments.
Some scientific theories are sometimes dismissed as mysticism when lacking empirical evidence.
The philosopher rejected all forms of mysticism in favor of logical analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rationalism - The doctrine that reason rather than experience is the foundation of certainty in knowledge
