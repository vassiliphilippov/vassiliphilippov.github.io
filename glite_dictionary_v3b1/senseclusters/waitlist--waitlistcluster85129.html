waitlist

verb
to put on waiting list
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪtˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈweɪtˌlɪst / (BrE)
to place someone on a list to wait for an opening or available spot, especially in a program or event

Examples

To waitlist a student
The university had to waitlist several qualified applicants due to limited space.
If the class is full, they will waitlist you and notify you if a spot opens.
Many students were waitlisted for the popular summer program this year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - to allow someone to enter or join
  • antonym: enroll - to officially register or sign up for something
