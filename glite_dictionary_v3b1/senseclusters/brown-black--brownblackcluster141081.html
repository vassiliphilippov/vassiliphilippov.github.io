brown-black

adjective
black with a tinge of brown
Pronunciation: / ˌbraʊnˈblæk / (AmE), / ˌbraʊnˈblæk / (BrE)
having a black color with a tinge of brown; dark brownish-black.

Examples

A brown-black dress
She wore a brown-black dress that shimmered in the evening light.
The bird had a brown-black plumage that blended well with the forest floor.
The dog’s fur is mostly brown-black with a few lighter patches near his paws.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: black - the darkest color, resulting from the absence of light
  • degree lower: brown - a color produced by mixing red, yellow, and black
