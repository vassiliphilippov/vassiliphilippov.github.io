crupper

noun
strap under horse's tail for saddle
5.2 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌpər / (AmE), / ˈkrʌpə / (BrE)
A strap attached to the back of a saddle and looped under a horse's tail to prevent the saddle from sliding forward.

Examples

Leather crupper strap
Over time, the leather crupper became worn and needed replacing for the horse's comfort.
The rider checked the crupper before setting off to ensure the saddle stayed securely in place.
Without a proper crupper, the saddle kept slipping forward on the steep mountain trail.
