Romani

noun
member of the Romani ethnic group
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mə.ni / (BrE)
A member of an ethnic group with origins in northern India, now living mainly in Europe, North Africa, and North America, traditionally known for their itinerant lifestyle and distinctive culture.

Examples

A Romani family
Many Romani migrated across Europe over the centuries, adapting to different cultures.
The Romani people have a rich tradition of music and storytelling.
There are large Romani communities in Romania, Bulgaria, and Spain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gypsy - another term for a member of the Romani people (now often considered offensive)
