neutrally

adverb
without strong style or bias
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəli / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəli / (BrE)
in a way that does not show strong emotion, opinion, or bias; without taking sides

Examples

Speak neutrally
The room was decorated neutrally to appeal to a wide range of guests.
She spoke neutrally during the debate, avoiding taking sides.
The report was written neutrally to ensure it did not favor any party.
The judge listened neutrally to both sides before making a decision.
He described the situation neutrally, without showing any personal bias.
During the debate, she responded neutrally to avoid offending anyone.
The moderator spoke neutrally during the debate to ensure fairness.
It is important to report the news neutrally without personal opinions.
She tried to respond neutrally to avoid offending anyone in the discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biasedly - in a way that shows preference for one side
  • antonym: boldly - in a way that is noticeable or stands out
  • antonym: emotionally - in a way that shows strong feelings
  • synonym: impartially - in a fair and unbiased way
  • synonym: objectively - in a way based on facts, not feelings or opinions
  • synonym: unobtrusively - in a way that does not attract attention
