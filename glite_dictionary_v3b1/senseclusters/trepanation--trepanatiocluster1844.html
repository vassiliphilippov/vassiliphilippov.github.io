trepanation

noun
skull drilling surgery
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɛpəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrɛpəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a hole is drilled or cut into the skull

Examples

Cranial trepanation
Archaeologists found evidence of trepanation in ancient human skulls.
Modern medicine rarely uses trepanation except in specific emergency cases.
The history of trepanation shows it was practiced in many early civilizations.

Related Terms
