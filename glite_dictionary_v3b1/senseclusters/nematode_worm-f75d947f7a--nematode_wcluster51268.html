nematode worm

noun
type of elongated unsegmented worm
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛməˌtoʊd wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈnɛmətəʊd wɜːm / (BrE)
an unsegmented worm with a long, slender, cylindrical body pointed at both ends; most are free-living but some are parasitic

Examples

A nematode worm species
Medical researchers are studying how a nematode worm invades its host to better understand parasitic infections.
Some diseases in crops are caused by nematode worms that damage plant roots.
The scientist found a nematode worm living in the soil sample collected from the garden.
