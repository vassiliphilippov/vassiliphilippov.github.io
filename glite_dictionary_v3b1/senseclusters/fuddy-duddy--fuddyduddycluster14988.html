fuddy-duddy

noun
old-fashioned or overly conservative person
9.7 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfÊŒdiËŒdÊŒdi / (AmE), / ËˆfÊŒdiËŒdÊŒdi / (BrE)
a person, especially an older one, who is considered old-fashioned, fussy, or overly conservative in attitude or appearance

Examples

An old fuddy-duddy
Don't be a fuddy-duddy; try joining us for salsa dancing tonight!
In her conservative clothes and stern attitude, she was sometimes called the office fuddy-duddy.
My uncle is such a fuddy-duddy when it comes to technology and prefers handwritten letters over emails.

Synonyms

  • fuddy
    9.9 0.01/m
    fuddy is a mildly old-fashioned label for a very conservative person, while fuddy-duddy feels more playful and fussy.
  • old fogey
    8.2 0.06/m
    old fogey is a slightly rude term for an older person who resists change, while fuddy-duddy sounds more playful.
  • old stick
    9.6 0.01/m
    old stick is a casual phrase for someone very conventional and set in ways, while fuddy-duddy implies fussy old-fashioned habits.
  • dowd
    9.9 0.00/m
    dowd is used for an unfashionable person, often a woman who dresses plainly, while fuddy-duddy focuses on fussy conservative attitudes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernist - a person who advocates new ideas or behaviors
  • synonym: square - a person considered conventional and old-fashioned
  • synonym: stick-in-the-mud - a person who is reluctant to try new things; very conservative
