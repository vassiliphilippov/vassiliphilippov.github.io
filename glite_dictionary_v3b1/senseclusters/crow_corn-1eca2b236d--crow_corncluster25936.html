crow corn

noun
plant of the genus Aletris
Pronunciation: / kɹoʊ kɔrn / (AmE), / krəʊ kɔːn / (BrE)
any of several perennial plants of the genus Aletris, with grasslike leaves and bitter roots traditionally used in folk remedies for colic

Examples

The crow corn plant
The pale flowers of crow corn bloom in late spring and are easily overlooked among grasses.
Traditional healers would sometimes use the bitter root of crow corn to treat stomach complaints.
We spotted several clusters of crow corn growing in the marshy field.
