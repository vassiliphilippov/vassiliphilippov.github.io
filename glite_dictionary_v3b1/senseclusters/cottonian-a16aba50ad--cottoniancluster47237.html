Cottonian

noun
follower of John Cotton
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑːˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / kɒˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
a follower or supporter of John Cotton and his theological views, especially in colonial America

Examples

A loyal Cottonian
As a devoted Cottonian, she supported his strict moral teachings in the colony.
Many Cottonians gathered to discuss the influence of John Cotton's sermons.
The historian wrote about the impact of Cottonian beliefs on early American society.
