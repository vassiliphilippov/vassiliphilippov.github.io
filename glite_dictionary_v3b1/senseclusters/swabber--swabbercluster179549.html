swabber

noun
sterile sampling tool
6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈswɑːb.ər/ (AmE), /ˈswɒb.ə/ (BrE)
a small sterile stick or pad used to collect biological samples by wiping a surface or body opening

Examples

Sterile swabber
The nurse used a swabber to collect a sample from the patient's throat.
Each swabber must be kept sterile before use to avoid contamination.
After taking the sample, the swabber was sealed in a special container.
