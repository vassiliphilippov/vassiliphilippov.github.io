clinker-built

adjective
constructed with overlapping planks (nautical)
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪŋkɚ bɪlt / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋkə bɪlt / (BrE)
(of a boat or ship) having a hull constructed with overlapping planks or boards.

Examples

Clinker-built boat
Many traditional Scandinavian boats are clinker-built, a testament to historic craftsmanship.
The old fishing boat was clinker-built to withstand rough northern seas.
You can easily spot a clinker-built vessel by the overlapping edges of its wooden planks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carvel-built - having a hull with planks laid edge to edge, not overlapping
