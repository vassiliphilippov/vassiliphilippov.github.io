chess clock

noun
dual timer used in chess
Pronunciation: / tʃɛs klɑk / (AmE), / tʃɛs klɒk / (BrE)
a device with two linked clocks and a switch, used to track each player's time in a chess game

Examples

A chess clock device
He forgot to press the chess clock and lost valuable minutes from his game.
Modern tournaments often use a digital chess clock instead of an analog one.
The players started the chess clock as soon as the opening move was played.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stopwatch - device used to measure the amount of time elapsed
  • synonym: timer - device that measures time intervals
