inversion

noun
change in optical rotation in chemistry
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A process in which the direction of optical rotation of a compound changes from dextrorotatory to levorotatory or vice versa.

Examples

Optical inversion
During the reaction, inversion occurs, shifting the solution from dextrorotatory to levorotatory.
Scientists measured the inversion of the substance by observing its change in optical rotation.
The inversion of sucrose produces a mixture of glucose and fructose with different optical properties.
