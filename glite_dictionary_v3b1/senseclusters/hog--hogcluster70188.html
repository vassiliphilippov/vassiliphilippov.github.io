hog

verb
take more than one's share; act greedily
Pronunciation: / hɑɡ / (AmE), / hɒɡ / (BrE)
to take or use more than one's share of something, often in a greedy way

Examples

To hog the remote
As soon as the pizza arrived, he started hogging the biggest slices for himself.
Don't hog all the blankets—let someone else have some.
She tends to hog the conversation whenever we're together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monopolize - to obtain exclusive possession or control of something
  • antonym: share - to use or enjoy something jointly with others
