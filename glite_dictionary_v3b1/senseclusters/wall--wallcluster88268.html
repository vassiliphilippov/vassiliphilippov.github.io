wall

noun
masonry fence or boundary structure
1.0
Pronunciation: / wɔl /; / wɑl / (AmE), / wɔːl / (BrE)
a vertical structure or surface that divides, encloses, or supports an area, such as in a building, fence, or as a rock face

Examples

A brick wall
He leaned against the cool wall as he waited for his friends.
The old stone wall at the edge of the garden is covered in ivy.
They built a high wall around the property to ensure privacy.
A crack appeared in the living room wall after the storm.
The children decorated the wall in their room with colorful posters.
They built a stone wall to separate the garden from the road.
She painted the interior walls of her kitchen a cheerful yellow.
The aquarium's thick glass walls allowed visitors to see even the largest fish clearly.
The laboratory's new containment vessel was reinforced with a double wall for extra safety.

  • synonym: partition - a structure dividing a space into parts
  • synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or bank around a fort, castle, or city
  • antonym: roof - the top covering of a building
  • degree lower: slope - a surface of which one end or side is at a higher level than another
