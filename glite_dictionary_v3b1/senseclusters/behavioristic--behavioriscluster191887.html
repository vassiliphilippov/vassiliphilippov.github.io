behavioristic

adjective
relating to behaviorism
Pronunciation: / bəˌheɪvjəˈrɪstɪk /; / bɪˌheɪvjəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / bɪˌheɪvjəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of behaviorism, a theory of learning that emphasizes observable behaviors over internal mental processes.

Examples

Behavioristic learning theory
His teaching style is distinctly behavioristic, emphasizing reinforcement and punishment.
Many early psychologists favored behavioristic methods over introspective ones.
The study was conducted using a behavioristic approach, focusing solely on observable actions.
Her research is rooted in behaviouristic psychology, often referencing classic conditioning experiments.
Many early 20th-century psychologists promoted behaviouristic methods to study learning and behavior.
The behaviouristic approach focuses solely on observable actions and disregards thoughts and emotions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: behavioral - relating to behavior, especially in psychology
  • synonym: behaviorist - someone who advocates or practices behaviorism
  • antonym: cognitive - relating to mental processes such as thinking and memory
  • antonym: cognitivist - someone who emphasizes internal mental processes; supporter of cognitivism
