pugnacious

adjective
eager to fight or argue
9.9 7.0
Pronunciation: /pʌɡ.ˈneɪ.ʃəs/ (AmE), /pʌɡ.ˈneɪ.ʃəs/ (BrE)
eager or quick to argue, quarrel, or fight; having a combative nature

Examples

A pugnacious boxer
Despite his small stature, the pugnacious boxer never backed down from a challenge.
Her pugnacious attitude made her stand out in the heated debate.
The pugnacious dog barked fiercely at anyone who came near its owner.
Despite his small size, the pugnacious dog barked at every stranger who approached.
She had a pugnacious attitude that often got her into arguments with her colleagues.
The debate became heated as the pugnacious candidate interrupted and challenged every point.

  • synonym: belligerent - hostile and aggressive; eager to fight
  • antonym: peaceful - not inclined to fight or argue; calm
