pachycephalosaurus

noun
herbivorous dinosaur with thick domed skull
9.9
Pronunciation: /pæk.ɪ.se.fə.lə.ˈɑːr.əs/ /; / /pæk.ɪ.se.fə.lə.ˈɔːr.əs/ (AmE), /pæk.ɪ.se.fə.lə.ˈsɔː.rəs/ (BrE)
A large, bipedal herbivorous dinosaur distinguished by a thick, domed skull roof, known as the largest boneheaded dinosaur ever discovered.

Examples

The pachycephalosaurus dinosaur
Fossil evidence suggests the pachycephalosaurus ate mainly plants and lived in what is now North America.
The pachycephalosaurus is famous for its extraordinary domed skull, which may have been used in head-butting contests.
We learned about the pachycephalosaurus in science class while studying Cretaceous dinosaurs.
