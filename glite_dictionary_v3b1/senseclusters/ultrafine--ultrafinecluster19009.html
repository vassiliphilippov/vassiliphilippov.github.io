ultrafine

adjective
extremely small
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈʌltrəˌfaɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌltrəˌfaɪn / (BrE)
extremely small in size, especially describing particles or fibers that are much smaller than usual

Examples

Ultrafine particles
The laboratory uses special equipment to measure ultrafine particles in the air.
She wore a scarf made from ultrafine silk threads that felt incredibly soft.
Modern filters are designed to trap even ultrafine dust and allergens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coarse - having a rough or large texture; not fine
  • synonym: microscopic - so small as to be visible only with a microscope
