milice

noun
French WWII militia
9.9
Pronunciation: / miˈlis / (AmE), / miˈliːs / (BrE)
a paramilitary force, especially the French militia that collaborated with the Nazis during World War II

Examples

French milice in WWII
The milice played a significant role in supporting the German occupation of France.
Many members of the milice were later prosecuted for their wartime actions.
The French Resistance often clashed with the milice during covert operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: militia - a military force made up of ordinary citizens
  • antonym: resistance - organized opposition to occupying forces, especially in WWII France
