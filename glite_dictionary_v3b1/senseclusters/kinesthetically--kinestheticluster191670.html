kinesthetically

adverb
in a kinesthetic manner
8.1
Pronunciation: / kɪˌnɛsˈθɛtɪkli /; / kɪnəsˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / kɪˌnɛsˈθɛtɪkli /; / kɪnɪsˈθɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that relates to or involves kinesthesia - the sense of body movement and position.

Examples

To learn kinesthetically
She learns most effectively kinesthetically, through activities that involve movement and touch.
The dancer expressed the story kinesthetically, using her body rather than words.
The test measured the ability to solve problems kinesthetically as well as visually.
He was able to navigate the maze kinaesthetically, sensing the walls with his body.
Many students learn best kinaesthetically, through hands-on activities and movement.
The therapist encouraged her patient to express emotions kinaesthetically through dance and movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: auditorily - in a manner relating to hearing; by means of sound
  • synonym: kinesthetic - relating to the sensation of movement or position
  • antonym: visually - in a manner relating to vision; by means of seeing
