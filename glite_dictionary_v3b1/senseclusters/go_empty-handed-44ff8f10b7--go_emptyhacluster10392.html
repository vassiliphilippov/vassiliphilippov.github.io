go empty-handed

verb
leave with nothing gained
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˌɛmpti ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˌɛmpti ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
to leave a place without obtaining or achieving anything; to receive nothing despite efforts

Examples

To go empty-handed home
After searching the whole market, she went empty-handed because nothing caught her eye.
Despite applying to several jobs, he went empty-handed after every interview.
Tom didn’t win any prizes at the fair and went empty-handed back home.
