opposition

noun
the relation between opposing things
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ɑːp.ə.ˈzɪʃ.ən/ /; / /əp.ə.ˈzɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ɒp.ə.ˈzɪʃ.ən/ /; / /əp.ə.ˈzɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the act, state, or process of opposing, resisting, or being in conflict with something or someone

Examples

Opposition to change
The novel explores the opposition between good and evil.
There was strong opposition between the two theories in the scientific community.
You can see the opposition of colors in the artist’s palette.
Environmental groups mounted fierce opposition to the construction project.
The government faced little opposition when passing the new law.
There has been strong opposition to the proposed tax increase.
After years of opposition, the two sides finally agreed to a compromise.
The workers faced strong opposition from management during the negotiations.
There was fierce opposition to the new government policies in several cities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - harmony; lack of conflict or opposition
  • synonym: confrontation - a hostile or argumentative situation or meeting between opposing parties
  • synonym: contradiction - the act of saying or showing the opposite
  • antonym: cooperation - the process of working together to the same end
  • synonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply; the act of fighting against something
  • antonym: support - assistance or backing for a cause or proposal
