resin

noun
sticky plant substance or synthetic material
4.7 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛz.ən / (AmE), / ˈrɛz.ɪn / (BrE)
a solid or semisolid sticky substance obtained from certain plants or produced synthetically, often used in making plastics, varnishes, or adhesives

Examples

Synthetic resin material
Modern adhesives often contain synthetic resin for extra strength and durability.
Pine trees secrete resin as a defense against insects and disease.
The artist used a clear resin to preserve natural specimens in jewelry.
