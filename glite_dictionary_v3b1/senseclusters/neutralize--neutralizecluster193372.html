neutralize

verb
make chemically neutral
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪz /; / ˈnutʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a substance chemically neutral or harmless, usually by a chemical reaction

Examples

To neutralize an acid
Adding enough sodium hydroxide will neutralize the acidic solution.
In the lab, they neutralized the sample before safe disposal.
The scientist carefully neutralized the acid with a base during the experiment.
Proper ventilation and disposal methods help neutralize harmful fumes in the facility.
Scientists worked quickly to neutralize the chemical spill before it spread.
The cleaning agent is designed to neutralize dangerous acids in laboratories.
The chemist needed to neutralise the acidic solution before disposing of it.
They used baking soda to neutralise the spilled vinegar on the floor.
You can neutralise a base by slowly adding an acid to it.
