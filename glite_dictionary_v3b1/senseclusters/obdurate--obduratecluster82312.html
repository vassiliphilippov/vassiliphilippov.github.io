obdurate

adjective
stubborn; persistent in wrongdoing
6.0 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːb.dər.ət/ /; / /ˈɑːb.djə.rət/ (AmE), /ˈɒb.djʊ.rət/ /; / /ˈɒb.dʒə.rət/ (BrE)
stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or behavior, even when others try to persuade you

Examples

An obdurate refusal
Despite repeated warnings, he remained obdurate in his refusal to apologize.
Even when confronted with clear evidence, the defendant was obdurate and denied any wrongdoing.
Her obdurate attitude made compromise impossible during the negotiations.
Even the obdurate judge was moved by the heartfelt plea for mercy.
His obdurate stance made compromise impossible in the negotiations.
She remained obdurate, refusing to forgive him despite his sincere apology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intransigent - unwilling to change one's views or to agree
  • antonym: soft-hearted - easily moved by sympathy or compassion
  • synonym: unyielding - not giving way to pressure; firm or inflexible
  • antonym: yielding - willing to give in or adapt
