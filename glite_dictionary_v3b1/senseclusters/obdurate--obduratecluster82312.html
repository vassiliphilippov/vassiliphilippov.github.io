Synonyms

  • obstinate
    7.5 1.15/m
    obstinate describes a person who refuses to change in everyday situations, while obdurate sounds more formal and fixed.
  • intransigent
    8.1 0.79/m
    intransigent means refusing to agree or give ground in talks, while obdurate shows a personal, stubborn refusal to change.
  • adamant
    6.8 3.55/m
    adamant stays firm and clear in speech, while obdurate suggests a harsher, more lasting refusal to change.
  • pertinacious
    9.8 0.05/m
    pertinacious holds firmly to a view for a long time, while obdurate often adds a sense of moral coldness or stubbornness.
  • stubborn
    2.9 8.86/m
    stubborn is the everyday word for refusing to change, while obdurate is more formal and implies stronger resistance.
  • inflexible
    4.7 1.29/m
    inflexible means unable or unwilling to adapt in many areas, while obdurate focuses on refusing to change an opinion.
  • headstrong
    4.1 0.87/m
    headstrong acts on will and often ignores advice, while obdurate stubbornly refuses to change beliefs despite others' persuasion.
