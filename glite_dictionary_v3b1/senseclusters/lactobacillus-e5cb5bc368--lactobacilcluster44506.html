Lactobacillus

noun
genus of lactic acid bacteria
5.8
Pronunciation: /læk.toʊ.bə.ˈsɪl.əs/ /; / /lək.toʊ.bə.ˈsɪl.əs/ (AmE), /læk.təʊ.bə.ˈsɪl.əs/ /; / /lək.təʊ.bə.ˈsɪl.əs/ (BrE)
a genus of rod-shaped, Gram-positive bacteria that ferment sugars into lactic acid, commonly found in the human gut and used in the production of yogurt and other fermented foods

Examples

Lactobacillus bacteria in yogurt
Lactobacillus is essential in the fermentation of yogurt and certain cheeses.
Many probiotic supplements contain live Lactobacillus cultures to support digestive health.
Researchers discovered a new strain of Lactobacillus in traditional fermented foods.
