fecundation

noun
making something fertile (agriculture)
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛkənˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfiːkʌnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something fertile, especially by applying fertilizer or manure

Examples

Soil fecundation
Improper fecundation can lead to reduced agricultural productivity.
Many traditional farming methods rely on natural fecundation through composting and manure.
The fecundation of the soil was necessary to improve this year's crop yield.
