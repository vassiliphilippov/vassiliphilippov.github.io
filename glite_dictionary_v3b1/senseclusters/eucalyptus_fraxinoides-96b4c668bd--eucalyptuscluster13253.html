Eucalyptus fraxinoides

noun
Australian tree with bark like ash, used for timber
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈkælɪptəs ˌfræksɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs ˌfraksɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A large tree native to southeastern Australia, with dark compact bark on the lower trunk and smooth white bark above, yielding timber similar to that of European or American ashes.

Examples

Tall Eucalyptus fraxinoides tree

The smooth white bark of Eucalyptus fraxinoides makes it easily recognizable among other tree species.
