butyl rubber

noun
synthetic rubber from isobutylene
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbjuː.təl ˈrʌb.ər/ /; / /ˈbjuː.taɪl ˈrʌb.ər/ (AmE), /ˈbjuː.təl ˈrʌb.ə/ /; / /ˈbjuː.taɪl ˈrʌb.ə/ (BrE)
a synthetic rubber made by polymerizing isobutylene, valued for its impermeability to gases and resistance to chemicals

Examples

Butyl rubber inner tubes
Because of its impermeability, butyl rubber is used in inner tubes and protective clothing.
The laboratory was stocked with sheets of butyl rubber for chemical experiments.
Tires made with butyl rubber have excellent resistance to air loss.
