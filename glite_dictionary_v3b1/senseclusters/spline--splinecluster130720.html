spline

noun
flexible drafting strip for drawing curves
8.1
Pronunciation: / splaɪn / (AmE), / splaɪn / (BrE)
a thin, flexible strip of wood, metal, or rubber used as a guide for drawing smooth curves or as a joining piece in construction or carpentry

Examples

Wooden spline strip

In traditional drafting, a spline helps create accurate, flowing lines.
The architect used a spline to draw the graceful curve of the roof.
A metal spline was inserted into the joint to reinforce the frame.
She drew the outline using a flexible spline to ensure a smooth curve.
The carpenter used a spline to create the curved edge of the table.
