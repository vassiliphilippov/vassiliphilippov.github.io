bandpass

noun
range of allowed frequencies
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbænd.pæs/ /; / /ˈbænd.pəs/ (AmE), /ˈbænd.pɑːs/ /; / /ˈbænd.pæs/ (BrE)
the range of frequencies that a device or filter allows to pass through while blocking others

Examples

Bandpass range
The engineer adjusted the bandpass to improve the clarity of the audio signal.
A narrow bandpass is useful for isolating specific frequencies in scientific measurements.
The device's bandpass determines which radio stations it can receive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: notch filter - a filter that blocks a narrow range of frequencies
