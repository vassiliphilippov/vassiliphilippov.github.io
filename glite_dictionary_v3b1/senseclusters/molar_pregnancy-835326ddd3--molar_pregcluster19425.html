A rare complication of pregnancy in which tissue that normally develops into the placenta becomes an abnormal mass of cysts, often leading to the loss of the fetus.

Examples

A molar pregnancy complication
A molar pregnancy is typically detected early due to unusual symptoms and rapid uterine growth.
The doctor diagnosed her condition as a molar pregnancy after reviewing the ultrasound results.
Treatment for a molar pregnancy may involve surgery to remove the abnormal tissue.

Synonyms

  • hydatidiform mole
    9.9 0.01/m
    a hydatidiform mole is a medical term for an abnormal uterine growth of cysts, while a molar pregnancy describes that pregnancy complication.
  • hydatid mole
    9.9 0.00/m
    a hydatid mole is the older name for cyst-like placental growths, while a molar pregnancy is the standard modern medical term.
