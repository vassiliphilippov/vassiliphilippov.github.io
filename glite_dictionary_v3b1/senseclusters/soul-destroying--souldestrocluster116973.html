soul-destroying

adjective
deeply demoralizing or depressing
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊl dɪˌstrɔɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊl dɪˌstrɔɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely dull, depressing, or demoralizing in a way that deadens one's spirit or enthusiasm.

Examples

A soul-destroying job
She found the endless meetings to be soul-destroying and uninspiring.
The repetitive data entry job was soul-destroying for many new employees.
Working in isolation for months on end can become truly soul-destroying.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demoralizing - causing someone to lose confidence or hope
  • antonym: uplifting - making someone feel better or more hopeful
