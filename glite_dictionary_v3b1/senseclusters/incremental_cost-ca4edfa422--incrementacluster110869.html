incremental cost

noun
additional cost per extra unit produced
6.8 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.krəˈmɛn.t̬əl kɑst / (AmE), / ˌɪŋ.krɪˈmɛn.təl kɒst / (BrE)
The amount by which total costs increase or decrease as a result of producing one additional or one fewer unit of output.

Examples

The incremental cost per unit
Due to efficiencies, the incremental cost per unit decreases as output increases.
The company calculated the incremental cost of expanding production by 1,000 units.
When making business decisions, it's important to consider the incremental cost of each option.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marginal cost - the cost of producing one additional unit of a product
