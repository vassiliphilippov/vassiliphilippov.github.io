footrope

noun
rope for standing aloft
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊtˌroʊp / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌrəʊp / (BrE)
a rope along the lower edge of a sail, or one for sailors to stand on while working high above the deck

Examples

Working on the footrope requires good balance and a steady hand.
The crew member gripped the footrope tightly while repairing the sail high above the deck.
Sailors must balance carefully on the footrope when furling the sails in strong winds.
He learned to walk along the footrope during his first week on the tall ship.
