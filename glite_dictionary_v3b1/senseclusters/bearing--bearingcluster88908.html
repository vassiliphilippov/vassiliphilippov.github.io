bearing

noun
direction or path, especially in navigation
Pronunciation: / ˈber.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the direction or path along which something moves or is located, especially measured relative to north; a direction or position

Examples

Find your bearing
If you lose your bearing in the forest, it's easy to get lost.
The captain checked the compass to determine the ship's bearing.
Turn left at the fork and keep a westward bearing for three miles.
