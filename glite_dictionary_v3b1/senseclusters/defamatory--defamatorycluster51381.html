(of statements or writing) damaging the reputation of a person or group; containing false or unjust accusations that discredit or malign.

Examples

Defamatory statements
His lawyer argued that the comments were defamatory and caused irreparable harm to her reputation.
Making defamatory statements without evidence can lead to legal consequences.
The magazine was sued for publishing a defamatory article about the actor.

Synonyms

  • calumnious
    9.9 0.02/m
    calumnious speech is false and aims to harm someone's reputation, while defamatory describes any damaging statement or writing.
  • calumniatory
    9.9 0.00/m
    calumniatory remarks aim to harm someone's name or standing, while defamatory is the wider word for reputation-damaging statements.
  • libelous
    8.4 0.45/m
    libelous articles are written and may lead to legal action, while defamatory can describe any damaging spoken or written statement.
  • slanderous
    7.3 0.53/m
    slanderous words are spoken and often brief, while defamatory covers both spoken and written remarks that damage reputation.
