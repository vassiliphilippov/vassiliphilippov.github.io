histrionics

noun
exaggerated emotional display
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪstriˈɑnɪks / (AmE), / ˌhɪstriˈɒnɪks / (BrE)
exaggerated dramatic behavior or displays of emotion intended to attract attention or achieve an effect

Examples

Dramatic histrionics display
No one was fooled by the manager's histrionics when the results were announced.
Please spare us your histrionics and just explain what happened calmly.
The actor's histrionics during the awards ceremony drew both applause and eye rolls from the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: restraint - emotional self-control or moderation
