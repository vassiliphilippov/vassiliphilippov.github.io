general anesthetic

noun
drug causing total unconsciousness
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛnərəl ænəsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˈdʒɛnərəl ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəl ænɪsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl anaesˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
a drug or agent that causes loss of sensation and consciousness throughout the entire body, used especially during surgical procedures

Examples

Related Terms

  • antonym: local anesthetic - an anesthetic causing loss of sensation in a specific area of the body
