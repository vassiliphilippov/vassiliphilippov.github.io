noncontingently

adverb
unconditionally
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈtɪndʒəntli / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈtɪndʒəntli / (BrE)
in a way that does not depend on any condition, circumstance, or event

Examples

Apply noncontingently always
The rule applies noncontingently, regardless of the specific situation.
Some philosophers argue that moral laws hold noncontingently for all rational beings.
The benefits are granted noncontingently, so everyone receives them equally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contingently - in a way that depends on certain conditions or circumstances
  • synonym: unconditionally - in a way that is not subject to any conditions
