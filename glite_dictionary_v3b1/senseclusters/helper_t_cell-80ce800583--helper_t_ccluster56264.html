helper T cell

noun
type of immune cell with a CD4 receptor
4.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…õlp…ôr ti s…õl / (AmE), / Ààh…õlp…ô tiÀê s…õl / (BrE)
A type of T cell with a CD4 receptor that recognizes antigens on infected cells and secretes lymphokines to stimulate other immune cells.

Examples

Helper T cell counts
A decrease in helper T cell numbers can lead to severe immunodeficiency.
HIV targets and destroys helper T cells, weakening the immune system.
Scientists studied how helper T cells activate B cells to produce antibodies.

Synonyms

    4.6 0.05/m
    CD4 T cell names the T lymphocyte by its CD4 marker in biology, while helper T cell is the common clinical name.
  • helper cell
    4.9 0.09/m
    helper cell is a shorter name for CD4-expressing T lymphocytes in explanations, while helper T cell is the full, precise name.
  • CD4 cell
    5.1 0.06/m
    CD4 cell is a common clinical label often used when talking about HIV counts, while helper T cell is the immunology term.
