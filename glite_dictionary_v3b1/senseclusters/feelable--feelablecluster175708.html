feelable

adjective
able to be sensed
4.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfiÀêl…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàfiÀêl…ôb…ôl / (BrE)
able to be felt or perceived by touch or sensation

Examples

Feelable surface
There was a feelable difference in the texture of the fabric.
The excitement in the room was almost feelable as the results were announced.
After the rain, the drop in temperature was immediately feelable on my skin.

Synonyms

  • touchable
    2.5 0.22/m
    touchable is something you can physically touch with your hand, while feelable is a more general word for being sensed by touch.
  • tactile
    5.1 3.09/m
    tactile describes things that give a clear sense of touch or texture, while feelable simply means able to be felt by touch.
  • tangible
    3.9 0.10/m
    tangible refers to something concrete you can touch or see, while feelable focuses only on being able to be sensed by touch.
  • palpable
    6.8 0.31/m
