allurement

noun
attractive quality; charm
Pronunciation: / əˈlʊr.mənt /; / əˈlʊrmənt / (AmE), / əˈljʊə.mənt /; / əˈljʊəmənt / (BrE)
the quality or act of attracting or tempting someone; charm, appeal, or enticement

Examples

The allurement of success
She could not resist the allurement of a career in the big city.
The allurement of the mysterious island drew many adventurers to its shores.
The old city's allurement lies in its winding streets and hidden cafés.
Despite the allurement of quick success, he chose to work honestly.
She was wary of the allurement offered by strangers promising gifts.
The allurement of easy money can lead people down the wrong path.
Despite the allurement of easy money, she chose to follow the law.
His allurement was not just in his looks, but in the way he spoke and laughed.
The allurement of the city lights drew her closer every time she visited.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attraction - the action or power of evoking interest, pleasure, or liking for someone or something
  • synonym: enticement - the act or an instance of attracting or tempting
  • antonym: repulsion - the action or power of repelling or being repelled
  • synonym: temptation - the desire to do something, especially something wrong or unwise
