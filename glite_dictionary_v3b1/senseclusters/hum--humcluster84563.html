hum

verb
make a low steady sound
1.0 2.64/m
Pronunciation: / hÊŒm / (AmE), / hÊŒm / (BrE)
to make a low, continuous, steady sound (like a machine, bees, or background noise)

Examples

Machines hum quietly
As you entered the room, you could hear the computers humming in the background.
Everything in the house was silent except for the refrigerator, which continued to hum.
The old air conditioner would always hum softly throughout the night.
A distant machine hummed throughout the night, lulling him to sleep.
Bees hum busily in the garden during spring.
The refrigerator began to hum as soon as it was plugged in.

Synonyms

  • buzz
    1.0 3.95/m
    buzz is a vibrating sound like bees or an electric object, while hum is a steady, low background noise.
  • thrum
    7.2 0.23/m
    thrum is a steady, rhythmic vibration you feel and hear, while hum is a more even, quiet continuous sound.
  • drone
    5.8 0.12/m
    drone is a long, low, monotonous sound often boring or steady, while hum is a neutral steady background noise.
  • whirr
    6.7 1.11/m
    whirr is a fast, vibrating machine sound with moving parts, while hum is a slower, steady background buzz.
