axoplasm

noun
axon cytoplasm
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæk.sə.plæ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈæk.sə.plæ.zəm/ (BrE)
the cytoplasm found inside the axon of a nerve cell, responsible for transporting materials along the nerve fiber

Examples

The axoplasm inside axon
The axoplasm carries nutrients and signals along the nerve fiber.
Damage to the axoplasm can disrupt communication between nerve cells.
Researchers studied the movement of proteins within the axoplasm to understand nerve function.
