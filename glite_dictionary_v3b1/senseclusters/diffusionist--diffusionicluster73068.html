diffusionist

noun
believer in cultural diffusion
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfjuːʒənɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that cultural traits, ideas, or technologies spread from one society to another through contact.

Examples

A cultural diffusionist
The diffusionist explained how agricultural practices spread across continents through trade.
Many early 20th-century scholars were strong diffusionists in their approach to cultural change.
A diffusionist might argue that writing systems developed through contact between ancient societies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cultural anthropologist - a person who studies cultures and their development
  • antonym: evolutionist - a person who believes in the gradual development of societies
