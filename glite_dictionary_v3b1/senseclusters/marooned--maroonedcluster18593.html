marooned

adjective
stranded and left behind
8.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈruːnd/ (AmE), /mə.ˈruːnd/ (BrE)
stranded and left behind or isolated in an inaccessible place, especially as a result of being left there intentionally or by circumstance

Examples

To be marooned alone
After the storm damaged their boat, the sailors were marooned on a small uninhabited island for days.
She felt marooned in the rural village, far from her friends and family.
The hikers became marooned when the river flooded and the bridge collapsed.
