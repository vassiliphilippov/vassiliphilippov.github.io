Cartesianism

noun
Descartes' philosophy
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈtiːʒəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / kɑːˈtiːzɪənɪzəm /; / kɑːˈtiːzɪəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical system and ideas developed by René Descartes, especially the belief that mind and body are separate

Examples

Cartesianism philosophy
Many modern philosophers have debated the principles of Cartesianism in relation to the mind-body problem.
The influence of Cartesianism can still be seen in contemporary discussions about consciousness.
Some scientists argue that Cartesianism separates mental and physical processes too strictly.
Many modern philosophers have been influenced by Cartesianism.
The concept of mind-body dualism is central to Cartesianism.
Students studied Cartesianism to understand the foundations of modern Western philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dualism - the belief that mind and body are fundamentally different
  • antonym: empiricism - the belief that knowledge comes from sensory experience
  • antonym: materialism - the belief that only physical matter exists
  • synonym: rationalism - the belief that reason is the main source of knowledge
