requeen

verb
to add new queen bee
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkwiːn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkwiːn / (BrE)
to introduce a new queen bee into a beehive, usually to improve the colony's health or productivity

Examples

To requeen a hive
The beekeeper decided to requeen the hive after noticing a drop in honey production.
It is common practice to requeen colonies every few years to maintain strong genetics.
After the old queen died, they had to requeen the entire colony to keep it thriving.
