Cardura

noun
trademarked antihypertensive drug (doxazosin)
8.1
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈdʊrə / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjʊərə / (BrE)
A trademarked antihypertensive drug (doxazosin) that relaxes blood vessels to lower blood pressure, also used to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia.

Examples

Cardura medication
If you miss a dose of Cardura, take it as soon as possible unless it's almost time for your next dose.
My doctor prescribed Cardura to help manage my high blood pressure.
Some men take Cardura to relieve the symptoms of an enlarged prostate.
