Spanish moss

noun
plant with trailing grey-green strands
Pronunciation: / ˈspænɪʃ mɑs / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ mɒs / (BrE)
A plant with long, grey-green hairlike strands that hangs from trees, especially in the southeastern United States and tropical America.

Examples

Spanish moss on trees
Some birds use Spanish moss to build their nests in the wild.
The live oaks were draped in Spanish moss, creating an eerie and beautiful landscape.
Tourists often photograph the Spanish moss hanging from southern trees.
