perithecium

noun
flask-shaped fruiting body in fungi
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈθiːsiəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈθiːsɪəm / (BrE)
A flask-shaped fruiting body (ascocarp) found in certain fungi, containing the asci where spores are formed.

Examples

A perithecium fruiting body
Identification of the perithecium is crucial for classifying certain species of fungi.
The fungus develops a perithecium to protect and disperse its spores.
Under the microscope, numerous perithecia can be observed on the decaying plant material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascocarp - fruiting body of ascomycete fungi
  • synonym: cleistothecium - a closed spherical ascocarp in fungi
