superacid

noun
extremely strong acid
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌæsɪd / (BrE)
an acid that is much stronger than pure sulfuric acid and can protonate even weak bases

Examples

A superacid solution
A superacid can dissolve substances that regular acids cannot.
Chemists use superacids to study reactions that are impossible with ordinary acids.
