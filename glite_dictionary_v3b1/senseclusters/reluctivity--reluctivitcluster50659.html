(physics) The resistance of a material to the establishment of a magnetic field within it, measured as the inverse of magnetic permeability.

Examples

Material reluctivity
In designing an electromagnet, the reluctivity of each component must be taken into account.
Soft iron has a low reluctivity, making it ideal for transformer cores.
The reluctivity of non-magnetic materials is relatively high compared to ferromagnetic ones.
