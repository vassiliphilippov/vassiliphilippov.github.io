disegno

noun
Renaissance art design
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˈseɪnjoʊ / (AmE), / diˈseɪnjəʊ / (BrE)
the concept in Renaissance art emphasizing the importance of design and drawing in the creation of a work

Examples

Renaissance disegno concept
The painter's understanding of disegno set his work apart from his contemporaries.
In Renaissance Italy, disegno was considered the foundation of all the visual arts.
Art historians often discuss the balance between disegno and colorito in classical paintings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colorito - the use of color in painting, especially in contrast to design
  • synonym: composition - the arrangement of elements in a work of art
