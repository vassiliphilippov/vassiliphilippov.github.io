express

adjective
sent or moving quickly, with few/no stops
2.6
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsprɛs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛs / (BrE)
(of transportation or delivery) operating or sent with few or no stops, and usually faster than usual.

Examples

Express delivery
He booked an express train to reach the city more quickly.
Taking the express bus saved us nearly an hour compared to the regular route.
The package was shipped via express delivery to ensure it arrived on time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: local - (of trains or services) stopping at all or most stations or stages
