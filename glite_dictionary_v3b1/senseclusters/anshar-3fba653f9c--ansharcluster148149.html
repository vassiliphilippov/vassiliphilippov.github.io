Anshar

proper noun
Babylonian father of the gods
Pronunciation: / ˈænʃɑr / (AmE), / ˈænʃɑː / (BrE)
In Babylonian mythology, the father of the gods, identified with the Assyrian god Ashur, and representing the totality of the upper world.

Examples

Anshar the god
Ancient texts describe Anshar as the supreme father of the Babylonian gods.
In some myths, Anshar is equated with the Assyrian deity Ashur.
The name Anshar in Sumerian signifies the entirety of the upper heavens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ashur - chief god of the Assyrian pantheon
