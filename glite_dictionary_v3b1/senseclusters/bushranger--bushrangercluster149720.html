bushranger

noun
Australian outlaw
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃˌreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person in Australia, especially in the 19th century, who lived by robbing travelers and hiding in the bush

Examples

The bushranger Ned Kelly
The legend of the bushranger Ned Kelly is well known in Australia.
Many stories about bushrangers have become part of Australian folklore.
The bushranger hid in the forest to avoid capture by the police.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandit - a robber, especially one who is a member of a gang
  • synonym: outlaw - a person who has broken the law and is hiding from authorities
  • antonym: policeman - a person whose job is to enforce the law
