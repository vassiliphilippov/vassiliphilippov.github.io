thorpe

noun
small village (archaic)
Pronunciation: / θɔrp / (AmE), / θɔːp / (BrE)
an old term for a small village or hamlet, especially found in English place names

Examples

A small thorpe
The old map showed a thorpe near the river.
Many English place names end with thorpe, indicating their origins as small villages.
In medieval times, a thorpe was often just a cluster of houses and farms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hamlet - a small village
  • degree higher: town - a larger settlement than a village
