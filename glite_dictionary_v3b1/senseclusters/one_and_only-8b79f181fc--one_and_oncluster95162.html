one and only

adjective
unique; without equal
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌn ənd ˈoʊnli /; / wʌn ənd ˈoʊn.li / (AmE), / ˌwʌn ənd ˈəʊnli /; / wʌn ənd ˈəʊn.li / (BrE)
used to emphasize that someone or something is unique or special; the only one

Examples

The one and only champion
Everyone cheered when the one and only champion entered the arena.
For his fans, there's no singer like the one and only Elvis Presley.
She is the one and only person who truly understands me.
She is truly my one and only, and I cannot imagine life without her.
The trophy is the one and only reminder of his championship days.
This was his one and only attempt to climb the mountain.
