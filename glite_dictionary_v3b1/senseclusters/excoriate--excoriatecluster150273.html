excoriate

verb
criticize severely; denounce
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɔːrɪeɪt / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something very harshly and publicly

Examples

To excoriated a politician
Many critics excoriated the film for its lack of originality.
She excoriated the company for its unethical business practices.
The editorial excoriated the government's response to the crisis.
The editor excoriated the politician for failing to keep his promises.
Many critics excoriated the film for its lack of originality and poor acting.
She was excoriated in the press after the scandal became public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condemn - to express complete disapproval of, typically in public
  • synonym: denounce - to publicly declare to be wrong or evil
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
