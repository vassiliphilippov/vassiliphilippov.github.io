riverway

noun
natural river channel
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪvəˌweɪ / (BrE)
the natural channel or course through which a river or stream flows, especially not designed for navigation

Examples

Natural riverway course
The old riverway was lined with willow trees and teemed with wildlife in the spring.
After the flood, the riverway shifted, leaving the village farther from the water.
Scientists studied how the riverway changed over decades due to erosion and sediment buildup.

Related Terms

  • antonym: canal - an artificial waterway constructed for navigation or irrigation
  • synonym: watercourse - a channel through which water flows, especially a river or stream
