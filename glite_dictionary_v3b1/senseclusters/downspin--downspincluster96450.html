downspin

noun
swift and dangerous downturn
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.spɪn / (BrE)
a rapid and often dangerous decline in condition, value, or fortune

Examples

A sharp downspin
After months of steady progress, the company suffered a sharp downspin in sales.
His career went into a downspin following the scandal.
The sudden collapse of the housing market caused a financial downspin for many families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downturn - a decline in economic, business, or other activity
  • antonym: upswing - an increase or improvement in condition, value, or fortune
