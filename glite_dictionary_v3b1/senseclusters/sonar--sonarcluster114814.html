sonar

noun
underwater sound detection system
1.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.nɑːr/ /; / /ˈsoʊ.nər/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.nɑː/ /; / /ˈsəʊ.nə/ (BrE)
A system or device that uses sound waves to detect and locate objects underwater by sending out acoustic pulses and measuring their echoes.

Examples

Underwater sonar system
Marine biologists often rely on sonar to track schools of fish.
Modern ships are typically equipped with advanced sonar systems for detecting obstacles underwater.
The submarine used its sonar to navigate through the dark waters.
