low density

noun
thinness; small amount of matter per volume
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˈdɛnsəti / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈdɛnsəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of having comparatively little mass or matter in a given volume; thinness or sparseness.

Examples

Low density material
Foam is lighter because of its low density compared to solid plastic.
Scientists monitor areas of low density in space to study cosmic phenomena.
The balloon rose quickly through the region of low density in the atmosphere.
