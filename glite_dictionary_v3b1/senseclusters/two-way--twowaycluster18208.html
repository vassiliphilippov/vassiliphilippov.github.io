two-way

adjective
involving two directions or parties
Pronunciation: / ˈtuˌweɪ /; / tuˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtuːweɪ /; / ˈtuː weɪ /; / ˈtuː.weɪ / (BrE)
involving or allowing movement, communication, or activity in both directions or between two parties; mutual or reciprocal

Examples

A two-way street
Effective two-way communication is essential in any relationship.
The street became a two-way road after the construction was completed.
They signed a two-way agreement to share their research findings.
The agreement was a two-way contract that required cooperation from both parties.
Their relationship was strictly two-way, with each person listening and responding equally.
This is a two-way street, meaning both sides must make concessions for it to work.
The city council decided to convert the street from two-way to one-way traffic to ease congestion.
We need a two-way radio to communicate with the rescue team.
You must be careful when driving on two-way roads in unfamiliar countries.
