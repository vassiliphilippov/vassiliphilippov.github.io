obsolete

verb
to make outdated
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːbsəˈliːt / (AmE), / ˈɒbsəliːt / (BrE)
to make something no longer useful or needed because it has been replaced by something newer or better

Examples

To obsolete old models
Digital cameras have obsoleted many types of film cameras in recent years.
The introduction of smartphones quickly obsoleted many traditional mobile phones.
New software updates can sometimes obsolete older hardware, making it unusable.
Digital cameras quickly obsoleted film cameras in the photography industry.
The introduction of smartphones has obsoleted many traditional electronic devices.
New regulations may obsolete certain manufacturing processes within a few years.
Smartphones have obsoleted many traditional devices like alarm clocks and cameras.
The introduction of new software quickly obsoleted the old system in the office.
Streaming services are obsoleting the need for physical DVDs and CDs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernize - to make something more modern
  • synonym: outdate - to make something old-fashioned or no longer useful
  • synonym: replace - to take the place of something
  • synonym: supersede - to take the place of something older or less modern
  • antonym: update - to make something more modern or up to date
