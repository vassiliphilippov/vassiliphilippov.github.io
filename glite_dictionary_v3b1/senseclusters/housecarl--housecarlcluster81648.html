housecarl

noun
medieval household servant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌkɑrl / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌkɑːl / (BrE)
a household retainer or domestic servant in medieval times, responsible for duties in the lord's household rather than military service

Examples

Medieval housecarl servant
The housecarl was busy preparing the great hall for the evening feast.
In the medieval manor, a trusted housecarl managed the daily affairs of the household.
The lord rewarded his loyal housecarl with new clothes and a place by the fire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retainer - a person who serves a household or noble family
  • synonym: servant - a person employed to perform domestic duties
