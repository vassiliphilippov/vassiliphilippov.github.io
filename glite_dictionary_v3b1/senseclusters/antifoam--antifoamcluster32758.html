antifoam

noun
foam-preventing agent
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæntiˌfoʊm / (AmE), / ˈæntiˌfəʊm / (BrE)
a substance added to a liquid to prevent or reduce the formation of foam

Examples

Antifoam agent
The engineer recommended adding antifoam to the mixture to improve processing efficiency.
Without antifoam, the detergent created too many bubbles during production.
A small amount of antifoam was enough to keep the solution clear and stable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defoamer - a chemical that eliminates or reduces foam in liquids
