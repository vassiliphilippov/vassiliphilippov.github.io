labial consonant

noun
consonant articulated with the lips
6.7 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.bi.əl ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.bi.əl ˈkɒn.sə.nənt/ (BrE)
a consonant sound in which the lips are used to produce the articulation, such as 'p', 'b', and 'm'

Examples

Labial consonants like p
Children often learn to pronounce labial consonants early because they are easy to articulate.
In English, the sounds represented by labial consonants include 'p', 'b', and 'm'.
