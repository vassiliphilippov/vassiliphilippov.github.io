counterconditioning

noun
therapy technique
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərkənˌdɪʃəˈnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəkənˌdɪʃəˈnɪŋ / (BrE)
a therapy technique that replaces an unwanted response to a stimulus with a desired response

Examples

Counterconditioning therapy
The psychologist used counterconditioning to help the child overcome his fear of loud noises.
Through counterconditioning, the patient learned to associate dogs with positive feelings instead of anxiety.
Many therapists use counterconditioning as part of treatment for phobias and anxiety disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conditioning - a learning process in which an association is made between a stimulus and a response
  • synonym: desensitization - a process that reduces emotional response to a stimulus
