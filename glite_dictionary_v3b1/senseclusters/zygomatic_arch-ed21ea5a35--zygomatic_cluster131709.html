zygomatic arch

noun
arch of bone joining cheekbone and temporal bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / zaɪɡəˈmætɪk ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / zaɪɡəˈmætɪk ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the bony arch on each side of the skull formed by the joining of the cheekbone and the temporal bone

Examples

The zygomatic arch bone
A fracture of the zygomatic arch can result from a direct blow to the face.
The pronounced zygomatic arch in primates helps support strong jaw muscles.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the zygomatic arch during the procedure.
