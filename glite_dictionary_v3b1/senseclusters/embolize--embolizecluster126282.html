embolize

verb
to block a vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛmbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become blocked by an embolus, or to cause a blood vessel to be blocked by an embolus

Examples

Blood vessel embolizes
If a blood clot travels to the lungs, it can embolize and cause a serious blockage.
Doctors may intentionally embolize a blood vessel to stop bleeding during surgery.
The artery embolized suddenly, leading to numbness in the patient's leg.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occlude - to block or close up a passage or opening
  • antonym: unblock - to remove an obstruction from something
