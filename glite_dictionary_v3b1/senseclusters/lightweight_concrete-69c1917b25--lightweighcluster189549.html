lightweight concrete

noun
a lighter form of concrete made with special aggregates
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtˌweɪt kənˈkrit / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌweɪt kənˈkriːt / (BrE)
concrete made with materials or techniques resulting in a lighter weight than conventional concrete, often using materials such as expanded clay, cork granules, or vermiculite instead of or in addition to standard aggregates

Examples

Lightweight concrete flooring
Engineers often choose lightweight concrete for flooring to reduce the load on building structures.
Thanks to its insulating properties, lightweight concrete is popular in modern eco-friendly construction.
The new stadium was built using lightweight concrete to improve energy efficiency and foundation stability.
