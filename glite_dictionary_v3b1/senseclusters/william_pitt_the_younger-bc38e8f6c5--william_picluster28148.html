William Pitt the Younger

proper noun
English Prime Minister (1759–1806)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəm pɪt ðə ˈjʌŋər / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm pɪt ðə ˈjʌŋə / (BrE)
(1759–1806) English statesman who served as Prime Minister of Great Britain, known for his leadership during the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars, and as the son of William Pitt the Elder.

Examples

William Pitt the Younger Prime Minister
William Pitt the Younger became Prime Minister at the age of just 24.
Historians often compare William Pitt the Younger and his father for their contributions to British politics.
The policies of William Pitt the Younger shaped British responses to the French Revolution.
