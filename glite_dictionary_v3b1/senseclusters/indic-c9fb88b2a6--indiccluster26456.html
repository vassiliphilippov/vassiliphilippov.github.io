Indic

noun
branch of Indo-Iranian languages
7.2 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.dɪk/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.dɪk/ (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-Iranian family of languages, comprising the languages native to the Indian subcontinent, such as Hindi, Bengali, Punjabi, and others.

Examples

Indic languages group
Linguists have traced the origins of many Indian languages back to the Indic branch.
Modern Indic languages, such as Hindi and Bengali, are spoken by millions today.
Sanskrit is considered an ancient Indic language with a rich literary tradition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Dravidian - a distinct language family of southern India, not part of Indic
  • degree higher: Indo-Iranian - a major branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Indic and Iranian
