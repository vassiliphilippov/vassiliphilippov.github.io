keel

noun
longitudinal beam of a ship's hull
9.0 5.9
Pronunciation: / kil / (AmE), / kiːl / (BrE)
a main longitudinal beam or plate running along the bottom of a ship's hull, which can extend downward to provide stability and structural strength

Examples

A ship's keel
A deep keel helps prevent a sailing yacht from capsizing in strong winds.
After the collision, divers inspected the vessel's keel for damage.
The ship's keel was laid in the dry dock before construction began.
