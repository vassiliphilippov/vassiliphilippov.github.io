sponge

verb
clean or absorb with a sponge
Pronunciation: / spʌndʒ / (AmE), / spʌndʒ / (BrE)
to clean, wipe, or soak up a liquid using a sponge or absorbent material

Examples

To sponge the table
After the picnic, we sponged off the table to remove the sticky spots.
He sponged the spill carefully until the floor was dry.
She sponges the bathroom tiles once a week to prevent mildew.
After the exam, she sponged the chalk marks off the blackboard.
He quickly sponged the table to get rid of the spilled juice.
The caretaker sponged away the graffiti from the school wall.
After the spill, he quickly sponged up the water from the floor.
She carefully sponged the stain on the tablecloth before putting it in the wash.
The nurse gently sponged his forehead to keep him cool.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard, often with a brush
  • synonym: wipe - to clean or dry something by rubbing
