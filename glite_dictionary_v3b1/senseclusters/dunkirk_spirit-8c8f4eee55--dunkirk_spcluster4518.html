Dunkirk spirit

noun
determination and resilience in adversity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌn.kɜrk ˈspɪɹ.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʌn.kɜːk ˈspɪɹ.ɪt / (BrE)
A strong sense of determination and resilience shown by a group of people facing adversity, especially through collective effort.

Examples

The Dunkirk spirit shown
Despite the severe flooding, the village pulled together with true Dunkirk spirit to rebuild their homes.
Faced with cutbacks, the team maintained their Dunkirk spirit and delivered the project on time.
In times of crisis, communities often rediscover their Dunkirk spirit and support one another.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defeatism - acceptance of defeat without struggle; lack of hope
  • synonym: grit - courage and resolve; strength of character
  • synonym: resilience - the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties
