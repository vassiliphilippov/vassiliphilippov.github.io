drawbench

noun
metalworking machine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɔˌbɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːˌbɛntʃ / (BrE)
a machine used to pull metal through a die to reduce its diameter or change its shape

Examples

A drawbench machine
The technician used a drawbench to reduce the thickness of the metal rod.
A drawbench is essential in the process of making fine wire for electronics.
After setting up the drawbench, the workers began pulling the steel through the die.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lathe - a machine for shaping materials by rotating them against a tool
  • synonym: wire-drawing machine - a machine for drawing metal wire through dies
