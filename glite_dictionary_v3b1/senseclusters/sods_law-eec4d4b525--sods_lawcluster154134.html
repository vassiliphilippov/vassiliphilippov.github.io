Sod's Law

noun

Pronunciation: /sɑːdz lɔː/ /; / /sɑːdz lɑː/ (AmE), /sɒdz lɔː/ (BrE)
(British, humorous) The supposed law stating that if something can go wrong, it will go wrong.

Examples

According to Sod's Law, it started raining just after I washed my car.
Whenever I forget my umbrella, Sod's Law guarantees it will pour with rain.
You know Sod's Law; if you're running late, every traffic light will be red.
