complementary

noun
color that forms a neutral pair with another
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpləˈmɛnˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛntəri / (BrE)
either of two colors that, when combined in the right proportions, produce white (for light) or gray (for pigments)

Examples

Complementary colors
Designers often pair complementaries to create high contrast in visual works.
Mixing two complementaries can result in a lifeless gray tone in painting.
On the color wheel, green and red are complementaries often used together in art.
