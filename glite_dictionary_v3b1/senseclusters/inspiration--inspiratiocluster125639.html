the act of drawing air or other gases into the lungs; inhalation, especially as part of the process of breathing

Examples

Inspiration of air
During strenuous exercise, the rate of inspiration increases to supply more oxygen to the body.
His deep inspiration was followed by a slow, controlled expiration.
The doctor measured the patient's rate of inspiration and expiration during the examination.

Synonyms

  • inhalation
    4.1 1.34/m
    inhalation is the clinical term for drawing air into the lungs, while inspiration is the everyday or general word.
  • breathing in
    1.0 0.75/m
    breathing in describes the simple action of taking air into the lungs, while inspiration is the formal noun for that action.
  • aspiration
    5.9 0.50/m
    aspiration can mean a single breath or a strong wish, while inspiration here means the physical act of inhaling air.
  • intake
    3.5 0.83/m
    intake usually refers to the amount or opening for air entering the body, while inspiration names the act of breathing in.
