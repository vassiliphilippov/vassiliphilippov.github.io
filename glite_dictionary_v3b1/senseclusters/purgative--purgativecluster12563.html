purgative

adjective
strongly laxative (adj.)
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡətɪv / (BrE)
(of a medicine or substance) having a strong laxative effect; tending to cause evacuation of the bowels.

Examples

Purgative effect
Because of its purgative properties, the compound is used in certain medical treatments.
The doctor prescribed a purgative medicine to quickly relieve the patient’s severe constipation.
This herbal tea is considered purgative and should be taken only under supervision.
