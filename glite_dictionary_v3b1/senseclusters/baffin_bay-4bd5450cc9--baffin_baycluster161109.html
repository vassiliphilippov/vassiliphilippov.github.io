Baffin Bay

proper noun
bay between Greenland and Canada
Pronunciation: / ˈbæf.ɪn beɪ / (AmE), / ˈbæf.ɪn beɪ / (BrE)
a large body of water between Greenland and northeastern Canada, connected to the Arctic Ocean to the north and the Atlantic Ocean via the Labrador Sea to the south, typically icebound in winter

Examples

Baffin Bay icebound winter
Each winter, Baffin Bay becomes largely icebound, limiting sea traffic through the region.
Explorers struggled to navigate the frozen waters of Baffin Bay during their Arctic expeditions.
The coastal communities bordering Baffin Bay depend on fishing as a main source of income.
