interrogatory

noun
legal written questions for a party
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tər.i / (BrE)
a formal set of written questions submitted to a party in a legal case to be answered under oath

Examples

Legal interrogatory
Each party was required to respond to the interrogatories within thirty days as part of the discovery process.
Failure to answer an interrogatory can result in penalties or sanctions from the court.
The defendant's attorney prepared a detailed interrogatory to gather more information about the case.
