ostracize

verb
to exclude from a group or society
9.9 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.strə.saɪz/ /; / /ˈɑː.strə.saɪz/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.strə.saɪz/ (BrE)
to deliberately exclude someone from a group or refuse to associate with them

Examples

After the scandal, many former friends chose to ostracize him from their social circle.
She felt ostracized at school because of her unusual interests.
The community decided to ostracize anyone who broke the club's code of conduct.
After criticizing the team's decision, he felt the group began to ostracize him.

She worries her peers will ostracize her for being different.

She felt hurt when her classmates began to ostracise her for her opinions.
Some cultures historically ostracised those who broke social norms.
