sigmoid sinus

noun
S-shaped venous channel in the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡ.mɔɪd ˈsaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.mɔɪd ˈsaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a large, S-shaped venous channel in the dura mater of the brain, located near the temporal and occipital bones, that drains blood from the brain into the internal jugular vein

Examples

The sigmoid sinus vein
A blood clot in the sigmoid sinus can result in serious neurological symptoms.
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the sigmoid sinus.
The sigmoid sinus is responsible for draining blood from the brain into the jugular vein.
