seafoam

noun
froth on seawater
Pronunciation: / ˈsiˌfoʊm / (AmE), / ˈsiːfəʊm / (BrE)
a light, frothy substance formed on the surface of the sea by the agitation of seawater, especially by breaking waves

Examples

White seafoam on waves
After the storm, thick seafoam covered the rocks along the coastline.
Children played at the edge of the water, fascinated by the swirling seafoam left by the waves.
The morning sun made the seafoam sparkle as it drifted across the sand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid
  • synonym: scum - a layer of dirt or froth on the surface of a liquid
