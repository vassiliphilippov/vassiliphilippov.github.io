encrust

verb
form a hard outer layer or crust
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkrʌst / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrʌst / (BrE)
to form or cover with a hard outer layer or crust

Examples

To encrust a ship
Barnacles encrust the hull of the old ship after years at sea.
Minerals slowly encrust the surface of the underwater rocks.
Over time, grime and salt began to encrust the statue's base.
After years at sea, the anchor became encrusted with barnacles.
Salt crystals can encrust the surface of rocks near the shoreline.
The ancient coins were encrusted with layers of dirt from centuries underground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bare - not covered or coated with anything
  • synonym: coat - to cover something with a layer of a substance
  • synonym: coating - a layer of a substance spread over a surface
