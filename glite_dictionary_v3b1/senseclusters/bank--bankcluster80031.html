bank

verb
to enclose with a bank (barrier)
Pronunciation: / bæŋk / (AmE), / bæŋk /; / baŋk / (BrE)
to build up, cover, or surround with a bank of earth or other material; or to cover a fire to control its burning

Examples

To bank a fire
During construction, workers bank the edge of the new road with earth to improve drainage.
Historically, farmers would bank their fields to protect crops from the wind.
The engineers banked the river to prevent flooding in the nearby village.
Before going to bed, she carefully banked the fire to keep it burning overnight.
In old houses, people often bank the coals in the fireplace to maintain warmth through the night.
The campers banked their fire with ash to ensure it would smolder until morning.
