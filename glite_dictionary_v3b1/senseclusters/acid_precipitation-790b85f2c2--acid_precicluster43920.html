acid precipitation

noun
precipitation made acidic by atmospheric pollution
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪd ˌprɛsəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˌprɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
Rain, snow, or other forms of precipitation that are unusually acidic, due to atmospheric pollution caused especially by industrial emissions.

Examples

Acid precipitation damage
Legislation has helped reduce the industrial emissions that contribute to acid precipitation.
Scientists have found that acid precipitation is damaging forests in the northeastern United States.
The increase in acid precipitation has led to the acidification of many lakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acid rain - precipitation with a high concentration of acids, mainly from pollution
