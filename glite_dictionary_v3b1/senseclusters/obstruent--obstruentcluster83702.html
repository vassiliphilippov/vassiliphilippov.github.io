obstruent

noun
consonant with obstructed airflow
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëbstruÀê…ônt / (AmE), / Àà…íbstruÀê…ônt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by obstructing the airflow in the vocal tract, either partially or completely, such as stops, fricatives, and affricates

Examples

Obstruent consonant sound
Fricatives such as 'f' and 's' are also classified as types of obstruents.
Many languages distinguish between obstruent and sonorant sounds in their phonological systems.
Plosives like 'p' and 'b' are classic examples of obstruent consonants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sonorant - consonant produced with uninterrupted airflow, like nasals and liquids
