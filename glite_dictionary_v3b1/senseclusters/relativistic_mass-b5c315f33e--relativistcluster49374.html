relativistic mass

noun
mass of a moving body relative to an observer
8.1 8.8
Pronunciation: /rel.ə.tɪ.ˈvɪs.tɪk mæs/ (AmE), /rel.ə.tɪ.ˈvɪs.tɪk mæs/ /; / /rel.ə.tɪ.ˈvɪs.tɪk mɑːs/ (BrE)
(Physics) The mass of a body in motion relative to an observer, equal to its rest mass multiplied by a factor greater than one that increases with velocity.

Examples

Relativistic mass increase
According to Einstein's theory, an object's relativistic mass increases as it approaches the speed of light.
In particle accelerators, the relativistic mass of subatomic particles rises significantly with their speed.
Physicists often distinguish between rest mass and relativistic mass when discussing bodies moving at high velocities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rest mass - the mass of an object when at rest
