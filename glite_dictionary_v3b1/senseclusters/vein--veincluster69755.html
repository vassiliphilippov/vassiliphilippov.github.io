vein

noun
thin layer of mineral ore in rock
6.3 1.11/m
Pronunciation: / veÉªn / (AmE), / veÉªn / (BrE)
a thin layer or deposit of mineral ore, typically running through rock or earth

Examples

A gold vein
After months of searching, geologists finally traced the vein to its source.
The gold was found in a narrow vein between layers of granite.
The miners discovered a rich vein of silver deep within the mountain.

Synonyms

  • mineral vein
    6.1 0.01/m
    mineral vein is a clearly marked ore layer between rock layers, while vein is the general word for such thin mineral deposits.
  • vein in the rock
    7.4 0.01/m
    vein in the rock forms when minerals fill a crack, while vein is a short word for that thin ore layer.
  • lode
    9.1 0.81/m
    lode is a large, rich mineral deposit often mined for profit, while vein usually means a thinner natural ore layer.
