virulence

noun
degree of a microorganism's harmfulness
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪrjələns / (AmE), / ˈvɪərʊləns / (BrE)
the degree of harmfulness or severity of a disease or the ability of a microorganism to cause disease

Examples

High virulence in bacteria
Researchers are studying the virulence factors that make certain bacteria more dangerous.
The virulence of the new strain of the virus has alarmed scientists across the globe.
The decline in virulence over time made the disease less deadly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pathogenicity - the ability of an organism to cause disease
