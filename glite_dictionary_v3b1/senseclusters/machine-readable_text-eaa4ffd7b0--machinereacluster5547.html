machine-readable text

noun
electronic text interpretable by computers
Pronunciation: / məˈʃin ˌriːdəbl̩ tɛkst / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn ˌriːdəbl̩ tɛkst / (BrE)
electronic text stored in a format that can be processed by a computer without human intervention

Examples

Machine-readable text format
Researchers need machine-readable text to efficiently process large volumes of data.
The scanned document was converted into machine-readable text for further analysis.
To comply with accessibility standards, the website must provide content as machine-readable text.
