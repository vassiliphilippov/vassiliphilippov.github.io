Probably; with considerable certainty or without much doubt.

Examples

It will likely happen
Given the dark clouds, it will likely rain this afternoon.
He will likely accept the offer, considering his current situation.
She will likely arrive before noon, so please be ready.

Synonyms

  • probably
    1.0 436/m
    probably is a neutral adverb used to show general chance, while likely is similar but also often used as an adjective.
  • in all probability
    3.8 0.69/m
    in all probability sounds slightly formal and stresses high chance, while likely is more neutral everyday language.
  • in all likelihood
    3.2 1.81/m
    in all likelihood feels formal and emphasizes a strong chance, while likely remains a common, shorter choice.
  • presumably
    4.3 23/m
    presumably expresses a reasoned guess based on facts, while likely states general probability without showing how you know.
  • chances are
    3.2 5.39/m
    chances are is a casual spoken phrase people use in conversation, while likely is more neutral and fits both speech and writing.
  • in the cards
    6.1 0.59/m
    in the cards is an informal idiom that suggests fate or planning, while likely simply states ordinary probability without destiny tone.
