fat

noun
tissue storing energy in bodies
Pronunciation: / fæt / (AmE), / fæt / (BrE)
a soft oily or greasy substance in animals or plants that stores energy and insulates the body

Examples

Animal fat
Seals rely on thick layers of fat to survive Arctic temperatures.
The doctor measured the patient's body fat percentage during the exam.
Too much fat around the organs can increase health risks.
Avocados are high in healthy fat and fiber.
Butter is rich in saturated fat.
Cutting too much fat from your diet may lead to health issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adipose tissue - technical term for body fat tissue
  • synonym: lipid - any of a group of naturally occurring molecules including fats, oils, and waxes
  • degree lower: oil - a viscous liquid derived from plants or animals, especially used as food
