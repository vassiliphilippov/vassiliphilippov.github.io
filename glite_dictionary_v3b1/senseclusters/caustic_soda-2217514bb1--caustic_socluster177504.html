caustic soda

noun
strong alkaline chemical; sodium hydroxide
3.4 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔstɪk ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːstɪk ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
A strongly alkaline, corrosive white substance (sodium hydroxide) used in manufacturing soap, paper, and various chemicals.

Examples

Handle caustic soda carefully
Always handle caustic soda with gloves, as it can burn your skin on contact.
Many industrial cleaning agents contain caustic soda as an active ingredient.
The soap-making process requires precise amounts of caustic soda for saponification.
