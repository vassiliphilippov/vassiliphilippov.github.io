self-proclaimed

adjective
declared by oneself, not by others
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf.prəˈkleɪmd /; / ˌsɛlf prəˈkleɪmd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.prəˈkleɪmd /; / ˌsɛlf prəˈkleɪmd / (BrE)
describing someone who claims a title, status, or identity for themselves, often without recognition or acceptance from others

Examples

A self-proclaimed expert
He called himself a self-proclaimed expert in the field, but few took him seriously.
Many self-proclaimed food critics share their opinions online.
The self-proclaimed leader announced his plans to reform the organization.
He is a self-proclaimed expert on Italian cuisine, though he has never visited Italy.
Many self-proclaimed gurus advertise their seminars online.
The city hosted a festival organized by a self-proclaimed art critic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-styled - describing oneself in a particular way, often without justification
