harborless

adjective
without shelter
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrbərləs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbələs / (BrE)
lacking a harbor or safe place; exposed and offering no refuge (especially for ships)

Examples

A harborless coast
The harborless coastline left ships vulnerable to the fierce winter storms.
After the fire, the family found themselves harborless and searching for a place to stay.
In a harborless world, people must create their own sense of safety and belonging.
The harborless coastline forced the sailors to search for a safe anchorage elsewhere.
Because the island was harborless, ships rarely visited its shores.
Early explorers avoided the harborless region due to the lack of shelter for their vessels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exposed - not protected or covered; open to danger
  • antonym: protected - kept safe from harm or danger
  • antonym: sheltered - protected from wind or danger, especially for ships
  • synonym: shelterless - without shelter or protection
