poll

noun
survey of public opinion
1.6 80/m
Pronunciation: / poÊŠl / (AmE), / pÉ™ÊŠl / (BrE)
an activity in which a number of people are asked questions in order to find out what they think about a subject, often conducted by interviewing a random sample of people

Examples

A poll result
A recent poll showed that most voters support the new policy.
The newspaper published the results of a poll on attitudes toward climate change.
They conducted a poll to find out which candidate was most popular.

Synonyms

  • opinion poll
    2.5 2.66/m
    opinion poll asks a sample of people about their views, while poll is the general activity of asking people their opinions.
  • public opinion poll
    3.1 1.15/m
    public opinion poll surveys a representative group to show public views, while poll refers to asking people about their opinions generally.
  • survey
    3.2 11/m
    survey collects data by asking people questions for analysis, while poll usually means asking a sample about their views on a topic.
  • canvass
    7.2 0.38/m
    canvass involves going out to ask voters or residents directly, while poll is a more general or survey-like questioning of a sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: survey - a general method for collecting information from people by asking questions
