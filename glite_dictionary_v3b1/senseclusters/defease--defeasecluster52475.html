defease

verb
to void a contract
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfiːz / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːz / (BrE)
to render a contract, obligation, or debt null and void, especially in legal or financial contexts

Examples

To defease a contract

A trust was established to defease the mortgage before the property was sold.
Lawyers worked together to defease the old agreement and draft a new contract.
