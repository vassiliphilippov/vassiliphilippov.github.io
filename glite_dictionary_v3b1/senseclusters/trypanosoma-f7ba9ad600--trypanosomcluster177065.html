Trypanosoma

noun
parasitic protozoa genus
Pronunciation: / traɪˌpænəˈsoʊmə / (AmE), / trɪˌpænəˈsəʊmə / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic protozoa that infects humans and animals, causing diseases such as sleeping sickness and Chagas disease

Examples

Trypanosoma parasite infection
Trypanosoma is transmitted to humans by the bite of infected tsetse flies.
Scientists study Trypanosoma to develop treatments for sleeping sickness.
Chagas disease is caused by a species of Trypanosoma found in Latin America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Plasmodium - a genus of protozoa that causes malaria
  • synonym: protozoan - a single-celled microscopic organism
