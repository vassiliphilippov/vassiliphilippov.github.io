cancel

noun
musical sign removing a previous accidental
9.7 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səl / (BrE)
A notation in musical scores indicating that a previous sharp or flat is to be disregarded.

Examples

Music cancel sign
A cancel is used in music to nullify a previous sharp or flat in a measure.
The composer marked a cancel before the note to restore it to its natural pitch.
When writing accidentals, a cancel can clarify the intended pitch for performers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: natural - the sign (♮) indicating a note is neither sharp nor flat
