sergeant

noun
senior English barrister (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
(historical) An English barrister of the highest rank, entitled to plead in the Court of Common Pleas.

Examples

English sergeant barrister
He was appointed sergeant before eventually rising to the position of judge.
In the 18th century, a sergeant wore a distinctive coif as a symbol of his status in court.
The title of sergeant was considered a prestigious honor among English barristers.
