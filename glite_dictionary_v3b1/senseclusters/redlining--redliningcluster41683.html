redlining

noun
discriminatory lending practice
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the discriminatory practice of denying services, especially loans or insurance, to residents of certain areas based on race or ethnicity

Examples

Redlining discrimination
The history of redlining has contributed to economic inequality in many American cities.
Banks were accused of redlining when they refused to offer mortgages in certain neighborhoods.
Laws were passed to prevent redlining and promote fair access to financial services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discrimination - unfair treatment of people based on group characteristics
  • antonym: inclusion - the practice of ensuring equal access and opportunities for all
  • synonym: segregation - the enforced separation of different groups in a community
