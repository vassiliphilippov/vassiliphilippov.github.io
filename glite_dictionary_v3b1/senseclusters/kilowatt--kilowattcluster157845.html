kilowatt

noun
unit of power (1,000 watts)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪl.əˌwɑt / (AmE), / ˈkɪl.əˌwɒt / (BrE)
a unit of power equal to 1,000 watts, used especially to measure electrical power

Examples

One kilowatt unit
Our house consumes about fifteen kilowatts during peak hours in the summer.
The new air conditioner uses less than one kilowatt of power when running at full capacity.
The solar panels generate five kilowatts of electricity on a sunny day.
