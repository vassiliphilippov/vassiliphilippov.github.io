potlatch

noun
ceremonial gift-giving feast (Pacific Northwest)
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑtˌlætʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɒt.lætʃ / (BrE)
a ceremonial feast among Indigenous peoples of the northwestern coast of North America in which the host distributes gifts to demonstrate wealth and social status

Examples

A potlatch feast
Anthropologists studied the social dynamics of the potlatch tradition.
During the potlatch, guests were presented with blankets, food, and other valuable gifts.
The chief hosted a lavish potlatch to celebrate the birth of his son.
