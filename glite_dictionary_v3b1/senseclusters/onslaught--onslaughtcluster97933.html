onslaught

noun
sudden intense outpouring, especially of speech or writing
5.7 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːnˌslɔt / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌslɔːt / (BrE)
a strong, rapid, and continuous flow of spoken or written communication; a sudden and overwhelming outpouring of something such as questions, complaints, or criticism

Examples

Onslaught of questions
During the meeting, the CEO dealt with an onslaught of criticism regarding the new policy.
Our customer service team struggled to manage the onslaught of complaints that arrived after the product recall.
The politician faced an onslaught of questions from reporters after the press conference.
