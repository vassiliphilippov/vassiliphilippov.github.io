swamp blackberry

noun
a North American bristly shrub with black berries
3.1
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ˈblækˌbɛri / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈblækbəri / (BrE)
a low-growing bristly shrub (Rubus hispidus) of eastern North America, bearing small, black, edible berries

Examples

The swamp blackberry bush
Birds are often attracted to the fruit of the swamp blackberry during late summer.
Researchers studied the impact of climate change on swamp blackberry populations in forested wetlands.
The swamp blackberry thrives in wet, acidic soils throughout the eastern United States.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blackberry - an edible soft fruit produced by various species of Rubus
  • synonym: dewberry - a trailing bramble with edible soft fruit similar to a blackberry
