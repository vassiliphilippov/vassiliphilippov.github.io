outdrink

verb
to drink more (alcohol)
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈdrɪŋk / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈdrɪŋk / (BrE)
to drink more alcohol than another person, especially in a contest or comparison

Examples

To outdrink friends
He tried to outdrink everyone at the party but ended up feeling sick.
No one could outdrink her during the celebration last night.
They made a bet to see who could outdrink the other, but both gave up quickly.
He claimed he could outdrink anyone in the bar, but he soon regretted it.
During the festival, she managed to outdrink all her friends without getting sick.
No one could outdrink the champion at last year's contest.

  • antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk
