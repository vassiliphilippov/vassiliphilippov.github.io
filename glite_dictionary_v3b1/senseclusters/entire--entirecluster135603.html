entire

adjective
(botany) smooth-edged (leaf or petal)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtaɪər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪə / (BrE)
(Botany) (of leaves or petals) having a smooth, continuous edge without indentations, teeth, or lobes.

Examples

Entire leaf edge
Many tropical species feature entire petals that form a smooth, unbroken outline.
The plant is easily recognized by its entire leaves, which lack any serrations.
You can distinguish it from similar plants by checking if the leaf margin is entire.
