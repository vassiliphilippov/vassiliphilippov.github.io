cadaverine

noun
toxic compound formed during putrefaction
Pronunciation: / kəˈdævəriːn / (AmE), / kəˈdævəriːn / (BrE)
a colorless, toxic compound with an unpleasant odor, formed during the decomposition of animal tissue

Examples

Unpleasant cadaverine smell
Exposure to high concentrations of cadaverine can be hazardous to health.
Scientists detected cadaverine in the decaying animal tissue during their experiment.
The foul smell was partially due to cadaverine produced as the body decomposed.
