strange attractor

noun
complex pattern in chaotic systems
7.2
Pronunciation: / streɪndʒ əˈtræktər / (AmE), / streɪndʒ əˈtræktə / (BrE)
In chaos theory, a complex pattern in a dynamical system toward which the system tends to evolve, where the approach is chaotic rather than predictable.

Examples

The strange attractor pattern
Researchers studied the strange attractor to understand the underlying order within chaotic phenomena.
The weather system can be modeled using a strange attractor to capture its unpredictable patterns.
When plotted, the trajectory of the particle formed a strange attractor in phase space.
