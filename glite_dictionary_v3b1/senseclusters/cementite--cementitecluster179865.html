cementite

noun
hard iron carbide in steel and cast iron
6.3 9.2
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈmen.taɪt/ /; / /sə.ˈmen.taɪt/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈmen.taɪt/ /; / /sə.ˈmen.taɪt/ (BrE)
a hard, brittle chemical compound of iron and carbon (Fe3C) present in steel and cast iron as a constituent affecting strength and hardness

Examples

Hard cementite compound
At lower cooling rates, cementite forms lamellae within pearlite structures.
In metallurgy, understanding the distribution of cementite is crucial for controlling the properties of steel.
The high carbon content caused the formation of cementite, which made the blade extremely hard but also brittle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ferrite - a relatively soft form of iron present in steel, contrasting with cementite
  • synonym: pearlite - a microstructure in steel composed of alternating layers of ferrite and cementite
