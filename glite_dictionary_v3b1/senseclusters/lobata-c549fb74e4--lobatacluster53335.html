Lobata

noun
order of ctenophores with large oral lobes
9.9
Pronunciation: / loʊˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / ləʊˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
An order of ctenophores (comb jellies) characterized by tentacles present only in the immature stage, a vertically compressed body, two large lobes near the mouth, and four pointed processes.

Examples

Lobata comb jellies
Biologists observed that Lobata use their lobes to catch plankton in coastal waters.
Several species in the order Lobata are known for their distinctive swimming patterns.
Unlike the cydippids, Lobata have large oral lobes and reduced tentacles in adulthood.
