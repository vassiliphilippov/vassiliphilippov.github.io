mobile

adjective
able to change social status or class
3.8 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈməʊbaɪl / (BrE)
able to move easily from one social status or class to another; characterized by the possibility of change in social position.

Examples

Mobile society
His family was quite mobile, moving up the social ladder within a generation.
Many consider modern societies to be more mobile than those of the past.
She admired the mobile nature of the city's workforce, with many people rising through the ranks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluid - likely or able to change easily, especially in social context
  • antonym: static - lacking movement or change, especially in social status
