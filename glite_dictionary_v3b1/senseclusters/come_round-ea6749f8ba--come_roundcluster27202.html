come round

verb
change your opinion
Pronunciation: / kʌm raʊnd / (AmE), / kʌm raʊnd / (BrE)
to change your opinion or decision, usually because someone has persuaded you or new information has become available

Examples

To come round to ideas
Eventually, she came round and agreed to support the proposal.
He might come round if you explain your reasons clearly.
It took him a while to come round to my way of thinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: change your mind - to decide to do something different from what you had planned
  • antonym: stand firm - to refuse to change your opinion or decision
