out

adverb
away from inside or hidden place
Pronunciation: / aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
away from the inside of a place or not at home

Examples

Step out the door
She looked out from behind the curtain to see who had arrived.
Smoke poured out from the chimney after the fire was lit.
The kitten sprang out of the box and ran across the room.
I'm sorry, but Jane is out at the moment and can't take your call.
The whole family was out when the package was delivered.
We decided to eat out instead of cooking at home.

  • antonym: in - located or moving inside a place or container
