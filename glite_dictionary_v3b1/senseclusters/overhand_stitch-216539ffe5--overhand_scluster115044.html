overhand stitch

noun
vertical sewing stitch over an edge
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌhænd stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəhænd stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a type of sewing stitch made by passing the needle and thread over the edge of the fabric in a vertical motion, usually to join two pieces or finish an edge

Examples

An overhand stitch on fabric
An overhand stitch is perfect for joining two pieces of felt together.
The instructions recommend an overhand stitch to secure the patches in place.
To finish the seam neatly, she used an overhand stitch along the raw edges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whipstitch - a similar sewing stitch passing over the edge but often at a slant
