eat one's heart out

verb
suffer from intense longing or sorrow
8.3 8.6
Pronunciation: / it wʌnz hɑrt aʊt / (AmE), / iːt wʌnz hɑːt aʊt / (BrE)
To feel intense longing, jealousy, or sorrow, usually because of unrequited love, missed opportunity, or envy.

Examples

To eat his heart out with regret
He sat by the window, eating his heart out with regret for letting her go.
She’s been eating her heart out ever since she heard the news about his engagement.
You can eat your heart out thinking about what might have been, but it won’t change the past.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pine - to feel a lingering, often nostalgic longing or grief
  • synonym: yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something
