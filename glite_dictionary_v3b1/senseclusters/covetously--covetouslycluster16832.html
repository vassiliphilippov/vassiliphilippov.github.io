covetously

adverb
in an envious or jealous way
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvətəsli /; / ˈkʌv.ə.t̬əs.li / (AmE), / ˈkʌvɪtəsli /; / ˈkʌv.ɪ.təs.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows a strong desire for something that belongs to someone else; enviously or greedily

Examples

Look covetously at things
He eyed his neighbor’s garden covetously, wishing for such beautiful flowers.
She watched covetously as her colleague accepted the award.
The child gazed covetously at the candy behind the glass.
He watched covetously as his colleague received the prestigious award.
She looked covetously at her neighbor's new car, longing to own one herself.
The children eyed the last slice of cake covetously, each hoping to claim it.
