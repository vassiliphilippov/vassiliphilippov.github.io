figurally

adverb
metaphorically
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪɡjɚəli / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡjʊrəli /; / ˈfɪɡjʊərəli / (BrE)
in a non-literal, metaphorical, or symbolic way; not literally

The myth was interpreted figurally to reveal deeper symbolic meanings.
In medieval art, animals were often depicted figurally to represent human virtues or vices.
The teacher explained the poem figurally, focusing on its use of imagery and symbolism.
