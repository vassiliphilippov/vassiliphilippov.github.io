alexia

noun
loss of ability to read (medical)
7.5
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛksiə / (AmE), / əˈlɛksɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person loses the ability to read, usually due to brain damage.

Examples

Alexia after stroke
After his stroke, the patient was diagnosed with alexia and could no longer read newspapers.
Doctors identified alexia as the cause of her sudden inability to comprehend written text.
Researchers studied cases of alexia to better understand how the brain processes language.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dyslexia - difficulty in learning to read or interpret words
