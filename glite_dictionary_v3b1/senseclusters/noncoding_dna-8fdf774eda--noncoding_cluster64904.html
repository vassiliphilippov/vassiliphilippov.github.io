noncoding DNA

noun
DNA not coding for proteins
3.8 4.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈkoʊ.dɪŋ ˈdiː.en.eɪ/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈkəʊ.dɪŋ ˈdiː.en.eɪ/ (BrE)
DNA sequences that do not code for proteins, often involved in regulation or other functions within the genome.

Examples

Noncoding DNA sequences
Although once considered 'junk', noncoding DNA has been found to play important roles in gene regulation.
Most of the human genome consists of noncoding DNA that does not produce proteins.
Scientists are still studying the functions of noncoding DNA in different organisms.
