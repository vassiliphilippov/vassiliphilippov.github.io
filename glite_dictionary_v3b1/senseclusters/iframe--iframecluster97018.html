iframe

noun
key video frame
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.freɪm/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.freɪm/ (BrE)
a frame in digital video encoding that is encoded independently and serves as a reference point for decoding and seeking

Examples

Video iframe
The video player quickly located the iframe to start playback from the correct position.
Editing software allows users to jump between each iframe for precise video cutting.
When streaming, the decoder relies on the next iframe to recover from errors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: B-frame - a video frame that is encoded using both previous and following frames as references
  • antonym: P-frame - a video frame that is encoded based on differences from a previous frame
  • synonym: keyframe - a frame that defines the starting or ending point of a smooth transition in video
