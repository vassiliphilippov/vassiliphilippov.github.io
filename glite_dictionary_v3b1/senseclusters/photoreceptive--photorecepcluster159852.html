photoreceptive

adjective
responsive to light
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊrɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊrɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
able to detect or respond to light, especially in living things, cells, or sensors

Examples

Photoreceptive cells
Certain algae are photoreceptive and move toward sources of light in their environment.
The photoreceptive cells in the human eye allow us to see in different lighting conditions.
Researchers study photoreceptive proteins to understand how plants sense sunlight.
The plant's photoreceptive cells help it grow toward sources of sunlight.
Scientists developed a photoreceptive coating that changes color when exposed to UV light.
Some animals have highly photoreceptive eyes that allow them to see in near darkness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonresponsive - not reacting or responding to stimuli
  • synonym: photosensitive - reacting to or affected by light
