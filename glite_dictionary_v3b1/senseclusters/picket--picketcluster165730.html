picket

noun
a protester outside a workplace
8.8 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪk.ɪt/ /; / /ˈpɪk.ət/ (AmE), /ˈpɪk.ɪt/ /; / /ˈpɪk.ət/ (BrE)
a person who is stationed outside a place of work as part of a protest, especially by a labor union, to dissuade or inform others entering the workplace

Examples

Union picket protest
A group of pickets stood outside the factory entrance, holding signs and chanting slogans.
During the strike, each picket took turns monitoring the gate to talk to arriving workers.
The union instructed its members not to cross the picket line until negotiations resumed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: scab - a worker who refuses to join a strike or continues to work during one
  • synonym: strike - a period of time when workers stop working as a form of protest
