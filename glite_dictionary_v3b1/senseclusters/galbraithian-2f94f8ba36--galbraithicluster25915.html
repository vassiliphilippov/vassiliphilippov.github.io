Galbraithian

noun
follower of Galbraith
9.9
Pronunciation: /gæl.ˈbreɪ.θiː.ən/ /; / /gæl.ˈbreɪ.θən/ (AmE), /gæl.ˈbreɪ.θiː.ən/ /; / /gæl.ˈbreɪ.θən/ (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the economic ideas and policies of John Kenneth Galbraith, especially favoring government intervention in markets

Examples

A Galbraithian thinker
As a proud Galbraithian, he believed that government should play a strong role in regulating the economy.
The professor, a noted Galbraithian, often criticized free-market policies in her lectures.
Many Galbraithians argue that public investment is essential for economic stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Keynesian - a person who supports the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes
  • antonym: laissez-faire - an economic philosophy advocating minimal government intervention
