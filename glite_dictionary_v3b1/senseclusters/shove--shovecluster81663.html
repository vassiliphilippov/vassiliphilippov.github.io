shove

verb
push roughly or forcefully
3.6 2.3
Pronunciation: /ʃʌv/ (AmE), /ʃʌv/ (BrE)
to push someone or something roughly or forcefully, often in a hurried or careless way

Examples

To shove the door
He had to shove the heavy door open with his shoulder.
People began to shove each other as the crowd moved forward.
She shoved her way through the busy market.
A woman accidentally shoved him aside while rushing to board the bus.
He had to shove his way through the crowd to catch the train.
The children shoved each other playfully as they entered the playground.
He shoved the door open with his shoulder when it was stuck.
Quickly shoving the papers into his bag, he rushed out of the office.
She shoved all her clothes into the suitcase in a hurry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jostle - to push, elbow, or bump against someone roughly
  • antonym: pull - to move something toward oneself, opposite of push
  • synonym: push - to move something or someone by pressing against it
  • synonym: thrust - to push something suddenly or violently in a specified direction
