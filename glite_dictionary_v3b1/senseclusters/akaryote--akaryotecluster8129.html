akaryote

noun
cell without a nucleus
8.3
Pronunciation: / əˈkæriˌoʊt / (AmE), / əˈkæriəʊt / (BrE)
A cell that lacks a nucleus, such as an erythrocyte.

Examples

A akaryote cell
Red blood cells are an example of an akaryote found in humans.
Scientists study akaryotes to understand how cellular functions occur without a nucleus.
Unlike most cells, an akaryote does not contain nuclear DNA.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eukaryote - an organism with cells that have a nucleus
  • synonym: prokaryote - a microscopic single-celled organism without a distinct nucleus
