right prism

noun
prism with rectangular sides perpendicular to bases
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
A prism whose sides are rectangles perpendicular to its two parallel bases.

Examples

A right prism shape
In a right prism, all the lateral faces are rectangles perpendicular to the bases.
Students constructed a model of a right prism using cardboard and glue for their geometry project.
The volume of a right prism can be calculated by multiplying the area of the base by the height.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oblique prism - a prism whose sides are not perpendicular to the bases
