immaterially

adverb
not importantly
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˈtɪriəli / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtɪəriəli / (BrE)
in a way that is not important or relevant; without significant effect or consequence

Examples

Immaterially affect the result
The mistake in the report affected the outcome only immaterially and did not change the final decision.
Whether the meeting is at 2 or 3 p.m. is immaterially different for most participants.
The new policy immaterially altered the company's day-to-day operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insignificantly - in a way that is not important or noticeable
  • synonym: irrelevantly - in a way that is not related or important to the matter
  • antonym: materially - in a way that is important or significant
