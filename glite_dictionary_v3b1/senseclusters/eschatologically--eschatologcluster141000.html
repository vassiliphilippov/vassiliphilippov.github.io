eschatologically

adverb
in relation to eschatology
Pronunciation: / ˌɛskəˌtɑːˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɛskətəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to eschatology; concerning the ultimate destiny of humans or the end of the world.

Examples

Interpreted eschatologically in religion
His theories are often interpreted eschatologically within religious philosophy.
The debate proceeded eschatologically, referencing concepts of final judgment and salvation.
The novel presents history eschatologically, focusing on themes of apocalypse and renewal.

Related Terms
