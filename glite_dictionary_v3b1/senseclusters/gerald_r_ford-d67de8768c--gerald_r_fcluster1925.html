Gerald R. Ford

proper noun
class of U.S. Navy aircraft carriers
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒer.əld ɑːr fɔːrd/ (AmE), /ˈdʒer.əld ɑː fɔːd/ (BrE)
a class of United States Navy nuclear-powered aircraft carriers that began with USS Gerald R. Ford (CVN-78), designed with advanced radar, weapons, and propulsion systems.

Examples

Gerald R. Ford-class carrier
The Gerald R. Ford-class carriers are being built with new radar and propulsion technologies.
Critics questioned the cost of each Gerald R. Ford-class ship during the budget hearings in Congress.
Several allied navies have studied the design of the Gerald R. Ford-class for future carrier projects.
