Ancylostoma

noun
parasitic hookworm genus
Pronunciation: / ˌænsɪˈlɒstəˌmə / (AmE), / ˌænsɪˈlɒstəˌmə / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic hookworms that infect the intestines of mammals, including humans and animals

Examples

Ancylostoma infection
The veterinarian diagnosed the puppy with an Ancylostoma infection after examining its stool sample.
Public health officials warned about the risks of Ancylostoma in areas with poor sanitation.
Researchers are studying how Ancylostoma species adapt to different mammalian hosts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hookworm - a parasitic worm that lives in the intestines of its host
