American spicebush

noun
aromatic North American shrub with yellow flowers and berries
8.7
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈspaɪsbʊʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈspaɪsbʊʃ / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, noted for its highly aromatic leaves and bark, yellow flowers, and scarlet or yellow berries

Examples

The American spicebush shrub
Botanists identify the American spicebush by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers.
The American spicebush is often planted in gardens for its fragrant foliage and attractive berries.
Wildlife such as birds are attracted to the berries produced by the American spicebush.
