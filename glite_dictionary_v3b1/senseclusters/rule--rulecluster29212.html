rule

noun
period of authority of ruler or government
2.0 42/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀêl / (AmE), / ruÀêl / (BrE)
the control, authority, or period of governance by a person, group, or government

Examples

Civil liberties were limited under military rule in the 1980s.
Historians debate the impact of Queen Victoria's rule on the British Empire.
The country prospered during the rule of the new emperor.

Synonyms

  • rulership
    7.5 0.36/m
    rulership means the position or period of a ruler's power and control, while rule means governance or authority by a person or group.
  • governance
    5.6 16/m
    governance describes the system and process of running a country or group, while rule means the actual control or period of power.
  • authority
    3.5 46/m
    authority is the power to give orders and make decisions, while rule is the wider control or time of governing.
  • dominion
    7.7 2.48/m
    dominion means legal or sovereign power over a territory, while rule is the general control or period by a ruler or government.
  • administration
    3.4 4.84/m
    administration refers to carrying out laws and managing tasks by officials, while rule is the act or time of governing and control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anarchy - a state of society without government or law
  • synonym: dominion - sovereignty or control, especially by a state or government
  • synonym: reign - period during which a sovereign rules
