aura

noun
radiant light shown around a saint
8.1 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…î…π…ô / (AmE), / Àà…îÀêr…ô / (BrE)
a radiant light or luminous glow seen or depicted as surrounding the head or body of a holy figure, such as a saint, in religious art

Examples

A glowing aura
A soft, glowing aura was illustrated to indicate the angel's purity.
In many icons, the aura signifies holiness and divinity.
The painter depicted a golden aura encircling the saint's head.

Synonyms

  • aureola
    9.4 0.00/m
    aureola is a narrow ring of light shown around the head of a saint in art, while aura is a broader glowing light.
  • halo
    5.8 1.03/m
    halo is the common circle of light drawn around a holy person's head, while aura describes a more general surrounding glow.
  • nimbus
    9.1 0.11/m
    nimbus is a ring or disk of light often drawn tightly around a saint's head, while aura is a looser luminous glow.
  • gloriole
    9.9 0.00/m
    gloriole is a small, old-fashioned word for a circular light around a saint in art, while aura is more general and modern.
  • glory
    8.6 8.17/m
    glory is the honor or shining light shown around holy figures in art, while aura is a visual glow surrounding the body or head.
