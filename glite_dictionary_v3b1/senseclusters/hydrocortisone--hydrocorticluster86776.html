hydrocortisone

noun
a steroid hormone; anti-inflammatory drug
5.5 6.1
Pronunciation: / haɪdroʊˈkɔrtəˌsoʊn / (AmE), / haɪdrəʊˈkɔːtɪsəʊn / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex that is involved in the body's response to stress and used medically to treat inflammation and allergies

Examples

Hydrocortisone replacement therapy
Patients with Addison's disease may require hydrocortisone replacement therapy.
The doctor prescribed hydrocortisone cream to reduce the inflammation on my skin.
The nurse applied a small amount of hydrocortisone to the allergic rash.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: adrenaline - another adrenal hormone involved in stress response
  • synonym: cortisol - the natural form of hydrocortisone produced in the body
  • degree higher: prednisone - a synthetic drug similar in effect to hydrocortisone
