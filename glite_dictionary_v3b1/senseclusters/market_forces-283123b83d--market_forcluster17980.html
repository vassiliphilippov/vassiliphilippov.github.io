market forces

noun
economic forces shaping markets
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkət ˈfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
the economic factors of supply and demand whose interaction determines prices and the allocation of resources in a market economy

Examples

Market forces of supply
Many economists argue that market forces will naturally correct imbalances in the economy.
The price of oil is largely determined by market forces rather than government intervention.
When market forces are left to operate freely, inefficient businesses often fail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: supply and demand - the relationship between the availability of goods and services and the desire for them
