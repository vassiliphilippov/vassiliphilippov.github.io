nervosa

x
Latin medical term
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɚˈvoʊsə / (AmE), / nəˈvəʊsə / (BrE)
a Latin term meaning 'nervous', used in medical names for certain eating disorders

Examples

Anorexia nervosa
The term nervosa is often used in the names of eating disorders such as anorexia nervosa.
Doctors sometimes explain that nervosa refers to the nervous origin of certain conditions.
In medical terminology, nervosa indicates a disorder related to the nervous system or mental state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anorexia nervosa - an eating disorder characterized by restricted food intake and fear of gaining weight
  • synonym: bulimia nervosa - an eating disorder involving episodes of binge eating followed by purging
