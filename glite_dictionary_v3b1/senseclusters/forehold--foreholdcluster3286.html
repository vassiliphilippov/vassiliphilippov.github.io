forehold

noun
front cargo space
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfɔːhəʊld / (BrE)
the forward cargo hold of a ship, used for storing goods or supplies

Examples

The forehold of a ship
The crew stored extra ropes in the forehold before setting sail.
Heavy machinery was carefully loaded into the forehold to balance the ship.
Inspectors checked the forehold for any signs of water leakage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aft hold - the rear cargo hold of a ship
  • synonym: hold - a large space in a ship for storing cargo
