leavening

noun
subtle influence that enlivens or modifies
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛvənɪŋ / (BrE)
a subtle influence that lightens, modifies, or enlivens something

Examples

Leavening effect
A leavening of optimism can transform even the gloomiest report.
Her remarks brought a welcome leavening of wit to the otherwise tedious debate.
The director's playful approach provided a leavening to the serious subject matter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: burden - something that weighs down or makes heavier
  • synonym: influence - the capacity to have an effect on something
