zinc deficiency

noun
insufficient zinc in the body
1.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /zɪŋk dɪ.ˈfɪ.ʃən.siː/ (AmE), /zɪŋk dɪ.ˈfɪ.ʃən.siː/ (BrE)
a condition caused by insufficient zinc in the diet or impaired zinc absorption, leading to health problems such as growth retardation, immune dysfunction, and skin lesions.

Examples

Chronic zinc deficiency
Chronic zinc deficiency can lead to weakened immune response and delayed wound healing.
Dietitians often recommend oysters and beef for people at risk of zinc deficiency.
Many developing countries face public health issues related to zinc deficiency among children.
