beta decay

noun
radioactive decay emitting beta particles
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.tə dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
radioactive decay of an atomic nucleus accompanied by the emission of a beta particle

Examples

Beta decay process
Scientists observed beta decay in the radioactive isotope during the experiment.
The process of beta decay is crucial in understanding how elements change in nature.
When beta decay occurs, a neutron in the nucleus transforms into a proton and emits an electron.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gamma decay - radioactive decay by emission of a gamma ray
