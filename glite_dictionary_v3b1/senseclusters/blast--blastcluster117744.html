blast

verb
fire forcefully or release suddenly
Pronunciation: / blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
to hit, shoot, or propel something with great force, especially in sports

Examples

To blast a ball
He blasted away at the targets during practice.
The hunters blast into the sky as the ducks fly overhead.
The soldiers began to blast their rifles at the approaching enemy.
She blasted her serve past her opponent in the final game.
The rookie blasted the ball over the fence for a home run.
The striker blasted the ball into the top corner of the net.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shoot - to fire a gun or other weapon
  • synonym: slam - to hit or push something with great force
  • synonym: smash - to hit something with great force so that it breaks or moves violently
  • antonym: tap - to hit something lightly
