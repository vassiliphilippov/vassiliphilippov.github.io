hypoxia

noun
insufficient oxygen in body tissues
1.0 7.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑksiə / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒksɪə / (BrE)
a condition in which there is an insufficient amount of oxygen reaching the tissues of the body

Examples

Severe hypoxia condition
Doctors monitor blood oxygen levels to detect potential hypoxia in patients with respiratory problems.
Mountain climbers may experience hypoxia at high altitudes due to the thinner air.
Prolonged hypoxia can cause serious damage to vital organs such as the brain.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anoxia - absence of oxygen in body tissues
  • antonym: hyperoxia - excess oxygen in body tissues
