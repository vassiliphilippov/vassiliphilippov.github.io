precipitation

noun
formation of a chemical precipitate
Pronunciation: / prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a solid substance is separated from a solution as a precipitate during a chemical reaction

Examples

Precipitation reaction
In analytical chemistry, precipitation is used to separate components from a mixture.
Temperature changes can influence the rate of precipitation during a chemical reaction.
The addition of silver nitrate to the solution resulted in the precipitation of silver chloride.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystallization - the process by which a solid forms, often from a solution, and grows into a crystal
