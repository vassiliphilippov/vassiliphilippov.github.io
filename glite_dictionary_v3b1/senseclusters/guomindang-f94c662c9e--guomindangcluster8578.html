Guomindang

proper noun
Chinese political party founded in 1911
Pronunciation: / ɡwoʊˈmɪnˌdɑːŋ / (AmE), / ɡwəʊˈmɪn.dæŋ / (BrE)
a Chinese political party, founded in 1911 by Sun Yat-sen, that governed China until 1949 and later became the ruling party of Taiwan.

Examples

The Guomindang party
After losing the civil war, the Guomindang retreated to Taiwan, where it established a government.
Sun Yat-sen is recognized as the founder of the Guomindang, which became a powerful force in modern Chinese history.
The Guomindang played a significant role in China's political development during the early twentieth century.
