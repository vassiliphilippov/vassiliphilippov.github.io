Vanilla planifolia

noun
species of orchid producing vanilla beans
7.9
Pronunciation: / vəˈnɪlə plæˌnɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / vəˈnɪlə plæˌnɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of climbing orchid native to Mexico and Central America, widely cultivated for its podlike fruit, which produces vanilla beans.

Examples

Vanilla planifolia orchid plant
Vanilla planifolia is the primary source of natural vanilla flavor used in desserts and perfumes.
Farmers in Madagascar grow large plantations of Vanilla planifolia for export.
The cultivation of Vanilla planifolia requires careful hand-pollination to produce beans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vanilla - a substance obtained from the vanilla bean, used for flavoring
