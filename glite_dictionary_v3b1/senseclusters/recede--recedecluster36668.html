recede

verb
move back; retreat
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsid / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːd / (BrE)
to move back or away, or to become less strong or noticeable (physically or in a more abstract sense)

Examples

Pain recedes
As the pain receded, she was finally able to get some rest.
His hairline has started to recede over the past few years.
The floodwaters began to recede after several days of heavy rain.
After the flood, the waters slowly began to recede from the streets.
As he grew older, his hairline started to recede steadily.
The footsteps receded into the distance until they were no longer audible.
As time passed, the pain of his loss began to recede, allowing him to move forward.
Her fears slowly receded as she grew more confident in her new surroundings.
The sound of the train's whistle receded into the distance until it was barely audible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward
  • synonym: diminish - to become less or smaller
  • synonym: fade - to gradually become less clear or strong
  • synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw
