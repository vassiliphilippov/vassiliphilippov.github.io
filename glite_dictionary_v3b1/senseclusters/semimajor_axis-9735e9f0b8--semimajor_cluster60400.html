semimajor axis

noun
half of the longest diameter of an ellipse
7.2 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈmeɪdʒər ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmeɪdʒə ˈakses / (BrE)
Half of the longest diameter of an ellipse; the distance from the center of an ellipse to its farthest edge.

Examples

The semimajor axis length
In an elliptical orbit, the semimajor axis determines the object's average distance from the focus.
The length of the semimajor axis is crucial for calculating the period of an orbiting body.
The planet's orbit has a semimajor axis of about 1.5 astronomical units.
