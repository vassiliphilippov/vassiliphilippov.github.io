burdensome

adjective
causing difficulty or hardship
7.1 1.84/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ùÀêd…ônÀås åm / (AmE), / Ààb…úÀêd…ônÀås åm / (BrE)
causing difficulty, worry, or hardship; difficult to bear or carry out

Examples

A burdensome task
Caring for her sick relative became increasingly burdensome over the months.
Filling out the paperwork was a particularly burdensome chore for the new employee.
The new regulations placed burdensome requirements on small businesses.

Synonyms

  • onerous
    8.1 2.14/m
    onerous describes a formal duty that needs heavy effort and causes strain, while burdensome is a general word for something hard to bear.
  • troublesome
    3.9 4.76/m
    troublesome causes worry, annoyance, or practical problems, while burdensome emphasizes hardship and difficulty to carry out.
  • arduous
    7.7 2.83/m
    arduous describes long, very hard work that tires you, while burdensome focuses on causing difficulty or worry.
  • taxing
    6.8 0.26/m
    taxing means demanding and tiring on your energy, while burdensome highlights the hardship or worry something brings.
  • cumbersome
    6.7 2.49/m
    cumbersome is large, heavy, and hard to handle, while burdensome means causing difficulty, worry, or hardship to bear.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easy - not difficult or demanding
  • synonym: onerous - requiring great effort; burdensome
  • synonym: taxing - demanding a lot of effort; burdensome
