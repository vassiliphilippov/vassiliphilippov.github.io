orotund

adjective
pompous or grand style (of speech or writing)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrəˌtʌnd / (AmE), / ˈɒrətʌnd / (BrE)
(of writing or speech) pompous or overly grand and impressive in style or tone

Examples

Orotund language
She mocked his orotund pronouncements, preferring straightforward explanations to flowery language.
The mayor's orotund style made his speeches sound more dramatic than substantial.
The novel's opening chapter is written in an orotund tone, full of elaborate metaphors and grand statements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombastic - high-sounding but with little meaning; inflated
  • synonym: grandiloquent - pompous or extravagant in style, especially in a way that is intended to impress
  • antonym: plain - simple or lacking embellishment in speech or writing
