phenotype

noun
observable characteristics of an organism
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːnəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈfiːnəʊtaɪp / (BrE)
the observable physical or biochemical characteristics of an organism, as determined by both genetic makeup and environmental influences

Examples

Human phenotype
Researchers studied the phenotype to understand how genetic mutations affect physical traits.
Some diseases only manifest when a certain phenotype is expressed in combination with environmental factors.
The phenotype of the plant was influenced by both its genes and the amount of sunlight it received.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genotype - the genetic makeup of an organism
