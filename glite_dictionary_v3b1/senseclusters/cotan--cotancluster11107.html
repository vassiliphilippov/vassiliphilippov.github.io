cotan

noun
cotangent function in trigonometry
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌtæn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌtæn / (BrE)
the cotangent; the ratio of the length of the adjacent side to the length of the opposite side in a right-angled triangle, or the reciprocal of the tangent of an angle

Examples

The cotan of angle
In trigonometry, the cotan of an angle is the ratio of the adjacent side to the opposite side.
The graph of the cotan function has vertical asymptotes where the tangent is zero.
You can use cotan to solve for unknown sides in a right triangle.
