pebibit

noun
unit of digital information (2^50 bits)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛbɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛbɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,024 tebibits or 2^50 (1,125,899,906,842,624) bits, used in computing.

Examples

One pebibit unit
Backup systems are now being designed to manage pebibit-scale data volumes.
The new storage array can hold up to two pebibits of data.
Transferring a pebibit of information over a network can take hours at standard speeds.
