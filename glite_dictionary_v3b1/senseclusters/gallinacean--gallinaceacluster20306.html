gallinacean

noun
heavy-bodied ground-feeding domestic or game bird
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /gæl.ɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /gæl.ə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /gæl.ɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /gæl.ə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A bird of the order Galliformes, which includes heavy-bodied, largely ground-feeding domestic or game birds such as chickens, turkeys, and pheasants.

Examples

A gallinacean bird
Chickens and turkeys are both considered gallinaceans by ornithologists.
Many gallinacean species prefer to forage for seeds on the forest floor.
The farmer kept a variety of gallinaceans for eggs and meat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: game bird - a bird hunted for sport or food, often including gallinaceans
  • antonym: waterfowl - aquatic birds such as ducks, contrasting with gallinaceans
