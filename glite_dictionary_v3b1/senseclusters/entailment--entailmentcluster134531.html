entailment

noun
inferred or implied consequence
5.1 6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈteɪlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈteɪlmənt / (BrE)
something that is inferred, deduced, or implied as a logical consequence.

Examples

Entailment of a result
Her statement carries the entailment that there may be more problems ahead.
In logic, entailment refers to the relationship between statements where one follows from another.
The new policy had legal entailments that were not immediately apparent.
