retroactively

adverb
with effect from a past date
5.5 5.8
Pronunciation: /re.troʊ.ˈæk.tɪv.liː/ (AmE), /re.trəʊ.ˈæk.tɪv.liː/ (BrE)
In a way that takes effect from a date in the past, typically applying to events that have already occurred.

Examples

Apply retroactively from date
If approved, your insurance coverage will begin retroactively from the date of application.
She received a bonus that was paid retroactively for her extra work last year.
The law was applied retroactively, affecting many previous cases.
