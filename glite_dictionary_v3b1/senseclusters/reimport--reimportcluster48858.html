reimport

verb
to import again
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪmˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to import something again, such as goods, data, or files, into a system or country for a second time

Examples

To reimport data
After correcting the errors, she needed to reimport the spreadsheet into the database.
The company decided to reimport the product after making improvements overseas.
If the file is missing data, you may have to reimport it to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: export - to send goods, data, or files out of a country or system
  • synonym: import - to bring goods, data, or files into a country or system
