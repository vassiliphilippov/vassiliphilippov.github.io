hang

verb
linger in an oppressive way
7.9
Pronunciation: / hæŋ / (AmE), / hæŋ / (BrE)
to remain in the air or atmosphere, or to stay present in a place (such as a smell, feeling, or mood)

Examples

Fog hangs in the air
A sense of dread hangs over the office as layoffs are announced.
Doubt hung over the outcome of the trial throughout the week.
His failure to apologize hung heavily on his mind for days.
A faint scent of perfume still hung in the room long after she left.
Heavy clouds hang over the city, threatening rain all day.
The mist hangs above the lake each morning, giving the landscape a mysterious look.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dangle - to hang or swing loosely
  • synonym: hover - to remain in one place in the air
  • synonym: weigh - to burden, especially mentally or emotionally
