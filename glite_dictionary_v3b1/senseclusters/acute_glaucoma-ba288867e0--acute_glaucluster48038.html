acute glaucoma

noun
rapidly developing severe glaucoma
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut ɡlɔˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ɡlɔːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A severe form of glaucoma in which sudden blockage of fluid outflow by the iris causes a rapid increase in intraocular pressure, often resulting in pain and potential vision loss if not treated promptly.

Examples

Severe acute glaucoma
A patient experiencing eye pain and blurred vision may be suffering from acute glaucoma.
If not treated quickly, acute glaucoma can lead to permanent vision loss.
Ophthalmologists warn that acute glaucoma is a medical emergency requiring immediate intervention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glaucoma - a group of eye diseases causing damage to the optic nerve due to high intraocular pressure
