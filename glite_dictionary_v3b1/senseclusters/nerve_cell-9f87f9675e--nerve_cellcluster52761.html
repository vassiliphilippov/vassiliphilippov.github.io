nerve cell

noun
cell in nervous system; neuron
6.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /nɜːrv sel/ (AmE), /nɜːv sel/ (BrE)
A cell in the nervous system specialized to conduct nerve impulses; also known as a neuron.

Examples

A nerve cell network
A human brain contains billions of nerve cells connected in complex networks.
Damage to a nerve cell can result in loss of sensation or movement.
The nerve cell transmits signals to other parts of the body.
