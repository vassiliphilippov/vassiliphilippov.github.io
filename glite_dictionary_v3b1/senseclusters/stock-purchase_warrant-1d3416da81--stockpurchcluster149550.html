stock-purchase warrant

noun
security giving right to buy stock
4.3
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈpɜrtʃəs ˈwɔrənt / (AmE), / stɒk ˈpɜːtʃəs ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
a security issued by a corporation that gives the holder the right to purchase a specified amount of common stock at a stated price, usually issued together with bonds or preferred stock

Examples

A stock-purchase warrant right
He decided to exercise his stock-purchase warrant when the market price exceeded the exercise price.
Her portfolio includes several stock-purchase warrants from different technology firms.
The company issued a stock-purchase warrant to attract more investors to its bond offering.
