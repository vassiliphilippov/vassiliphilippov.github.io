Rhus

noun
genus of sumac shrubs and small trees
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀês / (AmE), / ruÀês / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen shrubs and small trees, commonly known as sumacs, native to temperate and subtropical regions and distinguished from the related genus Toxicodendron.

Examples

In landscaping, Rhus is valued for its hardiness and ornamental foliage.
Some Rhus varieties are used to produce natural dyes and spices.
The leaves of Rhus species often turn bright red in the autumn.
