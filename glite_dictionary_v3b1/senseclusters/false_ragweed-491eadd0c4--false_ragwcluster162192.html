false ragweed

noun
North American plant causing hay fever
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈræɡˌwid / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈræɡˌwiːd / (BrE)
a tall annual plant native to central North America, found in moist rich soil, that produces large amounts of pollen and can cause contact dermatitis; a major cause of hay fever

Examples

False ragweed pollen
False ragweed can trigger severe allergy symptoms during late summer in many parts of North America.
False ragweed thrives in damp, rich soils and is often found along riverbanks.
Many people develop itchy rashes after touching false ragweed in the fields.
