genetic mutation

noun
alteration in genetic structure
5.5
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk mjuːˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk mjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a permanent alteration in the DNA sequence that makes up a gene; an event that changes genetic structure

Examples

A genetic mutation event
A single genetic mutation can sometimes lead to a serious inherited disease.
Researchers discovered a genetic mutation that increases the risk of developing cancer.
Some genetic mutations provide organisms with beneficial traits that help them survive.
