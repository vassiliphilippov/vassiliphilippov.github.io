kauri

noun
resin from New Zealand kauri trees used in varnishes
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəri / (BrE)
a resin obtained from the kauri trees of New Zealand, often found as a fossil and used in making varnishes and linoleum

Examples

Kauri resin pieces
Old deposits of kauri were discovered while digging the new road.
The craftsman used kauri to give the cabinet a beautiful glossy finish.
The varnish contains kauri resin, making it exceptionally durable.
