preponderance

noun
superiority in number or amount
9.9
Pronunciation: / prɪˈpɑːndərəns /; / prɪˈpɑndərəns / (AmE), / prɪˈpɒndərəns / (BrE)
the state of being greater in number, amount, or importance than something else

Examples

A preponderance of opinions
A preponderance of expert opinions supports the new policy.
The preponderance of evidence suggested that the company was responsible for the spill.
There was a clear preponderance of women at the conference this year.
A slight preponderance on one side caused the balance to tip.
In elections, even a minor preponderance of votes can decide the outcome.
The preponderance of evidence suggests the defendant is guilty.
In some debates, the preponderance of opinion favored immediate action.
The preponderance of power rested with the central government after the reforms.
There is a clear preponderance of evidence supporting the new theory.
