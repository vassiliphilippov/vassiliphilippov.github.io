sharpness

noun
harshness or abruptness of manner
3.8 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈʃɑːrp.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈʃɑːp.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being harsh, abrupt, or urgent in speech or manner

Examples

Sharpness in his voice
Despite the sharpness of his remarks, his intentions were good.
She regretted the sharpness with which she dismissed her friend's question.
The sharpness in his voice made everyone uneasy during the meeting.
Despite her usual calm, a sudden sharpness crept into her voice as she spoke.
Her words carried a surprising sharpness that caught everyone’s attention.
There was unmistakable sharpness in his tone when he addressed the issue.

  • antonym: bluntness - the quality of being uncompromisingly forthright or direct, sometimes to the point of rudeness
  • synonym: edge - a quality of urgency or intensity in a voice or manner
