perfect tense

noun
completed-action verb tense
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜr.fɪkt tɛns / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to indicate that an action has been completed at some point before now or before another event

Examples

The perfect tense form
In English, the perfect tense is formed using "have" or "has" and a past participle.
Learning how to use the perfect tense can help make your writing clearer.
We use the perfect tense to talk about actions that have a result in the present.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: past perfect - tense showing an action completed before a certain time in the past
  • degree higher: present perfect - tense showing an action completed before now or continuing until now
