Lolium temulentum

noun
a species of poisonous annual grass
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊliəm tɛˈmʌlɛntəm / (AmE), / ˈlɒliəm tɛˈmjʊlɛntəm / (BrE)
A species of weedy annual grass often found in grainfields and cultivated lands, with seeds that are sometimes considered poisonous.

Examples

Lolium temulentum grass
Farmers must carefully remove Lolium temulentum from their wheat fields to avoid contaminated crops.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of Lolium temulentum in cultivated farmland.
The seeds of Lolium temulentum have historically caused health problems when mixed with grain.
