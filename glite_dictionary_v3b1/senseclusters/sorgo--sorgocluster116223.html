sorgo

noun
sorghum grown for syrup
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A variety of sorghum cultivated chiefly for making syrup from its sweet juice.

Examples

Sweet sorgo syrup
Local producers use sorgo as a natural sweetener in traditional recipes.
The farmer planted sorgo to harvest both grain and syrup.
Unlike corn, sorgo is well-suited to drier climates and poor soil.
