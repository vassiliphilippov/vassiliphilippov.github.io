prognostic

noun
sign or omen of something to come
8.8 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ëÀê…°Ààn…ëÀêst…™k / (AmE), / pr…í…°Ààn…íst…™k / (BrE)
A sign or indication of a future event; an omen or predictive symptom.

Examples

A prognostic sign
Many considered the dark clouds a prognostic of the approaching storm.
Some physicians regard certain early symptoms as a prognostic for the development of the disease.
The sudden drop in stock prices was seen as a prognostic of economic recession.

Synonyms

  • omen
    4.9 3.20/m
    an omen is a clear event people read as a future sign, while a prognostic is a formal sign used by experts to predict outcomes.
  • presage
    8.7 0.04/m
    a presage is a feeling or small sign that warns of bad things to come, while a prognostic is a neutral predictive sign.
  • portent
    8.7 0.87/m
    a portent is a serious, often terrible sign of something huge about to happen, while a prognostic is any predictive sign.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harbinger - a person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another
  • synonym: omen - a sign that is believed to predict the future
  • synonym: sign - an event whose presence or occurrence indicates something
