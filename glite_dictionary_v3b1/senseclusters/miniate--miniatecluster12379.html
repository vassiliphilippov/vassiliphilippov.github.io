miniate

verb
to paint with red lead or vermilion
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪeɪt /; / ˈmɪniˌeɪt / (BrE)
to decorate or paint manuscripts with red pigment, especially red lead or vermilion

Examples

To miniate manuscripts
Artists sometimes miniated their works to highlight important passages and embellish texts.
In medieval times, scribes would miniate the edges of the parchment with bright vermilion.
The ancient manuscript was miniated with delicate red decorations on every page.
In the restoration process, special care was taken to miniate the faded headings using natural pigments.
Medieval scribes would often miniate the initial letters to enhance the manuscripts' beauty.
The artist miniated the margins of the book with intricate red illustrations.
