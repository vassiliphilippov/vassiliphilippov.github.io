lightspeed

noun
speed of light
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtspiːd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtspiːd / (BrE)
the speed at which light travels in a vacuum, considered the fastest possible speed in physics

Examples

Lightspeed travel
In science fiction, spaceships often travel at lightspeed to reach distant galaxies.
Nothing with mass can accelerate to lightspeed according to the laws of physics.
The announcement claimed their new technology could transmit data at nearly lightspeed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: c - the scientific symbol for the speed of light
  • degree lower: supersonic - faster than the speed of sound
