tame

adjective
very docile or submissive
7.6 2.9
Pronunciation: /teɪm/ (AmE), /teɪm/ (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) very gentle, docile, or submissive; showing a lack of assertiveness or spirit.

Examples

A tame person
Despite his qualifications, he was surprisingly tame during the interview, never challenging the panel.
Her tame response to the criticism made her colleagues think she lacked confidence.
The students were tame and obedient, quietly following every instruction without question.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality
  • synonym: meek - quiet, gentle, and easily imposed on; submissive
  • synonym: submissive - ready to conform to authority or will of others; meek
