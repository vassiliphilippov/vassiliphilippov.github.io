Black Vernacular

noun
variety of English spoken by African Americans
9.6 7.8
Pronunciation: /blæk vər.ˈnæk.jə.lər/ (AmE), /blæk və.ˈnæk.jə.lə/ /; / /blæk və.ˈnæk.jʊ.lə/ (BrE)
A nonstandard variety of American English spoken by many African Americans in the United States, often characterized by distinctive pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary.

Examples

Black Vernacular English
He grew up speaking Black Vernacular at home and learned Standard English at school.

The film realistically depicts conversations in Black Vernacular among its characters.
