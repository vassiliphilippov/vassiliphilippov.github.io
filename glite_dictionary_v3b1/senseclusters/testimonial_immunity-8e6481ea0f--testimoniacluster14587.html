testimonial immunity

noun
legal exemption from self-incrimination by testimony
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˌtɛstɪˈməʊniəl ɪˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
legal protection that prevents a witness's compelled testimony or any evidence derived from it from being used against them in prosecution, thereby removing the risk of self-incrimination

Examples

Testimonial immunity protection
Prosecutors offered testimonial immunity to ensure the key witness would testify truthfully.
The witness was granted testimonial immunity in exchange for his cooperation with the investigation.
Without testimonial immunity, she would have risked incriminating herself on the stand.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: transactional immunity - broader immunity protecting a witness from prosecution for offenses related to their testimony
  • synonym: use immunity - a type of immunity where only the witness's testimony and evidence derived from it cannot be used against them, but does not prevent prosecution by other evidence
