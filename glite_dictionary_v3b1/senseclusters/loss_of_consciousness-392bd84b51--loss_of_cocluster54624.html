loss of consciousness

noun
temporary unresponsiveness; being unconscious
2.9
Pronunciation: / lɔs əv ˈkɑnʃəsnəs / (AmE), / lɒs əv ˈkɒnʃəsnəs / (BrE)
a state in which a person becomes unaware of and unresponsive to their surroundings, temporarily losing awareness and the ability to respond.

Examples

A loss of consciousness episode
After the head injury, he suffered a brief loss of consciousness but quickly recovered.
Dehydration and heat can cause a sudden loss of consciousness during strenuous exercise.
The patient experienced a loss of consciousness after the accident and was taken to the emergency room.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: coma - a prolonged state of deep unconsciousness
  • degree lower: fainting - temporary loss of consciousness usually due to a lack of blood flow to the brain
  • synonym: unconsciousness - the state of being unconscious
