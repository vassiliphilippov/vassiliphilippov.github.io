gay liberation movement

noun
movement for homosexual rights and equality
2.5
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪ lɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ɡeɪ lɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a social and political movement advocating for the rights and equality of homosexual people, particularly against legal, social, and economic oppression

Examples

The gay liberation movement
Many activists contributed to the successes of the gay liberation movement over several decades.
The gay liberation movement gained momentum in the 1970s after the Stonewall riots.
The gay liberation movement played a crucial role in changing public attitudes toward homosexuality.
