Aedes aegypti

noun
disease-transmitting mosquito species
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.diːz iːˈdʒɪp.taɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː.diːz iːˈdʒɪp.tiː / (BrE)
a species of mosquito known for transmitting yellow fever, dengue fever, and other viral diseases

Examples

Aedes aegypti mosquito species
Aedes aegypti is primarily responsible for spreading yellow fever and dengue fever in many tropical regions.
Efforts to control Aedes aegypti populations can significantly reduce the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses.
Scientists tracked the spread of Aedes aegypti to understand patterns of disease outbreaks.
