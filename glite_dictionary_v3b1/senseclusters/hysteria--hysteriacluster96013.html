hysteria

noun
neurotic disorder with emotional and motor disturbances
7.5 6.4
Pronunciation: /hɪ.ˈstɪ.r.ɪə/ /; / /hɪ.ˈste.r.iː.ə/ (AmE), /hɪ.ˈstɪ.r.ɪə/ /; / /hɪ.ˈste.r.iː.ə/ (BrE)
A neurotic disorder marked by violent emotional outbreaks and disturbances of sensory and motor functions, historically used in psychiatry.

Examples

Diagnosed with hysteria
In the nineteenth century, women with unexplained physical symptoms were often labeled as suffering from hysteria.
Modern psychiatry has largely replaced the diagnosis of hysteria with more precise medical terms.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hysteria after her repeated emotional collapses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conversion disorder - psychiatric disorder with neurological symptoms not explained by medical conditions
