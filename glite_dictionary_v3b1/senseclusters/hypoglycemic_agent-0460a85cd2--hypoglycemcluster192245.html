hypoglycemic agent

noun
drug lowering blood sugar
7.2
Pronunciation: / haɪpoʊɡlaɪˈsiːmɪk ˈeɪdʒənt /; / haɪ.poʊ.ɡlaɪˈsiː.mɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / hʌɪpəʊɡlʌɪˈsiːmɪk ˈeɪdʒənt /; / haɪ.pəʊ.ɡlaɪˈsiː.mɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
a drug or substance that lowers the level of glucose in the blood, used to treat diabetes mellitus

Examples

A hypoglycemic agent drug
Insulin is a well-known hypoglycemic agent that lowers blood sugar levels quickly.
Some patients may require more than one hypoglycemic agent for effective glucose control.
The doctor prescribed a hypoglycemic agent to help manage her type 2 diabetes.
Research continues into developing safer hypoglycaemic agent options with fewer side effects.
Some patients may require more than one hypoglycaemic agent to manage their diabetes effectively.
The doctor prescribed a new hypoglycaemic agent to help control her blood sugar levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperglycemic agent - a substance that increases blood glucose
