brown trout

noun
a species of trout with brown coloring and spots
Pronunciation: / braʊn traʊt / (AmE), / braʊn traʊt / (BrE)
a European species of trout (Salmo trutta) with brownish coloring and dark spots, commonly found in rivers and widely introduced elsewhere, including North America

Examples

The brown trout fish
Scientists monitor the population of brown trout in local rivers to assess water quality.
She caught a large brown trout during her fishing trip to Scotland.
The brown trout is prized by anglers for its elusive behavior and fighting spirit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rainbow trout - a species of trout known for its colorful, rainbow-like markings
