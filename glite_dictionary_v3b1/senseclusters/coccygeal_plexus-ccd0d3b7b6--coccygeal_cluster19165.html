coccygeal plexus

noun
network of nerves near the coccyx
Pronunciation: / kɑkˈsɪdʒiəl ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / kɒkˈsɪdʒiəl ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a small network of nerves formed by the fifth sacral and coccygeal spinal nerves

Examples

The coccygeal plexus nerves
Injuries near the tailbone may sometimes affect the coccygeal plexus and cause pain.
Surgeons must avoid damaging the coccygeal plexus during certain lower back procedures.
The coccygeal plexus is important for innervation of the area around the coccyx.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sacral plexus - a network of nerves in the pelvic region formed by sacral nerves
