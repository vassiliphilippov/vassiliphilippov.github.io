Civil List

noun
British royal family expenses allowance
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪvəl lɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.əl lɪst / (BrE)
a sum of money allocated by the British Parliament each year to cover the official expenses of the British royal family

Examples

The Civil List money
During Queen Victoria's reign, the Civil List was a subject of frequent parliamentary debate.
Reforms in the 21st century led to the replacement of the Civil List by the Sovereign Grant.
The Civil List historically funded not only the royal household but also certain public services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: royal family - the immediate family of a reigning monarch
