preamplifier

noun
signal-boosting device
5.6
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈæm.plɪ.faɪ.ər/ /; / /prɪ.ˈæm.plɪ.faɪ.ər/ (AmE), /priː.ˈæm.plɪ.faɪ.ə/ /; / /prɪ.ˈæm.plɪ.faɪ.ə/ (BrE)
an electronic device that increases weak electrical signals before they are sent to a main amplifier

Examples

A preamplifier device
The microphone's weak signal is first sent to a preamplifier for boosting.
Without a preamplifier, the audio quality from the guitar would be too low.
Many home audio systems include a preamplifier to improve sound clarity.
The audio engineer connected the microphone to a preamplifier to improve the sound quality.
Without a preamplifier, the signal from the guitar would be too weak for the main system.
Many home theater systems include a preamplifier to handle multiple audio sources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amplifier - a device that increases the strength of electrical signals
  • antonym: attenuator - a device that reduces the strength of electrical signals
