preconception

noun
an opinion formed before sufficient evidence
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
an opinion or idea formed before having enough evidence or experience, often leading to bias

Examples

A preconception about people
His preconception of the country changed after he visited.
It's important to challenge your preconceptions when learning something new.
She entered the meeting with no preconceptions about the participants.
He approached the case without any preconception, striving to remain impartial.
Her preconception about the new employee was quickly proven wrong after their first project together.
It's important to challenge your own preconceptions when learning about different cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - a tendency to prefer one thing over another, often unfairly
  • synonym: prejudice - an unreasonable dislike or preference formed without enough reason
