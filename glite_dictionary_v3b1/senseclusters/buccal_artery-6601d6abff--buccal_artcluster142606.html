buccal artery

noun
artery supplying blood to the cheek area
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌk.əl ˈɑːr.t̬əɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.əl ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the maxillary artery to supply blood to the buccinator muscle and the cheek

Examples

The buccal artery branch
Damage to the buccal artery can lead to significant bleeding in the oral cavity.
During the dissection, the surgeon carefully identified the buccal artery.
The buccal artery supplies oxygenated blood to the tissues of the cheek.
