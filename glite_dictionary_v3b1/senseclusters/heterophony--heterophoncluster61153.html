heterophony

noun
musical texture variation
9.9
Pronunciation: /he.tə.rə.ˈfoʊ.ni/ (AmE), /he.tə.rə.ˈfəʊ.ni/ (BrE)
a musical texture in which two or more performers play different variations of the same melody at the same time

Examples

Heterophony in music
Many traditional ensembles use heterophony to create a rich and lively sound.
In some Asian folk music, heterophony is a common feature among group performers.
The choir demonstrated heterophony by singing the same melody with slight individual variations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monophony - a musical texture with a single melodic line
  • synonym: polyphony - a musical texture with two or more independent melodic lines
