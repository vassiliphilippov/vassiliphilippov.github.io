serranid

noun
fish of the Serranidae family
Pronunciation: / səˈrænɪd / (AmE), / səˈrænɪd / (BrE)
a fish of the family Serranidae, which includes groupers and sea basses, found mainly in warm coastal waters and valued as food and game fish

Examples

A serranid fish
Many anglers hope to catch a serranid because of its size and fighting ability.
The serranid population in the reef has declined due to overfishing.
The colorful patterns of the serranid make it popular among marine biologists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grouper - a large sea fish of the Serranidae family
  • synonym: sea bass - a marine fish related to groupers and serranids
