eerie

adjective
strange and frightening; supernatural
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɪri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
strange and frightening, making you feel uneasy or as if something supernatural is present

Examples

An eerie silence
An eerie silence filled the abandoned house after midnight.
The wind made an eerie noise as it whistled through the empty streets.
There was an eerie light coming from the forest at dusk.
An eerie light flickered in the fog across the empty street.
He described the experience as both beautiful and eerie at the same time.
The abandoned house had an eerie silence that made everyone uneasy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comforting - making you feel less worried or more at ease
  • synonym: creepy - causing an unpleasant feeling of fear or unease
  • antonym: ordinary - not unusual or special; normal
  • synonym: spooky - strangely frightening; like or suggesting ghosts
