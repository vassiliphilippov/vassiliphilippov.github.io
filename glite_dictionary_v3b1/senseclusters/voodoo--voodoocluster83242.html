voodoo

noun
Caribbean religious practice with magic and spirits
7.9 1.20/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàvuÀêduÀê /; / ÀàvuÀådu / (AmE), / ÀàvuÀêduÀê /; / ÀàvuÀê.duÀê / (BrE)
a religion of West African origin, especially practiced in Haiti and the Caribbean, involving rituals, spirits, and belief in magic

Examples

Haitian voodoo religion
Many people travel to Haiti to learn more about voodoo and its rituals.
Some believe that voodoo ceremonies can heal illnesses or cast spells.
The film portrayed voodoo in a sensationalized and inaccurate way.
Many people in Haiti practice voodoo as a central part of their cultural and spiritual life.
She studied the rituals of voodoo for her anthropology research project.
Some Westerners misunderstand voodoo, confusing its beliefs and traditions with stereotypes about witchcraft.

Synonyms

  • vodoun
    9.9 0.09/m
    vodoun is the Haitian name for the religion of spirits and rituals, while voodoo is the common English term.
  • voodooism
    9.9 0.00/m
    voodooism refers to a mix of West African beliefs and Catholic elements in the Caribbean, while voodoo names the religion more broadly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vodou - alternate spelling of voodoo, especially in Haitian context
  • synonym: witchcraft - practice of magical skills, spells, and abilities
