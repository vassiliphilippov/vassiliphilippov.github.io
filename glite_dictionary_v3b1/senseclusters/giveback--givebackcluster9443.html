giveback

noun
employee concession
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪvˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvˌbæk / (BrE)
a reduction in pay, benefits, or other concessions agreed to by employees, usually during negotiations with employers

Examples

Employee giveback
The workers agreed to a giveback in order to prevent further layoffs at the factory.
During the contract talks, management requested a giveback on health insurance benefits.
Union leaders explained the reasons for the giveback to their members at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefit - an advantage or payment provided to employees
  • synonym: concession - something given up or allowed, especially during negotiations
