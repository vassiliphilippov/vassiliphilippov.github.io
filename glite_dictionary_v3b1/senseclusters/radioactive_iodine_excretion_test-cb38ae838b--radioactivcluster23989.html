radioactive iodine excretion test

noun
test measuring excretion of radioactive iodine
5.3
Pronunciation: /reɪ.dɪ.ə.ˈæk.tɪv ˈaɪ.ə.daɪn ɪk.ˈskriː.ʃən test/ (AmE), /reɪ.dɪ.ə.ˈæk.tɪv ˈaɪ.ə.daɪn ɪk.ˈskriː.ʃən test/ (BrE)
a medical test that measures the amount of radioactive iodine excreted in the urine, used especially to assess thyroid function

Examples

Radioactive iodine excretion test procedure
Before taking the radioactive iodine excretion test, you may need to avoid certain medications.
The radioactive iodine excretion test is often used to help evaluate thyroid disorders.
Your doctor may order a radioactive iodine excretion test to check how your body processes iodine.
