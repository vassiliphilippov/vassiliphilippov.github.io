Examples

A plague of problems
Her little brother could be a plague when she was trying to do her homework.
The constant phone calls became such a plague that he stopped answering his phone.
The mosquitoes in the backyard were a real plague during the summer evenings.
After years of war, the country was struck by a plague of famine and disease.
Historians debate whether the fall of the city was due to a plague or simply bad governance.
The drought was such a severe plague that people believed it was divine punishment.
For years, unemployment was a plague on the small rural community.
The rise in crime has become plague for the neighborhood.
Traffic congestion is a daily plague in the city center.

Synonyms

  • scourge
    8.2 1.61/m
    a scourge causes great suffering over a wide area and sounds formal, while a plague can mean any serious problem or nuisance.
  • malady
    7.6 0.60/m
    a malady is a harmful condition often used in a figurative, medical sense, while a plague means a large persistent problem or annoying person.
