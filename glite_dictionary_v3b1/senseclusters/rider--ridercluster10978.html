rider

noun
additional clause to a legislative bill
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈraɪdə / (BrE)
an additional clause that is attached to a legislative bill, often having little connection with the bill's main topic

Examples

A bill rider
Controversy arose when a last-minute rider was introduced to the legislation.
Lawmakers often use a rider to add unrelated measures to an otherwise popular bill.
The senator attached a rider to the education bill to secure funding for her state.
