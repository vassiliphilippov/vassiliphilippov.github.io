joey

noun
baby marsupial
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒoʊi / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊi / (BrE)
a baby or young kangaroo or other marsupial, especially one that lives in its mother's pouch after birth

Examples

A joey kangaroo
The tiny joey clung tightly to its mother's fur as she hopped across the field.
At the wildlife sanctuary, visitors watched a joey take its first steps outside the pouch.
A rescued joey was bottle-fed by volunteers until it was strong enough to survive on its own.
The tiny joey peeked out of its mother's pouch for the first time.
At the wildlife sanctuary, we saw a rescued joey being bottle-fed by a caretaker.
A joey usually stays in its mother's pouch for several months before venturing out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - a fully grown animal or person
  • synonym: kangaroo - a large Australian marsupial with strong hind legs
  • synonym: pouch - a fold of skin on the belly of some female marsupials where young are carried
