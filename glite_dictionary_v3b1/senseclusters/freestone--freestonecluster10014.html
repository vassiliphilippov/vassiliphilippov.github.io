freestone

noun
fruit with pit that is easily separated from flesh
8.5 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfriː.stoʊ/ (AmE), /ˈfriː.stəʊ/ (BrE)
A fruit, especially a peach, whose flesh does not stick to the pit and can be easily separated.

Examples

A freestone peach
Many gardeners prefer to grow freestone varieties for their convenience in cooking and eating.
The freestone peaches are ideal for making pies because the flesh comes off the pit easily.
You can slice a freestone peach quickly without having to work around the pit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clingstone - fruit whose flesh adheres tightly to the pit
