ethicism

noun
belief in the primacy of ethics
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine that ethical principles and values are valid and important, often emphasizing the central role of ethics in personal or social conduct

Examples

Strict ethicism view
Critics argued that his strict ethicism left little room for pragmatism.
Her ethicism guided her decisions throughout her career.
The debate centered on whether ethicism should influence public policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moralism - the practice or doctrine of emphasizing morality
