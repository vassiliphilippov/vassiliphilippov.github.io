sonant

adjective
voiced speech sound
9.5 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.nənt/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.nənt/ (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords; voiced.

Examples

Sonant consonant
Linguists use the term sonant to describe sounds like 'b', 'd', and 'g'.
The letter 'z' is a sonant consonant because it is pronounced with vocal cord vibration.
Unlike the voiceless 's', the 'z' is a sonant because the vocal cords vibrate.

  • antonym: voiceless - (of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords
