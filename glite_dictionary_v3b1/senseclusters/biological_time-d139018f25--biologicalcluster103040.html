biological time

noun
timing of internal biological processes
3.1 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl taɪm / (BrE)
the timing or progression of internal biological processes or rhythms in living organisms, such as sleep cycles or cell division

Examples

Biological time cycles
Researchers measure biological time in fruit flies to study the effects of light on sleep patterns.
Some plants adjust their blooming according to seasonal changes in biological time.
Your biological time can be disrupted by crossing multiple time zones quickly, leading to jet lag.

Related Terms
