wetland

noun
area of land saturated with water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛtˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈwɛtˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of land where the soil is permanently or seasonally saturated with water, forming a distinct ecosystem with specific plant and animal species

Examples

A wetland area
Many migratory birds stop to rest in the wetland every spring.
The government has introduced measures to protect the remaining wetlands from development.
Wetlands act as natural water filters and provide important habitats for wildlife.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land with little rainfall
  • synonym: marsh - an area of low-lying, wet land with grassy vegetation
  • synonym: swamp - a type of wetland with trees and shrubs
