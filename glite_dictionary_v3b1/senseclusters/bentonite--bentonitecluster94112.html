bentonite

noun
absorbent clay formed from volcanic ash
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛn.təˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛntənaɪt / (BrE)
a soft, absorbent type of clay composed mainly of montmorillonite, formed from the weathering of volcanic ash

Examples

Soft bentonite clay
After the volcanic eruption, layers of bentonite were discovered in the rock strata.
Cosmetic products sometimes contain bentonite for its absorbent qualities.
The engineer used bentonite to seal the foundations and prevent water leakage.
