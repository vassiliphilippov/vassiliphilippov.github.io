foster

verb
bring up a child not one's own
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːstər / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə / (BrE)
to bring up or care for a child who is not one's own by birth, especially by formal arrangement

Examples

To foster a child
After her parents died, her aunt decided to foster her until she finished school.
Many families in the community choose to foster children who need a safe home.
They have fostered three siblings for over a year now.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adopt - to legally take another's child and bring it up as one's own
