dzo

noun
yak-cow hybrid
9.9
Pronunciation: / zoʊ / (AmE), / zəʊ / (BrE)
a hybrid animal produced by crossing a yak with a domestic cow, valued for its strength and hardiness

Examples

Mountain dzo
The dzo is commonly used as a pack animal in the high mountains of Tibet.
Farmers prefer the dzo because it is stronger and more productive than either parent species.
A dzo can survive in harsh climates where ordinary cattle would struggle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cattle - domesticated bovine animals kept for milk or meat
  • synonym: yak - a large, long-haired domesticated bovid native to the Himalayas
