crash

verb
suffer a sudden severe downturn
Pronunciation: / kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
(of prices, values, or systems) to undergo a sudden and severe decline or failure, especially financially.

Examples

Prices crash suddenly
After years of steady growth, the housing market suddenly crashed, causing widespread panic.
Investors lost millions when share prices crashed overnight in the global recession.
Many fear that if consumer confidence doesn't return, the economy will crash again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or fail suddenly, especially financially or structurally
  • antonym: recover - to return to a normal state after a decline
