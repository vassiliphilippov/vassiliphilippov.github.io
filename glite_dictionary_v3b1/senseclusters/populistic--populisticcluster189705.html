populistic

adjective
relating to populism
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːpjəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to populism or populist ideas, especially by appealing to ordinary people against elites, often using simple or emotional arguments

Examples

A populistic approach
The party's populistic approach focused on the needs of everyday citizens.
Critics argued that his populistic promises were unrealistic and oversimplified complex issues.
Many leaders use populistic language to gain support from large groups of voters.
The candidate's populistic promises attracted many voters who felt ignored by traditional politicians.
Critics accused the leader of using populistic language to stir up public anger.
The debate became populistic when speakers focused on emotions instead of facts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demagogic - using emotional appeals and prejudices to gain power
  • antonym: elitist - believing that a society or system should be led by an elite
  • synonym: populist - relating to or supporting the rights and power of ordinary people
  • antonym: rational - based on reason or logic rather than emotions
