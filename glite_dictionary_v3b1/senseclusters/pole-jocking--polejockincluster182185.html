pole-jocking

noun
seeking attention by excessive behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊl ˌdʒɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊl ˌdʒɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of showing off or competing for attention through excessive or inappropriate behavior

Examples

pole-jocking behavior
His pole-jocking during the televised interview made everyone uncomfortable.
Rather than collaborate, she resorted to pole-jocking to get noticed at the conference.
Some politicians are accused of pole-jocking instead of focusing on real issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grandstanding - acting in an ostentatious way to attract attention
  • antonym: humility - a lack of pride or pretension; modest behavior
  • synonym: showboating - showing off to attract attention
