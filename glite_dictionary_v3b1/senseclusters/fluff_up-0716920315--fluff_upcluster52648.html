fluff up

verb
make fuller or airier by shaking
4.0 2.8
Pronunciation: / flʌf ʌp / (AmE), / flʌf ʌp / (BrE)
to make something fuller, lighter, or airier by shaking or separating it, especially pillows, cushions, or hair

Examples

To fluff up pillows
After washing, remember to fluff up the towels so they stay soft.
Before the guests arrived, he fluffed up the cushions on the sofa.
She likes to fluff up the pillows each morning to keep them fresh.
