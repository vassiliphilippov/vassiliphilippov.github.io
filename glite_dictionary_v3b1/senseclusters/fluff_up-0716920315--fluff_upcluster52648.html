fluff up

verb
make fuller or airier by shaking
3.3 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / flÊŒf ÊŒp / (AmE), / flÊŒf ÊŒp / (BrE)
to make something fuller, lighter, or airier by shaking or separating it, especially pillows, cushions, or hair

Examples

To fluff up pillows
After washing, remember to fluff up the towels so they stay soft.
Before the guests arrived, he fluffed up the cushions on the sofa.
She likes to fluff up the pillows each morning to keep them fresh.

Synonyms

  • fluff
    4.1 1.29/m
    fluff makes hair, fur, or fabric stand out and fuller by brushing or shaking, while fluff up means making items airier and puffed.
  • plump up
    4.5 0.17/m
    plump up makes cushions or pillows rounder and fuller by patting or fluffing, while fluff up focuses on light, airy puffing.
  • shake up
    3.5 0.24/m
    shake up uses a quick shaking action to loosen or fluff material, while fluff up can include shaking or separating by hand.
  • tease
    6.8 1.74/m
    tease separates and raises hair fibers by combing or backcombing, while fluff up is a gentler action for pillows or hair.
