linker

noun
molecular connector
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkə / (BrE)
a short chemical group or sequence used to join two molecules or parts of a molecule, often providing spacing or flexibility

Examples

Chemical linker group
The scientists used a flexible linker to connect the two protein domains in their experiment.
Adding a short peptide linker can help maintain the function of both fused proteins.
A DNA linker was inserted to join the two genetic sequences for better expression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bridge - a structure or group that connects two parts in chemistry
  • synonym: spacer - a segment that separates or connects parts of a molecule
