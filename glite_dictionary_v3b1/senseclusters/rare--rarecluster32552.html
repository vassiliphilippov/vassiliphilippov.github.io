rare

adjective
not widely distributed or uncommon
Pronunciation: / rɛr / (AmE), / reə / (BrE)
not common or not happening often; uncommon or scarce

Examples

A rare animal
In the vast desert, rare patches of green were a welcome sight.
The rare herbs she collected grew only on these remote cliffs.
This species of bird is particularly rare in this region.
First editions of that novel are considered rare and highly collectible.
Genuine mistakes of this kind are rare in professional settings.
She used a rare word in her speech that few people understood.
A total solar eclipse is a rare phenomenon that fascinates scientists.
It is rare to find a painting by that artist outside of museums.
Such natural disasters are considered rare in this region.
