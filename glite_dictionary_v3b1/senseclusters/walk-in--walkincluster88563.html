walk-in

noun
a voluntary defector or informant
3.4 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔkˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌɪn / (BrE)
a person, especially an intelligence operative, who voluntarily approaches another country or organization to offer information or seek political asylum without prior arrangement.

Examples

A spy walk-in
A walk-in provided crucial intelligence to the agency during the Cold War.
Not every walk-in turns out to be a genuine source of information.
The embassy staff were unsure how to handle the unexpected walk-in seeking asylum.
