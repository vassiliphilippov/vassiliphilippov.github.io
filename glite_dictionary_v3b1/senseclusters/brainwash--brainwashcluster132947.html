to make someone adopt very different beliefs by using systematic and often forceful pressure or manipulation

Examples

To brainwash someone
It can be difficult to brainwash someone with strong beliefs.
She worried that her son would be brainwashed by extremist propaganda online.
The cult attempted to brainwash its members into abandoning their families.
He feared that someone would attempt to brainwash the children while they were abroad.
Media outlets have the power to brainwash the public with constant misinformation.
The cult tried to brainwash new members into total obedience.
She was afraid her son would be brainwashed by extremist groups online.
The cult leaders tried to brainwash new members into abandoning their families.
The government used propaganda to brainwash the public during the war.

Synonyms

  • indoctrinate
    8.1 2.23/m
    indoctrinate teaches people to accept beliefs without question, while brainwash uses strong pressure and manipulation to change beliefs completely.
  • reprogram
    5.6 0.22/m
    reprogram uses training or conditioning to change attitudes and habits, while brainwash implies forceful, manipulative pressure to alter core beliefs.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: coerce - to force someone to do something against their will
  • antonym: deprogram - to free someone from brainwashing or forced indoctrination
  • synonym: indoctrinate - to teach a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • degree lower: persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something through reasoning or argument
