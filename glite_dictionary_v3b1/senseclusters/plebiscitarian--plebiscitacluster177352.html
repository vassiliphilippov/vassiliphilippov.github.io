plebiscitarian

noun
supporter of plebiscites
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌplɛbɪsɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌplɛbɪsɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates deciding political questions by plebiscite or direct popular vote

Examples

A plebiscitarian leader
The new party leader was a well-known plebiscitarian who believed in direct democracy.
As a plebiscitarian, she campaigned for citizens to vote directly on major policy issues.
Critics argued that the plebiscitarian approach could oversimplify complex political decisions.
As a committed plebiscitarian, she believed every major law should be decided by popular vote.
The new party attracted many plebiscitarians who wanted more direct democracy.
Critics argued that a plebiscitarian approach could undermine representative institutions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarian - a person who favors strict obedience to authority
  • synonym: referendary - a person who supports or organizes referendums
  • synonym: referendist - a person who supports the use of referendums
