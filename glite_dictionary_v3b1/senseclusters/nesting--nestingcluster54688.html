nesting

noun
hierarchical arrangement
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement of elements placed within similar elements to form a hierarchy, especially in computing or programming

Examples

Nesting of loops
Good code style recommends minimizing nesting to improve readability and maintainability.
The document's nesting of sections and subsections made its structure clear.
Deep nesting in programming can make it harder to debug and understand the logic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embedding - the process of placing one element inside another
  • antonym: flattening - the process of reducing or removing hierarchical structure
