ring-barked

adjective
bark removed in ring
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪŋˌbɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋˌbɑːkt / (BrE)
having had a ring of bark removed from around the trunk or branch, usually causing harm to the tree

Examples

A ring-barked tree
The ring-barked tree showed signs of dying within a few months.
Farmers sometimes find ring-barked branches after animals damage the orchard.
A ring-barked trunk cannot transport water and nutrients effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: girdled - having a strip of bark removed in a ring around a tree
