pretor

noun
Roman magistrate; official in ancient Rome
5.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpriː.tɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈpriː.tɔː / (BrE)
an annually elected magistrate of the ancient Roman Republic, responsible for administering justice and other official duties

Examples

The pretor of Rome
A newly elected pretor would oversee legal cases for one year.
In ancient Rome, the pretor ranked just below the consul in authority.
The pretor played an essential role in maintaining order in the Roman Republic.
