Christian era

noun
period from Christ's birth (AD/CE)
3.5 2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstʃən ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃən ˈɪərə / (BrE)
The period of history dating from the birth of Jesus Christ, traditionally used as the basis for the calendar era called Anno Domini (AD) or Common Era (CE).

Examples

The Christian era dates
Many historical events in Europe are dated according to the Christian era.
Scholars debate whether the Christian era accurately marks the actual year of Jesus's birth.
The shift from the Jewish calendar to the Christian era calendar had a significant impact on historical chronology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Before Christ - years before the traditional date of Christ's birth, abbreviated as BC
  • synonym: Common Era - period of history beginning with the year 1 CE, equivalent to Anno Domini
