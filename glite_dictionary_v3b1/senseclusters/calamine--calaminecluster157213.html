calamine

noun
skin-soothing lotion
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæləmɪn / (BrE)
a pink powder or lotion used to soothe skin irritation, itching, or minor rashes

Examples

Calamine lotion
After getting poison ivy, he covered the rash with calamine to relieve the itching.
The pharmacist recommended calamine for her child's chickenpox spots.
Many people keep calamine in their medicine cabinet for minor skin irritations.
