countersign

verb
sign a document after another as confirmation
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsaɪn / (BrE)
to add one’s signature to a document after another person’s signature to confirm its authenticity or validity

Examples

To countersign a document
All orders above this amount must be countersigned by a supervisor.
Please countersign here to verify you have reviewed the agreement.
The manager will countersign the check before it is issued.

  • synonym: sign - to write one's name on a document
