affinity

noun
kinship by marriage or adoption
Pronunciation: / əˈfɪn.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / əˈfɪn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Anthropology) Kinship by marriage or adoption, as distinguished from kinship by blood.

Examples

Affinity by marriage
In some cultures, affinity is recognized with nearly as much ritual as blood kinship.
Legal questions sometimes arise concerning the rights of those connected by affinity rather than consanguinity.
Marriage created an affinity between the two families that lasted generations.
