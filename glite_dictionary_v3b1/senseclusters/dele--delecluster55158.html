dele

noun
proofreading delete mark
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːli / (AmE), / ˈdiːleɪ / (BrE)
a proofreading mark used to show that something should be deleted from a text

Examples

A dele mark
The teacher used a dele to show which word should be removed from the essay.
When proofreading, she marked several sentences with a dele for deletion.
The editor explained the meaning of the dele symbol to the new intern.
The proofreader marked a dele next to the unnecessary sentence in the manuscript.
You should use a dele to show that this word needs to be removed.
After seeing the dele in the margin, the writer deleted the repeated phrase.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delete - to remove or erase something
  • antonym: insert - to add text or content
  • antonym: stet - a proofreading mark meaning 'let it stand'
