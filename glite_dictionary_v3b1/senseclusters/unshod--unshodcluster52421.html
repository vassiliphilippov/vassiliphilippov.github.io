unshod

adjective
barefoot; not wearing shoes (esp. religious)
6.9 8.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃɑd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɒd / (BrE)
not wearing shoes (for people) or not having horseshoes (for animals); barefoot

Examples

Unshod feet
Members of the order took a vow to remain unshod as a sign of humility and poverty.
She noticed the children's unshod feet, covered in dust from playing in the field.
The unshod monks walked silently through the chapel, their feet bare on the cold stone floor.
Ancient warriors often fought unshod, trusting their feet to the rough ground.
The children ran unshod across the cool grass on a summer morning.
The horse was unshod, which made the rocky path difficult to navigate.
