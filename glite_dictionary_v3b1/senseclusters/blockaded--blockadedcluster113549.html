blockaded

adjective
prevented from entry or exit
Pronunciation: / blɑːˈkeɪdɪd / (AmE), / blɒˈkeɪdɪd / (BrE)
prevented from entering or leaving, often by force or obstacles, especially as part of an official or military action

Examples

Streets blockaded by trucks
During the protest, several streets were blockaded by large trucks.
The port remained blockaded for weeks, causing shortages in the city.
The soldiers kept the bridge blockaded to prevent anyone from crossing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barricaded - blocked with a physical barrier to prevent entry or exit
  • antonym: unobstructed - not blocked or impeded; free from barriers
