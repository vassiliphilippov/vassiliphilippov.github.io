Synonyms

  • exclude
    3.0 16/m
    exclude is to deliberately keep someone or something out of a group, while except is a general verb meaning to prevent inclusion.
  • omit
    3.4 3/m
    omit is to leave something out, sometimes by accident, while except implies actively preventing inclusion.
  • leave out
    2.3 15/m
    leave out is an everyday phrase for not including someone or something, while except sounds slightly more formal and deliberate.
  • leave off
    4.4 0.6/m
    leave off is used to stop or omit something in speech or lists, while except usually names what is not included.
