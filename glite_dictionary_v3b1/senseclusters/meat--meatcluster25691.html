meat

noun
edible part inside a seed or nut
6.9 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / mit / (AmE), / miÀêt / (BrE)
the edible inner part of a seed, grain, nut, or fruit stone, especially as opposed to the shell or outer covering

Examples

Nut meat
Pistachios are enjoyed for their delicious green meat.
She carefully cracked the walnut shell to extract the meat inside.
The meat of the almond is rich in healthy fats and protein.

Synonyms

  • kernel
    3.8 3.44/m
    a kernel is the hard, edible center of a seed or grain used for food, while meat names the edible inner part more broadly.
  • nutmeat
    3.7 0.06/m
    nutmeat is the soft edible part found inside a nut's shell and eaten alone, while meat refers to inner edible parts of seeds or fruits.
  • flesh
    1.5 6.23/m
    flesh is the soft, juicy inside of a fruit you bite into, while meat means the inner edible part of seeds, nuts, or stones.
