hemocoel

noun
invertebrate body cavity
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàhiÀêm…ôÀåsiÀêl / (AmE), / ÀàhiÀêm…ôÀåsiÀêl / (BrE)
the main body cavity in many invertebrates, where blood or circulatory fluid flows freely around the organs

Examples

Insect hemocoel
The hemocoel allows nutrients to circulate freely throughout the insect's body.
Unlike vertebrates, spiders have a large hemocoel instead of closed blood vessels.
The hemocoel fills with hemolymph, which bathes the internal organs directly.
Insects have a haemocoel instead of a closed circulatory system.
The haemocoel allows nutrients to reach all parts of the insect's body.
Blood in the haemocoel bathes the internal organs directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coelom - a fluid-filled body cavity found in many animals
