Voltairean

adjective
of Voltaire's ideas
Pronunciation: / voʊlˈtɛəriən / (AmE), / vɒlˈtɛəriən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas or style of Voltaire, especially support for civil liberties, freedom of thought, and religious tolerance

Examples

Voltairean ideals
Her Voltairean approach to debate encouraged open discussion and respect for different opinions.
The book presents a Voltairean critique of authority and promotes freedom of expression.
Many admire his Voltairean wit and commitment to civil liberties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enlightened - showing a rational, modern, and tolerant outlook
  • antonym: intolerant - unwilling to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own
