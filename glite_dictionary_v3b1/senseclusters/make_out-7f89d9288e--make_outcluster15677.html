make out

verb
claim or pretend (often falsely)
6.1 4.8
Pronunciation: / meɪk aʊt / (AmE), / meɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to claim, pretend, or present someone or something as being a particular way, often falsely

Examples

To make out a story
Don't make out that this was all my fault when it clearly wasn't.
He made out that he was sick to avoid going to the meeting.
She tried to make out that she didn't know anything about the missing documents.
He was made out to be the hero of the story, though others disagreed.
She tried to make out that she was an expert, but it was obvious she wasn't.
The article made out the politician to be a villain.
