vainness

noun
excessive pride
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪnnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively proud of one's appearance, abilities, or achievements

Examples

Her vainness about looks
Her vainness was obvious when she talked only about her own successes.
He was criticized for his vainness and constant need for compliments.
The actor's vainness made it difficult for him to accept any criticism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance
  • synonym: vanity - excessive pride in one's appearance or achievements
