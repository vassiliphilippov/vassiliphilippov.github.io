fiber-optic transmission system

noun
system for transmitting data via fiber optics
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbɚ ˈɑptɪk trænzˈmɪʃən ˈsɪstəm /; / ˈfaɪbər ˈɑptɪk trænˈsmɪʃən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə ˈɒptɪk trænzˈmɪʃən ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system that transmits information as light pulses through fiber optic cables, used for high-speed data communication, often over long distances.

Examples

A fiber-optic transmission system
A fiber-optic transmission system offers far greater bandwidth than traditional copper wire networks.
Maintaining a fiber-optic transmission system requires specialized tools and skills.
The hospital upgraded to a fiber-optic transmission system to ensure faster transfer of medical data.
A fibre-optic transmission system is less susceptible to electromagnetic interference than traditional copper cables.
Many telecommunications companies are investing heavily in fibre-optic transmission systems to improve their networks.
The new fibre-optic transmission system revolutionized the speed of internet connections in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: copper-wire transmission system - A communication system using copper wire cables instead of fiber optics.
  • synonym: fiber optics - the use of thin flexible fibers of glass or other transparent solids to transmit light signals
  • synonym: optical fiber - a flexible, transparent fiber made of glass or plastic, used to transmit light
