starch

noun
complex carbohydrate in plants; used as food and in industry
2.4 2/m
Pronunciation: / st…ërt É / (AmE), / st…ëÀêt É / (BrE)
A white, tasteless, odorless complex carbohydrate occurring chiefly in seeds, fruits, tubers, and other plant parts and used widely as a foodstuff, as well as in adhesives, paper, and textiles.

Examples

Potato starch in food
Corn and potatoes are rich sources of starch in many diets around the world.
Excessive consumption of starch can lead to weight gain if not balanced with other nutrients.
The laundry process used liquid starch to make the shirts crisp and wrinkle-free.
