hypostatic

adjective
underlying substance
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpəˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈstætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or based on an underlying substance, reality, or fundamental essence, especially in philosophy or theology

Examples

Hypostatic union
The philosopher explained the hypostatic nature of reality in his lecture.
In theology, the hypostatic union refers to the combination of divine and human natures in Christ.
Her argument focused on the hypostatic basis of moral values rather than their outward expression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: substantial - having substance or being essential
  • antonym: superficial - concerned only with the surface or appearance
