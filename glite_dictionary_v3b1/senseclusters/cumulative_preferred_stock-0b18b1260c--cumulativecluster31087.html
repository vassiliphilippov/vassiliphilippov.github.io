cumulative preferred stock

noun
preferred stock with accumulating unpaid dividends
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːmjəˌleɪtɪv prɪˈfɜrd stɑk / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊlətɪv prɪˈfɜːd stɒk / (BrE)
preferred stock whose unpaid dividends accumulate and must be paid before any dividends to common shareholders

Examples

Cumulative preferred stock dividends
If the company skips dividend payments, these are owed to holders of cumulative preferred stock before common shareholders get any dividends.
Investors appreciate cumulative preferred stock because it ensures missed dividends are eventually paid.
The firm's cumulative preferred stock holders received payment for several previous years when profits improved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preferred stock - stock with priority over common stock in dividends
