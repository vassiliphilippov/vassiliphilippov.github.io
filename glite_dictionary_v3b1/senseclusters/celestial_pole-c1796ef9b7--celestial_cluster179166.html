celestial pole

noun
point where Earth's axis meets celestial sphere
7.3 7.0
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈles.tʃəl poʊl/ /; / /sə.ˈles.tɪ.əl poʊl/ (AmE), /sə.ˈles.tʃəl pəʊl/ /; / /sə.ˈles.tɪ.əl pəʊl/ (BrE)
Either of the two points where the Earth's axis of rotation, projected into space, intersects the celestial sphere, known as the north celestial pole and the south celestial pole.

Examples

The celestial pole
As the Earth rotates, stars appear to revolve around the celestial pole.
Astronomers use the celestial pole as a reference point for mapping the night sky.
The north celestial pole is currently close to the star Polaris.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pole star - the bright star nearest to the north celestial pole, also called Polaris
