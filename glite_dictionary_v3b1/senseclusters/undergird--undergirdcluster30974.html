to support or strengthen something from beneath, or to provide a strong base or foundation (physically or in an abstract way, such as ideas or emotions)

Examples

To undergird a structure
Crews worked tirelessly to undergird the sinking vessel with strong cables.
Honesty and trust undergird every successful relationship.
Large steel beams undergird the foundation of the new building.
Her unshakable faith undergirded her determination to keep going during hard times.
Strong ethical values undergird the mission of the organization.
The Constitution has long undergirded democratic institutions in the country.
Strong ethical principles undergird our company's mission and decisions.
The research findings undergird the proposal for long-term policy changes.
These new reforms are designed to undergird economic stability for future generations.
