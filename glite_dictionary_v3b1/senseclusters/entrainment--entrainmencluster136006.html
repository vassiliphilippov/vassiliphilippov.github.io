entrainment

noun
mixing by fluid flow
8.8
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /en.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ (AmE), /ən.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /en.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ (BrE)
the process by which particles, droplets, or bubbles are captured and carried along by a moving fluid, or one fluid is mixed into another

Examples

Fluid entrainment
The entrainment of dust particles in the wind can affect air quality in urban areas.
Engineers studied the entrainment of water droplets into the air stream of the cooling tower.
Reducing entrainment is important to prevent unwanted substances from entering the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mixing - the process of combining two or more substances
  • antonym: separation - the process of keeping substances apart
