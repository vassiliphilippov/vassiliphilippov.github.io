reinjure

verb
to injure again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪndʒə / (BrE)
to hurt the same part of the body again after it was already injured

Examples

To reinjure a knee
The athlete was afraid he might reinjure his knee if he returned to the game too soon.
Doctors warned her not to reinjure her shoulder by lifting heavy objects.
After months of recovery, he finally played again but took care not to reinjure himself.
After returning to the field too soon, he managed to reinjure his knee during the first game.
Doctors warned her not to reinjure her shoulder by lifting heavy objects.
Wearing proper support can help prevent you from reinjuring the same area.
After returning to the field too soon, he managed to reinjure his ankle during the first game.
Doctors warned her not to reinjure her shoulder by lifting heavy objects.
If you reinjure the same muscle, recovery could take even longer.
