George Sand

proper noun
French novelist and feminist writer
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔrdʒ sænd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ sænd / (BrE)
(1804–1876) The pen name of Amantine Lucile Aurore Dupin, a French writer renowned for her novels, essays, and advocacy of women's rights and independence.

Examples

George Sand writer
In her literary career, George Sand challenged social conventions and promoted women's independence in her works.
Many critics admire the vivid rural settings described by George Sand in her novels.
The correspondence between George Sand and Chopin provides insight into the cultural life of 19th-century France.
