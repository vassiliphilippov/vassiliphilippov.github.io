migrant worker

noun
person who moves for temporary work
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪɡrənt ˈwɝːkər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪɡrənt ˈwɜːkə / (BrE)
a person who moves from place to place to find temporary work, especially in agriculture or manual labor

Examples

Migrant workers in fields
Labor laws have been updated to improve conditions for migrant workers.
Many migrant workers send money back to their families in their home countries.
The local economy relies heavily on migrant workers during the harvest season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanent employee - a worker employed on a long-term basis at one location
  • synonym: seasonal worker - a person employed only during certain seasons, often for agricultural work
