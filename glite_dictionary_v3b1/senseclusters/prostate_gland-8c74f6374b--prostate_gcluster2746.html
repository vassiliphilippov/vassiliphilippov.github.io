prostate gland

noun
male gland producing seminal fluid
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.steɪt ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.steɪt ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
a small, partly muscular gland in males, located at the base of the bladder, which produces a fluid that is a component of semen

Examples

The prostate gland
After surgery, his prostate gland was examined to check for signs of disease.
Prostate cancer develops in the tissues of the prostate gland and is one of the most common cancers in men.
The doctor explained that an enlarged prostate gland can cause difficulty during urination.
