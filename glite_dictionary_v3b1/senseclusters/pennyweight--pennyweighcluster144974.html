pennyweight

noun
unit of weight (24 grains)
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛniˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛniˌweɪt / (BrE)
a unit of weight equal to 24 grains, used especially in measuring precious metals and formerly in apothecary weight

Examples

Three pennyweights silver
He measured out three pennyweights of silver for the assay.
Old recipes sometimes call for ingredients by the pennyweight.
The ring weighs exactly one pennyweight of gold.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: grain - a unit of weight equal to 1/7000th of a pound avoirdupois, the basis of a pennyweight
  • degree higher: ounce - a larger unit of weight (16 pennyweights in a troy ounce)
