ill

noun
illness; bodily disorder (noun)
3.6 1.50/m
Pronunciation: / Éªl / (AmE), / Éªl / (BrE)
An illness, disease, or bodily disorder, often causing discomfort or giving reason to complain.

Examples

A serious ill
Decades of hard living brought on a variety of ills in old age.
Many social ills can be traced back to poverty and inequality.
The patient suffered from a chronic ill that made daily life difficult.

Synonyms

  • illness
    1.1 58/m
    illness is a medical problem with clear symptoms, while ill is a general, everyday word for being unwell.
  • sickness
    1.0 5.38/m
    sickness often means the state of being unwell or nauseous, while ill is the simple everyday term for illness.
  • disease
    2.3 194/m
    disease is a specific, often serious medical condition with clear causes, while ill is a broad, everyday way to say someone is unwell.
  • condition
    3.7 65/m
    condition often names a long-term health problem or ongoing issue, while ill usually refers to a short or general sickness.
  • ailment
    3.4 4.79/m
    ailment describes a minor or annoying health problem people live with, while ill covers any sickness or disease in general.
  • ill health
    3.0 1.00/m
    ill health means a general state of poor or long-term physical condition, while ill refers to a specific short illness.
