catkin

noun
slim drooping flower cluster
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈkat.kɪn / (BrE)
A slim, cylindrical flower cluster, typically with inconspicuous or no petals, often hanging and usually wind-pollinated.

Examples

Drooping catkins
Alder trees are easily recognized by their drooping catkins.
Bees are often attracted to the pollen of catkins from hazel trees.
The willow tree produced long, silver catkins in early spring.
