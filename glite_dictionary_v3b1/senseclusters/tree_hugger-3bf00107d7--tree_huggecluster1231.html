tree hugger

noun
(slang) derogatory term for environmentalist
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtri ˌhʌɡɚ /; / tri ˈhʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈtriː ˌhʌɡə /; / tri ˈhʌɡə / (BrE)
an informal or sometimes derogatory term for an environmentalist or conservationist, especially someone dedicated to protecting trees and nature

Examples

Tree hugger activists
Some people dismiss climate activists as tree huggers, but their concerns are becoming mainstream.
The protesters, labeled tree huggers by critics, chained themselves to the old oaks.
While he used to mock tree huggers, he now volunteers for several environmental organizations.
Despite being called a tree hugger, he continued his fight to save the local forest.
Many tree huggers volunteer for organizations that plant trees in urban areas.
She wore her tree hugger badge with pride at the environmental conference.
