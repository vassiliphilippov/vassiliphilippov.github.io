factorization

noun
decomposition of an expression into factors
1.8 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌfæk.tɚ.ɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌfæktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæk.tər.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌfæktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) the process of breaking down an expression into a product of simpler factors that, when multiplied together, yield the original expression.

Examples

Prime factorization
Prime factorization is often used in cryptography.
The factorization of 12 is 2 × 2 × 3.
Understanding factorization is crucial for solving quadratic equations.
Prime factorisation is used to express a number as a product of prime numbers.
She checked her answer by multiplying the factors from the factorisation back together.
The factorisation of quadratic equations is an important algebraic skill.
