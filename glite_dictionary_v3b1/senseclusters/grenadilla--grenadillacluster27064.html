grenadilla

noun
African hardwood tree
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪlə / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪlə / (BrE)
a small African tree (Dalbergia melanoxylon) valued for its dense, dark wood used in making musical instruments

Examples

African grenadilla tree
The clarinet was crafted from rare grenadilla to produce a rich, warm sound.
Many high-quality woodwind instruments use grenadilla because of its strength and tonal properties.
Conservationists are concerned about the overharvesting of grenadilla in its native African habitats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: African blackwood - the dense, dark wood from Dalbergia melanoxylon
  • synonym: rosewood - a type of hard, dark wood from several tropical trees
