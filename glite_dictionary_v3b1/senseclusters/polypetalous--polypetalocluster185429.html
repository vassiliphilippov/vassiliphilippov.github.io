polypetalous

adjective
having separate petals (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈpɛtələs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpɛtələs / (BrE)
(of a flower) having a corolla composed of many separate and distinct petals.

Examples

Polypetalous flower
Botanists often distinguish between polypetalous and gamopetalous flowers in classification.
The flower’s polypetalous nature makes it easy to count the individual petals.
The majority of roses are polypetalous, with each petal clearly separated from the others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gamopetalous - having petals that are fused or united
