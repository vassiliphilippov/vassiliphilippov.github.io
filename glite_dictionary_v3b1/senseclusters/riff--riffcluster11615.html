riff

verb
to improvise creatively
Pronunciation: / rɪf / (AmE), / rɪf / (BrE)
to improvise or speak in a spontaneous, creative way, especially in music or conversation

Examples

To riff on ideas
The comedian loved to riff on current events during his stand-up routine.
During the jam session, the guitarist began to riff and the band followed his lead.
She can riff on any topic and keep the conversation lively and fun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvise - to create or perform without preparation
  • antonym: rehearse - to practice or prepare in advance
