reestablishment

noun
restoring existence
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˈstæblɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈstæblɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of making something exist or function again after it has stopped or been absent

Examples

The reestablishment of relations
The reestablishment of diplomatic relations marked a new era for the two countries.
After years of neglect, the reestablishment of the park delighted local residents.
The reestablishment of order was necessary after the chaos of the storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolition - the act of formally ending or stopping something
  • synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original state
