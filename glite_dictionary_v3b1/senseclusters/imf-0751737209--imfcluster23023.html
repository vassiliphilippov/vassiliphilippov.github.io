IMF

noun
International Monetary Fund (UN agency)
3.8
Pronunciation: / aɪ ɛm ɛf / (AmE), / aɪ ɛm ɛf / (BrE)
an international financial institution, the International Monetary Fund, established to promote global economic stability by fostering monetary cooperation and stability among member countries

Examples

The IMF organization
Many developing nations have sought advice from the IMF on economic policy reforms.
The IMF provided financial assistance to the country during its economic crisis.
The annual meeting of the IMF attracts finance ministers and central bankers from all over the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: World Bank - another major international financial institution focusing on development and loans
