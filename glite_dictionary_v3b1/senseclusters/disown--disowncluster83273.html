disown

verb
refuse to acknowledge or reject relationship
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈoʊn / (AmE), / dɪsˈəʊn / (BrE)
to refuse to acknowledge, accept, or have any connection with someone or something; to reject as one's own (including legally preventing someone from inheriting)

Examples

To disown a child
After years of disagreements, his father finally disowned him.
She felt so betrayed that she decided to disown her former friend.
The company threatened to disown any product that failed to meet safety standards.
By choosing to disown her grandchildren, she ensured they would receive nothing from her estate.
She decided to disown her nephew in her will after years of estrangement.
The wealthy businessman threatened to disown his son if he continued with his reckless lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disinherit - to prevent deliberately from inheriting, especially by a legal act
  • synonym: repudiate - to refuse to accept or be associated with
