prudential

adjective
characterized by careful judgment
8.0 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / pruÀêÀàd…õn É…ôl / (AmE), / pruÀêÀàd…õn É…ôl / (BrE)
Based on or showing careful judgment, especially in business or financial matters.

Examples

Prudential financial decisions
Her prudential decisions helped the organization survive during difficult times.
Many banks implement prudential regulations to minimize financial risks.
The board adopted a prudential approach to investing the company's assets.

Synonyms

  • prudent
    6.2 7.02/m
    prudent is a general word for wise, careful choices in life, while prudential describes formal business or financial caution.
  • circumspect
    7.9 1.18/m
    circumspect is watchful for risks and moves carefully in many situations, while prudential refers more to formal financial or business judgment.
