inconscient

noun
unconscious mind
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkɑnˈsiːənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkɒnˈsjɑ̃ː / (BrE)
the unconscious part of the mind that contains thoughts, memories, or desires not accessible to conscious awareness

Examples

The inconscient mind
Some psychologists believe the inconscient influences our actions more than we realize.
Dreams are often thought to reveal hidden aspects of the inconscient.
Freud's theories focused on the power of the inconscient in shaping behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conscious - the part of the mind that is aware of thoughts and surroundings
  • synonym: unconscious - the part of the mind not within conscious awareness
