measured

adjective
regular or fixed in musical rhythm
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛʒɚd / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒəd / (BrE)
(of music) having notes of fixed rhythmic value or a regular, steady rhythm.

Examples

Measured rhythm
Her piano piece contrasted measured sections with moments of free, expressive tempo.
The composer preferred a measured approach, ensuring each note followed a strict rhythm.
Traditional marches are typically written in a measured style to maintain a steady pace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unmeasured - not having a regular or fixed rhythm (in music)
