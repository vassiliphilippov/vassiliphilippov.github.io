cunning

adjective
clever and deceitful
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
clever at achieving things, often by tricking or deceiving people; sly or crafty

Examples

Cunning trick
Her cunning plan fooled everyone at the office.
Politicians often need to be cunning to survive in the business.
The thief was so cunning that he left no evidence behind.
Her cunning plan helped the team win the competition against all odds.
It was only through his cunning strategy that the company managed to recover from its losses.
The thief used cunning methods to bypass the house's security system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas
  • antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or skill; lacking dexterity or skill
  • synonym: ingenious - clever, original, and inventive
  • antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment; innocent
  • synonym: sly - clever in concealing one's aims or actions; crafty
  • synonym: wily - skilled at gaining an advantage, especially deceitfully
