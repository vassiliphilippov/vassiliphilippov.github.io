Zbit

noun
unit of information (10^21 bits)
Pronunciation: / ˈziːˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɛdˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,000 exabits or 10^21 bits.

Examples

One Zbit unit
The theoretical limit for the new processor is close to 1 Zbit per second.
Transmitting a Zbit of data would require extremely advanced communication infrastructure.
With the rapid expansion of global data storage, companies are now talking about capacities measured in Zbits.
