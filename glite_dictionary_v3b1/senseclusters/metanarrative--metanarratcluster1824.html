metanarrative

noun
story about stories
9.8
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ /; / /mə.tə.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ (AmE), /me.tə.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ /; / /mə.tə.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ (BrE)
a narrative that comments on, frames, or refers to other narratives or the act of storytelling itself

Examples

Metanarrative about stories
The novel's metanarrative challenges readers to question the nature of storytelling itself.
In the play, the metanarrative is revealed when characters break the fourth wall and address the audience.
Many modern films use a metanarrative to comment on their own structure and themes.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: narrative - a spoken or written account of connected events; a story
