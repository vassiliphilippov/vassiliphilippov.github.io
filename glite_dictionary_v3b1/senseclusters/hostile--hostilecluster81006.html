hostile

showing or feeling strong dislike or opposition; unfriendly or antagonistic (of people, groups, or situations)

Examples

Hostile crowd
He faced a hostile audience that booed throughout his speech.
Her voice grew hostile when she heard the accusations.

The two hostile factions refused to negotiate a peace treaty.

Synonyms

  • antagonistic
    6.8 2.02/m
    antagonistic shows active opposition toward people or ideas, while hostile covers general unfriendly or opposing feelings in people or situations.
  • antipathetic
    8.4 0.07/m
    antipathetic feels a strong personal dislike toward someone, while hostile can mean broader unfriendly or opposing behavior or attitude.
  • inimical
    8.9 0.89/m
    inimical is formal and often means harmful or working against something, while hostile is more simply unfriendly or oppositional.
  • aggressive
    3.7 22/m
    aggressive describes forceful or ready-to-attack behavior, while hostile covers unfriendly feelings that may not be violent or active.
