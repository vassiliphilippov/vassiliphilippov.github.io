capital reserve

noun
company fund for special or unexpected expenses
2.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkæp.ɪ.təl rɪ.ˈzɜːrv/ /; / /ˈkæp.ɪ.təl riː.ˈzɜːrv/ (AmE), /ˈkæp.ɪ.təl rɪ.ˈzɜːv/ (BrE)
an amount of money set aside by a company from profits or capital surpluses, used to cover special or unforeseen expenses and not available for distribution as dividends to shareholders

Examples

A capital reserve fund
Dividends cannot be paid from a capital reserve, according to corporate regulations.
The company allocated some of its profits to a capital reserve to safeguard against future liabilities.
Unexpected lawsuits forced the firm to dip into its capital reserve last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: revenue reserve - a reserve created from company profits available for distribution as dividends
