sealed instrument

noun
formal contract with seal
Pronunciation: / siːld ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (AmE), / siːld ˈɪnstrəmənt / (BrE)
a formal contract signed by the parties and bearing a wax or embossed seal as evidence of authority or authenticity

Examples

A sealed instrument contract
Only a sealed instrument could transfer property in that jurisdiction in the 19th century.
The contract was classified as a sealed instrument and required the official seal to be valid.
They presented the sealed instrument as evidence of their agreement in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deed - a legal document that is signed and delivered, especially one regarding the ownership of property
