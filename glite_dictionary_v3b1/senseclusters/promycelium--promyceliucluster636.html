promycelium

noun
a spore-producing structure in some fungi
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊmaɪˈsiːliəm / (AmE), / prəʊmɪˈsiːliəm / (BrE)
a short-lived, often septate hyphal structure, functioning as the basidium in certain fungi, that produces spores during reproduction

Examples

A promycelium structure
Many rust fungi form a promycelium as part of their reproductive cycle.
The promycelium develops from the germinating teliospore and gives rise to basidiospores.
Under the microscope, the promycelium appeared as a delicate, septate thread.
