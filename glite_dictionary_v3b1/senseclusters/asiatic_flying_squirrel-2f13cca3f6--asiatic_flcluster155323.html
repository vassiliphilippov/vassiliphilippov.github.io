Asiatic flying squirrel

noun
gliding nocturnal rodent of Asia
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪʒiˈætɪk ˈflaɪɪŋ skwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪʒiˈætɪk ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈskwɪr.əl / (BrE)
A nocturnal rodent native to Asia, distinguished by furry membranes between its legs that enable it to glide between trees.

Examples

The Asiatic flying squirrel glides
Researchers spotted a rare Asiatic flying squirrel high in the Himalayan forest canopy.
The Asiatic flying squirrel uses folds of skin to glide effortlessly from tree to tree at night.
Unlike other rodents, the Asiatic flying squirrel can travel long distances through the air.
