desensitization technique

noun
method for reducing anxiety by gradual exposure
6.9 4.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.sen.sɪ.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən tek.ˈniːk/ (AmE), /dɪ.sen.sɪ.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən tek.ˈniːk/ (BrE)
a method used in behavior therapy to reduce anxiety or phobias by gradually exposing a person to the feared object or situation in a controlled and relaxed setting

Examples

A desensitization technique for fear
Many phobias can be effectively treated with a desensitization technique tailored to the individual.
The therapist used a desensitization technique to help the patient overcome her fear of flying.
Through repeated sessions using a desensitization technique, he became less anxious around dogs.
Many phobias can be effectively treated using a desensitisation technique as part of behavior therapy.
She learned to relax during each step of the desensitisation technique, gradually facing her anxiety triggers.
The therapist employed a desensitisation technique to help the patient overcome her fear of flying.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exposure therapy - psychological treatment involving controlled exposure to feared objects or situations
  • degree higher: flooding - behavior therapy technique using intense exposure to anxiety source
  • synonym: systematic desensitisation - structured technique for treating phobias through gradual exposure
  • synonym: systematic desensitization - a specific form of desensitization technique involving gradual exposure paired with relaxation
