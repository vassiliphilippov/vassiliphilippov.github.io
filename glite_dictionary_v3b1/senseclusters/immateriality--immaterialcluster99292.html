immateriality

noun
lack of physical substance
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmæɾəriˈæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌmatɪəriˈalɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not having physical substance or not consisting of matter

Examples

Immateriality of the soul
Many religions emphasize the immateriality of spiritual beings.
Quantum physics challenges our understanding of immateriality and matter.
The philosopher argued at length about the immateriality of the soul.

Related Terms

  • antonym: materiality - the quality of being material or having physical substance
