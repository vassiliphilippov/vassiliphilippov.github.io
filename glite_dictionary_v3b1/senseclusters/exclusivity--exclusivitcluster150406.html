exclusivity

noun
sole rights
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkskluːˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌɛkskluːˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
the state or condition of having sole rights to sell, supply, publish, or represent something, excluding others from participation

Examples

Exclusivity agreement
The company secured exclusivity for the new product in the European market.
Their contract guarantees exclusivity in distributing the designer's latest collection.
The artist valued exclusivity and chose to work with only one gallery.
