West Midland

noun
historical Middle English dialect in western Mercia
Pronunciation: / wɛst ˈmɪdlənd / (AmE), / wɛst ˈmɪdlənd / (BrE)
a dialect of Middle English that was spoken in the western part of the historical English region of Mercia.

Examples

West Midland dialect
Linguists study the West Midland dialect to trace the development of modern English.
Many medieval texts from the region are written in the West Midland variety of Middle English.
The West Midland dialect is notable for preserving certain Old English vowel sounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Middle English - the form of the English language spoken from c.1150 to c.1500
