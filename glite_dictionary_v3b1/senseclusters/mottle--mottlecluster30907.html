mottle

noun
irregular pattern of colored patches
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑtəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒtəl / (BrE)
an irregular arrangement of spots or patches of different colors, often forming a pattern on a surface

Examples

A brown mottle
The bird's eggs were marked with a brown mottle that helped camouflage them.
The marble floors were decorated with a delicate mottle of white and gray.
Through the trees, sunlight created a mottle of light and shadow on the grass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mottling - the process or result of forming mottles; a pattern of spots or blotches
  • antonym: stain - a colored patch or mark that is difficult to remove
