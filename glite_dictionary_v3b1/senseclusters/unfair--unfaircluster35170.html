unfair

adjective
not just or impartial; unjust
1.0 22/m

Not treating people equally, justly, or according to the rules; marked by injustice or bias.

Examples

An unfair decision
It would be unfair to blame her for a mistake that was not hers.
Raising the prices just before the holiday felt unfair to many customers.
They thought the judge's decision was completely unfair.

Synonyms

  • unjust
    4.0 5.67/m
    unjust sounds more formal and refers to moral or legal wrongs, while unfair is everyday and broadly means not equal.
  • discriminatory
    6.0 3.92/m
    discriminatory describes biased acts against a group or protected people, while unfair covers any unequal or wrong treatment.
  • inequitable
    8.0 0.84/m
    inequitable describes systems or choices that give unequal shares, while unfair is a general term for unjust treatment.
  • biased
    4.0 6.25/m
    biased shows a person's or group's unfair preference that favors someone, while unfair describes any action lacking equality or justice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fair - treating people equally and without favoritism or discrimination
  • synonym: unjust - not based on or behaving according to what is morally right and fair
