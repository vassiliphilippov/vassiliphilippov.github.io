knock out

verb
make unconscious by hitting
1.5 3.5
Pronunciation: /nɑːk aʊt/ (AmE), /nɒk aʊt/ (BrE)
to make someone become unconscious or unable to function, especially by hitting, force, illness, or tiredness

Examples

To knock out a boxer
He was accidentally knocked out when the ball hit his head.
The boxer managed to knock out his rival in the third round.
The medicine was so strong that it almost knocked out the patient.
The boxer delivered an uppercut that knocked out his rival in the third round.
The medication was so strong it completely knocked out my headache and made me sleepy.
We should be able to knock out this report before lunch if we all work together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: finish - to bring something to an end; complete
  • synonym: incapacitate - to make someone unable to function or work
  • antonym: revive - to bring someone back to consciousness
  • synonym: stun - to make someone unconscious or unable to think clearly
