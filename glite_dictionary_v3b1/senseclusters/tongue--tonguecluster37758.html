tongue

noun
narrow land jutting into sea or lake
8.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /tʌŋ/ (AmE), /tʌŋ/ (BrE)
a narrow or thin part that sticks out, such as a strip of land, a flame, or the part of a shoe under the laces

Examples

A tongue of land
At the northern end of the bay, a tongue of land separates two coves.
The lighthouse stands at the tip of a narrow tongue extending into the water.
Waves crashed against the rocky tongue jutting from the coastline.
As the building burned, tongues of flame shot out of the windows.
Fiery tongues darted from the campfire, reaching toward the night sky.
Lightning forked above the hills, its tongue illuminating the dark landscape.
He adjusted the tongue of his hiking boots to keep his feet comfortable.
Make sure the tongue is smooth before you tie your laces.
The tongue of my sneaker always slips to the side when I walk.

  • synonym: ribbon - a long narrow strip or band
