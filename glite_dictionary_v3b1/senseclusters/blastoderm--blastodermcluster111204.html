blastoderm

noun
layer of cells in the early embryo
Pronunciation: / ˈblæstəˌdɜrm / (AmE), / ˈblæstəˌdɜːm / (BrE)
the layer of cells forming on the inside surface of the blastula during early embryonic development.

Examples

The blastoderm layer
Researchers observed the development of the blastoderm under the microscope.
The blastoderm gives rise to all the tissues of the embryo.
Within hours after fertilization, a blastoderm forms around the yolk of the egg.
