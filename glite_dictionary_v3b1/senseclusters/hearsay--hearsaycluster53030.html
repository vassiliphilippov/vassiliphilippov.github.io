hearsay

adjective
received indirectly, not first-hand
3.5 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈhiːr.seɪ/ /; / /ˈhɪər.seɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhɪə.seɪ/ (BrE)
(of information or evidence) received from others rather than directly or first-hand.

Examples

Hearsay evidence
Much of the so-called news he shared was just hearsay and unverified.
The judge ruled the hearsay testimony inadmissible in court.
The report was based solely on hearsay evidence, not direct observation.
