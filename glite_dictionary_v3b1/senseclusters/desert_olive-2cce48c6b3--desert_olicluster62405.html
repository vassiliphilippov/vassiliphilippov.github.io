desert olive

noun
shrub native to southwestern US with black fruits
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛzərt ˈɑːlɪv / (AmE), / ˈdezət ˈɒlɪv / (BrE)
A spiny, branching deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States, bearing clusters of small yellow-white flowers before its leaves and later producing attractive black, berry-like fruits.

Examples

The desert olive shrub
Clusters of black berries appear on the desert olive each summer, attracting birds.
Gardeners appreciate the hardy nature of the desert olive despite its unassuming flowers.
The desert olive thrives in arid landscapes across the southwestern United States.
