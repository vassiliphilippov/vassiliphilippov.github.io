monoamine oxidase inhibitor

noun
antidepressant drug inhibiting monoamine oxidase
8.1 5.5
Pronunciation: /moʊ.nə.ˈeɪ.miːn ˈɑːk.sɪ.deɪs ɪn.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tər/ (AmE), /mɒn.ə.ˈeɪ.miːn ˈɒk.sɪ.deɪs ɪn.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a drug or substance that blocks the activity of monoamine oxidase, often used to treat depression by increasing levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain

Examples

A monoamine oxidase inhibitor drug
Doctors sometimes suggest switching to a monoamine oxidase inhibitor when other antidepressants are ineffective.
Taking a monoamine oxidase inhibitor requires avoiding certain foods to prevent side effects.
The patient was prescribed a monoamine oxidase inhibitor to help manage his depression.
