clause

noun
part of a sentence with subject and predicate
2.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /klɔːz/ (AmE), /klɔːz/ (BrE)
A group of words containing a subject and a predicate, forming part of a sentence or constituting a whole simple sentence.

Examples

A relative clause
A relative clause often begins with a word like 'who' or 'which'.
Complex sentences usually contain more than one clause.
In the sentence, 'When it rains, we stay inside,' the first clause is dependent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phrase - a group of words without a subject-predicate structure
