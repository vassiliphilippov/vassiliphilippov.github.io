enamor

verb
to inspire love or admiration
9.9 5.6
Pronunciation: /e.ˈnæm.ər/ /; / /ɪ.ˈnæm.ər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈnæ.mə/ /; / /e.ˈnæ.mə/ (BrE)
to cause someone to feel love, admiration, or fascination for someone or something

Examples

To enamor someone deeply
He was so enamored with the city that he decided to move there permanently.
The film's unique style enamored critics and audiences alike.
The young musician's talent quickly enamored everyone who heard her play.
She was quickly enamoured of the bustling city life.
The actor's performance enamoured critics and fans alike.
The charming storyteller enamoured the entire audience with his tales.
