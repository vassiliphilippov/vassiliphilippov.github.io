misquotation

noun
incorrect quotation
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪs.kwoʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.kwəʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
An incorrect or inaccurate quotation; the act or result of quoting someone or something incorrectly.

Examples

A misquotation error
A single misquotation can significantly change the meaning of a statement.
He apologized for the misquotation and corrected the error in the next edition.
The article contained a glaring misquotation from the interview.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quotation - a group of words taken from a text or speech and repeated by someone other than the original author or speaker
