dotage

noun
decline of mental faculties in old age
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊ.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period of life in which a person is old and often exhibits a decline in mental faculties, sometimes accompanied by foolish or excessive fondness

Examples

In old dotage
Despite his advanced dotage, the old professor's wit was as sharp as ever.
He fell in love in his dotage, much to his family's amusement.
She cared for her grandmother during the latter's dotage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: senility - the condition of being senile; deterioration of mental faculties due to old age
