dotage

noun
decline of mental faculties in old age
9.3 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdoÊŠ.tÉªdÊ’ / (AmE), / ËˆdÉ™ÊŠ.tÉªdÊ’ / (BrE)
the period of life in which a person is old and often exhibits a decline in mental faculties, sometimes accompanied by foolish or excessive fondness

Examples

In old dotage
Despite his advanced dotage, the old professor's wit was as sharp as ever.
He fell in love in his dotage, much to his family's amusement.
She cared for her grandmother during the latter's dotage.

Synonyms

  • senility
    7.3 0.56/m
    senility is gradual memory loss and mental weakness in old age, while dotage is a late-life state that often shows foolishness.
  • second childhood
    7.7 0.05/m
    second childhood is a return to childlike behaviour and dependence in old age, while dotage often means mental decline with foolishness.
