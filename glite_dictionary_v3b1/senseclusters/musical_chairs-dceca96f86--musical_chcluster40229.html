musical chairs

noun
children's game involving chairs and music
1.7 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmjuː.zɪ.kəl tʃeərz/ (AmE), /ˈmjuː.zɪ.kəl tʃeəz/ (BrE)
a children's game where players walk around chairs while music plays and must quickly sit when the music stops, with one fewer chair than players each round

Examples

A game of musical chairs
At the picnic, even the adults joined in a lively game of musical chairs.
The children played musical chairs at the birthday party until only one winner was left.
When the music stopped, everyone rushed to find a seat in musical chairs.
After several rounds, there was only one winner left in musical chairs.
The children played musical chairs in the garden while their parents watched.
The constant organizational changes at work felt like a game of musical chairs.
