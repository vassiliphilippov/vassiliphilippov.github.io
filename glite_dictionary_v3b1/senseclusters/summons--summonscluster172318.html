summons

noun
official order to appear, often in court
7.5 1.68/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÉ™mÉ™nz /; / ËˆsÊŒmÉ™nz / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒmÉ™nz / (BrE)
an official order or legal document requiring someone to appear at a specified place and time, especially in court

Examples

Court summons
Failure to respond to the summons could result in a fine.
She received a summons for jury duty in the mail.
The court issued a summons requiring him to testify next week.
Ignoring the summons could result in legal consequences.
She received a summons to appear in court next week.
The teacher issued a summons for the students to meet after class.
Failure to respond to the summons can lead to a default judgment.
He received a summons concerning the civil lawsuit.
The court sent a summons requiring her to appear next month.

Synonyms

  • citation
    6.4 1.69/m
    a citation orders someone to appear in court for a legal matter, while a summons is a general official court notice to appear.
  • process
    8.3 14/m
    process is a formal writ that starts a civil case and may lead to default judgment, while a summons orders someone to appear in court.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: command - an order to do something, often more forceful
  • degree lower: invitation - a request to be present or participate
  • synonym: subpoena - a written order requiring someone to attend court as a witness
