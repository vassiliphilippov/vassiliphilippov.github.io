slave-maker

noun
ant that enslaves other ants
Pronunciation: / ˈsleɪvˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
an ant that invades colonies of other ant species and captures their young to be raised as workers for its own colony

Examples

A slave-maker ant
Researchers observed a slave-maker entering a host nest during an organized attack.
Some slave-maker species have lost the ability to care for themselves and rely entirely on enslaved workers.
The slave-maker ant raids neighboring colonies and abducts larvae to serve its needs.
