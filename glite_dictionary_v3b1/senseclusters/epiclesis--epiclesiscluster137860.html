epiclesis

noun
invocation in liturgy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkliːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkliːsɪs / (BrE)
a part of a Christian liturgy in which the priest calls upon the Holy Spirit to consecrate the bread and wine

Examples

Eucharist epiclesis
During the Eucharist, the priest performed the epiclesis to invoke the Holy Spirit.
The epiclesis is a central moment in many Christian liturgical traditions.
Scholars study the wording of the epiclesis in different church rites.
