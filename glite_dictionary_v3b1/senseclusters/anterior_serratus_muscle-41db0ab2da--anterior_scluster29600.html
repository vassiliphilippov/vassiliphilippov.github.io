anterior serratus muscle

noun
muscle rotating scapula, raising ribs
4.8
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪriɚ səˈreɪtəs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə səˈreɪtəs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a muscle that aids in the rotation of the scapula and elevates the rib cage

Examples

Anterior serratus muscle injury
Injury to the anterior serratus muscle can cause winging of the scapula.
Proper warm-up exercises help prevent strains in the anterior serratus muscle.
The anterior serratus muscle plays a crucial role in the upward rotation of the shoulder blade.
