wonder

noun
curiosity; desire to know more
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʌndə / (BrE)
a feeling of curiosity, admiration, or amazement caused by something new, beautiful, or unexpected

Examples

Childlike wonder
He gazed at the stars in wonder, imagining what might lie beyond our world.
Her wonder about ancient civilizations inspired her to become an archaeologist.
The children's faces were filled with wonder as they explored the museum.
She listened with wonder as the scientist explained the mysteries of space.
The ancient temple filled him with a sense of wonder and curiosity.
The children gazed in wonder at the bright fireworks lighting up the sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amazement - A feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • synonym: awe - a feeling of reverential respect mixed with fear or wonder
  • synonym: curiosity - A strong desire to know or learn something
  • antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
