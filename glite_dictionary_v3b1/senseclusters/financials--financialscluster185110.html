financials

noun
company financial statements
4.1
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃəlz /; / faɪˈnænʃəlz / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəlz / (BrE)
the financial statements, records, or monetary details of a company, person, or organization

Examples

Company financials
The board examined the financials to assess the company's growth over the past year.
Investors often request a company's financials before making any major decisions.
The quarterly financials showed a significant increase in revenue and profit.
The company shared its financials with potential investors before the merger.
She reviewed her family's financials to plan for the upcoming school year.
Banks often require applicants to submit their financials when applying for a large loan.

  • degree higher: profits - the financial gain of a company
