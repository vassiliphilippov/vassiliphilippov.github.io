carding

noun
fiber preparation process
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːr.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of cleaning, separating, and aligning fibers such as wool or cotton using special brushes or machines before spinning

Examples

Carding wool fibers
Traditional textile workers spent hours carding wool before spinning it into yarn.
Modern factories use machines for carding large quantities of cotton quickly and efficiently.
The carding of fibers is an essential step in making smooth, even threads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combing - the process of further aligning fibers after carding to remove shorter fibers
  • degree higher: spinning - the process of twisting fibers together to make yarn or thread
