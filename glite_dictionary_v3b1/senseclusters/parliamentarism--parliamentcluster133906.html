parliamentarism

noun
parliament-based government
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrləˈmɛntəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɑːlɪəˈmɛntərɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of government in which parliament holds the main power and the executive branch is chosen by and accountable to parliament

Examples

Parliamentarism system
Many countries in Europe practice parliamentarism as their main form of government.
Debates about parliamentarism often compare it to presidential systems.
The rise of parliamentarism changed the balance of power between the executive and legislative branches.
The country's transition to parliamentarism marked a significant change in its political structure.
Many scholars debate the effectiveness of parliamentarism compared to presidential systems.
Supporters of parliamentarism argue that it allows for greater representation of the people.
The country adopted parliamentarism to ensure that the government is accountable to the legislature.
Understanding parliamentarism is essential for anyone interested in political science or government.
Debates in the assembly followed strict parliamentarism to maintain order and fairness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarianism - a system of government with strong central power and limited political freedoms
  • synonym: parliamentary democracy - a system of government where the parliament has the main authority
  • synonym: parliamentary procedure - the rules and methods used to conduct business in a parliament
  • synonym: parliamentary system - a political system where the government is elected by parliament
  • antonym: presidentialism - a system of government where the president is the head of state and government
