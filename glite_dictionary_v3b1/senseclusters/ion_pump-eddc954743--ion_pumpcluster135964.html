ion pump

noun
vacuum pump using ionization to remove gas
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.ɑn ˌpʌmp / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ɒn ˌpʌmp / (BrE)
a vacuum pump that removes gas by ionizing gas atoms or molecules and adsorbing them onto a metal surface

Examples

An ion pump device
If the ion pump fails, the experiment could be contaminated by residual gases.
Modern mass spectrometers often use an ion pump to achieve ultra-high vacuum.
The laboratory technician repaired the ion pump to restore the required vacuum conditions.
