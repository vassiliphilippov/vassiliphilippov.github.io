garrigue

noun
Mediterranean shrubland
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈriːɡ / (AmE), / ɡaˈriːɡ / (BrE)
a type of low, shrubby vegetation found on dry, rocky hills in the Mediterranean region, often with aromatic herbs

Examples

The garrigue plants
The hillsides were covered in fragrant garrigue stretching as far as the eye could see.
Many rare plants thrive in the unique environment of the garrigue.
Walking through the garrigue, we smelled wild thyme and rosemary in the warm air.
