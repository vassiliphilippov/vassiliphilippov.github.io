telemotor

noun
remote control device
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛlɪˌmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌməʊtə / (BrE)
a device that transmits mechanical movement to a distant location, often using hydraulic power, especially for steering on ships

Examples

Ship telemotor device
The engineer repaired the telemotor to restore steering control on the ship.
A malfunction in the telemotor caused the rudder to stop responding during the voyage.
Modern vessels often use electronic systems instead of a traditional telemotor for remote steering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actuator - a device that moves or controls a mechanism
  • synonym: transmitter - a device that sends signals or power to another location
