macrosporangium

noun
plant structure producing megaspores
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊspəˈrændʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊspəˈrandʒɪəm / (BrE)
A plant structure that produces megaspores, typically found in seed plants and some ferns and lycophytes.

Examples

The macrosporangium produces spores
Each macrosporangium gives rise to four megaspores, of which one becomes functional.
Scientists studied the development of the macrosporangium in various fern species.
The ovule of a seed plant is an example of a macrosporangium.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microsporangium - a plant structure that produces microspores
