turn

noun
short performance in a show
5.4 2.06/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn / (AmE), / t…úÀên / (BrE)
a short performance, act, or routine that is part of a longer entertainment program, especially in a variety show or theater

Examples

Comedy turn
Each performer had a ten-minute turn during the variety show.
She practiced her turn backstage before going on.
The comedian's turn was the highlight of the evening's entertainment.

Synonyms

  • number
    6.5 6.33/m
    a number is a short musical or stage piece often in a show's program, while a turn is a performer's slot.
  • act
    1.8 13/m
    an act is a short set by one performer or group in a show, while a turn is a similar single appearance.
  • bit
    4.6 2.12/m
    a bit is a very short comic or musical item inside a show, while a turn is any performer's brief appearance.
  • routine
    3.2 3.92/m
    a routine is a set of planned moves or jokes by one performer, while a turn is any single appearance in a show.
  • performance
    2.7 45/m
    a performance is the whole act of presenting a play, concert, or show to an audience, while a turn is one short segment.
