water absorption

noun
amount of water a material absorbs
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər əbˌzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə əbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process or amount of water a material takes in, typically measured by the weight increase after immersion for a specified time

Examples

Low water absorption
Low water absorption is important for materials used outdoors to prevent damage from moisture.
The engineers measured the water absorption of the bricks to ensure they met safety standards.
The laboratory report detailed the water absorption rates of several insulating foams.
