leap day

noun
February 29 in a leap year
3.7
Pronunciation: / lip deɪ / (AmE), / liːp deɪ / (BrE)
The extra day added to the calendar during a leap year, occurring as February 29 in the Gregorian calendar.

Examples

The leap day
2020 was the most recent year to include a leap day in its calendar.
Many people born on a leap day celebrate their birthday on February 28 in non-leap years.
The tradition of proposing marriage on a leap day dates back centuries in some cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leap year - a year with one extra day (366 days instead of 365)
