closing

noun
the act of shutting or ending something
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊzɪŋ /; / ˈkloʊ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzɪŋ /; / ˈkləʊ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of bringing something to an end, or the final part of something (such as a door, meeting, business, or speech)

Examples

The closing ceremony

The sudden closing of the store surprised many customers.

We attended the closing of the exhibition last night.
Her speech ended with a memorable closing that brought everyone to their feet.
In the closing of the meeting, the chairman summarized all the main points discussed.
The letter's closing was both warm and professional.
