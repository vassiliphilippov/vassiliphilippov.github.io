first strike

noun
general pre-emptive attack
Pronunciation: / f5dst stra6ak / (AmE), / f5cd0st stra6ak / (BrE)
a pre-emptive attack intended to neutralize an imminent threat before it can act or retaliate; used generally across domains (e.g., military, cyber).

Examples

Cyber first strike
The government launched a first strike against the network used to coordinate the attack.
Cybersecurity teams warned that a successful first strike could disable critical infrastructure for days.
Historians argued the general planned the first strike to prevent an imminent invasion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pre-emptive strike - an attack carried out to prevent or diminish an anticipated threat
  • antonym: second strike - a retaliatory attack carried out after being attacked
