anchovy pear

noun
West Indian fruit, often pickled
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.tʃoʊ.vi pɛr /; / ˈæntʃoʊvi pɛr / (AmE), / ˈæn.tʃəʊ.vi peə /; / ˈantʃəvi peə / (BrE)
a West Indian tree (Grias cauliflora) that produces an edible fruit resembling a mango, or the fruit itself, often pickled and eaten as a condiment

Examples

A jar of anchovy pear
She brought a jar of anchovy pear to the picnic for everyone to try.
The anchovy pear tree grows in tropical regions of the West Indies.
The pickled anchovy pear is a popular delicacy in the Caribbean.
In Jamaica, the anchovy pear is sometimes used to make pickles or eaten fresh.
The anchovy pear tree thrives in humid, lowland forests near rivers.
The tropical market was selling ripe anchovy pears alongside mangoes and papayas.
