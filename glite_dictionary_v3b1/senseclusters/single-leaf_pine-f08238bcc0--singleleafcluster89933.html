single-leaf pine

noun
a southwestern US pine tree with solitary needles and edible nuts
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəl liːf paɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl̩ liːf paɪn / (BrE)
a piñon pine native to the southwestern United States, characterized by having single needles and often several stems, valued for its edible seeds

Examples

The single-leaf pine tree
Native Americans have long collected seeds from the single-leaf pine for food.
The single-leaf pine thrives in dry regions and provides an important food source for wildlife.
Unlike other pines, the single-leaf pine has only one needle per bundle.
