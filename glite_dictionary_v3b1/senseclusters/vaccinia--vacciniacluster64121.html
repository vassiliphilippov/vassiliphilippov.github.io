(in biotechnology and medical research) an engineered form of the vaccinia virus used as a viral vector to deliver antigens or therapeutic genes in vaccines and oncolytic therapies.

Examples

Recombinant vaccinia vector
The research team used a recombinant vaccinia vector to deliver the tumor antigen in mice.
In the clinical trial, patients received a dose of modified vaccinia designed to attack cancer cells.
Scientists often choose vaccinia because its large genome allows insertion of multiple therapeutic genes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oncolytic virus - a virus used to infect and kill cancer cells and stimulate an anti-tumor immune response
  • synonym: viral vector - a virus modified to deliver genetic material into cells for vaccination or gene therapy
