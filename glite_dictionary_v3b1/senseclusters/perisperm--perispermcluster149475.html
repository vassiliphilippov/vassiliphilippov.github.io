perisperm

noun
nutritive seed tissue outside embryo sac
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəˌspɜrm / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪspɜːm / (BrE)
the nutritive tissue surrounding the embryo sac in some seeds, outside the embryo-containing sac

Examples

The perisperm tissue
In certain seeds, the perisperm can constitute the main storage tissue, replacing the endosperm.
The perisperm provides additional nutrients for the developing seedling in some plant species.
Unlike endosperm, perisperm originates from the nucellus tissue in the seed.
