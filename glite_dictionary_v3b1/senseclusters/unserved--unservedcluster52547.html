unserved

adjective
not legally delivered
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɜrvd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːvd / (BrE)
not having been formally given legal documents such as a summons or subpoena.

Examples

Unserved legal papers
The lawsuit could not proceed because the defendant remained unserved with the complaint.
If a party is unserved, the court may delay the hearing until proper notice is given.
The judge dismissed the case after learning that the documents were still unserved.

Related Terms

  • antonym: served - having been formally given legal documents
