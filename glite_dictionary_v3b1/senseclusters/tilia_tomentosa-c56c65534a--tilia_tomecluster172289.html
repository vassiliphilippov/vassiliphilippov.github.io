Tilia tomentosa

noun
species of large tree, silver linden
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪliə toʊˌmɛnˈtoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈtɪlɪə təʊˌmɛnˈtəʊsə / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and Asia Minor, commonly known as the silver linden, with distinctive leaves covered in white, woolly hairs on the underside.

Examples

Tilia tomentosa tree
Gardeners often plant Tilia tomentosa for shade and ornamental value in urban areas.
Several parks feature the Tilia tomentosa because of its attractive silvery foliage.
The leaves of Tilia tomentosa are easily recognized by the white, felt-like underside.
