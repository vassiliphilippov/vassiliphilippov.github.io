hit a bump

verb
encounter a problem or obstacle
Pronunciation: / hɪt ə bʌmp / (AmE), / hɪt ə bʌmp / (BrE)
to experience a problem or obstacle that slows progress or makes things more difficult

Examples

To hit a bump in progress
After months of smooth progress, the project hit a bump when the main supplier went bankrupt.
Negotiations hit a bump over disagreements on payment terms, but talks are continuing.
Our vacation plans hit a bump when our flight was unexpectedly delayed.
