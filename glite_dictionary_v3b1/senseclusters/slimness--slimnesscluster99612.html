slimness

noun
small margin; slight difference
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the condition of being only just sufficient, or involving a very small difference or margin, especially in competitions or elections

Examples

The slimness of the margin
Despite the slimness of the majority, the law was passed in parliament.
She was surprised by the slimness of the difference between the two teams' final scores.
The slimness of the victory left the candidates anxious until the very last vote was counted.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breadth - wide extent or margin
  • synonym: narrowness - the state of being limited in width or extent; also, small margin
