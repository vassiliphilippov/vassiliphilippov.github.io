disinvolvement

noun
withdrawal from participation
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈvɑlvmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈvɒlvmənt / (BrE)
the act, process, or state of not being involved or participating in something

Examples

Sudden disinvolvement
Her sudden disinvolvement from the committee left everyone wondering what had happened.
The manager noticed a gradual disinvolvement among team members after the policy change.
After months of active participation, his disinvolvement was unexpected and disappointing.
The manager's disinvolvement from the project surprised the entire team.
After the scandal, the company announced the disinvolvement of several key partners.
Legal advisors recommended the disinvolvement of the organization to avoid conflicts of interest.
The manager noticed a growing disinvolvement among employees during the long project.
Her disinvolvement in class discussions made it difficult for her to connect with classmates.
A sense of disinvolvement can lead to poor teamwork and missed opportunities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detachment - a state of being emotionally uninvolved or distant
  • synonym: disengagement - the process of withdrawing or detaching from involvement
  • synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from taking part in something
  • antonym: involvement - the act of participating or being engaged in something
