zoistic

adjective
relating to zoism
Pronunciation: / zoʊˈɪstɪk / (AmE), / zəʊˈɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to zoism, the belief that life or mental phenomena are caused by an animal vital force or animal magnetism

Examples

Zoistic theory
The doctor dismissed the zoistic explanation for the patient's recovery as unscientific.
In the nineteenth century, some healers followed zoistic theories involving animal magnetism.
The zoistic approach to medicine emphasized the influence of animal life force on health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vitalistic - relating to the doctrine that life processes are not explicable by physical or chemical phenomena alone
