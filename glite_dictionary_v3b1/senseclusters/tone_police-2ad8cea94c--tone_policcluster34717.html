tone police

noun
people who criticize others' manner of speaking
6.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /toʊn pə.ˈliːs/ /; / /toʊn poʊ.ˈliːs/ /; / /toʊn pə.ˈlɪs/ (AmE), /təʊn pə.ˈliːs/ /; / /təʊn pə.ˈlɪs/ (BrE)
people who focus on criticizing the manner or emotional tone of someone's speech rather than its content, often to dismiss or undermine their argument

Examples

Tone police criticize speech
During the meeting, the tone police interrupted to criticize the presenter's passionate approach.
She accused the tone police of derailing important conversations by focusing on how people expressed themselves.
Whenever controversial topics were discussed online, the tone police were quick to point out anyone's emotional responses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tone policing - the act of criticising the tone of someone's speech instead of its substance
