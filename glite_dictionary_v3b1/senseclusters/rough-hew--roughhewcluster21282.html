rough-hew

verb
cut or shape roughly
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌfˌhju / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌhjuː / (BrE)
to shape or cut roughly, without finishing or smoothing the surface, especially in wood or stone

Examples

To rough-hew stone
He rough-hewed a block of stone to form the base of the sculpture.
Many old cottages have beams that were rough-hewn by hand centuries ago.
The workers rough-hewed the logs before loading them onto the truck.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hew - to chop or cut (wood or stone), especially with an axe, pick, or other tool
