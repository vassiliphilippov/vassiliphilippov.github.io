accrual

noun
gradual increase or accumulation
7.2 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàkru…ôl / (AmE), / …ôÀàkruÀê…ôl / (BrE)
the process of accumulating or increasing something gradually over time, especially sums of money or benefits

Examples

The accrual of vacation
Company policy requires the accrual of vacation days for all full-time employees.
The accrual of benefits will begin on your first day of employment.
The accrual of interest on a savings account can significantly boost your balance over time.

Synonyms

  • accruement
    8.2 0.00/m
    accruement describes the act of accumulating money over time, while accrual is the common accounting term for that gradual increase.
  • accumulation
    3.4 4.38/m
    accumulation refers to the general gathering or result of collected items over time, while accrual focuses on financial or benefit increases.
