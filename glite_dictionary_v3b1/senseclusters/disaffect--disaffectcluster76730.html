disaffect

verb
to make someone lose affection or loyalty
7.6 8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.əˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to make someone feel less friendly, loyal, or supportive toward a person, group, or cause; to alienate the affection or loyalty of someone.

Examples

To disaffect team members
His harsh words began to disaffect loyal members of the team.
The government's actions could disaffect large sections of the population.
Years of unfair policies eventually disaffected many employees from the company.
