duke

noun
British nobleman of the highest rank
1.0
Pronunciation: / duːk / (AmE), / djuːk / (BrE)
a male nobleman of the highest hereditary rank, just below the king or queen, especially in Britain or other European countries

Examples

The duke of York
As a duke, he owned several large estates across England.
The duke attended the royal wedding in a ceremonial uniform.
The duke inherited the title after his father's passing.
After the king's death, the duke was considered the most powerful man in the realm.
Historical novels often feature a duke as a central character.
The duke hosted a grand ball at his country estate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duchess - a woman holding the rank of duke in her own right or as the wife of a duke
