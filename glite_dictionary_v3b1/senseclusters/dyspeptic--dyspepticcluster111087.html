dyspeptic

adjective
suffering from indigestion
6.6
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (BrE)
suffering from indigestion or being irritable or bad-tempered (often because of indigestion)

Examples

Dyspeptic feelings
After a heavy meal, he felt quite dyspeptic for the rest of the evening.
Certain foods can trigger dyspeptic symptoms in sensitive individuals.
The dyspeptic patient described his discomfort to the doctor.
After missing breakfast, he grew increasingly dyspeptic as the morning wore on.
Her dyspeptic mood made conversation difficult during the meeting.
The critic’s dyspeptic remarks soured the atmosphere at the event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amiable - having a friendly and pleasant manner
  • synonym: indigestion - discomfort or pain in the stomach associated with difficulty in digesting food
  • synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
