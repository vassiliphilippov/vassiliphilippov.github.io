backdate

noun
retroactive date on a document
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.deɪt/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.deɪt/ (BrE)
a date shown on a document or record that is earlier than the actual date when it was made or issued; a retroactive date.

Examples

A suspicious backdate
The accountant noticed a suspicious backdate on several invoices from last quarter.
Because of the error, the company removed the incorrect backdate before filing its taxes.
Lawyers argued the contract's backdate made the agreement void under state law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antedate - to assign to an earlier date; to date before the actual time
  • antonym: postdate - to assign a later date to a document or event; the opposite of backdate
