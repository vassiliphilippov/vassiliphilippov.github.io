pounds per square inch

noun
unit of pressure measurement
Pronunciation: / paʊndz pɚ skwɛr ɪntʃ / (AmE), / paʊndz pə skweə ɪntʃ / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to the pressure resulting from a force of one pound-force applied to an area of one square inch

Examples

Pounds per square inch pressure
Industrial compressors often generate over 100 pounds per square inch of pressure.
The pressure gauge reads 50 pounds per square inch, which is within the safe range.
The tire pressure should be 35 pounds per square inch for optimal performance.
