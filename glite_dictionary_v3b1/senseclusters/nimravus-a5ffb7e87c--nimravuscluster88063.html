Nimravus

noun
extinct genus of saber-toothed carnivorous mammal
Pronunciation: / nɪmˈreɪvəs / (AmE), / nɪmˈreɪvəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of prehistoric carnivorous mammals resembling saber-toothed tigers, known from fossil records

Examples

The Nimravus fossil
Scientists believe that Nimravus hunted in a manner similar to modern big cats.
The fossils of Nimravus reveal long canine teeth similar to those of true saber-toothed cats.
Unlike its relatives, Nimravus had a more slender build adapted for swift pursuit.
Scientists believe that Nimravus hunted prey in a manner similar to modern big cats.
The fossilized skull of a Nimravus shows the characteristic elongated canines.
Unlike true saber-toothed cats, Nimravus belonged to a separate family known as nimravids.
