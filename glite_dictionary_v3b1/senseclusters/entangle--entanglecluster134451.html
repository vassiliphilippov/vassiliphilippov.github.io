entangle

verb
involve in a difficult situation
2.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæŋɡəl / (BrE)
to involve someone or something in a difficult or complicated situation from which it is hard to escape

Examples

Entangled in problems
He found himself entangled in a web of lies and deceit.
Many nations were entangled in alliances that eventually led to war.
The company became entangled in legal disputes that lasted for years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ensnare - to catch or trap as if in a snare
  • antonym: free - to release from entanglement or confinement
