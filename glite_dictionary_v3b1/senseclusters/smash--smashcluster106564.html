smash

verb
devastate or humiliate emotionally
Pronunciation: / smæʃ / (AmE), / smæʃ / (BrE)
to completely defeat, ruin, or devastate someone or something (emotionally, financially, or abstractly)

Examples

Smash his hopes
He was smashed by the news that his proposal had been rejected.
Her confidence was completely smashed by the harsh criticism she received.
The team's defeat in the finals smashed their hopes of winning the championship.
A single poor investment can smash your finances if you're not careful.
The 2008 economic crisis smashed thousands of small enterprises across the country.
The unexpected lawsuit could smash even a successful business owner.
Activists hope to smash the corrupt regime through peaceful protest.
The police smashed the smuggling network in a coordinated raid.
The task force was created to smash organized crime in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to cause someone to go bankrupt (informal)
  • antonym: create - to bring into existence (opposite of destroy)
  • synonym: crush - to subdue or overwhelm emotionally or spiritually
  • synonym: defeat - to win a victory over; to overcome
  • synonym: destroy - to ruin emotionally beyond recovery
  • synonym: ruin - to destroy someone's finances or prospects
