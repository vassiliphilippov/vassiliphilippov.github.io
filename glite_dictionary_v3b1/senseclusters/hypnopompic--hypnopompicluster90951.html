hypnopompic

adjective
waking from sleep
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪpnəˈpɑːmpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɪpnəˈpɒmpɪk / (BrE)
relating to the state of consciousness experienced while waking up from sleep

Examples

Hypnopompic hallucinations
Some people report hypnopompic hallucinations as they transition from sleep to wakefulness.
The hypnopompic state can sometimes cause confusion or vivid dreams upon waking.
During the hypnopompic period, she often remembers fragments of her dreams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypnagogic - relating to the state just before falling asleep
