underpainting

noun
base paint layer
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.peɪn.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.peɪn.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
a first layer of paint applied to a canvas or panel that serves as a base for later layers in a painting

Examples

A underpainting layer
The artist started with a detailed underpainting to establish the main shapes and shadows.
A well-executed underpainting can add depth and richness to the final artwork.
She let the underpainting dry completely before adding the next layer of color.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ground - a prepared surface or coating on which a painting is made
  • antonym: overpainting - a layer of paint applied over an existing layer in a painting
