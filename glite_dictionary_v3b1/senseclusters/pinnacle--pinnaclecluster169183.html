pinnacle

noun
slender spire atop a buttress or tower
3.5 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪn.ə.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈpɪn.ə.kəl/ (BrE)
(architecture) A slender, upright spire, typically pointed, placed at the top of a buttress or tower as an ornamental feature or to add weight for stability.

Examples

Gothic pinnacle
Ornate stone carvings decorated the pinnacle, drawing the eye upward toward the sky.
Restorers carefully repaired the damaged pinnacle atop the ancient tower.
The Gothic cathedral was adorned with a pinnacle on each flying buttress.
