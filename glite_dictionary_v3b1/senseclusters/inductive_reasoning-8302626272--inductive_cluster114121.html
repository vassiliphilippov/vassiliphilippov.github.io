inductive reasoning

noun
reasoning from facts to principles
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌktɪv ˈrizənɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌktɪv ˈriːzənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of deriving general principles from specific observations or facts

Examples

Inductive reasoning process
Inductive reasoning allows scientists to form hypotheses based on patterns they observe in their data.
Teaching students the difference between inductive reasoning and deductive reasoning helps improve their critical thinking skills.
When faced with new information, she relied on inductive reasoning rather than jumping to broad conclusions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deductive reasoning - deriving specific conclusions from general principles
