remanufacture

noun
restored product
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌmænjuˈfæktʃər / (AmE), / ˌriːˌmænjuˈfæktʃə / (BrE)
a product that has been restored or rebuilt to a condition similar to new, often using original parts

Examples

A remanufacture smartphone
The company offers a remanufacture of popular smartphone models at a lower price.
Buying a remanufacture can be a cost-effective alternative to purchasing a brand new product.
He trusted the quality of the remanufacture because it came with a full warranty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: new - not existing before; made for the first time
  • synonym: rebuild - to build something again after it has been damaged or destroyed
  • synonym: refurbishment - the process of cleaning and repairing something to improve its condition
