boldface

noun
typeface with thick, heavy lines
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊldˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldˌfeɪs / (BrE)
a typeface or style of print with thick, heavy lines, used for emphasis

Examples

Boldface type

The headlines were printed in boldface to grab the reader's attention.
Words in boldface are defined in the glossary.

Instruction manuals often use boldface for warnings and notes.
Please print the headline in boldface to make it stand out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: italic - typeface with slanted characters, typically used for emphasis
  • degree lower: regular - a typeface with standard, upright weight
