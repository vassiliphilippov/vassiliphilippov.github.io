call-out

noun
public criticism or accusation
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔːl.aʊt/ /; / /ˈkɑːl.aʊt/ (AmE), /ˈkɔːl.aʊt/ (BrE)
a public statement criticizing or accusing someone (often on social media) intended to hold them accountable for perceived wrongdoing

Examples

Social media call-out
The influencer faced a harsh call-out after posting insensitive comments.
During the staff meeting, she publicly issued a call-out about the manager's safety lapses.
Students discussed how to respond thoughtfully to a call-out instead of reacting angrily online.
