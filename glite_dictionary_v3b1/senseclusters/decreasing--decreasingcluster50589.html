decreasing

adjective
becoming slower or quieter (music)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkriːsɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈkriːsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of tempo or volume in music) becoming gradually slower or quieter.

Examples

Decreasing volume
The composition features a decreasing intensity, both in speed and loudness, in the final movement.
The conductor signaled for a decreasing tempo as the piece neared its end.
The music faded out with a decreasing volume that left the audience in silence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: increasing - becoming greater in tempo or volume (music)
