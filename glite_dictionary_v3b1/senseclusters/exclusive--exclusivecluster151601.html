exclusive

noun
news report first released by one media outlet
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (BrE)
a news story or special item available only to one organization or group

Examples

A newspaper exclusive
The newspaper printed an exclusive about the prime minister’s resignation.
Their reporter managed to land an exclusive interview with the famous actor.
We were the first to break the story with our exclusive on the financial scandal.
The newspaper was proud to publish an exclusive about the celebrity's secret wedding.
Subscribers get access to special exclusives not available to the general public.
The company released an exclusive product for its most loyal customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commonplace - something ordinary or widely available
  • synonym: feature - a special article or item in a publication
  • synonym: scoop - a news report published or broadcast before others
