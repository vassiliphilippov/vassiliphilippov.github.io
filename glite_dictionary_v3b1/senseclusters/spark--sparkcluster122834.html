spark

noun
small sign of a feeling or quality
5.7
Pronunciation: / spɑrk / (AmE), / spɑːk / (BrE)
a small but noticeable amount of a quality or feeling, especially one that could develop into something larger

Examples

A spark of hope
He still showed a spark of hope, despite all the setbacks.
The teacher noticed a spark of curiosity in the students during the experiment.
There was a spark of excitement in her eyes when she saw the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glimmer - a faint sign of a feeling or quality
  • synonym: trace - a very small quantity, especially of something abstract
