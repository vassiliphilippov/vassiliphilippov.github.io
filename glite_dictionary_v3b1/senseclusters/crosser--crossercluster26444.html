crosser

noun
person or thing crossing
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsə / (BrE)
a person or thing that crosses from one side of something (like a road or border) to the other

Examples

A road crosser
The crosser waited for the light to turn green before moving forward.
Border guards checked the documents of every crosser entering the country.
A quick crosser darted across the busy street during rush hour.
The traffic light turned red to let the crosser walk safely.
A crosser waited patiently at the busy intersection before stepping onto the road.
Police officers helped an elderly crosser get across the street during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pedestrian - a person walking, especially across a road
  • synonym: traveler - a person who is traveling from one place to another
