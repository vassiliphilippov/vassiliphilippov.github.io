plowed

adjective
land turned over with a plow
Pronunciation: / ˈplaʊd /; / plaʊd / (AmE), / ˈplaʊd /; / plaʊd / (BrE)
(of land) broken up and turned over with a plow, especially to prepare it for planting crops.

Examples

Plowed farmland
After the spring rains, the soil in the plowed land was rich and dark.
Farmers prefer to plant seeds in plowed ground for better crop yields.
The plowed fields stretched as far as the eye could see.
After the rain, the ploughed field became difficult to walk across.
Rows of ploughed soil stretched to the horizon in the morning light.
The scent of ploughed earth filled the air as spring planting began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tilled - land that has been prepared for planting by turning the soil
  • antonym: unploughed - land not broken or turned over with a plow
  • antonym: unplowed - (of land) not having been turned over with a plow
