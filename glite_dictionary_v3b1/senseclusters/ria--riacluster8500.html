ria

noun
coastal inlet
Pronunciation: / ˈriːə / (AmE), / ˈriːə / (BrE)
a long, narrow coastal inlet formed by the partial flooding of a river valley by the sea

Examples

A ria coast
The Galician coast is famous for its many rias that stretch inland from the Atlantic Ocean.
Sailors navigated the winding ria to reach the small fishing village.
Birdwatchers often visit the ria to observe unique coastal wildlife.

  • synonym: fjord - a long, deep, narrow body of water between high cliffs, created by glacial activity
