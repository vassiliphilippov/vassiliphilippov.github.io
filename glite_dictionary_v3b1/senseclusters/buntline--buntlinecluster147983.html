buntline

noun
rope for raising sail
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌntˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌntˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a rope attached to the bottom of a square sail, used to pull the sail up toward the yard on a ship

Examples

Each buntline must be checked before the ship leaves the harbor.
He learned to tie a knot in the buntline during his first week at sea.
