Cyrenaicism

noun
doctrine of seeking immediate pleasure
Pronunciation: / saɪˈriːneɪɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / saɪˈriːniːəˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
The philosophical doctrine that advocates seeking immediate pleasure as the highest good, disregarding future consequences; the teachings of the Cyrenaics.

Examples

Cyrenaicism philosophy
Many scholars study Cyrenaicism to understand the origins of hedonistic philosophy.
The influence of Cyrenaicism can be seen in later discussions of ethical hedonism.
Unlike Epicureanism, Cyrenaicism emphasizes immediate rather than long-term pleasure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hedonism - the pursuit of pleasure as the highest good
