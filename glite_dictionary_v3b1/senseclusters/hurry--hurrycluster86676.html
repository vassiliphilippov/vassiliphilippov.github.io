Synonyms

    3.4 4/m
    haste is a strong, often formal word for moving quickly with carelessness, while hurry is a common state of rushed urgency.
  • hurriedness
    5.6 0.01/m
    hurriedness describes a person's or situation's constant rush and sloppiness, while hurry refers to a short state of urgency.
  • hastiness
    5.8 0.04/m
    hastiness shows acting or deciding too quickly without thought, while hurry names the general state of urgent rushing.
  • rush
    1.8 20/m
    rush is a sudden strong movement or exciting burst, while hurry is a state of urgent rushing that often causes carelessness.
