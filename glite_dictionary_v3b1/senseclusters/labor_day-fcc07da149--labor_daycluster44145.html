Labor Day

proper noun
first Monday in September; workers' holiday
1.4
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.bər deɪ/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.bə deɪ/ (BrE)
A public holiday observed on the first Monday in September in the United States and Canada, celebrating the achievements of workers.

Examples

Labor Day holiday
For many Americans, Labor Day marks the unofficial end of summer.
Many people enjoy barbecues and parades on Labor Day.
The company is closed every Labor Day as part of the national holiday.
