Lawrence

proper noun
town in northeastern Kansas, United States
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːrəns / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
A town in northeastern Kansas, United States, located on the Kansas River and known for its historical significance in the anti-slavery movement and the raids led by John Brown in 1856.

Examples

Lawrence town Kansas
During the Civil War era, Lawrence became a center for anti-slavery activism.
Many residents of Lawrence participate in local festivals celebrating the town's unique history.
The University of Kansas is located in Lawrence, attracting students from across the country.
