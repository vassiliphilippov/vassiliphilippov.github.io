water blister

noun
blister with clear watery fluid
4.1 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər ˈblɪs.tər/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə ˈblɪs.tə/ (BrE)
A blister containing clear, watery fluid, usually caused by friction or burns.

Examples

A water blister on skin
After spilling hot tea on his hand, a small water blister formed overnight.
It’s important not to pop a water blister to reduce the risk of infection.
She developed a water blister on her heel after hiking all day in new shoes.
