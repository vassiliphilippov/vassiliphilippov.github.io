light unit

noun
unit for measuring visible light
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the intensity or amount of visible light, such as the lumen or candela

Examples

A light unit measure
Different light units are used depending on whether you are measuring intensity or total output.
Scientists use the candela as a standard light unit in their experiments.
The brightness of the bulb was measured in light units called lumens.
