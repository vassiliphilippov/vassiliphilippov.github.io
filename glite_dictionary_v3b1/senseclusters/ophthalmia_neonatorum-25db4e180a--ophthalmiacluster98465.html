ophthalmia neonatorum

noun
conjunctivitis in newborns
8.8 8.4
Pronunciation: /ɑːf.ˈθæl.mi.ə niː.oʊ.nə.ˈtɔː.rəm/ /; / /ɑːp.ˈθæl.mi.ə niː.oʊ.nə.ˈtɔː.rəm/ (AmE), /ɒf.ˈθæl.mi.ə niː.əʊ.nə.ˈtɔː.rəm/ /; / /ɒp.ˈθæl.mi.ə niː.əʊ.nə.ˈtɔː.rəm/ (BrE)
A form of conjunctivitis occurring in newborns, usually contracted during passage through the birth canal, typically prevented with antiseptic drops.

Examples

Ophthalmia neonatorum infection
Cases of ophthalmia neonatorum have dramatically decreased since the introduction of prophylactic treatment.
If untreated, ophthalmia neonatorum can lead to serious complications, including blindness.
Routine administration of eye drops prevents ophthalmia neonatorum in most newborns.
