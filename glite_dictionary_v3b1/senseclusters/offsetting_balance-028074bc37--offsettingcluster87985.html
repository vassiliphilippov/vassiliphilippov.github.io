offsetting balance

noun
minimum deposit required for a loan
5.4 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsɛtɪŋ ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛtɪŋ ˈbæləns / (BrE)
a minimum credit balance that a bank requires a borrower to keep on deposit as a condition for granting a loan

Examples

An offsetting balance requirement
An offsetting balance can limit the borrower's access to all the funds from the loan.
By maintaining an offsetting balance, the company paid a higher effective interest rate.
The bank required an offsetting balance to secure the business loan.
