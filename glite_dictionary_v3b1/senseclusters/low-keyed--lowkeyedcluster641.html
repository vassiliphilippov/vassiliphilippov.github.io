low-keyed

adjective
restrained; not showy or intense
Pronunciation: / loʊˈkid / (AmE), / ləʊˈkiːd / (BrE)
Restrained or subdued in style, quality, or intensity; not elaborate or showy.

Examples

A low-keyed style
He prefers a low-keyed approach to leadership rather than being the center of attention.
Her low-keyed speech captivated the audience with its gentle tone.
The wedding ceremony was low-keyed and elegant, just as the couple wanted.

Related Terms

  • antonym: showy - very noticeable, elaborate, or attention-grabbing
  • synonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle or restrained way
