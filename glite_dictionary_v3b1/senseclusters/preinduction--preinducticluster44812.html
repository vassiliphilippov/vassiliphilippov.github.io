preinduction

noun
pre-anesthesia medication
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪnˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪnˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
a drug or dose given before the induction of anesthesia to prepare the patient for the procedure

Examples

Preinduction drug
The nurse administered a preinduction to help the patient relax before surgery.
Doctors often use preinduction to minimize anxiety and discomfort before anesthesia.
A suitable preinduction can make the process of intubation much smoother for both patient and staff.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: induction - the process of starting anesthesia
  • synonym: premedication - medication given before a medical procedure
