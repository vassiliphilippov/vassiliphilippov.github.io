heliocentric

adjective
having the sun as the center
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːlioʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːliəʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
Having the sun as the center, especially referring to an astronomical model in which the planets revolve around the sun.

Examples

A heliocentric model
Copernicus argued for a heliocentric universe instead of an Earth-centered one.
Modern astronomers accept the heliocentric theory as the accurate explanation for planetary motion.
The heliocentric model revolutionized our understanding of the solar system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: geocentric - having the Earth as the center, especially in astronomical models
