mackle

noun
blurred or doubled printed impression
Pronunciation: / ˈmækəl / (AmE), / ˈmakl / (BrE)
A printed mark or impression that is blurred or appears doubled, typically due to a printing error.

Examples

A mackle mark
A mackle on the cover spoiled the appearance of the first edition.
She noticed a slight mackle running through the headline.
The old newspaper was full of mackles that made the text hard to read.
