biomimicry

noun
nature-inspired design
7.7
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.ˈmɪm.ɪ.kriː/ /; / /baɪ.oʊ.ˈmɪm.ə.kriː/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.ˈmɪm.ɪ.kriː/ /; / /baɪ.əʊ.ˈmɪm.ə.kriː/ (BrE)
the design or creation of materials, structures, or systems by imitating models or processes found in nature

Examples

Biomimicry design
The new building's cooling system uses biomimicry to mimic how termite mounds regulate temperature.
Designers often turn to biomimicry for innovative solutions inspired by nature.
By applying biomimicry, scientists developed a water-repellent fabric based on lotus leaves.
Engineers used biomimicry to design a train that reduces noise by copying the shape of a kingfisher's beak.
The concept of biomimicry has led to the development of more efficient wind turbines inspired by whale fins.
Many architects now apply biomimicry to create buildings that are both beautiful and energy-efficient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioinspiration - the use of ideas from nature to inspire new technologies
  • synonym: bionics - the study of mechanical systems that function like living organisms
  • synonym: engineering - the application of scientific principles to design and build structures or devices
