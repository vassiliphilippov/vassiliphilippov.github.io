writ of right

noun
legal order to restore property
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɹɪt əv ɹaɪt / (AmE), / ɹɪt əv ɹaɪt / (BrE)
a legal order commanding that property, especially land, be restored to its rightful owner

Examples

A writ of right order
Historically, a writ of right was the highest remedy for those claiming property in feudal England.
The court issued a writ of right to return the disputed farmland to the family.
Without a writ of right, landowners had little means of reclaiming their land from unlawful possessors.
