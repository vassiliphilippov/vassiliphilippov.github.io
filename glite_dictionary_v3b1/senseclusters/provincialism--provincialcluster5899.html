provincialism

noun
lack of sophistication or broad-mindedness
6.8 7.4
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈvɪn.ʃə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /prə.ˈvɪn.ʃə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
narrow-mindedness or excessive focus on the customs and interests of one's own region, often with a lack of openness to other ideas or cultures

Examples

His provincialism attitude
His provincialism was apparent in his distrust of anything unfamiliar or foreign.
She criticized the provincialism that stifled cultural innovation in her small hometown.
The novel explores themes of provincialism and resistance to change in rural communities.
Critics accused the festival organizers of provincialism for excluding international performers.
His provincialism made it difficult for him to appreciate cultures outside his hometown.
The novel satirizes the provincialism of small-town life in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitanism - the quality of being familiar with and at ease in many different cultures
  • synonym: narrow-mindedness - the quality of having a limited perspective or intolerance
  • synonym: parochialism - a limited or narrow outlook, often focused on a local area
  • antonym: sophistication - the quality of being cultured, knowledgeable, or refined
