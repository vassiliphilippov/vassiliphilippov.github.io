surrogate

noun
substitute clinical marker
Pronunciation: / c8s c0r.59.6159t / (AmE), / c8s8cr.59.6159t / (BrE)
a substitute or stand-in used in technical or scientific contexts, such as medicine or statistics

Examples

Surrogate marker
In early trials, a surrogate endpoint like blood pressure is used to predict heart risk.
The researchers showed the change in the surrogate correlated with long-term patient survival.
Regulators sometimes accept a surrogate measure when direct clinical outcomes take many years.
We built a surrogate model to approximate the expensive simulator during optimization.
The team trained a Gaussian-process surrogate to speed up design evaluation across parameters.
Optimization relied on a fast surrogate instead of running the full physical simulation each time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biomarker - a measurable indicator of some biological state or condition, often used as a substitute outcome in research
  • synonym: endpoint - an outcome used to judge the effectiveness of a treatment in a clinical trial
  • synonym: metamodel - a model of a model; a simplified model used to approximate a more complex simulation
  • synonym: proxy - something that serves as a substitute or stand-in for another thing, often used to reduce cost or complexity
