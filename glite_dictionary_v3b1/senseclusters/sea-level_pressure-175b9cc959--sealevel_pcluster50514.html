sea-level pressure

noun
atmospheric pressure adjusted to sea level
Pronunciation: / ˌsiː ˌlɛvəl ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˌsiː ˌlɛvəl ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the atmospheric pressure at any location, adjusted to the equivalent pressure at sea level using a standard formula

Examples

Sea-level pressure map
Maps showing sea-level pressure are essential for tracking high and low pressure systems.
Meteorologists use sea-level pressure to compare weather systems across different locations.
The weather report indicated a drop in sea-level pressure before the storm approached.
