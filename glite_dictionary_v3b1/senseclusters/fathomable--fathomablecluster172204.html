fathomable

adjective
able to be measured for depth
Pronunciation: / ˈfæð.əm.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfæð.əm.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of water or depths) capable of being measured or sounded to determine its depth.

Examples

Fathomable waters
Early explorers sought fathomable passages through the dangerous reefs.
Only the harbor's fathomable channels allowed large ships to anchor safely.
The river was surprisingly fathomable despite its dark, murky waters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unfathomable - impossible to measure the depth of; impossible to understand
