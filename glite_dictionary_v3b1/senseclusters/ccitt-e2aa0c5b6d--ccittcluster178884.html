CCITT

noun
telecom standards body
8.0
Pronunciation: /siː siː aɪ tiː tiː/ (AmE), /siː siː aɪ tiː tiː/ (BrE)
a set of international technical standards for telecommunications developed by the former International Telegraph and Telephone Consultative Committee

Examples

CCITT standards
The CCITT standards were essential for ensuring global compatibility in early telephone networks.
Many fax machines were designed to comply with CCITT recommendations for data transmission.
Before ITU-T, the CCITT played a key role in setting international telecom protocols.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ISO - an international organization that develops and publishes standards
  • synonym: ITU-T - the current international standards body for telecommunications, successor to CCITT
