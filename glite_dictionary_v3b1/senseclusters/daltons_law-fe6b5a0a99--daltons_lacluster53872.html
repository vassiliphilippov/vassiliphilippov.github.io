Dalton's law

noun
law of mass ratios in compound formation
7.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îlt…ônz l…î / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀêlt…ônz l…îÀê / (BrE)
A law in chemistry stating that if two elements form more than one compound, the masses of one element that combine with a fixed mass of the other are in simple whole-number ratios.

Examples

Dalton's law of multiple proportions
According to Dalton's law, the ratio of oxygen to hydrogen in different oxides of hydrogen is always a simple whole number.
Chemistry students often study Dalton's law when learning about the composition of compounds.
Experiments in the early 19th century provided strong evidence in support of Dalton's law.

Synonyms

  • law of multiple proportions
