anisotropy

noun
property of directional dependence
9.2 8.9
Pronunciation: /æ.nɪ.ˈsɑː.trə.piː/ (AmE), /æ.nɪ.ˈsɒ.trə.piː/ (BrE)
The property of being directionally dependent, having different values when measured along different axes, especially in physical properties.

Examples

Material anisotropy property
In physics, anisotropy is often observed in materials with layered structures.
Researchers measured the magnetic anisotropy of the material to understand its behavior.
The anisotropy of the crystal means its hardness varies depending on the direction tested.
