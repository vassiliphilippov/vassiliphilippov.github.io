randori

noun
martial arts sparring
Pronunciation: / rɑnˈdɔri / (AmE), / rænˈdɔːri / (BrE)
a free-style practice or sparring session in martial arts, where techniques are tested in a realistic setting, sometimes against multiple attackers

Examples

Randori practice session
During randori, students have the chance to test their skills against resisting opponents.
The instructor encouraged everyone to participate in randori to improve their reaction time.
Unlike formal drills, randori allows for spontaneous movement and adaptation.
During randori, the student had to react quickly to attacks from all directions.
The sensei demonstrated randori techniques with three assistants attacking at once.
Practicing randori helps martial artists improve their awareness and adaptability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kata - a set sequence of movements in martial arts
  • synonym: sparring - practice fighting in martial arts or boxing
