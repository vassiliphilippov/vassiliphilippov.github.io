snowplow

verb
to clear obstacles for someone
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊˌplaʊ / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌplaʊ / (BrE)
to remove obstacles or difficulties from someone's path so they do not have to face problems, especially used about parents or guardians.

Examples

To snowplow a child's path
Some parents snowplow their children's lives, making sure they never face any real challenges.
She realized she had snowplowed every difficulty out of her son's way, leaving him unprepared for adulthood.
Teachers sometimes notice when families snowplow obstacles for students instead of letting them solve problems themselves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expose - to leave someone unprotected or subject to difficulties
  • synonym: shield - to protect someone from harm or difficulty
