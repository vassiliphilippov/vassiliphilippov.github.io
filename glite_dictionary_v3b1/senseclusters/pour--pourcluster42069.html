pour

verb
move in large numbers
1.5 10/m
Pronunciation: / p…îr / (AmE), / p…îÀê / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to move somewhere in large numbers, especially quickly and in a steady stream.

Examples

Crowds pour in
After the announcement, people poured out of the building in excitement.
Crowds began to pour into the stadium as soon as the gates opened.
Refugees poured across the border seeking safety.

Synonyms

    3.1 0.45/m
    flood in arrives suddenly and in overwhelming numbers, while pour describes people moving quickly in a steady flowing stream.
  • stream
    1.7 5.66/m
    stream means people move continuously in large amounts, while pour focuses on a fast, steady crowd entering a place.
  • flow
    1.6 19/m
    flow describes smooth, steady movement of people or things, while pour often shows quick, concentrated crowds moving into a place.
  • spill out
    3.6 2.04/m
    spill out shows people leaving or moving out suddenly from a place, while pour describes people moving into somewhere in large numbers.
  • swarm
    3.2 6.05/m
    swarm means crowds move in large, busy groups, often closely and noisily, while pour suggests a smooth steady stream of people.
