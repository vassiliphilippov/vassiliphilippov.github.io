laxist

noun
overly permissive person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlæksɪst / (AmE), / ˈlæksɪst / (BrE)
a person who is overly permissive or lenient, especially regarding moral or religious rules

Examples

A laxist leader
Some members of the community viewed the new leader as a laxist who ignored important traditions.
The teacher was labeled a laxist for allowing students to break the dress code.
In debates about church doctrine, he was often criticized as a laxist by more conservative members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permissivist - a person who allows or tolerates a great deal
  • antonym: rigorist - a person who is very strict in moral or religious matters
