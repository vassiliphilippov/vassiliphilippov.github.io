antinomic

adjective
contradictory principles
Pronunciation: / ˌæntɪˈnoʊmɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
involving or relating to a contradiction between two principles, laws, or ideas

Examples

Antinomic contradiction
The theory is criticized for its antinomic assumptions that cannot be reconciled.
Legal scholars debated the antinomic relationship between freedom and security.
Her argument was antinomic, presenting two ideas that could not both be true.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consistent - not containing any contradictions; in agreement
  • synonym: contradictory - containing elements that are in opposition or conflict
  • synonym: paradoxical - seemingly self-contradictory or opposed to common sense
