obdurateness

noun
stubbornness
9.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêbdj…ôr…ôt.n…ôs / (AmE), / Àà…íbdj ä…ôr…ôt.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being stubborn and refusing to change, often showing a lack of feeling or compassion

Examples

Her obdurateness in refusal
Her obdurateness in refusing to compromise frustrated everyone on the team.
Despite repeated requests, his obdurateness prevented any progress in the discussion.
The obdurateness of the committee delayed the approval of the new policy.
Despite their pleas, his obdurateness prevented any chance of forgiveness.
The manager's obdurateness made negotiations extremely difficult for the team.
Her obdurateness in refusing to help surprised everyone who knew her kindness.

Synonyms

  • stubbornness
    3.6 1.61/m

  • intransigence
    8.7 1.05/m
    intransigence is a firm refusal to compromise in arguments or politics, while obdurateness also implies a lack of feeling.
  • unyieldingness
    8.1 0.00/m
    unyieldingness is steady firmness that will not bend, while obdurateness adds emotional coldness or lack of compassion to that firmness.
  • inflexibility
    6.9 0.39/m
    inflexibility is a rigid unwillingness to change plans or rules, while obdurateness also carries a harsh, unfeeling personal attitude.
  • recalcitrance
    8.8 0.29/m
    recalcitrance is active resistance to authority or rules, while obdurateness focuses more on hard, unfeeling stubbornness rather than mere defiance.
