obdurateness

noun
stubbornness
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːbdjərət.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɒbdjʊərət.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being stubborn and refusing to change, often showing a lack of feeling or compassion

Examples

Her obdurateness in refusal
Her obdurateness in refusing to compromise frustrated everyone on the team.
Despite repeated requests, his obdurateness prevented any progress in the discussion.
The obdurateness of the committee delayed the approval of the new policy.
Despite their pleas, his obdurateness prevented any chance of forgiveness.
The manager's obdurateness made negotiations extremely difficult for the team.
Her obdurateness in refusing to help surprised everyone who knew her kindness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for the suffering of others
  • antonym: flexibility - the quality of being willing to change or adapt
  • synonym: hardheartedness - lack of sympathy or compassion
  • synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being unwilling to change or give in
