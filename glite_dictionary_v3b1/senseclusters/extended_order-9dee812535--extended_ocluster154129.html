extended order

noun
widely spaced military formation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛndɪd ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛndɪd ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a military formation in which troops are deployed at unusually wide intervals for skirmishing or adapting to terrain and tactical requirements.

Examples

Extended order formation
During the exercise, soldiers practiced moving in extended order across open ground.
The commander ordered the platoon to deploy into extended order before approaching the objective.
The unit advanced in extended order to reduce casualties from enemy fire.
