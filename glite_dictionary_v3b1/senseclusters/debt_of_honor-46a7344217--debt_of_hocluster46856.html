debt of honor

noun
obligation not legally enforceable but morally binding
5.5
Pronunciation: / dɛt əv ˈɑnɚ / (AmE), / dɛt əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
An obligation, especially one arising from a gambling loss or verbal promise, that is not legally enforceable but is considered to be morally binding.

Examples

A debt of honor
A debt of honor is not enforceable in court, but his conscience compelled him to pay.
He regarded the gambling loss as a debt of honor that had to be paid immediately.
She felt obliged to repay her debt of honor despite there being no legal requirement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moral obligation - an obligation arising from ethical or moral principles
