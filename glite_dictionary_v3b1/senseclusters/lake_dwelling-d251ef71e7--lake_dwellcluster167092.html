lake dwelling

noun
prehistoric house on piles over a lake
5.1
Pronunciation: / leɪk ˈdwɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / leɪk ˈdwɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
A prehistoric house or settlement built on piles or platforms in or near the edge of a lake, typically for protection or access to water.

Examples

A lake dwelling settlement
A model of a lake dwelling is displayed in the museum's prehistoric exhibit.
Archaeologists have discovered remains of a lake dwelling from the Bronze Age.
The northern European region is known for its ancient lake dwellings.
