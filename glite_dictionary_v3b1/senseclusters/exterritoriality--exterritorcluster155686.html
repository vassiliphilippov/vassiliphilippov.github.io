exterritoriality

noun
legal exemption abroad
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstɛˌtɛrɪˌtɔriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɛkstɛˌtɛrɪˌtɒriˈæləti / (BrE)
the condition of being exempt from the jurisdiction of local law, especially due to diplomatic status

Examples

Diplomatic exterritoriality
Diplomats are often granted exterritoriality to protect them from local prosecution.
The embassy staff enjoyed exterritoriality while working in the foreign capital.
Some international organizations operate under exterritoriality in host countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunity - protection from legal duties or penalties
  • antonym: jurisdiction - the official power to make legal decisions and judgments
