.45 caliber

adjective
relating to 0.45-inch gun bore size
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪnt ˈfɔːrti faɪv ˈkælɪbər /; / ˌfɔːr.ti ˈfaɪv ˈkæl.ɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt ˈfɔːti faɪv ˈkælɪbə /; / ˌfɔː.ti ˈfaɪv ˈkæl.ɪ.bə / (BrE)
(of a firearm or its ammunition) having a bore or bullet diameter of 0.45 inches (about 11.43 mm).

Examples

.45 caliber pistol
He carried a .45 caliber pistol in his holster at all times.
Many gun enthusiasts favor the .45 caliber for its stopping power.
The police retrieved several .45 caliber shells from the crime scene.
Collectors often seek original .45 calibre revolvers from the early twentieth century.
She purchased ammunition that was compatible with her .45 calibre handgun.
The officer carried a .45 calibre pistol as his sidearm.
