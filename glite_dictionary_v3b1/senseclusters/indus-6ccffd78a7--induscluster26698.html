Indus

proper noun
major river in South Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dəs / (BrE)
a major river in South Asia, originating in Tibet and flowing through India and Pakistan to the Arabian Sea

Examples

Indus River valley
Archaeologists have discovered ancient cities along the banks of the Indus.
Much of Pakistan’s agriculture relies on water from the Indus.
The Indus River was vital to one of the world’s earliest civilizations.
