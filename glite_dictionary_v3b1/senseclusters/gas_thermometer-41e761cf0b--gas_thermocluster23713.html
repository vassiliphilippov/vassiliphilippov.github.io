gas thermometer

noun
thermometer using gas pressure to measure temperature
2.4 4.9
Pronunciation: / ɡæs θərˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ɡæs θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a thermometer that measures temperature by the change in pressure of a gas kept at constant volume

Examples

A gas thermometer device
A gas thermometer is often used in laboratories to measure extremely high or low temperatures accurately.
Because a gas thermometer relies on the pressure of a gas, it offers precise temperature readings over a wide range.
Scientists calibrate the gas thermometer before conducting experiments to ensure reliable data.
