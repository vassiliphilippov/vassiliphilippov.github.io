baby farmer

noun
historical caretaker of babies for payment
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.bi ˈfɑr.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˈfɑː.mə / (BrE)
a person, historically, who cared for babies, typically illegitimately for a fee, often in unsanitary or neglectful conditions

Examples

Baby farmer in Victorian England
In Victorian England, a baby farmer might take in dozens of children at a time for a fee.
Newspaper articles from the 1800s often exposed the cruel practices of some baby farmers.
The authorities arrested the notorious baby farmer after discovering evidence of child neglect.
