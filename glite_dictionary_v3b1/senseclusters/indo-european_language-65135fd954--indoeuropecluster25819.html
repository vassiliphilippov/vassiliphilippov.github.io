Indo-European language

noun
member of major Eurasian language family
5.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.dəʊ jʊ.rə.ˈpiː.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈɪn.doʊ juː.rə.ˈpiː.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.dəʊ jʊə.rə.ˈpiː.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈɪn.dəʊ juː.rə.ˈpiː.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a member of the large family of languages originally spoken across Europe and parts of Asia by 1000 BC, including most major languages of Europe and western Asia, such as English, Spanish, Russian, Hindi, and Persian.

Examples

An Indo-European language family
English is an Indo-European language with roots tracing back thousands of years.
Hindi is considered an Indo-European language, belonging to the Indo-Aryan branch.
Linguists compare the structures of each Indo-European language to trace historical connections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Germanic language - languages within the Germanic branch of Indo-European, such as English, German, and Dutch
  • synonym: Romance language - languages derived from Latin, such as Spanish, French, and Italian
  • antonym: Sino-Tibetan language - family of languages of East Asia unrelated to Indo-European
