Ammophila

proper noun
genus of hunting wasps
Pronunciation: / ˌæməˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌæməˈfɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of solitary hunting wasps that dig burrows and provide food for their larvae by capturing caterpillars or other prey

Examples

Ammophila wasp burrow
The Ammophila wasp carefully dug a tunnel in the sandy soil to lay its eggs.
Researchers observed an Ammophila carrying a paralyzed caterpillar back to its burrow.
Many species in the genus Ammophila are known for their remarkable hunting skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sphecid wasp - a family of solitary wasps that dig burrows and hunt prey
