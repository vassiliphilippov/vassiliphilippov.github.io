hepatolenticular degeneration

noun
rare inherited disorder of copper metabolism
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛpətoʊˌlɛnˈtɪkjələr ˌdiːdʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɛpətəʊˌlɛnˈtɪkjʊlə ˌdiːdʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a rare inherited disease in which copper accumulates in the liver, brain, and other tissues, causing neurological and liver problems

Examples

Hepatolenticular degeneration disease
Early treatment of hepatolenticular degeneration is critical to prevent irreversible organ damage.
Neurological symptoms in hepatolenticular degeneration can include tremors and difficulty speaking.
The patient was diagnosed with hepatolenticular degeneration after tests revealed high copper levels in her liver.
