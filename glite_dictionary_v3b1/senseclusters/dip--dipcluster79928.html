dip

verb
slope downwards (of land or surface)
9.9 2.3
Pronunciation: /dɪp/ (AmE), /dɪp/ (BrE)
to move or slope downward, or to lower something briefly

Examples

The road dips down
As you hike, the trail dips into a small valley.
The hillside dips down to the edge of the lake.
The road dips sharply just before the bridge.
He quickly dipped his flag as the procession passed by.
Just before the final bow, she dipped her head in respect.
The dancer dipped her knee gracefully during the performance.
As the road curved, it seemed to dip out of sight for a moment.
Temperatures are expected to dip below freezing tonight.
The airplane began to dip gently as it approached the runway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lower - to move something to a position below what it was before
  • antonym: raise - to move something to a higher position
  • antonym: rise - to move upwards or slope upwards
  • synonym: sink - to go down below the surface or to a lower position
