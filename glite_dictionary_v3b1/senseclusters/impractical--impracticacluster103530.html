impractical

adjective
not workable; not practical
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
not sensible, realistic, or likely to work in practice; not suitable for practical use

Examples

An impractical idea
Buying such an expensive car is impractical given your budget.
His impractical ideas often clash with the realities of business.
The plan was interesting but completely impractical for our small team.
Buying such an expensive car is completely impractical for a family on a tight budget.
His plan to commute by helicopter was exciting but totally impractical.
She often dismisses impractical ideas that can't be put into action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - sensible and realistic; able to be done or used
  • synonym: unworkable - not able to be done or to work successfully
