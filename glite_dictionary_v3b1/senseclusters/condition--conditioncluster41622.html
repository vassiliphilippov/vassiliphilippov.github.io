condition

verb
train to respond in a certain way
2.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
to train or influence a person or animal to behave in a certain way, usually by repeated association or reinforcement

Examples

To condition a dog
Researchers condition mice to press a lever in exchange for food.
The dog was conditioned to sit at the sound of a bell.
Years of advertising have conditioned consumers to expect a sale every holiday season.
Children can be conditioned to follow routines through positive reinforcement.
The trainer used rewards to condition the dog to sit quietly during meals.
Years of strict practice conditioned her to respond calmly under pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discipline - to teach someone to obey rules or a code of behavior, using punishment to correct disobedience
  • synonym: train - teach a person or animal a particular skill or type of behavior through practice and instruction
