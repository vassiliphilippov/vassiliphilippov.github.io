bioenergy

noun
energy in living organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the energy produced, stored, or used by living organisms through biochemical processes

Examples

Cell bioenergy
Scientists study bioenergy to understand how cells power their activities.
The mitochondria are often called the powerhouses of the cell because they generate bioenergy.
Advances in bioenergy research could lead to new medical treatments for metabolic diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biochemical energy - energy produced by chemical processes in living things
  • synonym: chemical energy - energy stored in the bonds of chemical compounds
