buttonhole

noun
hole for button in fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌtnˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbʌtnˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small slit or hole in fabric, usually reinforced, through which a button is passed to fasten clothing or other items

Examples

A shirt buttonhole
Make sure each buttonhole is large enough for the button to pass through.
She carefully sewed a buttonhole on the new shirt to match the buttons.
The coat was missing a buttonhole, so it couldn't be fastened properly.
Her shirt was missing a buttonhole, so she couldn't fasten the top button.
If your coat buttonhole is torn, a seamstress can usually repair it easily.
The tailor carefully stitched each buttonhole by hand for a neat finish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: button - a small fastener, typically round, used for joining two parts of a garment
