rejection

noun
the act of refusing or dismissing
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛkʃən /; / rɪˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkʃən /; / rɪˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of refusing to accept, believe, approve, or agree with something or someone; being refused or dismissed

Examples

Despite many rejections, he remained determined to pursue his dreams.
His application for the scholarship ended in rejection.
She faced rejection after sending her manuscript to several publishers.
After facing rejection from several publishers, she finally found one willing to take a chance.
He took the rejection hard, but it motivated him to work even harder.
The fear of rejection can sometimes prevent people from expressing their true feelings.
After her job interview, she received a formal rejection letter from the company.
His proposal met with immediate rejection from the committee.
She was hurt by the rejection of her ideas during the meeting.
