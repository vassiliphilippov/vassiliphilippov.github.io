bell buoy

noun
navigational buoy with a bell
Pronunciation: / bɛl ˈbu.i / (AmE), / bɛl ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
A buoy equipped with a bell that sounds as the buoy moves, used as a navigational aid to warn boats and ships.

Examples

A bell buoy ringing
A bell buoy marked the entrance to the harbor, ringing softly as the waves rocked it.
Sailing at night, we listened for the sound of the bell buoy to guide us through the fog.
The coast guard checked the bell buoys regularly to ensure their bells were functioning properly.
