American basswood

noun
a large North American shade tree
9.4
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛɹ.ɪ.kən ˈbæs.wʊd / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹ.ɪ.kən ˈbæs.wʊd / (BrE)
A large North American tree with a broad, rounded crown and large, dark green leaves, valued for its shade and soft wood.

Examples

The American basswood tree
Many city parks plant American basswood trees to provide ample shade in the summer.
The American basswood is known for its rapid growth and graceful appearance.
Wood from the American basswood is often used for carving and making musical instruments.

Related Terms
