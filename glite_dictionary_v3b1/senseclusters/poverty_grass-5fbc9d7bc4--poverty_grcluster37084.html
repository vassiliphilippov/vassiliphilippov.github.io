poverty grass

noun
salt-tolerant coastal plant of northeastern North America
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːvərti ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈpɒvəti ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a small heathlike plant covered with white down, typically found growing on beaches in northeastern North America.

Examples

A patch of poverty grass
A patch of poverty grass grew along the sandy dunes by the sea.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats where poverty grass thrives.
The pale color and fuzzy texture make poverty grass easy to identify among the coastal vegetation.
