ineffectively

adverb
in an unsuccessful manner
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ɪ.ˈfek.tɪv.lɪ/ /; / /ɪn.ə.ˈfek.tɪv.lɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ɪ.ˈfek.tɪv.lɪ/ /; / /ɪn.ə.ˈfek.tɪv.lɪ/ (BrE)
In a way that does not produce the intended result or is not successful.

Examples

To explain ineffectively
She tried to explain her point, but did so ineffectively and was misunderstood.
The medicine was administered ineffectively, so the patient did not improve.
The new policy was implemented ineffectively, resulting in confusion among staff.
