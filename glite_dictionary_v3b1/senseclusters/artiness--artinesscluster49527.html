artiness

noun
affected artistic style
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrtinəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːtinəs / (BrE)
the quality of appearing overly artistic or affected in style, often in a way that seems insincere or pretentious

Examples

Unnecessary artiness
The director was accused of adding unnecessary artiness to the film's visuals.
Some critics praised the novel's style, while others complained about its artiness.
He tried to impress the audience, but his artiness felt forced and unnatural.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real
  • synonym: pretentiousness - the quality of trying to appear more important or artistic than is actually the case
