excitatory

adjective
causing excitation or stimulation
8.2 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªkËˆsaÉªtÉ™ËŒtÉ”ri / (AmE), / ÉªkËˆsaÉªtÉ™tÉ™ri / (BrE)
(of a substance, nerve, or stimulus) causing or able to cause excitation or stimulation, especially in nerve cells or muscles.

Examples

Excitatory nerve signals
In biological research, scientists study how excitatory signals lead to muscle contraction.
Some neurotransmitters have an excitatory effect on the nervous system, increasing activity in the brain.
The drug acts as an excitatory agent, stimulating the patient's heart rate.

Synonyms

  • excitative
    9.9 0.01/m
    excitative describes a drug or agent that produces biological stimulation, while excitatory focuses on nerves or signals causing muscle or nerve activity.
  • excitant
    9.9 0.00/m
    excitant names a substance that raises body or mind activity, while excitatory labels signals that cause nerve or muscle action.
  • stimulatory
    6.9 0.12/m
    stimulatory describes effects that increase activity in tissues or organs, while excitatory specifically names signals that trigger nerves or muscles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibitory - causing or able to cause inhibition (opposite of excitatory)
