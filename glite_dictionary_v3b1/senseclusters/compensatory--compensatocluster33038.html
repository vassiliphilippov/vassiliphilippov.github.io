compensatory

adjective
making up for loss
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɛnsəˌtɔri / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛnsətəri / (BrE)
given or intended to make up for a loss, damage, or disadvantage

Examples

Compensatory time off
The company offered compensatory time off to employees who worked during the holiday.
She received a compensatory payment after her luggage was lost by the airline.
Extra lessons were provided as a compensatory measure for students who missed class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: punitive - intended as punishment
  • synonym: reparative - serving to repair or make amends
