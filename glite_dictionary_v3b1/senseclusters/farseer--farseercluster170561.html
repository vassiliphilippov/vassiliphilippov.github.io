farseer

noun
person who predicts future
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑrˌsiːər /; / ˈfɑrˌsɪr / (AmE), / ˈfɑːˌsɪə / (BrE)
a person who predicts or claims to predict the future, either by supernatural ability or by skill and insight

Examples

The farseer predicts
The villagers trusted the farseer to guide them through difficult times.
Many stories tell of a farseer who warned the king of coming danger.
People traveled from distant lands to consult the famous farseer about their futures.
The company's founder was considered a farseer for predicting trends years before they happened.
Many looked to the farseer for guidance during uncertain times.
Her reputation as a farseer grew after she accurately forecasted several major industry changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the future
  • antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and deals with it accordingly
  • synonym: seer - a person who is supposed to be able to see the future
  • antonym: skeptic - a person who doubts or questions claims, especially about the supernatural
  • synonym: visionary - a person with original ideas about what the future will or could be like
