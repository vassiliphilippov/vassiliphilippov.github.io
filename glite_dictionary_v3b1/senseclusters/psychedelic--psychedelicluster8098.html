psychedelic

noun
hallucinogenic drug
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (BrE)
a drug that produces changes in perception, mood, and thought, often causing hallucinations

Examples

Take a psychedelic
The artist claimed that using a psychedelic inspired many of his most famous paintings.
Researchers are studying the effects of a psychedelic on mental health conditions.
Some people seek spiritual experiences by taking a psychedelic in controlled settings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hallucinogen - a substance that causes hallucinations
  • antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases alertness or energy
