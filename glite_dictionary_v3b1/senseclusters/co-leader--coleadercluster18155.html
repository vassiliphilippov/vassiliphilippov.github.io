co-leader

adjective
shared leadership
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌliːdər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌliːdə / (BrE)
serving jointly as a leader with one or more other people; shared leadership role

Examples

Co-leader role
The co-leader approach helped the team make decisions more collaboratively.
They introduced a co-leader model to ensure equal responsibility in the project.
A co-leader structure can prevent power from being concentrated in one person.

Related Terms

  • synonym: joint - involving two or more people together
  • antonym: sole - being the only one; single
