clothesline

noun
cord for hanging clothes to dry
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊzˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkləʊðzˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A strong cord, rope, or wire fixed between two points, on which clothes are hung to dry after washing.

Examples

A clothesline outside
A sudden rainstorm drenched all the shirts he had left on the clothesline.
She hung the wet sheets on the clothesline in the backyard to dry.
The old clothesline stretched between two wooden poles behind the house.

Related Terms
