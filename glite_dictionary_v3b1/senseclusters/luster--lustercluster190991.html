Synonyms

  • gloss
    3.8 2/m
    gloss is a smooth, shiny surface finish used on pottery or paint, while luster describes a soft, natural shine on materials.
  • sheen
    4.0 0.06/m
    sheen gives a soft, thin shine that catches light across a surface, while luster suggests a deeper, richer glow.
  • shininess
    2.0 0.03/m
    shininess names the general quality of reflecting light and looking bright, while luster implies a refined, often softer surface glow.
  • shine
    1.0 20/m
    shine means to emit or reflect bright light or polish, while luster refers to a softer, more subtle glossy appearance on objects.
  • glow
    1.2 14/m
    glow is an ongoing warm light often from heat or chemistry, while luster is a surface shine that reflects light gently.
