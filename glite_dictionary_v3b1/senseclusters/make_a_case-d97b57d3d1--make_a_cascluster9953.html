make a case

verb
present arguments for something
Pronunciation: / meɪk ə keɪs / (AmE), / meɪk ə keɪs / (BrE)
to present arguments or reasoning in support of something

Examples

To make a case for change
During the meeting, she tried to make a case for investing in new technology.
He skillfully made a case against the proposed changes in his report.
If you want the board’s approval, you need to make a case with clear evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: argue - to give reasons for or against something
  • antonym: oppose - to put forward reasons against something
