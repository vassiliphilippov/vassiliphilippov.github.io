sucker

noun
a gullible or easily deceived person
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌkɚ /; / ˈsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or tricked

Examples

A sucker for scams
Don’t be a sucker and fall for online scams.
He’s such a sucker—he believed every word of their story.
There’s a sucker born every minute, as the saying goes.
Don’t be a sucker; always read the fine print before signing anything.
He’s such a sucker for get-rich-quick schemes that friends worry about him.
Only a sucker would pay that much for a used car.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cynic - a person who believes people are motivated purely by self-interest
  • synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked or deceived
  • synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • synonym: victim - a person who is harmed or tricked in a deceptive act
