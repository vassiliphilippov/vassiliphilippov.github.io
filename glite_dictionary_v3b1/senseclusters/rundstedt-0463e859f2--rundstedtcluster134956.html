Rundstedt

proper noun
German field marshal (1875–1953)
Pronunciation: / ˈrʊnʃtɛt / (AmE), / ˈrʊnʃtɛt / (BrE)
(1875–1953) German field marshal during World War II, known for directing the conquest of Poland and leading the Ardennes counteroffensive.

Examples

Rundstedt in command
During the Battle of the Bulge, Hitler placed Rundstedt in command of the western front.
Field Marshal Rundstedt played a key role in the German invasion of Poland in 1939.
Historians often debate the military strategies employed by Rundstedt during World War II.
