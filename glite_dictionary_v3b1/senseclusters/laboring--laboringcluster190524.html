laboring

adjective
doing arduous or unpleasant work
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) engaged in arduous or unpleasant work, especially physical labor.

Examples

Laboring worker
Her father spent his life as a laboring worker in the steel mills.
The laboring men gathered in the fields before sunrise, ready for a long day.
You could see groups of laboring peasants tending to the land under the hot sun.
Many labouring miners toiled deep underground to extract coal for the community.
She admired the resilience of labouring mothers working tirelessly to support their families.
The labouring workers spent hours in the fields every day under the hot sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drudging - engaged in tedious, menial, or unpleasant work
  • antonym: idle - not working or active
  • synonym: toiling - engaged in hard, continuous work
