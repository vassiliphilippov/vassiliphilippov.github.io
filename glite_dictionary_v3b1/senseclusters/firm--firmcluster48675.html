firm

adjective, adverb
resolute; unwavering in purpose or belief
Pronunciation: / fɝːm /; / fɝm / (AmE), / fɜːm / (BrE)
showing strong determination or not changing your mind; resolute and unwavering

Examples

Firm decision
Even under pressure, she remained firm in her beliefs.
His firm decision gave the whole team confidence.
The principal was firm about enforcing the new rules.
He remained firm in his loyalty to the cause, even when others lost hope.
She has always been a firm friend to those in need, never wavering in her support.
The team is looking for firm allies who will not abandon them in difficult times.
Despite criticism, she stood firm in her decision.
The team remained firm in its resolve to improve.
You must hold firm to your principles, even under pressure.
