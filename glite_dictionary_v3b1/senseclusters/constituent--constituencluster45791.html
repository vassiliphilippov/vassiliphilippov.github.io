constituent

noun
part of a grammatical structure
Pronunciation: / kənˈstɪtʃuənt / (AmE), / kənˈstɪtjuːənt / (BrE)
(Grammar) A word, phrase, or clause that forms part of a larger grammatical structure.

Examples

Sentence constituent
A noun phrase can act as a single constituent within larger sentences.
Linguists often analyze how each constituent functions within complex grammatical structures.
The subject and predicate are each a constituent of the sentence.
