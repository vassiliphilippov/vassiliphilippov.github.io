world-class

adjective
of the highest, international standard
Pronunciation: / wɜrld klæs / (AmE), / wɜːld klɑːs / (BrE)
Of the highest quality; ranking among the best in the world.

Examples

A world-class expert
He is considered a world-class expert in artificial intelligence.
Her performance was truly world-class and received praise from critics everywhere.
The city aspires to develop a world-class transportation system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good
  • synonym: outstanding - exceptionally good
