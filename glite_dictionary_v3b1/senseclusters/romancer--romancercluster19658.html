romancer

noun
idealizer of facts
Pronunciation: / roʊˈmænsər / (AmE), / rəʊˈmænsə / (BrE)
a person who presents facts or events in an idealized, exaggerated, or romanticized way

Examples

A dreamy romancer
As a romancer, she always described her childhood as a magical adventure.
The journalist was criticized for being a romancer rather than reporting the facts accurately.
His reputation as a romancer made people question the truth of his stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realist - a person who accepts and deals with things as they really are
  • synonym: romanticizer - a person who makes something seem more attractive or ideal than it really is
