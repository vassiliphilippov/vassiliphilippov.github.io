manufacture

verb
to fabricate or invent (falsely)
Pronunciation: / ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃə / (BrE)
to invent or fabricate (a story, excuse, evidence, etc.), especially with the intent to deceive; to concoct

Examples

To manufacture a story
He attempted to manufacture an alibi to avoid suspicion.
Politicians sometimes manufacture stories to gain public support.
The witness was accused of trying to manufacture evidence in her testimony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrive - to devise or invent, often in a deceitful way
  • synonym: fabricate - to invent or concoct something false
  • synonym: fake - to forge or create something false
