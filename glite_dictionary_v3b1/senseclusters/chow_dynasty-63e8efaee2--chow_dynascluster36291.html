Chow dynasty

proper noun
Chinese dynasty, 1122–221 BC
9.6
Pronunciation: / tʃaʊ ˈdaɪnəsti / (AmE), / tʃaʊ ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from about 1122 to 221 BC, known for the rise of Confucianism and Taoism

Examples

The Chow dynasty rule
Chinese bronze art reached new heights under the Chow dynasty.
Many philosophical schools flourished during the Chow dynasty, including Confucianism and Taoism.
The Chow dynasty established lasting traditions in Chinese government and society.
