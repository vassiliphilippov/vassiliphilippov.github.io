ascidian tadpole

noun
larval stage of an ascidian resembling a tadpole
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪdiən ˈtædˌpoʊl / (AmE), / əˈsɪdiən ˈtædpəʊl / (BrE)
the free-swimming larval stage of an ascidian, characterized by a tail containing a notochord, resembling a tadpole

Examples

The ascidian tadpole larva
During this stage, the ascidian tadpole exhibits a notochord in its tail, similar to vertebrates.
Researchers studied the development of the ascidian tadpole to better understand chordate evolution.
The ascidian tadpole swims freely before settling on a surface to become a sessile adult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tadpole - larval stage of an amphibian (such as a frog or toad)
