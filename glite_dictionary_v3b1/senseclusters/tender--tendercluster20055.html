tender

adjective
(of plants) not hardy or easily damaged
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndər / (AmE), / ˈtendə / (BrE)
(of plants) not hardy; easily damaged or killed by cold or other adverse growing conditions.

Examples

Tender plants
Early spring frosts can easily kill tender plants in the garden.
Many tropical flowers are too tender to survive in temperate climates.
You should bring all tender seedlings indoors before the first frost.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hardy - (of plants) able to withstand difficult conditions; robust
