tetrodotoxin

noun
powerful nerve toxin from pufferfish
Pronunciation: / tɛˌtroʊdəˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / tɛˌtrɒdəˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a potent neurotoxin found especially in the organs of pufferfish, certain newts, and other animals, known for blocking nerve signals

Examples

Tetrodotoxin poison
Pufferfish must be prepared carefully because they contain tetrodotoxin, which is extremely dangerous to humans.
Scientists are studying tetrodotoxin for its potential use in pain relief medications.
The presence of tetrodotoxin in newts and pufferfish acts as a defense mechanism against predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neurotoxin - a toxin that acts on nerve cells
