cosmos

noun
the universe as an ordered whole
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑzmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɒs / (BrE)
the universe as an ordered whole, or any system seen as orderly and harmonious

Examples

The cosmos is vast
Many philosophers have pondered the origins of the cosmos.
She gazed up at the night sky, feeling small in the vast cosmos.
The telescope allowed scientists to look deeper into the cosmos than ever before.
Scientists study the cosmos to understand the origins of the universe.
Many philosophers have wondered about humanity's place in the vast cosmos.
The night sky gives us a glimpse into the mysteries of the cosmos.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion (often contrasted with 'cosmos' as order)
  • synonym: universe - all of space, time, and everything contained in it
