Phanariot

noun
Ottoman-era Greek ruler
Pronunciation: / fəˈnæriət / (AmE), / fəˈnæriət / (BrE)
a member of a prominent Greek family from the Phanar district of Istanbul, influential in the Ottoman Empire, especially as rulers or administrators

Examples

A Phanariot ruler
The Ottoman Empire often appointed a Phanariot to govern the principality of Moldavia.
Many Phanariots were influential in the administration of Wallachia during the 18th century.
After his term ended, the Phanariot returned to Constantinople to serve in the imperial court.
The Phanariots were known for their influence in the Ottoman court and administration.
Many Phanariot families held important positions as diplomats and governors in the empire.
The legacy of the Phanariot class can still be seen in the history of modern Greece and Turkey.
