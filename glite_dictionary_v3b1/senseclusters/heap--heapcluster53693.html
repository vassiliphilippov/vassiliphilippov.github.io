heap

verb
put in a pile or mass
2.4 1.97/m
Pronunciation: / hip /; / hiÀêp / (AmE), / hiÀêp / (BrE)
to put things in a pile or mass, or to pile up a large amount of something

Examples

To heap dirty clothes
She heaped all the dirty clothes in the corner of the room.
The workers heaped sand on the site to prepare for construction.
They heap leaves in the yard every autumn before raking them.
At the buffet, he heaped his plate with desserts of every kind.
She heaped the bowl with steaming rice until it nearly spilled over.
The children heaped their arms full of autumn leaves to bring inside.
She stood in the kitchen heaping chopped vegetables into the pan for the stew.
He heaped praise on his team after their successful project.
The children enjoyed heaping sand into a tall mound at the beach.

Synonyms

  • pile up
    3.1 4.96/m
    pile up lets things gather or makes a loose pile over time, while heap means to pile things into a mass or large amount.
  • stack
    1.6 11/m
    stack arranges things neatly, usually one on top of another, while heap means to put items into a loose pile or mass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pile - to place things in a heap or stack
  • antonym: scatter - to throw or spread things over a wide area
  • synonym: stack - to arrange things in a neat pile, especially in layers
