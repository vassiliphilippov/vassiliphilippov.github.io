senega

noun
North American perennial herb
5.2 9.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈniː.gə/ /; / /ˈse.nɪ.gə/ (AmE), /sə.ˈniː.gə/ /; / /ˈse.nɪ.gə/ (BrE)
A perennial bushy herb native to central and southern United States, known for its white flowers with green centers and often a purple crest; also called Seneca snakeroot.

Examples

Senega plant
Senega is often harvested in the summer when its flowers are in full bloom.
Some herbal remedies contain the powdered root of senega for treating respiratory conditions.
The white, green-centered flowers of senega make it easily identifiable in the wild.
