free energy

noun
thermodynamic capacity to do work
Pronunciation: / fri ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / fri ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
A thermodynamic quantity representing the capacity of a system to do work, usually measured in joules or ergs.

Examples

Gibbs free energy
Gibbs free energy is an important concept in chemical thermodynamics.
Physicists calculate free energy to determine whether a reaction will occur spontaneously.
The decrease in free energy indicates that the system has become more stable.

  • synonym: energy - capacity for doing work or producing change
