militant

adjective
aggressively combative or confrontational
1.6 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (BrE)
aggressively active, combative, or confrontational in support of a cause or belief

Examples

Militant protest tactics
His militant approach to union negotiations often led to heated arguments.
She took a militant stance on animal rights, demanding immediate action from lawmakers.
The militant protesters refused to back down despite police warnings.
Her militant attitude toward environmental protection inspired others to join the movement.
Some militant groups advocate violence as a means of achieving their objectives.
The government adopted a militant stance against its rivals, refusing all diplomatic negotiations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront; forceful
  • antonym: peaceful - not involving conflict or violence; calm
  • synonym: warlike - inclined to or ready for war
