proliferous

adjective
producing new buds
Pronunciation: / prəˈlɪfərəs / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfərəs / (BrE)
producing new individuals, buds, or shoots, or showing rapid and abundant growth or reproduction, especially in plants

Examples

Proliferous plant growth
The proliferous plant quickly spread across the garden bed.
Some species of moss are highly proliferous, covering rocks and trees in a short time.
Gardeners appreciate proliferous flowers for their ability to fill spaces rapidly.
The proliferous weeds quickly took over the entire garden within a few weeks.
Her proliferous creativity led to dozens of new paintings each year.
The proliferous spread of technology has changed the way people communicate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barren - not producing or incapable of producing offspring or growth
  • synonym: prolific - producing many works, results, or offspring
  • synonym: reproductive - able to produce offspring or new growth
  • antonym: sterile - unable to produce offspring or new growth
