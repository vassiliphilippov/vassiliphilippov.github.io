draft

noun
airflow regulator in a fireplace or stove
7.3 3.1
Pronunciation: / dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
a current of air or a device that controls the flow of air (especially in a room, fireplace, or stove)

Examples

Cold draft in the room

If you forget to close the draft, the room will quickly fill with cold air.
The old stove’s draft was stuck open, causing too much smoke.
A draft from the hallway made the candle flicker.

There was a chilly draft coming in under the door.
A cold draught made everyone in the room reach for their sweaters.
She complained about the draught coming in through the old window.
The fire burned brighter when a draught blew down the chimney.
