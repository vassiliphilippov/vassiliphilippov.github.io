executorship

noun
role of executor
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛkjətərʃɪp / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or responsibility of being an executor, managing the estate of a deceased person

Examples

The executorship of estate
She accepted the executorship after her uncle passed away.
The court confirmed his executorship of the estate last month.
Taking on the executorship can be a complex and time-consuming responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: administratorship - the position of managing an estate when there is no will
  • synonym: executor - a person appointed to carry out the terms of a will
