overwash

verb
to flow over
6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.wɑːʃ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.wɔːʃ/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.wɒʃ/ (BrE)
to flow or wash over the top of something, especially as water or waves covering land or a barrier

Examples

Waves overwash a road
During the hurricane, waves began to overwash the coastal road, making it impassable.
Engineers built a higher wall to prevent the river from overwashing the fields during storms.
If the tide rises quickly, it can overwash the sand dunes and damage the vegetation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flood - to cover an area with water, especially beyond normal limits
  • synonym: submerge - to cover completely with water
