Stevens' law

noun
psychophysics law relating sensation to stimulus intensity
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈstiː.vənz lɔː/ /; / /ˈstiː.vənz lɑː/ (AmE), /ˈstiː.vənz lɔː/ (BrE)
a principle in psychophysics stating that the perceived magnitude of a sensation increases as a power function of the stimulus intensity

Examples

Stevens' law in psychophysics
According to Stevens' law, the relationship between sound intensity and loudness is not linear.
Many psychology textbooks discuss Stevens' law when introducing sensory perception theories.
Researchers often compare Stevens' law and Fechner's law in experimental settings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Fechner's law - a related psychophysical law linking sensation and stimulus using a logarithmic function
