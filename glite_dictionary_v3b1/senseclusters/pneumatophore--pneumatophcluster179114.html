pneumatophore

noun
air-filled root used for respiration in some plants
6.7
Pronunciation: / nuˈmætəˌfɔr / (AmE), / njuːˈmatəˌfɔː / (BrE)
A specialized root, often found in marsh or swamp plants, that is air-filled and serves as a respiratory organ when submerged or exposed.

Examples

Swamp pneumatophores
Many swamp plants develop pneumatophores as an adaptation to low-oxygen environments.
Scientists observed the pneumatophore as it supplied oxygen to the submerged parts of the plant.
The mangrove tree produces pneumatophores that stick out of the muddy ground to help it breathe.
