delocalization

noun
loss of local identity
Pronunciation: / diˌloʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a community loses its local identity, ties, or cultural distinctiveness.

Examples

Cultural delocalization
Rapid delocalization has changed the character of many small towns in recent decades.
Some residents worry that delocalization will erase their unique cultural traditions.
The festival was created to resist delocalization and celebrate local heritage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: globalization - the process by which businesses or cultures become international
  • antonym: localization - the process of adapting or preserving local identity or culture
