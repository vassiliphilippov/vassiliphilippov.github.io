ledger line

noun
short line above/below staff for extra notes
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒə laɪn / (BrE)
a short line written above or below the staff in musical notation to extend its range for higher or lower notes

Examples

Ledger line in music
Beginners often struggle to read notes written on a ledger line.
That note is so high it needs a ledger line above the staff.
The composer used several ledger lines to notate the low bass passage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: staff - set of five parallel lines in musical notation
