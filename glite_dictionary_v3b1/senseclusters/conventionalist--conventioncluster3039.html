conventionalist

noun
believer in conventions
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that fundamental principles or truths are based on social agreement or convention rather than objective reality

Examples

A conventionalist philosopher
The philosopher was known as a conventionalist who argued that moral rules are shaped by society.
As a conventionalist, she believed that scientific laws depend on human agreement.
Many debates in linguistics involve the views of a conventionalist versus those of a realist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realist - a person who believes that truths exist independently of human perception or agreement
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports established customs or practices
