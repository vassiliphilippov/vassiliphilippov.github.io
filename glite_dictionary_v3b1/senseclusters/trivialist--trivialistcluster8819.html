trivialist

noun
believer in universal truth
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪviəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈtrɪviəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that every statement is true, including contradictions

Examples

A logical trivialist
A trivialist would accept both a claim and its opposite as true.
Philosophers rarely encounter a true trivialist in academic debates.
The idea of a trivialist challenges traditional views of logic and truth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paraconsistent - a person who accepts some contradictions without accepting all statements as true
  • antonym: realist - a person who believes that some statements are true and others are false
