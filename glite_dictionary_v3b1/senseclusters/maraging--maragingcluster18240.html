maraging

adjective
nickel-aged steel
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrˌeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːrˌeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
describing steel that is strengthened by aging after being alloyed with nickel

Examples

Maraging steel parts
The engineer recommended using maraging steel for the high-stress components of the machine.
Because of its strength, maraging steel is often chosen for aerospace applications.
The tools made from maraging alloys lasted much longer than those made from regular steel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardened - made hard or stronger, especially by heat treatment
  • antonym: soft - not hard or strong in structure
