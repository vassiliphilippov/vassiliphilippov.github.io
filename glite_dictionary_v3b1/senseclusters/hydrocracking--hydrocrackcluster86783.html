hydrocracking

noun
chemical process in oil refining
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊˈkrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈkrækɪŋ / (BrE)
a chemical process in petroleum refining where hydrocarbon molecules are broken down into lighter products, such as kerosene and gasoline, by adding hydrogen under high pressure with a catalyst

Examples

Hydrocracking process
Advances in hydrocracking have improved the efficiency of gasoline production.
During hydrocracking, large hydrocarbon molecules are transformed into smaller, more useful fuels.
The refinery's new hydrocracking unit allows it to convert heavier oils into valuable products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalytic cracking - chemical process to break down hydrocarbons using a catalyst, without hydrogen
