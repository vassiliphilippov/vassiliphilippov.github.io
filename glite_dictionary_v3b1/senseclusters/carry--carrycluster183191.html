carry

verb
involve as a result; entail
3.7 1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæɹi /; / ˈkæri / (AmE), / ˈkæɹi /; / ˈkæri / (BrE)
to have or possess an idea, feeling, memory, responsibility, mark, or feature in your mind, life, or on your body

Examples

To carry a memory
Any investment carries some level of risk.
The new law carries heavy fines for those who break it.
This surgery carries a small chance of complications.
Her smooth skin carried no hint of the illness she had overcome.
That painting carries the marks of centuries of neglect.
The old soldier still carries the scars of battle on his face.
Despite the years that have passed, she carried the pain with her.
He carries a tremendous sense of duty to his family.
She will always carry fond memories of her childhood wherever she goes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bear - to hold or carry something, especially responsibility or emotion
  • synonym: contain - to have something inside or as part of it
  • synonym: convey - to communicate or make known
  • synonym: entail - to involve something as a necessary part or result
  • antonym: exclude - to deliberately not involve or include something
  • antonym: lack - to be without, to not have
