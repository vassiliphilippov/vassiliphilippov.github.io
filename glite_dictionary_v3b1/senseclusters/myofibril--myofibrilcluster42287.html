myofibril

noun
contractile thread in muscle fiber
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.oʊˈfaɪ.brəl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈfaɪ.brɪl /; / ˌmʌɪ.ə(ʊ)ˈfɪ.brɪl / (BrE)
any of the elongated contractile threads found in striated muscle cells, responsible for muscle contraction

Examples

Myofibrils in muscle
Damage to myofibrils can lead to muscle weakness and fatigue.
Each muscle fiber contains hundreds of myofibrils that enable it to contract.
Under a microscope, myofibril structures appear as alternating light and dark bands.
Damage to the myofibrils can reduce a muscle's ability to contract.
The striated appearance of skeletal muscle is due to the arrangement of myofibrils.
Under a microscope, each muscle cell contains hundreds of myofibrils.
