annul

verb
declare legally invalid, cancel officially
6.3 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / É™ËˆnÊŒl / (AmE), / É™ËˆnÊŒl / (BrE)
to officially declare that something (such as a law, contract, or marriage) is no longer valid or in effect

Examples

To annul a contract
The board can annul any regulation that conflicts with state law.
The court decided to annul the previous judgment due to new evidence.
They are seeking to annul their marriage in court.
If either party breaches the agreement, we may annul the contract.
The court decided to annul the marriage due to fraud.
The government moved quickly to annul the disputed election results.

Synonyms

  • nullify
    7.2 1.23/m
    nullify cancels something's legal effect completely, while annul often means a formal declaration that it is not valid.
  • void
    7.1 1.33/m
    void declares a document or agreement legally without force, while annul names it officially invalid and often treats it as never valid.
  • invalidate
    6.1 2.77/m
    invalidate makes something legally or formally not valid, while annul is a formal declaration that ends its legal effect.
  • rescind
    7.9 2.09/m
    rescind cancels an order or agreement often by the same authority, while annul states it is legally void from the start.
  • revoke
    6.2 3.96/m
    revoke takes back a right, license, or decision, while annul declares a law or contract legally invalid from its origin.
  • vacate
    6.6 0.77/m
    vacate cancels a court judgment so it no longer stands, while annul more often declares the matter legally never valid.
  • quash
    8.1 0.70/m
    quash rejects or throws out a specific court ruling or charge, while annul declares the legal act itself invalid.
  • overturn
    5.6 5.85/m
    overturn reverses a decision or judgment by a higher body, while annul declares the act legally void rather than only reversed.
  • repeal
    4.3 9.24/m
    repeal ends a law through a legislature vote, while annul can be used by courts to declare an act invalid.
  • abrogate
    8.8 0.95/m
    abrogate formally abolishes a law or treaty, often in formal texts, while annul is a broader official declaration of invalidity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invalidate - to make something no longer legally valid
  • antonym: ratify - to make an agreement officially valid
  • synonym: revoke - to officially cancel a law, order, or agreement
  • antonym: validate - to confirm or make legally valid
  • synonym: void - to declare something to have no legal force
