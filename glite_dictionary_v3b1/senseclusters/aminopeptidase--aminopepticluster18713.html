aminopeptidase

noun
protein-digesting enzyme
8.9
Pronunciation: / əˌminoʊˈpɛptɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˈpɛptɪdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that removes amino acids from the amino end of a protein or peptide chain

Examples

aminopeptidase enzyme activity
The doctor explained that aminopeptidase helps break down proteins in the digestive system.
Researchers measured the activity of aminopeptidase in different tissue samples.
A deficiency in aminopeptidase can affect how the body processes certain proteins.
