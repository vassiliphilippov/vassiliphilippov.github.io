subalternity

noun
logical implication relation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˌɔlˈtɜrnɪti / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːlˈtɜːnɪti / (BrE)
the logical relationship in which a universal statement implies the truth of a corresponding particular statement in traditional logic

Examples

Logical subalternity
In classical logic, subalternity explains how 'All dogs are mammals' implies 'Some dogs are mammals'.
The professor illustrated subalternity using diagrams of universal and particular propositions.
Understanding subalternity is essential for analyzing syllogistic arguments in philosophy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contradiction - a logical relationship where two statements cannot both be true
  • synonym: subalternation - the process by which a universal statement implies a particular statement
