tetrameric

adjective
having four parts
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
consisting of four subunits or parts, especially referring to the structure of proteins or molecules

Examples

Tetrameric protein structure
Hemoglobin is a tetrameric protein found in red blood cells.
The enzyme functions as a tetrameric complex, with each subunit playing a specific role.
Researchers discovered that the receptor is tetrameric rather than dimeric, which changes how it interacts with other molecules.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dimeric - consisting of two subunits or parts
  • degree higher: pentameric - consisting of five subunits or parts
  • degree lower: trimeric - consisting of three subunits or parts
