iodotyrosine

noun
tyrosine molecule with added iodine
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.ə.doʊ.taɪˈr.oʊ.sin / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əʊ.dəʊ.taɪˈrəʊ.siːn / (BrE)
An amino acid derivative formed when a tyrosine molecule has iodine atoms added, occurring as an intermediate in the synthesis of thyroid hormones.

Examples

An iodotyrosine molecule
A deficiency in enzymes processing iodotyrosine can lead to thyroid problems.
During thyroid hormone synthesis, iodotyrosine acts as a key intermediate.
Researchers measured the concentration of iodotyrosine in the thyroid tissue samples.
