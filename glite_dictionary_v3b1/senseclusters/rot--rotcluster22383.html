rot

noun
biological decay or decomposition
4.0
Pronunciation: / rɑt / (AmE), / rɒt / (BrE)
decay or decomposition of organic matter, especially caused by bacteria or fungi

Examples

Wood rot
Bread left out in the open will quickly develop rot due to mold and bacteria.
The farmer checked his crops for any signs of rot after the flooding.
Wet weather can cause rot in wooden beams if they are not properly protected.
He noticed signs of rot on the old vegetables left in the fridge.
Prolonged dampness can lead to rot in wooden beams.
The smell of rot in the cellar was overwhelming.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposition
  • antonym: freshness - the quality or state of being recently made or obtained
  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something from decay
  • synonym: putrefaction - decomposition of organic matter, especially producing a foul smell
