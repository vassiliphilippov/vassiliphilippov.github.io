oxbow lake

noun
crescent-shaped lake formed by cut-off river meander
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksˌboʊ leɪk / (AmE), / ˈɒks.bəʊ leɪk / (BrE)
a crescent-shaped lake formed when a meander from a river is cut off from the main channel

Examples

An oxbow lake
After centuries, the river changed course, leaving behind a picturesque oxbow lake.
Children fished in the shallow waters of the oxbow lake during the summer months.
The old oxbow lake was a haven for migrating birds each spring.
