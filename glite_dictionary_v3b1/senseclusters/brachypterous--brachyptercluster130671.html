brachypterous

adjective
having short or rudimentary wings (of insects)
Pronunciation: / brəˈkɪp.tɚ.əs / (AmE), / brækˈɪp.tər.əs / (BrE)
(especially of certain insects) having very short or underdeveloped wings.

Examples

A brachypterous insect
Many grasshoppers in arid regions are brachypterous and cannot fly.
Some cockroaches exhibit brachypterous forms that are adapted to life without flight.
The scientist discovered a new brachypterous beetle species on the island.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macropterous - having long or fully developed wings (especially of insects)
