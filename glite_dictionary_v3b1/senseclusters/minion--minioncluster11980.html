minion

noun
servile follower or subordinate
1.7 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪn.jən/ (AmE), /ˈmɪn.jən/ (BrE)
a follower or subordinate of a powerful person, especially one who is servile or overly eager to please

Examples

A minion of a leader
His minion quickly fetched the documents and handed them over with a bow.
She treats her assistants like minions, ordering them around without any regard.
The villain surrounded himself with loyal minions to carry out his plans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boss - person who is in charge or commands others
  • synonym: lackey - a servant or someone who does menial tasks for another
