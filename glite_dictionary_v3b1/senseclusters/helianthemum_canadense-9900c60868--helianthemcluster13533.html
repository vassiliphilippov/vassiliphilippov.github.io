Helianthemum canadense

noun
wildflower with yellow blooms; frostweed
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːliˌænˈθɛməm ˌkænəˈdɛns / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˌænˈθɛməm ˌkanəˈdɛnsɪ / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to eastern North America, known for producing early yellow flowers and for ice crystals that form on its stems during the first frosts.

Examples

Helianthemum canadense yellow flowers
Botanists study Helianthemum canadense for its unusual response to the first frosts of the season.
In spring, Helianthemum canadense covers dry, sandy soils with bright yellow flowers.
The delicate stems of Helianthemum canadense glisten with frost crystals on cold autumn mornings.
