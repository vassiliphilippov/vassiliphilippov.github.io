whitewash

verb
cover up faults or wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtwɑʃ /; / ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtwɒʃ / (BrE)
to try to hide or cover up faults, errors, or unpleasant facts, often to make something seem better than it is

Examples

To whitewash a scandal
The politician tried to whitewash the scandal by releasing a vague public statement.
The report was criticized for attempting to whitewash the real issues.
We should not whitewash the company's mistakes, but address them openly.
Several officials whitewashed their involvement by blaming lower-level employees.
The committee was accused of whitewashing the investigation instead of holding anyone accountable.
The company attempted to whitewash the incident by releasing a vague and misleading report.
Politicians often attempt to whitewash their past mistakes during election campaigns.
She accused the report of trying to whitewash the organization's history of pollution.
The company tried to whitewash its environmental record by highlighting a few small recycling initiatives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a crime or mistake
  • antonym: expose - to reveal something hidden, especially wrongdoing or errors
  • synonym: gloss over - to treat or describe something as if it were not important
