Visigoth

proper noun
ancient Germanic people
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪzɪˌɡɑθ / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪˌɡɒθ / (BrE)
a member of a Germanic people who settled in parts of Europe and established a kingdom in Spain in the early Middle Ages

Examples

The Visigoths in Spain
The Visigoths played a significant role in the decline of the Western Roman Empire.
After crossing into Spain, the Visigoths established their own kingdom.
Historians study the migration of the Visigoths to understand early medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Goth - a member of an East Germanic people divided into Visigoths and Ostrogoths
  • antonym: Ostrogoth - a member of the eastern branch of the Goths
