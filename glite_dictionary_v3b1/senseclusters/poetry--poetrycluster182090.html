poetry

noun
literature in metrical form
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
Literature in metrical form, often expressing ideas and emotions through distinctive style, rhythm, and imagery.

Examples

Modern poetry
His latest book explores the boundaries between poetry and visual art.
Many people find comfort in reading poetry during difficult times.
She has been passionate about poetry since she was a child.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prose - written or spoken language in ordinary form, without metrical structure
  • synonym: verse - writing arranged with metrical rhythm, typically having rhyme
