forge

verb
make a fake copy to deceive
3.6 5.7
Pronunciation: / fɔrdʒ / (AmE), / fɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to make a copy of something, such as a document, signature, or work of art, with the intention of deceiving others

Examples

To forge a signature
Authorities arrested a group that had been forging counterfeit banknotes for years.
He was caught attempting to forge his boss's signature on the documents.
The criminal tried to forge the passport to gain access to the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authenticate - to prove or show that something is genuine
  • synonym: counterfeit - to make an exact imitation of something valuable with the intention to deceive or defraud
  • synonym: fake - to make something false in order to deceive
