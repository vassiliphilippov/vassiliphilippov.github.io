rascal

noun
dishonest or mischievous person
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈræskəl /; / ˈræs.kəl / (AmE), / ˈrɑːskəl /; / ˈrɑː.skəl / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who behaves in a mischievous or cheeky way, often playfully or harmlessly

Examples

A rascal child
She caught the little rascal sneaking cookies from the kitchen again.
That rascal stole my sandwich when I wasn't looking!
The young rascal played a prank on his teacher, but everyone laughed at his harmless mischief.
Everyone called her a rascal for playing harmless pranks on her classmates.
My little brother is a real rascal—he’s always getting into some kind of trouble.
The puppy acted like a rascal, stealing socks and hiding them under the sofa.
