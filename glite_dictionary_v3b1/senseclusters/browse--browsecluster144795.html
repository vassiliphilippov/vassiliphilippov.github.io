browse

noun
nibbling of vegetation by animals
Pronunciation: / braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
leaves, twigs, or other vegetation eaten by animals such as deer or goats

Examples

Fresh browse for deer
Fresh browse was placed in the enclosure to supplement the animals' regular diet.
Goats seek out tender browse along the hedgerows in early spring.
The deer moved slowly through the forest, stopping frequently to browse.
After the fire, new browse began to appear, attracting hungry elk.
Goats eat a variety of browse, including shrubs and low-hanging branches.
The forest provides plenty of browse during the spring for deer and other wildlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: graze - to feed on growing grass or other vegetation in a field
