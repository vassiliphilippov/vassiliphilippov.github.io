toileting

noun
using the toilet
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔɪlətɪŋ /; / ˈtɔɪlɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlətɪŋ /; / ˈtɔɪlɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of using the toilet, or helping someone (such as a child or patient) to use the toilet

Examples

Assisted toileting
Nurses often assist elderly patients with toileting to ensure their comfort and dignity.
Proper toileting routines are important for people recovering from surgery.
Children learn toileting skills as part of their early development.
The nurse provided assistance with toileting for elderly patients in the care home.
Parents often find toileting to be a challenging stage in their child's development.
Proper toileting routines can help prevent accidents and promote independence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continence - the ability to control urination and defecation
  • synonym: elimination - the process of expelling waste from the body
  • synonym: potty training - teaching a young child to use the toilet
