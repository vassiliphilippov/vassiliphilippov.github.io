heavy weather

noun
make something unnecessarily difficult
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛvi ˈwɛðər / (AmE), / ˌhɛvi ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
To make a situation more difficult or complicated than it needs to be.

Examples

Make heavy weather of tasks
Don’t make heavy weather of filling out this form—it’s quite straightforward.
He tends to make heavy weather of even the simplest tasks.
The team made heavy weather of what should have been an easy win.
