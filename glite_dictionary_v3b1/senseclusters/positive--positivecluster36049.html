positive

adjective
relating to philosophical positivism
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːz.ə.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of positivism, an approach in philosophy that asserts knowledge is exclusively derived from empirical and observable phenomena.

Examples

Positive philosophy
His arguments were deeply rooted in positive thinking as outlined by Comte.
The positive philosophy rejects metaphysical speculation in favor of observable facts.
The course focused primarily on positive methods in the social sciences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory
  • synonym: positivist - adhering to the philosophy of positivism
