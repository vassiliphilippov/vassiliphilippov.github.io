worn-out

adjective
too old or damaged from use
3.1 1.46/m
Pronunciation: / w…îrn Ààa ät / (AmE), / w…îÀên Ààa ät / (BrE)
too old or damaged from much use to be still usable; no longer useful because of being worn or exhausted

Examples

Worn-out shoes
After years of heavy use, his worn-out boots finally split at the seams.
She threw away the worn-out sofa and bought a new one.
This worn-out textbook is missing several pages and needs replacing.

Synonyms

  • battered
    3.7 0.00/m
    battered is damaged and shabby from rough use or knocks, while worn-out describes things too used or exhausted to be useful.
  • unusable
    2.3 1.12/m
    unusable means not fit to be used, while worn-out suggests something is tired or damaged by long use.
  • decrepit
    6.6 0.99/m
    decrepit looks old and weak from long age or neglect, while worn-out often means used or exhausted and no longer useful.
  • broken-down
    4.4 1.08/m
    broken-down stops working or fails, often as a machine, while worn-out means too used or tired to be useful.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battered - damaged by repeated use or blows
  • antonym: new - recently made or not yet used; the opposite of worn-out
