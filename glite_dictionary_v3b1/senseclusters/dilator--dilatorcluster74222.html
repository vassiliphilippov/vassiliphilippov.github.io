dilator

noun
drug causing dilation
3.2
Pronunciation: / daɪˈleɪtər / (AmE), / daɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A drug or agent that causes dilation of a part of the body, especially blood vessels or the pupils.

Examples

Dilator eye drops
Eye drops containing a dilator were used before the eye examination.
Some medications act as a dilator to improve blood flow in certain conditions.
The doctor administered a dilator to help open the patient's airways.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constrictor - a drug or agent that causes narrowing (constriction) of a part of the body
  • synonym: vasodilator - a drug that causes dilation of blood vessels
