bar

verb
obstruct or block passage
3.8 2.4
Pronunciation: /bɑːr/ (AmE), /bɑː/ (BrE)
to block, obstruct, or fasten something by placing a bar or similar object across it

Examples

To bar the door
A fallen tree barred the entrance to the park for several days.
Construction workers quickly barring the sidewalks to ensure no one could pass through the hazardous area.
The police barred the road to prevent protesters from reaching the city center.
Remember to bar the door when you leave for vacation.
She barred the window to keep out the wind.
The villagers hurried to bar the gates before nightfall.
