mix

verb
combine electronic signals (esp. audio)
Pronunciation: / mɪks / (AmE), / mɪks / (BrE)
to combine two or more electronic signals, such as audio tracks, to produce a single output or a new effect

Examples

To mix music tracks
During the concert, the sound technician mixed live signals on the fly.
Engineers carefully mix the vocals and instruments to create a balanced song.
She learned how to mix audio tracks for her band's new album.
