alienor

noun
person transferring property title
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààe…™li…ôÀån…îr / (AmE), / Ààe…™li…ôn…îÀê / (BrE)
A person from whom the title to property is transferred, especially in a legal transaction.

Examples

The alienor transfers property
A dispute arose when the alienor claimed not all conditions of the contract had been fulfilled.
In a sale of land, the alienor conveys the title to the buyer, known as the alienee.
The alienor must ensure that all documents are properly executed before the transfer of the property.

Synonyms

  • transferor
    8.6 0.11/m
    a transferor is the person who legally transfers title or property in a deal, while an alienor is an older legal name for that person.
  • grantor
    7.9 0.51/m
    a grantor gives property or legal rights in a formal deed, while an alienor is the legal name for the person who transfers title.
  • assignor
    9.0 0.05/m
    an assignor transfers rights or claims, often by contract, while an alienor specifically transfers property title in a legal conveyance.
