whole

noun
something complete formed by parts
1.1
Pronunciation: / hoʊl / (AmE), / həʊl / (BrE)
all of something; the entirety or a single complete unit made up of parts

Examples

The whole of it
Each chapter fits together to form the whole of the book.
The whole is often greater than the sum of its parts.
You can't judge the whole by looking at just one part.
It's more helpful to consider the company as a whole rather than as separate departments.
The whole of the book is devoted to the subject of astronomy.
They worked together to solve the problem as a whole, rather than individually.

Related Terms

  • antonym: part - a piece or segment of something considered in relation to a whole
