misattribute

verb
to assign wrongly
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪs.əˈtrɪb.juːt / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˈtrɪb.juːt / (BrE)
to wrongly say that someone is the author, creator, or source of something (such as a quote, work, or action)

Examples

To misattribute a quote
Many people misattribute that famous saying to Albert Einstein.
It is common to misattribute works of art when records are incomplete.
The journalist accidentally misattributed the quote to the wrong politician.
Many people misattribute that quote to Albert Einstein, but he never actually said it.
It is common to misattribute popular sayings to famous historical figures.
The journalist accidentally misattributed the statement to the wrong spokesperson.
It is easy to misattribute a famous quote to the wrong historical figure.
The teacher warned students not to misattribute the poem to Shakespeare.
People sometimes misattribute their feelings of anxiety to external events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attribute - to assign something to a particular person or source
  • synonym: misassign - to assign something incorrectly
