Thessalonian

proper noun
person from Thessalonica
Pronunciation: / ˌθɛsəˈloʊniən / (AmE), / ˌθɛsəˈləʊniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Thessalonica, an ancient city in Greece, or a member of the early Christian community there

Examples

Early Thessalonian Christian
The apostle Paul addressed his letters to the early Thessalonians in the New Testament.
A Thessalonian merchant traveled across the Aegean Sea to trade goods.
Many Thessalonians were known for their hospitality in ancient Greece.

  • synonym: Corinthian - a native or inhabitant of Corinth
