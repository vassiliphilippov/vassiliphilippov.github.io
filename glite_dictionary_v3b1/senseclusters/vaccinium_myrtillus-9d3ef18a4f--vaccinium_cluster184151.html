Vaccinium myrtillus

noun
European shrub bearing bilberries
Pronunciation: / vækˈsɪniəm mərˈtɪləs / (AmE), / vækˈsɪnɪəm məˈtɪləs / (BrE)
a small, erect European shrub of the heath family bearing solitary pinkish flowers and edible blue-black berries; also known as bilberry.

Examples

Vaccinium myrtillus berries
The fruit of Vaccinium myrtillus is prized for its sweet flavor and high antioxidant content.
The leaves and berries of Vaccinium myrtillus have been used in traditional medicine.
We found several Vaccinium myrtillus shrubs while hiking in the Scottish Highlands.
