patent of invention

noun
document granting rights to an invention
Pronunciation: / ˈpætənt əv ɪnˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənt əv ɪnˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
an official document granting an inventor the exclusive rights to make, use, or sell an invention for a certain period

Examples

A patent of invention
After years of hard work, she finally received a patent of invention for her innovative medical device.
His lawyer advised him to apply for a patent of invention before disclosing his idea publicly.
The firm specializes in securing patents of invention for new pharmaceutical compounds.
