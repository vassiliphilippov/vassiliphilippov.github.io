ransacking

noun
thorough, disorderly search
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrænˌsækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræn.sæk.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of searching for something thoroughly and often causing disorder or damage in the process

Examples

To ransack a room
After hours of ransacking the closet, he still couldn't find his old jacket.
Neighbors complained about the ransacking that occurred during the chaotic search for the missing documents.
The burglars' ransacking of the office left papers and files strewn everywhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ransack - to search thoroughly, often making a mess
  • synonym: search - the act of looking thoroughly for something
