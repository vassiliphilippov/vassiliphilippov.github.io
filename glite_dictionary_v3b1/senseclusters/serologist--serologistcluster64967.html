serologist

noun
medical scientist specializing in serology
9.1
Pronunciation: / səˈrɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / sɪəˈrɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a medical scientist who specializes in the study of blood serum, especially with regard to the response of the immune system to pathogens or introduced substances

Examples

A serologist studies serum
As a serologist, she contributed to identifying the source of an outbreak through laboratory testing.
Police consulted a serologist to analyze the crime scene's blood evidence for genetic markers.
The serologist examined the blood samples to detect any trace of infectious disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunologist - a scientist who studies the immune system
