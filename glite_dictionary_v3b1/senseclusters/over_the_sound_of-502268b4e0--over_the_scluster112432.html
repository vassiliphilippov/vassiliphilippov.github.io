over the sound of

preposition
unable to hear due to louder noise
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvɚ ðə ˈsaʊnd əv / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə ðə ˈsaʊnd əv / (BrE)
used to say that one cannot hear something because a louder noise is present

Examples

Shout over the sound of noise
He answered the phone but had to shout over the sound of construction outside.
I couldn't concentrate because she was yelling over the sound of the television.
The teacher struggled to speak over the sound of the thunderstorm.
