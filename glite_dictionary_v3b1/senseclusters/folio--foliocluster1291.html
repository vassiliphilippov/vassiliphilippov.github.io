folio

noun
large-format book or manuscript
7.0 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊlioʊ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
a book or manuscript made with large sheets of paper folded in the middle, producing two leaves or four pages; especially used to refer to an early or historical edition of a work

Examples

A rare folio
He purchased a rare folio containing medieval maps.
Scholars examined the ancient folio for marginal notes by its previous owners.
The library owns an original folio edition of Shakespeare's plays.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: octavo - a book or manuscript format with sheets folded three times to make eight leaves
  • degree lower: quarto - a book or manuscript format where each sheet is folded twice to make four leaves
