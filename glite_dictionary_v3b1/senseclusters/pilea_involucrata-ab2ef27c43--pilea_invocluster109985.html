Pilea involucrata

noun
species of Central/South American nettle with velvety leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪliə ˌɪnˌvɑlˈjʊkrətə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪliə ˌɪnˌvɒlˈjuːkrətə / (BrE)
A species of low, stingless nettle native to Central and South America, with velvety brownish-green toothed leaves and clusters of small green flowers.

Examples

Pilea involucrata plant
Pilea involucrata is a popular houseplant due to its unique, velvety leaves.
Gardeners appreciate Pilea involucrata for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
The care of Pilea involucrata requires regular watering but well-draining soil.
