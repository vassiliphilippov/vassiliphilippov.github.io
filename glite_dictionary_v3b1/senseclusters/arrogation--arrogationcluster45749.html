arrogation

noun
claiming or seizing (esp. by government) without right
Pronunciation: / ˌærəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌærəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of claiming or seizing something, especially by government authority, without justification or right

Examples

The arrogation of power
Critics condemned the arrogation of power by the new regime.
Many citizens feared the arrogation of individual rights under the new law.
The arrogation of private property by the state was met with public outrage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seizure - the action of taking possession of something by force or legal right
