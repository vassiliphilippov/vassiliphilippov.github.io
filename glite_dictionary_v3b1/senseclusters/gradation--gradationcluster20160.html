gradation

noun
relative position in a graded series
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡreɪˈdeɪʃ(ə)n /; / ɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a slight difference, change, or stage in degree or intensity, or the process of arranging things in a series of such stages

Examples

Color gradation
The artist carefully blended the colors to achieve subtle gradations on the canvas.
The sky displays beautiful gradations of blue as the sun sets.
There are many gradations of responsibility within the company, each with distinct duties.
Job responsibilities are often assigned based on a clear gradation of experience and skill.
The gradation of students by ability can be a controversial topic.
The artist created a beautiful gradation of colors in her painting.
