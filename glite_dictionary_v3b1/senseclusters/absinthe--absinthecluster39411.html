absinthe

noun
strong green liqueur with wormwood and anise
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.sɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈæb.sɪnθ / (BrE)
a strong green alcoholic liqueur flavored with wormwood and anise, traditionally associated with France and Switzerland

Examples

A glass of absinthe
Artists in 19th-century Paris were famously fond of drinking absinthe for inspiration.
Its unique flavor comes from the blend of wormwood, anise, and other herbs in absinthe.
The bartender poured a glass of absinthe and set it aflame as part of the ritual.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pastis - an anise-flavored spirit from France, similar to absinthe but without wormwood
