nonstaple

noun
nonessential product
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈsteɪpəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈsteɪpəl / (BrE)
a product or good that is not a basic necessity, often considered discretionary or a luxury item in economics

Examples

A nonstaple product
During economic downturns, people often cut back on nonstaple purchases like electronics and designer clothes.
The store specializes in nonstaple goods such as gourmet chocolates and imported wines.
Unlike rice or bread, a nonstaple is not required for daily living.

Related Terms

  • synonym: luxury - an item that is not essential and is associated with comfort or wealth
  • antonym: staple - a basic or essential item, especially food or product
