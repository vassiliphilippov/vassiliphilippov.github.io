livor

noun
postmortem skin discoloration
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪvɔr / (AmE), / ˈliːvɔː / (BrE)
a bluish or purplish discoloration of the skin caused by blood pooling under the skin, especially after death or injury

Examples

Postmortem livor
The forensic pathologist observed livor on the lower back of the deceased.
After several hours, livor becomes fixed and does not change position.
The presence of livor helped the investigators estimate the time of death.
The nurse noticed a large livor on the patient's leg after the accident.
After bumping his arm, a faint livor appeared within a few hours.
Doctors checked the livor to determine the extent of the internal bleeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bruise - an injury causing discoloration of the skin without breaking it
  • synonym: contusion - a region of injured tissue or skin in which blood capillaries have been ruptured
  • synonym: lividity - the pooling of blood in the body after death causing discoloration
  • antonym: pallor - an unhealthy pale appearance
