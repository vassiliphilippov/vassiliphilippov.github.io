mourn

verb
feel or show deep sadness
3.0 9.02/m
Pronunciation: / m…îrn / (AmE), / m…îÀên / (BrE)
to feel or show deep sadness, especially because someone has died, or to observe customs related to this sadness

Examples

To mourn a loss
It is natural to mourn after experiencing a significant loss.
Many people mourn the loss of a loved one in different ways.
She continues to mourn her childhood friend, even after many years have passed.
After his grandmother died, the whole family gathered to mourn according to tradition.
Many cultures mourn the loss of loved ones with special ceremonies and rites.
She continued to mourn her late husband for several years, wearing black as a sign of respect.

Synonyms

  • grieve
    3.8 5.97/m
    grieve is to feel deep, often private sorrow after a loss, while mourn includes public rituals or visible signs of sorrow.
  • lament
    5.0 6.88/m
    lament is an outward expression of sorrow, often spoken or cried out, while mourn involves deep feeling and social rituals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: celebrate - to observe with rejoicing; opposite in the context of mourning a death
  • synonym: grieve - to feel deep sorrow, especially for a death
  • antonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
