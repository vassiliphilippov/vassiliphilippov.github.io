patronize

verb
be a regular customer
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪz /; / ˈpatrənʌɪz / (BrE)
to be a regular customer of a business or to support it by using its services or buying its products

Examples

To patronize a store
Despite moving, she continued to patronize her favorite hair salon.
Many people prefer to patronize small, independent bookstores.
We always patronize our local bakery instead of the big supermarket.
Many artists struggled until wealthy collectors began to patronize their work.
The café is patronized by university students throughout the year.
We try to patronize local farmers' markets as much as possible.
Many artists are grateful to those who patronize their galleries.
She has patronized that café every morning since it opened.
We always patronize local shops to support small businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frequent - to visit or go to a place often
  • synonym: patronize - to regularly use the services of a business (American spelling)
  • synonym: support - to give help or encouragement to a business, event, etc.
