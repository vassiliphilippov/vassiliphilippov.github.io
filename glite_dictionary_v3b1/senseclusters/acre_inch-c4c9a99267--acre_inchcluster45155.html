acre inch

noun
a unit of volume for water over land
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪkər ɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈeɪkə ɪntʃ / (BrE)
A unit of volume equal to the amount of water required to cover one acre of land to a depth of one inch, or one twelfth of an acre-foot.

Examples

One acre inch
A reservoir with a capacity of 100 acre inches can irrigate several small fields.
Farmers often measure rainfall in acre inches to estimate its impact on their crops.
The irrigation system was designed to deliver one acre inch of water per week during the growing season.
