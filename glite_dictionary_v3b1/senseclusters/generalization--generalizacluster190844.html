generalization

noun
formulating general concepts from specific cases

the process of forming general concepts or principles by identifying common features from specific examples

Examples

Generalization of patterns
A key skill in mathematics is the generalization of patterns to solve a wide range of problems.
Scientists use generalization to draw broad conclusions from specific data gathered in experiments.
The child's generalization that all four-legged animals are dogs was corrected by his parents.
His generalization from a few experiments led to a widely accepted scientific principle.
It is important to avoid making a generalization based on limited evidence.
She explained how a generalization is formed by spotting patterns among individual cases.
Her research led to the generalisation that children learn languages in similar ways.
The risk of generalisation is that specific cases may be overlooked.
Through observation and analysis, scientists often formulate a generalisation about a phenomenon.
