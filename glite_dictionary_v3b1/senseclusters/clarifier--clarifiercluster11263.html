clarifier

noun
liquid-clearing agent
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈklæ.rə.faɪ.ər/ /; / /ˈklæ.rɪ.faɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈklæ.rɪ.faɪ.ə/ /; / /ˈklæ.rə.faɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a substance or chemical added to a liquid to remove suspended solids or haze and make it clear, especially in brewing, winemaking, or water treatment

Examples

Wine clarifier
The winemaker added a clarifier to the wine to remove unwanted particles before bottling.
In water treatment plants, a clarifier helps ensure the water is safe and clear for use.
Some brewers use a natural clarifier to improve the appearance of their beer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filter - a device or material that removes impurities from liquids or gases
  • synonym: fining agent - a substance used to clarify liquids by removing suspended particles
