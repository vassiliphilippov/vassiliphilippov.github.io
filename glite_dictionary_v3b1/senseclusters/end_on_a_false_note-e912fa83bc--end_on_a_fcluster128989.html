end on a false note

verb
finish disappointingly; not meet expectations
3.1
Pronunciation: / ɛnd ɑn ə fɔls noʊt / (AmE), / ɛnd ɒn ə fɔːls nəʊt / (BrE)
to conclude in a way that disappoints or does not meet expectations

Examples

To end on a false note badly
After hours of excitement, the event ended on a false note when the headline act canceled.
Despite a strong start, the meeting ended on a false note with unresolved tensions.
The movie ends on a false note, leaving viewers unsatisfied by the abrupt conclusion.
