pewter

noun
a grayish tin-based metal alloy
Pronunciation: / ˈpjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
a gray metal alloy containing mainly tin and a small amount of other metals, especially lead, once commonly used for tableware and decorative objects

Examples

A pewter plate
Many traditional dishes in Europe were once served on pewter plates.
She collects old pewter candlesticks from various countries.
The antique tankard was made of pewter and had intricate engravings.
