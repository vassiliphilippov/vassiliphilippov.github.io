runner bean

noun
a climbing bean plant with edible pods
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnɚ ˌbin /; / ˈrʌnər ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a climbing bean plant with red flowers, grown for its long green edible pods, especially popular in Britain

Examples

Fresh runner beans
In the UK, runner beans are a staple vegetable during the summer months.
She added fresh runner beans from the garden to the salad.
The bright red flowers of the runner bean plant attract bees and butterflies.
Runner beans are often served sliced and lightly steamed with a knob of butter.
She picked a bowlful of fresh runner beans from the garden for dinner.
The allotment was full of healthy runner bean plants climbing up their supports.
