conservative

adjective
opposed to change; favoring tradition
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɜːrvətɪv /; / kənˈsɝː.və.tɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvətɪv /; / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪv / (BrE)
unwilling to accept change or new ideas; favoring traditional values, styles, or methods (especially in politics, society, or personal life)

Examples

Conservative views
As a conservative politician, she opposed most policies aimed at reform.
Her parents have very conservative views about marriage and family life.
The school maintains a conservative dress code that has not changed in decades.
Many people in rural areas tend to be more conservative than those in cities.
My grandparents hold quite conservative views about marriage and family.
The senator is known for his conservative stance on social issues.
His opinions on relationships are unusually conservative for someone his age.
She comes from a conservative family that values tradition and order.
The company's dress code is quite conservative, requiring employees to wear formal attire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values
  • antonym: progressive - favoring or advocating progress, change, improvement, or reform
  • synonym: traditional - following customs and beliefs handed down from the past
