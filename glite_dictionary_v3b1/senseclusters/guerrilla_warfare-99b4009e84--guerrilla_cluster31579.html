guerrilla warfare

noun
irregular combat using hit-and-run tactics
5.5 0.54/m
Pronunciation: / g…ôÀàr…™l.…ô Ààw…îrÀåf…õr / (AmE), / …°…ôÀàr…™l.…ô Ààw…îÀê.fe…ô / (BrE)
A form of warfare in which small, mobile groups of irregular soldiers use hit-and-run tactics, ambushes, and mobility to fight larger, less-mobile traditional forces, often within territory occupied by the enemy.

Examples

Guerrilla warfare tactics
During the conflict, guerrilla warfare proved highly effective in the dense jungle terrain.
Many historians argue that guerrilla warfare changed the outcome of several major wars.
The rebels launched a campaign of guerrilla warfare against the occupying army.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional warfare - standard, formal military combat between regular armies
  • synonym: insurgency - an armed rebellion against authority, sometimes using guerrilla tactics
