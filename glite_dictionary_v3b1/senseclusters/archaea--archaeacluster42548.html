archaea

noun
microorganism domain
9.9 0.26/m

a group of single-celled microorganisms, different from bacteria and eukaryotes, often found in extreme environments like hot springs or deep ocean vents

Examples

Single-celled archaea

Unlike bacteria, archaea can survive in highly acidic or salty environments.

Some archaea play a crucial role in breaking down organic matter in wetlands.
