archaea

noun
microorganism domain
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈkiːə / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiːə / (BrE)
a group of single-celled microorganisms, different from bacteria and eukaryotes, often found in extreme environments like hot springs or deep ocean vents

Examples

Single-celled archaea
Scientists discovered new species of archaea living in extremely hot ocean vents.
Unlike bacteria, archaea can survive in highly acidic or salty environments.
The study of archaea has changed our understanding of the tree of life.
Scientists discovered new species of archaea living deep in the ocean near hydrothermal vents.
Unlike bacteria, archaea can thrive in extremely salty or acidic environments.
Some archaea play a crucial role in breaking down organic matter in wetlands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bacteria - single-celled microorganisms, a separate domain of life from archaea
  • antonym: eukaryote - organisms whose cells have a nucleus
  • synonym: prokaryote - an organism without a nucleus, including bacteria and archaea
