contiguity

noun
state of being in contact or touching
Pronunciation: / kənˌtɪɡjuˈɪti / (AmE), / kənˌtɪɡjuˈɪti / (BrE)
the state of being very close to or touching something; the condition of being in direct contact

Examples

The contiguity of buildings
Because of their contiguity, the two buildings appear to form a single structure.
Property values increased due to the contiguity to the city center.
The contiguity of the two countries has led to strong cultural connections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distance - the amount of space between two points
  • synonym: proximity - closeness in space, time, or relationship
