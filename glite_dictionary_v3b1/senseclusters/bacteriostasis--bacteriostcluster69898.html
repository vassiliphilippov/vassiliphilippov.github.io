bacteriostasis

noun
inhibition of bacterial growth
Pronunciation: / bækˌtɪri.oʊˈsteɪsɪs / (AmE), / bækˌtɪəri.əˈsteɪsɪs / (BrE)
the inhibition of the growth and reproduction of bacteria without their destruction

Examples

Bacteriostasis effect
In clinical settings, achieving bacteriostasis can help control infections without promoting resistance.
Researchers observed bacteriostasis in cultures treated with the new compound.
The antibiotic caused bacteriostasis rather than killing the bacteria completely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bactericide - a substance that kills bacteria
