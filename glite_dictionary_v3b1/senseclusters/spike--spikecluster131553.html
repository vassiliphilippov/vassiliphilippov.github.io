spike

noun
viral surface protein aiding entry
7.7 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / spaÉªk / (AmE), / spaÉªk / (BrE)
(biology, virology) a structural protein on the surface of certain viruses that forms spike-like projections and helps the virus attach to and enter host cells.

Examples

Virus spike protein
Mutations in the coronavirus spike protein can change how the virus binds to human cells.
Vaccines often target the viral spike to stimulate immunity and prevent the virus from entering cells.
Researchers studied the spike structure to design drugs that block receptor attachment and cell entry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: S protein - abbreviation commonly used in virology for the spike (surface) protein
  • synonym: spike protein - the full term for the viral surface protein that mediates receptor binding and membrane fusion
