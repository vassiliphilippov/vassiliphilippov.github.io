Klinefelter syndrome

noun
genetic disorder in males with an extra X chromosome
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪn.fɛl.tɚ ˌsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ˈklaɪn.fɛl.tə ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A genetic disorder in males caused by the presence of an extra X chromosome (XXY), typically resulting in small testes, long legs, development of breast tissue, reduced sperm production, and learning difficulties.

Examples

Klinefelter syndrome symptoms
Genetic counseling is recommended for families affected by Klinefelter syndrome.
Many individuals with Klinefelter syndrome may not show obvious symptoms until adulthood.
The doctor explained that his infertility was due to Klinefelter syndrome.
