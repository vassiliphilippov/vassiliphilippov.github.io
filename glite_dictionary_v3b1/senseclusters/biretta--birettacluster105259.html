biretta

noun
a stiff clerical cap with ridges
6.4
Pronunciation: / bəˈretə / (AmE), / bɪˈretə / (BrE)
a stiff square cap with three or four ridges across the crown, worn by Roman Catholic clergy and some members of the Anglican and academic communities

Examples

A biretta cap
A cardinal's biretta is often red and ornamented.
During the ceremony, the bishop placed his biretta on the altar.
The priest adjusted his biretta before beginning the procession.
