subtext

an underlying or hidden meaning in speech, writing, or art that is not stated directly

Examples

The subtext of speech
The film's political subtext became more apparent after the director's interview.
Her novel contains a powerful subtext about social inequality.
During the meeting, there was a clear subtext of disagreement among the team members.
The subtext of her compliment suggested she was not entirely sincere.
In many plays, the true feelings of the characters are revealed through subtext rather than dialogue.
He smiled, but the subtext of his words hinted at disappointment.
