rule over

verb
control or have authority over
4.2 1.44/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀêl Àào äv…ôr / (AmE), / ruÀêl Àà…ô äv…ô / (BrE)
to have control or authority over a person, group, or place

Examples

To rule over a country
After the revolution, a new government ruled over the region.
For decades, the dictator ruled over the country with an iron fist.
The queen ruled over a vast empire during her reign.

Synonyms

  • rule
    1.0 26/m
    rule is the basic verb for governing people or places, while rule over stresses having direct authority over a group or area.
  • govern
    2.9 13/m
    govern is used for formal authority over a country or organization, while rule over is more general for any person, group, or place.
  • command
    2.7 15/m
    command often means giving orders to people or troops, while rule over means holding authority over people, groups, or places.
  • control
    2.4 93/m
    control can mean managing or restricting things, while rule over implies legal or official authority over people or places.
  • dominate
    3.7 6.79/m
    dominate suggests having a stronger, often unequal power or influence, while rule over focuses on official authority over people or places.
  • hold sway
    6.6 1.04/m
    hold sway means having a strong influence or popularity without formal power, while rule over means official control or authority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominate - to have commanding influence on; exercise control over
  • synonym: govern - to officially control and direct the affairs of a state, city, or organization
  • antonym: submit - to accept or yield to the authority of another; antonym to 'rule over' in control relationship
