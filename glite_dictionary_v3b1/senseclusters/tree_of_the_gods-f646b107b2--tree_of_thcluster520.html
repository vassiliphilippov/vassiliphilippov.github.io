tree of the gods

noun
fast-growing Chinese deciduous tree, pollution-resistant
Pronunciation: / tri əv ðə ɡɑdz / (AmE), / triː əv ðə ɡɒdz / (BrE)
a fast-growing deciduous tree native to China, with sumac-like leaves and sweet but unpleasant-smelling flowers, often planted as a street tree due to its pollution resistance

Examples

The tree of the gods leaves
Despite its beauty, the tree of the gods is considered invasive in many areas.
Some gardeners avoid the tree of the gods because of the strong scent of its flowers.
The tree of the gods is often planted along city streets for its ability to thrive in polluted air.
