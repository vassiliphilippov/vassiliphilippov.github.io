contentiously

adverb
argumentatively
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛnʃəsli / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnʃəsli / (BrE)
in a way that causes or involves argument, disagreement, or controversy

Examples

Discussed contentiously
The panel discussed the issue contentiously, with each member defending their own position.
Neighbors contentiously argued over the property line for hours.
The law was contentiously debated in parliament before it was finally passed.
The two politicians debated contentiously throughout the televised interview.
She responded contentiously to every suggestion, making it hard to reach an agreement.
Discussions about the new policy were handled contentiously at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: argumentatively - in a way that shows a tendency to argue
  • antonym: peacefully - in a calm and non-argumentative manner
