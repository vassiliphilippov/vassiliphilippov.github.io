rebuff

noun
an abrupt rejection or refusal
5.0 7.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
an abrupt or ungracious refusal or rejection of an offer, request, or approach

Examples

A cold rebuff
After facing a rebuff at the door, he decided not to ask again.
Despite his polite offer to help, she gave him a sharp rebuff.
The company's proposal received a swift rebuff from the board of directors.
Despite the rebuff, she remained determined to pursue her project.
He regarded her absence as a personal rebuff and left the party early.
The proposal received a sharp rebuff from the committee.

  • synonym: rejection - the dismissing or refusing of a proposal, idea, etc.
  • synonym: snub - a sharp or abrupt rejection or dismissal
