well-set

adjective
strongly and firmly built
Pronunciation: / wɛl ˈsɛt / (AmE), / wɛl ˈsɛt / (BrE)
strongly and firmly built or constructed; having a solid, sturdy, and proportionate appearance.

Examples

A well-set man
He is a well-set man, compact and strong for his size.
The house was well-set and withstood heavy winds during the storm.
The sculpture was well-set on its base, lending it an air of permanence.
