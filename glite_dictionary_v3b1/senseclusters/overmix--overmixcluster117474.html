overmix

verb
to mix audio too much
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈmɪks / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈmɪks / (BrE)
to mix audio tracks or music excessively, resulting in a cluttered or distorted sound.

Examples

To overmix a song
If you overmix the song, the vocals will be buried under reverb and loud instruments.
The producer warned not to overmix the track or it would lose its clarity.
Some albums sound muddy because engineers overmix every instrument and effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overproduce - to add too many effects or layers to a recording
  • antonym: undermix - to mix audio tracks too little, resulting in a weak or unfinished sound
