determinacy

noun
consistent outcome property
8.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈtɜːr.mɪn.ə.siː/ /; / /dɪ.ˈtɜːr.mə.nə.siː/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈtɜː.mɪn.ə.siː/ /; / /dɪ.ˈtɜː.mə.nə.siː/ (BrE)
the quality of always producing the same result or behavior when given the same input, especially in computing or logic

Examples

Determinacy of an algorithm
The determinacy of the algorithm ensures that it always produces the same answer for the same data.
In mathematics, determinacy is important for proving that a process is reliable.
Programmers value determinacy because it makes software easier to test and debug.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consistency - the quality of always behaving or performing in a similar way
  • antonym: indeterminacy - the quality of not having a single, definite result or outcome
