lee tide

noun
tide flowing with the wind
Pronunciation: / li taɪd / (AmE), / liː taɪd / (BrE)
A tide that flows in the same direction as the wind is blowing.

Examples

Lee tide flows
Sailing in a lee tide can be risky for inexperienced sailors.
They waited to cross the bay until the lee tide had slackened.
With a strong lee tide, the boat drifted quickly along the coast.

Related Terms

  • antonym: weather tide - a tide that runs counter to the wind
