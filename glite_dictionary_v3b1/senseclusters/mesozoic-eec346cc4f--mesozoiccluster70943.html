Mesozoic

noun
geological era (230–63 million years ago)
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛzəˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːzəʊˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
the era in Earth's history from about 230 to 63 million years ago, following the Paleozoic and preceding the Cenozoic, characterized by the dominance of dinosaurs

Examples

The Mesozoic era
Important plant and animal evolution occurred throughout the Mesozoic period.
Many of the world's major landmasses began breaking apart during the Mesozoic.
The Mesozoic era is often called the Age of Reptiles due to the dominance of dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Paleozoic - geological era before the Mesozoic
