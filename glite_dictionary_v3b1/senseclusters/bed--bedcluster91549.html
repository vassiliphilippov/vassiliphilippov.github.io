bed

noun
foundation layer for a track or road
Pronunciation: / bɛd / (AmE), / bɛd / (BrE)
the bottom surface or a layer of earth, rock, or other material that acts as a base or foundation (such as for a river, road, or minerals)

Examples

A river bed
Engineers inspected the road bed for signs of erosion after the storm.
Heavy rain damaged the bed beneath the railway line, causing delays.
Large stones were laid to reinforce the bed supporting the new highway.
Many plants take root in the soft mud of the river bed.
Marine life thrives on the bed of the shallow lagoon.
The divers carefully explored the bed of the sunken lake for artifacts.
A rich bed of iron ore was discovered beneath the hillside.
Geologists identified several distinct beds of limestone throughout the region.
The miners drilled into the bed of coal to extract valuable resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foundation - the solid base on which a structure is built
  • synonym: layer - a sheet, quantity, or thickness of material lying over or under another
  • synonym: riverbed - the bottom of a river
  • synonym: seam - layer of mineral, especially coal, that can be mined
  • synonym: stratum - a layer of rock or soil with internally consistent characteristics
