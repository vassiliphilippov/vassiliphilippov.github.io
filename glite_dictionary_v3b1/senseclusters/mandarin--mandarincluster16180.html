mandarin

noun
small reddish-orange citrus fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈmændəˌrɪn /; / ˈmæn.də.rɪn / (AmE), / ˈmændərɪn /; / ˈmæn.dər.ɪn / (BrE)
a small, sweet citrus fruit with loose, orange skin, or the tree that produces it

Examples

A juicy mandarin
A bowl of mandarins brightened up the kitchen table.
She peeled a mandarin for her lunch.
The market sells sweet, juicy mandarins during the winter.
A single mandarin contains a high amount of vitamin C and is a healthy snack.
Imported mandarins are especially popular around the Lunar New Year.
The mandarin tree in our garden produces fruit with a thin, easy-to-peel skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tangerine - a similar small variety of citrus fruit
