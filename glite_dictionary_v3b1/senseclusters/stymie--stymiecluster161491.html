stymie

noun
golf: opponent's ball blocks shot
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪmi / (AmE), / ˈstaɪmi / (BrE)
In golf (historical), a situation in match play where an opponent's ball blocks the direct path between your ball and the hole.

Examples

Golf stymie
In early twentieth-century golf, a stymie often forced players to chip over another ball on the green.
Many golf purists reminisce about the days when the stymie was part of match play.
The introduction of the stymie rule added a unique challenge to golf strategy.
