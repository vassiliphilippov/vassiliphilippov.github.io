MAO

noun
enzyme catalyzing monoamine oxidation
6.9
Pronunciation: /maʊ/ (AmE), /maʊ/ (BrE)
an enzyme (monoamine oxidase) that catalyzes the oxidation of monoamines, including neurotransmitters such as serotonin, epinephrine, and norepinephrine

Examples

MAO enzyme
Research into MAO has helped explain certain mood disorders and their treatment.
Some antidepressant medications work by inhibiting MAO in the brain.
The activity of MAO varies widely among individuals and can influence mental health.
