stream

noun
continuous movement or flow
5.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /striːm/ (AmE), /striːm/ (BrE)
a continuous flow or movement of liquid, people, things, or ideas

Examples

A stream of people
A steady stream of customers entered the store as soon as it opened.
After the concert ended, a stream of people headed toward the exits.
We received a stream of messages congratulating us on our success.
A steady stream of visitors arrived throughout the afternoon.
Tears ran down her face in a silent stream.
The company has enjoyed a continuous stream of success over the past decade.
A stream of water poured from the broken pipe onto the floor.
Light filtered in through a narrow stream of air at the window's edge.
The volcano emitted a continuous stream of smoke and ash.

Related Terms

  • synonym: current - a body of water or air moving in a definite direction
  • synonym: flow - steady, continuous movement of something
  • degree higher: torrent - a strong and fast-moving stream of water or other liquid
  • degree lower: trickle - a small or slow flow of liquid
