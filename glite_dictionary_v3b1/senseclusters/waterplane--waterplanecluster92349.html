waterplane

noun
surface at waterline
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpleɪn / (BrE)
the horizontal surface of a floating object at the waterline, where it meets the water's surface

Examples

Ship's waterplane
The engineer measured the waterplane to calculate the ship's stability.
A vessel's waterplane area affects how it floats and moves in the water.
Designers must consider the waterplane when planning the hull shape of a boat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waterline - the line where the hull of a ship meets the surface of the water
