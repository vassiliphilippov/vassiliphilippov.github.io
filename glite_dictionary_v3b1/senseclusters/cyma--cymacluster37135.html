cyma

noun
S-shaped architectural cornice molding
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪmə / (AmE), / ˈsɪmə / (BrE)
(architecture) A type of molding used in a cornice, characterized by an S-shaped profile that is partly concave and partly convex.

Examples

A cyma molding
An expert carpenter skillfully shaped the cyma for the restoration project.
In classical architecture, a cyma can be either a cyma recta or a cyma reversa.
The decorative cyma added elegance to the building's cornice.
