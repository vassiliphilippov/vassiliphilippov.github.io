fascination

noun
intense interest or attraction
3.7 2.9
Pronunciation: /fæs.ə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /fæs.ɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /fæs.ɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /fæs.ə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a very strong interest, attraction, or admiration for something; the quality of being extremely interesting or attractive

Examples

Strong fascination with history
Her lifelong fascination with ancient Egypt inspired her career as an archaeologist.
I've always had a fascination for the mysteries of the universe.
The children watched the magician with fascination, amazed by every trick.
As a child, she had a fascination for dinosaurs and ancient fossils.
His fascination with astronomy led him to become an astrophysicist.
The city's fascination lies in its vibrant culture and rich history.
Despite the danger, there was a strange fascination that drew them closer to the erupting volcano.
Her lifelong fascination with astronomy began when she saw her first eclipse as a child.
The magician's performance held the audience in utter fascination.
