fading

noun
gradual weakening or loss of intensity
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪ.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.dɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming weaker, less intense, or less distinct over time

Examples

Color fading
Over time, there was a noticeable fading of the old photograph's colors.
Radio signals are subject to fading caused by changes in the atmosphere.
The fading of the music signaled the end of the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attenuation - the reduction of the force, effect, or value of something
  • antonym: strengthening - the process of making or becoming stronger
