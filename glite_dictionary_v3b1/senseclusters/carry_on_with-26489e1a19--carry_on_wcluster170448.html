Synonyms

  • carry on
    2.7 12/m
    carry on can be used alone to mean continue or behave strongly, while carry on with names the activity you return to after an interruption.
  • resume
    3.1 14/m
    resume means to start an activity again after a break, while carry on with is more common in everyday speech to mean continue.
  • get on with
    2.3 8/m
    get on with means to continue a task after an interruption and make progress, while carry on with is slightly more general in use.
  • continue
    1.0 200/m
    continue is a neutral verb for keeping an action going, while carry on with is a phrasal, more informal way to say the same thing.
  • proceed
    3.0 40/m
    proceed is more formal and often follows a plan or instruction, while carry on with is informal and focuses on returning to an activity.
  • keep on
    1.4 14/m
    keep on means to continue doing something, often repeatedly, while carry on with points to continuing a specific interrupted activity.
  • keep going
    1.6 17/m
    keep going means to continue without stopping or to keep energy up, while carry on with focuses on resuming a task after a pause.
  • press on
    3.9 1/m
    press on means to continue with strong effort despite problems, while carry on with simply means to continue an activity without that forceful tone.
  • push on
    3.7 1.0/m
    push on means to force yourself to continue through tiredness or trouble, while carry on with means just to continue the activity.
