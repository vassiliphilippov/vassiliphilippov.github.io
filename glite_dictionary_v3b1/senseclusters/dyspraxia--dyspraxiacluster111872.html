dyspraxia

noun
movement coordination disorder
9.8
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈpræk.sɪ.ə/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈpræk.sɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a condition that makes it difficult to plan and coordinate physical movements, including those needed for speech

Examples

Dyspraxia in children
Children with dyspraxia may struggle with tasks like tying their shoes.
A diagnosis of dyspraxia can help explain difficulties with handwriting and sports.
Therapists often work with people who have dyspraxia to improve their motor skills.
Children with dyspraxia may struggle to tie their shoelaces or button their shirts.
Speech therapy can help people with dyspraxia improve their communication skills.
His dyspraxia made it challenging to coordinate the movements needed for clear speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apraxia - a neurological disorder causing loss of ability to perform learned movements
  • antonym: coordination - the ability to use different parts of the body together smoothly
