takeout food

noun
food to be eaten off the premises
1.4 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààte…™kÀåa ät fuÀêd / (AmE), / Ààte…™ka ät fuÀêd / (BrE)
food prepared by a restaurant or store to be eaten elsewhere rather than on the premises

Examples

Takeout food order
After the meeting, everyone grabbed some takeout food to eat back at the office.
The city offers a wide variety of takeout food options, from pizza to Thai cuisine.
We ordered takeout food because we didn't feel like cooking last night.

Synonyms

  • takeout
    1.5 1.97/m
    takeout is the common short word for food sold to eat elsewhere, while takeout food names the same food more fully.
  • takeaway
    1.5 0.17/m
    takeaway is the British word for prepared food bought to eat elsewhere, while takeout food is the more American term.
  • carryout
    2.7 0.15/m
    carryout refers to food bought to eat elsewhere or the shop that sells it, while takeout food names the food itself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: takeaway - food prepared to be eaten elsewhere (chiefly British English)
