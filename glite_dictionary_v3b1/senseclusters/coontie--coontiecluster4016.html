coontie

noun
a woody plant (Zamia) yielding edible starch
6.7 9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkuːn.tiː/ /; / /ˈkuːn.tɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkuːn.tiː/ /; / /ˈkuːn.tɪ/ (BrE)
a small, tough, woody plant of Florida, the West Indies, and Cuba (Zamia integrifolia), with roots and stems that yield an edible starch called arrowroot.

Examples

The coontie plant
Early Floridians used coontie roots to produce a starchy flour for food.
The endangered coontie plant helps support populations of the Atala butterfly.
Traditional communities valued coontie for its edible arrowroot and resilience in harsh climates.
