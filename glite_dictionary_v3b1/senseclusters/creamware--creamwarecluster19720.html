creamware

noun
cream-colored pottery
Pronunciation: / ˈkriːmˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈkriːmˌweə / (BrE)
a fine, cream-colored earthenware pottery developed in the 18th century, often used for tableware

Examples

A set of creamware plates
The antique shop had a beautiful set of creamware plates from the 18th century.
Collectors often seek out rare pieces of creamware for their historical value.
She displayed her grandmother's creamware teapot in a glass cabinet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthenware - pottery made from clay and fired at low temperatures
  • antonym: porcelain - a hard, white, translucent ceramic material
