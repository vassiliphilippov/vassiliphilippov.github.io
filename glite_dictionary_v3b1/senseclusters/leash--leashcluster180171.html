leash

noun
rope or cord to restrain an animal
1.6
Pronunciation: / liːʃ / (AmE), / liːʃ / (BrE)
A long, thin piece of rope, chain, or cord used for holding and controlling an animal.

Examples

A dog leash
Please put your puppy on a leash before taking him outside.
She held the dog tightly by its leash as they crossed the busy street.
The park requires all pets to be kept on a leash at all times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lead - a strap or cord for leading an animal by the collar (chiefly British)
