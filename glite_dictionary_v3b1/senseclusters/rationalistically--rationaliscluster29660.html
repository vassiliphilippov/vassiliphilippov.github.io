rationalistically

adverb
in a rationalist way
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌræʃənəˈlɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌræʃənəˈlɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is based on or guided by rationalism or logical reasoning rather than experience or emotions

Examples

To think rationalistically
The philosopher argued rationalistically to support his theory, relying on logic rather than evidence.
Some scientists approach problems rationalistically, preferring reason over experimentation.
The policy was developed rationalistically, with little consideration for practical experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: logically - in a way that uses clear and sound reasoning
