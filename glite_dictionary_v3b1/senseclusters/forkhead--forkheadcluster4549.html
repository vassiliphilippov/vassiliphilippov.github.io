forkhead

noun
protein domain in genes
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrkˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkhɛd / (BrE)
a protein domain found in certain genes that regulates gene expression, especially during development

Examples

The forkhead domain
The forkhead domain is essential for the regulation of many developmental genes.
Mutations in the forkhead gene family can lead to various genetic disorders.
Researchers study forkhead proteins to understand their role in cell growth and differentiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeobox - a DNA sequence found in genes that regulate development
  • synonym: transcription factor - a protein that controls the rate of gene transcription
