public house

noun
a bar or tavern, especially in Britain
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk haʊs / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk haʊs / (BrE)
A building where alcoholic drinks are sold and consumed, often providing light meals and sometimes accommodation; commonly referred to as a 'pub' in Britain.

Examples

A public house pub
Many rural public houses in England have remained unchanged for decades.
The old public house offers both local beers and traditional food.
We met at the public house on the corner for a drink after work.
