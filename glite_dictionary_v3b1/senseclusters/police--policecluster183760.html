police

noun
the law enforcement organization
1.0 248/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôÀàlis / (AmE), / p…ôÀàliÀês / (BrE)
the official organization whose job is to enforce laws, prevent crime, and maintain public order

Examples

Call the police
She called the police to report a suspicious package outside her house.
The police are investigating the cause of the fire.
The police arrived at the scene within minutes of the accident.

Synonyms

  • police force
    2.0 6.08/m
    a police force is the organized body of officers who work in an area, while police are the everyday word for that organization.
  • police department
    1.3 5.87/m
    a police department runs local officers and public safety services in a city, while police are the general name for those officers.
  • law enforcement agency
    3.1 2.82/m
    a law enforcement agency is a formal body that investigates and enforces laws at many levels, while police are officers who patrol local communities.
  • constabulary
    8.1 0.69/m
    a constabulary is a traditional British name for a local policing body, while police are the general modern word for that organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: criminal - a person who breaks the law, the opposite group to police
  • synonym: law enforcement - the activity of ensuring obedience to laws, often by police
