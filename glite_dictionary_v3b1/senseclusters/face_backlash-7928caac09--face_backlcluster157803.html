face backlash

verb
receive strong public criticism
Pronunciation: / feɪs ˈbæklæʃ / (AmE), / feɪs ˈbæklæʃ / (BrE)
to experience a strong negative reaction or criticism, especially from the public or media, in response to one's actions or statements

Examples

To face backlash publicly
After releasing the controversial advertisement, the company faced backlash from consumers and advocacy groups.
Many celebrities have faced backlash on social media for their insensitive remarks.
The politician faced backlash for his comments during the televised debate.
