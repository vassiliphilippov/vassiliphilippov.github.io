Streptococcus

noun
genus of chain-forming bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌstrɛp.təˈkɑː.kəs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛp.təˈkɒk.əs / (BrE)
A genus of spherical, gram-positive bacteria that typically form chains or pairs, including many species that are important pathogens in humans and animals.

Examples

Streptococcus bacteria
Streptococcus bacteria are a common cause of throat infections in children.
Doctors tested for Streptococcus to diagnose the cause of her sudden fever.
The laboratory identified the strain as Streptococcus pyogenes, responsible for strep throat.
