crater

verb
to fall sharply
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkreɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkreɪ.tə/ (BrE)
to fall or drop suddenly and sharply, especially in value, performance, or reputation, often used informally.

Examples

Stock prices cratered
After the disappointing earnings report, the company's stock price cratered within hours.
Sales cratered following the negative reviews, forcing the store to close early.
His reputation cratered after the scandal was revealed in the press.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to suddenly fail or fall down completely
  • synonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed
  • antonym: rise - to increase in amount, value, or level
