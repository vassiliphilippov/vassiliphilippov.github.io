blotchy

adjective
covered with irregular spots or patches
5.0 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbl…ëÀêt Éi / (AmE), / Ààbl…ít Éi / (BrE)
having irregular or discolored spots or patches, often on skin or surfaces

Examples

Blotchy skin
She avoided wearing shorts because of her blotchy skin.
The old wallpaper had become blotchy over the years.
The rash left his arms looking blotchy and irritated.
Her face became blotchy after she spent too much time in the sun.
The allergic reaction left his arms red and blotchy.
The old painting's surface had turned blotchy with age and humidity.

Synonyms

  • splotchy
    5.6 0.28/m
    splotchy describes uneven, large patches and irregular marks, while blotchy refers to irregular discolored spots or patches on skin or surfaces.
  • spotty
    1.4 0.19/m
    spotty shows distinct small spots or patches that come and go, while blotchy means irregular discolored patches on skin or surfaces.
  • spotted
    1.0 0.10/m
    spotted simply means having clear, visible spots of a different color, while blotchy describes uneven discolored patches on skin or surfaces.
