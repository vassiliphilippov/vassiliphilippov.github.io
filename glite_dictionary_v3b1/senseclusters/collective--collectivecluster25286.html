collective

adjective
of or relating to collectivism or shared ownership
4.5
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of an organization, activity, or resource) set up on the principle of collectivism, especially owned and operated by the workers involved, often under government supervision.

Examples

Collective farm
A collective enterprise often emphasizes cooperation rather than individual profit.
Many farmers worked on collective farms during the Soviet era.
The new government established several collective workshops in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communal - shared by all members of a community; for common use
  • antonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing rather than a group
