horizontal tail

noun
horizontal stabilizer and elevator on an aircraft
4.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåh…îÀêr…™Ààz…ëÀênt…ôl te…™l / (AmE), / Àåh…ír…™Ààz…ínt…ôl te…™l / (BrE)
the horizontal stabilizing surface at the tail of an aircraft, typically including the stabilizer and elevator, used to control pitch and stability

Examples

The horizontal tail surface
Damage to the horizontal tail can affect an airplane's pitch control.
Modern jets often have advanced designs for the horizontal tail to improve performance.
The horizontal tail helps stabilize the aircraft during flight.

Synonyms

  • tailplane
    8.8 0.01/m
    a tailplane is the whole horizontal surface with attached elevator, while a horizontal tail often means the stabilizer plus elevator for pitch control.
  • horizontal stabilizer
    6.7 0.05/m
    a horizontal stabilizer is the fixed rear surface used mainly for balance, while a horizontal tail typically includes the stabilizer and elevator for pitch control.
