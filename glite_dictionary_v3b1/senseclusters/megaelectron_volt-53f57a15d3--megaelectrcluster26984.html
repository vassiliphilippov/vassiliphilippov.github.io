megaelectron volt

noun
unit of energy (1 million electron volts)
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmeɡ.ə ɪ.ˈlek.trən voʊlt/ (AmE), /ˈmeɡ.ə ɪ.ˈlek.trɒn vɒlt/ /; / /ˈmeɡ.ə ɪ.ˈlek.trɒn əʊlt/ (BrE)
A unit of energy equal to one million electron volts, commonly used in particle physics and nuclear physics.

Examples

One megaelectron volt
In nuclear reactions, the released energy is often given in megaelectron volt.
Particle accelerators can produce beams with energies of several hundred megaelectron volt.
The energy of the photon was measured at 2 megaelectron volt (MeV).
