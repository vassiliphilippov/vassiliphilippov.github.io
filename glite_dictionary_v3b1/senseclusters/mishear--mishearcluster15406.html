mishear

verb
to hear incorrectly
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈhɪr / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈhɪə / (BrE)
to hear something incorrectly or fail to understand what is said

Examples

To mishear instructions
Sometimes I mishear instructions in noisy environments and make mistakes.
She thought he said 'beach', but she had actually misheard him.
If you mishear a word, don't be afraid to ask for clarification.

If you mishear something important, do not hesitate to ask for clarification.

It is easy to mishear words in a noisy restaurant.
He apologized after realizing he had misheard her name.
