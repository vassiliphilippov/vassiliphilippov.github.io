cumin

noun
aromatic Mediterranean spice plant
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌmɪn / (BrE)
A small annual plant of the parsley family, native to the Mediterranean region, cultivated for its aromatic seeds used as a spice.

Examples

Cumin plant
Cumin is often used in Middle Eastern and Indian cuisines.
She sprinkled roasted cumin seeds on top of the curry.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of ground cumin to enhance the flavor.
