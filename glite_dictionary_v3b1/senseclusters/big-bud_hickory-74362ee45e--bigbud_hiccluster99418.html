big-bud hickory

noun
North American hickory tree with edible nut
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ bʌd ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / bɪɡ bʌd ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A smooth-barked North American hickory tree with 7 to 9 leaflets and a hard-shelled edible nut.

Examples

The big-bud hickory tree
Botanists identified the species as big-bud hickory by its distinctive smooth bark and clustered leaflets.
She collected nuts from the big-bud hickory as part of her autumn harvest.
The forest was home to several big-bud hickory trees, valued for their tough wood and edible nuts.
