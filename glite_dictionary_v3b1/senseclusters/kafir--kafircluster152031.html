kafir

noun
offensive term for non-believer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkæfɪə / (BrE)
an offensive term for a non-believer, especially used in Islamic contexts to refer to someone who does not accept Islam

Examples

Calling someone a kafir
The word kafir is considered highly offensive and should not be used in respectful conversation.
He was called a kafir because he did not follow the local religious customs.
Using the term kafir can cause deep offense and is generally avoided in modern discussions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: believer - a person who has religious faith
  • synonym: infidel - an offensive term for someone who does not believe in a particular religion
