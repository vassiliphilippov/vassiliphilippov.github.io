photoluminescence

noun
light emission after absorption
8.4
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.luː.mɪ.ˈnes.əns/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.luː.mɪ.ˈnes.əns/ (BrE)
the emission of light from a material after it absorbs photons (usually seen in scientific or laboratory contexts)

Examples

The photoluminescence emission
Scientists measured the photoluminescence of the crystal to determine its purity.
The photoluminescence technique revealed hidden defects in the solar cell material.
Researchers use photoluminescence to analyze the electronic properties of new semiconductors.
The photoluminescence of the mineral was measured under different wavelengths of light.
Researchers observed strong photoluminescence in the new semiconductor material.
The display uses photoluminescence to produce bright and vivid colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluorescence - light emitted by a substance that has absorbed light or other electromagnetic radiation
  • synonym: phosphorescence - light emitted by a substance without combustion or perceptible heat, lasting after exposure to radiation
