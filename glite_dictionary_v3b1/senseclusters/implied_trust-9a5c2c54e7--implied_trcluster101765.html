implied trust

noun
a trust created by law, not stated directly
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈplaɪd trʌst / (AmE), / ɪmˈplaɪd trʌst / (BrE)
A trust that is created by law based on the presumed intentions of the parties, rather than by express statement.

Examples

An implied trust by law
An implied trust can provide legal remedies even when no formal trust agreement was signed.
Property may be held in implied trust if the owner's intent can be inferred but was not explicitly declared.
The court determined that an implied trust existed based on the actions of the parties involved.

Related Terms

  • antonym: express trust - a trust created by explicit declaration or statement
