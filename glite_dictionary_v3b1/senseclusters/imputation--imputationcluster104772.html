imputation

noun
accusation of dishonesty or crime
4.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /ɪm.pjə.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪm.pjʊ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪm.pjʊ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of accusing or attributing blame, fault, or responsibility to someone

Examples

Imputation of blame
He strongly objected to the imputation of corruption made against him.
Her reputation suffered as a result of the imputation of theft.
The company issued a statement denying any imputation of unethical behavior.
Despite the imputation of dishonesty, she maintained her innocence.
He was offended by the imputation that his achievements were not earned through hard work.
The imputation of blame to the entire team was both unfair and demoralizing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attribution - the act of assigning or regarding something as being caused by someone or something
  • antonym: exoneration - the act of officially absolving someone from blame
