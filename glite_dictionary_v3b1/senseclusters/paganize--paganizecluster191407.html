paganize

verb
make pagan in character
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something pagan in character or spirit, especially by introducing pagan beliefs or elements

Examples

To paganize traditions
Many rural traditions were gradually paganized over the centuries.
Some scholars argue that the celebrations were intentionally paganized to encourage broader participation.
The emperor sought to paganize certain Christian holidays to win support from other groups.
During the Renaissance, artists sometimes paganised biblical themes in their paintings.
Some critics argue that ancient Roman festivals helped to paganise certain Christian traditions.
There was concern that commercial interests would paganise the originally religious holiday.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Christianize - to make Christian in character or influence
  • antonym: christianise - to make Christian in character or spirit
  • synonym: secularize - to make more secular or less religious
