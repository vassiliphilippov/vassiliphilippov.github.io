unlash

verb
untie or remove a lashing
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlæʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈlæʃ / (BrE)
to untie or remove the lashing from (something that has been secured with rope, cord, or straps)

Examples

To unlash the boat
Rain poured down as he struggled to unlash the boat from the dock.
She carefully unlashed the tent poles after the camping trip was over.
The sailors needed to unlash the cargo before unloading it from the ship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lash - to tie or bind tightly with a rope or cord
  • synonym: untie - to loosen or remove a tie or bond
