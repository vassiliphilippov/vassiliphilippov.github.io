binominal

adjective
having two names (genus and species)
3.0
Pronunciation: / baɪˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / baɪˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
Having or characterized by two names, especially in reference to the genus and species names in taxonomic classification.

Examples

Binominal scientific name
Every scientific name of an organism is a binominal composed of its genus and species.
Modern taxonomy relies on binominal nomenclature to ensure clarity and consistency.
The binominal system is used to identify species in biology.
