interpret

verb
translate spoken words between languages
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜrprət / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪt / (BrE)
to translate spoken words from one language into another, especially as an interpreter in real time

Examples

To interpret for guests
During the press conference, she interpreted the ambassador's remarks for the audience.
He was hired to interpret for the international panel discussion.
The student volunteers interpreted the guest speaker's answers in real time.
He was asked to interpret for the French guest during the meeting.
It can be difficult to interpret ancient texts written in a lost language.
She learned to interpret German conversations with ease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: misinterpret - to interpret wrongly or incorrectly
  • synonym: translate - to render written or spoken words from one language into another
