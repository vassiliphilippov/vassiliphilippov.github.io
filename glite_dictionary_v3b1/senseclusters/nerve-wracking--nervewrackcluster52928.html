Causing great anxiety or stress; extremely distressing or trying to the nerves.

Examples

A nerve-wracking situation
Giving a speech in front of hundreds can be incredibly nerve-wracking for many people.
The job interview was such a nerve-wracking experience that I could hardly sit still.
Waiting for the test results proved to be more nerve-wracking than taking the test itself.

Synonyms

  • nerve-racking
    5.0 0.78/m
    nerve-racking is an alternative form some writers use in texts, while nerve-wracking is the more common modern spelling.
  • stressful
    2.8 11/m
    stressful describes events that create general worry or pressure, while nerve-wracking means intensely upsetting to the nerves.
  • trying
    5.1 0.01/m
    trying describes something hard to endure or irritating, while nerve-wracking suggests extreme anxiety that strains the nerves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calming - making one feel less nervous or stressed
  • synonym: stressful - causing mental or emotional stress
