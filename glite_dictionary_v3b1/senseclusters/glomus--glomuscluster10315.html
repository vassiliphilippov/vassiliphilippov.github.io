glomus

noun
benign vascular tumor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊməs / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊməs / (BrE)
a usually benign, painful vascular tumor that arises from glomus cells, often found beneath fingernails or in the ear

Examples

Glomus tumor removal
The surgeon removed a glomus from under the patient's fingernail to relieve her pain.
A glomus in the ear can cause significant discomfort and sensitivity to cold.
Doctors identified the small, blue lesion as a glomus after a biopsy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemangioma - a benign tumor made up of blood vessels
  • antonym: malignant tumor - a cancerous growth that can spread to other parts of the body
