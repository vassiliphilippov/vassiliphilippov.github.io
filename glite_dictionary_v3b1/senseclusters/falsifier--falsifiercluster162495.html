falsifier

noun
person who intentionally falsifies information
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔlsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A person who deliberately alters information or facts to deceive or mislead.

Examples

A falsifier of documents
A skilled falsifier can make fake documents almost indistinguishable from the real ones.
As a known falsifier, his testimony was dismissed in court.
The accountant was accused of being a falsifier after auditing revealed manipulated records.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forger - a person who produces fake documents or works of art
  • antonym: truth-teller - a person who tells or reports the truth
