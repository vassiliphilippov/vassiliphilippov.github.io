evacuation

noun
removal of contents
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən /; / ɪˌvækjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing contents from something, especially from a container or from the body (such as discharging waste)

Examples

Bowel evacuation
A blocked sewer can prevent the proper evacuation of waste.
Medical staff ensured the complete evacuation of the patient's stomach contents.
The evacuation of the tank took several hours due to its size.
After the surgery, she experienced normal evacuation again.
Difficulty with evacuation can be a symptom of digestive problems.
The doctor asked if he had regular evacuation of his bowels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constipation - condition in which bowel evacuations are infrequent or difficult
  • synonym: defecation - the act of discharging feces from the body
