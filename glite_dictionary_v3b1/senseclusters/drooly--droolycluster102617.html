drooly

adjective
saliva-covered
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdruːli / (AmE), / ˈdruːli / (BrE)
covered with or producing a lot of saliva, especially in a messy or noticeable way

Examples

A drooly baby
The baby became drooly when she started teething and needed a bib.
After playing outside, the dog was tired and drooly on the kitchen floor.
He wiped his drooly mouth with a napkin after eating the juicy burger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - not wet or covered with liquid
  • synonym: salivating - producing saliva, often in anticipation of food
