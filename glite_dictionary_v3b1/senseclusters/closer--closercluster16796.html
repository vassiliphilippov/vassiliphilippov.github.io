closer

noun
baseball relief pitcher for final innings
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊzər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A relief pitcher who is brought in to finish a game by protecting a team's lead in the final inning or innings.

Examples

Baseball closer pitches last
As a rookie closer, he quickly made a name for himself with his fastball.
The team's closer struck out the final batter to secure the win.
They brought in their best closer to finish the ninth inning.
