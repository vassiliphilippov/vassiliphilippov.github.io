have several seats

verb
tell someone to be quiet or step back
9.4
Pronunciation: / hæv ˈsɛvrəl sits / (AmE), / hæv ˈsɛvrəl siːts / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone to stop talking, interjecting, or trying to draw attention, often because they are being presumptuous or out of line.

Examples

To have several seats now
If you think you can do my job better, please have several seats.
The fans told the critic to have several seats after his harsh comments.
When someone challenges your story without proof, just tell them to have several seats.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: have a seat - to take a seat; often inviting someone to sit
  • synonym: shut up - to stop speaking; to be quiet
