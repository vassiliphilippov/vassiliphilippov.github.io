snowblindness

noun
loss of vision from sunlight reflected by snow
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊˌblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
temporary loss of vision or impairment of sight caused by exposure to sunlight reflected by snow or ice

Examples

Severe snowblindness
Early explorers suffered severe snowblindness due to the intense glare off the Arctic ice.
High-altitude mountaineers often take precautions to avoid snowblindness during their expeditions.
Wearing proper sunglasses can help protect you from snowblindness while skiing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photokeratitis - inflammation of the cornea caused by ultraviolet light, often related to snowblindness
