sentient

adjective
able to perceive or feel; conscious
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tʃənt /; / ˈsɛnʃənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɪənt /; / ˈsɛnʃənt / (BrE)
able to perceive or feel things; having the capacity for sensation or consciousness

Examples

Sentient animals
It is believed that whales and dolphins are highly sentient creatures.
Some scientists debate whether artificial intelligence could ever become sentient.
The film explored the rights of sentient robots in a futuristic society.
Humans are considered sentient beings capable of complex emotions and thoughts.
She became painfully sentient of the tension in the room.
The novel explores a future where sentient robots interact with humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conscious - aware of and responding to one's surroundings; awake.
  • antonym: insentient - lacking the capacity to feel or perceive
