contingency fee

noun
fee paid to a lawyer only if successful
7.8 5.1
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪndʒənsi fiː / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒənsi fiː / (BrE)
a fee paid to a lawyer only if the case is won or resolved successfully

Examples

A contingency fee agreement
If the lawsuit is unsuccessful, the lawyer receives no contingency fee.
Many clients prefer hiring a lawyer with a contingency fee arrangement to avoid upfront costs.
The attorney agreed to represent them on a contingency fee basis, so payment depended on winning the case.
