shadblow

noun
North American tree/shrub (Amelanchier) with white flowers and edible fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædˌbloʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃad.bləʊ / (BrE)
Any of various North American trees or shrubs of the genus Amelanchier, with showy white flowers and edible blue-black or purplish fruit.

Examples

The shadblow tree
In early spring, the shadblow bursts into bloom before many other trees.
The garden's shadblow provided both shade and delicious berries.
We picked the ripe fruit from the shadblow near the riverbank.
