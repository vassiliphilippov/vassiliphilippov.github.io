gari

noun
granular cassava flour; staple West African food
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːri / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːri / (BrE)
A granular flour made from fermented, dried, and grated cassava, commonly used as a staple food in West African cuisine.

Examples

A bowl of gari
Many families eat gari with soup or stew as a main meal.
She bought a kilogram of gari from the market for breakfast.
Soaked gari with sugar and milk is a popular snack in Nigeria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cassava - a tropical plant with edible roots
