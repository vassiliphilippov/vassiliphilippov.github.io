agrochemical

noun
farm chemical
Pronunciation: / ˌæɡroʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌæɡrəʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
a chemical product used in agriculture to improve plant growth or protect crops from pests and diseases

Examples

Use of agrochemicals
The use of agrochemicals has increased crop yields in many countries.
Some farmers are reducing their reliance on agrochemicals to promote sustainable agriculture.
Proper handling of agrochemicals is important to protect the environment and human health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to help plants grow
  • antonym: organic - produced without synthetic chemicals
  • synonym: pesticide - a chemical used to kill pests, especially insects
