preliminary exam

noun
graduate-level qualifying examination
Pronunciation: / prɪˈlɪmɪˌnɛri ɪgˈzæm / (AmE), / prɪˈlɪmɪnəri ɪgˈzæm / (BrE)
an examination taken by graduate students to assess their readiness to continue in a program, often before advancing to candidacy

Examples

Pass the preliminary exam
All doctoral students must pass the preliminary exam before beginning their dissertation research.
Failure to pass the preliminary exam may result in dismissal from the graduate program.
She spent months preparing for her preliminary exam in biology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: qualifying exam - an official test to determine qualification for advancement in a program
