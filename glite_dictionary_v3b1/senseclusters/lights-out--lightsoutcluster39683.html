lights-out

noun
scheduled bedtime in institutions
4.6 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈlaɪts.aʊt/ (AmE), /ˈlaɪts.aʊt/ (BrE)
the scheduled time when lights must be turned off, especially as a signal for bedtime in places like schools, dormitories, or the military

Examples

Bedtime lights-out
After lights-out, the dormitory was completely silent.
The camp counselors announced lights-out at 10 p.m. sharp.
The soldiers were expected to be in their bunks by lights-out every night.
Boarding school rules required complete silence after lights-out each night.
He often read with a flashlight under the covers long after lights-out.
The sergeant blew his whistle at lights-out, and everyone had to be in bed.

  • antonym: wake-up call - a signal to wake up, especially in military or hotels
