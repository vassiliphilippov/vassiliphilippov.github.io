bill

noun
program or list of entertainment at an event
Pronunciation: / bɪl / (AmE), / bɪl / (BrE)
a printed or written notice, program, or advertisement, such as a poster, leaflet, or menu

Examples

A concert bill
A famous pianist was added to tonight's bill as a special guest.
The comedy club's bill featured several well-known comedians and a few up-and-coming acts.
The festival organizers announced the full bill of performers for the main stage.
She scanned the bill to see which play would be performed next.
The bill included a list of acts scheduled for the evening performance.
The waiter handed us the bill of fare so we could choose our meals.
A bill was posted on the community board advertising the music festival.
She received a bill in her mailbox with information about a new gym opening nearby.
The supermarket distributed a bill announcing its upcoming sales to local residents.
