guttural consonant

noun
consonant formed at the back of the mouth or throat
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌt̬ɚəl ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌt.ər.əl ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a consonant articulated in the back of the mouth or the throat, such as a velar, uvular, or pharyngeal sound

Examples

Guttural consonant sound
In Arabic, the letter 'خ' represents a guttural consonant not found in English.
Some people find it difficult to pronounce guttural consonants when learning new languages.
The sound of a guttural consonant can be quite harsh to unaccustomed ears.

Related Terms

  • antonym: labial consonant - a consonant produced with the lips
  • synonym: pharyngeal - a consonant articulated at the pharynx
  • synonym: velar - a consonant produced at the soft palate (the velum)
