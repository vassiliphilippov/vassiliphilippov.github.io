discriminant

noun
polynomial root indicator
8.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈskrɪ.mɪ.nənt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskrɪm.ɪ.nənt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈskrɪ.mɪ.nənt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskrɪm.ɪ.nənt/ (BrE)
a value calculated from a polynomial equation that shows what kind of solutions (real, complex, repeated) the equation has

Examples

Quadratic discriminant
The discriminant of a quadratic equation helps determine the number of real solutions.
If the discriminant is zero, the polynomial has a repeated root.
Students learned how to calculate the discriminant to analyze the roots of cubic equations.
The discriminant helps determine whether a quadratic equation has real or complex roots.
If the discriminant is zero, the equation has exactly one real solution.
A negative discriminant means the quadratic equation has no real solutions.

  • synonym: determinant - a value calculated from a square matrix, used in linear algebra
