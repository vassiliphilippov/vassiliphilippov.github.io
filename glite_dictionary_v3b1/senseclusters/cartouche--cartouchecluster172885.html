cartouche

noun
historical cartridge
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈtuʃ / (AmE), / kɑːˈtuːʃ / (BrE)
a paper or cartridge case containing gunpowder and a bullet or shot, used with firearms, especially in historical contexts

Examples

Paper cartouche for muskets
The soldier loaded his musket with a paper cartouche before the battle at dawn.
In the museum, you can see an original cartouche used during the Napoleonic wars.
The manual explained how to prepare a cartouche for use in early firearms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartridge - a case containing a charge and a bullet for a firearm
