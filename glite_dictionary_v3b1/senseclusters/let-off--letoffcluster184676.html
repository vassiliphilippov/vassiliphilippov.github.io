let-off

noun
excuse from punishment
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛt ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈlɛt ɒf / (BrE)
an act of being excused from punishment or a difficult situation, often with only a warning or minor consequence

Examples

A lucky let-off
The student received a let-off and was only given a warning for being late.
Many thought the fine was too small and that it was just a let-off for the company.
After explaining his situation, he got a let-off instead of a suspension.
