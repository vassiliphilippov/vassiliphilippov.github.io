scrofula

noun
tuberculosis affecting lymph nodes
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskroʊ.fjə.lə / (AmE), / ˈskrɒf.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A form of tuberculosis chiefly affecting the lymph nodes, especially in the neck, and causing swollen glands.

Examples

Swollen glands scrofula
In medieval Europe, kings were believed to heal scrofula by their touch.
Modern medicine rarely uses the term scrofula, now referring instead to tuberculous lymphadenitis.
The ancient physician diagnosed the child with scrofula due to the swollen glands in her neck.
