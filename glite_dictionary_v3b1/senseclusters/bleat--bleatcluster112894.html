bleat

verb
make a wavering animal cry; complain plaintively
6.0 0.4/m
Pronunciation: / blit / (AmE), / bliÀêt / (BrE)
(of a sheep, goat, or calf) to make a characteristic wavering cry; to speak or complain in a weak or plaintive way.

Examples

To bleat like a goat
Children visiting the farm laughed when the goats bleated loudly.
He continued to bleat about the unfair decision, but no one listened.
The lost lamb began to bleat for its mother.

Synonyms

  • baa
    5.9 0.00/m
    baa is the short sheep or lamb cry, while bleat can mean that sound or a weak, complaining human voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: moo - the characteristic sound made by a cow
  • synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or irritating way
