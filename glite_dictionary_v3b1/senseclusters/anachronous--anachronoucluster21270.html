anachronous

adjective
chronologically out of place
Pronunciation: / əˈnækrənəs / (AmE), / əˈnækrənəs / (BrE)
Belonging to a time other than the one being considered; chronologically out of place.

Examples

Anachronous event
A medieval knight at a space launch would be highly anachronous.
Her ideas seemed anachronous in today's rapidly changing world.
The use of quill pens in a modern office would be anachronous.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anachronistic - belonging to a period other than that being considered
