gallop

verb
to ride or move at a fast pace
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæləp /; / ˈɡæl.əp / (AmE), / ˈɡæləp /; / ˈɡæl.əp / (BrE)
to move or ride at the fastest pace of a horse or similar animal, with all feet off the ground together in each stride

Examples

To gallop back home
As the storm approached, they began to gallop back toward the stable.
She loves to gallop through the countryside on weekends.
The rider urged his horse to gallop across the open field.
As soon as the gate opened, the rider urged her horse to gallop ahead of the others.
Children watched in awe as the ponies galloped past the fence during the race.
The horses galloped across the open field, their manes flying in the wind.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: canter - to move at a pace between a trot and a gallop
  • degree lower: trot - to move at a pace faster than a walk but slower than a gallop
