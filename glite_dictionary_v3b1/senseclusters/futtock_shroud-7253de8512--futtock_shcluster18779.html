futtock shroud

noun
rope or chain supporting lower topmast
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfʌt.ək ʃraʊd/ (AmE), /ˈfʌt.ək ʃraʊd/ (BrE)
a rope or chain in a ship's rigging that supports the lower part of the topmast by connecting the edge of a platform (top) to the mast or shrouds below

Examples

Futtock shroud rope
A broken futtock shroud can be dangerous for sailors working on the upper rigging.
He replaced the old futtock shroud during the ship's annual maintenance.
The crew examined the futtock shroud for signs of wear before climbing aloft.
