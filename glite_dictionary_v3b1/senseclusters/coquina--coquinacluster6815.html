coquina

noun
soft shell limestone
Pronunciation: / koʊˈkiːnə / (AmE), / kəʊˈkiːnə / (BrE)
a soft, porous limestone made mostly of broken shell fragments, often used as a building material

Examples

Coquina stone wall
The historic fort was constructed from blocks of coquina found nearby.
Builders chose coquina for its ease of cutting and natural appearance.
You can still see walls made of coquina in the old part of the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: granite - a hard, durable igneous rock used in construction
  • synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate
