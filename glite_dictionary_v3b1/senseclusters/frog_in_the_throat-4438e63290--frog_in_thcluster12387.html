frog in the throat

noun
temporary hoarseness; difficulty speaking
Pronunciation: / frɑɡ ɪn ðə θroʊt / (AmE), / frɒɡ ɪn ðə θrəʊt / (BrE)
temporary hoarseness or difficulty in speaking, often caused by dryness or a need to clear one's throat

Examples

A frog in the throat
He cleared his throat, trying to get rid of the frog in the throat before continuing.
I always seem to get a frog in the throat when I wake up in the morning.
She had a frog in the throat during her speech, so her voice sounded raspy.
