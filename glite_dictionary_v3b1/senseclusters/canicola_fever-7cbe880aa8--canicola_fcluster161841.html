canicola fever

noun
an infectious disease causing fever and jaundice
Pronunciation: / ˌkænɪˈkoʊlə ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˌkanɪˈkəʊlə ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
An acute infectious disease affecting humans and dogs, characterized by fever, gastroenteritis, and mild jaundice.

Examples

Canicola fever symptoms
Jaundice was one of the symptoms observed in patients suffering from canicola fever.
Outbreaks of canicola fever have been reported in several regions this year, affecting both humans and dogs.
The veterinarian diagnosed the dog with canicola fever after it developed a high temperature and vomiting.
