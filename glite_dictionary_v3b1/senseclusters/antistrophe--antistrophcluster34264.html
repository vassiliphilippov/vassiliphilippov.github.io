antistrophe

noun
part of a choral ode in Greek drama
9.9 9.7
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈtɪs.trə.fiː/ (AmE), /æn.ˈtɪs.trə.fiː/ (BrE)
(in classical Greek drama) the section of a choral ode that responds to or mirrors a previous strophe; the second of two corresponding sections in a poem.

Examples

The antistrophe section
In ancient Greek plays, the chorus would sing the antistrophe while moving in the opposite direction to the strophe.
Scholars analyzed how the antistrophe echoed and contrasted with the themes presented in the strophe.
The poet structured his work into strophe and antistrophe to create a balanced rhythm.
