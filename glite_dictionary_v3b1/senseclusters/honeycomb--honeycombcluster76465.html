honeycomb

noun
structure of hexagonal cells
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌniˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪkəʊm / (BrE)
a structure consisting of a pattern of closely spaced hexagonal cells, typically resembling the wax comb built by bees, or any framework that imitates this pattern.

Examples

Honeycomb pattern
Engineers studied the honeycomb pattern to design more efficient building materials.
The artist carved a honeycomb motif into the wooden surface.
The lightweight panels have a honeycomb structure for added strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lattice - a regular arrangement of points or cells in space
