plessimeter

noun
instrument for medical percussion
8.8
Pronunciation: / plɛˈsɪmɪtər / (AmE), / plɛˈsɪmɪtə / (BrE)
a small, thin metal plate placed against the body and tapped with a plexor during medical percussion to detect differences in underlying tissues

Examples

A plessimeter plate
A plessimeter and a plexor are often used together in diagnostic percussion.
Medical students learn how to use a plessimeter during their physical examination classes.
The doctor placed the plessimeter on the patient's chest before tapping it gently.
