manikin

noun
life-size dummy for displaying clothes
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a life-size dummy or model of a human body, used for displaying, fitting clothes, teaching, or training

Examples

Store manikin
Each manikin was dressed in the latest fashion for the display.
The art class used a manikin to practice sketching human poses.
The designer arranged the dress carefully on the manikin in the shop window.
The medical students practiced emergency procedures on a manikin in the simulation lab.
A realistic manikin was placed in the store window to display the new clothing line.
During the first aid course, each participant used a manikin to learn chest compressions.
