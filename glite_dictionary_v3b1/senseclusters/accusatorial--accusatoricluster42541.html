accusatorial

adjective
relating to public accusation in legal trials
Pronunciation: / əˌkjuːzəˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / əˌkjuːzəˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to a legal process in which someone is publicly accused and tried for a crime, with the judge acting independently from the prosecution.

Examples

An accusatorial legal system
Many legal scholars debate the fairness of accusatorial versus inquisitorial models.
The U.S. legal system is largely accusatorial, relying on public trials and independent judges.
Unlike inquisitorial procedures, accusatorial systems emphasize the roles of prosecution and defense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inquisitorial - relating to a legal system where the judge actively investigates, as contrasted to accusatorial
