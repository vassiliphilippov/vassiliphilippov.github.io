bootlegging

noun
illegal making or selling of goods
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making, selling, or distributing goods illegally or without authorization

Examples

Bootlegging movies
Authorities cracked down on bootlegging in the local market, confiscating hundreds of counterfeit DVDs.
During Prohibition, bootlegging of alcohol became a lucrative but dangerous business.
The artist suffered financially due to widespread bootlegging of his music online.
