overblow

verb
to exaggerate; to blow too hard
5.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈbloʊ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.bloʊ/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈbləʊ/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.bləʊ/ (BrE)
to exaggerate the importance or seriousness of something, or to blow too strongly on a wind instrument

Examples

To overblow a problem
He tends to overblow minor problems until they seem huge.
If you overblow the flute, the note will sound harsh and unpleasant.
The media sometimes overblows small incidents, making them appear much more serious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it is
  • antonym: understate - to describe something as less important than it really is
