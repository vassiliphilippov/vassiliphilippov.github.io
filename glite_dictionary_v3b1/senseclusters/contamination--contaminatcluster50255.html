contamination

noun
corrupted or biased data in datasets
6.5 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / k\u0259n\u02cct\u00e6m\u026a\u02c8ne\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (AmE), / k\u0259n\u02cct\u00e6m\u026a\u02c8ne\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (BrE)
(in data science, research, and machine learning) the introduction of corrupted, biased, duplicated, or non-independent data into a dataset or evaluation set, producing misleading, invalid, or artificially improved results.

Examples

Data contamination
The study suffered from data contamination when training data leaked into the test set.
Engineers detected contamination in the dataset after finding duplicated records from the production system.
Preventing contamination between training and validation sets is essential for reliable machine-learning experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - systematic error in data or modeling that leads to unfair, inaccurate, or skewed results
  • synonym: data leakage - the accidental or improper inclusion of information from outside the training dataset that gives models access to test-set answers
