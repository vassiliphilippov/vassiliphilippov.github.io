influx

noun
arrival or entry in large numbers

Examples

A influx of people
A sudden influx of patients put a strain on the hospital's resources.
An influx of investment transformed the local economy in just a few years.
The city experienced an influx of tourists during the summer holiday.

Synonyms

  • inflow
    4.0 1.83/m
    inflow describes the steady movement of things into a place, while influx is the general arrival of many at once.
  • inrush
    6.4 0.05/m
    inrush means a sudden, strong flow into an area, while influx is a neutral term for people or things arriving.
  • flood
    2.4 5.89/m
    flood suggests an overwhelming, often hard to handle amount arriving quickly, while influx is a general arrival of many.
  • flood of visitors
    2.9 0.00/m
    flood of visitors describes a sudden crowd of people coming at once, while influx names a general arrival or entry.
