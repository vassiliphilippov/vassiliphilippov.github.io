influx

noun
arrival or entry in large numbers
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌflʌks / (AmE), / ˈɪn.flʌks / (BrE)
the arrival or entry of a large number of people or things; the process of flowing in

Examples

A influx of people
A sudden influx of patients put a strain on the hospital's resources.
An influx of investment transformed the local economy in just a few years.
The city experienced an influx of tourists during the summer holiday.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outflow - the movement of people or things out of a place
