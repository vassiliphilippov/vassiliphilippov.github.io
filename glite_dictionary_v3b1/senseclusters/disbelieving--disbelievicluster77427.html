Synonyms

  • unbelieving
    5.9 0.26/m
    unbelieving doubts or rejects religious beliefs openly, while disbelieving shows a lack of belief and refuses to accept those truths.
  • incredulous
    6.8 2.41/m
    incredulous shows surprise and cannot believe something just heard, while disbelieving means refusing to accept a claim's truth, often firmly.
  • nonbelieving
    5.4 0.00/m
    nonbelieving describes a general lack of belief in ideas or claims without strong feeling, while disbelieving implies actively refusing to accept them.
  • skeptical
    4.2 19/m
    skeptical questions ideas and waits for proof before accepting them, while disbelieving states a lack of belief and often rejects claims outright.
  • unconvinced
    5.1 1.50/m
    unconvinced is not persuaded by an argument and may change later, while disbelieving rejects truth and stands against the claim.
