wont to

adjective
in the habit of doing; accustomed
9.3 0.72/m

in the habit of doing something; accustomed or likely to do something regularly

Examples

wont to do
As she was wont to do, she took a walk each morning before breakfast.
He is wont to arrive late to meetings, much to the frustration of his colleagues.
The old professor was wont to share stories from his travels at the start of every class.

She was wont to lose herself in thought during long, quiet evenings by the fire.
