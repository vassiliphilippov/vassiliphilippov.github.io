submarine pitch

noun
baseball pitch thrown underhand or sidearm
9.4 6.0
Pronunciation: /sʌb.mə.ˈriːn pɪtʃ/ /; / /ˈsʌb.mə.riːn pɪtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.mə.riːn pɪtʃ/ /; / /sʌb.mə.ˈriːn pɪtʃ/ (BrE)
In baseball, a ball pitched with an underhand or sidearm motion, with the arm moving below the horizontal and often near parallel to the ground, instead of the usual overhand throw.

Examples

A submarine pitch style
A well-executed submarine pitch can be very difficult for hitters to track.
She learned how to throw a submarine pitch during baseball practice last summer.
The pitcher is known for his unique submarine pitch, which confuses many batters.
