low-growing

adjective
growing low to the ground (plant)
2.7
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˈɡroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈɡrəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing close to the ground; not tall or upright.

Examples

Low-growing plants
Many low-growing plants thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
The low-growing shrubs did not block the view of the lake.
This variety of low-growing thyme makes an excellent ground cover in gardens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tall - (of a plant) having a greater than average height
