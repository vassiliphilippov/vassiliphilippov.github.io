reopener

noun
cause to resume case
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈoʊpənər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈəʊpənə / (BrE)
an action, event, or statement that causes a discussion, investigation, or matter to be reopened

Examples

Case reopener event
The discovery of new evidence acted as a reopener for the old criminal case.
Her statement in court became a reopener of the long-settled dispute.
A recent audit served as a reopener for the investigation into the company's finances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closure - the act of bringing something to an end
  • synonym: trigger - something that initiates an action or event
