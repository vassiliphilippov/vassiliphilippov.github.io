Estates General

noun
French legislative assembly before 1789
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsteɪts ˈʤɛnərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈsteɪts ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the legislative assembly representing the three estates of pre-revolutionary France: the clergy, the nobility, and the commons, last convened in 1789

Examples

The Estates General assembly
Debates at the Estates General exposed deep divisions among the social classes.
Many historians see the meeting of the Estates General in 1789 as the start of the French Revolution.
The Estates General was summoned by King Louis XVI in response to the financial crisis.
