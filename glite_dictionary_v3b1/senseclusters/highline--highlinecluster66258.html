highline

verb
to transport on a cable
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to move or transport a person or load along a cable or rope stretched high above the ground

Examples

To highline a person
The rescue team had to highline the injured hiker across the river to safety.
Firefighters highlined equipment between the two buildings during the emergency drill.
In remote areas, workers sometimes highline supplies over deep ravines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haul - to pull or drag something with effort
  • antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position, often using a rope
