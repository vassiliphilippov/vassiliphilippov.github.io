fight

verb
resist or oppose strongly
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / faɪt / (AmE), / faɪt / (BrE)
to try hard to resist, oppose, or achieve something, especially with determination

Examples

To fight for rights
She decided to fight her illness with every resource available to her.
They promised to fight any attempt to close the school.
You shouldn't fight your feelings; sometimes it's okay to let go.
Despite many setbacks, he continues to fight for better healthcare policies.
She has spent her entire life fighting for equal rights for all people.
The organization fights to protect endangered species around the world.
After the accident, he had to fight for every breath.
He must fight to recover his strength after the operation.
She continued to fight against exhaustion during the final moments of the race.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - to publicly support or recommend a cause or policy
  • synonym: campaign - to work in an organized and active way toward a goal, especially a political or social one
  • synonym: oppose - to disapprove of and attempt to prevent something
  • synonym: struggle - to make forceful or violent efforts to get free of restraint or resist attack
  • antonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
