headlong

adverb
at great speed; recklessly
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.lɒŋ /; / ˈhɛdlɒŋ / (BrE)
in a very quick, hasty, or reckless way; without thinking carefully

Examples

Rush headlong
She rushed headlong into the room without knocking.
The car sped headlong down the empty street, barely missing the corner.
They dove headlong into the icy water, eager to win the race.
He went headlong down the stairs when he heard the phone ringing.
She rushed headlong into the project without considering the possible risks.
The car skidded headlong into a ditch during the storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cautiously - in a careful and well-considered manner
  • synonym: recklessly - without regard for danger or consequences
