Synonyms

  • rashly
    5.6 0.3/m
    rashly means acting quickly without thinking about consequences, while headlong also adds a strong sense of rushing and lack of care.
  • recklessly
    4.6 2/m
    recklessly describes doing something without caring about danger or results, while headlong emphasizes doing it very quickly and without thinking.
  • precipitately
    8.3 0.07/m
    precipitately means acting with excessive haste often in formal contexts, while headlong is more common and suggests sudden rushing action.
  • headily
    8.9 0.00/m
    headily means acting in a rash, impulsive way because of strong feeling, while headlong stresses quick, reckless movement.
  • headfirst
    3.3 1/m
    headfirst means going with the head leading, often literally, while headlong focuses on hasty or reckless action without thought.
