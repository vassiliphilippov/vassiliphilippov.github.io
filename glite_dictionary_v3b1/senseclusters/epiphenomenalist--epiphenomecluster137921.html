epiphenomenalist

noun
believer in epiphenomenalism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪfəˈnɑːmənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪfəˈnɒmɪnəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that mental events are caused by physical processes but do not influence them

Examples

An epiphenomenalist philosopher
The epiphenomenalist argued that consciousness is a byproduct of brain activity.
As an epiphenomenalist, she believed thoughts could not affect physical actions.
Many philosophers have debated the claims made by an epiphenomenalist about the mind-body relationship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dualist - a person who believes mind and body are separate
  • synonym: materialist - a person who believes only physical matter exists
