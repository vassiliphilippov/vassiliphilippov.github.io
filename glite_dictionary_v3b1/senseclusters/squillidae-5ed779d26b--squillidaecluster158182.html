Squillidae

noun
family of marine burrowing crustaceans
9.9
Pronunciation: / skwɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / skwɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine crustaceans (mantid shrimps) that burrow in mud or under stones in shallow water along seashores.

Examples

Squillidae family shrimps
Many species in the Squillidae family are known for their powerful claws and vibrant colors.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Squillidae family in the coastal waters of Indonesia.
The Squillidae typically make burrows in sandy or muddy areas near the seashore.
