solubilization

noun
making substances dissolve
7.8
Pronunciation: /sə.luː.bɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sə.lʊ.bɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sə.luː.bɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /səʊ.lʊ.bɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making a substance, such as a membrane or protein, dissolve in a liquid using detergents or solvents

Examples

Solubilization of proteins
The solubilization of proteins is a crucial step in many biochemical experiments.
Proper solubilization ensures that membrane components can be analyzed accurately.
Detergents are often used to aid the solubilization of hydrophobic molecules in the lab.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissolution - the act of dissolving something in a liquid
  • antonym: precipitation - the process of a substance coming out of solution
