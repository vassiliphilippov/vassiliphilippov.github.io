offensive

adjective
causing anger or annoyance
1.0
Pronunciation: / əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / əˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
causing anger, annoyance, or moral shock; likely to upset or offend people

Examples

Offensive language
Many people found his offensive comments unacceptable.
She apologized for her offensive behavior at the meeting.
That comedian's jokes are often considered offensive by some audiences.
Such conduct is offensive to the basic principles of justice.
The court found his actions offensive against the country's constitution.
Their plan was deemed offensive under international law.
Many people found the advertisement offensive and demanded that it be removed.
She apologized for her offensive remarks during the meeting.
The comedian's jokes were so offensive that several audience members walked out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insulting - expressing disrespect or scorn; causing offense
  • antonym: permissible - allowed by law or rules
  • synonym: shocking - causing surprise and dismay; morally disturbing
  • synonym: unlawful - not conforming to or permitted by law
