conservativeness

noun
cautious approach
5.2
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈsɜːr.və.tɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /kən.ˈsɜː.və.tɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being cautious, traditional, or resistant to change; preferring safe or low-risk approaches

Examples

Financial conservativeness
The company's conservativeness in financial reporting helped it avoid unexpected losses during the crisis.
Her conservativeness made her prefer safe investments over riskier opportunities.
Auditors praised the firm's conservativeness in valuing assets, which kept reported profits lower.
The conservativeness of the new statistical method helped reduce the number of false alarms.
Some scientists argue that too much conservativeness can make it harder to detect real effects.
Her conservativeness in interpreting the data ensured that only strong evidence was accepted.
Her conservativeness was evident in her preference for traditional clothing styles.
The board's conservativeness slowed the adoption of new technologies in the company.
Some people admire conservativeness for its focus on stability and tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caution - careful attention to avoid risk or mistakes
  • synonym: conservatism - a tendency to preserve existing conditions or traditions
  • antonym: progressiveness - the quality of being open to new ideas or change
  • synonym: prudence - careful and sensible decision-making
  • antonym: risk-taking - the act of taking chances or pursuing uncertain outcomes
