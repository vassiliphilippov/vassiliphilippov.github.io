workmate

noun
colleague; fellow worker
2.2 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…úÀêrkÀåme…™t / (AmE), / Ààw…úÀêkÀåme…™t / (BrE)
a person who works with you, especially someone with the same employer or at the same workplace

Examples

My workmate helps
Her workmate helped her finish the project before the deadline.
I had lunch with my workmate at the company cafeteria.
Many workmates attended the farewell party after John retired.

Synonyms

  • co-worker
    1.8 0.01/m
    co-worker is someone at the same company and often shares duties, while workmate sounds more informal for people you see every day.
  • colleague
    2.3 78/m
    colleague is used in professions and formal settings for people in the same field, while workmate is more casual and local.
  • fellow worker
    2.0 0.44/m
    fellow worker appears in older or formal texts and sounds collective, while workmate is a common, informal word for a workplace peer.

Related Terms
