hushed

adjective
quiet; spoken softly
4.1 2.07/m
Pronunciation: / hÊŒÊƒt / (AmE), / hÊŒÊƒt / (BrE)
very quiet or made quiet, often in a careful, respectful, or secretive way (used for voices, sounds, or places)

Examples

A hushed voice
She spoke in a hushed voice so as not to wake the baby.
The audience listened in hushed silence as the orchestra played softly.
Their hushed conversation drifted through the quiet library.
The museum had a hushed atmosphere as visitors admired the paintings.
They spoke in hushed voices so they would not disturb the sleeping baby.
A hushed silence fell over the crowd as the announcement began.

  • subdued
    4.2 0.96/m

  • quiet
    1.0 78/m
    quiet means not making much noise and being calm, while hushed often shows that people lower their voices carefully or secretly.
  • silent
    1.0 31/m
    silent means not speaking or making sound at all, while hushed means very quiet but not necessarily totally without sound.
