crayer

noun
historic cargo-carrying sailing vessel
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.ə / (BrE)
a two- or three-masted sailing vessel, formerly used for transporting cargo in the Baltic Sea and northern Europe

Examples

A crayer ship
Historians have reconstructed a model of a medieval crayer for the museum.
In the 16th century, the crayer was a common sight in Baltic ports.
The merchant's goods were loaded onto a crayer bound for Sweden.
