intussusception

noun
folding of a surface to form a pocket
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tə.sə.ˈsep.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.sə.ˈsep.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process in which a part of a structure folds into itself, creating a pocket or cavity within its own surface

Examples

Tissue intussusception
During embryonic development, intussusception helps form important body structures.
In biological systems, intussusception can occur in tissues to increase surface area.
Some microorganisms move by intussusception, where their cell membrane folds inward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invagination - the action or process of being turned inside out or folded back on itself to form a cavity or pouch
