telocentric chromosome

noun
chromosome with centromere at end
9.2
Pronunciation: /te.lə.ˈsen.trɪk ˈkroʊ.mə.soʊm/ /; / /te.lə.ˈsen.trɪk ˈkroʊ.mə.zoʊm/ (AmE), /te.lə.ˈsen.trɪk ˈkrəʊ.mə.səʊm/ /; / /te.lə.ˈsen.trɪk ˈkrəʊ.mə.zəʊm/ (BrE)
A chromosome in which the centromere is located at the very end, forming a straight rod shape.

Examples

A telocentric chromosome rod
A telocentric chromosome appears as a straight rod during microscopic examination because of the terminal centromere.
In mice, every chromosome is a telocentric chromosome, with the centromere positioned at the tip.
Unlike metacentric types, a telocentric chromosome has no arm on one side of the centromere.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: acrocentric chromosome - chromosome with centromere near one end, not terminal
  • degree lower: metacentric chromosome - chromosome with centromere in the middle
