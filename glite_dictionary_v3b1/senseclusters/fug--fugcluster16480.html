fug

noun
stuffy, smoky atmosphere
Pronunciation: / fʌɡ / (AmE), / fʌɡ / (BrE)
a stuffy, smoky, or stale atmosphere in a room or building, often unpleasant to breathe

Examples

The fug in room
After hours of meetings, the fug in the conference room became unbearable.
The fug of cigarette smoke filled the tiny pub.
When I entered the old apartment, the fug hit me almost immediately.
As we entered, the fug of stale tobacco smoke hit us immediately.
By morning, the fug in the tent was almost unbearable.
He opened a window to clear the fug from the small, crowded room.

  • synonym: haze - cloud of smoke, dust, or vapor
  • synonym: smog - fog mixed with smoke or other pollutants
