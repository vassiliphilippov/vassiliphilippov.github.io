Pollyanna

proper noun
overly optimistic person
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈænə / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈænə / (BrE)
a person who is extremely or unrealistically optimistic, always expecting the best in every situation

Examples

A real Pollyanna
Even during the crisis, he acted like a Pollyanna and believed everything would work out.
Her friends called her a Pollyanna because she always saw the bright side of every problem.
It is not always helpful to be a Pollyanna when facing serious challenges.
Even in difficult times, she remained a Pollyanna, always believing things would get better.
Some people called him a Pollyanna because of his constant cheerfulness, no matter the situation.
It's important to be positive, but acting like a Pollyanna can sometimes ignore real problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: optimist - a person who tends to expect the best
  • antonym: pessimist - a person who tends to expect the worst
  • antonym: realist - a person who accepts situations as they are and deals with them practically
