June beetle

noun
large North American leaf-eating beetle
6.4 4.4
Pronunciation: /dʒuːn ˈbiː.təl/ (AmE), /dʒuːn ˈbiː.təl/ /; / /dʒjuːn ˈbiː.təl/ (BrE)
A large, usually brown North American beetle, common in late spring, whose larvae feed on the roots of grasses and other plants.

Examples

A June beetle swarm
Every summer, June beetles gather around porch lights in swarms.
The larvae of the June beetle can damage lawns by eating grass roots.
We found a large June beetle resting on the back porch last night.

Related Terms
