preemptory

adjective
preventive action
8.0
Pronunciation: / priˈɛmptəri / (AmE), / priˈɛmptəri / (BrE)
done to prevent an anticipated event or to forestall someone else's action; intended to preempt.

Examples

Preemptory action
The general took a preemptory step to secure the border before any threat could arise.
A preemptory warning was issued to avoid possible confusion during the event.
They made a preemptory decision to cancel the meeting before any problems developed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preemptive - done to prevent something from happening by acting first
  • antonym: reactive - responding to an event after it has happened
