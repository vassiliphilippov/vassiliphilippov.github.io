consequentialist

adjective
focused on outcomes
9.2
Pronunciation: /kən.sɪ.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪst/ /; / /kɑːn.sɪ.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪst/ (AmE), /kɒn.sɪ.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪst/ /; / /kən.sɪ.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪst/ (BrE)
relating to the belief that the morality of an action depends on its consequences or outcomes

Examples

Consequentialist ethics theory
A consequentialist theory judges actions by the results they produce.
Her consequentialist perspective led her to consider the long-term effects of every decision.
Many debates in ethics contrast consequentialist and deontological approaches to morality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deontological - relating to the belief that actions are right or wrong in themselves, regardless of outcomes
  • synonym: utilitarian - relating to the idea that the best action is the one that produces the most good
