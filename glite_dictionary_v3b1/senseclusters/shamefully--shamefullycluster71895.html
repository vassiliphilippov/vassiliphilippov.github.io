shamefully

adverb
in a shameful or disgraceful way
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪmfəli / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmfəli / (BrE)
in a way that causes or shows shame, embarrassment, or disgrace

Examples

To shamefully neglect duties
He shamefully neglected his responsibilities at work.
She was shamefully treated by her colleagues after the mistake.
The politician acted shamefully to cover up the scandal.
He shamefully admitted that he had forgotten her birthday.
The student shamefully avoided eye contact after being caught cheating on the test.
She shamefully apologized for her rude behavior at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disgracefully - in a way that causes public shame or embarrassment
  • synonym: guiltily - in a way that shows guilt
  • antonym: honorably - in a manner showing honor or integrity
  • antonym: proudly - in a way that shows pride or satisfaction
