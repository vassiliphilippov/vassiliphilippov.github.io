collateral

adjective
additional, secondary; auxiliary
1.8 6.7
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
additional, secondary, or supporting something else; not the main or primary thing

Examples

Collateral damage
In some medical cases, collateral blood vessels can provide an alternative pathway for circulation.
The investigation revealed a number of collateral issues that needed attention.
The project offered several collateral benefits beyond its initial goals.
Strict security measures were implemented, with some collateral inconvenience to regular employees.
The airstrike caused significant collateral damage to nearby civilian infrastructure.
The new policy had several collateral effects that were not anticipated by lawmakers.
According to the report, collateral documents supported the main findings.
The committee considered several forms of collateral information during the investigation.
The lawyer introduced collateral evidence to strengthen her argument.
