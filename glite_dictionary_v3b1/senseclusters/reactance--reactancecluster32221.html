reactance

noun
resistance to control
9.2
Pronunciation: / riˈæktəns / (AmE), / riˈæktəns / (BrE)
a tendency to resist being influenced or controlled by others, especially when feeling one's freedom is threatened

Examples

Teen reactance
Teenagers often display reactance when they feel their independence is being limited.
Strict rules at the camp led to increased reactance among the children.
Managers should be aware of employee reactance when introducing new policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliance - the act of obeying or conforming to a rule or request
  • synonym: defiance - open resistance or bold disobedience
