bullpup

noun
compact firearm design
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlˌpʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌpʌp / (BrE)
a firearm design in which the action and magazine are located behind the trigger, making the weapon more compact

Examples

A bullpup weapon
The new bullpup design allows soldiers to maneuver more easily in tight spaces.
Many modern armies have adopted bullpup rifles for their compact size and balance.
A bullpup configuration can reduce the overall length of a weapon without sacrificing barrel length.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbine - a short-barreled lightweight rifle
  • antonym: conventional firearm - a firearm with the action and magazine in front of the trigger
  • synonym: rifle - a long gun designed to be fired from the shoulder
