herpes

noun
virus causing painful blisters or sores
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrpiːz / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz / (BrE)
any of a group of viruses causing painful blisters or sores on the skin or mucous membranes, especially around the mouth or genitals.

Examples

Herpes virus infection
Cold sores around the lips are often caused by the herpes virus.
She learned that herpes can be managed with antiviral medications and good health practices.
The doctor diagnosed the cluster of blisters as a possible case of herpes.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: herpes simplex - a specific type of herpes virus causing oral or genital sores
  • degree higher: herpes zoster - a herpes virus causing shingles
