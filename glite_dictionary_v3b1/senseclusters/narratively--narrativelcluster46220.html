narratively

adverb
in story structure
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈnæ.rə.tɪv.liː/ /; / /ˈnæ.rə.tɪv.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈnæ.rə.tɪv.liː/ /; / /ˈnæ.rə.tɪv.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that relates to the structure, style, or telling of a story or narrative

Examples

Used narratively in stories
The novel is narratively rich, weaving together several characters' stories.
The documentary is narratively straightforward, following a clear chronological order.
Critics praised the film for being narratively innovative and engaging.
Narratively, the film takes an unusual approach by starting at the end and working backward.
The book is narratively complex, with multiple timelines and perspectives.
Narratively, the story flows smoothly despite frequent flashbacks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptively - in a way that describes rather than tells a story
  • synonym: structurally - in a way that relates to the arrangement or organization of something
  • antonym: thematically - in terms of the theme or subject
