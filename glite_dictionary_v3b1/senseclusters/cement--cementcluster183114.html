cement

noun
bony substance covering tooth root
Pronunciation: / səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
a material used in dentistry to fill, attach, or cover parts of teeth

Examples

Dental cement filling
Damage to the cement can lead to dental problems such as periodontitis.
In dentistry, cement plays a key role in securing the tooth within the gum.
The cement layer is found beneath the gumline, covering the tooth's root.
After the root canal, a temporary cement was applied to protect the tooth.
Some types of dental cement can also be used to attach crowns or bridges.
The dentist used a special cement to fill the cavity in my tooth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filling - material used to fill a cavity in a tooth
