Synonyms

  • peril
    5.7 7.49/m
    peril is serious, immediate risk often used in urgent or dramatic contexts, while danger is a general possibility of harm.
  • jeopardy
    6.0 7.06/m
    jeopardy is a formal word for being in a risky situation, often used in legal speech, while danger is general.
  • threat
    2.3 82/m
    threat is a person or thing likely to cause harm or fear, often with intent, while danger is the general possibility of harm.
  • hazard
    3.4 13/m
    hazard is a physical source or condition that may cause injury or trouble, often in safety signs, while danger is broader.
  • risk
    1.9 264/m
    risk is the chance or probability that something bad may happen, used in planning and reports, while danger is the harm itself.
  • menace
    4.4 8.26/m
    menace is a person or thing that threatens harm in a worrying, often active way, while danger is a neutral general possibility.
