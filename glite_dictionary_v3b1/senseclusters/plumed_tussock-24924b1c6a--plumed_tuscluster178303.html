plumed tussock

noun
New Zealand grass with plume-like flowers
Pronunciation: / plumd ˈtʌsək / (AmE), / pljuːmd ˈtʌsək / (BrE)
a tall grass native to New Zealand, known for its plume-like flower heads

Examples

Plumed tussock grass
Gardeners often choose plumed tussock for its striking, ornamental flower heads.
In autumn, the seeds of the plumed tussock are carried away by the wind.
The plumed tussock grows abundantly in the grasslands of New Zealand.
