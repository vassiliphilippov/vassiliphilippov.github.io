precipitate

noun
solid formed from a solution (chemistry)
4.8 6.5
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.tət/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /prə.ˈsɪp.ɪ.tət/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.tət/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /prə.ˈsɪp.ɪ.tət/ (BrE)
a solid substance separated from a solution, typically as a result of a chemical reaction, often appearing as a suspension or after settling or filtering

Examples

A yellow precipitate
A yellow precipitate indicates the presence of certain metal ions in the solution.
After mixing the two solutions, a white precipitate formed at the bottom of the test tube.
The chemist filtered the precipitate from the liquid to analyze its composition.
