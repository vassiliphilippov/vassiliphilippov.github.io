lawyering

verb
to use legal tactics
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːjərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːjərɪŋ / (BrE)
to act as a lawyer or use legal knowledge and tactics to handle a situation, often by making technical arguments

Examples

To lawyer a contract
She spent the afternoon lawyering the contract to ensure her client was fully protected.
The negotiations dragged on as both sides kept lawyering every minor detail.
He is known for lawyering his way out of difficult situations with clever arguments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mediate - to try to end a dispute between two or more people or groups
