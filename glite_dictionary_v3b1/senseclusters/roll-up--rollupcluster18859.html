roll-up

noun
business consolidation
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈrəʊl ʌp / (BrE)
a business strategy in which several smaller companies are combined through acquisitions to form a larger company.

Examples

Roll-up strategy
The investment group executed a roll-up to create a leading player in the healthcare industry.
A successful roll-up can help small businesses compete with larger corporations.
The roll-up strategy allowed the company to expand rapidly across multiple regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquisition - the act of one company buying another
  • antonym: divestiture - the process of selling off subsidiary business interests
  • synonym: merger - the combination of two or more companies into one
