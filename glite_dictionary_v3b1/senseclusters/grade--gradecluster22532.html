grade

noun
unit of angle (one-hundredth of a right angle)
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪd / (AmE), / ɡreɪd / (BrE)
A unit of angle equal to one-hundredth of a right angle, used especially in surveying and engineering; also known as 'gon.'

Examples

A 90 grade angle
A right angle is exactly 100 grades in this measurement system.
In Europe, angles are sometimes given in grades rather than in degrees.
The surveyor measured the angle as 25 grades instead of degrees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degree - unit of angle equal to 1/360 of a circle
  • synonym: gon - unit of angle, same as grade (one-hundredth of a right angle)
