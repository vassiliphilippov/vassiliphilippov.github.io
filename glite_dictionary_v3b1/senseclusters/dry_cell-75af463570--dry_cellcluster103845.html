dry cell

noun
type of battery with paste electrolyte
2.9 3.3
Pronunciation: /draɪ sel/ (AmE), /draɪ sel/ (BrE)
A type of electric battery in which the electrolyte is a moist paste, not a free liquid, and the zinc container acts as the negative electrode; used in devices such as flashlights and portable radios.

Examples

A dry cell battery
He replaced the old dry cell in his flashlight before going camping.
Most portable radios use a dry cell as their power source.
The invention of the dry cell made batteries safer and more convenient for home use.

  • antonym: wet cell - type of battery with liquid electrolyte
