craniate

noun
animal with a skull (cranium)
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkreɪnɪeɪt / (BrE)
Any animal with a skull, including all vertebrates and their extinct relatives distinguished by the presence of a cranium enclosing the brain.

Examples

A craniate animal
All mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish are classified as craniates.
Scientists discovered a fossilized craniate that dates back over 500 million years.
The presence of a skull distinguishes a craniate from other chordates.
