cymene

noun
aromatic hydrocarbon found in cumin and thyme
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪmiːn / (AmE), / ˈsɪmiːn / (BrE)
Any of three isomeric, colorless, aromatic liquid hydrocarbons found in the volatile oils of cumin and thyme, used in the manufacture of synthetic resins.

Examples

cymene in oils
Industrially, cymene is used as an intermediate in producing synthetic resins.
Research into thyme’s medicinal properties often references the presence of cymene among its volatile compounds.
The volatile oil extracted from cumin contains a significant amount of cymene.
