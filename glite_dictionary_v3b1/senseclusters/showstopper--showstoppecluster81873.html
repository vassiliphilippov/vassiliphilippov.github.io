showstopper

noun
something exceptionally impressive or striking
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊˌstɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌstɒpə / (BrE)
something or someone that is exceptionally impressive or attention-grabbing, especially in a performance

Examples

A showstopper performance
Her performance was such a showstopper that the crowd gave a standing ovation.
That painting is a showstopper in the gallery—it’s what everyone comes to see.
The bride's dress was a real showstopper, dazzling everyone at the wedding.
Her solo in the musical was a real showstopper that had the whole audience on their feet.
That dress is a showstopper; everyone will be talking about it for weeks.
The cake at the wedding was a showstopper, both in taste and appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticlimax - a disappointing end to an exciting series of events
  • antonym: dud - something that fails to work or excite
  • synonym: highlight - the most outstanding part of an event or period
