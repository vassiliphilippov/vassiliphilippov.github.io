adductive

adjective
drawing toward body midline (muscles)
8.2
Pronunciation: / əˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / əˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
(of muscles) bringing together or drawing toward the midline of the body or toward an adjacent part.

Examples

Adductive muscles
Injury to adductive muscles can affect an athlete's balance and coordination.
Physical therapy often focuses on strengthening adductive muscles to improve mobility.
The adductive function of the muscle helps bring the arm closer to the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abductive - moving away from the midline of the body (muscles)
