endocrine gland

noun
gland that secretes hormones into the blood
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndəˌkraɪn ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊkraɪn ɡlænd / (BrE)
A gland that produces and secretes hormones directly into the bloodstream, forming part of the endocrine system.

Examples

Endocrine gland function
Damage to any endocrine gland can disrupt hormone balance in the body.
Doctors study each endocrine gland to diagnose hormone-related diseases.
The thyroid is an important endocrine gland that regulates metabolism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exocrine gland - gland that releases secretions through ducts
