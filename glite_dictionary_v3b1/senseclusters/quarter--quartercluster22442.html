quarter

noun
mercy to a defeated opponent
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔɚtɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə / (BrE)
mercy or clemency shown to someone who has been defeated, especially in battle

Examples

Ask for quarter
He asked for quarter, trusting in the honor of his opponents.
The enemy soldiers begged for quarter after their lines were broken.
To give no quarter means to show no mercy to the vanquished.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone
  • antonym: ruthlessness - showing no pity or mercy
