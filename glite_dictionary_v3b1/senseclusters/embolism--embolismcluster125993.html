embolism

noun
insertion of a day or month into a calendar
Pronunciation: / ˈɛm.bə.lɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈɛm.bə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the insertion of a day or month into a calendar, such as in leap years

Examples

Calendar embolism
A calendar embolism often involves adding a leap day or a leap month.
The ancient Romans used embolism to keep their lunar calendar in sync with the solar year.
The reform introduced a system of embolism to address inconsistencies in the old dating method.
