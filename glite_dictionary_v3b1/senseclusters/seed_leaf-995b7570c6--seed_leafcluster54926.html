seed leaf

noun
embryonic leaf in a seed
1.8 4.4
Pronunciation: /siːd liːf/ (AmE), /siːd liːf/ (BrE)
an embryonic leaf found in the seeds of seed-bearing plants, often the first leaf or pair of leaves to emerge during germination

Examples

The seed leaves emerge
After planting, the seed leaves were the first part of the sprout visible above the soil.
Different plants have either one or two seed leaves at the start of their development.
The seed leaf provides vital nutrients to the young plant as it grows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cotyledon - the botanical term for a seed leaf
