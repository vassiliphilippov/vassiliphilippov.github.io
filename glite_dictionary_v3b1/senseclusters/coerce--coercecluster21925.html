coerce

verb
compel by force or threat
6.4 5/m
Pronunciation: / ko äÀà…úrs / (AmE), / k…ô äÀà…úÀês / (BrE)
to make someone do something by using force or threats

Examples

To coerce a person
He tried to coerce her into signing the contract against her will.
No one should be coerced into making a confession.
The rebels coerced villagers to provide them with supplies.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • force
    2.3 130/m
    force uses physical power or influence to make someone do something, while coerce emphasizes making someone act by threats.
  • strong-arm
    7.5 0.7/m
    strong-arm uses threats or violence to make someone comply quickly, while coerce covers any use of force or pressure.
  • compel
    4.8 18/m
    compel makes someone do something because rules, duty, or need demand it, while coerce uses threats or force to make someone act.
  • intimidate
    4.9 13/m
    intimidate frightens someone to make them do what you want, while coerce means forcing action by threats or pressure.
  • pressure
    4.2 8/m
    pressure tries to persuade or push someone to act, often without threats, while coerce involves clear force or threats to make someone comply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compel - to pressure or drive someone to do something
  • synonym: force - to make someone do something against their will
  • antonym: persuade - to convince someone to do something through reasoning or discussion
