override

noun
manual control for bypassing automation
4.9 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvɚˌraɪd /; / ˈoʊvərˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌraɪd / (BrE)
a device or action that allows someone to take control over or bypass an automatic system, or to overrule a decision or rule

Examples

Manual override button
If the automatic doors get stuck, use the manual override.
The engineer engaged the override to stop the machine immediately.
The security system has an override in case of emergency.
Congress voted to override the president's veto, nullifying the original decision.
The judge issued an override to prevent the law from taking effect.
There is a manual override for the automatic safety system in case of emergency.

  • synonym: overrule - to rule against or reject the decision of another
  • antonym: validate - to make valid or confirm the effectiveness of something
