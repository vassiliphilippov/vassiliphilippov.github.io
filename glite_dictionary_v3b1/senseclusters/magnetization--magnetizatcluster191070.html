magnetization

noun
the process of becoming magnetic
8.4 5.4
Pronunciation: /mæɡ.nə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mæɡ.nə.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mæɡ.nə.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mæɡ.nə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a material becomes magnetic, either temporarily or permanently

Examples

Process of magnetization
After exposure to a magnetic field, the rod showed a clear increase in magnetization.
Demagnetizing a material involves reversing its magnetization process.
The magnetization of iron can be achieved by rubbing it with a strong magnet.
During the experiment, students observed the gradual magnetisation of the iron filings.
Heating a material can affect its magnetisation and sometimes cause it to lose its magnetic properties.
The magnetisation of the metal rod was achieved by stroking it with a magnet.
Rapid changes in magnetisation can affect how electronic devices function.
Researchers measured the magnetisation of the material at varying temperatures.
The magnetisation of the iron bar increased when it was placed near a strong magnet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnetization - US spelling of 'magnetisation'; the process of making a substance magnetic
