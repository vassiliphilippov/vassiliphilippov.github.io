flimmer

verb
to tremble slightly
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈflɪm.ər/ (AmE), /ˈflɪm.ə/ (BrE)
to move, tremble, or shine with a faint, wavering, or flickering light

Examples

To flimmer softly
The candle's flame began to flimmer as a breeze entered the room.
She watched the butterfly's wings flimmer gently in the sunlight.
Nervousness made his hands flimmer during the speech.
The candlelight began to flimmer as the wind picked up.
Stars flimmered faintly in the night sky above the quiet village.
The old television screen would sometimes flimmer before turning off completely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flicker - to shine unsteadily or with a wavering light
  • antonym: glow - to give out a steady light
  • synonym: quiver - to shake with slight, rapid movements
  • synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements
  • synonym: shimmer - to shine with a soft, tremulous light
  • synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically from emotion or cold
