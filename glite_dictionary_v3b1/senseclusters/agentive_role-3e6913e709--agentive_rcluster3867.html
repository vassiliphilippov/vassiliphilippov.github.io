agentive role

noun
semantic role of initiator in a clause
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒəntɪv roʊl / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒ(ə)ntɪv rəʊl / (BrE)
the semantic role of an animate entity that initiates or causes the action expressed by a verb in a clause

Examples

Agentive role in sentence
In the sentence 'Mary opened the door', 'Mary' occupies the agentive role.
Linguists analyze who takes the agentive role to understand sentence structure.
The agentive role is typically filled by the subject in active sentences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agent - the person or thing that performs the action in a sentence
  • antonym: patient - the entity that undergoes the action or is affected by it
