turn on

verb
suddenly become hostile or aggressive
2.4
Pronunciation: / tɝn ɑn / (AmE), / tɜːn ɒn / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to suddenly become hostile, unfriendly, or aggressive towards someone.

Examples

To turn on a friend
After years of loyalty, the politician's supporters suddenly turned on her when the scandal broke.
Friends can sometimes turn on each other over the smallest disagreements.
If you corner a wild animal, it might turn on you without warning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turn against - to become opposed or hostile to someone or something
