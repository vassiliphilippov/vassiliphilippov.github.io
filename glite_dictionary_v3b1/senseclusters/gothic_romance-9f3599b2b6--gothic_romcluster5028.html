Gothic romance

noun
literary genre featuring mystery and the supernatural
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk rəʊˈmæns / (BrE)
A literary genre characterized by mysterious, desolate, and often grotesque events, typically set in gloomy or medieval environments.

Examples

A Gothic romance novel
Mary Shelley's 'Frankenstein' is often considered a classic Gothic romance.
She enjoyed reading Gothic romance novels full of storms, secrets, and tragic characters.
The dark, haunted castle set the perfect scene for a chilling Gothic romance.
