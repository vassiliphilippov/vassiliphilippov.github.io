fairy godmother

noun
magical helper in fairy tales
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛri ˈɡɑdˌmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfeəri ˈɡɒdˌmʌðə / (BrE)
A woman in fairy tales who uses magical powers to help the protagonist or grant wishes, often bringing unexpected good fortune.

Examples

A magical fairy godmother
Every child wishes for a fairy godmother to grant their secret dreams.
In the story, the fairy godmother transformed a pumpkin into a carriage for Cinderella.
She often acted like a fairy godmother to students in need, offering guidance and support.
