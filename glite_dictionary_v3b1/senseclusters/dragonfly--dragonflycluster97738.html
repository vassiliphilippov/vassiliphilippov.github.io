dragonfly

noun
slender, fast-flying insect with flat, transparent wings
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡənˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdræɡənˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a fast-flying insect with a long, slender body and two pairs of large, transparent wings that are usually held out flat when at rest

Examples

A dragonfly insect
A bright blue dragonfly hovered over the pond, darting from one lily pad to another.
Children chased the dragonfly as it skimmed above the tall grass on a summer afternoon.
The wings of the dragonfly glistened in the sunlight as it rested on a reed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damselfly - a closely related insect, more delicate and with wings held together over the body at rest
