Saint John

proper noun
disciple of Jesus; author of biblical texts
6.0
Pronunciation: /seɪnt dʒɑːn/ /; / /sənt dʒɑːn/ (AmE), /seɪnt dʒɒn/ /; / /sənt dʒɒn/ (BrE)
A disciple of Jesus Christ in the New Testament, traditionally believed to be the author of the fourth Gospel, three epistles, and the book of Revelation.

Examples

Saint John the Apostle
In his sermon, the priest spoke about the vision of Saint John in the book of Revelation.
Many believe that Saint John wrote the Gospel that emphasizes love and faith.
Paintings often depict Saint John as a young man beside Jesus at the Last Supper.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apostle - one of the twelve chief disciples of Jesus
  • synonym: evangelist - writer of one of the four Gospels in the New Testament
