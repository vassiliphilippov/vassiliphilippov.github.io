wakeboard

noun
board for riding behind a boat on water
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪkˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈweɪkˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a buoyant board, similar to a surfboard, used to ride over water while being towed by a motorboat

Examples

A wakeboard ride
He bought a new wakeboard for the summer water sports season.
She learned how to balance on the wakeboard during her first lesson.
The wakeboard glided smoothly over the lake as the boat picked up speed.
After losing balance, she fell off her wakeboard with a big splash.
He strapped his feet onto the wakeboard and waited for the boat to accelerate.
Learning to turn on a wakeboard can be challenging but exciting for beginners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surfboard - long narrow board for riding ocean waves
