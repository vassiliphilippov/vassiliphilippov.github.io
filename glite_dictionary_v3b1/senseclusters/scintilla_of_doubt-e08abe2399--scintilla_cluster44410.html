scintilla of doubt

noun
a tiny amount of uncertainty
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈtɪlə əv daʊt / (AmE), / sɪnˈtɪlə əv daʊt / (BrE)
a very small or scarcely detectable amount of uncertainty or suspicion

Examples

A scintilla of doubt
Despite the evidence, she held onto a scintilla of doubt about his guilt.
I finished the exam without a scintilla of doubt that I had passed.
In his voice, there was not a scintilla of doubt about the decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shadow of a doubt - the slightest doubt or uncertainty
