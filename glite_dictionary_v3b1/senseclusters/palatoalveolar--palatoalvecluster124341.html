palatoalveolar

adjective
speech sound location
Pronunciation: / ˌpælət̬oʊˈælvioʊlər / (AmE), / ˌpælətəʊˈælvɪələ / (BrE)
describing a speech sound produced with the tongue near the hard palate and the alveolar ridge

Examples

Palatoalveolar sound
The 'sh' in 'shoe' is a classic example of a palatoalveolar sound in English.
Linguists study how palatoalveolar consonants are pronounced in different languages.
Children often learn to produce palatoalveolar sounds as their speech develops.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: alveolar - articulated with the tongue against the alveolar ridge
  • degree higher: palatal - articulated with the tongue against the hard palate
  • synonym: postalveolar - articulated just behind the alveolar ridge
