patch

noun
small contrasting area or section
2.6 2.3
Pronunciation: /pætʃ/ (AmE), /pætʃ/ (BrE)
a small area that is different from what surrounds it, such as a spot of color or a piece of ground with plants

Examples

A patch of grass
Leopards are known for the patches on their coats.
There was a patch of bright blue on the otherwise gray wall.
We found several patches of ice on the sidewalk this morning.
She grew strawberries in a small patch behind the house.
The children searched for frogs in the vegetable patch.
We planted herbs in a sunny patch of the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bed - a small area of ground for growing plants in a garden
  • degree higher: field - an area of open land, especially one planted with crops or pasture
  • synonym: plot - a small piece of land used for a particular purpose (such as gardening)
  • synonym: spot - a small round or irregular mark differing in color or surface
  • synonym: stain - a discolored or dirty spot or area
