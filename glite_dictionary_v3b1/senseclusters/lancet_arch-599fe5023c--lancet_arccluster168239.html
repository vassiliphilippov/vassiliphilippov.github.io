lancet arch

noun
pointed Gothic architectural arch
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlæn.sɪt ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈlæn.sɪt ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an acutely pointed arch with a tall, narrow form, typical of early Gothic architecture

Examples

A lancet arch window
Many buildings from the early Gothic period feature the distinctive lancet arch in their doorways.
The cathedral's windows are topped with beautiful lancet arches that draw the eyes upward.
The restoration work carefully preserved each original lancet arch in the ancient abbey.
