immunity

noun
official exemption from duty or penalty
5.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
official protection or exemption from a duty, penalty, or legal action, especially granted by authorities.

Examples

Legal immunity from prosecution
Because of his diplomatic status, he claimed immunity from prosecution.
The court granted her immunity after she agreed to cooperate with the investigation.
The witness was offered immunity in exchange for his testimony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liability - the state of being legally responsible for something
  • synonym: protection - the act of keeping someone or something safe from harm or loss
