ordain

verb
appoint as clergy (church)
Pronunciation: / ɔːrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to officially make someone a priest, minister, or other religious official by a special ceremony

Examples

To ordain a priest
After years of study, she was finally ordained as a minister.
Only bishops have the authority to ordain priests in that denomination.
The church plans to ordain three new deacons this Sunday.
He felt called to serve and hoped to be ordained by the end of the year.
She was ordained as a minister in 2015, after completing her theological studies.
The bishop will ordain three new priests during the ceremony on Sunday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consecrate - to make or declare sacred, especially for religious purposes
  • antonym: depose - to remove from office or position, especially in church context
