Maxwell's equations

noun
set of four fundamental electromagnetic equations
Pronunciation: / ˈmæksˌwɛlz ɪˌkweɪʒənz / (AmE), / ˈmækswɛlz ɪˌkweɪʒənz / (BrE)
The four fundamental differential equations formulated by James Clerk Maxwell, which describe the classical behavior of electric and magnetic fields and their interactions.

Examples

Maxwell's equations in physics
Engineers use Maxwell's equations when designing antennas and electrical circuits.
Every physics student must learn Maxwell's equations to understand electromagnetism.
The development of wireless communication was made possible thanks to Maxwell's equations.
