regurgitation

noun
rote repetition of learned information
Pronunciation: / rɪˌɡɝːdʒəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌɡɜːdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of repeating information exactly as learned, often without understanding or analysis

Examples

Mere regurgitation of facts
He criticized the curriculum for encouraging mere regurgitation rather than creativity.
Some students rely heavily on regurgitation of facts instead of critical thinking.
The final exam tested understanding, not just regurgitation of textbook material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: memorization - learning information by heart
