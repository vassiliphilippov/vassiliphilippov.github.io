desegregate

verb
end racial or ethnic separation
4.1 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈsɛɡrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈsɛɡ.rɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to end a policy or practice of keeping people of different races or ethnic groups separate; to open a place or organization to all groups equally

Examples

To desegregate schools
It took years of legal battles to finally desegregate the school district.
Many activists fought hard to desegregate the transportation system.
The city decided to desegregate its public swimming pools in the late 1960s.
