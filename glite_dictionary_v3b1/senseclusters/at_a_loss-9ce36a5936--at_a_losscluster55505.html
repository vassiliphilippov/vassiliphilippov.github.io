at a loss

adverb
for less than cost; making a loss
2.7 3.5
Pronunciation: /ət ə lɔːs/ /; / /æt ə lɔːs/ (AmE), /ət ə lɒs/ /; / /æt ə lɒs/ (BrE)
For less money than the original cost or value; making a financial loss.

Examples

Sell at a loss
Many investors panicked and sold their shares at a loss during the economic crisis.
The retailer was forced to sell winter coats at a loss to clear out old inventory.
They decided to sell their house at a loss after the market downturn.
