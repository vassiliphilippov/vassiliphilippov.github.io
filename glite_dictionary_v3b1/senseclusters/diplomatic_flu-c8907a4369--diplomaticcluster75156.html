diplomatic flu

noun
post-summit fatigue in diplomacy (humorous)
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk fluː / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk fluː / (BrE)
A humorous term for exhaustion or stress brought on by intensive diplomatic meetings, negotiations, or summits.

Examples

Diplomatic flu after talks
After a week of international talks, several delegates claimed to have diplomatic flu.
She used diplomatic flu as an excuse to skip the press conference following the tense negotiations.
Sometimes leaders blame diplomatic flu to avoid awkward diplomatic engagements.
