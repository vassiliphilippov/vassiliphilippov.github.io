coyness

noun
affected shyness to attract attention
4.6 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of pretending to be shy or modest in an attempt to be appealing or to attract attention

Examples

Playful coyness
He was both charmed and frustrated by her playful coyness during their conversation.
Her coyness was clearly intentional, making her all the more intriguing at the party.
Sometimes, excessive coyness can come across as insincere or manipulative.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boldness - the quality of being confident and daring
  • synonym: modesty - the quality of being unassuming or moderate in the estimation of one's abilities
  • synonym: shyness - the quality of being reserved or having or showing nervousness around other people
