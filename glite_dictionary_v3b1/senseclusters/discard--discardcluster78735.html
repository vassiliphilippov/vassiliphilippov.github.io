discard

noun
something thrown away
7.1
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkɑrd /; / ˈdɪs.kɑrd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːd /; / ˈdɪs.kɑːd / (BrE)
an object or item that has been thrown away or rejected because it is no longer wanted or needed

Examples

A discard pile
She picked up a discard from the pile to use in her art project.
The fisherman sorted through the discard to check if anything valuable had been thrown away.
The recycling center collects every discard left on the curb.
After sorting through her closet, she put all the discards into a donation box.
In poker, the player added his discard to the pile before drawing a new card.
The recycling bin was filled with old magazines and other discards from the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: keep - to retain something rather than get rid of it
  • synonym: reject - an object that has been refused or not accepted
