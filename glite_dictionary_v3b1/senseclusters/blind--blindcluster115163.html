blind

noun
something used to conceal true nature or purpose
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd / (AmE), / blaɪnd / (BrE)
a person, action, or device intended to conceal the true nature or purpose of something; a cover or pretense

Examples

Blind for a plan
He used volunteering as a blind to mask his true intentions.
The elaborate event was just a blind to distract people from the real plan.
The fake company acted as a blind for their illegal operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover - something that is used to hide or disguise something else
  • synonym: front - an appearance or activity used to disguise a person’s real intentions
  • synonym: pretext - a false reason given to hide the real purpose
