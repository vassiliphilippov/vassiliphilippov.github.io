mistake

noun
an incorrect action or decision
Pronunciation: / mɪˈsteɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈsteɪk / (BrE)
an incorrect action, decision, or part of something; an error or flaw

Examples

An honest mistake
It was an honest mistake, so no one blamed him.
Making a small mistake at work taught him to be more careful in the future.
She realized her mistake as soon as she sent the email to the wrong person.
His speech was clear and convincing, with hardly a mistake in it.
Please check this report for any mistake before we send it to the client.
The teacher pointed out every mistake in my essay so I could learn from them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake
  • antonym: correct - free from error or mistake
  • antonym: correction - the action of correcting a mistake
  • synonym: error - a mistake, especially in writing or calculation
