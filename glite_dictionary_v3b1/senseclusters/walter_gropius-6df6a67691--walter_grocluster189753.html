Walter Gropius

proper noun
German-American architect, Bauhaus founder
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔltɚ ˈgroʊpiəs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːltə ˈgrəʊpiəs / (BrE)
(1883–1969) German-born United States architect, founder of the Bauhaus school, influential in modern architecture.

Examples

Walter Gropius architect
Walter Gropius emphasized the unity of art, craft, and technology in building design.
Many students of architecture study the works of Walter Gropius to understand functional and modern design.
The iconic Barcelona Pavilion was influenced by the theories of Walter Gropius and the Bauhaus movement.
