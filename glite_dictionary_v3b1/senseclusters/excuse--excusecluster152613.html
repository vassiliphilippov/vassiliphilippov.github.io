excuse

verb
ask leave from engagement or gathering
2.2 1.5
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskjuz / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːz / (BrE)
to allow or give permission for someone to leave or be absent from a place or duty

Examples

Excuse yourself politely
During dinner, he stood up and excused himself to use the restroom.
If you need to leave early, simply excuse yourself and thank your host.
She politely asked to excuse herself from the meeting to take an urgent call.
Please excuse me from tomorrow's meeting if possible.
She was excused from jury duty due to her illness.
The teacher excused the students who had completed the assignment early.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pardon - to officially allow someone not to be punished or held to a requirement
