hyperpolarization

noun
increased polarization
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an increase in the alignment or polarization of atomic or nuclear spins, often used to enhance signals in imaging techniques like MRI

Examples

MRI hyperpolarization
The scientists achieved hyperpolarization to improve the sensitivity of their MRI scans.
Recent advances in hyperpolarization have made it possible to detect smaller tumors using imaging.
By applying hyperpolarization, researchers can observe metabolic processes in real time.
