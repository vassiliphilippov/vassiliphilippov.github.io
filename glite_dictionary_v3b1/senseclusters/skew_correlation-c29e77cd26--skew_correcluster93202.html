skew correlation

noun
nonlinear statistical relationship
6.4 9.3
Pronunciation: / skju ˌkɔːr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / skjuː ˌkɒr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statistical relationship between two variables in which the rate of change between them is not constant

Examples

A skew correlation pattern
A skew correlation can make data difficult to interpret using linear methods.
The researchers observed a skew correlation in the growth rates of the two species.
When variables display a skew correlation, traditional scatterplots may not reveal the true relationship.
