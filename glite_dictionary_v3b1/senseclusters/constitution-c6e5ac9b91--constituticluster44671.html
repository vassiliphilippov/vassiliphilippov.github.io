Constitution

proper noun
the foundational document of U.S. government
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.stəˈtu.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the fundamental document, ratified in 1788, that establishes the framework of government for the United States of America

Examples

The US Constitution document
Many legal scholars spend years studying the Constitution and its amendments.
The Constitution lays out the structure of the federal government and the rights of citizens.
There are ongoing debates about how the Constitution should be interpreted in modern times.
