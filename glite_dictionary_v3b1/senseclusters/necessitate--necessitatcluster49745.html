to make something necessary; to require as a result or consequence

Examples

To necessitate changes
Changing climate conditions necessitate adjustments in farming practices.
The complexity of the new software necessitates further staff training.
The surgery was necessitated by a severe injury to his knee.
Growing demand for the product necessitated an expansion of the factory.
Serious injuries sometimes necessitate immediate surgery.
The increase in traffic accidents may necessitate further road safety measures.

Synonyms

  • require
    1.1 364/m
    require often means to need or make something necessary in rules or plans, while necessitate is more formal and shows a cause.
  • call for
    3.2 20/m
    call for suggests a situation asks for a response or action, while necessitate is a more formal way to say something must happen.
  • entail
    4.8 11/m
    entail involves something as a necessary part or consequence, while necessitate means to make something required because of a cause.
  • need
    1.0 1430/m
    need expresses an everyday lack or necessity for people or things, while necessitate is more formal and means to cause something to be necessary.
