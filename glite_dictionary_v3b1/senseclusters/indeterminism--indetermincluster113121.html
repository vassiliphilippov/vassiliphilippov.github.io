indeterminism

noun
unpredictability of events
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈtɜːrmɪnɪzəm /; / ˌɪndɪˈtɜrmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈtɜːmɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or theory that not all events are determined by previous causes and that some things may happen by chance or unpredictably

Examples

Indeterminism in physics
Quantum physics often relies on the concept of indeterminism to explain unpredictable outcomes.
Some philosophers argue that indeterminism allows for true free will in human actions.
The indeterminism in weather patterns makes long-term forecasts challenging.
Philosophers have debated indeterminism and its implications for human freedom for centuries.
Quantum physics is often cited as scientific evidence supporting indeterminism in nature.
Some people reject indeterminism because they believe every event must have a cause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinism - the belief that all events are determined by causes and can be predicted
  • synonym: free will - the ability to choose actions independently of external causes
  • synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not able to be predicted
