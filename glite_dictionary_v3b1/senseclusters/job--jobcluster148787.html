job

noun
responsibility or duty
1.0
Pronunciation: / dʒɑb / (AmE), / dʒɒb / (BrE)
a task, duty, or piece of work that needs to be done, especially as part of your responsibilities or for payment

Examples

A cleaning job
Her job is to answer customer complaints promptly and professionally.
It was never my job to supervise the project.
It's the manager's job to ensure everyone follows safety procedures.
He gave up fixing the clock as a bad job after several failed attempts.
She did an outstanding job designing the new website.
You really did a thorough job cleaning the kitchen today.
Finishing the report was a job that took most of the evening.
He was given a challenging job designing the new website.
She completed the cleaning job much faster than expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: botch - a badly done piece of work
  • synonym: chore - a routine task or small job
  • synonym: difficulty - a situation that is hard to deal with or overcome
  • synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; a responsibility
  • synonym: problem - a matter or situation regarded as unwelcome or harmful needing to be dealt with
  • synonym: task - a piece of work to be done or undertaken
