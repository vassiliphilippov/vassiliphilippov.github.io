submaster

noun
secondary control
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a secondary control or authority, especially in audio mixing or as an assistant to a main master

Examples

Audio submaster control
The sound engineer adjusted the submaster to balance the audio levels during the concert.
In the school, the submaster was responsible for discipline when the headmaster was away.
Each section of the audio board had its own submaster for finer control over the mix.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assistant - a person who helps or supports someone in a higher position
  • antonym: master - a person or device with primary control or authority
