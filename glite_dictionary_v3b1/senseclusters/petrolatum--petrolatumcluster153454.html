petrolatum

noun
semisolid substance from petroleum
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛtrəˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A thick, oily, semisolid substance derived from petroleum, used in ointments, cosmetics, and as a lubricant.

Examples

Thick petrolatum ointment
Petrolatum is often used to protect minor cuts and burns.
Many lip balms use petrolatum as a key moisturizing ingredient.
The doctor recommended applying petrolatum to the wound to keep it moist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: petroleum jelly - semi-solid mixture of hydrocarbons used as ointment or lubricant
