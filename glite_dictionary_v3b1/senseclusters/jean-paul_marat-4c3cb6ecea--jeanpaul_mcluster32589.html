Jean-Paul Marat

proper noun
French revolutionary leader (1743–1793)
Pronunciation: / ʒɑn pɔl məˈrɑ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ pɔl maˈʁa / (BrE)
(1743–1793) A French revolutionary leader born in Switzerland, prominent in overthrowing the Girondists, who was assassinated in his bath by Charlotte Corday.

Examples

Jean-Paul Marat revolutionary leader
Jean-Paul Marat's radical writings inspired many revolutionaries throughout France.
Many painters have depicted Jean-Paul Marat at the moment of his death in the bath.
The assassination of Jean-Paul Marat became a symbol of the French Revolution's turmoil.
