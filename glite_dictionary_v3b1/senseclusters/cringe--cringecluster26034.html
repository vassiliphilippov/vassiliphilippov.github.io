cringe

verb
to feel embarrassed
4.9
Pronunciation: /krɪndʒ/ (AmE), /krɪndʒ/ (BrE)
to react with embarrassment, discomfort, or awkwardness, often by making a face or drawing back

Examples

To cringe with embarrassment
I always cringe when I hear people arguing loudly in public.
He cringed at the memory of his embarrassing mistake during the meeting.
They couldn't help but cringe as the awkward silence dragged on.

She cringed at the sound of her own voice on the recording.
Many people cringe when they see embarrassing photos from their childhood.
She could not help but cringe when her father started dancing at the party.
I always cringe at the sound of my own recorded voice.
He cringed as his friend told an awkward story in front of everyone.

  • synonym: recoil - to move back suddenly, often from fear or disgust
  • synonym: wince - to react with a slight movement or expression to pain or embarrassment
