hyperreactive

adjective
extremely reactive
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərriˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərriˈæktɪv / (BrE)
very likely to undergo a chemical reaction, often reacting quickly or violently with other substances

Examples

Hyperreactive chemical
The new chemical was so hyperreactive that it had to be stored in a special container.
Scientists observed a hyperreactive response when the substance was exposed to moisture.
Because the compound is hyperreactive, it must be handled with extreme caution in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inert - not likely to react chemically
  • degree lower: reactive - likely to undergo a chemical reaction
