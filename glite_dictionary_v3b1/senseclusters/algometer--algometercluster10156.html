algometer

noun
pain-measuring device (by pressure)
Pronunciation: / ælˈɡɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to measure sensitivity to pain, especially by applying controlled pressure to the skin or tissues

Examples

A algometer device
After the injury, an algometer was used to compare pain sensitivity between the affected and unaffected limbs.
Researchers often rely on an algometer to assess pressure-induced pain in clinical trials.
The doctor used an algometer to determine the patient's pain threshold during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dolorimeter - an instrument for measuring pain sensitivity or intensity
