warbly

adjective
quavering or shaky
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔːr.blɪ/ /; / /ˈwɔːr.bliː/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.blɪ/ /; / /ˈwɔː.bliː/ (BrE)
having a wavering, shaky, or trilling quality in sound or voice, often moving up and down in pitch

Examples

A warbly voice
The old radio produced a warbly sound that made the music hard to recognize.
Her warbly singing style set her apart from other performers.
A warbly note escaped from the flute as he tried to play softly.
The singer's warbly voice gave the song a unique and charming character.
We woke to the warbly calls of birds outside our window each morning.
Her flute playing was light and warbly, filling the hall with cheerful notes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flat - lacking variation in pitch or tone
  • synonym: melodious - having a pleasant musical sound
  • antonym: smooth - even and without interruptions or shakiness
  • synonym: warbling - singing or making sounds with rapid changes of pitch
  • synonym: wobbly - unsteady or shaky
