Sciurus

noun
genus of tree squirrels
Pronunciation: / saɪˈjʊrəs / (AmE), / saɪˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of moderate-sized, tree-dwelling squirrels found in Eurasia and the Americas.

Examples

The Sciurus squirrel
Researchers studied the behavior of several Sciurus species to understand their adaptability.
The genus Sciurus includes the common gray squirrel found in many North American parks.
The red squirrel, a member of the genus Sciurus, is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
