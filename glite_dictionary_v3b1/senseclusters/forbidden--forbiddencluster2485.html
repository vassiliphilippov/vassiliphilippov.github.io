Synonyms

  • prohibited
    3.7 0.01/m
    prohibited is officially not allowed by law or authority in formal contexts, while forbidden covers law, rules, or custom.
  • banned
    1.1 0.00/m
    banned is declared illegal or not allowed by an authority and often used in public rules, while forbidden is broader.
  • proscribed
    8.2 0.00/m
    proscribed is used in formal or legal texts to name things an authority rejects, while forbidden appears in general speech and rules.
  • outlawed
    5.0 0.00/m
    outlawed means made illegal by law, often after a formal process, while forbidden can mean banned by rules or custom too.
  • illicit
    6.3 3/m
    illicit describes things that are illegal or secret and often criminal, while forbidden covers legal, rule, or social bans more broadly.
  • verboten
    9.2 0.3/m
    verboten is a loanword often used for strict social or official bans with forceful tone, while forbidden is neutral and general.
  • unallowable
    6.4 0.01/m
    unallowable says something breaks rules or standards and sounds formal, whereas forbidden is common and used in many contexts.
  • off-limits
    4.6 5/m
    off-limits refers to places or topics you cannot enter or discuss, while forbidden covers any ban by law, rule, or custom.
  • taboo
    5.2 2/m
    taboo is a cultural ban often linked to feelings or shame, while forbidden can be legal, formal, or simple rule-based.
