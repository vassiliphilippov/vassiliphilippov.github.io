skirt

verb
move along the edge of
7.2
Pronunciation: / skɝt /; / skɝːt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
to go around or move along the edge or border of something

Examples

To skirt the edge
Clouds skirted the horizon as the storm approached.
The hikers skirted the base of the mountain to avoid climbing the steep path.
The new road will skirt the city to reduce traffic in the center.
A narrow path skirts the old stone wall.
Clouds skirted the tops of the distant mountains.
The road skirts the eastern edge of the village, avoiding the town center.
A winding road skirts the foot of the mountain, offering beautiful views.
The old city wall used to skirt the entire neighborhood.
The river skirted the farm, protecting it from the north wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: border - to form the edge or boundary of something
  • synonym: bypass - to go around rather than through
  • synonym: encircle - to surround or form a circle around
  • synonym: surround - to be all around (someone or something)
