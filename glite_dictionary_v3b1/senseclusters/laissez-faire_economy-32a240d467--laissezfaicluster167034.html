laissez-faire economy

noun
economy with minimal government interference
7.2 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / l…õse…™ Ààf…õr …™Ààk…ëÀên…ômi / (AmE), / l…õse…™ Ààfe…ô …™Ààk…ín…ômi / (BrE)
an economic system in which transactions, prices, and the allocation of resources are determined primarily by free market forces, with minimal government intervention.

Examples

A laissez-faire economy
In a laissez-faire economy, businesses operate with little or no government regulation.
Some economists argue that a laissez-faire economy leads to innovation and efficiency.
The nation's leaders debated whether a laissez-faire economy was the best path to growth.

Synonyms

  • free enterprise
    3.8 2.80/m
    free enterprise is a system where private businesses compete with little state control, while laissez-faire economy stresses minimal government interference.
  • private enterprise
    3.9 1.71/m
    private enterprise means businesses are owned privately and guided by markets, while laissez-faire economy focuses on minimal state intervention in all transactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: command economy - an economy in which production, prices, and allocation of resources are controlled by the government
  • synonym: free-market economy - an economy where prices and resources are determined by supply and demand, with little government control
