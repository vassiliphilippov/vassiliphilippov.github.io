Examples

A ruined building
After the fire, the historic house was completely ruined and unsafe to enter.
The scandal left his reputation utterly ruined in the eyes of the public.

Synonyms

  • destroyed
    2.8 0.00/m
    destroyed is completely damaged and made unusable, while ruined often stresses lasting loss that cannot be fixed or returned to its former state.
  • in ruins
    3.8 1.08/m
    in ruins describes a place or thing in complete decay or severe disrepair, while ruined means destroyed beyond repair or use.
  • ravaged
    5.9 0.01/m
    ravaged shows violent, wide damage by force or war, while ruined means it cannot be used or restored to its original state.
  • wrecked
    4.0 0.00/m
    wrecked is badly broken, often by accidents or disasters, while ruined means permanently unusable or destroyed in a lasting way.
  • shattered
    4.8 0.03/m
    shattered is broken into pieces or deeply shaken emotionally, while ruined means destroyed or harmed beyond repair in use or reputation.
  • finished
    4.4 0.02/m
    finished means no longer successful or able to continue, often about people or careers, while ruined means destroyed beyond repair.
