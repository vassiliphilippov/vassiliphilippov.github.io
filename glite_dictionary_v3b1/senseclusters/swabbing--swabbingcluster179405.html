swabbing

noun
collecting biological samples with a swab
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈswɑːb.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈswɒb.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in medicine) the act of taking a biological sample using a swab (for example, a nasal or throat swab) for laboratory or diagnostic testing.

Examples

Medical swabbing
The clinic recorded thousands of swabbing procedures during the peak of the flu season.
After exposure, workers received a phone call about their scheduled swabbing for COVID-19 on Monday.
Proper swabbing technique is essential to avoid false negatives in rapid antigen tests.
