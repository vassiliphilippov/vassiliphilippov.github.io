standard language

noun
most accepted variety of a language
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈstændɚd ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstændəd ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The variety of a language that is accepted as the norm for official, educational, and formal use, often considered the most correct form.

Examples

Standard language form
Broadcast news announcers typically speak in a standard language to be widely understood.
Some writers choose to blend regional dialects with standard language to achieve a unique style.
Students are often encouraged to use standard language in academic writing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dialect - regional or social variety of a language
  • antonym: vernacular - the language spoken by ordinary people in a region
