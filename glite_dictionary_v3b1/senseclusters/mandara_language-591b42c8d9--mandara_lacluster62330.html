Mandara language

noun
language of the Mandara mountains, Cameroon
Pronunciation: / mænˈdɑrə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / manˈdɑːrə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken in the Mandara mountains of Cameroon, noted for having only two vowel sounds

Examples

Mandara language in Cameroon
Only a few communities in Cameroon still speak the Mandara language.
Researchers documented several traditional stories in the Mandara language during their fieldwork in the region.
The Mandara language has fascinated linguists due to its remarkably simple vowel system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Chadic language - a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family spoken in parts of Central and Western Africa
