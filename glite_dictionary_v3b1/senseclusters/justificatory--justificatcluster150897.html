justificatory

adjective
intended to justify or defend
2.9
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈstɪfɪkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / dʒʌˈstɪfɪkətəri / (BrE)
intended to justify, defend, or provide a reason for something

Examples

A justificatory tone
His report adopted a strongly justificatory tone, seeking to explain every controversial decision.
Rather than admitting fault, the company issued a justificatory statement to the press.
The lawyer's justificatory arguments failed to convince the jury of his client's innocence.
His actions were driven by a justificatory motive rather than personal gain.
The lawyer presented a justificatory argument to defend her client.
The report lacked any justificatory evidence for the proposed changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accusatory - suggesting or assigning blame or fault
  • synonym: defensive - intended or used to defend or protect
  • synonym: justifying - serving to justify; showing something is reasonable
