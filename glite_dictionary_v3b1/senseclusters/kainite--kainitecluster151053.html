kainite

noun
a mineral used in fertilizers
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaɪnaɪt / (BrE)
a white or colorless mineral composed of hydrated magnesium sulfate and potassium chloride, used as a source of potassium salts and in fertilizers

Examples

White kainite mineral
Farmers often use kainite as an affordable potassium fertilizer.
Geologists discovered large deposits of kainite in the salt mines.
The soil was enriched with kainite to boost its potassium content.
