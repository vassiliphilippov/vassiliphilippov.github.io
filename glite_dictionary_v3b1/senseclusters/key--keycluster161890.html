key

noun
small low island or reef, esp. in Florida
Pronunciation: / kiː / (AmE), / kiː / (BrE)
a low, small, typically sandy island or coral reef, especially one off the southern coast of Florida

Examples

Florida key
Many species of birds and fish inhabit the coral reefs and keys along Florida's coast.
The hurricane caused significant erosion on the small key where the lighthouse stands.
We spent our vacation on a remote key in the Florida Keys, surrounded by clear blue water.
