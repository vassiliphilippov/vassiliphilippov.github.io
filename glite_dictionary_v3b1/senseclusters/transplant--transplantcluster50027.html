transplant

verb
move to a new place; replant
Pronunciation: / trænsˈplænt / (AmE), / trænsˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to move something, someone, a plant, or an idea from one place or situation to another

Examples

To transplant a tree
After the surgery, the patient needed time to recover from the decision to transplant her kidney.
Farmers transplant rice shoots by hand to ensure proper spacing in the flooded fields.
In the spring, gardeners often transplant seedlings into larger pots to promote healthy growth.
Authors sometimes transplant ancient myths into contemporary contexts to make them more relatable.
Many European traditions were transplanted to America by immigrants.
The director decided to transplant the Shakespearean play to a modern setting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replant - to plant again in another place
  • synonym: transfer - to move something or someone from one place to another
