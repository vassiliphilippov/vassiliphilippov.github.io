plague

verb
annoy or trouble persistently
3.0 4.6
Pronunciation: / pleɪɡ / (AmE), / pleɪɡ / (BrE)
to cause continual or serious trouble, suffering, or annoyance to someone or something

Examples

To plague someone daily
Anxiety has been plaguing her for weeks before the big exam.
Fans plagued the celebrity with endless requests for autographs.
Mosquitoes plague campers throughout the summer months.
She was plagued with doubts about her decision even years later.
The crops were plagued by a mysterious disease all summer.
The region has been plagued by frequent power outages this winter.
