reaction formation

noun
psychological defense mechanism of adopting opposite behavior
6.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈæk.ʃən fɔːr.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən fɔːr.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən fɔː.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(Psychology) A defense mechanism in which a person unconsciously adopts attitudes or behaviors that are the opposite of their unacceptable repressed desires, in order to hide those desires from themselves or others.

Examples

Reaction formation defense
His overly polite manner may just be a reaction formation masking his underlying anger.
Some people use reaction formation to deal with feelings they find unacceptable, like extreme generosity to hide selfishness.
The psychologist suggested that her hostility was actually a reaction formation against her feelings of affection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defense mechanism - an unconscious psychological strategy used to cope with reality and maintain self-image
