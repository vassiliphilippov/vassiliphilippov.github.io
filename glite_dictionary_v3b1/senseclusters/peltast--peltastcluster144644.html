peltast

noun
Greek light infantry
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛlˌtæst / (AmE), / ˈpɛltɑːst / (BrE)
a light infantry soldier in ancient Greece who fought as a skirmisher, armed with a small shield and javelins

Examples

Ancient Greek peltast
The peltast was valued for his speed and ability to harass enemy lines from a distance.
Unlike the heavily armored hoplites, a peltast relied on agility and light equipment.
