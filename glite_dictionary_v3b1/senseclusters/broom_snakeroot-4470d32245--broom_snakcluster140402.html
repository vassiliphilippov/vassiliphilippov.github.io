broom snakeroot

noun
Southwestern U.S. subshrub with yellow flower heads
Pronunciation: / bruːm ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / bruːm ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
A low-growing, sticky subshrub of the southwestern United States, characterized by narrow linear leaves, many slender branches, and numerous tiny yellow flower heads.
