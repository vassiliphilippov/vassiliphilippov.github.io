cyberattack

noun
attack on computers
4.4
Pronunciation: /saɪ.bər.ə.ˈtæk/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.bər.ə.tæk/ (AmE), /saɪ.bə.ə.ˈtæk/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.bə.ə.tæk/ (BrE)
a deliberate attempt to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems or networks

Examples

A major cyberattack
The government warned companies to prepare for a possible cyberattack on their networks.
After the cyberattack, the hospital's computer systems were offline for several days.
A major cyberattack can cause financial losses and damage a company's reputation.
The company suffered a major cyberattack that shut down its online services for hours.
Governments are investing more in security to prevent cyberattacks on critical infrastructure.
After the cyberattack, customers were advised to change their passwords immediately.
