percolation

noun
filtration process for extraction or purification
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpɜr.kəˈleɪ.ʃən /; / ˌpɝː.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of a liquid passing slowly through a filter or porous substance

Examples

Percolation of water
Proper percolation of water through the coffee grounds produces a richer flavor.
The percolation process can help remove impurities from drinking water.
They tested the soil's percolation rate before installing the septic system.
After heavy rain, the percolation of water slowed due to compacted soil.
Efficient percolation is important for removing impurities from drinking water.
Scientists studied the percolation of groundwater through different layers of rock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filtration - the process of removing solid particles from a liquid or gas by passing it through a filter
  • synonym: infiltration - the process of entering or permeating a substance, especially gradually
