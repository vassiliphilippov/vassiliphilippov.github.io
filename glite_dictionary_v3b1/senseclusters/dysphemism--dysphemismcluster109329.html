dysphemism

noun
offensive or disparaging expression
9.1
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfɛmɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɛmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an offensive or disparaging term used instead of a more neutral or inoffensive one

Examples

An offensive dysphemism
Describing a used car as a 'junk heap' is an example of dysphemism.
In literature, authors sometimes employ dysphemism to convey strong negative emotions.
Politicians often use dysphemism to attack their opponents and sway public opinion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: euphemism - a mild or inoffensive expression used in place of a harsh or blunt one
