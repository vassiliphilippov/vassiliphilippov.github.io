pituitary gland

noun
master gland at base of brain
3.0 4.3
Pronunciation: / pɪˈtu.əˌtɛr.i ɡlænd / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuː.ɪ.tər.i ɡlænd / (BrE)
a small, pea-shaped gland at the base of the brain that is the principal gland of the endocrine system, controlling other glands and many bodily functions

Examples

The pituitary gland controls hormones
Damage to the pituitary gland can disrupt the body's hormonal balance.
Doctors discovered a small tumor on her pituitary gland after a series of tests.
The pituitary gland releases hormones that regulate growth, metabolism, and reproduction.
