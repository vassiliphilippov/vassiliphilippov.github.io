conjugation

noun
chemical linking of molecules
7.0
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.dʒə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.dʒə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒn.dʒə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The chemical process of linking two molecules or molecular groups to form a conjugate, often used in chemistry, biochemistry, and pharmaceutical applications.

Examples

Chemical conjugation
Biopharmaceutical researchers use conjugation to attach drugs to antibodies for targeted cancer therapy.
The lab optimized the conjugation conditions to preserve protein activity after attachment.
Successful conjugations of fluorescent dyes enabled clear imaging of cellular structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioconjugation - the covalent linking of biomolecules, especially for research or therapeutic use
  • synonym: conjugate - a molecule formed when two chemical species are joined together
