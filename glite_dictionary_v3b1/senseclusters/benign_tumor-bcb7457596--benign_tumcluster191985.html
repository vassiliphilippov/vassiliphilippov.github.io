benign tumor

noun
non-cancerous tumor
3.9 2.9
Pronunciation: /bɪ.ˈnaɪn ˈtuː.mər/ /; / /bɪ.ˈnaɪn ˈtjuː.mər/ (AmE), /bɪ.ˈnaɪn ˈtjuː.mə/ /; / /bɪ.ˈnaɪn ˈtuː.mə/ (BrE)
a tumor that is not cancerous, does not invade surrounding tissues, and does not spread to other parts of the body

Examples

A benign tumor
Although the benign tumor was growing slowly, it did not threaten his health.
Most benign tumors can be removed surgically with little risk of recurrence.
The doctor explained that her benign tumor would not require aggressive treatment.
After surgery, the biopsy confirmed that the mass was a benign tumour and would not spread.
Although a benign tumour is not cancerous, it may still require removal if it causes discomfort.
The doctor reassured her that the lump was a benign tumour and not a cause for alarm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: malignant tumor - a tumor that is cancerous and can spread to other parts of the body
  • antonym: malignant tumour - a cancerous tumor that can spread to other parts of the body
  • degree lower: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue
