racetracker

noun
race monitoring device
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪs.træk.ər/ (AmE), /ˈreɪs.træk.ə/ (BrE)
a device or computer application that monitors and displays the real-time position, speed, or progress of participants in a race

Examples

Digital racetracker
The coach checked the racetracker to see each runner's position and lap time.
Using a racetracker, fans could follow their favorite cyclist throughout the entire race.
The new racetracker app provides live updates on every participant's progress during the marathon.
