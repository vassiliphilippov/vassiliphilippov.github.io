cartelize

verb
to form a cartel
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize companies or businesses into a cartel in order to control prices or limit competition

Examples

To cartelize companies
Several companies decided to cartelize in order to control the market and maximize profits.
Authorities launched an investigation after evidence showed the firms had begun to cartelize their operations.
If industries cartelize, consumers may face higher prices and fewer choices.
The companies decided to cartelize the industry to keep prices artificially high.
Authorities discovered that several firms had secretly cartelized the market for years.
Efforts to cartelize the sector were met with strong opposition from regulators.
Several large firms attempted to cartelize the industry to maximize their profits.
Laws were introduced to prevent companies from trying to cartelize the market.
Authorities investigated whether the manufacturers had secretly cartelized their production.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compete - to try to win or gain something against others
  • synonym: monopolize - to take complete control of a market or industry
