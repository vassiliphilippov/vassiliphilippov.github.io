alright

adverb
used to reinforce an assertion
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɔlˈraɪt / (AmE), / ɔːlˈraɪt / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that there is no doubt; certainly or indeed.

Examples

Talented alright
He's talented, alright, but he's not disciplined.
It's a tough job, alright, but someone has to do it.
The movie was scary, alright, so don't watch it alone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certainly - without any doubt; used for emphasis
  • antonym: possibly - indicating a lower degree of certainty
  • synonym: surely - used to emphasize the speaker’s firm belief in the truth of their statement
