rehospitalization

noun
return to hospital
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌhɑːspɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌhɒspɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of being admitted to a hospital again after having been discharged

Examples

Patient rehospitalization after discharge
The patient required rehospitalization just two weeks after her initial discharge.
Doctors are working to reduce the rate of rehospitalization among elderly patients.
Proper follow-up care can help prevent unnecessary rehospitalization.
The clinic tracks rehospitalization rates to evaluate the effectiveness of their follow-up care.
Reducing rehospitalization is a major goal for healthcare providers seeking to improve patient outcomes.
After his surgery, careful monitoring helped prevent an unnecessary rehospitalization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discharge - the act of officially allowing someone to leave a hospital
  • synonym: readmission - the process of being admitted again to a hospital or institution
