twist of the knife

noun
action that makes a bad situation worse
Pronunciation: / twɪst əv ðə naɪf / (AmE), / twɪst əv ðə naɪf / (BrE)
an action or remark that deliberately increases someone’s pain or distress, especially after a previous setback or disappointment

Examples

A sharp twist of the knife
Being laid off was bad enough, but hearing my boss praise my replacement was a real twist of the knife.
For her, the news coverage of his success was another twist of the knife after their painful breakup.
When he announced his promotion right after my rejection, it felt like a twist of the knife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: add insult to injury - to make a bad situation even worse by saying or doing something more hurtful
