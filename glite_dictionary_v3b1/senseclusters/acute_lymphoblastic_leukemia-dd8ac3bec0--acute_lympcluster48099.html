acute lymphoblastic leukemia

noun
a childhood cancer of white blood cells
5.7
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkjuːt lɪm.fə.ˈblæs.tɪk luː.ˈkiː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkjuːt lɪm.fə.ˈblæs.tɪk luː.ˈkiː.mi.ə/ /; / /ə.ˈkjuːt lɪm.fə.ˈblæs.tɪk ljuː.ˈkiː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
A type of acute leukemia marked by rapid proliferation of immature lymphoblast-like cells in the bone marrow, lymph nodes, spleen, and blood, most commonly found in children.

Examples

Acute lymphoblastic leukemia in children
Advancements in treatment have significantly improved survival rates for patients with acute lymphoblastic leukemia.
Researchers are developing new therapies to reduce side effects in children with acute lymphoblastic leukemia.
The child was diagnosed with acute lymphoblastic leukemia after experiencing persistent fatigue and frequent infections.
