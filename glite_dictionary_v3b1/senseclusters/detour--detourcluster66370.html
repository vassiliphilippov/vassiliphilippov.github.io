detour

verb
take a longer or alternate route
3.7 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdiÀêÀåt är / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀêt ä…ô / (BrE)
to travel by taking a longer or different route than usual, especially to avoid an obstacle or reach a destination indirectly.

Examples

To detour around traffic
Because of the parade downtown, drivers detoured along quieter streets to reach their offices.
Road construction forced the school bus to detour through a nearby neighborhood.
We had to detour around the flooded section of the road.

Synonyms

  • divert
    4.2 5.07/m
    divert makes someone or something change direction, while detour is taking a longer route to avoid an obstacle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bypass - to go around instead of through a place or obstacle
