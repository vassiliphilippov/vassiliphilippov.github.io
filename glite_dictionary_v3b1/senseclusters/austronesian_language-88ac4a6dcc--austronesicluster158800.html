Austronesian language

noun
language family of Southeast Asia and the Pacific
7.0 6.4
Pronunciation: /ɔː.stroʊ.ˈniː.ən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ /; / /ɑː.stroʊ.ˈniː.ən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ɔː.strəʊ.ˈniː.ən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a member of a large family of languages spoken across Southeast Asia, the Pacific Islands, and parts of Madagascar

Examples

Austronesian language family
Malagasy, spoken in Madagascar, is an Austronesian language despite the island's location off the coast of Africa.
Many Austronesian languages are found on islands scattered across the Pacific Ocean.
Tagalog is an Austronesian language spoken in the Philippines.
