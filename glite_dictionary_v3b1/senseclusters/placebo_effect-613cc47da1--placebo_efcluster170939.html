placebo effect

noun
improvement due to belief in a fake treatment
4.7 4.4
Pronunciation: / pləˈsiboʊ ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / pləˈsiːbəʊ ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a beneficial effect produced by a placebo treatment, which cannot be attributed to the properties of the placebo itself and must therefore be due to the patient's belief in the treatment

Examples

The placebo effect in medicine
Many patients experienced improvement in their symptoms due to the placebo effect.
Researchers must account for the placebo effect when testing new medications.
The placebo effect shows how powerful belief can be in influencing health outcomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nocebo effect - harmful effect resulting from negative expectations about a treatment
