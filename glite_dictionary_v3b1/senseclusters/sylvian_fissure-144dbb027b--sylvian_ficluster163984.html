Sylvian fissure

noun
deep groove dividing frontal and temporal lobes of the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlviən ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɪlviən ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
the deep groove separating the frontal and temporal lobes of the brain in both hemispheres; also known as the lateral sulcus

Examples

The Sylvian fissure groove
Damage to the Sylvian fissure region can affect speech and language processing in patients.
MRI scans clearly show the prominent Sylvian fissure separating the frontal and temporal lobes.
The Sylvian fissure is a key landmark that neurosurgeons use when mapping out brain surgeries.
