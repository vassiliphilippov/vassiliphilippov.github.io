Palmer Peninsula

proper noun
peninsula in Antarctica
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑlmər pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlmə pəˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
a large peninsula of Antarctica extending about 1,200 miles north toward South America, separating the Weddell Sea from the South Pacific

Examples

The Palmer Peninsula region
Early explorers mapped the Palmer Peninsula while searching for new sea routes.
Seals and penguins are commonly found along the icy coasts of the Palmer Peninsula.
The Palmer Peninsula is one of the regions of Antarctica most influenced by a warming climate.
