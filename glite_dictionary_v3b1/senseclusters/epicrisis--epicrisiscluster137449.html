epicrisis

noun
medical case summary
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
a medical summary or critical review of a patient's illness, treatment, and progress, usually written at discharge

Examples

Medical epicrisis
After the surgery, the physician prepared an epicrisis to document the patient's recovery and treatment.
The hospital requires an epicrisis for every patient before they are officially discharged.
Nurses often refer to the epicrisis to understand the key events of a patient's stay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discharge summary - a document summarizing a patient's hospital stay and treatment at discharge
  • synonym: progress note - a record of a patient's clinical status during treatment
