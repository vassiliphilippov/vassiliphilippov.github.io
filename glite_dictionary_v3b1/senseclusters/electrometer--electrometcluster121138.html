electrometer

noun
instrument for measuring voltage differences
1.0 7.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑmɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring electrostatic voltage differences without drawing current from the source

Examples

An electrometer device
Because an electrometer draws virtually no current, it prevents interference with sensitive circuits.
Modern electrometers are essential tools in many physics experiments.
The scientist used an electrometer to accurately detect minute voltage differences.
