bigmouth

noun
person who reveals secrets
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪɡˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
a person who talks too much or cannot keep secrets, especially by revealing private information

Examples

He's a bigmouth
Don't tell him your plans; he's such a bigmouth that everyone will know by tomorrow.
She regretted confiding in a bigmouth who couldn't keep her secret.
At the party, his reputation as a bigmouth made people careful about what they said around him.
Don't tell your secret to that bigmouth or everyone will know by tomorrow.
He has a reputation for being a bigmouth at the office.
She called her brother a bigmouth after he revealed her surprise party plans.
Everyone knows not to share secrets with a bigmouth because they cannot keep anything private.
He became known as a bigmouth after bragging about his new car to everyone at work.
She tried to apologize for being a bigmouth and promising not to gossip again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blabbermouth - a person who talks too much and reveals secrets
  • synonym: braggart - a person who boasts about achievements or possessions
  • antonym: confidant - a person you trust with private information
  • synonym: gossip - a person who likes to talk about other people's private lives
  • antonym: modest person - someone who does not boast or talk excessively about themselves
