lyophilize

verb
to dry by freezing and vacuum (science)
8.2 9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈlaɪ.ə.fɪ.laɪz/ /; / /ˈliː.ə.fɪ.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈlaɪ.ə.fɪ.laɪz/ /; / /ˈliː.ə.fɪ.laɪz/ (BrE)
to dry (a substance such as blood, serum, or tissue) by freezing and then reducing the surrounding pressure to allow the frozen water to sublimate directly from the solid to the gas phase

Examples

To lyophilize tissue samples
Scientists often lyophilize tissue samples to preserve them for future analysis.
Some vaccines are lyophilized to ensure stability during transportation.
The lab technician prepared to lyophilize the serum for long-term storage.
After collection, the vaccine was lyophilised to extend its shelf life and ease transportation.
Researchers often lyophilise tissue specimens to ensure long-term stability and prevent degradation.
The laboratory staff carefully lyophilised the blood samples to preserve them for analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze-dry - to dry (especially food or biological material) by freezing and then vaporizing the ice in a vacuum
