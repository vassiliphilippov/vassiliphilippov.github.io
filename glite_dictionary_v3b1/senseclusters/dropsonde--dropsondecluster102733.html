dropsonde

noun
air-dropped weather sensor
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɑpˌsɑnd / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌsɒnd / (BrE)
a weather instrument released from an aircraft to measure and transmit atmospheric data as it falls to the ground

Examples

A dropsonde instrument
The meteorologists deployed a dropsonde to gather data from inside the hurricane.
A dropsonde transmits temperature, humidity, and wind information as it falls through the atmosphere.
Scientists rely on the dropsonde to improve weather forecasting during severe storms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radiosonde - a weather instrument carried by balloon to measure atmospheric data
