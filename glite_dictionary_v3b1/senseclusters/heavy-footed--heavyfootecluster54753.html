heavy-footed

adjective
moving with a lack of ease or lightness
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛviˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛviˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
moving in a way that is clumsy, slow, or lacking lightness

Examples

A heavy-footed walk
After a long day at work, he made his way up the stairs in a heavy-footed manner.
Despite his excitement, his heavy-footed steps betrayed his exhaustion.
Her heavy-footed stride could be heard from across the hallway.
After running the marathon, he became heavy-footed on his walk home.
Despite her effort to be quiet, her heavy-footed steps echoed down the hallway.
The heavy-footed guard made it easy to hear him approaching, even from a distance.
