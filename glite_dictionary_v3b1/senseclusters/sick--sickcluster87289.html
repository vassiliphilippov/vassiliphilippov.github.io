sick

verb
to make ill
Pronunciation: / sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
to cause someone to become ill or to make someone feel physically sick.

Examples

Spoiled food sicked diners
The spoiled shellfish sicked several diners, who had to leave the restaurant.
The strong smell of paint sicked him and he had to step outside.
That roller coaster ride nearly sicked me after lunch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heal - to make someone healthy again
  • synonym: nauseate - to make someone feel sick or disgusted
