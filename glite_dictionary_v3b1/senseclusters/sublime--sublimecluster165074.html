sublime

adjective
inspiring great admiration or awe
4.6
Pronunciation: / səˈblaɪm / (AmE), / səˈblaɪm / (BrE)
of such excellence, beauty, or grandeur as to inspire great admiration or awe; elevated, lofty, or of the highest degree

Examples

Sublime beauty
As the orchestra played, the music reached a sublime intensity.
Her voice had a sublime quality that touched every listener.
The sublime beauty of the mountain left everyone speechless.
Her sublime kindness inspired deep respect in everyone she met.
Many found the poetry so sublime that it felt almost sacred.
The cathedral's sublime architecture left the visitors in silent awe.
He found the poetry so sublime that it changed the way he viewed life.
Her performance reached a sublime level that moved the entire audience to tears.
The philosopher's ideas are notable for their sublime moral vision.
