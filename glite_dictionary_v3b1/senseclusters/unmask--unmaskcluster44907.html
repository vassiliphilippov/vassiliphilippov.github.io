unmask

verb
remove a mask; reveal true identity
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmæsk / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːsk / (BrE)
to reveal the true identity, character, or nature of someone or something that was hidden or disguised

Examples

To unmask a villain
At midnight, all guests were required to unmask before leaving the masquerade ball.
During the play, the actor dramatically unmasked to reveal his true identity.
Investigators worked tirelessly to unmask the source of the anonymous threats.
His actions finally unmasked his true intentions to the team.
The detective worked for months to unmask the leader of the criminal organization.
The newspaper investigation helped to unmask the fraud behind the charity scam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden
  • synonym: disclose - to make known or reveal
  • synonym: expose - to make something visible or reveal it, especially something hidden
