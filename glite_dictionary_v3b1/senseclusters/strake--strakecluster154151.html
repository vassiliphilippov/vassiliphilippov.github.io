strake

noun
plank on the side of a ship
9.9 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / streÉªk / (AmE), / streÉªk / (BrE)
A continuous line of planks or plates forming part of the outer hull of a ship, especially a thick plank forming a ridge along the side of a wooden ship.

Examples

A hull strake
During restoration, the carpenter replaced a rotten strake on the port side.
Each strake was carefully shaped to follow the curve of the boat.
The shipbuilders fitted a new strake to strengthen the vessel's hull.
