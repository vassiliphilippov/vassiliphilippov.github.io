ruddy turnstone

noun
species of migratory shorebird
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌdi ˈtɜrnstəʊn / (AmE), / ˈrʌdi ˈtɜːnstəʊn / (BrE)
a medium-sized shorebird (Arenaria interpres) with distinctive reddish-brown and black markings, which breeds in the Arctic and migrates to South America and Australia for the winter

Examples

The ruddy turnstone bird
During spring, the distinctive patterns of the ruddy turnstone make it easy to spot among other wading birds.
Flocks of ruddy turnstones can often be seen turning over stones along rocky shorelines in search of insects.
The ruddy turnstone is famous for its long migratory journey from the Arctic to Australia.
