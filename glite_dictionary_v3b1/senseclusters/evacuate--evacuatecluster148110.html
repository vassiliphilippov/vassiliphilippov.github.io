evacuate

verb
remove air or gas to create a vacuum
4.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvækjueɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvakjueɪt / (BrE)
to remove air or gas from a container, such as a bulb or flask, in order to create a vacuum

Examples

To evacuate a flask
Before coating the mirror, they evacuated the flask to ensure a contaminant-free environment.
Properly evacuating the bulb is essential for the experiment to succeed.
The technician needed to evacuate the reaction vessel before adding the reactive gases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degas - to remove gases from a vessel or material
  • antonym: pressurize - to increase the pressure of the gas in a vessel, the opposite of creating a vacuum
