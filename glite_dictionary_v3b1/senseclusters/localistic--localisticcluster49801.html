localistic

adjective
focused on local interests
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊkəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or emphasizing local characteristics, interests, or concerns rather than broader or national ones

Examples

Localistic approach to issues
The mayor's localistic policies aimed to support small businesses in the community.
Critics argued that a localistic perspective could hinder cooperation between neighboring cities.
Her localistic approach prioritized the needs of her hometown over national concerns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: global - relating to or involving the whole world
  • synonym: parochial - having a limited or narrow outlook, especially focused on a local area
  • synonym: regional - relating to or characteristic of a particular region
