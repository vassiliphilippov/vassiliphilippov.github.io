echinococcosis

noun
parasitic disease from tapeworm larvae
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkaɪnəˌkɑːˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌkaɪnəˌkɒˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by the infestation of the body with the larval stage of echinococcus tapeworms, typically resulting in cyst formation in organs such as the liver or lungs.

Examples

Echinococcosis disease cysts
Echinococcosis is most commonly found in regions where livestock are raised closely with dogs.
Preventing echinococcosis involves controlling the spread of tapeworms among animals and humans.
The patient was diagnosed with echinococcosis after developing cysts in the liver.
