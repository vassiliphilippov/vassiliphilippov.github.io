sokeman

noun
feudal free tenant
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊk.mən/ /; / /ˈsoʊk.mæn/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊk.mən/ /; / /ˈsəʊk.mæn/ (BrE)
a free tenant in medieval England who held land by socage (paying rent or service to a lord), sometimes with the right to hold a local court

Examples

A sokeman in England
In medieval England, a sokeman enjoyed more rights than a serf but still owed duties to his lord.
The records show that each sokeman paid a fixed rent for the land he farmed.
Unlike villeins, sokemen could leave their land and seek work elsewhere.
In medieval England, a sokeman had the right to hold a local court on his land.
The manor records list each sokeman and the extent of his jurisdiction.
Unlike a serf, a sokeman enjoyed certain legal privileges and independence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeholder - a person who owns land outright
  • antonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system
  • antonym: villein - a feudal tenant entirely subject to a lord or manor
  • synonym: yeoman - a free man owning his own farm, especially in England
