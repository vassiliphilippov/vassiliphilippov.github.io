heterophile antibody

noun
antibody found in infectious mononucleosis cases
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛt̬.ə.rə.faɪl ˈæn.tiˌbɑd.i / (AmE), / ˈhɛt.ərə.faɪl ˈæn.tɪˌbɒd.i / (BrE)
an antibody present in the blood that reacts with antigens from different species, especially appearing in the blood of patients with infectious mononucleosis

Examples

A heterophile antibody test
Doctors ordered a heterophile antibody test to confirm the diagnosis of glandular fever.
Laboratory tests detected elevated levels of heterophile antibody in the patient's serum.
The presence of a heterophile antibody is a key diagnostic marker for infectious mononucleosis.
