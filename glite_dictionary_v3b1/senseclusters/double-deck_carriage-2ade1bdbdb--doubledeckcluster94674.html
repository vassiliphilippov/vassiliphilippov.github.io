double-deck carriage

noun
rail car with two passenger levels
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl dɛk ˈkæɹ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl dɛk ˈkær.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a railway passenger car with two levels of seating, designed to increase passenger capacity compared to a single-level carriage

Examples

A double-deck carriage train
European railways often use double-deck carriages for long-distance and regional routes.
Passengers can choose to sit upstairs or downstairs in a double-deck carriage.
The new commuter train includes several double-deck carriages to increase capacity during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bilevel car - a rail car with two levels of passenger seating
  • antonym: single-deck carriage - a rail car with only one level of passenger seating
