triclinic

adjective
with three unequal crystal axes at oblique angles
9.1 8.5
Pronunciation: /traɪ.ˈklɪn.ɪk/ /; / /trɪ.ˈklɪn.ɪk/ (AmE), /traɪ.ˈklɪn.ɪk/ /; / /trɪ.ˈklɪn.ɪk/ (BrE)
Having three unequal crystal axes that intersect at oblique angles, especially in reference to crystals or crystal systems.

Examples

Triclinic crystal system
In a triclinic lattice, all axes are unequal and none intersect at right angles.
Scientists studied the structure of the salt to confirm it was triclinic.
The mineral feldspar often crystallizes in the triclinic system.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: monoclinic - having three unequal axes with one oblique intersection
  • degree higher: orthorhombic - having three unequal axes all at right angles
