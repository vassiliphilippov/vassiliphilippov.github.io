nondepository financial institution

noun
institution that funds investments without deposits
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɪˈpɑzɪˌtɔri fəˈnænʃəl ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈpɒzɪtə.ri fəˈnænʃəl ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
a financial institution that does not accept deposits from the public but funds its investments through the sale of securities or insurance policies

Examples

A nondepository financial institution sells securities
A nondepository financial institution typically generates revenue from investment products rather than customer deposits.
Insurance companies are a common example of a nondepository financial institution.
Regulatory requirements differ for nondepository financial institutions compared with traditional banks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depository financial institution - a financial institution that accepts deposits from the public
