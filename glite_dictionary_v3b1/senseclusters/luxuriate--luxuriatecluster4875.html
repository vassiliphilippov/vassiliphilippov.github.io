luxuriate

verb
enjoy oneself greatly; take abundant pleasure
5.9 6.0
Pronunciation: / lʌgˈʒʊriˌeɪt /; / lʌɡˈʒʊriˌeɪt / (AmE), / lʌgˈzjʊərɪeɪt /; / lʌɡˈzjʊərɪeɪt / (BrE)
to take great pleasure in something, especially in comfort or luxury; to enjoy oneself extravagantly

Examples

To luxuriate in comfort
After a long day at work, she likes to luxuriate in a hot bubble bath.
On weekends, he enjoys luxuriating in bed with a good book and coffee.
They luxuriated in the comfort of the five-star hotel, enjoying every amenity.
After a long week, she loved to luxuriate in a hot bubble bath.
He luxuriated in the beautiful garden, enjoying the warm sunlight and gentle breeze.
They spent the afternoon luxuriating by the hotel pool, sipping cocktails and reading novels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstain - to deliberately avoid something enjoyable
  • synonym: indulge - to allow oneself to enjoy pleasures, often to excess
