shock treatment

noun
medical treatment involving induced convulsions
5.8 0.15/m
Pronunciation: /  É…ëk Ààtritm…ônt / (AmE), /  É…ík ÀàtriÀêtm…ônt / (BrE)
A medical treatment for certain mental illnesses involving the administration of electric shocks to induce convulsions, particularly used in psychiatry.

Examples

Shock treatment in psychiatry
After months of medication with little progress, the doctors recommended shock treatment for her severe depression.
His condition improved significantly after a series of shock treatments.
Many people have misconceptions about what shock treatment actually involves in modern psychiatry.

Synonyms

  • electroconvulsive therapy
    6.5 0.06/m
    electroconvulsive therapy is the formal medical procedure that uses controlled electric seizures in hospitals, while shock treatment is a general name for such psychiatric care.
  • electroshock therapy
    5.1 0.15/m
    electroshock therapy is the medical treatment that passes electric current to cause seizures, while shock treatment is a broader, less technical name.
  • electroshock
    7.5 0.45/m
    electroshock is the act of giving an electric seizure to a patient, while shock treatment sounds like the general name for this practice.
  • shock therapy
    5.1 0.26/m
    shock therapy includes electric or chemical shocks in treatment, while shock treatment usually refers specifically to electrical psychiatric therapy.
