stearyl

noun
C18H37 alkyl group
Pronunciation: / ˈstiːəˌrɪl / (AmE), / ˈstiːərɪl / (BrE)
the alkyl group or radical C18H37 derived from stearic acid, often found as a chemical substituent

Examples

Stearyl group in lipids
The stearyl group is commonly found in many types of lipids and surfactants.
Adding a stearyl chain can increase the hydrophobicity of a molecule.
Researchers studied the properties of compounds containing the stearyl radical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkyl - a group of carbon and hydrogen atoms arranged in a chain, derived from an alkane
  • synonym: stearic acid - a saturated fatty acid from which stearyl is derived
