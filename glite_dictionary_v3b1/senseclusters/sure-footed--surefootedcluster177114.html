sure-footed

adjective
confident and reliable in judgment or action
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʃɝˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʃʊəˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
Confident and reliable in judgment, decision-making, or action; not likely to make mistakes.

Examples

Sure-footed decisions
As a leader, he remained sure-footed even in times of crisis.
She gave a sure-footed response to the unexpected question during the interview.
The company made a sure-footed move that ensured its long-term success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty and self-assurance
  • antonym: uncertain - not able to rely on judgment, not sure or confident
