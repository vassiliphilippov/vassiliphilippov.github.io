atomism

noun
theory that mental phenomena are built from simple elements
9.4 8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈæt.ə.mɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈæt.ə.mɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
(Psychology) The theory that all mental phenomena can be reduced to simple elements such as sensations and feelings, which combine by association to form complex ideas.

Examples

Atomism in psychology
Many psychologists debated whether atomism could fully account for the complexity of human thought.
Some criticized atomism for oversimplifying the richness of mental life.
The theory of atomism posits that sensations and feelings are the building blocks of the mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: associationism - the theory that mental processes operate by the association of ideas
