vitamin P

noun
obsolete name for flavonoids (capillary health)
Pronunciation: / ˌvaɪtəˌmɪn ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn ˈpiː / (BrE)
an outdated term for a group of compounds, now known as flavonoids or bioflavonoids, that were once believed to be a vitamin essential for maintaining capillary strength

Examples

Vitamin P compounds
Although vitamin P is no longer considered a true vitamin, the compounds are still widely studied.
Citrus fruits were once thought to be a rich source of vitamin P.
Some early studies suggested that vitamin P was important for maintaining capillary strength.

Related Terms
