euthanasia

noun
painless killing of a suffering patient
Pronunciation: / juːθəˈneɪʒə / (AmE), / juːθəˈneɪziə / (BrE)
The painless killing of a patient suffering from an incurable and painful disease or in an irreversible coma, usually done to relieve suffering.

Examples

To perform euthanasia procedure
After much discussion, the family decided to consider euthanasia for their terminally ill relative.
Debates about the morality of euthanasia often arise in the media and political arenas.
Many countries have strict laws regarding euthanasia and assisted dying.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assisted suicide - the act of intentionally helping another person end their life
  • antonym: suicide - the act of intentionally causing one's own death
