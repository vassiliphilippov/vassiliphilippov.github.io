chaplain

noun
clergy minister in institution or organization
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæplɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃap.lɪn / (BrE)
a member of the clergy attached to a private chapel, institution, or to the armed forces, who provides religious services and guidance

Examples

Army chaplain
During his time in the army, he often sought advice from the chaplain.
Our school chaplain regularly leads morning assemblies and offers counseling to students.
The hospital employs a chaplain to offer support to patients and their families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clergyman - an ordained Christian minister
  • synonym: priest - an ordained minister of the Catholic, Orthodox, or Anglican Church
