overexposed

adjective
excessively at risk
6.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪk.ˈspoʊzd/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪk.ˈspəʊzd/ (BrE)
having too much exposure to a particular financial risk, market, asset, or counterparty, increasing the chance of significant loss

Examples

Overexposed to risk
The bank became overexposed to the technology sector and suffered heavy losses during the downturn.
Investors should avoid being overexposed to a single asset class to reduce risk.
After the merger, the company was overexposed to foreign currency fluctuations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protected - shielded from risk or harm
  • synonym: vulnerable - at risk of being harmed or affected
