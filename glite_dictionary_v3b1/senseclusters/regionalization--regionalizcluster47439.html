regionalization

noun
division into regions
Pronunciation: / ˌriːdʒənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːdʒənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of dividing or organizing an area into regions, especially for administrative, political, or functional purposes

Examples

The regionalization of services
The regionalization of healthcare services allowed for more tailored local solutions.
Many countries have adopted regionalization to address the unique needs of different areas.
Debates about regionalization often focus on balancing local autonomy with national unity.
The regionalization of education policies allowed schools to address local needs more effectively.
Economic regionalization can help boost development in less prosperous areas.
Cultural regionalization helped preserve local dialects and traditional crafts in the mountain villages.
The regionalization of healthcare services improved access for people living outside the capital.
Economic regionalization can help neighboring countries cooperate more closely on trade and development.
Many governments are considering regionalization to better address local needs and differences.

  • synonym: decentralization - the transfer of authority from central to local government
  • antonym: unification - the process of making or becoming a single unit
