countermovement

noun
preparatory downward motion
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmˌmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a preparatory downward movement of the body, especially before a jump, used to increase the power of the following upward motion

Examples

Countermovement jump
Athletes often use a countermovement to maximize their vertical jump height.
The coach explained how a quick countermovement could improve explosive power in sports.
Proper technique in the countermovement is essential for effective plyometric exercises.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: jump - an act of pushing oneself off a surface and into the air
  • synonym: preload - the act of loading muscles before a movement to increase force
