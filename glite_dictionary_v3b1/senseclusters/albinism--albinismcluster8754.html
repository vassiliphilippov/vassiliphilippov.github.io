albinism

noun
absence of pigmentation condition
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.bəˌnɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈal.bɪ.nɪ.z(ə)m / (BrE)
A congenital condition characterized by the absence of pigmentation in the skin, hair, and eyes.

Examples

Albinism in animals
Animals with albinism often have pale fur and pinkish eyes.
People with albinism are sensitive to sunlight because their skin lacks protective pigment.
The genetics of albinism have been studied extensively in recent years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vitiligo - disorder causing loss of skin color in patches
