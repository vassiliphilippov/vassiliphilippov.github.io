benzene

noun
aromatic chemical hydrocarbon (C6H6)
6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈben.ziːn/ (AmE), /ˈben.ziːn/ (BrE)
a colorless, flammable, and toxic liquid hydrocarbon with a distinctive aroma, used in chemical manufacturing; the simplest aromatic compound (C6H6)

Examples

benzene liquid
Chemists determined the ring structure of benzene in the nineteenth century, revolutionizing organic chemistry.
Laboratory safety protocols require careful storage of benzene due to its toxicity and high flammability.
Many industrial processes use benzene as a starting material for making plastics and synthetic fibers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phenol - an aromatic compound derived from benzene with a hydroxyl group
  • synonym: toluene - a related aromatic hydrocarbon used as a solvent
