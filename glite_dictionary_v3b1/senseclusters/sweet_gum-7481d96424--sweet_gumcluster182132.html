sweet gum

noun
aromatic resin from sweet gum tree
6.2
Pronunciation: / swit ɡʌm / (AmE), / swiːt ɡʌm / (BrE)
an aromatic resin obtained from the sweet gum tree, used in medicine, perfumes, and as a chewing gum base

Examples

Chewing sweet gum
Chewing sweet gum was common before commercial chewing gum was invented.
Collectors harvested sweet gum for use in early perfumes and medicines.
The indigenous people often used sweet gum as a natural remedy for wounds.
