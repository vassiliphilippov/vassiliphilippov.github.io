migration

noun
moving data or applications between computing environments
4.1
Pronunciation: /maɪ.ˈgreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /maɪ.ˈgreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process of moving data, applications, or workloads from one computing environment to another, such as from on-premises servers to cloud infrastructure.

Examples

Data migration
The company planned a migration to cloud services to reduce on-site hardware.
Our IT team scheduled the migration of all databases for the weekend to minimize downtime.
After testing, they began the migration of legacy applications into the new virtual environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data migration - the process of transferring data between storage types, formats, or systems
  • synonym: transfer - to move something from one place, person, or situation to another
