devastation

noun
severe damage or destruction
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛvəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛvəˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
great destruction or damage, especially as a result of war, disaster, or violence

Examples

War devastation

Decades of war led to social and economic devastation in the region.
She stared in shock at the devastation caused by the fire.
The hurricane left a trail of devastation along the coastline.
After the wildfire, the region struggled to recover from the devastation it caused.
The devastation left by the hurricane was visible throughout the city.
Years of war brought devastation to the countryside, leaving villages in ruins.

  • antonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former condition
  • synonym: ruin - the physical destruction or disintegration of something
