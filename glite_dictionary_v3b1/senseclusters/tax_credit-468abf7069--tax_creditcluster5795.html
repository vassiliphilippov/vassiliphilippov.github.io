tax credit

noun
direct reduction of tax owed
Pronunciation: / tæks ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / tæks ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
an amount that is subtracted directly from the total tax owed, reducing the taxpayer's liability regardless of their tax bracket

Examples

A tax credit amount
After installing solar panels, she was eligible for a substantial tax credit.
Small businesses can benefit from a tax credit for hiring veterans.
The government introduced a new tax credit for families with young children.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tax deduction - an amount subtracted from taxable income
