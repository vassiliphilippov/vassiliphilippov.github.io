electability

noun
likelihood of being elected
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being likely to win an election or be chosen by voters

Examples

Candidate electability
The party chose a candidate whose electability was seen as their best chance to win.
Debates about electability often influence which candidates receive the most support.
Some voters prioritize electability over a candidate's specific policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unpopularity - the state of not being liked or supported by many people
