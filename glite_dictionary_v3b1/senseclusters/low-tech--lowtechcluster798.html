low-tech

adjective
involving simple or basic technology
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ tɛk / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈtɛk / (BrE)
Using or involving only simple, basic technology rather than advanced or sophisticated methods.

Examples

A low-tech approach
He prefers a low-tech approach to gardening, avoiding power tools and chemicals.
Many people are returning to low-tech solutions like handwritten notes and paper calendars.
The school uses low-tech teaching methods that don’t require computers or tablets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high-tech - using advanced or sophisticated technology
