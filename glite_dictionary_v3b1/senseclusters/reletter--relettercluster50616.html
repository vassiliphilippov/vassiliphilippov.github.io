reletter

verb
to add new letters
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlɛtə / (BrE)
to put new letters on something, such as a sign, vehicle, or document, often to update or replace existing text

Examples

To reletter a sign
After the merger, the company relettered all its delivery vans with the new branding.
The museum relettered the old sign to make it easier to read for visitors.
When the address changed, they had to reletter the mailbox to show the new number.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erase - to remove written or printed marks
  • synonym: rename - to give a new name to something
