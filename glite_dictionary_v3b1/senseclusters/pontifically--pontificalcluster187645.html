pontifically

adverb
pompously, with authority
9.8
Pronunciation: / pɒnˈtɪfɪkli / (AmE), / pɒnˈtɪfɪkəli / (BrE)
in a pompous or self-important manner, as if speaking with great authority

Examples

To talk pontifically about politics
He spoke pontifically about the subject, as if he were the only expert in the room.
During the meeting, she pontifically dismissed all other suggestions without consideration.
The professor pontifically lectured the students, expecting no disagreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modestly - in a humble or unassuming way
  • synonym: pompously - in a self-important or arrogant way
