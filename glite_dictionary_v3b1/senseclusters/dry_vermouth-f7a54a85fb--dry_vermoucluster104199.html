dry vermouth

noun
pale, subtly bitter fortified wine
Pronunciation: / ˌdraɪ vərˈmuθ / (AmE), / ˌdraɪ vəːˈmuːθ / (BrE)
a type of pale, slightly bitter fortified wine, flavored with herbs and botanicals, often used in cocktails such as the martini.

Examples

A splash of dry vermouth
A classic martini recipe calls for gin and a splash of dry vermouth.
For a perfect Manhattan, some bartenders use both sweet and dry vermouth.
She added dry vermouth to the sauce to give it an herbal aroma.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sweet vermouth - a red, sweeter variety of vermouth
