pot liquor

noun
liquid left after cooking food in a pot
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑt ˌlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpɒt ˌlɪkə / (BrE)
the liquid left in a pot after cooking vegetables or meat, often used as a flavorful broth or base for soups and sauces

Examples

Hot pot liquor

Many Southern dishes use pot liquor from collard greens as a flavorful base.
