brave out

verb
face a challenge with courage
Pronunciation: / breɪv aʊt / (AmE), / breɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to face and endure a difficult or unpleasant situation with courage, without giving up or showing fear

Examples

To brave out a storm
Despite the criticism, she decided to brave out the controversy and defend her actions.
He tried to brave out the storm, refusing to seek shelter until it was over.
The company chose to brave out the economic downturn rather than cut jobs.
He managed to brave out the interview despite feeling nervous.
She decided to brave out the criticism rather than apologize.
The soldiers had to brave out the harsh winter on the front lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: face up to - to confront something, especially a problem, directly
  • antonym: give in - to stop resisting; to yield
  • synonym: tough it out - to endure a difficult situation without giving up
