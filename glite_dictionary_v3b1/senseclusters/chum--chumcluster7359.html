chum

verb
to bait water for fish
Pronunciation: / tʃʌm / (AmE), / tʃʌm / (BrE)
to scatter bait, usually chopped fish or meat, in water to attract fish for catching

Examples

To chum the water
The fishermen chummed the water to increase their chances of catching tuna.
Before casting their lines, they chum the area to attract more fish.
Some anglers prefer to chum with pieces of sardine to draw in larger predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bait - to place food to attract fish or animals
  • synonym: lure - to attract or entice, especially with bait
