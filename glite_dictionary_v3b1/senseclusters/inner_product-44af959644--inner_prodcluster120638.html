inner product

noun
the result of multiplying two vectors
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnɚ ˈprɑdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
a mathematical operation that assigns a real number to a pair of vectors, often representing the sum of the products of their corresponding components

Examples

Calculate the inner product
Calculating the inner product is essential in quantum mechanics.
If the inner product is zero, the vectors are orthogonal.
The inner product of two vectors is used to find the angle between them.

  • synonym: dot product - scalar product of two vectors
