reinvoke

verb
to call again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to call upon, use, or put into effect again, especially a rule, law, policy, or memory

Examples

To reinvoke a rule
After the incident, the school board decided to reinvoke the previous dress code policy.
The government may reinvoke emergency powers if the situation worsens.
He argued that it was necessary to reinvoke the original agreement to resolve the dispute.
After much debate, the council decided to reinvoke the emergency procedures from last year.
The teacher chose to reinvoke the classroom rules after several disruptions.
Sometimes, old traditions are reinvoked during important ceremonies.
After several years, the committee chose to reinvoke the original guidelines for safety.
The teacher may reinvoke the classroom rules if students become too noisy.
In times of crisis, leaders sometimes reinvoke old traditions to unite people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reinstate - to restore something to its previous position or condition
  • antonym: repeal - to officially cancel a law or rule
