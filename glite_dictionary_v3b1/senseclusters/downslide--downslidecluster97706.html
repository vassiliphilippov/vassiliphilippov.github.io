downslide

noun
period of decline
5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.slaɪd/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.slaɪd/ (BrE)
a period or process in which something declines or becomes worse

Examples

A downslide in profits
After years of steady growth, the company faced a sudden downslide in sales.
The team's performance has been on a downslide since the start of the season.
Many worry that the recent downslide in the economy will last for months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, or quality
  • synonym: downturn - a reduction in economic or business activity
  • antonym: improvement - a process of becoming better or making something better
