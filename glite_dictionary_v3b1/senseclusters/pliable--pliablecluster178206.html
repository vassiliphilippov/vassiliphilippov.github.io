pliable

adjective
easily influenced or led
Pronunciation: / ˈplaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈplaɪəbəl / (BrE)
easily influenced, led, or directed by others; susceptible to suggestion or control

Examples

Pliable minds
Children can be especially pliable to advertising messages and peer pressure.
His pliable nature made him a favorite among those looking to sway opinion.
The dictator sought to surround himself with pliable advisors who would never question his decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inflexible - unwilling to change or adapt; not easily influenced
  • synonym: malleable - easily influenced or shaped, especially by external forces
