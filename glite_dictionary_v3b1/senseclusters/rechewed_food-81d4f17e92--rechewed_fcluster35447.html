rechewed food

noun
food regurgitated and chewed again by ruminants
8.7
Pronunciation: / riˈtʃuːd fuːd / (AmE), / riˈtʃuːd fuːd / (BrE)
Food that has been regurgitated by a ruminant and chewed again as part of digestion.

Examples

Rechewed food of cows
Cows spend several hours each day chewing their rechewed food to aid digestion.
Researchers studied the texture of rechewed food in goats to understand their digestive process.
The process of regurgitating and re-masticating rechewed food helps sheep absorb more nutrients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cud - partly digested food regurgitated and chewed again by ruminants
