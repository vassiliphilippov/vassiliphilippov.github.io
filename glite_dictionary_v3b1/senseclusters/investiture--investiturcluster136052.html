investiture

noun
formal ceremony conferring office or rank
3.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɛstɪtʃər /; / ɪnˈvɛstɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstɪtjʊə /; / ɪnˈvɛstɪtʃə / (BrE)
a formal ceremony in which someone is given an official position, rank, or honor, often with the presentation of symbols or regalia

Examples

The investiture ceremony
Her investiture as university president included a solemn oath and the presentation of the university seal.
The prince's official investiture as heir to the throne was attended by dignitaries from around the world.
Thousands gathered to witness the investiture ceremony at the historic palace.
During the investiture, the monarch received the crown and official regalia.
She studied the traditions associated with royal investiture ceremonies in medieval Europe.
The investiture of the new king was attended by dignitaries from around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coronation - the act or ceremony of crowning a monarch
  • antonym: deposition - the act of removing someone from office
  • synonym: inauguration - the formal introduction or induction into an office
