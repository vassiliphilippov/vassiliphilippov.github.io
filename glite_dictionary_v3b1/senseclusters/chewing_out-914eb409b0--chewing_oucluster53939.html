chewing out

noun
a severe scolding
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃuːɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈtʃuːɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
an act of severely scolding or reprimanding someone

Examples

A chewing out at work
After the mistake was discovered, he knew a chewing out was coming.
She received a serious chewing out from her manager for being late again.
The coach gave the team a chewing out after their poor performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval or criticism
