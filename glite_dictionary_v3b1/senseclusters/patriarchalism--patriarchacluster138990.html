patriarchalism

noun
doctrine of paternal rule
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪtriˈɑrkəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpeɪtrɪˈɑːkəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a political doctrine that supports absolute authority of rulers by likening their power to a father's control over a family

Examples

Patriarchalism in monarchy
In the seventeenth century, patriarchalism was used to justify the king's absolute power.
The theory of patriarchalism compares a ruler's authority to that of a father over his family.
Historians debate the influence of patriarchalism on early modern political thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolutism - a political system in which a ruler holds total power
  • antonym: constitutionalism - the belief that government should be limited by laws or a constitution
  • synonym: patriarchy - a social system where men hold primary power
