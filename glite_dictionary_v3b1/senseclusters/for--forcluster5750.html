for

preposition
recipient or beneficiary
1.0
Pronunciation: / fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
used to indicate the person or thing that is intended to receive or benefit from something, or to show the purpose, reason, recipient, or duration related to an action or object

Examples

A gift for a friend
He bought a present for his friend’s birthday.
This letter is for you, delivered by hand this morning.
They saved a seat for their teacher at the front of the hall.
He saved money for his trip to Europe next summer.
They waited for over an hour before the bus finally arrived.
This gift is for you to celebrate your graduation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: against - in opposition to someone or something
  • synonym: because of - used to show the reason for something
  • synonym: to - used to indicate the direction or recipient of something
