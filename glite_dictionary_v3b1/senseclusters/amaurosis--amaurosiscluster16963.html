amaurosis

noun
loss of sight without eye disease
9.6 9.9
Pronunciation: /æ.mə.ˈroʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /æ.mə.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
Partial or total loss of sight that occurs without detectable disease of the eye itself, usually caused by disorders of the optic nerve, retina, or brain.

Examples

Amaurosis after stroke
After the stroke, the patient suffered from amaurosis affecting his left eye.
Her sudden onset of amaurosis led doctors to suspect an underlying neurological disorder.
Temporary amaurosis can sometimes occur following head trauma.
