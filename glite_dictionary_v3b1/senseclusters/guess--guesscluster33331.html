guess

verb
form an opinion without certain knowledge
Pronunciation: / ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
to form an opinion, estimate, or answer without enough information or certainty

Examples

To guess the answer
Are you guessing that the answer is true, or do you know for sure?
I can only guess what might happen next year in the market.
She guessed that he would be late, and she was right.
He tried to guess the number of candies in the jar.
I can only guess how much this job will cost.
If you had to guess his age, what would you say?
Can you guess how many candies are in the jar?
He guessed the answer to the riddle before anyone else.
She managed to guess the password on her first try.

Related Terms

  • antonym: know - to be aware of something with certainty
  • synonym: suppose - to assume something is true without proof
