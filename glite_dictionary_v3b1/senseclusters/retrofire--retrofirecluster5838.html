retrofire

noun
rocket deceleration device
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈre.troʊ.faɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈre.trəʊ.faɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a rocket engine or device that produces backward thrust to slow down or control the movement of a spacecraft

Examples

A retrofire device
The spacecraft used a retrofire to slow down before entering the planet's atmosphere.
Engineers checked the retrofire system to ensure a safe landing on the lunar surface.
A malfunction in the retrofire could cause the capsule to miss its reentry window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thruster - a small engine used to control the movement of a spacecraft
