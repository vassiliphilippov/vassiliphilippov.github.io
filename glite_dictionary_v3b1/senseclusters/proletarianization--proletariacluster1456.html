proletarianization

noun
becoming working class
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˌlɛtɛriənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌproʊlɪˌtɛriənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / prəʊˌlɛtəriənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌprəʊlɪˌtɛəriənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which people or groups become part of the working class, often by losing property, status, or independence, or by adopting working class conditions or culture

Examples

The proletarianization of farmers
The rapid proletarianization of farmers changed the social structure of the country.
Many historians study the proletarianization that occurred during the Industrial Revolution.
Economic shifts can lead to the proletarianization of formerly independent workers.
The proletarianization of rural communities occurred as more people moved to cities for factory jobs.
Some historians argue that proletarianization changed the social structure of nineteenth-century Europe.
Rapid proletarianization can lead to significant shifts in cultural values and lifestyles.
The proletarianization of artisans occurred rapidly during the rise of factory-based manufacturing.
Many sociologists study the proletarianization of professions in the modern economy.
Automation has contributed to the proletarianization of certain skilled trades over the past decades.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deskilling - the reduction of skill required to do a job
  • antonym: empowerment - the process of gaining power or control over one's work
  • antonym: gentrification - the process of making a neighborhood more middle-class, often displacing working-class residents
  • synonym: industrialization - the development of industries in a region or country
  • synonym: urbanization - the process of people moving to cities and adopting urban lifestyles
