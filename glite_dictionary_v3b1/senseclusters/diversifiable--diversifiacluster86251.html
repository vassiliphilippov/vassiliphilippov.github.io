diversifiable

adjective
able to be varied
Pronunciation: / daɪˌvɜrsɪˈfaɪəbəl / (AmE), / daɪˌvɜːsɪˈfaɪəbəl / (BrE)
able to be made more varied or spread out, especially to reduce risk or increase variety

Examples

A diversifiable risk
The company's investment portfolio is highly diversifiable with the right strategy.
Some risks are diversifiable by spreading assets across different industries.
Not all threats to a business are diversifiable through planning and preparation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrated - focused in one area or type
  • degree higher: diversified - made more varied or spread out
