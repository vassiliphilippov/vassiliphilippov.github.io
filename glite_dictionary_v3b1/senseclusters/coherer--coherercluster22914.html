coherer

noun
early radio detector
Pronunciation: / koʊˈhɪrər / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪərə / (BrE)
a device used in early wireless telegraphy to detect radio waves by changing its electrical resistance when exposed to them

Examples

Early radio coherer
The inventor demonstrated how the coherer could pick up wireless telegraph signals across the room.
Early experimenters built their own coherer to receive Morse code messages sent by radio.
The coherer was eventually replaced by more sensitive radio detectors.
