teasing

noun
giving hints to build anticipation
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtiː.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtiː.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
The act of releasing partial information or hints about a forthcoming product, event, or announcement in order to create interest and anticipation.

Examples

Marketing teasing
The marketing team's teasing before the launch kept fans guessing and boosted online chatter.
A well-timed teasing email boosted sign-ups and persuaded many customers to pre-order quickly.
They used subtle teasing in the trailer to build curiosity about the film's mysterious plot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hype - to promote something energetically to attract attention and excitement
  • synonym: tease - to arouse curiosity or interest by giving only partial information
