desulfurization

noun
removal of sulfur
Pronunciation: / diˌsʌlfərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsʌlfəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing sulfur or sulfur compounds from fuels, gases, or industrial emissions to reduce pollution

Examples

The desulfurization process
The desulfurization of coal is important for reducing harmful emissions from power plants.
Modern refineries invest heavily in desulfurization technology to meet environmental standards.
Effective desulfurization helps lower the amount of sulfur dioxide released into the atmosphere.
Modern power plants use desulfurization to reduce harmful emissions released into the atmosphere.
The desulfurization process is essential for producing cleaner fuels and meeting environmental regulations.
Engineers improved the efficiency of desulfurization systems in the refinery last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted substances, such as sulfur, in a material
  • synonym: purification - the process of making something pure by removing unwanted substances
