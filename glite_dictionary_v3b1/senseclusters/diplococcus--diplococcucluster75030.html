diplococcus

noun
bacterium occurring in pairs
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪpləˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A type of spherical bacterium that typically occurs in pairs, often gram-positive.

Examples

A diplococcus bacterium
After testing, the physician identified the presence of diplococcus in the patient's throat swab.
Some types of pneumonia are caused by a diplococcus known as Streptococcus pneumoniae.
The paired structure of a diplococcus distinguishes it from other spherical bacteria.
