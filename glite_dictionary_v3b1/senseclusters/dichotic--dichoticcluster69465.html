dichotic

adjective
different sounds to ears
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑtɪk / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the simultaneous presentation of different sounds to each ear, especially in psychological or auditory tests

Examples

A dichotic listening task
The psychologist used a dichotic listening task to study how the brain processes language.
In dichotic experiments, participants often hear different words in each ear at the same time.
A dichotic test can help identify auditory processing disorders in children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binaural - relating to or involving both ears
  • antonym: monaural - relating to or involving only one ear
