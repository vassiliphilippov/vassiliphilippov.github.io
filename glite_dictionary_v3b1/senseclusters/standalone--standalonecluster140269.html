standalone

adjective
independent; self-sufficient
Pronunciation: / ˈstændəˌloʊn / (AmE), / ˈstændələʊn / (BrE)
able to work or exist independently, without needing to be connected to or supported by something else

Examples

Standalone device
The new standalone application does not require an internet connection to work.
He bought a standalone GPS device for his car instead of using his phone.
This standalone unit can operate without being connected to a computer.
This software can be used as a standalone application without any additional plugins.
He bought a standalone house rather than an apartment in a complex.
The movie is a standalone story and does not require knowledge of previous films.
The company released a standalone app that does not require an internet connection.
They bought a standalone freezer to store extra food in the garage.
This novel can be read as a standalone story without knowing the previous books.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connected - joined or linked to something else
  • antonym: dependent - needing support or help from something else
  • synonym: independent - not needing help or support from others
  • synonym: self-contained - complete in itself; not needing external support
