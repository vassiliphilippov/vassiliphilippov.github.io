overpopulate

verb
cause to have too many inhabitants
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpɑpjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpɒpjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to cause a place or area to have too many people or animals living in it, so that it becomes crowded or unsustainable

Examples

To overpopulate an area
If you don't control breeding, rabbits will quickly overpopulate their habitat.
Rapid development can overpopulate an area and strain its natural resources.
Without predators, certain animal species may overpopulate the region and disrupt the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crowd - to fill a space so that it becomes too full
  • antonym: depopulate - to reduce the population of an area
