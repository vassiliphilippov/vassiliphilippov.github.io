burrow

noun
animal's shelter dug in ground
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌrəʊ / (BrE)
a hole or tunnel dug in the ground by an animal for shelter or habitation

Examples

An animal burrow
After hours of digging, the badger finally finished its new burrow under the hedge.
Foxes often sleep in a burrow during the day to stay hidden from predators.
The rabbit disappeared into its burrow when it heard us approaching.

Related Terms

  • synonym: den - the home of some wild animals, usually larger and more enclosed than a burrow
  • synonym: nest - a structure or place made or chosen by an animal to lay eggs or give birth
