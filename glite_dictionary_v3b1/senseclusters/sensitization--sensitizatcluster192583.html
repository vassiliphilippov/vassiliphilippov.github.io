sensitization

noun
making an organism sensitive to a substance
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛn.sə.t̬əˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌsɛn.sɪ.t̬əˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɛn.sə.tɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.sɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌsɛn.sɪ.tɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming sensitive to a substance, stimulus, or antigen, especially in a medical or biological context

Examples

The sensitisation of the immune system made subsequent contact with the substance dangerous.
