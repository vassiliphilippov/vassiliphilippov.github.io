commissurotomy

noun
surgical separation procedure
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.mɪ.ʃə.ˈrɑː.tə.mi/ (AmE), /kə.mɪ.ʃə.ˈrɒt.ə.mi/ /; / /kɒ.mɪ.ʃə.ˈrɒt.ə.mi/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the joining of two parts, especially heart valves, is cut or separated

Examples

Heart valve commissurotomy
The surgeon performed a commissurotomy to open the patient's narrowed mitral valve.
After the commissurotomy, the patient's heart function improved significantly.
A commissurotomy may be recommended for patients with severe valve stenosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anastomosis - surgical connection between two structures
  • synonym: valvotomy - surgical cutting of a valve to relieve constriction
