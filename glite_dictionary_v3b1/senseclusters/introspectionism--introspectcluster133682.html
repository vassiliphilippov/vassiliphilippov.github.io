introspectionism

noun
psychology doctrine based on introspection
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈspɛkʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈspɛkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine that psychology should be based primarily on data derived from introspection.

Examples

Introspectionism in psychology
Many early psychologists adhered to introspectionism before the rise of behaviorism.
Modern psychology rarely uses introspectionism as a methodological foundation.
The historical debates between introspectionism and other schools shaped the evolution of psychological science.
