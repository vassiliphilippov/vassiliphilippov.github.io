dryopithecine

noun
extinct ape, possible hominid ancestor
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdraɪoʊˈpɪθəˌsin / (AmE), / ˌdraɪəʊˈpɪθəsiːn / (BrE)
an extinct ape of the group Dryopithecinae, considered a possible ancestor of both anthropoid apes and humans

Examples

A dryopithecine skull
Museums often display dryopithecine skulls alongside those of other prehistoric primates.
Paleontologists believe the dryopithecine may bridge the gap between early apes and humans.
The discovery of a fossilized dryopithecine jaw shed light on ape evolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ape - a large primate without a tail, such as a gorilla or chimpanzee
