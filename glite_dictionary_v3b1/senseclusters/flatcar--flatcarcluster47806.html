flatcar

noun
railroad freight car without sides or roof
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈflætˌkɑː / (BrE)
a railroad freight car with a flat deck and no permanent sides or roof, used for transporting large or heavy items

Examples

A flatcar deck
A flatcar is ideal for carrying vehicles and oversized machinery that wouldn't fit in a boxcar.
The long train included several flatcars loaded with construction materials.
The workers loaded the steel beams onto the flatcar for transport across the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boxcar - a freight car with a roof and enclosed sides
  • synonym: gondola - a freight car with low sides, no roof, used for bulk cargo
