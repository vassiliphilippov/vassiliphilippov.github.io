Canada garlic

noun
North American wild onion plant
Pronunciation: / ˈkænədə ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / ˈkænədə ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
A North American wild onion (Allium canadense) with a strong garlic flavor, having bulbous roots and umbels of whitish or pinkish flowers.

Examples

Canada garlic plant
Native Americans once used Canada garlic as both food and medicine.
The bulbs of Canada garlic have a pungent flavor similar to cultivated garlic.
You can often find Canada garlic growing in meadows and along woodland edges.
