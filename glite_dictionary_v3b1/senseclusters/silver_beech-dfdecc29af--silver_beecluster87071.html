silver beech

noun
New Zealand beech tree with silvery bark
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvɚ ˌbiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌbiːtʃ / (BrE)
A species of beech tree native to New Zealand, noted for its pale, silvery bark.

Examples

A silver beech tree
A row of silver beech lined the riverbank, reflecting the afternoon sunlight.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the native silver beech in the South Island.
The forest was dominated by towering silver beech trees with their unmistakable pale trunks.
