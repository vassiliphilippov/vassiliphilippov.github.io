lady's-slipper

noun
a wildflower with a pouchlike lip
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪdiz ˌslɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˌslɪpə / (BrE)
A wildflower, especially of the genus Cypripedium, with a distinctive inflated pouchlike lip, native chiefly to North America.

Examples

The lady's-slipper flower
Gardeners often admire the unique pouch shape of the lady's-slipper, though it is tricky to cultivate.
On our hike, we found several blooming lady's-slippers along the shaded trail.
The rare lady's-slipper was spotted growing deep in the forest preserve.
