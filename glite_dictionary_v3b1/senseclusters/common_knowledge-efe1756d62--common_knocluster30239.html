common knowledge

noun
widely known information
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
information that is generally known and accepted by most people without needing proof

Examples

Common knowledge facts
Basic hygiene practices should be common knowledge in every household.
It's common knowledge that the sun rises in the east.
The rules of the road are considered common knowledge among drivers.
