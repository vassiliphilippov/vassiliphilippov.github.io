Eskimo

noun
indigenous Arctic peoples (now usually offensive)
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈes.kə.moʊ/ /; / /ˈes.kɪ.moʊ/ (AmE), /ˈe.skɪ.məʊ/ /; / /ˈe.skə.məʊ/ (BrE)
a member of indigenous peoples inhabiting the Arctic regions of northern Canada, Greenland, Alaska, and eastern Siberia; the term is now widely considered offensive, with 'Inuit' or 'Yupik' preferred in most contexts.

Examples

Eskimo communities
Researchers worked closely with Eskimo communities to study traditional Arctic survival methods.
The museum's exhibit explored the art and culture of the Eskimo peoples.
The word Eskimo is considered offensive in Canada and Greenland, where 'Inuit' is preferred.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Inuit - indigenous people of northern Canada, Greenland, and parts of Alaska (preferred term)
  • synonym: Yupik - indigenous people of western Alaska and far eastern Russia
