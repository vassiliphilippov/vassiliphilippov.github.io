octave

noun
feast and seven days after it (Christian calendar)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑk.teɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒk.tɪv / (BrE)
(in Christian liturgy) A feast day and the period of seven days following it, observed with special celebrations.

Examples

Easter octave celebration
During the octave, special prayers are recited each day to commemorate the feast.
Pilgrims visited the city throughout the octave following the holy day.
The church marked the octave of Easter with daily masses and processions.
