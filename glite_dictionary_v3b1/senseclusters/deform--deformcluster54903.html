deform

verb
become misshapen or distorted
1.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to change or spoil the normal shape of something, making it bent, twisted, or less attractive

Examples

To deform metal
After years of pressure, the old metal fence began to deform around the edges.
If plastic is heated too much, it can deform and lose its original shape.
The earthquake caused the railway tracks to deform, making travel unsafe.
Extreme pressure can deform metal pipes beyond repair.
If you drop the box, you might deform the contents inside.
The plastic will deform when exposed to high heat.
Excessive heat can deform plastic containers and make them unusable.
In some diseases, the bones may deform as the condition progresses.
The metal panel was deformed by the impact of the collision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distort - to twist out of shape
  • synonym: mutilate - to severely damage the appearance or structure of something
  • antonym: restore - to return something to its original shape or condition
  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight
  • synonym: warp - to bend or twist out of shape
