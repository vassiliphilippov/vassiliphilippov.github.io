mound-bird

noun
Australasian bird that incubates eggs in mounds
Pronunciation: / maʊnd bɝd / (AmE), / maʊnd bɜːd / (BrE)
A large-footed, short-winged bird of Australasia that builds mounds of decaying vegetation to incubate its eggs.

Examples

A mound-bird species
Many mound-birds are found in forests across Australia and nearby islands.
Scientists were fascinated by the unique incubation strategy of the mound-bird.
The mound-bird uses its large feet to pile up vegetation for its nest.
