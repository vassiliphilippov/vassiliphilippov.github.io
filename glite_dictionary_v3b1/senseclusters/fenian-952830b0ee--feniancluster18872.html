Fenian

noun
Irish nationalist member
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːniən / (AmE), / ˈfiːniən / (BrE)
a member of a 19th-century Irish nationalist organization that sought independence from British rule

Examples

A Fenian rebel
The British government closely monitored the activities of every Fenian in Ireland.
As a proud Fenian, he dedicated his life to the cause of Irish independence.
Many Fenians emigrated to America, where they continued their political efforts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nationalist - a person who advocates for national independence or self-government
  • antonym: unionist - a person who supports the political union between Ireland and Great Britain
