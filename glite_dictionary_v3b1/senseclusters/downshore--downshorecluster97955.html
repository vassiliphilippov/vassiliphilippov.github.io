downshore

adverb
along the coast
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌʃɔː / (BrE)
toward or along the shore, especially further down the coast from a given point

Examples

Walk downshore
We decided to walk downshore to explore the quieter parts of the beach.
Many fishermen travel downshore early in the morning to find better spots.
After lunch, the group headed downshore to visit the old lighthouse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downcoast - toward a point further along the coast
  • antonym: upshore - toward a point further up the coast
