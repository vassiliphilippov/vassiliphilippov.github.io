representative sample

noun
sample reflecting a population
4.7 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a sample that accurately reflects the characteristics of the larger population from which it is drawn

Examples

A representative sample of voters
A representative sample of voters was surveyed to predict the outcome of the election.
The researchers selected a representative sample to ensure the results would generalize to the entire population.
Without a representative sample, the findings of a study may be biased or unreliable.
