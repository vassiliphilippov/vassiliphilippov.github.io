to put together the parts of something to form a whole; to gather components or people into a group or structure

Examples

To assemble a machine

Synonyms

  • put together
    2.8 38/m
    put together is a casual phrase meaning to join parts to make something, while assemble sounds slightly more formal and general.
  • build
    1.0 210/m
    build means to make something by joining materials into a structure or object, while assemble often focuses on fitting parts together.
  • construct
    3.5 23/m
    construct means to build, especially large structures, in a formal way, while assemble often refers to joining smaller parts or people.
  • combine
    2.7 71/m
    combine means to mix or join items so they form one unit, while assemble implies arranging parts or people into a working whole.
  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather means to bring people or things together in one place, while assemble often also involves putting parts together into a whole.
  • collect
    1.1 40/m
    collect means to pick up or bring items together over time, while assemble suggests joining parts now to make a single object.
  • set up
    3.4 34/m
    set up means to establish or arrange a system, business, or event, while assemble focuses on putting physical parts or people together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disassemble - to take apart something that was assembled
  • synonym: gather - to collect things or people together
