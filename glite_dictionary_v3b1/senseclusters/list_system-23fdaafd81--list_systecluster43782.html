list system

noun
proportional representation electoral method
2.8
Pronunciation: / lɪst ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / lɪst ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
an electoral system based on proportional representation, in which voters choose from party lists and seats are allocated according to each list's share of the vote

Examples

The list system election
Debate continues over whether the list system provides better democracy than first-past-the-post elections.
Many European countries elect their parliaments using the list system to ensure fairer representation of political parties.
Under the list system, smaller parties can gain seats in proportion to the votes they receive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proportional representation - an electoral system allocating seats in proportion to votes received
