claw

noun
pincer on a crustacean or arthropod's limb
2.9 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / kl…î / (AmE), / kl…îÀê / (BrE)
a pincer-like grasping structure found on the limb of a crustacean or other arthropods, used for catching and holding things

Examples

A lobster's claw
Lobsters have a powerful claw that can easily crack shells.

The crab used its large claw to defend itself from predators.
