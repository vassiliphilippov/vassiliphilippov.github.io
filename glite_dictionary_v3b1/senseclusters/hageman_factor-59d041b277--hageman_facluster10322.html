Hageman factor

noun
blood-clotting plasma protein
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪɡəmən ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈheɪɡəmən ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a plasma protein involved in blood coagulation; its deficiency leads to longer clotting time of venous blood

Examples

Hageman factor protein
Hageman factor is also referred to as factor XII in coagulation studies.
Patients with a deficiency of Hageman factor may not exhibit abnormal bleeding despite prolonged clotting time.
The laboratory test revealed a low level of Hageman factor in his blood sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: factor XII - another term for Hageman factor, the twelfth clotting factor
