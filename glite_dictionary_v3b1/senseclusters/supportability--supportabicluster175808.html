supportability

noun
ability to be maintained
Pronunciation: / səˌpɔrtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / səˌpɔːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be supported, maintained, or kept operational over time

Examples

Supportability of software
The supportability of the new software was a key factor in its selection.
Engineers evaluated the supportability of the equipment before approving its purchase.
Good supportability ensures that a product can be easily maintained and repaired.
