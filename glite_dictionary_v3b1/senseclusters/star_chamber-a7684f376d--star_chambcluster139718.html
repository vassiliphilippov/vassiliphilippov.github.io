star chamber

noun
secretive decision-making body
8.8 8.2
Pronunciation: /stɑːr ˈtʃeɪm.bər/ (AmE), /stɑː ˈtʃeɪm.bə/ (BrE)
A group or meeting where decisions are made in secret by a select few, often without fairness or transparency.

Examples

A star chamber meeting
Critics accused the council of acting like a star chamber that excluded public input.
Many feared that the new regulations would be decided by a star chamber behind closed doors.
Some employees resented the star chamber atmosphere of upper management meetings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people
  • antonym: committee - a group of people appointed for a specific purpose, usually more open or formal
