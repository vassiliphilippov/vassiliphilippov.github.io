sheepishly

adverb
in an embarrassed or self-conscious way
5.1 1.40/m
Pronunciation: / Àà ÉiÀêp…™ Éli / (AmE), / Àà ÉiÀêp…™ Éli / (BrE)
In a shy, embarrassed, or self-conscious manner, typically as a result of having done something wrong or foolish.

Examples

Look sheepishly
After spilling his drink, Mark looked around sheepishly.
He sheepishly apologized for forgetting her birthday.
The student sheepishly admitted he hadn't done his homework.

Synonyms

  • shamefacedly
    7.9 0.06/m
    shamefacedly shows deep shame and regret after a mistake, while sheepishly shows quiet embarrassment and awkwardness after a small error.
  • abashedly
    8.1 0.01/m
    abashedly looks surprised and embarrassed in the moment, while sheepishly shows shy, guilty awkwardness after doing something foolish.
  • embarrassedly
    4.6 0.05/m
    embarrassedly expresses clear shame or awkwardness in front of others, while sheepishly often adds a timid, guilty tone.
  • bashfully
    3.5 0.18/m
    bashfully acts shy or coy in a gentle way, while sheepishly combines shyness with guilty or foolish embarrassment.
  • self-consciously
    6.0 1.53/m
    self-consciously shows awkwardness from being watched, while sheepishly shows embarrassed, sometimes guilty, reaction after a mistake.
  • shyly
    1.0 2.26/m
    shyly behaves in a timid, reserved way without needing guilt, while sheepishly adds embarrassment and a sense of having done something wrong.
