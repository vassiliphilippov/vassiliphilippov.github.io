intolerance

noun
physiological inability to tolerate a substance
4.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtɑː.lər.əns/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtɒl.ə.rəns/ (BrE)
a physiological inability to digest or metabolize a particular substance, causing unpleasant symptoms after exposure.

Examples

Lactose intolerance
Many people suffer from lactose intolerance, experiencing digestive distress after dairy consumption.
After the test, the doctor confirmed she had a gluten intolerance and advised dietary changes.
Histamine intolerance can cause headaches, flushing, and stomach pain after eating certain aged foods.

Related Terms

  • related: allergy - an immune-system reaction to a substance; related but often distinguished from intolerance.
  • synonym: sensitivity - reduced ability to tolerate a substance; often used interchangeably with intolerance.
