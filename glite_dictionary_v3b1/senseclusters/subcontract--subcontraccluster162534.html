subcontract

verb
hire another to do contracted work
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌb.kənˈtrækt / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.kənˈtrækt / (BrE)
to hire or arrange for another person or company to do part or all of a contract you are responsible for

Examples

To subcontract work
Due to time constraints, we will subcontract some aspects of the project.
The company decided to subcontract the electrical work to a local specialist firm.
They often subcontract labor to smaller agencies when demand increases.
Due to a lack of resources, the firm subcontracted part of the project.
Many IT firms subcontract their support services to companies overseas.
The company decided to subcontract the electrical work to a local specialist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delegate - to assign responsibility or tasks to another
  • synonym: outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier
