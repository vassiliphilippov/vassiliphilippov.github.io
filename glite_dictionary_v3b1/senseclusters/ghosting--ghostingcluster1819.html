ghosting

noun
sudden communication cutoff
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈgoʊ.stɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈgəʊ.stɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of suddenly ending all communication with someone without explanation, especially in a personal relationship or online context

Examples

Ghosting a friend
After their last argument, he started ghosting her and never replied to her messages again.
Many people find ghosting to be a hurtful way to end a friendship or relationship.
She realized she was being ghosted when all her calls and texts went unanswered for weeks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confronting - directly addressing or dealing with someone
  • synonym: ignoring - deliberately not paying attention to someone
