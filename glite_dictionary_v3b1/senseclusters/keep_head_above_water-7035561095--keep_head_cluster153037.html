keep head above water

verb
survive or cope with difficulties
Pronunciation: / kip hɛd əˈbʌv ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / kiːp hɛd əˈbʌv ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to manage to survive or cope with difficulties, especially financial ones

Examples

To keep head above water
Even after losing his job, he somehow continued to keep his head above water.
It's been tough with rising bills, but we’re just managing to keep our heads above water.
She took a second job so she could keep her head above water during hard times.
