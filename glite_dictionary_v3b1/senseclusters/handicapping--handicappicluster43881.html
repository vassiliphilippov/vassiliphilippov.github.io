handicapping

noun
assigning advantages
Pronunciation: / ˈhændiˌkæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændikæpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of assigning advantages or disadvantages to competitors to equalize their chances of winning in a contest or race

Examples

Handicapping the horses
The official is responsible for handicapping the horses before the race.
Fair handicapping ensures that all players have an equal chance to win.
In golf, handicapping allows players of different skill levels to compete together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balancing - making things equal or fair
  • antonym: favoritism - unfairly giving advantage to one side
