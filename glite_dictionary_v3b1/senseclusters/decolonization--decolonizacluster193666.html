decolonization

noun
process of gaining independence from colonial rule
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌkɒlənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a country or region gains independence and becomes free from colonial rule.

Examples

Decolonization of a country
Decolonization often involved both political negotiation and popular resistance movements.
Many historians study the lasting impacts of decolonization on former colonies.
The rapid decolonization of Africa changed the global political landscape in the mid-20th century.
Many former colonies faced economic challenges following decolonisation.
The United Nations played a significant role in supporting the process of decolonisation around the world.
The wave of decolonisation after World War II reshaped the political landscape of Africa and Asia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colonisation - the action of establishing control over indigenous people or area
  • synonym: independence - freedom from control or influence of another country or government
