Spirula

noun
genus of small deep-sea cephalopods
9.9
Pronunciation: / spaɪˈruːlə / (AmE), / spaɪˈruːlə / (BrE)
a genus of small deep-sea cephalopods known for their many-chambered internal spiral shells, resembling those of extinct belemnites

Examples

Spirula shell
Scientists studied a specimen of Spirula caught in deep ocean waters near the Azores.
The distinctive coiled shell of the Spirula makes it easily recognizable among cephalopods.
The rare Spirula shell sometimes washes up on tropical beaches, puzzling beachcombers with its delicate spiral form.
