cornucopian

adjective
believing in abundance
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔrnjuˈkoʊpiən / (AmE), / ˌkɔːnjuˈkəʊpiən / (BrE)
believing in or characterized by the idea that resources are abundant or limitless

Examples

Cornucopian optimism
The economist presented a cornucopian argument, insisting that technology would solve resource shortages.
Her cornucopian outlook led her to believe that food supplies would always meet demand.
Critics say the report is too cornucopian and ignores potential environmental limits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: malthusian - believing that resources are limited and population growth will cause shortages
  • synonym: optimistic - expecting the best possible outcome
