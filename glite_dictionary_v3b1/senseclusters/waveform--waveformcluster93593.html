waveform

noun
shape of a wave plotted against time
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvˌfɔrm /; / ˈweɪvfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈweɪvˌfɔːm /; / ˈweɪvfɔːm / (BrE)
the shape or graphical representation of a wave, as shown on a graph (such as for sound, voltage, or pressure over time)

Examples

Sound waveform
Different instruments produce different sound waveforms, affecting the quality of the tone.
Engineers analyzed the waveform to detect anomalies in the signal.
The oscilloscope displayed the waveform of the alternating current.
Each musical instrument produces a unique waveform that gives it its distinctive sound.
The oscilloscope displayed a waveform with sharp peaks and troughs.
Understanding the waveform helps engineers diagnose problems in electrical circuits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: signal - an electrical or electromagnetic current that carries data
  • synonym: wave - a disturbance that transfers energy through space or matter, often with regular shape
