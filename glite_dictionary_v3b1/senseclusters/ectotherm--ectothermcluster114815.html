ectotherm

noun
animal with external temperature regulation
8.7 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õk.to äÀåŒ∏…úrm / (AmE), / Àà…õk.t…ô äÀåŒ∏…úÀêm / (BrE)
An animal whose body temperature varies with that of its environment, rather than being internally regulated.

Examples

An ectotherm animal
Many amphibians are ectotherms, relying on environmental heat sources to stay active.
The lizard is an example of an ectotherm that basks in the sun to raise its body temperature.
Unlike mammals, ectotherms often become sluggish in cold weather.
