old-fashioned

adjective
not in fashion; outdated
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊld ˈfæʃənd / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈfæʃənd / (BrE)
not in style or no longer fashionable; characteristic of an earlier time or fashion

Examples

Old-fashioned style
His views on gender roles seem rather old-fashioned these days.
Many people still prefer old-fashioned ways of communicating like handwritten letters.
That dress looks a bit old-fashioned for a party tonight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiquated - very old and no longer useful or fashionable
  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past
  • synonym: outmoded - no longer fashionable
