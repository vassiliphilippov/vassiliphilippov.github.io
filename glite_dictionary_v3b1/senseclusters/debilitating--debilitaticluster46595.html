Causing a serious weakness or loss of strength and energy, often to the extent of interfering with normal activity.

Examples

A debilitating illness
Chronic pain can be debilitating, affecting every aspect of a person's daily life.
The country faced a debilitating drought that damaged crops and affected millions.
The disease had a debilitating effect on her health, leaving her bedridden for months.

Synonyms

  • disabling
    5.6 0.01/m
    disabling makes someone unable to do normal tasks, while debilitating drains strength and energy and interferes with daily activity.
  • incapacitating
    7.0 0.06/m
    incapacitating causes a person to be unable to act or move, while debilitating weakens them and reduces their energy for normal life.
  • crippling
    5.8 0.39/m
    crippling produces very severe or lasting harm that stops function, while debilitating brings weakness and low energy that may still allow some activity.
