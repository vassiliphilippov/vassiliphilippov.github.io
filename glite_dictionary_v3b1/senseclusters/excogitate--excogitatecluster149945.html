excogitate

verb
devise or think out through mental effort
9.9 8.8
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈskɑdʒɪˌteɪt /; / ɛkˈskɑːdʒɪteɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈskɒdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
to think very carefully and deeply about something, especially to come up with an idea or solution

Examples

To excogitate a plan
He spent all night excogitating ways to improve the team's performance.
It took the researchers weeks to excogitate a solution to the complex problem.
She managed to excogitate a novel approach to the design.
After hearing the proposal, he chose to excogitate privately before responding.
She likes to excogitate on philosophical issues late into the night.
The committee was urged to excogitate on the long-term consequences before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • synonym: invent - to create or design something that has not existed before
  • synonym: ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • synonym: ruminate - to think deeply about something
  • synonym: speculate - to form a theory about something without firm evidence
