besmirch

verb
damage someone's reputation
9.0 7.0
Pronunciation: /bɪ.ˈsmɜːrtʃ/ /; / /bə.ˈsmɜːrtʃ/ (AmE), /bɪ.ˈsmɜːtʃ/ /; / /bə.ˈsmɜːtʃ/ (BrE)
to damage the reputation or honor of someone or something; to make dirty or stained, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

To besmirch a name
His rivals tried to besmirch his good name during the election.
She would never besmirch his character with unfounded accusations.
The scandal threatened to besmirch the company's image for years to come.
She accused the newspaper of trying to besmirch her good name.
The mud on his clothes besmirched the white carpet as he walked through the room.
The scandal threatened to besmirch his otherwise impeccable reputation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defame - to harm someone's good reputation
  • antonym: honor - high respect; to regard with great respect
  • synonym: sully - to damage the purity or integrity of something
  • synonym: tarnish - to make or become less bright or a reputation less pure
