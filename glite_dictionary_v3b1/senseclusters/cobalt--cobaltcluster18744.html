cobalt

noun
a hard, magnetic metallic element (Co)
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌbɔlt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌbɔːlt / (BrE)
a hard, silver-white, magnetic metallic element, symbol Co, used in alloys and as a trace nutrient in living organisms

Examples

Cobalt metal
Cobalt is commonly used to make strong, wear-resistant alloys for jet engines.
Some vitamins and supplements provide cobalt as an essential trace element.
The mineral sample contained significant amounts of cobalt and nickel.
