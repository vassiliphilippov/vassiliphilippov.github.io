judicatory

noun
system or body of law courts
6.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪtɔri / (AmE), / dʒuːˈdɪkətəri / (BrE)
A system of law courts, or a tribunal, responsible for administering justice as part of the judicial branch of government.

Examples

A judicatory system
He appealed to a higher judicatory after losing his initial case.
International disputes are sometimes settled by an independent judicatory.
The federal judicatory plays a crucial role in interpreting constitutional law.
