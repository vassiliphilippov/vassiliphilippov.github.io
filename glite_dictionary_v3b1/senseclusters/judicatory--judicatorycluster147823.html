judicatory

noun
system or body of law courts
9.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íuÀêd…™Àåke…™t…îri / (AmE), / d íuÀêÀàd…™k…ôt…ôri / (BrE)
A system of law courts, or a tribunal, responsible for administering justice as part of the judicial branch of government.

Examples

A judicatory system
He appealed to a higher judicatory after losing his initial case.
International disputes are sometimes settled by an independent judicatory.
The federal judicatory plays a crucial role in interpreting constitutional law.

Synonyms

  • judicature
    9.9 0.08/m
    judicature covers the system and process of running courts and judges, while judicatory names a system of courts or a tribunal.
  • judicial system
    3.9 2.30/m
    judicial system refers to the network of courts and judges that decide law and cases, while judicatory refers to a court system or tribunal.
  • judiciary
    6.5 16/m
    judiciary is the body of judges and courts that interpret laws in a state, while judicatory can mean that system or a single tribunal.
  • court of justice
    3.8 0.14/m
    court of justice is a tribunal that hears and decides cases, while judicatory can mean either that tribunal or the wider court system.
  • tribunal
    7.4 7.07/m
    tribunal is a group or judge that decides disputes in a specific case, while judicatory is a term for a court system or tribunal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tribunal - a type of court or body established to settle certain types of disputes
