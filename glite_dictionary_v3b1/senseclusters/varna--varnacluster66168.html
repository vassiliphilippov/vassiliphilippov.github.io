varna

noun
each main social class in traditional Hindu society
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑrnə / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnə / (BrE)
(in Hinduism) each of the four broad social classes—Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, and Shudra—of traditional Vedic society, forming the basis of the caste system.

Examples

The varna system
During ancient times, one’s varna determined their social responsibilities and privileges.
Scholars still debate the relationship between varna and the thousands of jatis in India.
The Hindu concept of varna divides society into four main groups based on duties and occupations.
