suppurate

verb
produce or discharge pus (medically)
6.3 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌ.pjə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈsʌ.pjə.rət/ (AmE), /ˈsʌ.pjə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈsʌ.pjə.rət/ (BrE)
(of a wound or sore) to form or discharge pus, usually because of infection

Examples

To suppurate a wound
Certain infections can cause a sore to suppurate over time.
The doctor explained that the wound might suppurate if not kept clean.
The treatment is designed to help the abscess suppurate and release the pus.
If left untreated, the wound may begin to suppurate within a few days.
The doctor examined the suppurating sore on his arm with concern.
The infected cut suppurated, requiring medical attention to clean and dress it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fester - (of a wound or sore) to become infected and produce pus
  • antonym: heal - to become healthy or well again, especially after a wound
