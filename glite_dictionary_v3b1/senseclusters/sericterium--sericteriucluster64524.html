sericterium

noun
silk-producing gland in insects/spiders
Pronunciation: / sɛrɪkˈtɪəriəm / (AmE), / sɛrɪkˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a silk-producing gland found in certain insects, especially silkworms, and spiders

Examples

The sericterium gland
Damage to an insect’s sericterium can severely affect its ability to spin cocoons.
Scientists studied the structure of the spider’s sericterium to understand silk synthesis.
The silkworm’s sericterium is responsible for the production of raw silk threads.
