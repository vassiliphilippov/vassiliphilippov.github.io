staghound

noun
large hunting dog for stags
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈstæɡˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
a large, strong breed of hound formerly used for hunting stags and other large game, resembling but larger than a foxhound.

Examples

A staghound breed
Early English nobility often kept a pack of staghounds for sport.
The staghound was prized for its speed and strength during royal hunts.
Unlike the swift foxhound, the staghound was bred to tackle larger and more powerful prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foxhound - a breed of dog bred for hunting foxes by scent
