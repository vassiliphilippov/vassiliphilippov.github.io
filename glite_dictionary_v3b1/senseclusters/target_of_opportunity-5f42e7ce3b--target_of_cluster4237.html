target of opportunity

noun
unexpected chance for action or attack
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrɡət əv ˌɑpərˈtunəti / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt əv ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪti / (BrE)
A person or object that is not a primary or planned objective, but presents itself for advantageous action or engagement.

Examples

A target of opportunity
During the campaign, the politician became a target of opportunity for negative press.
Security weaknesses made the system a target of opportunity for cybercriminals.
The scout identified an armored vehicle as a target of opportunity and called in a strike.
