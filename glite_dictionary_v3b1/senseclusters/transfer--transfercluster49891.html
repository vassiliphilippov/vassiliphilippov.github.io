transfer

verb
move someone to another job or location
Pronunciation: / trænˈsfɚ /; / trænˈsfɝː /; / ˈtrænsfɚ /; / ˈtrænsfɚ, trænzˈfɚ /; / trænsˈfɚ /; / ˈtrænˌsfɚ, trænsˈfɚ / (AmE), / trɑːnˈsfɜː /; / ˈtrænsfəː /; / ˈtrænsfɜː /; / trænsˈfəː /; / ˈtrænsfə, trænzˈfə /; / trænsˈfɜː /; / ˈtrɑːnsfəː, trɑːnsˈfəː / (BrE)
to move someone or something from one place, position, group, or situation to another

Examples

To transfer an employee
After several years in sales, he was transferred to the marketing department.
The company decided to transfer her to their London office.
They transferred most of the staff to the new facility across town.
She was transferred from the New York branch to headquarters.
The case will be transferred to another court for further review.
The company decided to transfer him to their London office last year.
He transferring his books from the old shelf to the new one took all afternoon.
Please transfer all the files to the new computer before tomorrow.
She transferred the call to her manager for further assistance.
