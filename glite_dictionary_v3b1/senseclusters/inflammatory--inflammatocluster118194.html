inflammatory

adjective
intended to provoke anger or action
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈflæməˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈflæmət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) intended to arouse anger or strong emotion, or to provoke action or rebellion.

Examples

Inflammatory speech
Avoid making any inflammatory comments during the debate to keep things civil.
His article contained several inflammatory statements that incited strong public reactions.
The politician's inflammatory remarks led to widespread protests across the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incendiary - intended to stir up conflict or anger
  • synonym: provocative - causing annoyance or anger, or another strong reaction
