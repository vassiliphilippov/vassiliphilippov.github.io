clinging vine

noun
vine that attaches to supports or surfaces
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪŋɪŋ vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋɪŋ vaɪn / (BrE)
A vine that attaches itself to support structures or surfaces by wrapping around them or using specialized organs.

Examples

A clinging vine plant
Gardeners often use a clinging vine to create natural privacy screens.
She decided to plant a clinging vine near the porch to add some greenery.
The old wall was covered by a thick clinging vine that had grown for years.
