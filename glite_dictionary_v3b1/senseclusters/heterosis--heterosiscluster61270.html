heterosis

noun
superiority of hybrids over parents (genetics)
Pronunciation: / hɛt̬.əˈroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.əˈrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
(genetics) The tendency of a crossbred organism to exhibit qualities superior to those of either parent.

Examples

heterosis in plants
Plant breeders use heterosis to produce crops that grow faster and yield more than their parent strains.
Studies have shown that heterosis can result in greater disease resistance in crossbred plants.
The remarkable growth of the hybrid animals was attributed to heterosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hybrid vigor - Another term for heterosis, referring to the increased vitality of hybrids
