meadow jumping mouse

noun
North American rodent with long legs that leaps
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdoʊ ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ maʊs / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ maʊs / (BrE)
a small North American rodent (Zapus hudsonius) known for its long hind legs and ability to leap, found in meadows and grassy areas

Examples

The meadow jumping mouse
Researchers are studying the meadow jumping mouse to learn more about its unique leaping ability.
The meadow jumping mouse hibernates for much of the year in underground burrows.
While walking through the tall grass, we spotted a meadow jumping mouse hopping away quickly.
