clamor

noun
loud, persistent noise or outcry
6.1 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈklæ.mər/ (AmE), /ˈklæ.mə/ (BrE)
a loud and persistent outcry or noise, especially from a group of people, often expressing public demand or protest.

Examples

A clamor of voices
Despite the clamor in the auditorium, the speaker continued unperturbed.
The clamor of the protesters outside the building could be heard for blocks.
The government acted quickly to address the clamor for reform.
Suddenly, a clamor erupted in the hallway as the children rushed outside.
The clamor from the construction site made it hard to concentrate on my work.
The clamor of sirens and shouting filled the city streets late into the night.
A clamour of disapproval rose from the audience as the decision was announced.
Despite the public clamour, the policy remained unchanged.
The children made a clamour outside the school gates, waiting for the doors to open.

Related Terms

  • synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise
  • antonym: silence - absence of sound or noise
  • synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or excitement
