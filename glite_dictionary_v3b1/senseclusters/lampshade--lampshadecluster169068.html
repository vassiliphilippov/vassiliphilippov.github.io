lampshade

verb
to acknowledge awkwardness
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlæmpˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæmpˌʃeɪd / (BrE)
to openly acknowledge or draw attention to something awkward, unlikely, or unrealistic, especially in fiction or storytelling

Examples

To lampshade a plot hole
The author lampshaded the unlikely coincidence by having a character joke about it.
In the movie, the director lampshades the unrealistic action scenes with a witty comment.
The script lampshaded the plot twist so the audience would not be confused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: address - to deal with or discuss something directly
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
