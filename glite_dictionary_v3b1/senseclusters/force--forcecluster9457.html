force

noun
physical power or strength
2.3 72/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs / (AmE), / f…îÀês / (BrE)
physical power, strength, or violence; the ability to make something move or change (including in physics)

Examples

With force and power
He slammed the door shut with such force that the windows rattled.
She was surprised by the force of his handshake.
The wind battered the coastline with incredible force.
According to Newton's second law, force equals mass times acceleration.
The gravitational force keeps the planets in orbit around the sun.
The wind applied enough force to knock over the fence.
No force was needed; he left willingly after a brief conversation.
Overcoming resistance with force can create lasting resentment.
The police were accused of using excessive force during the protest.
