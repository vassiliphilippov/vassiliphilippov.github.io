Übermensch

noun
ideal superior person
Pronunciation: / ˈuːbɚˌmɛnʃ / (AmE), / ˈyːbɐˌmɛnʃ / (BrE)
a person in philosophy who transcends conventional morals and values, or an ideal superior human with superhuman qualities (especially in Nietzsche's philosophy)

Examples

Nietzsche's Übermensch
Nietzsche's concept of the Übermensch challenges people to create their own values.
Some philosophers debate whether the Übermensch is a realistic or desirable ideal.
The idea of the Übermensch has influenced literature, art, and modern philosophy.
Nietzsche introduced the concept of the Übermensch as an ideal for humanity to strive toward.
In the novel, the villain believed himself to be an Übermensch above all moral laws.
Some comic book heroes are inspired by the idea of the Übermensch with powers beyond normal humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: everyman - an ordinary or typical person
  • antonym: mortal - an ordinary human being without special powers
  • synonym: superman - an ideal superior person with extraordinary abilities or qualities
