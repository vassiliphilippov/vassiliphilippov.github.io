glycolysis

noun
biochemical sugar breakdown process
2.8 6.8
Pronunciation: /glaɪ.ˈkɑː.lə.sɪs/ /; / /glaɪ.ˈkɑː.lɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /glaɪ.ˈkɒl.ɪ.sɪs/ /; / /glaɪ.ˈkɒl.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the series of metabolic reactions in which glucose or other sugars are broken down to form pyruvic acid or lactic acid, releasing energy as ATP

Examples

Glycolysis process
Glycolysis is the first step in cellular respiration, providing energy for many cellular activities.
Defects in glycolysis can lead to metabolic disorders affecting energy levels.
During intense exercise, muscles rely on glycolysis for rapid ATP production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fermentation - anaerobic breakdown of sugars producing energy without oxygen
  • antonym: gluconeogenesis - metabolic process forming glucose from non-carbohydrate sources
