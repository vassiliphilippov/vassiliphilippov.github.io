firmer chisel

noun
woodworking chisel with thin blade
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrmər ˈtʃɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmə ˈtʃɪzl̩ / (BrE)
a chisel with a thin, flat blade used in woodworking for precise cutting and shaping of wood

Examples

A firmer chisel blade
He used a firmer chisel to carefully shape the joint in the oak table leg.
The set includes both a firmer chisel and a mortise chisel for different types of carpentry.
To achieve clean, straight cuts, a firmer chisel is often preferred by woodworkers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bevel-edge chisel - a chisel with beveled edges, often used for cabinetry and fine joints
  • antonym: mortise chisel - a chisel with a thick blade used for cutting mortises
