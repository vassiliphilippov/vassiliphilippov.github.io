giant

noun
mythical being of great size
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
an imaginary being or creature of enormous size and strength, often found in myths, fairy tales, or stories

Examples

A fairy tale giant
Children listened in awe as the tale described a fearsome giant with a booming voice.
In folklore, a giant often represents a formidable challenge for the hero.
There are many stories about a giant who lives at the top of a beanstalk.
In the fairy tale, Jack climbs a beanstalk and faces a fearsome giant.
Marine biologists recently discovered a giant squid lurking in the deep ocean.
The fossil belonged to a giant sloth that once roamed South America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colossus - someone or something that is extremely large or powerful
  • antonym: dwarf - a (mythical) being of very small size in folklore
  • synonym: ogre - a monstrous, often man-eating, being in folklore
