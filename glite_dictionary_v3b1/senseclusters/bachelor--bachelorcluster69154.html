bachelor

noun
knight of the lowest rank
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbætʃələr / (AmE), / ˈbatʃələ / (BrE)
a knight of the lowest rank in medieval times, permitted to display only a pennon as his standard

Examples

Medieval bachelor knight
In medieval England, a bachelor served under a higher-ranking knight and could not fly a full banner.
The chronicler noted that the bachelor distinguished himself during the tournament despite his lower status.
The young bachelor longed for the day he would be promoted to a banneret.
