finch

noun
small seed-eating songbird
Pronunciation: / fɪntʃ / (AmE), / fɪntʃ / (BrE)
A small songbird with a short, stout bill, typically adapted for eating seeds.

Examples

A small finch bird
Many birdwatchers enjoy spotting different species of finches in their gardens.
The finch's thick beak allows it to easily crack open seeds.
The bright yellow finch perched on a branch, singing its cheerful tune.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sparrow - another type of small seed-eating songbird
