dark adaptation

noun
eyes adjusting to darkness
6.7 5.2
Pronunciation: /dɑːrk æd.əpˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɑːrk əd.əpˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɑːk æd.əpˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɑːk əd.əpˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which the eyes adjust to low levels of light after exposure to brighter conditions, involving changes in the sensitivity of the retina

Examples

Dark adaptation process
After coming in from the bright sunlight, my dark adaptation took several minutes before I could see clearly.
As we entered the cave, our guide reminded us that dark adaptation was necessary to see the faint cave paintings.
The experiment measured how quickly dark adaptation occurred in different individuals.
