dilapidation

noun
process of becoming dilapidated
Pronunciation: / dɪˌlæpɪˈdeɪʃən /; / dɪˌlæpəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌlapɪˈdeɪʃ(ə)n /; / dɪˌlapɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of becoming run-down, damaged, or in disrepair, especially due to age or neglect

Examples

The dilapidation of buildings
Continuous neglect led to the dilapidation of the once-grand theater.
The dilapidation of the historic building alarmed preservationists in the community.
The city launched a project to reverse the dilapidation affecting its old industrial areas.
Despite its dilapidation, the old theatre retained some of its former charm.
The abandoned house fell into dilapidation after years without maintenance.
Tourists were surprised by the dilapidation of the once grand hotel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - gradual decline or decomposition of something
  • synonym: dereliction - state of being abandoned or neglected
  • synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse
  • antonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former condition
