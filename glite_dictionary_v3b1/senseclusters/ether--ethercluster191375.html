ether

noun
volatile liquid once used as an anesthetic
Pronunciation: / ˈiːθɚ / (AmE), / ˈiːθə / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid that was formerly used as an inhalation anesthetic

Examples

Ether anesthesia
In the nineteenth century, ether revolutionized surgery by allowing painless operations.
She studied the effects of ether on different types of patients in her research.
The surgeon administered ether before beginning the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anesthetic - a substance that causes loss of sensation
  • synonym: chloroform - another chemical once used as an anesthetic
