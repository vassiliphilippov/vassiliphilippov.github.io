superfluid

adjective
frictionless flow
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌfluːɪd / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌfluːɪd / (BrE)
having or relating to a state of matter that flows without friction or viscosity, especially at very low temperatures

Examples

Superfluid helium
At extremely low temperatures, helium becomes a superfluid and can climb the walls of its container.
Scientists study superfluid states to better understand quantum mechanics and unusual properties of matter.
The experiment demonstrated how a superfluid can flow endlessly without losing energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superconducting - having zero electrical resistance at low temperatures
  • antonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency and high resistance to flow
