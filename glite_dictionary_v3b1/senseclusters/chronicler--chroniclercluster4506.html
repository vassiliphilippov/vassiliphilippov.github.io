chronicler

noun
person who writes chronicles
7.2 2.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr…ëÀê.n…™.k…ôl.…ö / (AmE), / Ààkr…ín.…™.kl…ô / (BrE)
a person who records events and facts in the order in which they happened, often in the form of a chronicle

Examples

A chronicler of history
As a chronicler of her time, she kept meticulous daily journals.
Medieval chroniclers often embellished their accounts of historic battles.
The famous chronicler documented the king's reign in great detail.

Synonyms

  • annalist
    9.8 0.00/m
    annalist records events year by year in annals, while chronicler writes longer, continuous accounts of events in order.
  • logographer
    9.9 0.01/m
    logographer writes historical accounts often about ancient times, whereas chronicler keeps a running record of events in order.
  • recorder
    3.3 0.40/m
    recorder keeps official records for an organization, while chronicler records events as a historical narrative over time.

  • synonym: historian - a person who studies or writes about history
