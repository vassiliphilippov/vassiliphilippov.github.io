bivy

verb
to camp without tent
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbɪ.vi/ /; / /ˈbɪ.viː/ (AmE), /ˈbɪ.vi/ /; / /ˈbɪ.viː/ (BrE)
to spend the night outdoors in a simple shelter or without a tent, especially while camping or hiking

Examples

To bivy outdoors
We decided to bivy under the stars when we couldn't find a campsite.
After a long hike, they had to bivy on the mountain ledge for the night.
Sometimes it's more adventurous to bivy than to set up a full tent.
We decided to bivvy under the stars instead of carrying a heavy tent.
They bivvied on the mountain slope to save time for an early start.
If the weather stays clear, we might just bivvy by the lake tonight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bivouac - to camp temporarily in the open without a tent
  • synonym: camp - to stay outdoors in a tent or shelter
