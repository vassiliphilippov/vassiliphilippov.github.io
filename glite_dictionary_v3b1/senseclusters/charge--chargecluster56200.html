charge

noun
psychoanalytic psychic energy
9.7 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
In psychoanalysis, the amount of psychic or libidinal energy attached to an idea, person, or object.

Examples

A psychic charge
Freud described the charge of emotions that could become attached to childhood experiences.
In some cases, the charge may be redirected from one object to another.
The analyst spoke about the charge a patient invests in certain relationships.
