Westphalia

proper noun
1648 European treaties
Pronunciation: / wɛstˈfeɪliə / (AmE), / wɛstˈfeɪliə / (BrE)
the series of treaties signed in 1648 that ended the Thirty Years' War and established the modern concept of state sovereignty in Europe

Examples

Peace of Westphalia
The Peace of Westphalia marked a new era in international relations.
Many historians consider Westphalia the foundation of the modern nation-state system.
After Westphalia, European countries began to recognize each other's sovereignty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Thirty Years' War - a major European conflict from 1618 to 1648
  • synonym: Treaty of Versailles - the 1919 treaty that ended World War I
