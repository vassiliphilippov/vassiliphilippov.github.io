lime hydrate

noun
calcium hydroxide; caustic substance from quicklime
6.0
Pronunciation: / laɪm ˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / laɪm ˈhʌɪdreɪt / (BrE)
a white, caustic powder (calcium hydroxide) produced by adding water to quicklime, used in mortars, plasters, and many industrial processes

Examples

Lime hydrate powder
Lime hydrate is often used to adjust the pH level of soil in agriculture.
Exposure to lime hydrate without protective gear can cause serious skin irritation.
The workers mixed lime hydrate with water to form a paste for the wall plaster.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quicklime - calcium oxide, made by heating limestone
