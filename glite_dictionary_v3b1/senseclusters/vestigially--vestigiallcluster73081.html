vestigially

adverb
in a remnant way
8.9
Pronunciation: / vɛˈstɪdʒiəli / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪdʒiəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to a small, undeveloped, or remaining part of something that once was larger or more developed

Examples

Exists vestigially small
The animal's tail exists only vestigially, barely visible beneath its fur.
In some plants, leaves are present vestigially as tiny scales rather than full leaves.
The tradition survives vestigially in a few remote villages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rudimentarily - in a basic or undeveloped way
