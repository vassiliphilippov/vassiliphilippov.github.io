puristic

adjective
strict about purity
Pronunciation: / pjʊˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / pjʊəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
demanding or favoring strict purity and rejecting mixing or compromise, especially in morals, language, art, or design

Examples

A puristic approach
The organization took a puristic approach, refusing to collaborate with groups that did not share its exact values.
Her puristic attitude made it difficult to find common ground with more flexible colleagues.
Some critics argue that a puristic interpretation of tradition can hinder progress and adaptation.
The editor's puristic approach meant that only traditional words were accepted in the publication.
Some critics view puristic language policies as too restrictive for modern communication.
Her puristic tendencies made her avoid using any borrowed words in her writing.
The gallery featured a puristic style that emphasized clean lines and open spaces.
Her puristic views on language led her to avoid using foreign words.
Many modern designers adopt a puristic approach, focusing on function over decoration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values
  • synonym: dogmatic - insisting on strict adherence to principles or beliefs
  • synonym: minimalist - favoring simplicity and minimal elements
  • antonym: ornate - highly decorated or elaborate
  • antonym: progressive - favoring or promoting change and innovation
  • antonym: tolerant - willing to accept different views or practices
