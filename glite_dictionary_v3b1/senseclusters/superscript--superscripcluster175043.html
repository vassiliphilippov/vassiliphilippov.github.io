superscript

noun
character set above another
4.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.pər.skrɪpt/ (AmE), /ˈsuː.pə.skrɪpt/ (BrE)
a letter, number, or symbol that is written or printed above and slightly to the side of another character, often to indicate powers or references

Examples

A superscript number
In mathematical equations, the superscript indicates an exponent.
Please write the footnote number as a superscript after the relevant word.
The isotope of carbon is often written with a mass number as a superscript.
