penetrate

verb
enter a group or organization secretly
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnəˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛnɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to enter a group, organization, or place secretly, especially in order to obtain information or spy on its members

Examples

To penetrate a group
A spy was able to penetrate the enemy's inner circle without raising suspicion.
The agency managed to penetrate the criminal network and gather valuable intelligence.
They worked for months to penetrate the secretive organization and undermine its activities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infiltrate - to enter or gain access to an organization or place surreptitiously
