clearcut

verb
to remove all trees
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌkʌt / (BrE)
to cut down all the trees in a particular area, especially for logging or land use change

Examples

To clearcut a forest
The company decided to clearcut the forest to make way for new housing developments.
Environmentalists protested when they heard the plan to clearcut the old-growth woods.
After the area was clearcut, the landscape looked barren and empty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deforest - to remove trees from an area of land
  • antonym: reforest - to plant trees in an area where there were none before
