gazelle hound

noun
tall, swift hunting dog breed from Egypt/Asia
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈzɛl haʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛl haʊnd / (BrE)
an old breed of tall, swift, and keen-eyed hunting dogs resembling greyhounds, traditionally bred in Egypt and southwestern Asia for hunting gazelles

Examples

The gazelle hound breed
In ancient times, royalty in Egypt prized the gazelle hound for hunting game across the desert.
Many believe the gazelle hound to be an ancestor of the modern saluki.
The gazelle hound is known for its incredible speed and graceful appearance.
