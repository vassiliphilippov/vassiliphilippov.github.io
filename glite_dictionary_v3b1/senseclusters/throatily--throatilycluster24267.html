throatily

adverb
deep, rough sound
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈθroʊtɪli / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊtɪli / (BrE)
in a way that sounds deep, rough, or as if coming from the throat

Examples

To sing throatily
She sang throatily, giving the song a unique and powerful character.
The actor spoke throatily to portray the villain's menacing presence.
He cleared his throat and laughed throatily at the old story.
