Chelydridae

noun
family of freshwater snapping turtles
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪdrəˌdi / (AmE), / kɛˈlɪdrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large freshwater turtles that includes the genera Chelydra and Macrochelys, commonly known as snapping turtles.

Examples

The Chelydridae family
Biologists studied the evolutionary relationships within the Chelydridae to understand their adaptation to aquatic environments.
The family Chelydridae is best known for its powerful snapping turtles found across North America.
The largest member of the Chelydridae is the alligator snapping turtle, which can weigh over 100 kilograms.
