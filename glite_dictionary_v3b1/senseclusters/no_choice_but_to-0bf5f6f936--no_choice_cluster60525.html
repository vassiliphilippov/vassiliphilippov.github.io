no choice but to

x
forced to do something; no alternative
1.7
Pronunciation: / noʊ tʃɔɪs bət tu / (AmE), / nəʊ tʃɔɪs bət tuː / (BrE)
used to say that someone is forced to do something because there are no other options available

Examples

Have no choice but to accept
Due to the unexpected expenses, I had no choice but to borrow money from a friend.
The storm left us no choice but to postpone the outdoor concert.
With all flights canceled, she had no choice but to take a long train ride back home.
