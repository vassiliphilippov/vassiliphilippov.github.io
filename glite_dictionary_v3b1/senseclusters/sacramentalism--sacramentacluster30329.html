sacramentalism

noun
emphasis on rituals
9.9
Pronunciation: /sæ.krə.ˈmen.təl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /sæ.krə.ˈmen.təl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief or practice of placing excessive importance on religious rituals or ceremonies rather than inner faith or spiritual meaning

Examples

Excessive sacramentalism
Some theologians argue that sacramentalism can distract believers from the true meaning of faith.
The church was criticized for its sacramentalism and lack of focus on personal spirituality.
Debates about sacramentalism have shaped many religious reforms throughout history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ritualism - the practice of strict or excessive observance of rituals
  • antonym: spiritualism - the belief in the importance of spiritual matters over physical or ritual forms
