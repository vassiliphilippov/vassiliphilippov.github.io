rejectionist

noun
person who refuses
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who refuses to accept or agree with something, especially proposals, changes, or compromises

Examples

A rejectionist group
The rejectionist group refused to sign the peace agreement despite international pressure.
As a known rejectionist, she consistently voted against any proposed reforms.
During the debate, the rejectionist argued strongly against the new policy changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or resists something
  • antonym: supporter - a person who agrees with or backs something
