streetcar

noun
electric rail vehicle for urban transport
3.0 1.60/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàstriÀêtÀåk…ër / (AmE), / ÀàstriÀêtk…ëÀê / (BrE)
A wheeled vehicle that runs on rails along public streets and is powered by electricity, used for carrying passengers within a city or town.

Examples

An electric streetcar
He could see the approaching streetcar from his apartment window.
Many cities have brought back the streetcar as an eco-friendly way to commute.
The streetcar glided quietly down the tracks, stopping at every corner.

Synonyms

  • trolley car
    3.8 0.10/m
    a trolley car runs on electric rails along city streets for passengers, while a streetcar names this same urban rail vehicle.
  • tramcar
    3.4 0.03/m
    a tramcar runs on tracks in urban areas and carries passengers, while a streetcar refers to that city rail vehicle.
  • trolley
    2.5 1.95/m
    a trolley names the electric rail vehicle used in cities in everyday talk, while a streetcar emphasizes the vehicle running on public streets.
  • tram
    1.3 1.51/m
    a tram can mean a city rail vehicle or a track vehicle like a mine wagon, while a streetcar refers only to urban passenger trams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tram - rail vehicle for urban passenger transport, especially in British English
