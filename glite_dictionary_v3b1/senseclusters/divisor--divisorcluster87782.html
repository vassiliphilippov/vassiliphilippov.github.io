divisor

noun
number that divides another
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / dɪˈvʌɪzə /; / dɪˈvaɪzə / (BrE)
a number that divides another number exactly (in mathematics)

Examples

A divisor of 12
Every divisor of 12 can be found by listing its factors.
In the equation 12 ÷ 3 = 4, the number 3 is the divisor.
Zero cannot be used as a divisor in arithmetic.
Can you list all the positive divisors of 18?
Every divisor of a number is also a factor of that number.
Twelve is a divisor of both 24 and 36.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dividend - the number being divided in a division operation
  • synonym: factor - a number that divides another number exactly
