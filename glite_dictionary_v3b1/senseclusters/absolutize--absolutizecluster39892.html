absolutize

verb
to make absolute
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈæb.sə.luː.taɪz/ /; / /ˈæb.sə.ljuː.taɪz/ (AmE), /ˈæb.sə.ljuː.taɪz/ /; / /ˈæb.sə.luː.taɪz/ (BrE)
to make something absolute or treat it as having no limits or exceptions

Examples

To absolutize power
Some leaders try to absolutize their authority by removing checks and balances.
It is dangerous to absolutize any one viewpoint in a diverse society.
The new law could absolutize the power of the central government over local regions.
Some philosophers warn against attempts to absolutize moral principles in every situation.
It is dangerous to absolutize one perspective and ignore all others.
The debate centered on whether to absolutize freedom of speech or allow for exceptions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empower - to give someone more control or power
  • synonym: idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than reality
  • antonym: limit - to restrict or set boundaries for something
  • antonym: relativize - to consider or treat as relative or dependent on context
