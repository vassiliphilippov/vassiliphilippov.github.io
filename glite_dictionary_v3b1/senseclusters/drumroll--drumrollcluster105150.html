drumroll

noun
series for suspense
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌmˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌrəʊl / (BrE)
a series of events, announcements, or items presented quickly one after another to build suspense or emphasis, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

A drumroll of news
The marketing team created a drumroll of social media posts leading up to the product launch.
There was a drumroll of announcements before the final winner was revealed.
The teacher gave a drumroll of hints before announcing the correct answer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crescendo - a gradual increase in intensity or excitement
  • antonym: reveal - the moment something is made known or shown
