word-of-mouth

adjective
communicated verbally; by spoken recommendation
3.4 0.7/m
Pronunciation: / Àåw…ùÀêd …ôv Ààma äŒ∏ / (AmE), / Àåw…úÀêd …ôv Ààma äŒ∏ / (BrE)
(of information, publicity, etc.) communicated verbally rather than in writing; passed from person to person by spoken recommendations.

Examples

Word-of-mouth publicity
Much of the early success of the book was due to word-of-mouth publicity.
The restaurant became popular thanks to positive word-of-mouth reviews from diners.
They learned about the opportunity through word-of-mouth rather than official advertisements.

Synonyms

  • word of mouth
    2.6 2/m
    word of mouth means the informal process of people spreading recommendations by talking, while word-of-mouth is used as an adjective describing that communication.
  • by word of mouth
    3.5 0.4/m
    by word of mouth means doing something orally by speaking to people, while word-of-mouth refers to the spoken form rather than written publicity.
