word-of-mouth

adjective
communicated verbally; by spoken recommendation
7.8 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwɜːrd.əv.maʊθ/ /; / /ˈwɜːrd.ə.maʊθ/ (AmE), /ˈwɜːd.əv.maʊθ/ /; / /ˈwɜːd.ə.maʊθ/ (BrE)
(of information, publicity, etc.) communicated verbally rather than in writing; passed from person to person by spoken recommendations.

Examples

Word-of-mouth publicity
Much of the early success of the book was due to word-of-mouth publicity.
The restaurant became popular thanks to positive word-of-mouth reviews from diners.
They learned about the opportunity through word-of-mouth rather than official advertisements.
