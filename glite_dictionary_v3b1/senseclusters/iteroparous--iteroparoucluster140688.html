iteroparous

adjective
reproducing multiple times
Pronunciation: / ˌɪtəˈrɑːpərəs / (AmE), / ˌɪtəˈræpərəs / (BrE)
able to reproduce or breed multiple times during the course of a lifetime

Examples

Iteroparous animals reproduce
Salmon are not iteroparous because they die after spawning once.
Many perennial plants are iteroparous, producing seeds year after year.
Some species of turtles are iteroparous, laying eggs several times throughout their lives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: semelparous - reproducing only once in a lifetime
