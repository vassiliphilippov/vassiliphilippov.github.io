hoolihan

noun
lively dance; commotion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhuːlɪˌhæn / (AmE), / ˈhuːlɪˌhæn / (BrE)
an old term for a lively dance or a noisy commotion, now rarely used

Examples

Lively hoolihan dance
The barn was filled with laughter and music during the hoolihan last Saturday night.
After the announcement, the office erupted into a hoolihan of cheers and applause.
In old stories, a hoolihan often followed the harvest festival in the village.

  • synonym: uproar - a state of noisy excitement or confusion
