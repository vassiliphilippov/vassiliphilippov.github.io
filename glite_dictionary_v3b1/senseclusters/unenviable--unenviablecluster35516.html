unenviable

adjective
difficult or undesirable (situation)
6.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɛnviəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɛnviəbl /; / ʌnˈɛnviəbəl / (BrE)
unpleasant, undesirable, or not likely to be envied by others

Examples

An unenviable position
He inherited the unenviable responsibility of making budget cuts.
Reporters have an unenviable task when covering disasters.
She found herself in the unenviable position of having to fire several employees.
He gained unenviable notoriety for his role in the scandal.
She found herself in the unenviable position of having to choose between her job and her friend.
The team has the unenviable task of raising funds after multiple failures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desirable - wanted or wished for because it is attractive or useful
  • antonym: enviable - desirable or worthy of envy
  • synonym: undesirable - not wanted or desirable
