fothergilla

noun
genus of ornamental flowering shrubs
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːðərˈɡɪlə / (AmE), / ˌfɒðəˈɡɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing deciduous shrubs native to the southeastern United States, noted for their spiky white flowers in spring and vibrant autumn foliage

Examples

A fothergilla shrub
Many landscapers value fothergilla for its fiery red and orange leaves in autumn.
The fothergilla in our garden blooms with beautiful white flowers every spring.
We planted a row of fothergillas along the walkway to add seasonal color.
