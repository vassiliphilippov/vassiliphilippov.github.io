oxidation-reduction

noun
reversible redox chemical reaction

Pronunciation: /ɑːk.sə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən rɪ.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ /; / /ɑːk.sɪ.ˈdeɪ.ʃən rɪ.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɒk.sɪ.ˈdeɪ.ʃən rɪ.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ /; / /ɒk.sə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən rɪ.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (BrE)
A reversible chemical reaction in which one process is an oxidation and the reverse is a reduction, commonly known as a redox reaction.

Examples

An oxidation-reduction reaction
Photosynthesis involves complex oxidation-reduction reactions within plant cells.
Scientists studied the oxidation-reduction mechanisms involved in cellular respiration.
The process of rusting is an example of an oxidation-reduction reaction in everyday life.
