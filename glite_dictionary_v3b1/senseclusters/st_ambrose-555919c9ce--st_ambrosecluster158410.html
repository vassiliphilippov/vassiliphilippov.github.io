St. Ambrose

proper noun
early Church Father and bishop of Milan
8.9
Pronunciation: /seɪnt ˈæm.broʊz/ (AmE), /seɪnt ˈæm.brəʊz/ (BrE)
(c. 340–397) A Roman priest who became bishop of Milan, recognized as a Church Father, hymn composer, and Doctor of the Church, influential in shaping early Christianity.

Examples

St. Ambrose bishop of Milan
St. Ambrose is remembered for his influential sermons and theological writings in the early Christian Church.
Every year, Milan celebrates the feast day of St. Ambrose as an important local tradition.
Many hymns attributed to St. Ambrose are still sung in Christian liturgy today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Athanasius - another influential early Church Father and bishop
  • synonym: Augustine - an early Christian theologian and philosopher, also a Church Father
