cut in

verb
move in front of another vehicle with little space
Pronunciation: / kʌt ɪn / (AmE), / kʌt ɪn / (BrE)
(of a driver or vehicle) to move suddenly in front of another vehicle, leaving too little space for safe maneuvering.

Examples

A car cuts in ahead
A taxi cut in ahead of us, forcing me to brake hard.
It's so frustrating when drivers cut in without signaling on the highway.
The red car dangerously cut in just as I was about to change lanes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut off - to interrupt or block someone, especially when driving
  • synonym: merge - to join into moving traffic
