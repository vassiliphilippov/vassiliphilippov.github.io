thyrohyoid

noun
neck muscle for swallowing
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈθaɪ.roʊ.haɪ.ɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈθaɪ.rəʊ.haɪ.ɔɪd/ (BrE)
a small muscle in the neck that connects the thyroid cartilage to the hyoid bone and helps elevate the larynx during swallowing

Examples

Thyrohyoid muscle
The thyrohyoid plays an important role in raising the larynx during swallowing.
Damage to the thyrohyoid can affect a person's ability to speak and swallow.
During anatomy class, we identified the thyrohyoid among the other muscles of the neck.
