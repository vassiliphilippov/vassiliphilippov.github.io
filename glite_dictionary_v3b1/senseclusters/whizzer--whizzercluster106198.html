whizzer

noun
very skilled person
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪzər / (AmE), / ˈwɪzə / (BrE)
a person who is exceptionally skilled or talented at a particular activity

Examples

A math whizzer
She is a real whizzer when it comes to solving complex math problems.
The new employee turned out to be a whizzer at organizing large events.
Everyone in the office knows Tom is the computer whizzer they can rely on.

  • antonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field
