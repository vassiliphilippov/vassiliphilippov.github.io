expatriate

verb
leave one's country to live abroad
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈspeɪ.tri.eɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈspæ.tri.eɪt / (BrE)
to leave one's native country to live in another country permanently or for an extended period

Examples

To expatriate for work
After the political unrest, she decided to expatriate and start anew in Canada.
He expatriated from his homeland in search of better opportunities.
Many writers chose to expatriate during the war and live in Paris.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emigrate - to leave one's own country to settle in another
  • antonym: repatriate - to return to one's home country
