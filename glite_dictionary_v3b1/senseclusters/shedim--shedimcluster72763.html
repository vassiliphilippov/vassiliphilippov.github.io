shedim

noun
Jewish supernatural spirits
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪˈdiːm / (AmE), / ʃeɪˈdiːm / (BrE)
supernatural beings or spirits in Jewish folklore, often considered mischievous or malevolent

Examples

The shedim spirits
Ancient texts describe the shedim as spirits that dwell in deserted places.
Some legends warn travelers to avoid angering the shedim at night.
The rabbi told a story about the shedim who played tricks on villagers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: angel - a benevolent spiritual being in various religions
  • synonym: demon - an evil spirit or supernatural being
