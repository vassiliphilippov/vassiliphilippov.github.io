revetement

noun
protective facing for embankments or walls
9.9
Pronunciation: / rəˈvɛtmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛtmənt / (BrE)
a facing of stone, concrete, or other material built to support or protect an embankment, wall, or slope

Examples

Stone revetement wall
A stone revetement protects the highway embankment from flooding.
During construction, the slope was reinforced with a concrete revetement.
The engineers installed a revetement along the riverbank to prevent erosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retaining wall - a wall that holds back earth or water
