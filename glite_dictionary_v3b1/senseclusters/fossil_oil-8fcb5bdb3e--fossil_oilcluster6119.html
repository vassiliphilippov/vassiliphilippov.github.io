fossil oil

noun
oil from ancient organic matter; petroleum
2.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.səl ɔɪl/ /; / /ˈfɑː.səl ɔɪ.əl/ (AmE), /ˈfɒs.əl ɔɪl/ /; / /ˈfɒs.əl ɔɪ.əl/ (BrE)
A naturally occurring dark oil formed from the remains of ancient plants and animals, consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, and used as a fuel and raw material in industry.

Examples

Extracting fossil oil
Extracting fossil oil can have significant impacts on local ecosystems.
Many environmentalists are campaigning to reduce the world's reliance on fossil oil.
The company is investing in alternatives to fossil oil for energy production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crude oil - unrefined petroleum as it occurs naturally
  • synonym: petroleum - a liquid mixture of hydrocarbons present in certain rock strata and extracted for use as fuel
