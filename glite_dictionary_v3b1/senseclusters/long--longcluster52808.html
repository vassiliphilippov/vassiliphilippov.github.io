long

adjective
having more than usual or necessary
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋ / (BrE)
greater than usual in length, time, or amount

Examples

A long road
He is long on ambition, though his skills are still developing.
The company is long on ideas but short on practical solutions.
They were long on supplies, so there was never a shortage.
After a long discussion, the team decided on a new strategy.
The ceremony lasted much longer than anyone expected.
They waited for a long time before the train finally arrived.
She prefers to write with long pencils because they last longer.
The long road stretched ahead of us, disappearing into the distance.
This dress is too long for me and needs to be hemmed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short - having less than usual or necessary
  • degree higher: tall - having a greater than average height (for people or objects)
