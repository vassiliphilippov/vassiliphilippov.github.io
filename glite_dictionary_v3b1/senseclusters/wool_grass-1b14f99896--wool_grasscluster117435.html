wool grass

noun
tall grass with plume-like flower heads
Pronunciation: / wʊl ɡræs / (AmE), / wʊl ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A tall grass, often cultivated for its long, white-ribbed leaves and large, plume-like flower heads resembling those of pampas grass.

Examples

Tall wool grass plumes
Many landscapers choose wool grass for its striking plumes that last through the autumn.
The garden features several varieties of wool grass to add texture and interest to the landscape.
The soft, white tufts of wool grass sway gracefully in the breeze.
