force field

noun
area around an object exerting force
Pronunciation: / fɔrs fiːld / (AmE), / fɔːs fiːld / (BrE)
an area of space surrounding an object, especially a radiating body, within which it can exert a force on another object not in contact with it, typically through electromagnetic or other physical forces

Examples

Magnetic force field
A magnet creates a visible force field around itself, which can be demonstrated using iron filings.
In science fiction films, a force field is often portrayed as a shield that blocks attacks.
The scientist explained how an electromagnetic force field protects the planet from solar radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: field - a region affected by a particular force, such as gravity or magnetism
