laser interferometer

noun
interferometer using a laser light source
6.5 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.zər ɪn.tər.fər.ˈɑː.mə.tər/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.zə ɪn.tə.fə.ˈrɒm.ɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈleɪ.sə ɪn.tə.fə.ˈrɒm.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
An interferometer that uses a laser as its light source, allowing measurement of much larger path differences than classical interferometers due to the laser's coherence and intensity.

Examples

Laser interferometer device
Researchers used a laser interferometer to achieve precise measurements for their experiment.
The laser interferometer can detect incredibly small changes in distance caused by gravitational waves.
Unlike traditional models, the laser interferometer allows for greater sensitivity over long path lengths.
