contrasty

adjective
having strong contrasts in images
7.4 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.træ.sti/ /; / /ˈkɑːn.trə.sti/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.trɑː.sti/ /; / /ˈkɒn.trə.sti/ (BrE)
having strong or sharp differences in light, color, tone, or style (especially in images, art, or design)

Examples

A contrasty photo
If your photo is too contrasty, you can reduce the effect with careful editing.
Old black-and-white films often have a contrasty appearance due to the film stock used.
The photographer preferred a contrasty look to emphasize the drama in his images.
The artist used a contrasty palette to make the painting stand out.
Her photographs are known for their contrasty lighting and bold shadows.
The magazine cover looked more dramatic after a contrasty redesign.

  • antonym: flat - lacking contrast, appearing uniform in tone
  • antonym: muted - not bright or strong in color or tone
  • synonym: vivid - bright and intense in color or appearance
