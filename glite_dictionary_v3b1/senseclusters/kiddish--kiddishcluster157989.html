kiddish

adjective
childlike or immature
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɪdɪʃ / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of a child, especially in a naive or immature way

Examples

Kiddish behavior
Her kiddish excitement over the simple game was contagious to everyone around her.
The manager was frustrated by his team's kiddish arguments during the meeting.
Although he is an adult, his kiddish sense of humor makes him popular with children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: childish - typical of a child, especially in a negative or immature way
  • synonym: childlike - having qualities of a child, such as innocence or simplicity
  • antonym: mature - showing the mental and emotional qualities of an adult
