buffer

noun
compound that resists pH change

Pronunciation: /ˈbʌf.ər/ (AmE), /ˈbʌf.ə/ (BrE)
(chemistry) A solution or compound that resists changes in pH when an acid or alkali is added.

Examples

A buffer solution
Blood contains a buffer system that helps maintain a stable pH.
Scientists use a buffer to keep the pH constant during experiments.
The addition of a buffer prevented the solution from becoming too acidic.
