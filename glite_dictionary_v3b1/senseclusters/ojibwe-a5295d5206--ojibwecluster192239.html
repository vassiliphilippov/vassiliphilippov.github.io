Ojibwe

proper noun
Native American people
Pronunciation: / oʊˈdʒɪbweɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪbweɪ / (BrE)
a Native American people of the Great Lakes region, or the Algonquian language they speak

Examples

The Ojibwe people
The Ojibwe are known for their intricate beadwork and birchbark canoes.
Many Ojibwe communities still speak their traditional language today.
The Ojibwe have lived in the Great Lakes region for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Algonquian - a group of Native American peoples and languages including Ojibwe
  • synonym: Chippewa - another name for the Ojibwe people
