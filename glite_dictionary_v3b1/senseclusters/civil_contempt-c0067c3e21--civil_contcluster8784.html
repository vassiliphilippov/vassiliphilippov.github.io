civil contempt

noun
failure to comply with a court order (legal)
5.6 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪv.əl kən.ˈtempt/ (AmE), /ˈsɪv.əl kən.ˈtempt/ (BrE)
the offense of failing to comply with a court order that is intended to benefit another party, usually resulting in sanctions to compel compliance rather than punish

Examples

Civil contempt of court
If a party refuses to pay court-ordered child support, they may be held in civil contempt.
She was found in civil contempt for not complying with the custody arrangement.
The court issued a fine for civil contempt after the company failed to produce documents as ordered.

Related Terms

  • antonym: criminal contempt - an act that defies or disrespects a court's authority or dignity, punished as an offense against the court
