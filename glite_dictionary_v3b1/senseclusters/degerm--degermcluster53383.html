degerm

verb
to remove germs
7.4
Pronunciation: / diːˈdʒɜrm / (AmE), / diːˈdʒɜːm / (BrE)
to remove microorganisms from something, especially to clean or disinfect it to reduce germs

Examples

To degerm a wound
Nurses carefully degermed the wound with antiseptic before applying the bandage.
Before surgery, doctors always degerm their hands to prevent infection.
The cleaning staff degermed all surfaces in the hospital room after the patient was discharged.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contaminate - to make something impure by adding harmful substances or germs
  • synonym: disinfect - to clean something to destroy bacteria or viruses
  • synonym: sterilize - to make something completely free from bacteria or other living microorganisms
