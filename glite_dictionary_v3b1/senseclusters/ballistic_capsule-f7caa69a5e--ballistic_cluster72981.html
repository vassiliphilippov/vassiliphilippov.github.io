ballistic capsule

noun
spacecraft following a ballistic trajectory
Pronunciation: / bəˈlɪstɪk ˈkæpsəl / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪk ˈkæpsjuːl / (BrE)
a craft designed to travel in outer space, typically following a ballistic trajectory and used especially in early manned space missions

Examples

A ballistic capsule reentry
During reentry, the ballistic capsule followed a steep descent path through Earth's atmosphere.
Engineers designed the ballistic capsule to withstand the intense heat of atmospheric reentry.
The Mercury missions used a ballistic capsule to send astronauts into orbit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: space capsule - a small spacecraft for carrying humans into outer space
