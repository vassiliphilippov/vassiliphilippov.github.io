electron radiation

noun
emission of electrons in radioactive decay
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛk.trɑn ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the emission of beta particles (electrons) as a form of radiation during radioactive decay

Examples

Electron radiation emission
Protective shielding is used to limit exposure to electron radiation in laboratories.
Scientists detected electron radiation emitted from the decaying isotope.
The study measured the intensity of electron radiation from several radioactive materials.
