frigate

noun
18th–19th century square-rigged warship
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈfrɪɡət / (BrE)
a medium-sized, fast, square-rigged warship used in the 18th and 19th centuries, typically armed and employed for escorting and patrol duties

Examples

18th-century frigate
Historians consider the frigate a crucial vessel in 18th-century naval warfare.
The British navy deployed a frigate to patrol the newly discovered waters.
The old harbor museum features a restored frigate from the Napoleonic Wars.
