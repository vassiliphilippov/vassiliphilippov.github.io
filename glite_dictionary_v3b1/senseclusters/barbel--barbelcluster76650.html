barbel

noun
whisker-like organ on a fish
7.8 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːr.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈbɑː.bəl/ (BrE)
a slender, whisker-like tactile organ located near the mouth of certain fish, such as catfish or carp

Examples

Barbels on fish
Some species of fish rely on their barbels to detect changes in their environment.
The catfish uses its barbel to search for food in murky water.
You can see the barbels protruding from the sturgeon’s mouth.
