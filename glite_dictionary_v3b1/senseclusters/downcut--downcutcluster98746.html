downcut

verb
to erode downward
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (BrE)
to erode or cut downward into a surface, especially by the action of a river or stream

Examples

Rivers downcut canyons
Over thousands of years, rivers can downcut deep canyons into the landscape.
The stream continued to downcut its channel after the heavy rainfall.
Geologists study how water flow helps downcut valleys and shape the terrain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: build up - to accumulate or increase in height or amount
  • synonym: erode - to gradually wear away soil, rock, or land
  • synonym: incise - to cut into a surface sharply or deeply
