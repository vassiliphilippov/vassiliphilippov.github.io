pillory

verb
criticize harshly in public
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪlɚi /; / ˈpɪləri / (AmE), / ˈpɪləri /; / ˈpɪlɒri / (BrE)
to publicly criticize or ridicule someone, especially in a way that exposes them to scorn or contempt

Examples

To pillory a politician
Many celebrities have been pilloried online for their public statements.
She felt unfairly pilloried by the critics after her first novel was published.
The senator was pilloried in the media for his controversial remarks.
Online platforms can sometimes pillory people for minor mistakes, leading to intense public scrutiny.
She felt unfairly pilloried by her colleagues after the project failed.
The mayor was pilloried by the media for his handling of the crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lambaste - to criticize someone or something severely
  • synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful or contemptuous manner
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
