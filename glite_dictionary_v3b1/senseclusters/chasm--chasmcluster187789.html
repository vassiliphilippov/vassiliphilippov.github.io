chasm

noun
deep opening or gap in earth
Pronunciation: / ˈkæzəm / (AmE), / ˈkazəm / (BrE)
a deep, gaping opening or fissure in the earth's surface; a large or profound gap

Examples

A deep chasm
A narrow bridge spanned the chasm between the two cliffs.
After the earthquake, a vast chasm appeared in the main street.
The hikers peered cautiously into the dark chasm below.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abyss - a deep or seemingly bottomless chasm
  • synonym: gorge - a deep, narrow valley with steep rocky sides, often with a stream
