Tironian

adjective
of Tiro's shorthand
Pronunciation: / tɪˈroʊniən / (AmE), / tɪˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the system of shorthand invented by Tiro, a secretary of Cicero, in ancient Rome

Examples

Tironian shorthand system
The scholar studied the Tironian notes found in the ancient manuscript.
Some medieval texts include Tironian symbols to save space and time.
Learning the Tironian system can help historians read old Roman documents.
