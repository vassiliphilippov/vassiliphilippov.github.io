nandina

noun
evergreen Asian shrub
Pronunciation: / nænˈdiːnə / (AmE), / nænˈdiːnə / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to East Asia, often grown for its colorful foliage and red berries, also known as heavenly bamboo

Examples

The nandina shrub
The landscaper recommended planting nandina for its attractive red berries in winter.
Many gardeners appreciate nandina because it stays green throughout the year.
Birds are often drawn to the bright berries of the nandina in our backyard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bamboo - a tall grass with woody stems, often confused with nandina due to similar appearance
  • synonym: heavenly bamboo - another name for nandina, an evergreen shrub with bamboo-like leaves
