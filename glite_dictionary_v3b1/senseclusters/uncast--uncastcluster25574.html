uncast

verb
to remove from cast
9.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkæst / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɑːst / (BrE)
to remove someone from the cast of a play, film, or other performance

Examples

To uncast an actor
After the controversy, the director decided to uncast the lead actress and search for a replacement.
The producers had to uncast several performers due to scheduling conflicts before filming began.
When the script changed, the studio uncast the original villain and brought in a new actor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cast - to assign an actor to a role
  • synonym: recast - to assign a new actor to a role
