uncast

verb
to remove from cast
9.1
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈkæst/ /; / /ən.ˈkæst/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈkɑːst/ /; / /ən.ˈkɑːst/ (BrE)
to remove someone from the cast of a play, film, or other performance

Examples

To uncast an actor

The producers had to uncast several performers due to scheduling conflicts before filming began.
When the script changed, the studio uncast the original villain and brought in a new actor.
