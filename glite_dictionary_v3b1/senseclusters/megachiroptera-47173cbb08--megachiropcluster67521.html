Megachiroptera

noun
suborder of fruit bats
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛgəˌkaɪrəpˈtɛrə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˌkaɪərəpˈtɛərə / (BrE)
A suborder of bats, commonly known as fruit bats or flying foxes, characterized by large eyes and primarily frugivorous diets.

Examples

Megachiroptera fruit bats
Scientists studied the migratory patterns of Megachiroptera in Southeast Asia.
The rainforest is home to several species of Megachiroptera that feed on fruit and nectar.
Unlike their smaller relatives, Megachiroptera generally do not use echolocation to navigate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Microchiroptera - the suborder of small, echolocating bats
