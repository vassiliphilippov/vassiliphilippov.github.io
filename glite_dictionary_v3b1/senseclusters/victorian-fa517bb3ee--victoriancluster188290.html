Victorian

adjective
relating to the morals/manner of Victoria's reign
3.4 6.5
Pronunciation: / vɪkˈtɔriən / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːriən / (BrE)
having or showing very strict or old-fashioned moral standards, especially about sex and social behavior

Examples

Victorian values
Her parents hold very Victorian views about relationships and modesty.
Some consider these strict dress codes too Victorian for modern society.
The novel satirizes the Victorian obsession with propriety and appearances.
His Victorian views on marriage seem outdated in today's society.
She grew up in a Victorian household where even dancing was discouraged.
The school's Victorian rules prohibit any displays of affection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permissive - allowing great freedom of behavior
  • synonym: puritanical - Having strict moral or religious beliefs, especially about pleasure and sex
  • synonym: straitlaced - having very strict moral standards
