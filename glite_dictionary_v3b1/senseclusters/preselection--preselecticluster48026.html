preselection

noun
radio frequency filter
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːsəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːsɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a device or process that selects a narrow band of radio frequencies before the main receiver to improve signal reception

Examples

Radio preselection
The technician adjusted the preselection to reduce interference from nearby stations.
Modern radios often include a preselection stage to improve signal clarity.
Without proper preselection, the receiver picked up too much background noise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filter - a device that removes unwanted parts of a signal
  • synonym: preselector - a device that filters radio frequencies before the main receiver
