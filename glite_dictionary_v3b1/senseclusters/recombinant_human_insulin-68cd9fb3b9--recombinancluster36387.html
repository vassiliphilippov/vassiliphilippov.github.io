recombinant human insulin

noun
genetically engineered human insulin for diabetes treatment
4.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɑːmbɪnənt ˈhjuːmən ɪnˈsʊlɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒmbɪnənt ˈhjuːmən ɪnˈsʊlɪn / (BrE)
a genetically engineered form of human insulin produced using recombinant DNA technology, used to treat diabetes, especially in patients allergic to animal-derived insulins

Examples

Recombinant human insulin treatment
Many patients with type 1 diabetes rely on recombinant human insulin to manage their blood sugar levels.
Some people develop allergies to animal insulin, so doctors prescribe recombinant human insulin instead.
The introduction of recombinant human insulin in the 1980s revolutionized diabetes treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insulin - a hormone that regulates blood sugar levels
