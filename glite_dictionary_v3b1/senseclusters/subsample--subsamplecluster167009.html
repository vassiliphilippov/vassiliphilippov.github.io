subsample

noun
smaller sample group
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a smaller group or portion taken from a larger sample, dataset, or signal for analysis or testing

Examples

A subsample group
The researchers analyzed each subsample to ensure the results were consistent across different groups.
A subsample of the population was selected for a more detailed survey.
After dividing the main sample, each subsample underwent separate chemical testing.
The researchers analyzed a subsample of the survey responses to identify trends.
A subsample of the image was used to speed up the processing time.
By working with a subsample, the team reduced the amount of data they needed to store.

  • degree higher: sample - a part or quantity taken from a larger whole for testing or analysis
  • synonym: subset - a set that is part of a larger set
