physic

noun
a purgative medicine
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪzɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪk / (BrE)
a medicine that induces evacuation of the bowels; a purgative or laxative, especially as used in historical or medical contexts

Examples

A strong physic
After taking the physic, he soon felt an urgent need to visit the bathroom.
In the past, people often used herbal physic to cleanse the body.
The doctor prescribed a strong physic to relieve the patient's constipation.
