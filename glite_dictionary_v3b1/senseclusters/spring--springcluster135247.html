spring

verb
to develop suddenly (as of a problem or change)
4.1 2.1
Pronunciation: /sprɪŋ/ (AmE), /sprɪŋ/ (BrE)
to appear, happen, or be revealed suddenly or unexpectedly

Examples

Problems spring up
After years of peace, conflict suddenly sprang up between the two communities.
She was shocked when a problem sprang out of nowhere at the last minute.
The pipe sprang a leak during the freezing winter night.
He loves to spring surprises on his coworkers during team meetings.
She decided to spring the big announcement on her family at dinner.
The company CEO sprang several new policies on the staff without warning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appear - to begin to be seen or exist suddenly
  • antonym: conceal - to keep from being seen or known; to hide
  • synonym: occur - to happen, especially suddenly or by chance
  • synonym: reveal - to make known something that was secret or unknown
