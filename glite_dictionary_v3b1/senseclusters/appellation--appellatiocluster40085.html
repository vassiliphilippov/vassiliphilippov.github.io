appellation

noun
geographical name for wine origin
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A legally defined and protected geographical name used to identify where the grapes for a wine are grown.

Examples

French wine appellation
Producers must follow strict regulations to use the Champagne appellation.
The wine label indicated that it came from the Bordeaux appellation.
This region was recently granted its own official appellation status.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brand - a type of product manufactured by a particular company under a particular name
  • synonym: denomination - a name or designation, especially one for a class of things
