downslide

noun
sudden slope movement
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌslaɪd / (BrE)
a sudden movement of earth, rocks, or debris sliding downward on a slope

Examples

A downslide of rocks
After heavy rain, a downslide sent boulders across the highway, blocking traffic for hours.
The village was evacuated due to the risk of another downslide following the earthquake.
Engineers studied the area to prevent future downslides from damaging the road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avalanche - a large mass of snow, ice, or rock falling quickly down a mountain
  • synonym: landslide - a mass of earth or rock moving down a slope
