kinin

noun
protein causing inflammation
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪnɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaɪnɪn / (BrE)
a group of proteins in the body that cause blood vessels to widen and increase pain or inflammation

Examples

Blood kinin protein
The doctor explained that kinin levels rise during allergic reactions.
Scientists study how kinin affects blood pressure and inflammation.
An increase in kinin can make swelling and pain worse after an injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bradykinin - a specific kinin that causes blood vessels to widen
  • synonym: histamine - a compound involved in immune responses and inflammation
