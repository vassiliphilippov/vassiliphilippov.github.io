rose between two thorns

noun
pleasant person among unpleasant company
5.0
Pronunciation: / roʊz bɪˈtwin tu θɔrnz / (AmE), / rəʊz bɪˈtwiːn tuː θɔːnz / (BrE)
A person who is pleasant or attractive placed between two less pleasant or less attractive people, or in an unpleasant situation.

Examples

The rose between two thorns phrase
As the rose between two thorns, she managed to make the meeting more enjoyable for everyone.
At the dinner party, she was the rose between two thorns, bringing warmth to an awkward conversation.
Despite the tense atmosphere, James was the rose between two thorns and helped everyone get along.
