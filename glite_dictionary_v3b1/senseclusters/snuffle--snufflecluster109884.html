snuffle

verb
breathe noisily through nose
2.7 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsnʌf.əl/ (AmE), /ˈsnʌf.əl/ (BrE)
to breathe noisily through a blocked or congested nose, often because of illness, crying, or emotion

Examples

To snuffle with a cold
Allergy season makes her snuffle constantly at her desk.
He began to snuffle as the cold took hold of him.
The child snuffled noisily while looking for a tissue.
He snuffled through his words, tears running down his cheeks.
She couldn't help but snuffle as she explained what had upset her.
The child began to snuffle quietly after being told off.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sniffle - to sniff repeatedly, typically because of a cold or crying
  • synonym: snivel - to cry and sniffle, complain in a tearful way
  • synonym: snort - to force air noisily through the nose
