Articles of Confederation

proper noun
first U.S. constitution (1781–1789)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrtɪkəlz əv kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪklz əv kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the first constitution of the United States, ratified in 1781, which established a loose confederation of sovereign states and a weak central government

Examples
