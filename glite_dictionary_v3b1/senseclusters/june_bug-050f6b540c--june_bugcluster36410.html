June bug

noun
large North American beetle active in late spring
7.7 5.8
Pronunciation: / dʒuːn bʌɡ / (AmE), / dʒuːn bʌɡ / (BrE)
A large, usually brown, North American beetle commonly seen in late spring, whose larvae feed on the roots of grasses and similar plants.

Examples

A June bug beetle
A June bug landed on the porch light, attracted by its brightness.
Children caught June bugs in jars to watch them crawl and fly around.
The lawn was damaged because June bugs had eaten the grass roots as larvae.
