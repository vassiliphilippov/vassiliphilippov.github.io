bescreen

verb
to cover or shield
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɪˈskriːn / (AmE), / bɪˈskriːn / (BrE)
to cover, shield, or conceal as if with a screen; now archaic

Examples

To bescreen with hedges
The garden was bescreened by tall hedges, making it a private retreat.
In the old play, the heroine bescreens herself behind a curtain to avoid being seen.
Clouds bescreened the sun, casting the valley into shadow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expose - to leave uncovered or unprotected
