work time

noun
period when one is required to work
2.4 0.86/m
Pronunciation: / w…ùk ta…™m / (AmE), / w…úÀêk ta…™m / (BrE)
the period during which a person is expected or required to be working, especially as set by an employer or by law

Examples

Work time hours
During work time, employees are expected to stay focused on their assigned tasks.
He prefers to keep his personal phone off during work time.
Lunch breaks are not counted as part of your work time.

Synonyms

  • workday
    1.0 0.90/m
    workday is the main part of a day someone works for pay, while work time is the period an employer or law sets.
  • shift
    2.1 16/m
    shift is a scheduled block of working hours, often shared by teams, while work time means the hours one must be working.
  • duty period
    3.1 0.04/m
    duty period is an official span when someone is on duty, often in services, while work time is any employer-set required working period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leisure time - time when one is not working; free time
  • synonym: working hours - the times of day during which employees are expected to be at work
