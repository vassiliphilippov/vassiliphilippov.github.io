topicalize

verb
in linguistics, move topic to front of sentence
6.3 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑːp.ɪ.kə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈtɒp.ɪ.kə.laɪz/ (BrE)
(in linguistics) to give emphasis to part of a sentence by placing it at the beginning or giving it special intonation.

Examples

To topicalize the subject
In her analysis, Mary topicalized the subject to show its prominence in the statement.
Linguists study how speakers topicalize certain elements in conversation to highlight their importance.
Some languages frequently topicalize objects, moving them to the front of the sentence for emphasis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
