in a way that shows a strong belief that your own actions, opinions, or morals are better than those of others, often with a tone of moral superiority

Examples

To self-righteously insist
Even though she was wrong, she self-righteously insisted she had acted correctly.
He self-righteously condemned his colleagues for their lack of commitment.
The politician spoke self-righteously about ethics, ignoring his own questionable behavior.
