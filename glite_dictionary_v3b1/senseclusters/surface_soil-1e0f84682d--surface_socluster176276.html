surface soil

noun
top layer of soil
7.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.fəs sɔɪl/ /; / /ˈsɜːr.fɪs sɔɪl/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.fɪs sɔɪl/ /; / /ˈsɜː.fəs sɔɪl/ (BrE)
the uppermost layer of soil, found at the earth's surface, typically rich in organic material and nutrients

Examples

The surface soil
Farmers often test the surface soil to determine which nutrients are lacking before planting crops.
Heavy rain eroded the surface soil, leaving the roots of the plants exposed.
The quality of the surface soil greatly affects the types of vegetation that can grow in an area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subsoil - the layer of soil beneath the surface or topsoil
  • synonym: topsoil - the top layer of soil, often synonymous with surface soil
