torus

noun
ring-shaped surface; doughnut shape
8.8 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îr.…ôs / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀê.r…ôs / (BrE)
A surface or solid having the shape of a ring, generated by rotating a circle around an axis outside the circle; commonly described as a doughnut shape in geometry and mathematics.

Examples

A torus shape
In mathematics, a bagel is often used as an everyday example of a torus.
The inner tube of a bicycle is shaped like a torus and is filled with air.
The topology class studied the properties of a torus and compared it with those of a sphere.

Synonyms

  • toroid
    9.9 0.01/m
    toroid is a ring-shaped solid formed by rotating a circle around an outside axis, while torus is the usual mathematical name for that doughnut shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ring - a circular band, often used as a synonym in geometry
