epiphenomenal

adjective
secondary effect
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˌfɛˈnɑːmənl /; / ˌɛpɪfəˈnɑːmənl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˌfɪˈnɒmɪnəl /; / ˌɛpɪfəˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
happening as a secondary effect or byproduct, not as a main cause (often used about mental events being byproducts of physical processes)

Examples

Epiphenomenal mental events
Some scientists believe that emotions are epiphenomenal and do not directly influence physical processes.
The noise produced by the machine is merely an epiphenomenal result of its operation.
In certain theories, consciousness is considered epiphenomenal rather than a driving force in behavior.
Some scientists argue that consciousness is merely an epiphenomenal result of brain activity.
The debate over whether mental states are epiphenomenal continues in philosophy of mind.
He dismissed the idea that emotions are epiphenomenal and have no real influence on behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: causal - having the power to cause an effect
  • synonym: incidental - happening as a minor part or result of something else
  • antonym: primary - main or most important; not secondary
  • synonym: secondary - not primary; coming after or as a result of something else
