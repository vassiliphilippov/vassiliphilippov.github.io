shoot

verb
begin growth (of plants); produce buds or branches
2.8 0.95/m
Pronunciation: /  Éut / (AmE), /  ÉuÀêt / (BrE)
(of a plant) to produce buds, branches, or to begin to grow; to germinate.

Examples

Plants shoot up
If you water the bulbs now, they should shoot within a few weeks.
In spring, the rose bushes shoot new branches rapidly.
The seeds lay dormant all winter, and began to shoot when the weather warmed.

Synonyms

    2.0 5.80/m

  • bud

  • germinate

  • burgeon forth
