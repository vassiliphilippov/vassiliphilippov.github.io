red meat

noun
meat from mammals, dark before cooking
Pronunciation: / rɛd mit / (AmE), / rɛd miːt / (BrE)
meat from mammals (such as beef, lamb, pork, or venison) that is red or dark before cooking, as opposed to poultry or fish

Examples

A red meat dish
Beef and lamb are popular types of red meat in many cultures.
Doctors often recommend eating less red meat to reduce the risk of heart disease.
Unlike red meat, chicken is classified as white meat.
Many people try to avoid red meat for health reasons.
Nutritionists often compare red meat and white meat when discussing healthy diets.
The restaurant offers several dishes made with locally sourced red meat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: white meat - meat that is light in color before cooking, such as poultry or fish
