cloudy

adjective
not clear; containing suspended particles
2.3 0.78/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆklaÊŠdi / (AmE), / ËˆklaÊŠdi / (BrE)
(of a liquid) not clear or transparent; having suspended particles, as if clouded by sediment.

Examples

Cloudy water
After the storm, the river water became cloudy with silt and debris.
Homemade apple cider is often cloudy because it contains natural sediment.
She poured a cloudy solution into the test tube for the experiment.

Synonyms

  • turbid
    8.1 0.39/m
    turbid describes water thick with stirred-up sediment in a technical way, while cloudy means a liquid that is not clear.
  • murky
    3.7 2.01/m
    murky feels dark and hard to see in the water, while cloudy means a liquid that is not clear from suspended particles.
  • muddy
    1.1 6.92/m
    muddy often shows mud or soil mixed into water, while cloudy describes any liquid made not clear by particles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - (of liquids) transparent, free from cloudiness or particles
  • synonym: murky - (of liquids) dark or dirty with suspended particles; not clear
