cloudy

adjective
not clear; containing suspended particles
3.7 2.0
Pronunciation: /ˈklaʊ.diː/ (AmE), /ˈklaʊ.diː/ (BrE)
(of a liquid) not clear or transparent; having suspended particles, as if clouded by sediment.

Examples

Cloudy water
After the storm, the river water became cloudy with silt and debris.
Homemade apple cider is often cloudy because it contains natural sediment.
She poured a cloudy solution into the test tube for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - (of liquids) transparent, free from cloudiness or particles
  • synonym: murky - (of liquids) dark or dirty with suspended particles; not clear
