unsalable

adjective
impossible to sell
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈseɪləbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪləbəl /; / ʌnˈseɪləb(ə)l / (BrE)
Impossible to sell; not able to be sold, especially because it is undesirable or defective.

Examples

An unsalable product
Due to safety concerns, the recalled product became instantly unsalable.
His paintings were considered unsalable in the current art market.
The damaged goods were declared unsalable and removed from the shelves.
After the product recall, the toys became unsaleable due to safety concerns.
Expired food items are unsaleable and must be discarded according to regulations.
The artwork was so damaged that it was considered unsaleable by collectors.
