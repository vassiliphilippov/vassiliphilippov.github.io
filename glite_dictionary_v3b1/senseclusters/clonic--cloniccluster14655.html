clonic

adjective
relating to rapid muscle contractions and relaxations
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˈklɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a muscle contraction) characterized by rapidly alternating periods of contraction and relaxation, typically seen in certain neurological conditions.

Examples

Repeated clonic movements
Repeated clonic movements of the limbs can indicate certain types of epilepsy.
The doctor noted clonic spasms in the patient's right arm.
The patient experienced a clonic seizure during the neurological examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tonic - relating to continuous, sustained muscle contraction
