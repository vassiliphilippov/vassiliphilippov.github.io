common cotton grass

noun
marsh plant with cottony seed heads
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑm.ən ˈkɑt̬.n̩ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən ˈkɒt.n̩ ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial sedge (Eriophorum angustifolium) found in boggy or marshy ground, noted for its dense tufts of white, cottony seed heads.

Examples

The common cotton grass plant
In summer, the marsh was covered with tufts of common cotton grass swaying in the breeze.
Researchers studied how climate change affects the growth of common cotton grass in peat bogs.
The fluffy heads of common cotton grass are a familiar sight in wetland habitats across northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cottongrass - any plant of the genus Eriophorum, with fluffy white seed heads
