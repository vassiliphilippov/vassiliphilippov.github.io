confidence

noun
belief in oneself or abilities
2.1 35/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên.f…™.d…ôns / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.f…™.d…ôns / (BrE)
A feeling or belief that you can do something well or succeed at something; freedom from doubt about your abilities or qualities.

Examples

Answer with confidence
He answered the difficult question with confidence, impressing everyone in the room.
Her confidence grew with every successful presentation she gave at work.
The training helped restore her confidence after months of uncertainty.

Synonyms

  • self-confidence
    3.5 5.28/m
    self-confidence is belief in your own abilities and worth, while confidence is a general feeling you can succeed.
  • self-assurance
    3.5 0.69/m
    self-assurance is calm trust in your judgment and actions, while confidence is a broader belief about ability to succeed.
  • assurance
    3.3 3.82/m
    assurance is a more formal feeling of certainty or guarantee about success, while confidence is personal belief in ability.
  • sureness
    3.6 0.22/m
    sureness is a plain sense of certainty or lack of doubt, while confidence is belief you can do something well.
