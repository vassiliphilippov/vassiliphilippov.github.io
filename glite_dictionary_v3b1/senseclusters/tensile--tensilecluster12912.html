tensile

adjective
capable of being stretched or drawn out
7.2 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈten.saɪl/ /; / /ˈten.səl/ /; / /ˈten.ɪl/ (AmE), /ˈten.saɪl/ /; / /ˈten.saɪ.əl/ (BrE)
(of a material) able to be stretched or related to tension or stretching

Examples

Tensile strength
Engineers must calculate the tensile properties of materials before using them in design.
Nylon fibers are valued for their tensile strength and flexibility.
The bridge is constructed from tensile steel that can withstand extreme forces.
Engineers tested the tensile properties of the new synthetic fiber.
Rubber bands are known for their excellent tensile flexibility.
The bridge cables must have high tensile strength to safely support the deck.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brittle - easily broken, cracked, or snapped; not flexible
  • antonym: compressive - relating to or denoting compression
  • synonym: ductile - (of a material) able to be drawn out into a thin wire
  • synonym: malleable - (of a metal or material) able to be hammered or pressed into shape without breaking or cracking
