white chocolate

noun
a confection made from cocoa butter, milk, and sugar
3.8
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈtʃɑk(ə)lət / (AmE), / waɪt ˈtʃɒklət / (BrE)
a sweet, pale confection made from cocoa butter, sugar, milk solids, and often vanilla, but containing no cocoa solids

Examples

A bar of white chocolate
I prefer white chocolate to dark chocolate in my desserts.
She baked cookies with chunks of white chocolate and cranberries.
The cake was topped with shavings of white chocolate for added sweetness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dark chocolate - chocolate containing a high percentage of cocoa solids and little or no milk
  • synonym: milk chocolate - a sweet chocolate containing milk powder or condensed milk
