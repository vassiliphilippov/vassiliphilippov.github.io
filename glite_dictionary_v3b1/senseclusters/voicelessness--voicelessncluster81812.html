voicelessness

noun
loss or inability to produce voice
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔɪsləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪslɪsnəs / (BrE)
the state or condition of being unable to produce voice, especially due to a disorder of the vocal organs

Examples

Medical voicelessness
After throat surgery, Mary experienced temporary voicelessness and had to communicate by writing.
The singer feared that chronic laryngitis might lead to permanent voicelessness.
Therapy and rest helped him recover from a period of voicelessness following the illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphonia - loss of ability to speak due to vocal cord damage
