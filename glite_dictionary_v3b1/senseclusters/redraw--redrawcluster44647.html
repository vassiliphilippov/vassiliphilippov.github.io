redraw

verb
to withdraw repaid funds
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈdrɔ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdrɔː / (BrE)
to withdraw money that has previously been paid into a loan or mortgage account, making those funds available again.

Examples

To redraw funds
After making extra payments, she was able to redraw funds from her mortgage for home improvements.
Some loans allow you to redraw money you have already paid back if you need it later.
He chose to redraw a portion of his previous repayments to cover unexpected expenses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deposit - to put money into an account
  • synonym: withdraw - to take money out of an account
