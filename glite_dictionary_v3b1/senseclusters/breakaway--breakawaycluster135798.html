breakaway

noun
an act of separating or withdrawing
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkəˌweɪ / (BrE)
an act or instance of separating from a group, organization, or tradition, often to form something new or become independent

Examples

A sudden breakaway
The band's latest album reflects their breakaway from traditional rock influences.
The political party experienced a significant breakaway by several of its senior members.
There was a dramatic breakaway in the final lap of the cycling race.

Related Terms

  • synonym: secession - the action of formally withdrawing from an organization or alliance
  • synonym: split - an instance of dividing or breaking away, especially in organizations or groups
  • antonym: union - the action of joining together or being joined
