general

adjective
not specialized or limited; broad in scope
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɚəl /; / ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
including or involving all or most people, things, or cases; not specific, limited, or specialized; widespread or common

Examples

General interest
Her interests are general rather than focused on any one subject.
Most people have at least a general understanding of basic mathematics.
The company issued a general statement to address all inquiries at once.
Recent protests reflect a general sense of dissatisfaction in the community.
The general opinion was that the movie was disappointing.
There is a general understanding that office hours end at 5 p.m.
He gave a general overview of the company's strategy without going into details.
Her answer was very general and did not address any of the specific problems.
If you have any general questions about the application process, please ask now.
