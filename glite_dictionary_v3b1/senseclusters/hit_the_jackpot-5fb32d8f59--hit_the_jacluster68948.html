hit the jackpot

verb
have great success by luck
Pronunciation: / hɪt ðə ˈdʒækˌpɑt / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈdʒækˌpɒt /; / hɪt ðə ˈdʒækpɒt / (BrE)
to have great or unexpected success, especially as the result of luck

Examples

To hit the jackpot in business
After years of trying, she finally hit the jackpot with her bestselling novel.
If we get this new contract, our company will hit the jackpot.
When he found that rare coin at the garage sale, he really hit the jackpot.
After years of searching for the right job, she finally hit the jackpot with her new position.
He invested in a startup and really hit the jackpot when it became successful.
When the company launched its new product, they hit the jackpot in the market.
