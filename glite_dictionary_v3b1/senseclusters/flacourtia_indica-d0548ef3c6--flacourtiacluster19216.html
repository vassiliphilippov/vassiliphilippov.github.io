Flacourtia indica

noun
tropical shrub with acidic red fruits
Pronunciation: / fləˈkɔrti.ə ɪnˈdɪkə / (AmE), / fləˈkɔːti.ə ɪnˈdɪkə / (BrE)
a small shrubby tree native to Madagascar, cultivated in tropical regions as a hedge plant and for its deep red, acidic fruits resembling small plums

Examples

Flacourtia indica tree
Botanists studied the adaptability of Flacourtia indica in various soil types across Madagascar.
In India, Flacourtia indica is commonly planted as a living fence due to its thorny branches.
The fruits of the Flacourtia indica are often made into jams or preserves in many tropical countries.
