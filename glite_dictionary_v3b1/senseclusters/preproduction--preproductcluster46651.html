preproduction

noun
initial test manufacturing
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
a short initial manufacturing run or set of sample units made to test design, tooling, and assembly before full-scale production

Examples

Preproduction model testing
The company created a preproduction model to test the new smartphone's features before launching it.
Engineers discovered several design flaws during the preproduction phase, allowing them to make improvements.
A small preproduction batch was assembled to ensure the machines worked correctly before full-scale manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mass production - the manufacture of large quantities of standardized products
  • synonym: prototype - an early sample or model built to test a concept or process
