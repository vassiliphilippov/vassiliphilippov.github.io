bench

verb
remove a player from a game (sports)
Pronunciation: / bɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɛntʃ / (BrE)
to remove a player from a game or keep them out of the active lineup (in sports)

Examples

To bench a player
If you keep arguing with the referee, I'll have to bench you for the rest of the match.
She was benched for most of the second half due to her injury.
The coach decided to bench the striker after his third missed shot.
The coach decided to bench the star player after his poor performance in the first half.
She was benched for the final game due to a minor injury.
After missing several practices, he found himself benched for most of the season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: play - to participate actively in a game
  • synonym: substitute - replace a player on the field with another
