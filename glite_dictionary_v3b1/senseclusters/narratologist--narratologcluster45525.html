narratologist

noun
narrative scholar
Pronunciation: / ˌnærəˈtɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌnærəˈtɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies the structure and function of stories or narratives

Examples

The narratologist studies stories
The narratologist presented her research on the structure of modern novels at the conference.
As a narratologist, he analyzes how stories are constructed across different cultures.
Many narratologists focus on the ways narratives influence our understanding of history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: literary theorist - a person who studies theories about literature
  • antonym: storyteller - a person who tells or writes stories
