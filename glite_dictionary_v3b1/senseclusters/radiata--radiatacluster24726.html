radiata

noun
animals with radial symmetry
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈɑːtə / (BrE)
a group of animals characterized by radial symmetry, including jellyfish and similar organisms

Examples

Radiata animals group
Jellyfish and sea anemones are members of the radiata due to their body structure.
Scientists study the radiata to understand the evolution of animal body plans.
Unlike most animals, the radiata display symmetry around a central axis.
