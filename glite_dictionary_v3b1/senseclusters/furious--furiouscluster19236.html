furious

adjective
very violent or intense (of the elements)
Pronunciation: / ˈfjʊriəs / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəriəs / (BrE)
(of the weather or natural forces) extremely violent or intense, as if showing anger.

Examples

Furious storm
A furious storm rolled in suddenly, bringing heavy rain and lightning.
The furious winds battered the coastline for hours, uprooting trees and damaging houses.
The sailors struggled against the furious sea during the height of the tempest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling violence or agitation, especially of the weather
  • synonym: violent - using or accompanied by great physical force
