unearned income

noun
income received but not yet earned (accounting)
4.6 5.2
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈɜːrnd ˈɪn.kʌm/ (AmE), /ən.ˈɜːnd ˈɪn.kʌm/ (BrE)
(accounting) income that has been received but not yet earned, and is typically reported as a current liability on a company’s balance sheet.

Examples

Report unearned income
Accounting guidelines require firms to distinguish between unearned income and revenue already earned.
Interest collected in advance is classified as unearned income until it is earned over time.
The company reported a large amount of unearned income as a liability on its balance sheet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deferred income - income received but recognized in later periods
  • antonym: earned income - income that has been earned by providing goods or services
