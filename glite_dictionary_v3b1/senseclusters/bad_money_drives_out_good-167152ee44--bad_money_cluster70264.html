bad money drives out good

Pronunciation: /bæd ˈmʌn.ɪ draɪvz aʊt gʊd/ (AmE), /bæd ˈmʌn.ɪ draɪvz aʊt gʊd/ (BrE)
The principle that inferior or debased currency tends to circulate more widely than better-quality currency, causing the latter to disappear from circulation.

Examples

Bad money drives out good currency
If a government does not control the quality of its currency, bad money drives out good over time.
Many countries have learned through experience that bad money drives out good when they introduce debased coins.
The economist explained how bad money drives out good in situations where both circulations exist.
