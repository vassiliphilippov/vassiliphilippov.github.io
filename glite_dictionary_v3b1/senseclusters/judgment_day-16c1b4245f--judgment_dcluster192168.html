In Christian belief, the day at the end of the world when God will judge the actions of all people and determine their eternal destinies.

Examples

Judgment Day belief
Many people believe that on Judgment Day, everyone will be held accountable for their actions.
Stories about Judgment Day appear in literature and art throughout history.
The preacher warned his congregation to prepare for Judgment Day and live righteous lives.
Many believers await Judgement Day with hope and trepidation.
Stories and paintings often depict Judgement Day as a dramatic event.
The preacher spoke about the importance of preparing for Judgement Day.

Synonyms

  • Last Judgment
    9.3 0.37/m
    Last Judgment is the day when God officially judges every person and rewards or punishes them, while Judgment Day names that final event.
  • Day of Judgment
    8.6 0.48/m
    Day of Judgment appears in many religious texts as the moment God decides each soul's fate, while Judgment Day names that event.
  • Last Day
    8.7 1.26/m
    Last Day is a simple phrase many believers use for the end time when God judges people, while Judgment Day is the formal name.
  • crack of doom
    9.9 0.01/m
    crack of doom is an old, poetic phrase for the last divine judgment, while Judgment Day is the standard religious term used today.
  • eschaton
    9.9 0.07/m
    eschaton is the theological word for the whole end-time plan and ultimate destiny, whereas Judgment Day focuses on God's final judgment.
  • day of reckoning
    8.7 0.01/m
    day of reckoning often means any moment of final account or truth for people, while Judgment Day specifically means God's final judgment.
  • doomsday
    7.5 1.20/m
    doomsday often describes total destruction or the end of the world, whereas Judgment Day specifically names God's act of judging people.
