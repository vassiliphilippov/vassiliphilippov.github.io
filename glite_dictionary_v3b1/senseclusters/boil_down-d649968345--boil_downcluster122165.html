boil down

verb
reduce by boiling to concentrate
4.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /bɔɪl daʊn/ /; / /bɔɪ.əl daʊn/ (AmE), /bɔɪl daʊn/ /; / /bɔɪ.əl daʊn/ (BrE)
to cook a liquid until much of it has evaporated, making it thicker or more concentrated

Examples

To boil down a sauce
Simmer the sauce and let it boil down until it thickens.
The broth will boil down to a rich, flavorful base after several hours.
The fruit mixture needs to boil down before you make the jam.
Make sure the stock boils down to enhance its flavor before serving.
Simmer the mixture and let it boil down until slightly thickened.
The fruit was boiled down to make a rich, sweet syrup.
