bipinnate leaf

noun
leaf with doubly divided pinnate leaflets
9.2
Pronunciation: / baɪˌpɪneɪt lif / (AmE), / baɪˌpɪneɪt liːf / (BrE)
a leaf in which each primary leaflet is itself pinnately divided into smaller leaflets, as seen in some ferns and other plants

Examples

A bipinnate leaf structure
Each bipinnate leaf on the fern is divided into smaller leaflets, creating a feathery appearance.
Examining the bipinnate leaf under a microscope revealed two distinct levels of division.
The mimosa tree is known for its delicate bipinnate leaf structure.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: pinnate leaf - a leaf with leaflets arranged on either side of a central axis
