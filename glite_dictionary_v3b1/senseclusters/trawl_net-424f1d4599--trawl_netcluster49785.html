trawl net

noun
cone-shaped fishing net dragged in deep water
Pronunciation: / trɔl nɛt / (AmE), / trɔːl nɛt / (BrE)
a large, cone-shaped fishing net that is dragged along the sea bottom or through deep water to catch fish

Examples

A large trawl net
A massive trawl net brought up a variety of deep-sea creatures.
Modern trawl nets are designed to minimize environmental impact on the seabed.
The fishermen lowered the trawl net into the ocean to catch cod.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gill net - a net used for catching fish by their gills
  • synonym: trawl - a method of fishing by dragging a net through water
