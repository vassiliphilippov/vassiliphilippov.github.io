nonreproducible

adjective
cannot be copied
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌriːprəˈduːsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌriːprəˈdjuːsəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be copied, repeated, or duplicated

Examples

A nonreproducible result
The security watermark makes the certificate nonreproducible by standard photocopiers.
Some rare artworks are intentionally nonreproducible to prevent forgeries.
The scientist noted that the experiment's results were nonreproducible under the same conditions.
The scientist was concerned that the nonreproducible results might be due to a flaw in the experiment.
If a study is nonreproducible, its findings are less likely to be trusted by the scientific community.
The journal rejected the paper because the data were nonreproducible in follow-up tests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reproducible - able to be copied or duplicated accurately
  • synonym: unique - being the only one of its kind
