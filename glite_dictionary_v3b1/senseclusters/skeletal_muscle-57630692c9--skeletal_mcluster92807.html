skeletal muscle

noun
muscle attached to bones; striated muscle
4.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈskel.ɪ.təl ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /ˈskel.ə.təl ˈmʌs.əl/ (AmE), /ˈskel.ɪ.təl ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /ˈskel.ə.təl ˈmʌs.əl/ (BrE)
A muscle that is attached to bones and is responsible for moving parts of the skeleton, typically characterized by transverse stripes (striations).

Examples

Skeletal muscle contraction
Skeletal muscle contractions allow us to move our arms and legs easily.
Injuries to skeletal muscle may require physical therapy for full recovery.
Regular exercise strengthens the skeletal muscles throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cardiac muscle - specialized muscle tissue found only in the heart, also striated but not under voluntary control
  • antonym: smooth muscle - muscle tissue that is not under voluntary control and lacks striations, found in internal organs
