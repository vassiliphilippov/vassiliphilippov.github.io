narrowband

adjective
limited frequency range
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈnær.oʊ.bænd/ (AmE), /ˈnæ.rəʊ.bænd/ (BrE)
using or relating to a small range of frequencies for transmitting signals or data

Examples

Narrowband radio signal
The radio uses a narrowband signal to avoid interference from other channels.
Many older communication systems rely on narrowband technology for basic data transmission.
A narrowband connection is often slower than a broadband one but can be more reliable in remote areas.
