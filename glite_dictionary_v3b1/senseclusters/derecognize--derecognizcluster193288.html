derecognize

verb
withdraw official approval or acceptance
9.2 8.3
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈrek.əg.naɪz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈrek.əg.naɪz/ (AmE), /diː.ˈrek.əg.naɪz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈrek.əg.naɪz/ (BrE)
to withdraw official approval or recognition of someone, group, or entity, or to withdraw official acceptance of someone or something, especially in a formal, legal, or diplomatic context.

Examples

To derecognize a union
After policy changes, the committee officially derecognized the union as a bargaining representative.
Several countries threatened to derecognize the regime if it continued its human rights abuses.
The university decided to derecognize the student organization after repeated violations of the code of conduct.
The charity was derecognised by the council due to financial irregularities.
The university decided to derecognise the club after it violated several policies.
When a government derecognises a foreign state, diplomatic relations are usually suspended.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recognise - accept or approve officially
  • antonym: recognize - to acknowledge the existence or legality of something
  • synonym: withdraw - to remove or take away, especially officially
