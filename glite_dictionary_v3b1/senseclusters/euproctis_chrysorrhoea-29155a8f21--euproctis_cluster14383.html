Euproctis chrysorrhoea

noun
a European moth with a yellow tail tuft
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈprɑːktɪs ˌkraɪsəˈriːə / (AmE), / juːˈprɒktɪs ˌkrɪsəˈriːə / (BrE)
A white, furry-bodied European moth with a yellow tuft at the end of its tail, known as the brown-tail moth.

Examples

The Euproctis chrysorrhoea moth
Gardeners often try to control Euproctis chrysorrhoea to prevent damage to fruit trees.
The caterpillars of the Euproctis chrysorrhoea can cause skin irritation when touched.
The distinctive yellow tail tuft makes Euproctis chrysorrhoea easy to recognize among European moths.
