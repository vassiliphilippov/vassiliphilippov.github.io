radon

noun
a radioactive gaseous element (Rn)
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɒn / (BrE)
a radioactive gaseous chemical element (symbol Rn), the heaviest of the noble gases, formed by the decay of radium and occurring naturally, especially in areas with granite; inhalation is considered a health hazard

Examples

Radioactive radon gas
Granite-rich regions are more likely to have higher levels of radon in groundwater and soil.
Long-term exposure to radon can increase the risk of lung cancer in non-smokers.
Testing for radon in your home is important to reduce health risks from this radioactive gas.
