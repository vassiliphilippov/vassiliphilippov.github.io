oogonium

noun
egg precursor cell
Pronunciation: / oʊˈoʊɡoʊniəm / (AmE), / əʊˈəʊɡəʊniəm / (BrE)
a cell in the ovary that divides to form an egg cell during early development

Examples

Ovary oogonium
The oogonium divides several times before becoming a mature egg cell.
Scientists observed the development of the oogonium under a microscope.
Each oogonium has the potential to give rise to an oocyte during early development.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: oocyte - an immature egg cell in the ovary
  • antonym: spermatogonium - a cell in the testis that develops into a sperm cell
