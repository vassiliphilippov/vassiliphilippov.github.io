esophagoscopy

noun
examination of esophagus
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˌsɑfəˈɡɑskəpi / (AmE), / ɪˌsɒfəˈɡɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a flexible tube with a camera (an endoscope) is used to examine the inside of the esophagus

Examples

An esophagoscopy procedure
The doctor recommended an esophagoscopy to investigate the cause of her swallowing difficulties.
During the esophagoscopy, the specialist found signs of inflammation in the esophagus.
After the esophagoscopy, the patient was able to go home the same day.
The doctor performed an esophagoscopy to investigate the cause of the patient's swallowing difficulties.
After the esophagoscopy, the specialist discussed the findings with the patient and recommended further treatment.
A biopsy was taken during the esophagoscopy to check for any abnormal cells.
