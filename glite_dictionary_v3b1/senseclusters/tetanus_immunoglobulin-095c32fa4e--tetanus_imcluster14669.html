tetanus immunoglobulin

noun
antibody solution for tetanus protection
5.5 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈte.tə.nəs ɪ.mjuː.nə.ˈgɑːb.jə.lɪn/ (AmE), /ˈte.tə.nəs ɪ.mjuː.nə.ˈgɒb.jʊ.lɪn/ (BrE)
a sterile solution of antibodies obtained from people immunized against tetanus, used to provide temporary protection against the disease after possible exposure

Examples

tetanus immunoglobulin injection
Healthcare workers use tetanus immunoglobulin in emergency situations when vaccination status is uncertain.
Receiving tetanus immunoglobulin can provide immediate but short-term protection against developing tetanus.
The doctor administered tetanus immunoglobulin after the patient suffered a deep cut from rusty metal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tetanus antitoxin - an older term for a similar treatment; antibody preparation used to neutralize tetanus toxin
