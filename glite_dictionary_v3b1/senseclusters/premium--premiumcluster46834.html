premium

noun
extra amount paid above normal value
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːmiəm /; / ˈpriː.mi.əm / (AmE), / ˈpriːmiəm /; / ˈpriː.mi.əm / (BrE)
an amount paid or charged in excess of the usual or nominal value, especially due to scarcity, high demand, or as a special fee

Examples

Premium price
Because space downtown is limited, parking comes at a premium during rush hour.
Concert tickets for popular bands always sell at a premium on resale websites.
During the drought, farmers had to pay a premium for irrigation water.
Banks often charge a premium when exchanging large amounts of currency.
If you want to convert dollars to yen at the airport, expect to pay a significant premium.
Travelers try to avoid unnecessary premium fees by using local currency exchange offices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discount - an amount by which the regular price is reduced
  • synonym: fee - a payment made for a service
  • synonym: surcharge - an additional charge or payment above the regular price
