manifestation

noun
clear sign or indication of something
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmænəfəˈsteɪʃən /; / ˌmænɪfəˈsteɪʃən /; / ˌmæn.ə.fɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmænɪfɛˈsteɪʃən /; / ˌmanɪfəˈsteɪʃən /; / ˌmæn.ɪ.fɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an event, action, or sign that clearly shows or expresses a quality, feeling, idea, or disease

Examples

A clear manifestation
His anger found a sudden manifestation in his harsh words.
Rising seas are a clear manifestation of climate change.
The rash was the first manifestation of the illness.
Her sudden anger was a manifestation of years of suppressed frustration.
Symptoms are often the first manifestation of an underlying illness.
The rainbow was seen as a manifestation of hope after the storm.
Anger can find manifestation in subtle facial expressions.
Coughing is often a manifestation of an underlying medical issue.
The sudden outburst of laughter was a clear manifestation of her happiness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonstration - an action or display that shows something clearly
  • synonym: expression - the act of conveying thoughts or feelings
  • synonym: indication - a sign showing something exists or is likely to happen
  • synonym: sign - a thing that indicates the presence or existence of something else
  • antonym: suppression - the act of holding back or restraining an emotion or feeling
  • synonym: symptom - a feature regarded as indicating a condition or disease
