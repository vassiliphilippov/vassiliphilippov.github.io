liquidity crisis

noun
shortage of available cash in the economy
9.4
Pronunciation: / lɪˈkwɪdəti ˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈkwɪdɪti ˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
A situation in which there is a shortage of available cash or assets that can be quickly converted to cash, making it difficult for businesses and consumers to borrow or access funds, often resulting in high interest rates.

Examples

A liquidity crisis situation
A central bank may intervene in a liquidity crisis to stabilize financial markets and restore confidence.
After the financial crash, the country faced a severe liquidity crisis that hampered economic recovery.
Banks were reluctant to lend money during the liquidity crisis, worsening the situation for small businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credit crunch - a sudden reduction in the general availability of loans or credit
