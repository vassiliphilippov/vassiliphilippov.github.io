choke hold

noun
a restraining hold around the neck
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃoʊk hoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊk həʊld / (BrE)
A restraining hold in which one arm is placed tightly around another person's neck, typically from behind, to prevent their movement or cause unconsciousness.

Examples

A choke hold restraint
Police officers are no longer permitted to use a choke hold as a method of restraint.
She put him in a choke hold during their self-defense training session.
The wrestler managed to escape the choke hold just in time to avoid losing the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stranglehold - an extremely tight or restrictive grip around the neck
