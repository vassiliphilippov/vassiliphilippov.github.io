construct

verb
form by organizing parts (esp. ideas or arguments)
7.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to form or create something abstract, such as an idea, argument, or sentence, by combining different parts

Examples

To construct an argument
In his essay, he constructs a logical proof to support his claim.
She carefully constructed her argument before presenting it to the jury.
The scientist was able to construct a compelling theory from the available data.
Linguists study how children construct meaning through simple phrases.
The teacher asked each student to construct a short paragraph using the new vocabulary words.
When learning a new language, it can be challenging to construct a grammatically correct sentence.
Detectives must construct a possible sequence of actions from the clues left at the scene.
It took years for the historian to construct a timeline of the ancient civilization's decline.
She tried to construct the events of that night from scattered memories and old letters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: build - to make or form something by putting parts together; especially used for physical things, but also for abstract ideas
  • synonym: compose - to put together or create (especially in writing)
  • antonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it; opposite of create or construct
  • synonym: formulate - to express in a systematic or precise way
  • synonym: reconstruct - to assemble again in the mind, especially from evidence or memory
