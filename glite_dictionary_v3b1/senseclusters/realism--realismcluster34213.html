realism

noun
19th-century French artistic movement depicting reality
4.3 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈriːəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrɪə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
an artistic movement that emerged in 19th-century France, characterized by the depiction of subjects as they appear in everyday life, without embellishment or idealization.

Examples

Artistic realism
Gustave Courbet is often regarded as a leading figure in realism in French painting.
Many writers adopted realism to portray the struggles of ordinary people.
The shift to realism marked a significant break from the romantic idealism of previous art.

Related Terms

  • synonym: naturalism - a related artistic movement emphasizing detailed, lifelike depiction often influenced by science
