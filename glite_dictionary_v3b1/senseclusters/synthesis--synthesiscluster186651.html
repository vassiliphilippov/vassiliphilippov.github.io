synthesis

noun
reasoning from general to particular
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (BrE)
In logic and philosophy, reasoning from general principles to specific cases or from causes to effects.

Examples

Logical synthesis
Her argument relied on synthesis to derive concrete conclusions from abstract laws.
In scientific reasoning, synthesis allows researchers to predict outcomes based on established principles.
The philosopher explained the process of synthesis as moving from broad theories to individual examples.
