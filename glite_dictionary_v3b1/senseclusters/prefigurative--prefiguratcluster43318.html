prefigurative

adjective
vaguely prophetic; foreshadowing
Pronunciation: / priˈfɪɡjərətɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈfɪɡjʊrətɪv / (BrE)
Indistinctly prophetic; indicating or suggesting in advance what is to come, especially in a vague or symbolic way.

Examples

A prefigurative dream
Her dream was prefigurative, offering subtle clues about the events ahead.
Many scholars consider the painting to have a prefigurative quality, hinting at later historical upheavals.
The novel's early chapters are full of prefigurative symbolism that becomes clear only at the end.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prophetic - accurately predicting the future
  • synonym: symbolic - serving as a symbol, often suggestive
