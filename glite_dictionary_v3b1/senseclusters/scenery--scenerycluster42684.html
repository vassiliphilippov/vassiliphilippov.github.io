scenery

noun
painted stage backgrounds and structures
3.0 0.96/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀên…ôri / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀên…ôri / (BrE)
the painted backgrounds and structures used on a stage to represent a particular setting or location in a play or performance

Examples

Stage scenery
After every act, the stage crew changed the scenery to reflect the new setting.
The scenery for the play was so realistic that the audience felt transported to another world.
They designed the scenery to evoke a bustling city street in the 1920s.

Synonyms

  • scene
    3.7 2.87/m
    a scene is made of painted elements that show a place on stage, while scenery means the stage backgrounds and structures.
  • stage setting
    3.6 0.12/m
    a stage setting is the arrangement of scenery and props on stage, while scenery is the actual painted backgrounds and structures.
  • set
    6.0 17/m
    a set is the scenery and objects built for a performance or film, while scenery specifically names the painted backgrounds and structures.
  • setting
    3.0 1.90/m
    a setting is the place where the action happens or its onstage arrangement, while scenery refers to the painted backgrounds and stage structures.
  • mise en scene
    8.8 0.00/m
    mise en scene is the overall visual look of a film or play, while scenery means the painted backgrounds and stage structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: props - objects used on stage in a play or film, excluding scenery and costumes
  • synonym: set - the scenery and properties arranged for a performance of a play or film
