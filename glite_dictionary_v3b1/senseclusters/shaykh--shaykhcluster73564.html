shaykh

noun
Sufi spiritual leader
9.7
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ʃeɪk / (BrE)
a Sufi spiritual master or guide who leads and instructs disciples in religious practice and training

Examples

Sufi shaykh
The shaykh welcomed new students to the Sufi lodge for spiritual instruction.
Many people sought advice from the wise shaykh on matters of faith and daily life.
The shaykh led the group in meditation and prayer every evening.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disciple - a follower or student of a spiritual leader
  • synonym: guru - a spiritual teacher or guide, especially in Hinduism
