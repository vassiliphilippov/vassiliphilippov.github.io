suffocate someone's creativity

verb
stifle creative expression
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfəˌkeɪt ˈsʌmwʌnz kriˌeɪtɪˈvɪti / (AmE), / ˈsʌfəˌkeɪt ˈsʌmwʌnz kriːˈeɪtɪvɪti / (BrE)
to stifle or severely restrict a person's ability to express original ideas or be imaginative

Examples

To suffocate someone's creativity
A lack of artistic freedom can suffocate someone's creativity, making it hard for artists to express themselves.
Micromanaging employees may inadvertently suffocate someone's creativity and reduce workplace satisfaction.
Rigid rules in the classroom can suffocate someone's creativity and limit innovative thinking.
