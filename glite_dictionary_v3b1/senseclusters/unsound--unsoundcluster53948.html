unsound

adjective
not financially secure or reliable
7.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsaʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊnd / (BrE)
not financially secure or reliable; likely to lead to financial loss or instability.

Examples

Unsound investment
Investing in such an unsound enterprise is extremely risky.
Regulations were tightened after a series of unsound loans caused a market crisis.
The company collapsed due to its unsound financial practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sound - in good condition; financially secure
