rut

verb
to make a furrow or groove in a surface
Pronunciation: / rʌt / (AmE), / rʌt / (BrE)
to make a furrow or groove in a surface, especially by repeated passage or digging

Examples

To rut the road
Rainwater running down the hillside has rutted deep grooves into the earth.
Repeated footsteps rutted the narrow trail through the forest.
The heavy machinery rutted the dirt road after days of construction work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furrow - to make long narrow grooves or trenches in a surface
