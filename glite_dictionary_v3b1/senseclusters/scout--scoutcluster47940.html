scout

noun
talent recruiter in sports or entertainment
3.2 7/m
Pronunciation: / skaÊŠt / (AmE), / skaÊŠt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to search for or recruit talented people, or to watch for something and provide information (especially in sports, entertainment, or the military)

Examples

A talent scout
A scout offered him a contract after watching his performance at the tournament.
She worked as a scout for a major film studio, always searching for fresh talent.
The football team hired a new scout to find promising young players.
As a scout for the baseball team, her job was to watch young players in local leagues.
During the night, the scout remained alert to warn the others of approaching danger.
The army sent a scout ahead to gather information about enemy movements.

Synonyms

    3.7 0.4/m
    talent scout seeks performers or athletes at events and auditions, while scout can mean any recruiter or watcher in sports, military, or entertainment.
  • recruiter
    2.9 4/m
    recruiter finds and signs people for jobs or groups, while scout often focuses on spotting talent or watching and reporting information.
  • spotter
    4.4 0.9/m
    spotter watches closely to find targets, vehicles, or dangers and reports them, while scout also finds people or gathers wider information.
  • lookout
    3.7 3/m
    lookout stands watch to warn others about danger or arrival, while scout may search actively for talent or gather information over time.
