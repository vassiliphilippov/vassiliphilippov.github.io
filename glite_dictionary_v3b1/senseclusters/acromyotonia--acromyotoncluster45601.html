acromyotonia

noun
myotonia of the extremities
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.kroʊ.maɪ.əˈtoʊ.niː.ə/ /; / /æk.roʊ.maɪ.əˈtoʊ.niː.ə/ (AmE), /ə.krəʊ.maɪ.əˈtəʊ.niː.ə/ /; / /æk.rəʊ.maɪ.əˈtəʊ.niː.ə/ (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by myotonia (muscle stiffness) affecting only the extremities, resulting in spastic deformity of the hand or foot.

Examples

Acromyotonia of extremities
Spastic deformity of the foot is a common symptom in cases of acromyotonia.
The patient was diagnosed with acromyotonia after experiencing stiffness in her hands and feet.
Treatment for acromyotonia focuses on alleviating muscle stiffness in the extremities.
