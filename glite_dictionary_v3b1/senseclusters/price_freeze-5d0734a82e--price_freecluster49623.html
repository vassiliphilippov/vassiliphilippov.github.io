price freeze

noun
policy keeping prices at a fixed level
2.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /praɪs friːz/ (AmE), /praɪs friːz/ (BrE)
an official action or policy of keeping prices at their current level for a period of time, usually to control inflation

Examples

A price freeze policy
During the economic crisis, a price freeze helped many families afford basic necessities.
Supermarkets announced a price freeze on staple goods for the next three months.
The government imposed a price freeze on fuel to prevent sudden surges in costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: price control - government regulation of prices to control inflation or prevent increases
