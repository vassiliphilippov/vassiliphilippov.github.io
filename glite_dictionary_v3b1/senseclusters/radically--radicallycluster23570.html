radically

adverb
in a thorough or extreme way
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkli / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəli / (BrE)
In a way that is thorough, complete, or extreme; in a manner that fundamentally alters something.

Examples

To radically change
Her views on health care have become radically more progressive in recent years.
Scientists are thinking radically about how to solve the problem of climate change.
The new government radically changed the education policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fundamentally - in a basic and important way
  • antonym: moderately - to a moderate or limited extent
