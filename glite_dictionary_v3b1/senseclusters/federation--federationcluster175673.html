federation

noun
union of states or organizations
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of states, organizations, or parties that have joined together under a central authority while keeping some independence

Examples

A federation of states
Australia is a federation comprised of six states and several territories.
The European Union is not a true federation, but it has some federal elements.
The newly formed federation was designed to balance local and central authority.
After years of negotiation, the provinces agreed to create a federation to unify their policies.
Several trade unions formed a federation to better represent their members' interests.
The International Tennis Federation oversees major competitions and sets the rules for the sport.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confederation - an alliance of states with a weaker central authority
  • synonym: union - the action or fact of joining or being joined together
