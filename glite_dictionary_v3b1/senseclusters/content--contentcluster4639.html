content

verb
be satisfied with something (verb)
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
to make (yourself or someone) satisfied or pleased; to accept something and be content with it

Examples

To content yourself
After a long argument, they contented themselves with a compromise.
He could not afford to travel, so he contented himself with reading travel books.
She decided to content herself with a simple meal.
Nothing could content the restless child except a story before bed.
She tried to content herself with a brief response, though she wanted to say more.
The simple meal was enough to content him after a long day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissatisfy - to make someone feel unfulfilled or displeased
  • synonym: satisfy - to fulfill the desires, needs, or demands of
  • synonym: settle - to accept or agree to something less than desired
