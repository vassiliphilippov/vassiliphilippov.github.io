gemmiferous

adjective
bearing or reproducing by gemmae
Pronunciation: / dʒɛmˈɪfərəs / (AmE), / dʒɛmˈɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of a plant, fungus, etc.) bearing or reproducing by means of gemmae (small asexual reproductive bodies).

Examples

A gemmiferous plant
A gemmiferous fungus can spread quickly without the need for sexual reproduction.
Some liverworts are gemmiferous, producing gemmae that detach and develop into new plants.
The gemmiferous structures are easily visible under a microscope in this species of moss.
