some

determiner
an unspecified quantity or number
1.0
Pronunciation: / sʌm / (AmE), / sʌm / (BrE)
used to refer to an unspecified amount or number of something, especially when more than one or a little is intended

Examples

Some apples left
Some books were missing from the library shelf.
We bought some flowers to brighten up the room.
Would you like some tea with your breakfast?
Some people find it difficult to adapt to change.
After some years, he returned to his hometown.
We waited here for some weeks before we got any news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: any - used to refer to one or more of a thing, with a sense sometimes opposed to 'some' in questions and negatives
  • degree higher: many - a large number of people or things
  • degree lower: several - more than two but not many, a small number
