superorganism

noun
cooperative organism group
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌɔːrɡənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌɔːɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
a group of organisms, such as a colony of ants or bees, that functions as a single unit through cooperation

Examples

Ant superorganism
An ant colony is often considered a superorganism due to its cooperative behavior.
Scientists study the bee hive as a superorganism to understand collective intelligence.
The concept of a superorganism helps explain how some animal groups survive and thrive together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colony - a group of the same species living closely together
  • antonym: individual - a single organism, not part of a group acting as one
