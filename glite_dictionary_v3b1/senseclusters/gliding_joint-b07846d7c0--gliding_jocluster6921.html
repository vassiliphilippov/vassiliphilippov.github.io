gliding joint

noun
type of joint allowing sliding movement
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlaɪdɪŋ dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɡlaɪdɪŋ dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A joint between bones that allows only a sliding or gliding movement, such as those found between the small bones of the wrist and ankle.

Examples

A gliding joint movement
A gliding joint allows bones to move past one another without rotating or angling.
In anatomy class, we studied how a gliding joint differs from a hinge joint.
The carpals in the wrist are connected by gliding joints that permit a wide range of hand movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plane joint - type of synovial joint that allows only gliding movements
