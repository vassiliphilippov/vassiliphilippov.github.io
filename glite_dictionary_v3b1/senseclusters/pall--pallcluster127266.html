pall

noun
cloth covering for coffin or tomb
8.9 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / p…îl /; / p…ël / (AmE), / p…îÀêl / (BrE)
a heavy or dark cloth used as a covering, especially for a coffin, tomb, or as a curtain

Examples

Pall over the coffin
A black pall was draped over the coffin during the funeral service.
After the pall was removed, the family prepared for the final farewell.
The pall lent a somber tone to the ceremony as it covered the casket.
A richly embroidered pall hung in the center of the grand hall, softening the light.
The velvet pall blocked the sunlight from entering the study.
They drew the heavy pall across the window for privacy each evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drape - a piece of fabric hung to cover windows or enclose a space
