Inland Passage

noun
protected waterway from Seattle to Alaska
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌlænd ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪnlənd ˈpæsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a naturally protected waterway extending from Seattle to Skagway in southeastern Alaska, used mainly by ships and boats

Examples

The Inland Passage route
Cruise ships frequently sail the Inland Passage to enjoy the stunning views of southeastern Alaska.
The Inland Passage offers a safer route for vessels by protecting them from the rough open ocean.
Wildlife such as whales and eagles can often be seen along the Inland Passage.
