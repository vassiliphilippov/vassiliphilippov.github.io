creation science

noun
belief system supporting Genesis account with science
6.3
Pronunciation: / kriˈeɪʃən ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / kriˈeɪʃən ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
the attempt to provide scientific support for the account of creation described in the Book of Genesis, often in opposition to evolutionary theory

Examples

Creation science theory
Some schools debate whether to include creation science in their science curriculum.
Supporters of creation science argue it provides alternative explanations to evolution.
The court ruled that teaching creation science in public schools was unconstitutional.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creationism - the belief that the universe and life originated from specific acts of divine creation
  • antonym: evolutionary theory - the scientific theory explaining the origin of species by natural selection
