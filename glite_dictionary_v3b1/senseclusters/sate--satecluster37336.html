sate

verb
to satisfy fully
Pronunciation: / seɪt / (AmE), / seɪt / (BrE)
to satisfy (a desire or appetite) fully; to fill to the point of satisfaction

Examples

To sate hunger
A single glass of water was enough to sate my thirst.
Even after an hour of conversation, her curiosity was not completely sated.
The delicious meal sated his hunger after a long day of hiking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quench - to satisfy thirst, desire, or need
  • synonym: satiate - to satisfy fully or to excess
  • antonym: starve - to suffer or die from lack of food
