sate

verb
to satisfy fully
6.2 0.79/m
Pronunciation: / seÉªt / (AmE), / seÉªt / (BrE)
to satisfy (a desire or appetite) fully; to fill to the point of satisfaction

Examples

To sate hunger
A single glass of water was enough to sate my thirst.
Even after an hour of conversation, her curiosity was not completely sated.
The delicious meal sated his hunger after a long day of hiking.

Synonyms

  • satiate
    7.1 0.59/m
    satiate means to fill desire completely or even to excess, while sate means to fill someone until they no longer want more.
  • satisfy
    2.0 12/m
    satisfy makes someone pleased by meeting needs, while sate means to fill an appetite or desire completely.
  • slake
    9.0 0.18/m
    slake is used to quench thirst or desire, while sate means to fill appetite or desire fully and often to excess.

Related Terms
