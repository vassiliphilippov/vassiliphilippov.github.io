encumbered

adjective
weighed down; burdened
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌmbərd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmbəd / (BrE)
Loaded or weighed down, especially so as to be hindered in movement or action.

Examples

Encumbered with weight
His speech was so encumbered with technical jargon that few people could understand it.
She found herself encumbered with responsibilities she hadn’t anticipated.
The travelers were encumbered by bulky suitcases as they struggled through the narrow train aisle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burdened - carrying a heavy weight or load; weighed down
  • antonym: unencumbered - not burdened or impeded; free of encumbrance
