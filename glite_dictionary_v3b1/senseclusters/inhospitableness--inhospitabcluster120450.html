inhospitableness

noun
state of being inhospitable
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɑːspɪtəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɒspɪtəbəlnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being inhospitable, especially an environment or region lacking suitable conditions for life or growth.

Examples

Inhospitableness of land
Despite the inhospitableness of the mountainous region, some communities have managed to thrive.
Explorers were daunted by the inhospitableness of the Arctic landscape.
The inhospitableness of the desert makes it difficult for most plants and animals to survive there.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hospitality - the friendly reception and treatment of guests or strangers
  • synonym: inflexibility - the quality of being unwilling to change or adapt
