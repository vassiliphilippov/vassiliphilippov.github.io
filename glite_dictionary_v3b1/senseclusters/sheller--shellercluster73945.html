sheller

noun
one who or that which removes shells
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlə / (BrE)
a person or machine that removes the shells from something, such as peas or oysters

Examples

A sheller machine
Many farms employ a sheller during the busy season to help prepare fresh produce.
She worked as a sheller at the seafood factory, removing oysters from their shells all day.
The pea sheller made quick work of the vegetable harvest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shucker - person who removes shells from shellfish, especially oysters
