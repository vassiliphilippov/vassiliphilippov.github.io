pull through

verb
help survive or recover
Pronunciation: / pʊl θru / (AmE), / pʊl θruː / (BrE)
to survive or recover from a serious illness, difficulty, or dangerous situation (sometimes with help from others)

Examples

To pull through illness
Firefighters worked tirelessly to pull through those trapped in the collapsed building.
Thanks to her determination, she was able to pull through the most critical phase of her illness.
The doctors did everything they could to pull through the injured passengers after the accident.
Despite her serious injuries, she managed to pull through and return to work months later.
Doctors were unsure if he would pull through the night after the accident.
Their business was on the brink of bankruptcy, but somehow they pulled through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rescue - to free from harm, danger, or unpleasant situation
  • synonym: save - to rescue someone from danger or harm
  • antonym: succumb - fail to resist a negative force or die from it
  • synonym: survive - continue to live or exist, especially after a difficult event
