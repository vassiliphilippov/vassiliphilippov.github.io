bursting charge

noun
explosive to break open a shell or bomb
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrstɪŋ tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːstɪŋ tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a quantity of explosive material designed to explode so as to break open or disperse a shell, bomb, or similar device

Examples

The bursting charge explodes
Engineers checked the bursting charge before loading the mine onto the field.
The artillery shell contained a bursting charge that detonated on impact.
The primary function of the bursting charge is to break the shell apart and scatter its contents.
