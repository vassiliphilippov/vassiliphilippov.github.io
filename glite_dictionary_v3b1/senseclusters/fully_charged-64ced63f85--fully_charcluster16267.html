fully charged

adjective
charged to maximum capacity
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊli tʃɑrdʒd / (AmE), / ˈfʊli tʃɑːdʒd / (BrE)
(of a battery or device) charged with electricity to its maximum capacity; not needing further charging.

Examples

Fully charged battery
My electric scooter lasts all day when it's fully charged.
Please return the camera with its battery fully charged for the next user.
The phone was fully charged when I left the house this morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depleted - having little or no electric charge left
