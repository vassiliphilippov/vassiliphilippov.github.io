let alone

conjunction
used to emphasize a greater unlikelihood
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛt əˈloʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɛt əˈləʊn / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that something mentioned is even less likely or possible than something already stated.

Examples

Read, let alone write
He didn’t have time to read a book, let alone write one.
Most children can’t tie their shoes, let alone solve complex math problems.
The tiny boat can barely hold one person, let alone a group of adults.
