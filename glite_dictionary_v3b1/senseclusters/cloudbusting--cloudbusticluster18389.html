cloudbusting

verb
to disperse clouds
Pronunciation: / ˈklaʊdˌbʌstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdˌbʌstɪŋ / (BrE)
to attempt to break up or influence clouds, often to produce rain, using technical or scientific methods

Examples

To cloudbust the sky
Scientists were cloudbusting in hopes of ending the long drought in the region.
The team tried cloudbusting with new technology to increase rainfall for the crops.
After hours of cloudbusting, the sky finally opened and rain began to fall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloud seeding - the process of adding substances to clouds to encourage rain
