inchoate

adjective
not fully formed or developed
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkoʊ.ət / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊ.eɪt / (BrE)
just begun and not fully formed or developed; rudimentary.

Examples

An inchoate idea
His ideas were still inchoate and needed further development before presentation.
The project's structure remained inchoate despite long hours of planning.
They were alarmed by the inchoate threats circulating online.

Related Terms

  • antonym: developed - having grown to a more advanced or complete state
  • synonym: incipient - just beginning to appear or develop
