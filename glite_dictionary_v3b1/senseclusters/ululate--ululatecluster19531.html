ululate

verb
emit long, loud cries
Pronunciation: / ˈjuː.ljəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌl.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to emit long, loud, wavering cries, especially as an expression of strong emotion such as grief or joy

Examples

To ululate loudly
Mourners ululated during the funeral, filling the air with piercing, sorrowful cries.
She could hear wolves ululating in the distance on the dark mountain night.
The crowd began to ululate in joy as the team scored the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wail - to make a long, loud cry of pain or sadness
