critique

noun
critical evaluation essay or article
5.6 15/m
Pronunciation: / kr…™Ààtik / (AmE), / kr…™ÀàtiÀêk / (BrE)
a detailed analysis and evaluation of something, especially a work of art, literature, or theory (often in writing or speech)

Examples

A critique of the book
After reading the critique, the playwright made changes to the script.
She wrote a thoughtful critique of the latest novel.
The film received a harsh critique in the newspaper.
After reading his film critique, I decided not to watch the movie.
Her critique of the proposal highlighted several important weaknesses.
The editor wrote a thorough critique of the new novel for the magazine.

Synonyms

  • critical analysis
    5.1 0.90/m
    critical analysis examines a work in detail to judge its merits, while critique names a written evaluation often used for art or theory.
  • criticism
    3.7 8.40/m
    criticism is a written or spoken evaluation that judges a creative work, while critique is a detailed analysis often found in essays or talks.
  • critical review
    4.1 0.12/m
    critical review is an article offering evaluative analysis of a book or play, while critique is a broader detailed evaluation of work or theory.
  • review
    3.0 39/m
    review is a public article that gives an opinion about a film or book, while critique is a more detailed analysis and judgment.
  • critical appraisal
    6.3 0.14/m
    critical appraisal is a careful, often formal evaluation used to judge value or quality, while critique focuses on detailed analysis of works.
  • criticism
    3.0 8.59/m
    criticism is the act of analyzing and judging merits and faults, while critique is usually a written detailed evaluation of a work or theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: review - an assessment or appraisal of a work, often less thorough than a critique
