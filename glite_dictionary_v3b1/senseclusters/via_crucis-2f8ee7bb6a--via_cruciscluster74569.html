via crucis

noun
Christian devotional ritual
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌviːə ˈkruːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːə ˈkruːsɪs / (BrE)
a Christian devotional ceremony in which participants move from station to station, reflecting on the Passion of Christ

Examples

To walk the via crucis
On Good Friday, the congregation walked the via crucis together in silent prayer.
Many churches display artwork to mark each station of the via crucis during Lent.
Pilgrims traveled to Jerusalem to experience the original via crucis route.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stations of the cross - a series of images or stations representing Jesus's path to crucifixion
