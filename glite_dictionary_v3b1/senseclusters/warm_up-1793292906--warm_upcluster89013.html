warm up

verb
do gentle exercises before physical activity
1.0 1.9
Pronunciation: /wɔːrm ʌp/ /; / /wɑːrm ʌp/ (AmE), /wɔːm ʌp/ (BrE)
to do gentle exercises or movements to prepare the body for more intense physical activity

Examples

To warm up before exercise
She always warm up her muscles to prevent injury during her workouts.
The coach asked everyone to warm up with a light jog before practice.
The team warmed up before heading out onto the field.
It's important to warm up before going for a run to prevent injury.
She always spends ten minutes warming up with some light stretches.
The coach reminded the team to warm up thoroughly before the match.
Athletes always warm up before a big race to reduce the risk of injury.
It’s important to warm up your muscles before starting your workout.
She sings a few scales to warm up her voice before the performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool down - to perform gentle exercises after strenuous activity to help the body recover
