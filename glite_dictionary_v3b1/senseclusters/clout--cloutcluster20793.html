clout

verb
to influence strongly
Pronunciation: / klaʊt / (AmE), / klaʊt / (BrE)
to have or use strong influence or power to affect a situation or decision

Examples

To clout a decision
She managed to clout the committee's decision with her persuasive arguments.
The celebrity's endorsement clouted the outcome of the campaign significantly.
He hopes to clout the negotiations in favor of his company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: control - to have power over something or someone
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to or have no effect on
  • synonym: sway - to influence or control someone or something
