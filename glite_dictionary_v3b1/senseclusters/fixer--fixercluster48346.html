fixer

noun
person who arranges illicit deals
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪksər / (AmE), / ˈfɪksə / (BrE)
a person who arranges or solves problems for others, often using influence or working behind the scenes (sometimes in an underhand or illegal way)

Examples

Political fixer
A seasoned fixer can make legal troubles disappear for the right price.
The fixer promised to get the permits approved, but his fee was suspiciously high.
The journalist exposed a notorious fixer who manipulated city officials.
The company hired a fixer to handle the public relations crisis.
Whenever there is a problem, she acts as the team's fixer and finds a solution.
He became known as a reliable fixer who could resolve even the most difficult issues.
Whenever a problem arises in the company, the fixer is called in to handle it discreetly.
She built her reputation as a reliable fixer who could resolve even the toughest challenges.
The politician relied on his trusted fixer to manage sensitive negotiations behind the scenes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broker - person who arranges transactions between others
  • synonym: mediator - a person who helps others resolve disputes
  • synonym: problem solver - someone who finds solutions to difficult situations
  • antonym: troublemaker - a person who causes problems or difficulties
  • synonym: troubleshooter - a person skilled at solving problems or fixing issues
