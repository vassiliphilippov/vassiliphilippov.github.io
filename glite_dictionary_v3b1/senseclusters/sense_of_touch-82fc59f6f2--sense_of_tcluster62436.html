sense of touch

noun
ability to feel through physical contact
1.3
Pronunciation: / sɛns əv tʌtʃ / (AmE), / sɛns əv tʌtʃ / (BrE)
the faculty that allows a person or animal to perceive objects or forces through physical contact, especially with the skin or hands

Examples

The sense of touch in skin
Some people lose their sense of touch in their fingertips due to nerve damage.
The sense of touch is essential for recognizing textures and temperatures.
The baby's developing sense of touch helps it explore the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sense of sight - the faculty of seeing; vision
  • synonym: touch - the ability to perceive things by physical contact, especially with the skin
