fullness

noun
rich and pleasing quality in sensation
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rich, deep, or pleasing in sound, flavor, appearance, or feeling

Examples

Fullness of flavor
She admired the fullness of the wine's flavor.
The fullness of the colors in the sunset left everyone speechless.
The cello's tone had a surprising fullness that captivated the audience.
The fullness of her voice filled the concert hall with warmth.
The orchestra played with a new fullness that captivated the entire audience.
We admired the fullness of the fruit, which seemed ready to burst.

  • antonym: thinness - the quality of lacking richness or density
