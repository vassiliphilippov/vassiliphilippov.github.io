driftnet

noun
floating fishing net
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪftˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftˌnɛt / (BrE)
a large fishing net that floats freely in the water and catches fish as it drifts with the current

Examples

A driftnet fishing
Many countries have banned the use of driftnets to protect marine ecosystems.
A driftnet can stretch for kilometers and catch large numbers of fish at once.
Environmental groups campaign against driftnet fishing due to its impact on dolphins and turtles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gillnet - a fishing net that catches fish by their gills
  • antonym: trawl - a large net dragged along the sea bottom to catch fish
