recessional march

noun
music for exit processions
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛʃənl mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃənəl mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a piece of music played during the exit procession at a formal ceremony or service

Examples

The recessional march plays
The recessional march echoed through the hall as guests filed out in an orderly procession.
The organist played a stirring recessional march as the wedding party left the church.
Traditionally, a recessional march signals the end of the graduation ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: processional march - music played during an entrance procession
