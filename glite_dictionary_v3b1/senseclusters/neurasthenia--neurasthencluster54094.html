neurasthenia

noun
historical term for nervous exhaustion
9.6
Pronunciation: / nʊr.əsˈθi.ni.ə / (AmE), / njʊə.rəsˈθiː.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by physical and mental exhaustion, once believed to result from exhaustion of the nervous system; no longer used in modern medicine.

Examples

Neurasthenia symptoms
Although neurasthenia is no longer a recognized diagnosis, the term appears in historical literature.
In the 19th century, neurasthenia was diagnosed in patients suffering from unexplained fatigue and anxiety.
Many writers and artists of the era were said to suffer from neurasthenia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nervous breakdown - a sudden collapse in a person's mental health
