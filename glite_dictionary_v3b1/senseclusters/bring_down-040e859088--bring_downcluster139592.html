bring down

verb
reduce; make less
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the level, rate, or amount of something

Examples

To bring down costs
Our new strategy should bring down operating expenses significantly next year.
The government is trying to bring down inflation to help consumers.
They hope to bring down the cost of living by introducing new policies.
