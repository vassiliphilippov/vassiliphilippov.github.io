contrary

adjective
resistant to guidance or discipline
8.4 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.trə.riː/ /; / /kən.ˈtrə.riː/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.trə.riː/ /; / /ˈkɒn.triː/ (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) deliberately uncooperative or resistant to guidance or discipline.

Examples

Contrary child
His contrary attitude made teamwork very challenging for everyone involved.
She was in a contrary mood and refused to listen to any advice.
The child grew increasingly contrary after being told to tidy up the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliant - willing to comply, submissive
  • synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • synonym: wayward - difficult to control or predict because of unusual or perverse behavior
