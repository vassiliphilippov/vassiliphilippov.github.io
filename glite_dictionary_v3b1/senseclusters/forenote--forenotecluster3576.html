forenote

noun
introductory note
8.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îÀêrÀåno ät / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀên…ô ät / (BrE)
a note or short message placed at the beginning of a book or document, often to provide context or explanation

Examples

A forenote in book
The editor added a forenote to clarify the historical background of the text.
Before the first chapter, readers will find a forenote from the translator.
A brief forenote introduces the main themes discussed in the report.

Synonyms

  • preface
    4.4 2.87/m
    a preface is an introduction that the author writes to explain a book's purpose and background, while a forenote is any short opening note.
  • foreword
    4.7 1.44/m
    a foreword is a short introduction usually written by someone else, while a forenote is a general opening note giving context.

Related Terms

  • antonym: afterword - a concluding section at the end of a book
  • synonym: preface - an introduction to a book, typically stating its subject and scope
