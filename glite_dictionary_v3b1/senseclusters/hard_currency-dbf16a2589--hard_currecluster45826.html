hard currency

noun
stable, widely accepted currency
Pronunciation: / hɑrd ˈkɜrənsi / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
A currency that is widely accepted around the world, stable in value, and unlikely to depreciate suddenly.

Examples

International hard currency
After the political crisis, the value of their local money plummeted, and hard currency became essential for imports.
Many investors prefer to keep their assets in hard currency during times of economic instability.
The contract required all payments to be made in hard currency to avoid exchange rate risks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft currency - a currency that is likely to depreciate or is not widely accepted in international trade
