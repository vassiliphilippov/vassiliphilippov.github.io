milliampere

noun
unit of electric current (1/1000 ampere)
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪliˈæmpɪr / (AmE), / ˌmɪliˈæmpɪə / (BrE)
A unit of electric current equal to one thousandth of an ampere.

Examples

One milliampere
Many small electronic devices use less than a hundred milliamperes of current.
The current flowing through the circuit was measured at 10 milliamperes.
The sensors require only a few milliamperes to operate efficiently.
