Crataegus aestivalis

noun
species of hawthorn with edible scarlet fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌɛs.tɪˈveɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌiː.stɪˈveɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a species of hawthorn native to the southern United States, bearing juicy, acidic, scarlet fruit commonly used in jellies and preserves

Examples

Crataegus aestivalis berries
Many people collect the ripe, scarlet berries of Crataegus aestivalis to use in traditional jellies.
Native to the southern United States, Crataegus aestivalis thrives in wetlands and moist woodlands.
The fruit of Crataegus aestivalis is often made into delicious homemade jams.
