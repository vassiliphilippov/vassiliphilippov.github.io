rondo

noun
musical form with recurring main theme
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑn.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.dəʊ / (BrE)
a musical composition, often the final movement of a sonata, in which a principal theme alternates with contrasting themes

Examples

A rondo movement
In her piano recital, she chose to perform a well-known rondo by Beethoven.
Many composers used the rondo form to create dynamic and memorable conclusions.
The final movement of the symphony is written as a lively rondo.
