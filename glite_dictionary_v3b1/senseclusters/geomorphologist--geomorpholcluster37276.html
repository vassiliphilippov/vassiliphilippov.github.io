geomorphologist

noun
landform scientist
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːoʊˌmɔːrˈfɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˌmɔːˈfɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies how the Earth's surface and landforms are formed and change over time

Examples

A geomorphologist studies landforms
The geomorphologist analyzed river valleys to determine how they were shaped over time.
A team led by a geomorphologist discovered new evidence of ancient glaciers on Mars.
As a geomorphologist, she often travels to remote areas to study unusual landforms.
The geomorphologist presented her findings on how glaciers carve out valleys.
A team led by a geomorphologist mapped the changes in the river delta after the flood.
As a geomorphologist, he often travels to remote areas to study landforms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geologist - a scientist who studies the Earth's physical structure and substances
  • synonym: geomorphology - the scientific study of the Earth's surface features and their formation
  • synonym: planetary scientist - a scientist who studies planets, moons, and planetary systems
