heavy going

adjective
difficult or slow to progress through
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvi ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
difficult or slow to make progress with or through; requiring much effort or perseverance.

Examples

A heavy going path
After the storm, hiking up the muddy hill was really heavy going.
That textbook was heavy going, but I managed to finish it after several weeks.
The legal document is heavy going for anyone unfamiliar with technical language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arduous - requiring great effort; very difficult
  • antonym: easy going - not difficult; relaxed and uncomplicated
