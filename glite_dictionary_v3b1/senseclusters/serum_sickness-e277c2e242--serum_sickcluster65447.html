serum sickness

noun
delayed allergic reaction to injected serum
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəm ˌsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəm ˌsɪknəs / (BrE)
a delayed immune reaction resulting from the injection of foreign serum, characterized by symptoms such as fever, rash, and joint pain

Examples

Serum sickness symptoms
Doctors monitor for serum sickness in patients treated with animal-derived serums.
Symptoms of serum sickness include rash, fever, and swollen joints.
The patient developed serum sickness a week after receiving the antitoxin injection.
