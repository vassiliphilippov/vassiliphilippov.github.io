Khmer Rouge

noun
Cambodian communist group (1975–1999)
9.8
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmer ruːʒ/ /; / /kə.ˈmeər ruːʒ/ (AmE), /kə.ˈmeə ruːʒ/ /; / /kə.ˈmɪə ruːʒ/ (BrE)
a communist organization that ruled Cambodia from 1975 to 1979, responsible for the deaths of millions during its regime, and remained active as a guerrilla movement until 1999

Examples

The Khmer Rouge regime
During the reign of the Khmer Rouge, many Cambodians were forced to leave their homes and work in labor camps.
Historians continue to study the atrocities committed by the Khmer Rouge regime.
The Khmer Rouge took control of Cambodia in 1975, beginning a period of intense violence.
