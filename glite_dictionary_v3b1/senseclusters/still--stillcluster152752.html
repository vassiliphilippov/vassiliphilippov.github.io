still

adverb
to a greater extent (with comparatives)
1.0
Pronunciation: / stɪl / (AmE), / stɪl / (BrE)
even more; used to emphasize a greater degree or extent, especially with comparatives.

Examples

Still more difficult
Despite rising costs, finding affordable housing has become still more challenging in recent years.
She was upset and grew still more frustrated as she learned what happened.
The hike was difficult, but the final climb was still harder than anything before.

Related Terms

  • synonym: even - used to emphasize a greater degree in comparison
  • synonym: yet - used to intensify a comparative
