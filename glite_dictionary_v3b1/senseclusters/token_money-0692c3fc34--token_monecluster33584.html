token money

noun
currency with face value above material worth
7.3 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtoʊ.kən ˈmʌn.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈtəʊ.kən ˈmʌn.ɪ/ (BrE)
Coins or currency whose face value is greater than the value of the metal or material from which it is made.

Examples

Token money coins
Collectors often distinguish between token money and full-value coins made from precious metals.
Modern coins are usually token money, as their metal content is worth less than their face value.
The use of token money allows governments to produce coins at a low cost.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiat money - Currency that is declared legal tender by a government but has no intrinsic value
