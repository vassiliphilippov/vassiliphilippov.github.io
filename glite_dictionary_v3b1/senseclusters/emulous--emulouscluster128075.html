emulous

adjective
eager to equal or surpass others
8.2 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɛm.jə.ləs/ /; / /ˈɛm.jʊ.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈɛm.jʊ.ləs/ /; / /ˈɛm.jə.ləs/ (BrE)
eager or ambitious to equal, surpass, or imitate others, especially out of rivalry

Examples

An emulous spirit
He admired the emulous spirit of athletes who always strive to break records.
Her emulous nature drove her to outperform her colleagues at every opportunity.
The emulous students vied with each other for the top prize.
An emulous spirit drove the young athlete to outdo his peers in every competition.
In the emulous atmosphere of the startup scene, companies constantly seek to outshine one another.
She was emulous of her older sister's academic achievements, striving to match her success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complacent - self-satisfied and unconcerned with competing
  • synonym: imitative - copying or following something or someone
  • synonym: rivalrous - characterized by rivalry; competitive
