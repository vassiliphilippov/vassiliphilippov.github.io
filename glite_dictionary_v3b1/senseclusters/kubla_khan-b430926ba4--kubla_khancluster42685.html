Kubla Khan

proper noun
Mongolian emperor of China, founder of Yuan dynasty
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkuːblə ˈkɑn / (AmE), / ˈkuːblə ˈkɑːn / (BrE)
the Mongolian emperor of China (1215–1294), grandson of Genghis Khan, who completed the conquest of China, established the Yuan dynasty, and built his capital at the site of modern Beijing

Examples

Kubla Khan emperor
Kubla Khan played a crucial role in connecting East and West by inviting Marco Polo to his court.
Modern Beijing stands on the site where Kubla Khan built his magnificent capital.
The poem by Samuel Taylor Coleridge, titled Kubla Khan, was inspired by the emperor's legendary palace.
