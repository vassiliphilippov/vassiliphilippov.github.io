Francophone

adjective
French-speaking
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfræŋkəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkəfəʊn / (BrE)
using French as a language or relating to people or regions where French is spoken.

Examples

Francophone country
Canada has many Francophone communities, especially in the province of Quebec.
The conference provided translation services for both Francophone and Anglophone participants.
Several African countries are officially Francophone due to their colonial history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Anglophone - using English as a language or relating to English-speaking people
  • synonym: French-speaking - able to speak French or using French as a language
