cod

noun
a large edible marine fish
1.0 4/m
Pronunciation: / kÉ‘d / (AmE), / kÉ’d / (BrE)
a large marine fish with a spotted body, found in cold or temperate waters, valued as a food source and for its white, flaky flesh

Examples

Atlantic cod fish
Fried cod is a popular dish in many coastal towns.
She bought fresh cod at the fish market for dinner.
The fisherman was proud of the huge cod he caught early that morning.

Synonyms

  • codfish
    1.1 0.1/m
    codfish is the common market name for the edible, white-fleshed fish, while cod is the broader everyday word for the species.
  • Atlantic cod
    2.1 0.1/m
    Atlantic cod is the specific North Atlantic species often caught for food, while cod is the general name for similar cold-water fish.
  • Gadus morhua
    8.1 0.02/m
    Gadus morhua is the scientific name used by scientists and reports, while cod is the everyday name people use for the same fish.
