financial year

noun
twelve-month accounting period
1.0
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃəl jɪr / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl jɪə / (BrE)
a period of twelve months used by governments and businesses for accounting and tax purposes, which may not always coincide with the calendar year

Examples

The financial year ends
Different countries may define their financial year to start in different months.
Tax returns must be filed within six months after the close of each financial year.
The company's financial year ends in September, rather than December.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiscal year - an alternative term for financial year, especially in US English
