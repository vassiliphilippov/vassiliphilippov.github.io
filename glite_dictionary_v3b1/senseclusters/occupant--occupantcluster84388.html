occupant

noun
person living in or using a place
3.3 6.27/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêkj…ôp…ônt / (AmE), / Àà…íkj äp…ônt / (BrE)
a person who lives in or uses a particular house, room, seat, or place for a period of time

Examples

The occupant of a seat
Every seat had an occupant by the time the ceremony began.
Only one occupant was found in the building during the fire.
The new occupant of the office introduced herself to the team.

Synonyms

  • inhabitant
    2.8 14/m
    inhabitant is someone who lives in a place as part of its community, while occupant can also mean a short-term user of that place.
  • occupier
    4.1 0.26/m
    occupier uses or lives in a place, often for a long or legal reason, while occupant can be temporary or casual.
  • resident
    1.8 78/m
    resident lives in a place for a long time or permanently, while occupant may be short-term or only using the space.
  • dweller
    3.2 5.31/m
    dweller lives in or at a place and often feels tied to that spot, while occupant simply means someone who uses it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: owner - a person who legally possesses a property
  • synonym: resident - a person who lives somewhere permanently or for a long time
  • synonym: tenant - a person who rents and occupies property
