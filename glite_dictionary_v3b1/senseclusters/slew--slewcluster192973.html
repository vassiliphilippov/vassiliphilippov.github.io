slew

verb
slide or turn sideways, uncontrollably
7.6 1.18/m
Pronunciation: / sluÀê / (AmE), / sluÀê / (BrE)
to slide or turn sideways suddenly, often in an uncontrolled way (especially of a vehicle or object)

Examples

The car slewed sideways
She lost control and the bicycle slewed sideways into the hedge.
The car began to slew dangerously on the icy road.
The truck slewed across the highway to avoid the deer.
The car slewed across the icy road and hit the barrier.
The truck's back end slewed out on the wet pavement.
Without warning, the boat slewed to the right as the wind picked up.
She slued the chair around to face the window.
The car began to slue sideways on the icy road.
The ship's bow suddenly slued to starboard during the storm.

Synonyms

  • skid
    3.5 0.54/m
    skid is sliding suddenly and out of control, often on a wet road, while slew is a sudden sideways turn or slide.
  • swerve
    2.8 4.17/m
    swerve is a sudden turn, often to avoid something in the road, while slew is a sudden sideways turn or slide.
  • veer
    4.2 5.27/m
    veer is to change direction sharply, sometimes by steering, while slew implies a quick sideways slide or uncontrolled turn.
  • sheer
    7.8 0.05/m
    sheer is to swerve suddenly, often to avoid something, while slew suggests a sudden sideways slide or turn, usually uncontrolled.
  • slide
    1.0 56/m
    slide is to move smoothly across a surface, often slowly and controlled, while slew is a sudden sideways turn or uncontrolled slide.

Related Terms
