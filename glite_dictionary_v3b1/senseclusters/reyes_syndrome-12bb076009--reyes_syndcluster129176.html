Reye's syndrome

noun
rare childhood disease causing brain and liver problems
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈraɪz ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a rare but serious condition affecting children, characterized by sudden brain swelling and liver dysfunction, usually following a viral infection

Examples

Reye's syndrome in children
Aspirin is not recommended for children due to the risk of developing Reye's syndrome.
Doctors immediately considered Reye's syndrome when the child presented with vomiting and confusion after a bout of chickenpox.
The hospital launched an awareness campaign to educate parents about the dangers of Reye's syndrome.
