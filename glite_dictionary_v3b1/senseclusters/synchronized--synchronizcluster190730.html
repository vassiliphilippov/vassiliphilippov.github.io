synchronized

adjective
operating in unison; simultaneous
3.6 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪzd/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪzd/ (BrE)
happening, existing, or operating at the same time or in unison; made to occur together precisely

Examples

Synchronized swimming routine
The synchronized flashing of the fireflies lit up the night.
The athletes performed a synchronized dive into the pool.
Their actions were so synchronized that it seemed rehearsed.
Our watches were synchronised before the expedition began.
The dancers' synchronised movements impressed the judges.
Traffic lights are often synchronised to improve traffic flow in busy cities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coordinated - organized so that parts work together smoothly
  • synonym: simultaneous - happening or done at the same time
  • antonym: unsynchronised - not operating in unison; not coordinated in time
  • antonym: unsynchronized - not made to occur at the same time; not synchronized
