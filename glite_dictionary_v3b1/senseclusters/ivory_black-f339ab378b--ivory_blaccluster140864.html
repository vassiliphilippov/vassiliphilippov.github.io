ivory black

noun
a black pigment made from burnt ivory
1.7 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.və.riː blæk/ /; / /ˈaɪ.v.riː blæk/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.və.riː blæk/ /; / /ˈaɪ.v.riː blæk/ (BrE)
A deep black pigment formerly made by grinding burned ivory and mixing it with oil, often used in painting.

Examples

Ivory black pigment
Before synthetic pigments, ivory black was highly valued by painters of the Renaissance.
Some conservationists discourage the use of ivory black because it historically relied on elephant ivory.
The artist preferred using ivory black for its rich, deep tone in her oil paintings.
