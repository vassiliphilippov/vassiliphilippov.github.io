cohesive

adjective
forming a united whole; sticking together
6.3 4.23/m
Pronunciation: / ko äÀàhiÀês…™v / (AmE), / k…ô äÀàhiÀês…™v / (BrE)
forming a united whole; causing elements to stick or work together effectively

Examples

A cohesive argument
A cohesive argument is well-structured and logically connected.
The group became more cohesive after they spent a weekend working together on the project.
The team was praised for its cohesive approach to solving problems.
Effective communication helped make the community more cohesive.
Her argument was clear, logical, and highly cohesive.
The new team worked together as a truly cohesive unit.

Synonyms

  • coherent
    7.2 0.41/m
    coherent often describes parts that stick or fit together clearly, while cohesive describes parts that actively hold together and work.
  • united
    2.2 132/m
    united means people join and act together in agreement, while cohesive describes groups that naturally stick close and cooperate.
  • unified
    4.1 13/m
    unified is formed into a single whole, often by design, while cohesive describes parts that naturally hold together and work.
