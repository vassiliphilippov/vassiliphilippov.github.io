Pope Paul III

proper noun
pope who led Catholic reforms, 1534–1549
Pronunciation: / poʊp pɔl ðə θɜrd / (AmE), / pəʊp pɔːl ðiː θɜːd / (BrE)
(1468–1549) Italian pope from 1534 to 1549, known for excommunicating Henry VIII, initiating the Council of Trent, and promoting the Society of Jesus during the Counter Reformation.

Examples

Pope Paul III excommunicated king
Pope Paul III excommunicated Henry VIII in 1538, deepening the split between England and the Catholic Church.
As an influential leader, Pope Paul III promoted the Society of Jesus to support Catholic renewal.
The reforms initiated by Pope Paul III at the Council of Trent shaped the future of Roman Catholicism.
