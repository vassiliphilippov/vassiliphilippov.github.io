ossicle

noun
small bone, especially in the middle ear
9.6 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀês…™k…ôl / (AmE), / Àà…ís…™k…ôl / (BrE)
a very small bone, especially one of the three bones in the middle ear (the malleus, incus, and stapes)

Examples

Middle ear ossicle
Damage to an ossicle can result in hearing loss.
The surgeon carefully examined the ossicle for signs of damage.
The three ossicles of the middle ear transmit sound vibrations to the inner ear.

Synonyms

  • auditory ossicle
    7.7 0.01/m
    auditory ossicle names each of the three ear bones that carry sound, while ossicle is the general small-bone term.
  • ossiculum
    9.9 0.00/m
    ossiculum is a Latin medical term for a tiny bone, while ossicle is the common anatomical name used in general texts.
  • bonelet
    9.3 0.00/m
    bonelet is a rare or informal name for a tiny bone, while ossicle is the standard anatomical word.
