Sapphic

noun
a type of poetic meter by Sappho
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæfɪk / (AmE), / ˈsæfɪk / (BrE)
(in poetry) a line or stanza composed in the meter invented or used by the ancient Greek poet Sappho, typically with a specific arrangement of syllables and stresses.

Examples

A Sapphic stanza
Many ancient poets attempted to emulate the Sapphic in their own verses.
She wrote a poem in the Sapphic meter as a tribute to Sappho.
The structure of the Sapphic stanza is complex and requires careful attention to metrical detail.
