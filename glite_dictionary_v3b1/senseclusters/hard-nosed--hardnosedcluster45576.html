hard-nosed

adjective
practical and tough-minded
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdˌnoʊzd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌnəʊzd /; / ˈhɑːd.nəʊzd / (BrE)
having a practical, tough, and unsentimental attitude; not easily influenced by emotions

Examples

A hard-nosed attitude
Her hard-nosed negotiating style earned her respect among her colleagues.
Reporters admired his hard-nosed approach to investigating political corruption.
The company hired a hard-nosed manager to improve profits and cut unnecessary expenses.
Despite his hard-nosed reputation, he cared deeply for his team.
Her hard-nosed attitude helped the company survive difficult times.
The hard-nosed negotiator refused to compromise, insisting on every detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardheaded - practical and realistic; not influenced by emotions
  • antonym: idealistic - guided more by ideals or theory than by practicality
  • synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically; practical rather than theoretical
  • antonym: soft-hearted - easily moved by feelings of pity or sympathy
  • synonym: tough - strong and not easily influenced; able to endure hardship
