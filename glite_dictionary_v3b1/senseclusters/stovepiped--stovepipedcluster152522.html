stovepiped

adjective
information kept isolated in separate systems
9.9 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈstoʊv.paɪpt/ (AmE), /ˈstəʊv.paɪpt/ (BrE)
(of data or information) kept isolated in separate databases or systems, making sharing or integration difficult.

Examples

A stovepiped system
Because the company's records were stovepiped, it was difficult to generate a comprehensive report.
Data remained stovepiped across separate divisions, hindering efficient analysis.
Many organizations struggle with stovepiped systems that prevent collaboration between departments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: siloed - (of data or information) kept in isolation, not shared with others
