black crappie

noun
a species of dark-colored freshwater fish
5.1 6.9
Pronunciation: /blæk ˈkræ.piː/ (AmE), /blæk ˈkræ.piː/ (BrE)
a species of North American freshwater fish (Pomoxis nigromaculatus) of the sunfish family, distinguished by its dark coloration and irregular blotches

Examples

The black crappie fish
The black crappie is popular among anglers for its delicious flavor.
Unlike the white crappie, the black crappie has darker spots and a deeper body.
We caught several black crappie at the lake yesterday afternoon.
