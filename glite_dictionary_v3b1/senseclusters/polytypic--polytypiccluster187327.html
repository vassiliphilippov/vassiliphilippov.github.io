polytypic

adjective
having multiple types
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈtɪpɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈtɪpɪk / (BrE)
describing a group, especially a species, that contains several distinct types or forms

Examples

Polytypic species group
The butterfly genus is polytypic, with many different subspecies.
Scientists discovered that the plant family is polytypic and includes several unique varieties.
A polytypic species can adapt to a range of environments due to its diversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety
  • antonym: monotypic - having only one type or form
