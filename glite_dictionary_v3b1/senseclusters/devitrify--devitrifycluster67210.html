devitrify

verb
change from glassy to crystalline
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪtrəˌfaɪ /; / dɪˈvɪtrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪtrɪfaɪ /; / dɪˈvɪtrɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to change or cause to change from a glassy (vitreous) state to a crystalline state

Examples

To devitrify volcanic glass
Over time, the surface of the volcanic glass will devitrify due to exposure to moisture.
Some ceramics devitrify when reheated, affecting their transparency and strength.
The scientist observed that the sample began to devitrify during the cooling process.
Failure to control the temperature can cause molten glass to devitrify during the production process.
If cooled improperly, the glass may devitrify and lose its clarity.
Some ceramics manufacturers intentionally devitrify glazes to achieve a matte finish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystallize - to form or cause to form crystals
  • antonym: vitrify - to convert into glass or a glassy substance by heat
