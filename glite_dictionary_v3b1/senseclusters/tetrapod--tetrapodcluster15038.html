tetrapod

noun
vertebrate animal with four limbs
6.0 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈte.trə.pɑːd/ (AmE), /ˈte.trə.pɒd/ (BrE)
an animal with four feet, legs, or leg-like appendages, especially any member of the group of vertebrates that includes amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals

Examples

A tetrapod animal
Frogs, lizards, and humans are all examples of tetrapods with four limbs.
Modern birds, although they have wings instead of arms, are still classified as tetrapods.
Scientists believe that the first tetrapods evolved from ancient lobe-finned fishes.

  • synonym: quadruped - an animal that walks on four legs
