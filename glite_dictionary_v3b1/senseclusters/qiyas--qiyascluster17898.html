qiyas

noun
Islamic legal analogy
Pronunciation: / kiˈjɑːs / (AmE), / kiˈjɑːs / (BrE)
a method of analogical reasoning used in Islamic law to derive legal rulings for new situations by comparing them to established cases

Examples

Qiyas in law
The scholar used qiyas to determine the appropriate ruling for the new technology.
In Islamic jurisprudence, qiyas is an important method for addressing modern issues.
The court's decision was based on qiyas with previous cases involving similar circumstances.
The jurist used qiyas to determine the ruling for a modern financial transaction.
Students of Islamic law study qiyas as one of the main sources of jurisprudence.
Debates about the limits of qiyas are common among scholars of different schools.
Islamic scholars often use qiyas to address new issues not directly mentioned in the Quran.
The principle of qiyas allows for flexibility in interpreting religious law.
Debates about the validity of qiyas have shaped Islamic legal thought for centuries.
