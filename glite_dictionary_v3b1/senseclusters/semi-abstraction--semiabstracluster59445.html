semi-abstraction

noun
partly abstract art style
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmi ˌæbˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi ˌabˈstrakʃən / (BrE)
A style of art in which some elements are recognizable but are rendered in a way that is not fully realistic.

Examples

A semi-abstraction painting
Her latest exhibition explores semi-abstraction through vibrant colors and geometric shapes.
Many modern painters turned to semi-abstraction as a way to balance clarity with creativity.
The artist is known for his unique semi-abstraction that blends landscapes with abstract forms.

  • degree higher: abstraction - the quality of art that does not attempt to represent external reality; an abstract work of art
