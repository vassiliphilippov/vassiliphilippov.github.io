hope

verb
to desire and expect a positive outcome
3.4
Pronunciation: / hoʊp / (AmE), / həʊp / (BrE)
to want something to happen or be true, and believe it is possible

Examples

To hope for change
I hoped you would be able to join us for dinner.
She hopes that her application will be successful.
We are hoping for better weather tomorrow.
I sincerely hope that you can come to the wedding next month.
Many people hope for a better future for their children.
She hopes to finish her novel before the end of the year.
I hope to visit Japan next year if I can save enough money.
Many students hope for a fair chance in the competition.
She hoped to finish the project before the deadline, but it took longer than expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: despair - the complete loss or absence of hope
  • synonym: wish - to want something to happen or to be true, often with less expectation than hope
