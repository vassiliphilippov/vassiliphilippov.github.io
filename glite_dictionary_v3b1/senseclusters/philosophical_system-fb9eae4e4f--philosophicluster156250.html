philosophical system

noun
set of beliefs in a philosophy
Pronunciation: / fəˌlɑsəˈfɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / fəˌlɒsəˈfɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a set of beliefs or doctrines forming the basis of a particular philosophy, accepted as authoritative by a group or school

Examples

A philosophical system of beliefs
Plato developed a philosophical system that has influenced thinkers for centuries.
The university offers courses on every major philosophical system from antiquity to the present.
Within this philosophical system, all actions are judged according to their consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: philosophy - the study of fundamental nature of knowledge, reality, and existence
