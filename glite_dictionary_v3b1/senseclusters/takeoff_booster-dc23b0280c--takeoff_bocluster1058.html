takeoff booster

noun
first stage of a multistage rocket
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkˌɔf ˈbuːstər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɒf ˈbuːstə / (BrE)
the first stage of a multistage rocket that provides the initial thrust required for liftoff.

Examples

The takeoff booster engine
Engineers inspected the takeoff booster for signs of wear before the mission.
Once the takeoff booster burns out, the second stage ignites to propel the rocket further.
The takeoff booster separates after the rocket has cleared the launch pad.

Related Terms

  • synonym: booster - a device that increases force or power, especially in rocketry
