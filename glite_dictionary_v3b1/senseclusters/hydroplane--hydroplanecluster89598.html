hydroplane

verb
to slide on water
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəʊˌpleɪn / (BrE)
to slide uncontrollably on a wet surface, especially when a vehicle's tires lose contact with the road because of water

Examples

Cars hydroplane in rain
If you drive too fast in heavy rain, your car might hydroplane and become difficult to control.
The driver lost control when the truck began to hydroplane on the flooded highway.
Always slow down on wet roads to avoid hydroplaning and keep yourself safe.
The car hydroplaned on the highway after heavy rain made the road slippery.
If you drive too fast in the rain, your vehicle might hydroplane and become difficult to control.
She lost control when her motorcycle hydroplaned across a large puddle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grip - to hold firmly onto a surface
  • synonym: skid - to slide without control, especially on a slippery surface
