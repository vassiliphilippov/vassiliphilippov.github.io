Japanese lilac

noun
species of lilac plant with rose-lilac flowers
7.4
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈlaɪlæk / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈlɪlək / (BrE)
A species of lilac native to northern China, with oval-shaped leaves and abundant rose-lilac flowers blooming in early summer.

Examples

The Japanese lilac tree
Many landscapers recommend planting Japanese lilac trees along city streets for their hardiness.
The Japanese lilac in our garden blossoms beautifully every June.
The pale pink flowers of the Japanese lilac attract numerous bees and butterflies.
