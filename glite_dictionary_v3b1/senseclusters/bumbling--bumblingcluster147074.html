bumbling

adjective
clumsy; awkward in movement
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌmblɪŋ / (BrE)
awkward or clumsy, especially in physical movement or handling things, often in a way that seems confused or incompetent

Examples

Bumbling detective
His bumbling attempts to fix the sink only made the problem worse.
She charmed the audience with her bumbling but sincere stage presence.
The bumbling waiter spilled water all over the table while serving guests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things
  • antonym: dexterous - showing skill, especially with the hands
