anthropomorphosis

noun
giving human traits
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːfəsɪs / (BrE)
the act of giving human form or characteristics to something that is not human

Examples

Anthropomorphosis of animals
The anthropomorphosis of animals in cartoons helps children relate to the characters.
In ancient myths, the anthropomorphosis of natural forces was a common storytelling device.
The artist's sculpture is a striking example of anthropomorphosis, blending human and animal features.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dehumanization - the process of depriving something of human qualities
  • synonym: personification - the representation of something non-human as having human qualities
