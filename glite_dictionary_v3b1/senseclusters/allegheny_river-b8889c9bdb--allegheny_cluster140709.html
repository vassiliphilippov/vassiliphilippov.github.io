Allegheny River

proper noun
river in northeastern United States
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪˈɡeɪni ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡeɪni ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the northeastern United States that flows from Pennsylvania into New York and back through Pennsylvania to join the Monongahela River at Pittsburgh, forming the Ohio River

Examples

The Allegheny River flows
Fishing enthusiasts often visit the Allegheny River for its diverse fish species.
Many cities along the Allegheny River rely on it for their water supply.
The Allegheny River merges with the Monongahela at Pittsburgh to form the Ohio River.
