notwithstanding

preposition
despite; in spite of
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑtwɪθˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒtwɪθˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
in spite of something; despite what has just been mentioned or considered

Examples

Notwithstanding his efforts
Notwithstanding his lack of experience, he performed the task exceptionally well.
The law was passed, notwithstanding strong opposition from several groups.
She enjoyed the trip, notwithstanding the rainy weather throughout the week.
