Synonyms

  • force
    2.3 100/m
    force is a common direct verb that uses power or pressure to make someone act, while compel sounds more formal and necessary.
  • coerce
    6.4 4/m
    coerce means to make someone act by threats or strong pressure, while compel can be neutral and mean make something necessary.
  • oblige
    4.5 3/m
    oblige often means to make someone act because of duty, law, or circumstance, while compel focuses on forcing or making something necessary.
  • obligate
    6.7 4/m
    obligate is to bind someone legally or morally in formal settings, while compel is broader and appears in general situations.
  • make
    1.0 110/m
    make is a simple everyday verb meaning cause someone to do something, while compel is more formal and stronger in tone.
  • constrain
    5.6 3/m
    constrain means to limit choices or movement so someone acts in a certain way, while compel directly forces or makes something necessary.
