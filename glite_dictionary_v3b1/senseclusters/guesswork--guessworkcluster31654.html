guesswork

noun
conjecture; estimation without sufficient evidence
4.2 1.11/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°…õsÀåw…úrk / (AmE), / Àà…°…õsw…úÀêk / (BrE)
The process of forming an opinion, estimate, or answer without sufficient information or relying mainly on conjecture.

Examples

Pure guesswork
His explanation was mostly guesswork, as he had very few facts.
The results are so unpredictable that any forecast would be pure guesswork.
We can't rely on guesswork when making these important decisions.

Synonyms

  • guessing
    1.6 0.02/m
    guessing is the act of forming opinions without enough evidence, while guesswork is the overall process of relying on conjecture.
  • guess
    1.0 24/m
    guess is a single opinion or answer given without enough information, while guesswork is the ongoing process of making such guesses.
  • speculation
    4.8 17/m
    speculation is a more formal idea or theory made with limited proof, while guesswork is a casual process of unsupported guessing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information
  • synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory without firm evidence
