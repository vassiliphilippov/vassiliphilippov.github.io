every name in the book

noun
all possible insults (idiom)
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛvɹi neɪm ɪn ðə bʊk / (AmE), / ˈɛvɹi neɪm ɪn ðə bʊk / (BrE)
used to say that someone has called another person all possible or imaginable insults, often in anger or frustration.

Examples

Called every name in the book
After the disastrous meeting, his boss called him every name in the book.
In the heat of the moment, she called her friend every name in the book.
She was so upset that she called him every name in the book after the argument.
