Saint Vitus dance

noun
disorder with involuntary movements (chorea) in children
9.4 9.0
Pronunciation: /seɪnt ˈviː.təs dæns/ /; / /seɪnt ˈvaɪ.təs dæns/ (AmE), /seɪnt ˈviː.təs dɑːns/ /; / /seɪnt ˈvaɪ.təs dɑːns/ (BrE)
A disorder characterized by rapid, involuntary movements occurring mainly in children, especially as a complication of rheumatic fever; also known as chorea.

Examples

Saint Vitus dance disorder
Children suffering from rheumatic fever sometimes developed Saint Vitus dance as a complication.
In the 19th century, Saint Vitus dance was a common term for Sydenham's chorea.
The doctor recognized the involuntary jerking as symptoms of Saint Vitus dance.
