subplot

noun
secondary story
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌplɑt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌplɒt / (BrE)
a secondary story in a book, play, or movie that supports or contrasts with the main plot

Examples

Romantic subplot
The detective subplot kept readers guessing alongside the main adventure.
In the film, a humorous subplot provided relief from the intense drama.
The author skillfully wove a family subplot into the larger political narrative.
The detective novel features a romantic subplot that adds depth to the main mystery.
Several subplots in the film explore the lives of minor characters.
The author skillfully weaves each subplot into the overall narrative.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plot - the main story of a book, play, or movie
  • synonym: storyline - the sequence of events in a story
