pretesting

noun
assessment before instruction
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of assessing knowledge, skills, or abilities before the main test or instruction begins

Examples

Student pretesting
The teacher used pretesting to determine which topics needed more review.
Pretesting can help identify students' strengths and weaknesses before a course begins.
Many educational programs include pretesting as part of their evaluation process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or testing knowledge or skills
  • antonym: posttesting - assessment after instruction or an intervention
