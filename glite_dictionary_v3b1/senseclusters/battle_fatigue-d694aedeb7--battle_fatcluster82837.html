battle fatigue

noun
mental disorder from combat stress
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l fəˌtiɡ / (AmE), / ˈbætl fəˌtiːɡ / (BrE)
A psychological disorder caused by prolonged exposure to active combat, characterized by extreme fatigue, anxiety, and other mental disturbances; also known as combat stress or shell shock.

Examples

Battle fatigue symptoms
During World War II, doctors struggled to treat cases of battle fatigue among combat troops.
Many soldiers returning from the front lines showed clear signs of battle fatigue.
The documentary explores how battle fatigue impacted both veterans and their families after the war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: post-traumatic stress disorder - psychiatric disorder that can occur after experiencing or witnessing traumatic events
  • synonym: shell shock - psychological trauma caused by warfare, especially in World War I
