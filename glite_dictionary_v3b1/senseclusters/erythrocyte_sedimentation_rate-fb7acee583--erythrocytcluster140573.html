erythrocyte sedimentation rate

noun
medical test measuring how quickly red blood cells settle
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɪθrəˌsaɪt ˌsɛdɪˈmɛnˌteɪʃən reɪt / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəˌsaɪt ˌsɛdɪˈmenˌteɪʃən reɪt / (BrE)
the rate at which red blood cells settle to the bottom of a test tube under standardized laboratory conditions, often used as an indicator of inflammation or disease.

Examples

The erythrocyte sedimentation rate test
An abnormal erythrocyte sedimentation rate can signal a range of medical conditions.
Doctors often order an erythrocyte sedimentation rate test for patients with unexplained fevers.
The erythrocyte sedimentation rate was elevated, suggesting possible inflammation.
