epiphenomenally

adverb
as a by-product
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪfəˈnɑːmənəli / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪfəˈnɒmɪnəli / (BrE)
in a way that happens as a by-product or secondary effect, without influencing the main process

Examples

Happens epiphenomenally as effect
The sensation occurred epiphenomenally, without affecting the underlying neural activity.
Some philosophers argue that consciousness exists epiphenomenally and does not cause physical actions.
Her emotions arose epiphenomenally during the experiment, not influencing the outcome.
Some scientists argue that mental states exist epiphenomenally alongside physical brain processes.
The color change in the solution happened epiphenomenally as a result of the main reaction.
Economic growth may occur epiphenomenally from technological innovation rather than direct policy.
