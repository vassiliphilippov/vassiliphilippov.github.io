Golgi complex

noun
cellular organelle for protein processing
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊl.dʒi ˈkɑm.plɛks / (AmE), / ˈɡɒl.dʒi ˈkɒm.pleks / (BrE)
a network of membranous sacs and vesicles in the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells, involved in modifying, sorting, and packaging proteins and lipids for secretion or delivery to other organelles

Examples

The Golgi complex structure
Scientists observed changes in the Golgi complex during the cell's lifecycle.
Secretory cells contain a well-developed Golgi complex to handle the volume of materials they export.
The Golgi complex plays a crucial role in packaging proteins for transport.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Golgi apparatus - an organelle in cells for modifying and exporting proteins and lipids
