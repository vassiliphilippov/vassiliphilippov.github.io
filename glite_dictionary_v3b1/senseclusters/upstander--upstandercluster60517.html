upstander

noun
person who takes action
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌstændər / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstændə / (BrE)
a person who stands up for others or takes action against injustice or wrongdoing

Examples

A brave upstander
She became an upstander by reporting the unfair treatment she witnessed at work.
Teachers encourage students to be an upstander rather than ignore bullying.
As an upstander, he spoke out against discrimination in his community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bystander - a person who is present but does not take part or intervene
