graze

verb
eat small amounts; try different dishes
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪz / (AmE), / ɡreɪz / (BrE)
to eat small amounts of food throughout the day or try different dishes instead of eating one full meal

Examples

To graze on snacks
At the picnic, we preferred to graze on the different snack platters throughout the afternoon.
Instead of a formal dinner, guests could graze at the various food stations.
She likes to graze all day instead of sitting down for a big lunch.

  • synonym: nibble - eat something in small bites
