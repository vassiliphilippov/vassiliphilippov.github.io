native speaker

noun
person who speaks a language from childhood
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪɾɪv ˈspiːkər / (AmE), / ˈneɪtɪv ˈspiːkə / (BrE)
A person who has spoken a particular language from earliest childhood and uses it as their first language.

Examples

A native speaker of English
She is a native speaker of German and learned English at school.
Sometimes even a native speaker can struggle with complex grammar questions.
The course is taught by a native speaker to improve students' pronunciation.
