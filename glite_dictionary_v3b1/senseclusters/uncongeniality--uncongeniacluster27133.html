uncongeniality

noun
lack of congeniality or friendliness
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkənˌdʒiːniˈæləti /; / ˌʌn.kənˌdʒiː.niˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˌdʒiːniˈælɪti /; / ˌʌn.kənˌdʒiː.niˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being unfriendly, unpleasant, or not suitable; lack of friendliness or harmony

Examples

She was troubled by the uncongeniality of her new neighbors, who rarely spoke to her.
The uncongeniality of the work environment made collaboration difficult for everyone involved.
Despite the uncongeniality of the climate, the explorers pressed on with their research.
His quick departure reflected the uncongeniality of the atmosphere at the meeting.
Many employees left the firm due to the uncongeniality of the new management.
