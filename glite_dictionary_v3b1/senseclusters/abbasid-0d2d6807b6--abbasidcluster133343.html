Abbasid

proper noun
Islamic dynasty 750–1258
Pronunciation: / əˈbæsɪd / (AmE), / əˈbæsɪd / (BrE)
the dynasty that ruled much of the Islamic world from 750 to 1258, known for its capital in Baghdad and cultural achievements

Examples

The Abbasid Caliphate
The Abbasid Caliphate was famous for its advances in science and literature.
Baghdad became a major center of learning under the Abbasid rulers.
The decline of the Abbasid dynasty marked a turning point in Islamic history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Caliphate - an Islamic state led by a caliph, a religious and political leader
  • antonym: Umayyad - the dynasty that ruled the Islamic world before the Abbasids
