LEM

noun
Apollo moon-landing spacecraft
Pronunciation: / ɛl i ɛm / (AmE), / ɛl iː ɛm / (BrE)
a spacecraft used to transport astronauts from the command module to the surface of the moon and back, especially during the Apollo missions

Examples

The LEM spacecraft
Engineers at NASA worked tirelessly to perfect the LEM for the Apollo missions.
Television viewers watched in awe as the LEM touched down on the Moon in 1969.
The LEM separated from the command module to begin its descent to the lunar surface.
