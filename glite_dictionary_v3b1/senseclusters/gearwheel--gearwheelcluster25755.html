gearwheel

noun
toothed machine wheel
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪrˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪəˌwiːl / (BrE)
a wheel with teeth that meshes with another toothed wheel to transmit motion in machinery

Examples

A gearwheel in machinery
The mechanic replaced the broken gearwheel to restore the machine's function.
Inside the old clock, each gearwheel played a crucial role in keeping time.
A bicycle's speed can be changed by shifting the gearwheel to a different position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cogwheel - a wheel with a series of projections on its edge that transfers motion by engaging with another wheel
  • synonym: gear - a toothed wheel that works with others to alter the relation between the speed of an engine and the speed of the driven parts
