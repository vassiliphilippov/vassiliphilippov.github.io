stereochromy

noun
mural painting process using chemical fixation
7.6 9.9
Pronunciation: /ste.rɪ.oʊ.ˈkroʊ.miː/ /; / /ˈste.rɪ.oʊ.kroʊ.miː/ (AmE), /ste.rɪ.əʊ.ˈkrəʊ.miː/ /; / /ˈste.rɪ.əʊ.krəʊ.miː/ (BrE)
A method of mural painting in which pigments are made permanent by a chemical reaction involving lime, fluosilicic acid, and water glass.

Examples

Stereochromy mural painting
Artists experimenting with stereochromy achieved brilliance and durability in their wall paintings.
Compared to fresco, stereochromy requires more specialized chemical knowledge and materials.
The restoration team chose stereochromy to ensure the mural's colors would last for generations.
