spinnable

adjective
able to be influenced by biased information
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪn.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪn.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of information or a situation) capable of being presented or manipulated to convey a particular, often biased, interpretation.

Examples

Spinnable story
Marketing teams are often looking for spinnable angles on disappointing results.
The report was written in a deliberately neutral tone to be less spinnable by the media.
The senator's statement was highly spinnable, allowing journalists to interpret it in multiple ways.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spin - to present information in a way that creates a biased interpretation
