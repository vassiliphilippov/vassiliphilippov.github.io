distress

verb
cause difficulties or hardship
2.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɛs / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛs / (BrE)
to cause someone to suffer, feel pain, anxiety, or hardship

Examples

To distress people
Rising interest rates distressed many investors, leaving them struggling to meet financial obligations.
Sudden market changes can distress small businesses and lead to bankruptcy.
The unexpected repair costs distressed many homeowners facing tight budgets.
It distressed her to see the animal in pain.
Please don't distress yourself over such a minor problem.
The graphic images on the news distressed many viewers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - to make someone feel less unhappy or worried
  • synonym: impoverish - to make someone poor; to reduce to poverty
  • antonym: relieve - to make a bad situation better; to ease distress
  • synonym: upset - to make someone unhappy, worried, or angry
