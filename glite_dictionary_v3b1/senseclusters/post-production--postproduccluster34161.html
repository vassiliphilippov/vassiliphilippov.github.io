post-production

noun
film editing phase
3.8
Pronunciation: /poʊst prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /pəʊst prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of editing, adding effects, and finalizing film, video, or audio after recording or shooting is finished

Examples

Post-production editing process
After filming wrapped, the team began post-production to add special effects and music.
The documentary spent several months in post-production before it was released to the public.
Sound mixing is an important part of post-production for any major film.
The movie spent several months in post-production before it was ready for release.
Sound mixing and visual effects are important parts of post-production in filmmaking.
After filming wrapped, the team focused on post-production to complete the documentary.
The film looked very different after it went through post-production and special effects were added.
Sound engineers spent weeks in post-production to perfect the audio for the documentary.
Video games often require extensive post-production to fix bugs and improve graphics before release.

Related Terms

  • synonym: editing - the process of selecting and arranging film or video footage
  • antonym: pre-production - the planning and preparation phase before filming begins
  • antonym: production - the process of creating or recording media content
