underskin

noun
layer beneath skin
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.skɪn/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.skɪn/ (BrE)
the layer of tissue directly beneath the skin, also known as subcutaneous tissue

Examples

Underskin tissue
The injection is given into the underskin to allow slow absorption of the medicine.
Bruising can occur in the underskin after a minor injury.
Fat stored in the underskin helps insulate the body against cold temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epidermis - the outermost layer of the skin
  • synonym: subcutaneous tissue - the layer of tissue under the skin containing fat and connective tissue
