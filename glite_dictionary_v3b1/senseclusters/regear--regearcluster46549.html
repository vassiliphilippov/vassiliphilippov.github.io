regear

verb
to adjust gears again
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɡɪr / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɡɪə / (BrE)
to change or adjust the gears of a machine or vehicle again, usually to improve performance or adapt to new conditions

Examples

To regear a truck
After installing larger tires, they decided to regear the truck for better off-road capability.
The mechanic recommended that we regear the transmission to match the new engine.
Many car enthusiasts choose to regear their vehicles to improve acceleration.
