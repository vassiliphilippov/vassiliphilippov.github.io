firestorm

noun
intense, wind-driven fire
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə.stɔːm / (BrE)
A large and intense fire in which strong currents of air are drawn in by the rising column of hot air, often occurring over a severely bombed or burned area.

Examples

A firestorm spreads
Firefighters struggled to contain the firestorm as it swept through the forest.
The firestorm generated its own wind, making the blaze much harder to control.
The bombing created a firestorm that devastated the entire city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conflagration - a large, destructive fire
  • synonym: inferno - a large and dangerous fire
