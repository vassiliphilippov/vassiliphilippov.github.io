Thomas Nast

proper noun
U.S. political cartoonist (1840–1902)
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑməs næst / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs næst / (BrE)
(1840–1902) An American political cartoonist renowned for his influential cartoons; credited with creating the modern depictions of Uncle Sam, the Republican elephant, and the Democratic donkey.

Examples

Thomas Nast cartoons
Thomas Nast is often called the father of the American political cartoon.
Many of the political symbols used today were popularized by Thomas Nast.
The works of Thomas Nast had a significant impact on public opinion during his lifetime.
