experientialism

noun
knowledge from experience
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌspɪriˈɛnʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɪəriˈɛnʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that knowledge is primarily gained through personal experience rather than abstract reasoning or authority

Examples

Experientialism in education
Many educators believe that experientialism leads to deeper understanding by involving students in real-world activities.
The philosophy of experientialism values learning through doing rather than just reading or listening.
Some critics argue that experientialism overlooks the importance of theoretical knowledge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empiricism - the theory that knowledge comes mainly from sensory experience
  • antonym: rationalism - the theory that reason is the main source of knowledge
