photodiode

noun
light-sensitive device
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊtoʊˌdaɪoʊd / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊˌdaɪəʊd / (BrE)
an electronic device that generates an electric current when exposed to light, used to detect or measure light levels

Examples

A photodiode sensor
The camera's light meter relies on a photodiode to measure brightness accurately.
A photodiode is often used in remote controls to receive infrared signals.
Scientists installed a photodiode in the experiment to detect changes in light intensity.
The camera's sensor contains a photodiode that helps adjust the exposure automatically.
Engineers use a photodiode to measure the intensity of laser beams in experiments.
A photodiode can quickly detect changes in light, making it useful for safety systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photocell - a device that generates electricity when exposed to light
  • synonym: photodetector - a device that detects light and converts it into an electrical signal
  • synonym: solar cell - a device that converts sunlight into electrical energy
