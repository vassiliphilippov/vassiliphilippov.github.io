antiphase

noun
out-of-phase state
Pronunciation: / ˈæntiˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈæntiˌfeɪz / (BrE)
a state in which two waves or signals are exactly out of phase with each other, causing cancellation or reduction

Examples

Antiphase state
When the two signals are in antiphase, their amplitudes cancel each other out.
The experiment demonstrated how antiphase can reduce unwanted noise in audio systems.
Light waves in antiphase create a pattern of darkness due to destructive interference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destructive interference - the process where two waves cancel each other out
  • antonym: in phase - a state where two waves or signals are aligned in phase
