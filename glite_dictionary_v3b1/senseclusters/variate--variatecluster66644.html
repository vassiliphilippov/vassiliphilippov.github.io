variate

verb
to make different
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈveəriˌeɪt / (BrE)
to change or cause something to change; to make different in some way

Examples

To variate the pattern
The color of the sky can variate greatly depending on the weather conditions.
Scientists observed that the results would variate with each new experiment.
You can variate your workout routine to keep it interesting and effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alter - to change something, usually slightly
  • antonym: maintain - to keep something the same
  • synonym: vary - to change or differ in amount, degree, or nature
