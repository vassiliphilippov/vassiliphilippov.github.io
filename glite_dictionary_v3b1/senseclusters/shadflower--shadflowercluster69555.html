shadflower

noun
annual weed with small flowers and oblong capsules
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædˌflaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈʃadˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
An annual weed found in Europe and North America, characterized by a rosette of basal leaves and small flowers that develop into oblong seed capsules.

Examples

The shadflower weed
After flowering, the shadflower produces distinctive oblong seed capsules.
Gardeners consider the shadflower a persistent weed in many temperate regions.
The field was dotted with bright shadflower blooming among the grasses.
