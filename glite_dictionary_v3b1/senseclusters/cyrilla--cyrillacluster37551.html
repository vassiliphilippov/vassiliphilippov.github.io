cyrilla

noun
shrub/small tree with white flowers and colorful foliage
9.3
Pronunciation: / sɪˈrɪlə / (AmE), / saɪˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and Brazil, grown for its slender clusters of white flowers and orange or crimson foliage.

Examples

A cyrilla shrub
Along the wetland trail, we spotted a mature cyrilla displaying clusters of snowy blossoms.
Many gardeners choose cyrilla for its ability to thrive in both sun and partial shade.
The cyrilla adds year-round interest to gardens with its striking foliage and delicate flowers.
