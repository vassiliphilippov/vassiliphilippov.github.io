foot

noun
locomotory organ in invertebrates
4.9
Pronunciation: / fʊt / (AmE), / fʊt / (BrE)
an organ of locomotion or attachment in certain invertebrate animals, such as mollusks or worms

Examples

A clam's foot
A clam extends its foot to burrow into the sand.
A mollusk's foot is adapted for crawling or anchoring to surfaces.
The snail moves slowly using its muscular foot.
