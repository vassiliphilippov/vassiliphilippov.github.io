revive

verb
to bring someone back to consciousness
3.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈvaɪv/ /; / /riː.ˈvaɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈvaɪv/ /; / /riː.ˈvaɪv/ (BrE)
to make someone regain consciousness or become alert again after fainting or being unconscious

Examples

To revive a person
Splashing cold water on his face helped revive him.
The paramedics managed to revive her after she fainted at the concert.
They tried for several minutes to revive the swimmer who had stopped breathing.
After fainting in the heat, she began to revive as soon as she was moved to a cooler place.
He slowly revived after receiving an injection from the nurse.
The doctor managed to revive the patient using smelling salts.
A splash of cold water can quickly revive someone who has fainted.
The fresh air seemed to revive him after his long illness.
They hoped to revive the city's traditions with an annual festival.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exhaust - to make someone very tired, to drain energy from
  • antonym: faint - to temporarily lose consciousness
  • synonym: recover - to regain normal state after illness or unconsciousness
  • synonym: refresh - to make someone less tired or hot, giving new strength or energy
  • synonym: rejuvenate - to make someone look or feel younger, fresher, or more lively
  • synonym: resuscitate - to revive someone from unconsciousness or apparent death
