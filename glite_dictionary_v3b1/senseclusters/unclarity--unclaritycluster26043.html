unclarity

noun
lack of clarity
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈklærəti / (AmE), / ʌnˈklærəti / (BrE)
the quality of being cloudy, murky, or not transparent, especially in liquids or images

Examples

Unclarity of water
The unclarity of the lake water made it difficult to spot any fish below the surface.
Photographers often struggle with unclarity when shooting in foggy or misty conditions.
Due to the unclarity of the solution, the chemist repeated the filtration process.
