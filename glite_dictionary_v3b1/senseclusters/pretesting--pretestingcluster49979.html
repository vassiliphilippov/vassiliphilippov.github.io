pretesting

noun
trial testing phase
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of testing a questionnaire, product, or procedure on a small scale before full use to identify issues or improve effectiveness

Examples

Product pretesting
The marketing team conducted pretesting of the advertisement to see how people would react.
Before launching the new app, the developers did extensive pretesting to catch any bugs.
Effective pretesting can help researchers improve their survey questions before collecting real data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evaluation - the process of assessing something's value or effectiveness
  • antonym: implementation - the act of putting a plan or product into full use
  • synonym: pilot testing - a small-scale preliminary test of a method or product
