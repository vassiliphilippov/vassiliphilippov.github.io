pundit

noun
expert; authority, especially in media
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌn.dɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌn.dɪt / (BrE)
an expert or authority who is frequently called upon to give opinions in a particular subject, especially in the media

Examples

Sports pundits
Many sports pundits predicted that the team would win the championship this year.
She established herself as a leading pundit in environmental policy.
The political pundit was frequently interviewed on television to analyze the latest election results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amateur - person who engages in a pursuit without professional expertise
  • synonym: expert - person highly skilled or knowledgeable in a particular area
