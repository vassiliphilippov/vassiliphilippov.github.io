lobefin

noun
a type of prehistoric lobe-finned fish
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊbˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈləʊbˌfɪn / (BrE)
a fish of the order Crossopterygii, most of which are known only from fossils, characterized by fleshy, lobed, paired fins.

Examples

A lobefin fish
Most lobefins disappeared millions of years ago, leaving behind only a few fossilized remains.
Some lobefins are believed to be ancestors of the first land-dwelling animals.
The lobefin is of great interest to scientists studying the evolution of vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coelacanth - a living species of lobefin fish
