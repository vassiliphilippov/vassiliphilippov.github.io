laudatory

adjective
expressing praise or approval
8.1 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…îÀêd…ôÀåt…îri / (AmE), / Ààl…îÀêd…ôt…ôri / (BrE)
expressing praise or approval; giving commendation.

Examples

Laudatory comments
Most of his comments were laudatory, focusing on the positive aspects of the proposal.
She received a laudatory letter from management for her outstanding performance.
The critic wrote a laudatory review of the novel, praising its brilliant storytelling.

Synonyms

  • praising
    2.1 0.00/m
    praising shows that someone speaks well of a person or act, while laudatory describes language that gives praise.
  • complimentary
    4.2 0.90/m
    complimentary expresses kind words or admiration in everyday speech, while laudatory is a broader, slightly more formal term for praise.
  • glowing
    4.0 0.24/m

  • praiseful
    6.6 0.00/m
    praiseful sounds old-fashioned or poetic and shows praise in tone, while laudatory is a neutral modern word for approval.
  • approbatory
    8.9 0.00/m

  • approbative
    8.8 0.01/m
    approbative offers approval or praise in a formal way, whereas laudatory covers praise in many styles and contexts.
  • encomiastic
    9.9 0.01/m

  • panegyrical
    9.9 0.01/m

  • eulogistic
    8.9 0.03/m
