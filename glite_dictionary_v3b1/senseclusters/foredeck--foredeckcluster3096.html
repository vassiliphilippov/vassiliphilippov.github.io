foredeck

noun
front deck area of a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌdɛk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdɛk / (BrE)
the part of a ship's deck located between the bridge and the forecastle, usually at the front of the vessel

Examples

The foredeck area
Crew members gathered on the foredeck to secure the lines before docking.
During the storm, waves crashed over the foredeck, making it slippery and dangerous.
The captain stood on the foredeck to get a clear view of the approaching coastline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: afterdeck - the rear deck of a ship
