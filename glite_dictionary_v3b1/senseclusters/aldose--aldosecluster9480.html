aldose

noun
a sugar containing an aldehyde group
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.doʊs/ (AmE), /ˈæl.dəʊs/ (BrE)
A monosaccharide sugar that contains an aldehyde group at the end of its carbon chain, or exists in a hemiacetal form.

Examples

An aldose sugar
Glucose is an example of an aldose commonly found in nature.
Researchers tested several aldose sugars for their reactivity with enzymes.
The chemical structure of an aldose differs from that of a ketose.
