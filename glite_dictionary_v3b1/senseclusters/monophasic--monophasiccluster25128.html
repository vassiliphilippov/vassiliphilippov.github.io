monophasic

adjective
single-phase electrical
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈfeɪzɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈfeɪzɪk / (BrE)
having or using a single phase or polarity, especially in an electrical pulse, current, or system

Examples

Monophasic current
The doctor used a monophasic current to stimulate the patient's nerve during the procedure.
A monophasic waveform is often used in certain types of medical equipment.
Unlike biphasic pulses, monophasic stimulation delivers current in only one direction.
The laboratory equipment requires a monophasic power supply for safe operation.
Many small homes in rural areas are connected to a monophasic electrical grid.
Unlike three-phase motors, monophasic motors are often used in household appliances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biphasic - having two phases or polarities
  • antonym: polyphasic - using or relating to more than one phase, especially in electrical systems
  • synonym: single-phase - using or relating to a single alternating current phase
  • synonym: unipolar - having a single pole or polarity
