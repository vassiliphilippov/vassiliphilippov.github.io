civilization

noun
process of social development
2.1 3.2
Pronunciation: /sɪ.və.lɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.və.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sɪ.vɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.və.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process by which a society or place reaches an advanced stage of social and cultural development and organization.

Examples

The civilization of a country
Education plays a crucial role in the civilization of a society.
Historians study the process of civilization to understand cultural and technological progress.
The rise of agriculture was a turning point in the civilization of humankind.
Education is often considered a fundamental part of societal civilisation.
Some historians argue that trade was crucial to the civilisation of early societies.
The rise of Athens marked a new phase in the civilisation of ancient Greece.
Good conversation and generosity are the true marks of civilisation.
His manners and taste are the very model of civilisation.
She aspired to a level of civilisation in both thought and behavior.
