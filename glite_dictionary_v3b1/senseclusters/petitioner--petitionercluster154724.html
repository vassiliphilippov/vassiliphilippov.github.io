petitioner

noun
person who submits a petition to a court
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɪʃənər / (AmE), / pɪˈtɪʃənə /; / pəˈtɪʃənə / (BrE)
a person who formally asks for something, especially by presenting a petition to a court or authority

Examples

The petitioner in court
As the petitioner in the case, he was required to provide evidence supporting his claim.
The petitioner asked the court to protect her intellectual property rights.
The Supreme Court granted the petitioner a hearing on the matter.
As a petitioner, she addressed the council with a heartfelt plea for justice.
During the ceremony, each petitioner offered prayers and requests for a prosperous harvest.
The petitioner knelt before the king, hoping for mercy and a pardon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plaintiff - person who brings a case against another in court
  • antonym: respondent - person who responds to a petition, especially in court
  • synonym: suppliant - a person making a humble plea to someone in power or authority
