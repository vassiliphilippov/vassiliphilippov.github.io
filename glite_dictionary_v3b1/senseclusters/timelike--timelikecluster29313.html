timelike

adjective
relating to time in physics
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪmlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmlaɪk / (BrE)
having properties similar to time, especially describing intervals or directions in physics where cause and effect can occur

Examples

A timelike interval
In special relativity, a timelike interval means that one event can influence another.
The scientist explained that only timelike paths allow for a possible signal between two points.
A timelike separation indicates that the events could be connected by a moving observer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lightlike - relating to intervals or directions similar to the path of light in physics
  • antonym: spacelike - relating to intervals or directions more like space than time in physics
