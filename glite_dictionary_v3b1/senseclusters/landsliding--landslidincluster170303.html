landsliding

noun
earth movement; big win
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈlænd.slaɪ.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈlænd.slaɪ.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the movement of a mass of earth or rock down a slope, often suddenly and destructively; also, an overwhelming victory in an election or contest

Examples

A landsliding disaster
Heavy rain triggered a landsliding that destroyed several houses in the village.
The candidate won by landsliding, securing almost every vote in the district.
After the earthquake, emergency crews worked to clear the landsliding from the main road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - a sudden falling down or giving way
  • antonym: defeat - the act of losing in a competition or contest
  • synonym: landslide - a sudden and fast movement of earth or rock down a slope
  • synonym: victory - the act of winning a competition or contest
