Balaenopteridae

noun
family of large baleen whales (rorquals, blue whales)
Pronunciation: / bəˌliːnoʊpˈtɛrɪdiː /; / bəˌlinɑːpˈtɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɒpˈtɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large baleen whales, including rorquals and blue whales, with pleated throat grooves and streamlined bodies

Examples

Balaenopteridae whale family
Many species in the Balaenopteridae family are migratory, traveling thousands of miles between feeding and breeding grounds.
Scientists have classified the blue whale, the largest animal ever known, within the family Balaenopteridae.
The Balaenopteridae are distinguished by their pleated throat grooves, which allow expansion during feeding.
Research on Balaenopteridae has provided insight into the evolution of baleen whales.
The Balaenopteridae include some of the largest animals on the planet, such as the blue whale.
Unlike other cetacean families, Balaenopteridae are characterized by their throat pleats and feeding techniques.
