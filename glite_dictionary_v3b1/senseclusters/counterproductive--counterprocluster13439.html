counterproductive

adjective
having the opposite effect to what was intended
5.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /kaʊn.tər.prə.ˈdʌk.tɪv/ /; / /kaʊn.ər.prə.ˈdʌk.tɪv/ (AmE), /kaʊn.tə.prə.ˈdʌk.tɪv/ (BrE)
having the opposite effect to the one intended, making a problem worse instead of better or preventing progress toward a goal

Examples

Counterproductive actions
Implementing too many new policies at once may prove counterproductive rather than helpful.
Skipping meals to save time can be counterproductive to your health and productivity.
Yelling at your team members is often counterproductive and can damage morale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: productive - producing or able to produce large amounts or results
