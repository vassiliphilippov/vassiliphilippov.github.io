pedder

noun
itinerant seller
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛdər / (AmE), / ˈpɛdə / (BrE)
an old term for a person who travels from place to place selling goods

Examples

A pedder with baskets
The pedder arrived in town carrying baskets filled with ribbons and buttons.
Villagers eagerly awaited the pedder who brought news and goods from distant places.
In olden days, a pedder was a common sight on country roads.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: merchant - a person who buys and sells goods in large quantities
  • synonym: peddler - a person who travels to sell goods, especially small items
