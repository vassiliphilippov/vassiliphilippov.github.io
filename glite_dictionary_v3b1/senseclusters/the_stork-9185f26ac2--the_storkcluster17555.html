the stork

noun
metaphorical reference to baby delivery
Pronunciation: / ðə ˈstɔrk / (AmE), / ðə ˈstɔːk / (BrE)
used humorously or euphemistically to refer to the arrival of a baby, especially as if delivered magically by a bird

Examples

The stork brings babies
As children, we were told that the stork brought our baby brother.
Their friends announced that the stork would be visiting them soon.
Whenever someone was pregnant, jokes about the stork delivering a bundle would circulate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stork - a large long-legged wading bird of the family Ciconiidae
