do-gooderism

noun
excessive concern for doing good, often disliked
9.9 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈduː.ɡʊd.ər.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈduː.ɡʊd.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The attitude or practice of showing excessive or self-righteous concern for the welfare of others, often regarded with disapproval.

Examples

Do-gooderism attitude
His speech was filled with the kind of do-gooderism that some listeners found insincere.
Many people in the town resented the outsider's do-gooderism and preferred to handle matters themselves.
The editorial accused the government of unnecessary do-gooderism in its social welfare policies.
