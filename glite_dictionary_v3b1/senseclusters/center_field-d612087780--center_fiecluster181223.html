center field

noun
the middle part of the outfield in baseball
1.8 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.tər fiːld/ (AmE), /ˈsen.tə fiːld/ (BrE)
the area of the outfield in baseball directly behind second base and ahead of the catcher, between left field and right field

Examples

A ball in center field
A speedy player should cover a lot of ground in center field.
The batter hit a sharp line drive into center field.
The outfielder caught the fly ball deep in center field.
