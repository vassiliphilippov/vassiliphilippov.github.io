prime interest rate

noun
lowest bank loan rate for top customers
5.4
Pronunciation: / praɪm ˈɪntrəst reɪt / (AmE), / praɪm ˈɪntrəst reɪt / (BrE)
the lowest interest rate at which banks lend to their most creditworthy commercial customers

Examples

The prime interest rate
Lowering the prime interest rate can stimulate economic growth by encouraging more lending.
The prime interest rate has remained steady for several months, according to the central bank.
When the prime interest rate rises, borrowing money becomes more expensive for businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: base rate - the interest rate set by the central bank, influencing lending rates
  • synonym: discount rate - interest rate charged by central banks to commercial banks
