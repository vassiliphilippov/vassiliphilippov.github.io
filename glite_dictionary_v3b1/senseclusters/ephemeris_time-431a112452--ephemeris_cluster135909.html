(Astronomy) Time measured by the orbital motion of the Earth rather than by its rotation, used to provide a uniform time scale for astronomical calculations.

Examples

Ephemeris time scale
Accurate calculations of planetary positions depend on ephemeris time rather than solar time.
Before the adoption of atomic time, astronomers relied on ephemeris time for precise observations.
The concept of ephemeris time was introduced to address the irregularities in Earth's rotation.
