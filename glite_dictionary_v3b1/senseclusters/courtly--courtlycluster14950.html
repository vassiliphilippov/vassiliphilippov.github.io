courtly

adjective
refined and dignified; suitable for a royal court
7.2 1.24/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îrtli / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀêtli / (BrE)
having a very refined, elegant, or dignified manner or appearance, especially in a way regarded as suitable for a royal court

Examples

Courtly manners
Despite his humble origins, his courtly behavior set him apart from others.
She gave him a courtly bow before leaving the room.
The ambassador's courtly manners impressed everyone at the banquet.

Synonyms

  • refined
    4.1 0.21/m
    refined shows polished taste and subtle elegance in style, while courtly suggests that same elegance but fits royal, formal settings.
  • stately
    5.7 2.70/m
    stately looks grand and slow-moving, like a palace entrance, while courtly focuses on elegant manners for royal company.
  • dignified
    4.6 4.30/m
    dignified shows calm, serious self-respect in bearing, while courtly adds extra elegance and behavior fit for a royal court.
  • genteel
    7.7 1.74/m
    genteel sounds polite and refined but often a bit affected, while courtly feels more genuinely elegant and suited to royalty.
  • decorous
    7.9 0.48/m
    decorous means proper, polite behavior in public, while courtly carries richer elegance and style meant for royal or formal circles.
