silver-leaved nettle

noun
nightshade plant with silvery leaves and curdling berry
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvɚ ˌliːvd ˈnɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌliːvd ˈnɛtəl / (BrE)
a weedy nightshade plant with silvery leaves, violet, blue, or white flowers, and a round berry used to curdle milk, found from central United States to South America

Examples

The silver-leaved nettle plant
Some gardeners appreciate the unique foliage of the silver-leaved nettle in ornamental plantings.
The silver-leaved nettle is well known among farmers for its use in curdling milk.
Wild patches of silver-leaved nettle can often be seen blooming along the riverbanks.
