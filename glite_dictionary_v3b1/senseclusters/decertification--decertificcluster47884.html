decertification

noun
removal of certification
Pronunciation: / diˌsɜrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsɜːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing official approval, recognition, or certification from something or someone

Examples

Official decertification
The hospital risked decertification after repeated safety violations were discovered.
Union members worried that decertification would affect their workplace rights and benefits.
The agency announced the decertification of several schools that failed to meet the required standards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certification - the process of officially recognizing someone or something as having met certain standards
  • synonym: revocation - the act of officially cancelling something
