virus

noun
harmful or corrupting influence (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
A harmful or corrupting influence that spreads, often used metaphorically to describe ideas or attitudes.

Examples

The virus of fear
She warned that the virus of cynicism could undermine the team's morale.
Some believe that hatred behaves like a virus, quietly spreading from person to person.
The virus of misinformation can quickly infect entire communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blight - something that has a damaging effect
  • synonym: plague - something that causes continual trouble or distress, especially when spreading
