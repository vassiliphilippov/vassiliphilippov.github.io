mismatch

verb
to match things or people badly
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈmætʃ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈmætʃ / (BrE)
to pair or combine things or people in a way that does not fit or match well together

Examples

To mismatch the parts
If you mismatch the plugs and outlets, the devices might not work.
She accidentally mismatched her socks this morning before work.
The decorator warned us not to mismatch colors when choosing paint for the room.
If you mismatch the socks, you might end up wearing two different colors.
The technician accidentally mismatched the cables, causing the device to malfunction.
Be careful not to mismatch the data when entering it into the system.

  • synonym: mix up - to confuse or combine things incorrectly
