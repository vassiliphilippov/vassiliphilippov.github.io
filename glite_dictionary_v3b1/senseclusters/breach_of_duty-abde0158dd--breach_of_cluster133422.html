breach of duty

noun
failure to fulfill a legal or moral obligation
8.7
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ əv ˈduːti / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
the failure to fulfill a legal or moral obligation to exercise due care or perform a required action

Examples

A serious breach of duty
A breach of duty can lead to legal consequences for professionals such as doctors or lawyers.
The court must determine whether there was a breach of duty in the actions of the defendant.
The plaintiff argued that the company committed a breach of duty by ignoring safety regulations.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: duty of care - the legal obligation to avoid causing harm
  • synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something (legal sense)
