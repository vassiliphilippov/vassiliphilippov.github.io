come out

verb
to become revealed or known
2.9 34/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm aÊŠt / (AmE), / kÊŒm aÊŠt / (BrE)
(of information, facts, or the truth) to be revealed or become known to people, especially after being hidden or secret.

Examples

The truth comes out
It's only now that the real reasons for his resignation have come out.
Once the truth comes out, people may see things very differently.
The full story will come out eventually, no matter how hard they try to hide it.
