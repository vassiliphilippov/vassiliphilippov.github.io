microlithic

adjective
fine-grained texture
9.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kroʊ.ˈlɪ.θɪk/ /; / /mɪ.kroʊ.ˈlɪ.θɪk/ (AmE), /maɪ.krə.ˈlɪ.θɪk/ /; / /mɪ.krə.ˈlɪ.θɪk/ (BrE)
having a very fine-grained or microcrystalline texture, composed of extremely small mineral crystals

Examples

Microlithic texture
The basalt rock was described as microlithic due to its tiny, barely visible crystals.
Under the microscope, the geologist identified the microlithic structure of the volcanic ash.
Many volcanic rocks have a microlithic texture, making them difficult to study without special equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphanitic - having a fine-grained texture in igneous rocks
  • antonym: phaneritic - having a coarse-grained texture in igneous rocks
