trill

noun
rapid tongue vibration in speech
4.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /trɪl/ (AmE), /trɪl/ (BrE)
a rapid vibration of the tongue or uvula when pronouncing certain consonants, especially 'r'

Examples

A clear trill in speech
Children sometimes struggle to produce a clear trill when first learning to speak.
Some English dialects do not use a pronounced trill for the 'r' sound.
The linguist demonstrated a perfect trill when pronouncing the Spanish word 'perro'.
