Arthur Schopenhauer

proper noun
German philosopher, pessimist (1788–1860)
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrθɚ ˈʃoʊpənˌhaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːθə ˈʃəʊpənˌhaʊə / (BrE)
(1788–1860) A German philosopher known for his pessimistic philosophy and influential works on metaphysics, ethics, and aesthetics.

Examples

Arthur Schopenhauer philosophy
Arthur Schopenhauer argued that human desires are never fully satisfied, leading to inevitable suffering.
Many students of philosophy study the works of Arthur Schopenhauer to understand pessimism.
The influence of Arthur Schopenhauer can be seen in the writings of later existentialist thinkers.
