graphospasm

noun
muscular spasms during writing
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræfoʊˌspæzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡræfəʊˌspæzəm / (BrE)
A condition characterized by muscular spasms of the thumb and forefinger while writing with a pen or pencil.

Examples

Graphospasm while writing
After years of continuous handwriting, she developed graphospasm that made it difficult to hold a pen.
Physical therapy helped reduce the frequency of graphospasm during long writing sessions.
The patient's graphospasm worsened whenever he tried to sign important documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: writer's cramp - a similar condition causing hand cramps during writing
