maraging

noun
nickel-rich steel alloy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrˌeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːrˌeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
a very strong steel alloy that is hardened by age-hardening and contains a high proportion of nickel

Examples

Aircraft maraging
The aircraft manufacturer used maraging in the construction of the landing gear for added strength.
Because of its toughness, maraging is often chosen for critical engineering components.
Engineers selected maraging to ensure the durability of the high-performance tool.
