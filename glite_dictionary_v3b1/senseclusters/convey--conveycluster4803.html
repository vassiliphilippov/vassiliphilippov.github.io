convey

verb
transport or carry from place to place
3.6 3.0
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈveɪ/ (AmE), /kən.ˈveɪ/ (BrE)
to take, carry, or transport someone or something from one place to another

Examples

To convey goods
Medical supplies were conveyed to the remote village by helicopter.
She conveys her children to school each morning before work.
The airport shuttle will convey passengers to the hotel every hour.
The pipes convey water from the reservoir to the city.
She struggled to convey her excitement about the new project to her colleagues.
A good teacher can convey complex ideas in a simple way.
