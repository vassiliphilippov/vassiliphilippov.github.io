feasibility

noun
the quality of being possible
4.5 3.76/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåfiÀêz…ôÀàb…™l…™ti / (AmE), / ÀåfiÀêz…ôÀàb…™l…™ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being possible or doable; how easily something can be achieved or put into practice

Examples

Project feasibility study
Before starting the project, we need to assess its feasibility.
The committee discussed the feasibility of expanding into international markets.
The engineer questioned the feasibility of building a bridge in that location.

Synonyms

  • workability
    4.8 0.19/m
    workability describes how well a plan can be used in practice, while feasibility is the general chance something can be done.
  • viability
    4.7 4.22/m
    viability means a thing can work successfully and be useful in practice, while feasibility is about being possible to do.
  • achievability
    5.1 0.03/m
    achievability is the state of being possible to achieve, while feasibility measures how practical or easy it is to put into action.
  • attainability
    5.5 0.03/m
    attainability is the possibility of reaching a goal or level, while feasibility focuses on whether it can be done in practice.
  • doability
    4.9 0.01/m
    doability names the informal idea that something can be done, while feasibility is the formal measure of practical possibility and effort.
