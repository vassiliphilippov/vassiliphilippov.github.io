hybridoma

noun
hybrid cell for producing monoclonal antibodies
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪbrɪˈdoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪbrɪˈdəʊmə / (BrE)
a hybrid cell produced by fusing a lymphocyte with a tumor cell, used to produce specific monoclonal antibodies in culture

Examples

A hybridoma cell
Once the hybridoma line was established, it was maintained to produce antibodies indefinitely.
Researchers created a hybridoma to generate targeted antibodies for the study.
The production of monoclonal antibodies relies on the fusion process that yields a hybridoma.
