ELINT

noun
electronic intelligence from electromagnetic emissions
9.9 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêÀål…™nt / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêÀål…™nt / (BrE)
intelligence gathered by detecting and analyzing electromagnetic emissions, excluding those from radioactive sources, typically for military or espionage purposes

Examples

ELINT sensors
Advanced aircraft are equipped with ELINT sensors to gather crucial battlefield information.
Analysis of intercepted ELINT reveals the presence of new missile defense systems.
The military used ELINT to monitor enemy radar installations along the border.

Synonyms

  • electronics intelligence
    6.4 0.03/m
    electronics intelligence is technical analysis of foreign electromagnetic emissions for intelligence, while ELINT names the military discipline that gathers and studies those emissions.
  • electronic reconnaissance
    8.1 0.00/m
    electronic reconnaissance is the detection and location of foreign electromagnetic signals, while ELINT is the intelligence product built from those collection activities.
