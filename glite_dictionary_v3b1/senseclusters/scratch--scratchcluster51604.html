scratch

noun
a shallow abrasion or wound on the skin
1.0 1.4
Pronunciation: / skrætʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ / (BrE)
a thin mark, wound, or shallow depression made by scraping or scoring a surface or skin with something sharp

Examples

A scratch on the arm
Although it bled a little, the scratch healed quickly.
She got a scratch on her arm after climbing the fence.
The cat left a long scratch on the back of my hand.
Despite the fall, her car only had a minor scratch on the bumper.
He tried to buff out the scratch on his phone screen, but it was still visible.
There was a noticeable scratch on the door after the cat tried to get in.
Careless handling left a deep scratch on the car door.
She ran her finger along the scratch on the wall, wondering how it got there.
The table has a noticeable scratch right in the center.
