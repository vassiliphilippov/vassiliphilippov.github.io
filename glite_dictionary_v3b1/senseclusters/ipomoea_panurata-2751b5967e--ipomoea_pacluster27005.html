Ipomoea panurata

noun
a tropical American starchy-rooted plant
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.poʊˈmiː.ə pəˈnjʊr.ə.tə / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə pəˈnjʊə.rɑː.tə / (BrE)
A tropical American creeping or climbing perennial plant with a very large starchy root, sometimes believed to be a source of the sweet potato.

Examples

Ipomoea panurata root
Ipomoea panurata is sometimes mistaken for the true sweet potato due to its similar root.
Botanists have recently discovered a wild population of Ipomoea panurata in the Amazon basin.
The giant tuber of Ipomoea panurata can be an important food source in local diets.
