Cnut

proper noun
early 11th-century king of Denmark, England, and Norway
Pronunciation: / kəˈnut, ˈkɑː.nut / (AmE), / kəˈnjuːt, ˈkɑː.njuːt / (BrE)
(c. 995–1035) King of Denmark, England, and Norway in the early 11th century, notable for establishing a North Sea empire.

Examples

King Cnut of England
Cnut is remembered for his rule over England, Denmark, Norway, and parts of Sweden.
Historians often discuss how Cnut maintained control over his vast empire.
The famous story of Cnut and the tide has been told for centuries.
