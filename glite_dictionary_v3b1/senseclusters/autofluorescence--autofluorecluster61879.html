autofluorescence

noun
natural emission of light by a material
4.0 8.8
Pronunciation: /ɑː.toʊ.flʊ.ˈres.əns/ /; / /ɔː.toʊ.flʊ.ˈres.əns/ (AmE), /ɔː.tə.flʊ.ˈres.əns/ /; / /ɔː.tə.flʊə.ˈres.əns/ (BrE)
The natural emission of light by biological structures or materials when they absorb light, without the addition of external fluorescent dyes.

Examples

Natural autofluorescence emission
Researchers must account for autofluorescence when imaging certain types of cells.
Some plant tissues exhibit strong autofluorescence under a fluorescence microscope.
The autofluorescence of skin can complicate the interpretation of diagnostic images.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluorescence - the emission of light by a substance that has absorbed light
