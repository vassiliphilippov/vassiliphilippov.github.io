Battle of Bull Run

noun
Civil War battles in Virginia, 1861 and 1862
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬əl əv bʊl ˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv bʊl ˈrʌn / (BrE)
either of two significant battles during the American Civil War, fought in 1861 and 1862 in Virginia, in which Confederate forces defeated the Union army

Examples

The Battle of Bull Run fight
Many historians believe the Battle of Bull Run shattered any illusions of a quick victory in the Civil War.
The outcome of the Battle of Bull Run boosted the Confederacy’s confidence early in the war.
Tourists often visit the site of the Battle of Bull Run to learn about American history.
