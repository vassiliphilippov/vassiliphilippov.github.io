carry back

verb
deduct a loss or credit from a prior period's taxes
5.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.rɪ bæk/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.rɪ bæk/ (BrE)
to deduct a loss or unused tax credit from taxable income of a prior period, for the purpose of reducing the tax liability for that period

Examples

To carry back a loss
If a company incurs a net operating loss, it may carry back the loss to offset taxable income from the previous year.
The accountant advised us to carry back our unused tax credits to maximize refunds.
They carried back the loss, resulting in a substantial tax reduction for the firm.
