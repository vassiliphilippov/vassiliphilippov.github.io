sandbar

noun
narrow strip of sand formed by water
2.8 1.14/m

A long, narrow strip of sand in a river or along the shore, formed by the action of water currents.

Examples

A sandbar in river
At low tide, a sandbar stretches between the island and the mainland, allowing people to walk across.
Boats must be careful not to run aground on the sandbar near the river mouth.
Children built castles on the sandbar that emerged during the afternoon.

Synonyms

    3.2 0.10/m

  • batture
    9.9 0.01/m
    a batture is a strip of river sand that appears at low water, while a sandbar is a long, narrow sandbank formed by currents.
