radiogenic

adjective
from radioactive decay
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
produced by or relating to the process of radioactive decay

Examples

Radiogenic heat
The Earth's core generates radiogenic heat from the decay of radioactive elements.
Scientists study radiogenic isotopes to understand the age of rocks and minerals.
The presence of radiogenic gases can indicate ongoing radioactive decay deep underground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radioactive - emitting radiation as a result of nuclear decay
