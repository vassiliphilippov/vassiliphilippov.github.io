die

noun
tool for cutting threads on bolts or pipes
Pronunciation: / daɪ / (AmE), / daɪ / (BrE)
a tool or device used for cutting or shaping material, especially metal, in manufacturing

Examples

A metal die
He adjusted the die in the diestock before starting the threading process.
The machinist selected a die to thread the end of the metal rod.
You need a new die if you want to cut threads on those pipes.
A special die is required to cut threads into the pipe.
After being pressed in the die, the sheet metal took on its final shape.
The metal parts were produced using a steel die to ensure consistent dimensions.
