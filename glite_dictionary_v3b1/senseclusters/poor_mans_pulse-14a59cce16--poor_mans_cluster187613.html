poor man's pulse

noun
edible legume crop grown in India
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊr ˌmænz ˈpʌls / (AmE), / ˈpɔː ˌmanz ˈpʌls / (BrE)
a twining herb (Vigna aconitifolia) cultivated in India as a food and fodder crop, especially in arid regions

Examples

Poor man's pulse plant
Farmers often grow poor man's pulse because it thrives in poor, dry soils where other crops fail.
In many recipes, poor man's pulse is used as a substitute for more expensive lentils.
The drought-tolerant poor man's pulse is an important staple in rural Indian diets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moth bean - another common name for Vigna aconitifolia, the same species
