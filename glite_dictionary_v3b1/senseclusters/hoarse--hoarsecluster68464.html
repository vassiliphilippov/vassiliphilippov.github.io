hoarse

adjective
rough, harsh-sounding voice
3.4 2.62/m
Pronunciation: / h…îrs / (AmE), / h…îÀês / (BrE)
(of a voice) sounding rough and harsh, typically as a result of a sore throat, shouting, or strong emotion.

Examples

A hoarse voice
After catching a cold, Mark's hoarse cough worried his friends.
His voice sounded hoarse after cheering loudly at the match.
She spoke in a hoarse whisper that barely carried across the room.

Synonyms

    5.3 1.54/m

    croaky sounds thin and frog-like with a rasp, while hoarse sounds rough and harsh from shouting, illness, or strong feeling.
  • raspy
    5.8 1.16/m
    raspy is harsh and grating like sandpaper on the ear, while hoarse is rough and caused by strain or illness.
  • gravelly
    6.5 0.62/m
    gravelly is low and unpleasantly rough, while hoarse is a rough, harsh voice often due to a sore throat or shouting.
  • throaty
    6.1 0.93/m

  • harsh-voiced
    6.8 0.00/m
