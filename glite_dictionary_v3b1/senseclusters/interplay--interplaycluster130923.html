interplay

verb
to interact mutually
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to interact or have an effect on each other, especially in a way that influences the outcome

Examples

Flavors interplay well
The flavors of sweet and sour interplay perfectly in this dish.
In a good conversation, ideas interplay and lead to new insights.
Light and color interplay to create a beautiful effect in the painting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cooperate - to work together towards a common goal
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to or not interact with
  • synonym: interact - to act on or influence each other
