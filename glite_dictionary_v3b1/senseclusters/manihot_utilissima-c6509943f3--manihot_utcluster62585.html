Manihot utilissima

noun
tropical root plant; source of tapioca
9.9
Pronunciation: /məˈnɪ.hɑːt juː.tɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.mə/ /; / /ˈmæ.nɪ.hɑːt juː.tɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.mə/ (AmE), /məˈnɪ.hɒt juː.tɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.mə/ /; / /ˈmæ.nɪ.hɒt juː.tɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.mə/ (BrE)
a tropical plant with long, tuberous edible roots and brittle stems, cultivated for its starchy roots used in foods like tapioca and in certain alcoholic drinks

Examples

Manihot utilissima plant
Farmers in the region grow Manihot utilissima for its nutritious roots that are turned into flour.
The brittle stems of Manihot utilissima make it easy to propagate new plants.
Traditional dishes often use Manihot utilissima to produce tapioca and other starchy foods.
