divertimento

noun
a light, multi-movement instrumental composition
8.6
Pronunciation: / dəˌvɜrtɪˈmɛntoʊ / (AmE), / dɪˌvɛːtɪˈmɛntəʊ / (BrE)
a light, instrumental musical composition, often in several movements, typically for a small ensemble and having no fixed form

Examples

A divertimento piece
Although the divertimento has no strict structure, it usually consists of three or more movements.
Mozart composed a famous divertimento for strings that is still frequently performed in concerts.
The ensemble concluded the evening with a cheerful divertimento by Haydn.
