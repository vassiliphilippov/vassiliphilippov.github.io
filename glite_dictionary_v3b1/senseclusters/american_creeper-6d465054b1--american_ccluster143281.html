American creeper

noun
North American songbird with down-curved bill
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈkɹipər / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈkɹiːpə / (BrE)
a small North American songbird with a slender, down-curved bill, known for creeping along tree trunks in search of insects

Examples

The American creeper bird
Birdwatchers frequently spot the American creeper in mixed woodlands across the United States and Canada.
The American creeper is often seen spiraling up tree trunks in search of insects.
Unlike woodpeckers, the American creeper relies on its slender bill to probe bark for food.
