poignant

adjective
evoking sadness or distress
4.4 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪnjənt / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnjənt / (BrE)
evoking a strong feeling of sadness or deep emotion; deeply moving

Examples

A poignant moment
Her speech was especially poignant given the recent events in the community.
The film’s poignant ending left many people in tears.
There is a poignant beauty in memories of childhood.
She felt a poignant sense of nostalgia as she walked through her childhood home.
The film's ending was so poignant that many viewers were moved to tears.
There is a poignant beauty in the way the story captures loss and hope.

  • antonym: unaffecting - not evoking any strong emotion or feeling
