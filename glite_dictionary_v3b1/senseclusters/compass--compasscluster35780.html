compass

verb
travel around something (esp. the world)
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.pəs / (BrE)
to travel around or make a complete journey around (something, often the world), especially by ship or plane

Examples

To compass the world
He dreamed that one day he would compass the world, documenting every place he visited.
In ancient times, explorers sought to compass the globe in search of new lands.
The team planned to compass the island by boat before making landfall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circumnavigate - to sail or travel all the way around something, especially the world
