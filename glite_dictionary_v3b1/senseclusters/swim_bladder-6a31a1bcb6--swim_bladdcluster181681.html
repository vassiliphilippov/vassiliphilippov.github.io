swim bladder

noun
buoyancy organ in fish
Pronunciation: / swɪm ˈblædɚ / (AmE), / swɪm ˈblædə / (BrE)
an air-filled sac in many fish that helps them control their buoyancy in water

Examples

A swim bladder in fish
If a swim bladder is damaged, a fish may have trouble staying upright or floating normally.
Many fish rely on their swim bladder to stay at a particular depth without using much energy.
The evolution of the swim bladder has allowed bony fish to become efficient swimmers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: air bladder - another term for swim bladder or a similar air-filled sac in aquatic animals
