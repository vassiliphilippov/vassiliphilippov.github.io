recirculate

verb
to move fluid again
4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.sɜːr.kjə.leɪt/ /; / /ˈriː.sɜːr.kjʊ.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈriː.sə.kjʊ.leɪt/ /; / /ˈriː.sɜː.kjʊ.leɪt/ (BrE)
to move a liquid, gas, or air through a system again so it can be reused or kept clean

Examples

To recirculate the air
The aquarium filter will recirculate the water to keep the fish healthy.
Modern heating systems recirculate warm air throughout the house for better efficiency.
To save energy, some cars recirculate exhaust gases back into the engine.
The water pump is designed to recirculate the pool water to keep it clean.
Modern ventilation systems recirculate air to improve energy efficiency in buildings.
Some fish tanks recirculate water to maintain a healthy environment for the fish.
The air conditioning unit will recirculate the air to maintain a comfortable temperature.
Modern cars often recirculate cabin air to improve efficiency and reduce outside pollution.
To save water, the system is designed to recirculate it through multiple cleaning cycles.
