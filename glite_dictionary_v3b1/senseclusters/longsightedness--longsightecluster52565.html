longsightedness

noun
vision condition: distant clearer than near
4.2 6.4
Pronunciation: /lɑːŋ.ˈsaɪ.tɪd.nəs/ /; / /lɔːŋ.ˈsaɪ.tɪd.nəs/ (AmE), /lɒŋ.ˈsaɪ.tɪd.nəs/ (BrE)
a condition of the eyes in which distant objects are seen more clearly than near ones; hyperopia

Examples

Longsightedness in eyes
Her longsightedness made it difficult to read small print without glasses.
People with longsightedness often squint when doing close-up work.
Regular eye tests can help detect longsightedness early.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farsightedness - abnormal condition in which vision for distant objects is better than for near objects
  • antonym: nearsightedness - condition where near objects appear clearer than distant ones (myopia)
