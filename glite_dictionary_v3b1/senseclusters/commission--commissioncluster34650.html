commission

noun
formal written order or command
4.7 4.0
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈmɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
an official order, assignment, or the act of granting authority (including the document that gives someone authority or rank)

Examples

A royal commission
He was given a royal commission to serve as ambassador.
The general received her commission in a ceremony at the palace.
The president issued a commission authorizing the treaty negotiations.
After accepting the commission, he traveled abroad to oversee the construction project.
She was given a commission to investigate the financial irregularities within the company.
The government set up a commission to study climate change and recommend policy changes.
After years of dedication, she finally received her commission as a lieutenant in the army.
He proudly displayed his commission signed by the president above his desk.
The ceremony marked the official granting of commissions to the newly graduated cadets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone
  • synonym: assignment - a task or piece of work assigned to someone
  • antonym: decommission - to officially remove from service or withdraw authority
  • antonym: discharge - the removal of a person from office or armed forces
  • synonym: instruction - detailed direction or order
  • synonym: mandate - an official order to do something
  • synonym: mission - an important assignment given to a person or group
