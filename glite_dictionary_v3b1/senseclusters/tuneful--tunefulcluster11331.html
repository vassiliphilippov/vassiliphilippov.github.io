tuneful

adjective
having a pleasant, melodic sound
3.2 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀên.f…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàtjuÀên.f…ôl / (BrE)
having a pleasant and melodic sound; full of tune or melody

Examples

A tuneful song
She always hums tuneful tunes while working in the kitchen.
That was a particularly tuneful melody played on the violin.
The choir's tuneful voices echoed through the cathedral.

Synonyms

  • melodious
    4.0 0.56/m
    melodious is clearly musical and easy to hum or sing, while tuneful means having a pleasant melody or tune.
  • mellifluous
    8.5 0.35/m
    mellifluous is smoothly sweet and gentle to hear, while tuneful stresses having a clear melody or tune.
  • dulcet
    8.9 0.02/m
    dulcet is sweet and soothing like soft singing, while tuneful focuses on a strong, clear melody or tune.
  • euphonous
    8.9 0.00/m
    euphonous sounds formally pleasing and balanced, while tuneful means simply that music or singing has a clear melody.
