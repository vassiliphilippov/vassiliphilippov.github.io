neocolonial

adjective
indirect foreign control
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊkəˈloʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊkəˈləʊniəl / (BrE)
relating to indirect political, economic, or cultural control by one country over another, especially after formal colonialism has ended

Examples

Neocolonial influence
Many activists criticize neocolonial policies that keep developing countries economically dependent.
The company's investments were seen as a form of neocolonial influence in the region.
Some leaders argue that international aid can sometimes have neocolonial effects on local governance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anti-colonial - opposed to colonial rule or influence
  • synonym: imperialist - relating to extending a country's power through colonization or use of force
