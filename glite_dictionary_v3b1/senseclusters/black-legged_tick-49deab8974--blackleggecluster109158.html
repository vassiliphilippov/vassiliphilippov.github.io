black-legged tick

noun
tick species, vector of Lyme disease
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk ˌlɛɡd tɪk / (AmE), / ˈblæk ˌlɛɡd tɪk / (BrE)
A small parasitic tick, found in eastern North America, known for transmitting Lyme disease to humans.

Examples

A black-legged tick bite
A black-legged tick can transmit Lyme disease if it remains attached to the skin for several hours.
People hiking in New England should check for black-legged ticks after spending time outdoors.
Scientists studied the life cycle of the black-legged tick in its natural habitat.
