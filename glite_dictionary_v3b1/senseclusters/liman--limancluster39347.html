liman

noun
long narrow lagoon at a river's mouth
6.3
Pronunciation: / lɪˈmɑn / (AmE), / lɪˈmæn / (BrE)
a long, narrow lagoon formed at the mouth of a river, typically blocked off from the sea by a sandbar or spit

Examples

A liman lagoon
Geographers classify a liman as a type of coastal lagoon.
Many rivers along the Black Sea coast end in a liman formed by sandbars.
The biodiversity of a liman makes it important for local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lagoon - a shallow body of water separated from a larger sea by sandbars or coral reefs
