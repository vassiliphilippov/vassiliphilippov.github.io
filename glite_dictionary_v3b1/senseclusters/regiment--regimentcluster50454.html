regiment

verb
assign to a regiment (military)
4.6 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈre.dʒɪ.mənt/ /; / /ˈre.dʒə.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈre.dʒɪ.mənt/ /; / /ˈre.dʒə.mənt/ (BrE)
to organize people, especially soldiers, into regiments or structured military units

Examples

To regiment new soldiers
During wartime, it is common to regiment large groups of volunteers into structured units.
He was regimented into the infantry soon after joining the army.
The government decided to regiment the newly recruited soldiers for better organization.
During basic training, new soldiers are quickly regimented and taught discipline.
The general decided to regiment the recruits into three separate units.
They began to regiment volunteers as soon as the order was given.
