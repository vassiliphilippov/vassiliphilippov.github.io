CPI

noun
California Psychological Inventory, a personality test
Pronunciation: / si pi aɪ / (AmE), / siː piː aɪ / (BrE)
a standardized self-report questionnaire used to assess personality traits and behaviors, known as the California Psychological Inventory.

Examples

CPI personality test
Many corporate assessment programs include the CPI as part of their evaluation process.
Researchers often use the CPI to assess leadership qualities in candidates.
The psychologist administered the CPI to better understand the patient's behavioral tendencies.
