scrape

verb
injure or damage a surface by rubbing
1.2
Pronunciation: / skreɪp / (AmE), / skreɪp / (BrE)
to injure or damage the skin by rubbing it against something rough or hard

Examples

To scrape your knee
Be careful not to scrape your knees when you climb that tree.
He scraped his hand on the rough wall.
She scraped her elbow when she slipped on the pavement.
Be careful not to scrape your bottom when getting out of the pool.
He lost his balance on the playground and scraped his bottom on the ground.
If you slide down too quickly, you might scrape your bottom on the rough surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: graze - to scrape or break the surface of the skin
  • synonym: scratch - to make a slight cut or mark on a surface, especially skin, with something sharp
