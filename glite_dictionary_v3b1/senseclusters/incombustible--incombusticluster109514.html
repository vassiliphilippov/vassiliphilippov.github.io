incombustible

adjective
not able to burn
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəmˈbʌstəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈbʌstəbl / (BrE)
not able to catch fire or burn; incapable of combustion

Examples

An incombustible fabric
Because the fabric is incombustible, it is ideal for use in public spaces.
The walls of the laboratory were made from incombustible materials to ensure safety.
They replaced the old wooden roof with incombustible tiles to meet fire regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: combustible - able to catch fire and burn easily
