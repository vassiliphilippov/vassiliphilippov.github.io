flutey

adjective
flute-like shape
7.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfluÀêti / (AmE), / ÀàfluÀêti / (BrE)
having the shape, sound, or surface features resembling a flute, especially being long, narrow, or marked by shallow vertical grooves.

Examples

Flutey grooves
The ancient column's flutey grooves cast deep shadows as the sun moved across the sky.
She admired the flutey design of the vase, which made it look elegant and classical.
The pastry had a flutey edge, giving it a decorative and appealing appearance.

Synonyms

  • flute-like
    4.3 0.01/m
    flute-like is long, narrow, and often marked by parallel grooves, while flutey covers shape, sound, or surface resembling a flute.
  • fluty
    6.1 0.01/m
    fluty is clear, high, and musical in sound like a flute, while flutey focuses more on shape or surface grooves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grooved - having long, narrow cuts or lines on the surface
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from grooves
