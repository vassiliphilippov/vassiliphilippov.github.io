club moss

noun
primitive moss-like spore-bearing plant
Pronunciation: / klʌb mɑs / (AmE), / klʌb mɒs / (BrE)
a primitive evergreen plant that resembles moss but produces spores in club-shaped cones (strobiles), belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

Examples

Club moss plant
A single club moss can release thousands of spores from its club-shaped cones.
Scientists study club mosses to learn about the evolution of land plants.
The forest floor was covered with dense patches of club moss thriving in the shade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lycopodium - a genus of club mosses
