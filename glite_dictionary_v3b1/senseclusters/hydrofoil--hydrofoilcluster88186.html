hydrofoil

noun
a lifting surface for vessels in water
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdroʊˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəʊˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
a structure shaped like a flat or curved plate that lifts a boat or similar object as it moves through water, reducing drag and allowing higher speeds

Examples

Hydrofoil wing
Engineers tested a redesigned hydrofoil to improve the boat's speed and efficiency.
Fish fins can act as natural hydrofoils to help them maneuver quickly in the water.
The new ferry is equipped with a hydrofoil that enables it to glide smoothly above the waves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airfoil - a structure shaped to produce lift when moving through air
