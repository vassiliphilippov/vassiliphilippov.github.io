naturalism

noun
19th-century French realist art/literary movement
3.3 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈnæ.tʃə.rəl.ɪz.əm/ /; / /ˈnæ.tʃ.rəl.ɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ˈnæ.tʃə.rəl.ɪz.əm/ /; / /ˈnæ.tʃ.rəl.ɪz.əm/ (BrE)
A style and movement in art and literature in 19th-century France that aimed for highly detailed, realistic, and factual representation of life.

Examples

Naturalism in painting
Many artists rejected romanticism in favor of naturalism during that era.
The arrival of naturalism transformed the way French painters depicted everyday life.
Émile Zola's novels are closely associated with naturalism in literature.
