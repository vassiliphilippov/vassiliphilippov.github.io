mount a campaign

verb
to launch or begin an organized campaign
Pronunciation: / maʊnt ə kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / maʊnt ə kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
to organize and begin a planned series of activities aimed at achieving a particular result, especially in politics, business, or social causes

Examples

To mount a campaign successfully
In 2015, she successfully mounted a campaign to promote literacy in rural areas.
Several organizations joined forces to mount a campaign against the proposed law.
The candidate decided to mount a campaign to raise awareness about climate change.
