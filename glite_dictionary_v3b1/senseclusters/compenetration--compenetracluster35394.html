compenetration

noun
deep merging
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of two or more things deeply merging, interpenetrating, or permeating each other while retaining their identities

Examples

Compenetration of cultures
The compenetration of different musical styles gave the album a unique sound.
In the sculpture, the compenetration of shapes symbolizes harmony between nature and technology.
The artist achieved a sense of unity through the compenetration of light and shadow.
The compenetration of oil and water in an emulsion allows both to coexist in a stable mixture.
Artists sometimes discuss the compenetration of colors when describing how different hues blend yet remain distinct.
In certain materials, the compenetration of fibers increases both flexibility and strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fusion - the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity
  • synonym: interpenetration - the process of things passing into or through each other
  • antonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart
