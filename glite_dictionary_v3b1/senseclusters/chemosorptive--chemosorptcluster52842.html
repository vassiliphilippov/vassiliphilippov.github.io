chemosorptive

adjective
adsorbing by chemical forces
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛm.oʊ.sɔːrˈsɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɛm.əʊ.sɔːpˈtɪv / (BrE)
having the ability to adsorb substances through chemical rather than physical forces

Examples

A chemosorptive surface
A chemosorptive surface can selectively capture contaminants based on their reactivity.
Certain filters are designed to be chemosorptive, attracting molecules through chemical bonding.
The chemosorptive properties of the material make it highly effective for removing pollutants from the air.

Related Terms

  • antonym: physisorptive - having the capacity to adsorb by physical forces
