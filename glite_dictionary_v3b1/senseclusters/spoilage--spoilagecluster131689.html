spoilage

noun
process or result of being spoiled
2.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈspɔɪ.lɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈspɔɪ.lə.dʒ/ (AmE), /ˈspɔɪ.lɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈspɔɪ.lə.dʒ/ (BrE)
the process or result of something (especially food) becoming spoiled, ruined, or unfit for use, usually because of decay, damage, or contamination

Examples

Food spoilage
Food spoilage is a major concern for grocery stores.

The spoilage of fresh fruit can be reduced by refrigeration.

High temperatures can accelerate the spoilage of perishable goods.
The company reduced food spoilage by improving storage conditions.
They monitored the spoilage rate to minimize financial losses.
