Castor fiber

noun
Eurasian species of beaver
Pronunciation: / ˈkæs.tɔr ˈfaɪ.bər / (AmE), / ˈkæs.tɔː ˈfaɪ.bə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Eurasian beaver, a large semi-aquatic rodent native to Europe and northern Asia

Examples

Castor fiber beaver
Conservationists have reintroduced Castor fiber into several rivers in the United Kingdom.
The Eurasian beaver, or Castor fiber, was once widespread across Europe.
Unlike Castor canadensis, Castor fiber has a narrower tail and lighter fur.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Castor canadensis - the North American beaver, a related but distinct species
  • synonym: beaver - large, semiaquatic rodent known for building dams and lodges
