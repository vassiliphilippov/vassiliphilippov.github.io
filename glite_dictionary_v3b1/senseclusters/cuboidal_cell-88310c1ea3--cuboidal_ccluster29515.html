cuboidal cell

noun
cube-shaped epithelial cell
4.6 6.3
Pronunciation: / kjuːˈbɔɪ.dl̩ sɛl / (AmE), / kjuːˈbɔɪ.dəl sɛl / (BrE)
an epithelial cell that has a roughly cube-shaped appearance

Examples

cuboidal cells in glands
Damage to cuboidal cells can disrupt glandular function.
Each cuboidal cell has a centrally located nucleus and even proportions.
Under the microscope, the cuboidal cell layer forms the lining of small ducts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: columnar cell - an epithelial cell that is taller than it is wide, resembling a column
  • antonym: squamous cell - an epithelial cell that is flat and scale-like
