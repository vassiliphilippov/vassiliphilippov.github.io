pay lip service

verb
pretend support without real action
9.5 5.8
Pronunciation: /peɪ lɪp ˈsɜːr.vɪs/ /; / /peɪ lɪp ˈsɜːr.vəs/ (AmE), /peɪ lɪp ˈsɜː.vɪs/ /; / /peɪ lɪp ˈsɜː.vəs/ (BrE)
to express approval or support for something without intending to act on it or put it into practice

Examples

To pay lip service to ideas
Many companies pay lip service to diversity but fail to implement real change.
She always pays lip service to environmental issues during elections.
The government paid lip service to education reform without increasing funding.
