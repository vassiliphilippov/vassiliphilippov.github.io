helot

noun
serf or person in servitude
9.9 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ›lÉ™t / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ›lÉ™t / (BrE)
A member of a class of serfs in ancient Sparta, or more generally, a person in servitude or bound to the land, especially in a feudal system.

Examples

The helots of Sparta
During the reign of Sparta, the helots worked the land for their Spartan masters.
He was treated like a helot, with no rights or freedoms of his own.
The Middle Ages saw many peasants reduced to the status of a helot in feudal society.

Synonyms

  • serf
    9.4 1.61/m
    serf is a medieval peasant bound to a lord's land and cannot leave without permission, while a helot is a Spartan bonded labourer.
  • villein
    9.9 0.04/m
    villein is a medieval peasant legally tied to a manor with few rights, while a helot is the Spartan class kept in servitude.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeman - a person who is not a serf or slave; one who has full civil rights
  • synonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system to work on his lord's estate
  • synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and is forced to obey them
