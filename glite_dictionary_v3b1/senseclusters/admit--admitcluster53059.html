admit

verb
allow to join a group or community
2.2 2.4
Pronunciation: /əd.ˈmɪt/ (AmE), /əd.ˈmɪt/ (BrE)
to allow someone to join or become a member of a group, organization, or institution

Examples

To admit a new member
After much debate, the board decided to admit her into the professional association.
It can be difficult to admit new members into a private club.
The university will admit 200 new students this semester.
He was finally admitted to the elite university after years of effort.
Only certified doctors are admitted to practice medicine in this country.
She hopes to be admitted to the engineering society next year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exclude - to prevent someone from entering or being a member
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with
