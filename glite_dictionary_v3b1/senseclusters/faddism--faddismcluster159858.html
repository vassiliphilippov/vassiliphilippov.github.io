faddism

noun
following trends
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfædɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfædɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of enthusiastically following temporary and popular trends or fads

Examples

Faddism in fashion
The sudden popularity of certain diets is often a result of faddism in society.
Critics argue that faddism prevents people from making lasting lifestyle changes.
His interest in new hobbies rarely lasts, showing a tendency toward faddism.
The sudden popularity of the diet was just another example of faddism in the health industry.
Critics argue that faddism distracts from more meaningful and lasting cultural changes.
Many products succeed for a short time due to faddism before quickly disappearing from the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fad - a fashion or interest that is very popular for a short time
  • antonym: individualism - the practice of being independent and not following trends
  • antonym: tradition - a long-established custom or belief
  • synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
  • synonym: trendiness - the quality of being fashionable or up-to-date with trends
