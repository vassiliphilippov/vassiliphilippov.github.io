reaccommodate

verb
to adjust again
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈkɑːməˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈkɒməˌdeɪt / (BrE)
to adjust, arrange, or provide a new place or plan for someone or something after a change or disruption

Examples

To reaccommodate a passenger
After returning from a long trip, it took her weeks to reaccommodate to her daily routine.
The company helped employees reaccommodate after the office moved to a new city.
It can be difficult for children to reaccommodate to school after a long summer break.
After the flight was delayed, the airline tried to reaccommodate all the passengers on later flights.
The hotel staff had to reaccommodate guests when several rooms became unavailable due to maintenance.
When the speaker canceled, the organizers quickly reaccommodated the conference schedule.
After the conference room was double-booked, the staff worked quickly to reaccommodate the guests.
The hotel promised to reaccommodate all travelers whose reservations were affected by the renovation.
When the train was canceled, the company tried to reaccommodate passengers on later departures.
