emancipate

verb
grant equal rights or set free
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmæn.sɪˌpeɪt /; / ɪˈmænsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmæn.sɪ.peɪt /; / ɪˈmansɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to set someone free from legal, social, or political restrictions; to give someone equal rights or freedom, especially from slavery or oppression

Examples

To emancipate slaves
It took many years of activism to finally emancipate those who had been oppressed.
Several organizations fought to emancipate minorities from discriminatory laws.
The law was designed to emancipate women and grant them equal rights in the workplace.
Efforts to emancipate the oppressed continued throughout the century.
She fought tirelessly to emancipate women from oppressive laws.
The proclamation would emancipate all slaves in the rebel states.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enslave - to make someone a slave or deprive of freedom
  • synonym: liberate - to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression
