malinvestment

noun
unsound investment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmælɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ˌmælɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
investment of money or resources in a way that is unproductive, wasteful, or ultimately unprofitable, often because of misleading signals or poor judgment

Examples

A malinvestment in housing
The housing bubble was fueled by years of malinvestment encouraged by low interest rates.
Analysts warned that government subsidies could lead to malinvestment in unprofitable industries.
When the market corrected, many companies exposed their malinvestment and suffered heavy losses.
The housing bubble led to widespread malinvestment in properties that were never sold.
Economists warned that government subsidies could encourage malinvestment in unproductive industries.
After the market crash, many empty office buildings stood as evidence of malinvestment.
The financial crisis was worsened by years of malinvestment in risky assets.
Government subsidies sometimes lead to malinvestment in industries that cannot compete globally.
Economists warn that easy credit can encourage malinvestment and create economic bubbles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: investment - the act of putting money into something to earn a profit
  • synonym: misallocation - the incorrect or inefficient distribution of resources
