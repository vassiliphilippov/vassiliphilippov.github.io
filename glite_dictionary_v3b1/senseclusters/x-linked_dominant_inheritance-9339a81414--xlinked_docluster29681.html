X-linked dominant inheritance

noun
type of genetic inheritance involving the X chromosome
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɛks lɪŋkt dəˈmɪnənt ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (AmE), / ˈɛks lɪŋkt dəˈmɪnənt ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (BrE)
A hereditary pattern in which a dominant gene on the X chromosome results in a characteristic being expressed in offspring who inherit the gene.

Examples

X-linked dominant inheritance pattern
A genetic counselor explained how X-linked dominant inheritance can influence the risk of certain conditions in families.
In cases of X-linked dominant inheritance, the disease is more likely to occur in female than male offspring.
Some rare disorders are passed from parent to child through X-linked dominant inheritance, affecting both sons and daughters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: X-linked recessive inheritance - hereditary pattern where a recessive gene on the X chromosome is expressed
