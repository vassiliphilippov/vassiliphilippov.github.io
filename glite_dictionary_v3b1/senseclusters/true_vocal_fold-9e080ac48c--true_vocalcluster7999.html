true vocal fold

noun
lower vocal cords in the larynx producing sound
7.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / tru Ààvo äk…ôl fo äld / (AmE), / truÀê Ààv…ô äk…ôl f…ô äld / (BrE)
either of the two lower, ligamentous folds in the larynx that produce vocal tones when they are brought together and air passes between them

Examples

true vocal fold vibration
Damage to a true vocal fold can result in hoarseness and vocal fatigue.
During laryngoscopy, the doctor examined the patient's true vocal folds for signs of swelling.
Proper function of the true vocal folds is essential for normal speech and singing.

Synonyms

  • vocal cord
    3.5 1.67/m
    vocal cord is the common everyday name for the vibrating voice fold, while true vocal fold is the precise anatomical lower ligamentous fold.
  • vocal fold
    6.3 0.48/m
    vocal fold names the general mucous folds in the larynx, while true vocal fold means the lower ligamentous folds that produce vocal tones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: false vocal fold - upper pair of vocal folds in the larynx that do not produce sound
  • synonym: vocal cord - either of the folds in the larynx involved in sound production
