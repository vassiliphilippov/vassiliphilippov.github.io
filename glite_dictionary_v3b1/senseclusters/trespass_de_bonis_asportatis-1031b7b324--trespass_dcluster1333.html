trespass de bonis asportatis

noun
legal action for wrongful taking of goods
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɛs.pæs di ˌboʊ.nɪs æsˌpɔrˈteɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌtrɛs.pæs deɪ ˌbəʊ.nɪs æsˌpɔːˈteɪtɪs / (BrE)
A legal action to recover damages from someone who has wrongfully taken goods or property from the rightful owner.

Examples

Trespass de bonis asportatis legal action
Trespass de bonis asportatis provided a remedy when someone carried off another's property without permission.
Historically, trespass de bonis asportatis was used to protect possession of personal property.
The plaintiff filed a suit for trespass de bonis asportatis after his goods were unlawfully removed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conversion - the civil wrong of taking someone else's property
