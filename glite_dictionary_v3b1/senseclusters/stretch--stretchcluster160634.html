stretch

verb
extend scope or meaning
2.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /stretʃ/ (AmE), /stretʃ/ (BrE)
to extend or broaden the meaning, use, or amount of something, often beyond what is reasonable; to exaggerate, dilute, or make something last longer

Examples

To stretch the truth
Don’t stretch my patience any further with your excuses.
He tried to stretch the definition of success to include his own results.
The author likes to stretch the boundaries of traditional storytelling.
During hard times, families would stretch their meals with bread or rice.
The chef decided to stretch the sauce by mixing in some water.
We managed to stretch the stew by adding extra potatoes.
In hard times, he would stretch his supplies to make them last longer, often at the expense of quality.
Some unscrupulous traders stretch olive oil by mixing it with cheaper oils.
The bartender was caught trying to stretch the whiskey with water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adulterate - to make something poorer in quality by adding another substance
  • synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as being larger or greater than it really is
  • synonym: extend - to make something longer or larger in amount or degree
  • antonym: limit - to restrict or keep within boundaries
