burn that bridge

verb
to eliminate an option (often humorously)
Pronunciation: / bɝn ðæt brɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɜːn ðæt brɪdʒ / (BrE)
(used humorously) to destroy a relationship, opportunity, or future option, often when the issue arises, echoing the phrase 'cross that bridge when you come to it'.

Examples

To burn that bridge now
He always says we'll burn that bridge when we come to it, rather than plan ahead.
If you quit your job without notice, you might burn that bridge for future opportunities.
She laughed and said she’d burn that bridge when it became a real problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn one's bridges - to make a decision that cannot be reversed, eliminating the possibility of return
