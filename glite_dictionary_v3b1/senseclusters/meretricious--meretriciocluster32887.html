meretricious

adjective
deceptively attractive or insincere
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛrəˈtrɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛrɪˈtrɪʃəs / (BrE)
attracting attention in a flashy or deceptive way, but lacking real value or honesty

Examples

Meretricious jewelry
Her meretricious smile masked a lack of genuine warmth.
Many critics dismissed the film as a meretricious attempt to win awards through empty spectacle.
The politician's meretricious promises failed to convince skeptical voters.
Critics described the production as meretricious rather than genuinely innovative.
Her jewelry looked expensive but was actually quite meretricious upon closer inspection.
The parade was filled with meretricious floats covered in glitter and neon lights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flashy - ostentatiously attractive or impressive
  • synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • antonym: genuine - authentic and sincere, not false or deceptive
  • antonym: tasteful - showing good aesthetic judgment or appropriate style
