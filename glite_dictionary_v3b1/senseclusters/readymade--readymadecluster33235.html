readymade

adjective
conventional or formulaic
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdiˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdiˌmeɪd / (BrE)
lacking originality or creativity; conventional, formulaic, or prepared in advance for use

Examples

Readymade phrases
The movie was full of readymade jokes that the audience had heard many times before.
She avoided using readymade solutions and preferred to think creatively about each problem.
His essay was criticized for its readymade arguments and lack of personal insight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clichéd - overused and lacking originality
  • antonym: original - new and creative, not copied or conventional
