cover up

verb
hide or conceal the truth or something from view
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌv.ər əp/ /; / /ˈkʌv.ər ʌp/ (AmE), /ˈkʌv.ə əp/ /; / /ˈkʌv.ə ʌp/ (BrE)
to hide or conceal something so that it cannot be seen or discovered (can refer to physical things or information)

Examples

To cover up a scar
He wore a long scarf to cover up the scar on his neck.
Officials attempted to cover up evidence of their involvement in the scandal.
The company tried to cover up the incident, but the press discovered the truth.
He quickly put on long sleeves to cover up his injuries.
The company tried to cover up the mistake to avoid bad publicity.
They attempted to cover up the truth, but the facts eventually came out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceal - to hide or keep secret; not reveal
  • antonym: expose - to reveal the truth about something hidden
