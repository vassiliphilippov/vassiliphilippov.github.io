cover up

verb
hide or conceal the truth or something from view

to hide or conceal something so that it cannot be seen or discovered (can refer to physical things or information)

Examples

To cover up a scar
He wore a long scarf to cover up the scar on his neck.
Officials attempted to cover up evidence of their involvement in the scandal.
The company tried to cover up the incident, but the press discovered the truth.
He quickly put on long sleeves to cover up his injuries.
The company tried to cover up the mistake to avoid bad publicity.
They attempted to cover up the truth, but the facts eventually came out.

Synonyms

  • conceal
    3.7 19/m
    conceal is a neutral formal verb for hiding something thoroughly, while cover up often suggests hiding to avoid blame or discovery.
  • hide
    1.0 154/m
    hide is a common everyday verb for putting something out of sight, while cover up stresses secret or deliberate concealment.
  • secrete
    8.1 0.04/m
    secrete means to hide an item carefully in a small place, while cover up can mean hiding facts or objects more broadly.
  • cover
    1.0 102/m
    cover usually means to put something over or protect it, while cover up implies hiding something to avoid notice.
  • bury
    1.6 13/m
    bury often means to put something under earth or hide it completely, while cover up covers both objects and information.
  • hush up
    7.2 0.05/m
    hush up means to silence people about a scandal or mistake, while cover up is a broader term for hiding facts.
  • veil
    5.0 2.63/m
    veil often means to hide something partly or softly, while cover up suggests full concealment or deliberate secrecy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceal - to hide or keep secret; not reveal
  • antonym: expose - to reveal the truth about something hidden
