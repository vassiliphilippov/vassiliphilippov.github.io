ultrahigh frequency

noun
radio frequency (300–3000 MHz)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəˌhaɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˌhaɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
A radio frequency in the range of 300 to 3000 megahertz, used especially for television broadcasting, mobile phones, and radar.

Examples

The ultrahigh frequency band
Most modern wireless microphones operate within the ultrahigh frequency range.
Radar systems often use ultrahigh frequency waves for aircraft detection.
The television station broadcasts on the ultrahigh frequency band for better signal clarity.
