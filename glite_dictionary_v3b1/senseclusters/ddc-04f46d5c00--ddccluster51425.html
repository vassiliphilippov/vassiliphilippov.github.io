DDC

noun
antiviral drug for HIV, nucleoside analog
9.3 0.41/m
Pronunciation: / di di siÀê / (AmE), / diÀê diÀê siÀê / (BrE)
An antiviral drug used in the treatment of HIV infection; a nucleoside analog reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NARTI).

Examples

DDC antiviral drug

Clinical studies have shown that DDC can inhibit HIV replication by blocking reverse transcriptase.
