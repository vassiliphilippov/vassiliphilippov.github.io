crime against humanity

noun
serious violation of human rights
1.0
Pronunciation: / kraɪm əˈɡɛnst hjuːˈmænəti / (AmE), / kraɪm əˈɡɛnst hjuːˈmænəti / (BrE)
a serious offense involving widespread or systematic violations of human rights, such as murder, enslavement, or torture, especially when carried out against civilians

Examples

A crime against humanity
Many countries have laws to prosecute anyone accused of a crime against humanity.
Systematic torture of civilians is considered a crime against humanity under international law.
The international court found the general guilty of a crime against humanity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: war crime - a breach of the laws or customs of war
