peep

verb
make a short, high-pitched sound
1.5 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / pip / (AmE), / piÀêp / (BrE)
(of a bird or small animal) to make a short, high-pitched sound.

Examples

Chicks peep loudly
A tiny chick began to peep as soon as it hatched.
Late at night, the crickets peep quietly from the shadows.
We could hear the ducklings peeping softly in the grass.

Synonyms

  • chirp
    1.0 2.18/m
    chirp is a quick, high sound often made by small birds, insects, or devices, while peep is a soft bird or small-animal sound.
  • tweet
    2.7 0.09/m
    tweet is a light, musical chirp from a small bird, while peep is a quieter short, high sound from birds or small animals.
  • cheep
    3.9 0.05/m
    cheep is the short, high sound young birds make when calling for food, while peep applies to small bird or animal sounds.
  • chirrup
    8.1 0.01/m
    chirrup is repeated short, high notes often in series by birds or insects, while peep is usually a single brief sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirp - make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound (esp. of a small bird or insect)
