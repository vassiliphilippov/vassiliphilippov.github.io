peep

verb
make a short, high-pitched sound
Pronunciation: / pip / (AmE), / piːp / (BrE)
(of a bird or small animal) to make a short, high-pitched sound.

Examples

Chicks peep loudly
A tiny chick began to peep as soon as it hatched.
Late at night, the crickets peep quietly from the shadows.
We could hear the ducklings peeping softly in the grass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirp - make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound (esp. of a small bird or insect)
