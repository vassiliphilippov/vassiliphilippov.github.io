reverser

noun
one who overturns decisions
7.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrsər / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsə / (BrE)
a person or authority that overturns or annuls a previous decision, ruling, or judgment

Examples

Court reverser
The Supreme Court acted as a reverser by nullifying the lower court's ruling.
As a reverser, the committee changed the outcome of the previous vote.
The new evidence led the judge to become a reverser of the original verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overturner - someone who reverses or cancels a previous decision
  • antonym: upholder - someone who supports or maintains a previous decision
