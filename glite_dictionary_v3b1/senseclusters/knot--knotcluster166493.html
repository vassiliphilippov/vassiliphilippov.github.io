knot

noun
hard round part in wood where branch grew
3.0 4.03/m
Pronunciation: / nÉ‘t / (AmE), / nÉ’t / (BrE)
a hard lump or mass, such as in wood, muscles, or yarn

Examples

A knot in wood
The carpenter struggled to sand down the knot in the pine board.
There's a large knot near the center of the table, adding to its rustic look.
We had to saw around the knot because it was too tough for the blade.
After running for miles, she felt a knot forming in her calf muscle.
Tension brought a knot to his stomach before the interview.
The old carpenter's hands were covered in knots from years of hard work.
A knot in the thread can cause the fabric to feel rough in that spot.
Some fabrics include a decorative knot to add visual interest and texture.
The weaver noticed a small knot in the middle of the cotton yarn.
