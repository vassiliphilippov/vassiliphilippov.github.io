stemma

noun
simple eye with a single lens
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛmə / (AmE), / ˈstɛmə / (BrE)
a simple eye with a single lens, found especially in certain insects and arthropods

Examples

Insect stemma
Each stemma is capable of detecting light but not detailed images.
The larval stages of many insects possess a stemma on each side of the head.
Unlike compound eyes, a stemma has only a single simple lens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ocellus - a simple eye found in many invertebrates
