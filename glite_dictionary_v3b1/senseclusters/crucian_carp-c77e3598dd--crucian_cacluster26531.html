crucian carp

noun
a European freshwater fish similar to the goldfish
8.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkruÀê É…ôn k…ëÀêrp / (AmE), / ÀàkruÀê É…ôn k…ëÀêp / (BrE)
A small European freshwater fish closely related to the goldfish, known for its resilience and resemblance to wild goldfish.

Examples

A crucian carp fish
Anglers sometimes catch crucian carp in small ponds and lakes across Europe.
The crucian carp is able to survive in water with very low oxygen levels.
Unlike other carp species, the crucian carp has a distinctly rounded body and no barbels.
