cladogenesis

noun
branching speciation event
9.9
Pronunciation: /klæd.ə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /klæd.ə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the evolutionary process in which a species splits into two or more new species or lineages

Examples

Rapid cladogenesis
The rapid cladogenesis of mammals occurred after the extinction of the dinosaurs.
Researchers study cladogenesis to understand how biodiversity increases over time.
A burst of cladogenesis can lead to many new species in a relatively short period.
The rapid cladogenesis of finches on the islands led to many new species.
Scientists study cladogenesis to understand how biodiversity increases over time.
A burst of cladogenesis can occur when organisms enter a new environment with many available niches.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anagenesis - evolutionary change within a single lineage without branching
  • synonym: speciation - the formation of new and distinct species in the course of evolution
