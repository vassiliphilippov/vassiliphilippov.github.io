calif

noun
Muslim religious and civil leader; successor to Muhammad
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæl.ɪf/ (AmE), /ˈkæl.ɪf/ (BrE)
the chief civil and religious leader in a Muslim state, regarded as the successor to Muhammad and representative of Allah on earth

Examples

The calif of Baghdad
During the Abbasid era, the calif was considered both a political and spiritual authority.
Some historians debate the influence of each calif over the development of Islamic law.
The decline of the Ottoman calif marked a significant shift in the Muslim world.
