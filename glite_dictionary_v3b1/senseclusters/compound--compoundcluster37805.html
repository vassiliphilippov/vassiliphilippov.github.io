compound

verb
make something more intense or worse
1.0
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
to make (something bad or already negative) worse or more intense; to increase or intensify the effect of something, especially a problem or negative situation.

Examples

To compound a problem
Failing to apologize will only compound the tension between the two teams.
Heavy rain can compound the problems caused by flooding.
His lack of preparation compounded the difficulties he faced during the interview.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alleviate - to make less severe or intense
  • synonym: intensify - to make or become more intense
