shorten

verb
make shorter in length or scope
6.7 2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrtən /; / ˈʃɔr.tn̩ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtən /; / ˈʃɔː.tən / (BrE)
to make something shorter in length, duration, amount, or extent

Examples

To shorten an article
The editor asked him to shorten the article for publication.
The speech was too long and had to be shortened to fit the schedule.
They will shorten the route to save time on the marathon day.
Exercising regularly can shorten the duration of a cold.
She decided to shorten her speech to fit the time limit.
The tailor will shorten your trousers if they're too long.

You can shorten the pants by hemming them above the ankle.
