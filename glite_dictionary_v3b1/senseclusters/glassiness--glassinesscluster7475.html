glassiness

noun
dull or glazed look
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈglæ.sɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈglɑː.sɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈglæs.ɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of having a dull, unfocused, or glazed appearance, especially in the eyes due to illness, shock, or intoxication

Examples

Glassiness in the eyes
The glassiness in his eyes made the doctor suspect he was running a high fever.
After hearing the shocking news, a strange glassiness came over her expression.
Alcohol can cause temporary glassiness in a person's eyes.
