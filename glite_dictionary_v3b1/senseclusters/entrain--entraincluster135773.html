entrain

verb
to carry along
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈtreɪn / (AmE), / ɛnˈtreɪn / (BrE)
to carry or transport something along with a moving fluid, such as water or air

Examples

To entrain particles
The fast-moving river can entrain sand and small stones as it flows downstream.
Industrial processes sometimes entrain air bubbles in liquids, affecting product quality.
Strong winds may entrain dust particles and carry them over long distances.
Fast-moving streams can entrain small rocks and carry them downstream.
The ventilation system may entrain dust particles and spread them throughout the building.
Waves often entrain air bubbles as they crash onto the shore.
