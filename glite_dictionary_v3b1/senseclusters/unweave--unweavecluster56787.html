unweave

verb
undo woven strands
8.6 0.08/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàwiÀêv / (AmE), /  ånÀàwiÀêv / (BrE)
to undo the strands of something that has been woven together; to separate by reversing the process of weaving

Examples

To unweave a basket
After hours of patient work, she managed to unweave the intricate basket.
Carefully, he began to unweave the threads to see how the pattern was made.
The old legend tells of Penelope, who would unweave her tapestry each night.

Synonyms

  • unravel
    4.3 2.27/m
    unravel means threads come apart or someone pulls them out and can describe solving a problem, while unweave means to reverse weaving deliberately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: untangle - to free something from tangles; to disentangle
  • antonym: weave - to form fabric by interlacing threads
