mentalistic

adjective
attributing mental states
8.8
Pronunciation: /men.tə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /mən.tə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /men.tə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /mən.tə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
describing explanations or approaches that focus on the mind, mental states, or mental processes (such as beliefs, desires, or intentions), rather than physical explanations

Examples

Mentalistic explanation
The psychologist criticized the mentalistic approach for relying too much on unobservable concepts.
Some theories in animal behavior are considered mentalistic because they assume animals have intentions.
Her explanation was too mentalistic, focusing on what people might be thinking rather than what they actually did.
The psychologist criticized the mentalistic explanation for ignoring biological factors.
Some theories of learning are considered too mentalistic by behaviorists.
Her research takes a mentalistic perspective on language development in children.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behavioral - focusing on observable actions rather than mental states
  • antonym: physical - relating to the body or material things rather than the mind
  • synonym: psychological - relating to the mind or mental functions
