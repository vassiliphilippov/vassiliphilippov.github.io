high-class

adjective
pretentiously elegant or refined
1.0
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈklæs / (AmE), / haɪ ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
Pretentiously elegant or refined; having or affecting an appearance of superior quality or status.

Examples

A high-class event
Her high-class tastes made her seem unapproachable to some of her friends.
The event was quite high-class, with crystal chandeliers and waiters in tuxedos.
They dined at a high-class restaurant that was famous for its formal service.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downmarket - relatively cheap or aimed at low-income customers
  • synonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance
  • synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit
