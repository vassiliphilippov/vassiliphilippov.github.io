merchant

noun
person engaged in buying and selling goods
2.1 16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ùÀê.t É…ônt / (AmE), / Ààm…úÀê.t É…ônt / (BrE)
a person or company involved in the buying and selling of goods, especially one engaged in trade on a large scale or retail business

Examples

A merchant company
As a respected merchant, she built a thriving business in the city center.
Early explorers often partnered with a merchant to finance their voyages.
The village merchant sold everything from spices to silks.

Synonyms

  • merchandiser
    4.5 0.45/m
    a merchandiser buys and sells goods in retail stores and arranges products, while a merchant often runs larger-scale trade or wholesale business.
  • trader
    2.1 16/m
    a trader buys and sells goods or assets for profit in markets, while a merchant focuses on regular retail or large-scale trade.
  • vendor
    2.0 21/m
    a vendor sells goods or services directly, often at markets or as a supplier, while a merchant runs wider retail or wholesale trade.
  • seller
    1.0 18/m
    a seller offers goods or services to customers in many situations, often one-off, while a merchant runs a steady retail or trading business.
  • dealer
    2.7 16/m
    a dealer buys and sells specific goods like cars or art, often as a specialist, while a merchant runs general retail or wholesale trade.

  • synonym: trader - person who buys and sells goods or assets
