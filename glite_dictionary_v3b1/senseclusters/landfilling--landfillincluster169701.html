landfilling

noun
waste disposal method
Pronunciation: / ˈlændˌfɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændˌfɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of disposing of waste by burying it in the ground, typically in a designated site

Examples

Landfilling waste
Many cities rely on landfilling as their primary method of waste management.
Environmental groups are concerned about the long-term effects of landfilling on soil and water quality.
New regulations aim to reduce the amount of waste sent for landfilling each year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incineration - the process of burning waste materials to dispose of them
  • antonym: recycling - the process of converting waste into reusable material
