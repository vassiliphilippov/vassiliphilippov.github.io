denaturant

noun
substance causing denaturation
9.2
Pronunciation: / dɪˈneɪtʃərənt / (AmE), / dɪˈneɪtʃər(ə)nt / (BrE)
a substance that causes the denaturation of proteins or other biological molecules

Examples

An added denaturant
Alcohol is added as a denaturant to prevent the consumption of industrial ethanol.
The effectiveness of a denaturant depends on its concentration and the type of protein involved.
Urea is often used as a denaturant in protein studies to disrupt their structure.
