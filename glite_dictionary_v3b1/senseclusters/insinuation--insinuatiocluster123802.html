insinuation

noun
an indirect, often negative, suggestion
3.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.sɪ.njuː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.sɪ.njuː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
an indirect or subtle suggestion, often of something unpleasant or discreditable

Examples

A rude insinuation
He denied the insinuation that his company had acted unethically.
Her insinuation that he had cheated was deeply hurtful.
The article was filled with insinuation rather than clear evidence against the politician.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something illegal or wrong
  • synonym: hint - an indirect or subtle suggestion
  • synonym: innuendo - an allusive or oblique remark or hint, typically suggestive or disparaging
