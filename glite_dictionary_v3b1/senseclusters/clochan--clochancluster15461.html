clochan

noun
stepping-stone causeway
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊxən / (AmE), / ˈklɒxən / (BrE)
a causeway or a set of stepping-stones used to cross a stream, bog, or wet ground, especially in Ireland or Scotland

Examples

Stone clochan crossing
The villagers built a clochan to make it easier to cross the stream during rainy seasons.
We carefully stepped across the old clochan to avoid getting our boots wet.
In rural Ireland, a clochan often connects fields separated by marshy ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: causeway - a raised road or path across wet ground or water
  • synonym: footbridge - a small bridge for people to walk across
