high priest

noun
senior religious leader; chief priest
7.2 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / ha…™ prist / (AmE), / ha…™ priÀêst / (BrE)
a senior clergyman or dignitary, especially one holding the highest religious office in certain faiths or rituals.

Examples

Temple high priest
He was appointed as the high priest of the temple at a young age.
In many cultures, the high priest held significant political and spiritual power.
The high priest led the ceremony with dignity and authority.
