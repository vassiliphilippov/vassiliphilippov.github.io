CD4 cell

noun
type of immune system T cell with CD4 receptor
5.1
Pronunciation: /siː diː fɔːr sel/ (AmE), /siː diː fɔː sel/ (BrE)
A type of T cell that has CD4 receptors and helps coordinate the immune response, particularly by stimulating B cells and killer T cells; targeted by HIV.

Examples

CD4 cell count
A low CD4 cell count can indicate immune system weakness.
Doctors monitor CD4 cell levels in patients with HIV to assess disease progression.
Research has shown that CD4 cell recovery is possible with effective antiretroviral therapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cytotoxic T cell - a T cell that kills virus-infected or cancer cells
  • synonym: helper T cell - a T cell that helps activate other immune cells, especially B cells and cytotoxic T cells
