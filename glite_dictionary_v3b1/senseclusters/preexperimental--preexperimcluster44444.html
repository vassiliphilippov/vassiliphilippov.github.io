preexperimental

adjective
preliminary research design
9.0
Pronunciation: /priːk.spə.ˈrɪ.mən.təl/ /; / /prɪk.spə.ˈrɪ.mən.təl/ (AmE), /priːk.spə.ˈrɪ.mən.təl/ /; / /prɪk.spə.ˈrɪ.mən.təl/ (BrE)
describing a research design used for preliminary study that lacks full experimental control, such as not having a control group

Examples

Preexperimental study design
The researchers chose a preexperimental approach to gather initial data before designing a full study.
A preexperimental design often lacks a control group, making it less rigorous than other methods.
In their report, they described the limitations of using a preexperimental method for their investigation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: experimental - involving a fully controlled scientific experiment
  • synonym: pilot - a small-scale preliminary study
