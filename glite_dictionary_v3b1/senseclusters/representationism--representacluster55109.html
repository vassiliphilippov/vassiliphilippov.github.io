representationism

noun
realistic depiction theory
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˌzɛntəˈʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˌzɛntəˈʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory or practice in art or philosophy that emphasizes depicting real objects or scenes as they appear in reality

Examples

Representationism in art
The gallery's new exhibition highlights the influence of representationism in early twentieth-century painting.
Some critics argue that representationism limits artistic creativity by focusing too much on reality.
Her interest in representationism led her to study classical drawing techniques.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstractionism - the artistic movement emphasizing non-representational forms
  • synonym: realism - the artistic movement focused on representing subjects truthfully
