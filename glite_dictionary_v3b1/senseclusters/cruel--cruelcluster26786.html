cruel

adjective
willing to cause suffering
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːəl / (AmE), / ˈkruːəl / (BrE)
willing or likely to cause pain or suffering to others deliberately, or showing a lack of compassion.

Examples

A cruel person
He found it cruel to leave the injured animal alone in the cold.
History is full of cruel rulers who caused much suffering.
The teacher was criticized for her cruel treatment of the children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brutal - savagely violent or cruel
  • antonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature
  • antonym: merciful - showing compassion or forgiveness toward someone
