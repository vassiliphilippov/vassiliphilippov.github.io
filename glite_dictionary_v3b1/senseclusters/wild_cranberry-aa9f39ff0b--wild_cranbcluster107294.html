wild cranberry

noun
mat-forming shrub with red berries
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkrænˌbəri / (BrE)
an evergreen, mat-forming shrub native to North America and northern Eurasia, bearing small white flowers and red berries; its leaves turn red in autumn

Examples

The wild cranberry plant
In autumn, the leaves of the wild cranberry turn a brilliant shade of red.
The northern bogs are home to the wild cranberry, which provides fruit for both wildlife and people.
We spotted a patch of wild cranberry while hiking in the northern woods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cranberry - a small, red, tart berry or the plant that bears it
