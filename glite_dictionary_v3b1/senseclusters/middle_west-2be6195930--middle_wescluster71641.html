Middle West

proper noun
north-central region of the United States
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪd.əl ˈwɛst / (AmE), / ˌmɪd.əl ˈwɛst / (BrE)
a region in the north-central United States, often referred to as the heartland or breadbasket of America, typically including states such as Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Iowa, Missouri, Michigan, Wisconsin, Minnesota, Nebraska, and Kansas

Examples

The Middle West region
Corn and soybeans are major crops grown throughout the Middle West.
Many people in the Middle West value traditional values and a strong sense of community.
She moved to the Middle West after graduating from college to start her new job.
