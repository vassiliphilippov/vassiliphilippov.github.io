slow off the mark

adjective
slow to respond or start
Pronunciation: / sloʊ ɑf ðə mɑrk / (AmE), / sləʊ ɒf ðə mɑːk / (BrE)
(of a person) slow to start, react, or respond compared to others; not quick to act or notice something.

Examples

Slow off the mark in class
He's always slow off the mark when it comes to answering questions in class.
If you're slow off the mark, you might miss important opportunities at work.
The new player seemed slow off the mark compared to his teammates in the first half.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quick off the mark - able to react or respond quickly
