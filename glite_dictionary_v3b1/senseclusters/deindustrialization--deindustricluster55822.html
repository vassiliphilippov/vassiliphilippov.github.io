deindustrialization

noun
decline of industry
8.3
Pronunciation: / diˌɪndəˌstrɪəliˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌɪndəˌstrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌɪndʌˌstrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌɪndʌstrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which industrial activity in a region or country declines or is reduced, often as factories close or move elsewhere

Examples

Deindustrialization of cities
The city faced rapid deindustrialization after several major factories shut down in the 1980s.
Many workers lost their jobs due to deindustrialization and the relocation of manufacturing overseas.
Government policies attempted to slow deindustrialization by supporting new types of businesses.
The city experienced rapid deindustrialization after several major factories closed in the 1980s.
Many workers lost their jobs due to deindustrialization in the manufacturing sector.
Policies were introduced to help communities affected by deindustrialization recover and diversify their economies.
The city suffered from deindustrialization after many factories closed in the 1980s.
Widespread deindustrialization led to high unemployment and economic challenges in the region.
Government policies attempted to reverse deindustrialization by encouraging new types of businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, or value
  • synonym: economic decline - a reduction in economic activity and prosperity
  • antonym: industrialization - the process of developing industries in a region or country
