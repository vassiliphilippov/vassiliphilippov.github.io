dehumidify

verb
remove moisture from air or place
Pronunciation: / diˌhjuːˈmɪdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diːˌhjuːˈmɪdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove moisture from the air or a place, making it less humid

Examples

To dehumidify the air
After the flood, workers used special machines to dehumidify the building.
The air conditioner automatically dehumidifies the air in our living room during summer.
To prevent mold, it is important to dehumidify damp areas like basements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humidify - to make (air or a place) more humid
