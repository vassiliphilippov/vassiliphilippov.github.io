realistic

adjective
true-to-life; not idealized or abstract
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
representing things in a way that is true to life or as they actually are, without idealization or abstraction

Examples

Realistic painting

The artist's realistic painting captured every tiny detail of the street scene.
The film used advanced effects to create a highly realistic dinosaur.
