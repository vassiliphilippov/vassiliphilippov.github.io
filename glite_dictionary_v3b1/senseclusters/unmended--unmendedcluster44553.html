unmended

adjective
not reformed (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmɛndɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛndɪd / (BrE)
not reformed or corrected in character or behavior; remaining incorrigible or unrepentant (archaic).

Examples

Unmended character
The judge saw the unmended nature of the defendant and issued a stern warning.
Despite many chances, his unmended ways led him back to trouble.
In old tales, the unmended villain never found redemption.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incorrigible - unable to be corrected or reformed
  • antonym: reformed - improved in character or behavior
