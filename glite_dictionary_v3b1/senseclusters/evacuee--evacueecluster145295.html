evacuee

noun
person evacuated from danger
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvækjuˈi / (AmE), / ɪˌvæk.juːˈiː / (BrE)
a person who has been removed from a place of danger, especially during a war or disaster, for safety

Examples

War evacuees
During the war, thousands of children became evacuees sent to the countryside.
Many evacuees were housed in temporary shelters after the hurricane.
The government provided food and water to the evacuees arriving at the camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refugee - a person who has been forced to leave their country to escape danger
