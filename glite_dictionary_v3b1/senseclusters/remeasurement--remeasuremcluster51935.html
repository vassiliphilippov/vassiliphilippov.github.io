remeasurement

noun
accounting value adjustment
8.1
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈmeʒ.ɜːr.mənt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈmeʒ.ɜːr.mənt/ /; / /ə.ˈmeʒ.ɜːr.mənt/ (AmE), /riː.ˈmeʒ.ə.mənt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈmeʒ.ə.mənt/ /; / /ə.ˈmeʒ.ə.mənt/ (BrE)
an accounting adjustment that updates the value of assets, liabilities, or monetary items to reflect current exchange rates or market values

Examples

Currency remeasurement
The company recorded a remeasurement gain after updating its foreign currency balances.
Annual financial statements often include a remeasurement of assets to reflect market changes.
A sudden shift in exchange rates can result in a significant remeasurement loss for multinational firms.
