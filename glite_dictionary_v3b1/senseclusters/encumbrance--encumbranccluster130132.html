encumbrance

noun
legal claim against property
5.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (BrE)
A legal claim or liability on property, such as a lien or mortgage, that may affect its transfer or use.

Examples

Property encumbrance
A mortgage is an encumbrance that must be disclosed during the sale of real estate.
Before selling his house, he had to clear all encumbrances from the property title.
The buyer was relieved to learn that the land was free of any encumbrance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asset - something valuable owned, especially property or investments
  • synonym: lien - a legal right to keep possession of property until a debt is paid
  • synonym: mortgage - a legal agreement by which a bank lends money to purchase property and keeps title as security
