plane geometry

noun
geometry of two-dimensional figures
Pronunciation: / pleɪn dʒiˈɑmətri / (AmE), / pleɪn dʒiˈɒmətri / (BrE)
the branch of geometry that deals with shapes and figures in a two-dimensional plane, such as lines, circles, and polygons

Examples

Plane geometry shapes
In plane geometry, the angles of a triangle always add up to 180 degrees.
She found plane geometry easier to understand than solid geometry.
The textbook dedicates several chapters to plane geometry before introducing three-dimensional concepts.
