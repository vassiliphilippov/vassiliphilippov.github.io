land mass

noun
large continuous area of land
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlænd ˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈlænd ˌmæs / (BrE)
A large, continuous area of land, especially one not separated by water.

Examples

A large land mass
After the floodwaters receded, a new land mass emerged from the sea.
Australia is the smallest land mass generally recognized as a continent.
The movement of tectonic plates can cause a land mass to split or collide with another.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continent - one of the main large continuous land masses of the Earth
