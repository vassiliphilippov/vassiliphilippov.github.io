slippery

adjective
not to be trusted or relied upon
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪpɚi / (AmE), / ˈslɪpəri / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) not to be relied on or trusted; likely to evade or deceive.

Examples

Slippery politician
She found the politician’s answers slippery and evasive during the interview.
The lawyer had a slippery reputation, so his clients were always on guard.
You have to be careful—he’s a slippery character who never gives a straight answer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceitful - dishonest or misleading; not telling the truth
  • antonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful
