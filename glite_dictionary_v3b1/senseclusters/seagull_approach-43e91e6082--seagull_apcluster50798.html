seagull approach

noun
brief, hands-off management style
9.4 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.gʌl ə.ˈproʊtʃ/ /; / /ˈsiː.gəl ə.ˈproʊtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.gʌl ə.ˈprəʊtʃ/ /; / /ˈsiː.gəl ə.ˈprəʊtʃ/ (BrE)
A management style in which a person only becomes briefly involved, often making quick judgments or changes without real commitment or ongoing attention.

Examples

A seagull approach style
Critics argue that a seagull approach to leadership can damage morale and hinder progress.
Many employees find the seagull approach both disruptive and unproductive over the long term.
The new supervisor's seagull approach left the team feeling unsupported and confused.
