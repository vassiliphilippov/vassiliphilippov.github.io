recession

noun
period of economic decline
2.3
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a period of temporary economic decline during which trade and industrial activity are reduced, generally identified by a fall in GDP and employment lasting at least six months

Examples

An economic recession
During the last recession, unemployment rates rose sharply across all sectors.
Many businesses were forced to close during the recession due to declining demand.
The country entered a recession after two consecutive quarters of negative growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth
  • degree higher: depression - a prolonged and severe downturn in economic activity
