very high frequency

noun
radio frequency range: 30–300 MHz
4.1 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈve.rɪ haɪ ˈfriː.kwən.siː/ (AmE), /ˈve.rɪ haɪ ˈfriː.kwən.siː/ (BrE)
the range of radio frequencies from 30 to 300 megahertz, commonly used for FM radio, television broadcasts, and two-way radios

Examples

Very high frequency radio
Communication with aircraft is often carried out using very high frequency radio signals for reliable transmission.
FM radio stations typically broadcast on very high frequency bands to reach a wide audience.
Some television channels are transmitted on very high frequency rather than ultra high frequency bands.
