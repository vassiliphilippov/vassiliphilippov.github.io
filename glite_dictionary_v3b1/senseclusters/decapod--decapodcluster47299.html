decapod

noun
crustacean with ten legs
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəpɒd / (BrE)
A crustacean, such as a crab, lobster, or shrimp, that typically has five pairs of walking legs attached to the thorax.

Examples

A decapod crustacean
Many decapods, such as crabs and shrimp, are commercially valuable as seafood.
The biologist studied the unique swimming patterns of the decapod species.
The lobster is a well-known decapod found in many parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: arthropod - any invertebrate animal with an exoskeleton, a segmented body, and paired jointed appendages
  • synonym: crustacean - a large class of arthropods including crabs and lobsters
