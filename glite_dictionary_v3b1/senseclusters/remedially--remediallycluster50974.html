remedially

adverb
in a corrective way
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmiːdiəli / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːdiəli / (BrE)
in a manner intended to correct or improve a deficiency or problem

Examples

Taught remedially in math
The school provided extra classes so students could be taught remedially in math.
He was placed in a program where he learned remedially to overcome his reading difficulties.
Some children benefit from being instructed remedially to address specific learning gaps.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correctively - in a way that serves to correct
  • antonym: deficiently - in a way that shows a lack or deficiency
