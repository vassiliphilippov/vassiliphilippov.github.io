spoliarium

noun
Roman arena morgue
9.9
Pronunciation: / spoʊˈlɪəriəm / (AmE), / spəʊˈlɪəriəm / (BrE)
an area in ancient Roman arenas where the bodies of dead gladiators were taken after combat

Examples

Roman spoliarium
The famous painting depicts the spoliarium filled with the bodies of fallen gladiators.
Historians believe the spoliarium was a grim reminder of the cost of Roman entertainment.
After the games, the defeated were carried to the spoliarium beneath the arena.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: arena - a place where events or contests are held
  • synonym: morgue - a place where dead bodies are kept
