lappet

noun
fleshy fold of skin on birds or lizards
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlæp.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæp.ɪt / (BrE)
a fleshy, often wrinkled and sometimes brightly colored fold of skin that hangs from the neck or throat of certain birds, such as chickens and turkeys, or lizards

Examples

Turkey lappet
Male chickens often have a prominent lappet beneath their beaks.
Many lizards display a colorful lappet to attract mates or ward off rivals.
The turkey's bright red lappet wobbled as it walked around the yard.
