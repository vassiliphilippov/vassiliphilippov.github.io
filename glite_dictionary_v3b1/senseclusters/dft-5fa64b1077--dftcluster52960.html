DFT

noun
discrete Fourier transform
9.6 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / di …õf ti / (AmE), / diÀê …õf tiÀê / (BrE)
a mathematical process that converts a sequence of values into components of different frequencies, known as the discrete Fourier transform

Examples

DFT analysis of signals
The engineer used DFT to identify the dominant frequencies in the sampled signal.
A DFT is often applied in digital signal processing to analyze frequency content.
Learning how to compute a DFT is essential in many engineering courses.
