monosemous

adjective
having only one meaning
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.nəˈsiː.məs / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈsiː.məs / (BrE)
(of a word) having only one meaning; not polysemous.

Examples

A monosemous word
Linguists find it rare for a frequently used verb to be completely monosemous.
The dictionary entry showed that 'yacht' is mostly monosemous, unlike more ambiguous terms.
Unlike many English words, 'saxophone' is monosemous and refers only to the musical instrument.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polysemous - having multiple meanings
