scientific method

noun
systematic procedure for scientific investigation
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a systematic approach to investigation, based on observation, measurement, and experiment, and the formulation, testing, and modification of hypotheses.

Examples

The scientific method process
By using the scientific method, researchers can ensure their results are based on evidence rather than opinion.
She taught her students how to apply the scientific method to everyday problems.
The scientific method is essential for conducting reliable research in biology and chemistry.
