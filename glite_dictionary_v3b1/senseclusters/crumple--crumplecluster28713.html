crumple

verb
collapse or fall apart suddenly
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to suddenly fall down or collapse, especially because of force or weakness

Examples

To crumple to the ground
During the earthquake, several poorly built houses crumpled to the ground.
The cardboard box crumpled under the weight of the books.
The old bridge began to crumple after years of neglect and heavy traffic.
He crumpled under the pressure and fainted in front of the crowd.
She watched as the old building slowly crumpled during the demolition.
The exhausted runner crumpled to the ground at the finish line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buckled - to bend or give way under pressure
  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in suddenly
  • synonym: disintegrate - to break into small parts or pieces
