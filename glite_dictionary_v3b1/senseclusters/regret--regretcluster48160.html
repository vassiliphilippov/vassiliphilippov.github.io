regret

verb
feel sad about a loss or absence
1.9 1.1
Pronunciation: / rəˈɡrɛt /; / rɪˈɡrɛt / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛt / (BrE)
to feel sadness, disappointment, or remorse about something lost, missed, or a past action

Examples

To regret a mistake
He deeply regretted the loss of his childhood home after it was sold.
I regret the absence of your laughter at our gatherings.
She will always regret not spending more time with her late grandmother.
I deeply regret the decision I made last year.
She regretted not telling the truth when she had the chance.
You may regret it later if you act too hastily.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cherish - to hold something dear; antonym in the sense of losing vs. appreciating
  • synonym: feel sorry - to experience sympathy or regret for someone or something
  • synonym: miss - to feel the absence of someone or something with longing
  • antonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
  • synonym: repent - to feel or express sincere regret or remorse about one's wrongdoing or sin
