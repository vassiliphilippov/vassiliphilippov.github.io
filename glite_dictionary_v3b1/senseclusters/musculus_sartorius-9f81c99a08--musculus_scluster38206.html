musculus sartorius

noun
longest muscle in the human thigh
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskjələs sɑrˈtɔriəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs sɑːˈtɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
a long muscle in the thigh that assists in rotating the leg into a cross-legged position; the longest muscle in the human body

Examples

The musculus sartorius muscle
Damage to the musculus sartorius can affect the ability to sit comfortably cross-legged.
During anatomy class, the musculus sartorius was identified as the longest skeletal muscle in the human body.
The musculus sartorius plays a key role in allowing the leg to cross over the opposite knee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sartorius - the same muscle, often called simply 'sartorius' in anatomy
