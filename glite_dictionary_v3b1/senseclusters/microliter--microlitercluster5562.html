microliter

noun
small volume unit
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəˌliːtə / (BrE)
a unit of volume equal to one millionth of a liter, commonly used in scientific measurements

Examples

One microliter of solution
The technician added exactly one microliter of solution to each test tube.
A single drop of water contains about fifty microliters.
Accurate measurement of a microliter is important in laboratory experiments.
The technician measured exactly five microliters of solution for the experiment.
Each drop of the medication contains about two microliters of liquid.
Accurate pipettes are essential when working with microliter volumes in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: liter - the basic unit of volume in the metric system
  • degree higher: milliliter - a unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter
  • degree lower: nanoliter - a unit of volume equal to one billionth of a liter
