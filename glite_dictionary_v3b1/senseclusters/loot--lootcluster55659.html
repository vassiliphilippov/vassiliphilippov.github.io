loot

verb
steal goods, especially during chaos
4.2 2.32/m
Pronunciation: / luÀêt / (AmE), / luÀêt / (BrE)
to steal goods from a place, especially during a war, riot, or disaster

Examples

To loot a store
Several people tried to loot the abandoned warehouses after the storm hit the city.
The army threatened to arrest anyone attempting to loot shops during the unrest.
Thieves broke into the museum to loot valuable artifacts amid the confusion.

Synonyms

  • plunder
    6.0 1.54/m
    plunder is stealing goods by force in war or disaster, while loot is stealing during riots or disorder.
  • pillage
    8.1 1.33/m
    pillage is taking goods by force during war or civil disorder, while loot is stealing goods in chaotic situations.
  • despoil
    9.3 0.44/m
    despoil means violently taking valuables and causing damage, while loot is more general stealing during riots or disaster.
  • ransack
    6.3 1.51/m
    ransack is searching a place roughly and making a mess, while loot focuses on taking goods during disorder.
  • foray
    7.9 0.00/m
    foray means a sudden raid to take goods briefly, while loot is the act of stealing during war or riots.

Related Terms
