concrescence

noun
integration into unity
9.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈkres.əns/ /; / /kən.krə.ˈsens/ (AmE), /kɒn.ˈkres.əns/ /; / /kɒn.krə.ˈsens/ (BrE)
the process by which multiple experiences or influences are combined into a single unified entity, especially in philosophy.

Examples

Concrescence of ideas
In process philosophy, concrescence refers to the unification of many experiences into a single event.
The artist described the concrescence of ideas that led to her final painting.
Whitehead's theory explains how concrescence brings together diverse influences into one actual occasion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into separate parts
  • synonym: integration - the act of combining parts into a whole
  • synonym: synthesis - the combination of different ideas or things to form a whole
