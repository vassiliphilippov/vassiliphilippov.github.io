plowman

noun
man who plows fields
3.1 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈplaʊ.mən/ (AmE), /ˈplaʊ.mən/ (BrE)
a man who drives a plow, especially one who works on a farm plowing fields

Examples

A plowman on a farm
In medieval England, the life of a plowman was marked by hard physical labor.
The plowman rose early each morning to tend the fields.
The poem described a solitary plowman working beneath a cloudy sky.
In old English poetry, the life of a humble ploughman is often portrayed as noble and honest.
The ploughman guided his horses steadily across the field from dawn until dusk.
The village held a festival in honor of the local ploughman and his hard work.
