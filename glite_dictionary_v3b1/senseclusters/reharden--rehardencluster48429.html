reharden

verb
to become tough again
8.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈhɑːr.dən/ /; / /rɪ.ˈhɑːr.dən/ /; / /rə.ˈhɑːr.dən/ (AmE), /riː.ˈhɑː.dən/ /; / /rɪ.ˈhɑː.dən/ /; / /rə.ˈhɑː.dən/ (BrE)
to make someone emotionally or morally harder, less sympathetic, or more unfeeling again after having softened.

Examples

To reharden his heart
After years in the harsh environment, the experience seemed to reharden his attitude toward others.
The betrayal by her closest friend only served to reharden her heart.
Difficult times can sometimes reharden people who had once become more open and trusting.
