branched chain ketoaciduria

noun
rare inherited metabolic disorder (maple syrup urine disease)
Pronunciation: / brænʧ tʃeɪn ˌkitoʊˌæsɪˈdjʊriə / (AmE), / brɑːnʧ tʃeɪn ˌkiːtəʊˌæsɪˈdjʊəriə / (BrE)
An inherited metabolic disorder in which the urine emits a sweet, maple syrup-like odor; without treatment, it can cause severe neurological problems and early childhood death.

Examples

Branched chain ketoaciduria disorder
Doctors diagnosed the infant with branched chain ketoaciduria after detecting a maple syrup odor in his urine.
Genetic counseling is important for families with a history of branched chain ketoaciduria.
Untreated branched chain ketoaciduria can quickly lead to severe brain damage or death in young children.
