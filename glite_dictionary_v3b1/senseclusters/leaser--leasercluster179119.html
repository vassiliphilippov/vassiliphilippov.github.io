leaser

noun
person granting lease
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈliːsər / (AmE), / ˈliːsə / (BrE)
a person or company that grants a lease of property, equipment, or goods to another party

Examples

Leaser of a house
The leaser is responsible for maintaining the property during the rental period.
Before signing the contract, the leaser explained all the terms to the tenant.
A leaser may require a security deposit from the person renting the equipment.
The leaser is responsible for making sure the building meets safety standards.
If the tenant damages the apartment, the leaser may withhold part of the deposit.
A leaser must provide written notice before increasing the rent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lessee - a person or company who receives a lease and uses the property
  • synonym: lessor - a person or company that leases property to another
