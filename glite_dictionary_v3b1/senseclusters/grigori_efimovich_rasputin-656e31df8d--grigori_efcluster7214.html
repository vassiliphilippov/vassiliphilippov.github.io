Grigori Efimovich Rasputin

proper noun
Russian mystic and royal adviser (1872–1916)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪˈɡɔri ɛˈfiməvɪtʃ ræˈspjutɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːri ɪˈfiːməvɪtʃ ræsˈpjuːtɪn / (BrE)
A Russian mystic and self-proclaimed holy man who became a close adviser to the family of Nicholas II, the last Emperor of Russia, and was assassinated by nobles who believed he threatened the monarchy (1872–1916).
