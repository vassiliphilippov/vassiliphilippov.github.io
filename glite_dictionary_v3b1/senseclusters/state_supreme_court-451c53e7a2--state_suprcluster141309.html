state supreme court

noun
highest court in a U.S. state
7.3
Pronunciation: / steɪt səˈpriːm kɔrt / (AmE), / steɪt səˈpriːm kɔːt / (BrE)
the highest judicial court within a state of the United States, which has ultimate appellate jurisdiction over state law matters

Examples

The state supreme court
Every U.S. state has its own state supreme court to interpret state laws and constitutions.
She argued the case before the state supreme court last year, hoping for a favorable ruling.
The state supreme court overturned the lower court's decision after a lengthy appeal process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: supreme court - the highest court, especially at the national level
