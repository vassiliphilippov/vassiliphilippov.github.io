alienability

noun
transferability in law
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪliənəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌeɪliənəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be transferred or sold to another person, especially in legal contexts

Examples

The alienability of land
The alienability of land allows owners to sell or transfer their property freely.
Some rights, such as voting, lack alienability and cannot be transferred to others.
Lawyers debated the alienability of certain intellectual property assets in court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inalienability - the quality of not being able to be transferred or sold
  • synonym: transferability - the ability to be transferred from one person to another
