fatal

adjective
causing ruin or disaster
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəl / (BrE)
causing disaster, ruin, or having extremely serious or decisive consequences

Examples

Fatal mistake
A fatal decision during the negotiations ruined the project.
That kind of carelessness can have fatal consequences for everyone involved.
The company made a fatal error that led to its bankruptcy.
It was on that fatal evening that the course of history was altered forever.
Many historians debate whether the treaty was a fatal mistake for the nation.
The fatal decision to launch the project before adequate testing changed the company's future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calamitous - causing great damage or suffering
  • synonym: disastrous - causing great harm, damage, or suffering
  • synonym: fateful - having far-reaching and decisive importance
  • antonym: fortunate - bringing good fortune; resulting in good outcomes
