potassium chlorate

noun
white salt used in matches and explosives
6.1
Pronunciation: / pəˌteɪsiəm ˈklɔːreɪt / (AmE), / pəˌtæsiəm ˈklɔːreɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt (KClO3) used in matches, fireworks, explosives, and as a disinfectant or bleaching agent

Examples

Potassium chlorate crystals
Potassium chlorate is a critical ingredient in the production of fireworks due to its oxidizing properties.
Due to its potential hazards, handling potassium chlorate requires strict safety measures.
The laboratory stored several kilograms of potassium chlorate for use in chemical experiments.

  • synonym: sodium chlorate - a related chemical salt (NaClO3) used as a herbicide and bleaching agent
