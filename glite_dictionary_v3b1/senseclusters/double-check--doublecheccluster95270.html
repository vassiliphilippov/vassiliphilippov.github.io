double-check

verb
check again to be sure
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌb.əl ˈtʃek/ (AmE), /ˈdʌb.əl ˈtʃek/ (BrE)
to check something again to make sure it is correct, accurate, or safe

Examples

To double-check the work
Could you double-check these figures to make sure there are no mistakes?
I should double-check the locks before we leave for the weekend.
She double-checked her answers before turning in the test.
Did you double-check the doors to make sure they were locked?
He always double-checks his emails for errors before sending them.
Just to be certain, she double-checked the calculations before submitting the report.
