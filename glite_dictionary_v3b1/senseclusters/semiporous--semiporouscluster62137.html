semiporous

adjective
partially permeable
7.9
Pronunciation: /se.mɪ.ˈpɔː.rəs/ /; / /sə.mɪ.ˈpɔː.rəs/ (AmE), /se.mɪ.ˈpɔː.rəs/ /; / /sə.mɪ.ˈpɔː.rəs/ (BrE)
partly porous; allowing some substances to pass through but blocking others

Examples

A semiporous membrane
The laboratory used a semiporous membrane to separate the two solutions.
Because the material is semiporous, it allows air to flow but keeps dust out.
A semiporous barrier can help control moisture without completely blocking it.
The filter is semiporous, so it lets some water through but blocks larger particles.
Scientists studied the semiporous membrane to understand how gases moved across it.
Because the rock was semiporous, moisture collected only on its outer layers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impermeable - not allowing any substances to pass through
  • degree lower: nonporous - not allowing liquid or air to pass through; without pores
  • degree higher: permeable - allowing liquids or gases to pass through
  • degree higher: porous - having many small holes that allow liquid or air to pass through
