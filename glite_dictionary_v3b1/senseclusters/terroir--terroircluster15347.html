terroir

noun
local growing conditions
9.1
Pronunciation: / tɛrˈwɑr / (AmE), / tɛˈrwɑː / (BrE)
the combination of natural or environmental factors (such as soil, climate, and landscape) in a place that influence the taste, quality, or character of crops, food, or drink produced there

Examples

Unique terroir of valley
The unique terroir of the valley gives the wine its distinctive flavor.
Farmers believe that the terroir is just as important as the grape variety.
Cheese from this region is famous for its terroir and traditional methods.
The wine's distinctive taste is a result of the vineyard's terroir.
Chefs often highlight the terroir of ingredients to showcase their unique origins.
Coffee grown in this region has a special terroir that sets it apart from others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: climate - the typical weather conditions of a place
  • synonym: microclimate - the climate of a small, specific area that differs from the surrounding region
  • synonym: region - a particular area or part of a country or the world
