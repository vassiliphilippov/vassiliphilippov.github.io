overboard

adverb
to an excessive degree
4.8 2.11/m
Pronunciation: / Àào äv…ôrÀåb…îrd / (AmE), / Àà…ô äv…ôb…îÀêd / (BrE)
to an excessive or extreme degree; beyond what is reasonable or necessary

Examples

Go overboard with gifts
He tends to go overboard with his holiday decorations every year.
The editor thought the journalist went overboard in describing the event's significance.
When it comes to gift giving, she often goes overboard and spends more than she should.

Synonyms

  • excessively
    3.0 3.81/m
    excessively means doing something to a much greater degree than needed in normal situations, while overboard suggests going beyond reason.
  • overly
    3.5 14/m
    overly describes doing or feeling too much in a simple, everyday way, while overboard implies excess that is extreme or dramatic.
  • inordinately
    7.5 0.75/m
    inordinately is used in formal contexts to mean unusually large or extreme, while overboard often feels more informal and dramatic.
  • unduly
    6.9 2.35/m
    unduly often appears in legal or formal speech to mark something unfairly excessive, while overboard sounds more casual and emotional.
  • exorbitantly
    7.3 0.16/m
    exorbitantly usually describes very high cost or price levels, while overboard refers to any action that is excessively extreme.
  • too much
    1.2 101/m
    too much is a common phrase meaning more than needed or wanted, while overboard suggests going farther into excess.
  • over-the-top
    5.2 3.33/m
    over-the-top is an informal phrase for exaggerated or extreme behavior, while overboard can be used more broadly to mean excessive.
  • too far
    2.6 19/m
    too far is a phrase for crossing a line or limit in behavior, while overboard focuses on acting to an excessive degree.
  • absurdly
    5.0 2.00/m
    absurdly means something is so unreasonable it seems silly or ridiculous, while overboard simply means more than is needed.
  • to a fault
    7.1 0.65/m
    to a fault describes having a good quality in excess, while overboard covers any excess and can be negative in many contexts.
