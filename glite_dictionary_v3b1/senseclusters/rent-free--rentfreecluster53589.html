rent-free

adverb
preying on someone's mind without their control
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛntˈfri / (AmE), / ˌrɛntˈfriː / (BrE)
(informal) Continually occupying someone's thoughts, attention, or emotions without their consent, especially in a way that causes annoyance or distraction.

Examples

Lives rent-free mentally
His old boss still lives rent-free in his mind, even after all these years.
She realized she was letting the argument occupy her thoughts rent-free.
The criticism was harsh, but I refuse to let it stay rent-free in my head.
