parlement

noun
historical parliament
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːrləmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːləmənt / (BrE)
an old or historical spelling of 'parliament', referring to a legislative body, especially in England or France

Examples

English parlement
The English parlement met to discuss the king's new laws.
In medieval France, the parlement played a key role in the administration of justice.
Historical documents often refer to the British legislature as the parlement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parliament - the supreme legislative body in a country
