shifty-eyed

adjective
having a nervous or deceitful appearance
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪfti aɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃɪfti aɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) having an appearance or manner that suggests nervousness, dishonesty, or deceit.

Examples

A shifty-eyed person
A shifty-eyed waiter kept glancing nervously around the restaurant.
She felt uncomfortable under his shifty-eyed gaze during the interview.
The detective was immediately wary of the shifty-eyed man lurking near the entrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furtive - attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
