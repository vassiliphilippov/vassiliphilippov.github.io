ECU

noun
former European currency unit
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈiːsiːˈjuː / (AmE), / ˈiːsiːˈjuː / (BrE)
the former unit of account used by the European Community before the introduction of the euro

Examples

The ECU currency
The ECU was used for accounting purposes before the euro was introduced.
In 1999, the ECU was officially replaced by the euro as the European currency.
Many financial contracts in Europe were once denominated in ECU rather than national currencies.
