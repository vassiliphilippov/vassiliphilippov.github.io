depth of knowledge

noun
educational taxonomy of cognitive levels
Pronunciation: / dɛpθ əv ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / dɛpθ əv ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in education) a taxonomy or framework that classifies tasks and activities by their cognitive demand into levels, used to design and assess learning and test items.

Examples

Depth of knowledge levels
The teacher redesigned tasks to match the depth of knowledge required for the state assessment.
Curriculum planners used depth of knowledge levels to ensure questions demanded different cognitive skills.
When writing exams, she checked each item against the depth of knowledge framework for balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bloom's taxonomy - an influential framework for categorizing educational goals; a related approach to classifying cognitive skills.
  • synonym: taxonomy - a system for classifying concepts or tasks into ordered categories; similar concept used in education.
