untrain

verb
to remove learned habits
6.5
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈtreɪn/ /; / /ə.ˈtreɪn/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈtreɪn/ /; / /ə.ˈtreɪn/ (BrE)
to lose or cause to lose skills, habits, or behaviors that were previously learned or taught

Examples

To untrain bad habits
It can be difficult to untrain a dog from bad habits once they are established.
The company had to untrain employees from outdated procedures before introducing the new system.
Parents sometimes struggle to untrain children from behaviors they picked up at school.
If you stop practicing regularly, you may quickly untrain and forget important techniques.
Athletes can untrain during long breaks from competition or exercise.
After leaving the job, she began to untrain in the specialized skills she once used daily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detrain - to lose physical fitness or conditioning
  • synonym: retrain - to teach new skills or habits, often after unlearning old ones
  • antonym: train - to teach or develop skills or habits
