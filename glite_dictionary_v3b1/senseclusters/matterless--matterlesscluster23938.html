matterless

adjective
without physical substance
Pronunciation: / ˈmætərləs / (AmE), / ˈmætələs / (BrE)
not composed of physical substance; having no material or physical form.

Examples

Matterless soul
The philosopher argued that the soul is matterless, existing independently of any physical body.
Some theories suggest that consciousness is a matterless phenomenon, not tied to the brain's material structure.
In the story, the ghost appeared as a matterless presence, unable to touch or move objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immaterial - not consisting of matter; not physical
  • synonym: incorporeal - having no physical body or form
  • antonym: material - having physical substance; consisting of matter
