fluoroform

noun
a colorless gaseous chemical compound (CHF3)
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈflʊər.oʊˌfɔːrm / (AmE), / ˈflʊərəʊˌfɔːm / (BrE)
A colorless gaseous compound, CHF3, similar to chloroform but containing fluorine instead of chlorine, used in organic synthesis and as a refrigerant.

Examples

Fluoroform gas
Because it is nonflammable, fluoroform is sometimes used as a refrigerant in specialized equipment.
Industrial chemists use fluoroform as a reagent in the synthesis of various organic compounds.
The structure of fluoroform is similar to that of chloroform, but it has fluorine instead of chlorine atoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chloroform - a colorless, volatile liquid (CHCl3) formerly used as an anesthetic
