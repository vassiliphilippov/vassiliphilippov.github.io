CRT

noun
cathode-ray tube; display device
7.7 0.77/m
Pronunciation: / si …ër ti / (AmE), / si …ëÀê tiÀê / (BrE)
a device consisting of a vacuum tube in which a hot cathode emits an electron beam, which is focused and deflected before striking a phosphorescent screen, commonly used in older television and computer displays

Examples

Old CRT monitor
Many vintage televisions contain a CRT that is bulky and heavy.
Scientists first used a CRT to investigate how electrons behaved inside a vacuum.
The old computer monitor used a CRT instead of an LCD screen.

Synonyms

  • cathode-ray tube
    6.0 0.27/m
    a cathode-ray tube is the full technical name for the older vacuum display device, while CRT is the short, common abbreviation used in speech and labels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cathode-ray tube - device using electron beams to create images on a screen
