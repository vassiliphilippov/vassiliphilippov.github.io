pelvimetry

noun
measurement of the dimensions of the pelvis
8.6 7.8
Pronunciation: /pel.ˈvɪm.ə.triː/ (AmE), /pel.ˈvɪm.ə.triː/ (BrE)
the measurement of the dimensions of the pelvis, especially to assess whether a woman can give birth vaginally

Examples

Pelvimetry in pregnancy
Pelvimetry is often performed in late pregnancy to assess the likelihood of a successful vaginal delivery.
In cases of suspected cephalopelvic disproportion, pelvimetry can provide critical information for delivery planning.
Some doctors use imaging techniques for pelvimetry to obtain more accurate measurements of the pelvis.
