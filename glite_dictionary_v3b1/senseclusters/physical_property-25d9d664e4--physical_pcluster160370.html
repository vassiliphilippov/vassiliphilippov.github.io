physical property

noun
measurable characteristic of matter (e.g., density, color)
5.5 3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈprɑːpərti / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
a characteristic of a material or substance that can be observed or measured without changing its composition, such as density, color, or melting point

Examples

Common physical property
Hardness is a common physical property used to identify minerals.
Scientists examined the physical property of the metal to determine its suitability for construction.
Water's boiling point is an important physical property in chemistry.
