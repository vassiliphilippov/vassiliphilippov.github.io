noncatastrophic

adjective
not disastrous
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkætəˈstrɑfɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkætəˈstrɒfɪk / (BrE)
not causing or involving complete destruction, disaster, or severe damage

Examples

A noncatastrophic failure
The engineers confirmed that the crack in the bridge was noncatastrophic and did not pose an immediate risk.
After the storm, most of the damage to the neighborhood was noncatastrophic and could be fixed quickly.
The company faced a noncatastrophic setback that delayed the project by only a few days.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catastrophic - causing great damage or disaster
  • synonym: minor - not serious or significant
