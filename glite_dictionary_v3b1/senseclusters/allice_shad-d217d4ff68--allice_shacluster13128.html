allice shad

noun
a European migratory shad fish
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪs ˌʃæd / (AmE), / ˈælɪs ˌʃad / (BrE)
a species of shad (Alosa alosa) native to European coastal waters and rivers, known for its silvery body and migratory habits

The allice shad migrates upstream to spawn in European rivers each spring.
The decline of the allice shad population is a concern for conservationists.
