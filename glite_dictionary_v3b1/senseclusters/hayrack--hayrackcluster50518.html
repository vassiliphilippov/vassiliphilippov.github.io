hayrack

noun
frame for holding or transporting hay
7.2 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈheɪ.ræk/ (AmE), /ˈheɪ.ræk/ (BrE)
a rack or frame for holding or carrying hay, either for transport or for animals to feed from

Examples

A hayrack for animals
The old farmer added a hayrack to his wagon to carry more feed during harvest.
They repaired the broken hayrack before heading out to collect the bales.
Without a hayrack, the load kept spilling onto the road as they transported the hay.
A wooden hayrack stood in the middle of the pasture, piled high with fresh hay.
Goats gathered around the hayrack, munching on the sweet-smelling grass.
The farmer filled the hayrack before bringing the cows into the barn.
