subordinate

verb
treat as less important
7.8 5.1
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈbɔːr.də.neɪt/ /; / /sə.ˈbɔːr.dɪ.neɪt/ (AmE), /sə.ˈbɔː.də.neɪt/ /; / /sə.ˈbɔː.dɪ.neɪt/ (BrE)
to treat or consider someone or something as less important or to make dependent on something else

Examples

To subordinate personal goals
He felt that his own goals had been subordinated to the needs of the company.
Management sometimes subordinates employee welfare to profit.
The needs of the group often subordinate individual desires.
He was forced to subordinate his personal desires to the needs of the group.
She is unwilling to subordinate her principles for the sake of convenience.
The company’s interests were subordinated to those of the shareholders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dominate - to have power and influence over
  • synonym: downgrade - to lower in rank or value
  • antonym: elevate - to raise something to a higher rank or position
