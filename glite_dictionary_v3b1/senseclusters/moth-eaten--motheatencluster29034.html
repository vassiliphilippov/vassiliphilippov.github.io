moth-eaten

adjective
stale or outdated; no longer original
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑθˌitn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɒθˌiːtn̩ / (BrE)
(of ideas, things, etc.) lacking originality or freshness; outdated or stale.

Examples

Moth-eaten ideas
His moth-eaten argument failed to impress the panel of judges.
I wish he would stop repeating those moth-eaten jokes every year.
The museum displayed several moth-eaten traditions that few people observe today.
