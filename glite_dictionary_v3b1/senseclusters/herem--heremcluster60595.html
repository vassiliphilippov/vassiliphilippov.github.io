herem

noun
consecrated property
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrɛm / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɛm / (BrE)
a person or object formally set apart for religious purposes or dedicated to God, especially in ancient Jewish tradition

Examples

Herem offering
The ancient community placed the golden vessel under herem as an offering to their deity.
According to tradition, land declared as herem could not be used for personal gain.
The priest explained the significance of herem in the context of religious dedication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consecration - the act of declaring something sacred
  • antonym: profane - not sacred or religious; secular
