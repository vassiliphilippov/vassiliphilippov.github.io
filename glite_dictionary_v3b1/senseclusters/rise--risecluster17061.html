rise

verb
move upward
3.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /raɪz/ /; / /raɪs/ (AmE), /raɪz/ /; / /raɪs/ (BrE)
to move upward, go higher, or ascend (physically, including objects, people, celestial bodies, or substances)

Examples

Smoke rises upward
A column of smoke began to rise from the chimney.
After the rain, mist would rise from the river.
The sun will rise at 6:30 tomorrow morning.
As the dough ferments, it will rise and may spill over the bowl.
The sunken leaves rise gently to the surface of the pond in the morning.
Watch as the bubbles rise to the top of the glass.
As the full moon rises, it casts a glow across the water.
Every morning, we watch the sun rise over the mountains.
Venus is often the first planet to rise in the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascend - to go up; to climb or move upward
  • antonym: descend - to move downward
  • synonym: expand - to increase in size, volume, or amount
  • antonym: fall - to move downwards; opposite of rise
  • antonym: set - (of a celestial body) to go below the horizon
  • antonym: sink - to go down below the surface of something, especially of a liquid
  • synonym: swell - to increase in size or volume, often gradually
