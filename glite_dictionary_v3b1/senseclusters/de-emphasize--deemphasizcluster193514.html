de-emphasize

verb
reduce importance or emphasis
8.4
Pronunciation: / diˈɛmfəˌsaɪz /; / diːˈɛm.fə.saɪz / (AmE), / diˈɛm.fə.saɪz /; / diːˈɛm.fə.saɪz / (BrE)
to reduce the importance or emphasis given to something

Examples

To de-emphasize problems
In her speech, she de-emphasized the problems and focused on the achievements.
Some modern teaching methods de-emphasize rote memorization in favor of critical thinking.
The company tried to de-emphasize its role in the controversy during the press conference.
Companies may de-emphasise short-term profits to focus on long-term growth.
Some historians de-emphasise the role of individual leaders in shaping historical events.
The teacher tried to de-emphasise competition and foster a collaborative learning environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downplay - to make something seem less important
  • antonym: emphasise - to give special importance to something
  • antonym: emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
