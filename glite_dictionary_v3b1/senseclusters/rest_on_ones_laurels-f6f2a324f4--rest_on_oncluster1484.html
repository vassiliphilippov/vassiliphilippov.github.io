rest on one's laurels

verb
stop striving after past achievements
Pronunciation: / rɛst ɑn wʌnz ˈlɔrəlz / (AmE), / rɛst ɒn wʌnz ˈlɔːrəlz / (BrE)
to be satisfied with what one has already achieved and stop putting in effort towards further success

Examples

To rest on her laurels proudly
After winning several awards, she refused to rest on her laurels and kept working hard.
He started resting on his laurels after getting promoted, which affected his performance.
If you rest on your laurels, you may soon be overtaken by more ambitious competitors.
