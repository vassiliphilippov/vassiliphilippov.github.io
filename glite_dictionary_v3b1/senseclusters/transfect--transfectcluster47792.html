transfect

verb
to insert genetic material
9.9
Pronunciation: / trænˈsɛkt / (AmE), / trænˈsɛkt / (BrE)
to introduce foreign DNA or genetic material into living cells, usually for research or experimental purposes

Examples

To transfect cells
Scientists transfect cells with a new gene to study its effects on protein production.
The laboratory team successfully transfected the mouse cells using a nonviral method.
To observe gene silencing, researchers often transfect small interfering RNA into cultured cells.
Researchers transfect the cells with a fluorescent gene to observe protein expression.
The laboratory successfully transfected the human cells to study the effects of the new DNA sequence.
To test the hypothesis, the team will transfect the sample cells with the modified plasmid.

Related Terms

  • antonym: infect - to introduce a virus or microorganism into a cell or organism
  • synonym: transform - to genetically alter a cell by introducing new DNA
