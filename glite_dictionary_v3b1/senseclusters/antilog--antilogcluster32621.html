antilog

noun
inverse function of a logarithm
6.8 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.tɪ.lɑːg/ /; / /ˈæn.tə.lɑːg/ (AmE), /ˈæn.tɪ.lɒg/ /; / /ˈæn.tə.lɒg/ (BrE)
the number that results when a given logarithm is used as the exponent of the base; the inverse function of a logarithm.

Examples

The antilog of a number
In statistical calculations, scientists often use the antilog to transform log-transformed data back to its original scale.
The antilog of 2 is 100 when using base 10.
To find the original number, you need to take the antilog of the logarithm.
