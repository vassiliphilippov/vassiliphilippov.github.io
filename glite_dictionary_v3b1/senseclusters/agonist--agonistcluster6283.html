agonist

noun
a muscle causing movement
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡənɪst / (AmE), / ˈæɡənɪst / (BrE)
a muscle that contracts to produce a specific movement while another muscle relaxes

Examples

Quadriceps agonist muscle
During a squat, the quadriceps function as the agonist muscles responsible for straightening the knee.
The biceps acts as the agonist during elbow flexion, while the triceps relaxes.
When you lift a glass, your forearm muscles serve as the agonist for the movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antagonist - a muscle that opposes the action of an agonist
