zig

noun
a sharp turn in one direction
7.8
Pronunciation: / zɪɡ / (AmE), / zɪɡ / (BrE)
a sharp or sudden turn in one direction, especially as one part of a zigzag pattern

Examples

A sharp zig
Every zig and zag in the road tested the driver's skills.
The path was full of sharp zigs that made walking a challenge.
The skier made a sudden zig to avoid an obstacle on the slope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: zag - a sharp turn in the opposite direction to a 'zig'
  • synonym: zigzag - a pattern or path involving successive sharp turns in alternating directions
