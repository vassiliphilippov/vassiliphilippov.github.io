straitlaced

adjective
excessively proper or morally strict
8.4 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstreÉªtËŒleÉªst / (AmE), / ËˆstreÉªtËŒleÉªst / (BrE)
Excessively strict in manners, morals, or opinion; having or showing very traditional or formal beliefs and disapproving of anything seen as improper.

Examples

A straitlaced person
Despite his straitlaced upbringing, he grew to embrace more liberal ideas.
Her parents are so straitlaced that they disapprove of any form of casual dating.
The headmistress was known for her straitlaced views on discipline and decorum.

Synonyms

  • straight-laced
    7.1 0.12/m
    straight-laced is a hyphenated form used to describe very proper behavior, while straitlaced names excessive strictness in manners and morals.
  • tight-laced
    8.1 0.00/m
    tight-laced often suggests exaggerated, old-fashioned propriety and control, while straitlaced focuses on strict manners and moral views.
  • puritanical
    8.2 1.02/m
    puritanical strongly forbids pleasure and self-indulgence, while straitlaced more broadly means being very strict about manners and morals.
  • Victorian
    8.2 1.22/m
    Victorian describes old-fashioned moral attitudes tied to a past era, while straitlaced describes strong, present-day strictness in manners.
  • prudish
    7.1 0.52/m
    prudish is easily shocked by sexual or naked things and avoids them, while straitlaced means broadly strict in manners and morals.
  • priggish
    9.9 0.11/m
    priggish acts self-righteous and shows moral superiority toward others, while straitlaced focuses on strict, traditional rules and behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior
  • synonym: upright - very honest, moral, and righteous in behavior
