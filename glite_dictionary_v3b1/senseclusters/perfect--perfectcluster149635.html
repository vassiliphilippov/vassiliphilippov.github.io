perfect

adjective
flawless; complete in every way
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝː.fɪkt /; / ˈpɜrfɪkt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt /; / ˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
having all the required qualities; as good as possible; without any flaws or errors

Examples

After years of practice, his technique became nearly perfect.

The bride wore a perfect white dress that fit her beautifully.
Her answer was perfect, leaving the examiner thoroughly impressed.
The chef's soufflé had a perfect texture and rose exactly as it should.
With perfect timing, the doors opened just as they arrived.
