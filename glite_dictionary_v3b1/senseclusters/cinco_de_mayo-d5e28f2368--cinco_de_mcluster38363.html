Cinco de Mayo

noun
Mexican holiday on May 5th
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪŋkoʊ də ˈmaɪoʊ / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋkəʊ də ˈmaɪəʊ / (BrE)
A holiday celebrated on May 5th, especially in Mexico and the United States, commemorating the Mexican victory over French forces at the Battle of Puebla in 1862.

Examples

Cinco de Mayo holiday
Many cities across the United States host large parades and festivals for Cinco de Mayo.
Mexican restaurants are especially busy on Cinco de Mayo, serving traditional foods and drinks.
Schools often hold special events to teach students about the history behind Cinco de Mayo.
