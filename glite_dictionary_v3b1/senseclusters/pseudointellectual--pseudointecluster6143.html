pseudointellectual

adjective
pretending to be intellectual
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl / (BrE)
pretending to be intellectual or knowledgeable, but lacking real understanding or sincerity

Examples

Pseudointellectual remarks
He made several pseudointellectual comments to impress the group, but no one was convinced.
The article was criticized for its pseudointellectual arguments that lacked real substance.
She adopted a pseudointellectual attitude, quoting philosophers she had never actually read.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intellectual - having or showing a high degree of mental ability
  • synonym: pretentious - trying to appear more important or knowledgeable than is true
