weather-bound

adjective
delayed or confined by bad weather
1.7 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈweð.ər.baʊnd/ (AmE), /ˈweð.ə.baʊnd/ (BrE)
delayed or confined by bad weather conditions, especially unable to proceed or move due to weather

Examples

Our flight was weather-bound and couldn't take off until the next morning.
The hikers were weather-bound in their cabin for two days during the storm.
