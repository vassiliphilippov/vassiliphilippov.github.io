obelisk

noun
tall, tapering stone monument
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊbəlɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒbəlɪsk / (BrE)
a tall, four-sided stone pillar that tapers towards a pointed, pyramidal top, often erected as a monument or landmark

Examples

Ancient obelisk
A striking obelisk stands in the center of the city square, attracting many tourists.
Many Roman emperors transported Egyptian obelisks to decorate their cities.
The ancient Egyptians constructed the obelisk to honor the sun god Ra.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monument - a statue, building, or other structure erected to commemorate a notable person or event
