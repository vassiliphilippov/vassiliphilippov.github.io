disequilibrium

noun
loss or lack of balance
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsiˌkwɪˈlɪbriəm / (AmE), / ˌdɪsiːkwɪˈlɪbriəm / (BrE)
a loss or lack of balance, especially in a system where some forces or elements outweigh others

Examples

Sense of disequilibrium
Inner ear infections can cause a sense of disequilibrium and dizziness.
The sudden influx of capital created a disequilibrium in the local economy.
Trade policies may lead to disequilibrium between imports and exports.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imbalance - a state of being out of balance
