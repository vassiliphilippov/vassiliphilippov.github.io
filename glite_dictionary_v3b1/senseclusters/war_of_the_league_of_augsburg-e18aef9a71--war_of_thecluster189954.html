War of the League of Augsburg

proper noun
a major European war (1689–1697) involving France and a coalition of states
9.9
Pronunciation: /wɔːr əv ðə liːɡ əv ˈɔːgz.ɜːrg/ (AmE), /wɔː əv ðə liːɡ əv ˈɔːgz.ɜːg/ (BrE)
a war fought from 1689 to 1697 in which Louis XIV of France was opposed by a coalition of European powers including Spain, the Holy Roman Empire, England, and the Dutch Republic

Examples

The War of the League of Augsburg
During the War of the League of Augsburg, many European nations united against France's growing influence.

The War of the League of Augsburg reshaped the balance of power in Europe during the late seventeenth century.
