range

noun
extent or area of influence, operation, or activity
4.5 2.5
Pronunciation: /reɪndʒ/ (AmE), /reɪndʒ/ (BrE)
the extent, area, or limits within which something acts, operates, or varies (including influence, effectiveness, values, or motion)

Examples

Range of influence
The range of this law does not extend to private property transactions.
The museum's collection covers a wide range of artistic styles and periods.
With a range of influence beyond national borders, the company became a global player.
She spoke about the range of skills required for the job.
The product has a wireless range of up to 100 meters indoors.
This medication is only effective within a certain range of dosages.
Due to his injury, his range of motion in the arm is limited.
The missile has a range of 500 kilometers, allowing it to reach distant targets.
This wireless network has a limited range, so you need to stay close to the router.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambit - the scope, extent, or bounds of something
  • synonym: extent - the area covered by something; the size or scale
  • antonym: limit - a point or level beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass
  • synonym: reach - the distance within which something can be reached or effective
  • synonym: scope - extent of the area or subject matter something deals with or applies to
