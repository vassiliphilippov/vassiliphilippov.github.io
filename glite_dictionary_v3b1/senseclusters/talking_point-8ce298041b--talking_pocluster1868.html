talking point

noun
key remark or subject for discussing a topic
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtɔː.kɪŋ pɔɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈtɔː.kɪŋ pɔɪnt/ (BrE)
a subject or remark that is likely to support an argument or generate discussion, especially one prepared in advance

Examples

Prepare talking points
She prepared several talking points to ensure the meeting stayed focused.
That statistic is often used as a talking point by critics of the policy.
The candidate's economic plan became a major talking point in last night's debate.
