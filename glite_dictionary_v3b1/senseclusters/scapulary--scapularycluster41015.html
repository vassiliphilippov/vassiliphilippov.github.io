scapulary

noun
monastic garment worn over the shoulders
Pronunciation: / ˈskæpjəˌlɛri / (AmE), / ˈskapjʊləri / (BrE)
A long, wide piece of woolen cloth worn over the shoulders with an opening for the head, forming part of a monastic habit.

Examples

A scapulary cloth
As part of his vows, he received a brown woolen scapulary from the abbot.
The scapulary is often seen hanging over the tunic in religious ceremonies.
The monk wore his scapulary as a symbol of his devotion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scapular - a similar religious garment or badge; alternate spelling
