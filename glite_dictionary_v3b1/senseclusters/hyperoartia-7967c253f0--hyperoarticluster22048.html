Hyperoartia

noun
group of jawless fish (lampreys)
9.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˌpɛroʊˈɑːrtiə / (AmE), / haɪˌpɛərəʊˈɑːtɪə / (BrE)
A group of jawless fish comprising lampreys, distinguished from hagfishes by anatomical features.

Examples

Hyperoartia lampreys
Lampreys, classified under Hyperoartia, are well-known for their distinctive round, suckerlike mouths.
Scientists study the evolutionary differences between Hyperoartia and hagfishes to understand vertebrate history.
Unlike hagfishes, members of Hyperoartia possess well-developed eyes and dorsal fins.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hagfish - an eel-shaped jawless fish related to lampreys but in a different group
  • synonym: lamprey - a jawless fish with a round, sucking mouth
