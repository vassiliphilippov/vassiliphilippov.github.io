metapopulation

noun
interacting populations
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˌpɑpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of separate populations of the same species that interact with each other in a particular area

Examples

A metapopulation group
The survival of the butterfly depends on the stability of its metapopulation.
Conservationists study the metapopulation to understand how isolated groups of animals interact.
A metapopulation can persist even if some local populations go extinct.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: population - all the members of a species living in a particular area
