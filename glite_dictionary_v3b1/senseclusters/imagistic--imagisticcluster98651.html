imagistic

adjective
using vivid imagery
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by clear, vivid imagery, especially in poetry, language, or art

Examples

An imagistic style
The poet's imagistic style creates vivid pictures in the reader's mind.
Her imagistic descriptions made the landscape come alive on the page.
Modern artists often use imagistic techniques to evoke strong emotions.
The poet's imagistic style makes every scene come alive in the reader's mind.
Her paintings are highly imagistic, filled with bright colors and detailed shapes.
Critics praised the film for its imagistic storytelling and powerful visual metaphors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evocative - bringing strong images or feelings to mind
  • antonym: literal - using words in their basic sense without imagery
  • synonym: picturesque - visually attractive, like a picture
