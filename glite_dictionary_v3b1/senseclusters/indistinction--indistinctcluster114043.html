indistinction

noun
lack of recognition
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
the state of lacking clear recognition, fame, or distinction; being obscure or unimportant

Examples

Lifelong indistinction

The artist feared that her achievements would fade into indistinction after her death.
He lived a quiet life of indistinction, never seeking the spotlight.
