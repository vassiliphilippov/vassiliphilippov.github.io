nonnarrative

noun
work without story
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈnærətɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈnærətɪv / (BrE)
a work, such as a film, poem, or artwork, that does not follow a story or narrative structure

Examples

A nonnarrative film
The director's latest film is a nonnarrative that focuses on visual impressions rather than a storyline.
Some modern art installations are intentionally nonnarrative to encourage personal interpretation.
The poetry collection was praised for its nonnarrative approach, breaking away from traditional storytelling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstract - art or work that does not represent recognizable reality
  • antonym: narrative - a work that tells a story or presents events in sequence
