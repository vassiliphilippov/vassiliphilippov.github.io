mutual exclusiveness

noun
quality of not being able to coexist or be true together
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtʃuəl ɪkˈskluːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəl ɪkˈskluːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of two or more things being such that they cannot all be true or occur at the same time

Examples

Mutual exclusiveness of events
The two hypotheses are in mutual exclusiveness; if one is true, the other must be false.
Their arguments rest on the mutual exclusiveness of the two statements.
You must choose one or the other due to their mutual exclusiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutual exclusivity - quality of being mutually exclusive
