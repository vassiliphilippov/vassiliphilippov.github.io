suiter

noun
legal claimant
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːtər / (AmE), / ˈsuːtə / (BrE)
a person who brings a legal action in a court of law; a plaintiff

Examples

A suiter files a case
The suiter presented strong evidence to support his claim in court.
After months of preparation, the suiter finally appeared before the judge.
The lawyer advised the suiter about the possible outcomes of the case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - a person being sued or accused in a court of law
  • synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
