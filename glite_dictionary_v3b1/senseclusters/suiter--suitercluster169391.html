suiter

noun
legal claimant
8.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsuÀêt…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàsuÀêt…ô / (BrE)
a person who brings a legal action in a court of law; a plaintiff

Examples

A suiter files a case
The suiter presented strong evidence to support his claim in court.
After months of preparation, the suiter finally appeared before the judge.
The lawyer advised the suiter about the possible outcomes of the case.

Synonyms

  • plaintiff
    7.5 29/m
    plaintiff is the standard legal word for someone who brings a case to court, while suiter is a less common synonym.
  • suer
    8.7 0.00/m
    suer is someone who sues to get money or recover a right, while suiter names the same role but is less used.
  • complainant
    7.0 1.48/m
    complainant formally makes a complaint and appears in many legal or official settings, while suiter specifically means a plaintiff in court.
