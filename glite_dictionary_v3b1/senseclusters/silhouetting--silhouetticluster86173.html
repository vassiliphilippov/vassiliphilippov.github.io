silhouetting

noun
dark outline effect
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪluˈɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɪluːˈɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of showing something as a dark shape or outline against a lighter background

Examples

Silhouetting a mountain
The artist achieved a dramatic effect by silhouetting the mountain range against the sunset.
Careful silhouetting in photography can create striking and memorable images.
The movie's opening scene uses silhouetting to introduce the main character in a mysterious way.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backlighting - lighting from behind to create a silhouette effect
  • antonym: highlighting - making something stand out by emphasizing it
