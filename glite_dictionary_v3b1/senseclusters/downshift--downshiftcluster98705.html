downshift

verb
to simplify life
5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.ʃɪft/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.ʃɪft/ (BrE)
to adopt a simpler, less stressful lifestyle by reducing work hours, responsibilities, or consumption

Examples

To downshift your life
After years in a high-pressure job, she decided to downshift and focus on her family.
Many people are downshifting to find more balance and happiness in their lives.
He plans to downshift by moving to a smaller town and working part-time.
