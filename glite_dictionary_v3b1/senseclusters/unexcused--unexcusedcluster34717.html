unexcused

adjective
not excused or justified
3.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnɪkˈskjuzd / (AmE), / ʌnɪkˈskjuːzd / (BrE)
(of an action or absence) not excused or justified by a valid reason or permission.

Examples

An unexcused absence
Any unexcused absence from class may affect your grade.
Repeated unexcused absences can lead to dismissal from the team.
The company has a strict policy regarding unexcused tardiness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excused - having been officially forgiven or permitted
