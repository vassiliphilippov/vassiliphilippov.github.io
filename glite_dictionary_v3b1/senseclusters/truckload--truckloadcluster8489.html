truckload

noun
large quantity
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌkˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈtrʌkˌləʊd / (BrE)
an amount or quantity that fills a truck; often used to mean a very large amount of something

Examples

A truckload of vegetables
The farmers brought a truckload of fresh vegetables to the market early in the morning.
She has a truckload of experience in managing large projects.
We received a truckload of donations after the community fundraiser.
They delivered a truckload of gravel to the construction site.
We received a truckload of new furniture for the office yesterday.
The farmer sold an entire truckload of apples at the market.
They delivered a full truckload of fresh produce to the supermarket early this morning.
The construction site received another truckload of bricks for the new building.
We donated a truckload of clothes and toys to the local charity last weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boatload - an amount that fills a boat; a large quantity
  • synonym: carload - the amount a railroad car can carry
  • synonym: cartload - an amount that fills a cart; a large quantity
  • antonym: handful - a small quantity
  • synonym: shipment - a quantity of goods sent together
