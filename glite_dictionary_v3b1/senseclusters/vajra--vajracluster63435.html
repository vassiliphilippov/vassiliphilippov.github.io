vajra

noun
ritual weapon; Indra's thunderbolt
8.7 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːdʒrə / (AmE), / ˈvɑːdʒrə / (BrE)
A ritual weapon symbolizing both the properties of a diamond (indestructibility) and a thunderbolt (irresistible force), originally the thunderbolt of the god Indra in Hindu mythology.

Examples

The vajra weapon
Ancient texts describe the vajra as a weapon wielded by Indra, king of the gods.
In Tibetan Buddhism, a vajra is used as a symbol of unbreakable strength and spiritual power.
The priest held the vajra during the ceremony to invoke divine power.
