kilodyne

noun
unit of force
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkiː.loʊ.daɪn/ /; / /ˈkɪl.ə.daɪn/ (AmE), /ˈkiː.ləʊ.daɪn/ /; / /ˈkɪl.ə.daɪn/ (BrE)
a unit of force equal to one thousand dynes, used in the centimeter-gram-second (CGS) system

Examples

One kilodyne force
The scientist recorded a force of 3 kilodynes during the experiment.
In older physics textbooks, force is sometimes measured in kilodynes instead of newtons.
A kilodyne is equal to one thousand dynes in the CGS system of units.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dyne - a unit of force in the CGS system equal to 10⁻⁵ newtons
  • degree higher: newton - the SI unit of force, equal to the force needed to accelerate 1 kg by 1 m/s²
