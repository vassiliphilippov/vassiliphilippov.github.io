nucleoprotein

noun
protein bound to nucleic acid in cell nuclei
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌnu.kli.oʊˈproʊ.tin / (AmE), / ˌnjuː.klɪ.əʊˈprəʊ.tiːn / (BrE)
a complex compound composed of a protein bound to a nucleic acid, found in the nuclei of all living cells

Examples

nucleoprotein complex
Each chromosome consists primarily of DNA and nucleoprotein structures.
Scientists are studying how nucleoprotein complexes regulate gene expression inside the cell nucleus.
The influenza virus contains a nucleoprotein that plays a key role in its replication.
