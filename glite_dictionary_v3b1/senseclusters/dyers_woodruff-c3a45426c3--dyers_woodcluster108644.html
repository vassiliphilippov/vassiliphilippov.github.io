dyer's woodruff

noun
European plant used in dyeing fabrics
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.ərz ˈwʊd.rəf/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.əz ˈwʊd.rʌf/ (BrE)
a creeping European perennial plant (Asperula tinctoria) with red or pinkish-white flowers and red roots, sometimes used as a substitute for madder in dyeing fabrics

Examples

Dyer's woodruff plant
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of dyer's woodruff across various European regions.
Textile artists sometimes prefer dyer's woodruff over madder for its unique hue.
The roots of dyer's woodruff were traditionally used to produce a red dye for fabrics.
