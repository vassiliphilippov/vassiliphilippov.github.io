credit card

noun
card for buying on credit
2.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkre.dɪt kɑːrd/ /; / /ˈkrə.dɪt kɑːrd/ (AmE), /ˈkre.dɪt kɑːd/ /; / /ˈkrə.dɪt kɑːd/ (BrE)
A small plastic card issued by a bank or financial company, allowing the holder to purchase goods or services on credit, with the obligation to pay back later, often with interest.

Examples

A credit card payment
If you lose your credit card, report it to your bank immediately.
Many online stores require a credit card for purchases.
She paid for the concert tickets with her credit card.

Related Terms

  • antonym: debit card - a payment card that deducts money directly from a checking account
