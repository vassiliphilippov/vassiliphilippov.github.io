mediate

verb
be intermediate or a link between two things
5.5 4.92/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmiËdiËŒeÉªt / (AmE), / ËˆmiËdiËŒeÉªt / (BrE)
to occupy an intermediate or middle position or to serve as a connecting link or stage between two things

Examples

Air mediates contact
A layer of air mediates between the ocean and the atmosphere.
The book mediates the gap between scientific research and public understanding.
This cultural movement mediates between traditional customs and modern practices.

Synonyms

  • act as a liaison
    5.2 0.05/m
    act as a liaison is a person who helps groups talk and work together, while mediate is a broader role linking two sides.
  • serve as a conduit
    7.5 0.12/m
    serve as a conduit is a channel that passes information between parties, while mediate implies an active middle role connecting them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intervene - to come between so as to prevent or alter a result or course of events
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart
