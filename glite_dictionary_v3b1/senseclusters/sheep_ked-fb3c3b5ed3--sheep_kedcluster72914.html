sheep ked

noun
parasitic fly of sheep and cattle
7.6
Pronunciation: / ʃiːp kɛd / (AmE), / ʃiːp kɛd / (BrE)
a wingless parasitic fly (Melophagus ovinus) that infests sheep and sometimes cattle, feeding on their blood

Examples

A sheep ked fly
A severe sheep ked problem can lead to irritation and wool damage in sheep.
The farmer treated his flock to eliminate the infestation of sheep keds.
Veterinarians often check for sheep ked during regular animal health inspections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: louse fly - a group of parasitic flies with similar habits
