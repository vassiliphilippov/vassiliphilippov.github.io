Cherenkov

proper noun
blue light phenomenon
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʃəˈrɛŋkɔv / (AmE), / tʃəˈrɛŋkɒf / (BrE)
relating to the blue light produced when a charged particle moves faster than light in a medium

Examples

Cherenkov effect
The Cherenkov effect is often observed in nuclear reactors as a blue glow.
Scientists use Cherenkov detectors to study high-energy particles in physics experiments.
The discovery of Cherenkov radiation was a major breakthrough in understanding particle physics.
