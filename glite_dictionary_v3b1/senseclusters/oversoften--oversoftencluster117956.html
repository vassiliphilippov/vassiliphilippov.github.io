oversoften

verb
to make too mild
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsɔfən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɒfən / (BrE)
to make something excessively mild, gentle, or lacking in strength, often reducing its effectiveness or impact

Examples

To oversoften criticism
If you oversoften your criticism, people may not understand the seriousness of the issue.
The teacher worried that new policies would oversoften classroom discipline and reduce respect.
Trying to oversoften the rules can sometimes lead to confusion and inconsistency.
