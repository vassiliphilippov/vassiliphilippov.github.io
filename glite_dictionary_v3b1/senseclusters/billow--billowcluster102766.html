billow

verb
to swell or bulge outward
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪləʊ / (BrE)
to swell, rise, or move outward in a rolling, wave-like mass (such as smoke, clouds, or fabric)

Examples

Smoke billows upward
Smoke started to billow from the chimney as the fire grew stronger.
The flag would billow dramatically whenever the wind picked up.
The white curtains began to billow in the summer breeze.
Clouds of steam billowed up when he opened the dishwasher after the cycle finished.
Her white dress could be seen billowing in the wind as she walked along the beach.
Thick black smoke began to billow from the windows of the burning building.
Clouds of dust billowed up behind the cars on the dirt road.
Dark smoke began to billow from the burning building, filling the sky.
The sails billowed in the strong wind as the ship moved out to sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balloon - to expand or swell out, usually with air or gas
  • synonym: surge - to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward
