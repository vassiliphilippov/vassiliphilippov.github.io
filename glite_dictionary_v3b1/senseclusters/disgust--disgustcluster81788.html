Synonyms

  • revulsion
    6.3 0.9/m
    revulsion is a sudden, often physical feeling of strong disgust, while disgust can be broader and include moral disapproval.
  • repulsion
    6.2 0.1/m
    repulsion is a strong dislike that often causes a physical urge to pull away, while disgust includes moral disapproval too.
  • repugnance
    8.4 0.1/m
    repugnance is a strong aversion often used in formal contexts, while disgust is common in everyday speech and feeling.
  • loathing
    5.2 2/m
    loathing is intense hatred mixed with disgust and anger, while disgust may be less hateful and more about revulsion.
  • abhorrence
    7.8 0.09/m
    abhorrence is deep moral horror and strong hate, while disgust can be milder and also mean physical revulsion.
  • detestation
    8.6 0.04/m
    detestation is extreme hatred often aimed at a person or action, while disgust covers both physical and moral dislike.
