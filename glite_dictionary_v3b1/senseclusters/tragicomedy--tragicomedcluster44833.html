tragicomedy

noun
literary work mixing tragedy and comedy
Pronunciation: / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
a literary or dramatic work that combines elements of both tragedy and comedy

Examples

A tragicomedy play
Her new novel has been described as a tragicomedy about family life in the modern world.
Many Shakespearean works blur the line between tragedy and tragicomedy.
The play is a brilliant tragicomedy that explores love and loss with humor and pathos.
Audiences often find tragicomedy relatable because it mirrors both the joys and pains of real existence.
Shakespeare’s tragicomedy ‘The Tempest’ blends elements of sorrow and humor.
The director decided to stage an original tragicomedy that reflected the complexities of modern life.
