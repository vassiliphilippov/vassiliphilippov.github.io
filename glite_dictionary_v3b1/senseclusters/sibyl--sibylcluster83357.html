sibyl

noun
female prophet or fortune-teller
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪl /; / ˈsɪb.ɪl / (BrE)
a woman in ancient times believed to have the power to foretell the future; a female prophet or oracle

Examples

The sibyl foretells
In art, the sibyl is often depicted holding a scroll or book of visions.
Legends describe the sibyl as delivering her prophecies in mysterious riddles.
The ancient Greeks would consult a sibyl before making important decisions.
During the ceremony, the sibyl pronounced prophecies in a trance-like state.
Many legends describe how a sibyl would deliver her messages from a cave or sacred location.
The ancient Greeks believed each sibyl could see into the future and interpret the will of the gods.

  • synonym: prophetess - a female prophet; a woman who foretells events
  • synonym: soothsayer - a person who predicts the future; fortune-teller
