colza oil

noun
oil from rapeseed, used in food and industry
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊlzə ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒlzə ɔɪl / (BrE)
a light yellow to brown oil obtained from rapeseed, used as an edible oil, lubricant, or fuel for lamps

Examples

Colza oil as lubricant
Some manufacturers choose colza oil as a natural lubricant in their machinery.
The chef prefers to use colza oil because of its light flavor in salad dressings.
Traditional lamps were once fueled with colza oil before electricity became widespread.
