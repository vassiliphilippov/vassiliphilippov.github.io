independence

noun
freedom from control or influence
2.6 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndəˈpɛndəns /; / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
the state of being free from the control or influence of others, especially when a country becomes free from another country

Examples

After years of struggle, the country finally achieved independence from colonial rule.
Financial independence is often considered an important milestone in adulthood.
Living on her own gave her a strong sense of independence.
Many Americans celebrate the Fourth of July as the anniversary of their country's independence.
Relations with Britain changed dramatically after the colonies achieved independence.
The signing of the Treaty of Paris in 1783 formally recognized American independence.
