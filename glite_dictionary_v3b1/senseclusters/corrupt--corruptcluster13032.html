corrupt

adjective
dishonest or immoral, lacking integrity
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
willing to act dishonestly for personal gain; lacking moral integrity

Examples

A corrupt official
He exposed the corrupt practices that plagued the entire organization.
The corrupt official took bribes to influence the decision.
Voters are demanding reforms to remove corrupt politicians from office.
Many believed that the entire system was corrupt and needed reform.
She refused to participate in the corrupt practices that others on the team accepted.
The government official was found to be corrupt after accepting large bribes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dishonest - not honest; disposed to cheat or defraud
  • antonym: upright - strictly honorable or honest; adhering to high moral principles
