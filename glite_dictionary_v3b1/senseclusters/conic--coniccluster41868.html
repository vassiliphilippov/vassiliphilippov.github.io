conic

noun
curve from plane intersecting a cone (geometry)
8.6 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀê.n…™k / (AmE), / Ààk…í.n…™k / (BrE)
In geometry, a curve formed by the intersection of a plane with a circular cone; common examples are ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas.

Examples

A conic curve
He drew a conic on the board to illustrate the concept to his students.
The ellipse is one type of conic found in geometry.
Understanding each conic helps solve various astronomical problems.
