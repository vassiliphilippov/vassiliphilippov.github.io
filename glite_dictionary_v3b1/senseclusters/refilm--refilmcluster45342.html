refilm

verb
to film again
3.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàf…™lm / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàf…™lm / (BrE)
to film something again, usually to improve or replace the original version or footage

Examples

To refilm a scene
The director decided to refilm the ending to make it more exciting for the audience.
After reviewing the footage, the team had to refilm several scenes due to poor lighting.
Sometimes actors are called back months later to refilm important parts of a movie.
The director decided to refilm the scene for better lighting.
After reviewing the footage, the team had to refilm several interviews.
They refilmed the ending to make it more exciting for the audience.

Synonyms

  • reshoot
    5.2 0.15/m
    reshoot means filming scenes again to correct or improve shots, while refilm means filming the whole work again to replace footage.
  • rerecord
    3.8 0.06/m
    rerecord is making a new audio or video recording to replace a previous one, while refilm specifically means filming again.
  • retape
    4.9 0.00/m
    retape is recording sound or video again often onto tape or old formats, while refilm means making a new filmed version.

Related Terms
