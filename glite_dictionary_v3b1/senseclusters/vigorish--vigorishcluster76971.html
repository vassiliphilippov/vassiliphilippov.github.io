vigorish

noun
operator's commission on bets or profits
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪɡərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡərɪʃ / (BrE)
a fee or percentage taken by a gambling operator, bookmaker, or criminal organization as commission on bets or profits

Examples

Vigorish on bets
Bookmakers make their money by charging a vigorish on every wager.
He complained that the vigorish was too high for him to make a profit.
The casino takes a small vigorish from each bet placed at the table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rake - a commission taken by a gambling house
