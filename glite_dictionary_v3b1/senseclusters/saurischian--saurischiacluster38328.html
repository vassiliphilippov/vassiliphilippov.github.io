saurischian

noun
dinosaur with a three-pronged pelvis
5.2 9.6
Pronunciation: /sɔː.ˈrɪs.kɪ.ən/ /; / /sɑː.ˈrɪs.kɪ.ən/ (AmE), /sɔː.ˈrɪs.kɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a dinosaur belonging to one of the two main divisions, having a three-pronged pelvis resembling that of a crocodile, and including both herbivorous and carnivorous species

Examples

Saurischian dinosaur
Both plant-eating and meat-eating dinosaurs can be classified as saurischians.
Scientists discovered new fossil evidence confirming the creature was a saurischian, not an ornithischian.
The Tyrannosaurus rex is a well-known example of a saurischian dinosaur.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ornithischian - dinosaur having a pelvis more like that of birds
