prowler

noun
person who sneaks around, often for crime
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpraʊlɚ /; / ˈpraʊlər / (AmE), / ˈpraʊlə / (BrE)
a person or animal who moves quietly and secretly around a place, often with the intention of committing a crime or stealing

Examples

A prowler at night
Police arrested a suspected prowler lurking behind the houses late at night.
The alarm went off when a prowler tried to enter through the back door.
The neighborhood watch was established after several reports of a prowler were made.
The security cameras recorded a prowler sneaking around the backyard late at night.
Neighbors were warned to be alert after a prowler was seen near several homes.
The dog barked loudly when it sensed a prowler outside the window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burglar - person who breaks into houses to steal
  • antonym: guard - a person who protects a place from danger or crime
  • synonym: intruder - person who enters a place without permission
