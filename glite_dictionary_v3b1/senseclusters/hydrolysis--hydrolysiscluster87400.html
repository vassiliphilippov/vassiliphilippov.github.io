hydrolysis

noun
chemical reaction involving water splitting compounds
5.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.ˈdrɑːr.lə.sɪs/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɑːr.lə.səs/ (AmE), /haɪ.ˈdrɒ.lɪ.sɪs/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɒ.lə.sɪs/ (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which water breaks down a compound, splitting chemical bonds and producing new substances

Examples

Hydrolysis reaction
During digestion, enzymes use hydrolysis to break down proteins into amino acids.
Industrial applications often use hydrolysis to process complex chemicals into simpler components.
The hydrolysis of fats results in glycerol and fatty acids.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dehydration synthesis - a chemical reaction that forms a compound by removing water
