Bastille

proper noun
fortress and prison in Paris, stormed in 1789
8.0
Pronunciation: /bæ.ˈstiːl/ /; / /bæ.ˈstɪl/ (AmE), /bæ.ˈstiːl/ /; / /bæ.ˈstɪl/ (BrE)
A fortress in Paris, originally built in the 14th century and later used as a prison, famously stormed and destroyed on July 14, 1789, at the start of the French Revolution.

Examples

Bastille Day
Every year, France celebrates Bastille Day as its national holiday.
Historians often discuss the political symbolism of the Bastille in revolutionary France.
The people of Paris stormed the Bastille on July 14, 1789, marking the beginning of the French Revolution.
