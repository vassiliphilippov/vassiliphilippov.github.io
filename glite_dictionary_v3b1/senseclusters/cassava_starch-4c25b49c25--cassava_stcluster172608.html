cassava starch

noun
starch from the cassava plant root
Pronunciation: / kəˈsɑːvə stɑrtʃ / (AmE), / kəˈsɑːvə stɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a fine, white starch obtained by leaching and drying the root of the cassava plant, used as a food ingredient and the source of tapioca

Examples

cassava starch powder
Bubble tea pearls are often made from cassava starch for their signature chewy consistency.
In many tropical countries, cassava starch is a staple ingredient in local dishes.
The baker used cassava starch to give the bread a light and chewy texture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tapioca - starch extracted from cassava root, often used for puddings
