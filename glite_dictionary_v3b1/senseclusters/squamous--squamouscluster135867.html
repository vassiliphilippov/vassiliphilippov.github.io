squamous

adjective
scale-like shape
Pronunciation: / ˈskweɪməs / (AmE), / ˈskweɪməs / (BrE)
having a flat, thin, scale-like shape or appearance, especially in cells or tissue

Examples

Squamous cells
The pathologist identified squamous cells in the tissue sample under the microscope.
The outermost layer of the skin consists of squamous epithelial cells that protect the body.
Some cancers, such as squamous cell carcinoma, begin in these flat, scale-like cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cuboidal - having a cube-like shape, especially in cells
  • synonym: scaly - covered with or resembling scales
