dicer

noun
enzyme that makes siRNA
8.7
Pronunciation: / c8da6as59r / (AmE), / c8da6as59 / (BrE)
(biology) an enzyme that cuts long double-stranded RNA into short pieces (small interfering RNAs, siRNAs) used in RNA interference to regulate or silence genes.

Examples

Dicer enzyme action
Researchers found that the dicer enzyme cuts long double-stranded RNA into short fragments.
Without functional dicer, cells cannot produce siRNAs and gene silencing fails in experiments.
The lab technician used antibodies to detect dicer levels in the tissue samples.
