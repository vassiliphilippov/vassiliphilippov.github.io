anabatic

adjective
rising up a slope (wind, air)
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.əˈbæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈbat.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a wind or air current) rising up a slope or hillside, especially due to heating of the ground surface.

Examples

An anabatic wind
Meteorologists observed a strong anabatic current rising along the southern hillside.
Pilots must account for anabatic conditions when flying near sloped terrains on sunny days.
The anabatic winds made hiking up the mountain noticeably warmer in the afternoon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: katabatic - (of a wind) moving downhill or down a slope
