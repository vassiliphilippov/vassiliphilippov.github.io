transplant

noun
organ or tissue moved in surgery
3.7 0.88/m
Pronunciation: / tr√¶nÀàspl√¶nt / (AmE), / tr√¶nÀàspl…ëÀênt / (BrE)
an organ or tissue that has been moved from one person's body to another's in a medical operation to replace a damaged or missing part

Examples

A kidney transplant
After the transplant, he had to take medication for the rest of his life.
Doctors monitored the transplant closely for signs of rejection.
The patient received a transplant to replace her failing kidney.

Synonyms

  • allograft
    8.6 0.09/m
    an allograft is a medical transplant from another person with different genes, while a transplant is tissue moved between people in surgery.
  • homograft
    8.8 0.01/m
    a homograft is a tissue transplant from a donor of the same species with different genes, while a transplant is moving tissue between people.
  • graft
    6.1 1.23/m
    a graft is a piece of living tissue placed into a person during surgery, while a transplant is moving whole organs or tissues between people.
