transplant

noun
organ or tissue moved in surgery
Pronunciation: / trænˈsplænt / (AmE), / trænˈsplɑːnt / (BrE)
an organ or tissue that has been moved from one person's body to another's in a medical operation to replace a damaged or missing part

Examples

A kidney transplant
After the transplant, he had to take medication for the rest of his life.
Doctors monitored the transplant closely for signs of rejection.
The patient received a transplant to replace her failing kidney.

Related Terms

  • synonym: graft - tissue or organ transplanted surgically
