husk

noun
dry outer covering of seeds or fruits
2.2 2.7
Pronunciation: /hʌsk/ (AmE), /hʌsk/ (BrE)
the dry outer covering of some seeds or fruits, especially grains or corn, that is usually removed before eating or processing

Examples

Corn husk
Several birds were picking at the husk left after harvesting the corn.
The floor was covered in sunflower seed husk after the party.
The rice husk is removed before the grain is sold for consumption.
After threshing, only the husk remained on the ground.
The peanut's husk must be removed before roasting the nut.
They used the husk of the corn to make crafts for the festival.

Related Terms

  • antonym: core - the central part of a fruit, containing the seeds
  • synonym: shell - hard outer covering of nuts, eggs, or some seeds
