nitrazepam

noun
a benzodiazepine sedative drug
9.0
Pronunciation: / nɪˈtræzəˌpæm / (AmE), / nɪˈtrazɪˌpam / (BrE)
a hypnotic and sedative drug of the benzodiazepine class, used for the short-term treatment of insomnia and certain seizures

Examples

nitrazepam tablets
Doctors sometimes prescribe nitrazepam to help patients cope with severe insomnia.
Long-term use of nitrazepam is generally not recommended due to the risk of dependence.
The pharmacist checked the prescription before dispensing nitrazepam tablets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diazepam - another drug in the benzodiazepine class
  • degree lower: insomnia - persistent difficulty in falling or staying asleep
