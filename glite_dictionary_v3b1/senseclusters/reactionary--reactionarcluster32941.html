reactionary

noun
person opposing progress or reform
8.8 6.8
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈæk.ʃə.nər.iː/ /; / /riː.ˈæk.ʃɪ.nər.iː/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən.ə.ri/ /; / /riː.ˈæk.ʃən.ə.ri/ (BrE)
A person who strongly opposes social or political progress or reform and favors a return to a previous state of affairs.

Examples

A reactionary politician
As a reactionary, he criticized all attempts to modernize the education system.
Some historians label the movement's leaders as reactionary forces resisting change.
The new law was opposed by every major reactionary in parliament.
