Ural-Altaic

noun
proposed group of Eurasian languages
Pronunciation: / ˌjʊrəl ˈæltaɪɪk / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəl ˈæltaɪɪk / (BrE)
a term for a (now mostly rejected) postulated group of languages that includes many indigenous languages of Russia and Central Asia, but not Russian itself.

Examples

The Ural-Altaic languages
Many older linguistic textbooks reference the Ural-Altaic hypothesis, despite modern doubts about its validity.
Scholars once grouped Finnish and Turkish together under the Ural-Altaic languages, but this classification is controversial today.
The theory that Turkic and Finno-Ugric languages are part of the Ural-Altaic family is now largely rejected by linguists.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Altaic - (disputed) language family including Turkic, Mongolic, and Tungusic languages
  • degree lower: Uralic - language family of northern Eurasia including Finnish, Hungarian, Estonian, etc.
