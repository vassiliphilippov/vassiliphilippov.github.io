equipollence

noun
logical equivalence
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkwəˈpɑːləns / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈpɒlɪns / (BrE)
the state or condition of being logically equivalent, having the same truth value or consequences as something else

Examples

Equipollence of statements
The mathematician demonstrated the equipollence of the two complex formulas.
In logic, equipollence means that two statements always have the same truth value.
The debate centered on the equipollence between competing philosophical theories.
