prosaicness

noun
dullness; lack of excitement
9.7
Pronunciation: / prəˈzeɪɪknəs / (AmE), / prəˈzeɪɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being dull, ordinary, or lacking excitement; commonplaceness

Examples

The prosaicness of life
Despite the prosaicness of the task, she approached it with diligence.
Her writing is marked by a certain prosaicness that fails to capture the reader's imagination.
They found the prosaicness of city life calming after years of constant travel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dullness - the quality of being uninteresting or lacking brightness
  • antonym: vividness - the quality of being intensely deep or bright; liveliness
