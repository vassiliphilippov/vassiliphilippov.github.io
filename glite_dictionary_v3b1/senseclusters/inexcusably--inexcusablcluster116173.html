inexcusably

adverb
in an inexcusable manner
4.5 5.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ɪk.ˈskjuː.zə.bliː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ɪk.ˈskjuː.zə.bliː/ (BrE)
in a way that cannot be excused or justified

Examples

Inexcusably careless
Arriving late once is forgivable, but forgetting the meeting entirely is inexcusably careless.
He spoke to the staff inexcusably harshly, which damaged their morale.
The manager acted inexcusably when she blamed her team for her own errors.
His rude behavior at the meeting was inexcusably disrespectful to his colleagues.
She inexcusably forgot her best friend's birthday despite several reminders.
The project was delayed inexcusably, causing frustration among all the stakeholders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excusable - able to be justified or forgiven
  • antonym: excuseably - in a manner that can be excused
  • synonym: inexcusable - too bad to be excused or justified
  • synonym: unjustifiably - without a valid reason or justification
