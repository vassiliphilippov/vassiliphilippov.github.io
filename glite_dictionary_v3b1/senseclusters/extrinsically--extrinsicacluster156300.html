extrinsically

adverb
from external sources
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈstrɪnsɪkli / (AmE), / ɛkˈstrɪnsɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is caused by or comes from outside rather than from within

Examples

To be extrinsically motivated
Some students are extrinsically motivated by grades rather than a love of learning.
The company's success was extrinsically influenced by changes in the global market.
He values achievements that are extrinsically recognized by others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intrinsically - in a way that comes from within or is inherent
