flame

noun
glowing part of fire
Pronunciation: / fleɪm / (AmE), / fleɪm / (BrE)
the bright, hot, visible part of a fire

Examples

A campfire flame
The flame from the campfire kept us warm on the chilly night.
She watched the flame dance on the tip of the match before lighting the candle.
A sudden gust of wind made the flame flicker and almost go out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blaze - a strong, bright fire
  • degree lower: ember - a small, glowing piece of coal or wood in a dying fire
  • degree higher: inferno - a large and dangerous fire
  • antonym: smoke - the visible vapor and gases given off by a burning substance
