full

adjective
having ample or wide fabric (for garments)
Pronunciation: / fʊl / (AmE), / fʊl / (BrE)
(of a garment) having ample or wide fabric, especially so as to hang loosely or form folds.

Examples

A full skirt
She wore a full skirt that swayed as she danced.
The dress was made from full sleeves that gathered at the wrist.
This season's collection features coats with full shapes and generous fabric.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tight - (of clothing) fitting closely to the body
  • synonym: wide - having a great extent from side to side
