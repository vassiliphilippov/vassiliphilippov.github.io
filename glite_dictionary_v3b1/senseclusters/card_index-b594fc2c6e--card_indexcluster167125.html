card index

noun
system of organizing information with cards
4.1 3.6
Pronunciation: /kɑːrd ˈɪn.deks/ (AmE), /kɑːd ˈɪn.deks/ (BrE)
an alphabetical listing of items, each recorded on a separate card, used especially for organizing information such as books in a library

Examples

A card index system
Before computers, the card index was the primary method for managing library collections.
She spent hours searching through the card index to locate the book she needed for her research.
The library still maintains a card index as a backup to its electronic catalog.
