insectivore

noun
organism that primarily eats insects
9.9 7.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɛktəˌvɔr /; / ɪnˈsɛktɪˌvɔr / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛktɪˌvɔː / (BrE)
an animal or organism that mainly eats insects (especially small mammals like moles and shrews)

Examples

An insectivore animal
Many birds are insectivores during the summer months, feeding on abundant insects.
Some bats are insectivores, consuming thousands of insects each night.
The anteater is a classic example of an insectivore in the animal kingdom.
Many insectivores are nocturnal, searching for prey during the night.
Most moles are insectivores that feed underground on worms and insects.
The shrew is an example of an insectivore found in many temperate regions.
