knockoff

noun
unauthorized copy or imitation
6.0 1.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ëÀêkÀå…ëf / (AmE), / Ààn…íkÀå…íf / (BrE)
An unauthorized copy or imitation of something, especially a product or item of popular design.

Examples

A knockoff jersey
He prefers the real thing and would never buy a knockoff jersey.
She realized her new handbag was a knockoff after noticing the misspelled brand name.
The market was full of knockoff watches that looked like expensive brands.

Synonyms

  • clone
    5.5 1.92/m
    a clone copies a product's design closely and without permission, while a knockoff is a cheaper, informal imitation of popular items.
  • dupe
    7.8 0.03/m
    a dupe is a cheap, informal copy sold as a substitute, while a knockoff is an unauthorized imitation of a popular product.
  • fake
    3.5 2.37/m
    a fake looks like a real item to fool buyers, while a knockoff imitates a design but may not try to pass as genuine.
  • counterfeit
    6.7 2.88/m
    a counterfeit is made to deceive by copying trademarks and sell as real, while a knockoff copies a design without always forging brands.
  • replica
    3.6 6.22/m
    a replica is an exact or museum-style copy, often made for display, while a knockoff is an unauthorized, commercial imitation sold cheaply.
  • imitation
    3.7 0.00/m
    an imitation is made to look like another thing in general, while a knockoff is a specific unauthorized copy of a popular product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterfeit - a fraudulent imitation of something valuable
  • antonym: original - the genuine or first version, not a copy
