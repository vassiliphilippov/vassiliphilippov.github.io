nonpasserine bird

noun
bird not of order Passeriformes
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈpæsəˌraɪn bɝd / (AmE), / nɒnˈpæsəraɪn bɜːd / (BrE)
A bird that does not belong to the order Passeriformes, typically including species that are chiefly arboreal, such as those in the order Coraciiformes.

Examples

Colorful nonpasserine birds
Many nonpasserine birds are known for their vibrant plumage and unique calls.
The nonpasserine bird perched quietly on a branch, blending into the surrounding foliage.
Unlike sparrows, nonpasserine birds often have different foot structures and nesting habits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: passerine - a bird of the order Passeriformes, perching birds or songbirds
