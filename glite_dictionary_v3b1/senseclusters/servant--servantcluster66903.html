servant

noun
person working for another, usually in a household
1.0 21/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…úÀêrv…ônt / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀêv…ônt / (BrE)
a person who works for another, especially in someone's home, performing domestic duties or other services

Examples

A household servant
As a servant, she was expected to be discreet and efficient in her duties.
In the 19th century, it was common for large households to have several servants.
The wealthy family employed a live-in servant to help with cleaning and cooking.

Synonyms

  • house servant
    2.3 0.06/m
    a house servant is employed to live and work inside a household doing daily chores, while a servant can work in many roles.
  • domestic
    5.8 0.52/m
    a domestic works inside a home doing cleaning or cooking for a family, while a servant may include other paid roles.
  • domestic help
    2.6 0.14/m
    domestic help does basic household chores like cleaning and cooking for pay, while a servant can have broader duties or formal status.
  • retainer
    7.0 0.62/m
    a retainer is a formal household worker kept by a family for long-term service, while a servant is a general person who works for someone.
