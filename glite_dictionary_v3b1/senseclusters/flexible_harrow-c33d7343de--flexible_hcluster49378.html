flexible harrow

noun
farm tool for weeding with flexible tines
Pronunciation: / ˈflɛksəbəl ˈhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈflɛksɪbəl ˈhærəʊ / (BrE)
an agricultural implement with fine spring tines on a frame that flexes to follow ground contours, used for weeding crops

Examples

A flexible harrow tool
A flexible harrow adapts to uneven ground, making it ideal for large crop areas.
Before planting, the crew ran a flexible harrow across the soil to prepare a smooth seedbed.
The farmer used a flexible harrow to remove weeds from the freshly planted field.
