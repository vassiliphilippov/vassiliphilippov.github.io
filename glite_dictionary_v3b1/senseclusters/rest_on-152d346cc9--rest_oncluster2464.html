rest on

verb
be based on; depend on (something)
Pronunciation: / rɛst ɑn /; / ˈrɛst ɑn / (AmE), / rɛst ɒn /; / ˈrɛst ɒn / (BrE)
to be based on or depend on something for support (such as an idea, reason, or success)

Examples

Success rests on effort
Much of Western philosophy rests on the ideas of ancient Greek thinkers.
Our conclusions rest on the data collected during the experiment.
The validity of his argument rests on several questionable assumptions.
His success rested on years of hard work and dedication.
The argument rests on the assumption that everyone has equal access to resources.
You can rest on my shoulder if you need to take a break.
