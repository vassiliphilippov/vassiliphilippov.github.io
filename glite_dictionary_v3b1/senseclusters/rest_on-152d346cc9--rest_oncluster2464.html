rest on

to be based on or depend on something for support (such as an idea, reason, or success)

Examples

Success rests on effort
Much of Western philosophy rests on the ideas of ancient Greek thinkers.
Our conclusions rest on the data collected during the experiment.
The validity of his argument rests on several questionable assumptions.

Synonyms

  • depend on
    1.0 24/m
    depend on is used when something or someone needs help or support, while rest on describes what something is based on.
  • found
    3.9 11/m
    found means to base an idea or theory on facts, while rest on often describes that same basic support.
  • repose on
    8.2 0.00/m
    repose on is a formal phrase for being based on reasons or evidence, while rest on is more common in speech.
  • rely on
    1.0 51/m
    rely on means to depend on someone for help or trust, while rest on can describe what an idea depends on.
  • build on
    3.4 15/m
    build on means to use something as a start to make more progress, while rest on often means simply to be based on.
  • count on
    1.8 20/m
    count on is informal and means trusting someone to do something, while rest on usually describes a basis or support.
