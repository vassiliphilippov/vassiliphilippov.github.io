new

adjective
not used; in original condition
1.0 160/m
Pronunciation: / nu / (AmE), / njuÀê / (BrE)
Not showing signs of use, wear, or exposure; in original, unused condition.

Examples

A new car
She drove a new car that had never been on the road before.
The book was so clean, it appeared almost new.
The furniture in the showroom looked new and untouched.

Synonyms

  • unused
    1.0 0.8/m
    unused means not used before and still untouched, while new means in original, unused condition and shows no wear.
  • brand-new
    1.2 12/m
    brand-new means fresh from the maker and completely unused, while new means in original, unused condition more generally.
  • unworn
    3.1 0.07/m
    unworn means not worn or showing any signs of use, while new means unused and in its original condition.
  • mint
    5.1 0.01/m
    mint means in perfect, as-new condition often for collectibles, while new means simply unused or not worn.
  • fresh
    1.0 19/m
    fresh means recently made or seen and can feel lively, while new focuses on lack of use and original condition.
  • nondistressed
    5.1 0.00/m
    nondistressed means not damaged or artificially aged, while new means unused and in original, unworn condition.
