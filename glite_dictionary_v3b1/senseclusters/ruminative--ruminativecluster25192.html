ruminative

adjective
deeply thoughtful; contemplative
Pronunciation: / ˈruːmɪnəˌtɪv / (AmE), / ˈruːmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
Thinking deeply or seriously about something, often in a reflective or contemplative manner.

Examples

A ruminative mood
His ruminative mood was evident during the quiet, reflective dinner with friends.
She became ruminative as she gazed out of the window, lost in her own thoughts.
The novel is praised for its ruminative exploration of grief and identity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contemplative - involving prolonged thought; thoughtful
  • synonym: thoughtful - absorbed in thought; reflective
