spruce squirrel

noun
squirrel of northern North America
9.9
Pronunciation: / sprus ˈskwɝː.əl / (AmE), / spruːs ˈskwɪr.əl / (BrE)
a small squirrel found in northern United States and Canada, known for inhabiting coniferous forests, especially those with spruce trees

Examples

A spruce squirrel in forest
A spruce squirrel can be identified by its bushy tail and reddish-brown fur.
Many hikers enjoy watching spruce squirrels gather cones in the early morning light.
The spruce squirrel is commonly seen darting among the branches in northern forests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: red squirrel - a small tree squirrel found in Europe and North America, known for its reddish fur
