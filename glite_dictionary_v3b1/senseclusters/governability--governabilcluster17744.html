governability

noun
ability to be governed
7.5
Pronunciation: /ɡʌv.ər.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ /; / /ɡə.vər.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɡʌv.ə.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ /; / /ɡə.və.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be governed or effectively managed by a government or authority

Examples

The governability of a country
The governability of a country depends on its institutions and the rule of law.
Political reforms were introduced to improve the governability of the region.
Low governability can lead to instability in a country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manageability - the quality of being easy to control or manage
  • antonym: ungovernability - the state of being impossible or very difficult to govern
