tigerfish

noun
aggressive freshwater fish
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪɡərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, aggressive freshwater fish with sharp teeth, found in African rivers and lakes

Examples

The fierce tigerfish
The tigerfish is famous among anglers for its strength and speed.
Many tourists visit Africa hoping to catch a tigerfish in the wild.
With its razor-sharp teeth, the tigerfish is a fearsome predator in the river.
The fisherman was excited to catch a massive tigerfish during his trip to the Congo River.
Local guides warn that tigerfish are aggressive and should be handled with care.
Many tourists visit African lakes hoping to see the powerful tigerfish in its natural habitat.

  • synonym: piranha - a small, aggressive freshwater fish with sharp teeth found in South America
