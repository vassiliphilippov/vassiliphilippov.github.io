tartine

noun
long tedious writing
9.5
Pronunciation: /tɑːr.ˈtiːn/ (AmE), /tɑː.ˈtiːn/ (BrE)
an informal term for a long, tedious piece of writing or speech, often repetitive or overly detailed

Examples

A boring tartine
The professor's lecture turned into a tartine that left the students bored and restless.
I struggled to finish his email because it was just a tartine about minor details.
Her speech became a tartine when she repeated the same points over and over.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harangue - a lengthy and aggressive speech
  • synonym: rant - a long, angry, or tedious speech
  • antonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points
