too far

adverb
beyond acceptable or reasonable limits
2.6 18/m
Pronunciation: / tu f…ër / (AmE), / tuÀê f…ëÀê / (BrE)
beyond what is reasonable, acceptable, or allowed; to an excessive degree

Examples

Gone too far
He realized he had gone too far when his friends stopped laughing at his joke.
Her prank was funny at first, but she pushed it too far and caused problems.
Sometimes, criticism can go too far and become hurtful.

Synonyms

  • excessively
    3.0 4/m
    excessively means doing something to a much larger degree than needed, while too far suggests crossing a reasonable limit.
  • overly
    3.5 14/m
    overly describes something more than is normal or needed, while too far highlights going beyond what is reasonable.
  • unduly
    6.9 2/m
    unduly means more than is fair or justified in a formal way, while too far covers any excess beyond reason.
  • exorbitantly
    7.3 0.2/m
    exorbitantly describes very high cost or amount that is not reasonable, while too far can mean any excessive action.
  • immoderately
    7.6 0.07/m
    immoderately means acting without restraint or limits, while too far focuses on crossing a clear reasonable or acceptable line.
  • absurdly
    5.0 2/m
    absurdly suggests something is wildly unreasonable or ridiculous, while too far is a more general term for being excessive.
  • over-the-top
    5.2 3/m
    over-the-top feels informal and flashy, often about style or drama, while too far is broader about excess or limits.
  • to a fault
    7.1 0.8/m
    to a fault describes a strong quality shown too much, often positive turned negative, while too far warns of passing a reasonable limit.
  • over the line
    8.2 0.7/m
    over the line means behaving in an unacceptable or rude way, while too far usually means simply going beyond reasonable limits.
