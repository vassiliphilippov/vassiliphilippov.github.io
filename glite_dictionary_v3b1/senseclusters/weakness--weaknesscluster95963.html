weakness

noun
a flaw or weak point
Pronunciation: / ˈwiknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknəs / (BrE)
a fault or flaw in someone's character or something that makes someone or something less effective or strong

Examples

A character weakness
He admitted that a tendency to procrastinate was his main weakness at work.
Her biggest weakness is her inability to say no to anyone who asks for help.
The team’s defensive weakness was exposed in the second half.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strength - a strong or advantageous quality; the opposite of a weakness
