shout

verb
speak loudly or call out
Pronunciation: / ʃaʊt / (AmE), / ʃaʊt / (BrE)
to speak, call, or say something in a loud voice, often to be heard, to attract attention, or to express strong emotion (including in anger)

Examples

To shout for help

The coach had to shout instructions over the noise of the crowd.
Don't shout at me—I'm standing right here.
He shouted for help when he saw the smoke.
She had to shout over the noise of the crowd to be heard.

She had to shout over the noise of the construction site to be heard.
They shouted for help when the boat started sinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abuse - to speak to someone in an insulting or offensive way
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly
  • synonym: yell - to shout loudly, typically in pain, anger, or excitement
