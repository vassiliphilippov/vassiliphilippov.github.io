baitfish

noun
small fish for bait
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small fish used as bait to attract and catch larger fish

Examples

A baitfish on hook
The fisherman placed a live baitfish on his hook to attract a big bass.
Shops near the lake sell baitfish to anglers every morning.
Using baitfish can increase your chances of catching predatory fish.
Anglers often use live baitfish to attract larger game fish in freshwater lakes.
The river was teeming with baitfish, drawing in flocks of hungry birds.
Fishermen caught several baitfish before heading out to sea for bigger catches.

  • antonym: lure - an artificial object used to attract fish
  • synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish often used as bait
  • antonym: predatory fish - fish that hunt and eat other fish
