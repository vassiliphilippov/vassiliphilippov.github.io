future perfect tense

noun
a verb tense for completed future actions
Pronunciation: / ˈfjʊtʃɚ pɚˈfɛkt tɛns / (AmE), / ˈfjʊːtʃə pəˈfɛkt tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to express an action that will be completed before a specified time in the future

Examples

The future perfect tense
In English, the future perfect tense is formed with 'will have' plus the past participle of the verb.
She will have graduated by June, which is a use of the future perfect tense.
Teachers often introduce the future perfect tense to indicate actions that will be finished before a certain moment in the future.
