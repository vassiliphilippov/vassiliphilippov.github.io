two-winged insect

noun
an insect with only one pair of wings
6.3
Pronunciation: / tuˈwɪŋd ˈɪn.sɛkt / (AmE), / tjuːˈwɪŋd ˈɪn.sɛkt / (BrE)
an insect with one pair of functional wings and reduced hind wings, commonly found in the order Diptera, which includes flies, mosquitoes, and gnats.

Examples

A two-winged insect fly
Flies are a common example of a two-winged insect found around homes.
Many two-winged insects are important for pollination as well as spreading disease.
The scientist studied the structure of a two-winged insect under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fly - a small flying insect of the order Diptera
  • synonym: mosquito - a slender long-legged fly known for biting and sucking blood
