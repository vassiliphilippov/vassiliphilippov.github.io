rectifier diode

noun
diode used to convert AC to DC
4.9 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktəˌfaɪɚ ˈdaɪoʊd / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪfaɪə ˈdaɪəʊd / (BrE)
a diode designed to allow electrical current to pass in only one direction, typically used to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) and handle high levels of current.

Examples

High-power rectifier diodes
High-power rectifier diodes are essential for industrial equipment that requires reliable DC voltage.
If a rectifier diode fails, it can cause the whole circuit to malfunction.
The rectifier diode was used to convert the alternating current into direct current in the power supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bridge rectifier - a device consisting of four diodes arranged to convert AC to DC
  • synonym: diode - an electronic component allowing current to flow in one direction
