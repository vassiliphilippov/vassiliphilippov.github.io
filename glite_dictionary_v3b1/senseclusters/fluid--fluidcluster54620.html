fluid

adjective
able to flow and change shape
5.1 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfluː.ɪd/ /; / /ˈflʊ.ɪd/ /; / /ˈfluː.əd/ (AmE), /ˈfluː.ɪd/ /; / /ˈflʊ.ɪd/ /; / /ˈfluː.əd/ (BrE)
(of a substance or material) able to flow and easily change shape; not solid or fixed in form.

Examples

Fluid material
Because oil is fluid at room temperature, it easily adapts to the shape of its container.
Her movements on the dance floor were remarkably fluid, mesmerizing the audience.
The lava became more fluid as it neared the mountain’s base, flowing rapidly downhill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
  • antonym: solid - having a definite shape and volume; not able to flow
