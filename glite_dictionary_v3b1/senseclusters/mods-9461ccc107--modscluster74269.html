Mods

noun
British 1960s youth subculture
8.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / mÉ‘dz / (AmE), / mÉ’dz / (BrE)
A British youth subculture originating in London in the early 1960s, characterized by fashionable dress, short hair, a fondness for rhythm and blues music, and traveling on motor scooters.

Examples

1960s Mods style
A group of Mods gathered at the seaside, dressed impeccably in tailored jackets.
Many Mods were passionate about the latest rhythm and blues records.
The Mods were known for their sharp suits and love of scooter culture.
