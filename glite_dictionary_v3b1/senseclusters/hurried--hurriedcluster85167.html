hurried

adjective
done or acting with haste
Pronunciation: / ˈhɝid / (AmE), / ˈhʌrɪd / (BrE)
Done or acting with excessive speed or urgency; performed quickly, often without careful attention.

Examples

A hurried apology
She gave a hurried apology before rushing out the door.
The report was so hurried that it contained several errors.
They prepared a hurried dinner due to the sudden arrival of guests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: careful - acting with care and attention
  • antonym: leisurely - acting or done at one's ease; unhurried
  • synonym: rushed - done quickly or under pressure; not thorough
