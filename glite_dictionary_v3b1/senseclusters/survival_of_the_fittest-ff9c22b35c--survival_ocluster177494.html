survival of the fittest

noun
evolutionary process favoring best-adapted organisms
5.7
Pronunciation: / sərˈvaɪvəl əv ðə ˈfɪtəst / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvəl əv ðə ˈfɪtɪst / (BrE)
the principle that only the organisms best adapted to their environment are likely to survive and reproduce, especially as a process in evolution

Examples

Survival of the fittest principle
Animal species undergo survival of the fittest as environments change and challenges arise.
Many believe that capitalism is an economic form of survival of the fittest.
The phrase survival of the fittest is often misunderstood as meaning only the strong survive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: natural selection - the process by which organisms better adapted to their environment survive and produce more offspring
