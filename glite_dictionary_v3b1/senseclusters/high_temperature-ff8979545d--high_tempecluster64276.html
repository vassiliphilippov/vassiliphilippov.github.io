high temperature

noun
an elevated degree of heat
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈtɛmpɚtʃɚ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
a state or condition in which the degree of heat present is above normal or average; an elevated temperature

Examples

A high temperature outside
A high temperature can be a sign of infection in children.
Bacteria often multiply rapidly at high temperatures.
The city experienced unusually high temperatures during the summer heatwave.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fever - an abnormally high body temperature
  • antonym: low temperature - a condition of having little heat or being cold
