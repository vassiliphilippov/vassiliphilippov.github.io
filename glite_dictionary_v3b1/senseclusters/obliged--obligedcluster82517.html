obliged

adjective
under moral or social obligation
4.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / É™ËˆblaÉªdÊ’d / (AmE), / É™ËˆblaÉªdÊ’d / (BrE)
Feeling as if one must do something because of a moral or social duty.

Examples

Be obliged to help
Guests are not obliged to bring a gift, but it is appreciated if they do.
I felt obliged to help my friend move after he asked so politely.
She did not want to go to the party, but she felt obliged to attend.

Synonyms

  • duty-bound
    5.8 0.36/m
    duty-bound feels a strong, formal pull to act from one's sense of duty, while obliged feels more like social or moral pressure.
  • obligated
    4.2 0.62/m
    obligated often refers to legal or formal duty enforced by rules, while obliged suggests a social or personal feeling of duty.
  • bound
    6.0 1.36/m
    bound usually means legally or contractually required to act, while obliged describes feeling one should act from social or moral duty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not under obligation, not required to act
  • synonym: obligated - required to do something by law or rule
