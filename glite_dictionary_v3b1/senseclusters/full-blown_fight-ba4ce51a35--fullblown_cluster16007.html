full-blown fight

noun
fully developed and intense conflict
Pronunciation: / ˌfʊlˈbloʊn faɪt / (AmE), / ˌfʊlˈbləʊn faɪt / (BrE)
A conflict or argument that has developed completely and reached its most intense or serious stage.

Examples

A full-blown fight
After hours of tension, their disagreement became a full-blown fight in front of everyone.
The movie ended with a full-blown fight between the two main characters.
What started as a minor dispute soon erupted into a full-blown fight.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: scuffle - a short, confused fight or struggle at close quarters
