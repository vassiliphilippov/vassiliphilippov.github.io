divagation

noun
wandering from main topic
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪvəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪvəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a wandering or deviation from the main path, topic, or course (either literally or in speech/writing)

Examples

Occasional divagation
His speech was structured, but there was occasional divagation that confused the main argument.
Readers may find the author's frequent divagations both entertaining and distracting.
The professor's divagation into personal anecdotes made the lecture run over time.
During our hike, a sudden divagation led us off the marked trail and into the woods.
Her lecture was filled with divagation from the main topic, which confused the audience.
The author's frequent divagation made the book challenging to follow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
  • synonym: diversion - an instance of turning something aside from its course
