skeletal

adjective
extremely thin, like a skeleton
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛlətl / (AmE), / ˈskɛlətl / (BrE)
Extremely thin, as if reduced to the bones, especially due to illness, hunger, or cold.

Examples

Skeletal appearance
After months on the run, the stray dog looked skeletal and weak.
His face had become so skeletal that his cheeks seemed hollow.
Many famine victims appeared skeletal because of prolonged hunger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food
  • synonym: gaunt - extremely thin and bony, especially from hunger or illness
  • antonym: plump - having a full, rounded shape, opposite of very thin
