hurry

verb
move or act quickly
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜri / (AmE), / ˈhʌri / (BrE)
to move or act quickly, usually because there is not much time

Examples

To hurry to school
If we hurry, we can still catch the bus.
She told everyone to hurry or they would be late for the meeting.
They hurried through the rain to reach the shelter.
If we hurry, we can catch the next train before it leaves.
She had to hurry to finish her assignment on time.
They hurried down the street as the rain started to pour.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dawdle - to move slowly and waste time
  • synonym: rush - to move quickly or with urgent haste
