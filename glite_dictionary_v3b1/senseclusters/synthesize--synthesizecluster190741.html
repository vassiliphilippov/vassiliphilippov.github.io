synthesize

verb
combine elements to form a whole
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnθəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθəsaɪz /; / ˈsɪnθɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to combine different things, ideas, or substances to form a complete or more complex whole, especially in scientific or intellectual contexts

Examples

To synthesize ideas
Her report managed to synthesize several different perspectives into a cohesive argument.
Modern computers can synthesize realistic speech from written text.
The scientist attempted to synthesize new compounds by combining existing chemicals.
Her essay skillfully synthesizes the ideas of several experts into a coherent argument.
Plants synthesize their food from sunlight through the process of photosynthesis.
Researchers were able to synthesize the new compound in the laboratory.
Modern chemists can synthesise complex molecules that do not exist in nature.
She attempted to synthesise traditional and modern perspectives in her research.
The computer can synthesise human speech with remarkable accuracy.
