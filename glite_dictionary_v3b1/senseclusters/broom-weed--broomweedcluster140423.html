broom-weed

noun
annual plant with woody branches and yellow flowers
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbruːmˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈbruːmˌwiːd / (BrE)
An annual plant of the southwestern United States with rigid, woody branches, sticky foliage, and yellow flowers.

Examples

Yellow broom-weed flowers
Large fields of broom-weed turn yellow when the flowers are in full bloom.
The rancher struggled to control the spread of broom-weed on his property.
