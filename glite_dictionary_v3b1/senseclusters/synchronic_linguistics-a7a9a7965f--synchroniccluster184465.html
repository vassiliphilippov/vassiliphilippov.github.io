synchronic linguistics

noun
study of language at a given time
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈkrɑnɪk lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / sɪnˈkrɒnɪk lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the study of a language at a specific point in time, without considering its historical development

Examples

Synchronic linguistics study
Synchronic linguistics focuses on language as it exists at a particular moment, rather than on historical changes.
Ferdinand de Saussure's theories greatly influenced the development of synchronic linguistics.
Some linguists prefer synchronic linguistics because it offers a snapshot of current language structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diachronic linguistics - the study of language change over time
