buffoon

noun
ridiculous or clownish person
Pronunciation: / bəˈfuːn /; / bəˈfun / (AmE), / bəˈfuːn / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a silly, ridiculous, or clownish way to amuse others

Examples

A buffoon on stage
His loud jokes made everyone laugh, but many considered him a buffoon rather than a clever entertainer.
She was tired of being treated like a buffoon at work just for making people laugh.
The politician's antics earned him a reputation as a buffoon among his critics.
After his outburst at the meeting, everyone thought of him as a buffoon.
He acted like a buffoon to keep the children entertained during the party.
The king kept a buffoon at court to make his guests laugh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clown - a comic entertainer or someone who amuses others by jokes or antics
  • synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • synonym: jester - historical entertainer who amuses with jokes and antics
  • antonym: sage - person regarded as wise or sensible
