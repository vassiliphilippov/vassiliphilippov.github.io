norther

noun
a wind blowing from the north
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrðər / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðə / (BrE)
A strong wind that blows from the north, often bringing cold weather.

Examples

A strong norther wind
A sudden norther swept across the plains, dropping the temperature quickly.
Fishermen stayed in port to avoid the strong norther expected tonight.
The crops suffered damage when an early norther hit the region in October.

Related Terms

  • antonym: souther - a wind that blows from the south
