ship's bell

noun
nautical time unit signaled by a ship's bell
2.7
Pronunciation: / ʃɪps bɛl / (AmE), / ʃɪps bɛl / (BrE)
(nautical) One of the eight half-hour divisions of time marked by the striking of a ship's bell, traditionally used to signal the passage of time on board ship; eight bells marks 4:00, 8:00, or 12:00 o'clock, a.m. or p.m.

Examples

Eight ship's bells
At four ship's bells, the crew gathered for their midday meal.
The captain checked his pocket watch and listened for the sound of the ship's bell to signal the next change of watch.
The watch ended at eight ship's bells, just as the sun rose over the horizon.
