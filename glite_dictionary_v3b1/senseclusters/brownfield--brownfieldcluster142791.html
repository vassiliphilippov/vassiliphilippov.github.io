brownfield

noun
abandoned industrial land
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnˌfiːld / (BrE)
a piece of land previously used for industrial or commercial purposes, often contaminated or abandoned, that may require cleanup before reuse

Examples

An old brownfield site
The city is investing in cleaning up the old brownfield to build new housing.
Developers face challenges when redeveloping a brownfield due to possible soil contamination.
Turning a brownfield into a park can revitalize a neglected neighborhood.
The city is investing in the redevelopment of a large brownfield near the river.
Developers must assess environmental risks before building on a brownfield site.
Turning a brownfield into a public park can revitalize a neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • antonym: greenfield - land that has never been used for industrial or commercial purposes
