constitutional economics

noun
economic study of constitutional frameworks
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.stəˈtu.ʃə.nəl ˌɛk.əˈnɑ.mɪks / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən.əl ˌiː.kəˈnɒm.ɪks / (BrE)
A branch of economics that studies how constitutional rules and frameworks affect economic policy and outcomes.

Examples

constitutional economics study
He wrote his doctoral dissertation on constitutional economics and its implications for democratic governance.
Some policy reforms are best analyzed through the lens of constitutional economics.
The field of constitutional economics examines how constitutions structure the incentives of government officials.

Related Terms
