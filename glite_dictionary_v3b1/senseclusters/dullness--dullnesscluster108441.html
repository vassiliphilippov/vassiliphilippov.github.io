dullness

noun
lack of brightness or vividness
5.3 1.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌl.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈdʌl.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of lacking visual brightness or vividness; not being shiny or bright

Examples

Dullness of color
After years of sun and rain, the metal lost its shine and showed clear dullness.
Clouds often bring a dullness to the landscape that makes everything seem muted.
The dullness of the paint made the room appear dreary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brightness - the quality of being visually bright or luminous
  • synonym: dimness - the quality of being faint or not clear with regard to light
