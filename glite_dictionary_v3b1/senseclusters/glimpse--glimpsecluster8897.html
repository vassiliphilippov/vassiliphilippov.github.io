glimpse

noun
a brief look
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɡlɪmps / (AmE), / ɡlɪmps / (BrE)
a brief or quick look at something; a short or partial view

Examples

A quick glimpse
He only managed a brief glimpse of the answer before the page turned.
She caught a glimpse of the celebrity as he hurried through the airport.
Through the open window, I had a glimpse of the garden beyond.
Out of the corner of his eye, he got a glimpse of something moving in the shadows.
She caught a glimpse of the actor before he disappeared into the car.
The tour offered visitors a rare glimpse into the ancient temple's inner chambers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glance - a brief look; similar meaning
  • degree higher: look - to direct one's gaze in a specified direction
  • synonym: peek - a quick or furtive look
  • antonym: stare - to look for a long time; opposite in duration
