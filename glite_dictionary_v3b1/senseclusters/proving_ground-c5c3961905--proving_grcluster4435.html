proving ground

noun
place for testing new things
Pronunciation: / ˈpruvɪŋ ˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpruːvɪŋ ˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a place where new equipment, technologies, or ideas are tested to determine their effectiveness or reliability

Examples

A proving ground for products
The company uses its innovation center as a proving ground for experimental products.
The job served as a proving ground for young engineers eager to test their skills.
The new missile was first launched at a military proving ground in the desert.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laboratory - a place equipped for scientific experiments and research
  • synonym: test site - a place where tests are conducted, especially for weapons or equipment
