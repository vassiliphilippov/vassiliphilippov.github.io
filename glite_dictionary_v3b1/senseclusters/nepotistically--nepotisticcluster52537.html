nepotistically

adverb
with family favoritism
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛpəˈtɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌnɛpəˈtɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that shows favoritism to relatives, especially by giving them jobs or advantages

Examples

Hired nepotistically a relative
The director hired his brother nepotistically, ignoring more qualified applicants.
Some companies are criticized for promoting employees nepotistically rather than based on merit.
She was accused of acting nepotistically when she gave her cousin a key role in the project.
