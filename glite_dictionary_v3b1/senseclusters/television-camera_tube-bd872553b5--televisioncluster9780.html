television-camera tube

noun
device converting images to electronic signals
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləˈvɪʒən ˈkæmərə tuːb / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈvɪʒən ˈkæmərə tjuːb / (BrE)
a device, formerly used in television cameras, that scans an optical image and converts it into electronic signals for transmission or processing.

Examples

Old television-camera tube
Early broadcast studios relied on television-camera tubes to capture and transmit live images.
Modern digital cameras have replaced the television-camera tube with more efficient image sensors.
The invention of the television-camera tube revolutionized the television industry in the twentieth century.
