blunt

adjective
not sharp; rounded or broad end
1.0 3.95/m
Pronunciation: / blÊŒnt / (AmE), / blÊŒnt / (BrE)
not sharp or pointed; having a dull or rounded edge or end

Examples

Blunt knife
Be careful, the blunt scissors won't cut paper easily.
The chef replaced his blunt knife because it was difficult to slice vegetables.
The child drew with a blunt crayon, making thick and uneven lines.
A blunt razor can make shaving uncomfortable and even cause skin irritation.
After months of use, the chef realized his favorite knife had become blunt.
You need a sharp scissors for fabric; this one is too blunt to cut cleanly.

Synonyms

  • dull
    1.0 1.17/m
    dull describes a blade or edge that has lost sharpness, while blunt means it has a rounded or non-pointed end.
  • obtuse
    7.5 0.05/m
    obtuse describes a leaf tip that is broadly rounded, while blunt applies more generally to any object with a dull or non-pointed edge.

Related Terms
