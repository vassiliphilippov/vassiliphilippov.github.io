pemmican

noun
concentrated food of dried meat and fat
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛmɪkən / (AmE), / ˈpɛmɪkən / (BrE)
A traditional food made from lean dried meat that is pounded fine and mixed with melted fat, sometimes with added berries, especially used by Indigenous peoples of North America.

Examples

Traditional pemmican food
Explorers relied on pemmican as a lightweight and high-calorie food during their journeys.
He learned how to prepare pemmican from a local Indigenous community.
Traditional pemmican was often made with berries to add flavor and nutrition.
