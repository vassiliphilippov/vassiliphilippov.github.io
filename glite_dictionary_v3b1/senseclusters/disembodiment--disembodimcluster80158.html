disembodiment

noun
state without body
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɛmˈbɑːdimənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪmˈbɒdimənt / (BrE)
the state of being separated from a physical body or having no physical form

Examples

Soul's disembodiment
The novel describes the disembodiment of the soul after a near-death experience.
Virtual reality can create a sense of disembodiment as users feel detached from their physical selves.
Philosophers have long debated the possibility of disembodiment in the context of consciousness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: embodiment - the state of having a physical body or form
  • synonym: incorporeality - the quality of not having a physical body
