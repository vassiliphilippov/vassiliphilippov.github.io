endemic typhus

noun
infectious disease from flea bites

An acute infectious disease caused by rickettsia bacteria and transmitted by the bite of an infected flea, characterized by fever, chills, muscle aches, and rash.

Examples

endemic typhus fever
During the outbreak, several patients were diagnosed with endemic typhus after developing fever and rash.

Her research focuses on understanding how endemic typhus spreads in urban environments.
