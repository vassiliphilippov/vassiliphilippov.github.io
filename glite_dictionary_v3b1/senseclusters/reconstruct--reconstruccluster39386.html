reconstruct

verb
to build or restore again
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌkt /; / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌkt /; / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to build or restore something again after it has been damaged or destroyed

Examples

To reconstruct a building
After the storm, they decided to reconstruct the damaged wing of their house.
The team will reconstruct the old farmhouse using original materials where possible.
We need permission from the city to reconstruct this historic building.
After the fire, they had to reconstruct their entire home from the ground up.
Engineers worked tirelessly to reconstruct the railway after the earthquake.
The city plans to reconstruct the bridge that collapsed during the storm.
After the flood, volunteers worked tirelessly to reconstruct the damaged homes in the community.
Authorities hope to reconstruct the bridge within a year to restore traffic flow.
The museum plans to reconstruct the ancient statue from its scattered fragments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demolish - to destroy a building completely
  • synonym: rebuild - to build again after destruction
  • synonym: renovate - to repair and improve a building, especially an old one
  • synonym: repair - to fix or mend (something) that is broken or damaged
  • synonym: restore - to bring back to former or original state
