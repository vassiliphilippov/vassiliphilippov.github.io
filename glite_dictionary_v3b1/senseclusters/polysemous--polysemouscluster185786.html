polysemous

adjective
having more than one meaning (of words)
7.4
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪsɪməs / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪsɪməs / (BrE)
(of a word) having multiple meanings or senses.

Examples

Polysemous word
English contains many polysemous terms that often confuse learners.
Researchers analyzed how polysemous words are understood in different contexts.
The word 'bank' is polysemous, as it can refer to a financial institution or the side of a river.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monosemous - (of a word) having only one meaning or sense
