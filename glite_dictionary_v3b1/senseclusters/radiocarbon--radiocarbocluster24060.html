radiocarbon

noun
radioactive carbon isotope
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈkɑrbən / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
a naturally occurring radioactive isotope of carbon, used especially in radiocarbon dating to determine the age of ancient objects

Examples

The radiocarbon isotope
Levels of radiocarbon in the atmosphere have varied over centuries.
Scientists used radiocarbon to date the ancient wooden artifact.
The presence of radiocarbon in fossils helps determine their age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbon-14 - the specific radioactive isotope of carbon with mass number 14
