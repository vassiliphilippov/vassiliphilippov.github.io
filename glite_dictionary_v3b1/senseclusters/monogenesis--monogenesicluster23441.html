monogenesis

noun
single origin theory
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the theory or process by which diverse things originate from a single source or ancestor

Examples

Monogenesis of languages
The idea of monogenesis suggests that all human languages share a common origin.
Some scientists debate whether life on Earth is a result of monogenesis or multiple origins.
The theory of monogenesis is often contrasted with polygenesis in discussions about human ancestry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polygenesis - the theory that things originate from multiple sources or ancestors
