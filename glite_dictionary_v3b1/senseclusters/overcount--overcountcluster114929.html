overcount

verb
to count too many
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
to count something more than once or report a number higher than the actual value

Examples

To overcount the votes
If you overcount the survey responses, the results will be inaccurate.
The auditor warned that the company might overcount its inventory if not careful.
Researchers must ensure they do not overcount participants when analyzing the data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miscount - to count incorrectly
  • antonym: underestimate - to estimate a number lower than the actual value
