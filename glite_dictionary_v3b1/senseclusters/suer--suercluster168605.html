suer

noun
person who initiates a lawsuit
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːə / (BrE)
a person who brings a case to court, especially one who sues for redress or the recovery of a right

Examples

A suer in court
After months of negotiation, the suer finally agreed to a settlement.
In civil cases, the suer must prove the respondent's liability.
The suer provided extensive evidence to support her claim in court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - the person being accused or sued in a court of law
  • synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
