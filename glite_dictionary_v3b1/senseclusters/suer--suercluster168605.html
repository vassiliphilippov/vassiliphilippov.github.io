suer

noun
person who initiates a lawsuit
8.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsuÀê…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàsjuÀê…ô / (BrE)
a person who brings a case to court, especially one who sues for redress or the recovery of a right

Examples

A suer in court
After months of negotiation, the suer finally agreed to a settlement.
In civil cases, the suer must prove the respondent's liability.
The suer provided extensive evidence to support her claim in court.

Synonyms

  • plaintiff
    7.5 29/m
    plaintiff is the formal legal name for someone who starts a court lawsuit, while suer is a more general term for that person.
  • complainant
    7.0 1.48/m
    complainant makes a formal complaint or opens legal proceedings in court, while suer specifically sues to recover a right or redress.
  • claimant
    5.1 2.41/m
    claimant makes a claim for a right, benefit, or title, while suer brings a court suit aiming to obtain legal redress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - the person being accused or sued in a court of law
  • synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
