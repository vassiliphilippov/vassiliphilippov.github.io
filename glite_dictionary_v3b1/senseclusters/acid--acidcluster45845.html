acid

adjective
having acidic properties
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈasid / (BrE)
having the properties or characteristics of an acid; typically sharp, sour, or corrosive in nature.

Examples

Acid solution
Certain chemicals give the water an acid reaction.
Lemon juice has distinctly acid properties that help it act as a natural cleaner.
The solution had an acid taste, making it unsuitable for drinking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alkaline - having the properties or characteristics of an alkali
