family Myristicaceae

noun
botanical family of aromatic tropical trees
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli maɪˌrɪstɪˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli mɪˌrɪstɪˈsiːaɪ / (BrE)
A family of aromatic tropical trees, known as the nutmeg family, characterized by trees with fleshy, arillate seeds.

Examples

Botanists are studying seed dispersal mechanisms within the family Myristicaceae.
Many members of the family Myristicaceae thrive in humid, lowland forests of Southeast Asia.
The family Myristicaceae includes well-known species such as the true nutmeg tree.
