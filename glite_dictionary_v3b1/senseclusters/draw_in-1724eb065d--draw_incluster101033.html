draw in

verb
move closer to surround or approach
2.6
Pronunciation: / drɔː ɪn / (AmE), / drɔː ɪn / (BrE)
to move closer and surround or approach something or someone, often with the intention of capture or control

Examples

To draw in the group
As the competitors tired, the pack started to draw in on the leader.
The fog began to draw in, making it hard to see the coastline.
The soldiers continued to draw in around the building, preparing for their final move.
