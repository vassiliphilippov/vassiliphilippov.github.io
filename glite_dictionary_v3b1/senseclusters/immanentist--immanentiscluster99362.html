immanentist

noun
believer in divine immanence
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmənəntɪst / (AmE), / ˈɪmənəntɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that the divine or ultimate reality exists within the world rather than outside it

Examples

An immanentist thinker
As an immanentist, she saw the sacred in everyday life and nature.
The debate between the immanentist and the transcendentalist shaped the philosophy class.
Many modern theologians are influenced by immanentist ideas about divinity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: transcendentalist - a person who believes the divine exists beyond the physical world
