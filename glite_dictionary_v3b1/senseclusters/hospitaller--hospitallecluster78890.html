hospitaller

noun
member of hospital religious order
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːspɪˌtælər / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪtələ / (BrE)
a member of a religious order, especially in the Middle Ages, dedicated to caring for the sick and running hospitals

Examples

A hospitaller in hospital
During the Crusades, a hospitaller would offer care to wounded knights and pilgrims.
He dressed in the traditional robes of a hospitaller, ready to begin his duties at the monastery hospital.
The hospitallers devoted their lives to charity and tending to the sick in their communities.
