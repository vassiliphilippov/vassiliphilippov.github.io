indiscrimination

noun
inability to distinguish
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˌskrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an inability or reduced ability to perceive or distinguish between different sensory stimuli

Examples

Indiscrimination of sounds
The audiologist noted the patient's indiscrimination between similar tones during the hearing assessment.
Visual indiscrimination can make it difficult for some people to tell colors apart.
Children with sensory processing issues may show indiscrimination when identifying different textures.
