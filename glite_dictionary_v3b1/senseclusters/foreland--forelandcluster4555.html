foreland

noun
land at a forward edge or margin
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌlænd /; / ˈfɔːrˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.lənd / (BrE)
an area of land that sticks out into the sea or forms the forward edge of something, such as a headland or promontory

Examples

A foreland by the sea
A wide foreland protects the cliffs from direct wave impact.
The coastal foreland is home to many unique bird species.
The mountain foreland stretched out before the rising peaks.
From the clifftop, we had a stunning view of the foreland jutting out into the blue waters below.
The ancient lighthouse stood atop the foreland, guiding ships through the night.
Waves crashed against the rocky foreland during the storm, sending spray high into the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: headland - a narrow piece of land that sticks out from the coast into the sea
  • synonym: promontory - a high point of land that juts out into the sea or a large lake
