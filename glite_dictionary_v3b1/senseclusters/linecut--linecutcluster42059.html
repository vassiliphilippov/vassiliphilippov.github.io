linecut

noun
print made from a line drawing
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪnˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnˌkʌt / (BrE)
a print or engraving made from a line drawing, used in printing or illustration, usually showing strong contrast between black lines and white spaces

Examples

A linecut print
He collects books illustrated with linecuts from the early twentieth century.
The magazine featured a beautiful linecut of the newly built city hall.
This linecut was produced directly from the artist's original pen-and-ink sketch.
Artists often preferred linecut techniques for illustrating scientific works.
The book includes several linecuts created by nineteenth-century engravers.
The old magazine featured a detailed linecut of the city's skyline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engraving - a print made from an engraved plate, block, or other surface
  • antonym: halftone - photographic reproduction with varying tones, as opposed to strong black-and-white lines
  • synonym: woodcut - a print made from a design cut in wood
