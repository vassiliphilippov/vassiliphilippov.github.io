force

verb
make move by using strength
Pronunciation: / fɔrs / (AmE), / fɔːs / (BrE)
to move, push, or squeeze something or someone with physical effort or strength

Examples

To force the door
He forces the window closed whenever it gets windy.
She had to force the door open after it got stuck.
The mechanic forced the bolt free using a metal bar.
He forced himself between the closing elevator doors at the last second.
She managed to force her car into a narrow parking spot.
We had to force our suitcase into the overhead compartment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jam - to force something into a space
  • antonym: pull - to move something toward oneself using force
  • synonym: push - to move something by exerting force on it, often away from oneself
  • synonym: shove - to push someone or something roughly
  • synonym: squeeze - to force into a small space
