force

verb
make move by using strength
1.9 26/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs / (AmE), / f…îÀês / (BrE)
to move, push, or squeeze something or someone with physical effort or strength

Examples

To force the door
He forces the window closed whenever it gets windy.
She had to force the door open after it got stuck.
The mechanic forced the bolt free using a metal bar.
He forced himself between the closing elevator doors at the last second.
She managed to force her car into a narrow parking spot.
We had to force our suitcase into the overhead compartment.

Synonyms

  • push
    1.0 108/m
    push moves something away using steady pressure from hands or body, while force means moving or squeezing with strong effort.
  • drive
    1.0 28/m
    drive pushes or propels something forward with power over time, while force refers to any moving, pushing, or squeezing effort.
  • shove
    2.7 1.91/m
    shove is a quick, strong push often done roughly, while force covers moving, pushing, or squeezing by strength more generally.
  • squeeze
    2.8 12/m
    squeeze presses something tightly to reduce space or shape, while force means to move, push, or squeeze using strength.
  • muscle
    6.6 2.99/m
    muscle means to force one's way in using strength or influence, while force simply means to move, push, or squeeze physically.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jam - to force something into a space
  • antonym: pull - to move something toward oneself using force
  • synonym: push - to move something by exerting force on it, often away from oneself
  • synonym: shove - to push someone or something roughly
  • synonym: squeeze - to force into a small space
