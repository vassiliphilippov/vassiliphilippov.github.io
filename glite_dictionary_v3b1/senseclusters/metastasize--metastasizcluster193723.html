metastasize

verb
spread of disease within the body
8.7 6.8
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈtæ.stə.saɪz/ (AmE), /mə.ˈtæ.stə.saɪz/ (BrE)
(of cancer or disease) to spread from the original place to other parts of the body

Examples

To metastasize to liver
Doctors discovered that the tumor had metastasized to her liver.
If left untreated, certain cancers can metastasize very quickly.
The news that his cancer had metastasized was devastating to his family.
Doctors were concerned that the cancer might metastasize if not treated promptly.
Researchers are investigating how cancer cells metastasize to distant organs.
When a tumor metastasizes, it can make treatment much more difficult.
Doctors discovered that the tumor had metastasised to the patient's liver and lungs.
If left untreated, the cancer may metastasise and become more difficult to manage.
The oncologist explained how rapidly some cancers can metastasise.
