Malayo-Polynesian

adjective
of the Malayo-Polynesian language family
8.8
Pronunciation: / məˌleɪoʊˌpɑːləˈniːʒən / (AmE), / məˌleɪəʊˌpɒlɪˈniːʒən / (BrE)
relating to the Malayo-Polynesian branch of the Austronesian language family, which includes languages spoken in Southeast Asia, the Pacific, and parts of Madagascar

Examples

Malayo-Polynesian languages
Many languages spoken in Indonesia and the Philippines are classified as Malayo-Polynesian.
Scholars have studied the migration patterns of Malayo-Polynesian speakers across the Pacific islands.
The Malayo-Polynesian languages are known for their complex systems of verb affixation.
