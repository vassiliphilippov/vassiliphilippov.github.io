collared lizard

noun
a lizard with a collar-like neck marking
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑlərd ˈlɪɡzɚd / (AmE), / ˈkɒləd ˈlɪɡzəd / (BrE)
a robust long-tailed lizard of central and western United States and northern Mexico, characterized by two dark bands around its neck resembling a collar

Examples

A collared lizard in grass
A young collared lizard darted across the trail and disappeared into the grass.
Hikers in the American Southwest may spot a collared lizard basking on sunlit rocks.
The collared lizard is known for the distinctive black bands around its neck.
