Ruminantia

noun
suborder of cud-chewing mammals
Pronunciation: / ruːˌmɪniˈænʃiə /; / ˌruːmɪˈnænʃiə / (AmE), / ruːˌmɪniˈantɪə /; / ˌruːmɪˈnantɪə / (BrE)
a group (suborder) of mammals, including cattle, sheep, goats, deer, antelopes, giraffes, and camels, that chew cud

Examples

Giraffes, included in the Ruminantia, have evolved long necks for feeding on tall trees.

The digestive system of the Ruminantia allows them to extract nutrients from tough plant material.
Cows, sheep, and deer all belong to the Ruminantia suborder.
