Jean Racine

proper noun
French tragedian and dramatist (1639–1699)
Pronunciation: / ʒɑn rəˈsin / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ ʁaˈsin / (BrE)
(1639–1699) A French dramatist known for his tragedies inspired by Greek and Roman themes and his association with Jansenism.

Examples

Jean Racine French playwright
Many critics regard Jean Racine as one of the finest French playwrights of the 17th century.
She studied the influence of Jean Racine on modern drama in her literature course.
The works of Jean Racine often explore complex human emotions and moral dilemmas.
