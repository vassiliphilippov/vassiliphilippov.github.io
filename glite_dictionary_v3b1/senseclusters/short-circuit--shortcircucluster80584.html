short-circuit

verb
bypass or avoid a difficulty
6.3 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʃɔrt ˈsɜr.kɪt /; / ˌʃɔrt ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
to avoid, bypass, or prevent a process or development, often by taking a shortcut or impeding progress

Examples

To short-circuit the process
He tried to short-circuit the lengthy approval process by contacting the director directly.
Some companies attempt to short-circuit regulations, risking legal trouble in the process.
You can't simply short-circuit hard work if you want lasting results.
Budget cuts could short-circuit the entire research project before it even starts.
Fear of failure may short-circuit your attempts to try new things.
New regulations could short-circuit the adoption of innovative technology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward or make progress
  • synonym: bypass - to go around or avoid something
  • synonym: hinder - to create difficulties resulting in delay or obstruction
  • synonym: impede - to delay or prevent by obstructing; hinder
