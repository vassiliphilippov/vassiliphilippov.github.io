substantial

adjective
having real substance or reality
Pronunciation: / səbˈstæn.ʃəl / (AmE), / səbˈstæn.ʃəl / (BrE)
having real substance; existing in reality or capable of being regarded as fact; not imaginary

Examples

Substantial evidence found
After days of searching, they finally found substantial evidence supporting their theory.
His achievements are substantial, not just wishes or unfounded rumors.
The argument lacks substantial grounds and relies mostly on speculation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insubstantial - lacking substance or reality; imaginary or not real
