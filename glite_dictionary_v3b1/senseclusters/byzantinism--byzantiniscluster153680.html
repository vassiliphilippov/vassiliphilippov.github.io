byzantinism

noun
excessive complexity
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɪˈzæntɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / bɪˈzæntɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
a system, practice, or style marked by excessive complexity, bureaucracy, or intrigue, often making processes difficult to understand or navigate

Examples

The byzantinism of rules
The byzantinism of the new regulations frustrated everyone trying to follow them.
Critics argued that the company's byzantinism slowed innovation and discouraged new ideas.
He was overwhelmed by the byzantinism of the university's administrative procedures.
