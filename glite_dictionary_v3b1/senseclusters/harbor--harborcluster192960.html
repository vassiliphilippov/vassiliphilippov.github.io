harbor

verb
secretly hide or shelter (someone)
4.8 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːr.bər/ (AmE), /ˈhɑː.bə/ (BrE)
to give shelter or refuge to someone, especially secretly and often with reference to fugitives or criminals

Examples

To harbor a criminal
Anyone found to harbor a fugitive will be prosecuted.
She was accused of harboring a known criminal in her apartment.
The family decided to harbor the refugees despite the risks.
Anyone found to harbour a fugitive will be prosecuted under the law.
It's a crime to harbour anyone who is running from the police.
She was accused of having harboured the escaped prisoner in her basement for weeks.

  • synonym: conceal - to hide or keep secret
  • antonym: expose - to make something visible or known
