gill

noun
respiratory organ in aquatic animals
Pronunciation: / ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪl / (BrE)
a respiratory organ found in many aquatic animals, used to extract oxygen from water

Examples

Fish gills
Fish use their gills to absorb oxygen from the water.
The biologist examined the gills of the newly discovered species for signs of disease.
Unlike mammals, most aquatic animals rely on gills for respiration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lung - respiratory organ in air-breathing animals
