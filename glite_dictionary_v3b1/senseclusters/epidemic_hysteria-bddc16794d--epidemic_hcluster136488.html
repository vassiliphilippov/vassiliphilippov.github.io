epidemic hysteria

noun
widespread, simultaneous panic or agitation in a group
6.3 7.0
Pronunciation: /e.pɪ.ˈdem.ɪk hɪ.ˈstɪr.i.ə/ /; / /e.pə.ˈdem.ɪk hɪ.ˈstɪr.i.ə/ (AmE), /e.pɪ.ˈdem.ɪk hɪ.ˈstɪə.ri.ə/ /; / /e.pə.ˈdem.ɪk hɪ.ˈstɪə.ri.ə/ (BrE)
A phenomenon in which a large group of people simultaneously exhibit extreme emotional agitation or irrational behavior, often spreading quickly within the community.

Examples

An epidemic hysteria outbreak
A sudden outbreak of epidemic hysteria caused panic throughout the city.
Reports of illness in the school led to an incident of epidemic hysteria among students and teachers.
Social media can sometimes accelerate the spread of epidemic hysteria by amplifying fear and rumors.
