directional microphone

noun
microphone designed to pick up sound from one direction
5.9 3.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈrek.ʃən.əl ˈmaɪ.krə.foʊn/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrek.ʃən.əl ˈmaɪ.krə.foʊn/ /; / /də.ˈrek.ʃən.əl ˈmaɪ.krə.foʊn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈrek.ʃən.əl ˈmaɪ.krə.fəʊn/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrek.ʃən.əl ˈmaɪ.krə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
A microphone designed to capture sound mainly from a particular direction, minimizing sound from other directions.

Examples

A directional microphone
Some podcast hosts prefer a directional microphone for its ability to focus on their speech.
The music producer used a directional microphone to isolate the singer's voice from background noise.
When filming outdoors, a directional microphone helps reduce the sound of wind and crowd chatter.
