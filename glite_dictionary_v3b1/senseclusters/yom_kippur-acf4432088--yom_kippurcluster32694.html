Yom Kippur

noun
Jewish Day of Atonement and fasting
5.8
Pronunciation: /joʊm kɪ.ˈpʊr/ /; / /jɑːm kɪ.ˈpʊr/ (AmE), /jəʊm kɪ.ˈpʊə/ (BrE)
The holiest day in the Jewish calendar, observed with fasting and prayer as a day of atonement and repentance, occurring on the 10th of Tishri.

Examples

Yom Kippur fasting
Every year, Jews around the world solemnly observe Yom Kippur with a day of fasting and prayer.
Many people spend Yom Kippur abstaining from work and focusing on spiritual renewal.
On Yom Kippur, the synagogue is filled with worshippers seeking forgiveness and reflection.
