gloam

noun
twilight; dusk after sunset (archaic)
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊm / (AmE), / ɡləʊm / (BrE)
(literary or archaic) The period of twilight or dusk just after sunset.

Examples

The gloam of evening
A soft breeze rose as the gloam enveloped the quiet village.
In the gloam, the outlines of the trees grew hazy and mysterious.
They wandered through the fields in the gentle gloam of evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dusk - the darker stage of twilight, just before night
  • synonym: twilight - the soft light from the sky when the sun is below the horizon
