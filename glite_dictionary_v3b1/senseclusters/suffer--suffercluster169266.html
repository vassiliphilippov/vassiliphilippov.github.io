suffer

verb
experience pain or distress
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfɚ /; / ˈsʌfər /; / ˈsʌf.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌfə /; / ˈsʌf.ə / (BrE)
to experience pain, illness, distress, or something unpleasant or harmful

Examples

To suffer pain
After eating the spoiled food, he began to suffer from severe stomach cramps.
Many people suffer from chronic back pain.
She had to suffer in silence after the accident.
He suffered severe criticism after the incident was made public.
Many ancient towns suffered destruction during the war.
The activists refused to flee and suffered imprisonment for their beliefs.
He suffered head injuries in the car accident.
Many people suffer from allergies during the spring months.
She suffered a broken wrist when she slipped on the ice.

  • antonym: prosper - to be successful, especially financially; to thrive
