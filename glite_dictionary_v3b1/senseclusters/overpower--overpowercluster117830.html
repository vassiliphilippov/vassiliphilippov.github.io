to strongly affect or overwhelm someone so they cannot think or react normally, especially with emotions, sensations, or stimuli

Examples

Overpower the senses
He was overpowered by a sudden wave of nostalgia when he entered his old school.
She tried to remain calm, but overpowering fear made her voice tremble.

Synonyms

    overwhelm affects someone emotionally or mentally so they cannot cope, while overpower emphasizes strong force that stops clear thinking.
  • overcome
    4.1 6.52/m
    overcome leaves a person unable to resist or control emotions suddenly, while overpower stresses an external force that blocks normal thought.
  • sweep over
    4.6 0.86/m
    sweep over hits someone suddenly like a wave of feeling, while overpower points to a strong force that overwhelms thought.
  • overtake
    5.1 2.18/m
    overtake surprises a person with sudden emotion so they lose control, while overpower suggests being crushed by a strong force.
  • whelm

    whelm means to engulf or cover someone, often in older or literary use, while overpower is common and direct in speech.
