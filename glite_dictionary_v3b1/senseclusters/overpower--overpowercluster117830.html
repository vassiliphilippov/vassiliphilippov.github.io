overpower

verb
to be strongly affected by something
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to strongly affect or overwhelm someone so they cannot think or react normally, especially with emotions, sensations, or stimuli

Examples

Overpower the senses
He was overpowered by a sudden wave of nostalgia when he entered his old school.
She tried to remain calm, but overpowering fear made her voice tremble.
The strong perfume quickly overpowered my senses as I walked into the shop.
She was overpowered by grief and couldn't speak for several long, quiet minutes.
The strong smell of paint overpowered everyone as soon as they entered the room.
He tried to stay calm, but his emotions overpowered him during the speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone less affected by strong emotion
  • synonym: overwhelm - to overpower emotionally or physically
  • synonym: subdue - to bring under control or overcome
