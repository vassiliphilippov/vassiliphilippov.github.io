scrape

verb
to scratch repeatedly
1.7
Pronunciation: / skreɪp / (AmE), / skreɪp / (BrE)
to scratch, rub, or remove material from a surface by rubbing it with something hard or sharp

Examples

To scrape paint
He scraped his nails along the chalkboard during class.
She scraped at the old paint with a knife until the surface was clean.
The cat scraped at the armchair until threads came loose.
Be careful not to scrape the paint off the walls while moving the furniture.
He fell and scraped his knee on the pavement.
She used a spoon to scrape the burnt bits from the bottom of the pan.
She used a knife to scrape the ice off the windshield before driving to work.
You need to scrape the old paint from the wall before applying a new coat.
He scraped his boots on the mat to remove the mud before entering the house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrade - to wear away by friction
  • antonym: polish - to make a surface smooth and shiny
  • synonym: rub - to move something back and forth against a surface
  • synonym: scratch - to make shallow cuts or marks with a sharp object, especially repeatedly
