potato blight

noun
disease of potatoes caused by fungus
7.4 4.2
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈteɪ.toʊ blaɪt/ (AmE), /pə.ˈteɪ.təʊ blaɪt/ (BrE)
A plant disease affecting potatoes, especially caused by the fungus Phytophthora infestans, resulting in rot of leaves and tubers.

Examples

Potato blight disease
A new strain of potato blight has emerged, threatening potato yields worldwide.
Farmers constantly monitor their fields for signs of potato blight to prevent crop loss.
The spread of potato blight devastated crops across Ireland in the nineteenth century.
