hyperpolarization

noun
increase in membrane potential
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an increase in a cell's membrane potential, making the inside more negative than its usual resting state

Examples

Neuron hyperpolarization
During hyperpolarization, the neuron becomes less likely to fire an action potential.
The process of hyperpolarization helps regulate the timing of nerve impulses in the brain.
Certain medications can cause hyperpolarization of cardiac cells, affecting heart rhythm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depolarization - a decrease in membrane potential making the inside less negative
