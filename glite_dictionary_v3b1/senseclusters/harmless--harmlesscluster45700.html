harmless

adjective
not able to cause harm; safe
1.0 10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërml…ôs / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêml…ôs / (BrE)
not causing or capable of causing harm or injury; safe.

Examples

Harmless to humans
After testing, the chemical was found to be harmless to humans.
She assured us that the prank was meant to be harmless fun.
The snake was completely harmless despite its frightening appearance.

Synonyms

  • innocuous
    6.5 2.63/m
    innocuous is not harmful or likely to offend in everyday settings, while harmless means not able to cause harm.
  • noninjurious
    3.9 0.00/m
    noninjurious causes no physical injury in medical or legal talk, while harmless is more common for things that do no harm.
  • nondamaging
    3.5 0.01/m
    nondamaging does not cause damage to people or objects, while harmless means it cannot cause injury.
  • safe
    1.0 153/m
    safe means protected from danger or risk in a situation, while harmless describes something that does not cause harm.
  • benign
    6.2 3.53/m
    benign seems gentle or medically nonsevere and often feels positive, while harmless simply means not able to harm.
  • innocent
    1.0 6.01/m
    innocent often means not guilty or without bad intent, while harmless focuses only on lacking harmful power.
