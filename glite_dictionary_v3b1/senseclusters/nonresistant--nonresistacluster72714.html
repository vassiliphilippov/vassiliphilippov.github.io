nonresistant

adjective
not resistant; susceptible
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.rɪˈzɪstənt /; / ˌnɑn.rɪˈzɪs.tənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈzɪstənt /; / ˌnɒn.rɪˈzɪs.tənt / (BrE)
not resisting or unable to resist; easily affected by something or yielding to force, pressure, or influence

Examples

The nonresistant patient
Because their immune systems are nonresistant, the elderly are at greater risk during flu season.
She was nonresistant to the new medication, so the treatment proved highly effective.
Some crops are genetically nonresistant to certain pests and require additional protection.
He remained nonresistant during the entire negotiation process.
Materials that are nonresistant to water will quickly deteriorate in humid conditions.
The bacteria were shown to be nonresistant to the common antibiotic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compliant - inclined to agree with others or obey rules
  • antonym: resistant - offering resistance to something or someone
  • synonym: susceptible - likely to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
