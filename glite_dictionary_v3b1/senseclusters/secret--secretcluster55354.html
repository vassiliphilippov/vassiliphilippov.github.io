secret

adjective
high-level official classification
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːkrət / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrɪt / (BrE)
(of official documents or information) classified at a high level, above confidential and below top secret, and protected from general access for security reasons.

Examples

Secret documents
She was denied access to files labeled secret due to security clearance regulations.
Some information is classified as secret, requiring special authorization to view.
The government released a formerly secret report after twenty years.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: confidential - a lower level of official classification, below 'secret'
  • degree higher: top secret - the highest level of official classification for documents
