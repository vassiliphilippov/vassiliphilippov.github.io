heat-seeking missile

noun
missile that tracks heat emissions
6.5 4.1
Pronunciation: /hiːt ˈsiː.kɪŋ ˈmɪs.əl/ (AmE), /hiːt ˈsiː.kɪŋ ˈmɪs.əl/ /; / /hiːt ˈsiː.kɪŋ ˈmɪs.aɪl/ (BrE)
a missile equipped with a guidance system that homes in on the infrared radiation emitted by targets, such as aircraft engines

Examples

Modern heat-seeking missiles
Modern heat-seeking missiles can home in on the exhaust heat of jet engines.
Pilots use flares to divert incoming heat-seeking missiles away from their planes.
The fighter jet launched a heat-seeking missile at the enemy aircraft.
