streetwise

adjective
shrewd and experienced in city life
Pronunciation: / ˈstriːtˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstriːtˌwaɪz / (BrE)
Having experience and knowledge necessary to deal with the dangers and challenges of life in a city, especially the ability to avoid trouble or take advantage of opportunities.

Examples

Be streetwise in city
Growing up in New York City made her especially streetwise and alert to her surroundings.
His streetwise attitude helped him avoid scams and trouble.
It's important to be streetwise when traveling alone in unfamiliar urban areas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naïve - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • synonym: savvy - well-informed and shrewd; knowledgeable, perceptive
