yin-yang

noun
Chinese dualism of opposites in balance
7.8 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈjɪn.jæŋ/ (AmE), /ˈjɪn.jæŋ/ (BrE)
a concept from Chinese philosophy describing how seemingly opposite or contrary forces are interconnected and interdependent in the natural world, and how they give rise to each other as they interrelate

Examples

The yin-yang balance
Many people seek a yin-yang balance between work and relaxation in their lives.
The artist used black and white to symbolize yin-yang in her painting.
The peaceful garden was designed to embody the principles of yin-yang.
