gray

adjective
of a color between black and white
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ / (BrE)
of a color between black and white; having an achromatic color of any lightness intermediate between black and white.

Examples

Gray sweater
She chose a gray sweater because it matched almost everything in her wardrobe.
The sky was covered with gray clouds all afternoon.
Wisps of gray hair peeked out from under the old man's hat.
Mist rose over the grey pavement after the rain stopped.
She wore a grey dress that matched the overcast sky.
The old wolf's fur had turned mostly grey with age.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: black - of the darkest color, the result of the absence of light
  • synonym: gray - alternative spelling (especially in American English) of 'grey'
  • degree higher: white - of the color of snow or milk; the lightest color
