preganglionic

noun
nerve before ganglion
Pronunciation: / priˌɡæŋɡliˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / priˌɡæŋɡliˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
a nerve cell or fiber that originates in the central nervous system and projects to an autonomic ganglion

Examples

A preganglionic neuron
A single preganglionic can synapse with many postganglionic neurons in the autonomic ganglion.
Damage to a preganglionic neuron can disrupt signals to the body's organs.
Researchers studied how each preganglionic fiber connects to the autonomic nervous system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postganglionic - a nerve cell or fiber that originates in an autonomic ganglion
