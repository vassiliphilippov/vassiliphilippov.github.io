Federal

adjective
of the Union in the Civil War (US history)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛd.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈfed.ər.əl / (BrE)
relating to the northern United States and those supporting the Union during the American Civil War.

Examples

Federal uniform
He wore a blue Federal uniform, marking him as a soldier for the Union.
Many Federal generals later became prominent figures in American politics.
The Federal army advanced quickly to reclaim the city from Confederate forces.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Confederate - relating to the southern states during the American Civil War
  • synonym: Union - the northern states supporting the federal government during the Civil War
