passband

noun
range of allowed frequencies
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsˌbænd / (BrE)
the range of frequencies that can pass through a filter or system without significant loss or reduction in strength

Examples

The passband range
The engineer adjusted the passband to allow only the desired radio frequencies through the filter.
A wider passband can improve sound quality but may also let in unwanted noise.
Digital filters are designed to have a sharp cutoff outside the passband.
The radio only receives signals within its designated passband.
Engineers adjusted the filter to widen the passband for better audio quality.
If the frequency falls outside the passband, the signal will be greatly reduced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandwidth - the width of a frequency range that a system can transmit
  • antonym: stopband - a range of frequencies blocked by a filter
