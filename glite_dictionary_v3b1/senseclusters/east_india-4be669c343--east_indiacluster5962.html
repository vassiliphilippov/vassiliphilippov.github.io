East India

proper noun
historical term for Southeast Asian islands
4.9
Pronunciation: / ist ˈɪndi.ə / (AmE), / iːst ˈɪndɪə / (BrE)
a historical term for a group of islands in Southeast Asia, mainly the Malay Archipelago, lying between Asia and Australia

Examples

East India islands
During the colonial era, traders traveled extensively through the East India islands in search of spices.
Many rare species can be found only in the rainforests of East India.
Maps from the seventeenth century often labeled the region as East India rather than as Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: East Indies - the islands of Southeast Asia between Asia and Australia
  • antonym: West Indies - the islands of the Caribbean Sea
