Burgundian

noun
East Germanic people
Pronunciation: / bɜrˈɡʌndiən / (AmE), / bɜːˈɡʌndiən / (BrE)
a member of the Burgundians, an East Germanic people who lived in late Roman and early medieval Europe

Examples

Ancient Burgundian tribe
A skilled Burgundian craftsman contributed to the construction of the ancient city.
The Burgundians played a significant role in the history of early medieval Europe.
Historians study the migration patterns of the Burgundian people during the fall of the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Frank - a member of a West Germanic people in early medieval Europe
  • synonym: Goth - a member of another East Germanic people in ancient Europe
