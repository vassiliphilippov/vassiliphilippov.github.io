buttoned-down

adjective
very conventional; unimaginative
Pronunciation: / ˌbʌtndˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌbʌtndˈdaʊn / (BrE)
(of a person, attitude, or environment) very conventional, unimaginative, and conservative, especially in dress or behavior.

Examples

A buttoned-down style
Despite his buttoned-down appearance, he had a quirky sense of humor outside of work.
She felt out of place in the buttoned-down office, where everyone wore gray suits and spoke formally.
The company's buttoned-down culture discouraged innovative thinking and risk-taking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conventional - following traditional forms and expectations
  • antonym: unconventional - not bound by or conforming to convention
