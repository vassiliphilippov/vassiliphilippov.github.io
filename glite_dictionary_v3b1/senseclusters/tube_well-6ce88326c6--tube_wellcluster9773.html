tube well

noun
well made by inserting a tube to reach water
6.0
Pronunciation: / tuːb wɛl / (AmE), / tjuːb wɛl / (BrE)
A well constructed by driving a tube or pipe into the ground to reach water-bearing strata, used for extracting groundwater.

Examples

A tube well pump
A new tube well was drilled to irrigate the fields during the dry season.
After the old hand pump failed, they relied on the tube well for water.
The villagers installed a tube well to provide clean drinking water for their community.
