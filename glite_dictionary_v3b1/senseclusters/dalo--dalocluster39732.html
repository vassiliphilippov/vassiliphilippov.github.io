dalo

noun
Pacific herb grown for edible root or ornament
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːləʊ / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant of the Pacific islands, cultivated in tropical regions for its edible root and in temperate regions as an ornamental for its large, glossy leaves; also known as taro.

Examples

Fresh dalo root
She bought fresh dalo from the market to prepare for tonight’s dinner.
The glossy leaves of the dalo plant make it popular in temperate ornamental gardens.
Traditional Fijian dishes often feature dalo as a staple side root.

Related Terms

  • synonym: taro - A tropical plant grown for its starchy edible root, also called Colocasia esculenta
