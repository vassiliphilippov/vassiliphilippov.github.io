webbing

noun
skin or membrane connecting animal toes
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛbɪŋ / (BrE)
a membranous structure resembling a web, especially the skin connecting the toes of some animals, such as birds and amphibians

Examples

Duck's toe webbing
Many aquatic birds have webbing between their toes to help them swim efficiently.
The duckling's webbing was clearly visible as it paddled in the pond.
The frog's webbing allows it to move quickly through water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: membrane - a thin layer of tissue covering a surface; a biological partition
