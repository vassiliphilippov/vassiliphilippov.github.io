spore

noun
reproductive cell of plants, fungi, or microorganisms
Pronunciation: / spɔr / (AmE), / spɔː / (BrE)
a small, typically single-celled reproductive body produced by nonflowering plants, fungi, and some microorganisms, capable of developing into a new individual without sexual fusion

Examples

A mushroom spore
Fern plants produce spores on the undersides of their leaves.
Many mushrooms release their spores into the air to reproduce.
Under the microscope, the scientist identified a single spore from the mold sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seed - the fertilized, matured ovule of a flowering plant, capable of developing into a new plant
