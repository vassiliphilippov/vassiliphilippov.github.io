overcommitment

noun
excessive resource allocation
6.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ (BrE)
the practice or state of allocating more resources, especially in computing, than are physically available, risking performance issues or failure

Examples

Server overcommitment
The system administrator warned that overcommitment of memory could lead to server crashes.
Virtual machines may run slowly if there is significant overcommitment of CPU resources.
To maximize efficiency, some data centers rely on overcommitment but monitor performance closely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overallocation - the act of assigning more resources than are available
  • antonym: undercommitment - the act of allocating fewer resources than are available
