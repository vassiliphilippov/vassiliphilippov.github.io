signum

noun
Roman military standard
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪg.nəm/ (AmE), /ˈsɪg.nəm/ (BrE)
a military standard, banner, or emblem used by ancient Roman units to identify and rally soldiers

Examples

Roman signum banner
The soldiers gathered around the signum before heading into battle.
Each Roman cohort carried its own signum as a symbol of unity and pride.
Losing the signum in battle was considered a great dishonor for the unit.
