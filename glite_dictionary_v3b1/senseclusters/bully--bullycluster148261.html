bully

verb
intimidate or threaten
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
to intimidate, threaten, or harm someone, especially someone weaker or less powerful

Examples

To bully a classmate
He used his size to bully the smaller children on the playground.
She refused to be bullied into making a decision she was uncomfortable with.
Some managers bully their employees into working overtime without extra pay.
He used to bully his classmates at school until the teachers intervened.
She refused to let anyone bully her into making a decision she wasn't comfortable with.
The manager was accused of trying to bully the staff into working overtime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coerce - to force someone to do something by using threats or intimidation
  • synonym: harass - to trouble or annoy someone persistently
  • synonym: intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone, especially to make them do what you want
  • antonym: protect - to keep someone safe from harm or danger
