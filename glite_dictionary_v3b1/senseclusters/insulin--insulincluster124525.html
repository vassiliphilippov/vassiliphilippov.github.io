insulin

noun
hormone controlling blood sugar
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.sjə.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the pancreas that regulates the amount of sugar in the blood

Examples

Insulin hormone
A shortage of insulin can cause serious health complications.
Diabetics may need daily insulin injections to manage their condition.
Research into insulin has led to improved treatments for diabetes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: glucagon - a hormone that raises blood sugar levels
