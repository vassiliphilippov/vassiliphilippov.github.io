Fabian

adjective
gradual, cautious strategy; avoiding direct conflict
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.bɪ.ən / (BrE)
using a cautious and gradual approach to achieve a goal, especially by avoiding direct confrontation and wearing down opposition over time

Examples

Fabian tactics
He was criticized for his Fabian reluctance to engage in direct debate.
The party adopted a Fabian strategy, gradually implementing reforms over several years.
Their Fabian tactics frustrated opponents who expected swift confrontation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggressive - pursuing goals forcefully and directly
  • synonym: delaying - causing a postponement or retardation of action
