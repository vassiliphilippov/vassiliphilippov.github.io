detrital

adjective
made of fragments
9.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtraɪtəl / (AmE), / dɪˈtraɪtəl / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of loose fragments or particles, especially those resulting from the breakdown of rock

Examples

Detrital rock fragments
The geologist studied the detrital layers to understand the history of the river valley.
Sandstone often contains a high percentage of detrital grains from older rocks.
The detrital material at the bottom of the lake was analyzed for mineral content.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consolidated - formed into a solid mass
  • synonym: fragmentary - consisting of small parts or pieces
