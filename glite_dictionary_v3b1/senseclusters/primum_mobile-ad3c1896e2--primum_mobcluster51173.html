primum mobile

noun
first cause; prime mover in philosophy
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪməm ˈmoʊbɪli / (AmE), / ˌpraɪməm ˈməʊbɪli / (BrE)
(in philosophy) a first cause or prime mover that initiates motion or existence but is itself uncaused, often referring to God or a similar ultimate agent.

Examples

The primum mobile cause
In medieval philosophy, the primum mobile was considered the source of all motion in the universe.
Many theologians debate whether a primum mobile is necessary to explain existence.
The concept of a primum mobile reflects humankind’s search for an initial, uncaused cause.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prime mover - the initial cause of motion or existence, especially in philosophical contexts
  • synonym: uncaused cause - a philosophical concept referring to something that causes others without being caused itself
