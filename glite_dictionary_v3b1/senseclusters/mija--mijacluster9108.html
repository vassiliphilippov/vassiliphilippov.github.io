mija

noun
affectionate for daughter
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːhɑː / (AmE), / ˈmiːhɑː / (BrE)
an affectionate term used to address a daughter, young girl, young woman, or close female friend (especially in Spanish-speaking communities)

Examples

Dear mija
Her grandmother smiled and said, "mija, come help me in the kitchen."
Whenever she was sad, her mother would comfort her by calling her mija.
In many families, parents affectionately refer to their daughters as mija.
Don't worry, mija, I'll walk you home after the party tonight.
Her grandmother smiled and said, 'Come here, mija, let me give you a hug.'
You did a great job on your test, mija!

Related Terms

  • synonym: dear - a term of endearment for someone you care about
  • antonym: mijo - an affectionate term for a boy or young man, from Spanish 'mi hijo'
  • antonym: son - a male child in relation to his parents
  • synonym: sweetheart - a person who is loved, especially a child or young person
