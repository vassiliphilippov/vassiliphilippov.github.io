pretext

verb
to impersonate for information
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛkst / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtɛkst / (BrE)
to obtain information or access by pretending to be someone else or using a false identity, especially for fraudulent purposes

Examples

To pretext information
The hacker was pretexting as a company manager to gain access to confidential files.
She reported that someone had been pretexting her bank to obtain her account details.
Security experts warn employees about the dangers of pretexting in social engineering attacks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authenticate - to prove that something is real or genuine
  • synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • synonym: impersonate - to pretend to be another person
