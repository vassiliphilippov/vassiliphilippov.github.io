karat

noun
unit measuring gold purity
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkær.ət/ (AmE), /ˈkær.ət/ (BrE)
A unit of measurement for the purity of gold, based on a scale of 24; 24-karat gold is pure gold.

Examples

Karat gold purity
She prefers jewelry with the highest karat rating for its purity.
The necklace was marked as 24-karat gold, indicating it was pure.
This ring is made of 18-karat gold, giving it a rich, yellow color.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carat - unit of weight for gems (note: 'carat' in British English is also used for gold purity)
