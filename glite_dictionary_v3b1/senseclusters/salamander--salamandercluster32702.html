salamander

noun
a lizard-like amphibian
Pronunciation: / ˈsæləˌmændər / (AmE), / ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
a small amphibian with a slender body and tail, typically resembling a lizard, which lives on land but returns to water to breed

Examples

A forest salamander
A brightly colored salamander darted under a log in the forest.
Many species of salamander are threatened by habitat loss and pollution.
The salamander spends most of its life on land and returns to ponds only to breed.
