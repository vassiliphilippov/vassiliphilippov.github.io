intuitionistic

adjective
guided by intuition
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnˌtuːɪʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪntjuːɪʃəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
based on or guided by immediate intuition rather than formal proof or systematic reasoning

Examples

Intuitionistic choice
Her decision about the design was intuitionistic, guided more by feeling than by market research.
The artist's intuitionistic approach led to a unique and original painting.
Some leaders rely on intuitionistic thinking when making important choices under pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analytical - using logical reasoning or systematic analysis
  • synonym: intuitive - based on what feels true without conscious reasoning
