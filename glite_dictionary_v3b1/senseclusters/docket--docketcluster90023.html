docket

verb
to summarize and list a legal document
7.9 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkɪt / (BrE)
to officially record or enter something (such as a case or document) onto a list or schedule, especially in a legal or official context

Examples

To docket a case
Attorneys docketed the motion for summary judgment in the official record.
The court clerk will docket each case as soon as the paperwork arrives.
The legal team dockets every new document to ensure nothing is overlooked.
After the appeal was filed, it was docketed for a hearing next month.
The court clerk docketed several new cases this morning.
The judge asked if the matter had been properly docketed before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: register - to record or enroll officially
  • antonym: remove - to take off a schedule or list, usually with reference to cases or tasks
  • synonym: schedule - arrange or plan (an event) to take place at a particular time
