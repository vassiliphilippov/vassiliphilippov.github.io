web

noun
the weblike part of a feather
3.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /web/ (AmE), /web/ (BrE)
a fine net or structure made by a spider, or a thin, flat or connecting part in animals or fabric (such as a spider's web, the web of a feather, the skin between animal toes, or a woven fabric)

Examples

A spider web
Damage to the web of the feather can affect a bird's ability to fly.
Feathers have a central shaft with a web on each side.
The bird's colorful web gleamed in the sunlight.
A smooth web was formed before the cloth was cut into pieces.
As the web moved through the loom, imperfections were carefully removed.
The machine produced a continuous web of fine linen.
Most ducks have a web between their toes to help them swim.
Otters use the web between their paws for efficient movement in water.
The frog stretched out its foot, showing the thin web connecting its toes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloth - fabric made by weaving or knitting fibers
  • synonym: cobweb - a spider's web, especially an old or dusty one
  • synonym: net - a piece of material made of string or thread woven together
  • synonym: textile - type of cloth or woven fabric
  • synonym: vane - the flattened, expanded part of a feather on either side of the shaft
  • synonym: webbing - the skin or material forming the web between toes; also refers to strong woven fabric
