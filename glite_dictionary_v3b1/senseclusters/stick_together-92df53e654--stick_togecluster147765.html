stick together

verb
remain loyal and united
Pronunciation: / stɪk təˈɡɛðər /; / stɪk təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / stɪk təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to remain united and support each other, especially during difficult times

Examples

To stick together always
During hard times, it is important for family to stick together.
Even when their business failed, Mark and his brother stuck together and started over.
No matter what happens, the members of our team always stick together.
It's important for friends to stick together when facing adversity.
No matter what happens, we promised to stick together through thick and thin.
The siblings managed to stick together despite the challenges they faced.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall out - to stop being friendly or united
  • synonym: stand by - to support or remain loyal to someone
