monocular vision

noun
vision using one eye
4.5 5.1
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈnɑː.kjə.lər ˈvɪʒ.ən/ /; / /mə.ˈnɑː.kjʊ.lər ˈvɪʒ.ən/ (AmE), /mə.ˈnɒ.kjʊ.lə ˈvɪʒ.ən/ /; / /mɒ.ˈnɒ.kjʊ.lə ˈvɪʒ.ən/ (BrE)
the ability to see with only one eye, or the use of a single eye for vision as opposed to both eyes together

Examples

Loss of monocular vision
After the accident, he had to adjust to life with monocular vision.
Owls primarily rely on binocular rather than monocular vision to judge distances accurately.
Some species of fish have only monocular vision, limiting their depth perception.
