Embiodea

noun
order of web-spinning insects
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmbioʊˈdiːə / (AmE), / ˌɛmbɪəʊˈdiːə / (BrE)
an order of small insects known as web spinners, characterized by their ability to produce silk from glands in their front legs to spin protective webs or tunnels

Examples

Embiodea web spinners
Scientists discovered a new species in the Embiodea order in the forests of South America.
The unique adaptations of Embiodea make them fascinating subjects for entomological research.
Unlike spiders, members of Embiodea spin silk with glands on their front legs.

Related Terms
