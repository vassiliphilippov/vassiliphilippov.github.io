inviability

noun
inability to survive
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnvaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnvaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state of being unable to survive, live, or develop normally.

Examples

Inviability of embryos
Genetic defects can lead to the inviability of embryos.
The inviability of the species in harsh climates has been well documented by scientists.
Environmental changes may increase the inviability of certain animal populations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nonviability - the state of not being viable or able to live
  • antonym: viability - the ability to survive or develop successfully
