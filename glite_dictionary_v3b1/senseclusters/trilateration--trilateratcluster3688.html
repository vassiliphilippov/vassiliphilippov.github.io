trilateration

noun
positioning by distances
Pronunciation: / traɪˌlætəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / traɪˌlætəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of determining the position of a point by measuring its distances from three known locations

Examples

Trilateration method
Modern GPS systems rely on trilateration to pinpoint your location anywhere on Earth.
Surveyors use trilateration to accurately map out large areas of land.
The concept of trilateration is essential for understanding how satellite navigation works.

Related Terms

  • synonym: triangulation - a method of determining position by measuring angles from known points
