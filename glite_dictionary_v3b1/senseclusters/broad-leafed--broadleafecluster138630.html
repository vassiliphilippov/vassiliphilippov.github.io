broad-leafed

adjective
having broad, flat leaves
5.0 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔd ˌliːft / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd ˌliːft / (BrE)
Having relatively wide, flat leaves rather than needlelike or scalelike leaves, especially of plants or trees.

Examples

Broad-leafed plant
A broad-leafed plant usually needs more sunlight compared to a needle-leaved one.
Many deciduous trees are broad-leafed, losing their leaves in autumn.
The forest contains a mix of coniferous and broad-leafed species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: needle-leaved - having slender, needle-shaped leaves
