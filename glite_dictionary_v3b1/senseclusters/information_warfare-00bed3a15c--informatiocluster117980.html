information warfare

noun
use of information to gain advantage in conflict
3.9 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən ˈwɔrfɛr / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən ˈwɔːfɛə / (BrE)
the use of information and information technology as a means to gain military or strategic advantage over rivals, especially during conflict or crisis

Examples

Information warfare tactics
Modern military strategists consider information warfare just as important as traditional combat operations.
Some governments invest heavily in information warfare to influence foreign elections and public opinion.
The rise of the internet has greatly expanded the reach and impact of information warfare.
