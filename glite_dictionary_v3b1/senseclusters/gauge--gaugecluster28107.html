gauge

noun
measuring instrument or device

an instrument used for measuring and indicating the amount, level, or contents of something, such as thickness, pressure, or rainfall

Examples

A pressure gauge
A rain gauge is used to measure the amount of precipitation in a specific area.
He checked the pressure gauge to make sure the tire was properly inflated.
The fuel gauge on the dashboard shows the tank is almost empty.

Synonyms

  • gage
    7.9 0.26/m
    gage is an older or alternate spelling seen on some instruments, while gauge is the common modern spelling for the same tool.
  • measuring instrument
    3.1 0.15/m
    measuring instrument is a formal general name for devices that show amounts, while gauge often refers to a specific tool showing pressure or thickness.
  • measuring device
    3.0 0.27/m
    measuring device is a broad everyday term for tools that find sizes or amounts, while gauge usually names instruments that display those readings.
  • meter
    3.2 7.26/m
    meter is an instrument that measures and often records quantity or flow over time, while gauge mainly shows an immediate level or pressure.
  • measure
    1.4 10/m
    measure is a small container or tool used to pour or count amounts, while gauge is an instrument that shows levels like pressure.
