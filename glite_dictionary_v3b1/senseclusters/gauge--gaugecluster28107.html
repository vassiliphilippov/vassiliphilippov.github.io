gauge

noun
measuring instrument or device
2.4 3.9
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring and indicating the amount, level, or contents of something, such as thickness, pressure, or rainfall

Examples

A pressure gauge
A rain gauge is used to measure the amount of precipitation in a specific area.
He checked the pressure gauge to make sure the tire was properly inflated.
The fuel gauge on the dashboard shows the tank is almost empty.
