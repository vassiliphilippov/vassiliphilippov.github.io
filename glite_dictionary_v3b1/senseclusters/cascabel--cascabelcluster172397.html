cascabel

noun
cannon rear knob
Pronunciation: / ˈkæskəˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈkæskəˌbɛl / (BrE)
the rounded knob at the rear end of a cannon, used for handling or attaching ropes

Examples

Cannon cascabel
The artillery crew gripped the cascabel to move the cannon into position.
A thick rope was tied around the cascabel to help control the recoil after firing.
The ornate cascabel on the old cannon was engraved with the maker's initials.
