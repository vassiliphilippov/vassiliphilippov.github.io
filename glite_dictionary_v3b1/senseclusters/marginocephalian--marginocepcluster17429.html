marginocephalian

noun
type of herbivorous dinosaur
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑrdʒɪnoʊsɪˈfeɪliən / (AmE), / ˌmɑːdʒɪnəʊsɪˈfeɪliən / (BrE)
a member of a group of herbivorous dinosaurs characterized by a bony shelf or frill at the back of the skull, including pachycephalosaurs and ceratopsians

Examples

A marginocephalian dinosaur
Ceratopsians, a subgroup of marginocephalians, often had prominent facial horns.
Researchers discovered that marginocephalians displayed a wide variety of horn shapes.
The fossil belonged to a marginocephalian, a group known for their elaborate skulls.
