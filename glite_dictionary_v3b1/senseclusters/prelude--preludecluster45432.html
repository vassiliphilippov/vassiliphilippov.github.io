prelude

noun
something that comes before another event
5.9 2.25/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…õl.jud / (AmE), / Ààpr…õl.juÀêd / (BrE)
an action or event that happens before and leads to or introduces a more important event or situation

Examples

A prelude to war
His speech was intended as a prelude to the evening's festivities.
The months of negotiations were merely a prelude to the bigger announcement that followed.
The thunderstorm seemed to be a prelude to the heavy rain that drenched the city.

Synonyms

  • overture
    7.2 0.44/m
    an overture is a formal opening or proposal that introduces something larger, while a prelude is an event that leads into it.
  • preliminary
    5.2 0.52/m
    a preliminary is a preparatory action before the main event, while a prelude specifically introduces and leads into the following important event.
  • precursor
    7.2 3.78/m
    a precursor is something that comes before and signals what will happen next, while a prelude is an event that actively introduces that next situation.
  • lead-in
    4.9 0.41/m
    a lead-in is a short opening section for a story or show, while a prelude is an earlier action that leads to a larger event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aftermath - the consequences or aftereffects following an event
  • synonym: overture - an introduction to something more substantial; often used for music or events
