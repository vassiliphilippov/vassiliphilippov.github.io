given

adjective
inclined or disposed toward
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvən / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'to') having a tendency or inclination toward something; habitually disposed to behave in a particular way.

Examples

Given to worry
He is given to daydreaming during long meetings.
She became given to sudden bouts of melancholy in winter.
The author is given to using complex metaphors in his writing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: averse - having a strong dislike of or opposition to something
  • synonym: inclined - disposed or willing to do something
  • synonym: prone - inclined or likely to do something
