helicity

noun
particle spin direction
9.9
Pronunciation: /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the component of a particle's intrinsic spin measured along the direction of its motion, indicating whether spin and momentum are aligned or opposed

Examples

Electron helicity
The experiment measured the electron's helicity to confirm predictions about parity violation.
Physicists often discuss helicity when analyzing the properties of subatomic particles.
A particle's helicity can determine how it interacts with other particles in certain reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirality - the property of an object being non-superimposable on its mirror image
  • synonym: spin - the intrinsic angular momentum of a particle
