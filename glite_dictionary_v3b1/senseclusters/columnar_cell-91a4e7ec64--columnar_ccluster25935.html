columnar cell

noun
elongated, column-shaped epithelial cell
3.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.lə.nər sel/ (AmE), /ˈkɒl.ə.nə sel/ (BrE)
an epithelial cell that is elongated and shaped like a column, sometimes possessing cilia

Examples

Ciliated columnar cells
Certain columnar cells in the respiratory tract are topped with cilia.
The lining of the intestine is composed of columnar cells that aid in nutrient absorption.
Under the microscope, the difference between columnar cells and squamous cells is clearly visible.
