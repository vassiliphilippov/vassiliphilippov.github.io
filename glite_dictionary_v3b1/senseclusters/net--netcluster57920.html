net

noun
meshed trap for catching animals
Pronunciation: / nɛt / (AmE), / nɛt / (BrE)
a piece of mesh fabric or structure used for catching, enclosing, or dividing things, or as a goal in sports

Examples

Fishing net
Farmers often use a net to keep birds away from their crops.
The fisherman repaired his net before heading out to sea.
We found a butterfly caught in a net near the field.
Players must hit the shuttlecock over the net to score points in badminton.
She adjusted the height of the badminton net before the match started.
The tennis ball just grazed the net and dropped onto the other side.
Fans erupted with cheers when the puck finally hit the net.
She spent hours practicing her shots on the backyard net.
The ball sailed past the goalkeeper and into the net for the winning goal.
