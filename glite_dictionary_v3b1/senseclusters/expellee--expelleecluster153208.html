expellee

noun
person forced to leave
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌspɛlˈiː /; / ɪkˌspɛˈliː / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛlˈiː /; / ɪkˌspɛˈliː / (BrE)
a person who has been forced to leave a place, especially a country, often for political or legal reasons

Examples

War expellee
After the conflict, the expellee struggled to rebuild his life in a new country.
International organizations provided aid to the expellees who had lost their homes.
The government debated policies to support expellees returning to their homeland.
After the conflict, thousands of expellees sought shelter in neighboring countries.
The international community discussed the rights of expellees at the summit.
Aid organizations provided food and medical care to the expellees arriving at the camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deportee - a person who has been deported from a country
  • synonym: exile - a person who has been forced to live away from their home country
  • synonym: refugee - a person who flees their country to escape danger or persecution
  • antonym: resident - a person who lives in a particular place
