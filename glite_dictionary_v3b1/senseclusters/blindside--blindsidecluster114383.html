blindside

verb
to surprise and harm unexpectedly
4.1 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈblaɪnd.saɪd/ (AmE), /ˈblaɪnd.saɪd/ (BrE)
to surprise or shock someone in an unexpected and often unpleasant way

Examples

To blindside with news
She was completely blindsided by the news of her colleague's resignation.
The financial crisis blindsided many small business owners.
The new policy changes blindsided most employees, leaving them unprepared.
She felt blindsided by the criticism during the meeting.
The announcement of the merger completely blindsided the employees.
The company was blindsided by the sudden drop in sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambush - attack by surprise
  • synonym: shock - to cause someone to feel upset or disturbed unexpectedly
  • synonym: surprise - to cause someone to feel sudden wonder or amazement
  • antonym: warn - safeguard from surprise or danger by informing
