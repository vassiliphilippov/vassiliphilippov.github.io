crater

noun
bowl-shaped depression from impact
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkreɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkreɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a large, bowl-shaped hole or depression in the ground, especially caused by an explosion, impact, or volcanic activity

Examples

A giant crater
After the explosion, a huge crater appeared in the middle of the street.
Scientists studied the crater to learn more about the asteroid impact.
The crater left by the meteorite was over a kilometer wide.
From the airplane, the volcanic crater was clearly visible.
Scientists studied the crater to understand the eruption's effects.
The lava slowly filled the crater at the peak of the volcano.
