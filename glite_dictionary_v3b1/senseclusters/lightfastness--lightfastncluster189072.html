lightfastness

noun
resistance to fading
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtˌfæstnəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌfɑːstnəs / (BrE)
the ability of a material, especially a pigment or dye, to resist fading when exposed to light

Examples

High lightfastness pigment
High lightfastness is important for artists who want their paintings to last for many years.
Manufacturers test the lightfastness of fabrics to ensure they do not fade quickly in sunlight.
The lightfastness of a pigment determines how well it maintains its color over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colorfastness - the resistance of a material's color to fading or running
  • antonym: fading - the process of losing color or brightness
