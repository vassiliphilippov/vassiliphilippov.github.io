New World beaver

noun
North American species of beaver
Pronunciation: / nu wɝld ˈbiːvɚ / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld ˈbiːvə / (BrE)
A large aquatic rodent native to North America, closely related to but distinct from the Eurasian beaver.

Examples

The New World beaver
Scientists study the impact of New World beavers on wetland environments.
The New World beaver is found throughout North America except for parts of Florida.
Unlike the Eurasian species, the New World beaver has a more rounded tail.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Old World beaver - species of beaver native to Eurasia
  • synonym: beaver - large, semiaquatic rodent known for building dams
