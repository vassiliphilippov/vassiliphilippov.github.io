insource

verb
to bring work in-house
7.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈsɔːrs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈsɔːs/ (BrE)
to transfer work or services from an external provider back to a company's own employees or departments

Examples

To insource IT support
The company decided to insource its IT support to improve service quality.
After years of outsourcing, they began to insource manufacturing to reduce costs.
Many firms choose to insource critical operations for better control and flexibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internalize - to make something part of an organization's own operations
  • antonym: outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier
