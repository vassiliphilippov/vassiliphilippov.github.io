atrium

noun
chamber of the heart; connected chamber
3.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.tri.əm/ /; / /ˈeɪ.triː.əm/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.tri.əm/ /; / /ˈeɪ.triː.əm/ (BrE)
An anatomical chamber of the heart that receives blood entering the heart from the veins, or any chamber that is connected to other chambers or passageways.

Examples

Heart atrium
Blood returning from the body flows into the right atrium of the heart.
Oxygenated blood enters the left atrium before moving into the ventricle.
The design of the building included a spacious atrium with a glass roof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hall - large entrance room or lobby in a building
  • antonym: ventricle - lower chamber of the heart that pumps blood out
