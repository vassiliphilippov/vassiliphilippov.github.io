wax paper

noun
moisture-resistant paper for wrapping food
4.6 2.1
Pronunciation: /wæks ˈpeɪ.pər/ (AmE), /wæks ˈpeɪ.pə/ (BrE)
A type of paper coated with wax or paraffin to make it moisture-resistant, commonly used for wrapping food.

Examples

Wax paper for food
Always line the pan with wax paper before pouring in the batter.
He wrapped the sandwich in wax paper to keep it fresh.
You can use wax paper to separate layers of cookies in a tin.
