single-seeded

adjective
containing only one seed
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈsidɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˈsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a fruit or plant) containing only one seed.

Examples

A single-seeded fruit
A peach is not single-seeded because it contains a hard pit with a seed inside.
Many wildflowers produce single-seeded pods that help with wind dispersal.
The olive is a single-seeded fruit commonly used in Mediterranean cuisine.
