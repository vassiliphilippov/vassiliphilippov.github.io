four flush

noun
bluffing in poker by pretending to have a flush
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr ˌflʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɔː ˌflʌʃ / (BrE)
(in poker) an attempt to deceive other players by pretending to have a flush when one actually holds only four cards to a flush; by extension, any act of bluffing or showy pretense to impress or mislead.

Examples

A four flush bluff
After his four flush in the last hand, no one trusted his poker face anymore.
He tried to four flush his way to victory, but everyone saw through his act.
Her confident sales pitch turned out to be a four flush with little substance behind it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being honest and not intending to deceive
