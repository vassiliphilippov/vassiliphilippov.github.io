primitive person

noun
person from an early stage of civilization
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪm.ə.tɪv ˈpɝː.sən / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv ˈpɜː.sən / (BrE)
a person belonging to an early or undeveloped stage of civilization, especially as considered in anthropological or historical contexts (now often considered outdated or inappropriate)

Examples

The primitive person society
Anthropologists once studied the customs of the primitive person to understand early human societies.
Early explorers often described indigenous groups as primitive persons, reflecting the biases of their time.
The term primitive person is now regarded as outdated and insensitive in modern scholarship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: civilized person - person belonging to a developed or advanced society
