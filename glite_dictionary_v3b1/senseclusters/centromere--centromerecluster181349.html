centromere

noun
chromosome region joining sister chromatids
9.9 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.trə.mɪr/ /; / /ˈsen.troʊ.mɪr/ (AmE), /ˈsen.trəʊ.mɪə/ (BrE)
the condensed region of a chromosome that joins two sister chromatids and becomes visible during cell division

Examples

The centromere region
A defect in the centromere can lead to improper separation of chromatids.
During mitosis, the centromere ensures that each daughter cell receives an identical set of chromosomes.
Researchers studied the structure of the centromere to understand chromosome behavior in cells.

  • synonym: chromosome - thread-like structure of nucleic acids and proteins carrying genetic information
