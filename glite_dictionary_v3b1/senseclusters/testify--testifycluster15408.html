to make a formal statement of what is true, especially in a court of law

Examples

To testify in court
Several experts will testify for the prosecution in tomorrow's session.
She testified under oath that she had never seen the defendant before.
The witness was asked to testify about what he saw on the night of the accident.

Synonyms

  • take the stand
    6.0 1.89/m
    take the stand is to give spoken testimony in court during a trial, while testify means making a formal statement under oath.
  • depose
    8.0 0.99/m
    depose means giving a formal sworn statement, often outside court, while testify usually means speaking under oath in court.
  • bear witness
    4.6 1.84/m
    bear witness means giving evidence or confirming the truth in many settings, while testify refers to formal statements in court.
  • attest
    6.6 6.89/m
    attest means to officially confirm or sign that something is true, while testify means to speak as a witness in court.
  • swear
    4.6 36/m
    swear means to make a solemn oath or promise, often to tell truth, while testify is giving formal evidence in court.
