Guinevere

proper noun
King Arthur's wife in Arthurian legend
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡwɪnəˌvɪr / (AmE), / ˈɡwɪnɪvɪə / (BrE)
(in Arthurian legend) the wife of King Arthur, known for her affair with Sir Lancelot, which contributed to the downfall of the Knights of the Round Table.

Examples

Guinevere in legend
At the medieval festival, an actress portrayed Guinevere in a theatrical reenactment.
Many poets and writers have retold the tragic love story of Guinevere and Lancelot.
The betrayal of Guinevere is a central theme in Arthurian legend.
