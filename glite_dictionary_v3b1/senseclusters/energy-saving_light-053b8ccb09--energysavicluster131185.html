energy-saving light

noun
a low-energy light bulb
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnɚdʒi ˌseɪvɪŋ laɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi ˌseɪvɪŋ laɪt / (BrE)
a light bulb designed to use less energy than traditional incandescent bulbs, such as compact fluorescent or LED types

Examples

Energy-saving light bulb
Energy-saving lights are now required in many new buildings by law.
Many people prefer energy-saving lights because they last much longer than regular bulbs.
We replaced all the old bulbs with energy-saving lights to reduce our electricity bill.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: LED bulb - a light bulb that uses light-emitting diodes and saves energy
  • synonym: compact fluorescent lamp - a type of energy-saving bulb using fluorescent technology
  • degree lower: incandescent bulb - a traditional electric bulb that is less efficient
