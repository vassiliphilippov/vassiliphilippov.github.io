post meridiem

adverb
after noon; between noon and midnight
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊst məˈrɪdi.əm / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst məˈrɪdi.əm / (BrE)
(chiefly in time notation) the period from noon to midnight; occurring after 12:00 in the day.

Examples

Seven post meridiem
Our meeting begins at seven post meridiem sharp, so please be on time.
The store remains open until ten post meridiem during the summer months.
The train is scheduled to depart at 3 post meridiem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: a.m. - before noon; the period from midnight to noon
