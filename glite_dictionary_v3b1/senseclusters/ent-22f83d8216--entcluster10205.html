Ent

proper noun
giant tree creature
Pronunciation: / ɛnt / (AmE), / ɛnt / (BrE)
a giant, sentient tree creature from fantasy literature, especially in the works of J.R.R. Tolkien

Examples

The Ent creature
The Ent spoke slowly, its deep voice echoing through the ancient forest.
In Tolkien's stories, an Ent can uproot itself and walk across great distances.
Legends say that an Ent can live for thousands of years, guarding the woods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orc - a monstrous creature in fantasy literature, often an enemy of Ents
  • synonym: treefolk - tree-like beings in fantasy fiction
