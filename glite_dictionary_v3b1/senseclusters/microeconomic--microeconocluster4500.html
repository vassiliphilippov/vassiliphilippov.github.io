microeconomic

adjective
relating to microeconomics

relating to the branch of economics concerned with individual households, firms, and markets, rather than the economy as a whole.

Examples

Microeconomic theory
Government policies are often designed to address specific microeconomic issues, such as pricing or labor markets.
Most microeconomic theories focus on how individuals make decisions in the marketplace.
The report identified several microeconomic factors that contributed to the company's growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macroeconomic - relating to the economy as a whole, not to individual parts
  • synonym: microeconomics - the study of the actions of individual consumers and businesses
