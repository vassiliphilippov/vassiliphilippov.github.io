ganglioside

noun
nerve cell lipid
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæŋɡliəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡaŋɡlɪəˌsaɪd / (BrE)
a complex lipid found in nerve cell membranes, important for cell signaling in the nervous system

Examples

A ganglioside molecule
Scientists discovered that a ganglioside plays a key role in brain development.
The concentration of ganglioside in nerve cells can affect how signals are transmitted.
Certain diseases are linked to abnormal levels of ganglioside in the nervous system.
