squeeze

noun
period of financial or resource pressure
5.6
Pronunciation: / skwiz / (AmE), / skwiːz / (BrE)
a situation where financial or other pressures make it difficult to manage resources or obtain money

Examples

A financial squeeze
Small companies are feeling the squeeze as the costs of materials continue to rise.
The new tax regulations have put a squeeze on profit margins across the industry.
There has been a serious squeeze on charitable donations this year.
Consumers felt the effects of the squeeze as banks tightened their lending criteria.
During the financial crisis, many small businesses struggled due to a severe squeeze on credit.
The central bank intervened to alleviate the squeeze in the money market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credit crunch - a sudden reduction in the general availability of loans or credit
  • synonym: crunch - a critical financial situation or tightness of resources
  • synonym: pinch - a situation where resources or money are in short supply
