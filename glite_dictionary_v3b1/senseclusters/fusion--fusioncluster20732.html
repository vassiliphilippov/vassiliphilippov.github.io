fusion

noun
combining images from both eyes
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of combining the images from both eyes into a single visual perception

Examples

Visual fusion
Patients with impaired fusion may experience double vision or dizziness.
Successful fusion prevents conflicting images and allows us to see the world as a single scene.
The brain's ability to achieve fusion is essential for normal depth perception.
