chrism

noun
consecrated oil used in Christian rites
8.8 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɪ.zəm/ /; / /krɪsm/ (AmE), /ˈkrɪ.zəm/ /; / /krɪsm/ (BrE)
A consecrated oil, often mixed with balsam, used in certain Christian religious ceremonies such as baptism, confirmation, and ordination.

Examples

The chrism oil
The bishop blessed the chrism that would be used for confirmations throughout the diocese.
The priest applied the chrism to the child's forehead during the baptism ceremony.
This sacred chrism is prepared each year before Easter.
