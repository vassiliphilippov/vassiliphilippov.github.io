religiosity

noun

Examples

Strong religiosity
Excessive religiosity can occasionally lead to intolerance of other viewpoints.
His obvious religiosity sometimes made people question the sincerity of his beliefs.
The politician's displays of religiosity were widely seen as pandering to voters.
Her religiosity was evident in her daily practices and rituals.
The community is known for its strong religiosity and frequent religious gatherings.
He questioned whether outward religiosity always reflected true inner faith.

Synonyms

  • religiousness
    5.0 0.28/m
    religiousness is the everyday word for being religious and practicing faith, while religiosity sounds more formal and academic.
  • devoutness
    6.7 0.04/m
    devoutness shows deep personal faith and prayer, while religiosity refers to the general quality of strong religious feeling or practice.
  • piety
    7.7 3.69/m
    piety is respectful worship focused on duties and moral practice, while religiosity is the broader quality of strong religious feeling.
  • devotion
    4.6 2.65/m
    devotion is deep love or service often shown in acts, while religiosity describes a person's overall strong religious attitude.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impiety - lack of piety or reverence, especially for a god
  • synonym: piety - devoutness or reverence to religion
  • antonym: secularism - the belief that religion should not play a role in government or public life
  • synonym: zealotry - fanatical and uncompromising pursuit of religious, political, or other ideals
