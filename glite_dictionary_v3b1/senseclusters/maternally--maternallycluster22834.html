maternally

adverb
from the mother (inheritance)
5.6
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈtɜːr.nəl.iː/ /; / /mə.ˈtɜːr.nəl.ɪ/ (AmE), /mə.ˈtɜː.nəl.iː/ /; / /mə.ˈtɜː.nəl.ɪ/ (BrE)
(in genetics and biology) with relation to the mother as the source or route of inheritance or transmission; originating from the mother.

Examples

Maternally inherited genes
The disease is maternally inherited because the mutation exists only in mitochondrial DNA passed from mothers.
Population studies showed the trait was maternally transmitted across three successive generations.
Genetic counselors explained the condition is maternally determined and cannot be passed through the father's line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maternal - relating to or coming from the mother
  • antonym: paternally - in a way transmitted from or relating to the father; opposite of maternally (in inheritance contexts)
