avant-garde

adjective
radically innovative; experimental (especially in arts)
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌævɑnˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌavɒ̃ˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
favoring or introducing new and experimental ideas and methods, especially in the arts; radically original or innovative

Examples

Avant-garde style
Critics praised the avant-garde film for its originality and daring style.
Her avant-garde fashion designs often surprised and intrigued the public.
The avant-garde artist challenged traditional ideas about painting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed
  • synonym: innovative - introducing new ideas; original and creative
