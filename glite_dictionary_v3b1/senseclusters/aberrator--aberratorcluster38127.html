aberrator

noun
optical correction device
Pronunciation: / ˌæbəˈreɪtər / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a device or software used to introduce or correct optical aberrations in imaging systems

Examples

Optical aberrator
The laboratory technician adjusted the aberrator to test the camera's response to lens distortions.
Using an aberrator, researchers can study how optical flaws affect image quality.
The new software aberrator allows engineers to simulate various types of lens imperfections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrector - a device that corrects errors or distortions in a system
  • synonym: simulator - a device or program that imitates the operation of a real system
