suppositum

noun
substitute person or thing
Pronunciation: / səˈpoʊzɪtəm / (AmE), / səˈpɒzɪtəm / (BrE)
a person or thing that is substituted for another, especially as a replacement or stand-in (archaic or specialized use)

Examples

Royal suppositum
In the old legend, a suppositum was placed in the royal family to claim the throne.
The philosopher discussed the concept of a suppositum in his treatise on identity.
Some believed the child was a suppositum and not the true heir.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or primary person or thing, not a substitute
  • synonym: replacement - a person or thing that takes the place of another
  • synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another
