Star Chamber

noun
notorious former English court
9.9
Pronunciation: / stɑr ˈtʃeɪm.bɚ / (AmE), / stɑː ˈtʃeɪm.bə / (BrE)
A former English court of law, known for its secretive proceedings, harsh punishments, and abuse of power, abolished in 1641.

Examples

The Star Chamber court
Many viewed the Star Chamber as a symbol of royal tyranny and injustice.
References to the Star Chamber often arise when discussing abuses of judicial power.
The Star Chamber was infamous for conducting trials in secret and issuing harsh sentences.
