suit

noun
old-fashioned word for courtship
Pronunciation: / suːt / (AmE), / sjuːt /; / suːt / (BrE)
the act of trying to win someone's affection, especially for marriage, or a formal request or petition

Examples

Marriage suit
His persistent suit over the summer eventually won her heart.
Many suitors came to pay their suit to the young heiress.
She rejected his suit, choosing instead to remain single.
In medieval times, a suit could be brought to the noble's court by any of his subjects.
She presented her suit to the council, hoping for a favorable decision.
The villagers filed a suit before the king to address their grievances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: courtship - the act of seeking someone's affections with the intention of marriage
  • synonym: petition - a formal written request, typically to an authority
