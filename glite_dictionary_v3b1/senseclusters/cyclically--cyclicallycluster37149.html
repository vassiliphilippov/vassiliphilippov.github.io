cyclically

adverb
in repeating cycles
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪklɪkli / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that repeats in cycles or at regular intervals

Examples

To grow cyclically
The economy tends to grow and contract cyclically over the years.
Many plants flower cyclically, blooming at the same time each year.
The tides rise and fall cyclically due to the moon's gravitational pull.
The seasons change cyclically, bringing different weather patterns throughout the year.
In this experiment, the machine operates cyclically to test each sample in turn.
The software updates cyclically to ensure all users have the latest features.

Related Terms

  • synonym: periodically - at regular intervals of time
  • antonym: randomly - in a way that happens without a pattern or plan
