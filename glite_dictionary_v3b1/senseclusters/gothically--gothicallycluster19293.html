gothically

adverb
in Gothic style
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪkli / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or resembles the Gothic style, mood, atmosphere, or historical culture (such as Gothic architecture or literature)

Examples

Gothically built cathedral
The cathedral was gothically decorated with pointed arches and stained glass windows.
Her novel is gothically atmospheric, filled with dark castles and mysterious characters.
The room was gothically furnished, evoking the grandeur of medieval Europe.
The old mansion was gothically furnished, with dark wood and stained glass windows.
She dressed gothically for the party, wearing black lace and dramatic makeup.
The novel is gothically written, full of mysterious settings and eerie events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: classically - in a manner relating to ancient Greek or Roman style
  • synonym: gothic - relating to the Gothic style or mood
  • synonym: medievally - in a manner relating to the Middle Ages
