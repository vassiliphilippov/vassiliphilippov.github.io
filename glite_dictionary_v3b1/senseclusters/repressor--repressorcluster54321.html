repressor

noun
agent that suppresses or inhibits activity
8.3 7.2
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈpres.ər/ /; / /rə.ˈpres.ər/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈpres.ə/ /; / /rə.ˈpres.ə/ (BrE)
a substance, gene, or agent that acts to suppress or inhibit a process, especially the expression of genes in a cell.

Examples

A repressor gene
A repressor gene produces a protein that prevents the transcription of other genes.
Chemical repressors can be used to control unwanted cellular activity in experiments.
In genetics, a repressor binds to specific DNA sequences to inhibit gene expression.
