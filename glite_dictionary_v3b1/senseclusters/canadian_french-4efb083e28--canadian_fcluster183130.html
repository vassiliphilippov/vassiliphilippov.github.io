Canadian French

noun
variety of French spoken in Canada
Pronunciation: / kəˈneɪdiən frɛntʃ / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən frɛntʃ /; / kəˈneɪdiən frɛn(t)ʃ / (BrE)
the variety of the French language spoken in Canada, especially in Quebec, with distinctive pronunciation, vocabulary, and grammar compared to standard French

Examples

Canadian French accent
He took a class to improve his accent in Canadian French.
Many people in Quebec speak Canadian French as their first language.
The vocabulary of Canadian French includes words that are rarely used in France today.
Linguists study the differences between Canadian French and the French spoken in Paris.
Many immigrants to Quebec quickly adapt to Canadian French when speaking with locals.
My cousins grew up bilingual, speaking both English and Canadian French at home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Metropolitan French - French as spoken in France (standard French)
  • synonym: Quebec French - French as spoken in the province of Quebec
