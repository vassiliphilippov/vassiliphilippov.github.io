pictorialist

noun
advocate of pictorialism
Pronunciation: / pɪkˈtɔːriəlɪst / (AmE), / pɪkˈtɔːrɪəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices pictorialism, especially in art or photography, emphasizing artistic style over realism

Examples

A pictorialist photographer
As a dedicated pictorialist, she often manipulated her photographs to create a dreamy, artistic effect.
The exhibition featured works by a famous pictorialist who influenced early twentieth-century photography.
Many critics debated whether the pictorialist approach truly captured the essence of the subject.
The pictorialist used soft focus and special techniques to give his photographs a painterly effect.
Many early twentieth-century exhibitions featured the work of at least one pictorialist.
As a pictorialist, she believed that photography should be considered a fine art.

  • synonym: pictorialism - an artistic movement in photography emphasizing beauty and style over realism
  • antonym: realist - a person who favors representing things as they really are
