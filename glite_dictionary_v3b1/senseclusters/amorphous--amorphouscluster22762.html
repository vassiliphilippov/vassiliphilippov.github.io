amorphous

adjective
unclear; lacking clear structure
Pronunciation: / əˈmɔrfəs /; / əˈmɔɹfəs / (AmE), / əˈmɔːfəs /; / əˈmɔː.fəs / (BrE)
lacking clear structure, organization, or focus; vague or badly organized (especially for explanations, groups, or systems)

Examples

Amorphous plan
Her amorphous explanation left everyone confused about what had really happened.
His ideas on the subject were so amorphous that nobody knew what he really wanted.
The committee produced an amorphous set of recommendations that lacked specific direction.
An amorphous alliance of protestors gathered in the square, each with different demands.
Because it lacked defined leadership, the movement was criticized for being amorphous and ineffective.
The committee remained an amorphous collection of individuals, without clear goals or structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaotic - in a state of utter confusion or disorder
  • antonym: coherent - logical and well-organized
  • synonym: disorganized - lacking order or planning
  • synonym: nebulous - unclear or ill-defined
  • synonym: vague - unclear; not specific or definite
