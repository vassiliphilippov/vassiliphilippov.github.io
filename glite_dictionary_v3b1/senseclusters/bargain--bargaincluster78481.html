bargain

verb
negotiate an agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrɡən /; / ˈbɑːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡən /; / ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
to discuss or negotiate the terms of a deal, often to get a better price or conditions

Examples

To bargain for a lower price
Farmers would often bargain at the market to get the best deal for their goods.
She refused to bargain, insisting the price was already fair.
They spent hours trying to bargain with the car dealer over the final price.
She refused to bargain and paid the amount on the price tag.
Tourists often enjoy the experience of bargaining in local markets.
We spent hours trying to bargain with the street vendors over the price of souvenirs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compromise - to settle a dispute by mutual concessions
  • synonym: haggle - to argue persistently, especially about a price
  • synonym: negotiate - try to reach an agreement through discussion
  • antonym: refuse - to decline to agree or accept terms
