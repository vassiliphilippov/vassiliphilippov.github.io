harborer

noun
disease carrier
8.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërb…ôr…ôr / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêb…ôr…ô / (BrE)
a person or animal that carries and can spread a disease, parasite, or pest to others

Examples

Harborer of disease
The mosquito is a common harborer of malaria in tropical regions.
Doctors warned that the patient could be a silent harborer of the infection.
Wild animals often act as a harborer for various parasites and diseases.
The mosquito is a well-known harbourer of malaria parasites in many tropical regions.
Scientists studied the rat population as a potential harbourer of dangerous bacteria.
Controlling the spread of disease often involves targeting the harbourer species in the environment.

Synonyms

  • carrier
    5.8 2.77/m
    a carrier often holds and passes a disease without symptoms, while a harborer carries and can spread parasites, pests, or disease.
  • transmitter
    6.2 0.23/m
    a transmitter spreads disease between individuals or species directly, while a harborer simply carries organisms that can later infect others.
  • infector
    7.6 0.01/m
    an infector actively causes infection in others by passing germs, while a harborer may only carry pests or parasites without causing immediate illness.
