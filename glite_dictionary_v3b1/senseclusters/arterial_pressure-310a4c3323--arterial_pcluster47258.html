arterial pressure

noun
blood pressure in the arteries
4.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ˈtɪ.r.iː.əl ˈpreʃ.ər/ (AmE), /ɑː.ˈtɪə.r.iː.əl ˈpreʃ.ə/ (BrE)
the pressure exerted by circulating blood on the walls of the arteries

Examples

Arterial pressure level
An increase in arterial pressure can put extra strain on the heart and blood vessels.
Doctors routinely measure arterial pressure to assess cardiovascular health.
Low arterial pressure may suggest problems such as dehydration or blood loss.
