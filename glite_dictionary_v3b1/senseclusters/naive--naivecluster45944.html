naive

adjective
assuming feature independence (ML)
7.7
Pronunciation: / naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)
(in machine learning and statistics) making a simplifying independence assumption about features; applied to models (e.g., naive Bayes) that assume features are conditionally independent given the class.

Examples

Naive Bayes model
The team evaluated a naive Bayes classifier despite its independence assumptions in feature design.
Because the dataset was small, she chose a naive model to avoid overfitting and complexity.
Even though simple, the naive approach produced useful predictions in early prototype tests.
