pair production

noun
creation of an electron-positron pair by a photon
6.4 9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈper prəˌdʌkʃən /; / per pr59ccd8ck8359n / (AmE), / ˈpeə prəˌdʌkʃən /; / pe59 pr59ccd8ck8359n / (BrE)
the process in which a high-energy photon (or photons) is converted into an electron and a positron, sometimes involving a nearby atomic nucleus

Examples

Pair production process
Pair production occurs when a photon of sufficient energy interacts with an atomic nucleus.
In high-energy physics experiments, scientists often observe pair production as evidence of particle interactions.
The concept of pair production is essential for understanding certain cosmic ray phenomena.
In high-energy experiments, scientists observed pair production from two colliding photons in the detector.
The theory predicts pair production can occur in a very strong electric field without a nucleus present.
Researchers aim to create laboratory conditions where pair production happens between laser photons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Breit–Wheeler process - a specific photon–photon mechanism in which two photons collide and create an electron–positron pair
  • synonym: Schwinger effect - pair creation from a very strong electric field, predicted by quantum electrodynamics
