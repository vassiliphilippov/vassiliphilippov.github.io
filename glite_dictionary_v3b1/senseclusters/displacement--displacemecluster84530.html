displacement

noun
replacing another using unfair methods
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of taking the place of another, especially using unfair or underhanded tactics

Examples

Displacement of a leader
Rumors spread about the displacement of key staff members through political maneuvering.
The manager's displacement of her rival shocked the entire office.
There was outrage over the displacement of workers following the corporate merger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replacement - the act of replacing or being replaced
  • antonym: restoration - the act of putting something back in its original place
