semitendinosus

noun
thigh muscle
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌtɛndɪˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌtɛndɪˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
a muscle in the back of the thigh that helps bend the knee and is sometimes used in surgery

Examples

The semitendinosus muscle
The semitendinosus is one of the main muscles used when you bend your knee.

The surgeon used the semitendinosus as a graft to repair the damaged ligament.
In anatomy class, we learned about the function of the semitendinosus in knee movement.
