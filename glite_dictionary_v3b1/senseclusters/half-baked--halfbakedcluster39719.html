half-baked

adjective
not fully thought out; impractical
4.5 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhæfˈbeɪkt / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈbeɪkt / (BrE)
not fully developed, planned, or sensible; impractical or incomplete (used for ideas, plans, or proposals)

Examples

Half-baked idea
His half-baked plan to start a business with no experience quickly fell apart.
The board rejected the half-baked proposal because it lacked important details.
We shouldn’t implement any half-baked ideas without proper discussion and research.
He regretted making a half-baked promise he couldn't keep.
Her half-baked solution did not solve any of the underlying issues.
The committee rejected the half-baked proposal for lack of detail and research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crazy - extremely foolish or impractical (of ideas/plans)
  • synonym: ill-conceived - badly planned or thought out
  • antonym: well-thought-out - carefully considered and planned
