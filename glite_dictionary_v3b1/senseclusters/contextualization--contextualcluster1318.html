contextualization

noun
software configuration process
Pronunciation: / kənˌtɛkstʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌtɛkstjʊəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of configuring software or systems by applying environment-specific settings or metadata, especially at startup.

Examples

Software contextualization
Automated contextualization allows cloud servers to adapt to different regional requirements during deployment.
The IT team improved contextualization to ensure each virtual machine received the correct network settings.
Proper contextualization is essential for software to function correctly in various environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: configuration - the arrangement or setup of software or systems
  • synonym: customization - the process of modifying something to suit a particular individual or task
