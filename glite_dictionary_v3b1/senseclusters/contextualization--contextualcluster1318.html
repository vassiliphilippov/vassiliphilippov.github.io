contextualization

noun
software configuration process
8.2
Pronunciation: /kən.tekst.ʃuː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kən.tekst.ʃuː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of configuring software or systems by applying environment-specific settings or metadata, especially at startup.

Examples

Software contextualization
