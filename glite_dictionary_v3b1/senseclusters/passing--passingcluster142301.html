passing

noun
forward throw play (American football)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪŋ / (BrE)
In American football, a play in which one player throws the ball forward to a teammate.

Examples

Accurate passing wins games
Excellent passing allowed them to score a touchdown in the final quarter.
The coach emphasized the importance of quick passing to break through the defense.
The team relied heavily on passing to gain yardage during the game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rushing - a play in American football involving running with the ball rather than passing
