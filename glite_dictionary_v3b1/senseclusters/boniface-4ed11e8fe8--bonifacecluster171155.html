Boniface

proper noun
Anglo-Saxon Christian missionary and saint

(c. 680–754) An Anglo-Saxon missionary and Christian saint who spread Christianity in Frisia and Germany and was martyred in Frisia.

Examples

Saint Boniface
Boniface played a crucial role in the Christianization of Germany during the 8th century.
Many churches in Europe are dedicated to Boniface in recognition of his missionary work.
The feast day of Boniface is celebrated on June 5th by many Christian denominations.
