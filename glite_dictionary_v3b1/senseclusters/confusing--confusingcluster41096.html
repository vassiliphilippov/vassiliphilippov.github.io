confusing

adjective
causing confusion; hard to understand
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
difficult to understand and likely to make people feel confused or unclear

Examples

A confusing message
All the different instructions made the task more confusing than it needed to be.
I found the plot of the movie extremely confusing and hard to follow.
The new software interface is quite confusing for first-time users.
Her explanation of the policy was confusing and left everyone asking questions.
I found the plot of the movie confusing, especially in the second half.
The instructions were so confusing that nobody knew what to do next.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - easy to understand; not confusing
  • synonym: perplexing - causing confusion or puzzlement
  • synonym: puzzling - causing one to feel confused or perplexed
