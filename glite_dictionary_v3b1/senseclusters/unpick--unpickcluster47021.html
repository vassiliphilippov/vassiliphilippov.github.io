unpick

verb
analyze or resolve something complex
7.4 5.3
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈpɪk/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪk/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈpɪk/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪk/ (BrE)
to carefully analyze or take apart something complex in order to understand or solve it

Examples

To unpick a problem
It took the lawyer hours to unpick the complicated legal arguments in the case.
She tried to unpick what had gone wrong in the failed experiment.
The historian aimed to unpick the causes of the conflict over decades.
The detective unpicked the suspect's story to find inconsistencies.
She patiently unpicked the stitching to repair the torn seam.
In his review, the journalist unpicked the film's plot and highlighted its weaknesses.
