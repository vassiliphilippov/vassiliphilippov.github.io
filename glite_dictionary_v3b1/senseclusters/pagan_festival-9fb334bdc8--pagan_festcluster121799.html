pagan festival

noun
festival tied to pagan traditions
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪɡən ˈfɛstəvəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡən ˈfɛstɪvəl / (BrE)
A festival or celebration associated with pagan beliefs, religions, or traditions, often held to mark seasonal or religious events in pre-Christian cultures.

Examples

A pagan festival celebration
In ancient Rome, the Saturnalia was a famous pagan festival marked by feasting and gift-giving.
Many modern traditions around May Day have roots in an old pagan festival.
People gathered to celebrate the pagan festival with dancing and music around the bonfire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: religious festival - a festival observed by followers of a religion
