Marburg virus

noun
a virus causing Marburg disease
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑːr.bɜːrɡ ˈvaɪ.rəs/ (AmE), /ˈmɑː.bɜːg ˈvaɪ.rəs/ (BrE)
a highly infectious filovirus that causes Marburg disease in humans and nonhuman primates; transmitted by animals and considered a potential bioweapon

Examples

Marburg virus infection
Hospitals implemented strict isolation measures for patients with suspected Marburg virus infection.
Scientists are studying how the Marburg virus is transmitted between animals and humans.
The outbreak of Marburg virus in Africa raised international health concerns.
