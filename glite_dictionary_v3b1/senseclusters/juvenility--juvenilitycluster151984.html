juvenility

noun
youthfulness; immaturity
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒuːvəˈnɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːvəˈnɪləti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being young or immature, often marked by a lack of experience or maturity.

Examples

His juvenility shows
He gradually overcame his juvenility as he gained more experience in the field.
Her juvenility was apparent in the way she handled the difficult situation.
The manager criticized the team's juvenility and urged them to act more professionally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: maturity - the state of being fully developed or experienced
