sensitivity

noun
responsiveness to external stimuli
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛn.səˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.sɪˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The ability to respond to external stimuli; the faculty of sensation in the body.

Examples

Skin sensitivity
Researchers measured the nerve sensitivity at various points on the subject's skin.
Some patients experience increased sensitivity to light after the procedure.
The animal's heightened sensitivity may help it detect predators more easily.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of responsiveness to stimuli
  • synonym: sensibility - the capacity to feel or perceive sensations
