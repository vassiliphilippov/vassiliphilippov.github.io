headscarf

noun
cloth worn over the head and tied under the chin
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌskɑrf / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌskɑːf / (BrE)
a piece of cloth worn on the head, often by women, for warmth, modesty, religious reasons, or style

Examples

A headscarf worn
He recognized her from the bright headscarf she always wore.
Many women in the village wear a headscarf as part of their traditional dress.
She wore a colorful headscarf to protect her hair from the wind.
Many women in the village wear a headscarf as part of their daily attire.
She chose a bright red headscarf to match her dress for the festival.
In some cultures, a headscarf is worn for religious reasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hat - a covering for the head, usually with a brim
  • synonym: kerchief - a square piece of cloth worn on the head or around the neck
