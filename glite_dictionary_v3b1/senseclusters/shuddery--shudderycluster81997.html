shuddery

adjective
causing fear or a feeling of dread
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʌdɚi / (AmE), / ˈʃʌdəri / (BrE)
causing fear, terror, or an involuntary shiver; provoking a shudder of horror or dread

Examples

A shuddery story
She told a shuddery story about the ghost that haunted the old hotel.
The abandoned mansion had a shuddery atmosphere that made us hesitate to enter.
There's something shuddery about walking alone through the fog at midnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scary - causing fear or alarm
  • degree higher: terrifying - causing extreme fear
  • degree lower: unsettling - causing anxiety or unease, often in a milder way
