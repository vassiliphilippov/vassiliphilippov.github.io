pictograph

noun
symbol in picture writing
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪk.təˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.təˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A symbol or graphic character representing a word or idea, used in picture writing systems.

Examples

Ancient pictograph writing
Early Native American tribes often used pictographs to communicate important messages.
Researchers identified a pictograph that represented the sun on the cave wall.
The museum displayed several ancient pictographs carved into stone tablets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hieroglyph - a character of the ancient Egyptian writing system based on pictorial symbols
  • synonym: ideogram - a written symbol representing an idea or object rather than a particular word or speech sound
