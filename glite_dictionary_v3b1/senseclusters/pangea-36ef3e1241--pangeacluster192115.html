Pangea

noun
prehistoric supercontinent
7.9
Pronunciation: / pænˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / panˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A hypothetical supercontinent that included all of Earth's landmasses before they separated, existing during the late Paleozoic and early Mesozoic eras; its breakup began in the Triassic period.

Examples

The Pangea supercontinent
Many fossil distributions make sense only if Pangea once existed.
Scientists believe that Pangea began breaking up about 175 million years ago.
The theory of Pangea explains how continents drifted apart over millions of years.
Millions of years ago, Pangaea began to break apart, eventually forming today's continents.
Scientists believe that Earth’s climate was very different when Pangaea existed.
The theory of Pangaea helps explain similarities in fossil records across different continents.
