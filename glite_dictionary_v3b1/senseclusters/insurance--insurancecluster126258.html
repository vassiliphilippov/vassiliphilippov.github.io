insurance

noun
arrangement for compensation against loss or damage
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈʃʊrəns /; / ɪnˈʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃʊərəns /; / ɪnˈʃɔː.rəns / (BrE)
an arrangement in which you pay a company or organization to protect you against loss, damage, illness, or death

Examples

Health insurance
I bought insurance to protect my house against fire and theft.
She works for a large insurance company in New York.
Travelers are advised to purchase health insurance before going abroad.
It is important to have insurance in case of an accident or theft.
Many homeowners consider insurance against natural disasters essential for peace of mind.
She bought travel insurance before going abroad to cover any medical emergencies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: risk - Possibility of loss or damage
