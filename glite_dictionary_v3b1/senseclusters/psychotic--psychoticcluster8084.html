psychotic

noun
person with severe mental disorder
Pronunciation: / saɪˈkɑtɪk / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒtɪk / (BrE)
a person suffering from psychosis, a severe mental disorder characterized by a disconnection from reality

Examples

A psychotic patient
After years of therapy, the psychotic gradually learned to distinguish reality from his illusions.
Some psychotics may experience vivid hallucinations and strong delusional beliefs.
The hospital has a special unit for treating psychotics who require intensive care.
