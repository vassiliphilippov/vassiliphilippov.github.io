at the feet of

preposition
in submission or learning from a superior
Pronunciation: / æt ðə fit əv / (AmE), / at ðə fiːt əv / (BrE)
in a state of submission, deference, or learning with respect to someone seen as a superior or master

Examples

Trust at the feet of mentor
He placed his trust completely at the feet of his mentor.
She spent years at the feet of great scientists to master her craft.
The disciples learned patiently at the feet of their teacher.
