linear

adjective
long and narrow leaf shape
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪə / (BrE)
(of a leaf) long and narrow with nearly parallel sides, like a straight line.

Examples

Linear leaf
Botanists described the specimen as having small, linear foliage along its stem.
The grass blades are linear, giving the field a sleek, uniform appearance.
This plant is identified by its linear leaves, which can be easily distinguished from broader types.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: lanceolate - (of a leaf) shaped like a lance tip; longer than wide and tapering at the end
