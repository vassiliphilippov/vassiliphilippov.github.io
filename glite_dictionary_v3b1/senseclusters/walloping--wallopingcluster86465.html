walloping

noun
a heavy or decisive defeat
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒləpɪŋ / (BrE)
A heavy or decisive defeat; a thorough beating, especially in a sport or competition.

Examples

A walloping loss
After last night's walloping, the coach promised big changes for the next match.
The team suffered a walloping in the championship game, losing by six goals.
They gave their rivals a real walloping in front of a cheering crowd.

  • antonym: victory - success in defeating an opponent
