Hussitism

noun
movement inspired by Jan Hus
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌsɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhʌsɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
The religious doctrines and reform movement inspired by Jan Hus in 15th-century Bohemia, considered a precursor to the Protestant Reformation.

Examples

Hussitism religious movement
Many historians consider Hussitism a crucial step toward the broader Protestant Reformation.
Religious conflicts erupted in Bohemia over the rise of Hussitism in the early 15th century.
The spread of Hussitism profoundly shaped the religious and political landscape of Central Europe.
