chicory plant

noun
plant grown for edible roots and leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪkəri plænt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkəri plɑːnt / (BrE)
A perennial plant with bright blue flowers, cultivated for its edible roots and leaves used in salads.

Examples

Fresh leaves from the chicory plant
Fresh leaves from the chicory plant add a pleasantly bitter flavor to salads.
Gardeners enjoy growing the chicory plant for its vibrant blue flowers and edible leaves.
The chicory plant is often used as a coffee substitute due to its flavorful roots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endive - a similar leafy plant sometimes called chicory
