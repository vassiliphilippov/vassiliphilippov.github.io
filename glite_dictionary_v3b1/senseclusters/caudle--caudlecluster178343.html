caudle

noun
old medicinal drink
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔdəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdl / (BrE)
a warm drink made from wine or ale mixed with bread, eggs, sugar, and spices, formerly given to sick people

Examples

A warm caudle drink
In the past, a caudle was often served to patients recovering from illness.
The recipe for caudle includes wine, eggs, sugar, and a pinch of spice.
She sipped a warm caudle to soothe her sore throat on a chilly evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: posset - a hot drink of milk curdled with wine or ale, often used as a remedy
