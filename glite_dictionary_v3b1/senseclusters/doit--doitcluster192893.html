doit

noun
trivial amount
Pronunciation: / dɔɪt / (AmE), / dɔɪt / (BrE)
a very small amount or something of little value, often used figuratively to mean a trifle.

Examples

Not worth a doit
He did not care a doit about the criticism he received.
She refused to spend a single doit on unnecessary luxuries.
The old saying goes that honesty is worth more than a doit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fortune - a large amount of money or assets
  • synonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount
  • synonym: trifle - something of little value or importance
