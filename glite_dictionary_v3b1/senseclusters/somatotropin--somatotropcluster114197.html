somatotropin

noun
growth-stimulating pituitary hormone
2.8 7.8
Pronunciation: /soʊ.mə.tə.ˈtroʊ.pɪn/ (AmE), /səʊ.mə.tə.ˈtrəʊ.pɪn/ (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates growth and cell reproduction in humans and animals

Examples

Somatotropin hormone
Doctors may prescribe somatotropin to children with growth hormone deficiency.
Excess production of somatotropin in adults may lead to acromegaly.
Levels of somatotropin can be measured through specific blood tests.
