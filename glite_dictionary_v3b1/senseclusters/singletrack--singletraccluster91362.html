singletrack

noun
narrow path or road
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəlˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡlˌtræk /; / ˈsɪŋɡl̩ˌtræk / (BrE)
a narrow road, path, or trail where only one vehicle, bike, or person can go at a time

Examples

Mountain singletrack
The mountain bikers followed a winding singletrack through the forest.
On the remote island, the only access was a rough singletrack barely wide enough for a car.
Hikers must yield to each other on the narrow singletrack to avoid collisions.
Mountain bikers love the challenge of riding on singletrack through the woods.
The park recently added several miles of singletrack for hikers and cyclists.
He prefers singletrack because it feels more adventurous than wider trails.

Related Terms

  • antonym: doubletrack - a wider trail that allows two people to travel side by side
  • degree higher: road - a wide way leading from one place to another, especially for vehicles
  • synonym: trail - a path or track made for walking, cycling, or riding
