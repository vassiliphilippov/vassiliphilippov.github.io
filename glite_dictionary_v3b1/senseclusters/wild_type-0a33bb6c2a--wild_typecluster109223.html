wild type

noun
natural genetic form
4.8
Pronunciation: /waɪld taɪp/ (AmE), /waɪld taɪp/ (BrE)
the typical form of a species or gene as it occurs naturally, especially used in genetics to contrast with mutants

Examples

A wild type fly
Scientists observed that the wild type fruit flies had red eyes, unlike the mutants.
The new drug was tested on both wild type and genetically modified mice.
In nature, the wild type gene is usually the most common version found in a population.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mutant - an organism or gene that has undergone a mutation
  • synonym: normal - conforming to the standard or typical form
