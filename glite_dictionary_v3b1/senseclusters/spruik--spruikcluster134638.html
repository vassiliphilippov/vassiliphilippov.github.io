spruik

noun
promotional pitch
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / spruÀêk / (AmE), / spruÀêk / (BrE)
a promotional speech or pitch intended to attract customers, attention, or support

Examples

A sales spruik
The travel agent gave a lively spruik to encourage people to join the tour.
After his impressive spruik, many customers lined up to try the new product.
She prepared a short spruik to present at the business expo.

Synonyms

  • sales pitch
    3.2 1.19/m
    a sales pitch is a short talk that tries to get someone to buy something, while a spruik is broader promotional speech.
  • sales talk
    3.0 0.06/m
    a sales talk is an informal conversation that persuades people about a product, while a spruik is a public promotional speech.
  • spiel
    7.9 1.06/m
    a spiel is a smooth, often long persuasive talk that sounds rehearsed, while a spruik is a public promotional speech.
  • pitch
    4.2 5.94/m
    a pitch can mean a vendor's spot or a short persuasive presentation, while a spruik is specifically a promotional speech to attract customers.
