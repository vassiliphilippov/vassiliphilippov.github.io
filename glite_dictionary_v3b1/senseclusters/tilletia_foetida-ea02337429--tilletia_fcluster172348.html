Tilletia foetida

noun
species of fungus causing wheat bunt
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɪˈlɛtiə foʊˈiːtɪdə / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛtiə fəʊˈɛtɪdə / (BrE)
A species of fungus that causes bunt disease in wheat, similar to Tilletia caries.

Examples

Tilletia foetida fungus
Proper seed treatment can help control the spread of Tilletia foetida in wheat crops.
Research found that Tilletia foetida was responsible for reduced wheat yields in infected regions.
The spores of Tilletia foetida can persist in soil for several years, posing a challenge for farmers.
