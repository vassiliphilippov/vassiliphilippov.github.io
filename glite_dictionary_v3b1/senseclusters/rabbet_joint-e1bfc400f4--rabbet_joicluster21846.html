rabbet joint

noun
woodworking joint with rabbeted boards
9.5 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈræb.ət dʒɔɪnt/ /; / /ˈræb.ɪt dʒɔɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈræb.ət dʒɔɪnt/ /; / /ˈræb.ɪt dʒɔɪnt/ (BrE)
A joint made by fitting together two boards, each with a rabbet cut, so that their edges interlock.

Examples

A rabbet joint in wood
A rabbet joint provides a strong and neat way to join two pieces of wood at a right angle.
He carefully measured each board before cutting the rabbet joint required for the frame.
The cabinetmaker used a rabbet joint to secure the back panel to the sides.
