Cyrillic

noun
Slavic alphabet derived from Greek
Pronunciation: / səˈrɪlɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪlɪk / (BrE)
the alphabet derived from the Greek alphabet and used for writing various Slavic languages, such as Russian, Bulgarian, and Serbian.

Examples

The Cyrillic alphabet
Bulgarian officially switched to the Cyrillic alphabet in the ninth century.
He learned to write his name in Cyrillic during his language class.
Many Russian books are printed in Cyrillic, which can be challenging for beginners to read.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Greek alphabet - the script from which Cyrillic is derived
  • antonym: Latin alphabet - the alphabet used for English and many other languages
