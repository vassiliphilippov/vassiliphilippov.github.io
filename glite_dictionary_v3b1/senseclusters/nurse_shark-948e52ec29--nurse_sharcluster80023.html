nurse shark

noun
a type of bottom-dwelling shark
9.9 5.1
Pronunciation: /nɜːrs ʃɑːrk/ (AmE), /nɜːs ʃɑːk/ (BrE)
a bottom-dwelling shark found in warm shallow coastal waters of the Americas and parts of the Indo-Pacific, known for its sluggish behavior and distinctive barbels near the mouth

Examples

A nurse shark with barbels
A nurse shark can be identified by the fleshy barbels on its snout.
Divers observed a group of nurse sharks swimming lazily near the coral reef.
The nurse shark is often seen resting on the sea floor during the day.
