thromboplastin

noun
laboratory reagent for clotting tests
Pronunciation: / \u02cc\u03b8r\u0251\u02d0mbo\u028a\u02c8pl\u00e6st\u026an / (AmE), / \u02cc\u03b8r\u0252mb\u0259\u028a\u02c8pl\u00e6st\u026an / (BrE)
(in laboratory medicine) a reagent containing tissue factor (and usually phospholipids) used to initiate the extrinsic coagulation pathway in diagnostic clotting tests such as the prothrombin time (PT).

Examples

Lab thromboplastin test
In the lab, technicians used a thromboplastin reagent to start the PT assay.
To measure clotting, the pathologist added thromboplastin to the patient's plasma and timed clot formation.
Several manufacturers supply standardized thromboplastin preparations for consistent prothrombin time testing across laboratories.

Related Terms

  • related: prothrombin time (PT) - a laboratory test that measures how long blood takes to clot; commonly performed using thromboplastin reagents
  • synonym: tissue factor - a protein that initiates the extrinsic pathway of blood coagulation; the active component of many thromboplastin reagents
