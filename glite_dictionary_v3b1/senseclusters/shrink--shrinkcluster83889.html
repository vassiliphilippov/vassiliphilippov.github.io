shrink

verb
become or make smaller
2.9
Pronunciation: / ʃrɪŋk / (AmE), / ʃrɪŋk / (BrE)
to become or make something smaller in amount, value, or extent; to decrease

Examples

Profits shrink quickly
Doctors tried to shrink the tumor using radiation therapy.
If you wash this shirt in hot water, it might shrink.
The market for traditional phones continues to shrink year after year.
Attendance at the event shrank dramatically after the weather turned bad.
My jeans always shrink if I put them in the dryer.
The company's profits have continued to shrink over the past three years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contract - to become smaller or draw together
  • antonym: expand - to become or make larger in size or amount
