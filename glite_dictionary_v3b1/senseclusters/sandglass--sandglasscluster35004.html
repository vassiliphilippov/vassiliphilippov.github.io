sandglass

noun
device to measure time by sand flow
Pronunciation: / ˈsændˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈsændˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure time, consisting of two glass bulbs connected by a narrow passage, through which sand flows from the upper to the lower bulb in a fixed amount of time

Examples

A sandglass timer
A sandglass was often used by sailors to measure shifts during their watch.
He turned the sandglass over to begin timing the experiment.
The old sandglass on the mantelpiece measured exactly one hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hourglass - a device for measuring time by the flow of sand between two bulbs
