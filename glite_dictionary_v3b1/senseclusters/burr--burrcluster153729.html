burr

noun
rough edge left after cutting or drilling
Pronunciation: / bɜr / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
A rough edge or small piece of material left on a workpiece after it has been cut, drilled, or machined.

Examples

Metal burr edge
After cutting the metal sheet, a small burr was left along the edge.
The technician used a file to smooth down the burr created by the drill.
You should always remove any burrs to ensure a smooth surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ridge - a raised strip, often left on a surface after machining
