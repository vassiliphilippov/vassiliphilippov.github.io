tacking

noun
nautical: changing direction by turning through the wind
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtækɪŋ / (BrE)
(nautical) The action of changing a ship's course by turning its bow through the wind so that the wind changes from one side to the other.

Examples

Sailboat tacking
Proper tacking is essential for competitive sailboat racing.
Sailing into the wind required frequent tacking to maintain our course.
The crew prepared for tacking as the wind shifted suddenly.

  • synonym: tack - the action of changing direction in sailing by turning the bow through the wind
