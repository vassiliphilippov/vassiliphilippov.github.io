socialize

verb
prepare for life in society
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to train or prepare someone (a person or animal) to behave in a way that is acceptable in society

Examples

To socialize children
It is important to socialize children so they understand how to get along with others.
Schools play a key role in helping young people socialize into the broader community.
Some parents worry about how best to socialize their children for life in a diverse society.
Puppies should be socialized early to prevent behavioral problems later.
The program helps parents understand how to socialize their toddlers.
Young children need to be socialized so that they can interact well with others.
Parents play a crucial role in helping their children socialise from an early age.
Schools provide opportunities for students to socialise and learn accepted social norms.
Some animals, like wolves, need to socialise with their pack to develop normal behavior.

  • antonym: isolate - to cause (a person or place) to be or remain alone or apart from others
