witter

verb
to talk aimlessly
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtər / (AmE), / ˈwɪtə / (BrE)
to talk for a long time about unimportant or trivial things, often in an annoying or boring way

Examples

To witter on endlessly
She would often witter on about her cats for hours without stopping.
Please don't witter during the meeting; let's stay focused on the agenda.
He tends to witter when he's nervous, filling the silence with small talk.
She tends to witter on about her cats whenever we meet for coffee.
Stop wittering and get to the point, please.
He wittered for ages about the traffic before finally starting the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk quickly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • antonym: be concise - to express oneself clearly and briefly
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write in a long, confused, or aimless way
