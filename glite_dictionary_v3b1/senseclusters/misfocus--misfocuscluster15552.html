misfocus

noun
mistaken emphasis
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
a situation in which attention or emphasis is directed to the wrong person, issue, or aspect by mistake

Examples

A misfocus on details
The team's misfocus on minor details delayed the completion of the project.
A common misfocus in meetings is spending too much time on less important topics.
Her misfocus on appearance rather than skills affected her job performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distraction - something that draws attention away from the main issue
  • antonym: focus - concentration of attention on the main issue
