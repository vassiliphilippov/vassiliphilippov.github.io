squelch

noun
cutting, silencing remark
Pronunciation: / skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
a forceful or cutting remark that silences or humiliates someone

Examples

A sharp squelch
Her sarcastic squelch left him speechless in front of everyone.
She delivered a perfect squelch to the heckler during the debate.
The room fell silent after his witty squelch shut down the argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: put-down - an insulting or humiliating remark
  • synonym: retort - a quick, witty, or cutting reply
