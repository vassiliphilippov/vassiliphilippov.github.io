turgidness

noun
swollen or distended state (biological)
Pronunciation: / c8t5ard926adn59s / (AmE), / c8t5cd0d926adn59s / (BrE)
(in biology and medicine) the state of being swollen or distended because of internal fluid pressure, especially in cells or tissues.

Examples

Cell turgidness
Under drought stress, the botanist observed reduced turgidness in many leaf cells.
The surgeon noted abnormal turgidness of the tissue, suggesting internal fluid accumulation.
Laboratory staining made the increased turgidness of the cells clearly visible under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flaccidity - the state of being limp or lacking normal firmness; opposite of turgidness
  • synonym: turgor - internal pressure within plant cells that helps keep them firm
