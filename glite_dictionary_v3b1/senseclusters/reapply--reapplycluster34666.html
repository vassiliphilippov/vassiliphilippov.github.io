reapply

verb
to apply again
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to submit an application again for something, especially after being rejected or after a previous attempt

Examples

To reapply for a job
If your visa is denied, you can reapply after correcting the mistakes in your application.
She plans to reapply for the scholarship next semester after improving her grades.
Many candidates choose to reapply for the same position after gaining more experience.
After her visa was denied, she decided to reapply the following year.
You can reapply for the scholarship if you did not receive it the first time.
He plans to reapply to several universities with a stronger personal statement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apply - to formally request something, such as a job or admission
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove or take back an application or request
