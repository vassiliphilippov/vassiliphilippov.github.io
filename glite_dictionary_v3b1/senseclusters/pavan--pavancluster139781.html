pavan

noun
Renaissance dance music
Pronunciation: / pəˈvɑn / (AmE), / pəˈvɑːn / (BrE)
A slow, stately piece of music composed for the pavane, a Renaissance dance.

Examples

A musical pavan
Composers of the Renaissance often wrote a pavan to accompany social dances.
The elegant steps of the pavane were matched by the somber melody of the pavan.
The orchestra performed a beautifully haunting pavan by a sixteenth-century composer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pavane - a slow processional dance of the Renaissance period
