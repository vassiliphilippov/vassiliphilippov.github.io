sorb apple

noun
acidic fruit of the sorb tree
9.2
Pronunciation: / sɔrb ˈæpəl / (AmE), / sɔːb ˈæpəl / (BrE)
A small, acidic fruit with a gritty texture, produced by the sorb tree (Sorbus domestica).

Examples

A sorb apple fruit
Because of its tart flavor, the sorb apple is sometimes used to make preserves and jams.
The children picked a ripe sorb apple from the old tree in the orchard.
The gritty texture of a fresh sorb apple makes it quite distinct from other fruits.
