metamerism

noun
color matching phenomenon
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈtæmərɪzəm / (AmE), / mɪˈtæmərɪzəm / (BrE)
a phenomenon where two colors appear the same under one light source but different under another

Examples

Metamerism under different lights
The artist was surprised by metamerism when her painting looked different under fluorescent lights.
Manufacturers test for metamerism to ensure fabric colors match in all lighting conditions.
Because of metamerism, two car parts may seem identical in the showroom but not outside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: color difference - the degree to which two colors are perceived as different
  • synonym: color matching - the process of making colors appear the same
