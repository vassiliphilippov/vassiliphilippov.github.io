Kuiper belt object

noun
minor planet beyond Neptune in the solar system
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪpər bɛlt ˈɑbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈkʌɪpə bɛlt ˈɒbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
a small celestial body orbiting the Sun in the region beyond Neptune, within the Kuiper belt at the edge of the solar system

Examples

A Kuiper belt object
Astronomers recently discovered a new Kuiper belt object with an unusual orbit.
Many Kuiper belt objects are composed mostly of ice and rock.
The New Horizons spacecraft was sent to study a Kuiper belt object after its flyby of Pluto.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trans-Neptunian object - an object in the solar system that orbits the Sun at a greater average distance than Neptune
