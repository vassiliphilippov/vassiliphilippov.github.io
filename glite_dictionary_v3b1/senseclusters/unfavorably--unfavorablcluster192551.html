unfavorably

adverb
in a disapproving or adverse way
5.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbli / (BrE)
in a way that shows disapproval or lack of approval; in a disparaging or adverse manner

Examples

Speak unfavorably about
Many critics spoke unfavorably about the new policy changes.
She viewed the proposal unfavorably due to its lack of detail.
The report was received unfavorably by the committee members.
Her work was judged unfavourably compared to her colleagues'.
The committee responded unfavourably to his proposal.
The policy has been described unfavourably in several recent reports.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critically - in a way that expresses disapproval or adverse comments
  • antonym: favorably - in a way that shows approval or support
