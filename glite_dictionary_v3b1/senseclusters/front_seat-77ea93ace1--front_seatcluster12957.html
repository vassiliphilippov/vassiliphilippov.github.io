front seat

noun
seat next to driver in a vehicle
Pronunciation: / frʌnt siːt / (AmE), / frʌnt siːt / (BrE)
the seat in the front of a vehicle, usually next to the driver

Examples

The front seat
He leaned over from the front seat to adjust the radio.
In most countries, children must use a car seat if they ride in the front seat.
She called shotgun so she could sit in the front seat on the way to the concert.

Related Terms

  • antonym: back seat - the seat behind the front seats in a car
