metamorphic

adjective
relating to change by metamorphosis, esp. rocks
6.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈmɔːr.fɪk/ /; / /mə.tə.ˈmɔːr.fɪk/ (AmE), /mə.tə.ˈmɔː.fɪk/ /; / /me.tə.ˈmɔː.fɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or resulting from metamorphosis, especially referring to rocks that have been changed in form by heat, pressure, or other natural forces.

Examples

Metamorphic rock formation
Marble is a type of metamorphic rock formed from limestone.
The geologist analyzed the metamorphic changes that had occurred over millions of years.
The region contains impressive metamorphic stages in the geological record.
