universalist

adjective
relating to universalism (inclusion of all)
Pronunciation: / juˌnɪvərˈsælɪst / (AmE), / juːˌnɪvəˈsælɪst / (BrE)
relating to or advocating universalism, especially the belief that all people will eventually be saved or included.

Examples

Universalist beliefs
He argued from a universalist perspective, seeking solutions that benefit everyone.
Her universalist views on religion emphasized the inclusion of all faiths.
The organization adopted a universalist approach to human rights.

Related Terms

  • antonym: particularist - pertaining to particularism (exclusive focus on individual groups or interests)
