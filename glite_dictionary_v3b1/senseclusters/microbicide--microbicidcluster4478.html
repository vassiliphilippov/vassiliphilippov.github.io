microbicide

noun
agent that kills microbes
Pronunciation: / maɪˈkroʊbɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / maɪˈkrəʊbɪsaɪd /; / maɪˈkrəʊbɪˌsaɪd / (BrE)
a substance that kills or stops the growth of microbes (such as bacteria, viruses, or fungi), often used to prevent infection

Examples

A microbicide substance
Researchers are developing a new microbicide to help prevent the spread of sexually transmitted infections.
The microbicide gel must be applied before contact to be effective.
Many hospitals use microbicide solutions to disinfect medical equipment.
Scientists are developing a new microbicide to help prevent the spread of infections.
The gel contains a microbicide that targets harmful bacteria and viruses.
Using a microbicide can reduce the risk of contracting certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antimicrobial - a substance that kills or stops the growth of microorganisms
  • synonym: antiseptic - a substance that prevents the growth of disease-causing microorganisms
  • synonym: disinfectant - a chemical used to destroy harmful microorganisms
  • degree lower: microbe - a microscopic organism such as a bacterium or virus
