intraosseous

adjective
inside a bone
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈɑsiəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈɒsiəs / (BrE)
located within or occurring inside a bone

Examples

Intraosseous line
The emergency team used an intraosseous line to deliver fluids when veins were inaccessible.
An intraosseous injection is sometimes necessary in critical medical situations.
Doctors may choose the intraosseous route for rapid medication delivery in children.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intravenous - located within or administered into a vein
