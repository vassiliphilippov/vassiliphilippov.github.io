hyperflexion

noun
excessive joint bending
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
excessive bending of a joint beyond its normal range of motion

Examples

Sudden hyperflexion injury
The gymnast's injury was caused by sudden hyperflexion of her wrist during practice.
Doctors warned that repeated hyperflexion of the neck could lead to long-term damage.
Protective gear can help prevent hyperflexion injuries in contact sports.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: flexion - the act of bending a joint
  • antonym: hyperextension - excessive straightening of a joint beyond its normal range
