Quercus cerris

noun
a species of oak tree (Turkey oak)
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs ˈsɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈsɛrɪs / (BrE)
a large deciduous oak tree native to central and southern Europe and Asia Minor, with lance-shaped leaves bearing spiked lobes; also known as the Turkey oak

Examples

Quercus cerris tree
Botanists studied the spread of Quercus cerris across southern Europe.
In certain regions, the Quercus cerris is valued for providing shade along rural roads.
The leaves of the Quercus cerris are easily recognized by their spiked lobes.
