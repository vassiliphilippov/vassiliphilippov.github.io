outflank

verb
gain an advantage over; get the better of
7.3 5.6
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈflæŋk / (AmE), / aʊtˈflæŋk / (BrE)
to gain an advantage over someone, especially by using clever or indirect methods; to get the better of an opponent

Examples

To outflank a rival
Lawyers often attempt to outflank their opponents with technical legal strategies.
She tried to outflank her adversary in the negotiations with a surprising offer.
The team managed to outflank their competition by launching a new product first.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outmaneuver - to gain an advantage over by skillful or clever moves
  • synonym: outwit - to get the better of by being more clever
  • antonym: succumb - to fail to resist; to be defeated by
