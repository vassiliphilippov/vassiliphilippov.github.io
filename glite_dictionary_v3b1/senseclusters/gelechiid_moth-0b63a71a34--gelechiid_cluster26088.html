gelechiid moth

noun
moth of the Gelechiidae family, pest larvae
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈlɛki.ɪd mɑθ / (AmE), / dʒəˈlɛkɪɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
A small, slender-winged moth of the family Gelechiidae, whose larvae are often agricultural pests.

Examples

A gelechiid moth infestation
A single gelechiid moth infestation can devastate entire fields of potatoes.
Scientists are developing new methods to control the spread of the gelechiid moth in greenhouses.
The cotton crop was severely damaged by gelechiid moth larvae this year.
