superposition

noun
placement of one object atop another to show alignment

Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.pɚ.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pə.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Geometry) The act of placing one object over another to show that the two coincide in position and shape.

Examples

Superposition of triangles
In geometry, superposition is used to prove that two triangles are congruent by placing one over the other.
