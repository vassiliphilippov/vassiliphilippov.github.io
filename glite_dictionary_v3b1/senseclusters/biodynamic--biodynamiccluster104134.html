biodynamic

adjective
body-centered therapy
8.2
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.daɪ.ˈnæ.mɪk/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.daɪ.ˈnæ.mɪk/ (BrE)
relating to a form of therapy or massage that focuses on releasing emotional tension through physical sensations and movement.

Examples

Biodynamic massage therapy
She specializes in biodynamic massage to help clients release deep-seated emotional stress.
Many people find biodynamic therapy effective for connecting their physical and emotional well-being.
The clinic offers biodynamic sessions as part of its holistic health program.

Related Terms

  • antonym: psychodynamic - relating to the interrelation of the unconscious and conscious mental and emotional forces
  • synonym: somatic - relating to the body, especially as distinct from the mind
