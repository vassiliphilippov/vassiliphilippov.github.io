solemnization

noun
formal public performance of a ceremony
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːləmnəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɑːləm.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɒləmnʌɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɒl.əm.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the formal and public performance of a ceremony, sacrament, religious or legal rite, especially a marriage, with appropriate or officially recognized rituals

Examples

The solemnization of marriage
Legal documents were required before the solemnization of their marriage could proceed.
Many cultures view the solemnization of important rites as necessary for community recognition.
The solemnization of the wedding took place in the cathedral with a full congregation.
Certain countries require a public solemnisation for a marriage to be legally recognized.
The solemnisation of their marriage was held in the cathedral.
The priest oversaw the solemnisation of the religious ceremony with great reverence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: celebration - a special social event, such as a ceremony, held to mark a particular occasion
