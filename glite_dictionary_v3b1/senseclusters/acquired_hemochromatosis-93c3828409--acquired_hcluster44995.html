acquired hemochromatosis

noun
iron overload from transfusions or diet
8.2
Pronunciation: / əˈkwaɪɚd ˌhiːmoʊˌkroʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪəd ˌhiːməʊˌkrəʊməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A form of hemochromatosis that develops as a result of repeated blood transfusions or excessive intake of iron-rich foods, rather than being inherited.

Examples

Acquired hemochromatosis from transfusions
Doctors monitor iron levels closely in conditions where acquired hemochromatosis is a potential risk.
The patient developed acquired hemochromatosis after years of chronic blood transfusions for anemia.
Unlike genetic cases, acquired hemochromatosis can often be managed by reducing iron intake and limiting transfusions.
