carbonization

noun
conversion of organic material to carbon by heating
9.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ërb…ôna…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåk…ëÀêb…ôn å…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the process of converting organic material, especially coal, into carbon or carbon-containing residues by heating in the absence of air; notably, the destructive distillation of coal to produce coke.

Examples

Coal carbonization process
During carbonization, volatile compounds are removed, leaving behind almost pure carbon.
Modern coke ovens are designed to maximize the efficiency of carbonization processes.
The carbonization of coal is a crucial step in producing coke for the steel industry.
Advances in carbonisation technology have improved energy efficiency in the industry.
During carbonisation, volatile substances are driven off, leaving carbon-rich coke behind.
The carbonisation of coal produces coke used in steel manufacturing.
