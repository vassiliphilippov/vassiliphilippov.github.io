pom-pom

noun
automatic anti-aircraft gun
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑm pɑm / (AmE), / ˈpɒm pɒm / (BrE)
a type of automatic anti-aircraft gun, especially one used during the early 20th century, designed to fire rapidly at aircraft

Examples

Ship pom-pom gun
During the war, ships were often equipped with a pom-pom to defend against air raids.
The distinctive sound of the pom-pom could be heard across the harbor during the attack.
The gun crew quickly fired the pom-pom at the approaching enemy planes.
