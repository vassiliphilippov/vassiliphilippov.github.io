defeasibility

noun
ability to be overturned
Pronunciation: / dɪˌfiːzəˈbɪlɪti /; / dɪˌfiːzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌfiːzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of something that can be made invalid, overturned, or undone, especially by new evidence or under certain conditions (used in logic, science, or law)
