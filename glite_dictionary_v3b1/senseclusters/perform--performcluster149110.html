perform

verb
carry out a task or action
1.3 108/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàf…îrm / (AmE), / p…ôÀàf…îÀêm / (BrE)
to do or carry out an action, task, duty, or function

Examples

To perform a task
She was asked to perform several tasks during her first week.
The surgeon will perform the operation tomorrow morning.
You must perform your duties with care and attention.
Doctors must perform their duties with great care and responsibility.

The technicians performed repairs on the damaged machinery last night.
Only trained surgeons are allowed to perform this operation.

The computer can perform multiple tasks at once without slowing down.

Synonyms

  • do
    1.0 1183/m
    do is a short, common verb for taking action in many situations, while perform sounds a bit more formal.
  • carry out
    1.8 31/m
    carry out means to complete a specific task or instruction step by step, while perform is a general word for doing actions.
  • execute
    3.6 13/m
    execute means to carry out a plan precisely or officially, while perform is a broader word for doing tasks or duties.
  • exercise
    3.3 10/m
    exercise means to fulfill duties or use a power in a role, while perform is a general word for doing actions or tasks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: execute - to carry out or put into effect a plan, order, or course of action
  • antonym: fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal or completing a task
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to carry out an action or duty
