plover

noun
a type of shore bird with a short bill
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplʌv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌv.ə / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized wading bird with a short, straight bill and long, pointed wings, typically found on shorelines and belonging to the family Charadriidae

Examples

A plover bird
The distinctive call of the plover could be heard over the sound of the crashing waves.
The golden plover migrates great distances between its breeding and wintering grounds.
We watched a flock of plovers feeding along the muddy seashore at dawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandpiper - a wading bird related to the plover, often with a longer bill
