aculeate

adjective
having a stinger or sharp point
Pronunciation: / əˈkjuːliˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈkjuːliːət / (BrE)
having or resembling a stinger or sharp point, especially used to describe insects such as bees and wasps

Examples

Aculeate insects
Bees, ants, and wasps are among the most common aculeate insects found in gardens.
Many aculeate insects use their stingers for defense against predators.
The biologist compared the stinging mechanisms of several aculeate species.
