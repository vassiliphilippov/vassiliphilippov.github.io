chondrocyte

noun
cartilage cell
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑndroʊˌsaɪt /; / ˈkɑndrəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒndrəsʌɪt /; / ˈkɒndrəsiːt / (BrE)
a cell found in cartilage that produces and maintains the cartilaginous matrix

Examples

A chondrocyte cell
A chondrocyte is essential for the growth and repair of cartilage in joints.
Scientists study chondrocytes to understand how cartilage heals after injury.
Healthy chondrocytes help keep your joints flexible and pain-free.
A chondrocyte is responsible for producing the materials that make up cartilage.
Damage to chondrocytes can lead to joint problems and cartilage breakdown.
Researchers study chondrocyte behavior to develop new treatments for arthritis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartilage - a firm, flexible connective tissue
  • synonym: osteocyte - a bone cell that maintains bone tissue
