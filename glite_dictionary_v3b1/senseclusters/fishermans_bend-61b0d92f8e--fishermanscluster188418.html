fisherman's bend

noun
type of knot for ropes
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃərmənz bɛnd / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃəmənz bend / (BrE)
a knot used for securing a rope to a spar or ring, especially in sailing or fishing

Examples

A fisherman's bend knot
A properly tied fisherman's bend can hold even under wet and slippery conditions.
Before casting off, the sailor checked that the fisherman's bend was secure around the ring.
He tied the rope to the dock using a fisherman's bend to ensure it would not slip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anchor bend - a knot for tying a rope to an object such as an anchor or ring
