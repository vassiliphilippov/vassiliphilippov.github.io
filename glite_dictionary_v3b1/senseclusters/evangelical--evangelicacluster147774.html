evangelical

noun
Bible-focused Christian
6.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˌvan.dʒɪˈlɛk.əl / (BrE)
a member of a Christian group that emphasizes the authority of the Bible and the importance of personal faith

Examples

An evangelical speaks
Many evangelicals believe in sharing their faith with others through outreach programs.
The conference attracted thousands of evangelicals from across the country.
Some evangelicals are active in social and political movements based on their beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Protestant - a member of the branch of Christianity that separated from the Roman Catholic Church
  • synonym: fundamentalist - a person who believes in strict, literal interpretation of religious texts
  • antonym: secularist - a person who advocates separation of religion from civic affairs
