bleed

verb
to extract money or possessions unfairly
7.7 0.62/m
Pronunciation: / bliÀêd / (AmE), / bliÀêd / (BrE)
to obtain money or possessions from someone, typically in a relentless or unfair way

Examples

To bleed someone dry
If you let them, these companies will bleed you dry with hidden charges.
Over the years, the lawyer bled his clients with endless fees.
The mechanic tried to bleed me for every penny during the repair.

Synonyms

  • gouge
    8.3 0.53/m
    gouge charges very high or unfair prices to take money, while bleed means to obtain money relentlessly or unfairly.
  • wring
    7.4 2.08/m
    wring forces or pressures someone to give up money or goods, while bleed means to obtain possessions unfairly and relentlessly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drain - to gradually deprive of money or resources
  • synonym: exploit - to make use of someone or something unfairly for one's own advantage
