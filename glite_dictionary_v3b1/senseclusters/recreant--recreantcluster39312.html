recreant

noun
coward; faint-hearted person
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkriənt /; / ˈriːkriənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkriənt / (BrE)
a person who is cowardly or betrays their cause; a coward or traitor

Examples

A recreant betrays
No recreant would dare to show his face among the brave warriors.
Shakespeare often used the word recreant to insult a cowardly character.
The knight was branded a recreant after fleeing the battlefield.
He was branded a recreant for abandoning his comrades in battle.
In the eyes of his peers, the recreant could never regain their trust.
The knight was declared a recreant and cast out of the order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or leader
  • synonym: traitor - a person who betrays someone or something
