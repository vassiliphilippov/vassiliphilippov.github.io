unaccompanied

adjective
happening or existing without something else
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pə.nid / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pə.nid / (BrE)
(of a state or event) happening or existing without something else that might usually be present at the same time.

Examples

Unaccompanied price rise
An increase in price unaccompanied by improved quality can frustrate customers.
She entered the room unaccompanied, drawing everyone's attention.
The patient had a fever unaccompanied by any other symptoms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accompanied - existing or happening together with something else
