fortune

noun
large amount of wealth
2.8 16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îrt É…ôn / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêt ÉuÀên / (BrE)
a large amount of money or wealth; great prosperity

Examples

A fortune in gold
He made a fortune investing in real estate before the market boomed.
It cost a fortune to renovate the old mansion.
She inherited a fortune from her great aunt.
It costs a fortune to send all three children to private school.
She inherited a fortune from her late uncle and decided to travel the world.
They spent a fortune renovating their new house, but the results were stunning.

Synonyms

  • wealth
    1.9 9.71/m
    wealth is a broad store of money and valuable things, while fortune means a large sum of money or personal prosperity.
  • riches
    1.4 0.20/m
    riches means many valuable things or money that people own, while fortune usually points to a single large amount or general prosperity.
