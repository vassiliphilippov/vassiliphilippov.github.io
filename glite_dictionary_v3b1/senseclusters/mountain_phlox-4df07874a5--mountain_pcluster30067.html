mountain phlox

noun
a perennial flowering plant used as ground cover
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊntən floʊks / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn flɒks / (BrE)
a low, tufted perennial phlox with needle-like evergreen leaves and pink or white flowers, native to the United States and widely cultivated as a ground cover

Examples

Pink mountain phlox
Ground covers like mountain phlox help prevent soil erosion on slopes.
The mountain phlox thrives in well-drained, sunny locations and is popular with gardeners.
We planted mountain phlox in the rock garden for its vibrant spring blooms.
