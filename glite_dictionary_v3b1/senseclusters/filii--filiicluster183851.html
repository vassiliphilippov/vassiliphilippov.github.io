filii

noun
Latin genitive of son
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪli.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɪli.aɪ / (BrE)
the Latin genitive singular form of 'filius', meaning 'of the son', used to indicate possession or relationship

Examples

Filii patris
In the phrase 'liber filii', it means 'the book of the son' in Latin.
The inscription included the word filii to show that the property belonged to the son.
Students of Latin often learn that filii is used to indicate possession by a son.
