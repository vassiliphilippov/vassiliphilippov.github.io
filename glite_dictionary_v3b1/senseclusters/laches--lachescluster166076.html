laches

noun
legal delay defense
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.tʃɪz/ /; / /ˈlæ.tʃɪz/ (AmE), /ˈlæ.tʃɪz/ /; / /ˈleɪ.tʃɪz/ (BrE)
a legal principle where a claim is denied because someone waited too long to act, causing harm to the other party

Examples

laches legal defense
The defendant successfully argued laches because the plaintiff waited too long to file the lawsuit.
Due to laches, the court found that the delay had unfairly prejudiced the other party.
If you do not act promptly, your claim may be barred by laches.
The court dismissed the case due to the plaintiff's laches in filing the complaint.
Because of the company's laches, the equipment was not repaired in time, leading to further damage.
A claim may be denied if laches is proven, showing an unreasonable delay in seeking relief.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diligence - careful and persistent effort or work
  • synonym: equity - fairness or justice in the way people are treated, especially in law
  • synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
  • synonym: statute of limitations - a law setting the maximum time after an event to bring a legal action
