dhava

noun
Indian tree used for timber and gum
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːvə / (AmE), / ˈdɑːvə / (BrE)
an Indian tree of the family Combretaceae that is a source of timber and gum

Examples

Dhava tree
Forests of dhava trees are common in the drier regions of central India.
Local people harvest gum from the dhava to use in traditional medicines.
The wood of the dhava is highly valued for construction in rural India.
