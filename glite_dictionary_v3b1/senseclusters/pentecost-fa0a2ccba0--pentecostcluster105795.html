Pentecost

noun
Jewish holy day marking the giving of the Ten Commandments
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛn.tɪˌkɑst / (AmE), / ˈpɛntɪkɒst / (BrE)
In Judaism, a holy day observed on the sixth of Sivan, commemorating the giving of the Ten Commandments to Moses on Mount Sinai.

Examples

Jewish Pentecost holiday
Many Jewish communities gather to read scripture and pray on Pentecost.
On Pentecost, families often decorate their homes with flowers and greenery.
The traditions of Pentecost include the reading of the Ten Commandments in synagogue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Shavuot - Jewish festival also known as Pentecost, celebrating the giving of the Torah
