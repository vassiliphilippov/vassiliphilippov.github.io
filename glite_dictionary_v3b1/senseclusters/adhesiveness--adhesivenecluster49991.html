adhesiveness

noun
the quality of sticking together
6.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / …ôdÀàhiÀês…™vn…ôs / (AmE), / …ôdÀàhiÀês…™vn…ôs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to stick or hold together firmly, especially the tendency of different surfaces to cling to each other

Examples

Strong adhesiveness
Because of its strong adhesiveness, Super Glue can bond a wide variety of materials.
Scientists measured the adhesiveness of the new polymer on metal surfaces.
The adhesiveness of the tape made it perfect for sealing boxes during the move.

Synonyms

    6.2 1.07/m
    adhesion is the physical joining of surfaces by attractive forces, while adhesiveness names the general quality of sticking or holding together.
  • adherence
    6.1 0.45/m
    adherence means firm sticking to a surface or between materials, while adhesiveness describes the general ability to hold surfaces together.
  • stickiness
    3.9 0.43/m
    stickiness is the everyday sense of how tacky something feels, while adhesiveness is the broader ability to hold different surfaces together.
  • tackiness
    4.2 0.01/m
    tackiness is a slight, touchable stickiness often felt on surfaces, while adhesiveness is the general property of sticking or holding together.
  • gluiness
    7.3 0.00/m
    gluiness is a glue-like, often messy stickiness in touch, while adhesiveness is the neutral, general quality of holding surfaces together.
