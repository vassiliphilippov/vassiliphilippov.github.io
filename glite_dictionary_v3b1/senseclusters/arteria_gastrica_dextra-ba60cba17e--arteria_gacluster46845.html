arteria gastrica dextra

noun
artery supplying lower stomach (medical)
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪəriə ɡæsˈtrɪkə ˈdɛkstrə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ɡæsˈtrɪkə ˈdɛkstrə / (BrE)
A branch of the hepatic artery that supplies blood to the pyloric portion of the stomach along the lesser curvature.

Examples

The arteria gastrica dextra branch
An aneurysm in the arteria gastrica dextra is an uncommon but serious condition.
During the operation, the arteria gastrica dextra was carefully ligated to control blood flow.
The surgeon identified the arteria gastrica dextra along the lesser curvature of the stomach.
