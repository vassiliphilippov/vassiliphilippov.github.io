hiss

verb
to express with a hissing sound
3.9
Pronunciation: / hɪs / (AmE), / hɪs / (BrE)
to express something or utter words with a hissing sound, often to show disapproval or as a warning

Examples

To hiss a warning
'Leave now,' he hissed under his breath, fearing they would be overheard.
She hissed at him to be quiet during the performance.
The protestors hissed their disapproval as the speaker took the stage.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: shout - to speak very loudly or cry out
  • degree lower: whisper - to speak very softly without using vocal cords
