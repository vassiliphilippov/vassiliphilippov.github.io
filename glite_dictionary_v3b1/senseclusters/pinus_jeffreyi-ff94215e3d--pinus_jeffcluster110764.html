Pinus jeffreyi

noun
a pine tree native to western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈdʒɛf.ri.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nəs ˈdʒɛf.ri.aɪ / (BrE)
A tall pine tree native to western North America, with long blue-green needles in clusters of three and elongated cones; sometimes considered a variety of ponderosa pine.

Examples

Pinus jeffreyi tree
Botanists surveyed several stands of Pinus jeffreyi in the Sierra Nevada region.
The distinctive needles of Pinus jeffreyi help differentiate it from other pines.
The forest was dominated by Pinus jeffreyi, stretching tall against the blue sky.
