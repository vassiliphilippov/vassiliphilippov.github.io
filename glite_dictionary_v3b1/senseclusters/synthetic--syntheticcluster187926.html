(of a proposition) having its truth or falsity dependent on observation or factual evidence, rather than purely on meanings of words or logic.

Examples

Synthetic proposition
Logical positivists distinguished between analytic and synthetic propositions in their philosophy.
The statement 'all bachelors are unmarried' is analytic, while 'all bachelors are unhappy' is a synthetic claim.
To verify a synthetic statement, one must consult empirical facts, not just meanings.
