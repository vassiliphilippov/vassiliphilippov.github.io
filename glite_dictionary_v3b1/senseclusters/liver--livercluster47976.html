liver

noun
large organ that processes nutrients and detoxifies the body
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
a large, reddish-brown organ in the upper right part of the abdomen that produces bile, processes nutrients, detoxifies harmful substances, and performs various metabolic functions

Examples

Human liver
Alcohol abuse can cause serious damage to your liver.
He was diagnosed with a disease that affects the liver.
The doctor explained the important functions of the liver during the biology class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heart - muscular organ that pumps blood through the body
  • synonym: kidney - organ that filters waste from blood and produces urine
