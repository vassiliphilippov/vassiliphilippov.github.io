outgrowth

noun
natural result of development
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
a result or development that comes from something else

Examples

Outgrowth of change
Her confidence was an outgrowth of years of hard work and persistence.
The internet is an outgrowth of advances in computer technology.
Urban sprawl can be seen as an outgrowth of rapid population growth.
Her interest in politics was a natural outgrowth of her involvement in community service.
Modern jazz is an outgrowth of earlier styles of music.
The environmental movement was an outgrowth of growing public concern about pollution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: byproduct - something produced in addition to the main item
  • antonym: cause - what brings something about
  • synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • synonym: emergence - the process of coming into existence; becoming visible
  • synonym: origin - the point at which something begins or arises
