substantial

adjective
relating to the core or essence of something
4.8 5.0
Pronunciation: / səbˈstænʃəl / (AmE), / səbˈstænʃəl / (BrE)
relating to the essential or fundamental part of something; concerned with real or core aspects rather than superficial ones.

Examples

Substantial argument made
Her critique offered substantial arguments rather than emotional appeals.
The philosopher focused on the substantial aspects of reality in his writings.
We should address the substantial issues, not just the surface details.

Related Terms

  • antonym: superficial - concerned only with the surface or apparent aspects, not the real essence
