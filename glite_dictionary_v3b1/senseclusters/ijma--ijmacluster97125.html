ijma

noun
Islamic legal consensus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪdʒˈmɑː / (AmE), / ɪdʒˈmɑː / (BrE)
the consensus or agreement of Islamic scholars or the Muslim community on a religious or legal issue, considered an authoritative source in Islamic law

Examples

Islamic ijma
The doctrine of ijma is considered a key source of Islamic law after the Quran and Hadith.
Many scholars debated whether ijma had been reached on the issue of inheritance.
Without ijma, some legal questions in Islamic law remain open to interpretation.
In Islamic jurisprudence, ijma is considered a key source of law after the Quran and Hadith.
Scholars debated whether there was true ijma on the interpretation of the new regulation.
The council reached ijma on the issue, making the decision binding for the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consensus - general agreement among a group
  • antonym: disagreement - lack of consensus or accord
  • synonym: fatwa - a legal opinion or ruling by an Islamic scholar
  • synonym: sharia - Islamic law based on the Quran and traditions
