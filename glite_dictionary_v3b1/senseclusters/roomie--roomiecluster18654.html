roomie

noun
informal term for roommate
7.9 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀêmi / (AmE), / ÀàruÀêmi / (BrE)
an informal term for a person with whom you share a room or apartment; a roommate

Examples

My roomie at home
I'm meeting my roomie for dinner after work.
My roomie and I split the rent evenly every month.
She got along well with her college roomie.

Synonyms

  • roommate
    1.1 14/m
    roommate is the neutral, standard word used in writing or speech, while roomie sounds informal and friendly.
  • flatmate
    2.5 0.13/m
    flatmate is mainly British and means someone who shares an apartment, while roomie is informal and friendly in speech.
  • housemate
    1.8 0.69/m
    housemate is someone who lives in the same house, while roomie implies sharing a room or being a casual, close roommate.
