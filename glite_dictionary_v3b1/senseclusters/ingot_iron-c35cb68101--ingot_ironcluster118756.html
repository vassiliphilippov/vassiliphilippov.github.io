ingot iron

noun
high-purity iron used in metallurgy
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡət ˈaɪrn / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡət ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Iron of high purity, typically produced in ingot form by direct casting from the blast furnace and used in the manufacture of certain steel products.

Examples

High purity ingot iron
Historically, ingot iron was preferred for making certain steel alloys due to its low impurity content.
The engineer specified the use of ingot iron in the construction project to guarantee durability.
The pipes were manufactured from ingot iron to ensure maximum purity and strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pig iron - crude iron as first obtained from a furnace, used in making steel
