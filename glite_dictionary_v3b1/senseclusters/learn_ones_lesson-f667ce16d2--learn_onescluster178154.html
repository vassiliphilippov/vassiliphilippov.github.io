learn one's lesson

verb

1.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /lɜːrn wʌnz ˈles.ən/ /; / /lɜːrn wʌns ˈles.ən/ (AmE), /lɜːn wʌnz ˈles.ən/ /; / /lɜːn wʌns ˈles.ən/ (BrE)
to understand from a bad experience or mistake and change your behavior to avoid repeating it

Examples

He didn't lock his bicycle, but after it was stolen he definitely learned his lesson.
If you spend all your money at once, you will quickly learn your lesson.
