flux

noun
rate of flow through a surface (physics)
Pronunciation: / flʌks / (AmE), / flʌks / (BrE)
the rate at which energy, particles, or a physical quantity passes through a surface (especially in physics)

Examples

Energy flux
Neutron flux was highest near the reactor core.
Scientists measured the heat flux across the boundary layer of the material.
The solar flux received by the earth changes with the sun's activity.
A sudden change in solar flux can affect satellite communications.
Scientists calculated the energy flux to analyze the star's radiation.
The magnetic flux through the coil is measured in webers.
Engineers measured the magnetic flux to design the new motor.
In a simple bar magnet, the flux flows from the north to the south pole outside the magnet.
The strength of the flux around the coil affects the induced current.

Related Terms

  • synonym: field - the region influenced by a particular force, such as magnetic or electric
  • synonym: flow - the movement of a substance from one place to another
