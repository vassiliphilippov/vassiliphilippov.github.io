sideswipe

noun
brief verbal attack or dig
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.swaɪp/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.swaɪp/ (BrE)
a brief, indirect verbal attack or critical remark aimed at someone or something.

Examples

A sideswipe at someone
During the interview she made a quiet sideswipe at her former employer that caught everyone's attention.
The mayor's speech included a thinly veiled sideswipe at the opposition over their voting record.
Online, a sarcastic comment became a sideswipe that sparked a heated debate under the post.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jab - a quick, sharp remark intended to criticize or annoy
