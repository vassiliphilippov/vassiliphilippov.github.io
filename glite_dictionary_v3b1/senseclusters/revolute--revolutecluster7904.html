revolute

adjective
leaf margin rolled under
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛvəˌlut / (AmE), / ˈrɛvəljuːt / (BrE)
having leaf margins that are rolled backward toward the underside of the leaf

Examples

Revolute leaf margins
The cactus has revolute leaves that help it conserve moisture in arid environments.
Botanists identified the species by its distinctive revolute leaf edges.
Some plants develop revolute margins as an adaptation to dry climates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: involute - having leaf margins rolled inward toward the upper side
