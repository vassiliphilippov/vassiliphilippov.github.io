jobber

noun
wholesale trader; middleman in commerce
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɑbər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒbə / (BrE)
a person or company that buys large quantities of goods from producers or importers and sells them to retailers or other merchants

Examples

A jobber sells goods
As a jobber, he was responsible for negotiating bulk prices with manufacturers.
Many small retailers rely on a jobber to stock their shelves efficiently.
The grocery store purchased most of its products from a jobber who supplied several local shops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wholesaler - a person or company that sells goods in large quantities at lower prices to retailers
