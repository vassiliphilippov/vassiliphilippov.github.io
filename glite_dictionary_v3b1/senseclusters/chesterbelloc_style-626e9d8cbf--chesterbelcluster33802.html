Chesterbelloc style

noun
literary style of Chesterton and Belloc
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛstərbəˌlɒk staɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛstəˌbɛlək staɪl / (BrE)
A literary style reminiscent of the works and approaches of G.K. Chesterton and Hilaire Belloc, characterized by wit, paradox, and robust argumentation.

Examples

Chesterbelloc style writing
Critics praised the essay’s Chesterbelloc style, noting its blend of irony and erudition.
He delivered the lecture in a Chesterbelloc style that was both forceful and entertaining.
The author's latest book adopts a distinctly Chesterbelloc style in its arguments and humor.
