plugged

adjective
coin with base metal inserted
5.3 8.1
Pronunciation: /plʌgd/ (AmE), /plʌgd/ (BrE)
(of a coin) altered by inserting a piece of base metal, typically to replace valuable material and reduce its intrinsic value.

Examples

Plugged coin
A careful examination showed that the gold piece had been plugged with a small disc of a cheaper metal.
In the nineteenth century, it was not uncommon to find plugged coins in circulation.
The collector discovered that the old silver dollar was actually plugged and less valuable than it appeared.
