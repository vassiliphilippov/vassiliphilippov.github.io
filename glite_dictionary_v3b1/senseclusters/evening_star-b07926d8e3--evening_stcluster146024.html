evening star

noun
Venus or other planet seen at sunset
Pronunciation: / ˈivnɪŋ stɑɹ / (AmE), / ˈiːvnɪŋ stɑː / (BrE)
a bright planet, most often Venus, that is visible in the western sky at sunset

Examples

The evening star shines
For centuries, sailors have used the evening star to navigate at dusk.
People often mistake the evening star for a particularly bright star, but it is actually a planet.
The evening star shone brightly above the horizon as the sun set.

Related Terms

  • antonym: morning star - a bright planet, especially Venus, visible in the eastern sky just before sunrise
