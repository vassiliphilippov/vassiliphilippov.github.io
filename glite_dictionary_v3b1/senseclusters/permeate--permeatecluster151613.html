permeate

verb
spread through; fill every part
6.0 3/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úr.mi.e…™t /; / Ààp…ùÀê.miÀåe…™t /; / Ààp…ùÀê.mi.e…™t / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀê.mi.e…™t / (BrE)
to spread or pass through every part of something; to be present throughout

Examples

Excitement permeates the crowd
A feeling of excitement permeated the crowd as the band took the stage.
A strong scent of lavender permeated the entire room as soon as we opened the door.
The spirit of generosity permeates every aspect of the community's work.
Cultural traditions often permeate each other when diverse communities live closely together.
In metaphysics, some philosophers argue that mind and body permeate one another.
The boundaries between art and technology increasingly permeate in modern design.
During the rainy season, water can permeate the soil and reach the roots of plants.
New ideas about sustainability have gradually permeated the company's culture.
The smell of fresh bread permeated the kitchen, making everyone hungry.

Synonyms

  • pervade
    6.7 4/m
    pervade spreads a feeling or influence through every part of a place, while permeate means to pass through all parts.
  • suffuse
    7.6 2/m
    suffuse gradually spreads color, light, or a feeling over or through something, while permeate focuses on passing into every part.
  • penetrate
    5.0 4/m
    penetrate moves through a barrier or surface, often with force, while permeate simply fills or passes through all parts.
  • diffuse
    5.5 4/m
    diffuse spreads out widely across an area or to many people, while permeate implies passing into every part.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interpenetrate - to penetrate each other mutually
  • synonym: penetrate - to pass into or through; to enter forcibly
  • synonym: saturate - to fill completely so that no more can be absorbed
