run out

verb
(of liquid) flow out and become lost
Pronunciation: / rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
for a liquid to flow out of a container or space, often unintentionally

Examples

Water runs out
If you don't tighten the cap, the water might run out of the bottle.
She didn't notice the juice running out of the carton until it was too late.
Some oil ran out of the engine and stained the driveway.
After the storm, water ran out of the overflowing barrel and into the garden.
Make sure the bathwater doesn't run out onto the floor.
The oil slowly ran out of the broken engine, staining the driveway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drain - to flow away from a place or container
  • synonym: leak - to flow out of a hole or crack
  • synonym: spill - (of liquid) accidentally flow over the edge of its container
