semiconcrete

adjective
partly tangible
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmikɑnˈkriːt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪkɒnˈkriːt / (BrE)
partly concrete; having some but not all qualities of being concrete or tangible

Examples

Semiconcrete examples
The theory uses semiconcrete examples to help students understand the main idea.
Some mathematical objects are considered semiconcrete because they have both abstract and tangible aspects.
In art, a semiconcrete form might suggest a recognizable object without fully depicting it.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: concrete - existing in a material or physical form; real or solid
