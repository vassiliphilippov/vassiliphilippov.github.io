falsifiability

noun
ability to be disproven
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔlsəˌfaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌfɔːlsɪfaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a statement or theory that allows it to be proven false by evidence or experiment

Examples

The falsifiability of a theory
The falsifiability of a hypothesis is essential for it to be considered scientific.
Philosophers often debate the falsifiability of certain claims in psychology.
Without falsifiability, a theory cannot be properly tested or challenged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: testability - the quality of being able to be tested by experiment or observation
  • antonym: unfalsifiable - not able to be proven false by evidence or experiment
  • synonym: verifiability - the quality of being able to be proven true
