open fire

verb
begin shooting or attacking aggressively
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˈfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to begin shooting weapons or to begin attacking aggressively, especially with criticism.

Examples

To open fire with criticism
During the debate, she did not hesitate to open fire on her opponent's policies.
The troops received an order to open fire as soon as the enemy crossed the line.
Without warning, the guards suddenly opened fire on the fleeing suspects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cease fire - stop shooting, especially temporarily
