plowing

noun
the act of tilling soil with a plow
2.8 2.1
Pronunciation: /ˈplaʊ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈplaʊ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of breaking up and turning over soil with a plow in preparation for planting crops

Examples

Field plowing
After the heavy rains, plowing the fields became much more difficult for the farmers.
Modern technology has greatly increased the efficiency of plowing large areas of farmland.
The annual cost of plowing and planting was higher than expected.
He enjoyed watching the ploughing competitions at the rural fair.
The ploughing of the fields was completed before the spring rains arrived.
Traditional ploughing methods require a strong team of oxen or horses.
