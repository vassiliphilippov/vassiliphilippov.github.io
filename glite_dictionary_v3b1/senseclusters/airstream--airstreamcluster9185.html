airstream

noun
airflow generated by a propeller or engine
6.3 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õrÀåstriÀêm / (AmE), / Ààe…ôÀåstriÀêm / (BrE)
the flow of air that moves rearward as a result of an aircraft propeller or jet engine

Examples

Jet airstream
Birds sometimes get caught in the airstream behind an aircraft during takeoff.
Engineers measured the airstream to ensure optimal thrust and efficiency for the small plane.
The powerful airstream created by the propeller buffeted the ground crew as they loaded the cargo.

Synonyms

  • slipstream
    8.0 0.45/m
    slipstream is the backward flow of air behind a moving aircraft that others can follow, while airstream is air from engines or propellers.
  • wash
    8.4 0.65/m
    wash is the backward air flow from a plane's propeller or wing felt as turbulence nearby, while airstream is rearward flow from engines or propellers.
  • race
    8.4 0.31/m
    race is a rare or old word for air pushed back by a propeller on a plane, while airstream names that rearward flow.
