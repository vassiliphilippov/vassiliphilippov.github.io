airstream

noun
airflow generated by a propeller or engine
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌstriːm / (AmE), / ˈeəˌstriːm / (BrE)
the flow of air that moves rearward as a result of an aircraft propeller or jet engine

Examples

Jet airstream
Birds sometimes get caught in the airstream behind an aircraft during takeoff.
Engineers measured the airstream to ensure optimal thrust and efficiency for the small plane.
The powerful airstream created by the propeller buffeted the ground crew as they loaded the cargo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airflow - the movement of air, especially around or through an object
