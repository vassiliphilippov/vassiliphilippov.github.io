slug

noun
imperial unit of mass (≈14.6 kg)
Pronunciation: / slʌɡ / (AmE), / slʌɡ / (BrE)
a unit of mass in the imperial system, equal to the mass that accelerates by 1 foot per second squared when a force of one pound is applied, approximately 14.59 kilograms

Examples

A slug of mass
In the imperial system, force in pounds and mass in slugs maintain consistent units.
One slug is equivalent to approximately 14.6 kilograms in the metric system.
The spacecraft's mass was measured at 2,000 slugs for design calculations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kilogram - SI unit of mass, equivalent to 1,000 grams
