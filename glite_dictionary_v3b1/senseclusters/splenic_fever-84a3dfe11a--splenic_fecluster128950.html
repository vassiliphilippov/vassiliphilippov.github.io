splenic fever

noun
infectious animal disease, also called anthrax
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsplɛnɪk ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈspliːnɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A highly infectious disease of animals, especially cattle and sheep, that can be transmitted to humans; also known as anthrax.

Examples

Splenic fever disease
Farmers are advised to vaccinate livestock against splenic fever to prevent outbreaks.
Humans can contract splenic fever through direct contact with infected animals.
The sudden death of several cattle was later attributed to splenic fever.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthrax - a highly infectious disease caused by Bacillus anthracis
