dextran

noun
water-soluble polysaccharide
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkstræn / (AmE), / ˈdɛkstræn / (BrE)
a water-soluble polysaccharide used in medical and laboratory applications, such as chromatography and protein stabilization

Examples

Dextran beads in labs
The laboratory used dextran beads to separate proteins by size during chromatography.
Doctors sometimes use dextran solutions to increase blood volume in patients.
Researchers found that dextran helps stabilize certain enzymes during storage.

  • synonym: cellulose - a polysaccharide used as a support material in chromatography
