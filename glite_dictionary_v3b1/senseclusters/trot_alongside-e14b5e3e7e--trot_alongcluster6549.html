trot alongside

verb
move at a steady pace beside someone
Pronunciation: / trɑt əˈlɔŋˌsaɪd / (AmE), / trɒt əˈlɒŋˌsaɪd / (BrE)
to move at a steady, moderate pace next to someone or something, often with short, quick steps

Examples

To trot alongside a horse
Horses often trot alongside the carriage during parades in the city.
She paused to let her friend trot alongside before continuing down the trail.
The little boy watched his puppy trot alongside him as they walked to the park.
