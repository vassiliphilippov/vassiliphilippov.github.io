overcommit

verb
to take on too much
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to promise or agree to do more than you can realistically handle (tasks, time, effort, or resources)

Examples

To overcommit your time
Be careful not to overcommit when you already have a busy schedule.
She realized she had overcommitted herself and needed to cancel some appointments.
It's easy to overcommit at work if you don't set clear boundaries.
If you overcommit at work, you may find it hard to finish all your tasks on time.
He tends to overcommit to social events and ends up feeling exhausted.
Try not to overcommit yourself when planning your weekly schedule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overextend - to commit oneself beyond what is reasonable or safe
  • antonym: undercommit - to promise or agree to do less than one is capable of
