mavrodaphne

noun
Greek red grape
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːvroʊˈdæfni / (AmE), / ˌmævrəʊˈdæfni / (BrE)
a dark-skinned red grape variety from Greece, mainly grown in the Peloponnese and used to make sweet or fortified wines

Examples

Mavrodaphne grapes
The famous dessert wine is made from the mavrodaphne grape grown in western Greece.
She visited a vineyard where mavrodaphne was harvested for traditional sweet wines.
Many Greek winemakers specialize in producing fortified wines from mavrodaphne grapes.
