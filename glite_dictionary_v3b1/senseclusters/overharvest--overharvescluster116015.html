overharvest

verb
to harvest excessively
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈhɑrvəst / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhɑːvɪst / (BrE)
to remove too many plants or animals from nature, causing harm to their populations or the environment

Examples

To overharvest animals
If we overharvest fish, their numbers may never recover.
Many forests are threatened because people overharvest valuable trees for timber.
Strict regulations are needed to prevent companies from overharvesting natural resources.
