wine and dine

verb
entertain with abundant food and drink
Pronunciation: / waɪn ənd daɪn / (AmE), / waɪn ənd daɪn / (BrE)
to entertain someone with elaborate or lavish food and drink, often to impress or please them

Examples

He wined and dined his guests with a three-course meal and expensive champagne.
The company is known for wining and dining potential investors to impress them.
They plan to wine and dine the clients at a fancy restaurant to close the deal.
On our trip, we wined and dined every night in the city's best bistros.
The company wined and dined new clients before signing the contract.
They love to wine and dine their friends at fancy restaurants.
