niacin

noun
a B vitamin essential for nerves and digestion
9.4 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈnaɪ.ə.sɪn/ /; / /ˈnaɪ.ə.sən/ (AmE), /ˈnaɪ.ə.sɪn/ /; / /ˈnaɪ.ə.sən/ (BrE)
a vitamin of the B complex, essential for the metabolism of living cells, particularly for the normal function of the nervous system and digestive tract

Examples

Niacin vitamin
A deficiency of niacin can lead to the disease pellagra.
Doctors sometimes recommend extra niacin to lower cholesterol levels.
Many breakfast cereals are fortified with niacin to help meet daily nutritional needs.
