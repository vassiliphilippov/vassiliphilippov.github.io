snap

noun
a sudden sharp noise
3.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /snæp/ (AmE), /snæp/ (BrE)
a sudden, sharp sound or movement, often made by breaking, closing, or moving something quickly (such as fingers, lids, or jaws)

Examples

A loud snap
He listened for the snap of the mousetrap in the quiet kitchen.
The sudden snap of a branch startled the hikers.
There was a loud snap as the frozen twig broke underfoot.
At the magician's snap, the rabbit vanished from the hat.
He kept time to the music with a rhythmic snap of his fingers.
She got everyone's attention with a loud snap of her fingers.
He gave a quick snap to start the rhythm for the band.
The teacher signaled silence with a sudden snap of her fingers.
With a loud snap, she got everyone's attention during the meeting.
