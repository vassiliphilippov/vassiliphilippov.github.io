landowning

noun
landowner class
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlændˌoʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændˌəʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the group of people or the condition of owning land, especially as a source of wealth or social status

Examples

Landowning class
The landowning elite held significant power in rural communities for centuries.
Reforms aimed to reduce the influence of the landowning class in government decisions.
Conflicts often arose between landowning families and tenant farmers over property rights.
The rise of landowning families shaped the region's political and economic history.
Debates about landowning often focus on issues of inequality and access to resources.
Changes in landowning patterns affected rural communities across the country.

  • synonym: landownership - the possession of land as property
  • antonym: tenancy - the state of renting or leasing land from another
  • antonym: tenant - a person who rents land or property from a landlord
