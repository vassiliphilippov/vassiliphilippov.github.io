acetone

noun
a volatile solvent and chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˈæsəˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈæsɪtəʊn / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, flammable liquid compound (CH3COCH3) used chiefly as a solvent and in the production of plastics and other chemicals

Examples

Acetone solvent liquid
Acetone evaporates quickly when exposed to air due to its high volatility.
Laboratory staff handle acetone with care because it is highly flammable.
Nail polish remover often contains acetone as its main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solvent - a substance, typically liquid, that dissolves other substances
