stroboscope

noun
device emitting flashes to show moving objects as stationary
9.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstroÊŠbÉ™ËŒskoÊŠp / (AmE), / ËˆstrÉ’bÉ™skÉ™ÊŠp / (BrE)
a scientific instrument that produces regular flashes of light to illuminate a moving object so that it appears stationary or in slow motion

Examples

A stroboscope device
A stroboscope can make a fan's spinning blades appear completely still to the naked eye.
The mechanic used a stroboscope to check the rotation speed of the engine's crankshaft.
With the help of a stroboscope, scientists were able to observe the motion of the butterfly's wings in slow motion.

Synonyms

  • strobe
    6.3 1.38/m
    strobe makes very rapid flashes that sync with moving objects, while stroboscope is a lab instrument that shows motion as slow or still.
  • strobe light
    3.7 0.39/m
    strobe light gives bright, brief flashes often for effects in clubs or shows, while stroboscope is a scientific tool for precise motion study.
