crenel

noun
notch in a battlement
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛnəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɛnəl / (BrE)
a notch or open space between two raised sections (merlons) in a battlement, used historically for defense and observation

Examples

Castle crenel
During the tour, the guide explained how soldiers used every crenel for both attack and defense.
Each crenel provided a narrow opening for defenders to launch arrows at attackers.
The archers took aim through the crenel of the castle wall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: merlon - the solid upright section between two crenels in a battlement
