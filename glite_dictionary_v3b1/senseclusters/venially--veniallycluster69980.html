venially

adverb
minorly or excusably
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈviːniəli / (AmE), / ˈviːniəli / (BrE)
in a way that is minor, easily excused, or not seriously wrong

Examples

To be venially late
He was venially late to the meeting, so no one minded.
The student venially forgot to bring her notebook, but the teacher did not punish her.
Arriving venially after the start time, she quietly took her seat without causing a disturbance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forgivably - in a way that can be forgiven
  • antonym: gravely - in a serious or severe way
