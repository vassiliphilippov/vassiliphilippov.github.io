rise

noun
an upward slope or incline
Pronunciation: / raɪz / (AmE), / raɪz / (BrE)
an upward movement, slope, or small elevation (in land, water, or a surface)

Examples

A gentle rise
Engineers measured the rise and run to determine the angle of the ramp.
The rise of the hill made cycling much more challenging.
There is a gentle rise at the north end of the field.
After the storm, a new rise formed in the riverbed.
The boat rocked gently with each rise of the waves.
We set up camp on a slight rise that overlooked the meadow.
After the rocket's rise, the crowd erupted in applause.
The rise of the hot-air balloon was slow but graceful.
The rise of the sun over the mountains created a breathtaking view.
