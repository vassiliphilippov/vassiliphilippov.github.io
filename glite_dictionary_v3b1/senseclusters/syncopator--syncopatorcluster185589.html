syncopator

noun
device or plugin that creates syncopation
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪŋ.kə.ˈpeɪ.tər/ (AmE), /sɪŋ.kə.ˈpeɪ.tə/ (BrE)
(in music production) a hardware device or software plugin that creates or applies rhythmic syncopation to musical patterns or beats.

Examples

Digital syncopator
Many producers rely on a syncopator plugin to add groove and unexpected accents to drum patterns.
He patched the hardware syncopator into his setup to create shifting offbeat hi-hat rhythms.
The new drum machine functions as a live syncopator when sequencing complex, human-like grooves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plugin - a software add-on for digital audio workstations used to generate or process sound.
  • synonym: rhythm generator - a device or program that produces rhythmic patterns for music production.
