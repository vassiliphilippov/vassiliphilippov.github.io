dullish

adjective
slightly blunt
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʌlɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat dull or not very sharp, especially referring to a blade or cutting tool

Examples

Dullish scissors
The old scissors felt dullish and struggled to cut through the fabric.
After months of use, the chef noticed his favorite knife had become dullish.
You might want to sharpen that dullish blade before preparing dinner.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dull - not sharp; lacking a cutting edge
  • antonym: sharp - having a fine edge or point; able to cut easily
