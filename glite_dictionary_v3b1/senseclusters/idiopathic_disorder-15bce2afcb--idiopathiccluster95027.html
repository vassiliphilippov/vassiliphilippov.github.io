idiopathic disorder

noun
disease of unknown cause
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A disease or condition that arises from internal dysfunctions with no identifiable external cause.

Examples

An idiopathic disorder case
Researchers are studying potential genetic factors involved in idiopathic disorder development.
Several forms of epilepsy are considered idiopathic disorders because their origins remain unclear.
The patient was diagnosed with an idiopathic disorder after extensive testing failed to reveal a cause.

  • synonym: idiopathic - arising spontaneously or from an obscure or unknown cause
