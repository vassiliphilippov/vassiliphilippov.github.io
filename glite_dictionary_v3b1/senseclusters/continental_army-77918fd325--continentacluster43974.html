Continental Army

noun
American army in the Revolutionary War
Pronunciation: / kənˌtɪnəntəl ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / kənˌtɪnəntəl ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
the army established by the American colonies in 1775 to fight for independence from Britain during the American Revolution

Examples

The Continental Army in 1775
George Washington was appointed commander-in-chief of the Continental Army in 1775.
Many soldiers in the Continental Army endured harsh winters and shortages during the war.
The Continental Army played a crucial role in securing American independence from Britain.
