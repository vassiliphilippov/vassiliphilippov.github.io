cross-leaved heath

noun
dwarf European shrub with rose flowers
Pronunciation: / krɔs livd hiθ / (AmE), / krɒs liːvd hiːθ / (BrE)
A small European shrub (Erica tetralix) of the heather family, with rose-colored, bell-shaped flowers and leaves arranged in whorls of four.

Examples

The cross-leaved heath shrub
Botanists recorded the presence of cross-leaved heath among other native heather species.
Gardeners value cross-leaved heath for its resilience in poor, acidic soils.
The cross-leaved heath added a splash of pink to the damp moorland landscape.
