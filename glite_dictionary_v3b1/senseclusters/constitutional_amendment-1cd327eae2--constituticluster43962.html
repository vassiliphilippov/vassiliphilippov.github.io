constitutional amendment

noun
official change to a constitution
3.2 4.2
Pronunciation: /kən.stɪ.ˈtjuː.ʃən.əl ə.ˈmend.mənt/ /; / /kɑːn.stə.ˈtuː.ʃən.əl ə.ˈmend.mənt/ (AmE), /kɒn.stɪ.ˈtjuː.ʃən.əl ə.ˈmend.mənt/ /; / /kɒn.stɪ.ˈtuː.ʃən.əl ə.ˈmend.mənt/ (BrE)
an official change or addition made to a constitution, especially a written constitution of a country or organization

Examples

A constitutional amendment
A constitutional amendment requires approval from two-thirds of both houses.
Debates over the latest constitutional amendment lasted for several months in parliament.
The country passed a constitutional amendment to guarantee freedom of speech.
