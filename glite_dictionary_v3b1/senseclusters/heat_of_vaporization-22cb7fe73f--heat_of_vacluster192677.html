heat of vaporization

noun
heat needed to turn liquid into vapor
5.8 5.7
Pronunciation: /hiːt əv veɪ.pə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /hiːt əv veɪ.pə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the amount of heat required to convert a unit mass of a liquid into vapor without a change in temperature

Examples

The heat of vaporization amount
Scientists measured the heat of vaporization to study the properties of the new compound.

During boiling, the heat of vaporisation is absorbed without any temperature increase.
Scientists measured the heat of vaporisation to understand how much energy is needed for the phase change.
The heat of vaporisation of water is much higher than that of alcohol.
