present-day

adjective
of the current time, modern
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛzənt ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈprɛzənt ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
existing or occurring at the current time; belonging to the present.

Examples

Present-day styles
It is interesting to compare ancient art to present-day styles.
The present-day climate is very different from what it was a century ago.
These present-day problems require new solutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past
  • antonym: old-fashioned - typical of or belonging to a previous era; not modern
