Synonyms

  • modern-day
    3.0 5.73/m
    modern-day describes things typical of our era in everyday speech, while present-day refers more generally to the current time.
  • present
    1.0 91/m
    present means existing or happening now in a situation, while present-day emphasizes the idea of the current era or age.
  • current
    1.0 231/m
    current means happening now and can feel temporary or short-term, while present-day names the wider condition of our time.
  • contemporary
    4.2 44/m
    contemporary describes people or things from the same time and often links to art or culture, while present-day means the current era.
