impulse explosive

noun
explosive used for propulsion in weapons
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmˌpʌls ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pʌls ɪkˈspləʊ.sɪv / (BrE)
an explosive substance used to propel projectiles from guns, rockets, missiles, torpedoes, or depth charges

Examples

Traditional impulse explosives
Research continues into safer alternatives to traditional impulse explosives for military applications.
Rocket engines rely on impulse explosive to achieve rapid acceleration.
The torpedo's propulsion was enabled by an impulse explosive charge.
