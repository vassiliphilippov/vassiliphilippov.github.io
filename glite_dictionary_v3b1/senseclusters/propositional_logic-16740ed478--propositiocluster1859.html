propositional logic

noun
branch of symbolic logic about propositions
7.3
Pronunciation: / prəˌpɑːzɪʃənl ˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / prəˌpɒzɪʃənl ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
a branch of symbolic logic that studies propositions and their logical relationships, using connectives such as 'and', 'or', and 'not'
