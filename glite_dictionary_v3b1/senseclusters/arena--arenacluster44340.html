arena

noun
central area of a Roman amphitheater
3.1
Pronunciation: / əˈriːnə / (AmE), / əˈriːnə / (BrE)
In ancient Rome, the central area of an amphitheater where contests and spectacles took place, originally covered with sand.

Examples

Roman arena
Historians have reconstructed what the original arena at the Colosseum might have looked like.
In ancient Rome, gladiators fought in the arena before cheering crowds.
The sand of the arena was stained with the marks of the fierce battle.
