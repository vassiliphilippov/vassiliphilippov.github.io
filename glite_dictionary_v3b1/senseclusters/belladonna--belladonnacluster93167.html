belladonna

noun
poisonous Eurasian plant; source of atropine
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛləˈdɑnə / (AmE), / ˌbɛləˈdɒnə / (BrE)
a poisonous Eurasian plant (Atropa belladonna), also known as deadly nightshade, whose roots and leaves are used to make a drug (atropine) for medicine

Examples

Belladonna plant
Doctors once used belladonna extracts to dilate patients' pupils for eye examinations.
She carefully identified the wild plant to ensure it was not belladonna before picking the berries.
The berries of the belladonna plant are highly poisonous when ingested.
Old herbal remedies sometimes included extracts of belladonna, despite its toxicity.
Researchers are studying how belladonna can be used safely in modern medicine.
The doctor prescribed a belladonna tincture to help alleviate her muscle spasms.
