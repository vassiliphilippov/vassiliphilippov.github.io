shuffle

noun
slow, dragging walk
3.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈʃʌf.əl/ (AmE), /ˈʃʌf.əl/ (BrE)
A way of walking in which you drag your feet along the ground without lifting them fully.

Examples

A tired shuffle
After hours of standing, her tired legs could only manage a shuffle toward the door.
The old man walked down the hallway with a slow shuffle, barely lifting his feet.
You could hear the distinct shuffle of slippers on the kitchen floor early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shamble - To walk with an awkward, dragging gait
  • antonym: stride - To walk with long, decisive steps
