tumulus

noun
burial mound; earth-covered tomb
8.6 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
(archaeology) A mound of earth and stones raised over a grave or burial site, especially in prehistoric times.

Examples

A large tumulus
A large tumulus stood on the edge of the ancient village, marking the burial site of a chieftain.
Many visitors to the region are fascinated by the grassy tumuli scattered across the landscape.
The archaeologists uncovered a tumulus dating back to the Bronze Age.
