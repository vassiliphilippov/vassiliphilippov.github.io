tumulus

noun
burial mound; earth-covered tomb
9.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀêmj…ôl…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàtjuÀêmj äl…ôs / (BrE)
(archaeology) A mound of earth and stones raised over a grave or burial site, especially in prehistoric times.

Examples

A large tumulus
A large tumulus stood on the edge of the ancient village, marking the burial site of a chieftain.
Many visitors to the region are fascinated by the grassy tumuli scattered across the landscape.
The archaeologists uncovered a tumulus dating back to the Bronze Age.

Synonyms

  • burial mound
    7.1 0.21/m
    burial mound is the common name for an ancient grave heap, while tumulus is the formal archaeological term.
  • grave mound
    8.8 0.03/m
    grave mound is a simple descriptive phrase for a heap over a grave, while tumulus is the technical archaeological word.
  • barrow
    9.5 0.10/m
    barrow is a traditional British word for a burial heap, while tumulus is the formal archaeological term used worldwide.

Related Terms
