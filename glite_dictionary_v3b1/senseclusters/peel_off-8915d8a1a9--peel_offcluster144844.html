peel off

verb
remove outer layer by stripping
3.4 1.10/m
Pronunciation: / pil …îf /; / pil …ëf / (AmE), / piÀêl …íf / (BrE)
to remove the outer layer or covering from something, especially by pulling it away

Examples

To peel off a sticker
Carefully peel off the label from the jar so you can reuse it.
He used a knife to peel off the skin of the apple before eating it.
The old paint began to peel off the walls after years of neglect.
After the hike, she peeled off her muddy socks and threw them in the hamper.
He struggled to peel off the wet plaster from his arm after the cast was removed.
The children laughed as they tried to peel off sticky labels from their notebooks.
He peeled off his wet jacket and hung it by the door.
She peeled off the sticker from the back of her new phone.
The label peeled off easily, leaving the bottle clean.

Synonyms

  • peel back
    3.4 0.87/m
    peel back removes a covering slowly and carefully, while peel off often means pulling a layer away quickly or in pieces.
  • pull off
    2.7 4.00/m
    pull off removes something by pulling it away from its attachment, while peel off usually refers to removing an outer layer.
  • strip off
    3.7 1.50/m
    strip off removes a covering completely and often quickly, while peel off suggests peeling thin layers away by hand.
  • strip
    2.7 1.96/m
    strip removes a covering or contents to leave something bare, while peel off focuses on pulling a thin outer layer away.
