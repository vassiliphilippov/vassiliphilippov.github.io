reauthorize

verb
to permit again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɔːθəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɔːθəraɪz / (BrE)
to give official permission or approval for something again

Examples

To reauthorize a program
The government decided to reauthorize the emergency relief program for another year.
After much debate, the committee voted to reauthorize the research grant.

The committee must reauthorize the project before any new funds are released.
If your access expires, you will need to reauthorize your account to continue using the service.
Congress voted to reauthorize the education program for another five years.
You must reauthorize your account to continue using the service.
