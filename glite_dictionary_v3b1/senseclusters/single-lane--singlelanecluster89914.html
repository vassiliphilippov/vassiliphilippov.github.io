single-lane

adjective
having only one lane for both traffic directions
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.gəl leɪn/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.gəl leɪn/ (BrE)
(of a road) consisting of only one lane for vehicles traveling in both directions, so that vehicles may need to pull over to let others pass.

Examples

A single-lane road
Drivers must be cautious when meeting on a single-lane highway.
The village is reached by a single-lane road winding through the countryside.
Traffic can be slow on single-lane bridges during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dual carriageway - a road with separated lanes for traffic in each direction
  • degree higher: two-lane - having two lanes, usually one for each direction
