saltate

verb
move by leaping or jumping in succession
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsæl.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæl.teɪt / (BrE)
(of particles, especially sand or biological organisms) to move by a series of leaps or jumps, particularly as a result of wind, water, or muscular action.

Examples

Particles saltate in wind
During dry spells, fine dust particles may saltate before settling back on the ground.
Some insects saltate as a primary mode of movement through tall grasses.
The sand grains saltate across the desert, carried by strong gusts of wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hop - to move by jumping on one foot
  • synonym: leap - to jump or spring a long way
