parable

noun
a story illustrating a moral or spiritual lesson
Pronunciation: / ˈpær.ə.bəl /; / ˈpærəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.bl̩ /; / ˈpærəbəl / (BrE)
a simple story that teaches a moral or spiritual lesson

Examples

A parable story
In his sermon, the priest explained the deeper meaning behind a well-known parable.
Jesus often taught his followers in parables to help them understand his message.
The parable of the Good Samaritan is one of the most famous biblical stories.
Aesop's parable involving the tortoise and the hare is known by almost everyone.
Many religious texts include a parable to teach fundamental moral principles.
The pastor used a parable to explain the importance of forgiveness to the congregation.
