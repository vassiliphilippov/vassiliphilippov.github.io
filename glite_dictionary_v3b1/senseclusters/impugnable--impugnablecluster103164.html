impugnable

adjective
able to be challenged or discredited
7.7 7.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpjuː.nə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpjuːn.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpjuː.nə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpjuːn.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
Open to doubt, challenge, or criticism; capable of being called into question.

Examples

An impugnable claim
His explanation of the missing funds was highly impugnable and did not satisfy the auditors.
Scientific results should be repeatable and therefore impugnable when new evidence arises.
The witness's credibility proved impugnable under cross-examination.

  • antonym: unimpeachable - impossible to discredit or challenge
