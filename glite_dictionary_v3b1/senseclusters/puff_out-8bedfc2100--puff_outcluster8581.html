puff out

verb
to swell or expand
3.5 0.56/m
Pronunciation: / pÊŒf aÊŠt / (AmE), / pÊŒf aÊŠt / (BrE)
to swell or expand or to cause something to swell or expand, especially by filling with air or pride

Examples

To puff out the chest
He took a deep breath and tried to puff out his chest to appear more confident.
The pastries puffed out nicely in the oven, making them look delicious and airy.
When the wind blew harder, the sail began to puff out and catch more air.

Synonyms

    puff up makes something swell by air, liquid, or feeling, while puff out often implies a quick, visible swelling or proud posture.
  • puff
    4.1 2.05/m
    puff makes something swell or grow with a short breath or action, while puff out usually stresses full outward swelling.
  • inflate
    2.9 3.08/m
    inflate means fill something with air or gas until it grows round and firm, while puff out can mean swell more gently.
  • blow up
    2.7 0.58/m
    blow up fills a thing like a tyre or balloon with air until large, while puff out can be gentler or figurative.
  • balloon
    4.7 1.67/m
    balloon means grow quickly in size or volume, often suddenly, while puff out focuses on gentle swelling or pride-filled shape.
  • swell
    3.2 7.61/m
    swell means become larger from many causes like injury or water, while puff out often implies air or a proud display.
  • bloat
    4.9 1.13/m
    bloat means become uncomfortably swollen, often from gas or food, while puff out is milder and can be used for pride.
  • distend
    8.2 0.84/m

  • bulge
    2.9 0.92/m
