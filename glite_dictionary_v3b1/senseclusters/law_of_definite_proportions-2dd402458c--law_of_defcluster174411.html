law of definite proportions

noun
chemical law on composition of substances
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ˈdɛfɪnət prəˈpɔrʃənz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈdɛfɪnət prəˈpɔːʃənz / (BrE)
A law in chemistry stating that every pure substance always contains the same elements combined in the same proportions by weight.

Examples

The law of definite proportions
Dalton used the law of definite proportions as a foundation for his atomic theory.
The law of definite proportions states that a chemical compound always contains exactly the same proportion of elements by mass.
Without the law of definite proportions, chemists could not accurately formulate chemical equations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: law of constant composition - another name for the law of definite proportions
  • degree higher: law of multiple proportions - related chemical law about ratios in compounds of same elements
