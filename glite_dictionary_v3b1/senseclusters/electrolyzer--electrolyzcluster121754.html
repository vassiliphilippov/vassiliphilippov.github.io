electrolyzer

noun
device for electrolysis
7.6
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trə.laɪ.zər/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trə.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trə.laɪ.zə/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trə.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a device that uses electricity to cause a chemical change, usually to split substances into their elements through electrolysis

Examples

An electrolyzer device
The factory installed a new electrolyzer to produce chlorine for water treatment.
A modern electrolyzer can separate valuable metals from their ores using electricity.
Engineers tested the electrolyzer to ensure it could efficiently process large volumes of saltwater.
The electrolyzer produced hydrogen for the fuel cell.
A modern electrolyzer can efficiently generate clean hydrogen using renewable energy sources.
Engineers installed a new electrolyzer to support the plant's green energy initiative.

  • antonym: fuel cell - a device that generates electricity from chemical reactions, often using hydrogen
