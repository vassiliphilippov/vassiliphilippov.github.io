periodize

verb
to divide into periods
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪr.ɪ.ə.daɪz/ (AmE), /ˈpɪə.rɪ.ə.daɪz/ (BrE)
to divide something, especially history or a process, into distinct periods or phases

Examples

To periodize history
Historians often periodize ancient civilizations to better understand their development.
The coach decided to periodize the training schedule to maximize athletes' performance.
It is common to periodize art history into movements such as Renaissance and Baroque.
