decent

adjective
appropriate or proper for a context
1.6 10/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdiÀês…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀês…ônt / (BrE)
socially or morally acceptable; proper or suitable for a situation

Examples

A decent person
It is only right to give her a decent burial surrounded by her loved ones.
The host ensured that every guest received a decent welcome upon arrival.
Wearing formal clothing was considered decent for the solemn ceremony.
Despite his mistakes, people still consider him a decent man at heart.
It's important to treat others in a decent and respectful manner.
She comes from a decent family and was raised with strong values.

Synonyms

  • proper
    2.2 42/m
    proper follows set social rules and sounds more formal for situations, while decent is a general, morally acceptable choice.
  • respectable
    3.0 7.72/m
    respectable is seen by society as worthy of respect and status, while decent means simply morally or socially acceptable.
  • fitting
    2.6 13/m
    fitting suits a particular person, place, or purpose, while decent refers to being morally or socially acceptable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fitting - suitable or appropriate; right for a particular purpose or occasion
  • antonym: improper - not suitable or appropriate for the circumstances
  • synonym: respectable - regarded by society to be good, proper, or correct
