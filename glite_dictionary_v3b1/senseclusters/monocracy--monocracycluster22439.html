monocracy

noun
absolute rule by one person
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / m…ôÀàn…ëÀêkr…ôsi / (AmE), / m…ôÀàn…íkr…ôsi / (BrE)
A form of government in which a single person holds absolute power, unrestricted by laws, constitution, or opposition.

Examples

A monocracy government
Debates raged over whether monocracy could ever ensure fair governance for its citizens.
The country descended into monocracy when the general seized power and dissolved the parliament.
Throughout history, monocracy has often led to abuse of authority and lack of political freedoms.

Synonyms

  • one-man rule
    8.2 0.04/m
    one-man rule is an everyday phrase about a single absolute ruler acting without checks, while monocracy is the formal name for that system.
  • autocracy
    8.2 0.96/m
    autocracy is a political system where one person holds unlimited authority, while monocracy names absolute personal rule beyond laws and opposition.
  • dictatorship
    4.8 10/m
    dictatorship is rule by a single leader or small group using absolute power, while monocracy means rule by one person only.
  • absolutism
    8.1 0.61/m
    absolutism is a historical idea of a ruler's complete power, while monocracy is the specific term for one person holding absolute rule.

Related Terms
