Santee Dakota

noun
eastern branch of the Sioux people
Pronunciation: / ˈsæn.ti dəˈkoʊ.tə / (AmE), / ˈsæn.ti dəˈkəʊ.tə / (BrE)
a member of the eastern branch of the Sioux people, traditionally living in the upper Midwest of the United States

Examples

The Santee Dakota people
Many Santee Dakota were relocated to reservations in the mid-19th century.
She is studying the language of the Santee Dakota as part of her research on indigenous cultures.
The Santee Dakota have a rich tradition of storytelling and ceremonies.
