Prunus salicina

noun
Japanese plum tree species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpruːnəs səˈlaɪkɪnə / (AmE), / ˌpruːnəs səˈlɪkɪnə / (BrE)
a small tree native to China and Japan that bears large yellow to red plums, typically considered less flavorful than European plums; also called the Japanese plum

Examples

Prunus salicina tree
Bottled juice made from Prunus salicina is popular in parts of East Asia.
Some gardeners prefer Prunus salicina for its resilience to disease despite its less intense flavor.
The orchard included several Prunus salicina trees, which produced abundant plums each summer.
