noble gas

noun
a chemically inert gaseous element (e.g. helium, neon)
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊbəl ˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl ˌɡas / (BrE)
any of the chemically inert gaseous elements in group 18 of the periodic table, such as helium, neon, and argon

Examples

A noble gas element
Helium is a noble gas commonly used in balloons and scientific applications.
Neon, another noble gas, gives off a bright glow when electrified in signs.
The chemical properties of each noble gas are characterized by their lack of reactivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inert gas - a gas that does not readily react with other substances
