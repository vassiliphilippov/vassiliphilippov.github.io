line

noun
conformity with rules or standards
1.0
Pronunciation: / laɪn / (AmE), / laɪn / (BrE)
A state or condition of conformity to rules, standards, or expected behavior.

Examples

Step out of line
Anyone who steps out of line will be disciplined according to the school's regulations.
Her views are not in line with the company policy, so she was asked to reconsider.
The children were praised for staying in line during the field trip.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disorder - lack of order or conformity
  • synonym: order - a state in which everything is in its correct place or follows rules
  • synonym: rule - a principle or regulation governing conduct
