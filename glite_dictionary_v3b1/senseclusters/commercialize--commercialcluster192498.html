commercialize

verb
make commercial; turn into a business
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɝːʃəˌlaɪz /; / kəˈmɝː.ʃə.l̩.aɪz /; / kəˈmɝːʃəlaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz /; / kəˈmɜː.ʃəl.aɪz / (BrE)
to make something available for sale or operated mainly to make money, often losing its original character or values

Examples

To commercialize new products
Many companies aim to commercialize new medical technologies as quickly as possible.
The festival organizers plan to commercialize the event by selling merchandise and sponsorships.
There is a debate about whether art loses value when it is commercialized.
Several companies are trying to commercialize new sustainable energy technologies.
Some critics argue that reality television has commercialized entertainment at the expense of creativity.
The festival has become too commercialized in recent years, losing its original spirit.
Some artists refuse to commercialise their work, preferring creative freedom over profit.
The company plans to commercialise its latest medical technology next year.
The once-traditional village has started to commercialise its cultural festivals to attract tourists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercialize - to make something commercial; American English spelling
  • synonym: monetize - to convert into or express in the form of money
  • synonym: privatize - to transfer from public or government control to private ownership
