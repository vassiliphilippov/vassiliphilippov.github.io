maroon

verb
leave someone stranded or isolated
Pronunciation: / məˈruːn /; / məˈrun / (AmE), / məˈruːn / (BrE)
to leave someone in a place from which they cannot escape, especially on an island

Examples

To maroon someone
During the mutiny, the captain was marooned on a remote rock with only a small supply of food.
He was accidentally marooned in the small town when his car broke down.
The sailors feared they would be marooned on the deserted island after the storm.
After the shipwreck, the survivors were marooned for several months.
Pirates would sometimes maroon traitors as punishment, leaving them to fend for themselves.
The mutineers planned to maroon the captain on a deserted island.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something behind, often in a difficult situation
  • synonym: strand - to leave someone or something in a helpless or difficult position
