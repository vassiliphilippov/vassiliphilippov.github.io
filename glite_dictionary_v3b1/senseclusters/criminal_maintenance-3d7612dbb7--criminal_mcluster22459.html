criminal maintenance

noun
illegal support/interference in a lawsuit
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪmɪnəl ˌmeɪntəˈnɛns / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəl ˌmeɪntɪˈnəns / (BrE)
(law) the act of improperly supporting or interfering in a lawsuit by someone who has no direct interest in it, with the intent to obstruct justice or stir up unnecessary litigation.

Examples

Criminal maintenance in law
His involvement in the lawsuit was considered criminal maintenance under local statutes.
Legal reforms were introduced to reduce cases of criminal maintenance in civil litigation.
The court ruled that criminal maintenance had occurred, making the proceedings void.

Related Terms

  • synonym: champerty - an agreement where a person with no interest supports a lawsuit for a share of the proceeds
