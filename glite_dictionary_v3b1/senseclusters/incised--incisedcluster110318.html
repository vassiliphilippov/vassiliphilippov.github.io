incised

adjective
sharply and deeply indented
5.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an edge or surface) having sharply and deeply cut lines or indentations.

Examples

Incised valley walls
A deeply incised valley cut through the mountains, revealing layers of rock.
The ancient vase had incised patterns along its rim.
The artist carefully incised lines into the metal plate for the print.

Related Terms

  • antonym: embossed - having a raised or relief design
  • synonym: engraved - decorated by carving or cutting into a surface
