reroute

verb
to change route
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈraʊt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈruːt / (BrE)
to change the route, direction, or path of something so that it goes a different way than originally planned

Examples

To reroute traffic
Because of the accident, the police had to reroute traffic through a side street.
The airline decided to reroute the flight due to bad weather conditions.
Construction work on the bridge means they will reroute buses for the next two weeks.
Due to road construction, the city had to reroute all traffic through a different neighborhood.
The package was rerouted to the correct address after the delivery error was discovered.
When the river flooded, engineers worked quickly to reroute the water away from homes.
The airline had to reroute several flights because of the storm.
After the accident, police officers worked quickly to reroute traffic around the blocked intersection.
We will reroute the delivery to your new address as requested.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divert - to cause something to change direction
  • synonym: redirect - to send something to a different place or along a different path
