rapid transit

noun

Pronunciation: /ˈræ.pɪd ˈtræn.zɪt/ /; / /ˈræ.pəd ˈtræn.zɪt/ /; / /ˈræ.pɪd ˈtræn.sɪt/ (AmE), /ˈræ.pɪd ˈtræn.zɪt/ /; / /ˈræ.pɪd ˈtræn.sɪt/ /; / /ˈræ.pɪd ˈtrɑːn.zɪt/ (BrE)
A system of public transportation in a city, typically using underground or elevated trains, designed for fast, frequent service.

Examples

City rapid transit

Many major cities rely on rapid transit systems to reduce traffic congestion and pollution.
The new rapid transit line will connect the suburbs to the downtown area.
