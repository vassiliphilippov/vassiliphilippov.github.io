Campylobacter

proper noun
bacteria causing food poisoning
9.2
Pronunciation: /kæm.pɪ.lə.ˈbæk.tər/ /; / /kæm.paɪ.lə.ˈbæk.tər/ (AmE), /kæm.pɪ.lə.ˈbæk.tə/ /; / /kæm.paɪ.lə.ˈbæk.tə/ (BrE)
a genus of bacteria that can cause food poisoning and gastrointestinal illness in humans

Examples

Campylobacter genus
Eating undercooked poultry is a common way to get infected with Campylobacter.
Doctors often test for Campylobacter when patients have severe stomach cramps and diarrhea.
Proper food handling can help prevent the spread of Campylobacter in the kitchen.
