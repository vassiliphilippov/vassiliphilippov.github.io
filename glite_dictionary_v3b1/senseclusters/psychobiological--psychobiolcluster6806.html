psychobiological

adjective
mind-body interaction
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the interaction between psychological and biological processes

Examples

Psychobiological effects
The study explored the psychobiological effects of chronic stress on the immune system.
Her research focuses on psychobiological responses to emotional trauma in children.
Doctors are interested in the psychobiological mechanisms underlying anxiety disorders.
Researchers are interested in the psychobiological mechanisms that link stress to physical illness.
The therapy program takes a psychobiological approach to treating anxiety disorders.
Her work explores the psychobiological development of children exposed to trauma.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: biological - relating to living organisms and life processes
  • synonym: biopsychological - relating to the biological basis of psychological processes
  • degree lower: psychological - relating to the mind or mental processes
