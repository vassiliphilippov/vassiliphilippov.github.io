chinchilla

noun
small Andean rodent with soft fur
Pronunciation: / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (BrE)
a small South American rodent with soft, dense pearly-grey fur, native to the Andes and often bred in captivity for its fur.

Examples

A pet chinchilla
Fur coats made from chinchilla pelts are prized for their exceptional softness.
Many people choose a chinchilla as a pet because of its soft, luxurious fur.
The chinchilla is native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guinea pig - a small domesticated rodent often kept as a pet
  • degree lower: rabbit - a small burrowing mammal with long ears, sometimes bred for fur
