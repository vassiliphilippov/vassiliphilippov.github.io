lockstep

noun
rigid conformity or procedure
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑkˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌstɛp / (BrE)
a way of doing something in which people act or think in exactly the same way, often without questioning it

Examples

In lockstep thinking
Critics say that the board acted in lockstep, ignoring alternative proposals.
Many companies innovate instead of working in lockstep with industry traditions.
The two political parties often move in lockstep when passing budget bills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conformity - compliance with standards, rules, or laws
  • antonym: independence - freedom from outside control; self-governance
  • synonym: uniformity - the quality of being the same in all cases
