Lonicera hirsuta

noun
species of hairy honeysuckle
9.9
Pronunciation: / loʊˈnɪsərə hɪrˈsuːtə / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə hɪəˈsuːtə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous shrub, called hairy honeysuckle, with twining stems, hairy leaves, and spikes of yellow-orange flowers, native to northeastern America.

Examples

Lonicera hirsuta shrub
Botanists surveyed a forest in Michigan and found several thriving Lonicera hirsuta shrubs.
The Lonicera hirsuta in my garden has just started blooming with bright yellow-orange flowers.
Unlike other honeysuckles, Lonicera hirsuta is distinguished by its notably hairy leaves.
