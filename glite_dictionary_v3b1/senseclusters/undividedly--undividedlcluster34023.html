undividedly

adverb
jointly, without division
6.8
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dɪ.ˈvaɪ.dɪd.liː/ /; / /ən.dɪ.ˈvaɪ.dɪd.liː/ (AmE), /ʌn.dɪ.ˈvaɪ.dɪd.liː/ /; / /ən.dɪ.ˈvaɪ.dɪd.liː/ (BrE)
in a manner that is joint, collective, and without physical division, especially regarding ownership or rights.

Examples

Undividedly owned land
The land was undividedly owned by all the heirs after their father's death.
They agreed to manage the property undividedly until a final decision was made.
The siblings inherited the estate undividedly, sharing all rights and responsibilities equally.

  • antonym: separately - individually, not together
