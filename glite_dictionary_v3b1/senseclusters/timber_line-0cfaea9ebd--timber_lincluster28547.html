timber line

noun
upper limit of tree growth
7.7 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪmbɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə laɪn / (BrE)
the altitude or latitude on a mountain or in a region beyond which trees are unable to grow, usually due to low temperatures or short growing seasons

Examples

Above the timber line
Above the timber line, only grasses and low shrubs can survive the harsh conditions.
As they climbed higher, the landscape changed dramatically at the timber line.
The hikers stopped to rest near the timber line, where the last stunted trees grew.
