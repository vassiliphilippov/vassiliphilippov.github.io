indecorous

adjective
improper; lacking good taste or manners
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛkərəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛkərəs / (BrE)
improper or not showing good manners; not in keeping with accepted standards of behavior or taste

Examples

An indecorous remark
His indecorous remarks shocked many people at the formal dinner.
Laughing loudly during the ceremony was considered indecorous by the elders.
The teacher reprimanded the students for their indecorous conduct in the hallway.
His indecorous remarks shocked everyone at the formal dinner.
It was considered indecorous to interrupt the speaker during the ceremony.
The committee disapproved of the indecorous conduct displayed by some attendees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decorous - in keeping with good taste and propriety; polite and restrained
  • synonym: improper - not conforming to accepted standards of behavior or morals
  • synonym: unseemly - not proper or appropriate; unbecoming
