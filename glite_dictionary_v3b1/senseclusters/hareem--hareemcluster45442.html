hareem

noun
women's quarters in a Muslim household
6.6
Pronunciation: / həˈriːm / (AmE), / həˈriːm / (BrE)
The part of a Muslim household reserved for wives, concubines, and female relatives, traditionally secluded from men.

Examples

The hareem quarters
The old palace still preserves the private hareem where royal women once lived.
The sultan spent most of his evenings in the hareem, surrounded by his family.
Women in the hareem were often shielded from contact with visitors and outsiders.
