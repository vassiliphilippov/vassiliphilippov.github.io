beetleweed

noun
a perennial herb with glossy, heart-shaped leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːtəlˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈbiːtəlˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tufted evergreen perennial herb with spikes of tiny white flowers and glossy, round to heart-shaped leaves, which turn coppery, maroon, or purplish in autumn.

Examples

The beetleweed plant
Gardeners appreciate beetleweed for its evergreen foliage and small white flowers.
In fall, the leaves of beetleweed develop a striking purplish tint.
The shady corner of our garden is perfect for growing beetleweed.
