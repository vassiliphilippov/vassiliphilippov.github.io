interdictor

noun
one who imposes a ban
9.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈdɪk.tər/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈdɪk.tə/ (BrE)
a person or authority who formally imposes a prohibition or ban, especially by legal or religious order

Examples

Legal interdictor
The court appointed an interdictor to oversee the enforcement of the new regulations.
As the official interdictor, she had the authority to ban certain activities in the region.
The role of interdictor was crucial during times of religious conflict.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permitter - a person who allows or authorizes something
  • synonym: prohibitor - a person who forbids or prevents something
