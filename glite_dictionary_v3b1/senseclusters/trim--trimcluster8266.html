trim

verb
reduce the amount or size of something
5.6 2.2
Pronunciation: /trɪm/ (AmE), /trɪm/ (BrE)
to cut, remove, or reduce parts of something to make it neater, smaller, or more efficient (such as hair, plants, objects, or spending)

Examples

To trim hair
In tough economic times, governments often trim spending on public services.
The company decided to trim its budget by cutting unnecessary expenses.
You should trim your daily sugar intake for better health.
Before framing the picture, she carefully trimmed the excess paper from the edges.
He trimmed the hedge so it was neat and even.
The chef trimmed the fat off the steak before cooking it.
Gardeners often trim back shrubs to promote new growth.
It is important to trim your roses in early spring for healthier blooms.
She carefully trimmed the hedges to keep them neat and tidy.
