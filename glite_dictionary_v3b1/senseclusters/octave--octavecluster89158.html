octave

noun
musical interval of eight notes
4.8 1.90/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆÉ‘k.tÉªv / (AmE), / ËˆÉ’k.tÉªv / (BrE)
a musical interval spanning eight notes, in which the first and eighth note have the same name and the upper note has twice the frequency of the lower

Examples

High octave note
Moving up one octave doubles the frequency of the note.
The pianist played the melody an octave higher for a brighter sound.
When the choir reaches the chorus, the sopranos often sing in octave with the tenors.

Synonyms

  • musical octave
    3.6 0.00/m
    musical octave names the interval where the higher note vibrates at twice the frequency, while octave is the common shorter musical term.
  • ottava
    9.5 0.00/m
    ottava is an Italian score marking that tells players to play a passage an octave higher or lower, while octave names the interval itself.
