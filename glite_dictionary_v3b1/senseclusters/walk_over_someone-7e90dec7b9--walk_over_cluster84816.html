walk over (someone)

verb
treat with disrespect or take advantage of
1.0
Pronunciation: / wɑk ˈoʊvɚ ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / wɔːk ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to treat someone with disrespect or without consideration, often by taking advantage of them

Examples

To walk over a friend
He realized he was letting his friends walk over him and decided to be more assertive.
If you always agree to everything, people might start to walk over you.
You can't just let her walk over you at work; you deserve respect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stand up to - to resist or not allow oneself to be mistreated
  • synonym: take advantage of - to exploit someone for personal gain, often unfairly
