waterline

noun
line showing where water meets ship's hull
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the level at which the surface of the water meets the hull of a ship when it is afloat, often marked by a painted line

Examples

The waterline on a ship
Barnacles often gather just below the waterline on boats.
Inspectors checked to make sure the cargo stayed below the waterline.
The red waterline was freshly painted on the side of the cargo ship.
