silliness

noun
foolish or absurd behavior
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪli.nəs /; / ˈsɪlinəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪnɪs /; / ˈsɪlinəs / (BrE)
the quality or behavior of being silly, foolish, or not serious

Examples

Childish silliness
His silliness during the meeting made everyone laugh despite the serious topic.
Sometimes a little silliness is all it takes to brighten a dull day.
The teacher appreciated the children's silliness as it showed their creativity.
His constant silliness made it hard for the group to focus on their work.
She was known for her spontaneous silliness that always made her friends laugh.
There was a lot of silliness at the party, with people playing games and telling jokes.

  • antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious or earnest
