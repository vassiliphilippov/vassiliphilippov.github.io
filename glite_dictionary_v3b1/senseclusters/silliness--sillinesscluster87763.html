Synonyms

  • foolery
    6.1 0.08/m
    foolery means playful or silly acts that people do for fun, while silliness also covers general foolish or not serious behavior.
  • frivolity
    6.7 0.65/m
    frivolity describes light, playful behavior that ignores serious matters, while silliness can include both play and simple foolishness.
  • unseriousness
    7.0 0.06/m
    unseriousness is the state of not taking things seriously, while silliness often adds a foolish or playful action to that mood.
  • inanity
    9.5 0.48/m
    inanity refers to emptiness or extreme lack of sense or meaning, while silliness often means light foolishness or playful acting.
  • fatuousness
    9.5 0.02/m
    fatuousness is dull, unintelligent foolishness often seen as offensive, while silliness is milder, silly or not-serious behavior.
