Musa paradisiaca

noun
a species of banana plant
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːsə pəˌrædɪˈsaɪəkə / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsə pəˌrædɪˈsaɪəkə / (BrE)
A species of banana plant found in tropical and subtropical regions, known for bearing clusters of edible, starchy, greenish fruits.

Examples

Musa paradisiaca plant
In tropical gardens, Musa paradisiaca is often cultivated for its starchy fruits.
Scientists studied the genetic diversity of Musa paradisiaca in different regions.
The fruits of Musa paradisiaca can be cooked and eaten much like plantains.
