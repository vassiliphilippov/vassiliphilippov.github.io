baby buster

noun
member of post-baby boom generation
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbʌs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbʌs.tə / (BrE)
a person born during the generation that follows the baby boom, when birth rates declined sharply

Examples

A baby buster generation
Many baby busters entered the workforce during a period of economic uncertainty.
The term baby buster refers to people born after the postwar baby boom ended.
Unlike their baby boomer parents, baby busters often had smaller families.

Related Terms

  • antonym: baby boomer - a person born during the post-World War II baby boom (roughly 1946–1964)
