March 25

proper noun
Annunciation festival; traditional quarter day (UK, Ireland)
5.1
Pronunciation: /mɑːrtʃ ˈtwen.tɪ fɪfθ/ /; / /mɑːrtʃ ˈtwen.tɪ faɪv/ (AmE), /mɑːtʃ ˈtwen.tɪ fɪfθ/ /; / /mɑːtʃ ˈtwen.tɪ faɪv/ (BrE)
A Christian festival commemorating the announcement by the angel Gabriel to the Virgin Mary of the Incarnation, also known as the Annunciation; a traditional quarter day for rent and legal payments in England, Wales, and Ireland.

Examples

March 25 festival
Historically, rents and contracts in England were often due on March 25, known as Lady Day.
Many Christians attend special church services on March 25 to celebrate the Annunciation.
The school holiday coincided with March 25, marking an important date in the religious calendar.
