testing ground

noun
place or situation for experimentation
1.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛstɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a place or situation where new ideas, methods, or products are tried out and evaluated before wider use

Examples

A testing ground for ideas
Her classroom was a testing ground for different teaching techniques.
The city became a testing ground for new transportation technology.
The policy acted as a testing ground for larger reforms across the country.
Her classroom became an informal testing ground for creative teaching techniques.
The company used the small market as a testing ground before launching the product nationwide.
The new curriculum will serve as a testing ground for educational reforms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laboratory - a place equipped for scientific experiments, research, or teaching
  • synonym: proving ground - a place where something is tested, often for new technology or methods
