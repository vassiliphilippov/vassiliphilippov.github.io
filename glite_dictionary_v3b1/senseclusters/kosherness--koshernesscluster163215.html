kosherness

noun
quality of being kosher
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkoʊ.ʃər.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈkəʊ.ʃə.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality or state of being kosher; the property of conforming to Jewish dietary laws.

Examples

The kosherness of food
Many families consult religious authorities to confirm the kosherness of their Passover ingredients.
Questions about the kosherness of imported food have arisen within the community.
The rabbi examined the kitchen to determine the kosherness of the utensils.
