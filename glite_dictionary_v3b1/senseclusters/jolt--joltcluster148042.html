jolt

verb
to disturb or startle someone
5.7
Pronunciation: / dʒoʊlt / (AmE), / dʒəʊlt / (BrE)
to startle or disturb the composure of someone, often suddenly or unexpectedly

Examples

To jolt someone awake
Her sharp question jolted him out of his daydream.
The dramatic news jolted everyone in the office.
The unexpected criticism jolted the team into reconsidering their strategy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone relaxed or less disturbed
  • synonym: startle - to cause someone to feel sudden shock or surprise
  • synonym: unsettle - to make someone feel anxious or uncomfortable
