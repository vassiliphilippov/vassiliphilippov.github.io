floor

noun
right to speak in an assembly
Pronunciation: / flɔr / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
the area or right to speak in a formal assembly or legislative chamber

Examples

Take the floor
After a heated debate, the senator was finally given the floor to present her argument.
Members must request the floor before they can make a motion in the assembly.
The chairman denied him the floor due to time constraints.
After hours of discussion on the floor, the bill was finally passed.
She was recognized from the floor to present her amendment to the proposal.
The senator took the floor to deliver an impassioned speech on education reform.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chamber - a room used for legislative or judicial business
  • synonym: podium - a raised platform for speakers or performers
