muscular contraction

noun
shortening or tensing of a muscle
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskjələr kənˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊlə kənˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a muscle or muscle fiber shortens or tenses, often resulting in movement or increased tension in a body part

Examples

A muscular contraction in calf
A sudden muscular contraction in his calf caused him to stumble during the race.
Cramps are often the result of involuntary muscular contractions in the body.
During exercise, repeated muscular contractions help build strength and endurance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: muscle relaxation - the process by which a muscle returns to its resting state after contraction
