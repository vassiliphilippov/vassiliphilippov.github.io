failover

noun
automatic backup switch
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfeɪl.oʊ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈfeɪl.əʊ.və/ (BrE)
the process or event of automatically switching to a backup system when the main system fails

Examples

Automatic failover system
The IT team set up failover to keep the servers running during maintenance.
A reliable failover system is essential for online banking services.
Without failover, a single hardware problem could bring down the entire network.
The server's failover system activated immediately when the main hardware stopped working.
To ensure continuous service, the company invested in a reliable failover solution.
During the network outage, failover to the secondary site kept the website online.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backup - a copy or system kept in case the main one fails
  • antonym: failure - the state of not working or stopping to function
  • synonym: recovery - the process of restoring a system after a failure
  • synonym: redundancy - the use of extra systems or components for backup
