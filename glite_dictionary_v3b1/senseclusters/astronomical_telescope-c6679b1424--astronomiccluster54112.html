astronomical telescope

noun
telescope for observing celestial objects
Pronunciation: / əˌstrɑːnəmɪkəl təˈlɛskoʊp / (AmE), / əˌstrɒnəmɪkəl tɪˈlɛskəʊp / (BrE)
a telescope designed to collect and record electromagnetic radiation from celestial objects and sources in space.

Examples

Modern astronomical telescope
Most modern astronomical telescopes are equipped with advanced digital cameras for imaging.
My grandfather showed me how to use his old astronomical telescope to look at the Moon.
The astronomical telescope allowed scientists to observe distant galaxies in unprecedented detail.
