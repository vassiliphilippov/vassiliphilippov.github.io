emanation

noun
something that is emitted or radiated
Pronunciation: / ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
something (such as a gas, light, or feeling) that comes out from a source; the act or process of sending out something from a source

Examples

A emanation of light
A faint emanation of perfume filled the air as she entered the room.
Scientists measured the radioactive emanation coming from the rocks.
The emanation of light from the lamp brightened the dark hallway.
His calmness had an emanation that soothed everyone around him.
The emanation of heat from the engine was felt throughout the room.
They studied the emanation of radioactive particles from the substance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up something
  • synonym: emission - the act of sending out something such as gas or radiation
