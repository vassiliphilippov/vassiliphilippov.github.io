Shih

noun
Wade-Giles romanization
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃɪ / (AmE), / ʃɪ / (BrE)
the Wade-Giles romanization 'Shih', used in older English texts to represent the Mandarin syllable 'shi' in Chinese words and names

Examples

Shih romanization
In many older dictionaries, the character is transliterated as Shih instead of the modern 'shi'.
The name Shih Huang Ti appears in historical texts using the Wade-Giles system.
Researchers often encounter Shih in English translations of Chinese classics published before 1979.
