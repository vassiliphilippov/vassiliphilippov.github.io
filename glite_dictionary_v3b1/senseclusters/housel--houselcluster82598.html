housel

noun
archaic household
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊzəl / (AmE), / ˈhaʊzəl / (BrE)
an archaic term for the people who live together in a house; a household.

Examples

A nobleman's housel
In medieval times, a nobleman's housel often included many servants and workers.
The entire housel gathered in the great hall for the evening meal.
Records from the old village list each housel and its members in detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: family - a group of people related by blood or marriage living together
  • synonym: household - the people living together in a single home
