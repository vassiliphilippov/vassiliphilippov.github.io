sweptwing

adjective
aircraft with sweptback wings
9.4 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈswɛptˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɛptˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) having wings angled backward from their root toward the tips, improving high-speed performance.

Examples

Sweptwing aircraft design
Many jet fighters are equipped with sweptwing structures to reduce air resistance at high velocities.
The distinctive look of the sweptwing plane made it stand out on the runway.
The engineers decided to use a sweptwing design to enhance the aircraft's speed and stability.
