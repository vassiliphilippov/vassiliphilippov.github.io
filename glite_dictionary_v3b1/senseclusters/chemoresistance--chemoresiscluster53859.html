chemoresistance

noun
resistance to chemotherapy
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊrɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊrɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the ability of cancer cells or other disease-causing cells to resist the effects of chemotherapy drugs

Examples

Cancer chemoresistance
Scientists are developing new drugs to overcome chemoresistance in aggressive cancers.
The patient's tumor showed significant chemoresistance, making treatment more challenging.
Understanding the mechanisms of chemoresistance can help improve cancer therapy outcomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chemosensitivity - the susceptibility of cells to the effects of chemotherapy drugs
  • synonym: drug resistance - the ability of disease-causing organisms or cells to withstand drug treatment
