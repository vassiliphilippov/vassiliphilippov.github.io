sellout

noun
betrayal of principles for gain
7.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈse.laʊt/ (AmE), /ˈse.laʊt/ (BrE)
an act of betraying one's principles, country, or cause for personal gain or advantage.

Examples

A political sellout
His decision to support the new policy was seen as a political sellout.
Many fans accused the band of being a sellout after they changed their musical style.
She promised never to be a sellout and always to stand by her beliefs.
