antinomian

adjective
rejecting established religious or moral laws
9.9 9.3
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈnoʊ.miː.ən/ /; / /æn.taɪ.ˈnoʊ.miː.ən/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈnəʊ.miː.ən/ /; / /æn.taɪ.ˈnəʊ.miː.ən/ (BrE)
relating to or characterized by the rejection of established laws, especially in the context of religious or moral laws.

Examples

Antinomian views
Her antinomian beliefs led her to reject the authority of church doctrine.
Some radical groups in the 17th century were considered antinomian because they challenged traditional religious laws.
The preacher warned against antinomian interpretations of scripture that encouraged lawlessness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legalist - one who adheres strictly to law or religious rules
