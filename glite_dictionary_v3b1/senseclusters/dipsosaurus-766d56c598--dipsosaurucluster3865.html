Dipsosaurus

proper noun
genus of desert iguanas
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪp.soʊˈsɔːr.əs / (AmE), / dɪp.səˈsɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of iguanid lizards commonly known as desert iguanas, native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Examples

Dipsosaurus lizard
Scientists observed a Dipsosaurus basking on a sunlit rock in the Mojave Desert.
The Dipsosaurus thrives in the extreme heat of North America's deserts.
Unlike many other lizards, the Dipsosaurus is specially adapted to survive in arid environments.
