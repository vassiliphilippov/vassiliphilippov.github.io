prime

noun
canonical hour at about 6 a.m.
9.7
Pronunciation: / praɪm / (AmE), / praɪm / (BrE)
(in Christian liturgy) the second of the canonical hours, traditionally observed around 6 a.m.

Examples

Morning prime prayer
Benedictine monks would gather for prime early each morning.
Few modern churches still observe prime as a distinct canonical hour.
The service at prime marked the beginning of a new day's work in the monastery.
