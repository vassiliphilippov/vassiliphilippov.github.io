proteosome vaccine

noun
vaccine using proteosomes, may be inhaled
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtiˈoʊsoʊm vækˈsin / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtiˈəʊsəʊm vækˈsiːn / (BrE)
a type of vaccine formulated with proteosomes, which can be administered by inhalation to induce immune response.

Examples

Proteosome vaccine by inhalation
Researchers are developing a proteosome vaccine to target respiratory infections more effectively.
The new proteosome vaccine showed promising results when administered through an inhaler.
Unlike traditional injections, the proteosome vaccine can be delivered directly to the lungs.
