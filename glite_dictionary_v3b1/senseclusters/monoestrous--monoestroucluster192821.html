monoestrous

adjective
having a single annual estrous cycle
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnoʊˈɛstrəs /; / moʊˈnɛstrəs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈɛstrəs /; / məʊˈnɛstrəs / (BrE)
Having one estrous cycle in a year.

Examples

A monoestrous animal
Most wild foxes are monoestrous, breeding only once during the year.
The monoestrous reproductive pattern is common among large mammals such as bears and deer.
Unlike dogs, which are polyestrous, wolves are monoestrous and mate only in late winter.
Bears are considered monestrous animals, experiencing only one breeding season each year.
Because the wolf is monestrous, its reproductive cycle is strictly seasonal.
The veterinarian explained that foxes are monestrous and mate only once annually.
