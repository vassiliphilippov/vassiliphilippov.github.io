harvest

verb
gather crops or natural products
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrvəst / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvɪst / (BrE)
to gather crops or other natural products from the land at the time when they are ripe or ready to be collected

Examples

To harvest wheat
Farmers usually harvest wheat in late summer or early autumn.
The villagers worked together to harvest the rice before the monsoon rains arrived.
They must harvest the apples quickly to prevent them from spoiling on the trees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gather - to collect things together, especially from various places
  • synonym: reap - to cut and gather (a crop) from a field
