lactic acid

noun
carboxylic acid found in sour milk and fruits
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌlæktɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌlæktɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A clear, odorless, and syrupy carboxylic acid found in sour milk, certain fruits, and produced in muscles during exercise.

Examples

Lactic acid in muscles
After intense exercise, lactic acid can build up in the muscles, causing temporary soreness.
Many skincare products use lactic acid for gentle exfoliation.
Yogurt owes its tangy flavor partly to the presence of lactic acid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citric acid - a weak organic acid found in citrus fruits
