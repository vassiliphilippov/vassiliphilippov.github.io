Australopithecus africanus

noun
extinct hominid species from southern Africa
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɔː.streɪ.loʊ.ˈpɪθ.ə.kəs æf.rɪ.ˈkæn.əs/ /; / /ɔː.strə.loʊ.ˈpɪθ.ə.kəs æf.rɪ.ˈkæn.əs/ (AmE), /ɒs.trə.lə.ˈpɪθ.ə.kəs ə.ˈfrɪ.kə.nəs/ /; / /ɔː.strə.lə.ˈpɪθ.ə.kəs æf.rɪ.ˈkæn.əs/ (BrE)
An extinct species of gracile hominid that lived in southern Africa about 3 million years ago, considered an early ancestor of modern humans.

Examples

Australopithecus africanus fossils
Fossils of Australopithecus africanus have provided insight into early human evolution.
Researchers believe Australopithecus africanus walked upright and had a mixture of ape and human features.
The famous Taung Child is a specimen of Australopithecus africanus.
