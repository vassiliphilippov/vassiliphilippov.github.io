siphonage

noun
liquid drawn by siphon
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪfənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪfənɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process of drawing liquid through a pipe or tube using atmospheric pressure and gravity, often causing unwanted flow in plumbing

Examples

Plumbing siphonage problem
Improper venting can lead to siphonage in household plumbing systems.
The plumber explained that siphonage was causing water to be drawn out of the toilet trap.
To prevent siphonage, modern plumbing systems include special vents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backflow - the unwanted reverse flow of liquid in a system
  • synonym: siphoning - the act of drawing liquid through a tube using a siphon
