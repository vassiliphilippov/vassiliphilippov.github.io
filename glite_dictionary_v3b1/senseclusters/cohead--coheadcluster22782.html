cohead

verb
to lead jointly
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈhɛd / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈhɛd / (BrE)
to serve as a joint leader or manager of an organization, department, or project with one or more others

Examples

To cohead a project
The two executives will cohead the new division to ensure balanced leadership.
After the merger, she agreed to cohead the marketing department with her colleague.
They decided to cohead the project to combine their expertise and experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cochair - to serve as a joint chairperson of a committee or meeting
  • synonym: lead - to be in charge of a group or organization
  • antonym: solo - to do something alone, without others
