blockchain

noun
digital transaction ledger
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈblɑkˌtʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈblɒkˌtʃeɪn / (BrE)
a digital system for recording information or transactions securely in linked blocks, making it difficult to change or tamper with

Examples

Blockchain record system
Many cryptocurrencies rely on a blockchain to keep records secure and transparent.
The company is exploring how blockchain technology can improve supply chain management.
Every transaction on the blockchain is visible to all participants in the network.
Many companies are exploring how blockchain can improve the security of their transactions.
A blockchain stores data in a way that makes it nearly impossible to alter without detection.
The rise of cryptocurrencies has increased interest in blockchain technology worldwide.
