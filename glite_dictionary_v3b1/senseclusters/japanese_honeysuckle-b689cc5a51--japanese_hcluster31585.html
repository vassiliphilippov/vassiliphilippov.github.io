Japanese honeysuckle

noun
a fragrant East Asian climbing plant
5.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /dʒæp.ə.ˈniːz ˈhʌn.ɪ.sʌk.əl/ (AmE), /dʒæp.ə.ˈniːz ˈhʌn.ɪ.sʌk.əl/ (BrE)
a trailing evergreen or semi-evergreen climbing plant native to East Asia, with fragrant white flowers that turn yellow with age, often grown as an ornamental and known for becoming invasive in some areas

Examples

Japanese honeysuckle plant
Many conservationists worry that Japanese honeysuckle is crowding out native plant species.
She planted Japanese honeysuckle along the trellis to enjoy its fragrant blooms every summer.
The garden fence was covered in Japanese honeysuckle, filling the air with a sweet scent.
