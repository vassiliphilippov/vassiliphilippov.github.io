multipolar

adjective
with multiple poles
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
having more than two poles or points of polarity, especially in magnets, electrical devices, or nerve cells

Examples

Multipolar motor design
The engineer designed a multipolar motor for greater efficiency in the new device.
Certain nerve cells are classified as multipolar because they have several extensions.
A multipolar connector allows multiple electrical signals to pass through a single interface.
