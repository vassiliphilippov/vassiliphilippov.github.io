Whartonian

adjective
of Edith Wharton
Pronunciation: / wɑːrˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / wɔːˈtɔːniən / (BrE)
relating to the works, style, or themes of the writer Edith Wharton

Examples

Whartonian novel style
The film's depiction of upper-class society is strikingly Whartonian in its attention to detail.
Her writing style is often described as Whartonian because of its sharp social observations.
Critics praised the novel for its Whartonian exploration of class and morality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Whartonesque - in the manner or style of Edith Wharton
