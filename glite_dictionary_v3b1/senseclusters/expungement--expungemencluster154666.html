expungement

noun
complete removal
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspʌndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌndʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of completely erasing or removing something from a document, record, or memory

Examples

Expungement of records
The expungement of outdated policies helped the organization modernize its procedures.
After the error was discovered, the report underwent a complete expungement of the incorrect data.
Historians debated the expungement of certain events from the official record.
