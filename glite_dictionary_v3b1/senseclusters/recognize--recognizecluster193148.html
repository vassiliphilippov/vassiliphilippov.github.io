recognize

verb
detect or identify using the senses
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkəɡˌnaɪz /; / ˈrɛkəgˌnaɪz /; / ˈrɛk.əg.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛkəɡnʌɪz /; / ˈrɛk.əɡ.naɪz / (BrE)
to identify or detect someone or something using the senses or memory, especially by seeing or hearing them again

Examples

To recognize a face
Despite the fog, she managed to recognize her friend's car parked on the street.
He could barely recognize the voice on the other end of the phone.
The children were excited when they recognized their teacher at the grocery store.
I could barely recognize him after all these years.
She didn't recognize the song at first, but then remembered the melody.
They instantly recognized the logo from its distinctive shape and color.
After so many years, she could still recognise her childhood friend in the crowd.
It took him a moment to recognise the song playing on the radio.
She failed to recognise the signature on the document at first.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detect - to discover or identify the presence of something
  • synonym: identify - to establish or indicate who or what (someone or something) is
  • antonym: misidentify - to identify incorrectly or fail to recognise
  • synonym: notice - to become aware of something or someone by sight, hearing, or other senses
  • synonym: recognize - identify as already known (US spelling)
