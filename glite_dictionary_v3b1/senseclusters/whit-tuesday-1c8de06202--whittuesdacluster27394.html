Whit-Tuesday

noun
Tuesday after Whitmonday; part of Whitsuntide
Pronunciation: / wɪt ˈtuːzdeɪ / (AmE), / wɪt ˈtjuːzdeɪ / (BrE)
The Tuesday following Whitmonday, observed in some Christian traditions as part of Whitsuntide.

Examples

Whit-Tuesday holiday
In some regions, Whit-Tuesday was marked by local fairs and special church services.
Pilgrims gathered in the village on Whit-Tuesday for the annual procession.
The tradition of celebrating Whit-Tuesday has faded in most parts of the country.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Whitmonday - the Monday after Pentecost, part of Whitsuntide
  • synonym: Whitsuntide - the week following Whitsunday, also called Pentecost
