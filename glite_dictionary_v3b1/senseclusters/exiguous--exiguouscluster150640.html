exiguous

adjective
very small in amount
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪɡjuːəs / (BrE)
very small in amount; scanty or meager.

Examples

An exiguous amount
Her exiguous contribution hardly made a difference to the outcome.
The committee was forced to work with exiguous resources at their disposal.
With such an exiguous salary, saving money was nearly impossible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ample - enough or more than enough; plentiful
  • synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality; scanty
