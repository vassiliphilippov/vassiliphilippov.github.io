speciesism

noun
belief in species superiority
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈspiːʃiˌzɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspiːsiːˌzɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that one species (especially humans) is superior to others and deserves different or greater moral consideration or rights

Examples

Speciesism belief
Many animal rights activists argue that speciesism is an unfair and harmful way of thinking.
The philosopher wrote a book challenging the idea of speciesism in modern society.
Debates about animal testing often involve discussions about speciesism and moral responsibility.
Many activists argue that factory farming is a form of speciesism that treats animals as mere commodities.
The documentary explores how speciesism influences our attitudes toward animal rights and welfare.
Critics of speciesism believe that all sentient beings deserve equal moral consideration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarianism - the belief that all people or beings deserve equal rights and opportunities
  • synonym: racism - the belief that some races are superior to others
  • synonym: sexism - the belief that one sex or gender is superior to another
