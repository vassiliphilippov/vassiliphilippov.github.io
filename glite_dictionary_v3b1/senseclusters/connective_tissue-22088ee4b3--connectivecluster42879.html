connective tissue

noun
body tissue supporting or connecting organs
1.0 4.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɛktɪv ˌtɪs.u / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪv ˌtɪs.juː / (BrE)
tissue that supports, binds, or separates other tissues and organs in the body, consisting mainly of cells such as fibroblasts and fibers like collagen

Examples

Body connective tissue
Some diseases, such as lupus, can directly affect the body's connective tissue.
Swelling can occur when connective tissue is damaged after an injury.
Tendons are composed primarily of connective tissue that links muscles to bones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epithelial tissue - tissue forming the outer layer of body surfaces and lining hollow structures
