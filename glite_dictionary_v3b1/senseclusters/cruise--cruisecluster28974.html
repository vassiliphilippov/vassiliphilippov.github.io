cruise

verb
travel at a steady, moderate speed
8.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /kruːz/ (AmE), /kruːz/ (BrE)
to travel or move around at a steady or relaxed pace, often for pleasure or sightseeing

Examples

To cruise along
The airplane will cruise at an altitude of 30,000 feet for most of the flight.
The ship cruised steadily through calm waters as the passengers looked out at the sunset.
We decided to cruise along the coast to enjoy the scenery at a relaxed pace.
Every winter, hundreds of tourists cruise along the Alaskan coast to admire the glaciers.
They decided to cruise around the Mediterranean for their summer holiday.
We spent the afternoon cruising on the lake, enjoying the sunshine and scenery.
He loved to cruise the city with his friends, music blasting from the speakers.
On Friday nights, teenagers often cruise up and down Main Street in their cars.
They spent the evening cruising the downtown area in their vintage convertible.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: crawl - move slowly; travel at a very slow speed
  • synonym: drive - to operate and control a vehicle
  • synonym: sail - to travel on water in a ship or boat, especially as a leisure activity
  • degree higher: speed - move quickly; travel at high speed
  • synonym: voyage - a long journey involving travel by sea or in space
