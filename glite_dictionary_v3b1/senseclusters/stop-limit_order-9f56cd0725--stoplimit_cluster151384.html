stop-limit order

noun
order to buy/sell with stop and limit prices
5.5 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑpˌlɪmɪt ˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɒpˌlɪmɪt ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
An order to buy or sell a security that combines the features of a stop order and a limit order: it is activated at a specified stop price and then becomes a limit order to buy or sell at a specified price or better.

Examples

A stop-limit order
A stop-limit order allows investors to specify both a stop price and a limit price for their trade.
She placed a stop-limit order to manage her risk if the stock price suddenly dropped.
Traders often use a stop-limit order to avoid selling shares below a certain price during volatile markets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limit order - order to buy or sell at a specified price or better
  • synonym: stop order - order executed when a specified price is reached
