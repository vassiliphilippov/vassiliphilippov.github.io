rhesus factor

noun
blood group antigen (Rh factor)
3.2 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.səs ˈfæk.tər/ (AmE), /ˈriː.səs ˈfæk.tə/ (BrE)
a substance in the blood of most people, which determines whether someone's blood type is Rh-positive or Rh-negative and plays a crucial role in blood transfusions and pregnancy.

Examples

Rhesus factor blood test
Blood donors are screened for their rhesus factor to ensure compatibility.
Problems in pregnancy can arise if the mother and fetus have different rhesus factor types.
The doctor explained that her baby was at risk due to her negative rhesus factor.
