line

noun
insincere or slick persuasive talk
1.7 6.4
Pronunciation: /laɪn/ (AmE), /laɪn/ (BrE)
a remark or set of remarks intended to impress, persuade, or deceive, often considered insincere or rehearsed

Examples

A clever line

Sarah laughed at the cheesy line he used to get her attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest or genuine in speech
