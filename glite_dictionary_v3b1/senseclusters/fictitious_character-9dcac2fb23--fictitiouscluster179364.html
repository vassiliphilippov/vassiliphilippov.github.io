fictitious character

noun
imaginary person in fiction
2.9
Pronunciation: / fɪkˈtɪʃəs ˈkærɪktər / (AmE), / fɪkˈtɪʃəs ˈkærɪktə / (BrE)
An imaginary person invented for and represented in a work of fiction, such as a novel, play, or film.

Examples

A fictitious character in a story
Although he seems real, John is just a fictitious character in the movie.
She based her latest fictitious character on stories from her childhood.
Sherlock Holmes is a famous fictitious character created by Arthur Conan Doyle.
