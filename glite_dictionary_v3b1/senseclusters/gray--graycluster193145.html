gray

adjective
intermediate in character or position
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ / (BrE)
Having a character, quality, or position that is intermediate or indeterminate, especially between clearly defined extremes.

Examples

Gray area
His views are not extreme but rather occupy a gray zone between the two parties.
Privacy rights are often a gray matter in the world of social media.
The issue falls into a gray area between traditional law and modern ethics.
Ethical dilemmas often fall into a grey region, lacking definite right or wrong answers.
Many legal issues exist in a grey area where the rules are unclear.
The company's policy leaves a grey zone for employee behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning
  • antonym: black-and-white - clearly defined, with no ambiguity or uncertainty
