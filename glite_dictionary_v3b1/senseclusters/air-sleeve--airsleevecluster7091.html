air-sleeve

noun
device for showing wind direction
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr sliːv / (AmE), / ˈeə sliːv / (BrE)
A truncated cloth cone mounted on a mast, used to show the direction of the wind, especially at airports.

Examples

A air-sleeve on mast
A brightly colored air-sleeve is essential for safe operations at small airfields.
Pilots rely on the air-sleeve at the end of the runway to check wind direction before takeoff.
The air-sleeve outside the hangar showed the wind blowing to the east.
