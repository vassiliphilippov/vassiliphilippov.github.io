amblyopia

noun
decreased vision not due to eye disease
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæm.bliˈoʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˌæm.blɪˈəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
A condition in which reduced vision occurs in one or both eyes due to abnormal visual development, without any detectable organic defect in the eye.

Examples

amblyopia diagnosis
People with amblyopia often do not realize they have reduced vision in one eye until tested.
The doctor diagnosed the child with amblyopia and recommended patching therapy.
Untreated amblyopia in early childhood can lead to permanent vision problems.
