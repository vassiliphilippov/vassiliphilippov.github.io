embase

verb
to set on a base
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ɛmˈbeɪs / (BrE)
to place or mount something on a base, pedestal, or foundation

Examples

To embase a statue
The artist decided to embase the sculpture on a marble pedestal for the exhibition.
Care must be taken to properly embase heavy monuments to ensure their stability.
They will embase the new statue in the center of the plaza next week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: install - to set something in position for use
  • synonym: mount - to fix or place something on a support
  • antonym: remove - to take something away from its place
