epiphany

noun
sudden realization
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɪfəni / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪfəni / (BrE)
a sudden and important realization or insight that brings new understanding or clarity

Examples

A sudden epiphany
During the meeting, he had an epiphany that helped solve the team's biggest problem.
After months of confusion, a sudden epiphany made everything clear to her.
Reading the book gave me an epiphany about how I should live my life.
During the meeting, he experienced an epiphany that helped solve the team's biggest challenge.
After days of thinking, she finally had an epiphany about how to approach the project.
Reading the book gave me an epiphany about my own life choices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confusion - a lack of understanding or clarity
  • synonym: insight - a clear and deep understanding of a complicated problem or situation
  • synonym: revelation - a surprising and previously unknown fact made known
