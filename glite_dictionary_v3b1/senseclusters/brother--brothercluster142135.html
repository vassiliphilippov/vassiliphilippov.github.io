brother

noun
close male friend; companion
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbrʌðɚ /; / ˈbrʌðər / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
a close male friend, comrade, or member of the same group, often used as a term of address or camaraderie

Examples

A brother in arms
He considers Mark his brother, even though they are not related by blood.
They stood by each other like true brothers through thick and thin.
What's up, brother? Ready for the big game tonight?
At the rally, he greeted each activist as brother and welcomed everyone warmly.
During the meeting, she addressed the new members as brother and sister to emphasize unity.
Union leaders often refer to each other as brother in solidarity speeches.
All the brothers in the fraternity welcomed the new members warmly.
He considered everyone in the monastery to be his brother.
The union brothers stood in solidarity during the strike.
