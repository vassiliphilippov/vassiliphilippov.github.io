potter around

verb
move about in a leisurely, aimless way
8.8 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.tər ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /ˈpɑː.tər ə.ˈraʊn/ (AmE), /ˈpɒt.ə ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /ˈpɒt.ə ə.ˈraʊn/ (BrE)
to move about or occupy oneself in a leisurely, aimless, or desultory way, often without a specific goal

Examples

To potter around the house
He spent the morning pottering around the house, fixing a few things here and there.
Instead of rushing, she prefers to potter around her studio, organizing and thinking.
On Sunday afternoons, I like to potter around the garden without any real purpose.
