run off

verb
flow away or drain off as waste (liquid)
6.2 2.5
Pronunciation: /rʌn ɔːf/ /; / /rʌn ɑːf/ (AmE), /rʌn ɒf/ /; / /rʌn ɔːf/ (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow away or be lost as waste, often draining away from a surface or area.

Examples

After watering the garden, some of the water will run off into the street.
Heavy rainfall can cause chemicals to run off into nearby rivers and lakes.
The excess oil ran off the pan and made a mess on the stove.
