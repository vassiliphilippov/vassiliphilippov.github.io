run off

verb
flow away or drain off as waste (liquid)
2.9 0.48/m
Pronunciation: / rÊŒn É”f / (AmE), / rÊŒn É’f / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow away or be lost as waste, often draining away from a surface or area.

Examples

Rainwater runs off the roof
After watering the garden, some of the water will run off into the street.
Heavy rainfall can cause chemicals to run off into nearby rivers and lakes.
The excess oil ran off the pan and made a mess on the stove.

Synonyms

  • flow off
    3.2 0.02/m
    flow off moves slowly away from a surface in a steady stream, while run off means liquid drains away and is lost.
  • flow away
    3.2 0.09/m
    flow away moves in a gentle current from one place to another, while run off describes liquid lost as waste.
  • drain
    1.0 16/m
    drain sends liquid away through pipes or ground, while run off refers to surface water that flows away as waste.
  • flow out
    2.9 1.00/m
    flow out pours or moves outward from a container or place, while run off usually means liquid drains away from a surface.
