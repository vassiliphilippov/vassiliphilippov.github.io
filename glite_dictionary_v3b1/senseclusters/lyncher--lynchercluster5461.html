lyncher

noun
public attacker (figurative)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪntʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɪntʃə / (BrE)
a person who aggressively attacks or shames someone publicly, especially online, to damage their reputation (figurative use)

Examples

Online lyncher
The journalist faced a group of online lynchers after publishing her controversial opinion piece.
Social media can quickly turn people into lynchers when a rumor spreads.
He refused to join the lynchers who were attacking his colleague's reputation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defender - a person who protects or supports someone
  • synonym: harasser - a person who repeatedly annoys or threatens someone
