radiochemical

noun
radioactive chemical
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
a chemical substance that is radioactive or has been labeled with a radioactive isotope, used in scientific research or medicine

Examples

A radiochemical tracer
The scientist added a radiochemical to the solution to monitor the reaction.
Hospitals use radiochemicals in certain diagnostic imaging procedures.
Proper handling of a radiochemical is essential to ensure laboratory safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radioisotope - an isotope with radioactive properties
  • synonym: tracer - a substance used to track chemical processes, often radioactive
