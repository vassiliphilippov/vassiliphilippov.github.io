coercively

adverb
using force or threats
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɜrsɪvli / (AmE), / kəʊˈɜːsɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that uses force or threats to make someone do something

Examples

Act coercively
The manager acted coercively to force the team to accept the new policy.
No one should be treated coercively in the workplace, regardless of the situation.
The confession was obtained coercively, raising concerns about its validity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forcibly - using physical force or power
  • antonym: voluntarily - done by free choice or will
