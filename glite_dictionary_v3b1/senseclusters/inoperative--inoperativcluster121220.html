inoperative

adjective
not working or not in effect
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɑːpərətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
Not functioning, valid, or having effect; not working or not in force.

Examples

Inoperative law
After the new law passed, the old regulation became inoperative.
The company was forced to close its factory because the equipment was inoperative.
The emergency exit was marked as inoperative during the repairs.
