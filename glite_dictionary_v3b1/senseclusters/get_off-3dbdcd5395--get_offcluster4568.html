get off

verb
escape punishment or be acquitted (law)
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɔf /; / ɡɛt ɑf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to avoid punishment or escape unpleasant consequences, especially in a legal case

Examples

To get off charges
His lawyer managed to get off all the charges despite strong evidence.
Many believe the wealthy can easily get off with minor consequences in court.
The defendant was shocked to actually get off after such a long trial.
He was caught cheating on the exam but somehow got off without any penalty.
Many believe that wealthy defendants are more likely to get off in court.
The suspect managed to get off with only a warning from the police.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquit - to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty
  • antonym: convict - to declare someone to be guilty of a criminal offense
  • synonym: get away with - to do something wrong without being punished
  • antonym: pay for - to receive punishment for something wrong
