hyperexcitability

noun
excessive responsiveness
9.2
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.ɪk.saɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /haɪ.pər.ɛk.saɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.rɪk.saɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /haɪ.pə.rɛk.saɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
a condition or state of being unusually or excessively responsive or easily excited, especially in nerves, muscles, or behavior

Examples

Nerve hyperexcitability
The doctor noted the patient's hyperexcitability during the neurological examination.
Children with hyperexcitability may have trouble sitting still in class.
Stressful environments can increase hyperexcitability in sensitive individuals.
Muscle hyperexcitability can cause cramps and spasms in some medical conditions.
Doctors observed hyperexcitability in the patient's nerve responses during the examination.
Certain medications may reduce hyperexcitability to help control involuntary muscle movements.

  • antonym: hypoexcitability - reduced ability of nerves or muscles to respond to stimuli
  • synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily stimulated or excited, especially in nerves or muscles
