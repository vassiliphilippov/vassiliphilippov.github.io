catchpenny

adjective
cheaply made to attract buyers
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈkatʃˌpɛni / (BrE)
(of an object) made or designed to attract attention and sell quickly, usually at the expense of quality or value.

Examples

Catchpenny goods
She avoided buying catchpenny decorations, preferring something with more lasting value.
Street markets are often filled with catchpenny trinkets and souvenirs.
The store displayed a range of catchpenny gadgets designed to appeal to tourists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shoddy - badly made or done
  • synonym: tawdry - showy but cheap and of poor quality
