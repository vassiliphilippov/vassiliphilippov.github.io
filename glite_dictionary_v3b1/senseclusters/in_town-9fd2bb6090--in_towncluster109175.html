in town

adverb
visiting a city
1.1
Pronunciation: / ɪn taʊn / (AmE), / ɪn taʊn / (BrE)
present in a particular town or city, especially as a visitor or for a short time.

Examples

A friend in town
My cousin is in town for a conference and wants to meet for dinner.
Whenever she is in town, she visits her favorite bakery.
The famous author will be in town next week to give a talk at the library.

Related Terms

  • antonym: out of town - not present in a particular town or city
