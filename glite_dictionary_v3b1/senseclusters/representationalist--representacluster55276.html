representationalist

noun
believer in mental representation
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that mental states or perceptions represent the external world.

Examples

A representationalist philosopher
The representationalist explained that our thoughts are images of the world around us.
As a representationalist, she argued that perception is not direct but mediated by mental representations.
Many philosophers debate the claims made by a representationalist about the nature of consciousness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealism - the belief that reality is mentally constructed or immaterial
  • synonym: realist - a person who believes that reality exists independently of perception
