fault

noun
crack in the earth's crust (geology)
5.6
Pronunciation: / fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
(Geology) a crack in the earth's crust along which there has been movement and displacement of the sides relative to each other.

Examples

Earthquake fault
Geologists surveyed the fault to assess the risk of future seismic activity.
The city is located directly above a major fault, making it prone to earthquakes.
The movement of tectonic plates caused a new fault to form in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fracture - a crack or break in a hard object, especially a bone or rock
