preignition

noun
early engine ignition
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a condition in an engine where the fuel-air mixture ignites before the spark plug fires, often causing engine damage

Examples

Engine preignition problem
Repeated preignition can lead to serious engine damage if not addressed quickly.
The mechanic explained that preignition was causing the engine to overheat.
Using the wrong type of fuel may increase the risk of preignition in your car.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knocking - abnormal combustion in an engine causing knocking sounds
  • antonym: misfire - failure of the fuel-air mixture to ignite properly in an engine
