bearskin

noun
tall ceremonial fur hat worn by British soldiers
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌskɪn / (BrE)
a tall fur hat, traditionally made of bearskin, worn as part of the ceremonial uniform by certain British soldiers, such as the Guards regiments

Examples

A soldier's bearskin hat
During the parade, the soldiers marched in perfect formation wearing their traditional red tunics and bearskins.
The Queen's Guard stood motionless in their impressive bearskins outside Buckingham Palace.
The tall bearskin made the ceremonial uniform even more imposing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: busby - a tall military fur hat, similar in style to the bearskin, but usually smaller
