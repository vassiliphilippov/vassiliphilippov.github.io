indifferentism

noun
belief differences unimportant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪfərəntɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfərəntɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or attitude that differences in beliefs, opinions, or religions are unimportant or do not matter

Examples

Indifferentism about opinions
Indifferentism can sometimes lead to a lack of engagement in important debates.
Some critics argue that indifferentism undermines the value of holding strong convictions.
The rise of indifferentism in society has sparked discussions about the importance of belief systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dogmatism - the tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true
  • synonym: tolerance - acceptance of different opinions or beliefs
