diamante

noun
diamond-shaped poem
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːəˈmɑːnteɪ / (AmE), / ˌdiːəˈmænˌteɪ / (BrE)
a poem of seven lines arranged in a diamond shape, often contrasting two ideas or subjects

Examples

Diamante poem
The teacher asked the class to write a diamante about friendship and loneliness.
Her diamante poem beautifully contrasted summer and winter in just seven lines.
We displayed each student's diamante on the wall to create a colorful diamond pattern.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acrostic - a poem in which the first letters of each line spell out a word or message
  • synonym: haiku - a short Japanese poem with three lines and a 5-7-5 syllable pattern
