unreturnable

adjective
irreversible state
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈtɜrnəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈtɜːnəbl / (BrE)
not able to be restored to a previous condition or state; impossible to reverse or undo.

Examples

Unreturnable damage
The damage caused by the fire was unreturnable, leaving the building beyond repair.
Once the document is shredded, the information becomes unreturnable.
Some decisions in life are unreturnable, so it is important to think carefully before acting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irreversible - impossible to change back to a previous state
  • antonym: reversible - able to be changed back to a previous state
