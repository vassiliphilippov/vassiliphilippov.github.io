ascriptive

adjective
assigned by traits
8.7
Pronunciation: / əˈskrɪptɪv / (AmE), / əˈskrɪptɪv / (BrE)
relating to the assignment of characteristics, status, or roles to someone or something, often based on traits present at birth

Examples

Ascriptive status
In many societies, ascriptive status such as race or gender can affect opportunities.
The report analyzed how ascriptive characteristics influence educational outcomes.
Some sociologists argue that ascriptive factors play a major role in social inequality.

  • synonym: attributive - relating to the assignment of qualities or features
