engine

noun
war machine or device (historical)
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a machine or mechanical device used in warfare, such as a battering ram, catapult, or artillery piece, especially in historical contexts

Examples

Siege engine
Ancient texts describe complex engines designed to launch projectiles over great distances.
During medieval battles, armies would construct engines to breach enemy defenses.
The fortress was besieged with massive engines that hurled stones at the walls.
