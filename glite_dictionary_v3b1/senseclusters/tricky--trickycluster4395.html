tricky

adjective
not to be trusted; deceitful
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪki / (AmE), / ˈtrɪki / (BrE)
deceitful or skilled at achieving things by cunning or sly methods; not to be trusted

Examples

A tricky person
It was a tricky operation that relied on deception and cunning.
The contract was written by some tricky lawyers who added hidden fees.
You should be careful—he is a tricky businessman who bends the truth.
He used a tricky move to outwit his rivals and win the game.
She admired how tricky her opponent was during the debate, always turning questions back around.
The tricky salesman convinced many people to buy things they didn't need.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cunning - skilled in achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion
  • synonym: dishonest - not honest; likely to cheat or lie
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: sly - having or showing a cunning and deceitful nature
  • antonym: trustworthy - reliable and can be trusted
