comorbidity

noun
additional medical condition
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊmɔːrˈbɪdəti / (AmE), / ˌkəʊmɔːˈbɪdəti / (BrE)
the presence of two or more diseases or medical conditions in a person at the same time

Examples

Comorbidity in patients
Doctors must consider comorbidity when planning treatment for patients with chronic illnesses.
The study found a high rate of comorbidity between anxiety and depression.
Managing comorbidity can make treating the primary disease more complex.
Doctors must consider comorbidity when planning treatment for elderly patients.
The study found a high rate of comorbidity between anxiety and depression.
Managing comorbidity can make healthcare more complex and challenging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complication - a secondary disease or condition that develops during the course of a primary disease
  • degree lower: disease - a disorder or illness affecting the body or mind
  • synonym: multimorbidity - the coexistence of multiple chronic diseases or conditions
  • antonym: primary condition - the main disease or disorder present in a patient
