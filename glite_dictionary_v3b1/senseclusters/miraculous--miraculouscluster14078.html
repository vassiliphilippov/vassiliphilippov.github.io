miraculous

adjective
timely or fortunate as if by a miracle
1.0
Pronunciation: / məˈrækjələs /; / mɪˈrækjələs / (AmE), / mɪˈrækjʊləs /; / mɪˈrækjələs / (BrE)
very surprising, fortunate, or extraordinary, as if caused by a miracle

Examples

A miraculous event
After months of drought, a miraculous downpour brought relief to the farmers.
Her recovery from the illness was considered miraculous by the doctors.
It was a miraculous escape, with everyone surviving the accident unharmed.
After the storm, the survival of the small cottage seemed almost miraculous.
Doctors described her recovery from the illness as miraculous, given the seriousness of her condition.
The rescue of the trapped miners was truly miraculous and amazed everyone around the world.

  • synonym: providential - happening at a favorable time; opportune, as if by divine intervention
  • antonym: unlucky - not fortunate; marked by bad luck
