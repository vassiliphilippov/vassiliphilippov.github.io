replot

verb
to rewrite storyline
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈplɑt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈplɒt / (BrE)
to change or rewrite the storyline, sequence of events, or structure of a narrative work such as a novel, play, or film

Examples

To replot a story
After receiving feedback, the author decided to replot the entire second half of the book.
The director worked with the screenwriter to replot several key scenes for better pacing.
Sometimes, writers must replot their stories to fix inconsistencies or improve character development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: edit - to prepare written material by correcting or modifying it
  • synonym: revise - to change or improve something, especially a written work
