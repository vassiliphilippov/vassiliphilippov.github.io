underlay

noun
physical network infrastructure beneath an overlay
7.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / c88cnd5accle6a / (AmE), / c88cnd59le6a / (BrE)
(in computer networking) the physical or lower-level network infrastructure (routers, switches, and links) that provides connectivity and carries traffic for virtual or logical 'overlay' networks.

Examples

Network underlay
The data center upgraded the underlay to reduce latency for critical customer services.
Network engineers monitor the underlay separately from overlays to pinpoint hardware failures quickly.
For secure multi-tenant clouds, cloud providers isolate traffic on the physical underlay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: network infrastructure - the physical hardware and links that carry network traffic
  • antonym: overlay - a virtual or logical network built on top of a physical network (the underlay)
