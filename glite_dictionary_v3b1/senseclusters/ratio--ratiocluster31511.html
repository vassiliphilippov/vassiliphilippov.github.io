ratio

noun
the quantitative relationship between two numbers
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
the relationship between two numbers or amounts, showing how many times one value contains or is contained within the other

Examples

Ratio of students to teachers
That company has a low debt-to-equity ratio, making it attractive to investors.
The ratio of sugar to flour in the recipe is 2 to 1.
To solve the problem, you need to simplify the ratio of boys to girls.
Mix the ingredients in a 2:1 ratio for best results.
The ratio of men to women in the club is surprisingly high.
The school maintains a student-to-teacher ratio of 15 to 1.
