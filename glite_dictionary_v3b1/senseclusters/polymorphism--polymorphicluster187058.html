polymorphism

noun
different crystal forms of a compound
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈmɔːrfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
The existence of different forms or crystal structures in the same chemical compound.

Examples

Crystal polymorphism
Some minerals display polymorphism, forming different crystal structures under varying conditions.
Temperature and pressure changes can induce polymorphism in certain chemical substances.
The polymorphism of carbon allows it to appear as both graphite and diamond.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allotropy - The existence of two or more different physical forms of a chemical element.
