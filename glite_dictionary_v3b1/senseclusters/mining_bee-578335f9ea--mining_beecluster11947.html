mining bee

noun
solitary ground-nesting bee (genus Andrena)
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ bi / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ biː / (BrE)
A solitary bee of the genus Andrena, typically nesting in the ground and frequently seen in the spring.

Examples

A mining bee nest
Every spring, mining bee colonies can be seen emerging from their underground nests in the garden.
Some people mistake a mining bee for a honeybee, but they are solitary and less aggressive.
The mining bee is important for pollinating early-flowering plants and crops.
