citadel

noun
fortified stronghold in a city
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtəˌdɛl / (AmE), / ˈsɪtədəl / (BrE)
a strong fortress in or near a city, used as a shelter and defense in times of attack or war

Examples

A citadel fortress
Over centuries, the citadel endured many sieges but was never captured.
The ancient city was dominated by a towering citadel overlooking the river.
When the enemy approached, the townspeople retreated to the safety of the citadel.
