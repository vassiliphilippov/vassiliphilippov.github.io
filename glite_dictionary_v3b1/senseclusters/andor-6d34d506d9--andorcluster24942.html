and/or

conjunction
either or both options
Pronunciation: / ænd ɔr / (AmE), / ænd ɔː / (BrE)
used to show that one, the other, or both/all of the listed options are possible or allowed

Examples

Use and/or options
You can choose soup and/or salad with your meal.
Applicants may submit their documents online and/or by mail.
The meeting will be held in person and/or via video call, depending on your preference.
You can choose soup, salad, and/or a sandwich with your lunch special.
Applicants may submit their portfolios online and/or by mail, depending on their preference.
The meeting will address safety, security, and/or maintenance issues as needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: and - used to link words or phrases, meaning both together
  • synonym: or - used to link alternatives, meaning one or the other
