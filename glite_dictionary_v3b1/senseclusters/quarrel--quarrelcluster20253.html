quarrel

verb
to argue angrily
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrəl / (BrE)
to have an angry argument or disagreement with someone, especially about something unimportant

Examples

To quarrel with friends
Please don't quarrel with your friends over such trivial matters.
The siblings quarreled over who would get to use the computer first.
They have quarreled so often that they barely speak anymore.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion, to consent
  • synonym: argue - to exchange reasons or opinions, often in a heated way
