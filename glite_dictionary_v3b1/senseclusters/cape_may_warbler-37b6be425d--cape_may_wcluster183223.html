Cape May warbler

noun
a North American wood warbler species
9.4
Pronunciation: / keɪp meɪ ˈwɔrb(ə)lɚ / (AmE), / keɪp meɪ ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
A North American wood warbler (Setophaga tigrina) with olive green and yellow plumage, marked with black streaks.

Examples

A Cape May warbler bird
A flock of Cape May warblers visited the garden during their spring migration.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot a Cape May warbler during its brief stopover in the region.
The Cape May warbler is known for its striking yellow and black markings.
