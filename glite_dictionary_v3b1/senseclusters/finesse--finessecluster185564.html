finesse

verb
to handle skillfully
6.0
Pronunciation: /fɪ.ˈnes/ (AmE), /fɪ.ˈnes/ (BrE)
to handle or manage something in a skillful and subtle way, especially to achieve a desired result

Examples

To finesse a problem
She finessed the difficult conversation so that everyone felt heard and respected.
The lawyer finessed the contract details to benefit her client without causing conflict.
He finesses every challenge at work with calm and creativity.
She was able to finesse a difficult conversation with her boss and reach an agreement.
The chef had to finesse the recipe to suit the guests' dietary needs.
He managed to finesse his way through the complicated paperwork without any errors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bungle - to handle something clumsily or badly
  • synonym: manage - to handle or control a situation successfully
  • synonym: maneuver - to move or guide something carefully and skillfully
  • antonym: mishandle - to deal with something badly or ineffectively
