Chandi

proper noun
fierce Hindu goddess; aspect of Devi
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃʌndi / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌndi / (BrE)
A fierce and malevolent aspect of Devi, the Hindu mother goddess, often associated with destruction and power.

Examples

Chandi goddess power
Legends tell of Chandi's fury, which destroyed all evil threatening the balance of the world.
Many devotees worship Chandi during the festival of Navaratri for her power and protection.
The ancient text 'Devi Mahatmya' describes the battles fought by Chandi against various demons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Devi - the principal Hindu mother goddess; embodiment of female power
  • synonym: Durga - a major Hindu goddess known for slaying demons; another aspect of Devi
