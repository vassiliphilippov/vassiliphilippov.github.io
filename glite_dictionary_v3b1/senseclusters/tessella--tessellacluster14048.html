tessella

noun
small block in mosaic work
6.2
Pronunciation: / təˈsɛl.ə / (AmE), / tɛˈsɛl.ə / (BrE)
A small tessera; a single small block, usually of stone, tile, or glass, used in the creation of mosaics.

Examples

A tessella piece
The artist selected a blue tessella to complete the sky in her mosaic design.
The restorer carefully replaced each missing tessella in the ancient mosaic pattern.
Under the microscope, every tiny tessella was visible in astonishing detail.
