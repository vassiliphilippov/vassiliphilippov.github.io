Church Slavic

noun
liturgical Slavic language used in Orthodox Christianity
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʃɜrtʃ ˈslævɪk / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ ˈslævɪk / (BrE)
the literary Slavic language used in Orthodox Christian liturgy and scripture translation, especially in the early Slavic church

Examples

Church Slavic language
Biblical texts were first translated into Church Slavic in the 9th century.
Many old manuscripts in Russia are preserved in Church Slavic.
The priest recited the prayers in Church Slavic during the service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Old Church Slavonic - the oldest attested Slavic language, predecessor to Church Slavic
