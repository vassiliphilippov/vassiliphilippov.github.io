jitter

verb
to shake nervously
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪtə / (BrE)
to move or shake with quick, nervous, or jerky motions

Examples

To jitter nervously
He could not stop his hands from jittering before the big presentation.
The camera jittered as the bus drove over the rough road.
Nervous energy made her legs jitter under the table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shake - to move quickly back and forth or up and down
  • antonym: steady - to make or become still and stable
  • synonym: tremble - to shake slightly, often from fear or cold
