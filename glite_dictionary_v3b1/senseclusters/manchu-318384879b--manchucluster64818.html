Manchu

noun
member of Manchurian ethnic group
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.tʃu / (AmE), / ˈman.tʃuː / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people from Manchuria, northeastern China, who ruled China during the Qing dynasty (17th–20th centuries)

Examples

A Manchu person
He traces his ancestry back to a prominent Manchu clan.
Many Manchu traditions are preserved in northeastern China today.
The Manchu established the Qing dynasty after conquering China in the 17th century.
