Lammas

noun
Christian harvest festival (August 1)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæm əs /; / ˈlæməs / (AmE), / ˈlæm əs /; / ˈlæməs / (BrE)
a festival held on August 1 to mark the beginning of the harvest, originally a Christian tradition and now also celebrated by some modern Pagan groups

Examples

Lammas festival
Lammas once served as an important division of the farming year in rural England.
In the Middle Ages, farmers brought loaves baked from new wheat to mark Lammas.
The villagers celebrated Lammas with a special church service and a communal feast.
Many local covens hold a ritual to celebrate Lammas with breads and grain offerings each August.
She marked Lammas by baking a loaf and giving thanks for the season's first harvest.
Community members gathered outdoors to share songs and stories for Lammas at the hilltop.
