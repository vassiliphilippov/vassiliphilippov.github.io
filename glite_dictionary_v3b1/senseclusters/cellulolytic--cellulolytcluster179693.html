cellulolytic

adjective
breaking down cellulose
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛljəloʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛljʊləˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
able to break down or decompose cellulose, especially by enzymatic action

Examples

A cellulolytic fungus
Some fungi are highly cellulolytic and play a key role in decomposing plant matter.
Researchers study cellulolytic bacteria to improve the efficiency of biofuel production.
The cellulolytic activity in the compost helps break down tough plant fibers.
