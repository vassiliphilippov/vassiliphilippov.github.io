mechanistic

adjective
relating to mechanical or physical explanation of phenomena
4.5 5.9
Pronunciation: /me.kə.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ /; / /mə.kə.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /me.kə.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ /; / /mə.kə.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
explaining or understanding things as if they work like a machine, only by physical or mechanical processes

Examples

A mechanistic view
Her explanation of the mind was criticized for being too mechanistic and simplistic.
Some critics argue that a purely mechanistic view of life overlooks human creativity.
The scientist's mechanistic approach ignored the complex factors influencing behavior.
During the Enlightenment, many thinkers described the universe as a mechanistic system.
Some scientists criticized the mechanistic interpretation of the human brain.
The philosopher rejected mechanistic explanations for consciousness, arguing they were too simplistic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deterministic - viewing events as determined by preceding causes
  • synonym: mechanism - the theory that natural phenomena can be explained by physical causes
  • antonym: organic - explaining phenomena as developing like living organisms, not purely mechanical
  • antonym: vitalistic - relating to the theory that life involves vital forces beyond physical and chemical processes
