Walter Raleigh

proper noun
English courtier, explorer, and writer (1552–1618)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔltɚ ˈrɑli / (AmE), / ˈwɔːltə ˈrɔːli / (BrE)
(1552–1618) An English courtier, explorer, soldier, and writer who was a favorite of Queen Elizabeth I, attempted to establish English colonies in America, and is credited with bringing potatoes and tobacco to England.

Examples

Walter Raleigh explorer
Walter Raleigh was instrumental in the early English attempts to colonize North America.
Many books discuss the adventurous life of Walter Raleigh and his relationship with Queen Elizabeth I.
Potatoes and tobacco are said to have been introduced to England by Walter Raleigh.
