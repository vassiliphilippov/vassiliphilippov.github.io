strand

verb
to leave someone helpless or isolated
Pronunciation: / strænd / (AmE), / strænd / (BrE)
to leave someone or something in a place where they cannot escape or get help, or (of a ship) to run aground and be stuck

Examples

To strand a car
Her car broke down, stranding her in the middle of nowhere.
If we miss the last ferry, we'll be stranded on the island until tomorrow.
The heavy snowstorm stranded hundreds of drivers on the highway overnight.
Heavy fog caused the ship to strand off the coast, requiring a rescue mission.
Several fishing vessels were stranded after the sudden storm drove them onto the sandbar.
The captain feared that the strong winds would strand the boat on the rocky shore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ground - (of a ship) to run aground or touch the bottom
  • synonym: maroon - to leave someone trapped and isolated, especially on an island
