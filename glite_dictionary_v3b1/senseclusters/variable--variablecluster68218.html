variable

noun
star with changing brightness
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriəbəl / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbl / (BrE)
an astronomical star whose brightness changes perceptibly over time

Examples

Variable star
Many amateur astronomers enjoy observing variable stars and recording their light curves.
Some variables change brightness over a period of just a few hours.
The astronomer discovered a new variable in the constellation Cygnus.
