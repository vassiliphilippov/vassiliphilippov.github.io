repellant

adjective
causing aversion or repelling
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛlənt / (BrE)
causing strong dislike, aversion, or disgust

Examples

Repellant behavior
I found his arrogant attitude truly repellant.
Some people consider the smell of garlic repellant, while others love it.
The film's violence was so repellant that many viewers left the theater early.
She found the cruelty of the film's villain utterly repellant.
Some people consider the idea of eating insects to be repellant.
The rotten food had a repellant odor that made us all feel sick.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repellent - serving or tending to repel; causing aversion or dislike
