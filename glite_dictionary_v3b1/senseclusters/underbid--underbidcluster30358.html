underbid

verb
to bid lower than another
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈbɪd /; / ˌʌndɚˈbɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbɪd / (BrE)
to offer to do work or sell something for a lower price than someone else, especially to win a contract or auction

Examples

To underbid a contract
Several companies tried to underbid each other to win the government contract.
She decided not to underbid this time, as the profit margin was already low.
The contractor underbid his rivals and secured the renovation project.
If you underbid on a house, the seller may reject your offer.
She underbid everyone at the auction and walked away with a great deal.
We decided to underbid our competitors to win the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outbid - to offer more than someone else in a bidding process
  • antonym: overbid - to offer more than others in a bidding process
