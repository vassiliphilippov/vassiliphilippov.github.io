General Certificate of Secondary Education

noun
British secondary school qualification
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnərəl səˌtɪfɪkət əv ˌsɛkəndɛri ˌɛdʒʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl səˌtɪfɪkət əv ˌsɛkəndri ˌɛdjʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A public examination in various subjects taken in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland by students, usually at age 16, marking the completion of compulsory secondary education.

Examples

A General Certificate of Secondary Education exam
Many employers require at least a General Certificate of Secondary Education in English and mathematics.
She received excellent results in her General Certificate of Secondary Education exams last year.
The General Certificate of Secondary Education is often the minimum qualification for further education in the UK.
