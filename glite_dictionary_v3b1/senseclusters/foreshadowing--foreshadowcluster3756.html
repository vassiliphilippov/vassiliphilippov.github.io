foreshadowing

noun
vague advance indication in a story
Pronunciation: / foʊrˈʃædoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈʃædəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving an advance indication or hint of what is to come in a story or event, often used in literature or film to prepare the audience for future developments

Examples

Effective foreshadowing in stories
Critics praised the director’s effective foreshadowing of future events throughout the play.
Subtle foreshadowing in the film's opening scenes hinted at the final twist.
The author used foreshadowing early in the novel to suggest the tragedy that would unfold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion
  • synonym: omen - a sign of something about to happen
