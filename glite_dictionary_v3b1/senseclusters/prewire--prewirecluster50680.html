prewire

verb
to install wiring early
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈwaɪə / (BrE)
to install electrical wiring or electronic connections in a building or device before it is completed or used

Examples

To prewire a house
The builders decided to prewire the office for internet access before the walls were finished.
It is common to prewire new homes for surround sound systems during construction.
They prewired the conference room so that installing projectors later would be easier.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retrofit - to add new technology or features to older systems
  • synonym: wire - to install electrical wiring in something
