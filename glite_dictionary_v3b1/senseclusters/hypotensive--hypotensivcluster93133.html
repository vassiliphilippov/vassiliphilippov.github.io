hypotensive

adjective
having low blood pressure
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
having abnormally low blood pressure.

Examples

Hypotensive patient
Certain medications can make people hypotensive if taken in large doses.
He felt dizzy and faint because he was hypotensive throughout the morning.
The patient was diagnosed as hypotensive after her blood pressure dropped below normal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypertensive - having abnormally high blood pressure
