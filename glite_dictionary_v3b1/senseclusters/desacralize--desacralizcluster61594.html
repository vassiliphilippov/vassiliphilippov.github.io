desacralize

verb
transfer from sacred to secular use
Pronunciation: / diˈsækrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsakrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to transfer something from religious (ecclesiastical) to secular possession, use, or control; to make non-sacred

Examples

To desacralize a church
Before converting the monastery into apartments, the building was officially desacralized by the church authorities.
Many churches were desacralized after the revolution and turned into museums or public spaces.
The government decided to desacralize the ancient temple, allowing it to be used for civic events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: secularize - to make secular rather than religious
