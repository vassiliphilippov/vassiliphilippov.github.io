stoppage

noun
obstruction in a pipe or tube
5.0 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààst…ëÀêp…™d í / (AmE), / Ààst…íp…™d í / (BrE)
an obstruction that prevents the normal flow of liquid or gas in a pipe or tube

Examples

Pipe stoppage
A plumber found a stoppage deep within the main sewer pipe.
Heavy rainfall sometimes leads to a stoppage in old drainage systems.
The stoppage in the sink was caused by grease buildup.

Synonyms

  • blockage
    4.1 2.24/m
    blockage is a common word for something that clogs a pipe, while stoppage sounds slightly more formal for the same situation.
  • occlusion
    8.7 0.49/m
    occlusion is a technical term used for blocked vessels or teeth, while stoppage is the everyday word for a pipe obstruction.
  • obstruction
    4.4 2.61/m
    obstruction is a broad word for anything that blocks a path or flow, while stoppage refers more to a pipe or tube losing its normal flow.
  • closure
    4.4 2.97/m
    closure means the act of closing or shutting something on purpose, while stoppage usually means an accidental block that stops liquid or gas flow.
