ruminant

adjective
relating to cud-chewing animals
Pronunciation: / ˈruːmɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈruːmɪnənt / (BrE)
relating to animals that chew the cud, especially those of the suborder Ruminantia, such as cattle, sheep, and deer.

Examples

Ruminant mammals
Some ruminant mammals, such as giraffes, have specialized stomachs for regurgitating and rechewing food.
The cow is a typical ruminant species, adapted to digest tough plant material.
Veterinarians study ruminant physiology to better understand the digestive processes of livestock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: herbivorous - feeding only on plants; relating to plant-eating animals
