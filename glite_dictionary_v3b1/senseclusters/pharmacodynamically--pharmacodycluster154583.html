pharmacodynamically

adverb
relating to drug effects
9.8
Pronunciation: /fɑːr.mə.koʊ.daɪ.ˈnæm.ɪ.kəl.i/ /; / /fɑːr.mə.kə.daɪ.ˈnæm.ɪ.kəl.i/ (AmE), /fɑː.mə.kəʊ.daɪ.ˈnæm.ɪ.kəl.i/ /; / /fə.mə.kəʊ.daɪ.ˈnæm.ɪ.kəl.i/ (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the effects of drugs on the body or how drugs act within the body

Examples

To act pharmacodynamically
The two drugs are pharmacodynamically equivalent, meaning they have similar effects on the body.
Researchers compared the medications pharmacodynamically to determine which was more effective.
Although chemically different, the treatments act pharmacodynamically in a similar way.
