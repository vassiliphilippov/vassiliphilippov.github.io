panduriform leaf

noun
fiddle-shaped leaf
Pronunciation: / ˌpændjʊˈrɪfɔrm liːf / (AmE), / ˌpændjʊˈrɪfɔːm liːf / (BrE)
A leaf shaped like a fiddle, with a broad, rounded middle and narrower ends.

Examples

A panduriform leaf shape
Identifying a panduriform leaf requires careful attention to its fiddle-like outline.
Some species in the genus possess at least one panduriform leaf during their development.
The botanist noted the presence of panduriform leaf shapes among the rare specimens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lyrate leaf - a leaf shaped somewhat like a lyre, with the terminal blade larger than the rest
