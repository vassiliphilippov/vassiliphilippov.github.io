fullscreen

verb
to display full screen
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlˌskriːn / (AmE), / ˈfʊlˌskriːn / (BrE)
to make a program, video, or image occupy the entire screen of a device

Examples

To fullscreen a video
If you fullscreen the presentation, everyone in the room will be able to see the slides clearly.
She quickly fullscreened the movie to enjoy it without any distractions from other apps.
You can fullscreen the game by clicking the icon in the corner of the window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maximize - to make a window or application as large as possible on a screen
  • antonym: minimize - to reduce a window or application to an icon or taskbar
