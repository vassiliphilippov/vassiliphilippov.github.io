laminitis

noun
disease causing hoof inflammation in horses
Pronunciation: / læməˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / læmɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A disease in horses and other hoofed animals involving inflammation of the tissue (laminae) that connects the hoof to the pedal bone, often causing severe pain and lameness.

Examples

Laminitis in horses
Outbreaks of laminitis can occur when horses consume excessive rich pasture grass.
Proper hoof care is essential to prevent laminitis in ponies and other equines.
The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with laminitis after noticing its reluctance to walk.
