perpetual warrant

noun
warrant with no expiration date
Pronunciation: / pərˈpɛtʃuəl ˈwɔrənt / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃu.əl ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
A financial security that gives the holder the right to purchase shares at a specified price and has no expiration date.

Examples

A perpetual warrant
He purchased a perpetual warrant to secure the right to buy shares whenever he chooses.
Investors appreciate that a perpetual warrant has no fixed expiration date, offering more flexibility.
The company issued a perpetual warrant as part of its effort to raise capital.

Related Terms

  • synonym: warrant - a security entitling the holder to buy shares
