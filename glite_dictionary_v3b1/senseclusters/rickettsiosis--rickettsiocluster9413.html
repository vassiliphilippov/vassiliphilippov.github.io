rickettsiosis

noun
disease caused by rickettsial bacteria
Pronunciation: / rɪˌkɛtsiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / rɪˌkɛtsiˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by ticks, mites, or lice carrying rickettsial bacteria

Examples

Rickettsiosis infection
Doctors confirmed that the patient was suffering from rickettsiosis after several tests came back positive.
Early symptoms of rickettsiosis can resemble those of the flu, making it difficult to diagnose.
Ticks are common carriers of the bacteria that cause rickettsiosis in humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: typhus - a group of diseases caused by rickettsial bacteria
