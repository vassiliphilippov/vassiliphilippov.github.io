mislaid

adjective
temporarily lost or forgotten
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈleɪd / (AmE), / mɪsˈleɪd / (BrE)
temporarily lost, especially because it has been put in an unusual or forgotten place

Examples

Mislaid important papers
Many students have mislaid their assignments after the school trip.
She found her mislaid keys under the stack of newspapers.
The mislaid document was finally located after hours of searching.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: lost - unable to be found; gone missing
  • synonym: misplaced - lost temporarily through being put in the wrong place
