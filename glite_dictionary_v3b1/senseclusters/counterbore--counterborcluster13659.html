counterbore

verb
to enlarge hole opening
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəbɔː / (BrE)
to enlarge the opening of a drilled hole so that a screw or bolt head can sit flush with or below the surface

Examples

To counterbore a hole
The carpenter needed to counterbore the holes for the bolts.
Before inserting the screws, she carefully counterbored each hole to ensure a smooth finish.
You should counterbore the wood so the screw heads do not stick out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: countersink - to enlarge the rim of a hole so a screw head sits below the surface
  • synonym: drill - to make a hole in something using a tool
