materialism

noun
focus on material possessions over values
Pronunciation: / məˈtɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a tendency to consider material possessions and physical comfort as more important than ethical values or spiritual well-being

Examples

Modern materialism
He rejected materialism in favor of a more spiritual way of life.
Many people criticize materialism as a shallow pursuit that ignores deeper values.
The rise of consumer culture has often been linked to increasing materialism in society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence
  • synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
