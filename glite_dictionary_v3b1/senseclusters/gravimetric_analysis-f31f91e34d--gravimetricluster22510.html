gravimetric analysis

noun
quantitative chemical analysis by measuring mass
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrævəˈmɛtrɪk əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈmɛtrɪk əˈnaləsɪs / (BrE)
a method of quantitative chemical analysis in which the amount of an analyte is determined by measuring mass

Examples

Gravimetric analysis method
In gravimetric analysis, the substance is weighed both before and after a chemical reaction.
Many classical laboratory methods rely on gravimetric analysis for accurate results.
The purity of the compound was determined using gravimetric analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: volumetric analysis - quantitative chemical analysis based on measuring volume
