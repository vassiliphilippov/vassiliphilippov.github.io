epiclesis

noun
prayer for divine help
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkliːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkliːsɪs / (BrE)
a prayer or formal invocation calling on a deity or divine power for help, blessing, or presence, especially in religious ceremonies

Examples

Ceremony epiclesis
During the ancient ritual, the priest recited an epiclesis to invite the presence of the divine.
The congregation joined in the epiclesis, seeking guidance and blessing for the community.
An epiclesis is often included in ceremonies to call for spiritual protection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benediction - a blessing, especially at the end of a religious service
  • synonym: invocation - a prayer or request for help from a higher power
