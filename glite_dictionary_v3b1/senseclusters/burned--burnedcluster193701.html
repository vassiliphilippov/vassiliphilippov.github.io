burned

adjective
ruined by being overcooked (food)
1.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùÀênd /; / b…ùÀênt / (AmE), / b…úÀênd /; / b…úÀênt / (BrE)
(of food) spoiled by being overcooked or charred, often turning black or having a bitter taste.

Examples

Burned toast
He accidentally left the pizza in the oven until it was burned.
Nobody wanted to eat the burned cookies from the first batch.
The toast was completely burned and tasted bitter.
His omelette turned burnt because he forgot to flip it in time.
She apologized for the burnt cookies fresh out of the oven.
The toast was completely burnt and inedible.

Synonyms

  • scorched
    4.6 0.15/m
    scorched is dried or damaged by intense heat, while burned means food is charred or overcooked and often tastes bitter.
  • blackened
    3.4 0.03/m
    blackened is dark and charred on the outside from smoke or high heat, while burned describes food spoiled by overcooking or bitter taste.
  • overdone
    2.9 0.02/m
    overdone is cooked too long and often dry but still edible, while burned implies charring or a black, bitter result spoiling the food.
