concession

noun
something yielded in negotiation
6.5 3.4
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈseʃ.ən/ (AmE), /kən.ˈseʃ.ən/ (BrE)
something that is given up or yielded, especially during a negotiation or argument

Examples

A major concession
He made a concession to the opposition to ensure the bill was passed.
No major concession was offered, despite hours of negotiation.
The union managed to secure several important concessions from the management during the talks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compromise - an agreement reached by each side making concessions
  • antonym: demand - something requested or insisted upon in negotiations
  • degree higher: surrender - the act of giving up something, especially in response to force
