space

noun
blank character between words
1.0
Pronunciation: / speɪs / (AmE), / speɪs / (BrE)
a blank area in writing or printing, used to separate words or sentences

Examples

A space between words
Always put a space between your first and last name on the form.
The programmer accidentally added an extra space in the password field.
When typing, pressing the bar creates a space between words.
He forgot to insert a space, resulting in words running together.
In traditional typesetting, a thin space can help align the text more precisely.
The printer added a space between each word to improve readability.
