charm

verb
protect using magic or charms
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrm / (AmE), / tʃɑːm / (BrE)
to use magic or supernatural powers to protect, control, or influence someone or something

Examples

To charm a house
He tried to charm his house by hanging protective amulets above the door.
She claimed to charm the newborn by whispering ancient spells.
The villagers believed the priest could charm their fields against evil spirits.
Legends say that wizards could charm wild animals to obey their commands.
The witch tried to charm the villagers into forgetting what they saw.
They believed the old amulet could charm anyone who wore it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bewitch - to cast a spell on; to affect by witchcraft
  • synonym: enchant - to put someone or something under a spell; to bewitch
