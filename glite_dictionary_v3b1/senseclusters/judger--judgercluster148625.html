judger

noun
person who judges
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒə / (BrE)
a person who judges or forms opinions about others or situations

Examples

A quick judger
He did not want to be labeled as a judger in the group discussion.
Some people are natural judgers, always quick to form opinions about others.
Instead of being a judger, try to understand different perspectives before deciding.
She tries not to be a judger and listens to everyone's opinions.
Being a constant judger can make it hard to form close friendships.
Instead of acting as a judger, he approached the situation with empathy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critic - a person who expresses opinions about the good and bad qualities of something
  • synonym: evaluator - a person who assesses or appraises something
  • antonym: observer - a person who watches or notices something without forming judgments
  • antonym: supporter - a person who gives encouragement or approval
