hydroxyacetic acid

noun
a translucent organic acid found in plants
Pronunciation: / haɪˌdrɑksiəˈsɛtɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksiəˈsiːtɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a translucent, crystalline organic compound found in sugar cane, sugar beets, and unripe grapes, used especially in cosmetics and as a chemical reagent

Examples

Hydroxyacetic acid in cosmetics
Due to its small molecular size, hydroxyacetic acid penetrates the skin more effectively than some other acids.
Researchers extracted hydroxyacetic acid from sugar beets for use in laboratory tests.
The skincare product contains hydroxyacetic acid to help exfoliate and brighten the skin.
