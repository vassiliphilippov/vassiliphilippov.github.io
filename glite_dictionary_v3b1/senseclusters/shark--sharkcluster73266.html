shark

verb
behave cunningly or deceptively
4.4
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ʃɑːk / (BrE)
(of a person) to behave in a cunning or deceptive way, especially to take advantage of others.

Examples

To shark money
She refused to shark her colleagues to get ahead at work.
Some gamblers will shark their opponents by pretending to be inexperienced.
They accused him of trying to shark his way into a better deal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or fraudulently
  • synonym: swindle - to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions
