hoopla

noun
noisy publicity or fuss
Pronunciation: / ˈhuːplɑ / (AmE), / ˈhuːplɑː / (BrE)
noisy or sensational publicity or excitement surrounding an event or activity, often used to attract attention.

Examples

Media hoopla
Despite all the hoopla in the media, the film failed to impress critics.
Don't believe the hoopla—the new restaurant is actually quite average.
The product launch was surrounded by so much hoopla that expectations were set unrealistically high.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • synonym: hype - extravagant or intensive publicity or promotion
