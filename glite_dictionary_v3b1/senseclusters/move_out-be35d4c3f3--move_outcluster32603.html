move out

verb
leave one's home or office permanently
Pronunciation: / muːv aʊt / (AmE), / muːv aʊt / (BrE)
to leave your home, residence, or office permanently and take your belongings with you

Examples

To move out of a house
After graduation, Sarah decided to move out and find her own apartment.
It took them a week to move out and clean the entire house.
The company will move out of the old building next month.
After getting a new job, he decided to move out and find his own apartment.
The landlord asked the tenants to move out by the end of the lease.
We're planning to move out next summer when the kids finish school.

Related Terms

  • antonym: move in - to begin living in a new home or office
