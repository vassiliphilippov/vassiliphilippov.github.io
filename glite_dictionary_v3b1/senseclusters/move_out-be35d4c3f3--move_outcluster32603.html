move out

verb
leave one's home or office permanently
1.9 3.78/m
Pronunciation: / muÀêv a ät / (AmE), / muÀêv a ät / (BrE)
to leave your home, residence, or office permanently and take your belongings with you

Examples

To move out of a house
After graduation, Sarah decided to move out and find her own apartment.
It took them a week to move out and clean the entire house.
The company will move out of the old building next month.
After getting a new job, he decided to move out and find his own apartment.
The landlord asked the tenants to move out by the end of the lease.
We're planning to move out next summer when the kids finish school.

Synonyms

  • move house
    2.1 0.08/m
    move house means changing your place of residence to a different home, while move out means leaving your home and taking your belongings.
  • leave home
    1.4 4.79/m
    leave home refers to leaving your family house to live independently, while move out can mean leaving any residence permanently.
  • pull up stakes
    7.2 0.18/m
    pull up stakes is an informal phrase for leaving your home and moving elsewhere, while move out is neutral and widely used.
  • relocate
    3.7 9.13/m
    relocate often sounds formal and can mean moving people or offices to a new place, while move out focuses on leaving a home.
  • vacate
    4.5 2.65/m
    vacate usually means leaving a place so it becomes empty or available, while move out implies taking your belongings and leaving permanently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leave - to go away from a place
  • antonym: move in - to begin living in a new home or office
