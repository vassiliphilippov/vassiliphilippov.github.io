F layer

noun
highest region of the ionosphere
8.2
Pronunciation: /ef ˈleɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ef ˈleɪ.ə/ (BrE)
the highest region of the ionosphere, situated from about 90 to 600 miles above the Earth, characterized by a high concentration of free electrons and important for long-range radio transmission

Examples

The F layer ionosphere
During solar storms, fluctuations in the F layer can cause disruptions in GPS signals.
Radio operators often rely on the F layer to communicate over great distances, especially at night.
The F layer contains the highest density of free electrons among the ionospheric layers.
