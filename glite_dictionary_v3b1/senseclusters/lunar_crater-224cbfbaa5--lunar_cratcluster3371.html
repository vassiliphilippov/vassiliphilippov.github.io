lunar crater

noun
a crater on the Moon's surface
Pronunciation: / ˈluːnɚ ˈkreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈluːnə ˈkreɪtə / (BrE)
a crater or depression on the surface of the Moon, usually formed by the impact of a meteorite or asteroid

Examples

Lunar crater formation
Astronomers are studying the formation of lunar craters caused by asteroid impacts.
From Earth, some lunar craters are visible with a simple telescope.
The Apollo missions landed near a large lunar crater to collect rock samples.
Scientists believe the lunar crater was formed by a massive meteor impact millions of years ago.
The astronauts discovered an unusually large lunar crater near their landing site.
The telescope revealed the sharp edges of a lunar crater under the full moon's light.

  • synonym: crater - a large, bowl-shaped cavity in the ground
