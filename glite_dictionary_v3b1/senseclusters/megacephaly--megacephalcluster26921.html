megacephaly

noun
abnormally large head condition
3.6 8.5
Pronunciation: /meg.ə.ˈsef.ə.lɪ/ (AmE), /meg.ə.ˈsef.ə.lɪ/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person has an abnormally large head, not caused by increased intracranial pressure; the overgrowth is symmetrical.

Examples

Symmetrical megacephaly
Genetic factors can sometimes lead to megacephaly in infants.
The child was diagnosed with megacephaly after doctors noticed the unusually large size of his head.
Unlike hydrocephalus, megacephaly does not result in increased intracranial pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macrocephaly - condition of having an unusually large head
  • antonym: microcephaly - condition of having an abnormally small head
