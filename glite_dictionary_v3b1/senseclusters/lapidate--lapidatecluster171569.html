lapidate

verb
kill by throwing stones
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlæpɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to kill someone by throwing stones at them, usually as a form of execution

Examples

To lapidate a criminal
In some historical accounts, traitors were lapidated as a public spectacle.
The ancient law permitted a mob to lapidate those convicted of adultery.
The community refused to lapidate the accused, despite pressure from outside authorities.
