overanalyze

verb
to examine too much
6.5
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈæn.ə.laɪz/ /; / /oʊ.və.ˈræn.ə.laɪz/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈræn.ə.laɪz/ /; / /əʊ.vər.ˈæn.ə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to think about or examine something so much that it becomes unhelpful or confusing

Examples

To overanalyze a problem
He tends to overanalyze every decision, which makes it hard for him to act quickly.
After the meeting, she overanalyzed her boss's comments and worried she had made a mistake.
It's easy to overanalyze a simple message and read too much into it.
It's easy to overanalyze a simple situation and end up feeling confused.
She tends to overanalyze every decision, which makes her anxious.
Try not to overanalyze his words; he probably didn't mean anything by them.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: analyze - to examine something in detail to understand it
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
  • synonym: overthink - to think about something too much or for too long
