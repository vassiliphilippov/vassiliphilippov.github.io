Aloeaceae

noun
a plant family including aloes (botanical)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæloʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌaləʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, formerly recognized as distinct from Liliaceae, but not widely accepted in modern classification

Examples

The Aloeaceae family
Older botanical texts often refer to the Aloeaceae as a separate family from the Liliaceae.
Some scientists no longer recognize Aloeaceae as a valid plant family.
The Aloeaceae include many succulent species adapted to dry climates.
Modern classification often merges Aloeaceae into the larger family Asphodelaceae.
Some taxonomists still recognize the Aloeaceae as a distinct plant family.
The Aloeaceae includes the well-known genus Aloe, famous for its medicinal properties.
