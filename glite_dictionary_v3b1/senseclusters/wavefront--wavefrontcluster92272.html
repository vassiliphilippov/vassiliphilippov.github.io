wavefront

noun
imaginary surface in a wave (physics)
9.1 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈweɪv.frʌnt/ (AmE), /ˈweɪv.frʌnt/ (BrE)
(physics) an imaginary surface connecting all points reached at the same time by a propagating wave through a medium.

Examples

A wavefront in physics
In physics, a wavefront can be spherical, planar, or any other geometry depending on the source.
Researchers measured how the sound wavefront traveled through the different layers of material.
The shape of the wavefront determines how the light will focus through a lens.
