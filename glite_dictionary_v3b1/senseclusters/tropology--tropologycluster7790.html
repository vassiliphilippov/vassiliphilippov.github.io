tropology

noun
moral interpretation
Pronunciation: / troʊˈpɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / trəˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the interpretation of religious texts that focuses on drawing moral or ethical lessons for practical conduct, especially in Christian tradition

Examples

Tropology in sermons
The professor explained how tropology helps readers find ethical guidance in ancient scriptures.
Medieval theologians often used tropology to teach moral lessons from biblical stories.
In his sermon, the priest relied on tropology to connect scripture to daily life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegory - a symbolic interpretation of a text
  • antonym: literalism - interpreting texts according to their explicit meaning
