trackside

noun
area beside track
Pronunciation: / ˈtrækˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrækˌsaɪd / (BrE)
the area immediately beside a racetrack or railway track, often used by spectators, officials, or workers

Examples

Fans at trackside
Fans gathered trackside to cheer as the race cars sped past.
Security personnel were stationed trackside to ensure the safety of the event.
We enjoyed the excitement from our spot trackside during the final lap.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grandstand - a seating area for spectators at a racetrack or stadium
  • synonym: sideline - the area just outside the main field or track
