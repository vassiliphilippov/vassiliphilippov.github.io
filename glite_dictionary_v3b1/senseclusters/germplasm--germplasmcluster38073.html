germplasm

noun
genetic breeding material
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɜrmˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmplæzəm / (BrE)
the genetic material or reproductive cells of plants or animals, used for breeding, research, or conservation, and responsible for passing traits to offspring

Examples

Germplasm banks
Scientists store germplasm in special banks to preserve rare plant species for future generations.
The research team collected germplasm from wild relatives to improve crop resistance.
Conservation programs rely on germplasm to maintain the genetic diversity of endangered animals.
Scientists preserve germplasm in seed banks to protect plant diversity for future generations.
The theory of germplasm was important in early genetics research.
Modern breeding programs rely on high-quality germplasm to improve crop yields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gene - a unit of heredity in living organisms
  • synonym: gene pool - the total genetic diversity found within a population or species
  • synonym: genetic material - the hereditary material in living organisms
  • antonym: somatic cell - any cell of the body except reproductive cells
