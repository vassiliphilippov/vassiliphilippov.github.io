photostatic

noun
old photographic copy
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈstætɪk / (BrE)
a photographic copy of a document made using a photostat or similar copying process, especially before modern photocopiers

Examples

A photostatic document
The lawyer requested a photostatic of the birth certificate for the records.
Before digital scanners, offices often used a photostatic to duplicate important documents.
He found an old photostatic of the letter in his grandfather's files.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or primary version of a document
  • synonym: photocopy - a paper copy of a document made with a photocopier
