deadly

adverb
extremely; very (intensifier)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdli / (AmE), / ˈdɛdli / (BrE)
Used to emphasize the degree or intensity of something; extremely or excessively.

Examples

Deadly serious
I'm deadly serious about my intention to move abroad next year.
She was deadly earnest when she spoke about the cause.
The meeting was deadly boring, and I struggled to stay awake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extremely - to a very great degree; very
  • antonym: moderately - to a certain degree, but not very; somewhat
