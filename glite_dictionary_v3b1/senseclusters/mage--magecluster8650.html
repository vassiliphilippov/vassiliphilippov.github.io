mage

noun
magic user
9.5
Pronunciation: / meɪdʒ / (AmE), / meɪdʒ / (BrE)
a person with magical powers, especially in fantasy stories or ancient times

Examples

A mage with powers
The powerful mage used ancient spells to protect the kingdom from harm.
In the story, a young mage learns to control her magical abilities.
The group relied on the wisdom of their mage during dangerous adventures.
The mage cast a powerful spell to protect the village.
In the game, you can choose to play as a mage or a warrior.
Legends tell of a wise mage who could control the elements.
The ancient mage was said to possess knowledge of the stars and the future.
In many fantasy novels, a mage can cast powerful spells to protect the kingdom.
The villagers sought the advice of the wise mage during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: muggle - a person without magical powers (in fantasy contexts)
  • synonym: priest - a person authorized to perform religious ceremonies
  • synonym: sorcerer - a person who practices magic or sorcery
  • synonym: wizard - a man with magical powers, especially in legends and fantasy
