dehull

verb
to remove husk
7.2 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / diËˆhÊŒl / (AmE), / diËˆhÊŒl / (BrE)
to remove the outer shell or husk from seeds, grains, or legumes

Examples

To dehull rice
Farmers often dehull rice before it is sold in stores.
The machine can dehull hundreds of sunflower seeds in a minute.
It is important to dehull beans before cooking to improve their texture.

Synonyms

  • hull
    5.5 0.62/m
    hull is to remove the outer husk from seeds or grain by hand or machine, while dehull names that processing action.
  • husk
    3.9 0.18/m
    husk means stripping the leafy outer cover from corn or seeds by hand, while dehull is a broader, often industrial term.
  • shuck
    6.4 1.39/m
    shuck is to pull off a shell or husk, often from corn or oysters, while dehull usually refers to seed processing.
  • shell
    2.1 2.09/m
    shell means removing a hard shell from nuts or seafood, whereas dehull refers mainly to taking husks from seeds or grains.
