dump one's load

verb
express emotional burden
4.2
Pronunciation: / dʌmp wʌnz loʊd / (AmE), / dʌmp wʌnz ləʊd / (BrE)
to express or relieve one's emotional burdens or stress, often by talking about one's problems

Examples

To dump my load of feelings
After the stressful meeting, I called my best friend just to dump my load and feel lighter.
She felt better after she could dump her load about the day's frustrations.
Sometimes people need to dump their load before they can move on.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vent - to express feelings, especially anger or frustration, openly
