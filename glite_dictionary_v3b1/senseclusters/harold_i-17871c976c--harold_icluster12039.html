Harold I

proper noun
king of England (1035–1040)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛr əld ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ˈhæ rəld ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
King of England from 1035 to 1040, the illegitimate son of Canute who seized the throne in 1037.

Examples

King Harold I
Harold I ruled England for a short and turbulent period after Canute's death.
After the death of Harold I, the throne passed to Harthacanute.
Some historians debate whether Harold I was rightfully the king or merely a usurper.
