calendar method of birth control

noun
contraception method using menstrual cycle timing
Pronunciation: / ˈkælənˌdɑr ˌmɛθəd əv bɝθ kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈkælɪndə ˈmɛθəd əv bɜːθ kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
a method of natural family planning in which a woman estimates her fertile period based on the assumption that ovulation occurs 14 days before her next period, and abstains from intercourse during the calculated fertile days to prevent pregnancy

Examples

The calendar method of birth control estimate
Because of its limitations, the calendar method of birth control is considered less reliable than other contraceptive options.
Some couples rely on the calendar method of birth control to help prevent unplanned pregnancies.
The calendar method of birth control requires careful tracking of menstrual cycles to determine fertile days.
