take leave

verb
depart or go away
6.0 3.9
Pronunciation: / teɪk liv / (AmE), / teɪk liːv / (BrE)
to formally say goodbye and leave a place or person

Examples

To take leave politely
After a brief discussion, he took leave of his colleagues and left the office.
Before sunrise, the soldiers took leave of their families and boarded the bus.
She politely took leave from the gathering before heading home.
After the ceremony, the ambassador took leave of the assembled guests with a warm handshake.
Before departing for his new job abroad, she took leave of her family and friends.
He politely took leave of his colleagues at the end of the dinner.
