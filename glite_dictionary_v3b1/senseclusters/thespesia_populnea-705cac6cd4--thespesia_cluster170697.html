Thespesia populnea

noun
pantropical coastal tree with showy flowers and valuable wood
9.9
Pronunciation: / θɛsˈpiːziə ˌpɑpjʊlˈniːə / (AmE), / θɛsˈpiːziə ˌpɒpjʊlˈniːə / (BrE)
a pantropical tree, typically found along seacoasts, cultivated for its rounded heart-shaped leaves, showy yellow and purple flowers, and valuable pink to dark red close-grained wood; also produces oil from its seeds

Examples

Thespesia populnea tree
Along tropical beaches, you can often spot the distinctive flowers of the Thespesia populnea.
The seeds of Thespesia populnea are used to produce oil in some cultures.
The wood of the Thespesia populnea is prized for its durability in boat building.
