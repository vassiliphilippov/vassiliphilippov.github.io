In Christian theology, the divine Word of God; the second person of the Trinity, incarnate in Jesus Christ.

Examples

The Logos of God
In Christian doctrine, the Logos is identified with Jesus as the divine Word made flesh.
Many early Church fathers wrote extensively about the concept of the Logos.
The Gospel of John opens with, "In the beginning was the Logos, and the Logos was with God, and the Logos was God."

Synonyms

    9.9 13/m

  • Son
    9.8 40/m
    Son is a relational title that stresses Jesus as God's second person in the Trinity, while Logos focuses on divine speech and reason.
