rationalize

verb
make (a system) more efficient or logical
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a system, organization, or process more efficient by organizing it logically or removing unnecessary parts

Examples

To rationalize a process
The company decided to rationalize its manufacturing process to reduce costs and increase productivity.
The government aimed to rationalize public services by implementing new technologies and management strategies.
They rationalized the workflow to eliminate unnecessary steps and duplication of effort.
Government regulations forced the airline industry to rationalize its routes and eliminate less profitable flights.
The company decided to rationalize its workforce by laying off redundant staff.
To improve their financial health, the store owners chose to rationalize their product lines.
After being acquired, the new owners rationalised all the manufacturing processes.
The company decided to rationalise its operations to cut unnecessary costs.
The government is rationalising the healthcare system in an effort to improve efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: organize - to arrange systematically; order
  • synonym: simplify - make something simpler by removing complexity or unnecessary elements
  • synonym: streamline - to improve the efficiency of a process or organization
