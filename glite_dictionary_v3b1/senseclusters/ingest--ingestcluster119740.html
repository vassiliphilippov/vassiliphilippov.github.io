ingest

verb
take in food or substance
3.6 4.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆdÊ’É›st / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆdÊ’É›st / (BrE)
To take food, drink, or another substance into the body, typically by swallowing or eating.

Examples

To ingest food
Doctors recommend that you ingest plenty of fluids when you are sick.
Some plants can be toxic if ingested by pets.
The patient had accidentally ingested a small object while eating.

Synonyms

  • consume
    2.4 14/m
    consume is used for eating or using things broadly, while ingest is a more formal, medical word for taking substances into the body.
  • take in
    2.5 0.62/m
    take in means to eat or drink in a particular or regular way, while ingest is a formal word for taking substances into the body.
  • eat
    1.0 309/m
    eat means putting food in your mouth and chewing it, while ingest covers eating or swallowing many kinds of substances into the body.
  • swallow
    1.0 14/m
    swallow means moving something from the mouth down the throat, while ingest covers the whole act of taking substances into the body.
  • have
    1.0 167/m
    have is a common, casual word for eating or drinking at a meal, while ingest is more formal and used in medical contexts.
  • take
    1.0 216/m
    take is a very general verb for using or receiving something, while ingest specifically means putting substances into the body by mouth.

Related Terms
