readdress

verb
to change form of address
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to change the way someone is addressed, such as by using a different title or name.

Examples

To readdress someone formally
After her promotion, colleagues began to readdress her as Dr. Morris during meetings.
When he became a judge, people started to readdress him as 'Your Honor'.
The teacher asked the students to readdress their classmate by her new last name.

Related Terms

  • synonym: address - to speak to or refer to someone using a particular title or name
  • synonym: rename - to give a new name to someone or something
