brush

noun
a brief encounter with danger or trouble
8.5
Pronunciation: / brʌʃ / (AmE), / brʌʃ / (BrE)
a brief or minor encounter or conflict, or a short contact with something or someone

Examples

A brush with danger
He had a brush with illness during his travels abroad.
She narrowly escaped in a brush with the law.
Their brush with disaster made them more cautious in the future.
A brush with fame gave her a brief moment in the spotlight.
He had a close brush with disaster when his car skidded on the ice.
Her hand had a brush with his as they reached for the door at the same time.
She narrowly avoided a brush with the law when the police stopped her for speeding.
The two players had a brush on the field, but nothing serious happened.
They had a brief brush over the new company policy during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confrontation - a hostile or argumentative situation or meeting between opposing parties
  • synonym: run-in - an encounter, especially one involving conflict or trouble
  • synonym: skirmish - a minor fight or battle, usually between small groups
  • synonym: touch - a brief contact with something
