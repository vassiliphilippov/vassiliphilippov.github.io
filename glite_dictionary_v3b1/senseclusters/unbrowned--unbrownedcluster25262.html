unbrowned

adjective
not turned brown
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbraʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbraʊnd / (BrE)
not made brown in color, by cooking, heat, or finishing; remaining pale or in its original lighter shade

Examples

Unbrowned vegetables
The chef served the vegetables unbrowned to keep their natural flavor and color.
She preferred her toast unbrowned, just lightly warmed and still soft.
The cookies on the top rack remained unbrowned after the short baking time.
The antique sword was unbrowned, making it more vulnerable to rust over time.
Collectors often prefer unbrowned firearms to see the original metalwork.
He noticed the metal parts were unbrowned and needed protective treatment.
The chef instructed us to add the mushrooms while they were still unbrowned.
Leave the bread in the oven longer if it is still unbrowned on top.
The recipe calls for unbrowned onions to keep the flavor mild.

Related Terms

  • antonym: browned - having turned brown in color, especially from cooking or exposure
  • synonym: pale - having a light or whitish color
