chorus pedal

noun
musical effect device for simulated chorus sound
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrəs ˈpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrəs ˈpɛdəl / (BrE)
an electronic device used with electric musical instruments to create the effect of multiple sounds by combining a short delay with slight changes in pitch

Examples

A chorus pedal effect
He added a chorus pedal to his setup to give his guitar a richer tone.
Some musicians prefer to use a digital chorus pedal for consistent sound quality.
The shimmering effect in that song is created by a chorus pedal on the bass guitar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flanger - an effects unit producing a swirling or swooshing sound by mixing two identical signals with varying delay
  • synonym: phaser - an audio effect created by altering the phase of a signal to produce a sweeping sound
