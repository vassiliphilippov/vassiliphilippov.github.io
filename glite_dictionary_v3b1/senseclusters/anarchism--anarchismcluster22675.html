anarchism

noun
political theory rejecting government
2.9 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈænərkɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈanəkɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A political theory and movement that advocates for the abolition of all forms of government and hierarchical authority.

Examples

Anarchism political theory
Debates about anarchism often emphasize the tension between freedom and order.
She wrote her thesis on the history of anarchism in Europe.
The principles of anarchism attract those who believe in a society free from government control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarianism - favoring strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • synonym: libertarianism - a political philosophy advocating minimal state intervention
