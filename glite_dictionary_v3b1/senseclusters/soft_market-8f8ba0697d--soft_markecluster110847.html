soft market

noun
market with more sellers than buyers
2.1
Pronunciation: / sɔft ˈmɑɹkɪt / (AmE), / sɒft ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
A market in which supply exceeds demand, making it easier for buyers to purchase and harder for sellers to achieve favorable prices.

Examples

A soft market
Due to an oversupply in the housing sector, it's currently a soft market for buyers.
Insurance companies often compete aggressively on price when there is a soft market.
The firm struggled to sell its products at premium prices in a soft market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hard market - a market characterized by more buyers than sellers, making it difficult for buyers to get favorable prices
