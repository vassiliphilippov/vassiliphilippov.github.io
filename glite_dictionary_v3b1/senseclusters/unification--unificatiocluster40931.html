unification

noun
merging data sources (IT)
4.8
Pronunciation: /juː.nɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /juː.nə.fə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /juː.nɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /juː.nə.fə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in information technology) the process of merging separate data sources or identity records to produce a single, consistent dataset or customer profile.

Examples

Data unification
The data team achieved unification of customer records from three platforms to create a single view.
Successful unification of product catalogs allowed the company to reduce duplicate listings across marketplaces.
Their CRM strategy prioritized unification so marketing could target each user with a consistent profile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data integration - the process of combining data from different sources to provide a unified view
  • synonym: identity resolution - the process of matching and merging records that represent the same real-world person or entity
