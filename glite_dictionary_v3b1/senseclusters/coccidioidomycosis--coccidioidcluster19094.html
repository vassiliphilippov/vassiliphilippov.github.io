coccidioidomycosis

noun
fungal infection of lungs and skin
Pronunciation: / kɑkˌsɪdioʊˌdaɪməˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɒkˌsɪdi.əʊˌdaɪməˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with the fungus Coccidioides, affecting mainly the lungs, and sometimes the skin, and characterized by symptoms such as cough, fever, excessive sputum, and nodules.

Examples

Coccidioidomycosis lung infection
Coccidioidomycosis, often called Valley fever, occurs primarily in arid regions.
Doctors treated her coccidioidomycosis with antifungal medication over several months.
The patient was diagnosed with coccidioidomycosis after extensive respiratory testing.
