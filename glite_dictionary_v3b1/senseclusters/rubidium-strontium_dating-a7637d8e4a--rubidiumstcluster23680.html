rubidium-strontium dating

noun
geological dating using rubidium and strontium isotopes
Pronunciation: / ruːˈbɪdiəm ˈstrɒntiəm ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ruːˈbɪdiəm ˈstrɒntiəm ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of determining the age of rocks and minerals by measuring the ratio of radioactive rubidium to its decay product, strontium

Examples

Rubidium-strontium dating method
Scientists often use rubidium-strontium dating to estimate the age of ancient geological formations.
Some rocks are too young or too altered for rubidium-strontium dating to provide reliable results.
The accuracy of rubidium-strontium dating depends on the careful measurement of isotope ratios in rocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radiometric dating - a method of dating geological specimens by determining the relative proportions of particular radioactive isotopes present in a sample
