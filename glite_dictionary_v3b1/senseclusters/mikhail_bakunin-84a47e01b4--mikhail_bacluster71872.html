Mikhail Bakunin

proper noun
Russian anarchist and revolutionary (1814–1876)
9.2
Pronunciation: /mɪ.ˈkeɪl bə.ˈkuː.nɪn/ /; / /mɪ.ˈkaɪl bə.ˈkuː.nɪn/ (AmE), /mɪ.ˈkaɪl bə.ˈkuː.nɪn/ /; / /mɪ.ˈkeɪl bə.ˈkuː.nɪn/ (BrE)
(1814–1876) A Russian anarchist and revolutionary, known for his influence on anarchist theory and for his association and later opposition to Karl Marx.

Examples

Mikhail Bakunin anarchist leader
Mikhail Bakunin was a leading figure in the international anarchist movement of the 19th century.
During the First International, Mikhail Bakunin became a prominent rival of Karl Marx.
Many of Mikhail Bakunin's writings influenced modern political thought on liberty and authority.
