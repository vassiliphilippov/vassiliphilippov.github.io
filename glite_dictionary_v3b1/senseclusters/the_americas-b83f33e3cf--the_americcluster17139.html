the Americas

proper noun
North and South America
2.1
Pronunciation: / ði əˈmɛrɪkəz / (AmE), / ði əˈmɛrɪkəz / (BrE)
the continents of North and South America (sometimes including Central America), considered together as a region

Examples

The Americas continents
Many indigenous peoples have lived in the Americas for thousands of years.
European explorers arrived in the Americas in the late 15th century.
The wildlife of the Americas is incredibly diverse, from polar bears in the north to jaguars in the south.
Many indigenous cultures thrived in the Americas long before European explorers arrived.
The biodiversity of the Americas includes rainforests, deserts, and mountain ranges.
European colonization dramatically changed the history of the Americas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: America - the continents of North and South America, or sometimes just the United States
  • antonym: Eurasia - the combined continental landmass of Europe and Asia
  • antonym: Europe - a continent east of the Americas, often contrasted in historical contexts
  • synonym: the New World - a term for the Americas, especially during the Age of Exploration
