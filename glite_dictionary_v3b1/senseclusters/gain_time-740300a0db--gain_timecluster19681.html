gain time

verb
delay to allow more time
2.1 4.9
Pronunciation: /geɪn taɪm/ (AmE), /geɪn taɪm/ (BrE)
to deliberately delay or prolong something so as to allow for more time before a deadline or decision

Examples

To gain time before deadline
By requesting a break, he managed to gain time to prepare his answer.
She asked irrelevant questions just to gain time before the meeting ended.
The negotiators tried to gain time by prolonging the discussions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buy time - to postpone an event or delay action to allow more time to prepare
  • antonym: lose time - to waste time or have less time than needed
