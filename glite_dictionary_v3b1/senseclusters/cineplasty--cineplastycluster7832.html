cineplasty

noun
surgical muscle modification
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnəˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪˌplæsti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure that modifies muscles or soft tissues to improve movement or allow control of an artificial limb

Examples

cineplasty surgery
The surgeon recommended cineplasty to improve the patient's ability to control his artificial arm.
After undergoing cineplasty, she could move her prosthetic hand more naturally.
Advances in cineplasty have helped many amputees regain functional movement.
The surgeon recommended cineplasty to help the patient regain movement in her hand.
After the accident, he underwent cineplasty to improve the function of his arm muscles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amputation - surgical removal of a limb or part of a limb
  • synonym: myoplasty - surgical repair or reconstruction of muscle
