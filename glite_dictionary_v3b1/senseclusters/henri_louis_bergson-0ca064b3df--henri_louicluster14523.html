Henri Louis Bergson

proper noun
French philosopher; theorist of élan vital
Pronunciation: / ɑ̃ˈʁi lwi bɛʁkˈsɔ̃ / (AmE), / ɒ̃ˈʁi lwi bɛːkˈsɒ̃ / (BrE)
(1859–1941) A French philosopher known for developing the concept of élan vital and influential theories on time, consciousness, and evolution.

Examples

Henri Louis Bergson philosopher
Henri Louis Bergson's concept of élan vital significantly influenced early twentieth-century philosophical thought.
Many students of philosophy study the works of Henri Louis Bergson to understand his views on intuition and time.
The Nobel Prize in Literature was awarded to Henri Louis Bergson in 1927 for his rich ideas.
