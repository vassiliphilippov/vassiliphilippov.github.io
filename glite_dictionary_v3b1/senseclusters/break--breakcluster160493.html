break

noun
game won against opponent's serve (tennis)
2.0
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
(in tennis) a game won by a player when their opponent is serving.

Examples

A break of serve
He was up two breaks in the second set, putting pressure on his opponent.
Securing an early break can make a big difference in a tennis match.
She failed to capitalize on her break and lost the following game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hold - (in tennis) winning a game on one's own serve
