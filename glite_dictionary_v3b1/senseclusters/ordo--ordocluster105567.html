ordo

noun
church service guide
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəʊ / (BrE)
a liturgical book or official list that prescribes the order of church services and rites for particular days or occasions

Examples

Church ordo
The bishop referred to the ordo to ensure the ceremony followed the correct sequence.
Each year, a new ordo is published to guide the clergy through the liturgical calendar.
Without the ordo, organizing the special service would have been much more difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: missal - a book containing the texts used in the Catholic Mass
  • synonym: ritual - a book of rites or ceremonies
