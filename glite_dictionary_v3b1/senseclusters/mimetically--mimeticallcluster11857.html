mimetically

adverb
in an imitative way
8.4
Pronunciation: / mɪˈmɛtɪkli / (AmE), / mɪˈmɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that imitates or copies the actions, appearance, sounds, or style of something or someone else

Examples

To act mimetically
The child danced mimetically, copying every move of her older sister.
He painted mimetically, closely following the style of the old masters.
The robot moved mimetically to resemble human gestures during the demonstration.
The actor moved mimetically to capture the gestures of the famous dancer.
In her poem, the words are arranged mimetically to echo the sound of falling rain.
The artist painted the scene mimetically, carefully reproducing every detail she observed.
The actor conveyed the story mimetically, relying only on facial expressions and body language.
Children often learn mimetically by copying the actions of adults around them.
The dance troupe performed mimetically, telling the tale without uttering a single word.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expressively - in a way that shows feelings or ideas clearly
  • synonym: imitatively - in a manner that copies or mimics something
  • antonym: originally - in a way that is new or not copied from others
  • antonym: verbally - using words or speech
