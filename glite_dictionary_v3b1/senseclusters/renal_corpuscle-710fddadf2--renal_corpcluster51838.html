renal corpuscle

noun
kidney structure for blood filtration
8.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀên…ôl Ààk…îÀêrp…ôs…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàriÀên…ôl Ààk…îÀêp…ôsl / (BrE)
A structure in the kidney consisting of Bowman's capsule and a glomerulus, found at the enlarged end of a nephron and responsible for filtering blood.

Examples

The renal corpuscle structure
Damage to the renal corpuscle can impair kidney function and waste removal.
Each nephron contains a renal corpuscle that filters blood plasma.
The renal corpuscle plays a crucial role in the first step of urine formation.

Synonyms

  • malpighian corpuscle
    9.9 0.00/m
    malpighian corpuscle is the traditional name for the same kidney filtering unit, while renal corpuscle is the standard anatomical term.
  • malpighian body
    9.9 0.00/m
    malpighian body refers to the capsule-and-glomerulus unit in older texts, while renal corpuscle is the current anatomical and clinical term.
