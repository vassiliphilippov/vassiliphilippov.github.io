split

noun
dividing shares to increase number without changing value
4.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /splɪt/ (AmE), /splɪt/ (BrE)
an increase in the number of a corporation's outstanding shares by issuing more shares to current shareholders, without changing the total value of their holdings

Examples

A stock split
After the split, each shareholder owned twice as many shares as before.
Analysts predicted that a stock split could boost trading volume.
The company declared a three-for-one split to attract more investors.
