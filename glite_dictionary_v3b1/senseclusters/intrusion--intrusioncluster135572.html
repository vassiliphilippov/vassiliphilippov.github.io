intrusion

noun
geological: molten rock entering fissures
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the process or result of molten rock forcing its way into existing rock formations beneath the Earth's surface

Examples

Basalt intrusion
Basalt intrusion can significantly alter the composition of the surrounding rock layers.
Geologists studied the intrusion to understand the area's volcanic history.
The granite intrusion formed deep beneath the Earth's surface millions of years ago.
Geologists studied the structure of the ancient intrusion to understand the region's volcanic history.
The granite intrusion is visible along the northern edge of the valley.
This mountain range was largely shaped by a massive intrusion millions of years ago.

  • antonym: extrusion - the process of molten rock flowing onto the Earth's surface
