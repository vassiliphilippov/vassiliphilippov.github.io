reverb

verb
to add reverberation
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.vɜːrb/ /; / /riː.ˈvɜːrb/ (AmE), /ˈriː.vɜːb/ (BrE)
to add reverberation to a sound or recording, often using electronic or digital effects

Examples

To reverb a track
The engineer decided to reverb the guitar track to create a sense of space.
You can reverb your vocals to make them sound more atmospheric in the mix.
They often reverb the drums in live recordings for a fuller sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: echo - to repeat or reflect a sound
