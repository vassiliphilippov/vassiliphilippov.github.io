sacrifice fly

noun
baseball play where a fly ball scores a run
Pronunciation: / ˈsækrəˌfaɪs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsækrɪˌfaɪs flaɪ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a fair fly ball that enables a runner to score after the catch, with the batter being put out but credited with an RBI.

Examples

A sacrifice fly hit
He hit a sacrifice fly in the ninth inning to win the game.
The team scored their first run on a sacrifice fly to center field.
With runners on base, she attempted a sacrifice fly to bring in the lead runner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sacrifice bunt - a bunt intended to advance a runner at the expense of an out
