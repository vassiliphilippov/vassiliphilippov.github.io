understudied

adjective
not well researched
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈstʌdid / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstʌdid / (BrE)
not researched, examined, or analyzed enough; lacking sufficient study or attention

Examples

An understudied topic
The environmental impact of microplastics in rivers remains largely understudied.
Many rare diseases are still understudied due to limited funding and awareness.
The cultural traditions of this remote community are surprisingly understudied by anthropologists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neglected - not given enough attention or care
  • synonym: overlooked - not noticed or considered
  • antonym: well-studied - researched or examined thoroughly
