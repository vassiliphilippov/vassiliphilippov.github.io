ion

noun
charged atom or molecule
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
an atom or molecule that has an electric charge because it has gained or lost one or more electrons

Examples

A ion with charge
A chlorine ion carries a negative charge because it has gained an extra electron.
The flow of ions across the cell membrane is essential for nerve impulses.
When table salt dissolves in water, each sodium atom becomes a ion.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: anion - a negatively charged ion
  • degree higher: cation - a positively charged ion
