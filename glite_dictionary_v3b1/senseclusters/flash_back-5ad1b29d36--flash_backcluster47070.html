flash back

verb
return to a previous time or event
1.0 4.3
Pronunciation: /flæʃ bæk/ (AmE), /flæʃ bæk/ (BrE)
(of a film, story, or person) to return suddenly and vividly to a previous event or time in memory or narrative.

Examples

To flash back suddenly
During the interview, she flashed back to the moment she won her first award.
Sometimes a familiar smell will make me flash back to my childhood summers.
The novel often flashes back to his early days in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foreshadow - to indicate or suggest something beforehand
  • synonym: reminisce - to recall past experiences or events
