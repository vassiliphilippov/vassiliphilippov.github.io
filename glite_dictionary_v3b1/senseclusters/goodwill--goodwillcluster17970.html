goodwill

noun
intangible business asset from reputation or advantage
Pronunciation: / ɡʊdˈwɪl /; / ˌɡʊdˈwɪl / (AmE), / ɡʊdˈwɪl /; / ˌɡʊdˈwɪl / (BrE)
(Accounting) an intangible asset representing the value of a business's reputation, customer relationships, and other non-physical advantages

Examples

Business goodwill
After years of excellent service, the store built up significant goodwill in the local community.
Loss of key clients may result in a decrease in goodwill on the balance sheet.
The company's goodwill was valued at several million dollars in the acquisition deal.
During the acquisition, the buyer paid a premium for the goodwill associated with the brand.
Impairment of goodwill must be tested annually in accordance with accounting standards.
The company's goodwill increased significantly after the successful launch of its new product line.
