equalization

noun
compensation for channel distortion
7.4 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåiÀêkw…ôla…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåiÀêkw…ôla…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
(in telecommunications and signal processing) the process of compensating for channel-induced distortion or frequency-dependent fading so that the received signal has a flat frequency response, often using adaptive filters or algorithms.

Examples

Signal equalization
The receiver uses adaptive equalisation algorithms to mitigate severe intersymbol interference on the radio channel.
Modern modems rely on equalisation to undo frequency-dependent fading before decoding the data stream.
Engineers tested several equalisation techniques to flatten the channel response in varying environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equaliser - a device or algorithm that performs equalisation by adjusting frequency response or compensating for channel effects.
  • synonym: equalization - American spelling; the process of making equal or, in signal processing, compensating for channel distortion.
