Amitabha

proper noun
Buddhist celestial Buddha
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːmɪˈtɑːbə / (AmE), / ˌæmɪˈtɑːbə / (BrE)
a celestial Buddha in Mahayana Buddhism, representing infinite light and compassion and presiding over the Pure Land

Examples

Amitabha Buddha
Many devotees chant the name of Amitabha during meditation to seek rebirth in the Pure Land.
The temple's main hall features a large statue of Amitabha surrounded by offerings and candles.
Stories about Amitabha emphasize his boundless compassion and promise of salvation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Amitayus - a form of Buddha associated with infinite life
  • synonym: Shakyamuni - the historical Buddha, Siddhartha Gautama
