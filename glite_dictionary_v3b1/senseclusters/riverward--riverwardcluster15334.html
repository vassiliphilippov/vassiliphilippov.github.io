riverward

adjective, adverb
toward a river
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvərwərd / (AmE), / ˈrɪvəwəd / (BrE)
toward, facing, or on the side nearest a river (used to describe position or direction)

Examples

Riverward side
The hotel offers beautiful views from its riverward balconies.
They planted trees along the riverward edge of the property to prevent erosion.
The riverward windows let in a cool breeze from the water each morning.
The explorers traveled riverward to find a suitable place to camp for the night.
As the fog lifted, the fishermen rowed riverward in search of a better catch.
Birds flew riverward at dusk, following the winding water below.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downstream - in the direction the river flows
  • synonym: riverside - located beside or near a river
  • antonym: seaward - facing or moving toward the sea
  • antonym: upstream - toward or in the direction from which a river flows
