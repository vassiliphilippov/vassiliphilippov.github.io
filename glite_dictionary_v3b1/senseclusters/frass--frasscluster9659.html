frass

noun
insect waste fertilizer
Pronunciation: / fræs / (AmE), / frɑːs / (BrE)
the excrement and debris produced by insects, especially larvae, often found on plants or used as an organic fertilizer

Examples

frass from insects
Gardeners often add frass to their soil to improve plant growth naturally.
The presence of frass on leaves can indicate an insect infestation in the garden.
Some organic farmers collect frass from insect farms to use as a sustainable fertilizer.
Gardeners often spot frass beneath leaves as a sign of caterpillar activity.
The entomologist collected samples of frass to study the feeding habits of the larvae.
A pile of frass at the base of the tree indicated an insect infestation.
