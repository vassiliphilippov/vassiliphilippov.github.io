offshoring

noun
moving business abroad
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːf.ʃɔr.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈɔːf.ʃɔr.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈɑːf.ʃoʊr.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈɒf.ʃɔː.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈɒf.ʃəʊ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the practice of moving jobs, business operations, or company assets to another country, usually to reduce costs or avoid regulations

Examples

Offshoring business operations
Many companies use offshoring to take advantage of lower labor costs in other countries.
The government is considering new policies to discourage offshoring of manufacturing jobs.
Critics argue that offshoring can lead to job losses in the company's home country.
The company decided that offshoring its manufacturing would help it stay competitive in the global market.
Critics argue that offshoring can lead to job losses in the home country.
Many technology firms have adopted offshoring to take advantage of lower labor costs overseas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: onshoring - the practice of moving business operations back to the home country
  • synonym: outsourcing - the practice of hiring external companies to perform business tasks
  • antonym: reshoring - the process of bringing jobs or business operations back to the home country
