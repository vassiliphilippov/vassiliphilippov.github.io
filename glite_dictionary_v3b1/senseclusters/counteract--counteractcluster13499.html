counteract

verb
act against; neutralize
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntɚˈækt /; / ˌkaʊntərˈækt / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərˈækt / (BrE)
to act against something in order to reduce or neutralize its effect

Examples

To counteract effects
Government measures were put in place to counteract rising inflation.
She took quick action to counteract the spread of the virus.
The new drug is designed to counteract the effects of high blood pressure.
Certain vitamins can counteract the harmful effects of free radicals in the body.
He drank coffee to counteract the drowsiness caused by the medication.
The new government measures are designed to counteract inflation.
Drinking water can counteract the effects of dehydration.
The government introduced new laws to counteract tax evasion.
They hired extra staff to counteract the increased workload.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neutralize - to make something ineffective by applying an opposite force or effect
  • antonym: reinforce - to strengthen or support, especially with additional personnel or material
