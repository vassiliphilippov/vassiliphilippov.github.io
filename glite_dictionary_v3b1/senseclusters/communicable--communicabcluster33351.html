communicable

adjective
(of disease) able to be transmitted by infection
4.2 0.46/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàmjun…™k…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / k…ôÀàmjuÀên…™k…ôbl / (BrE)
(of a disease) able to be transmitted from one person or organism to another, usually by infection.

Examples

Communicable disease
Measles is a highly communicable disease that can spread quickly in unvaccinated populations.
Some illnesses are not communicable and cannot be passed from person to person.
The hospital has strict procedures for handling patients with communicable infections.

Synonyms

  • transmissible
    5.3 0.21/m
    transmissible is a technical medical word for diseases that can spread between organisms, while communicable is a standard medical term for such diseases.
  • transmittable
    3.9 0.09/m
    transmittable describes conditions that can be passed to others in many contexts, while communicable names diseases spread by infection.
  • infectious
    3.9 5.66/m
    infectious refers to disease caused by infection and often spreading between people, while communicable simply means able to be transmitted.
  • contagious
    3.1 4.46/m
    contagious spreads easily between people and also describes feelings or ideas, while communicable usually refers to diseases passed by infection.
  • contractible
    6.5 0.00/m
    contractible means a disease can be caught by a person, while communicable focuses on the disease's ability to move between organisms.
  • catching
    3.5 0.00/m
    catching is a casual word for a disease that spreads easily between people, while communicable is the formal medical term for the same idea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagious - (of a disease) spread by contact; infectious.
  • synonym: infectious - (of a disease) liable to be transmitted to people, organisms, etc.; contagious.
  • antonym: noncommunicable - (of a disease) not transmissible directly from one person to another.
