Canadian red pine

noun
pine tree with reddish bark native to eastern North America
7.4
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈneɪ.dɪ.ən red paɪn/ (AmE), /kə.ˈneɪ.dɪ.ən red paɪn/ (BrE)
A pine tree native to eastern North America, characterized by long needles in pairs and reddish bark.

Examples

The Canadian red pine tree
The Canadian red pine is commonly planted for timber production due to its straight trunk.
The reddish bark of the Canadian red pine makes it easy to distinguish from other pines.
We hiked through a forest dominated by towering Canadian red pine trees.
