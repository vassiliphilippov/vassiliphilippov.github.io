innominata

noun
brain region
Pronunciation: / ɪˌnɑməˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
a region of the basal forebrain beneath the globus pallidus, involved in arousal and memory functions

Examples

Innominata damage
Damage to the innominata can affect both memory and alertness in patients.
Researchers are investigating the role of the innominata in neurodegenerative diseases.
The innominata is located beneath the globus pallidus in the basal forebrain.

Related Terms
