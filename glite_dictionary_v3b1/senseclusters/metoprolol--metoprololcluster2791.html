metoprolol

noun
a beta-blocker drug for heart conditions
5.9
Pronunciation: / məˈtoʊprəlɔl / (AmE), / məˈtəʊprəlɒl / (BrE)
a beta-blocker drug used to treat high blood pressure, angina, heart rhythm disorders, and to reduce the risk of further heart problems after a heart attack

Examples

Take metoprolol daily
After his heart attack, he started taking metoprolol to prevent further complications.
My doctor prescribed metoprolol to help control my blood pressure.
Some patients may experience fatigue or dizziness as side effects of metoprolol.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beta-blocker - a drug that blocks beta-adrenergic receptors, reducing blood pressure and heart rate
