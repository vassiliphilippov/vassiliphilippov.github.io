poster child

noun
representative child for a cause or charity
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊstər tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a person (originally a child) or thing that is a typical or prominent example of a cause, movement, or ideal

Examples

The poster child for change
He was once seen as the poster child for overcoming adversity in the community.
The young girl became the poster child for the charity's cancer awareness campaign.
Their son became a poster child for the national diabetes association.
After his speech, she became the poster child for mental health awareness.
He's often cited as the poster child of success in the face of adversity.
The company is now a poster child for innovation in the tech sector.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exemplar - a person or thing serving as a typical example or excellent model
  • synonym: icon - a person or thing regarded as a representative symbol or as worthy of veneration
  • synonym: symbol - something that represents or stands for something else, especially a concept
