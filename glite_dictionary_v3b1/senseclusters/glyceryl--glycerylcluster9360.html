glyceryl

noun
chemical radical from glycerol
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪsərɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɪl / (BrE)
a trivalent chemical radical derived from glycerol by removing the three hydroxyl groups

Examples

Glyceryl chemical radical
Nitroglycerin is an example of a compound containing the glyceryl radical.
Researchers studied the reactivity of glyceryl derivatives in synthetic reactions.
Several pharmaceuticals are produced using glyceryl esters as intermediates.
