gospel

noun
unquestionable truth or principle
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːspəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl / (BrE)
an idea, principle, or doctrine that is accepted as absolutely true or authoritative

Examples

Gospel truth
Don't take her advice as gospel; it's just one opinion among many.
For years, the textbook's claims were considered gospel in the academic community.
He treated every word his mentor said as gospel and never questioned it.
Don't treat every expert opinion as gospel; it's important to think critically.
For some, the words of their leader are regarded as gospel and never questioned.
His interpretation of the rules was taken as gospel by the entire committee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • antonym: myth - a widely held but false belief or idea
