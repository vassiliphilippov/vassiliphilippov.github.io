greater peritoneal sac

noun
main cavity of the peritoneum
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪtər ˌpɛrətoʊˈniːəl sæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˌpɛrɪtəʊˈniːəl sæk / (BrE)
the main cavity of the peritoneum, considered as a potential space within the abdominal cavity and separated from the lesser peritoneal sac

Examples

The greater peritoneal sac cavity
Inflammation in the greater peritoneal sac can cause pain throughout the abdomen.
Surgeons must be careful to avoid damaging structures within the greater peritoneal sac during abdominal procedures.
The greater peritoneal sac communicates with the lesser sac through the omental foramen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lesser peritoneal sac - the smaller cavity of the peritoneum behind the stomach
