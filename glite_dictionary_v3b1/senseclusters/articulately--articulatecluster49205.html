articulately

adverb
in a clear and eloquent manner
Pronunciation: / ɑɹˈtɪkjələtli /; / ɑːrˈtɪkjələtli / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjələtli /; / ɑːˈtɪkjʊlətli / (BrE)
in a clear, fluent, and effective way; expressing ideas or speech so that they are easy to understand

Examples

To speak articulately
If you want your message heard, present your ideas articulately and with confidence.
She spoke articulately during the debate, impressing both the judges and the audience.
The professor explained the complex topic articulately so that everyone could understand.
Despite her nerves, she answered every question articulately and thoughtfully.
She spoke articulately about the issues facing the community.
The student articulately defended his position during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eloquently - in a fluent and persuasive manner
  • antonym: inarticulately - in a manner that is not clear or effective
