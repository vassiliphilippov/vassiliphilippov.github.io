Malus angustifolia

noun
southern crabapple tree/shrub
Pronunciation: / ˈmæləs ˌæŋɡəˈstoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪləs ˌæŋɡʊˈstəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of small tree or shrub native to the southeastern United States, cultivated for its ornamental rose-colored blossoms; also known as the southern crabapple.

Examples

Malus angustifolia tree
Many gardeners in the southeastern U.S. cultivate Malus angustifolia as a decorative tree.
The Malus angustifolia brightens southern gardens with its vibrant rose-colored blossoms each spring.
Wild Malus angustifolia can often be found at the edges of forests in Alabama.
