Pycnanthemum

proper noun
genus of American mountain mint plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɪkˈnænθəməm / (AmE), / pɪkˈnænθɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of mint plants native to North America, commonly known as American mountain mint, valued for its aromatic properties and small white or purple flowers.

Examples

Pycnanthemum plant
Pycnanthemum grows well in well-drained soils and attracts many pollinators to gardens.
Botanists identified the species as part of the Pycnanthemum genus.
The aromatic leaves of Pycnanthemum have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
