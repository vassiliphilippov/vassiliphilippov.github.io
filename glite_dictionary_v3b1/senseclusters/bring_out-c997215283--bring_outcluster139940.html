bring out

verb
make noticeable or apparent
3.2
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / brɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to make qualities, features, or details more noticeable or easy to see

Examples

Bring out the color
A good teacher can bring out a student's hidden talents.
Spicy food tends to bring out the flavor of the sauce.
The new lighting will bring out the colors of the artwork.
Her choice of accessories brings out the color of her eyes.
The editor's comments brought out the main ideas in the article.
The new lighting really brings out the beauty of the painting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accentuate - to make something more noticeable
  • antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something from being noticed
  • synonym: reveal - to make something known that was previously secret or hidden
  • synonym: set off - to highlight by contrast
  • antonym: suppress - to prevent something from being seen or known
