Hesiod

proper noun
ancient Greek poet (8th century BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːsiəd / (AmE), / ˈhiːsiəd / (BrE)
(c. 8th century BC) An ancient Greek poet whose surviving works, including 'Theogony' and 'Works and Days,' are key sources for Greek mythology and early Greek rural life.

Examples

Ancient poet Hesiod
In his book, the professor compares Homer and Hesiod as foundational poets of Greek literature.
Many scholars study Hesiod to learn about ancient Greek mythology and agriculture.
The poetry of Hesiod offers insight into the values and beliefs of his era.
