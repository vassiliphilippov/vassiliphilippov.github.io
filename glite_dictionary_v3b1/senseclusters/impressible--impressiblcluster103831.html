impressible

adjective
capable of receiving an imprint
8.4
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpres.ə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpres.ɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpres.ə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpres.ɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a material or surface) capable of receiving an impression, mark, or imprint from a stamp, mold, or other object; able to be molded or stamped.

Examples

Impressible clay surface
Wet clay is highly impressible, accepting fine details from the wooden stamping tool.
Because the soft metal was more impressible than expected, the die left a clear decorative mark.
Archaeologists noted the coin's surface was no longer impressible after centuries of wear and corrosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malleable - able to be shaped or formed, especially by pressure or hammering
  • synonym: moldable - capable of being shaped or formed in a mold
  • antonym: rigid - not easily bent, changed, or shaped; stiff
