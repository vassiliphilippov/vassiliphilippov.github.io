resistance

noun
device that opposes electric current
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
an electrical device, such as a resistor, that reduces the flow of electric current in a circuit

Examples

Variable resistance device
Adjusting the resistance changes the brightness of the lamp.
The engineer replaced the broken resistance to restore the circuit's function.
We measured the resistance using an ohmmeter.
