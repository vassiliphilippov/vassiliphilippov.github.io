timbales

noun
Latin percussion drums
8.0
Pronunciation: / tɪmˈbɑːleɪz / (AmE), / tɪmˈbɑːleɪz / (BrE)
a pair of shallow, single-headed metal drums played with sticks, commonly used in Latin American music

Examples

Latin timbales drums
The band featured a skilled musician on timbales during the lively salsa number.
He bought a new set of timbales to improve his Latin jazz performances.
The sharp, metallic sound of the timbales stood out in the percussion section.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bongos - a pair of small, open-bottomed hand drums
  • synonym: congas - tall, narrow drums played with the hands
