shawl

verb
to wrap with a shawl
Pronunciation: / ʃɔl / (AmE), / ʃɔːl / (BrE)
to cover or wrap someone or something with a shawl, or to envelop as if with a shawl, often figuratively.

Examples

To shawl a baby
She shawled the baby in a soft blanket before stepping out into the cold evening.
The mountain was shawled in mist at dawn, hiding its peak from view.
He gently shawled his grandmother's shoulders to keep her warm during the chilly night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unwrap - to remove a covering from something
