overstretch

verb
to exceed limits
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstrɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstrɛtʃ / (BrE)
to push something or someone beyond their normal limits or capacity, often causing strain or difficulty

Examples

To overstretch your budget
If you overstretch your budget, you may run into financial trouble.
The hospital staff were overstretched during the busy flu season.
He tends to overstretch himself by taking on too many projects at once.

Related Terms

  • antonym: limit - to keep within a certain amount or boundary
  • synonym: overextend - to commit more resources or effort than can be managed
  • synonym: strain - to put too much pressure or demand on something
