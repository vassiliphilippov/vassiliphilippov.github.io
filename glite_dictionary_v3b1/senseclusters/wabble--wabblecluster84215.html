wabble

noun
brief unsteady movement
Pronunciation: / ˈwæbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒbəl / (BrE)
a brief unsteady movement or momentary wobble, especially from side to side.

Examples

A sudden wabble
A sudden wabble in the steering made him slow the car and check the tyres.
The table had a slight wabble because one leg was shorter than the others.
She noticed a wabble in the camera as the wind picked up during filming.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and not moving or changing
  • synonym: wobble - an unsteady movement from side to side
