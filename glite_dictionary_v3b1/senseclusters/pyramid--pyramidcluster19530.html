pyramid

verb
enlarge holdings in financial markets using profits as margin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪrəmɪd /; / ˈpɪr.əˌmɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪərəmɪd /; / ˈpɪə.rə.mɪd /; / ˈpɪɹ.əˌmɪd / (BrE)
to use profits or assets to increase your investment in stages, especially by using the value of current holdings to buy more

Examples

To pyramid investments
By pyramiding, he quickly increased his stake, but also his exposure to losses.
Many traders choose to pyramid their positions during a strong bull market.
She wisely decided not to pyramid her investments, avoiding excessive risk.
He lost everything when he tried to pyramid his small initial investment.
Some investors pyramid aggressively, hoping to amplify returns through leveraging.
The trader attempted to pyramid his profits by reinvesting them into larger positions.
She managed to pyramid her initial investment into a substantial fortune over the years.
Speculators attempted to pyramid their holdings in the booming market.
The company aims to pyramid its profits by reinvesting earnings every quarter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: average down - reduce average cost per share by buying more at lower prices
  • synonym: average up - increase average cost per share by buying more at higher prices
  • synonym: escalate - to increase rapidly or become more intense
  • synonym: speculate - to engage in risky financial transactions
