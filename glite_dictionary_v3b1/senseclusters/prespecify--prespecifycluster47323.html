prespecify

verb
to set in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈspɛsɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈspɛsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to state or determine something in advance, before an event or process begins

Examples

To prespecify conditions
The criteria for the experiment were prespecified to ensure consistency across all trials.
You must prespecify your preferences before the system can generate recommendations.
All deadlines were prespecified in the contract to avoid confusion later.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predetermine - to decide or establish something in advance
  • synonym: specify - to state something clearly and exactly
