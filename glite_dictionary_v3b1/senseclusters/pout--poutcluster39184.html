pout

verb
push out lips to show annoyance or sadness
2.6
Pronunciation: / paʊt / (AmE), / paʊt / (BrE)
to push out your lips, especially to show annoyance or disappointment

Examples

To pout in anger
He sat in the corner, pouting over his missed opportunity.
She began to pout when she didn't get her way.
The little boy pouted and refused to talk to anyone.
He tends to pout whenever things don't go his way at work.
Instead of arguing, she simply pouted and looked away.
The little girl began to pout when her brother took her toy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smile - to make a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression
  • synonym: sulk - to be silent or morose, usually out of annoyance or disappointment
