hemodilution

noun
reduced blood concentration
8.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåhiÀêmo äda…™ÀàluÀê É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåhiÀêm…ô äda…™ÀàluÀê É…ôn / (BrE)
a medical condition or process where the concentration of blood cells or hemoglobin is reduced by adding extra fluid to the bloodstream

Examples

Hemodilution during surgery
The patient experienced hemodilution after receiving large amounts of intravenous fluids during surgery.
Doctors monitor for hemodilution to ensure that blood tests accurately reflect a patient's true condition.
Excessive hemodilution can lead to misleadingly low hemoglobin levels in laboratory results.
During surgery, hemodilution can help reduce the need for blood transfusions.
The doctor explained that hemodilution lowers the risk of complications from blood loss.
Modern techniques use hemodilution to maintain blood volume while minimizing cell concentration.

Synonyms

  • antonym: hemoconcentration - an increase in the concentration of blood cells due to loss of plasma
  • synonym: hemodilute - to reduce the concentration of blood cells by adding fluid
