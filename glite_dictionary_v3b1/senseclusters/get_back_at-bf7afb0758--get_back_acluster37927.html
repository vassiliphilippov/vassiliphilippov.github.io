get back at

verb
retaliate or take revenge
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ˈbæk æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈbak at / (BrE)
to do something in order to punish or get revenge on someone who has harmed you

Examples

To get back at a rival
After being betrayed, he vowed to get back at those responsible.
She finally found a way to get back at her noisy neighbors.
They plotted to get back at the team that cheated them out of victory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense or mistake
  • synonym: retaliate - to make an attack or assault in return for a similar attack
