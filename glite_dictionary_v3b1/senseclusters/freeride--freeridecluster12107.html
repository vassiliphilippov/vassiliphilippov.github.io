freeride

adjective
for extreme riding
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌraɪd / (BrE)
designed for or relating to a style of riding that emphasizes freedom, tricks, and challenging terrain, especially in biking, skiing, or snowboarding

Examples

Freeride bike
He bought a new freeride bike built for large jumps and technical downhill sections.
The shop carries a range of freeride skis for advanced skiers who love off-piste adventures.
She prefers freeride snowboards because they handle rough terrain and big jumps better.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross-country - designed for traveling long distances over varied terrain
  • synonym: downhill - designed for descending steep slopes, especially in sports
