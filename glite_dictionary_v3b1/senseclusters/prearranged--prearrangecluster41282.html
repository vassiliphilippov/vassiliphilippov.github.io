prearranged

adjective
planned in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːəˈreɪndʒd / (AmE), / ˌpriːəˈreɪndʒd / (BrE)
arranged, planned, or decided before something happens

Examples

A prearranged meeting
The two companies held a prearranged meeting to discuss the merger.
All the questions in the interview were prearranged to avoid surprises.
The outcome of the contest seemed prearranged, disappointing many fans.
The guests arrived at the prearranged location for the surprise party.
We followed a prearranged plan to ensure everything went smoothly.
Their meeting was not accidental but carefully prearranged by both sides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: planned - decided or arranged before it happens
  • synonym: scheduled - planned to happen at a particular time
  • antonym: spontaneous - happening without planning or prearrangement
