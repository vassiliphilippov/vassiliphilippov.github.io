cheek

noun
rude or impudent remark
Pronunciation: / tʃik / (AmE), / tʃiːk / (BrE)
rude or disrespectful speech or behavior; impudence

Examples

Show cheek to adults
He answered back with surprising cheek for someone so young.
She had the cheek to tell her boss how to do his job.
You’ve got some cheek coming in late and making demands!
I can't believe he had the cheek to criticize your work in front of everyone.
It takes a lot of cheek to park in the boss's spot.
She had the cheek to ask for a raise after coming in late every day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: audacity - bold and daring behavior, often rude
  • synonym: impertinence - lack of respect; rudeness
  • synonym: impudence - rude or disrespectful behavior; boldness
  • antonym: respect - polite behavior or speech; opposite of rudeness
