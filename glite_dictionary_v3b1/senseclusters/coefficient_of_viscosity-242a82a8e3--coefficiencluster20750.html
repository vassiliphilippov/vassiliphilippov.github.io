coefficient of viscosity

noun
measure of a fluid's resistance to flow
5.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɪfɪʃənt əv vɪˈskɒsəti / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪfɪʃənt əv vɪˈskɒsɪti / (BrE)
A measure of a fluid's resistance to flow when an external force is applied.

Examples

Coefficient of viscosity of oil
Engine oil is selected based on its coefficient of viscosity.
The coefficient of viscosity for water changes with temperature.
The experiment measured the coefficient of viscosity of glycerin using a falling sphere method.
