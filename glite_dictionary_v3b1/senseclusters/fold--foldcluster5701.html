fold

verb
bend so one part covers another
Pronunciation: / foʊld / (AmE), / fəʊld / (BrE)
to bend or double something over on itself so that one part covers another part

Examples

To fold clothes
John likes to fold his clothes as soon as they come out of the dryer.
Please fold the napkin in half and place it on the table.
She neatly folded the letter before putting it into the envelope.
If you fold the map wrong, it won't fit back in your pocket.
The sofa can easily fold into a bed for guests.
When not in use, the table legs fold neatly underneath.
