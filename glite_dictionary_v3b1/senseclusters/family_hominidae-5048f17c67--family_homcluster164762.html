family Hominidae

noun
taxonomic family including humans and ancestors
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfæmɪli hoʊˈmɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli həʊˈmɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A biological family that includes modern humans and their extinct immediate ancestors.

Examples

The family Hominidae
Scientists debate whether certain ancient remains should be classified within the family Hominidae.
The evolutionary history of the family Hominidae provides insight into human origins.
The fossil was identified as belonging to the family Hominidae, which includes modern humans.

Related Terms
