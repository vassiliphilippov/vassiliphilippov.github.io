skyhook

noun
imaginary hook suspended from the sky
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌhʊk / (BrE)
a fanciful device, imagined as a hook suspended from the sky, often used humorously to refer to something impossible or absurd

Examples

An impossible skyhook
He claimed he could lift the car with a skyhook, but everyone knew it was just a trick.
In engineering jokes, a skyhook is something used to describe an impossible solution.
The mechanic sent the apprentice searching for a skyhook, knowing full well it didn't exist.
