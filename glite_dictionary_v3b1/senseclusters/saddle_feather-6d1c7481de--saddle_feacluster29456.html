saddle feather

noun
long feather on a bird's lower back
9.4 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæd.əl ˌfɛð.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl ˌfeð.ə / (BrE)
a long, narrow feather located on the back of a domestic fowl, especially near the base of the tail

Examples

Bright saddle feathers
Experienced breeders can identify certain chicken breeds by the shape and color of their saddle feathers.
The rooster’s brightly colored saddle feathers were especially impressive during the show.
When molting, chickens may lose some of their saddle feathers, affecting their appearance temporarily.
