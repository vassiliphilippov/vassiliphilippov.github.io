mudstone

noun
fine-grained rock
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌstəʊn /; / ˈmʌd.stəʊn / (BrE)
a fine-grained sedimentary rock made from compacted mud or clay particles

Examples

A mudstone sample
The geologist collected samples of mudstone from the riverbank for analysis.
Layers of mudstone can reveal important information about ancient environments.
Fossils are often preserved in mudstone because of its fine texture.
The geologist discovered a layer of mudstone beneath the surface of the field.
Fossils are sometimes found preserved in ancient mudstone deposits.
The riverbank was made up of soft mudstone that crumbled easily underfoot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock made mainly of sand-sized mineral particles
  • synonym: shale - a fine-grained sedimentary rock that splits easily into thin layers
