visual field

noun
the entire area visible to the eye at once
4.8 0.57/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…™ íu…ôl fiÀêld / (AmE), / Ààv…™ íj ä…ôl fiÀêld / (BrE)
the entire area that can be seen by the eye at one time, including central and peripheral vision, when the gaze is fixed in one direction

Examples

The visual field area
A blind spot is a region of the visual field where no image is detected.
As he turned his head, the object moved into his visual field.
Doctors test the visual field to diagnose certain eye and brain disorders.

Synonyms

    3.7 0.82/m

  • field of regard
    8.7 0.00/m
    field of regard describes the whole physical area a stationary eye can see at once, while visual field refers to central and peripheral sight.
  • field of view
    4.2 2.06/m
    field of view is the visible area through a lens or scene at one moment, while visual field is the eye's full central and peripheral sight.
