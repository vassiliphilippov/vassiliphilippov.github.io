bushel of apples

noun
a measure of apples (one bushel)
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃəl əv ˈæpəlz / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃəl əv ˈap(ə)lz / (BrE)
a quantity of apples measuring one bushel, commonly used as a unit of measurement in agriculture

Examples

A bushel of apples basket
Every autumn, the orchard sells a bushel of apples for a discounted price.
The recipe called for half a bushel of apples to make enough cider for everyone.
They picked nearly three bushels of apples during their family outing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bushel - a unit of volume used in dry measure, equal to 8 gallons (approx. 36.4 liters in the UK, 35.2 liters in the US)
