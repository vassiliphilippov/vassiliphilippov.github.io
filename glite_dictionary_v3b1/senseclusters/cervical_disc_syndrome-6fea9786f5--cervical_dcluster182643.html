cervical disc syndrome

noun
nerve root compression in the neck region
6.7 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrvɪkəl dɪsk sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪkəl dɪsk ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by compression of spinal nerve roots in the neck, characterized by neck pain, muscle weakness, and abnormal sensations.

Examples

cervical disc syndrome pain
Physical therapy is often recommended as an initial treatment for cervical disc syndrome.
Symptoms of cervical disc syndrome can include both muscular weakness and numbness in the hands.
The patient was diagnosed with cervical disc syndrome after complaining of persistent neck pain and tingling in his arms.
