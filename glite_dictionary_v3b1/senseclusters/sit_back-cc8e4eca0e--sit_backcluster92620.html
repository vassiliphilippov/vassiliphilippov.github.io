sit back

verb
settle into a relaxed sitting position
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɪt bæk / (AmE), / sɪt bak /; / sɪt bæk / (BrE)
to relax or settle into a comfortable position, often to rest or observe without taking part

Examples

To sit back and relax
After a long day, she likes to sit back and read a good book.
He sat back in his chair, looking satisfied with the results.
Just sit back and enjoy the performance; you don't need to do anything.
After finishing dinner, he liked to sit back and enjoy a good book.
Don’t just sit back—get involved if you want things to change.
Sometimes you need to sit back and watch how events unfold.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sit up - to move into or remain in a more upright seated position
  • antonym: take action - to do something in order to achieve a result
