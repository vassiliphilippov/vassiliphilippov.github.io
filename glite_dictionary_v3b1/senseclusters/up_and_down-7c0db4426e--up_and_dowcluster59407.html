up and down

adverb
alternately upward and downward
Pronunciation: / ʌp ənd daʊn / (AmE), / ʌp ənd daʊn / (BrE)
in both directions, or back and forth, often over the full length or area of something

Examples

Look up and down the ladder
He looked up and down the ladder to check for any damage.
She glanced up and down the street before crossing.
The elevator went up and down all day.
She traveled up and down the coast searching for the perfect spot.
The dog ran up and down the stairs all afternoon.
The teacher paced up and down the hallway, waiting for news.
He searched up and down for his missing wallet but couldn't find it.
She walked up and down the hallway, lost in thought.
The guards patrolled up and down the fence every hour.
