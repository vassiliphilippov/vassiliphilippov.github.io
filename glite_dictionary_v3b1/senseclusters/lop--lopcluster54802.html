lop

verb
cut off branches or parts of a plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɑp / (AmE), / lɒp / (BrE)
to cut off branches or parts of a plant or tree, usually to tidy it or help it grow

Examples

To lop broken branches
After the storm, workers lopped off all the broken branches from the trees.
It is important to lop the dead branches off the apple tree in early spring.
She had to lop several limbs to keep the hedge tidy and healthy.
He used an axe to lop off the dead branches from the tree.
She carefully lopped away the damaged leaves from her plant.
The gardener lopped the hedge to keep it neat and tidy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prune - to trim a tree, shrub, or bush by cutting away dead or overgrown branches
