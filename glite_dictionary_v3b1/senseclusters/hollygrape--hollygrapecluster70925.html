hollygrape

noun
ornamental evergreen shrub of Pacific North America
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑliˌɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌɡreɪp / (BrE)
An ornamental evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, with dark green pinnate leaves, clusters of yellow flowers, and blue-black berries.

Examples

The hollygrape shrub
Birds are attracted to the blue berries produced by the hollygrape in late summer.
The hollygrape is often planted in gardens for its attractive foliage and bright yellow flowers.
The glossy leaves of the hollygrape resemble those of true holly, though the plants are not closely related.
