brachialis

noun
upper arm muscle
Pronunciation: / brəˈkiːəlɪs / (AmE), / brəˈkɪəlɪs / (BrE)
a muscle in the upper arm that assists in bending the elbow joint

Examples

The brachialis muscle
The brachialis is located beneath the biceps and plays a key role in elbow flexion.
Injuries to the brachialis can make it difficult to lift or carry objects.
Physical therapists often target the brachialis during rehabilitation exercises for the arm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biceps - a large muscle in the upper arm that flexes the elbow
  • antonym: triceps - a muscle in the upper arm that extends the elbow
