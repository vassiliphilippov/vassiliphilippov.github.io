bribe

verb
make illegal payments for influence
4.1 4.92/m
Pronunciation: / braÉªb / (AmE), / braÉªb / (BrE)
to illegally give money or gifts to someone in authority to persuade them to do something dishonest or favorable to you

Examples

To bribe an official
He denied that he had ever bribed a public servant.
She attempted to bribe the official to gain a construction permit.
The company was accused of bribing foreign politicians to secure contracts.

Synonyms

  • buy off
    6.8 0.70/m
    buy off means giving money or a reward to stop someone acting, while bribe means illegally paying authority for dishonest favors.
  • grease someone's palm
    8.1 0.00/m
    grease someone's palm is an informal idiom for secretly giving money to an official, while bribe is the general legal term.
  • buy
    8.9 20/m
    buy is used informally to mean paying someone for influence, while bribe is the clear word for illegal payment to persuade authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupt - to make someone act dishonestly in return for money or gifts
  • antonym: extort - to obtain something by force, threats, or other unfair means
