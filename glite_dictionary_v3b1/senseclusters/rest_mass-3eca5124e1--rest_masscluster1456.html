rest mass

noun
mass of an object at rest (physics)
7.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /rest mæs/ (AmE), /rest mæs/ /; / /rest mɑːs/ (BrE)
(physics) the mass of a body as measured when it is at rest relative to an observer; an intrinsic property of matter independent of its motion or gravitational influence.

Examples

Rest mass of particle
According to Einstein's theory, energy and rest mass are equivalent.
Even if the particle accelerates, its rest mass remains unchanged.
The rest mass of an electron is a fundamental constant in physics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relativistic mass - mass of a body in motion relative to an observer
