szlachta

noun
Polish noble class
Pronunciation: / ʃˈlɑxtə /; / ˈʃlɑxtə / (AmE), / ʃˈlɑxtə /; / ˈʃlɑxtə / (BrE)
a member of the historical Polish nobility, or the noble class itself, especially in Poland and neighboring regions

Examples

The szlachta class
Her ancestor was a proud szlachta who owned several estates in the countryside.
The szlachta played a significant role in the politics of historical Poland.
Many traditions of the szlachta are still celebrated in Polish culture today.
The szlachta held significant privileges in the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth.
Many members of the szlachta owned large estates and influenced national politics.
During the partitions of Poland, the szlachta lost much of its traditional power.

  • synonym: noble - a person of high social rank by birth or title
  • antonym: peasant - a member of a low social class, usually a farmer or laborer
  • antonym: peasantry - the class of rural, agricultural laborers or small farmers
