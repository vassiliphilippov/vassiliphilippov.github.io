brackish

adjective
slightly salty (of water)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbræk.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbræk.ɪʃ / (BrE)
(of water) slightly salty, typically because it is a mixture of seawater and fresh water.

Examples

Brackish water
Fish that thrive in brackish environments can often adapt to both saltwater and freshwater.
The estuary contains brackish water where the river meets the sea.
They built a pier over the brackish lagoon, which was teeming with unique plant life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - not salty, especially of water
  • synonym: saline - containing or impregnated with salt
