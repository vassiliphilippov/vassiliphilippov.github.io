saxhorn

noun
valved brass wind instrument family
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsæksˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈsæksˌhɔːn / (BrE)
Any of a family of brass wind instruments, similar to a bugle but with valves, used primarily in brass bands.

Examples

A saxhorn instrument
Adolphe Sax invented the saxhorn, greatly influencing the sound of modern brass ensembles.
Brass bands in the 19th century frequently featured various types of saxhorns.
The musician demonstrated the deep, mellow tone of his saxhorn during the concert.
