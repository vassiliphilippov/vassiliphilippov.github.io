cherrywood

noun
wood from cherry trees
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛriˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛriˌwʊd / (BrE)
wood from cherry trees, often used in furniture, carpentry, or for smoking food

Examples

Cherrywood furniture
The dining table was made from polished cherrywood and looked elegant in the room.
They chose cherrywood for the new cabinets because of its rich color and durability.
A craftsman carefully carved the chair legs out of solid cherrywood.
The chef used cherrywood chips to give the chicken a subtle, sweet flavor.
A beautiful table made from cherrywood stood in the center of the dining room.
Many barbecue enthusiasts prefer cherrywood for its mild smoke when grilling meats.
