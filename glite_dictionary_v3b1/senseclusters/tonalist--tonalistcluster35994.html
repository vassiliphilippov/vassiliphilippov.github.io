tonalist

noun
artist using tonal gradations
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊnəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈtəʊnəlɪst / (BrE)
an artist who uses subtle gradations of tone in painting, especially associated with the Tonalism movement

Examples

Tonalist landscape painting
The museum's new exhibition highlights the work of a renowned tonalist from the early 20th century.
As a tonalist, she preferred muted colors and soft transitions in her landscapes.
Many critics praised the tonalist for his ability to capture atmosphere with gentle shifts in tone.
