fud

noun
spreading fear or doubt
9.9
Pronunciation: /fʌd/ (AmE), /fʌd/ (BrE)
informal: the spreading of fear, uncertainty, or doubt, especially as a tactic to influence people's opinions or decisions

Examples

Fud about competitors
The company spread fud about its competitor to discourage customers from switching brands.
Online forums are often full of fud when new technology is released.
He accused the media of using fud to influence public opinion during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reassurance - the act of removing fear or doubt
  • synonym: scaremongering - the act of deliberately spreading frightening or worrying information
