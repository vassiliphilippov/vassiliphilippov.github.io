sleuth

noun
detective; investigator
5.6 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / sluÀêŒ∏ / (AmE), / sluÀêŒ∏ / (BrE)
A detective, especially one who investigates and follows clues to solve crimes.

Examples

A sleuth solves mysteries
As an amateur sleuth, she loved reading mystery novels and solving puzzles.
The famous sleuth solved the case with his remarkable attention to detail.
The police brought in their best sleuth to follow the trail left by the thief.

Synonyms

  • detective
    2.7 54/m
    a detective is a professional who follows evidence and solves crimes, while a sleuth often feels more informal and clever.
  • investigator
    3.4 35/m
    an investigator is an official who examines evidence in formal inquiries, while a sleuth suggests someone who follows clues more privately or playfully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes and catch criminals
