quinella

noun
bet on top two finishers
9.9
Pronunciation: /kwɪ.ˈne.lə/ (AmE), /kwɪ.ˈne.lə/ (BrE)
a bet in which you choose the first and second place finishers in a race, in any order

Examples

A quinella bet
He placed a quinella on the two fastest horses in the race.
Winning the quinella paid out more than he expected at the track.
She studied the odds before choosing her quinella for the big event.
He won a large payout after his quinella bet on the horse race was successful.
Many gamblers prefer a quinella because it does not require picking the exact order of finishers.
She placed a quinella on horses three and five at the racetrack.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exacta - a bet predicting the first and second place finishers in exact order
  • degree higher: trifecta - a bet predicting the first, second, and third place finishers in exact order
