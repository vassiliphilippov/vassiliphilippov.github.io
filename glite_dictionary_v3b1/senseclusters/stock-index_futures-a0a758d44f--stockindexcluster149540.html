stock-index futures

noun
futures contract based on a stock index
6.7
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈɪnˌdɛks ˈfjʊtʃɚz / (AmE), / stɒk ˈɪndɛks ˈfjuːtʃəz / (BrE)
A futures contract whose value is based on the price of a particular stock index, allowing investors to speculate on or hedge against future movements in the broader market.

Examples

Stock-index futures contract
Many investors use stock-index futures to hedge their portfolios against market volatility.
The value of stock-index futures rose sharply ahead of the economic report.
Traders monitor stock-index futures each morning to gauge possible market openings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stock index - a statistical measure of the performance of a group of stocks
