bust

verb
make a sudden police raid
3.3
Pronunciation: / bʌst / (AmE), / bʌst / (BrE)
for the police to raid a place or arrest someone, especially suddenly

Examples

Police bust a gang
Officers busted the warehouse after receiving a tip about stolen goods.
The police decided to bust the illegal gambling den last night.
They were caught off guard when the authorities busted into the apartment.
The police busted the gang during a late-night raid.
He was busted for cheating on the exam after the teacher caught him.
They were busted trying to sneak into the concert without tickets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrest - to take someone into custody by legal authority
  • synonym: catch - to capture or seize someone doing something wrong
  • synonym: raid - to make a sudden attack on a place, often by police or military
  • antonym: release - to set someone free from custody
