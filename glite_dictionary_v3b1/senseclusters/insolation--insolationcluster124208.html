insolation

noun
sudden collapse from sun or heat
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
sudden physical collapse caused by exposure to intense sunlight or excessive heat

Examples

Insolation heatstroke
Doctors attributed several cases of fainting at the festival to insolation caused by extreme heat.
The hiker suffered from insolation after hours of walking under the intense summer sun.
Without adequate shade, children at the camp were at risk of insolation.
