perviousness

noun
quality of being penetrable
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úr.vi.…ôs.n…™s / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀê.vi.…ôs.n…™s / (BrE)
The quality of allowing something, such as people, light, or substances, to pass through or penetrate.

Examples

High perviousness material
Because of its high perviousness, the glass allowed sunlight to flood the room.
Engineers tested the perviousness of the soil to determine how water would flow through it.
The perviousness of the fabric made it unsuitable for waterproof clothing.

Synonyms
