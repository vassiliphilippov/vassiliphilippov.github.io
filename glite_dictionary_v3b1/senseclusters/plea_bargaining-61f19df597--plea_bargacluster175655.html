plea bargaining

noun
negotiated agreement to plead guilty to a lesser charge
8.6 3.7
Pronunciation: /pliː ˈbɑːr.gən.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /pliː ˈbɑː.gən.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
(Law) The process by which a defendant and prosecutor negotiate an agreement where the defendant pleads guilty to a lesser charge in exchange for concessions from the prosecutor, such as the dismissal of more serious charges.

Examples

Plea bargaining agreement
Plea bargaining is commonly used to resolve criminal cases before they go to court.
Public opinion about plea bargaining varies, with some viewing it as a practical necessity and others as undermining justice.
The defense attorney recommended plea bargaining to avoid a lengthy trial.
