Arawn

proper noun
Celtic god; lord of Annwfn
Pronunciation: / əˈrɑːn / (AmE), / əˈraʊn / (BrE)
In Celtic mythology, the lord of Annwfn, the otherworld or land of fairies.

Examples

Arawn of Annwfn
In Welsh legend, Arawn is known as the ruler of the otherworld, Annwfn.
Some modern fantasy writers draw inspiration from the figure of Arawn in their work.
The story of Pwyll and Arawn is a central tale in the Mabinogion.
