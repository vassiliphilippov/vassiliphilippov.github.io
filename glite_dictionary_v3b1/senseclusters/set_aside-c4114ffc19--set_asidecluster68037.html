(law) to annul or revoke a legal decision or judgment, making it no longer valid.

Examples

Court sets aside verdict
If the appeal is successful, the court may set aside the conviction.
The judge decided to set aside the earlier ruling due to new evidence.
The lawyer filed a motion to set aside the verdict on procedural grounds.

Synonyms

  • vacate
    6.6 0.77/m
    vacate is what a court does to cancel a judgment or order, while set aside names the act of annulling that judgment.
  • rescind
    7.9 2.09/m
    rescind is a formal act that cancels a law, order, or contract, while set aside refers specifically to annulling a court judgment.
  • revoke
    6.2 3.96/m
    revoke is to take back or end the effect of a law, license, or right, while set aside annuls a court judgment.
