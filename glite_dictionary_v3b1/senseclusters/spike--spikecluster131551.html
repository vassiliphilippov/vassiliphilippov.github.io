spike

noun
type of flower cluster (botany)
3.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /spaɪk/ (AmE), /spaɪk/ (BrE)
a type of flowering or fruiting stalk in plants, where flowers or seeds are attached directly to the main stem

Examples

A flower spike
Botanists examined the structure of the spike to identify the grass species.
In wheat, each spike consists of numerous closely packed, sessile florets.
The lavender plant displays a beautiful spike of purple flowers each summer.
Each ear of corn starts as a green spike emerging from the stalk.
The length and density of the spike affect the crop's overall yield.
The wheat spike contains multiple grains tightly packed along the stem.
