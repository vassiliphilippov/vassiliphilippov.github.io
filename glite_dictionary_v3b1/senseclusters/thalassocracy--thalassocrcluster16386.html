thalassocracy

noun
sea-based power
9.9
Pronunciation: /θə.læs.ə.ˈkrɑː.siː/ /; / /θæl.ə.sə.ˈkrɑː.siː/ (AmE), /θə.læs.ə.ˈkrɒs.iː/ /; / /θæl.ə.sə.ˈkrɒs.iː/ (BrE)
a state, society, or system whose power and influence are based on control of the sea and maritime trade

Examples

A thalassocracy state
The Phoenicians established a thalassocracy that dominated Mediterranean trade routes for centuries.
Venice was a powerful thalassocracy during the Middle Ages, controlling much of the Adriatic Sea.
Historians often compare the British Empire to earlier thalassocracies because of its naval dominance.
Ancient Athens was famous for its thalassocracy in the Mediterranean region.
The rise of the British Empire marked a new era of thalassocracy in world history.
Losing control of key ports weakened their thalassocracy and influence over trade routes.
