unbound

adjective
not restrained or tied down
4.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbaʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbaʊnd / (BrE)
not restrained or tied down; free from physical or figurative bonds.

Examples

Unbound spirit
After years of captivity, the once-caged bird was finally unbound and soared into the sky.
His imagination seemed unbound by conventional limits, leading to extraordinary creations.
The prisoners were unbound and allowed to walk freely after the peace agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bound - restrained or tied down; fastened
  • synonym: free - not under the control or power of another; able to act or be as one wishes
