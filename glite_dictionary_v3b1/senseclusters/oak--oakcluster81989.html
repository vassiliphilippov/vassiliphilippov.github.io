oak

noun
a tree with lobed leaves and acorns
Pronunciation: / oʊk / (AmE), / əʊk / (BrE)
A large deciduous or evergreen tree of the genus Quercus, typically having lobed leaves and bearing acorns.

Examples

An oak tree
Many traditional barrels are made from oak wood, prized for its strength and flavor.
The forest was composed mainly of towering oak and beech trees.
The old oak in the garden provides shade during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ash - a deciduous tree with pinnate leaves and winged seeds
  • antonym: maple - a deciduous tree with lobed leaves, known for its sap and wood
