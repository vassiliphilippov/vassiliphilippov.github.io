disestablish

verb
deprive a church of official state status
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ /; / ˌdɪsɪˈstæblɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ /; / ˌdɪsɪˈstæblɪʃ / (BrE)
to remove the official status or government support of a church or other organization

Examples

To disestablish the church
He argued that to disestablish the church would create religious equality.
Reformers campaigned to disestablish the state-supported church.
The government voted to disestablish the Church of England in Wales.
The government voted to disestablish the national church after years of debate.
Several countries have disestablished their official religions in the past century.
Activists campaigned to disestablish the organization’s privileged legal status.

  • antonym: establish - to set up an organization or system on a permanent basis
