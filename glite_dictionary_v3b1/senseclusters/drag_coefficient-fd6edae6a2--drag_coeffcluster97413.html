drag coefficient

noun
measure of aerodynamic resistance
7.3 6.4
Pronunciation: /dræg koʊ.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ /; / /dræg koʊ.ɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ (AmE), /dræg kəʊ.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ /; / /dræg kəʊ.ɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ (BrE)
A dimensionless number representing the ratio of the drag force acting on an object moving through a fluid to the product of the fluid density, the object's reference area, and the square of the velocity. It measures how easily an object moves through air or another fluid.

Examples

Low drag coefficient
A low drag coefficient is essential for aircraft to achieve high speeds with minimal power.
Changes in the shape of the building helped lower its drag coefficient against strong winds.
The designer worked to reduce the drag coefficient of the new sports car for better fuel efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerodynamic drag - resistance experienced by a body moving through air
