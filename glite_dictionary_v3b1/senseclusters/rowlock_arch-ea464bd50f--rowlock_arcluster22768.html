rowlock arch

noun
arch with multiple rows of wedge-shaped stones
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.lɑk ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.lɒk ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch built with more than one concentric row of wedge-shaped stones (voussoirs).

Examples

A rowlock arch design
Masons carefully constructed the rowlock arch using alternating colors of brick for visual interest.
Restoring the old bridge required rebuilding the damaged rowlock arch to match the original design.
The historic building features a decorative rowlock arch above its main entrance.
