deportable

adjective
subject to removal
6.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpɔːrtəbl / (AmE), / dɪˈpɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be legally removed from a country by government authorities

Examples

Deportable immigrants
After his visa expired, he became deportable under immigration law.
The court ruled that the individual was deportable due to violations of his residency permit.
If you commit certain crimes, you may be considered deportable by the authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expellable - able to be forced to leave a place or country
  • antonym: inadmissible - not allowed to enter or stay in a country
