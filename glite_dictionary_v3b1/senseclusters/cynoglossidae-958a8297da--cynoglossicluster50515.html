Cynoglossidae

noun
family of flatfishes (tonguefishes)
Pronunciation: / saɪnəˈɡlɒsɪdi / (AmE), / saɪnəˈɡlɒsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of flatfishes known as tonguefishes, characterized by their elongated bodies and asymmetrical, flattened shape, found chiefly in warm coastal waters.

Examples

The Cynoglossidae family
Marine biologists identified a new species within the Cynoglossidae family during their recent expedition.
Members of the Cynoglossidae family are commonly called tonguefishes due to their tongue-shaped bodies.
The Cynoglossidae are primarily found in tropical and subtropical marine environments.
