knifelike

adjective
having a sharp or distinct edge or shape
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪfˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈnaɪf.laɪk / (BrE)
having a sharp, thin, or distinct edge or shape resembling that of a knife

Examples

Knifelike edge
Her words had a knifelike edge that made him wince.
The cold wind cut through the valley with a knifelike intensity.
The mountain ridge stood out in a knifelike silhouette against the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blunt - having a dull edge or point
  • synonym: sharp - having a fine or keen edge or point
