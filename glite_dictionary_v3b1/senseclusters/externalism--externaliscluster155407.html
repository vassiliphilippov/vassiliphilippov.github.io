externalism

noun
moral motivation theory
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɜrnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that moral judgments do not necessarily motivate action without an independent desire or external factor.

Examples

Moral externalism
In her philosophy class, she argued for externalism in the debate about moral motivation.
Many contemporary ethicists discuss externalism when considering how people are moved to act morally.
The professor explained that externalism requires an outside desire to turn moral beliefs into action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: internalism - the theory that moral judgments themselves motivate action
