gray matter

noun
brain tissue composed of nerve cell bodies
9.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /greɪ ˈmæt.ər/ (AmE), /greɪ ˈmæt.ə/ (BrE)
The darker tissue of the brain and spinal cord, consisting mainly of nerve cell bodies and branching fibers, forming the cerebral cortex and other nerve centers; also used informally to mean intelligence or brainpower.
