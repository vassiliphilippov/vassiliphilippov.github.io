Prakrit

noun
ancient vernacular Indic language(s)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈprɑː.krɪt/ (AmE), /ˈprɑː.krɪt/ (BrE)
Any of several ancient vernacular Indic languages of northern and central India, used from the 3rd century BC to the 4th century AD, and distinguished from Sanskrit.

Examples

Prakrit languages
Linguists study Prakrit texts to understand the evolution of modern Indo-Aryan languages.
Many ancient Indian plays feature dialogue in Prakrit to reflect everyday speech.
The Jain manuscripts were originally written in Prakrit rather than Sanskrit.
