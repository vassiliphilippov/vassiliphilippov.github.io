go bad

verb
stop functioning or fail (machines, systems)
2.3 3.0
Pronunciation: /goʊ bæd/ (AmE), /gəʊ bæd/ (BrE)
(of a machine or system) to stop operating or functioning; to break down or fail.

Examples

The engine goes bad
After ten years of use, the motor finally went bad and had to be replaced.
If your computer begins to go bad, you might lose important files.
The dishwasher started to go bad and eventually stopped working altogether.

Related Terms

  • synonym: give out - to stop functioning, especially gradually or after a period of use (machines, systems)
  • synonym: malfunction - to fail to function properly
