Secale cereale

noun
cereal grass, also called rye
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkeɪli səˈriːəleɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈkeɪli sɛˈriːəliː / (BrE)
a hardy annual cereal grass cultivated for its edible grain, especially in northern Europe and North America; also known as rye.

Examples

Secale cereale grain
Secale cereale is widely grown in Europe for its grain, which is used to make bread and whiskey.
Farmers often plant Secale cereale as a cover crop to improve soil quality.
The cold-resistant nature of Secale cereale makes it suitable for cultivation in northern climates.
