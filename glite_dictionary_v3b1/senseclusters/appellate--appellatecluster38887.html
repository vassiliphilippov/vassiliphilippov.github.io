appellate

adjective
relating to legal appeals
7.9 9.1
Pronunciation: / əˈpɛlət / (AmE), / əˈpɛlət / (BrE)
relating to appeals or the process of appealing, especially in a legal context

Examples

Appellate court
She specializes in appellate law and often works on appeal cases.
The case was heard by an appellate court after the initial verdict was challenged.
The judge cited several appellate rulings to support her decision.
Decisions made by lower courts can be challenged in an appellate court.
He specializes in appellate law, focusing on cases that are being appealed to higher courts.
The appellate court overturned the original verdict after reviewing the case.

  • antonym: trial - hearing and judging a legal case at first instance
