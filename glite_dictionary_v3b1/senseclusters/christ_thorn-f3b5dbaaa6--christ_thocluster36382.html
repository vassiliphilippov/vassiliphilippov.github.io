Christ thorn

noun
spiny Madagascan shrub; Euphorbia milii
Pronunciation: / kraɪst θɔrn / (AmE), / kraɪst θɔːn / (BrE)
a spiny shrub native to Madagascar, with long woody stems, few leaves, and small flowers with scarlet bracts, often grown as a houseplant and sometimes called crown-of-thorns

Examples

The Christ thorn shrub
Gardeners often propagate Christ thorn from cuttings for indoor decoration.
She carefully watered the Christ thorn on her windowsill, avoiding its sharp spines.
The Christ thorn is popular as an ornamental plant due to its colorful bracts.
