Ptolemy II Philadelphus

proper noun
king of Egypt, son of Ptolemy I
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.lə.miː ðə ˈsek.ənd fɪ.ˈlæd.əl.fəs/ /; / /ˈtɑː.lə.miː tuː fɪ.ˈlæd.əl.fəs/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.lə.miː ðə ˈsek.ənd fɪ.ˈlæd.əl.fəs/ /; / /ˈtɒ.lə.miː tuː fɪ.ˈlæd.əl.fəs/ (BrE)
the son of Ptolemy I and king of Egypt from circa 283–246 BC, known for commissioning the translation of the Hebrew Bible into Greek, the Septuagint

Examples

Ptolemy II Philadelphus king Egypt
Ptolemy II Philadelphus ruled Egypt during a period of cultural and economic prosperity.
Historians often credit Ptolemy II Philadelphus with expanding the Library of Alexandria.
The translation of the Septuagint was traditionally attributed to Ptolemy II Philadelphus.
