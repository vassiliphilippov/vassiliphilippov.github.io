loosen up

verb
become more relaxed or less tense
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsən ʌp /; / ˈlusən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ʌp / (BrE)
to relax or become less tense, stressed, anxious, or formal; to help someone do this

Examples

To loosen up after work
After a long week at work, I just want to loosen up and watch a movie.
She found it hard to loosen up in front of the camera at first.
The coach told the players to loosen up before the big game.
A couple of jokes helped everyone loosen up before the meeting started.
She always loosens up after a glass of wine.
You need to loosen up and stop worrying about every little detail.
It took Ben a while to loosen up and join the conversation at the party.
The atmosphere was stiff at first, but everyone began to loosen up after a few jokes.
Yoga classes can help you loosen up after a stressful day.
