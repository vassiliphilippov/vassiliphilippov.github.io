disproportionate

adjective
out of proportion; unbalanced
5.9 5.30/m

too large or too small in comparison with something else; not in correct or balanced proportion

Examples

A disproportionate number
A disproportionate number of resources were allocated to a single department.
The costs grew disproportionate to the project's actual needs.
The punishment seemed disproportionate to the minor offense he committed.
The media gave disproportionate attention to a relatively small issue.

They allocated a disproportionate amount of resources to one department, neglecting the others.

Synonyms

  • disproportional
    6.7 0.12/m
    disproportional is a less common variant seen in some texts, while disproportionate is the usual adjective for imbalance.
  • out of proportion
    4.6 1.87/m
    out of proportion describes something exaggerated or extreme in a situation, while disproportionate names an incorrect size or balance.
  • incommensurate
    9.9 0.09/m
    incommensurate sounds formal and fits measurements or values, while disproportionate covers general size or degree mismatch.
  • misproportioned
    7.4 0.01/m
    misproportioned often describes badly shaped objects or bodies, while disproportionate focuses on size or degree compared to something else.
  • imbalanced
    4.6 0.49/m
    imbalanced implies lack of balance or stability, while disproportionate highlights an incorrect relation in size or amount.
  • undue
    6.0 4.32/m
    undue means more than is fair or necessary, while disproportionate points to a wrong size or degree compared with something else.
