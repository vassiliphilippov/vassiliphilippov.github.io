scruples

noun
moral or ethical doubts or reservations
7.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàskruÀêp…ôlz / (AmE), / ÀàskruÀêplz / (BrE)
feelings of doubt or hesitation that arise from moral or ethical considerations

Examples

Moral scruples
Despite her scruples, she went along with the risky plan.
He had no scruples about lying to get what he wanted.
Her scruples prevented her from accepting the bribe.

Synonyms

  • scruple
    8.4 1.02/m
    scruple names a single moral doubt about an action, while scruples refers to many small moral doubts guiding a person's choices.
  • qualm
    8.4 2.30/m
    qualm is a sudden uneasy feeling about doing something, while scruples are moral doubts that guide a person's choice.
  • compunction
    9.6 0.65/m
    compunction is a sharp guilty feeling that can stop someone from acting, while scruples are broader moral doubts and hesitations.
  • misgiving
    5.9 2.48/m
    misgiving is a worried doubt about the outcome or wisdom of an action, while scruples are moral hesitations about rightness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compunction - a feeling of guilt or moral scruple that prevents or follows bad action
  • synonym: conscience - a person's moral sense of right and wrong, guiding their behavior
  • synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • antonym: ruthless - having or showing no pity or compassion for others
