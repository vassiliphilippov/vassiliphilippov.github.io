rearticulation

noun
expressing again differently
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɑːrtɪkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɑːtɪkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing or forming something again in a new or different way

Examples

Rearticulation of goals
The committee called for a rearticulation of the company's mission to reflect its new values.
After receiving feedback, the author began the rearticulation of several key arguments in the essay.
The rearticulation of the plan helped everyone understand the new direction more clearly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restatement - the act of stating something again in a different way
  • synonym: revision - the process of changing or improving something
