fishway

noun
structure for fish passage
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃˌweɪ / (BrE)
a structure built in or around barriers in rivers or streams to allow fish to pass upstream or downstream

Examples

A fishway structure
The new fishway was designed to help trout migrate past the hydroelectric dam.
Engineers studied the river to determine the best location for a fishway.
Without a fishway, many species would struggle to reach their spawning grounds upstream.
The new fishway allows salmon to swim upstream past the hydroelectric dam.
Engineers designed a fishway to help restore the river's natural fish population.
Without a fishway, many species would be unable to complete their migration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dam - a barrier built to block or control the flow of water in a river
  • synonym: fish ladder - a series of steps or pools that help fish bypass obstacles in rivers
