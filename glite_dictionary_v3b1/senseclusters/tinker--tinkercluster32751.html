tinker

noun
itinerant repairer of metal utensils
3.1 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪŋ.kər/ (AmE), /ˈtɪŋ.kə/ (BrE)
a person who repairs, adjusts, or experiments with mechanical things, especially in an informal or amateur way (historically, a traveling repairer of pots and pans)

Examples

A tinker fixes pots
In the nineteenth century, a tinker might knock on your door offering to repair your broken cookware.
Stories about the life of a travelling tinker are common in British folklore.
The old tinker wandered from village to village with his tools slung over his shoulder.
As a child, he was a tinker who loved taking apart radios to see how they worked.
Many successful inventors started as mere tinkers, driven by curiosity about how things function.
The garage became a paradise for the amateur tinker and his many gadgets.
