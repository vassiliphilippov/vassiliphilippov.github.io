gladius

noun
Roman short sword
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlædiəs / (BrE)
a short sword used by Roman soldiers in ancient times

Examples

Roman gladius
The Roman soldier drew his gladius before the battle.
A gladius was essential equipment for every legionary in the Roman army.
Historians have found many examples of the gladius in ancient ruins.
