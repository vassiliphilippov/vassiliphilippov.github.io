Lepus europaeus

noun
species of large hare (European hare)
Pronunciation: / ˈliːpəs ˌjʊəroʊˈpeɪəs / (AmE), / ˈliːpəs ˌjʊərəˈpiːəs / (BrE)
a large species of hare native to Europe and parts of Asia, now also found in North America, known for not turning white in winter

Examples

The Lepus europaeus hare
Scientists are studying the adaptation of Lepus europaeus in its newly introduced North American habitats.
The Lepus europaeus is commonly seen in open fields across much of Europe.
Unlike some other hares, Lepus europaeus does not change its fur color to white during winter.
