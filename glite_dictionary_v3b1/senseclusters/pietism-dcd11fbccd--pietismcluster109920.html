Pietism

noun
German Lutheran movement emphasizing personal piety
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpaɪətɪzəm / (BrE)
A movement within the Lutheran Church in Germany during the 17th and 18th centuries that emphasized personal piety, devotion, and a more heartfelt practice of faith over formal doctrine.

Examples

Pietism movement in Germany
His interest in Pietism led him to focus on personal religious experience rather than strict adherence to church doctrine.
Many scholars attribute the rise of modern evangelical movements to the principles of Pietism.
The influence of Pietism transformed Lutheran worship practices in 18th-century Germany.

  • synonym: piety - devotion to religious duties and practices
