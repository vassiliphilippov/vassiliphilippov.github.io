leach energy

verb
to drain energy or morale
Pronunciation: / liːtʃ ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / liːtʃ ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
to gradually drain someone or something of energy or enthusiasm

Examples

To leach energy slowly
After hours of arguing, she felt the conflict had leached energy from her spirit.
Constant negativity can leach energy from even the most optimistic team.
These tedious meetings always leach energy out of the entire department.

Related Terms

  • antonym: invigorate - to give energy or strength to
  • synonym: sap - to gradually weaken or drain (energy, strength, etc.)
