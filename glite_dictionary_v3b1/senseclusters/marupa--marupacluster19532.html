marupa

noun
Amazon valley timber tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈruːpə / (AmE), / məˈruːpə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to the Amazon valley, known for its light, brittle timber that is resistant to insect attack.

Examples

Marupa wood
Exporters prize marupa wood for making lightweight furniture and interior panels.
In the Amazon valley, marupa trees are often found along riverbanks.
The locals built their huts using marupa because of its resistance to insects.
