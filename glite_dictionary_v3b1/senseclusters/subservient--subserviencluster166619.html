subservient

adjective
excessively obedient to authority
7.6 1.03/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôbÀàs…úr.vi.…ônt /; / s…ôbÀàs…ùÀê.vi.…ônt / (AmE), / s…ôbÀàs…úÀê.vi.…ônt / (BrE)
too willing to obey or serve others; excessively obedient or servile

Examples

Subservient worker
He disliked working under managers who preferred subservient employees to independent thinkers.
Journalists must avoid being subservient to government interests if they want to remain credible.
She adopted a subservient manner in hopes of pleasing her superiors.
He refused to play the subservient role any longer in the relationship.
Her subservient attitude made it easy for her manager to take advantage of her.
The servant delivered his message in a subservient tone, bowing deeply as he left.

Synonyms

  • servile
    7.4 0.30/m
    servile is more extreme and grovelling in tone, while subservient means overly ready to obey or serve.
  • obsequious
    9.3 0.48/m
    obsequious flatters and seeks favor in an obvious, insincere way, while subservient simply obeys or serves too much.
  • slavish
    7.9 0.50/m
    slavish copies or follows without thought, often killing creativity, while subservient focuses on strong obedience or service.
  • submissive
    5.1 2.13/m
    submissive yields or agrees quietly in many situations, while subservient suggests an excessive, needy readiness to obey or serve.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assertive - confident and self-assured; not submissive
  • antonym: defiant - openly resisting authority or control
  • synonym: obedient - willing to obey
  • synonym: servile - having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • synonym: slavish - showing no originality; blindly imitative or submissive
