wave angle

noun
angle of radio wave relative to antenna array
1.0
Pronunciation: / weɪv ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / weɪv ˈæŋɡl / (BrE)
the angle at which a radio wave arrives at or departs from an antenna array, measured with respect to the axis of the array

Examples

Radio wave angle
Accurate measurement of the wave angle is essential for precise localization of radio signals.
By analyzing the wave angle, technicians can reduce interference between multiple transmitters.
The engineer adjusted the antenna to optimize reception based on the wave angle.
