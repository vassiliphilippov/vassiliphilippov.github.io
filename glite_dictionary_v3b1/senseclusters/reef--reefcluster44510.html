reef

verb
roll up part of a sail
6.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /riːf/ (AmE), /riːf/ (BrE)
to reduce the area of a sail by rolling or folding part of it and securing it, especially in strong winds

Examples

To reef the sail
Before the storm hit, she had to reef both sails for safety.
He reefed the jib to make the boat easier to handle in rough seas.
The sailors quickly reefed the mainsail as the wind picked up.
Before the storm hit, the crew decided to reef the mainsail for safety.
It's important to reef early when winds start picking up at sea.
The captain shouted to the team, 'Quickly reef the sails before we capsize!'
He learned how to reef the mainsail during his first sailing lesson.
The captain ordered the crew to reef the sails as the wind picked up.
We had to reef quickly to avoid being overpowered by the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furl - to roll up and secure a sail
  • antonym: unreef - to restore a sail to full size by undoing the reef
