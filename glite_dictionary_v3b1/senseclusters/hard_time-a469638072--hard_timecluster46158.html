hard time

noun
period of difficulty
Pronunciation: / hɑrd taɪm / (AmE), / hɑːd taɪm / (BrE)
a period when someone experiences difficulty, trouble, or hardship

Examples

Having a hard time
He gave me a hard time about missing the meeting, but I explained my situation.
She had a hard time adjusting to her new job, but eventually things improved.
We're going through a hard time financially, but we're confident it will get better.
After losing his job, he went through a hard time for several months.
Many people have a hard time adjusting to new environments.
She admitted that finals week was always a hard time for her.

  • antonym: easy time - a period with no significant difficulty
