Indianization

noun
spread of Indian culture
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndiənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndiənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more Indian in character, culture, or composition, often by spreading Indian culture or replacing foreign elements

Examples

The Indianization process
The Indianization of Southeast Asia is evident in the region's ancient temples and religious practices.
Historians debate the extent of Indianization in early Cambodian society.
Through trade and migration, Indianization influenced local languages and art forms.
The Indianization of the festival brought traditional music and dance to the community.
Many restaurants underwent Indianization to appeal to local tastes and preferences.
The curriculum's Indianization included more content about Indian history and literature.
The Indianization of the education system aimed to reflect local values and traditions.
After independence, Indianization became a priority in government institutions.
Many companies encouraged the Indianization of their management teams to better serve the local market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acculturation - the process of cultural change and adaptation
  • synonym: indigenization - the process of making something more native or local
  • antonym: westernization - the adoption of Western ideas or culture
