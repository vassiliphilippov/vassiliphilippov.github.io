Macaca

noun
genus of Old World monkeys
Pronunciation: / məˈkɑːkə / (AmE), / məˈkɑːkə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys that includes macaques, such as the rhesus monkey and the crab-eating macaque.

Examples

Macaca monkey species
Researchers observed behavioral patterns among several Macaca species in the wild.
The crab-eating macaque, a member of the Macaca genus, is found throughout Southeast Asia.
The genus Macaca includes widely known species used in scientific research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macaque - any monkey of the genus Macaca
  • synonym: rhesus monkey - a species of macaque (Macaca mulatta)
