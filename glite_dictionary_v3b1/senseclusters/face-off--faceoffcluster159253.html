face-off

noun
ice hockey method of starting play
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪs ˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs ˌɒf / (BrE)
(in ice hockey and similar sports) the method of starting play, in which a referee drops the puck between two opposing players.

Examples

Hockey face-off
After every goal, there is another face-off to restart the game.
She won the face-off and quickly passed the puck to her teammate.
The game began with a face-off at center ice.
