Lancasterian

noun
supporter of monitor method
Pronunciation: / ˌlæŋkəˈstɪəriən / (AmE), / ˌlæŋkəˈstɪəriən / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices the Lancasterian method of education, which uses student monitors to teach others

Examples

A Lancasterian teacher
The school hired a Lancasterian to implement the new peer-teaching program.
As a dedicated Lancasterian, she trained older students to help instruct their classmates.
In the 1800s, many educators identified as Lancasterians and promoted the monitorial system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bell system - an educational method similar to the Lancasterian system, developed by Andrew Bell
  • synonym: monitorial - relating to a system where older students teach younger ones
