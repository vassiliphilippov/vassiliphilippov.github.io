histrionic

adjective
overly theatrical or dramatic

Excessively dramatic or theatrical in manner or style; relating to acting or stage performances, often with artificial or affected behavior.

Examples

A histrionic gesture
During the meeting, she became histrionic, waving her arms dramatically to make her point.
He dismissed her complaints as histrionic exaggerations, saying she always overreacted.
The actor's histrionic performance was both captivating and over the top.

Synonyms

  • stagey
    8.7 0.06/m
    stagey is artificial and seems meant for the stage, while histrionic is formal-sounding but also overly dramatic and affected.
  • overdramatic
    4.9 0.29/m
    overdramatic is openly exaggerated and emotional, while histrionic emphasizes theatrical, affected show and sometimes stage-like behavior.
  • melodramatic
    5.6 1.95/m
    melodramatic shows strong, over-the-top emotion like in soap scenes, while histrionic focuses on acting style and affected manner.
  • theatrical
    4.8 6.98/m
    theatrical can mean simply related to the theater or showy acting, while histrionic implies excessive, attention-seeking dramatic behavior.
  • theatric
    6.7 0.01/m
    theatric refers to theater or a dramatic style but sounds less common and more formal, while histrionic means overly affected and showy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural - not affected; genuine or casual in manner
  • synonym: theatrical - exaggerated and dramatic, like in theater
