thiobacteria

noun
sulfur-oxidizing bacteria genus
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪ.oʊ.bækˈtɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌθʌɪ.əʊ.bækˈtɪə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
Bacteria of the genus Thiobacillus, known for oxidizing sulfur compounds.

Examples

Thiobacteria oxidize sulfur
Thiobacteria play a significant role in the cycling of sulfur in the environment.
Researchers studied thiobacteria to understand how they contribute to soil fertility.
The presence of thiobacteria can accelerate the breakdown of certain inorganic compounds.
