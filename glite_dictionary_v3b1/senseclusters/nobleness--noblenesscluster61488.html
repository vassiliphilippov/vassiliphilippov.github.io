nobleness

noun
moral or intellectual elevation
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊb(ə)lnəs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊb(ə)lnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having an elevated mind, character, or ideals, especially showing moral excellence or dignity

Examples

The nobleness of character
He was admired for both the nobleness of his intentions and his unwavering honesty.
Her nobleness shone through in the way she treated everyone with respect and fairness.
In times of crisis, true nobleness is revealed by one's actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: baseness - lack of moral principles; the opposite of elevated character
  • synonym: nobility - the quality of being noble in character, mind, birth, or rank
