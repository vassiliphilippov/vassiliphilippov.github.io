serve (someone) right

verb
to be deserved as punishment or consequence
3.6
Pronunciation: / sɝv ˈsʌmwʌn raɪt / (AmE), / sɜːv ˈsʌmwən raɪt / (BrE)
(of a punishment or unpleasant result) to be deserved because of a person's own actions or behaviour.

Examples

Serves him right
He lied repeatedly, so it serves him right that nobody trusts him now.
If she gets a parking ticket, it will serve her right for parking illegally.
If the boss gets in trouble, it will serve her right for being dishonest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deserve - to be worthy of or qualified for something based on actions
