paregmenon

noun
literary device using related words
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛɡmɪˌnɑn / (AmE), / pəˈrɛɡmɪnən / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which words with the same root are used together in close proximity.

Examples

A paregmenon example
The phrase 'live a long life' is a classic example of paregmenon.
Using paregmenon, the author linked 'judge' and 'judgment' within the same sentence.
Writers often employ paregmenon to create a poetic or emphatic effect in their prose.
