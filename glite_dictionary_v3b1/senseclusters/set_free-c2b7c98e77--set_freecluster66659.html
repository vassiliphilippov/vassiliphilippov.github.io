set free

verb
release from captivity or restriction
Pronunciation: / sɛt fri / (AmE), / sɛt friː / (BrE)
to release someone or something from confinement, captivity, or restriction; to grant freedom to

Examples

To set free prisoners
After months of negotiation, the government finally set free all of the hostages.
The bird was injured but eventually recovered and was set free in the wild.
The court ordered the wrongly accused man to be set free immediately.

Related Terms

  • antonym: imprison - to put or keep someone in prison or captivity
  • synonym: liberate - to set free, especially from confinement or oppression
  • synonym: release - to allow someone or something to go free
