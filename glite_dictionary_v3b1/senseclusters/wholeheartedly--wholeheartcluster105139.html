wholeheartedly

adverb
with complete sincerity or commitment
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊlˈhɑrtɪdli / (AmE), / ˌhəʊlˈhɑːtɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that shows complete sincerity, enthusiasm, or commitment; without any reservation or holding back

Examples

To wholeheartedly agree
I wholeheartedly agree with your view on education reform.
She wholeheartedly supports her team's decision and has no doubts about it.
They wholeheartedly embraced the new project, eager to see it succeed.
