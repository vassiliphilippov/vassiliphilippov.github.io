trustbuster

noun
a federal agent breaking up monopolies
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌstˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌstˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a federal official or agent who seeks to break up corporate trusts and monopolies, especially by enforcing antitrust laws

Examples

A trustbuster agent
During the early 20th century, a trustbuster would investigate and prosecute companies violating antitrust laws.
Many progressives supported the actions of the trustbuster in breaking up monopolies.
The president was known as a trustbuster for his efforts to regulate big business.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antitrust - relating to laws that oppose monopolies and promote competition
