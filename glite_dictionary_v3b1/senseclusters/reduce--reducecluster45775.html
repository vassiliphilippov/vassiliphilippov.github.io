reduce

verb
chemistry: remove oxygen or add electrons/hydrogen
2.1 5.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈduːs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈdjuːs/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈdjuːs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈdʒuːs/ (BrE)
(in chemistry) To remove oxygen from a compound, add hydrogen to it, or cause it to gain electrons.

Examples

To reduce a compound
During the experiment, the compound was reduced by adding hydrogen.
In electrochemistry, a species is reduced when it gains electrons during a reaction.
Iron(III) oxide can be reduced to iron using carbon monoxide in a blast furnace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oxidize - to combine with oxygen or lose electrons (chemistry opposite)
