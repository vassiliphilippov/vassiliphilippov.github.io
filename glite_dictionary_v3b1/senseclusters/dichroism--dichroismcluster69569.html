dichroism

noun
property of showing two colors from different directions
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪˌkroʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɪkrəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
The property of a crystal, substance, or material to show two different colors when viewed from two different directions.

Examples

Dichroism in crystals
Polaroid sunglasses work by exploiting the dichroism of certain materials.
Scientists studied the dichroism in the crystal to determine its optical axis.
The mineral exhibits dichroism, appearing green from one angle and brown from another.
