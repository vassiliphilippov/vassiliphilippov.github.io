fire-bellied toad

noun
European toad with red/orange underside patches
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ ˌbɛlid toʊd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌbɛlid təʊd / (BrE)
A small toad found in central and eastern Europe, distinguished by the bright red or orange patches mixed with black on its underside.

Examples

The fire-bellied toad
Conservationists are concerned about the declining habitat of the fire-bellied toad.
The fire-bellied toad displays its bright underbelly as a warning to predators.
We spotted a fire-bellied toad near the edge of the pond last spring.
