cardiac resuscitation

noun
emergency procedure to restore heart function
6.3 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdiæk rɪˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiæk rɪˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an emergency medical procedure that combines external cardiac massage and artificial respiration to restore blood circulation and breathing in a person who has suffered cardiac arrest

Examples

Emergency cardiac resuscitation
Doctors said that immediate cardiac resuscitation prevented brain damage after the incident.
Prompt cardiac resuscitation can mean the difference between life and death in cardiac arrest situations.
The paramedics performed cardiac resuscitation to try to revive the unconscious patient.

  • synonym: cardiopulmonary resuscitation - emergency procedure that combines chest compressions and artificial ventilation
