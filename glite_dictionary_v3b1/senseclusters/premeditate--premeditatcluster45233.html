premeditate

verb
plan or consider ahead of time
4.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /priːm.ˈe.də.teɪt/ /; / /priːm.ˈe.dɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /priːm.ˈe.dɪ.teɪt/ /; / /priːm.ˈe.də.teɪt/ (BrE)
to think about or plan something before doing it, especially before carrying out an important or harmful act

Examples

To premeditate a crime
Jurors had to decide whether the act was premeditated or done in the heat of the moment.
She would never premeditate such a harmful action against her friends.
The crime was premeditated and carefully executed.
It's important to premeditate before making such a significant commitment.
She tried to premeditate her actions to avoid making impulsive decisions.
The crime was found to be premeditated rather than spontaneous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: improvise - to do something without preparation
  • synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
