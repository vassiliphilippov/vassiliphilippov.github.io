disinter

verb
dig up a buried body or object
Pronunciation: / dɪs.ɪnˈtɝː /; / dɪsˈɪntɚ / (AmE), / dɪs.ɪnˈtɜː /; / dɪsˈɪntə / (BrE)
to dig up and remove a buried body or object, usually for investigation or examination

Examples

To disinter a body
Historians plan to disinter the time capsule buried under the old building.
The court ordered that the body be disinterred for a new autopsy.
The forensic team had to disinter the remains to perform further tests.
Authorities needed a court order to disinter the coffin.
It can be controversial to disinter graves for scientific reasons.
The archaeologists plan to disinter the remains for further study.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bury - to put a dead body or something else in the ground and cover it with earth
  • synonym: exhume - to dig out something buried, especially a body
