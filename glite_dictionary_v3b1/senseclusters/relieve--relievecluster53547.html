relieve

verb
remove or ease burden or distress
Pronunciation: / rɪˈliv / (AmE), / rɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to make pain, stress, worry, or an unpleasant feeling less severe; to ease or reduce discomfort or burden

Examples

To relieve pain
A warm bath can relieve muscle tension and stress.
The medication helped relieve her headache after a long day at work.
We must do something to relieve the suffering of people affected by the disaster.
Applying a cold compress can relieve swelling.
She took deep breaths to relieve her tension after the stressful meeting.
This medicine will relieve your back pain within an hour.
He tried to relieve the tension in the room by making a joke.
The medication helped relieve her headache within an hour.
Volunteers brought food and water to relieve the suffering of the flood victims.
