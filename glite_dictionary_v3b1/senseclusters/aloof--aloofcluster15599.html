aloof

adjective, adverb
in a distant or reserved manner
6.3 2.54/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàluf /; / …ôÀàluÀêf / (AmE), / …ôÀàluÀêf / (BrE)
not friendly, distant, or uninvolved in attitude or manner

Examples

Remain aloof from others
During the meeting, she remained aloof, avoiding participation in the discussion.
Even when praised for her achievements, she responded aloof, keeping her emotions hidden.
He watched the festivities aloof, preferring not to join in the celebration.
Despite their efforts to include her, she stayed aloof and hardly spoke.
His aloof attitude made it difficult for coworkers to get to know him.
She remained aloof during the meeting, rarely engaging with anyone else.

Synonyms

  • distant
    4.9 3.77/m
    distant feels remote and quiet around others, while aloof shows clear lack of friendliness and keeps emotional distance.
  • standoffish
    7.1 0.43/m
    standoffish acts cold and keeps people at arm's length, while aloof is quietly uninvolved and not friendly.
  • offish
    8.2 0.01/m
    offish is slightly distant and slow to join social talk, while aloof stays apart and shows little friendliness.
  • unapproachable
    6.6 0.25/m
    unapproachable looks hard to talk to and puts others off, while aloof is distant but more passive in manner.
  • reserved
    3.7 0.00/m
    reserved keeps feelings private and speaks little, while aloof is more openly unfriendly and keeps clear social distance.
  • detached
    5.5 2.69/m
    detached stays emotionally uninvolved and calm, while aloof also seems cold but often shows more social distance.
  • cold
    4.4 39/m
    cold has a hard, unfriendly tone and little warmth, while aloof is distant without strong harshness in manner.
  • icy
    3.3 1.67/m
    icy uses very cold, cutting words or looks, while aloof keeps a cool, distant manner but is less actively harsh.
