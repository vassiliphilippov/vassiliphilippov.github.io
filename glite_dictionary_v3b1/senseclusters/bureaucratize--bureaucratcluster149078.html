bureaucratize

verb
to add bureaucracy
8.0
Pronunciation: / bjʊˈrɑːkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / bjʊəˈrɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more bureaucratic by adding official rules, procedures, or administrative systems

Examples

To bureaucratize a company
The company decided to bureaucratize its operations to ensure compliance with new regulations.
As organizations grow, they often bureaucratize and become less flexible.
Critics argue that efforts to bureaucratize education can slow down innovation.
The new manager did not want to bureaucratize the department and slow down decision-making.
Some fear that government funding will bureaucratize the organization and reduce its flexibility.
Efforts to bureaucratize the process led to more paperwork and longer approval times.
The new manager did not want to bureaucratize the team's flexible way of working.
Some fear that government funding will bureaucratize the charity's simple operations.
Efforts to bureaucratize the process led to more paperwork and slower decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centralize - to bring under a single authority or control
  • synonym: formalize - to make something more formal or structured
  • synonym: regulate - to control or direct by rules or regulations
  • antonym: streamline - to make a process simpler and more efficient
