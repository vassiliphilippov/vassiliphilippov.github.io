center

noun
midpoint or central point
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tɚ /; / ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə /; / ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
the middle point or area of something, or a part that is equally distant from all sides

Examples

Center of the circle
Draw a circle and mark its center with a small dot.
Measure from the center of the table to each edge to ensure it's even.
The statue was placed exactly at the center of the square.
She found herself standing right at the center of the crowd.
The statue was placed in the center of the square for everyone to see.
The table was set in the center of the room, surrounded by chairs.
Enemy forces broke through the centre of their formation.
Reinforcements arrived just in time to hold the centre.
The commander ordered more troops to the centre of the line.
