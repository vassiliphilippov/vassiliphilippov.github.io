hostage

verb
to hold as hostage
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɒstɪdʒ / (BrE)
to hold someone as a hostage in order to demand something or force concessions.

Examples

To hostage a victim
The rebels were hostaging several tourists to pressure the government.
Authorities negotiated for hours with the criminals hostaging the bank employees.
International law forbids hostaging civilians during armed conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kidnap - to take someone away illegally by force, often to demand money or concessions
  • antonym: release - to set someone free from captivity
