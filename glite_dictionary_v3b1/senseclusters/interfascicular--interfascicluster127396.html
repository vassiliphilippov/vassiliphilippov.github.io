interfascicular

adjective
between fiber bundles
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərfəˈsɪkjələr / (AmE), / ˌɪntəfæˈsɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
located or occurring between bundles of fibers, especially in plants or muscles

Examples

The interfascicular space
The interfascicular spaces in the plant stem help transport nutrients between tissues.
Damage to the interfascicular region can affect muscle function and flexibility.
Researchers studied the interfascicular areas to understand how fibers are organized in the tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intrafascicular - located within a bundle of fibers
