Assyrian

noun
extinct language of ancient Mesopotamia
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪriən / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language once spoken by the Assyrians of ancient Mesopotamia

Examples

Assyrian script
Few people today can read or translate Assyrian, since the language has been extinct for centuries.
Scholars have deciphered ancient inscriptions written in Assyrian to learn more about early Mesopotamian culture.
The Assyrian language employed cuneiform script for administrative records and royal decrees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Akkadian - the extinct Semitic language from which Assyrian descended
  • synonym: Babylonian - an ancient variant of the Akkadian language; contemporary with Assyrian
