flyover country

noun
rural areas often ignored by travelers
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌoʊvɚ ˌkʌntri / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌəʊvə ˌkʌntri / (BrE)
regions of rural or less-populated areas, especially in the central United States, considered unremarkable or often ignored by travelers flying between major cities on the coasts

Examples

Flyover country regions
Living in flyover country can mean fewer job opportunities but a greater sense of community.
Many urban dwellers have never visited flyover country and hold misconceptions about the people who live there.
The politician made a point to campaign throughout flyover country, not just in the big cities.
