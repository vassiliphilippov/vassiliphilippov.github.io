coltsfoot

noun
a perennial herb with white flowers and rounded leaves
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊltsˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɒltsˌfʊt / (BrE)
a perennial herb with large, rounded leaves (resembling a colt's foot) and yellow or white flowers, sometimes used in traditional medicine

Examples

The coltsfoot plant
Gardeners admire the coltsfoot for its unique round leaves and delicate white flowers.
The leaves of the coltsfoot turn a purplish color in the fall.
You can often find coltsfoot growing in damp woodlands and along riverbanks.
Herbalists once used coltsfoot as a remedy for coughs and other respiratory ailments.
In early spring, coltsfoot blooms brighten the roadsides before any leaves appear.
The large, hoof-shaped leaves make coltsfoot easy to identify among wild plants.
