gentrification

noun
urban renewal with resident displacement
8.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /dʒen.trɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dʒen.trɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of renovating and improving a deteriorated urban neighborhood by influx of more affluent residents, often resulting in displacement of poorer inhabitants

Examples

Gentrification of neighborhoods
Many residents expressed concerns about gentrification pushing out long-standing communities.
Some argue that gentrification improves city infrastructure, while others worry about cultural loss.
The gentrification of the neighborhood has led to rising property prices and new businesses opening.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: displacement - the enforced movement of people from their homes, often by economic or social pressures
  • synonym: urban renewal - the process of improving and modernizing parts of a city
