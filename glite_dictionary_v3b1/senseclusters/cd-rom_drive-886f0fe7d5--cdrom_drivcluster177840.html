CD-ROM drive

noun
computer device for reading CD-ROMs
1.2
Pronunciation: / siː diː rɑm draɪv / (AmE), / siː diː rɒm draɪv / (BrE)
A device in or connected to a computer that reads information from CD-ROM discs.

Examples

A CD-ROM drive device
Modern laptops often do not include a CD-ROM drive as most software is now downloaded online.
Please insert the installation disc into your CD-ROM drive to begin the setup process.
The computer's CD-ROM drive stopped working after several years of use.

  • synonym: DVD drive - device for reading and writing DVDs
