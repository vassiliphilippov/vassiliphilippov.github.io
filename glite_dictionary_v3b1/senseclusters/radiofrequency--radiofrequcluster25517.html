radiofrequency

noun
range of radio waves
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
an electromagnetic wave or signal in the frequency range used for radio transmission or medical treatments (typically 3 kHz to 300 GHz)

Examples

Radiofrequency signal

Medical treatments sometimes use radiofrequency energy to heat and destroy abnormal tissue.

Many wireless devices communicate using radiofrequency signals.
The technician measured the radiofrequency levels in the laboratory to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: audiofrequency - an electrical signal with a frequency within the range of human hearing, below radiofrequency
  • degree higher: microwave - an electromagnetic wave with a frequency higher than radiofrequency, used in radar and cooking
  • antonym: ultrasound - sound waves with frequencies above human hearing, used in medical imaging
