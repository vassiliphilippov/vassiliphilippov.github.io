misprise

noun
mistaken belief
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈpraɪz / (AmE), / mɪsˈpraɪz / (BrE)
an incorrect understanding or mistaken belief about something

Examples

A misprise of facts
The report was based on a misprise of the facts presented by the witnesses.
Her argument stemmed from a common misprise about how the system works.
It is important to clarify any misprise before making a final decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: misapprehension - a failure to understand something correctly
  • synonym: misconception - an incorrect idea or understanding
  • antonym: understanding - the ability to comprehend or grasp something correctly
