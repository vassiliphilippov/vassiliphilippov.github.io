celiac trunk

noun
major abdominal artery branching from the aorta
9.7
Pronunciation: / siˈliː.æk trʌŋk / (AmE), / siˈliː.ək trʌŋk / (BrE)
a major artery in the abdomen that arises from the abdominal aorta just below the diaphragm and divides into the left gastric, common hepatic, and splenic arteries

Examples

The celiac trunk artery
A blockage in the celiac trunk may lead to reduced blood flow to the stomach, liver, and spleen.
Damage to the celiac trunk can cause serious complications due to its supply to major abdominal organs.
The surgeon carefully identified the celiac trunk before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: abdominal aorta - the main artery in the abdomen supplying blood to abdominal organs
  • degree lower: common hepatic artery - an artery branching from the celiac trunk supplying blood to the liver
  • degree lower: left gastric artery - an artery branching from the celiac trunk supplying blood to the stomach
  • degree lower: splenic artery - an artery branching from the celiac trunk supplying blood to the spleen
