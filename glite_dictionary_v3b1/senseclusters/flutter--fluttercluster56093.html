flutter

verb
move quickly back and forth
2.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈflʌ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈflʌ.tə/ (BrE)
to move or make something move quickly and lightly back and forth or up and down, like wings, leaves, or curtains

Examples

Leaves flutter in wind
His eyelids fluttered open as he slowly woke up.
She watched the butterfly flutter from flower to flower.
The leaves began to flutter in the gentle breeze.
Dozens of colorful birds fluttered above the treetops at dawn.
She watched the moth flutter nervously against the windowpane.
The butterfly began to flutter its delicate wings as it landed on the flower.
A butterfly will often flutter from one flower to another in the garden.
His heart seemed to flutter nervously before his name was called.
The curtains fluttered gently in the evening breeze.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dart - move suddenly and quickly
  • synonym: flap - to move up and down or back and forth quickly and repeatedly, especially wings
  • synonym: flicker - to shine unsteadily; to move unsteadily or rapidly
  • synonym: flit - move swiftly and lightly
  • antonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously along, as without effort or resistance; especially of flight without flapping
