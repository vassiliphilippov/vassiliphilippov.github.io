flutter

verb
to beat quickly and irregularly (esp. heart)
4.4 0.92/m

(of the heart or pulse) to beat or move with a rapid, irregular motion, especially from excitement, nervousness, or illness.

Examples

Hearts flutter nervously
During the tense meeting, his heart kept fluttering in his chest.
Her pulse fluttered when she heard the exciting news.
I felt my heart flutter with anticipation before stepping onto the stage.

Synonyms
