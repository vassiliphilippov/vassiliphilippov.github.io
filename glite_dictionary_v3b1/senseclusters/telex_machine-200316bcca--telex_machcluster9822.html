telex machine

noun
telegraph-connected message-sending device
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛlɛks məˈʃin / (AmE), / ˈtiːlɛks məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A device resembling a typewriter, used to send and receive typed messages over telegraph lines, often as part of the telex communication network.

Examples

The telex machine system
Before email, the telex machine was essential for fast business correspondence across continents.
She typed the order on the telex machine and sent it directly to the supplier overseas.
The company installed a telex machine to handle urgent international communications.
