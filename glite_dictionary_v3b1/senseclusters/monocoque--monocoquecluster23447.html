monocoque

adjective
shell-bearing structure
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnəˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəkɒk / (BrE)
describing a vehicle, aircraft, or structure whose outer shell supports most of the load, rather than an internal frame

Examples

Monocoque car body
The new sports car features a monocoque design for improved safety and reduced weight.
Many modern airplanes use monocoque construction to maximize strength and efficiency.
Engineers chose a monocoque structure to simplify the manufacturing process and enhance durability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: space frame - a structure with an internal framework supporting the load
  • synonym: unibody - having a single, unified body structure
