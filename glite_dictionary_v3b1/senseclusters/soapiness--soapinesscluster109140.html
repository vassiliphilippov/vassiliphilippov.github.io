soapiness

noun
melodramatic, soap-opera–like quality
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.pi.nəs/ /; / /ˈsoʊ.pɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.pi.nəs/ /; / /ˈsəʊ.pɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being sentimental, melodramatic, or exaggerated in a way that resembles a soap opera; excessive emotion or schmaltz in storytelling or presentation.

Examples

Movie soapiness
The ad's soapiness overwhelmed the message, turning a serious issue into melodrama.
Critics complained that the film's unnecessary soapiness made its characters feel unreal and exaggerated.
She avoids shows with too much soapiness; she prefers subtle, realistic drama instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melodrama - a dramatic work or style marked by exaggerated emotions and plot
  • synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia in a way considered insincere or overdone
