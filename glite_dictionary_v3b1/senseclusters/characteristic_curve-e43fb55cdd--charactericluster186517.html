characteristic curve

noun
electronics device property graph
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkær.ɪktəˈrɪstɪk kɝːv / (AmE), / ˌkær.ɪk.təˈrɪs.tɪk kɜːv / (BrE)
(in electronics) a graph showing how a particular property of a device changes in relation to another variable.

Examples

An electronic characteristic curve
A change in temperature can shift the characteristic curve of many electronic components.
Engineers use the characteristic curve to determine a transistor's operating range.
The diode's characteristic curve illustrates the relationship between voltage and current.
