in time

adverb
in correct rhythm (music/dance)
Pronunciation: / ɪn taɪm / (AmE), / ɪn taɪm / (BrE)
in the correct rhythm; synchronised with a beat, especially in music or dance.

Examples

Dance in time
If you don't clap in time, it throws off the whole performance.
She struggled at first but was soon dancing in time with the group.
The orchestra played perfectly in time with the conductor's baton.

Related Terms

  • antonym: out of time - not in correct rhythm or synchronization, especially in music or dance
