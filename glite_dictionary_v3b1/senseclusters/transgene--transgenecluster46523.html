transgene

noun
gene inserted from another organism
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænzˌdʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈtranzˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that has been artificially introduced into the genome of another organism

Examples

A transgene in DNA
A glowing mouse was created by adding a jellyfish transgene to its DNA.
The presence of the transgene was confirmed by genetic testing.
The researchers inserted a transgene to study its impact on plant growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gene - a unit of heredity in a living organism
