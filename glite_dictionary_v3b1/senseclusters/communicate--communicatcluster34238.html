communicate

verb
share or exchange information
2.3 52/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàmjun…™Àåke…™t / (AmE), / k…ôÀàmjuÀên…™ke…™t / (BrE)
to share or exchange information, ideas, or feelings with someone, especially by speaking, writing, or using another medium

Examples

To communicate ideas clearly
It's important to communicate your needs clearly in relationships.
She finds it difficult to communicate her feelings to her family.
They only communicate with each other by email these days.
It's important to communicate your needs clearly in any relationship.
She found it difficult to communicate her emotions to her family.
We communicated regularly by email while she was abroad.
It can be challenging to communicate complex ideas clearly to a large audience.
Please communicate this information to your supervisor as soon as possible.
The company uses emails and newsletters to communicate important updates to staff.

Synonyms

  • convey
    3.4 23/m
    convey often expresses ideas clearly in formal or written situations, while communicate covers many ways to share information in general.
  • express
    2.3 112/m
    express shows feelings or ideas through words or art, while communicate includes many ways to send information or emotions.
  • share
    1.0 43/m
    share means giving thoughts or feelings to others in a friendly way, while communicate is a broader act of sending information.
  • intercommunicate
    6.5 0.00/m
    intercommunicate means two or more people exchange ideas directly with each other, while communicate can mean one-way or two-way sharing.
  • talk
    1.0 988/m
    talk usually refers to speaking in person or casually with others, while communicate covers speaking, writing, and other media too.
  • speak
    1.0 374/m
    speak focuses on using spoken words or giving a speech, while communicate covers speech plus writing and other ways to share ideas.
  • tell
    1.0 686/m
    tell means say something to someone, often to inform or instruct, while communicate includes many forms and may be two-way.
  • inform
    1.0 62/m
    inform means give facts or news to someone, usually one-way, while communicate covers both facts and feelings and can be mutual.
  • pass on
    2.7 6.49/m
    pass on often means give something or relay a message to another person, while communicate is the general act of sharing information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convey - to make ideas or feelings known to someone
  • synonym: express - to put thoughts or feelings into words or actions
  • synonym: inform - to give knowledge or information to
  • antonym: miscommunicate - to fail to communicate clearly; to communicate incorrectly
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give or communicate information
