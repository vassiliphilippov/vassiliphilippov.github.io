observant

adjective
quick to notice; attentive to details
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɜrvənt /; / əbˈzɝːvənt / (AmE), / əbˈzɜːvənt / (BrE)
quick to notice or perceive things; attentive to details or surroundings

Examples

Observant detective
His observant nature made him an excellent detective.
She is very observant and always notices when something has changed in her office.
You have to be observant to spot the birds hidden in the trees.
Children tend to be especially observant when it comes to changes in their environment.
It pays to be observant while walking through the forest, as you might spot rare wildlife.
The detective was very observant and quickly noticed the small clue left behind.

  • antonym: inattentive - not paying attention; not observant
  • antonym: incurious - not eager to know or learn something
