swarm

verb
move in large numbers
3.2 6.05/m
Pronunciation: / sw…îrm / (AmE), / sw…îÀêm / (BrE)
to move somewhere in large numbers, often making a place busy or lively

Examples

Swarm into the street
Bees swarm out of their hive when disturbed.
Fans began to swarm into the stadium as soon as the gates opened.
Reporters swarmed around the celebrity, eager for a statement.
By noon, the marketplace was swarming with shoppers looking for fresh produce and crafts.
Dozens of reporters swarmed around the famous actor as soon as he stepped outside.
Tourists swarm the city every summer, making it lively and crowded.
