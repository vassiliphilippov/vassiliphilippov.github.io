control grid

noun
electrode in a vacuum tube controlling electron flow
7.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl ɡrɪd / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl ɡrɪd / (BrE)
an electrode positioned between the cathode and anode in a vacuum tube, used to control the flow of electrons within the tube

Examples

The control grid electrode
Adjusting the voltage on the control grid can amplify or reduce the signal strength.
Early radios relied heavily on the efficient design of the control grid for audio amplification.
The control grid in a vacuum tube regulates the flow of electrons from the cathode to the anode.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anode - the electrode through which current flows into a device; positive electrode
  • synonym: cathode - the negative electrode in a vacuum tube or other device
  • synonym: vacuum tube - an electronic device using thermionic emission to control current, often used before transistors
