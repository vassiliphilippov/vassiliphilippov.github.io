ocellus

noun
eyelike marking (esp. in animals)
8.8
Pronunciation: / oʊˈsɛləs / (AmE), / əʊˈsɛləs / (BrE)
a marking on an animal that resembles an eye, typically a spot of color within a ring of another color, such as found on the wings of butterflies or the feathers of peacocks

Examples

Peacock feather ocellus
Each feather of the peacock features a distinctive blue and green ocellus.
Some fish have a black ocellus near their tails to confuse attackers.
The butterfly's wings displayed a bright ocellus that resembled a real eye to scare off predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eyespot - an eyelike marking, especially in animals and plants
