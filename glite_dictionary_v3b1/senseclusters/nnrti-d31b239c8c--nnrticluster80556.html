NNRTI

noun
antiviral drug for HIV treatment
Pronunciation: / ɛn ɛn ɑr ti aɪ / (AmE), / ɛn ɛn ɑː tiː aɪ / (BrE)
(medicine) any of a class of antiviral drugs that prevent HIV replication by binding directly to reverse transcriptase and inhibiting the conversion of viral RNA to DNA; usually used in combination therapy.

Examples

An NNRTI drug
Drug resistance can sometimes develop when an NNRTI is used alone rather than in combination with other HIV medications.
Many clinical guidelines recommend including an NNRTI in first-line HIV therapy unless contraindicated.
The doctor prescribed an NNRTI as part of the patient's antiretroviral treatment regimen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: NRTI - antiviral drugs that inhibit reverse transcriptase by mimicking nucleosides
