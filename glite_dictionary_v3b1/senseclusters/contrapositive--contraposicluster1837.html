contrapositive

noun
reversed negative statement
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntrəˈpɑːzətɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
a statement formed by negating and reversing the hypothesis and conclusion of a conditional statement in logic or mathematics

Examples

The contrapositive of a rule
The contrapositive of the statement 'If it rains, the ground gets wet' is 'If the ground does not get wet, it does not rain.'
In mathematics, proving the contrapositive is often easier than proving the original statement.
