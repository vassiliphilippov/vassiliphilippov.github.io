dayflower

noun
a plant of the Commelinaceae family
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪˌflaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.fləʊ.ə / (BrE)
Any plant of the family Commelinaceae, typically having blue, purple, or white flowers that open in the morning and fade by evening.

Examples

A dayflower plant
Several species in the dayflower family are native to tropical and subtropical regions.
Some gardeners appreciate the dayflower for its delicate petals and ease of care.
The striking blue dayflower blooms briefly each morning in the summer garden.
