epistrophe

noun
repetition at sentence ends (rhetoric)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪstrəfi / (BrE)
The repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses, sentences, or verses.

Examples

Epistrophe in speeches
Martin Luther King's speeches are noted for their masterful use of epistrophe.
The speech used epistrophe to powerful effect, repeating 'we shall overcome' at the end of each sentence.
Writers often use epistrophe to emphasize a recurring idea or theme.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anaphora - repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses
