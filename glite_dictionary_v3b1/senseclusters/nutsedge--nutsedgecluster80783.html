nutsedge

noun
a sedge plant with edible tubers
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌtˌsɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌsɛdʒ / (BrE)
A widely distributed perennial sedge plant, especially of the genus Cyperus, with small edible, nutlike underground tubers.

Examples

nutsedge plant
Gardeners often struggle to control nutsedge because of its hardy underground tubers.
In some cultures, nutsedge tubers are harvested and eaten as a snack.
The nutsedge flourished in the wet fields, making it difficult for crops to thrive.
