tight money

noun
economic condition with scarce credit
7.5 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / taÉªt ËˆmÊŒni / (AmE), / taÉªt ËˆmÊŒni / (BrE)
An economic situation in which credit is difficult to obtain and interest rates are high.

Examples

Tight money period
During periods of tight money, businesses may struggle to borrow funds for expansion.
Economists warn that prolonged tight money can slow down economic growth.
The central bank's policies led to tight money and higher loan rates across the country.

Synonyms

  • credit crunch
    4.5 0.45/m
    a credit crunch is a sudden shortage of loans and higher rates, while tight money describes monetary policy that makes credit scarce.
  • tightness
    6.4 0.02/m
    a tightness means a general scarcity of money or loans in the economy, while tight money refers to high interest policy.
  • stringency
    8.7 0.04/m
    a stringency is a strict shortage or limit on credit that causes hardship, while tight money names the economic state of scarce credit and high rates.
