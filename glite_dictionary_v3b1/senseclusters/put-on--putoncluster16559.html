put-on

noun
humorous imitation of someone's style
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊt ɑn /; / ˈpʊtˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈpʊt ɒn /; / ˈpʊtˌɒn / (BrE)
an act, story, or imitation meant to trick, deceive, or amuse people by pretending something is true when it is not

Examples

A funny put-on
Her British accent was just a put-on to amuse her friends at the party.
I couldn't tell if his outrage was real or just a put-on.
The comedian's sketch was a clever put-on of the famous actor's mannerisms.
At first, I thought his wild story was just a put-on to get attention.
Everyone laughed when they realized the TV interview was only a put-on.
The article turned out to be a complete put-on, written as a joke.
At first, I believed his outrageous claims, but then I suspected it was a put-on.
He realized too late that the story about winning the lottery was just a put-on.
They decided to stage a put-on during the office meeting to lighten the mood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hoax - a trick meant to deceive
  • synonym: impression - an imitation of a person, especially one done to entertain
  • synonym: joke - something said or done to make people laugh
  • synonym: parody - a humorous or satirical imitation of a serious piece of literature or writing
  • degree lower: prank - a playful trick
  • degree higher: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud
