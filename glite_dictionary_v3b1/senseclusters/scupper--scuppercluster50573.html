scupper

noun
drain on a ship's deck
9.2 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈskʌp.ər/ (AmE), /ˈskʌp.ə/ (BrE)
A drain in the side of a ship's deck that allows water to flow overboard.

Examples

Deck scupper
Barnacles can sometimes clog a scupper, leading to dangerous pools of water on deck.
Heavy rain poured across the deck, but the scupper quickly drained the water away.
The sailors checked to make sure each scupper was clear of debris before the storm hit.
