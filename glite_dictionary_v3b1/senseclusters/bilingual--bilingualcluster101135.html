bilingual

noun
person fluent in two languages
Pronunciation: / baɪˈlɪŋɡwəl / (AmE), / baɪˈlɪŋɡwəl / (BrE)
a person who is able to speak two languages with equal fluency.

Examples

A bilingual person
Many bilingual employees are in high demand at international companies.
Schools often offer programs to help bilingual children succeed academically.
She grew up as a bilingual in both Spanish and English.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monolingual - a person who speaks only one language
  • degree higher: polyglot - a person who knows and can use several languages
