nondelegation

noun
refusal to delegate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌdɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌdɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the practice or policy of not assigning tasks, authority, or responsibilities to others

Examples

Nondelegation of work
The manager's strict nondelegation led to delays and employee frustration.
A culture of nondelegation can prevent team members from developing new skills.
Her preference for nondelegation meant she was often overwhelmed with work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delegation - the act of assigning tasks or authority to others
