imperial

noun
luggage box on roof of old coach
4.8
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəl / (BrE)
a box or trunk for luggage carried on the roof of a coach, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries

Examples

Imperial trunk
In Victorian times, the imperial was an essential feature for overnight stagecoach journeys.
Rain leaked through the old imperial, soaking some of the suitcases on top of the carriage.
The coachman loaded the imperial with passengers' bags before setting off for London.
