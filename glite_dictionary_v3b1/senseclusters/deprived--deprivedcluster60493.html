deprived

adjective
lacking basic necessities
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpraɪvd / (AmE), / dɪˈpraɪvd / (BrE)
Lacking the basic necessities of life, such as food, shelter, or a healthy environment.

Examples

A deprived neighborhood
He grew up in a deprived neighborhood, but went on to achieve great success.
Many children from deprived backgrounds struggle to access quality education.
The charity aims to support families living in deprived areas of the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: privileged - having special advantages or opportunities
