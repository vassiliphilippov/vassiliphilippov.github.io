trainbearer

noun
person holding the train of a gown
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪnˌbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnˌbeərə / (BrE)
a person, typically a child or attendant, who holds up the train of a gown or robe during a ceremonial occasion

Examples

A trainbearer at ceremony
During the coronation, a trainbearer walked behind the queen to keep her elaborate robe from touching the ground.
Historical paintings often depict a trainbearer playing a key role in royal processions.
The young trainbearer followed the bride down the aisle, carefully lifting the delicate lace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attendant - a person who waits on or serves others, especially as part of a ceremony
