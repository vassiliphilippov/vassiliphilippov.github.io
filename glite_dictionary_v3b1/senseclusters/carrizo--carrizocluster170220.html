carrizo

noun
tall North American reed used for mats and arrows
6.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈriːzoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈriːzəʊ / (BrE)
A tall North American reed with broad leaves and large feathery flower clusters, found in wet areas and used for making mats, screens, and arrow shafts.

Examples

Tall carrizo reed
Ancient hunters fashioned arrow shafts from the strong stems of carrizo.
Local artisans use carrizo to weave sturdy mats and baskets.
The riverbank was lined with tall carrizo swaying gently in the breeze.
