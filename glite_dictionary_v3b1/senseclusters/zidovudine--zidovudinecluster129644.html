zidovudine

noun
antiviral drug for HIV/AIDS treatment
Pronunciation: / zɪˈdoʊvjudiːn / (AmE), / zɪˈdəʊvjʊdiːn / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used in the treatment of HIV/AIDS, known for side effects such as liver damage and bone marrow suppression

Examples

zidovudine treatment
Clinical trials showed that zidovudine can help slow the progression of HIV infection.
Serious side effects of zidovudine include anemia and liver problems.
The doctor prescribed zidovudine as part of the patient's antiretroviral therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Retrovir - trade name for zidovudine, used in HIV/AIDS treatment
