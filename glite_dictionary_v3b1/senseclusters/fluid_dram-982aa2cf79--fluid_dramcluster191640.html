fluid dram

noun
British unit of liquid capacity
8.6 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfluː.ɪd dræm/ /; / /ˈflʊ.ɪd dræm/ /; / /ˈfluː.ɪd drɑːm/ (AmE), /ˈfluː.ɪd dræm/ /; / /ˈfluː.ɪd drɑːm/ (BrE)
a small unit of liquid measurement, historically used in pharmacy; equal to about one eighth of a fluid ounce (about 3.55–3.7 milliliters).

Examples

A fluid dram measure
Each fluid dram contains precisely 60 minims, making it useful for small doses.
Recipes from the 19th century sometimes call for a fluid dram of a particular ingredient.
The pharmacist measured out a fluid dram of the tincture for the patient.
A fluid dram is about 3.55 milliliters in the metric system.
In old recipes, a fluid dram was sometimes used to measure liquid ingredients.
The prescription required exactly one fluid dram of the medication.
A fluid drachm is equivalent to approximately 3.7 milliliters.
Doses were measured in fluid drachms before the metric system became standard.
The old prescription called for one fluid drachm of tincture to be mixed with water.
