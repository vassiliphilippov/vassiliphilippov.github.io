high-tension meeting

noun
meeting marked by intense pressure or stress
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈtɛnʃən ˈmitɪŋ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈtɛnʃən ˈmiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
a meeting in which there is intense pressure, stress, or emotional strain among the participants

Examples

A high-tension meeting lasts hours
Even seasoned executives felt uncomfortable in the high-tension meeting that lasted several hours.
Negotiations broke down during the high-tension meeting between the two rival companies.
The board called a high-tension meeting after the financial losses were announced.
