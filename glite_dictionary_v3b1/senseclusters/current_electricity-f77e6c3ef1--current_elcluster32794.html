current electricity

noun
flowing electric charge
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝːrənt ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənt ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
electricity resulting from the flow of electric charge through a conductor, as opposed to static electricity.

Examples

Current electricity flow
Current electricity is what powers the lights and appliances in your home.
The principles of current electricity are fundamental to understanding how circuits operate.
Unlike static electricity, current electricity involves a continuous movement of electrons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: static electricity - an accumulation of electric charge on a surface, not moving as a current
