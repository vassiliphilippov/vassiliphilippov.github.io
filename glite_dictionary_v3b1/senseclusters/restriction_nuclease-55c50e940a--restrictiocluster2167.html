restriction nuclease

noun
enzyme cutting DNA at specific sites
7.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˌstrɪkʃən nuːˈkliːeɪs / (AmE), / rɪˌstrɪkʃən njuːˈkliːeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that cleaves nucleic acid molecules at specific sequences, producing defined fragments; widely used in genetic engineering and molecular biology

Examples

A restriction nuclease enzyme
A restriction nuclease recognizes and cleaves at a specific sequence within the genome.
Modern cloning techniques rely heavily on the precision of restriction nucleases.
Scientists used a restriction nuclease to cut the DNA into smaller fragments for analysis.
