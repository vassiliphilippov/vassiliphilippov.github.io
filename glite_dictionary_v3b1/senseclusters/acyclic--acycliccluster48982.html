acyclic

adjective
with an open chain structure (chemistry)
7.6
Pronunciation: / eɪˈsaɪklɪk /; / eɪˈsaɪklɪk, æˈsɪklɪk / (AmE), / eɪˈsɪklɪk /; / eɪˈsɪklɪk, æˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
not forming a ring or closed loop; especially (of a chemical compound) having an open chain structure

Examples

An acyclic compound
Simple alkanes such as hexane are considered acyclic compounds.
The laboratory synthesized an acyclic hydrocarbon to compare its properties with those of a ring compound.
Unlike benzene, which is aromatic and cyclic, butane is acyclic.
In organic chemistry, an acyclic compound contains carbon atoms arranged in open chains rather than rings.
The mathematician studied acyclic graphs, which contain no closed loops.
The plant exhibits an acyclic arrangement of leaves, meaning they grow in a spiral rather than in whorls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cyclic - having atoms arranged in a ring or closed chain
