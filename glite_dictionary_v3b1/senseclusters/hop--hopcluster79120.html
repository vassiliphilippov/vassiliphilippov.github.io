hop

verb
move by jumping on one foot or in short jumps
1.0 4/m
Pronunciation: / hÉ‘p / (AmE), / hÉ’p / (BrE)
to jump or move with small, quick jumps, usually on one foot or lightly from place to place

Examples

To hop on one foot
After twisting his ankle, he had to hop on one foot to the car.
The bird will often hop quickly from branch to branch searching for food.
The little girl loves to hop down the sidewalk on her way home.
A sparrow will often hop from branch to branch as it searches for food.
He injured his left foot and had to hop on his right leg to reach the door.
The little girl likes to hop over the cracks in the sidewalk on her way to school.
A frog can easily hop a log in the woods.
She hopped the fence to retrieve her ball.
The child managed to hop the small stream without getting wet.
