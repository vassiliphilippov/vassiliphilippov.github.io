oyez

interjection
courtroom call
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.jeɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔɪ.eɪz / (BrE)
a traditional call used to command silence and attention in a court or public assembly

Examples

To shout oyez in court
The bailiff called out oyez three times to begin the court session.
At the start of the ceremony, the town crier shouted oyez to gather everyone's attention.
You may still hear oyez in traditional legal proceedings in some countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: order - a command for silence or attention in a formal setting
