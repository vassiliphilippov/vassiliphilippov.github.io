Amanita mappa

noun
a poisonous European mushroom
Pronunciation: / əˈmænɪtə ˈmæpə / (AmE), / əˈmænɪtə ˈmapə / (BrE)
A poisonous mushroom species, often confused with the death cap, found in Europe and recognized by its yellowish cap and white gills.

Examples

Amanita mappa mushroom
Both amateur and experienced foragers should beware of Amanita mappa due to its high toxicity.
Identifying Amanita mappa can be challenging, as its appearance is similar to other dangerous species.
The Amanita mappa is notorious for being mistaken for the deadly death cap mushroom.
