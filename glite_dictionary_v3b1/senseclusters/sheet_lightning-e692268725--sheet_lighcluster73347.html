sheet lightning

noun
diffuse lightning seen as a broad flash
7.9 5.0
Pronunciation: / ʃit ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / ʃiːt ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
lightning that appears as a broad, diffuse flash of light, usually caused by distant lightning reflecting off clouds or being diffused by them.

Examples

Sheet lightning flashes
During the summer storm, sheet lightning illuminated the entire horizon in eerie flashes.
From her window, she watched sheet lightning light up the night sky without any thunder.
Unlike forked lightning, sheet lightning does not show jagged bolts but rather a soft glow behind the clouds.
