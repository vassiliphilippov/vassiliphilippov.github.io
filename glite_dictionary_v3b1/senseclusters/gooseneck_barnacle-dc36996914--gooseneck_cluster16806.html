gooseneck barnacle

noun
type of stalked barnacle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡuːsˌnɛk ˈbɑrnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsˌnɛk ˈbɑːnəkəl / (BrE)
a type of stalked barnacle that attaches itself to ship hulls, rocks, or floating timber, notable for its long, flexible stalk

Examples

Gooseneck barnacles on rocks
Fishermen dislike gooseneck barnacles because they often attach themselves to the hulls of boats.
On the rocky shore, we found several gooseneck barnacles clinging to a piece of driftwood.
The gooseneck barnacle has a distinctive long stalk that helps it anchor to floating debris.
