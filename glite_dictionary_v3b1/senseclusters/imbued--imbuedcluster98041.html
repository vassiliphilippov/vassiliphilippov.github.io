imbued

adjective
deeply filled or permeated (with)
4.0
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbjud / (AmE), / ɪmˈbjuːd / (BrE)
deeply filled with or permeated by a particular quality, idea, or feeling (usually used with 'with')

Examples

Imbued with compassion
Her writing is imbued with compassion for those less fortunate.
The ceremony was imbued with centuries-old traditions and symbolism.
The painting is imbued with a sense of melancholy and nostalgia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deprived - not having or lacking a specified quality
  • synonym: infused - filled or permeated with a quality or feeling
