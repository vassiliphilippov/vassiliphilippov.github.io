family Hystricidae

noun
the family of Old World porcupines
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli hɪˈstrɪsɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli hɪˈstrɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, nocturnal rodents commonly known as Old World porcupines, native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

Examples

Family Hystricidae porcupines
Biologists are studying the habitat preferences of animals in the family Hystricidae to aid conservation efforts.
The family Hystricidae includes several species of large porcupines found across Africa and Asia.
Unlike their New World relatives, members of the family Hystricidae have quills that are not barbed.
