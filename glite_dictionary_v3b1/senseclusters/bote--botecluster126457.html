bote

noun
old legal compensation
Pronunciation: / boʊt / (AmE), / bəʊt / (BrE)
an old legal term for compensation or a right to take wood from land, especially for fuel or repairs

Examples

Medieval bote rights
In medieval England, peasants were sometimes granted bote to collect wood for heating their homes.
The right of bote allowed tenants to gather timber for repairing fences and buildings.
Historical documents mention disputes over bote between landowners and villagers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensation - something given to make up for loss or injury
  • antonym: forfeit - something lost or surrendered as a penalty
