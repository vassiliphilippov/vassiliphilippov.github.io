ketch

noun
two-masted sailing vessel
8.8
Pronunciation: / kɛtʃ / (AmE), / kɛtʃ / (BrE)
a sailing vessel with two masts, where the smaller mizzen mast is located forward of the rudderpost

Examples

A ketch sailing boat
His dream was to restore a classic wooden ketch and sail around the world.
The harbor was dotted with fishing ketches returning after a day at sea.
They sailed upriver in a sturdy ketch designed for long ocean voyages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: yawl - a sailing vessel with two masts, the mizzen aft of the rudderpost
