gnostic

adjective
relating to esoteric spiritual knowledge
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːstɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
relating to esoteric or mystical knowledge, especially spiritual knowledge believed to provide insight into divine truths.

Examples

Gnostic spiritual knowledge
She claimed a gnostic understanding of human destiny that differed from mainstream religious views.
Some ancient sects were known for their gnostic teachings, emphasizing secret spiritual knowledge.
The novel explores gnostic themes of enlightenment and hidden wisdom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agnostic - a person who believes nothing is known or can be known of the existence or nature of God or of anything beyond material phenomena
