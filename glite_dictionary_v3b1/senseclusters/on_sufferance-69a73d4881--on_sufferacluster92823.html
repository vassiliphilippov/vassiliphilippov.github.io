on sufferance

adverb
reluctantly allowed, not formally permitted
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑn ˈsʌf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ɒn ˈsʌf.ər.əns / (BrE)
allowed to remain or continue only because someone else reluctantly permits it, not because of any right or entitlement.

Examples

Remain on sufferance
He remains on the committee on sufferance, as the others would prefer he leave.
She was allowed to stay in her old room on sufferance after her parents moved away.
The tenant occupied the apartment on sufferance, knowing he could be asked to leave at any time.
