hyperglycemic

adjective
high blood sugar
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərɡlaɪˈsiːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəɡlaɪˈsiːmɪk /; / ˌhaɪpəɡlʌɪˈsiːmɪk /; / ˌhaɪpəɡlɪˈsiːmɪk / (BrE)
having or causing an abnormally high level of sugar (glucose) in the blood

Examples

Hyperglycemic state
The doctor warned that the medication could make patients hyperglycemic if not monitored closely.
After eating a large amount of sweets, he became temporarily hyperglycemic.
A hyperglycemic state can be dangerous for people with diabetes if left untreated.
If you feel dizzy or thirsty, you might be hyperglycemic and should check your blood sugar.
The nurse monitored the hyperglycemic patient closely during his hospital stay.
A hyperglycemic episode can occur if a person with diabetes eats too much sugar.
The doctor noticed that the patient was hyperglycemic after reviewing the blood test results.
A hyperglycemic episode can be dangerous if not treated quickly.
People with diabetes must monitor their blood sugar to avoid becoming hyperglycemic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diabetic - relating to or affected by diabetes
  • antonym: hypoglycemic - having a low level of glucose in the blood
