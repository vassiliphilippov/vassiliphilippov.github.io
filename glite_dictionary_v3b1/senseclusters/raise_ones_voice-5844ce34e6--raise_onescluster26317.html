raise one's voice

verb
speak loudly or shout
1.0
Pronunciation: / reɪz wʌnz vɔɪs / (AmE), / reɪz wʌnz vɔɪs / (BrE)
to speak more loudly, especially because one is angry or wants to be heard

Examples

To raise his voice loudly
He had to raise his voice so everyone in the crowd could hear him.
Please do not raise your voice during the meeting, even if you're upset.
She never raises her voice, no matter how stressful the situation gets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shout - to speak very loudly or call out
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly
