heteroploidy

noun
abnormal chromosome number condition
7.1 9.9
Pronunciation: /he.tə.rə.ˈplɔɪ.diː/ /; / /hə.tə.rə.ˈplɔɪ.diː/ (AmE), /he.tə.rə.ˈplɔɪ.diː/ /; / /hə.tə.rə.ˈplɔɪ.diː/ (BrE)
The condition of having a chromosome number that differs from the normal or typical number for a species.

Examples

Heteroploidy in cells
Certain cancers are associated with heteroploidy due to irregular chromosome numbers in tumor cells.
Researchers are studying how heteroploidy affects genetic stability in plants.
The scientist noted heteroploidy in several of the cell samples under the microscope.
