nontrivial

adjective
not simple or easy
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈtrɪviəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈtrɪviəl / (BrE)
not simple, easy, or insignificant; requiring real effort, thought, or having meaningful substance

Examples

A nontrivial problem
Proving the theorem was a nontrivial challenge for the students in the advanced mathematics class.
Designing a secure computer system is a nontrivial task that requires careful planning.
The project involved several nontrivial decisions that affected the final outcome.
Finding a nontrivial solution to the equation required several hours of careful work.
The project presented some nontrivial challenges that the team had to overcome.
Proving this theorem is a nontrivial task even for experienced mathematicians.
Solving this equation is a nontrivial task that requires advanced mathematics.
The company faced nontrivial challenges when expanding into new markets.
Developing a vaccine in such a short time was a nontrivial achievement.
