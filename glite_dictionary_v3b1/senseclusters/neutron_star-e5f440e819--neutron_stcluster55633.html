neutron star

noun
dense collapsed star composed of neutrons
7.4 3.40/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆnuËŒtrÉ‘n stÉ‘r / (AmE), / ËˆnjuËtrÉ’n stÉ‘Ë / (BrE)
A very dense, compact celestial object formed when a massive star collapses under its own gravity, consisting almost entirely of neutrons.

Examples

A neutron star collapse
A neutron star can be just 20 kilometers across yet more massive than our sun.
After the supernova, the core collapsed into a neutron star.
Scientists detected gravitational waves produced by the collision of two neutron stars.
