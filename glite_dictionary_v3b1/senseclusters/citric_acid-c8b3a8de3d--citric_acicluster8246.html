citric acid

noun
a weak acid found in citrus fruits
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪ.trɪk ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈsɪt.rɪk ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈsɪ.trɪk ˈæs.əd/ (AmE), /ˈsɪ.trɪk ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈsɪt.rɪk ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈsɪ.trɪk ˈæs.əd/ (BrE)
a weak, water-soluble acid found in many fruits, especially citrus fruits, and used as a flavoring and preservative

Examples

Citric acid in fruits
Bakers sometimes add citric acid to their doughs to give bread a slightly tangy flavor.
Lemon juice contains a high concentration of citric acid, giving it its sour taste.
Many soft drinks use citric acid as a natural preservative and flavor enhancer.
