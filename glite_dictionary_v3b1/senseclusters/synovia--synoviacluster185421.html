synovia

noun
lubricating fluid in joints
7.9 8.4
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnoʊviə / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊviə / (BrE)
A thick, slippery fluid secreted by the synovial membrane, which lubricates joints and tendon sheaths.

Examples

Thick synovia fluid
Excessive production of synovia can contribute to swelling in arthritic joints.
Healthy synovia is essential for smooth and pain-free joint movement.
The surgeon examined the synovia to determine the extent of the joint's inflammation.
