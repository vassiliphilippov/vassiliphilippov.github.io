ungregarious

adjective
unsociable; preferring solitude
Pronunciation: / ʌnɡrɪˈɡɛriəs /; / ˌʌnɡrɪˈɡɛriəs / (AmE), / ʌnɡrɪˈɡɛəriəs /; / ˌʌnɡrɪˈɡɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
not sociable; tending to be alone or not form groups (used for people or animals)

Examples

An ungregarious person
Despite his colleagues' invitations, he remained ungregarious and preferred to eat lunch alone.
She was an ungregarious child, often found reading by herself rather than playing with others.
The ungregarious nature of the animal made it difficult to study in groups.
Most insects in this group are ungregarious and rarely form colonies.
The ungregarious nature of the species makes it difficult to observe in the wild.
Unlike its relatives, this bird is ungregarious and prefers to live alone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gregarious - fond of company; sociable
