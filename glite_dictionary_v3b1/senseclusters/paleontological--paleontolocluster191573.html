paleontological

adjective
relating to paleontology or fossils
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˌɑːntəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl /; / ˌpeɪliˌɑntəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpaliːɒnˌtɒlədʒɪkəl /; / ˌpæliˌɒntəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with paleontology, the study of prehistoric life forms through fossils

Examples

Paleontological studies
He published several paleontological studies focusing on dinosaur fossils.
The paleontological record provides crucial evidence about the evolution of life on Earth.
The museum’s paleontological collection attracts both researchers and the general public.
Funding for palaeontological expeditions has increased in recent years.
She published several palaeontological studies on the evolution of early mammals.
The museum houses an impressive palaeontological collection from the Jurassic period.
