install

verb
place in office or position
7.7 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈstɔːl/ /; / /ɪn.ˈstɑːl/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈstɔːl/ (BrE)
to formally place someone in an official position or office, often with a ceremony

Examples

To install a leader
She was installed as CEO after the former director retired.
The new board members were installed during a formal ceremony last Friday.
They install a new chairperson every two years in accordance with their bylaws.
After winning the election, she was instaled as the new mayor during a public ceremony.
The university will instal its new chancellor next week in a formal event.
Traditionally, the judge must be officially instaled before presiding over cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inaugurate - to formally begin or induct into office
  • synonym: install - to formally place someone in an office, position, or role
  • antonym: remove - to take someone out of an office or position
