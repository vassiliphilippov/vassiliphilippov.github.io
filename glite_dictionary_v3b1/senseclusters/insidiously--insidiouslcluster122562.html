insidiously

adverb
in a subtle and harmful way
3.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɪdiəsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪdiəsli / (BrE)
In a gradual, subtle, and usually harmful or treacherous manner.

Examples

To act insidiously
Corruption can spread insidiously throughout an organization if not detected early.
The disease advanced insidiously, showing no symptoms until it was too late.
The effects of the policy change occurred insidiously over several years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: openly - in an open and non-secretive manner
  • synonym: subtly - in a delicate or indirect manner
