marrowfat pea

noun
a large variety of pea, used in canning
8.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmær.oʊ.fæt piː/ (AmE), /ˈmær.əʊ.fæt piː/ (BrE)
a large variety of pea which is often matured and processed for canning or making mushy peas

Examples

Canned marrowfat peas
Because they are matured longer on the plant, marrowfat peas have a firmer texture than garden peas.
Canned marrowfat peas are a popular ingredient in many traditional British recipes.
In the UK, marrowfat peas are often used to make mushy peas served with fish and chips.
