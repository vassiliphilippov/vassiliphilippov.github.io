Don

proper noun
Celtic goddess in Welsh mythology
Pronunciation: / dɑn / (AmE), / dɒn / (BrE)
A goddess in Welsh mythology, regarded as the mother of Gwydion and Arianrhod and corresponding to the Irish goddess Danu.

Examples

Welsh goddess Don
In Welsh mythology, Don is revered as the mother goddess of several important deities.
Scholars often compare the legends of Don with those of the Irish goddess Danu.
The children of Don play significant roles in many ancient Celtic stories.
