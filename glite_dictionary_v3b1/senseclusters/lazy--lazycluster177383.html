lazy

adjective
unwilling to work or make effort
1.0 18/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆleÉªzi / (AmE), / ËˆleÉªzi / (BrE)
Unwilling to work or use energy; disinclined to activity or effort.

Examples

A lazy student
Don't be lazy; help us clean up after dinner.
He was too lazy to get out of bed on Saturday morning.
Some students become lazy about completing their assignments.

Synonyms

  • slothful
    7.2 0.38/m
    slothful is habitually slow and idle in action, while lazy is a general unwillingness to work or use energy.
  • indolent
    8.0 0.21/m
    indolent is a formal word for someone who avoids effort by habit, while lazy is the everyday term for unwillingness.
  • shiftless
    8.4 0.36/m
    shiftless shows lack of ambition and initiative, while lazy only says a person is unwilling to work.
  • work-shy
    8.5 0.01/m
    work-shy describes someone who avoids paid work or duty, while lazy means not wanting effort in general.
  • lackadaisical
    8.6 0.33/m
    lackadaisical shows low energy and careless attitude, while lazy focuses on unwillingness to do work or effort.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diligent - showing care and effort in work; antonym of 'lazy'
  • synonym: indolent - habitually lazy or avoiding activity; synonym of 'lazy'
  • synonym: slothful - lazy, disinclined to effort; synonym of 'lazy'
