thermoelastic

adjective
temperature-induced deformation
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜːrmoʊɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to how materials change shape or size when temperature changes affect their elasticity

Examples

Thermoelastic material behavior
Engineers studied the thermoelastic behavior of the bridge materials under extreme heat.
The thermoelastic response of the polymer was measured during the experiment.
Understanding thermoelastic effects is important when designing components for spacecraft.
The thermoelastic properties of the alloy were tested under varying temperatures and loads.
Researchers studied thermoelastic effects to improve the durability of spacecraft materials.
A thermoelastic response can cause a material to expand or contract when heated or cooled.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: elastic - able to return to original shape after being stretched or compressed
  • antonym: inelastic - not able to return to original shape after deformation
  • synonym: thermal expansion - increase in size of a material due to heat
  • synonym: thermomechanical - relating to the combined effects of temperature and mechanical forces
