catadromous

adjective
migrating from freshwater to sea to spawn (of fish)
Pronunciation: / kəˈtædrəməs / (AmE), / kəˈtædrəməs / (BrE)
(of fish) migrating from fresh water to the sea to spawn.

Examples

Catadromous fish migrate
Many eel species are catadromous, living in rivers but spawning at sea.
Scientists studied the catadromous life cycle of the European eel.
The catadromous migration pattern is the opposite of anadromous fish like salmon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anadromous - (of fish) migrating from the sea into fresh water to spawn
