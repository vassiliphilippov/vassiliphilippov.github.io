wobbly

adjective
unsteady or shaky
3.6 2.36/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwÉ‘bli / (AmE), / ËˆwÉ’bli / (BrE)
unsteady and likely to shake or move from side to side, often because of weakness or a defect

Examples

A wobbly step
After spinning in circles, she stood up and took a few wobbly steps.
He felt nervous crossing the wobbly old bridge over the stream.
The table became wobbly after one of the legs broke.

Synonyms

  • unsteady
    3.3 1.64/m
    unsteady means not firm or balanced and can describe people or objects, while wobbly suggests side-to-side shaking from a defect.
  • shaky
    3.3 2.85/m
    shaky feels insecure or likely to fail and can be physical or mental, while wobbly focuses on side-to-side movement.
  • rickety
    5.9 1.84/m
    rickety is weak and likely to collapse because of poor build, while wobbly mainly means it shakes or rocks.
  • tottery
    8.4 0.01/m
    tottery describes a person who walks or stands unsteadily from weakness, while wobbly often describes objects that shake.
  • wonky
    7.7 0.18/m
    wonky is informal and can mean crooked or not working right, while wobbly refers to shaking or unstable movement.
  • tippy
    7.5 0.23/m
    tippy means likely to tip over easily, especially a small object, while wobbly describes side-to-side shaking without tipping.
  • unstable
    4.4 6.17/m
    unstable suggests long-term insecurity or sudden change in condition, while wobbly usually means a short, shaky side-to-side movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unstable - not firmly fixed; likely to move or fall
