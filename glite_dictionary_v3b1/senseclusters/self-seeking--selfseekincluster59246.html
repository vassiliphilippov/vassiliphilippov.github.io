self-seeking

noun
pursuit of personal advantage
4.5
Pronunciation: / sɛlfˈsiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛlfˈsiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of pursuing one's own interests or advantage, often with little or no consideration for others.

Examples

His self-seeking
His self-seeking became obvious when he betrayed his colleagues for personal gain.
She was accused of self-seeking during the campaign, putting her ambitions above those of the community.
They warned against self-seeking as a destructive force within the organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • synonym: selfishness - concern with one's own advantage to the exclusion of others
