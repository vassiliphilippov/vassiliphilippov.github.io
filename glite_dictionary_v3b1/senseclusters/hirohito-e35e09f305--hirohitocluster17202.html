Hirohito

proper noun
Emperor of Japan (1926–1989)
7.9
Pronunciation: / hiˌroʊˈhiːtoʊ / (AmE), / hɪˌrəʊˈhiːtəʊ / (BrE)
(1901–1989) Emperor of Japan from 1926 to 1989, who renounced his divine status and became a constitutional monarch after Japan's defeat in World War II.

Examples

Emperor Hirohito of Japan
After the war, Hirohito became a symbol of Japan's new constitutional monarchy.
During World War II, Hirohito was the emperor of Japan and a prominent figure in world affairs.
Many historians study the role Hirohito played in the events leading up to Japan's surrender.
