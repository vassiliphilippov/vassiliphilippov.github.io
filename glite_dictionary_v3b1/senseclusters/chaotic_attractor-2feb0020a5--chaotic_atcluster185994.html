chaotic attractor

noun
attractor causing chaotic movement in phase space
8.8 9.0
Pronunciation: /keɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk ə.ˈtræk.tər/ (AmE), /keɪ.ˈɒ.tɪk ə.ˈtræk.tə/ (BrE)
in chaos theory, an attractor in a dynamical system where trajectories tend toward a complex, non-repeating pattern, exhibiting chaotic behavior

Examples

A chaotic attractor pattern
In certain equations, a chaotic attractor can cause outcomes to diverge dramatically from small changes in initial conditions.
Scientists study the properties of a chaotic attractor to understand long-term behavior in complex systems.
The weather's unpredictability is sometimes explained by reference to a chaotic attractor in atmospheric dynamics.
