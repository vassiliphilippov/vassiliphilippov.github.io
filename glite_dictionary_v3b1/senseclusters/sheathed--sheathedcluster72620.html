sheathed

adjective
enclosed in a protective covering
Pronunciation: / ʃiðt / (AmE), / ʃiːðt / (BrE)
enclosed or covered with a protective layer or case, sometimes used in combination to describe being encased in a particular material

Examples

Sheathed in copper
Copper-sheathed hulls protected ships from barnacles in the eighteenth century.
The cat's sheathed claws retracted harmlessly as it stretched.
The knight drew his sheathed sword with a swift motion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unsheathed - not enclosed in a protective covering
