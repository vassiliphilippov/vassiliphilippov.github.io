treasonously

adverb
in a traitorous way
Pronunciation: / ˈtriːzənəsli / (AmE), / ˈtriːzənəsli / (BrE)
in a way that betrays trust or loyalty, especially to one's country or government

Examples

Act treasonously
The official was accused of acting treasonously by passing confidential information to a foreign power.
Speaking treasonously against the government was considered a serious crime in that country.
He was punished for behaving treasonously during the time of war.
He treasonously shared the company's secrets with a competitor for personal gain.
The general was accused of acting treasonously by supporting the enemy's plans.
She treasonously undermined her friend's confidence by spreading false rumors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disloyally - in a way that shows lack of loyalty
  • antonym: faithfully - in a way that shows loyalty and support
  • antonym: loyally - in a way that shows support or allegiance
  • synonym: traitorously - in a manner showing betrayal or disloyalty
