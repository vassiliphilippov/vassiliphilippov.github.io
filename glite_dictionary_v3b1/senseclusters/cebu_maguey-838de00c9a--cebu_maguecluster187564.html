Cebu maguey

noun
fiber from Philippine agave for coarse twine
Pronunciation: / sɛˈbuː məˈɡeɪ / (AmE), / sɛˈbuː məˈɡeɪ / (BrE)
A hard fiber obtained from Philippine agave plants, used for making coarse twine and rope.

Examples

Coarse Cebu maguey fiber
Coarse sacks woven from Cebu maguey were used to transport crops to the market.
Demand for Cebu maguey increased as industries sought natural fibers for packaging.
The fishermen made nets from Cebu maguey because of its strength and durability.
