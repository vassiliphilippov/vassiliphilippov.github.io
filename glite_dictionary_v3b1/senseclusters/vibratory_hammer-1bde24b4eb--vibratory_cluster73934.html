vibratory hammer

noun
machine for driving piles using vibrations
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪbrəˌtɔri ˈhæmər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪbrətəri ˈhamə / (BrE)
A machine that uses vibrations to drive piles into or extract them from the ground in construction work.

Examples

A vibratory hammer machine
A vibratory hammer generates less noise than a traditional impact pile driver, making it suitable for urban areas.
The construction crew used a vibratory hammer to install the steel beams quickly and efficiently.
The engineers recommended extracting the old piles with a vibratory hammer to avoid damaging nearby structures.
