actually

adverb
in fact; really; in actual fact
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈaktʃ.u.əli /; / ˈæk.tʃu.əli /; / ˈaktʃʊəli / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is really true, especially when it is surprising, unexpected, or contradicts what was said before

Examples

Used actually to emphasize
I didn’t think I’d like the movie, but I actually enjoyed it a lot.
Many people believe the rumor, but it’s not actually true.
She said she was busy, but she was actually at the café with her friends.
I was surprised that he actually finished the entire marathon.
She actually apologized for her mistake, which we didn't expect.
We thought the shop was closed, but it was actually open all night.
Actually, I was planning to leave early this evening if that's all right.
He didn't apologize—as a matter of fact, he seemed actually rather pleased.
I don't mind helping at all, actually.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apparently - as far as one knows or can see; seemingly
  • degree higher: literally - used for emphasis, especially to state something is true in a strict sense
  • synonym: really - in actual fact; truly; genuinely
