Charadriiformes

noun
order of aquatic birds (shorebirds, gulls, etc.)
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˌrædriˈɪfɔrˌmiz / (AmE), / kəˌrædriˈɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic birds, including shorebirds and gulls, found along coasts and inland waters, most of which feed on animal life

Examples

Charadriiformes shorebirds and gulls
Many species of Charadriiformes migrate thousands of miles each year along coastal flyways.
Scientists study Charadriiformes to understand the evolution of shorebirds.
The ecology of Charadriiformes makes them important indicators of wetland health.
