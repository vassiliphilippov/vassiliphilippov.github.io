Baconian

adjective
of Francis Bacon's ideas
9.7
Pronunciation: /beɪ.ˈkoʊ.nɪ.ən/ /; / /bə.ˈkoʊ.nɪ.ən/ (AmE), /bə.ˈkəʊ.nɪ.ən/ /; / /beɪ.ˈkəʊ.nɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the ideas, methods, or philosophy of Francis Bacon, especially his emphasis on empirical scientific method

Examples

Baconian method
The research team adopted a Baconian approach, relying on careful observation and experimentation.
Her Baconian attitude led her to question assumptions and seek evidence before drawing conclusions.
Many modern scientists are influenced by Baconian principles in their work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory
  • antonym: theoretical - based on theory rather than practical evidence
