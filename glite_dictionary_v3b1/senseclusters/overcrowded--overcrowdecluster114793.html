overcrowded

adjective
too full of people
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkraʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkraʊdɪd / (BrE)
containing more people or things than the space can handle, making it uncomfortable or less effective

Examples

An overcrowded hospital
The hospital was so overcrowded that patients had to wait in the hallway.
Many classrooms in the city are overcrowded, making it hard for students to learn.
During the festival, the buses became overcrowded with tourists and locals.
The subway was so overcrowded during rush hour that people could barely move.
Many schools in the city are overcrowded, making it difficult for students to learn comfortably.
The shelter became overcrowded after the storm, and some people had to sleep outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cramped - uncomfortably small or crowded
  • degree lower: crowded - having too many people or things in one place
  • synonym: packed - filled with as many people or things as possible
  • antonym: spacious - having plenty of space
