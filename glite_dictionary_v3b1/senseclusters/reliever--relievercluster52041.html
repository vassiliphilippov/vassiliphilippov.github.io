reliever

noun
baseball pitcher who does not start the game
4.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlivɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A pitcher who enters the game after the starting pitcher, usually to replace them and continue pitching.

Examples

Baseball reliever
As a rookie, she quickly established herself as a dependable reliever for the club.
Managers often rely on a reliever to get the team out of difficult situations.
The team brought in a reliever after the starter gave up five runs.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: closer - a relief pitcher designated to pitch in the final innings
  • antonym: starter - the pitcher who begins the baseball game for a team
