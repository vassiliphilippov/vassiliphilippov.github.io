limpy

adjective
having a limp
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmpi / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpi / (BrE)
moving or walking in a way that shows a limp or weakness

Examples

Limpy walk
After twisting his ankle, he had a limpy walk for several days.
The old cat moved in a limpy manner across the garden.
Her limpy steps made it clear she was still recovering from her injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lame - unable to walk properly because of injury or weakness
  • antonym: steady - moving in a stable and even way
