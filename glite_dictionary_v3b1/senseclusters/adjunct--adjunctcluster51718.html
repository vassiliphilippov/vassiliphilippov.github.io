adjunct

noun
optional grammatical element in a sentence
9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (BrE)
(Grammar) A word, phrase, or clause added to a sentence to give extra information but which is not essential to its structure.

Examples

A grammar adjunct
In the sentence 'She ran quickly,' the word quickly is an adjunct that describes how she ran.
Temporal adjuncts like 'yesterday' or 'this morning' can specify when an action took place.
The phrase 'with a smile' acts as an adjunct in 'He greeted me with a smile.'
