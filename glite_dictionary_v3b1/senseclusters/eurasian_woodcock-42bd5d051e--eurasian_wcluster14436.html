Eurasian woodcock

noun
migratory woodland bird of Europe and Asia
Pronunciation: / jʊˈreɪʒən ˈwʊdkɑk / (AmE), / jʊəˈreɪʒ(ɪ)ən ˈwʊdkɒk / (BrE)
a short-legged, long-billed migratory bird (Scolopax rusticola) native to Europe and Asia, noted for its mottled brown plumage and woodland habitat

Examples

The Eurasian woodcock bird
Hunters in some regions prize the Eurasian woodcock for its elusive nature and striking plumage.
The Eurasian woodcock migrates across great distances between its breeding and wintering grounds.
We spotted a Eurasian woodcock in the dense undergrowth during our morning walk.
