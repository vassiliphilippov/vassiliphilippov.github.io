auscultate

verb
listen to body sounds for diagnosis
9.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːskəlteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːskəlteɪt / (BrE)
to examine (a patient) by listening to the sounds made by the body, typically using a stethoscope

Examples

To auscultate the heart
After placing the stethoscope, she began to auscultate the patient's heart rhythm carefully.
Doctors frequently auscultate to detect murmurs or other irregularities during an examination.
The physician will auscultate your chest to check for abnormal lung sounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auscultation - the act of listening to the internal sounds of the body, usually with a stethoscope
