disconfirm

verb
to prove untrue
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskənˈfɜrm / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to show that something is not true or is not supported by evidence

Examples

To disconfirm a theory
The scientist tried to disconfirm the results by repeating the experiment with new methods.
Additional data may disconfirm the initial assumptions made by the research team.
If the facts disconfirm your theory, you should consider revising your hypothesis.
The scientist's findings disconfirm the widely accepted theory.
If the results disconfirm our assumptions, we will need to revise our model.
New evidence may disconfirm what was previously believed to be true.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirm - to show that something is true or correct
  • synonym: disprove - to show that something is not true
  • synonym: refute - to prove that something is wrong or false
