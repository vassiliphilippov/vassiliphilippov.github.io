rheostat

noun
device for varying electrical current
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈriː.əˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈriː.əʊ.stæt / (BrE)
an electrical device that allows the adjustment of current flow by varying resistance, typically using a sliding contact

Examples

Adjust the rheostat
Adjust the rheostat to increase the resistance and reduce the current.
Old theater lighting systems often used a rheostat for dimming the lights.
The brightness of the lamp was controlled with a rheostat connected to the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: potentiometer - an adjustable resistor for measuring or controlling voltage
