capture

noun
removal of a piece in chess
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.t̓ʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə / (BrE)
in chess and similar games, the act of removing an opponent's piece from the board as a result of a permitted move

Examples

Chess capture
Her queen made a brilliant capture that changed the course of the game.
Pawn capture is a common tactic in the opening of a chess match.
The capture of his knight left him at a serious disadvantage.
