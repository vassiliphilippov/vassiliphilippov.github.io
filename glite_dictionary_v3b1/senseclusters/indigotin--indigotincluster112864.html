indigotin

noun
blue dye from indigo or synthetic sources
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪgətɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪgətɪn / (BrE)
a blue dye obtained from indigo plants or made synthetically, used especially in textiles

Examples

Natural indigotin dye
Chemists analyzed the sample and confirmed that it contained natural indigotin extracted from plants.
Modern denim often gets its characteristic color from synthetic indigotin.
The brilliant color of the fabric was achieved by adding indigotin during the dyeing process.
