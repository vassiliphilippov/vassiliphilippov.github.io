thermoset

adjective
permanently hardened by heat (material)
7.5 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈθɜːr.moʊ.set/ /; / /ˈθɜːr.mə.set/ (AmE), /ˈθɜː.məʊ.set/ /; / /ˈθɜː.mə.set/ (BrE)
(of a material) becoming permanently hard and rigid when heated or cured, unable to be remelted.

Examples

Thermoset plastic material
Epoxy adhesives are widely known for their thermoset properties in construction projects.
Once cured, the material becomes a thermoset and cannot be reshaped by heating.
Phenolic compounds are a classic example of a thermoset plastic used in manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thermoplastic - (of a material) becoming soft when heated and hard when cooled repeatedly
