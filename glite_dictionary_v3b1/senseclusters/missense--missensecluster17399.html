missense

noun
genetic mutation type
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌsɛns / (BrE)
a genetic mutation in which a single nucleotide change results in a different amino acid in a protein

Examples

A missense in DNA
A missense in the gene caused the protein to function abnormally.
Doctors discovered a rare missense that explained the patient's symptoms.
The study focused on the effects of a single missense in the DNA sequence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonsense - a mutation that introduces a premature stop codon in a gene
  • antonym: silent mutation - a mutation that does not change the amino acid sequence of a protein
