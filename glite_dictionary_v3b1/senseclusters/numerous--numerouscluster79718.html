Synonyms

  • multitudinous
    7.9 0.3/m
    multitudinous describes very large crowds in a formal way, while numerous is common and less literary.
  • legion
    6.8 0.00/m
    legion often describes a very large number in formal or poetic speech, while numerous is more neutral everyday adjective.
  • myriad
    7.0 6/m
    myriad often appears as 'a myriad of' to mean countless examples, while numerous means many but is less extreme.
  • countless
    2.5 18/m
    countless means too many to count and feels very strong, while numerous means many but could be counted.
  • innumerable
    5.4 3/m
    innumerable stresses that something cannot be counted, while numerous just says there are many without that impossibility.
