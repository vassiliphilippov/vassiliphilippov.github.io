numerous

adjective
existing in large numbers

Pronunciation: / ÀànuÀê.m…ô.r…ôs / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀê.m…ô.r…ôs / (BrE)
Existing or occurring in large numbers; many.

Examples

A numerous number
She has received numerous awards for her work in the community.
The company has numerous offices around the world.
We faced numerous challenges during the project, but we succeeded in the end.

Synonyms

  • many
    1.0 0.41/m
    many is an everyday word for a large number of items, while numerous sounds more formal and adjective-like.
  • multitudinous
    7.9 0.26/m
    multitudinous describes very large crowds in a formal way, while numerous is common and less literary.
  • legion
    6.8 0.00/m
    legion often describes a very large number in formal or poetic speech, while numerous is more neutral everyday adjective.
  • myriad
    7.0 5.22/m
    myriad often appears as 'a myriad of' to mean countless examples, while numerous means many but is less extreme.
  • countless
    2.5 17/m
    countless means too many to count and feels very strong, while numerous means many but could be counted.
  • innumerable
    5.4 3.13/m
    innumerable stresses that something cannot be counted, while numerous just says there are many without that impossibility.
