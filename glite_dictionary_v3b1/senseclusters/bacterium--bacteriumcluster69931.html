bacterium

noun
single-celled microorganism; often disease-causing
Pronunciation: / bækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A microscopic single-celled organism with no nucleus, some of which can cause disease or play a role in various biochemical processes.

Examples

A bacterium cell
A bacterium can multiply rapidly under the right conditions, sometimes doubling every twenty minutes.
The doctor explained that the infection was caused by a harmful bacterium found in contaminated water.
Unlike a virus, a bacterium can often be treated with antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteria - plural form of bacterium
  • antonym: virus - microscopic infectious agent, not a cell
