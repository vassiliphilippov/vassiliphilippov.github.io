Prakrit language

noun
ancient vernacular Indic language
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈprɑː.krɪt ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈprɑː.krɪt ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (BrE)
any of the vernacular ancient Indic languages of northern and central India, distinct from Sanskrit, and recorded from the 3rd century BC to the 4th century AD.

Examples

Prakrit language texts
Jain scriptures were often composed in the Prakrit language rather than in Sanskrit.
Scholars have studied texts written in Prakrit language to understand ancient Indian society.
The spread of Buddhism contributed to the literary development of several Prakrit languages.
