sonication

noun
ultrasound tissue treatment
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɒnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of focused ultrasound waves to destroy or reduce unwanted tissue without surgery, especially as a medical treatment

Examples

Medical sonication treatment
The doctor recommended sonication to treat the patient's tumor without invasive surgery.
Recent advances in sonication have made it possible to target tissue more precisely.
Many clinics now offer sonication as a noninvasive alternative to traditional procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: surgery - medical treatment involving an operation
  • synonym: ultrasound therapy - medical treatment using high-frequency sound waves
