colorlessness

noun
lack of color
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚlɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌləlɪsnəs /; / ˈkʌlə.lɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being without color or hue; lacking chromatic color

Examples

The colorlessness of lips
Doctors checked the patient for signs such as colorlessness of the lips or fingers.
He admired the colorlessness of the crystal, which made it seem pure and flawless.
The colorlessness of the sky suggested an overcast and gloomy afternoon.
The colourlessness of the walls made the room feel cold and sterile.
The artist deliberately used colourlessness to evoke a sense of emptiness in his paintings.
The fog had a peculiar colourlessness, making it hard to distinguish any objects through it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colorful - full of color; having much or varied color
  • synonym: colorlessness - the quality or state of being without color; mainly American spelling
