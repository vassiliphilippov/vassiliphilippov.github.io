tRNA

noun
molecule that transports amino acids during protein synthesis
6.8
Pronunciation: /tiː ɑːr en eɪ/ /; / /ˈtræns.fər ɑːr en eɪ/ (AmE), /tiː ɑː en eɪ/ /; / /ˈtræns.fə ɑː en eɪ/ (BrE)
A type of RNA molecule that helps build proteins by carrying specific amino acids to the ribosome during protein synthesis according to the instructions in mRNA.

Examples

tRNA carries amino acids
Each type of tRNA carries a specific amino acid to the growing protein chain.
Scientists study how mutations in tRNA can affect the accuracy of protein assembly.
The tRNA matches its anticodon with a codon on the mRNA strand during protein synthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: messenger RNA - RNA that carries genetic information from DNA to the ribosome
  • synonym: ribosomal RNA - RNA component of the ribosome
