deictic

adjective
context-dependent in meaning (linguistics)
7.7 9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪk.tɪk/ /; / /ˈdeɪk.tɪk/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪk.tɪk/ /; / /ˈdeɪk.tɪk/ (BrE)
(of a word or expression) referring to or dependent on the context in which it is used, such as time, place, or speaker-related terms (e.g., 'here', 'now', 'I').

Examples

Deictic expression
'Now' and 'there' are both deictic words whose reference depends on who is speaking and when.
She explained the difference between deictic and non-deictic expressions in her linguistics class.
The word 'this' is a classic deictic term, as its meaning shifts with context.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indexical - (of a word or expression) indicating or pointing to something by virtue of context; similar technical term in linguistics
