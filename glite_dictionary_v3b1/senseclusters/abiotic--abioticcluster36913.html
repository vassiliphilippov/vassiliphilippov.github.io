abiotic

adjective
non-living elements
6.7
Pronunciation: /eɪ.baɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk/ /; / /ə.baɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /eɪ.baɪ.ˈɒ.tɪk/ /; / /ə.baɪ.ˈɒ.tɪk/ (BrE)
relating to non-living physical and chemical elements in an environment, such as sunlight, temperature, or minerals

Examples

Abiotic factors
Sunlight and temperature are important abiotic factors in an ecosystem.
The scientist studied how abiotic conditions like soil type affect plant growth.
Changes in abiotic elements, such as rainfall, can impact the entire environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biotic - relating to living things in an environment
  • synonym: inanimate - not alive, especially not in the manner of animals and humans
