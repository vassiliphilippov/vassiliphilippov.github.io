cedar elm

noun
a species of elm tree native to southern US and Mexico
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːdɚ ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə ɛlm / (BrE)
a medium-sized elm tree of the southern United States and Mexico, known for its spreading, pendulous, corky branches

Examples

A large cedar elm
A large cedar elm shaded the old farmhouse, its branches covered with rough corky bark.
Gardeners often plant cedar elms for their resistance to drought and urban conditions.
The cedar elm thrives in the hot, dry climates of Texas and northern Mexico.
