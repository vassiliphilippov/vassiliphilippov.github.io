prerelease

noun
early version or event
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːrɪˈliːs / (AmE), / ˌpriːrɪˈliːs / (BrE)
a version or event that happens before the official release of a product, such as software or a movie

Examples

A prerelease event
The company offered a prerelease of the game to select players for feedback.
Reviewers received a prerelease copy of the film before it hit theaters.
Many bugs were fixed after the prerelease version was tested by users.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beta - a test version of software released before the final version
  • antonym: release - the official launch or publication of a product
