grease one's palms

verb
bribe someone; make illegal payments
8.5 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / …°riÀês w ånz p…ëÀêmz / (AmE), / …°riÀês w ånz p…ëÀêmz / (BrE)
to give someone money secretly or illegally in order to persuade them to do something for you, especially to use their influence or position dishonestly; to bribe someone

Examples

To grease a few palms
Rumor has it that the contract was secured by greasing a few palms along the way.
They tried to grease the inspector's palms to avoid a hefty fine.
To get anything done at the government office, you often have to grease someone's palms.

Synonyms

  • grease someone's palm
    8.1 0.00/m
    grease someone's palm is a direct, common spoken phrase for secret bribery, while grease one's palms is the standard idiom form.
  • bribe
    4.1 4.92/m
    bribe means illegally paying or offering gifts to influence someone, while grease one's palms is an informal idiom for the same secret act.
  • buy
    8.9 20/m
    buy can mean to pay someone for favors or influence, often in casual speech, while grease one's palms is an informal idiom for secret bribery.

Related Terms
