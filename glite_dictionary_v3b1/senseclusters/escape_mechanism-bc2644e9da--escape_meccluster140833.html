escape mechanism

noun
behavior avoiding unpleasant reality
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskeɪp ˌmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp ˌmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
a mental process or behavioral pattern that helps a person avoid facing unpleasant realities or emotions

Examples

An escape mechanism in mind
Daydreaming can serve as an escape mechanism when reality feels overwhelming.
Many people develop an escape mechanism to cope with traumatic experiences.
Watching television became her favorite escape mechanism after stressful days at work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defense mechanism - an unconscious psychological strategy used to cope with reality and maintain self-image
