pretrial

adjective
before a trial
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈtraɪəl / (BrE)
happening or existing before a court trial begins

Examples

Pretrial hearing
The judge scheduled a pretrial hearing to address legal motions.
During the pretrial phase, both sides exchanged important documents.
A pretrial agreement was reached, avoiding the need for a full trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: posttrial - happening or existing after a court trial
  • synonym: preliminary - coming before the main event or activity
