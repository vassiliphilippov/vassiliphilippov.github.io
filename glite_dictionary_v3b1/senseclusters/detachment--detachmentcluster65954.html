detachment

noun
small military unit separated from main force
7.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtatʃmənt / (BrE)
a small group of troops that is separated from a larger military unit for a particular purpose

Examples

A detachment of soldiers
A detachment of soldiers was sent ahead to scout the enemy position.
A small detachment remained behind to guard the supplies.
The general ordered a detachment to secure the bridge overnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: platoon - a subdivision of a company of soldiers, usually containing two or more squads
  • synonym: squad - a small military unit or group
