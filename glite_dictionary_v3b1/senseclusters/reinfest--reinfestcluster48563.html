reinfest

verb
to become infested again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈfɛst / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɛst / (BrE)
to become or make infested again by pests or parasites after a previous infestation was removed

Examples

To reinfest with pests
If the area is not cleaned thoroughly, it may reinfest with cockroaches within weeks.
The dog was treated for fleas, but it could reinfest if exposed again.
Farmers worry that their crops will reinfest after the initial pesticide treatment wears off.
If you do not seal the cracks, termites may reinfest your house within a few months.
The veterinarian warned that the dog could reinfest the home if its bedding was not cleaned.
After the initial treatment, the crops were reinfested by locusts during the rainy season.

  • synonym: infest - to overrun or inhabit in large numbers, especially as pests
