vena obturatoria

noun
vein draining hip joints and thigh muscles
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪnə ˌɑb.tʃəˈreɪ.ti.ə / (AmE), / ˌviː.nə ˌɒb.tjʊˈreɪ.tɪ.ə / (BrE)
a vein formed by the union of tributaries from the hip joints and thigh muscles, which drains into the internal iliac vein

Examples

vena obturatoria vein
Injury to the vena obturatoria can lead to significant bleeding in pelvic fractures.
The vena obturatoria drains blood from the muscles surrounding the inner thigh.
The surgeon carefully identified the vena obturatoria during the pelvic procedure.
