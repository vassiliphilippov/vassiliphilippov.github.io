stalk

noun
loose plant material separated from seeds
2.4
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)
The material made up of seed coverings and small pieces of stem or leaves that have been separated from the seeds, especially after threshing or processing.

Examples

Burning stalk after harvest
Farmers often burn the stalk after harvesting the crops to clear the fields.
She used the leftover stalk as bedding for the animals.
The floor was covered with stalk and other plant debris from the grain processing.
