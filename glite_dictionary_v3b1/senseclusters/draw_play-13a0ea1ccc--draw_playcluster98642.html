draw play

noun
football play: fake pass, then run
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɔː ˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔː ˌpleɪ / (BrE)
An offensive play in American football in which the quarterback pretends to pass before handing the ball to a running back, who attempts to run through the defensive line.

Examples

A draw play in football
On third down, the coach called for a draw play instead of a traditional pass.
The team executed a perfect draw play that caught the defense off guard.
Their running back gained forty yards on a draw play in the second quarter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pass play - A play where the quarterback throws the ball forward
  • synonym: running play - Any play where the ball is advanced by running rather than passing
