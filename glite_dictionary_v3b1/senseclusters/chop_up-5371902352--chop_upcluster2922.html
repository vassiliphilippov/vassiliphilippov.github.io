chop up

verb
cut into small pieces repeatedly
1.9 1.38/m
Pronunciation: / tÊƒÉ‘p ÊŒp / (AmE), / tÊƒÉ’p ÊŒp / (BrE)
to cut something into smaller pieces using repeated sharp blows, especially with a knife or axe

Examples

To chop up vegetables
Before adding the carrots to the stew, make sure to chop up all the vegetables.
She asked her brother to chop up some wood for the fireplace.
The chef chopped up the onions quickly and added them to the pan.

Synonyms

  • cut up
    1.1 1.81/m
    cut up means cutting or tearing food or cloth into small pieces, while chop up means cutting into smaller pieces with repeated sharp blows.
  • chop
    1.0 8.10/m
    chop means cut with a sharp blow, often one or a few strikes, while chop up means cutting into many small pieces with repeated blows.
