escalation

noun
an increase in seriousness, intensity, or cost
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɛskəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛskəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a rapid or gradual increase in seriousness, intensity, or cost, especially as a response to an existing situation or threat

Examples

An escalation in spending
He warned that an escalation in spending could damage the company's financial health.
Negotiators are trying to prevent any escalation in the conflict.
The sudden escalation of tensions surprised the international community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: increase - an instance of becoming greater or larger
  • antonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less in amount
