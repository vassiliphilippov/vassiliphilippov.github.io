nihil obstat

noun
official Church declaration of no objection to publication
9.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnaɪ.hɪl ˈɑːb.stæt/ /; / /ˈniː.hɪl ˈɑːb.stæt/ /; / /ˈnɪ.hɪl ˈɑːb.stæt/ (AmE), /ˈnaɪ.hɪl ˈɒb.stæt/ /; / /ˈniː.hɪl ˈɒb.stæt/ /; / /ˈnɪ.hɪl ˈɒb.stæt/ (BrE)
an official statement (especially in the Roman Catholic Church) that a publication has been examined and contains nothing objectionable to faith or morals

Examples

A nihil obstat statement
A nihil obstat is required for Catholic religious texts to ensure doctrinal accuracy.
Before printing, the book was granted a nihil obstat by Church authorities.
The manuscript received a nihil obstat before proceeding to publication.
A nihil obstat does not imply endorsement, only that nothing in the work is objectionable to faith or morals.
Before publication, the manuscript received the Church's nihil obstat and was deemed free of doctrinal error.
The bishop granted a nihil obstat after carefully reviewing the theological content of the text.
