restorative

adjective
promoting recovery or health
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstɔr.ə.tɪv /; / rəˈstɔr.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈstɒr.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
having the ability to make someone feel better, stronger, or healthier again

Examples

Restorative sleep
A good night's sleep can have a remarkably restorative effect on your mood and energy.
She drank a restorative herbal tea to help her recover from the cold.
The spa offered a range of restorative treatments designed to help guests relax and heal.
A walk in the crisp morning air can have a truly restorative effect on your mood.
After several days of illness, she found a restorative soup helpful in regaining her strength.
Many people find meditation to be a restorative practice for both mind and body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: debilitating - making someone weak or feeble
  • synonym: invigorating - making someone feel more energetic
  • synonym: recuperative - having the ability to restore health or strength
  • synonym: reviving - giving new energy or life
