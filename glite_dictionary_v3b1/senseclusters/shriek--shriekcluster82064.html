shriek

noun
high-pitched, piercing sound
2.7 3.99/m
Pronunciation: /  Érik / (AmE), /  ÉriÀêk / (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched cry or sound, often showing fear, pain, excitement, or laughter

Examples

A loud shriek
A shriek echoed through the empty hallway, startling everyone nearby.
The brakes let out a piercing shriek as the bus stopped suddenly.
There was a shriek of alarm when the lights suddenly went out.
A sudden shriek startled everyone in the quiet library.
The child let out a shriek when she saw the spider.
Their laughter turned into a shriek as the roller coaster took off.

Synonyms

  • scream
    1.0 19/m
    a scream is a loud, often long cry used for fear or pain, while a shriek is shorter and more high-pitched.
  • squeal
    2.9 2.09/m
    a squeal is a long, high, often excited or protesting cry, while a shriek is usually shorter, sharper, and more urgent.
  • screech
    2.9 2.03/m
    a screech is a harsh, piercing sound like brakes or birds, while a shriek is a high human cry showing fear or pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scream - a loud, high-pitched cry
  • synonym: screech - a loud, harsh, piercing cry
  • antonym: whisper - a soft or confidential tone of voice
  • synonym: yell - a loud, sharp cry or shout
