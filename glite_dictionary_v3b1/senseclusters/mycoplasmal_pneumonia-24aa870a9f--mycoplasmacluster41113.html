mycoplasmal pneumonia

noun
acute pneumonia caused by mycoplasma bacteria
4.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kə.ˈplæz.məl nuː.ˈmoʊ.njə/ /; / /maɪ.kə.ˈplæz.məl nə.ˈmoʊ.njə/ /; / /maɪ.kə.ˈplæz.məl njuː.ˈmoʊ.njə/ (AmE), /maɪ.kə.ˈplæz.məl njuː.ˈməʊ.niː.ə/ /; / /maɪ.kə.ˈplæz.məl nuː.ˈməʊ.niː.ə/ (BrE)
An acute respiratory illness with high fever and cough, caused by mycoplasma bacteria, primarily affecting children and young adults.

Examples

Mycoplasmal pneumonia infection
During the outbreak, several students developed mycoplasmal pneumonia and were treated with antibiotics.
The doctor diagnosed her with mycoplasmal pneumonia after reviewing her symptoms and chest X-ray.
Unlike typical pneumonia, mycoplasmal pneumonia often presents with a lingering, dry cough.
