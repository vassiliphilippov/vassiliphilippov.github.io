apatheia

noun
state of indifference
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈθiːə / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈθiːə / (BrE)
a state of being without passion or emotion, showing indifference or lack of concern

Examples

Philosophical apatheia
The philosopher valued apatheia as a way to remain calm in difficult situations.
Her apatheia toward the heated debate surprised everyone in the room.
In ancient Stoic thought, apatheia was considered a sign of wisdom and self-control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
