papaya family

noun
family of tropical trees, including papaya
Pronunciation: / pəˈpaɪ.ə ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / pəˈpɑː.jə ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
A family of trees native to tropical America and Africa, characterized by milky sap and large palmately lobed leaves, including the papaya.

Examples

The papaya family trees
Members of the papaya family can be found in both Africa and the Americas.
Scientists studied the milky sap unique to the papaya family for its medicinal properties.
The papaya family includes several species of tropical fruit-bearing trees.
