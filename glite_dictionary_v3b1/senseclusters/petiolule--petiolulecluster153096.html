petiolule

noun
stalk of a leaflet
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːtiəˌluːl / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɪəˌljuːl / (BrE)
the small stalk that attaches a leaflet to the main stem of a compound leaf

Examples

The petiolule length
Botanists measured the length of each petiolule to study leaf development.
If a petiolule is damaged, the corresponding leaflet may wither and die.
The petiolule connects each leaflet to the main rachis of a compound leaf.

Related Terms

  • synonym: petiole - the stalk attaching a leaf to the plant stem
