coccidia

noun
microscopic protozoan parasites
9.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑkˈsɪdiə / (AmE), / kɒkˈsɪdiə / (BrE)
A group of microscopic, spore-forming, single-celled protozoan parasites that infect the cells of animals, often causing disease.

Examples

Infectious coccidia parasites
Some species of coccidia can cause severe diarrhea in livestock.
The presence of coccidia is a common problem in crowded animal shelters.
Veterinarians routinely check for coccidia in stool samples from young puppies and kittens.
