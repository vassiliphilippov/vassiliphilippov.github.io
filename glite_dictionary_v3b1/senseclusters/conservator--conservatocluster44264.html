conservator

noun
court-appointed guardian or manager
8.1 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàs…ùÀê.ve…™.t…ö / (AmE), / k…ônÀàs…úÀê.v…ô.t…ô / (BrE)
a person appointed by a court to manage the affairs of a minor or of someone who is legally incapable of managing their own affairs

Examples

Court-appointed conservator
After the accident, a conservator was named to protect his interests.
As her conservator, he was responsible for making all legal and medical decisions on her behalf.
The court appointed a conservator to oversee the financial affairs of the elderly woman.

Synonyms

  • legal guardian
    3.4 0.68/m
    legal guardian cares for a person's welfare and daily needs, while conservator mainly manages that person's financial and legal affairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guardian - a person who is legally appointed to care for another person, especially a child
  • antonym: ward - a person, especially a minor, who is under the protection or care of a guardian or conservator
