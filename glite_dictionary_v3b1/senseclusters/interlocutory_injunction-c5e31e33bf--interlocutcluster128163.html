interlocutory injunction

noun
temporary court order during a trial
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈlɑːk.tər.i ɪn.ˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.tər.ˈlɔːk.tər.i ɪn.ˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈlɒk.tə.ri ɪn.ˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən/ (BrE)
A court order issued during a trial to maintain the status quo or preserve the subject matter of the litigation until the trial is concluded.

Examples

An interlocutory injunction order
The company sought an interlocutory injunction to prevent its competitor from using the disputed technology.
The judge granted an interlocutory injunction to preserve the parties' rights until the case is decided.
Without an interlocutory injunction, important evidence might have been destroyed before the final judgment.

  • synonym: injunction - a court order that compels or prevents an action
