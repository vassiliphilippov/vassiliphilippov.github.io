Douglas hemlock

noun
a type of Douglas fir tree
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌɡləs ˈhɛm.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs ˈhem.lɒk / (BrE)
A tall Douglas fir tree native to northwestern North America, known for its short needles and egg-shaped cones.

Examples

The Douglas hemlock tree
The Douglas hemlock is highly valued for its strong, durable timber.
The short needles and egg-shaped cones help identify the Douglas hemlock among other evergreens.
We hiked through a forest dominated by ancient Douglas hemlock trees.
