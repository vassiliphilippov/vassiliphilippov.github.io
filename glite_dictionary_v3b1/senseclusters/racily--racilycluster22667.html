racily

adverb
in a lively or slightly risqué manner
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪsɪli / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is lively, entertaining, and slightly risqué or suggestive.

Examples

To speak racily
She spoke racily about her adventures abroad, making everyone laugh.
The comedian told jokes racily without crossing the line into vulgarity.
The novel is written racily, keeping the reader thoroughly entertained.

Related Terms

  • synonym: racy - lively, entertaining, and slightly improper or sexual
