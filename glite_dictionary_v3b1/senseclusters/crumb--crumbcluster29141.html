crumb

verb
remove crumbs from a surface
Pronunciation: / krʌm / (AmE), / krʌm / (BrE)
to remove, break into, or coat with crumbs (especially bread crumbs)

Examples

To crumb the table
After dinner, she crumbed the table with quick, practiced strokes.
He used a small brush to crumb the table between courses.
The waiter politely crumbed the cloth before serving dessert.
She spent the afternoon crumbing cutlets for the family dinner.
The chef carefully crumbed each chicken breast before frying it to a golden brown.
You should crumb the fish fillets before placing them in the oven.
Carefully crumb the cake before adding the frosting.
She crumbed the bread for the casserole topping.
The chef crumbed the biscuits into the bowl.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bread - to cover with bread crumbs or a breading mixture before cooking
  • synonym: clean - to make something free from dirt or mess
  • synonym: crumble - to fall apart or break into small pieces
