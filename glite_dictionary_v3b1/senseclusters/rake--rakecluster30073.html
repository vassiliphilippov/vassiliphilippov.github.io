rake

noun
dissolute, fashionable man
9.5
Pronunciation: / reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
A man who is habituated to immoral conduct, especially one who is fashionable, dissolute, and given to self-indulgence, particularly in matters of sex, drinking, and gambling.

Examples

A wealthy rake
The elegant party attracted every rake and debutante in the city.
Though he tried to hide it, his reputation as a rake preceded him wherever he went.
Throughout the novel, the protagonist is depicted as a notorious rake who constantly courts scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: libertine - a person who behaves without moral principles or a sense of responsibility
  • synonym: profligate - recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources; a licentious person
  • antonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity
