curtate cycloid

noun
cycloid from point inside rolling circle
Pronunciation: / kɚˌteɪt ˈsaɪklɔɪd / (AmE), / kɜːˌteɪt ˈsʌɪklɔɪd / (BrE)
A cycloid curve generated by a point located inside the circumference of a rolling circle.

Examples

A curtate cycloid curve
If the tracing point is moved closer to the center, a curtate cycloid is formed instead of a standard cycloid.
Mechanical engineers may use a curtate cycloid to design gear tooth profiles for reduced wear.
The trajectory described by the center of a wheel is a special case of the curtate cycloid.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cycloid - the curve traced by a point on the rim of a rolling circle
  • antonym: prolate cycloid - a cycloid generated by a point outside the rolling circle
