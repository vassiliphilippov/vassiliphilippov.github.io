Sin

proper noun
Akkadian moon god
Pronunciation: / sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
In Akkadian mythology, the god of the Moon, counterpart of the Sumerian deity Nanna.

Examples

Akkadian Sin god
Archaeologists discovered ancient tablets referencing Sin as the main lunar deity of Akkadian civilization.
Many myths depict Sin traveling across the night sky in a radiant chariot.
The worship of Sin was especially prominent in the city of Ur.
