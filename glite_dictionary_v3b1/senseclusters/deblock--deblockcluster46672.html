deblock

verb
to remove blockage
6.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / diÀêÀàbl…ëk / (AmE), / diÀêÀàbl…ík / (BrE)
to remove a blockage or obstruction from something, such as a pipe, channel, or machine

Examples

To deblock a pipe
The plumber arrived quickly to deblock the kitchen sink after it became clogged.
Engineers worked overnight to deblock the main water channel and restore the flow.
You may need special tools to deblock a heavily obstructed pipe.

Synonyms

  • unblock
    3.1 0.2/m
    unblock is the common verb for removing an obstruction so something can work again, while deblock often names removing blockages in pipes or machines.
  • unclog
    3.2 0.2/m
    unclog often means clearing thick, sticky blockages like hair or grease in pipes, while deblock means removing blockages more generally.
  • unstop
    4.1 0.01/m
    unstop is mainly used for drains or gutters to allow flow again, while deblock can apply to pipes, channels, or machines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: block - to obstruct or stop something from moving
