boggy

adjective
soft and wet like a bog
8.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑː.giː/ /; / /ˈbɔː.giː/ (AmE), /ˈbɒ.giː/ (BrE)
(of ground or soil) soft and wet, like a bog; often difficult to walk on or build upon.

Examples

A boggy field
After three days of rain, the field was so boggy that we couldn't play soccer.
The trail became increasingly boggy as we got closer to the swamp.
We had to cross a boggy patch of land to reach the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marshy - (of land) relating to or resembling a marsh; wet and soft
  • synonym: muddy - (of ground) covered or filled with mud; wet and sticky
  • synonym: swampy - (of land) like a swamp; wet and soft
