Guernsey elm

noun
type of English elm tree
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɜrnzi ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːnzi ɛlm / (BrE)
a variety of English elm with upright branches and broader leaves, cultivated for its distinctive form

Examples

The Guernsey elm tree
Botanists studied the Guernsey elm to understand its resistance to disease.
The Guernsey elm is prized in parks for its attractive, upright shape.
Unlike some other elms, the Guernsey elm has noticeably broader leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: English elm - a species of elm native to Europe, the main species from which the Guernsey elm derives
  • degree higher: elm - a type of deciduous tree characterized by serrated leaves and a strong wood
