compassion

noun
deep sympathy for others' suffering
2.2 2.3
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpæ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpæ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a strong feeling of sympathy for someone who is suffering, together with a desire to help them

Examples

Compassion for others
His compassion led him to volunteer at the animal shelter every weekend.
She showed immense compassion for the earthquake victims by organizing relief efforts.
The nurse treated each patient with patience and compassion during their difficult recovery.
He spoke with great compassion about the victims of the disaster.
Her compassion for the homeless inspired her to volunteer at the shelter every weekend.
Showing compassion can make a huge difference in someone’s life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share the feelings of another
  • antonym: indifference - lack of interest, concern, or sympathy
  • synonym: pity - the feeling of sorrow and compassion caused by the suffering and misfortunes of others
