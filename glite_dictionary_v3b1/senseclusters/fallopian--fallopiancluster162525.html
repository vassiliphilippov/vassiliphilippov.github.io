fallopian

noun
female reproductive tube
5.1
Pronunciation: / fəˈloʊpiən / (AmE), / fəˈləʊpiən / (BrE)
either of the pair of tubes in female mammals that carry eggs from the ovaries to the uterus

Examples

A fallopian tube
A blockage in the fallopian tube can make it difficult for a woman to become pregnant.
Doctors examined her fallopian tubes to check for any signs of infection.
The fertilized egg travels through the fallopian tube before reaching the uterus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oviduct - a tube through which an ovum or egg passes from an ovary
  • antonym: uterus - the organ in female mammals where offspring develop
