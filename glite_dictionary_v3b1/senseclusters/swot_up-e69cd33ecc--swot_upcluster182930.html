swot up

verb
study intensively (esp. for an exam)
8.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / swÉ‘t ÊŒp / (AmE), / swÉ’t ÊŒp / (BrE)
to study something intensively, especially in preparation for an exam

Examples

To swot up for exams
He swotted up on his lines the night before the audition.
If you swot up on the topic, you'll feel more confident in the interview.
She had to swot up on chemistry before the entrance exam.

Synonyms

  • mug up
    8.3 0.01/m
    mug up means studying intensively for an exam in casual talk, while swot up is the same phrasal study term.
  • swot
    9.1 0.01/m
    swot means to study intensively for exams as a base verb, while swot up is the phrasal form often used for the same practice.
  • bone up
    7.6 0.36/m
    bone up is intensive short study done shortly before an exam, while swot up can mean intensive studying without that time limit.
  • cram
    3.9 0.57/m
    cram is rapid, last-minute studying done in a short time before an exam, while swot up does not always imply speed.
  • grind away
    6.3 0.02/m
    grind away is persistent, long effort studying or working over time, while swot up focuses on intense exam preparation.
  • get up
    5.3 1.63/m
    get up is used to mean intensive exam study in some contexts, while swot up clearly means focused exam preparation.
