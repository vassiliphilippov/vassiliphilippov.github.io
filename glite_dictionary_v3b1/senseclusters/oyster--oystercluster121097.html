oyster

noun
small tender dark meat on a fowl's back
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
a small, tender, rounded piece of dark meat located on each side of the backbone of a fowl, considered a delicacy

Examples

Chicken oyster piece
He always saves the oysters for last when carving a turkey.
Many consider the oyster to be the tastiest part of a roasted bird.
The chef carefully removed the oysters from the chicken before serving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wishbone - the forked bone in the chest of a bird
