beam

noun
a column or ray of light
3.4 12/m
Pronunciation: / biÀêm / (AmE), / biÀêm / (BrE)
a line or ray of light, or a stream of electromagnetic radiation such as a laser or X-ray

Examples

A beam of light
A single beam of light shone through the window.
He followed the flashlight's narrow beam through the darkness.
The lighthouse sent a powerful beam across the water.
A laser beam was used to scan the barcodes on the packages.
Doctors use a focused X-ray beam for certain types of medical imaging.
The scientist directed a beam of light onto the sample to observe the reaction.

Synonyms

  • beam of light
    2.5 0.94/m
    a beam of light is a narrow column from a lamp or laser, while beam can also mean any line of light or radiation.
  • ray
    1.0 27/m
    a ray is a very thin straight line of light or energy, while beam is often a wider stream or focused radiation.
  • shaft of light
    3.5 0.47/m
    a shaft of light is a visible column that shines through an opening, while beam is any line or stream of light or radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ray - a narrow beam of light, heat, or other energy
  • degree lower: wave - a disturbance that transfers energy through space or matter
