corrosively

adverb
in a damaging way
7.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊsɪvli / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊsɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that gradually undermines, weakens, or damages something, especially in a sharp or biting way

Examples

Speak corrosively
The manager spoke corrosively about the team's recent failures, lowering everyone's morale.
Rumors spread corrosively through the office, slowly eroding trust among colleagues.
His humor was often corrosively sarcastic, making others uncomfortable during meetings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destructively - in a way that causes damage or harm
  • antonym: gently - in a mild or kind manner
  • synonym: harshly - in a severe or unkind manner
