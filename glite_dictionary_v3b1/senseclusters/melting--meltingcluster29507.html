melting

noun
process of turning solid to liquid by heat
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which heat causes a solid to turn into a liquid

Examples

The melting of snow
High temperatures accelerated the melting of glaciers in the region.
The melting of ice in spring often leads to flooding in some areas.
The scientist carefully observed the melting of the metal in the furnace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freezing - the process of turning from liquid to solid
  • synonym: fusion - the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity; also a term for melting in physics
