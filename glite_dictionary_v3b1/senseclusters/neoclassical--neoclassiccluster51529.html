neoclassical

adjective
revival of classical style
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈklæsɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to a revival or adaptation of classical styles, especially in art, architecture, or literature.

Examples

Neoclassical art style
During the 18th century, neoclassical art became popular throughout Europe.
Her paintings reflect a neoclassical approach, blending traditional techniques with modern subjects.
The architect designed a neoclassical building with columns and pediments reminiscent of ancient Greece.
