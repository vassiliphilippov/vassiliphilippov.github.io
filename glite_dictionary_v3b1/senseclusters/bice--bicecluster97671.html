bice

noun
blue-green pigment
Pronunciation: / baɪs / (AmE), / baɪs / (BrE)
a blue or green pigment made from basic copper carbonate, used in painting and art

Examples

bice pigment
The painter used bice to achieve a rich blue tone in the sky.
Historically, bice was a popular pigment for creating green shades in medieval manuscripts.
She admired the subtle green created by mixing bice with other natural pigments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ultramarine - a deep blue pigment used in art
  • synonym: verdigris - a green pigment made from copper acetate
