Dichondra

noun
genus of creeping perennial herbs
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑːndrə / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒndrə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical and subtropical creeping perennial herbs, some species of which are often used as ground cover in gardens.

Examples

Dichondra ground cover
Dichondra is often used as an attractive ground cover in warm climates.
Gardeners sometimes plant Dichondra instead of grass for a soft, green lawn.
The leaves of Dichondra are kidney-shaped and grow close to the ground.
