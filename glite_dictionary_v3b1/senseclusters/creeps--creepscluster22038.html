creeps

noun
disease of cattle and sheep due to dietary deficiency
Pronunciation: / krips / (AmE), / kriːps / (BrE)
A disease of cattle and sheep caused by a dietary deficiency, marked by anemia, bone softening, and a slow, stiff gait.

Examples

Creeps in cattle
Proper mineral supplements can help prevent creeps in young calves.
The farmer noticed the creeps affecting several sheep after a change in their diet.
Veterinarians diagnosed the herd with creeps based on symptoms of weakness and bone deformity.
