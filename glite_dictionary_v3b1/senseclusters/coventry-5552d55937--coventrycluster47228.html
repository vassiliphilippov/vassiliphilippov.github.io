Coventry

proper noun
city in central England
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvəntri / (AmE), / ˈkʌvəntri / (BrE)
A city in central England known for its historical significance, industrial heritage, and as the home of Lady Godiva; heavily bombed during World War II.

Examples

Coventry city center
During the Second World War, Coventry was heavily damaged by aerial bombardment.
Many automotive companies originated in Coventry during the industrial revolution.
The legend of Lady Godiva is still associated with the city of Coventry.
