decimate

verb
kill one in every ten (historical)
6.4 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈde.sə.meɪt/ /; / /ˈde.sɪ.meɪt/ (AmE), /ˈde.sɪ.meɪt/ /; / /ˈde.sə.meɪt/ (BrE)
(historical) To kill one in every ten (of a group of people), especially as a punishment used in the Roman army.

Examples

Romans decimate soldiers
Historical records confirm that the Romans would decimate their own troops for grave offenses.
In ancient Rome, commanders would sometimes decimate a unit to punish rebellion.
The general ordered his men to decimate the mutinous legion as a warning to others.
