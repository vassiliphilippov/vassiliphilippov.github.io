merse

noun
marshy lowland
Pronunciation: / mɜrs / (AmE), / mɜːs / (BrE)
a stretch of low, marshy land, especially near a river, used in some regional or historical contexts

Examples

The merse land
The cattle grazed peacefully on the lush merse beside the riverbank.
In old maps, the area was marked as a broad merse prone to flooding in spring.
Wildflowers grew in abundance across the damp merse after the heavy rains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with decaying plant matter
  • antonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain
  • synonym: marsh - an area of low, wet land often covered with tall grasses
