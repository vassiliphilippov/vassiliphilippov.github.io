Evangelical United Brethren Church

noun
former Methodist denomination
Pronunciation: / ˌivænˈdʒɛlɪkəl ˈjuːnaɪtɪd brəˈðrɪn tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌiːvanˈdʒelɪkəl ˈjuːnaɪtɪd brəˈðriːn tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a former Protestant denomination in the Methodist tradition, formed in the United States in 1946 and later merged with the Methodist Church in 1968 to form the United Methodist Church.

Members of the Evangelical United Brethren Church played a significant role in American religious history.
