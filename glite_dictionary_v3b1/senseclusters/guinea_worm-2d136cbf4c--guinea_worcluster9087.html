Guinea worm

noun
parasitic roundworm causing dracunculiasis
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪni wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni wɜːm / (BrE)
a parasitic worm (Dracunculus medinensis) that infects humans and causes a painful disease, usually after drinking contaminated water

Examples

A Guinea worm infection
A Guinea worm infection can cause severe pain and discomfort as the worm emerges through the skin.
The eradication campaign against the Guinea worm has greatly reduced the number of cases worldwide.
Travelers to certain regions are warned about the risk of contracting Guinea worm disease from contaminated water.
After drinking untreated water during her travels, she contracted a Guinea worm infection.
The eradication campaign aims to eliminate Guinea worm disease worldwide.
The presence of the Guinea worm in rural communities has been a major public health concern.
