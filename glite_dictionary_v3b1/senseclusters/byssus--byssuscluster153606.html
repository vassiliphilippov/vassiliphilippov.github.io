byssus

noun
mollusk filaments for attachment
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪsəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪsəs / (BrE)
a bundle of strong, silky filaments secreted by certain mollusks, especially mussels, which are used to attach themselves to surfaces.

Examples

Byssus threads
A tangle of byssus threads secured several shells to the pier.
Scientists studied the strength of the byssus to develop new adhesives.
The mussel anchored itself to the rock using its byssus.
