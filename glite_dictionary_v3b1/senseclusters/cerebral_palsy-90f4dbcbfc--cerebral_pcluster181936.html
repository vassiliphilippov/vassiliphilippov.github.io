cerebral palsy

noun
disorder causing impaired motor control
5.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈse.rə.brəl ˈpɔːl.ziː/ /; / /sə.ˈriː.brəl ˈpɔːl.ziː/ (AmE), /ˈse.rə.brəl ˈpɔːl.ziː/ /; / /ˈse.rə.brəl ˈpæl.ziː/ (BrE)
A permanent disorder of movement and posture caused by brain damage occurring before, during, or shortly after birth, often resulting in involuntary muscle spasms and impaired motor function.

Examples

Cerebral palsy diagnosis
Her son was diagnosed with cerebral palsy shortly after birth, but he has made remarkable progress.
Living with cerebral palsy presents daily challenges, but supportive therapy can make a significant difference.
The hospital has a special program to help children with cerebral palsy improve their mobility skills.
