sleight of hand

noun
manual dexterity for tricks or illusions
8.2 6.0
Pronunciation: / slaɪt əv hænd / (AmE), / slaɪt əv hand / (BrE)
skillful and quick use of the hands to perform tricks or deceive others, especially in magic

Examples

A sleight of hand trick
He stole the watch using a remarkable sleight of hand technique.
Mastering sleight of hand requires patience, practice, and a sharp eye for detail.
The magician's sleight of hand left the audience utterly amazed.
Card sharps often rely on sleight of hand to win at poker.
The magician's sleight of hand left the audience baffled and amazed.
With remarkable sleight of hand, she made the coin disappear before our eyes.

  • synonym: trickery - the use of tricks to deceive someone
