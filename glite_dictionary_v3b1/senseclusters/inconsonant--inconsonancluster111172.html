inconsonant

adjective
harsh or clashing sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
producing a harsh or clashing sound; not in musical consonance; dissonant.

Examples

Inconsonant note
The orchestra's performance was marred by an inconsonant note that stood out sharply.
Some modern composers intentionally use inconsonant harmonies to create tension in their music.
The two singers' voices were inconsonant, making the duet sound jarring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consonant - producing a harmonious or agreeable sound
  • synonym: dissonant - producing an unpleasant or harsh sound
