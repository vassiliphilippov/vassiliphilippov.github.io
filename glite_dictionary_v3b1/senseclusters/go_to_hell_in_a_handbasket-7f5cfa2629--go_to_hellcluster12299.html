go to hell in a handbasket

verb
deteriorate rapidly; go badly wrong
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tə hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbæskɪt /; / ɡoʊ tu hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbæskət / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbɑːskɪt /; / ɡəʊ tuː hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbɑːskɪt / (BrE)
to decline or become much worse very quickly, especially in a way that is out of control (informal)

Examples

To go to hell in a handbasket fast
After the new policy was introduced, everything seemed to go to hell in a handbasket.
Their vacation plans went to hell in a handbasket after their flight was cancelled.
When the economy crashed, the business went to hell in a handbasket within months.
After the new management took over, the company started going to hell in a handbasket.
If we don't fix the leaks now, the whole house will be going to hell in a handbasket soon.
Many believe the country's education system is going to hell in a handbasket due to budget cuts.
