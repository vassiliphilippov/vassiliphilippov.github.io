portal hypertension

noun
high blood pressure in the portal vein system
5.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpɔːr.təl haɪ.pər.ˈten.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈpɔː.təl haɪ.pə.ˈten.ʃən/ (BrE)
an increase in blood pressure within the portal vein system, typically caused by obstruction in the liver, often leading to complications such as enlarged spleen and collateral veins

Examples

Doctors monitor portal hypertension to prevent life-threatening complications in people with cirrhosis.
Patients with portal hypertension often present with swollen abdomen and varicose veins.
