short ton

noun
U.S. weight unit, 2,000 pounds
5.4
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt tʌn / (AmE), / ʃɔːt tɒn / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in the United States, equal to 2,000 pounds (approximately 907.18 kilograms).

Examples

A short ton
A short ton is different from a metric ton, so check the conversion before ordering materials.
Steel production is often measured by the short ton in the United States.
The shipment weighed nearly five short tons and required special handling.
