up to one's neck in alligators

adjective
overwhelmed by problems
Pronunciation: / ʌp tə wʌnz nɛk ɪn ˈælɪˌɡeɪtərz / (AmE), / ʌp tuː wʌnz nɛk ɪn ˈælɪɡeɪtəz / (BrE)
to be very busy dealing with a difficult situation or to be overwhelmed by problems

Examples

To be up to one's neck in alligators
After the new client arrived, I was up to my neck in alligators for weeks.
At tax time, accountants are often up to their necks in alligators.
We're up to our necks in alligators trying to finish all these reports before Friday.
