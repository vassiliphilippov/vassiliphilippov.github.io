salt pork

noun
cured fatty pork meat
Pronunciation: / sɔlt pɔrk / (AmE), / sɒlt pɔːk / (BrE)
fatty pork from the back, sides, or belly of a pig, preserved by salting, often used in cooking or as a flavoring.

Examples

A piece of salt pork
During the colonial era, salt pork was a staple food for sailors.
He added chunks of salt pork to the stew for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for browning some salt pork before adding the vegetables.
