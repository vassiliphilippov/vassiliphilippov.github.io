New Style calendar

noun
the Gregorian calendar, post-1582 reform
3.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /nuː staɪl ˈkæl.ən.dər/ /; / /njuː staɪl ˈkæl.ən.dər/ (AmE), /njuː staɪl ˈkæl.ən.də/ /; / /nuː staɪl ˈkæl.ən.də/ (BrE)
the calendar introduced by Pope Gregory XIII in 1582 to reform the Julian calendar, now in general use as the Gregorian calendar

Examples

The New Style calendar
Many historical documents refer to dates before 1752 as Old Style or New Style calendar entries.
Russia did not adopt the New Style calendar until after the Bolshevik Revolution.
The shift to the New Style calendar corrected a significant drift in the timing of the equinox.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gregorian calendar - the calendar currently used internationally, introduced in 1582 to replace the Julian calendar
  • antonym: Julian calendar - the calendar used before the Gregorian calendar, established by Julius Caesar
