coprecipitation

noun
joint precipitation process
9.4
Pronunciation: /koʊ.prɪ.sɪ.pɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kəʊ.prɪ.sɪ.pɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a process in which two or more substances come out of a solution together (precipitate together)

Examples

Coprecipitation of metals
The laboratory experiment demonstrated coprecipitation of several metal ions from the chemical solution.
Scientists often use coprecipitation to isolate impurities during the purification process.
Accurate measurement of trace elements can be achieved through coprecipitation techniques.
The researchers used coprecipitation to confirm that the two proteins interact in the cell.
Successful coprecipitation of the molecules indicated a strong binding between them.
By analyzing the coprecipitation results, the team identified new molecular partners.
In chemical analysis, coprecipitation can be used to isolate trace metals from a solution.
The laboratory technician monitored the reaction to minimize coprecipitation of unwanted ions.
Accidental coprecipitation may lead to contamination of the final product in some experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolation - the act of separating a substance from a mixture
  • synonym: precipitation - the process of a substance being separated from a solution as a solid
