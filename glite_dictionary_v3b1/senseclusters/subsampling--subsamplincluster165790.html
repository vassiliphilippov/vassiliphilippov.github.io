subsampling

noun
statistical resampling method
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.səm.plɪŋ/ /; / /ˈsʌb.sæm.plɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.səm.plɪŋ/ /; / /ˈsʌb.sæm.plɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of selecting a smaller sample from a larger set for analysis, processing, or to reduce data size

Examples

Subsampling data set

Image subsampling is common in digital cameras to save storage space.

  • antonym: oversampling - the process of taking more samples than necessary from a dataset
  • degree higher: sampling - the process of selecting a subset from a population for analysis
  • antonym: upsampling - increasing the sampling rate or resolution of a signal or data
