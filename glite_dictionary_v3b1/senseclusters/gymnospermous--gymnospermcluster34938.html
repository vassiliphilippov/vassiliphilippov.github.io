gymnospermous

adjective
relating to gymnosperm plants
Pronunciation: / dʒɪm.nəˈspɜr.məs / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˈspɜː.məs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of gymnosperms, a group of seed-producing plants whose seeds are not enclosed within an ovary

Examples

Gymnospermous plants
Pine trees are gymnospermous and produce seeds without fruit.
The gymnospermous plants dominated the landscape during the Mesozoic era.
Unlike angiosperms, gymnospermous species do not have flowers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: angiospermous - relating to flowering plants with seeds enclosed within an ovary
