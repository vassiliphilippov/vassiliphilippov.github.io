ship of the line

noun
historic warship used in naval battles
7.9
Pronunciation: / ʃɪp əv ðə laɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪp ɒv ðə laɪn / (BrE)
a large warship used from the 17th to the mid-19th century, designed for battling in the main line of naval conflict

Examples

A ship of the line battle
During the battle, every ship of the line formed a disciplined formation.
Many museums have models of famous ships of the line on display.
The ship of the line played a crucial role in 18th-century naval warfare.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battleship - a large armored warship with heavy guns
