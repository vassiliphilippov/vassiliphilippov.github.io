regenerate

verb
to renew or restore; to undergo regeneration
1.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈdʒe.nə.reɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈdʒe.nə.reɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈdʒe.nə.reɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈdʒe.nə.reɪt/ (BrE)
to grow again or be renewed after being lost or damaged (especially of body parts, tissue, or cells)

Examples

To regenerate tissue
After the injury, liver cells slowly regenerate and restore the organ’s normal function.
Researchers hope to find ways to help damaged nerves regenerate more effectively.
Some species of lizards can regenerate their tails if they are lost.
After the injury, the lizard's tail began to regenerate within a few weeks.
Some plants can regenerate from just a small cutting of stem or root.
When damaged tissues regenerate, the new cells often restore their original function.
After losing its tail, the lizard began to regenerate a new one over several weeks.
Certain animals can regenerate lost limbs after an injury.
Researchers hope to find ways for the human body to regenerate damaged tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: degenerate - to deteriorate or decline; opposite of regenerate
  • synonym: heal - to become healthy again; to return to a normal state after injury
  • synonym: renew - to make new again; restore
  • synonym: repair - to restore something damaged to its original condition
