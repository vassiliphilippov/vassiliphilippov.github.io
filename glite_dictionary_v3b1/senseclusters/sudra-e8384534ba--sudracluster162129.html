Sudra

noun
member of the lowest Hindu caste
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːdrə / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdrə / (BrE)
a member of the lowest of the four main Hindu castes, traditionally assigned to menial labor or service occupations

Examples

A Sudra caste
In traditional Indian society, a Sudra was expected to serve the upper castes.
Many Sudras found economic opportunities in urban areas during the twentieth century.
The social mobility of a Sudra was extremely limited by the rigid caste system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Brahmin - a member of the highest Hindu caste, traditionally priests or scholars
  • antonym: Kshatriya - a member of the second Hindu caste, traditionally warriors or rulers
  • antonym: Vaishya - a member of the third Hindu caste, traditionally merchants or farmers
