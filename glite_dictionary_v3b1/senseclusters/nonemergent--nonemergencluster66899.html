nonemergent

adjective
not urgent
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnɪˈmɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪˈmɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
not requiring immediate attention or action; not urgent or sudden

Examples

A nonemergent condition
The nurse explained that the patient's condition was nonemergent and could be treated the next day.
All nonemergent surgeries were postponed to prioritize critical cases during the crisis.
If your symptoms are nonemergent, please schedule a regular appointment instead of visiting the emergency room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: routine - regular and not requiring special attention
  • antonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention
