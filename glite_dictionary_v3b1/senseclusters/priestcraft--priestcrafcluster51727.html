priestcraft

noun
manipulation of secular affairs by priests (derogatory)
9.9 9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpriːst.kræft/ (AmE), /ˈpriːst.krɑːft/ (BrE)
the use of religious authority or priestly practices to control people or affairs, often for personal or institutional gain (usually with a negative or manipulative meaning)

Examples

Priestcraft control
Debates about priestcraft were common during the Enlightenment.
Some critics accused the church of priestcraft when it began to intervene in political matters.
The novel exposes the dangers of priestcraft and unchecked religious power.
Some writers accused the church leadership of using priestcraft to maintain power over the people.
The reformer denounced priestcraft and called for greater religious transparency.
The villagers distrusted the old superstitions and the priestcraft that kept them alive.
