oxhide

noun
Bronze Age metal ingot
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒksˌhaɪd / (BrE)
a metal ingot, especially from the Late Bronze Age, shaped like an ox's hide with four corner projections

Examples

Ancient oxhide ingot
Archaeologists discovered an ancient oxhide at the bottom of the Mediterranean Sea.
The museum displayed a large oxhide that was once used in Bronze Age trade.
Each oxhide weighed about 30 kilograms and was shaped for easy transport.
