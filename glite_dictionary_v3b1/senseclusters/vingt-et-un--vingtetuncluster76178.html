vingt-et-un

noun
early form of blackjack card game
8.4
Pronunciation: / vɛ̃t‿eɪ ˈʌn / (AmE), / vɛ̃t‿eɪ ˈʌn / (BrE)
An early form of the card game blackjack, in which players try to hold cards totaling a higher count than the banker's, without exceeding 21.

Examples

Playing vingt-et-un cards
In some old novels, characters are often depicted playing vingt-et-un in smoky clubs.
Many 18th-century gamblers played vingt-et-un at private parties in Paris.
The rules of vingt-et-un were the precursor to the modern game of blackjack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blackjack - A card game where players aim to get a hand value of 21 or as close as possible without exceeding it.
