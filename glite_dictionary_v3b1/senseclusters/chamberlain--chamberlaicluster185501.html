chamberlain

noun
municipal or royal treasurer (historical)
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbəlɪn / (BrE)
an officer who manages the financial affairs of a municipal corporation or royal household, especially historically.

Examples

City chamberlain

Historically, the chamberlain was responsible for collecting taxes and maintaining city accounts.
The city appointed a new chamberlain to oversee its budget and spending.
