stack the deck

verb
arrange something unfairly to one's advantage
Pronunciation: / stæk ðə dɛk / (AmE), / stak ðə dɛk / (BrE)
to arrange a situation unfairly so as to achieve a desired outcome or advantage

Examples

To stack the deck unfairly
It's unfair to stack the deck so that nobody else has a chance to win.
She knew someone was trying to stack the deck against her during the job interview.
The company tried to stack the deck by hiring only supporters to serve on the committee.
