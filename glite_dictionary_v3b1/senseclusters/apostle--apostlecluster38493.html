apostle

noun
early Christian teacher or missionary
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑsəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
an important early teacher of Christianity, especially one of the twelve disciples chosen by Jesus, or a Christian missionary to a people

Examples

The twelve apostles
She devoted her life to missionary work and was known as an apostle of compassion.
St. Paul is often called the thirteenth apostle because of his influence on early Christianity.
The twelve apostles spread the teachings of Jesus throughout the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disciple - a follower or student of a teacher, especially Jesus
