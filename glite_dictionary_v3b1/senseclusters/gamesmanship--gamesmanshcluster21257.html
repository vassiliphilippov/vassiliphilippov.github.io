gamesmanship

noun
questionable tactics to win (not illegal)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪmzmənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪmzmənʃɪp / (BrE)
The use of cunning or questionable methods, though not technically against the rules, to gain an advantage and win a game or competition.

Examples

Gamesmanship in sports
Critics argue that excessive gamesmanship can undermine the spirit of fair play.
Her clever use of gamesmanship gave her the upper hand without breaking any rules.
The team was accused of gamesmanship after their players frequently delayed the match.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sportsmanship - fair and generous behavior during sport or competition
