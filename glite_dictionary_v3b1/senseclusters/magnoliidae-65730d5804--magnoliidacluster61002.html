Magnoliidae

noun
subclass of primitive flowering plants
Pronunciation: / mæɡˌnoʊliˈaɪdiː /; / mæɡˌnoʊli.aɪˈdiː / (AmE), / mæɡˌnəʊlɪˈaɪdiː /; / mæɡˌnəʊli.aɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a large subclass of primitive flowering plants, including magnolia and related families, with well-developed flower parts and separate carpels

Examples

The Magnoliidae subclass
Many botanists consider Magnoliidae to represent the earliest branch of flowering plants.
Some classifications treat Magnoliidae as a superorder rather than a subclass.
The family Magnoliaceae is a prominent member of the Magnoliidae subclass.
Many botanists regard Magnoliidae as the most primitive group of living flowering plants.
Research on Magnoliidae has improved our understanding of early angiosperm evolution.
The families within Magnoliidae share several morphological characteristics, such as separate carpels and well-developed perianths.
