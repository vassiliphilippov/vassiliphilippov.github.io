double decomposition

noun
type of chemical reaction with compound exchange
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˌdiˌkɑmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌdiːkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction between two compounds in which parts of each are interchanged to form two new compounds (AB+CD→AD+CB)

Examples

A double decomposition reaction
A double decomposition reaction often results in the formation of a precipitate.
Teachers use simple experiments to demonstrate double decomposition to chemistry students.
When you mix silver nitrate and sodium chloride, a double decomposition takes place and silver chloride is formed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metathesis - chemical reaction in which ions are exchanged between two compounds
