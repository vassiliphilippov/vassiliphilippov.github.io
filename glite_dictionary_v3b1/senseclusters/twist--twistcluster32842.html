twist

noun
dance popular in the 1960s
4.6
Pronunciation: / twɪst / (AmE), / twɪst / (BrE)
a vigorous dance popular in the 1960s, in which dancers twist their hips and arms to the beat of the music, usually performed by couples but not requiring contact

Examples

The twist dance
At the party, everyone joined in to dance the twist when the DJ played an old rock and roll song.
She learned the steps of the twist from her father, who danced it at high school dances.
The resurgence of 1960s fashion brought the twist back onto the dance floor.
