shearer

noun
person who shears sheep
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪrər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪərə / (BrE)
a person or machine that cuts wool from sheep or cuts other materials (like leather, metal, or textiles) using shears

Examples

The shearer cuts wool
After the competition, the shearer was praised for both speed and precision.
Each spring, a skilled shearer is hired to help with the sheep shearing on the farm.
The shearer moved quickly and efficiently through the flock, expertly removing the wool.
Each shearer in the workshop is responsible for a specific type of textile.
Experienced shearers are in high demand during periods of peak production.
The shearer skillfully trimmed the rough edges from the leather hide.
The experienced shearer finished the entire flock in just a few hours.
A modern shearer can process hundreds of sheep in a single day.
The factory uses a mechanical shearer to cut large sheets of metal.
