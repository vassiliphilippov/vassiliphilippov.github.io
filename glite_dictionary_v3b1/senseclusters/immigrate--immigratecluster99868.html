immigrate

verb
come to settle in a new country
Pronunciation: / ˈɪməˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to come to a country to live there permanently

Examples

To immigrate to Canada
After years of waiting, her family finally immigrated to Canada.
Many scientists immigrate to the United States to pursue research opportunities.
She immigrated as a child and quickly adapted to her new environment.
After years of planning, her family finally decided to immigrate to Canada.
Many people immigrate to countries with better job opportunities.
The new policies made it easier for skilled workers to immigrate legally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emigrate - to leave one's country to settle in another
  • synonym: migrate - to move from one region to another, often temporarily or periodically
