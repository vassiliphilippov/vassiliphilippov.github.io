Gnosticism

noun
early Christian religious movement based on esoteric knowledge
9.9 9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈnɑːs.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈnɒs.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A religious movement, especially in the early Christian era, that taught salvation is attained through esoteric knowledge (gnosis) and viewed the material world as created by a lesser divinity.

Examples

Gnosticism religious movement
Many ancient texts associated with Gnosticism were discovered near Nag Hammadi in Egypt.
Some historians argue that elements of Gnosticism influenced later religious philosophies.
The teachings of Gnosticism emphasize personal spiritual knowledge over orthodox religious beliefs.
