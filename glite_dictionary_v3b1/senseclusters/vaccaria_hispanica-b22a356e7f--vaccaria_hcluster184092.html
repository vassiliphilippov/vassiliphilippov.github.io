Vaccaria hispanica

noun
a European flowering plant, also called cowherb
Pronunciation: / vækˈkɛəriə hɪˈspænɪkə / (AmE), / vækˈkeəriə hɪˈspanɪkə / (BrE)
A European annual plant with pale rose-colored flowers, often found in grainfields and sometimes cultivated as an ornamental flower; also known as cowherb or soapwort.

Examples

Vaccaria hispanica plant
Vaccaria hispanica often grows wild in European grainfields and roadsides.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Vaccaria hispanica for its delicate, rose-colored blooms.
You may also find Vaccaria hispanica listed under the common name 'cowherb' in plant guides.
