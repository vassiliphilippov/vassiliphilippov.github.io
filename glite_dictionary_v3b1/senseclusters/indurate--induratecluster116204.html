indurate

verb
make or become hardened or tough
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndjʊˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪndjʊˌreɪt / (BrE)
(formal) to cause someone or something to become hardened or toughened, either physically or emotionally; to habituate to something harsh or unpleasant.

Examples

To indurate emotions
Repeated exposure to violence can indurate a person’s emotions over time.
The constant friction began to indurate the surface of his hands.
Years of hardship would eventually indurate him to life’s challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harden - make or become hard or harder
  • antonym: soften - make or become less hard or severe
