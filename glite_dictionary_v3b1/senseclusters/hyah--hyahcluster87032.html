hyah

interjection
animal-urging shout
9.9 0.80/m
Pronunciation: / hja / (AmE), / hja / (BrE)
a shout or exclamation used to urge someone or an animal to move or to encourage effort

Examples

Shout hyah loudly
The cowboy shouted hyah as he tried to get the stubborn mule to move.
With a loud hyah, she encouraged the oxen to pull the cart up the hill.
He gave a quick hyah and the horse broke into a trot.
With a loud hyah, the team pushed the heavy cart up the hill.
He shouted hyah as he lifted the box onto the shelf.
The workers all yelled hyah together to move the large log.

Synonyms

  • hup
    9.9 0.15/m
    hup is a short command used to move people quickly or keep marching rhythm, while hyah is a shout to urge animals or encourage effort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: come on - an exclamation used to encourage someone to make an effort
  • synonym: giddy-up - a command to a horse to go faster
  • synonym: heave-ho - an exclamation used to coordinate group effort in lifting or pulling
  • antonym: whoa - a command to a horse to stop
