release

noun
the act of discharging something
5.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlis / (AmE), / rɪˈliːs / (BrE)
the act or process of allowing a substance, energy, or other entity to escape or be discharged

Examples

A release of chemicals
An accidental release of hazardous chemicals prompted an evacuation of the area.
During the reaction, there was a sudden release of energy.
The new medication helps control the release of insulin in the body.
A sudden release of water flooded the fields after the dam broke.
The engineer monitored the release of steam from the pressure valve.
Toxic gas release during the experiment was carefully controlled by the scientists.
