unring

verb
to undo effects
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈrɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪŋ / (BrE)
to attempt to reverse or undo the effects of something that has already happened, especially a statement or action.

Examples

To unring the bell
Once the secret was revealed, it was impossible to unring the damage it caused.
He tried to unring his harsh words, but the apology could not erase them.
You cannot unring the bell after making such a public announcement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: repeat - to do or say something again
  • synonym: reverse - to undo or go back to a previous state
  • synonym: undo - to cancel or take back an action
