butyl alcohol

noun
flammable solvent alcohol
Pronunciation: / ˈbjuːtəl ˈæl.kəˌhɑl / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪl ˈæl.kəˌhɒl / (BrE)
A flammable alcohol derived from butanes, commonly used as a solvent in industry and laboratories.

Examples

Butyl alcohol solvent
In the lab, the technician used butyl alcohol as a solvent for the experiment.
Many cleaning products contain butyl alcohol because of its effectiveness in dissolving oils.
Proper storage is essential because butyl alcohol is highly flammable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butanol - another name for butyl alcohol, a four-carbon alcohol
  • degree lower: ethyl alcohol - a two-carbon alcohol, also known as ethanol
  • degree lower: isopropyl alcohol - a three-carbon alcohol, used as a disinfectant
