germ

noun
microorganism that may cause disease
1.0 4.62/m
Pronunciation: / d í…ùm / (AmE), / d í…úÀêm / (BrE)
a very small living thing, especially one that can cause disease

Examples

Dangerous germ
Doctors recommend covering your mouth when you sneeze to prevent the spread of germs.
It's important to wash your hands regularly to get rid of germs.
Many parents worry about their children catching germs at school.

Synonyms

  • microbe
    5.1 6.78/m
    microbe is a tiny organism named in casual and scientific talk, while germ is the common, everyday word for such disease-causing organisms.
  • pathogen
    5.7 5.62/m
    pathogen is a specific organism or agent that causes disease in a host, while germ is a general, everyday name for similar tiny disease-causers.
  • bug
    4.6 3.76/m
    bug is an informal word for a microbe that makes you ill, while germ is a broader everyday term for tiny disease-causing organisms.
  • micro-organism
    3.9 0.01/m
    micro-organism is a formal scientific term for life forms too small to see, while germ is the casual word often linked to disease.
