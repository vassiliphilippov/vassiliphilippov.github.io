Pelagian

adjective
of Pelagius's teachings
Pronunciation: / pəˈleɪdʒiən / (AmE), / pəˈleɪdʒiən / (BrE)
relating to the teachings of Pelagius, especially the belief that original sin does not taint human nature and that humans can choose good without divine aid

Examples

Pelagian doctrine
The council condemned the Pelagian doctrine as heretical in the early fifth century.
His views on free will were considered distinctly Pelagian by his contemporaries.
The debate centered on Pelagian ideas about human nature and moral responsibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Augustinian - relating to the teachings of St. Augustine, especially the belief in original sin and the need for divine grace
