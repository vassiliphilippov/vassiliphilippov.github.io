natural science

noun
a science of the physical world
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃərəl ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl ˈsʌɪəns / (BrE)
a branch of science that deals with the physical world, including subjects such as physics, chemistry, biology, and related fields

Examples

Natural science studies
He specialized in natural science during his university studies.
Many consider physics to be the most fundamental natural science.
The museum offers fascinating exhibits on the history of natural science.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: life science - sciences that deal with living organisms and life processes
  • degree lower: physical science - the sciences dealing with non-living systems, as opposed to biological sciences
  • antonym: social science - the scientific study of human society and social relationships
