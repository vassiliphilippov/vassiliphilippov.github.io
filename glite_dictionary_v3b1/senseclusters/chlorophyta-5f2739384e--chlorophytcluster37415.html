Chlorophyta

noun
a division of green algae
Pronunciation: / kloʊˈrɑːfəˌtə /; / klɔːˈrɑːfɪtə / (AmE), / klɔːˈrɒfɪtə / (BrE)
a large group of mostly freshwater green algae that contain chlorophyll a and b, store food as starch, have cellulose cell walls, and are considered ancestors of land plants

Examples

Chlorophyta green algae
Chlorophyta are known for their distinctive bright green color due to the presence of chlorophyll a and b.
Many freshwater ponds and lakes are home to various species belonging to Chlorophyta.
Scientists believe that land plants evolved from ancient forms of Chlorophyta.
Common forms of Chlorophyta include green algae found in ponds and lakes.
Many species in the Chlorophyta play an important role in freshwater ecosystems.
Scientists believe that land plants evolved from ancestral Chlorophyta.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Rhodophyta - division of red algae, related group of algae distinguished from Chlorophyta
  • synonym: green algae - photosynthetic algae of the division Chlorophyta
