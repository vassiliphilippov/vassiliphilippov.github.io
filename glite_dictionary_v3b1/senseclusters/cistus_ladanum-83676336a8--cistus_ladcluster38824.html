Cistus ladanum

noun
shrub yielding fragrant resin used in perfumes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstəs ləˈdænəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəs ləˈdeɪnəm / (BrE)
A shrub with white flowers and sticky stems and leaves, yielding a fragrant resin used in perfumes, especially as a fixative.

Examples

Cistus ladanum shrub
Botanists studied the ecological role of Cistus ladanum in Mediterranean habitats.
Perfumers often rely on Cistus ladanum as a fixative to make scents last longer.
The sticky leaves of Cistus ladanum are harvested to obtain a valuable resin for perfumes.
