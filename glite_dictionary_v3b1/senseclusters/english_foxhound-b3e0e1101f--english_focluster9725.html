English foxhound

noun
English breed of foxhunting dog
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈfɑksˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈfɒksˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
a breed of dog originally developed in England for hunting foxes in packs, slightly larger than the American foxhound

Examples

The English foxhound breed
A group of English foxhounds led the pack during the traditional hunt.
She adopted an English foxhound from a rescue shelter last year.
The English foxhound is known for its stamina and keen sense of smell when hunting.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: American foxhound - a similar but slightly smaller breed of foxhound developed in America
