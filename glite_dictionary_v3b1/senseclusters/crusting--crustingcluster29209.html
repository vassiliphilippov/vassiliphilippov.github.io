crusting

noun
hard surface layer
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌstɪŋ / (BrE)
a hard or dry layer that forms on the surface of something, often as a result of drying or freezing

Examples

A crusting of snow
A crusting of snow covered the ground after the storm.
The bread developed a thick crusting as it cooled on the counter.
After the wound dried, a crusting formed to protect the skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coating - a thin layer covering a surface
  • synonym: crust - a hard outer layer or covering
  • antonym: softening - the process of becoming less hard or rigid
