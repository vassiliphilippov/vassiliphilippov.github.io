procedural

adjective
relating to court procedures or methods
7.4 4.07/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ôÀàsid í…ôr…ôl / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàsiÀêd í…ôr…ôl / (BrE)
relating to the rules or methods that govern the operation of courts, as distinct from the substantive principles of law.

Examples

Procedural law
A procedural error led to the dismissal of the lawsuit.
The judge explained that the issue was purely procedural and did not affect the outcome of the case.
There are both substantive and procedural aspects to criminal law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: substantive - relating to the actual principles or rights at issue in law, as opposed to procedure
