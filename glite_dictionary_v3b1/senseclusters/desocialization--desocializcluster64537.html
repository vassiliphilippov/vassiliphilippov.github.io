desocialization

noun
loss of social skills
Pronunciation: / diˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person loses or fails to develop the social skills, norms, or behaviors expected by their society

Examples

Child desocialization
Prolonged institutional care can lead to desocialization in children, making it hard for them to interact with others.
The effects of desocialization are often seen in individuals who have experienced extreme neglect or isolation.
Therapists work to reverse desocialization by helping clients relearn basic social skills.
Extended periods in solitary confinement can result in desocialization and difficulty interacting with others.
The sudden move to remote work led to feelings of desocialization among some employees.
After years of living alone in the wilderness, he struggled with desocialization when returning to society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
  • antonym: socialization - the process of learning the norms and behaviors of a society
