irrationalist

adjective
favoring intuition over reason
8.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈræʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
valuing intuition, emotion, or faith over reason or logical thinking

Examples

Irrationalist thinking style
The philosopher was known for his irrationalist views, often prioritizing feelings over logic.
Some art movements are considered irrationalist because they reject traditional rules and embrace emotion.
Her irrationalist approach to decision-making sometimes led to unpredictable results.
