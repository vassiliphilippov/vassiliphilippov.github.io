shepherd

verb
guide or watch over carefully
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpəd / (BrE)
to guide or look after people or a group in a protective or mentoring way

Examples

To shepherd a group
He was chosen to shepherd the new recruits during their training period.
The teacher shepherded her students through the complex project with patience and care.
Throughout the trip, the guide shepherded the visitors safely through the crowded city.
As mentor, she was chosen to shepherd the interns through their first year.
The board relied on him to shepherd the organization during the merger.
With patience, he continues to shepherd the difficult reforms to completion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guide - to show or lead the way for someone
  • synonym: lead - to guide or direct a group or process
  • synonym: oversee - to supervise or watch over people or work
