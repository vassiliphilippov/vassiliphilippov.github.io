cardioversion

noun
restoring heart rhythm
5.1
Pronunciation: /kɑːr.di.oʊ.ˈvɜːr.ʒən/ /; / /kɑːr.dɪ.oʊ.ˈvɜːr.ʒən/ (AmE), /kɑː.di.əʊ.ˈvɜː.ʒən/ /; / /kɑː.dɪ.əʊ.ˈvɜː.ʒən/ (BrE)
a medical procedure that restores a normal heart rhythm by using electricity or medication

Examples

Cardioversion procedure
The patient underwent cardioversion to correct his abnormal heart rhythm.
Doctors may recommend cardioversion if medications do not restore a normal heartbeat.
After the successful cardioversion, her heart returned to a regular rhythm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrhythmia - an irregular heartbeat
  • synonym: defibrillation - a procedure using electric shock to restore heart rhythm
