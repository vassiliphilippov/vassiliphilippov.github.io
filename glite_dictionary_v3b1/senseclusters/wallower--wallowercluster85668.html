wallower

noun
one who wallows
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːloʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɒləʊə / (BrE)
a person or animal that wallows, especially in mud or in self-pity or negative emotions

Examples

A wallower in mud
The pig was a true wallower in the muddy pen.
He became a wallower in his own sadness after the breakup.
Among the herd, one buffalo stood out as a constant wallower in the riverbank mud.

Related Terms

  • antonym: avoider - someone who keeps away from something
  • synonym: indulger - someone who allows themselves to enjoy something excessively
