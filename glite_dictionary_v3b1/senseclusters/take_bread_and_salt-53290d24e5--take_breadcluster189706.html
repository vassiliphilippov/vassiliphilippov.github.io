take bread and salt

verb
to establish a strong friendship or bond
9.9
Pronunciation: / teɪk brɛd ənd sɑlt / (AmE), / teɪk brɛd ənd sɒlt / (BrE)
to establish a strong friendship or bond, often through the act of sharing food, especially bread and salt, as a gesture of trust or hospitality

Examples

To take bread and salt
During the ceremony, the two leaders took bread and salt to signify their new partnership.
In many cultures, to take bread and salt with someone means to welcome them as a trusted friend.
It is customary in some regions for guests to take bread and salt with their hosts upon arrival.
