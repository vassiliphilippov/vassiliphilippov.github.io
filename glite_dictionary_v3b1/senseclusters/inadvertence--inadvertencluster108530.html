inadvertence

noun
inattentiveness to duties or responsibilities
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.ədˈvɝː.təns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˈvɜː.təns / (BrE)
lack of attention or carelessness that leads to mistakes or unintentional errors

Examples

Sheer inadvertence
His financial mistake was caused by sheer inadvertence rather than intentional wrongdoing.
She apologized for her inadvertence in forgetting the meeting.
The loss of important files was due to inadvertence, not malice.
Because of her inadvertence, an important deadline was missed.
The contract was voided on the grounds of inadvertence by one of the parties.
The error in the report was due to inadvertence rather than intentional misconduct.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attentiveness - paying close attention to something
  • synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
