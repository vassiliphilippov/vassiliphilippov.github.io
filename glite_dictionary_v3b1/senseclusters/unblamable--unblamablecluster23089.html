unblamable

adjective
free from blame
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbleɪməbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈbleɪməbəl / (BrE)
not deserving blame or free from fault or criticism

Examples

Completely unblamable behavior
The judge found his behavior to be completely unblamable in the situation.
She acted in an unblamable manner throughout the entire process.
His record at work was unblamable, with no complaints or issues reported.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blameless - not responsible for any wrongdoing or fault
  • antonym: culpable - deserving blame or responsibility for a wrongdoing
