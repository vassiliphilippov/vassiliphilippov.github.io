tenpenny

adjective
nail size (three-inch)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈtɛnˌpɛni / (BrE)
describing a nail that is about three inches long, commonly used in carpentry

Examples

Tenpenny nail size
The carpenter asked for a box of tenpenny nails to finish the frame.
You should use tenpenny nails when joining thick wooden boards together.
He bought several sizes, but the tenpenny nails were perfect for the job.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: eightpenny - describing a nail that is about two and a half inches long
  • degree higher: sixteenpenny - describing a nail that is about three and a half inches long
