foster-child

noun
a child raised by foster parents
3.1 2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːstər tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a child who is raised by people who are not their biological parents, usually through an official fostering arrangement

Examples

A foster-child family
After being moved to several homes, the foster-child finally found a family where she felt truly welcome.
As a foster-child, he faced many challenges but grew into a resilient adult.
The couple decided to adopt their foster-child after caring for him for two years.
