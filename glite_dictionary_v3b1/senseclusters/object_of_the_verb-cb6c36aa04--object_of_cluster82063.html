object of the verb

noun
recipient of the verb's action
Pronunciation: / ˈɑbdʒɛkt əv ðə vɝb / (AmE), / ˈɒbdʒɛkt əv ðə vɜːb / (BrE)
the noun, noun phrase, or pronoun that receives the direct action of a verb in a sentence

Examples

The object of the verb
In the sentence 'She reads books,' 'books' is the object of the verb.
The teacher asked students to identify the object of the verb in each sentence.
Understanding the object of the verb can help you analyze sentence structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subject - the part of a sentence that performs the action of the verb
