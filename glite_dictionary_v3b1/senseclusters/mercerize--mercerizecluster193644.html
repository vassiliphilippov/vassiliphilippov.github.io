mercerize

verb
treat fabric for strength and shine
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜːrsəˌraɪz /; / ˈmɜrsəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsəraɪz / (BrE)
to treat cotton or other fabric with a chemical process to make it stronger, shinier, and more able to absorb dye

Examples

To mercerize cotton fabric
After you mercerize the fabric, it becomes both stronger and more receptive to vibrant colors.
The company decided to mercerize the thread to achieve a silk-like sheen for its premium line.
To improve its durability and appearance, manufacturers often mercerize cotton fabrics before dyeing them.
Fabrics that have been mercerised often feel smoother and look more luxurious.
The factory will mercerise the cotton to give it a brighter finish.
They specialize in mercerising textiles for high-end fashion brands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bleach - to remove color from fabric or material using chemicals
  • synonym: mercerize - to treat cotton or fabric to increase luster and strength (American spelling)
