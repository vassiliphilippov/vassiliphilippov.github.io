in effect

adverb
in actuality or reality; practically speaking
4.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn ɪ.ˈfekt/ /; / /ɪn ə.ˈfekt/ (AmE), /ɪn ɪ.ˈfekt/ /; / /ɪn ə.ˈfekt/ (BrE)
used to indicate that something is the case in practice or reality, even if not formally acknowledged

Examples

In effect the leader
In effect, she took control of the project, even though she wasn't officially in charge.
In effect, the agreement means we will be working together for another year.
His resignation was, in effect, forced by the board of directors.
