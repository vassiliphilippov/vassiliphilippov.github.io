family Toxotidae

noun
the family of archerfishes
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli tɑkˈsɑtəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli tɒkˈsɒtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical fishes known as archerfishes, recognized for their ability to shoot jets of water to knock insects into the water.

Examples

Family Toxotidae fishes
All members of the family Toxotidae share the remarkable trait of shooting jets of water at prey.
Research on the family Toxotidae has revealed unique adaptations for hunting insects above water.
The family Toxotidae includes several species of archerfishes native to Southeast Asia and Australia.
