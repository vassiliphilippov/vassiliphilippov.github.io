Curie temperature

noun
temperature where ferromagnetism is lost
4.1 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkjʊri ˌtɛmpɚˌtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəri ˌtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
the temperature above which a ferromagnetic material loses its ferromagnetic properties and becomes paramagnetic

Examples

The Curie temperature
Iron loses its ferromagnetic properties above its Curie temperature.
Researchers measured the Curie temperature of the new alloy to determine its practical applications.
When heated beyond the Curie temperature, the material became paramagnetic and remained so as it cooled.
