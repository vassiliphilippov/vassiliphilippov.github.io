ad prosequendum

noun
court-appointed prosecutor
9.9
Pronunciation: /æd prəˈsek.wən.dəm/ /; / /æd proʊ.seˈkwen.dəm/ (AmE), /æd prəˈsek.wən.dəm/ /; / /æd prəʊ.seˈkwen.dəm/ (BrE)
a person appointed by a court to prosecute a legal action on behalf of another, especially a minor or incapacitated person

Examples

Ad prosequendum guardian
The judge named an ad prosequendum to act for the child in the lawsuit.
Without an ad prosequendum, the incapacitated adult could not pursue the legal claim.
The court's appointment of an ad prosequendum ensured the minor's interests were represented.

Related Terms
