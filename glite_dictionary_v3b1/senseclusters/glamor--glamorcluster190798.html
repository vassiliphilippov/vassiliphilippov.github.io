glamor

noun
alluring beauty or charm
4.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈglæm.ər/ (AmE), /ˈglæ.mə/ (BrE)
An attractive or exciting quality that makes someone or something seem appealing, often in a romantic, showy, or sophisticated way; alluring beauty or charm, often with an element of sex appeal.

Examples

Hollywood glamor
Despite the apparent glamor of his job, it involved long hours and little sleep.
She was drawn to the glamor of Hollywood and dreamt of becoming a movie star.
The party was filled with glamor and excitement, dazzling all who attended.
Despite her job's apparent glamour, the work was actually exhausting and routine.
Many people are drawn to the glamour of city life, dreaming of fame and excitement.
The actress's glamour captivated audiences everywhere she went.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully attractive or charming
  • synonym: glamour - variant British spelling with the same meaning
  • antonym: plainness - lack of embellishment or attraction
