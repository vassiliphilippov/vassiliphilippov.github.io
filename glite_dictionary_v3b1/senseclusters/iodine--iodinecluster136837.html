iodine

noun
a nonmetallic chemical element used in medicine and photography
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪəˌdin /; / ˈaɪədiːn / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.diːn /; / ˈaɪədiːn / (BrE)
a nonmetallic chemical element (symbol I), a dark purple-black substance used especially in medicine, photography, and dyes

Examples

Pure iodine element
Doctors often use iodine to disinfect wounds before surgery.
Seaweed is a natural source of iodine, important for thyroid health.
The dark purple vapor of heated iodine is distinctive and easily recognizable.
Doctors sometimes apply iodine to cuts to prevent infection.
Seaweed is a natural source of iodine in the diet.
The dark stains on the cloth were from a drop of iodine.
