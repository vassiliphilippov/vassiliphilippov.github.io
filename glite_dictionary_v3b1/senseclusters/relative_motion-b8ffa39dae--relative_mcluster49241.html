relative motion

noun
movement measured from a specific viewpoint
1.1 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
movement of an object or system as measured or observed from a specified frame of reference, rather than in absolute terms

Examples

Relative motion of objects
Engineers account for relative motion when designing parts that slide or rotate against each other.
The laws of physics remain the same for all observers in relative motion.
The perception of speed depends on relative motion between the observer and the object.
