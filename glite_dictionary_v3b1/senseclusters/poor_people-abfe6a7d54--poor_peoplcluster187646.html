poor people

noun
people lacking wealth or possessions
1.0 6.88/m
Pronunciation: / p är Ààpip…ôl / (AmE), / p…îÀê ÀàpiÀêpl / (BrE)
people who have little money or few possessions; those who lack wealth or material resources.

Examples

Help poor people
Government programs aim to help poor people access healthcare and education.
Many charities provide food and shelter to poor people in the city.
The gap between the wealthy and poor people continues to widen in many countries.

Synonyms

  • poor
    1.3 17/m
    poor refers to people seen as a single social group, whereas poor people refers to individuals who lack money or possessions.
  • needy
    1.0 1.37/m
    needy describes people who lack basic necessities and need help, while poor people simply lack money or possessions.
  • less fortunate
    3.3 1.94/m
    less fortunate describes people who have fewer advantages or chances in life, while poor people lack money or possessions.
