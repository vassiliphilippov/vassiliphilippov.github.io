audacity

noun
fearless daring; boldness
Pronunciation: / ɔˈdæsəti / (AmE), / ɔːˈdæsəti /; / ɔˈdæsəti / (BrE)
the willingness to take bold risks, sometimes in a way that shocks or disregards rules

Examples

The audacity to act
His audacity impressed the recruiters during the high-risk operation.
It took a lot of audacity to start a business in such uncertain times.
She had the audacity to speak out against the board's decision.
His audacity shocked everyone at the meeting when he spoke out against the director.
I admired her audacity in challenging the established rules so openly.
She had the audacity to ask for a raise after only one week on the job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks; courage
  • antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness
