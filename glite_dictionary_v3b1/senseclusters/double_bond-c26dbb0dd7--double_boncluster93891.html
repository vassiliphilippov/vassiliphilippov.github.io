double bond

noun
covalent bond sharing two electron pairs
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl bɒnd / (BrE)
a covalent bond in which two pairs of electrons are shared between two atoms

Examples

A double bond in chemistry
A carbon atom can form a double bond with another carbon atom in molecules like ethylene.
Molecules with a double bond often have different shapes compared to those with single bonds.
The presence of a double bond affects the chemical reactivity of the compound.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: single bond - a covalent bond in which one pair of electrons is shared between two atoms
  • degree higher: triple bond - a covalent bond in which three pairs of electrons are shared between two atoms
