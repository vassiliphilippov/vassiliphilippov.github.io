bulimia

noun
pathological excessive hunger
6.5
Pronunciation: / bəˈlɪmiə / (AmE), / bjuˈlɪmɪə / (BrE)
An abnormal and excessive hunger or appetite, especially when medically caused, such as by brain disorders.

Examples

Medical bulimia
After the accident, she developed bulimia due to damage to the hunger-regulating centers in her brain.
Doctors warned that brain lesions in the patient were responsible for his severe bulimia.
Persistent, uncontrollable eating episodes may indicate an underlying medical issue such as bulimia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anorexia - lack or loss of appetite for food
