disputation

noun
formal debate or argument
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃən /; / ˌdɪspjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal debate or argument, especially one involving strong disagreement or a vote

Examples

A disputation debate
After a lengthy disputation, the council took a vote to settle the issue.
The medieval university often required students to engage in a disputation to demonstrate their mastery of a subject.
The philosophy seminar concluded with a spirited disputation on ethics and morality.
During the medieval period, public disputation was a key part of academic life.
His loud disputation with the committee drew attention from the entire office.
The university hosted a disputation between philosophers on the nature of reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - a situation where all parties share the same opinion
  • synonym: argument - a discussion where people disagree, often strongly
  • synonym: debate - a formal discussion on a particular topic in a public meeting or legislative assembly
  • synonym: discussion - an act or process of talking about something formally or informally
