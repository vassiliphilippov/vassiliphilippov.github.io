readopt

verb
to adopt again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈdɑːpt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈdɒpt / (BrE)
to adopt something again, especially after it was previously abandoned or discontinued

Examples

To readopt a plan
After several years, the city council decided to readopt the recycling program.
The school board voted to readopt the previous dress code after complaints from parents.
Many organizations readopt old policies when new approaches do not work as expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to give up or stop using something
  • synonym: reinstate - to restore something to its former position or condition
