ghastly

adjective
horrifying; shockingly repellent
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæs.tli /; / ˈɡæsli / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːst.li /; / ˈɡɑːstli / (BrE)
causing horror, fear, or shock; extremely unpleasant, often suggesting death or something supernatural

Examples

A ghastly scream
She let out a ghastly scream when she saw the shadowy figure.
The crime scene was a ghastly sight that left the detectives shaken.
The hospital treated many victims with ghastly injuries after the accident.
She awoke from a ghastly nightmare, drenched in sweat and shaking.
The abandoned house had a ghastly silence that made everyone uneasy.
The scene of the accident was so ghastly that witnesses turned away in horror.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gruesome - causing repulsion or horror; grisly
  • synonym: horrific - extremely disturbing or terrifying
  • synonym: macabre - disturbing and horrifying because of involvement with or depiction of death and injury
  • antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment
