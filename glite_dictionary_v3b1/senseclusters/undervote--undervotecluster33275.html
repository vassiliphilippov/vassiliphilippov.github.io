undervote

verb
to cast fewer votes
8.0
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈvoʊt/ /; / /ˈʌn.dər.voʊt/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈvəʊt/ /; / /ˈʌn.də.vəʊt/ (BrE)
to cast fewer votes than allowed in an election contest, or to leave a contest unmarked on a ballot.

Examples

To undervote in elections
If you undervote in a local election, your ballot may still be counted for other races.
Some people intentionally undervote when they do not support any candidate in a contest.
Election officials noticed that many voters undervoted the city council section of the ballot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstain - to choose not to vote in an election or on a particular issue
  • antonym: overvote - to cast more votes than permitted in an election contest
