crooked

adjective
bent; dishonest
2.6 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʊkɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪd / (BrE)
bent or twisted out of shape; not straight; also, behaving dishonestly or fraudulently.

Examples

Crooked businessman
A crooked smile appeared on her face as she planned her next move.
The old man walked with a crooked cane that barely supported him.
The politician was accused of making crooked deals for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bent - shaped or forced into a curve or angle, especially not straight
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere, opposite of 'crooked' (dishonest sense)
  • antonym: straight - not bent or curved; honest; opposite of 'crooked' in physical or moral sense
