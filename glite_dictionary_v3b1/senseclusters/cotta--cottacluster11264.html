cotta

noun
clerical overgarment
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtə / (AmE), / ˈkɒtə / (BrE)
a short, white, sleeveless garment worn by clergy or choir members over a cassock during church services

Examples

A cotta garment
The priest put on a cotta before leading the Sunday service.
Each choir member wore a clean white cotta over their cassock.
The old photograph showed altar boys dressed in black cassocks and white cottas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surplice - a loose white linen vestment worn by clergy and choir members
