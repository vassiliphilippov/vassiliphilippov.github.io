battle of Pharsalus

noun
decisive Roman battle, Caesar vs. Pompey, 48 BC
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬.əl əv fɑrˈseɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv fɑːˈsɑː.ləs / (BrE)
a decisive battle in 48 BC during the Roman Civil War in which Julius Caesar defeated Pompey the Great.

Examples

The battle of Pharsalus
During the battle of Pharsalus, Caesar's tactical genius secured his victory over Pompey.
Many historians study the consequences of the battle of Pharsalus for the Roman Republic.
The battle of Pharsalus marked a turning point in Roman history.
