restore

verb
bring back to original state or position
3.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstɔr / (AmE), / rɪˈstɔː / (BrE)
to bring back a former situation, function, or position, especially one that existed previously or was lost

Examples

To restore someone to power
After a long absence, he was finally restored to his former position.
The government promised to restore law and order after the unrest.
The museum plans to restore the ancient statue to its original state.

Related Terms

  • antonym: remove - take away something from its position
