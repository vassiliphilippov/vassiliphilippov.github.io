reimpose

verb
to put in place again
6.5 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê.…™mÀàpo äz / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê.…™mÀàp…ô äz / (BrE)
to impose something again, especially after it has previously been lifted or ended

Examples

To reimpose restrictions
After lifting the restrictions briefly, the government decided to reimpose them due to rising cases.
If the rules are broken again, authorities will likely reimpose the ban.
The company may reimpose the dress code policy after recent complaints.

Synonyms

    6.1 0.44/m
    reinstitute brings back a rule, system, or practice formally, while reimpose means to impose that rule again after it was lifted.
  • reinstate
    5.8 4.62/m
    reinstate returns someone or a rule to their former state or position, while reimpose focuses on forcing a rule back after it was lifted.
  • reenact
    6.6 0.12/m
    reenact makes a law or rule valid again by formal approval, while reimpose means to put a previously lifted restriction back into force.
  • reinvoke
    8.1 0.02/m
    reinvoke calls on a rule, right, or memory for use again, while reimpose means to force a rule back on people after it ended.
