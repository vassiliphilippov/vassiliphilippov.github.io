wood coal

noun
charcoal made from wood
5.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /wʊd koʊl/ (AmE), /wʊd kəʊl/ /; / /wʊd kɒl/ (BrE)
a carbon-rich material obtained by heating wood or other organic matter in the absence of air; charcoal

Examples

Wood coal drawing stick
Early artists sometimes drew with sticks of wood coal for bold, dark lines.
The blacksmith used wood coal to heat the metal in the forge.
Traditional water filters often contain wood coal to trap impurities.

Related Terms
