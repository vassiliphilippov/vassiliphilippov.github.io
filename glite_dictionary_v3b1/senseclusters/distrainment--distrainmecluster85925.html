distrainment

noun
seizure for debt
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstreɪnmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈstreɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act or legal process of seizing someone's property to enforce payment of a debt

Examples

Distrainment for unpaid rent
The landlord obtained a distrainment to recover unpaid rent from the tenant.
After repeated warnings, the court authorized distrainment of the debtor's assets.
The threat of distrainment encouraged the business owner to settle the outstanding debt.
The landlord initiated distrainment when the tenant failed to pay rent for several months.
Historically, distrainment was used to recover debts by seizing livestock or goods.
Modern laws have replaced distrainment with other forms of debt collection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distraint - the act of seizing someone's property to obtain payment of a debt
  • synonym: foreclosure - the legal process of taking possession of a mortgaged property when payments are not made
  • synonym: repossession - the act of taking back property after a buyer defaults on payments
