lysogenic

adjective
capable of producing or undergoing lysis
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪsəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌlaɪsəˈdʒɛnɪk /; / ˌlɪsəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or capable of undergoing lysogeny, where a virus integrates its genetic material into a host cell and may later cause cell destruction

Examples

Lysogenic virus infection
Certain bacteria can become lysogenic after infection by specialized bacteriophages.
The lysogenic cycle allows a virus to integrate its genome into the DNA of its host cell.
The switch between the lytic and lysogenic phases depends on environmental signals.
Certain bacteriophages follow a lysogenic cycle, integrating their DNA into the host cell.
Research on lysogenic viruses has advanced our understanding of gene regulation.
The lysogenic pathway allows the virus to remain dormant for extended periods.
