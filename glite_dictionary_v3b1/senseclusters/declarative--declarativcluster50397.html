declarative

adjective
expressing a statement (grammar)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈklær.ə.tɪv /; / dɪˈklɛrəˌtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈklær.ət.ɪv /; / dɪˈklærətɪv / (BrE)
expressing or relating to statements or declarations, especially in grammar or computing

Examples

Declarative sentence
'He went to the store' is an example of a declarative sentence.
In English, most sentences are in the declarative mood, simply stating facts or opinions.
The teacher explained how to change a declarative sentence into a question.
Her answer was direct and declarative, leaving no room for ambiguity.
In computer science, a declarative language tells the computer what to do, not how to do it.
The teacher asked the students to write a declarative sentence about their weekend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: imperative - giving an order or command, especially in grammar
  • synonym: indicative - the verb mood used for making factual statements
  • antonym: interrogative - having the form or force of a question, especially in grammar
