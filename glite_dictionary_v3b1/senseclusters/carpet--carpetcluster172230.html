carpet

verb
cover thickly like a carpet
4.7 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêr.p…ôt / (AmE), / Ààk…ëÀê.p…™t / (BrE)
to cover a surface with or as if with a thick layer, like a carpet

Examples

Snow carpeted the ground
A thick layer of snow carpeted the streets by morning.
In spring, wildflowers carpet the hillside in vibrant colors.
Leaves carpeted the garden after last night's storm.
A layer of snow carpeted the ground overnight, hiding every path and stone.
Bright leaves carpeted the park after the autumn wind passed through.
In spring, wildflowers carpet the hills with vibrant colors.

Synonyms

  • plaster
    3.8 2.25/m
    plaster covers a surface thickly with wet or sticky material, while carpet means to cover a surface as if with a thick, rug-like layer.
  • coat
    2.8 13/m
    coat covers something with a layer of a particular substance like paint or dust, while carpet suggests a thick, rug-like covering.
  • overlay
    4.2 0.95/m
    overlay puts a layer of another material on top of a surface for protection or finish, while carpet implies a thick, rug-like covering.
