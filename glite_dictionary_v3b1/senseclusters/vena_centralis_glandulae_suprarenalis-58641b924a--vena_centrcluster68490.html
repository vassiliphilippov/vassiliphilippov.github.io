vena centralis glandulae suprarenalis

noun
central vein of the adrenal gland
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪnə sɛnˈtrælɪs ˈɡlændjəˌli suːprəˈreɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈviːnə sɛnˈtrɑːlɪs ˈɡlændjʊli ˌsuːprəˈreɪnælɪs / (BrE)
The central vein of the adrenal gland, which drains blood from the gland; on the right side it empties into the inferior vena cava, and on the left into the left renal vein.

Examples

The vena centralis glandulae suprarenalis vein
During adrenal surgery, care must be taken when ligating the vena centralis glandulae suprarenalis to prevent hemorrhage.
Histological examination revealed obstruction in the vena centralis glandulae suprarenalis following trauma.
The vena centralis glandulae suprarenalis differs in its drainage pattern on the right and left sides of the body.
