cohead

noun
joint leader
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌhɛd / (BrE)
a person who shares the position of leader or head with one or more others

Examples

A cohead of department
After the merger, both directors became coheads of the new department.
The school appointed two coheads to manage the academic program together.
As cohead of the committee, he shared responsibilities equally with his colleague.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cochair - a person who shares the position of chairperson with another
  • synonym: leader - a person who leads or is in charge
  • antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority of another
