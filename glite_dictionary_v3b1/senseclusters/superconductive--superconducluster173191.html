superconductive

adjective
no electrical resistance
8.0
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.kən.ˈdʌk.tɪv/ (AmE), /suː.pə.kən.ˈdʌk.tɪv/ (BrE)
having the property of allowing electricity to flow without resistance (superconductivity), usually at very low temperatures

Examples

Superconductive materials
Scientists are searching for superconductive materials that work at higher temperatures.
The cable's superconductive properties make it ideal for advanced power grids.
A superconductive magnet is used in MRI machines for medical imaging.

At extremely low temperatures, certain materials become superconductive and can carry electricity without loss.
