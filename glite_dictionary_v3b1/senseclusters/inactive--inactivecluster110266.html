inactive

adjective
not moving; motionless
6.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈæktɪv / (BrE)
not moving, not physically or mentally active, or lacking energy

Examples

An inactive person
After a long period of exercise, she sat inactive on the bench.
The animal remained inactive for several hours, conserving its energy.
The volcano has been inactive for decades, showing no signs of movement.
After his surgery, Mark led an inactive lifestyle for several months.
Prolonged periods of being inactive can negatively affect both health and mood.
The club became inactive due to a lack of members and enthusiasm.
After his surgery, Tom felt too inactive to join his friends for a walk.
Her inactive attitude made it difficult for the team to complete the project on time.
Many people become inactive during the winter months due to shorter days and colder weather.
