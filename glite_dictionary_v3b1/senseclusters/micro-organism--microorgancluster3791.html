micro-organism

noun
organism too small to be seen unaided
3.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåma…™.kro äÀà…îr.…°…ôÀån…™z.…ôm / (AmE), / Àåma…™.kr…ô äÀà…îÀê.…°…ôn.…™.z…ôm / (BrE)
An organism, such as a bacterium, virus, or fungus, that is too small to be seen without a microscope.

Examples

A micro-organism under microscope
Researchers used a microscope to observe the micro-organism swimming in the water sample.
Some micro-organisms are beneficial, while others can cause disease.
The fermentation process relies on micro-organisms to convert sugar into alcohol.

Synonyms

  • microorganism
    5.1 3.33/m
    microorganism is the unhyphenated term used in scientific and general writing, while micro-organism appears with a hyphen.
  • microbe
    5.1 6.78/m
    microbe often means a tiny germ that can cause disease and sounds informal, while micro-organism is a neutral scientific term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: germ - commonly used term for a disease-causing micro-organism
  • synonym: microbe - another term for a microorganism
