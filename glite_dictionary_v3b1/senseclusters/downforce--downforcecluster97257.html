downforce

noun
downward applied force
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.fɔːrs/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.fɔːs/ (BrE)
a downward force applied to an object by a person, machine, or weight, usually to hold it in place or increase pressure

Examples

Apply downforce with clamps
The clamps provided enough downforce to keep the metal sheet steady during the process.
Applying extra downforce helps prevent the material from slipping while being cut.
The technician adjusted the machine to increase the downforce on the workpiece.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lift - an upward force that opposes the weight of an object
  • synonym: pressure - the continuous physical force exerted on or against an object
