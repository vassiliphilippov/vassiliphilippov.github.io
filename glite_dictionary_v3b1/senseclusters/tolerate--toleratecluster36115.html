tolerate

verb
endure a substance or condition without harm
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːləˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɒləˌreɪt / (BrE)
to be able to endure or withstand a drug, poison, pathogen, or environmental condition without adverse effects

Examples

To tolerate a drug
After months of treatment, she finally began to tolerate the medication without feeling ill.
Desert plants tolerate intense heat and prolonged drought better than most other species.
Some people cannot tolerate high doses of antibiotics without experiencing side effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of something
  • synonym: withstand - to resist successfully; remain undamaged or unaffected
