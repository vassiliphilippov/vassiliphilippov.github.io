relative density

noun
ratio of a substance's density to a standard
2.7 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈdɛnsəti / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈdɛnsəti / (BrE)
The ratio of the density of a substance to the density of a standard, typically water for liquids or air for gases.

Examples

Relative density of water
If an object's relative density is less than one, it will float in water.
Scientists use relative density to identify unknown liquids in the laboratory.
The relative density of mercury is much higher than that of water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: specific gravity - the ratio of the density of a substance to that of a reference substance
