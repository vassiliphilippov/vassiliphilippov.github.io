external carotid artery

noun
artery supplying blood to face and head
4.6
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɝnəl kəˈroʊt̬ɪd ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜː.nəl kəˈrɒ.tɪd ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
the artery that branches from the common carotid artery and supplies blood to the face, tongue, and external parts of the head.

Examples

The external carotid artery supplies face
A blockage in the external carotid artery can affect blood flow to the face and scalp.
During the procedure, the doctor carefully avoided damage to the external carotid artery.
The surgeon located the external carotid artery before proceeding with the operation.
