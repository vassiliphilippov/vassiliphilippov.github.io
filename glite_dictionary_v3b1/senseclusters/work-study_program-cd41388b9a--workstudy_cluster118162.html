work-study program

noun
educational plan combining work and study
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːk ˌstʌdi ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk ˌstʌdi ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
an educational system in which students alternate periods of paid employment with periods of formal study, gaining practical experience alongside academic learning

Examples

A work-study program
Many students find that a work-study program balances academic requirements with real-world skills development.
She participated in a work-study program that allowed her to gain valuable job experience while finishing her degree.
The university offers a work-study program to help students offset tuition costs with part-time employment.
