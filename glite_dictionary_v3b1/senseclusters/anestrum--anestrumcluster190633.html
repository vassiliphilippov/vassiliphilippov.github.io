anestrum

noun
period of sexual inactivity in female mammals
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈnɛstrəm /; / ˌænoʊˈstrʌm / (AmE), / əˈniːstrəm /; / ˌanɪˈɒstrəm / (BrE)
In nonhuman mammals, a period of sexual inactivity between two periods of estrus.

Examples

The anestrum period
During anestrum, hormonal levels in the female animal's body are significantly reduced.
Research on reproductive cycles often focuses on transitions between anestrum and estrus in mammals.
The veterinarian noted that the mare was currently in anestrum and would not be receptive to breeding.
Hormone levels drop and reproduction pauses throughout anoestrum in these mammals.
Many wild animals enter anoestrum during the non-breeding season.
The vet explained that the cow was in anoestrum and not ready to breed.
