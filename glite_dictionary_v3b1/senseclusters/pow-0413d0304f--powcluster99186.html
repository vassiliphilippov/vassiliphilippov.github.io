POW

noun

(abbr., in computing and cryptocurrencies) short for 'proof of work': a system in which participants perform difficult computer calculations to prove they did computational work, which helps validate transactions and add new blocks to a blockchain.

Examples

Bitcoin POW system
Bitcoin's security depends on POW, which requires miners to solve difficult cryptographic puzzles.
The team implemented a lightweight POW to prevent spam without heavy energy use.
Many critics argue that POW consumes too much electricity and harms the environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proof-of-work - the full phrase for this consensus mechanism in which work proves validity
