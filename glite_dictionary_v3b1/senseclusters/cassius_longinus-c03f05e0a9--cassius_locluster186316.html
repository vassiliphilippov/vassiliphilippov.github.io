Cassius Longinus

proper noun
Roman conspirator against Julius Caesar
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsi.əs lɒnˈdʒaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈkæsi.əs lɒnˈdʒaɪnəs / (BrE)
(died 42 BC) A Roman senator and general, known as a leading instigator in the conspiracy to assassinate Julius Caesar.

Examples

Cassius Longinus senator
Cassius Longinus was a chief architect of the plot to kill Julius Caesar.
Many historians debate whether Cassius Longinus was motivated by personal ambition or love of Rome.
Shakespeare's play 'Julius Caesar' portrays Cassius Longinus as a shrewd and persuasive conspirator.
