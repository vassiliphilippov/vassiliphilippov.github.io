mainlander

noun
person from mainland
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪnlændər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnləndə / (BrE)
a person who comes from or lives on the main part of a country or continent, rather than on an island or outlying region

Examples

A mainlander person
The mainlander was fascinated by the unique traditions of the island community.
Many mainlanders visit the islands during the summer to enjoy the beaches.
Locals sometimes find it difficult to understand the perspective of a mainlander.
The mainlander was fascinated by the island's unique traditions and culture.
During the summer, many mainlanders visit the coastal islands for relaxation.
Locals sometimes feel that mainlanders do not understand island life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: islander - a person who lives on or comes from an island
