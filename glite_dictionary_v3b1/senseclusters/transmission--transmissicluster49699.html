transmission

noun
fraction of radiant energy passing through a substance
4.8
Pronunciation: / trænˈsmɪʃən / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the fraction of radiant energy that passes through a substance, especially as specified in physics or engineering

Examples

Light transmission rate
High ultraviolet transmission is a key property of this plastic.
Scientists measured the transmission of infrared energy through the filter.
The glass had a transmission of 80 percent for visible light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transmittance - the ratio of transmitted to incident radiant energy
