homozygosity

noun
state of having identical gene alleles
9.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / hoʊˌmoʊzaɪˈɡɑsəti / (AmE), / hɒˌməʊzaɪˈɡɒsəti / (BrE)
the state of having two identical alleles for a particular gene or genetic locus

Examples

High homozygosity level
A high degree of homozygosity often results from inbreeding.
In genetics, homozygosity can increase the risk of some inherited diseases.
Researchers measured the level of homozygosity in different populations to study genetic diversity.
