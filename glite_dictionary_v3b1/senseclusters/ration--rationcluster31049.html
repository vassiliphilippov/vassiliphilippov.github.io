ration

noun
fixed portion allotted, especially during scarcity
5.2 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈræ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a fixed amount of something (such as food or supplies) officially allowed to each person, especially during a shortage or in wartime

Examples

A daily ration
Chocolate was considered a luxury and included in a soldier’s ration.
During the war, each family received a weekly ration of meat and butter.
We had to survive on half our usual ration of water in the desert.
During the expedition, their ration was reduced to conserve supplies.
Each soldier was given a ration of bread and cheese for the day.
The emergency ration included dried fruit and energy bars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allowance - an amount of something given or permitted, often regularly
  • synonym: portion - a share or part of a whole, especially food
  • synonym: quota - a fixed share or portion assigned to someone or something
