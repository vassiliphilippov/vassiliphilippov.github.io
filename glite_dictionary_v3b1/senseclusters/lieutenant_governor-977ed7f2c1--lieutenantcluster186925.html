lieutenant governor

noun
deputy to a U.S. state governor
3.9 5.1
Pronunciation: /luː.ˈten.ənt ˈgʌv.ər.nər/ /; / /ljuː.ˈten.ənt ˈgʌv.ər.nər/ (AmE), /lef.ˈten.ənt ˈgʌv.ən.ə/ /; / /lə.ˈten.ənt ˈgʌv.ən.ə/ (BrE)
an elected official who acts as the deputy to the governor of a state in the United States, and in some other countries, often succeeding the governor if that office is vacated

Examples

The lieutenant governor office
Each state elects a lieutenant governor during the general election.
The lieutenant governor assumed office when the governor resigned unexpectedly.
The lieutenant governor presided over the Senate in her official capacity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deputy - a person appointed to act as a substitute
  • degree higher: governor - chief executive of a state or province
