cash account

noun
brokerage account settled with cash
2.7 3.6
Pronunciation: / kæʃ əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / kæʃ əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
An account with a securities brokerage in which transactions are settled on a cash basis, requiring full payment for purchases by the settlement date.

Examples

A cash account balance
The investor opened a cash account to avoid the risks associated with leverage.
Unlike a margin account, a cash account does not allow you to borrow funds to make purchases.
You must pay the full amount owed when you buy stocks through a cash account.

Related Terms

  • antonym: margin account - a brokerage account allowing clients to borrow funds for investments
