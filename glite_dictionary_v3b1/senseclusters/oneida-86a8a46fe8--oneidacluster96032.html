Oneida

proper noun
Native American people
Pronunciation: / oʊˈnaɪdə / (AmE), / əʊˈnaɪdə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from New York State, or the people as a group

Examples

The Oneida people
The Oneida were one of the original members of the Iroquois Confederacy.
Many Oneida people still live in New York and Wisconsin today.
The Oneida language is being taught to younger generations to preserve their heritage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Iroquois - a group of Native American peoples including the Oneida
  • synonym: Mohawk - another Native American people of the Iroquois Confederacy
