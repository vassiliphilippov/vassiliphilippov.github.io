appall

verb
to shock or horrify
9.2
Pronunciation: / əˈpɔl / (AmE), / əˈpɔːl /; / əˈpɑːl / (BrE)
to shock, horrify, or deeply disturb someone

Examples

Many were appalled by the amount of litter left after the concert.
The cruelty of the experiment appalled the observers.
Her lack of empathy truly appalled everyone at the meeting.
It appalls me that people can treat animals so cruelly.
The level of violence in the movie appalled some viewers.
I was utterly appalled by the conditions in the hospital.
The violence in the movie appalled many viewers.
Their lack of compassion continues to appal me.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or joy to someone
  • synonym: disgust - cause to feel revulsion or strong disapproval
  • synonym: horrify - to shock or disgust someone deeply
  • synonym: shock - to surprise and horrify someone; similar meaning
