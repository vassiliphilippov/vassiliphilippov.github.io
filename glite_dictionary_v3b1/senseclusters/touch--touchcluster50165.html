touch

verb
affect emotionally
Pronunciation: / tʌtʃ / (AmE), / tʌtʃ / (BrE)
to affect someone emotionally or have an emotional impact

Examples

To touch the heart
His speech touched everyone in the audience.
She was deeply touched by the generosity of strangers.
The story touches on themes of loss and hope, moving readers to tears.
Her kindness touched everyone in the community.
The economic downturn did not touch his determination to succeed.
These regulations may touch all aspects of daily life.

  • antonym: leave cold - to fail to affect emotionally
  • synonym: move - to cause strong feelings of emotion in someone
