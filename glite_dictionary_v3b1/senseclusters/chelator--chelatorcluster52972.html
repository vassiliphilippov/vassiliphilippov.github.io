chelator

noun
metal-binding compound
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkiː.leɪ.tər/ /; / /kə.ˈleɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkiː.leɪ.tə/ /; / /kə.ˈleɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a substance or chemical that binds to metal ions and is used to remove them from solutions or the body

Examples

A chelator removes metals
Doctors sometimes use a chelator to remove excess iron from the body.
A chelator can help treat heavy metal poisoning by binding to toxic metals.
The laboratory added a chelator to the solution to prevent metal contamination.
The doctor prescribed a chelator to help remove excess iron from the patient's blood.
Scientists use a chelator in the laboratory to separate metal ions from solutions.
A chelator can be essential in treating cases of heavy metal poisoning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chelating agent - a substance that binds to metal ions to form a stable complex
  • synonym: ligand - a molecule that binds to another, often a metal ion
