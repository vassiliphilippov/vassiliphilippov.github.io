land

verb
come down and rest after being in the air
1.6
Pronunciation: / lænd / (AmE), / lænd / (BrE)
to come down and rest on a surface, or to bring something or someone to a surface (such as an aircraft, ship, or person)

Examples

To land a plane
A butterfly landed softly on her shoulder.
He waited for the ball to land before trying to catch it.
The plane will land at the airport in thirty minutes.
During the conflict, anti-aircraft fire landed two enemy planes.
The rebels attempted to land the military helicopter to prevent supplies from arriving.
They successfully landed the intruding jets before they could escape.
Authorities watched the boat land several passengers on the remote beach.
The fishermen landed their catch on the docks early in the morning.
The smugglers decided to land the cargo at night to avoid detection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bring down - to cause an aircraft to fall or descend, often by force
  • synonym: disembark - leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle
  • antonym: take off - to become airborne; to leave the ground
