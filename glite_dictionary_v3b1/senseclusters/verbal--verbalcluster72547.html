verbal

noun
verb-derived word
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
a word formed from a verb that functions as a noun, adjective, or adverb, such as a gerund or participle

Examples

A verbal in grammar
In English grammar, a verbal can act as a noun, adjective, or adverb.
Gerunds, infinitives, and participles are all types of verbal used in sentences.
Understanding how to use a verbal correctly can improve your writing skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gerund - a noun made from a verb by adding -ing
  • synonym: participle - a verb form used as an adjective or to form verb tenses
