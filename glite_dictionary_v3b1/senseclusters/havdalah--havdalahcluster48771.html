havdalah

noun
Jewish end-of-Sabbath ritual
9.9
Pronunciation: /hæv.ˈdɑː.lə/ /; / /hɑːv.ˈdɑː.lə/ /; / /hæv.də.ˈlɑː/ (AmE), /hæv.ˈdɑː.lə/ /; / /hɑːv.ˈdɑː.lə/ /; / /hæv.də.ˈlɑː/ (BrE)
a Jewish religious ceremony held at the end of the Sabbath to mark the beginning of the new week, often involving blessings over wine, spices, and a braided candle

Examples

Havdalah ceremony
The family gathered to perform havdalah at sunset on Saturday.
During havdalah, a special braided candle and sweet spices are used.
Many people sing traditional songs as part of the havdalah ceremony.
Every Saturday night, the family gathers for havdalah to mark the end of the Sabbath.
The sweet smell of spices filled the room during the havdalah ceremony.
He learned the traditional blessings for havdalah at his synagogue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kiddush - a Jewish blessing recited over wine to sanctify the Sabbath or a holiday
  • synonym: sabbath - the Jewish day of rest, observed from Friday evening to Saturday evening
