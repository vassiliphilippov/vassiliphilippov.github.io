oil-soluble

adjective
able to dissolve in oil
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl ˈsɑːljəbəl / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈsɒljʊbəl / (BrE)
capable of being dissolved in oil, rather than in water or other solvents

Examples

Oil-soluble dyes
The paint contains oil-soluble dyes for superior color durability.
These vitamins are oil-soluble and should be taken with a meal containing fat.
To remove oil-soluble stains, you need a solvent that mixes with grease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: water-soluble - able to be dissolved in water
