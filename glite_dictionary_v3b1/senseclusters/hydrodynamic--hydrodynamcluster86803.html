hydrodynamic

adjective
relating to the motion of fluids
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the branch of physics concerned with the motion of fluids (especially liquids) and the forces acting on them

Examples

A hydrodynamic shape
A whale's body shape is highly hydrodynamic, reducing drag as it swims.
Researchers measured the hydrodynamic forces acting on the bridge supports during the flood.
The hydrodynamic properties of the new boat design allow it to travel faster through water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrodynamics - the branch of physics dealing with fluids in motion
