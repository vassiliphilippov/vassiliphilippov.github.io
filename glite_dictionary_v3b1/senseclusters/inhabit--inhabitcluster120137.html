inhabit

verb
exist or be present in (something)
2.8 3.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈhæ.bɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈhæ.bət/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈhæ.bɪt/ (BrE)
to exist or be present in something, often in a metaphorical or abstract way

Examples

Feelings inhabit a mind

Doubt began to inhabit his thoughts after the incident.
Strange ideas inhabit her imagination at night.
Dreams and hopes inhabit the hearts of young people everywhere.
Lively traditions inhabit every corner of the ancient town.
Sweet memories inhabit this house, making it feel alive with the past.
