belowground

adjective
underneath the ground
Pronunciation: / bɪˈloʊˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / bɪˈləʊˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
situated or happening beneath the surface of the ground

Examples

Belowground roots
Archaeologists uncovered ancient tools buried belowground at the site.
Some animals spend most of their lives living belowground to avoid predators and extreme weather.
Tree roots spread extensively belowground in search of water and nutrients.
Many animals create belowground burrows for protection from predators.
The building has a large belowground parking facility to save space above.
The scientists measured belowground biomass to study root development.
