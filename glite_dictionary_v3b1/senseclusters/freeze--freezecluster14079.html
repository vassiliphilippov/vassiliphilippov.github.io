freeze

verb
anesthetize by cold
2.5
Pronunciation: / friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
to feel extremely cold or to make part of the body numb or insensitive with cold

Examples

To freeze a tooth
Dentists often freeze a tooth with a cold spray before working on it.
The doctor will freeze the mole before removing it to numb the area.
They used ice to freeze his finger and reduce the pain after the injury.
I always freeze in my office because the air conditioning is too strong.
She forgot her coat and started to freeze while waiting outside.
They froze during the long walk home in the snow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heat - make (something) warm or hot, the opposite of making something cold or frozen
  • synonym: numb - make (a part of the body) lose sensation, typically by cold or anesthetic
  • antonym: roast - to be or feel very hot
  • synonym: shiver - to tremble or shake as a result of cold or fear
