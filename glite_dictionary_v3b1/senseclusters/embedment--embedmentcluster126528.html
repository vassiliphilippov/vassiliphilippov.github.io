embedment

noun
fixing into material
6.7
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbɛdmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛdmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of being fixed firmly or surrounded by another material (such as fossils in rock or particles in sediment)

Examples

Fossil embedment
The embedment of fossils in rock layers helps scientists study ancient life forms.
Proper embedment of cables in the wall ensures safety and durability.
Engineers checked the embedment of the anchor bolts before starting construction.
Scientists studied the embedment of gravel to understand changes in river habitats.
High embedment of stones can affect the movement of aquatic insects in the stream.
The embedment of rocks by fine sediment was greater after the heavy rainfall.
