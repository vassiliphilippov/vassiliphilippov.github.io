Quercus rubra

noun
northern red oak tree species
9.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkw…úrk…ôs ÀàruÀêbr…ô / (AmE), / Ààkw…úÀêk…ôs ÀàruÀêbr…ô / (BrE)
the northern red oak; a large deciduous tree native to eastern North America, with broad lobed leaves and less durable wood than white oak

Examples

Quercus rubra tree
Quercus rubra is valued for its fast growth and attractive wood grain.
Many city parks plant Quercus rubra thanks to its hardiness and shade.
The leaves of Quercus rubra turn a brilliant red in the autumn.

Synonyms

  • northern red oak
    6.1 0.00/m
    northern red oak is a common English name for the tree with spiny-lobed leaves and coarse, less durable wood, while Quercus rubra is the formal botanical name.
  • Quercus borealis
    8.9 0.00/m
    Quercus borealis appears as an alternative scientific name in some texts for the spiny-lobed oak, while Quercus rubra is the standard botanical name.
