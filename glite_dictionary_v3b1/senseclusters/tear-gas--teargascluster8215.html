tear-gas

noun
crowd-control chemical
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪr ˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈtɪə ˌɡæs / (BrE)
a chemical substance that causes irritation to the eyes and respiratory system, used to disperse crowds by making people cry and cough

Examples

Police tear-gas
Police used tear-gas to break up the protest when the crowd became unruly.
Many people covered their faces to protect themselves from the tear-gas in the air.
The effects of tear-gas can last for several minutes after exposure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pepper spray - a chemical spray causing irritation, used for self-defense or crowd control
  • degree lower: smoke bomb - a device that releases smoke for concealment or signaling
