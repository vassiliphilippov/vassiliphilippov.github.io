reintervention

noun
additional medical procedure
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
a second or additional medical or surgical procedure performed after an initial intervention has failed or complications have arisen

Examples

Surgical reintervention
After the initial operation failed, the surgeon recommended a reintervention to address the complications.
The risk of needing a reintervention increases if the first procedure is not successful.
Doctors closely monitor patients to determine if a reintervention becomes necessary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intervention - a medical or surgical procedure to treat a condition
