parliamentarianism

noun
parliamentary procedures
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrləməntɛˈriːənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɑːlɪəˌmɛntˈeəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
the system or practice of conducting legislative or deliberative assemblies according to established parliamentary rules and procedures

Examples

Parliamentarianism in meetings
The club adopted parliamentarianism to ensure fair and orderly meetings.
Training in parliamentarianism helped the committee resolve disputes efficiently.
Many organizations rely on parliamentarianism to guide their decision-making processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anarchy - a state of disorder due to absence of authority or rules
  • synonym: parliamentary procedure - the rules and methods for conducting meetings and making decisions in assemblies
