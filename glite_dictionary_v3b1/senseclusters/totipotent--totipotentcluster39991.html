totipotent

adjective
able to develop into any cell type
8.5 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈtoʊ.tɪ.poʊ.tənt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.tɪ.pə.tənt/ /; / /ˈtɒ.tɪ.pə.tənt/ (BrE)
(of a cell) capable of developing into any type of cell or a complete organism.

Examples

Totipotent cell
A single totipotent zygote can give rise to an entire organism.
Biologists study totipotent cells to understand early development and regeneration.
Embryonic stem cells are considered totipotent because they can form any cell type in the body.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: multipotent - (of a cell) able to give rise to a limited range of cells within a tissue
  • degree lower: pluripotent - (of a cell) able to develop into many, but not all, cell types
