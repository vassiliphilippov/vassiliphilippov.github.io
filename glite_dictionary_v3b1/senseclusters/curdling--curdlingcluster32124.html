curdling

noun
forming semisolid lumps in a liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝːdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːdlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of forming semisolid lumps in a liquid, usually referring to the coagulation of milk or similar substances

Examples

Curdling of milk
During cheese making, curdling is an essential step to separate curds from whey.
Improper storage may lead to the curdling of cream in your refrigerator.
The curdling of milk can occur if lemon juice is added too quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clotting - the process of forming clots or thick lumps in a liquid
  • synonym: coagulation - the process of a liquid changing to a thickened or solid state, especially of blood or milk
