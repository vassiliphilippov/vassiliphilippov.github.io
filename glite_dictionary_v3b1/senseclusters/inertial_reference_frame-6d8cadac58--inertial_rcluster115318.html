inertial reference frame

noun
frame of reference where Newton's first law holds
8.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀà…ùÀê É…ôl Ààr…õf…ö…ôns fre…™m / (AmE), / …™nÀà…úÀê É…ôl Ààr…õf…ôr…ôns fre…™m / (BrE)
A coordinate system or physical frame of reference in which a body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted on by outside forces, as described by Newton's first law of motion.

Examples

An inertial reference frame
Gravity complicates matters because Earth is only approximately an inertial reference frame.
The laws of physics take their simplest form in an inertial reference frame.
When analyzing the motion of the train, physicists chose an inertial reference frame for their calculations.

Synonyms

  • inertial frame
