set in one's ways

adjective
unwilling to change habits or opinions
4.6
Pronunciation: / sɛt ɪn wʌnz weɪz / (AmE), / sɛt ɪn wʌnz weɪz / (BrE)
unwilling to change one's habits, beliefs, or ways of doing things

Examples

Many managers become set in their ways after years of running the company.
My grandfather is set in his ways and refuses to try new technologies.
