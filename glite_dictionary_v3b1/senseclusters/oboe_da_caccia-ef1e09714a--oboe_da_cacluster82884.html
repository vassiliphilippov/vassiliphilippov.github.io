oboe da caccia

noun
a historical alto oboe
Pronunciation: / oʊˌboʊ də ˈkɑtʃə / (AmE), / əʊˌbəʊ də ˈkatʃə / (BrE)
a historical alto member of the oboe family, used chiefly in the Baroque period and regarded as a predecessor of the English horn.

Examples

The oboe da caccia instrument
It is rare to find a musician who specializes in the oboe da caccia today.
Many musicologists consider the oboe da caccia to be the forerunner of the English horn.
The oboe da caccia is featured in some of Bach's sacred cantatas.
