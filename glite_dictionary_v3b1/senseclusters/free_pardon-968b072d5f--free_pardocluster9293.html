free pardon

noun
official forgiveness of a crime
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfri ˌpɑrdn / (AmE), / ˈfriː ˌpɑːdn / (BrE)
an official act by which a person is formally forgiven for a crime and freed from the consequences of their conviction

Examples

A free pardon
A free pardon restores all civil rights lost as a result of conviction.
After decades in prison, she received a free pardon from the government.
The court granted him a free pardon after new evidence proved his innocence.
