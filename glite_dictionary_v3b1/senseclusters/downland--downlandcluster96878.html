downland

noun
open chalk hills
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of open chalk hills, especially found in southern England, often covered with grassland

Examples

Rolling downland hills
She enjoyed hiking across the rolling downland in the spring.
The sheep grazed peacefully on the grassy downland above the village.
Many rare wildflowers can be found on the chalky soils of the downland.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lowland - an area of land that is lower than the surrounding land
  • synonym: upland - an area of high or hilly land
