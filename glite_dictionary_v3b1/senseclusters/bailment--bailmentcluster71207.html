bailment

noun
transfer of property in trust (legal term)
7.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪlmənt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪlmənt / (BrE)
the act of delivering personal property by one party (the bailor) to another (the bailee) in trust for a specific purpose, under an agreement to return or otherwise dispose of it as directed

Examples

Contract of bailment
The contract of bailment requires the bailee to care for the goods entrusted to them.
The jeweler accepted the ring as a bailment for cleaning and repair.
When you leave your car with a valet service, a bailment is created by law.
