letterbox

noun
widescreen video format
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtərˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəbɒks / (BrE)
a widescreen video format in which black bars are added above and below the image to preserve the original aspect ratio

Examples

Letterbox format movie
The movie was presented in letterbox format to maintain its original cinematic look.
Some viewers prefer letterbox because it shows the entire width of the film as intended.
Older televisions often displayed black bars when playing letterbox DVDs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fullscreen - a video format that fills the entire screen, often cropping the original image
  • synonym: widescreen - a format with a wider aspect ratio than standard
