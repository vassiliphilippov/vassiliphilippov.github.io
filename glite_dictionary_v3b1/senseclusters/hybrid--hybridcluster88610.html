hybrid

noun
word made from parts of different languages
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪbrɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪbrɪd / (BrE)
A word formed by combining elements from different languages, such as a prefix from one language and a root from another.

Examples

A hybrid word
'Automobile' is another hybrid, combining Greek and Latin elements.
Some linguists caution against coining unnecessary hybrids in technical vocabulary.
The term 'television' is a classic hybrid with roots in Greek and Latin.
