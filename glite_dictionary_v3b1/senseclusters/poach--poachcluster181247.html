poach

verb
to hunt or catch animals illegally
1.0 5.0
Pronunciation: /poʊtʃ/ (AmE), /pəʊtʃ/ (BrE)
to hunt, catch, or kill animals illegally, especially on someone else's land or in a protected area

Examples

To poach animals
He was caught poaching fish in the river without a permit.
Several villagers were arrested for poaching deer in the protected forest.
The rangers patrol the park to prevent people from poaching endangered animals.
Authorities arrested three men who tried to poach rhinos in the national park.
It is a crime to poach wildlife in many countries worldwide.
Some animals are endangered because people continue to poach them for their fur and tusks.
