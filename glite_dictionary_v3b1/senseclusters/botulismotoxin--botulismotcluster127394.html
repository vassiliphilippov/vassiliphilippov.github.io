botulismotoxin

noun
toxin causing botulism
Pronunciation: / boʊˌtjuːlɪzmoʊˈtɒksɪn / (AmE), / bəʊˌtjuːlɪzməʊˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a highly potent toxin produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum, responsible for causing botulism and sometimes used in medical or bioweapon contexts
