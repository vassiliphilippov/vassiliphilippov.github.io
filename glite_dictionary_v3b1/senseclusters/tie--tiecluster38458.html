tie

noun
equality of score in a contest
Pronunciation: / taɪ / (AmE), / taɪ / (BrE)
a situation in a contest or game where two or more competitors have the same score or result

Examples

A tie game
After three intense rounds, the chess tournament resulted in a tie.
If there is a tie, the winner will be decided in overtime.
The match ended in a tie when both teams scored two goals each.
Despite their best efforts, the quiz ended in a tie between the top two students.
If there is a tie, the rules state that the game must go into overtime.
The championship match ended in a tie after both teams scored two goals each.

Related Terms

  • synonym: draw - a result in which neither side wins in a game or contest
  • antonym: loss - defeat in a contest, the opposite of win or tie
  • antonym: win - victory in a contest, the opposite of tie or draw
