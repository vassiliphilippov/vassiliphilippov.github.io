LOX

noun
liquid oxygen used in rocket engines
9.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / lÉ‘ks / (AmE), / lÉ’ks / (BrE)
a bluish, translucent, magnetic liquid form of oxygen obtained by compressing and cooling gaseous oxygen below its boiling point; used as an oxidizer in rocket propellants

Examples

Storing LOX safely requires specialized insulated containers.
Technicians carefully handled the LOX tanks due to the extremely low temperatures required.
The rocket was fueled with LOX and kerosene for the test launch.
