weak

adjective
lacking bodily strength
1.0
Pronunciation: / wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
lacking physical strength or power; not strong in body, muscles, or structure; easily damaged or unable to withstand force

Examples

Weak muscles
After a long illness, she felt too weak to get out of bed.
He was so weak from hunger that he could barely stand.
The weak patient struggled to lift the heavy suitcase.
If the bridge is built with weak materials, it might collapse under heavy loads.
She was afraid her argument was too weak to convince the panel.
The company went bankrupt because its financial foundation was weak.
His grip was so weak that he could barely hold the bag.
The weak pillar cracked under the weight of the roof.
The old bridge had become too weak to carry vehicles safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness
  • antonym: strong - having great physical power or strength
