Stillson wrench

noun
type of pipe wrench with self-tightening jaws
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪlsən rɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈstɪlsən rɛntʃ / (BrE)
a large pipe wrench with L-shaped adjustable jaws that grip tighter as more pressure is applied to the handle

Examples

A large Stillson wrench
He used a Stillson wrench to loosen the stubborn pipe fitting under the sink.
The plumber reached for his trusty Stillson wrench when the regular spanner wouldn't do the job.
With a firm grip from the Stillson wrench, the corroded pipe finally turned.
