myeloblastic

adjective
about myeloblasts
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪələˈblæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪələˈblæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving myeloblasts, which are immature cells found in bone marrow

Examples

Myeloblastic cells
The doctor explained that myeloblastic cells are a key feature of certain types of leukemia.
Researchers are studying myeloblastic changes in bone marrow to better understand blood cancers.
A myeloblastic process can indicate the early stages of acute myeloid leukemia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lymphoblastic - relating to lymphoblasts, immature cells that develop into lymphocytes
  • synonym: myeloid - relating to bone marrow or the cells derived from it
