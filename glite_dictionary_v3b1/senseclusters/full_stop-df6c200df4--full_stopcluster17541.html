full stop

noun
definitive end; complete halt
Pronunciation: / fʊl stɑp / (AmE), / fʊl stɒp / (BrE)
a definitive end to something; a complete halt

Examples

A sudden full stop

His resignation put a full stop to any hopes of reconciliation.
The negotiations reached a full stop after the latest disagreement.
