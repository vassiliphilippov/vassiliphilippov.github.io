torment

noun
severe suffering or affliction
2.7 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔːrˌmɛnt /; / ˈtɔrˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈtɔːmɛnt /; / ˈtɔː.mənt /; / ˈtɔːmənt / (BrE)
severe physical or mental suffering; great pain or distress, or the act of causing it

Examples

A life of torment
She lived through years of torment after the accident.
The memory of his failure was a constant torment to him.
The prisoners endured unimaginable torment during their captivity.
After the accident, he was in such torment that he could not sleep for days.
Her illness caused her constant physical torment until the very end.
The prisoner endured endless torment at the hands of his captors.
For some children, social media can become a source of torment rather than enjoyment.
She endured years of torment at the hands of her cruel stepmother.
The constant torment by the bullies made his schooldays miserable.
