torment

noun
severe suffering or affliction
5.6 4.52/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îÀêrÀåm…õnt /; / Ààt…îrÀåm…õnt / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀêm…õnt /; / Ààt…îÀê.m…ônt /; / Ààt…îÀêm…ônt / (BrE)
severe physical or mental suffering; great pain or distress, or the act of causing it

Examples

A life of torment
She lived through years of torment after the accident.
The memory of his failure was a constant torment to him.
The prisoners endured unimaginable torment during their captivity.
After the accident, he was in such torment that he could not sleep for days.
Her illness caused her constant physical torment until the very end.
The prisoner endured endless torment at the hands of his captors.
For some children, social media can become a source of torment rather than enjoyment.
She endured years of torment at the hands of her cruel stepmother.
The constant torment by the bullies made his schooldays miserable.

Synonyms

  • agony
    3.9 8.87/m
    agony is an extreme, sharp suffering felt in body or mind, while torment includes that pain and also the act of causing it.
  • anguish
    6.8 6.68/m
    anguish is deep emotional pain that people feel inside, while torment can mean that pain or the act of causing it.
  • torture
    7.2 5.48/m
    torture is deliberate, often violent infliction of pain on someone, while torment also covers suffering but not always violent intent.
  • excruciation
    9.9 0.00/m
    excruciation is the infliction of extremely intense pain, often in formal language, while torment covers severe suffering or causing it.
  • pain
    1.8 47/m
    pain is the general feeling of hurt in body or mind, while torment is stronger and can mean active causing of that hurt.
  • suffering
    2.8 0.00/m
    suffering is the experience of pain or hardship over time, while torment often means extreme pain or the act that causes it.
  • misery
    2.8 16/m
    misery is a long state of great unhappiness and hardship, while torment refers to intense physical or mental suffering or its causing.
