ice-cream bean

noun
evergreen tree with edible pods, native to the Americas
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪs ˌkrim ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈaɪs ˌkriːm ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an ornamental evergreen tree native to tropical and subtropical America, known for its masses of white flowers and elongated edible pods containing sweet, cottony pulp

Examples

The ice-cream bean tree
Children in South America enjoy eating the sweet pulp from the ice-cream bean pods.
The ice-cream bean attracts many pollinators with its clusters of white flowers.
We planted an ice-cream bean in our yard for its ornamental value and delicious fruit.
