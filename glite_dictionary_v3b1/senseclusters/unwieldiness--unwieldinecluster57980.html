unwieldiness

noun
difficulty in being managed or controlled
8.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwildi.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwiːldi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to control, manage, or handle, typically because of complexity, size, or awkwardness

Examples

Unwieldiness of the system
He criticized the unwieldiness of the committee's structure as a barrier to progress.
The unwieldiness of the new regulations made them hard to implement effectively.
Their argument centered on the unwieldiness of the current tax code.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manageability - the quality of being easy to control or deal with
  • synonym: unwieldy - difficult to handle because of size or complexity
