ground-effect machine

noun
vehicle riding on air cushion
7.1 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊnd ɛˌfɛkt məˈʃin / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ɪˌfɛkt məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A vehicle that travels over land or water by riding on a cushion of air created by jet engines.

Examples

A ground-effect machine vehicle
In the 1960s, the invention of the ground-effect machine revolutionized amphibious transportation.
Military forces used a ground-effect machine to quickly traverse the marshy terrain.
The ground-effect machine skimmed across the lake, leaving barely a ripple behind it.
