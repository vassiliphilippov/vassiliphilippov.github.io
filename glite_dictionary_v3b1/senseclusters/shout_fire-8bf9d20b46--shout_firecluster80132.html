shout fire

verb
falsely warn of danger by crying 'fire'
Pronunciation: / ʃaʊt ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ʃaʊt ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to falsely warn others of danger, especially by calling out 'fire', causing unnecessary panic or alarm

Examples

To shout fire falsely
He was warned not to shout fire just to get attention during the assembly.
It is against the law to shout fire in a crowded theater without cause.
The phrase 'don't shout fire in a crowded theater' is often cited in free speech debates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cry wolf - to raise a false alarm repeatedly
