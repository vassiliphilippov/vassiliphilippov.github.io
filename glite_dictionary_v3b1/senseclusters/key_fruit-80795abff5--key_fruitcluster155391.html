key fruit

noun
winged seed of ash, elm, or maple
9.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ki Ààfrut / (AmE), / kiÀê ÀàfruÀêt / (BrE)
A winged, usually one-seeded, indehiscent fruit such as those found on ash, elm, or maple trees.

Examples

Key fruit from trees
Children often collect the spinning key fruit from elms during autumn.
Scientists studied the dispersal methods of the ash's key fruit in windy environments.
The maple tree releases its key fruit in the fall, which spin as they fall to the ground.
