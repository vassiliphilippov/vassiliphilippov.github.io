tongue tie

noun
congenital condition limiting tongue movement
6.2 6.6
Pronunciation: /tʌŋ taɪ/ /; / /tʊŋ taɪ/ (AmE), /tʌŋ taɪ/ /; / /tʊŋ taɪ/ (BrE)
A congenital condition in which the strip of skin beneath the tongue is unusually short or tight, restricting tongue movement.

Examples

tongue tie condition
If tongue tie is left untreated, it can affect a child's speech development.
Surgery was performed to correct the baby's tongue tie.
The newborn was diagnosed with a tongue tie that made breastfeeding difficult.
