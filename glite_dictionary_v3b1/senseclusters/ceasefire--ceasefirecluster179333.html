ceasefire

noun
temporary truce
3.7 4.28/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêsÀåfa…™…ôr /; / ÀàsiÀêsÀåfa…™…ö / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêsÀåfa…™…ô / (BrE)
an agreement or period when fighting is temporarily stopped, usually to allow negotiations or humanitarian aid

Examples

A ceasefire agreement
After months of conflict, the leaders finally agreed to a ceasefire to begin peace negotiations.
The ceasefire was broken when fighting resumed in the border region.
International observers monitored the ceasefire to ensure both sides followed the agreement.
After weeks of intense fighting, both sides agreed to a ceasefire to discuss peace terms.
The ceasefire allowed humanitarian organizations to deliver food and medicine to the affected areas.
Despite the ceasefire, there were reports of minor clashes along the border.
The two countries agreed to a ceasefire to allow humanitarian aid into the conflict zone.
After weeks of intense fighting, a fragile ceasefire was finally declared.
Negotiators are working hard to extend the current ceasefire and prevent further violence.

Synonyms

  • truce
    4.0 5.03/m
    a truce is an agreement to stop fighting for a short time, while a ceasefire often pauses combat to allow talks or aid.
  • armistice
    7.4 0.92/m
    an armistice is a formal halt between nations, while a ceasefire can be shorter and used to allow talks or aid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: armistice - a formal agreement to end fighting in a war
  • antonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or fight, often between countries
  • antonym: hostilities - acts of fighting in a war
  • synonym: truce - an agreement to stop fighting temporarily
  • antonym: war - a state of armed conflict between groups
