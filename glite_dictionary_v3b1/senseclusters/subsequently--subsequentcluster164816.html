subsequently

adverb
afterward; at a later time
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.sɪ.kwənt.li / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.kwənt.li / (BrE)
After something else has happened; following in time or order.

Examples

Was subsequently released
He was arrested and subsequently released without charge.
She moved to Paris and subsequently found a job in fashion.
The committee will meet next week, and subsequently issue a report on its findings.

  • antonym: previously - before the time mentioned
