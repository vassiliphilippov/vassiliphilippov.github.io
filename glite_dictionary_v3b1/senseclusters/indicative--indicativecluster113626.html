indicative

adjective
showing or suggesting; serving as a sign
1.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'of') showing or suggesting something; serving as a sign or indication of something.

Examples

Indicative of a problem
His nervous behavior is indicative of his anxiety about the exam.
Low levels of rainfall are indicative of a developing drought.
The sudden drop in sales was indicative of a larger economic problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suggestive - implying something without stating it directly
  • synonym: symptomatic - serving as a symptom or sign of something
