bode

verb
be an omen of a future event
6.1 7.2
Pronunciation: / boʊd / (AmE), / bəʊd / (BrE)
to be an omen of a particular outcome, often indicating whether something good or bad will happen

Examples

Bode well
His cheerful attitude does not bode well for the seriousness of the meeting.
Rising unemployment figures bode ill for the country's economic future.
The grey clouds bode a heavy storm later in the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: augur - to predict or be a sign of a future outcome
  • synonym: portend - to be a sign or warning that something is likely to happen
