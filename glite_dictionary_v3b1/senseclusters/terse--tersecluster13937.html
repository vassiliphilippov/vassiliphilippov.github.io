terse

adjective
brief and direct, often abrupt
7.0 1.19/m
Pronunciation: / t…úrs / (AmE), / t…úÀês / (BrE)
using few words and often not seeming friendly or polite; brief and direct in a way that may seem unfriendly or abrupt.

Examples

A terse reply
His terse reply made it clear he did not want to discuss the matter further.
She appreciated his terse style of writing, which got straight to the point.
The announcement was terse, offering no explanation for the sudden change.

Synonyms

  • curt
    7.2 0.11/m
    curt is very brief and sounds rude or unfriendly, while terse is direct and may feel blunt or abrupt.
  • brusque
    7.7 0.68/m
    brusque speaks in a blunt, rough way that can seem unfriendly, while terse is short and plainly abrupt.
  • laconic
    8.3 0.54/m
    laconic uses very few words and feels concise or mysterious, while terse is short and often seems unfriendly or abrupt.
  • short
    5.6 4.57/m
    short can describe an abrupt, impatient reply that seems rude, while terse is deliberately brief and may feel blunt or unfriendly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • synonym: laconic - using very few words
  • antonym: verbose - using more words than necessary
