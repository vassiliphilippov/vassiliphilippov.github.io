liability

noun
legal responsibility; financial obligation
Pronunciation: / laɪˈæbɪlɪti / (AmE), / laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
a legal or financial responsibility or obligation, especially to pay money or damages

Examples

Legal liability
If you cause an accident, you may have a liability to pay damages.
Reducing tax liability is a common goal in financial planning.
The company listed its total liability to creditors in its annual report.
If you borrow someone else's car, you may assume liability for any damage you cause.
The company accepted full liability for the accident and paid compensation to the victims.
Their insurance policy protects them against financial liability arising from workplace injuries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asset - something valuable owned by a person or company
  • synonym: debt - something, typically money, that is owed or due
