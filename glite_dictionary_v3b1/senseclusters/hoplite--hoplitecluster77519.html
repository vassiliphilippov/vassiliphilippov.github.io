hoplite

noun
Greek foot soldier
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːplaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɒplaɪt / (BrE)
a heavily armed foot soldier of ancient Greece, usually with a shield and spear

Examples

Ancient Greek hoplite
The hoplite marched in formation with his fellow soldiers, shields locked together.
During the battle, each hoplite relied on his armor and discipline to protect the line.
Museums often display the distinctive helmets worn by a hoplite in ancient Greece.
The hoplite formation was crucial to the military success of ancient Greek armies.
Each hoplite carried a round shield called an aspis and wore a bronze helmet.
Historians study the tactics used by the hoplites in famous battles like Marathon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cavalry - soldiers who fight on horseback
  • synonym: infantryman - a soldier who fights on foot
