gradualist

noun
supporter of slow change
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈgrædʒ.ə.wəl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈgrædʒ.ʊə.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈgrædʒ.ə.wəl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈgrædʒ.ʊə.lɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes in or supports making changes slowly and step by step rather than all at once

Examples

A gradualist leader
As a gradualist, she preferred to introduce new policies one at a time.
The gradualist approach to education reform gained support among teachers and parents.
Unlike revolutionaries, gradualists believe that lasting change happens through small steps.
