double rhyme

noun
two-syllable rhyme in poetry
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl raɪm / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl raɪm / (BrE)
A rhyme in which the last two syllables of each word or line correspond in sound.

Examples

A double rhyme pattern
In English poetry, a double rhyme can create a musical, flowing effect.
The poet skillfully used a double rhyme at the end of each stanza.
Words like 'motion' and 'notion' are examples of a double rhyme.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feminine rhyme - rhyme with a stressed followed by an unstressed syllable
  • antonym: single rhyme - a rhyme involving only one syllable
