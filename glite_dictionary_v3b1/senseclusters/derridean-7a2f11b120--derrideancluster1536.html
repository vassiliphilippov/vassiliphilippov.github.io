Derridean

noun
follower of Derrida
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛrɪˈdiːən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrɪˈdiːən / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the philosophical ideas and methods of Jacques Derrida, especially deconstruction

Examples

A Derridean thinker
The Derridean challenged traditional interpretations of the novel during the seminar.
As a committed Derridean, she often questioned the stability of language in her essays.
Many critics debated the influence of Derridean thought on modern literary theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deconstructionist - a person who practices or supports deconstruction in philosophy or literary theory
  • antonym: structuralist - a person who supports structuralism, a different approach in philosophy and literary theory
