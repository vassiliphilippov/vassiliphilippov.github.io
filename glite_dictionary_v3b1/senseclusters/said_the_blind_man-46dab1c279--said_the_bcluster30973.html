said the blind man

x
sarcastic retort; humorous disbelief
3.6
Pronunciation: / sɛd ðə blaɪnd mæn / (AmE), / sɛd ðə blaɪnd mæn / (BrE)
used humorously to express disbelief or sarcasm, often following the phrase 'I see' or a similar statement.

Examples

"Right, said the blind man"
After hearing a far-fetched excuse, he rolled his eyes and muttered, “Right, said the blind man.”
Whenever someone made a dubious claim, she would respond with, “Sure you do, said the blind man.”
“I understand everything now,” he said, and his friend replied, “I see, said the blind man.”
