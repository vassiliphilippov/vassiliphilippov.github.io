Pollyannish

adjective
unrealistically optimistic
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈænɪʃ / (BrE)
always extremely optimistic, especially in a way that seems unrealistic or naive

Examples

A Pollyannish attitude
His Pollyannish outlook made him believe every problem had a simple solution.
Some critics called her Pollyannish for ignoring the obvious challenges ahead.
Even in difficult times, he remained Pollyannish and refused to give up hope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cynical - believing people are motivated by self-interest; distrustful of others' motives
  • synonym: optimistic - expecting the best possible outcome
  • antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are; practical
