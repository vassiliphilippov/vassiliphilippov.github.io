adaptivity

noun
ability to adjust
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌædæpˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌædæpˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the ability of a system, person, or organism to adjust to changes or new conditions

Examples

The adaptivity of systems
The adaptivity of the software allows it to function in different environments.
Animals living in harsh climates often show remarkable adaptivity to survive.
Her adaptivity helped her succeed in a rapidly changing workplace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptability - the quality of being able to change or be changed easily
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being unable or unwilling to change
