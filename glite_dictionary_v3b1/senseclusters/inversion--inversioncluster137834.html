inversion

noun
medical turning inward of an organ
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a medical condition in which an organ is turned inward or inside out, such as when the upper part of the uterus is pulled into the cervical canal after childbirth

Examples

Uterine inversion
Prompt medical intervention is required when an inversion of the organ is suspected.
The obstetrician quickly recognized the rare complication of uterine inversion after delivery.
Vaginal inversion can occur under certain pathological conditions and requires surgical correction.
