turtling

noun
defensive strategy
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜːrtlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːtlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of staying in a defensive position and avoiding action, especially in games or competitions

Examples

Defensive turtling strategy
His turtling made it difficult for his opponent to win the game quickly.
Some players rely on turtling to wait for the perfect moment to strike.
The coach warned that too much turtling could lead to a boring match.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attacking - taking aggressive action against an opponent
  • synonym: defending - protecting oneself from attack or harm
