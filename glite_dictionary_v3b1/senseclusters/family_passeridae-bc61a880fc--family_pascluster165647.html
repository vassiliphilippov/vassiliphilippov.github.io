family Passeridae

noun
family of true sparrows (birds)
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli pæˈsɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pæˈsɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds known as the true sparrows, found mainly in the Old World.

Examples

The family Passeridae birds
Many ornithologists study the family Passeridae to understand the evolution of small passerine birds.
The family Passeridae includes a variety of true sparrow species found across Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Unlike Old World sparrows from the family Passeridae, New World sparrows belong to a different family altogether.
