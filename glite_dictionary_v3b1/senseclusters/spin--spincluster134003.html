spin

noun
favorable presentation of information (esp. in politics/media)
7.7
Pronunciation: / spɪn / (AmE), / spɪn / (BrE)
a particular way of presenting or interpreting information, especially to make it seem more favorable or appealing; often used in politics or the media

Examples

Political spin
Every political party gives its own spin to the latest economic figures.
She saw through the candidate's attempt to add spin to his previous mistakes.
The press secretary tried to put a positive spin on the unexpectedly poor results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - a tendency to present information in a way that reflects a particular point of view
  • synonym: slant - a particular point of view or way of considering something
