NOC

noun
undercover agent without official cover
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈen.oʊ.siː/ (AmE), /ˈen.əʊ.siː/ (BrE)
An undercover agent who operates without the protection or status of official government cover, typically for an intelligence agency.

Examples

An undercover NOC
As a NOC, she had no diplomatic immunity if caught by the host nation.
Many NOCs lead double lives, their true allegiances hidden even from close friends.
The intelligence agency deployed a NOC to gather information in the foreign country.
