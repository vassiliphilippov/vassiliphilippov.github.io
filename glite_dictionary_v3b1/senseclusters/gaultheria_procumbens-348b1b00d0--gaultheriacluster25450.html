Gaultheria procumbens

noun
creeping shrub yielding wintergreen oil
Pronunciation: / ɡɔlˈθɪriə proʊˈkʌmbənz / (AmE), / ɡɔːlˈθɪəriə prəʊˈkʌmbənz / (BrE)
A low, creeping shrub native to eastern North America, with white bell-shaped flowers and red, spicy, berrylike fruit, whose aromatic leaves yield wintergreen oil.

Examples

Gaultheria procumbens red berries
In the forest undergrowth, Gaultheria procumbens can be easily identified by its bright red berries.
Many herbal teas include leaves from Gaultheria procumbens for their minty flavor.
The aromatic leaves of Gaultheria procumbens are often distilled to produce wintergreen oil.
