red-brick

adjective
relating to newer British universities
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌbrɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌbrɪk / (BrE)
(of a British university) founded in the late 19th or 20th century, as opposed to older institutions like Oxford or Cambridge; also relating to or characteristic of such universities.

Examples

Red-brick university
Many red-brick institutions expanded rapidly during the 20th century.
She studied law at a red-brick university in Manchester.
The city’s red-brick school has a strong engineering department.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ancient university - a British university founded before the 19th century
  • synonym: civic university - another term for a university with a focus on vocational or civic education, often overlaps with 'red-brick'
