outride

verb
ride better or faster than others
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈraɪd / (AmE), / aʊtˈraɪd / (BrE)
to ride better, faster, or further than someone or something else

Examples

To outride a rival
As the group approached the final hill, she managed to outride all her rivals.
No one could outride the old cowboy on his trusty horse.
The young cyclist trained hard so he could outride his competition in the next race.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outperform - to do better than someone
