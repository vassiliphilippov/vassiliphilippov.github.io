smoke-dried

adjective
dried by exposure to smoke (usually food)
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsmoʊkˌdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkˌdraɪd / (BrE)
(especially of meat or fish) dried and preserved by exposure to wood smoke.

Examples

Smoke-dried meat
Many African cuisines rely on smoke-dried meats for their rich flavor and long shelf life.
The chef prepared a traditional dish using smoke-dried fish from the local market.
We sampled smoke-dried sausages that had been hanging in the smokehouse for weeks.
