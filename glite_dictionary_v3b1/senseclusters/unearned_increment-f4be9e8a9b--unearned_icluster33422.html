unearned increment

noun
rise in property value not due to owner's actions
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɝːnd ˈɪŋ.krə.mənt / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːnd ˈɪŋ.krɪ.mənt / (BrE)
an increase in the value of property resulting from external factors, such as economic growth or market developments, rather than from the owner's efforts or investment

Examples

An unearned increment in value
Landowners sometimes receive an unearned increment as urban expansion increases property demand.
Taxation policies may target the unearned increment to ensure fairness between investors and the public.
The developer benefited from a substantial unearned increment when the government built a new railway nearby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: windfall - an unexpected gain or piece of good fortune
