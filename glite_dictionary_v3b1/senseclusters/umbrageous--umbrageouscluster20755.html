umbrageous

adjective
easily offended or angered
Pronunciation: / ʌmˈbreɪdʒəs / (AmE), / ʌmˈbreɪdʒɪəs / (BrE)
feeling or showing anger, especially at something perceived as unjust or offensive; easily offended or resentful

Examples

Umbrageous mood
He has an umbrageous temperament, quick to take offense at the smallest slight.
She grew umbrageous when her honesty was called into question during the meeting.
The villagers became umbrageous at the unfair land reforms imposed upon them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indignant - feeling or showing anger at unfair treatment
  • synonym: outraged - extremely angry or shocked, especially at injustice
