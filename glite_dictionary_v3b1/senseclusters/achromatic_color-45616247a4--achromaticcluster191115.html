achromatic color

noun
color without hue (white, grey, black)
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪkroʊˈmætɪk ˈkʌlər /; / ˌeɪ.kroʊˈmæt.ɪk ˈkʌl.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌeɪkrəʊˈmætɪk ˈkʌlə /; / ˌeɪ.krəˈmæt.ɪk ˈkʌl.ə / (BrE)
A color that lacks hue, such as white, grey, or black.

Examples

An achromatic color palette
Artists often use an achromatic color palette to create dramatic black-and-white images.
Black is considered an achromatic color because it does not contain hue.
In design, choosing an achromatic color scheme can evoke a sense of simplicity and elegance.
Designers often use achromatic colour schemes to create a minimalist and clean aesthetic.
In photography, an achromatic colour is any shade without a distinctive hue, like black, white, or gray.
On the artist’s palette, achromatic colour provides a contrast to vivid hues.
