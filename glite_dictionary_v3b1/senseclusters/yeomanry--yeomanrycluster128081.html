yeomanry

noun
British volunteer cavalry force (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈjoʊmənri / (AmE), / ˈjəʊmənri / (BrE)
a British volunteer cavalry force, originally formed in 1761 for home defense and later incorporated into the Territorial Army

Examples

British yeomanry cavalry
Many members of the yeomanry were recruited from the rural gentry and landowners.
The yeomanry played a vital role in defending the British homeland during the Napoleonic Wars.
Today, some regiments trace their origins back to the original yeomanry units established in the 18th century.
