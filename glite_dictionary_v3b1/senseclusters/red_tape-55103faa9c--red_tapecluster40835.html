red tape

noun
excessive bureaucratic procedures
Pronunciation: / rɛd teɪp / (AmE), / rɛd teɪp / (BrE)
excessive bureaucracy or strict official rules that cause delay or make things difficult

Examples

Too much red tape
It took weeks to get the permit because of all the red tape.
She grew frustrated with the amount of red tape involved in processing the application.
The project was delayed by unnecessary red tape at every stage.
Getting a building permit involved so much red tape that we almost gave up.
She was frustrated by the endless red tape delaying her visa application.
The new law was designed to cut through red tape and speed up the process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bureaucracy - a system of administration marked by officialism, red tape, and proliferation
  • synonym: inefficiency - the state of not achieving maximum productivity; wasting effort or resources
