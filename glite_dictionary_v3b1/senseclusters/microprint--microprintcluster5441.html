microprint

noun
very small print readable with magnification
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.kroʊ.prɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.krəʊ.prɪnt / (BrE)
Extremely small printed text, typically only readable with a magnifying device, used mainly as a security feature.

Examples

Small microprint text
It's impossible to read the microprint with the naked eye.
Some passports contain microprint that reveals hidden information under magnification.
The banknote uses microprint along its edge to prevent counterfeiting.
