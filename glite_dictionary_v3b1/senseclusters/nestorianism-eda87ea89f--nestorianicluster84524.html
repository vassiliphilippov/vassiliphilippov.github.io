Nestorianism

noun
Christian doctrine distinguishing Christ's divine and human natures
9.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / n…õÀàst…îri…ôÀån…™z…ôm / (AmE), / n…õÀàst…îÀêr…™…ôÀån…™z…ôm / (BrE)
The theological doctrine, attributed to Nestorius, that Christ exists as both the Son of God and the man Jesus, and that these two natures are distinct; opposed to the Roman Catholic view that Christ is fully God.

Examples

Nestorianism doctrine
Modern references to Nestorianism often appear in discussions of early Christian history.
Scholars debate whether Nestorianism truly divided Christ's two natures as critics claim.
The council condemned Nestorianism as heresy in the fifth century.

Synonyms

  • Nestorian belief
    9.9 0.00/m
    Nestorian belief names specific doctrines about Christ's two natures, while Nestorianism is the historical movement that promotes them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Monophysitism - Doctrine asserting Christ has only a single divine nature
  • synonym: dyophysitism - Doctrine asserting Christ has two natures, divine and human
