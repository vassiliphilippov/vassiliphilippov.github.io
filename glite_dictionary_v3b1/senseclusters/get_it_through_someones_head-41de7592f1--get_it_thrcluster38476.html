get it through someone's head

verb
make someone understand or accept something
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɪt θruː ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪt θruː ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to manage to make someone understand or accept something, often after repeated effort

Examples

To get it through her head
Eventually, she managed to get it through her head that the project was cancelled.
I hope they finally get it through their heads that honesty is the best policy.
No matter how many times I explain it, I can't get it through his head that he needs to be on time.
