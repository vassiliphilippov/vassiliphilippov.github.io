declaration of estimated tax

noun
tax statement estimating yearly tax liability
7.4 8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən əv ˌɛstəˈmeɪtəd tæks / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən əv ˌɛstɪˈmeɪtɪd tæks / (BrE)
a statement filed by a taxpayer estimating the amount of tax due for the year, required when income tax withheld is insufficient to cover the total liability

Examples

File a declaration of estimated tax
A self-employed individual must file a declaration of estimated tax if their withholding is not sufficient.
Failure to submit a declaration of estimated tax can result in penalties by the tax authorities.
The accountant helped her prepare her declaration of estimated tax for the upcoming year.
