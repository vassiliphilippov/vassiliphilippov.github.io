haplobiontic

adjective
single haploid phase
Pronunciation: / ˌhæpləbaɪˈɑntɪk / (AmE), / ˌhæpləbaɪˈɒntɪk / (BrE)
describing an organism whose life cycle consists mainly of a single haploid phase, except for the zygote

Examples

Haplobiontic green algae
Certain green algae are haplobiontic, with only the zygote being diploid.
A haplobiontic life cycle is common among some types of fungi and algae.
Researchers studied the haplobiontic nature of these organisms to understand their reproduction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diplobiontic - having both haploid and diploid multicellular stages in the life cycle
