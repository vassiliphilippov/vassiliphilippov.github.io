homoscedasticity

noun
equal variance property
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊmoʊskɛˌdæˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌhɒməʊskɛˌdæˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
the property of a set of data having equal variance across different groups or samples

Examples

Equal homoscedasticity variance
Statistical tests often assume homoscedasticity to ensure valid results.
Researchers checked for homoscedasticity before interpreting the regression output.
Violating homoscedasticity can lead to misleading conclusions in data analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heteroscedasticity - the property of having unequal variance across groups or samples
