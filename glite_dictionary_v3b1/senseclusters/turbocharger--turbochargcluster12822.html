turbocharger

noun
engine power device
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrboʊˌtʃɑrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌtʃɑːdʒə / (BrE)
a device that increases an engine's power by forcing extra air into the combustion chamber using a turbine

Examples

Car turbocharger
The mechanic installed a new turbocharger to boost the car's performance.
Many modern diesel engines use a turbocharger for better fuel efficiency and power.
After upgrading the turbocharger, the vehicle could accelerate much faster than before.

Related Terms

  • synonym: supercharger - a device that increases engine power by compressing air, usually driven mechanically
