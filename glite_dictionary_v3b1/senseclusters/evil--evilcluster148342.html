Examples

Synonyms

  • wicked
    4.6 6.51/m
    wicked is morally wrong and often used in everyday speech to describe bad actions, while evil is broader and more serious.
  • nefarious
    8.8 2.41/m
    nefarious describes secret, criminal plans and sounds formal in writing, while evil is the general word for moral harm.
  • villainous
    7.3 0.77/m
    villainous describes people or acts like a story villain and feels strong and theatrical, while evil covers real moral wrongdoing.
  • diabolical
    8.2 1.57/m
    diabolical sounds extreme and devilish, often used for cruel clever plans, while evil is a broader moral wrong.
  • vicious
    4.9 3.74/m
    vicious is violent, cruel, and aimed to hurt others in action, while evil describes moral badness and harm in general.
