hagfish

noun
eel-like scavenging marine fish
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhæɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhæɡ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A primitive, eel-like marine fish with a round mouth surrounded by tentacles, known for feeding on dead or dying fish by boring into their bodies.

Examples

A hagfish in ocean
A single hagfish can enter a carcass and consume it from the inside out.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of the hagfish in the deep sea to better understand scavenger species.
The hagfish produces slime as a defense mechanism when threatened by predators.
