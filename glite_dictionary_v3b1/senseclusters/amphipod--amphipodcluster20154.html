amphipod

noun
small crustacean of the order Amphipoda
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.fəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈam.fɪˌpɒd / (BrE)
a small, laterally compressed crustacean of the order Amphipoda, typically found in marine or freshwater environments

Examples

Small amphipods in water
Many fish feed on amphipods as a primary source of food in their habitat.
Scientists discovered a rare species of amphipod living in the deep ocean trench.
The sandy shore was teeming with amphipods that scurried among the seaweed.
