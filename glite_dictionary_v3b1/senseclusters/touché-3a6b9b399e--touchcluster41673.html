touché

interjection
acknowledging a good point
8.9
Pronunciation: / tuˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / tuˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
used to acknowledge that someone has made a good or clever point in a discussion or argument

Examples

Touche after a smart point
When she pointed out the flaw in his argument, he simply replied, touché.
After a clever comeback in the debate, the audience laughed and someone said, touché.
He admitted defeat with a smile and a quick touché after losing the chess match.
When she pointed out my mistake, I could only reply with a quick touché.
"You got me there," he said, raising his hand in a gesture of touché.
After his witty comeback, everyone at the table laughed and said touché.

Related Terms

  • synonym: checkmate - used to indicate a decisive or winning move in an argument
  • synonym: well played - used to praise someone's clever remark or move
  • synonym: well said - an expression used to praise a good remark
