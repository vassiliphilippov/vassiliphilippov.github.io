noun
conciseness in style or expression
6.7 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…úrsn…ôs / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀêsn…™s / (BrE)
the quality of being brief and concise in expression; using few words to convey much meaning

Examples

Remarkable terseness
His speech was remarkable for its terseness, avoiding unnecessary detail and getting straight to the point.
In legal writing, terseness can help clarify complex arguments for the reader.
The editor admired the terseness of the author's style, which conveyed deep meaning in just a few words.

Synonyms

  • conciseness
    5.5 0.08/m
    conciseness shows clear, economical wording used in many texts, while terseness often feels sharper or more abrupt in tone.
  • succinctness
    6.3 0.03/m
    succinctness gives a compact, polished idea with no extra words, while terseness can sound blunt or cold.
  • concision
    7.7 0.15/m
    concision prefers tight, plain language to cut clutter, while terseness may often suggest a sharper, harsher tone.
  • laconism
    9.9 0.01/m
    laconism names a classical, literary short style used for effect, while terseness simply means using few words.
  • brevity
    5.3 1.48/m
    brevity focuses on shortness of words or time in speech, while terseness stresses the compact, sometimes sharp way of speaking.
  • briefness
    3.4 0.03/m
    briefness often refers to short duration or notes in time, while terseness points to concise, tightly worded expression.
