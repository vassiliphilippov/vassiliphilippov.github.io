overarm

adjective
with arm moved from above the shoulder
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvərˌɑːm / (BrE)
With the arm moved forward and downward from above the shoulder, especially in throwing or hitting sports actions.

Examples

An overarm throw
He threw the baseball overarm, aiming for a fast pitch.
She prefers to serve overarm in tennis for more power.
The bowler delivered the ball overarm for greater speed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underarm - with the arm moved forward from below or at shoulder level
