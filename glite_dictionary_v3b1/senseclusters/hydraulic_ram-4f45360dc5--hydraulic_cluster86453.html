hydraulic ram

noun
water-lifting device using kinetic energy
5.7
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑːlɪk ræm / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk ræm / (BrE)
a device for raising water to a higher level, using the kinetic energy of flowing water without an external power source

Examples

A hydraulic ram device
A hydraulic ram can operate continuously as long as there is a steady flow of water.
Many remote communities use a hydraulic ram to move water without electricity.
The old farm relied on a hydraulic ram to supply water to the hillside tanks.
