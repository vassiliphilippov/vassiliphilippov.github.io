saprophyte

noun
organism feeding on dead organic matter
Pronunciation: / ˈsæprəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæprəʊfaɪt / (BrE)
an organism, especially a fungus or bacterium, that feeds on and derives nourishment from dead or decaying organic matter

Examples

Saprophyte fungus
Bacterial saprophytes play an essential role in breaking down decaying organisms in the soil.
Many species of fungi are saprophytes that recycle nutrients from dead plant material.
Unlike parasites, saprophytes obtain their nutrients from non-living matter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parasite - an organism that lives on or in another and benefits at the host's expense
