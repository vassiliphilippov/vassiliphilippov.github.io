bifurcate

verb
divide into two branches
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.fɚ.keɪt /; / ˈbaɪ.fɚˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɪ.fə.keɪt /; / ˈbɪ.fɜː.keɪt / (BrE)
to split or divide into two parts or branches

Examples

To bifurcate a river

If left unchecked, the path may bifurcate and confuse hikers.
The river will bifurcate into two distinct streams at this point.
Urban planners decided to bifurcate the main road to ease traffic congestion.
