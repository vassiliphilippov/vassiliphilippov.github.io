paralyzingly

adverb
in a disabling way
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlaɪzɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌlaɪzɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that completely stops someone from moving, acting, or responding, often because of fear, shock, or overwhelming emotion

Examples

Paralyzingly nervous
He was paralyzingly nervous before his first big performance on stage.
The news was paralyzingly shocking, leaving everyone speechless for several minutes.
She found the silence in the room paralyzingly uncomfortable and could not speak.
The news was so shocking it left her paralyzingly unable to respond for several minutes.
The team faced paralyzingly complex problems that halted their progress.
Fear can be paralyzingly strong, stopping people from taking any action.
The news was paralyzingly shocking, leaving everyone speechless for several minutes.
He found the silence in the room paralyzingly awkward and struggled to say anything.
The mountain's beauty was paralyzingly breathtaking, making her forget to take a photo.
