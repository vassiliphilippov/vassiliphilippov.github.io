initial rhyme

noun
repetition of initial consonant sounds in poetry
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɪʃəl raɪm / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃəl raɪm / (BrE)
the repetition of the same consonant sound at the beginning of each stressed syllable in a line of verse

Examples

Initial rhyme in poetry
In Old English poetry, initial rhyme was more common than end rhyme.
The phrase 'Peter Piper picked a peck' is a classic example of initial rhyme.
The poet uses initial rhyme to create a musical quality in the verse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alliteration - the occurrence of the same letter or sound at the beginning of adjacent or closely connected words
