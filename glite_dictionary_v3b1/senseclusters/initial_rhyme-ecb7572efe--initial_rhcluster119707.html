Examples

Initial rhyme in poetry
In Old English poetry, initial rhyme was more common than end rhyme.
The phrase 'Peter Piper picked a peck' is a classic example of initial rhyme.
The poet uses initial rhyme to create a musical quality in the verse.

Synonyms

  • head rhyme
    8.6 0.00/m
    head rhyme is the usual term for repeating the same initial consonant in stressed syllables in a poem, while initial rhyme is the technical label.
  • beginning rhyme
    6.0 0.00/m
    beginning rhyme repeats the same consonant sound at stressed syllables, often called alliteration in speech, while initial rhyme refers to that poetic device.
  • alliteration
    5.1 0.61/m
    alliteration means repeating initial sounds in nearby words, often regardless of stress, while initial rhyme targets repeated consonants in stressed syllables only.

  • synonym: alliteration - the occurrence of the same letter or sound at the beginning of adjacent or closely connected words
