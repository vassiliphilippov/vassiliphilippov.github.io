immunomodulatory

adjective
modifying immune response
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˈmɑdʒəˌlɛtɔri / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˈmɒdjʊlətəri / (BrE)
affecting or modifying the way the immune system responds or functions

Examples

Immunomodulatory drug
The doctor prescribed an immunomodulatory drug to help control the patient's autoimmune disorder.
Researchers are studying the immunomodulatory effects of certain natural compounds on the body.
Some therapies have immunomodulatory properties that can benefit people with chronic inflammation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: immunostimulatory - increasing the activity of the immune system
  • degree lower: immunosuppressive - reducing the activity of the immune system
