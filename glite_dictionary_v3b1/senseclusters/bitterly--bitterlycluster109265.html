bitterly

adverb
with strong resentment or bitterness
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪtɚli / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəli / (BrE)
in a way that shows or expresses strong negative emotion, such as anger, resentment, or disappointment; with great intensity or harshness

Examples

Bitterly criticizes mistakes
After the loss, the team captain bitterly criticized the referee's decisions.
He bitterly opposed the new policy, feeling deeply wronged by its implications.
She bitterly regretted the harsh words she had spoken.
After the defeat, the team was bitterly disappointed and refused to talk to reporters.
He bitterly regretted not taking the job offer when he had the chance.
She spoke bitterly about her unfair treatment at work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harshly - in a severe or unkind manner
  • antonym: mildly - in a gentle or slight manner
  • synonym: resentfully - in a way that shows resentment
  • antonym: sweetly - in a kind or pleasant manner
