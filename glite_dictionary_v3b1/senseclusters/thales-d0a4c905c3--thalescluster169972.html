Thales

proper noun
ancient Greek philosopher and astronomer
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈθeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈθeɪliːz / (BrE)
(circa 624–546 BC) A presocratic Greek philosopher and astronomer, regarded as the founder of physical science, who proposed that water is the originating principle of all matter and is known for predicting a solar eclipse.

Examples

Thales the philosopher
Thales is renowned for his belief that water is the fundamental substance underlying all things.
According to Aristotle, Thales was the first to look for underlying principles in nature.
Many historians consider Thales to be the founder of Western philosophy.
