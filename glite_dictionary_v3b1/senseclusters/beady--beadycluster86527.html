beady

adjective
small, shiny, and round (esp. eyes)
5.1 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbiÀêdi / (AmE), / ÀàbiÀêdi / (BrE)
(of eyes or objects) small, round, and shining, especially suggesting watchfulness or intensity.

Examples

Beady eyes
Her beady little eyes darted around the room, missing nothing.
She noticed his beady eyes watching her from the shadows.
The crow cocked its head, its beady gaze unblinking and intense.

Synonyms

  • beadlike
    3.2 0.01/m
    beadlike describes small, round, shiny objects like craft beads, while beady specifically describes small shining eyes that look watchful.
  • buttonlike
    4.8 0.01/m
    buttonlike describes a small round shape like a clothing button, while beady refers to small shining eyes that seem watchful.
