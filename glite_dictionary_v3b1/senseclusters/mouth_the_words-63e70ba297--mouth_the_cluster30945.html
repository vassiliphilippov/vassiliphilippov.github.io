mouth the words

verb
to form words with lips silently
Pronunciation: / maʊð ðə wɚdz / (AmE), / maʊð ðə wɜːdz / (BrE)
to move one's lips to form words without producing any sound, often to give the impression of speaking or singing

Examples

To mouth the words silently
During the performance, she nervously mouthed the words to the national anthem.
He forgot the lines and had to mouth the words while the others sang.
The child pretended to sing by simply mouthing the words to the song.
