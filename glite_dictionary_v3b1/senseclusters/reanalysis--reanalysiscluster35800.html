reanalysis

noun
new linguistic interpretation
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the process of interpreting the structure or form of a word or phrase in a new way, often leading to a change in its grammatical or morphological analysis

Examples

Linguistic reanalysis
The word 'hamburger' is often cited as an example of reanalysis in linguistics.
Through reanalysis, speakers sometimes create new words or grammatical forms.
A famous case of reanalysis is the change from 'an ekename' to 'a nickname'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: misanalysis - an incorrect analysis or interpretation
  • synonym: reinterpretation - the act of interpreting something in a different way
