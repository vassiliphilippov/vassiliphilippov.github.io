direct

verb
guide actors in plays or films
3.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /də.ˈrekt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈrekt/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈrekt/ /; / /də.ˈrekt/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt/ (BrE)
to manage, control, or guide the actions or performance of people, groups, or projects (including in art, music, or business)

Examples

To direct a movie
He will direct both the actors and the technical crew during rehearsals.
It is challenging to direct a movie with a large cast of characters.
She was asked to direct the school’s annual theater production.
Please direct the driver to the nearest gas station.
She directed the rescue team through the dense forest to the injured hikers.
The traffic officer directed the cars around the accident site.
He will direct Beethoven’s Ninth Symphony with the City Orchestra next week.
She was invited to direct the university choir at the annual concert.
The renowned composer often directs her own pieces during live performances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conduct - to guide an orchestra or choir during a musical performance
  • synonym: guide - to lead or show the way to someone
  • synonym: manage - to be in charge of; to administer or supervise
  • synonym: mastermind - to plan and control a difficult or complex project or action
  • antonym: mismanage - to manage something badly or wrongly
  • synonym: produce - to oversee and finance the making of a film or play
  • synonym: steer - to control the direction of a vehicle or course
  • synonym: supervise - to oversee or direct the work of others
