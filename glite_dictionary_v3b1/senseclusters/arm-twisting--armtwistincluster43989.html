arm-twisting

noun
forceful persuasion or pressure
6.1 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrm.twɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɑːm.twɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of strong personal pressure or influence to persuade someone to do something, typically through direct and forceful means

Examples

Strong arm-twisting
After much arm-twisting, the board finally approved the new policy.
She refused to give in, despite his persistent arm-twisting.
The manager used some arm-twisting to get the team to work overtime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats
  • degree lower: persuasion - the action of persuading someone or of being persuaded to do or believe something
