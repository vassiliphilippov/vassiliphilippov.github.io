arm-twisting

noun
forceful persuasion or pressure
6.3 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ërm.tw…™st…™≈ã / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀêm.tw…™st…™≈ã / (BrE)
the use of strong personal pressure or influence to persuade someone to do something, typically through direct and forceful means

Examples

Strong arm-twisting
After much arm-twisting, the board finally approved the new policy.
She refused to give in, despite his persistent arm-twisting.
The manager used some arm-twisting to get the team to work overtime.

Synonyms

  • jawboning
    8.7 0.03/m
    jawboning is public pressure by leaders using strong talk to influence people, while arm-twisting is direct, personal forceful persuasion.
  • coercion
    6.9 5.94/m
    coercion uses force or threats to make someone act, while arm-twisting is forceful persuasion often without open threats.
  • pressurization
    6.1 0.00/m
    pressurization means applying pressure or influence in a formal or gradual way, while arm-twisting is direct, personal pressure.

Related Terms
