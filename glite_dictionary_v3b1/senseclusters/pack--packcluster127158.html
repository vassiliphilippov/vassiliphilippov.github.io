pack

verb
press down tightly to compact
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /pæk/ (AmE), /pæk/ (BrE)
to press, compress, or cram things or people closely together

Examples

To pack coffee grounds
Before making espresso, you should pack the coffee grounds tightly in the filter.
Gardeners often pack the soil firmly around new plants to help them grow.
She used a spoon to pack the brown sugar into the measuring cup.
He packed the snow into a ball to make a snowman.
She packed the clothes into the small backpack as tightly as she could.
We packed shredded paper into the box to protect the fragile vase.
Fans began to pack the stadium hours before the game started.
Spectators packed into the court to hear the verdict.
The small restaurant was packed on Friday night, with no tables left.
