bifrontal

adjective
having two fronts
9.9
Pronunciation: / baɪˈfrʌntəl / (AmE), / baɪˈfrʌntəl / (BrE)
having two fronts or faces, especially referring to a building or object with two main sides or facades

Examples

Bifrontal building design
The architect designed a bifrontal building that faces both the street and the park.
A bifrontal approach allows the house to have entrances on two different roads.
The museum's bifrontal structure makes it accessible from either side of the plaza.

Related Terms

  • synonym: double-faced - having two faces or sides
  • antonym: unifacial - having only one face or side
