displacer

noun
liquid level device
9.0
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪsər / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪsə / (BrE)
a buoyant object in industrial instruments whose change in buoyancy is used to measure the level of a liquid

Examples

Tank displacer
The engineer installed a displacer in the tank to monitor the water level automatically.
Regular maintenance of the displacer ensures accurate readings in industrial processes.
A faulty displacer can lead to incorrect measurements and potential safety risks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: float - a device that floats on liquid and indicates its level
