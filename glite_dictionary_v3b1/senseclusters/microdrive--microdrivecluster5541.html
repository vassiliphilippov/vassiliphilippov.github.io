microdrive

noun
precise adjustment device
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌdraɪv / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌdraɪv / (BrE)
a laboratory device used to make very small, precise adjustments of electrodes or probes inside the brain during experiments

Examples

Brain microdrive
The scientist used a microdrive to position the electrode with extreme accuracy in the rat's brain.
Modern neuroscience labs often rely on a microdrive for precise control during brain recording experiments.
After calibrating the microdrive, the technician was able to adjust the probe by just a few micrometers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: micromanipulator - a device for making fine adjustments to laboratory instruments
