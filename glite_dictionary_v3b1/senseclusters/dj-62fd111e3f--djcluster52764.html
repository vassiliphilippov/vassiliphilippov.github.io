DJ

noun
person who plays music at events or on radio
Pronunciation: / diː ˈdʒeɪ /; / diː dʒeɪ / (AmE), / diː ˈdʒeɪ /; / diː dʒeɪ / (BrE)
a person who selects and plays recorded music for an audience, especially at clubs, parties, or on the radio

Examples

Club DJ
At the wedding, a professional DJ played all the couple's favorite tracks.
She became a popular DJ on the local radio station last year.
The DJ kept the crowd dancing all night with a great mix of songs.
Our local radio DJ has a great sense of humor and introduces new music every week.
She worked as a DJ in several nightclubs before producing her own albums.
The DJ played my favorite song at the party, and everyone started dancing.
