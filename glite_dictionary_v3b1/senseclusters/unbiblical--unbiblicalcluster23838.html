unbiblical

adjective
not in the Bible
7.4
Pronunciation: /ʌn.bɪ.ˈbɪ.kəl/ /; / /ən.bɪ.ˈbɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ʌn.bɪ.ˈbɪ.kəl/ /; / /ən.bɪ.ˈbɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
not supported by or not in agreement with the Bible or its teachings

Examples

Unbiblical teachings
Some church traditions are considered unbiblical because they have no basis in scripture.
He argued that the practice was unbiblical and should not be followed by believers.
The idea seemed unbiblical to her, as she could not find it in any passage.
Many theologians argued that the new doctrine was unbiblical and should not be adopted.
He refused to support any policy he believed to be unbiblical in nature.
The practice was criticized as unbiblical by several members of the congregation.
