fund

verb
provide money to pay debts or interest
3.5
Pronunciation: / fʌnd / (AmE), / fʌnd / (BrE)
to provide or save money for a particular purpose, project, or expense

Examples

To fund a project
Governments often fund infrastructure improvements through the sale of bonds.
She helped fund the initiative with a generous donation.
The new project was funded by several international banks.
Every month, she funds a portion of her salary into her retirement account.
Many people fund their travel by setting aside small amounts regularly.
The company plans to fund a scholarship program for local students.
Several charities fund medical research for rare diseases.
The university decided to fund the construction of a new library this year.
They are seeking investors willing to fund their technology startup.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defund - to withdraw financial support
  • synonym: finance - to provide funding or capital for something
  • synonym: save - to keep money for future use instead of spending it
  • antonym: spend - to use money to pay for something
  • synonym: sponsor - to support something financially, especially an event or activity
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something that is expected or desired, such as funds
