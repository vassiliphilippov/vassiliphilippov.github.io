LP

noun
long-playing phonograph record (33⅓ rpm)
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛlˈpi / (AmE), / ˌɛlˈpiː / (BrE)
a long-playing phonograph record (usually played at 33⅓ revolutions per minute), used for music or audio recordings

Examples

Many collectors prize rare jazz LPs for their superior sound quality.
She bought a vintage turntable to play her favorite LP albums.
