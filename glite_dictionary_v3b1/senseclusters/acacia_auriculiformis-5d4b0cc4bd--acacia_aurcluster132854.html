Acacia auriculiformis

noun
species of Australian acacia tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈkeɪʃə ɔːˌrɪkjələˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə ɔːˌrɪkjʊləˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of Australian tree in the acacia genus, known for producing materials used in tanning.

Examples

Acacia auriculiformis tree
Farmers planted rows of Acacia auriculiformis to prevent soil erosion on their land.
In Australia, Acacia auriculiformis is valued for both its wood and its fast growth.
The bark of Acacia auriculiformis is commonly used in leather tanning industries.
