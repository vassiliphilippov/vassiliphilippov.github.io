sympatholytic

noun
agent blocking sympathetic nerves
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪm.pə.θə.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ /; / /sɪm.pə.θoʊ.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ (AmE), /sɪm.pə.θə.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ /; / /sɪm.pə.θəʊ.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ (BrE)
a drug or agent that inhibits the actions of the sympathetic nervous system

Examples

A sympatholytic agent
The doctor prescribed a sympatholytic to help control the patient's high blood pressure.
Some sympatholytics are used to treat anxiety by reducing the body's stress response.
After taking the sympatholytic, the patient's heart rate returned to normal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sympathomimetic - a drug that stimulates the sympathetic nervous system
