hearth money

noun
historical Catholic payment to the pope
Pronunciation: / hɑrθ ˈmʌni / (AmE), / hɑːθ ˈmʌni / (BrE)
An annual payment made by Roman Catholics to support the papal see.

Examples

Pay hearth money
Catholic families were required to pay hearth money as a sign of loyalty to Rome.
Historians study hearth money to understand the financial ties between local communities and the papacy.
