Synonyms

  • under
    1.0 2/m
    under is used for something directly lower or covered, while below indicates a lower place or level in space or measurements.
  • underneath
    1.0 7/m
    underneath sits directly under something and often feels covered, while below just means at a lower place or position.
  • beneath
    1.0 1/m
    beneath sounds more formal or literary and can mean morally lower, while below is neutral and marks a lower place in space.
