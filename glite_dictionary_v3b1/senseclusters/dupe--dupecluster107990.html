dupe

noun
cheap imitation of a branded product
7.8 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / duÀêp / (AmE), / djuÀêp / (BrE)
(informal) a cheap imitation or replica of a branded or designer product, sold as a substitute for the original.

Examples

A dupe bag
She bought a dupe handbag that closely resembled the designer original but cost much less.
Beauty bloggers often review inexpensive dupes of luxury makeup to help readers save money.
If you prefer the look but not the price, consider buying a dupe instead of an authentic purse.

Synonyms

  • knockoff
    6.0 1/m
    a knockoff is a cheap, unofficial copy sold as a style substitute, while a dupe is the informal name for such budget alternatives.
  • imitation
    3.5 3/m
    an imitation is a general copy made to look like a product and can be a substitute, while a dupe is an informal cheap version.
  • clone
    5.5 2/m
    a clone is an unauthorized close copy of a product design, while a dupe is an informal cheap imitation sold as an alternative.
  • replica
    3.6 6/m
    a replica is a careful, often exact copy made for display or collectors, while a dupe is an informal cheap substitute for everyday use.
  • counterfeit
    6.7 3/m
    a counterfeit is made to pass as the real branded product and break the law, while a dupe is a cheap, informal substitute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authentic - genuine; not a fake or imitation
  • synonym: knockoff - a product made to look like a more expensive brand, usually cheaper and unauthorized
  • synonym: replica - an exact copy or close reproduction of something, often of value or art
