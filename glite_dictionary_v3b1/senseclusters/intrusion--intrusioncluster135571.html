intrusion

noun
unauthorized entry onto property
6.0 6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the act of entering a place, situation, or someone's privacy without permission or in an unwelcome way

Examples

Unwanted intrusion
Burglary is considered an unlawful intrusion into a private residence.
Security cameras were installed to help deter intrusion onto the property.
The police responded quickly to the alarm that signaled an intrusion at the warehouse.
Scientists were concerned about the intrusion of non-native species into the local ecosystem.
The intrusion of loud noises disrupted the wildlife in the area.
The sudden intrusion of hundreds of tourists changed the peaceful village atmosphere.
Any intrusion into someone's privacy should be avoided whenever possible.
She considered the questions an unwelcome intrusion into her personal life.
The security system detected an intrusion and immediately alerted the police.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invasion - the act of entering a place with the intent to occupy it, often by force
  • antonym: invitation - the act of inviting someone to enter or participate
  • synonym: trespass - entry onto someone's property without permission
