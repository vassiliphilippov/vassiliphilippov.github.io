digressive

adjective
tending to stray from the main topic
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɡrɛsɪv /; / dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
departing from the main subject or topic in speech or writing; tending to stray or digress

Examples

A digressive speech
His lecture became increasingly digressive as he began discussing unrelated personal stories.
She was criticized for making a digressive comment during the meeting.
The author's digressive style made it difficult to follow the main argument of the essay.
He entertained the audience with a digressive speech full of amusing anecdotes.
Her digressive writing style made the article hard to follow.
The professor's digressive lectures often wandered far from the course material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discursive - moving from topic to topic without order; rambling
  • antonym: focused - concentrating on a single subject or goal
  • synonym: rambling - lengthy and confused or inconsequential speech or writing
  • antonym: relevant - closely connected or appropriate to the matter at hand
  • synonym: tangential - only slightly connected to what is important; diverging from the main point
