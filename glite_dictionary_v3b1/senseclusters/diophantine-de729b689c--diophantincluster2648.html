Diophantine

noun
integer-solution problem
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈfæntaɪn / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈfæntaɪn / (BrE)
a mathematical problem or equation in which only integer solutions are sought, especially in number theory

Examples

A tough Diophantine
The mathematician spent years working on a difficult Diophantine that required creative thinking.
Solving a Diophantine can be challenging because only whole number answers are allowed.
In number theory, a classic Diophantine often asks for integer solutions to polynomial equations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Diophantine equation - an equation that seeks integer solutions
  • degree higher: algebraic equation - an equation involving algebraic expressions
