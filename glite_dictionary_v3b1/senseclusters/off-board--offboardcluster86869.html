off-board

adjective
located off the main circuit board
Pronunciation: / ɑf bɔrd / (AmE), / ɒf bɔːd / (BrE)
(in electronics) located separately from the main circuit board (PCB) and connected externally rather than being mounted directly on the board.

Examples

An off-board battery
The design uses an off-board battery pack to reduce heat on the main PCB.
Engineers connected an off-board sensor to the motherboard for easier replacement during testing.
To improve signal quality, they moved the amplifier to an off-board module near the antenna.

Related Terms

  • synonym: external - located outside the main device or system; not built into the primary board or enclosure.
  • antonym: on-board - mounted on or integrated into the main circuit board (PCB).
