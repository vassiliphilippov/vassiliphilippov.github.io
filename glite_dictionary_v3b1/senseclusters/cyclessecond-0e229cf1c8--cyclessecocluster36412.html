cycles/second

noun
unit of frequency (one cycle per second)
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkəlz səˈkənd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəlz səˈkənd / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one complete cycle per second; also known as one hertz.

Examples

cycles/second unit
Early radios were tuned to specific frequencies measured in cycles/second.
The clock in this computer runs at 3 million cycles/second.
The specification lists the signal frequency as 60 cycles/second.
