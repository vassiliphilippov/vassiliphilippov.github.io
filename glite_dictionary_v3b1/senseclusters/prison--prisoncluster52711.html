prison

noun
facility for confining people as punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzən / (AmE), / ˈprɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
A building or institution where people are kept as punishment for a crime or while awaiting trial.

Examples

A prison cell
After the trial, he was sentenced to five years in prison.
Many families are affected when a loved one is sent to prison.
She works as a counselor in a high-security prison downtown.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime
  • antonym: release - the act of allowing someone to go free from prison
