Alcyonacea

noun

Pronunciation: /æl.sɪ.ə.ˈneɪ.sɪ.ə/ /; / /æl.saɪ.ə.ˈneɪ.sɪ.ə/ (AmE), /æl.sɪ.ə.ˈneɪ.sɪ.ə/ /; / /æl.saɪ.ə.ˈneɪ.sɪ.ə/ (BrE)
An order of soft corals, characterized by their flexible, fleshy bodies and absence of stony skeletons, found primarily in tropical and subtropical oceans.

Examples

Alcyonacea soft corals
Marine biologists study Alcyonacea to understand coral reef biodiversity and health.
The Alcyonacea are widely distributed in shallow tropical reefs around the world.
Unlike hard corals, members of the Alcyonacea do not build massive calcium carbonate skeletons.
