wand

noun
ceremonial staff or rod
Pronunciation: / wɑnd / (AmE), / wɒnd / (BrE)
a slender rod, often used as a symbol of authority or in ceremonial settings

Examples

Ceremonial wand
During the ceremony, the priest carried a golden wand as a symbol of office.
The judge tapped the podium with her ceremonial wand.
The magician lifted his wand to begin the ritual.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rod - a straight, thin stick or bar
  • synonym: scepter - an ornamental staff carried by rulers on ceremonial occasions
