strip mine

noun
open-pit mine for near-surface minerals
Pronunciation: / strɪp maɪn / (AmE), / strɪp maɪn / (BrE)
an open-pit mine, especially for coal, where mineral seams are close to the surface and the overlying soil and rock are removed in strips

Examples

A coal strip mine
After years of extraction, the abandoned strip mine became a habitat for various wildlife.
Environmentalists protested against the creation of a new strip mine near the national park.
The company plans to expand its strip mine operations in the region next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: open-pit mine - a surface mining technique of extracting rock or minerals from the earth by their removal from an open pit
  • antonym: underground mine - a type of mine where minerals are extracted from below the earth's surface
