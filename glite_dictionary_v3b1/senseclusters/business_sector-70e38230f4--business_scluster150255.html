business sector

noun
part of the economy consisting of businesses
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪznɪs ˌsɛktɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪs ˌsɛktə / (BrE)
The part of a country's economy made up of companies, commercial enterprises, and organizations engaged in business activities, considered collectively.

Examples

The business sector grows
Collaboration between government and the business sector can help create more jobs.
Environmental regulations were met with concern from the business sector.
The business sector is seeking new opportunities for growth after the economic downturn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: private sector - part of the economy operated by private businesses
  • antonym: public sector - part of the economy controlled by government
