differentiated

adjective
made different or distinct
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (BrE)
made or become different or distinct from others, especially as a result of development or specialisation

Examples

Highly differentiated products
The cells in the sample were highly differentiated under the microscope.
The curriculum was differentiated to meet the needs of all students.
Their company offers differentiated products that stand out in the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinct - clearly different or separate
  • antonym: uniform - not changing or varying; having the same form
