chemoprophylactic

adjective
disease-preventing drug
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˌproʊfəˈlæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˌprɒfɪˈlæktɪk / (BrE)
describing a drug or treatment used to prevent disease, especially infection, by chemical means

Examples

Chemoprophylactic drug treatment
Doctors may recommend a chemoprophylactic medication before traveling to regions with high malaria risk.
The use of chemoprophylactic drugs has greatly reduced the incidence of certain infectious diseases.
Some vaccines are considered chemoprophylactic because they help prevent illness before exposure.

  • antonym: therapeutic - relating to the treatment of disease
