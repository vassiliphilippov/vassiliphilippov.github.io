fault

noun
a weakness or imperfection
1.0 1.6
Pronunciation: /fɔːlt/ /; / /fɑːlt/ (AmE), /fɔːlt/ /; / /fɒlt/ (BrE)
a mistake, error, or responsibility for something wrong; being to blame for a problem or bad situation

Examples

At fault for the mistake
Every system has its fault, no matter how sophisticated it is.
He readily admitted his faults during the performance review.
Honesty about one's faults is essential for personal growth.
He always tries to find a fault in other people's work instead of his own.
She admitted her fault and apologized to everyone affected.
The accident was entirely my fault because I wasn’t paying attention.
Don't blame yourself for something that isn't your fault.
I know it was my fault that we missed the train.
The accident was entirely her fault, not yours.
