octopus

noun
sea mollusk with eight arms
Pronunciation: / ˈɑk.tə.pʊs / (AmE), / ˈɒk.tə.pəs / (BrE)
A sea animal with a soft oval body and eight long, flexible tentacles; a type of cephalopod.

Examples

A giant octopus
At the aquarium, children watched the octopus squeeze through a narrow opening.
Scientists discovered a new species of octopus in the deep sea.
The octopus quickly changed color to blend in with the rocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cuttlefish - a marine cephalopod with a broad body, eight arms, and two tentacles
  • synonym: squid - another type of cephalopod with a more elongated body and ten arms
