mole sieve

noun
fine strainer for molecular separation
9.8
Pronunciation: / moʊl siːv / (AmE), / məʊl siːv / (BrE)
a device or material with fine pores used to separate molecules by size, commonly used in chemical processes for filtration or purification

Examples

A mole sieve device
After mixing the ingredients, she filtered them through a mole sieve for optimal clarity.
Industrial processes often rely on a mole sieve to separate gases or liquids by molecular size.
The laboratory technician used a mole sieve to purify the chemical solution.
