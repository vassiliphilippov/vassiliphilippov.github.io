radiopaque

adjective
not transparent to X-rays; shows white on radiographs
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪ.di.oʊˈpeɪk / (AmE), / ˌreɪ.dɪ.əʊˈpeɪk / (BrE)
Not allowing X-rays or other forms of radiation to pass through; appearing white or light on a radiograph.

Examples

Radiopaque substance
Barium sulfate is a radiopaque substance used to enhance imaging during X-rays.
The doctor looked for radiopaque areas on the X-ray to identify possible blockages.
These surgical instruments must be radiopaque so that they are visible on scans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radiolucent - allowing radiation to pass through; the opposite of radiopaque
