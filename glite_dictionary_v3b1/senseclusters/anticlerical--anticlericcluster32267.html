anticlerical

noun
opponent of clergy power
9.4
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈkle.rɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈkle.rɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
a person who opposes the influence or power of the clergy or religious institutions

Examples

An anticlerical activist
The outspoken anticlerical challenged the church's involvement in political affairs.
During the revolution, many anticlericals demanded reforms to limit religious authority.
He was known as an anticlerical who advocated for secular education in schools.

  • synonym: secularist - a person who supports separation of religion from government
