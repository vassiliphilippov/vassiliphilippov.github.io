delocalize

verb
remove from usual place
Pronunciation: / diˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to move or remove something from its usual place, especially when moving jobs, services, or industries to another location

Examples

To delocalize jobs
Global economic forces can delocalize industries, causing manufacturing to move abroad.
In chemistry, electrons may delocalize and become shared among several atoms.
To delocalize the protein, the scientists altered its amino acid sequence.
The company decided to delocalize its customer service department to another country to cut expenses.
Many factories have delocalized their production lines to regions with lower labor costs.
After the economic downturn, several firms began to delocalize jobs to maintain profitability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralize - to bring activities together in one place
  • antonym: localize - to confine or limit to a particular place
  • synonym: outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside or foreign supplier
  • synonym: relocate - to move to a new place or location
