meandering

noun
aimless wanderings
6.1
Pronunciation: / miˈændərɪŋ / (AmE), / miˈændərɪŋ / (BrE)
a series of aimless wanderings or thoughts that move without clear direction or purpose

Examples

A meandering walk
His meandering through the old city led him to discover hidden cafes and quiet streets.
The professor's lecture was full of meandering that made it hard to follow the main point.
She enjoyed her morning meandering in the park, letting her thoughts drift freely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject
  • antonym: focus - concentration on a particular subject or activity
  • synonym: rambling - talking or walking in a lengthy and confused way
