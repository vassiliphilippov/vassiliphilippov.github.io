lipophilic

adjective
having an affinity for fats or lipids
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪpəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) having an affinity for or ability to dissolve in lipids or fats.

Examples

Lipophilic substance
Because vitamin E is lipophilic, it accumulates in fatty tissues within the body.
Researchers measured the lipophilic properties of the chemical to determine its toxicity in aquatic life.
The new drug is highly lipophilic, allowing it to cross cell membranes easily.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrophilic - having an affinity for water; easily dissolved in or mixed with water
