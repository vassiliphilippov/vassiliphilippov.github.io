bidimensional

adjective
two-dimensional
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪdaɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪdɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
having or involving two dimensions, such as length and width

Examples

Bidimensional shape
The artist created a bidimensional image using only lines and shapes.
Maps are often bidimensional, showing features in length and width but not height.
A bidimensional analysis can simplify complex data for easier understanding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: three-dimensional - having or involving length, width, and depth
  • synonym: two-dimensional - having or appearing to have only length and width
