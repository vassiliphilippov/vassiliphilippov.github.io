latah

noun
exaggerated startle syndrome
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːtɑː / (AmE), / ˈlɑːtɑː / (BrE)
a condition, mainly found in Southeast Asia, marked by exaggerated startle responses and automatic imitation of others' words or actions

Examples

latah condition
Researchers observed several cases of latah among villagers after a loud noise startled the group.
The woman with latah would often mimic words spoken by others immediately after being surprised.
Medical professionals in Southeast Asia are familiar with the symptoms of latah.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Tourette syndrome - a neurological disorder with involuntary movements and sounds
  • synonym: startle reflex - an automatic reaction to a sudden unexpected stimulus
