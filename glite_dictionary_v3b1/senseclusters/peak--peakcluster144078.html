peak

verb
reach the highest point
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /piːk/ (AmE), /piːk/ (BrE)
(of a process, event, or activity) to reach the highest or maximum point in degree, intensity, or value.

Examples

To peak in performance
Many athletes peak in their late twenties, achieving their best performances during this time.
Sales usually peak during the holiday season and then decline.
The city's population peaked just before the economic downturn began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culminate - to reach the highest or a climactic point
  • antonym: wane - to decrease or diminish in degree after reaching a high point
