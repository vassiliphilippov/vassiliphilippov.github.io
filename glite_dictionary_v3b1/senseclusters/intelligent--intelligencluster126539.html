intelligent

adjective
showing good judgment
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛlədʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒənt / (BrE)
Showing good judgment or practical sense; characterized by sound reasoning and decision-making.

Examples

An intelligent choice
Choosing a smaller, fuel-efficient car was an intelligent move in today's economy.
She offered an intelligent solution to the complex problem facing the team.
The committee made an intelligent decision after reviewing all the evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • synonym: sensible - having or showing good sense or judgment
  • synonym: sound - based on good judgment or reasoning
