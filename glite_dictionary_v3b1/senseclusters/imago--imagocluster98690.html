imago

noun
idealized mental image, esp. of a parent (psychoanalysis)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɑːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈmɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) an idealized mental image of a person, especially a parent, formed in childhood and carried into adulthood.

Examples

Childhood imago
His persistent search for perfection was shaped by an unrealistic imago of his father.
In therapy, she explored how her childhood imago influenced her adult relationships.
Psychoanalytic theory suggests the imago plays a crucial role in personality development.
