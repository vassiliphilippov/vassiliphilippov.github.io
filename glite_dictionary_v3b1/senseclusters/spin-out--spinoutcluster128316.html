spin-out

verb
to prolong unnecessarily
6.7
Pronunciation: /spɪn aʊt/ (AmE), /spɪn aʊt/ (BrE)
to make something last longer than necessary by deliberately prolonging it.

Examples

To spin-out a story
The director tried to spin-out the movie's ending to build suspense.
She managed to spin-out her presentation so it filled the entire hour.
Some authors spin-out their stories to publish more books in a series.
