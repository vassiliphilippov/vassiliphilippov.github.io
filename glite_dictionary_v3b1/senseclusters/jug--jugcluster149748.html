jug

verb
to stew meat in a jug
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /dʒʌg/ (AmE), /dʒʌg/ (BrE)
to cook or stew food in a jug or covered container, usually with liquid

Examples

To jug a rabbit
Many old English cookbooks include instructions for how to jug game birds or small animals.
The chef will jug the hare with a rich red wine sauce for tonight's special.
Traditionally, people would jug rabbit to tenderize the meat and enhance the flavor.
The chef jugged the rabbit with red wine and herbs for several hours.
In traditional English cuisine, hare is often jugged to create a rich, flavorful dish.
They jugged the meat overnight to keep it tender and moist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: roast - to cook food using dry heat in an oven or over a fire
  • synonym: stew - to cook food slowly in liquid in a closed dish
