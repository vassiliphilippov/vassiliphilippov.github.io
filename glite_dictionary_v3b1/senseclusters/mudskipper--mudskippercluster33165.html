mudskipper

noun
amphibious fish from Africa and Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌskɪpər / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌskɪpə / (BrE)
a small fish found in tropical coastal regions of Africa and Asia that can move on land using its strong pectoral fins

Examples

A mudskipper fish
Children watched as a mudskipper hopped across the muddy shore at low tide.
Researchers observed several mudskippers competing for territory on the exposed riverbank.
The mudskipper can breathe both in water and on land, making it unique among fish.
