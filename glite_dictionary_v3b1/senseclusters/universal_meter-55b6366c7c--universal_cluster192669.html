a former unit of length, defined as the length of a free seconds pendulum, differing from the modern metre by about half a centimetre, introduced by Tito Livio Burattini in 1675

Examples

The universal meter unit
Burattini proposed the universal meter as an alternative to other units of measurement in the 17th century.
The universal meter was designed to provide a consistent standard of length based on the seconds pendulum.
Unlike the modern metre, the universal meter is slightly longer due to its definition.
Before the adoption of the metric system, the universal metre was suggested as a standard measure.
The length of a universal metre was based on the swing of a pendulum.
Tito Livio Burattini proposed the universal metre in the 17th century as a universal unit of length.
