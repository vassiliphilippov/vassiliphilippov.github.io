shrewdly

adverb
in a clever or astute manner
6.0 0.79/m
Pronunciation: / Àà ÉruÀêdli / (AmE), / Àà ÉruÀêdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows clever judgment, sharp insight, or astuteness, especially in practical matters.

Examples

To shrewdly judge
By shrewdly observing the market trends, he made several profitable investments.
She shrewdly negotiated the contract to ensure the best possible terms for her company.
The detective shrewdly deduced who committed the crime from the smallest details.

Synonyms

  • astutely
    6.5 0.42/m
    astutely describes acting with clear, sharp insight, while shrewdly points to practical cleverness and quick judgment in real situations.
  • cannily
    7.4 0.14/m
    cannily suggests clever, careful planning with foresight, while shrewdly stresses quick, practical judgment in business or everyday choices.
  • sagaciously
    8.9 0.03/m
    sagaciously means showing deep wisdom and long-term thought, while shrewdly focuses on fast, practical insight for immediate problems.
  • cleverly
    3.0 2.85/m
    cleverly shows intelligence, skill, or inventiveness, while shrewdly centers on practical, often cautious judgment in real situations.
