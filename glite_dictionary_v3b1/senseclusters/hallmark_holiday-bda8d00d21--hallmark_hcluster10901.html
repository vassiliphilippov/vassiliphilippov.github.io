Hallmark holiday

noun
commercially-oriented holiday
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔlˌmɑrk ˈhɑləˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːlmɑːk ˈhɒlədeɪ / (BrE)
A holiday that is perceived to have been created or promoted primarily for commercial purposes, especially by greeting card companies such as Hallmark.

Examples

A Hallmark holiday
Critics argue that Mother's Day has been turned into a Hallmark holiday by companies profiting from it.
He refused to celebrate what he called a Hallmark holiday, saying it lacked genuine significance.
Some people see Valentine's Day as just another Hallmark holiday created to sell cards and gifts.
