Ashurbanipal

proper noun
king of Assyria; builder of Nineveh library
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːˌʃʊrˈbænɪˌpɑːl / (AmE), / ˌæʃʊəˈbænɪˌpɑːl / (BrE)
(668–627 BC) King of Assyria, known for building a great palace and a famous library at Nineveh.

Examples

Ashurbanipal king of Assyria
Ashurbanipal was renowned for creating one of the world's earliest libraries in Nineveh.
Many clay tablets from Ashurbanipal's library are now in major museums around the world.
The reign of Ashurbanipal marked a period of great cultural achievement in Assyria.
