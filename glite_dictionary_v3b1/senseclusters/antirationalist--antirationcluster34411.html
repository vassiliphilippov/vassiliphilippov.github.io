antirationalist

noun
opponent of rationality
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˈræʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈræʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who opposes or rejects the use of reason or rationality as the main basis for belief or action

Examples

An antirationalist thinker
The philosopher was labeled an antirationalist for challenging the value of logic in human affairs.
Some critics view the movement as antirationalist because it dismisses scientific evidence.
During the discussion, an antirationalist perspective emphasized the importance of intuition over analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrationalist - a person who emphasizes instinct or emotion over reason
  • antonym: rationalist - a person who believes reason is the main source of knowledge
