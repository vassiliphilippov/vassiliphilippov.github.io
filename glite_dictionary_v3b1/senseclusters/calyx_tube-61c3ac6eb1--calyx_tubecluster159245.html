calyx tube

noun
tubular flower structure bearing sepals and stamens
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.lɪks tuːb / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.lɪks tjuːb / (BrE)
A cuplike, ringlike, or tubular structure of a flower that bears the sepals, stamens, and calyx, found especially in plants of the rose family.

Examples

The calyx tube of rose
Botanists examined the calyx tube to distinguish between different rose species.
The calyx tube of the rose supports both the sepals and the petals.
The flower's calyx tube was fused and formed a protective layer around the developing fruit.
