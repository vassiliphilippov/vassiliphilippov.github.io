polishable

adjective
can be polished
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlɪʃəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be made smooth and shiny by polishing

Examples

Polishable stone surface
This type of stone is highly polishable and is often used for countertops.
The metal must be polishable to achieve the desired mirror finish.
Only polishable surfaces will look their best after waxing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - not shiny or bright; lacking gloss
  • degree higher: shiny - having a smooth, bright surface that reflects light
