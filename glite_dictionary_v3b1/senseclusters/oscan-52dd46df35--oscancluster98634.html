Oscan

noun
ancient people of southern Italy
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.skən/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.skən/ (BrE)
a member of an ancient people of southern Italy who spoke the Oscan language, especially in the region of Campania

Examples

An Oscan tribe member
Archaeologists discovered pottery believed to have belonged to an Oscan family in Campania.
Many artifacts uncovered in the region were inscribed with the Oscan language.
The Oscan resisted Roman rule fiercely during the early Republic.
