impracticability

noun
quality of being impossible to use or implement
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpræktɪkəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpræktɪkəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to put into practice or to use effectively

Examples

The impracticability of plans
Due to its impracticability, the proposal was rejected by the committee.
Engineers cited the impracticability of using outdated technology for the new project.
The impracticability of the plan became clear after several failed attempts to implement it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impracticality - the state of being impractical; lacking practicality
  • antonym: practicability - the quality of being able to be done or put into practice
