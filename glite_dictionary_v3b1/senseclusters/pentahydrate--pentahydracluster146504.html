pentahydrate

noun
compound with five waters
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛntəˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound that contains five molecules of water of crystallization per formula unit

Examples

A pentahydrate compound
Copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate is bright blue in color and commonly used in chemistry labs.
The label indicated that the chemical was a pentahydrate, meaning it contained five water molecules.
When heated, a pentahydrate will lose its water of crystallization and change its appearance.
