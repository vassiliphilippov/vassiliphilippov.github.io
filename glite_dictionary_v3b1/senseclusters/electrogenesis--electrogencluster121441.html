electrogenesis

noun
generation of electrical impulses
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈdʒen.ɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈdʒen.ɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
the process by which electrical impulses or membrane potentials are generated in nerve, muscle, or other excitable cells

Examples

Neural electrogenesis
The study focused on how electrogenesis occurs in the neurons of the brain.
Disorders affecting electrogenesis can lead to serious heart rhythm problems.
Scientists are exploring artificial ways to stimulate electrogenesis in damaged muscle tissue.

  • synonym: depolarization - a change in a cell's membrane potential making it more positive
