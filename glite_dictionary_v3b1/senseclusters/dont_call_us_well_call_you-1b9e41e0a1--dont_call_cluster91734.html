don't call us, we'll call you

x
polite phrase to end communication, often after rejection
Pronunciation: / doʊnt kɔl ʌs, wil kɔl ju / (AmE), / dəʊnt kɔːl ʌs, wiːl kɔːl juː / (BrE)
used to tell someone that their application or request has been rejected, often as a way to end communication politely or dismissively

Examples

Don't call us, we'll call you after audition
After the audition, the director simply said, don't call us, we'll call you, leaving her feeling disappointed.
The company responded to all applicants with the standard, don't call us, we'll call you message by email.
When he finished the job interview, the manager ended with, don't call us, we'll call you, making the outcome clear.
