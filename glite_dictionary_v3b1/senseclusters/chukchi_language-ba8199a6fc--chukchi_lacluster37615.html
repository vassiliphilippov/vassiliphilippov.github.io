Chukchi language

noun
language spoken by the Chukchi people of Siberia
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃʌktʃi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌktʃi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an indigenous language of northeastern Siberia, spoken by the Chukchi people, notable for pronounced differences in male and female speech

Examples

Chukchi language spoken in Siberia
Few people outside Siberia study the Chukchi language due to its unique structure and limited resources.
Linguists are fascinated by how the Chukchi language differs in pronunciation between men and women.
The survival of the Chukchi language is vital for preserving the cultural identity of the Chukchi people.
