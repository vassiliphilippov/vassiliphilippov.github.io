subseries

noun
botanical taxonomic rank
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic rank in botany below a series, used to group closely related species within a genus

Examples

Botanical subseries
The botanist placed the rare orchid in a new subseries based on its unique characteristics.
Several species in the genus were grouped together under the same subseries for easier classification.
Her research focused on the evolutionary relationships within a subseries of flowering plants.

  • degree higher: series - a taxonomic rank above subseries in botanical classification
