hypoglossal

adjective
under the tongue
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpəˈɡlɑsəl / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈɡlɒsəl / (BrE)
relating to the area beneath the tongue, especially to the twelfth cranial nerve that controls tongue movements

Examples

Hypoglossal function test
The hypoglossal nerve is essential for proper speech and swallowing.
Damage to the hypoglossal nerve can result in difficulty moving the tongue.
Doctors examined the patient's hypoglossal function after the surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sublingual - situated or applied under the tongue
