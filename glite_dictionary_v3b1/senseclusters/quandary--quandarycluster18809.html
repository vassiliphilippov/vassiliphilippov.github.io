quandary

noun
state of uncertainty or perplexity
8.2 1.76/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkw…ëÀên.d…ô.ri /; / Ààkw…ëÀên.d…õ…π.i / (AmE), / Ààkw…ín.d(…ô).ri /; / Ààkw…ín.d…ôri / (BrE)
a state of uncertainty or confusion about what to do, especially in a difficult situation

Examples

In a quandary situation
After losing his job, Mark found himself in a real quandary about how to pay his bills.
She faced a moral quandary when asked to lie for her friend.
The committee was in a quandary over whether to approve the risky project.
After losing his passport abroad, he found himself in a real quandary.
She was in a quandary about whether to accept the new job or stay at her current one.
Their sudden silence left us in a quandary over how to proceed with the project.

  • irresolution
    8.0 0.20/m

  • uncertainty
    4.0 27/m

  • conundrum
