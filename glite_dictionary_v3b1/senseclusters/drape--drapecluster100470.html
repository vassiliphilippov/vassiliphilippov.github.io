drape

verb
arrange cloth or fabric in loose folds
Pronunciation: / dreɪp / (AmE), / dreɪp / (BrE)
to hang, arrange, or cover something loosely with cloth or material (including for decoration or in a way that resembles fabric)

Examples

To drape a cloth
A silk cloth was draped over the piano for the ceremony.
He likes to drape blankets over the couch to create a cozy look.
She carefully draped the scarf around her shoulders before going out.
A flag was draped over the coffin during the ceremony.
He likes to drape his coat over the back of the chair when he arrives home.
She carefully draped the table with a bright red cloth for the celebration.
A heavy blanket was draped across the foot of the bed.
She draped her jacket over the back of the chair when she arrived.
The cat draped itself across the windowsill, enjoying the sun.
