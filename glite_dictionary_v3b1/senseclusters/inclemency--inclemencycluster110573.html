inclemency

noun
severe or unpleasant weather
7.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈklɛmənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈklɛmənsi / (BrE)
(formal) the quality of being severe, rough, or unpleasant, especially referring to weather or climate.

Examples

Inclemency of weather
Because of the inclemency of the day, the picnic was postponed.
Flights were canceled due to the inclemency of the weather.
The game's organizers feared inclemency would keep crowds away.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clemency - mildness, especially of weather
