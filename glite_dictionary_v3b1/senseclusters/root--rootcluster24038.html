root

noun
solution of an equation
7.0
Pronunciation: / ruːt / (AmE), / ruːt / (BrE)
a value that satisfies an equation, especially a number that when multiplied by itself a certain number of times gives another number (mathematics)

Examples

Square root
Each quadratic equation has a maximum of two roots.
The root of the equation x² = 9 is 3 or -3.
To solve this problem, you need to find the root of the function.
The root of 9 is 3 because 3 times 3 equals 9.
The cube root of 8 is 2, since 2 multiplied by itself three times is 8.
You need to find the root of this equation to solve the problem.
