dosa

noun
Buddhist anger/aversion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈdəʊsə / (BrE)
in Buddhism, a mental state of aversion, anger, or ill will considered one of the three unwholesome roots

Examples

Overcome dosa feelings
Practicing mindfulness can help reduce dosa and promote a more peaceful mind.
Buddhist teachings warn that dosa leads to suffering and should be overcome.
He recognized his feelings of dosa and tried to respond with patience instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • synonym: ill will - a feeling of hostility or dislike
  • antonym: metta - loving-kindness; a positive mental state in Buddhism
