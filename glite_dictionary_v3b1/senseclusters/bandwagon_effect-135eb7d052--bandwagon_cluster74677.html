bandwagon effect

noun
the tendency to follow trends as they gain popularity
9.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbænd.wæɡ.ən ɪ.ˈfekt/ /; / /ˈbænd.wæɡ.ən ə.ˈfekt/ (AmE), /ˈbænd.wæɡ.ən ɪ.ˈfekt/ /; / /ˈbænd.wæɡ.ən ə.ˈfekt/ (BrE)
the tendency for people to adopt certain behaviors, styles, or attitudes simply because others are doing so, especially as a trend gains wider acceptance

Examples

The bandwagon effect
Analysts warned that the soaring stock prices might be driven more by the bandwagon effect than by company fundamentals.
During elections, early poll results can trigger a bandwagon effect, influencing how people decide to vote.
The viral video gained millions of views due to the bandwagon effect among social media users.

Related Terms

  • synonym: herd mentality - the tendency of people to conform to the behavior of others
  • synonym: peer pressure - influence from members of one's peer group
