Arafat

proper noun
Palestinian leader
Pronunciation: / ˈærəˌfæt / (AmE), / ˈærəˌfæt / (BrE)
Yasser Arafat (1929–2004), a Palestinian political leader who led the Palestine Liberation Organization and served as president of the Palestinian National Authority.

Examples

Yasser Arafat leader
Many people remember Arafat for his role in the peace process between Israelis and Palestinians.
Arafat was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1994 along with two Israeli leaders.
The international community often looked to Arafat for leadership during times of conflict in the Middle East.
