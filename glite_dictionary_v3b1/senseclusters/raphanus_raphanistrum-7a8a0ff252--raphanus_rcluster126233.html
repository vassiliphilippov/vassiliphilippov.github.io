Raphanus raphanistrum

noun
wild radish; Eurasian weed with podlike fruits
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈræf.ə.nəs ræf.əˈnɪs.trəm / (AmE), / ˈræf.ənəs ræf.əˈnɪs.trəm / (BrE)
A Eurasian wild plant of the mustard family, also known as wild radish, with yellow, white, or mauve flowers and podlike fruits, often considered a weed.

Examples

Raphanus raphanistrum wild plant
Raphanus raphanistrum is commonly found in agricultural fields where it competes with crops.

The flowers of Raphanus raphanistrum may be yellow, white, or lilac in color.
