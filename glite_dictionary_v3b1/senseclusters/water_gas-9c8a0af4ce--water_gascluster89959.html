water gas

noun
mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide gas
6.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər gæs/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər gæs/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə gæs/ (BrE)
a mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide, produced by passing steam over heated coal or coke, and used as a fuel or in chemical synthesis

Examples

Water gas mixture
By blowing steam over red-hot coke, engineers generated water gas rich in hydrogen and carbon monoxide.
Historically, water gas was used as a fuel for lighting and heating before the widespread use of natural gas.
The chemical plant produces water gas for industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coal gas - gaseous fuel made by the destructive distillation of coal
  • synonym: producer gas - a similar fuel gas containing nitrogen, produced by air and steam blowing over hot coal
