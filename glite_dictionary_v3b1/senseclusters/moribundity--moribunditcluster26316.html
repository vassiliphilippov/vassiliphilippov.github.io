moribundity

noun
state near death
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔːrɪˈbʌndɪti / (AmE), / ˌmɒrɪˈbʌndɪti / (BrE)
the state of being near death or in a condition of terminal decline

Examples

The moribundity of language
The moribundity of the ancient language was evident as fewer people spoke it each year.
Critics pointed to the moribundity of the company as a sign of inevitable closure.
The museum's exhibits reflected the moribundity of traditions that were once vibrant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - a gradual loss of strength or quality
  • antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active
