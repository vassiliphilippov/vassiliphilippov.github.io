Plinian eruption

noun
explosive volcanic eruption with high ash plume
Pronunciation: / ˈplɪniən ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ˈplɪniən ɪˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
A type of volcanic eruption characterized by the violent expulsion of gas and ash high into the atmosphere, often reaching several miles in height.

Examples

A Plinian eruption column
Scientists study ancient ash layers to understand the frequency of Plinian eruptions in this region.
The 1980 Mount St. Helens event was classified as a Plinian eruption due to its massive vertical ash column.
The force of a Plinian eruption can disrupt air travel thousands of miles away.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Strombolian eruption - a volcanic eruption marked by the bursting of gas bubbles
  • degree lower: Vulcanian eruption - a volcanic eruption that is more explosive than Strombolian but less than Plinian
