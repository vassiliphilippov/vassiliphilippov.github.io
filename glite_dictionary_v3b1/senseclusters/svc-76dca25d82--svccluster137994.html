SVC

noun
large upper vein
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs vi ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌɛs viː ˈsiː / (BrE)
the superior vena cava, the large vein that carries deoxygenated blood from the upper body to the heart's right atrium

Examples

Heart SVC vein
The doctor checked the SVC for any signs of blockage during the procedure.
A blood clot in the SVC can cause serious complications for the patient.
Imaging revealed that the SVC was compressed by a nearby tumor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inferior vena cava - the large vein that carries blood from the lower body to the heart
