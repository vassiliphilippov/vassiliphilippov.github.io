distractingly

adverb
in an attention-diverting way
5.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪŋ.liː/ /; / /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪŋ.lɪ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪŋ.liː/ /; / /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪŋ.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that makes it hard to focus or pay attention because something draws your attention away

Examples

Distractingly vivid
The bright colors on the screen were distractingly vivid during the presentation.
He tapped his pen distractingly while I was trying to concentrate on my work.
The smell from the kitchen was distractingly strong during the meeting.
The music was distractingly loud, making it difficult to concentrate on the conversation.
Her tie was distractingly colorful and drew everyone's attention during the meeting.
The lights on the billboard flashed distractingly as we drove past at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmly - in a way that is peaceful and does not disturb
  • synonym: disruptively - in a way that interrupts or disturbs
  • synonym: noticeably - in a way that is easily seen or noticed
  • antonym: subtly - in a way that is not obvious or easily noticed
