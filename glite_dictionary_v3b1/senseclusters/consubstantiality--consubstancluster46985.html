consubstantiality

noun
same substance doctrine
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑnsəbˌstænʃiˈæləti /; / ˌkɑːnsəbˌstænʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəbˌstænʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
the state of being of the same substance or essence, especially in religious, philosophical, or theological contexts

Examples

The consubstantiality doctrine
The doctrine of consubstantiality was a central issue at the Council of Nicaea.
Many theologians have written about the consubstantiality of the Son and the Father.
Debates over consubstantiality influenced the development of early Christian creeds.
The concept of consubstantiality is important in many theological debates.
Philosophers have discussed the consubstantiality of mind and body for centuries.
The church council affirmed the consubstantiality of the Son with the Father.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diversity - the state of being different or varied
  • antonym: heteroousios - the doctrine that Christ is of a different substance from God the Father
  • synonym: homoousios - the doctrine that Christ is of the same substance as God the Father
  • synonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
