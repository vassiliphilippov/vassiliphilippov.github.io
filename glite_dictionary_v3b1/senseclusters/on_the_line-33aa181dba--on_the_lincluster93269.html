on the line

x
at risk; in jeopardy
3.3
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə laɪn / (AmE), / ɒn ðə laɪn / (BrE)
at risk of being lost, harmed, or affected by the outcome of something

Examples

Put on the line
She put her career on the line by speaking out against the policy.
The CEO knew that his job was on the line if profits didn't improve.
With the championship on the line, the team played their best game of the season.
