high ground

noun
position of superiority or advantage
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / haɪ ˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a position of advantage or superiority, especially in a debate, competition, or moral situation

Examples

Gain the high ground
In negotiations, she always tries to gain the high ground by being well-prepared.
Politicians often claim the moral high ground to appeal to voters.
The company maintained the high ground during the controversy by acting transparently.
Politicians often strive for the high ground to appear more trustworthy to the public.
She refused to compromise her principles and maintained the high ground throughout the debate.
Taking the high ground in this dispute gave our company a reputation for integrity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position
  • antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable condition or circumstance that reduces chances of success or effectiveness
  • synonym: moral high ground - the feeling or claim of being morally superior
