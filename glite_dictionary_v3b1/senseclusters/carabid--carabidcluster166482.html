carabid

noun
ground beetle
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəbɪd / (AmE), / ˈkærəbɪd / (BrE)
a ground beetle belonging to the family Carabidae, typically found on the soil surface and often predatory

Examples

A carabid beetle
The scientist collected several carabids from the leaf litter for her research.
A shiny black carabid darted under a rock as we approached.
Farmers appreciate the presence of carabids because they help control pest insects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beetle - an insect of the order Coleoptera with hard wing cases
  • degree lower: ladybug - a small, round beetle often red with black spots
