hush money

noun
money paid to keep something secret
Pronunciation: / hʌʃ ˈmʌni / (AmE), / hʌʃ ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money given to someone to persuade them to keep something secret, especially to prevent the disclosure of damaging or embarrassing information.

Examples

Hush money payment
It is illegal to offer hush money to witnesses in a trial.
She refused to accept hush money and told the truth instead.
The politician was accused of paying hush money to cover up the scandal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blackmail - demanding payment or other benefits by threatening to reveal compromising information
  • synonym: bribe - money or gifts given to influence someone's actions dishonestly
