PT boat

noun
small fast torpedo boat
Pronunciation: / pi ti boʊt / (AmE), / piː tiː bəʊt / (BrE)
a small, fast, lightly armored military boat equipped with torpedoes, used chiefly by the U.S. Navy during World War II.

Examples

A PT boat attack
A PT boat could strike quickly and disappear, making it ideal for surprise attacks.
John read about the heroic missions of PT boats in the Pacific theater.
The museum displays a restored PT boat that served in World War II.
