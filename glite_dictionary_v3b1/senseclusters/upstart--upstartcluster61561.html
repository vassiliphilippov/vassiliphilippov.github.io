upstart

noun
new company challenging incumbents
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈʌp.stɑːrt/ (AmE), /ˈʌp.stɑːt/ (BrE)
a newly founded company or organization that quickly grows or challenges established competitors, often seen as bold or disruptive.

Examples

Upstart company
The upstart company disrupted the industry with an innovative subscription service.
Investors bet on the young upstart after its platform attracted millions of users.
Many incumbents dismissed the upstart, only to face rapid market share losses.

Related Terms
