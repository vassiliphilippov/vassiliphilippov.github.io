hedonism

noun
belief that pleasure is the highest good
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːdəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhiːdənɪzəm /; / ˈhiːdənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief or philosophy that pleasure or happiness is the most important goal in life

Examples

Hedonism philosophy
Her philosophy of hedonism led her to seek out new experiences and delights wherever she traveled.
Some critics argue that modern society encourages hedonism at the expense of deeper values.
The ancient Greeks debated whether hedonism could truly lead to a meaningful life.
He rejected hedonism in favor of a more disciplined lifestyle.
Some philosophers argue that hedonism fails to provide a solid foundation for morality.
The culture of ancient Rome is often associated with decadence and hedonism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asceticism - the practice of severe self-discipline and abstention from pleasure, often for religious reasons
