overhear

verb
hear without the speakers' knowledge
1.0 1.4
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈhɪr/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ˈiːr/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈhɪə/ (BrE)
to hear what someone is saying without their knowledge, usually by accident

Examples

To overhear a conversation
I happened to overhear my colleagues talking about the new project.
She was embarrassed when she realized I had overheard their conversation.
While waiting outside, he overheard a secret that changed everything.
