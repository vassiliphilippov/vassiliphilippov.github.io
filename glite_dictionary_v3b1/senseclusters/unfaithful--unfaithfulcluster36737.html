unfaithful

adjective
not true to the facts or original
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
not true to the facts or original; not accurately representing the original or not trustworthy

Examples

Unfaithful copy
The movie was criticized as an unfaithful adaptation of the novel.
Their copy was an unfaithful reproduction, missing many important details.
This translation is rather unfaithful to the original text.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faithful - accurately representing the original; trustworthy
