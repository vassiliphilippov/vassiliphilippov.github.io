resign

verb
accept something as inevitable

to accept something unpleasant or inevitable; to submit oneself to a situation without resistance, often used reflexively as 'resign oneself to'

Examples

To resign oneself
After months of searching for a new job without success, he finally resigned himself to his current position.
The team had to resign themselves to defeat after the opposing side scored the winning goal.
Unable to change the outcome, she resigned herself to living in the city.

Synonyms

  • accept
    3.5 60/m
    accept is used to tolerate or agree to a situation calmly, while resign means to submit oneself to something unpleasant.
  • reconcile
    5.8 2/m
    reconcile means come to terms and find peace with a problem, while resign means to accept it without resisting.
  • acquiesce
    8.4 2/m
    acquiesce means agree quietly to a proposal or demand, while resign means accept an unpleasant situation without resisting.
  • give in
    3.0 8/m
    give in means stop resisting and agree after pressure, while resign means accept something as inevitable without protest.
  • submit
    4.1 13/m
    submit means yield to authority or control, often under pressure, while resign means accept a situation personally and without fighting.
