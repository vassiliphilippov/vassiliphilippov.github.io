resign

verb
accept something as inevitable
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪn / (BrE)
to accept something unpleasant or inevitable; to submit oneself to a situation without resistance, often used reflexively as 'resign oneself to'

Examples

To resign oneself
After months of searching for a new job without success, he finally resigned himself to his current position.
The team had to resign themselves to defeat after the opposing side scored the winning goal.
Unable to change the outcome, she resigned herself to living in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of something; to oppose
  • synonym: submit - to accept or yield to a superior force or to the authority or will of another person
