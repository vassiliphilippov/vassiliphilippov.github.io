Capoten

noun
brand name for blood pressure/heart failure medicine
7.2
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈpəʊ.tən/ /; / /kə.ˈpoʊ.tən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈpəʊ.tən/ (BrE)
A brand name for a drug (captopril) used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure by blocking the formation of angiotensin, resulting in vasodilation.

Examples

Capoten medication
After being diagnosed with hypertension, her doctor prescribed Capoten to help lower her blood pressure.
Some patients experience dizziness when starting Capoten, especially after the first dose.
The cardiologist discussed the benefits and side effects of Capoten with the patient before starting treatment.
