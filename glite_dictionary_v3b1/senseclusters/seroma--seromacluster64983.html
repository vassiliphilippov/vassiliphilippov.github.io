seroma

noun
fluid pocket after surgery
Pronunciation: / sɪˈroʊmə / (AmE), / sɪˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
a pocket of clear fluid that collects under the skin after surgery or injury

Examples

A seroma after surgery
After her surgery, a seroma developed near the incision site and needed to be monitored.
The nurse explained that a seroma is a common complication after certain medical procedures.
Doctors often use a needle to drain a seroma to speed up healing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: edema - swelling caused by excess fluid in body tissues
  • antonym: hematoma - a swelling filled with blood, usually caused by injury
