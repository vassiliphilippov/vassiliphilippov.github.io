patroonship

noun
patroon's estate or office
Pronunciation: / pəˈtruːnʃɪp / (AmE), / pəˈtruːnʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, authority, or estate of a patroon, especially in Dutch colonial America

Examples

A patroonship estate
The Dutch granted a patroonship to encourage settlement along the Hudson River.
Under the patroonship system, landowners held vast estates and controlled local governance.
Many early settlers worked on a patroonship in exchange for a share of the crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manor - a large country house with lands; the principal house of a landed estate
  • antonym: tenant - a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord
