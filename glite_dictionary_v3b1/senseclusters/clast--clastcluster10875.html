clast

noun
rock fragment in clastic rock (geology)
Pronunciation: / klæst / (AmE), / klɑːst / (BrE)
(Geology) a fragment of rock that forms part of a clastic rock, such as sediment or gravel.

Examples

A clast fragment
Each clast in the conglomerate reveals information about the source rock.
Geologists analyze the shape of a clast to infer its transportation history.
The sandstone is composed of clasts of varying sizes cemented together.
