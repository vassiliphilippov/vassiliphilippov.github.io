suffer

verb
become worse in quality or value
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfər / (AmE), / ˈsʌfə / (BrE)
to become worse in quality, value, or condition; to be negatively affected

Examples

Suffer a loss
His grades suffered after he started working longer hours.
Our team's performance will suffer if we lose our best player.
Restaurants often suffer during economic downturns.
Her argument suffers when important facts are omitted from the discussion.
The delicate flavor of the tea suffers if the water is too hot.
The poet's work suffers in translation, losing much of its original beauty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deteriorate - to become worse in quality or value
  • antonym: improve - to become better in quality or condition
  • synonym: lose - fail to retain or have something, or to be deprived of a quality or advantage
