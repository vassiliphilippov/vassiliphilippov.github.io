wean

verb
gradually detach affection or dependence
5.9 1.26/m
Pronunciation: / win / (AmE), / wiÀên / (BrE)
to gradually detach someone's affection or dependence from something or someone to which they have become attached

Examples

To wean off habits
After years of devotion, it was hard to wean him from his old habits.
Parents often struggle to wean their children from their dependence on television.
She tried to wean herself off her attachment to social media.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accustom - to make someone used to something, often by gradual exposure
  • synonym: detach - to separate or disengage something
