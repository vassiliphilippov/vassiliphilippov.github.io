pseudodementia

noun
depression-related memory loss
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊdɪˈmɛnʃə / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊdɪˈmɛnʃə / (BrE)
a condition in which a person shows dementia-like symptoms that are actually caused by another disorder, often depression

Examples

Pseudodementia symptoms
The doctor explained that pseudodementia can often be reversed if the underlying depression is treated.
Unlike true dementia, pseudodementia may improve significantly with appropriate therapy.
Family members were relieved to learn the memory problems were due to pseudodementia and not permanent brain damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dementia - a decline in mental ability severe enough to interfere with daily life
  • synonym: depression - a mental health disorder causing persistent sadness and loss of interest
