corollary

noun
natural consequence or result
8.2 1.81/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îÀêr…ôÀåleri / (AmE), / k…ôÀàr…íl…ôri / (BrE)
A result or effect that follows naturally from something else; a logical or practical consequence.

Examples

A natural corollary
A rise in energy prices is often a corollary of increasing international demand.
In economic theory, a corollary to market competition is the reduction of prices for consumers.
One corollary of the new law is that fewer people will qualify for assistance.

Synonyms

  • consequent
    7.1 0.04/m
    consequent is a direct result of a premise or cause, while corollary is a conclusion drawn as a logical follow-up.

  • synonym: result - something that happens as a consequence, outcome, or effect
