macrospore

noun
large spore developing into female gametophyte
Pronunciation: / ˈmækroʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈmæk.rəʊ.spɔː / (BrE)
the larger of the two types of spore produced by heterosporous plants, which develops in the ovule into a female gametophyte

Examples

The macrospore develops
In seed plants, the macrospore develops into the female gametophyte, which produces eggs.
Research into fern reproduction often focuses on how the macrospore matures within the ovule.
Unlike the microspore, the macrospore is much larger and gives rise to female reproductive structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microspore - the smaller of the two spores in heterosporous plants, developing into a male gametophyte
