oligopsonistic

adjective
few buyers control market
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːlɪgɑpˌsoʊˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪgɒpˌsəʊˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to a market in which a small number of buyers have significant control over purchases

Examples

An oligopsonistic market
The coffee industry is often described as oligopsonistic because a few companies buy most of the beans.
Farmers face challenges in oligopsonistic markets where only a handful of buyers set the prices.
An oligopsonistic structure can limit sellers' bargaining power in agricultural sectors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: competitive - relating to a market with many buyers and sellers
  • degree lower: monopsonistic - relating to a market with only one buyer
