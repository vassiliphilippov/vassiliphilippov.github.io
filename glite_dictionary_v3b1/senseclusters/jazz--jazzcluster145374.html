jazz

verb
make more lively or stylish
4.6
Pronunciation: /dʒæz/ (AmE), /dʒæz/ (BrE)
to make something more lively, attractive, or exciting, often by adding energy, style, or decorations

Examples

To jazz up a room
They jazz the conference this year by adding a surprise performance.
The organizers jazzed the keynote speech with video highlights and upbeat music.
She is jazzing the presentation slides with brighter colors and playful icons.
They jazzed up the living room with new cushions and bright curtains.
The chef jazzed the salad by adding colorful vegetables and a zesty dressing.
She jazzes her outfits with bold accessories to stand out at events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brighten - to make something more cheerful or lively
  • antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; opposite of lively
  • synonym: liven (up) - to make something more energetic or interesting
  • synonym: spice up - to make something more exciting or appealing
