motion with variable acceleration

noun
non-uniformly accelerated motion
3.1 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.ʃən wɪð ˈver.i.ə.bəl æk.sə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈmoʊ.ʃən wɪθ ˈvær.i.ə.bəl ək.sə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.ʃən wɪð ˈveə.ri.ə.bəl æk.sə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈməʊ.ʃən wɪθ ˈveə.ri.ə.bəl ək.sə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
motion in which the acceleration is not constant, typically because the force acting on the object changes over time

Examples

Motion with variable acceleration in physics
During freefall with air resistance, the object experiences motion with variable acceleration.
Rotating objects attached to springs often demonstrate motion with variable acceleration as the applied torque changes.
The car's speed increased irregularly, showing motion with variable acceleration due to the shifting engine force.
