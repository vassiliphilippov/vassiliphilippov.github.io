homolog

noun
related chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊməˌlɔɡ /; / ˈhɑːməˌloʊɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɒməlɒɡ / (BrE)
a compound belonging to a homologous series, differing from others in the series by a repeating structural unit, often CH2.

Examples

Alkane homolog
Propane is a homolog of ethane, differing by one CH2 group in the alkane series.
Scientists studied each homolog in the series to understand their chemical properties.
Butanol is a homolog of propanol, with an additional CH2 group in its structure.
Each homologue in the series has a similar structure but a different chain length.
Methanol and ethanol are homologues because they differ by a CH2 group.
The scientist studied the properties of each homologue to understand the series better.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analogue - a compound with a structure similar to another but differing in a certain component
