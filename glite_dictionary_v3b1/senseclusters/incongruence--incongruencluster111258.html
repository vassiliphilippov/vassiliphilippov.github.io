incongruence

noun
lack of congruence
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɑŋɡruəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒŋɡruəns / (BrE)
the state of not being congruent or not matching, especially in mathematics such as geometry or modular arithmetic

Examples

Mathematical incongruence
The incongruence between the two angles meant the triangles could not be considered identical.
Mathematicians study incongruence to understand when numbers do not share the same remainder.
There was clear incongruence in the measurements, so the figures could not be matched.

Related Terms

  • antonym: congruence - the state of being congruent or matching in shape or value
  • synonym: incongruity - the state of being out of place or not matching
