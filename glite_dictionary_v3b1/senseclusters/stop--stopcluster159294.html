stop

verb
bring something to an end
Pronunciation: / stɑp / (AmE), / stɒp / (BrE)
to cause something to end, cease, or not continue; to prevent or block something from happening or moving

Examples

To stop the noise
He asked her to stop playing the piano after 10 p.m.
The coach told the players to stop when the whistle blew.
You have to stop worrying about things you can't control.
If you’re not sure what to do next, just stop and wait for instructions.
Sometimes it’s best to stop and see how the situation develops before acting.
The meeting was interrupted when the manager asked everyone to stop and listen.
Heavy snow can stop the mountain pass for days at a time.
Police tried to stop the entrance to the stadium with barricades.
Protesters managed to stop the main highway by blocking it with vehicles.
