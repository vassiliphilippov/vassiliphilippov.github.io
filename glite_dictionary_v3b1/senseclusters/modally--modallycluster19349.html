modally

adverb
in modal style
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊdəli / (AmE), / ˈməʊdəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to musical modes, especially by using modal scales or harmony instead of tonal harmony

Examples

Modally structured music
The composer structured the piece modally, giving it a unique and ancient sound.
Many folk songs are performed modally rather than following traditional major or minor keys.
The band experimented modally to create a different atmosphere in their latest album.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tonally - in a way that relates to tonal harmony or key centers
