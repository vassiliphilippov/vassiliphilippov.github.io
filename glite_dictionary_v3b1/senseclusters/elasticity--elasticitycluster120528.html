elasticity

noun
responsiveness to change
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlæˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / ɪˌlæˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
the degree to which something changes in response to another change, especially the responsiveness of demand or supply to changes in price in economics

Examples

Price elasticity
The elasticity of demand determines how much sales will drop if prices increase.
Economists often analyze the elasticity of supply to predict market reactions.
Rubber bands are known for their high elasticity, allowing them to stretch and return to shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt easily
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not changing easily
