overdo

verb
do something excessively
3.6 2.28/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàdu / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàduÀê / (BrE)
to do something to an excessive or extreme degree, often more than is necessary or advisable

Examples

To overdo exercise
If you overdo it at the gym, you might feel sore the next day.
She tends to overdo her makeup for formal events.
Try not to overdo the spices when cooking this dish.

Synonyms

  • go overboard
    4.4 1.57/m
    go overboard means doing too much in actions or plans, while overdo means doing something to an excessive, unnecessary degree.
  • go too far
    4.2 7.21/m
    go too far often refers to speech or behavior that crosses limits, while overdo focuses on doing things excessively.
  • exaggerate
    3.1 16/m
    exaggerate means making a story, fact, or feeling seem larger than it is, while overdo means acting to an excessive degree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as greater than it really is
  • antonym: underdo - to do something to a lesser degree than necessary
