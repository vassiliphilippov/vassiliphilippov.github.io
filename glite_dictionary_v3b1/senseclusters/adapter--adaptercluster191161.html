adapter

noun
short DNA/RNA sequence for sequencing
6.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdæp.tər/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdæp.tə/ (BrE)
(in molecular biology and DNA sequencing) a short DNA or RNA sequence that is attached to DNA fragments so they can be amplified, indexed, and bound to a sequencing platform during library preparation.

Examples

DNA adapter
The lab technician ligated sequencing adapters to the DNA fragments before PCR amplification.
To identify samples, the team added unique barcode adapters during library preparation.
She explained that a damaged adapter can prevent fragments from binding to the flow cell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: linker - a short sequence used to join or connect DNA fragments; often synonymous with adapter in sequencing contexts
  • synonym: oligonucleotide - a short DNA or RNA molecule used in molecular biology procedures
