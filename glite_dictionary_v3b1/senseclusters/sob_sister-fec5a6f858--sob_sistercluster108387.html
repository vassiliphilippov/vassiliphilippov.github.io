sob sister

noun
journalist writing sentimental stories
6.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / sɑb ˈsɪstər / (AmE), / sɒb ˈsɪstə / (BrE)
A journalist, traditionally a woman, who specializes in writing sentimental or emotionally charged stories, especially for newspapers.

Examples

sob sister journalist
Although the term sob sister is considered outdated, it highlights the gendered nature of early journalism.
In the 1920s, every major newspaper had at least one sob sister covering the big trials.
The accused hoped the sob sister would write a sympathetic piece about his troubled past.
