large-mouthed

adjective
having a relatively large mouth
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑrdʒ ˌmaʊθt / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ ˌmaʊθt / (BrE)
Having a mouth that is relatively large in proportion to the rest of the body or face.

Examples

A large-mouthed grin
Her pet had a large-mouthed grin that made it look as if it were smiling all day.
Several large-mouthed frogs were found near the pond during the survey.
The large-mouthed bass is a popular freshwater game fish in North America.
