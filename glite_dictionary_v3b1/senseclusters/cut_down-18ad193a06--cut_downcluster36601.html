cut down

verb
to fell or shorten by cutting
1.0 1.7
Pronunciation: /kʌt daʊn/ (AmE), /kʌt daʊn/ (BrE)
to make a tree or plant fall by cutting it at the base

Examples

To cut down a tree
Farmers often cut down corn stalks after harvest to clear the land.
It took hours to cut down all the tall grass in the field.
The workers will cut down the old oak tree tomorrow.
Many trees were cut down to make way for the new road.
The champion was cut down in the final round by a powerful punch.
The lumberjack cut down the old oak tree in less than an hour.
Before building the house, they had to cut down several large oaks on the land.
Farmers often cut down sugarcane with machetes during the harvest season.
The workers used chainsaws to cut down the old trees along the road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fell - to cut down (a tree)
  • degree lower: plant - living organism that is cut down with this action
  • antonym: raise - to lift or set upright; to cause to stand up
