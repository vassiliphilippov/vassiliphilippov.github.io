hemistich

noun
half-line of verse
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛmɪˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛmɪstɪk / (BrE)
half of a line of verse in poetry, often separated by a pause or caesura

Examples

A hemistich in poetry
In ancient Greek poetry, each hemistich was separated by a strong pause.
The poet skillfully balanced the two hemistichs in every line of the epic.
A caesura often divides a line into two hemistichs for dramatic effect.
In classical poetry, a hemistich often appears on either side of a caesura.
The playwright used a hemistich to create a dramatic pause in the dialogue.
Students were asked to identify the hemistich in each line of the poem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caesura - a pause in a line of poetry, often dividing it into hemistichs
  • degree higher: verse - a single line of poetry
