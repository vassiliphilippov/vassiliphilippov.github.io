drifty

adjective
moving off course
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪfti / (AmE), / ˈdrɪfti / (BrE)
tending to move off course or be carried away by wind, current, or lack of control

Examples

Drifty boat
The small boat felt oddly drifty in the current, slowly sliding away from the shore.
On icy roads, the car became drifty and hard to control.
Her thoughts grew drifty as she stared out the window during the long meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed, not moving off course
  • synonym: unstable - likely to change or move unexpectedly
