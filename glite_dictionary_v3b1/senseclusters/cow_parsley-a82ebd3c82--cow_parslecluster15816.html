cow parsley

noun
tall weed with white flowers in the carrot family
8.7 7.2
Pronunciation: /kaʊ ˈpɑːr.sliː/ /; / /kaʊ ˈpɑːr.slɪ/ (AmE), /kaʊ ˈpɑːs.lɪ/ /; / /kaʊ ˈpɑːs.liː/ (BrE)
a tall, white-flowered biennial plant of the carrot family, native to Europe and Asia and often found growing wild in fields and along roadsides; sometimes regarded as a weed.

Examples

Cow parsley plant
Cow parsley grows in abundance along the rural hedgerows each spring.
Some gardeners consider cow parsley a weed and remove it from their lawns.
The delicate white flowers of cow parsley sway in the breeze by the roadside.
