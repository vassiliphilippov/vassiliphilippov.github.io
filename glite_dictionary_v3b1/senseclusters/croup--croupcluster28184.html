croup

noun
respiratory disease in young children
Pronunciation: / kruːp / (AmE), / kruːp / (BrE)
A disease of infants and young children characterized by a harsh cough, hoarseness, fever, and difficulty breathing due to inflammation of the air passages.

Examples

A child with croup
During the winter, cases of croup often increase among infants and toddlers.
Parents should seek medical attention if their child with croup has trouble breathing or stridor.
The doctor diagnosed the child with croup after hearing his barking cough and examining his throat.
