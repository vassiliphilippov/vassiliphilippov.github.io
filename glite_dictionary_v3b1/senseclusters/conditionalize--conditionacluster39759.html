conditionalize

verb
to make conditional
8.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to express or restate something in the form of a conditional statement, often using an if-then structure

Examples

To conditionalize a statement
The instructor asked the students to conditionalize their statements using if-then clauses.
Researchers often conditionalize their findings to account for different variables.
You should conditionalize your argument to show it only applies under certain circumstances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generalize - to make a broad statement based on specific cases
  • synonym: hypothesize - to suggest or form a possible explanation or theory
