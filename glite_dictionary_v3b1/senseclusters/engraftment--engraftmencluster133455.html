engraftment

noun
transplant growth process
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡræftmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrɑːftmənt / (BrE)
the process by which transplanted cells or tissue start to grow and function in the recipient's body

Examples

Bone marrow engraftment
After the bone marrow transplant, doctors monitored the patient's engraftment closely.
Successful engraftment is essential for the recovery of patients receiving stem cell transplants.
The speed of engraftment can affect the overall outcome of the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rejection - the process by which a recipient's body does not accept transplanted tissue
  • synonym: transplantation - the act of transferring cells, tissue, or organs from one body to another
