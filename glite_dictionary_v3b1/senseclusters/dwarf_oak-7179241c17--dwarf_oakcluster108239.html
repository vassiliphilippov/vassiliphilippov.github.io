dwarf oak

noun
a small shrubby oak tree of North America
3.9 6.1
Pronunciation: /dwɔːrf oʊk/ (AmE), /dwɔːf əʊk/ (BrE)
A small, deciduous shrubby oak of northeastern and central United States, known for its sweet edible nuts and dense thickets.

Examples

The dwarf oak tree

The dense thickets of dwarf oak provide shelter for numerous small animals.
