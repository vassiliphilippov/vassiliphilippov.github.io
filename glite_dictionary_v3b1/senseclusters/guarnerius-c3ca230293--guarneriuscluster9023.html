Guarnerius

proper noun
famous Italian violin maker or family
Pronunciation: / ɡwɑrˈnɛriəs / (AmE), / ɡwɑːˈnɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A member of a famous Italian family of violin makers, especially Giuseppe Guarneri; Guarnerius violins are highly prized by musicians.

Examples

Guarnerius family member
A Guarnerius from the 18th century can fetch millions at an auction.
Collectors value a Guarnerius almost as highly as a Stradivarius due to their exceptional sound.
The violinist played a rare Guarnerius that was over 200 years old.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Amati - another renowned family of Italian violin makers
  • synonym: Stradivarius - violins made by Antonio Stradivari, also highly prized
