dynasty

noun
powerful family ruling line
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪnəsti / (AmE), / ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
A series of rulers or leaders from the same family, especially one that maintains power for several generations.

Examples

A dynasty family
A new dynasty emerged after the old royal family was overthrown.
The Ming dynasty ruled China for nearly three centuries.
The company has been controlled by the same dynasty for generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monarchy - a form of government with a monarch at the head
