liberatory

adjective
causing release or freedom
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbərəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərətəri / (BrE)
causing or intended to bring about freedom or liberation from constraints or oppression

Examples

A liberatory effect
The novel had a liberatory effect on readers who felt trapped by tradition.
Many activists see education as a liberatory force in society.
Her liberatory journey began when she decided to speak openly about her experiences.
The activists organized a liberatory campaign to challenge unfair laws.
Her teaching methods are considered liberatory because they encourage independent thinking.
The new policy had a liberatory impact on the rights of the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cathartic - providing psychological relief through the open expression of strong emotions
  • synonym: emancipatory - intended to free people from social or political restrictions
  • antonym: oppressive - causing someone to feel weighed down or restricted
