antifouling

noun
marine protective coating
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈfaʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈfaʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
a paint, coating, or method used to prevent the growth of marine organisms like barnacles and algae on underwater surfaces

Examples

A layer of antifouling
Before launching the yacht, they applied a new layer of antifouling to the hull.
Regular use of antifouling helps prevent barnacles from attaching to the boat's bottom.
The marina recommends checking your antifouling every year for maximum protection.
The shipyard applied a new antifouling coating to the vessel's hull before launching it.
Regular antifouling treatments help maintain a boat's speed and fuel efficiency.
Advances in antifouling technology have reduced the need for frequent underwater cleaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cleaning - the act of removing dirt or unwanted substances
  • antonym: fouling - the accumulation of unwanted material on solid surfaces
  • synonym: paint - a colored liquid applied to surfaces for protection or decoration
  • synonym: protective coating - a layer applied to surfaces to guard against damage or contamination
