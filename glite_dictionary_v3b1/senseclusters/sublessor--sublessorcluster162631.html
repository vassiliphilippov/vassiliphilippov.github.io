sublessor

noun
person who sublets
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈlɛsər / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈlɛsə / (BrE)
a person who leases property to someone else after renting it from the original owner

Examples

The sublessor collects rent
The sublessor is responsible for collecting rent from the subtenant each month.
Before moving in, the subtenant signed an agreement with the sublessor.
A sublessor must follow the terms of the original lease when subletting the property.

Related Terms

  • antonym: landlord - a person who owns and rents out property
  • antonym: subtenant - a person who rents property from a sublessor
