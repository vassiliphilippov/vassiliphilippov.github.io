generic

noun
unbranded product, especially a drug
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛrɪk / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛrɪk /; / dʒɪˈnɛrɪk / (BrE)
a product, especially a drug or other consumer good, sold without a brand name, often as a cheaper alternative to branded products

Examples

A generic product
Consumers are often surprised by how effective a generic can be compared to its branded counterpart.
Many people opt for the generic because it offers the same quality at a lower price.
The pharmacy stocks both generic and brand-name medications for each prescription.
He preferred a labeled varietal over the cheaper generic.
Some European producers market surplus grapes as generic rather than designated wine.
The restaurant served only generic with its lunch specials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brand-name - a product sold under a well-known brand name
  • antonym: proprietary - an item manufactured and sold under the name of a particular company
  • synonym: table wine - wine of ordinary quality not labeled by variety or region
