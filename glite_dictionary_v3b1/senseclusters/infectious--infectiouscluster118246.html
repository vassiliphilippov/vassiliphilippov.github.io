infectious

adjective
easily spread; contagious
4.0 1.79/m

(of a disease or emotion) able to be passed easily from one person to another by infection or through influence.

Examples

Infectious laughter
His enthusiasm was so infectious that everyone joined in the project with excitement.
Laughter can be infectious in a room full of friends.
The flu is highly infectious and spreads quickly in crowded places.

Synonyms

    3.1 4.46/m
    contagious is easily spread as a disease or feeling to people nearby, while infectious also means passed by influence or infection.
  • catching
    3.5 0.00/m
    catching sounds informal and shows a disease spreads easily between people, while infectious can also mean an emotion spreads between people.
  • communicable
    4.2 0.46/m
    communicable is a formal medical word for diseases you can pass to others, while infectious also covers feelings or influence.
  • infective
    7.4 0.17/m
    infective refers to something that causes or spreads infection in the body, while infectious can also describe a spreading emotion.
  • transmissible
    5.3 0.21/m
    transmissible is a formal term for conditions passed between people or animals, while infectious also describes feelings spreading between people.
  • transmittable
    3.9 0.09/m
    transmittable means able to be passed from one place or person to another, while infectious often adds the idea of emotional spread.
