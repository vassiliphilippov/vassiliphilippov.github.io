trichroism

noun
showing three colors in crystals
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtraɪ.kroʊ.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈtraɪ.krəʊ.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The property of certain crystals to show three different colors when observed from three different directions.

Examples

Trichroism in crystals
Scientists use polarized light to study trichroism in tourmaline crystals.
The mineral exhibited striking trichroism, changing from blue to green to yellow depending on the viewing angle.
Understanding trichroism helps gemologists identify certain gemstones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pleochroism - showing two or more colors when viewed from different angles (in crystals)
