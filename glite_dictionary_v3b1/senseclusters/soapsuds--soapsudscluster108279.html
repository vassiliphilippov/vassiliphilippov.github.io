soapsuds

noun
froth of bubbles from soap or detergent
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊpˌsʌdz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌsʌdz / (BrE)
the mass of small bubbles that forms on the surface of water when soap or detergent is mixed with it and agitated

Examples

White soapsuds
Her hands were covered in soapsuds as she washed the dishes.
The children played in the soapsuds that overflowed the bathtub.
Too much detergent created a mountain of soapsuds in the washing machine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed in or on a liquid
  • synonym: lather - foam produced by soap and water
