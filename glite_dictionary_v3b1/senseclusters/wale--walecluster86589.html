wale

noun
plank or ridge on a wooden ship's side
Pronunciation: / weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
A thick plank or band running lengthwise along the side of a wooden ship, serving as a structural reinforcement or protection.

Examples

Ship's wale
A thick wale protected the ship from scrapes against the dock.
Shipbuilders repaired the damaged wale to strengthen the hull before setting sail again.
The old sailing vessel still had its original wale intact along the starboard side.
