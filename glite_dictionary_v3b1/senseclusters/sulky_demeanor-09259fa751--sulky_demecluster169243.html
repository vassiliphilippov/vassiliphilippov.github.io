sulky demeanor

noun
gloomy or moody attitude
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌlki diˈmiːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌlki dɪˈmiːnə / (BrE)
a gloomy or moody attitude, typically shown through facial expression or body language

Examples

Sulky demeanor after loss
Despite winning second place, his sulky demeanor made it clear he wasn't satisfied.
She sat in the corner with a sulky demeanor, refusing to join the conversation.
The manager noticed the intern's sulky demeanor after being assigned extra work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sulky - moody or sullen, typically due to disappointment or annoyance
