postmodernity

noun
era after modernity
9.3
Pronunciation: /poʊst.mə.ˈdɜːr.nə.tɪ/ /; / /poʊst.mɑː.ˈdɜːr.nə.tɪ/ (AmE), /pəʊst.mə.ˈdɜː.nə.tɪ/ /; / /pəʊst.mɒ.ˈdɜː.nɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the period or condition after modernity, marked by skepticism toward traditional ideas, fragmented values, and changing cultural identities

Examples

Rise of postmodernity
The rise of postmodernity changed how people viewed art, culture, and history.
Debates about truth and reality are central to discussions of postmodernity in philosophy.
Many universities offer courses that examine the impact of postmodernity on society.
Many scholars debate when postmodernity began and how it differs from modernity.
The rise of digital media is often seen as a key feature of postmodernity.
In postmodernity, traditional social roles and identities have become more fluid and diverse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernity - the period or quality of being modern, often before postmodernity
  • synonym: postmodernism - a movement or attitude rejecting traditional ideas and embracing diversity
