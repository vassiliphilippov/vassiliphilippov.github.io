take form

verb
develop into a clear or distinct entity
2.8
Pronunciation: / teɪk fɔrm / (AmE), / teɪk fɔːm / (BrE)
to start to develop or become clear and recognizable in shape, character, or existence

Examples

To take form clearly
Over time, the sculpture began to take form before our eyes.
The vague idea for a novel started to take form in her mind.
Their business proposal gradually took form as they discussed their goals.
After months of planning, the new community center finally began to take form.
Her novel ideas started to take form as she outlined her chapters.
The painter watched the image take form on the canvas with each new brushstroke.

  • synonym: materialize - to become actual or real; to take physical form
