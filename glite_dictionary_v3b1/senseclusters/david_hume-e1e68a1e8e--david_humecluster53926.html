David Hume

proper noun
Scottish philosopher (1711–1776)
Pronunciation: / ˌdeɪvɪd ˈhjuːm / (AmE), / ˌdeɪvɪd ˈhjuːm / (BrE)
(1711–1776) A Scottish philosopher known for his influential work on skepticism, empiricism, and the limits of human knowledge.

Examples

David Hume philosopher
David Hume is often cited as a founder of modern empiricism in philosophy.
In his writings, David Hume questioned the existence of causality as we intuitively understand it.
Many scholars have debated David Hume's views on the limitations of human reason.
