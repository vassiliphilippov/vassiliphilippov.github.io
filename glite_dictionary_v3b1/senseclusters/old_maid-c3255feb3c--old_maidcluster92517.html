old maid

noun
elderly unmarried woman (outdated, sometimes derogatory)
Pronunciation: / oʊld meɪd / (AmE), / əʊld meɪd / (BrE)
an older woman who has never married, often with a negative or old-fashioned meaning

Examples

An old maid woman
In Victorian times, a woman over thirty was often labeled an old maid if she was not married.
She feared being called an old maid by her relatives, who pressured her to settle down.
The term old maid is rarely used today and is considered outdated and offensive by many.
In Victorian times, being called an old maid was seen as a serious insult.
Many considered her an old maid simply because she chose not to marry.
The term old maid is now widely regarded as outdated and offensive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bachelor - an unmarried man
  • synonym: spinster - an older unmarried woman, especially one regarded as past marriageable age
