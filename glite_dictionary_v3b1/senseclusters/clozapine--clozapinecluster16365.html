clozapine

noun
antipsychotic drug for schizophrenia
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊzəˌpin / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzəpiːn / (BrE)
an antipsychotic drug used as a sedative and prescribed for treatment-resistant schizophrenia, known for having relatively few side effects

Examples

clozapine medication
After switching to clozapine, her symptoms improved significantly with minimal side effects.
Patients who do not respond to other medications may benefit from clozapine for their schizophrenia.
The doctor carefully monitored his blood count while he was taking clozapine.
