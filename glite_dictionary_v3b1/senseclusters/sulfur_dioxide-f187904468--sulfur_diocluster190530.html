sulfur dioxide

noun
toxic gas, cause of acid rain
3.8 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌl.fər daɪ.ˈɑːk.saɪd/ /; / /ˈsʌl.fər daɪ.ˈɔːk.saɪd/ (AmE), /ˈsʌl.fə daɪ.ˈɒk.saɪd/ (BrE)
a colorless, toxic gas (SO2) with a pungent odor, produced naturally by volcanoes and used in industry; a major contributor to air pollution and acid rain

Examples

Sulfur dioxide gas
Sulfur dioxide is released during the combustion of fossil fuels such as coal and oil.
Exposure to high levels of sulfur dioxide can cause respiratory problems.
The eruption spewed ash and sulfur dioxide into the atmosphere, affecting air quality for weeks.
High levels of sulphur dioxide in the air can trigger respiratory problems in sensitive individuals.
Industrial plants must monitor their sulphur dioxide emissions to reduce environmental impact.
When sulphur dioxide is released from volcanoes, it can lead to the formation of acid rain.
