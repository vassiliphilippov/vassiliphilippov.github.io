regreen

verb
to restore eco-friendliness
Pronunciation: / riːˈɡriːn /; / riˈɡriːn / (AmE), / riːˈɡriːn /; / riˈɡriːn / (BrE)
to make an area green again or environmentally friendly by planting trees, plants, or using eco-friendly practices

Examples

To regreen a city
The city plans to regreen its parks by planting thousands of new trees.
After years of pollution, the company decided to regreen its manufacturing process.
Many organizations are working to regreen urban areas by introducing more green spaces.
The community worked together to regreen the abandoned lot with native plants.
After years of drought, efforts to regreen the countryside have begun.
Volunteers helped regreen the schoolyard by planting new grass and shrubs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deforest - to remove trees from an area
  • synonym: green - to make environmentally friendly
  • antonym: pollute - to make harmful to the environment
  • synonym: reforest - to plant trees in an area where there were forests before
