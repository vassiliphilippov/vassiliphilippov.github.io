make fun of

verb
mock or ridicule someone or something
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /meɪk fʌn əv/ /; / /meɪk fʌn ə/ (AmE), /meɪk fʌn əv/ /; / /meɪk fʌn ɒv/ (BrE)
to tease, mock, or laugh at someone in a way that may be funny but is often unkind or embarrassing

Examples

To make fun of a friend
Please don't make fun of his accent; it's unkind.
The children were upset when their classmates made fun of their new shoes.
They kept making fun of the movie, even though I enjoyed it.
It is unkind to make fun of people because of their mistakes.
Please stop making fun of your little brother; you're hurting his feelings.
The students began to make fun of the teacher's accent during class.
It's not kind to make fun of others for their mistakes.
She always makes fun of her brother when he tries to sing.
The children made fun of his new haircut, but he laughed along with them.
