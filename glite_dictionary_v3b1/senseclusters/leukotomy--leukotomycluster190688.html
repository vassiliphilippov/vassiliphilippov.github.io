leukotomy

noun
surgical operation severing brain nerve tracts
6.1
Pronunciation: / luːˈkɑtəmi /; / luːˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / luːˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which nerve fibers connecting the frontal lobe to other parts of the brain are severed, often resulting in significant changes in personality or cognition

Examples

A leukotomy operation
He underwent a leukotomy in the 1950s to treat his severe depression.
Scientific studies have documented personality changes that can follow a leukotomy.
The medical community has largely abandoned leukotomy due to its severe side effects.
After undergoing a leucotomy, the patient exhibited significant changes in personality and behavior.
The psychiatrist discussed the history and consequences of leucotomy as a treatment for severe mental illness.
The use of leucotomy has been widely criticized due to its irreversible effects on the brain.
