Drukpa

proper noun
Buddhist lineage member
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʊk.pə / (AmE), / ˈdrʊk.pə / (BrE)
a member of a Buddhist lineage or community mainly found in Bhutan and Tibet

Examples

Drukpa monks
The Drukpa are respected for their spiritual teachings in Bhutan.
Many tourists visit monasteries to watch the Drukpa monks perform traditional dances.
The Drukpa community plays an important role in Tibetan Buddhism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lama - a teacher of the Dharma in Tibetan Buddhism
  • synonym: monk - a member of a religious community of men
