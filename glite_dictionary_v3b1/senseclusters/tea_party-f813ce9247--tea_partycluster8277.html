tea party

proper noun
U.S. conservative political movement
Pronunciation: / c8ti ccp51rti / (AmE), / c8tid0 ccp51d0ti / (BrE)
(in the United States) a conservative political movement and loose coalition that emerged in the late 2000s, advocating smaller government, lower taxes, and reduced federal spending.

Examples

The Tea Party movement
The Tea Party movement gained national attention after large protests calling for smaller government and lower taxes.
Several Republican candidates publicly embraced Tea Party ideas to win conservative primary voters in 2010.
Researchers studied how the Tea Party organized grassroots support through social media and local town halls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Tea Party movement - alternative name for the U.S. conservative political movement emphasizing limited government and fiscal conservatism.
  • antonym: progressive movement - a political movement advocating social reform and expanded government programs; often contrasted with Tea Party goals.
