chromoplast

noun
pigment-containing plastid in plants
6.0 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊ.məˌplæst / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.məˌplɑːst / (BrE)
a type of plastid in plant cells that contains pigments other than chlorophyll, typically yellow or orange carotenoids, giving color to fruits and flowers

Examples

A chromoplast in plants
Scientists studied the structure of the chromoplast to better understand how plants store carotenoid pigments.
The brilliant red and orange hues of autumn leaves are due to the accumulation of pigments in the chromoplasts.
Tomatoes ripen as their chloroplasts transform into chromoplasts, resulting in a vibrant red color.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chloroplast - plastid containing chlorophyll for photosynthesis
  • antonym: leucoplast - colorless plastid involved in storage in plant cells
