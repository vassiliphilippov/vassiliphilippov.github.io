tilak

noun
forehead religious mark
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪlək / (AmE), / ˈtɪlək / (BrE)
a colored mark or symbol worn on the forehead, especially by Hindus, as a sign of religious or spiritual significance

Examples

A red tilak
During the festival, each participant received a red tilak on their forehead as a blessing.
The priest applied a sacred tilak to the boy before the ceremony began.
Wearing a tilak is a common practice in many Hindu rituals and celebrations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bindi - a decorative dot worn on the forehead, especially by Hindu women
