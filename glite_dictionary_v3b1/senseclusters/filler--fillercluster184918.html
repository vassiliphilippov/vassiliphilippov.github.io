filler

noun
substance for filling gaps or holes
4.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪl.ər/ (AmE), /ˈfɪl.ə/ /; / /ˈfɪl.eə/ (BrE)
a substance or item used to fill cracks, holes, gaps, or to add volume or bulk to something (including in construction, cosmetics, or general use)

Examples

Filler for cracks
Before painting, apply filler to any cracks in the wall.
Most car bodywork repairs require a special filler to smooth out dents.
The carpenter used a wood filler to repair the deep scratches in the table.
Some TV shows add extra scenes as fillers to reach the required length.
The recipe uses breadcrumbs as a filler to make the meatloaf more substantial.
This paint includes a filler to cover small cracks in the wall.
She opted for filler injections to smooth her nasolabial folds before the event.
Many clinics now offer fillers to restore cheek volume in patients seeking subtle rejuvenation.
After consultation, he chose a small amount of filler to add volume to his lips.

Related Terms

  • antonym: core - the central or most important part of something
  • synonym: dermal filler - a cosmetic injectable (often hyaluronic acid) used to smooth wrinkles or add volume
  • synonym: makeweight - something added to complete a required weight or quantity
  • synonym: putty - a soft material for sealing or filling holes, typically in woodwork or glass panes
