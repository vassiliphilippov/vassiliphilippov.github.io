characterology

noun
study of character traits
Pronunciation: / ˌkærɪktəˈrɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌkærɪktəˈrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study or practice of inferring personality or moral traits from physical appearance, handwriting, or facial features, now considered pseudoscientific

Examples

Characterology by appearance
In the nineteenth century, some doctors practiced characterology to judge patients' natures from their faces.
The museum displayed old books on characterology and other outdated sciences.
Modern psychology rejects characterology as lacking scientific evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phrenology - the study of skull shapes to determine character, now discredited
  • synonym: physiognomy - the practice of assessing a person's character from their facial features
