nasal vowel

noun
vowel pronounced through mouth and nose
5.3 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈneɪ.zəl ˈvaʊ.əl/ (AmE), /ˈneɪ.zəl ˈvaʊ.əl/ (BrE)
A vowel sound produced with airflow passing through both the mouth and the nose due to the lowering of the velum.

Examples

A nasal vowel sound
French has several nasal vowels that do not exist in English.
Linguists study how nasal vowels are articulated in different languages.
The nasal vowel in the word 'bon' distinguishes it from similar words in French.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oral vowel - a vowel sound produced only through the mouth, without nasal airflow
