nonfluent aphasia

noun
severe impairment of speech/writing expression
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / n…ënÀàflu…ônt …ôÀàfe…™ í…ô / (AmE), / n…ínÀàfluÀê…ônt …ôÀàfe…™zi…ô / (BrE)
A type of aphasia in which the ability to express oneself by speech or writing is severely impaired, while comprehension may be relatively preserved.

Examples

Nonfluent aphasia speech
After the stroke, he was diagnosed with nonfluent aphasia and struggled to form complete sentences.
Her therapist developed a program to help her communicate despite nonfluent aphasia.
People with nonfluent aphasia may understand speech but find it difficult to articulate words.

Synonyms

  • Broca's aphasia
    7.5 0.00/m
    Broca's aphasia causes severe trouble speaking and writing, while nonfluent aphasia describes the same pattern with preserved understanding.
  • motor aphasia
    8.5 0.00/m
    motor aphasia impairs speech and writing motor control severely, while nonfluent aphasia focuses on poor expression despite fair comprehension.
  • expressive aphasia
    7.7 0.01/m
    expressive aphasia emphasizes difficulty producing language in speech or writing, while nonfluent aphasia specifically labels this type with comprehension relatively spared.
  • ataxic aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    ataxic aphasia involves irregular, slurred speech often from motor timing problems, while nonfluent aphasia is marked by limited speech and preserved understanding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fluent aphasia - a type of aphasia with fluent, often nonsensical speech
