tumbly

adjective
unsteady or unstable
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌmbli / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbli / (BrE)
tending to tumble, fall, or roll easily; unsteady or unstable

Examples

Tumbly old chair
The tumbly ladder made it difficult to reach the top shelf safely.
Children laughed as their tumbly toy car rolled down the bumpy hill.
After the storm, the old fence became quite tumbly and leaned to one side.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not likely to fall
  • synonym: unstable - not steady or firm; likely to fall or change
