tramp

verb
walk heavily or with effort
1.2 4.5
Pronunciation: /træmp/ (AmE), /træmp/ (BrE)
to walk or travel with heavy steps, often over a long distance or rough ground, usually with effort

Examples

To tramp through mud
He tramped across the city, exhausted but determined to reach his destination.
They tramped through the muddy field, their boots sinking with every step.
We could hear the soldiers tramping down the road in the early morning.
The hikers tramped across the hills despite the pouring rain.
They spent the afternoon tramping around the city in search of a museum.
We decided to tramp through the muddy fields to reach the village.
She enjoys tramping over hills and across fields in all kinds of weather.
They spent the summer tramping through the national parks of New Zealand.
We tramped along muddy country lanes for hours before reaching the village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hike - to walk a long distance, especially for pleasure or exercise
  • synonym: march - to walk with regular and forceful steps, often as part of a group
  • synonym: plod - to walk doggedly and slowly with heavy steps
  • synonym: roam - to move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically
  • synonym: stomp - to walk with heavy, forceful steps
  • synonym: trek - to go on a long, difficult journey on foot
  • synonym: wander - to move about without a fixed course, aim, or goal
