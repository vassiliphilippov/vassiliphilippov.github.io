espouse

verb
adopt or support a cause or belief
Pronunciation: / ɪˈspaʊz / (AmE), / ɪˈspaʊz / (BrE)
to adopt or support a cause, belief, or way of life

Examples

To espouse a cause
Many politicians choose to espouse popular causes during their campaigns to gain support.
She openly espouses equal rights for all citizens, regardless of background.
The company decided to espouse a new environmental policy to reduce its carbon footprint.
Many philosophers espouse rationality as the primary basis for ethics.
She decided to espouse the environmental movement after learning about climate change.
The company has espoused a strict policy of sustainability in all its operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adopt - to take up or start to use an idea or method
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
