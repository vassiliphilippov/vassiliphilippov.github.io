unreasonableness

noun
irrational quality
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈriːzənəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːzənəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being not reasonable, fair, or sensible; irrational or stubborn behavior

Examples

The unreasonableness of behavior
The court found that the policy's unreasonableness justified its cancellation.
Lawyers argued that the contract's terms showed clear unreasonableness and should not be enforced.
A judge may intervene if there is evidence of unreasonableness in an administrative decision.
The unreasonableness of the new policy frustrated many employees.
She was surprised by the unreasonableness of his demands during the negotiation.
It is difficult to argue with someone whose unreasonableness prevents compromise.
His unreasonableness made it impossible to reach an agreement during the discussion.
The manager's unreasonableness frustrated the employees who were hoping for a compromise.
She apologized later for her unreasonableness during the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrationality - the state of being illogical or unreasonable
  • synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn or difficult to persuade
  • antonym: reasonableness - the quality of being fair, sensible, or moderate
  • synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being unwilling to change one's mind or attitude
