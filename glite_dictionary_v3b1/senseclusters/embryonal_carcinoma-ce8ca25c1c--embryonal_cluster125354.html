embryonal carcinoma

noun
malignant testicular tumor
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmbriˈoʊnəl ˌkɑrsɪˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɛmbriˈəʊnəl ˌkɑːsɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor of the testis that resembles embryonic tissue and occurs most commonly in young men.

Examples

An embryonal carcinoma tumor
Pathologists identified the tumor as an embryonal carcinoma under the microscope.
The patient was diagnosed with an embryonal carcinoma after experiencing testicular swelling.
Treatment for embryonal carcinoma often involves surgery followed by chemotherapy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: seminoma - a type of testicular cancer consisting of uniform cells
  • degree higher: teratoma - a type of testicular tumor containing several types of tissue
