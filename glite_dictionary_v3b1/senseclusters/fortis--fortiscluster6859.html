fortis

adjective
strong consonant
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrtɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtɪs / (BrE)
describing a consonant pronounced with greater force, tension, or breath than its counterpart

Examples

Fortis consonant sound
In English, the fortis sounds include the voiceless stops like 'p', 't', and 'k'.
Linguists often compare fortis and lenis consonants to study differences in pronunciation.
The teacher explained that a fortis consonant is produced with more muscular effort.
