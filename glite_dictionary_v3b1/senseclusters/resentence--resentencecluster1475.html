resentence

verb
to give new sentence
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
to give a new or different legal sentence or punishment to someone who has already been sentenced

Examples

To resentence a prisoner
After the appeal, the court decided to resentence the convicted man to a shorter prison term.
The new evidence led the judge to resentence her for a lesser offense.
If the law changes, some prisoners may be eligible to be resentenced under the new guidelines.
The court decided to resentence the prisoner after new evidence was presented.
If the appeal is successful, the judge may resentence the defendant to a lighter punishment.
After reviewing the case, the panel agreed to resentence the individual based on updated guidelines.
