ride

verb
to depend on something (figurative)
5.3 9.61/m
Pronunciation: / raÉªd / (AmE), / raÉªd / (BrE)
(of an outcome or situation) to depend on or be determined by something.

Examples

Ride on success
Much of our success in the market will ride on how customers react to the new product.
The entire season rode on the final match against their rivals.
Their hopes for promotion ride on the outcome of this meeting.

Synonyms

  • depend on
    1.7 67/m
    depend on means something is decided or affected by another thing, while ride is a more informal, idiomatic way to say this.
  • hinge on
    4.2 3.10/m
    hinge on suggests an outcome depends completely on one factor, while ride can be more casual and less absolute.
  • turn on
    4.1 1.55/m
    turn on is a neutral phrasal verb for dependence but ride is more idiomatic and often used in casual speech.
