mooring line

noun
rope or cable securing a boat
8.2 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmʊr.ɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.rɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
a strong rope or cable used to tie a boat or ship to a dock, quay, or other fixed object to keep it in place

Examples

Strong mooring line

High winds strained the mooring line as the boat rocked in the harbor.
The sailor secured the yacht with a mooring line before stepping onto the dock.
