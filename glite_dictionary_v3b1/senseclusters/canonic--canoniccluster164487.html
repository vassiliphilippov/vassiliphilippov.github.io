canonic

adjective
included in a religious canon, especially biblical
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɑnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪk / (BrE)
included in or relating to a recognized canon, especially a book accepted as part of the Bible

Examples

Canonic books
Not all early Christian writings are recognized as canonic by every denomination.
Scholars debate whether the Gospel of Thomas should be considered canonic.
The Council deemed only certain texts to be canonic and worthy of inclusion in the Bible.
