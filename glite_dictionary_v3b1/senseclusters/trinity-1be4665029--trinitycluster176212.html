Trinity

noun
threefold nature of God in Christianity
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪnəti / (AmE), / ˈtrɪnɪti / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the union of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as three persons in one God.

Examples

The Trinity symbol
Many churches display symbols representing the Trinity in their stained glass windows.
The concept of the Trinity distinguishes Christian theology from other monotheistic religions.
The doctrine of the Trinity is fundamental to most forms of Christianity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Godhead - the essential nature or being of God, often referenced in Trinitarian doctrine
