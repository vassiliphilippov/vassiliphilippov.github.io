sensillum

noun
insect sensory organ
9.9
Pronunciation: /sen.ˈsɪl.əm/ (AmE), /sen.ˈsɪl.əm/ (BrE)
a small sensory organ or structure on the body of insects and other arthropods that detects environmental stimuli

Examples

Tiny sensilla on antennae
The antennae of the moth are covered with tiny sensilla that help it detect smells.
Scientists examined the sensillum under a microscope to study how insects sense their environment.
Each sensillum on the beetle's leg responds to touch or chemical signals.
