cotton rose

noun
Chinese shrub with color-changing flowers
6.1 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.tən roʊz/ /; / /ˈkɔː.tən roʊz/ (AmE), /ˈkɒt.ən rəʊz/ /; / /ˈkɒt.ən rəʊzeɪ/ (BrE)
A Chinese shrub or small tree (Hibiscus mutabilis) with large white or pink flowers that turn deep red by evening; widely cultivated and naturalized in southeastern United States.

Examples

Blooming cotton rose
Botanists admire the cotton rose for its ability to shift from white or pink to deep red in a single day.
The cotton rose adorns many southern gardens with its vibrant, color-changing blooms.
We planted a cotton rose near the porch, and its blossoms have become the highlight of the yard.
