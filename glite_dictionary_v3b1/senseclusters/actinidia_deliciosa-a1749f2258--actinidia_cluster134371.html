Actinidia deliciosa

noun
species of plant producing kiwifruit
Pronunciation: / ækˌtɪnɪdiə dɪˈlɪʃiə / (AmE), / ækˌtɪnɪdɪə dɪˈlɪsiə / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to China, widely cultivated for its edible kiwifruit with fuzzy brown skin and green flesh.

Examples

Actinidia deliciosa climbing vine
Botanists first identified Actinidia deliciosa as a distinct species native to China.
Growers in New Zealand have made Actinidia deliciosa a major agricultural export.
The fuzzy fruit of Actinidia deliciosa is commonly known as kiwifruit around the world.
