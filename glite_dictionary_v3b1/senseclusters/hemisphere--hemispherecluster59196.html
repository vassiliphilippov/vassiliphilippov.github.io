hemisphere

noun
half of the Earth, e.g. northern or southern
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛmɪsfɪə / (BrE)
Either of the two halves of the Earth, usually divided into northern and southern, or eastern and western, by the equator or a meridian.

Examples

Northern hemisphere
Australia is located in the Southern hemisphere.
Many migratory birds cross from one hemisphere to the other each year.
The equator divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern hemispheres.
