white

adjective
restricted to white people only (segregation)
Pronunciation: / waɪt / (AmE), / waɪt / (BrE)
(of a place, facility, or organization) restricted to white people only, especially as a result of racial segregation.

Examples

White schools
In the era of segregation, some schools were designated as white institutions only.
Signs marked certain public facilities as white during the 1950s in some regions.
The restaurant maintained a white clientele and refused to serve people of color.

Related Terms

  • synonym: segregated - restricted or separated according to race or other characteristic
