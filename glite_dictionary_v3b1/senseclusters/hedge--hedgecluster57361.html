hedge

verb
restrict or limit, as by boundaries
Pronunciation: / hɛdʒ / (AmE), / hɛdʒ / (BrE)
to restrict or limit someone or something, especially by creating boundaries or obstacles

Examples

To hedge progress
Local ordinances hedge the development of new businesses in the area.
She felt hedged in by her family's expectations and rules.
The old laws hedge disadvantaged groups in with unnecessary regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberate - to set free from constraints or restrictions
  • synonym: restrict - to limit the amount or range of something
