misimpression

noun
mistaken belief
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsɪmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
a mistaken idea or belief about something

Examples

A misimpression about facts
She left the meeting with a misimpression about the project's timeline.
It is easy to get a misimpression if you do not have all the facts.
His comments created a misimpression that the company was struggling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impression - an idea, feeling, or opinion about something or someone
  • synonym: misconception - an incorrect understanding or interpretation
