tone

verb
alter the color or tone
Pronunciation: / toʊn / (AmE), / təʊn / (BrE)
to change or give a particular color or shade to something, especially in art or photography

Examples

To tone a canvas
Artists often tone the canvas with a thin wash before painting to set the mood.
She learned how to tone her hair to achieve a subtle silver hue.
The photographer decided to tone the print for a warmer appearance.
Old photographs were often toned to enhance their appearance or longevity.
The photographer decided to tone the black-and-white image for a warmer effect.
They used a special chemical to tone the print blue.

  • synonym: tint - to apply a color tint to something, especially a photograph; similar meaning
