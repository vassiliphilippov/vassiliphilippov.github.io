endosteal

noun
implant inside bone
9.9
Pronunciation: /en.də.ˈstiː.əl/ /; / /en.doʊ.ˈstiː.əl/ (AmE), /en.də.ˈstiː.əl/ /; / /en.də.ˈstɪ.əl/ (BrE)
a dental or orthopedic implant that is placed inside the bone to support a prosthetic device or fixation

Examples

Endosteal implant
The dentist recommended an endosteal to replace the missing tooth in the patient's jaw.
After the accident, an endosteal was used to stabilize the fractured bone.
Modern dental procedures often use an endosteal for long-term tooth replacement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implant - a device placed in the body to support or replace a part
  • antonym: subperiosteal - an implant placed on top of the bone but under the gum
