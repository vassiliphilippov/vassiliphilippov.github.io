sprinkle

verb
to scatter liquid drops lightly
1.0 1.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsprɪŋ.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈsprɪŋ.kəl/ (BrE)
to scatter small drops of liquid or particles over a surface

Examples

To sprinkle sugar
If you sprinkle the cake with a little water, it won’t dry out so quickly.
Remember to sprinkle the plants every morning during the summer.
She likes to sprinkle water on the floor before cleaning it.
Rain began to sprinkle lightly over the city in the late afternoon.
She likes to sprinkle cinnamon on her morning coffee.
The gardener sprinkled seeds evenly across the flower bed.
