prowl

verb
move stealthily or aimlessly
6.8 4.3
Pronunciation: /praʊl/ (AmE), /praʊl/ (BrE)
to move about quietly and secretly, often in search of something or as if hunting

Examples

To prowl the streets
He would often prowl the streets late at night with no particular destination.
Security guards prowl the building at night looking for anything unusual.
The cat began to prowl around the empty house as darkness fell.
He would prowl the halls, looking for any sign of trouble.
Police officers continued to prowl the neighborhood after a series of break-ins.
The cat likes to prowl the garden at night, searching for mice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully in order to avoid being heard or noticed
  • synonym: loiter - stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • degree lower: roam - to wander or travel around with no fixed purpose
  • synonym: skulk - to move stealthily or furtively
  • synonym: wander - move about aimlessly or without fixed course
