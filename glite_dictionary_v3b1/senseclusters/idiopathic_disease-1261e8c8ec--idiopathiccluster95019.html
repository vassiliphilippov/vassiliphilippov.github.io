idiopathic disease

noun
disease of unknown cause
Pronunciation: / ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease that arises from internal dysfunction without an identifiable external or specific cause.

Examples

An idiopathic disease case
Because the idiopathic disease has no known cause, treatment focuses on managing symptoms.
Researchers are still trying to understand the origins of this idiopathic disease.
The patient was diagnosed with an idiopathic disease after all known causes were ruled out.

  • synonym: primary disease - a disease not caused by another disease or condition
