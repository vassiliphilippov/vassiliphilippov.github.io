nudge

verb
gently encourage or prompt
4.1 1.97/m
Pronunciation: / nÊŒdÊ’ / (AmE), / nÊŒdÊ’ / (BrE)
to encourage or prompt someone to take action, especially by gently pestering or persuading them

Examples

To nudge someone to act
He kept nudging me about finishing the project until I finally did it.
She tried to nudge him into applying for the new job.
Sometimes you need to nudge your friends to get them to try something new.

Synonyms

  • prompt
    3.6 23/m
    prompt makes someone act or reply quickly on cue, while nudge is a small, gentle push or reminder to act.
  • prod
    3.6 0.83/m
    prod pushes or urges someone with a bit more pressure or poke, while nudge is a softer, lighter prompt to act.
  • coax
    4.1 5.15/m
    coax gently persuades someone over time with kind words and patience, while nudge is a quick, light push to act.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to cause someone not to want to do something
  • synonym: encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • synonym: prod - to poke or urge someone into action
