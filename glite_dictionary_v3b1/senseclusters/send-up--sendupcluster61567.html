Examples

Synonyms

  • parody
    5.0 6.26/m
    parody imitates a specific work's style to make fun, while send-up exaggerates and mocks a person, group, or work.
  • spoof
    5.3 0.77/m
    spoof is a light, often film or TV imitation that jokes about style, while send-up targets a person's or work's manner.
  • takeoff
    6.3 0.30/m
    takeoff copies someone's voice or manner in a short, funny way, while send-up is a broader mocking imitation of style.
  • lampoon
    8.0 0.44/m
    lampoon is a sharp written or spoken satire that attacks someone, while send-up is mainly a playful imitation of style.
  • burlesque
    8.7 0.12/m
    burlesque is a theatrical or literary imitation that exaggerates for comic effect, while send-up is any mocking imitation of style.
  • travesty
    7.7 1.97/m
    travesty is a grotesque, distorted imitation that often shocks, while send-up is a humorous parody that mocks style without making it absurd.
  • pasquinade
    9.9 0.01/m
    pasquinade is a public, often posted satire aimed at an individual, while send-up is a general humorous imitation or parody of style.
