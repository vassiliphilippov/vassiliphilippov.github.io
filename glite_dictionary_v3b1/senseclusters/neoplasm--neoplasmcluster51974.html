neoplasm

noun
abnormal tissue growth; tumor
Pronunciation: / ˈniː.oʊˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈniː.əʊˌplazəm / (BrE)
An abnormal mass of tissue that arises through uncontrolled cell growth and serves no physiological function; a tumor.

Examples

Cancerous neoplasm
Researchers are developing new drugs to target cancerous neoplasms more effectively.
Some neoplasms are benign, while others may be malignant and require treatment.
The doctor discovered a neoplasm in the patient's lung during a routine scan.
