sidedness

noun
bias or partisanship
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.dɪd.nəs/ /; / /ˈsaɪd.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.dɪd.nəs/ /; / /ˈsaɪd.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of supporting or favoring one side in an argument, contest, or debate; bias or partisanship

Examples

Political sidedness
The judge was careful to avoid any sidedness during the trial.
Critics accused the documentary of sidedness in its portrayal of the political issue.
Teachers should strive for fairness and avoid sidedness when discussing controversial topics in class.
