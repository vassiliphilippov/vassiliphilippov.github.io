quasi

adjective
seeming; having some resemblance
8.1 2/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkw…ëÀêzi / (AmE), / Ààkw…ëÀêza…™ / (BrE)
Seeming to be something but not really; having some but not all features of a certain kind.

Examples

Quasi leadership role
He held a quasi leadership role within the organization.
The committee reached a quasi decision, but nothing was finalized.
The contract was seen as a quasi agreement and not fully enforceable.

Synonyms

  • virtual
    4.1 11/m
    virtual is almost the real thing in effect or result, while quasi means it only has some of those features.
  • pseudo
    7.7 2/m
    pseudo looks false or deceptive and often claims to be real, while quasi describes something that partly resembles the real thing.
