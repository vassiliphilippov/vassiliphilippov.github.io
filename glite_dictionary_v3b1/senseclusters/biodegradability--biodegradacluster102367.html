biodegradability

noun
ability to decompose naturally
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊdɪˌɡreɪdəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊdɪˌɡreɪdəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance to be broken down naturally by living organisms, especially bacteria

Examples

The biodegradability of plastics
The biodegradability of these bags makes them a better choice for the environment.
Scientists are testing the biodegradability of new materials for use in packaging.
Understanding the biodegradability of chemicals is important for protecting natural ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decomposability - the ability to break down into simpler substances
  • antonym: durability - the ability to last a long time without breaking down
