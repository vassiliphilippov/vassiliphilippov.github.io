undeliverable

adjective
not able to deliver
4.2
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dɪ.ˈlɪv.ər.ə.bəl/ /; / /ən.dɪ.ˈlɪv.ər.ə.bəl/ /; / /ʌn.də.ˈlɪv.ər.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ʌn.dɪ.ˈlɪv.rə.bəl/ /; / /ən.dɪ.ˈlɪv.rə.bəl/ (BrE)
not able to be delivered to the intended recipient or location (such as a package, letter, or in finance)

Examples

Undeliverable package
The package was marked undeliverable after several failed delivery attempts.
In some markets, certain contracts are undeliverable and must be settled in cash.
If the address is incorrect, the letter will be considered undeliverable and returned to the sender.
The letter was marked undeliverable because the address was incomplete.
If the recipient has moved, the package may become undeliverable and be returned to the sender.
Emails sent to an invalid address are often reported as undeliverable by the system.
