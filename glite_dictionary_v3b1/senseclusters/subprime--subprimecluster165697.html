subprime

noun
high-risk loan
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈpraɪm / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈpraɪm / (BrE)
a loan made to a borrower with poor credit, or the market for such loans

Examples

A subprime mortgage
The bank stopped offering subprime to reduce its exposure to risky borrowers.
Many homeowners defaulted on their subprime during the financial crisis.
Regulations on subprime have become stricter since the market collapse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: high-risk loan - a loan with a high chance of not being repaid
  • antonym: prime loan - a loan given to a borrower with good credit
