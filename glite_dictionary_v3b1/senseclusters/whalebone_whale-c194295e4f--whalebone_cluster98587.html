whalebone whale

noun
whale that filters plankton with plates of whalebone
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪlˌboʊn weɪl / (AmE), / ˈweɪlˌbəʊn weɪl / (BrE)
a whale with plates of whalebone (baleen) along the upper jaw, used for filtering plankton from water

Examples

A whalebone whale species
Scientists study the migration patterns of several whalebone whale species each year.
The whalebone whale feeds by filtering tiny crustaceans from the sea through its baleen plates.
Unlike toothed whales, a whalebone whale lacks teeth and relies on baleen for feeding.
