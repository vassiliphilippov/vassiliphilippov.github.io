leaf

noun
sheet of paper in a book or manuscript
7.7 2.38/m
Pronunciation: / lif / (AmE), / liÀêf / (BrE)
a single sheet of paper in a book or manuscript, especially one with writing or printing on it

Examples

A leaf of paper
A torn leaf was missing from the back of the old book.
She carefully turned the delicate leaf of the ancient manuscript.
The librarian found several loose leaves inside the cover.

Synonyms

  • folio
    8.6 0.23/m
    folio is a formal term for a large manuscript sheet used in books and old papers, while a leaf is a single book page.
  • sheet
    1.0 19/m
    sheet means a single flat piece of paper often used for printing or writing, while a leaf is specifically a sheet inside a book.
  • sheet of paper
    1.0 2.84/m
    sheet of paper is a general flat page used for writing or printing, while a leaf is a sheet found in a book.

Related Terms

  • synonym: page - one side of a sheet of paper in a book or publication
