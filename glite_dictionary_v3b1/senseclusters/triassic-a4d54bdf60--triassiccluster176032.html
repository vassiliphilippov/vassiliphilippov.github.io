Triassic

noun
a geological period from 252 to 201 million years ago
6.5 7.4
Pronunciation: /traɪ.ˈæs.ɪk/ (AmE), /traɪ.ˈæs.ɪk/ (BrE)
the period of geological time from about 252 to 201 million years ago, marked by the emergence of dinosaurs, marine reptiles, and significant volcanic activity

Examples

Triassic period fossils
Fossils from the Triassic provide clues about early reptiles.
The Triassic period saw the first appearance of dinosaurs on Earth.
The separation of continents began during the Triassic era.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Jurassic - the geological period following the Triassic, notable for large dinosaurs
  • degree lower: Permian - the geological period preceding the Triassic
