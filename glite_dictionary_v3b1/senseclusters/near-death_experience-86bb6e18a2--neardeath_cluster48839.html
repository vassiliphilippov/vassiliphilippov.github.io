near-death experience

noun
experience of coming close to death
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪr dɛθ ɪkˈspɪriəns / (AmE), / ˈnɪə dɛθ ɪkˈspɪəriəns / (BrE)
an experience reported by people who have come close to death, often involving sensations such as detachment from the body, feelings of peace, seeing a light, or reviewing one's life

Examples

A vivid near-death experience
After the accident, she described her near-death experience as peaceful and full of vivid lights.
Many people who have had a near-death experience report seeing their lives flash before their eyes.
The documentary explored stories of individuals who claimed to have a near-death experience during medical emergencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: out-of-body experience - an experience in which a person seems to perceive the world from a location outside their physical body
