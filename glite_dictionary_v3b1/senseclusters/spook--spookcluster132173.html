spook

verb
to frighten or startle (especially animals)
4.8 3.37/m
Pronunciation: / spuÀêk / (AmE), / spuÀêk / (BrE)
to suddenly frighten or disturb (a person or animal), often causing a startled or panicked reaction

Examples

To spook a horse
She didn't want to spook the deer, so she approached quietly.
The loud thunder spooked the cattle, causing them to run.
Unexpected movements can easily spook young horses.

Synonyms

  • startle
    3.3 5.90/m
    startle makes someone jump or move quickly from surprise, while spook means suddenly frighten or disturb, causing panic or alarm.
  • jolt
    4.3 0.92/m
    jolt shocks or disturbs a person's calm suddenly, while spook specifically frightens or disturbs and may cause a panicked reaction.
  • fright
    3.2 0.03/m
    fright makes someone feel fear or alarm, while spook implies sudden disturbance that startles or panics people or animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - make tranquil or soothe, especially after fright
  • synonym: startle - cause someone to feel sudden shock or alarm
