SSRI

noun
type of antidepressant drug
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs ˌɛs ˌɑːr ˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˌɛs ˌɑː ˈraɪ / (BrE)
an antidepressant drug that increases serotonin levels in the brain by inhibiting its reuptake, used to treat depression and other mood disorders

Examples

SSRIs for depression
Doctors often prescribe SSRIs to patients experiencing chronic depression.
Not all antidepressants are SSRIs; some belong to different classes.
She experienced fewer side effects while taking an SSRI compared to older antidepressants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antidepressant - a drug used to alleviate the symptoms of depression
  • degree lower: tricyclic antidepressant - an older class of antidepressant drugs
