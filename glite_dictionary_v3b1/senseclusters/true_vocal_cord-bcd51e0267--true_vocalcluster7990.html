true vocal cord

noun
the primary sound-producing cords in the larynx
5.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / tru Ààvo äk…ôl k…îrd / (AmE), / truÀê Ààv…ô äk…ôl k…îÀêd / (BrE)
either of the two bands of tissue in the larynx that vibrate to produce sound when air passes between them; the main structures responsible for voice production

Examples

The true vocal cord tissue
Injury to a true vocal cord can result in permanent changes to a person's voice.
Swelling of the true vocal cord may cause hoarseness and difficulty speaking.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's true vocal cords for any signs of abnormal growth.

Synonyms

  • vocal cord
    3.5 1.67/m
    vocal cord is the common name for the vibrating tissue in the throat, while true vocal cord is the specific anatomical term.
  • vocal fold
    6.3 0.48/m
    vocal fold is a medical term for the vibrating larynx tissue, while true vocal cord is the anatomical name for the same voice-producing bands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: false vocal cord - the upper pair of folds in the larynx not involved in voice production
  • synonym: vocal fold - one of the folds of tissue in the larynx, including both true and false vocal cords
