illusiveness

noun
quality of being unreal
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being deceptive, unreal, or difficult to catch or understand

Examples

The illusiveness of treasure
The illusiveness of the treasure made many explorers waste years searching for it.
She was frustrated by the illusiveness of happiness, which always seemed just out of reach.
The magician's performance relied on the illusiveness of his tricks to amaze the audience.
The illusiveness of the magician's tricks left the audience in awe.
Artists often use light and shadow to enhance the illusiveness of their paintings.
The illusiveness of the mirage made travelers believe there was water ahead.
The illusiveness of the solution made the puzzle even more challenging for the students.
Many artists try to capture the illusiveness of beauty in their work.
He was frustrated by the illusiveness of a clear answer to his question.
