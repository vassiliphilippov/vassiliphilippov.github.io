Lynx

noun
genus of wild cats with tufted ears
9.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /lɪŋks/ (AmE), /lɪŋks/ (BrE)
A genus of medium-sized wild cats, including the Eurasian lynx, Canada lynx, Iberian lynx, and bobcat, characterized by tufted ears, short tails, and spotted coats.

Examples

The Lynx is known for its distinctive ear tufts and solitary behavior in forested areas.
Wildlife biologists study the differences between members of the Lynx genus, such as the bobcat and the Eurasian lynx.
