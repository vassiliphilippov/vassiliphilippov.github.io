tutor

verb
give individual instruction or teach privately
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtə / (BrE)
to teach or give individual or private instruction to someone, especially to help them improve in a subject

Examples

To tutor a student
He has tutored high school students in mathematics for several years.
She tutored me in Spanish every weekend before the exams.
This semester I am tutoring three children who need help with reading.
She is tutoring three students in the evenings to prepare them for the exam.
After school, he started tutoring his neighbor in mathematics.
Many college students earn extra money by tutoring younger children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coach - to train or tutor a person in a subject or skill
  • synonym: instruct - to teach or educate someone
  • antonym: learn - to gain knowledge or skill by studying or being taught
  • synonym: teach - to give instruction or lessons to others
