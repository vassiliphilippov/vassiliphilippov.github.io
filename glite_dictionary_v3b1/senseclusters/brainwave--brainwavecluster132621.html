brainwave

noun
electrical activity in the brain
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪnwəv / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnwɛɪv / (BrE)
A rapid fluctuation of electrical activity between parts of the brain's cortex, detectable by an electroencephalograph.

Examples

Brainwave patterns
Different types of brainwaves, such as alpha and beta waves, are observed during sleep and wakefulness.
Meditation can alter the frequency of your brainwaves.
The EEG showed abnormal brainwave patterns during the patient's seizure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brain rhythm - a rhythmic fluctuation of electrical activity in the brain
