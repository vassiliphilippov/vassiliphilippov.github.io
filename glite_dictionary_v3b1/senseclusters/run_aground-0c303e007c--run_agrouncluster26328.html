run aground

verb
(of a vessel) become stuck on ground or shore
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: / rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / rʌn əˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a ship or boat) to become stuck on the shore or in shallow water and unable to move

Examples

To run aground in shallow water
If the yacht runs aground in shallow water, it may take hours to refloat it.
Many old ships have run aground on these treacherous reefs over the centuries.
The ferry ran aground during last night's storm, causing a major rescue operation.
Several fishing boats have run aground in the shallow waters of the bay this year.
The captain took quick action to avoid running aground on the hidden rocks.
The ship ran aground during last night's storm and could not be refloated.
Fishermen had to be rescued after their boat ran aground on the rocky coast.
If you don't pay attention to the tide, you might run aground in shallow water.
The cargo ship ran aground during the storm and could not be freed for days.
