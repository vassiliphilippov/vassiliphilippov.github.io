novelty

noun
the quality of being new or unusual
4.7 6.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ëÀêv…ôlti / (AmE), / Ààn…ív…ôlti / (BrE)
the quality of being new, original, or different, often in a way that is interesting or surprising

Examples

The novelty wears off
Although the novelty of working from home wore off, many still enjoy its flexibility.
The novelty of the new smartphone quickly attracted attention from tech enthusiasts.
The exhibition's main appeal lay in the sheer novelty of its interactive displays.
The novelty of their approach caught the judge's attention.
The product's novelty soon wore off, and sales declined.
Traveling to a new country brings a sense of novelty to everyday life.

Synonyms

  • freshness
    2.5 0.47/m
    freshness suggests a lively, new feeling you notice at once, while novelty names the general quality of being new or different.
  • originality
    4.5 4.57/m
    originality means creative, not copied ideas by people, while novelty is the simple quality of being new or surprising.
  • newness
    1.9 1.20/m
    newness means the state of having just appeared or been made, while novelty adds interest, originality, or surprise.
