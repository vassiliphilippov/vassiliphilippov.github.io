vulgarise

verb
make something more accessible to the public
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (BrE)
to make an idea or subject simpler or more accessible to the general public, often by reducing its complexity or sophistication

Examples

To vulgarise a theory
By simplifying the theory, the author was able to vulgarise it for a wider audience.
Some critics argue that television has vulgarised classic literature for mass consumption.
The professor tried to vulgarise scientific concepts to make them understandable to laypeople.

Related Terms

  • synonym: popularize - to make something popular or widely known
  • antonym: refine - to improve or make something more sophisticated
