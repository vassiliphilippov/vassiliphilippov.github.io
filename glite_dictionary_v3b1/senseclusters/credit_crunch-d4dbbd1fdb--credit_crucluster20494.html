credit crunch

noun
economic shortage of available loans
6.6 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkred.ɪt krʌntʃ/ /; / /ˈkred.ət krʌntʃ/ (AmE), /ˈkred.ɪt krʌntʃ/ /; / /ˈkred.ət krʌntʃ/ (BrE)
an economic condition in which loans are hard to obtain due to a shortage of available funds for lending and often high interest rates

Examples

The credit crunch period
After the credit crunch, obtaining a mortgage became much more difficult for young families.
Banks tightened their lending criteria during the credit crunch of 2008.
Many small businesses struggled to survive the severe credit crunch that hit the economy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credit squeeze - an economic situation with restricted lending and less available credit
