convexo-concave

adjective
convex on one side, concave on the other (more convex)
5.6 9.0
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈvek.soʊ kən.ˈkeɪv/ /; / /kɑn.ˈvek.soʊ kɑn.ˈkeɪv/ (AmE), /kɒn.ˈvek.səʊ kɒn.ˈkeɪv/ /; / /kən.ˈvek.səʊ kɒn.ˈkeɪv/ (BrE)
Having one side convex and the other concave, with the convexity being greater than the concavity.

Examples

A convexo-concave surface
A convexo-concave surface has unique reflective properties compared to a flat or uniform curve.
Many optical instruments use convexo-concave mirrors for image correction.
The lens was convexo-concave, allowing it to bend light in a specific way.
