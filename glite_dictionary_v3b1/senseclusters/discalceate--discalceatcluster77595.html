discalceate

adjective
barefoot, especially in religious orders
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkæl.si.eɪt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkalsɪeɪt / (BrE)
(of members of some religious orders) barefoot or wearing only sandals, especially as a sign of humility or poverty.

Examples

Discalceate friars
In the Middle Ages, discalceate friars were respected for their commitment to humility.
Many discalceate monks take a vow of poverty as part of their spiritual discipline.
The discalceate branch of the order is easily recognized by their simple sandals.
