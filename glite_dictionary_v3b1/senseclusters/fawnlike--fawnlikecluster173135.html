fawnlike

adjective
excessively flattering
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔnˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːnˌlaɪk / (BrE)
showing excessive flattery or submissive affection, often to gain favor; obsequious.

Examples

Fawnlike praise
His fawnlike behavior toward the manager was obvious to everyone in the office.
She offered fawnlike praise, hoping to be chosen for the project.
The intern's fawnlike attitude made her seem insincere to her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others; self-reliant
  • synonym: obsequious - excessively eager to please or obey
  • synonym: sycophantic - behaving in a flattering or servile way to gain advantage
