Theravada Buddhism

noun
the conservative branch of Buddhism in Southeast Asia
6.9
Pronunciation: /θə.rə.ˈvɑː.də ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /tə.rə.ˈvɑː.də ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /θə.rə.ˈvɑː.də ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /tə.rə.ˈvɑː.də ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A branch of Buddhism that emphasizes personal salvation through individual effort and strict adherence to the original teachings found in Pali scriptures; prevalent in countries such as Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, Laos, and Cambodia.

Examples

Theravada Buddhism teachings
Theravada Buddhism places great emphasis on the earliest Buddhist scriptures written in Pali.
Academic courses on Theravada Buddhism discuss the importance of meditation and ethical living.
Many monks in Thailand follow Theravada Buddhism as their spiritual path.
