antidepressant

noun
drug used to treat depression
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.t̬i.dɪˈprɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.dɪˈprɛs.ənt / (BrE)
a drug or substance used to treat depression, typically by affecting chemical levels in the brain

Examples

Take an antidepressant pill
Her doctor changed her antidepressant after she experienced unwanted side effects.
Many people take an antidepressant to help manage symptoms of clinical depression.
Research is ongoing into new types of antidepressant medications with fewer adverse reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antianxiety - drug used to reduce anxiety
  • antonym: stimulant - drug that increases alertness or activity
