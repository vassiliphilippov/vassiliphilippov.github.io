main clause

noun
independent clause in a complex sentence
3.2 2.7
Pronunciation: /meɪn klɔːz/ /; / /meɪn klɑːz/ (AmE), /meɪn klɔːz/ (BrE)
a clause in a complex sentence that can stand alone as a complete sentence

Examples

A main clause alone
Each complex sentence must have at least one main clause to be grammatically complete.
In the sentence, "I stayed home because it was raining," 'I stayed home' is the main clause.
The main clause of the sentence expresses the writer's opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: independent clause - a clause that can stand alone as a sentence
  • antonym: subordinate clause - a clause that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence
