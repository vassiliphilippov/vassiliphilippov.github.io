oculopharyngeal muscular dystrophy

noun
an inherited muscular disease affecting eyelid and throat muscles
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɑː.kjə.loʊ.fə.ˈrɪn.dʒiː.əl ˈmʌs.kjə.lər ˈdɪs.trə.fiː/ (AmE), /ɒ.kjʊ.lə.fə.ˈrɪn.dʒiː.əl ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə ˈdɪs.trə.fiː/ (BrE)
A rare inherited form of muscular dystrophy that begins in adulthood or middle age, first affecting the muscles of the eyelids and throat and progressing slowly, often causing difficulty swallowing.

Examples

Oculopharyngeal muscular dystrophy symptoms
Doctors diagnosed him with oculopharyngeal muscular dystrophy after he experienced drooping eyelids and difficulty eating.
Genetic counseling was recommended for families affected by oculopharyngeal muscular dystrophy.
Her father developed oculopharyngeal muscular dystrophy in his fifties, first noticing issues with swallowing.
