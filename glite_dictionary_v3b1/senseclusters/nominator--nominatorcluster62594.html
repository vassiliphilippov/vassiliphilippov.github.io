nominator

noun
person who proposes a candidate
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑː.məˌneɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɒm.ɪ.neɪ.tər / (BrE)
a person who formally proposes someone as a candidate for a position, appointment, or election

Examples

The nominator proposes
Each nominator may propose only one individual for membership each year.
The nominator must submit a letter explaining why the candidate deserves the award.
The role of a nominator is crucial in fair and transparent elections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nominee - a person who is proposed or formally entered as a candidate
