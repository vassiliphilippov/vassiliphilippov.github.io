weakened

adjective
diluted with water
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːkənd / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) made less strong or potent by the addition of water or another diluting agent.

Examples

Weakened drink
He drank a glass of weakened whiskey to avoid getting too intoxicated.
Laboratory instructions called for a weakened solution before use.
The soup tasted bland because it was weakened with too much water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrated - (of a liquid) made stronger by removing water or other diluting agents
  • synonym: diluted - (of a liquid) made weaker by adding water or another substance
