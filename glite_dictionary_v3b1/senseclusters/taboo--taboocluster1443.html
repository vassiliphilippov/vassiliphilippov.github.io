taboo

verb
to forbid as unacceptable
Pronunciation: / təˈbu /; / təˈbuː / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
to forbid or prohibit something because it is considered socially or culturally unacceptable

Examples

To taboo a subject
Some societies taboo discussing certain topics in public.
The community leaders decided to taboo the use of plastic bags to protect the environment.
In some cultures, it is common to taboo eating specific foods during religious festivals.
In some cultures, eating certain foods is tabooed for religious reasons.
The subject of money was tabooed during family gatherings.
They taboo any discussion of politics at the dinner table.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit or let something happen
  • antonym: permit - to allow something to happen
  • synonym: prohibit - to formally forbid something by law or rule
