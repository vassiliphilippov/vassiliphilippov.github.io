microcyte

noun
abnormally small red blood cell
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkrəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrəʊsʌɪt / (BrE)
an abnormally small red blood cell, typically less than 5 microns in diameter, often found in certain forms of anemia

Examples

A microcyte cell
In cases of thalassemia, patients often have many microcytes in their bloodstream.
The blood test revealed a high number of microcytes, suggesting a possible case of iron-deficiency anemia.
The presence of microcyte populations is a key indicator in diagnosing certain blood disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erythrocyte - a red blood cell
  • antonym: macrocyte - an abnormally large red blood cell
