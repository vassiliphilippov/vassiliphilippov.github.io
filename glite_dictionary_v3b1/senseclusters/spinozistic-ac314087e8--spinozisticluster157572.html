Spinozistic

adjective
of Spinoza's philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˌspɪnoʊˈzɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌspɪnəʊˈzɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the philosophy or ideas of Baruch Spinoza, especially his views on ethics, nature, or God

Examples

Spinozistic philosophy
The professor gave a Spinozistic interpretation of the relationship between nature and God.
Her worldview is deeply Spinozistic, emphasizing rational understanding and unity.
Many modern philosophers have been influenced by Spinozistic ideas about ethics and reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Cartesian - relating to the philosophy of René Descartes
  • synonym: Spinozan - relating to the philosophy of Spinoza
