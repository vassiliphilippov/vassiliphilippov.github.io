take it on the chin

verb
accept failure or criticism bravely
6.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /teɪk ɪt ɑːn ðə tʃɪn/ /; / /teɪk ɪt ɔːn ðə tʃɪn/ (AmE), /teɪk ɪt ɒn ðə tʃɪn/ (BrE)
to accept failure, criticism, or difficulty bravely and without complaining

Examples

To take it on the chin bravely
Even after losing the finals, she managed to take it on the chin and congratulated her opponent.
He always takes it on the chin when someone criticizes his work and tries to improve.
Our team has to take it on the chin after that unexpected defeat last night.
Even after losing the championship game, the team decided to take it on the chin and congratulated their opponents.
She knew she would face criticism, but she was prepared to take it on the chin.
When the company announced layoffs, many employees had to take it on the chin without complaint.
