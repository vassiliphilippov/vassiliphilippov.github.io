cubic yard

noun
a unit of volume (1 yard × 1 yard × 1 yard)
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkjuːbɪk ˈjɑrd / (AmE), / ˌkjuːbɪk ˈjɑːd / (BrE)
a unit of volume equal to the volume of a cube with sides of one yard (approximately 0.7646 cubic meters), commonly used for measuring materials such as soil, sand, or gravel

Examples

A cubic yard of soil
A cubic yard is often used to measure concrete for small construction jobs.
He ordered three cubic yards of topsoil for the garden project.
The truck can carry up to five cubic yards of gravel in one trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cubic meter - a metric unit of volume equal to a cube with edges one meter long
