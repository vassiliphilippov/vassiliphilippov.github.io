detraining

noun
getting off a train
Pronunciation: / diːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of getting off a train, especially when passengers disembark at a station or in an emergency

Examples

Detraining at the station
After the train stopped at the station, passengers began detraining in an orderly manner.
In case of an emergency, detraining procedures must be followed to ensure everyone's safety.
The conductor announced that detraining would take place on the left side of the train.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boarding - the act of getting onto a train, ship, or aircraft
  • synonym: disembarkation - the act of leaving a vehicle, especially a train, ship, or aircraft
