imperviously

adverb
in an impenetrable way
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɜːrviəsli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːviəsli / (BrE)
in a way that does not allow something to pass through or be affected

Examples

Sealed imperviously
The laboratory containers were sealed imperviously to prevent any contamination.
She remained imperviously calm despite the chaos around her.
The raincoat protected him imperviously from the heavy downpour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impenetrably - in a way that cannot be entered or passed through
  • antonym: perviously - in a way that allows something to pass through
