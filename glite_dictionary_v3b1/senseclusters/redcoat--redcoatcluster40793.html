redcoat

noun
British soldier in red uniform
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.kəʊt / (BrE)
A British soldier, especially one serving during the American Revolution, so called because of the red uniform coat.

Examples

A redcoat soldier
During the battle, a single redcoat charged across the field, drawing the attention of the colonial troops.
Many stories from the American Revolution mention redcoats marching in strict formation.
The distinctive uniform of a redcoat made British soldiers easy targets for the enemy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lobsterback - derogatory term for a British soldier during the American Revolution
