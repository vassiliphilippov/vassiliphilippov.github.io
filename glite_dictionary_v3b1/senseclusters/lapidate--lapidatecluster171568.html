lapidate

verb
throw stones at as punishment or ritual
Pronunciation: / ˈlæpɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlapɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to throw stones at (someone or something), especially as a form of punishment or ritual

Examples

To lapidate animals
Authorities warned pilgrims not to lapidate animals as part of the ceremony.
During the festival, participants lapidate large pillars to symbolize casting out evil.
The ancient law permitted crowds to lapidate those accused of severe crimes.

  • synonym: stone - to throw stones at (someone), usually to injure or kill them
