double obelisk

noun
typographical symbol for third footnote/cross-reference
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl oʊˈbɛ.lɪsk / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl əʊˈbɛl.ɪsk / (BrE)
a typographical symbol (‡) used to indicate a third footnote or cross-reference in printed text, after the asterisk and dagger have been used

Examples

The double obelisk symbol
Please note the information marked with a double obelisk at the bottom of the page.
The double obelisk is less commonly seen than the asterisk or dagger in most books.
When all other symbols were taken, the editor used the double obelisk to mark the third footnote.
