hotly

adverb
in a heated or intense manner
4.1 2.53/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ‘tli / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ’tli / (BrE)
in an intense or heated manner, showing strong emotions or energy

Examples

To hotly debate
She denied the accusations hotly, insisting that she was innocent.
The issue was hotly contested by experts on both sides.
The two candidates debated hotly during the final minutes of the televised discussion.

Synonyms

  • heatedly
    5.1 0.43/m
    heatedly is used for strong angry or passionate speech, while hotly means in an intense, emotionally charged way.
  • vehemently
    7.4 2.99/m
    vehemently is a forceful, often formal way to show strong feeling, while hotly is more general intense emotion.
  • passionately
    2.8 5.01/m
    passionately shows deep positive feeling or strong love, while hotly covers any intense or heated emotion.
  • furiously
    3.0 4.60/m
    furiously expresses strong anger and fast action, while hotly means intense emotion that is not only anger.
  • fiercely
    3.7 7.61/m
    fiercely suggests force or aggression in action or feeling, while hotly focuses on heated emotion or strong debate.
  • feverishly
    5.1 1.25/m
    feverishly describes hurried, restless energy as if ill or excited, while hotly means emotionally intense or strongly felt.
