timescale

noun
system for measuring time
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪmˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌskeɪl /; / ˈtaɪmskeɪl / (BrE)
a period or system of time used to describe, measure, or plan when events happen

Examples

A short timescale
The project was completed on a much shorter timescale than originally planned.
Geologists study the Earth's history using a detailed timescale of eras and periods.
Understanding the timescale of climate change helps scientists predict future trends.
The scientists studied climate change over a long timescale of thousands of years.
We need to agree on a realistic timescale for completing the new building.
Different projects may require different timescales depending on their complexity.
The construction will be finished within the agreed timescale.
We need to set a realistic timescale for completing the research project.
Due to delays, the timescale for the new software release has been extended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronology - the arrangement of events in the order they happened
  • antonym: deadline - the latest time by which something must be completed
  • synonym: duration - the length of time something lasts
  • synonym: timeline - a graphical representation of events in chronological order
