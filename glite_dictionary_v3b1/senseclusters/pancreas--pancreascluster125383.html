pancreas

noun
gland behind the stomach; secretes enzymes and hormones
Pronunciation: / ˈpæŋ.kri.əs / (AmE), / ˈpaŋ.krɪ.əs / (BrE)
a large gland behind the stomach that secretes digestive enzymes and hormones, including insulin

Examples

The pancreas gland
Diseases such as pancreatitis can severely affect the function of the pancreas.
Insulin, a hormone essential for regulating blood sugar, is produced by the pancreas.
The surgeon carefully removed the damaged pancreas during the operation.
