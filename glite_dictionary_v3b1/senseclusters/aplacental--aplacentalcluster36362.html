aplacental

adjective
having no placenta (biology)
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.pləˈsɛn.təl / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.pləˈsɛn.təl / (BrE)
(of animals) having no placenta.

Examples

Aplacental animals
Marsupials are considered aplacental because they do not develop a true placenta.
Monotremes such as the platypus are aplacental mammals.
The study focused on reproductive differences between placental and aplacental species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: placental - having a placenta; relating to mammals with a placenta
