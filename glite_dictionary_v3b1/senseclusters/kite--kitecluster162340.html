kite

verb
soar or fly like a kite
9.4 5.2
Pronunciation: /kaɪt/ (AmE), /kaɪt/ (BrE)
(of a person or thing) to soar or glide through the air like a kite.

Examples

Birds kite above
Seagulls kite along the beachfront, riding the ocean breeze.
She watched the small plane kiting over the ridge as the sun set in the distance.
The glider kited gracefully above the valley for more than an hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glide - move smoothly and continuously through the air without power
  • synonym: hover - remain suspended in one place in the air
  • antonym: sink - move downward, especially below the surface of a liquid or downward through air
