clink

verb
make a high, sharp sound
8.5 4.2
Pronunciation: /klɪŋk/ (AmE), /klɪŋk/ (BrE)
to make a short, sharp, metallic or glassy sound by striking objects together

Examples

Coins clink together
Coins clink against each other as I drop them into the jar.
Her keys clinked in her pocket with every step she took.
The glasses clink as we toast to the newlyweds.
She clinked her spoon against the cup while stirring her tea.
The keys clinked together in his pocket as he walked.
They raised their glasses and clinked them in celebration.
Everyone at the table raised their glasses to clink in honor of the bride and groom.
She gently clinked her glass with his before taking a sip of wine.
The guests smiled and clinked their champagne glasses to celebrate the new year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chink - produce a light ringing sound, especially metals or glass
  • degree higher: clang - to make a loud, resonant, metallic sound
  • degree higher: clank - make a loud, metallic sound
  • synonym: tinkle - to make a light, high, ringing sound
  • synonym: toast - raise glasses and drink as an expression of honor or good wishes
