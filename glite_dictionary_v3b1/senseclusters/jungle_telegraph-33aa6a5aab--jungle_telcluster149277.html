jungle telegraph

noun
informal rumor or news network
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌŋɡəl ˈtɛləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl ˈtelɪɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an informal network for the rapid exchange of news or rumors among people

Examples

The jungle telegraph spreads news
Rumors about the manager's resignation spread quickly through the jungle telegraph.
Thanks to the jungle telegraph, everyone heard about the surprise party before it happened.
You can't keep anything secret in this office—the jungle telegraph works too fast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grapevine - an informal means of transmitting information, gossip, or rumor
