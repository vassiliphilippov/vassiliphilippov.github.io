obelus

noun
pointed pillar or stake
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑbələs / (AmE), / ˈɒbɪləs / (BrE)
an archaic term for a pointed pillar, stake, or small obelisk, often used as a marker or monument

Examples

Stone obelus marker
The ancient stone obelus marked the entrance to the forgotten village.
Historians discovered a small obelus near the ruins, likely used as a boundary marker.
A weathered obelus stood in the center of the old town square for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obelisk - a tall, four-sided, narrow monument with a pointed top
  • synonym: stake - a strong wooden or metal post with a pointed end
