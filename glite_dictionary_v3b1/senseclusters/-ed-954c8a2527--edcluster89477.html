-ed

adjective
adjective-forming suffix
Pronunciation: / ɛd / (AmE), / ɛd / (BrE)
a suffix added to verbs to form adjectives that describe a state or feeling resulting from an action

Examples

-ed to show feelings
The word 'bored' uses the -ed suffix to show someone feels uninterested.
Adding -ed to 'excite' creates 'excited', describing a feeling of enthusiasm.
She felt very 'tired' after the long day, with -ed showing the result of her effort.

Related Terms

  • antonym: -ing - a suffix forming adjectives from verbs to indicate an ongoing action or state
