practice

verb
repeat an activity to improve skill
Pronunciation: / ˈpræktɪs /; / ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪs /; / ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
to do something repeatedly in order to learn or improve a skill

Examples

To practice every day
Athletes practice regularly to maintain their performance.
If you practice speaking out loud, your pronunciation will get better.
She practices the piano every evening to improve her technique.
If you practice regularly, your piano skills will improve.
She practices her speech every evening before the big event.
The band practiced for hours to perfect their performance.
Before the big game, the team practised their passing and shooting skills for hours.
She spends an hour each morning practising her French pronunciation.
You should practise every day if you want to master the guitar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drill - to train or practice repeatedly
  • degree lower: memorize - to learn something so well that you can remember it exactly
  • degree higher: perfect - to make something as good as it can be through repeated practice
  • synonym: practice - to do something repeatedly to improve skill (American English spelling)
  • synonym: rehearse - to prepare for a public performance by practicing
