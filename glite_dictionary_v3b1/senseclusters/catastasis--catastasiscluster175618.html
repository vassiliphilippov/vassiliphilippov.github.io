catastasis

noun
pre-climax scene setting
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈtæstəsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈtæstəsɪs / (BrE)
the part of a drama or speech that sets the scene and develops the conflict before the climax

Examples

Dramatic catastasis
In classical plays, the catastasis often reveals the main conflict before the story reaches its peak.
The director emphasized the catastasis to help the audience understand the characters' motivations.
Literary scholars analyze the catastasis to see how tension is built before the climax.

Related Terms

  • antonym: climax - the most intense or important point of a story
  • synonym: exposition - the introduction of background information in a story
