Carthusian

noun
member of the Carthusian religious order
Pronunciation: / kɑːrˈθuːʒən / (AmE), / kɑːˈθjuːziən / (BrE)
a member of the Carthusian order, a contemplative Roman Catholic religious order founded by St Bruno in 1084.

Examples

A Carthusian monk
A prominent Carthusian philosopher contributed to medieval theological debates.
The Carthusian devoted his life to prayer and solitude in the monastery.
Tours of the ancient cloister highlight where Carthusian monks once lived in silence.
