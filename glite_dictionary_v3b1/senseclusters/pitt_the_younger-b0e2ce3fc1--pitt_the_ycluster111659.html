Pitt the Younger

proper noun
British Prime Minister (1759–1806)
Pronunciation: / pɪt ði ˈjʌŋɚ / (AmE), / pɪt ðə ˈjʌŋə / (BrE)
(1759–1806) The British Prime Minister and son of Pitt the Elder, known for his leadership during the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic wars.

Examples

Pitt the Younger Prime Minister
Pitt the Younger became Prime Minister of Britain at the remarkably young age of 24.
Historians often compare the policies of Pitt the Younger to those of his famous father.
The reforms introduced by Pitt the Younger had a lasting impact on the British financial system.
