tyrosinase

noun
melanin-producing enzyme
Pronunciation: / taɪˈrɑːsɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / tɪˈrɒsɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that helps produce melanin, influencing the color of skin, hair, and eyes

Examples

Tyrosinase makes melanin
A lack of tyrosinase can result in lighter skin or hair.
Scientists study tyrosinase to better understand conditions like albinism.
The activity of tyrosinase is essential for the production of melanin in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that speeds up chemical reactions in the body
  • synonym: melanin - a natural pigment that gives color to skin, hair, and eyes
