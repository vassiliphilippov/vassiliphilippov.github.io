semiwoody

adjective
partly woody
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈwʊdi / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈwʊdi / (BrE)
partly but not fully woody in structure, especially describing plant stems that are somewhat rigid but not completely hard

Examples

Semiwoody stems
The garden features several semiwoody plants that survive mild winters without damage.
Some perennials develop semiwoody stems as they mature, making them more resilient.
The botanist explained that semiwoody shrubs are common in Mediterranean climates.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: herbaceous - having soft, non-woody stems
  • degree higher: woody - having a hard, rigid structure like wood
