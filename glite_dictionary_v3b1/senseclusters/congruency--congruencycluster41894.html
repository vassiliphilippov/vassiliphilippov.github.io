congruency

noun
geometric equivalence
Pronunciation: / kənˈɡruːənsi / (AmE), / kənˈɡruːənsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of two geometric figures having the same shape and size, so one can be exactly mapped onto the other

Examples

Triangle congruency
The teacher explained congruency by showing how two triangles could be perfectly overlapped.
In geometry, congruency is used to prove that two shapes are exactly the same in size and form.
The students checked the congruency of the rectangles by measuring their sides and angles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congruence - the quality of being in agreement or having the same shape and size in geometry
  • antonym: similarity - the state of two figures having the same shape but not necessarily the same size
