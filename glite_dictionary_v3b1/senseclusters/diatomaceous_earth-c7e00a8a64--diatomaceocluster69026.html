diatomaceous earth

noun
soft, powdery soil from diatom fossils
8.1 6.4
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ə.tə.ˈmeɪ.ʃəs ɜːrθ/ (AmE), /daɪ.ə.tə.ˈmeɪ.ʃəs ɜːθ/ (BrE)
A soft, light, powdery soil made from the fossilized remains of diatoms, often used as a filtering material or insecticide.

Examples

diatomaceous earth powder
Gardeners often use diatomaceous earth to control pests without harmful chemicals.
Many swimming pool filters rely on diatomaceous earth because of its fine filtration properties.
The laboratory technician added a layer of diatomaceous earth to the column before beginning the experiment.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: fuller's earth - a different fine clay material used for filtering and cleaning
