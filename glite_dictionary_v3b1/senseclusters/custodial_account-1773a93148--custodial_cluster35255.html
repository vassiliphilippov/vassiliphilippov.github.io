custodial account

noun
account managed for a minor by an adult
6.4 5.0
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈstoʊ.diː.əl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ /; / /kʌ.ˈstoʊ.diː.əl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (AmE), /kə.ˈstəʊ.diː.əl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ /; / /kʌ.ˈstəʊ.diː.əl ə.ˈkaʊnt/ (BrE)
an account held by a brokerage or financial institution for a minor, managed by an adult guardian until the minor reaches legal age

Examples

A custodial account for a child
Funds in a custodial account legally belong to the minor but are overseen by an adult until the child comes of age.
Many grandparents choose to open a custodial account as a gift for their grandchildren's future.
The parents set up a custodial account to save for their daughter's college expenses.
