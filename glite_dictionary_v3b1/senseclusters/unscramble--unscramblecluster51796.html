unscramble

verb
make clear or intelligible again
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈskræmbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈskræmbəl / (BrE)
to arrange things that are mixed up or confused so that they are in the correct order or easy to understand again

Examples

To unscramble the message
After the storm, engineers had to unscramble the network signals for TV broadcasts.
It took her several minutes to unscramble the encrypted text file.
The detective worked hard to unscramble the clues and solve the mystery.
Can you unscramble these letters to make a word?
The teacher asked the students to unscramble the sentences for their homework.
It took her a while to unscramble the puzzle pieces and see the full picture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decode - to convert a coded message into ordinary language
  • antonym: scramble - to mix up or make difficult to understand
  • synonym: sort - to arrange things in a particular order
