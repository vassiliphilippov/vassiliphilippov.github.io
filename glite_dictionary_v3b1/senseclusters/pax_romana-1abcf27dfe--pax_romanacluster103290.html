Pax Romana

noun
period of peace in the Roman Empire
8.6
Pronunciation: /pæks roʊ.ˈmɑː.nə/ /; / /pæks rə.ˈmɑː.nə/ (AmE), /pæks rəʊ.ˈmɑː.nə/ /; / /pæks rə.ˈmɑː.nə/ (BrE)
the long period of relative peace and stability throughout the Roman Empire, lasting from 27 BC to AD 180 under Roman rule

Examples

The Pax Romana period
During the Pax Romana, Roman law and order were effectively maintained over vast territories.
Historians often cite the Pax Romana as a time of prosperity and expansion for the Roman Empire.
The stability provided by the Pax Romana encouraged trade and cultural exchange across the Mediterranean region.
