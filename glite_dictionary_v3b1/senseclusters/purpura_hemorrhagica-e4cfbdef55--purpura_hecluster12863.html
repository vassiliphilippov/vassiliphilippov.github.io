purpura hemorrhagica

noun
type of purpura with low platelet count
9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈpjʊr.ə/ /; / /pɜːr.ˈpjʊr.ə/ (AmE), /pɜː.ˈpjʊə.rə/ /; / /pə.ˈpjʊə.rə/ (BrE)
A form of purpura characterized by widespread bleeding into the skin and mucous membranes, associated with a reduction in circulating blood platelets.

Examples

Purpura hemorrhagica skin bleeding
The patient was diagnosed with purpura hemorrhagica after developing widespread skin lesions and a low platelet count.
Treatment for purpura hemorrhagica often involves addressing the underlying cause of the platelet deficiency.
Veterinarians are particularly alert to purpura hemorrhagica in horses following infections.
