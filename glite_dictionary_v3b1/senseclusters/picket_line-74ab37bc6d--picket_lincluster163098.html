picket line

noun
line of striking or protesting workers
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪkɪt laɪn /; / ˈpɪk.ɪt ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt laɪn /; / ˈpɪk.ɪt ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a group or line of people, usually workers, standing or marching outside a place (such as a workplace) to protest or prevent others from entering during a strike or demonstration

Examples

The picket line protest
Crossing the picket line during a strike is seen as controversial by many employees.
Police kept a close eye on the picket line to ensure the demonstration remained peaceful.
The workers formed a picket line outside the factory gates to protest wage cuts.
Crossing a picket line is often seen as breaking solidarity with fellow workers.
Police were called to maintain order near the picket line.
The striking workers formed a picket line in front of the factory gate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protest - the act of expressing disagreement or objection
  • synonym: strike - an organized stoppage of work as a form of protest
