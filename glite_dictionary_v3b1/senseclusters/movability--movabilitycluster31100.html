movability

noun
the quality of being movable
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being movable or capable of being moved or rearranged.

Examples

The movability of walls
Architects considered the movability of walls to create a flexible living area.
The movability of the furniture allows the space to be easily reconfigured for different events.
The exhibition relied on the movability of its modular display units.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immovability - the quality of being impossible to move
  • synonym: mobility - the ability to move or be moved easily
