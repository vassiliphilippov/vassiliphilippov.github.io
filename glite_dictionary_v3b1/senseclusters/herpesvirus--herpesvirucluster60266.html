herpesvirus

noun
virus causing infections
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrpiːsˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːzˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a type of virus that can cause infections such as cold sores, chickenpox, and shingles in humans and animals

Examples

The herpesvirus family
The herpesvirus family includes viruses that cause cold sores and chickenpox.
Doctors are researching new treatments for herpesvirus infections in humans.
A herpesvirus can remain dormant in the body and reactivate later in life.
