epidemic

adjective
affecting many at once; rapidly spreading
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpəˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease or of something resembling a disease) affecting many people in a community at the same time.

Examples

Epidemic rumors
Rumors became epidemic during the political scandal.
The city faced an epidemic crisis of measles among young children.
Unemployment was almost epidemic in certain parts of the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endemic - (of a disease) regularly found in a particular area or group
  • degree higher: pandemic - (of a disease) prevalent over a whole country or the world
