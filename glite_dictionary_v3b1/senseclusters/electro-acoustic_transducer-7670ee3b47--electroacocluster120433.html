electro-acoustic transducer

noun
device converting electrical and acoustic energy
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊ əˈkuːstɪk trænzˈduːsər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊ əˈkuːstɪk trænzˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
A device that converts electrical energy into sound (acoustic energy) or sound into electrical energy.

Examples

An electro-acoustic transducer device
A microphone is a type of electro-acoustic transducer that converts sound waves into electrical signals.
Researchers tested the electro-acoustic transducer in both underwater and air environments.
The new headphones use an advanced electro-acoustic transducer to deliver superior sound quality.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: loudspeaker - a device that converts electrical signals into sound
  • degree lower: microphone - a device that converts sound into an electrical signal
  • synonym: transducer - a device that converts one type of energy to another
