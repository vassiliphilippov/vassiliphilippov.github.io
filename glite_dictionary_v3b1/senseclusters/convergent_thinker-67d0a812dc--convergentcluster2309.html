convergent thinker

noun
person who solves problems by synthesizing information
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝːdʒənt ˈθɪŋkər / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒənt ˈθɪŋkə / (BrE)
a person who tends to focus on a given problem as stated and synthesizes information to reach a single, correct solution

Examples

A convergent thinker solves problems
As a convergent thinker, she excelled at finding the most practical solution to complex problems.
Many math teachers value students who are strong convergent thinkers for their analytical skills.
Unlike his creative peers, he preferred the structured approach of a convergent thinker.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divergent thinker - a person who generates creative ideas by exploring many possible solutions
