isovolumetric process

noun
thermodynamic process at constant volume
9.5 8.0
Pronunciation: /aɪ.sə.vɑːl.jə.ˈme.trɪk ˈprɑː.ses/ /; / /aɪ.sə.vɑːl.jə.ˈme.trɪk ˈprɑː.səs/ /; / /aɪ.soʊ.vɑːl.jə.ˈme.trɪk ˈprɑː.ses/ (AmE), /aɪ.sə.vɒl.jə.ˈme.trɪk ˈprɒ.ses/ /; / /aɪ.sə.vɒl.jə.ˈme.trɪk ˈprɒ.səs/ /; / /aɪ.sə.vɒl.jə.ˈme.trɪk ˈprəʊ.ses/ (BrE)
a thermodynamic process in which the volume of a closed system remains constant while other properties, such as pressure or temperature, may change

Examples

An isovolumetric process in gas
During an isovolumetric process, the pressure of the gas may change, but its volume remains fixed.
Heating a gas in a sealed rigid container is an example of an isovolumetric process.
The concept of an isovolumetric process is fundamental in understanding how engines operate under constant volume conditions.
