scabrous

adjective
rough or scaly to the touch
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈskæbɹəs / (AmE), / ˈskæbɹəs / (BrE)
rough to the touch; having a surface covered with small projections or scales

Examples

Scabrous skin
A scabrous patch on the lizard’s skin indicated a possible infection.
Some tree trunks are scabrous, making them easy to identify by touch alone.
The scabrous leaves of the plant felt harsh against my fingers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, free from roughness or projections
