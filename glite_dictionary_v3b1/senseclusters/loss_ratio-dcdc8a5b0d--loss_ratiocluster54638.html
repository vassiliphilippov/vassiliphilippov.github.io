loss ratio

noun
insurance: ratio of claims paid to premiums received
Pronunciation: / lɔs ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / lɒs ˈreɪʃiəʊ / (BrE)
the proportion of insurance claims paid by a company in a given period compared to the premiums it has received in that period

Examples

High loss ratio
A high loss ratio can indicate financial problems for insurers, as it suggests claims outweigh premium income.
Regulators often examine the loss ratio to assess the stability of an insurance company.
The insurance company's loss ratio increased significantly after a year of severe storms.
