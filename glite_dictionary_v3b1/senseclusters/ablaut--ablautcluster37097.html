ablaut

noun
vowel change in related words
Pronunciation: / ˈæbˌlaʊt / (AmE), / ˈablaʊt / (BrE)
a change in the quality or length of a vowel in related words or forms to indicate grammatical distinctions, as seen in 'sing', 'sang', 'sung'.

Examples

Ablaut vowel change
In Indo-European languages, ablaut is a common way of forming grammatical contrasts.
Many English verbs, such as 'swim', 'swam', and 'swum', show ablaut patterns.
The study of ablaut helps explain irregular verb forms in major European languages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: umlaut - a change of a vowel influenced by a following vowel, especially in Germanic languages
