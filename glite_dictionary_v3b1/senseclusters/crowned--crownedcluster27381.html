crowned

adjective
having a crown or crown-like feature
4.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkraÊŠnd / (AmE), / ËˆkraÊŠnd / (BrE)
having a crown, or something resembling a crown, on top; often used in combination to describe something topped or adorned as with a crown.

Examples

Crowned head
She wore a crowned tiara at the gala event.
The city was overlooked by a hill crowned with ancient ruins.
The statue is crowned with a wreath symbolizing victory.

Synonyms

  • capped
    3.9 0.00/m
    capped means covered on top like with a cap or protective layer, while crowned suggests a decorative crown-like top.
  • topped
    3.1 0.00/m
    topped describes having a particular thing on the upper surface, while crowned implies a crown-like or ornamental top.
  • surmounted
    6.2 0.00/m
    surmounted means something is placed on top of another in a formal or structural way, while crowned suggests a decorative crown-like top.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uncrowned - not having a crown; not formally invested with a crown
