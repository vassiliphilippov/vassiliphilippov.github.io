liquor

noun
distilled alcoholic drink
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
A strong alcoholic drink that is distilled rather than fermented, such as whiskey, vodka, or rum.

Examples

A glass of liquor
She doesn't drink beer or wine, but she sometimes enjoys liquor at parties.
Some countries place high taxes on imported liquor.
The bartender poured a shot of liquor into the glass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beer - alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain, not distilled
  • synonym: spirit - strong distilled alcoholic drink
  • antonym: wine - alcoholic beverage made from fermented fruit, not distilled
