Winchester

noun
a kind of lever-action rifle
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪntʃɪstər / (AmE), / ˈwɪntʃɪstə / (BrE)
a type of lever-action repeating rifle, originally made by the Winchester Repeating Arms Company, noted especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

A Winchester rifle
He proudly displayed his antique Winchester above the fireplace.
She learned to shoot using a classic Winchester on her uncle's ranch.
The old Winchester was passed down through several generations in the family.
