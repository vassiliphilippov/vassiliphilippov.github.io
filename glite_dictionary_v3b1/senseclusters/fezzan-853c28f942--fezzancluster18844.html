Fezzan

proper noun
Libyan desert region
Pronunciation: / fɛˈzæn / (AmE), / fɛˈzæn / (BrE)
a desert region in southwestern Libya, known for its oases, historical significance, and role as a former administrative territory

Examples

Fezzan desert region
The ancient trade routes passed through Fezzan on their way across the Sahara.
Many travelers are fascinated by the unique landscapes of Fezzan and its remote oases.
Archaeologists have discovered important artifacts in Fezzan dating back thousands of years.
The Sahara Desert covers much of Fezzan, making it one of the driest regions in Libya.
Historically, Fezzan was an important center for trans-Saharan trade routes.
After World War II, the French established control of parts of Fezzan and administered them separately.
