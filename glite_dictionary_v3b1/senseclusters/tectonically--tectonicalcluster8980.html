tectonically

adverb
on a massive scale
Pronunciation: / tɛkˈtɑnɪkli / (AmE), / tɛkˈtɒnɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that causes or involves very large-scale, fundamental, or dramatic changes, especially in social, political, or economic structures.

Examples

Tectonically changed industry
The industry has changed tectonically in the past decade due to new technology.
Political alliances shifted tectonically after the unexpected election results.
The company restructured tectonically, affecting every department and employee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dramatically - in a way that is sudden or extreme
  • antonym: incrementally - in small or gradual steps
