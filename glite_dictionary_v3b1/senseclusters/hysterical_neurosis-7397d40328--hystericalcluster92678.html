hysterical neurosis

noun
neurotic disorder with emotional and sensory/motor disturbances
7.0 7.9
Pronunciation: /hɪ.ˈste.rɪ.kəl nʊr.ˈoʊ.səs/ /; / /hɪ.ˈste.rɪ.kəl njuːr.ˈoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /hɪ.ˈste.rɪ.kəl njuː.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /hɪ.ˈste.rɪ.kəl njʊ.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A neurotic disorder marked by sudden emotional outbursts and disturbances in sensory or motor functions.

Examples

Hysterical neurosis symptoms
Early psychiatrists often categorized unexplained physical ailments as hysterical neurosis.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hysterical neurosis after ruling out organic causes for her symptoms.
Treatment for hysterical neurosis has changed significantly since the term was widely used in the 20th century.
