creolization

noun
pidgin to creole process
7.9
Pronunciation: /kriː.oʊ.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kriː.əʊ.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which elements from different languages blend to form a new, stable language (a creole), often through contact between communities

Examples

Creolization of languages
The creolization of language often results from contact between different cultural groups.
Linguists study creolization to understand how new languages develop in diverse societies.
The rapid creolization in the region led to the emergence of a unique local dialect.
The creolization of languages in the Caribbean resulted in unique dialects spoken today.
Researchers observed creolization as communities with different native tongues interacted over generations.
The rapid creolization of speech on the island reflected its diverse cultural influences.
