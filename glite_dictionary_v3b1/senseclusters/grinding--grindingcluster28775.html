the process by which rock particles are worn down by friction caused by water, wind, or ice

Examples

Grinding of rocks
Erosion by wind involves the grinding of sand particles against rock surfaces.
Over time, the constant grinding of water smooths out sharp corners of stones in the riverbed.
The slow grinding of rocks by glaciers shapes many mountain valleys.

Synonyms

  • abrasion
    6.0 0.17/m
    abrasion is the act of rocks rubbing and wearing a surface in streams or wind, while grinding is the bit by bit wearing of particles.
  • corrasion
    9.9 0.00/m
    corrasion is mechanical wearing by particles carried in water or wind, while grinding is the rubbing that makes rock pieces smaller.
  • attrition
    7.4 0.31/m
    attrition is when rocks wear down by hitting and rubbing each other during transport, while grinding focuses on friction by water, wind, or ice.
  • detrition
    9.9 0.00/m
    detrition is the gradual wearing away of materials by rubbing, often in machines or nature, while grinding refers to natural rock particle wear.
  • wearing away
    5.1 0.03/m
    wearing away describes any slow erosion of a surface by water, wind, or use, while grinding means rock particles are worn by friction.
