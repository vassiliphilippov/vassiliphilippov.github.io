diffidently

adverb
in a shy or lacking-confidence manner
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪfɪdəntli / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪdəntli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a lack of confidence or shyness; not assertively

Examples

To speak diffidently
He spoke diffidently during the meeting, barely raising his voice above a whisper.
She answered the interviewer’s questions diffidently, unsure of her qualifications.
The young artist diffidently displayed his paintings at the community center.
