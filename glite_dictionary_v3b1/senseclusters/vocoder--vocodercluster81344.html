vocoder

noun
speech processing device
Pronunciation: / voʊˈkoʊdər / (AmE), / vəʊˈkəʊdə / (BrE)
an electronic device or software that processes or changes the human voice, often to create special effects like a robotic sound

Examples

A vocoder device
The musician used a vocoder to create robotic-sounding vocals for the song.
Modern smartphones sometimes use a vocoder to compress voice data for calls.
Engineers improved the vocoder to make speech sound clearer over long-distance connections.
The musician used a vocoder to give his voice a futuristic, robotic sound in the performance.
Many electronic songs feature vocals processed through a vocoder for a unique effect.
She experimented with a vocoder to blend her singing with synthesized sounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autotune - software that corrects or alters the pitch of a voice
  • antonym: decoder - a device or program that converts encoded data back into its original form
  • synonym: synthesizer - an electronic instrument that generates sounds, often imitating musical instruments or voices
