Yggdrasil

noun
cosmic tree in Norse mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈɪɡ.drəˌsɪl / (AmE), / ˈʏɡ.dræs.ɪl / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) the immense ash tree connecting the nine worlds, whose branches and roots hold together the heavens, earth, and the underworld.

Examples

The Yggdrasil tree
In Norse legends, Yggdrasil stands at the center of the cosmos, linking all the realms.
Many modern fantasy stories draw inspiration from the myth of Yggdrasil.
The roots of Yggdrasil are said to reach into heaven, earth, and the underworld.
