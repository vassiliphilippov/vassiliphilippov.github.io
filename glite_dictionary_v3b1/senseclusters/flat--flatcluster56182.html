flat

verb
to sing off-key
8.0
Pronunciation: /flæt/ (AmE), /flæt/ (BrE)
to sing or play a musical note lower than the correct pitch, especially unintentionally.

Examples

To flat a note
The choir was flatting on the last verse, so the director stopped rehearsal to correct pitch.
If you keep flatting that note, try listening more closely to the piano.
She realized she was flatting during her solo and quickly adjusted her pitch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sharp - to sing or play a note higher than the correct pitch
  • synonym: sing off-key - to sing with incorrect pitch, not matching the intended notes
