bound

adjective
obligated or compelled (by law, duty, or oath)
6.0 1.36/m
Pronunciation: / baÊŠnd / (AmE), / baÊŠnd / (BrE)
obligated or required to do something, especially by law, duty, or contract

Examples

Bound by law
As a sworn officer, he is bound to uphold the law at all times.
She felt morally bound to tell the truth, no matter the consequences.
The committee members are bound by strict confidentiality agreements.
As citizens, we are bound by law to pay taxes honestly.
He felt bound by his promise to help his friend move on Saturday.
She is bound by contract to work for the company for two more years.

Synonyms

  • obligated
    4.2 0.62/m
    obligated means legally or morally required to act, while bound suggests a formal duty often tied to law, contract, or promise.
  • required
    1.4 0.00/m
    required is officially demanded by law or rules, while bound refers to being obligated by law, duty, or contract.
  • mandatory
    3.8 16/m
    mandatory is set by law or official rules and leaves no choice, while bound means obligated by law, duty, or contract.
  • compulsory
    4.2 4.36/m
    compulsory is required by law or rule and often applies to people or actions, while bound means being under an obligation.
  • obligatory
    5.3 2.57/m
    obligatory describes something that rules expect others to follow, while bound describes a person obliged by law, duty, or contract.
  • obliged
    4.4 0.00/m
    obliged often means feeling you must act for moral or social reasons, while bound stresses a formal duty or legal requirement.
  • duty-bound
    5.8 0.36/m
    duty-bound shows a strong personal sense of responsibility, while bound covers legal, contractual, or duty-based obligations more generally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not bound or constrained; at liberty
  • synonym: obligated - required by law, contract, or moral duty
