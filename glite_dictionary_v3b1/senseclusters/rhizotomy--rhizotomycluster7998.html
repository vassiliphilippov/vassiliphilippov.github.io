rhizotomy

noun
surgical cutting of nerve roots
9.9
Pronunciation: / raɪˈzɑtəmi / (AmE), / raɪˈzɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which spinal nerve roots are severed, typically to relieve pain or prevent severe muscle spasms

Examples

A rhizotomy surgery
A rhizotomy is sometimes considered when other treatments for nerve pain have failed.
After undergoing a rhizotomy, she experienced significant improvement in her muscle spasms.
The doctor recommended a rhizotomy to help manage the patient’s chronic back pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laminectomy - surgical removal of a vertebral lamina
  • synonym: neurectomy - surgical removal or cutting of a nerve
