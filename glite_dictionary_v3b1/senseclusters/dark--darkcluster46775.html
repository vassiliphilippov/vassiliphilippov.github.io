dark

adjective
lacking light; shaded or black
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɑrk / (AmE), / dɑːk / (BrE)
having little or no light, not bright; shaded or black.

Examples

Dark night sky
As the storm approached, the sky turned dark and heavy clouds gathered overhead.
She prefers reading in a well-lit room rather than in a dark space.
We couldn't see anything in the dark alley behind the building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - full of light; shining strongly
  • degree lower: dim - lacking in brightness; not clearly seen or illuminated
