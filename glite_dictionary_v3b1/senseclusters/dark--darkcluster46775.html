dark

adjective
lacking light; shaded or black
1.0 105/m
Pronunciation: / d…ërk / (AmE), / d…ëÀêk / (BrE)
having little or no light, not bright; shaded or black.

Examples

Dark night sky
As the storm approached, the sky turned dark and heavy clouds gathered overhead.
She prefers reading in a well-lit room rather than in a dark space.
We couldn't see anything in the dark alley behind the building.

Synonyms

  • lightless
    5.1 0.36/m
    lightless is completely without any light, while dark simply means having little or no light or being shaded.
  • shadowed
    3.5 0.00/m
    shadowed is made darker by actual shadows on it, while dark describes low light or blackness generally.
  • shadowy
    3.3 2.45/m
    shadowy is partly hidden in patches of shade and can seem unclear, while dark means low light or black.
