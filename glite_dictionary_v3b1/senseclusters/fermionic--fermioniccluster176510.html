fermionic

adjective
of fermions
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɚˈmɑːnɪk / (AmE), / fɜːˈmɪɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or having the properties of a fermion, a type of subatomic particle in physics

Examples

Fermionic particles
Electrons are examples of particles with fermionic behavior.
The fermionic nature of certain particles determines how they interact in quantum systems.
Physicists study fermionic systems to understand the structure of matter at the smallest scales.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bosonic - relating to or having the properties of a boson
