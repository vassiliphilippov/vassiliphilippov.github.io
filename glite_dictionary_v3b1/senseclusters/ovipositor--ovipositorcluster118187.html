ovipositor

noun
egg-laying organ in female insects/fish
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɪˈpɑː.zɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vɪˈpɒz.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
a tubular organ at the end of the abdomen of many female insects and some fishes, used for laying eggs

Examples

The ovipositor organ
Scientists examined the structure of the ovipositor under the microscope.
Some fish have a specialized ovipositor to help place eggs in hidden spots.
The female wasp uses her ovipositor to deposit eggs inside host insects.
