velvet plant

noun
plant of the Verbascum genus with woolly leaves and spikes of flowers
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛlvət plænt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt plɑːnt / (BrE)
any of various plants of the genus Verbascum that have large, usually woolly leaves and spikes of yellow, white, or purplish flowers

Examples

Velvet plant with yellow flowers
Many herbalists use parts of the velvet plant in traditional remedies.
The velvet plant is often grown in gardens for its striking flower spikes and soft leaves.
We saw a field full of blooming velvet plants during our hike.
