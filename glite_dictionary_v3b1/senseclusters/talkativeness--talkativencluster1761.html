talkativeness

noun
the quality of talking a lot
4.7 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îk…ôt…™vn…ôs / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀêk…ôt…™vn…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of talking a lot; the tendency to speak frequently or at length

Examples

His talkativeness increased
Despite his usual talkativeness, he was unusually quiet during the interview.
Excessive talkativeness can be distracting during meetings when focus is required.
Her talkativeness made her popular at parties, where she could always start a conversation with anyone.

Synonyms

  • loquaciousness
    9.9 0.05/m
    loquaciousness is a formal word for being very talkative and using many words, while talkativeness is general tendency to speak often.
  • loquacity
    9.9 0.06/m
    loquacity means the habit of using many words and speaking a lot, while talkativeness is a broader, everyday tendency to speak.
  • garrulousness
    8.9 0.03/m
    garrulousness is talking too much about small, unimportant things, while talkativeness simply means speaking often or at length.
  • garrulity
    9.9 0.03/m
    garrulity is an old or formal term for excessive, trivial talking, while talkativeness is a neutral word for speaking a lot.
  • chattiness
    4.4 0.01/m
    chattiness is friendly, informal talking in a lively way, while talkativeness can be neutral and not always social.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loquacity - the quality of being very talkative; loquaciousness
  • antonym: reticence - the quality of being reserved or reluctant to speak
