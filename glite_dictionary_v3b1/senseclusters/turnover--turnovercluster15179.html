turnover

noun
rate at which employees are replaced
4.2 4.44/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ùÀênÀåo äv…ö / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀênÀå…ô äv…ô / (BrE)
the rate at which employees leave a workforce and are replaced during a particular period of time

Examples

Staff turnover
Reducing employee turnover is a key goal for many human resources departments.
Seasonal jobs often have a naturally high turnover compared to permanent positions.
The company's high turnover rate made it difficult to maintain consistent quality.

Synonyms

  • employee turnover
    3.3 0.18/m
    employee turnover describes staff leaving and replacement in a company, while turnover is a broader term used in different contexts.
  • turnover rate
    4.0 0.66/m
    turnover rate emphasizes the measured percentage of staff change in a period, while turnover names the general phenomenon of staff change.
  • churn
    5.1 0.25/m
    churn mainly names customers or users leaving a service and appears in business contexts, while turnover refers to employees leaving jobs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attrition - gradual reduction of work force by employees leaving and not being replaced
  • antonym: retention - the ability to keep employees in an organization
