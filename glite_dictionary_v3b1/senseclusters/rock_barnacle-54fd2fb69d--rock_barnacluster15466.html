rock barnacle

noun
barnacle found on rocks in intertidal areas
8.1
Pronunciation: / rɑk ˈbɑrnəkəl / (AmE), / rɒk ˈbɑːnəkəl / (BrE)
a species of barnacle that attaches itself to rocks, especially in intertidal zones

Examples

Rock barnacles on rocks
Children collected shells and noticed many rock barnacles attached to the rocks.
Scientists studied how rock barnacles survive exposure to air during low tide.
The rock barnacle thrives on wave-swept shores, clinging tightly to boulders.
