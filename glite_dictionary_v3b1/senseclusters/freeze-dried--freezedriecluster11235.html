freeze-dried

adjective
preserved by freeze-drying
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːz ˌdraɪd /; / ˈfriːzˌdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈfriːz ˌdraɪd /; / ˈfriːzˌdraɪd / (BrE)
(of food or other material) preserved by removing moisture through freezing and then drying in a vacuum

Examples

Freeze-dried food
Instant coffee is often made from freeze-dried granules.
She packed some freeze-dried meals for the hiking trip.
The freeze-dried strawberries tasted almost as good as fresh ones.
Many campers prefer freeze-dried meals because they are lightweight and easy to prepare.
The laboratory used freeze-dried serum samples to ensure accurate testing.
The museum preserves rare plant specimens in a freeze-dried state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dehydrated - (of food) having had its moisture removed, typically by heat
  • antonym: fresh - (of food) not preserved; recently obtained or prepared
  • synonym: lyophilized - dried by freeze-drying; especially of biological material
