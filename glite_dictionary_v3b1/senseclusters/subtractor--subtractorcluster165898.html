subtractor

noun
electronic circuit for subtraction
Pronunciation: / səbˈtræktər / (AmE), / səbˈtræktə / (BrE)
An electronic circuit that performs subtraction between two numbers, especially binary numbers, in a computing or digital system.

Examples

Electronic subtractor circuit
Engineers designed a new high-speed subtractor to improve calculation efficiency.
The subtractor is an essential part of the arithmetic logic unit in a computer processor.
The digital circuit includes both an adder and a subtractor for various operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adder - An electronic circuit that performs addition of binary numbers.
