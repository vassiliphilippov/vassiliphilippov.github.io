decohesion

noun
loss of unity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkoʊˈhiːʒən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəʊˈhiːʒən / (BrE)
the process or state in which parts that were held together lose their unity and separate

Examples

The decohesion of metal
The decohesion of the metal led to cracks in the structure.
Repeated stress caused decohesion between the layers of the material.
Scientists studied the decohesion of cells under different chemical treatments.
Engineers studied the decohesion of the adhesive under extreme temperatures.
Microscopic decohesion at the paint-metal interface caused the protective coating to peel off.
Repeated stress led to decohesion between the layers of the composite material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delamination - the separation of layers in a material
  • synonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart
