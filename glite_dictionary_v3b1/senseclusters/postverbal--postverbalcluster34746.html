postverbal

adjective
after the verb
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
occurring or placed after the verb in a sentence

Examples

A postverbal position
In English, direct objects are usually postverbal and follow the main verb.
The linguist explained that adverbs can have postverbal positions in some languages.
In the sentence 'They built a house,' 'a house' is a postverbal noun phrase.
