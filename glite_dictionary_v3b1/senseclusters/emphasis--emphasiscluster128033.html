emphasis

noun
special importance or intensity in expression
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmfəsɪs /; / ˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (BrE)
special importance, value, or attention given to something to make it stand out or be noticed

Examples

Strong emphasis on safety
His denial carried an unusual emphasis that made everyone believe him.
She spoke with great emphasis, making her point clear to everyone in the room.
The teacher placed strong emphasis on the importance of regular study habits.
By repeating the word, she gave extra emphasis to her point.
In English, word order can change the emphasis of a sentence.
The teacher placed emphasis on the importance of good study habits.
Her decorations add a bold emphasis to the otherwise simple design scheme.
The report gives greater emphasis to environmental issues than previous versions.
The teacher placed particular emphasis on the importance of teamwork in her lecture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deemphasize - to reduce or minimize the importance of something
  • antonym: insignificance - lack of importance or significance
  • synonym: stress - special attention or importance attached to something
  • antonym: understate - to present or express with less force, emphasis, or importance than is necessary
