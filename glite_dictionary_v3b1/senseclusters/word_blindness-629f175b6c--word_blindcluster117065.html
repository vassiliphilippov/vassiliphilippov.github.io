word blindness

noun
inability to perceive written words
5.5 7.8
Pronunciation: / wɝːd ˈblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / wɜːd ˈblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
An inability to perceive written words, usually resulting from brain injury or developmental disorder; alexia.

Examples

Word blindness after stroke
After his stroke, the doctor diagnosed him with word blindness, making it difficult for him to read even simple sentences.
Her word blindness was evident when she struggled to identify printed letters on the page.
Researchers are studying therapies to help patients with word blindness regain reading abilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alexia - the loss of the ability to understand written or printed language, usually due to brain injury
