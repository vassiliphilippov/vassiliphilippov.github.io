remuddle

verb
to confuse or mix up again
4.4
Pronunciation: / riˈmʌd.əl / (AmE), / riˈmʌd.əl / (BrE)
to make (something) confused or disorganized again after it was previously cleared up or settled

Examples

To remuddle the schedule
After the meeting, he remuddled the schedule, making it hard for everyone to follow.
She tends to remuddle her plans whenever she gets nervous about a decision.
They had just sorted things out when someone remuddled all the arrangements again.
