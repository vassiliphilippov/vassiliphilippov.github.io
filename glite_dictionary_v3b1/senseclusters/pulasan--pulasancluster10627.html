pulasan

noun
tropical fruit tree like rambutan
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: /puː.ˈlɑː.sən/ /; / /pʊ.ˈlæs.ən/ (AmE), /puː.ˈlɑː.sən/ /; / /pʊ.ˈlæs.ən/ (BrE)
A tropical tree of Southeast Asia (Nephelium mutabile), closely related to the rambutan and bearing edible, sweet, reddish fruits with a thick rind.

Examples

Pulasan tree harvest
Farmers harvest pulasans in the late summer, when the fruits turn a deep red color.
The pulasan is prized for its sweet, juicy flesh, making it a favorite in Southeast Asian markets.
Unlike the rambutan, the skin of the pulasan is easier to twist open by hand.
