Tyndale

proper noun
English translator and Protestant reformer
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪn.deɪl/ /; / /ˈtɪn.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈtɪn.deɪl/ /; / /ˈtɪn.dəl/ (BrE)
William Tyndale (c.1494–1536), English scholar and Protestant reformer renowned for translating the Bible into English, laying the foundation for later versions such as the King James Bible.

Examples

Tyndale Bible translation
Tyndale devoted his life to making the Bible accessible to English speakers.
Tyndale was executed for his efforts to translate the Scriptures.
Many phrases from Tyndale's translation are still used in modern English.
