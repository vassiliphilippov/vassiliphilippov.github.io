abstractness

noun
quality of being abstract
Pronunciation: / æbˈstrækt.nəs / (AmE), / æbˈstrækt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical or concrete existence

Examples

The abstractness of ideas
Philosophy often deals with a level of abstractness not found in everyday conversations.
Some critics argue that the abstractness of modern art makes it difficult to appreciate.
The abstractness of his explanation left the students confused about the practical application.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concreteness - the quality of being specific and real rather than abstract
