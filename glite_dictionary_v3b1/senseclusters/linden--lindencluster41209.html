linden

noun
soft wood from linden trees
7.9 0.01/m

The soft, light-colored wood obtained from any of various linden trees, used in making crates, boxes, carving, and millwork.

Examples

Linden wood carving
Some of the best traditional millwork uses linden for its smooth finish and ease of shaping.
The artist preferred to carve figures from linden because of its fine, even grain.
They received crates made of linden, which were lightweight but sturdy.

Synonyms

  • basswood
    6.7 0.07/m
    basswood is the soft, light-colored wood used for crates and carvings, while linden names the trees and the wood they produce.
  • limewood
    5.8 0.06/m
    limewood is pale, soft wood often used for carving and sculpture, while linden is the general wood used for crates, boxes, and millwork.
