shield fern

noun
fern with shield-shaped sori coverings
3.9 6.3
Pronunciation: / ʃild fɝn / (AmE), / ʃiːld fɜːn / (BrE)
A fern belonging to certain genera, such as Dryopteris, Polystichum, or Lastreopsis, distinguished by shield-shaped coverings over the sori on the undersides of the fronds.

Examples

A shield fern plant
Botanists can identify a shield fern by the characteristic shape of its sori coverings.
Many woodland gardens feature the shield fern for its attractive, hardy foliage.
The shield fern thrives in shaded, moist environments typical of temperate forests.
