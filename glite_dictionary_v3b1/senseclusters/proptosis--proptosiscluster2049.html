proptosis

noun
eye bulging
9.9
Pronunciation: / prɑpˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / prɒpˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal forward bulging or displacement of the eye from its socket

Examples

Proptosis of eye
The doctor noticed proptosis in the patient's right eye during the examination.
Severe proptosis can be a sign of an underlying medical condition requiring urgent attention.
Treatment for proptosis depends on the cause, such as infection, tumor, or thyroid disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exophthalmos - abnormal protrusion of the eyeball
