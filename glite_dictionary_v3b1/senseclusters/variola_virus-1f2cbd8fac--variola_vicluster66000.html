variola virus

noun
virus causing smallpox in humans
Pronunciation: / vəˈraɪə.lə ˈvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / vəˈraɪ.ə.lə ˈvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
the virus responsible for causing smallpox in humans; a member of the Orthopoxvirus genus

Examples

The variola virus infection
Scientists study the variola virus to better understand historical pandemics.

There are concerns that the variola virus could be used as a bioweapon if samples are released.
