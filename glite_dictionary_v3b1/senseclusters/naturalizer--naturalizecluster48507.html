naturalizer

noun
established non-native species
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəlʌɪzə / (BrE)
a plant or animal introduced to a new area that becomes established and lives as if it were native

Examples

A naturalizer plant spreads
The daffodil is a popular naturalizer in many gardens, spreading year after year.
Some birds act as a naturalizer by adapting quickly to new environments.
Gardeners often choose plants that are good naturalizers for low-maintenance landscapes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invasive species - a non-native species that spreads and may harm the environment
  • antonym: native species - a species that naturally occurs in a particular area
