potentially

adverb
possibly, with the capacity to happen
1.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛnʃəli / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that shows the possibility of becoming actual or real; possibly, but not yet achieved or existing

Examples

Could potentially happen
The new technology could potentially revolutionize the way we communicate.
There are potentially serious consequences to ignoring these warning signs.
This medicine is potentially life-saving if administered quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actually - in fact; in reality
  • synonym: possibly - by chance; in a way that something might happen
