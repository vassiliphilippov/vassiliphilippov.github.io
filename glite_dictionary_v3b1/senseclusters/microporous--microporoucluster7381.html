microporous

adjective
with tiny pores
8.7
Pronunciation: /maɪ.krə.ˈpɑːr.əs/ /; / /maɪ.krə.ˈpɔːr.əs/ (AmE), /maɪ.krə.ˈpɔː.rəs/ /; / /maɪ.krə.ˈpɒ.rəs/ (BrE)
having extremely small pores or openings, often only visible under a microscope

Examples

Microporous membranes
The laboratory uses microporous membranes to filter out bacteria from water samples.
A microporous surface can improve the efficiency of certain chemical reactions.
Scientists developed a microporous material that allows air to pass but blocks dust particles.
The microporous filter allows only very small particles to pass through.
Scientists developed a microporous membrane for advanced water purification systems.
The microporous material increased the efficiency of the chemical reaction.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: macroporous - having large pores, typically greater than 50 nanometres in diameter
  • degree higher: mesoporous - having pores of intermediate size, typically 2–50 nanometres in diameter
  • antonym: nonporous - not allowing liquid or air to pass through; without pores
  • degree lower: porous - having many small holes that allow liquid or air to pass through
