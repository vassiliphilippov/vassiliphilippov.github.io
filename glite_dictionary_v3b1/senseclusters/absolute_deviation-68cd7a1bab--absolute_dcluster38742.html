absolute deviation

noun
difference from the mean or median (statistics)
1.7
Pronunciation: / ˌæbsəluːt dɪˌviːeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæbsəluːt dɪˌviːeɪʃən / (BrE)
In statistics, the absolute difference between a value in a data set and a specified point, usually the mean or median.

Examples

The absolute deviation value
A smaller absolute deviation indicates that the data are clustered closely around the mean.
Researchers calculate the absolute deviation to measure how much the data points vary.
The absolute deviation of 8 from the mean of 5 is 3.
