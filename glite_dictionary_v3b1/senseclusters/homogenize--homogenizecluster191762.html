homogenize

verb
make uniform by mixing
3.0
Pronunciation: / həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz /; / həˈmɑːdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪz /; / hɒˈmɒdʒɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to make something uniform or the same throughout, especially by thoroughly mixing its parts (for example, to mix milk so the fat is evenly distributed)

Examples

To homogenize milk
Before bottling, the company will homogenize the milk to ensure consistency.
Modern manufacturing often requires machines to homogenize raw materials before further processing.
The chef used a blender to homogenize the soup's ingredients.
Before bottling, the dairy workers homogenized the milk for consistency.
Most commercially sold milk is homogenized to distribute the fat evenly throughout the liquid.
The company homogenizes its milk to ensure a smooth texture and prevent cream separation.
Over time, the cultures in the city began to homogenize due to increased interaction.
The milk must be homogenized to prevent the cream from separating.
The two liquids homogenized quickly when stirred together in the beaker.
