laryngismus

noun
spasm of the laryngeal muscles
7.5 9.7
Pronunciation: /læ.rɪn.ˈdʒɪz.məs/ /; / /lə.ˈrɪn.dʒɪz.məs/ (AmE), /læ.rɪn.ˈdʒɪz.məs/ /; / /lə.ˈrɪn.dʒɪz.məs/ (BrE)
A spasm of the laryngeal muscles, leading to a temporary closure of the larynx and difficulty breathing.

Examples

Laryngismus spasm
Children are more susceptible to laryngismus after upper respiratory infections.
During the operation, the patient experienced laryngismus, which briefly interrupted their breathing.
The sudden onset of laryngismus can cause panic due to difficulty inhaling.
