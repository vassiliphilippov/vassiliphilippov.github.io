ancient trade route

noun
historical pathway for commerce
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪnʃənt treɪd ruːt / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt treɪd ruːt / (BrE)
a historical pathway or route used by people in the distant past for the exchange of goods and commerce

Examples

An ancient trade route
Archaeological findings along ancient trade routes reveal what goods were commonly transported in the past.
Historians study ancient trade routes to understand economic and cultural exchanges between civilizations.
The Silk Road is the most famous ancient trade route connecting Asia and Europe.
