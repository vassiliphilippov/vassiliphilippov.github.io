lability

noun
chemical instability
9.5
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /lə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /lə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /lə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the tendency of a chemical substance or compound to change, decompose, or react easily and rapidly

Examples

Chemical lability
The lability of the compound made it unsuitable for long-term storage in the laboratory.
Researchers studied the lability of the new drug to determine how quickly it would break down in the body.
Due to its high lability, the chemical must be handled with special precautions to prevent unwanted reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instability - the state of being likely to change or fail
  • antonym: stability - the state of being stable and not likely to change
