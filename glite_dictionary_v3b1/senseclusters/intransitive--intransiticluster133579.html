intransitive

adjective
(of a verb) not taking a direct object
4.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtræn.sə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtræn.zɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb) not taking or requiring a direct object.

Examples

Intransitive verb example
In the sentence 'She sleeps', 'sleeps' is an intransitive verb.
Many English verbs can be both intransitive and transitive, depending on the context.
The verb 'arrive' is intransitive because you cannot 'arrive something'.
