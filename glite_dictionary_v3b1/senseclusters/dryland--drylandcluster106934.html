dryland

adjective
arid region farming
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌlænd / (BrE)
relating to or adapted for regions with little rainfall, especially where farming is done without irrigation

Examples

Dryland farming
Farmers in dryland areas must choose crops that can survive with little water.
The dryland climate makes traditional irrigation methods impractical for most farmers.
Researchers are developing new techniques for dryland agriculture to improve food security.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arid - having very little rain or moisture
  • antonym: irrigated - supplied with water by artificial means
