scutum

noun
shield-like structure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskjuːtəm / (AmE), / ˈskjuːtəm / (BrE)
a shield-like structure, especially a hard plate on the back of some animals or an ancient Roman shield

Examples

Tick scutum
The scientist examined the tick's scutum under a microscope to identify the species.
In ancient Rome, soldiers carried a large scutum for protection in battle.
The beetle's scutum is a distinctive feature that helps it blend into its environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shield - a broad piece of armor carried for protection
