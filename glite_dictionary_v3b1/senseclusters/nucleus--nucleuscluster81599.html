nucleus

noun
central cell part containing genetic material
5.3 1.35/m
Pronunciation: / ÀànuÀê.kli.…ôs / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀê.kli.…ôs / (BrE)
the central and most important part of a cell, containing DNA and RNA and responsible for growth and reproduction

Examples

Cell nucleus
Damage to the nucleus can disrupt a cell's ability to reproduce.
Scientists observed the nucleus under a powerful microscope to study cell division.
The nucleus of each cell contains the organism's genetic code.

Synonyms

  • cell nucleus
    3.2 0.16/m
    cell nucleus is the clear textbook name for the membrane-bound part with genetic material, while nucleus is the general, shorter word.
  • karyon
    9.9 0.00/m
    karyon is a technical scientific name used by specialists for the cell's DNA-containing center, while nucleus is the common everyday term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cell membrane - thin structure surrounding the cell nucleus
  • antonym: cytoplasm - material within a cell excluding the nucleus
