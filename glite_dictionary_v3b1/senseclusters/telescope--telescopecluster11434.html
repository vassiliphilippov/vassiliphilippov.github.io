telescope

verb
collapse or slide together (like sections)
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
to collapse or slide parts together so that they fit inside one another, or to become crushed together by such a movement

Examples

To telescope train cars
Several train cars telescoped during the crash, causing severe damage.
The designer created a table that can telescope to fit smaller spaces.
The metal pole can telescope down to half its original length for easy storage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in suddenly
  • antonym: expand - to increase in size, range, or amount; opposite of collapse
