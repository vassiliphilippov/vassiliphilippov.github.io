tail

verb
follow closely, often secretly
Pronunciation: / teɪl / (AmE), / teɪl / (BrE)
to follow someone closely, especially secretly, often to observe or catch them

Examples

To tail a suspect
Police officers tailed the car as it left the parking lot.
She noticed someone tailing her through the crowded market.
The detective decided to tail the suspect for several days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chase - pursue in order to catch
  • synonym: follow - move or travel behind someone or something
