ludditism

noun
opposition to technology
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌdɪtɪzəm /; / ˈlʌdɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlʌdɪtɪzəm /; / ˈlʌdɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
opposition to new technology or industrial change, sometimes shown by protesting or destroying machines

Examples

Ludditism protests machines
The rise of automation in factories led to renewed discussions about ludditism in the modern workforce.
Some critics accused the group of ludditism for resisting the adoption of new digital tools.
Historians study ludditism to understand the social impact of the Industrial Revolution.
The company's management accused the union of ludditism for resisting the introduction of automation.
Some critics see ludditism in the community's refusal to adopt new farming equipment.
His ludditism became clear when he argued against using computers in the classroom.
Some critics accused the group of ludditism when they protested against the introduction of new factory robots.
The rise of ludditism in the early 1800s reflected workers' fears about losing their jobs to machines.
Modern ludditism can be seen in movements that resist digital technology in schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressivism - support for or advocacy of social reform and new technology
  • synonym: technophobia - fear or dislike of advanced technology or complex devices
