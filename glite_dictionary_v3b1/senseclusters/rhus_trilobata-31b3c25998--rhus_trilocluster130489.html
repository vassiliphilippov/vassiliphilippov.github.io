Rhus trilobata

noun
a Californian shrub with trifoliate leaves and edible fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / rus traɪˈloʊbətə / (AmE), / ruːs trɪˈlɒbətə / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub native to California and parts of western North America, with unpleasantly scented, usually trifoliate leaves and edible fruit

Examples

Rhus trilobata shrub
Gardeners often plant Rhus trilobata for its drought resistance and vibrant autumn foliage.
Many animals feed on the fruits of Rhus trilobata during the late summer months.
The berries of Rhus trilobata were traditionally used by Native Americans to make beverages.
