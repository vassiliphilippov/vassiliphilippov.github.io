International Society for Krishna Consciousness

proper noun
Hindu-based religious movement founded in 1966
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəl səˈsaɪəti fɔr ˈkrɪʃnə kənˈʃɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl səˈsaɪəti fə ˈkrɪʃnə kənˈʃɪsnəs / (BrE)
A religious movement founded in the United States in 1966, based on Vedic scriptures and the worship of Krishna; known for joyful mantra chanting, distinctive dress, vegetarianism, and celibacy.
