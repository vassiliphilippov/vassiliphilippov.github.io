nauseate

verb
cause moral disgust or aversion
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔziˌeɪt /; / ˈnɔːziˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɔːzieɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel sick or very disgusted

Examples

To nauseate someone
Many people nauseate at the very thought of animal cruelty.
She found his lack of empathy so troubling that it truly nauseated her.
The politician's blatant lies nauseated the public and fueled outrage.
He was so nauseated by the graphic scenes that he had to leave the room.
Seeing the rotten food in the fridge was enough to nauseate her.
The strong smell of bleach can nauseate some people instantly.
