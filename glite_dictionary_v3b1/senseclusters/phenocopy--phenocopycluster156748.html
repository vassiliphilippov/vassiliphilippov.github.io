phenocopy

verb
to mimic genetic trait
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːnəˌkɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈfiːnəˌkɒpi / (BrE)
to cause an organism to develop a trait by environmental means that mimics a genetic mutation

Examples

To phenocopy a disease
Researchers found that certain drugs can phenocopy genetic disorders in laboratory animals.
Environmental stressors may phenocopy inherited traits, making diagnosis more challenging.
Scientists used chemicals to phenocopy the effects of a specific gene mutation in plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitate - to copy or mimic the appearance or behavior of something
