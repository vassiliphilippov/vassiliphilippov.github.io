Maryland yellowthroat

noun
North American warbler species
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛrɪlənd ˈjɛloʊˌθroʊt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪlənd ˈjɛləʊˌθrəʊt / (BrE)
A small North American warbler (Geothlypis trichas), notable for the male's black facial mask and yellow throat.

Examples

The Maryland yellowthroat bird
During migration, Maryland yellowthroats can be seen in a variety of habitats across North America.
The male Maryland yellowthroat is easily recognized by its black mask and bright yellow throat.
We spotted a Maryland yellowthroat singing near the edge of the marsh.
