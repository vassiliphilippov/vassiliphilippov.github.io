carrageenan

noun
substance extracted from red seaweed
Pronunciation: / ˌkærəˈɡiːnən / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈɡiːnən / (BrE)
A substance extracted from red seaweed, used as a thickener or stabilizer in food products and other applications.

Examples

Carrageenan thickener
Many dairy products, such as yogurt, contain carrageenan as a thickening agent.
Some people choose foods without carrageenan due to concerns about digestive health.
The scientist studied the gelling properties of carrageenan extracted from seaweed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gelatin - a substance used as a gelling agent in food and pharmaceuticals
