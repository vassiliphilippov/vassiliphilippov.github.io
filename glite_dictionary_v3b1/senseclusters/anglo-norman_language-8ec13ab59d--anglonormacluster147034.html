Anglo-Norman language

noun
medieval Norman French of England
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæŋ.gloʊ ˈnɔːr.mən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈæŋ.gləʊ ˈnɔː.mən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (BrE)
The variety of Norman French spoken and written in England during the Middle Ages, especially by the ruling classes, law courts, and in literature.

Examples

Anglo-Norman language documents
Documents from the 12th century were often written in Anglo-Norman language rather than English.
Many legal terms in English come from the Anglo-Norman language used by medieval lawyers.
The Anglo-Norman language influenced English vocabulary, especially in law and government.
