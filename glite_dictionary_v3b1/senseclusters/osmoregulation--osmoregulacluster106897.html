osmoregulation

noun
water and salt balance
Pronunciation: / ˌɑzməˌrɛɡjuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒzməˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which living organisms control the balance of water and salts in their bodies

Examples

Osmoregulation in animals
Fish rely on osmoregulation to adapt to changes in water salinity.
Proper osmoregulation is essential for human kidneys to maintain fluid balance.
Desert animals have special adaptations for osmoregulation in dry environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeostasis - the maintenance of stable internal conditions in an organism
