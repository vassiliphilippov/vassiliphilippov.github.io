predisposition

noun
an inclination or tendency to interpret
5.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /priː.dɪ.spə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.dɪ.spə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.dɪ.spə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a tendency or inclination to think, interpret, or behave in a particular way, often based on prior factors or inherent qualities

Examples

Predisposition to believe
A person’s predisposition to believe a story may affect how they understand the facts.
His predisposition to distrust strangers made it hard for him to make new friends.
The jury's predisposition toward the defendant was a concern for a fair trial.
Despite his predisposition to skepticism, he kept an open mind during the discussion.
Genetics can influence a person's predisposition to certain diseases.
Her predisposition to anxiety made her cautious in unfamiliar situations.

  • antonym: impartiality - the state of being unbiased or fair
