Agrippina

proper noun
Roman empress, granddaughter of Augustus
Pronunciation: / əˌɡrɪˈpinə / (AmE), / əˌɡrɪˈpiːnə / (BrE)
(c. 14 BC–AD 33) The granddaughter of Augustus, wife of Germanicus, and mother of Caligula and Agrippina the Younger; a prominent Roman noblewoman.

Examples

Agrippina the Younger
Agrippina was a key figure in the early Roman imperial family, known for her influence and tragic fate.
Many historians have written extensively about Agrippina's role as the mother of Emperor Caligula.
The political intrigues surrounding Agrippina shaped the course of Roman history.
