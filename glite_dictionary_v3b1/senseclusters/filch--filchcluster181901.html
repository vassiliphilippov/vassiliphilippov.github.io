filch

verb
steal something stealthily
Pronunciation: / fɪltʃ / (AmE), / fɪltʃ / (BrE)
to steal (something, especially a small item) in a stealthy or sneaky manner

Examples

To filch small coins
He tried to filch a few coins from the cash register when no one was looking.
She had a bad habit of filching pens from the office supply closet.
The pickpocket was caught after attempting to filch a wallet from a tourist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pilfer - steal (things of little value) in small amounts
  • synonym: steal - take (another person's property) without permission or legal right
