authorize

verb
give official permission or power
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.θə.raɪz/ /; / /ˈɑː.θə.raɪz/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.θə.raɪz/ (BrE)
to give official permission for something or to give someone the power to do something

Examples

To authorize a project
The board is authorizing an expansion of the project next year.
The manager authorized the purchase of new office equipment.
You must be authorized before you can access those documents.
Congress failed to authorize new spending for the program this year.
She refused to authorize the release of confidential information.
The manager must authorize all payments before they are processed.
Guests must be authorised to enter this area of the building.
She is authorised to sign documents on behalf of the company.
The bank did not authorise the transaction due to suspicious activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authorize - to give official permission or power (US spelling)
  • synonym: empower - give someone the authority or power to do something
  • antonym: forbid - to order someone not to do something; to refuse to allow
  • synonym: permit - to give permission for something to happen
