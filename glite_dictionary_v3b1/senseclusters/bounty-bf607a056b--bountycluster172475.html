Bounty

proper noun
historic British ship of 1789 mutiny
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
the British naval ship HMS Bounty, famous for the 1789 mutiny by part of its crew against Captain William Bligh

Examples

The Bounty ship
After the mutiny, the Bounty was sailed to Pitcairn Island by the mutineers.
Captain Bligh showed remarkable navigation skills after being set adrift from the Bounty.
The story of the Bounty mutiny has inspired numerous books and films.
