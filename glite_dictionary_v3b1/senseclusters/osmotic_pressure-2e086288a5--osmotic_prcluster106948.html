osmotic pressure

noun
pressure to prevent osmosis
Pronunciation: / ɑzˈmɑtɪk ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ɒzˈmɒtɪk ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure required to prevent the flow of a solvent into a solution through a semipermeable membrane by osmosis

Examples

Osmotic pressure measurement
By measuring osmotic pressure, scientists can determine the concentration of solutes in a solution.
Medical professionals monitor osmotic pressure to understand water movement across cell membranes.
The osmotic pressure of seawater is much higher than that of fresh water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: osmolarity - measure of solute concentration related to osmosis
  • degree higher: turgor pressure - pressure exerted by fluid in a cell
