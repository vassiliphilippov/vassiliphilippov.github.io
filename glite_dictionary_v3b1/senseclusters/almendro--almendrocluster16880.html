almendro

noun
tropical hardwood tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / alˈmɛndroʊ / (AmE), / alˈmɛndrəʊ / (BrE)
a large tropical tree native to Central and South America, valued for its dense, durable hardwood

Examples

Tropical almendro tree
The almendro is an important source of timber in many Central American countries.
Carpenters prefer almendro wood for building strong and long-lasting furniture.
Many animals find shelter in the branches of the towering almendro tree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardwood - wood from broad-leaved trees, often used in construction
