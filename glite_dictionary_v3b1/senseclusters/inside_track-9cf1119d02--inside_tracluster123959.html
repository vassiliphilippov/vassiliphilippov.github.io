inside track

noun
advantageous position in a competition
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌsaɪd træɪk /; / ˈɪnˌsaɪd ˈtræk / (AmE), / ˌɪn.saɪd ˈtræk / (BrE)
an advantage or privileged position that gives someone a better chance of success, often because of special knowledge or access

Examples

Have the inside track to success
Because of his close friendship with the mayor, he had the inside track on the contract.
Her experience in the company gave her the inside track during the promotion process.
Investors with the inside track can anticipate market movements before others.
Anyone with the inside track will hear about the new opportunities before others do.
Because her uncle works there, she has the inside track for the internship.
He hoped his connections would give him the inside track in the election.
