cleistocarp

noun
closed spore-bearing structure in fungi
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪstəˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈklaɪstəˌkɑːp / (BrE)
A closed spore-bearing structure of certain fungi, from which spores are released only when the structure decays or disintegrates.

Examples

A cleistocarp structure
Researchers studied the cleistocarp structure to understand how it protects the fungal spores.
The cleistocarp of this fungus remains intact until it decays, allowing spores to disperse only then.
Unlike other molds, this species forms a cleistocarp that does not open spontaneously.
