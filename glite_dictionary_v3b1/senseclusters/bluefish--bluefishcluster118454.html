bluefish

noun
a bluish marine food and game fish
4.3 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbluː.fɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈbluː.fɪʃ/ (BrE)
A bluish, warm-water marine fish (Pomatomus saltatrix), popular as food and game and known for following schools of small fish into shallow waters.

Examples

A bluefish swims
A large bluefish swam near the surface, following a school of baitfish.
Grilled bluefish is a local delicacy in many seaside towns.
The fishermen caught several bluefish off the coast during the early morning.
