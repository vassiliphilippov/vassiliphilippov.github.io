A system of automobile insurance in which a person injured in an automobile accident recovers damages up to a certain amount from their own insurance company, regardless of who was responsible for the accident.

Examples

No fault automobile insurance system
No fault automobile insurance allows drivers to file claims with their own insurer after an accident, no matter who caused it.
Many states have adopted no fault automobile insurance laws to reduce litigation over minor crashes.
With no fault automobile insurance, you may receive compensation faster, but with limits on lawsuits for damages.
