mischief-making

noun
the act of causing trouble or annoyance
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪstʃɪfˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪfˌmeɪ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of behaving in a way that deliberately causes trouble, annoyance, or harm to others

Examples

Constant mischief-making
His constant mischief-making at the office eventually got him into trouble with his boss.
Rumors and mischief-making can quickly destroy trust within a team.
The teacher warned the students that any mischief-making during the trip would not be tolerated.
