reallocation

noun
the act of allocating again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌæl.əˈkeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌriːˌæləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌæl.əˈkeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌriːˌaləˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of assigning or distributing something again

Examples

Staff reallocation
A sudden increase in demand may require the reallocation of staff among departments.
After the budget cuts, a reallocation of funds was necessary to support essential services.
The company announced a reallocation of resources to focus on renewable energy projects.
After reviewing the project, management decided a reallocation of responsibilities was necessary.
Budget cuts forced a reallocation of funds across all departments.
The reallocation of seats in Congress followed the national census results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocation - the act of distributing or assigning resources or tasks
  • synonym: redistribution - the act of distributing again in a different way
