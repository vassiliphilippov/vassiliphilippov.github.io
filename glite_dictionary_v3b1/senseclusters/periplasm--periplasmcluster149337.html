periplasm

noun
bacterial membrane space
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrɪˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪˌplazəm / (BrE)
the space between the inner and outer membranes of certain bacteria, containing various enzymes and proteins

Examples

The periplasm contains enzymes that help bacteria process nutrients from their environment.
