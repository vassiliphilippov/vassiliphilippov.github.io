invasiveness

noun
tendency to spread or invade, especially medically
5.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of tending to spread or intrude, especially referring to the ability of organisms or diseases to penetrate and affect healthy tissues or environments.

Examples

Invasiveness of a species
Some plants are known for their invasiveness, quickly overtaking native species in a new environment.
Strict regulations are needed to control the invasiveness of non-native animal populations.
The invasiveness of the tumor made it difficult for surgeons to remove it completely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressiveness - the quality of being aggressive, similar to invasiveness in certain contexts
