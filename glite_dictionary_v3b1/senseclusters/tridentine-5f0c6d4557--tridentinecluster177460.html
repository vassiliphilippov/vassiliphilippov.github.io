Tridentine

noun
supporter of Latin Mass
Pronunciation: / traɪˈdɛntaɪn / (AmE), / traɪˈdɛntaɪn / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices the traditional Latin Mass as established by the Council of Trent

Examples

A devoted Tridentine
As a devoted Tridentine, she attended the Latin Mass every Sunday.
The group of Tridentines organized a petition to preserve traditional liturgical practices.
Many Tridentine Catholics value the rituals and language of the pre-Vatican II Mass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernist - a person who supports modern ideas or reforms in the church
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports traditional practices or beliefs
