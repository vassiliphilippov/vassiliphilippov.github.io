heretofore

adverb
up to now; before this time
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪr.ɪˈtʊr / (AmE), / ˌhɪə.rɪˈtɔː / (BrE)
Up to this time; before now.

Examples

Had heretofore remained
He denied all allegations, which had heretofore remained unaddressed.
Such discoveries had heretofore been considered impossible.
The company has adopted technology that was heretofore unavailable to small businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hitherto - up to this time; until now
  • antonym: subsequently - after this; later
