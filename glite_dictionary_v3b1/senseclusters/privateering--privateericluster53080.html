privateering

verb
to attack ships legally
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪvətˈɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpraɪvətˈɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
to attack or capture enemy merchant ships with government authorization during wartime as a privateer

Examples

To privateer enemy ships
During the war, several ships began privateering to disrupt enemy trade routes.
He made his fortune by privateering under a letter of marque from the government.
The crew spent months privateering in the Caribbean, capturing valuable cargo from enemy vessels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: piracy - the act of attacking ships at sea without legal authority
  • synonym: privateer - a person or ship authorized by a government to attack enemy ships
