scourge

verb
devastate or cause great destruction
6.7
Pronunciation: / skɝːdʒ /; / skɝdʒ / (AmE), / skɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to cause great suffering, devastation, or harsh punishment (literally or figuratively)

Examples

Fires scourge the land
A series of wildfires scourged large swathes of forest throughout the summer.
The invading army scourged the land, leaving nothing but ruins in their wake.
Years of war scourged the region, destroying towns and displacing thousands of people.
During the debate, she scourged her opponent's record with relentless criticism.
Historically, leaders would scourge dissidents to deter others from rebellion.
The critics scourged the politician for his unethical conduct.

  • synonym: excoriate - to criticize severely and publicly
  • synonym: punish - to inflict a penalty for wrongdoing
  • antonym: repair - to restore to a good or sound condition
