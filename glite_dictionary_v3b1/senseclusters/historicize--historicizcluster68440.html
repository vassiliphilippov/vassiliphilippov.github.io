historicize

verb
to give historical context
7.9
Pronunciation: / hɪˈstɔːrɪˌsaɪz /; / hɪˈstɔrɪˌsaɪz /; / hɪˈstɔːrəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to place something in its historical context or interpret it as part of history

Examples

To historicize an event
The documentary aims to historicize the events of the 1960s for a new generation.
Scholars often historicize literary works to better understand their meaning.
It is important to historicize social movements to see how they have evolved over time.
The professor encouraged students to historicize the events discussed in class.
It is important to historicize literature to better understand its themes and influences.
Many documentaries attempt to historicize current issues by linking them to past events.
The author attempts to historicize the events by relating them to the political climate of the time.
It is important to historicize these traditions to understand their original meaning.
Scholars often historicize works of art to reveal their significance in different eras.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contextualize - to place something in a particular context
  • antonym: decontextualize - to remove something from its context
  • antonym: modernize - to make something more modern or up to date
