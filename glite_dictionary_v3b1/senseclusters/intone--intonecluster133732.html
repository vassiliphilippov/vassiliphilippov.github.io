intone

verb
say something in a measured, monotonous voice
7.5 5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtoʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtəʊn / (BrE)
to say or chant something in a slow, regular, and often monotonous or musical voice

Examples

To intone names slowly
As the bell rang, the teacher intoned the students' names in a rhythmic manner.
He would intone the rules in a flat, unchanging voice at every meeting.
The priest began to intone the evening prayers in the dimly lit chapel.
At the ceremony, the priest began to intone the ancient prayers.
Every evening, the monks intone their hymns in the chapel.
She would intone the names slowly, giving each one special significance.
He intoned the warning in a low, serious voice that caught everyone's attention.
She would intone every sentence, making her stories sound almost like music.
The priest began to intone the opening words of the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chant - to say or shout repeatedly in a singsong tone
  • synonym: recite - to say aloud from memory, often formally
