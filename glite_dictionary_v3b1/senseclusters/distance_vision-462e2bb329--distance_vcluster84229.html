distance vision

noun
ability to see distant objects clearly
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstəns ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəns ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the ability to see objects clearly that are 20 feet (about 6 meters) or more away from the viewer

Examples

Good distance vision
Her distance vision was excellent, but she struggled to read small print up close.
The eye doctor tested his distance vision using a standard eye chart.
You need good distance vision to drive safely at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: near vision - the ability to see objects clearly at close range, usually within about 40 cm (16 inches)
