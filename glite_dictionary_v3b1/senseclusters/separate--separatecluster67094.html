separate

adjective
kept apart by group (e.g., by race or sex)
1.1 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛpɚˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛpəˌreɪt / (BrE)
Kept apart or divided from others according to race, sex, class, religion, or another group characteristic.

Examples

Separate schools
Many facilities were separate for different races during the 20th century.
The organization advocates for separate classes based on students’ academic abilities.
The school has separate bathrooms for boys and girls.
