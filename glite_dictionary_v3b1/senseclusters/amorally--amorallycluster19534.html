amorally

adverb
without regard to morality
6.7
Pronunciation: / əˈmɔːrəli / (AmE), / əˈmɒrəli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows no concern for whether something is right or wrong; without regard to morality.

Examples

Act amorally
He made his decisions amorally, indifferent to questions of right or wrong.
Some characters in the novel behave amorally, exploiting others without guilt.
The company operated amorally, focusing solely on profit regardless of ethical concerns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immorally - in a way that goes against moral principles
