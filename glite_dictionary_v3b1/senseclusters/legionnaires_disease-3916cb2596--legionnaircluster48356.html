Legionnaires' disease

noun
severe pneumonia caused by Legionella bacteria
7.5 4.5
Pronunciation: /liː.dʒən.ˈeərz dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /liː.dʒən.ˈeəz dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A severe form of pneumonia caused by infection with Legionella bacteria, often resulting in fever, muscle aches, cough, and sometimes fatal respiratory illness.

Examples

Legionnaires' disease infection
After staying at the hotel, several guests were diagnosed with Legionnaires' disease and required hospitalization.
Doctors are trained to recognize the symptoms of Legionnaires' disease in patients with severe pneumonia.
The outbreak of Legionnaires' disease was traced back to a contaminated water system in the building.
