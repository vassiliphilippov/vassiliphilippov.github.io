stress incontinence

noun
involuntary urine leakage with exertion
5.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /stres ɪn.ˈkɑːn.tə.nəns/ (AmE), /stres ɪn.ˈkɒn.tɪ.nəns/ (BrE)
a condition in which involuntary leakage of urine occurs during physical activities that increase pressure on the bladder, such as coughing, laughing, or lifting

Examples

Stress incontinence symptoms
Effective treatment for stress incontinence can greatly improve a patient's quality of life.
Many women experience stress incontinence following childbirth due to weakened pelvic muscles.
Sarah was diagnosed with stress incontinence after she started leaking urine when sneezing and exercising.

Related Terms

  • antonym: urge incontinence - involuntary leakage of urine accompanied by or immediately preceded by urgency
