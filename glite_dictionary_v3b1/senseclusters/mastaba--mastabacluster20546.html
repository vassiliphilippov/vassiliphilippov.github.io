mastaba

noun
ancient Egyptian rectangular tomb
Pronunciation: / məˈstɑbə / (AmE), / ˈmæstəbə / (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian tomb with a rectangular base, sloping sides, and a flat roof, typically made of mud-brick

Examples

An ancient mastaba tomb
Archaeologists uncovered a well-preserved mastaba near the old capital of Memphis.
The ancient Egyptians buried their elite in mastabas before the construction of pyramids began.
The design of the pyramid evolved from the simpler mastaba structure.
