mesocarnivore

adjective
moderate meat-eating
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsoʊˈkɑrnɪˌvɔr / (AmE), / ˌmiːsəʊˈkɑːnɪˌvɔː / (BrE)
relating to animals or diets that include a moderate proportion of meat, typically 50-70% of the diet.

Examples

Mesocarnivore diet
The red fox is considered a mesocarnivore because it eats both meat and plant material.
Many wild canids are classified as mesocarnivore due to their balanced diets.
Ecologists study how mesocarnivore species adapt to changes in food availability.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: carnivorous - eating mostly or only meat
  • degree lower: omnivorous - eating both plants and animals
