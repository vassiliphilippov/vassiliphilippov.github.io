binary

noun
opposite pair
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪnəri / (BrE)
a pair of things or groups that are opposites or mutually exclusive, often used in philosophy or social sciences

Examples

Gender binary
Many social theories challenge traditional binaries such as male and female.
The debate was framed in simple binaries of right and wrong.
Modern philosophy often questions the usefulness of strict binaries in understanding complex issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuum - a continuous sequence or range, not divided into opposites
  • synonym: duality - the state of having two parts, often opposites
  • synonym: polarity - the presence of two opposite or contrasting principles
