thrash

verb
move violently or uncontrollably
3.6
Pronunciation: / θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
to move or stir about in a violent or uncontrolled way

Examples

Branches thrash wildly
During the storm, the branches would thrash against the windows all night long.
The hooked fish tried to thrash free of the line as it was reeled in.
The startled horse began to thrash in its stall, kicking and bucking wildly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flail - to wave or swing energetically or uncontrollably
