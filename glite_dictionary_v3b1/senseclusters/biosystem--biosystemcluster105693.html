biosystem

noun
biological system
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.oʊ.sɪs.təm/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.əʊ.sɪs.təm/ (BrE)
a functional biological system within an organism, composed of organs, tissues, and cells working together for a specific purpose

Examples

Human biosystem
The human circulatory biosystem transports blood and nutrients throughout the body.
Scientists study each biosystem to understand how organs and tissues interact.
Damage to one biosystem can affect the overall health of an organism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ecosystem - a community of living organisms interacting with their environment
  • synonym: organ system - a group of organs that work together to perform a specific function
