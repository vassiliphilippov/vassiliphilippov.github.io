check

verb
correspond or be consistent with
2.9
Pronunciation: / tʃɛk / (AmE), / tʃɛk / (BrE)
to examine, look at, or verify something to make sure it is correct, safe, or as expected

Examples

To check the facts
His explanation didn't check with the facts the police gathered.
The numbers on both lists check, confirming there was no mistake.
Your account of the accident checks with what the witnesses reported.
Before a long drive, you should always check your tire pressure.
She checked her assignment for any mistakes before submitting it.
The technician will check the wiring before installing the new equipment.
I always check my emails in the morning to make sure I haven't missed anything important.
Please check that all the windows are locked before you leave the office.
The inspector will check the equipment for any signs of damage.
