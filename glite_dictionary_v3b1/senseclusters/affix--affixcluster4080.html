affix

noun
linguistic element added to a word
3.6
Pronunciation: / əˈfɪks / (AmE), / əˈfɪks / (BrE)
a morpheme or element added to a word, such as a prefix or suffix, to alter its meaning or function

Examples

A word affix
Suffixes like '-ed' and '-ing' are common types of affix in English.
The word 'unhappy' contains the prefix un- as an affix.
When you add an affix to a root word, you can create a new meaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prefix - an affix placed at the beginning of a word
  • antonym: root - the base form of a word to which affixes are added
  • synonym: suffix - an affix placed at the end of a word
