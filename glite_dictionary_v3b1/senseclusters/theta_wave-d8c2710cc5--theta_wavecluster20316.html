theta wave

noun
a low-frequency brainwave seen in relaxed or drowsy states
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈθeɪtə weɪv / (AmE), / ˈθiːtə weɪv / (BrE)
a brainwave pattern characterized by low frequency and low amplitude, typically observed in a person who is awake but relaxed or drowsy

Examples

A theta wave pattern
A prominent burst of theta wave activity was recorded as the patient became drowsy.
During meditation, the brain often shows increased theta wave activity, indicating deep relaxation.
Scientists analyzed the subject’s theta waves to study the transition from wakefulness to sleep.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: alpha wave - a type of brainwave seen in relaxed, awake states, higher frequency than theta
  • degree lower: delta wave - a type of brainwave seen in deep sleep, lower frequency than theta
