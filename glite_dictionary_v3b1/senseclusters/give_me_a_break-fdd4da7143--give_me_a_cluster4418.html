give me a break

x
request for leniency or relief
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv mi ə breɪk / (AmE), / ɡɪv miː ə breɪk / (BrE)
Used to ask someone to stop criticizing or pressuring you, or to request relief from a difficult situation.

Examples

Give me a break now
Give me a break—I've been working nonstop for twelve hours!
If you expect me to finish that by tomorrow, give me a break!
When he blamed me for the mistake, I wanted to shout 'give me a break!'

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut me some slack - to be less strict or critical with someone
