pound-foolish

adjective
unwise with large sums of money
Pronunciation: / paʊnd ˈfuːlɪʃ / (AmE), / paʊnd ˈfuːlɪʃ / (BrE)
Unwise or careless in managing large sums of money, even if careful with small amounts.

Examples

Being pound-foolish with money
Being pound-foolish led the company to make costly mistakes.
It's pound-foolish to skip regular maintenance and face expensive repairs later.
She is penny-wise but pound-foolish when it comes to investments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: penny-wise - Careful in saving small amounts of money
