calcium-channel blocker

noun
drug reducing calcium flow, used to treat heart conditions
6.1 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkæl.sɪ.əm ˈtʃæn.əl ˈblɑː.kər/ /; / /ˈkæl.siː.əm ˈtʃæn.əl ˈblɑː.kər/ (AmE), /ˈkæl.sɪ.əm ˈtʃæn.əl ˈblɒk.ə/ /; / /ˈkæl.siː.əm ˈtʃæn.əl ˈblɒk.ə/ (BrE)
a drug that reduces the movement of calcium into the cells of the heart and blood vessels, used to treat conditions such as high blood pressure, angina, or arrhythmia

Examples

A calcium-channel blocker drug
He experienced fewer episodes of angina after starting a calcium-channel blocker.
Some people experiencing migraines may benefit from taking a calcium-channel blocker.
The doctor prescribed a calcium-channel blocker to help control her high blood pressure.
