ancestor

noun
person from whom one is descended
Pronunciation: / ˈænˌsɛstər / (AmE), / ˈæn.ses.tər / (BrE)
a person from whom one is descended, especially one more distant than a grandparent

Examples

Family ancestors
Many cultures honor their ancestors with special ceremonies.
My family can trace one ancestor back to the seventeenth century.
She discovered that her ancestors had emigrated from Ireland.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descendant - a person who is descended from an ancestor
