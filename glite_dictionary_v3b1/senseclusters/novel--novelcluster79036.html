novel

adjective
new and unusual
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːvəl /; / ˈnɑvəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəl / (BrE)
new, original, or unusual; not seen or experienced before

Examples

Novel idea
She appreciated the novel approach to solving the old problem.

The chef came up with a novel way to present the traditional dish.
A novel idea can sometimes change an entire industry.
Her approach to the design was truly novel and impressed the judges.
The scientist proposed a novel solution to a longstanding problem.
