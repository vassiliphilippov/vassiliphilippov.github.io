interest

noun
legal or financial share in something
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.tɚ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈɪn.trəst / (BrE)
(law, finance) A right, share, or involvement in an asset, business, or property, often giving the holder a stake in its ownership or income.

Examples

An interest in property
Several partners each have an interest in the estate.
She sold her interest in the company to a new investor.
The conflict of interest prevented him from voting on the board's decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liability - being responsible for something, especially legally
  • degree higher: ownership - the state of owning something
  • synonym: stake - a share or interest in a business or enterprise
