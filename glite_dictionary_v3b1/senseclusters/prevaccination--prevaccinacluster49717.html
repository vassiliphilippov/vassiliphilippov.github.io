prevaccination

noun
vaccination before exposure
7.1
Pronunciation: /priː.væk.sɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.væk.sə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.væk.sɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.væk.sə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of giving a vaccine to someone before they are exposed to a disease

Examples

Prevaccination program
The prevaccination program helped prevent the spread of the virus in the community.
Doctors recommend prevaccination for travelers visiting regions with high infection rates.
Public health officials emphasized the importance of prevaccination before the flu season began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunization - the process of making a person immune to a disease, typically by vaccination
  • antonym: postexposure vaccination - vaccination given after exposure to a disease
