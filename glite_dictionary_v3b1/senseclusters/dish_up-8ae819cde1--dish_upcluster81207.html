dish up

verb
serve food or provide something
3.6
Pronunciation: / dɪʃ ʌp / (AmE), / dɪʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to serve food onto plates or at the table, usually from a larger container

Examples

To dish up dinner
After hours in the kitchen, she was finally ready to dish up dinner.
Could you please dish up some more rice for the guests?
The chef loves to dish up creative meals that surprise his customers.
Every Sunday, Grandma would dish up a hearty roast dinner for the family.
She offered to dish up the soup while I set the table.
The chef dished up the main course with a flourish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear away - to remove dishes and food after a meal
  • synonym: dish out - to distribute food or something else, sometimes in a casual or generous manner
  • synonym: serve - to present food to someone at a meal
  • synonym: serve up - to present or provide food or something for people's enjoyment
