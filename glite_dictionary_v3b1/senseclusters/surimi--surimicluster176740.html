surimi

noun
imitation seafood paste
Pronunciation: / sʊˈriːmi / (AmE), / sʊˈriːmi / (BrE)
a processed fish paste, often shaped and flavored to imitate crab, lobster, or other seafood

Examples

Processed surimi seafood
Many sushi rolls contain surimi as a substitute for real crab meat.
The chef used surimi to create a seafood salad that was both affordable and tasty.
You can find packages of surimi in the refrigerated section of most supermarkets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitation crab - a seafood product made from surimi to resemble crab meat
  • degree higher: seafood - edible marine animals such as fish and shellfish
