four-in-hand

noun
type of necktie or necktie knot
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr ɪn ˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːr ɪn ˌhænd / (BrE)
A long necktie tied with a slipknot so that one end hangs in front of the other.

Examples

A four-in-hand tie
Choosing a silk four-in-hand can make any suit appear more elegant.
He wore a four-in-hand to the wedding for a classic look.
My grandfather always preferred a four-in-hand over a bow tie.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Windsor knot - a wide, triangular necktie knot
  • antonym: bow tie - a necktie in the form of a bow
