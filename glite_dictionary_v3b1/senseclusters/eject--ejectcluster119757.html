to force a liquid or substance out of something; to squirt or discharge

Examples

To eject liquid
Some animals eject ink or venom as a defense mechanism.
The clown's flower ejected water into the startled audience.
The malfunctioning pump ejected a stream of oil across the floor.
Certain air fresheners eject fragrance into the air at set intervals.
Some plants eject seeds forcefully to aid in dispersal.
The printer will eject excess ink during its cleaning cycle.

Synonyms

  • force out
    4.2 0.41/m
    force out means to push something out by pressure, while eject implies a sudden, often mechanical expulsion from inside.
  • expel
    5.0 2.23/m
    expel is a formal word for forcing something out, while eject often suggests a quick, violent push from a container.
  • discharge
    4.4 2.05/m
    discharge means to send out liquid or gas in a controlled way, while eject often feels sudden and forceful.
  • squirt
    2.3 2.61/m
    squirt means to make liquid shoot out in a thin, quick jet, while eject can mean forcing larger or solid items out.
  • spout
    3.7 0.70/m
    spout means to gush out in a strong stream, while eject usually means to force something out suddenly from inside.
  • spew
    5.1 7.06/m
    spew means to throw out large amounts quickly and messily, while eject often describes a single, directed expulsion.
  • release
    2.7 27/m
    release means to let a substance go or escape gently, while eject implies forcing it out with pressure or sudden action.
