mean

verb
intend to refer to; have in mind
1.0 1.5
Pronunciation: /miːn/ (AmE), /miːn/ (BrE)
to intend, have in mind, or have a particular purpose when saying or doing something

Examples

To mean something
Did you mean Sarah when you mentioned someone who was always helpful?
If you don't mean the report I wrote, which document are you talking about?
When I said 'they', I actually meant the new students in the class.
I didn't mean to upset you; it was an accident.
She meant the gift as a sign of gratitude.
When he said 'I'll handle it,' he really meant it.
He didn’t realize the comment was meant only as a joke.
The cake was meant for Sarah’s birthday, not for everyday dessert.
These tickets were meant for the front row, but we got them by chance.

  • synonym: imply - to suggest something without stating it directly
  • synonym: intend - to have a plan or purpose in mind
  • synonym: plan - to intend to do something in the future
  • synonym: refer - to mention or allude to someone or something
  • synonym: say - to utter words to convey information, an opinion, a feeling, or intention
