four-lined leaf bug

noun
a yellow or orange leaf bug with four black stripes
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr laɪnd liːf bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈfɔː laɪnd liːf bʌɡ / (BrE)
A small yellow or orange leaf bug with four black stripes down its back, commonly found in central and eastern North America.

Examples

The four-lined leaf bug
A yellow and black pattern makes the four-lined leaf bug easy to identify among other insects.
Gardeners often watch for four-lined leaf bugs in late spring when they begin to feed on new growth.
The four-lined leaf bug caused visible damage to the plants in my garden.
