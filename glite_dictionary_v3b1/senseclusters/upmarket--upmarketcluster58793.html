upmarket

adjective
for affluent customers; high quality
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌpˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
intended for or appealing to people with higher incomes; of superior quality or more expensive than average.

Examples

An upmarket restaurant
The new restaurant has a very upmarket atmosphere, attracting celebrities and wealthy businesspeople.
They moved to an upmarket neighborhood with designer stores and gourmet coffee shops.
This brand is known for its upmarket image and premium pricing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downmarket - intended for or appealing to people with lower incomes; less expensive or lower in quality
