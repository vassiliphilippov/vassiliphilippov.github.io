cataplexy

noun
sudden muscle weakness
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəˌplɛksi / (AmE), / ˈkatəˌplɛksi / (BrE)
a sudden and brief loss of muscle control, often triggered by strong emotions such as laughter or surprise

Examples

Sudden cataplexy attack
During moments of intense laughter, people with narcolepsy may experience cataplexy and suddenly lose muscle strength.
The doctor explained that cataplexy is often triggered by strong emotions like excitement or anger.
She collapsed to the ground due to a sudden episode of cataplexy after hearing surprising news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: narcolepsy - a sleep disorder causing excessive daytime sleepiness and sudden sleep attacks
  • synonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move part or most of the body
