punitive damages

noun
extra damages awarded as punishment in law
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpjuːnɪtɪv ˈdæmɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈpjuːnɪtɪv ˈdamɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
Damages awarded by a court that exceed simple compensation and are intended to punish the defendant for malicious or willful misconduct.

Examples

Punitive damages awarded
In some cases, punitive damages can be several times greater than compensatory damages.
She was ordered to pay punitive damages for her deliberate and malicious actions.
The jury decided to award punitive damages to deter similar conduct by the corporation in the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compensatory damages - damages awarded to compensate actual loss
