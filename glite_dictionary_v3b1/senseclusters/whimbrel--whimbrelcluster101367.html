whimbrel

noun
wading bird species
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪmbrəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪmbrəl / (BrE)
a medium-sized wading bird with a long, down-curved bill, found in coastal areas

Examples

A whimbrel bird
We watched a whimbrel searching for food along the muddy shoreline.
The distinctive call of the whimbrel could be heard across the salt marsh.
During migration, flocks of whimbrels stop to rest on coastal beaches.
We watched a whimbrel searching for food along the sandy beach at low tide.
The distinctive call of the whimbrel echoed across the salt marshes in spring.
During migration, flocks of whimbrels can be seen resting on coastal mudflats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: curlew - a large wading bird with a long, curved bill
  • synonym: sandpiper - a small or medium-sized wading bird often found along shores
