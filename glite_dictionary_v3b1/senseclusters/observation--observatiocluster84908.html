observation

noun
act of measuring or recording; scientific noting
1.0 2.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːbzɚˈveɪʃən /; / ˌɑb.zɚˈveɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɑːb.zɚˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒbzəˈveɪʃən /; / ˌɒb.zəˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of watching, noticing, or paying attention to something, especially to gain information (in general or scientific contexts)

Examples

Scientific observation
Accurate observation is essential for collecting reliable data in experiments.
The observation of changes in temperature was repeated every hour.
The scientist recorded each observation carefully in her notebook.
Her observation of the children's behavior was detailed and precise.
Security cameras rely on constant observation to keep the building safe.
Through careful observation, the scientist discovered important patterns in the data.
Her observation of wildlife inspired her career in biology.
The scientist made an important discovery through careful observation of the experiment.
Under close observation, the patient’s condition began to improve.
