dhak

noun
East Indian tree with bright orange-red blooms
Pronunciation: / dɑk / (AmE), / dɑːk / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Butea monosperma) known for its intense vermilion, velvet-textured flowers and its use as a source of yellow dye.

Examples

The dhak tree
The dhak tree is often called the 'flame of the forest' because of its vivid colors.
The forest was ablaze with the vibrant flowers of the dhak in spring.
Villagers collect dhak blossoms to make natural dye for fabrics.
