rice grass

noun
a grass of the genus Oryzopsis
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪs ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈraɪs ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any perennial grass of the genus Oryzopsis, especially those resembling or related to wild rice, found in North America and used as forage.

Examples

Rice grass plant
Bison once grazed on rice grass across the vast prairies of North America.
Scientists are studying rice grass for its potential in restoring degraded rangelands.
The sandy soils of the region provide ideal conditions for rice grass to thrive.
