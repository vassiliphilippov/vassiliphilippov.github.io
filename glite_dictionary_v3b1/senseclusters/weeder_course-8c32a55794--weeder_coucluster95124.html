weeder course

noun
course designed to eliminate unqualified students
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːdər kɔrs / (AmE), / ˈwiːdə kɔːs / (BrE)
A university or college course intended to eliminate unqualified or less capable students from a particular program or field of study.

Examples

A weeder course exam
If you can't pass the weeder course, you have to change your major.
Many students struggle with the weeder course in their first year of engineering.
The biology department is known for its tough weeder course that only half the class passes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gatekeeper course - a course intended to determine whether students can proceed to advanced study
