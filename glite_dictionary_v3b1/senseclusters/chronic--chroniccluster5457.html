chronic

adjective
long-lasting or recurring (especially illness)
4.7 35/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr…ëÀê.n…™k /; / Ààkr…ëÀên…™k / (AmE), / Ààkr…ín.…™k /; / Ààkr…ín…™k / (BrE)
lasting for a long time or constantly recurring, especially about an illness, problem, or situation

Examples

Chronic pain
His chronic lateness eventually led to disciplinary action at work.
She suffered from chronic back pain that made everyday activities difficult.
The country has faced a chronic shortage of clean water for decades.
He struggled with chronic back pain for most of his adult life.
Her chronic tardiness eventually led to her losing the job.
The city faces chronic housing shortages that have lasted for decades.

Synonyms

  • persistent
    4.9 9.71/m
    persistent means something that keeps coming back or refuses to stop, while chronic describes a long-lasting or recurring illness or problem.
  • long-term
    3.0 55/m
    long-term describes effects or plans that last over many months or years, while chronic usually refers to long-lasting illnesses or problems.
  • continuing
    2.8 0.01/m
    continuing means still happening now without stopping, while chronic refers to something long-lasting or repeatedly returning, often about illness.
  • sustained
    3.9 2.48/m
    sustained means kept at a steady level without pause or weakening, while chronic may be long-term but sometimes comes and goes.
