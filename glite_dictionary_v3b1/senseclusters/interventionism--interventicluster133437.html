interventionism

noun
active medical approach
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or policy of actively intervening, especially by using medical procedures or treatments rather than conservative approaches

Examples

Medical interventionism
Some doctors favor interventionism and recommend surgery even for minor conditions.
The hospital's policy of interventionism led to more frequent use of advanced treatments.
Critics argue that excessive interventionism can increase risks without improving patient outcomes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressiveness - a tendency to take forceful or proactive action, especially in treatment
  • antonym: conservatism - a preference for minimal intervention or noninvasive approaches in medicine
