Dalton's law

noun
law on pressures in gas mixtures
6.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îlt…ônz l…î / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀêlt…ônz l…îÀê / (BrE)
a scientific law stating that the pressure exerted by a mixture of gases is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of the individual gases in the mixture

Examples

Dalton's law of partial pressures
According to Dalton's law, the total pressure in the container is 5 atmospheres.
In chemistry class, we used Dalton's law to calculate the pressure contribution of each gas.
The engineer applied Dalton's law to determine the oxygen level in the air sample.
