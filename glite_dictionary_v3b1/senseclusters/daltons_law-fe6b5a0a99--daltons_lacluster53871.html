Synonyms

  • law of partial pressures
    0.00/m5.5
    Defines total as sum of contributions each gas would make alone under same conditions
    vsDalton's law: A scientific law stating that in a mixture of gases, the total pressure is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of each individual gas, with each partial pressure being what the gas would exert if it were alone in the same volume and temperature.
  • Dalton's law of partial pressures
    0.00/m7.0
    Says total effect equals sum of each gas's effect if each alone occupied full volume
    vsDalton's law: A law stating that the total pressure exerted by a mixture of gases equals the sum of the partial pressures each gas would exert if it alone occupied the entire volume at the same temperature.
