derail a plan

verb
disrupt a plan or arrangement
5.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈreɪl ə plæn / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪl ə plæn / (BrE)
to disrupt or prevent the successful progress of an arrangement, plan, or discussion

Examples

To derail a plan
External factors sometimes derail a plan no matter how well it is prepared.
She was afraid that any mistake might derail a plan months in the making.
The mayor's resignation could derail a plan to revitalize the city center.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance a plan - help progress a plan or project
  • synonym: thwart - prevent someone from accomplishing something
