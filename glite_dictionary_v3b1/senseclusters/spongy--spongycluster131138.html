spongy

adjective
absorbent and yielding, like a sponge
2.2 1.08/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆspÊŒndÊ’i / (AmE), / ËˆspÊŒn.dÊ’i /; / ËˆspÊŒndÊ’i / (BrE)
soft, porous, and able to absorb liquid easily, like a sponge

Examples

A spongy surface
After the rain, the mat was so spongy that it dripped when stepped on.
She pressed the spongy moss and water oozed out easily.
The bread had a spongy texture that soaked up the soup perfectly.
After the rain, the ground was soft and spongy under our feet.
She preferred spongy cushions for extra comfort.
The cake had a spongy texture that made it light and airy.

Synonyms

  • spongelike
    4.9 0.02/m
    spongelike is soft and porous and often absorbs and releases liquids easily, while spongy describes something that soaks up liquid.
  • spongiform
    8.3 0.00/m
    spongiform often describes a sponge-like texture in medical or scientific contexts, while spongy refers to a soft, absorbent surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquids easily
  • antonym: dry - lacking moisture; not able to absorb
  • antonym: firm - solid and not easily compressed
  • synonym: porous - having many small holes that allow air or liquid to pass through
