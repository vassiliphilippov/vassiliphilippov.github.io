theatricalism

noun
exaggerated behavior

Pronunciation: /θiː.ˈæ.trɪ.kəl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /θɪ.ˈæ.trɪ.kəl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /θɪ.ˈæ.trɪ.kəl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /θiː.ˈæ.trɪ.kəl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
deliberately dramatic, exaggerated behavior or the use of theatrical style to attract attention, especially outside of theater

Examples

  • antonym: realism - the practice of representing things as they are, without exaggeration
  • synonym: showmanship - the skill of presenting things in an entertaining or dramatic way
  • antonym: understatement - the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
