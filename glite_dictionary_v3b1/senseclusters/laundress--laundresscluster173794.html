laundress

noun
woman who washes clothes for others
7.3 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…în.dr…™s / (AmE), / Ààl…îÀên.dr…ôs / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to wash and iron clothes, usually for other people

Examples

The laundress washes clothes
As a laundress, she worked long hours washing and pressing uniforms for the hospital.
In Victorian times, it was common for single women to earn a living as a laundress.
The wealthy family hired a laundress to care for their laundry every week.

Synonyms

  • launderer
    3.6 0.03/m
    a launderer works as a person or business washing and ironing clothes for others, while a laundress is specifically a woman doing that work.
  • washerwoman
    7.2 0.19/m
    a washerwoman washes other people's clothes for pay, while a laundress also commonly irons clothing as part of her job.
  • laundrywoman
    4.7 0.01/m
    a laundrywoman washes clothes and linens for others, while a laundress often does both washing and ironing for paying customers.
  • washwoman
    6.9 0.01/m
    a washwoman works from her own home washing other people's clothes, while a laundress usually refers to a woman employed to wash and iron clothes.
