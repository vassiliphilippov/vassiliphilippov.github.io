Asclepias verticillata

noun
a species of milkweed native to eastern US
Pronunciation: / əˈskliːpiəs vərˌtɪsɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / əˈskliːpiəs vɜːˌtɪsɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of milkweed native to the eastern United States, with slender narrow leaves arranged in whorls and clusters of greenish-white flowers.

Examples

Asclepias verticillata plant
Gardeners often plant Asclepias verticillata to attract butterflies and support pollinators.
The narrow leaves and greenish-white flowers of Asclepias verticillata make it easy to identify in the wild.
Unlike some other milkweed species, Asclepias verticillata thrives in dry, sandy soils.
