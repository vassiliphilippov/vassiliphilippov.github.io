containment

noun
policy to limit a hostile power's expansion
2.3 7.0
Pronunciation: / kənˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
the policy of preventing the expansion of a hostile power or ideology, especially by forming strategic alliances or using diplomatic measures

Examples

Cold War containment policy
Containment required a network of military alliances and significant financial commitment.
Some historians debate the effectiveness of containment in limiting Soviet influence in Eastern Europe.
The doctrine of containment shaped U.S. foreign policy during the Cold War.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appeasement - the policy of making concessions to avoid conflict with a hostile power
  • synonym: deterrence - the policy of discouraging an enemy from taking an action by instilling doubt or fear of the consequences
