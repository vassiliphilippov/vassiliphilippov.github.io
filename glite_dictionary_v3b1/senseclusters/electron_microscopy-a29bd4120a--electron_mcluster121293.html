electron microscopy

noun
microscopy using electron microscopes
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrɑn maɪˈkrɑskəpi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɒn maɪˈkrɒskəpi / (BrE)
The use of electron microscopes to obtain highly magnified images of objects, especially for scientific or medical research.

Examples

Electron microscopy images
Advances in electron microscopy have revolutionized our understanding of cell biology.
Detailed images of the crystal structure were obtained through electron microscopy.
The laboratory specializes in electron microscopy of viruses and bacteria.
