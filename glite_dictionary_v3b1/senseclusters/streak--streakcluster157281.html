streak

verb
mark with lines or spots of color
Pronunciation: / stɹik /; / striːk / (AmE), / stɹiːk /; / striːk / (BrE)
to mark or cover something with long, thin lines or patches of a different color or texture

Examples

To streak hair
Mud streaked his cheeks after he fell during the game.
Sunlight streaked the walls with golden patches in the late afternoon.
The artist streaked her canvas with bold strokes of red and yellow.
Rainwater began streaking the windows, leaving long marks down the glass.
The artist streaked blue paint across the canvas to create a sense of movement.
After running through the mud, his shoes were streaked with brown lines.
