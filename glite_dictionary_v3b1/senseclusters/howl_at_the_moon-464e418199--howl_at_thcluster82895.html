howl at the moon

verb
express strong emotion or longing
7.1
Pronunciation: / haʊl æt ðə muːn /; / haʊl æt ðə mun / (AmE), / haʊl æt ðə muːn /; / haʊl ət ðə muːn / (BrE)
to make a loud, emotional cry (like a wolf), or to express strong feelings such as frustration or longing in a dramatic way, often at night

Examples

To howl at the moon loudly
After losing the game, he wanted nothing more than to howl at the moon in frustration.
She spent the night alone, feeling the urge to howl at the moon over her lost love.
Some people howl at the moon when faced with challenges they cannot change.
She stood in the empty field and howled at the moon, feeling free and wild.
Sometimes people just need to howl at the moon to let out their frustration.
The wolves howled at the moon all night, sending chills down our spines.
