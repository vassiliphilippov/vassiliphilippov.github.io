deus ex machina

noun
unexpected agent that resolves a difficulty
Pronunciation: / ˌdeɪəs ɛks ˈmɑːkɪnə / (AmE), / ˌdeɪ.ʊs ɛks ˈmækɪnə / (BrE)
an unexpected power or event that appears and resolves a seemingly hopeless situation, especially in a play or story

Examples

A deus ex machina ending
Critics disliked the film's ending, saying the hero was rescued by a deus ex machina that felt unearned.
The billionaire investor acted as a deus ex machina, saving the company from bankruptcy at the last moment.
Whenever the story gets stuck, an author shouldn't simply introduce a deus ex machina to resolve everything.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plot device - an element introduced to advance or resolve a plot
