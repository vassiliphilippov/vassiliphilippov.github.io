shoot

verb
take a photograph
3.2 21/m
Pronunciation: /  Éut / (AmE), /  ÉuÀêt / (BrE)
to take a photograph or make a film

Examples

To shoot a photo
During our vacation, I love to shoot landscapes at sunset.
She plans to shoot every major landmark during her travels.
The photographer asked us to shoot several poses for the magazine.
She spent the afternoon shooting photographs in the city park.
The director decided to shoot the final scene at sunset for better lighting.
We plan to shoot our family portrait tomorrow morning.

Synonyms

  • take
    1.7 76/m
    take means to make a photograph or film in neutral everyday speech, while shoot is a more casual word used for photos or filming.
  • photograph
    2.1 14/m
    photograph means to take a still picture with a camera, while shoot can mean taking photos or recording moving images.
  • film
    1.0 17/m
    film means to record moving images for movies or video, while shoot can mean both filming and taking photographs.
  • snap
    2.8 3.27/m
    snap means to take a quick, informal photograph in one moment, while shoot can mean either photos or making a film.

Related Terms

  • synonym: film - to record moving images for a film
  • synonym: photograph - to take a photograph of something or someone
  • synonym: snap - to take a quick photograph
