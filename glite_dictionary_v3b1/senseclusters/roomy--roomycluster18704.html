roomy

adjective
having plenty of space
2.1 3.14/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀêmi / (AmE), / ÀàruÀêmi / (BrE)
(of a building, room, or vehicle) having plenty of space; large and comfortable.

Examples

A roomy car
She prefers roomy offices with large windows.
The minivan is surprisingly roomy and can fit all our luggage.
Their new house is much more roomy than the old apartment.

Synonyms

  • spacious
    2.5 5.19/m
    spacious suggests a large, open area and light feeling, while roomy emphasizes comfortable, easily used space inside a room or vehicle.
  • commodious
    7.4 0.29/m
    commodious is formal and suggests a roomy, comfortable interior for people, while roomy feels common and everyday for homes or cars.
  • capacious
    7.0 0.60/m
    capacious describes something that can hold a large amount, while roomy focuses on comfort and open living space.
  • airy
    2.3 1.79/m
    airy feels light and well ventilated with lots of windows, while roomy stresses large, comfortable usable space.
