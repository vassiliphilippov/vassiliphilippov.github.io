beam of light

noun
a column or stream of light
Pronunciation: / biːm əv laɪt / (AmE), / biːm əv laɪt / (BrE)
a narrow column or stream of light, typically emitted from a source such as a lamp, laser, or beacon

Examples

A beam of light shines
A single beam of light shone through the clouds, illuminating the old church tower.
Nighttime search parties often use a strong beam of light to find people in the dark.
The scientist directed a beam of light at the crystal to observe its properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flash - a sudden brief burst of light; contrasting duration
  • synonym: ray of light - a narrow line or shaft of light; similar meaning
