beam of light

noun
a column or stream of light

a narrow column or stream of light, typically emitted from a source such as a lamp, laser, or beacon

Examples

A beam of light shines
A single beam of light shone through the clouds, illuminating the old church tower.
Nighttime search parties often use a strong beam of light to find people in the dark.
The scientist directed a beam of light at the crystal to observe its properties.

Synonyms

  • light beam
    2.2 0.35/m
    light beam is a narrow visible column of light often seen in dust or fog, while beam of light names that same stream.
  • beam
    3.4 12/m
    beam is a short word for a line of light or radiation, while beam of light is specific to a light column.
  • ray
    1.0 27/m
    ray is a thin line of light or energy that may travel far, while beam of light is a thicker visible column from a source.
  • shaft of light
    3.5 0.47/m
    shaft of light is a column that shines through an opening, while beam of light can come directly from any lamp or beacon.
