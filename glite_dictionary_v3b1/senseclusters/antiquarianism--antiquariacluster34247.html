antiquarianism

noun
study of antiquities
Pronunciation: / ˌæntɪˈkwɛriənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈkweəriənɪzəm /; / ˌæntɪˈkwɛːrɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
a strong interest in collecting, studying, or admiring ancient or historical objects and antiques

Examples

Antiquarianism in collecting
The museum's collection reflects a deep antiquarianism in its focus on rare manuscripts and artifacts.
His antiquarianism led him to spend years cataloging ancient coins from the Roman Empire.
Critics argued that the historian's antiquarianism overlooked the broader social context of the period.
Her interest in antiquarianism inspired her to visit museums and historical sites around the world.
The club promotes antiquarianism by organizing lectures on ancient artifacts and rare manuscripts.
Many libraries owe their rare book collections to the passion for antiquarianism among past scholars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiquarian - a person who studies or collects antiques
  • synonym: archaeology - the scientific study of ancient peoples and cultures through their material remains
  • synonym: historic - relating to history or past events
  • antonym: modernism - a movement favoring modern ideas and methods over traditional ones
