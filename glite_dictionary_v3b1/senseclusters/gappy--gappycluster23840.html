gappy

adjective
having gaps
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæpi / (AmE), / ˈɡæpi / (BrE)
having noticeable gaps, spaces, or missing parts; not complete or continuous

Examples

A gappy fence
The old fence looked gappy after several boards fell out.
He smiled, showing his gappy teeth where some were missing.
The report was gappy and left out important information.
The old book was gappy, with several pages missing from the middle.
His explanation was gappy and left us confused about the main points.
The fence looked gappy after some of the boards fell out during the storm.
Her gappy smile made her look both unique and charming.
The dentist explained that a gappy set of teeth is quite common in children.
He felt self-conscious about his gappy front teeth when speaking in public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - without gaps or interruptions
  • antonym: even - having no gaps or spaces between parts
  • synonym: fragmentary - consisting of small parts that are not connected or complete
  • antonym: full - containing all that is needed; complete
  • synonym: gap-toothed - having a noticeable space between the teeth
  • synonym: incomplete - not finished or lacking some parts
  • synonym: spaced - having spaces between parts
