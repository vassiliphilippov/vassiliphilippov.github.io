fenestra cochleae

noun
opening in the cochlea of the inner ear
9.2
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɛstrə ˈkɑkliˌi / (AmE), / fəˈnɛstrə ˈkɒkliˌi / (BrE)
A round opening in the cochlea of the inner ear, also called the round window, that connects the middle ear with the cochlea.

Examples

The fenestra cochleae opening
Damage to the fenestra cochleae can impair the function of the inner ear.
Sound vibrations are transmitted through the oval window and exit via the fenestra cochleae.
The surgeon carefully examined the fenestra cochleae to assess its condition before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: fenestra vestibuli - oval window; another opening between middle ear and inner ear
  • synonym: round window - another name for fenestra cochleae, opening in the cochlea
