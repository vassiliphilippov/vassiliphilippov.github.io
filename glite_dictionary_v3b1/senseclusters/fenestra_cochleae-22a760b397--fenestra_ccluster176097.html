fenestra cochleae

A round opening in the cochlea of the inner ear, also called the round window, that connects the middle ear with the cochlea.

Examples

The fenestra cochleae opening
Damage to the fenestra cochleae can impair the function of the inner ear.

The surgeon carefully examined the fenestra cochleae to assess its condition before proceeding.

Synonyms

  • fenestra rotunda
    8.6 0.00/m
    fenestra rotunda is described as an opening in the middle ear wall, while fenestra cochleae names the round window into the cochlea.
  • fenestra of the cochlea
    9.0 0.00/m
    fenestra of the cochlea is an English descriptive phrase for the small opening, while fenestra cochleae is the Latin anatomical term used in labels.
  • round window
    7.6 0.27/m
    round window is the common English name for the small membranous opening, while fenestra cochleae is the formal Latin anatomical term.
