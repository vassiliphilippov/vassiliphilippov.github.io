venial

adjective
slight or forgivable fault, esp. in religion
8.3 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàviÀêni…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàviÀêni…ôl / (BrE)
(of a fault or offense) minor and easily forgiven; not serious

Examples

A venial fault
His mistake was considered venial and quickly forgiven by the community.
In theology, a venial sin does not sever one's relationship with God.
The priest explained that missing Mass out of forgetfulness is a venial sin, not a mortal one.
Arriving a few minutes late was considered a venial offense in her office.
She apologized for her venial oversight in forgetting to reply to the email.
The committee agreed that his mistake was a venial error and did not penalize him.

Synonyms

  • forgivable
    3.3 0.59/m
    forgivable is easily forgiven in everyday life, while venial is a moral term for a minor, pardonable offense.
  • excusable
    3.6 0.37/m
    excusable describes actions that have a clear reason or excuse, while venial names a small, easily forgiven moral fault.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forgivable - able to be forgiven; pardonable
  • antonym: mortal - (in religious context) describing a sin that is grave and results in complete separation from grace
  • synonym: pardonable - able to be pardoned or forgiven
  • antonym: unforgivable - not able to be forgiven; inexcusable
