tergiversate

verb
abandon one's beliefs or allegiances
9.9
Pronunciation: / tərˈdʒɪvərˌseɪt / (AmE), / təˈdʒɪvəseɪt / (BrE)
to abandon one's beliefs, principles, or allegiances, often in a way that is seen as evasive or indecisive

Examples

To tergiversate from loyalty
After years of loyalty, she chose to tergiversate and join the rival faction.
Some critics accused the author of tergiversating on his long-held philosophical views.
Under questioning, the politician began to tergiversate, avoiding a direct answer and sidestepping his earlier commitments.

Related Terms
