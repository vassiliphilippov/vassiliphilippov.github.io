elastic modulus

noun
ratio of stress to strain in a material
7.3 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / …™Àål√¶st…™k Ààm…ëÀêd í…ôl…ôs / (AmE), / …™Àål√¶st…™k Ààm…ídj äl…ôs / (BrE)
A quantity expressing a material's ability to resist deformation, defined as the ratio of applied stress to resulting strain.

Examples

The elastic modulus value
An engineer measures the elastic modulus to evaluate how a material will deform under stress.
Materials with a low elastic modulus are more flexible and easier to stretch.
The elastic modulus of steel is much higher than that of rubber.

Synonyms

  • modulus of elasticity
    7.5 0.07/m
    modulus of elasticity is the formal technical term used in engineering texts for a material's stiffness, while elastic modulus is the general name.
  • coefficient of elasticity
    7.9 0.00/m
    coefficient of elasticity is a physics phrase for the stress-to-strain ratio, while elastic modulus is the common engineering term for the same stiffness.

Related Terms
