trivalent

adjective
having three parts
Pronunciation: / traɪˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / traɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
having a valence of three, or involving three components or elements

Examples

Trivalent vaccine
A trivalent vaccine protects against three different viruses.
In chemistry, a trivalent atom can form three bonds with other atoms.
The new medication is trivalent, targeting three separate strains of the disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bivalent - having a valence of two or involving two components
  • antonym: monovalent - having a valence of one or involving one component
