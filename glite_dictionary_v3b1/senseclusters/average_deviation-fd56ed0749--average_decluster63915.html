the mean of the absolute deviations of data values from a central point, used as a measure of statistical dispersion or variability.

Examples

The average deviation value
Calculating the average deviation helps summarize the variability in a dataset.
If the data are tightly clustered, the average deviation will be relatively small.
The average deviation provides an alternative to standard deviation for measuring data spread.

Synonyms

  • average absolute deviation
    8.1 0.00/m
    average absolute deviation averages absolute differences from a central value (mean or median), while average deviation is a general name for that dispersion measure.
  • mean deviation
    7.1 0.02/m
    mean deviation averages absolute differences from the mean, while average deviation can refer to such dispersion measured from any central point.
  • mean deviation from the mean
    8.1 0.00/m
    mean deviation from the mean gives the average absolute distance to the mean, while average deviation may use other central values.
