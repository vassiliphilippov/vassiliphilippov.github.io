indebt

verb
to become obligated
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛt / (BrE)
to cause someone (including yourself) to owe money or feel obligated to another person or organization

Examples

To indebt oneself
He did not want to indebt himself to his relatives after the accident.
Many students indebt themselves to pay for their university education.
She refused to indebt her family by taking out another loan.
Taking out a large loan can indebt you for many years.
The generous gift seemed to indebt her to her friend.
He did not want to indebt himself by borrowing money from relatives.
A large loan can indebt a student for many years after graduation.
The generous gift seemed to indebt him to his friend.
Unwise spending may indebt a family beyond their ability to pay.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - to release from debt or obligation
  • synonym: obligate - to make someone legally or morally bound to do something
