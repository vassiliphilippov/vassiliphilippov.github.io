nonmetal

noun
element without metallic properties
3.7 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈnɑːn.met.əl/ /; / /ˈnɑːn.mə.təl/ (AmE), /ˈnɒn.met.əl/ /; / /ˈnɒn.mə.təl/ (BrE)
A chemical element that lacks the typical properties of metals, such as electrical conductivity, malleability, and luster.

Examples

Oxygen is a nonmetal
Oxygen, a nonmetal, is an essential component of the air we breathe.
The periodic table groups elements into categories such as metals and nonmetals.
Unlike copper, which is a metal, sulfur is classified as a nonmetal.
