Muhammadan calendar

noun
Islamic lunar calendar starting from 622 AD
8.0
Pronunciation: / məˈhæmədən ˈkæləndɚ / (AmE), / məˈhæmədən ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the lunar calendar used by Muslims, beginning from 622 AD (the year of the Hegira), in which the start of the year cycles through the solar year over 32 years

Examples

The Muhammadan calendar
Historical Islamic events are often recorded according to the Muhammadan calendar.
Ramadan always falls at a different time of year because the Muhammadan calendar is shorter than the Gregorian.
The Muhammadan calendar is based on the cycles of the moon rather than the sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Islamic calendar - the lunar calendar used by Muslims, also known as the Hijri calendar
