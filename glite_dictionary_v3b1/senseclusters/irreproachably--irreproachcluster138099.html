irreproachably

adverb
in a blameless, faultless manner
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈproʊtʃəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈprəʊtʃəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is completely blameless or cannot be criticized.

Examples

Irreproachably fair
Her record as a judge was considered irreproachably fair and impartial.
She behaved irreproachably throughout the investigation, earning everyone's respect.
The ambassador conducted himself irreproachably during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reprehensibly - in a blameworthy or shameful manner
