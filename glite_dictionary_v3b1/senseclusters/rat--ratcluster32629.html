rat

verb
to desert or betray a group
9.1 7.0
Pronunciation: /ræt/ (AmE), /ræt/ (BrE)
(informal) to betray, inform on someone, or act as a strikebreaker

Examples

To rat on friends
He was accused of trying to rat on his colleagues to get a promotion.
Nobody likes someone who will rat just for personal gain.
Some members decided to rat when the going got tough.
Anyone who chooses to rat will be seen as betraying the cause.
She refused to rat on her coworkers and stood with them throughout the protest.
Union members were furious when he decided to rat during the steelworkers' strike.
He finally admitted that he ratted out the others to save himself.
Nobody wanted to rat on their friends to the police.
She was afraid someone might rat on her for skipping class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to be disloyal; to give up or abandon a cause or person
  • synonym: defect - to abandon one's country or group for another, often for personal gain
  • synonym: scab - person who works while others are on strike
  • synonym: snitch - to secretly inform on someone
  • synonym: strikebreaker - a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike
  • synonym: tell on - to inform on someone, especially to an authority
