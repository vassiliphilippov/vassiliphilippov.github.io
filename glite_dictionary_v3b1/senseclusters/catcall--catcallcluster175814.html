catcall

verb
make loud, mocking or suggestive calls
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈkatˌkɔːl / (BrE)
to shout, whistle, or make sexually suggestive or mocking comments at someone, especially in public

Examples

To catcall a woman
A group of men began to catcall the actress as she walked down the street.
She felt uncomfortable when construction workers catcalled her on her way to work.
They were reprimanded for catcalling at the passing students outside the college gates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heckle - to interrupt a performer or speaker with derisive or aggressive comments
