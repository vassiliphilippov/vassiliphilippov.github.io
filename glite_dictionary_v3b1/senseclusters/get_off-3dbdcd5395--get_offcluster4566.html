get off

verb
dismount from a horse or animal
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to leave or dismount from a vehicle, animal, or other place you are on or in

Examples

To get off the bus
After a long ride, he finally decided to get off his horse and rest.
Please get off the mule carefully, as the ground is uneven.
The cowboy got off his horse quickly when he reached the camp.
He almost forgot to get off the bus at his stop yesterday.
Passengers should get off the plane as quickly as possible.
Please remember to get off at the next station.

Related Terms

  • antonym: board - to get on or enter a vehicle, such as a bus or train
  • synonym: dismount - to get down from a horse or other mount
  • antonym: get on - to enter or climb onto a bus, train, etc.
