dull

verb
make less sharp or intense
4.3 2.8
Pronunciation: /dʌl/ (AmE), /dʌl/ (BrE)
to make or become less sharp, intense, bright, lively, or clear (used for pain, feelings, sounds, brightness, interest, etc.)

Examples

To dull the pain
Painkillers may temporarily dull the sensation of pain, but they do not cure its cause.
Repeated use can dull the blade of a pair of scissors.
Years of neglect had dulled the shine of the antique silver.
If not polished regularly, metal surfaces will dull and look lifeless.
Sunlight can dull the color of fabrics over time.
The silverware began to dull after years of use.
Exposure to sunlight over time will dull the bright colors of the fabric.
Frequent cleaning with harsh chemicals can dull the surface of your furniture.
The years of neglect had dulled the once-glossy paint on the old house.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brighten - to make or become more bright or shiny
  • synonym: deaden - to make something (such as a sound or feeling) less strong or intense
  • synonym: diminish - make or become less
  • synonym: fade - to lose brightness, color, or strength; to become less noticeable or intense
  • synonym: numb - deprived of physical sensation or unable to feel
  • antonym: sharpen - to make or become sharp or sharper
  • synonym: tarnish - to lose or cause to lose luster, especially as a result of exposure to air or moisture
