fuffle

noun
minor commotion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌfəl / (BrE)
a minor disturbance, fuss, or commotion, usually over something unimportant

Examples

A classroom fuffle
There was a small fuffle in the hallway before class started.
The teacher quickly settled the fuffle that broke out over the missing homework.
A brief fuffle erupted when someone accidentally knocked over the stack of books.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of peace and quiet without disturbance
  • synonym: fuss - unnecessary excitement or activity about something unimportant
  • degree higher: uproar - a loud or impassioned noise or disturbance
