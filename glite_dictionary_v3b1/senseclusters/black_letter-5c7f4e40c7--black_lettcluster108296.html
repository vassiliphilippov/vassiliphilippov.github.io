black letter

noun
a medieval-style heavy typeface
4.3 8.2
Pronunciation: /blæk ˈlet.ər/ (AmE), /blæk ˈlet.ə/ (BrE)
a style of heavy, angular typeface with thick, sharply defined lines, commonly used in Western Europe from the 15th to 18th centuries

Examples

Black letter text
He struggled to read the black letter text in the old document.
Many early English books were printed using black letter typefaces.
The ancient manuscript was written entirely in black letter.
