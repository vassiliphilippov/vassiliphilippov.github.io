haulm

noun
stems of certain crops used for thatching or bedding
Pronunciation: / hɑm / (AmE), / hɔːm / (BrE)
the stems or stalks of plants such as beans, peas, potatoes, or grasses, especially when used collectively after harvesting for purposes like thatching or bedding

Examples

Potato haulm
After the potatoes were harvested, the haulm was left to dry in the field.
Farmers sometimes use haulm as bedding for livestock during the winter months.
The old cottage was roofed with haulm collected from nearby meadows.
