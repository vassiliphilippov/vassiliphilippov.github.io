irrational

adjective
not based on reason or logic
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈræʃ.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃ.ən.əl / (BrE)
not based on or using reason or clear thinking; lacking logical explanation or justification.

Examples

Irrational fear
He has an irrational fear of spiders that keeps him from going outdoors.
Her irrational outburst surprised everyone in the meeting.
It is irrational to expect success without any preparation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illogical - not making sense according to logic
  • antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with reason or logic
