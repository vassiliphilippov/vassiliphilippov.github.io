ductile

adjective
able to be shaped or drawn out (metals)
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌk.təl / (AmE), / ˈdʌk.taɪl / (BrE)
able to be drawn out into a thin wire or bent into shape without breaking; easily shaped or molded, especially describing metals.

Examples

Ductile metal wire
Copper is a highly ductile metal, making it ideal for electrical wiring.
Gold is both ductile and malleable, allowing it to be shaped into fine jewelry.
The blacksmith praised the ductile nature of the heated steel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brittle - hard but likely to break or shatter easily
  • synonym: malleable - able to be easily shaped or hammered without breaking
