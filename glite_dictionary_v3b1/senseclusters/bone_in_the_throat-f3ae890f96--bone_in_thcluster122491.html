bone in the throat

noun
persistent source of irritation or discomfort
6.6
Pronunciation: / boʊn ɪn ðə θroʊt / (AmE), / bəʊn ɪn ðə θrəʊt / (BrE)
A source of continual irritation or persistent discomfort, especially something difficult to ignore or resolve.

Examples

A bone in the throat problem
For years, the unresolved contract remained a bone in the throat of management.
Her criticism was like a bone in the throat that he couldn't ignore.
The delays in the project became a real bone in the throat for the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thorn in one's side - something or someone that continually annoys or causes trouble
