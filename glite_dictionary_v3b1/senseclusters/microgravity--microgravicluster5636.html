microgravity

adjective
very low gravity
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈɡrævəti / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈɡrævəti / (BrE)
relating to or caused by conditions of extremely weak gravity, such as those found in space or orbiting spacecraft

Examples

Microgravity experiment
Scientists conduct microgravity research on the International Space Station to learn how materials behave in space.
The microgravity environment allows plants to grow differently than they do on Earth.
Special aircraft can create short periods of microgravity for training astronauts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gravitational - relating to the force of gravity
  • synonym: weightless - having no apparent weight due to lack of gravity
