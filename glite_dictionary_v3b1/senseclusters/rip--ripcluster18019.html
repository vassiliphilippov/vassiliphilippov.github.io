rip

verb
steal something without consent (informal)
8.8 5.14/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªp / (AmE), / rÉªp / (BrE)
(informal) To steal something, especially quickly or without the owner's knowledge.

Examples

To rip a wallet
He bragged about ripping music off the internet for free.
Someone tried to rip my phone from my pocket when I wasn't looking.
The thieves ripped several laptops from the store last night.

Synonyms

  • rip off
    6.3 1.75/m
    rip off is a phrasal verb used for taking things quickly and secretly, while rip is informal and slangy for the same theft.
  • jack
    8.6 0.97/m
    jack means to steal quickly in a sneaky way in casual speech, while rip emphasises fast theft without the owner's notice.
  • knock off
    8.4 0.12/m
    knock off is slang for stealing something by theft, while rip is more informal and often means taking goods quickly and secretly.
  • steal
    1.0 96/m
    steal is the general verb for taking someone's property without permission, while rip is informal and implies quick or sneaky theft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: steal - take (another person's property) without permission or legal right and without intending to return it
