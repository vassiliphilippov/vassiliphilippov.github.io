religious right

noun
US political movement for religious conservatism
5.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪdʒəs raɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs raɪt / (BrE)
A political movement in the United States advocating social and political conservatism, often emphasizing traditional religious values and promoting policies such as school prayer and support for religious organizations.

Examples

The religious right movement
Debate over school prayer has long been a key issue for the religious right in American politics.
Many members of Congress align themselves with the religious right to support conservative legislation.
The religious right played a significant role in the outcome of the last presidential election.

Related Terms

  • antonym: religious left - A political movement advocating progressive values grounded in religious beliefs
  • synonym: social conservatism - Political philosophy that favors traditional values and social institutions
