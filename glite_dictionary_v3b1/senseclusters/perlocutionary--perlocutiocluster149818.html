perlocutionary

adjective
effect on listener
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɜrluːˈkjuːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌpɜːləˈkjuːʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to the effect that an utterance has on a listener, such as persuading, frightening, or amusing them

Examples

A perlocutionary effect
A perlocutionary statement might cause someone to feel surprised or worried.
The teacher's perlocutionary comments encouraged the students to study harder.
Jokes often have a perlocutionary effect by making people laugh.

Related Terms
