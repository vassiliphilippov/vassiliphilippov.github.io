massive

adjective
having great mass or weight
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈmasɪv /; / ˈmæsɪv / (BrE)
very large, heavy, or solid in size or amount

Examples

A massive rock
Astronomers discovered a massive star at the edge of the galaxy.
The massive oak tree had stood in the field for centuries.
The boulder was so massive that it took several machines to move it.
A massive crowd gathered for the concert, filling the entire stadium.
The massive stone walls of the castle have stood for centuries.
The artist’s massive bronze sculpture dominates the center of the plaza.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enormous - very large in size, quantity, or extent
  • synonym: heavy - of great weight; difficult to lift or move
  • antonym: light - of little weight; not heavy
  • synonym: monumental - extremely large, impressive, or important
