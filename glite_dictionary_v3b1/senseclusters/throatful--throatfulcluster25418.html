throatful

noun
angry reprimand
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈθroʊtfʊl / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊtfʊl / (BrE)
an angry or severe verbal reprimand, especially when someone is scolded loudly or harshly

Examples

A throatful of shouting
After arriving late to practice, he got a real throatful from the coach in front of everyone.
She gave her brother a throatful for borrowing her car without asking.
The manager delivered a throatful to the team after their poor performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: scolding - an angry rebuke or criticism
  • synonym: telling-off - an act of scolding or reprimanding someone
