call

verb
lure by imitating animal sound
Pronunciation: / kɔl /; / kɑl / (AmE), / kɔːl / (BrE)
to shout, cry out, or make a loud sound to attract attention or communicate

Examples

To call for help
Expert hunters can call wild turkeys successfully during the mating season.
She tried to call the fox by mimicking its high-pitched bark.
With practice, he learned to call ducks using a handmade whistle.
At dawn, the robins call from the treetops to welcome the new day.
During mating season, the frogs call loudly to attract partners.
We heard a distant owl calling in the darkness of the forest.
During the event, Jim expertly called the dances, ensuring everyone stayed in rhythm.
She learned how to call at the local square dance club every Friday night.
The caller began to call each new step to guide the dancers through the routine.
