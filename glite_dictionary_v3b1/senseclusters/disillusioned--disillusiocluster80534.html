disillusioned

adjective
having lost belief or trust
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪˈluʒənd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒənd / (BrE)
Having lost faith or trust in something formerly admired or believed to be true; disappointed after discovering a reality that falls short of expectations.

Examples

Feeling disillusioned with promises
After years with the company, Sarah became disillusioned with its broken promises and left for a new job.
He grew disillusioned after realizing that fame did not bring genuine happiness.
Many young voters felt disillusioned by the lack of political change in recent years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disenchanted - having lost hope or enthusiasm for something one admired
  • antonym: optimistic - expecting the best; hopeful
