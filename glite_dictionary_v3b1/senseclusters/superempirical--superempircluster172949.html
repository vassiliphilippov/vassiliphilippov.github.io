superempirical

adjective
beyond observation
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərɛmˈpɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɛmˈpɪərɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to knowledge or concepts that go beyond what can be observed or measured by experience

Examples

Superempirical knowledge
Some philosophers argue that certain truths are superempirical and cannot be proven by scientific methods.
The debate centered on whether the concept was superempirical or could be tested through observation.
His explanation included several superempirical assumptions that went beyond measurable evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empirical - based on observation or experience
  • synonym: transcendental - relating to knowledge beyond ordinary experience
