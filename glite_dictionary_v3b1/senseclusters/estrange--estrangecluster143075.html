estrange

verb
make someone feel alienated or distant
7.2 6.0
Pronunciation: / ɛˈstreɪndʒ /; / ɪˈstreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to cause someone to become distant, unfriendly, or no longer emotionally close to another person, group, or environment

Examples

To estrange close friends
Her decision to move abroad seemed to estrange her from her closest friends.
The constant arguments began to estrange him from his family.
Years of neglect finally estranged the two brothers, who barely spoke after that.
Constant arguments can estrange even the closest of friends.
Her political beliefs gradually estranged her from her colleagues at work.
Years of living abroad had estranged him from his family and friends.
