direct injection diesel

noun
diesel engine with direct injection technology
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən ˈdiːzəl / (AmE), / daɪˈrɛkt ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən ˈdiːzəl / (BrE)
a diesel engine in which fuel is injected directly into the combustion chamber, improving efficiency and reducing fuel consumption compared to indirect injection designs

Examples

A direct injection diesel engine
A direct injection diesel has lower emissions compared to older diesel technologies.
Many modern cars use direct injection diesel engines for better fuel efficiency and performance.
The mechanic explained the benefits of upgrading to a direct injection diesel system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indirect injection diesel - a diesel engine where fuel is injected into a pre-combustion chamber rather than directly into the main chamber
