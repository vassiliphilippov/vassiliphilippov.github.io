opaque

noun
non-transparent object
Pronunciation: / oʊˈpeɪk / (AmE), / əʊˈpeɪk / (BrE)
an object or material that does not allow light to pass through

Examples

A black opaque
The laboratory used several opaques to block out unwanted light during the experiment.
Artists sometimes use opaques in their paintings to create areas that do not reflect light.
Heavy opaques were installed on the windows to ensure complete darkness in the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: translucent - allowing some light to pass through but not transparent
  • antonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
