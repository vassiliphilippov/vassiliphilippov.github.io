wild buckwheat

noun
desert shrub of western US with small clustered flowers
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈbʌkˌwiːt / (AmE), / waɪld ˈbʌkwiːt / (BrE)
a low-growing shrub with spreading branches and loose clusters of small flowers, native to desert regions of the western United States

Examples

Wild buckwheat shrub
Wild buckwheat thrives in the arid landscapes of California and Utah.
Botanists studied the unique flowering patterns of wild buckwheat during the spring.
Many desert animals rely on wild buckwheat as part of their habitat.
