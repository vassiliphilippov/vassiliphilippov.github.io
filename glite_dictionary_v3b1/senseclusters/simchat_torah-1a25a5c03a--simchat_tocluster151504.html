Simchat Torah

noun
Jewish festival marking end of Torah reading cycle
8.4
Pronunciation: / sɪmˌxɑt toʊˈrɑ / (AmE), / sɪmˌxɑːt tɔːˈrɑː / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a Jewish festival held on the 22nd or 23rd of Tishri to celebrate the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings.

Examples

Simchat Torah festival
During Simchat Torah, the congregation completes the final verses of Deuteronomy and immediately begins Genesis again.
Every year, our synagogue celebrates Simchat Torah with dancing and singing.
Many families attend services together for Simchat Torah to mark this joyful occasion.
