glaucoma

noun
eye disease causing optic nerve damage
6.2 5.0
Pronunciation: /glɔː.ˈkoʊ.mə/ /; / /glaʊ.ˈkoʊ.mə/ (AmE), /glɔː.ˈkəʊ.mə/ /; / /glaʊ.ˈkəʊ.mə/ (BrE)
A disease of the eye characterized by damage to the optic nerve, often resulting in impaired vision and potentially leading to blindness.

Examples

Eye glaucoma disease
Early detection of glaucoma can help prevent permanent vision loss.
Many forms of glaucoma develop slowly and show no warning signs until vision is affected.
The doctor diagnosed her with glaucoma after noticing increased pressure in her eyes.
