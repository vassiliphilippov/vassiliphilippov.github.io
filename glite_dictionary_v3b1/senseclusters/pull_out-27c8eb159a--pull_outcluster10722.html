pull out

verb
withdraw from an activity or obligation
1.8
Pronunciation: / pʊl aʊt / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to withdraw from an activity, commitment, or place; to decide not to continue or to leave

Examples

To pull out of a deal
He will pull out from the agreement if the terms are not revised.
Several companies decided to pull out of the project after the initial plans changed.
She had to pull out of the tournament due to a knee injury.
Due to safety concerns, the company decided to pull out of the project.
Once the train pulls out of the station, it quickly gains speed.
The last soldiers pulled out at dawn after the peace agreement was signed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commit - to pledge or bind to a certain course or policy
  • synonym: drop out - to leave an activity, school, or competition before finishing
  • antonym: move in - to arrive and begin occupying a place, the opposite action
  • synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw, especially from danger or difficulty
  • synonym: withdraw - to remove oneself or cease to participate
