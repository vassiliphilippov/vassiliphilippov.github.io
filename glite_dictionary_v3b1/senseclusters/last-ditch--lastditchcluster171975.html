last-ditch

adjective
final attempt or effort
Pronunciation: / ˈlæst dɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːst dɪtʃ / (BrE)
(of an action or effort) made as a final attempt to achieve something, usually when facing failure or disaster.

Examples

A last-ditch effort
Despite a last-ditch appeal, the decision was not overturned.
The team launched a last-ditch offensive in the final minutes of the match.
They made a last-ditch effort to save the company from bankruptcy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desperate - feeling or showing a hopeless sense that a situation is so bad as to be impossible to deal with
  • synonym: final - coming at the end; last
