level off

verb
become flat or remain steady
3.9 1.30/m

to become steady or stop changing after a period of rising or falling; to become flat or stable

Examples

The price levels off
After a steep climb, the trail finally levelled off near the summit.
House prices have started to level off after several years of rapid growth.
The pilot waited for the plane to level off before turning off the seatbelt sign.
After years of rapid growth, the company's profits finally leveled off last year.
As the pilot guided the aircraft higher, she prepared to level off at cruising altitude.
Once the rain subsides, water levels in the river should level off and remain steady.

Synonyms

  • stabilize
    4.3 4.02/m
    stabilize is used when something becomes more steady or stops changing gradually, while level off describes a trend that becomes flat after rising or falling.
  • plateau
    5.1 0.17/m
    plateau means stop increasing and stay the same for a time, while level off can mean becoming steady after rising or falling.
  • flatline
    6.1 0.09/m
    flatline means stop changing and stay at an unchanging level, while level off is a neutral term for becoming steady.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fluctuate - to change frequently and unpredictably, opposite of leveling off
  • synonym: stabilize - to make or become steady or unlikely to change
