potassium bicarbonate

noun
chemical compound used in baking and medicine
Pronunciation: / pəˌtæsiəm baɪˈkɑrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / pəˌtæsɪəm baɪˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white crystalline powder (KHCO3) used as a leavening agent in baking and as an antacid

Examples

Potassium bicarbonate powder
Bakers sometimes substitute potassium bicarbonate for baking soda to reduce sodium content in recipes.
Doctors may recommend potassium bicarbonate to treat mild cases of acid indigestion.
The laboratory stocks several chemicals, including potassium bicarbonate for use in experiments.
