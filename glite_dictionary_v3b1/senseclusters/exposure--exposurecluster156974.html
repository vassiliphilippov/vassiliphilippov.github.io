exposure

noun
abandonment without shelter or protection
2.8
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of abandoning someone, especially an infant, without shelter or protection, typically by leaving them outdoors

Examples

Infant exposure
Historical records reveal cases of exposure in remote villages during harsh winters.
In past centuries, exposure of unwanted infants was a tragic but common practice.
The law was designed to prevent the exposure of children by desperate parents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abandonment - the act of leaving someone or something behind permanently
