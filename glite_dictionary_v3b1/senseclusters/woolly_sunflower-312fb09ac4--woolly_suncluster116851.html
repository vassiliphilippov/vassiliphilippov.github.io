woolly sunflower

noun
plant of the genus Eriophyllum
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli ˈsʌnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A common name for any plant of the genus Eriophyllum, native to western North America, with yellow daisy-like flowers and often soft, woolly leaves.

Examples

The woolly sunflower plant
The woolly sunflower thrives in dry, open areas along the western coast of the United States.
We spotted several woolly sunflowers growing along the hiking trail last spring.
Wildlife is often attracted to the bright yellow blooms of the woolly sunflower.
