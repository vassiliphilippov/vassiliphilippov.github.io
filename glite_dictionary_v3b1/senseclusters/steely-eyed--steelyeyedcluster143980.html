steely-eyed

adjective
having a firm, determined expression
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstiːliˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈstiːliˌaɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their expression) showing firmness and determination; having a hard, unyielding look in the eyes.

Examples

A steely-eyed look
A steely-eyed negotiator is essential in tough business deals.
She remained steely-eyed in the face of criticism, refusing to back down.
The general gave a steely-eyed stare as he delivered his orders to the troops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: determined - showing firmness of purpose
  • synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • antonym: weak-willed - lacking firmness of purpose; easily influenced
