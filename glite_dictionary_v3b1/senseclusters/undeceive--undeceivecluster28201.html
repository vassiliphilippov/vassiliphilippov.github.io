undeceive

verb
to free from deception or false belief
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.dɪˈsiv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈsiːv / (BrE)
to cause someone to stop being deceived or misled; to free from illusion or false belief

Examples

To undeceive someone
He was grateful to be undeceived before making a serious mistake.
It took some time to undeceive the public after the rumors had spread.
She finally managed to undeceive her friend about the true nature of the scheme.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
