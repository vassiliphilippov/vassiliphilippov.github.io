unblanched

adjective
not visibly shaken
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblæntʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɑːntʃt / (BrE)
not showing fear, shock, or embarrassment; not made pale or visibly shaken.

Examples

Unblanched courage
Despite the threats, she remained unblanched and continued reading her statement to the court.
He listened to the shocking news unblanched, showing no sign of fear or surprise.
The soldier stood unblanched before the enemy, refusing to back down.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blanched - made pale or white from shock or fear
  • synonym: undaunted - not intimidated or discouraged by difficulty or danger
  • synonym: unfazed - not disturbed or affected by something unexpected
