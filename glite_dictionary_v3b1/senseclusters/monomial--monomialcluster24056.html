monomial

adjective
single-term (math)
Pronunciation: / məˈnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / məˈnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
consisting of or relating to a single term, especially in algebraic expressions or functions

Examples

Monomial expression
A monomial expression like 3x is simpler than a polynomial with several terms.
The teacher asked the class to identify which equations were monomial and which were not.
In algebra, a monomial function contains only one nonzero term.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: binomial - an expression consisting of two terms
  • antonym: polynomial - an expression consisting of multiple terms
