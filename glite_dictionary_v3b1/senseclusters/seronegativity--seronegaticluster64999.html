seronegativity

noun
absence of antibodies
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪroʊˌnɛɡəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌsɪərəʊˌnɛɡəˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the state of not having specific antibodies in the blood serum, indicating no prior exposure or infection

Examples

Seronegativity in blood
The patient's seronegativity suggested they had not been exposed to the disease.
Routine testing confirmed the seronegativity of all new blood donors.
Researchers studied the effects of seronegativity in populations at risk for infection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: seropositivity - the presence of specific antibodies in the blood serum
