case-fatality proportion

noun
percentage of cases resulting in death (epidemiology)
4.7
Pronunciation: / keɪs fəˈtæləti prəˈpɔrʃən / (AmE), / keɪs fəˈtæləti prəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
the proportion of individuals with a particular disease who die from that disease, typically expressed as a percentage or per 1,000 cases

Examples

The case-fatality proportion rate
A low case-fatality proportion suggests that the disease is often survivable with proper treatment.
Public health officials monitor the case-fatality proportion to assess the severity of epidemics.
The case-fatality proportion for the new virus was higher than in previous outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: case-fatality rate - the proportion of deaths within a designated group of cases over a certain period
