abseil

noun
controlled descent down a rock face using a rope
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæbˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈabseɪl / (BrE)
a controlled descent down a vertical surface, such as a cliff or wall, using a rope secured at a higher point

Examples

A safe abseil
A rope was anchored at the top to ensure a safe abseil for all participants.
She completed her first abseil with the help of an experienced instructor.
The climbers prepared to abseil down the cliff face after reaching the summit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rappel - to descend a rock face by using a rope (chiefly American English equivalent)
