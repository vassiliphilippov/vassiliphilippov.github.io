blockiness

noun
visible digital blocks
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈblɑː.kɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈblɒ.kɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of an image or video appearing as visible blocks or squares, often due to low resolution or compression

Examples

Blockiness in a video
The blockiness in the photo made it hard to recognize faces clearly.
Streaming at a low bitrate can cause noticeable blockiness in fast-moving scenes.
Graphic designers try to reduce blockiness when editing digital images for print.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pixelation - the effect of displaying an image with visible pixels
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of being free from visible blocks or roughness
