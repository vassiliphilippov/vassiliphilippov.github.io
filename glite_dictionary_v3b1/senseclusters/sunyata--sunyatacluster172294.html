sunyata

noun
meditative emptiness
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃuːnˈjɑːtə / (AmE), / ʃuːnˈjɑːtə / (BrE)
in Buddhism, the concept of emptiness or the absence of inherent existence in all things, and the meditative state of inner emptiness or non-attachment

Examples

The Buddhist sunyata
During meditation, he tried to reach a state of sunyata to quiet his mind.
Many Buddhist teachings emphasize the importance of understanding sunyata for spiritual growth.
She described her experience of sunyata as a profound sense of peace and detachment.
The teacher explained how sunyata is central to understanding Buddhist philosophy.
Meditation on sunyata can help practitioners let go of attachments and ego.
Many Buddhist texts discuss sunyata as the true nature of all phenomena.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attachment - emotional connection or clinging
  • synonym: emptiness - the state of containing nothing; void
  • synonym: selflessness - the absence of a permanent, unchanging self
