hold in

verb
restrain or suppress an emotion or reaction
1.0 3.7
Pronunciation: /hoʊld ɪn/ /; / /hoʊld ən/ (AmE), /həʊld ɪn/ /; / /həʊld ən/ (BrE)
to restrain or suppress an emotion, reaction, or urge so that it is not shown or released

Examples

To hold in laughter
He tried to hold in his laughter during the serious ceremony.
She couldn't hold in her excitement when she saw the surprise.
Sometimes it's unhealthy to hold in your feelings for too long.

Sometimes it's better to express your feelings than to hold in your anger.
