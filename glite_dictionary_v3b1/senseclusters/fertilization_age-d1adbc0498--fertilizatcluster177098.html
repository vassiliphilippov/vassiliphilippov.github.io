fertilization age

noun
embryo age from fertilization
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfɜrtəlɪˈzeɪʃən eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌfəːtɪlʌɪˈzeɪʃən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
The age of an embryo or fetus measured from the time of fertilization.

Examples

Fertilization age of embryo
Doctors use fertilization age to accurately track the early development of embryos in IVF procedures.
Research studies compare outcomes based on fertilization age rather than the mother's last menstrual period.
The fertilization age is typically two weeks less than the gestational age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gestational age - age of an embryo or fetus from the first day of the mother's last menstrual period
