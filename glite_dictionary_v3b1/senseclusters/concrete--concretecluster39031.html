concrete

adjective
existing in material or physical form
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.krit /; / ˈkɑːnˌkrit / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kriːt /; / ˈkɒŋkriːt / (BrE)
existing in a real, physical form; not abstract or imaginary

Examples

Concrete evidence
Photographs can provide concrete proof of your presence at the event.
She prefers dealing with concrete facts rather than abstract theories.
The explanation lacked concrete examples and was difficult to understand.
The teacher gave a concrete example to help the students understand the concept.
We need concrete evidence before making any accusations.
Her plans became more concrete after she spoke with her advisor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstract - existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical or material existence
  • synonym: tangible - able to be touched or physically felt
