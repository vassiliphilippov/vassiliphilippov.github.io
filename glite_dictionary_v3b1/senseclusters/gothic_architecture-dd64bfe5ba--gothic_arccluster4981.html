Gothic architecture

noun
medieval European style with pointed arches
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk ˌɑːrkətɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk ˌɑːkɪtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a style of European architecture from the 12th to 16th centuries, known for pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses

Examples

Gothic architecture style
Elements of Gothic architecture can be seen in numerous churches across Europe.
Many tourists visit Paris to admire the Gothic architecture of Notre-Dame.
The cathedral is a stunning example of Gothic architecture with its soaring spires and pointed arches.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Renaissance architecture - the architectural style following Gothic, emphasizing symmetry and classical elements
  • degree lower: Romanesque architecture - the architectural style preceding Gothic, with rounded arches
