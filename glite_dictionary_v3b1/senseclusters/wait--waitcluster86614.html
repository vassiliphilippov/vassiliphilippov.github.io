wait

noun
a period of waiting
Pronunciation: / weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
a period of time when you stay in one place or do nothing, expecting something to happen or someone to arrive

Examples

A long wait
A sudden wait in the conversation made everyone feel awkward.
After a long wait, the train finally arrived at the station.
The doctor's office had an hour-long wait before I could be seen.
After a long wait, the doctor finally arrived.
There was a short wait before the next train.
We had to endure a two-hour wait at the restaurant.
