aftereffect

noun
a delayed effect following an event or treatment
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæf.tɚ.ɪˌfɛkt /; / ˈæftərɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.tər.ɪˌfɛkt /; / ˈɑːftərɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
a result or consequence that happens after an event, action, or treatment, often with a delay

Examples

Common aftereffect of treatment
One common aftereffect of chemotherapy is fatigue that lasts for days.
Researchers are studying the long-term aftereffects of concussions in athletes.
The patient experienced severe aftereffects several hours after the medication was given.
Many people experience a mild headache as an aftereffect of the medication.
The most noticeable aftereffect of the storm was days of flooding in the valley.
There can be psychological aftereffects following a traumatic event.

  • synonym: side effect - an unwanted or secondary effect of a drug or therapy
