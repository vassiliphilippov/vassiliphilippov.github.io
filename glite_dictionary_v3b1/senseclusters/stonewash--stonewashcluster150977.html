stonewash

verb
wash fabric with stones for a worn appearance
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊnˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
to wash fabric, especially denim, with stones to give it a faded, worn look

Examples

To stonewash denim
After being stonewashed, the denim feels softer and looks lighter.
Many brands stonewash their jeans to create a fashionable, distressed look.
She prefers to stonewash her own jackets for a unique vintage style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acid-wash - to treat fabric with acid to give it a faded look
