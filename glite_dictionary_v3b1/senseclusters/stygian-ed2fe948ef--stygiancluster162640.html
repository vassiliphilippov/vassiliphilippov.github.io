Stygian

adjective
dark and gloomy, hellish
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪdʒiən / (AmE), / ˈstɪdʒiən /; / ˈstɪdʒɪən / (BrE)
extremely dark, gloomy, or forbidding, especially in a way that suggests the River Styx or the underworld in Greek mythology

Examples

A Stygian darkness
His poetry often evokes Stygian images of despair and desolation.
She wandered through the Stygian gloom of the ancient forest, barely able to see a step ahead.
The power outage plunged the neighborhood into complete Stygian darkness.
A thick, Stygian mist crept across the graveyard, making everything seem eerie and forbidding.
He described the underground tunnels as a Stygian maze that no light could penetrate.
The abandoned house was plunged into a Stygian darkness as the sun set behind the hills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing
  • antonym: heavenly - of or relating to heaven, blissful
  • synonym: infernal - of or relating to hell
