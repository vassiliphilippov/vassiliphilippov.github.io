hardwired

adjective
permanently fixed
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdˌwaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌwaɪəd / (BrE)
permanently fixed or built in a way that cannot be easily changed, especially as part of a system or behavior

Examples

Hardwired behavior
Some behaviors seem to be hardwired into human nature.
Unlike software, the device's security features are hardwired and cannot be changed.
Humans are often hardwired to respond to certain emotional cues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inherent - existing as a natural or essential part
  • synonym: innate - existing from birth; natural
  • antonym: learned - acquired through experience or education
