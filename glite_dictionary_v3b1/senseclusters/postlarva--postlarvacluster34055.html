postlarva

noun
stage after larva
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈlɑːrvə / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
a young animal, especially an aquatic one, that has just passed the larval stage but is not yet fully mature

Examples

The postlarva stage
The postlarva of the shrimp is especially sensitive to changes in water temperature.
Scientists studied the growth rate of each postlarva in the laboratory tanks.
After hatching, the fish enters the postlarva stage before becoming a juvenile.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: adult - a fully mature animal
  • synonym: juvenile - a young animal that is not yet fully grown
  • degree lower: larva - an immature form of an animal that undergoes metamorphosis
