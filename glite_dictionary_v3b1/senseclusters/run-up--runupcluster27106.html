run-up

noun
sudden increase (esp. in finance)
5.9 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÊŒn ÊŒp / (AmE), / ËˆrÊŒn ÊŒp / (BrE)
A rapid or substantial increase in amount, price, or level over a short period, especially in financial or economic contexts.

Examples

Stock run-up
After a significant run-up, technology stocks began to decline in value.
Analysts attributed the run-up in oil prices to geopolitical tensions.
There was a dramatic run-up in housing values during the early 2000s.

Synonyms

  • runup
    7.5 0.01/m
    runup is the unhyphenated form seen in reports, while run-up describes a rapid financial increase over a short period.
  • jump
    3.3 3.21/m
    jump is a sudden sharp rise in level or value, while run-up describes a short period of rising prices.
  • hike
    3.6 4.19/m
    hike is a noticeable increase often set by policy or sellers, while run-up emphasizes rapid market-driven rises.
  • step-up
    3.6 0.63/m
    step-up is a rise that can be gradual or planned, while run-up refers to a fast, substantial increase in a short time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall - a decrease or drop in amount or level
  • synonym: rise - an upward movement or increase
