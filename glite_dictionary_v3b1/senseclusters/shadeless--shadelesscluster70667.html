shadeless

adjective
without shading
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪdləs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdləs / (BrE)
having no shading, gradation, or variation in light and dark; appearing flat in tone or color

Examples

Shadeless painting
The artist's early work was criticized for being shadeless and lacking depth.
A shadeless photograph can appear dull and lifeless compared to one with strong contrasts.
Her drawing looked shadeless, with no variation to suggest light or shadow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flat - lacking depth or variation in tone or color
  • synonym: monochrome - having only one color or shades of one color
  • antonym: shaded - having areas of light and dark to show depth
