skene

noun
Greek theater backdrop
Pronunciation: / skiːˈniː / (AmE), / skiːˈniː / (BrE)
a structure at the back of the stage in ancient Greek theaters, used as a backdrop and dressing area for actors

Examples

Ancient skene building
In ancient Greek drama, the skene provided a place for actors to change costumes.
The remains of the original skene can still be seen at some archaeological sites.
Decorations on the skene often helped set the scene for the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proscenium - the part of a theater stage in front of the curtain
