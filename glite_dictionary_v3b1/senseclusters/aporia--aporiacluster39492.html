aporia

noun
rhetorical doubt
Pronunciation: / əˈpɔriə / (AmE), / əˈpɔːriə / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which a speaker expresses doubt or uncertainty, often to engage the audience or highlight a problem

Examples

Rhetorical aporia
The philosopher used aporia to challenge his students' assumptions about knowledge.
In her speech, she skillfully employed aporia to make the audience question their beliefs.
Writers often use aporia to create suspense and encourage deeper thinking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - the state of being sure or confident
  • synonym: irony - the use of words to express something different from their literal meaning
