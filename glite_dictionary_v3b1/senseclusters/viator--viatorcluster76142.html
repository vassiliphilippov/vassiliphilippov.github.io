viator

noun
Roman official messenger
9.9
Pronunciation: /vaɪ.ˈeɪ.tər/ (AmE), /vaɪ.ˈeɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an official messenger or attendant in ancient Rome who carried orders or performed errands for a magistrate

Examples

Roman viator
A viator was often sent to deliver urgent messages between Roman officials.
The magistrate relied on his trusted viator to summon citizens to court.
In ancient Rome, the duties of a viator included running errands for government leaders.
