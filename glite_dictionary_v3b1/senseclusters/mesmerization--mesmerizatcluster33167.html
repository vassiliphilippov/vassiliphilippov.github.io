mesmerization

noun
historical trance induction
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛzmərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛzməraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the historical practice or theory of inducing a trance-like state through 'animal magnetism', as developed by Franz Mesmer

Examples

Historical mesmerization treatment
In the 18th century, mesmerization was believed to cure various illnesses through invisible forces.
The doctor performed mesmerization on patients, claiming it would balance their internal energies.
Interest in mesmerization faded as scientific understanding of medicine advanced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypnosis - a trance-like state of focused attention and increased suggestibility
