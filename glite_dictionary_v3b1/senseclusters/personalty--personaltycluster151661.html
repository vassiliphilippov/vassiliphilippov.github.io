personalty

noun
movable property; not real estate
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úrs…ôn…ôlti / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀês…ôn…ôlti / (BrE)
(law) movable property; all personal possessions as opposed to real estate (immovable property).

Examples

All personalty possessions
All the deceased's personalty must be identified for probate purposes.
The will specified how his personalty would be divided among his children.
Unlike real estate, personalty includes items such as cars and jewelry.

Synonyms

  • personal property
    3.1 1.36/m
    personal property refers to movable belongings such as furniture, vehicles, and jewelry, while personalty is its legal term.
  • personal estate
    6.1 0.03/m
    personal estate covers a person's movable assets and effects, while personalty is the older legal word for those possessions.
  • private property
    2.8 4.64/m
    private property often means ownership by individuals, but personalty specifically names movable possessions rather than land or buildings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chattel - an item of personal, movable property
  • antonym: realty - real estate; immovable property
