householding

noun
grouping as one household
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊshoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊshəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of grouping multiple people or accounts as a single household for billing, census, or marketing purposes

Examples

Householding for billing
Many companies use householding to send a single bill to families living at the same address.
Census data collection often relies on householding to count people living together as one unit.
Banks may offer special services to customers after confirming their householding status.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individualization - the process of treating people or accounts separately
