hair cell

noun
inner ear sensory cell
9.9 4.5
Pronunciation: /heər sel/ (AmE), /heə sel/ (BrE)
a sensory cell in the inner ear, especially in the organ of Corti, that responds to sound vibrations

Examples

Hair cells in cochlea
Damage to the hair cells in the cochlea can result in permanent hearing loss.
Researchers are developing ways to regenerate hair cells to treat deafness.
The organ of Corti contains thousands of specialized hair cells responsible for detecting sound.
