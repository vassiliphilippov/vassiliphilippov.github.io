scripting

noun
preparing set lines
Pronunciation: / ˈskrɪptɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪptɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of preparing predetermined words or lines for people to use in interactions or performances

Examples

Scripting customer calls
The company introduced scripting to help customer service agents handle difficult calls more effectively.
Actors often rely on scripting to ensure their performances are consistent each night.
Strict scripting was required for all sales presentations to maintain the brand's message.

Related Terms

  • antonym: improvisation - the act of creating or performing spontaneously without preparation
  • synonym: rehearsal - the process of practicing something before performing it
