retorsion

noun
retaliatory action
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɔrʒən /; / rɪˈtɔːrʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
a retaliatory action or response taken against someone or something, especially as a reaction to an unfriendly act

Examples

Political retorsion

After the company imposed new rules, the employees considered retorsion through a coordinated protest.
The organization issued a statement warning of possible retorsion in response to the unfair treatment.
The country imposed retorsion measures in response to the new tariffs.
Diplomats warned that retorsion could escalate the conflict between the two nations.
International law allows for retorsion when a state faces unfriendly acts from another.
