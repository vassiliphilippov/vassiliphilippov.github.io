etymology

noun
origin and history of a word
Pronunciation: / ˌɛtəˈmɑlədʒi /; / ˌɛt̬ɪˈmɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛtɪˈmɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of the origin and history of words, including how their forms and meanings have changed over time

Examples

The etymology of words
Many linguists are fascinated by the etymology of common English words.
The etymology of the word "hospital" can be traced back to Latin.
When writing the dictionary, she researched the etymology of every entry.
Her passion for etymology led her to research the history of hundreds of English words.
Many linguists specialize in etymology to better understand the evolution of language.
The etymology of the word 'salary' reveals its origins in the Latin word for salt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: derivation - the formation of a word from another or from a root
  • synonym: lexicology - the study of the form, meaning, and behavior of words
  • synonym: semantics - the study of meaning in language
