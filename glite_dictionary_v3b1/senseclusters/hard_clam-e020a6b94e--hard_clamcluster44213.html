hard clam

noun
edible American Atlantic clam
6.7
Pronunciation: / hɑrd klæm / (AmE), / hɑːd klæm / (BrE)
a large, edible clam found along the Atlantic coast of North America, whose heavy shell was once used as money by some Indigenous peoples

Examples

A hard clam shell
Fishermen harvested hundreds of hard clams from the bay this morning.
She prepared a delicious chowder using fresh hard clams from the local market.
The shells of the hard clam were once highly valued as wampum by Native Americans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quahog - another name for the hard clam, especially in regional usage
