abreaction

noun
release of emotional tension (psychoanalysis)
8.8 9.1
Pronunciation: /æb.ri.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /ə.bri.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /æb.ri.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /ə.bri.ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the release or expression of emotional tension, often by reliving a traumatic experience.

Examples

Emotional abreaction
His sudden outburst was seen by the therapist as a form of abreaction.
The therapy session provided a safe space for abreaction to occur.
Through abreaction, the patient was able to confront and release repressed emotions.
