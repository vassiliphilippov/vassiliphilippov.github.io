Gloriosa superba

noun
ornamental poisonous climbing plant
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊˈriːoʊsə suːˈpɜːrbə / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈrɪəʊsə suːˈpɜːbə / (BrE)
A perennial climbing herb native to tropical Africa and Asia, known for its showy red, yellow, or purple flowers and poisonous parts.

Examples

Gloriosa superba plant
All parts of Gloriosa superba are toxic, so care must be taken when handling the plant.
In some regions, Gloriosa superba is used in traditional medicine despite its poisonous nature.
The vibrant flowers of the Gloriosa superba make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
