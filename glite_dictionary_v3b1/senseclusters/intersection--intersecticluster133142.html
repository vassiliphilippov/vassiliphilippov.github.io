intersection

noun
the act or process of crossing or meeting
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of crossing or overlapping, or the shared area or ideas between things

Examples

Intersection of ideas
In chess, the intersection of strategies determines the outcome of the game.
The intersection of the two roads was blocked by police tape.
The detective waited for the intersection of the suspect's and victim's paths.
The intersection of linguistics and psychology has led to new insights in cognitive science.
Their research focuses on the intersection between technology and society.
We discussed the intersection of culture and politics in our seminar.

  • synonym: overlap - the extent to which two concepts, groups, or sets share elements
  • antonym: union - the act of joining together or combining
