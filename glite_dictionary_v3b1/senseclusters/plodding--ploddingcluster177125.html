plodding

adjective
slow, laborious movement
Pronunciation: / ˈplɑdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplɒdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of movement or progress) slow, steady, and lacking in excitement or energy, often in a laborious way.

Examples

A plodding pace
Although his running was plodding, he finished the marathon with determination.
The horse maintained a plodding gait as it pulled the heavy cart up the hill.
The team made plodding progress through the dense paperwork.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brisk - quick and energetic
  • synonym: leaden - heavy and slow, lacking liveliness
