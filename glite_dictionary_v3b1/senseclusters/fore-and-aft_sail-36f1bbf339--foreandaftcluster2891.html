fore-and-aft sail

noun
sail rigged in the keel's direction, not on a yard
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔr ən ˈæft seɪl / (AmE), / ˌfɔːr ən ˈɑːft seɪl / (BrE)
a sail that is rigged along the line of the keel rather than at right angles to it, not set on a yard

Examples

A fore-and-aft sail
The skipper explained the advantages of using a fore-and-aft sail in changing wind conditions.
The yacht was equipped with a large fore-and-aft sail to increase its speed.
Unlike traditional square sails, the fore-and-aft sail allows for better maneuverability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: square sail - a sail set at right angles to the keel
