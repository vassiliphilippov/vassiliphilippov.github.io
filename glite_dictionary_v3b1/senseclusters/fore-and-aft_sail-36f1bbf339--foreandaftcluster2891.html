fore-and-aft sail

noun
sail rigged in the keel's direction, not on a yard
9.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔːr.ən.dæft seɪl/ /; / /ˈfɔːr.æn.dæft seɪl/ (AmE), /ˈfɔːr.ən.dɑːft seɪl/ /; / /ˈfɔːr.ən.dæft seɪl/ (BrE)
a sail that is rigged along the line of the keel rather than at right angles to it, not set on a yard

Unlike traditional square sails, the fore-and-aft sail allows for better maneuverability.
