marm

noun
disapproving term for woman
9.9
Pronunciation: / mɑrm / (AmE), / mɑːm / (BrE)
an older woman, often regarded as strict, fussy, or unpleasant, usually used disapprovingly

Examples

A strict marm
The children whispered about the strict marm who lived at the end of the street.
Some people thought the new neighbor was a bit of a marm because of her constant complaints.
He joked that his aunt was a real marm when it came to house rules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lady - a polite or formal term for a woman
  • synonym: matron - an older married woman, often with authority
