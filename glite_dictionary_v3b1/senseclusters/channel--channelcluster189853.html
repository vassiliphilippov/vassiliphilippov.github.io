channel

noun
bodily passage or tube
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.əl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.əl / (BrE)
a passage or tube in the body, lined with epithelial cells, that conveys a secretion or other substance

Examples

Narrow channel in tissue
A substance can move rapidly through a narrow channel within the tissue.
Specialized channels in the skin release sweat to the surface.
The tear channel becomes blocked, causing the eye to water excessively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canal - a tubular passage in the body
  • synonym: duct - a tube or vessel in the body that transports fluids
