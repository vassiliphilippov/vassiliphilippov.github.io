neurotypical

adjective
typical brain development
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
having typical neurological development or functioning, especially as contrasted with autism or neurodivergence

Examples

Neurotypical development
Most neurotypical children adapt easily to changes in routine.
The event was designed to be inclusive for both neurotypical and neurodivergent participants.
Some neurotypical people may not understand the challenges faced by those on the autism spectrum.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neurodivergent - having neurological development or functioning that differs from what is considered typical
