fodder

noun
coarse animal feed; livestock food
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑː.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɒd.ə / (BrE)
coarse food for livestock, typically made from whole plants, leaves, or stalks of cereal crops

Examples

Animal fodder
Corn stalks are commonly used as fodder on many farms.
Drought led to a shortage of fodder for the cattle this year.
The farmer stored bales of fodder in the barn for the winter.
