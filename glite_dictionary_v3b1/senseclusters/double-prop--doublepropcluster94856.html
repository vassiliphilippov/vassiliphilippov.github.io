double-prop

noun
plane with two counter-rotating propellers
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl prɑp / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl prɒp / (BrE)
A propeller plane fitted with an engine that drives two propellers in opposite directions to improve stability.

Examples

A double-prop plane
Pilots often prefer a double-prop for improved performance in turbulent conditions.
The engineer explained how the double-prop system enhances aircraft stability.
This museum features a rare World War II double-prop on display.
