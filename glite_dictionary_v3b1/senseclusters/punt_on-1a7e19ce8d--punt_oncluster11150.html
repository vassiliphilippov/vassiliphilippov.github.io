punt on

verb
postpone a decision or action
8.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / pÊŒnt É‘n / (AmE), / pÊŒnt É’n / (BrE)
to postpone making a decision or taking action on something, often to avoid commitment or because the situation is unclear

Examples

To punt on a decision
I'm not ready to decide today, so let's just punt on this for now.
The board chose to punt on the budget proposal until more information became available.
Whenever tough choices arise, they tend to punt on the issue rather than face it.

Synonyms

  • sit on
    4.2 1.29/m
    sit on is to delay deciding while you think it through, whereas punt on suggests putting off a decision to avoid commitment.
