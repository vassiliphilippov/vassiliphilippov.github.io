Synonyms

  • pellucid
    9.9 0.04/m
    pellucid is a very clear, formal word for transparent liquids or glass, while transparent is the general see-through adjective.
  • limpid
    7.4 0.22/m
    limpid describes very clear liquids or eyes with calm look, while transparent means simply allowing light to pass through.
  • crystal clear
    3.1 0.42/m
    crystal clear says something is perfectly easy to see through, while transparent is the neutral term for see-through things.
  • see-through
    3.9 0.61/m
    see-through describes thin cloth you can see through, while transparent is a wider adjective for anything that lets light pass.
  • clear
    1.0 43/m
    clear often means free from dirt or obstacles and can be used for view, while transparent strictly means see-through.
  • lucid
    4.8 0.14/m
    lucid often means easy to understand or mentally clear, while transparent describes physical materials that let light through.
  • diaphanous
    9.6 0.48/m
    diaphanous means very thin, delicate fabric that almost shows shapes behind it, while transparent means fully see-through.
  • gauzy
    6.7 0.99/m
    gauzy describes loosely woven, semi-transparent fabric that blurs shapes, while transparent means clear and easily seen through.
