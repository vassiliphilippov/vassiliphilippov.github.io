segregated

adjective
separated or kept apart from others
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛɡ.rəˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡ.rɪˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of people or things) separated or kept apart from others, especially on the basis of race, religion, or other differences.

Examples

Segregated schools
Many schools in the past were segregated by race.
Residents lived in a segregated neighborhood for decades.
The company was criticized for its segregated workspaces.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrated - combined or mixed together, especially referring to people of different backgrounds
  • synonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not joined or connected
