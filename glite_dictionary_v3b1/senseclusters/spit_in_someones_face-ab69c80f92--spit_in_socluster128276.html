spit in someone's face

verb
show strong contempt or insult
2.6
Pronunciation: / spɪt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz feɪs / (AmE), / spɪt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz feɪs / (BrE)
to show strong contempt or disrespect for someone, often by acting in a way that deliberately insults or humiliates them

Examples

To spit in someone's face openly
By refusing to apologize, he might as well have spit in her face.
Her decision to leave the company was seen as an attempt to spit in their face after years of poor treatment.
The politician's comments felt like he was spitting in the voters' faces.
