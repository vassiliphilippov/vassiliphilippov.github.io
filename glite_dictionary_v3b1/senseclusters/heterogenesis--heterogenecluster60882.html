heterogenesis

noun
alternation of different life cycle forms
5.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the alternation of two or more distinct forms or generations in the life cycle of a plant or animal, especially with different modes of reproduction

Examples

Heterogenesis in life cycle
Some parasites exhibit heterogenesis, alternating between sexual and asexual phases during their life cycles.
The study focused on organisms that use heterogenesis as a reproductive strategy.
Understanding heterogenesis is important in the context of evolutionary biology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homogenesis - reproduction involving only one form or generation
  • synonym: metagenesis - alternation between sexual and asexual generations in an organism's life cycle
