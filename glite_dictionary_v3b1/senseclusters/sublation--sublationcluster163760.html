sublation

noun
removal or abolition
Pronunciation: / sʌˈbleɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌˈbleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing, abolishing, or cancelling something, especially in a formal or philosophical context

Examples

Sublation of a rule
The philosopher discussed the sublation of old beliefs in the process of developing new ideas.
Legal sublation of outdated laws can help modernize a country's legal system.
The sublation of certain traditions was necessary for social progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: establishment - the act of starting or creating something
  • synonym: withdrawal - the act of removing or taking something away
