projection

noun
the act of sticking or extending outwards
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a part or feature that sticks out or extends from a surface or object

Examples

A projection on the wall
A small projection on the map marked the peninsula's tip.
The architect admired the delicate projection of the balcony from the facade.
The cliff had a narrow projection that jutted out over the sea.
Branches are projections extending from the trunk of a tree.
In anatomy, the bone has a small projection where muscles attach.
The projection on the side of the building provides extra shade in the afternoon.
A projection on the ceiling housed the smoke detector.
Sharp projections on the rock made climbing difficult.
The lizard's back was covered in small bony projections.
