role-play

verb
to act as a character
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌpleɪ / (BrE)
to act out or perform the part of a character in a situation, story, or game, often for practice, training, or entertainment

Examples

To role-play a scene
During the workshop, participants role-played different customer service scenarios to improve their communication skills.
The teacher asked the students to role-play a conversation between a doctor and a patient.
In therapy, clients sometimes role-play difficult situations to practice their responses.
