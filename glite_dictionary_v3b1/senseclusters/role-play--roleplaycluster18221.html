Synonyms

  • roleplay
    4.7 0.14/m
    roleplay is the single-word form often used in modern texts, while role-play names the same acting activity for practice or fun.
  • act out
    3.7 1.30/m
    act out is to perform a scene or feeling in front of others, while role-play often means practicing a character in training or games.
  • play the role of
    3.0 2.51/m
    play the role of is a phrase used in plays and films to say someone performs a character, while role-play suggests practice or a game.
  • act
    2.1 19/m
    act means performing as an actor on stage or film, while role-play is pretending a character often for training or entertainment.
  • enact
    4.8 3.21/m
    enact is a more formal word for staging or performing a scene, while role-play feels informal and is used for practice or games.
