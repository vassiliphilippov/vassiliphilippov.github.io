settleability

noun
ability to settle
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛtələˈbɪləti /; / ˌsɛtələˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌsɛtələˈbɪləti /; / ˌsɛtələˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of particles or materials (such as sludge or soil) to settle out of suspension or compact under their own weight

Examples

Settleability of sludge
Engineers measure the settleability of sludge to design effective wastewater treatment systems.
Poor settleability can lead to cloudy water in the final treatment stage.
The laboratory test showed improved settleability after adding the coagulant.
Engineers tested the settleability of the construction site soil before starting the project.
The settleability of wastewater sludge affects how easily it can be separated from water.
High settleability in fill material can lead to uneven building foundations over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compaction - the process of becoming more dense or compacted
  • synonym: sedimentation - the process by which particles settle to the bottom of a liquid
  • synonym: settling - the process of sinking or compacting under weight
  • antonym: stability - the quality of being stable and not likely to move or change
