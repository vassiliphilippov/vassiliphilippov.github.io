Kepler's law of planetary motion

noun
one of three laws describing planetary motion (Kepler, astronomy)
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkep.lərz lɑː əv ˈplæn.ə.ter.iː ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkep.lərz lɔː əv ˈplæn.ə.ter.iː ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkep.lərz lɑː əv ˈplæn.ɪ.tri ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈkep.ləz lɔː əv ˈplæn.ɪ.tri ˈməʊ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkep.ləz lɔː əv ˈplæn.ɪ.təri ˈməʊ.ʃən/ (BrE)
one of the three scientific laws formulated by Johannes Kepler describing the motion of planets around the Sun, outlining their elliptical orbits, area speeds, and the relationship between orbital period and distance from the Sun.

Examples

Kepler's law of planetary motion
According to Kepler's law of planetary motion, planets move in elliptical orbits around the Sun.
Scientists can predict the positions of planets using Kepler's law of planetary motion.
The explanation of Mars's orbit was greatly improved after the discovery of Kepler's law of planetary motion.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Kepler's first law - the law stating planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus
  • degree higher: Kepler's third law - the law relating the square of a planet's orbital period to the cube of its average distance from the Sun
