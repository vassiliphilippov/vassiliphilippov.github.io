Bercy butter

noun
flavored butter with wine, shallots, and parsley
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrsi ˌbʌtər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːsi ˌbʌtə / (BrE)
A butter mixture creamed with white wine, shallots, and parsley, used especially as a sauce for grilled meats and fish.

Examples

Bercy butter sauce
He topped the grilled steak with a generous dollop of Bercy butter for extra flavor.
The chef prepared Bercy butter by mixing shallots and parsley into softened butter with a splash of white wine.
You can store Bercy butter in the refrigerator for several days and use it to enhance fish dishes.
