wave equation

noun
mathematical equation describing wave propagation
Pronunciation: / weɪv ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / weɪv ɪˈkweɪʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical equation, typically a partial differential equation, that describes how waves, such as sound or light waves, propagate through a medium

Examples

The wave equation solution
By solving the wave equation, we can predict the behavior of light in fiber optic cables.
Physicists often use the wave equation to model the movement of electromagnetic waves in space.
The wave equation is fundamental in understanding how sound travels through air and other media.
