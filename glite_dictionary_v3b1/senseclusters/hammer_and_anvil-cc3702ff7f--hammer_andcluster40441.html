hammer and anvil

noun
tools for metalworking; mutual shaping relationship (metaphor)
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmər ənd ˈænvəl / (AmE), / ˈhæmə ənd ˈænvɪl / (BrE)
A pair consisting of a hammer and an anvil, especially as tools used together in metalworking; also used metaphorically to describe a relationship in which one party or force shapes or influences the other, often through repeated interaction.

Examples

The hammer and anvil tools
The blacksmith skillfully used the hammer and anvil to shape the horseshoe.
The process became a hammer and anvil situation, with both sides influencing the outcome.
Their discussions acted as a hammer and anvil, refining each other's ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anvil - a heavy iron block used in metalworking
  • synonym: hammer - a hand tool with a heavy head used for striking
