bullnosed plane

noun
carpenter's plane with front-positioned blade
8.4 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbʊl.noʊzd pleɪn/ (AmE), /ˈbʊl.nəʊzd pleɪn/ (BrE)
a small carpenter's plane with the cutting edge set close to the front edge, used for work in confined spaces or close to corners

Examples

A bullnosed plane tool
Because of its compact size, the bullnosed plane is ideal for detailed joinery work.
He used a bullnosed plane to smooth the wood close to the corner of the cabinet.
The carpenter reached for his bullnosed plane to finish the tight space near the end of the board.
