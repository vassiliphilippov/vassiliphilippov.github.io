cataract

noun
large waterfall; violent rush of water
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəˌrækt / (AmE), / ˈkatərakt / (BrE)
A large waterfall, especially one with a steep drop and intense flow of water.

Examples

A mighty cataract
During the rainy season, the river transforms into a powerful cataract.
The mighty cataract thundered down the cliffs, sending mist into the air.
Tourists gathered to admire the breathtaking view of the cataract at sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waterfall - A flow of water falling from a height
