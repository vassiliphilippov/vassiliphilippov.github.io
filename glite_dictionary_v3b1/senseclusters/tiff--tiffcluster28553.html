tiff

verb
to have a minor quarrel
6.8
Pronunciation: / tɪf / (AmE), / tɪf / (BrE)
to have a small argument or quarrel, especially between friends or partners

Examples

To tiff over chores
They sometimes tiff about chores, but it never lasts long.
After they tiffed over dinner plans, they quickly apologized to each other.
It is normal for siblings to tiff over small things from time to time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or not argue
  • synonym: argue - to exchange opposing views, often in a heated way
  • synonym: quarrel - to have an angry disagreement or fight
