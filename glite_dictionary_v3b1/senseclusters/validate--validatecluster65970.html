validate

verb
make legally or officially valid
1.3 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈvæl.ɪ.deɪt/ /; / /ˈvæl.ə.deɪt/ (AmE), /ˈvæl.ɪ.deɪt/ /; / /ˈvæl.ə.deɪt/ (BrE)
to confirm or make something officially valid, correct, or acceptable (including by providing evidence or proof)

Examples

To validate a decision
Her election was validated after a review confirmed the results.
The court must validate the contract before it can be enforced.
The government agency refused to validate his license to practice medicine.
She needed to validate her parking ticket before leaving the garage.
The experiment was designed to validate the scientist's hypothesis.
The results validate our earlier conclusions about the effectiveness of the new drug.
Please validate your parking ticket at the machine before leaving the garage.
The bank can validate your identity with this form of identification.
The results need to be validated by independent researchers to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something
  • antonym: invalidate - to make something invalid or null, especially legally
  • antonym: refute - to prove to be false or incorrect
