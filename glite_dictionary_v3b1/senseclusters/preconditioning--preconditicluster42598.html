preconditioning

noun
protective stress exposure
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
a medical or biological process in which mild stress is applied to increase resistance to later, more severe stress or injury

Examples

Tissue preconditioning
Scientists use preconditioning to help tissues survive periods of low oxygen.
The study showed that preconditioning with mild heat stress improved recovery after injury.
Doctors are exploring preconditioning as a way to protect the heart during surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - the process of becoming better suited to an environment or stress
  • synonym: conditioning - the process of training or adapting to a stimulus
