grasp

noun
understanding or knowledge
Pronunciation: / ɡræsp / (AmE), / ɡrɑːsp / (BrE)
an understanding or knowledge of a subject, idea, or situation

Examples

A grasp of English
Despite his efforts, his grasp of the language remains limited.
She has an excellent grasp of mathematics and logic.
The book gave me a clearer grasp of the political situation.
His grasp of the new regulations is impressive and thorough.
She has a strong grasp of mathematical concepts, which helps her solve complex problems.
Without a firm grasp of the basics, it is hard to advance in this subject.
