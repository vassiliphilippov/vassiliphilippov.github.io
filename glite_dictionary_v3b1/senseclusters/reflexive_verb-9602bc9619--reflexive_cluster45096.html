reflexive verb

noun
grammatical term; verb acting on itself
9.4
Pronunciation: / rəˈflɛksɪv vɝb /; / rəˈflɛksɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
a verb whose subject and object are the same person or thing, usually used with a reflexive pronoun (like 'myself', 'yourself', etc.)

Examples

Reflexive verb example
'He prides himself on his work'—here 'prides' is used as a reflexive verb.
In some languages, every reflexive verb uses a special pronoun.
In the sentence 'She dressed herself', 'dressed' is a reflexive verb.
He explained that 'enjoy oneself' is considered a reflexive verb in English grammar.
In Spanish, many actions are expressed using a reflexive verb.
Wash is a reflexive verb in the sentence 'She washed herself.'

Related Terms

  • antonym: transitive verb - a verb that requires a direct object
