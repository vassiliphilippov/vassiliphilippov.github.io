vestibular vein

noun
vein draining the inner ear structures
8.0 7.2
Pronunciation: /və.ˈstɪ.bjə.lər veɪn/ (AmE), /və.ˈstɪ.bjʊ.lə veɪn/ (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the saccule and utricle in the inner ear

Examples

Vestibular vein in ear
Damage to a vestibular vein can affect the function of the saccule and utricle.
During surgery on the inner ear, the vestibular vein must be identified and preserved.
The vestibular vein plays a key role in draining blood from the inner ear's balance organs.
