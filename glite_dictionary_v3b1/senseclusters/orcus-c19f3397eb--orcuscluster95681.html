Orcus

proper noun
Roman god of the underworld
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…îrk…ôs / (AmE), / Àà…îÀêk…ôs / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of the underworld, associated with death and the punishment of souls; the counterpart to the Greek god Hades.

Examples

Roman god Orcus
In ancient texts, Orcus was feared as the punisher of those who broke oaths.
Many myths describe Orcus as similar to the Greek god Hades.
The temple dedicated to Orcus was said to be located in central Italy.
