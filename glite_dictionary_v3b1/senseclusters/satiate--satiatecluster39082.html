to satisfy a desire, appetite, or need fully or to excess; to make someone feel they have had enough or too much

Examples

To satiate hunger
A large meal can easily satiate even the hungriest appetite.
Her curiosity was finally satiated after hearing the entire story.
The lavish dessert buffet will satiate anyone with a sweet tooth.
After three generous helpings of dinner, he felt completely satiated.
She satiated her sweet tooth by indulging in rich chocolate cake.
The buffet was so abundant that even the hungriest guest could satiate themselves.

Synonyms

  • sate
    6.2 0.79/m
    sate fills hunger or desire fully and is more common in speech, while satiate often suggests being overfull.
  • satisfy
    2.0 12/m

  • slake
    9.0 0.18/m
