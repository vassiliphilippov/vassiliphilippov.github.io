entirely

adverb
completely; solely, without others involved
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtaɪɚli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪəli / (BrE)
completely; fully; with nothing else included or involved

Examples

Done entirely alone
She completed the project entirely on her own, without any help.
The responsibility for the mistake was entirely his.
The school is entirely devoted to supporting children with special needs.
He relied entirely on her advice to make the final decision.
The audience was entirely silent during the emotional performance.
The plan is entirely different from anything we've tried before.

  • antonym: partly - to a limited extent; not fully
