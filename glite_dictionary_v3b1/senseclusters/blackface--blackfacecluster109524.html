blackface

noun
makeup used to imitate Black people (offensive)
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈblæk.feɪs / (BrE)
A practice in which a performer paints their face with dark makeup to mimic and caricature a Black person, especially in minstrel shows; now recognized as offensive.

Examples

Blackface makeup
Discussions about blackface often include its history in minstrel performances.
Many people were offended when the comedian appeared in blackface for the sketch.
The use of blackface in early American theater is now widely condemned as racist.
