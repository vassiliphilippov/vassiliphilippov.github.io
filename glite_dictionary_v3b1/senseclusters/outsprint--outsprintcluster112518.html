outsprint

verb
to run faster
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈsprɪnt / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈsprɪnt / (BrE)
to run faster than someone else, especially in a race or competition

Examples

To outsprint a rival
He managed to outsprint his rival in the last 50 meters of the race.
Despite a slow start, she outsprinted everyone to win the gold medal.
No one could outsprint the champion during the final lap.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lag - to fall behind in a race or competition
  • synonym: overtake - to move ahead of someone, especially in a race
