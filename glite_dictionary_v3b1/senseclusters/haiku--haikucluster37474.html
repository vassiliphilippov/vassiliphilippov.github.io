haiku

noun
a Japanese three-line poem
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪku / (AmE), / ˈhaɪkuː / (BrE)
A traditional Japanese poem of three lines, typically containing 17 syllables in a 5-7-5 pattern, often focusing on nature or an ephemeral moment.

Examples

A haiku poem
Many students tried composing their first haiku in English class.
She wrote a haiku about the beauty of a cherry blossom tree.
The poet shared a haiku at the open-mic night, captivating the audience with its simplicity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tanka - a Japanese poem of five lines and 31 syllables
