zombie

noun
reanimated dead body (in folklore)
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈzɑːm.bi / (AmE), / ˈzɒm.bi / (BrE)
a dead body brought back to life by supernatural means, especially in stories, folklore, or voodoo beliefs

Examples

A zombie rises
In the story, a witch uses a secret potion to raise a zombie from its grave.
Movies about the zombie apocalypse have become extremely popular in recent years.
The villagers were terrified when a zombie was seen wandering through the fields at night.
According to voodoo belief, a zombie is a dead person brought back to life by magic.
Legends describe how a sorcerer could use a spirit to create a zombie from a corpse.
The villagers feared the arrival of a zombie in their community.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: corpse - A dead body, usually of a human but not reanimated
  • synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person
  • synonym: ghoul - Evil spirit or phantom, especially one supposed to rob graves and feed on corpses
