central scotoma

noun
central blind spot in vision
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəl skoʊˈtoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl skəʊˈtəʊmə / (BrE)
A blind spot affecting the center of the field of vision, including the fixation point.

Examples

A central scotoma spot
A central scotoma made it difficult for the patient to see faces and read text.
Macular degeneration often results in the development of a central scotoma.
The doctor identified a central scotoma during the visual field examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scotoma - an area of partial or complete loss of vision surrounded by a field of normal vision
