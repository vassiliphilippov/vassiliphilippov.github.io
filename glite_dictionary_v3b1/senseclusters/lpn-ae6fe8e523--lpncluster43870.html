LPN

noun
licensed practical nurse; state-licensed caregiver
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl.piˈɛn / (AmE), / ˌɛl.piˈɛn / (BrE)
a nurse who is licensed by a state after completing specific training to provide routine care for the sick

Examples

LPN nurse
After completing her training, she began working as an LPN at the local hospital.
Every LPN must renew their license regularly to continue practicing.
The clinic hired three new LPNs to help manage the patient load.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: RN - a registered nurse with more advanced qualifications than an LPN
