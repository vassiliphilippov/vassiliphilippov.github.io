Rushdie

proper noun
British novelist of Indian origin
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌʃdi / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃdi / (BrE)
Salman Rushdie (born 1947), a British writer of Indian origin known for his novels, including 'The Satanic Verses,' which sparked significant controversy and led to a religious fatwa against him.

Examples

Rushdie Booker Prize
Rushdie won the Booker Prize for his novel 'Midnight's Children.'
Many literary critics consider Rushdie's writing both imaginative and provocative.
The controversy surrounding Rushdie brought global attention to issues of free speech and censorship.
