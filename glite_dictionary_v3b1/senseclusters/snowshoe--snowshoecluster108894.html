snowshoe

verb
travel on snowshoes
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊˌʃu /; / ˈsnoʊˌʃuː / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌʃuː / (BrE)
to travel or walk over snow using snowshoes strapped to your feet

Examples

To snowshoe across fields
After lunch, we decided to snowshoe through the forest to enjoy the fresh snow.
Last winter, she snowshoed across the lake to visit her friend on the other side.
Many tourists love to snowshoe along mountain trails when visiting the Alps.
We spent the morning snowshoeing across the frozen lake to reach the remote cabin.
Last winter, she snowshoed through the forest to enjoy the peaceful scenery.
They plan to snowshoe up the mountain trail after the fresh snowfall.
