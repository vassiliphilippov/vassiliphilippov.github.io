underrun

verb
haul (rope or cable) onto a boat
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈrʌn / (BrE)
(of a rope, cable, or net) to haul onto a boat, especially for inspection or repair.

Examples

To underrun a cable
The crew had to underrun the cable after the storm caused tangles.
They underran the line to inspect it for wear and tear.
We underrun the fishing nets every morning to check for damage.
