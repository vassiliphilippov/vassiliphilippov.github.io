Carolina parakeet

noun
extinct parrot native to the United States
Pronunciation: / kəˈrəʊlɪnə ˈpærəˌkiːt / (AmE), / kəˈrɛlɪnə ˈpærəkiːt / (BrE)
an extinct species of small parrot that was native to the eastern, midwestern, and plains regions of the United States

Examples

The Carolina parakeet bird
Scientists have studied the extinction of the Carolina parakeet to better understand human impact on wildlife.
The Carolina parakeet was once common in the forests of the southeastern United States.
The bright green feathers of the Carolina parakeet made it especially vulnerable to hunters.
