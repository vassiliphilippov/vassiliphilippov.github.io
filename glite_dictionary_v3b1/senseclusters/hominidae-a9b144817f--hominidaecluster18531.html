Hominidae

noun
the family of humans and great apes
7.7 7.9
Pronunciation: /hɑː.mɪ.ˈnaɪ.diː/ /; / /hɑː.mɪ.ˈniː.diː/ (AmE), /hɒ.mɪ.ˈnaɪ.diː/ /; / /hɒ.mɪ.ˈniː.diː/ (BrE)
the biological family that includes modern humans, their immediate extinct ancestors, and other great apes such as chimpanzees, gorillas, and orangutans

Examples

The Hominidae family
All modern humans, as well as chimpanzees and gorillas, are classified within Hominidae.
The fossil was identified as belonging to the Hominidae family due to its skeletal features.
The study of Hominidae evolution sheds light on the origins of our own species.
