unload

verb
remove cargo from a vehicle or container
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈloʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈləʊd / (BrE)
to remove goods, cargo, or people from a vehicle, container, or similar place

Examples

To unload a truck

Please unload your suitcases from the car so we can take them inside.
The workers began to unload the boxes from the truck at sunrise.
It took an hour to unload all the passengers at the station.
Please unload your groceries at the counter before parking your car.
The workers began to unload the boxes from the truck.
