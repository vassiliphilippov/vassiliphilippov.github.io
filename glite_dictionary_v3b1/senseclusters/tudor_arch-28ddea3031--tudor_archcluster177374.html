Tudor arch

noun
low, slightly pointed arch in Tudor architecture
6.1 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈtuː.dər ɑːrtʃ/ /; / /ˈtjuː.dər ɑːrtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈtjuː.də ɑːtʃ/ /; / /ˈtuː.də ɑːtʃ/ (BrE)
An arch of low height with a slightly pointed or elliptical shape, typically formed from four centers, characteristic of Tudor architecture.

Examples

A Tudor arch shape
Architects often use the Tudor arch to evoke the charm of English Renaissance buildings.
The doorway was framed by a beautiful Tudor arch that gave the house a historic appearance.
The window is topped with a classic Tudor arch, adding elegance to the room.
