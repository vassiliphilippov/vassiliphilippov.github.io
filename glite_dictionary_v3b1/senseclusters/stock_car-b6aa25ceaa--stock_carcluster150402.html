stock car

noun
racing car based on a standard production model
9.1
Pronunciation: / stɑk kɑr / (AmE), / stɒk kɑː / (BrE)
A racing car with the basic frame and structure of a commercially available automobile, often modified for speed and safety in competitions.

Examples

Stock car racing
He spent years restoring an old Chevy into a competitive stock car.
Many American motorsport events feature stock cars rather than open-wheel racers.
The stock car sped past the finish line, thrilling the cheering crowd.
