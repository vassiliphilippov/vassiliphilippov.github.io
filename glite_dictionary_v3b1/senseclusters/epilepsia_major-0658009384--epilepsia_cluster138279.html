epilepsia major

noun
form of epilepsy with loss of consciousness and spasms
8.9 7.8
Pronunciation: /e.pɪ.ˈlep.siː.ə ˈmeɪ.dʒər/ /; / /ɪ.pɪ.ˈlep.siː.ə ˈmeɪ.dʒər/ (AmE), /e.pɪ.ˈlep.siː.ə ˈmeɪ.dʒə/ /; / /ɪ.pɪ.ˈlep.siː.ə ˈmeɪ.dʒə/ (BrE)
a severe type of epileptic seizure with loss of consciousness and strong, whole-body convulsions (formerly called 'grand mal')

Examples

Severe epilepsia major
During the nineteenth century, epilepsia major was a common term for major convulsive episodes.

Doctors once used the term epilepsia major to describe severe generalized seizures.
Symptoms of epilepsia major include a sudden loss of consciousness and violent muscle contractions.
The patient suffered an epilepsia major at work and was rushed to the hospital.
