jump

verb
to skip or bypass
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to pass over or bypass something, especially unintentionally or by omission

Examples

To jump a line
He accidentally jumped a line while copying the text, so it didn't make sense.
If you jump a step in the process, the recipe won't turn out right.
Please do not jump any pages in the manual, or you'll miss important information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miss - to fail to include or notice
  • synonym: skip - to omit or move past something
