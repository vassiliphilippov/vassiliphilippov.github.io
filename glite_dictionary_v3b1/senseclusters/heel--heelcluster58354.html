heel

verb
to tilt or lean to one side
7.3 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / hil /; / hiÀêl / (AmE), / hiÀêl / (BrE)
(of a boat or object) to tilt or lean to one side, especially because of wind or movement

Examples

Boat heels over
As the waves grew, the vessel began to heel dangerously to one side.
The old ferry would often heel in rough weather, making the passengers uneasy.
The strong wind caused the sailboat to heel sharply to port.
The strong wind heeled the sailboat dangerously to port during the storm.
As the yacht heeled sharply, everyone grabbed onto the railings for support.
If the boat heels too much, it can become unstable and capsize.

Synonyms

  • list
    8.8 3.51/m
    list is an unintended leaning of a ship to one side, while heel describes a boat tilting from wind or movement.
  • tilt
    2.0 16/m
    tilt means to move or tip into a sloping position, while heel often refers to a boat leaning to one side from wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: list - (of a vessel) to lean to one side
  • antonym: right - to return a ship or boat to an upright position
  • synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
