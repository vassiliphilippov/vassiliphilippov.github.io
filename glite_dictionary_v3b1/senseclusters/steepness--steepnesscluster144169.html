steepness

noun
degree of how steep something is
3.3 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / Ààstip.n…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàstiÀêp.n…ôs / (BrE)
The quality or degree of being steep, especially of a slope or incline.

Examples

The steepness of slope
Be careful when driving down the hill because its steepness can be deceptive.
Engineers measured the steepness of the slope to assess the risk of landslides.
The steepness of the mountain trail made the climb challenging even for experienced hikers.

Synonyms

  • grade
    4.6 2.07/m
    grade shows how steep a road or path is in everyday terms, while steepness is the general quality of a slope.
  • gradient
    5.7 1.57/m
    gradient is the technical measure of how sharply a surface slopes, while steepness is the general degree of an incline in plain use.
  • slant
    3.4 1.18/m
    slant means a tilt or angle often used for lines or views, while steepness means how sharp a slope is.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradualness - the quality of being gradual, opposite of steepness
  • synonym: incline - a slope or slanted surface
