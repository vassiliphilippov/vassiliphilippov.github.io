hostile expedition

noun
military operation in a foreign country
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑstəl ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈhɒstaɪl ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a military operation undertaken to achieve a specific objective in a foreign country, especially one involving armed conflict

Examples

A hostile expedition launched
Historical records describe a hostile expedition launched against the neighboring kingdom.
The general led his troops on a hostile expedition across the border, aiming to secure new territory.
The government sent a hostile expedition to quell the uprising in the distant region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: campaign - a series of military operations intended to achieve a particular objective
  • synonym: invasion - an instance of entering a place by force with an army
