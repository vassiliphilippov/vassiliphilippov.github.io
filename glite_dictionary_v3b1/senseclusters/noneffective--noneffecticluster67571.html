noneffective

adjective
not in force
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌnʌnɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnʌnɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
not having legal force or not currently in effect, especially referring to laws, rules, or agreements

Examples

Noneffective law
The new regulation was declared noneffective until it passed through the final approval process.
Because the contract was unsigned, it remained noneffective and could not be enforced.
Any changes to the policy are noneffective until officially announced by the board.
