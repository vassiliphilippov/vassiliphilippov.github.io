sibylline

adjective
mysterious and prophetic
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪbəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪliːn /; / ˈsɪbɪˌliːn / (BrE)
mysterious, enigmatic, or like a prophecy; difficult to interpret or understand

Examples

A sibylline message
Her sibylline remarks hinted at changes to come, but gave no specifics.
The novel's ending was deliberately sibylline, leaving readers to interpret its true meaning.
The priest delivered a sibylline warning that left everyone puzzled.
His speech was so sibylline that it left the audience puzzled and intrigued.
She delivered a sibylline warning that seemed to conceal more than it revealed.
The author is known for his sibylline remarks, always open to multiple interpretations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure
  • synonym: enigmatic - difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious
  • synonym: oracular - resembling an oracle; mysterious and prophetic
  • synonym: prophetic - relating to or characteristic of a prophet or prophecy
