noninvasiveness

noun
quality of not being invasive (medical)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnɪnˈveɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪnˈveɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of not involving cutting into the body or entering it with instruments, especially in medical procedures.

Examples

Medical noninvasiveness
Many patients prefer treatment methods that emphasize noninvasiveness to reduce risk and discomfort.
Researchers highlighted the noninvasiveness of the new technique as a major advantage over traditional surgery.
The noninvasiveness of ultrasound makes it a preferred option for many diagnostic tests.
