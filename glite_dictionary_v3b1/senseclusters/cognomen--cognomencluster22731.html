cognomen

noun
surname or family name
Pronunciation: / kɑgˈnoʊmən / (AmE), / kɒgˈnəʊmən / (BrE)
a surname or family name, as distinguished from a given name; especially used in ancient Rome to denote a family within a clan

Examples

Roman cognomen
Her cognomen was widely recognized among scholars of history.
Many Roman citizens had a cognomen that indicated their family lineage.
The ancient inscription revealed both the man's praenomen and his cognomen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: given name - the personal name given to an individual at birth or baptism, as opposed to the family name
  • synonym: surname - the family name passed from one generation to another
