northern red oak

noun
large North American deciduous tree

A large deciduous tree native to eastern North America, recognized for its broad leaves with spiny-tipped lobes and rounded crown; its wood is coarse-grained and less durable than that of white oaks.

Examples

The northern red oak tree
Autumn brings vibrant red and orange leaves to the northern red oak lining our street.
The northern red oak is often planted in parks for its attractive shape and fast growth.
Woodworkers sometimes use northern red oak in furniture, though it is less durable than white oak.

Synonyms

  • Quercus rubra
    9.1 0.00/m

  • Quercus borealis
    8.9 0.00/m
