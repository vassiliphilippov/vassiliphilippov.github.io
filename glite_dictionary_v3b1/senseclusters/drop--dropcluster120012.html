drop

verb
remove and leave (cargo or people) somewhere
2.7 18/m
Pronunciation: / drÉ‘p / (AmE), / drÉ’p / (BrE)
to remove (cargo, people, etc.) from a vehicle and leave them at a particular place

Examples

To drop someone off
Could you drop me at the train station on your way to work?
She asked the taxi to drop her outside the main entrance.
The driver dropped the boxes at the warehouse before heading back.

Synonyms

  • set down
    3.2 0.13/m
    set down means removing passengers or cargo from a vehicle in a formal, careful way, while drop is a common everyday verb for this action.
  • put down
    3.4 0.82/m
    put down means placing people or things out of a vehicle at a stop, while drop is used more broadly for leaving them.
  • drop off
    3.0 9.27/m
    drop off means delivering someone or something to a place and leaving them there, while drop is a more general verb.
  • unload
    2.6 6.55/m
    unload means taking goods or cargo out of a vehicle, often by heavy work, while drop can mean leaving people or items anywhere.
  • offload
    3.9 0.44/m
    offload means removing goods or cargo from a vehicle, often in a work setting, while drop is the general act of leaving people or things.
