pall

verb
become less interesting or appealing
4.3
Pronunciation: / pɔl /; / pæl / (AmE), / pɔːl / (BrE)
to become or make something become less interesting, appealing, or enjoyable; to lose freshness or appeal

Examples

Excitement palls
At first, the movie was thrilling, but the endless action sequences soon palled.
Her enthusiasm for the new diet quickly palled as the meals became repetitive.
The novelty of the job began to pall after only a few months.
After being left open overnight, the champagne began to pall and tasted flat.
Craft beer enthusiasts know that their drink can pall if not stored properly.
If the cider is exposed to too much air, it will quickly pall.
After a few days, the excitement of the new job began to pall.
The novelty of city life soon palled on him, and he longed for the countryside.
What at first seemed thrilling can quickly pall if repeated too often.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or weary
  • synonym: cloy - to disgust or sicken with excess sweetness or richness
  • antonym: enthuse - to make (someone) interested or excited
  • synonym: fade - to lose strength or brightness
  • degree higher: interest - the feeling of wanting to know or learn about something, which can diminish as something palls
  • synonym: stale - (of food or drink) no longer fresh or pleasant to eat or drink
  • synonym: wane - to decrease in size, extent, or degree; to diminish in intensity
