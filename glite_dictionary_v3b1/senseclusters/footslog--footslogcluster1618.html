footslog

verb
walk with effort, especially when tired
9.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfʊt.slɑːg/ (AmE), /ˈfʊt.slɒg/ (BrE)
to walk heavily and with effort, especially over long distances or difficult ground, often when tired

Examples

To footslog long distances
After a long day, the hikers footslogged back to their camp exhausted.
She was not looking forward to footslogging across the forest path again in the morning.
We had to footslog through mud and rain for miles before reaching the village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: march - to walk with regular steps, often as part of a group or for a long distance
  • antonym: stroll - to walk in a leisurely, relaxed way
  • synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically from exhaustion or harsh conditions
