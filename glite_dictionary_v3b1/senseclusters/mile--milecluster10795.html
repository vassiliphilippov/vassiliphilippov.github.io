mile

noun
unit of length (1,609.344 meters)
1.0 180/m
Pronunciation: / maÉªl / (AmE), / maÉªl / (BrE)
a unit of linear measurement equal to 1,760 yards, 5,280 feet, or exactly 1,609.344 meters, chiefly used in the United States and the United Kingdom

Examples

A mile long
He can run a mile in just under five minutes.
The city is ten miles from here by road.
The nearest town is about three miles away.

Synonyms

  • international mile
    3.0 0.00/m
    international mile is the standardized length used in modern measures and tables, while mile is the general traditional unit in use.
  • statute mile
    3.0 0.01/m
    statute mile is the legally defined measurement set by law for land use, while mile is the ordinary name people use.
  • land mile
    2.0 0.01/m
    land mile is the common name used to distinguish the land measure from a nautical mile, while mile is the general unit of distance.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: yard - a unit of length equal to 3 feet or 0.9144 meters
