fuel

verb
supply with fuel
2.0 6.48/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfju…ôl /; / ÀàfjuÀê…ôl / (AmE), / Ààfj ä…ôl / (BrE)
to supply or put fuel into something (such as a vehicle or machine) so it can work

Examples

To fuel a generator
The firefighters worked quickly to fuel the back-up generators during the blackout.
They stopped at the roadside station to fuel the truck before heading into the mountains.
Traditional steam locomotives were often fueled by coal shoveled directly into the firebox.
All aircraft must fuel before the long-haul flight.
The ship was scheduled to fuel at the next port.
The tanker fueled in Bahrain before continuing its journey.
Drivers stopped at the station to fuel their cars for the long journey.
The mechanics will fuel the plane before takeoff.
The workers fueled the ship early in the morning.

Synonyms

  • refuel
    3.0 1.25/m
    refuel means adding more fuel so a vehicle can keep going, while fuel means supplying fuel more generally to make something work.

Related Terms
