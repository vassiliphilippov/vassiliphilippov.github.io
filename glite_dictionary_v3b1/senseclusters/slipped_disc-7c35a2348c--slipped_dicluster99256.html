slipped disc

noun
back injury involving a spinal disc
4.0 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆslÉªpt dÉªsk / (AmE), / ËˆslÉªpt dÉªsk / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a disc between the bones of the spine ruptures or moves out of place, often causing pain, especially in the lower back

Examples

A slipped disc injury
After lifting a heavy box, he found out he had a slipped disc and needed physical therapy.
Because of her slipped disc, she had to take time off work to recover.
The doctor explained that a slipped disc can put pressure on nearby nerves, causing intense back pain.

Synonyms

  • herniated disc
    5.1 0.13/m
    a herniated disc is soft inner tissue pushing out and pressing nerves, while a slipped disc is the disc moving or tearing in the spine.
  • ruptured intervertebral disc
    6.5 0.00/m
    a ruptured intervertebral disc is the disc tearing between vertebrae and often causing nerve pain, while a slipped disc can also mean disc movement.
