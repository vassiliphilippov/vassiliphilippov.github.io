wet-nurse

noun
woman hired to breastfeed another's child
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛt.nɜrs / (AmE), / ˈwɛt.nɜːs / (BrE)
a woman who is employed to breastfeed the child of another person

Examples

A wet-nurse for a baby
Her ancestor had worked as a wet-nurse in Victorian England.
In centuries past, it was common for royal infants to be fed by a wet-nurse.
The wealthy family hired a wet-nurse to care for their newborn daughter.
