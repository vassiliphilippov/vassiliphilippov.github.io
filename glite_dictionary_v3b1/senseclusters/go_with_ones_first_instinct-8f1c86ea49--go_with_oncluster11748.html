go with one's first instinct

verb
act on your initial intuition
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ wɪð wʌnz fɜrst ˈɪnstɪŋkt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð wʌnz fɜːst ˈɪnstɪŋkt / (BrE)
to decide or act according to your initial intuition or immediate feeling, rather than overthinking or second-guessing

Examples

To go with your first instinct
If you don't know which painting to choose, just go with your first instinct.
She regretted changing her answer and wished she had just gone with her first instinct during the test.
Whenever I'm uncertain, I try to go with my first instinct instead of overanalyzing the situation.
