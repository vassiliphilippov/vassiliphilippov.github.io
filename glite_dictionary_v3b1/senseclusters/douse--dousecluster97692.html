Synonyms

  • extinguish
    4.4 2/m
    extinguish is a formal term for putting out flames or lights, while douse is a common verb for the same action.
  • put out
    2.0 3/m
    put out is a casual phrasal verb used to stop fires or lights, while douse is slightly more vivid and physical.
  • snuff out
    4.7 0.3/m
    snuff out means ending a flame often by covering it, while douse suggests putting out by pouring or wetting.
  • snuff
    5.7 2/m
    snuff means pinching or covering a candle to stop its flame, while douse usually involves pouring liquid to put out fires.
  • smother
    5.3 0.6/m
    smother means covering or pressing to cut off oxygen and stop a fire, while douse implies using water or liquid.
  • blow out
    2.4 1/m
    blow out means extinguishing a small flame by blowing air on it, while douse usually means pouring water or liquid to stop burning.
