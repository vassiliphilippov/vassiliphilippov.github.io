on the gripping hand

adverb
introduces a third, contrasting point of view
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə ˈɡrɪpɪŋ hænd / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ˈɡrɪpɪŋ hænd / (BrE)
used to introduce a contrasting or alternative point of view, usually as a third consideration after 'on the one hand' and 'on the other hand'

Examples

On the gripping hand phrase
I could drive or take the train; on the gripping hand, staying home might be best.
She might accept the offer, or she could decline; on the gripping hand, she hasn't replied at all.
We've considered the advantages and disadvantages, but on the gripping hand, there's still the issue of cost.
