Chionochloa conspicua

noun
tall New Zealand grass used for thatching
9.9
Pronunciation: / kiˌoʊnoʊˈkloʊ.ə kənˈspɪkjuːə / (AmE), / kiˌɒnəˈkləʊ.ə kənˈspɪkjuːə / (BrE)
A large New Zealand grass with tall flower stems, traditionally used by Maori people for thatching roofs.

Examples

Tall Chionochloa conspicua grass
Botanists studied Chionochloa conspicua to understand its ecological role in native grasslands.
Large clumps of Chionochloa conspicua can often be seen on New Zealand hillsides.
The Maori people traditionally used Chionochloa conspicua for thatching the roofs of their houses.
