nondemocracy

noun
non-democratic government
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɪˈmɑkrəsi /; / ˌnɑn.dɪˈmɑː.krəsi / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈmɒkrəsi /; / ˌnɒn.dɪˈmɒ.krəsi / (BrE)
a country or system of government that is not democratic or does not allow free and fair elections

Examples

A nondemocracy country
Many nondemocracies restrict access to independent media and limit political opposition.
The report compared the economic growth of democracies and nondemocracies over the past decade.
Citizens in a nondemocracy often have little influence over government decisions.
The country transitioned from a nondemocracy to a democratic system after years of political reform.
Living under a nondemocracy often means citizens have limited freedom to express their opinions.
International organizations criticized the government's move toward nondemocracy and lack of free elections.
The country was considered a nondemocracy because its elections were neither free nor fair.
Many citizens protested against the nondemocracy that limited their rights and freedoms.
International observers criticized the government for maintaining a nondemocracy despite holding regular elections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autocracy - a system of government by one person with absolute power
  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: dictatorship - a government ruled by a dictator with total power
