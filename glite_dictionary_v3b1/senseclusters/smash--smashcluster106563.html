smash

verb
hit a ball overhead forcefully (tennis)
1.0
Pronunciation: / smæʃ / (AmE), / smæʃ / (BrE)
to hit, strike, or collide with great force, including in sports (such as a powerful shot in tennis)

Examples

To smash the ball
He often smashes the ball to finish rallies quickly.
She leapt up to smash the ball past her opponent at the net.
The crowd cheered as she smashed the overhead for a winning point.
A baseball smashed through the window during the game.
Lightning smashed into the old oak tree during last night's storm.
The car smashed into the wall, leaving a trail of debris behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to separate or cause to separate into pieces as a result of a blow or strain
  • synonym: crash - to collide violently, especially with a loud noise
  • antonym: lob - (in tennis) a stroke that lifts the ball high over an opponent
