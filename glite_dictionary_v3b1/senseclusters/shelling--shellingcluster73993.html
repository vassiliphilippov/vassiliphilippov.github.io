shelling

noun
artillery bombardment
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the firing of artillery shells over an area to bombard it heavily, often without aiming at a specific target

Examples

Heavy shelling attack
After hours of shelling, the soldiers finally advanced into the city.
Civilians sought shelter underground to escape the intense shelling.
The town endured constant shelling during the siege.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombardment - a continuous attack with bombs, shells, or other missiles
