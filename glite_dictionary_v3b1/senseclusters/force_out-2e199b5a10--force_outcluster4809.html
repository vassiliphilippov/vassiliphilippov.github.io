force out

noun
baseball play where runner is put out by force
7.2 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs a ät / (AmE), / f…îÀês a ät / (BrE)
An act in baseball where a base runner is put out by a fielder holding the ball and touching the base the runner is forced to advance to before the runner arrives.

Examples

A baseball force out
He was disappointed to be retired on a force out at third.
The inning ended with a close force out at second base.
The shortstop made a quick throw to second base for the force out.

Synonyms

  • force-out
    7.8 0.00/m
    force-out is the hyphenated baseball name for this put-out play, while force out is the same idea written as two words.
  • force play
    7.3 0.01/m
    force play is the baseball phrase for a play that puts a runner out, while force out names the act of that put-out.
  • force
    7.3 0.68/m
    force is the short baseball word for a put-out on a forced advance, while force out is the fuller two-word form.
