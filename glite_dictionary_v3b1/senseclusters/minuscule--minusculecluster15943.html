minuscule

noun
lowercase letter; typographical character
6.2 5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnəˌskjul / (AmE), / ˈmɪnəskjuːl / (BrE)
A lowercase letter, especially in historical contexts where typographers distinguished such characters from uppercase (majuscule) letters.

Examples

A minuscule character
Modern editors often distinguish between minuscule and majuscule forms when transcribing ancient texts.
The introduction of minuscule letters greatly improved the legibility and speed of writing.
The medieval manuscript was written entirely in minuscule, making it more compact.

Related Terms
