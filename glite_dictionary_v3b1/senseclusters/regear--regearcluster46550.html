regear

verb
to reorganize strategy
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɡɪr / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɡɪə / (BrE)
to change the structure, strategy, or organization of something to adapt to new conditions or goals

Examples

To regear a company
The company decided to regear its operations to focus on digital services.
After facing new regulations, the team had to regear their approach to compliance.
Many organizations regear quickly in response to changing market demands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to change in order to suit new conditions
  • antonym: maintain - to keep something unchanged
  • synonym: restructure - to organize something in a new way
