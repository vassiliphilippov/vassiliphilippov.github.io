jump-start

verb
to start or re-start energetically
3.8
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp stɑrt / (AmE), / dʒʌmp stɑːt / (BrE)
to start or re-start something vigorously or energetically, especially a process, activity, or project that has stalled or is inactive

Examples

To jump-start a project
After the long break, the team needed some motivation to jump-start their productivity.
She brought in a consultant to jump-start the company's marketing efforts.
The government hopes to jump-start the economy by investing in new infrastructure projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kick-start - to provide fresh impetus or initiate something energetically
