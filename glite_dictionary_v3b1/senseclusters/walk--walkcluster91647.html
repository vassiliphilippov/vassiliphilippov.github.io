walk

verb
allow a batter to reach first base; baseball term
Pronunciation: / wɑk / (AmE), / wɔːk / (BrE)
(in baseball) to allow a batter to go to first base after four balls, or to advance to first base in this way

Examples

To walk a batter
Sometimes it's a good strategy to walk a dangerous hitter.
The pitcher decided to walk the batter rather than risk a home run.
With the bases loaded, the pitcher accidentally walked in a run.
He was disappointed not to get a hit, but happy with his ability to walk and get on base.
The batter managed to walk after the pitcher threw four consecutive balls.
The pitcher began to struggle, allowing two players to walk in the same inning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: base on balls - a baseball term for being allowed to go to first base due to four balls
