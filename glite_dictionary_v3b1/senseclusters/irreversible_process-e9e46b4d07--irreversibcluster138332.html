irreversible process

noun
a process that cannot be reversed
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɪvərˈsəbl ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪːvəˈsɜːbl ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a process or change that cannot be undone or returned to its original state, especially in science or engineering contexts

Examples

An irreversible process in science
In thermodynamics, combustion is often cited as an irreversible process.
Melting ice in a warm room is an irreversible process because the water cannot easily turn back into ice without intervention.
Once rusting begins, it is an irreversible process unless special treatments are applied.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reversible process - a process that can be reversed, returning to its original state
