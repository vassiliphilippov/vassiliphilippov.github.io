first lady

noun
leading woman in a field
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
The most prominent or influential woman in a particular field, group, or profession.

Examples

First lady of jazz
In jazz, Ella Fitzgerald is often dubbed the first lady of song.
She is regarded as the first lady of American theater for her decades of outstanding performances.
The first lady of finance delivered the keynote speech at the conference.
