backstage

noun
area of a theater out of audience view
3.6
Pronunciation: / bækˈsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / bækˈsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
the part of a theater or performance venue that is out of sight of the audience, where preparations and other activities occur

Examples

Theater backstage
After the show, fans lined up to get a glimpse backstage.
Only authorized personnel are allowed backstage during the performance.
She waited backstage anxiously before her debut.
