reillumination

noun
renewed understanding
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˌluːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of gaining renewed understanding, insight, or clarity, especially after a period of confusion or uncertainty

Examples

Reillumination of an idea
The teacher's explanation led to a sudden reillumination of the difficult concept for the students.
After a long period of doubt, she experienced reillumination and felt confident in her beliefs again.
The discussion provided a much-needed reillumination of our goals and direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to understand
  • antonym: confusion - a state of being unclear or uncertain
  • synonym: enlightenment - a state of gaining knowledge or understanding
