instrumentalization

noun
treating as a tool
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstrəˌmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrʊˌmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating something or someone as a means to achieve a particular goal, rather than valuing it for itself

Examples

Instrumentalization of people
The instrumentalization of education for economic gain is a topic of ongoing debate.
Many activists oppose the instrumentalization of nature for short-term profit.
Some critics warn against the instrumentalization of relationships in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciation - recognition of the value or worth of something or someone
  • synonym: exploitation - the act of using someone or something unfairly for your own advantage
