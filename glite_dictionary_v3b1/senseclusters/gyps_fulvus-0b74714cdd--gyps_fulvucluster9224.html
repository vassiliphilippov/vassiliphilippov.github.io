Gyps fulvus

noun
large vulture of Europe/North Africa
Pronunciation: / dʒɪps ˈfʊlvəs / (AmE), / dʒɪps ˈfʊlvəs / (BrE)
A large vulture of southern Europe and northern Africa, noted for its pale body plumage and black wings; also known as the griffon vulture.

Examples

The Gyps fulvus vulture
The Gyps fulvus plays an important role in the ecosystem by cleaning up animal carcasses.
We spotted a Gyps fulvus soaring above the cliffs during our trip to Spain.
Wildlife reserves have implemented policies to protect the habitat of the endangered Gyps fulvus.
