break

noun
sudden dash to escape
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
a sudden escape or movement, especially from confinement or control

Examples

A break for freedom
Suddenly, the dog saw an opening and made a break for the gate.
The prisoner made a daring break from his cell during the night.
With the guards distracted, she spotted her chance and went for a break.
Authorities were on high alert after the late-night break at the county jail.
The break from the maximum-security prison shocked the entire city.
The break was discovered at dawn when two inmates were found missing.
