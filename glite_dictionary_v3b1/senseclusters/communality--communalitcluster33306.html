communality

noun
shared variance (statistics)
9.4
Pronunciation: /kə.mjuː.ˈnæl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /kə.mjuː.ˈnæl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /kə.mjuː.ˈnæl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /kə.mjuː.ˈnæl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the proportion of a variable's total variance explained by common factors in factor analysis, indicating shared variance with other variables

Examples

Communality in factor analysis
The communality of a variable helps researchers understand how much it shares with other variables in the analysis.
High communality values suggest that most of the variable's variance is explained by common factors.
If a variable has low communality, it may not be well represented by the factor model.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniqueness - the proportion of a variable's variance not explained by common factors
