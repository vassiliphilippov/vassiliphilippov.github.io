pierce

verb
(of a sound) go through sharply or shrilly
8.9 3.7
Pronunciation: /pɪrs/ (AmE), /pɪəs/ (BrE)
(of a sound) to go through something sharply or shrilly, especially so as to be strongly noticed or felt.

Examples

A siren pierces silence
A siren pierced the calm of the early morning.
Her shrill laughter pierced the silence in the hall.
Thunderous applause pierced the stillness of the theater.
