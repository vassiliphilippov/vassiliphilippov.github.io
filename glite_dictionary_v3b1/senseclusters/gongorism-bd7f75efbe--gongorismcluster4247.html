Gongorism

noun
literary style marked by ornate elegance
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːŋɡəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒŋɡərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Affected and ornate style of writing introduced into Spanish literature by the poet Luis de Góngora.

Examples

Excessive Gongorism style
Critics accused him of excessive Gongorism in his complex metaphors and convoluted syntax.
Modern scholars reevaluate Gongorism as an innovative rather than merely affected style.
The poet's early works show little trace of Gongorism, but later poems are highly ornate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceptismo - Contrasting Spanish literary style focused on cleverness and wit
  • synonym: culteranismo - Ornate literary style associated with Góngora
