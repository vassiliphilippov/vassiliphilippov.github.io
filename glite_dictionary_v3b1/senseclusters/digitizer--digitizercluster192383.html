digitizer

noun
person who converts materials to digital form
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪdʒ.ɪ.taɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈdɪdʒ.ɪ.taɪ.zə/ (BrE)
A person who converts physical materials (documents, photographs, manuscripts, audio or video) into digital formats, often by scanning, photographing, or otherwise creating digital files and preparing them for storage or access.

Examples

Archive digitizer
The library hired a digitiser to convert its archives into searchable digital copies.
Our team of digitisers scanned and tagged thousands of photographs for the museum's online collection.
After the flood, a volunteer digitiser rescued fragile manuscripts by creating high-resolution images.
