reactively

adverb
in response to events
Pronunciation: / riˈæktɪvli / (AmE), / riˈæktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that responds to events or actions after they have happened, rather than acting in advance

Examples

Responds reactively to problems
The government acted reactively to the crisis instead of preparing in advance.
If you always respond reactively, you may miss opportunities to prevent problems.
The team handled the situation reactively, waiting for issues to arise before taking action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proactively - in a way that takes action before events happen
  • synonym: responsively - in a way that reacts quickly to something
