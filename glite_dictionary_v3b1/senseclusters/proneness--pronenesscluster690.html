The state or quality of being likely or disposed to do or experience something, especially something undesirable.

Examples

Proneness to mistakes
He was aware of his proneness to forget appointments and set multiple reminders.
Her proneness to worry made it hard for her to relax in stressful situations.
Researchers studied the proneness of certain individuals to develop allergies.

Synonyms

  • propensity
    7.3 4.66/m
    propensity is a natural, often neutral tendency to act in a certain way, while proneness suggests being likely to experience something undesirable.
  • susceptibility
    7.1 3.04/m
    susceptibility means being easily affected or harmed by something, while proneness means a general likelihood to suffer or do something undesirable.
