holocephalian

noun
a type of cartilaginous fish (Holocephali)
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊloʊˈsɛfəliən / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.əʊˈsɛf.ə.li.ən / (BrE)
a cartilaginous fish of the subclass Holocephali, characterized by a highly compressed head and a body that tapers into a long tail; includes chimaeras and related species

Examples

A holocephalian fish
Paleontologists discovered well-preserved holocephalian fossils in the ancient seabed.
The holocephalian is easily recognized by its distinctive long, tapering tail.
Unlike sharks, holocephalian species have a single gill cover and lack scales.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chimaera - a cartilaginous fish of the order Chimaeriformes, related to holocephalians
