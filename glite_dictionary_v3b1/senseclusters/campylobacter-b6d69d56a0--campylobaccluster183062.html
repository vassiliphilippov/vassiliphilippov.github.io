Campylobacter

noun
bacteria causing infection
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkæmpɪloʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / ˌkæmpɪləʊˈbæktə / (BrE)
a type of bacteria that can cause intestinal infections in humans, often leading to diarrhea and stomach pain

Examples

Campylobacter infection
The doctor confirmed that the patient had a Campylobacter infection after eating undercooked chicken.
Proper food handling can help prevent Campylobacter from contaminating meals.
Symptoms of Campylobacter infection include diarrhea, fever, and abdominal cramps.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Escherichia coli - a bacteria species that can cause intestinal illness
  • synonym: Salmonella - a type of bacteria that causes food poisoning
