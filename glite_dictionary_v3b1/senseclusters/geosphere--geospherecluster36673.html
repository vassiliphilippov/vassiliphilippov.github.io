geosphere

noun
solid part of the Earth
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒioʊˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the solid part of the Earth, including the crust and outer mantle, excluding the atmosphere and hydrosphere

Examples

The geosphere layers
Deep drilling projects provide valuable data about the composition of the geosphere.
Scientists study the geosphere to understand earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.
The geosphere interacts with the atmosphere and hydrosphere in Earth's system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atmosphere - the layer of gases surrounding the planet
  • antonym: hydrosphere - all the water on the Earth's surface
  • synonym: lithosphere - the rigid outer layer of the Earth, including the crust and upper mantle
