immaterial

adjective
not made of physical matter
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪməˈtɪriəl /; / ˌɪməˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪməˈtɪəriəl /; / ˌɪməˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
not made of physical matter; having no physical form or substance; incorporeal

Examples

Immaterial spirit
According to some philosophers, thoughts are immaterial and cannot be measured like physical objects.
Many religions believe in immaterial spirits or souls that exist apart from the physical world.
The ghost was described as an immaterial presence, felt but never seen.
Many philosophers debate whether the soul is immaterial or has some hidden physical component.
Some scientists reject the idea of immaterial forces affecting the physical world.
The story describes immaterial beings that cannot be seen or touched by humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incorporeal - not having a physical body or material existence
  • antonym: material - consisting of matter; having a physical form
