anticlericalism

noun
opposition to clergy
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈklɛrɪkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈklɛrɪkəlɪzəm / (BrE)
opposition to the influence or involvement of the clergy or religious leaders, especially in public or political affairs

Examples

Anticlericalism movement
The rise of anticlericalism in the country led to new laws limiting the church's power.
His strong anticlericalism was evident in his speeches against religious involvement in politics.
During the revolution, anticlericalism became a common attitude among many citizens.
The rise of anticlericalism led to significant changes in the country's education system.
Many political movements in the 19th century were influenced by strong anticlericalism.
Debates about anticlericalism often focus on the role of religion in government.
The rise of anticlericalism in the 19th century led to major changes in church-state relations.
Many political leaders promoted anticlericalism to limit the church's influence over education.
Laws inspired by anticlericalism reduced the privileges of religious institutions in several countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clericalism - support for the power or influence of the clergy
  • synonym: secularism - the belief that religion should not influence government or public affairs
