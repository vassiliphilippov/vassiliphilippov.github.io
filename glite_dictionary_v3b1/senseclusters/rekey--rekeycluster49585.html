rekey

verb
to change lock key
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkiː / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkiː / (BrE)
to change a lock so that it works with a new key and old keys no longer work

Examples

To rekey a lock
After the break-in, the building manager decided to rekey all the office doors.
It is common practice to rekey the locks when a new tenant moves in.
The locksmith can rekey your front door so your old keys will not work anymore.
After losing her keys, she had the front door rekeyed for security.
The office manager decided to rekey all the locks after the staff change.
It is often cheaper to rekey a lock than to replace it entirely.

  • antonym: lock - to fasten or secure with a lock
