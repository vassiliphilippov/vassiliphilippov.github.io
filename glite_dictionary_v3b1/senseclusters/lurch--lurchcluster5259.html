lurch

verb
to move or loiter aimlessly
Pronunciation: / lɝːtʃ / (AmE), / lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
(of a person) to loiter or move about aimlessly, often in a hesitant or uncertain manner.

Examples

To lurch around
Groups of people lurched along the street late at night, with no clear destination.
He spent the afternoon lurching from one corner of the park to another, seemingly lost in thought.
The teenager would often lurch around the shopping mall after school with nothing to do.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loiter - to stand or wait around aimlessly or without apparent purpose
