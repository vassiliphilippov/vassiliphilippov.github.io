levorotatory

adjective
rotating polarized light to the left
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌliːvoʊˈroʊtəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˌliːvəˈrəʊtətəri / (BrE)
(of a compound or substance) rotating the plane of polarized light to the left or counterclockwise.

Examples

Levorotatory compound
Glucose in its natural form is levorotatory and rotates polarized light to the left.
Identifying whether a compound is levorotatory or dextrorotatory is important in stereochemistry.
The chemist explained that the levorotatory isomer had different physiological effects than its dextrorotatory counterpart.
