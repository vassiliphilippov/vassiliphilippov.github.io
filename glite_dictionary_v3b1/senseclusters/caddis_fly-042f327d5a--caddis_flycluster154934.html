caddis fly

noun
small moth-like aquatic insect
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkædɪs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkadɪs flʌɪ / (BrE)
A small, moth-like insect with two pairs of hairy, membranous wings; its larvae are aquatic and often build protective cases from materials found in water.

Examples

The caddis fly larvae
The caddis fly larvae build protective cases out of sand and small twigs underwater.
Trout often feed on caddis fly larvae, making them popular among fly fishermen.
We observed a caddis fly resting on the riverbank during our field study.
