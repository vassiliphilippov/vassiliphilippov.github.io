caddis fly

noun
small moth-like aquatic insect
9.8 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæd.ɪs flaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkæd.ɪs flaɪ/ (BrE)
A small, moth-like insect with two pairs of hairy, membranous wings; its larvae are aquatic and often build protective cases from materials found in water.
