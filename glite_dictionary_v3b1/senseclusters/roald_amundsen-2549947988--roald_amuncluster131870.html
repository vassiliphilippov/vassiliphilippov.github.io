Roald Amundsen

proper noun
Norwegian polar explorer, first at South Pole
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.ɑːld ˈɑːm.ʊn.sən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.ɑːld ˈæm.ʊn.sən / (BrE)
(1872–1928) Norwegian explorer, known for being the first to traverse the Northwest Passage and the first to reach the South Pole in 1911.

Examples

Roald Amundsen explorer
Roald Amundsen led the first expedition to successfully reach the South Pole in 1911.
Many historians regard Roald Amundsen as one of the greatest polar explorers of all time.
The ship used by Roald Amundsen during his Arctic journey was named the Gjøa.
