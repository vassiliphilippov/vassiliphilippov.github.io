covary

verb
to change together
8.7
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈver.iː/ /; / /koʊ.ˈver.iː/ (AmE), /kə.ˈveə.riː/ /; / /kəʊ.ˈveə.riː/ (BrE)
to change in a related or corresponding way with another variable

Examples

To covary together
The growth rates of the two species often covary in response to environmental changes.
Researchers found that income and education levels covary across different regions.
If two variables covary, a change in one is usually matched by a change in the other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correlate - to have a mutual relationship or connection
  • antonym: independent - not affected by or related to another variable
