infirm

verb
to weaken physically
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɜrm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to make someone weak or feeble, especially in health or strength

Examples

Illness infirmed him
Years of illness had infirmed his body, leaving him unable to walk unaided.
The long drought infirmed the crops, making the harvest much smaller than usual.
Repeated injuries eventually infirmed the athlete, forcing her to retire early.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strengthen - to make stronger or more powerful
  • synonym: weaken - to make less strong or powerful
