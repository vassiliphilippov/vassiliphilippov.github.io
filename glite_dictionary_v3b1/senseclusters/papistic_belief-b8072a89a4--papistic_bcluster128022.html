papistic belief

noun
belief supporting authority of the Pope
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈpɪstɪk bəˈliːf / (AmE), / pəˈpɪstɪk bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
a belief or doctrine supporting the authority or practices of the Pope, especially as associated with the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

Papistic belief doctrine
Accusations of papistic belief could have serious consequences in 17th-century England.
Many reformers opposed the spread of papistic beliefs during that period of religious upheaval.
The council condemned those who openly professed papistic beliefs in the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Protestant belief - beliefs relating to Protestant churches and opposed to papal authority
