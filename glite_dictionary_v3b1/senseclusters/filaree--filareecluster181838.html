filaree

noun
a weed with fern-like leaves and upright seed pods
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪləˈri / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈriː / (BrE)
A low-growing Eurasian plant naturalized in North America, with reddish stems, fern-like leaves, and slender upright seed pods, often used as forage.

Examples

The filaree plant
Cattle often graze on filaree in the open pastures during spring.
Some gardeners consider filaree a weed, while others appreciate it as valuable forage.
You can identify filaree by its small, reddish-lavender flowers and distinctive seed pods.
