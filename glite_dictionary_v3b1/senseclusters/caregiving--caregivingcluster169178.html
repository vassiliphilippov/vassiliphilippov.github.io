caregiving

noun
providing regular help
3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈker.ɡɪv.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkeə.ɡɪv.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act or profession of regularly providing care and assistance to someone who needs help, especially due to age, illness, or disability

Examples

Family caregiving
Many families struggle to balance work and caregiving responsibilities for elderly relatives.
Professional caregiving can provide essential support for people with chronic illnesses.
The emotional impact of caregiving is often underestimated by those who have never done it.
Many families rely on caregiving services to help elderly relatives remain at home.
She found caregiving both rewarding and emotionally challenging.
The demand for professional caregiving has increased as the population ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babysitting - the act of taking care of children temporarily
  • synonym: caretaking - the act of looking after someone or something
  • antonym: neglect - failure to provide necessary care or attention
  • synonym: nursing - the profession or act of caring for the sick or elderly
