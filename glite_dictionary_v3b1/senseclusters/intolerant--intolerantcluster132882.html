intolerant

adjective
unwilling to accept differing opinions
4.6 3.83/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆtÉ‘lÉ™rÉ™nt /; / ÉªnËˆtÉ‘lÉšÉ™nt / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆtÉ’lÉ™rÉ™nt / (BrE)
unwilling to accept or respect opinions, beliefs, or behaviors that are different from one's own

Examples

An intolerant attitude
A healthy society cannot thrive if its members are overly intolerant of differences.
He became increasingly intolerant of criticism as he gained more authority.
Her intolerant attitude made it difficult for anyone with opposing views to speak freely.
He grew more intolerant as he became set in his ways.
Some people are intolerant of any criticism, no matter how constructive.
The school does not tolerate intolerant attitudes toward students from diverse backgrounds.

Synonyms

  • narrow-minded
    5.1 1.68/m
    narrow-minded is unwilling to consider new ideas and shows mental rigidity, while intolerant refuses to accept different beliefs.
  • closed-minded
    5.2 0.39/m
    closed-minded is quick to reject unfamiliar views and change, while intolerant is generally unwilling to accept different opinions.
  • illiberal
    8.2 0.33/m
    illiberal is formal and often used for views that limit free speech or debate, while intolerant means unwilling to accept other opinions.
  • bigoted
    7.8 3.42/m
    bigoted shows strong hostile attachment to one belief and attacks others, while intolerant simply refuses to accept different opinions.
  • prejudiced
    5.0 1.25/m
    prejudiced judges people unfairly because of group traits or bias, while intolerant is a broader unwillingness to accept different beliefs or behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bigoted - having or revealing an obstinate belief in the superiority of one's own opinions
  • antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
  • antonym: tolerant - willing to accept opinions or behaviors different from one's own
