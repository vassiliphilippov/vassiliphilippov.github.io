reemploy

verb
to use again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪmˈplɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈplɔɪ / (BrE)
to use something, such as a method, idea, or device, again after it has been used before.

Examples

To reemploy a method
The engineers decided to reemploy the previous design to save time on the new project.
After reviewing the results, the manager chose to reemploy the same strategy for the next campaign.
Sometimes it is more efficient to reemploy old solutions rather than invent new ones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discard - to throw something away or stop using it
  • synonym: reuse - to use something again after it has already been used
