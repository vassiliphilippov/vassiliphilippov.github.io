lash

verb
strike forcefully with a whip or rod
3.0
Pronunciation: / læʃ / (AmE), / læʃ / (BrE)
to strike or hit someone or something forcefully with a whip or as if with a whip

Examples

To lash the waves
During the storm, waves lashed the sides of the ship.
He threatened to lash anyone who tried to escape.
The cruel guard lashed the prisoner for disobedience.
She lashed out at the intruder with a heavy stick.
The wind began to lash the windows during the storm.
Waves lashed the shore as the storm intensified.
