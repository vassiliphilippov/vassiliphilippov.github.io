dugout

noun
shelter for players in baseball
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌɡˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡaʊt / (BrE)
either of two low shelters on either side of a baseball field where players and coaches sit during a game

Examples

Baseball dugout bench
Coaches shouted instructions from the dugout throughout the game.
She waited nervously in the dugout, hoping to be called up to bat.
The players returned to the dugout as rain started to fall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bench - long seat for several people, often used to refer to the area where substitute players sit
  • degree higher: field - the area where a sporting event takes place
