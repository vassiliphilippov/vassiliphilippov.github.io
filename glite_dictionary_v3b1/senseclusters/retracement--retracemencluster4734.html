retracement

noun
temporary price reversal
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtreɪsmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtreɪsmənt / (BrE)
a temporary reversal in the direction of a financial asset's price before it resumes its original trend

Examples

Price retracement
After a sharp rise, the currency saw a retracement before climbing higher again.
Traders often look for a retracement as a potential buying opportunity in an uptrend.
The market's retracement lasted only a few days before the rally continued.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correction - a decline in the price of a financial asset after a recent rise
  • antonym: trend - the general direction in which something is developing or changing
