Gradgrind

proper noun
fact-focused person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrædˌɡraɪnd / (AmE), / ˈɡrædˌɡraɪnd / (BrE)
a person who is excessively concerned with facts and practicality, ignoring imagination or emotion (like the character Thomas Gradgrind in Dickens's 'Hard Times')

Examples

A real Gradgrind
The new principal was a real Gradgrind, caring only about test scores.
His Gradgrind approach left little room for creativity in the classroom.
She called him a Gradgrind because he dismissed any idea that was not supported by data.
The new principal was a real Gradgrind, caring only about test scores and discipline.
Some critics called the policy makers Gradgrinds for ignoring creativity in education.
He approached every problem like a Gradgrind, focusing only on facts and figures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: imaginative - having or showing creativity or inventiveness
  • synonym: martinet - a strict disciplinarian
  • synonym: pedant - a person overly concerned with rules or details
  • antonym: visionary - someone with imagination and original ideas
