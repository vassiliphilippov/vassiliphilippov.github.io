cost of living

noun
average expense for basic necessities
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /kɑːst əv ˈlɪv.ɪŋ/ /; / /kɔːst əv ˈlɪv.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /kɒst əv ˈlɪv.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the amount of money needed to pay for basic necessities such as food, housing, and clothing, usually measured as an average for a particular place or time.

Examples

The cost of living index
A higher cost of living can make it difficult for families to save money.
The city’s cost of living has increased sharply over the past year.
They moved to a rural area to escape the rising cost of living in the city.
