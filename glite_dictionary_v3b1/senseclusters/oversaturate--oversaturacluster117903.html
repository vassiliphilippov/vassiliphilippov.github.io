oversaturate

verb
to add too much
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsætʃəreɪt / (BrE)
to add so much of a substance that no more can be absorbed or dissolved

Examples

To oversaturate the soil
If you oversaturate the soil with water, the plants may not survive.
Photographers must be careful not to oversaturate colors in their images.
Adding too much sugar can oversaturate the solution and cause crystals to form.
If you oversaturate the solution, crystals will start to form at the bottom.

The artist tried not to oversaturate the colors in the painting to keep it looking natural.
