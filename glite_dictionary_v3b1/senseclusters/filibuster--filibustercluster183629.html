filibuster

noun
obstructive speech in legislature
8.4 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪl.ə.bʌs.tər/ /; / /ˈfɪl.ɪ.bʌs.tər/ (AmE), /ˈfɪl.ɪ.bʌs.tə/ /; / /ˈfɪl.ə.bʌs.tə/ (BrE)
A lengthy speech or series of speeches made to delay or prevent a legislative action, especially in a parliamentary or legislative context.

Examples

A long filibuster
Ending a filibuster in the Senate requires a special majority vote known as cloture.
The opposition threatened a filibuster unless their amendments were considered.
The senator launched a filibuster to stop the bill from coming to a vote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blockade - an act of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving; similar metaphorically as an obstruction
  • antonym: expedite - to speed up the progress; opposite meaning to filibuster
