briar root

noun
pipe-making shrub root
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪər ruːt / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə ruːt / (BrE)
the hard root or wood of a shrub, especially used for making tobacco pipes and carved objects

Examples

A briar root pipe
The craftsman selected a piece of briar root to carve a new tobacco pipe.
Pipes made from briar root are valued for their durability and heat resistance.

Collectors often seek out vintage briar root pipes for their durability and craftsmanship.
He admired the smooth finish of the briar root handle on the walking stick.

Related Terms

  • synonym: briar - a type of shrub whose root is used for making pipes
  • synonym: burl - a hard, knotty growth on a tree used for woodworking
  • antonym: meerschaum - a soft white mineral used to make tobacco pipes
