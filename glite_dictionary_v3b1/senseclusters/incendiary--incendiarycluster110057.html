incendiary

noun
fire-starting bomb or device
5.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛndiəri / (BrE)
a bomb or device designed to cause fires, especially effective against flammable targets

Examples

Incendiary bomb
During World War II, incendiaries caused widespread fires in many cities.
The factory was badly damaged after an incendiary was dropped during the air raid.
The military used an incendiary to destroy the enemy's fuel depot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firebomb - a bomb designed to start fires
