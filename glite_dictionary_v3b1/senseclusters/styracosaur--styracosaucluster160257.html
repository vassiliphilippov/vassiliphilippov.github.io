styracosaur

noun
horned dinosaur with spikes, late Cretaceous
9.1
Pronunciation: / staɪˈrækəˌsɔr / (AmE), / staɪˈræk.ə.sɔː / (BrE)
a ceratopsian dinosaur from the late Cretaceous period, known for its long nose horn and multiple large spikes around the edge of its bony frill

Examples

A styracosaur dinosaur
A fossilized styracosaur skull was discovered in Alberta, shedding new light on its evolutionary history.
Scientists believe the styracosaur used its impressive horns for defense against predators.
The museum's exhibit features a life-sized replica of a styracosaur skeleton.

Related Terms
