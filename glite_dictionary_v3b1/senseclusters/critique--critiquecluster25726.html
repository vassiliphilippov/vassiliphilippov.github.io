critique

verb
evaluate or analyze critically
6.7 3.77/m
Pronunciation: / kr…ôÀàtik / (AmE), / kr…™ÀàtiÀêk / (BrE)
to evaluate or analyze something in a detailed and critical way, especially with regard to its merits and faults

Examples

To critique an essay
Can you critique my essay and suggest improvements?
She was invited to critique the latest performance at the local theater.
The professor asked us to critique the research article in detail.

Synonyms

  • evaluate
    3.2 56/m
    evaluate judges the value or quality of something in a general or formal way, while critique gives a detailed, critical analysis.
  • review
    2.9 31/m
    review examines something to form an opinion for readers or users, while critique focuses on detailed analysis of merits and faults.
  • assess
    3.0 67/m
    assess estimates the value, quality, or condition in tests or reports, while critique gives a deeper, critical look at merits and faults.
  • appraise
    4.8 1.02/m
    appraise judges the worth or market value of an object or property, while critique analyzes merits and faults in detail.
  • scrutinize
    6.2 6.45/m
    scrutinize examines something very closely to find errors or details, while critique assesses merits and faults and offers judgment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of something
  • synonym: review - to examine or assess something critically
