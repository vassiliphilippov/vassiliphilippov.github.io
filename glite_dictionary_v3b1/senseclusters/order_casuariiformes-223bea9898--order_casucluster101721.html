order Casuariiformes

noun
taxonomic order of cassowaries and emus
Pronunciation: / ˈkæʒuˌɛəriːəˌfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈkæzjʊərɪˌfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of large, flightless birds that includes cassowaries and emus, native to Australia and New Guinea.

Examples

Scientists have been studying the evolution of the order Casuariiformes to understand the loss of flight in birds.
The order Casuariiformes consists of large, flightless birds found primarily in Australia and New Guinea.
