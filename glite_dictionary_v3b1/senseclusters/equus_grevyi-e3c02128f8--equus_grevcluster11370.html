Equus grevyi

noun
a species of zebra with narrow stripes
Pronunciation: / ˈikwəs ˈɡrɛvi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛkwəs ˈɡrɛvi.aɪ / (BrE)
A large species of zebra, native to East Africa, distinguished by its narrow, closely spaced stripes; also known as Grevy's zebra.

Examples

The Equus grevyi zebra
Conservationists are working to protect the dwindling populations of Equus grevyi in Kenya and Ethiopia.
The Equus grevyi is the largest of all zebra species, known for its slender stripes.
Unlike other zebras, Equus grevyi has a white belly and large rounded ears.
