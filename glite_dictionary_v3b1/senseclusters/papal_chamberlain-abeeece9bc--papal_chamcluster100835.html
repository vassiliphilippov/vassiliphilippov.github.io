Papal Chamberlain

noun
former papal court official (pre-1968)
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪpəl tʃæmˈbɛrlɪn / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəl ˈtʃeɪmbəlɪn / (BrE)
A former court title bestowed by the Pope upon high-ranking clergy or laypersons, often from noble families, serving as personal attendants in the papal household before 1968.

Examples

The Papal Chamberlain title
During the Renaissance, the Papal Chamberlain wielded significant influence in the Vatican court.
He was appointed Papal Chamberlain for his years of faithful service to the church.
Several members of noble families aspired to become Papal Chamberlain to the Pope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chamberlain - a person who manages the household of a sovereign or noble
