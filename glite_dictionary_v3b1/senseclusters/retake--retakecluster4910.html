retake

noun
a repeated shot or scene in film or photography
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌteɪk /; / ˈriˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈriːˌteɪk /; / ˈriːteɪk / (BrE)
an instance of doing something again, such as reshooting a scene, retaking a photograph, or repeating an exam

Examples

A film retake
Due to equipment failure, the actors had to do several retakes.
Everyone was relieved when the scene was perfect and no retake was needed.
The director called for a retake after noticing a mistake in the background.
After failing the first exam, he scheduled a retake for the following week.
The director was not satisfied and called for a retake of the final scene.
Students who miss the test will have a chance for a retake next month.
