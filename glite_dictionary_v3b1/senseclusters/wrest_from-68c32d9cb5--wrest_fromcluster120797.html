wrest from

verb
forcibly take away
8.9
Pronunciation: / rɛst frʌm / (AmE), / rɛst frɒm / (BrE)
to forcibly take something away from someone, especially after a struggle

Examples

To wrest power from
After a lengthy legal battle, they finally wrested from the corporation the compensation they deserved.
She tried to wrest from his hands the confidential files he was hiding.
The rebels managed to wrest from the government control of the radio station.
