acute inclusion body encephalitis

noun
form of viral encephalitis affecting the brain
6.8
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut ɪnˈkluʒən ˈbɑdi ɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ɪnˈkluːʒən ˈbɒdi ˌɛn.sɛf.əˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A common form of acute encephalitis, usually caused by herpes simplex virus type 1, which typically affects the temporal and frontal lobes of the brain.

Examples

Acute inclusion body encephalitis caused by virus
Her memory loss was later attributed to acute inclusion body encephalitis caused by the herpes simplex virus.
Symptoms of acute inclusion body encephalitis can include fever, confusion, and seizures.
The neurologist suspected acute inclusion body encephalitis when the MRI showed abnormalities in the temporal lobe.
