swig

verb
drink quickly or greedily
3.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /swɪg/ (AmE), /swɪg/ (BrE)
To drink a large amount of liquid quickly or in one swallow, especially greedily or in one draught.

Examples

To swig a bottle
He grabbed the bottle and took a long swig of water after his run.
She swigged her coffee before heading out the door.
They swigged beer and laughed loudly late into the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gulp - to swallow hurriedly or greedily
  • antonym: sip - to drink a small amount slowly
