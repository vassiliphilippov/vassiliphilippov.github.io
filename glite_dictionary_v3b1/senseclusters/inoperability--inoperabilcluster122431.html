inoperability

noun
inability to function
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌɑːpərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌɒpərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state of being unable to function or work together, especially in systems or devices

Examples

Device inoperability
The inoperability of the two computer systems delayed the project for several weeks.
Engineers are working to resolve the inoperability between the new device and existing networks.
Software updates aim to reduce inoperability issues in future versions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incompatibility - the state of being unable to exist or work together
  • antonym: operability - the ability to function or be used
