kick the habit

verb
give up a bad habit
Pronunciation: / kɪk ðə ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / kɪk ðə ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
to stop a bad habit or a regular, undesirable behavior, especially one that is difficult to give up

Examples

To kick the habit successfully
After years of trying, he finally managed to kick the habit of biting his nails.
Many people struggle to kick the habit when it comes to smoking.
She joined a support group to help her kick the habit of overeating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quit - to stop doing something, especially a habit, activity, or job
