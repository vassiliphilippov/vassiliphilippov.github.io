prewash

verb
to wash before main wash
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈpriːwɒʃ / (BrE)
to wash something briefly or lightly before the main washing process to remove dirt or stains

Examples

To prewash clothes
It is a good idea to prewash heavily soiled clothes before putting them in the washing machine.
Some detergents are designed to help you prewash tough stains by hand.
The instructions say to prewash the fabric to prevent shrinking after sewing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rinse - to wash something quickly with water
  • degree higher: wash - to clean something using water and usually soap
