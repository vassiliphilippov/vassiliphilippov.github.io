seething

adjective
in constant agitation or turmoil
Pronunciation: / ˈsiðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːðɪŋ / (BrE)
full of intense but unexpressed emotion or in a state of constant activity or agitation.

Examples

Seething with anger
Beneath his calm appearance, he was actually seething with anger.
Her mind was a seething jumble of thoughts after the argument.
The seething crowd surged forward, unable to contain its excitement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous
  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
