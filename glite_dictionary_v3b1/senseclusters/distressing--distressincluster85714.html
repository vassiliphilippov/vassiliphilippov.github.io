distressing

adjective
causing anxiety or sadness
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
causing anxiety, sorrow, pain, or emotional upset

Examples

A distressing event
It was a distressing experience to watch their home burn down.
She found it distressing to see her friend in so much pain.
The news reports contained several distressing images from the disaster.
It can be distressing to watch animals suffer in such conditions.
She found the news about her friend's illness particularly distressing.
The most distressing aspect of the disaster was the loss of so many lives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comforting - making someone feel less worried or more at ease
  • synonym: disturbing - causing worry, anxiety, or emotional upset
  • synonym: upsetting - causing unhappiness or disappointment
