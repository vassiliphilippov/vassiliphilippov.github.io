scurvy

noun
disease from vitamin C deficiency
7.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈskɜːr.viː/ (AmE), /ˈskɜː.viː/ (BrE)
A disease resulting from a deficiency of vitamin C, characterized by swollen gums, weakness, and bleeding under the skin.

Examples

Scurvy disease
Fresh fruits and vegetables are essential to prevent scurvy.
His gums bled easily, leading the doctor to suspect scurvy.
Many sailors in the 18th century suffered from scurvy during long sea voyages.
