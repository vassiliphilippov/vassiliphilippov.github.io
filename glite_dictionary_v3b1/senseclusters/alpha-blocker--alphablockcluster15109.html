alpha-blocker

noun
drug that blocks alpha-adrenergic receptors
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.fə ˌblɑk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə ˌblɒk.ə / (BrE)
a drug that blocks alpha-adrenergic receptors, used especially to treat conditions like high blood pressure and benign prostatic hyperplasia by relaxing muscles of the prostate and bladder

Examples

An alpha-blocker drug
She experienced fewer urinary symptoms after starting treatment with an alpha-blocker.
Some patients with an enlarged prostate find relief with alpha-blockers.
The doctor prescribed an alpha-blocker to help manage his high blood pressure.
