Loloish language

noun
a branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊloʊɪʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈləʊləʊɪʃ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family spoken by various hill tribes in northern Myanmar (Burma) and surrounding regions

Examples

Loloish language family
Many hill tribes in Yunnan province communicate using a Loloish language.
Scholars have long debated the classification of the Loloish language within the Sino-Tibetan family.
The Loloish language group encompasses several dialects spoken across Southeast Asia.
