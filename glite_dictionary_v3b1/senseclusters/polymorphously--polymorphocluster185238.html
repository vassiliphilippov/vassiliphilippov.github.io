polymorphously

adverb
in various forms
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈmɔːrfəsli / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfəsli / (BrE)
in a way that involves or shows many different forms or types

Examples

To polymorphously change forms
The artist creates sculptures that can be interpreted polymorphously by different viewers.
Certain chemicals react polymorphously depending on temperature and pressure.
The story unfolds polymorphously, allowing readers to see multiple perspectives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniformly - in a way that is always the same
  • synonym: variously - in different ways or forms
