knock

verb
make light, repeated taps
1.0 30/m
Pronunciation: / nÉ‘k / (AmE), / nÉ’k / (BrE)
to strike a surface, especially a door, to make a sharp sound (often to get attention)

Examples

To knock on the door
He could hear someone knocking lightly on the kitchen window.
She began to knock on the desk to get everyone's attention.
Stop knocking your pencil against the table; it's distracting.
Please knock before you enter my office.
She walked up to the house and knocked on the door.
The visitor knocked three times, waiting for someone to answer.

Synonyms

  • knock on the door
    1.0 3/m
    knock on the door means hitting a door to get attention, while knock can mean a similar strike on any surface.
  • rap
    3.7 0.5/m
    rap is a quick, sharp hit that makes a short sound, while knock is often used for doors to get attention.
  • tap on
    2.7 1/m
    tap on is a light, gentle touch on a surface, while knock is a firmer strike meant to make a clear sound.
  • tap
    1.0 13/m
    tap is a soft touch or small sound made by fingers or a tool, while knock is stronger and aims to attract attention.
  • bang
    1.0 14/m
    bang is a loud, sudden noise from heavy force, while knock is a shorter, often polite strike used to call attention.
