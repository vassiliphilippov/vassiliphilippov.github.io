sorrowfully

adverb
in a sad or regretful manner
4.6 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ëÀêro äf…ôli / (AmE), / Ààs…ír…ô äf…ôli / (BrE)
in a way that shows or expresses sadness or regret

Examples

Look sorrowfully at photos
He gazed sorrowfully at the old photographs, lost in memories of the past.
She sorrowfully accepted the news that her friend was moving away.
They spoke sorrowfully about the accident that had changed their lives.
He spoke sorrowfully about his childhood experiences.
She looked sorrowfully at the broken vase on the floor.
The child waved sorrowfully as the train pulled away from the station.

Synonyms

  • sadly
    1.0 6.18/m
    sadly is used in everyday talk for simple sadness, while sorrowfully sounds slightly more formal and emotional.
  • mournfully
    4.7 0.67/m
    mournfully sounds like someone grieving and showing deep sorrow, while sorrowfully can mean sadness or regret without strong grief.
  • dolefully
    7.2 0.15/m
    dolefully looks or sounds mournful and gloomy, often in a quiet way, while sorrowfully simply shows sadness.
  • dolorously
    9.9 0.02/m
    dolorously feels formal or old-fashioned and shows deep pain, while sorrowfully is more common and less literary.
  • melancholically
    7.7 0.02/m
    melancholically describes a quiet, deep, thoughtful sadness over time, while sorrowfully can mean simple or momentary sorrow.
  • plaintively
    6.7 0.52/m
    plaintively sounds like a sad, pleading voice or complaint, while sorrowfully simply shows feeling of sorrow without begging.
  • dejectedly
    6.5 0.34/m
    dejectedly looks or acts low and beaten, as after failure, while sorrowfully describes general sadness or regret without the same posture.
  • regretfully
    3.8 0.83/m
    regretfully expresses apology or wishing things had been different, while sorrowfully focuses on feeling sad or mournful.
