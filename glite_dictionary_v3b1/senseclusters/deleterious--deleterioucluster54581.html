Synonyms

  • detrimental
    6.3 2/m
    detrimental often describes something that causes harm in a clear way, while deleterious usually refers to harm to living things.
  • harmful
    2.6 8/m
    harmful is a plain, common word for causing damage, while deleterious is more formal and often used about health.
  • injurious
    7.0 0.4/m
    injurious often means causing physical injury or harm, while deleterious usually refers to damage to living things.
  • damaging
    3.1 4/m
    damaging describes harm that can affect objects, reputation, or people, while deleterious especially implies harm to living organisms.
  • pernicious
    8.2 1/m
    pernicious means slowly or subtly very harmful and often unseen, while deleterious is a general formal word for harm.
  • destructive
    4.2 11/m
    destructive means causing great ruin or breaking things, while deleterious usually means harm or damage especially to living things.
