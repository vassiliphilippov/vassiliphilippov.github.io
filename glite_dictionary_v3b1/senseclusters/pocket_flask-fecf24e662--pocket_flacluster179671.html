pocket flask

noun
small container for spirits
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkɪt flæsk / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt flɑːsk / (BrE)
A small, flat container designed to carry alcoholic spirits, typically carried in a pocket.

Examples

A pocket flask of whiskey
He slipped his pocket flask into his jacket before heading out for the evening.
She offered me a sip from her pocket flask during the chilly outdoor concert.
The detective discreetly took a drink from his pocket flask while waiting in the shadows.
