ammunition

noun
information used in argument or debate
6.1 4.7
Pronunciation: /æm.jə.ˈnɪʃ.ən/ /; / /æm.jʊ.ˈnɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /æm.jʊ.ˈnɪʃ.ən/ /; / /æm.jə.ˈnɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
information or evidence used to support or defend an argument, or to attack someone else's argument

Examples

Ammunition for arguments
His careless comment became ammunition for his detractors in the debate.
She refused to share details about her plan, not wanting to provide her rivals with ammunition.
The report gave the opposition plenty of ammunition to criticize the government’s handling of the crisis.
