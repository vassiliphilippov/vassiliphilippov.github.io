GAO

noun
U.S. government accountability agency
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒiː.eɪ.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈdʒiː.eɪ.əʊ/ (BrE)
an independent U.S. federal agency that serves as the investigative arm of Congress, ensuring the accountability of the executive branch and the government to Congress and the public

Examples

The GAO report
According to the GAO, the new policy failed to save significant taxpayer money.
Congress often requests audits and investigations from the GAO.
The GAO released a report highlighting inefficiencies in federal spending.
