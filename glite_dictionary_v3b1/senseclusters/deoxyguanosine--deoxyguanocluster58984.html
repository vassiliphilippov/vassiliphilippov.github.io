deoxyguanosine

noun
a nucleoside component of DNA
Pronunciation: / diˌɑksɪˈɡwɑnoʊˌsiːn / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪˈɡwɑːnəʊˌsiːn / (BrE)
A nucleoside found in DNA, consisting of the base guanine attached to a deoxyribose sugar.

Examples

Deoxyguanosine in DNA
Deoxyguanosine is one of the four main nucleosides found in DNA.
Certain chemical reactions can modify deoxyguanosine, leading to mutations in genetic material.
Researchers measured the amount of deoxyguanosine in the DNA samples to assess damage.
