Augean stables

noun
mythologically filthy stables cleaned by Hercules
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈdʒiən ˈsteɪbəlz / (AmE), / ɔːˈdʒiːən ˈsteɪbəlz / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the extremely dirty stables that Hercules cleaned by diverting two rivers through them; often used figuratively to describe a place or situation marked by great filth, disorder, or corruption, especially one requiring extraordinary effort to clean up.

Examples

The filthy Augean stables
Reforming the tax code was as daunting as tackling the legendary Augean stables.
The new management was faced with the task of cleaning out the company's Augean stables.
Years of corruption had turned the agency into political Augean stables.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Herculean - requiring great strength or effort; relating to Hercules
