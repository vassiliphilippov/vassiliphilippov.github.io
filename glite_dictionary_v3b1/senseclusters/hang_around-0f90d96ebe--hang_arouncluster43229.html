hang around

verb
stay in a place without purpose
1.0 1.8
Pronunciation: /hæŋ ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /hæŋ ə.ˈraʊn/ (AmE), /hæŋ ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /hæŋ ə.ˈraʊn/ (BrE)
to stay or spend time in a place without any specific purpose or activity

Examples

To hang around the mall
He spent the afternoon hanging around the mall, waiting for his friends.
There is no point to hang around if the shop is closed for the day.
We used to hang around the park after school, just talking and relaxing.
After school, the kids like to hang around the playground before going home.
There's no reason to hang around if the meeting is over.
We spent the afternoon hanging around the city center, just people-watching.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leave - to depart from a place
  • synonym: linger - stay in a place longer than necessary, typically because of reluctance to leave
  • synonym: loiter - stand or wait around idly or without apparent purpose
