polar star

noun
the star Polaris, near the north celestial pole
4.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpo äl…ö st…ër / (AmE), / Ààp…ô äl…ô st…ëÀê / (BrE)
the bright star, also known as Polaris, situated nearly at the north celestial pole and used for navigation; the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor.

Examples

The polar star
As darkness fell, the polar star became clearly visible above the horizon.
Sailors have relied on the polar star for centuries to guide their way at night.
The polar star marks the direction of true north in the sky.
