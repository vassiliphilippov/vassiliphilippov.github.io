Utahraptor

noun
genus of large Cretaceous carnivorous dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːtɑːˌræptər / (AmE), / ˈjuːtɑːˌræptə / (BrE)
A genus of large, advanced, bipedal, carnivorous dinosaurs from the early Cretaceous period, known from fossils found in North America; includes the single species Utahraptor ostrommaysorum.

Examples

The Utahraptor dinosaur
Fossils of Utahraptor were first found in Utah during the early 1990s.
Scientists believe that Utahraptor was one of the largest raptors of its kind.
The discovery of Utahraptor provided new insights into the size range of dromaeosaurid dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dromaeosaur - a family of agile, bipedal, predatory dinosaurs closely related to Utahraptor
