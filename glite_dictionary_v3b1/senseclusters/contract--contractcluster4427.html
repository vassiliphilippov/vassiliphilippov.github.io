contract

verb
to become smaller or tighter by squeezing
6.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈtrækt / (AmE), / kənˈtrækt /; / kənˈtrakt / (BrE)
to become smaller, tighter, or more restricted, or to make something smaller or shorter

Examples

Muscles contract quickly
In cold temperatures, metals typically contract and become denser.

She could feel her throat contract with anxiety before her speech.

Rubber bands tend to contract when they are placed in cold water.
His pupils contracted quickly as he stepped into the bright sunlight.

The rubber band contracts as soon as you let go of it.
