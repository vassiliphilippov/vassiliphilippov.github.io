sanguinely

adverb
in a blood-red way
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋɡwɪnli / (AmE), / ˈsaŋɡwɪnli / (BrE)
in a manner that is blood-red or ruddy in color, often used in a literary or archaic context.

Examples

Sky glows sanguinely
The horizon glowed sanguinely as the sun began to rise over the mountains.
Her cheeks flushed sanguinely in the cold winter air, giving her a lively appearance.
The artist painted the sunset sanguinely, capturing the deep reds and vibrant hues of the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pale - lacking color or brightness
  • synonym: ruddy - having a healthy, red color
