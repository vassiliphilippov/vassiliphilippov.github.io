wide

adjective
broad in scope or content
1.0 160/m
Pronunciation: / waÉªd / (AmE), / waÉªd / (BrE)
large in extent, distance, or scope; covering a broad area or range (can refer to physical size, amount, or being open/roomy)

Examples

A wide range
Her interests are wide, ranging from science to literature.
The new law gives the committee wide authority to investigate corruption.
The organization launched a wide range of initiatives to support education.
The organization has a wide scope of activities, from education to healthcare.
The river flows through a wide valley surrounded by mountains.
There is a wide range of options available to customers.
He sat in silence, his wide eyes fixed on the stage.
Her wide eyes scanned the dark room in fear.
She looked at him with wide eyes, clearly shocked by the news.

Synonyms

  • broad
    1.0 26/m
    broad describes something with great extent from side to side and often fits ideas or categories, while wide means general physical size or range.
  • extensive
    4.1 34/m
    extensive describes covering a large area or range and often refers to amount or scope, while wide means general breadth or size.
  • spacious
    2.5 5.19/m
    spacious describes a room or place with lots of empty space and comfort, while wide refers to general extent or range.
  • expansive
    5.4 3.77/m
    expansive describes something very large, often with an impressive or open feeling, while wide means plain physical size or scope.
