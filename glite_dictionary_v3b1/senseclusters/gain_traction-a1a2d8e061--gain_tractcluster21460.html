gain traction

verb
acquire physical grip
3.4
Pronunciation: /geɪn ˈtræk.ʃən/ (AmE), /geɪn ˈtræk.ʃən/ (BrE)
(literal) to acquire or increase grip or friction against a surface so that movement or stability becomes possible or easier.

Examples

Tires gain traction
After several attempts, the tires finally gained traction and the truck climbed the snowy hill.
Spread grit on the ramp so the delivery cart can gain traction while workers move boxes.
With new tread patterns, the winter boots are gaining traction on icy sidewalks this season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grip - frictional force or hold that prevents sliding and allows secure contact with a surface
  • antonym: slip - to slide unintentionally because of insufficient friction or grip
