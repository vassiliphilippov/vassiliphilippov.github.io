Necator

proper noun
parasitic hookworm genus
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪˈkeɪtər / (AmE), / nɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic hookworms that infect humans and animals, sometimes causing disease

Examples

Necator hookworm species
The most common species of Necator infects millions of people worldwide, especially in tropical regions.
Doctors identified the presence of Necator larvae in the patient's stool sample.
Proper sanitation can help prevent infections caused by Necator hookworms.
