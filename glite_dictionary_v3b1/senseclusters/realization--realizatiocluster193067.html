realization

noun
becoming fully aware or understanding
3.6 13/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê…ôla…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Àår…™…ôla…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the act of becoming fully aware of something; an instance of understanding or perceiving something clearly

Examples

Sudden realization
A sudden realization struck her as she read the letter.
Her realization of the danger came too late to avoid the accident.
Winning the award was a realization of a lifelong dream for him.
She came to the realisation that she needed to change her habits.
The realisation that he had left his wallet at home struck him suddenly.
There was a growing realisation among scientists about the seriousness of climate change.

Synonyms

  • recognition
    2.7 5.15/m
    recognition is noticing or accepting something clearly, while realization is the sudden personal understanding of what that means.
  • insight
    4.9 51/m
    insight is a deep, sudden grasp of a truth, while realization is the moment you become clearly aware of it.
  • awakening
    4.4 4.89/m
    awakening is a gradual or sudden process of becoming aware, while realization is a specific clear moment of understanding.
  • understanding
    1.1 115/m
    understanding is a steady ability to know or grasp meaning, while realization is a single act of suddenly seeing that meaning.
  • awareness
    3.2 54/m
    awareness is the general state of knowing about something, while realization is the sudden clear moment when you grasp it fully.
