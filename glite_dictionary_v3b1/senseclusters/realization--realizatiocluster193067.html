realization

noun
becoming fully aware or understanding
3.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /riː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of becoming fully aware of something; an instance of understanding or perceiving something clearly

Examples

Sudden realization
A sudden realization struck her as she read the letter.
Her realization of the danger came too late to avoid the accident.
Winning the award was a realization of a lifelong dream for him.
She came to the realisation that she needed to change her habits.
The realisation that he had left his wallet at home struck him suddenly.
There was a growing realisation among scientists about the seriousness of climate change.

  • antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
  • synonym: realization - American spelling of 'realisation', meaning moment of understanding
  • synonym: recognition - the act of recognizing or being recognized, especially something previously known
