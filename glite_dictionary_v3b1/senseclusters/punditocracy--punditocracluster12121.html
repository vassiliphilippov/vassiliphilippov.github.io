punditocracy

noun
rule by commentators
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpʌndɪˈtɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / ˌpʌndɪˈtɒkrəsi / (BrE)
a group of influential commentators or experts, especially in politics or the media, who shape public opinion

Examples

The punditocracy influences politics
Some critics argue that the punditocracy has too much influence over political debates.
The rise of cable news channels has strengthened the power of the punditocracy in shaping opinions.
Many people feel disconnected from decisions made by the punditocracy rather than elected officials.
The punditocracy often shapes public opinion through television and newspaper columns.
Many people feel that the punditocracy is out of touch with ordinary citizens.
After the debate, the punditocracy offered their analysis on who performed best.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aristocracy - a class of people holding exceptional rank and privileges
  • synonym: commentariat - the collective body of commentators and analysts in the media
  • synonym: establishment - the dominant group in a society or field
