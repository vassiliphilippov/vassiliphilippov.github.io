enervation

noun
serious weakening and loss of energy
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnərˈveɪʃən /; / ˌɛnɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of weakness, extreme fatigue, or lack of energy

Examples

Total enervation
Months of relentless stress led to a feeling of enervation that he found hard to shake.
Some critics blamed the team's late-game enervation for their unexpected defeat.
The patient's enervation was evident after weeks of illness.
After the long illness, he felt a deep enervation that made simple tasks difficult.
The oppressive heat contributed to a general enervation among the workers.
There was an undeniable enervation in his manner, as if he had no will to act.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhaustion - extreme physical or mental fatigue
  • antonym: invigoration - the act of giving strength or energy
  • antonym: vigor - active bodily or mental strength or energy
  • synonym: weakness - the state of lacking strength
