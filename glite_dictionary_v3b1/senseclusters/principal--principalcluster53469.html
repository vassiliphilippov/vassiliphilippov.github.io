principal

noun
original sum of a loan or investment
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
the original sum of money invested or borrowed, on which interest is calculated

Examples

Principal amount
If you pay more than the minimum, the extra goes toward the principal.
The principal on the mortgage is $200,000, which is separate from the interest.
You must repay the principal plus any accumulated interest by next year.
After ten years, he paid off the principal of his mortgage.
She invested the principal and used the interest for her expenses.
The loan must be repaid with interest on the principal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capital - wealth in the form of money or assets
  • antonym: interest - money paid regularly at a particular rate for the use of borrowed money
