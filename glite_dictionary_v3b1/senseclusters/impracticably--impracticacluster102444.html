In a way that is impossible to carry out or use effectively; to an impracticable degree.

Examples

An impracticably complex plan
His plan was impracticably complex for such a tight deadline.
The cost of renovating the historic building was impracticably high for the charity.
The mountain path was impracticably steep after the heavy snowfall.

    unfeasibly means it is not possible or very hard to do, while impracticably means it is not practical to carry out.
  • unachievably
    8.4 0.00/m
    unachievably means people cannot reach or complete it, while impracticably means it is not sensible or possible to put into action.
  • unattainably
