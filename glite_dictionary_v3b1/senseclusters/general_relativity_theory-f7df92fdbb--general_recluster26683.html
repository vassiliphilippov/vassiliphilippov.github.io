general relativity theory

noun
Einstein's theory of gravitation and space-time
1.3 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒen.ər.əl re.lə.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː ˈθiː.ə.riː/ /; / /ˈdʒen.rəl re.lə.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː ˈθɪə.riː/ (AmE), /ˈdʒen.ə.rəl re.lə.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː ˈθɪə.riː/ /; / /ˈdʒen.rəl re.lə.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː ˈθiː.ə.riː/ (BrE)
The theory of gravitation developed by Albert Einstein, generalizing special relativity and introducing the principle of equivalence, describing gravity as a property of the curvature of space-time.

Examples

Einstein's general relativity theory
Einstein's general relativity theory revolutionized the way we understand gravity and the universe.
Modern astrophysics relies on general relativity theory to explain black holes and the expansion of the universe.
The predictions of general relativity theory have been confirmed by many experiments, such as the bending of light by massive objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Newtonian gravity - Classical theory of gravity by Isaac Newton, replaced by general relativity
  • degree lower: special relativity - Einstein's theory concerning the relationship of space and time excluding gravity
