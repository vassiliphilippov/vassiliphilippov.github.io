ku-chiku

noun
a large species of bamboo
Pronunciation: / ku tʃiːku / (AmE), / kuː ˈtʃiːkuː / (BrE)
a large species of bamboo with thick-walled stems, native to China and possibly Japan, and widely cultivated elsewhere

Examples

A large ku-chiku bamboo
Many botanical gardens around the world feature impressive specimens of ku-chiku.
The gardener planted a row of ku-chiku along the backyard fence for privacy.
The thick walls of the ku-chiku make it ideal for construction purposes.
