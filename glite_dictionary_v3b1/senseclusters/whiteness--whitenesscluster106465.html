whiteness

noun
moral purity or innocence
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally pure or innocent, without knowledge of evil or wrongdoing

Examples

Moral whiteness
In literature, angels are often depicted with whiteness to symbolize their lack of sin.
She valued the whiteness of his character, untouched by the world's corruption.
The child's whiteness was evident in her honest and trusting nature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: guilt - the fact of having committed a specified or implied offense or crime
  • synonym: innocence - the state of being innocent, morally or legally
