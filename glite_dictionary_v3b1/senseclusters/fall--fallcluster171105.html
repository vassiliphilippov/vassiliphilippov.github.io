fall

verb
to lose office or power (of government, etc.)
5.6 27/m
Pronunciation: / f…îl / (AmE), / f…îÀêl / (BrE)
to lose power, be defeated, die in battle, or suffer ruin (of people, governments, organizations, etc.)

Examples

The government fell
After months of protests, the government finally fell amid widespread unrest.
Many believed the old regime would fall sooner or later due to corruption.
On his return, he found that the monarchy had fallen and a republic was declared.
He promised never to fall as his predecessor did during the crisis.
Many startups fall due to poor financial planning during their first year.
The defending champions could fall if they underestimate their opponent in the finals.
Her brother fell in the last battle of the war.
The mighty stag fell to the hunter's arrow.
Thousands of soldiers fell defending their homeland.
