matriline

noun
mother's lineage
9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmæ.trə.laɪn/ /; / /ˈmæ.trə.lɪn/ (AmE), /ˈmæ.trə.laɪn/ /; / /ˈmæ.trə.lɪn/ (BrE)
a line of descent in a family traced through the mother's side or female ancestors

Examples

The matriline of ancestors
In some cultures, property is passed down the matriline rather than the patriline.
Scientists study mitochondrial DNA to trace ancestry along the matriline of a family.
Her family history was carefully recorded through the matriline for several generations.
In some cultures, family names are inherited through the matriline rather than the father's side.
The researcher traced the genetic disorder along the matriline of the family tree.
Traditions and property were passed down the matriline for generations in that community.
