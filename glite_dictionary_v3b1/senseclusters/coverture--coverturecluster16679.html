coverture

noun
historical legal doctrine
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌv.ər.tʃər/ /; / /ˈkʌv.ə.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈkʌv.ə.tʃə/ (BrE)
a former legal doctrine in which a married woman's legal rights and obligations were subsumed by those of her husband

Examples

Coverture law
Under coverture, married women could not own property or sign contracts in their own names.
The abolition of coverture was a significant step toward women's legal independence.
Historians often discuss coverture when examining women's rights in the nineteenth century.
