mark

noun
crossed lines marking; sign or symbol
2.4 1.0
Pronunciation: /mɑːrk/ (AmE), /mɑːk/ (BrE)
a visible sign, line, spot, scratch, or stain on a surface

Examples

A mark on the wall
She drew a mark on the wall to indicate the starting point.
The carpenter used a crisscross mark to show where to cut.
There was a strange mark on the paper made up of intersecting lines.
She looked for any mark of improvement in his behavior.
The early frost left a mark on the crops in the field.
There was no mark on the wall to show where the picture had hung.
Each animal track left a mark in the soft mud after the rain.
The children left a mark on the wall with their crayons.
There was a strange mark on the old letter, possibly a signature or seal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance
  • synonym: crosshatch - a pattern of intersecting lines, especially in drawing or engraving
  • synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin after a wound or injury has healed
  • synonym: sign - a perceptible indication or evidence of something
  • synonym: stain - a colored mark that is difficult to remove
  • synonym: trace - a very small amount or sign showing the former presence of something
