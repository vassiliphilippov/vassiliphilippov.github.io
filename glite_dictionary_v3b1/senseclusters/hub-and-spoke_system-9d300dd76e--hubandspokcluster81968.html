hub-and-spoke system

noun
transportation system linking outlying points to a central hub
Pronunciation: / hʌb ənd spoʊk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / hʌb ənd spəʊk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system of transportation, especially air travel, in which local or regional routes connect to a central hub airport, from which long-distance flights operate.

Examples

A hub-and-spoke system in airports
Many major carriers use a hub-and-spoke system, routing passengers through a central airport for connecting flights.
Regional flights in a hub-and-spoke system often feed into large, international airports.
The airline moved to a hub-and-spoke system to improve efficiency and reduce operational costs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: point-to-point system - a transportation system where flights or routes go directly between destinations without a central hub
