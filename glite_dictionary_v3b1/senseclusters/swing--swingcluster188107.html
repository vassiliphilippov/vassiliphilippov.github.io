swing

noun
state of lively or vigorous activity
Pronunciation: / swɪŋ / (AmE), / swɪŋ / (BrE)
a lively or vigorous state or period of activity, especially as part of a process or event

Examples

The swing of things
It took me a week to get into the swing of my new job.
The conference was in full swing by the second day, with everyone actively participating.
The festival really went with a swing after the music started.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lull - a temporary pause or less active period
  • synonym: momentum - the force or speed gained by a moving object or process
