sati

noun
widow-burning custom
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːti / (AmE), / ˈsʌti / (BrE)
a former Hindu custom in which a widow was burned alive on her husband's funeral pyre, now illegal

Examples

The sati custom
The British colonial government officially banned sati in India in 1829.
Many reformers campaigned against the cruel practice of sati in the 19th century.
Stories of sati have been depicted in literature and films to highlight its tragic history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: widow - a woman whose spouse has died
  • synonym: widow immolation - the act of a widow being burned on her husband's funeral pyre
