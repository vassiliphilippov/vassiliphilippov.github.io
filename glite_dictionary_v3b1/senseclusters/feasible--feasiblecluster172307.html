feasible

adjective
possible or achievable
4.9 12/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfiÀêz…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàfiÀêz…ôbl / (BrE)
Possible to do easily or conveniently; capable of being achieved or put into effect.

Examples

A feasible plan
Completing the project in two days is simply not feasible without additional help.
Installing solar panels on the roof seems like a feasible solution for cutting energy costs.
They conducted a study to determine whether the proposal was technically feasible.
