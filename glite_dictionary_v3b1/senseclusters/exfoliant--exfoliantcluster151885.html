exfoliant

adjective
removing dead skin
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɛksˈfoʊliənt / (AmE), / ɛksˈfəʊliənt / (BrE)
causing or designed to remove surface layers, especially dead skin cells, from the body.

Examples

Exfoliant cream
She prefers using an exfoliant scrub to keep her skin smooth and clear.
This exfoliant cream gently removes dead skin cells without irritation.
Dermatologists often recommend an exfoliant product for people with dry or rough skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrasive - having a rough quality that is used for cleaning or smoothing
  • antonym: moisturizing - adding moisture to the skin
