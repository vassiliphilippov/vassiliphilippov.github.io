complexation

noun
formation of a complex
8.9
Pronunciation: /kəm.plek.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kəm.plek.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of forming a complex compound by the combination of molecules or ions, especially in chemistry

Examples

Complexation of metal ions
The complexation of metal ions with organic molecules is crucial in many biological systems.
Researchers studied the complexation process to better understand how drugs interact with proteins.
Changes in temperature can affect the rate of complexation in chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissociation - the process by which a compound breaks apart into separate components
