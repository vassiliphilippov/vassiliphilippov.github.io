stave

noun
crosspiece between chair legs
Pronunciation: / steɪv / (AmE), / steɪv / (BrE)
a long, narrow piece of wood used for support or as part of the structure of something (such as a chair, barrel, or bucket)

Examples

Barrel stave
A sturdy stave keeps the chair's legs from splaying out over time.
One of the staves was broken, making the stool unstable.
The craftsman carved a decorative stave to connect the front legs of the chair.
A single broken stave can cause a bucket to leak water.
The cooper carefully shaped each stave to fit perfectly in the barrel.
The wine soaked into the oak staves, giving the drink its distinctive flavor.
