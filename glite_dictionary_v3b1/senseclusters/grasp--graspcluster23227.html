grasp

verb
hold something firmly
4.3
Pronunciation: / ɡræsp / (AmE), / ɡrɑːsp / (BrE)
to take and hold something firmly with the hands or fingers

Examples

To grasp a hand
He grasped her hand tightly as they crossed the street.
She managed to grasp the rope just in time.
The child was grasping his favorite toy while he slept.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grab - to seize suddenly or quickly
  • antonym: release - to allow something held to go free
