equivalence

noun
state of being equal or interchangeable
5.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈkwɪ.və.ləns/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈkwɪ.və.ləns/ (BrE)
the state or condition of being equal, interchangeable, or having the same value, meaning, or effect as something else

Examples

Mathematical equivalence
In mathematics, equivalence is used to show that two statements can be substituted for one another.
Many people debate the equivalence of different qualifications when applying for jobs.
The judge found no equivalence between the two cases, as the circumstances differed greatly.
He argues for the moral equivalence of different cultures.
The treaty established equivalence in military power between the nations.
There is an equivalence between the two mathematical expressions.
His apology was seen as the moral equivalence of an admission of guilt.
In mathematics, equivalence refers to the relationship between two entities that are comparable in a specific way.
There is a strong equivalence between the two theories proposed by the scientists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
  • synonym: equivalency - the state of being equivalent; alternative form of 'equivalence'
  • antonym: inequality - the state of not being equal
