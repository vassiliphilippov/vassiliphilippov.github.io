precure

noun
partial curing process
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌkjʊr / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌkjʊə / (BrE)
a preliminary or partial curing of a material, such as rubber or resin, before final processing

Examples

A precure stage
The technician inspected the batch after the precure to ensure correct hardness before final vulcanization.
A proper precure is essential to achieve the desired properties in the finished product.
If the precure is incomplete, the material may not perform well during final processing.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cure - the process of hardening or finishing a material by chemical or physical means
  • degree higher: vulcanization - the process of treating rubber with sulfur to improve its properties
