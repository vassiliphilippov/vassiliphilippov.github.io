box huckleberry

noun
a North American evergreen shrub
9.5
Pronunciation: /bɑːks ˈhʌk.əl.bər.iː/ (AmE), /bɒks ˈhʌk.əl.bə.riː/ (BrE)
a creeping evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, with small shiny box-like leaves and flavorless berries

Examples

Box huckleberry shrub
Botanists were excited to find a large colony of box huckleberry thriving undisturbed for centuries.
The box huckleberry prefers shady, wooded slopes in its natural habitat.
Unlike other related species, the berries of the box huckleberry are nearly flavorless.
