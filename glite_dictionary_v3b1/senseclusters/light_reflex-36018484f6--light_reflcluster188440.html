light reflex

noun
automatic pupil contraction in response to light
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌriːflɛks / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌriːflɛks / (BrE)
the automatic contraction of the pupil in response to bright light or certain drugs, causing it to become smaller

Examples

The light reflex response
An absent light reflex may indicate a problem with the optic nerve or brain function.
Bright sunlight can trigger the light reflex and cause your pupils to constrict.
The doctor shone a flashlight into her eyes to check her light reflex.
