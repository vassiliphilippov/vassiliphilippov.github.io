postmodernism

noun
art, literature, or architecture movement rejecting modernism
3.8 6.9
Pronunciation: /poʊst.ˈmɑː.dər.nɪz.əm/ (AmE), /pəʊst.ˈmɒd.ə.nɪz.əm/ (BrE)
a movement in art, literature, and especially architecture that developed as a reaction against the principles and practices of modernism, often characterized by a departure from established conventions, eclectic styles, and a tendency toward irony and self-reference

Examples

Postmodernism in architecture
His architecture exemplifies postmodernism through its playful forms and ironic references to history.
Many critics argue that postmodernism blurs the boundaries between high and low culture.
The new art gallery showcased the influence of postmodernism on contemporary sculpture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernism - a movement in art, literature, and architecture based on modern ideas and rejecting traditional styles
