lamer

adjective
more unimpressive
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪmər / (AmE), / ˈleɪmə / (BrE)
more weak, ineffective, or unimpressive than something else.

Examples

A lamer excuse
His excuse for being late was even lamer than the one he gave yesterday.
The sequel's plot was lamer and less exciting than the original movie.
I thought her first attempt was bad, but the second was somehow lamer.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: lame - weak, unconvincing, or unimpressive
  • degree higher: lamest - most weak, ineffective, or unimpressive
  • antonym: stronger - more effective or impressive
