fauld

noun
piece of armor below breastplate
6.2
Pronunciation: / fɔld / (AmE), / fɔːld / (BrE)
A piece of armor consisting of horizontal metal bands attached below the breastplate to protect the lower torso.

Examples

A fauld armor
A dented fauld revealed the ferocity of the fight that took place so long ago.
Museum visitors marveled at the elaborate fauld attached to the centuries-old suit of armor.
The knight's fauld was expertly crafted to provide mobility during battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cuirass - breastplate as part of body armor
  • synonym: tasset - a piece of armor for the upper thighs
