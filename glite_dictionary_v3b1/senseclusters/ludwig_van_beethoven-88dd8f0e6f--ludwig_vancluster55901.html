Ludwig van Beethoven

proper noun
famous German composer (1770–1827)
Pronunciation: / ˈlʊd.wɪɡ væn ˈbeɪ.toʊ.vən / (AmE), / ˈlʊd.vɪɡ van ˈbeɪ.tə.vən / (BrE)
(1770–1827) A German composer and pianist, regarded as one of the greatest figures in Western classical music, known especially for his symphonies and chamber music, and for continuing to compose after he became deaf.

Examples

Ludwig van Beethoven symphonies
Ludwig van Beethoven is celebrated for his nine symphonies, which have influenced generations of composers.
Many music lovers consider Ludwig van Beethoven the greatest composer of all time.
The life of Ludwig van Beethoven was marked by both triumph and adversity, particularly his struggle with deafness.
