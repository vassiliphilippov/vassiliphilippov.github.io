outrace

verb
to surpass in progress
5.9
Pronunciation: /aʊ.ˈtreɪs/ (AmE), /aʊ.ˈtreɪs/ (BrE)
to surpass or get ahead of someone or something, especially in progress, development, or achievement rather than in a physical race

Examples

To outrace the competition
The company hopes to outrace its competitors by adopting new technologies quickly.
Medical research sometimes outraces the regulations meant to control it.
Her ambition helped her outrace others in her field and achieve early success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lag - to fall behind in progress or development
  • synonym: outpace - to move or develop faster than someone or something
  • synonym: outperform - to do better than someone or something
