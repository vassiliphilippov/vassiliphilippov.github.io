flam

verb
to deceive by lying
9.9
Pronunciation: /flæm/ (AmE), /flæm/ (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone, especially by lying or making up a story

Examples

To flam a friend
He flammed his way into the party with a fake invitation.
She realized too late that the salesman had flammed her with false promises.
They tried to flam the teacher by inventing an excuse for missing homework.

  • antonym: honest - telling the truth and not lying or cheating
