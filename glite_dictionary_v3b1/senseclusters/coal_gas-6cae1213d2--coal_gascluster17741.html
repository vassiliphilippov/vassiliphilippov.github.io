coal gas

noun
gas produced from coal, used for heating/lighting
3.8
Pronunciation: / koʊl ɡæs / (AmE), / kəʊl ɡas / (BrE)
A gaseous mixture obtained by the distillation of bituminous coal, formerly used for heating and lighting.

Examples

Coal gas heating
Exposure to coal gas can be dangerous due to the presence of carbon monoxide.
Many Victorian cities relied on coal gas for street lighting before the widespread adoption of electricity.
The process of making coal gas was a major industry in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: town gas - Manufactured gas for lighting and heating, often made from coal
