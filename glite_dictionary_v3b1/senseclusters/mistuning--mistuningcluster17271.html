mistuning

noun
tuning error in systems
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈtuːnɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪsˈtjuːnɪŋ / (BrE)
an error or incorrect result in tuning something, such as a machine or a musical instrument

Examples

A mistuning error
Engineers discovered that a slight mistuning in the rotor blades led to unexpected vibrations.
The performance of the device was affected by mistuning in its electronic components.
Regular maintenance can help prevent mistuning and ensure the system runs smoothly.
The mistuning of the piano was obvious during the concert.
Even a slight mistuning can make a violin sound unpleasant.
He blamed the strange sound on the mistuning of one of the strings.

  • antonym: tuning - the adjustment of a system to achieve the desired frequency or performance
