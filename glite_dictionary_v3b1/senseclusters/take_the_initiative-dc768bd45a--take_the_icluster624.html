take the initiative

verb
be the first to act
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk ði ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (AmE), / teɪk ði ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (BrE)
to be the first person to act in a situation, especially to achieve something or make something happen

Examples

To take the initiative early
He always encourages his students to take the initiative in their own learning.
If you want a promotion, you need to take the initiative and show leadership.
She was the only team member to take the initiative and contact the client directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: take charge - to assume control or responsibility for something
  • antonym: wait for instructions - to not act until told what to do
