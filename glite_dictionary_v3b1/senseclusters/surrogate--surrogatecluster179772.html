surrogate

noun
a substitute person
6.8 4.79/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀê.…ô.…°…ôt /; / Ààs…úr.…ô.…°…ôt / (AmE), / Ààs år.…ô.…°…ôt / (BrE)
a person who acts as a substitute or representative for another, especially in a specific role or function

Examples

A surrogate leader
In some cultures, a surrogate may perform ceremonial duties on behalf of another person.
The organization appointed a surrogate to make decisions during her absence.
When their leader was sick, a trusted surrogate led the meeting in his place.
As a child advocate, she served as a surrogate for children without legal guardians.
The ambassador acted as a surrogate for the president during the negotiations.
When the CEO was unavailable, a surrogate attended the meeting in her place.
