lowlights

noun
darkest image areas
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊˌlaɪts / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlaɪts / (BrE)
the darkest parts of a photograph, image, or scene where there is little or no light

Examples

Photo lowlights
The artist carefully painted the lowlights to add depth and contrast to the portrait.
Adjusting the lowlights in the photo made the details in the shadows more visible.
Photographers often enhance the lowlights to create a dramatic effect in their images.

Related Terms

  • antonym: highlights - the brightest parts of a photograph or image
  • synonym: shadows - dark areas in a picture caused by blocking light
