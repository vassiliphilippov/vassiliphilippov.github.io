Synonyms

  • magnate
    7.9 1/m
    a magnate is a wealthy, powerful business person often mentioned in news, while a mogul is the broader, common word for such leaders.
  • tycoon
    5.5 2/m
    a tycoon builds and controls large business empires and sounds dramatic, while a mogul is the general term for a very powerful businessperson.
  • baron
    6.1 1/m
    a baron is a powerful figure tied to one industry, like a press baron, while a mogul names any very wealthy business leader.
  • business leader
    3.0 2/m
    a business leader directs companies and focuses on management or strategy, while a mogul implies great personal wealth and wide industry power.
  • big businessman
    3.4 0.01/m
    a big businessman is an informal, vague way to say a rich company owner, while a mogul suggests exceptional wealth and top industry power.
