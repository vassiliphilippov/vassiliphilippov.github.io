bookful

adjective
full of book knowledge
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkfəl / (AmE), / ˈbʊkfʊl / (BrE)
having much knowledge gained from books; showing learning or bookishness.

Examples

Bookful knowledge
Her bookful answers impressed the examiners, though they wanted more practical examples.
He sounded bookful in the meeting, quoting many authors but offering few original ideas.
The essay was bookful, packed with references but lacking personal insight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bookish - showing a strong interest in reading or studying
  • antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • synonym: learned - having much knowledge from study
