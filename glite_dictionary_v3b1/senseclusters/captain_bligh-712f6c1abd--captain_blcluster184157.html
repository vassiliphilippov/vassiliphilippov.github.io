Captain Bligh

proper noun
British naval captain of the Bounty mutiny
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.tən blaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪn blaɪ / (BrE)
(1754–1817) British naval officer best known as captain of the H.M.S. Bounty during the 1789 mutiny, who survived a remarkable voyage to safety after being set adrift by mutineers.

Examples

Captain Bligh of Bounty
After being set adrift by his crew, Captain Bligh navigated thousands of miles to safety.
The story of Captain Bligh and the Bounty mutiny has fascinated people for generations.
The strict discipline of Captain Bligh was blamed by some for the famous mutiny.
