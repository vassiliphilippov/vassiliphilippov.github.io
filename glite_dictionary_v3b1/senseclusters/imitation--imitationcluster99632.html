imitation

noun
the act of copying someone or something
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of copying the behavior, style, or actions of another person or thing

Examples

Child imitation
Children often learn new skills through imitation rather than direct instruction.
Parrots are known for their remarkable imitation of human speech.
The comedian's imitation of the president made the audience laugh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emulation - effort to match or surpass someone by imitation
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unusual, not imitative
