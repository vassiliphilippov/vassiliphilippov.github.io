take the game to them

verb
confront an opponent aggressively
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə ɡeɪm tu ðəm / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ɡeɪm tʊ ðəm / (BrE)
to approach an opponent or rival with an assertive and aggressive strategy rather than waiting defensively

Examples

To take the game to them aggressively
If we take the game to them, we might force them into making mistakes.
The coach urged the players to take the game to them right from the start.
The team decided to take the game to them instead of sitting back and defending.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against an opponent
  • antonym: defend - to protect oneself or one's side instead of attacking
