resonate

verb
produce or echo with a deep sound
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛzəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪt / (BrE)
(of a sound) to produce or be filled with a deep, full, reverberating sound; to echo or vibrate with resonance.

Examples

Resonate in the hall
Her laughter resonated in the empty room, making the moment feel less lonely.
The singer's deep voice resonated throughout the concert hall, leaving the audience mesmerized.
Thunder resonated across the mountains, filling the valleys with sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: echo - to be repeated or reverberated as a sound
