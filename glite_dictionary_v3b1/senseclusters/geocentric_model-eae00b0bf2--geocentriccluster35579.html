geocentric model

noun
theory placing Earth at the universe's center
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiː.oʊˈsɛn.trɪk ˈmɑː.dəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˈsɛn.trɪk ˈmɒd.əl / (BrE)
The theory, especially held in ancient astronomy, that Earth is at the center of the universe and all celestial bodies revolve around it.

Examples

The geocentric model theory
For centuries, many civilizations subscribed to the geocentric model before the heliocentric theory gained acceptance.
Some ancient texts describe the universe according to the geocentric model.
The geocentric model was eventually replaced by the work of Copernicus and Galileo.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heliocentric model - the astronomical model that places the Sun at the center of the solar system
