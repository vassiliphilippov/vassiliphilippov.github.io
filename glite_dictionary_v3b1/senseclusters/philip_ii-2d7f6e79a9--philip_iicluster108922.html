Philip II

proper noun
King of Spain and Portugal (1527–1598)
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlɪp səˈkənd / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp ˌsɪˈkənd / (BrE)
(1527–1598) King of Spain and Portugal, husband of Mary I of England, known for supporting the Counter-Reformation and sending the Spanish Armada to invade England.

Examples

Philip II of Spain
Philip II ruled over Spain during a period of great imperial expansion.
Many historians regard Philip II as one of the most powerful monarchs of the 16th century.
The defeat of the Spanish Armada marked a turning point in Philip II's ambitions.
