nonteleological

adjective
not purpose-based
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌtɛliəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒntɛliəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
not related to or based on the idea of purpose, design, or final causes in nature or events

Examples

A nonteleological explanation
The scientist offered a nonteleological explanation for the evolution of complex traits.
His philosophy is strictly nonteleological, focusing on causes rather than purposes.
Many modern theories in biology are fundamentally nonteleological in their approach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mechanistic - explaining phenomena by physical or natural causes, not purpose
  • antonym: teleological - related to purpose or final causes
