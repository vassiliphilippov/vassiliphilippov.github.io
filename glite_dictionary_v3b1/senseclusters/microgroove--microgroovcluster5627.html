microgroove

adjective
for narrow-groove records
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌɡruːv / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌɡruːv / (BrE)
relating to records, equipment, or formats that use very narrow grooves, especially on vinyl records.

Examples

Microgroove record player
The old turntable only plays microgroove records, not the older shellac discs.
She bought a microgroove stylus to improve the sound quality of her vinyl collection.
Collectors often seek out rare microgroove albums from the 1950s and 1960s.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monophonic - relating to sound reproduction using a single channel
  • synonym: stereophonic - relating to sound reproduction using two or more channels
