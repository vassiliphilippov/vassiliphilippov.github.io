whirl

noun
state of rapid or confused movement
Pronunciation: / wɜrl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
A state or act of rapid, confused, or hectic movement or activity.

Examples

A whirl of activity
After losing his job, he spent months in a whirl of emotions and decisions.
The announcement sent the office into a whirl of excitement and speculation.
They found themselves caught in a whirl of activity as the festival began.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of tranquility or quietness; opposite of confusion or hectic activity
  • synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
