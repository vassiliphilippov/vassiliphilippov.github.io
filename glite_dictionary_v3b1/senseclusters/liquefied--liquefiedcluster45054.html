liquefied

adjective
turned into liquid form
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) changed into a liquid form, usually by heating or applying pressure

Examples

Liquefied natural gas
Oxygen can be liquefied under low temperatures and high pressure.
She studied a sample of liquefied hydrogen in the laboratory.
The plant stores enormous quantities of liquefied natural gas for export.
During the experiment, the chemist carefully handled the liquefied sulfur.
The tanker was filled with liquefied natural gas for shipment overseas.
Under high heat, the metal became liquefied and began to flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
  • antonym: solidified - having become solid
