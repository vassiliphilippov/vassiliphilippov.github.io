TFR

noun
signal analysis method
9.9
Pronunciation: / ti ɛf ɑr / (AmE), / tiː ɛf ɑː / (BrE)
a technical method for displaying how the frequency content of a signal changes over time

Examples

TFR analysis
The researcher used a TFR to analyze the changing frequencies in the recorded music.
A TFR can reveal important details about speech patterns that are not visible in a simple waveform.
Engineers often rely on a TFR when studying complex audio signals in real time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spectrogram - a visual representation of the spectrum of frequencies in a signal as it varies with time
  • antonym: waveform - a graphical representation of the shape of a signal
