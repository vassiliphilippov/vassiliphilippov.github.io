balk

verb
hesitate or refuse to comply
9.9 7.4
Pronunciation: / bɑk /; / bɔːk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
to hesitate or refuse to accept an idea or undertake an action; to be unwilling to comply or proceed

Examples

To balk at work
Many employees balk at working overtime during the holidays.
She started to sign the contract, but balked when she read the fine print.
The board will likely balk at the proposal if the costs are too high.
He baulked at the idea of working overtime without extra pay.
Many investors baulk at putting money into high-risk ventures.
When asked to apologize, she baulked and refused to say another word.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balk - to hesitate or refuse to comply; to stop short
  • antonym: comply - to act in accordance with a wish or command
  • synonym: refuse - to indicate or show that one is not willing to do something
