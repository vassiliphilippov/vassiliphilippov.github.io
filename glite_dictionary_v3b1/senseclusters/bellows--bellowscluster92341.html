bellows

noun
device for blowing air, often on fires or instruments
6.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbel.oʊz/ (AmE), /ˈbel.əʊz/ (BrE)
a device with an airbag that emits a stream of air when squeezed together, used to blow air on a fire or supply air to a pipe organ or other instruments

Examples

Pump the bellows
As the fire flickered out, she pumped the bellows to revive it.
Pipe organs are powered by air from a bellows operated by hand or machine.
The blacksmith used a large bellows to stoke the flames.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blower - a device for producing a current of air
