dog

verb
follow closely and persistently
Pronunciation: / dɔg / (AmE), / dɒg / (BrE)
to follow closely and persistently, especially with the intention of catching or harassing

Examples

To dog his steps
Even after his resignation, controversy continued to dog the politician.
The detectives dogged the suspect through the busy streets for hours.
The paparazzi dogged the celebrity everywhere she went.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness
  • synonym: follow - to come or go after in the same direction
  • synonym: pursue - to follow with intent to catch or achieve
