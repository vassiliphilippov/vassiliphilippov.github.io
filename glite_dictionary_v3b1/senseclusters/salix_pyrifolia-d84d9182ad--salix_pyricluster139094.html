Salix pyrifolia

noun
a shrubby North American willow
Pronunciation: / ˈsælɪks paɪrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks paɪrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A small shrubby willow tree native to eastern North America, with leaves that emit a balsam-like odor when crushed.

Examples

Salix pyrifolia tree
Gardeners appreciate Salix pyrifolia for its resilience in moist, cold climates.
Scientists found a cluster of Salix pyrifolia growing along the wetland edge.
The Salix pyrifolia is easily identified by the balsam scent of its leaves when crushed.
