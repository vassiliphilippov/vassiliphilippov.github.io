westernize

verb
adopt Western customs or practices
6.9 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərˌnaɪz /; / ˈwɛstərnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɛstənaɪz / (BrE)
to adopt or cause to adopt the customs, culture, values, or practices of Western countries, especially Europe or North America

Examples

To westernize culture
After the revolution, the government tried to westernize the education system.
Many societies have chosen to westernize certain aspects of their culture.
The city has rapidly westernized over the past few decades, with new businesses and fashions.
After decades of isolation, the nation began to westernise rapidly, adopting new fashions and technologies.
Many countries have attempted to westernise their education systems to compete internationally.
The government tried to westernise the economy by encouraging foreign investment and privatization.

  • synonym: modernize - to make modern or up to date
  • synonym: westernize - to make more Western in character (American spelling)
