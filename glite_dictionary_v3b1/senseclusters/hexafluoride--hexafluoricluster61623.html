hexafluoride

noun
compound with six fluorines
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛksəˈflʊraɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛksəˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which six fluorine atoms are bonded to another element

Examples

Hexafluoride compound
Sulfur hexafluoride is used as an insulating gas in electrical equipment.
Scientists studied the properties of uranium hexafluoride during the enrichment process.
The laboratory ordered a new cylinder of hexafluoride for their chemical experiments.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: octafluoride - a compound with eight fluorine atoms bonded to another element
  • degree lower: tetrafluoride - a compound with four fluorine atoms bonded to another element
