pip

verb
defeat completely (verb)
Pronunciation: / pɪp / (AmE), / pɪp / (BrE)
to defeat someone by a very small margin, especially in a contest or race

Examples

To pip a rival
Our team pipped theirs with a last-second goal.
She pipped her rival in the final minutes of the race.
The underdogs managed to pip the champions in a stunning upset.
She pipped her rival at the finish line by just a second.
The underdog team pipped the favorites in the last minute of the match.
He pipped his opponent to the championship by a single point.

  • antonym: lose - fail to win (the opposite of 'pip')
  • synonym: thrash - defeat heavily, especially in a match
  • synonym: trounce - defeat decisively in a contest
