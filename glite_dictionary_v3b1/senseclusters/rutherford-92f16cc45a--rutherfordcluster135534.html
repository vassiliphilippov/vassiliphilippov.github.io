Rutherford

proper noun
British physicist who discovered the atomic nucleus
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌðɚˌfɝd / (AmE), / ˈrʌðəfəd / (BrE)
Ernest Rutherford (1871–1937), British physicist born in New Zealand who discovered the atomic nucleus and proposed the nuclear model of the atom.

Examples

Rutherford atomic nucleus
Rutherford is often called the father of nuclear physics.
Many scientists were influenced by Rutherford's groundbreaking research on radioactivity.
