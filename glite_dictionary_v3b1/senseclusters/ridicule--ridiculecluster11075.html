ridicule

verb
to mock or make fun of
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪdɪˌkjul / (AmE), / ˈrɪdɪkjuːl / (BrE)
to mock or make fun of someone or something in a contemptuous way

Examples

To ridicule a mistake
It is unfair to ridicule someone for their accent or background.
Many critics ridiculed the artist's latest work, calling it childish.
The comedian ridiculed politicians during his stand-up show last night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deride - to express contempt for; ridicule
  • synonym: mock - to make fun of someone in a cruel way
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something
