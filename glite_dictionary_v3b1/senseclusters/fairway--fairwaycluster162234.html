fairway

noun
golf course area between tee and green
6.2 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfeɪr.weɪ/ (AmE), /ˈfeə.weɪ/ (BrE)
an open, obstacle-free passage, especially the mowed area between the tee and green on a golf course, or a navigable channel for ships

Examples

The fairway on golf
He landed his ball safely on the fairway with his first shot.
Players aim to keep the ball on the fairway for an easier approach to the green.
The grass on the fairway is kept much shorter than in the rough.
All ships must remain within the fairway to avoid running aground.
Buoys mark the edges of the fairway in the bay to guide approaching vessels.
The new marina was built outside the main fairway to prevent interference with ship traffic.
Golfers try to keep their ball on the fairway to have a clear shot at the green.
The river's fairway was marked by buoys to guide passing vessels safely.
The ship entered the fairway with caution to avoid running aground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: channel - a navigable route through shallow water, often used for ships
  • antonym: rough - area on a golf course where the grass is longer and less maintained
