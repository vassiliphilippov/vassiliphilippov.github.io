ticket

verb
issue a fine or penalty notice
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪk.ɪt /; / ˈtɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪk.ɪt /; / ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
to give someone an official notice of a fine or penalty for breaking a law, especially in traffic or parking

Examples

To ticket a car
Parking officers regularly ticket vehicles that block fire hydrants.
She was ticketed for speeding on the highway last month.
The police will ticket any car left in the no-parking zone overnight.
The police officer ticketed the car for parking in a no-parking zone.
If you park here without a permit, you may get ticketed by the city authorities.
She was ticketed for speeding on the highway last weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fine - charge a person money as a penalty for an offense
  • antonym: warn - to give someone a warning instead of a penalty
