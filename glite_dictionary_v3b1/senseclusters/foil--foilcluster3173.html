foil

noun
flat or curved surface for lift or control
3.9
Pronunciation: / fɔɪl / (AmE), / fɔɪl / (BrE)
A flat or curved surface, such as a metal plate, designed to move through water or air in a way that affects lift, stability, or direction, as used on boats, aircraft, or in engineering devices.

Examples

Boat foil design
Engineers tested the new foil design in the wind tunnel to maximize efficiency.
Some modern surfboards are equipped with a hydro foil for higher speed and smoother rides.
The yacht's foil allows it to glide quickly above the water's surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrofoil - a boat or surface designed to lift a vessel out of water
