thyrsus

noun
type of dense flower cluster
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜrsəs / (AmE), / ˈθɜːsəs / (BrE)
A dense flower cluster in which the main axis is racemose and the branches are cymose, as seen in plants like the lilac or horse chestnut.

Examples

A thyrsus flower cluster
Botanists identified the inflorescence as a thyrsus due to its mixed branching structure.
Each thyrsus on the horse chestnut tree was covered with delicate white flowers.
The lilac bush produced a beautiful thyrsus of purple blooms in late spring.
