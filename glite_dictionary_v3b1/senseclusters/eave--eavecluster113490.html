eave

noun
roof edge
4.6
Pronunciation: / iv / (AmE), / iːv / (BrE)
the lower edge of a roof that projects beyond the side of a building

Examples

Rain from the eave
Rainwater dripped steadily from the eave after the storm.
Swallows often build their nests beneath the eave of old barns.
Icicles hung from every eave during the cold winter months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gable - the triangular upper part of a wall at the end of a ridged roof
  • synonym: overhang - a part that extends beyond the main structure
