cross

noun
breeding animals/plants to produce hybrids
5.9 8/m
Pronunciation: / krÉ”s / (AmE), / krÉ’s / (BrE)
the act or result of breeding different species or varieties of animals or plants; or the hybrid offspring produced

Examples

A cross breed
A successful cross of the two breeds resulted in a new hybrid.
The geneticist analyzed the results of the cross in the laboratory.
The scientist performed a cross between two tomato varieties to improve disease resistance.
A mule is a cross between a horse and a donkey.
The lab created a cross to study resistance to disease.
This new rose variety is actually a cross of two different species.

Synonyms

  • hybrid
    3.8 4/m
    hybrid is an offspring of parents from different species or varieties, while cross names the breeding act or the hybrid result.
  • crossbreed
    4.1 0.1/m
    crossbreed is an offspring produced from different breeds or varieties, while cross can mean the breeding process or the hybrid offspring.
  • crossing
    7.2 0.5/m
    crossing is the genetic act of breeding different varieties to make hybrids, while cross refers to that act or to the offspring.
  • crossbreeding
    4.4 0.1/m
    crossbreeding is the deliberate process of mixing breeds to get desired traits, while cross covers both that process and the offspring.
  • hybridization
    6.1 0.6/m
    hybridization is the scientific term for making hybrids in breeding projects, while cross is a general word for process or offspring.
  • interbreeding
    6.2 0.3/m
    interbreeding is breeding between different populations or species, sometimes casual or natural, while cross usually names deliberate breeding or the hybrid.
