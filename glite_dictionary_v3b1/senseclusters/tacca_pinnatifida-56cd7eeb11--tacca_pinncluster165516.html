Tacca pinnatifida

noun
tropical plant with edible starch-yielding root
Pronunciation: / ˈtækə pɪˌnætɪˈfaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈtakə pɪˌnatɪˈfiːdə / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to the East Indies, Polynesia, and Australia, cultivated for its large edible root which is used to produce Otaheite arrowroot starch

Examples

Tacca pinnatifida edible root
Tacca pinnatifida is a key source of traditional starch in many Pacific Island cuisines.
Farmers in eastern Indonesia grow Tacca pinnatifida for both local food and export markets.
The starchy root of Tacca pinnatifida is harvested to produce Otaheite arrowroot.
