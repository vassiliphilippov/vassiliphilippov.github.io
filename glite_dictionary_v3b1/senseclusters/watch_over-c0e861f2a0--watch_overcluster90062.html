watch over

verb
observe or monitor closely
Pronunciation: / wɑtʃ ˈoʊvɚ /; / wɑtʃ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / wɒtʃ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to look after or supervise someone or something to make sure they are safe or nothing bad happens

Examples

To watch over children
Can you watch over my bag while I go to the restroom?
Security cameras watch over the entrance day and night.
She was assigned to watch over the children on the field trip.
Could you watch over my laptop while I step out for a minute?
Parents are expected to watch over their children during the field trip.
The security guard watches over the building throughout the night.

  • synonym: oversee - supervise or watch over work or people
  • synonym: supervise - to oversee and direct the work or actions of others
