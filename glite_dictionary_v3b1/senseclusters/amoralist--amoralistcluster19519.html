amoralist

noun
person rejecting conventional moral distinctions
6.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈmɔrəlɪst / (AmE), / əˈmɒrəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that traditional moral distinctions between right and wrong are invalid or irrelevant

Examples

An amoralist thinker
Nietzsche is sometimes interpreted as an amoralist due to his critique of established moral values.
Some critics argue that an amoralist cannot distinguish right from wrong in the traditional sense.
The philosopher was often labeled an amoralist because he dismissed conventional ethical standards.
