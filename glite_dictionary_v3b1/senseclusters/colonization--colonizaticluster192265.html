colonization

noun
the act of establishing colonies
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌkɒlənʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of establishing control over a territory and its indigenous people and settling it with people from another country as a colony

Examples

European colonization
European colonization changed the political and cultural landscape of many continents.
Space agencies are discussing the potential colonization of Mars in the future.
The colonization of Australia had a profound impact on the indigenous population.
European colonisation transformed the Americas in the 16th century.
Historians continue to debate the long-term impact of colonisation on indigenous populations.
The colonisation of large parts of Africa had profound cultural and political effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act or process of establishing control over a territory and settling it with people from another country (American spelling)
  • antonym: decolonization - the act of withdrawing from a colony, leaving it independent
  • synonym: settlement - the process of establishing a new community in a new place
