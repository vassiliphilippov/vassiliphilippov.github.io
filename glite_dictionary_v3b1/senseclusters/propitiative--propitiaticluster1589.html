propitiative

adjective
intended to appease or reconcile
9.9
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɪʃiˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / prəˈpɪʃɪətɪv / (BrE)
intended to reconcile or appease; having the purpose of making peace or gaining favor

Examples

A propitiative gesture
As a propitiative gesture, he apologized and promised to change his behavior.
Her speech took on a propitiative tone, hoping to calm the restless crowd.
The king made a propitiative offering to the gods before the battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appeasing - intended to pacify or placate
  • synonym: conciliatory - intended or likely to placate or pacify
  • antonym: provocative - causing anger or another strong reaction
