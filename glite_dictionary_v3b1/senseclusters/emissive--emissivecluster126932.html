emissive

adjective
able to emit particles
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
giving off or emitting light, energy, electrons, or other particles, especially in scientific or technical contexts

Examples

Highly emissive material
The new material is highly emissive and can release electrons when heated.
Scientists measured how emissive the surface became under different light conditions.
A photocathode must be emissive to function effectively in electronic devices.
The new television uses an emissive display technology for brighter colors.
Scientists measured the emissive properties of the heated metal surface.
LED bulbs are more emissive and energy-efficient than traditional incandescent lights.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorptive - able to absorb energy or particles
  • synonym: radiant - emitting light or energy
