submerse

verb
sink below the surface (esp. water)
7.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôbÀàm…úrs / (AmE), / s…ôbÀàm…úÀês / (BrE)
to put or cause something to go under the surface of a liquid, especially water

Examples

To submerse in water
Heavy rains can quickly submerse low-lying roads and fields.
She watched the stone submerse beneath the surface of the pond.
The diver carefully submersed himself in the clear blue water.
Before painting, submerse the brush in water to prevent the bristles from drying out.
Please submerse the test tubes in the water bath for five minutes.
The diver submersed himself to observe the coral reef up close.

Synonyms

  • submerge
    3.9 4.19/m
    submerge means putting or going completely under water, while submerse focuses on causing something to go beneath a liquid's surface.
  • immerse
    4.2 1.15/m
    immerse means dipping something fully into a liquid so it is covered, while submerse emphasizes making something go under the liquid's surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: submerge - to go or be under water or another liquid
