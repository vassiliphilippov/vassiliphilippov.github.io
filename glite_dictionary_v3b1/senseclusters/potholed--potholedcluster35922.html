potholed

adjective
having many potholes or pits
5.5 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêtÀåho äld / (AmE), / Ààp…íth…ô äld / (BrE)
(of a road or surface) having many potholes or small holes and pits that make it rough or uneven.

Examples

A potholed road
The potholed road slowed traffic to a crawl as drivers dodged the damaged patches.
We had to replace a tire after driving down a particularly potholed highway.
Years of neglect had left the city streets badly potholed and difficult to navigate.

Synonyms

  • pitted
    4.4 0.00/m
    pitted means a surface has many small holes or cavities, while potholed refers to roads with potholes that make driving rough.
  • cratered
    4.5 0.00/m
    cratered describes a surface covered with larger, deep holes like craters, while potholed means roads with many smaller driving potholes.
  • pockmarked
    7.4 0.01/m
    pockmarked means covered in many small pits or scars often on skin or old walls, while potholed refers to rough road surfaces.
  • pocked
    7.5 0.02/m
    pocked describes surfaces or skin marked by small pits or scars, while potholed specifically names roads with potholes.
