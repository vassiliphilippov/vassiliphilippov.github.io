potholed

adjective
having many potholes or pits
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːtˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈpɒthəʊld / (BrE)
(of a road or surface) having many potholes or small holes and pits that make it rough or uneven.

Examples

A potholed road
The potholed road slowed traffic to a crawl as drivers dodged the damaged patches.
We had to replace a tire after driving down a particularly potholed highway.
Years of neglect had left the city streets badly potholed and difficult to navigate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth - having an even or level surface without holes or roughness
