ecclesiology

noun
study of the Christian Church (theology)
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkliːsiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːsiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of the nature, structure, and functions of the Christian Church, especially as a branch of theology

Examples

Modern ecclesiology study
Modern ecclesiology considers both historical and contemporary issues facing the church.
She specialized in ecclesiology for her doctoral thesis at the seminary.
Some debates in ecclesiology revolve around the role of laypeople in the church community.
