functional

adjective
designed to serve a specific function or use
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌŋkʃənl̩ /; / ˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənl /; / ˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (BrE)
designed to be practical and useful rather than decorative; serving a particular purpose

Examples

Functional kitchen
Her kitchen is both stylish and functional for preparing large meals.
The design of the new office is highly functional, focusing on efficiency and workflow.
We chose a functional sofa that doubles as a guest bed.
The functional design of the building makes it easy to navigate and comfortable to use.
These chairs are more functional than decorative, perfect for offices or waiting rooms.
We wanted a functional kitchen, not just one that looks good in photos.
Her office is functional, focusing on practical needs rather than decoration.
The company underwent a functional reorganization to improve efficiency.
The distinction between structural and functional aspects is important in biology.

  • synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice
  • antonym: structural - relating to the structure or physical arrangement of something
