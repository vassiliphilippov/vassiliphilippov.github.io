Eskimo-Aleut language

noun
Arctic language family (Eskimo and Aleut)
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛskɪmoʊ əˈluːɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɛskɪməʊ əˈluːɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of languages spoken in the Arctic regions of North America and northeastern Siberia, including Eskimo (Inuit and Yupik) and Aleut.

Eskimo-Aleut language family

The Eskimo-Aleut language family is distinct from the Indo-European languages spoken in Europe.
