regreen

verb
to become green again
6.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈgriːn/ /; / /rɪ.ˈgriːn/ (AmE), /riː.ˈgriːn/ /; / /rɪ.ˈgriːn/ (BrE)
to become green again, especially as vegetation or land recovers its green color after drought, fire, or winter

Examples

Fields regreen after rain
After the heavy rains, the fields began to regreen and looked full of life again.
It can take several months for a burned forest to regreen after a wildfire.
The park slowly regreened as spring arrived and new grass started to grow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: wither - to become dry and lose color or life
