Italian sonnet

noun
sonnet with abbaabba cdecde rhyme scheme
8.0 6.7
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈtæl.jən ˈsɑː.nɪt/ /; / /aɪ.ˈtæl.jən ˈsɑː.nɪt/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈtæl.jən ˈsɒn.ɪt/ (BrE)
A sonnet composed of an octave with the rhyme scheme abbaabba, followed by a sestet with the rhyme scheme cdecde or cdcdcd.

Examples

An Italian sonnet form
Many English poets have experimented with the Italian sonnet form in their works.
She wrote her poem as an Italian sonnet, following the traditional rhyme scheme.
The Italian sonnet usually presents a problem in the octave and a solution in the sestet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Petrarchan sonnet - a sonnet form identical to the Italian sonnet
  • antonym: Shakespearean sonnet - a sonnet with a rhyme scheme abab cdcd efef gg
