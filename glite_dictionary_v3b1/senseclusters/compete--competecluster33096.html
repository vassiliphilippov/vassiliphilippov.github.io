compete

verb
to strive against others to win
2.6 61/m
Pronunciation: / k…ômÀàpit / (AmE), / k…ômÀàpiÀêt / (BrE)
to try to win or gain something by defeating or establishing superiority over others who are trying to do the same

Examples

To compete in games
Athletes from all over the world compete in the Olympic Games.
My sister and I always compete to see who finishes their homework first.
Several local businesses compete for the title of best restaurant in town.

Synonyms

  • contest
    2.8 2.99/m
    contest is used for formal races or elections and focused events, while compete describes trying to win in general situations.
  • vie
    6.2 3.59/m
    vie implies eager rivalry and strong effort to beat others, while compete is a general try to win.
  • contend
    3.7 6.81/m
    contend often means to struggle or argue for a position, while compete focuses on trying to win against others.
  • outvie
    9.9 0.00/m
    outvie suggests trying to outdo others by competing harder, while compete simply means to try to win.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contend - to struggle to surmount a difficulty or danger; to compete
  • antonym: cooperate - to work together with others for a common purpose
