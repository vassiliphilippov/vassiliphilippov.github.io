melanic

adjective
darkly pigmented
8.6
Pronunciation: / məˈlænɪk / (AmE), / mɛˈlænɪk / (BrE)
having or relating to dark pigmentation, especially caused by the presence of melanin

Examples

The melanic butterfly
The melanic variety of the butterfly is more common in polluted urban areas.
Scientists studied the melanic traits in certain populations of birds.
A melanic mouse blends easily into the dark volcanic rocks of its habitat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: albinistic - lacking normal pigmentation
  • synonym: melanistic - having an increased amount of dark pigmentation
