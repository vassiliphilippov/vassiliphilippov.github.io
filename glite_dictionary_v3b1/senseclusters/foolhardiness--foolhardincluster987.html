foolhardiness

noun
recklessness; disregard for danger
8.2 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfulÀåh…ërdin…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàfuÀêlÀåh…ëÀêd…™n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of taking reckless or unnecessary risks and giving little thought to danger or consequences

Examples

Foolhardiness in action
Acting out of foolhardiness, he challenged the storm despite all the warnings.
Everyone was astonished by the foolhardiness of her proposal, given the obvious risks involved.
His foolhardiness led him to attempt the climb without any safety gear.

Synonyms

    5.6 0.18/m
    rashness is hasty action or speech without thinking, while foolhardiness usually means taking bold, unnecessary physical risks.
  • daredevilry
    8.8 0.00/m
    daredevilry is daring, thrill-seeking danger for excitement, while foolhardiness is taking risky actions with little thought.
  • imprudence
    7.7 0.09/m
    imprudence is poor judgment or unwise choices in many matters, while foolhardiness focuses on needless risky actions.
  • heedlessness
    7.8 0.07/m
    heedlessness is ignoring warnings or details through carelessness, while foolhardiness is choosing dangerous risks with little concern.
  • incautiousness
    8.3 0.00/m
    incautiousness is a lack of carefulness that causes mistakes, while foolhardiness means taking bold risks without thinking of danger.
  • riskiness
    4.5 0.18/m
    riskiness means having a high chance of danger or loss, while foolhardiness is a person's reckless choice to accept such danger.
