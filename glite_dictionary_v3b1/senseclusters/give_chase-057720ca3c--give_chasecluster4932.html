give chase

verb
begin to pursue or chase
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv tʃeɪs / (AmE), / ɡɪv tʃeɪs / (BrE)
to start chasing or pursuing someone or something in order to catch them

Examples

To give chase quickly
As soon as the thief ran from the scene, the detective gave chase.
The children laughed as they gave chase to the dog around the garden.
The guards immediately gave chase when they saw the prisoners escaping.
Several cyclists gave chase after the leader broke away from the pack.
The cat saw a bird and instinctively gave chase across the garden.
When the thief ran from the shop, the security guard gave chase immediately.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flee - to run away from someone or something
  • synonym: pursue - to follow and try to catch or reach someone or something
