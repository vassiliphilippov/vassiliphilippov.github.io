carnassial tooth

noun
tooth for cutting flesh in carnivores
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈnæsiəl tuθ / (AmE), / kɑːˈnasɪəl tuːθ / (BrE)
a large, sharp-edged tooth in carnivorous mammals, specifically the last upper premolar and first lower molar, adapted for cutting flesh

Examples

The carnassial tooth sharp edge
Fossil evidence shows that the carnassial tooth was present in many ancient carnivorous species.
The lion's carnassial teeth are perfectly adapted for shearing meat from bone.
Veterinarians often check the carnassial tooth in dogs for signs of infection.
