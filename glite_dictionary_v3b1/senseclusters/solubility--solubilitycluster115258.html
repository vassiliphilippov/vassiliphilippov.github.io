solubility

noun
amount of substance that dissolves in solvent
1.4 3.9
Pronunciation: /sə.luː.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ /; / /sɑː.lə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /sɒl.jʊ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /sə.luː.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the ability of a substance to dissolve in a liquid (especially in chemistry)

Examples

Solubility in water
Chemists measure the solubility of substances to understand their behavior in different solvents.
The solubility of sugar increases as the temperature of water rises.
The low solubility of calcium carbonate limits its effectiveness in certain solutions.
Scientists measured the solubility of the new drug to determine how it should be administered.
The solubility of sugar in water increases when the temperature rises.
Understanding the solubility of various minerals helps geologists study rock formation processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insolubility - the inability of a substance to dissolve in a solvent
  • synonym: soluble - able to be dissolved, especially in water
