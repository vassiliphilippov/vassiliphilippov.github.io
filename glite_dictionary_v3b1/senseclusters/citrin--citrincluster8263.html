citrin

noun
a vitamin related to capillary strength
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtrɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrɪn / (BrE)
a vitamin once thought to prevent capillary fragility, now identified as a mixture of bioflavonoids, especially vitamin P

Examples

Natural citrin sources
Most citrus fruits are natural sources of citrin.
Researchers once believed citrin to be an essential vitamin for humans.
The deficiency of citrin can result in increased capillary fragility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioflavonoid - a group of plant-derived compounds with antioxidant properties
  • synonym: vitamin P - another name for bioflavonoids, once thought to be a vitamin
