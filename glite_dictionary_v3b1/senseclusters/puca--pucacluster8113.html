puca

noun
Irish folklore spirit
Pronunciation: / ˈpuːkə / (AmE), / ˈpuːkə / (BrE)
a mischievous spirit or creature from Irish folklore, often said to change shape and cause trouble or mischief

Examples

A puca spirit
In many Irish tales, a puca appears at night to play tricks on travelers.
Some believe a puca can bring both good and bad fortune, depending on its mood.
The villagers warned children not to follow a puca into the woods after dark.
