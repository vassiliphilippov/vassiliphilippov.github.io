electromagnetic radiation

noun

Radiation consisting of energy waves associated with electric and magnetic fields, produced by the acceleration of charged particles and including radio waves, visible light, X-rays, and more.

Examples

Electromagnetic radiation waves
Cell phones operate by transmitting electromagnetic radiation to communicate with nearby towers.
Doctors use certain types of electromagnetic radiation, such as X-rays, to see inside the human body.
The sun emits vast amounts of electromagnetic radiation, including visible light and ultraviolet rays.

Synonyms

  • electromagnetic wave
    4.2 0.41/m
    an electromagnetic wave is a moving oscillation of electric and magnetic fields through space, while electromagnetic radiation names the whole range of those waves.
  • nonparticulate radiation
    7.0 0.00/m
    nonparticulate radiation is radiation made of waves rather than particles, while electromagnetic radiation describes that wave-based energy across the spectrum.
