gel

verb
begin to work well together
4.1
Pronunciation: / d925bl / (AmE), / d925bl / (BrE)
to begin to come together or work effectively as a group, plan, or arrangement; to cohere.

Examples

The team gels well
The new teammates didn't gel until after the retreat and several honest conversations.
After months of disagreement, the committee finally gelled and adopted a clear plan.
It took a few tries, but the pieces of the proposal began to gel into a coherent idea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cohere - to hold or stick together; to be logically or aesthetically consistent
  • antonym: fall apart - to fail to stay together or to stop functioning effectively
