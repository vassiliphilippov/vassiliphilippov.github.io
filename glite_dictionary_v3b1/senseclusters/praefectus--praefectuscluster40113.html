praefectus

noun
Roman military officer
9.9
Pronunciation: /preɪ.ˈfek.təs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfek.təs/ (AmE), /preɪ.ˈfek.təs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfek.təs/ (BrE)
a military officer in charge of a unit, camp, or specific corps in ancient Roman armies

Examples

Military praefectus of a camp
The praefectus was responsible for maintaining discipline within the camp.
During the battle, the praefectus gave orders to the soldiers under his command.
Archaeologists discovered an inscription naming a praefectus who served in Britain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centurion - a commander of a hundred soldiers in the Roman army
  • synonym: legatus - a high-ranking Roman military officer, often a legion commander
