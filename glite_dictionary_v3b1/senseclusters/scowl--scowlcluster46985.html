scowl

verb
frown with anger or displeasure
4.1 4.44/m
Pronunciation: / skaÊŠl / (AmE), / skaÊŠl / (BrE)
to frown in an angry or displeased way

Examples

To scowl at someone
He scowled in frustration as the car refused to start.
She couldn't help but scowl at the disappointing news.
The teacher began to scowl when the students ignored her instructions.

Synonyms

  • frown
    1.0 20/m
    frown wrinkles the brow and can show concentration or mild displeasure, while scowl shows clear anger or stronger displeasure.
  • lour
    9.9 0.00/m
    lour looks sullen and dark, often in a literary or old-fashioned tone, while scowl is a plain angry or displeased frown.

Related Terms
