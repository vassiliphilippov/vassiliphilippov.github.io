rout

noun
overwhelming defeat
Pronunciation: / raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
a complete and overwhelming defeat, especially in a contest or battle

Examples

A political rout
Last week's election was a political rout for the opposition party.
The army was forced into a rout after their defenses collapsed completely.
The game ended in a rout, with the home team winning by fifty points.
After the rout last weekend, the coach promised major changes to the lineup.
The final score reflected a complete rout rather than a close contest.
The team's unexpected rout stunned their rivals and delighted their fans.

  • antonym: victory - act of defeating an opponent or enemy
