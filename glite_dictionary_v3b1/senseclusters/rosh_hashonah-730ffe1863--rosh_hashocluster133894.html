Rosh Hashonah

proper noun
Jewish New Year festival
7.2
Pronunciation: / roʊʃ həˈʃoʊnə / (AmE), / rəʊʃ həˈʃɒnə / (BrE)
the Jewish New Year festival, celebrated in early autumn, marked by prayer, reflection, and the blowing of the shofar

Examples

Rosh Hashonah festival
During Rosh Hashonah, it is customary to wish others a sweet and prosperous New Year.
Every year, my family gathers to celebrate Rosh Hashonah with traditional prayers and meals.
The sound of the shofar marks the beginning of Rosh Hashonah in synagogues around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Passover - a major Jewish festival in spring commemorating the exodus from Egypt
  • degree higher: Yom Kippur - the Jewish Day of Atonement, following Rosh Hashanah
