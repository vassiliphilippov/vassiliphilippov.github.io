Babylonish

adjective
of Babylon; luxurious or corrupt
Pronunciation: / ˈbæbəˌloʊnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbæbɪˌləʊnɪʃ / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Babylon, especially suggesting luxury, splendor, or moral corruption

Examples

Babylonish luxury
The palace was decorated in a Babylonish style, with gold and precious stones everywhere.
Critics called the festival's extravagance almost Babylonish in its display of wealth.
He warned against adopting Babylonish customs that might lead to moral decline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupt - morally depraved or dishonest
  • synonym: luxurious - characterized by great comfort or elegance
