cromorne

noun
Renaissance double-reed woodwind instrument
6.5
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmɔrn / (AmE), / krəʊˈmɔːn / (BrE)
a woodwind instrument of the Renaissance with a double reed and a curved tube, also known as a 'crooked horn'.

Examples

The cromorne instrument
Few musicians today play the cromorne, but its unique sound is highly valued by specialists.
He restored an antique cromorne for the museum's collection of historical instruments.
The early music ensemble featured a cromorne in their Renaissance program.
