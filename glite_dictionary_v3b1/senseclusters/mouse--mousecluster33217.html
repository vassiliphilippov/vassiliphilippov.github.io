mouse

verb
to move quietly
7.3 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / maÊŠs / (AmE), / maÊŠs / (BrE)
to move about quietly or stealthily, especially in search of something or to catch mice.

Examples

To mouse in the barn
The cat spent the afternoon mousing in the old barn, hoping to catch its next meal.
She was mousing around the kitchen, searching for a midnight snack without waking anyone.
Foxes are known for mousing in fields at dusk, listening for the faintest sounds.

Synonyms

    5.3 2.57/m

  • skulk
    7.7 1.00/m
    skulk means to move secretly to avoid being noticed or caught, while mouse means to move about quietly, often to search or catch something.
