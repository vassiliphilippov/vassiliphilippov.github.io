CFS

noun
unit of water flow
6.3
Pronunciation: / si ɛf ɛs / (AmE), / si ɛf ɛs / (BrE)
a unit of flow equal to one cubic foot per second, commonly used to measure water flow in rivers and streams

Examples

River CFS reading
The river's flow reached 2,000 CFS after heavy rainfall in the mountains.
Engineers monitor CFS to predict possible flooding during the spring thaw.
A sudden increase in CFS can indicate a release of water from an upstream dam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cubic meter per second - a metric unit of flow equal to one cubic meter per second
