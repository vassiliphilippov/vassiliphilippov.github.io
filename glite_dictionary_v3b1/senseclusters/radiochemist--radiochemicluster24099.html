radiochemist

noun
chemist specializing in nuclear chemistry
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈkɛmɪst / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈkɛmɪst / (BrE)
a chemist who specializes in the chemistry of radioactive substances and nuclear processes

Examples

A radiochemist studies radioactivity
As a radiochemist, she developed new ways to isolate radioactive isotopes for medical research.
Marie Curie is one of the most renowned radiochemists in history.
The company is hiring an experienced radiochemist to work on its nuclear waste management team.
