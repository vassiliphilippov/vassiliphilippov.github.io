upset

verb
form or shape metal by pressure
7.8 8.5
Pronunciation: /əp.ˈset/ /; / /ˈʌp.set/ (AmE), /ʌp.ˈset/ /; / /əp.ˈset/ (BrE)
to form or shape metal, especially by thickening or swelling, through the use of pressure, a swage, or blows.

Examples

To upset metal
Some machine processes upset the metal before forging to create greater strength.
The welder upset the steel using a swage block to achieve the desired thickness.
To increase the head size, the blacksmith will upset the end of the heated rod.
