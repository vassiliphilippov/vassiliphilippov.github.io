condensation

noun
psychoanalytic process of merging ideas in dreams
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.dɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
In psychoanalysis, an unconscious process by which two or more ideas or images combine into a single symbol, especially in dreams.

Examples

Condensation in dreams
Freud described condensation as a key mechanism in the formation of dreams.
In dream interpretation, condensation can lead to a single image representing multiple ideas.
Psychoanalysts study how condensation shapes the symbolic content of our unconscious.
