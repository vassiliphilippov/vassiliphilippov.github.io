salar

noun
historical commander title
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɑːˈlɑːr / (AmE), / sɑːˈlɑː / (BrE)
a historical title for a commander or leader, especially in Persianate or South Asian societies

Examples

A salar commands troops
The salar was responsible for organizing the defense of the city during the invasion.
In ancient times, a salar would lead troops into battle and make strategic decisions.
The caravan's safety depended on the experience and wisdom of its salar.
