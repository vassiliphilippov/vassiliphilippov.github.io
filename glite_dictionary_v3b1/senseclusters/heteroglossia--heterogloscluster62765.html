heteroglossia

noun
multiple voices or languages
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈɡlɑːsiə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈɡlɒsiə / (BrE)
the presence of multiple voices, languages, or points of view within a single text or context

Examples

Literary heteroglossia
The heteroglossia in the novel allows different characters to express unique perspectives.
Scholars often analyze heteroglossia to understand the complexity of literary works.
The film's heteroglossia reflects the diversity of the community it portrays.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monoglossia - the use of only one language or voice
  • synonym: polyglossia - the coexistence of several languages in a society or text
