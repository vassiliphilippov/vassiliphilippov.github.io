inductive

adjective
arising from inductance (physics, engineering)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
(of a property or phenomenon) arising from or caused by inductance, especially in electrical circuits.

Examples

Inductive circuit property
An inductive reactance increases as the frequency of the alternating current rises.
In transformers, inductive coupling is an essential mechanism for energy transfer.
The circuit's inductive properties must be considered when designing filters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capacitive - arising from capacitance, as in capacitive reactance
