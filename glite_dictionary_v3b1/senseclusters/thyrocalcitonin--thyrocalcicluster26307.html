thyrocalcitonin

noun
thyroid hormone that lowers blood calcium
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪroʊˌkælsɪˈtoʊnɪn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərəʊˌkælsɪˈtəʊnɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that lowers blood calcium levels and inhibits bone resorption

Examples

thyrocalcitonin hormone
Production of thyrocalcitonin increases in response to elevated calcium levels.
Researchers are studying the effects of thyrocalcitonin on bone density in elderly patients.
The doctor explained how thyrocalcitonin helps regulate the amount of calcium in the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calcitonin - a hormone involved in regulating calcium levels in the blood
