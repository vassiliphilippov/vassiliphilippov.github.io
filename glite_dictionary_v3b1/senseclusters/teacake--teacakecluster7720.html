teacake

noun
small cake or biscuit served with tea
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːˌkeɪk /; / ˈtiˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈtiː.keɪk /; / ˈtiːkeɪk / (BrE)
a small, sweet cake, bun, or biscuit, often served with tea and sometimes containing dried fruit

Examples

A warm teacake
She enjoyed a warm teacake with her afternoon tea.
The bakery makes delicious teacakes filled with currants.
Would you like a teacake or a scone with your coffee?
In Britain, a teacake often contains currants or other dried fruit.
She placed a freshly baked teacake on the plate beside the teapot.
