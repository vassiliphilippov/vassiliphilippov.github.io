doddering

adjective
physically or mentally weak with age
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːdərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdərɪŋ / (BrE)
weak and unsteady, especially because of old age; showing signs of physical or mental decline due to age

Examples

A doddering old man
Despite his doddering appearance, he still enjoyed telling childhood stories.
Her doddering grandfather often forgot where he put his glasses.
The doddering old man shuffled slowly down the hospital corridor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decrepit - worn out or weakened by old age
  • antonym: spry - active and lively, especially for an older person
