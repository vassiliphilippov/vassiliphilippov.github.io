dualize

verb
to form a dual
Pronunciation: / ˈduːəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to form or replace something with its dual, especially in mathematics such as vector spaces, linear programs, or categories

Examples

To dualize a structure
In order to simplify the problem, the mathematician decided to dualize the structure.
You can dualize a linear program to analyze its constraints from a different perspective.
The process to dualize a category is fundamental in advanced mathematics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invert - to reverse the order or position of something
  • antonym: primal - the original form before dualization in mathematics
