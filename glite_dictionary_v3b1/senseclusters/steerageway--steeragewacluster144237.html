steerageway

noun
minimum speed for steering a vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪrɪdʒˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈstɪərɪdʒˌweɪ / (BrE)
(Nautical) The minimum speed at which a vessel can be effectively steered or maneuvered.

Examples

Minimum steerageway speed
Even at low speed, the pilot kept just enough steerageway to maintain control in the narrow channel.
The captain was concerned the ship would lose steerageway in the calm waters.
Without sufficient steerageway, the vessel responded sluggishly to the helm.
