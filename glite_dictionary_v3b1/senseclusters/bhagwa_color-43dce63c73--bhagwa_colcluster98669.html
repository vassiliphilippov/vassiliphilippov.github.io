bhagwa color

noun
political symbol of Hindu nationalism
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌ.gwə ˈkʌl.ər/ /; / /ˈbɑː.gwə ˈkʌl.ər/ /; / /ˈbʌ.gwə ˈkɔːl.ər/ (AmE), /ˈbʌ.gwə ˈkʌl.ə/ /; / /ˈbɑː.gwə ˈkʌl.ə/ (BrE)
(in politics) a saffron/orange color used as a public symbol or brand of Hindu nationalist political groups, appearing on flags, banners, and promotional materials to signal ideological affiliation.

Examples

Businesses sometimes adopt bhagwa color in logos to signal alignment with right-wing political groups.
