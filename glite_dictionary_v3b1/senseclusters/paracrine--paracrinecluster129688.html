paracrine

adjective
cell signaling to neighbors
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌkraɪn / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌkraɪn / (BrE)
describing a type of cell signaling in which a cell produces signals that affect nearby cells

Examples

Paracrine signaling pathway
In paracrine communication, a cell releases molecules that influence nearby cells in the tissue.
The growth factors acted in a paracrine manner to stimulate wound healing.
Researchers studied how paracrine signals affect the development of neighboring nerve cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autocrine - describing cell signaling where a cell targets itself
  • antonym: endocrine - describing cell signaling where hormones act on distant cells
