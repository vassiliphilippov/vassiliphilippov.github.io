nonpopular

adjective
not widely liked
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈpɑpjələr / (AmE), / nɒnˈpɒpjʊlə / (BrE)
not popular; lacking widespread approval, support, or acceptance

Examples

A nonpopular decision
The director's nonpopular decision led to protests from the staff.
Some nonpopular opinions can still be important for discussion and progress.
The candidate's nonpopular stance on taxes cost him many votes in the election.

Related Terms

  • antonym: popular - liked or supported by many people
  • synonym: unpopular - not liked or approved by many people
