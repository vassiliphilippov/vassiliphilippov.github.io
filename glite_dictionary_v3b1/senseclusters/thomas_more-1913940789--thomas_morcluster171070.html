Thomas More

proper noun
English statesman and author of 'Utopia'
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.məs mɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.məs mɔː/ (BrE)
(1478–1535) An English statesman, lawyer, and author, noted for opposing Henry VIII's divorce and execution, and for his work 'Utopia' describing an ideal society.

Examples

Thomas More author
Thomas More was executed in 1535 for refusing to accept Henry VIII as head of the Church of England.
His book 'Utopia' made Thomas More famous throughout Europe.
Many regard Thomas More as a symbol of integrity and moral courage.
