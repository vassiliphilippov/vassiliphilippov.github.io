poetizer

noun
writer of poor or trivial poetry
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊɪtˌaɪzər /; / ˈpoʊ.əˌtaɪz.ər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɪtˌaɪzə /; / ˈpəʊ.ɪˌtaɪ.zə / (BrE)
A person who writes rhymes or verses, especially of poor quality; often used contemptuously for inferior poets.

Examples

A poetizer writes verses
Literary critics often dismiss his work as the output of a minor poetizer.
She hoped to become a respected poet, not just a trivial poetizer seeking attention.
Though he fancied himself a great writer, most considered him merely a poetizer with little talent.
In the 19th century, literary circles often labeled lesser talents as mere poetisers.
Many critics dismissed him as nothing more than a poetiser with little real talent.
Though she dreamed of acclaim, most regarded her as a mediocre poetiser rather than a true poet.
