Australian stilt

noun
long-legged Australian wading bird
Pronunciation: / əˈstreɪljən stɪlt / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪlɪən stɪlt / (BrE)
a long-legged wading bird native to brackish marshes in Australia, known for its three-toed feet and slender build

Examples

The Australian stilt bird
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of the Australian stilt in eastern Australia.
The Australian stilt is known for its strikingly long pink legs and black-and-white plumage.
We observed an Australian stilt feeding along the edge of the brackish marsh.
