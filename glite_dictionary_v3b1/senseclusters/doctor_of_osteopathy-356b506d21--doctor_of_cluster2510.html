Doctor of Osteopathy

noun
professional degree in osteopathy
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑktɚ əv ˌɑstiˈɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ˌɒstiˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
A professional doctoral degree in osteopathic medicine, allowing the holder to practice as an osteopathic physician (DO).

Examples

Doctor of Osteopathy degree
After graduating as a Doctor of Osteopathy, she started her own clinic specializing in holistic care.
He proudly listed 'DO'—Doctor of Osteopathy—after his name on the clinic sign.
In the United States, a Doctor of Osteopathy is fully licensed to prescribe medication and perform surgery.
