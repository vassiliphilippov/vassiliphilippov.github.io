noun

A track made of steel rails along which trains run; a system of tracks and related infrastructure for train transportation.

Examples

High-speed railway
A new railway would boost trade and travel between the two cities.
Maintenance crews work overnight to repair the damaged railway tracks.
The children were fascinated by the old railway running through the countryside.

Synonyms

  • railroad
    1.0 23/m
    railroad refers to the system or company that runs trains in American English, while railway is the British term for the same network.
  • railway line
    2.1 0.25/m
    railway line is a specific route of tracks between places, while railway can mean the whole system or company.
  • rail line
    2.8 1.36/m
    rail line is a set of tracks forming one route for trains, while railway can mean the larger network or company.
  • railroad track
    2.5 2.24/m
    railroad track is the pair of metal rails a train runs on, while railway can mean the whole track system or company.
