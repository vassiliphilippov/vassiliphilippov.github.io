A medical imaging technique that uses reflected high-frequency sound waves to create an image of internal body structures, especially organs and fetuses.

Examples

Echography scan
A technician performed echography to examine the patient's liver for any abnormalities.
Advancements in echography have greatly improved the detection of heart conditions.
The doctor used echography to monitor the development of the baby during pregnancy.

Synonyms

  • ultrasonography
    5.1 0.10/m
    ultrasonography is the formal clinical name for imaging with high frequency sound waves, while echography is a more general term for the same scan.
  • sonography
    5.6 0.09/m
    sonography uses high frequency sound to create images of the body, while echography is the medical term often used in reports.
  • ultrasound
    4.1 5/m
    ultrasound is the common everyday name for scans using sound waves, while echography is the technical medical term for them.
