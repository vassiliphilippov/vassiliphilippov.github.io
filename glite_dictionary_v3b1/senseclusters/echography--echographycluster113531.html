echography

noun
medical imaging by ultrasound
1.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /ek.ˈɑː.grə.fiː/ (AmE), /ek.ˈɒg.rə.fiː/ (BrE)
A medical imaging technique that uses reflected high-frequency sound waves to create an image of internal body structures, especially organs and fetuses.

Examples

Echography scan
A technician performed echography to examine the patient's liver for any abnormalities.
Advancements in echography have greatly improved the detection of heart conditions.
The doctor used echography to monitor the development of the baby during pregnancy.
