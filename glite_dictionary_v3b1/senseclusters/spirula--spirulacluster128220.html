spirula

noun
tropical cephalopod with a coiled internal shell
4.7
Pronunciation: / spɪˈruːlə / (AmE), / spaɪˈruːlə / (BrE)
a small tropical deep-sea cephalopod of the genus Spirula, characterized by large eyes, short arms, and a many-chambered internal shell coiled in a flat spiral

Examples

The spirula shell
Marine biologists study the spirula to understand the evolution of cephalopods.
The spirula is rarely seen alive because it inhabits deep tropical ocean waters.
The unusual shell of the spirula often washes up on beaches after storms.
