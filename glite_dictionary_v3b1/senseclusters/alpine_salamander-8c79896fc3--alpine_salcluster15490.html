alpine salamander

noun
ovoviviparous salamander of the Alps
9.9 9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈælpaɪn səˈlændər / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn ˈsaləməndə / (BrE)
A type of salamander native to the Alps, known for being ovoviviparous, giving birth to live young rather than laying eggs.

Examples

The alpine salamander
The alpine salamander is well adapted to life in the cold, mountainous regions of Europe.
Unlike many amphibians, the alpine salamander gives birth to live young.
We spotted an alpine salamander while hiking in the Austrian Alps after a rainstorm.
