thyroid cartilage

noun
largest cartilage of the larynx (Adam's apple)
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪˌrɔɪd ˈkɑrtɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈθaɪərɔɪd ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the largest cartilage of the larynx, commonly known as the Adam's apple, forming the front wall of the voice box

Examples

The thyroid cartilage front wall
His thyroid cartilage is more prominent, forming a noticeable Adam's apple.
Swelling around the thyroid cartilage may indicate an injury to the larynx.
The doctor explained that a fractured thyroid cartilage could affect speech.
