maraging

noun
steel hardening process
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrˌeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːrˌeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the metallurgical process of age-hardening nickel-rich steels to increase their strength and toughness

Examples

Maraging process
The maraging of the steel components resulted in exceptional strength for the aircraft parts.
Engineers must carefully control the maraging process to achieve the desired material properties.
Advances in maraging have led to the development of stronger and more durable alloys.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annealing - a process of heating and cooling metal to reduce hardness
  • synonym: tempering - a heat treatment process to increase toughness of metals
