free-form

noun
unstructured work
Pronunciation: / ˈfriː fɔːrm / (AmE), / ˈfriː fɔːm / (BrE)
a work, composition, or performance that does not follow a fixed structure or established rules

Examples

An abstract free-form
The artist's latest painting is a free-form that breaks away from traditional styles.
During the concert, the band played a free-form that amazed the audience with its creativity.
Her poetry often takes a free-form, allowing her to express ideas without strict patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvisation - a performance created spontaneously without preparation
  • antonym: structured - organized according to a set pattern or rules
