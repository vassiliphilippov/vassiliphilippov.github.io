overfishing

noun
excessive fishing
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of catching too many fish from a body of water, causing the fish population to decline

Examples

To stop overfishing
Many species are threatened by overfishing in the world's oceans.
Strict regulations were introduced to prevent overfishing and protect marine life.
The collapse of the cod population was a direct result of decades of overfishing.
Strict regulations are needed to prevent overfishing in the world's oceans.
Many coastal communities are affected by overfishing and the resulting loss of income.
Scientists warn that overfishing could lead to the collapse of important fish stocks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservation - the protection and management of natural resources
  • synonym: depletion - the reduction in the number or quantity of something
  • synonym: overharvesting - removing too many resources from nature, leading to depletion
  • antonym: sustainable fishing - catching fish in a way that maintains healthy populations
