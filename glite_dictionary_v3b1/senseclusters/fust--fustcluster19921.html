fust

noun
musty smell
9.9
Pronunciation: /fʌst/ (AmE), /fʌst/ (BrE)
a stale or musty smell, especially from old wood, wine, or damp places

Examples

A fust of old books
The old attic was filled with the fust of forgotten books and furniture.
After years of being closed, the cottage had a strong fust that lingered in every room.
He wrinkled his nose at the fust coming from the ancient wine cellar.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freshness - the quality of smelling clean and new
  • synonym: mustiness - the quality of smelling old, damp, or stale
