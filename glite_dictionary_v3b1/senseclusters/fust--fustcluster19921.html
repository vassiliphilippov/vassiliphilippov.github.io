fust

noun
musty smell
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / fÊŒst / (AmE), / fÊŒst / (BrE)
a stale or musty smell, especially from old wood, wine, or damp places

Examples

A fust of old books
The old attic was filled with the fust of forgotten books and furniture.
After years of being closed, the cottage had a strong fust that lingered in every room.
He wrinkled his nose at the fust coming from the ancient wine cellar.

Synonyms

  • mustiness
    7.2 0.08/m
    mustiness is the general, dull smell of damp rooms and old things, while fust names that stale scent in wood or wine.
  • musty smell
    3.4 0.21/m
    musty smell describes a damp, unpleasant odor in closed spaces, while fust often refers to the same smell in old wood or wine.
  • moldiness
    6.8 0.01/m
    moldiness is the smell or taste of mold and decay on things, while fust names a stale scent found in old wood or wine.
