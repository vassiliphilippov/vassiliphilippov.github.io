interferon

noun
a virus-fighting protein produced by cells
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈfɪrɑn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərɒn / (BrE)
a protein produced by cells in response to viral infection that inhibits the replication of viruses

Examples

The interferon protein
Scientists are studying how interferon can be used to treat certain diseases.
The doctor explained that interferon therapy could help fight the viral infection.
When a cell is infected by a virus, it releases interferon to alert neighboring cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cytokine - a category of signaling proteins involved in immune responses
