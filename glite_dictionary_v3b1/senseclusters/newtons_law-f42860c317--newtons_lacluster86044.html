Newton's law

noun
one of Newton's three laws of motion
3.8 0.35/m
Pronunciation: / ÀànuÀêt…ônz l…î / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀêt…ônz l…îÀê / (BrE)
any of three fundamental laws formulated by Isaac Newton that describe the relationship between the motion of an object and the forces acting on it in classical mechanics.

Examples

Newton's law of motion
According to Newton's law, an object at rest will remain at rest unless acted upon by a force.
Engineers rely on Newton's law when designing vehicles and structures subjected to various forces.
The motion of the planets can be explained by Newton's law of universal gravitation.

Synonyms

  • Newton's law of motion
    3.5 0.03/m
    Newton's law of motion is the full textbook name for the three rules about forces and motion, while Newton's law is a shorter common name.
  • law of motion
    3.9 0.29/m
    a law of motion is a general rule about forces and movement, while Newton's law refers specifically to Newton's three laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: law of acceleration - Newton's second law of motion, relating the force acting upon an object to its mass and acceleration
  • synonym: law of action and reaction - Newton's third law of motion, stating that every action has an equal and opposite reaction
  • synonym: law of inertia - Newton's first law of motion, stating that an object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a force
