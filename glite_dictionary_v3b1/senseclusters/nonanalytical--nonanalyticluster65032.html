nonanalytical

adjective
not analyzable
8.7
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ /; / /nɑːn.æn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /nɒn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ /; / /nɒn.æn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
not involving, based on, or able to be examined by analysis or logical reasoning

Examples

Nonanalytical thinking style
The artist's approach was entirely nonanalytical, relying on intuition rather than breaking things down logically.
Some problems are so complex that they require a nonanalytical way of thinking.
His explanation was nonanalytical, focusing on the overall impression instead of detailed reasoning.
Her decision-making style is often nonanalytical, relying more on experience than on data.
The artist's process was deliberately nonanalytical, focusing on emotion rather than technique.
Some problems require a nonanalytical approach to find creative solutions.
