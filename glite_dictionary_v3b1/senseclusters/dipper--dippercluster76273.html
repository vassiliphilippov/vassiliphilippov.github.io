dipper

noun
small bird that dives in streams
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a small, stout bird that frequents fast-moving streams, diving and feeding underwater; species of the family Cinclidae.

Examples

A river dipper bird
On our hike, a dipper paused on a mossy rock before plunging beneath the water.
The dipper is known for its ability to walk underwater in search of insects.
We spotted a dipper darting in and out of the rushing stream.
