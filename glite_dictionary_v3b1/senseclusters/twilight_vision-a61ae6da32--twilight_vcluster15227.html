twilight vision

noun
ability to see in low light
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪlaɪt ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the ability to see in reduced illumination, such as in moonlight or at dusk

Examples

Twilight vision ability
As we age, our twilight vision often deteriorates, making it harder to drive at night.
Cats rely on their twilight vision to navigate through dimly lit environments.
Owls have remarkable twilight vision, allowing them to hunt efficiently at dusk.
