duty-bound

adjective
obliged by duty or morality
Pronunciation: / ˈduːti baʊnd / (AmE), / ˈdjuːti baʊnd / (BrE)
required by duty or moral obligation to do something; having a strong sense of responsibility to act.

Examples

To be duty-bound
As a doctor, she felt duty-bound to help the injured stranger.
He was duty-bound to report the incident to the authorities.
They considered themselves duty-bound to honor their parents' wishes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not constrained by duty or obligation
  • synonym: obliged - compelled or required to do something out of a sense of duty or moral responsibility
