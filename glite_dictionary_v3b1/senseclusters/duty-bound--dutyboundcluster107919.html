duty-bound

adjective
obliged by duty or morality
5.8 0.36/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀêti ba änd / (AmE), / ÀàdjuÀêti ba änd / (BrE)
required by duty or moral obligation to do something; having a strong sense of responsibility to act.

Examples

To be duty-bound
As a doctor, she felt duty-bound to help the injured stranger.
He was duty-bound to report the incident to the authorities.
They considered themselves duty-bound to honor their parents' wishes.

Synonyms

  • obligated
    4.2 0.62/m
    obligated is required by law or personal conscience to act, while duty-bound expresses a strong moral responsibility to act.
  • bound
    6.0 1.36/m
    bound is often used for legal or contractual duty, while duty-bound stresses a personal, moral sense of responsibility.
  • obliged
    4.4 0.00/m
    obliged feels like a social or moral push to act politely, while duty-bound shows a firm inner responsibility to act.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not constrained by duty or obligation
  • synonym: obliged - compelled or required to do something out of a sense of duty or moral responsibility
