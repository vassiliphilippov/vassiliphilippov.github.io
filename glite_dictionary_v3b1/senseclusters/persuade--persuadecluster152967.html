persuade

verb
cause someone to do or believe something by reasoning
4.9 2.3
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈsweɪd/ (AmE), /pə.ˈsweɪd/ (BrE)
to convince someone to do something or to believe something by giving reasons or appealing to them

Examples

To persuade someone
No matter what he said, he could not persuade his parents to change their minds.
She managed to persuade her friend to join the gym with her.
The advertisement is designed to persuade people to try the new product.
His charm and determination helped him persuade colleagues to join his cause.
She tried to persuade the committee to support the proposal, but they remained unconvinced.
The politician's speech failed to persuade voters to change their minds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convince - to cause someone to believe firmly in the truth of something
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
