Examples

To persuade someone
No matter what he said, he could not persuade his parents to change their minds.

Synonyms

  • convince
    2.7 73/m
    convince makes someone believe something is true, while persuade often aims to get someone to act or accept an idea.
  • bring round
    4.4 0.01/m
    bring round persuades someone to accept an idea or choice, while persuade is the general verb for convincing by reason or appeal.
  • induce
    4.9 6.33/m
    induce is a more formal verb that often means cause someone to act, while persuade focuses on convincing by reasons or appeal.
  • sway
    3.6 4.33/m
    sway influences someone's opinion or decision gently over time, while persuade involves actively giving reasons or appeals to change belief.
  • win over
    3.3 3.17/m
    win over gains someone's support or agreement for you, while persuade is the broader act of convincing by argument or appeal.
  • coax
    4.1 5.15/m
    coax gently and patiently encourages someone with kindness, while persuade covers many methods of convincing, like reason or appeal.
  • cajole
    7.7 1.20/m
    cajole uses flattery and repeated appeals to get someone to do something, while persuade is the general act of convincing.
  • prevail upon
    7.7 0.33/m
    prevail upon persuades someone who is reluctant after effort, while persuade is a general word for convincing by reason or appeal.
  • twist someone's arm
    4.4 0.02/m
    twist someone's arm pressures or forces someone informally to comply, while persuade usually means convincing by reasons, discussion, or friendly appeal.
