American Civil War

proper noun
the U.S. civil war (1861–65) between North and South
3.0
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˌsɪvəl ˈwɔɹ / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˌsɪvəl ˈwɔː / (BrE)
The war fought in the United States from 1861 to 1865 between the Northern states (the Union) and the Southern states (the Confederacy) over issues including states' rights and slavery.

Examples

The American Civil War battle
Many historians have studied the causes of the American Civil War in great detail.
Some battlefields from the American Civil War are now popular tourist destinations.
The American Civil War led to the abolition of slavery in the United States.
