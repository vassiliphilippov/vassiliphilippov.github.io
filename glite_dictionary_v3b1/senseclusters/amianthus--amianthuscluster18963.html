amianthus

noun
asbestos fabric
Pronunciation: / ˌæmiˈænθəs / (AmE), / ˌæmiˈænθəs / (BrE)
a fire-resistant cloth or fabric made from asbestos fibers

Examples

Amianthus cloth
The ancient Greeks used amianthus to make lamp wicks that would not burn away.
A rare piece of amianthus fabric was found in the ruins of the old temple.
Because of its fireproof qualities, amianthus was once used to wrap valuable manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asbestos - a group of minerals used for their fire-resistant properties
