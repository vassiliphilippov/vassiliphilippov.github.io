PPT

noun
parts per thousand
Pronunciation: / pi pi ti / (AmE), / piː piː tiː / (BrE)
a unit meaning 'parts per thousand', used to express the concentration of a substance in a mixture or solution

Examples

35 PPT salt
The laboratory measured the salt content as 35 PPT in the seawater sample.
A concentration of 2 PPT is considered safe for most aquatic plants.
The report listed the pollutant level at 0.5 PPT, well below the danger threshold.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: PPM - parts per million, a unit for very small concentrations
  • degree lower: percent - one part in a hundred, a common way to express proportions
