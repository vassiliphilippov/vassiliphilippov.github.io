innocent

adjective
lacking worldly experience; naïve
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnəsənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsənt /; / ˈɪnəs(ə)nt / (BrE)
lacking experience, knowledge, or sophistication; naive or unaware

Examples

Innocent child
He gave me an innocent look, completely unaware of the social rules he had broken.
Her innocent questions revealed how little she knew about city life.
The tourists' innocent excitement charmed the locals who were used to more worldly visitors.
He arrived in the city wholly innocent of its unwritten rules.
She was innocent of the workings of local government.
Tourists often remain innocent of cultural customs when visiting new countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: experienced - having knowledge or skill from doing something
  • synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something specified
  • synonym: naïve - having a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • antonym: sophisticated - having a great deal of worldly experience and knowledge
  • synonym: uninitiated - not familiar with something; inexperienced
