positivist

adjective
relating to positivism (philosophy)
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːz.ɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of positivism, a philosophical theory asserting that only factual knowledge gained through observation and experience is meaningful.

Examples

Positivist approach
Her views on law are firmly rooted in the positivist tradition.
Many positivist thinkers argue that only observable phenomena should be studied in science.
The university course explores the influence of positivist ideology on social research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: positivism - philosophical theory that knowledge is exclusively derived from empirical and observable evidence
