posterior root

noun
dorsal sensory root of a spinal nerve
4.8 7.5
Pronunciation: /pɔː.ˈstɪr.jər ruːt/ /; / /poʊ.ˈstɪr.jər ruːt/ /; / /pɑː.ˈstɪr.jər ruːt/ (AmE), /pɒ.ˈstɪə.riː.ə ruːt/ /; / /pə.ˈstɪə.riː.ə ruːt/ (BrE)
one of the two roots of a spinal nerve that extends dorsally from the spinal cord and contains sensory nerve fibers

Examples

Posterior root nerve
Damage to the posterior root can cause loss of sensation in certain areas of the skin.
In spinal anatomy, the posterior root is distinguished from the anterior root by its function and location.
The posterior root carries sensory information from the body to the spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anterior root - the front (ventral) root of a spinal nerve, contains motor fibers
