watchtower

noun
tower for observation or guarding
4.3 0.89/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ëÀêt ÉÀåta ä…ôr / (AmE), / Ààw…ít ÉÀåta ä…ô / (BrE)
A tall structure built to provide a wide view for observing and guarding an area, often used for spotting danger or overseeing prisoners.

Examples

Prison watchtower
A fire was quickly spotted from the watchtower and the alert was sounded.
Several watchtowers were constructed along the prison walls to monitor the inmates.
The guard climbed the watchtower to scan the horizon for any approaching threats.

Synonyms

  • lookout station
    4.4 0.01/m
    a lookout station is a high structure used for surveillance and public safety, while a watchtower focuses on guarding and spotting danger.
  • observation tower
    4.2 0.14/m
    an observation tower is built tall for sightseeing and watching, while a watchtower is mainly for guarding and spotting danger.
  • lookout
    3.4 1.16/m
    a lookout is any elevated place used for keeping watch and seeing far distances, while a watchtower is a guarded tower for spotting danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lookout - a place or structure from which to keep watch
  • synonym: observation tower - a tower built for the purpose of observing an area
