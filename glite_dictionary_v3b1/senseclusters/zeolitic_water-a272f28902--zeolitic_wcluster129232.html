zeolitic water

noun
water adsorbed by a zeolite mineral
9.5
Pronunciation: / zoʊˈɪlɪtɪk ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / zɪəˈlɪtɪk ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water molecules held within the structure of a zeolite mineral, typically by adsorption or inclusion in its porous framework.

Examples

Zeolitic water in crystals
Scientists measured the amount of zeolitic water present in the crystal structure.
The zeolitic water can be removed by heating the mineral at high temperatures.
The process of dehydration releases the zeolitic water from the pores of the zeolite.
