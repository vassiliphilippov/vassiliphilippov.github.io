Tribulus terrestris

noun
sprawling weed with spiny fruits
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪbjələs tɪˈrɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪbjʊləs tɛˈrɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A tropical annual plant with sprawling stems and spiny fruits, known for being a poisonous pasture weed.

Examples

Tribulus terrestris weed problem
Tribulus terrestris can quickly cover large areas, causing problems for grazing livestock.
Farmers often struggle to control Tribulus terrestris because its spiny fruits attach to tires and animal hooves.
Some herbal supplements use extracts from Tribulus terrestris for their purported medicinal properties.
