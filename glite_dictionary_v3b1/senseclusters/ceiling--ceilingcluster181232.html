ceiling

noun
lowest cloud layer altitude (meteorology)
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(Meteorology) The altitude of the lowest layer of clouds covering more than half the sky, important in aviation and weather reporting.

Examples

Cloud ceiling
Due to a low ceiling, several flights were delayed until conditions improved.
Pilots were warned that the ceiling was too low for safe landing.
The weather report indicated a ceiling of 800 feet, restricting visibility for small aircraft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloud base - the lowest altitude of the visible portion of a cloud
