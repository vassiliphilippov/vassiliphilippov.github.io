hypocenter

noun
center of explosion
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪpoʊˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪpəʊˌsɛntə / (BrE)
the point on the ground directly beneath the center of an explosion, especially a nuclear bomb or earthquake.

Examples

Hypocenter of the explosion
The rescue team searched for survivors near the hypocenter of the explosion.
Radiation levels were highest at the hypocenter after the nuclear detonation.
Scientists studied the damage patterns radiating from the hypocenter of the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epicenter - the point on the Earth's surface directly above an earthquake's origin
  • antonym: periphery - the outer edge or boundary of an area
