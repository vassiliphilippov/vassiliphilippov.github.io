tachypnea

noun
rapid breathing
Pronunciation: / tæˈkɪpniə / (AmE), / tækˈɪpnɪə /; / tækɪpˈniːə / (BrE)
abnormally rapid breathing, usually as a sign of illness or a medical condition

Examples

Abnormal tachypnea rate
The doctor noticed tachypnea in the infant shortly after birth.
Mild tachypnea often resolves on its own within a few days in newborns.
Nurses monitored the baby's tachypnea to ensure there were no complications.
The doctor became concerned when she observed tachypnea in the young child.
After running up the stairs, he experienced mild tachypnea that quickly resolved.
Persistent tachypnea can be a sign of respiratory infection or heart problems.
The nurse noticed the patient's tachypnea and immediately alerted the doctor.
Children with high fevers often develop tachypnea as their bodies try to cool down.
After running up the stairs, he experienced brief tachypnea before his breathing returned to normal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bradypnea - abnormally slow breathing
  • antonym: bradypnoea - an abnormally slow rate of breathing
  • synonym: hyperventilation - breathing at an abnormally rapid rate, often deeper than normal
