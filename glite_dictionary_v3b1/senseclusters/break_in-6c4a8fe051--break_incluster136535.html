break in

verb
interrupt or intrude without invitation
5.7 3.4
Pronunciation: / breɪk ɪn / (AmE), / breɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt or intrude on a conversation or activity without invitation or permission

Examples

Break in during a meeting
I didn't want to break in, but I had something important to add.
Please don't break in while others are speaking during the meeting.
She tends to break in on conversations without realizing it.
I hate when people break in while I'm still talking.
If you need to break in, please raise your hand first.
She tried to break in on their conversation, but they ignored her.
