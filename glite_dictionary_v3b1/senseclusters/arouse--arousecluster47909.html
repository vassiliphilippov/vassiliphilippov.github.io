arouse

verb
cause feelings or responses
6.0
Pronunciation: / əˈraʊz / (AmE), / əˈraʊz / (BrE)
to cause a particular feeling, emotion, or response to be felt or expressed

Examples

To arouse interest
Her speech was intended to arouse public interest in the issue.
The film managed to arouse strong emotions among the audience.
The news did not arouse much excitement in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evoke - bring or recall a feeling, memory, or image to mind
  • antonym: quell - to put an end to a feeling, often by force; suppress
  • synonym: stimulate - encourage something to develop or become more active
