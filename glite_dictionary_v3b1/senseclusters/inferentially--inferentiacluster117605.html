inferentially

adverb
by inference
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəli / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that is based on inference or reasoning, rather than direct observation

Examples

To inferentially reason
The scientist inferentially determined the presence of water on the planet from the data collected.
We can only inferentially understand the motives behind his actions, as he never explained them.
The report was written inferentially, relying on indirect evidence rather than firsthand accounts.
The scientist inferentially analyzed the data to reach a conclusion about the experiment.
Results were inferentially supported by statistical tests comparing the two groups.
She argued inferentially, using evidence to support her claims rather than stating them directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deductively - by using logical reasoning from general principles
  • antonym: directly - in a way that involves direct observation or experience
  • synonym: logically - in a way that uses clear reasoning
