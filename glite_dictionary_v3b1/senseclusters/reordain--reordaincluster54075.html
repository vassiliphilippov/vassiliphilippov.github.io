reordain

verb
to appoint again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɔrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to appoint or assign someone again to an office or position, especially in a non-religious context

Examples

To reordain a leader
After reviewing his performance, the board decided to reordain him as chairperson for another year.
The committee may reordain the current leader if no other candidates are nominated.
Due to her effective management, the council voted unanimously to reordain her for a second term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dismiss - to remove someone from a position
  • synonym: reappoint - to assign someone to a position again
