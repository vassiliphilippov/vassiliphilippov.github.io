prelacteal

noun
feed before breastfeeding
9.9
Pronunciation: / priːˈlæk.ti.əl / (AmE), / priːˈlæk.ti.əl / (BrE)
a feed or substance given to a newborn before the mother's first breastfeeding

Examples

A prelacteal feed
Some cultures traditionally give a prelacteal to newborns before the mother begins breastfeeding.
Doctors now recommend avoiding any prelacteal feeds to support exclusive breastfeeding.
The nurse explained the risks of offering a prelacteal to the baby in the first hours after birth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colostrum - the first form of milk produced by mammals after giving birth
