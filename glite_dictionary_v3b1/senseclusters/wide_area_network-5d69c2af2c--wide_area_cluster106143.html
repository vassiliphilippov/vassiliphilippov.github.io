wide area network

noun
network covering a large geographic area
1.3 2.8
Pronunciation: /waɪd ˈe.rɪ.ə ˈnet.wɜːrk/ /; / /waɪd ˈeə.rɪ.ə ˈnet.wɜːrk/ (AmE), /waɪd ˈeə.rɪ.ə ˈnet.wɜːk/ /; / /waɪd ˈe.rɪ.ə ˈnet.wɜːk/ (BrE)
a computer network that covers a large geographic area, typically connecting multiple smaller networks such as local area networks

Examples

A wide area network
Many internet service providers use a wide area network to manage long-distance data traffic.
Setting up a wide area network can link several local area networks across cities.
The company's offices in different countries are connected by a wide area network.
