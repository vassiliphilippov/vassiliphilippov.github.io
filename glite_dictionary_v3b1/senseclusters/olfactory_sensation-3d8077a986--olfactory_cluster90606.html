olfactory sensation

noun
sensation of smell caused by airborne chemicals
Pronunciation: / ɑlˈfæktəɹi sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ɒlˈfæktəri sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the sensation experienced when the olfactory receptors in the nose are stimulated by airborne chemicals, resulting in the perception of smell

Examples

An olfactory sensation of smoke
A sudden olfactory sensation warned her of smoke drifting from the kitchen.
Researchers study how different chemicals cause distinct olfactory sensations in the brain.
The aroma of fresh coffee triggered a powerful olfactory sensation in him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gustatory sensation - the sensation produced by stimulation of the taste buds
  • synonym: smell - the faculty or act of perceiving odours
