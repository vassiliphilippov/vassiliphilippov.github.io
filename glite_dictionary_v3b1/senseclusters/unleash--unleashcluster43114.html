unleash

verb
to release suddenly (emotion, force)
4.3 12/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàli É / (AmE), /  ånÀàliÀê É / (BrE)
to suddenly release or set free something powerful or previously held back, such as an emotion, force, or animal

Examples

Heavy rainfall can unleash dangerous floods in the region.
She was careful not to unleash her anger during the meeting.
The new policy may unleash a wave of innovation across the industry.
