hectoliter

noun
metric unit of volume (100 liters)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛk.təˌliː.tər /; / ˈhɛktəˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈhɛk.təˌliː.tə /; / ˈhɛktəliːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to 100 liters, commonly used for measuring liquids such as wine or beer

Examples

A hectoliter of beer
A shipment of beer arrived in massive barrels, each containing several hectoliters.
The vineyard produced over 500 hectoliters of wine this season.
Water consumption rose to nearly one hectoliter per household per day.
A hectolitre of water weighs about 100 kilograms.
The brewery produced over 500 hectolitres of beer last month.
Wine is often sold in bulk by the hectolitre in European countries.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: liter - metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
