lick into shape

verb
make something acceptable by improving it
Pronunciation: / lɪk ˈɪntu ˌʃeɪp / (AmE), / lɪk ˈɪntə ˌʃeɪp / (BrE)
to improve or refine something, especially by persistent effort, until it is in an acceptable or desired condition

Examples

To lick into shape a plan
It took several attempts to lick into shape the final version of her thesis.
The new manager was hired to lick into shape the struggling department.
They worked all weekend to lick into shape the script for the school play.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polish - to make something better or more refined
  • degree lower: rough up - to treat or work at something only superficially or quickly
