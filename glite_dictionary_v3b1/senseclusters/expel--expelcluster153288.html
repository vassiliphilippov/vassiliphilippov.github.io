expel

verb
force out a substance
2.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɛl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛl / (BrE)
to force out or discharge a substance, such as gas or liquid, from a body or container

Examples

To expel gas
After digestion, the body expels waste through the intestines.
Some aquatic plants expel oxygen as a byproduct of photosynthesis.
The old factory expelled smoke into the atmosphere every day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up a substance
  • synonym: discharge - to allow a substance to flow out from where it has been confined
  • synonym: emit - to send out (a substance, such as gas or light)
