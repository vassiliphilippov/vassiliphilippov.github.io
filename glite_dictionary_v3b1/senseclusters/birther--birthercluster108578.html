birther

noun
citizenship conspiracy theorist
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.θər/ (AmE), /ˈbɜː.θə/ (BrE)
a person who doubts or claims that a public figure, especially a politician, was not born in the country they claim citizenship in (usually to question their legitimacy)

Examples

Birther conspiracy theory
The birther movement gained attention during the presidential election.
Many dismissed the claims made by birther activists as unfounded.
He was labeled a birther after repeatedly questioning the leader's birthplace.
Several birthers continued to question the president's citizenship despite clear evidence.
The news program discussed how birther claims spread rapidly on social media.
He was labeled a birther after repeatedly demanding to see official birth documents.
