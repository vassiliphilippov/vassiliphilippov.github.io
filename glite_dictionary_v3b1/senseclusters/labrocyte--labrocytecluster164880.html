labrocyte

noun
a connective tissue cell involved in allergic responses
Pronunciation: / ˈlæbrəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæbrəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
A large connective tissue cell that contains histamine, heparin, and serotonin, which are released during allergic reactions or in response to injury or inflammation.

Examples

Labrocytes release histamine
Labrocytes release histamine during the body's allergic response.
Research into labrocyte functions has advanced treatments for allergic conditions.
The doctor explained that inflammation was partly caused by overactive labrocytes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mast cell - a cell filled with basophil granules, found in connective tissue and releasing histamine and other substances during inflammatory and allergic reactions
