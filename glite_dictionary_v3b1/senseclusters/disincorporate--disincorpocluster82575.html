disincorporate

verb
to dissolve legal status
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈkɔːrpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈkɔːpəreɪt / (BrE)
to officially end the legal status of a corporation, organization, or town, so it no longer exists as a legal entity

Examples

To disincorporate a town
The government decided to disincorporate the town after its population dropped below the required minimum.
After years of inactivity, the board voted to disincorporate the nonprofit organization.
Legal procedures must be followed to disincorporate a municipality in this state.
The city council decided to disincorporate the small town due to its declining population.
After years of inactivity, the members voted to disincorporate the organization.
Legal procedures must be followed to properly disincorporate a company.
The city council voted to disincorporate after years of financial difficulties.
If the population drops too low, the state may require the town to disincorporate.
Residents debated whether to disincorporate and let the county manage local services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissolve - to officially end the existence of an organization or body
  • antonym: incorporate - to form into a legal corporation or organization
