complexed

adjective
having a psychological complex
8.2
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛkst / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛkst / (BrE)
affected by a psychological complex, often feeling insecure or self-conscious about something.

Examples

To feel complexed about
She always felt complexed about her accent when speaking in public.
Many teenagers become complexed about their appearance during high school.
He seemed complexed by his lack of experience compared to his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty and self-assurance
  • synonym: insecure - not confident or assured; uncertain and anxious
