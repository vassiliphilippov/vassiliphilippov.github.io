unwilted

adjective
fresh and firm
3.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɪltɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪltɪd / (BrE)
not wilted; still fresh, firm, and upright, especially referring to plants or leaves

Examples

Unwilted leaves
She picked unwilted flowers from the garden for the vase.
The chef insisted on using only unwilted spinach in the salad.
After a night in water, the bouquet looked completely unwilted again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fresh - recently picked or not spoiled
  • antonym: wilted - limp and drooping, usually due to lack of water
