liquid state

noun
state of matter that flows easily
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪd steɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd steɪt / (BrE)
The physical condition in which a substance is fluid, has a fixed volume but no fixed shape, and is nearly incompressible.

Examples

The liquid state
Mercury is unusual because it is in a liquid state at ordinary temperatures.
Water is found naturally in the liquid state at room temperature.
When heated, ice changes from a solid to a liquid state.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gaseous state - the state in which matter has neither definite shape nor volume
  • synonym: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
  • antonym: solid state - the state in which matter has a definite shape and volume
