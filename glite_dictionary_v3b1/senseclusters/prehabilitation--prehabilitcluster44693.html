prehabilitation

noun
pre-surgery preparation
8.2
Pronunciation: /priː.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
planned physical, mental, or educational activities done before surgery or medical treatment to improve recovery and reduce complications

Examples

Surgery prehabilitation program
Doctors recommend prehabilitation to help patients build strength before major operations.
Participating in prehabilitation can lead to a faster and smoother recovery after surgery.
The hospital offers a prehabilitation program for patients scheduled for joint replacement.
Doctors often recommend prehabilitation to help patients build strength before starting cancer treatment.
Her prehabilitation program included exercise, nutrition advice, and stress management techniques.
By participating in prehabilitation, he recovered more quickly after his medical procedure.

  • antonym: rehabilitation - therapy to restore health or normal life after illness or surgery
