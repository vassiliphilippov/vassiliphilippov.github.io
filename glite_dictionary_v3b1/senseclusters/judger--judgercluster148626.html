judger

noun
person making decisions
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒə / (BrE)
a person who makes a formal decision or ruling in a legal, administrative, or disciplinary hearing

Examples

Courtroom judger
The judger listened carefully to both sides before announcing the decision.
In the competition, the judger evaluated each performance based on strict criteria.
As a fair judger, she always considered all the evidence before making a ruling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arbitrator - a person chosen to settle a dispute outside of court
  • synonym: judge - a person who has the authority to decide cases in a court of law
  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something but does not make decisions
