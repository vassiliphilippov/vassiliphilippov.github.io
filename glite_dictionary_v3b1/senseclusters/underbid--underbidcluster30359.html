underbid

verb
bid less than one's hand merits (cards)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈbɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbɪd / (BrE)
To bid less than the true value or strength of one's hand, especially in card games.

Examples

To underbid in bridge
He has a tendency to underbid because he often doubts his luck.
Players are cautioned not to underbid when their cards are strong.
She accidentally underbid her hand, missing the chance for a higher score.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overbid - to bid more than the strength of the hand warrants (cards)
