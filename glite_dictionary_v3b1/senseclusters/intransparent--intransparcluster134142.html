intransparent

adjective
not open or clear
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrænˈspɛrənt / (AmE), / ˌɪntrænˈspærənt / (BrE)
not open, clear, or honest; refusing to share information or make decisions understandable to others

Examples

Intransparent company rules
The company's intransparent policies made it hard for employees to know how decisions were made.
Critics accused the government of being intransparent about the new law's true purpose.
An intransparent process can lead to mistrust among team members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opaque - not transparent or difficult to understand
  • synonym: secretive - not willing to share information with others
  • antonym: transparent - open and easy to understand or see through
