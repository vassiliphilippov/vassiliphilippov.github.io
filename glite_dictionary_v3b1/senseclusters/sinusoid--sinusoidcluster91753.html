sinusoid

noun
wave-shaped curve of y = sin x
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪnəˌsɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪ.njuˌsɔɪd / (BrE)
A curve described by the equation y = sin x; a wave-shaped curve representing the sine function.

Examples

A sinusoid curve
The sinusoid is commonly used to model alternating current in electrical engineering.
The teacher explained how the sinusoid relates to the properties of the sine function.
When graphed, the sinusoid has a smooth, repetitive wave-like form.
