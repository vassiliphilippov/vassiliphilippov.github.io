Caiman sclerops

noun
a species of caiman with bony eye ridges
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪ.mən ˈsklɛr.ɑps / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.mən ˈsklɪə.rɒps / (BrE)
A species of caiman with bony ridges above the eyes, native to regions from southern Mexico to Argentina.

Examples

Caiman sclerops species
Researchers observed a group of Caiman sclerops basking by the riverbank in the Amazon.
The Caiman sclerops inhabits freshwater lakes and slow-moving rivers across Central and South America.
The Caiman sclerops is known for the bony ridges above its eyes, resembling a pair of spectacles.
