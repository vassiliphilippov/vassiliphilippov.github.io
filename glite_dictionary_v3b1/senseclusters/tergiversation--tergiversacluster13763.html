tergiversation

noun
evasion by ambiguous language
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌtɜːrdʒɪvərˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɜːdʒɪvəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of avoiding a straightforward statement or action by using ambiguous or evasive language; equivocation.

Examples

Political tergiversation
Critics accused her speech of blatant tergiversation, as she never addressed the question directly.
Repeated tergiversation eroded the public's trust in the company's official statements.
The senator's tergiversation during the interview left many confused about his true position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equivocation - the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and straightforward
