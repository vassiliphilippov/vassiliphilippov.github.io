fuss

verb
pay excessive or motherly attention
3.5 1.31/m
Pronunciation: / fÊŒs / (AmE), / fÊŒs / (BrE)
to pay excessive attention or care to someone, often in a way that seems unnecessary or overprotective

Examples

To fuss over children
He fussed over his guests, making sure everyone had enough to eat and drink.
She always fusses over her grandchildren when they come to visit.
Stop fussing over me; I'm perfectly capable of tying my own shoes.

Synonyms

  • overprotect
    5.1 0.12/m
    overprotect shields someone from risk and stops their independence, while fuss means giving unnecessary, often nervous attention or care.
  • mother
    5.1 0.50/m
    mother shows strong, often maternal care and protection, while fuss is more general, anxious or excessive attention without maternal tone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for someone properly
  • synonym: pamper - to give someone a lot of attention and comfort, often more than necessary
