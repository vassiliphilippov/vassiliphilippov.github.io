feed off

verb
derive nourishment or energy from something
Pronunciation: / fid ɔf / (AmE), / fiːd ɒf / (BrE)
to gain nourishment, energy, or motivation from something; to take sustenance, inspiration, or benefit from a source

Examples

To feed off chemicals
Certain bacteria feed off chemicals released by underwater vents.
She seems to feed off the excitement of the audience during her performances.
The rumors quickly fed off people's fears and spread throughout the town.
