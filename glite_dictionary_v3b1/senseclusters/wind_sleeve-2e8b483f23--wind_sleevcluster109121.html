wind sleeve

noun
device showing wind direction and strength
Pronunciation: / wɪnd sliːv / (AmE), / wɪnd sliːv / (BrE)
A truncated cloth cone attached to a pole, used to indicate the direction and strength of the wind, especially at airports.

Examples

Airport wind sleeve
Pilots check the wind sleeve before takeoff to assess the wind's direction and intensity.
The wind sleeve at the airfield was pointing directly east, indicating a strong breeze from the west.
The airport replaced its torn wind sleeve with a new, brightly colored one so it was visible from a distance.
