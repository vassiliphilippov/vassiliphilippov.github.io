macular edema

noun
swelling of the macula in the eye
5.8 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmækjələr ɪˈdimə / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊlə ɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which swelling of the macula in the eye occurs due to fluid leakage and accumulation, often resulting in impaired vision

Examples

Macular edema swelling
Patients with diabetes are at increased risk for developing macular edema.
The ophthalmologist explained that her blurred vision was caused by macular edema.
Treatment of macular edema often involves injections or laser therapy to reduce the swelling.
