pledgee

noun
person holding something as security for a pledge
Pronunciation: / plɛdˈʒi / (AmE), / plɛdˈʒiː / (BrE)
a person or organization to whom something is given as security for a loan or obligation

Examples

The pledgee sells property
If the borrower defaults, the pledgee may sell the pledged property to recover the loan.
The pledgee is entitled to retain the collateral until the debt is fully repaid.
The contract clearly stated the rights and duties of both the pledgor and the pledgee.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pledger - person who makes or gives a pledge
