braky

adjective
covered with brambles and undergrowth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪki / (AmE), / ˈbreɪki / (BrE)
(of land) covered with thick undergrowth such as brambles, ferns, or similar plants

Examples

A braky field
After years of neglect, the garden had become utterly braky and wild.
The hikers struggled to make their way through the braky hillside.
The path wound through a dense, braky forest that scratched at our legs.
She described the braky valley as both wild and beautiful.
The path was overgrown and braky, making it hard to pass.
We often hiked through the braky hills in late summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overgrown - covered with plants that have grown in an uncontrolled way
  • synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or trees
