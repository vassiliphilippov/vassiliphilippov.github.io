Meiji

proper noun
Japanese historical era
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒi / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒi / (BrE)
the period of Japanese history from 1868 to 1912, marked by rapid modernization and reform

Examples

Meiji era reforms
During the Meiji period, Japan rapidly adopted Western technology and institutions.
Many important social changes took place in Japan under the Meiji government.
The Meiji era is often credited with transforming Japan into a modern nation.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Edo - the Japanese era before Meiji, from 1603 to 1868
  • degree higher: Taisho - the Japanese era following Meiji, from 1912 to 1926
