climbing salamander

noun
a North American salamander adapted for climbing
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪmɪŋ səˌlæməndər / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ səˌlæməndə / (BrE)
any of several North American salamanders adapted for climbing, with well-developed limbs and long, squared-off toes

Examples

A climbing salamander on branch
Researchers found a climbing salamander resting on a moss-covered branch after it had rained.
The climbing salamander has specialized toes that help it cling to tree bark.
Unlike other amphibians, the climbing salamander spends much of its time off the ground in shrubs and trees.
