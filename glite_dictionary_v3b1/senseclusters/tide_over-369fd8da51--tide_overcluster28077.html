tide over

verb
help through a difficult period
4.4
Pronunciation: / taɪd ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / taɪd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to give someone what they need to get through a difficult time until things improve

Examples

To tide over a crisis
A loan from her parents helped tide over the difficult time after she lost her job.
The advance payment will tide over the company until sales pick up next quarter.
We borrowed some money from friends to tide over the summer months.
A small loan helped tide over the family until he could find another job.
I keep snacks in my desk to tide over my hunger in the afternoon.
She asked her parents to tide over her until her next paycheck arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: get by - manage with difficulty to survive or make progress
  • synonym: help out - to assist someone in a time of need
  • synonym: make do - manage with the limited resources one has
  • synonym: sustain - to support physically or mentally for a period of time
