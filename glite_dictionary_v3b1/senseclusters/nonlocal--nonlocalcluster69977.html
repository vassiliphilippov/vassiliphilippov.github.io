nonlocal

adjective
across distance
7.2
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
not limited to a single location; involving interactions, effects, or dependencies that occur across distance rather than at a single point

Examples

Nonlocal effects
Quantum physics often discusses nonlocal effects that cannot be explained by classical theories.
The researchers observed nonlocal interactions between particles separated by large distances.
Some computer algorithms use nonlocal information to improve accuracy across an entire network.

Related Terms

  • synonym: global - relating to or affecting the whole world or a large area
  • antonym: local - restricted to a specific place or area
