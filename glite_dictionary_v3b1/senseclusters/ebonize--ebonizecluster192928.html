ebonize

verb
make wood or material look like ebony
8.1 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.bə.naɪz/ /; / /ˈe.bə.naɪz/ (AmE), /ˈiː.bə.naɪz/ /; / /ˈe.bə.naɪz/ (BrE)
to stain or treat (wood or another material) so that it looks like ebony; to make black in appearance

Examples

To ebonize wood
The craftsman decided to ebonize the chair legs for a more elegant appearance.
They used a special dye to ebonize the wood, giving it a shiny, dark finish.
To match the existing décor, she asked the carpenter to ebonize the bookshelf.
He is carefully ebonising the picture frame to create a bold contrast with the artwork.
Many 19th-century cabinets were ebonised to mimic the luxurious look of real ebony.
The craftsman decided to ebonise the chair legs to match the rest of the furniture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blacken - to make black or darker, especially by burning or staining
  • synonym: ebonize - alternative spelling, especially in American English
  • antonym: whiten - to make or become white
