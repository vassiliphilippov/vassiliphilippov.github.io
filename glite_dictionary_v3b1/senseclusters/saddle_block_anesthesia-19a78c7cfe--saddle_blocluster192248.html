saddle block anesthesia

noun
regional anesthesia for lower body (saddle area)
Pronunciation: / ˈsæd.əl blɑk ˌæn.əsˈθiʒ.ə /; / ˈsæd.əl blɑk ˌæn.əsˈθi.ʒə / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl blɒk ˌan.əsˈθiː.zɪə /; / ˈsæd.əl blɒk ˌæn.əsˈθiː.zɪə / (BrE)
a regional anesthesia technique in which the areas of the body that would touch a saddle are numbed by injecting a local anesthetic into the spinal cord

Examples

Saddle block anesthesia technique
Saddle block anesthesia effectively numbs the pelvic region while allowing the patient to remain conscious.
The patient received saddle block anesthesia to ensure a pain-free delivery during childbirth.
The surgeon recommended saddle block anesthesia for the minor rectal procedure.
A saddle block anaesthesia is commonly used for procedures involving the lower anus or genital area.
During the operation, the patient remained awake thanks to the saddle block anaesthesia.
The doctor recommended a saddle block anaesthesia before the childbirth to numb the pelvic area.
