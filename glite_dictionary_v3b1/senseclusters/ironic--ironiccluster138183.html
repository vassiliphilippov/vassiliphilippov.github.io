ironic

adjective
showing or involving irony
3.1 4.0
Pronunciation: /aɪ.ˈrɑː.nɪk/ (AmE), /aɪ.ˈrɒ.nɪk/ (BrE)
happening in a way that is different from what is expected, often producing an amusing or poignant result; relating to or characteristic of irony.

Examples

Ironic twist
Her ironic smile made it clear that she doubted his story.
It is ironic that the fire station burned down last night.
The fact that the doctor fell ill on his own birthday was deeply ironic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ironic - using words to express something different from or opposite to the literal meaning
  • antonym: sincere - showing genuine, honest feelings without irony
