whiteness

noun
quality or state of being white in color
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtnəs /; / ˈwaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being white in color or having a light or fair complexion

Examples

The whiteness of snow
After several washes, the shirt lost its original whiteness and became slightly gray.
She admired the whiteness of the paint on the newly renovated walls.
The whiteness of the fresh snow made the landscape look magical.
During the winter, his whiteness made him appear almost ghostly in the dim light.
The whiteness of her skin was accentuated by the dark dress she wore.
The model's whiteness was admired in beauty magazines throughout the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blackness - the quality or state of being black in color
  • degree lower: paleness - the quality or state of being pale in color
  • synonym: palor - an unhealthy pale appearance of the skin
