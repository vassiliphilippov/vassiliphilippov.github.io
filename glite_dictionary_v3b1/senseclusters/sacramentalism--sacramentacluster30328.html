sacramentalism

noun
belief in sacraments' power
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that sacraments or religious rituals are actual means by which divine grace is given or are necessary for salvation

Examples

Christian sacramentalism
The church's teachings are rooted in sacramentalism, emphasizing the importance of rituals like baptism and communion.
Debates about sacramentalism have shaped many Christian traditions throughout history.
