G clef

noun
musical clef marking G above middle C
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒi ˌklɛf / (AmE), / ˈdʒi ˌklɛf / (BrE)
A musical clef that designates the G above middle C on the second line of the staff.

Examples

The G clef symbol
Most beginner piano music in the right hand uses the G clef.
The G clef is also known as the treble clef in many music textbooks.
The violin part is written in the G clef, making it easy for the player to read high notes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: treble clef - a type of G clef commonly used for higher pitched instruments
