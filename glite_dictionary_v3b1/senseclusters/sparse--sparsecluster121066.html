sparse

adjective
thinly scattered; not dense
Pronunciation: / spɑrs / (AmE), / spɑːs / (BrE)
(of things scattered or spread) thinly dispersed or distributed; not dense in arrangement or frequency.

Examples

A sparse crowd
Attendance at the meeting was sparse due to the weather.
Her sparse notes made it hard to prepare for the exam.
The sparse vegetation in the desert offers little shade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dense - closely compacted in substance
  • synonym: scattered - distributed or occurring at widely spaced and irregular intervals
