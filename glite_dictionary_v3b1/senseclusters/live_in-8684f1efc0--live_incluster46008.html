live in

verb
reside at one's workplace, especially at employer's home
Pronunciation: / lɪv ɪn / (AmE), / lɪv ɪn / (BrE)
(of a domestic worker or employee) to reside at the place where one works, especially in the employer's house.

Examples

To live in a house
Many nannies are required to live in with the families they work for.
Since my job is out of town, I have to live in during the week.
The new caretaker will live in to look after the property full-time.
