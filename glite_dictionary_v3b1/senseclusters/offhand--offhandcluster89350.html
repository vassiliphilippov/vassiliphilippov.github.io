offhand

adjective, adverb
casually thoughtless or inconsiderate
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhænd / (BrE)
in a casually inconsiderate or thoughtless way; showing little interest or concern (adverb or adjective)

Examples

Offhand remark
He gave an offhand response that made her feel ignored.
Janet's offhand attitude offended her colleagues during the meeting.
The waiter seemed offhand when taking our order, barely making eye contact.
He dismissed the question offhand, not giving it a second thought.
She made an offhand remark that offended several people in the room.
When asked about the budget, he answered offhand without checking any facts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: careless - failing to give sufficient attention or thought
  • antonym: deliberately - in a careful and intentional manner
  • synonym: offhandedly - in an offhand manner
  • synonym: spontaneously - happening without planning
  • antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration for others
