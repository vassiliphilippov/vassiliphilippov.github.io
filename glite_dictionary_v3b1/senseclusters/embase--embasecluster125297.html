embase

verb
to degrade or abase
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈbeɪs/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈbeɪs/ (BrE)
to lower in rank, status, or moral character; to degrade or abase (archaic or literary)

Examples

To embase a person
The king's enemies sought to embase his reputation through false accusations.
In the old tale, the hero refused to embase himself by betraying his friends.
Writers sometimes embase their characters to show the effects of pride and downfall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abase - to lower in rank, esteem, or dignity
  • synonym: degrade - to treat someone with disrespect or lower their status
  • antonym: exalt - to raise in rank, honor, or status
