portamento

noun
musical sliding technique
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpɔrtəˈmɛntoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtəˈmɛntəʊ / (BrE)
a smooth, continuous glide from one musical note to another, especially in singing or playing an instrument

Examples

Violin portamento
The violinist used portamento to connect the notes in a smooth, expressive way.
Singers often employ portamento in opera to add emotion between phrases.
The score included a portamento marking, instructing the pianist to slide between the notes.
The singer used portamento to smoothly connect the high and low notes in the melody.
A skilled violinist can add emotion to a piece by playing with portamento between notes.
The teacher demonstrated portamento on the piano to show how to glide from one key to another.
The singer used portamento to connect the high and low notes beautifully.
A skilled violinist can perform a smooth portamento between two distant pitches.
In jazz, portamento adds expressiveness to both vocal and instrumental performances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glissando - a rapid slide through a series of consecutive notes
  • antonym: staccato - a musical style with short, detached notes
