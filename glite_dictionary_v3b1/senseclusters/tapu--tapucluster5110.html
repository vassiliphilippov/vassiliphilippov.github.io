tapu

verb
to make sacred or forbidden
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːpuː / (AmE), / ˈtɑːpuː / (BrE)
to declare something as sacred or forbidden, placing it under special restriction according to traditional Polynesian custom

Examples

To tapu a place
The elders decided to tapu the spring until the ceremony ended next month.
It is customary to tapu certain foods during important rituals in the village.
They tapued the forest to protect it from being disturbed by outsiders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit or let something happen
  • synonym: prohibit - to formally forbid something by law or rule
  • synonym: sanctify - to make holy or set apart as sacred
