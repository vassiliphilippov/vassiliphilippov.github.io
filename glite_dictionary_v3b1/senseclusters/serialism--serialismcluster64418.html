serialism

noun
musical technique using ordered notes
3.2 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪr.ɪ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsɪr.ɪ.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈsɪə.rɪ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsɪə.rɪ.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A method of composition in 20th-century music that uses a fixed sequence of notes as the basis for a piece.

Examples

The serialism method
His interest in serialism led him to study the methods of Schoenberg and Webern.
Many composers in the early twentieth century explored serialism as a new approach to structure their works.
The opera was composed using serialism, resulting in a complex and innovative soundscape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atonality - music not based on a key or tonal center, often used with serialism
  • synonym: twelve-tone - a type of serialism specifically using twelve notes in a set order
