alfileria

noun
weed with reddish stems and small purple flowers
3.3
Pronunciation: / æl.fəˈlɪər.i.ə / (AmE), / al.fɪˈlɪə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A European plant (Erodium cicutarium) naturalized in the southwestern United States and Mexico, with reddish, spreading stems, fernlike leaves, small reddish-purple flowers, and slender upright fruits, often grown as forage.

Examples

The alfileria plant
In arid regions, alfileria provides valuable early spring forage for livestock.
Ranchers often plant alfileria to help restore overgrazed pastures.
The slender fruits of alfileria stick upright like tiny needles.

Related Terms
