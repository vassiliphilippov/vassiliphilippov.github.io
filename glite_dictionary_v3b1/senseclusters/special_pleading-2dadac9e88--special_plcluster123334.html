special pleading

noun
argument that disregards opposing evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ˈplidɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈpliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A form of reasoning in which a person ignores or omits unfavorable evidence to support a particular case or argument.

Examples

Political special pleading
A debate filled with special pleading weakens the credibility of any argument.
Accusing others of bias while ignoring your own faults is a classic case of special pleading.
The politician's response was nothing but special pleading, as he avoided addressing the criticism directly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ad hominem - argument attacking an opponent's character instead of answering the argument
  • synonym: rationalization - the action of attempting to justify or explain away controversial behavior or feelings
