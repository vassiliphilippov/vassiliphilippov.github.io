roundabout way

noun
temporary detour or indirect route
2.9 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈraʊn.də.baʊt weɪ/ (AmE), /ˈraʊn.də.baʊt weɪ/ (BrE)
a road or route that avoids an obstacle or blockage by taking a longer, less direct path, often used temporarily while the main route is closed

Examples

A roundabout way home
Emergency vehicles were able to use a roundabout way to get past the blocked road.
The construction forced us to take a roundabout way to reach the highway.
When the bridge collapsed, traffic had to follow a roundabout way through the next town.
