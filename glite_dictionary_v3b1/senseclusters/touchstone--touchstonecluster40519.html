touchstone

noun
standard for comparison or judgment
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌtʃˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a standard or criterion by which something is judged or recognized; a basis for comparison

Examples

The touchstone of quality
His performance became the touchstone by which all future actors were judged.
Honesty is a touchstone for evaluating a person's character.
The Constitution is often used as a touchstone for determining the legality of new laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference for comparison
  • synonym: yardstick - a standard used for comparison
