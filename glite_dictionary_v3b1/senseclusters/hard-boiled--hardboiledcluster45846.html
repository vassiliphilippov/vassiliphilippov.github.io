hard-boiled

adjective
egg cooked until yolk is solid
1.8 1.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːrd.bɔɪld/ (AmE), /ˈhɑːd.bɔɪld/ (BrE)
(of an egg) cooked in boiling water until both the white and yolk are solid.

Examples

Hard-boiled egg
He likes his eggs hard-boiled with a pinch of salt.
She prepared a hard-boiled egg for breakfast.
To make a salad, chop up one hard-boiled egg and mix it in.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft-boiled - (of an egg) cooked so the white is set and the yolk remains soft
