jacket

noun
metal shell casing for ammunition
4.1 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒækət /; / ˈdʒækɪt /; / ˈdʒæk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒækɪt /; / ˈdʒakɪt /; / ˈdʒæk.ɪt / (BrE)
an outer covering or casing that protects or decorates something (such as a bullet, tooth, DVD, or potato skin)

Examples

A bullet jacket
Modern bullets often use a steel jacket to improve performance and reduce barrel wear.
Technicians inspected the jacket for signs of deformation after firing several rounds.
The copper jacket helps prevent lead fouling in the barrel of the rifle.
A porcelain jacket can closely match the color of your natural teeth.
He had a gold jacket fitted after his root canal treatment.
The dentist recommended a jacket to protect the damaged tooth.
Each DVD is packaged in a plastic jacket for protection.
She slipped the book back into its jacket to keep the dust off.
The vinyl album came in a colorful jacket with photographs of the band.
