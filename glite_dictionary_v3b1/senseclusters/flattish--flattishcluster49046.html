flattish

adjective
somewhat flat
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈflætɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflætɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat flat; not completely level but lacking much height or curve

Examples

Flattish landscape
The landscape was flattish, with only a few gentle hills in the distance.
She chose a flattish pillow because she prefers less neck support.
The bread came out flattish after the dough failed to rise properly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: flat - having a level surface without curves or bumps
  • antonym: uneven - not level or smooth
