operator

noun
speculator in financial markets
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːpəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɒpəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who aggressively speculates and trades in stock or commodity markets, often to make short-term profits

Examples

Stock market operator
During volatile market periods, an operator can influence prices with large trades.
Some consider the operator a risk-taker, while others see them as reckless gamblers.
The financial news reported that a well-known operator made millions in a single week.
