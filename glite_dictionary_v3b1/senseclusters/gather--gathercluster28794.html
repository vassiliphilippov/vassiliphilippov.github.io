gather

verb
increase or develop gradually
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə / (BrE)
to increase or accumulate gradually over time

Examples

To gather speed
After a slow start, the campaign started to gather momentum.
As the storm moved inland, it began to gather strength rapidly.
The train gradually gathered speed as it left the station.
As dust gathered in the corners, the room started to look neglected.
Assignments continued to gather, making him feel overwhelmed before exams.
Books and papers gather on my desk if I don't tidy up regularly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accelerate - to begin to move or develop faster
  • synonym: accumulate - to gather or collect, especially over time
  • antonym: diminish - to become less or to decrease in strength, size, or amount
  • synonym: pile up - to become larger by being added together; to accumulate
