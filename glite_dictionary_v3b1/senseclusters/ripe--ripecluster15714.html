ripe

adjective
fully prepared or eager
Pronunciation: / raɪp / (AmE), / raɪp / (BrE)
(of a person, group, mind, situation, or time) fully prepared, mature, or ready for something

Examples

Ripe for change
After months of political turmoil, the public was ripe for reform.
The students were ripe for a new challenge after mastering the material.
With rumors spreading across the company, employees were ripe for a major announcement.
After years of study, his ripe judgment was sought by everyone in the firm.
She has a ripe understanding of complex philosophical ideas.
The professor's ripe mind made his lectures insightful and inspiring.
After months of negotiations, the conditions were finally ripe for an agreement.
The time is ripe for new opportunities in the market.
With public support growing, the moment is ripe for reform.

Related Terms

  • antonym: green - inexperienced or immature (in this context)
  • synonym: mature - fully developed in body or mind
  • synonym: ready - prepared for something to happen or to do something
  • antonym: unripe - not ready or suitable (for an event or purpose)
