family Cinclidae

noun
bird family: dippers or water ouzels
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli sɪŋˈklaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪŋˈklaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, aquatic passerine birds known as dippers or water ouzels, found near fast-flowing streams in the Northern Hemisphere.

Examples

family Cinclidae birds
Researchers observed members of the family Cinclidae diving underwater in search of insects.
The family Cinclidae includes birds that are specially adapted to life in and around fast-flowing streams.
Unlike most passerines, the family Cinclidae displays remarkable swimming and diving abilities.
