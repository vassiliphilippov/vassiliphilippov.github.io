wean

verb
gradually stop feeding mother's milk
4.3 1/m
Pronunciation: / wiÀên / (AmE), / wiÀên / (BrE)
to gradually stop feeding an infant or young animal its mother's milk and introduce other foods, or to accustom (someone) to doing without something they are dependent on

Examples

To wean a baby
After she weaned her child, he started eating solid food.
It's important to wean kittens slowly to ensure their health.
Many mothers choose to wean their babies after six months.
