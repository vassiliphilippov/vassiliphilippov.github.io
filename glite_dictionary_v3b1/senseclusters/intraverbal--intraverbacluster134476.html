intraverbal

adjective
verbal response to speech
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.trə.ˈvɜːr.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.trə.ˈvɜː.bəl/ (BrE)
relating to a verbal response that is prompted by other verbal behavior rather than by objects or events

Examples

Intraverbal response type
In behavioral therapy, an intraverbal exchange might involve answering a question without repeating the exact words.
Teachers use intraverbal prompts to encourage students to respond with related information.
A child giving the correct answer to 'What do cows drink?' is showing an intraverbal skill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: echoic - relating to repeating what someone else has said
  • antonym: tact - a verbal response to a nonverbal stimulus
