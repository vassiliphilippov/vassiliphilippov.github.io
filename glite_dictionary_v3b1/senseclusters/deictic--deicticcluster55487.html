deictic

noun
word specifying identity or location in context
9.9 8.5
Pronunciation: / daɪkˈtɪk / (AmE), / daɪkˈtɪk / (BrE)
A word that specifies identity, place, or time relative to the position or perspective of the speaker or hearer in the context of communication.

Examples

A deictic word
In the sentence 'I am leaving now,' the word 'now' functions as a deictic.
Linguists study how deictics shape the meaning of utterances depending on context.
Words like 'here', 'that', and 'now' are classic examples of deictics in English.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonstrative - a word like 'this' or 'that' used to indicate specific things
  • synonym: pronoun - a word that can replace a noun and whose meaning varies depending on context
