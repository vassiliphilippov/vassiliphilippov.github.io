coordinate

verb
organize parts to work together
1.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɔːrdəˌneɪt /; / ˈkoʊ.ɔr.dənˌeɪt /; / ˈkoʊ.ˌɔr.də.neɪt /; / koʊˈɔrdəˌneɪt /; / koʊˈɔr.dən.eɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪt /; / kəʊˈɔː.dɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to organize people, activities, or things so that they work together well or match each other

Examples

To coordinate a meeting
Can you coordinate your schedule with mine so we can meet next week?
She was hired to coordinate the company's international projects.
The event manager will coordinate the catering, music, and decorations for the party.
She was hired to coordinate the different teams involved in the project.
The school principal asked him to coordinate the annual science fair.
They need someone to coordinate all the travel arrangements for the conference.
Sometimes, the colors and patterns simply do not coordinate well.
The team's efforts coordinate to produce impressive results.
These two projects coordinate perfectly to achieve the desired outcome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collaborate - to work together with others to achieve something
  • antonym: disconnect - to separate or break the connection between
  • antonym: disorganize - to destroy the organization or order of; make disordered
  • synonym: integrate - to combine parts to make a whole
  • synonym: organize - to arrange systematically; to order
