crooked-stemmed aster

noun
North American aster species with crooked stems
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʊkɪd stɛmd ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪd stɛmd ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
A North American wildflower species (Symphyotrichum prenanthoides) of the aster family, characterized by its crooked or angled stems and small, daisy-like flowers.

Examples

The crooked-stemmed aster flower
Gardeners appreciate the resilience of the crooked-stemmed aster in shaded gardens.
The crooked-stemmed aster blooms late in the summer along stream banks and in moist woodlands.
We spotted a cluster of crooked-stemmed asters during our hike through the wetlands.
