eyewitness

noun
person who has seen an event firsthand
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.wɪt.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.wɪt.nəs/ (BrE)
a person who has personally seen an event, especially a crime or accident, and can describe what happened

Examples

An eyewitness to a crime
As an eyewitness, she gave a detailed account of the robbery in court.
The journalist spoke to an eyewitness who saw the fire break out.
The police interviewed an eyewitness to confirm the details of the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: witness - a person who sees an event and may testify about it
