equity

noun
value of property minus what is owed
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkwəti / (AmE), / ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the value or ownership you have in something, such as property or a company, after debts are subtracted

Examples

Home equity
After years of mortgage payments, their equity in the house had grown significantly.
She used the equity in her home to secure a loan for her business.
The bank calculates the equity by subtracting the remaining mortgage from the property's market value.
Investors were eager to increase their equity in the growing tech company.
She owns 40% equity in her family business.
The startup raised funds by selling equity to several venture capitalists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asset - something valuable owned, especially property
  • antonym: debt - money owed by an entity, contrasted with ownership interest
  • synonym: stock - shares representing ownership in a company
