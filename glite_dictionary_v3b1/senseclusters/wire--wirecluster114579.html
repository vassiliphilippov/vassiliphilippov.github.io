wire

noun
metal conductor for electricity
3.8 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪ.ər/ /; / /waɪr/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a thin, flexible strand or rod of metal, used for carrying electricity, fastening things, or making cages and fences

Examples

Electric wire
The electrician replaced the damaged wire to restore power to the house.

You should never touch a live wire with your bare hands.
A bird perched carefully on the topmost wire of the cage.
She twisted a length of wire to fasten the two pieces of wood together.
The garden fence was reinforced with sturdy wire to keep the animals out.
