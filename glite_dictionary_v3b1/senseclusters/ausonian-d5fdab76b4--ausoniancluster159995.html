Ausonian

noun
ancient Italic person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ɔːˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
a person from ancient Italy, especially a member of the Ausones or, poetically, any native or inhabitant of Italy

Examples

An Ausonian tribe
The museum displayed artifacts created by an Ausonian community from the Bronze Age.
Legends about the Ausonian people have been passed down through generations in southern Italy.
Historians study the language and culture of the Ausonian civilization to learn about early Italic societies.
In the epic poem, the hero was described as an Ausonian proud of his heritage.
The ancient ruins were once home to many Ausonians in southern Italy.
Literary works sometimes use Ausonian to evoke the classical spirit of Italy.
