mogul sauce

noun
a spiced sauce like Worcestershire, used with meat
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊɡəl sɔs / (AmE), / ˈməʊɡl sɔːs / (BrE)
a dark, spiced sauce similar to Worcestershire sauce, formerly manufactured by John Burgess & Son and Crosse & Blackwell, used to accompany meat dishes

Examples

Burgess's mogul sauce
Burgess’s mogul sauce was once a staple of 19th-century English dining tables.
Old British cookbooks often recommend mogul sauce as a flavorful addition to roast beef.
The chef recreated a Victorian recipe using mogul sauce to evoke traditional flavors.
