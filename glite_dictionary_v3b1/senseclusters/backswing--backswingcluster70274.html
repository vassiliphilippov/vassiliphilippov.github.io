backswing

noun
pre-strike movement
4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.swɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.swɪŋ/ (BrE)
the backward movement of a club, racket, limb, or similar object before swinging or striking, especially in sports

Examples

Golf backswing
Her golf backswing was smooth and controlled, helping her hit the ball accurately.
The coach advised him to slow down his backswing for better power in his serve.
Improving your backswing can make a big difference in both tennis and baseball.
The golfer's backswing was slow and controlled before he hit the ball.
She paused at the top of her backswing to focus before throwing the ball.
A smooth backswing helps generate more power when using a hammer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follow-through - the forward motion after hitting the ball
  • synonym: swing - a movement back and forth or from side to side
