Non-Ugric

noun
branch of Finno-Ugric languages excluding Hungarian
Pronunciation: / nɑn ˈjuːɡrɪk / (AmE), / nɒn ˈjuːɡrɪk / (BrE)
referring to the branch of the Finno-Ugric languages that includes Finnish and Estonian but not Hungarian

Examples

Non-Ugric languages
Finnish and Estonian are classified as Non-Ugric languages within the Finno-Ugric family.
Linguists debate whether Sami belongs with the Non-Ugric group.
Unlike Hungarian, the Non-Ugric languages retain certain archaic features.
