Ochroma lagopus

noun
Central American tree with light wood
Pronunciation: / oʊˈkroʊmə ləˈɡoʊpəs / (AmE), / əʊˈkrəʊmə ləˈɡəʊpəs / (BrE)
A forest tree native to lowland Central America with very light, strong wood used for making floats, rafts, and crafts.

Examples

Ochroma lagopus tree
Ochroma lagopus is cultivated in plantations for commercial balsa wood production.
Rafts constructed from Ochroma lagopus wood were used by indigenous peoples for centuries.
The wood of the Ochroma lagopus is prized for its use in model-making due to its lightness.
