homostylous

adjective
having styles of equal length (botany)
8.0
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑː.stɪl.əs / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒs.tɪl.əs / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) having all the styles of the same length.

Examples

Homostylous flower species
A homostylous species promotes self-pollination due to the uniformity of style length.
Botanists identified the plant as homostylous after examining its floral structures.
Primroses are sometimes homostylous, with all flowers showing styles of equal length.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterostylous - (of plants) having styles of differing lengths
