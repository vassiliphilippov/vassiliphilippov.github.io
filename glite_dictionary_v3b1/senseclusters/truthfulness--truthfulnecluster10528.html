truthfulness

noun
factual accuracy of AI outputs
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtruːθ.fəl.nəs/ /; / /ˈtruːθ.fʊl.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈtruːθ.fəl.nəs/ /; / /ˈtruːθ.fʊl.nəs/ (BrE)
(in computing and artificial intelligence) the degree to which information produced by a model or system is factually accurate and not misleading or fabricated.

Examples

Truthfulness of information
The study measured the truthfulness of the chatbot's answers against a verified fact database.
Researchers reward improved model truthfulness during training to reduce harmful misinformation in deployments.
When evaluating systems, they separate style and coherence from the model's factual truthfulness.
