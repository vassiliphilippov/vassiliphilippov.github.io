whoof

verb
to expel air suddenly
9.9
Pronunciation: / wuːf / (AmE), / wuːf / (BrE)
to expel air or breathe out suddenly, producing a puffing or coughing noise

Examples

He whoofed out air
He whoofed out a breath after running up the stairs.
The dog whoofed as it chased the ball across the yard.
She tried to whoof the smoke away after the candle tipped over in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cough - to force air out of the lungs suddenly with a sharp sound
  • synonym: huff - to breathe out loudly, especially in annoyance or effort
