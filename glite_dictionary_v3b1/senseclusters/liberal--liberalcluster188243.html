liberal

noun
supporter of laissez-faire economics
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbərəl, ˈlɪbrəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəl, ˈlɪbrəl / (BrE)
A person who supports an economic theory advocating laissez-faire and self-regulating markets, emphasizing minimal government intervention.

Examples

A liberal economist
As a committed liberal, he argued against government interference in the free market.
Some economists identify as liberals, supporting policies that allow businesses to self-regulate.
The liberal members of the committee advocated deregulation and open competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimum governmental interference in the economic affairs of individuals and society
