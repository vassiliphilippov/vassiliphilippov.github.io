hot

adjective
frequently accessed data (IT)
4.9 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / h51t / (AmE), / h52t / (BrE)
(in information technology) Frequently accessed or actively used data or content that is prioritized for fast storage, caching, or immediate processing.

Examples

Hot data
The database caches hot data to speed up queries during peak hours.
Sysadmins keep transaction logs on SSDs because hot files need much faster access than archived records.
To reduce costs, the company moved cold backups but kept frequently used, hot content on high-performance storage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: active - currently in use or frequently accessed by users or systems.
  • antonym: cold - seldom accessed data stored on slower, cheaper storage as a cost-saving measure.
  • degree lower: warm - data accessed occasionally; a middle tier between hot and cold storage.
