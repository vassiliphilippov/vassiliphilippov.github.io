meatiness

noun
amount of meat
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːtinəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːtinəs / (BrE)
the quality or amount of edible meat present in an animal carcass, cut, or fish

Examples

Meatiness of the steak
The chef preferred cuts with more meatiness for his signature steak dish.
Farmers aim to improve the meatiness of their livestock through selective breeding.
The meatiness of the fish made it ideal for grilling at the barbecue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fleshiness - the quality of having a lot of flesh or meat
  • antonym: leanness - the quality of having little fat or meat
