suffocating

adjective
making it hard to breathe (heat or lack of air)
5.1 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒfÉ™ËŒkeÉªtÉªÅ‹ / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒfÉ™keÉªtÉªÅ‹ / (BrE)
(of a place or atmosphere) making it difficult to breathe, especially because of lack of fresh air, heat, or being tightly enclosed.

Examples

A suffocating room
She opened a window to let in fresh air and break the suffocating stillness.
The suffocating atmosphere in the crowded subway made me feel dizzy.
We had to step outside to escape the suffocating heat inside the room.

Synonyms

  • choking
    5.0 0.01/m
    choking makes breathing hard because of dust, smoke, or heat, while suffocating describes a place that blocks fresh air.
  • stifling
    5.3 0.43/m
    stifling feels very hot and oppressive and makes breathing difficult, while suffocating emphasizes lack of fresh air or tight space.
  • smothering
    6.7 0.02/m
    smothering is heavy heat or air that seems to press on you, while suffocating is about poor airflow or tight enclosure.
  • stuffy
    2.7 0.73/m
    stuffy describes a room that feels old and unpleasant to breathe in, while suffocating implies stronger lack of fresh air.
  • close
    3.4 1.98/m
    close means the air is warm and without flow, making people feel uncomfortable, while suffocating hides extra danger from heat or tight space.
  • unbreathable
    7.1 0.01/m
    unbreathable means air cannot pass and makes a place unusable, while suffocating describes conditions that make breathing hard.
  • unventilated
    6.3 0.07/m
    unventilated is a space with no air circulation or vents, while suffocating focuses on the result of hard breathing or tightness.
  • airless
    4.7 0.24/m
    airless means almost no air is present and breathing may be impossible, while suffocating usually means poor airflow or oppressive heat.
  • asphyxiating
    9.6 0.01/m
    asphyxiating describes a condition that can cause severe oxygen loss, while suffocating is used for places that make breathing difficult.
