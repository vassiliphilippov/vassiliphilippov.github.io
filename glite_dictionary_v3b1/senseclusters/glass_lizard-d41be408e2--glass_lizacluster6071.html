glass lizard

noun
a legless lizard with a fragile tail
9.9 7.3
Pronunciation: /glæs ˈlɪz.ərd/ /; / /gɑːs ˈlɪz.ərd/ (AmE), /gɑːs ˈlɪz.əd/ /; / /glæs ˈlɪz.əd/ (BrE)
a slender, legless lizard of Europe, Asia, and North America, with fragile tail and vestigial limbs, able to break and regenerate its tail easily

Examples

A glass lizard tail
If a predator grabs its tail, a glass lizard can break it off and escape.
The glass lizard is often mistaken for a snake because it has no legs.
Unlike snakes, the glass lizard has movable eyelids and external ear openings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legless lizard - a lizard without visible legs but not a snake
