flamboyantly

adverb
in a strikingly elaborate or showy way
6.9
Pronunciation: / flæmˈbɔɪəntli / (AmE), / flamˈbɔɪəntli / (BrE)
in a way that is very noticeable, confident, and deliberately attracts attention, often using bright colors or elaborate style

Examples

To flamboyantly dress
He waved his cape flamboyantly before the audience during the performance.
She entered the room flamboyantly, drawing everyone's attention with her bright red dress.
The artist flamboyantly signed her paintings with a bold flourish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modestly - in a quiet or unassuming way
  • synonym: ostentatiously - in a showy or pretentious way
