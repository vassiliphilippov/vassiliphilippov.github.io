leave

verb
forget to take something when departing
1.0 177/m
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liÀêv / (BrE)
to abandon or leave someone or something behind, either intentionally or by forgetting

Examples

To leave a bag behind
Don't leave your phone in the taxi by mistake.
He often leaves his glasses on the kitchen table and can't find them later.
She realized too late that she had left her passport at home.
After twenty years together, he finally left her, and she was heartbroken.
Don't forget to feed the fish if you leave town for a few days.
She was upset when her friends left her at the party without telling her.

Synonyms

  • leave behind
    2.6 20/m
    leave behind means going away without taking someone or something with you, while leave covers both leaving intentionally or by forgetting.
  • abandon
    3.1 36/m
    abandon means leaving someone or something permanently and often shows neglect, while leave can be temporary or happen by accident.
  • forsake
    6.9 1.92/m
    forsake means to abandon someone in their time of need, while leave is a more general word and can include forgetting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something behind, especially permanently or irresponsibly
  • antonym: bring - to take or carry someone or something to a place or person
  • synonym: forget - to fail to remember to bring something
