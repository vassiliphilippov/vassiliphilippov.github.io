monomorphic

adjective
single form or shape
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
having only one form, shape, or appearance within a population or group

Examples

Monomorphic butterfly population
The butterfly population is monomorphic, so all individuals have the same wing pattern.
Unlike many other species, the fish remain monomorphic throughout their lives.
In this plant species, flowers are monomorphic and do not vary in color or shape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polymorphic - having multiple forms or appearances within a population
  • synonym: uniform - remaining the same in all cases and at all times
