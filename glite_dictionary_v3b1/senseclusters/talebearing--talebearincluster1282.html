talebearing

adjective
prone to reveal secrets; gossiping
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪlˌbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌbɛə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
prone to reveal secrets or confidential information; given to gossiping or telling tales.

Examples

A talebearing person
Her talebearing led to mistrust among her colleagues.
People tired of his constant talebearing and refused to share secrets with him.
The group warned against talebearing because it damaged friendships.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gossiping - talking about the private affairs of others
  • synonym: slander - making false and damaging statements about someone
