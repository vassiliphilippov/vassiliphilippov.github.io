sunscald

noun
skin injury from sunlight
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌnˌskɔld / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌskɔːld / (BrE)
an injury to the skin of a person or animal caused by excessive exposure to sunlight, often resulting in redness or damage

Examples

Painful sunscald
The farmer noticed sunscald on the cow's ears after several days of intense sunlight.
Children should wear hats to prevent sunscald when playing outside in the summer.
Veterinarians often treat cases of sunscald in livestock during heat waves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frostbite - injury to skin caused by extreme cold
  • synonym: sunburn - redness and pain of the skin caused by too much sun
