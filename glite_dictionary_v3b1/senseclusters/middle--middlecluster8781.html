middle

noun
central point or period of something
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl / (BrE)
the central part or point of something, equally distant from the beginning and end (in time, space, or process)

Examples

The middle of the road
Heavy rain fell during the middle of April, causing floods in some areas.
She got bored in the middle of the movie and left the cinema early.
We reached the middle of the conference just as the keynote speaker began.
She was sitting right in the middle of the row.
The story became most exciting in the middle of the novel.
There's a fountain in the middle of the park.
She placed the vase right in the middle of the table for everyone to see.
The playground is located in the middle of the park, surrounded by trees.
We found ourselves in the middle of a heated debate about the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beginning - the starting point of a period or process
  • synonym: center - the point that is equally distant from every side or surface
  • antonym: edge - the boundary or furthest point of something
  • antonym: end - the final point of a period or process
