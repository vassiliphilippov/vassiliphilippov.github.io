specific weight

noun
weight per unit volume
2.4
Pronunciation: / spəˈsɪfɪk weɪt / (AmE), / spəˈsɪfɪk weɪt / (BrE)
the weight per unit volume of a material, typically measured in newtons per cubic meter.

Examples

Specific weight of material
Air has a lower specific weight than most liquids.
Engineers must consider the specific weight of materials when designing structures.
The specific weight of water changes slightly with temperature and pressure.
