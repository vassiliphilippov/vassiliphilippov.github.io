cavern

noun
large, dark enclosed space (literal or figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈkævərn / (AmE), / ˈkævən / (BrE)
a large cave or a large, dark, enclosed space (can also be used metaphorically for something vast and gloomy)

Examples

A dark cavern
Her loneliness echoed in the cavern of her empty apartment.
The abandoned warehouse was a cavern of darkness and dust.
When the lights went out, the room felt like a cavern filled with shadows.
Stalactites hung from the ceiling of the vast cavern.
The guide led us through the winding tunnels into a massive cavern filled with crystals.
They explored the dark cavern hidden beneath the mountains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cave - a natural underground space, typically larger than a grotto
