kilted

verb
to gather into pleats
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɪltɪd / (BrE)
to tuck up, hitch, or gather a skirt or garment into pleats so that it resembles a kilt.

Examples

Kilted skirt
She waded across the stream with her skirt kilted to keep it from getting wet.
The children kilted their trousers before splashing in the shallow water.
He kilted his robe to climb the steep hillside more easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pleat - to fold fabric into even, narrow folds
  • antonym: untuck - to release something that has been tucked up
