surge

verb
move forward/upward suddenly and powerfully
5.3 3.3
Pronunciation: /sɜːrdʒ/ (AmE), /sɜːdʒ/ (BrE)
to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward, like a wave, crowd, or emotion

Examples

Crowds surge forward
Excitement surged through the stadium when the team scored the winning goal.
High waves surged against the rocky shoreline during the storm.
The crowd began to surge forward as soon as the gates opened.
Excitement surged through the stadium as the team scored the winning goal.
The crowd began to surge toward the exit once the doors opened.
Waves surged against the rocks during the storm, making the harbor dangerous.
As the wind picked up, the sails caused the yacht to surge forward through the water.
During the storm, the tide would surge unexpectedly, catching swimmers off guard.
The boat began to surge as the strong waves hit the shore.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ebb - move away or recede (usually of the tide), opposite of surge
  • synonym: heave - to rise or lift, especially with effort or force
  • antonym: plunge - to fall or move downward or suddenly decrease
  • synonym: soar - to increase rapidly above the usual level
