willful

adjective
habitually disobedient or stubborn
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪl.fəl /; / ˈwɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪl.fəl /; / ˈwɪlfʊl / (BrE)
deliberately doing something, especially in a way that is disobedient or in defiance of rules or authority; often used for a person or their actions that are stubborn or habitually resistant to authority.

Examples

A willful child
Despite repeated warnings, he remained willful in his opposition to authority.
Her willful disregard for the rules often got her into trouble at school.
The willful child refused to follow any instructions, no matter how much his parents pleaded.
Her wilful disregard for the rules led to her suspension from school.
His wilful decision to ignore the warning put everyone at risk.
The court found him guilty of wilful damage to property.
He was dismissed for his wilful disregard of company policies.
Her wilful refusal to follow the rules frustrated her teachers.
The child's wilful behavior often led to conflict at home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accidental - happening by chance, not planned
  • antonym: compliant - willing to comply or obey
  • synonym: headstrong - energetic and determined to do as one pleases, even if advised otherwise
  • synonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • synonym: willful - Deliberately disobedient or stubborn (American English spelling)
