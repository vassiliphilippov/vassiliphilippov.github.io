(of workers) to stop working as a form of protest, usually in order to obtain better working conditions, pay, or other demands.

Examples

To go on strike over pay
Hundreds of workers decided to go on strike over low wages and long hours.
The teachers threatened to go on strike if their demands were not met.
The union voted unanimously to go on strike next week.

Synonyms

  • strike
    3.2 7.23/m
    strike is the basic verb for stopping work in protest, while go on strike is the common phrasal way workers say it.
  • strike work
    5.7 0.00/m
    strike work is a verb phrase used to describe stopping work for protest, while go on strike is more common in everyday speech.
  • down tools
    8.1 0.02/m
    down tools means workers stop working, often as an informal or sudden protest action, while go on strike is the usual formal phrase.
  • walk out
    4.1 0.41/m
    walk out means employees leave their workplace to protest or pressure, while go on strike emphasizes stopping work as an organized protest.
