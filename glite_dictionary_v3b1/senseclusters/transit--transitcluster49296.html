transit

verb
enable to pass through
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /træn.ˈzɪt/ /; / /træn.ˈsɪt/ (AmE), /træn.ˈzɪt/ /; / /træn.ˈsɪt/ /; / /trɑːn.ˈzɪt/ (BrE)
to pass or travel through or across a place, or to cause something to do so

Examples

To transit goods
After customs clearance, the goods were transited through the port to their final destination.
Energy resources are often transited through pipelines that cross several countries.
The new ferry system will transit passengers across the bay every morning.
He will transit several countries during his journey to Asia.
Millions of goods are transited through this port each year.
The ships transit the canal every day on their way to different continents.
