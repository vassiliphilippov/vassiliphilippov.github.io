lesser of two evils

noun
the less harmful of two bad options
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛsɚ əv tu ˈivəlz / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə əv tuː ˈiːvɪlz / (BrE)
the less harmful or undesirable of two unpleasant choices

Examples

The lesser of two evils
She didn't like either job offer, but she picked the lesser of two evils.
Sometimes you have to settle for the lesser of two evils rather than holding out for something perfect.
When faced with two corrupt candidates, voters felt compelled to choose the lesser of two evils.
