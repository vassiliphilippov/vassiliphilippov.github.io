ration out

verb
distribute in limited shares
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃ.ən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ən aʊt / (BrE)
to distribute something in fixed or limited amounts, especially when resources are scarce

Examples

To ration out food
After the storm, volunteers began rationing out food to residents in need.
During the drought, the officials decided to ration out water to each household.
Supplies were rationed out fairly among the soldiers in camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocate - to distribute resources or duties for a particular purpose
  • antonym: hoard - to accumulate and store away (as the opposite intention of rationing)
