hydrophilic

adjective
having an affinity for water
4.6 5.3
Pronunciation: /haɪ.drə.ˈfɪ.lɪk/ (AmE), /haɪ.drə.ˈfɪ.lɪk/ (BrE)
Having a strong affinity for water; tending to dissolve in, mix with, or be wetted by water.

Examples

Hydrophilic materials
Certain medical implants are coated with hydrophilic materials to improve their compatibility with body tissues.
Soap molecules have a hydrophilic end that binds to water and a hydrophobic end that binds to grease.
The fabric is hydrophilic, allowing sweat to evaporate quickly and keeping the wearer comfortable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrophobic - having a strong aversion to water
