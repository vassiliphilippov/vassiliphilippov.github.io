lather

noun
froth produced by soap or detergent
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːðə / (BrE)
a mass of tiny bubbles formed by soap, detergent, or similar cleaning substances when mixed with water

Examples

Soap lather
She worked the soap into a thick lather before washing her hands.
The barber covered his client's face with a creamy lather before shaving him.
The car gleamed as the sponge created a rich lather on its surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid, often by agitation
  • synonym: scum - a layer of froth or dirt on the surface of a liquid
