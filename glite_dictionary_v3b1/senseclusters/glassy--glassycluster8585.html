glassy

adjective
dull, unfocused, or lifeless eyes
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæsi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːsi / (BrE)
(of eyes) showing no expression or liveliness; dull and unresponsive in appearance, often with a shiny surface.

Examples

Glassy eyes
After hours of studying, his gaze became glassy and unfocused.
Her glassy eyes stared into the distance, betraying no emotion.
The patient’s glassy stare concerned the nurse, who called for the doctor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alert - attentive and responsive in appearance
  • synonym: glazed - (of eyes) having a fixed, shiny, and expressionless appearance
  • synonym: lifeless - without life or energy
