satyr

noun
mythical woodland deity (Greek/Roman)
9.1 9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈseɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈsæ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈsæ.tə/ /; / /ˈseɪ.tə/ (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a member of a class of lustful, drunken woodland gods, often depicted as having a man's upper body and the legs, ears, and tail of a goat, and serving as an attendant to Bacchus (Dionysus).

Examples

Greek satyr god
Ancient myths often describe satyrs as companions to Dionysus, indulging in wine and revelry.
She dressed as a satyr for the masquerade ball, complete with horns and a furry tail.
The painting depicted a mischievous satyr chasing nymphs through the forest.
