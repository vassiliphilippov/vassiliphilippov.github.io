four-o'clock family

noun
family of flowering plants (Nyctaginaceae)
Pronunciation: / fɔr əˈklɑk ˈfæməli / (AmE), / fɔːr əˈklɒk ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants (Nyctaginaceae) in the order Caryophyllales, including herbs, shrubs, and vines such as the four-o'clock flower.

Examples

Members of the four-o'clock family often have brightly colored flowers that open in the afternoon.

The four-o'clock family contains about 30 genera of flowering plants found in tropical and subtropical regions.
