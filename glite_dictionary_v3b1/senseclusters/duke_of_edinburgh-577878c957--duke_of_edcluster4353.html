Duke of Edinburgh

proper noun
British royal title, esp. for Prince Philip
Pronunciation: / duːk əv ˈɛdənˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / djuːk əv ˈɛdɪnbərə / (BrE)
the title held by the British prince who was the husband of Queen Elizabeth II, especially referring to Prince Philip (1921–2021)

Examples

The Duke of Edinburgh title
After the passing of Prince Philip, the Duke of Edinburgh title was intended to pass to Prince Charles.
Prince Philip was made Duke of Edinburgh when he married Princess Elizabeth in 1947.
The title Duke of Edinburgh is associated with many charitable initiatives, including the Duke of Edinburgh's Award.
