mucin

noun
substance in mucus protecting body surfaces
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːsɪn / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsɪn / (BrE)
a nitrogenous substance found in mucous secretions that serves as a lubricant protecting body surfaces

Examples

Mucin in mucus
Mucous membranes in the nose produce mucin to trap dust and microbes.
Researchers studied the role of mucin in maintaining a healthy digestive tract.
Some eye drops contain mucin to help lubricate and protect the surface of the eye.
