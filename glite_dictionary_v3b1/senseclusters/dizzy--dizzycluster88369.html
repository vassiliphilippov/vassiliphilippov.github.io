dizzy

adjective
sensation of spinning or unsteadiness
1.0 11/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÉªzi / (AmE), / ËˆdÉªzi / (BrE)
having a sensation of spinning around and losing one's balance; likely to fall or feel faint

Examples

Feel dizzy after spinning
After spinning around in circles, the children laughed and fell over, dizzy from the game.
Looking down from the tall tower made me feel a little dizzy and uneasy.
She stood up too quickly and suddenly felt dizzy, grabbing the table for support.

Synonyms

  • giddy
    4.9 0.53/m
    giddy feels like a whirling, light-headed rush that makes you unsteady, while dizzy means a spinning loss of balance.
  • woozy
    5.7 1.02/m
    woozy feels weak and unsteady, as if you might faint, while dizzy describes a stronger spinning loss of balance.
  • lightheaded
    4.4 0.52/m
    lightheaded feels weak and near fainting, often without a spin, while dizzy describes a distinct spinning sensation and loss of balance.
