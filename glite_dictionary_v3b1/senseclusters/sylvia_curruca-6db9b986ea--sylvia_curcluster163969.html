Sylvia curruca

noun
species of small Old World warbler
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlviə kəˈruːkə / (AmE), / ˈsɪlviə kʌˈrʊkə / (BrE)
A small Old World warbler, commonly known as the lesser whitethroat, similar to the greater whitethroat but smaller, found across Europe and Asia.

Examples

The Sylvia curruca bird
Birdwatchers in Europe often seek out the elusive Sylvia curruca during migration season.
The Sylvia curruca is easily distinguished by its grey head and white throat.
Unlike its larger cousin, the Sylvia curruca tends to stay hidden in dense shrubs.
