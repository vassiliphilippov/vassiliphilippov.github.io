pass off

verb
disregard or dismiss as unimportant
2.5
Pronunciation: / pæs ɑf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
to treat something, especially criticism or an insult, as unimportant or not worth serious attention

Examples

To pass off criticism
He attempted to pass off their concerns, but they insisted on answers.
It's not wise to pass off criticism without considering it.
She tried to pass off his rude remark as a joke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brush off - to reject or ignore someone or something
  • synonym: dismiss - to decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
  • antonym: pay attention to - to focus one's attention on something
