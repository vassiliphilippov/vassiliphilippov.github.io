spinalis

noun
back muscle near spine
Pronunciation: / spaɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / spaɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
a muscle of the back, part of the erector spinae group, running close to the spine along the vertebral column

Examples

Spinalis muscle pain
The spinalis helps maintain posture by supporting the spine during movement.
Injury to the spinalis can cause back pain and limit flexibility.
Physical therapists often target the spinalis when treating lower back problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erector spinae - a group of muscles running along the spine that help straighten and rotate the back
  • synonym: multifidus - a deep muscle in the back that stabilizes the vertebrae
