crucian

noun
freshwater carp species
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːʃən / (AmE), / ˈkruːʃən / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of the carp family, native to Europe and Asia

Examples

Crucian carp
The lake is known for its healthy population of crucian.
Anglers often try to catch crucian because they are challenging and fun to fish for.
A single crucian can live for many years in a well-maintained pond.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae
  • synonym: goldfish - a small, orange or red freshwater fish related to the carp
