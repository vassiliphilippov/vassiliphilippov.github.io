Kaposi's sarcoma

noun
type of cancer causing purplish skin lesions
7.8
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈpoʊ.siːz sɑːr.ˈkoʊ.mə/ (AmE), /kə.ˈpəʊ.siːz sɑː.ˈkəʊ.mə/ (BrE)
A rare type of cancer characterized by purplish or brownish skin lesions, often associated with AIDS, that can spread to lymph nodes and internal organs.

Examples

Kaposi's sarcoma skin lesions
During the early years of the AIDS epidemic, Kaposi's sarcoma was frequently seen in patients.
She developed Kaposi's sarcoma, which prompted her to seek specialized cancer treatment.
The doctors diagnosed him with Kaposi's sarcoma after noticing the characteristic lesions on his legs.
