self-conscious

adjective
uncomfortably aware of oneself
1.0 3.7
Pronunciation: /self.ˈkɑːn.ʃəs/ /; / /self.ˈkɑːn.tʃəs/ (AmE), /self.ˈkɒn.ʃəs/ /; / /self.ˈkɒn.tʃəs/ (BrE)
nervous or uncomfortable because you are too aware of yourself, especially when you think others are watching or judging you

Examples

To feel self-conscious
He became self-conscious when he realized everyone was watching him.
Many people are self-conscious about speaking in public.
She felt self-conscious wearing the bright red dress to the party.
He became self-conscious when everyone in the room stared at him.
She felt self-conscious about her new hairstyle at the party.
Teenagers are often self-conscious about their appearance.
Adolescents are often self-conscious about their appearance.
He felt self-conscious about his accent when speaking in public.
Mary became self-conscious after noticing everyone was watching her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confident - feeling or showing assurance, especially in oneself
  • synonym: embarrassed - feeling awkward or ashamed, often due to self-awareness
  • synonym: shy - nervous or timid in the company of others
