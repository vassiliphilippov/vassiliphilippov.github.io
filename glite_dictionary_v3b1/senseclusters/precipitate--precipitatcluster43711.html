precipitate

verb
cause to be thrown suddenly or violently
2.7 6.4
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /prə.ˈsɪp.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.tət/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /prə.ˈsɪp.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsɪp.ɪ.tət/ (BrE)
to cause someone or something to be suddenly thrown or sent into a particular state, place, or situation; or to fall suddenly and rapidly into a state or place

Examples

Precipitate into trouble
Her careless remark precipitated him into a fit of anger.
The demolition team precipitated the old building into a heap of rubble within seconds.
The sudden landslide precipitated several cars down the hillside.
After the announcement, the stock market began to precipitate into a sharp decline.
Heavy rainfall caused the boulders to precipitate down the mountainside.
The boat lost its balance and threatened to precipitate everyone into the cold water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catapult - to launch or hurl suddenly and forcefully
  • synonym: hurl - throw something with great force
  • synonym: plunge - to fall or move suddenly downward or forward
  • antonym: restrain - to hold back or prevent from moving forward
