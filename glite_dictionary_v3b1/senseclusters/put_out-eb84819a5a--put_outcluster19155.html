Synonyms

  • put forth
    4.1 2/m
    put forth is used to make an effort or try something, while put out means to exert oneself to get something done.
  • exert
    4.1 2/m
    exert is a formal verb for making a strong physical or mental effort, while put out is a more casual phrasal way to say this.
  • put one's shoulder into
    6.8 0.01/m
    put one's shoulder into means working hard in a focused, physical way, while put out is a general phrase for making effort.
  • put one's shoulder to the wheel
    7.5 0.09/m
    put one's shoulder to the wheel suggests starting steady, sustained hard work, while put out only means making a significant effort.
  • go all out
    4.1 2/m
    go all out means trying with maximum energy and often urgency, while put out means making a strong but less extreme effort.
