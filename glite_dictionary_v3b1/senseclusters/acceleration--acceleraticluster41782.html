acceleration

noun
increase in speed or rate of change
Pronunciation: / ækˌsɛləˈreɪʃən /; / əkˌsɛləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əkˌsɛləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an increase in the speed or rate at which something happens or changes; the process of becoming faster

Examples

Rapid acceleration of growth
Improved training techniques led to an acceleration of athletes' progress.
The acceleration of technological innovation has transformed many industries.
There has been a noticeable acceleration in the growth of urban populations.
Improved acceleration is a key feature of the new electric vehicle.
She felt the sudden acceleration as the roller coaster descended the hill.
The car's acceleration made it ideal for highway driving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deceleration - a decrease in the speed or rate at which something happens or changes
  • degree lower: slowing - the act of becoming slower
  • degree higher: speed - the rate at which something moves or happens
