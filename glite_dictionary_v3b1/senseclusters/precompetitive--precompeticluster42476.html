precompetitive

adjective
before commercial competition
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkəmˈpɛtətɪv / (AmE), / ˌpriːkəmˈpɛtɪtɪv / (BrE)
relating to activities or research conducted jointly by organizations before direct commercial competition begins, often to share basic knowledge.

Examples

Precompetitive research project
Several companies joined a precompetitive alliance to develop new safety standards for the industry.
The research was conducted in a precompetitive phase, allowing all participants to benefit from the findings.
Universities and businesses often collaborate on precompetitive projects before launching commercial products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collaborative - involving two or more parties working together
  • antonym: competitive - relating to rivalry between organizations or individuals
