recordable

adjective
eligible for official record
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔrdəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being officially entered into a public record or registry, especially for legal purposes

Examples

Recordable contract
The contract was not recordable until all parties had signed it.
Only recordable documents can be filed with the county clerk's office.
The judge determined that the agreement was not recordable under state law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: registrable - able to be officially registered or recorded
  • antonym: unrecordable - not able to be officially entered into a record or registry
