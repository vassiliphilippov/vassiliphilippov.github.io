truthful

adjective
always telling the truth; honest
Pronunciation: / ˈtruθfəl /; / ˈtruθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtruːθf(ə)l /; / ˈtruːθ.fəl / (BrE)
always telling the truth or expressing what is true; honest and not lying

Examples

A truthful answer
She is known to be truthful even when it is difficult.
The witness gave a truthful account of the events.
We need leaders who are open and truthful with the public.
His truthful nature made him a trusted friend.
It is important to be truthful when answering questions on official forms.
She gave a truthful account of what happened during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dishonest - not honest; intending to mislead or cheat
  • synonym: honest - telling the truth and not lying, cheating, or stealing
