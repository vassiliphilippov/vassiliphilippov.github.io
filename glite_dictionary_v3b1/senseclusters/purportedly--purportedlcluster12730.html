purportedly

adverb
allegedly; as claimed
7.2 2.08/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàp…ùÀêt…™dli / (AmE), / p…ôÀàp…îÀêt…™dli / (BrE)
as is claimed or alleged, but not necessarily proven to be true

Examples

He was purportedly seen
He was purportedly seen at the scene of the crime, though no evidence supports this claim.
The new medicine is purportedly effective, but more research is needed to confirm its benefits.
The policy was introduced to improve safety, purportedly in response to recent accidents.

Synonyms

  • allegedly
    5.4 16/m
    allegedly is used when someone is officially accused or reported without proof, while purportedly is more neutral and general.
  • supposedly
    3.4 24/m
    supposedly is a casual word for what people believe or say, while purportedly sounds more formal and uncertain.
  • putatively
    8.1 0.39/m
    putatively appears in formal or academic texts to mean commonly assumed, while purportedly is neutral in normal reporting.
  • ostensibly
    7.5 5.84/m
    ostensibly describes how something looks or is presented, while purportedly simply reports a claim without implying appearance.
  • reportedly
    3.7 16/m
    reportedly says information comes from reports or sources, while purportedly focuses on the claim's truth regardless of source.
  • reputedly
    6.1 0.65/m
    reputedly refers to what people say by reputation over time, while purportedly refers to a claimed fact now.
  • assertedly
    7.9 0.01/m
    assertedly presents something as a firm claim often without evidence, while purportedly just reports that something is claimed.
