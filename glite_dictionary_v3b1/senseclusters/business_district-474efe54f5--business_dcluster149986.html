business district

noun
central commercial area of a city
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪznəs ˌdɪstrɪkt / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪs ˌdɪstrɪkt / (BrE)
The central area in a town or city where most business and commercial activities take place.

Examples

The business district center
Most of the city's banks and corporate offices are located in the business district.
The downtown business district becomes crowded during rush hour as workers commute home.
Tourists usually stay in hotels near the business district for easy access to shopping and dining.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downtown - the main business part of a city
  • antonym: suburb - an outlying residential district of a city
