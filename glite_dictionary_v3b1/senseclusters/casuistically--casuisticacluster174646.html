casuistically

adverb
in a case-based way
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæʒuˈɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌkæzjuˈɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that applies general moral principles to specific cases, especially to resolve ethical problems

Examples

Casuistically solve cases
The lawyer argued casuistically to find a solution that fit the unique circumstances of the case.
She approached the ethical dilemma casuistically, considering how general rules applied to the situation.
During the discussion, he responded casuistically rather than relying on strict rules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analytically - in a way that involves careful examination of details
  • antonym: dogmatically - in a way that follows strict principles without considering specific cases
