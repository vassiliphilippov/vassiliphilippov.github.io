unselective

adjective
not selective; indiscriminate
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.səˈlɛk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.sɪˈlɛk.tɪv / (BrE)
not choosing carefully or not showing preference; indiscriminate.

Examples

Unselective appetite
He had an unselective appetite for books, reading whatever he could find.
Her unselective taste in music meant she listened to just about everything on the radio.
The drug is unselective and affects both healthy and diseased cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indiscriminate - not making careful choices; lacking in selectivity
  • antonym: selective - carefully choosing or discriminating among options
