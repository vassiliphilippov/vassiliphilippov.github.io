cartridge ejector

noun
firearm mechanism that ejects empty shell
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑr.trɪdʒ ɪˈdʒɛk.tər / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ ɪˈdʒɛk.tə / (BrE)
a mechanism in a firearm that automatically expels the empty cartridge case after a round is fired

Examples

The cartridge ejector mechanism
He noticed a problem with the cartridge ejector when his rifle failed to expel the casing.
Modern firearms are designed with an improved cartridge ejector for reliability.
The cartridge ejector worked flawlessly, clearing the spent shell after each shot.
