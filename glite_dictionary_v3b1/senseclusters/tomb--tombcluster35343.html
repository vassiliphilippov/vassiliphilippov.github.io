tomb

noun
burial chamber or grave
1.0
Pronunciation: / tuːm / (AmE), / tuːm / (BrE)
a large stone structure or underground chamber used for the burial of a dead person, often marked with an inscription or monument

Examples

Ancient tomb
Archaeologists discovered artifacts inside a sealed tomb dating back thousands of years.
The ancient pharaoh was laid to rest in a grand tomb beneath the desert sands.
Tourists visited the historic tomb to pay their respects to the legendary leader.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grave - a place in the ground where a dead person is buried
  • synonym: mausoleum - a large, stately tomb or building housing tombs
