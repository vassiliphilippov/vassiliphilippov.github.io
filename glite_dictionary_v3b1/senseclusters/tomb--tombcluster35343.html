tomb

noun
burial chamber or grave
3.0 11/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀêm / (AmE), / tuÀêm / (BrE)
a large stone structure or underground chamber used for the burial of a dead person, often marked with an inscription or monument

Examples

Ancient tomb
Archaeologists discovered artifacts inside a sealed tomb dating back thousands of years.
The ancient pharaoh was laid to rest in a grand tomb beneath the desert sands.
Tourists visited the historic tomb to pay their respects to the legendary leader.

Synonyms

  • sepulcher
    9.9 0.29/m
    a sepulcher is a small stone burial room for one person, while a tomb is often larger and marks the dead publicly.
  • burial chamber
    6.3 0.12/m
    a burial chamber is an enclosed underground space for placing a body, while a tomb often also has a visible stone monument or inscription.
  • burial vault
    7.9 0.07/m
    a burial vault is an underground chamber built to protect coffins, while a tomb often serves as a larger memorial stone structure.
  • vault
    6.9 0.89/m
    a vault is an underground space for several burials, while a tomb is often a single, named stone monument for the dead.
  • crypt
    7.6 2.54/m
    a crypt is an underground burial room beneath a church, while a tomb is a general stone burial place often marked with inscriptions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mausoleum - a large, stately tomb or building housing tombs
