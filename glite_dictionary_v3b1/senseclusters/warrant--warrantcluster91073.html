warrant

verb
justify or make necessary
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
to provide adequate grounds to justify or make something necessary or appropriate

Examples

Warrants further action
His recent behavior hardly warrants their trust.
Nothing could warrant that level of excitement.
The evidence does not warrant such extreme measures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excuse - to make allowances for or forgive a fault
  • synonym: justify - to show to be right or reasonable
  • antonym: prohibit - to formally forbid by law, rule, or other authority
