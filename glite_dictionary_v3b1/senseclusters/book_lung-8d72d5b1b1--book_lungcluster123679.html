book lung

noun
respiratory organ in arachnids
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /bʊk lʌŋ/ (AmE), /bʊk lʌŋ/ (BrE)
an organ found in many arachnids, consisting of thin, leaf-like folds of membrane for breathing

Examples

Book lung in arachnids
Scorpions possess a book lung that helps them absorb oxygen from the air.
Spiders breathe through a specialized organ called a book lung located in their abdomen.
The thin folds inside the book lung increase the surface area for gas exchange.
