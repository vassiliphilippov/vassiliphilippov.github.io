doubtful

adjective
open to doubt or suspicion
3.0 3.96/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdaÊŠtfÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆdaÊŠtfÊŠl / (BrE)
Open to doubt or suspicion; considered unlikely or uncertain to be true or genuine.

Examples

Doubtful story
His explanation for returning home so late sounded doubtful to his parents.
Many investors are doubtful about the accuracy of the company's financial statements.
The authenticity of the painting remains doubtful despite further investigation.

Synonyms

  • uncertain
    2.8 14/m
    uncertain is not clearly decided or fixed, while doubtful suggests something people question or think may be untrue.
  • in question
    5.8 13/m
    in question is seen as doubted or under review by others, while doubtful suggests people suspect it is not true.
  • dubious
    6.5 8.03/m
    dubious often feels suspicious or untrustworthy, while doubtful means something may be untrue or not clearly proved.
  • questionable
    3.9 12/m
    questionable often suggests poor quality or possible dishonesty, while doubtful focuses on being uncertain or unlikely to be true.
  • unlikely
    1.9 40/m
    unlikely means having little chance of happening, while doubtful often adds a feeling that something is wrong or not genuine.
  • implausible
    7.0 2.34/m
    implausible feels hard to believe because it seems unreasonable, while doubtful may only mean it is unsure or possibly false.
  • dubitable
    9.9 0.00/m
    dubitable is formal and rare for things open to doubt, while doubtful is common and used in everyday speech about uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certain - free from doubt or confusion
  • synonym: dubious - hesitating or doubting; not to be relied upon
