logwood tree

noun
dye-yielding tree of Central America and West Indies
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔɡˌwʊd tri / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌwʊd triː / (BrE)
a spiny shrub or small tree native to Central America and the West Indies, with bipinnate leaves and clusters of small bright yellow flowers, whose hard heartwood is used to make a black dye

Examples

The logwood tree flowers
Botanists studied the unique yellow flowers produced by the logwood tree.
The logwood tree was once a vital resource for the dye industry in the Caribbean.
The heartwood of the logwood tree is prized for creating natural black dyes.
