bittering

noun
beer flavor additive
7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbɪt.ər.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbɪt.ər.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a substance, especially hops, added to beer or other beverages to give a bitter taste

Examples

Bittering hops
The brewer added extra bittering to achieve a more balanced flavor in the beer.
Different types of bittering can change the taste profile of a craft ale.
Without enough bittering, the beer tasted overly sweet and lacked complexity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sweetener - a substance used to make food or drink taste sweet
