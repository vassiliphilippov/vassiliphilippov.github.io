lead up

verb
to prepare or set in motion before an event
4.0 5.85/m
Pronunciation: / liÀêd  åp / (AmE), / liÀêd  åp / (BrE)
to cause or prepare something to happen, especially by creating the necessary conditions or events beforehand

Examples

To lead up to event
Her months of training led up to her winning the marathon.
Many small changes led up to the company's major restructuring.
The tense negotiations led up to the signing of the peace treaty.

Synonyms

  • set the stage
    3.9 3.58/m
    set the stage creates clear conditions and a likely situation for an event to occur, while lead up refers to making those events happen beforehand.
  • tee up
    5.2 0.09/m
    tee up arranges or schedules something to be ready for the next step, while lead up implies causing events beforehand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: precede - to come before something in time or order
