blind

adjective
unable to see
1.0 25/m
Pronunciation: / blaÉªnd / (AmE), / blaÉªnd / (BrE)
Unable to see because of injury, disease, or a congenital condition.

Examples

Blind person

The school offers support for blind and visually impaired students.

Synonyms

  • sightless
    5.0 0.42/m

  • unsighted
    8.8 0.01/m
    unsighted describes lacking sight and appears in formal or older usage, while blind often refers to medical or birth causes.
  • unseeing
    7.0 0.03/m
    unseeing often describes not looking or appears in poetic language, while blind denotes actual inability to see from injury or disease.
