Dionysus

proper noun
Greek god of wine, fertility, and drama
Pronunciation: / daɪ.əˈnaɪ.səs / (AmE), / daɪ.əˈnaɪ.səs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of wine, fertility, and drama; associated with revelry and ecstasy.

Examples

Dionysus god of wine
In ancient art, Dionysus is often depicted holding a cup of wine and surrounded by satyrs.
Many plays performed in Athens were dedicated to Dionysus, who was honored as a patron of the theater.
The festival of Dionysus celebrated both the joys of wine and the mysteries of life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bacchus - Roman name for the god of wine and revelry
