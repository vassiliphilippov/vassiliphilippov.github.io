ground noise

noun
unwanted background noise in signals
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd nɔɪz / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd nɔɪz / (BrE)
unwanted background noise in a sound or electrical system that cannot be separated from the desired signal

Examples

Unwanted ground noise
High-quality cables can minimize ground noise in audio recordings.
Measurement accuracy was affected because ground noise obscured the true signal.
The engineers struggled to detect the weak signal due to excessive ground noise in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: background noise - general unwanted noise present in a system
  • synonym: signal-to-noise ratio - the ratio of the desired signal to background noise
