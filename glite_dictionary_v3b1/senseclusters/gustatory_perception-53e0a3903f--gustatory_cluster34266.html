gustatory perception

noun
the sense of taste
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌstəˌtɔri pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡʌstətəri pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the sensation of taste produced when taste buds detect chemicals in a substance in the mouth

Examples

Gustatory perception of taste
Certain medical conditions can diminish a person's gustatory perception.
Children often have sharper gustatory perception than adults, making them sensitive to bitter tastes.
The wine tasting relied heavily on his refined gustatory perception.

Related Terms

  • antonym: olfactory perception - the sense of smell, especially as it relates to perception of odors
  • synonym: taste - the sensation produced when a substance in the mouth reacts chemically with taste receptor cells
