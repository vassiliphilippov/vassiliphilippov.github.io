Synonyms

  • extinguish
    4.4 1.93/m
    extinguish is a formal word often used in writing and reports, while put out is everyday and common for fire or cigarette.
  • snuff out
    4.7 0.23/m
    snuff out means ending a light by pinching or covering it, while put out is a general, everyday way to stop something burning.
  • douse
    5.1 1.01/m
    douse means pouring water or liquid to stop a fire quickly, while put out is a general verb used for any extinguishing.
  • blow out
    2.4 1.53/m
    blow out means using a breath to stop a small flame like a candle, while put out covers many ways to extinguish fire.
  • smother
    5.3 0.54/m
    smother means cover a small fire to cut its air and stop it slowly, while put out is more general and neutral.
  • snuff
    5.7 1.44/m
    snuff means pinch or cover a candle to stop its flame in one quick move, while put out is more general.
  • stub out
    5.3 0.50/m
    stub out means press the cigarette end to stop it burning on a surface, while put out can mean any way to extinguish.
