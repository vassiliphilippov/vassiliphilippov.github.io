fatigue crack

noun
crack caused by metal fatigue
Pronunciation: / fəˈtiɡ kræk / (AmE), / fəˈtiːɡ kræk / (BrE)
a crack in a metal or material caused by repeated stress or cycles of loading and unloading, typically due to metal fatigue.

Examples

A fatigue crack in metal
Repeated loading and unloading caused a fatigue crack to form in the bridge's steel framework.
Routine inspections are designed to detect fatigue cracks before they become dangerous.
The engineer identified a fatigue crack in one of the aircraft's wing supports.

  • degree lower: crack - a narrow break or opening, especially in a solid material
  • synonym: metal fatigue - weakening of metal due to repeated stress
