turbulently

adverb
in a violent or stormy manner
7.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ùÀê.bj…ôl…ônt.li /; / Ààt…ùÀê.bj…ô.l…ônt.li / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀê.bj ä.l…ônt.li / (BrE)
in a violent, disorderly, or agitated way

Examples

Move turbulently in air
Feelings stirred turbulently within him as he listened to the news.
The plane shook turbulently as it flew through the storm clouds.
Waves crashed turbulently against the rocky shore during the hurricane.
His emotions shifted turbulently as he listened to the upsetting news.
Storm clouds moved turbulently across the darkening sky.
The water in the rapids flowed turbulently after the heavy rains.

Synonyms

  • tumultuously
    7.7 0.03/m

  • chaotically
    5.6 0.21/m
    chaotically means wild, confused, and without order, while turbulently implies violent, agitated motion that feels rough or unstable.
