tribunal

noun
judicial body or assembly
7.4 7.07/m
Pronunciation: / tra…™ÀàbjuÀên…ôl / (AmE), / tra…™ÀàbjuÀên(…ô)l / (BrE)
a body or assembly, often with one or more judges, that has the authority to judge, adjudicate, or determine claims or disputes

Examples

A tribunal hearing
After months of hearings, the tribunal finally issued its verdict.
She brought her complaint before the regional tribunal seeking justice.
The tribunal convened to review the evidence and deliver a ruling.

Synonyms

  • court
    1.4 186/m
    a court is a public place where judges hear and decide legal cases, while a tribunal is any body that rules on disputes.
  • judicatory
    9.9 0.05/m
    a judicatory is a system of courts that administers justice over a region, while a tribunal is a single body that decides disputes.
