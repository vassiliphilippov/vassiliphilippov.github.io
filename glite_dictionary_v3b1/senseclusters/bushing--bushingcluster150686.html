bushing

noun
insulating lining for conductors
8.6 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbʊʃ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbʊʃ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
an insulating sleeve or lining inserted into an opening to protect or insulate an electric conductor passing through it

Examples

Electrical bushing
A bushing is used to prevent electrical shorts where the wire passes through the metal panel.
Replacing the damaged bushing improved the safety of the equipment.
The electrician installed a bushing to insulate the power cable at the entry point.
