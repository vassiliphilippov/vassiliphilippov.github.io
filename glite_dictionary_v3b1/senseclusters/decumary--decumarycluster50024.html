decumary

noun
woody climber with white flower clusters
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkjuːmɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈkjuːməri / (BrE)
A woody climbing plant of the southeastern United States, noted for its white flowers in compound terminal clusters.

Examples

The decumary plant
Botanists studied the decumary for its unique climbing habit and floral arrangement.
The decumary grows abundantly in the woodlands of Georgia and Alabama.
We admired the delicate white blooms of the decumary during our summer hike.
