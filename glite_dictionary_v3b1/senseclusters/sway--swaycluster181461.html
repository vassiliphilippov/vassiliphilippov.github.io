sway

verb
move back and forth
3.4 2.1
Pronunciation: /sweɪ/ (AmE), /sweɪ/ (BrE)
to move slowly or rhythmically from side to side or back and forth

Examples

To sway the baby
She gently swayed the baby to help him fall asleep.
The bridge seemed to sway under the weight of the crowd.
The tall grass began to sway in the evening breeze.
She tried to keep her balance as she swayed down the deck of the boat.
The dancer swayed gracefully to the rhythm of the music.
The tall grass began to sway gently in the evening breeze.
She tried to stand still, but her body started to sway gently.
The old bridge would sway whenever a truck passed over it.
The trees began to sway in the strong wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rock - to move (something or someone) gently back and forth or from side to side
  • antonym: steady - to make or become stable and not move back and forth
  • synonym: swing - to move back and forth or from side to side while suspended or as if suspended
