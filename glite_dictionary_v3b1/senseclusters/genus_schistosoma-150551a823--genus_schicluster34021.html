genus Schistosoma

noun
type genus of blood flukes (Schistosomatidae)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌskɪs.təˈsoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌskɪst.əˈsəʊ.mə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Schistosomatidae, consisting of parasitic blood flukes that cause schistosomiasis in humans and animals.

Examples

Genus Schistosoma parasites
Infection with flukes from the genus Schistosoma is prevalent in many tropical regions.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of the genus Schistosoma to develop better treatments for schistosomiasis.
The genus Schistosoma includes several species known to cause disease in humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: schistosome - any parasitic worm of the genus Schistosoma
  • degree higher: schistosomiasis - disease caused by infection with Schistosoma species
