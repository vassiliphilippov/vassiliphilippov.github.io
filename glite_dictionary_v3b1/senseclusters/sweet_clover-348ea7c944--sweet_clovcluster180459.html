sweet clover

noun
a forage and soil-improving plant
Pronunciation: / swit ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / swiːt ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
an erect annual or biennial plant, especially of the genus Melilotus, grown widely for hay and soil improvement

Examples

Sweet clover plant
Farmers often plant sweet clover to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
Forage made from sweet clover is both nutritious and fragrant.
The fields were covered in yellow sweet clover in late summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clover - a plant of the genus Trifolium, often used for fodder
