Juniperus communis

noun
species of juniper with aromatic berries
Pronunciation: / dʒuːˈnɪpərəs kəˈmjunɪs / (AmE), / dʒuːˈnɪpərəs kəˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
A Eurasian and North American evergreen shrub or small tree with needle-like leaves and blue-black berries, commonly used to flavor gin.

Examples

Juniperus communis berries
Juniperus communis grows abundantly across the moors and rocky slopes of Europe and North America.
In Iceland, Juniperus communis is the only native conifer found along the coasts.
The berries of Juniperus communis are essential for imparting the distinctive flavor to gin.
