prograde

adjective
forward motion (astronomy)
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
moving or rotating in the normal or forward direction, especially as used in astronomy to describe planetary motion

Examples

Prograde planetary motion
Most planets in our solar system have prograde orbits around the Sun.
The satellite was launched into a prograde trajectory to match Earth's rotation.
Astronomers observed that the moon's prograde motion was consistent with the planet's rotation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retrograde - moving or rotating in the opposite or backward direction
