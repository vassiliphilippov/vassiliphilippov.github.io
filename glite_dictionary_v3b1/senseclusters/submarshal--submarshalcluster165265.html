submarshal

noun
assistant law officer
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
a law-enforcement officer who serves under a marshal and has authority to enforce laws and make arrests

Examples

Law submarshal
The submarshal was responsible for delivering court documents to the parties involved.
After the arrest, the submarshal escorted the suspect to the police station.
During the parade, the submarshal assisted the marshal in maintaining order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deputy - an assistant with power to act when the superior is absent
  • degree higher: marshal - a law-enforcement officer with authority over others
