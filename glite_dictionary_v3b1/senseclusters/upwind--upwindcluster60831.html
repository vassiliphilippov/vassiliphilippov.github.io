upwind

adverb
against the direction of the wind

in or toward the direction from which the wind is blowing; against the wind

Examples

Walk upwind carefully
She walked upwind so the scent wouldn't reach the animals.
The hikers moved upwind to avoid the campfire smoke.
The sailors tacked upwind to reach the harbor.
Smoke from the campfire drifted upwind, carried back toward the tent.
The sailors decided to move upwind to get a better position during the race.
When hiking, it's best to stay upwind of the herd to avoid alerting the animals.
Always approach a wild animal from the upwind side if you want to remain undetected.
The hikers stood upwind of the campfire to avoid the smoke.
The unpleasant odor seemed stronger when we were upwind of the factory.

Synonyms

  • against the wind
    3.7 0.61/m
    against the wind means moving opposite to the wind's flow, while upwind is the common term for moving toward the wind.
  • into the wind
    3.9 1.16/m
    into the wind means moving so you face the wind directly, while upwind means moving toward or against the wind.
  • windward
    7.5 0.00/m
    windward means on the side toward the wind, while upwind is used more for moving or standing toward the wind.
  • to the windward
    8.1 0.01/m
    to the windward is a nautical phrase for being toward the wind, while upwind is a general word used on land or sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downwind - in or toward the same direction as the wind is blowing
  • antonym: leeward - on or toward the side sheltered from the wind
