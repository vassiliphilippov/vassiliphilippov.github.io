interrupter

noun
device interrupting electric current
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪn.təˈrʌp.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈrʌp.tə / (BrE)
a device that automatically interrupts an electric current in a circuit

Examples

Electric interrupter
If the interrupter fails, the circuit may be damaged by overheating.
Older telegraphs used a mechanical interrupter for signaling purposes.
The engineer installed an interrupter to protect the equipment from power surges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circuit breaker - a device that stops the flow of electricity in a circuit as a safety measure
