internationalization

noun
bringing under international control
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪn.tɚˌnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪn.təˌnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of bringing something under international control, influence, or management

Examples

Internationalization of policy
Debates over the internationalization of environmental policy have grown in recent decades.
One consequence of the agreement was the internationalization of the disputed territory.
The conflict resulted in the partial internationalization of the region's administration.
After years under local authority, the internationalisation of the region was proposed by the United Nations.
Debates about the internationalisation of space law have intensified in recent years.
The internationalisation of the canal was supported by several countries to ensure open access.
