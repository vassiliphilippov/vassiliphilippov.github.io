edmontosaurus

noun
duck-billed North American dinosaur (Cretaceous)
Pronunciation: / ɛdˌmɒntəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ɛdˌmɒntəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a large, plant-eating, duck-billed dinosaur that lived in North America during the late Cretaceous period, known from fossilized remains including rare mummified specimens with skin impressions.

Examples

The edmontosaurus dinosaur
Fossilized skin impressions found with the edmontosaurus mummy provide rare insight into dinosaur biology.
Scientists believe the edmontosaurus moved in herds across what is now western Canada.
The museum's new exhibit features a nearly complete edmontosaurus skeleton discovered in Alberta.
