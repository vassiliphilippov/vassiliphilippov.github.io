nucleocapsid

noun
virus protein shell
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːkliəˈkæpsɪd / (AmE), / ˌnjuːkliəˈkæpsɪd / (BrE)
the structure in a virus made up of its genetic material and the protective protein shell around it

Examples

The nucleocapsid structure
The nucleocapsid plays a crucial role in protecting the viral genome from damage.
Researchers studied the structure of the nucleocapsid to develop new antiviral drugs.
Without its nucleocapsid, the virus would not be able to infect host cells effectively.
The nucleocapsid protects the viral RNA from being degraded by enzymes.
Scientists analyzed the nucleocapsid to understand how the virus assembles inside host cells.
Mutations in the nucleocapsid can affect how the virus replicates and spreads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capsid - the protein shell of a virus, sometimes without genetic material
  • antonym: envelope - the outer lipid layer that surrounds some viruses
