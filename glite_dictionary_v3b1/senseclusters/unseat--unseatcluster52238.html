unseat

verb
remove from office or position
4.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /ə n.ˈsiːt/ /; / /ʌn.ˈsiːt/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈsiːt/ /; / /ə n.ˈsiːt/ (BrE)
to remove someone from a position of power or authority, especially in politics

Examples

To unseat a leader
A corruption scandal could potentially unseat several senior officials.
The opposition party is determined to unseat the current president in the next election.
Voters hoped their voices would unseat the unpopular mayor.
A major scandal was enough to unseat several longtime members of parliament.
The opposition party hopes to unseat the incumbent governor in the upcoming election.
Their campaign strategy is designed specifically to unseat political incumbents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depose - to remove someone from office suddenly and forcefully
  • antonym: install - to place someone in a position of power or office
  • synonym: oust - to remove someone from a position or place, especially through force or authority
