psychopathic personality

noun
personality disorder involving lack of empathy and morality
3.8 7.2
Pronunciation: /saɪ.kə.ˈpæθ.ɪk pɜːr.sə.ˈnæl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /saɪ.koʊ.ˈpæθ.ɪk pɜːr.sə.ˈnæl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /saɪ.kə.ˈpæθ.ɪk pɜː.sə.ˈnæl.ə.tɪ/ /; / /saɪ.kə.ˈpæθ.ɪk pɜː.sə.ˈnæl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
a personality disorder marked by a lack of morality and emotional response, often showing violent behavior without guilt; now usually referred to as antisocial personality disorder

Examples

A psychopathic personality disorder
A psychopathic personality often displays charm and manipulation without genuine emotion.
He was diagnosed with a psychopathic personality after showing no remorse for his crimes.
The term psychopathic personality appears in early psychiatric literature but is rarely used today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antisocial personality disorder - a personality disorder characterized by persistent antisocial, irresponsible, or criminal behavior
  • synonym: sociopathic personality - an older term for antisocial personality disorder, emphasizing social aspects
