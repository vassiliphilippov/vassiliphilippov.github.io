cobble

verb
to assemble quickly
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒbəl / (BrE)
to put something together quickly and roughly, often using whatever is available

Examples

To cobble a plan
They had to cobble a solution together before the meeting started.
We managed to cobble a meal from the few ingredients left in the fridge.
The team cobbled a plan to finish the project on time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: construct - to build or put together carefully
  • synonym: improvise - to create or perform without preparation
