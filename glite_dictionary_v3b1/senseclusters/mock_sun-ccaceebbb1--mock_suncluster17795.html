mock sun

noun
bright atmospheric optical phenomenon; sundog
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / mÉ‘k sÊŒn / (AmE), / mÉ’k sÊŒn / (BrE)
a bright spot appearing on either side of the sun, caused by the refraction of sunlight through ice crystals in the atmosphere; also called a sundog or parhelion

Examples

A bright mock sun
A mock sun often appears on cold winter mornings when ice crystals are abundant in the air.
Photographers waited for the rare mock sun to brighten the sky during the icy weather.
We could clearly see a mock sun forming to the right of the true sun at sunset.

Synonyms

  • sundog
    9.9 0.05/m
    sundog is the common everyday name for the bright spot beside the sun, while mock sun is a less used name.
  • parhelion
    9.9 0.00/m
    parhelion is the scientific term used by scientists for the bright spot beside the sun, while mock sun is the more common everyday name.
