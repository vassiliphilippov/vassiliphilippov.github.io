cellulose acetate

noun
synthetic material used in fibers and films
6.0 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛljəˌloʊs ˈæsəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊləʊz ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a synthetic compound made by treating cellulose with acetic acid, used in making fibers, fabrics, photographic films, and varnishes

Examples

Cellulose acetate film
Old photographic films were commonly made out of cellulose acetate before digital technology became popular.
Some eyeglass frames are produced using cellulose acetate for its strength and flexibility.
The company manufactures cellulose acetate fibers for use in textiles and cigarette filters.
