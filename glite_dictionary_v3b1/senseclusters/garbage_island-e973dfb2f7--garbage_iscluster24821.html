Garbage Island

noun
large floating mass of ocean trash
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrbɪdʒ ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbɪdʒ ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
a large accumulation of floating waste and debris in the ocean, often formed by ocean currents

Examples

The Garbage Island problem
Efforts to reduce plastic waste could help prevent the growth of another Garbage Island.
Scientists have been studying the environmental impact of the Garbage Island in the Pacific Ocean.
The sight of a floating Garbage Island shocked the crew during their journey.
