pterygoid

noun
jaw muscle
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛrɪˌɡɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪˌɡɔɪd / (BrE)
either of two muscles (medial or lateral) in the jaw that help move the mandible for chewing and speaking

Examples

Pterygoid muscle movement
The dentist explained that the pterygoid muscles are important for moving the jaw side to side.
Injury to a pterygoid can make it difficult to chew or speak clearly.
The surgeon carefully avoided the pterygoid during the jaw operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: masseter - a major jaw muscle used for chewing
  • synonym: temporalis - a broad muscle on the side of the head that helps move the jaw
