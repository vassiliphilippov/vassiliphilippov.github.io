big

adjective
having a great capacity or tendency (esp. before nouns)
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ / (AmE), / bɪɡ / (BrE)
(especially before a noun) having a great capacity for or tendency toward a particular activity or quality.

Examples

A big fan
As a big supporter of the arts, she donates generously every year.
He's known as a big spender when it comes to luxury items.
She's always been a big eater, never turning down a second helping.

Related Terms

  • antonym: moderate - not large, not great in intensity or amount
  • synonym: prodigious - remarkably or impressively great in extent or degree
