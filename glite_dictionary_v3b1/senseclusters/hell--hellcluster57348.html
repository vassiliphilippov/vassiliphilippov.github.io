hell

noun
a cause of great suffering or difficulty
1.5
Pronunciation: / hɛl / (AmE), / hɛl / (BrE)
a situation or experience that is extremely difficult, painful, or full of suffering

Examples

A living hell
After losing his job and home, his life became a living hell.
Some people say that war is hell because of the suffering it brings.
Waiting for the medical results was sheer hell for her.
After the accident, living in that hospital was absolute hell for him.
For many, the long, sleepless nights before exams are pure hell.
The city became a hell during the riots, with chaos on every street.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heaven - a place or state of perfect happiness, often contrasted with hell
  • synonym: inferno - a place or situation resembling hell in chaos or danger
  • antonym: paradise - an ideal, peaceful place; the opposite of hell
