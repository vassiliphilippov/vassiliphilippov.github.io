Cepheid

noun
variable star type
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːfiɪd / (AmE), / ˈsiːfiɪd / (BrE)
a type of star that regularly changes its brightness in a predictable pattern, used by astronomers to measure distances in space

Examples

Cepheid variable star
A Cepheid can be identified by its regular pattern of brightening and dimming.
Scientists rely on Cepheids to calculate the distance to distant galaxies.
The discovery of a new Cepheid helped improve our understanding of the universe's scale.
