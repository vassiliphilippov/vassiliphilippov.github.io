ruffle

verb
walk with a proud, showy gait
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.əl / (BrE)
to walk with a proud or showy gait, often in an attempt to impress others

Examples

To ruffle through the room
After winning the competition, Mark couldn't help but ruffle through the office with pride.
He ruffled down the hallway, eager for everyone to notice his new suit.
She ruffled past the crowd, head held high and shoulders back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strut - to walk with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant or conceited gait
  • synonym: swagger - to walk or behave in a very confident and typically arrogant or aggressive way
