view

noun
the range visible to the eye
1.0 68/m
Pronunciation: / vjuÀê / (AmE), / vjuÀê / (BrE)
what you can see from a particular place; a scene or scenery

Examples

A beautiful view
Keep your valuables out of view to avoid theft.
The mountain disappeared from view as the fog rolled in.
They watched until the ship passed out of view beyond the horizon.
She stopped to take in the view of the snow-capped mountains.
The apartment has a magnificent view of the ocean from the balcony.
Tourists climbed the hill to enjoy the panoramic view of the city below.
