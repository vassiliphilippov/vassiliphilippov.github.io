throw to the wolves

verb
abandon to danger or hardship
5.2
Pronunciation: / θɹoʊ tə ðə wʊlvz / (AmE), / θɹəʊ tə ðə wʊlvz / (BrE)
to leave someone in a difficult or dangerous situation without help or protection, especially for one's own benefit

Examples

To throw to the wolves alone
Rather than admit his own mistake, he decided to throw his assistant to the wolves.
She felt betrayed when her friends threw her to the wolves during the scandal.
The company was quick to throw its junior staff to the wolves when profits dropped.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to be disloyal or abandon someone in need
  • synonym: sacrifice - to give up someone or something for the benefit of others
