photocatalysis

noun
light-driven reaction
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊkəˈtæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊkəˈtæləsɪs / (BrE)
a chemical reaction that is sped up by the presence of light and a catalyst

Examples

Photocatalysis reaction
Scientists use photocatalysis to break down pollutants in water using sunlight.
The process of photocatalysis can help create cleaner energy sources.
Recent advances in photocatalysis have improved the efficiency of solar-powered chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalysis - the acceleration of a chemical reaction by a catalyst
