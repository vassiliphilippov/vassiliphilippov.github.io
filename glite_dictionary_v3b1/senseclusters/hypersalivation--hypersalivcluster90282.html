hypersalivation

noun
excessive saliva
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌsælɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˌsælɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
excessive production of saliva, often occurring as a medical symptom

Examples

Excessive hypersalivation
The doctor noted hypersalivation as a possible side effect of the new medication.
Some neurological conditions can cause hypersalivation in patients.
Wearing new dental braces sometimes leads to temporary hypersalivation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drooling - uncontrolled flow of saliva from the mouth
  • antonym: xerostomia - abnormal dryness of the mouth due to lack of saliva
