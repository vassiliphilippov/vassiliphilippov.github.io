gender

noun
social or personal identity (not necessarily biological)
2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒen.dər/ (AmE), /ˈdʒen.də/ (BrE)
A person's social or personal identity and experience of being male, female, both, neither, or another identity; how someone expresses or understands their gender, which may not match biological sex.

Examples

Preferred gender
At the clinic, patients can now record their preferred gender on intake forms.
Many schools teach about gender as roles and identities, not just biological differences.
She came out and began using a new name that matched her gender identity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gender identity - a person's internal sense of their own gender (male, female, both, neither, or another identity)
  • antonym: sex - the biological distinction between male and female
