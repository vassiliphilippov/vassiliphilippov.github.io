safety blitz

noun
defensive play by a safety in football
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti blɪts / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti blɪts / (BrE)
(in American football) a defensive play in which a safety attempts to rush the quarterback by breaking through the offensive line.

Examples

A safety blitz sack
During the third quarter, the defense executed a successful safety blitz that resulted in a sack.
Fans cheered when the unexpected safety blitz disrupted the offense's play.
The coach called a safety blitz to increase pressure on the quarterback.
