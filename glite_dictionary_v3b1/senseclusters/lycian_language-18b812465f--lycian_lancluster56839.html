Lycian language

noun
extinct Anatolian language of ancient Lycia
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪsiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language formerly spoken by the ancient Lycians in what is now southwestern Turkey

Examples

The Lycian language study
Linguists study the Lycian language to learn about the history of Anatolian peoples.
Scholars have deciphered ancient inscriptions written in the Lycian language.
The Lycian language became extinct after the conquest by the Romans.
