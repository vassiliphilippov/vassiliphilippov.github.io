crowd

noun
large group of people or things
1.0 92/m
Pronunciation: / kraÊŠd / (AmE), / kraÊŠd / (BrE)
A large number of people or things gathered closely together in one place.

Examples

A big crowd
A large crowd gathered in the square to hear the mayor speak.
She pushed her way through the crowd to reach the front of the stage.
The flowers attracted a crowd of buzzing bees.

Synonyms

  • multitude
    4.6 1.24/m
    multitude is a very large number of people often used in formal speech, while crowd is a common everyday word for many people.
  • press
    5.1 1.15/m
    press is a tightly packed group of people who push close together, while crowd means people gathered closely in one place.
  • swarm
    3.4 3.37/m
    swarm is a busy, moving group often uncontrolled or insect-like, while crowd is simply many people gathered together in one place.

Related Terms

  • antonym: handful - a small number of people or things
  • synonym: mob - a large, disorderly crowd of people
  • synonym: multitude - a great number of people or things
  • synonym: throng - a large, densely packed crowd of people or animals
