crowd

noun
large group of people or things
Pronunciation: / kraʊd / (AmE), / kraʊd / (BrE)
A large number of people or things gathered closely together in one place.

Examples

A big crowd
A large crowd gathered in the square to hear the mayor speak.
She pushed her way through the crowd to reach the front of the stage.
The flowers attracted a crowd of buzzing bees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: handful - a small number of people or things
  • synonym: mob - a large, disorderly crowd of people
  • synonym: multitude - a great number of people or things
  • synonym: throng - a large, densely packed crowd of people or animals
