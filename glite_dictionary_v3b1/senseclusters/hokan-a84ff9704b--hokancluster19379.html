Hokan

noun
family of Amerindian languages
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈhəʊkən / (BrE)
A proposed family of Amerindian languages formerly spoken in California and parts of the southwestern United States and northwest Mexico.

Examples

Hokan language family
Linguists have debated the classification of the Hokan language family for over a century.
Some Hokan languages are now extinct, while others are only sparsely documented.
The Hokan hypothesis connects languages spoken in California, Arizona, and northern Mexico.
