crackling

noun
residue from rendered animal fat
Pronunciation: / ˈkræk.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkræk.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the crisp, browned residue left after rendering animal fat, often eaten as a snack or used in cooking

Examples

Pork crackling
Crackling is a traditional treat made from pork fat in many cultures.
She sprinkled crackling over her salad for extra crunch.
The chef saved the crackling to add flavor to the stew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scratching - crispy pork rind or skin, especially in British English
