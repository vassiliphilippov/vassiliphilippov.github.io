prudently

adverb
in a careful, wise manner
5.0 0.79/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpruÀêd…ôntli / (AmE), / ÀàpruÀêd…ôntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows careful judgment, thought for the future, or wisdom in avoiding unnecessary risks.

Examples

To prudently plan ahead
He prudently avoided driving in the storm, waiting for the weather to clear.
She invested her savings prudently, choosing low-risk bonds over volatile stocks.
The company prudently decided to postpone expansion until the market stabilized.

Synonyms

  • prudentially
    7.7 0.04/m
    prudentially is used in formal financial or practical settings to avoid risk, while prudently means general careful action for future safety.
  • judiciously
    6.5 0.82/m
    judiciously means making decisions with careful, fair thought, while prudently focuses more on avoiding future risks.
  • wisely
    2.3 7.03/m
    wisely shows good judgment and intelligence in many situations, while prudently especially means acting to avoid future risks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recklessly - in a way that lacks caution or consideration
  • synonym: wisely - in a way that shows good judgment
