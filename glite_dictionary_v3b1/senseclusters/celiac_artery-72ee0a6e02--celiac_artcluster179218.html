celiac artery

noun
major abdominal artery
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːli.æk ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsiːli.æk ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
a major artery that branches off the abdominal aorta below the diaphragm, supplying blood to the stomach, liver, and spleen

Examples

The celiac artery branch
A blockage of the celiac artery can reduce blood flow to vital abdominal organs.
Aneurysms affecting the celiac artery are considered rare but serious conditions.
The surgeon carefully examined the celiac artery during the procedure.
