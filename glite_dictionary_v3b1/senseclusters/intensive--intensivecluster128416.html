intensive

noun
concentrated course
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a course or period of study that is very thorough and concentrated, often completed in a short time

Examples

A French intensive
She signed up for an intensive in French to prepare for her trip.
The university offers a summer intensive for students who want to earn extra credits quickly.
After completing the intensive, he felt much more confident in his language skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crash course - a rapid and intense course of study
  • synonym: seminar - a class or meeting for discussion or training
  • synonym: workshop - a short, intensive educational program
