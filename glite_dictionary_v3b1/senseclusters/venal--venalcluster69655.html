venal

adjective
open to bribery or corruption
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnəl / (AmE), / ˈviːnəl / (BrE)
willing to behave dishonestly, especially by accepting money in exchange for doing something immoral or illegal

Examples

A venal officer
Charges were filed against the venal officer who allegedly accepted money to overlook illegal activities.
Many feared that the system was run by venal politicians prioritizing personal gain over public good.
The report accused several officials of being venal and accepting bribes during the contract tendering process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupt - willing to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain
  • antonym: honest - not willing to accept bribes or act dishonestly
