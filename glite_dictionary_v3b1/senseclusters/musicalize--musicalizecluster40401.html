musicalize

verb
to make musical
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːzɪkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something musical or set it to music (such as adapting a play, poem, or scene into a musical form or giving it musical qualities)

Examples

The composer was asked to musicalize the poem for a school choir performance.
It took several weeks to musicalize the folk tune for a jazz band.
She loves to musicalize simple melodies into complex arrangements for different ensembles.
The director decided to musicalize the scene by adding a rhythmic soundtrack.
Artists often musicalize spoken word poetry to create a unique performance.
The documentary uses sound effects to musicalize everyday city noises.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to change something to suit a new purpose
  • synonym: arrange - to adapt a piece of music for performance by particular instruments or voices
  • synonym: dramatize - to adapt a story or event for dramatic presentation
  • synonym: orchestrate - to arrange music for an orchestra or group of instruments
