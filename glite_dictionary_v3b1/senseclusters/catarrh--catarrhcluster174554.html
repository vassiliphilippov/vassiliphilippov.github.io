catarrh

noun
inflammation of nose/throat with mucus
Pronunciation: / kəˈtɑr / (AmE), / kəˈtɑː / (BrE)
inflammation of the mucous membranes in the nose and throat, typically with increased mucus production

Examples

Nasal catarrh
During the winter, many people suffer from catarrh, which leads to stuffy noses and sore throats.
His persistent cough was eventually diagnosed as catarrh caused by a chronic sinus infection.
The doctor explained that catarrh is a common symptom during the early stages of a cold.
