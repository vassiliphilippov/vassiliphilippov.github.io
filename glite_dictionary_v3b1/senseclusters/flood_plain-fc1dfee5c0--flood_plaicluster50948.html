flood plain

noun
flat land beside a river that floods
3.7 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / flÊŒd pleÉªn / (AmE), / flÊŒd pleÉªn / (BrE)
an area of flat land next to a river, formed mainly of river sediments and prone to flooding when the river overflows

Examples

A flood plain area
Developing houses on a flood plain can increase the risk of flood damage.
During the rainy season, the flood plain is often completely submerged.
Farmers benefit from the rich soil found in the flood plain of the river.

Synonyms

  • floodplain
    4.6 1.71/m
    floodplain is a common one-word term for low ground by a river that floods, while flood plain appears as an alternative two-word form.
  • alluvial plain
    5.8 0.08/m
    alluvial plain is land formed by river sediments and used in geology, while flood plain stresses its flat, flood-prone nature.
  • bottomland
    7.2 0.48/m
    bottomland is low-lying river land often wooded and fertile, while flood plain refers to flatter sediment plains that are prone to flooding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvial plain - a flat landform created by deposition of sediment over a long period by one or more rivers
