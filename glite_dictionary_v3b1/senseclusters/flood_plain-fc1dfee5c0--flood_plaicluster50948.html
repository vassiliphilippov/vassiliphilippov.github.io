flood plain

noun
flat land beside a river that floods
8.3 3.1
Pronunciation: /flʌd pleɪn/ (AmE), /flʌd pleɪn/ (BrE)
an area of flat land next to a river, formed mainly of river sediments and prone to flooding when the river overflows

Examples

A flood plain area
Developing houses on a flood plain can increase the risk of flood damage.
During the rainy season, the flood plain is often completely submerged.
Farmers benefit from the rich soil found in the flood plain of the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvial plain - a flat landform created by deposition of sediment over a long period by one or more rivers
