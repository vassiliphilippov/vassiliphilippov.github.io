Synonyms

  • rear
    7.0 0.5/m
    rear often means to build large structures in a formal way, while erect is a general term for putting up buildings.
  • put up
    3.0 2/m
    put up is an informal phrasal verb for quickly building signs or fences, while erect sounds more formal and official.
  • raise
    3.7 4/m
    raise often means to build something by putting parts together over time, while erect stresses creating a finished structure.
  • set up
    2.7 40/m
    set up can mean arranging equipment, starting a business, or building temporary things, while erect usually means building permanent structures.
