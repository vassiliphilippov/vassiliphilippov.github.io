Baltic

noun
branch of Indo-European languages (Lithuanian, Latvian, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔl.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.tɪk / (BrE)
the branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Lithuanian, Latvian, and Old Prussian; related to the Slavic languages and noted for its preservation of archaic features

Examples

Baltic language family
Lithuanian is a living example of a Baltic language still spoken today.
Some linguists study Baltic to reconstruct aspects of Proto-Indo-European.
The similarities between Slavic and Baltic suggest a close historical relationship.

Related Terms
