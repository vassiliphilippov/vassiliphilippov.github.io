contrary

noun
relation between two opposing statements in logic
7.9 0.8/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên.tr…õr.i / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.tr…ô.ri / (BrE)
A relation between two statements in which both cannot be true at the same time, but both may be false.

Examples

Logical contraries
In classical logic, two propositions are said to be contraries if both cannot be true but both can be false.
The statements 'All swans are white' and 'No swans are white' are contraries because they can't both be true.
When constructing a logical argument, understanding the difference between contraries and contradictories is essential.
