backcross

verb
mate a hybrid with one of its parents
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌkrɔs / (AmE), / ˈbækˌkrɒs / (BrE)
to mate a hybrid organism with one of its parents or with an individual genetically similar to its parent, especially to achieve offspring with a desired trait

Examples

To backcross animals
Sometimes breeders backcross animals to preserve rare genetic characteristics in the offspring.
The scientists decided to backcross the hybrid plants with the original strain to reinforce certain traits.
They backcrossed the first generation hybrids with one parent to improve disease resistance.
