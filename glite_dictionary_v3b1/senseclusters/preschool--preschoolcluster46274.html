preschool

noun
school for young children before elementary
1.3 12/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpriÀåskuÀêl / (AmE), / ÀàpriÀêskuÀêl / (BrE)
An educational institution for children who are too young to attend elementary school, typically for ages 3 to 5.

Examples

A preschool program
Many parents look for a preschool with experienced teachers and a strong curriculum.
My daughter starts preschool next month and she's very excited to make new friends.
The preschool provides a safe and nurturing environment for young children to learn and play.

Synonyms

  • nursery school
    1.6 1.12/m
    nursery school is an institution that prepares three- to five-year-olds for primary school, while preschool is the broader term for early education.
  • prekindergarten
    2.8 0.28/m
    prekindergarten is a short program before kindergarten for children, while preschool is the general institution serving the same age group.
  • nursery
    1.0 2.96/m
    nursery is a place where young children are cared for or watched during the day, while preschool focuses on early education.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: kindergarten - a class or school for young children, typically five years old, that serves as an introduction to school
  • synonym: nursery school - a school for children who are not old enough to attend kindergarten or elementary school
