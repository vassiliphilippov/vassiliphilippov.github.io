self-denying

adjective
willing to deny one's own interests
3.4
Pronunciation: / sɛlf dɪˈnaɪɪŋ /; / ˌsɛlf.dɪˈnaɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛlf dɪˈnaɪɪŋ /; / ˌself.dɪˈnaɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
willing to give up your own interests or desires for the sake of others or a greater cause

Examples

Self-denying attitude
His self-denying nature was admired by everyone in the community.
It takes a self-denying attitude to volunteer long hours without expecting recognition.
She led a self-denying life, always putting the needs of others before her own.
He led a self-denying life, always putting his family’s needs ahead of his own.
Her self-denying actions inspired others to volunteer for the cause.
The nurse was known for her self-denying devotion to her patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: self-indulgent - allowing oneself to have or do what one wants
  • synonym: selfless - concerned more with others than oneself
