interglacial

adjective
between ice ages
7.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈɡleɪ.ʃəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈɡleɪ.ʃəl/ (BrE)
relating to or occurring during a warmer period between two ice ages when glaciers retreat.

Examples

Interglacial climate period
Scientists study interglacial periods to understand how Earth's climate changes over time.
During an interglacial phase, temperatures rise and glaciers shrink significantly.
Fossil evidence from interglacial times reveals different plant and animal distributions.
