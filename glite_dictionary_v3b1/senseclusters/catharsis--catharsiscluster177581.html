catharsis

noun
purging of the bowels with medicine
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑrsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːsɪs / (BrE)
The process of purging the body, especially the bowels, by the use of a cathartic medicine.

Examples

Medical catharsis
Excessive use of drugs to induce catharsis can lead to dehydration and other complications.
In ancient medicine, catharsis was a common treatment for various illnesses.
The physician recommended catharsis to relieve the patient's severe constipation.
