bounce

verb
eject from a place or premises
Pronunciation: / baʊns / (AmE), / baʊns / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place, especially a club, bar, or other premises

Examples

To bounce a guest
If you don't have an invite, they'll probably bounce you at the entrance.
Last night, they bounced three unruly guests from the club.
The bouncer threatened to bounce anyone causing trouble at the door.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - to allow entry to a place
  • synonym: eject - to force someone to leave a place
