harry

verb
raid and cause destruction repeatedly
8.2 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhæri / (AmE), / ˈhæri / (BrE)
(of soldiers or attackers) to raid or attack a place or people repeatedly, causing destruction or distress, especially during war.

Examples

To harry a village
During the war, enemy troops harried the countryside, burning villages and taking supplies.
The city was constantly harried by raiders, forcing its inhabitants to fortify their defenses.
Viking invaders would often harry coastal settlements, leaving devastation in their wake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: raid - a sudden attack on a place, often causing damage or theft
