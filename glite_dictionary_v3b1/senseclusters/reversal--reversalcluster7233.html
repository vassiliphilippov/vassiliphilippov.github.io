reversal

noun
change to an opposite direction or position
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɝːsəl /; / rɪˈvɜrsəl /; / rɪˈvɜːrsəl /; / rɪˈvɜr.səl / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsəl /; / rɪˈvɜː.səl / (BrE)
the act or process of changing to the opposite direction, position, order, or state

Examples

A sudden reversal of direction
A sudden reversal in the wind's direction surprised the sailors.
The reversal of the magnet's polarity altered the device's function.
The camera produces a reversal of the scene, with left and right swapped.
A reversal of the sequence changed the outcome entirely.
The sudden reversal of traffic flow caused confusion among the drivers.
The team managed a remarkable reversal of fortune in the second half of the match.
After years of supporting the policy, the government announced a reversal in its position.
His speech marked a reversal of his previous views on climate change.
The company's sudden reversal left many employees confused about the future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: about-face - a complete change of attitude or opinion
  • antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on or maintaining without change
  • synonym: inversion - the act of turning something upside down or inside out
  • synonym: turnaround - an abrupt or unexpected change, especially in policy or opinion
