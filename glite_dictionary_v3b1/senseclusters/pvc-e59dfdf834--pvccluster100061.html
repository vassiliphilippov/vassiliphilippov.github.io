PVC

noun
synthetic plastic polymer (polyvinyl chloride)
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpiːˌviːˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌpiːˌviːˈsiː / (BrE)
a tough, synthetic plastic polymer made from vinyl chloride, used especially in pipes, cables, and a wide range of products

Examples

PVC water pipes
Most modern plumbing pipes are made from PVC because it is durable and corrosion-resistant.
Old rubber hoses were replaced with PVC tubing to improve the system's performance.
The electrician recommended using PVC cables for the new installation due to their safety features.
