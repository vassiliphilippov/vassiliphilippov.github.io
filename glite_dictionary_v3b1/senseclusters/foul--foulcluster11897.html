foul

verb
break the rules in a game or sport
Pronunciation: / faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
to break the rules in a sport, especially by making illegal contact with another player

Examples

To foul a player
If you foul another player, you risk a yellow card.
The defender fouled the striker in the penalty area.
The referee blew the whistle when he saw her foul the opponent.
The referee gave a yellow card after he fouled the opponent during the match.
Players who keep fouling may be sent off the field.
She accidentally fouled her rival while trying to block the shot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obey - to follow the rules or instructions
  • synonym: violate - to break or fail to comply with a rule or law
