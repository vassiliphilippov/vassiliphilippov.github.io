Mexican standoff

noun
stalemate or deadlock with no clear winner
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈstændˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈstændɒf / (BrE)
a situation in which no side can achieve victory or gain an advantage over the others, resulting in a deadlock

Examples

A Mexican standoff situation
The final scene of the movie features a dramatic Mexican standoff between the main characters.
The negotiations ended in a Mexican standoff, with neither party willing to compromise.
Three rival companies found themselves in a Mexican standoff over the acquisition deal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breakthrough - an act or instance of making progress, especially after a stalemate
  • synonym: impasse - a situation in which progress is impossible, especially because of disagreement
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation with no possible progress or victory
