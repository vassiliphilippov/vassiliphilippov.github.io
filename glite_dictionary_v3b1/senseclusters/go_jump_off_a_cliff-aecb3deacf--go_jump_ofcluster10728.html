go jump off a cliff

x
expression of strong rejection or disapproval
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ dʒʌmp ɑf ə klɪf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ dʒʌmp ɒf ə klɪf / (BrE)
(informal, often hyperbolic) Used to tell someone you strongly reject, disapprove of, or are annoyed by what they have said or done.

Examples

To go jump off a cliff
He told his critics to go jump off a cliff when they doubted his success.
If you don't like my decision, you can just go jump off a cliff.
She felt so irritated, she nearly shouted, 'Why don't you go jump off a cliff!'
