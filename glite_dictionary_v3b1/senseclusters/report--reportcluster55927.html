report

verb
announce findings or results
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to give or present information about something, especially as a job or official duty

Examples

To report the news
Authorities report that the bridge is safe for travel after the inspection.
Several witnesses reported seeing the suspect leave the building.
The scientists reported their findings at the international conference.
He has reported extensively on environmental issues throughout his career.
Several journalists reported from the front lines of the conflict.
She was sent to report on the presidential campaign for the national news agency.
He reported on the team's progress during the weekly meeting.
Please report any suspicious activity to the authorities immediately.
The journalist reported the events of the protest in detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: announce - to make known publicly or officially
  • antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
  • synonym: cover - to report on an event or topic, especially as a journalist
  • synonym: describe - give a detailed account in words
  • degree lower: investigate - to carry out research or inquiry, especially for news reporting
