constricting

adjective
limiting or restricting freedom
2.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrɪktɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪktɪŋ / (BrE)
(of circumstances) tending to limit or restrict freedom, movement, or development.

Examples

A constricting feeling
Many people feel that living in a small town can be constricting compared to city life.
She left her job because she found the atmosphere too constricting for creativity.
The new law was criticized for its constricting impact on civil liberties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restrictive - limiting the freedom or action of someone or something
