rub off on

verb
(of a quality) transfer by influence or example
5.8 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / rÊŒb É”f É‘n / (AmE), / rÊŒb É’f É’n / (BrE)
(of a quality or feeling) to be transferred from one person to another through association, especially by influence or example.

Examples

To rub off on others
His cheerful attitude tends to rub off on everyone he meets.
I hope some of her determination will rub off on me during the project.
Their professionalism quickly rubbed off on the rest of the staff.
