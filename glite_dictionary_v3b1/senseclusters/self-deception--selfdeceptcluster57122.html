self-deception

noun
deceiving oneself; comforting false belief
3.1 5.3
Pronunciation: /self.dɪ.ˈsep.ʃən/ (AmE), /self.dɪ.ˈsep.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of deceiving oneself by believing something that is not true, typically because it is comforting or convenient

Examples

Self-deception in thinking
He fell victim to self-deception and ignored all the warning signs.
Her self-deception made it impossible for her to see the truth about the situation.
Psychologists often discuss self-deception as a barrier to personal growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delusion - false belief or opinion that is firmly maintained despite being contradicted
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or free from deceit
