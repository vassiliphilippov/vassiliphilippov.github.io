civil script

noun
form of Cyrillic alphabet introduced by Peter the Great
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪvəl skrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl skrɪpt / (BrE)
a simplified form of the Cyrillic alphabet, introduced by Tsar Peter the Great in the early 18th century for printing secular texts

Examples

The civil script alphabet
Historians often study how the civil script influenced Russian literacy and education reforms.
The civil script marked a departure from the traditional church Slavonic used for religious texts.
Under Peter the Great, the civil script was adopted for publishing secular literature in Russia.
