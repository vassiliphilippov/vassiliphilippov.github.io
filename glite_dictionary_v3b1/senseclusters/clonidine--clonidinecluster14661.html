clonidine

noun
antihypertensive medication
Pronunciation: / kloʊˈnɪdin / (AmE), / kləʊˈnɪdiːn / (BrE)
A drug used to lower high blood pressure, sometimes also used to treat other conditions such as ADHD or anxiety.

Examples

Clonidine medicine
Clonidine is sometimes given to children with ADHD to help manage symptoms.
Some patients may use clonidine patches if they have trouble swallowing pills.
The doctor prescribed clonidine to help control her blood pressure.
