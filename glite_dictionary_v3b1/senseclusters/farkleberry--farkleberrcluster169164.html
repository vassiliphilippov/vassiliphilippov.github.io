farkleberry

noun
North American shrub/tree with black berries
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑr.kəlˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈfɑː.kəlˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the eastern United States (Vaccinium arboreum), with black, inedible berries.

Examples

The farkleberry shrub
Botanists study the farkleberry because it is one of the hardiest species in the genus Vaccinium.
During our hike, we spotted several farkleberry shrubs, but their berries are not edible.
The farkleberry is often found growing on rocky hillsides in the southeastern United States.
