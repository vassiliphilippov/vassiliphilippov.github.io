sluff

noun
minor snow avalanche
9.7
Pronunciation: / slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
a small mass of loose surface snow that breaks away and slides down a slope, causing a minor avalanche

Examples

Snow sluff
The skier triggered a small sluff that slid harmlessly down the mountain.
After the fresh snowfall, a sluff covered the lower part of the trail.
Climbers watched as a sluff tumbled down the slope ahead of them.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: avalanche - a large mass of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside
  • synonym: slide - a movement of loose material down a slope
