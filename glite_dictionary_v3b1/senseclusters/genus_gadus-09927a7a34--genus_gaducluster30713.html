genus Gadus

noun
type genus of codfishes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪdəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Gadidae, containing the typical codfishes.

Examples

Genus Gadus codfishes
In marine biology, the genus Gadus is recognized as the archetype of codfishes.
Scientists have reclassified certain species within the genus Gadus after genetic studies clarified their relationships.
The genus Gadus includes several species prized for their edible flesh, such as the Atlantic cod.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gadidae - the family of fish that includes cods and related species
  • synonym: cod - a large marine fish with a small barbel on the chin, important as food
