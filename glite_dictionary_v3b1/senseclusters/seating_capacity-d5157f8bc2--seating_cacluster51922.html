seating capacity

noun
maximum number of people a space can seat
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːtɪŋ kəˌpæsəti / (AmE), / ˈsiːtɪŋ kəˌpæsəti / (BrE)
the maximum number of people that can be seated in a particular venue, vehicle, or space

Examples

Theater seating capacity
Due to safety regulations, the theater cannot exceed its seating capacity.
The new bus model was designed with an increased seating capacity to accommodate more passengers.
The stadium has a seating capacity of 50,000, making it the largest in the country.
