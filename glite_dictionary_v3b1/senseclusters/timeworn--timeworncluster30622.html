timeworn

adjective
overused and unoriginal; trite
6.7 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪmˌwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌwɔːn / (BrE)
(of an idea, phrase, or practice) repeated so often that it has become unoriginal or stale; trite through overuse.

Examples

Timeworn phrase
His speech was filled with timeworn clichés that failed to impress the audience.
Instead of offering timeworn advice, the teacher encouraged original thinking.
The movie relies on a timeworn plot device, making it very predictable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - (of an idea or expression) new and original, not repeated or overused
  • synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) lacking significance through having been overused
  • synonym: trite - (of a remark, opinion, or idea) lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
