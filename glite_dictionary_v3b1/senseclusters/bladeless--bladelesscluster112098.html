bladeless

adjective
without a blade
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbleɪdləs / (AmE), / ˈbleɪdləs / (BrE)
describing a procedure or device that operates without the use of a traditional metal blade, often using a laser or other technology instead

Examples

Bladeless surgery
The surgeon recommended a bladeless technique to reduce the risk of complications.
Many patients prefer bladeless eye surgery because it is less invasive.
Advances in technology have made bladeless procedures more common in modern medicine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bladed - using or having a blade
  • synonym: laser-assisted - using a laser instead of a blade or scalpel
