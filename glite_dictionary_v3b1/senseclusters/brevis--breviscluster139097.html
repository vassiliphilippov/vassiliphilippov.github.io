brevis

noun
Latin for short
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛvɪs / (AmE), / ˈbriːvɪs / (BrE)
a Latin term meaning 'short', used in anatomical names to indicate a shorter muscle or structure.

Examples

Brevis muscle
The extensor pollicis brevis helps extend the thumb in the human hand.
In anatomy, the term brevis distinguishes a shorter muscle from a longer one with a similar name.
The peroneus brevis is located in the lower leg and assists with foot movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: longus - a Latin term meaning 'long', used in anatomical names for longer muscles
