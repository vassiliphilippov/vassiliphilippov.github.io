confront

verb
present someone with a challenge or accusation
2.4 3.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / kənˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
to face someone directly, especially in a challenging, accusing, or hostile way

Examples

To confront a person
She confronted her manager about the unfair treatment she received at work.
The detective confronted the suspect with new evidence during the interview.
When confronted with the facts, he finally admitted his mistake.
During the debate, the two candidates confronted each other over policy differences.
She is preparing to confront her fears and speak in public.
The manager had to confront the employee about repeated lateness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuse - to state that someone has done something wrong
  • antonym: avoid - to keep away from or not confront
  • synonym: challenge - to invite someone to contest or dispute something
  • synonym: face - to meet or deal with directly
  • antonym: ignore - to refuse to acknowledge or deal with something
