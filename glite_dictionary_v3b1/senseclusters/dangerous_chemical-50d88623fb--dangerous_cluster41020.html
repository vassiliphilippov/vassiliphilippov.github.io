dangerous chemical

noun
harmful substance used in industry
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪn.dʒɚ.əs ˈkɛm.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈdeɪn.dʒər.əs ˈkɛm.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
A substance that has the potential to cause harm, injury, or damage, especially one used in manufacturing or industrial processes.

Examples

A dangerous chemical substance
Proper storage of any dangerous chemical is essential to prevent accidents.
The leak involved a dangerous chemical that required a full evacuation of the plant.
Workers must wear protective gear when handling a dangerous chemical in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmless chemical - a substance that does not cause harm or injury
  • synonym: toxic chemical - a substance that can cause serious harm or death if absorbed or ingested
