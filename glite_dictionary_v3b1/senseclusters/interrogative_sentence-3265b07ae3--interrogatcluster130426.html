interrogative sentence

noun
sentence that asks a question
4.9
Pronunciation: / ɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv ˈsɛn.təns / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv ˈsɛn.təns / (BrE)
A sentence that asks a question and expects a response, typically ending with a question mark.

Examples

An interrogative sentence asks
An interrogative sentence usually begins with a question word such as 'who,' 'what,' or 'why.'
Can you identify whether this is an interrogative sentence or a statement?
Teachers often ask students to write an interrogative sentence during grammar exercises.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative sentence - a sentence that makes a statement
  • antonym: exclamatory sentence - a sentence that expresses strong feeling
  • antonym: imperative sentence - a sentence that gives a command or instruction
