cusp

noun
turning point
6.3
Pronunciation: / kʌsp / (AmE), / kʌsp / (BrE)
the point or moment when something is about to change or a new stage begins

Examples

On the cusp of change
She stood on the cusp of a new career, ready to embrace new challenges.
The country is on the cusp of major technological change.
In mathematics, a cusp is the point where two curves meet sharply.
She is on the cusp of starting her new career.
The country stood at the cusp of major political change.
Many teenagers feel they are on the cusp of adulthood as they finish high school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brink - the edge or verge of something about to happen
  • antonym: end - the final point or conclusion
  • synonym: peak - the highest point or maximum
  • synonym: threshold - the starting point of an experience or event
