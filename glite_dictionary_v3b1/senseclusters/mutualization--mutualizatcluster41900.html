mutualization

noun
sharing financial risk
7.9
Pronunciation: /mjuː.tʃu.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mjuː.tʃə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mjuː.tʃu.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mjuː.tʃə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of sharing or pooling financial risk, liabilities, or debt among several parties so that responsibility is held jointly

Examples

Mutualization of risk
The mutualization of insurance companies allowed policyholders to become part-owners.
Debates over the mutualization of public debt have intensified during economic crises.
Some banks underwent mutualization to better align their interests with those of their customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: privatization - the process of transferring ownership or responsibility from a group to private individuals
