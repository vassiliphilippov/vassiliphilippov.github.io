over-the-counter derivative

noun
privately negotiated financial contract
6.2 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚ ðə ˈkaʊntɚ dɪˈrɪvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈkaʊntə dɪˈrɪvətɪv / (BrE)
A financial contract, such as a swap or forward, that is traded and privately negotiated directly between two parties rather than through a regulated exchange.

Examples

An over-the-counter derivative contract
Many large institutions use over-the-counter derivatives to hedge against currency and interest rate risks.
Regulatory bodies are working to increase oversight of over-the-counter derivatives after the financial crisis.
The lack of transparency in over-the-counter derivative markets can make pricing more complex.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exchange-traded derivative - a derivative contract traded on a formal exchange
