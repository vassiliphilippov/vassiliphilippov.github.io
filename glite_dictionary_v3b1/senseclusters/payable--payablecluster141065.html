(in blockchain programming, especially Solidity) able to receive cryptocurrency; used to mark a function or address that can accept Ether or other native tokens.

Examples

Payable function
In Solidity, a function must be payable to accept Ether sent with a transaction.
The contract's fallback method was not payable, so incoming transfers reverted and failed.
Always mark deposit-receiving functions payable during development to prevent accidental transaction reverts.
