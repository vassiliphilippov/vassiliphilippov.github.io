videoconference

verb
to meet by video
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪdioʊˌkɑːnfərəns / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊˌkɒnfərəns / (BrE)
to hold or participate in a meeting using video and internet technology, often from different locations

Examples

To videoconference with clients
We will videoconference with the partner company next Tuesday instead of traveling there.
Many employees now videoconference from home to stay connected with their teams.
The manager prefers to videoconference rather than schedule long in-person meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: meet in person - to gather physically in the same place for a meeting
  • synonym: teleconference - to hold a meeting by telephone or electronic means
