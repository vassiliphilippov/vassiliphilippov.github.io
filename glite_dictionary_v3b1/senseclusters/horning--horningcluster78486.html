horning

noun
Scottish debt procedure
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
a historical Scottish legal procedure in which a debtor was publicly declared an outlaw by the sounding of a horn

Examples

Declared by horning
In medieval Scotland, horning was used to publicly shame those who failed to pay their debts.
The process of horning involved a public ceremony with the blowing of a horn.
After repeated warnings, the merchant was subjected to horning for refusing to settle his accounts.
