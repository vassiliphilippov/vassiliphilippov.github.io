remanence

noun
remaining trace
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈre.mə.nəns/ (AmE), /ˈre.mə.nəns/ (BrE)
a small remaining part, trace, or lingering effect of something that has mostly disappeared

Examples

A remanence of smell
Even after the paint was removed, a remanence of color could be seen on the wall.
The remanence of her perfume lingered in the empty room long after she had left.
Scientists studied the remanence of magnetism in the ancient rocks to learn about Earth's history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absence - the state of being completely gone or missing
  • synonym: residue - a small amount left after the main part is gone
  • synonym: trace - a very small amount or sign of something
