anthropogeography

noun
study of human impact
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpoʊdʒiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəʊdʒiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study of how human populations and activities interact with, influence, and are influenced by the natural environment and geography

Examples

Anthropogeography studies humans

Her research in anthropogeography focuses on the effects of deforestation caused by human settlement.

Anthropogeography explores how cultural practices vary in different regions of the world.
Researchers in anthropogeography analyze how cities develop in response to their environments.
The field of anthropogeography helps explain migration patterns across continents.

Modern anthropogeography examines the effects of globalization on population movement.
