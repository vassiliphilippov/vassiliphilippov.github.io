intestinal flora

noun
microorganisms living in the intestines
5.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
the microorganisms, especially bacteria, that normally live in the intestines and help maintain its healthy functioning

Examples

Healthy intestinal flora
A healthy intestinal flora is important for the digestive and immune systems.
Antibiotics can disrupt the balance of intestinal flora, leading to digestive problems.
Probiotics are often taken to help restore normal intestinal flora after illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gut microbiota - the community of microorganisms living in the digestive tract
  • synonym: intestinal fauna - the animal organisms in the intestines, sometimes used as an analogy
