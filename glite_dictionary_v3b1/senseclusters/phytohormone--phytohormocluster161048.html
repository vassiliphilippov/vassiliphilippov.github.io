phytohormone

noun
plant hormone; growth regulator in plants
7.8 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Àåfa…™to äÀàh…îÀêrmo än / (AmE), / Àåfa…™t…ô äÀàh…îÀêm…ô än / (BrE)
A substance produced by plants that regulates growth and development, acting as a hormone within the plant.

Examples

A phytohormone substance
Auxin is a well-known phytohormone involved in root development.
Ethylene, a gaseous phytohormone, plays a key role in fruit ripening.
Research on phytohormones has advanced our understanding of plant growth mechanisms.
