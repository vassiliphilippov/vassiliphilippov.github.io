barotrauma

noun
pressure-related injury
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌbæroʊˈtrɔmə / (AmE), / ˌbærəʊˈtrɔːmə / (BrE)
physical injury to body tissues caused by a difference in pressure between inside and outside the body

Examples

Barotrauma to ears
Divers must ascend slowly to avoid barotrauma to their ears and lungs.
Airplane passengers sometimes experience mild barotrauma during takeoff and landing.
Improper use of ventilators can result in barotrauma in patients with lung conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decompression sickness - a condition caused by rapid decrease in pressure, leading to gas bubbles in the body
  • synonym: injury - physical harm or damage to the body
