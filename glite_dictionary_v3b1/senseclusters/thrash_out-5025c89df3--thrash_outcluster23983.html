thrash out

verb
discuss thoroughly to reach agreement
Pronunciation: / θræʃ aʊt / (AmE), / θræʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss a problem or issue thoroughly in order to reach a solution or agreement

Examples

To thrash out a problem
The parties finally thrashed out their differences and agreed on a compromise.
They are still thrashing out a solution to the budget crisis.
We spent several hours trying to thrash out the details of the contract.
Before launching the project, the committee will thrash out the plan in detail.
The parties spent hours trying to thrash out a suitable compromise.
They met late into the night to thrash out their remaining disagreements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hammer out - discuss and negotiate a solution in detail
  • synonym: iron out - to resolve difficulties or disagreements by discussion
  • synonym: work out - to find a solution to a problem by thinking or discussing
