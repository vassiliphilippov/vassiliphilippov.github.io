gastric acid

noun
digestive fluid in the stomach
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæstrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
the digestive fluid produced in the stomach, containing hydrochloric acid and enzymes to help break down food

Examples

Gastric acid in stomach
After eating spicy foods, he felt a burning sensation caused by gastric acid.
Some medications work by reducing the amount of gastric acid produced.
The excessive production of gastric acid can lead to stomach ulcers.
