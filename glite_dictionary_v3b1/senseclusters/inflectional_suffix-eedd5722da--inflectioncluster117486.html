inflectional suffix

noun
suffix marking grammar, not core meaning

Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆflÉ›kÊƒÉ™nl ËˆsÊŒfÉªks / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆflÉ›kÊƒÉ™nÉ™l ËˆsÊŒfÉªks / (BrE)
a suffix that is added to the end of a word to indicate grammatical information, such as tense, number, or case, without changing its core meaning or word class

Examples

An inflectional suffix '-ed'
Adding the inflectional suffix '-ed' to 'walk' forms the past tense 'walked'.
In English, '-s' is an inflectional suffix that indicates plural nouns.
Languages like Spanish use many inflectional suffixes to mark verb conjugations.
