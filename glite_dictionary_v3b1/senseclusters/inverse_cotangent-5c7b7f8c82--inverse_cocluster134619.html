inverse cotangent

noun
the inverse function of cotangent
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌvɝs koʊˈtæn.dʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɜːs kəʊˈtæn.dʒənt / (BrE)
The inverse function of the cotangent; the angle whose cotangent is a given number.

Examples

The inverse cotangent function
In trigonometry, the inverse cotangent function is often symbolized as arccot.
The inverse cotangent is used to find the angle given the cotangent of a value.
To solve the equation, take the inverse cotangent of both sides.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: arctangent - related trigonometric inverse function for tangent
  • antonym: cotangent - the trigonometric function whose inverse is the inverse cotangent
