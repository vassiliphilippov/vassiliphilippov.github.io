Mahayanism

noun
doctrine of Mahayana Buddhism
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.həˈjɑː.nɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌma.hɑːˈjɑː.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the religious doctrine or system of Mahayana Buddhism, emphasizing the ideal of the bodhisattva and universal salvation.

Examples

Mahayanism Buddhist teaching
Mahayanism places strong emphasis on the bodhisattva path and compassion for all beings.
Many scholars study Mahayanism to understand the development of East Asian Buddhism.
The influence of Mahayanism can be seen in the art and literature of several Asian countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Theravada - the more traditional and conservative form of Buddhism
