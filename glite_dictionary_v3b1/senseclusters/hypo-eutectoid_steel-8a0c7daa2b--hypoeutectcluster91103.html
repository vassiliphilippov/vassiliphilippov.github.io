hypo-eutectoid steel

noun
steel with less than 0.9% carbon
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊ juːˈtɛk.tɔɪd stiːl / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊ juːˈtɛk.tɔɪd stiːl / (BrE)
Steel that contains less than 0.9% carbon by weight, resulting in properties and structures distinct from steels with higher carbon content.

Examples

Hypo-eutectoid steel properties
Many structural components are made from hypo-eutectoid steel due to its excellent toughness and weldability.
Quenching and tempering hypo-eutectoid steel can significantly improve its mechanical properties.
The microstructure of hypo-eutectoid steel includes both ferrite and pearlite phases.
