puff up

verb
praise extravagantly
Pronunciation: / pʌf ʌp / (AmE), / pʌf ʌp / (BrE)
to praise someone or something with exaggerated or excessive enthusiasm

Examples

To puff up a review
Critics puffed up the new play, calling it a masterpiece.
Her boss would often puff up her contributions in meetings.
The media tends to puff up celebrities for even small achievements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: belittle - to make something or someone seem unimportant (opposite)
  • synonym: overpraise - to praise excessively
