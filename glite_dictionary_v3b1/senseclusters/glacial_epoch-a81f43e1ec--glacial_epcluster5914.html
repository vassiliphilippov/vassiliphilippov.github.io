glacial epoch

noun
period of extensive glaciation in Earth's history
7.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈgleɪ.ʃəl ˈep.ək/ /; / /ˈgleɪ.ʃɪ.əl ˈep.ək/ /; / /ˈgleɪ.ʃəl ˈiː.pək/ (AmE), /ˈgleɪ.ʃəl ˈiː.pɒk/ /; / /ˈgleɪ.ʃəl ˈe.pɒk/ /; / /ˈgleɪ.ʃɪ.əl ˈe.pɒk/ (BrE)
a period in Earth's history when glaciers covered large areas of the surface

Examples

The glacial epoch
During the last glacial epoch, much of northern Europe was covered in thick ice sheets.
Scientists study sediment samples to understand climate changes during different glacial epochs.
The migration patterns of ancient humans were greatly influenced by the conditions of the glacial epoch.
During a glacial epoch, sea levels drop as water becomes locked in ice sheets.
Many species adapted to survive the harsh conditions of the last glacial epoch.
The Pleistocene is perhaps the best-known glacial epoch in Earth's history.
