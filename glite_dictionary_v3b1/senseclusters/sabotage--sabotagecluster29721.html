sabotage

noun
deliberate destruction or obstruction
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsæbəˌtɑdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsæbətɑːʒ / (BrE)
the deliberate destruction, damage, or obstruction of something, especially for political or military advantage

Examples

An act of sabotage
Evidence of sabotage was found in the damaged communication cables.
The police suspected that the fire was an act of sabotage against the factory.
They increased security after several incidents of sabotage had disrupted production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vandalism - willful or malicious destruction of property
