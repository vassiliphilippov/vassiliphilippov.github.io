omnitude

noun
state of totality
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːmnɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈɒmnɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the state or quality of being all things or encompassing everything; totality

Examples

The omnitude of everything
The philosopher pondered the omnitude of existence.
Some poets try to capture the omnitude of human experience in their work.
The concept of omnitude suggests a unity that includes all things, both seen and unseen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: part - a piece or segment of a whole
  • synonym: totality - the state of being complete or whole
  • synonym: universality - the quality of being universal or applicable to all
