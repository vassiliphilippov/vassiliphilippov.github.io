dragoon

noun
European cavalry soldier (historical)
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /drə.ˈguːn/ (AmE), /drə.ˈguːn/ (BrE)
a member of a European military unit, especially in the 17th and 18th centuries, formerly composed of heavily armed cavalrymen who were trained to fight on foot as well as on horseback

Examples

A dragoon soldier
A squadron of dragoons charged across the field, sabers drawn.
The dragoon dismounted quickly, ready to fight alongside the infantry.
The museum showcased the uniform worn by a British dragoon during the Napoleonic Wars.
