theocratic

adjective
relating to a theocracy or religious government
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌθiːəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌθiːəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a theocracy; governed or influenced by religious authority.

Examples

A theocratic regime
A theocratic regime often bases its laws on sacred texts.
During the Middle Ages, some European states adopted a theocratic system of rule.
The country's theocratic constitution gives ultimate authority to religious leaders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
