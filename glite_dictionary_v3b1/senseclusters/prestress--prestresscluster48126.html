prestress

verb
to apply internal stress
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈstrɛs / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈstrɛs / (BrE)
to apply internal stress to a material or structural element before it is used, usually to increase its strength or performance

Examples

To prestress concrete beams
Engineers prestress concrete beams to help them withstand heavy loads in bridges.
The construction team prestressed the steel cables before pouring the foundation.
It is important to prestress certain materials to prevent cracks and structural failure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reinforce - to strengthen or support with additional material
