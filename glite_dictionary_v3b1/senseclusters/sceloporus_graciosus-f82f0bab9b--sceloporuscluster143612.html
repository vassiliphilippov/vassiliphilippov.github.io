Sceloporus graciosus

noun
a species of western North American lizard
Pronunciation: / skɛˈlɑːpərəs ɡreɪˈsioʊsəs / (AmE), / skɛˈlɒpərəs ɡreɪˈsɪəsəs / (BrE)
A species of small ground-dwelling lizard, known as the sagebrush lizard, found in western United States, especially between the Rocky and Sierra Nevada Mountains.

Examples

Sceloporus graciosus lizard
Researchers studied the behavior of Sceloporus graciosus in the Great Basin region.
The Sceloporus graciosus is commonly found in dry, open habitats with sparse vegetation.
Unlike some lizards, the Sceloporus graciosus remains active during cooler temperatures.
