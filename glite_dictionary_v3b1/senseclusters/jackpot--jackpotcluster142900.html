jackpot

noun
large cash prize; outstanding reward
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒækˌpɑt /; / ˈdʒæk.pɑt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.pɒt / (BrE)
a large cash prize or reward, especially one won in a lottery or gambling game; also, any especially impressive or valued success

Examples

Hit the jackpot
After months of searching for the perfect job, Tom finally hit the career jackpot with an amazing offer.
This year’s science fair offers a $10,000 jackpot to the student with the most innovative project.
Winning the jackpot changed her life overnight, allowing her to travel the world and buy a new house.
Poker players eagerly watched as the jackpot reached its highest level ever.
She dreamed of hitting the jackpot at the casino and becoming rich overnight.
The jackpot grew larger with every ticket that went unsold.

Related Terms

  • antonym: booby prize - a prize given as a joke, typically for last place
  • antonym: consolation prize - a lesser prize awarded to a non-winner
  • synonym: prize - something won in a competition or lottery
  • synonym: windfall - a sudden, unexpected gain or piece of good fortune
  • synonym: winner-take-all - a situation where one winner receives all the accumulated stakes
