infirmity

noun
physical or mental weakness (esp. from age)

physical or mental weakness, especially caused by old age or illness

Examples

Old age infirmity
After his long illness, John struggled with a lingering infirmity that limited his activities.
Despite her physical infirmities, she remained cheerful and active in the community.
The infirmity of old age made it difficult for her to walk unassisted.

Synonyms

  • frailty
    5.0 0.34/m
    frailty is a fragile, age-related loss of strength and balance, while infirmity refers to general physical or mental weakness from illness or age.
  • debility
    8.2 0.11/m
    debility is a medical term for long-lasting physical weakness after illness, while infirmity covers general weakness from age or sickness.
  • feebleness
    4.8 0.02/m
    feebleness shows low physical strength or power and often sounds old-fashioned, while infirmity names medical or age-related weakness.
  • weakness
    1.4 7.48/m
    weakness is a broad lack of strength or ability in body or mind, while infirmity specifically suggests illness or age-caused weakness.
