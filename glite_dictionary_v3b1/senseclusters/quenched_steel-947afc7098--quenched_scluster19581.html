quenched steel

noun
steel hardened by rapid cooling
9.9
Pronunciation: / kwɛntʃt stil / (AmE), / kwɛntʃt stiːl / (BrE)
steel that has been rapidly cooled, usually by immersion in water or oil, to increase its hardness

Examples

Quenched steel tools
Special tools made from quenched steel are resistant to wear and deformation.
The bridge was constructed using quenched steel to ensure maximum strength and durability.
The company specializes in manufacturing quenched steel components for heavy machinery.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: tempered steel - steel that has been reheated and then cooled to reduce brittleness after hardening
