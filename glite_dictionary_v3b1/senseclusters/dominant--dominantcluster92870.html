dominant

noun
fifth note of a diatonic scale (music)
5.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.mə.nənt/ /; / /ˈdɑː.mɪ.nənt/ (AmE), /ˈdɒ.mɪ.nənt/ /; / /ˈdɒ.mə.nənt/ (BrE)
(Music) The fifth note or degree of a diatonic scale, which is important in establishing the key and harmony of a piece of music.

Examples

Dominant note
In the key of C major, the dominant is the note G.
Many classical pieces use the dominant to create tension before returning to the home key.
The melody moved from the tonic to the dominant before resolving.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: subdominant - the fourth note of a diatonic scale, below the dominant
  • antonym: tonic - the first note of a diatonic scale, serving as the home note
