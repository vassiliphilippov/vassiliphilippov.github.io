OER

noun
oxygen effect ratio
Pronunciation: / oʊ iː ɑːr / (AmE), / əʊ iː ɑː / (BrE)
the ratio by which the presence of oxygen increases the biological effect of radiation on cells or tissues

Examples

OER in radiation study
Researchers calculated the OER to determine how oxygen levels affect radiation therapy outcomes.
A higher OER means that oxygen greatly increases the effectiveness of radiation treatment.
The laboratory experiment measured the OER in different types of tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxygen effect - the influence of oxygen on the response of cells to radiation
