diallel

noun
genetic crossing design
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈælɛl / (AmE), / daɪˈælɛl / (BrE)
an experimental design in genetics where all possible crosses are made among a group of genotypes

Examples

A diallel study
The researcher set up a diallel to analyze genetic variation among the crop varieties.
A complete diallel allows scientists to study all possible genetic combinations in a group.
Using a diallel design, the team evaluated the performance of each hybrid cross.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crossbreeding - the process of mating individuals from different breeds or varieties
  • antonym: selfing - the process of self-fertilization in plants or animals
