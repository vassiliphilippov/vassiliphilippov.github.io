tomb

verb
to bury in a tomb
9.3
Pronunciation: /tuːm/ (AmE), /tuːm/ (BrE)
to place a dead body in a tomb or grave, usually as part of a burial ceremony

Examples

To tomb a king
The pharaoh was tombed with many valuable artifacts to accompany him in the afterlife.
After the ceremony, the family tombed their loved one in the ancient mausoleum.
Legends say the treasure was tombed alongside the great warrior centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bury - to place a dead body in the ground
  • synonym: entomb - to place in a tomb, especially with ceremony
  • antonym: exhume - to dig out a body from the ground
