denaturation

noun
loss of natural structure
7.8
Pronunciation: /diː.næ.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.næ.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.næ.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a process in which proteins or nucleic acids lose their natural structure and function due to external factors

Examples

Protein denaturation
High temperatures can cause the denaturation of enzymes in food.
The scientist studied how pH changes led to denaturation of the protein sample.
Alcohol is often used in laboratories to induce denaturation of DNA.

Related Terms

  • antonym: renaturation - the process by which denatured proteins or nucleic acids regain their original structure
