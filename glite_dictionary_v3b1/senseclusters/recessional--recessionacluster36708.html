recessional

noun
hymn sung as clergy and choir exit
7.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a hymn or piece of music sung or played at the end of a religious service while the clergy and choir leave

Examples

Recessional hymn
After the service, the organist played a solemn recessional while the clergy walked down the aisle.
At weddings, couples often select a special recessional to celebrate their exit.
The choir sang a beautiful recessional as the congregation prepared to leave the church.

Related Terms

  • antonym: processional - a hymn or music played at the start of a service as clergy proceed to the altar
