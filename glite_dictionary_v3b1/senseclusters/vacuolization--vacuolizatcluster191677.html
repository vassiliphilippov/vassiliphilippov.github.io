vacuolization

noun
formation of vacuoles in cells or tissue
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌvæk.ju.ə.lɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌvækjuˌɑləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvæk.ju.ə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌvækjuːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state in which vacuoles form or appear within a cell or tissue, often as a result of disease or cellular activity

Examples

Vacuolization in cells
Neuronal vacuolization is often indicative of certain neurodegenerative diseases.
The pathologist noted significant vacuolization in the tissue sample, suggesting cell injury.
There was marked vacuolization of the liver cells observed under the microscope.
Abnormal vacuolisation is commonly seen in certain neurodegenerative diseases.
Extensive vacuolisation of the liver cells was observed under the microscope.
The presence of vacuolisation indicates significant cellular injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vacuole - a small cavity in the cytoplasm of a cell, enclosed by a membrane
