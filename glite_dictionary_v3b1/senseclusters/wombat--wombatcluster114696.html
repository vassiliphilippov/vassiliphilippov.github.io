wombat

noun
Australian burrowing marsupial
5.7 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑmˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈwɒm.bæt / (BrE)
A burrowing herbivorous marsupial native to Australia, about the size of a badger, with short legs and a short tail.

Examples

A wombat animal
During their trip to Australia, they spotted a wild wombat crossing the trail.
The wombat spent hours digging a new burrow beneath the eucalyptus trees.
Unlike kangaroos, the wombat walks on all fours and is active mainly at night.
