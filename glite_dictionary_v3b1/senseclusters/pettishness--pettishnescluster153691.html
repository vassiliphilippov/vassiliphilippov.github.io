pettishness

noun
petty irritability or bad temper
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛtɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily irritated or bad-tempered, especially in a childish or petty way

Examples

Childish pettishness
Even minor setbacks would trigger his pettishness, much to the annoyance of his colleagues.
Her pettishness made it difficult to have a calm discussion during the meeting.
The child's pettishness became evident when she did not get her way.

Related Terms

  • antonym: good-naturedness - the quality of having a pleasant and easygoing temperament
  • synonym: petulance - the quality of being childishly sulky or bad-tempered
