discourse

verb
speak or write formally; discuss
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌkɔrs /; / dɪsˈkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkɔːs /; / dɪsˈkɔːs / (BrE)
to speak or write at length or in detail about a topic, especially in a formal way

Examples

To discourse on ideas
She enjoyed discoursing with her colleagues on philosophical topics.
The professor began to discourse on the consequences of climate change.
They discoursed at length about their plans for the future.
During the seminar, the professor discoursed at length on the impact of technology in society.
Historians often discourse about the causes of major world events.
She discoursed eloquently on the themes of justice and morality in her essay.
During dinner, the guests would often discourse at length about philosophy and science.
She began to discourse on the importance of environmental conservation to the assembled audience.
The professor was invited to discourse on medieval literature at the conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: converse - to engage in conversation
  • synonym: debate - to argue about a subject, especially in a formal manner
  • synonym: discuss - to talk about something with others
  • synonym: dissertate - to deliver a formal or extended discourse
  • synonym: lecture - to give an educational talk to an audience
