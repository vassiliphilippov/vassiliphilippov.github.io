induction

noun
the act of initiating a process or state
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of starting or causing something to begin, especially as an initial stage

Examples

Medical induction
Rapid induction of labor was necessary for the health of the mother and child.
The induction of chemical reactions can be triggered by light or heat.
The induction of sleep using medication is common before surgery.
His induction into the role triggered a series of positive changes in the department.
The induction of new policies led to a more efficient workflow across the company.
The scientist's discovery resulted in the induction of numerous studies in the same field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: initiation - the action of beginning something
  • antonym: termination - the act of bringing something to an end
