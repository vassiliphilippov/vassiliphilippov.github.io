Bonferroni correction

noun
statistical adjustment
Pronunciation: / ˌbɑːnfəˈroʊni kəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɒnfəˈrəʊni kəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
a statistical adjustment used to reduce the chance of false positives when making multiple comparisons or tests

Examples

The Bonferroni correction method
The researchers used the Bonferroni correction to adjust for multiple comparisons in their analysis.
Applying a Bonferroni correction helps prevent false positives when testing several hypotheses at once.
Many scientific studies rely on the Bonferroni correction to ensure their results are statistically valid.
Researchers applied the Bonferroni correction to control for errors in their multiple comparisons.
Using a Bonferroni correction helps ensure that significant results are not due to chance.
The Bonferroni correction is commonly used in clinical trials with several outcome measures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: false discovery rate - the expected proportion of false positives among all significant results
  • synonym: multiple testing correction - a method to adjust significance levels when performing several statistical tests
