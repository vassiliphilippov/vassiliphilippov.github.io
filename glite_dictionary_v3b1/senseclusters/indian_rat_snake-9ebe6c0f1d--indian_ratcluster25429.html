Indian rat snake

noun
large non-venomous Asian snake
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ræt sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ræt sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, non-venomous snake (Ptyas mucosa), native to South and Southeast Asia, known for entering buildings in search of prey

Examples

The Indian rat snake
An Indian rat snake slithered into the kitchen while chasing a mouse.
Farmers often welcome the presence of Indian rat snakes because they help control pests around the property.
Many residents have reported sightings of Indian rat snakes in urban areas during the rainy season.
