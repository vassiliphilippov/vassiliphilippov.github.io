touchy-feely

adjective
openly expressive of emotion or affection
3.3 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtʌtʃ.iː ˈfiː.liː/ (AmE), /ˈtʌtʃ.iː ˈfiː.liː/ (BrE)
openly or excessively showing emotions and affection, especially in a way that may make others uncomfortable

Examples

A touchy-feely person
His touchy-feely approach to management wasn't appreciated by everyone in the office.
She finds all those touchy-feely team-building exercises uncomfortable and unnecessary.
The charity event took on a touchy-feely atmosphere, with plenty of hugs and emotional speeches.
His management style is considered too touchy-feely for the fast-paced corporate environment.
She prefers direct feedback and dislikes touchy-feely conversations at work.
The team-building exercise became touchy-feely when everyone was asked to share their deepest fears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affectionate - showing fondness or tenderness physically or emotionally
  • antonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically
  • synonym: sentimental - having or showing excessive emotions or feelings
