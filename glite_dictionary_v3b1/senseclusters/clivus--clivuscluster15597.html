clivus

noun
sloping street
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪvəs / (AmE), / ˈklaɪvəs / (BrE)
a sloping street or incline, especially one named or used in ancient Rome

Examples

Roman clivus street
The ancient clivus was lined with statues and led directly to the city gate.
Archaeologists discovered remnants of a Roman clivus beneath the modern street.
Pilgrims walked up the steep clivus to reach the temple at the top of the hill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain
  • antonym: plain - a large area of flat land with few trees
  • synonym: slope - an area of ground that slopes upward or downward
