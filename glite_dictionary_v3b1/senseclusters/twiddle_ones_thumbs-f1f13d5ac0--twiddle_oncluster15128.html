twiddle one's thumbs

verb
do nothing while waiting; be idle
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪd.əl wʌnz θʌmz / (AmE), / ˈtwɪd.əl wʌnz θʌmz / (BrE)
to do nothing or be idle, often while waiting for something to happen

Examples

To twiddle your thumbs idly
Don't just twiddle your thumbs—help me set up the chairs for the event.
He refused to twiddle his thumbs when there was work that could be done.
While waiting for the meeting to start, she just twiddled her thumbs and stared out the window.

Related Terms

  • antonym: busy oneself - to occupy oneself; to do something useful
  • synonym: idle - not active; doing nothing
