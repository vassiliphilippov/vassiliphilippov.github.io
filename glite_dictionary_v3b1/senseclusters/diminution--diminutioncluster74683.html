diminution

noun
the act of reducing or decreasing
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪməˈnuːʃən /; / ˌdɪməˈnuːʃən / (AmE), / dɪmɪˈnjuːʃən /; / ˌdɪmɪˈnjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something smaller or less; a reduction in size, amount, or importance

Examples

Diminution in value
The company reported a diminution in profits over the last quarter.
The new measures led to a diminution in air pollution levels across the city.
There has been a noticeable diminution of support for the government in recent months.
A diminution in enthusiasm among team members affected the project's progress.
The company reported a diminution of profits due to rising costs.
There has been a noticeable diminution in the size of the polar ice caps over recent decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym: increase - the process of becoming or making greater in size or amount
  • synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
