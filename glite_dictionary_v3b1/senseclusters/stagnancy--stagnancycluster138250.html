stagnancy

noun
state of being stagnant, especially in liquids
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡnənsi /; / ˈstæɡ.nən.si / (AmE), / ˈstaɡnənsi /; / ˈstæɡ.nən.si / (BrE)
a state of not moving, changing, or developing; lack of activity or progress

Examples

Economic stagnancy
Economic stagnancy can harm a country's development and prosperity.
The stagnancy of the pond encouraged the growth of algae and mosquitoes.
The water's stagnancy after the flood posed a health risk to residents.
After years of stagnancy, the town finally saw a burst of new business.
Periods of economic stagnancy can lead to increased unemployment and frustration.
The stagnancy in the art market worried gallery owners and artists alike.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activity - the state of being active, energetic action
  • synonym: stagnation - the state of not flowing or moving; lack of activity or growth
