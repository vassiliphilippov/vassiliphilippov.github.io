germ plasm

noun
hereditary material in germ cells
Pronunciation: / dʒɜrm ˌplæzəm / (AmE), / dʒɜːm ˌplɑːzəm / (BrE)
the protoplasm in germ cells containing chromosomes and genes, considered responsible for heredity in many organisms

Examples

Germ plasm cells
August Weismann proposed that only germ plasm carries heritable information between generations.
Researchers isolated the germ plasm to study genetic transmission in fruit flies.
The concept of germ plasm was central to early theories of heredity.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cytoplasm - the material within a cell, excluding the nucleus
  • synonym: germ cell - a reproductive cell that gives rise to gametes
  • degree higher: nucleus - the central part of a cell containing genetic material
