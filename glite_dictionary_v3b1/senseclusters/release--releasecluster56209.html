release

noun
device that disengages a mechanism
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlis / (AmE), / rɪˈliːs / (BrE)
a device, such as a button or lever, that causes part of a mechanism to operate or become disengaged when pressed or activated

Examples

Camera release button
She pressed the release to open the camera back for loading film.
The emergency release must be accessible in case the door gets stuck.
This model features a quick release for easy removal of the battery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catch - a device that holds something in place and releases it
