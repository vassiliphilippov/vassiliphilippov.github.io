quassia family

noun
family of tropical trees and shrubs (Simaroubaceae)
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːsiə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkwæsɪə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs with bitter bark, usually producing dry, one-seeded winged fruit; the botanical family Simaroubaceae.

Examples

The quassia family trees
Botanists classify the tree as part of the quassia family due to its winged fruit.
Many shrubs in the quassia family are found in tropical forests around the world.
The bitter bark of many trees in the quassia family is used to make herbal medicines.
