Spalacidae

noun
family of burrowing rodents
Pronunciation: / spəˈlæsəˌdi / (AmE), / spəˈlasɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing rodents, including blind mole-rats, bamboo rats, root rats, and zokors, found mainly in Eurasia and Africa.

Examples

Spalacidae family rodents
Biologists study Spalacidae to understand the evolution of burrowing behavior.
Many members of Spalacidae are known for their reduced eyesight due to subterranean living.
The family Spalacidae includes several species adapted to life underground.
