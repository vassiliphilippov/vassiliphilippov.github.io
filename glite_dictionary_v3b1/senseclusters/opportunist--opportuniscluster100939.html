opportunist

adjective
taking advantage of opportunities, often unethically
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpərˈtuːnɪst / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪst / (BrE)
Taking immediate advantage of any opportunity, often without considering principles or ethics.

Examples

Opportunist behavior
In politics, an opportunist strategy often attracts criticism for disregarding ethics.
Some viewed his opportunist behavior as shrewd, while others saw it as dishonest.
The opportunist approach of the company led to several questionable business decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opportunistic - using opportunities to gain personal advantage, often at the expense of ethics
  • antonym: principled - acting according to moral values or principles
