proventriculus

noun
insect digestive organ
9.9
Pronunciation: /prə.ven.ˈtrɪ.kjʊ.ləs/ /; / /proʊ.ven.ˈtrɪ.kjʊ.ləs/ (AmE), /prə.ven.ˈtrɪ.kjə.ləs/ /; / /prə.ven.ˈtrɪ.kjʊ.ləs/ (BrE)
a muscular part of the digestive tract in insects and some other invertebrates, located between the crop and midgut, that grinds or filters food

Examples

Insect proventriculus
The scientist dissected the ant to observe its proventriculus under the microscope.
Food passes from the crop into the proventriculus before reaching the midgut in many insects.
Damage to the proventriculus can affect how efficiently an insect digests its food.
