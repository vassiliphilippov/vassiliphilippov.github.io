Ericaceae

noun
the heath or heather plant family
9.9
Pronunciation: /er.ɪ.ˈkeɪ.siː.iː/ /; / /ə.rɪ.ˈkeɪ.siː.iː/ (AmE), /e.rɪ.ˈkeɪ.siː.iː/ /; / /ə.rɪ.ˈkeɪ.siː.iː/ (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, commonly known as the heath or heather family, that includes heathers, blueberries, rhododendrons, and similar shrubs.

Examples

Ericaceae plant family
Blueberries are a fruit produced by shrubs in the Ericaceae family.
Many garden plants, such as azaleas and rhododendrons, belong to the Ericaceae.
Members of the Ericaceae family thrive in acidic soils and are often found in heathlands and forests.
