noil

noun
textile from short fibers
9.9
Pronunciation: /nɔɪl/ (AmE), /nɔɪl/ (BrE)
short fibers left over after processing silk or other textiles, or the fabric or yarn made from these fibers, often with a textured appearance

Examples

Silk noil fabric
The designer chose soft noil for the summer dresses because of its textured appearance.
Silk noil is popular for its natural look and comfortable feel.
The artisan wove a scarf using noil yarn to create a unique, nubby texture.
The artisan chose noil to create a fabric with a unique, nubby texture.
Because noil is made from shorter fibers, the resulting cloth feels less smooth than pure silk.
Some eco-friendly brands use noil to reduce waste in textile production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slub - a lump or thick place in yarn or fabric, often giving a textured appearance
