absorption unit

noun
unit of measurement for absorption
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔr.pʃən ˈjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːp.ʃən ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the amount of absorption of electromagnetic radiation by a substance

Examples

One absorption unit
One absorption unit is often used to quantify how much light a sample absorbs in spectrophotometry.
Scientists compare absorption units between samples to determine concentration levels.
The instrument reported an increase of several absorption units after the sample was introduced.
