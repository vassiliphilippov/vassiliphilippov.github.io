chunk

verb
group together indiscriminately
4.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /tʃʌŋk/ (AmE), /tʃʌŋk/ (BrE)
to group or organize items together into larger units or sections

Examples

To chunk information
Analysts often chunk similar data points instead of examining them separately.
It's unhelpful to chunk every complaint as the same issue.
The teacher decided to chunk all late assignments into one folder.
Teachers often chunk information so that students can remember it more easily.
The software can chunk similar files together to make searching easier.
You should chunk your work into smaller pieces to avoid feeling overwhelmed.
