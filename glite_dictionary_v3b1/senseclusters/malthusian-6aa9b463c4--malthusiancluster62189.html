Malthusian

noun
believer in Malthusian population theory
7.4 8.3
Pronunciation: /mæl.ˈθuː.zɪ.ən/ /; / /mæl.ˈθjuː.zɪ.ən/ (AmE), /mæl.ˈθjuː.zɪ.ən/ /; / /mæl.ˈθuː.zɪ.ən/ (BrE)
A person who believes in the population theory proposed by Thomas Malthus, which suggests that population growth tends to outstrip the growth of resources, leading to social and economic problems.

Examples

A strict Malthusian
As a dedicated Malthusian, he argued that unchecked population growth would inevitably cause famine.
Debates between the Malthusian economists and their critics shaped modern demography.
She described herself as a cautious Malthusian, supporting policies for sustainable development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Neo-Malthusian - someone who adapts Malthus's theory for modern contexts, focusing on population control policies
