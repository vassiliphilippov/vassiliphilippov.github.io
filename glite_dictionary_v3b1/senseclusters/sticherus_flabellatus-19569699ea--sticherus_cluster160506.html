Sticherus flabellatus

noun
Australasian fern with fanlike fronds
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪkərəs fləˈbɛlətəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪtʃərəs fləˈbɛlətəs / (BrE)
A large fern native to Australasia with fan-shaped, repeatedly forked fronds; sometimes classified in the genus Gleichenia.

Examples

Sticherus flabellatus fern plant
Sticherus flabellatus often grows abundantly in moist, shaded forests of eastern Australia.
Some botanists debate whether Sticherus flabellatus should instead be placed in the genus Gleichenia.
The delicate, fan-shaped fronds of Sticherus flabellatus make it popular among fern enthusiasts.
