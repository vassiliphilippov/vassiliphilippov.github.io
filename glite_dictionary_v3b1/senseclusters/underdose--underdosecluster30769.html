underdose

noun
insufficient dose
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌdoʊs / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌdəʊs / (BrE)
an amount of a drug or treatment that is less than the required or effective dose

Examples

An underdose of medicine
The patient received an underdose of antibiotics, which failed to clear the infection.
Doctors warn that an underdose can be as harmful as an overdose in some cases.
An underdose of pain medication left her still feeling uncomfortable after surgery.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dose - a measured amount of a drug or medicine
  • antonym: overdose - an excessive or dangerous amount of a drug or treatment
