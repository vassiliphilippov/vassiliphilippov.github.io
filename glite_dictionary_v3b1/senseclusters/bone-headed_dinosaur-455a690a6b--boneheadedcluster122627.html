bone-headed dinosaur

noun
herbivorous dinosaur with thick bony skull crown
Pronunciation: / boʊn ˈhɛdɪd ˈdaɪnəˌsɔr / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈhɛdɪd ˈdaɪnəsɔː / (BrE)
a bipedal herbivorous dinosaur characterized by a thick, domed bony crown on its skull, especially of the family Pachycephalosauridae

Examples

A herd of bone-headed dinosaurs
Many scientists believe that the bone-headed dinosaur roamed in herds during the late Cretaceous period.
Paleontologists discovered a partial skeleton of a bone-headed dinosaur in the Montana badlands.
The bone-headed dinosaur was known for its thick skull, possibly used for head-butting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pachycephalosaur - herbivorous dinosaur with a thick domed skull
