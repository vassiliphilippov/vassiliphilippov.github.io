yellow spot

noun
central retinal area (macula) for sharp vision
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ spɑt / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ spɒt / (BrE)
a small yellowish central area of the retina, rich in cones, responsible for clear, detailed vision

Examples

Eye yellow spot
Damage to the yellow spot can lead to central vision loss.
Ophthalmologists often examine the yellow spot when diagnosing retinal diseases.
The yellow spot is essential for tasks such as reading and recognizing faces.
