noncumulative

adjective
not accumulating dividends
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈkjuːmjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈkjuːmjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
describing a security, especially preferred stock, whose unpaid dividends do not accumulate and are not paid in the future

Examples

Noncumulative preferred shares
The company issued noncumulative preferred shares, so missed dividends would not be paid later.
Investors should check if their preferred stock is noncumulative before making a purchase.
With noncumulative dividends, shareholders cannot claim unpaid amounts from previous years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cumulative - describing something that increases or builds up over time
