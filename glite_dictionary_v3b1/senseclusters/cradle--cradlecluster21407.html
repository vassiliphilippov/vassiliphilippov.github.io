cradle

noun
rocking trough used by gold miners
2.3 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkreɪ.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈkreɪ.dəl/ (BrE)
a box-like trough that can be rocked back and forth, used by gold miners to separate gold from earth and gravel in water

Examples

Gold cradle
A well-made cradle could process far more dirt than simple panning.
Early prospectors often relied on a cradle to find gold in riverbeds.
The old miner loaded soil into the cradle and began separating gold from the silt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rocker - another term for a miner's cradle; a rocking mining device for separating gold
