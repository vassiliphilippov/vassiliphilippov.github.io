flecainide

noun
an antiarrhythmic medication
Pronunciation: / fləˈkeɪnaɪd / (AmE), / flɛˈkaɪnaɪd / (BrE)
a medication used to treat certain types of serious irregular heartbeats (arrhythmias), often as a last resort

Examples

Flecainide medication
After experiencing frequent palpitations, her doctor prescribed flecainide to help regulate her heartbeat.
Patients taking flecainide require close monitoring due to possible serious side effects.
The cardiologist explained that flecainide should only be used when other treatments have not worked.
