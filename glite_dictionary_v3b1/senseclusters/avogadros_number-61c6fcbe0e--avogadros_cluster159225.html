Avogadro's number

noun
the number of entities in a mole; about 6.022 × 10²³
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌævəˈɡɑdroʊz ˌnʌmbər / (AmE), / ˌævəˈɡædrəʊz ˌnʌmbə / (BrE)
The number of elementary entities (such as atoms or molecules) in one mole of a substance, approximately 6.022 × 10²³.

Examples

One Avogadro's number
One mole of any substance contains Avogadro's number of particles.
Scientists use Avogadro's number to perform calculations in chemistry and physics.
The mass of a mole of carbon atoms is determined by Avogadro's number.
