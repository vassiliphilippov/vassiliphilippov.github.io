trunkless

adjective
without a trunk
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌŋkləs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋkləs / (BrE)
lacking a trunk, especially the main part of a body, tree, or structure

Examples

Trunkless statue remains
The ancient statue was trunkless, with only its legs remaining on the sand.
A trunkless tree stood in the field, its branches sprouting directly from the roots.
The artist sculpted a trunkless figure to symbolize incompleteness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trunked - having a trunk or main central part
