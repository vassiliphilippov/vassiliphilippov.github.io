graft

noun
joining of plant parts or tissues
7.2
Pronunciation: / ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
the act or process of joining parts of plants together so they grow as one, or a piece of plant tissue joined in this way

Examples

A rose graft
A well-performed graft can produce hardier fruit trees.
She studied the technique of graft in her botany class.
The graft of the rose cutting onto the rootstock was successful.
The gardener attached the graft to the rootstock to create a stronger tree.
After a few weeks, the graft began to grow and produce new leaves.
Successful grafts can combine the best qualities of two different plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cutting - a piece of a plant used to grow a new plant
  • synonym: scion - a young shoot or twig used for grafting onto another plant
