grace

noun
kindness and compassion; mercy
5.4
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ɡreɪs / (BrE)
kindness, compassion, or forgiveness, especially as unearned favor (including in religious contexts)

Examples

Show grace to others
Even in defeat, the athlete showed remarkable grace towards his competitors.
She accepted the criticism with grace and humility, never blaming others.
The queen's grace was evident in her generous pardon of those who opposed her.
Many Christians believe that salvation is attained only through grace and not by works alone.
She thanked God for the grace that helped her through difficult times.
The concept of divine grace is central to many Christian doctrines.
After confessing his sins, he felt he was living once more in a state of grace.
She believed that her recovery was only possible through God's grace.
The priest spoke about the importance of grace in achieving spiritual growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cruelty - lack of compassion or kindness
  • synonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone
  • antonym: sin - an immoral act considered to be a transgression against divine law
