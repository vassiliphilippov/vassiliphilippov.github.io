backstop

verb
act as support or backup
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækstɒp / (BrE)
to provide support, protection, or a safety net; to act as a backup in case the primary system or plan fails

Examples

To backstop a plan
A reserve fund was created to backstop emergency repairs in the city.
She backstopped the team by double-checking all the calculations.
The government agreed to backstop the banks if the crisis worsens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: support - to give assistance or help to someone or something
