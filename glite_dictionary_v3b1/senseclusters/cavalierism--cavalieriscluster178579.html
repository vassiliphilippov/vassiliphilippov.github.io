cavalierism

noun
gallant manner
9.9
Pronunciation: /kæv.ə.ˈlɪr.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /kæv.ə.ˈlɪə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a manner or style characterized by gallantry, courtliness, or dashing bravado, especially as associated with cavaliers.

Examples

Heroic cavalierism
The novel's hero displayed a certain cavalierism that made him both charming and unpredictable.
His cavalierism at the ball impressed many of the guests with his bold and courteous manners.
Some critics saw his cavalierism as outdated, while others admired his old-fashioned style.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boorishness - rude or insensitive behavior
  • synonym: chivalry - the qualities of an ideal knight, such as bravery and courtesy
  • synonym: gallantry - polite and brave behavior, especially by men towards women
