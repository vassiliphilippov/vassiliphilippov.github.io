ethanal

noun
a volatile liquid aldehyde (acetaldehyde)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθəˌnæl / (AmE), / ˈiːθəˌnæl / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, water-soluble liquid aldehyde (CH₃CHO), mainly used in making acetic acid, perfumes, and drugs

Examples

Ethanal chemical compound
Ethanal is an important intermediate in the synthesis of acetic acid.
Exposure to high concentrations of ethanal can irritate the eyes and respiratory system.
Perfume manufacturers use ethanal because of its distinctive scent and volatility.
