epiglottal

noun
consonant sound type
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈɡlɑtəl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtəl / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by constricting the area around the epiglottis in the vocal tract

Examples

An epiglottal consonant
The rare language includes an epiglottal that is difficult for most speakers to pronounce.
Linguists recorded the use of an epiglottal in several indigenous dialects.
Producing an epiglottal requires precise control of the throat muscles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pharyngeal - a consonant sound produced in the pharynx
