dematerialization

noun
conversion to electronic form
Pronunciation: / diˌmətɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌməˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting physical certificates or records, especially financial securities, into electronic form

Examples

Stock dematerialization
The dematerialization of stock certificates has made trading faster and more secure.
Many companies encourage dematerialization to reduce paperwork and improve efficiency.
With dematerialization, investors can now manage their portfolios entirely online.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digitization - the process of converting information into a digital format
  • antonym: materialization - the process of becoming physical or tangible
  • synonym: paperless - using electronic documents instead of paper
