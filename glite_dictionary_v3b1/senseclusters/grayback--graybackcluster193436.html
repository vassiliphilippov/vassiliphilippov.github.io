grayback

noun
louse or Confederate soldier
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbæk /; / ˈɡreɪˌbak / (BrE)
a louse, especially one infesting soldiers during wartime; also used for certain fish or, historically, Confederate soldiers in the American Civil War

Examples

A grayback louse
During the war, many soldiers complained about graybacks infesting their uniforms.

Historians sometimes refer to Confederate soldiers as graybacks in old documents.
A lone greyback was captured and brought into the Union camp for questioning.
Stories of the bravery of the greybacks were told for generations in the South.
The Union lines were soon attacked by a group of greybacks moving through the woods.
