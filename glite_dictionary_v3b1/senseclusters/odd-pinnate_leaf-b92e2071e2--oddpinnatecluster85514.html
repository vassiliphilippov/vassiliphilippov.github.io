odd-pinnate leaf

noun
pinnate leaf with a terminal leaflet
9.1 7.4
Pronunciation: /ɑːd ˈpɪn.ət liːf/ /; / /ɑːd ˈpɪn.eɪt liːf/ (AmE), /ɒd ˈpɪn.ət liːf/ /; / /ɒd ˈpɪn.eɪt liːf/ (BrE)
A pinnate leaf with a single terminal leaflet at the apex, resulting in an odd number of leaflets.

Examples

An odd-pinnate leaf
Botanists distinguish between even-pinnate and odd-pinnate leaf forms when classifying certain species.
The ash tree is known for its odd-pinnate leaf structure, with a single leaflet at the tip.
You can identify an odd-pinnate leaf by counting the leaflets—there will always be an odd number.
