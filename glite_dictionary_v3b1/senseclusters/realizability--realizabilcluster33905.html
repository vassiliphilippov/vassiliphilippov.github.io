realizability

noun
possibility of achievement
Pronunciation: / ˌriːələˌzaɪləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be made real, achieved, or put into practice

Examples

Realizability of a plan
The realizability of the project depends on the available resources and time.
Before investing, they assessed the realizability of the proposed business plan.
Some ideas are creative, but their realizability is limited by current technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feasibility - the possibility that something can be done or achieved
  • antonym: impossibility - the state of not being able to be done or achieved
