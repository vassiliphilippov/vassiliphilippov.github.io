disparate

adjective
fundamentally different; distinct
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪspərət /; / ˈdɪspərət, dɪˈspærət / (AmE), / ˈdɪspərət /; / ˈdɪspərət, dɪˈspærət / (BrE)
essentially different in kind; not able to be compared

Examples

Two disparate ideas
Her interests are so disparate that it's hard to find common ground.
The museum displays disparate works ranging from classical sculptures to modern installations.
The survey compared disparate groups from across the country.
Her art collection features disparate styles, ranging from traditional landscapes to abstract sculptures.
Scientists often collaborate across disparate fields to solve complex problems.
The committee was composed of disparate individuals from various professional backgrounds.
