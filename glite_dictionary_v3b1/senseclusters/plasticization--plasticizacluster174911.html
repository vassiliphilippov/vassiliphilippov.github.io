plasticization

noun
permanent deformation process
8.9
Pronunciation: /plæs.tɪ.saɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /plæs.tə.saɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /plæs.tɪ.saɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /plæs.tə.saɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a solid material becomes permanently deformed and loses its ability to return to its original shape under stress

Examples

Plasticization under stress
The plasticization of polymers allows manufacturers to create flexible plastic products.
Repeated stress led to the plasticization of the metal beam, causing it to bend permanently.
Understanding plasticization is important in materials science and engineering design.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deformation - the process of changing shape, especially permanently, under force
  • antonym: elasticity - the ability of a material to return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed
