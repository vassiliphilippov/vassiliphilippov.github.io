native

adjective
original inhabitant of a place
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪ.t̬ɪv /; / ˈneɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
belonging to or originating from a particular place by birth or natural development (of people, things, or language)

Examples

Native language speaker
Many native tribes have lived in the Amazon rainforest for thousands of years.
Museums often display the art and artifacts of native cultures.
The native people of the island still speak their traditional language.
After ten years away, he returned to his native country to visit family.
Although she has lived abroad for years, her native language is Spanish.
She spoke proudly of her native city and its rich history.
Several native speakers volunteered to help teach the language course.
The native wildlife of Australia is unlike that found anywhere else in the world.
This flower is not native to Europe and was introduced by early settlers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foreign - not belonging to the place where a person was born
  • synonym: indigenous - originating or occurring naturally in a place; native
