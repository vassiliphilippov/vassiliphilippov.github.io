prophage

noun
dormant phage genome
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌfeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
the dormant form of a bacteriophage's genetic material inside a bacterial cell, which can later become active

Examples

Dormant prophage form
The prophage remains inactive in the bacterial cell until certain conditions trigger its activation.
Scientists study how a prophage can switch from a dormant state to producing new viruses.
Some bacteria carry a prophage in their DNA, which can influence their behavior and resistance.
A prophage can stay inactive in a bacterial cell for many generations before becoming active.
Scientists study how a prophage may influence the traits of its bacterial host.
When triggered, the prophage can exit the bacterial genome and begin producing new viruses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteriophage - a virus that infects and replicates within bacteria
  • antonym: lytic cycle - the process where a virus actively replicates and destroys the host cell
  • synonym: virus - a microscopic infectious agent that replicates only inside living cells
