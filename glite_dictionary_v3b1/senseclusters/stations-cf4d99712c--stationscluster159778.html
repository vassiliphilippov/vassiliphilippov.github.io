Stations

noun
Roman Catholic devotional prayers commemorating Jesus' path to crucifixion
6.8 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈsteɪʃənz / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a devotion consisting of fourteen prayers recited while meditating on a series of images or carvings representing the stages of Jesus' journey to crucifixion.

Examples

The Stations of the Cross
Many worshippers pray the Stations during Lent as a way to reflect on Christ's suffering.
Our church held a special service to walk the Stations on Good Friday.
She paused before each of the fourteen Stations, meditating deeply on their significance.
