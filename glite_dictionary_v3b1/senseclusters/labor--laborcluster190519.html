labor

noun
a task or piece of work
3.7 1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbɚ /; / ˈleɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə /; / ˈleɪ.bə / (BrE)
work or effort, especially physical work or a task that requires effort

Examples

Hard labor at work
Completing the bridge was a massive labor that required teamwork and persistence.
The scientist devoted years of labor to his groundbreaking research.
Their greatest labor was rebuilding the village after the storm.
After a day of intense labor, she was exhausted but satisfied.
His labor was rewarded with a fair wage and a sense of pride.
Many immigrants found labor in factories during the early 20th century.

He regarded the restoration of the old house as his greatest labour.
Writing the manuscript was a true labour of love.
