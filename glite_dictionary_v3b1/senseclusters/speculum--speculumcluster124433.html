speculum

noun
mirror in optical instruments
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛkjələm / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊləm / (BrE)
a mirror, typically made of polished metal, used in optical instruments such as telescopes to reflect light

Examples

Telescope speculum
Astronomers once crafted the speculum by hand to ensure maximum reflectivity.
The antique telescope contained a speculum made of highly polished metal.
Unlike glass mirrors, a metal speculum was prone to tarnishing over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mirror - a reflective surface, usually glass coated with a metal amalgam, that reflects a clear image
