(in molecular biology/biochemistry) a molecule, typically a protein or enzyme complex, that directly carries out or executes a biochemical action in a biological pathway (for example, a nuclease that cleaves DNA).

Examples

Protein effector
The CRISPR effector protein Cas9 cleaves target DNA to edit genes.
Pathogens secrete effectors that manipulate host cells and promote infection.
Researchers identified a novel effector whose nuclease activity destroys viral genomes in vitro.
