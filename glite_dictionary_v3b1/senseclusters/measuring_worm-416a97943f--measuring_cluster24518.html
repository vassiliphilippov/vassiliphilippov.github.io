measuring worm

noun
caterpillar of Geometridae moths, moves in loops
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛʒərɪŋ wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒərɪŋ wəːm / (BrE)
a small hairless caterpillar, especially of the family Geometridae, with legs only on its front and rear segments, noted for its characteristic looping movement

Examples

A measuring worm moves
Gardeners sometimes find measuring worms eating the leaves of ornamental plants.
The measuring worm moves in a distinct looping motion as it inches along the branch.
The unique gait of the measuring worm helps it blend in with twigs and stems.
