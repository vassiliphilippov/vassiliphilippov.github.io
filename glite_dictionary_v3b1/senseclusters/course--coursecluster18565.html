course

verb
to flow or move, especially of liquids
4.8 1.82/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrs / (AmE), / k…îÀês / (BrE)
to move or flow steadily and smoothly, especially over a distance (used for liquids, clouds, etc.)

Examples

Water courses through pipes
After the rain, water coursed along the street, carrying leaves and debris.
Blood coursed through his veins as his heart raced.
Tears began to course down her cheeks as she listened to the sad news.
Clouds coursed swiftly across the evening sky, driven by a strong wind.
The crowd watched as the horses coursed across the open field.
The river coursed through the deep valley after the heavy rain.

Synonyms

  • flow
    1.0 23/m
    flow moves steadily and continuously like a river or air, while course implies a smooth, directed movement over distance.
  • stream
    1.7 5.66/m
    stream moves continuously in a steady flow often in large amounts, while course emphasizes smooth movement along a set path.
  • run
    2.7 21/m
    run moves or spreads like a liquid and can suggest fast or messy movement, while course stresses smooth, steady travel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flow - to move steadily and continuously in a current or stream
  • synonym: race - to move or progress swiftly or at full speed
  • synonym: travel - to make a journey, typically of some length or abroad
