lynchpin

noun
a pin holding a wheel on an axle
6.7 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪntʃ.pɪn/ (AmE), /ˈlɪntʃ.pɪn/ (BrE)
A pin inserted through the end of an axle to keep a wheel in position.

Examples

Axle lynchpin
Before setting out, Tom checked that each lynchpin was securely fastened to the axle.
The wagon's wheel almost fell off when the lynchpin broke during the ride.
Without a proper lynchpin, the cart wheel would have slipped off easily.
