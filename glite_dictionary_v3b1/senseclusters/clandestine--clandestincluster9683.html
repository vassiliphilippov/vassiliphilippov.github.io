clandestine

adjective
kept secret, often for illegal or unauthorized reasons
6.6
Pronunciation: / klænˈdɛstɪn / (AmE), / klænˈdɛstɪn / (BrE)
Kept secret or done secretly, especially because it is illicit or unauthorized.

Examples

A clandestine meeting
Her involvement in clandestine activities led to her sudden disappearance.
The organization held clandestine meetings to discuss their plans in private.
The report uncovered a network of clandestine operations taking place across the border.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret or hidden
  • synonym: secret - not known or seen or not meant to be known or seen by others
