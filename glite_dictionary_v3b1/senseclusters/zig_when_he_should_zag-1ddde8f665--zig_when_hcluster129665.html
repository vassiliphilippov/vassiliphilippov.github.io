zig when he should zag

verb
make the wrong move or choice
Pronunciation: / zɪɡ wɛn hi ʃəd zæɡ / (AmE), / zɪɡ wɛn hi ʃəd zæɡ / (BrE)
to make the wrong move or choice, especially by acting contrary to what is needed in a situation

Examples

To zig when he should zag
Every time he faced an important decision, he would zig when he should zag.
Sometimes in life, we all zig when we should zag and learn from it.
The company's new CEO seemed to zig when he should zag during the merger talks.

  • synonym: zigzag - move forward by going sharply left and right, as in a zigzag path
