Eragrostis curvula

noun
species of perennial South African grass
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛrəˌɡrɑstɪs kɜrˈvjuːlə / (AmE), / ɛrəˌɡrɒstɪs kɜːˈvjuːlə / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to South Africa, with densely clumped flexible stems, widely introduced for erosion control, especially in the United States.

Examples

Eragrostis curvula grass
Eragrostis curvula is often planted along highways to prevent soil erosion.
Many farmers use Eragrostis curvula as a forage grass for livestock in arid regions.
The rapid growth of Eragrostis curvula makes it effective for quickly stabilizing disturbed land.
