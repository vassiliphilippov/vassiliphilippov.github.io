consumable

noun
item used up
Pronunciation: / kənˈsuːməbəl / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːməbəl / (BrE)
an item that is intended to be used up and replaced regularly, such as office supplies or food

Examples

Office consumables
The company ordered more consumables like paper and toner for the office.
Food and cleaning products are typical consumables in any household.
Hospitals must keep a steady supply of medical consumables for daily operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose, usually not used up
  • synonym: supply - an amount of something available for use
