murderous intent

noun
intention to kill or seriously harm
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝːdərəs ɪnˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdərəs ɪnˈtɛnt / (BrE)
the intention to kill someone or cause them serious harm, often with malice or premeditation

Examples

Murderous intent shown
He was charged because his murderous intent was evident from his threats.
Her eyes flashed with murderous intent as she seized the weapon.
The story centers on a villain with murderous intent toward the protagonist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accidental - happening by chance, not intended or planned
  • synonym: malice aforethought - the intention to kill or harm, required as an element of some crimes
  • synonym: premeditation - the action of planning something, especially a crime, beforehand
