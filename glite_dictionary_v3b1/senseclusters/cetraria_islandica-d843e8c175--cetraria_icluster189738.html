Cetraria islandica

noun
lichen species used in medicine and food
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɛˈtreɪriə aɪˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / sɛˈtreːriə ɪˈlændɪkə / (BrE)
A lichen species with branched, flattened, partly erect thallus, found in mountainous and Arctic regions, used in traditional medicine and as food for humans and livestock.

Examples

Cetraria islandica lichen plant
Cetraria islandica, also known as Iceland moss, has been used for centuries in traditional herbal remedies.
In some Nordic regions, Cetraria islandica was an important source of nutrition during harsh winters.
Scientists study Cetraria islandica for its potential medicinal compounds and resilience in Arctic environments.
