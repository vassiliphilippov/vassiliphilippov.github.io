UK English

noun
English as used in the United Kingdom
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː ˈkeɪ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˈkeɪ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
The variety of English spoken and written in the United Kingdom, characterized by its own spelling, vocabulary, and pronunciation.

Examples

UK English spelling
He switched his software settings from American English to UK English for more familiar spelling.
Many people prefer to use UK English spelling in their essays at British universities.
Writers often choose UK English or American English depending on their intended audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: American English - English as spoken and written in the United States
  • synonym: British English - English as spoken and written in Britain
