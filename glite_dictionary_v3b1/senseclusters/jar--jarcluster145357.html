jar

verb
move with a sudden, jerky motion
5.3 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / d í…ër / (AmE), / d í…ëÀê / (BrE)
to move or be moved with a sudden, jerky motion; to shake or jolt suddenly

Examples

To jar the table
She jarred the table so hard that the vase nearly fell off.
The impact jarred the paintings on the wall, making them hang crookedly.
The sudden stop caused the passengers to jar forward in their seats.
His arm was badly jarred when he tripped on the stairs.
The loud noise jarred her out of her daydream.
The sudden stop jarred everyone in the car.

Synonyms

  • jolt
    3.7 1.70/m
    a jolt is a sudden, rough push that shifts something, while a jar is a sudden jerky movement that makes objects shake
  • jerk
    2.0 15/m
    a jerk is a sharp quick movement that pulls something, while a jar is a sudden jerky shake or jolt
  • shake
    1.0 50/m
    a shake is a back-and-forth or up-and-down movement often repeated, while a jar is a sudden, single jerky jolt

Related Terms

  • synonym: jolt - to move with a sudden rough movement
  • synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements
