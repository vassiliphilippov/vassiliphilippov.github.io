platinum thermometer

noun
temperature-measuring device using platinum resistance
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈplæt.nəm θərˈmɑː.mə.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈplæt.nəm θəˈmɒ.mɪ.tə / (BrE)
A thermometer that measures temperature by detecting changes in the resistance of a platinum wire coil.

Examples

A platinum thermometer coil
A platinum thermometer is often preferred in scientific research for its accuracy and stability.
Because of its high sensitivity, the platinum thermometer is ideal for calibrating other temperature measurement devices.
The laboratory used a platinum thermometer for precise temperature measurements during the experiment.
