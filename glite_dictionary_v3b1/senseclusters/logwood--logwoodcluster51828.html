logwood

noun
tree yielding wood for dye
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
a spiny shrub or small tree native to Central America and the West Indies, known for its hard, dark heartwood used to produce a black dye

Examples

Logwood tree
Early textile industries depended on logwood to create richly colored fabrics.
In the Caribbean, logwood was once a major export commodity.
The heartwood of the logwood tree is highly valued for producing dyes.
