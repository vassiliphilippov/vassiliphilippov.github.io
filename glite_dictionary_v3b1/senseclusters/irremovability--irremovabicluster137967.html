irremovability

noun
quality of not being removable
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to remove from an office, position, or place

Examples

The irremovability of officials
Discussions on the irremovability of certain officials often lead to debates about institutional reform.
He questioned the irremovability of the board members, fearing a lack of accountability.
The irremovability of judges is a key principle in many legal systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irremovable - unable to be removed
  • antonym: removability - the quality of being able to be removed
