Alfred Tennyson

proper noun
English Victorian poet laureate (1809–1892)
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈælfrəd ˈtɛnɪsən / (AmE), / ˈælfrɪd ˈtɛnɪsən / (BrE)
(1809–1892) English poet who served as Poet Laureate during much of Queen Victoria's reign, famous for works such as 'In Memoriam' and 'The Charge of the Light Brigade'.

Examples

Alfred Tennyson poet laureate
Alfred Tennyson was appointed as Poet Laureate by Queen Victoria in 1850.
Alfred Tennyson's poetry captures the spirit and anxieties of the Victorian era.
Many students study Alfred Tennyson when learning about nineteenth-century English literature.
