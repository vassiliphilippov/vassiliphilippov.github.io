blamer

noun
person who blames others
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbleɪ.mər/ (AmE), /ˈbleɪ.mə/ (BrE)
a person who often blames others for problems or mistakes instead of accepting responsibility themselves

Examples

A blamer at work
As a constant blamer, he never took responsibility for his own errors at work.
The team grew frustrated with the blamer who always pointed fingers after a loss.
Instead of being a blamer, try to reflect on your own actions first.
As a constant blamer, he rarely admitted his own role in the team's failures.
The meeting was unproductive because the blamer kept pointing fingers at everyone else.
Instead of being a blamer, try to find solutions and take responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuser - a person who claims someone has done something wrong
  • antonym: admitter - a person who accepts responsibility for their own actions
  • antonym: scapegoat - a person who is unfairly blamed for something
  • synonym: scapegoater - a person who unfairly blames others for problems
