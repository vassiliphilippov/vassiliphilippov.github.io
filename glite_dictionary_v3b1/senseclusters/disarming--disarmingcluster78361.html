disarming

adjective
making less hostile or suspicious
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
tending to make someone less hostile or suspicious, often by charm or friendliness.

Examples

Disarming smile
His disarming honesty made it hard to stay angry with him for long.
She gave him a disarming smile that instantly eased the tension in the room.
The defendant spoke with a disarming sincerity that won over many jurors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charming - pleasant or attractive in a way that delights others
  • antonym: threatening - having a hostile or intimidating quality
