crackback

noun
football blocking move
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkrækˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkrækˌbæk / (BrE)
a block in American football where a player moves back toward the ball to block an opponent, often from the side

Examples

A crackback block
The wide receiver delivered a powerful crackback that helped spring the running back for a big gain.
A penalty was called after the defender was hit with an illegal crackback during the play.
Coaches warn players about the risks of performing a crackback because of safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - an action to stop or impede an opponent in sports
  • antonym: tackle - an attempt to stop an opponent by knocking them down or stopping their movement
