beatification

noun
act of declaring someone blessed in Catholicism
Pronunciation: / biˌædəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / biˌatɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the official act by which the Pope declares that a deceased person has lived a holy life and is worthy of public veneration, marking the first step toward sainthood (canonization).

Examples

Pope's beatification ceremony
Her life of charity and devotion led to her beatification by the Pope.
The beatification ceremony attracted thousands of pilgrims from around the world.
The Church marked the anniversary of his beatification with a special mass.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: canonization - the act of officially declaring someone a saint in the Roman Catholic Church
