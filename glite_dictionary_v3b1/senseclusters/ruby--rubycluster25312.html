ruby

noun
deep red precious stone; gemstone
Pronunciation: / ˈruːbi / (AmE), / ˈruːbi / (BrE)
a transparent deep red precious stone used as a gemstone, especially in jewelry

Examples

A ruby ring
She wore a beautiful necklace with a large ruby pendant.
The ruby glowed vividly under the museum lights.
They used a synthetic ruby to create the first working laser.
He gave her a ring featuring a sparkling ruby surrounded by diamonds.
The auction displayed a rare Burmese ruby valued at millions of dollars.
The queen wore a beautiful necklace set with a large ruby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diamond - a clear, colorless, precious stone of pure carbon
  • degree lower: emerald - a green precious stone consisting of a chromium-rich variety of beryl
  • degree lower: sapphire - a transparent blue variety of corundum used as a gemstone
