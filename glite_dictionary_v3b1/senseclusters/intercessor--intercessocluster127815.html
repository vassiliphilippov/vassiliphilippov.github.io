intercessor

noun
person who mediates between parties
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈsɛsɚ /; / ˌɪntərˈsɛsər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛsə / (BrE)
a person who intervenes or mediates between people, often to help resolve a disagreement or to act on someone else's behalf

Examples

An intercessor helps
She acted as an intercessor during the tense negotiations, guiding both sides towards compromise.
The company brought in an intercessor to help resolve the dispute between the two departments.
Without a skilled intercessor, the conflict might have escalated into a legal battle.
The intercessor spoke to both sides to help resolve the conflict peacefully.
In many traditions, a priest acts as an intercessor between people and the divine.
She became an intercessor for her friend, pleading his case to the authorities.
During the heated debate, she acted as an intercessor to help both sides reach an agreement.
The villagers turned to the priest as an intercessor to pray for rain during the drought.
He served as an intercessor between the two families, helping them resolve their conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - a person who supports or speaks in favor of someone
  • antonym: antagonist - a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something
  • synonym: arbitrator - a person appointed to settle a dispute
  • synonym: mediator - a person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement
  • antonym: opponent - a person who is against or opposes another
