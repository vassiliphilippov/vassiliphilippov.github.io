Parthenocissus

noun
genus of woody climbing vines
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑrθənoʊˈsɪsəs /; / ˌpɑːrθənoʊˈsɪsəs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθɪnəˈsɪsəs /; / ˌpɑːθənəʊˈsɪsəs / (BrE)
a genus of woody climbing vines with tendrils that have small, disklike adhesive tips, including species such as Virginia creeper and Boston ivy
