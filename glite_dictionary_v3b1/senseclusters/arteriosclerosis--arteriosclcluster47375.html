arteriosclerosis

noun
hardening of the arteries
7.4 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / …ërÀåt…™ri.o ä.skl…ôÀàro ä.s…™s / (AmE), / …ëÀêÀåt…™…ôri.…ô ä.skl…ôÀàr…ô ä.s…™s / (BrE)
A condition in which the walls of the arteries become thickened and stiff, restricting blood flow.

Examples

Arteriosclerosis disease
Her diagnosis of arteriosclerosis prompted her to follow a strict diet and exercise routine.
Lifestyle changes can help prevent the development of arteriosclerosis in at-risk individuals.
The doctor explained that arteriosclerosis could lead to serious complications if not managed properly.

Synonyms

  • arterial sclerosis
    5.6 0.01/m
    arterial sclerosis is a medical variant often used in clinical texts, while arteriosclerosis is the standard term for artery hardening.
  • hardening of the arteries
    3.6 0.14/m
    hardening of the arteries is a plain everyday phrase people use in speech and writing, while arteriosclerosis is the formal medical name.
  • induration of the arteries
    8.9 0.00/m
    induration of the arteries is a technical phrase suggesting local hardening of tissue, while arteriosclerosis means general arterial thickening and stiffening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atherosclerosis - a specific type of arteriosclerosis involving buildup of fatty plaques in artery walls
