paean

noun
formal praise or song of triumph
9.9 0.8/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpi…ôn /; / ÀàpiÀê.…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàpiÀê…ôn /; / ÀàpiÀê.…ôn / (BrE)
a song, speech, or piece of writing that expresses enthusiastic praise or triumph, sometimes originally sung in ancient Greece to thank or honor a god

Examples

A paean of praise
At the ceremony, the poet delivered a heartfelt paean in honor of her mentor.
Editorials across the country wrote paeans to the retiring leader's achievements.
The author's new book is a paean to nature and the great outdoors.
Ancient Greeks would sing a paean after a battle to thank the gods for victory.
His speech ended with a rousing paean to friendship and unity.
The poet's latest collection is a heartfelt paean to the resilience of the human spirit.

Synonyms

  • panegyric
    9.9 0.2/m
    a panegyric is a formal public speech of high praise, while a paean is often a song or short hymn of triumph and thanks.
  • encomium
    9.9 0.2/m
    an encomium is a formal written praise often used in speeches or tributes, while a paean is usually a song or hymn giving triumphant thanks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dirge - a lament for the dead, especially one forming part of a funeral rite
  • synonym: eulogy - a speech or piece of writing that praises someone, often after death
  • synonym: hymn - a religious song or poem of praise to God or a god
  • antonym: invective - insulting, abusive, or highly critical language
  • synonym: panegyric - a public speech or published text in praise of someone
