bushmaster

noun
venomous tropical snake
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a large, highly venomous snake native to Central and South America, known for its dangerous bite

Examples

Venomous bushmaster snake
The bushmaster is rarely seen because it hides deep in the rainforest.
Researchers studied the venom of the bushmaster to develop new antivenoms.
Locals warn travelers to be cautious of the bushmaster when hiking in the jungle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fer-de-lance - a highly venomous pit viper found in Central and South America
  • antonym: python - a large non-venomous constricting snake
  • synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a rattle at the end of its tail
