absinthe oil

noun
bitter oil from wormwood for absinthe flavoring
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.sɪnθ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈæb.sɪnθ ɔɪl / (BrE)
A dark, bitter oil obtained from wormwood leaves, used to flavor absinthe liqueurs.

Examples

Dark absinthe oil
Historically, absinthe oil was extracted from wormwood to create traditional absinthe drinks.
Pure absinthe oil can be extremely bitter if not carefully diluted in the recipe.
The distiller added a few drops of absinthe oil to enhance the liqueur's herbal notes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wormwood - a bitter plant used in medicine and as a flavoring
