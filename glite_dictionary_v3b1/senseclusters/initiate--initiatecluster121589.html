initiate

verb
formally admit to a group with a rite
2.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to formally accept someone into an exclusive society or group, often with a special ceremony or ritual

Examples

To initiate a member
Many cultures initiate young people into adulthood with traditional rites.
She was initiated into the secret society after passing several tests.
The fraternity will initiate new members at the ceremony this weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admit - to officially allow someone to join a group or institution
  • antonym: exclude - to prevent someone from entering a group or society
