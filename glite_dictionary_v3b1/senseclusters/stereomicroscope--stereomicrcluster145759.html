stereomicroscope

noun
3D-view microscope
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛrioʊˈmaɪkrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəʊˈmaɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
a microscope that gives a three-dimensional view of a specimen at low magnification, often used for dissection or inspection

Examples

A stereomicroscope view
The entomologist used a stereomicroscope to study the fine details of the butterfly's wings.
Students examined plant leaves under a stereomicroscope to observe their surface structure.
A stereomicroscope allows scientists to manipulate small objects while viewing them in three dimensions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compound microscope - a microscope that uses multiple lenses for high magnification of small specimens
  • synonym: dissecting microscope - a microscope designed for low magnification observation of a sample, often used in dissection
