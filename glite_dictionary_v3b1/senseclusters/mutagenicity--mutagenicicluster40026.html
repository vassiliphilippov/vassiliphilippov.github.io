mutagenicity

noun
ability to cause mutations
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːtəˌdʒəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtəˌdʒəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance or agent to cause genetic mutations

Examples

The mutagenicity of pesticides
Scientists are concerned about the mutagenicity of certain pesticides used in agriculture.
The laboratory conducted tests to determine the mutagenicity of the new drug compound.
Strict regulations exist to limit exposure to chemicals with high mutagenicity in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carcinogenicity - the ability of a substance to cause cancer
  • antonym: harmlessness - the quality of not causing harm or damage
  • synonym: toxicity - the degree to which a substance is poisonous
