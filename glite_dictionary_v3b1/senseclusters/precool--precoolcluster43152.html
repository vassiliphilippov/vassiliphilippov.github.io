precool

verb
to cool beforehand
Pronunciation: / priːˈkuːl / (AmE), / priːˈkuːl / (BrE)
to cool (or become cool) in advance, especially before use, storage, or further processing

Examples

To precool equipment
It is important to precool the laboratory equipment before starting the experiment.
The technician allowed the engine to precool before performing any repairs.
You should precool the storage room to keep the produce fresh during transport.
You should precool the oven before baking the bread to ensure even cooking.
Farmers often precool vegetables to keep them fresh during transport.
The technician advised us to precool the equipment before starting the experiment.
