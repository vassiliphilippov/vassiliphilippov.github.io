rigorism

noun
strict rule-following
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈrɪg.ər.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈrɪg.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈrɪg.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
strict and uncompromising adherence to rules, laws, or moral principles without allowing exceptions

Examples

Strict rigorism in rules
The judge's rigorism left no room for mercy in his decisions.
Her rigorism sometimes made it difficult for her to compromise with others.
Religious rigorism can lead to very strict interpretations of doctrine.
