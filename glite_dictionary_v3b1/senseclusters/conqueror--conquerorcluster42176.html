conqueror

noun
a person who conquers by force
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑŋkərər / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋkərə / (BrE)
a person who wins a country, people, or territory by force, especially in war

Examples

The conqueror wins land
After years of war, the conqueror claimed the throne and established his own rule.
History remembers Alexander the Great as a famous conqueror of ancient empires.
Many admired the conqueror for his strategic brilliance, but others resented his brutality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defeated - a person who has been beaten in battle or competition
  • synonym: victor - a person who defeats an opponent; winner
