eastern chinquapin

noun
shrubby chestnut tree with edible nuts
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈistɚn tʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈiːstən tʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (BrE)
A shrubby chestnut tree of the southeastern United States, noted for its small edible nuts.

Examples

The eastern chinquapin tree
Gardeners in the South often plant the eastern chinquapin for its ornamental value and nut production.
The eastern chinquapin is prized for its sweet, edible nuts that wildlife enjoy.
You can identify an eastern chinquapin by its narrow leaves and spiky seed pods.
