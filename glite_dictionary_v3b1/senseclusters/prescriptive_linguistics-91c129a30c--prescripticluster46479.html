prescriptive linguistics

noun
rules-focused branch of linguistics
Pronunciation: / prɪˈskrɪptɪv lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪptɪv lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with establishing rules for how a language should be used, prescribing 'correct' usage rather than describing actual use

Examples

Prescriptive linguistics rules
Critics argue that prescriptive linguistics can ignore natural changes in spoken language.
Some grammar books are based on prescriptive linguistics and insist on strict rules of usage.
The debate between prescriptive linguistics and descriptive approaches shapes how language is taught in schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptive linguistics - study of actual language use as it is spoken or written
