pyrrole

noun
nitrogen ring compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪroʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɪrɒl / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile liquid organic compound with a five-membered ring containing one nitrogen atom, used in chemical synthesis

Examples

Pure pyrrole liquid
The laboratory ordered pure pyrrole for use in advanced organic chemistry experiments.
Many important biological molecules, such as heme, contain a pyrrole ring structure.
The synthesis of pharmaceuticals often starts with a pyrrole compound as a key building block.
