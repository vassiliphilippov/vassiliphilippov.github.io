siamese connection

noun
fire department water inlet fitting with multiple couplings
Pronunciation: / saɪˈæmiz kəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / saɪˈæmiːz kəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
an external fitting on a building that allows fire engines to pump water into its sprinkler or standpipe system via two or more hose couplings

Examples

A siamese connection fitting
During inspections, firefighters test each siamese connection to ensure proper operation in an emergency.
Maintenance workers checked that the siamese connection was free of debris and easy to access.
The fire crew quickly attached the hoses to the siamese connection at the front of the building.
