pressurizer

noun
one who applies pressure
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃəˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a person or organization that applies pressure to others to influence their decisions or actions

Examples

Political pressurizer
The union became a strong pressurizer during the contract negotiations.
As a known pressurizer, she often persuaded city officials to change their policies.
The group acted as a pressurizer to ensure the company improved its environmental standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influencer - someone who affects the decisions or actions of others
  • synonym: lobbyist - a person who tries to influence politicians or officials
  • antonym: supporter - a person who supports or backs a cause
