get a clue

verb
become aware or understand
4.8
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ə klu / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə kluː / (BrE)
to begin to understand or realize something; to become aware of what is happening or what needs to be done

Examples

To get a clue quickly
If you want to keep your job, you should get a clue about what your boss expects.
When it started to rain, she finally got a clue and brought the laundry inside.
You need to get a clue if you think that project will finish itself.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be oblivious - to be unaware of or fail to notice something
  • synonym: catch on - to begin to understand or realize something
  • synonym: wise up - to become aware of the truth about a situation
