external jugular vein

noun
a major vein in the neck
4.3
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈstɜːr.nəl ˈdʒʌg.jə.lər veɪn/ /; / /ɪk.ˈstɜːr.nəl ˈdʒʌg.jʊ.lər veɪn/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈstɜː.nəl ˈdʒʌg.jʊ.lə veɪn/ /; / /ɪk.ˈstɜː.nəl ˈdʒʌg.jə.lə veɪn/ (BrE)
a vein formed by the junction of the posterior auricular and retromandibular veins, located in the neck and emptying into the subclavian vein

Examples

Doctors sometimes use the external jugular vein for intravenous access in emergency situations.
Swelling of the external jugular vein can be a sign of heart problems.
The external jugular vein is often visible when a person lies down with their head turned to one side.
