homestead

verb
settle government-granted land
8.0 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊm.sted/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊm.sted/ (BrE)
to settle on land granted by the government and establish a home there, especially as under homesteading laws

Examples

To homestead land
During the land rush, many people hoped to homestead and build a new life.
His grandparents were among the first to homestead in that part of Nebraska.
In the late 1800s, thousands of families moved west to homestead vast tracts of prairie.
