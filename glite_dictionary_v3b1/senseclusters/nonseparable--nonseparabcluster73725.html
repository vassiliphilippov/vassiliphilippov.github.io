nonseparable

adjective
cannot be separated
7.6
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɛpərəbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɛpərəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be separated from something else, especially describing an affix or particle that always remains attached to its base word.

Examples

Nonseparable prefix
In some languages, certain prefixes are always nonseparable from the main verb.
The teacher explained that 'un-' is a nonseparable prefix in English.
Linguists often discuss whether a particle is nonseparable or can stand alone.

  • antonym: separable - able to be separated or divided
