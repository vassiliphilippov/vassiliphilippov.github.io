polycystic kidney disease

noun
chronic kidney disorder with multiple cysts
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈsɪstɪk ˈkɪdni dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsɪstɪk ˈkɪdni dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A chronic kidney disorder characterized by the presence of many cysts in the kidneys, often causing them to enlarge and leading to impaired kidney function.

Examples

Polycystic kidney disease symptoms
Her family history of polycystic kidney disease increased her risk of kidney problems.
Many patients with polycystic kidney disease develop high blood pressure as a complication.
There is ongoing research into new treatments for polycystic kidney disease.

  • degree higher: kidney failure - loss of kidney function, often resulting from polycystic kidney disease
