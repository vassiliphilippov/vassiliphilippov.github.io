detraining

noun
loss of fitness
Pronunciation: / diːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the loss of physical fitness or performance that occurs after stopping regular training or exercise

Examples

Athlete detraining break
Athletes may experience detraining if they take a long break from their usual workouts.
After several weeks of detraining, her muscle strength began to decline.
Coaches warn that detraining can quickly reverse the benefits of months of hard work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deconditioning - the process of losing physical fitness due to inactivity
  • antonym: training - the process of improving fitness through regular exercise
