trifocal

noun
three-power lens
6.8
Pronunciation: / traɪˈfoʊkəl / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊkəl / (BrE)
a lens or eyeglasses with three different optical powers or sections for seeing at different distances

Examples

A trifocal lens
After her cataract surgery, she was fitted with a trifocal to help her see clearly at all distances.
The optometrist recommended a trifocal for patients who need correction for near, intermediate, and far vision.
Modern trifocals can be made as contact lenses or implanted during eye surgery.
After turning fifty, he started wearing trifocal glasses to help with reading and driving.
Her new trifocal lenses make it easier to see her computer, books, and distant objects.
The optometrist recommended trifocal glasses for patients who need help at three different distances.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bifocal - a lens with two optical powers for near and distance vision
  • degree higher: multifocal - a lens with multiple optical powers for different distances
