turn down

verb
refuse entrance, membership, or participation
1.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /tɜːrn daʊn/ (AmE), /tɜːn daʊn/ (BrE)
to refuse or reject something, such as an offer, invitation, request, or person

Examples

To turn down an offer
Many people were turned down at the door due to limited space.
She was disappointed when the committee turned down her request to join.
The club turned down several applicants this year.
After much thought, I decided to turn down the invitation to the party.
He was hurt when she turned down his proposal.
She turned down his job offer despite the generous salary.
Many scientists will turn down new hypotheses if there is no supporting data.
She chose to turn down the report, stating there were too many errors to believe it.
The committee decided to turn down his explanation because of insufficient evidence.
