turn down

verb
refuse entrance, membership, or participation
2.9 11/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn da än /; / t…ùn Ààda än / (AmE), / t…úÀên da än /; / t…úÀên Ààda än / (BrE)
to refuse or reject something, such as an offer, invitation, request, or person

Examples

To turn down an offer
Many people were turned down at the door due to limited space.
She was disappointed when the committee turned down her request to join.
The club turned down several applicants this year.
After much thought, I decided to turn down the invitation to the party.
He was hurt when she turned down his proposal.
She turned down his job offer despite the generous salary.
Many scientists will turn down new hypotheses if there is no supporting data.
She chose to turn down the report, stating there were too many errors to believe it.
The committee decided to turn down his explanation because of insufficient evidence.

Synonyms

  • decline
    3.4 16/m
    decline is a polite, often formal way to refuse an offer, while turn down is more common in speech.
  • reject
    1.9 52/m
    reject means to refuse something firmly or to say it is not acceptable, while turn down is used for offers.
  • pass up
    4.1 2.40/m
    pass up means to choose not to take a good opportunity, while turn down usually means refuse an offer or invitation.
  • shoot down
    3.8 1.35/m
    shoot down means to reject an idea or plan sharply and quickly, while turn down usually describes refusing offers.
  • dismiss
    3.0 25/m
    dismiss means to decide something is not worth considering, while turn down means to refuse an offer or invitation.
  • refuse
    1.7 70/m
    refuse means saying you do not accept or believe something, while turn down means saying no to an offer or invitation.
