competent

adjective
legally qualified or authorized
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːm.pə.tənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pɪ.tənt / (BrE)
(of a person, court, authority, etc.) having the legal authority or qualification to do something.

Examples

Competent court
Only a competent witness is permitted to give testimony in a legal proceeding.
The contract can only be signed by a competent authority under the law.
The judge determined that the case should be heard by a competent court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incompetent - not legally qualified or lacking authority
