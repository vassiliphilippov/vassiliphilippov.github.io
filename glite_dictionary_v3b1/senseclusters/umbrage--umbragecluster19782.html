A feeling of offence or annoyance caused by a remark or action.

Examples

Take umbrage at words
He left in a huff, clearly showing umbrage at being overlooked.
Many readers might take umbrage at the article's harsh criticisms.
She took umbrage at his insensitive remark during the meeting.

Synonyms

    3.9 8.78/m

  • indignation
    5.5 4.17/m
    indignation is a strong, moral anger at unfair treatment, while umbrage is a milder personal feeling of offence.
  • dudgeon
    9.9 0.13/m
