umbrage

noun
offence or annoyance
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʌm.brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌm.brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A feeling of offence or annoyance caused by a remark or action.

Examples

Take umbrage at words
He left in a huff, clearly showing umbrage at being overlooked.
Many readers might take umbrage at the article's harsh criticisms.
She took umbrage at his insensitive remark during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delight - A feeling of great pleasure or happiness
  • synonym: offence - Annoyance or resentment brought about by a perceived insult
  • synonym: resentment - Bitter indignation at having been treated unfairly
