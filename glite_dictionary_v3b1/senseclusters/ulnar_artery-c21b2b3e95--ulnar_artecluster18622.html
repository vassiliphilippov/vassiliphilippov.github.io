ulnar artery

noun
artery of the forearm supplying wrist and hand
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʌlnər ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈʌlnə ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
the large artery branching from the brachial artery that supplies blood to the muscles of the forearm, wrist, and hand

Examples

The ulnar artery supplies blood
An injury to the ulnar artery may affect circulation to the hand.
Blood flow in the ulnar artery can be measured using ultrasound technology.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the ulnar artery during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radial artery - artery in the forearm running parallel to the ulnar artery
