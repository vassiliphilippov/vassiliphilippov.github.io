tonometry

noun
measurement of intraocular pressure
Pronunciation: / təˈnɑmətri / (AmE), / təˈnɒmətri / (BrE)
The measurement of intraocular pressure, especially by determining the force needed to make a slight indentation in the cornea.

Examples

Tonometry test
Advancements in tonometry have made the examination more comfortable and accurate.
Regular tonometry is recommended for patients at risk of elevated eye pressure.
The doctor performed tonometry to check for signs of glaucoma.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ophthalmoscopy - examination of the interior of the eye
