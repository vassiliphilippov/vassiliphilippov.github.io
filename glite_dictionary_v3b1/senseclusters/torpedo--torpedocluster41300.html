torpedo

verb
to ruin suddenly
5.1
Pronunciation: / tɔrˈpiːdoʊ / (AmE), / tɔːˈpiːdəʊ / (BrE)
to destroy, ruin, or put an end to something suddenly and completely

Examples

To torpedo a plan
The scandal torpedoed his chances of winning the election.
A single mistake could torpedo months of hard work on the project.
Her sudden resignation torpedoed the company's plans for expansion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sabotage - to deliberately destroy or damage something
  • antonym: save - to prevent something from being destroyed or ruined
  • synonym: wreck - to completely destroy or severely damage something
