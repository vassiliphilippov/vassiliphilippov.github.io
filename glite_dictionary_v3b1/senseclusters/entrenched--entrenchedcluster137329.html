entrenched

adjective

firmly established and difficult to change; deeply ingrained (especially of attitudes, habits, or positions)

Examples

Deeply entrenched beliefs
Gender roles remain entrenched in some parts of society.
He has entrenched beliefs about politics that are hard to challenge.
The company's culture is so entrenched that new ideas meet great resistance.
Despite criticism, the politician held on to his entrenched position.
Social norms can become entrenched over generations, making reform a slow process.
The company faced difficulties in changing its entrenched management practices.
