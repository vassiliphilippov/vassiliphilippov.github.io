crackle

verb
make a series of sharp, snapping noises
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl / (BrE)
to make a series of short, sharp, snapping or popping sounds

Examples

Leaves crackle underfoot
As he walked through the frosty grass, it crackled under his boots.
Old vinyl records sometimes crackle when played on a turntable.
The fire crackled warmly in the fireplace as they talked.
I love to listen to bacon crackle in the frying pan.
The fire began to crackle as the wood caught alight.
The old vinyl record would crackle every time the needle passed over a scratch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crunch - make a loud, crushing noise, especially when being pressed or crushed
  • antonym: hiss - to make a sibilant sound like that of the letter 's'
  • synonym: pop - make a quick, sharp sound, like a small explosion
  • synonym: snap - make a sudden, sharp cracking sound
