legman

noun
person doing errands
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛɡˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡˌmæn / (BrE)
a person who does practical tasks or fieldwork, especially gathering information, for someone else (such as a reporter or helper)

Examples

The legman gathers information
The legman gathered information from several sources before reporting back to the editor.
As a legman, she spent her days running errands and collecting data for the research team.
The detective relied on his legman to interview witnesses and check out leads.
The legman was sent to deliver a message to the rival gang.
Police arrested the legman while he was gathering information on the street.
As a trusted legman, he often handled risky errands for the group.
The legman rushed to the scene to gather firsthand information for the breaking news story.
As a skilled legman, she spent her days interviewing people and collecting facts on the ground.
Every newspaper relies on a dedicated legman to cover events outside the office.
