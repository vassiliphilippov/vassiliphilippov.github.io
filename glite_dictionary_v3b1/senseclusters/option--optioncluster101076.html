option

noun
right to buy/sell at set price (finance)
7.4 20/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêp. É…ôn / (AmE), / Àà…íp. É…ôn / (BrE)
a legal right to buy or sell property or securities at a specified price within a set period, the right being purchased and forfeited if not exercised by the expiration date

Examples

A stock option
Investors use an option to hedge against potential losses in the stock market.
She purchased an option on 100 shares of the company at $50 each.
The option expires at the end of the month if it is not exercised.
