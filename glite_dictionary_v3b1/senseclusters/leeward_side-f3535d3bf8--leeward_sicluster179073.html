leeward side

noun
side sheltered from the wind

Examples

The leeward side of island
During the storm, boats anchored on the leeward side of the island remained safe.
Plants thrive on the leeward side where they are protected from harsh winds.
The hikers set up camp on the leeward side of the mountain to avoid the strong gusts.

Synonyms

  • lee side
    6.3 0.13/m
    lee side is the common, everyday phrase sailors and hikers use for a sheltered spot, while leeward side is more formal.
  • leeward
    6.9 0.35/m
    leeward usually names the sheltered direction on a boat or hill, while leeward side refers to that sheltered side more clearly.
  • lee
    7.4 0.40/m
    lee means the sheltered area often used by sailors for safety, while leeward side is the fuller phrase used in formal descriptions.
