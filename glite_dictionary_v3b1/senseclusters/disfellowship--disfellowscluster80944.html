disfellowship

verb
to expel from group
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈfel.oʊ.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈfel.əʊ.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
to formally exclude someone from membership or participation in a group, especially a religious community

Examples

To disfellowship a member
The congregation voted to disfellowship the member after several warnings.
If someone breaks the rules repeatedly, the group may disfellowship them.
After the investigation, the committee decided to disfellowship two individuals.
