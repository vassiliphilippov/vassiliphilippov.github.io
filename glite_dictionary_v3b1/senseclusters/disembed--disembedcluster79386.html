disembed

verb
to remove from context
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪmˈbɛd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪmˈbɛd / (BrE)
to remove something from its original context or setting

Examples

To disembed main idea
The teacher asked students to disembed the main idea from the supporting details in the text.
It can be challenging to disembed cultural values from language use.
Researchers often need to disembed data from its original context for analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: embed - to fix or set something firmly in a surrounding mass or context
  • synonym: extract - to remove something from a place or context
