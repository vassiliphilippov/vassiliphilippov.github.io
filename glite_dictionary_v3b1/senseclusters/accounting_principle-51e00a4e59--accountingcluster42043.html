accounting principle

noun
standard or rule in accounting practices
3.0
Pronunciation: / əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ ˈprɪn.sə.pəl / (AmE), / əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl / (BrE)
A standard or guideline governing accounting practices, used to determine the proper recording and reporting of financial transactions.

Examples

Basic accounting principles
All financial statements must comply with the relevant accounting principles to ensure accuracy and transparency.
Changing an accounting principle usually requires full disclosure in a company's financial reports.
Revenue recognition is an accounting principle that determines when income should be recorded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accounting standard - a formal rule or guideline for financial reporting in accounting
  • synonym: generally accepted accounting principles - widely recognized principles guiding accounting in a specific jurisdiction
