arboreal salamander

noun
tree-dwelling Californian salamander
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈbɔriəl ˈsæləˌmændər / (AmE), / ɑːˈbɔːriəl ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
a yellow-spotted brown salamander (Aneides lugubris) native to California woodlands, known for its climbing ability and nocturnal habits

Examples

The arboreal salamander climbs trees
Because of its strong jaws, the arboreal salamander can defend itself against predators.
Researchers discovered an arboreal salamander sheltering in a tree cavity.
The arboreal salamander is most active at night in the moist forests of California.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tree salamander - any salamander of the genus Aneides, known for living in trees
