starting block

noun
device for runners to brace feet at race start
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑrtɪŋ blɑk / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ blɒk / (BrE)
a device consisting of a pair of adjustable footrests used by sprinters at the start of a race to brace their feet for a faster and more stable start

Examples

Adjust the starting block
Before the race began, each athlete adjusted their starting block for maximum comfort and grip.
He slipped as he pushed off the starting block, costing him valuable time.
Using the starting block correctly can improve a sprinter's acceleration from the line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: starting line - the line from which a race begins
