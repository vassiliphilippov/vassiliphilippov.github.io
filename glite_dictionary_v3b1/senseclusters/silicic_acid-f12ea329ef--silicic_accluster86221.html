silicic acid

noun
a jellylike weak acid from hydrated silica
Pronunciation: / sɪˌlɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sɪˌlɪsɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a jellylike weak acid, formed by hydrating silica, and existing chiefly in solution or as insoluble silicate minerals

Examples

Silicic acid solution
At neutral pH, silicic acid remains largely uncharged and soluble in solution.
Geologists analyze silicic acid content in groundwater to study mineral weathering.
The formation of silicic acid occurs when silica reacts with water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: silica - a hard, unreactive, colorless compound that occurs as quartz and in sand, flint, and many rocks
