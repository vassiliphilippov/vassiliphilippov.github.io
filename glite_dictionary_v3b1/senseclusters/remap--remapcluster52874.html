remap

verb
to redraw boundaries
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈmæp / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmæp / (BrE)
to redraw or change the boundaries of areas such as electoral districts or administrative regions, often for political or population reasons

Examples

To remap districts
The city council decided to remap the school districts to better reflect population changes.
After the census, officials must remap the voting regions to ensure fair representation.
The committee will remap administrative zones to improve local governance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redistrict - to divide or organize an area into new districts
  • synonym: reorganize - to arrange or structure something in a new way
