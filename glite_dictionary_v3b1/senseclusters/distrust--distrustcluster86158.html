distrust

verb
regard with suspicion; lack trust
2.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstrʌst/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstrʌst/ (BrE)
to regard someone or something as untrustworthy or with suspicion; to have no faith or confidence in

Examples

To distrust advice
If you distrust the source, double-check the information before sharing it.
Many people distrust politicians because of broken promises.
She began to distrust his explanations after catching him in several lies.
