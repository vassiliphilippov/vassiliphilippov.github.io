fret

noun
ornamental geometric design of repeated lines
Pronunciation: / frɛt / (AmE), / frɛt / (BrE)
an ornamental design made up of repeated straight lines, often forming a geometric pattern in relief on architecture or objects

Examples

A Greek fret border
Ancient Greek temples often featured a fret as a decorative border.
The artisan carefully carved a classic fret into the wooden panel above the door.
The ceiling was adorned with an intricate fret running along the edges of the room.
