stem

noun
tube of a tobacco pipe
Pronunciation: / stɛm / (AmE), / stɛm / (BrE)
the long, thin, supporting part of a plant or a similar part of an object (such as a pipe)

Examples

A flower stem
He replaced the cracked stem of his pipe before lighting it again.
Please don't grip the stem too tightly or it might break.
The stem of the tobacco pipe was carved from cherry wood.
Please cut the stem of the flower before placing it in water.
The mushroom's stem is tall and slender.
The rose's stem was covered with sharp thorns.
She carefully trimmed the stem of the rose before placing it in the vase.
The apple hung from its stem on the tree, ready to be picked.
Wine glasses with long stems are often considered more elegant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: root - the part of a plant that typically lies below the surface and anchors the plant
  • synonym: stalk - the slender structure that supports a leaf or flower
