Okinawa campaign

noun
WWII battle for Okinawa, 1945
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊkɪˈnɑwə kæmˌpeɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊkɪˈnɑːwə kæmˌpeɪn / (BrE)
the military campaign fought from April to June 1945 in which United States forces captured the island of Okinawa from Japan during World War II

Examples

The Okinawa campaign battle
Historians frequently cite the Okinawa campaign as a turning point in the Pacific War.
The Okinawa campaign was one of the bloodiest battles of World War II's Pacific Theater.
Veterans of the Okinawa campaign often speak of the intense combat and difficult conditions they faced.
