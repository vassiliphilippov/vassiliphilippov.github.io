extradite

verb
to hand over a fugitive to another country
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.strəˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strəˌdaɪt / (BrE)
to officially hand over a person accused or convicted of a crime to the authorities of another country or state where the crime was committed

Examples

To extradite a criminal
If caught abroad, he might be extradited to the United States for prosecution.
The government agreed to extradite the suspect to face charges in France.
They are seeking to extradite several individuals wanted in connection with the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deport - to expel a foreigner from a country, usually back to their home country
  • synonym: repatriate - to send someone back to their own country, especially for legal or humanitarian reasons
