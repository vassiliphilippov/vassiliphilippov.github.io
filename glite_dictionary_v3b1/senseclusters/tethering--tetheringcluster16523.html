tethering

noun
fastening to a point
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of fastening an animal or object to a fixed point with a rope, chain, or similar device to restrict movement

Examples

Tethering a goat
The tethering of the boat kept it from drifting away during the storm.
Proper tethering of pets is important to ensure their safety outdoors.
He inspected the tethering of the equipment before starting the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tying - the act of fastening or securing with a string or cord
  • antonym: unfastening - the act of releasing something from being fastened
