blind to

adjective
unaware of or ignoring something
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd tu / (AmE), / blaɪnd tʊ / (BrE)
unaware of or deliberately ignoring (something, especially a risk, problem, or fact)

Examples

Be blind to risks
Don't be blind to the opportunities right in front of you.
She was blind to the dangers that everyone else could see.
The management stayed blind to employees’ complaints for too long.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alert to - aware of and paying attention to something
  • synonym: oblivious to - unaware of or not concerned about something
