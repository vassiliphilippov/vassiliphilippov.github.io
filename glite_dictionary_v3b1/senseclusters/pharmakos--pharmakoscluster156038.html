pharmakos

noun
ancient sorcerer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑrməˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməkɒs / (BrE)
a person in ancient times believed to be a sorcerer, poisoner, or practitioner of harmful magic

Examples

Ancient pharmakos sorcerer
In some ancient rituals, a pharmakos was chosen to carry away the community's sins.
The villagers feared the arrival of a pharmakos who was rumored to bring misfortune.
Legends describe the pharmakos as someone who could heal or harm with secret knowledge.
