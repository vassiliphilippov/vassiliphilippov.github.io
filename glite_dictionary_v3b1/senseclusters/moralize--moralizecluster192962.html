moralize

verb
interpret as having moral meaning
6.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔː.rə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.rə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to interpret or explain the moral meaning of something, especially a story or event

Examples

To moralize a story
Critics often moralize works of fiction, looking for deeper ethical meanings.
It is tempting to moralize historical events by assigning good and bad characters to each side.
The teacher tried to moralize the fable for the students so they would learn a life lesson.
Authors often moralise stories by highlighting the consequences of the characters' actions.
It is easy to moralise a fable by stating its lesson at the end.
Some critics moralise traditional myths to teach ethical values to children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moralize - interpret or explain the moral meaning (American spelling)
  • synonym: preach - to deliver a moral or religious message, often with moral instruction
  • synonym: sermonize - to deliver or compose moral lessons, especially in a tedious way
