chess

noun
strategy board game for two players
1.0
Pronunciation: / tʃɛs / (AmE), / tʃɛs / (BrE)
a game for two players, played on a checkered board with 16 pieces each, in which the aim is to checkmate the opponent's king

Examples

Play chess
Many people consider chess to be the ultimate test of strategy and skill.
She has been practicing chess every day to prepare for the tournament.
The library hosts a weekly chess club for players of all ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: checkmate - a winning move in chess where the opponent's king cannot escape capture
  • antonym: draughts - another board game for two players, known as checkers in American English
