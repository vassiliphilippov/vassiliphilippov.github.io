raise eyebrows

verb
cause surprise or disapproval
2.2
Pronunciation: / reɪz ˈaɪˌbɹaʊz /; / reɪz ˈaɪˌbraʊz / (AmE), / reɪz ˈaɪˌbraʊz / (BrE)
to cause people to feel surprised, shocked, or disapproving, especially because something is unusual or unexpected

Examples

To raise eyebrows at events
Some of his fashion choices always raise eyebrows at formal events.
The new policy is likely to raise eyebrows among the staff.
Their sudden departure from the meeting raised eyebrows around the office.
Her decision to quit her job and travel the world really raised eyebrows in the office.
The politician's comments during the interview raised eyebrows among viewers.
When the company announced massive bonuses for executives, it immediately raised eyebrows among the staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: offend - to cause displeasure or disapproval
  • synonym: shock - to cause a sudden and strong surprise
