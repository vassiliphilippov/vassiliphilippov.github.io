mononucleosis

noun
infectious disease with fever and swollen lymph nodes
6.5 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / Àåm…ëÀêno äÀånuÀêkliÀào äs…™s / (AmE), / Àåm…ín…ô äÀånjuÀêkl…™Àà…ô äs…™s / (BrE)
an infectious disease marked by fever, swollen lymph nodes, and an increased number of mononuclear white blood cells, often caused by the Epstein-Barr virus

Examples

Mononucleosis infection
Because mononucleosis is sometimes spread by saliva, it's commonly called the 'kissing disease'.
Her recovery from mononucleosis took several weeks due to prolonged weakness and swollen glands.
The doctor diagnosed him with mononucleosis after he developed a sore throat and persistent fatigue.

Synonyms

  • infectious mononucleosis
    4.2 0.05/m
    infectious mononucleosis is the precise medical name for the acute disease with fever and raised white cells, while mononucleosis is its simpler name.
  • glandular fever
    4.2 0.02/m
    glandular fever often affects young adults and brings fever and fatigue, while mononucleosis highlights swollen lymph nodes and raised mononuclear blood cells.
  • mono
    7.4 0.33/m
    mono is an informal, everyday name used by young people for the illness, while mononucleosis is the formal medical term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glandular fever - another name for mononucleosis, especially in British English
