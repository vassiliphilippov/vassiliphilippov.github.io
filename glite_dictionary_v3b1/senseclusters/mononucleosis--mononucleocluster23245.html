mononucleosis

noun
infectious disease with fever and swollen lymph nodes
6.2 5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˌnuːkliˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˌnjuːklɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease marked by fever, swollen lymph nodes, and an increased number of mononuclear white blood cells, often caused by the Epstein-Barr virus

Examples

Mononucleosis infection
Because mononucleosis is sometimes spread by saliva, it's commonly called the 'kissing disease'.
Her recovery from mononucleosis took several weeks due to prolonged weakness and swollen glands.
The doctor diagnosed him with mononucleosis after he developed a sore throat and persistent fatigue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glandular fever - another name for mononucleosis, especially in British English
