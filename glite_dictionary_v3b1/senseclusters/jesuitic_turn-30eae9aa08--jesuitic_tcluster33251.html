Jesuitic turn

noun
complex or cunning argument
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛzuˈɪtɪk tɜrn / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛzjuˈɪtɪk tɜːn / (BrE)
A complex or cunning argument, especially one involving subtle or deceptive reasoning.

Examples

A Jesuitic turn in argument
The lawyer introduced a Jesuitic turn in her closing argument, surprising the jury.
Their discussion took a Jesuitic turn as they tried to outwit each other with logic.
When questioned, he replied with such a Jesuitic turn that no one was sure what he meant.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: devious - Using dishonest tricks and indirect methods
  • synonym: sophistry - Subtly deceptive reasoning or argumentation
