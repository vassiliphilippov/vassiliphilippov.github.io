lurk

verb
to be present secretly or without purpose
4.2
Pronunciation: / lɝːk /; / lɝk /; / lɜrk / (AmE), / lɜːk / (BrE)
to stay hidden or move secretly in a place, often with a negative or suspicious purpose

Examples

To lurk in shadows
Security asked anyone lurking in the hallway without a pass to leave.
The teenagers would often lurk near the playground after dark.
Who is that man lurking outside our building every evening?
He was caught lurking behind the bushes, waiting for someone to pass by.
The thief decided to lurk in the shadows until his target arrived.
Wolves often lurk near watering holes, hidden from view.
A cat may lurk in the shadows, waiting for an unsuspecting mouse.
Predators often lurk underwater, remaining still until their prey approaches.
She noticed someone lurking near the entrance and alerted security.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambush - to lie in wait to make a surprise attack
  • antonym: emerge - to come out into view after being hidden
  • synonym: hang around - to stay in a place without doing anything in particular
  • degree lower: hide - to conceal oneself
  • synonym: loiter - to stand or wait around idly without apparent purpose
