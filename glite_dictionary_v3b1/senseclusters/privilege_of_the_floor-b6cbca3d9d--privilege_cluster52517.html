privilege of the floor

noun
special right to enter a legislative chamber
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ əv ðə flɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ əv ðə flɔː / (BrE)
The right to be admitted onto the floor of a legislative assembly while it is in session, granted by special permission to non-members.

Examples

Privilege of the floor granted
He received the privilege of the floor, allowing him to address the assembly directly.
Only a few journalists were granted the privilege of the floor during the heated debate.
The senator's guests were given the privilege of the floor for the opening ceremony.
