patriarchate

noun
male-headed social organization
9.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpeɪ.triː.ɑːr.kət/ /; / /ˈpeɪ.triː.ɑːr.keɪt/ (AmE), /ˈpeɪ.triː.ɑː.kət/ /; / /ˈpæ.triː.ɑː.kət/ (BrE)
A social system or organization in which the male is the head of the family and descent is traced through the male line.

Examples

Ancient patriarchate society
Many ancient societies functioned under a patriarchate, passing property and titles from father to son.
Sociologists have studied the impact of the patriarchate on gender roles throughout history.
The tribe was organized into a patriarchate, with the eldest man holding authority over the family.
