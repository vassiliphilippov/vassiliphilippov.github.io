Arenaria melanocephala

noun
Pacific coast turnstone bird species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛr.əˈnɛr.i.ə ˌmɛl.ə.nəˈsɛf.ə.lə / (AmE), / ɛ.rəˈnɛə.ri.ə ˌmɛl.ə.nəˈsiː.fə.lə / (BrE)
A species of turnstone bird found along the Pacific coast of North America, known for its black head and mottled plumage.

Examples

Arenaria melanocephala bird
During migration season, birdwatchers gather to observe the Arenaria melanocephala in large flocks.
The Arenaria melanocephala is frequently spotted along rocky Pacific coastlines searching for food.
Unlike its Atlantic relative, the Arenaria melanocephala prefers the western shores of North America.
