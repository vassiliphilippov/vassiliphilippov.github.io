danger zone

noun
area with high risk or danger
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪndʒɚ zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒə zəʊn / (BrE)
an area or situation where there is a high risk of harm or danger

Examples

A danger zone
Pilots are trained to avoid flying through any danger zone marked on their navigation charts.
Rescue workers are prohibited from entering the danger zone until it is deemed safe.
The chemical spill created a danger zone that forced nearby residents to evacuate immediately.
He knew that by ignoring the warning signs, he was heading straight into the danger zone.
Only authorized personnel are allowed to enter the danger zone near the construction site.
When the river rises above a certain level, the surrounding land becomes a danger zone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: safe zone - an area where there is little or no risk of harm
