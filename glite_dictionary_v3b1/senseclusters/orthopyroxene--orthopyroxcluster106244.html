orthopyroxene

noun
silicate mineral group
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθoʊpaɪˈrɑksiːn / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊpaɪˈrɒksiːn / (BrE)
a group of silicate minerals commonly found in igneous and metamorphic rocks, typically containing iron and magnesium

Examples

Common orthopyroxene mineral
The geologist identified orthopyroxene in the volcanic rock sample.
Many meteorites contain significant amounts of orthopyroxene crystals.
Under the microscope, orthopyroxene appears as elongated, pale-colored grains.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clinopyroxene - a subgroup of pyroxene minerals with a different crystal structure
  • synonym: pyroxene - a group of silicate minerals found in many rocks
