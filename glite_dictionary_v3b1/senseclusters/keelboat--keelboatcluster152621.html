keelboat

noun
long river boat with a keel, no sails
Pronunciation: / ˈkilˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːlˌbəʊt / (BrE)
A long, narrow river boat with a shallow draft and a keel, used for carrying freight and moved by rowing, punting, or towing, rather than by sails.

Examples

A keelboat on river
Early settlers often traveled west by keelboat, pushing upstream against the current.
The traders loaded goods onto the keelboat for transport down the Mississippi River.
Without sails, the keelboat had to be towed by men walking along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barge - a flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight, especially on canals and rivers
