kyphosis

noun
abnormal spinal curvature
Pronunciation: / kaɪˈfoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈfəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an excessive outward curve of the spine that causes a hunched or rounded back

Examples

Severe kyphosis curve
Physical therapy can help manage mild cases of kyphosis and improve spinal alignment.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with kyphosis after noticing the pronounced hump on his upper back.
Years of poor posture contributed to the gradual development of kyphosis in the elderly woman.
Early intervention can prevent kyphosis from progressing in young patients.
Severe kyphosis may cause discomfort and affect a person's posture and appearance.
The doctor diagnosed him with kyphosis after noticing the unusual curvature of his upper back.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lordosis - an abnormal inward curvature of the spine
  • synonym: scoliosis - an abnormal lateral curvature of the spine
