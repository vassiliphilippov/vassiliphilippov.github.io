REIT

noun
real estate investment trust (finance)
6.8 1.21/m
Pronunciation: / riÀê a…™ tiÀê / (AmE), / riÀê a…™ tiÀê / (BrE)
A company that owns, operates, or finances income-producing real estate, whose shares are traded on stock exchanges like stocks.

Examples

REIT shares
Many investors consider REIT shares for their potential dividends and diversification in a portfolio.
The new shopping center was purchased by a large REIT specializing in commercial properties.
You can buy REIT stocks on major exchanges just like you would any other public company.
