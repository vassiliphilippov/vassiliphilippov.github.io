prevail

verb
continue to be valid or applicable
5.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈveɪl/ /; / /priː.ˈveɪl/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈveɪl/ /; / /priː.ˈveɪl/ (BrE)
to continue to exist, be accepted, or remain in force, especially for a long time or despite difficulties

Examples

Old rules prevail
Despite new evidence, the old regulations still prevail in this area.
The theory prevails even after decades of scientific debate.
Traditional customs prevail in many rural communities despite modernization.
Ancient traditions still prevail in some rural communities.
Despite many challenges, the old beliefs prevail among the villagers.
Superstitions sometimes prevail long after science has provided explanations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endure - to continue to exist; to last over time
  • antonym: fade - to gradually disappear or lose importance
  • synonym: hold - to remain valid or in effect
  • synonym: obtain - (formal) to be valid or applicable
  • synonym: persist - to continue to exist or happen, especially for longer than expected
