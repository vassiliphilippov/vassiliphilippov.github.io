supersolid

adjective
solid with frictionless flow
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈsɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈsɒlɪd / (BrE)
having or relating to a phase of matter that combines solid structure with the ability to flow without friction, like a superfluid.

Examples

Supersolid phase
Scientists discovered a supersolid phase in helium at extremely low temperatures.
The unique properties of a supersolid challenge our understanding of matter.
A supersolid can exhibit both rigid structure and the ability to flow without resistance.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: solid - a state of matter with fixed shape and volume
  • synonym: superfluid - a phase of matter that flows without friction
