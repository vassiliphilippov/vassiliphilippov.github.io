undo

verb
remove or reverse an effect or property
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈdu / (AmE), / ʌnˈduː / (BrE)
to reverse, cancel, or remove the effect of an action, decision, or its consequences

Examples

To undo a mistake
She tried to undo the damage caused by her earlier comments.
The new evidence will undo much of the public's confidence in the report.
Years of progress were undone by a single careless mistake.
He tried to undo the damage caused by his mistake.
She wished she could undo the harsh words she had said.
You can undo your last change by pressing Ctrl+Z.

Related Terms

  • antonym: redo - to do something again or restore its state
  • synonym: reverse - to turn something the opposite way or change the effect to its opposite
