undo

verb
remove or reverse an effect or property
2.6 3.04/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàdu / (AmE), /  ånÀàduÀê / (BrE)
to reverse, cancel, or remove the effect of an action, decision, or its consequences

Examples

To undo a mistake
She tried to undo the damage caused by her earlier comments.
The new evidence will undo much of the public's confidence in the report.
Years of progress were undone by a single careless mistake.
He tried to undo the damage caused by his mistake.
She wished she could undo the harsh words she had said.
You can undo your last change by pressing Ctrl+Z.

Synonyms

  • nullify
    6.8 0.83/m
    nullify makes a rule or action lose its legal or practical power, while undo simply reverses or removes an earlier action's effect.
  • cancel out
    4.0 1.07/m
    cancel out neutralizes one effect by another so the result disappears, while undo removes or reverses a previous action directly.
  • cancel
    1.0 9.36/m
    cancel makes something invalid or stops it from happening, while undo reverses or removes the earlier action's effect.
  • revert
    4.2 5.85/m
    revert returns a system or state back to how it was before, while undo removes the effect of a specific action.
  • unmake
    7.5 0.33/m
    unmake undoes or destroys something that was created, while undo is the general act of reversing a previous action or result.
  • reverse
    4.9 7.14/m
    reverse officially cancels or overturns a decision in law or policy, while undo is a broader act of removing an action's effect.
  • rescind
    7.9 2.09/m
    rescind officially cancels a law, order, or agreement, while undo refers to reversing or removing an earlier action more generally.
  • revoke
    6.2 3.96/m
    revoke officially takes away the validity or right of something, while undo describes reversing an action or its effect in general use.
  • overturn
    5.6 5.85/m
    overturn cancels or reverses a decision often by a higher court or body, while undo means remove or reverse an action's effect broadly.
