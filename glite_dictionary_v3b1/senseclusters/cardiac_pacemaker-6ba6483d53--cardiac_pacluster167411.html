cardiac pacemaker

noun
tissue regulating heartbeat
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrdiæk ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiæk ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
A region of specialized heart tissue that regulates the heartbeat by generating electrical impulses.

Examples

Cardiac pacemaker tissue
A malfunction in the cardiac pacemaker may require the implantation of an artificial device.
Damage to the cardiac pacemaker can lead to serious heart rhythm disorders.
The cardiac pacemaker ensures that your heart beats in a regular rhythm.
