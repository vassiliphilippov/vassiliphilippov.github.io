crackpotism

noun
rule by eccentrics
Pronunciation: / ˈkrækˌpɑːtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkrækˌpɒtɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality, state, or situation of being dominated by eccentric, absurd, or wildly impractical ideas or people

Examples

Crackpotism in ideas
The new policy was criticized as an example of crackpotism by many experts in the field.
Some feared that crackpotism was taking over the organization after several bizarre decisions.
The rise of crackpotism in leadership led to confusion and instability within the company.
Many dismissed his invention as mere crackpotism, but he was convinced it would change the world.
The book is filled with entertaining stories of crackpotism from history.
She worried that her new theory would be seen as crackpotism by her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eccentricity - unusual or odd behavior or ideas
  • synonym: lunacy - extreme foolishness or madness
  • antonym: rationality - the quality of being based on reason or logic
  • antonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind and reasonable ideas
