PCI

noun
computer hardware interface
5.8
Pronunciation: /piː siː aɪ/ (AmE), /piː siː aɪ/ (BrE)
a standard interface used to connect hardware components, such as graphics cards, inside a computer

Examples

PCI slot
The graphics card fits into a PCI slot on the motherboard.
Older computers often use PCI slots for network and sound cards.
To upgrade your system, check if it supports PCI devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: AGP - a high-speed point-to-point channel for attaching a graphics card to a computer's motherboard
  • degree higher: PCIe - a newer, faster standard for connecting hardware components inside a computer
