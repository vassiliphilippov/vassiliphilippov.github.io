fish fuddle

noun
tree yielding fish poison, native to West Indies/Florida
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ ˈfʌdəl / (AmE), / fɪʃ ˈfʌdəl / (BrE)
A small tree native to the West Indies and Florida, with large odd-pinnate leaves and clusters of red-striped purple to white flowers, which produces decorative curly winged seedpods and yields substances used as fish poisons.

Examples

The fish fuddle tree
In botanical gardens, the fish fuddle attracts attention with its unique curly seedpods.
The locals used the bark of the fish fuddle to poison fish in shallow waters.
The wood from the fish fuddle is sometimes used in traditional crafts in the Caribbean.
