Rocky Mountains cherry

noun
shrub with sweet edible fruit, western US
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑki ˈmaʊntənz ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈrɒki ˈmaʊntɪnz ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A dwarf ornamental shrub native to the western United States, bearing large, sweet black to red and yellow edible fruit.

Examples

The Rocky Mountains cherry fruit
Early settlers enjoyed making preserves from the ripe Rocky Mountains cherries.
The Rocky Mountains cherry is often planted in gardens for its attractive flowers and fruit.
The sweet, colorful fruit of the Rocky Mountains cherry attracts many birds in late summer.
