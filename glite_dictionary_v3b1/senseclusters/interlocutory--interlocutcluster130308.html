interlocutory

adjective
temporary legal decision
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈlɑːk.jə.tər.ɪ/ /; / /ɪn.tər.ˈlɑːk.juː.tər.ɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈlɒk.jə.tə.rɪ/ /; / /ɪn.tə.ˈlɒk.juː.tə.rɪ/ (BrE)
relating to a temporary or provisional decision or order, especially in a legal case, that is not final

Examples

An interlocutory order
The court issued an interlocutory injunction to prevent the sale of the property until the trial ended.
Lawyers discussed whether the interlocutory order could be appealed before the case was resolved.
An interlocutory decision is not the final judgment in a legal dispute.
The judge issued an interlocutory order to maintain the status quo until the trial.
An interlocutory decision was made to address the urgent issue before a final ruling.
The board released an interlocutory statement while the investigation was still ongoing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: final - coming at the end; not to be changed
  • synonym: preliminary - coming before the main or final part
  • synonym: provisional - arranged or existing for the present, possibly to be changed later
  • synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
