achromaticity

noun
absence of chromatic color
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪkroʊməˈtɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌeɪkrəʊməˈtɪsəti / (BrE)
the property of being without color; the absence of chromatic content in visual perception or materials

Examples

The achromaticity of images
He preferred the achromaticity of black-and-white photographs to the vibrancy of color images.
Scientists measured the achromaticity of the lens to assess its optical performance.
The achromaticity of the design gave it a minimalist and elegant appearance.
