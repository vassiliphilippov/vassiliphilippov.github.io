Red River

proper noun
tributary of the Mississippi River in the southern US
2.2
Pronunciation: /red ˈrɪv.ər/ (AmE), /red ˈrɪv.ə/ (BrE)
a river in the southern United States, a tributary of the Mississippi River that flows eastward from Texas along the southern boundary of Oklahoma and through Louisiana

Examples

The Red River floods
Heavy rains caused the Red River to overflow its banks last spring.
The Red River forms part of the border between Texas and Oklahoma.
The cotton fields near the Red River are especially fertile because of the rich soil.
After heavy rains, the Red River sometimes floods low-lying areas in Louisiana.
Many historical events in the region took place along the Red River.
The Red River forms much of the border between Texas and Oklahoma.
