charade

noun
false outward show; pretense
5.3 7.4
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ʃəˈrɑːd / (BrE)
an act or event that is clearly false or intended to deceive; a false outward show or pretense

Examples

A charade of happiness
She grew tired of maintaining the charade of happiness at social gatherings.
The announcement was nothing but a charade meant to appease the public.
Their supposed friendship was a charade to mask their rivalry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceit - the action or practice of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful
  • synonym: masquerade - a false show or pretense
  • synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
