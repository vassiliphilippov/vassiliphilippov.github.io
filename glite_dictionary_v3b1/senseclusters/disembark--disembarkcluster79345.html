disembark

verb
leave a ship, plane, or vehicle
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbɑrk / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle after a journey

Examples

To disembark from plane
After a long flight, passengers disembarked quickly to stretch their legs.
The captain announced that we would disembark in thirty minutes.
Tourists lined up to disembark once the ferry had docked.

Related Terms

  • antonym: embark - to board a ship, aircraft, or vehicle for a journey
