onomatopoeia

noun
words imitating natural sounds
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːnəˌmætəˈpiːə / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˌmætəˈpɪə / (BrE)
the use or formation of words that imitate or suggest the natural sound associated with the object or action involved, such as 'buzz' or 'sizzle'

Examples

Onomatopoeia words
Many children's books use onomatopoeia to make stories more engaging and fun.
Poets often use onomatopoeia to create vivid sound effects in their writing.
The word 'meow' is an example of onomatopoeia because it resembles the sound a cat makes.
