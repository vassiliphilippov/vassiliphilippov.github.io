mask

verb
shield from light
6.2 3.1
Pronunciation: /mæsk/ (AmE), /mɑːsk/ (BrE)
to cover, conceal, or hide something so it cannot be seen or recognized (physically or metaphorically), or to put a mask on someone or something

Examples

To mask the smell
Artists sometimes mask certain areas of a canvas to protect them from paint or light exposure.
To photograph the eclipse safely, astronomers often mask their telescopes' lenses.
You should mask the sensor to prevent direct sunlight from affecting the readings.
Heavy makeup can mask the signs of tiredness on your face.
She tried to mask her disappointment with a forced smile.
The strong scent of mint masks the smell of onions in the dish.
He likes to mask the vegetables with a creamy cheese sauce to make them more appealing.
The chef decided to mask the fish in a rich béchamel sauce before baking.
You should mask the lamb with a mint glaze for extra flavor.

  • synonym: disguise - to alter appearance to conceal identity
  • antonym: reveal - to make known or visible
