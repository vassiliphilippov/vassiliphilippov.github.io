chivalrous

adjective
courteous and attentive, esp. to women
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪvəlrəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəlrəs / (BrE)
showing courage, kindness, and polite, protective behavior, especially like an ideal knight

Examples

A chivalrous gesture
He held the door open in a chivalrous gesture that made her smile.
It was chivalrous of him to offer his seat to the elderly lady.
Many see his old-fashioned manners as chivalrous rather than outdated.
The chivalrous knight offered his seat to the elderly woman on the crowded bus.
He was praised for his chivalrous actions during the emergency, helping everyone stay calm.
Her chivalrous behavior impressed her friends, as she always looked out for others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: courteous - polite and respectful in manner
  • synonym: gallant - showing special attention or courtesy to women
  • degree lower: polite - having or showing good manners and respect
  • antonym: rude - impolite or disrespectful
