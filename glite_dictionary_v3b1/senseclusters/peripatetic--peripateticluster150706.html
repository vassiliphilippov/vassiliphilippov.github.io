peripatetic

adjective
traveling from place to place
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəpəˈtɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk / (BrE)
traveling from place to place, especially on foot or for work

Examples

Peripatetic teacher
For years, he worked as a peripatetic musician, performing in different towns each night.
She led a peripatetic lifestyle, moving from city to city every few months.
The professor’s peripatetic career took him to universities around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: itinerant - traveling from place to place, especially for work
  • antonym: settled - having a fixed or permanent home
