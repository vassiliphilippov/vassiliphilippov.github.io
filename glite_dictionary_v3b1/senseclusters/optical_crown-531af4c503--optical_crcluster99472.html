optical crown

noun
type of low-dispersion optical glass
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl kraʊn / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl kraʊn / (BrE)
a type of optical glass characterized by low dispersion and low refractive index, used in lenses and optical instruments

Examples

Optical crown glass
Engineers often choose optical crown for lenses because of its low dispersion properties.
Modern camera systems may use a combination of optical crown and flint glass to correct chromatic aberration.
The microscope's objective lens was made from high-quality optical crown to ensure sharp imaging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crown glass - a general term for a type of glass with similar optical properties
  • antonym: flint glass - a type of optical glass with high dispersion
