judder

verb
shake or vibrate forcefully
8.4 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊ’ÊŒdÉš / (AmE), / ËˆdÊ’ÊŒdÉ™ / (BrE)
(of an object, vehicle, etc.) to shake or vibrate rapidly and intensely, typically making a loud noise.

Examples

The car judders hard
He felt the steering wheel judder in his hands when he braked suddenly.
The car began to judder as it climbed the steep hill.
The washing machine juddered violently during the spin cycle.

Synonyms

  • shake
    1.0 50/m
    shake is a general quick movement or tremble, while judder is an intense, noisy vibration often felt in objects or vehicles.
  • rattle
    2.2 9.73/m
    rattle makes repeated short, sharp noises from loose parts, while judder is a heavy, rapid vibration that often sounds and shakes the whole object.
  • vibrate
    2.8 6.01/m
    vibrate moves in small, quick back-and-forth motions, while judder is a stronger, louder shaking often felt through vehicles or tools.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shudder - to tremble or shake, typically from fear or cold
  • antonym: smooth - moving or operating without jerking or vibration
  • synonym: vibrate - to move rapidly back and forth
