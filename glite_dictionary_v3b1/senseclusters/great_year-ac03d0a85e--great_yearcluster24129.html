great year

noun
astronomical period of precessional cycle
6.2
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt jɪr / (AmE), / ɡreɪt jɪə / (BrE)
the period required for one complete cycle of the precession of the equinoxes, approximately 25,800 years

Examples

The great year cycle
In mythology, a great year was sometimes linked to cycles of renewal or rebirth.
Some astronomers study ancient records to better estimate the length of a great year.
The precession of the equinoxes completes one great year roughly every 25,800 years.
