presser

noun
person who pressures
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsər / (AmE), / ˈprɛsə / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who pressures or coerces others to do something, often through persuasion or intimidation

Examples

A contract presser
The presser kept calling until I finally agreed to sign the contract.
Nobody wanted to deal with the presser who always tried to get his way.
She became known as a presser after convincing several colleagues to change their votes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bully - a person who uses strength or power to harm or intimidate others
  • synonym: coercer - a person who forces someone to do something by using threats or pressure
  • antonym: supporter - a person who encourages or helps others
