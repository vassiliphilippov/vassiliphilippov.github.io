shylocking

verb
to overcharge exploitatively
Pronunciation: / ˈʃaɪlɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪlɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
to unfairly or exploitatively charge someone extremely high prices or fees for goods or services

Examples

To shylocking customers
Some lenders were accused of shylocking desperate families during the crisis.
The mechanic was shylocking customers by charging double the normal rate for repairs.
Critics say the company is shylocking its clients with hidden fees and surcharges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gouge - to overcharge or swindle someone
  • synonym: rip off - to cheat someone by charging too much
  • antonym: undercharge - to charge less than the correct or fair price
