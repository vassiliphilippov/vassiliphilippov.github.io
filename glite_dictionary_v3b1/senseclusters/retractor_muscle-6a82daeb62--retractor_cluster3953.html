retractor muscle

noun
muscle that retracts a body part
5.4
Pronunciation: / ɹɪˈtræktɚ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ɹɪˈtræktə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a muscle that retracts an organ or body part by drawing it back toward the body

Examples

Retractor muscle function
After surgery, the patient's retractor muscle took several weeks to regain normal function.
The retractor muscle in some snails helps pull their body back into the shell for protection.
Veterinarians studied the retractor muscle to understand how certain animals control limb movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extensor muscle - a muscle that extends or straightens a limb or body part
  • degree lower: flexor muscle - a muscle that serves to bend a limb or body part
