Synonyms

  • patrician
    8.6 0.68/m
    patrician is refined and shows upper-class manners in formal settings, while aristocratic describes belonging to the nobility or its manners.
  • highbred
    7.4 0.01/m
    highbred shows good breeding and polite manners in company, while aristocratic refers more broadly to noble origin or style.
  • noble
    4.4 4.75/m
    noble names people of hereditary rank and titles in history and law, while aristocratic highlights class and refined manners.
  • highborn
    7.5 0.11/m
    highborn means born into the upper class by family, while aristocratic can mean both birth and the behavior of the upper class.
  • wellborn
    9.1 0.01/m
    wellborn describes people born into gentle or upper families, while aristocratic often stresses both rank and refined manners.
  • blue-blooded
    8.2 0.10/m
    blue-blooded sounds informal and a little joking about noble birth, while aristocratic sounds neutral and formal about the nobility.
