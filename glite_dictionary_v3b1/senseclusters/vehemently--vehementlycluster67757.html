vehemently

adverb
with force or strong feeling
7.4 2.99/m
Pronunciation: / Ààvi…ôm…ôntli / (AmE), / ÀàviÀê.…ô.m…ônt.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows strong feeling, force, or passion; intensely or forcefully.

Examples

To vehemently oppose
He vehemently opposed the idea, arguing his case with force and conviction.
She vehemently protested the new policy during the heated meeting.
The politician vehemently denied all allegations brought against him.
