photoperiodism

noun
response to day length
9.1
Pronunciation: /foʊ.tə.ˈpɪr.iː.ə.dɪz.əm/ (AmE), /fəʊ.tə.ˈpɪə.riː.ə.dɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the response of an organism to the length of day or night, often affecting growth or behavior

Examples

Photoperiodism in plants
Many plants rely on photoperiodism to know when to start flowering in the spring.
Scientists study photoperiodism to understand how animals adapt to seasonal changes.
The timing of leaf fall in some trees is controlled by photoperiodism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circadian rhythm - the natural, internal process that regulates the sleep-wake cycle
  • synonym: phototropism - growth or movement of an organism in response to light
