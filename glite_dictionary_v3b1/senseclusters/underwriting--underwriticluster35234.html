underwriting

noun
guaranteeing securities sale
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of guaranteeing and arranging the sale of new securities, agreeing to buy any unsold shares or bonds

Examples

Stock underwriting
The investment bank's underwriting of the new stock issue helped the company raise capital.
Without proper underwriting, the risk of financial loss increases for both the issuer and investors.
Many financial institutions specialize in underwriting to support companies entering public markets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guarantee - a formal promise to accept responsibility if something fails
  • synonym: insurance - a contract providing financial protection against loss
