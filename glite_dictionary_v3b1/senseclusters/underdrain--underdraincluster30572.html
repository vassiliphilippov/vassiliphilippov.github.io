underdrain

verb
to install drainage pipes
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərdreɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌndədreɪn / (BrE)
to install subsurface pipes or channels to remove excess water from land or a structure

Examples

To underdrain land
The engineers decided to underdrain the sports field to prevent water from pooling after heavy rain.
Farmers often underdrain their fields to improve crop growth in wet areas.
The construction crew will underdrain the new road to protect it from water damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drain - to remove liquid from a place
  • antonym: flood - to cover with water, especially excessively
