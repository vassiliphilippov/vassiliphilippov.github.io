monogenic disease

noun
genetic disorder caused by a single gene
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊnəˈdʒɛnɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈdʒɛnɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease inherited as the result of a mutation in a single gene pair.

Examples

A monogenic disease mutation
Cystic fibrosis is a well-known example of a monogenic disease caused by mutations in the CFTR gene.
Genetic counseling can help families understand the risks associated with monogenic disease.
Researchers have identified several monogenic diseases that follow a predictable inheritance pattern.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polygenic disease - a disease influenced by many genes
