field hockey

noun
outdoor team sport with sticks and ball
2.9 2.0
Pronunciation: /fiːld ˈhɑː.kiː/ /; / /fɪld ˈhɑː.kiː/ (AmE), /fiːld ˈhɒ.kiː/ /; / /fiːld ˈhɒk.ɪ/ (BrE)
A team sport played on grass or turf in which players use curved sticks to hit a small hard ball into the opposing team's goal.

Examples

Many countries consider field hockey one of their most popular outdoor games.

The school has a top-ranked field hockey team that competes at the national level.
