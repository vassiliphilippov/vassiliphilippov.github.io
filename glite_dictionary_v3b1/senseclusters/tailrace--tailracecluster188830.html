tailrace

noun
outflow channel from a mill or turbine
5.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪlˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌreɪs / (BrE)
A channel or watercourse that carries water away from a mill, water wheel, or turbine after it has done its work.

Examples

The tailrace channel
After passing through the turbine, the water flows out through the tailrace into the river.
Debris had collected in the tailrace, reducing the efficiency of the water wheel.
The engineers designed a tailrace to ensure the smooth discharge of water from the power plant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: headrace - the channel that brings water to a mill or turbine
