crown glass

noun
type of flat spun window glass
Pronunciation: / kraʊn ɡlæs / (AmE), / kraʊn ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A type of glass made by blowing molten glass into a globe and spinning it into a flat disk, formerly used for windows.

Examples

Old crown glass window
Historic buildings often feature crown glass in their original window panes.
The unique swirling patterns of crown glass make it highly prized by conservators.
Unlike modern plate glass, crown glass has small variations in thickness and clarity.
