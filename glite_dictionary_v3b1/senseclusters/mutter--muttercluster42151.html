mutter

noun
low, indistinct complaint or remark
6.8 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌt̬ɚ /; / ˈmʌtər / (AmE), / ˈmʌtə / (BrE)
a low, indistinct sound or complaint, often spoken quietly or in the background

Examples

A quiet mutter
A quiet mutter spread through the crowd as the news was announced.
His only response was a disgruntled mutter under his breath.
She ignored his mutter about the cold food.
She could hear the faint mutter of the rain against the window.
The constant mutter of voices in the café made it hard to concentrate.
There was a mutter of discontent among the crowd as the decision was announced.
