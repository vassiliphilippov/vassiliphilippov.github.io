brooding

adjective
deeply or seriously thoughtful
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbruːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbruːdɪŋ / (BrE)
showing deep or serious thought, often with a slightly dark or somber mood

Examples

Brooding expression
She sat by the window, lost in a brooding silence as the rain fell.
The novel's brooding protagonist spends hours contemplating his fate.
There was a brooding intensity in his eyes that made everyone uneasy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: thoughtful - absorbed in thought; reflective
