nonvoter

noun
person not allowed to vote
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈvoʊtər / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈvəʊtə / (BrE)
a person who is not legally allowed to vote in an election, often due to age, citizenship, or legal reasons

Examples

Underage nonvoter
Because she was only sixteen, she was considered a nonvoter in the national election.
Citizenship status can determine whether someone is a nonvoter or eligible to participate.
After his conviction, he became a nonvoter and could not participate in local elections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: voter - a person who is legally allowed to vote in an election
