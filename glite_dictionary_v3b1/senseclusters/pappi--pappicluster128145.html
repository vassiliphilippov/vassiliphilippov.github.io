pappi

noun
seed bristles
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpæpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpæpaɪ / (BrE)
fine bristles or hairs attached to the seeds of some plants, helping them disperse by wind

Examples

The pappi on seeds
The pappi on dandelion seeds allow them to travel long distances on the wind.
Botanists studied the structure of pappi to understand how seeds are dispersed.
Many plants in the daisy family have pappi that look like tiny parachutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pappus - the tuft of hairs on a seed that aids in dispersal
