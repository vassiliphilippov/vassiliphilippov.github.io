associationism

noun
theory that ideas are linked by association
6.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /ə.soʊ.siː.ˈeɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ə.səʊ.siː.ˈeɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
(psychology) the theory that mental processes operate by the association of ideas or experiences.

Examples

Associationism theory
Early psychologists debated whether associationism could explain all aspects of learning and memory.
Many introductory psychology courses discuss associationism alongside other historical approaches.
The principles of associationism influenced later theories of cognition and behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behaviorism - the theory that human and animal behavior can be explained in terms of conditioning, without appeal to thoughts or feelings
