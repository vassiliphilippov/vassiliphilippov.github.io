shut down

verb
stop operating (business, machine, etc.)
2.9 18/m
Pronunciation: / ÊƒÊŒt daÊŠn / (AmE), / ÊƒÊŒt daÊŠn / (BrE)
to stop or turn off the operation of a machine, business, or system, either temporarily or permanently

Examples

To shut down a computer
Before you leave the office, please shut down your computer properly.
The company decided to shut down its overseas factories due to rising costs.
They had to shut down production temporarily after the accident.
During the storm, authorities decided to shut down the airport.
She had to shut down her computer because it was overheating.
The factory will shut down next week for maintenance.

    1.1 3.13/m
    switch off means flicking a switch to stop a light or machine, while shut down can end a whole system or business.
  • shut off
    2.6 3.79/m

  • deactivate
    3.9 1.65/m
    deactivate makes a device or function inactive, often in technical settings, while shut down can be temporary or permanent.
  • halt
    2.4 14/m
    halt brings movement or a process to an immediate stop, while shut down refers to stopping machines, businesses, or systems.
  • suspend
    3.9 6.82/m
    suspend officially stops an activity or service for a short time, while shut down can mean stopping permanently or temporarily.
  • close up
    3.5 1.24/m
    close up means to stop a shop or place from opening, sometimes briefly, while shut down covers machines or whole systems.
  • close down
    2.9 3.72/m
    close down ends a business or organization permanently, while shut down can be used for both temporary and permanent stops.
  • close
    1.0 117/m
