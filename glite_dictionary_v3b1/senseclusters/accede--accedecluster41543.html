accede

verb
to assume an office or position
8.4 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / …ôkÀàsiÀêd / (AmE), / …ôkÀàsiÀêd / (BrE)
to assume an official position or office, especially with reference to a throne or leadership role

Examples

To accede to the throne
After the king's death, his daughter acceded to the throne and began her reign.
He will accede to the presidency next month, following the official ceremony.
It is customary for the heir apparent to accede immediately upon the ruler's abdication.

Synonyms

  • take one's place on the throne
    8.4 0.01/m
    take one's place on the throne describes stepping onto the monarch's seat in ceremony, while accede names formally assuming that leadership role.
  • succeed
    4.4 10/m
    succeed often means legally follow someone into a role, while accede is a formal way to assume that office or title.
  • take office
    3.6 5.99/m
    take office means to begin serving after election or appointment, while accede sounds more formal and can imply royal succession.
  • come to power
    4.2 2.77/m
    come to power means gaining political control, often by force or election, while accede focuses on formally assuming an official position.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abdicate - to formally give up a position of power, especially a throne
  • synonym: ascend - to rise to a position, such as a throne or office
