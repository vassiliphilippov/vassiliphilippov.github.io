strident

adjective
forceful and unpleasantly loud or insistent
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪdənt /; / ˈstraɪdnt / (AmE), / ˈstraɪdnt / (BrE)
loud, harsh, and forceful in sound or manner, often unpleasant or aggressive

Examples

Strident voice
Her strident voice made it difficult for others to express their opinions.
The editorial took a strident tone, criticizing the government's new policy harshly.
The union issued strident demands for better working conditions during the negotiations.
Critics found the politician's speech too strident for their liking.
Her strident voice could be heard from across the street.
The strident alarm startled everyone in the building.
His strident criticism of the policy drew mixed reactions from the audience.
Protesters made strident demands for immediate political change.
The strident tone of her voice startled everyone in the room.
