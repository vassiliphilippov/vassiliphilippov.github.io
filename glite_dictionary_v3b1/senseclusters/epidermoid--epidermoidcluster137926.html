epidermoid

noun
tumor of epidermal cells
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdɜːrmɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɜːmɔɪd / (BrE)
a tumor made up of squamous (epidermal) cells, often malignant and also called an epidermoid carcinoma

Examples

Epidermoid tumor
The surgeon removed the epidermoid to prevent further complications.
After the diagnosis, the patient learned that the epidermoid was malignant.
Doctors monitored the growth of the epidermoid with regular scans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benign tumor - a non-cancerous growth of cells
  • synonym: carcinoma - a type of cancer that starts in epithelial cells
