save

verb
spend less; buy for less money
Pronunciation: / seɪv / (AmE), / seɪv / (BrE)
to spend less or use less of something, especially money or resources, in order to avoid waste

Examples

To save money
If you shop during the sale, you can save quite a bit of money.
She always looks for coupons to save on groceries.
We managed to save 30% by booking our flights early.
By making her own lunch, she managed to save a lot of money over time.
If you want to buy a car, you should save as much as possible each month.
Turning off the lights when you leave a room can save electricity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: economize - to reduce expenses; use less money
  • antonym: spend - to pay out money or use up resources
  • antonym: splurge - to spend money freely or extravagantly
