save

verb
spend less; buy for less money
1.0 36/m
Pronunciation: / seÉªv / (AmE), / seÉªv / (BrE)
to spend less or use less of something, especially money or resources, in order to avoid waste

Examples

To save money
If you shop during the sale, you can save quite a bit of money.
She always looks for coupons to save on groceries.
We managed to save 30% by booking our flights early.
By making her own lunch, she managed to save a lot of money over time.
If you want to buy a car, you should save as much as possible each month.
Turning off the lights when you leave a room can save electricity.

Synonyms

  • economize
    5.0 0.60/m
    economize means using money or time carefully to avoid waste, while save often means keeping resources by using less.
  • conserve
    3.4 5.91/m
    conserve means protecting resources by careful use or management, while save is more about reducing use or spending.
  • cut back on
    3.2 4.15/m
    cut back on means reducing how much you use or buy to lower costs, while save means spending or using less to avoid waste.
  • cut back
    2.9 7.86/m
    cut back means reducing the amount, size, or use of something to spend less, while save means keeping resources by using less.
  • cut costs
    3.0 2.86/m
    cut costs means lowering the money spent in a business or plan, while save covers using less in many everyday situations.
  • curtail spending
    4.3 0.04/m
    curtail spending means formally reducing how much money is spent, often by organizations, while save is a more general act of using less.
