LBW

noun
low birth weight
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl biː ˈdʌbljuː / (AmE), / ˌɛl biː ˈdʌbljuː / (BrE)
a medical abbreviation for a baby born weighing less than 2,500 grams (about 5 pounds 8 ounces)

Examples

LBW baby
Doctors closely monitored the LBW infant to ensure healthy development.
Babies classified as LBW may need special care in the hospital.
The hospital has a program to support parents of LBW newborns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal birth weight - a baby born weighing 2,500 grams or more
  • synonym: premature - born before the usual time of birth
