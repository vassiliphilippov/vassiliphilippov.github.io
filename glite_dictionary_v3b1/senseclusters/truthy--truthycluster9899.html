truthy

adjective
appearing true
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtruːθi / (AmE), / ˈtruːθi / (BrE)
seeming to be true or believable, often based on feeling or incomplete evidence rather than actual proof

Examples

Truthy story
His story sounded truthy, but there was no real evidence to support it.
The politician made a truthy claim that appealed to people's emotions rather than facts.
Some rumors are truthy enough to spread quickly, even if they are not actually true.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credible - able to be believed; convincing
  • antonym: false - not true; incorrect
  • synonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable
