quiet

noun
period of calm weather
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪət /; / ˈkwaɪ.ət / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət /; / ˈkwaɪ.ət / (BrE)
a state of calm, peace, or silence; the absence of noise or disturbance

Examples

A moment of quiet
After hours of heavy rain, there was a welcome quiet before the next wave of the storm.
Sailors waited for the quiet to end before raising anchor again.
The sudden quiet after the howling winds made the air seem eerily peaceful.
After the children left, the house settled into a deep quiet.
She closed the door and enjoyed the quiet of the empty room.
We sat in quiet, listening to the rain tapping on the windows.
Despite the chaos outside, she maintained an inner quiet throughout the crisis.
He sought quiet in meditation to ease his troubled mind.
Her quiet was undisturbed even during the most stressful situations.
