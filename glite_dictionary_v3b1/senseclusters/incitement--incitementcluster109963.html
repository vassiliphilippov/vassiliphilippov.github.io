incitement

noun
the act of urging someone to action
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of encouraging or urging someone to do something bad, illegal, or violent

Examples

An incitement to violence
His speech was accused of being an incitement to violence among the protesters.
She faced charges for the incitement of a public disturbance.
The law strictly prohibits the incitement of hatred against any group.
Publishing such content could be regarded as an incitement to break the law.
The leader's speech was seen as an incitement to violence by many people.
There was no clear incitement to riot in the article, according to the investigation.
His speech was seen as an incitement to violence by the authorities.
The government passed laws to prevent incitement of hatred between different groups.
There was no evidence of incitement to commit any criminal act.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deterrence - the act of discouraging an action through fear or doubt
  • antonym: deterrent - something that discourages someone from taking action
  • antonym: discouragement - the act of trying to prevent something by making it seem difficult or by making someone less willing to do it
  • synonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence to do something
  • synonym: instigation - the act of causing an event or action to happen, often something negative
  • synonym: provocation - the action of provoking or stirring up
