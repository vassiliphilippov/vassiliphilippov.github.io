knit one's brows

verb
frown or show concern
Pronunciation: / nɪt wʌnz braʊz / (AmE), / nɪt wʌnz braʊz / (BrE)
to frown or contract the eyebrows, especially as a sign of concentration, confusion, or concern

Examples

Knit his brows in concentration
He knit his brows in concentration as he read the contract.
She tends to knit her brows whenever she's trying to solve a difficult problem.
Whenever bad news arrives, people often knit their brows in worry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frown - to contract the brow in displeasure or concentration
