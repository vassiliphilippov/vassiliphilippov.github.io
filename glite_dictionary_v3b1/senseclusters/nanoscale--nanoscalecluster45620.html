nanoscale

noun
nanometer size range
Pronunciation: / ˈnænoʊˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈnanəʊskeɪl / (BrE)
a size range typically measured in nanometers, especially relevant to atoms, molecules, and very small structures

Examples

Nanoscale structures
Scientists can manipulate materials at the nanoscale to create new properties.
The nanoscale allows researchers to study the behavior of individual molecules.
Many modern technologies rely on components built at the nanoscale.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: atomic scale - the size range of individual atoms, smaller than nanoscale
  • degree higher: microscale - a size range measured in micrometers, larger than nanoscale
