ex gratia

adverb
done as a favor, not legally required
9.9 7.8
Pronunciation: / ɛks ˈɡreɪʃiə / (AmE), / ɛks ˈɡreɪʃə / (BrE)
(of a payment) made as a favor or out of goodwill, not because of any legal obligation.

Examples

Ex gratia payment
After the delay, passengers received ex gratia compensation from the airline.
The company offered an ex gratia payment to the injured worker, even though they were not legally liable.
The government made an ex gratia donation to families affected by the disaster.
