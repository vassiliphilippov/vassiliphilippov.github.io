OTC security

noun
security traded outside formal exchanges
1.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ tiː siː sɪ.ˈkjʊr.ə.ti/ /; / /oʊ tiː siː sɪ.ˈkjʊr.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /əʊ tiː siː sɪ.ˈkjʊə.rɪ.ti/ /; / /əʊ tiː siː sɪ.ˈkjʊə.rə.ti/ (BrE)
A security, such as a stock or bond, that is traded in the over-the-counter market rather than on a formal exchange.

Examples

OTC security trading
Investors should be aware that an OTC security may be less liquid than exchange-traded stocks.
Many small companies raise capital by offering an OTC security to the public.
The company opted to list its OTC security instead of applying for a major exchange.
