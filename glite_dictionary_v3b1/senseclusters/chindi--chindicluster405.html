chindi

noun
Navajo evil spirit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪndi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪndi / (BrE)
in Navajo belief, a harmful spirit left behind after a person's death that can bring misfortune to the living

Examples

A chindi spirit
According to Navajo tradition, a chindi may linger after a person's death and cause harm.
The family performed rituals to protect themselves from the influence of a chindi.
Stories about the chindi are often told to teach respect for the dead.
