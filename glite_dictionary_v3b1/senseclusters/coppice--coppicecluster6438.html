coppice

verb
to cut trees for regrowth
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑpɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒpɪs / (BrE)
to cut trees or shrubs down to ground level to encourage new growth, especially as a woodland management technique

Examples

To coppice the woods
The woodland was coppiced every few years to provide a sustainable supply of firewood.
Farmers often coppice willow trees to encourage rapid regrowth for basket making.
After the trees were coppiced, new shoots quickly appeared from the old stumps.
The foresters coppice the willow trees every few years to maintain healthy growth.
Local conservation groups regularly coppice sections of the woodland to support wildlife habitats.
Farmers sometimes coppice hazel trees to provide materials for fencing and crafts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fell - to cut down a tree
  • synonym: prune - to trim a tree or shrub by cutting away dead or overgrown branches
