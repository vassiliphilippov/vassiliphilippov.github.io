traceless

adjective
leaving no trace
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsləs / (BrE)
leaving no sign, mark, or evidence behind; impossible or very difficult to detect or find

Examples

Traceless escape
The thief left the house traceless, making it difficult for the police to find any clues.
After the rain, the chalk drawings on the sidewalk became completely traceless.
The spy moved through the building in a traceless manner, leaving no evidence of his presence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invisible - impossible to see; not visible
  • antonym: obvious - easily seen or noticed; clear
  • synonym: undetectable - impossible to discover or notice
