rediscount

noun
repeat discounting
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (BrE)
the act of discounting a bill, note, or other financial instrument again, usually by a central bank for commercial banks

Examples

The central bank introduced a rediscount policy to provide liquidity to commercial banks.
Banks may use the rediscount facility to manage their short-term funding needs.
A sudden increase in rediscount activity can signal stress in the financial system.
