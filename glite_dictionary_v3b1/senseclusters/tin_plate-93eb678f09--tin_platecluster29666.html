tin plate

noun
iron or steel sheet coated with tin
5.3 3.3
Pronunciation: /tɪn pleɪt/ (AmE), /tɪn pleɪt/ (BrE)
a thin sheet of iron or steel coated with tin to protect it from rust, used especially to make containers and kitchenware

Examples

Tin plate sheet
Cans are often made from tin plate to prevent the contents from rusting.
Old biscuit tins were typically crafted from brightly painted tin plate.
The workers shaped sheets of tin plate into kitchen utensils at the factory.
