pseudotuberculosis

noun
tuberculosis-like infection
9.9
Pronunciation: /suː.doʊ.tuː.bər.kjʊ.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ /; / /suː.doʊ.tuː.bər.kʊ.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /sjuː.dəʊ.tjuː.bə.kjʊ.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ /; / /suː.dəʊ.tuː.bə.kjʊ.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a bacterial infection in animals or humans that resembles tuberculosis but is caused by different bacteria

Examples

Pseudotuberculosis infection
The veterinarian diagnosed the goat with pseudotuberculosis after laboratory tests.
Humans can contract pseudotuberculosis through contact with infected animals or contaminated food.
Symptoms of pseudotuberculosis may include fever, swollen lymph nodes, and abdominal pain.
