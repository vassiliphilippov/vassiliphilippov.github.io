dechristianization

noun
loss of Christian faith
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˌkrɪstʃənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌkrɪstjənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing or removing Christian beliefs, practices, or influence from a person, society, or institution

Examples

The dechristianization process
The rapid dechristianization of the region led to major changes in cultural traditions.
Historians have studied the dechristianization that occurred during the French Revolution.
Personal dechristianization can be a gradual process influenced by many factors.
During the revolution, the government promoted dechristianization by closing churches and removing religious symbols.
The policy of dechristianization led to significant changes in cultural traditions and public ceremonies.
Historians debate the long-term effects of dechristianization on European societies.

  • antonym: conversion - the process of adopting a new religion or faith
