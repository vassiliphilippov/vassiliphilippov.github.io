bladdernose

noun
Arctic seal with inflatable nose sac
Pronunciation: / ˈblædərˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈblædəˌnəʊz / (BrE)
a medium-sized blackish-grey seal with a large inflatable sac on its head, found in Arctic and northern Atlantic waters

Examples

The bladdernose seal
A bladdernose can be easily identified by the distinctive hood on its head.
Researchers observed a group of bladdernoses on the Arctic ice floe.
The bladdernose inflates its nose sac when threatened or during mating displays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hooded seal - a species of seal (Cystophora cristata) with a similar inflatable nasal sac
