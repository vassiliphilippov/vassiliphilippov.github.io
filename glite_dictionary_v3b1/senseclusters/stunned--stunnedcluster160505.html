stunned

adjective
knocked unconscious; dazed
1.9 3.0
Pronunciation: / stʌnd / (AmE), / stʌnd / (BrE)
so shocked, surprised, or affected (physically or emotionally) that you cannot react or think clearly

Examples

Stunned and silent
After the accident, she lay stunned and unresponsive for several minutes.
He was stunned by a blow to the head and collapsed on the ground.
The boxer remained stunned after taking a powerful punch in the final round.
After hitting his head, he lay stunned on the ground, unable to move.
She was stunned by the unexpected news of her friend's arrival.
The audience sat stunned, struggling to process what had just happened.
He stood in stunned silence, unable to process what had happened.
She looked stunned when she heard the unexpected news.
The team was stunned by their loss in the final seconds of the game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive
  • synonym: astonished - feeling great surprise or amazement
  • synonym: dazed - unable to think clearly, often because of a shock or blow
  • synonym: shocked - feeling a sudden, intense surprise or disturbance
  • synonym: unconscious - not awake or aware, especially after being hit
  • antonym: unfazed - not perturbed or affected by surprise or shock
