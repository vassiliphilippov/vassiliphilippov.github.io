C.E.

adverb
abbreviation for 'Common Era' (dating years)
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsiː ˈiː / (AmE), / ˌsiː ˈiː / (BrE)
abbreviation for 'Common Era', used in dating years in the same way as A.D. but preferred in secular contexts

Examples

C.E. dates
Many historians now use C.E. instead of A.D. to indicate dates in the modern era.
The first written references to this tradition date from 300 C.E..
The temple was constructed around the 2nd century C.E..

Related Terms

  • synonym: A.D. - abbreviation for 'Anno Domini', used for years after the birth of Christ
  • antonym: B.C.E. - abbreviation for 'Before Common Era', secular equivalent of B.C.
