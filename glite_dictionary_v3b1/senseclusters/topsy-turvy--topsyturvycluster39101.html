topsy-turvy

adverb
in confusion or disorderly haste
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑpsi ˈtɜːrvi /; / ˌtɑːpsi ˈtɝːvi / (AmE), / ˌtɒpsi ˈtɜːvi / (BrE)
in a disorderly, confused, or upside-down way (used to describe how something happens)

Examples

Scattered topsy-turvy
Papers were scattered topsy-turvy all over the desk during the search.
The children ran topsy-turvy through the playground after the bell rang.
With the sudden news, everyone went topsy-turvy rushing to prepare.
After the children played, the living room looked topsy-turvy.
Her thoughts were all topsy-turvy after the surprising news.
The books on his desk were stacked topsy-turvy after the move.
