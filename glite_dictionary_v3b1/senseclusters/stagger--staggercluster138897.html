stagger

verb
arrange in an offset or alternating order
6.2 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈstæ.ɡər/ (AmE), /ˈstæ.ɡə/ (BrE)
to arrange things so that they are not in a straight line or do not happen at the same time, but are spaced out for practical reasons

Examples

To stagger the schedule
Please stagger the desks so that students have enough space between them.
The company chose to stagger the workstations to improve sight lines across the office.
The decorator decided to stagger the paintings along the hallway for a more dynamic look.
To reduce crowding, the company staggered employee lunch breaks throughout the afternoon.
The payments for the new equipment were staggered over several months to ease the financial burden.
Teachers staggered assignment deadlines so students would not be overwhelmed at once.

Related Terms

  • antonym: align - to place or arrange things in a straight line or proper position
  • antonym: concentrate - to bring together at the same time or place
  • synonym: schedule - to plan events or tasks at specific times
