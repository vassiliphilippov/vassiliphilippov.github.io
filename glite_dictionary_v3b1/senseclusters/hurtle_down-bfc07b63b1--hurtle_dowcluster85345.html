hurtle down

verb
move swiftly and uncontrollably downward
6.7 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…úrt…ôl da än / (AmE), / Ààh…úÀêtl da än / (BrE)
to move swiftly and uncontrollably downward or along a path, often with great speed and force

Examples

To hurtle down a hill
He lost his balance and started to hurtle down the steep hill uncontrollably.
Rocks began to hurtle down the mountainside after the loud explosion.
The sleds would hurtle down the icy track with astonishing speed.

Synonyms

  • plummet
    5.8 6.38/m
    plummet means fall very fast and straight down, while hurtle down implies moving swiftly and often uncontrollably along or downward.
  • plunge
    3.8 7.00/m
    plunge means fall or move suddenly down, often into something, while hurtle down stresses great speed and loss of control.
  • pelt down
    7.2 0.04/m
    pelt down means move very quickly, often headlong, while hurtle down implies powerful, often uncontrollable downward motion.
