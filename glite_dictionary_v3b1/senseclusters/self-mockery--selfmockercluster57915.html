self-mockery

noun
humorous criticism of oneself
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ˈmɑːkəri / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈmɒkəri / (BrE)
the act of making humorous or gentle fun of oneself; the quality of not taking oneself too seriously

Examples

Self-mockery in speech
He handled criticism with a healthy dose of self-mockery and good humor.
Her self-mockery made everyone feel at ease during the speech.
The comedian's self-mockery kept the audience laughing throughout the show.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-deprecation - the act of undervaluing oneself, often humorously
