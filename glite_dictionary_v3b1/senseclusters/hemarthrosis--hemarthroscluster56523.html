hemarthrosis

noun
joint bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmɑːrˈθroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɑːˈθrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
bleeding into a joint cavity, often resulting in swelling and pain

Examples

Hemarthrosis joint bleeding

Repeated hemarthrosis can lead to long-term joint damage if not treated promptly.
Athletes with bleeding disorders are at higher risk for hemarthrosis after injuries.
