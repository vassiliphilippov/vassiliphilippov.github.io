fletched

adjective
with feathers on arrow
8.3
Pronunciation: / flɛtʃt / (AmE), / flɛtʃt / (BrE)
having feathers, vanes, or fins attached to help something (like an arrow, dart, or rocket) fly straight or stay stable in the air

Examples

Fletched arrows
The hunter preferred fletched arrows for greater stability during flight.
Each fletched shaft was carefully crafted for the archery competition.
He noticed that the fletched arrows flew straighter than the plain ones.
The archer carefully inspected each fletched arrow before the competition began.
A fletched dart flies straighter and more accurately than one without feathers.
The museum displayed several ancient fletched projectiles used by early hunters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unfletched - without feathers or vanes attached to an arrow
