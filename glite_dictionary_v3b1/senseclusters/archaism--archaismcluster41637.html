archaism

noun
use of old-fashioned language or style
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrkiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the use or presence of words, phrases, or styles that are old-fashioned or no longer in everyday use

Examples

Old-fashioned archaism
The novel's dialogue was filled with archaism, making it sound as if it were written centuries ago.
The poet often employed archaism to evoke a sense of history in his work.
Using archaism can sometimes make a modern text difficult for readers to understand.
