cloistered

adjective
sheltered; providing privacy or seclusion
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔɪstɚd /; / ˈklɔɪstərd / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstəd / (BrE)
kept away from the outside world; living in privacy or seclusion, often used for people in religious communities

Examples

A cloistered life
She spent her childhood in a cloistered boarding school far from the city.
The university was criticized for its cloistered and insular approach to academic life.
They enjoyed a cloistered afternoon together in the quiet of the garden.
He chose a cloistered life devoted to prayer and study.
Raised in a cloistered community, she knew little of the outside world.
The monks led a cloistered existence within the monastery walls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exposed - not protected or sheltered
  • synonym: isolated - far away from other places or people; remote
  • antonym: open - not shut or secluded; exposed to view or social contact
  • synonym: secluded - providing seclusion or privacy
