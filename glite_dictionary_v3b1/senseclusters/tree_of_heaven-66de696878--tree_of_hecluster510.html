tree of heaven

noun
a rapidly growing Asian street tree (Ailanthus altissima)
9.9 6.1
Pronunciation: / tri əv ˈhɛvən / (AmE), / triː əv ˈhɛvən / (BrE)
a fast-growing deciduous tree (Ailanthus altissima) native to China, with leaves resembling those of sumac and clusters of sweet-smelling but unpleasantly scented flowers, commonly planted as a street tree due to its tolerance of urban pollution

Examples

The tree of heaven flowers
Clusters of yellow-green flowers give the tree of heaven its distinct, somewhat pungent scent in the summer.
Many gardeners consider the tree of heaven an invasive species due to its rapid spread.
The tree of heaven is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil and polluted urban areas.
