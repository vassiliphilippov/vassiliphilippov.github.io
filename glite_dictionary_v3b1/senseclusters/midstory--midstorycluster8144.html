midstory

noun
middle forest layer
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪd.stɔː.riː/ /; / /ˈmɪd.stɔːr.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈmɪd.stɔː.riː/ /; / /ˈmɪd.stɔː.ɪ/ (BrE)
the layer of vegetation in a forest that is located between the understory and the canopy

Examples

Forest midstory
Many birds and insects make their homes in the midstory of tropical forests.
The midstory receives less sunlight than the canopy but more than the understory.
Some tree species are found mainly in the midstory rather than in the upper or lower layers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: canopy - the uppermost layer of branches and leaves in a forest
  • antonym: understory - the layer of vegetation beneath the forest canopy
