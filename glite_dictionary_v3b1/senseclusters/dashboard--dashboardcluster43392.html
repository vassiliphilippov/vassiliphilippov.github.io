dashboard

noun
panel protecting from splashes in vehicles
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdæʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːʃ.bɔːd / (BrE)
a panel or screen on a vehicle positioned in front of the driver or occupants to protect against splashing water, mud, or debris, originally used in horse-drawn carriages

Examples

Coach dashboard
Early automobiles borrowed the dashboard design from horse-drawn coaches.
Farmers often crafted a simple dashboard for their carts to keep out splashes during rainy travel.
The old carriage featured a wooden dashboard to shield passengers from flying mud.
