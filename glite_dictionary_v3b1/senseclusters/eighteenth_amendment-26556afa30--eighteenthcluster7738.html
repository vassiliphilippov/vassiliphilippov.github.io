Eighteenth Amendment

noun
U.S. constitutional amendment prohibiting alcohol (1920–1933)
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪˈtinθ əˌmɛndmənt / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
the amendment to the United States Constitution, adopted in 1920, that prohibited the manufacture, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages, later repealed in 1933

Examples

The Eighteenth Amendment
Many Americans ignored the Eighteenth Amendment by purchasing illegal alcohol from bootleggers.
The Eighteenth Amendment ushered in an era known as Prohibition in the United States.
The Eighteenth Amendment was eventually repealed by the Twenty-First Amendment in 1933.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Prohibition - the period during which the Eighteenth Amendment was in effect, banning alcohol in the U.S.
  • antonym: Twenty-First Amendment - the amendment that repealed the Eighteenth Amendment
