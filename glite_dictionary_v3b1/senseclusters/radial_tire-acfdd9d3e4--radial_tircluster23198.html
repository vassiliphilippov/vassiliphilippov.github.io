radial tire

noun
tire with radial-ply casing
5.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.dɪ.əl ˈtaɪ.ər/ /; / /ˈreɪ.dɪ.əl taɪr/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.dɪ.əl ˈtaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A pneumatic tire in which the cords of the plies are arranged at 90 degrees to the direction of travel, providing greater flexibility and performance.

Examples

A radial tire design
Modern cars are typically equipped with radial tires for better fuel efficiency and handling.
Thanks to its radial tire design, the vehicle experienced a smoother ride on rough roads.
The mechanic recommended replacing the old bias-ply tires with radial tires.
