mujahidin

noun
Muslim guerrilla fighter
Pronunciation: / muːˈdʒɑːhɪdiːn / (AmE), / muːˈdʒɑːhɪdiːn / (BrE)
A member of a group of Muslim guerrilla fighters engaged in a jihad, especially in contexts of armed struggle.

Examples

The mujahidin fighters
Several books document the tactics and struggles of the Afghan mujahidin.
Some view the mujahidin as freedom fighters, while others regard them differently.
The mujahidin fought against foreign occupation for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jihad - a struggle or fight against the enemies of Islam
