elitist

noun
person favoring rule by an elite
Pronunciation: / əˈliːtɪst /; / ɪˈliːtɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈliːtɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that certain people or groups are superior and should have more power or privilege

Examples

An elitist leader
As an elitist, he firmly believes that only the most capable individuals should govern.
Many critics described the politician as an elitist who was out of touch with ordinary citizens.
She was labeled an elitist for supporting policies that benefit only a select group.
The club was criticized for only accepting elitists who valued status over kindness.
Many elitists believe that only a select few deserve to make important decisions.
He was called an elitist because he dismissed the opinions of ordinary people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarian - a person who believes in equality for all people
  • antonym: populist - person who supports the rights of ordinary people against the elite
  • synonym: snob - a person who looks down on others they consider less important
