afropavo

noun
genus of bright-colored Congo peafowl
Pronunciation: / ˌæfroʊˈpeɪvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌafrəˈpɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the pheasant family, known as the Congo peafowl, with both sexes displaying bright coloration, native to the Congo Basin.

Examples

The afropavo bird
Researchers observed a group of afropavos feeding in the dense rainforest.
The afropavo is the only peafowl species native to Africa.
Unlike the Indian peafowl, in afropavo both males and females are brightly colored.
