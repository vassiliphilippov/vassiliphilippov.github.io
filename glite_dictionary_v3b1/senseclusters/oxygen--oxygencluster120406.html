oxygen

noun
chemical element (O), essential atmospheric gas
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdʒən / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol O), a colorless, odorless gas that is essential for breathing and makes up about 21% of Earth's atmosphere

Examples

Oxygen gas
Oxygen is essential for most life forms on Earth.
Fish obtain oxygen from the water using their gills.
The patient was given supplemental oxygen after surgery.
Doctors gave the patient oxygen to help her breathe more easily.
Plants produce oxygen during photosynthesis, making it essential for most life on Earth.
The chemical reaction created bubbles of oxygen gas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: air - the invisible mixture of gases that surrounds the Earth, mainly nitrogen and oxygen
  • degree lower: hydrogen - the lightest chemical element, atomic number 1
  • degree higher: ozone - a triatomic form of oxygen (O₃)
