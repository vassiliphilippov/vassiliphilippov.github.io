hitch

verb
walk with a limp or impediment
Pronunciation: / hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
to walk with a limp or with difficulty, usually because of an injury or physical limitation

Examples

To hitch across the street
After twisting his ankle, he had to hitch across the parking lot.
She hitched along the corridor, leaning on her cane for support.
The injured player tried to hitch off the field after the tackle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hobble - to walk in an awkward way, usually because of pain or injury
  • synonym: limp - to walk with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot
