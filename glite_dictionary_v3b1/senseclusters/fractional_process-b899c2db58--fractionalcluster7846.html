fractional process

noun
separation by heating
6.0 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfrækʃənəl ˈproʊses / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl ˈprəʊses / (BrE)
a method of separating a substance into its components by applying heat, often using differences in boiling points

Examples

A fractional process of separation
Many industrial chemicals are separated by a fractional process that relies on their different boiling points.
Petroleum is refined through a fractional process to produce gasoline and other fuels.
The laboratory used a fractional process to isolate the various chemicals present in the mixture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fractional distillation - the process of separating components of a liquid mixture by differences in boiling point
