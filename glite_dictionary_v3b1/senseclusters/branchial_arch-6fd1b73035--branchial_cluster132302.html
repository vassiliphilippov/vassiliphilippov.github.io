branchial arch

noun
supporting arch for gills in fishes
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbrænki.əl ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrænki.əl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
one of a series of bony or cartilaginous arches located on each side of the pharynx in fishes and aquatic amphibians, supporting the gills

Examples

The branchial arch structure
In embryology, the branchial arch plays a significant role in head and neck formation.
Researchers examined the development of each branchial arch in amphibian larvae.
The branchial arches are vital for the respiratory system of fish.
