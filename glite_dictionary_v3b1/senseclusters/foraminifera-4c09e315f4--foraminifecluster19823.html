Foraminifera

noun
single-celled protist with a shell
Pronunciation: / fəˌræm.əˈnɪf.ə.rə / (AmE), / fəˌræm.ɪˈnɪf.ər.ə / (BrE)
Any of a large group of single-celled aquatic protists that typically have perforated shells, often found as fossils and important in the study of geology and paleontology.

Examples

Foraminifera fossils
Foraminifera are used by geologists to date and correlate rock layers from different locations.
Scientists studied the shells of Foraminifera to reconstruct ancient ocean temperatures.
The limestone was full of fossilized Foraminifera visible under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foraminifer - a single organism of the Foraminifera group
