elastically

adverb
with strong responsiveness
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæstɪkli / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a strong, proportional response to changes in another variable, especially in economics or statistics

Examples

Demand changes elastically
In response to the new tax, consumer demand dropped elastically across several industries.
The supply of the product adjusted elastically when market conditions changed.
Prices in the technology sector often react elastically to shifts in consumer interest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibly - in a way that can change or adapt easily
  • antonym: rigidly - in a way that does not change or adapt
