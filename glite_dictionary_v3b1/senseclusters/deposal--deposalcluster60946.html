deposal

noun
giving sworn evidence
8.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpoʊzəl / (AmE), / dɪˈpəʊzəl / (BrE)
the act of giving sworn evidence or a formal statement, especially one recorded for use in court

Examples

Court deposal
The lawyer requested a deposal from the witness before the trial began.
Her deposal was crucial in clarifying the events of that evening.
The court accepted his deposal as valid evidence in the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deposition - a formal statement of evidence given under oath
  • synonym: testimony - a formal written or spoken statement given in a court of law
