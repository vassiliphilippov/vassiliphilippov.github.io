LED

noun
light-emitting diode; a device that emits light when powered
1.4 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl ˌi ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌɛl ˌiː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a semiconductor device (light-emitting diode) that emits light when an electric current passes through it; the color depends on the material used

Examples

A red LED
A red LED on the charger indicates that the device is still powering up.
He replaced the old bulbs with energy-efficient LEDs in his kitchen.
The screen uses dozens of LED lights to produce vivid colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diode - an electronic device allowing current to flow in one direction
