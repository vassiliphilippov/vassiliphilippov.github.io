co-beneficiary

noun
person sharing a benefit with others
2.7 5.6
Pronunciation: /koʊ ben.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ər.iː/ /; / /koʊ ben.ɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ər.iː/ (AmE), /kəʊ ben.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ər.iː/ /; / /kəʊ ben.ɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ər.iː/ (BrE)
a person who, along with one or more others, is entitled to receive the same benefit, such as from a trust, insurance policy, or will

Examples

A co-beneficiary share
As a co-beneficiary, she was entitled to half of the estate.
Each co-beneficiary has equal rights to the life insurance payout.
The trust named Sarah as a co-beneficiary along with her brother, meaning they must share all distributions.
