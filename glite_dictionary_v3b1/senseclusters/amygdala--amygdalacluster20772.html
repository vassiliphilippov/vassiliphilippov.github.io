amygdala

noun
almond-shaped brain structure involved in emotion
Pronunciation: / əˈmɪɡdələ / (AmE), / əˈmɪɡdələ / (BrE)
A small, almond-shaped mass of gray matter in the brain's temporal lobe, involved in processing emotions and part of the limbic system.

Examples

The amygdala activity
Activity in the amygdala increases when we perceive potential threats in our environment.
Research suggests that a damaged amygdala can affect emotional reactions and memory formation.
The amygdala is known to play a crucial role in generating fear responses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hippocampus - another part of the limbic system, involved in memory
  • synonym: hypothalamus - brain structure involved in hormone regulation; part of limbic system
