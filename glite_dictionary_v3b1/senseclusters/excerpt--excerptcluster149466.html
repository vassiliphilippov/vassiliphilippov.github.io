excerpt

noun
a selected passage or extract
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkˌsɝpt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɜːpt / (BrE)
a short extract from a larger written work, piece of music, or film

Examples

An excerpt from a book
She read aloud an excerpt from a famous speech to inspire the audience.
The article began with an excerpt from an unpublished letter.
The essay includes a brief excerpt from the author's first novel.
