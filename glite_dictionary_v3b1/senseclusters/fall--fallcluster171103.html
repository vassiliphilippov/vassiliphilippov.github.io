fall

verb
be assigned or given (duty, etc.)
6.4
Pronunciation: / fɔl / (AmE), / fɔːl / (BrE)
to be assigned, transferred, or passed to someone (duties, rights, inheritance, etc.)

Examples

Duties fall to her
After his brother moved away, it fell to Mark to take care of their parents.
Much of the work falls on a small group of dedicated volunteers every year.
When the manager resigned, the responsibility to lead the project fell on Sarah.
After lengthy legal proceedings, the property fell to the rightful heirs.
The family estate fell to William after his uncle passed away.
The title of baron fell to the next male heir in the family.
After their uncle died, the old manor fell to Michael and his brother.
The title of duke fell to the elder son upon his father's death.
When the distant relative was found, the entire fortune fell to her.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accrue - to accumulate or be received by someone, often by right
  • synonym: devolve - be passed on to someone, especially as a responsibility
  • synonym: inherit - to receive property or a title on the death of the previous holder
  • synonym: rest - (of a responsibility or burden) be placed on someone
