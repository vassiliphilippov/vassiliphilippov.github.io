substituent

noun
replacing group in molecule
8.9
Pronunciation: / səbˈstɪtʃuənt / (AmE), / səbˈstɪtjʊənt / (BrE)
an atom or group of atoms that replaces another atom or group in a molecule

Examples

A substituent group
A chlorine atom can act as a substituent on a carbon chain in organic chemistry.
The properties of a molecule can change depending on which substituent is attached.
Scientists studied how each substituent affected the reactivity of the compound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: functional group - a group of atoms responsible for the characteristic reactions of a molecule
