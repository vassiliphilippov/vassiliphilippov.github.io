toile

noun
test garment
9.5
Pronunciation: /twɑːl/ /; / /twɔːl/ (AmE), /twɑːl/ /; / /twɔːl/ (BrE)
a test garment or mock-up made from inexpensive fabric, used to check fit and design before making the final piece

Examples

Dressmaker's toile
The designer created a toile to make sure the dress would fit perfectly before using the expensive silk.
Making a toile allows tailors to adjust the pattern without wasting costly materials.
She tried on the toile so the seamstress could pin and alter the shape as needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prototype - an early sample or model used to test a concept
