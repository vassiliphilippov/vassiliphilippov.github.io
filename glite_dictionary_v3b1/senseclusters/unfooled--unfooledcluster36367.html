unfooled

adjective
not deceived
7.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàfuÀêld / (AmE), /  ånÀàfuÀêld / (BrE)
not deceived or tricked; aware of the truth or reality of a situation

Examples

Remain unfooled by tricks
Despite the magician's clever tricks, the audience remained unfooled throughout the show.
He tried to lie about his whereabouts, but his parents were unfooled by his story.
The detective was unfooled by the suspect's attempt to create a false alibi.

Synonyms

  • undeluded
    8.5 0.02/m
    undeluded is not tricked and sees things clearly, while unfooled describes someone who is not deceived or taken in.
  • unillusioned
    7.2 0.01/m
    unillusioned sees facts without false belief, while unfooled is more common for saying someone is not deceived.
  • undeceived
    8.1 0.02/m
    undeceived has the sense of no longer being under a false idea, while unfooled simply means not tricked.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - quick to notice and understand things
  • antonym: fooled - deceived or tricked
