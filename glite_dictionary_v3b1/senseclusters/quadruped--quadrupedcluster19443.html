quadruped

noun
four‑legged robot
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɑː.drə.ped/ (AmE), /ˈkwɒ.drə.ped/ (BrE)
(in robotics) a four-legged robotic platform or robot designed to walk, run, or carry payloads using four legs.

Examples

Quadruped robot
The research team tested the new quadruped robot on uneven terrain during field trials.
An agile quadruped navigated the rocky path and carried sensors across the study site.
Engineers improved battery life so the quadruped could operate outdoors for longer shifts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biped - an entity that moves using two legs; often contrasted with four‑legged platforms in robotics.
  • synonym: robot - a machine capable of carrying out programmed tasks automatically; quadrupeds are a type of robot.
