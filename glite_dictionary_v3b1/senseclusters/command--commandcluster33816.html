Synonyms

  • order
    1.3 40/m
    order is giving an authoritative instruction to someone, while command also means having authority or control over people or things.
  • dictate
    5.0 8/m
    dictate means to give orders in a strict, one-sided way, while command can mean both to order and to lead.
  • enjoin
    8.6 0.6/m
    enjoin is a formal legal order telling someone to do or stop something, while command is broader authority to give orders.
  • instruct
    2.8 9/m
    instruct is to give directions on how to do a task, while command focuses more on ordering and using authority.
  • rule over
    4.2 1/m
    rule over means to govern or hold power over a place or people, while command also includes giving orders directly.
  • control
    2.4 80/m
    control is having power to manage or restrain people or things, while command emphasizes giving orders and formal authority.
