command

verb
to exercise control or authority
1.3 2.3
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmænd/ (AmE), /kə.ˈmɑːnd/ /; / /kə.ˈmænd/ (BrE)
to have or exercise authority or control over people or things; to give orders

Examples

To command a group
He commanded respect from everyone in the organization.
She was chosen to command the project and lead the team to success.
The general commands the entire army with strict discipline.
He was promoted after commanding the battalion successfully.
She commands the respect of everyone in her unit.
The general commanded a huge army during the war.
She commands everyone in the office with confidence and authority.
The general commanded the troops to advance toward the city at dawn.
The teacher commanded the students to stop talking and pay attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: control - to have power over something or someone
  • antonym: forbid - to order someone not to do something; prohibit
  • synonym: lead - to be in charge of a group or organization
  • antonym: obey - to follow commands or orders
  • synonym: order - to tell someone to do something; to instruct
