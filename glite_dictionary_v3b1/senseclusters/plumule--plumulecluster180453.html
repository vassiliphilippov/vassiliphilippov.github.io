plumule

noun
embryonic shoot
Pronunciation: / ˈpluːmjuːl / (AmE), / ˈpluːmjuːl / (BrE)
the part of a plant embryo that develops into the shoot and first true leaves

Examples

Seed plumule growth
The plumule is the first part of the seedling to appear above the soil.
If the plumule is damaged, the plant may not develop proper leaves.
Botanists study the plumule to understand early plant growth.
During germination, the plumule pushes upward to form the young plant's first leaves.
The scientist carefully observed the plumule as it emerged from the seed.
