overconcentration

noun
excessive focus
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkənˌsɛnˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkɒnˌsɛnˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
excessive focus on a single task, idea, or detail that causes neglect of other important aspects or reduces effectiveness

Examples

Overconcentration on details
His overconcentration on the financial report made him miss important updates from other departments.
Teachers warn that overconcentration on test scores can harm students' overall learning.
The team's overconcentration on one strategy left them unprepared for unexpected challenges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distraction - a lack of focus or attention
  • synonym: obsession - an idea or thought that completely fills someone's mind
