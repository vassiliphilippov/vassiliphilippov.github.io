cynicism

noun
general distrust or disbelief in sincerity
4.4 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
A general distrust of the motives or sincerity of others; the belief that people are motivated chiefly by self-interest.

Examples

Political cynicism
Her cynicism about politics made her distrust every campaign promise.
His cynicism prevented him from believing anyone acted out of pure kindness.
There is growing cynicism among young people regarding the motives of corporations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: optimism - belief that good will prevail or things will turn out well
  • synonym: skepticism - a general attitude of doubt or disbelief
