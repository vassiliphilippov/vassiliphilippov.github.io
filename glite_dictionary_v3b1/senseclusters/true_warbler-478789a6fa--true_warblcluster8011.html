true warbler

noun
small Old World songbird (family Sylviidae)
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: / tru ˈwɔrblɚ / (AmE), / tru ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
any of various small, active, typically brownish or greyish songbirds of the Old World belonging to the family Sylviidae, distinct from New World warblers.

Examples

A true warbler species
Many true warbler species have a melodious song that attracts birdwatchers from around the world.
The naturalist spent months observing the elusive true warbler in the forests of Eastern Europe.
Unlike the brightly colored American warblers, the true warbler is typically a subdued brown or grey.
