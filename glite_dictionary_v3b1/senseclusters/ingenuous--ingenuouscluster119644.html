ingenuous

adjective
innocent and open; straightforward
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs /; / ɪnˈdʒɛnjuːəs / (BrE)
innocent, open, and honest; unable to hide feelings or intentions

Examples

An ingenuous answer
He gave an ingenuous response, not even trying to hide his disappointment.
Her ingenuous smile made it clear she was unaware of the secret.
The child's ingenuous curiosity led to many innocent questions.
Despite her age, she maintained an ingenuous curiosity about the world around her.
He gave an ingenuous account of what happened, clearly unaware of the possible consequences.
Her ingenuous smile made it hard not to trust her intentions immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artless - free from deceit, cunning, or craftiness; naturally simple or innocent
  • antonym: disingenuous - not candid or sincere; pretending that one knows less than one really does
  • synonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment; innocent
  • antonym: worldly - experienced and sophisticated; not naive
