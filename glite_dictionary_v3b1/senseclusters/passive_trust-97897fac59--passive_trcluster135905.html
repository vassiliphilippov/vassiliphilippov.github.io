passive trust

noun
trust where trustee is inactive
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪv trʌst / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv trʌst / (BrE)
A legal arrangement in which the trustee has no active duties to perform and merely holds the property for the beneficiary.

Examples

A passive trust
In a passive trust, the trustee simply holds the assets for the benefit of another person.
Modern law sometimes prohibits the creation of a passive trust without any duties imposed on the trustee.
The court determined that the arrangement constituted a passive trust rather than an active trust.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active trust - a trust in which the trustee has active duties to perform
