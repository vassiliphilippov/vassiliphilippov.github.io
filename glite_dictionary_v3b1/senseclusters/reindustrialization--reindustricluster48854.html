reindustrialization

noun
renewed industrial growth
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˌdʌstriələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌriːɪnˌdʌstriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˌdʌstriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of restoring or strengthening a country's or region's industrial sector, often after decline, usually through government policies or modern technologies

Examples

The reindustrialization process
The government launched a reindustrialization initiative to create more manufacturing jobs in the region.
Many economists debate the long-term effects of reindustrialization on the national economy.
After years of decline, reindustrialization brought new factories and technology to the city.
Government policies encouraged reindustrialization to boost the local economy and create jobs.
The city underwent reindustrialization after decades of factory closures and unemployment.
Experts debated the long-term effects of reindustrialization on the environment and society.
Government policies encouraged reindustrialization to boost employment and economic growth in the region.
The city underwent reindustrialization after decades of factory closures and job losses.
Modern reindustrialization often focuses on sustainable practices and high-tech manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deindustrialization - the decline of industrial activity in a region or economy
  • synonym: industrialization - the development of industries in a country or region
