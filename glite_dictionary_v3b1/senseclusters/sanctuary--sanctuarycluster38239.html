sanctuary

noun
sacred place in a religious setting
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋktʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.tju.ə.ri / (BrE)
a sacred or holy place, especially in a religious building, or the area around the altar in a church

Examples

Church sanctuary
Only a few chosen individuals were allowed to enter the holy sanctuary.
The Torah scrolls are stored in the sanctuary of the synagogue.
The ancient temple's sanctuary was reserved for priests and sacred rituals.
A railing separated the sanctuary from the rest of the church.
Only clergy and certain officials are allowed inside the sanctuary during the ceremony.
The choir processed into the sanctuary for the start of the service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altar - a table or flat-topped block used as the focus for a religious ritual
  • synonym: chancel - the part of a church containing the altar and seats for the clergy and choir
