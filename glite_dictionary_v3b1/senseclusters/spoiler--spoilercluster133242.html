spoiler

noun
device reducing car lift at speed
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔɪlər /; / ˈspɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
a device on a car or aircraft that changes airflow to reduce lift or improve control and stability

Examples

Car spoiler
A damaged spoiler can negatively affect a vehicle's aerodynamics at high speed.
He installed a new spoiler on his sports car to improve its performance.
The race car's spoiler helps it stay firmly on the track during sharp turns.
Modern jetliners are equipped with multiple spoilers for improved control.
The pilot deployed the spoiler to help slow the plane during landing.
When the spoiler was raised, the aircraft's descent rate increased noticeably.
