John Wycliffe

proper noun
English theologian and church reformer
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪklɪf / (AmE), / ˈwɪklɪf / (BrE)
(c. 1328–1384) An English theologian and reformer known for criticizing Roman Catholic doctrine and helping to inspire the Protestant Reformation.

Examples

John Wycliffe reformer
John Wycliffe is often regarded as a forerunner of the Protestant Reformation.
Many scholars have studied the works of John Wycliffe to understand early reformist ideas.
The translation of the Bible into English was supervised by John Wycliffe and his followers.
