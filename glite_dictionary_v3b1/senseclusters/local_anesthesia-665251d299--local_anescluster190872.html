local anesthesia

noun
loss of sensation in a local area
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəl ˌænəsˈθiʒə /; / ˈloʊ.kəl ˌæn.əsˈθi.ʒə / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl ˌanəsˈθiːziə /; / ˈləʊ.kəl ˌæn.əsˈθiː.zi.ə / (BrE)
a loss of sensation in a specific, limited area of the body, usually caused by the application or injection of a local anesthetic agent

Examples

Local anesthesia injection
Minor surgical procedures are often performed under local anesthesia to minimize recovery time.
Patients receiving local anesthesia remain awake and aware but do not feel pain in the treated region.
The dentist used local anesthesia to numb the area before removing the wisdom tooth.
Local anaesthesia is preferred for small surgical procedures to reduce recovery time.
After receiving local anaesthesia, the patient remained awake during the minor surgery.
The dentist used local anaesthesia to numb the area before removing the tooth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general anaesthesia - loss of sensation and consciousness throughout the entire body
  • antonym: general anesthesia - loss of sensation over the entire body, typically involving unconsciousness
  • synonym: local anesthesia - US spelling of 'local anaesthesia'
