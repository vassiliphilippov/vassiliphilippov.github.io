sublevel

noun
atomic orbital type
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.lev.əl/ /; / /ˈsʌb.lə.vəl/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.lev.əl/ /; / /ˈsʌb.lə.vəl/ (BrE)
a subdivision of an electron shell in an atom, defined by a specific type of atomic orbital such as s, p, d, or f

Examples

Electron sublevel
Each electron shell is divided into one or more sublevels based on the type of orbital.
The 2p sublevel can hold up to six electrons in an atom.
Chemistry students learn how electrons fill each sublevel according to specific rules.
