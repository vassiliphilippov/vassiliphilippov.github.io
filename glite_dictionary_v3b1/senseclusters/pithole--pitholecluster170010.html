pithole

noun
hole or cavity
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɪthəʊl / (BrE)
a hole or cavity, especially one formed by decay, excavation, or natural processes

Examples

A pithole in tree
The old tree had a large pithole in its trunk where insects had burrowed.
After years of erosion, a pithole appeared in the middle of the field.
The archaeologists carefully examined the pithole for any signs of ancient artifacts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pit - a large hole in the ground
