scholastics

noun
medieval philosophers
9.9
Pronunciation: /skə.ˈlæs.tɪks/ (AmE), /skə.ˈlæs.tɪks/ (BrE)
medieval philosophers and theologians who used formal logic to explain religious and philosophical doctrines, especially within scholasticism

Examples

Medieval scholastics
The scholastics played a key role in shaping medieval European thought.
Many scholastics wrote extensive commentaries on Aristotle's works.
Universities in the Middle Ages were often led by prominent scholastics.

  • synonym: scholastic - a person who practices or supports scholasticism
  • synonym: theologian - a person who studies religious beliefs and theology
