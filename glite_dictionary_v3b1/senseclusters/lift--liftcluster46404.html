lift

noun
upward aerodynamic force
6.1 3.25/m
Pronunciation: / lÉªft / (AmE), / lÉªft / (BrE)
The upward force produced by the movement of air over an airfoil, such as a wing, which acts to oppose gravity.

Examples

Wing lift
Engineers carefully calculate the amount of lift needed for safe takeoff.
Increasing the angle of attack generally increases lift up to a certain point.
The lift generated by the airplane's wings allows it to stay airborne.

Synonyms

  • aerodynamic lift
    5.8 0.05/m
