metonymic

adjective
relating to metonymy; using association for naming
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈnɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈnɪmɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) using or relating to metonymy; substituting the name of one thing with that of something closely associated with it.

Examples

Metonymic expression
'The pen is mightier than the sword' contains a metonymic usage, where 'pen' stands for writing and 'sword' for military power.
The phrase 'the White House issued a statement' is a metonymic way of referring to the U.S. president or their administration.
Using 'Hollywood' to represent the American film industry is a metonymic expression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metonymy - the substitution of the name of an attribute for that of the thing meant
