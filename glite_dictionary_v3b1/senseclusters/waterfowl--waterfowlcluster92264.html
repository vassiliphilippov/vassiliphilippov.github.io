waterfowl

noun
aquatic bird found near fresh water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚˌfaʊl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌfaʊl / (BrE)
any bird that lives on or near fresh water, especially ducks, geese, or swans, typically adapted for swimming.

Examples

Wild waterfowl
Hunters must obtain special permits to hunt certain types of waterfowl in this region.
Several species of waterfowl can be observed on the lake during the summer months.
The wetlands provide a crucial habitat for migrating waterfowl each spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duck - a common type of waterfowl with a flat bill and webbed feet
  • synonym: goose - a large waterfowl with a long neck and webbed feet
  • synonym: swan - a large, graceful waterfowl with a long neck
