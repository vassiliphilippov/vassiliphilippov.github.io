instate

verb
to appoint officially
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to formally put someone into an official position or office

Examples

To instate a leader
The committee will instate the new director at the ceremony next month.
After the election, the council quickly instated the chosen candidate as mayor.
It took several weeks to instate the new policies after the leadership change.
The university will instate a new president at the ceremony next month.
After the election, the committee quickly instated the new board members.
It took several weeks to instate the new policies across all departments.
The board decided to instate her as the new manager after a long search.
He was instated as president during a formal ceremony at the university.
The committee will instate the new members at next week's meeting.
