courter

noun
one seeking favor
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtə / (BrE)
a person who seeks the favor, support, or approval of someone, often for personal gain or advantage

Examples

Courter of power
During the election, the mayor became a skilled courter of influential business leaders.
As a courter of public opinion, she attended every community event in the city.
The artist was a frequent courter of wealthy patrons to fund his new projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admirer - someone who shows respect or approval for another
  • antonym: opponent - a person who is against or competes with another
  • synonym: suitor - a person who seeks someone's favor, especially in marriage or support
