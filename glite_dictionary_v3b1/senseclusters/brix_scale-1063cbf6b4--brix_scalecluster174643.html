Brix scale

noun
system for measuring sugar concentration
7.2
Pronunciation: / brɪks skeɪl / (AmE), / brɪks skeɪl / (BrE)
A system used for measuring the concentration of sugar in a liquid solution, expressed in degrees Brix.

Examples

The Brix scale reading
A reading of 20 on the Brix scale indicates a solution contains 20% sugar by weight.
Juice manufacturers use the Brix scale to measure the sugar content of their products.
The Brix scale is commonly used in the wine industry to determine grape ripeness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Baumé scale - A similar hydrometer scale used for liquids based on specific gravity
