extinguishment

noun
ending or cancellation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of putting an end to something, such as a fire, debt, right, or obligation

Examples

The extinguishment of fire
The extinguishment of the fire was achieved after several hours of hard work by the firefighters.
The court ordered the extinguishment of the company's outstanding debts.
After the treaty, there was a formal extinguishment of the old legal rights.
The extinguishment of the fire was completed by the firefighters within an hour.
After the extinguishment of the debt, the company was finally free of its financial obligations.
The treaty called for the extinguishment of all hostilities between the two nations.

  • synonym: termination - the act of bringing something to an end
