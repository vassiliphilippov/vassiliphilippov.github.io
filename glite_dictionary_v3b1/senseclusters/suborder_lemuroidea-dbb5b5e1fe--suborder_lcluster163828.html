suborder Lemuroidea

noun
taxonomic group of lemur-like primates
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.ɔːr.dər liː.mər.ɔɪ.ˈdiː.ə/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.ɔː.də liː.mə.rɔɪ.ˈdiː.ə/ (BrE)
A former taxonomic grouping of primates that includes lemurs, lorises, aye-ayes, and indriids, used in some classifications instead of or as a subdivision of Prosimii.

Examples

The suborder Lemuroidea group
In older zoological texts, lemurs and lorises were often classified under the suborder Lemuroidea.
Some taxonomists treat the suborder Lemuroidea as synonymous with Prosimii, while others distinguish between the two.
The aye-aye, an unusual primate from Madagascar, was traditionally placed in the suborder Lemuroidea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Prosimii - a primate suborder including lemurs, lorises, and tarsiers
  • synonym: Strepsirrhini - the primate suborder including lemurs and lorises under current taxonomy
