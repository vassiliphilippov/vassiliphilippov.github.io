appendicular skeleton

noun
limbs and their girdles in the skeleton
Pronunciation: / əˌpɛnˈdɪkjələr ˈskɛlɪtən / (AmE), / əˌpɛndɪˈkjʊlə ˈskɛlɪtən / (BrE)
the portion of the skeleton that consists of the limbs and the girdles (pectoral and pelvic) that attach them to the axial skeleton

Examples

The appendicular skeleton limbs
An injury to the appendicular skeleton can affect mobility and limb function.
In anatomy class, we studied the appendicular skeleton separately from the axial skeleton.
The appendicular skeleton includes all the bones of the arms and legs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: axial skeleton - the part of the skeleton consisting of the skull, vertebral column, and rib cage
