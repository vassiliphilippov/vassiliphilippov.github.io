hypnotic trance

noun
state of consciousness under hypnosis
3.7 6.8
Pronunciation: /hɪp.ˈnɑː.tɪk træns/ (AmE), /hɪp.ˈnɒ.tɪk trɑːns/ (BrE)
a state of altered consciousness induced by hypnosis, in which the person is highly suggestible and responsive to the hypnotist's instructions

Examples

Enter a hypnotic trance
During the stage show, several volunteers were put into a hypnotic trance and made to perform amusing acts.
Some people find it easier than others to reach a hypnotic trance during therapy sessions.
The patient entered a hypnotic trance and responded readily to the therapist's suggestions.
