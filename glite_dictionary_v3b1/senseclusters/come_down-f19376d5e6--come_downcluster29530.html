come down

verb
move downward; become lower
2.8 7.58/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm daÊŠn / (AmE), / kÊŒm daÊŠn / (BrE)
to move downward, become lower, or decrease in value, amount, or mood

Examples

Prices come down
As the sun set, the temperature began to come down quickly.
The prices of houses finally came down after years of steady increase.
You could hear the excitement in the stadium come down after the game ended.
If property values come down any further, it could be a good time to buy.
She was excited at first, but her mood really came down after the argument.
The cost of gasoline has finally come down after months of high prices.

Synonyms

  • go down
    1.9 13/m
    go down means to decrease in amount or level in many situations, while come down can mean moving or falling lower.
  • drop
    1.0 61/m
    drop describes a quick fall in value or level, while come down also covers moving physically or becoming lower more generally.
  • fall
    1.0 164/m
    fall is a sudden downward movement or loss, while come down can mean either physical descent or a general decrease.
  • sink
    1.0 23/m
    sink means to go down slowly or emotionally lose strength, while come down covers many ways of moving or becoming lower.
  • descend
    2.7 15/m
    descend means to go down from a higher place in a formal sense, while come down is more common and less formal.
  • fall off
    2.6 2.40/m
    fall off means to decrease in amount or quality, often steadily, while come down can mean simple movement or any decrease.
  • drop away
    3.3 0.06/m
    drop away means to fall or lessen gradually over time, while come down covers both quick and slow moves to a lower state.
  • die down
    2.7 2.37/m
    die down means to become less strong or loud over time, while come down includes falling, lowering, or losing value.
  • turn down
    3.6 0.38/m
    turn down means a process or trend begins to decrease after rising, while come down simply means to move or become lower.
  • decline
    3.4 0.23/m
    decline refers to a surface sloping or to a gradual fall in level, while come down is broader and used in many contexts.
