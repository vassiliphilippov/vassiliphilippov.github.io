Atlantic croaker

noun
a fish found in the Atlantic, known for croaking sound
Pronunciation: / ətˈlæntɪk ˈkroʊkər / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk ˈkrəʊkə / (BrE)
a silvery fish of North American Atlantic waters, noted for the croaking sound it makes; it has dark markings and small barbels on the chin

Examples

The Atlantic croaker fish
Fishermen often catch Atlantic croaker along the eastern coast of the United States.
Grilled Atlantic croaker is a popular dish in some coastal communities.
The distinctive sound made by the Atlantic croaker earned it its name.
