redhorse sucker

noun
a freshwater fish with reddish fins
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd.hɔrs ˈsʌk.ər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.hɔːs ˈsʌk.ə / (BrE)
A North American freshwater fish of the sucker family, noted for its reddish fins.

Examples

The redhorse sucker fish
Anglers often identify the redhorse sucker by its distinctive reddish fins.
During spawning season, schools of redhorse suckers can be seen in shallow water.
The redhorse sucker is commonly found in rivers and streams throughout the Midwest.
