juggle

verb
throw and keep objects in the air simultaneously
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡl /; / ˈdʒʌɡl̩ / (BrE)
to throw and keep several objects in the air by catching and throwing them repeatedly, usually as a performance or skill

Examples

To juggle balls
He amazed the crowd by juggling flaming torches with ease.
It takes hours of practice to juggle five clubs without dropping any.
She learned to juggle three balls at the same time during the circus camp.
He juggled the tennis balls for several minutes without dropping any.
She tried to juggle three apples at once during the performance.
The footballer managed to juggle the ball down the field skillfully.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drop - to let something fall
  • synonym: toss - to throw something lightly or casually
