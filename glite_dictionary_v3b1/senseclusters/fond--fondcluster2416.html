fond

adjective
excessively loving or indulgent
3.0 0.87/m
Pronunciation: / fÉ‘nd / (AmE), / fÉ’nd / (BrE)
Excessively or foolishly loving and indulgent, often resulting in spoiling someone.

Examples

Fond parent
Her fond mother could never say no, and as a result, she was terribly spoiled.
Some teachers are so fond of their favorite students that they overlook their faults.
The old man was fond of his grandchildren, showering them with gifts at every visit.

Synonyms

  • overfond
    8.1 0.01/m
    overfond is excessively attached and often foolishly loving, while fond is overly affectionate and likely to spoil someone.
  • doting
    6.8 0.00/m
    doting is extremely attentive and shows silly, loving care, while fond means being overly loving and apt to spoil someone.
  • overindulgent
    7.0 0.06/m
    overindulgent is allowing too much and giving unlimited treats, while fond focuses on loving feelings that lead to spoiling.
  • indulgent
    6.1 3.02/m
    indulgent is lenient and allows desires often for comfort, while fond implies excessive love that causes spoiling.
