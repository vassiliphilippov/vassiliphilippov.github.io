Mycenaean civilization

noun
ancient Greek Bronze Age culture (c. 1400–1100 BC)
8.8
Pronunciation: /maɪ.sən.ˈiː.ən sɪ.və.lɪz.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /maɪ.sɪ.ˈniː.ən sɪ.və.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /maɪ.sɪ.ˈniː.ən sɪ.vɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /maɪ.sən.ˈiː.ən sɪ.vɪ.lɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the ancient Greek civilization of the Late Bronze Age centered on Mycenae, flourishing from about 1400 to 1100 BC, known for its palatial states, art, and writing system.

Examples

Mycenaean civilization in Greece
Linear B script was used by the Mycenaean civilization to keep administrative records.
Many myths and legends of Greece are rooted in the Mycenaean civilization.
Scholars have studied the architecture of the Mycenaean civilization to understand its social structure.
Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable artifacts from the Mycenaean civilisation in mainland Greece.
Scholars believe the collapse of the Mycenaean civilisation led to the Greek Dark Ages.
The Mycenaean civilisation is often credited with influencing later Greek culture, including myth and architecture.
