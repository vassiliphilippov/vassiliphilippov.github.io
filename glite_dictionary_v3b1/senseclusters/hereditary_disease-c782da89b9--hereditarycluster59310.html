hereditary disease

noun
disease inherited genetically
Pronunciation: / həˈrɛdəˌtɛri dɪˈziz / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛdɪtəri dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease or disorder that is passed from parents to their children through genes

Examples

A hereditary disease gene
Because cystic fibrosis is a hereditary disease, genetic counseling is often recommended for affected families.
Doctors explained that the symptoms pointed to a rare hereditary disease running in the family.
Sickle cell anemia is a well-known hereditary disease that affects millions worldwide.
