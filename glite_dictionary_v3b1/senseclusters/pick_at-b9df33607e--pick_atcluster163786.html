pick at

verb
eat without appetite; nibble at food
Pronunciation: / pɪk æt / (AmE), / pɪk æt / (BrE)
to eat only small amounts of food, often without interest or appetite

Examples

To pick at food
He didn't seem hungry and just picked at his lunch for an hour.
She picks at her dinner whenever she's feeling upset.
The child was picking at his vegetables instead of eating them properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nibble - to take small bites of something
