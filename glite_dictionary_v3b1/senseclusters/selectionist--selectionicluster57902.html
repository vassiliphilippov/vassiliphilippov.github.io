selectionist

noun
believer in natural selection
8.3
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that natural selection is the main driving force in evolution

Examples

A selectionist scientist
The selectionist emphasized the importance of adaptation in shaping species over time.
Many biologists identify as selectionists when discussing the mechanisms of evolution.
A heated debate arose between the selectionist and those who favored other evolutionary theories.
The selectionist presented new evidence supporting the role of natural selection in evolution.
Many modern biologists identify as selectionists when discussing evolutionary theory.
A heated debate arose between the selectionist and the proponent of alternative evolutionary mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Darwinist - a person who supports Charles Darwin's theory of evolution by natural selection
  • antonym: Lamarckian - a person who believes in evolution by inheritance of acquired characteristics
