dropsy

noun
old term for swelling from excess fluid (edema)
9.9 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdrÉ‘psi / (AmE), / ËˆdrÉ’psi / (BrE)
a condition characterized by swelling of body tissues due to an abnormal accumulation of watery fluid; now known as edema

Examples

Dropsy swelling
A build-up of fluid in the abdomen can signal dropsy in its advanced stages.
In Victorian literature, dropsy is frequently mentioned as a cause of suffering.
The patient's legs were swollen due to dropsy after weeks of illness.

Synonyms

  • edema
    8.0 1.23/m
    edema is the common modern medical name for swelling from excess fluid, while dropsy is an older word once used for it.
  • hydrops
    9.9 0.00/m
    hydrops is a technical medical term for abnormal fluid swelling in a body part, while dropsy is the older, nontechnical name.

Related Terms

  • synonym: edema - swelling caused by excess fluid trapped in body tissues (modern term)
