arytaenoid

noun
cartilage at the back of the larynx
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈrɪt.ə.nɔɪd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈrɪt.ə.nɔɪd/ (BrE)
either of two small cartilages at the back of the larynx to which the vocal folds are attached

Examples

The arytaenoid cartilage
Damage to the arytaenoid cartilage can affect the voice and breathing.
Movement of the arytaenoid controls the tension and position of the vocal folds.
The surgeon carefully examined both arytaenoid cartilages during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cricoid - the ring-shaped cartilage of the larynx
  • synonym: thyroid cartilage - the largest cartilage of the larynx, also known as the Adam's apple
