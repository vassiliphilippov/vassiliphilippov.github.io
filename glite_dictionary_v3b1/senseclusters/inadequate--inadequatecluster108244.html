inadequate

adjective
not sufficient; not good enough
2.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈæ.də.kwət/ /; / /ɪn.ˈæ.də.kweɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈæ.də.kwɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈæ.dɪ.kwət/ /; / /ɪn.ˈæ.də.kwɪt/ (BrE)
not enough or not good enough to meet a need, purpose, or standard; insufficient

Examples

An inadequate explanation
His explanation for being late was clearly inadequate and unconvincing.
Many students feel that support for mental health is still inadequate at the university.
The report found the safety measures were inadequate to protect workers.
His explanation was inadequate and failed to address the main concerns.
Many students felt they had received inadequate preparation for the final exam.
The shelter provided inadequate food and water for all the evacuees.
