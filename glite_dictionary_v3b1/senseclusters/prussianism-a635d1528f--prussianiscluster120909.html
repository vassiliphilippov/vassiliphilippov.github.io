Prussianism

noun
strict discipline
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprʌʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprʌʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
strict military discipline and authoritarian practices, especially those associated with Prussia or its traditions

Examples

Strict Prussianism
The company's management style was criticized for its Prussianism and lack of flexibility.
Some historians argue that Prussianism influenced the country's approach to education and government.
The strict uniforms and routines at the academy reflected a sense of Prussianism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarianism - enforcing strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • antonym: liberalism - belief in individual freedoms and less government control
  • synonym: militarism - belief in maintaining a strong military and being prepared to use it aggressively
