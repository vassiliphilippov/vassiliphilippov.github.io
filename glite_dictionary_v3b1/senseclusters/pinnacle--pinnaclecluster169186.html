pinnacle

verb
to raise to a high or exalted position
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪnəkəl / (BrE)
to put someone or something at the highest point or to reach the highest point or climax

Examples

To pinnacle a career
He feared being pinnacled as a genius, preferring to work quietly behind the scenes.
The artist was pinnacled by critics who hailed her as the greatest of her generation.
Their victory in the championship pinnacled the team's remarkable season.
Her career pinnacled when she won the international award for best actress.
The festival pinnacled with a spectacular fireworks display on the final night.
After years of hard work, their efforts finally pinnacled in a successful product launch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culminate - to reach the highest or most decisive point
  • antonym: debase - to lower in value or dignity; degrade
  • antonym: decline - to decrease or move downward after a high point
  • synonym: exalt - to raise to a higher rank or position
  • synonym: peak - to reach the highest point or maximum
