ruin

noun
a state of complete destruction
3.2 6.60/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀê…™n /; / ÀàruÀê.…™n / (AmE), / ÀàruÀê…™n /; / ÀàruÀê.…™n / (BrE)
a state or process of complete destruction, damage, or loss (including reputation, finances, or general condition)

Examples

The ruin of a business
Decades of neglect led to the slow ruin of the historic theater.
His gambling habits brought financial ruin to the entire family.
The once beautiful city was left in ruin after the war.
His reputation suffered complete ruin after the scandal was revealed.
The ruin of the ancient city was caused by a powerful earthquake.
The company's financial ruin was triggered by a single disastrous investment.
After the financial collapse, her reputation was in ruin.
Gambling addiction brought about the ruin of many promising careers.
The scandal led to his complete ruin within the company.

Synonyms

  • ruination
    7.6 0.33/m
    ruination describes the process of being completely destroyed or losing value, while ruin names that broken state or outcome.
  • destruction
    3.0 45/m
    destruction is violent or total damage that makes things unusable, while ruin can mean loss of money, reputation, or condition.
  • devastation
    4.8 5.06/m
    devastation is extreme, wide damage from disaster that leaves places empty, while ruin covers physical or reputational loss.
  • rack and ruin
    7.4 0.03/m
    rack and ruin describes old buildings falling apart from neglect, while ruin can mean complete destruction or loss in many areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catastrophe - a sudden event causing great damage or suffering
  • synonym: collapse - a sudden and complete failure or breakdown
  • synonym: decay - gradual destruction or decomposition
  • synonym: demolition - the act of destroying something, especially a building
  • synonym: destruction - the act of destroying or demolishing something
  • synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or suffering
  • synonym: disgrace - loss of respect or honor
  • antonym: restoration - the action of returning something to a former or original state
  • antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose, opposite of ruin
