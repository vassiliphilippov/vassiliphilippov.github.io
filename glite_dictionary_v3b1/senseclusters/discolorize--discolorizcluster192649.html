discolorize

verb
make lighter in color; remove color
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkʌlərˌaɪz /; / dɪsˈkʌlɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌləraɪz /; / dɪsˈkʌl.ə.raɪz /; / dɪsˈkʌl(ə)raɪz / (BrE)
to cause something to become white or lighter in color; to remove or fade the color from.

Examples

To discolorize fabric
Strong cleaning chemicals can easily discolorize delicate fabrics.
The artist chose to discolorize the photograph, giving it a vintage appearance.
Years of washing have discolorized the pattern on this old quilt.
Exposing the painting to harsh light may discolorise its vibrant colors over time.
Sunlight can discolorise fabrics left in the window for too long.
The chemical treatment will discolorise the stains and restore the shirt's original shade.
After being left outdoors, the paint began to discolourise from exposure to sunlight.
Chlorine can discolourise your clothes if used excessively in washing.
The sun gradually discolourised the curtains over the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bleach - make or become white or lighter by chemical action or sunlight
  • antonym: darken - to make something darker in color
  • synonym: fade - to gradually lose color or brightness
