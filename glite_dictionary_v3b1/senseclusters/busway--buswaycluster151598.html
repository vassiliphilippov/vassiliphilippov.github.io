busway

noun
enclosed power conductors
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌsˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌsˌweɪ / (BrE)
an enclosed system of electrical conductors used to distribute large amounts of electric power within a building or industrial facility

Examples

Factory busway system
The engineers installed a new busway to safely distribute electricity throughout the factory.
Upgrading the busway allowed the building to handle higher electrical loads.
A damaged busway can cause serious power failures in industrial plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: busbar - a metallic strip or bar used for conducting electricity within a switchboard or distribution board
  • synonym: cable tray - a system used to support insulated electrical cables for power distribution
