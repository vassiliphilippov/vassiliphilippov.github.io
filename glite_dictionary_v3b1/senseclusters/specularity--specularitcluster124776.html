specularity

noun
mirror-like reflectiveness
9.4
Pronunciation: /spek.jə.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /spek.jʊ.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /spek.jə.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /spek.jʊ.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a surface that reflects light in a shiny or mirror-like way (including in computer graphics)

Examples

Specularity of a surface
The specularity of the polished marble floor made the entire room appear brighter.
Artists often adjust the specularity of objects in digital paintings to create realistic lighting effects.
The low specularity of the wall paint helped reduce glare in the office.
Increasing the specularity of the material made the car model look more realistic in the rendering.
Artists often adjust specularity to control how shiny or matte a digital object appears.
The highlight on the glass was created by setting a high specularity value in the shader.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diffuseness - the quality of scattering light in many directions, lacking sharp reflections
  • synonym: glossiness - the quality of being shiny and smooth
  • antonym: matte - a surface that is dull and not shiny
