mishpocha

noun
extended family network, often of Jewish origin
7.8
Pronunciation: / mɪʃˈpoʊxə / (AmE), / mɪʃˈpɒxə / (BrE)
(informal, often humorous) The entire network of family relatives by blood or marriage, and sometimes close friends regarded as family.

Examples

The mishpocha gathers
During the holidays, the mishpocha gathers for a big family meal.
Even distant cousins are considered part of the mishpocha in their tradition.
She invited the whole mishpocha to the wedding, making it a lively celebration.
