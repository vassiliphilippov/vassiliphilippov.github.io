bankfull

adjective
full to river banks
Pronunciation: / ˈbæŋkfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkfʊl / (BrE)
describing a river or stream that is filled to the top of its banks, just before overflowing

Examples

Bankfull river
After days of rain, the river was bankfull and close to flooding the fields.
The stream reached bankfull conditions during the spring thaw.
When the water is bankfull, authorities monitor the area for possible floods.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: flooded - covered or filled with water beyond normal levels
  • degree lower: low - having a small amount of water, well below the banks
