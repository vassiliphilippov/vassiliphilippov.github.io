apple-polish

verb
to flatter for personal gain
Pronunciation: / ˈæpəlˌpɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæpəlˌpɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
to flatter someone in an insincere way, typically to gain favor or advantage

Examples

To apple-polish a teacher
He tried to apple-polish his way into a promotion by constantly complimenting his boss.
It's obvious when someone is apple-polishing rather than giving genuine praise.
She apple-polished the teacher so often that her classmates noticed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brown-nose - to try to win favor with someone in authority by flattering them
  • synonym: flatter - to praise excessively in order to please someone
  • synonym: ingratiate - to try to make oneself liked by someone, often through flattery
