pacer

noun
speed setter in race
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsə / (BrE)
a person or animal that sets the speed in a race or activity for others to follow

Examples

Marathon pacer
The pacer kept a steady speed so the runners could achieve their target time.
In the horse race, the pacer led the group for the first mile.
During the cycling event, a pacer helped the team maintain a consistent rhythm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follower - a person who comes after or follows the lead of another
  • synonym: leader - a person who leads or is in front in a race or activity
  • synonym: pace-setter - someone who sets the pace in a race or activity
