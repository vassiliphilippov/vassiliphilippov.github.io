excessively praising or flattering; showing or expressing insincere or obsequious admiration

Examples

Adulatory remarks
His adulatory remarks towards his boss seemed insincere to everyone in the room.
She was uncomfortable with the adulatory attention she received after her performance.
The author's adulatory preface praised the critics in glowing terms.

Synonyms

  • flattering
    4.2 2.08/m
    flattering is everyday praise that often aims to please or gain favor, while adulatory sounds more formal and excessive.
  • fawning
    7.4 0.01/m
    fawning is overly affectionate praise shown to win someone's approval, while adulatory is a broader, formal word for excessive praise.
  • sycophantic
    9.2 0.24/m
    sycophantic describes eager, flattering behavior toward powerful people to gain advantage, while adulatory means excessive praise in general.

Related Terms

  • antonym: critical - expressing disapproval or judgement, opposite of complimentary or adulatory
  • synonym: flattering - giving praise or compliments, sometimes insincerely
