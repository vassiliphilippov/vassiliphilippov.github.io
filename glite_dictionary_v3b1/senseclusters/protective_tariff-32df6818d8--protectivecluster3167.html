protective tariff

noun
tariff to protect domestic industry
1.4 5.3
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈtekt.ɪv ˈtær.ɪf/ /; / /proʊ.ˈtekt.ɪv ˈtær.ɪf/ /; / /prə.ˈtekt.ɪv ˈte.rɪf/ (AmE), /prə.ˈtekt.ɪv ˈtær.ɪf/ /; / /prə.ˈtekt.ɪv ˈte.rɪf/ (BrE)
A tariff imposed on imports to protect domestic industries from foreign competition by making imported goods more expensive.

Examples

A protective tariff
A protective tariff can discourage consumers from buying cheaper foreign goods.
Many economists debate the effectiveness of a protective tariff in promoting economic growth.
The government introduced a protective tariff to help struggling local manufacturers.
