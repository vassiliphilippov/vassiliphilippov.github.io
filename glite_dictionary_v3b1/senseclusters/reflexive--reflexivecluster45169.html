reflexive

adjective
referring back to itself; self-referential
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛksɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase, especially a pronoun) referring back to the subject of the clause or sentence; (of an action or process) directed back on itself.

Examples

Reflexive pronoun
In the sentence "She taught herself," "herself" is a reflexive pronoun.
Some verbs, like "enjoy oneself," are considered reflexive because they refer back to the subject.
The entire process became reflexive as each step influenced the next in a feedback loop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reciprocal - (of pronouns or actions) indicating mutual relation or action
