house

noun
aristocratic or royal family line
1.0 7.9
Pronunciation: / haʊs / (AmE), / haʊs / (BrE)
A family, especially a noble or royal one, regarded as an ancestral line and often used in reference to dynasties or hereditary ruling families.

Examples

House of Windsor
Many European nations were once ruled by the House of Habsburg.
She traces her ancestry back to the ancient House of Plantagenet.
The House of Windsor remains the current royal family of the United Kingdom.
