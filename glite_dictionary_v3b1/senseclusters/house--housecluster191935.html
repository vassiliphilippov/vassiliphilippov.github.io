house

noun
aristocratic or royal family line

A family, especially a noble or royal one, regarded as an ancestral line and often used in reference to dynasties or hereditary ruling families.

Examples

House of Windsor
Many European nations were once ruled by the House of Habsburg.
She traces her ancestry back to the ancient House of Plantagenet.
The House of Windsor remains the current royal family of the United Kingdom.

Synonyms

  • royal house
    4.7 0.1/m
    a royal house is the named family of kings or queens who rule publicly, while a house is any noble ancestral family line.
  • royal line
    4.9 0.06/m
    a royal line is the direct blood descent from a monarch, while a house is the wider ancestral family often linked to rule.
  • dynasty
    5.3 6/m
    a dynasty is a series of rulers from one family who keep power across generations, while a house names the ancestral family itself.
  • family line
    3.2 0.3/m
    a family line is the descent from a common ancestor and often non-royal, while a house usually means a noble or royal ancestral family.
