suprahistorical

adjective
beyond historical context
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːprəˌhɪˈstɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːprəˌhɪˈstɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
existing or considered as valid beyond the boundaries of historical context or time

Examples

A suprahistorical truth
Some philosophers argue that certain moral truths are suprahistorical and apply to all societies.
The legend is often viewed as having a suprahistorical meaning that transcends any specific era.
Her analysis treats the artwork as suprahistorical, focusing on universal themes rather than its time period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: historical - relating to history or past events
  • synonym: timeless - not affected by the passage of time or changes in history
