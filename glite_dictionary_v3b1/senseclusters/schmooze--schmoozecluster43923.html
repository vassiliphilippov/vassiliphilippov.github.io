schmooze

verb
talk casually and amiably
8.6 0.31/m
Pronunciation: /  ÉmuÀêz / (AmE), /  ÉmuÀêz / (BrE)
to talk informally and in a friendly, sometimes ingratiating way, often to gain an advantage

Examples

To schmooze with guests
He schmoozed his way into an invitation to the exclusive event.
She loves to schmooze with her colleagues at office parties.
The sales manager spent the evening schmoozing potential clients.

Synonyms

  • sweet-talk
    5.3 0.52/m
    sweet-talk uses flattery to persuade someone, while schmooze is friendly ingratiating talk used to gain an advantage.
  • jawbone
    9.3 0.00/m
    jawbone means to chat casually and idly with people, while schmooze implies friendly, often self-serving conversation to gain advantage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • synonym: gossip - to talk about the personal lives of others, often informally
