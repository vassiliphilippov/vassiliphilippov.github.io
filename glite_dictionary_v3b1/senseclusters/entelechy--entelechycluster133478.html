entelechy

noun
actualization of potential (philosophy)
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnˈtɛləki / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɛlɪki / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The realization of potential; the condition of something whose essence is fully actualized, as opposed to its potential state.

Examples

The entelechy of life
According to Aristotle, the acorn's entelechy is its development into a fully grown oak tree.
Her artistic talent reached its entelechy only after years of persistent work.
In philosophy, entelechy refers to a being's potential becoming fully realized.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actuality - the state of existing in reality; being actual rather than potential
  • antonym: potentiality - possibility or capacity to develop into something in the future
