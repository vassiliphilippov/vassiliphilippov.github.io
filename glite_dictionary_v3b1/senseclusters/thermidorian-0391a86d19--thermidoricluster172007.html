Thermidorian

adjective
moderate after radicalism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜːrmɪˈdɔːriən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːmɪˈdɔːriən / (BrE)
relating to a shift from radical or revolutionary policies to moderation or conservatism, especially as seen in the Thermidorian Reaction during the French Revolution

Examples

Thermidorian shift
The government adopted a Thermidorian approach, moving away from its earlier revolutionary policies.
Historians often describe the period as a Thermidorian reaction to the excesses of the revolution.
Her Thermidorian attitude surprised supporters who expected more radical reforms.
The Thermidorian regime brought an end to the most radical phase of the French Revolution.
Historians often debate the impact of Thermidorian policies on post-revolutionary France.
Many former revolutionaries became Thermidorian supporters after the fall of Robespierre.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radical - favoring extreme changes or reforms
  • synonym: reactionary - opposing political or social progress or reform
  • synonym: revolutionary - relating to or supporting a revolution
