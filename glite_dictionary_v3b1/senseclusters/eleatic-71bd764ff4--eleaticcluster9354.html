Eleatic

adjective
of Elea philosophers
9.9
Pronunciation: /e.lɪ.ˈæ.tɪk/ /; / /ə.lɪ.ˈæ.tɪk/ (AmE), /e.lɪ.ˈæ.tɪk/ /; / /ə.lɪ.ˈæ.tɪk/ (BrE)
relating to the ancient Greek school of philosophy from Elea, known for the belief that reality is unchanging

Examples

Eleatic philosophy
The Eleatic philosophers argued that true reality is eternal and unchanging.
His theories reflect an Eleatic view, emphasizing the illusion of change.
Many ancient debates contrasted Eleatic ideas with those of other Greek thinkers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Heraclitean - relating to Heraclitus, who believed in constant change
  • synonym: Parmenidean - relating to Parmenides, a key Eleatic philosopher
