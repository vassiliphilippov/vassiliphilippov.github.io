mudhole

noun
hole filled with mud
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a hole or depression in the ground that is filled with mud or muddy water

Examples

A deep mudhole
After the heavy rain, a large mudhole formed at the end of the driveway.
The children laughed as they jumped into the mudhole and splashed each other.
The farmer's boots were covered in mud after he stepped into a deep mudhole in the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ditch - a long, narrow hole dug in the ground, often for water
  • synonym: puddle - a small pool of water, especially after rain
