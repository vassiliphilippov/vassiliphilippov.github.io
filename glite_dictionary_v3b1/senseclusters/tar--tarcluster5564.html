tar

noun
dark, sticky residue from distillation
4.1 4.47/m
Pronunciation: / t…ër / (AmE), / t…ëÀê / (BrE)
a thick, dark, sticky substance obtained as a residue from the distillation of organic materials such as coal or wood, used especially for waterproofing, paving roads, and sealing surfaces

Examples

Hot tar road
Roads are often paved with a mixture of gravel and tar to provide a durable surface.
Smelling the heated tar, I knew the construction crew was repairing the street again.
The workers applied a thick layer of tar to seal the roof against rain.

Synonyms

  • pitch
    6.8 2.25/m
    pitch is a darker, firmer material used to seal ships and roofs, while tar is a softer sticky residue used mainly for paving roads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphalt - a black, sticky substance similar to tar used in road surfacing
