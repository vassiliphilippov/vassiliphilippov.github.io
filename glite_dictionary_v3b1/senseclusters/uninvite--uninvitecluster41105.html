uninvite

verb
to withdraw invitation
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪnˈvaɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈvaɪt / (BrE)
to withdraw an invitation that was previously given to someone

Examples

To uninvite a guest
After the misunderstanding, she decided to uninvite him from her birthday dinner.
It is considered rude to uninvite someone after they have already accepted.
He had to uninvite several guests due to the limited space at the venue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disinvite - to cancel an invitation previously extended
  • antonym: invite - to ask someone to attend an event or join an activity
