inefficiency

noun
lack of efficiency
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
Lack of efficiency or effectiveness; the state or quality of being inefficient.

Examples

Government inefficiency
Government agencies are often criticized for their inefficiency in delivering public services.
He was fired due to repeated inefficiency in managing his team.
The company's inefficiency led to increased costs and missed deadlines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: efficiency - the ability to do something well without wasting resources
  • synonym: ineffectiveness - the quality of not producing the intended result
