dequeue

noun
removal from a queue
8.8
Pronunciation: / diːˈkjuː / (AmE), / diːˈkjuː / (BrE)
the action or process of removing an item from a queue, especially in computing

Examples

A dequeue operation
A dequeue operation removes the first item from the processing queue.
The system performs a dequeue every time a task is completed.
After each dequeue, the next item in line becomes available for processing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enqueue - the action of adding an item to a queue
  • synonym: removal - the act of taking something away
