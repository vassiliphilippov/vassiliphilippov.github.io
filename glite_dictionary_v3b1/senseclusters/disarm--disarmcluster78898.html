disarm

verb
make someone less hostile or angry
3.9 0.94/m
Pronunciation: / d…™sÀà…ërm / (AmE), / d…™sÀà…ëÀêm / (BrE)
to make someone less hostile or angry, often by charm or kindness; to win over an opponent's goodwill

Examples

To disarm with kindness
Her gentle words quickly disarmed his anger, and they began to talk calmly.
His sense of humor could easily disarm even the most skeptical interviewer.
The candidate's humble response disarmed critics in the audience.

Synonyms

    7.0 2.10/m

  • pacify
    5.2 0.64/m
    pacify is to calm or soothe someone who is upset, while disarm is to win an opponent's goodwill using charm and friendliness.
  • appease
    5.8 2.83/m
    appease is to calm someone by giving in to their demands, while disarm is to win goodwill through charm without yielding.
  • defuse
    4.5 2.04/m
    defuse is to make a tense or dangerous situation calmer, while disarm is to win over a person by charm or kindness.
