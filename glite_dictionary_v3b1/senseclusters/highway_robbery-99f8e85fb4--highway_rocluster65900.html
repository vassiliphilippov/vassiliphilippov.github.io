highway robbery

noun
unfairly high price or charge
5.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààha…™Àåwe…™ Ààr…ëÀêb…ôri / (AmE), / Ààha…™we…™ Ààr…íb…ôri / (BrE)
a situation in which something is sold at an unreasonably high price, often considered unfair or exploitative.

Examples

Highway robbery prices
It's highway robbery to pay so much for such a simple meal.
She said that paying $50 for parking downtown was highway robbery.
The price they charge for bottled water at the stadium is highway robbery.

Synonyms

  • daylight robbery
    6.9 0.01/m
    daylight robbery is a phrase for an obvious, outrageous overcharge people notice at once, while highway robbery names an unfair, exploitative high price.
  • price gouging
    6.6 0.66/m
    price gouging is charging excessive prices, often illegal or during shortages, while highway robbery is an informal idiom for an outrageously high price.
