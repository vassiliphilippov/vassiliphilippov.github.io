irony

noun
witty language expressing scorn or insults

the use of words to say the opposite of what you really mean, often to be funny or to criticize

Examples

Dry irony
His irony often left his audience unsure whether he was joking or criticizing.
She spoke with such cutting irony that her remarks felt like veiled insults.
Some people don't understand subtle irony and take the words at face value.
He smiled at the irony that his safety training caused him to break his arm.
The irony of her quitting right after being promoted was not lost on anyone.
There is a certain irony in calling a huge man 'Tiny'.

Synonyms

  • ironism
    9.1 0.00/m
    ironism is a rarer, literary term for using opposite words in writing, while irony is the common, everyday device people use to be funny or critical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest or genuine; lack of irony
