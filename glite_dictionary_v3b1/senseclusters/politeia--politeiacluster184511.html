politeia

noun
Greek state constitution
Pronunciation: / pəˌlɪˈteɪə / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈteɪə / (BrE)
the organization, constitution, or system of government of a state, especially as discussed in ancient Greek philosophy

Examples

Athenian politeia
Plato's politeia explores the ideal structure of a just society.
The concept of politeia was central to ancient Greek political thought.
Scholars debate how best to translate politeia in the context of classical philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constitution - the system of fundamental principles by which a state is governed
  • synonym: government - the group of people with the authority to govern a country or state
