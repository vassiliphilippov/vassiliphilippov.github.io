arenaceous rock

noun
sedimentary rock made of sand grains
Pronunciation: / ˌɛrəˈneɪʃəs rɑk / (AmE), / ˌɛərəˈneɪʃəs rɒk / (BrE)
a sedimentary rock consisting mainly of sand or sand-sized grains

Examples

Layer of arenaceous rock
Geologists identified a layer of arenaceous rock beneath the clay deposits.
Much of the desert landscape consists of exposed arenaceous rock that has eroded over time.
The quarry is known for its extensive arenaceous rock formations, ideal for construction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandstone - type of sedimentary rock composed almost entirely of sand-sized mineral particles
