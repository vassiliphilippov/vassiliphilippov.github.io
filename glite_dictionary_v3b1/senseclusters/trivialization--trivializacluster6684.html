trivialization

noun
making less important
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɪviələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrɪviəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something seem less important, serious, or significant than it really is

Examples

Trivialization of problems
The trivialization of climate change in the media can lead to public misunderstanding.
Many activists are concerned about the trivialization of important social issues.
His comments contributed to the trivialization of the team's hard work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exaggeration - the act of making something seem more important or serious than it is
  • synonym: minimization - the act of reducing the importance or seriousness of something
