gap

noun
a brief interruption or break
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡæp / (AmE), / ɡap / (BrE)

Examples

A technical issue caused a sudden gap in the broadcast.
Her speech continued after a brief gap for questions from the audience.
There was a long gap between the first and second acts of the play.
