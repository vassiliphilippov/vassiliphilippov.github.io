crossflow

adjective
fluid moves across
6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɑːs.floʊ/ /; / /ˈkrɔːs.floʊ/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒs.fləʊ/ (BrE)
arranged so that a fluid or gas moves across a component or channel rather than along its length, especially in engines or heat exchangers

Examples

Crossflow engine design
The engineer designed a crossflow heat exchanger to maximize cooling efficiency.
A crossflow filter allows water to pass across the membrane, improving filtration.
Many modern engines use crossflow cylinder heads for better air intake and exhaust flow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parallel-flow - arranged so that fluid or gas moves in the same direction as the component or channel
