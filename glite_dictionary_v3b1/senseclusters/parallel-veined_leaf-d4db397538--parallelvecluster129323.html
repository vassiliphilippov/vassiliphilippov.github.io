parallel-veined leaf

noun
leaf with parallel veins
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl veɪnd liːf / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛl veɪnd liːf / (BrE)
a leaf in which the veins run parallel to each other from the base to the tip, typically found in many monocotyledonous plants

Examples

A parallel-veined leaf
Grasses are a common example of plants with parallel-veined leaves.
Most monocots, like lilies and orchids, feature a parallel-veined leaf pattern.
The botanist identified the plant by its distinct parallel-veined leaf structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: net-veined leaf - a leaf with veins that form a network
