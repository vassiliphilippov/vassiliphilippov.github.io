order

verb
appoint to a clerical post (religious)
8.4 1.24/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…îrd…ôr / (AmE), / Àà…îÀêd…ô / (BrE)
to appoint someone officially to a clerical position, especially as a priest or minister in certain Christian churches

Examples

To order a priest
After years of study, she was finally ordered as a priest in the Anglican Church.
He was ordered into the priesthood despite his initial reluctance.
The bishop will order several new deacons during the ceremony next week.
