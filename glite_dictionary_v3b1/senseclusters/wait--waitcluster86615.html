wait

verb
delay before acting
Pronunciation: / weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
to stay in one place or delay action until something happens or someone arrives; to look forward to something expected

Examples

To wait for help
Let's wait for a few more days before we proceed.
She chose to wait until she had more information before responding.
The committee decided to wait before making any final decisions.
He is waiting to hear if he got the job.
She could hardly wait to see her friends again after the long summer break.
We waited for the results, hoping for good news.
If you wait here, someone will assist you shortly.
She decided to wait for her friend at the station.
We had to wait outside the restaurant until our table was ready.

Related Terms

  • antonym: act - to do something, especially after waiting
  • synonym: anticipate - to look forward to or prepare for something
  • synonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned
  • antonym: depart - to leave a place, especially in contrast to waiting
  • synonym: expect - to regard something as likely to happen
  • antonym: hurry - to do something quickly
