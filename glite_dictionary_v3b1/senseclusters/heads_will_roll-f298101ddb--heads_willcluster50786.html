heads will roll

verb
people will be punished or fired
4.9
Pronunciation: / hɛdz wɪl roʊl / (AmE), / hɛdz wɪl rəʊl / (BrE)
used to warn that people will be punished or dismissed, especially because of a serious mistake or failure

Examples

Heads will roll after scandal
After the scandal, the manager announced that heads will roll.
If deadlines are missed again, heads will roll in the department.
The team knew that heads will roll if the client was not satisfied.
