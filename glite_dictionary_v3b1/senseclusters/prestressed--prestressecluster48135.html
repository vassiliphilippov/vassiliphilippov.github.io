prestressed

adjective
strengthened before use
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈstrɛst / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈstrɛst / (BrE)
describing concrete or other material that has been strengthened by applying internal stress before use

Examples

Prestressed concrete
The engineers used prestressed concrete to ensure the bridge could handle heavy traffic.
Many modern buildings rely on prestressed beams for added durability and safety.
The new parking garage features prestressed floor slabs to support more vehicles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reinforced - made stronger with additional material or support
