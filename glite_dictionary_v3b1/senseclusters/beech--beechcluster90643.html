beech

noun
a type of large deciduous tree
6.5 3.5
Pronunciation: /biːtʃ/ (AmE), /biːtʃ/ (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of north temperate regions with smooth grey bark, rounded spreading crown, and small triangular edible nuts enclosed in burs

Examples

A tall beech tree
A giant beech towered over the garden, providing shade all summer long.
The forest path was lined with ancient beech trees whose leaves rustled in the wind.
We collected the small nuts that fell from the beech and roasted them over a fire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oak - a large tree with lobed leaves and acorns, common in temperate regions
