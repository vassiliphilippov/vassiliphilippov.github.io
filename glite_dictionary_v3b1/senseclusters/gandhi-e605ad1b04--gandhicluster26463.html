Gandhi

proper noun
Indian leader and advocate of nonviolent resistance
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑndi / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːndi / (BrE)
Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (1869–1948), Indian political and spiritual leader who advocated nonviolent resistance in the struggle for India's independence from Great Britain.

Examples

Gandhi led India
Gandhi led India to independence through a campaign of nonviolent resistance.
Many people have visited the places where Gandhi once lived and worked.
The teachings of Gandhi continue to inspire civil rights movements around the world.
