ruffle

verb
make ripples on water
5.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌf.əl/ (AmE), /ˈrʌf.əl/ (BrE)
to disturb the surface or smoothness of something, making it uneven or causing small waves, ripples, or fluttering

Examples

The sudden breeze began to ruffle the calm surface of the pond.

The cat ruffled its fur when it sensed danger nearby.

The bird's wings ruffled the surface of the lake as it landed.
