greet at the door

verb
welcome someone at the entrance
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɡriːt ət ðə dɔr / (AmE), / ɡriːt ət ðə dɔː / (BrE)
to welcome someone immediately as they arrive, usually by meeting them at the entrance

Examples

To greet at the door politely
It's considered polite to greet at the door rather than having visitors let themselves in.
The hostess made a point to greet at the door despite the crowd inside.
Whenever guests arrive, we always greet at the door to make them feel welcome.
