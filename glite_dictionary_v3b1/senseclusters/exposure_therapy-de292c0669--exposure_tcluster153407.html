exposure therapy

noun
psychological treatment for anxiety/trauma
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊʒɚ ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊʒə ˈθerəpi / (BrE)
a form of psychological treatment in which a person is exposed to the source of their anxiety or trauma in a controlled setting to help overcome it

Examples

Exposure therapy treatment
Extensive research supports the effectiveness of exposure therapy in reducing symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder.
Many patients with phobias benefit significantly from exposure therapy as part of their treatment plan.
The psychologist recommended exposure therapy to help her cope with the aftermath of the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cognitive behavioral therapy - a type of psychotherapy that helps patients change unhelpful thinking or behavior patterns
