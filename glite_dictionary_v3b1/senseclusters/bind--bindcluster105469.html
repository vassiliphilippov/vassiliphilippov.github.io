bind

verb
form a chemical bond
Pronunciation: / baɪnd / (AmE), / baɪnd / (BrE)
to attach, join, or stick closely to something; to form a close connection (including in chemistry or computing)

Examples

To bind to a surface
In this reaction, the nitrogen atom binds to the carbon atom.
Some metals bind strongly with oxygen, forming oxides.
The enzyme binds to the substrate at the active site.
Proteins can bind to the surface of the cell membrane.
Some types of paint do not bind well to damp walls.
The glue will bind the paper to the cardboard securely.
During startup, the server binds the socket to port 8080 so clients can connect.
You must bind the event handler to the button to receive click events during testing.
After the call, the configuration variable was bound to a new value for that session.
