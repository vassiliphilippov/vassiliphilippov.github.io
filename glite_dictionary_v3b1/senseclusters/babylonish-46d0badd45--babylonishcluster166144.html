Babylonish

adjective
corrupt and idolatrous
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbæbɪˌloʊnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbæbɪˌləʊnɪʃ / (BrE)
morally or spiritually corrupt and idolatrous, especially in reference to a church, government, or society, as symbolized by biblical Babylon

Examples

Babylonish practices
The reformers denounced the old rituals as Babylonish and demanded a return to simpler worship.
He accused the ruling elite of maintaining a Babylonish system filled with greed and false values.
Some writers described the city as Babylonish, warning of its spiritual dangers.
