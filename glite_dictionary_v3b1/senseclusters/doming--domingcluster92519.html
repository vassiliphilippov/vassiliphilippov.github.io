doming

noun
upward crust deformation
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
the geological process in which rock layers or the Earth's crust are pushed upward to form a dome-shaped structure

Examples

Geological doming
The doming of the region created a series of gentle hills over millions of years.
Scientists observed doming in the landscape as magma pushed up from beneath the Earth's crust.
Satellite images revealed new doming activity near the volcanic field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subsidence - the sinking or downward settling of the Earth's surface
  • synonym: uplift - the raising of part of the Earth's surface
