infield

noun
field near farmhouse
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌfiːld / (BrE)
a field near a farmhouse, often used for growing crops or for intensive farming

Examples

Farm infield
The farmer rotated crops in the infield to keep the soil healthy.
Vegetables were usually grown in the infield because it was close to the house.
After the harvest, sheep grazed in the empty infield during the winter months.
