polyacrylamide

noun
water-absorbing polymer
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliəˈkrɪləˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒliəˈkrɪləˌmaɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic polymer used in scientific, industrial, and water treatment applications, especially for making gels

Examples

polyacrylamide gel
Scientists use polyacrylamide gels to separate proteins in laboratory experiments.

Disposable diapers often contain polyacrylamide to absorb and lock in liquid.
The researchers prepared a polyacrylamide gel to analyze the protein samples.
Many water treatment plants use polyacrylamide to help remove impurities from drinking water.
The technician mixed the polyacrylamide solution carefully to ensure accurate results in the experiment.
