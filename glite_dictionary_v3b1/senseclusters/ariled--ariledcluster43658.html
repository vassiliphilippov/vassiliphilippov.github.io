ariled

adjective
having a fleshy seed covering (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrɪld / (AmE), / ˈɑːrɪld / (BrE)
(of some seeds) having a fleshy and usually brightly colored covering known as an aril.

Examples

Ariled seeds
Botanists classify seeds as ariled when they develop a soft outer layer from the plant.
Many tropical fruits produce ariled seeds to attract animals for dispersal.
The bright red pomegranate seeds are ariled and edible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aril - the fleshy appendage covering some seeds
