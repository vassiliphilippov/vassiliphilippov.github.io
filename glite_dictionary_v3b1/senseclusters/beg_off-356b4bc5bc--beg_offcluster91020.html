beg off

verb
ask to be excused from a commitment
5.4 5.6
Pronunciation: /beg ɔːf/ /; / /beg ɑːf/ (AmE), /beg ɒf/ /; / /beg ɔːf/ (BrE)
to ask to be excused or to withdraw from a commitment, obligation, or planned activity, usually by giving an excuse

Examples

To beg off a task
He tried to beg off the project, saying he was too busy.
I'm sorry, but I have to beg off from the dinner party tonight.
She begged off attending the meeting due to a family emergency.
He respectfully begged off joining the committee due to his busy schedule.
I have to beg off dinner tonight because I'm not feeling well.
She's always quick to beg off when extra work is assigned.
