knickerbocker

noun
New York City resident
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪkərˌbɑkər / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəbɒkə / (BrE)
a native or long-established resident of New York City, especially one descended from the city's early Dutch settlers

Examples

Old New York knickerbocker
Her grandfather was a proud knickerbocker who traced his roots back to the city's earliest days.
The museum exhibit highlighted the traditions of the old knickerbocker families of Manhattan.
He considered himself a true knickerbocker, having lived in New York City his entire life.
