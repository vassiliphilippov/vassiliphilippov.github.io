burnt lime

noun
white crystalline oxide; calcium oxide
7.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùnt la…™m / (AmE), / b…úÀênt la…™m / (BrE)
A white, caustic, crystalline substance (calcium oxide) produced by heating limestone, used in making cement, calcium hydroxide, and for various industrial purposes.

Examples

Burnt lime powder
Contact with water causes burnt lime to react violently, producing heat and calcium hydroxide.
Large quantities of burnt lime are needed in the steel manufacturing process.
The workers used burnt lime to prepare mortar for the ancient stone walls.

Synonyms

  • calcium oxide
    5.3 0.01/m
    calcium oxide is the precise chemical name used in science and industry, while burnt lime is the traditional name for the same substance.
  • quicklime
    8.3 0.11/m
    quicklime is the common industrial name used on building sites and factories, while burnt lime is the older term for calcium oxide.
  • unslaked lime
    9.9 0.00/m
    unslaked lime is lime that has not been mixed with water and is very reactive, while burnt lime is the dry heated calcium oxide used in industry.
  • calcined lime
    8.1 0.00/m
    calcined lime is produced by heating limestone in a kiln, while burnt lime is the common name for the resulting calcium oxide.
  • caustic lime
    6.6 0.00/m
    caustic lime is named for its strong alkaline burn and is used in agriculture, while burnt lime is the general industrial name.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quicklime - another name for calcium oxide
  • degree higher: slaked lime - calcium hydroxide, produced by adding water to burnt lime
