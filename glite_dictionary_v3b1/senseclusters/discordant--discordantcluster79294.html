discordant

adjective
not in agreement or harmony
7.4 1.16/m
Pronunciation: / d…™sÀàk…îrd…ônt / (AmE), / d…™sÀàk…îÀêd…ônt / (BrE)
not in agreement or harmony; clashing or conflicting, especially in sound or ideas

Examples

Discordant sounds
He played a discordant note that disrupted the harmony of the piece.
The colors in this painting seem discordant rather than blended.
Their opinions were often discordant, leading to frequent debates.
Her views were discordant with the rest of the team.
The discordant notes made the music difficult to listen to.
There was a discordant mixture of voices shouting in the hall.

Synonyms

  • dissonant
    7.1 0.15/m
    dissonant is used for harsh or clashing sounds and ideas, while discordant is a more general lack of agreement or harmony.
  • clashing
    4.9 0.00/m
    clashing shows a strong, sharp mismatch in look or sound, while discordant is a broader term for lack of harmony.
  • conflicting
    4.2 0.85/m
    conflicting describes ideas or interests that directly oppose each other, while discordant often covers clashes in sound or opinion.
  • disharmonious
    7.8 0.03/m
    disharmonious feels unpleasant or out of tune to the ear, while discordant can mean simply not in agreement or harmony.
  • inharmonious
    8.3 0.03/m
    inharmonious sounds or looks rough and uneven, while discordant covers both clashes of sound and differences in ideas.
  • unharmonious
    7.3 0.01/m
    unharmonious does not form a pleasing whole in sound or design, while discordant implies an active clash or disagreement.
  • inconsonant
    9.8 0.01/m
    inconsonant is a more formal word for inconsistency or mismatch, while discordant is more common and used in many contexts.
  • incongruent
    7.9 0.37/m
    incongruent looks wrong or does not fit in form or content, while discordant means lacking harmony or agreement in a wider sense.
  • inharmonic
    9.9 0.03/m
    inharmonic describes tones or sounds that lack musical harmony, while discordant also applies to clashing ideas or nonmusical things.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concordant - in agreement; consistent
  • synonym: dissonant - lacking harmony; harsh in sound
  • antonym: harmonious - in agreement or forming a pleasing whole
