blitzer

noun
person making sudden attack
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪtsər / (AmE), / ˈblɪtsə / (BrE)
a person who carries out a sudden, intense attack or campaign to overwhelm an opponent or target

Examples

Political blitzer
The blitzer launched a rapid campaign that caught the opposition by surprise.
During the protest, a skilled blitzer coordinated several flash demonstrations across the city.
In the debate, she acted as a blitzer, overwhelming her opponent with quick arguments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attacker - a person who attacks someone or something
  • antonym: defender - a person who protects or resists attack
