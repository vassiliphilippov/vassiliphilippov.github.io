perceived

adjective
felt or recognized without direct evidence
2.1 3.6
Pronunciation: / pərˈsiːvd /; / pərˈsivd / (AmE), / pəˈsiːvd / (BrE)
noticed, felt, or recognized (not necessarily proven or measured); observed or regarded in a particular way

Examples

The perceived problem
Her perceived slight influenced how she treated her colleagues.
The resignation stemmed from a perceived lack of support by management.
There was a perceived threat in the room even though nothing was visible.
Noise levels in the room were higher than perceived by most listeners.
The perceived risk was much higher than the actual risk involved.
There was a perceived difference in the way the two groups responded to the treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: real - actually existing or present
  • synonym: sensed - detected or felt without direct evidence
  • antonym: unnoticed - not observed or detected; not perceived
