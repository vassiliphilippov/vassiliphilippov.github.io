generation

noun
the average period between parents and their children
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the average period, typically considered as about 20–30 years, during which children grow up and become adults, and have children of their own

Examples

Generation gap
A generation ago, computers were rare in households.
He hoped future generations would learn from their mistakes.
That tradition disappeared after just one generation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cohort - a group of people born around the same time
