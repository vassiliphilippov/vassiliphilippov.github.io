McBurney's point

noun
anatomical site related to appendicitis
Pronunciation: / məkˈbɝniːz pɔɪnt / (AmE), / məkˈbɜːniːz pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a point one third of the way along a line drawn from the right anterior superior iliac spine to the umbilicus, usually the site of greatest tenderness in cases of acute appendicitis

Examples

McBurney's point pain
A patient complaining of pain at McBurney's point may be suffering from acute appendicitis.
Tenderness at McBurney's point is a classic clinical sign in the diagnosis of appendicitis.
The doctor pressed gently on the McBurney's point to check for signs of appendicitis.
