Lachesis

proper noun
snake genus; mythological figure
Pronunciation: / ˈlækəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈlækəsɪs / (BrE)
a genus of large, venomous pit vipers known as bushmasters, or a figure in Greek mythology who measured the thread of life

Examples

Lachesis snake species
The Lachesis snake is one of the largest venomous snakes in the Americas.
In Greek mythology, Lachesis was responsible for measuring the length of each person's life.
Researchers studied the habitat of Lachesis to better understand its behavior in the wild.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Atropos - a Fate in Greek mythology who cuts the thread of life
  • synonym: bushmaster - a large, venomous snake found in Central and South America
