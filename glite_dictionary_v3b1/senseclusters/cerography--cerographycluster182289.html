cerography

noun
engraving technique using waxed plates
Pronunciation: / sɛˈrɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / sɛˈrɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of engraving designs on a wax-coated plate to create a printing surface, often finished with electrotyping

Examples

Cerography printing process
As a student of graphic arts, she became fascinated by cerography and its place in publishing history.
In the 19th century, cerography allowed for inexpensive reproduction of detailed maps.
The museum showcased historical printing methods, including cerography and copperplate engraving.
