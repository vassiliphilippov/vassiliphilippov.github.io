discriminable

adjective
able to be distinguished
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskrɪmɪnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnəbəl / (BrE)
able to be distinguished or recognized as different; capable of being discriminated.

Examples

A discriminable feature
Discriminable features are essential for accurate image recognition by software.
The difference between the two colors was barely discriminable to the human eye.
The reviewer highlighted several discriminable qualities in the artist's work.

  • antonym: indistinguishable - not able to be distinguished or discriminated
