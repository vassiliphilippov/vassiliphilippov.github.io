kinesin

noun
cell transport protein
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkaɪ.nə.sɪn/ /; / /ˈkɪn.ə.sɪn/ (AmE), /ˈkaɪ.nə.sɪn/ /; / /ˈkɪn.ə.sɪn/ (BrE)
a protein that transports materials within cells by moving along microtubules

Examples

Kinesin protein transport
The movement of vesicles inside the cell is powered by kinesin.
Researchers study how kinesin carries nutrients along microtubules.
Without kinesin, essential materials would not reach their destinations within the cell.

Related Terms
