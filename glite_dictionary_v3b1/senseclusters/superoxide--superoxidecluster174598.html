superoxide

noun
a compound containing the O2− anion
6.6 6.6
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.ˈɑːk.saɪd/ (AmE), /suː.pə.ˈɒk.saɪd/ (BrE)
a chemical compound or anion (O2−) formed by adding an electron to an oxygen molecule, important in chemistry and biology

Examples

The superoxide ion
Excess superoxide in the body can cause oxidative stress and damage to tissues.
Potassium superoxide is used in breathing masks to generate oxygen.
The enzyme superoxide dismutase plays a vital role in removing superoxide radicals from the cell.
Researchers measured the levels of superoxide in mitochondrial samples to assess cellular health.
SOD enzymes help to break down harmful superoxide into oxygen and hydrogen peroxide.
The presence of superoxide radicals in the cell can cause oxidative stress and damage.
