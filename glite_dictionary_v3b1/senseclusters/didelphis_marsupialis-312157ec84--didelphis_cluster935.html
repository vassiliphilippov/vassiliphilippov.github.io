Didelphis marsupialis

noun
species of North and South American opossum
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdɛlfɪs mɑːrˌsuːpiˈælɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈdɛlfɪs mɑːˌsjuːpɪˈɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A species of opossum native to the eastern United States and parts of Central and South America, known for its omnivorous diet and habit of feigning death when threatened.

Examples

Didelphis marsupialis species
Researchers studied the diet of Didelphis marsupialis in both rural and urban habitats.
The Didelphis marsupialis is often found near suburban areas where food is abundant.
When threatened, Didelphis marsupialis is known to play dead, a tactic called thanatosis.
