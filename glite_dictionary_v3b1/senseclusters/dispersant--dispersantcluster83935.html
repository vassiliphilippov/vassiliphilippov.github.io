dispersant

adjective
causing dispersion
6.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɜrsənt / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsənt / (BrE)
causing or helping to cause particles or substances to spread out and become evenly distributed.

Examples

Dispersant effect
The dispersant properties of the chemical help prevent clumping in the solution.
A dispersant agent was added to the mixture to ensure even distribution of particles.
Scientists tested several dispersant compounds to improve the paint's consistency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coagulating - causing particles to come together and form a mass
  • synonym: dispersive - tending to spread out or scatter
