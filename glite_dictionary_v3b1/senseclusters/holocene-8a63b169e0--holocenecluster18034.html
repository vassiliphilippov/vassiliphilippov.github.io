Holocene

noun
current geological epoch since last ice age
7.6 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊ.lə.siːn/ (AmE), /ˈhɒl.ə.siːn/ /; / /ˈhəʊ.lə.siːn/ (BrE)
The geological epoch that began approximately 11,700 years ago, following the last major ice age, and continues to the present day.

Examples

The Holocene epoch
Geologists debate whether the Earth has entered a new epoch after the Holocene.
Many scientists study climate change by examining evidence from the Holocene period.
The Holocene is marked by the development of human civilizations and agriculture.
