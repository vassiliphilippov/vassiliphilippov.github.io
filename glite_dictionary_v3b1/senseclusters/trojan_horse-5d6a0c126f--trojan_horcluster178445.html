Trojan horse

noun
someone who undermines from within
7.4 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtro äd í…ôn h…îrs /; / Ààtro ä.d í…ôn h…îrs / (AmE), / Ààtr…ô äd í…ôn h…îÀês /; / Ààtr…ô ä.d í…ôn h…îÀês / (BrE)
a person, thing, or strategy that seems harmless but is actually intended to cause harm or secretly achieve a hidden goal

Examples

A Trojan horse strategy
Many saw the new employee as a Trojan horse working for the competition.
The government feared infiltration by a Trojan horse intent on leaking secrets.
Vigilance is essential to detect a Trojan horse within the organization.
Many saw the generous offer as a Trojan horse designed to manipulate public opinion.
She realized too late that the partnership was a Trojan horse for corporate espionage.
The IT team warned that the new email attachment could be a Trojan horse carrying malware.
