wavy-grained

adjective
timber with irregular, wavy fibers
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvi ɡreɪnd / (AmE), / ˈweɪvi ɡreɪnd / (BrE)
(of timber) having fibers that run irregularly rather than in parallel, giving a wavy appearance to the grain.

Examples

Wavy-grained timber
Due to its wavy-grained structure, this type of timber is more challenging to plane smoothly.
Some woodworkers specifically seek out wavy-grained boards for their striking appearance in finished furniture.
The cabinet was made from wavy-grained walnut, which gave it a beautifully unique texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: straight-grained - of timber; having fibers running predominantly parallel to each other
