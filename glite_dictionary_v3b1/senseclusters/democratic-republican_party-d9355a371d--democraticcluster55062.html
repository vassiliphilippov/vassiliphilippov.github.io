Democratic-Republican Party

noun
early 19th-century U.S. political party
7.7
Pronunciation: / dɛməˌkrætɪk rɪˈpʌblɪkən ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / dɛməˌkrætɪk rɪˈpʌblɪkən ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a former major political party in the United States in the early 19th century that opposed the Federalist Party and favored limiting the powers of the federal government through a strict interpretation of the Constitution
