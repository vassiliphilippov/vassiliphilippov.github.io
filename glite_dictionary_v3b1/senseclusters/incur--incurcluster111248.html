incur

verb
become subject to something unwelcome
4.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɝː / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜː / (BrE)
to become subject to something unwelcome or unpleasant as a result of one's actions

Examples

To incur costs
By breaking the rules, he incurred the wrath of his teammates.
If you fail to pay your taxes on time, you may incur penalties.
The company incurred significant losses during the recession.

  • antonym: avoid - to keep away from or prevent something unpleasant
