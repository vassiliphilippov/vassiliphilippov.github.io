microsporidian

noun
microscopic parasite of arthropods and fishes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊspɔːˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊspəˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
a microscopic, spore-forming parasite that infects and destroys host cells, particularly in arthropods and fishes

Examples

A microsporidian parasite
Researchers are developing new treatments to target microsporidian infections in commercially important shrimp species.
Some microsporidians have complex life cycles involving several developmental stages within their arthropod hosts.
The microsporidian infects the intestinal cells of its host, often causing disease in fish populations.
