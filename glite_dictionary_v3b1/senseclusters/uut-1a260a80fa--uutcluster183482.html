Uut

noun
former symbol for element 113 (nihonium)
9.9
Pronunciation: / juː ˈjuː tiː / (AmE), / juː ˈjuː tiː / (BrE)
The former chemical symbol for ununtrium, a radioactive transuranic element with atomic number 113. Now known as nihonium (Nh).

Examples

The element Uut
Before being named nihonium, element 113 was temporarily called Uut.
No significant quantity of Uut exists in nature due to its extreme instability.
The discovery of Uut was an important milestone in nuclear chemistry research.
