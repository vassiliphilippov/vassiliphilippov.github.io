ice-free

adjective
not covered with ice; open to travel
Pronunciation: / aɪs friː / (AmE), / aɪs friː / (BrE)
(of a place or body of water) not covered by ice and open to travel or movement.

Examples

An ice-free river
After a mild spring, the river became ice-free much earlier than usual.
Scientists monitored the ice-free areas of the lake to study aquatic life.
The shipping route remained ice-free throughout the winter, allowing vessels to pass safely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: icy - covered with or consisting of ice
