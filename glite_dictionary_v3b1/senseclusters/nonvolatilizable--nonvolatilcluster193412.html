nonvolatilizable

adjective
not capable of being vaporized easily
Pronunciation: / nɑnˌvoʊ.ləˈtɪl.aɪ.zə.bəl /; / ˌnɑnˌvɑləˈtaɪlɪzəbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˌvɒl.əˈtɪl.ɪ.zə.bəl /; / ˌnɒnvəˈlatɪlʌɪzəbəl / (BrE)
(of a substance) not capable of being easily converted into vapor; not readily volatilizing.

Examples

Nonvolatilizable substance
Bicarbonate is considered a nonvolatilizable buffer in biological systems.
The nonvolatilizable residue left behind suggested the presence of stable inorganic compounds.
Weak acids, such as hydrochloric acid, are not nonvolatilizable and can vaporize under certain conditions.
Many salts are nonvolatilisable and remain as solids when heated.
Sulfuric acid is a nonvolatilisable substance under typical laboratory conditions.
The chemist separated the volatile and nonvolatilisable components of the mixture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nonvolatile - not easily vaporized
  • antonym: volatile - (of a substance) easily evaporated at normal temperatures
