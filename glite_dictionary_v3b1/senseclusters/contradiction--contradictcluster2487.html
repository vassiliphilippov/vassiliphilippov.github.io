contradiction

noun
act of disagreeing with a statement
4.1 2.09/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀên.tr…ôÀàd…™k. É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåk…ín.tr…ôÀàd…™k. É…ôn / (BrE)
the act of stating that someone else's statement is wrong or not true

Examples

Contradiction of his claim
Any contradiction of his opinions would quickly lead to an argument.
She learned to avoid open contradiction of her supervisor during meetings.
The witness's statement was met with immediate contradiction from the defense attorney.

Synonyms

  • rebuttal
    6.7 3.10/m
    a rebuttal is a reasoned argument meant to disprove another person's point, while a contradiction is simply saying that statement is wrong.
  • counterstatement
    7.3 0.02/m
    a counterstatement is a formal opposing remark often made in response, while a contradiction directly states that someone else's claim is wrong.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - the state of sharing the same opinion; absence of disagreement
  • synonym: denial - the act of stating something is not true
