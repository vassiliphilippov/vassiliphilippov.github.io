on thin ice

preposition
in a risky or dangerous situation
6.1
Pronunciation: / ɑn ˈθɪn aɪs / (AmE), / ɒn ˈθɪn aɪs / (BrE)
in a situation where one is at risk of trouble, danger, or negative consequences

Examples

To be on thin ice
After missing the last meeting, Mark knew he was on thin ice with his manager.
She realized she was on thin ice when the teacher gave her a warning.
You’re on thin ice if you keep ignoring the company’s rules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: safe - not at risk; not in danger
  • synonym: skating on thin ice - being in a risky or precarious position
