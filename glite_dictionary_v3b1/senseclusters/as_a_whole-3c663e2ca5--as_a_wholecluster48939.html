as a whole

adverb
in its entirety; all parts considered
1.8
Pronunciation: / æz ə hoʊl / (AmE), / æz ə həʊl / (BrE)
considering all parts or members of something together; in its entirety

Examples

As a whole group
As a whole, the country's economy has improved over the last decade.
It's important to evaluate the proposal as a whole rather than focus on individual elements.
The class, as a whole, did exceptionally well on the exam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in part - only when considering some parts, not the entirety
  • synonym: overall - including everything or everyone; in general
