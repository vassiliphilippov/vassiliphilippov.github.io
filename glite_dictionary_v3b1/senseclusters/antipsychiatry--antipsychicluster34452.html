antipsychiatry

noun
opposition to psychiatry
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪsaɪˈkaɪətri / (AmE), / ˌæntɪsaɪˈkaɪətri / (BrE)
a movement or set of ideas that opposes or criticizes traditional psychiatric methods and practices

Examples

Antipsychiatry movement
The antipsychiatry movement gained attention in the 1960s for challenging mainstream mental health treatments.
Some authors have written extensively about antipsychiatry and its impact on mental health care.
Debates between supporters of psychiatry and antipsychiatry continue in academic circles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: psychiatry - the medical study and treatment of mental illness
  • synonym: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behavior
