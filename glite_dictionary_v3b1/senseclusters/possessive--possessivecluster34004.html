possessive

adjective
desirous of owning; unwilling to share
Pronunciation: / pəˈzɛsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv / (BrE)
wanting to own or control things or people and not willing to share them

Examples

Possessive partner
Her possessive attitude made it difficult for her siblings to play with any of her belongings.
Some people can become possessive in relationships, not wanting their partner to spend time with others.
The little boy was so possessive of his toys that he refused to lend them to his friends.
He grew possessive of his friends in college, not wanting to share them with anyone.
Her boyfriend can be quite possessive and dislikes her talking to other men.
Some parents become overly possessive of their children as they grow older.

Related Terms

  • synonym: controlling - having a strong need to dominate or influence others
  • antonym: detached - emotionally uninvolved or indifferent
  • antonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • synonym: jealous - feeling or showing envy of someone or their achievements and advantages
