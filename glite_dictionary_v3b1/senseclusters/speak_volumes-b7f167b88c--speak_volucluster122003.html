speak volumes

verb
convey much meaning without words
3.6
Pronunciation: / spiːk ˈvɑl.juːmz / (AmE), / spiːk ˈvɒl.juːmz / (BrE)
to convey a great deal of meaning or information, especially without using words

Examples

Speak volumes about feelings
Her expression spoke volumes about her feelings on the matter.
His silence during the meeting spoke volumes more than any words could.
The empty streets spoke volumes about the impact of the new policy.
