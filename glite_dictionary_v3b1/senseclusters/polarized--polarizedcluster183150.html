polarized

adjective
uneven electrical charge
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpoʊ.lə.raɪzd/ (AmE), /ˈpəʊ.lə.raɪzd/ (BrE)
having an uneven distribution of electrical charge or magnetic poles, resulting in distinct positive and negative regions

Examples

Polarized molecules
The water molecule is polarized, which allows it to dissolve many substances.
When exposed to an electric field, the atoms became polarized and aligned in a specific direction.
A polarized material can interact differently with light depending on its orientation.
