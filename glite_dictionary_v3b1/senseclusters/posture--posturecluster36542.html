posture

noun
capacity or readiness, especially of the military
7.7 3/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêst É…ôr / (AmE), / Ààp…íst É…ô / (BrE)
the capability or condition of an organization, especially the armed forces, in terms of personnel and equipment that determines its ability to confront threats or engage in conflict

Examples

Military posture
Officials debated whether the current military posture was adequate to deter aggression.
Recent budget cuts have raised concerns about the nation's strategic posture.
The country's defensive posture has improved significantly over the past decade.

Synonyms

  • military posture
    7.3 0.1/m
    military posture describes a nation's armed forces' strength and readiness in official planning, while posture can mean any organization's condition.
  • military capability
    5.5 1/m
    military capability focuses on what forces can do in action, while posture covers their condition and visible readiness.
  • military strength
    4.3 0.6/m
    military strength counts soldiers, weapons, and resources, while posture describes both those numbers and how ready the force appears.
  • strength
    5.2 4/m
    strength refers to the numbers of troops and equipment, while posture adds readiness, organization, and the ability to meet threats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: readiness - state of being prepared for action or use
  • synonym: strength - capacity or power to withstand force or attack
