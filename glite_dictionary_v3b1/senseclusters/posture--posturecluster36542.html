posture

noun
capacity or readiness, especially of the military
3.6 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːstʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɒstʃə / (BrE)
the capability or condition of an organization, especially the armed forces, in terms of personnel and equipment that determines its ability to confront threats or engage in conflict

Examples

Military posture
Officials debated whether the current military posture was adequate to deter aggression.
Recent budget cuts have raised concerns about the nation's strategic posture.
The country's defensive posture has improved significantly over the past decade.
