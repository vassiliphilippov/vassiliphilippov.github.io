Comtism

noun
Auguste Comte's positivist philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˈkɒmˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
The positivist philosophy developed by Auguste Comte, advocating that metaphysics and theology should be replaced by a hierarchy of sciences, with mathematics at the base and sociology at the top.

Examples

Comtism philosophy
Auguste Comte's Comtism laid the groundwork for later positivist thought.
Many sociologists have studied the influence of Comtism on the development of modern social sciences.
The rejection of metaphysics in favor of scientific inquiry characterizes Comtism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: positivism - The philosophy that science is the only valid knowledge, closely related to Comte's ideas
