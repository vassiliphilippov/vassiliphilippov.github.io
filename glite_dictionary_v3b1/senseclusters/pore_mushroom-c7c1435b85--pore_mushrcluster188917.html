pore mushroom

noun
mushroom with pores or tubes beneath cap
9.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / p…îr Ààm å ÉÀå…πuÀêm / (AmE), / p…îÀê Ààm å ÉÀåruÀêm / (BrE)
A woody mushroom with a spore-bearing surface composed of tubes or pores, belonging to the families Polyporaceae or Boletaceae, often persistent and woody at maturity.

Examples

A pore mushroom tube
Experts classify the pore mushroom by examining the tube-like pores on its underside.
Many hikers enjoy searching for pore mushrooms growing on old logs deep in the forest.
The thick, woody texture of the pore mushroom makes it easy to identify among other fungi.

Synonyms

  • polypore
    9.9 0.03/m
    polypore is the standard name used by mushroom experts and in field guides, while pore mushroom is a broader, descriptive name.
  • pore fungus
    9.1 0.00/m
    pore fungus is a more scientific term for the whole organism, while pore mushroom names the woody fruiting body you see on logs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bolete - a mushroom of the family Boletaceae, typically with pores instead of gills
  • synonym: polypore - a type of fungus with pores or tubes on the underside of the fruiting body
