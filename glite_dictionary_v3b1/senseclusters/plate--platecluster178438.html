plate

noun
baseball: base where batter stands and runs score
Pronunciation: / pleɪt / (AmE), / pleɪt / (BrE)
(baseball) the base consisting of a flat, often pentagonal, rubber slab at which the batter stands and which must be touched by a runner to score a run.

Examples

Home plate
After touching every base, he made sure to tag plate for the home run.
The batter stepped up to the plate as the crowd cheered.
The runner slid across plate just ahead of the throw, scoring the winning run.

Related Terms

  • synonym: base - one of the four stations a runner must touch to score in baseball
  • synonym: home plate - the same base in baseball; the rubber slab the batter stands at
