reinsurance

noun
insurance for insurance companies to share risk
5.7 7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.ɪnˈʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˈʃʊə.rəns / (BrE)
insurance purchased by an insurance company from another company in order to share or mitigate risk for certain policies or portfolios.

Examples

Reinsurance can help insurance companies cover exceptionally large or risky policies.

Without reinsurance, a single disaster could bankrupt a small insurer.
