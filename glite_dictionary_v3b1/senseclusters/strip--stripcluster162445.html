strip

noun
thin, narrow piece of material
5.0
Pronunciation: / strɪp / (AmE), / strɪp / (BrE)
a long, narrow piece of material, such as wood, metal, cloth, or land

Examples

A strip of cloth
A thin strip of metal runs along the edge of the table.
He nailed a strip of wood to the wall to hang the pictures.
The model boat was made from several strips of balsa wood.
A narrow strip of land separated the two gardens.
He used a strip of cloth to bind the wound.
The carpenter cut a strip of wood for the frame.
He tore a strip of cloth to use as a bandage.
The carpenter nailed a strip of wood along the edge of the shelf for extra support.
They placed colored strips of paper along the floor for the celebration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: band - a flat, thin strip or loop of material used as a fastener or as decoration
  • synonym: ribbon - a long, narrow strip of fabric, typically decorative
  • antonym: sheet - a broad, flat piece of material (opposite in width to 'strip')
  • antonym: slab - a broad, flat, thick piece of stone, wood, or other material (contrasts in width and thickness to strip)
  • synonym: slat - a thin, narrow strip of wood or metal, especially one of a series forming a barrier or support
