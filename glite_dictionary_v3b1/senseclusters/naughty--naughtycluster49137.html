naughty

adjective
badly behaved; disobedient
1.0 2.94/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…îti / (AmE), / Ààn…îÀêti / (BrE)
(especially of a child) badly behaved or disobedient.

Examples

Naughty child
If you are naughty at school, you might get a warning from the teacher.
She scolded her naughty puppy for chewing the shoes.
The naughty boy was sent to his room for misbehaving.

Synonyms

  • bratty
    5.5 0.47/m
    bratty describes a spoiled, rude child who acts badly at home or school, while naughty means simply disobedient or badly behaved.
  • mischievous
    4.1 0.42/m
    mischievous often means intending to cause trouble or harm in a playful way, while naughty means being disobedient or badly behaved.

Related Terms

  • antonym: well-behaved - behaving in a polite and proper manner
