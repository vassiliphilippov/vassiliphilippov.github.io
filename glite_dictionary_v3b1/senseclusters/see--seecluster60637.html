see

verb
find out, determine (by inquiry)
1.0 136/m
Pronunciation: / siÀê / (AmE), / siÀê / (BrE)
to make sure, check, or ensure that something happens or is done

Examples

To see that it works
Can you see if there are any messages for me?
Let me see if the store is still open before we go.
She called the office to see whether her package had arrived.
Can you see that the guests have enough to eat?
He will see that the documents are delivered on time.
Please see that all the lights are switched off before you leave.
See if you can find the answer in the textbook before we ask the teacher.
Before making any decisions, let's see what our options are.
I'll see whether I have time to help you tomorrow.

Synonyms

  • see to it
    3.5 3.64/m
    see to it arranges and checks that a task is done for you, while see means to make sure something happens.
  • make sure
    1.1 132/m
    make sure is to actively check or do something yourself to be certain, while see means to make certain something happens.
  • ensure
    2.8 110/m
    ensure is a formal verb used in writing to make something certain, while see is more general and often used in speech.
  • assure
    3.5 15/m
    assure is often used to comfort or promise a person about a result, while see focuses on making something happen.
  • guarantee
    2.8 53/m
    guarantee promises a result or quality and can sound strong or legal, while see simply means to make sure.
  • insure
    3.0 1.10/m
    insure usually means protect with insurance or financial cover, while see means to make certain something happens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascertain - to find out with certainty

  • synonym: consider - to think about carefully; contemplate or deliberate
  • synonym: decide - to make a choice or come to a conclusion
  • synonym: ensure - to make certain that something will happen
  • synonym: verify - to check that something is true or correct
