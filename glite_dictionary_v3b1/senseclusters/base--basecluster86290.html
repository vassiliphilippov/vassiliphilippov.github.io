base

noun
structural unit of nucleic acids
Pronunciation: / beɪs / (AmE), / beɪs / (BrE)
a phosphoric ester of a nucleoside; the basic structural unit of nucleic acids such as DNA or RNA

Examples

DNA base
A mutation often involves a change in a single base in the DNA sequence.
Each base in RNA complements a specific base in DNA.
The four types of bases found in DNA are adenine, thymine, cytosine, and guanine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nucleotide - a compound consisting of a nucleoside linked to a phosphate group
