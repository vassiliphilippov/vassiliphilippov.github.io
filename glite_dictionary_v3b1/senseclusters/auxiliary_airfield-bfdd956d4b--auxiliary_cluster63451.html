auxiliary airfield

noun
supporting or secondary airfield
4.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɑːg.ˈzɪl.jə.riː ˈer.fiːld/ /; / /ɔːg.ˈzɪl.jə.riː ˈer.fiːld/ (AmE), /ɔːg.ˈzɪl.jə.riː ˈeə.fiːld/ (BrE)
An airfield used in a supporting or secondary role, often to relieve traffic from a main airfield or for emergency use.

Examples

The auxiliary airfield base
During training exercises, pilots practiced emergency landings at the remote auxiliary airfield.
The auxiliary airfield is maintained for use during adverse weather or maintenance work at the main airport.
The military constructed an auxiliary airfield nearby to handle overflow traffic from the main base.
