brim

verb
to be full to the point of overflowing
4.0
Pronunciation: / brɪm / (AmE), / brɪm / (BrE)
to be full to the top or to fill something to the top, sometimes with a strong feeling

Examples

To brim with joy
Her heart brimmed with joy at the sight of her family.
His eyes brimmed with tears as he listened to the moving story.
The glass brimmed with water, ready to spill at any moment.
He brimmed the jug until the milk nearly spilled.
She brimmed the glass with sparkling water for her guest.
The river threatened to brim over its banks after the heavy rains.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empty - to remove contents from; not full
  • synonym: overflow - to flow over the edge
  • synonym: spill - to come out of a container
