statute law

noun
law enacted by legislation
6.2 0.03/m

Law codified and enacted by a legislative authority, as opposed to law originating from judicial decisions or custom.

Examples

Changes to statute law
Legal reforms typically involve changes to existing statute law by parliament.
Matters not covered by statute law are often decided based on common law principles.
The government passed new statute law to regulate online privacy.

Synonyms

  • statutory law
    5.9 0.18/m
    statutory law is the body of laws passed by a legislature, while statute law refers to laws enacted by a legislative authority.
  • legislation
    4.4 41/m
    legislation is laws or a set of laws passed or proposed by a legislature, while statute law means enacted law by legislative authority.
