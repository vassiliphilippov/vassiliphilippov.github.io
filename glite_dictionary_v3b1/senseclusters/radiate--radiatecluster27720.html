radiate

verb
spread out from a center
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈreɪdieɪt / (BrE)
to extend or spread outward (or inward) from a central point or focus

Examples

Colors radiate outward
Brilliant colors radiated out from the center of the painting, drawing the eye.
Sunbeams radiate in all directions from the clouds after the storm.
The city's main roads radiate from the central square like spokes on a wheel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emanate - to spread out from a source
