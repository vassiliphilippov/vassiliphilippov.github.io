talipot palm

noun
tall palm tree of India and Sri Lanka
Pronunciation: / ˈtælɪˌpɑm / (AmE), / ˈtælɪˌpɑːm / (BrE)
a tall palm tree native to southern India and Sri Lanka, known for its enormous leaves used as umbrellas, fans, or writing material

Examples

The talipot palm tree
In ancient times, monks would write sacred texts on strips cut from the talipot palm.
The garden features a magnificent talipot palm towering above the other trees.
The leaves of the talipot palm are so large that they are often used as makeshift umbrellas during monsoon rains.
