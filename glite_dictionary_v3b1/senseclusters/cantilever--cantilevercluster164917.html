cantilever

noun
projecting beam fixed at one end
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.təˌli.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɪˌliː.və / (BrE)
a long projecting horizontal beam or structure that is fixed at only one end and carries a load at the other end or along its length.

Examples

A bridge cantilever
Engineers tested the strength of the cantilever under heavy loads.
The balcony was supported by a steel cantilever extending from the building's main frame.
The new bridge design uses a massive cantilever to span the river without supporting pillars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: girder - a large iron or steel beam used for building bridges and large buildings
