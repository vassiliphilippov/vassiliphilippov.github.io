localistic

adjective
brain region-specific
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊkəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the idea that mental processes or functions are located in specific, separate areas of the brain

Examples

Localistic brain theory
The scientist presented a localistic theory of memory, suggesting each type is stored in a different brain area.
Some psychologists argue against localistic models, preferring to see the brain as working as a whole.
Her research supports a localistic approach to understanding how language is processed in the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holistic - emphasizing the whole rather than individual parts
