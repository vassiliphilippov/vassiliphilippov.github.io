thief

noun
person who steals property
1.0 22/m
Pronunciation: / Œ∏iÀêf / (AmE), / Œ∏iÀêf / (BrE)
A person who steals another person's property, especially by stealth and without using force or violence.

Examples

A thief steals money
After the party, she realized a thief had stolen her wallet from her purse.
Security cameras captured the thief in the act of breaking into the store.
The thief slipped out the window and disappeared into the night.

Synonyms

    7.6 0.03/m

  • purloiner
    9.6 0.00/m

  • larcener
    9.9 0.00/m

  • stealer
    4.6 0.09/m

Related Terms

  • synonym: burglar - a person who illegally enters buildings, usually at night, to steal
  • synonym: robber - a person who takes property unlawfully, often by force or threat
  • antonym: victim - a person harmed by another's actions, often in theft
