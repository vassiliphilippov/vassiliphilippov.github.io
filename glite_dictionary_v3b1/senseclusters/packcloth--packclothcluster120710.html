packcloth

noun
durable fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈpækˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈpækˌklɒθ / (BrE)
a strong, coarse fabric used for making bags, tents, or for covering goods

Examples

Strong packcloth fabric
The backpack was made from tough packcloth to handle heavy loads.
They used packcloth to cover the equipment during the rainy season.
A tent constructed with packcloth can resist wind and water effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canvas - a heavy, durable fabric used for tents and sails
  • antonym: muslin - a lightweight cotton fabric
