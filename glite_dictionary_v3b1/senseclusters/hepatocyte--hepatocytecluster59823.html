hepatocyte

noun
liver cell
Pronunciation: / hɪˈpætəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / hɪˈpætəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a main type of cell in the liver that performs essential metabolic, detoxifying, and synthetic functions

Examples

A hepatocyte cell
A single hepatocyte can carry out hundreds of different metabolic reactions in the liver.
Damage to hepatocytes can impair the liver's ability to detoxify harmful substances.
Researchers studied how hepatocytes regenerate after liver injury.
Hepatocytes are responsible for producing bile, which aids in digestion.
Damage to hepatocytes can lead to serious liver diseases such as hepatitis.
Researchers studied how hepatocytes respond to different medications in the laboratory.
