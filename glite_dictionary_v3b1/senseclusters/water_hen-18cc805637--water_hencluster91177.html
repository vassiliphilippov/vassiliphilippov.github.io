water hen

noun
aquatic bird of the genus Gallinula
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ hɛn /; / ˈwɔtər hɛn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə hɛn / (BrE)
a small aquatic bird, such as a moorhen or coot, that lives near water

Examples

A water hen bird
A family of water hens built their nest on the riverbank last spring.
The distinctive red frontal shield makes the water hen easy to identify among other water birds.
We watched a water hen gliding gracefully across the pond near the reeds.
A flock of water hens gathered at the edge of the lake.
The distinctive call of the water hen echoed across the marsh.
We often see water hens paddling through the reeds in early spring.
