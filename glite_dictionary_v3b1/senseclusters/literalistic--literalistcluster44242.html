literalistic

adjective
strictly literal
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪt̬əˈrælɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɪtərəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
tending to interpret words, texts, or statements in a strictly literal or exact way, without considering deeper meaning

Examples

A literalistic interpretation
His literalistic approach to the instructions caused him to miss the intended shortcut.
A literalistic interpretation of the law can sometimes lead to unfair results.
She gave a literalistic answer, ignoring the humor in the question.

Related Terms

  • antonym: figurative - departing from a literal use of words; metaphorical
  • synonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
