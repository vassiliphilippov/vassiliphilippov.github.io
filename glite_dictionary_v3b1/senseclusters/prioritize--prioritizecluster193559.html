prioritize

verb
arrange by importance
1.0
Pronunciation: / praɪˈɔːrɪˌtaɪz /; / praɪˈɔrɪtaɪz / (AmE), / praɪˈɒrɪtəʌɪz /; / praɪˈɒrɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or deal with things in order of importance or urgency

Examples

To prioritize tasks
It's important to prioritize your own well-being when making decisions.
The team will prioritize customer feedback in the next project phase.
We need to prioritize the most urgent tasks before starting anything else.
She learned to prioritise her health over her work after falling ill.
The manager will prioritise projects based on their impact and urgency.
You need to prioritise your assignments if you want to meet all the deadlines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deprioritize - to make something less important or urgent
  • synonym: prioritize - assign a priority to, especially in American English
  • synonym: rank - to assign a position or order to
