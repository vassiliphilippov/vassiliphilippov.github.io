downslide

noun
downward slope
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌslaɪd / (BrE)
a downward slope or incline on a surface, often leading to a lower level or area

Examples

A grassy downslide
The children enjoyed running down the grassy downslide behind their house.
Heavy rain caused rocks to tumble down the steep downslide near the road.
They built a retaining wall at the base of the steep downslide to prevent soil erosion.
