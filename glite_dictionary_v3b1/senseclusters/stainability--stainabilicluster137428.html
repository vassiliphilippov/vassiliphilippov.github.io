stainability

noun
capacity of cells to absorb specific dyes
8.9
Pronunciation: /steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
(cytology) the capacity of cells or cell parts to absorb and retain specific dyes when stained for microscopic examination.

Examples

Cell stainability
Poor stainability can hinder accurate identification of cellular structures under the microscope.
Researchers evaluated the stainability of the cells to differentiate between healthy and abnormal tissue.
The stainability of the tissue samples varies greatly depending on the type of dye used.
