Edda

noun
Old Icelandic mythological poems
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈed.ə/ (AmE), /ˈed.ə/ (BrE)
either of two Old Icelandic collections of mythological and heroic poems, composed between the 9th and 13th centuries, and serving as primary sources for Norse mythology.

Examples

The Edda poems
Scholars often refer to the Edda as the best source for understanding Viking beliefs.
Snorri Sturluson's Prose Edda is distinct from the older Poetic Edda.
The Edda contains many stories about Norse gods, including Odin and Thor.
