obligation

noun
legal agreement requiring action or payment

a legal or financial agreement or contract that requires you to pay money or do something, often with a penalty if you do not

Examples

A legal obligation
If you break the terms of the obligation, you may have to pay a penalty.
She failed to meet her payment obligation by the agreed deadline.
The company is under a legal obligation to provide safe working conditions.
A government obligation typically guarantees regular payments to the bondholder.
He purchased a municipal obligation as a way to invest his savings safely.
The company issued an obligation to investors, promising repayment with interest in five years.

Synonyms

  • financial obligation
    4.5 0.31/m
    a financial obligation is an amount you must legally pay to someone, while an obligation can be any legal or contractual duty.
  • liability
    5.1 22/m
    liability is a legal responsibility to pay money or damages, while obligation focuses on any legal or financial contractual duty.
  • legal duty
    4.0 0.23/m
    legal duty is a rule you must follow because law requires it, while obligation often comes from an agreement or contract.
  • commitment
    3.2 16/m
    commitment is a promise or plan someone makes and keeps, while obligation is a formal legal or financial duty with possible penalties.
