trishaw

noun
three-wheeled vehicle
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌʃɔ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪʃɔː / (BrE)
a three-wheeled, usually pedal-powered vehicle used for carrying passengers (and sometimes goods), especially in some Asian countries

Examples

A trishaw ride
The tourist enjoyed a ride through the city in a colorful trishaw.
Vendors often use a trishaw to transport goods to the market.
Children waved as the trishaw passed by their school in the morning.
We took a trishaw tour through the old streets of the city.
The trishaw driver waited patiently while his passengers took photos.
In some Asian cities, a trishaw is a popular way for tourists to get around.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bicycle - a two-wheeled vehicle powered by pedals
  • synonym: bicycle taxi - a bicycle adapted to carry passengers for hire
  • synonym: rickshaw - a two- or three-wheeled passenger vehicle pulled by a person
  • degree higher: taxi - a car for hire with a driver, used by passengers for a fare
