callithumpian

adjective
of a noisy, boisterous parade or celebration
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkæləˈθʌmpiən / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈθʌmpiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a noisy, boisterous parade or celebration, especially one marked by discordant noise and rowdy behavior.

Examples

A callithumpian parade
Children created a callithumpian procession down the street, making as much noise as possible.
The town's annual parade had a decidedly callithumpian atmosphere, with pots banging and horns blasting.
Their callithumpian celebration lasted well into the night, delighting some neighbors and annoying others.
