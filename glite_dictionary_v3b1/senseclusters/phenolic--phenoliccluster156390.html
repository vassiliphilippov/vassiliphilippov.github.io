phenolic

noun
a synthetic thermosetting resin
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑlɪk / (AmE), / fɪˈnɒlɪk / (BrE)
a synthetic resin made by polymerizing phenol with formaldehyde, used especially in molded or laminated products and adhesives

Examples

Phenolic resin
Many pool balls are crafted out of durable phenolic resin to withstand frequent impacts.
The electrical insulator was made from phenolic for its excellent heat resistance.
The lab technician mixed the chemicals to create a strong phenolic used in circuit boards.
