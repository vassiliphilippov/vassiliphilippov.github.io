cable

noun
nautical unit of length
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
A unit of length used at sea, equal to one tenth of a nautical mile (about 185.2 meters or 202 yards).

Examples

A cable length
According to the charts, the harbor is two cables deep in that area.
He reported the vessel was drifting about one cable offshore after the storm.
The ship anchored half a cable from the shore to avoid hitting the rocks.
