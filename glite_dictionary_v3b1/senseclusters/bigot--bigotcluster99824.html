bigot

noun
intolerant, prejudiced person
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡət / (BrE)
a person who is intolerant toward those holding different opinions; a prejudiced person

Examples

A bigot person
He was labeled a bigot because of his narrow-minded remarks about other cultures.
Her comments revealed that she was a bigot who refused to listen to other viewpoints.
It is important to stand up to bigots, even if they are in your own family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fanatic - a person filled with excessive and single-minded zeal
  • antonym: tolerant - willing to accept opinions different from one's own
