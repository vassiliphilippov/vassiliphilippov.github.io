methemoglobin

noun
oxygen-deficient hemoglobin
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn /; / ˌmɛθiˌmoʊɡloʊˈbɪn / (AmE), / ˌmiːtˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn /; / ˌmiːthɪˈməʊɡləʊbɪn /; / ˌmiːθiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
a form of hemoglobin in the blood that cannot carry oxygen effectively because the iron is in the ferric (Fe3+) state

High levels of methemoglobin in the blood can cause serious health problems if not treated quickly.

Exposure to certain chemicals can increase methaemoglobin levels in the bloodstream.
High concentrations of methaemoglobin may lead to a condition called methemoglobinemia.
