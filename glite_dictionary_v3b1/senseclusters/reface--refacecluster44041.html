reface

verb
put new facing on a garment
8.1 5.4
Pronunciation: / riˈfeɪs /; / ˌriˈfeɪs / (AmE), / riˈfeɪs /; / ˌriːˈfeɪs / (BrE)
to cover the surface of something (such as a garment or building) with new material to make it look new or fresh

Examples

To reface a coat
After years of wear, she decided to reface her favorite coat.
Carefully refacing the collar can extend the life of your garment.
The tailor agreed to reface the jacket to give it a newer look.
After years of weather damage, the company refaced the office building with modern panels.
Many homeowners choose to reface their kitchen cabinets instead of replacing them.
The city decided to reface the historic clock tower to preserve its character.
