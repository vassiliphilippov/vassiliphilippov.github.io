depression

noun
serious medical mood disorder
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈpre.ʃən/ /; / /də.ˈpre.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈpre.ʃən/ (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which a person experiences persistent feelings of deep sadness and loss of interest in activities, requiring clinical attention.

Examples

Clinical depression diagnosis
After months of persistent sadness and fatigue, she was diagnosed with clinical depression.
Individuals diagnosed with depression often require support from mental health professionals.
Treatment options for major depression may include therapy and medication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mania - an abnormally elevated mood, opposite of depression in mood disorders
  • synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or sorrow
