patterning

noun
developmental organization
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpæt.ər.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpæt.ə.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which cells or tissues develop organized spatial arrangements and structures during the growth of an embryo

Examples

Embryonic patterning
Chemical signals play a crucial role in patterning tissues during embryonic development.
Errors in patterning can lead to developmental abnormalities in the organism.
Scientists study patterning to understand how complex body structures are formed from simple cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: differentiation - the process by which cells become specialized in structure and function
  • synonym: organization - the arrangement of parts into a structured whole
