untangle

verb
free from difficulty or complication
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to free someone or something from a difficult situation or a complicated problem; to disentangle

Examples

To untangle a problem
It took hours to untangle the complex legal issues involved in the case.
Management is working to untangle the confusion over the new policy.
She tried hard to untangle herself from the financial mess she was in.
