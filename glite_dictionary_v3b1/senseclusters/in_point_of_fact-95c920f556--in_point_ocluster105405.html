in point of fact

adverb
actually; in reality
7.5 4.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn pɔɪnt əv fækt/ /; / /ən pɔɪnt əv fækt/ (AmE), /ɪn pɔɪnt əv fækt/ /; / /ən pɔɪnt əv fækt/ (BrE)
used to emphasize the truth or reality of a statement, often when it contrasts with what was previously said or assumed

Examples

In point of fact

She seemed angry, but in point of fact she was just tired after a long day.
While many assumed the project was a failure, in point of fact it exceeded expectations.
