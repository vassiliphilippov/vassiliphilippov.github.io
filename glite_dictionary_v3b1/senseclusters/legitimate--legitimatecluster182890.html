Synonyms

  • lawful
    2.9 4.73/m
    lawful is allowed or recognized by law in official settings, while legitimate also means accepted for right reasons.
  • licit
    7.7 0.22/m
    licit is permitted by law or rules but sounds formal or rare, while legitimate is common and means lawful or acceptable.
  • allowable
    2.7 1.66/m
    allowable is what rules permit in a specific case, while legitimate carries stronger sense of being rightly lawful or justified.
  • valid
    3.1 33/m
    valid is officially in force or based on sound reasons, while legitimate emphasizes being lawful, acceptable, or justifiable.
  • justified
    4.1 0.19/m
    justified has a clear, good reason behind an action, while legitimate adds legal or wider social acceptance to that reason.
  • justifiable
    5.1 3.07/m
    justifiable can be shown as reasonable or defensible in argument, while legitimate also needs legal or official acceptance.
