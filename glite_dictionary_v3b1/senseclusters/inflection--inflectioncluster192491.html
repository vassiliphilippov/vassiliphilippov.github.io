the way the voice changes in pitch, tone, or loudness when speaking; patterns of intonation or stress in speech

Examples

Voice inflection
English has a variety of inflection patterns that indicate surprise or doubt.
Her voice had a gentle inflection at the end of each question.
The actor used clever inflection to express subtle emotions in his speech.
Actors use inflection to convey emotion and meaning in their performances.
The teacher's inflection made the lesson much more engaging for the students.
You can often tell someone's mood from the inflection in their voice.

Synonyms

  • intonation pattern
    5.6 0.01/m
    intonation pattern shows the typical rise and fall used to mark questions or statements, while inflection covers voice changes in pitch, tone, or loudness.
  • intonation
    4.6 0.67/m
    intonation is the rise and fall of pitch that changes meaning, while inflection also includes tone, loudness, and stress patterns in speech.
  • prosody
    8.1 0.19/m
    prosody is the overall rhythm and stress that shapes speech feeling, while inflection focuses on specific pitch, tone, or loudness changes.
  • modulation
    6.3 0.13/m
    modulation is the active variation of pitch or loudness speakers use for effect, while inflection names the general voice-change patterns.
  • tone of voice
    3.1 1.87/m
    tone of voice shows a person's emotional sound or attitude when speaking, while inflection refers to pitch, tone, or loudness patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice when speaking
  • antonym: monotone - a continuing sound, especially of someone's voice, that is unchanging in pitch
