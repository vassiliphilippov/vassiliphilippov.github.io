inflection

noun
variation of stress and intonation in speech
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈflɛkʃən /; / ɪnˈflɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃən /; / ɪnˈflɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the way the voice changes in pitch, tone, or loudness when speaking; patterns of intonation or stress in speech

Examples

Voice inflection
English has a variety of inflection patterns that indicate surprise or doubt.
Her voice had a gentle inflection at the end of each question.
The actor used clever inflection to express subtle emotions in his speech.
Actors use inflection to convey emotion and meaning in their performances.
The teacher's inflection made the lesson much more engaging for the students.
You can often tell someone's mood from the inflection in their voice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice when speaking
  • antonym: monotone - a continuing sound, especially of someone's voice, that is unchanging in pitch
