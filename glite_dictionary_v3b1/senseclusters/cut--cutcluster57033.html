cut

adjective
edited to have parts removed
3.5
Pronunciation: / kʌt / (AmE), / kʌt / (BrE)
(of a film, text, etc.) with parts removed, especially to make it shorter or more suitable for a particular purpose.

Examples

Cut version
Several scenes were missing from the cut edition of the novel.
She preferred the cut film because it was shorter and easier to follow.
The studio released a cut version of the controversial documentary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uncut - not having parts removed; complete
