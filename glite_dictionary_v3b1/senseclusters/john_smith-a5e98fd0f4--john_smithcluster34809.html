John Smith

proper noun
English explorer, Jamestown founder (1580–1631)
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn smɪθ / (AmE), / dʒɒn smɪθ / (BrE)
(1580–1631) An English explorer and soldier who played an important role in the establishment of the Jamestown colony in Virginia and is known for his association with Pocahontas.

Examples

John Smith explorer
John Smith played a significant role in the early years of the Jamestown settlement.
As an explorer, John Smith mapped much of the Chesapeake Bay region.
Many historians study the life of John Smith and his interactions with Pocahontas.
