esterification

noun
formation of an ester
Pronunciation: / ˌɛstərɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛstərɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which an acid and an alcohol combine to form an ester and water

Examples

The esterification reaction
The esterification of acetic acid and ethanol produces ethyl acetate and water.
In organic chemistry labs, students often perform esterification to create fragrant compounds.
Industrial esterification is used in the manufacture of plastics and synthetic fibers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ester - a compound formed from an acid and an alcohol
  • antonym: hydrolysis - a chemical reaction in which water breaks down a compound
