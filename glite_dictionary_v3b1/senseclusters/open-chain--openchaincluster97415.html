open-chain

adjective
not having a ring structure (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən tʃeɪn / (BrE)
(of a molecule) having an open, unbranched or branched chain structure, not forming a ring.

Examples

Open-chain molecule
Alkanes such as hexane are typical examples of open-chain hydrocarbons.
Glucose can exist in both open-chain and cyclic forms, depending on the conditions.
The scientist explained that the compound was open-chain rather than a ring structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cyclic - (of a molecule) having a closed ring structure
