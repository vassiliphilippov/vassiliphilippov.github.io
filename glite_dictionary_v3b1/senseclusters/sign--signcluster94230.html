sign

noun
event seen as an omen or message
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: / saɪn / (AmE), / saɪn / (BrE)
an event, object, or occurrence that shows something exists, is happening, or will happen; an omen or indication

Examples

A sign of change
Many people took the sudden change in weather as a sign of good fortune to come.
She believed the rainbow was a sign that her prayers had been answered.
Superstitions often arise from seeing unusual events as a sign of something significant.
Many birds returning early are a sign that spring is near.
She took his silence as a sign that he was upset.
The pale color of his face was a clear sign that he was unwell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evidence - something that shows something exists or is true
  • synonym: indication - something that points out or shows
  • synonym: omen - an event regarded as a portent of good or evil
  • synonym: symptom - a sign of a disease or problem
