voting trust

noun
arrangement transferring voting rights to trustees
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊ.tɪŋ trʌst / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.tɪŋ trʌst / (BrE)
An arrangement in which shareholders transfer their voting rights to trustees while retaining ownership of their shares.

Examples

A voting trust agreement
By placing their shares in a voting trust, investors unified their influence on company decisions.
Shareholders agreed to form a voting trust that would last five years.
The company's management set up a voting trust to solidify control before the merger.
