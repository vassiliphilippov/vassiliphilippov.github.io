institutionalism

noun
emphasis on institutions
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃə.nəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of emphasizing established institutions, formal rules, and procedures, often prioritizing stability over change

Examples

Strong institutionalism belief
The university's strong institutionalism made it slow to adopt new teaching methods.
Critics argue that excessive institutionalism can prevent organizations from adapting to change.
Her research focused on how institutionalism shapes government decision-making processes.
Modern institutionalism explores how laws and organizations influence economic development.
Some critics argue that institutionalism overlooks the role of individual action in society.
The rise of institutionalism changed how political scientists study government systems.
The school’s strict institutionalism left little room for creative teaching methods.
Critics argue that institutionalism can stifle innovation in large organizations.
Over time, the charity’s flexible methods were lost to institutionalism, replaced by strict rules and paperwork.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bureaucracy - a system of government or management with many rules and procedures
  • antonym: individualism - the belief in the importance of individual rights and independence
  • antonym: innovation - the introduction of new ideas or methods
  • synonym: structuralism - a theory focusing on structures in society or language
  • synonym: traditionalism - the upholding of tradition or established customs
