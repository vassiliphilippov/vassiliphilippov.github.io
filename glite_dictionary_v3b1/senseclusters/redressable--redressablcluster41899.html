redressable

adjective
able to be remedied
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdrɛsəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈdrɛsəbəl / (BrE)
able to be corrected, remedied, or set right, especially by legal means

Examples

A redressable injury
The court found the injury to be redressable through financial compensation.
Not every mistake is redressable by law, especially if no harm was done.
The lawyer argued that the client's losses were redressable under current regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irreparable - impossible to repair or correct
  • synonym: remediable - able to be remedied or corrected
