put the case

verb
present arguments in support
8.4
Pronunciation: / pʊt ðə keɪs / (AmE), / pʊt ðə keɪs / (BrE)
to present arguments or advocate in support of a particular point of view or proposal

Examples

To put the case clearly
At the meeting, she put the case for investing in renewable energy.
He skillfully put the case against further budget cuts during the discussion.
The lawyer will put the case to the jury tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: argue - to give reasons or cite evidence in support of an idea
  • synonym: make the case - to argue in favor of something, often in a formal setting
