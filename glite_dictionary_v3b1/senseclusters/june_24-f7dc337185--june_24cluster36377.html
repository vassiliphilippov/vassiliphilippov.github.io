June 24

noun
quarter day (England, Wales, Ireland)
4.4 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / d íun tw…õn.ti Ààf…îr / (AmE), / d íuÀên Àåtw…õnti Ààf…îÀê / (BrE)
A quarter day observed on the 24th of June in England, Wales, and Ireland, traditionally associated with the payment of rents and other annual dues.

Examples

June 24 Midsummer Day
The contract was signed to begin on June 24, coinciding with Midsummer Day.
Traditionally, June 24 marks one of the four quarter days in the British calendar.
Under the old lease, the rent was due every June 24 and December 25.
