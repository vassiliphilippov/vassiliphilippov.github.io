Pacific yew

noun
tree species of western North America
8.1
Pronunciation: / pəˈsɪfɪk juː / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk juː / (BrE)
A small or medium-sized evergreen tree (Taxus brevifolia) native to the Pacific coast of North America, valued for its fine hard wood.

Examples

The Pacific yew tree
Hiking through the old-growth forest, we spotted a rare Pacific yew along the trail.
Scientists discovered that bark from the Pacific yew contains compounds used in cancer treatment.
The Pacific yew is known for its tough, close-grained wood used in specialty products.
