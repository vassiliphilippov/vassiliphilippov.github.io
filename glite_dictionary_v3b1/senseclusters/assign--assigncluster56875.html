assign

verb
select for a specific purpose
3.8
Pronunciation: / əˈsaɪn / (AmE), / əˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to give someone a particular task, duty, or position of responsibility

Examples

To assign a task
Sarah was assigned to organize the annual meeting this year.
The manager will assign each intern to a different department.
The system can automatically assign tasks based on skill sets.
Each employee was assigned a specific task for the event.
She is assigning the available computers to the students now.
The teacher assigned a new project to the class yesterday.
She was assigned the role of team leader for the event.
Teachers often assign homework to reinforce lessons.
The manager will assign you to a new project next week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocate - to distribute resources or duties for a particular purpose
  • synonym: appoint - to assign a job or role to someone
  • synonym: delegate - to give someone a duty or task
  • synonym: distribute - to give out shares or portions of something
  • antonym: remove - to take away a person from a role or task
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give out or allocate something
