biblicism

noun
biblical expression
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪblɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbɪblɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or style of expression taken from or closely imitating the language of the Bible

Examples

A biblicism in speech
The preacher's speech was full of biblicism, echoing the language of ancient scriptures.
Writers sometimes use biblicism to give their work a solemn or traditional tone.
Her essay included several examples of biblicism to emphasize moral lessons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaism - a word or expression that is old-fashioned or no longer in common use
  • synonym: biblical idiom - an expression or phrase originating from the Bible
