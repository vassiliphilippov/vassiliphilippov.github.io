cruelty

noun
deliberate causing of pain or suffering
3.2 7.64/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkruÀê…ôlti /; / ÀàkruÀêlti / (AmE), / ÀàkruÀê…ôlti / (BrE)
behavior, actions, or an attitude that deliberately causes pain or suffering to others; the quality of being cruel

Examples

Animal cruelty
He was known for his cruelty towards animals, which made people avoid him.
Stories of cruelty during the war are still difficult to listen to.
The cruelty of the punishment shocked everyone in the courtroom.
Acts of cruelty against prisoners are condemned by international law.
She was shocked by the cruelty he showed toward innocent people.
The animal shelter rescued dogs that had suffered terrible cruelty at the hands of their former owners.
He could not understand the cruelty behind such hateful words.
The defendant's cruelty was evident in the complete lack of remorse he showed.
There was a certain cruelty in her laugh that made everyone uncomfortable.

Synonyms

  • brutality
    5.6 8.05/m
    brutality is extreme violent action that causes severe harm to people, while cruelty is the general habit of causing pain deliberately.
  • inhumanity
    6.6 0.76/m
    inhumanity is very cruel behavior showing a great lack of compassion, while cruelty is causing pain on purpose.
  • barbarity
    8.4 0.48/m
    barbarity is brutal, often savage violence and acts, while cruelty is the general willingness to hurt others.
  • inhuman treatment
    8.3 0.03/m
    inhuman treatment is the act of deliberately causing great pain and suffering, while cruelty is the broader quality of harming others.
  • abuse
    3.2 44/m
    abuse is cruel or violent treatment, often repeated over time, while cruelty is a general behavior of causing pain deliberately.
  • ill-treatment
    6.4 0.18/m
    ill-treatment is cruel or inhumane treatment over a period, while cruelty covers the general act or quality of causing suffering.
  • mercilessness
    8.4 0.02/m
    mercilessness is a complete lack of mercy or kindness in action, while cruelty describes causing pain or suffering deliberately.
  • pitilessness
    8.3 0.01/m
    pitilessness shows no pity or compassion, often in feeling, while cruelty is the active behavior that harms others intentionally.
  • ruthlessness
    7.3 0.73/m
    ruthlessness is harsh action to reach goals without pity, while cruelty is a general readiness to cause pain or suffering.

Related Terms
