transhumanist

noun
advocate of human enhancement
8.6
Pronunciation: / trænzˈhjuːmənɪst / (AmE), / trænzˈhjuːmənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or supports using advanced technology to enhance human physical and mental abilities

Examples

A transhumanist thinker
The transhumanist community believes that technology can help overcome many human limitations.
As a dedicated transhumanist, she supports research into artificial intelligence and life extension.
Some critics argue that a transhumanist vision of the future raises important ethical questions.
The transhumanist community supports research into artificial intelligence and biotechnology.
As a transhumanist, she believes technology can help humans overcome biological limitations.
Many transhumanists discuss the ethical challenges of merging humans with machines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bioconservative - a person who opposes the use of technology to alter human nature
  • synonym: futurist - a person who studies or predicts future trends, especially in technology
  • antonym: humanist - a person who values human welfare and emphasizes human potential
