analytical cubism

noun
early phase of Cubism in art
7.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌnælɪˈtɪkəl ˈkjuːˌbɪzəm / (AmE), / əˌnalɪˈtɪkəl ˈkjuːbɪzəm / (BrE)
the early phase of Cubism characterized by the analysis of forms, reduction to geometric shapes, and a monochromatic palette, pioneered by Picasso and Braque between 1907 and 1912.

Examples

Analytical cubism style
Art historians consider analytical cubism a groundbreaking approach to depicting three-dimensional form on a flat canvas.
During analytical cubism, artists focused on muted colors and dissected forms rather than vibrant hues.
The works from Picasso's analytical cubism period often deconstruct objects into angular, overlapping planes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: synthetic cubism - the later phase of Cubism using simpler shapes and brighter colors
