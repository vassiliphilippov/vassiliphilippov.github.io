overplay

verb
exaggerate acting or performance
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to exaggerate or overdo an action or performance, especially in acting, making it less realistic or effective

Examples

To overplay a role
Directors often advise new performers not to overplay their roles on stage.
Some critics felt she overplayed the character's anger, making it seem unnatural.
The actor tended to overplay his emotions in dramatic scenes.
The actor overplayed his role, making the character seem unrealistic.
If you overplay your strengths in an interview, it might sound insincere.
The coach warned the team not to overplay their recent success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it really is
  • synonym: overact - to act a part with exaggerated emotion or expression
  • antonym: underplay - to act or present with restraint or subtlety
