overlearn

verb
to practice excessively
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈlɜrn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlɜːn / (BrE)
to practice or train something so much that it leads to memorization and a loss of ability to adapt or generalize.

Examples

To overlearn training data
If you overlearn the training data, your model may not perform well on new information.
Students sometimes overlearn facts for exams and struggle to apply them in real situations.
The coach warned the team not to overlearn the routine, so they could adapt during the game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overfit - to train a model so much it memorizes data and cannot generalize
  • antonym: underlearn - to practice or train too little to achieve mastery
