tree worm

noun
worm that harms trees
3.6
Pronunciation: / tri wɜrm / (AmE), / triː wɜːm / (BrE)
A worm that infests and damages trees, often by feeding on their leaves, bark, or wood.

Examples

Tree worm infestation
Apple orchards are often monitored to prevent infestations of tree worms.
The damage caused by tree worms can be severe if not treated early.
The forester noticed several tree worms burrowing under the bark of the old oak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caterpillar - larval stage of butterflies and moths, many species damage trees
  • synonym: pest - an animal that harms plants or trees
