throw off

verb
disrupt or confuse plans or routine
2.3
Pronunciation: / θroʊ ɔf / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
to cause confusion or disruption in someone’s plans or schedule

Examples

To throw off plans
Changing the meeting time threw off my entire afternoon schedule.
Her travel plans were thrown off by a delayed flight.
The sudden noise threw off the speaker’s concentration during his presentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand
  • synonym: disrupt - to interrupt the normal progress of something
