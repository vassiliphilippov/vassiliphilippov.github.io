fossil copal

noun
partly mineralized copal from underground
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːsl koʊˈpɑːl / (AmE), / ˈfɒsl kəʊˈpɑːl / (BrE)
Copal that has been partly mineralized and is found underground, representing an intermediate stage between resin and amber.

Examples

Fossil copal jewelry
Some jewelry is made from fossil copal, which is less expensive than true amber.
The geologist carefully extracted a piece of fossil copal from the ancient sediment.
Unlike fresh copal, fossil copal shows some degree of mineralization due to its age.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: amber - a hard yellowish to brownish translucent fossil resin
  • degree lower: copal - a hard, natural resin obtained from tropical trees
