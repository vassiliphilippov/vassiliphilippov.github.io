suffuse

verb
to spread through or over
6.6
Pronunciation: / səˈfjuz / (AmE), / səˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to gradually spread through or over something, especially with a color, light, feeling, or quality

Examples

Light suffuses the room
A warm glow suffused the room as the sun set behind the trees.
Her face was suffused with joy when she heard the good news.
Tears suffused his eyes, making everything look blurry for a moment.
A gentle light suffused the room as the sun rose.
A sense of calm suffused the audience during the soothing music.
Her face was suffused with embarrassment when she realized her mistake.
A gentle pink glow suffused the sky at dawn.
A sense of calm suffused the room as peaceful music played softly.
Warmth suffused his voice as he spoke to the children.
