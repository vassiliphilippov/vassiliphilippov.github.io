suffuse

verb
to spread through or over
7.6 1.55/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàfjuz / (AmE), / s…ôÀàfjuÀêz / (BrE)
to gradually spread through or over something, especially with a color, light, feeling, or quality

Examples

Light suffuses the room
A warm glow suffused the room as the sun set behind the trees.
Her face was suffused with joy when she heard the good news.
Tears suffused his eyes, making everything look blurry for a moment.
A gentle light suffused the room as the sun rose.
A sense of calm suffused the audience during the soothing music.
Her face was suffused with embarrassment when she realized her mistake.
A gentle pink glow suffused the sky at dawn.
A sense of calm suffused the room as peaceful music played softly.
Warmth suffused his voice as he spoke to the children.

Synonyms

  • imbue
    7.7 0.08/m
    imbue fills something deeply with a quality or color, often intentionally, while suffuse implies a softer, gradual spread.
  • permeate
    6.0 2.85/m
    permeate spreads through every part and fills something thoroughly, while suffuse suggests a gentler, gradual spread of color or feeling.
  • bathe
    6.8 0.61/m
    bathe covers or surrounds something in light or quality, while suffuse means a gentler, spreading presence through or over things.
  • diffuse
    5.5 4.17/m
    diffuse spreads or scatters widely across an area or audience, while suffuse suggests flowing slowly through a surface or feeling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drain - to remove a quality or substance from something
  • synonym: imbue - to fill or inspire deeply with a feeling or quality
  • synonym: infuse - to fill or pervade with a quality or feeling
  • synonym: pervade - to spread through every part of something
  • synonym: stain - to color or mark by permeating
