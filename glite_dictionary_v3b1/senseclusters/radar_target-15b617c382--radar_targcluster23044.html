radar target

noun
object or echo on radar display
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪˌdɑr ˈtɑrɡət / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɑː ˈtɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
An object or echo on a radar display that indicates the position of a reflecting surface.

Examples

A radar target on screen
A sudden radar target appeared on the ship's display during the storm.
Engineers checked the strength of the radar target to ensure signal clarity.
The pilot interpreted the radar target as a possible approaching aircraft.
