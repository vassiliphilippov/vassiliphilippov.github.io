eparchy

noun
Eastern Orthodox Church diocese
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈɛpɑːki / (BrE)
A district under the pastoral care of a bishop in the Eastern Orthodox Church; an ecclesiastical province or diocese.

Examples

Orthodox eparchy
He has served as a priest in the eparchy for over twenty years.
Several parishes within the eparchy gathered for the annual festival.
The new bishop was assigned to lead the eparchy in the northern region.
