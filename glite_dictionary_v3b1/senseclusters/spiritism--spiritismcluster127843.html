spiritism

noun
belief in communication with spirits
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɪr.ɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that spirits of the dead or other non-physical entities can communicate with and influence the living, often through mediums

Examples

Spiritism belief
Many people turned to spiritism in the 19th century, seeking to contact loved ones who had passed away.
She became interested in spiritism after attending several séances.
The practice of spiritism often involves mediums who claim to speak with spirits.
