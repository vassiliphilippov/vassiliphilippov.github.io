intercut

verb
to alternate scenes
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkʌt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkʌt / (BrE)
to alternate or insert different scenes, shots, or elements within a sequence, especially in film or video editing

Examples

To intercut scenes
The documentary intercuts interviews with real footage to tell a powerful story.
To create tension, the editor intercut scenes of the chase with flashbacks.
The film intercuts between the hero's childhood and present-day struggles.
The documentary intercuts interviews with real footage to create a powerful narrative.
In the film, action scenes are intercut with flashbacks to reveal the character's past.
The author intercut the main story with diary entries to provide different viewpoints.
The documentary intercuts interviews with real footage from the event to create a powerful narrative.
In the movie, the director intercut scenes of the past and present to build suspense.
They skillfully intercut action sequences with moments of quiet reflection throughout the film.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alternate - to arrange or occur in turn repeatedly
  • synonym: combine - to join or mix two or more things together
  • synonym: crosscut - to alternate between two or more scenes in a film
  • synonym: edit - to prepare film or video by selecting and arranging scenes
  • antonym: separate - to keep things apart or not mix them
  • synonym: splice - to join two pieces of film or video together
