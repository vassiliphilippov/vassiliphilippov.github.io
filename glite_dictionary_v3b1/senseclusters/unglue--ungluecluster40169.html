unglue

verb
to upset emotionally
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡluː / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡluː / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose their composure or become emotionally unsettled

Examples

Bad news unglues her
The sudden criticism seemed to unglue her, and she struggled to respond.
He was completely unglued by the unexpected news and needed time to recover.
Don't let a small mistake unglue you during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful or relaxed
  • synonym: disturb - to interrupt the calm or order of someone
  • synonym: unsettle - to make someone feel anxious or disturbed
