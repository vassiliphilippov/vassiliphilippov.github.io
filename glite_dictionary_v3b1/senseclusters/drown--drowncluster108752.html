drown

verb
to be overwhelmed
Pronunciation: / draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
to be overwhelmed or unable to cope because of a large amount of something, especially work, problems, or emotions

Examples

Drown in work
She felt like she was drowning in paperwork after her colleague left.
Many students are drowning in assignments during exam season.
He was drowning in debt and did not know where to turn for help.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manage - to succeed in dealing with something difficult
  • synonym: overwhelm - to give someone too much of something to deal with
  • synonym: struggle - to have difficulty coping with something
