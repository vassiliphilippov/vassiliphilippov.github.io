arthrospore

noun
resting spore cell in some fungi and algae
7.6 9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.θroʊ.spɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.θrəʊ.spɔː/ (BrE)
a spore-like cell formed when a hypha or filament breaks into separate thick-walled cells, found in some fungi, algae, or bacteria

Examples

A arthrospore cell
Certain molds reproduce by forming arthrospores along their hyphae under adverse conditions.
The presence of arthrospores can be an important diagnostic feature in identifying fungal species.
When the environment becomes unfavorable, some algae produce arthrospores to survive until conditions improve.
Certain bacteria produce arthrospores under stressful environmental conditions.
The presence of arthrospores can help distinguish different types of fungal infections.
Under the microscope, arthrospores appear as distinct, jointed fragments of a filament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conidium - an asexual, non-motile spore found in fungi
  • synonym: spore - a reproductive cell in fungi, plants, and some bacteria
