denaturalize

verb
make less natural or unnatural
3.3
Pronunciation: / diˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈnatʃərəlaɪz /; / diˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪz / (BrE)
To make less natural or to make unnatural.

Examples

To denaturalize a process
Excessive editing can denaturalize the flow of a documentary film.
Some critics argue that city life tends to denaturalize human behavior.
The constant use of digital effects may denaturalize the actor's performance.
Attempts to denaturalise traditional art forms have sparked much debate among critics.
Excessive processing can denaturalise the taste of fresh food products.
Urban planning often denaturalises landscapes, replacing greenery with concrete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artificialize - make something more artificial
  • synonym: denature - to alter the natural qualities of something
  • antonym: naturalize - make something more natural or conform to nature
