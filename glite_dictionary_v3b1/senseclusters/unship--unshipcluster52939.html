unship

verb
to unseat suddenly
8.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈʃɪp/ /; / /ən.ˈʃɪp/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈʃɪp/ /; / /ən.ˈʃɪp/ (BrE)
to remove someone or something from their seat or position suddenly, especially by a quick or unexpected movement

Examples

He was nearly unshipped by the unexpected stop, but managed to hold on tightly.
