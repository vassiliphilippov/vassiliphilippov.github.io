foot

noun
poetic unit of rhythm
8.2 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / fÊŠt / (AmE), / fÊŠt / (BrE)
(in prosody) A group of two or three syllables forming the basic unit of rhythm in a line of poetry.

Examples

A poetic foot
An iamb is a foot with an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable.
In classical poetry, a foot may consist of one stressed and two unstressed syllables.
The poem contains five feet per line, creating a steady rhythm.
