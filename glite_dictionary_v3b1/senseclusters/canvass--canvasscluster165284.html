canvass

noun
survey of public opinion
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.vəs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs / (BrE)
an instance of trying to discover public opinion on a particular subject, especially by interviewing a sample of people

Examples

A canvass of voters
A canvass of local residents revealed widespread support for building the new community center.
The government ordered a canvass to gauge the public's reaction to the new policy.
The results of the canvass helped the campaign refine their strategy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poll - an inquiry into public opinion by questioning a random sample
