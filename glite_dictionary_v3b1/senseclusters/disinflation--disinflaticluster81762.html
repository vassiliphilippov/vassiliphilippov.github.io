disinflation

noun
reduction in the rate of price increases
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a reduction in the rate at which prices increase, especially as a deliberate policy to stabilize the economy

Examples

Economic disinflation
Many analysts predict a period of disinflation following the new fiscal policies.
Rapid disinflation can sometimes lead to higher unemployment rates.
The central bank is aiming for disinflation to keep the economy stable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deflation - reduction of the general level of prices in an economy
  • antonym: inflation - a general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money
