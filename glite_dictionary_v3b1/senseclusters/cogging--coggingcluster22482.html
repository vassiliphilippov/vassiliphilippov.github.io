cogging

noun
metalworking process
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
a metalworking process in which hot metal is repeatedly hammered or rolled to reduce its size and improve its structure

Examples

Cogging hot metal
The steel plant uses cogging to prepare large metal bars for further processing.
After cogging, the metal has a more uniform structure and is easier to shape.
Modern mills have automated much of the cogging process to increase efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forging - shaping metal by heating and hammering
  • synonym: rolling - reducing metal thickness by passing it through rollers
