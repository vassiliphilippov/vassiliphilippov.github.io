visionary

adjective
not practical; speculative
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
(of ideas or plans) not practical or likely to be realized; based on speculation or imagination rather than realism.

Examples

Visionary ideas
Many considered his visionary plans for urban development to be unrealistic and unworkable.
The company dismissed her visionary proposals as too ambitious and speculative for their current resources.
While the project's goals were visionary, investors wanted a more practical and attainable approach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impractical - not adapted for use or action; not sensible or realistic
  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice; focused on what is possible or achievable
  • antonym: realistic - having or showing a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved or expected
