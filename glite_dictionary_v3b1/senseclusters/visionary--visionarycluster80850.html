visionary

adjective
not practical; speculative

(of ideas or plans) not practical or likely to be realized; based on speculation or imagination rather than realism.

Examples

Visionary ideas
Many considered his visionary plans for urban development to be unrealistic and unworkable.
The company dismissed her visionary proposals as too ambitious and speculative for their current resources.
While the project's goals were visionary, investors wanted a more practical and attainable approach.

Synonyms

  • airy
    6.9 0.47/m
    airy suggests vague, lightweight ideas with little detail, while visionary describes imaginative plans that are unlikely to be realized.
  • impractical
    5.3 3.61/m
    impractical describes something that does not work in real life, while visionary focuses on imaginative but unlikely plans.
  • utopian
    7.9 2.58/m
    utopian shows ideal plans for a perfect world, while visionary means imaginative ideas that are often not realistic.
  • wild-eyed
    8.0 0.04/m
    wild-eyed describes extreme, excited schemes that seem crazy, while visionary is imaginative but may often be unrealistic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice; focused on what is possible or achievable
