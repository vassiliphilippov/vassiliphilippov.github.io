ruination

noun
complete destruction; devastating loss
Pronunciation: / ˌruːnəˈʃən /; / ˌruː.nəˈʃən /; / ˌruːnˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌruːɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌruː.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act, process, or state of being completely destroyed, ruined, or losing all value, hope, or reputation

Examples

Complete ruination
Gambling addiction can bring financial ruination to families.
The scandal led to the ruination of his political career.
Years of neglect resulted in the ruination of the historic building.
Many ancient cities fell into ruination over centuries of war and neglect.
She feared ruination if the storm destroyed her family’s home.
The company faced ruination after the financial scandal became public.
He blamed his ruination on risky investments and poor decisions.
The financial scandal led to the ruination of several prominent companies.
Years of drought brought the once-thriving region to complete ruination.
