hard time

noun
imprisonment in maximum security prison
Pronunciation: / hɑrd taɪm / (AmE), / hɑːd taɪm / (BrE)
a period of imprisonment served in a maximum security prison, especially one that is particularly severe or harsh

Examples

Serve hard time
After the trial, he was sentenced to five years of hard time for armed robbery.
Many inmates struggle to adjust when they are released after serving hard time.
She warned her nephew that committing such a crime could land him in hard time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prison sentence - a period of time someone is ordered to spend in prison
