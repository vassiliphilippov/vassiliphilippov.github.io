thermoacidophile

noun
microorganism thriving in acid and heat
Pronunciation: / ˌθɝːmoʊəˈsɪdəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊəˈsɪdəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a microorganism, especially a type of archaebacterium, that thrives in highly acidic and high-temperature environments

Examples

A thermoacidophile microbe
Researchers discovered a new species of thermoacidophile in the acidic pools of Yellowstone.
Scientists study thermoacidophiles to understand how life can survive in hostile conditions.
The hot springs are inhabited by thermoacidophiles that can withstand extreme acidity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alkaliphile - organism that thrives in alkaline (basic) environments
  • synonym: extremophile - organism that thrives in extreme environments
