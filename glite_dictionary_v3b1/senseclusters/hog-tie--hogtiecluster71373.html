hog-tie

verb
to tie someone's limbs together tightly
8.1 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːg.taɪ/ /; / /ˈhɔːg.taɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhɒg.taɪ/ (BrE)
to tie together the hands and feet (or all four legs) of a person or animal so that they cannot move

Examples

To hog-tie calves
Cowboys would hog-tie calves during rodeos as part of the competition.
She felt as though her parents had hog-tied her with their strict rules.
The police hog-tied the suspect to prevent him from escaping.
Police officers sometimes hog-tie unruly suspects to prevent injury.
She threatened to hog-tie her brothers if they didn't stop fighting.
The rancher had to hog-tie the calf to keep it from escaping.
After catching the animal, they quickly hog-tied it to keep it still.
She learned to hog-tie a calf as part of the ranch work.
The cowboy demonstrated how to hog-tie a calf during the rodeo.
