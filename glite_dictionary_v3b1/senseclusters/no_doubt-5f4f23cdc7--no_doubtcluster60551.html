no doubt

adverb
certainly; admittedly; without question
1.8 29/m
Pronunciation: / noÊŠ ËˆdaÊŠt / (AmE), / nÉ™ÊŠ ËˆdaÊŠt / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something is certainly true or very likely, often to concede a point before adding a contrasting opinion.

Examples

No doubt about it
No doubt, she's a talented musician, but her live performances need improvement.
No doubt, this report will cause some controversy among the experts.
He will, no doubt, want to discuss the matter with you soon.

Synonyms

  • without doubt
    2.3 0.72/m
    without doubt is a formal phrase used to stress something is certain, while no doubt often concedes before adding a contrast.
  • certainly
    1.0 226/m
    certainly expresses firm agreement or clear emphasis in speech, while no doubt usually signals concession before a contrasting opinion.
  • for certain
    1.3 5.00/m
    for certain sounds informal and personal when saying something is definite, while no doubt often serves as a mild concession.
  • beyond a doubt
    3.0 0.34/m
    beyond a doubt is strong and absolute in tone, often used for legal or final claims, while no doubt is a milder concession.
  • beyond doubt
    2.8 0.56/m
    beyond doubt is a formal, sometimes literary phrase for complete certainty, while no doubt is more conversational and weaker.
  • surely
    1.0 66/m
    surely expresses expectation or surprise and can ask for agreement, while no doubt more often concedes then adds contrast.

  • synonym: doubtless - without doubt; certainly
  • antonym: perhaps - possibly but not definitely
