chromatic

adjective
able to refract light without color separation
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / krəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of a lens or optical device) able to refract light without causing separation of colors into a spectrum.

Examples

Chromatic lens
A chromatic lens improves image quality by reducing color fringing in photographs.
Advances in optical engineering have made chromatic corrections more efficient.
The telescope uses a chromatic objective to avoid color distortion.
