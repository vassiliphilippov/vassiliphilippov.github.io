bipropellant

adjective
using two propellants
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪprəˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / ˌbaɪprəˈpɛlənt / (BrE)
using or involving two separate propellants, typically a fuel and an oxidizer, in a propulsion system or engine

Examples

Bipropellant engine
The spacecraft was equipped with a bipropellant engine for efficient maneuvering in space.
Engineers chose a bipropellant system to achieve higher thrust during the rocket launch.
A bipropellant design allows for more precise control of the rocket's speed and direction.
