Pellicularia filamentosa

noun
fungus causing black splotches on potatoes
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɛlɪˈkjʊləriə fɪˌlɑmənˈtoʊsə / (AmE), / pɛlɪˈkjʊləriə fɪˌlæmɛnˈtəʊsə / (BrE)
a fungus that causes a disease in potatoes, characterized by black scurfy spots on the tubers

Examples

Pellicularia filamentosa fungus
Farmers noticed that Pellicularia filamentosa was responsible for the black scurf on their potato crops.
Researchers are studying ways to prevent Pellicularia filamentosa from infecting potato tubers.
The spread of Pellicularia filamentosa can significantly reduce potato yield if not controlled.
