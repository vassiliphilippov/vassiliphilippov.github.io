leno

noun
open-weave technique
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈliːnəʊ / (BrE)
a weaving technique in which adjacent warp yarns are twisted around each other to create stable open spaces in the fabric

Examples

Leno weave technique
The artisan used the leno technique to create a lightweight, breathable fabric for summer scarves.
Because of its strength and openness, leno is often chosen for making curtains and mosquito nets.
Students practiced weaving a leno sample to understand how the twisted warps form stable holes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gauze - a thin, open-weave fabric often made using the leno technique
  • antonym: plain weave - a basic weaving pattern with no twisted warps
