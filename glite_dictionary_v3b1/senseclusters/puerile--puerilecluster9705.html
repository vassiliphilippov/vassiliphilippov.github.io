puerile

adjective
childlike or characteristic of children
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpjʊrəl /; / ˈpjʊr.əl / (AmE), / ˈpjʊə.raɪl / (BrE)
childish, silly, or immature (like a child, especially in a negative way)

Examples

Puerile behavior
He displayed a puerile fascination with the most trivial topics.
Some medical texts describe a normal child's breathing as puerile in character.
The diagnosis was based on the presence of puerile breathing sounds.
Critics dismissed the film as a puerile attempt at comedy, lacking any real depth.
His puerile jokes failed to amuse the serious audience.
The debate quickly descended into puerile name-calling and personal insults.

Related Terms

  • synonym: juvenile - childish or immature; not fully developed in attitude or behavior
  • antonym: mature - having or showing the mental and emotional qualities of an adult
