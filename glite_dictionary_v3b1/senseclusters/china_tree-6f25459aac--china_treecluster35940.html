China tree

noun
evergreen tree with saponin-rich fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪ.nə ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.nə ˌtriː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree of tropical America whose pulpy fruit contains saponin and was used as soap by Native Americans.

Examples

Soap from China tree fruit
A China tree grows in the garden, thriving in the tropical climate.
The Native Americans used the fruit of the China tree to make a natural soap.
The saponin-rich fruit of the China tree has long been valued for its cleansing properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: soapberry - tree with fruit used to make soap; another name for the China tree
