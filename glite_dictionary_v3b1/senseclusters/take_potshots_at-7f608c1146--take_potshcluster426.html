take potshots at

verb
criticize or mock casually
8.2
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈpɑtˌʃɑts æt / (AmE), / teɪk ˈpɒtˌʃɒts æt / (BrE)
to make critical or mocking remarks about someone or something, often casually or unfairly

Examples

To take potshots at critics
Critics regularly take potshots at the film industry for lack of originality.
He didn't offer solutions; he just took potshots at everyone else's ideas.
It's easy to take potshots at politicians, but understanding their challenges is harder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something
