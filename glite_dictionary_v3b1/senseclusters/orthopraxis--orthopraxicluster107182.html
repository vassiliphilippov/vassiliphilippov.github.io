orthopraxis

noun
priority of correct practice
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθəˈpræksɪs /; / ˌɔrθoʊˈpræksɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈpræksɪs /; / ˌɔːθəʊˈpræksɪs / (BrE)
the belief or practice that correct actions, rituals, or practices are more important than correct beliefs, especially in religion or medicine

Examples

Orthopraxis in religion
Some religious traditions value orthopraxis more than strict adherence to doctrine.
The community's focus on orthopraxis is seen in their daily rituals and charitable acts.
Debates about orthopraxis versus orthodoxy have shaped the group's identity for decades.
The community valued orthopraxis and expected everyone to follow traditional rituals.
In some faiths, orthopraxis is considered as important as holding the right beliefs.
Medical professionals are trained in orthopraxis to ensure patient safety and effective care.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heteropraxis - incorrect or nonstandard practice, especially in religion
  • antonym: orthodoxy - the doctrine that emphasizes correct belief or doctrine
