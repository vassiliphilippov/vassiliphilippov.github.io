reticent

adjective
not talkative; reserved
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtɪsənt /; / ˈrɛt̬.ə.sənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪsənt /; / ˈrɛt.ɪ.sənt / (BrE)
not willing to share thoughts or feelings easily; reserved or inclined to keep silent

Examples

A reticent person
Although he was usually reticent, he surprised everyone by delivering a heartfelt speech.
She remained reticent about her plans for the future.
The reticent witness answered only after being asked direct questions.
Despite the prying questions, he remained reticent about his personal life.
She gave a reticent smile and avoided eye contact during the meeting.
Their new manager is reticent with praise, preferring to give feedback privately.
Although he was very talented, Jack remained reticent about his achievements.
She is naturally reticent, preferring to listen rather than speak in meetings.
The manager seemed reticent when asked about the company's future plans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forthcoming - willing to talk or share information
  • synonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotions or opinions
  • synonym: taciturn - habitually silent or uncommunicative
  • antonym: talkative - inclined to talk a lot; chatty
