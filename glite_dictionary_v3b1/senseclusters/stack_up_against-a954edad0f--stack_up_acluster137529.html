stack up against

verb
accumulate so as to disadvantage
Pronunciation: / stæk ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / stak ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
(of evidence, arguments, or factors) to accumulate so as to disadvantage someone or something, or create an unfair situation.

Examples

Problems stack up against you
Negative reviews began to stack up against the new product, hurting its sales.
Several obstacles stacked up against us, making the project much harder to complete.
The evidence continued to stack up against the defendant throughout the trial.
