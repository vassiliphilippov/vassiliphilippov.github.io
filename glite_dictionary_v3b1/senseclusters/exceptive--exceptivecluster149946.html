exceptive

adjective
involving an exception
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
making or involving an exception; allowing something to be excluded from a general rule or statement

Examples

Exceptive clause
The contract contains an exceptive provision that allows certain employees to work remotely.
An exceptive rule was added to address unique cases not covered by the main policy.
The lawyer pointed out the exceptive language in the agreement that could affect the outcome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclusive - not including something; leaving out
  • antonym: inclusive - including everything or everyone
