barracouta

noun
a large southern marine food fish
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌbær.əˈkuː.tə / (AmE), / ˌbær.əˈkuː.tə / (BrE)
a large predatory marine fish of southern waters, especially valued as a food fish in Australia, New Zealand, and southern Africa

Examples

Grilled barracouta fish
A grilled barracouta is a popular dish at seaside restaurants in southern Australia.
Fishermen in New Zealand regularly catch barracouta for both local consumption and export.
The waters off Cape Town are known for their abundant barracouta population.
