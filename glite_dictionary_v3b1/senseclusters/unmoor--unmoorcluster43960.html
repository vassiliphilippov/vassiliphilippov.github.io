unmoor

verb
release a boat or ship from moorings
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmɔɹ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɔː / (BrE)
to release (a boat or ship) from its moorings so that it can move freely

Examples

To unmoor the boat
If you unmoor the boat now, we can catch the evening tide.
They decided to unmoor the boat at dawn and set sail for the island.
When the weather cleared, the crew quickly unmoored the ship and headed home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: moor - to attach a boat or ship by a cable or rope to a fixed object
