Boxer

noun
member of a Chinese secret society (Boxer Rebellion)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑksər / (AmE), / ˈbɒksə / (BrE)
a member of a Chinese secret society that led the unsuccessful Boxer Rebellion against foreign influence in China in 1900

Examples

The Boxers rebellion
Many foreign diplomats were trapped in Beijing by the Boxers during the 1900 uprising.
The Boxer believed they could become invulnerable to bullets through martial arts rituals.
The Boxer movement was fueled by anti-foreign and anti-Christian sentiments in late 19th-century China.
