divan

noun
Muslim council of state
6.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvæn / (AmE), / dɪˈvɑːn / (BrE)
a council or its meeting place (such as a council chamber or law court) in some Muslim countries, especially in historical contexts

Examples

Ottoman divan chamber
Members of the divan held considerable power in the Ottoman administration.
Reforms were debated extensively within the divan before implementation.
The sultan convened the divan to discuss matters of governance.
Historical records describe the legal decisions made in the divan.
The divan served as the heart of political and judicial activity in the Ottoman Empire.
The sultan addressed his advisers in the divan early each morning.
