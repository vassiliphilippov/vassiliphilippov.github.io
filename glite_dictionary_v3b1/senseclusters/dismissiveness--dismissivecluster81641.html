dismissiveness

noun
showing disregard
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or attitude of showing that something or someone is unimportant or not worth considering

Examples

Dismissiveness of opinions
Her dismissiveness made it clear she did not value their opinions.
The manager's dismissiveness toward employee feedback led to low morale.
I was hurt by his dismissiveness when I tried to share my ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disdain - a feeling that someone or something is unworthy of respect
  • antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
