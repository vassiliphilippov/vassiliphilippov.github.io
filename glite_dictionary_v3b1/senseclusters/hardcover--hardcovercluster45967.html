hardcover

noun
book with rigid covers
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdˌkʌvər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌkʌvə / (BrE)
A book bound with rigid protective covers, typically made of cardboard, cloth, or leather.

Examples

A hardcover book
She prefers reading hardcover books because they are more durable and look elegant on a shelf.
The library has a pristine hardcover copy of the novel on display.
The new release is available in both hardcover and paperback formats.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paperback - a book with a flexible paper cover
