themself

pronoun
reflexive singular 'they'
Pronunciation: / ðəmˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ðəmˈsɛlf / (BrE)
the reflexive form of 'they' used to refer to a single person when gender is unspecified or nonbinary

Examples

Themself introduced
Each participant should introduce themself at the start of the meeting.
If someone feels unwell, they should take care of themself first.
A student may excuse themself from class if they are not feeling well.

Related Terms

  • antonym: herself - reflexive form of 'she'
  • antonym: himself - reflexive form of 'he'
  • synonym: themselves - reflexive form of 'they' for plural or sometimes singular
