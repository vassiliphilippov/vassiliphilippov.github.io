blue wall

noun
police code of silence
Pronunciation: / blu wɑl / (AmE), / bluː wɔːl / (BrE)
an unofficial code among police officers not to report or testify against a colleague’s wrongdoing, maintaining secrecy and solidarity within the force

Examples

Police blue wall
After the scandal, the chief promised to break down the blue wall protecting corrupt officers.
It took a whistleblower to penetrate the blue wall and expose the cover-up.
Many critics argue that the blue wall prevents accountability within police departments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: code of silence - an unspoken agreement to withhold information for group protection
