metacyclic

adjective
parasite life stage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
relating to a particular stage in the life cycle of certain parasites, often the infectious form for hosts

Examples

Metacyclic parasite stage
The metacyclic stage of the parasite is responsible for transmitting the disease to humans.
Researchers studied how the metacyclic form adapts to survive in the host's body.
Only the metacyclic trypanosomes are capable of infecting new hosts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infective - able to cause infection
  • antonym: noninfective - not able to cause infection
