mandibular bone

noun
lower jawbone in vertebrates
7.9 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmæn.dɪ.bjə.lər boʊn/ /; / /ˈmæn.dɪ.bjʊ.lər boʊn/ (AmE), /ˈmæn.dɪ.bjʊ.lə bəʊn/ /; / /ˈmæn.dɪ.bjə.lə bəʊn/ (BrE)
the bone forming the lower jaw in vertebrates, hinged to allow the mouth to open and close

Examples

The mandibular bone
Certain mammals have a uniquely shaped mandibular bone to help them break down tough food.
During the examination, the dentist found a small fracture in the mandibular bone.
In the fossil record, the mandibular bone often provides clues about an animal's diet and lifestyle.
