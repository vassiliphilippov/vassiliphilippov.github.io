draconian

adjective
excessively harsh or severe measures
Pronunciation: / drəˈkoʊniən / (AmE), / drəˈkəʊniən / (BrE)
(of laws or measures) excessively harsh or severe.

Examples

A draconian law
Critics argue that the new tax regulations are overly draconian.
Some people consider curfews for teenagers to be draconian measures.
The government imposed draconian laws to suppress the uprising.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lenient - (of punishment or a person in authority) tolerant; not strict
