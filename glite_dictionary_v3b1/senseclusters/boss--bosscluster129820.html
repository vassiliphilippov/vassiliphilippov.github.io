boss

verb
to command others
3.1
Pronunciation: / bɑs / (AmE), / bɒs / (BrE)
to give orders to or control others in a commanding way

Examples

To boss people around
He always tries to boss his classmates around during group projects.
Nobody likes it when she bosses everyone without listening to their ideas.
As a child, I hated being bossed by my older siblings.
She tends to boss her colleagues around during group projects.
Nobody likes it when someone tries to boss everyone else in the team.
As the oldest sibling, he would often boss the others without meaning to.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command - to direct with authority
  • synonym: control - to exercise power over others
  • antonym: obey - to follow orders or instructions
  • synonym: order - to tell someone what to do
