greenbrier

noun
woody prickly vine of eastern US
6.8 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈgriːn.braɪ.ər/ /; / /ˈgriːn.braɪr/ (AmE), /ˈgriːn.braɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A woody climbing plant of the eastern United States with prickly stems, shiny leathery leaves, small greenish flowers, and clusters of inedible black berries.

Examples

Greenbrier climbing plant
The sharp thorns of greenbrier make it difficult for animals and people to pass through.

You can find greenbrier growing along the forest edge, often forming thick, impenetrable tangles.
