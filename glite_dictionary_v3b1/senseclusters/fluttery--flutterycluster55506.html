fluttery

adjective
light and nervous
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌtəri / (AmE), / ˈflʌtəri / (BrE)
feeling or sounding nervous, excited, or shaky in a light and quick way

Examples

Fluttery feeling
She felt fluttery with excitement before her performance.
His heart was fluttery as he waited for the results.
The curtains gave a fluttery movement in the gentle breeze.
Her voice sounded fluttery as she tried to explain what happened.
He felt a fluttery sensation in his stomach before the big presentation.
The leaves made a fluttery movement in the gentle breeze.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing nervousness or excitement
  • synonym: nervous - feeling worried or anxious
  • antonym: steady - firm and not shaking or moving
  • synonym: tremulous - shaking or quivering slightly
