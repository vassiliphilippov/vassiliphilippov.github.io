preteach

verb
to teach in advance
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈtitʃ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈtiːtʃ / (BrE)
to teach information or skills to students before they are needed in a lesson or activity

Examples

To preteach vocabulary
Teachers often preteach key vocabulary before starting a new topic.
It is helpful to preteach difficult concepts to students who need extra support.
The instructor decided to preteach the main ideas to ensure everyone understood the lesson.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preview - to introduce or show something before it is used or discussed
  • antonym: reteach - to teach something again
