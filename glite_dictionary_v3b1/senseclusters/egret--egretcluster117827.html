egret

noun
a type of white heron with long plumes
Pronunciation: / ˈiːɡrət / (AmE), / ˈiːɡrɪt / (BrE)
a long-legged, usually white heron with long, delicate plumes that are especially prominent during the breeding season

Examples

A white egret
A graceful egret stood motionless at the edge of the pond, waiting for a fish to swim by.
During spring, the egrets display their elegant white plumes in the wetlands.
The nature photographer captured a close-up of the egret in flight against a clear blue sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heron - a large, long-legged wading bird related to egrets
