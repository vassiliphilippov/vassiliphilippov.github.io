Krebs cycle

noun

a sequence of chemical reactions in living cells, particularly in mitochondria, through which the oxidation of acetyl compounds produces energy, carbon dioxide, and water; also called the citric acid cycle

Examples

The Krebs cycle process
Biology students must learn each step of the Krebs cycle to understand cellular respiration.

Synonyms

  • citric acid cycle
    7.3 0.02/m
    citric acid cycle is the standard scientific name for the energy-making reactions in mitochondria, while Krebs cycle is the common shorter name.
  • Krebs citric acid cycle
    6.9 0.00/m
    Krebs citric acid cycle is a combined formal name found in textbooks, while Krebs cycle is the shorter common name used in speech.
  • tricarboxylic acid cycle
    5.2 0.00/m
    tricarboxylic acid cycle is a technical formal term used in scientific papers, while Krebs cycle is the everyday name teachers and students use.
