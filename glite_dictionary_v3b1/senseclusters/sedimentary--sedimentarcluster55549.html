sedimentary

adjective
relating to or formed by sediment
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛdəˈmɛntəri /; / ˌsɛdəˈmɛn.t̬ə.ɹi / (AmE), / ˌsɛdɪˈmɛntri /; / ˌsɛd.ɪˈmen.tə.ri / (BrE)
formed from layers of sediment (such as sand, mud, or small pieces of rock) that have been deposited and solidified, especially referring to rocks or geological formations

Examples

Sedimentary rock layers
Most oil reservoirs are found within sedimentary basins beneath the surface.
Scientists studied the sedimentary layers to understand past climates and environmental changes.
The region is famous for its sedimentary rock formations, which reveal millions of years of Earth's history.
Many fossils are found in sedimentary layers, which were formed by ancient seas.
The sedimentary deposits indicated that the area was once underwater.
The scientist collected samples of sedimentary rock from the riverbank for analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: igneous - relating to rocks formed by solidification from molten magma
  • antonym: metamorphic - relating to rocks that have been transformed by heat and pressure
