trickle

verb
flow slowly in drops or a thin stream
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkl / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to flow slowly and irregularly, often in drops or a thin stream.

Examples

To trickle down
A small stream of water began to trickle down the windowpane.
Donations continued to trickle in after the charity event ended.
Tears trickled slowly down her cheeks as she listened to the story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dribble - flow in drops or a thin, unsteady stream
  • degree higher: stream - flow continuously in a steady flow
