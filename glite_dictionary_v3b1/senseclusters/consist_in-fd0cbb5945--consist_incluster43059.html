consist in

verb
to have its essence in
3.6
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɪst ɪn / (AmE), / kənˈsɪst ɪn / (BrE)
to have something as its essential quality, nature, or element; to be embodied in.

Examples

Happiness consists in love
For many people, happiness consists in spending time with loved ones.
True friendship consists in supporting each other through difficult times.
Wisdom often consists in knowing the limits of one's knowledge.
