parakeratosis

noun
skin disease in animals
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌparəkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease in farm animals, especially pigs, caused by zinc deficiency and marked by thick, scaly, inflamed skin

Examples

Pig parakeratosis
The veterinarian diagnosed the pig with parakeratosis after noticing its rough, scaly skin.
Proper nutrition can help prevent parakeratosis in young farm animals.
Farmers learned to recognize the signs of parakeratosis to treat their livestock early.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dermatitis - inflammation of the skin
