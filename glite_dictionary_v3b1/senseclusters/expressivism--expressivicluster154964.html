expressivism

noun
moral attitude theory
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsprɛsɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛsɪˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that moral statements express attitudes or emotions rather than stating objective facts

Examples

Moral expressivism
According to expressivism, saying 'Lying is bad' shows disapproval rather than stating a fact.
Many philosophers debate whether expressivism can fully explain the meaning of moral language.
Some critics argue that expressivism fails to account for the objectivity of moral claims.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emotivism - the view that moral statements express emotional attitudes
  • antonym: moral realism - the belief that moral statements describe objective facts
