consumer price index

noun
measure of average consumer prices
Pronunciation: / kənˈsuːmɚ praɪs ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːmə praɪs ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
an official measure of the average change over time in the prices paid by consumers for goods and services.

Examples

The consumer price index rises
Economists watch changes in the consumer price index to track inflation.
Many wage contracts are adjusted according to the consumer price index.
The government reported that the consumer price index rose by 2% last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflation - reduction of the general level of prices in an economy
  • synonym: inflation - general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money
