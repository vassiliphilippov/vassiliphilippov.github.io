northern phalarope

noun
Arctic-breeding wading bird species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrðərn fəˈlɛroʊp / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən fəˈlærəʊp / (BrE)
a small wading bird (Phalaropus lobatus) that breeds in Arctic regions and is often seen in large flocks at sea

Examples

A northern phalarope flock
A flock of northern phalaropes was spotted feeding just offshore during the migration season.
Many birdwatchers travel to Arctic regions hoping to catch a glimpse of the northern phalarope in its breeding habitat.
The northern phalarope is known for its unique spinning behavior as it forages.
