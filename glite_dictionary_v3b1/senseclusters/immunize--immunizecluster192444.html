immunize

verb
grant legal immunity from prosecution
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪmjʊnaɪz / (BrE)
(in law) to officially grant legal immunity from prosecution to a person, typically in exchange for testimony or cooperation.

Examples

To immunize a witness
Authorities may immunize minor offenders to build a case against higher-level suspects.
The attorney negotiated to have his client immunized before giving any statements.
The witness was immunized by the prosecution in exchange for his testimony.
Authorities agreed to immunise her so she could provide crucial information in court.
He refused to immunise the defendant, fearing it might undermine the investigation.
The witness was immunised in return for testifying against the main suspect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prosecute - to institute legal proceedings against someone
