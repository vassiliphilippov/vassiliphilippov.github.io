prima facie

adjective, adverb
at first sight; based on initial impression
5.2 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpriː.mə ˈfeɪ.siː/ /; / /ˈpraɪ.mə ˈfeɪ.ʃiː/ (AmE), /ˈpriː.mə ˈfeɪ.ʃiː/ /; / /ˈpraɪ.mə ˈfeɪ.ʃiː/ (BrE)
based on first impression or at first sight, before further examination (used as an adjective or adverb)

Examples

Prima facie evidence
It was prima facie obvious that the company had breached the contract.
The evidence appeared prima facie convincing, though further investigation was needed.
This claim may seem true prima facie, but closer analysis reveals flaws.
Her explanation seems plausible, but it’s only prima facie and may not hold up under scrutiny.
The evidence presented in court established a prima facie case against the accused.
There was a prima facie breach of contract that needed further investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apparent - seeming real or true, but not necessarily so
  • synonym: at first glance - at the first quick look
  • synonym: on the face of it - as it appears initially
  • synonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface
