dysvascular

adjective
poor blood supply
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈvæskjələr / (AmE), / dɪsˈvæskjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or caused by poor or abnormal blood supply to tissues

Examples

dysvascular tissue
The surgeon noted that the tissue was dysvascular and would heal slowly.
Patients with dysvascular limbs are at higher risk for developing ulcers.
A dysvascular condition can lead to serious complications if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ischemic - relating to a restriction in blood supply
  • antonym: vascular - relating to blood vessels or good blood supply
