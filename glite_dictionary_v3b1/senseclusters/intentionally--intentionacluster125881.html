Deliberately; in a way that is done on purpose rather than by accident.

Examples

Done intentionally
He broke the vase intentionally to make a point during the discussion.
If you intentionally avoid the meeting, your absence will be noticed.
She intentionally left her phone at home to enjoy a quiet evening.

Synonyms

  • on purpose
    1.6 6/m
    on purpose is used in everyday speech to show something is done deliberately, while intentionally is neutral and formal.
  • deliberately
    3.7 20/m
    deliberately often suggests careful thought or slow action, while intentionally simply means done on purpose.
  • consciously
    3.7 9/m
    consciously means acting with awareness and thought in the moment, while intentionally means acting on purpose even if not obvious.
  • purposely
    3.5 4/m
    purposely is commonly used to mean done on purpose in speech, while intentionally is slightly more formal.
  • purposefully
    3.9 3/m
    purposefully shows clear aim or determination, while intentionally focuses on being done on purpose without that drive.
  • by design
    4.0 2/m
    by design implies a planned outcome or pattern, while intentionally simply means done on purpose.
  • by choice
    3.1 2/m
    by choice stresses a personal decision or lack of pressure, while intentionally means done on purpose regardless of choice.
  • knowingly
    3.9 5/m
    knowingly implies awareness often of facts or consequences, while intentionally focuses simply on doing something on purpose.
  • wittingly
    7.5 0.2/m
    wittingly is a rare, slightly formal word for acting with knowledge, while intentionally is common and widely used.
