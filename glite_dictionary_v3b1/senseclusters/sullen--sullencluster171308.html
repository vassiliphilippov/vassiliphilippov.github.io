sullen

adjective
(of sky) dark, gloomy, overcast
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌl.ən / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.ən / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) dark and gloomy because of clouds; giving the impression of sadness or brooding.

Examples

Sullen sky
A sullen sky hung above the park, making the afternoon feel much colder and darker.
The sullen clouds loomed over the city, threatening to release a heavy rain at any moment.
The fisherman glanced warily at the sullen horizon, knowing a storm was on its way.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - having lots of light, not dark
  • synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially as to appear depressing or frightening
