riff

noun
repeated musical phrase in jazz
6.8
Pronunciation: / rɪf / (AmE), / rɪf / (BrE)
a short, repeated musical phrase, especially in jazz, that is typically used as a background for a solo improvisation

Examples

A catchy riff
Classic jazz often features an energetic riff repeated beneath the improvisation.
During the saxophone solo, the band kept a steady riff going in the background.
The guitarist played a catchy riff that set the tone for the entire song.
