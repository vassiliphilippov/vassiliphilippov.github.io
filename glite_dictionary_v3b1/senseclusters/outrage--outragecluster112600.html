outrage

verb
cause intense anger or disgust
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.reɪdʒ / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel intense anger, disgust, or revulsion

Examples

To outrage the crowd
His insensitive remarks outraged everyone at the meeting.
Many people were outraged by the graphic scenes shown in the documentary.
The government's decision to cut funding outraged local communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shock - to cause someone to feel surprised or upset
