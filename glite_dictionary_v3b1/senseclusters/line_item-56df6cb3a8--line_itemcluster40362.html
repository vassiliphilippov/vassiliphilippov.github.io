line item

noun
individual entry in a budget or bill
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪn ˌaɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn ˌaɪtəm / (BrE)
an individual entry listed separately in a budget, bill, or other financial statement, often specifying an allocated amount or description.

Examples

A line item budget
The committee debated whether to approve each line item in the proposed spending plan.
The president used his veto power to reject a controversial line item in the appropriations bill.
We need to review the line items before finalizing the budget for next year.
