duodenum

noun
first part of the small intestine
7.0
Pronunciation: / duːˈɑdənəm, ˌduəˈdinəm / (AmE), / ˌdjuːəˈdiːnəm, duːˈɒdɪnəm / (BrE)
the first part of the small intestine immediately beyond the stomach, leading to the jejunum

Examples

The duodenum section
After food leaves the stomach, it enters the duodenum for further digestion.
Bile and pancreatic juices mix with the chyme in the duodenum.
The ulcer was found in the duodenum during the endoscopy.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: jejunum - the section of the small intestine between the duodenum and the ileum
  • degree lower: stomach - the organ in the body where food begins to be digested
