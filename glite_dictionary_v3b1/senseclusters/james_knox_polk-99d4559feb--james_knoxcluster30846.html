James Knox Polk

proper noun
11th U.S. President (1795–1849), expansionist
5.8
Pronunciation: / dʒeɪmz nɑks poʊk / (AmE), / dʒeɪmz nɒks pəʊk / (BrE)
(1795–1849) the 11th President of the United States, noted for his expansionist policies that led to the Mexican War and the annexation of large parts of the American southwest.

Examples

James Knox Polk presidency
James Knox Polk served as President of the United States from 1845 to 1849.
Many historians credit James Knox Polk with significantly expanding U.S. territory.
The policies of James Knox Polk led to the annexation of California and much of the southwest.
