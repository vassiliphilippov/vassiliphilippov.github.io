pepper root

noun
North American herb with pungent roots
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛpər ruːt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə ruːt / (BrE)
A North American herb (Cardamine diphylla or related species) known for its pungent, scaly or toothed roots.

Examples

The pepper root plant
Botanists identified the plant as pepper root due to the flavor of its roots.
Native Americans traditionally used pepper root for both food and medicine.
The pungency of pepper root makes it a distinctive addition to wild salads.
