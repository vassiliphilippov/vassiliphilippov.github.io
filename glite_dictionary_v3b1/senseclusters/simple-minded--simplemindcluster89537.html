simple-minded

adjective
lacking subtlety or intelligence
6.9 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəlˌmaɪndɪd /; / ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəlˌmaɪndɪd /; / ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
lacking intelligence, subtlety, or sophistication; naive or unsophisticated

Examples

A simple-minded person
His simple-minded approach to a complex issue missed many important details.
She dismissed the theory as a simple-minded explanation for a complicated situation.
The film's plot was criticized for being too simple-minded and predictable.
Despite his age, the boy remained simple-minded and believed every story he was told.
She spoke in a simple-minded way, ignoring the complexities of the situation.
The plan was dismissed as simple-minded and failed to address the real issues.

  • synonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something; credulous
  • synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgement
  • antonym: sophisticated - having or showing a lot of experience and knowledge about the world
