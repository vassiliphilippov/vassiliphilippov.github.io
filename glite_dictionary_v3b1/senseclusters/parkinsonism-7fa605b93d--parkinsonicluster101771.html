Parkinsonism

noun
neurological disorder with tremors and rigidity
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑrkɪnˈsoʊnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɑːkɪnˈsəʊnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A degenerative disorder of the central nervous system characterized by tremor, muscular rigidity, and impaired coordination, often resembling or related to Parkinson's disease.

Examples

Parkinsonism symptoms
Certain medications can cause drug-induced Parkinsonism with symptoms similar to Parkinson's disease.
Physical therapy is often recommended to help manage the symptoms of Parkinsonism.
The patient was diagnosed with Parkinsonism after experiencing persistent tremors and muscle stiffness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Parkinson's disease - A specific degenerative neurological disorder causing motor symptoms such as tremor and rigidity
