crystallographer

noun
scientist who studies crystals
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪstəˈlɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəˈlɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
A scientist who studies the structure and properties of crystals, typically using techniques such as X-ray diffraction.

Examples

A crystallographer studies crystals
As a crystallographer, she spent years analyzing the atomic structure of minerals.
Every research team needed at least one experienced crystallographer to interpret their materials data.
The Nobel Prize was awarded to a crystallographer who revealed the structure of complex proteins.
