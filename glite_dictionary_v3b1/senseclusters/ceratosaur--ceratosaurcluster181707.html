ceratosaur

noun
swift-running Jurassic theropod dinosaur
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈse.rə.toʊ.sɔːr/ /; / /ˈse.rə.tə.sɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈse.rə.tə.sɔː/ /; / /ˈse.rə.təʊ.sɔː/ (BrE)
a primitive, medium-sized theropod dinosaur of the Jurassic period, characterized by swift running, grasping hands with sharp claws, and a short horn between the nostrils

Examples

The ceratosaur dinosaur
Fossils of the ceratosaur have been found in North America, revealing much about its evolution.
Paleontologists believe the ceratosaur was an agile and efficient carnivore of its time.
The ceratosaur was notable for the horn on its snout, a distinctive feature among Jurassic predators.
