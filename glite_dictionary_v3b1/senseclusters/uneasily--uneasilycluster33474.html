uneasily

adverb
with worry or discomfort
4.2 3.13/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàiÀêz…ôli / (AmE), /  ånÀàiÀêz…ôli / (BrE)
In a way that shows worry, discomfort, or anxiety.

Examples

To move uneasily
He shifted uneasily in his seat during the tense discussion.
She glanced uneasily around the crowded room, searching for a familiar face.
The students waited uneasily for the results to be announced.

Synonyms

  • anxiously
    3.2 4.65/m
    anxiously shows strong worry and nervousness while uneasily describes general worry or mild discomfort in many situations.
  • worriedly
    2.0 0.53/m
    worriedly shows clear, simple worry in a person's tone while uneasily covers worry or also physical discomfort in many cases.
  • nervously
    2.3 10/m
    nervously shows jittery movement or trembling while uneasily can mean general worry, discomfort, or mild anxiety.
  • apprehensively
    6.0 0.31/m
    apprehensively suggests fear that something bad will happen while uneasily covers worry or discomfort without strong fear.
  • edgily
    6.0 0.05/m
    edgily feels tense and easily irritated while uneasily shows worry or discomfort without the angry edge.
  • tensely
    3.9 0.56/m
    tensely shows muscle tightness and mental strain while uneasily focuses more on worry or a general feeling of unease.
  • agitatedly
    5.7 0.07/m
    agitatedly sounds or moves in a restless, excited way while uneasily is calmer, showing worry or simple physical discomfort.
  • uncomfortably
    3.4 3.57/m
    uncomfortably causes physical pain or an awkward position while uneasily mainly shows worry, anxious feelings, or mental discomfort.
