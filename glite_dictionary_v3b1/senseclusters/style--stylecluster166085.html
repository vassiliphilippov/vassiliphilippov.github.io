style

noun
slender part of a flower’s pistil
5.1 8.0
Pronunciation: / staɪl / (AmE), / staɪl / (BrE)
(biology) a slender stalk or structure in a plant or animal, such as the part of a flower connecting the ovary and stigma, or a bristlelike appendage in animals

Examples

Flower style
A cut or damaged style can prevent the fertilization of the flower.
In many lilies, the style is long and clearly visible above the petals.
The pollen travels down the style to reach the ovary in the process of fertilization.
Some marine worms possess a cartilaginous style that aids in their movement.
The flower's style serves as a passage for pollen to reach the ovary.
Under the microscope, the style appeared as a long, thin structure attached to the ovary.
