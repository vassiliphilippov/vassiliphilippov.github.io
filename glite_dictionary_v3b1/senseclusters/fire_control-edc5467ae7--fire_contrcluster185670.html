fire control

noun
process of directing artillery or gunfire
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the process of preparing and directing the delivery of artillery or gunfire onto a target, typically in military operations

Examples

Advanced fire control system
Modern tanks are equipped with advanced fire control systems for precise targeting.
The fire control officer calculated the data needed to hit the approaching enemy vehicles.
Training included several exercises focusing on fire control and coordination between units.
