Laver

proper noun
Australian tennis player, Grand Slam winner
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈleɪvə / (BrE)
Rod Laver (born 1938), Australian tennis player who was the second man to win all four Grand Slam singles titles in one year (1962) and achieved the feat again in 1969.

Examples

Laver tennis player
Laver is widely considered one of the greatest tennis players of all time.
Rod Laver won the Grand Slam twice, making history in 1962 and 1969.
The annual Laver Cup is named after Laver in honor of his achievements.
