factionalist

adjective
supporting a faction
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfækʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfækʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
showing strong support for a particular faction, often promoting division within a group or organization

Examples

Factionalist politics
The leader's factionalist approach caused further splits within the organization.
Critics accused the committee of making factionalist decisions that ignored the needs of the whole group.
A factionalist attitude can prevent cooperation and progress in political parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: partisan - strongly supporting a particular group or cause
  • antonym: unifying - bringing people together or making them united
