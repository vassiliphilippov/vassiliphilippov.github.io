neologistic

adjective
using new words
Pronunciation: / niˌɑːləˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / niˌɒləˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
containing or using newly created words, especially as seen in certain speech or thought disorders.

Examples

Neologistic speech
The patient's neologistic language made it difficult for the doctor to understand his thoughts.
Some forms of schizophrenia are characterized by neologistic speech patterns.
Her writing style was so neologistic that readers struggled to grasp her meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - following traditional or commonly accepted forms
  • synonym: neologism - a newly coined word or expression
