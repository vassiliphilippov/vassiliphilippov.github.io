reasonably

adverb
in a sensible or rational way
Pronunciation: / ˈriːzənəbli / (AmE), / ˈriːzənəbli / (BrE)
in a sensible or rational manner; with good judgment or intelligence

Examples

Reasonably handle a problem
He handled the emergency reasonably and kept everyone calm.
If you explain your concerns reasonably, people are more likely to listen.
The committee members discussed the proposal reasonably before reaching a decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrationally - in an unreasonable or illogical manner
  • synonym: sensibly - in a way that shows good sense or judgment
