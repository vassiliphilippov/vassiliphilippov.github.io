astragalus

noun
ankle bone connecting foot and leg
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈstræɡjələs / (AmE), / əˈstræɡjələs / (BrE)
the bone in the ankle that connects the foot to the leg by articulating with the tibia and fibula to form the ankle joint

Examples

The astragalus bone
A fracture of the astragalus can severely limit mobility in the affected foot.
Because of its crucial role in ankle movement, injury to the astragalus requires careful treatment.
The surgeon examined the patient's astragalus after the accident to assess the damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: talus - the ankle bone; another common name for the astragalus
