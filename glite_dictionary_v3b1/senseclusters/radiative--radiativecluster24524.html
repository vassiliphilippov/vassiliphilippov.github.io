radiative

adjective
emitting energy
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪətɪv /; / ˈreɪdiətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the emission or transfer of energy as radiation (such as light, heat, or electromagnetic waves)

Examples

Scientists study radiative transfer to understand how energy moves through the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorptive - able to take in energy, especially heat or light
  • antonym: conductive - relating to the transfer of energy by conduction
  • antonym: convective - relating to the transfer of energy by convection
  • antonym: nonradiative - not involving the emission of radiation
  • synonym: radiant - emitting light or heat
  • synonym: radioactive - emitting radiation due to unstable atomic nuclei
