chili powder

noun
spiced ground chili pepper powder
3.2 1.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃɪ.liː ˈpaʊ.dər/ (AmE), /ˈtʃɪ.liː ˈpaʊ.də/ (BrE)
A powder made from ground chili peppers, often mixed with spices such as cumin, garlic, and oregano, used to add flavor and heat to dishes.

Examples

Chili powder spice
Mexican recipes often call for chili powder as a key ingredient.
She added a spoonful of chili powder to the stew for extra heat.
The blend of chili powder and cumin gave the dish a rich flavor.
