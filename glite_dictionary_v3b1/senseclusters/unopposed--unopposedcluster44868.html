unopposed

adjective
having no opposition
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
Not opposed; having no opposition or not opposed by anyone.

Examples

Unopposed election
The bill passed through parliament unopposed after a unanimous vote.
The mayor was elected unopposed after all other candidates withdrew.
With no one to challenge them, the team advanced unopposed to the finals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opposed - being in conflict or having opposition
