Jataka

noun
Buddha's past life story
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɑːtəkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːtəkə / (BrE)
a story about one of the previous lives of the Buddha, often used to teach moral lessons in Buddhist tradition

Examples

A Jataka story
The teacher shared a Jataka to help the students understand the value of kindness.
Many Buddhist temples display paintings that depict scenes from the Jataka tales.
He read a Jataka story to his children before bedtime, teaching them about generosity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fable - a short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral
  • synonym: parable - a simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson
