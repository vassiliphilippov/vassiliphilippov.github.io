shoestring

noun
a very small amount of money
8.7 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈʃuː.strɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʃuː.strɪŋ/ (BrE)
a very small amount of money, especially just enough for basic needs or to do something cheaply (often used in the phrase 'on a shoestring')

Examples

On a shoestring budget
After losing his job, he was forced to live on a shoestring for several months.
The charity operates on a shoestring but manages to help hundreds of people each year.
They started their business with nothing but a shoestring and a big dream.
He produced the film on a shoestring, using borrowed equipment and volunteer actors.
They started their business on a shoestring and gradually built it up.
With only a shoestring to spend, their vacation was very basic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fortune - a very large amount of money
  • synonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount of money paid to someone as an allowance or wage
