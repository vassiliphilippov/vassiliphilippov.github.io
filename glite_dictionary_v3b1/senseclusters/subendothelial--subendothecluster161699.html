subendothelial

adjective
beneath endothelium
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˌɛndoʊˈθiːliəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɛndəʊˈθiːliəl / (BrE)
located or occurring beneath the layer of endothelial cells lining blood vessels

Examples

Subendothelial space
Cholesterol can accumulate in the subendothelial space of arteries.
The subendothelial layer plays a key role in the development of atherosclerosis.
Researchers observed inflammation in the subendothelial region of the blood vessel wall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endothelial - relating to the thin layer of cells lining blood vessels
