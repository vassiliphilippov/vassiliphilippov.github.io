decompression

noun
relieving or reducing pressure (medical/technical)
5.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /diː.kəm.ˈpreʃ.ən/ /; / /dɪ.kəm.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (AmE), /diː.kəm.ˈpreʃ.ən/ /; / /dɪ.kəm.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the process of relieving or reducing pressure, especially by returning a compressed person or object gradually to normal atmospheric pressure

Examples

Medical decompression
A controlled decompression is crucial when treating patients with severe altitude sickness.
Divers must ascend slowly to avoid decompression sickness caused by a sudden reduction in pressure.
The crew underwent decompression after spending hours in the hyperbaric chamber.
