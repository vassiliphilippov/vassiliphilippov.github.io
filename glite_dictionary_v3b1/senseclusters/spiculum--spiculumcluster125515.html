spiculum

noun
small pointed structure in some invertebrates
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪkjələm / (AmE), / ˈspɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
A small, slender, pointed structure, often serving as a skeletal element in certain marine and freshwater invertebrates, such as sponges and corals.

Examples

A spiculum in coral
Researchers identified the coral species by examining the shape of the spiculum.
The rigidity of the skeleton is provided by each spiculum embedded in the tissue.
Under the microscope, the spiculum of the sponge was clearly visible.
