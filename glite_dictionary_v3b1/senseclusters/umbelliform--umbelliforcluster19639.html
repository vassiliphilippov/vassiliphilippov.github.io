umbelliform

adjective
shaped like an umbel (flower cluster)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: /  åmÀàb…õl.…™.f…îrm / (AmE), /  åmÀàb…õl.…™.f…îÀêm / (BrE)
Having the shape or form of an umbel; resembling an umbel, especially as seen in certain flowers or inflorescences.

Examples

Umbelliform flower shape
Several members of the carrot family display umbelliform inflorescences.
The umbelliform arrangement of the blossoms is a distinguishing feature of this species.
The plant produces umbelliform clusters of tiny white flowers in midsummer.

Synonyms

  • umbellate
    9.9 0.00/m
    umbellate is a botanical term for flower clusters with stalks from one point, while umbelliform means having an umbel shape.
  • umbellar
    9.9 0.00/m
    umbellar is used to say a plant bears or has an umbel, while umbelliform stresses the general umbel-like shape.
  • umbel-like
    9.9 0.00/m
    umbel-like is a simple descriptive phrase for things that look like an umbel, while umbelliform is more formal botanical language.
