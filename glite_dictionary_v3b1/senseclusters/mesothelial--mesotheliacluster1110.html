mesothelial

noun
mesothelium cell
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsəˈθiːliəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊˈθiːliəl / (BrE)
a cell that forms part of the mesothelium, the thin membrane lining body cavities and covering internal organs

Examples

A mesothelial cell
A single mesothelial was observed in the fluid sample under the microscope.
Damage to the mesothelial can lead to inflammation in the lining of the lungs.
Researchers studied how a mesothelial responds to infection in the abdominal cavity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epithelial cell - a cell forming the lining of body surfaces and cavities
