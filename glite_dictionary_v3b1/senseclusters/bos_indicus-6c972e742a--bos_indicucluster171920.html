Bos indicus

A species of cattle originating in South Asia, known for a shoulder hump, loose skin, and resistance to heat and parasites; includes breeds used for crossbreeding in the Americas.

Examples

Bos indicus cattle
Many ranchers in the southern United States crossbreed Bos indicus cattle with local breeds to improve hardiness.
The Bos indicus breeds are well adapted to hot, humid climates due to their heat resistance.
The characteristic hump on the shoulders distinguishes Bos indicus from other cattle species.
