thylacine

noun
carnivorous marsupial, Tasmanian tiger
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪ.ləˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈθʌɪ.ləˌsiːn / (BrE)
a rare, doglike carnivorous marsupial of Tasmania, also known as the Tasmanian tiger, with distinctive stripes on its back; believed to be extinct

Examples

The thylacine animal
Despite many reported sightings, there is no evidence that the thylacine still survives in the wild.
Scientists are analyzing old photographs to learn more about the behavior of the thylacine.
The thylacine, or Tasmanian tiger, was declared extinct in the 20th century.
