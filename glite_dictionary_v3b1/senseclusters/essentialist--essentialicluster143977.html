essentialist

noun
believer in fixed essence
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɛnʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that things have fundamental, unchanging characteristics that define their true nature

Examples

An essentialist philosopher
As an essentialist, she argued that gender differences are rooted in biology.
The debate between the essentialist and the constructivist shaped the philosophy seminar.
Many critics challenge the views held by an essentialist in social theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructivist - a person who believes that knowledge and meaning are constructed rather than inherent
  • synonym: realist - a person who accepts things as they are or believes in the existence of reality
