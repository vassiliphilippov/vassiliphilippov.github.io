news agency

noun
organization that supplies media with news
2.5 1.46/m
Pronunciation: / nuz Ààe…™.d í…ôn.si / (AmE), / njuÀêz Ààe…™.d í…ôn.si / (BrE)
An organization that gathers news reports and supplies them to newspapers, broadcasters, and other media outlets.

Examples

A news agency office
Many newspapers rely on a news agency for international news coverage.
Reuters is a well-known news agency that provides stories to media outlets worldwide.
The news agency reported on the earthquake just minutes after it struck.

Synonyms

    4.0 0.07/m
    a press agency collects and distributes news to newspapers and broadcasters, while a news agency supplies reports to media outlets generally.
  • wire service
    3.9 0.66/m

  • press association
    3.9 0.09/m
    a press association provides news, often for member newspapers or regional outlets, while a news agency serves many kinds of media.
  • news organization
    2.7 3.46/m
