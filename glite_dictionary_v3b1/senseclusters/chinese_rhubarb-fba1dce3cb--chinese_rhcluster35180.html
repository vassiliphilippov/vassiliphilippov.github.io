Chinese rhubarb

noun
medicinal plant used as a laxative
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪˌniz ˈruː.bɑrb / (AmE), / ˌtʃaɪˈniːz ˈruː.bɑːb / (BrE)
a large-leaved plant (Rheum palmatum) native to China and Tibet, traditionally used for its strong laxative properties

Examples

Chinese rhubarb plant
Doctors sometimes prescribe Chinese rhubarb as a natural remedy for constipation.
Many herbalists value Chinese rhubarb for its effectiveness as a laxative.
The root of Chinese rhubarb has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
