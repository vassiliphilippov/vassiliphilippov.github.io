pore

verb
to study something intently
7.2 2.81/m
Pronunciation: / p…îr / (AmE), / p…îÀê / (BrE)
to read or look at something very carefully and attentively, especially for a long time

Examples

To pore over books
He pores over scientific journals every evening after dinner.
She pored over the old letters, searching for clues about her ancestry.
The students were poring over their notes before the big exam.

Synonyms

  • pore over
    5.5 2.50/m
    pore over means read or study papers closely for a long time, while pore refers to the same careful, long reading.
  • peruse
    7.0 2.79/m
    peruse means examine or read something carefully and in detail, while pore suggests reading something attentively for a long time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: skim - to read something quickly without paying close attention
  • synonym: study - to devote time and attention to acquiring knowledge
