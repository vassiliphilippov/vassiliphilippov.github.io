shore up

verb
to support or reinforce something

Examples

To shore up support
Extra funding was used to shore up the struggling company during the crisis.
The engineers had to shore up the old bridge to prevent it from collapsing.
They placed wooden beams to shore up the sagging wall until repairs could be made.
Engineers were called in to shore up the collapsing wall.
She worked extra hours to shore up her chances of getting a promotion.
The company took measures to shore up its financial position after the crisis.

Synonyms

  • prop up
    3.6 4/m
    prop up supports something physically or figuratively to stop it falling, while shore up focuses on steadying weak things to prevent failure.
  • bolster
    4.3 9/m
    bolster gives support or encouragement to ideas or plans, while shore up often means repairing or strengthening to avoid collapse.
  • fortify
    5.1 0.3/m
    fortify makes something stronger, often by adding defenses or long-term strength, while shore up implies quick, temporary support.
  • shore
    7.1 4/m
    shore supports a structure with solid braces or beams in construction, while shore up is broader and can be figurative too.
  • prop
    4.1 5/m
    prop holds something up from below with a physical object, while shore up includes both quick repairs and figurative strengthening.
