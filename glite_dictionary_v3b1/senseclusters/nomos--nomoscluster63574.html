nomos

noun
law or custom concept
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊmɒs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊmɒs / (BrE)
a concept of law, custom, or social order, especially as discussed in ancient Greek philosophy

Examples

Greek nomos of law
In ancient Greece, the idea of nomos was central to discussions about justice and governance.
Sociologists often refer to nomos when analyzing the foundations of social order.
The debate contrasted natural law with the human-made rules of nomos.

Related Terms

  • synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving
  • synonym: law - a system of rules recognized by a community
