hardwired

adjective
fixed in hardware
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdˌwaɪrd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌwaɪəd / (BrE)
implemented directly in hardware circuitry and not changeable by software, often requiring physical modification to alter.

Examples

Hardwired feature
The security features are hardwired into the device and cannot be changed by updating the software.
Unlike software-based controls, a hardwired function requires physical changes to modify its behavior.
Some older computers have hardwired instructions that cannot be updated without replacing components.

Related Terms

  • synonym: built-in - included as a permanent part of something
  • antonym: programmable - able to be changed or controlled by software
