pentazocine

noun
synthetic analgesic drug
Pronunciation: / pɛnˈtæzəˌsin / (AmE), / pɛnˈtazəsiːn / (BrE)
A synthetic analgesic drug used to relieve moderate to severe pain, less addictive than morphine.

Examples

pentazocine pain relief
Nausea can sometimes occur as a side effect when taking pentazocine.
Some hospitals choose pentazocine because it has a lower risk of addiction than morphine.
The doctor prescribed pentazocine for the patient's postoperative pain management.
