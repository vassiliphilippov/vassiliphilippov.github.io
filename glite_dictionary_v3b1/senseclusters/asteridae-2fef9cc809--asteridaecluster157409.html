Asteridae

noun
major group of flowering plants in botany
9.9
Pronunciation: / æs.təˈraɪ.di.i /; / ˌæstɚˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌastəˈraɪdiːiː /; / ˌæstəˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
a large group or subclass of flowering plants with fused petals, including families such as Solanaceae and Compositae

Examples

The Asteridae group
Many common garden flowers, such as petunias, belong to the Asteridae.
Some plant taxonomists now divide the Asteridae into several different groups based on genetic evidence.
The family Solanaceae is traditionally included within the Asteridae.
Many ornamental flowers, like petunias and morning glories, belong to the Asteridae.
Recent botanical studies have refined the classification of the Asteridae based on genetic evidence.
The Asteridae include plants that are important both ecologically and economically, such as potatoes and bell peppers.
