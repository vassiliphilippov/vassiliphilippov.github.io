CinemaScope

noun
widescreen film format
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnəməˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəməˌskəʊp / (BrE)
a widescreen film format used in movie production to create a wider image on the screen

Examples

CinemaScope movie format
Many epic movies of the 1950s were filmed in CinemaScope to impress audiences with their wide visuals.
The director chose CinemaScope to capture the vast landscapes in the adventure film.
Restoring old CinemaScope films allows modern viewers to experience their original grandeur.

Related Terms

  • antonym: standard format - the traditional film format with a narrower aspect ratio
  • synonym: widescreen - a film or television format with a wider aspect ratio than standard
