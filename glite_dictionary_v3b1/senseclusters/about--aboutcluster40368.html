Synonyms

  • around
    1.0 98/m
    around is used to say something is nearby or present in a place, while about means in the area or nearby.
  • thereabouts
    4.9 0.06/m
    thereabouts means in or near that place or area, while about is a general word for being nearby or around.
  • hereabouts
    5.4 0.04/m
    hereabouts refers to this general area near the speaker, while about is neutral and can mean near any place.
  • around
    1.0 157/m
    around can describe moving to many places or wandering, while about focuses on being in or near an area.
  • thereabout
    6.7 0.01/m
    thereabout means near that location in older or rare use, while about is common modern English for being nearby.
