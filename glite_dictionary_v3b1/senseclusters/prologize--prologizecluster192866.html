prologize

verb
compose or deliver a prologue
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊləˌɡaɪz /; / ˈproʊ.ləˌɡaɪz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊləˌɡaɪz /; / ˈprəʊ.ləˌɡaɪz / (BrE)
to write or deliver a prologue, especially to a literary work or performance

Examples

To prologize a play
After prologizing at length, the director finally began the presentation.

The author chose to prologize before the start of his new novel, setting the stage for the story.
Rather than letting the work speak for itself, the author insisted on prologuizing every chapter.
She prologuized at length, explaining the historical context before her main argument.
The playwright chose to prologuize before the first act, setting the scene for the audience.
