solipsistic

adjective
self-centered philosophy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːlɪpˈsɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪpˈsɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the belief that only one's own mind or self is sure to exist or can be known.

Examples

Solipsistic philosophy
Her solipsistic worldview made it difficult for her to empathize with others.
The novel explores a solipsistic character who doubts the existence of anyone else.
Critics described the artist's work as solipsistic, focused entirely on personal experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: egocentric - thinking only of oneself, without regard for others
  • antonym: selfless - concerned more with the needs of others than with oneself
