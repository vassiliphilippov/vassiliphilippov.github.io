latinity

noun
proficiency in Latin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ləˈtɪnəti / (AmE), / ləˈtɪnɪti / (BrE)
knowledge of or skill in the Latin language, especially in writing or speaking Latin well

Examples

Fluent latinity
Her impressive latinity allowed her to translate ancient texts with ease.
The professor's latinity was evident in his fluent recitation of Roman poetry.
Students admired his latinity during the Latin language competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classicism - proficiency in classical languages or literature
  • antonym: illiteracy - lack of ability to read or write
