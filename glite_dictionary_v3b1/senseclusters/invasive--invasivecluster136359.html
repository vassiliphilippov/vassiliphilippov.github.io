invasive

adjective
involving entry into the body (medicine)
6.3 5.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈveɪ.sɪv/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈveɪ.sɪv/ (BrE)
(of medical procedures) involving entering the body, typically by cutting or puncturing the skin or by inserting instruments.

Examples

Invasive surgery
Doctors explained that an invasive procedure might be necessary to diagnose the problem.
Many modern tests are now invasive, requiring minimal incisions or use of catheters.
The patient opted for a less invasive surgical technique to reduce recovery time.
