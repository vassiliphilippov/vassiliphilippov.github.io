sift through

verb
examine carefully, especially in detail
7.3
Pronunciation: / sɪft θru / (AmE), / sɪft θruː / (BrE)
to examine a large number of things carefully, usually to find something specific or to organize them

Examples

To sift through evidence
Detectives are sifting through evidence to uncover new leads in the case.
I spent the morning sifting through old emails to find the information I needed.
The committee had to sift through hundreds of résumés before making a decision.
