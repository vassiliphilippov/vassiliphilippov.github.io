paratyphoid

noun
type of infectious intestinal disease
4.0 8.4
Pronunciation: /pær.ə.ˈtaɪ.fɔɪd/ (AmE), /pær.ə.ˈtaɪ.fɔɪd/ (BrE)
any of several infectious intestinal diseases similar to but usually less severe than typhoid fever, caused by bacteria of the genus Salmonella

Examples

Paratyphoid fever
After returning from abroad, she was diagnosed with paratyphoid and treated promptly.
Doctors monitored cases of paratyphoid in the area during the summer months.
The outbreak of paratyphoid was traced to contaminated water in the village.
