drily

adverb
in a dry, understated manner
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪli / (AmE), / ˈdraɪli / (BrE)
In a dry, ironic, or matter-of-fact manner, often with understated humor or sarcasm.

Examples

To speak drily
"I suppose you expected this outcome," she said drily, not looking up from her book.
"That's just perfect," he remarked drily, rolling his eyes.
Mark responded drily to the joke, hinting at his hidden amusement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dryly - in a dry or laconic manner
