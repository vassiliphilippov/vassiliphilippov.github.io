Mucuna pruriens utilis

noun
cultivated variety of annual vine used for manure and grazing
Pronunciation: / muˈkuːnə pruˈriːenz ˈjuːtɪlɪs / (AmE), / mjuˈkuːnə pruˈriːenz ˈjuːtɪlɪs / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of Mucuna pruriens, an annual woody vine native to Asia with long clusters of purplish flowers and densely hairy pods, grown in the southern United States for green manure and grazing

Examples

Mucuna pruriens utilis plant
Because of its rapid growth, Mucuna pruriens utilis is ideal for green manure in tropical climates.
Cattle grazed peacefully among the rows of Mucuna pruriens utilis.
The farmer planted Mucuna pruriens utilis as a cover crop to enrich the soil.
