tabard

noun
sleeveless outer garment with coat of arms
Pronunciation: / ˈtæbərd / (AmE), / ˈtæbəd / (BrE)
a sleeveless outer garment worn over clothes, often for identification, as a uniform, or (historically) over armor by knights or heralds

Examples

A tabard uniform
During the ceremony, the herald's tabard shimmered in the sunlight.
The knight wore a crimson tabard adorned with his family's crest.
The museum displayed a medieval tabard alongside a suit of armor.
Each volunteer wore a bright yellow tabard to stand out during the festival.
The museum guide put on a tabard before leading the school group through the exhibits.
In medieval times, a knight's tabard displayed his coat of arms over his armor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uniform - a distinctive set of clothing worn by members of the same organization
