railroad track

noun
the rails trains run on
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌrɑd træk / (AmE), / ˈreɪlrəʊd træk / (BrE)
a pair of metal rails fixed to the ground, providing a pathway for trains to run on

Examples

A railroad track section
A freight train thundered down the railroad track late last night.
Children are warned never to play near a railroad track.
The old factory was built right next to the railroad track.

Related Terms

  • synonym: railway track - a pair of rails for train travel, chiefly British form
