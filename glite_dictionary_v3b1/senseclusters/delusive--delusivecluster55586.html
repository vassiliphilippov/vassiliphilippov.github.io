delusive

adjective
giving a false or misleading impression
2.5
Pronunciation: / dɪˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
giving a false or misleading impression; deceptive or unreal.

Examples

A delusive hope
His dreams of immediate wealth proved to be delusive and quickly faded away.
Many people developed a delusive confidence in the safety of the new medication.
The marketing campaign made delusive promises that the product could not fulfill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceptive - intending to mislead or give a false impression
  • antonym: genuine - authentic; truly what something is said to be
  • synonym: illusory - based on illusion; not real
