foolproof

adjective
incapable of failure or misuse
3.9 1.90/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfuÀêlÀåpruÀêf / (AmE), / ÀàfuÀêlÀåpruÀêf / (BrE)
(of a design, method, or plan) so simple, reliable, or well-constructed that it cannot go wrong or be used incorrectly.

Examples

A foolproof plan
Her recipe is so foolproof that anyone can bake a perfect cake on the first try.
The new software comes with a foolproof installation process that even beginners can handle.
We need a foolproof plan to make sure nothing goes wrong during the event.

Synonyms

  • fail-safe
    5.6 0.10/m
    fail-safe is designed to keep working or stay safe even when parts fail, while foolproof is simple and cannot be used wrongly.
  • bulletproof
    5.8 0.48/m
    bulletproof is very strong and hard to break or defeat, while foolproof means a simple system people cannot use wrongly.
  • unfailing
    4.9 0.49/m
    unfailing means always reliable over time and never weakens, while foolproof describes a simple method people cannot use incorrectly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faulty - prone to mistakes or failure
  • synonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance
  • synonym: unfailing - always effective or dependable
