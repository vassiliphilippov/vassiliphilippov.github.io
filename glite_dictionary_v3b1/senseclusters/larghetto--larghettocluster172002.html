larghetto

noun
slow tempo in music, between largo and adagio
8.3
Pronunciation: / lɑrˈɡɛtoʊ / (AmE), / lɑːˈɡɛtəʊ / (BrE)
(music) a passage or piece played at a slow tempo, slightly faster than largo but slower than adagio.

Examples

A larghetto passage
She practiced the larghetto passage repeatedly to capture the subtle changes in tempo.
The conductor asked the orchestra to start the piece at larghetto speed, not too slow but unhurried.
The second movement of the symphony is marked larghetto, giving it a gentle and relaxed feel.
