order Plectognathi

noun
a fish order including pufferfish and boxfish
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.dər plek.tə.ˈnæ.θaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.də plek.tə.ˈnæ.θaɪ/ (BrE)
a former order of fishes including boxfishes, filefishes, pufferfishes, ocean sunfishes, and triggerfishes, known for their fused jaw bones and distinctive body shapes

Examples

The order Plectognathi
In older zoological texts, you might encounter references to the order Plectognathi.
Scientists once classified puffers and triggerfish under the order Plectognathi before taxonomic revisions.
The order Plectognathi included fishes with unique jaw structures and tough skins.
