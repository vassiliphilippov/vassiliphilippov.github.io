surplus

noun
amount greater than needed
3.1 16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀê.pl…ôs / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀê.pl…ôs / (BrE)
an amount of something that is more than what is needed or used

Examples

A surplus of food
After the harvest, there was a surplus of wheat that could be sold to neighboring towns.
If you have a surplus of food, consider donating it to those in need.
The company reported a huge surplus in their annual earnings this year.

Synonyms

  • excess
    3.3 13/m
    excess is often unwanted because it is too much, while surplus is extra amount left after need or use.
  • surplusage
    9.9 0.06/m
    surplusage is an older formal word used in law or books, while surplus is the common modern word for extra amount.
  • overabundance
    5.7 0.6/m
    overabundance shows a very large amount that can cause problems, while surplus is any extra amount beyond need.
  • overrun
    5.9 1/m
    overrun refers to something that exceeds expected limits often in numbers or costs, while surplus means extra left after need.
