puy

noun
medieval poetry contest
9.9
Pronunciation: / pwi / (AmE), / pwi / (BrE)
a medieval or early modern French society or contest that awarded prizes for poetry or music

Examples

A poetry puy
The poet traveled to France to participate in the annual puy and share his latest verses.
Winning a prize at the puy was a great honor for musicians in medieval times.
Many famous songs were first performed at a puy before becoming popular across Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eisteddfod - a Welsh festival of literature, music, and performance
  • synonym: tournament - a competition involving a series of contests
