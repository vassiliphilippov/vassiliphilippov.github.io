squat

verb
to sit or rest low and wide (structure/object)
5.8
Pronunciation: / skwɑt / (AmE), / skwɒt / (BrE)
(of an object or structure) to sit or rest close to the ground, often with a broad or disproportionately wide appearance.

Examples

A warehouse squats by river
An enormous warehouse squats by the river, its roof barely visible above the embankment.
In the dim light, the old statue squatted at the far end of the garden, sturdy and low.
The small cottage squatted on the edge of the marsh, almost hidden by reeds.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: loom - to appear as a large, indistinct, and often threatening shape
