photocell

noun
light-detecting electrical device

A device that detects and measures light or other electromagnetic radiation by converting it into an electrical signal.

Examples

A photocell sensor
A faulty photocell can cause automatic doors to malfunction.
Streetlights often turn on automatically at dusk thanks to a photocell sensor.
The security system uses a photocell to detect when someone enters the room.

Synonyms

  • photoelectric cell
    4.6 0.02/m
    a photoelectric cell is the technical term for devices that convert light into electrical energy, while a photocell is the general light-sensing device.
  • photodetector
    7.2 0.19/m
    a photodetector senses light and converts it to an electrical signal for measurement, while a photocell is a common name for such devices.
  • electric eye
    5.3 0.03/m
    an electric eye detects light for automatic control or security use, while a photocell is any device that converts light into an electrical signal.
