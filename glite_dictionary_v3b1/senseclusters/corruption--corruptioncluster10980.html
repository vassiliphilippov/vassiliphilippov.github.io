corruption

noun
moral depravity or moral decay
1.0 3.7
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈrʌp.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrʌp.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or state of being morally depraved or making someone immoral; loss of virtue or moral principles

Examples

Moral corruption
He feared that exposure to such decadence would lead to spiritual corruption.
The corruption of the city's leaders led to a decline in public morals.
Writers of the period often lamented the corruption they witnessed in society.
She worried about the corruption of her son's values after he moved to the city.
The novel explores the gradual corruption of a once-innocent community.
They investigated allegations of moral corruption within the leadership of the organization.
