unalienated

adjective
not transferred (property)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈeɪliəˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈeɪliəneɪtɪd / (BrE)
not transferred, sold, or surrendered; still belonging to the original owner, especially in legal or property contexts

Examples

Unalienated land
The land remained unalienated after decades, still owned by the original family.
Certain rights are considered unalienated and cannot be given away or sold.
The court confirmed that the property was unalienated and under the owner's full control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alienated - transferred or sold to another party
