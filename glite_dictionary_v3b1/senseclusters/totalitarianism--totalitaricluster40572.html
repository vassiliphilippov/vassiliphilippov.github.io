totalitarianism

noun
absolute dictatorial government system
Pronunciation: / toʊˌtæləˈtɛriənɪzəm /; / toʊˌtæləˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / təʊˌtæləˈteəriənɪzəm /; / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
a political system or belief in which the government has absolute power and controls all aspects of public and private life

Examples

Totalitarianism government control
Debates about totalitarianism often focus on how governments suppress dissent and individual rights.
Many historians argue that totalitarianism emerged in the twentieth century with regimes like Nazi Germany and the Soviet Union.
The novel describes a future society under ruthless totalitarianism, where citizens have no personal freedoms.
Academics often debate the causes and consequences of totalitarianism in modern history.
He feared that new laws might pave the way for totalitarianism in his country.
The rise of totalitarianism in the twentieth century led to major global conflicts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarianism - strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • antonym: democracy - a form of government in which power is vested in the people
