desalting

noun
removal of salt
3.7
Pronunciation: / diːˈsɔltɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈsɔːltɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of removing salt from water or other materials to make them suitable for use or consumption

Examples

Modern desalting technology
Modern desalting technology is essential for providing fresh water in arid regions.
The desalting of seawater helps supply drinking water to many coastal communities.
Engineers are working to make desalting processes more energy-efficient and affordable.
Modern ships are equipped with desalting systems to provide fresh water for the crew.
The desalting of seawater is essential in regions with limited freshwater resources.
Engineers are developing more efficient desalting technologies to address global water shortages.
