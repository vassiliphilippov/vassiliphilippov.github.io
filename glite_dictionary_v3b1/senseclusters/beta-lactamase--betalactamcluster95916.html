beta-lactamase

noun
an enzyme causing penicillin resistance
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.tə ˈlæk.tə.meɪz/ /; / /ˈbiː.tə ˈlæk.tə.meɪz/ (AmE), /ˈbiː.tə ˈlæk.tə.meɪz/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.tə ˈlæk.tə.meɪz/ (BrE)
an enzyme produced by certain bacteria that breaks down penicillin and related antibiotics, making the bacteria resistant to these drugs

Examples

Beta-lactamase enzyme
Many hospital-acquired infections are caused by bacteria that produce beta-lactamase.
Some antibiotics are combined with inhibitors to block beta-lactamase activity and restore effectiveness.
The presence of beta-lactamase in a bacterial strain makes it resistant to penicillin-based drugs.
