canecutter

noun
swamp rabbit of southeastern U.S.
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪnˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnˌkʌtə / (BrE)
a large swamp rabbit found in the southeastern United States, especially in swamps and lowlands.

Examples

The canecutter rabbit
Because of its size and habitat, the canecutter is sometimes mistaken for a beaver from a distance.
Hunters in Louisiana sometimes spot a canecutter darting through the marshes at dawn.
The canecutter is well adapted to living in swamps and often feeds on thick stands of cane.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swamp rabbit - a large cottontail rabbit native to swamps of the southern U.S.
