interdict

verb
formally forbid or prohibit
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈdɪkt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdɪkt / (BrE)
to formally prohibit or forbid something, especially by an authoritative order

Examples

To interdict a book
Religious leaders attempted to interdict certain books they considered harmful to society.
The court decided to interdict the sale of the disputed property until the case was resolved.
The school board may interdict students from using their phones during class hours.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forbid - to order someone not to do something
  • synonym: prohibit - to formally forbid something by law or authority
