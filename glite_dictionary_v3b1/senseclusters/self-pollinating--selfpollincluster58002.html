self-pollinating

adjective
fertilizing with own pollen
5.7 4.5
Pronunciation: /self.ˈpɑː.lə.neɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /self.ˈpɑː.lɪ.neɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈself.pɑː.lə.neɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /self.ˈpɒl.ɪ.neɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /self.ˈpɒl.ə.neɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
(of a plant) able to fertilize itself with its own pollen rather than requiring pollen from another plant.

Examples

Self-pollinating plants
Some flowers are not self-pollinating and depend on bees for reproduction.
This variety of pea is self-pollinating and easy to grow in home gardens.
Tomatoes are often self-pollinating, so they do not need insects to bear fruit.
