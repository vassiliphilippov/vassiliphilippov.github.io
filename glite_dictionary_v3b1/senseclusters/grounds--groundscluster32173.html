grounds

noun
land set aside for a special purpose
2.8 3.73/m
Pronunciation: / É¡raÊŠndz / (AmE), / É¡raÊŠndz / (BrE)
an area of land that is used or set aside for a specific purpose, such as recreation, a building site, or burial.

Examples

Burial grounds
The family gathered at the burial grounds for the memorial service.
The university grounds are beautifully landscaped with gardens and old trees.
Visitors strolled through the museum grounds enjoying the outdoor sculptures.

Synonyms

  • piece of ground
    2.8 0.17/m
    a piece of ground is a single defined area often used for one purpose, while grounds means land set aside for various uses.
  • plot of ground
    2.3 0.08/m
    a plot of ground is a small marked area often for growing plants, while grounds means larger land set aside for activities or burial.
  • premises
    3.4 0.01/m
    premises are the land and buildings used for business, while grounds mean mainly open land set aside for specific purposes.

Related Terms
