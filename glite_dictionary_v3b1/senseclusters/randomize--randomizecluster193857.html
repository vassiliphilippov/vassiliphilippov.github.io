randomize

verb
arrange in random order
Pronunciation: / ˈræn.dəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræn.də.maɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or order something in a random or unpredictable manner, especially for experimental or statistical purposes

Examples

To randomize sample groups
Researchers randomized the sample groups to prevent bias in the study.
Teachers sometimes randomize seating arrangements to encourage interaction.
The software can randomize the list of participants for the experiment.
It is important to randomise the questions on the test to prevent cheating.
The researchers decided to randomise the sample groups to avoid bias.
You should randomise the order of the playlist for a new listening experience.
