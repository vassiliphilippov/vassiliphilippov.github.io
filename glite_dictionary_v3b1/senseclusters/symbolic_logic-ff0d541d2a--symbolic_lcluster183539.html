symbolic logic

noun
formal logic using symbols
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈbɑlɪk ˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪk ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
a branch of logic in which symbols are used to represent logical forms and arguments, focusing on the structure rather than the content of statements

Examples

Symbolic logic study
He studied symbolic logic as part of his philosophy degree.
Mathematicians use symbolic logic to prove theorems rigorously.
The textbook covers principles of symbolic logic in great detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formal logic - the study of logic using formal systems and symbolic representation
  • synonym: mathematical logic - the application of logic to mathematics
