tonic accent

noun
emphasis by pitch instead of loudness
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑː.nɪk ˈæk.sənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒ.nɪk ˈæk.sənt / (BrE)
an emphasis on a syllable or word produced by a change in pitch rather than by increased loudness

Examples

The tonic accent pitch
In certain languages, the tonic accent marks the main pitch rise on a syllable.
Linguists study how the tonic accent changes the meaning of spoken phrases.
Unlike stress in English, a tonic accent depends on pitch rather than loudness.
