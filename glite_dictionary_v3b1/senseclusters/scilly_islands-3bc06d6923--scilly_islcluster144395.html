Scilly Islands

proper noun
archipelago off southwestern England
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪli ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈsɪli ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
an archipelago of small islands off the southwestern coast of England, near the entrance to the English Channel, known historically as a haven for smugglers and pirates

Examples

The Scilly Islands archipelago
Legend says that pirates once hid their treasure on the Scilly Islands.
The Scilly Islands are famous for their mild climate and stunning scenery.
We took a ferry from Penzance to the Scilly Islands for our summer holiday.
