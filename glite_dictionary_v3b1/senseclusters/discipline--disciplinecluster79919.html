discipline

noun
training to improve strength or self-control
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsəplɪn /; / ˈdɪs.əˌplɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɪsəplɪn /; / ˈdɪs.ɪ.plɪn / (BrE)
the practice or system of training people to obey rules or a code of behavior, often using punishment or strict control

Examples

Strict discipline
Good discipline is essential for achieving your long-term goals.
Military training requires a high level of discipline and commitment.
The yoga class helped him develop both physical and mental discipline.
Military life requires a certain discipline that many civilians find challenging.
The athletes followed a strict discipline to prepare for the competition.
The school emphasizes the importance of discipline in maintaining a productive learning environment.
After the incident, the company took steps to improve discipline in the workplace.
She believes that positive discipline is more effective than harsh punishment.
The school implemented strict discipline to maintain order among students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chaos - a state of complete disorder and confusion
  • synonym: order - a condition in which rules are obeyed and everything is arranged properly
  • antonym: punishment - the infliction of a penalty
  • synonym: regimen - a prescribed course of action or way of life, especially in a systematic way
  • antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement; opposite of punishment
  • synonym: self-control - the ability to control one's actions or feelings
