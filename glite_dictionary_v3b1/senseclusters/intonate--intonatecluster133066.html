intonate

verb
recite with musical intonation
4.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtoʊ.neɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtəʊ.neɪt/ (BrE)
to say or recite something with a particular tone, pitch, or rhythm, often in a musical or dramatic way

Examples

To intonate a hymn
During the ceremony, the monks intonated ancient hymns in unison.
He stood at the podium and intonated the chant solemnly, filling the hall with sound.
The priest began to intonate the evening prayers as the congregation listened.
He would intonate each phrase as if telling a dramatic story.
She tried to intonate the poem with emotion, pausing at just the right moments.
The actor was asked to intonate the lines with a sense of urgency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chant - to sing or say something in a repetitive, rhythmic, and musical manner
  • synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice in speaking
