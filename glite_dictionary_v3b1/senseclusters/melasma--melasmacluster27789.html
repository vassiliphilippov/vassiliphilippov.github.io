melasma

noun
patchy facial skin discoloration
5.3 7.8
Pronunciation: / məˈlæzmə / (AmE), / mɛˈlæzmə / (BrE)
A condition marked by tan or brown discoloration of the skin, especially on a woman's face, commonly associated with pregnancy or the use of oral contraceptives.

Examples

Melasma on face
Many pregnant women experience melasma, often called the 'mask of pregnancy'.
The dermatologist diagnosed her with melasma after examining the dark patches on her cheeks.
Wearing sunscreen daily can help prevent melasma from worsening.
