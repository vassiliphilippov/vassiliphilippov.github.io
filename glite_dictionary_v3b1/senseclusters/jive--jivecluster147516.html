jive

verb
to speak insincerely
Pronunciation: / dʒaɪv / (AmE), / dʒaɪv / (BrE)
to talk in a way that is insincere, misleading, or intended to deceive

Examples

To jive about money
He always jives when he wants to avoid the truth.
Stop jiving and tell me what really happened.
You can't trust her because she tends to jive when things get difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be honest - to speak truthfully and sincerely
  • synonym: lie - to say something that is not true
  • synonym: mislead - to give a false idea or impression
