cellaring

noun
storing for aging
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsel.ə.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsel.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of storing wine or other products in a cellar to allow them to age and improve in quality

Examples

Proper cellaring improves wine
Proper cellaring can improve the flavor and quality of certain wines.
The winery recommends cellaring this bottle for at least five years before opening.
Good cellaring conditions include a cool, dark, and humid environment.
