umami

noun
savory taste
Pronunciation: / uˈmɑːmi / (AmE), / uˈmɑːmi / (BrE)
a savory taste, recognized as one of the five basic tastes alongside sweet, sour, bitter, and salty

Examples

Strong umami taste
The chef used soy sauce to enhance the umami in the soup.
Tomatoes are naturally rich in umami, making them a popular ingredient in many dishes.
Parmesan cheese adds a strong umami flavor to pasta.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bitter - having a sharp, pungent taste or smell
  • synonym: savory - having a salty or spicy flavor rather than a sweet one
  • antonym: sweet - having the taste characteristic of sugar or honey
