back road

noun
minor rear roadway
3.1
Pronunciation: /bæk roʊd/ /; / /bæk rəʊd/ (AmE), /bæk rəʊd/ /; / /bæk roʊd/ (BrE)
a small, quiet road that is not a main road, often used for local access or to avoid traffic

Examples

A quiet back road
The delivery truck used the back road to avoid the busy main street.
Residents prefer walking along the quiet back road behind the houses.
Garbage is collected every Monday from the back road behind the apartment complex.
They took a back road to avoid the heavy traffic on the main highway.
We discovered a charming village while driving along a back road in the countryside.
Cyclists often prefer a back road because it is quieter and safer than busy streets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alley - a narrow passageway between or behind buildings
  • synonym: country road - a road in a rural area, often less traveled
  • antonym: highway - a main public road, especially one connecting major towns or cities
  • antonym: main road - a major or primary road used for through traffic
