wash away

verb
remove by washing with water or liquid
1.0 1.9
Pronunciation: /wɑːʃ ə.ˈweɪ/ /; / /wɔːʃ ə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /wɒʃ ə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
to remove or carry something away by the action of water or another liquid

Examples

Floods wash away roads
A strong current can wash away small bridges during a flood.
She tried to wash away the ink stain, but it was stubborn.
The heavy rain managed to wash away all the mud from the driveway.
Over time, nature will wash away all traces of the old path.
She hoped that a good cry would wash away her sadness.
The summer rains will wash away the dust from the streets.
