groyne

noun
structure built from a shore to prevent beach erosion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡrɔɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɔɪn / (BrE)
a low wall or barrier built of stone or concrete, projecting from a beach into the sea to prevent erosion by interrupting water movement and limiting the movement of sediment

Examples

A groyne on beach
Over time, the gooynes along this shoreline have changed the shape of the beach.
The engineers decided to construct a groyne to reduce coastal erosion.
Walking along the beach, we noticed how the old groyne had collected a lot of sand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breakwater - barrier built out into a body of water to protect a coast or harbor from the force of waves
