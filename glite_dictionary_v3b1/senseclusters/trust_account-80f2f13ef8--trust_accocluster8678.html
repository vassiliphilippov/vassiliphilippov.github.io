trust account

noun
bank account held for a beneficiary
4.4 0.2/m
Pronunciation: / trÊŒst É™ËˆkaÊŠnt / (AmE), / trÊŒst É™ËˆkaÊŠnt / (BrE)
A bank account in which one person (the trustee) holds and manages funds for the benefit of another (the beneficiary), with control during the trustee's lifetime and the balance payable to the beneficiary afterward.

Examples

A trust account holder
After her grandmother passed away, the trust account was released to her as the named beneficiary.
Many parents set up a trust account for their children to ensure financial security in the future.
The lawyer managed the client's inheritance using a trust account until the beneficiary turned eighteen.
