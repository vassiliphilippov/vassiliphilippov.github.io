dovetail

noun
interlocking wood joint
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊvˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvteɪl / (BrE)
a joint between two pieces of wood, made by fitting a series of interlocking wedge-shaped projections (tenons) into corresponding notches (mortises) to form a strong, tight connection

Examples

A wooden dovetail
A well-made dovetail adds both strength and beauty to furniture.
Learning to cut a perfect dovetail is a milestone for many woodworkers.
The craftsman used a classic dovetail to connect the corners of the drawer.
