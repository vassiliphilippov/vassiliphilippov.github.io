on life support

preposition
maintained with minimal support to prevent failure
Pronunciation: / ɑn laɪf səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ɒn laɪf səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
kept alive or maintained with minimal resources or intervention, especially to prevent failure or collapse, as with medical machines or in a figurative sense

Examples

On life support machines
After the accident, the patient remained on life support for several days.
That old computer system is basically on life support until they can upgrade it.
The company was kept on life support with emergency funding from the government.

Related Terms
