cytolysin

noun
substance that destroys animal cells
9.9
Pronunciation: / saɪˈtoʊlɪsɪn / (AmE), / saɪˈtɒlɪsɪn / (BrE)
a substance, often a protein, that causes the destruction or lysis of animal cells by disrupting their membranes

Examples

A cytolysin protein
A potent cytolysin can contribute to severe infections by damaging host cells.
Scientists studied the effects of the cytolysin on liver tissue cultures.
The bacteria release a cytolysin that destroys nearby animal cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemolysin - a substance that causes the destruction of red blood cells
