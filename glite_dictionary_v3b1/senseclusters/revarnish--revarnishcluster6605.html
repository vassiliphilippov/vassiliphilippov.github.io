revarnish

verb
to superficially improve
7.4
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈvɑːr.nɪʃ/ /; / /rə.ˈvɑːr.nɪʃ/ (AmE), /riː.ˈvɑː.nɪʃ/ /; / /rə.ˈvɑː.nɪʃ/ (BrE)
to improve the appearance or reputation of something by making superficial changes, often to hide faults

Examples

To revarnish an image
The politician tried to revarnish his reputation after the controversy by launching a charity campaign.
After years of neglect, the museum attempted to revarnish its public image with a new exhibition.
The company hoped to revarnish its brand by updating its logo and website design.
