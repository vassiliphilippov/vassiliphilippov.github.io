overcount

noun
excess counted amount
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvɚˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkaʊnt /; / ˈəʊvəˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
a number or instance where something has been counted more than once, resulting in a total that is too high

Examples

An overcount of people
The audit revealed an overcount in the reported number of participants at the event.
After reviewing the data, the team corrected the overcount in the sales figures.
A simple mistake in the spreadsheet led to an overcount of the total inventory.
The census revealed an overcount in several urban districts due to duplicate entries.
An overcount of ballots forced the election officials to audit the polling stations overnight.
Researchers corrected the overcount after discovering that some participants were listed twice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overestimate - an estimate that is higher than the actual amount
  • antonym: undercount - an instance of counting less than the actual amount
  • antonym: underestimate - a number that is lower than the actual amount
