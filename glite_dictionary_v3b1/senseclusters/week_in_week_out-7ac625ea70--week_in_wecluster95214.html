week in, week out

adverb
for many weeks without interruption
4.1 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / wiÀêk …™n wiÀêk a ät / (AmE), / wiÀêk …™n wiÀêk a ät / (BrE)
continuously or regularly, especially for many weeks and without change

Examples

Week in, week out work
He delivers the paper week in, week out without ever missing a day.
She goes to the library week in, week out to study for her exams.
The shop is busy week in, week out during the holiday season.

Synonyms

  • week after week
    1.0 0.88/m
    week after week describes doing something again and again for many weeks without fail, while week in, week out stresses unchanging, continuous repetition.
  • every week
    1.0 11/m
    every week says something happens once each week as a regular schedule, while week in, week out carries stronger sense of continuous, unchanging repetition.
  • each week
    1.0 7.08/m
    each week marks that something happens every seven days on a routine basis, while week in, week out implies long, steady repetition without change.
  • weekly
    1.0 2.62/m
    weekly describes something that happens once every week, often as a label, while week in, week out suggests continuous, repeated action over many weeks.
  • week by week
    1.1 0.26/m
    week by week describes things that change or are measured each week step by step, while week in, week out means steady, unchanging repetition.
