muley

adjective
hornless livestock
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːli / (AmE), / ˈmjuːli / (BrE)
naturally without horns, especially referring to cattle or livestock

Examples

Muley cattle
The farmer preferred muley cattle because they were easier to handle.
A muley bull stood quietly in the pasture, lacking the horns of the others.
Some breeds are naturally muley, making them safer for ranch workers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: horned - having horns
  • synonym: polled - naturally hornless, especially in cattle or sheep
