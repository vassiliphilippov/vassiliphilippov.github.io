Merry Andrew

noun
buffoon or clown; jokester
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛri ˈændruː / (AmE), / ˈmɛri ˈandruː / (BrE)
a person who acts as a clown or buffoon, especially to amuse others; a jocular entertainer

Examples

The Merry Andrew clown
At the festival, he played the Merry Andrew, telling jokes to anyone who would listen.
He was dismissed as a Merry Andrew, but his antics cheered everyone up.
In the old play, a Merry Andrew provided comic relief between serious acts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clown - a comic entertainer, especially in a circus
