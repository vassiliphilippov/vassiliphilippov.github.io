gerontocratic

adjective
ruled by elders
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛrəntəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛrəntəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a system or government ruled by old people

Examples

Gerontocratic government
The organization had a gerontocratic structure, with most leaders over the age of seventy.
Critics argued that the gerontocratic council was out of touch with the younger generation.
Some believe a gerontocratic system can lead to resistance to change and innovation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: democratic - relating to a government ruled by the people
  • synonym: oligarchic - relating to a government ruled by a small group
