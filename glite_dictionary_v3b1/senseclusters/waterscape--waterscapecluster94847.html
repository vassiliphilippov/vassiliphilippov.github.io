waterscape

noun
real-world scene dominated by water
4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwɑː.tər.skeɪp/ /; / /ˈwɔː.tər.skeɪp/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə.skeɪp/ /; / /ˈwɒ.tə.skeɪp/ (BrE)
the visible arrangement or character of bodies of water in a place; a real-world scene or area dominated by water, such as rivers, lakes, harbors, or coastlines.

Examples

The city waterscape
Urban planners redesigned the harbor to enhance the waterscape for residents and visitors.
From the hilltop we admired the rugged waterscapes of estuaries and tidal channels below.
Environmental surveys mapped the city's waterscape to guide habitat restoration and flood planning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: landscape - the visible features of an area of land; contrasted with scenes dominated by water
  • synonym: seascape - a view or depiction specifically of the sea; often used for coastal scenes
