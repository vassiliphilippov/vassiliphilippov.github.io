gonadotropic hormone

noun
hormone stimulating gonads
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊˌnædoʊˈtrɑpɪk ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ɡəʊˌnædəˈtrɒpɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland or placenta that stimulates the gonads and regulates reproductive activity

Examples

The gonadotropic hormone regulates reproduction
During pregnancy, the placenta produces a gonadotropic hormone called human chorionic gonadotropin.
Follicle-stimulating hormone and luteinizing hormone are both types of gonadotropic hormone involved in reproduction.
The doctor explained that abnormal levels of gonadotropic hormone could impact fertility.
