shivah

noun
Jewish seven-day mourning period
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪvə / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvə / (BrE)
In Judaism, a period of seven days of mourning observed after the death of a close relative.

Examples

To observe shivah
After his grandmother's passing, the family gathered to observe shivah together.
During shivah, friends provided meals and support for the bereaved family.
Visitors came to offer condolences while she was sitting shivah at her home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mourning - The act of expressing sorrow for someone's death
