crystalloid

noun
medical fluid solution
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɪs.təl.ɔɪd/ /; / /ˈkrɪs.tə.lɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈkrɪs.təl.ɔɪd/ /; / /ˈkrɪs.tə.lɔɪd/ (BrE)
a solution containing small molecules, such as salts, that can easily pass through cell membranes, often used in medical treatment

Examples

Crystalloid solution
The doctor ordered a crystalloid infusion to quickly restore the patient's fluid balance.
Nurses often use crystalloid solutions like saline during surgery to maintain blood pressure.
A crystalloid is preferred over a colloid for treating mild dehydration in most cases.
