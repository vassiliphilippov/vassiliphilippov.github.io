schistosome

noun
a parasitic flatworm of the genus Schistosoma
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪstəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈskɪstəsəʊm / (BrE)
Any of a genus of parasitic flatworms that live in the blood vessels of mammals and can cause schistosomiasis.

Examples

A schistosome infection
A schistosome infection can result in severe liver and intestinal damage if left untreated.
Researchers observed the life cycle of the schistosome under laboratory conditions.
The doctor diagnosed the infection as being caused by a schistosome species.
