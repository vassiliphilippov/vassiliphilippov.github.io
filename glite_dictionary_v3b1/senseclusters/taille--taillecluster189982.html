taille

noun
French royal tax
Pronunciation: / taɪ / (AmE), / taɪ / (BrE)
a tax formerly imposed by the French monarchy on the common people before the French Revolution

Examples

French taille tax
The heavy taille was deeply resented by French peasants in the eighteenth century.
Many historians believe the taille played a role in causing the French Revolution.
Only the commoners were required to pay the taille, while the nobility were exempt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gabelle - a French tax on salt before the Revolution
  • synonym: poll tax - a tax levied as a fixed sum on every liable individual
