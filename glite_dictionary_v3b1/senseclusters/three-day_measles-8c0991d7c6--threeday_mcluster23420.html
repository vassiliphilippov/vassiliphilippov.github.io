three-day measles

a contagious viral disease, also known as rubella, that is a milder form of measles and usually lasts for three or four days; it can be harmful to a fetus if contracted during early pregnancy

Examples

Three-day measles rash
After developing a rash and fever, Mark was diagnosed with three-day measles by his pediatrician.
Many children were vaccinated against three-day measles as part of their routine immunizations.
The doctor explained that three-day measles is usually mild but can be serious during pregnancy.
