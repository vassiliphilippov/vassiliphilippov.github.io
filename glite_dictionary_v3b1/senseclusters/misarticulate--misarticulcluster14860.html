misarticulate

verb
to express unclearly
8.6
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ɑːr.ˈtɪk.jə.lət/ (AmE), /mɪs.ɑː.ˈtɪk.jə.lət/ (BrE)
to express or state something unclearly or ineffectively, making it difficult to understand.

Examples

To misarticulate an idea
He tended to misarticulate his thoughts when he was nervous during presentations.
The instructions were misarticulated, leading to confusion among the team members.
She realized she had misarticulated her feelings and tried to explain again more clearly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: articulate - to express ideas or feelings clearly
  • synonym: mumble - to speak in a way that is not clear
