banded rudderfish

noun
a fish species that follows ships
Pronunciation: / ˈbændɪd ˈrʌdərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd ˈrʌdəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a species of fish (Seriola zonata) known for following ships, found in coastal waters of North and South America

Examples

A banded rudderfish school
Fishermen spotted a school of banded rudderfish trailing their ship for miles.
The banded rudderfish often swims alongside boats near the coast.
Young banded rudderfish are known to seek shelter near floating debris or vessels.
