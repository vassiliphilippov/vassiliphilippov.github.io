agranulosis

noun
acute blood disorder with reduced granulocytes
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌɡræn.jəˈloʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / əˌɡran.jʊˈləʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
An acute blood disorder characterized by a severe reduction in granulocytes, often as a result of radiation or drug therapy.

Examples

Severe agranulosis condition
Chemotherapy can sometimes cause agranulosis, increasing a patient’s risk of infection.
Early detection of agranulosis is crucial to prevent severe complications.
The patient was closely monitored for signs of agranulosis after starting the new medication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agranulocytosis - an acute condition marked by the absence or extreme reduction of granulocytes in the blood
