guy cable

noun
cable or wire used for structural bracing
9.9 5.0
Pronunciation: /gaɪ ˈkeɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /gaɪ ˈkeɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
a strong cable or wire used to provide support and stability to structures by bracing them, especially tents, towers, or poles.

Examples

A guy cable supports
A strong wind snapped one guy cable, causing the pole to lean dangerously.
Always check each guy cable before pitching your tent to ensure it is secure.
The tall radio tower was held upright by several guy cables anchored to the ground.
