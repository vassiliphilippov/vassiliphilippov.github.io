voiceless consonant

noun
consonant produced without vocal cord vibration
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔɪsləs ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪslɪs ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced without vibration of the vocal cords.

Examples

A voiceless consonant sound
Children sometimes confuse 't' and 'd' because one is a voiceless consonant and the other is voiced.
Teachers often teach students the difference between voiceless consonants and their voiced counterparts.
The letter 'p' in 'pat' is a voiceless consonant in English.

Related Terms

  • antonym: voiced consonant - consonant produced with vibration of the vocal cords
