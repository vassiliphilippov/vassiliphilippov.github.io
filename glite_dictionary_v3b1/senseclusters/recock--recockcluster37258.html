recock

verb
to tilt again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkɑk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkɒk / (BrE)
to tilt or set something, especially the head, at an angle again, often to show attention or adjust posture

Examples

To recock your head
She had to recock her head to hear the quiet conversation behind her.
He paused to recock his hat after the wind blew it sideways.
The dog would recock its ears every time it heard a new sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight
  • synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
