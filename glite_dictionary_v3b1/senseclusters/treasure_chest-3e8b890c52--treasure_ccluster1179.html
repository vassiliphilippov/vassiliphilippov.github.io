treasure chest

noun
box for storing valuables or treasure
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛʒər tʃɛst /; / ˈtrɛʒɚ tʃɛst / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə tʃɛst / (BrE)
a large, strong box used to store money, jewels, or other valuable things, often thought of as being filled with treasure or used by pirates

Examples

Legend says a treasure chest is hidden somewhere in the old castle's basement.
The pirates buried their treasure chest on a deserted island before sailing away.
Children imagined finding a treasure chest full of gold at the end of their adventure.
She discovered an old treasure chest in the attic, filled with mysterious keepsakes.
The pirates buried the treasure chest on the remote island.
