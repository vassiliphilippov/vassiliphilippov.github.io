racemization

noun
loss of optical activity
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪsəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræsɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an optically active compound becomes a mixture containing equal amounts of its enantiomers, resulting in no optical activity

Examples

Amino acid racemization
The racemization of the amino acid reduced its effectiveness in the biological reaction.
Scientists studied the rate of racemization under different temperature conditions.
During storage, some drugs may undergo racemization, affecting their safety and potency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resolution - the process of separating a racemic mixture into its enantiomers
