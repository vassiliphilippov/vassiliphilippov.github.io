barbacan

noun
defensive tower in castle architecture
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːrbəkən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəkæn / (BrE)
a tower or outwork forming part of the defensive system of a castle or fortified place, typically positioned at a gate or entrance

Examples

A barbacan tower
Archaeologists uncovered the remains of a ruined barbacan near the fortress gate.
Many medieval fortifications featured a barbacan to guard their main entrances.
The old castle's barbacan overlooked the surrounding moat, providing an additional line of defense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barbican - main outwork or fortification defending a gate or bridge, especially in medieval castles
