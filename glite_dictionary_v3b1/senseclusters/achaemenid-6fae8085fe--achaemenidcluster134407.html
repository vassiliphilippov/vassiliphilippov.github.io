Achaemenid

proper noun
ancient Persian dynasty
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈkiːmənɪd / (AmE), / əˈkiːmənɪd / (BrE)
the ancient Persian dynasty and empire that ruled from about 550 to 330 BC, known for its vast territory and influence

Examples

The Achaemenid dynasty
The Achaemenid Empire was famous for its network of roads and postal system.
Many historians study the art and architecture of the Achaemenid period.
Darius the Great was one of the most notable rulers of the Achaemenid dynasty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Persian Empire - the empire ruled by the Persians, especially under the Achaemenid dynasty
  • antonym: Seleucid - the dynasty that succeeded the Achaemenids in Persia
