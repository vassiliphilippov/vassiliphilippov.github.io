demigoddess

noun
partly divine woman
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛmiˌɡɑdɪs /; / ˈdɛmiˌɡɑdəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛmiˌɡɒdɪs / (BrE)
a female being in mythology who is partly divine or a minor goddess, often with supernatural powers

Examples

Mythical demigoddess
The legend tells of a demigoddess who could control the winds and the sea.
Many ancient myths feature a powerful demigoddess as a central character.
The villagers prayed to the demigoddess for protection during the storm.
In ancient myths, a demigoddess often played the role of a protector of nature.
The villagers believed the mysterious woman was a demigoddess who blessed their crops.
Legends tell of a powerful demigoddess who could control the winds and the rain.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: mortal - a human being who is not divine
