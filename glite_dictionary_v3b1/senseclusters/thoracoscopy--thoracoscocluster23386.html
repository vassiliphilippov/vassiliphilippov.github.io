thoracoscopy

noun
chest examination procedure
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌθɔrəˈkɑskəpi / (AmE), / ˌθɔːrəˈkɒskəpi /; / ˌθɒrəˈkɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a thin tube with a camera (a thoracoscope) is inserted into the chest to examine or operate on the lungs or other organs

Examples

Thoracoscopy procedure
The surgeon used a thoracoscopy to inspect the patient's lungs for signs of disease.
After the thoracoscopy, the doctors were able to diagnose the cause of the chest pain.
A thoracoscopy is less invasive than traditional open chest surgery and often has a quicker recovery time.
The doctor recommended a thoracoscopy to investigate the cause of the patient's chest pain.
During the thoracoscopy, the surgeon was able to remove a small sample of lung tissue for testing.
Recovery time after a thoracoscopy is usually much shorter than after traditional open surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endoscopy - a procedure using a camera to look inside the body
  • synonym: laparoscopy - a procedure to examine the inside of the abdomen with a camera
  • antonym: thoracotomy - a surgical procedure involving an incision into the chest wall
