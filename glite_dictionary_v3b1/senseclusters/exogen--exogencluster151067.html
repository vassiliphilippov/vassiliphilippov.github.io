A woody flowering plant whose stem grows by adding layers on the outside and which has two cotyledons in the seed; a dicotyledon.

Examples

A exogen plant
Many familiar trees, such as the maple and elm, are examples of exogens.
The oak tree is classified as an exogen due to its method of stem growth.
Unlike an endogen, an exogen adds new wood to the outside of its trunk each year.
