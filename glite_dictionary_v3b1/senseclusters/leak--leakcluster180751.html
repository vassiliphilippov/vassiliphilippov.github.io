leak

verb
(of information) to become known
4.1 9.05/m
Pronunciation: / lik / (AmE), / liÀêk / (BrE)
to make secret or confidential information known to the public

Examples

To leak information
Government documents have sometimes leaked ahead of an official announcement.
Rumors about the new product leaked online last week.
The news quickly leaked to the public despite the company's best efforts.
After hours of speculation, the company found out who leaked their confidential plans.
Someone must have leaked the information to the press, as it was not meant for public knowledge.
The details of the government report were leaked to several newspapers last week.

Synonyms

  • divulge
    6.2 2.33/m
    divulge is used to tell private or secret facts to others, while leak means those facts become public without permission.
  • give away
    3.4 3.57/m
    give away often means revealing a secret by accident or carelessly, while leak means secret information reaches the public.
  • disclose
    4.1 13/m
    disclose is a formal act of making information known, often with permission, while leak implies unauthorized public release.
  • reveal
    2.3 134/m
    reveal can mean showing anything hidden or surprising, while leak specifically means confidential information becomes public.
  • expose
    4.1 21/m
    expose often shows wrongdoing or harm to the public, while leak simply means secret information becomes known to people.
  • uncover
    3.5 20/m
    uncover means to find or discover hidden facts, while leak refers to someone making secret information known to the public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden; not reveal
  • synonym: disclose - to make (secret or new information) known
  • synonym: reveal - to make something known that was secret
