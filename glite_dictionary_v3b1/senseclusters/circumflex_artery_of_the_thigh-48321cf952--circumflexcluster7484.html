circumflex artery of the thigh

noun
artery supplying blood to hip and thigh
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrkəmˌflɛks ˈɑrtəri əv ðə θaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌflɛks ˈɑːtəri əv ðə θaɪ / (BrE)
an artery supplying blood to the hip joint and thigh muscles, usually referring to either the medial or lateral circumflex femoral artery

Examples

The circumflex artery of the thigh
A blockage in the circumflex artery of the thigh may lead to reduced blood supply to the surrounding muscles.
During surgery, the circumflex artery of the thigh must be carefully preserved to avoid complications.
The patient's pain was traced to an injury near the circumflex artery of the thigh.
