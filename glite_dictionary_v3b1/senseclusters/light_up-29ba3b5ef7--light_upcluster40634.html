light up

verb
to become happy; to illuminate; to scold (informal)
4.1 2.79/m
Pronunciation: / laÉªt ËˆÊŒp / (AmE), / laÉªt ËˆÊŒp / (BrE)
1. (of a person's face or eyes) to become suddenly very happy or excited; 2. to make a place bright with light; 3. (informal) to reprimand or criticize someone strongly.

Examples

Her face lights up
Her eyes lit up when she saw the birthday cake.
If you keep talking back, the coach will light up at you during practice.
The city skyline lights up at night for the festival.

Synonyms

  • brighten up
    2.7 0.63/m
    brighten up makes something or someone more cheerful or brighter, while light up often means sudden happiness or making a place bright.
  • light
    1.0 54/m
    light means to start a fire or make something shine, while light up often adds suddenness or shows a face becoming very happy.
  • illuminate
    4.1 7.69/m
    illuminate means to shine light on and make something visible, while light up is more general and used in everyday speech.
  • glow
    1.0 5.77/m
    glow shows a steady, healthy brightness on someone's face, while light up describes a sudden bright smile or quick excitement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: darken - to make or become less bright
  • synonym: illuminate - to light up or make brighter
  • synonym: scold - to reprimand somebody angrily
