light up

verb
to become happy; to illuminate; to scold (informal)
Pronunciation: / laɪt ˈʌp / (AmE), / laɪt ˈʌp / (BrE)
1. (of a person's face or eyes) to become suddenly very happy or excited; 2. to make a place bright with light; 3. (informal) to reprimand or criticize someone strongly.

Examples

Her face lights up
Her eyes lit up when she saw the birthday cake.
If you keep talking back, the coach will light up at you during practice.
The city skyline lights up at night for the festival.

Related Terms

  • antonym: darken - to make or become less bright
  • synonym: illuminate - to light up or make brighter
  • synonym: scold - to reprimand somebody angrily
