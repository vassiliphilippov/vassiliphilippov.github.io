clawback

noun
reclaiming previously paid money
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈklɔːˌbak / (BrE)
the act of reclaiming money that has already been paid or awarded, usually by legal or regulatory means

Examples

A clawback provision
A clawback provision in the contract allowed the company to recover overpaid funds.
Many believe a clawback of improperly awarded tax credits is necessary to balance the budget.
The government imposed a clawback on bonuses awarded to executives involved in the scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recovery - the process of regaining possession or control of something lost
  • synonym: refund - money that is returned, especially to a customer who is not satisfied
