sweat

noun
hard work; effort
2.3 1.90/m
Pronunciation: / swÉ›t / (AmE), / swÉ›t / (BrE)
Physical or mental effort; hard work, especially when it is strenuous.

Examples

Hard sweat at work
After hours of sweat, they finally repaired the engine.
Getting accepted to the university took a lot of sweat and determination.
She put her heart and sweat into preparing for the competition.

Synonyms

  • exertion
    5.0 3.66/m
    exertion is strong physical or mental activity that uses energy, while sweat names hard work, often physical and tiring.
  • effort
    1.0 101/m
    effort means the attempt or energy used to do something, while sweat refers to hard, often strenuous work.
  • sweat of one's brow
    5.8 0.09/m
    sweat of one's brow is a phrase about earning a living through long hard work, while sweat names hard effort.
  • travail
    8.8 1.22/m
    travail is long, painful work that often feels like a struggle, while sweat is general hard or strenuous effort.
