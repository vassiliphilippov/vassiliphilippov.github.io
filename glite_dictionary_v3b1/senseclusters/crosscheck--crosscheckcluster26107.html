crosscheck

noun
illegal ice hockey check with stick
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑsˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
An illegal move in ice hockey or similar sports, where a player checks an opponent using the shaft of their stick held with both hands.

Examples

A hockey crosscheck
A deliberate crosscheck can result in a serious injury and suspension.
He was sent to the penalty box after delivering a hard crosscheck to his opponent.
The referee called a penalty for a crosscheck against the defenseman.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bodycheck - a legal move to block or stop an opponent in ice hockey
