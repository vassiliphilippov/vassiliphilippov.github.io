sanctify

verb
make pure or free from sin or guilt
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋktəˌfaɪ /; / ˈsæŋk.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaŋktɪfʌɪ /; / ˈsaŋk.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make someone or something holy or sacred, especially by a religious ceremony or blessing

Examples

To sanctify a place
Some rituals are performed to sanctify the place before important events.
The priest hoped to sanctify the ceremony by blessing the participants.
Through sincere repentance, she believed she could sanctify her soul.
Many cultures sanctify marriages through special rituals or vows.
The priest was called to sanctify the church before the opening ceremony.
They hope to sanctify their new home with a blessing from their spiritual leader.
The priest will sanctify the church with a special ceremony.
These waters are believed to sanctify those who bathe in them.
They sanctified the new altar before the festival began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bless - to invoke or bestow favor or holiness upon
  • antonym: defile - make dirty or impure, especially morally or spiritually
  • antonym: desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect
  • synonym: purify - make clean or free from impurities or guilt
