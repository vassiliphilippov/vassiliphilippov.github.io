establishment

noun
process of a species becoming established in a habitat
3.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt / (BrE)
(ecology) The process by which a plant or animal becomes successfully established in a new habitat.

Examples

Establishment of a species
Rainfall patterns play a vital role in the establishment of new plant communities.
The establishment of invasive species can drastically alter local ecosystems.
Tree establishment is critical to the success of reforestation projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act of a species colonizing a new area
  • antonym: extinction - the process of a species dying out
