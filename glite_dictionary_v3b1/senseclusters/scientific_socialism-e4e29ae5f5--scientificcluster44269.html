scientific socialism

noun
Marxian socialism based on historical and empirical principles
8.2 0.04/m

A form of socialism, chiefly associated with Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, which holds that social and economic development is determined by historical forces such as class struggle and economic determinism, often emphasizing a theoretical and empirical basis for achieving socialist goals.

Examples

Scientific socialism theory
His research paper explained the core principles of scientific socialism as laid out by Marx and Engels.
Many 20th-century revolutions claimed inspiration from the theory of scientific socialism.
The debate compared scientific socialism with utopian approaches to social change.

Synonyms

  • Marxism
    6.4 0.43/m
    Marxism presents broad political and economic theories about class struggle and communism, while scientific socialism treats these ideas as a systematic, theory-based path to social change.
