float

noun
delay between check deposit and payment
8.4
Pronunciation: / floʊt / (AmE), / fləʊt / (BrE)
the period of time between when a check is deposited and when the funds are actually withdrawn from the payer's account

Examples

Bank float
Banks often take advantage of the float to earn interest on deposited funds.
Because of the float, the check had not yet been deducted from her account.
The float period can vary depending on the banks involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clearance - the process of settling a check and transferring the funds
