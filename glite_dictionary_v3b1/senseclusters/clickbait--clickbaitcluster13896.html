clickbait

noun
sensational online headline
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪkˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɪkˌbeɪt / (BrE)
sensational or misleading online content or headlines designed to attract attention and encourage people to click

Examples

A clickbait headline
The website used clickbait to get more visitors, but the articles were disappointing.
She was annoyed by the amount of clickbait in her social media feed.
Many people fall for clickbait headlines that exaggerate the truth.
The article was just clickbait with a misleading headline and little real information.
Many social media users complain about the amount of clickbait in their news feeds.
He accidentally spent an hour reading clickbait instead of doing his homework.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bait-and-switch - a deceptive marketing tactic involving misleading offers
  • synonym: headline - the title of a news article or story
  • antonym: hoax - a trick or deception, often false information
  • synonym: misinformation - false or misleading information spread deliberately
  • antonym: news - factual reports about recent events
