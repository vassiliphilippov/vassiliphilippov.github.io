reconquest

noun
regaining lost territory
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkɑːŋkwɛst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkɒŋkwɛst / (BrE)
the act of regaining control, especially over land or territory, that was previously lost

Examples

The reconquest of land
The army planned a reconquest of the region that had been occupied for years.
After decades of foreign rule, the people dreamed of reconquest and freedom.
The history book describes the reconquest of the city in vivid detail.
The army planned a reconquest of the territory that had been lost in the previous war.
After years of decline, the movement began its reconquest of cultural influence in the region.
The reconquest of the city was celebrated as a major victory by the returning leaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conquest - the act of taking control of a place by force
  • antonym: loss - the state of no longer having something
  • synonym: recapture - the act of taking something back by force
