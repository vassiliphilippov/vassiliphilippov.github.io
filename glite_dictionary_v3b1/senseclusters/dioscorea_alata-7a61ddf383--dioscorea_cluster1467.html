Dioscorea alata

noun
a cultivated yam species with edible tubers
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˌɑskɔːriə əˈlɑːtə / (AmE), / daɪˌɒskɔːriə əˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
a species of yam native to Southeast Asia and widely cultivated for its large, edible, starchy tuber with white or sometimes purple flesh

Examples

Dioscorea alata tuber
Dioscorea alata is commonly known as the purple yam in many Southeast Asian cuisines.
Farmers in Polynesia have long relied on Dioscorea alata for its high-yielding, nutritious tubers.
The distinctive color of some varieties of Dioscorea alata makes them popular in desserts and ice cream.

Related Terms
