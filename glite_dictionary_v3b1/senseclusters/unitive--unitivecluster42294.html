unitive

adjective
regarding as one unit
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈjuː.nɪ.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈjuː.nɪ.tɪv/ (BrE)
describing something that treats or regards a group as a single unit rather than as separate individuals

Examples

Unitive approach
The unitive approach in grammar treats the team as a single entity.
A unitive reading of the law considers the organization as one body, not as separate members.
In some cultures, a unitive perspective is preferred over an individualistic one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collective - describing a group acting as a whole
  • antonym: individualistic - emphasizing individuals rather than the group as a whole
