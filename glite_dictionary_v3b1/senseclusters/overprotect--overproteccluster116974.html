overprotect

verb
protect someone excessively
1.0 4.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.prə.ˈtekt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.prə.ˈtekt/ (BrE)
to protect someone too much, in a way that stops them from facing difficulties, taking risks, or becoming independent

Examples

To overprotect a child
He accused his sister of trying to overprotect him after his accident.
If you overprotect your pets, they may become anxious in new situations.
Some parents tend to overprotect their children, not allowing them to walk to school alone.
Some parents tend to overprotect their children, not allowing them to take any risks.
Teachers can overprotect students by not letting them solve problems on their own.
You shouldn't overprotect your dog; a little dirt won't hurt him.
