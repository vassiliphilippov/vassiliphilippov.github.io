sound spectrum

noun
distribution of sound energy by frequency
8.5
Pronunciation: / saʊnd ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the distribution of the intensity or energy of sound as a function of frequency for a particular sound source

Examples

The sound spectrum analysis
Engineers analyzed the sound spectrum to identify the cause of the noise.
The sound spectrum of a violin is different from that of a flute.
You can use software to visualize the sound spectrum of your recorded voice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frequency spectrum - the overall distribution of signal intensity by frequency, often used in electronics and physics
