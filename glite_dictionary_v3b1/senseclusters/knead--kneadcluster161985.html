knead

verb
press or massage someone's body
4.3 0.76/m
Pronunciation: / nid / (AmE), / niÀêd / (BrE)
to press, rub, or squeeze someone's body, especially with the hands, in order to relax muscles or relieve pain

Examples

To knead sore muscles
During the session, he used his strong hands to knead the knots out of my back.
She gently kneaded her grandmother's stiff muscles until they relaxed.
The therapist began to knead my shoulders to help ease the tension.

Synonyms

  • massage
    3.3 3.47/m
    massage uses smooth rhythmic rubbing and light pressure to relax muscles and improve blood flow, while knead means pressing and squeezing muscles more deeply.
  • rub down
    4.1 0.07/m
    rub down often means a quick, light massage or cleaning after exercise, while knead means pressing and squeezing muscles more deeply to relieve pain.
