emerge

verb
to happen or appear as a result
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmɜːrdʒ/ /; / /ˈiː.mərdʒ/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmɜːdʒ/ /; / /iː.ˈmɜːdʒ/ (BrE)
to become known, apparent, or come into existence (such as an idea, fact, or situation)

Examples

New facts emerge
A consensus is gradually emerging among industry leaders about the need for reform.
Several new problems have emerged since we last met to discuss the project.
The idea first emerged as a response to the economic crisis.
After hours of discussion, a clear solution finally emerged from the group.
Details about the new technology began to emerge during the conference.
Over time, new facts emerged that changed everyone's understanding of the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appear - to become visible or come into view
  • synonym: arise - to occur as a result of, to come into being
  • synonym: result - to happen as a consequence
  • antonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
