Communist Party of Kampuchea

proper noun
Cambodian communist party (Khmer Rouge regime)
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjunɪst ˈpɑrti əv kɑmˈpuːtʃiə / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪst ˈpɑːti ɒv kæmˈpuːtʃɪə / (BrE)
the communist political party that ruled Cambodia between 1975 and 1979, responsible for the Khmer Rouge regime and the deaths of millions; active until 1999

Examples

The Communist Party of Kampuchea regime
After being overthrown in 1979, the Communist Party of Kampuchea continued its insurgency in remote parts of Cambodia.
Led by Pol Pot, the Communist Party of Kampuchea sought to transform Cambodian society through radical and violent means.
The Communist Party of Kampuchea seized power in Cambodia in 1975, leading to a devastating period in the country's history.
