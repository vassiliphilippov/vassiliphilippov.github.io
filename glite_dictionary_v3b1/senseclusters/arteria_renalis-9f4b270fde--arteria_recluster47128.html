arteria renalis

noun
artery supplying the kidneys
7.2
Pronunciation: / ɑrˌtɪəriə rɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriə rɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
the artery that branches from the abdominal aorta and supplies blood to the kidneys, adrenal glands, and ureters

Examples

The arteria renalis artery
A blockage in the arteria renalis can lead to hypertension and reduced kidney function.
In kidney transplants, the arteria renalis must be carefully connected to ensure proper blood flow.
The surgeon identified the arteria renalis before proceeding with the procedure.
