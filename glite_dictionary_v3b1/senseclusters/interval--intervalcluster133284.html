interval

noun
difference in pitch between notes (music)
Pronunciation: / ɪn.tɚ.vəl / (AmE), / ɪn.tə.vəl / (BrE)
(Music) The difference in pitch between two notes.

Examples

A musical interval
A major third is a musical interval commonly found in chords.
The interval of a perfect fifth is heard at the beginning of 'Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star.'
The teacher asked us to identify the interval between C and G.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: octave - an interval spanning eight notes in diatonic scale
  • degree lower: semitone - an interval of a half step between notes
  • degree higher: tone - an interval of a whole step between notes
