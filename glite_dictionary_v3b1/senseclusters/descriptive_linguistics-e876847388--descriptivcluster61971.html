descriptive linguistics

noun
study of a language's structure without judgments
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskrɪptɪv lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the study and systematic description of a language's structure and usage at a particular point in time, without making value judgments

Examples

Descriptive linguistics study
In descriptive linguistics, the goal is to record how people actually use language, not how they should use it.
Many modern grammars are based on the methods of descriptive linguistics.
She conducted fieldwork in descriptive linguistics to document endangered languages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prescriptive linguistics - the study of language that sets rules or standards for correct usage
  • synonym: structural linguistics - a branch of linguistics analyzing language as a system of structurally related elements
