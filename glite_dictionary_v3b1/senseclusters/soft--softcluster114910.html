soft

adjective
diffuse, gentle light; not harsh
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɑft /; / sɔft / (AmE), / sɒft / (BrE)
gentle or mild; not harsh, strong, or intense (used for light, color, sound, weather, or action)

Examples

Soft light
Morning sunlight filtered through the curtains, filling the room with a soft glow.
She placed a sheet over the lamp to make the lighting soft and pleasant for reading.
The photographer preferred to use soft light to create a natural, flattering look.
Please sing this verse soft to create a gentle atmosphere.
The conductor asked the orchestra to play the next section soft.
The music gradually shifted from loud to soft during the performance.
A soft knock at the door caught his attention.
She spoke in a soft voice so as not to disturb the others.
The room was filled with the soft sounds of classical music.
