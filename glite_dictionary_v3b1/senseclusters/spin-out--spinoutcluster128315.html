spin-out

verb
to create new company
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪn aʊt / (AmE), / ˈspɪn aʊt / (BrE)
to create a new independent company from part of a larger organization, often to commercialize technology or research

Examples

To spin-out a business
The university plans to spin-out a new company to commercialize the discovery.
Several tech firms have spun-out innovative startups from their research divisions.
The board decided to spin-out the software unit as an independent business.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divest - to sell off part of a business or investment
  • antonym: merge - to combine two or more companies into one
