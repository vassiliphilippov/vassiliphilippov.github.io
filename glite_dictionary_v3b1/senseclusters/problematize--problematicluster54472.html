problematize

verb
to treat as a problem
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːbləˌmətaɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɒbləˌmaɪz /; / ˈprɒbləˌmətaɪz / (BrE)
to present or consider something as a problem or as something that should be critically discussed or analyzed

Examples

To problematize social issues
The documentary aims to problematize the way society views mental health.
Some critics problematize the use of technology in modern classrooms.
Her research problematizes the assumption that all cultures value independence equally.
The professor encouraged students to problematize the assumptions behind the research.
By problematizing common beliefs, the author invites readers to think more critically.
Some critics argue that problematizing every issue can make solutions harder to find.
The author aims to problematize the common assumptions about gender roles in society.
Some critics problematize the use of technology in modern education.
The documentary problematizes the idea that economic growth always leads to happiness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: question - to express doubt about or challenge something
