hidden tax

noun
a tax paid unknowingly by consumers
1.9 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪdən tæks / (AmE), / ˈhɪdən tæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed on goods or services that is not obvious to the consumer, so it is paid without the consumer's direct awareness, such as sales tax included in the purchase price.

Examples

A hidden tax in price
Many consumers are unaware of the hidden tax embedded in the price of gasoline.
Sales tax included in the price tag is a common example of a hidden tax.
The government sometimes raises revenue through a hidden tax rather than increasing income tax.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indirect tax - a tax levied on goods or services rather than income or profits
