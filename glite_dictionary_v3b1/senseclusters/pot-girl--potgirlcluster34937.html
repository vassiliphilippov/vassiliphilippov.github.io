pot-girl

noun
female servant in an inn or pub
8.5 9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːt.gɜːrl/ (AmE), /ˈpɒt.gɜːl/ (BrE)
a female worker in an inn or public house who serves customers and does various chores, especially related to cleaning pots and utensils

Examples

The pot-girl serves
After a long day, the pot-girl was allowed a simple meal by the fireplace.
In Victorian novels, the pot-girl is often depicted as the lowest in the household hierarchy.
The pot-girl rose before dawn to clean the inn’s kitchen and scrub the pots.
