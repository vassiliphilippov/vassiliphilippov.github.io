mola

noun
large ocean-dwelling fish; ocean sunfish
8.1 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.lə/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.lə/ (BrE)
A very large, ocean-dwelling fish with an oval, laterally compressed body, high dorsal and anal fins, and a rudder-like tail; found in warm seas worldwide, also known as the ocean sunfish.

Examples

A mola fish
A mola's unusual shape and massive size make it an unforgettable sight for divers.
Researchers tagged a mola to better understand its long-distance migrations in tropical waters.
The mola can weigh over a thousand kilograms and is often seen basking near the surface of the ocean.

  • synonym: ocean sunfish - A common English name for the mola, a large pelagic fish with a distinctive flattened body
