Salix sericea

noun
North American willow species
Pronunciation: / ˈsælɪks səˈriːsiə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks sɛˈrɪsɪə / (BrE)
A species of North American willow with greyish silky-hairy leaves that often turn black when dried.

Examples

Salix sericea leaves
Botanists often collect samples of Salix sericea along riverbanks for ecological studies.
The leaves of Salix sericea are covered with fine, silky hairs, giving them a grayish appearance.
When dried, the foliage of Salix sericea tends to turn black, which is distinctive among willows.
