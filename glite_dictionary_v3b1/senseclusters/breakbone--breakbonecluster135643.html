breakbone

noun
bone-breaker
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌbəʊn / (BrE)
a person or thing that breaks bones, or is known for causing bone-breaking injuries, often used figuratively or in older texts

Examples

A breakbone fighter
The old pugilist earned a reputation as a breakbone feared by opponents in the ring.
In the harsh winter, the icy roads became a real breakbone for careless travelers.
Legends spoke of a giant known as the breakbone who could shatter stones with his fists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bruiser - a strong, tough person, especially a boxer or fighter
  • antonym: gentle - not harsh or violent; mild in action
