continental glacier

noun
enormous glacier covering much of a continent
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.tənˈen.t̬əl ˈɡleɪ.ʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnen.təl ˈɡleɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
a very large glacier that covers a significant part of a continent and spreads outward from a central region

Examples

A continental glacier spreads
During the last Ice Age, a continental glacier extended over much of North America.
Much of Antarctica is covered by a vast continental glacier that holds most of the world's fresh water.
The movement of a continental glacier can dramatically reshape the landscape beneath it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ice sheet - a mass of glacier ice covering an area of more than 50,000 square kilometers
  • antonym: valley glacier - a glacier that forms in a mountain valley and flows down through it
