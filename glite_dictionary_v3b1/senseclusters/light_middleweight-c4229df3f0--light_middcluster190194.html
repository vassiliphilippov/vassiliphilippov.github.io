light middleweight

noun
boxing or martial arts weight division
2.9 4.3
Pronunciation: / laɪt ˈmɪdəlˌweɪt / (AmE), / laɪt ˈmɪdəlweɪt / (BrE)
a weight division in boxing and other combat sports for competitors who are heavier than welterweights but lighter than middleweights (in amateur boxing, up to 156 pounds or 71 kg)

Examples

After months of training, he finally moved up to the light middleweight division.

He won gold in the light middleweight category at the national championships.
She trained hard to stay within the light middleweight limit before the tournament.
The light middleweight division has produced many talented amateur boxers over the years.
