waterlogged

adjective
saturated with water (of soil or land)
5.1 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər.lɑːgd/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər.lɑːgd/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə.lɒgd/ (BrE)
(of land or soil) saturated with water, making it soft and difficult to walk or grow plants on.

Examples

A waterlogged field
After heavy rain, the football pitch became completely waterlogged.
Hiking was impossible because the path was waterlogged and muddy.
The fields were so waterlogged that planting had to be postponed for another week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boggy - (of ground) soft and wet like a bog
  • synonym: swampy - (of land) resembling a swamp; very wet and soft
