ropey

adjective
forming viscous or thread-like filaments
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊpi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpi / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) forming viscous or glutinous threads.

Examples

Ropey milk
Some bacteria can cause a milk sample to become ropey as it spoils.
The cheese had turned ropey after being left out overnight.
The scientist described the texture of the slime as unusually ropey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency
