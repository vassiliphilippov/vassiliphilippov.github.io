inert

noun
nonreactive substance
7.1 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / …™Ààn…úrt / (AmE), / …™Ààn…úÀêt / (BrE)
a substance that does not react easily with other substances, often used to prevent chemical reactions

Examples

An inert in storage
The laboratory stores inert in sealed containers to avoid contamination.
Helium is a common inert used in scientific experiments.
Adding an inert can help prevent unwanted chemical reactions during the process.

Synonyms

  • inactive
    6.0 0.22/m
    inactive describes a substance that does not take part in reactions, while inert often means it resists or prevents reactions.
  • unreactive
    6.3 0.01/m
    unreactive refers to a substance that does not tend to react under given conditions, while inert often means it prevents reactions.
  • nonreactive
    4.6 0.08/m
    nonreactive describes a substance that shows no change when exposed to others, while inert is often used to stop chemical reactions.
  • neutral
    5.2 0.42/m
    neutral means it has little chemical activity and can mean neither acid nor base, while inert is used to stop reactions.
