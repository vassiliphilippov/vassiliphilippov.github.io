inert

noun
nonreactive substance
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɜrt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt / (BrE)
a substance that does not react easily with other substances, often used to prevent chemical reactions

Examples

An inert in storage
The laboratory stores inert in sealed containers to avoid contamination.
Helium is a common inert used in scientific experiments.
Adding an inert can help prevent unwanted chemical reactions during the process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction
  • synonym: inert gas - a gas that does not react easily with other substances
