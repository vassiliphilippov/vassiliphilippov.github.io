look up to

verb
admire or respect someone
Pronunciation: / lʊk ʌp tu / (AmE), / lʊk ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
to admire and respect someone, often seeing them as a role model

Examples

To look up to a hero
I have always looked up to my older brother for his kindness and strength.
Many young athletes look up to Serena Williams as a role model.
She hopes her students will look up to her as a positive influence.
It feels good when someone says they look up to you.
Many young musicians look up to their idols for inspiration.
She has always looked up to her grandmother for strength and wisdom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: look down on - to regard (someone) with a feeling of superiority
