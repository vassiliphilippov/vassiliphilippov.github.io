fill someone's shoes

verb
take over someone's role or duties
2.3
Pronunciation: / fɪl ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / fɪl ˈsʌmwənz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to take over someone's position, duties, or responsibilities, often with the implication that it is difficult to perform as well as they did

Examples

To fill someone's shoes well
After the CEO resigned, it took months to find someone to fill her shoes.
As the new captain, he knew he would have to fill some big shoes.
When their lead designer left, no one was quite ready to fill his shoes.
