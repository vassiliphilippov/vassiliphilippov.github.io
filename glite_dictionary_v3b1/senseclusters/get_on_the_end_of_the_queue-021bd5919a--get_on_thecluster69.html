get on the end of the queue

verb
join the back of a queue
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɑn ði ɛnd əv ðə kju / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ði ɛnd əv ðə kjuː / (BrE)
to join a line of people at its furthest point from the front

Examples

To get on the end of the queue
He sighed, realizing he'd have to get on the end of the queue for coffee.
If you're waiting for tickets, please get on the end of the queue like everyone else.
Tourists were told to get on the end of the queue outside the museum entrance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cut in line - to move ahead in a queue unfairly; to skip to the front
  • synonym: join the queue - to join a line of people waiting for something
