imagery

noun
descriptive language
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (BrE)
descriptive language that appeals to the senses and creates vivid mental pictures (especially in literature)

Examples

Poetic imagery
The author's use of imagery made the forest seem alive with color and sound.
Artists often rely on imagery to express emotions that are hard to describe with words.
The vivid imagery in the novel helped readers picture every scene clearly.
The author uses rich imagery to help readers picture the setting of the story.
Her poems are filled with natural imagery that appeals to all five senses.
Teachers often encourage students to use imagery in their creative writing assignments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: figurative language - language that uses figures of speech to be more effective or persuasive
  • antonym: literal language - language that means exactly what it says, without embellishment
