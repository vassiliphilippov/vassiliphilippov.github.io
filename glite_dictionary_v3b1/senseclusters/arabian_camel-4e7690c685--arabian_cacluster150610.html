Arabian camel

noun
one-humped camel of North Africa and Asia
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈreɪ.bɪ.ən ˈkæm.əl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈreɪ.bɪ.ən ˈkæm.əl/ (BrE)
a large camel native to northern Africa and southwestern Asia, with a single hump on its back, adapted to hot desert environments

Examples

The Arabian camel desert
The Arabian camel is perfectly adapted to survive long periods without water in the desert.
Traders in ancient times relied on the Arabian camel for transporting goods across the Sahara.
Unlike its two-humped cousin, the Arabian camel has only a single hump.
