karpas

noun
Passover ritual vegetable
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈpɑs / (AmE), / kɑːˈpɑːs / (BrE)
a green vegetable, such as parsley or celery, used in the Passover Seder ritual and dipped in salt water

Examples

Passover karpas ritual
At the beginning of the Seder, each guest dips karpas into salt water as part of the tradition.
The family placed fresh parsley as karpas on the Seder plate before the meal began.
Children enjoy searching for the karpas and learning about its meaning during Passover.
