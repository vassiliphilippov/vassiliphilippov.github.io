court of appeals

noun
a court that reviews lower court decisions
5.0 1.90/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrt …ôv …ôÀàpi…ôlz / (AmE), / k…îÀêt …ôv …ôÀàpiÀêlz / (BrE)
A court that has the authority to review and overturn decisions made by lower courts or agencies.

Examples

The court of appeals
A decision by the court of appeals can set important legal precedents.
After losing the initial case, she took her complaint to the court of appeals.
The court of appeals overturned the verdict after reviewing new evidence.

Synonyms

  • appellate court
    7.4 1.35/m
    an appellate court is a formal legal body that reviews lower court decisions, while a court of appeals is the legal name for that body.
  • appeals court
    4.4 2.49/m
    an appeals court hears appeals from lower courts in everyday speech, while a court of appeals is the more formal legal term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appellate court - a court having the power to review judgments from lower courts
