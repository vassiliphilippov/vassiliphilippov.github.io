seep

verb
pass or leak slowly through small openings
2.7 7.15/m
Pronunciation: / siÀêp / (AmE), / siÀêp / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to pass slowly or leak through small openings or pores.

Examples

To seep slowly
Oil can seep from old machinery and contaminate the ground.
The news of their engagement slowly seeped into the community over the weekend.
Water began to seep through the cracks in the basement walls after the heavy rain.

Synonyms

  • ooze
    3.9 4.24/m
    ooze leaks out slowly and often looks thick or visible, while seep moves slowly through tiny holes or pores.
  • percolate
    6.4 0.41/m
    percolate makes liquid move slowly through many tiny spaces in a material, while seep means leaking through small openings.
  • trickle
    3.4 5.63/m
    trickle flows in small drops or a thin stream, often down a surface, while seep moves slowly through pores.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leak - (of a liquid or gas) to escape through a hole or crack
  • synonym: percolate - (of a liquid) to filter gradually through a porous surface
