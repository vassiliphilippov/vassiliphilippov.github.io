expedient

noun
means to an end (often unethical)
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspidiənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspiːdiənt / (BrE)
a means of achieving a particular end, especially one that is convenient but possibly improper or immoral

Examples

A temporary expedient
As a temporary expedient, they arranged a meeting online rather than in person.
He took the easiest expedient rather than the most ethical solution.
The government saw the curfew as a necessary expedient to restore order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: device - something devised or contrived, especially for a specific purpose
  • synonym: stratagem - a plan or scheme, especially one used to outwit an opponent
