rebound tenderness

noun
pain on abdominal pressure release (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈriˌbaʊnd ˈtɛndərnəs / (AmE), / ˈriːbaʊnd ˈtɛndənəs / (BrE)
Pain felt when pressure applied to the abdomen is suddenly released, especially a sign of peritoneal inflammation.

Examples

Rebound tenderness in abdomen
Rebound tenderness is often considered a classic sign in cases of acute appendicitis.
The doctor checked for rebound tenderness to rule out peritonitis.
The patient experienced significant rebound tenderness when the physician removed his hand from the abdomen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guarding - involuntary tensing of the abdominal muscles to protect inflamed organs
  • synonym: rigidity - stiffness or inflexibility of the abdominal muscles, a sign of inflammation
