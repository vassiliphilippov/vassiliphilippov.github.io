Synonyms

  • almost
    1.0 466/m
    almost is the common everyday word for something not complete, while nearly sounds slightly more formal and fits broader contexts.
  • virtually
    3.6 51/m
    virtually means in practice almost complete or true, while nearly is the plain adverb used to say something is not quite finished.
  • just about
    2.7 39/m
    just about sounds conversational and means almost exactly, while nearly is a neutral adverb used in both speech and formal writing.
  • practically
    2.5 26/m
    practically often means in real use something is almost true for practical reasons, while nearly only says it is close.
  • more or less
    2.1 21/m
    more or less gives a rough estimate and sounds informal, while nearly says something is almost exact and closer to the truth.
