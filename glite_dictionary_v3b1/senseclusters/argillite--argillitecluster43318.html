argillite

noun
a compact sedimentary rock of clay
5.4 9.3
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ˈdʒɪ.laɪt/ (AmE), /ɑː.ˈdʒɪ.laɪt/ (BrE)
a fine-grained sedimentary rock composed mainly of hardened clay particles, distinguished from shale by its lack of fissility and from slate by its absence of cleavage

Examples

A argillite rock
Geologists identified the outcrop as argillite due to its compact, non-cleaving structure.
The museum display included a large piece of argillite carved by indigenous artists.
Unlike slate, argillite does not break into thin sheets, making it more durable for some uses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shale - a fine-grained sedimentary rock that splits easily into thin layers
  • synonym: slate - a fine-grained metamorphic rock that splits easily into thin smooth layers
