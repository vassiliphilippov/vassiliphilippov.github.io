tiddle

verb
to fidget with
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪd.əl/ (AmE), /ˈtɪd.əl/ (BrE)
to move, touch, or play with something in a light, idle, or playful way

Examples

To tiddle with a pen
He would often tiddle with his pen during long meetings at work.
Stop tiddling with your phone and pay attention to the lesson.
She nervously tiddled with her necklace while waiting for the results.
She tiddled the puppy's nose, making it wiggle with delight.
The child liked to tiddle the bubbles floating in the air.
He gently tiddled the keys on the piano before starting to play.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fidget - to make small movements, especially with the hands, due to restlessness
  • synonym: poke - to touch or prod something quickly with a finger or object
  • antonym: still - to stop moving or remain motionless
  • synonym: stroke - to move your hand gently over something
  • synonym: twiddle - to turn or play with something using the fingers, often absentmindedly
