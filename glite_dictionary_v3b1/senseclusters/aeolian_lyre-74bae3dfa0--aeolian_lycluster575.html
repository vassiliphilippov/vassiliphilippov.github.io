aeolian lyre

noun
wind-played harp-like instrument
8.5
Pronunciation: / iˈoʊliən ˈlaɪər / (AmE), / iːˈəʊliən ˈlaɪə / (BrE)
A stringed instrument, similar to a harp, with strings all tuned to the same note, designed to produce sound when the wind passes over them.

Examples

An aeolian lyre instrument
He installed an aeolian lyre in his garden to enjoy the music of the wind.
In romantic poetry, the aeolian lyre often symbolizes inspiration from nature.
The aeolian lyre creates haunting melodies as the breeze moves through its strings.

Related Terms
