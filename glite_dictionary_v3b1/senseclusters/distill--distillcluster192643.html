distill

verb
extract by distillation
6.5
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
to purify or extract a liquid by heating it to make vapor and then cooling it to collect the liquid, or to undergo this process

Examples

To distill pure water
The laboratory will distill pure water from seawater for the experiment.
The process uses heat to distill alcohol from the fermented mixture.
They distill essential oils from lavender flowers using steam.
After fermentation, the mixture must distill for several hours to produce the final spirit.
During the experiment, the solution began to distill as we applied gentle heat.
Some essential oils distill naturally when exposed to the right conditions.
After the experiment, the apparatus began to distill small amounts of clear fluid.
Some plants distill moisture onto their leaves overnight.
The laboratory device was designed to distill water vapor from the mixture.
