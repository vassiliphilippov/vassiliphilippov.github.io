unwarily

adverb
without caution or care
7.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàw…õr…™li / (AmE), /  ånÀàw…õÀêr…™li / (BrE)
Without caution or care; in a manner that is not alert to danger or risk.

Examples

To unwarily trust strangers
Unwarily trusting strangers can sometimes lead to trouble.
He answered the tricky question unwarily, giving away more than he intended.
She walked unwarily into the trap, never suspecting a thing.

Synonyms

  • incautiously
    7.0 0.05/m
    incautiously is done without caution or prudence, often taking visible risks, while unwarily means not alert to specific danger.
  • heedlessly
    6.5 0.19/m
    heedlessly means acting without regard for possible consequences, while unwarily focuses on not being alert to danger.
  • carelessly
    1.0 2.35/m
    carelessly shows lack of attention or concern in many tasks, while unwarily stresses not noticing or sensing danger.
  • unthinkingly
    5.8 0.33/m
    unthinkingly means doing something without thinking first, while unwarily means doing it without being alert to danger.
