omnisciently

adverb
with complete knowledge
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɑmˈnɪʃəntli / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪʃəntli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows complete or unlimited knowledge of all things.

Examples

Told omnisciently in story
The story is told omnisciently, revealing every character's private thoughts and feelings.
She seemed to speak omnisciently about events she could not possibly have witnessed.
The historian wrote omnisciently, as if he had been present at every important moment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: all-knowing - having complete or unlimited knowledge
  • antonym: ignorantly - in a manner showing lack of knowledge
