pour out

verb
emerge or flow in large numbers
2.9 1.38/m
Pronunciation: / p…îr a ät / (AmE), / p…îÀê a ät / (BrE)
(of people or things) to emerge or flow out in large numbers or amounts, usually in a continuous stream.

Examples

Crowds pour out
As the bell rang, students poured out of their classrooms and filled the hallways.
Hundreds of fans began to pour out of the stadium after the match ended.
When the doors finally opened, a crowd poured out into the bright sunlight.

Synonyms

  • flow out
    2.9 1.00/m
    flow out moves outward in a steady stream, while pour out often means a large, continuous rush of people or things.
  • stream
    1.7 5.66/m
    stream flows continuously like a river or crowd, while pour out usually suggests a heavy, directed flow from a place.
  • spill out
    3.6 2.04/m
    spill out moves out suddenly and often by accident, while pour out describes a steady, large flow from a place.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spill out - to come out in a rush or stream, often used for crowds or things overflowing
