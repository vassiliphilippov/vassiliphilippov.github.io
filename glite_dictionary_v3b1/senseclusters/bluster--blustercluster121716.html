bluster

verb
blow violently (wind)
1.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈblʌs.tər/ (AmE), /ˈblʌs.tə/ (BrE)
(of wind or weather) to blow violently and with loud noise; to be gusty or turbulent.

Examples

Winds bluster outside
The north wind continued to bluster throughout the night, rattling the windows.
Thunderclouds gathered as the storm began to bluster across the coast.
Waves crashed against the pier while the wind blustered through the harbor.
