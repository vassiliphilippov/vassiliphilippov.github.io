Sirenia

noun
order of aquatic mammals
Pronunciation: / saɪˈriːniə / (AmE), / saɪˈriːniə / (BrE)
an order of large, plant-eating aquatic mammals including manatees, dugongs, and the extinct Steller's sea cow

Examples

The order Sirenia
Extant members of Sirenia are found in warm coastal waters around the world.
Scientists believe that Sirenia evolved from terrestrial mammals millions of years ago.
The order Sirenia is distinguished by its paddle-like flippers and herbivorous diet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manatee - a large, plant-eating aquatic mammal of tropical waters
