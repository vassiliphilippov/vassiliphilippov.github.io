nymphalid

noun
a butterfly of the Nymphalidae family
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪmfəlɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɪmfəlɪd / (BrE)
A butterfly of a large family (Nymphalidae), typically with brightly colored wings and reduced, nonfunctional forelegs folded on the breast.

Examples

A nymphalid butterfly
Many nymphalid butterflies are admired for their striking patterns and vibrant colors.
The forelegs of a typical nymphalid are small and not used for walking.
The monarch is a well-known nymphalid found throughout North America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butterfly - an insect with two pairs of large, colorful wings
