triumvirate

noun
group of three joint rulers or officials
7.4
Pronunciation: / traɪˈʌmvɪˌreɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈʌmvɪərət / (BrE)
A group of three people, especially men, holding power or office jointly, particularly in certain historical contexts.

Examples

A triumvirate of leaders
A new triumvirate emerged to guide the project through its next phase.
In the company’s early days, decision-making was handled by a triumvirate of founders.
The Roman Republic was once ruled by a triumvirate of powerful leaders.
