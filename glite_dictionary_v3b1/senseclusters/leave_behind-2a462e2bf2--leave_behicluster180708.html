leave behind

verb
be survived by after death
Pronunciation: / liv bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / liːv bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person) to be survived by (people, possessions, etc.) after one's death.

Examples

Leave behind a family
She sadly left behind her beloved children and a devoted husband.
The late professor left behind a vast collection of rare books and manuscripts.
When he died unexpectedly, he left behind many friends who cherished his kindness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: survive - continue to live after someone else's death
