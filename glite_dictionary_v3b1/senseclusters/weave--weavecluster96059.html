weave

verb
move or sway from side to side
3.3 2.4
Pronunciation: /wiːv/ (AmE), /wiːv/ (BrE)
to move in a winding or side-to-side way, especially to avoid obstacles

Examples

To weave through traffic
As she hurried down the crowded hallway, she weaved from side to side.
The cyclist had to weave through heavy traffic during rush hour.
The dog began to weave across the path, sniffing at different spots.
As the parade moved forward, the performers began to weave between the floats.
The cyclist had to weave through heavy traffic to reach his destination on time.
The narrow road weaves its way up the hillside, offering stunning views.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meander - to follow a winding or intricate course
  • synonym: swerve - to turn aside abruptly from a straight course
  • synonym: wander - to move about without a fixed course
  • synonym: wind - to follow a curving or twisting course
  • synonym: zigzag - to move forward by making sharp turns first to one side and then to the other
