chortle

verb
laugh quietly or with restraint
9.7 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃɔːr.təl/ /; / /ˈtʃɔːr.əl/ (AmE), /ˈtʃɔː.təl/ (BrE)
to laugh in a joyful, gleeful, or quiet way, often with a snort or chuckle

Examples

To chortle with delight
He chortled quietly as he read the amusing text message.
Samantha couldn't help but chortle at the comedian's clever punchline.
The children chortled with delight as the puppy chased its tail.
The children chortled with delight when they saw the puppy do a trick.
She could not help but chortle at his clever joke during the meeting.
He chortled softly as he read the amusing message on his phone.
The children chortled with delight when they saw the puppy's funny tricks.
He couldn't help but chortle at the comedian's clever joke.
She chortles every time she remembers that silly story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chuckle - to laugh quietly in a restrained manner
  • antonym: cry - to shed tears, often from sadness
  • synonym: giggle - to laugh in a light, silly way
  • degree higher: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously
  • synonym: snicker - to laugh quietly in a partly suppressed way
  • antonym: sob - to cry noisily, making loud, convulsive gasps
