delisting

noun
removal from a list
6.4
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈlɪ.stɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈlɪ.stɪŋ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈlɪ.stɪŋ/ /; / /diː.ˈlɪ.stɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of removing something (such as a company, stock, medicine, or building) from an official list or register, especially when a company's shares are no longer traded on a stock exchange

Examples

Delisting from exchange
The company faced delisting from the stock exchange after failing to meet requirements.
After the delisting of the drug, patients had to look for alternative treatments.
The historic building's delisting sparked debate among preservationists and city officials.
The sudden delisting of the company surprised many investors and analysts.
After failing to meet the exchange's requirements, the firm faced delisting from the market.
Shareholders were notified about the upcoming delisting and its possible effects on their investments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deregistration - the process of removing something from an official register
  • antonym: listing - the act of adding something to an official list
  • synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or off a list
