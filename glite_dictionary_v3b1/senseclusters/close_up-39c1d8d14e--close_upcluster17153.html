close up

verb
bring together edges or parts
Pronunciation: / kloʊz ʌp / (AmE), / kləʊz ʌp / (BrE)
to bring together or seal the edges of something so there is no gap or opening; to block or seal an entrance or passage

Examples

To close up a gap
After reading, don't forget to close up the book and put it back on the shelf.
Please close up the wound with these stitches.
The umbrella was damaged and wouldn't close up properly.
After the storm, workers had to close up the gaps in the dam.
The old tunnels were closed up to prevent unauthorized entry.
They decided to close up the entrance with bricks for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open up - to make or become open
  • synonym: seal off - to block entry to something, often for safety or security
