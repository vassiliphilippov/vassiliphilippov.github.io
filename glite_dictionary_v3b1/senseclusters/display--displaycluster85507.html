to show something or make it visible or apparent, especially so it can be seen by others

Examples

To display a message
He tried not to display his disappointment when he heard the news.
She likes to display her collection of rare stamps in a glass case.
The museum will display the artist's paintings in a special exhibition next month.

Synonyms

  • show
    1.0 232/m
    show is a common everyday verb for making something visible to others, while display sounds slightly more formal and public.
  • exhibit
    3.4 27/m
    exhibit refers to showing something publicly or in a gallery, while display covers showing anything to make it visible to others.
  • present
    2.8 104/m
    present often means formally introduce or show something to an audience, while display focuses on making it visible to people.

Related Terms
