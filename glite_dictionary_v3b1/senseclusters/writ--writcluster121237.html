writ

noun
legal order issued by a court
8.2 2.16/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªt / (AmE), / rÉªt / (BrE)
(law) a formal written order issued by a court or judicial officer, commanding the person to whom it is addressed to perform or refrain from performing a specified act.

Examples

A writ of execution
A writ of execution allows authorities to enforce a judgment by seizing assets.
She received a writ ordering her to appear before the court next month.
The judge issued a writ of habeas corpus demanding the prisoner's release.

Synonyms

  • judicial writ
    8.2 0.01/m
    judicial writ is the formal court document used in legal proceedings, while writ is the general term for such court orders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: injunction - a court order requiring a person to do or cease doing a specific action
  • synonym: summons - an official order to appear in court
