machicolate

verb
to add projecting galleries (machicolations) to a building
8.4
Pronunciation: / məˈtʃɪkəˌleɪt / (AmE), / məˈtʃɪkəleɪt / (BrE)
to supply (a building or wall) with machicolations, i.e., projecting galleries with openings through which defenders could drop things on attackers below

Examples

To machicolate castle walls
Medieval architects often machicolated castle walls as a protective measure.
The fortress was machicolated to improve its defenses against invaders.
They planned to machicolate the northern tower during the restoration project.
