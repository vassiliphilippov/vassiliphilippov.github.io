efferent neuron

noun
neuron carrying impulses from brain or spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈɛf.ɚ.ənt ˈnʊrˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈef.ər.ənt ˈnjʊə.rɒn / (BrE)
a nerve cell that carries impulses away from the brain or spinal cord to effector organs such as muscles or glands

Examples

Efferent neuron to muscles
Damage to an efferent neuron can result in loss of muscle function.
Scientists studied how an efferent neuron activates glands during stress responses.
The muscle contracted after an efferent neuron transmitted the signal from the spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • antonym: afferent neuron - neuron conducting impulses toward the central nervous system
