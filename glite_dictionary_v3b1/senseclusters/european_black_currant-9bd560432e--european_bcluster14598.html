European black currant

noun
European shrub with edible black berries
8.2
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən blæk ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən blæk ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
A shrub native to Europe, widely cultivated for its edible black aromatic berries, used in cooking and preserves.

Examples

The European black currant bush
Farmers in northern Europe often grow European black currant for commercial production.
The European black currant is prized for its rich flavor in jams and desserts.
Vitamin C content is remarkably high in the European black currant.

Related Terms
