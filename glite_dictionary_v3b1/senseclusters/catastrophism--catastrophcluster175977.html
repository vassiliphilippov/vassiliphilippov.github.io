catastrophism

noun
geological theory of disasters
Pronunciation: / kəˈtæstrəˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəˌfɪzəm / (BrE)
the scientific theory that Earth's features or history were shaped by sudden, violent events rather than gradual processes

Examples

Catastrophism in geology
The idea of catastrophism was once widely accepted in early geology.
Some scientists debated whether catastrophism or gradual change better explained mountain formation.
Modern theories often combine elements of catastrophism and uniformitarianism to explain Earth's history.
The scientist explained catastrophism as a way to understand mass extinctions in Earth's history.
Some geologists debate whether catastrophism or gradual change better explains the formation of certain landscapes.
The idea of catastrophism suggests that sudden disasters have shaped life on our planet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniformitarianism - the theory that Earth's features developed gradually over time
