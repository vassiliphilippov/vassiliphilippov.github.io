national committee

noun
main executive body of a political party
1.1 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəl kəˈmɪti / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
the main executive body of a political party, typically consisting of members representing different regions or groups within the party and responsible for organizing conventions and planning campaigns

Examples

The national committee meets
She was appointed to the national committee after serving as a state representative for several years.
The national committee oversees the planning and execution of the party’s national convention.
The Democratic national committee met to discuss the party's strategy for the upcoming election.
