tailcoat

noun
formal coat with long back tails
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪlˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈteɪl.kəʊt / (BrE)
A formal coat with a short front and long back tails, traditionally worn by men as part of full evening dress.

Examples

A tailcoat and bow tie
He looked distinguished in his black tailcoat and white bow tie at the gala.
In the 19th century, gentlemen often dressed in a tailcoat for formal events.
The orchestra conductor wore a classic tailcoat for the evening performance.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: morning coat - a formal coat with sloping front edges, usually worn in the morning
