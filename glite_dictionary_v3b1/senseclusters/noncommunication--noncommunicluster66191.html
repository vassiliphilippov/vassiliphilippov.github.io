noncommunication

noun
failure to disclose
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the failure to disclose information that should be revealed, especially in legal, insurance, or official contexts

Examples

Legal noncommunication
The contract was voided because of the seller's noncommunication about previous damage.
In some cases, noncommunication of important facts can lead to legal consequences.
The insurance claim was denied due to noncommunication of relevant information by the applicant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concealment - the act of hiding or not revealing something
  • antonym: disclosure - the act of making information known
