lumpy jaw

noun
chronic jaw infection causing swelling
6.8 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌmpi dʒɔ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmpi dʒɔː / (BrE)
a chronic bacterial infection causing swelling and hard lumps in the jaw, typically affecting the face and neck regions, especially in cattle or humans

Examples

Lumpy jaw infection
Lumpy jaw is less common in humans but can occur as a complication of actinomycosis.
People with lumpy jaw often experience chronic swelling and may require long-term antibiotic treatment.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with lumpy jaw after noticing the hard swelling along its jawline.
