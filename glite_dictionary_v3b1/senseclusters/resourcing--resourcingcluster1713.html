resourcing

noun
allocating resources
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɔrsɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːsɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of providing or allocating resources (such as people, money, equipment, or materials) for a project or task

Examples

Project resourcing
Careful resourcing is necessary to ensure the project stays on schedule and within budget.
The manager is responsible for the resourcing of all new initiatives in the department.
Inadequate resourcing can lead to delays and lower quality outcomes.
The company improved its resourcing to ensure every department had the tools they needed.
Effective resourcing is essential for the success of large construction projects.
Due to budget cuts, resourcing for the new initiative was limited.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocation - the act of distributing resources for a purpose
  • antonym: deprivation - the lack of necessary resources or supplies
  • synonym: funding - the act of providing money for a particular purpose
  • synonym: provision - the act of supplying or making available
  • synonym: staffing - the process of hiring or assigning employees
