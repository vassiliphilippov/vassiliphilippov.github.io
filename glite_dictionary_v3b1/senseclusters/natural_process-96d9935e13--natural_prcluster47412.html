natural process

noun
process occurring in nature
3.2 1.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn√¶t É.…öl Ààpr…ëÀê.ses / (AmE), / Àànat É.…ôr…ôl Ààpr…ô ä.ses / (BrE)
A process that occurs in or is produced by nature, rather than by human action or intention.

Examples

A natural process of erosion
Erosion is a natural process that shapes landscapes over thousands of years.
Photosynthesis is a natural process by which plants produce energy from sunlight.
The decomposition of organic matter is a natural process vital to soil health.

Synonyms

  • natural phenomenon
    3.7 0.78/m
    a natural phenomenon is an observable event in nature, while a natural process is a regular series of changes that happen without human cause.
  • activity
    1.9 51/m
    activity often means a living or environmental function that runs regularly, while natural process covers any natural sequence of change.
  • action
    5.1 6.16/m
    action often means a single change caused by force, while natural process refers to a longer series of natural changes.
