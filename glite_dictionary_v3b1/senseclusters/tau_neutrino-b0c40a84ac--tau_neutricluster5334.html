tau neutrino

noun
subatomic particle; neutrino type
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /taʊ nuː.ˈtriː.noʊ/ /; / /tɔː nuː.ˈtriː.noʊ/ (AmE), /taʊ njuː.ˈtriː.nəʊ/ /; / /tɔː njuː.ˈtriː.nəʊ/ (BrE)
A type of neutrino associated with the tau lepton, forming one of the three known neutrino flavors in particle physics.

Examples

The tau neutrino particle
Scientists at Fermilab detected the elusive tau neutrino after years of careful experimentation.
The tau neutrino interacts only via the weak nuclear force, making it extremely difficult to observe directly.
The discovery of the tau neutrino confirmed the existence of the third type of neutrino predicted by particle physics theory.
