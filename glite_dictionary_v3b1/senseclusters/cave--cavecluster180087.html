cave

verb
to collapse inward
3.5
Pronunciation: / keɪv / (AmE), / keɪv / (BrE)
to collapse or fall inward, especially of a structure or surface giving way under pressure.

Examples

The roof caved in
The old bridge finally caved after years of neglect and heavy storms.
Rescuers rushed to help when the tunnel suddenly started caving in during construction.
The roof of the abandoned warehouse caved under the weight of the snow.
