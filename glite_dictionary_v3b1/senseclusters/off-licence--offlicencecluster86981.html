off-licence

noun
shop selling alcohol to take away
Pronunciation: / ˈɔf ˌlaɪ.səns / (AmE), / ˈɒf ˌlaɪ.səns / (BrE)
A shop licensed to sell alcoholic drinks for consumption off the premises.

Examples

The off-licence shop
He stopped at the off-licence to pick up a bottle of wine before dinner.
Many off-licences stay open late on weekends to serve last-minute customers.
You must be over 18 to buy alcohol from an off-licence in the UK.
