mellowed

adjective
softened or gentler through age or experience
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛloʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.əʊd / (BrE)
(of a person, feeling, or thing) made softer, gentler, or more relaxed, especially as a result of age or experience.

Examples

Mellowed personality
Her mellowed attitude made family gatherings much more enjoyable.
The wine had mellowed over the years, gaining a smooth, rich flavor.
With age, his sharp criticism mellowed into thoughtful advice.
