dispersant

noun
substance for distribution
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɜrsənt / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsənt / (BrE)
a substance added to a liquid or mixture to help break up and distribute another substance, especially to clean up pollution

Examples

Oil dispersant
The cleanup team used a dispersant to help break up the oil slick in the water.
Adding a dispersant can prevent particles from settling at the bottom of the tank.
Scientists tested a new dispersant to improve the distribution of chemicals in the solution.
The cleanup crew used a dispersant to treat the oil spill.
Adding a dispersant to the mixture helped prevent the particles from settling at the bottom.
Scientists tested a new dispersant to improve water quality after the chemical leak.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coagulant - a substance that causes particles to clump together
  • synonym: emulsifier - a substance that helps mix liquids that normally do not combine
