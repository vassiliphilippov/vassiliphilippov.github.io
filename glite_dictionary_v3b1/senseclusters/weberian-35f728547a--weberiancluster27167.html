Weberian

adjective
of Max Weber's theories
Pronunciation: / veɪˈbɛriən / (AmE), / veɪˈbɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to the theories, ideas, or approach of sociologist Max Weber

Examples

Weberian theory in sociology
The Weberian model of bureaucracy emphasizes rationality and efficiency in organizations.
Her research takes a distinctly Weberian approach to understanding social action.
Many debates in sociology contrast Weberian and Marxist perspectives on class and power.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Marxist - relating to the theories of Karl Marx
