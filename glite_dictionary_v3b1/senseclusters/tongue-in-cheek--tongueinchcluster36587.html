tongue-in-cheek

adjective
humorous or ironic, not serious
Pronunciation: / ˌtʌŋ ɪn ˈtʃik / (AmE), / ˌtʌŋ ɪn ˈtʃiːk / (BrE)
intended to be humorous or ironic; not meant to be taken seriously

Examples

Tongue-in-cheek joke
His comments about quitting his job were clearly tongue-in-cheek and not meant to be taken seriously.
She gave a tongue-in-cheek response to the reporter's intrusive question.
The article offered some tongue-in-cheek suggestions for how to survive a zombie apocalypse.
He gave a tongue-in-cheek apology that made everyone laugh.
Her tongue-in-cheek remarks often left people wondering if she was serious or joking.
The article took a tongue-in-cheek look at modern dating trends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: facetious - treating serious issues with deliberately inappropriate humor
  • synonym: sarcastic - marked by or given to using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • antonym: sincere - genuine or free from pretense
