universal quantifier

noun
symbol denoting 'for all' in logic
8.4 7.7
Pronunciation: /juː.nə.ˈvɜːr.səl ˈkwɑːn.tɪ.faɪ.ər/ (AmE), /juː.nɪ.ˈvɜː.səl ˈkwɒn.tɪ.faɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a symbol or term in logic that indicates a statement applies to all members of a particular set or class

Examples

The universal quantifier symbol
In mathematical proofs, the universal quantifier is often represented by the symbol ∀.
The statement 'For every x, P(x) holds' uses a universal quantifier.
Understanding how a universal quantifier differs from an existential quantifier is fundamental in logic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: existential quantifier - a logical quantifier expressing that a proposition is true for at least one member of a set
