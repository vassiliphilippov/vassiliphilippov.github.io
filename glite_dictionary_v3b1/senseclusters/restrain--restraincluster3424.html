restrain

verb
hold back or limit movement
1.0 3.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from moving freely or acting as they wish, usually by physical means or other controls

Examples

To restrain a child
Parents sometimes need to restrain their children from dangerous activities.
She could barely restrain herself from laughing during the serious meeting.
The police had to restrain the suspect to prevent him from fleeing the scene.
She struggled to restrain her excited dog as it tried to chase the squirrels.
Strong straps are used to restrain patients who might harm themselves.
The police officer had to restrain the man to prevent him from running away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • antonym: release - to set free from confinement or restraint
  • synonym: restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
