moss-trooper

noun
historical border-region marauder
7.7
Pronunciation: / mɑsˈtruːpər / (AmE), / mɒsˈtruːpə / (BrE)
(historical) A marauder and plunderer, especially one who operated in the bogs and border country between England and Scotland in the 17th century.

Examples

The moss-trooper raided villages
Disguised as soldiers, the moss-trooper launched a daring raid across the moorlands.
Stories of moss-troopers terrorizing villages are common in border country folklore.
The Scottish authorities struggled for years to capture the notorious moss-troopers.
