in the lurch

adverb
abandoned in a difficult situation
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə lɝtʃ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
in a difficult or vulnerable situation, especially when abandoned by others at a crucial time

Examples

Left in the lurch
After the project failed, many employees felt they had been left in the lurch.
She promised to help but disappeared, leaving me in the lurch at the last minute.
When the manager quit unexpectedly, the staff was suddenly in the lurch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leave high and dry - to abandon someone in a difficult situation
  • synonym: let down - to fail to support or help someone as expected
