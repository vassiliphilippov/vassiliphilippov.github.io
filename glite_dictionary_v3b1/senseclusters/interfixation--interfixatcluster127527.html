interfixation

noun
word formation by inserting a linking element
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.fɪk.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.fɪk.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The formation of a new word by inserting an interfix—a linking element—between two morphemes.

Examples

Interfixation in words
Linguists study interfixation to understand how words are constructed from smaller units.
The process of interfixation is common in some agglutinative languages such as Turkish.
Without interfixation, certain compound words would sound awkward or be hard to pronounce.
