lymph node

noun
small organ of the lymphatic system
3.1
Pronunciation: / lɪmf noʊd / (AmE), / lɪmf nəʊd / (BrE)
A small, bean-shaped organ of the lymphatic system that filters lymph and produces lymphocytes to help fight infection.

Examples

A lymph node swelling
Cancer can sometimes spread to a nearby lymph node.
Infections can cause lymph nodes to become enlarged and tender.
The doctor felt her neck for any swollen lymph nodes during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gland - an organ in the body that produces a particular substance
