Examples

To eject a person

The airline ejected the passenger for refusing to obey the rules.
The manager threatened to eject customers who were behaving aggressively.

Synonyms

  • expel
    5.0 7.71/m
    expel is an official act to remove someone from a group, while eject means to force them out quickly.
  • force out
    4.2 0.41/m
    force out means pushing someone or something out by pressure or effort, while eject often suggests a sudden physical removal.
  • throw out
    3.0 5.66/m
    throw out is a casual phrase for removing someone or something, while eject feels more formal and more forceful in action.
  • drive out
    3.8 3.70/m
    drive out means forcing people to leave an area, often over time or by pressure, while eject is a quick, immediate expulsion.
  • remove
    1.5 150/m
    remove means taking something away or getting rid of it in many ways, while eject implies forcing it out physically.
