Cinchona calisaya

noun
South American medicinal plant, source of quinine
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈkoʊnə kæliˈsaɪə / (AmE), / sɪnˈkəʊnə kælɪˈsaɪə / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree with large glossy leaves and fragrant yellow, green, or red flowers, cultivated for its medicinal bark, which is a source of quinine.

Examples

Cinchona calisaya tree
Botanists identified the tree as Cinchona calisaya due to its distinctive glossy leaves.
The bark of the Cinchona calisaya is widely used in the production of quinine.
Traditional healers have harvested Cinchona calisaya for its medicinal properties for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quinine - a bitter medicinal compound obtained from cinchona bark
