unfortunate

adjective
having bad luck or unfavorable results
1.2 21/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàf…î…πt É…ôn…ôt /; /  ånÀàf…îrt É…ôn…ôt / (AmE), /  ånÀàf…îÀêt É…ôn…ôt / (BrE)
having or resulting in bad luck, or describing something as regrettable, inappropriate, or likely to have a bad result

Examples

An unfortunate event
It was an unfortunate accident that caused the delay.
She made an unfortunate choice by leaving her umbrella at home.
The team's unfortunate performance led to their early exit from the tournament.
Choosing that color for the walls was an unfortunate mistake.
His unfortunate remark caused tension among the team members.
It was an unfortunate decision to cancel the event at the last minute.
He took her silence as an unfortunate omen before the contest.
Many sailors considered it unfortunate to set sail on a Friday.
The timing of the announcement was especially unfortunate given the political climate.
