leaseholder

noun
person holding property by lease
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlisˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈliːsˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who holds the right to use and occupy property or land under a lease agreement, usually for a specified period

Examples

The leaseholder pays rent
As a leaseholder, she is responsible for paying ground rent each year.
Several leaseholders attended the meeting to discuss extending their leases.
The leaseholder must obtain permission from the landlord before making changes to the property.

Related Terms

  • antonym: landlord - a person who rents out property to a leaseholder
  • synonym: tenant - a person occupying land or property rented from a landlord; related legal term
