hypophysis

noun
the pituitary gland; master endocrine gland
9.2 9.3
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.ˈfɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /haɪ.pɒ.ˈfɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
the pituitary gland; a small organ at the base of the brain that regulates the release of hormones in the endocrine system

Examples

The hypophysis gland
A tumor of the hypophysis can disrupt hormone production throughout the body.
Damage to the hypophysis may cause serious endocrine disorders.
The hypophysis controls the release of hormones from several other glands in the body.
