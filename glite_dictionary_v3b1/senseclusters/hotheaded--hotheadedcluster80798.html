hotheaded

adjective
quick to anger; impulsive
4.9 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑtˌhɛdəd /; / ˈhɑtˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
easily angered or acting quickly and impulsively without thinking

Examples

A hotheaded person
His hotheaded actions got him into trouble with his boss.
She can be quite hotheaded when she feels someone is being treated unfairly.
The president accused his opponent of making hotheaded decisions during the crisis.
After the argument, his hotheaded response made the situation worse.
He apologized later for his hotheaded remarks during the meeting.
She's known for being hotheaded and making quick decisions without thinking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • synonym: impulsive - acting without thinking, especially because of emotion
  • antonym: levelheaded - Calm and sensible; exercising sound judgment
  • synonym: reckless - Without regard for danger or consequences
