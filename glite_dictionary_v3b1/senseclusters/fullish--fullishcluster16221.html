fullish

adjective
somewhat full
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfʊlɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat full; nearly but not completely full

Examples

A fullish room
The auditorium was fullish by the time the concert started, but there were still a few empty seats.
After dinner, my plate was still fullish because I couldn't finish everything.
The parking lot looked fullish, so we decided to look for a spot elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: almost full - nearly filled to capacity
  • degree higher: full - completely filled; containing as much as possible
  • degree lower: half-full - filled to about half its capacity
