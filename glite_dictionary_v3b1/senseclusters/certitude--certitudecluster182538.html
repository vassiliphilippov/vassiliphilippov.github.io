certitude

noun
absolute certainty; complete confidence
8.3 0.94/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀê.t…™.tud / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀê.t…™.tjuÀêd / (BrE)
absolute certainty or confidence in something, sometimes exceeding what is warranted by evidence

Examples

Absolute certitude
His certitude about winning the case surprised even his closest friends.
She spoke with such certitude that no one dared question her conclusions.
While evidence was lacking, there was a sense of certitude in his voice.

Synonyms

  • certainty
    3.4 16/m
    certainty is the state of being completely sure in fact, while certitude suggests absolute confidence that may ignore weak evidence.
  • conviction
    4.5 19/m
    conviction is a firmly held belief that needs no proof, while certitude implies absolute certainty that can exceed the evidence.
  • sureness
    3.6 0.22/m
    sureness is the personal feeling of confidence in action or choice, while certitude is a stronger, often unwarranted certainty.

Related Terms
