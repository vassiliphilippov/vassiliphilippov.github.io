to run or go after someone or something in order to catch them

Examples

Police chase after a thief
Police had to chase after the suspect when he ran from the scene.
The children began to chase after the stray puppy in the park.
Tourists sometimes chase after famous actors hoping to get a photograph.

Synonyms

  • chase
    1.0 29/m

  • give chase
    4.1 0.56/m

  • go after
    2.1 25/m
    go after is a casual phrase for following or trying to get someone, while chase after implies active running or pursuit to catch.
  • pursue
    3.5 71/m
    pursue is a more formal word that can mean following or trying to obtain something, while chase after focuses on catching by running.
  • run down
    4.1 0.72/m
    run down means to pursue until someone is caught or stopped, while chase after often means to run after someone to catch them.
  • hunt
    1.0 9.35/m
    hunt describes a long, often relentless search to catch or kill, while chase after is usually shorter and about running to catch.
  • dog
    6.8 0.70/m
    dog as a verb means to follow someone closely and annoy them, while chase after simply means to run after to catch them.
  • trail
    2.5 3.51/m
    trail means to follow someone quietly at a distance to watch or catch them, while chase after means to run after closely.
  • tail
    5.9 2.15/m

  • follow
    1.0 137/m
