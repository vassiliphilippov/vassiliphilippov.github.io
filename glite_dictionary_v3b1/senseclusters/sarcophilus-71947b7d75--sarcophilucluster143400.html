Sarcophilus

proper noun
marsupial genus
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɑːrˈkɑfɪləs / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒfɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of carnivorous marsupials native to Australia, best known for including the Tasmanian devil

Examples

Sarcophilus genus
The Sarcophilus genus is best known for the Tasmanian devil, its only living species.
Scientists study Sarcophilus to understand the evolution of carnivorous marsupials in Australia.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting Sarcophilus from disease and habitat loss.
