discourtesy

noun
rude or impolite behavior
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkɝːtəsi /; / dɪsˈkɜrtəsi / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɜːtəsi / (BrE)
rude or impolite behavior or actions; a lack of courtesy or respect for others

Examples

A discourtesy to others
His constant discourtesy towards the staff made him unpopular at work.
Leaving the meeting early without explanation was seen as a serious discourtesy.
The guide apologized for any discourtesy his team might have shown to the guests.
His discourtesy towards the guests surprised everyone at the dinner party.
She was offended by the obvious discourtesy in his tone.
The company apologized for any discourtesy its staff may have shown.
He was dismissed for his repeated discourtesy towards customers.
She apologized for the discourtesy shown during the meeting.
Such discourtesy is not tolerated in professional settings.
