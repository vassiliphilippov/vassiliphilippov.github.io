benevolent

adjective
generous and charitable
Pronunciation: / bəˈnɛvələnt / (AmE), / bəˈnɛvələnt /; / bɪˈnɛvələnt / (BrE)
kind and generous; willing to help others or show goodwill

Examples

A benevolent neighbor
A benevolent neighbor provided meals for the families after the storm.
Her benevolent attitude inspired others to volunteer at the shelter.
The foundation relied on a benevolent donor to fund its scholarship program.
A benevolent organization provided food and shelter to the homeless.
She gave him a benevolent smile that put him at ease.
The benevolent gentleman donated much of his fortune to help the poor.
A benevolent smile spread across her face as she listened to the children.
The benevolent woman donated a large sum to the charity each year.
Under his benevolent leadership, the community flourished and felt supported.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charitable - generous in giving help, especially to the needy
  • synonym: kind - having or showing friendliness, generosity, and consideration
  • antonym: malevolent - having or showing a wish to do evil to others
  • synonym: philanthropic - showing a desire to help others, especially by giving money
