please

verb
to make someone happy or satisfied
1.0 11/m
Pronunciation: / pliz /; / pliÀêz / (AmE), / pliÀêz / (BrE)
to make someone happy or satisfied; to give pleasure to someone

Examples

To please your friend
It pleases me to see you so happy.
The new design will please most of our customers.
We hope these changes please everyone involved.
Her thoughtful gift truly pleased him on his birthday.
It is difficult to please everyone in a large group.
The chef always tries to please his customers by preparing their favorite dishes.

Synonyms

  • gratify
    4.6 0.90/m
    gratify gives a strong sense of pleasure or meets desires, while please is the common verb for making someone happy.
  • satisfy
    2.0 12/m
    satisfy meets needs or expectations and makes someone content, while please focuses more on giving simple pleasure.
  • delight
    2.1 6.35/m
    delight gives great joy or surprise and feels intense, while please is milder, meaning to give simple happiness.
  • gladden
    3.5 0.13/m
    gladden makes someone feel joyful or brings gladness, while please is more common and often means to make someone happy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delight - to give great pleasure or satisfaction
  • antonym: displease - to cause someone to feel unhappy or annoyed
