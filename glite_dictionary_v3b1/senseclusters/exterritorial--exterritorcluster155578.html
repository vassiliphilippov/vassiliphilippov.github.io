exterritorial

adjective
outside territorial limits or jurisdiction
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstɛˌrɪˈtɔriəl /; / ˌɛkstɛrɪˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌɛkstɛˌrɪˈtɔːriəl /; / ˌɛkstɛrɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
not subject to the laws or authority of the country where it is located, especially for diplomatic reasons

Examples

Exterritorial diplomatic area
Certain areas may be considered exterritorial for diplomatic reasons.
He studied the impacts of exterritorial rights on maritime boundaries.
The embassy staff were granted exterritorial status under international law.
Certain embassy buildings are considered exterritorial and not subject to local police authority.
The ambassador's residence enjoys exterritorial status under international law.
Some foreign officials have exterritorial privileges that protect them from prosecution in the host country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extraterritorial - existing or taking place outside the territory or jurisdiction of a state
