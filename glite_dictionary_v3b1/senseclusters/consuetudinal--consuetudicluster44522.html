consuetudinal

noun
manual of group or monastic customs
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːnsjuːəˈtuːdnəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsjuːɪˈtjuːdɪn(ə)l / (BrE)
A manual that describes the customs, especially the ceremonial practices, of a particular group or monastic order.

Examples

A consuetudinal manual
A rare consuetudinal from the twelfth century is preserved in the monastery library.
She studied the medieval consuetudinal to understand the daily rituals of the order.
The monks followed the rules outlined in their consuetudinal during the ceremony.
