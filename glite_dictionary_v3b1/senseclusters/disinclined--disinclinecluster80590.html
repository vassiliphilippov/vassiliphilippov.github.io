Unwilling or reluctant to do something, often because of mild dislike or disapproval.

Examples

To be disinclined to help
After a long day's work, he was disinclined to go out again.
Many voters are disinclined to support changes they do not fully understand.
She seemed disinclined to accept his invitation to dinner.

Synonyms

    3.5 19/m
    reluctant shows hesitation or doubt about doing something, while disinclined suggests mild unwillingness often from dislike or low interest.
  • unwilling
    3.4 12/m
    unwilling means not wanting to do something and often refuses, while disinclined suggests a softer, milder reluctance.
  • indisposed
    6.8 0.01/m
    indisposed usually describes being unwilling or slightly ill and refusing, while disinclined focuses on mild dislike or reluctance.
  • loath
    6.9 1.37/m

  • averse
    6.4 2.04/m
    averse expresses a strong dislike or opposition to something, while disinclined implies only mild reluctance or slight disapproval.
