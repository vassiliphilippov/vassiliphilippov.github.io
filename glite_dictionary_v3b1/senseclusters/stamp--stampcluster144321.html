stamp

verb
impress or raise a design on a surface
4.4 1.9
Pronunciation: /stæmp/ (AmE), /stæmp/ (BrE)
to imprint, mark, or shape something by pressing with a tool or machine

Examples

To stamp a logo
The artist used a metal tool to stamp the logo onto the book cover.
The company can stamp your initials in relief on personalized stationery.
They stamped each medal with the event's insignia, leaving a raised impression.
Special machines stamp identification numbers onto the parts as they move along the line.
The factory stamps coins from sheets of metal.
They stamped the plastic bottles into their final shapes.
She stamped the metal plate with the company logo.
The children stamped their hands in paint and pressed them onto the paper.
The official sealed the document by stamping his signature on the bottom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emboss - to decorate with a raised pattern or design
  • synonym: engrave - to carve or cut a design or text into a hard surface
  • antonym: erase - to remove or wipe out a mark or imprint
  • synonym: forge - to shape (metal objects) by heating and hammering
  • synonym: imprint - to mark by pressing or stamping
