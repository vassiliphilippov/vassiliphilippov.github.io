Onoclea sensibilis

noun
a species of fern (sensitive fern)
Pronunciation: / ˌɑː.nəˈkliː.ə sɛnˈsɪb.ɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒ.nəˈkliː.ə sɛnˈsɪb.ɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of fern native to eastern North America and eastern Asia, known for its pinnately divided fronds that fold slightly when touched.

Examples

Onoclea sensibilis fern
Gardeners often plant Onoclea sensibilis near ponds for its decorative, lush foliage.
The Onoclea sensibilis thrives in moist woodlands throughout eastern North America.
When touched, the fronds of Onoclea sensibilis tend to fold slightly, which inspired its common name, sensitive fern.
