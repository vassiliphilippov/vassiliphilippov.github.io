violate

verb
disregard a law, rule, or promise
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt/ (BrE)
to break or fail to follow a law, rule, agreement, or promise

Examples

To violate the law
He promised to keep my secret, but he violated my trust by telling everyone.
The company will face penalties if they violate the terms of the agreement.
You must not violate the school rules, or you could be suspended.
Any student who violates the school's code of conduct may face suspension.
Building without a permit violates local regulations.
The new advertising campaign violates the company's ethical guidelines.
