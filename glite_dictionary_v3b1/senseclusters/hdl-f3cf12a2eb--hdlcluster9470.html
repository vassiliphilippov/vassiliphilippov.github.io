HDL

noun
high-density lipoprotein (blood cholesterol)
3.2 3.30/m
Pronunciation: / Àåe…™t É ÀådiÀê Àà…õl / (AmE), / Àåe…™t É ÀådiÀê Àà…õl / (BrE)
a lipoprotein in the blood known as high-density lipoprotein, which helps transport cholesterol and is thought to reduce the risk of heart disease

Examples

High HDL cholesterol
A diet rich in healthy fats can help raise HDL in the bloodstream.
Doctors often test your HDL levels to assess heart health.
High HDL is generally considered beneficial for cardiovascular health.

Synonyms

  • high-density lipoprotein
    4.1 0.12/m

  • alpha-lipoprotein
    7.2 0.00/m
    alpha-lipoprotein is an older scientific name sometimes used in research papers, while HDL is the modern lab term and common abbreviation.
