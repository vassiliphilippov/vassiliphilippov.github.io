plow

verb
to turn over soil with a plow
4.9 1.4
Pronunciation: /plaʊ/ (AmE), /plaʊ/ (BrE)
to turn over and break up the soil with a plow, especially in preparation for planting crops

Examples

To plow a field
Every spring, the farmers plow their fields to get ready for planting.
Heavy rain made it difficult to plow the muddy field.
They used a team of horses to plow the land before sowing the seeds.
Every spring, they plough the soil before planting new crops.
She watched as the oxen slowly ploughed the old pasture land.
The farmer used a tractor to plough the field after the harvest.

  • synonym: till - to prepare land for growing crops
