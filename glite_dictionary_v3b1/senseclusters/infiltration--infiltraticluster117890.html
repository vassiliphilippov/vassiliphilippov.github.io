infiltration

noun
secret entry, especially for military purposes
4.6 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.fɪl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.fəl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.fɪl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.fəl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of entering or penetrating a place or group, especially secretly and gradually, such as the surreptitious entry of individuals or small groups into enemy positions without detection

Examples

Secret infiltration
Special forces are trained in covert infiltration behind enemy lines.
The company fears industrial infiltration by competitors seeking trade secrets.
The spy's infiltration of the enemy's headquarters went undetected for months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exfiltration - the process of withdrawing subjects from an area, especially military withdrawal by stealth
  • synonym: penetration - the action of entering or passing into or through something
