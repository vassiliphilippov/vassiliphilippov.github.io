parcel

noun
a piece of land
4.9 2.21/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêrs…ôl / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀês…ôl / (BrE)
a piece or section of land, especially one considered as part of an estate or for sale

Examples

A parcel of land
Each parcel was clearly marked with its boundaries and lot number.
The developer purchased a large parcel near the highway for the new shopping center.
They own a parcel of land by the river where they plan to build a cabin.

Synonyms

  • parcel of land
    2.9 0.49/m
    a parcel of land is sold or owned as a single unit, while parcel can mean any piece of land.
  • piece of land
    1.8 1.23/m
    a piece of land is a distinct area often marked by borders, while parcel usually names land offered for sale or ownership.
  • piece of ground
    2.8 0.17/m
    a piece of ground is a physical area you can see on the surface, while parcel is a legal unit for sale.
  • plot of land
    3.0 0.97/m
    a plot of land is often small and used for a garden or building, while parcel covers land as a sale unit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plot - a small marked-out area of land for a specific purpose
  • synonym: tract - a large area of land
