loosen

verb
make less tight or secure
3.5 1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsən /; / ˈlusən / (AmE), / ˈluː.sən /; / ˈluːsən / (BrE)
to make something less tight or more relaxed, or to become less tight or more relaxed (such as shoelaces, knots, or a grip)

Examples

To loosen tight laces
After walking for hours, he stopped to loosen his shoelaces.
Could you please loosen the jar lid for me?
She tried to loosen the knot, but it was very tight.
As the hours passed, his grip on the steering wheel loosened and he relaxed.
Gradually, the knot loosened despite my efforts to keep it tight.
The rules have loosened in recent years, giving students more flexibility.
After hours of work, she finally managed to loosen the knot.
He used a wrench to loosen the bolt before removing it.
The rules have started to loosen as the situation improves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relax - to make or become less tense or tight
  • antonym: tighten - to make something tight or tighter
  • synonym: untie - to open something by untying a knot
