addictiveness

noun
quality causing dependence
6.1
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdɪk.tɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdɪk.tɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of something that makes people likely to become dependent on it or unable to stop using it

Examples

The addictiveness of apps
The addictiveness of social media apps keeps many users online for hours every day.
Scientists are studying the addictiveness of certain foods high in sugar and fat.
Some medications are carefully regulated because of their potential addictiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dependency - a state of relying on or being controlled by something
  • synonym: habit-forming - making people likely to develop a habit or dependence
  • antonym: harmlessness - the quality of not causing harm or dependence
