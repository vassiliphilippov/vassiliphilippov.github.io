louver

noun
parallel slat in a window or door for ventilation
Pronunciation: / ˈluːvər / (AmE), / ˈluːvə / (BrE)
one of a set of parallel slats, typically in a door or window, designed to let in air while keeping out rain or direct sunlight.

Examples

Window louver slats
Cleaning each louver in the window frame can be a tedious job.
The louver in the bathroom window allows fresh air to circulate while protecting against rain.
The architect chose a wooden louver design for the front door to improve ventilation.
He replaced the damaged louvre to improve the airflow through the pantry.
Modern homes often use adjustable louvres to control ventilation and light.
The louvre in the window allowed cool air to circulate throughout the room.
