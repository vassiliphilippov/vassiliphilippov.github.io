warm up

verb
become more friendly or open
1.0
Pronunciation: / wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to become more friendly, relaxed, or willing to talk, especially after being reserved or shy

Examples

To warm up to people
At first, she was quite shy, but she began to warm up after an hour.
He didn't warm up until he realized we had shared interests.
It usually takes new team members some time to warm up to the group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool down - to become less emotional or less friendly
  • synonym: open up - to become more willing to talk or share feelings
