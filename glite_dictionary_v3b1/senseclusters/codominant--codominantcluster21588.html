codominant

adjective
equal tree leaders
Pronunciation: / koʊˈdɑːmɪnənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈdɒmɪnənt / (BrE)
describing two or more stems, branches, or leaders of a tree that grow to similar size and strength, with none being clearly dominant

Examples

Codominant tree trunks
The oak tree had two codominant trunks that grew side by side for decades.
Arborists recommend removing one codominant leader to prevent the tree from splitting.
The presence of codominant branches can make a tree more vulnerable to storm damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dominant - having the most power or influence; leading
