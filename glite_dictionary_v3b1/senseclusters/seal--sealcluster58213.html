seal

verb
to close securely or with a seal
1.9
Pronunciation: / siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
to close, fasten, or make something airtight or watertight, often by using a seal or a protective layer

Examples

To seal a box
She carefully sealed the envelope to keep its contents private.
The bakery seals its bread in airtight bags to keep it fresh.
They sealed the container to prevent leaks during transport.
Be sure to seal the envelope so the contents don't fall out.
Before winter, we seal the windows to keep cold air from getting inside.
The company sealed the roof to prevent leaks during the rainy season.
After finishing the letter, she carefully sealed it with red wax.
Please seal the envelope before you mail it.
The official pressed the wax and sealed the document with his signet ring.
