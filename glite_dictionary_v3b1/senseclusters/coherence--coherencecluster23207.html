coherence

noun
state of being connected or sticking together
4.2 6.7
Pronunciation: / koʊˈhɪrəns / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪərəns / (BrE)
the quality of being connected, logical, and forming a unified whole

Examples

Logical coherence
Scientists studied the coherence of water molecules under changing temperatures.
The coherence of the argument made it persuasive to everyone in the room.
The strength of a team often depends on the coherence of its members.
A strong thesis statement gives a paper greater coherence and unity.
Scientists value coherence in theories because it makes them easier to understand and test.
The essay lacks coherence, making it hard to follow the writer’s main argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consistency - the quality of always behaving or performing in a similar way
  • antonym: incoherence - lack of logical connection or consistency
