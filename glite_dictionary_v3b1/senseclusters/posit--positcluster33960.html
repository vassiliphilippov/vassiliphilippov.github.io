posit

verb
assume or suggest as a fact or principle
6.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.zət/ /; / /ˈpɑː.zɪt/ (AmE), /ˈpɒ.zɪt/ (BrE)
to suggest, assume, or propose something as a fact or principle, especially as a basis for reasoning or argument

Examples

To posit an idea
Many philosophers posit the existence of a universal moral law.
Some theories posit a close relationship between language and thought.
The scientist posited that life could exist on other planets.
He posited an interesting theory about language development in children.
Some scientists posit the existence of parallel universes.
The philosopher posited that all knowledge begins with experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assume - suppose something to be the case without proof
  • antonym: deny - to state that something is not true, the opposite of posit
  • synonym: postulate - suggest or assume the existence or truth of something as a basis for reasoning
  • synonym: propose - to put forward (a plan or suggestion)
