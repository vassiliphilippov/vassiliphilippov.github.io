profess

verb
admit into a religious order
Pronunciation: / prəˈfɛs / (AmE), / prəˈfɛs / (BrE)
to formally join a religious order or make solemn religious vows

Examples

To profess religious vows
After two years of novitiate, he was finally professed into the monastic community.
She was professed as a nun at the age of twenty-three.
The abbey will profess five new sisters at this year's ceremony.
After years of contemplation, she finally professed as a nun last spring.
He decided to profess and dedicate his life to the monastery.
Many young women profess each year during the annual ceremony at the convent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: take vows - to formally commit by oath, especially in a religious context
