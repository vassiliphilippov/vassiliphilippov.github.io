shock

noun
an electric jolt felt in the body
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk / (AmE), / ʃɒk / (BrE)
a sudden and uncomfortable reflex reaction caused by the passage of an electric current through the body

Examples

Electric shock
After repairing the outlet, the electrician described the minor shock he had received.
She accidentally touched the wire and felt a strong shock run through her hand.
The warning label said there was a risk of shock if the device was misused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electric shock - injury or sensation caused by electric current passing through the body
