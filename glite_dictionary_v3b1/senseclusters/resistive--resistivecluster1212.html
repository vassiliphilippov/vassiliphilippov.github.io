resistive

adjective
showing electrical resistance
6.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstɪv / (BrE)
exhibiting or relating to electrical resistance; offering opposition to the flow of electric current.

Examples

Resistive wire
Resistive materials are used in the construction of heating elements.
A resistive load converts electrical energy into heat.
The wire was designed to be highly resistive to control the current flow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conductive - allowing electricity to flow easily
