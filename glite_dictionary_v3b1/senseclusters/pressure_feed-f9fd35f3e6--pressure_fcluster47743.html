pressure feed

noun
system for lubricating engines under pressure
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər fid / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə fiːd / (BrE)
A system in which lubrication oil is delivered to engine bearings by means of a pump under pressure, commonly used in internal combustion engines.

Examples

Pressure feed system
Modern car engines rely on a pressure feed system to ensure efficient lubrication.
Older engines often used splash lubrication instead of a pressure feed system.
The mechanic explained that a faulty pump could disrupt the pressure feed to the bearings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: splash lubrication - method of engine lubrication where oil is splashed onto parts without pressure
