palmatifid

adjective
(of a leaf) having palm-shaped clefts
Pronunciation: / pælˈmeɪ.tɪ.fɪd / (AmE), / pælˈmeɪ.tɪ.fɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf) divided in a manner resembling an open hand, with the divisions (clefts) reaching towards the base but not as deeply as in palmately lobed leaves.

Examples

Palmatifid leaf shape
In botany, a palmatifid leaf is distinct from a palmate-lobed one.
Some geraniums feature palmatifid leaves that are deeply but not completely divided.
The maple has varieties with palmatifid leaves, making them appear like open hands.
