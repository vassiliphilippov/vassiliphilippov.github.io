coccidiomycosis

noun
fungal infection affecting lungs and skin
Pronunciation: / kɑkˌsɪdioʊmaɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɒkˌsɪdiəʊmaɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A lung and skin infection caused by certain fungi, especially found in arid regions, characterized by cough, sputum, and nodules.

Examples

Coccidiomycosis lung infection
People living in desert regions are at higher risk of contracting coccidiomycosis.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with coccidiomycosis after reviewing the chest X-ray and symptoms.
The spread of coccidiomycosis is influenced by dust storms carrying fungal spores.

Related Terms

  • synonym: valley fever - another name for coccidiomycosis
