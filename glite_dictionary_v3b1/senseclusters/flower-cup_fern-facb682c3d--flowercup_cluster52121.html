flower-cup fern

noun
a North American fern with bipinnate fronds and chestnut stipes
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈflaʊər ˌkʌp ˌfɜrn / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə ˌkʌp ˌfɜːn / (BrE)
a slender fern of northern North America with shiny chestnut-colored stalks and bipinnate fronds, usually having distinct sori along the edges

Examples

A flower-cup fern plant
A patch of flower-cup ferns lined the shaded trail by the river.
Many botanists admire the chestnut-colored stalks of the flower-cup fern during their field studies.
The flower-cup fern thrives in the moist woodlands of northern Canada and Alaska.
