woodshedding

verb
to practice intensively
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌʃɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌʃɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
to practice something privately and intensively, especially to improve musical skill or performance

Examples

To woodshed a song
The guitarist spent hours woodshedding before his big concert.
She has been woodshedding her piano pieces every evening this week.
Many jazz musicians believe in woodshedding alone to master their improvisation skills.
