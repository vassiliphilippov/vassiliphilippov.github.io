subgroup

noun
group subset (mathematics)
6.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.gruːp/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.gruːp/ (BrE)
(Mathematics) A subset of a group that is itself a group under the same operation.

Examples

A math subgroup
A subgroup of a group must include the identity element of the original group.
Every subgroup inherits the group operation from its parent group.
The set of even integers forms a subgroup of the group of all integers under addition.
