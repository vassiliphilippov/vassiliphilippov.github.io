ependymal

adjective
lining brain ventricles
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɛndɪməl / (AmE), / ɪˈpɛndɪməl / (BrE)
relating to the thin membrane that lines the ventricles of the brain and the central canal of the spinal cord

Examples

Ependymal layer
The ependymal layer plays a crucial role in producing cerebrospinal fluid.
Damage to ependymal cells can affect the flow of fluid in the central nervous system.
Researchers studied the ependymal tissue to understand its function in brain health.

  • synonym: ventricular - relating to the ventricles of the brain or heart
