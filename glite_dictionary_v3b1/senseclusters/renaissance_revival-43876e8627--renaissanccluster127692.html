Renaissance revival

noun
19th-century architectural style based on Italian Renaissance
Pronunciation: / rɪˈneɪsəns rɪˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / rɪˈneɪsəns rɪˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
A 19th-century architectural style that draws inspiration from a variety of classic Italian Renaissance forms, distinct from Grecian or Gothic revivals.

Examples

Renaissance revival architecture
His library was designed according to Renaissance revival principles, featuring elaborate columns and arches.
Many public buildings in the 19th century were constructed in the Renaissance revival style, emphasizing grandeur and symmetry.
The Renaissance revival movement influenced architects throughout Europe and North America.
