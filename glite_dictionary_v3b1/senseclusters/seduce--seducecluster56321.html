seduce

verb
to attract romantically
Pronunciation: / sɪˈdus / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to attract someone and persuade them to do something, often by making them feel special or desired, especially in a romantic or sexual way

Examples

To seduce customers
The advertisement tried to seduce customers with promises of luxury and comfort.
She was seduced by his charm and sense of adventure.
He is skilled at seducing people into joining his projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attract - to draw someone's interest or attention
  • antonym: repel - to drive away or discourage someone from approaching
  • synonym: tempt - to try to persuade someone to do something, especially something wrong or unwise
