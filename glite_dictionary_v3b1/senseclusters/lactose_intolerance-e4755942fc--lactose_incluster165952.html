lactose intolerance

noun
inability to digest lactose in dairy products
1.9 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlæktoʊs ɪnˈtɑlərəns / (AmE), / ˈlaktəʊs ɪnˈtɒlərəns / (BrE)
the inability to digest lactose, a sugar found in milk and dairy products, due to a deficiency of lactase enzyme

Examples

Lactose intolerance symptoms
A doctor diagnosed his stomach pains as a symptom of lactose intolerance.
Many adults develop lactose intolerance as they age and reduce their intake of dairy products.
She avoids cheese and ice cream because of her lactose intolerance.
