fetometry

noun
measurement of a fetus
7.8
Pronunciation: / fiˈtɑmətri /; / fiˈɒtəˌmɛtri / (AmE), / fiˈtɒmətri /; / fɪˈɒtəmətri / (BrE)
the measurement of a fetus, especially the diameter of its head, typically by ultrasound or radiography, to assess growth and development during pregnancy

Examples

Fetometry by ultrasound
Accurate fetometry allows doctors to detect possible complications during pregnancy.
Advances in ultrasound have greatly improved the precision of fetometry.
Prenatal fetometry is a key tool in assessing fetal growth and development.
Foetometry is commonly performed during ultrasounds to track fetal development.
Accurate foetometry can help detect abnormalities in fetal growth before birth.
The doctor explained the results of the foetometry to the expectant parents.
