Ceres

proper noun
Roman goddess of agriculture
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.riːz / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of agriculture, crops, and fertility; the Roman counterpart of the Greek goddess Demeter.

Examples

Ceres goddess of crops
In ancient Roman religion, Ceres was worshipped as the protector of crops and grain.
Statues of Ceres often depict her holding a sheaf of wheat, symbolizing abundance.
The story of Ceres and her daughter's abduction explains the changing seasons in Roman mythology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Demeter - Greek goddess of agriculture and fertility, counterpart of Ceres
