water bitternut

noun
hickory tree with bitter nuts
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔɾɚ ˈbɪtɚˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈbɪtənʌt / (BrE)
A species of hickory tree native to the southern United States, known for its numerous narrow leaflets and bitter nuts.

Examples

The water bitternut tree
Botanists have noted that the water bitternut typically prefers wet, low-lying areas.
Farmers often avoid harvesting the water bitternut due to its particularly bitter nuts.
The swamp was dotted with old water bitternut trees, their leaves shimmering in the midday sun.
