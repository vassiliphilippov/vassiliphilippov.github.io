changeling

noun
child secretly substituted for another
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪndʒlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒlɪŋ / (BrE)
a child believed to have been secretly exchanged by fairies or supernatural beings for the parents' real child

Examples

A changeling child
In folklore, a changeling was often blamed for a child's sudden illness or strange behavior.
Some parents believed their sickly baby had been swapped with a changeling by fairies.
The story describes how villagers tried to force a changeling to reveal its true identity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foundling - an abandoned child of unknown parents
