postbellum

noun
period after a war
9.9
Pronunciation: /poʊst.ˈbel.əm/ (AmE), /pəʊst.ˈbel.əm/ (BrE)
the period of time immediately following a war, especially in reference to social and political changes

Examples

The postbellum period
Many historians study the postbellum to understand how societies recover from conflict.
The postbellum era brought significant changes to the country's political landscape.
Literature from the postbellum often reflects themes of rebuilding and reconciliation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antebellum - the period before a war, especially the American Civil War
  • synonym: reconstruction - the period after the American Civil War when the South was rebuilt
