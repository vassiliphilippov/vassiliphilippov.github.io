canzone

noun
Renaissance instrumental piece
Pronunciation: / kænˈzoʊni / (AmE), / kanˈtsəʊneɪ / (BrE)
an instrumental musical composition, especially from the Renaissance or Baroque period, often based on vocal music forms

Examples

Baroque canzone music
The ensemble performed a beautiful canzone inspired by Renaissance vocal music.
During the concert, the musicians played a lively canzone that showcased their technical skill.
Many composers of the Baroque era wrote at least one canzone for instrumental groups.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ricercar - a type of early instrumental composition similar to a fugue
  • synonym: sonata - a composition for one or more instruments, typically in several movements
