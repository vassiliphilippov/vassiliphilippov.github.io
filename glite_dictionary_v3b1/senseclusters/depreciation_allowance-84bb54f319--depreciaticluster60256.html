depreciation allowance

noun
deduction for loss in asset value over time
8.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.priː.ʃiː.ˈeɪ.ʃən ə.ˈlaʊ.əns/ /; / /dɪ.priː.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən ə.ˈlaʊ.əns/ (AmE), /dɪ.priː.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən ə.ˈlaʊ.əns/ /; / /dɪ.priː.ʃiː.ˈeɪ.ʃən ə.ˈlaʊ.əns/ (BrE)
an amount deducted from taxable income or profits to account for the decrease in value of an asset over time due to wear, age, or obsolescence.

Examples

The depreciation allowance amount
Each year, a depreciation allowance is calculated for the value lost in the building.
Proper accounting for depreciation allowance helps reflect the true cost of using company assets.
The company claimed a depreciation allowance on its machinery to reduce taxable profits.
