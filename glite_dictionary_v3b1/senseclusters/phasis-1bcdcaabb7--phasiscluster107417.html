Phasis

proper noun
ancient river and town
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsɪs / (BrE)
the ancient name of a river (now the Rioni) and the town at its mouth in western Georgia, known from Greek and Roman sources

Examples

The Phasis river
Ancient Greek explorers traveled up the Phasis in search of new trade routes.
The city at the mouth of the Phasis was a key port in the region of Colchis.
Legends say Jason and the Argonauts sailed the Phasis during their quest for the Golden Fleece.
