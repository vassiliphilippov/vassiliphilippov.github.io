diploic vein

noun
vein in the spongy part of cranial bones
8.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈploʊ.ɪk veɪn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈpləʊ.ɪk veɪn/ (BrE)
a vein located in the spongy part (diploë) of the cranial bones, draining blood from the skull bones

Examples

Diploic vein in skull
Damage to a diploic vein can increase the risk of hematoma during skull surgery.
Radiologists sometimes notice an enlarged diploic vein on cranial imaging studies.
The surgeon identified a diploic vein during the cranial procedure to prevent excessive bleeding.
