refetch

verb
to retrieve again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɛtʃ / (BrE)
to retrieve or obtain something again, especially data or information that was previously fetched

Examples

To refetch data
If the results are outdated, you can refetch the latest data from the server.
The app will automatically refetch information if the connection is lost and restored.
After making changes, remember to refetch the page to see the updates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fetch - to go and bring back something
  • synonym: reload - to load something again, especially a web page or data
