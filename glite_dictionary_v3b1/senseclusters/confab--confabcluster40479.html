confab

verb
talk informally; have a casual conversation
7.5 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.fæb /; / kənˈfæb / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fæb /; / kənˈfab / (BrE)
to have an informal conversation or discussion, either casually or to exchange ideas or make a decision

Examples

To confab with friends
After the meeting, we stayed to confab about our weekend plans.
The two colleagues would often confab by the water cooler about the latest office news.
While waiting for the bus, they started to confab about nothing in particular.
Let's confab over lunch about the next steps for the proposal.
The project leaders decided to confab before making any final decisions.
We confabbed in the hallway to make sure everyone agreed on the plan.
