modalism

noun
Trinity as modes
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.də.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.də.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the doctrine that the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are different modes or aspects of one God, rather than distinct persons

Examples

Modalism doctrine
Early church councils debated whether modalism accurately described the nature of the Trinity.
Some theologians rejected modalism because it denies the distinct persons of the Trinity.
The controversy over modalism influenced the development of Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monarchianism - the belief in the indivisible unity of God, often denying the Trinity
  • antonym: trinitarianism - the doctrine that God exists as three distinct persons in one being
