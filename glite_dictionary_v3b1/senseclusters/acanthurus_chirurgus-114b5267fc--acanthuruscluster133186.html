Acanthurus chirurgus

noun
surgeonfish species from the West Indies
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌkænˈθʊr.əs ʃɜrˈɡuːs / (AmE), / əˌkænˈθʊər.əs ʃɜːˈɡjuːs / (BrE)
A species of surgeonfish native to the West Indies, known for a sharp spine on each side of its tail.

Examples

Acanthurus chirurgus fish
Researchers recently observed a school of Acanthurus chirurgus near the coast of Barbados.
The Acanthurus chirurgus is often spotted grazing on algae in Caribbean reef environments.
The sharp tail spine of the Acanthurus chirurgus gives it the common name 'doctorfish'.
