Jagannatha

proper noun
Hindu god; form of Vishnu
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈɡɑnəθə / (AmE), / dʒəˈɡanəθə / (BrE)
A form of the Hindu god Vishnu, worshipped especially in Odisha, India, and often associated with the annual chariot festival at Puri.

Examples

Jagannatha chariot festival
Every year, thousands of devotees gather in Puri to worship Jagannatha during the famous chariot festival.
Legends say that Jagannatha is an avatar of Vishnu, known for his universal benevolence.
The temple of Jagannatha is a major pilgrimage site for Hindus across India.
