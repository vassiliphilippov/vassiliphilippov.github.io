semiepiphyte

noun
plant epiphytic part of its life
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈɛpɪˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈɛpɪfaɪt / (BrE)
A plant that grows as an epiphyte for part of its life cycle but is rooted in soil at another stage.

Examples

A semiepiphyte plant
Many tropical orchids are considered semiepiphytes because they start life on trees but later root in the ground.
Researchers found that semiepiphyte populations thrive in areas where both trees and soil are abundant.
Some semiepiphytes rely on both aerial and soil roots during their growth stages.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: epiphyte - a plant that grows on another plant but is not parasitic
