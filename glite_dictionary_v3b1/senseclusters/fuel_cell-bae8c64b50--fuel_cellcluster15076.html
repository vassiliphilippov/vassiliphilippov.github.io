fuel cell

noun
device generating electricity from chemical reaction
5.4 3.7
Pronunciation: /fjuːl sel/ /; / /ˈfjuː.əl sel/ /; / /ˈfjʊ.əl sel/ (AmE), /ˈfjuː.əl sel/ /; / /fjuːl sel/ /; / /ˈfjʊ.əl sel/ (BrE)
A device that generates electricity through a chemical reaction between a fuel (such as hydrogen) and an oxidant (such as oxygen).

Examples

A fuel cell device
During the demonstration, a fuel cell converted hydrogen and oxygen into clean electricity.
Scientists are developing more efficient fuel cells for use in electric vehicles.
The fuel cell powered the car for over 400 miles without needing to be recharged.
