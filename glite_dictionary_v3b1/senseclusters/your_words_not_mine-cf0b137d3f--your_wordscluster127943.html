your words, not mine

x
distancing oneself from another's statement
Pronunciation: / jɔɹ wɝdz nɑt maɪn / (AmE), / jɔː wɜːdz nɒt maɪn / (BrE)
used to emphasize that you are repeating someone else's statement, not expressing your own opinion

Examples

Your words, not mine phrase
"I think he's lazy—your words, not mine," she said quickly.
"This plan is doomed to fail—your words, not mine, just to be clear," he replied.
When accused of agreeing, she insisted, "Your words, not mine."
