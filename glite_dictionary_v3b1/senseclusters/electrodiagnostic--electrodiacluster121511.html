electrodiagnostic

noun
electrical medical test
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˌdaɪəgˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌdaɪəgˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
a medical test or procedure that uses electrical recordings to assess nerve or muscle function

Examples

An electrodiagnostic exam
The doctor ordered an electrodiagnostic to help diagnose the cause of the patient's muscle weakness.
An electrodiagnostic can reveal problems with nerve function that are not visible on standard scans.
Before surgery, the specialist performed an electrodiagnostic to assess the patient's nerve health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromyography - a test that records electrical activity of muscles
  • synonym: nerve conduction study - a test measuring how fast electrical signals move through nerves
