Adalia bipunctata

noun
species of ladybird beetle (two-spot)
Pronunciation: / əˈdeɪliə baɪˌpʌŋkˈteɪtə / (AmE), / əˈdeɪliə bɪˌpʌŋkˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of ladybird beetle with a red body and one black spot on each wing cover, commonly known as the two-spot ladybird.

Examples

The Adalia bipunctata beetle
Children were excited to find a bright Adalia bipunctata crawling on the leaf.
Scientists observed a decline in Adalia bipunctata numbers due to environmental changes.
The Adalia bipunctata is often used in gardens to control aphid populations naturally.

Related Terms
