musculus sphincter vesicae

noun
bladder sphincter muscle
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs ˈsfɪŋk.tər ˈve.zɪ.kaɪ/ /; / /ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˈsfɪŋk.tər ˈve.zɪ.kiː/ (AmE), /ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˈsfɪŋk.tə ˈve.zɪ.kaɪ/ /; / /ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˈsfɪŋk.tə ˈve.zɪ.kiː/ (BrE)
a ring of muscle in the urinary bladder that controls the release of urine by surrounding the bladder's opening into the urethra

Examples

The musculus sphincter vesicae muscle
Damage to the musculus sphincter vesicae can lead to urinary incontinence.
During urination, the musculus sphincter vesicae relaxes to allow the flow of urine.
The contraction of the musculus sphincter vesicae is essential for continence.
