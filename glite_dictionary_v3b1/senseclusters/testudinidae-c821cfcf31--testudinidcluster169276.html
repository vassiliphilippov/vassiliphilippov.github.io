Testudinidae

noun
family of land tortoises
Pronunciation: / tɛsˌtjuːdəˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / tɛsˌtjuːdɪˈnɪdɪ / (BrE)
a family of land-dwelling tortoises characterized by domed shells and stumpy, elephantine legs, found in warm regions around the world

Examples

Testudinidae tortoise family
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Testudinidae to better understand their unique adaptations.
The Testudinidae family includes some of the largest tortoises in the world, such as the Galápagos giant tortoise.
Unlike sea turtles, Testudinidae are strictly terrestrial and adapted to dry environments.
