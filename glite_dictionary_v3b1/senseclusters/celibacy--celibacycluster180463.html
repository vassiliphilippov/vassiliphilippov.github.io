celibacy

noun
abstinence from sexual relations
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛl.ɪ.bə.si /; / ˈsɛl.ə.bə.si / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ɪ.bə.si / (BrE)
the state of not having sexual relations, especially for religious or personal reasons (sometimes also not marrying)

Examples

Celibacy for priests
His decision to embrace celibacy surprised many of his friends.
Some people practice celibacy for spiritual or philosophical reasons.
The priest chose a life of celibacy as part of his dedication to the church.
After taking his religious vows, he embraced celibacy for the rest of his life.
She made a commitment to lifelong celibacy and never married.
The tradition of celibacy is observed by monks in several religions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstinence - the practice of restraining oneself from indulging in something, typically alcohol or sex
  • synonym: chastity - the practice of refraining from extramarital, or especially from all, sexual intercourse
  • antonym: marriage - the state of being married
