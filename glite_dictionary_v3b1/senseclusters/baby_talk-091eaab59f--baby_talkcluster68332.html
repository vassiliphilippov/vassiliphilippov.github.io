baby talk

noun
imitation of a child's speech by adults
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌtɔk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌtɔːk / (BrE)
a mode of speaking in which adults imitate the simplified speech characteristic of young children, often in a playful or affectionate way

Examples

Adult baby talk
He found baby talk charming when adults interacted with infants.
The teacher discouraged parents from using baby talk when helping their children learn to speak.
Whenever she spoke to her puppy, she used baby talk to make him feel loved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: childish speech - language resembling that of a child
