depersonalize

verb
make impersonal; treat as an object
7.5 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / diÀàp…ùÀês…ôn…ôÀåla…™z / (AmE), / diÀêÀàp…úÀês…ôn…ôÀåla…™z /; / diÀêÀàp…úÀês…ôn…ôla…™z / (BrE)
to make something impersonal or treat it as an object, removing individual or personal qualities

Examples

To depersonalize art
Mass production can depersonalize art, stripping away the creator's unique touch.
Some hospitals depersonalize patients by referring to them only by room numbers.
The company was criticized for policies that depersonalize employee relationships.
Modern technology can sometimes depersonalise communication between people.
Some critics argue that certain medical procedures depersonalise patient care.
The new policies may depersonalise the workplace, making employees feel like numbers rather than individuals.

Synonyms

    7.3 0.01/m
    impersonalize makes something less personal and more objective, while depersonalize means treating something as an object and removing individual qualities.
  • objectify
    7.5 1.17/m
    objectify treats a person as an object and ignores their individuality, while depersonalize makes something impersonal by removing personal qualities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: objectify - to treat as an object rather than as a person
  • antonym: personalize - to make something personal or individual
