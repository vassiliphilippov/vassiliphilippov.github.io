phenetic

adjective
based on observable traits
9.5
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to classifying or analyzing things based on observable characteristics, not on inferred or genetic relationships

Examples

Phenetic classification method
The researchers used a phenetic approach to group the artifacts by their visible features.
In linguistics, a phenetic analysis can help identify dialects based on pronunciation patterns.
Unlike genetic methods, phenetic classification relies only on observable similarities.
The phenetic method groups plants by their visible features rather than their evolutionary history.
Scientists used a phenetic approach to compare the physical traits of different bird species.
A phenetic classification can sometimes group unrelated organisms together based on similar appearances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: morphological - relating to the form or structure of things
  • antonym: phylogenetic - relating to classification based on evolutionary relationships
