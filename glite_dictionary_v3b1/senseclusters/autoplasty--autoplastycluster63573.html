autoplasty

noun
grafting tissue from oneself (medical)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɔtəˌplæsti /; / ˈɔtoʊˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊˌplæsti /; / ˈɔːtəʊˌplasti / (BrE)
surgical repair or reconstruction of a part of the body using tissue taken from another part of the same person's body

Examples

Autoplasty surgery
After the accident, autoplasty was chosen to reconstruct the damaged facial tissue.
Developments in autoplasty have improved recovery times for patients requiring tissue grafts.
The surgeon performed an autoplasty using skin from the patient's thigh to repair the burn.
Advances in autoplasty have improved outcomes for burn victims needing skin reconstruction.
Thanks to autoplasty, the patient avoided the risk of tissue rejection associated with donor grafts.
The surgeon recommended autoplasty to reconstruct the patient's nose after the injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alloplasty - surgical repair using tissue from a donor or artificial material
  • synonym: autograft - a graft of tissue from the same individual
  • synonym: graft - a piece of living tissue transplanted surgically
