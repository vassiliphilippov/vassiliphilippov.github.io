barrage balloon

noun
large tethered balloon used to block enemy aircraft
6.3
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɑːʒ bəˈluːn / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ bəˈluːn / (BrE)
a large balloon or blimp, usually held by cables, used to deter low-flying enemy aircraft, especially during wartime by suspending obstacles in their path

Examples

Large barrage balloon
During World War II, barrage balloons were deployed over London to defend against air raids.
Historians describe how the network of barrage balloons changed the landscape during the Blitz.
The silver barrage balloons floated above the city, making it dangerous for low-flying enemy planes.
