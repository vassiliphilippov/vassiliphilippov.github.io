euthanize

verb
cause painless death to relieve suffering

to intentionally cause the painless death of a person or animal, especially to relieve suffering

Examples

To euthanize a pet
After years of illness, the family decided to euthanize their beloved cat.
Some countries allow doctors to euthanize terminally ill patients in certain cases.
The veterinarian had to euthanize the injured dog to prevent further suffering.
New laws regulate how and when doctors may euthanise terminally ill patients.
Some families must make the difficult choice to euthanise a beloved pet.
The veterinarian decided to euthanise the horse to end its suffering.

Synonyms

  • mercy kill
    8.6 0.00/m
    mercy kill is an informal, emotional phrase people use to talk about ending suffering, while euthanize sounds clinical and neutral.

Related Terms

  • synonym: euthanize - U.S. spelling with same meaning, to cause a painless death
  • synonym: mercy kill - to end someone's life to relieve suffering
  • synonym: put down - to kill an animal humanely, typically to relieve suffering
