nominalist

noun
philosopher who rejects universals
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑː.mə.nəl.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈnɒm.ɪ.nə.lɪst / (BrE)
a philosopher who believes that universals or general ideas are only names without any corresponding reality and that only particular objects exist

Examples

Nominalist philosopher
As a nominalist, she argued that only individual objects exist in reality.
Many debates between nominalist and realist philosophers still influence contemporary metaphysics.
The medieval nominalist William of Ockham questioned the existence of abstract universals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realist - philosopher who believes universals exist independently of particulars
