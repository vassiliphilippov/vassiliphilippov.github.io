Tripleurospermum inodorum

noun
a species of weed with white flowers; scentless mayweed
Pronunciation: / trɪp.li.ˌʊr.oʊˈspɜːr.məm ɪˈnoʊdərəm / (AmE), / trɪp.ljuː.ˌɔːrˈɒ.spə.məm ɪˈnəʊdərəm / (BrE)
a species of annual weed with white, daisy-like flowers and finely divided leaves, common in Europe and also found in North America; sometimes known as scentless mayweed

Examples

Tripleurospermum inodorum plant
Tripleurospermum inodorum often appears in fields and roadsides across Europe during the summer months.
Although similar to chamomile, Tripleurospermum inodorum lacks the characteristic scent.
Farmers frequently remove Tripleurospermum inodorum to prevent it from spreading in their crops.
