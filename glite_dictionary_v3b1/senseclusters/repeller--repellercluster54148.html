repeller

noun
one who drives away
7.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛlər / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛlə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes others to feel strong dislike or aversion and drives them away

Examples

A social repeller
His negative attitude made him a repeller in social situations.
The strong odor acted as a repeller to most of the guests.
She became a repeller after repeatedly criticizing her friends.
