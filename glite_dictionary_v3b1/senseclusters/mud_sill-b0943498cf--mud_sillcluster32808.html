mud sill

noun
bottom timber of a wall
Pronunciation: / mʌd sɪl / (AmE), / mʌd sɪl / (BrE)
the lowest horizontal timber or beam forming the base of a wall, usually in contact with the ground or foundation

Examples

The mud sill beam
After the heavy rain, water seeped in under the mud sill.
The barn’s mud sill was replaced with treated wood to prevent future damage.
The carpenter checked the mud sill for rot before starting repairs on the house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sill - the horizontal piece forming the bottom of a window or door frame
