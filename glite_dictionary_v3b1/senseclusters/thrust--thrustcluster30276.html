thrust

noun
pointed critical remark
Pronunciation: / θrʌst / (AmE), / θrʌst / (BrE)
a pointed or critical remark, especially one intended as criticism; a verbal jab or attack

Examples

A sharp thrust
During the debate, his witty thrusts kept the audience entertained.
She couldn't ignore the subtle thrust in his comment.
The politician's speech included several thrusts at his opponents' policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barb - a cutting remark intended to hurt or criticize
  • synonym: jab - a sudden critical remark or insult
