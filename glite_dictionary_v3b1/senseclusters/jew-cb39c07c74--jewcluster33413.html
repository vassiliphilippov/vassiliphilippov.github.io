Jew

noun
member of the Jewish people
2.4
Pronunciation: / dʒuː / (AmE), / dʒuː / (BrE)
a person who belongs to the group of people whose traditional religion is Judaism, or who is descended from Jewish ancestors

Examples

A Jew practicing Judaism
As a practicing Jew, he observes the major Jewish holidays each year.
Her grandparents were both Jews who emigrated from Eastern Europe.
She identifies as a Jew ethnically, though she is not religious.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Gentile - a person who is not Jewish
