peroxyl

noun
reactive oxygen group
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɑksɪl / (AmE), / pəˈrɒksɪl / (BrE)
a reactive chemical group or radical containing two oxygen atoms bonded together, often involved in oxidation reactions

Examples

The peroxyl radical
The peroxyl radical is important in many biological oxidation processes.
Scientists study peroxyl groups to understand how antioxidants work in the body.
Formation of a peroxyl radical can lead to cell damage if not controlled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peroxide - a compound containing an oxygen-oxygen single bond
  • synonym: radical - an atom or group of atoms with an unpaired electron
