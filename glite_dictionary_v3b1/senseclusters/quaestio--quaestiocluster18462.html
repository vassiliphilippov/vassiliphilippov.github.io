quaestio

noun
Roman judicial inquiry
9.9
Pronunciation: /kweɪ.ˈstiː.oʊ/ /; / /kwes.ˈtiː.oʊ/ (AmE), /kweɪ.ˈstiː.əʊ/ /; / /kwes.ˈtiː.əʊ/ (BrE)
an official inquiry or judicial investigation, especially a Roman court or commission examining crimes or public charges

Examples

A quaestio on crime
The Roman senate convened a quaestio to address the accusations of treason against the governor.
Historians studied the records of the quaestio to understand ancient legal procedures.
A quaestio was often established to investigate serious crimes in the Roman Republic.
