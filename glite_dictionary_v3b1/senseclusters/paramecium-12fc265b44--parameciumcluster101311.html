Paramecium

noun
freshwater ciliate microorganism genus
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈmiːsiəm / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈmiːsiəm / (BrE)
a genus of freshwater single-celled ciliates with an oval body and a long, deep oral groove, commonly found in ponds and streams

Examples

A Paramecium cell
A drop of pond water may contain dozens of Paramecium swimming around rapidly.
Science students often observe Paramecium under the microscope to learn about cell structure.
The Paramecium uses its cilia to move and gather food.
