scouring rush

noun
a rough-stemmed horsetail plant used for cleaning
Pronunciation: / ˈskaʊərɪŋ ˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊərɪŋ ˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
an evergreen horsetail plant (Equisetum hyemale) with jointed, rough-edged stems, formerly used for scouring utensils

Examples

The scouring rush plant
Botanists often identify scouring rush by its unique, rough-edged green stems.
Early settlers used the abrasive stems of scouring rush to clean their pots and pans.
The scouring rush grew abundantly along the riverbank, its tough stems brushing against the rocks.
