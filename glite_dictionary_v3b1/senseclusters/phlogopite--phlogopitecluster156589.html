phlogopite

noun
a brown mineral of the mica group
Pronunciation: / floʊˈɡɑːpˌaɪt / (AmE), / fləˈɡɒp.aɪt / (BrE)
A brown mineral of the mica group, consisting of a hydrous silicate of potassium, magnesium, and aluminum.

Examples

Sheet of phlogopite
Sheets of phlogopite are often used as electrical insulators due to their heat resistance.
The geologist identified the shiny brown flakes as phlogopite in the rock sample.
The presence of phlogopite suggested that the rock formed in a magnesium-rich environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mica - a group of minerals with layered, flexible sheets
