water-insoluble

adjective
not soluble in water

not able to dissolve in water; incapable of forming a solution with water.

Examples

Water-insoluble substance
Chalk is considered water-insoluble and will not dissolve even after hours in water.
Plastic bags made of water-insoluble polymers persist in the environment.
The pill is coated with a water-insoluble material to protect it from dissolving in the stomach.

Synonyms

  • non-water-soluble
    5.6 0.00/m
    non-water-soluble describes a substance that cannot dissolve in water, while water-insoluble is the standard adjective for the same property.
  • insoluble
    6.4 0.32/m
    insoluble means not able to dissolve in a liquid and is used broadly, while water-insoluble specifies inability to dissolve in water.
  • indissoluble
    8.8 0.01/m
    indissoluble sounds formal and often means impossible to dissolve or break up, while water-insoluble only means not soluble in water.

Related Terms
