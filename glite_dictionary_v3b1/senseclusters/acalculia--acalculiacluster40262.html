acalculia

noun
inability to calculate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪˌkælkjuˈliːə / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌkælkjʊˈlɪə / (BrE)
a disorder characterized by the inability to perform simple mathematical calculations, often due to brain injury or disease

Examples

acalculia after injury
After the accident, he developed acalculia and could no longer add simple numbers.
The neurologist explained that acalculia is often seen after certain types of brain injuries.
Her acalculia made everyday tasks like shopping and budgeting very challenging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aphasia - loss of ability to understand or express speech
  • synonym: dyscalculia - difficulty in learning or understanding arithmetic
