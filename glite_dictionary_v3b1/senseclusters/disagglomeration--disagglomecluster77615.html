disagglomeration

noun
breaking up clusters
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˌɡlɑməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˌɡlɒməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of breaking up or spreading out a group of people, businesses, or activities so they are less clustered, especially in a city or industry

Examples

Disagglomeration of businesses
Urban planners studied the effects of disagglomeration on city growth and transportation.
The disagglomeration of industries led to more balanced economic development across the region.
Government policies encouraged disagglomeration to reduce congestion in major metropolitan areas.
The city's disagglomeration led to the growth of new suburbs and business parks.
Urban planners studied the effects of disagglomeration on transportation and infrastructure needs.
After the factory closed, the region experienced significant disagglomeration as workers moved away.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agglomeration - the process of gathering together into a cluster or mass
  • synonym: decentralization - the transfer of activities or authority away from a central location
