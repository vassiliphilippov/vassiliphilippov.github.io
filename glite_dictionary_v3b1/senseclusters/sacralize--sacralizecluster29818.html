sacralize

verb
to make sacred
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪkrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsakrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something sacred or treat it as holy or deserving of religious respect

Examples

To sacralize a place
Many ancient societies would sacralize rivers and mountains as part of their religious beliefs.
The ceremony was designed to sacralize the new temple before it opened to the public.
Some people sacralize certain traditions, treating them as untouchable and deeply meaningful.
The community decided to sacralize the old tree, turning it into a place of worship.
Rituals are often used to sacralize important objects or locations in many religions.
Some leaders attempt to sacralize national symbols to inspire unity among citizens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consecrate - to declare something sacred or dedicate formally to a religious purpose
  • antonym: desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with disrespect
