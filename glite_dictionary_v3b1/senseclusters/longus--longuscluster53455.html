longus

adjective
long muscle (anatomy)
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋɡəs / (BrE)
used in scientific or anatomical names to show that something (like a muscle) is long or elongated

Examples

Flexor longus muscle
The flexor longus is important for toe movement.
In anatomy class, we learned that the peroneus longus helps stabilize the foot.
The abductor pollicis longus is responsible for moving the thumb away from the hand.
The muscle named flexor longus is longer than its counterpart.
In taxonomy, the term longus often describes species with elongated features.
The scientist chose longus as the species name because of the animal's long tail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: long - having great length
