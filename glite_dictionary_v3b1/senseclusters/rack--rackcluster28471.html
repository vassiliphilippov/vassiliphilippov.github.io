rack

verb
obtain by force or intimidation
5.1
Pronunciation: / ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially money, through force, threats, or intimidation

Examples

To rack money
For years, the mob had been racking money from shopkeepers in the neighborhood.
The corrupt officials tried to rack extra fees from small business owners.
The gang racked large sums from local merchants through threats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coerce - to persuade or force someone to do something by use of threats
  • synonym: extort - to obtain something by force, threats, or unfair means
