secessionist

noun
advocate of political withdrawal
Pronunciation: / səˈsɛʃənɪst / (AmE), / sɪˈsɛʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates or supports the withdrawal of a group or region from a political entity, such as a nation or state

Examples

A secessionist leader
He has always identified as a proud secessionist in his political writings.
Several secessionist leaders were arrested during the protests.
The secessionist movement gained momentum after the election.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unionist - a person opposing secession and supporting the union
