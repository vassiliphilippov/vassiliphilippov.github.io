imposer

noun
one who enforces rules
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
a person who forces something, such as rules or burdens, on others without their consent

Examples

Strict imposer of rules
The new principal was criticized as an imposer of unnecessary regulations.
No one wanted to be seen as an imposer when suggesting changes to the team's routine.
Some leaders are natural imposers, always insisting on their own way.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power who often imposes strict rules
  • synonym: enforcer - a person who compels others to follow rules or laws
  • antonym: follower - a person who accepts and follows rules set by others
