postcontact

noun
period after contact
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstkənˈtækt / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstkənˈtækt / (BrE)
the period or state that occurs after initial contact between different groups, societies, cultures, or species

Examples

The postcontact period
Many indigenous communities faced dramatic changes during the postcontact era.
Researchers study the postcontact effects on language and culture after colonization.
The postcontact period brought new technologies but also significant challenges for local populations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: precontact - the period before initial contact between groups or cultures
