western blind snake

noun
burrowing snake of western US with reduced eyesight
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn blaɪnd sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən blaɪnd sneɪk / (BrE)
a small, slender, burrowing snake found in the deserts, rocky hillsides, and beach sands of the western United States, noted for its reduced or absent eyesight

Examples

The western blind snake
A western blind snake may be mistaken for a worm due to its shiny, slender body.
The western blind snake spends most of its life underground, emerging only at night or after rain.
You can occasionally find a western blind snake beneath rocks or debris in arid regions of California.
