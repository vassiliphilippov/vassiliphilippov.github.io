genin

noun
non-sugar part of glycoside
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnɪn / (BrE)
the non-sugar part of a glycoside molecule, often released when the glycoside is broken down by hydrolysis

Examples

Glycoside genin
The scientist separated the genin from the glycoside to analyze its chemical properties.
After hydrolysis, the genin was identified as the active component responsible for the plant's effects.
Researchers are studying how the genin interacts with other molecules in the body.

Related Terms
