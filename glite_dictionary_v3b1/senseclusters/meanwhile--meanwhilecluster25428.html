meanwhile

adverb
in the intervening time
1.6 61/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàminÀåwa…™l / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀênÀåwa…™l / (BrE)
during the period between two events; at the same time

Examples

Meanwhile set the table
I'll start dinner now; meanwhile, could you set the table?
She applied for jobs, and meanwhile, she continued her studies.
The train was delayed, so meanwhile we waited patiently at the station.
I'm cooking dinner; meanwhile, could you please set the table?
She left for the store. Meanwhile, he finished cleaning the kitchen.
The children played outside. Meanwhile, their parents prepared a surprise inside the house.

Synonyms

  • meantime
    3.0 2.12/m
    meantime refers to the intervening period between events, while meanwhile signals what happens during that same period.
  • in the meantime
    2.8 22/m
    in the meantime describes what happens while you wait between events, while meanwhile introduces a simultaneous action.
  • at the same time
    1.0 75/m
    at the same time says two things happen together at once, while meanwhile often links events across a period.
  • concurrently
    5.8 2.64/m
    concurrently means actions overlap in time, often formally, while meanwhile is more general and fits everyday storytelling.
  • simultaneously
    4.2 28/m
    simultaneously describes events that happen at the exact same moment, while meanwhile covers actions happening during a period.
  • contemporaneously
    8.4 0.23/m
    contemporaneously is used in formal writing to mean happening in the same period, while meanwhile works in casual narratives.
  • contemporarily
    7.0 0.01/m
    contemporarily means at the same time in a similar era, while meanwhile is common in speech and storytelling.
