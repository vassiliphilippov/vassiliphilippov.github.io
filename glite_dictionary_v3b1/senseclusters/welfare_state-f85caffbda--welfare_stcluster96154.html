welfare state

noun
government system providing social welfare
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛlˌfɛr steɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɛlfeə ˌsteɪt / (BrE)
A system of government in which the state assumes primary responsibility for the social and economic well-being of its citizens, typically through programs such as public health, housing, pensions, and unemployment benefits.

Examples

The welfare state system
Debate continues over the sustainability of the welfare state in times of economic hardship.
Opponents argue that a welfare state reduces individuals' motivation to work.
The Scandinavian countries are often cited as examples of a welfare state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: social democracy - a political, social, and economic philosophy promoting welfare state policies within a capitalist framework
