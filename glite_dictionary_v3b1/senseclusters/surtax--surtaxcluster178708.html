surtax

verb
impose an extra tax
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.tæks / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tæks / (BrE)
to impose an additional tax on something, especially above existing taxes

Examples

To surtax profits
If the new law passes, companies may be surtaxed on their record profits this year.
Some politicians propose to surtax sugary drinks as a public health measure.
The government decided to surtax imports of luxury vehicles to curb excessive spending.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tax - to impose a compulsory financial charge
  • antonym: untax - to remove or exempt from tax
