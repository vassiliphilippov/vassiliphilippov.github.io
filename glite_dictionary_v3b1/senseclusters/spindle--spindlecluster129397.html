spindle

verb
to make long and thin
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪndəl /; / ˈspɪndl / (AmE), / ˈspɪndəl /; / ˈspɪndl / (BrE)
to shape something into a long, thin, round form, or to wind fibers into yarn or place objects onto a spindle

Examples

To spindle wood
The craftsman spindled the wood to create elegant chair legs.
She carefully spindled the clay into thin rods for her art project.
The machine can spindle metal bars into precise cylindrical shapes.
The factory workers were spindling cotton fibers into thread for the textile mill.
She carefully spindled the receipts onto the metal rod to keep them organized.
Old documents were spindled and stored in the archive room for future reference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flatten - to make something flat or flatter
  • synonym: spin - to make thread or yarn by twisting fibers together
  • synonym: taper - to become gradually thinner toward one end
  • antonym: unwind - to remove something that has been wound or twisted
