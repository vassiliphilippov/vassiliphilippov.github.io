thresh

verb
to beat or thrash hard
9.9
Pronunciation: / θrɛʃ / (AmE), / θrɛʃ / (BrE)
to beat or strike someone or something hard, especially as punishment; to give a thrashing to

Examples

To thresh the carpet
He was so furious that he began to thresh the carpet with his cane.
In the old tales, the villain would often thresh his enemies into submission.
The angry farmer threatened to thresh the mischievous boys if they damaged his crops again.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful; to pardon
  • synonym: thrash - to beat soundly or severely
