keratoconus

noun
eye condition where cornea bulges outward
9.0 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrətoʊˈkoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˌkɛrətəˈkəʊnəs / (BrE)
A degenerative condition of the eye in which the cornea becomes progressively thinner and bulges into a cone-like shape, leading to vision distortion.

Examples

Keratoconus eye disease
Advancements in treating keratoconus have significantly improved the quality of life for many patients.
After being diagnosed with keratoconus, he began exploring options for corneal treatment.
The doctor explained that keratoconus was the cause of her blurry and distorted vision.
