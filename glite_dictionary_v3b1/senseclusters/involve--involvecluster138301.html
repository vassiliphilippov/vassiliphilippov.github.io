involve

verb
connect closely, often incriminatingly
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to connect closely with a situation or event, especially in a way that may be incriminating or have significant consequences

Examples

Involve a crime
Her sudden disappearance may involve criminal activity according to the police investigation.
The company was found to involve itself in fraudulent financial practices.
The prosecutors claim the emails involve several high-profile politicians in the scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implicate - to show (someone) to be involved in a crime
