eliminate

verb
remove a variable from equations (math)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
(in mathematics) To remove an unknown variable from two or more equations, usually by substitution or other algebraic manipulation.

Examples

To eliminate a variable
By adding the two equations together, we can eliminate one of the unknowns.
The teacher showed us how to eliminate a variable using substitution.
To solve for x, you must first eliminate y from the system of equations.
