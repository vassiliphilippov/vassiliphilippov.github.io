magistery

noun
alchemical substance
Pronunciation: / məˈdʒɪstəri / (AmE), / məˈdʒɪstəri / (BrE)
an archaic term for a substance believed to have magical or medicinal powers, especially in alchemy

Examples

Alchemical magistery
In ancient texts, a magistery was often described as a cure for many ailments.
The alchemist spent years perfecting his magistery in hopes of discovering the secret to eternal life.
Belief in the power of a magistery was common among medieval healers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elixir - a magical or medicinal potion
  • synonym: panacea - a remedy for all diseases or problems
