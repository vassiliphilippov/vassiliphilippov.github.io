Proterozoic eon

noun
geological eon (2,500–544 million years ago)
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtəroʊˈzoʊɪk ˈiːɒn /; / proʊˌtɛrəˈzoʊɪk ˈiː.ɒn / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtərəʊˈzəʊɪk ˈiːən /; / prəʊˌtɛrəʊˈzəʊɪk ˈiː.ən / (BrE)
the geological eon lasting from about 2,500 to 544 million years ago, marked by the appearance of bacteria, fungi, and the earliest multicellular organisms

Examples

The Proterozoic eon period
Scientists study rocks from the Proterozoic eon to learn about the planet's early atmosphere.
The Proterozoic eon saw the emergence of simple multicellular life forms in Earth's oceans.
The end of the Proterozoic eon is marked by the rise of more complex life during the Cambrian period.
During the Proterozoic aeon, simple organisms began to evolve into more complex forms.
Geologists often study rocks dating back to the Proterozoic aeon to understand Earth's early atmosphere.
The fossil record from the Proterozoic aeon provides insight into the earliest multicellular life.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Archean eon - the eon before the Proterozoic, marked by the formation of the Earth's crust and earliest life
  • degree higher: Phanerozoic eon - the eon following the Proterozoic, marked by abundant animal life
  • synonym: Precambrian - the eon before the Cambrian period, including the Proterozoic
