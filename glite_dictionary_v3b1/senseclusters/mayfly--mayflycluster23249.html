mayfly

noun
short-lived aquatic-winged insect
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
A slender insect with delicate, transparent wings that has an aquatic larval stage and an adult stage that typically lasts less than two days.

Examples

A mayfly insect
A mayfly emerged from the water, its delicate wings shining in the sunlight.
Many anglers imitate the mayfly when tying fishing lures, as trout feed on them during hatching season.
The fleeting life of a mayfly has made it a symbol of transience in literature.
