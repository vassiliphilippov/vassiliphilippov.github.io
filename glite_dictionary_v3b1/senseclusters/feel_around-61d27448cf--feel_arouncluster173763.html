feel around

verb
search by touching or groping
3.5 0.57/m
Pronunciation: / fil …ôÀàra änd / (AmE), / fiÀêl …ôÀàra änd / (BrE)
to search for something by touching or groping, especially in the dark or when unable to see clearly

Examples

To feel around in the dark
He feels around under the sofa cushions, hoping to find the lost remote.
I had to feel around for the light switch in the dark room.
She felt around in her bag until she found her phone.

Synonyms

  • grope
    6.3 2.88/m
    grope means searching with your hands in the dark and often clumsily, while feel around is a gentler, general way to search by touch.
  • feel
    2.3 89/m
    feel means touching or examining something with your hands to find it, while feel around is a phrase for searching more carefully in the dark.
  • scrabble
    6.5 0.81/m
    scrabble means searching by touch in a hurried, awkward way, while feel around is a neutral phrase for searching carefully by touch.
