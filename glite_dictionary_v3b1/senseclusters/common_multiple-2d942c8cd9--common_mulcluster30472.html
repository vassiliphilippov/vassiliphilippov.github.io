common multiple

noun
number divisible by two or more numbers
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmən ˈmʌltɪpəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒmən ˈmʌltɪpl̩ / (BrE)
a number that is a multiple of two or more other numbers

Examples

A common multiple of numbers
12 is a common multiple of 3 and 4.
The teacher asked the class to list the first common multiple of 2 and 5.
When adding fractions, finding a common multiple can simplify the calculation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: common divisor - a number that divides two or more numbers exactly
  • degree lower: least common multiple - the smallest common multiple of two or more numbers
