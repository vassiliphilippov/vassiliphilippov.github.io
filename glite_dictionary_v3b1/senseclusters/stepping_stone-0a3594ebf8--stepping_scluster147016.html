stepping stone

noun
intermediary host used in cyberattacks
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈstep.ɪŋ stoʊn/ (AmE), /ˈstep.ɪŋ stəʊn/ (BrE)
(in computing and cybersecurity) a computer, server, or network host used as an intermediary to reach further targets or to hide an attacker's origin; often a compromised machine used to pivot within a network.

Examples

A stepping stone server
The incident report showed the attacker used a compromised stepping stone to reach internal servers.
During the exercise, analysts identified several stepping stones that allowed lateral movement across the network.
To reduce risk, they isolated the suspected stepping stone and began forensic imaging immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jump host - a host used to connect between different security zones or networks; sometimes used as an intermediary in attacks
  • synonym: pivot - in cybersecurity, to move from one compromised host to other systems within a network
