longicorn beetle

noun
beetle with long antennae
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑŋɪˌkɔrn ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋɪˌkɔːn ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A beetle characterized by an elongated body and very long antennae, belonging to the family Cerambycidae.

Examples

A longicorn beetle specimen
Collectors prize rare specimens of the longicorn beetle for their unique appearance and impressive size.
Many types of wood-boring pests, including the longicorn beetle, can damage timber structures.
The longicorn beetle is known for its strikingly long antennae, sometimes exceeding its own body length.
