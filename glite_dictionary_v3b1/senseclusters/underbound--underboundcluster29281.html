underbound

adjective
insufficiently bound
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbaʊnd / (BrE)
bound with less material or strength than is standard or required, especially referring to books or documents

Examples

An underbound book
The rare edition was underbound, causing the pages to detach after only a few uses.
Because the report was underbound, the cover started to fall off within a week.
Libraries often avoid purchasing underbound books due to their lack of durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loose - not firmly or tightly fixed in place
  • antonym: well-bound - securely and properly bound
