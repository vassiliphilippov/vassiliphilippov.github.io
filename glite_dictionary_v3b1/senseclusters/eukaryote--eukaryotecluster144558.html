eukaryote

noun
organism with membrane-bound nucleus
8.3
Pronunciation: / juˈkæriˌoʊt / (AmE), / juːˈkæri.əʊt / (BrE)
an organism whose cells have a membrane-bound nucleus and other specialized organelles, distinct from bacteria and archaea

Examples

A eukaryote cell
All animals and plants are classified as eukaryotes because their cells contain nuclei.
Fungi are also considered eukaryotes due to their complex cell structure.
The distinction between prokaryotes and eukaryotes is fundamental in biology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prokaryote - an organism whose cells lack a membrane-bound nucleus
