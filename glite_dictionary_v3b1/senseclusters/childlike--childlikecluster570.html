childlike

adjective
having qualities of a child (innocence/trust)
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (BrE)
(of an adult) having the good qualities, such as innocence, trust, or simplicity, that are typical of a child

Examples

Childlike innocence
He viewed the world with a childlike sense of wonder.
Her childlike curiosity made her a joy to work with.
The artist captured a childlike innocence in her paintings.
Despite her age, she maintained a childlike trust in the goodness of people.
He looked at the painting with a childlike sense of wonder.
Her childlike enthusiasm was infectious and made the experience more enjoyable for everyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: childish - (of an adult or their behavior) silly or immature; typical of a child in a negative way
