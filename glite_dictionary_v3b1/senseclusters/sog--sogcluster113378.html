sog

verb
to make very wet
Pronunciation: / sɑɡ / (AmE), / sɒɡ / (BrE)
to make something very wet, heavy, or soggy by soaking it with liquid

Examples

Sauce sogs bread
The heavy sauce sogged the bread, making it impossible to eat.
After the rainstorm, water sogged the ground and made it muddy.
She accidentally sogged her notes by spilling her drink on them.
The heavy rain began to sog the field, making it difficult to walk across.
If you leave the towel outside, the dew will sog it overnight.
Spilled juice can sog your papers if you are not careful.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dampen - to make something slightly wet
  • antonym: dry - to remove moisture from something
  • synonym: soak - to make something completely wet by immersing it in liquid
