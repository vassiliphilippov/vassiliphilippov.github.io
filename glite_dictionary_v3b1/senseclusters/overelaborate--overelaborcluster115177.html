overelaborate

verb
to add too much detail
8.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.və.rɪ.ˈlæb.ə.reɪt/ /; / /oʊ.və.rə.ˈlæb.ə.reɪt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.rɪ.ˈlæb.ə.reɪt/ /; / /əʊ.və.rə.ˈlæb.ə.reɪt/ (BrE)
to add excessive detail or complexity to something, making it more complicated than necessary

Examples

To overelaborate a story
Try not to overelaborate your explanation, or people may get confused.
The report was difficult to read because the author had overelaborated every section.
If you overelaborate your ideas, the main message might be lost.
