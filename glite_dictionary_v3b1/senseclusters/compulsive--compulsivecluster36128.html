compulsive

adjective
motivated by an irresistible urge
4.1 4.5
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpʌl.sɪv/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpʌl.sɪv/ (BrE)
unable to control an urge or behavior; driven by a strong, irresistible need to do something

Examples

Compulsive behavior
He was a compulsive achiever, never satisfied with ordinary results.
Her compulsive need to succeed pushed her to work late every night.
The compulsive desire for perfection sometimes led her to neglect her own well-being.
He sought help for his compulsive gambling before it ruined his finances.
Her compulsive need to check her phone made it difficult to focus at work.
The film explores the causes of compulsive lying and its effects on relationships.

Related Terms

  • synonym: driven - highly motivated and determined person
  • synonym: obsessive - persistently preoccupied with a particular idea or activity
  • antonym: unmotivated - lacking drive or willingness to act
