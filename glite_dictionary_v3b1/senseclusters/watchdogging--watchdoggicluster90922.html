watchdogging

noun
system fault monitoring
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑtʃˌdɔɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌdɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of using a hardware or software watchdog to monitor a system and automatically detect and recover from faults

Examples

System watchdogging
The engineers implemented watchdogging to ensure the server would automatically recover from crashes.
Without proper watchdogging, the device could freeze and require manual intervention.
Modern embedded systems rely on watchdogging to maintain continuous operation and reliability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monitoring - the act of observing and checking something over time
  • synonym: supervision - the act of overseeing a process or activity
