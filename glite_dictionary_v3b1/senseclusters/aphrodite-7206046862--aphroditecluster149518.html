Aphrodite

proper noun
Greek goddess of love and beauty
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌæf.rəˈdaɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˌæf.rəˈdaɪ.ti / (BrE)
The goddess of love and beauty in ancient Greek mythology, traditionally regarded as a daughter of Zeus and identified with the Roman goddess Venus.

Examples

Aphrodite goddess of love
In Greek mythology, Aphrodite was born from the sea foam.
Many ancient sculptures depict Aphrodite as the ideal of beauty.
The story of Aphrodite and Adonis is well known in classical literature.
