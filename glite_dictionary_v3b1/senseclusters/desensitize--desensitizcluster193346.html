desensitize

verb
make less sensitive
6.1 5.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsen.sə.taɪz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈsen.sɪ.taɪz/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsen.sɪ.taɪz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈsen.sə.taɪz/ (BrE)
to make someone or something less sensitive or less responsive to physical, emotional, or other forms of stimulation, usually by repeated exposure

Examples

To desensitize viewers
Repeated exposure to violent movies can desensitize viewers to real-life violence.
Some allergy treatments aim to desensitize patients to specific allergens.
Years of working in the emergency room have desensitized her to the sight of blood.
Continuous exposure to violent media can desensitize viewers to real-life suffering.
Doctors sometimes desensitize patients to allergens through gradual exposure.
Repeated warnings about storms may desensitize people, causing them to ignore alerts.
Repeated exposure to violence can desensitise people to its impact.
The doctor used cream to desensitise the patient's skin before the injection.
Watching horror films may desensitise viewers over time.
