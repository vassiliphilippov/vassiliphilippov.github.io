tin ear

noun
insensitivity to language or music
9.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / tɪn ɪr / (AmE), / tɪn ɪə / (BrE)
an inability or insensitivity to appreciate musical sounds, pitch, or subtleties in music, language, or tone

Examples

Have a tin ear
Despite being a famous critic, she has a tin ear for dialogue in her own novels.
His tin ear made him oblivious to the awkward phrasing in his speech.
Some politicians seem to have a tin ear when it comes to the language of ordinary people.
Despite years of lessons, her tin ear prevented her from enjoying music fully.
He wanted to join the choir, but his tin ear made it nearly impossible for him to sing in tune.
With my tin ear, even the simplest melodies sound confusing and off-key.
Despite his enthusiasm, his tin ear made singing in tune impossible.
She has a tin ear for diplomatic language and often offends people unintentionally.
When it comes to poetry, his tin ear prevents him from appreciating subtle rhythms.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ear - ability to perceive or appreciate sounds or music
  • antonym: musical ear - ability to perceive and appreciate music accurately
  • antonym: perfect pitch - the ability to identify or reproduce a given musical note without a reference tone
  • synonym: tone-deaf - unable to perceive differences in musical pitch
