entrustment

noun
formal transfer of responsibility
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrʌstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrʌstmənt / (BrE)
the act of formally giving someone responsibility for something important or valuable, often involving trust

Examples

The entrustment of responsibility
The entrustment of the company's finances to an experienced accountant brought peace of mind to the owners.
Legal documents confirmed the entrustment of guardianship to her uncle after her parents passed away.
Proper entrustment of duties ensures that important tasks are managed by qualified individuals.
The entrustment of the company's finances to a new manager required careful consideration.
Parents felt a great sense of entrustment when leaving their children with the babysitter.
Her entrustment with the museum's artifacts made her proud and cautious every day.
The entrustment of confidential documents to the new manager was a sign of trust.
Parents often feel anxious about the entrustment of their children to a babysitter.
Successful entrustment of duties can help a team work more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assignment - the act of transferring a right or responsibility to another
  • synonym: delegation - the act of giving someone else authority or responsibility
  • antonym: retention - the act of keeping something rather than giving it to someone else
  • antonym: withholding - the act of keeping something back or not giving it
