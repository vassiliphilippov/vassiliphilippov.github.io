tangle

verb
involve in a complicated situation
Pronunciation: / ˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to involve someone in a difficult or complicated situation, often unwillingly or by accident

Examples

To tangle someone in trouble
He managed to tangle her in his legal troubles despite her warnings.
The journalist became tangled in a web of conflicting interests during her investigation.
Try not to tangle yourself in office politics at your new job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embroil - to involve deeply in an argument or difficult situation
  • synonym: entangle - to get someone involved in a difficult or complicated situation
