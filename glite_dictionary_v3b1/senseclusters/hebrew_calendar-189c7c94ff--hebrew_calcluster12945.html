Hebrew calendar

noun
Jewish lunisolar calendar system
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːbruː ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the calendar traditionally used in Judaism, based on lunar months and periodically adjusted to the solar year, dating creation to 3761 BC.

Examples

The Hebrew calendar year
In 2022, Rosh Hashanah began on a different date in the Hebrew calendar compared to the Gregorian calendar.
Many Jewish holidays are determined by the Hebrew calendar rather than the Gregorian calendar.
The Hebrew calendar occasionally includes a leap month to keep its months aligned with the seasons.
