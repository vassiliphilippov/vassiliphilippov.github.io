nonconcurrent

adjective
not simultaneous
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
not occurring or existing at the same time as something else

Examples

Two nonconcurrent events
The two meetings are nonconcurrent, so you can attend both without any conflict.
Because the classes are nonconcurrent, students can enroll in each one during different semesters.
The research studies were nonconcurrent, making direct comparison of results more difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concurrent - existing or happening at the same time
  • antonym: simultaneous - happening or existing at the same time
