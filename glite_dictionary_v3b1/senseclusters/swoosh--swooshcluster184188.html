swoosh

noun
a rushing sound (e.g. of air or water)
5.8 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / swuÀê É / (AmE), / swuÀê É / (BrE)
a rushing sound produced by the sudden movement of air, water, or another fluid

Examples

A swoosh of wind
I heard a swoosh as the wind rushed past the open window.
The swoosh of water filled the room as the waves crashed against the shore.
With a swoosh, the basketball zipped through the net.

Synonyms

  • whoosh
    4.2 1.16/m
    whoosh is a rushing sound from moving air or liquid often used in writing, while swoosh refers to the sudden smooth sound of one movement.
  • whiz
    6.4 0.26/m
    whiz is a buzzing or hissing sound made by something moving very fast through air, while swoosh is a rushing fluid sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whoosh - a soft sound made by something moving quickly through the air
