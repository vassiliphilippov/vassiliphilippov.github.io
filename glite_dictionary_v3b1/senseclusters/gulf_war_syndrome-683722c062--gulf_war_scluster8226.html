Gulf War syndrome

noun
medical condition affecting 1991 Gulf War veterans
Pronunciation: / ɡʌlf wɔr ˈsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ɡʌlf wɔː ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a medical condition of uncertain origin affecting veterans of the 1991 Gulf War, characterized by symptoms such as fatigue, headaches, dizziness, nausea, rashes, joint pain, and respiratory problems

Examples

Gulf War syndrome symptoms
Doctors still debate how best to diagnose and treat Gulf War syndrome.
Many veterans have reported symptoms associated with Gulf War syndrome since returning home.
The origins and causes of Gulf War syndrome remain a subject of ongoing medical research.
