Colin Powell

proper noun
U.S. general and Secretary of State (1937–2021)
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊlɪn ˈpaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪn ˈpaʊəl / (BrE)
(1937–2021) United States general and statesman, the first African American to serve as Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, and later Secretary of State under President George W. Bush.

Examples

Colin Powell Secretary of State
Colin Powell was appointed Secretary of State in 2001 by President George W. Bush.
As Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, Colin Powell played a major role in the Gulf War.
Many admired Colin Powell for his leadership and dedication to public service.
