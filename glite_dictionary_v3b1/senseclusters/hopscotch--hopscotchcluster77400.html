hopscotch

noun
children's hopping game
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑpˌskɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɒpˌskɒtʃ / (BrE)
a children's game where players hop through numbered spaces drawn on the ground, often using a small object as a marker

Examples

Play hopscotch outside
Memories of carefree summers often include games of hopscotch in the park.
She drew the hopscotch pattern with chalk and invited her friends to join.
The children were playing hopscotch on the sidewalk after school.
During recess, the students eagerly played hopscotch together.
She tossed her stone onto the hopscotch board and began hopping.
The children drew a hopscotch grid on the pavement with chalk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skipping - a game involving jumping over a rope swung under the feet
