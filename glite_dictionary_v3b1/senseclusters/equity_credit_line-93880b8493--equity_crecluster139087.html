equity credit line

noun
loan secured by home equity
9.7 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkwəti ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈekwɪti ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (BrE)
a loan that is secured by the equity value in the borrower's home, allowing the homeowner to borrow against the value of their property

Examples

An equity credit line loan
If you have sufficient equity in your house, you may qualify for an equity credit line.
The bank offered her a lower interest rate on the equity credit line compared to a personal loan.
They used an equity credit line to finance their home renovation project.
