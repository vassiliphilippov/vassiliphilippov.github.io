breech-loading

adjective
of guns, loaded at the rear (breech)
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ ˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / briːtʃ ˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a gun) designed to be loaded at the rear end of the barrel rather than the muzzle.

Examples

Breech-loading gun
During the demonstration, the instructor explained how a breech-loading shotgun functions.
Many 19th-century cannons were converted to breech-loading mechanisms for improved firepower.
The army adopted breech-loading rifles because they could be reloaded more quickly than older muskets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: muzzle-loading - (of a gun) designed to be loaded from the open end of the barrel (the muzzle)
