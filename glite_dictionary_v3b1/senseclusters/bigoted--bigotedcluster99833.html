bigoted

adjective
intolerant of differing beliefs or opinions
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪɡətɪd / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡətɪd / (BrE)
Having or showing a strong, unreasonable attachment to a particular belief or opinion, and an intolerance toward those who hold different views.

Examples

Bigoted remarks
Many people found his bigoted remarks deeply offensive and divisive.
She refused to engage in bigoted conversations about other cultures.
The school promotes respect and understanding instead of bigoted behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust based on preconceived ideas
  • antonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow opinions that differ from one's own
