byproduct

noun

A secondary result, often unintended or unexpected, that happens in addition to the main outcome.

Examples

Success as a byproduct
Innovation is often a byproduct of creative problem-solving rather than the main goal itself.
Stress can be a byproduct of working too many hours without rest.
The new law had some unexpected byproducts that affected small businesses.
