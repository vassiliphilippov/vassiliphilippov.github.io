radula

noun
mollusk feeding organ
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrædʒələ / (AmE), / ˈradjʊlə / (BrE)
a ribbon-like organ in mollusks that is used to scrape or cut food before ingestion

Examples

Snail radula organ
The snail uses its radula to scrape algae from rocks in the aquarium.
Scientists examined the radula of the mollusk to understand its feeding habits.
Without a functioning radula, the sea slug would be unable to eat properly.
