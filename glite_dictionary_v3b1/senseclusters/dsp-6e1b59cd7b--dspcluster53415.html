DSP

noun
digital signal processing
Pronunciation: / diː ɛs piː / (AmE), / diː ɛs piː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for digital signal processing, a method of analyzing and modifying digital signals such as audio or video

Examples

DSP improves sound
Many smartphones use DSP to improve the quality of phone calls and music playback.
Engineers rely on DSP to filter out noise from recorded audio signals.
The new software update includes advanced DSP features for video enhancement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digital signal processing - the use of digital methods to analyze and change signals
