genus Agaricus

noun
genus of mushrooms including edible species
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈɡærɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈɡarɪkəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Agaricaceae, comprising gill fungi with brown spores, including several edible species such as the common mushroom

Examples

The genus Agaricus mushrooms
Biologists often study the genus Agaricus to learn more about fungal diversity and classification.
Some species within the genus Agaricus are prized for their edibility, while others can be toxic.
The commercial cultivation of genus Agaricus has made mushrooms widely available throughout the world.

Related Terms
