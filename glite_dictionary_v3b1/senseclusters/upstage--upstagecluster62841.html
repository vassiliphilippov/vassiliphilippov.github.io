upstage

verb
attract attention away from someone
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʌpˈsteɪdʒ /; / ˈʌpˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌʌpˈsteɪdʒ /; / ˈʌpsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
to attract attention away from someone else, especially by outshining them

Examples

To upstage a friend
He was careful not to upstage his friend at the awards ceremony.
Her quick wit always tends to upstage her colleagues during meetings.
The comedian tried not to upstage the host, but the audience kept laughing at his jokes.
He hates it when someone tries to upstage him at family gatherings.
She tried to upstage her colleague during the meeting by interrupting with her own ideas.
The comedian's unscripted remarks completely upstaged the main performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eclipse - to make someone less important or impressive by comparison
  • synonym: outshine - to be more impressive or attractive than someone else
