dec

noun
celestial coordinate; declination (astronomy)
9.9
Pronunciation: /dek/ (AmE), /dek/ (BrE)
(astronomy) The angular distance of a celestial body north or south of the celestial equator, measured in degrees and used with right ascension to specify celestial positions.

Examples

Star dec angle
Astronomers recorded the star’s dec as +45 degrees, placing it well north of the celestial equator.
The spreadsheet includes the dec and RA values for every object in the catalog.
To locate the nebula, you need its right ascension and dec coordinates.
