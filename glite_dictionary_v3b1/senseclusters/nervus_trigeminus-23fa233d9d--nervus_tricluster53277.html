nervus trigeminus

noun
main sensory nerve of the face
Pronunciation: / ˌnɜrvəs traɪˈdʒɛmənəs / (AmE), / ˌnɜːvəs traɪˈdʒɛmɪnəs / (BrE)
the main sensory nerve of the face and the motor nerve controlling the muscles of chewing (mastication)

Examples

The nervus trigeminus nerve
Damage to the nervus trigeminus can lead to a sudden onset of facial pain.
The nervus trigeminus plays a crucial role in transmitting facial sensations to the brain.
The dentist explained that anesthesia would temporarily numb the nervus trigeminus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trigeminal nerve - main sensory nerve of the face and motor to muscles of mastication
