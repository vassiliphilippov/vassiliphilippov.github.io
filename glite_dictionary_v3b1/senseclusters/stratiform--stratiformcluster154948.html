stratiform

adjective
layered in structure
Pronunciation: / ˈstrætɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈstrætɪfɔːm / (BrE)
formed or arranged in layers, especially describing clouds or precipitation that is widespread and brings steady rain

Examples

Stratiform clouds
The meteorologist explained that stratiform clouds usually bring steady, light rain.
Sedimentary rocks often have a stratiform appearance due to their layered formation.
A blanket of stratiform fog covered the valley early in the morning.
The weather report predicted stratiform clouds bringing steady light rain throughout the afternoon.
Pilots often encounter stratiform precipitation during long flights across temperate regions.
Unlike thunderstorms, stratiform rain usually falls gently and covers a wide area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cumuliform - having the form of cumulus clouds, not layered
  • synonym: layered - arranged in layers
