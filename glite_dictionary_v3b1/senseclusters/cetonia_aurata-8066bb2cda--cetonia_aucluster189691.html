Cetonia aurata

noun
metallic green European beetle (rose chafer)
9.3
Pronunciation: / siˈtoʊniə ɔˈrɑːtə / (AmE), / sɛˈtəʊniə ɔˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
A common metallic green European beetle whose larvae feed on plant roots and adults feed on leaves and flowers, especially of roses; also known as the rose chafer.

Examples

Cetonia aurata beetle
Gardeners often notice Cetonia aurata feeding on the petals of their roses each summer.
The larvae of Cetonia aurata are known to cause damage to the roots of ornamental plants.
The metallic sheen of the Cetonia aurata makes it one of the most eye-catching beetles in European gardens.
