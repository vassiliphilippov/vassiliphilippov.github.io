phenomenism

noun
focus on sensory experience
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑːməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / fəˈnɒmɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
an artistic or literary approach that emphasizes immediate sensory experience and surface appearance rather than deeper meaning

Examples

Phenomenism in art
The critic noted the phenomenism in the author's descriptions, which focused on vivid sensory details.
Her paintings are celebrated for their phenomenism, capturing the play of light and color in each moment.
Modern poetry sometimes adopts phenomenism to evoke immediate feelings rather than hidden meanings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impressionism - an art movement focusing on capturing immediate impressions
  • antonym: realism - an artistic style aiming to depict subjects as they appear in everyday life
