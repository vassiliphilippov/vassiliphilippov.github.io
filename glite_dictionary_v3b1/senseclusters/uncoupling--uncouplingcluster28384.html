uncoupling

noun
disrupted energy process
9.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌplɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌplɪŋ / (BrE)
a process in which the normal connection between oxidation and ATP production in cells is disrupted, causing energy to be released as heat instead of stored.

Examples

Mitochondrial uncoupling
Researchers observed increased uncoupling in the mitochondria of animals exposed to cold temperatures.
The process of uncoupling can help regulate body heat by releasing energy as heat instead of storing it.
Some drugs are designed to induce uncoupling in cells to affect metabolism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coupling - the normal connection between oxidation and ATP production in cells
