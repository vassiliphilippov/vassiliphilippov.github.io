begrudgingly

adverb
reluctantly
6.5
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡrʌdʒɪŋli / (AmE), / bɪˈɡrʌdʒɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that shows you do something unwillingly or with resentment

Examples

To begrudgingly accept
She begrudgingly handed over the keys after losing the bet.
They begrudgingly accepted the new rules set by management.
He begrudgingly apologized for his mistake, clearly not happy about it.
She begrudgingly agreed to help her brother with his homework, even though she wanted to relax.
He begrudgingly congratulated his rival after losing the competition.
The team begrudgingly accepted the new rules, though they preferred the old system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: willingly - in a way that shows readiness or eagerness
