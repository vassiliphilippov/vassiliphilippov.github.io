damsel

noun
young unmarried woman (archaic/literary)
9.0 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdæm.zəl/ (AmE), /ˈdæm.zəl/ (BrE)
a young, unmarried woman, especially one regarded as delicate or in need of protection; now used mainly in literary or old-fashioned contexts

Examples

The damsel in distress
In the tale, a brave knight came to rescue the damsel from the dragon.
She looked like a damsel straight out of a romantic medieval story.
Tired of being treated as a helpless damsel, she decided to solve her own problems.
