spatchcock

verb
split and flatten poultry for cooking
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈspætʃ.kɑk / (AmE), / ˈspætʃ.kɒk / (BrE)
to prepare a bird, especially poultry, by splitting it open and flattening it for roasting or grilling

Examples

To spatchcock a bird
She has never spatchcocked a bird before, but the instructions were easy to follow.
The chef decided to spatchcock the chicken before grilling it over the open flame.
You should spatchcock your turkey; it cooks faster and more evenly that way.
