own the road

verb
to drive confidently or assertively
Pronunciation: / oʊn ðə roʊd / (AmE), / əʊn ðə rəʊd / (BrE)
to drive in a confident or assertive manner, often behaving as if no one else matters on the road

Examples

To own the road confidently
He acted like he owned the road, weaving in and out of traffic without a care.
The sports car seemed to own the road as it sped past slower vehicles.
Whenever she gets behind the wheel, she tries to own the road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominate - to have control or power over
