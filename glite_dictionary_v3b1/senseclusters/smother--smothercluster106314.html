smother

verb
cover completely, envelop
6.3 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðə / (BrE)
to cover something completely so that it is difficult to see or reach

Examples

To smother pancakes with syrup
She likes to smother her pancakes in syrup every morning.
Snow began to smother the garden, hiding all signs of the plants underneath.
The chef will smother the vegetables in a rich, creamy sauce.
A thick fog smothered the city, hiding buildings and streets from view.
Heavy snow smothered the garden, burying the flowers and shrubs under a white blanket.
She smothered the pancakes with syrup until not a single spot was visible.
