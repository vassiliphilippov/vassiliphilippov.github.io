catarrhal fever

noun
livestock disease causing fever and respiratory swelling
9.2 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfiː.vər/ (AmE), /ˈfiː.və/ (BrE)
Any of several infectious diseases of livestock characterized by fever and inflammation (especially swelling and discharge) of the respiratory tract.

Examples

Catarrhal fever in livestock
Farmers are advised to vaccinate to reduce the risk of catarrhal fever in their livestock.
Outbreaks of catarrhal fever can cause serious economic losses in dairy herds.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with catarrhal fever after noticing swelling in the respiratory tract.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cold - a mild viral infection with catarrhal symptoms in humans
  • degree higher: malignant catarrhal fever - a severe viral disease of cattle, sheep, and other ungulates
