A toxic, white crystalline acidic compound derived from benzene, used in the manufacture of chemicals and as a disinfectant and antiseptic; also known as phenol.

Examples

Carbolic acid fumes
Exposure to carbolic acid fumes can cause severe irritation of the eyes and respiratory system.
In the early 20th century, carbolic acid was commonly used as an antiseptic despite its toxicity.
The surgeon used carbolic acid to disinfect the instruments before the operation.
