incardination

noun
clergy attachment process
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kɑːr.dɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kɑːr.də.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.kɑː.dɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kɑː.də.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the formal process by which a clergy member becomes officially attached to a specific diocese or church authority

Examples

The incardination process
After years of service abroad, his incardination into the local diocese was finally approved.
The bishop explained the process of incardination to the new priests during the orientation.
Without proper incardination, a priest cannot officially serve in a different diocese.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excardination - the formal release of a clergy member from a diocese or church authority
  • synonym: ordination - the process of officially making someone a member of the clergy
