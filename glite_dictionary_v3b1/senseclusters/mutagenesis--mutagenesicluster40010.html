mutagenesis

noun
process causing genetic mutation
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːtəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtəˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which genetic mutations are produced, typically through exposure to physical or chemical agents

Examples

Chemical mutagenesis
Chemical mutagenesis is often used to create new plant varieties for agricultural research.
Researchers study mutagenesis to understand how genetic diseases develop in humans.
The bacteria underwent mutagenesis after being exposed to radiation in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutation - a change in the DNA sequence of an organism
