forward pass

noun
a pass thrown downfield in sports
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrwɚd pæs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd pɑːs / (BrE)
a throw or pass of the ball towards the opponent's end of the field, typically from behind the line of scrimmage, often to gain distance downfield

Examples

A forward pass in football
It is illegal to throw more than one forward pass on a single play in American football.
The quarterback threw a forward pass that was caught for a touchdown.
The team's strategy relies heavily on the forward pass to move the ball quickly down the field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lateral pass - a pass thrown parallel to or away from the opponent's goal line
