ejection seat

noun
emergency aircraft seat for pilot escape
Pronunciation: / ɪˈdʒɛkʃən sit / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛkʃən siːt / (BrE)
A special seat in an aircraft that can be forcibly ejected in an emergency, allowing the pilot to escape and descend by parachute.

Examples

An ejection seat in aircraft
After the crash, investigators found the empty ejection seat several miles from the wreckage.
Modern fighter jets are equipped with an ejection seat for each crew member.
The pilot pulled the lever and was launched from the jet by the ejection seat.
