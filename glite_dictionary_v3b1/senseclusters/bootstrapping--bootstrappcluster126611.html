bootstrapping

noun
system initialization process
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of starting or building a computer system or program from a minimal initial state, often by loading basic code first.

Examples

Computer bootstrapping
The bootstrapping sequence loads the operating system when you turn on your computer.
Developers often use bootstrapping to compile a new programming language from a minimal base.
Understanding bootstrapping is essential for anyone interested in system architecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: initialization - the process of preparing something for use, especially in computing
  • antonym: shutdown - the process of turning off a computer system
