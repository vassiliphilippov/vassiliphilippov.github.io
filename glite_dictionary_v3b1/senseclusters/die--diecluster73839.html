die

verb
cease to live
1.6 1.0
Pronunciation: /daɪ/ (AmE), /daɪ/ (BrE)
to stop living; to cease to be alive (including literal and spiritual death)

Examples

To die peacefully
Her grandmother died peacefully at home last night.
Many soldiers died during the war.
Without water, the plants will quickly die.
Many hope to peacefully die in their sleep at an old age.
No one wants to die alone without loved ones by their side.
Some species of salmon die shortly after spawning in the river.
He feared that without forgiveness, his soul would die eternally.
Many believe that those who turn away from faith will die in a spiritual sense.
The preacher claimed that those who reject love and compassion are destined to die spiritually.

Related Terms

  • antonym: live - to remain alive; the opposite of die
  • synonym: pass away - polite or softened synonym for 'die'
  • synonym: perish - to die, especially in a sudden or violent way
