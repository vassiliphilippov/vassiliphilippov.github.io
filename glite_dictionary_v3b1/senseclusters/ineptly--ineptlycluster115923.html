ineptly

adverb
in a clumsy or unskillful way
8.6 4.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈept.liː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈept.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that shows a lack of skill, ability, or competence; clumsily or incompetently

Examples

To ineptly handle tasks
He tried to fix the computer, but he did it ineptly and made things worse.
She handled the situation ineptly, causing confusion among the staff.
The project was ineptly managed from the beginning, leading to its failure.
She tried to explain the problem, but ineptly made it more confusing.
The inexperienced waiter handled the order ineptly, causing several mistakes.
The stage crew moved the props ineptly, leading to visible mishaps during the scene.
