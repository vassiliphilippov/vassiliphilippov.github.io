powder monkey

noun
person who carried gunpowder on warships
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊdər ˌmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə ˌmʌŋki / (BrE)
a person, especially a young boy, who carried gunpowder from the magazine to the guns on a warship, especially during the age of sail

Examples

The powder monkey on ship
During the battle, the powder monkey risked his life ferrying gunpowder to the cannons.
Many powder monkeys were young boys chosen for their speed and agility.
The dangerous work of a powder monkey was crucial during naval engagements.
