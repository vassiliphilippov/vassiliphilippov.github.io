semiliquid

adjective
partly liquid; between solid and liquid
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈlɪkwɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈlɪkwɪd / (BrE)
Partly liquid; having a consistency between solid and liquid.

Examples

A semiliquid substance
At room temperature, honey is a semiliquid that flows slowly.
The lava oozed out of the volcano in a thick semiliquid state.
The mixture should have a semiliquid texture before you pour it into the pan.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: liquid - A substance that flows freely but is of constant volume.
  • degree lower: solid - Not liquid or fluid; firm and stable in shape.
