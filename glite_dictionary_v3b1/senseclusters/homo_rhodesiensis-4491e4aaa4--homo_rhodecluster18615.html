Homo rhodesiensis

noun
extinct African early human species
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊmoʊ roʊˌdiːziˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ rəʊˌdiːziˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
an extinct species of early human known from fossil remains in Africa, resembling Neanderthals in some features

Examples

Homo rhodesiensis fossils
Fossil evidence of Homo rhodesiensis has provided important insights into human evolution in Africa.
Many researchers debate whether Homo rhodesiensis should be considered a separate species or part of Homo heidelbergensis.
The skull attributed to Homo rhodesiensis was discovered in Zambia in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Neanderthal - an extinct species or subspecies of archaic humans who lived in Eurasia
