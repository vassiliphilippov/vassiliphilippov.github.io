inclined

adjective
sloping; at an angle
3.9 0.72/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆklaÉªnd / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆklaÉªnd / (BrE)
sloping or forming an angle with a horizontal or vertical surface

Examples

Inclined plane
An inclined plane helps to move heavy objects upward with less effort.
The roof is inclined at a steep angle to allow snow to slide off.
We walked up the inclined path that led to the summit.

Synonyms

  • sloping
    3.1 0.01/m
    sloping describes a surface that rises or falls gently, while inclined refers to something set at an angle to level.
  • slanted
    3.9 0.01/m
    slanted leans or tilts to one side and looks uneven, while inclined means placed at an angle to a horizontal or vertical line.
  • tilted
    2.5 0.03/m
    tilted is used for something moved from upright or level, while inclined describes a fixed slope or angled position.
  • angled
    3.3 1.25/m
    angled is set at a particular angle often by design, while inclined simply describes a general sloping position.
  • tipped
    3.8 0.00/m
    tipped leans or hangs with one end lower than the other, while inclined names any object set on an angle.
  • pitched
    4.3 0.00/m
    pitched describes roofs or surfaces built with a slant for rain run-off, while inclined covers any angled surface.
  • oblique
    7.5 0.96/m
    oblique slants in a less direct or more technical way and feels formal, while inclined is common descriptive language for slopes.
