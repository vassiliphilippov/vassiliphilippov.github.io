sight

verb
to catch sight of; to notice visually
Pronunciation: / saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
to suddenly see or notice something or someone, especially briefly or unexpectedly

Examples

To sight a friend
He quickly sighted his friends in the crowded room.
She sighted a rare bird while walking through the forest.
The sailor sighted land after many days at sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overlook - to fail to notice something
  • synonym: see - to perceive with the eyes; to look at
  • synonym: spot - to notice or recognize something or someone, especially in a crowd
