FCS

noun
NCAA Football Championship Subdivision
7.2
Pronunciation: /ef siː es/ /; / /əf siː əs/ (AmE), /ef siː es/ /; / /əf siː əs/ (BrE)
(in U.S. college sports) the Football Championship Subdivision, the NCAA Division I subdivision of football that determines its champion via playoffs.

Examples

FCS football playoffs
North Dakota State advanced to the national semifinals in the FCS playoffs last season.
Several small universities choose the FCS because it fits their athletic budgets and scholarships.
His coaching career began with an FCS program known for developing NFL players.

Related Terms

  • antonym: FBS - the Football Bowl Subdivision, the other NCAA Division I football subdivision that uses bowl games and different scholarship rules.
