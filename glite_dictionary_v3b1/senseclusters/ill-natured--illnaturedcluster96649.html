ill-natured

adjective
irritable, unpleasant in temperament
4.9
Pronunciation: / ɪl ˈneɪtʃərd / (AmE), / ɪl ˈneɪtʃəd / (BrE)
Having a bad temper or unpleasant disposition; easily annoyed or unkind.

Examples

An ill-natured person
Despite his ill-natured reputation, he occasionally shows kindness to others.
He can be quite ill-natured when things do not go his way.
Her ill-natured remarks quickly ruined the cheerful atmosphere in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bad-tempered - easily annoyed or made angry
  • antonym: good-natured - having a pleasant, kind, and friendly disposition
