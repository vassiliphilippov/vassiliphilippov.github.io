Synonyms

  • release
    2.7 20/m
    release lets a substance, gas, or energy go free in many situations, while discharge often also means emptying contents.
  • effuse
    9.1 0.00/m
    effuse pours out or emits a substance freely and abundantly, while discharge is a general term for letting something out.
  • exude
    6.5 0.2/m
    exude sends out liquid slowly in drops or small amounts, while discharge covers all ways of letting substances out.
  • eject
    4.1 0.9/m
    eject forces a liquid or object out quickly, often by a sudden push, while discharge can be gentle or technical.
  • expel
    5.0 2/m
    expel forces a substance out, usually by strong pressure or authority, while discharge simply means to let it out.
  • exhaust
    4.8 0.5/m
    exhaust expels gas or vapour from a system like an engine, while discharge also includes general releasing or emptying.
  • empty
    1.0 8/m
    empty removes all contents so a container has no substance left, while discharge can mean any release, not total removal.
