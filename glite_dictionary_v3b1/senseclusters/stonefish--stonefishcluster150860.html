stonefish

noun
venomous marine fish resembling a rock
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a venomous tropical marine fish that resembles a piece of rock, found in the Indo-Pacific region

Examples

A stonefish sting
A stonefish can deliver a venomous sting if provoked or touched.
The diver was careful not to step on a stonefish hidden among the rocks.
While snorkeling, she spotted a stonefish camouflaged on the ocean floor.
