pitched battle

noun
organized, fierce battle at a chosen site
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃt ˈbæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / pɪtʃt ˈbætl̩ / (BrE)
a fierce and organized battle in which opposing forces fight in predetermined positions at a chosen time and place

Examples

A pitched battle
After weeks of maneuvering, the generals finally agreed to a pitched battle to settle the war.
Historians described Waterloo as a classic example of a pitched battle.
The two armies prepared for a pitched battle in the open field.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: skirmish - a minor or brief fight between small groups of troops
