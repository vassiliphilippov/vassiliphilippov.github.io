submillimeter

noun
wavelengths under 1 mm
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈmɪlɪˌmitər / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈmɪlɪˌmiːtə / (BrE)
the part of the electromagnetic spectrum with wavelengths shorter than one millimeter, often used in astronomy and remote sensing

Examples

Submillimeter astronomy
Astronomers use submillimeter observations to study the cold dust in distant galaxies.
The telescope is designed to detect submillimeter wavelengths for advanced space research.
Scientists analyze submillimeter data to learn more about the formation of stars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infrared - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light but shorter than microwaves
  • degree lower: microwave - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than submillimeter waves
