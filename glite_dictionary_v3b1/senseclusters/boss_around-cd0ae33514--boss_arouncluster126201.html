boss around

verb
to act bossy or domineering towards someone
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɒs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / bɒs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to give orders to someone in an annoying or domineering way, especially when you have no right to do so

Examples

To boss around siblings
As the oldest sibling, she would sometimes boss around her younger brothers.
His manager would often boss around the team, making everyone feel frustrated.
Stop trying to boss around your classmates; they don't like it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bully - to intimidate or mistreat someone weaker or in a subordinate position
