decapod crustacean

noun
crustacean with five pairs of legs
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkəˌpɑd krʌˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəpɒd krʌˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a crustacean with five pairs of legs attached to the thorax, including crabs, lobsters, and shrimp

Examples

Decapod crustacean species
Crabs, as decapod crustaceans, have a hard shell and ten limbs.
Marine biologists study decapod crustaceans to understand their role in ocean ecosystems.
The lobster is a well-known decapod crustacean found in many parts of the world.
