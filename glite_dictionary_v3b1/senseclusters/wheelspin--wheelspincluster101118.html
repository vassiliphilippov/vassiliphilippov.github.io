wheelspin

noun
loss of traction
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwiːl.spɪn/ (AmE), /ˈwiːl.spɪn/ (BrE)
the rapid spinning of a vehicle's wheels without gaining traction on the surface, often causing loss of control

Examples

Car wheelspin on ice
The icy road caused severe wheelspin as the car tried to move forward.
Too much acceleration on wet pavement can result in wheelspin and loss of control.
The driver eased off the gas to reduce wheelspin on the slippery surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skid - a sliding movement of a vehicle caused by loss of traction
  • antonym: traction - the grip of a tire on a road or surface
