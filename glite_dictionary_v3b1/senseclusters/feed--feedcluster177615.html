feed

verb
exploit or profit from something
1.8 36/m
Pronunciation: / fid / (AmE), / fiÀêd / (BrE)
to supply or provide something (such as information, material, or support), either literally or figuratively, to a process, system, or feeling

Examples

To feed information
Certain companies feed on people's fear to sell their products.
He was accused of trying to feed on her vulnerability during the negotiations.
Scammers often feed on people's lack of technical knowledge.
Her determination feeds her ambition to succeed.
Rumors like these only feed the public's anxiety about safety.
The data you enter will feed directly into the results analysis.
Fresh data is constantly fed into the system for real-time analysis.
The operator feeds raw material into the machine to keep production running smoothly.
You should feed the paper into the shredder slowly to avoid jams.

Synonyms

  • feed in
    3.8 0.23/m
    feed in supplies material continuously into a machine or system, while feed means to provide things more generally to a process.
  • feed into
    4.1 1.85/m
    feed into contributes information or influence that helps shape something, while feed is a broader act of supplying material or support to a system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exploit - to use someone or something for one's own benefit, especially unfairly
  • synonym: gratify - to give pleasure or satisfaction to
  • synonym: inject - to introduce something forcibly or deliberately
  • synonym: nourish - to provide with substances necessary for growth; often used figuratively
  • antonym: starve - to deprive something of what is needed, especially sustenance or resources
  • synonym: supply - to provide or make available what is needed
