subtly

adverb
in a delicate or indirect way
5.1 5.52/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒtli / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒtli / (BrE)
In a manner that is so delicate or precise as to be difficult to analyze or describe; not obvious or blatant.

Examples

To subtly change
She subtly changed the topic to avoid answering the question directly.
The artist subtly blended the colors to create a smooth transition between shades.
Their criticism was subtly expressed, so as not to offend anyone.

Synonyms

  • imperceptibly
    7.5 1.28/m
    imperceptibly is almost undetectable and happens slowly, while subtly shows a small, clever difference that is not obvious.
  • faintly
    2.7 6.14/m
    faintly means only a weak or small amount is felt or seen, while subtly means a quiet, clever difference.
  • delicately
    3.7 4.56/m
    delicately is done gently with care or sensitivity, while subtly is about slight, hard to notice meaning or effect.
