Mimosaceae

noun
family of plants with sensitive leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / mɪˌmoʊsəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / mɪˌməʊsəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of spiny woody plants (usually shrubs or small trees), known for sensitive leaves that respond to touch; often grouped within the family Leguminosae.

Examples

Mimosaceae plant family
Botanists often classify Mimosaceae as a subfamily within the Leguminosae.
Some members of the Mimosaceae respond visibly to touch due to their sensitive leaves.
The Mimosaceae family includes plants such as acacia and mimosa.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Leguminosae - the pea family; the broader plant family that often includes Mimosaceae
