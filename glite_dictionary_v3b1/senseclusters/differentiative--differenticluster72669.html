differentiative

noun
distinguishing feature
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃiˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃɪətɪv / (BrE)
a feature or factor that distinguishes one thing from another

Examples

A differentiative factor
The presence of a unique protein acted as a differentiative between the two cell types.
Language can be a powerful differentiative in identifying cultural groups.
In the study, color was the main differentiative used to separate the categories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinction - a difference that sets things apart
  • antonym: similarity - a quality that makes things alike
