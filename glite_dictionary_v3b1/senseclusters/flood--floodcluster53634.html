flood

verb
supply or fill with an excess of
Pronunciation: / flʌd / (AmE), / flʌd / (BrE)
to supply or fill something with a very large or excessive amount, especially more than is needed or wanted

Examples

To flood the market
Cheap imports can flood local markets, harming domestic producers.
During the sale, the store was flooded with discounted merchandise.
Every spring, companies flood the market with new smartphone models.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deplete - to use up or reduce the supply of
  • synonym: glut - to supply or fill to excess
