jerked meat

noun
dried strips of preserved meat
8.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /dʒɜːrkt miːt/ (AmE), /dʒɜːkt miːt/ (BrE)
Meat, especially beef, that has been cut into strips and dried in the sun or by wind, often for preservation.

Examples

Traditional jerked meat
She learned to make traditional jerked meat by drying strips of beef in the sun.
The pioneers relied on jerked meat as a staple food during their long journeys.
You can store jerked meat for months without refrigeration.

  • synonym: jerky - dried, salted meat, especially beef
