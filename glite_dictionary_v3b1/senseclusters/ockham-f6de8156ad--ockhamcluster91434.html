Ockham

proper noun
English philosopher; formulated Occam's Razor
Pronunciation: / ˈɑkəm / (AmE), / ˈɒkəm / (BrE)
(William of Ockham) English scholastic philosopher (c.1285–1347/49) known for formulating Occam's Razor, a principle of parsimony in reasoning.

Examples

Ockham razor principle
In his lectures, he often referenced the work of Ockham on logic and theology.
Many philosophers discuss Ockham's views on nominalism and simplicity.
The principle of parsimony is often attributed to Ockham and known as Occam's Razor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Occam's Razor - principle that the simplest explanation is usually correct
