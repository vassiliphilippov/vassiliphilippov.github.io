probiotic

noun
beneficial microorganism, often in the gut
3.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /proʊ.baɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk/ /; / /prəʊ.baɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /prəʊ.baɪ.ˈɒ.tɪk/ (BrE)
A bacterium or microorganism that is beneficial to health, especially those found in the digestive system.

Examples

Probiotic bacteria
Scientists continue to research the effects of probiotic supplements on overall immunity.
Some yogurt brands contain live probiotic cultures that support digestive health.
Taking a daily probiotic may help restore balance to your gut microbiome after antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prebiotic - a substance that promotes the growth of beneficial microorganisms
