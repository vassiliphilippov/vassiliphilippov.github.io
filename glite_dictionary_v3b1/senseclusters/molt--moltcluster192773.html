molt

noun
shedding of skin or outer layer in animals
Pronunciation: / moʊlt / (AmE), / məʊlt / (BrE)
the process by which an animal periodically sheds its outer covering, such as feathers, skin, cuticle, or exoskeleton

Examples

A bird's molt
A bird's molt may affect its ability to fly temporarily.
Snakes undergo a molt several times a year as they grow.
The tarantula was sluggish before its last molt.
After its annual moult, the crab emerges with a larger, softer shell.
Many birds look scruffy during their moult in late summer.
The snake undergoes a moult several times a year as it grows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: molt - alternative spelling (American English) for the shedding process in animals
  • synonym: molting - process of shedding skin or feathers
