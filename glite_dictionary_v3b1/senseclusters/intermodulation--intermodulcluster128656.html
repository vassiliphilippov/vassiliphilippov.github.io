intermodulation

noun
mixing of signals
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the mixing of two or more signals to produce additional frequencies not present in the original signals

Examples

Intermodulation distortion
Engineers must account for intermodulation when designing radio systems.
The presence of intermodulation can cause unwanted noise in audio equipment.
Reducing intermodulation is important for clear communication in wireless networks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interference - the effect of overlapping signals causing distortion
  • synonym: modulation - the process of varying a signal to transmit information
