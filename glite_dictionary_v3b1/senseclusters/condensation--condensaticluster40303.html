condensation

noun
change from gas to liquid or solid
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.dənˈseɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkɑn.dɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a gas changes into a liquid or solid, or the result of this process (such as water droplets forming on a surface)

Examples

Condensation on glass
Condensation formed on the windows as the warm air met the cold glass.
The condensation of water vapor into droplets is what creates clouds.
You can see condensation on a cold drink on a hot day.
Clouds form in the atmosphere due to the condensation of water vapor.
The engineer explained how condensation inside the pipes could affect the system’s efficiency.
The process of condensation increases the density of a gas as it turns into a liquid.
Drops of condensation formed on the outside of the cold drink glass.
Overnight, condensation collected on the windows due to the drop in temperature.
The condensation on the bathroom mirror made it difficult to see after my shower.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compression - the act of pressing something into a smaller space
  • antonym: evaporation - the process of a liquid changing into a vapor
  • antonym: expansion - the process or result of becoming larger or more extensive
