turn a blind eye

verb
deliberately ignore wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / tɝn ə blaɪnd aɪ / (AmE), / tɜːn ə blaɪnd aɪ / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore something wrong, illegal, or unacceptable, pretending not to notice it

Examples

To turn a blind eye to problems
Many governments turn a blind eye to environmental violations.
She couldn't turn a blind eye any longer to the signs of neglect.
The manager chose to turn a blind eye to the team's frequent tardiness.
Many government officials were accused of turning a blind eye to corruption in the city.
She often turns a blind eye to her son's mischief, believing he will learn from experience.
The teacher decided to turn a blind eye to the playful note passing during class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignore - to refuse to take notice of or acknowledge
  • synonym: overlook - to fail to notice or consider; to ignore on purpose
