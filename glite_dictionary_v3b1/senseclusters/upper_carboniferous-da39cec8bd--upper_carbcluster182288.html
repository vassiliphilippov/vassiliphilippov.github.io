Upper Carboniferous

noun
later subdivision of the Carboniferous period
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpɚ ˌkɑrbəˈnɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˌkɑːbəˈnɪfərəs / (BrE)
the later subdivision of the Carboniferous period, occurring approximately 310 to 280 million years ago, known for its warm climate and extensive swampy land

Examples

The Upper Carboniferous period
Fossil remains from the Upper Carboniferous provide valuable insights into prehistoric plant life.
Geologists study rock layers from the Upper Carboniferous to understand Earth's ancient environments.
The Upper Carboniferous was a time of vast swamp forests that later formed major coal deposits.
