industrialization

noun
development of industry on a large scale
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌdʌstriələˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪnˌdʌstriəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdʌstriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which an economy is transformed from primarily agricultural to one based on the manufacturing of goods and services, usually involving the extensive development of industry.

Examples

The industrialization process
As industrialization spread, countries experienced significant economic and environmental changes.
Many historians believe industrialization improved living standards but also created new social problems.
The rapid industrialization of the 19th century transformed many rural areas into bustling cities.
Environmental challenges often accompany rapid industrialisation.
Government policies played a vital role in fostering industrialisation throughout the nineteenth century.
The rapid industrialisation of the country transformed the lives of millions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: urbanization - the process by which towns and cities are formed and become larger
