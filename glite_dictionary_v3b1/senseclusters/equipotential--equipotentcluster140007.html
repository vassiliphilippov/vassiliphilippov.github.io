equipotential

noun
area of equal potential
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkwəpəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪpəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a line, surface, or region where every point has the same potential, especially in an electric or gravitational field

Examples

Equipotential line
The scientist marked the equipotential on the map to indicate areas of equal voltage.
In physics class, we learned how to draw an equipotential around a charged object.
The equipotential helped engineers design safer electrical systems by showing where the voltage was constant.
