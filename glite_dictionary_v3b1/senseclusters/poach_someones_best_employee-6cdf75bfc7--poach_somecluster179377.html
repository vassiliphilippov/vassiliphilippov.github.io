poach someone's best employee

verb
lure away another firm's top employee
Pronunciation: / poʊtʃ sʌmˈwʌnz bɛst ɛmˈplɔɪi / (AmE), / pəʊtʃ sʌmˈwʌnz bɛst ɪmˈplɔɪi / (BrE)
to try to persuade the most talented or valuable employee of another company or organization to leave their job and work for you instead

Examples

Rival firms often attempt to poach someone’s best employee during industry downturns when morale is low.
