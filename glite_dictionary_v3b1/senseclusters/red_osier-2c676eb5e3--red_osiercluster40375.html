red osier

noun
North American shrub with red twigs and white flowers
Pronunciation: / ɹɛd ˈoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɹɛd ˈəʊziə / (BrE)
a common North American shrub (Cornus sericea) with reddish-purple twigs and white flowers, often found in wetlands

Examples

Red osier shrub
Gardeners often plant red osier for its attractive stems and wildlife value.
In early summer, the red osier blooms with clusters of small white flowers.
The wetland was lined with red osier that added a splash of color even in winter.
