merely

adverb
only; simply and nothing more
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪrli / (AmE), / ˈmɪəli / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is only what is stated and nothing more or less; simply.

Examples

To merely say
I merely pointed out the mistake; I wasn't criticizing anyone.
She wasn't angry; she merely wanted some answers.
The instructions are merely suggestions, not strict rules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: only - no more than; nothing other than
  • synonym: simply - only; merely and nothing more
