stay over

verb
spend the night away from home
1.0
Pronunciation: / steɪ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / steɪ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to spend the night somewhere other than one's home, especially by invitation.

Examples

To stay over at home
If you miss the last train, you can always stay over at my place.
Sarah decided to stay over after the dinner party rather than drive home late at night.
The children often stay over at their grandparents' house on weekends.
