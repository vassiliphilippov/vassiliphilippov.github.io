beleaguer

verb
surround and besiege
8.4 7.5
Pronunciation: /bə.ˈliː.gər/ /; / /bɪ.ˈliː.gər/ (AmE), /bə.ˈliː.gə/ /; / /bɪ.ˈliː.gə/ (BrE)
to surround a place with armed forces, typically to force it to surrender; to besiege

Examples

To beleaguer a city
During the war, the capital became heavily beleaguered and isolated from reinforcements.
Enemy troops continued to beleaguer the city through the winter, cutting off all supplies.
The fortress was beleaguered for months before finally surrendering to the attackers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encircle - to form a circle around; to surround
  • antonym: liberate - to set free, especially from occupation or oppression
