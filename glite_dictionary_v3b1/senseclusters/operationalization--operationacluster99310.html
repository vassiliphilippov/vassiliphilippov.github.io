operationalization

noun
defining for measurement
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpəˌreɪʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˌreɪʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of defining a concept so that it can be measured, tested, or implemented in practice (especially in research)

Examples

Operationalization of a concept
The operationalization of abstract ideas is essential for scientific research.
Without proper operationalization, it is difficult to measure social phenomena accurately.
Researchers discussed the operationalization of key variables before starting the experiment.
The operationalization of stress in this study involved using a standardized questionnaire.
Clear operationalization of variables is essential for reliable scientific research.
Different researchers may use different operationalizations for the same psychological concept.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceptualization - the process of forming an idea or concept
  • synonym: definition - a statement that explains the meaning of a word or concept
  • synonym: implementation - the act of putting a plan or system into operation
  • synonym: measurement - the process of determining the size, amount, or degree of something
