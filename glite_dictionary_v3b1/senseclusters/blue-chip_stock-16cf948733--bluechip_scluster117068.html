blue-chip stock

noun
share in a financially stable major company
Pronunciation: / bluː tʃɪp stɑk / (AmE), / bluː tʃɪp stɒk / (BrE)
A share of a large, nationally recognized company known for its financial stability, reliability, and consistent dividends.

Examples

A blue-chip stock company
Buying a blue-chip stock is often considered a safe investment strategy.
Many investors seek out blue-chip stocks for their long-term portfolios.
The steady performance of blue-chip stocks makes them popular during uncertain economic times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: growth stock - a stock expected to grow at an above-average rate compared to other companies
  • antonym: penny stock - stock of a small company that trades at a low price, usually with higher risk
