forth

adverb
away from a place; out from a position
3.8
Pronunciation: / fɔrθ / (AmE), / fɔːθ / (BrE)
away from a starting point or forward from a particular time or place (especially in formal or literary contexts)

Examples

From this day forth
From this day forth, we vowed to remember our promise.
He commanded his followers to go forth and spread his teachings.
With a heavy heart, she set forth from her childhood home.
From the sixth century forth, major changes transformed the region.
Let it be known from this day forth that the policy will change.
The tradition has continued from that year forth in our community.
