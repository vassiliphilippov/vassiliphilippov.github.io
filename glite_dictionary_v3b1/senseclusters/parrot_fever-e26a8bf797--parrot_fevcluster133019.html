parrot fever

noun
infectious disease caught from birds, esp. parrots
5.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpær.ət ˈfiː.vər/ /; / /ˈpe.rət ˈfiː.vər/ (AmE), /ˈpær.ət ˈfiː.və/ /; / /ˈpe.rət ˈfiː.və/ (BrE)
An infectious disease (psittacosis) in humans, caused by bacteria transmitted from infected birds, especially parrots, leading to atypical pneumonia.

Examples

Parrot fever infection
After rescuing an abandoned parrot, she developed a high fever and was diagnosed with parrot fever.
Proper hygiene is essential to prevent the spread of parrot fever from birds to humans.
The veterinarian warned that parrot fever can be transmitted to people who handle sick birds.
