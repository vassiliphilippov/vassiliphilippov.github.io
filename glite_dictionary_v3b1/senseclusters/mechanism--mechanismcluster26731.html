mechanism

noun
philosophical theory of physical explanation
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛkənɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The theory that all natural phenomena can be explained entirely by physical or biological processes, without recourse to metaphysics or the supernatural.

Examples

Mechanism in philosophy
Critics of mechanism claim it overlooks the role of subjective experience.
Some philosophers argue that mechanism cannot account for consciousness.
The rise of mechanism in the 17th century marked a shift in scientific thinking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vitalism - the theory that living things are fundamentally different from non-living things because of a 'vital force'
