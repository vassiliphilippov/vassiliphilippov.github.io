Phalacrocorax carbo

noun
large dark seabird used in traditional fishing
Pronunciation: / fæˌlækrəˌkɔræks ˈkɑrboʊ / (AmE), / fæˌlɑːkrəˌkɔːræks ˈkɑːbəʊ / (BrE)
A large, dark-colored seabird (the great cormorant) with a long neck and a distensible pouch for holding fish, sometimes trained in Asia for fishing.

Examples

The Phalacrocorax carbo bird
A Phalacrocorax carbo can consume large numbers of fish due to its voracious appetite.
Fishermen in some Asian countries have trained the Phalacrocorax carbo to help them catch fish.
The Phalacrocorax carbo is often seen perched on rocks, drying its wings in the sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cormorant - large, dark waterbird known for its fishing ability
