tire

noun
rubber covering for a wheel
Pronunciation: / taɪɚ /; / taɪər / (AmE), / taɪə / (BrE)
a thick rubber covering, typically filled with air, that fits around the rim of a wheel of a vehicle and provides traction, absorbs shock, and protects the wheel

Examples

A car tire
Bicycle tires can be inflated with a simple hand pump.
I had to replace the tire on my car after it went flat.
The mechanic checked each tire for wear and tear before the long trip.
It's important to check your car's tyre pressure regularly for safe driving.
She noticed that the front tyre of her bicycle was flat after riding over a sharp stone.
The mechanic replaced the worn tyre with a new one before the long journey.
