affect

verb
pretend to feel (to deceive)
Pronunciation: / əˈfɛkt / (AmE), / əˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to pretend to have or feel something, especially to deceive others

Examples

To affect an accent
Despite his nervousness, he managed to affect a calm demeanor during the interview.
He would often affect an interest in sports just to fit in with his friends.
She tried to affect indifference when she saw her ex at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feign - to pretend to be affected by a feeling or injury
  • synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
