pellucid

adjective
transparently clear; allowing light through
Pronunciation: / pəˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / pɛˈljuːsɪd / (BrE)
(especially of a liquid or material) transparently clear; allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen.

Examples

Pellucid water
She admired the pellucid sky, a flawless blue expanse stretching overhead.
Sunlight poured through the pellucid waters of the mountain stream, revealing the smooth stones beneath.
The vase was crafted from pellucid glass, allowing every detail inside to be seen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • degree lower: translucent - allowing light to pass through diffusely
  • synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
