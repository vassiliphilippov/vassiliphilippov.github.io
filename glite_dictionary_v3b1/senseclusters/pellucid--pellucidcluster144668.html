pellucid

adjective
transparently clear; allowing light through
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôÀàluÀês…™d / (AmE), / p…õÀàljuÀês…™d / (BrE)
(especially of a liquid or material) transparently clear; allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen.

Examples

Pellucid water
She admired the pellucid sky, a flawless blue expanse stretching overhead.
Sunlight poured through the pellucid waters of the mountain stream, revealing the smooth stones beneath.
The vase was crafted from pellucid glass, allowing every detail inside to be seen.

Synonyms

  • limpid
    7.4 0.2/m

  • crystal clear
    3.1 0.4/m
    crystal clear feels like everyday speech for perfect transparency, while pellucid is a single formal adjective used in writing.
  • hyaline
    9.9 0.03/m
    hyaline is a technical word for glass-like transparency seen in biology or medicine, while pellucid is general and literary.
  • crystalline
    6.3 0.7/m
    crystalline suggests clarity like a crystal and sharp form, while pellucid stresses clear, see-through material rather than structure.
  • clear
    1.0 40/m
    clear is a broad everyday word for something free from cloud or blocks, while pellucid means perfectly see-through and precise.
  • lucid
    4.8 0.1/m
    lucid often means mentally clear or easy to understand, while pellucid refers to a visibly clear liquid or surface.
