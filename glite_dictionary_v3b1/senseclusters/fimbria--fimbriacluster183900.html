fimbria

noun
fringe-like projection around a Fallopian tube
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪmbriə / (AmE), / ˈfɪmbriə / (BrE)
a fringe or fringe-like structure, especially at the end of a Fallopian tube or on the surface of some bacteria

Examples

The fimbria of tube
Damage to the fimbria can affect fertility in women.
During ovulation, the fimbria sweep over the ovary to capture the released egg.
The fimbria of the Fallopian tube helps guide the egg from the ovary.
The fimbria of the fallopian tube helps capture the egg after ovulation.
Certain bacteria use fimbria to attach themselves to host cells.
Under the microscope, the fimbria appeared as delicate, finger-like projections.
