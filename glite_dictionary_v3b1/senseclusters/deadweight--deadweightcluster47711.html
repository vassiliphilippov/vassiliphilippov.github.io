deadweight

noun
test or ballast weight
5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈded.weɪt/ (AmE), /ˈded.weɪt/ (BrE)
a heavy, inert weight used as ballast or to test the strength or capacity of equipment or structures

Examples

A large deadweight was placed on the platform to ensure the scale was accurate.
They removed the deadweight after completing the safety inspection of the bridge.
