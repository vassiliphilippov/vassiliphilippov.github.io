nervous strain

noun
nervousness from mental stress
Pronunciation: / ˈnɝːvəs streɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs streɪn / (BrE)
A state of nervousness or tension caused by prolonged mental stress or pressure.

Examples

Nervous strain from work
After weeks of working overtime, he finally broke down from nervous strain.
Exams and deadlines caused a great deal of nervous strain for the students.
The manager recognized the nervous strain his team was under and called for a break.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stress - mental or emotional strain resulting from challenging circumstances
