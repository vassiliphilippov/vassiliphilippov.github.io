intercrop

noun
crop grown with others
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.tər.krɑːp/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.tə.krɒp/ (BrE)
a crop grown among or between other crops in the same field to improve yield, soil fertility, or pest control

Examples

A legume intercrop
Farmers often plant an intercrop of legumes to improve soil nitrogen levels.
The use of an intercrop can help reduce pest problems in the main crop.
By adding an intercrop, the field produced a higher overall yield that season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover crop - a crop grown to protect and enrich the soil
  • antonym: monoculture - the cultivation of a single crop in a given area
