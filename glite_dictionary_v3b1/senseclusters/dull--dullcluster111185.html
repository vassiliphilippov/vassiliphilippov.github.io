dull

adjective
lacking color brightness/saturation
Pronunciation: / dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
not bright, shiny, or vivid; lacking color, light, or luster (used for colors, surfaces, weather, or light)

Examples

Dull colors
After several washes, the shirt's once bright red was now dull and faded.
She preferred lively hues instead of the dull greens in the fabric.
The paint on the wall had become a dull shade of blue over the years.
I prefer bright days to dull ones, as the lack of sunshine affects my mood.
The sky was dull and overcast, making the whole afternoon seem gloomy.
Winter mornings are often dull, with barely any sunlight breaking through the thick clouds.
Clouds covered the sky, leaving the afternoon in a dull gray light.
Her jewelry looked particularly dull and needed to be polished.
The metal had a dull finish after years of exposure to the elements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - full of light or sunshine (of weather or the sky)
  • antonym: lustrous - having a gentle sheen or soft glow
  • antonym: shiny - having a bright or glossy surface
  • antonym: vivid - (of a color) intensely deep or bright
