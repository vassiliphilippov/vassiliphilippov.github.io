agonist

noun
drug or substance that activates a receptor
6.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæ.gə.nɪst/ (AmE), /ˈæ.gə.nɪst/ (BrE)
a substance, such as a drug, that binds to a receptor in a cell and triggers a physiological response

Examples

Opioid agonist drug
Opioid agonists are used to treat pain by activating opioid receptors in the brain.
Researchers compared the effects of the agonist to those of an antagonist in the experimental group.
The new drug acts as an agonist by stimulating the same receptors as the natural hormone.

  • antonym: antagonist - a substance that interferes with or inhibits the physiological action of another
