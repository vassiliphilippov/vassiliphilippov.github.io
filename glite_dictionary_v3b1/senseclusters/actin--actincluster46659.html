actin

noun
a protein involved in cell structure and movement
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪn / (AmE), / ˈæktɪn / (BrE)
a protein found in muscle and other cells, existing in either a globular or fibrous form, that plays a key role in cell movement and structure

Examples

Actin protein
Muscle contraction relies on the interaction between actin and myosin filaments.
Researchers observed the formation of actin fibers in the migrating cells.
The structure of actin is crucial for maintaining cell shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: myosin - another protein that interacts with actin in muscle contraction
