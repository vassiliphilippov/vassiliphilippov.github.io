unstudied

adjective
lacking knowledge from study
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstʌd.id / (AmE), / ʌnˈstʌd.id / (BrE)
lacking knowledge or familiarity gained through study, often in a particular subject or field

Examples

Unstudied in history
He remains unstudied in the classics despite years at university.
Her opinions on astronomy are quite unstudied and lack depth.
The applicant appeared unstudied in recent technological developments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • antonym: learned - having acquired much knowledge through study
