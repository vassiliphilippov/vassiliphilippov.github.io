go without saying

verb
be obvious, not need stating
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ wɪðaʊt ˈseɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪðaʊt ˈseɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
to be so obvious or self-evident that it does not need to be stated

Examples

It goes without saying
It goes without saying that one should always double-check their work before submitting it.
It goes without saying that trust is important in any relationship.
Your hard work goes without saying, and it's greatly appreciated by everyone.
