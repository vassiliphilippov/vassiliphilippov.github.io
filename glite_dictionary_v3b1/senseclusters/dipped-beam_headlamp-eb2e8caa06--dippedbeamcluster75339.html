dipped-beam headlamp

noun
vehicle headlamp setting (low beam)
8.7 4.3
Pronunciation: / dɪpt biːm ˈhɛdˌlæmp / (AmE), / dɪpt biːm ˈhɛdlamp / (BrE)
A headlamp setting on a vehicle that provides sufficient forward and side light without causing glare to other drivers; also known as low beam.

Examples

Use dipped-beam headlamp
Always use your dipped-beam headlamp when driving at night in city traffic.
Make sure your dipped-beam headlamp is working properly before driving in foggy conditions.
The car's dipped-beam headlamps automatically switch on when it detects another vehicle ahead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high beam - a setting on a vehicle's headlamp providing maximum forward illumination
  • synonym: low beam - a setting on a vehicle's headlamp providing less intense light for oncoming traffic
