shrink from

verb
avoid or be unwilling to do something
Pronunciation: / ʃrɪŋk frəm / (AmE), / ʃrɪŋk frɒm / (BrE)
to avoid doing something that is difficult, unpleasant, or one's responsibility, often because of fear or unwillingness

Examples

To shrink from duty
He would never shrink from his responsibilities, no matter how challenging they became.
Many leaders shrink from making unpopular decisions, but true leadership requires courage.
She did not shrink from confronting the issue, even though it was uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shirk - to avoid or neglect (a duty or responsibility)
