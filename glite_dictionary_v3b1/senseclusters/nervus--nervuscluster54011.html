nervus

noun
Latin for nerve
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs / (BrE)
a Latin term meaning 'nerve', used in anatomical or scientific names for nerves.

Examples

Nervus medianus
In anatomy textbooks, the word nervus is often used in the names of specific nerves.
The facial nerve is sometimes referred to as nervus facialis in scientific literature.
Medical students learn the Latin term nervus when studying the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nerve - a bundle of fibers transmitting impulses in the body
