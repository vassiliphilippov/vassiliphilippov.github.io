adequation

noun
philosophical correspondence
9.9
Pronunciation: /æ.də.ˈkweɪ.ʃən/ /; / /æ.dɪ.ˈkweɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /æ.də.ˈkweɪ.ʃən/ /; / /æ.dɪ.ˈkweɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the correspondence or agreement between a thought, representation, or theory and the reality it describes, especially in philosophy

Examples

Adequation of theory and reality
The philosopher discussed the adequation of human knowledge to objective reality.
Some theories are criticized for lacking adequation with observable evidence.
Debates about truth often focus on the adequation between language and the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correspondence - a close similarity or match between two things
  • antonym: discrepancy - a lack of compatibility or similarity between two or more facts
