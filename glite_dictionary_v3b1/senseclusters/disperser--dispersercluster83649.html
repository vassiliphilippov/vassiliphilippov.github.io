disperser

noun
organism that spreads
6.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɜrsər / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsə / (BrE)
an individual organism that moves away from its birthplace or group to settle elsewhere, helping spread genes or establish new populations

Examples

Animal disperser
A single disperser can introduce new genetic material to an isolated population.
Some seeds rely on animals as dispersers to reach distant locations.
The study tracked each disperser to understand how new colonies are formed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonizer - an organism that establishes itself in a new area
  • synonym: migrant - an organism that moves from one region to another
  • antonym: resident - an organism that stays in its birthplace or original group
