presumer

noun
arrogant person
9.9
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzuːmər / (AmE), / prɪˈzjuːmə / (BrE)
a person who acts arrogantly or takes liberties, assuming they have the right to do so without permission

Examples

An arrogant presumer
The board saw him as a presumer when he made decisions without consulting anyone.
No one appreciated the presumer who always acted as if he was in charge.
She was called a presumer for taking credit for the team's work without asking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humble person - someone who is modest and does not take liberties
  • synonym: presumptuous person - someone who oversteps boundaries or acts without proper authority
