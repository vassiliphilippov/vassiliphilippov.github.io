weak

adjective
not strong in intensity or magnitude
4.4
Pronunciation: / wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
not strong or intense in degree, clarity, brightness, loudness, or force; barely perceptible or deficient in magnitude.

Examples

Weak voice
He spoke in such a weak voice that no one could hear him clearly.
The weak light from the lamp barely illuminated the room.
The colors in the painting had become weak over time, losing their original vibrancy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faint - barely perceptible; lacking strength, clarity, or intensity
  • antonym: strong - having great intensity or force
