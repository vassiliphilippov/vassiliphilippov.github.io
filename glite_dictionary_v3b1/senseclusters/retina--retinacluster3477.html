retina

noun
light-sensitive membrane in the eye
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtənə / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪnə / (BrE)
the thin, light-sensitive membrane lining the back of the eye that receives images and sends visual signals to the brain via the optic nerve

Examples

The retina of eye
Light entering the eye is focused on the retina, where it is converted to nerve signals.
Scientists study diseases of the retina to better understand how we see.
The doctor explained that damage to the retina can lead to serious vision problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cornea - transparent front part of the eye
  • synonym: optic nerve - nerve transmitting visual information from retina to brain
