fiducial

adjective
based on trust; serving as a reference standard
Pronunciation: / fɪˈduːʃəl / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəl / (BrE)
used as a fixed standard or reference point for comparison or measurement, especially in scientific or technical contexts

Examples

Fiducial marker
Scientists use a fiducial marker to ensure accurate measurements in their experiments.
The agreement is fiducial in nature, relying on mutual trust between both parties.
The banker acted in a fiducial capacity, holding assets on behalf of his client.
A fiducial line was drawn to serve as the baseline for all future surveys.
Scientists rely on fiducial points in an image to calibrate their measurements.
The engineer used a fiducial marker to ensure accurate alignment of the components.
