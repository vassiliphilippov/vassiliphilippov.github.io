cash flow

noun
money received and spent over a period
4.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /kæʃ floʊ/ (AmE), /kæʃ fləʊ/ (BrE)
the total amount of money being received and spent by a business or individual during a particular period, especially the difference between income and outgoings, not including non-cash expenses.

Examples

Positive cash flow
A negative cash flow can signal financial trouble if it persists for several quarters.
Strong cash flow is essential for small businesses to meet their operational expenses.
The company's cash flow improved significantly after launching the new product line.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: profit - financial gain after all expenses
  • degree higher: revenue - total income generated before expenses
