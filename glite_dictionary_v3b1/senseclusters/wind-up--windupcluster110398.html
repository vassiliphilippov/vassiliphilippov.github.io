wind-up

noun
company closure process
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
the formal process of closing a company and distributing its assets to pay creditors

Examples

Company wind-up
The company entered wind-up proceedings after failing to recover from its financial losses.
Creditors were paid from the assets during the wind-up of the business.
The board discussed the legal steps required for the wind-up at the final meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bankruptcy - the legal status of being unable to pay debts
  • synonym: liquidation - the process of bringing a business to an end and distributing its assets
