group insurance

noun
insurance bought collectively by a group
6.1
Pronunciation: / ɡruːp ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ɡruːp ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
insurance purchased by a group, typically employees of a company, providing coverage to individual members usually at a reduced rate.

Examples

Group insurance coverage
By joining the firm, you become eligible for comprehensive group insurance coverage.
Many workers prefer group insurance because it often has lower premiums than individual policies.
The company offers group insurance as part of its employee benefits package.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individual insurance - insurance policy purchased by an individual rather than a group
  • synonym: life insurance - insurance that pays out a sum on the death of the insured person
