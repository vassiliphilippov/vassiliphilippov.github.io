tobacco hornworm

noun
a green caterpillar pest of tobacco plants
5.8 7.2
Pronunciation: /tə.ˈbæk.əʊ ˈhɔːrn.wɜːrm/ /; / /tə.ˈbæk.oʊ ˈhɔːrn.wɜːrm/ (AmE), /tə.ˈbæk.əʊ ˈhɔːn.wɜːm/ (BrE)
a large green caterpillar with white stripes that feeds on tobacco and related plants, often a pest in gardens and farms

Examples

The tobacco hornworm caterpillar
A single tobacco hornworm can cause significant damage to a tobacco crop if left unchecked.
Gardeners often struggle to control the tobacco hornworm because it can quickly strip plants of their leaves.
The tobacco hornworm is distinguished from its close relative, the tomato hornworm, by its seven white stripes.
