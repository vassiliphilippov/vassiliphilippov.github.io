relief

noun
the reduction or removal of something unpleasant
2.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈliːf/ /; / /riː.ˈliːf/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈliːf/ (BrE)
the reduction or removal of pain, worry, or discomfort, or the feeling of comfort and happiness when something unpleasant ends

Examples

A sigh of relief
He sighed with relief when he realized the danger had passed.
She felt immediate relief after taking the medication.
The doctor promised that the new treatment would bring some relief from her symptoms.
It was a huge relief to finally finish the exam.
She breathed a sigh of relief when she heard the good news.
The rain was a welcome relief after weeks of drought.
After weeks of worrying about the exam, she felt immense relief when it was finally over.
He sighed in relief when he heard that his family was safe.
The cool breeze brought a sense of relief on the hot summer day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravation - the act of making something worse or more unpleasant
  • synonym: alleviation - the act of making something less severe
  • antonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or unease
  • synonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint
  • antonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • synonym: ease - a state of being comfortable or free from stress
