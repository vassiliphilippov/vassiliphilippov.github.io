gregarious

adjective
sociable; fond of company
9.8
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈɡɛriəs / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡeəriəs / (BrE)
(of a person) fond of company; sociable.

Examples

Gregarious people socialize
Many salespeople are gregarious and thrive in social environments.
She was so gregarious that her parties were always lively and well-attended.
Unlike his gregarious friends, Mark preferred to read quietly at home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: introverted - preferring to be alone or with a few close friends
  • synonym: sociable - enjoying being with other people
