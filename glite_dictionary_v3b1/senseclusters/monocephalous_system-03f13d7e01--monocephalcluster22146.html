monocephalous system

noun
system governed by a single leader
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˈsɛfələs ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈsɛfələs ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system or organization governed by a single leader or authority.

Examples

A monocephalous system leader
Critics argue that a monocephalous system may stifle innovation and dissent.
In a monocephalous system, decisions are typically made faster but with less input from others.
The government operated as a monocephalous system with supreme authority placed in the president.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polycephalous system - a system led by multiple leaders or authorities
