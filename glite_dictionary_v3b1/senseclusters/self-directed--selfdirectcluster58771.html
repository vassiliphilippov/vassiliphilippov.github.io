self-directed

adjective
of an account controlled by the holder
6.6
Pronunciation: /self.dɪ.ˈrek.tɪd/ /; / /self.daɪ.ˈrek.tɪd/ (AmE), /self.dɪ.ˈrek.tɪd/ /; / /self.daɪ.ˈrek.tɪd/ (BrE)
(of an investment account or financial vehicle) controlled and managed by the account holder rather than by a custodian, trustee, or financial advisor; allowing the holder to choose and manage investments directly.

Examples

Self-directed IRA
He opened a self-directed IRA to choose his own stocks and real estate investments.
Many investors prefer self-directed accounts when they want control over alternative investments.
With a self-directed plan, she could add private equity that standard IRAs would not allow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: custodial (account) - an account managed by a custodian or financial institution rather than directly by the account holder.
  • synonym: self-managed - managed directly by the investor rather than by a professional manager or advisor.
