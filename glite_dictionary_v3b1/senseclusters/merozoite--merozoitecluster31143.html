merozoite

noun
asexual cell stage in sporozoan life cycle
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛroʊˈzɔɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɛrəʊˈzɔɪt / (BrE)
a cell formed by the asexual division of a sporozoan parasite, especially in the life cycle of malaria-causing organisms, which can invade new host cells

Examples

A merozoite cell
After invading red blood cells, each merozoite matures and divides again.
Scientists observed that the release of merozoites coincided with fever spikes in patients.
The malaria parasite multiplies in the liver, producing thousands of merozoites that enter the bloodstream.
