narcotic

adjective
inducing mental lethargy
Pronunciation: / nɑrˈkɑtɪk / (AmE), / nɑːˈkɒtɪk / (BrE)
causing a state of mental sluggishness or drowsiness; inducing mental lethargy.

Examples

Narcotic effect
Her voice had a narcotic effect, making it hard for anyone to stay focused.
The film's slow pace and repetitive dialogue created a narcotic atmosphere that lulled viewers into boredom.
The professor's narcotic lecture left several students nearly asleep by the end.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stimulating - causing increased alertness or activity
