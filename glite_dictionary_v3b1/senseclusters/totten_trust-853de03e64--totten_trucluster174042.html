Totten trust

noun
bank account payable on death to beneficiary
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːtən trʌst / (AmE), / ˈtɒtən trʌst / (BrE)
A bank account in which the depositor names themselves as trustee for another person, so that the funds pass directly to the beneficiary upon the depositor's death.

Examples

A Totten trust account
Banks often recommend a Totten trust as a simple estate planning tool.
He set up a Totten trust so his granddaughter would receive the funds automatically.
The money in the Totten trust went directly to her son after she passed away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: payable-on-death account - bank account that transfers funds to a beneficiary upon the account holder's death
