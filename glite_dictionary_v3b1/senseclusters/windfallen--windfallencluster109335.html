windfallen

adjective
blown down by wind
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndˌfɔlən / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌfɒlən / (BrE)
having fallen to the ground as a result of being blown down by the wind, especially referring to fruit or wood

Examples

Windfallen fruit
After the storm, the ground was covered with windfallen branches from the old oak tree.
Farmers quickly gathered the windfallen apples before they spoiled in the wet grass.
The carpenter used windfallen wood to make rustic furniture for the cottage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallen - having dropped to the ground
  • antonym: standing - still upright and not fallen
