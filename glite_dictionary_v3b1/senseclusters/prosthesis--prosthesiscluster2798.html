prosthesis

noun
artificial body part replacement
Pronunciation: / prɑːsˈθiːsɪs / (AmE), / prɒsˈθiːsɪs / (BrE)
an artificial device that replaces a missing part of the body, such as a limb, tooth, or eye

Examples

A prosthesis limb
After losing his leg in the accident, he was fitted with a prosthesis.
Modern technology allows for highly functional prostheses that closely mimic natural movement.
The dentist recommended a dental prosthesis to replace her missing tooth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implant - an object inserted into the body, often medical
  • synonym: orthosis - a device used to support or correct musculoskeletal issues
