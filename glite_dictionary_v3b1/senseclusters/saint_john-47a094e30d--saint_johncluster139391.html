Saint John

proper noun
river in Maine and New Brunswick
Pronunciation: / seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
a river that rises in northern Maine, USA, and flows northeast through New Brunswick, Canada, emptying into the Bay of Fundy

Examples

The Saint John River
Salmon fishing is popular along the Saint John during the summer months.
The Saint John River is vital to the communities of New Brunswick.
The waters of the Saint John eventually flow into the Bay of Fundy.
