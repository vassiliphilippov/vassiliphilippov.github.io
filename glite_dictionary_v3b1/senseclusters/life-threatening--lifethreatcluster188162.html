life-threatening

adjective
potentially fatal; posing risk of death
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪf ˌθrɛtənɪŋ /; / ˈlaɪfˌθrɛtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ˌθrɛtənɪŋ /; / ˈlaɪfˌθrɛtənɪŋ / (BrE)
serious enough to potentially cause death

Examples

Life-threatening condition
Doctors warned that the allergic reaction could become life-threatening without prompt medical care.
During the hurricane, many families faced life-threatening conditions in their homes.
The patient was admitted with a life-threatening infection that required immediate treatment.
A life-threatening allergic reaction requires immediate medical attention.
Carbon monoxide poisoning can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
Doctors acted quickly because her condition was life-threatening.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: fatal - causing death
  • degree lower: serious - requiring careful consideration due to possible danger
