tediously

adverb
in a tiresome or boring manner
5.8 0.25/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtiÀêdi…ôsli / (AmE), / ÀàtiÀêdi…ôsli / (BrE)
In a way that is tiresome, repetitive, or boring, especially because of length or monotony.

Examples

Explained tediously long
He explained every step of the process tediously, taking hours to finish.
She tediously copied each number by hand, barely making any mistakes.
The lecture dragged on tediously, putting most of the students to sleep.

Synonyms

  • tiresomely
    5.5 0.06/m
    tiresomely causes boredom or small annoyance through long, repeating actions and slow pace, while tediously focuses on being long, repetitive, and dull.
  • monotonously
    6.2 0.16/m
    monotonously repeats the same sound or action in a dull, steady way, while tediously describes something boring because of long, unchanging sameness.
  • boringly
    2.7 0.11/m
    boringly makes people lose interest by being plainly dull, while tediously highlights length, repetition, or a slow, tiresome process.
  • uninterestingly
    5.3 0.01/m
    uninterestingly fails to attract attention or feeling, while tediously points to long, repetitive, or monotonous experience that bores people.
  • prosily
    9.9 0.00/m
    prosily sounds dull and unimaginative, often about writing, while tediously focuses on length or boring repetition in many contexts.
