Procne

proper noun
mythical Greek woman
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːkni / (AmE), / ˈprɒkni / (BrE)
a figure in Greek mythology who was transformed into a swallow after avenging her sister's suffering

Examples

Procne in mythology
The tragic story of Procne is told in Ovid's Metamorphoses.
In the myth, Procne is transformed into a swallow as a result of her actions.
Many ancient poets wrote about the sorrow of Procne and her sister Philomela.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Philomela - Procne's sister in Greek mythology, also transformed into a bird
  • synonym: swallow - a small migratory bird, into which Procne was transformed
