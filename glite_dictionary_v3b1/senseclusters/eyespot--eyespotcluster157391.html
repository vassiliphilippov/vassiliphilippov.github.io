eyespot

noun
eyelike marking on animals
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌspɒt / (BrE)
a round marking resembling an eye, especially one consisting of a colored spot surrounded by a ring of another color, as found on the wings of some butterflies or the feathers of certain birds

Examples

Butterfly eyespots
Scientists observed that the butterfly's eyespots help to confuse birds and avoid attacks.
The fish uses a bright eyespot near its tail to trick potential predators.
The peacock's feathers are decorated with beautiful eyespots that deter predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ocellus - a small simple eye or eyespot, especially in invertebrates and in reference to a marking
