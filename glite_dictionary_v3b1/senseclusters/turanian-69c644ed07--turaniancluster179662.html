Turanian

noun
person from Turan
9.9
Pronunciation: / tuˈreɪniən / (AmE), / tjʊˈreɪniən /; / tjʊəˈreɪniən / (BrE)
a person belonging to the Turanian peoples or an outdated term for a supposed racial group from Central Asia (historical/obsolete)

Examples

Ancient Turanian tribes
The historian described the migration patterns of ancient Turanian tribes across the steppes.
He identified himself as a Turanian due to his family's Central Asian heritage.
Legends about the Turanian people are common in the folklore of several countries.
The term Turanian is no longer used in modern anthropology.
Old textbooks sometimes classified Central Asian peoples as Turanian based on outdated theories.
Scholars now recognize that the concept of Turanian lacks scientific basis.
