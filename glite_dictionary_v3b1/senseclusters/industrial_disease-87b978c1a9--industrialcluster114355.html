industrial disease

noun
work-related illness caused by industrial conditions
3.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌstriəl dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəl dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease or disability caused by conditions of employment, especially resulting from prolonged exposure to harmful substances or repetitive actions at work.

Examples

Industrial disease from work
Many miners suffered from industrial disease due to years of exposure to coal dust.
Occupational health regulations aim to reduce the risk of industrial disease in the workplace.
The company was sued after several workers developed an industrial disease linked to chemical fumes.

  • synonym: occupational disease - A disease caused by work or working conditions
