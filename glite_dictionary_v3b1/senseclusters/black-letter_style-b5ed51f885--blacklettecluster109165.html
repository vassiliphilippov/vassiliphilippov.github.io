black-letter style

noun
formal, precise legal writing style
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk ˌlɛtɚ staɪl / (AmE), / ˈblæk ˌlɛtə staɪl / (BrE)
a formal style of legal writing, marked by strict adherence to established language and precise word usage

Examples

Black-letter style writing
Academic papers often require a black-letter style to clearly outline legal principles.
Some students find it challenging to write in black-letter style due to its demanding precision.
The lawyer's black-letter style impressed the senior partners with its clarity and rigor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legalese - language that is characteristic of legal documents, often hard to understand
