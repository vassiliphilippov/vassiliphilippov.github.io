browse

verb
eat small amounts; sample dishes
Pronunciation: / braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
to eat small amounts of food, especially by sampling a variety of dishes; to graze lightly rather than have a full meal

Examples

To browse on snacks
At food markets, he enjoys browsing on local snacks rather than eating a single dish.
She prefers to browse when there's a big spread, rather than piling her plate high.
We decided to browse at the buffet, sampling a little of everything instead of sitting down for a full meal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: feast - to eat a lot at a large meal
  • synonym: graze - to eat small amounts of food throughout the day
