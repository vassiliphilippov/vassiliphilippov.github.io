clonus

noun
rhythmic muscle spasm (medical)
6.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkloʊ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈkləʊ.nəs/ /; / /ˈklɒ.nəs/ (BrE)
A rapid, rhythmic series of involuntary muscle contractions and relaxations, typically caused by a neurological disorder.

Examples

Muscle clonus reflex
After the stroke, she exhibited clonus when her foot was rapidly dorsiflexed.
Persistent ankle clonus can be a sign of upper motor neuron lesions.
The neurologist noted clonus in the patient's right ankle during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convulsion - a sudden, violent, involuntary contraction of muscles
