subalternity

noun
state of subordination
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˌɔlˈtɜrnɪti / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːlˈtɜːnɪti / (BrE)
the condition of being subordinate, marginalized, or excluded, especially in social or political contexts

Examples

Subalternity of minorities
The documentary highlights the subalternity of indigenous peoples in modern society.
Her research focuses on the subalternity experienced by women in rural areas.
Literature often gives voice to those living in subalternity under oppressive regimes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dominance - the state of having power or control over others
  • synonym: marginalization - the process of being pushed to the edge of society or a group
