Cabbala

noun
Jewish mystical tradition
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈbɑːl.ə / (AmE), / kəˈbælə / (BrE)
A system of esoteric Jewish mysticism and theosophy, originating from rabbinical teachings and Hebrew scriptures, developed between the 7th and 18th centuries.

Examples

The Cabbala teachings
He became fascinated by the symbolism and philosophy of the Cabbala during his religious studies.
Many scholars study Cabbala to better understand Jewish mysticism and its influence on Western spirituality.
The Cabbala teaches that hidden meanings are encoded in the Hebrew scriptures.
