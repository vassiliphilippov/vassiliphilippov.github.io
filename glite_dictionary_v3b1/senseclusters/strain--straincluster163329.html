strain

verb
remove solids from liquid by filtering
5.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /streɪn/ (AmE), /streɪn/ (BrE)
to remove solid matter from a liquid by passing it through a filter, sieve, or similar device

Examples

To strain pasta
After boiling the vegetables, she strained them to remove excess water.
Carefully strain the mixture to separate the pulp from the juice.
He used a fine mesh to strain the sauce before serving.
After boiling the vegetables, she carefully strained the broth to remove any solids.
He strained the pasta in a colander to separate it from the water.
Remember to strain the sauce before serving to ensure it is smooth.
After boiling the carrots, she strained them to make baby food.
The recipe instructs you to strain the cooked tomatoes before adding them to the sauce.
You can strain the soup using a fine mesh sieve for a smoother texture.
