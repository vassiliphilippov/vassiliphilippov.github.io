crown

noun
center high point of a cambered road
5.3 4.1
Pronunciation: / kraʊn / (AmE), / kraʊn / (BrE)
the highest or top part of something, such as a head, mountain, tree, hat, or road

Examples

Crown of a hill
Potholes often form near the edge, not the crown, of a well-designed road.
The engineer explained that the crown needs to be higher for proper drainage.
Water easily drains off the crown of the road after heavy rain.
He adjusted his hat so the crown sat securely on his head.
Rainwater had soaked through the crown of his old felt hat.
The crown of her hat was decorated with a bright red ribbon.
A gentle breeze ruffled the crown of her hair.
He massaged the crown of his head to relieve the tension.
The toddler bumped the crown of his head on the table edge.
