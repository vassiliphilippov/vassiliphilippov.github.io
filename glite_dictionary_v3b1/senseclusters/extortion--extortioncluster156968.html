extortion

noun
the act of obtaining something by force or misuse of authority
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɔɹʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
the act or crime of getting something, especially money, from someone by using threats, force, or misuse of authority

Examples

Criminal extortion case
The company's investigation revealed years of extortion by corrupt employees.
The mayor was accused of extortion for demanding payments from local businesses.
Through extortion, the official collected illegal fees from citizens seeking permits.
Police arrested the gang members for their involvement in extortion schemes targeting shop owners.
The businessman was found guilty of extortion after threatening his rival with violence.
Victims of extortion often feel trapped and afraid to report the crime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blackmail - the act of demanding money from someone by threatening to reveal compromising information
  • synonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats
