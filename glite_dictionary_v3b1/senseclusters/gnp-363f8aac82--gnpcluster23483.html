GNP

noun
Gross National Product; an economic measure
4.2 3/m
Pronunciation: / d íi …õn pi / (AmE), / d íiÀê …õn piÀê / (BrE)
Gross National Product; a former measure of a country's economy, representing the total market value of all goods and services produced by a nation's citizens and capital in a given period, typically one year.

Examples

The GNP measure
In historical data, GNP figures are often compared with GDP to analyze trends.
Many economists now prefer GDP over GNP as a measurement tool.
The country's GNP grew rapidly during the 1980s.
