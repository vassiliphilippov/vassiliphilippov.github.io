chemotactic

adjective
cell movement by chemicals
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˈtæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˈtæktɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing movement in response to a chemical stimulus

Examples

The chemotactic response
The chemotactic properties of the substance help guide immune cells to sites of infection.
Scientists studied how chemotactic signals influence the movement of bacteria in different environments.
A chemotactic response allows white blood cells to locate and attack invading pathogens.
Certain bacteria exhibit chemotactic behavior by moving toward nutrients in their environment.
The chemotactic response of immune cells helps them locate sites of infection quickly.
Researchers studied the chemotactic movement of cells under different chemical gradients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemotaxis - the movement of cells or organisms in response to chemical stimuli
  • antonym: phototactic - relating to movement in response to light
