mirage

noun
something illusory or unattainable
8.4 6.1
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈrɑːʒ/ /; / /mɪ.ˈrɑːʒ/ (AmE), /mɪ.ˈrɑːʒ/ /; / /mɪ.ˈrɑːdʒ/ (BrE)
an idea, hope, or wish that seems real or possible but is in fact illusory or impossible to achieve

Examples

A mirage of success
For many, the promise of easy wealth is just a mirage that always remains out of reach.
Freedom seemed like a distant mirage as they struggled under the oppressive regime.
His hopes for a perfect life turned out to be nothing more than a mirage.

  • synonym: illusion - a deceptive appearance or impression
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
