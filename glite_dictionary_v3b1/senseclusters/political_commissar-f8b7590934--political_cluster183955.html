political commissar

noun
ideological overseer in civilian organisations

A non-military official in a civilian organisation who enforces loyalty to a particular political ideology or party line; often used metaphorically and pejoratively.

Examples

Company political commissar
The company promoted a political commissar to review editorial decisions and enforce the new ideological rules.
She joked that human resources had become a political commissar policing employees' social media posts.
Critics accused the university's dean of acting like a political commissar censoring dissenting speakers.
