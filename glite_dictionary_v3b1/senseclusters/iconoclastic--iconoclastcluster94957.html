iconoclastic

adjective
opposing religious images
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: /aɪ.kə.nə.ˈklæs.tɪk/ (AmE), /aɪ.kɒ.nə.ˈklæs.tɪk/ /; / /aɪ.kə.nə.ˈklæs.tɪk/ (BrE)
characterized by opposition to the use of religious images; rejecting or destroying icons, especially in a religious context

Examples

Iconoclastic movement
Some Protestant reformers took an iconoclastic approach, destroying religious statues and images.
The iconoclastic movement in Byzantium opposed the veneration of icons in churches.
The early Islamic tradition was distinctly iconoclastic, forbidding the depiction of living beings in religious art.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: iconoclastic - attacking or ignoring cherished beliefs and traditions
  • antonym: idolatrous - characterized by worship or excessive devotion to idols or images
