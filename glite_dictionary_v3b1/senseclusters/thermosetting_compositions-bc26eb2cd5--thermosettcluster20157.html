thermosetting compositions

noun
materials that harden irreversibly when heated
3.3 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈθɜːr.moʊ.set.ɪŋ kɑːm.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ənz/ /; / /ˈθɜːr.moʊ.set.ɪŋ kəm.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ənz/ (AmE), /ˈθɜː.məʊ.set.ɪŋ kɒm.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ənz/ (BrE)
materials that become hard and rigid when heated and cannot be reshaped or remolded after curing

Examples

Thermosetting compositions harden quickly
Bakelite is one of the earliest thermosetting compositions developed for industrial use.
Epoxy resins are common thermosetting compositions used in adhesives and coatings.
Unlike thermoplastics, thermosetting compositions cannot be melted and reshaped once they have set.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thermoplastic - a material that becomes soft when heated and can be reshaped
  • synonym: thermosetting - a material that hardens permanently when heated
