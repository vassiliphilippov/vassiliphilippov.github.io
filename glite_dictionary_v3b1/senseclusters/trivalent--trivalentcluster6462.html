trivalent

adjective
having a valence of three
7.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtraɪ.və.lənt/ /; / /ˈtraɪ.veɪ.lənt/ (AmE), /ˈtraɪ.və.lənt/ /; / /ˈtraɪ.veɪ.lənt/ (BrE)
(of a chemical element or compound) having a valence of three; able to form three bonds or unite with three atoms.

Examples

Trivalent element
Aluminum is a trivalent element and often forms compounds by sharing three electrons.
The ion in question is trivalent, carrying a charge of plus three.
Vaccines can be trivalent, offering protection against three different strains of a virus.
