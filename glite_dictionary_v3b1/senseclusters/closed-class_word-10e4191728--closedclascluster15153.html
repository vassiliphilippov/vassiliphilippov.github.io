closed-class word

noun
a grammatical word with limited membership
9.0 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkloʊzd.klæs wɜːrd/ (AmE), /ˈkləʊzd.klɑːs wɜːd/ (BrE)
A word that belongs to a category with a fixed set of members, such as prepositions, conjunctions, or pronouns, and serves a grammatical function rather than conveying concrete meaning.

Examples

A closed-class word category
Personal pronouns represent a typical type of closed-class word used for grammatical structure.
Prepositions like 'in' and conjunctions like 'and' are examples of closed-class words in English.
Unlike nouns and verbs, a closed-class word rarely accepts new additions to its category.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open-class word - a word belonging to a category with potentially unlimited membership, such as nouns or verbs
