grade point average

noun
a student's academic performance score
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪd pɔɪnt ˈævərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪd pɔɪnt ˈævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a numerical measure of a student's academic performance, typically calculated by dividing the total number of grade points earned by the number of credits attempted

Examples

Minimum grade point average
Most graduate schools require a minimum grade point average for admission.
My grade point average improved significantly after I retook the math course.
She maintained a high grade point average throughout her four years at university.
