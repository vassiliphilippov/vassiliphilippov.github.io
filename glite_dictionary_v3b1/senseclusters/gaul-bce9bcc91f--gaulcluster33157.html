Gaul

proper noun
ancient Celtic people
Pronunciation: / ɡɔl / (AmE), / ɡɔːl / (BrE)
a member of the ancient Celtic people who lived in the region now known as France and nearby areas

Examples

Gaul tribes
The Gaul tribes were known for their fierce resistance against Roman armies.
Julius Caesar wrote about his campaigns in Gaul in his famous commentaries.
Artifacts from ancient Gaul have been discovered throughout modern-day France.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Celt - a member of the ancient Indo-European people of western Europe
  • antonym: Roman - a citizen of ancient Rome
