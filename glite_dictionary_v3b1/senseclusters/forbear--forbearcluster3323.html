forbear

verb
to refrain from doing or saying something
8.1 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrÀàb…õr / (AmE), / f…îÀêÀàbe…ô /; / f…îÀêÀàb…õ…ô / (BrE)
to refrain from doing or saying something, especially through self-control or patience

Examples

To forbear making a comment
Although tempted by the cake, he managed to forbear eating a second slice.
He tried to forbear making any comment during the heated discussion.
She could not forbear laughing at the unexpected joke.
He managed to forbear from commenting on the mistake.
She could hardly forbear a smile at his enthusiasm.
We should forbear making judgments until we know the whole story.

Synonyms

  • refrain
    4.2 8/m
    refrain means deliberately avoid doing something, while forbear shows patient self control in stopping words or actions.
  • hold back
    4.2 4/m
    hold back often means restraining emotions or information in the moment, while forbear implies patient, deliberate self control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indulge - to allow oneself to enjoy the pleasure of something
  • synonym: refrain - to stop oneself from doing something
  • antonym: yield - to give way to something; opposite of resist
