pietist

noun
devout Christian movement
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪətɪst / (AmE), / ˈpaɪətɪst / (BrE)
a member of a religious movement that emphasizes personal faith, devotion, and heartfelt religious experience over formal rituals or doctrine

Examples

A devoted pietist
The pietist believed that true faith came from a personal relationship with God.
Many pietists gathered for prayer meetings focused on spiritual renewal.
As a pietist, she valued inner devotion more than outward ceremonies.
The pietist spent hours each day in prayer and reflection.
As a pietist, she believed that personal devotion was more important than rituals.
The community was founded by a group of pietists seeking a simpler spiritual life.
