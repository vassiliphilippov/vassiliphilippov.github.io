distribution law

noun
statistical distribution of energy among molecules
8.7 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪstrəˈbjuːʃən lɔ / (AmE), / ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən lɔː / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A principle stating that the total energy in a group of molecules is not distributed equally but follows a statistical distribution around an average value.

Examples

The distribution law in chemistry
According to the distribution law, most molecules have energies close to the average value.
The distribution law explains how energy is shared among molecules in a closed system.
Understanding the distribution law is essential for studying chemical kinetics and thermodynamics.
