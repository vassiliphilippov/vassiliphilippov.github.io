shoe-leather tactic

noun
method involving physical, in-person effort
8.2 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈʃuː.leð.ər ˈtæk.tɪk/ (AmE), /ˈʃuː.leð.ə ˈtæk.tɪk/ (BrE)
A method of investigation, campaigning, or work that requires physical effort, such as travelling or visiting people in person, rather than relying on remote or desk-based activities.

Examples

Shoe-leather tactics in campaigning
Building trust with voters often requires shoe-leather tactics like meeting them face to face.
Her campaign relied heavily on shoe-leather tactics to reach every neighborhood in the district.
Journalists frequently use shoe-leather tactics to uncover stories that aren't available online.
