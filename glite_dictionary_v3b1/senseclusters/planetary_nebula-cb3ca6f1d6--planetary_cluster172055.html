planetary nebula

noun
bright shell of gas around dying star
5.9 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈplænəˌtɛri nəˈbjulə / (AmE), / ˈplænətəri nɪˈbjuːlə / (BrE)
An expanding, glowing shell of ionized gas ejected from certain types of stars late in their life, illuminated by intense radiation from the central star.

Examples

A planetary nebula shell
Astronomers observed a planetary nebula in a distant part of the galaxy.
Each planetary nebula marks the final stages of a medium-sized star’s evolution.
The colorful appearance of a planetary nebula is due to gases energized by ultraviolet radiation.
