sisham

noun
East Indian timber tree (Dalbergia sissoo)
9.6 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪ.ʃəm/ (AmE), /ˈsɪ.ʃəm/ (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Dalbergia sissoo) whose leaves are used for fodder and which produces a hard, dark brown timber used in shipbuilding and for making railroad ties.

Examples

Sisham wood
Many pieces of furniture in the region are crafted from sisham wood due to its durability.
Railroad ties made from sisham timber can withstand harsh conditions for many years.
The leaves of the sisham tree are often gathered to feed livestock.
