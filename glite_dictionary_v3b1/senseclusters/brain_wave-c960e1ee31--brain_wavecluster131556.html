brain wave

noun
pattern of electrical activity in the brain
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪn ˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn weɪv / (BrE)
A pattern of electrical activity in the brain, detectable with an electroencephalograph (EEG).

Examples

Brain waves in sleep
During deep sleep, different brain waves are observed in the EEG results.
Meditation has been shown to alter certain brain wave patterns in participants.
The doctor analyzed the patient's brain waves to diagnose the condition.
