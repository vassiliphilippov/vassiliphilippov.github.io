(phonetics) A consonant sound produced with the tongue near or against the back of the alveolar ridge.

Examples

The post-alveolar consonant sound
In English, the 'sh' sound in 'shoe' is a classic example of a post-alveolar consonant.
Language students often practice distinguishing post-alveolar consonants from other types during pronunciation exercises.
