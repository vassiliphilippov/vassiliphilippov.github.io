post-alveolar consonant

noun
consonant pronounced just behind the alveolar ridge
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊst ælˈviːələr ˈkɑnsəˌnənt / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst ælˈviːələ ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
(phonetics) A consonant sound produced with the tongue near or against the back of the alveolar ridge.

Examples

The post-alveolar consonant sound
In English, the 'sh' sound in 'shoe' is a classic example of a post-alveolar consonant.
Language students often practice distinguishing post-alveolar consonants from other types during pronunciation exercises.
The teacher explained that /ʃ/ and /ʒ/ are both post-alveolar consonants.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: alveolar consonant - a consonant articulated at the alveolar ridge
  • degree higher: palatal consonant - a consonant articulated with the body of the tongue against the hard palate
