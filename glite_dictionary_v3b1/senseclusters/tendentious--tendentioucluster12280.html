tendentious

adjective
showing a strong or controversial bias
Pronunciation: / tɛnˈdɛnʃəs / (AmE), / tɛnˈdɛnʃəs / (BrE)
showing or expressing a strong, often controversial, bias or point of view

Examples

A tendentious article
Critics argued that her review was both unfair and tendentious.
It is important to distinguish objective facts from tendentious accounts when analyzing history.
The article offered a tendentious interpretation of the events, favoring one side of the debate.
Her tendentious remarks made it clear which side of the issue she supported.
Many historians criticized the book for being too tendentious and lacking objectivity.
The article offered a tendentious view of the political situation, sparking immediate debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - showing unfair preference or prejudice
  • antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • antonym: unbiased - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions; impartial
