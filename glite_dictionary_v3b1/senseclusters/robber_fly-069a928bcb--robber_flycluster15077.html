robber fly

noun
predatory fly with strong, bee-like body
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑbər flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɒbə flaɪ / (BrE)
A large, fast-flying predatory fly with a robust, bee-like body and a proboscis adapted for sucking the juices of other insects caught in flight.

Examples

Large robber flies
Farmers appreciate the presence of robber flies because they help control pest populations.
The robber fly snatched a smaller insect out of the air before I could blink.
With its strong, hairy body, the robber fly is often mistaken for a bumblebee.
