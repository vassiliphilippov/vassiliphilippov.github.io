Examples

To put aside worries

Synonyms

  • put away
    3.4 0.15/m
    put away means stopping thoughts or worries for a time, while put aside refers to dismissing them from your attention.
  • brush aside
    4.5 1.07/m
    brush aside treats ideas or worries as unimportant and ignores them, while put aside often means pausing attention temporarily.
  • push aside
    4.1 0.42/m
    push aside refuses to consider someone or an idea and feels forceful, while put aside often means setting something away for now.
  • shrug off
    3.9 2.07/m
    shrug off treats criticism or worry as unimportant and shows no reaction, while put aside focuses on stopping attention briefly.
  • sweep aside
    4.7 0.23/m
    sweep aside rejects ideas or objections quickly and often with force, while put aside usually means suspending thoughts without strong rejection.
  • dismiss
    3.0 25/m
    dismiss decides something is not worth considering, often in formal speech, while put aside is more like pausing or ignoring it.
  • cast aside
    3.7 0.97/m
    cast aside rejects or gets rid of someone or something as unwanted and likely permanent, while put aside can be temporary and less final.
  • let go
    3.7 16/m
    let go means stop worrying and release emotional ties, while put aside means stop thinking about something, often temporarily.
