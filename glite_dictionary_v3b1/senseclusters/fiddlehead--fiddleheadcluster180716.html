fiddlehead

noun
young, coiled fern frond (often edible)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪd.əlˌhɛd /; / ˈfɪdəlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.əlˌhɛd /; / ˈfɪd(ə)lhɛd / (BrE)
the young, coiled frond of a fern, especially one that is eaten as a vegetable

Examples

Fresh fiddleheads vegetable

The chef sautéed fiddlehead ferns with garlic and olive oil for a seasonal side dish.
The curled fiddlehead is prized both for its flavor and its unique appearance.
