synthetic substance

noun
artificially made chemical compound
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
A substance produced artificially by chemical reactions rather than occurring naturally.

Examples

A synthetic substance
Many plastics are classified as synthetic substances because they do not occur in nature.
The laboratory successfully created a new synthetic substance with unique properties.
Unlike natural materials, a synthetic substance may be engineered for specific industrial uses.
