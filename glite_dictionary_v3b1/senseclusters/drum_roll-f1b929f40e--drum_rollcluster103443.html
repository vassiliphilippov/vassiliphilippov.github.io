drum roll

noun
rapid, continuous drum beating for emphasis
Pronunciation: / drʌm roʊl / (AmE), / drʌm rəʊl / (BrE)
a rapid, continuous beating on a drum, especially a snare drum, often used for emphasis or to build suspense

Examples

A drum roll starts
As the curtain rose, a drum roll heightened the audience's anticipation.
The host asked for a drum roll before announcing the winner.
With a dramatic drum roll, the magician revealed his final trick.
