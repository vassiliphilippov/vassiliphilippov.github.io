CIO

noun
Congress of Industrial Organizations (labor federation)
Pronunciation: / si aɪ oʊ / (AmE), / siː aɪ əʊ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for Congress of Industrial Organizations, a federation of North American industrial unions that merged with the American Federation of Labor in 1955.

Examples

CIO labor union
Many major strikes were led by the CIO in the mid-twentieth century.
The CIO played a key role in organizing industrial workers during the 1930s and 1940s.
When the CIO merged with the AFL, it created the AFL-CIO, the largest federation of unions in the U.S.
