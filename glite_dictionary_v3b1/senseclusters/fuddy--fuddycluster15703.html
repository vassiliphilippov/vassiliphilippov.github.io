fuddy

noun
old-fashioned person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌdi / (AmE), / ˈfʌdi / (BrE)
a person who is old-fashioned or overly conservative in habits, tastes, or attitudes

Examples

A fuddy at work
Everyone teased him for being a fuddy who refused to use a smartphone.
She was called a fuddy because she preferred handwritten letters over emails.
Despite being young, he acted like a fuddy and disliked new trends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fuddy-duddy - a person with old-fashioned ideas and habits
  • antonym: modernist - a person who favors new ideas and modern ways
