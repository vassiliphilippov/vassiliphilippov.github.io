grabby

adjective
excessively greedy or acquisitive
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræbi / (AmE), / ˈɡræbi / (BrE)
immoderately eager to acquire things, especially money or possessions; greedy.

Examples

A grabby person
His grabby attitude made it difficult for him to share anything with others.
She warned her children not to be grabby when the birthday presents were being handed out.
The company's grabby executives were always looking for new ways to maximize profits, regardless of the impact on employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avaricious - having or showing an extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something than necessary or expected
  • synonym: greedy - having an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
