bristle grass

noun
grass with arching bristly spikes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪsəl ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsl ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
Any of several grasses found in grasslands or woodlands, characterized by arching spikes with long, stiff bristles beneath each spikelet.

Examples

Bristle grass spikes
Researchers studied the distribution of bristle grass in temperate grasslands.
Some species of bristle grass are commonly used as forage for livestock.
The field was dotted with bristle grass swaying gently in the wind.
