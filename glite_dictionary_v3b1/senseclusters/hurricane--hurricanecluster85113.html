hurricane

noun
violent tropical storm
2.2 29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…úÀêr.…™.ke…™n / (AmE), / Ààh år.…™.k…ôn / (BrE)
A violent tropical storm with very strong winds and heavy rain, typically occurring in the Atlantic Ocean and northeastern Pacific.

Examples

A hurricane storm
Many residents evacuated as the hurricane approached the shoreline.
The hurricane caused widespread flooding and power outages across the region.
The coastal city was devastated by a hurricane last year.

Synonyms

    3.8 1.37/m
    a typhoon forms in the western Pacific or Indian Ocean, while a hurricane forms in the Atlantic and northeastern Pacific.
  • cyclone
    3.7 2.18/m
    a cyclone is a rotating tropical storm term used broadly, while a hurricane specifically names that storm in the Atlantic region.

Related Terms
