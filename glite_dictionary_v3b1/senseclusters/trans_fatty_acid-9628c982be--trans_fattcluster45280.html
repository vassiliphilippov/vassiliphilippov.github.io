trans fatty acid

noun
hydrogenated unsaturated fatty acid
2.6 3.3
Pronunciation: /trænz ˈfæ.tɪ ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /træns ˈfæ.tɪ ˈæs.ɪd/ (AmE), /trænz ˈfæ.tɪ ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /træns ˈfæ.tɪ ˈæs.ɪd/ (BrE)
A type of unsaturated fatty acid that has been hydrogenated, altering its structure; commonly found in processed foods and associated with health risks.

Examples

A trans fatty acid type
Most commercially baked goods contain some level of trans fatty acid as a result of partial hydrogenation.
Nutritionists recommend avoiding foods high in trans fatty acids for better heart health.
The label warned that the product was free from trans fatty acids.
