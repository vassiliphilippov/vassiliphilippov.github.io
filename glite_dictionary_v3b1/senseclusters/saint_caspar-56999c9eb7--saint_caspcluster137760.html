(in Christian tradition) one of the three Magi or wise men who, according to the Gospel of Matthew, visited the infant Jesus bearing gifts, traditionally associated as the scholar from India who brought gold.

Examples

Saint Caspar the Magus
In traditional nativity scenes, Saint Caspar is often portrayed as the Magus offering gold to the infant Jesus.
Many legends describe Saint Caspar as a scholar from India who traveled to Bethlehem.
The gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh were brought by Saint Caspar, Saint Melchior, and Saint Balthazar respectively.
