bountiful

adjective
abundant; producing in large amounts
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊn.tɪ.fəl /; / ˈbaʊntɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈbaʊn.tɪ.fəl /; / ˈbaʊntɪfəl / (BrE)
producing or giving in large amounts; abundant or generous

Examples

A bountiful harvest
Her garden is always bountiful, filled with vegetables and flowers throughout the summer.
The farmers celebrated a bountiful harvest after a season of heavy rains.
We were grateful for the bountiful meal that was prepared for the holiday.
He is known for his bountiful donations to local charities every year.
Her bountiful compliments boosted the team's morale.
The bountiful host made sure everyone left with plenty of food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • synonym: plentiful - existing or produced in large quantities
  • antonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand; in short supply
  • antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous
