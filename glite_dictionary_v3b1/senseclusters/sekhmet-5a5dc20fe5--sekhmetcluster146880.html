Sekhmet

proper noun
lion-headed Egyptian goddess
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkˌmɛt / (AmE), / ˈseɪk.mɛt / (BrE)
An Egyptian goddess with the head of a lion, representing the life-destroying power of the sun.

Examples

Sekhmet lioness goddess
In ancient temples, Sekhmet was often depicted as a fierce lioness goddess.
Legends say that Sekhmet's wrath was both feared and respected in ancient Egypt.
Priests prayed to Sekhmet for protection from diseases and disaster.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bastet - Egyptian cat goddess associated with protection and fertility
  • synonym: Ra - the chief sun god in Egyptian mythology
