concatenation

noun
series of connected things or events
4.4
Pronunciation: / kɑnˌkætəˈneɪʃən /; / kənˌkætəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌkætɪˈneɪʃən /; / kɒnˌkætɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a series or process of things (such as events, ideas, or objects) being linked together in a chain or sequence

Examples

In computer science, concatenation is used to combine two strings into one.
The concatenation of several errors led to the system's failure.
