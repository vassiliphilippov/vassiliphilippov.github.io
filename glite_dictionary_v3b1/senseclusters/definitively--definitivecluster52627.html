definitively

adverb
clearly and finally
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɪnətɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪnɪtɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is clear, final, and leaves no doubt

Examples

To definitively prove
The scientist definitively proved the theory with new evidence.
After months of debate, the committee definitively decided on the new policy.
He definitively rejected all accusations during the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conclusively - in a way that proves something and ends doubt
  • antonym: uncertainly - in a way that is not clear or definite
