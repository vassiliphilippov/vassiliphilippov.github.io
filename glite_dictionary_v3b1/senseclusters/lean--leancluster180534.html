lean

adjective
containing little or no excess
9.9 2.1
Pronunciation: /liːn/ (AmE), /liːn/ (BrE)
thin or lacking excess; having little extra fat, resources, or material

Examples

A lean body
After years of plenty, the company had to adjust to a lean budget with very few luxuries.
The organization operated efficiently even during lean times when resources were scarce.
Their lean spending habits helped them save for emergencies.
After a year of intense training, he became noticeably lean and fit.
She admired his lean physique, a result of years of swimming.
The cheetah is a lean animal designed for speed and agility.
After a lean year, many companies were forced to cut back on spending.
During the 1930s, families endured several lean years due to the Great Depression.
Farmers hope next season will be better after this lean harvest.
