myelinated

adjective
having a myelin sheath (of nerves)
9.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.ə.lɪ.neɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ˈmaɪ.ə.lə.neɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.ə.lɪ.neɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ˈmaɪ.ə.lə.neɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
(of nerve fibers or neurons) covered with a layer of myelin, which increases the speed of nerve impulse transmission.

Examples

Myelinated nerve fibers
Damage to myelinated fibers can lead to neurological disorders.
Some peripheral nerves are myelinated while others are not.
The rapid conduction of electrical signals in myelinated neurons allows for quick reflexes.
