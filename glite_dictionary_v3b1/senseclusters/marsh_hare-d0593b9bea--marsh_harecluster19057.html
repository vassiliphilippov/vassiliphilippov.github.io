marsh hare

noun
rabbit of southeastern US marshes
Pronunciation: / mɑrʃ hɛr / (AmE), / mɑːʃ heə / (BrE)
a rabbit species inhabiting marshy coastal regions of the southeastern United States, especially from North Carolina to Florida

Examples

The marsh hare species
Biologists studied the diet of the marsh hare to understand its adaptation to brackish environments.
The marsh hare is rarely seen outside the wetlands of the southeastern United States.
Unlike the eastern cottontail, the marsh hare has longer legs suited for swampy terrain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wood rabbit - another name for marsh hare, especially in the same region
