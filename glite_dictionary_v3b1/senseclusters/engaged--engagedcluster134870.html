engaged

adjective
built against a wall (architecture)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a column or similar architectural feature) built against or attached to a wall and partly projecting from it.

Examples

Engaged column
Many Roman temples featured engaged columns as part of their structural design.
The architect specified engaged pilasters to visually strengthen the building's exterior walls.
The classical facade is decorated with engaged columns between the windows.
