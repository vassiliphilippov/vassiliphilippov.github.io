get in the swim

verb
become involved or engaged
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɪn ðə swɪm / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ðə swɪm / (BrE)
to become involved or actively engaged in an activity or situation

Examples

To get in the swim of things
After moving to a new city, it took her a while to get in the swim of things.
He found it hard at first, but soon managed to get in the swim at his new job.
If you want to succeed here, you need to get in the swim and show some initiative.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stand aside - to not get involved in an activity or situation
