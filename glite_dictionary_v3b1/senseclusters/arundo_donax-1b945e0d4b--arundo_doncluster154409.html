Arundo donax

noun
large perennial grass, giant reed
Pronunciation: / əˈrʌndoʊ ˈdoʊnæks / (AmE), / əˈrʌndəʊ ˈdəʊnaks / (BrE)
A large perennial grass, often found by rivers or in ditches, with jointed stems and large grey-white feathery flower clusters; also known as giant reed.

Examples

The Arundo donax plant
Efforts are underway to control the rapid spread of Arundo donax in wetland areas.
Gardeners often plant Arundo donax as an ornamental grass for its striking feathery plumes.
The banks of the river were dominated by dense stands of Arundo donax.
