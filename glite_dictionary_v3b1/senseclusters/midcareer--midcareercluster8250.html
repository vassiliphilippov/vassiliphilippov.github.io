midcareer

noun
middle career stage
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪd.kəˈrɪr / (AmE), / ˌmɪd.kəˈrɪə / (BrE)
the middle stage of a person's professional working life, typically after initial experience but before late career or retirement.

Examples

A midcareer move
Many professionals seek new challenges during their midcareer to avoid stagnation.
She decided to pursue further education at midcareer to advance her prospects.
A midcareer change can be both exciting and daunting for many workers.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: early career - the initial stage of a person's professional working life
  • degree higher: late career - the final stage of a person's professional working life, often approaching retirement
  • synonym: midlife - the middle period of a person's life, often considered between young adulthood and old age
