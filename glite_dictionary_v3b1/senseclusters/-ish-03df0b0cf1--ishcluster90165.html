-ish

x
adjective-forming suffix
4.4
Pronunciation: / ɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪʃ / (BrE)
a suffix added to nouns or adjectives to mean 'having the quality of' or 'somewhat like'

Examples

-ish ending in 'foolish'
When you add -ish to 'fool', you get 'foolish', meaning 'like a fool'.
The color of the sky was blueish, not completely blue but close.
Calling someone 'childish' means they behave in a way similar to a child.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -like - a suffix meaning 'similar to' or 'characteristic of'
