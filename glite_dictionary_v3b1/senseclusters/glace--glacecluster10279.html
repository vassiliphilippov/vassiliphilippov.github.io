glace

noun
concentrated stock glaze
Pronunciation: / ɡlɑːˈseɪ / (AmE), / ɡlæˈseɪ / (BrE)
a highly concentrated, reduced stock or jelly used as a rich glaze or sauce in cooking

Examples

Meat glace
The chef brushed a spoonful of glossy glace over the roast to enrich its flavor.
A small amount of glace can add depth and richness to sauces.
After hours of simmering, the stock was reduced to a thick glace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demi-glace - a rich brown sauce made by reducing stock and sauce
  • degree lower: stock - a flavored liquid base made by simmering bones, meat, or vegetables
