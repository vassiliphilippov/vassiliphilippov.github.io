levitation

noun
rising and floating in the air
Pronunciation: / ˌlɛvəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɛvɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of rising or causing something to rise and float in the air, often without visible means of support

Examples

Magnetic levitation
Scientists have demonstrated levitation using powerful magnets to lift objects.
Stories of levitation often appear in historical accounts of mystics and spiritualists.
The magician amazed the audience with his levitation act.

Related Terms

  • synonym: floatation - the process of floating or making something float
