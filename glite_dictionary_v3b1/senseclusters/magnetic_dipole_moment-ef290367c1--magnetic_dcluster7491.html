magnetic dipole moment

noun
strength and orientation of a magnetic source
3.0
Pronunciation: / məgˈnɛtɪk ˈdaɪpoʊl ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / mægˈnɛtɪk ˈdʌɪpəʊl ˈməʊm(ə)nt / (BrE)
the measure of the strength and orientation of a magnetic source, especially as associated with a current loop or particle, determining the magnetic field it produces

Examples

The magnetic dipole moment strength
In physics, the magnetic dipole moment is an essential concept for understanding atomic structure.
The compass needle aligns itself according to the Earth's magnetic dipole moment.
The electron's magnetic dipole moment affects how it interacts with external magnetic fields.
