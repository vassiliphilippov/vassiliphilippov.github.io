interventionist

adjective
favoring active involvement
7.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈven.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈven.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
favoring or involving active interference or involvement, especially by a government or authority, in affairs or situations.

Examples

Interventionist policy
The new president adopted an interventionist approach to economic policy.
Critics argue that interventionist strategies can sometimes make problems worse.
Some countries prefer a interventionist role in international conflicts, while others do not.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hands-off - not involving oneself or interfering
  • synonym: interfering - involving oneself in a situation without invitation
  • antonym: noninterventionist - opposed to interfering or becoming involved in affairs
