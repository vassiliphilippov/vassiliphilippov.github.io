durum wheat

noun
hard wheat variety used in pasta and bread
Pronunciation: / ˈdʊrəm ˌwiːt / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəm ˌwiːt / (BrE)
a hard variety of wheat with dark kernels high in gluten, used especially for making pasta and some breads

Examples

Durum wheat pasta
Durum wheat is the main ingredient in authentic Italian pasta.
Farmers in North Dakota grow large quantities of durum wheat for export.
The chef recommends using durum wheat flour to achieve the perfect texture in homemade pasta.
