calendaring

noun
court case scheduling
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪndərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of scheduling a case or matter to be heard on an official court calendar or docket

Examples

Court calendaring
The judge ordered the calendaring of the case for a hearing in two weeks.
Proper calendaring ensures that all parties are notified of upcoming court dates.
After the motion was filed, the attorney handled the calendaring to secure a timely hearing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: docketing - the process of entering a case onto a court's official list
  • antonym: postponement - the act of delaying or deferring a scheduled event
