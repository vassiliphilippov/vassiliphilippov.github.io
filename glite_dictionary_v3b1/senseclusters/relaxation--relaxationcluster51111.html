relaxation

noun
return to equilibrium after disturbance (physics)
Pronunciation: / ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) The process by which a system returns to equilibrium, typically at an exponential rate, after being disturbed.

Examples

Relaxation time constant
Scientists measured the relaxation rate of the excited electrons as they returned to their ground state.
The relaxation of the magnetic field occurs shortly after the external force is removed.
Thermal relaxation times vary depending on the material properties and system size.
