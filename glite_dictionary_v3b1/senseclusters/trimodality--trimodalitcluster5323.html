trimodality

noun
use of three senses
9.9
Pronunciation: / traɪmoʊˈdæləti / (AmE), / traɪməʊˈdælɪti / (BrE)
the use or involvement of three different sensory modalities, such as sight, hearing, and touch, in perception or testing

Examples

Trimodality sensory test
The experiment tested memory recall using a trimodality approach involving sight, sound, and touch.
Researchers found that trimodality learning improved students' ability to retain new information.
The device was designed to support trimodality feedback for a more immersive user experience.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bimodality - the use of two sensory modalities
  • synonym: multimodality - the use of multiple sensory modalities in communication or testing
  • antonym: unimodality - the use of a single sensory modality
