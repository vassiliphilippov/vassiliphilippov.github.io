cardiac arrest

noun
sudden stopping of the heart
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑr.di.æk əˈrɛst / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.di.æk əˈrɛst / (BrE)
A sudden stop in effective heart function, causing loss of blood circulation and, if untreated, death.

Examples

Sudden cardiac arrest
Immediate CPR is crucial when someone goes into cardiac arrest.
Paramedics responded quickly to the report of cardiac arrest at the local gym.
The patient suffered a cardiac arrest during surgery, but was successfully resuscitated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heart attack - sudden interruption of blood supply to the heart
  • antonym: resuscitation - the act of reviving someone from unconsciousness or apparent death
