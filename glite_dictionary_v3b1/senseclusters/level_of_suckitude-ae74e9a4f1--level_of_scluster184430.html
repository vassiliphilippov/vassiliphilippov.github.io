level of suckitude

noun
degree of poor quality or performance (informal)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvəl əv ˈsʌkɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl əv ˈsʌkɪtjuːd / (BrE)
A humorous or informal measure of how poor or bad the quality or performance of something is.

Examples

The level of suckitude
His attempt at cooking last night matched the highest level of suckitude I've ever witnessed.
I didn't think the software could get worse, but its latest update proves a new level of suckitude.
The team's level of suckitude reached new heights after their third defeat in a row.
