foveate

verb
to focus precisely
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊviˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊviˌeɪt / (BrE)
to focus the eyes or a camera on a specific point, especially using the fovea for sharp vision

Examples

To foveate a target
The new camera can foveate on objects to capture them in sharp detail.
Virtual reality headsets use technology to foveate images where the user is looking.
Birds often foveate on prey before swooping down to catch it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blur - to make unclear or less focused
  • synonym: focus - to direct attention or vision to a particular point
