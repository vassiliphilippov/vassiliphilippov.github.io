clap

verb
strike wings together in flight
2.1
Pronunciation: / klæp / (AmE), / klæp / (BrE)
to strike wings together audibly, especially in flight (of birds)

Examples

Birds clap wings
As the falcon descended, it clapped its wings with impressive force.
Geese sometimes clap their wings loudly as they take off from the ground.
The startled heron clapped its wings and soared away from the pond.
A great heron flew overhead, its broad wings clapping in steady rhythm.
As the pigeons took off, their wings clapped sharply against the morning air.
The sudden flight made the birds' wings clap loudly, startling everyone nearby.
