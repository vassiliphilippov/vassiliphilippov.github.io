irreproducible

adjective
impossible to reproduce or duplicate
9.6 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™riÀåpro äÀàduÀês…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / Àå…™riÀêpr…ôÀàdjuÀês…ôbl / (BrE)
(of a result, action, or object) impossible to reproduce or duplicate; unable to be repeated exactly.

Examples

Irreproducible scientific results
Scientific claims must be verifiable and not based on irreproducible results.
The initial experiment generated interesting data, but the findings were ultimately irreproducible.
With poor documentation, the software's behavior became effectively irreproducible for other researchers.

Synonyms

  • unreproducible
    9.5 0.01/m
    unreproducible describes a result that cannot be repeated exactly, while irreproducible is an almost identical formal term used in reports.
  • nonreproducible
    8.6 0.01/m
    nonreproducible appears as a technical variant in writing, while irreproducible is the common formal adjective for results that cannot be repeated.
  • uncopyable
    8.1 0.01/m
    uncopyable means impossible to copy because of uniqueness or design, while irreproducible refers to results or actions that cannot be repeated exactly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duplicate - to make an exact copy of something
  • antonym: reproducible - able to be reproduced or duplicated
