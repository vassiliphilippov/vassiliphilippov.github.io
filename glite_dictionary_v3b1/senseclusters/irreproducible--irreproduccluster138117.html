irreproducible

adjective
impossible to reproduce or duplicate
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪriˌproʊˈduːsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪriːprəˈdjuːsəbl / (BrE)
(of a result, action, or object) impossible to reproduce or duplicate; unable to be repeated exactly.

Examples

Irreproducible scientific results
Scientific claims must be verifiable and not based on irreproducible results.
The initial experiment generated interesting data, but the findings were ultimately irreproducible.
With poor documentation, the software's behavior became effectively irreproducible for other researchers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duplicate - to make an exact copy of something
  • antonym: reproducible - able to be reproduced or duplicated
