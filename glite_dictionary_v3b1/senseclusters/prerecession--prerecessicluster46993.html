prerecession

noun
time before recession
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːrɪˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːrɪˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the period of time immediately before an economic recession begins

Examples

The prerecession period
During the prerecession, consumer confidence was high and spending increased across many sectors.
Analysts studied the prerecession indicators to better predict future downturns.
The prerecession optimism led many companies to expand too quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth
  • antonym: recession - a period of economic decline
