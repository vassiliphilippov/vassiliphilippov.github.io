hunker down

verb
take shelter or assume a safe position
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌŋ.kɚ daʊn /; / ˈhʌŋkɚ daʊn /; / ˈhʌŋkər daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋ.kə daʊn /; / ˈhʌŋkə daʊn / (BrE)
to take shelter, settle in, or prepare for safety or a difficult period, often by staying in one place

Examples

To hunker down at home
During the blizzard, we decided to hunker down and wait for the roads to clear.
The soldiers hunkered down in their trenches as the enemy approached.
When the tornado warnings sounded, the whole family hunkered down in the basement.
During the drill, everyone was told to hunker down and stay still until the danger passed.
She hunkered down by the campfire, warming her hands in the cold night air.
The children hunkered down behind a fallen tree to wait out the storm.
As the hurricane approached, the residents decided to hunker down and wait it out.
During the power outage, we hunkered down with candles and blankets until morning.
With deadlines looming, the team knew they had to hunker down and focus on the work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bunker down - (chiefly American) to take shelter or refuge, usually in anticipation of difficulty
  • synonym: crouch - to lower the body close to the ground by bending the knees
  • antonym: expose oneself - to place oneself in a position without protection
  • antonym: stand up - to rise to a standing position
  • synonym: take cover - to seek protection from danger, especially by physically hiding
