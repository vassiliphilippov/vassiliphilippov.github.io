winged bean

noun
a tropical twining plant with winged pods
8.9
Pronunciation: / wɪŋd bin / (AmE), / wɪŋd biːn / (BrE)
a tropical climbing plant producing edible pods with four wing-like ridges and tuberous roots, cultivated in the Old World tropics

Examples

The winged bean plant
Farmers in Southeast Asia often cultivate winged bean for its nutrient-rich pods and roots.
The seeds and leaves of the winged bean are also edible and highly nutritious.
You can add sliced winged bean to stir-fries and salads for extra crunch and flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Psophocarpus tetragonolobus - scientific name for the winged bean plant
