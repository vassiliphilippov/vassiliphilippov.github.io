fid

noun
bar for ship rigging
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɪd / (AmE), / fɪd / (BrE)
a wooden or metal bar used to support, fasten, or separate parts of a ship's rigging

Examples

Mast fid
The sailor inserted a fid to secure the mast in place.
A fid is essential when splicing thick ropes on a sailing vessel.
He used a metal fid to separate the strands of the rope before tying the knot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cleat - a fitting on a ship to which ropes are secured
  • synonym: pin - a small rod used to fasten or secure parts together
