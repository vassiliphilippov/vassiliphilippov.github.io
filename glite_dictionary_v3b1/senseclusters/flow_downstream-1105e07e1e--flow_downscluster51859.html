flow downstream

verb
move toward lower point in a river
Pronunciation: / floʊ ˈdaʊnˌstriːm / (AmE), / fləʊ ˈdaʊnstriːm / (BrE)
to move in the direction that a river or stream is flowing, towards a lower point or the mouth

Examples

To flow downstream quickly
After heavy rains, debris can flow downstream and cause blockages near bridges.
Salmon usually swim upstream, but logs and other objects naturally flow downstream with the current.
The pollutants from the factory tend to flow downstream, affecting communities along the river bank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flow upstream - to move in the direction opposite to the flow of a river
