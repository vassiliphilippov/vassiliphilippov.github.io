persuadability

noun
ease of being convinced
7.6
Pronunciation: / pərˌsweɪdəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / pəˌsweɪdəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or degree of being easily convinced or influenced by others

Examples

High persuadability
Her persuadability made her a target for aggressive sales tactics.
The politician's persuadability was seen as both a strength and a weakness by his advisors.
Because of his high persuadability, he often changed his mind after talking to others.
The persuadability of the audience made the speaker's job much easier.
Mark's high persuadability means he often changes his mind after hearing new arguments.
Advertisers study the persuadability of different age groups to tailor their messages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convincibility - the ability to be convinced
  • synonym: influence - the power to affect how someone thinks or acts
  • antonym: resistance - the ability to withstand being persuaded
  • antonym: stubbornness - the quality of being unwilling to change one's mind
  • synonym: susceptibility - the state of being easily affected or influenced
