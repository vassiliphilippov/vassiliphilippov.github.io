carbonize

verb
unite with carbon (chemistry/metallurgy)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːr.bəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
to unite or cause to unite with carbon, especially in a chemical or metallurgical process

Examples

To carbonize steel
Modern steel production often requires manufacturers to carbonize the metal to improve its hardness.
Some industrial processes carbonize organic materials at high temperatures to create carbon-rich products.
The engineers developed a technique to carbonize the surface of the alloy for enhanced durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carburize - to combine or impregnate with carbon, especially in metals
