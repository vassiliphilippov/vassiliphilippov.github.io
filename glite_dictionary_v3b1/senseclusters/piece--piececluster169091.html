piece

noun
portion of a natural object
1.0 160/m
Pronunciation: / pis / (AmE), / piÀês / (BrE)
a part, portion, or share of something that has been separated or divided from a larger whole (such as food, objects, or profits)

Examples

They each took a piece of fruit from the basket.
Each piece of the puzzle fits perfectly into place.

The sculpture was missing a crucial piece when it was discovered.

Would you like another piece of pizza?

Synonyms

    1.0 388/m
    part is a component of a whole used in many contexts, while piece is a general smaller portion separated from something larger.
  • portion
    2.1 46/m
    portion is an amount set aside or served, often food, while piece is a general part taken from a larger whole.
  • share
    1.6 45/m
    share is a part divided among people or owners, often money or profit, while piece is any separated part of something.
  • slice
    1.0 16/m
    slice is a thin, flat cut of food like bread or meat, while piece is a more general portion from something larger.
  • slice
    3.6 4.69/m
    slice is an informal way to name a share, like a slice of the market, while piece is a neutral part of a whole.
  • segment
    3.0 33/m
    segment is a clearly cut section, often technical or market-based, while piece is a general part separated from a larger whole.
