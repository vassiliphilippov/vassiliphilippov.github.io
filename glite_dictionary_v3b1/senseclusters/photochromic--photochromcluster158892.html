photochromic

noun
light-sensitive material
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtəˈkroʊmɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈkrəʊmɪk / (BrE)
a material or substance that changes color when exposed to light, especially ultraviolet light

Examples

A photochromic changes color
The scientist placed the photochromic on the windowsill to observe its color change in sunlight.
Modern sunglasses often use a photochromic to automatically darken when exposed to bright light.
They tested a new photochromic to see how quickly it would respond to ultraviolet rays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photosensitive - reacting to light, especially by changing physically or chemically
