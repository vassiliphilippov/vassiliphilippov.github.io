Cicero

proper noun
Roman orator and writer
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪsəˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪsərəʊ / (BrE)
a renowned Roman statesman, orator, and writer of the first century BCE, famous for his speeches and philosophical works

Examples

Cicero the Roman orator
Cicero is often quoted for his eloquent speeches and philosophical works.
Many students study the writings of Cicero to learn about Roman rhetoric.
The influence of Cicero can be seen in modern legal and political thought.
