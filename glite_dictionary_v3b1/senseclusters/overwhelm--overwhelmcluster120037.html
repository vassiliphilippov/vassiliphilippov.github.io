Examples

To overwhelm with emotion
Grief can sometimes overwhelm us when we least expect it.
She was so overwhelmed by the kindness of strangers that she began to cry.

Synonyms

  • overcome
    4.1 6/m
    overcome means being strongly affected by emotion suddenly and losing control, while overwhelm means being unable to cope with strong feelings.
  • overpower
    5.7 1/m
    overpower means forcefully affect someone so they cannot think or act, while overwhelm covers strong emotional or mental pressure.
  • sweep over
    4.6 0.8/m
    sweep over means a feeling hits suddenly and covers people quickly, while overwhelm can be slow or sudden and crushes coping ability.
  • overtake
    5.1 2/m
    overtake means a feeling comes on suddenly and stops a person from reacting, while overwhelm is stronger and may last longer.
  • drown
    3.5 4/m
    drown means be buried under too much work or feeling until one cannot cope, while overwhelm is broader and includes shock.
  • deluge
    7.4 0.6/m
    deluge means flood someone with a large amount of tasks or information quickly, while overwhelm can be emotional or mental beyond coping.
  • weigh down
    4.9 0.6/m
    weigh down means make someone feel sad, worried, or burdened over time, while overwhelm often means sudden loss of coping.
