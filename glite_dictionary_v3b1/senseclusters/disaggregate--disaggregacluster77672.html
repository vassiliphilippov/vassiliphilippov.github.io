disaggregate

verb
to separate parts
7.5
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈæɡ.rə.ɡeɪt/ /; / /dɪs.ˈæɡ.rɪ.ɡeɪt/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈæɡ.rə.ɡeɪt/ /; / /dɪs.ˈæɡ.rɪ.ɡeɪt/ (BrE)
to separate something into its individual parts or groups, especially for analysis

Examples

To disaggregate data
The researchers disaggregated the survey results to study each age group individually.
It is important to disaggregate the data by gender to identify any disparities.
Before making conclusions, analysts often disaggregate complex information into smaller, more manageable pieces.
The scientists disaggregate the results to better understand each subgroup's response.
It is important to disaggregate the statistics by age and gender for accurate analysis.
Before making decisions, the company will disaggregate the sales data by region.
The researchers disaggregated the survey results by age group to identify trends.
It is important to disaggregate the data to understand differences between regions.
The report disaggregates the statistics to show how each department performed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggregate - to combine several things into a whole
  • synonym: analyze - to examine something in detail to understand it better
  • synonym: separate - to divide into different parts or groups
