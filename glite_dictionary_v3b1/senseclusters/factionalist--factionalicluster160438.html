factionalist

noun
supporter of a faction
Pronunciation: / ˈfækʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfækʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or promotes the interests of a particular faction within a group or organization

Examples

A factionalist leader
The political debate was disrupted by a vocal factionalist who refused to compromise.
As a known factionalist, she often argued for her group's interests over the common good.
The organization struggled to make progress because each factionalist pushed their own agenda.

Related Terms

  • synonym: partisan - a strong supporter of a party, cause, or person
  • antonym: unifier - a person who brings people together
