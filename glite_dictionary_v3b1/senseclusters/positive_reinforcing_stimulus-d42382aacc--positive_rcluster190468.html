positive reinforcing stimulus

noun
rewarding stimulus increasing a behavior
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzətɪv rɪˈfɔrsɪŋ ˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv rɪˈfɔːsɪŋ ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
a stimulus that, when presented after a behavior, increases the likelihood that the behavior will occur again

Examples

A positive reinforcing stimulus
Receiving praise from a teacher can serve as a positive reinforcing stimulus for good classroom behavior.
Salary bonuses act as a positive reinforcing stimulus for employees to achieve higher performance.
The therapist used a piece of candy as a positive reinforcing stimulus to encourage the child to complete the task.

Related Terms

  • antonym: negative reinforcing stimulus - a stimulus that, when removed after a behavior, increases the likelihood of the behavior
  • synonym: reinforcer - something that increases the probability of a behavior
