unloose

verb
free or release from restraint
6.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlus /; / ʌnˈluːs / (AmE), / ʌnˈluːs / (BrE)
to release or make something less tight; to set free or loosen

Examples

To unloose the bonds
Ancient myths often tell of heroes who unloose magical creatures from their bonds.
The guard decided to unloose the prisoner after hearing his story.
With a single gesture, she unloosed the chains and set the captive bird free.
Before taking off your shoes, remember to unloose the laces.
She carefully unloosed the ribbon wrapped around the present.
The storm unloosed several tiles from the old roof.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • synonym: loosen - make less tight; untie or relax
  • synonym: release - to set free from confinement; to allow to move freely
  • antonym: tighten - make tighter or more secure
