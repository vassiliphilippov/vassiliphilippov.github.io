enter

verb
to go into a place
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə / (BrE)
to go or come into a place or area

Examples

To enter a room
As the sun set, the hikers entered the dense forest.
Please make sure to sign in before you enter the conference room.
You must enter the building through the main doors.
As the curtain rises, the actor enters to a silent, expectant audience.
She waited for her cue to enter the stage, heart pounding with excitement.
The main character will enter from the left wing during the second act.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey or trip
  • antonym: exit - to go out or leave a place
