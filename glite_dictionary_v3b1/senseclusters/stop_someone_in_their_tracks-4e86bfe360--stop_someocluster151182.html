stop (someone) in (their) tracks

verb
to suddenly halt someone through surprise or shock
3.9
Pronunciation: / stɑpt ɪn ðɛr træks / (AmE), / stɒpt ɪn ðə træks / (BrE)
to cause someone to suddenly stop what they are doing or thinking, usually due to surprise, shock, or amazement

Examples

To stop someone in their tracks
As he walked down the street, a familiar voice stopped him in his tracks.
The announcement of her promotion stopped her in her tracks and left her speechless.
The news was so shocking that it stopped everyone in their tracks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shock - to greatly surprise or upset someone
