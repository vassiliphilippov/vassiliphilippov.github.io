to cause strong feelings, enthusiasm, or agitation in someone; to make someone feel excited or emotionally stirred

Examples

To excite the crowd
The coach's speech managed to excite the players before the big game.
The fireworks excited the children, who couldn't stop cheering.
The news of the upcoming festival really excited the community.
Her passionate speech excited strong emotions in the audience.
The new discovery will surely excite interest among scientists.
The prospect of traveling abroad always excites him.
It doesn't take much to excite children at a birthday party.
The announcement of the new project will excite the entire team.
The spectacular fireworks display really excited the crowd last night.

Synonyms

  • enthuse
    5.5 0.50/m
    enthuse is used to show strong public enthusiasm, while excite is a broader word for causing strong feelings or agitation.
  • rouse
    5.1 1.38/m
    rouse wakes or pushes someone into action or strong feeling, while excite can simply make someone feel enthusiastic or emotionally stirred.
  • arouse
    4.1 5.72/m
    arouse brings up a particular feeling or interest, while excite focuses on causing strong general emotions or agitation.
  • stir
    3.7 7.40/m
    stir moves people with a deep, often quiet emotion, while excite more often causes lively enthusiasm or visible agitation.
  • exhilarate
    6.9 1.16/m
    exhilarate makes someone feel very happy and lively, while excite can mean any strong feeling, even agitation or worry.
  • thrill
    2.1 9.11/m
    thrill gives a sudden strong pleasure or excitement, while excite covers both sudden and slower feelings, including agitation.
  • electrify
    4.1 0.67/m
    electrify shocks and thrills a crowd with sudden intense feeling, while excite can cause many kinds of strong emotion more generally.
  • stimulate
    3.7 21/m
    stimulate makes someone more active, alert, or interested, while excite mainly means arousing strong emotion or agitation.
