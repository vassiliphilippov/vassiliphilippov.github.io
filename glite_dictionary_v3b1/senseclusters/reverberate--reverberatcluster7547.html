reverberate

verb

to echo, resound, or be repeatedly reflected, especially of sound, light, or similar things

Examples

Sound reverberates loudly
Her laughter reverberated around the empty room, making it feel less lonely.
The sound of the bell reverberated through the vast cathedral, lingering in the air.
Thunder reverberated throughout the valley, startling the hikers with its intensity.
Her footsteps reverberated off the empty hallway walls, creating a deep echo.
In the auditorium, every word reverberated clearly, allowing the audience to hear perfectly.
The sound of thunder reverberated through the canyon, making the ground tremble.
Sunlight reverberated off the glass buildings, making the street almost blindingly bright.
The shockwaves from the blast reverberated across the valley, rattling windows miles away.
The sound of the explosion reverberated through the narrow streets for several seconds.

Synonyms

  • resound
    5.3 1.21/m
    resound fills a place with a loud, carrying sound, while reverberate means sound that echoes and bounces repeatedly.
  • resonate
    5.1 2.03/m
    resonate makes a deep, full vibration you can feel, while reverberate is more about many reflected sounds in a space.
  • echo
    1.4 3.60/m
    echo repeats a sound as a clear single reflection from a surface, while reverberate means many overlapping reflections.
  • ring
    1.0 22/m
    ring produces a bright, bell-like tone that can be heard clearly, while reverberate means sound that echoes and lingers.
  • reecho
    7.3 0.00/m
    reecho returns a sound again and again in a simple repeat, while reverberate describes a long, fading cascade of echoes.
