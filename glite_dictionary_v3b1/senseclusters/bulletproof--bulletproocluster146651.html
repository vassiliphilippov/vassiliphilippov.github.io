bulletproof

adjective
impossible to disprove or attack
1.6 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbʊl.ɪt.pruːf/ /; / /ˈbʊl.ət.pruːf/ (AmE), /ˈbʊl.ɪt.pruːf/ /; / /ˈbʊl.ət.pruːf/ (BrE)
incapable of being disproved, refuted, or penetrated by criticism; without any flaws or loopholes

Examples

Bulletproof plan
Her lawyer presented a bulletproof argument in court that the prosecution could not challenge.
His bulletproof plan left no room for error or misunderstanding.
They needed a bulletproof contract to make sure all responsibilities were clear.
