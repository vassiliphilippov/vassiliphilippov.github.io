temporize

verb
delay to gain time or avoid decision
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəraɪz / (BrE)
to delay making a decision or taking action, especially in order to gain time, avoid conflict, or avoid committing oneself

Examples

To temporize with delay
If we temporize any longer, we might miss the opportunity.
She tried to temporize by changing the subject whenever the question came up.
The negotiators chose to temporize rather than confront the issue directly.
She tried to temporise by raising minor objections during the meeting.
The committee continued to temporise despite growing pressure to make a decision.
The manager chose to temporise rather than commit to a clear plan immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dawdle - to waste time and move slowly
  • antonym: decide - to come to a resolution or make a final choice
  • antonym: hasten - to act quickly or speed up a process
  • synonym: procrastinate - delay or postpone action
