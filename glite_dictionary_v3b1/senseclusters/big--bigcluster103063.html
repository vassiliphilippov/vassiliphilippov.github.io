big

adjective
loud and resonant (of sound)
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ / (AmE), / bɪɡ / (BrE)
loud, strong, or powerful in intensity (such as a sound or natural force)

Examples

A big noise
She played a big chord on the piano that echoed through the room.
The band's sound was especially big during the chorus, making the audience cheer.
The singer's big voice filled the concert hall with energy.
A big wind knocked over several trees during the storm.
The sailors prepared for a big storm that was forecast to hit the coast.
The tent collapsed in the big gusts that swept through the campsite.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentle - mild, not strong or intense
  • synonym: loud - producing a lot of noise
  • synonym: strong - having great power or force
