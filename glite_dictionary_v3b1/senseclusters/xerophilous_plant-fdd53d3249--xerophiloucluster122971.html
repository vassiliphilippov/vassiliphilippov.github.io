xerophilous plant

noun
plant adapted to dry environments
8.5 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌzɪəˈrɑːfɪləs ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˌzɪəˈrɒfɪləs ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that is adapted to survive in environments with very limited water supply, such as deserts or dry regions.

Examples

xerophilous plants in deserts
Botanists study xerophilous plants to understand how life adapts to extreme dryness.
Many xerophilous plants have thick, waxy leaves to minimize water loss.
The cactus is a classic example of a xerophilous plant found in desert regions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrophyte - plant adapted to living in water or very wet environments
  • antonym: mesophyte - plant adapted to environments with moderate water
