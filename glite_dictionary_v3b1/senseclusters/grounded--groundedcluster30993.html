grounded

adjective
based on facts
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndɪd / (BrE)
based on solid facts, principles, or evidence; having a firm and realistic foundation

Examples

Grounded in fact
Her arguments are always grounded in careful research and logical reasoning.
The teacher praised the student for having a grounded understanding of the subject.
Their approach to solving problems is grounded in practical experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: founded - having a basis in something
  • antonym: unfounded - not based on facts or evidence
