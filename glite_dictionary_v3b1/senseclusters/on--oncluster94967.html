on

preposition
position or state
Pronunciation: / ɑn / (AmE), / ɒn / (BrE)
used to show position above or touching a surface, or to indicate a state, activity, or involvement

Examples

On the table
She placed her keys on the kitchen counter before making coffee.
The children are on vacation from school this week.
He is working on a new project for his company.

Related Terms

  • antonym: off - not on or removed from a surface
  • synonym: upon - on top of or resting on
