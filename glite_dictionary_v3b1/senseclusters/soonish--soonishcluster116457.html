soonish

adjective, adverb
fairly soon
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsuːnɪʃ / (BrE)
happening or expected to happen fairly soon, but not at a specific time (informal)

Examples

A soonish time
Let’s meet for coffee at a soonish time, maybe late morning or early afternoon.
The repairs should be finished soonish, but I can’t give you an exact hour.
We’ll send out the invitations soonish so everyone has time to respond.
I'll meet you at the cafe soonish, maybe in half an hour.
The package should arrive soonish, but I can't give an exact time.
He said he'd finish the report soonish, so expect it later today.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: imminent - about to happen very soon
  • antonym: later - at a time after the present or expected time
  • synonym: soon - in a short time from now
