co-pastor

verb
to share pastoral duties
6.4
Pronunciation: /koʊ ˈpæ.stər/ /; / /koʊ ˈpɑː.stər/ (AmE), /kəʊ ˈpɑː.stə/ /; / /koʊ ˈpɑː.stə/ (BrE)
to serve as a pastor of a church together with one or more other people, sharing leadership and responsibilities

Examples

To co-pastor a church
They decided to co-pastor the church to better serve the growing congregation.
For several years, the two ministers have co-pastored the rural parish, dividing their responsibilities equally.
It can be challenging to co-pastor when leadership styles are very different.
