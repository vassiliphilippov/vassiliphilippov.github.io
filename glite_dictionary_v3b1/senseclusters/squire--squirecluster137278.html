squire

noun
young noble attendant to a knight
6.0 9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskwaɪr / (AmE), / ˈskwaɪə / (BrE)
a young nobleman who acted as an attendant and apprentice to a knight before becoming a knight himself, especially in medieval times

Examples

Medieval squire
As a squire, he learned the arts of combat and chivalry from his master.
Every aspiring knight first served as a squire to gain experience and training.
The young squire tended to his knight’s armor and horse before battle.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: knight - a man awarded a nonhereditary title of honor, often serving as a mounted soldier in the Middle Ages
  • degree lower: page - a boy acting as a knight's attendant as the first stage of training for chivalry
