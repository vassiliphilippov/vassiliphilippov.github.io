Rubia cordifolia

noun
an Asian herb used for red dye
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈruː.bi.ə ˌkɔr.dɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈruː.bɪ.ə ˌkɔː.dɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.ə / (BrE)
a perennial climbing or creeping herb native to East India and other Asian regions, used especially for making red dye

Examples

Rubia cordifolia red dye
Dye extracted from Rubia cordifolia was highly valued in ancient Asian cultures.
For centuries, Rubia cordifolia roots have been used to produce a deep red textile dye.
The herbalist recommended Rubia cordifolia for its traditional medicinal properties in Ayurveda.
