Bloomerist philosophy

noun
educational theory advocating women's rights
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbluːmərɪst fəˈlɑːsəfi / (AmE), / ˈbluːmərɪst fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
An educational theory advocating for the advancement of women's rights, associated with the 19th-century Bloomerist movement.

Examples

Bloomerist philosophy principles
Her teaching methods were inspired by the principles of Bloomerist philosophy.
Many suffragists adopted the Bloomerist philosophy to champion social change.
The Bloomerist philosophy played a significant role in the progress of co-educational schools.

Related Terms
