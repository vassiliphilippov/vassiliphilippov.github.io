carbachol

noun
glaucoma treatment drug
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbəkɒl /; / ˈkɑrbəˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbəkɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used in medicine and research to stimulate nerves or muscles, especially to treat glaucoma or help the bladder and intestines work

Examples

Carbachol eye drops
The ophthalmologist prescribed carbachol to help lower the patient's eye pressure.
After surgery, the doctor used carbachol to stimulate the patient's bladder function.
Some patients with glaucoma use carbachol eye drops as part of their daily treatment.
Researchers used carbachol to stimulate muscle contractions in the laboratory experiment.
The doctor prescribed carbachol eye drops to help reduce the patient's intraocular pressure.
In pharmacology studies, carbachol is often used to activate muscarinic receptors in tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetylcholine - a natural neurotransmitter involved in nerve signal transmission
  • antonym: atropine - a drug that blocks the effects of acetylcholine
  • synonym: pilocarpine - a drug used to treat glaucoma by reducing eye pressure
