acetic anhydride

noun
chemical used in organic synthesis
8.2
Pronunciation: / əˈsiːtɪk ænˈhaɪdraɪd / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪk anˈhʌɪdrɪd / (BrE)
a colorless liquid chemical compound (C4H6O3) derived from acetic acid, widely used as an acetylating agent in organic synthesis, including the production of pharmaceuticals and, illicitly, heroin from opium.

Examples

Acetic anhydride chemical compound
Acetic anhydride is commonly used in laboratories for synthesizing aspirin.
Strict regulations control the sale and distribution of acetic anhydride due to its potential misuse.
The illegal production of heroin often involves the use of acetic anhydride.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetylating agent - a substance that introduces an acetyl group into a compound
