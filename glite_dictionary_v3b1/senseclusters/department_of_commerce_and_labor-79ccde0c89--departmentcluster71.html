Department of Commerce and Labor

noun
former US executive department (1903–1913)
6.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpɑɹtmənt əv ˈkɑmɚs ənd ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtmənt ɒv ˈkɒmɜːs ənd ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
a former executive department of the United States government, established in 1903 and divided into the Department of Commerce and the Department of Labor in 1913

Examples

The Department of Commerce and Labor
After the Department of Commerce and Labor was split, separate departments for commerce and labor were created.
Congress established the Department of Commerce and Labor to address the changing economic landscape of the early 20th century.
The Department of Commerce and Labor was responsible for overseeing both commercial and labor issues until its division in 1913.
