white-throated sparrow

noun
North American songbird with a white throat
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌwaɪt ˈθroʊtɪd ˈspɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˈθrəʊtɪd ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
A common North American finch with a distinctive white patch on its throat and black-and-white striped crown.

Examples

A white-throated sparrow bird
A white-throated sparrow perched on the fence, singing its familiar sweet tune.
During migration, flocks of white-throated sparrows can often be seen in city parks.
The distinct song of the white-throated sparrow is a welcome sound in the spring woods.
