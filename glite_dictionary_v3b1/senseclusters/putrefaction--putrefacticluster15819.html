putrefaction

noun
decay of organic matter with foul odor
Pronunciation: / ˌpjuːtrəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of decay or rotting in organic matter, usually producing a bad smell

Examples

Animal putrefaction
Rapid putrefaction set in after the animal died in the summer heat.
Scientists studied the stages of putrefaction in decomposing organisms.
The smell of putrefaction coming from the abandoned building was overwhelming.
Rapid putrefaction occurs if a dead animal is left in warm, humid conditions.
Scientists studied the bacteria responsible for putrefaction in organic material.
The smell of putrefaction made the abandoned house unbearable to enter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposing
  • synonym: decomposition - breakdown of organic substances, especially after death
  • degree lower: fermentation - chemical breakdown, especially by microorganisms, but not necessarily involving foul odors
  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something from decaying
