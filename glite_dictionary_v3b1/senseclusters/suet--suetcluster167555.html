suet

noun
hard fat from beef or sheep used in cooking
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːət / (AmE), / ˈsuːɪt / (BrE)
the hard fat found around the kidneys and loins of beef or sheep, used in cooking

Examples

Raw suet fat
Melted suet can be used to make fat balls for feeding wild birds in winter.
The butcher set aside a quantity of suet for making pastries and dumplings.
Traditional British puddings often use suet to give them a rich texture.
