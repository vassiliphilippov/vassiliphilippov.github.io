epigenetic

adjective
gene activity changes
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to changes in gene activity that do not involve alterations to the DNA sequence

Examples

Epigenetic changes in genes
Scientists are studying how epigenetic factors influence the development of certain diseases.
Diet and stress can lead to epigenetic modifications that affect gene expression.
The research focused on epigenetic changes passed from one generation to the next.

  • antonym: genetic - relating to genes or heredity
