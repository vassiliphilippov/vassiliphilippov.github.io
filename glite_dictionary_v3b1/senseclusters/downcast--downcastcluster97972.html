downcast

adjective
eyes or glance directed downward
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnkɑːst / (BrE)
(of eyes, look, or glance) directed downward, especially as a sign of sadness or modesty.

Examples

Downcast eyes
She avoided my gaze, her downcast eyes revealing her discomfort.
The child's downcast look made it clear that he was feeling ashamed.
With a downcast glance, he admitted his mistake to the group.
