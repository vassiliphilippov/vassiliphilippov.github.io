intervalometer

noun
timing device for cameras
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərvəˈlɑːmətər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəvəˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a device that automatically triggers or controls equipment at set time intervals, especially used in photography

Examples

A intervalometer device
The photographer set up his intervalometer to capture the stars moving across the night sky.
Using an intervalometer, she created a time-lapse video of the city waking up at dawn.
He bought an intervalometer to automate the process of taking photos every few seconds.
The photographer used an intervalometer to capture a series of images for a time-lapse video.
By connecting the intervalometer to the camera, she could take a photo every minute automatically.
Scientists set up an intervalometer to record temperature readings at regular intervals throughout the night.
