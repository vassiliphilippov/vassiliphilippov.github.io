stand

verb
occupy a place or position
9.9 1.0
Pronunciation: /stænd/ (AmE), /stænd/ (BrE)
to be in an upright position or to put something in an upright position; to occupy a place or position (physically or metaphorically)

Examples

To stand in line
He stood at the center of attention during the entire ceremony.
The new hospital will stand at the corner of Main Street.
We stand united in our pursuit of justice.
After hours of standing in line, my feet became sore.
She prefers to stand while giving her presentations.
We had to stand for the entire concert because there were no seats.
The new tower will stand nearly 300 meters upon completion.
The statue stands over ten meters high in the city square.
This pine tree stands sixty feet tall, making it the tallest in the forest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lie - to be in a flat position on a surface
  • synonym: measure - to have a specified length, height, or width
  • antonym: sit - to be in a position in which one's weight is supported by one's buttocks rather than one's feet
  • synonym: upright - vertical; placed in a straight, vertical position
