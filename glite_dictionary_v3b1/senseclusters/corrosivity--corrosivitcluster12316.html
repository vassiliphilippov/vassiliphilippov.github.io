corrosivity

noun
gradual damaging quality
Pronunciation: / kəˌroʊˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / kəˌrəʊˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
the quality of gradually causing damage or weakening, especially to something non-physical like trust or morale

Examples

Corrosivity of criticism
The corrosivity of constant criticism eventually undermined the team's confidence.
Many were alarmed by the corrosivity of the rumors spreading through the community.
The corrosivity of distrust can slowly erode even the strongest relationships.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beneficence - the quality of doing good or causing benefit
  • synonym: destructiveness - the quality of causing damage or harm
  • synonym: harmfulness - the quality of causing harm
