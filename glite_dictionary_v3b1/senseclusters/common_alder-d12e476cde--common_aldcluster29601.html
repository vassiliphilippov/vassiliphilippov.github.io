common alder

noun
a species of alder tree (Alnus glutinosa)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈɔldɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a medium-sized deciduous tree (Alnus glutinosa) with brown-black bark, woody catkins, and leaves hairy beneath, commonly found along wet areas in Europe and western Asia

Examples

The common alder tree
In spring, the common alder produces small, woody catkins that hang from its branches.
The common alder is often planted along riverbanks to prevent soil erosion.
We identified the tree as a common alder by examining the hairy undersides of its leaves.
