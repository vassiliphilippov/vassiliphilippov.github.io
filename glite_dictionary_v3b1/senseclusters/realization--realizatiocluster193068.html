realization

noun
completed/enriched musical composition by another
Pronunciation: / ˌriələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌriːələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of completing or elaborating a piece of music, often by adding harmonies, instrumentation, or finishing unfinished sections

Examples

Realization of a score
His realization of the composer's notes brought the long-lost opera to life on stage.
Scholars praised her realization of the Baroque score for its sensitive use of period instruments.
The conductor presented a new realization of the unfinished symphony using the composer's original sketches.
Composers often invite musicians to attempt their own realization of a figured bass.
Her acclaimed realization of Bach's unfinished fugue brought the piece to life for modern audiences.
The pianist's realization of the Baroque manuscript added new melodic lines and harmony.
Her realisation of the incomplete score brought new depth to the composer's intentions.
The realisation of the Baroque sonata required a skilled harpsichordist to interpret the figured bass.
The quartet performed a modern realisation of the unfinished chamber piece.
