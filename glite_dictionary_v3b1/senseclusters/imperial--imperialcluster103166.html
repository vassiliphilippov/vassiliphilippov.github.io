imperial

adjective
relating to an emperor or empress
5.5 6.2
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəl / (BrE)
relating to an empire, emperor, or empress; royal, majestic, or suitable for a ruler

Examples

Imperial palace
She wore a gown of imperial purple, fit for an empress.
The imperial palace stood at the heart of the city, towering over all other buildings.
The museum displayed weapons and jewelry from the imperial court of ancient China.
British measures often use the imperial system, which differs from the metric system.
The imperial army marched across the provinces, expanding the emperor’s territory.
The palace had an imperial style that showcased the grandeur of the former empire.
During the imperial era, rulers were admired for their wealth and power.
He entered the room with an imperial air, commanding attention from everyone present.
The palace was decorated in an imperial style, with gold accents and grand chandeliers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: common - ordinary; not related to royalty or nobility
  • synonym: regal - suitable for a king or queen; royal
  • antonym: republican - relating to a republic, opposed to imperial or monarchical rule
  • synonym: royal - relating to a king or queen
