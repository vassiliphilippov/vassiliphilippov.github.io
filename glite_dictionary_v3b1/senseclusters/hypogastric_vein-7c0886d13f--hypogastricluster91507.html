hypogastric vein

noun
pelvic vein forming the common iliac vein
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.poʊˈɡæs.trɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəʊˈɡas.trɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the pelvic region that unites with the external iliac vein to form the common iliac vein

Examples

Hypogastric vein in pelvis
Blood from the pelvic organs drains through the hypogastric vein into the common iliac vein.
Damage to the hypogastric vein can result in severe internal bleeding during pelvic trauma.
The hypogastric vein is important in surgical procedures involving the pelvic area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internal iliac vein - a large vein that drains blood from the pelvis (modern anatomical term)
