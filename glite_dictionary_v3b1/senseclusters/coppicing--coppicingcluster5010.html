coppicing

noun
woodland management method
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑpɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒpɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of woodland management in which trees are regularly cut back to encourage new shoots and growth

Examples

Traditional coppicing method
Traditional coppicing has been practiced in European woodlands for centuries to produce firewood and fencing materials.
The nature reserve uses coppicing to create habitats for a variety of wildlife species.
Regular coppicing encourages healthy regrowth and helps maintain the biodiversity of the forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pollarding - a tree management technique involving cutting upper branches to promote new growth
