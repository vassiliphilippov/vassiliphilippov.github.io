Kochab

proper noun
star in Ursa Minor
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊkæb / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkæb / (BrE)
a bright star in the constellation Ursa Minor, also known as Beta Ursae Minoris

Examples

Star Kochab in Ursa Minor
The ancient Egyptians used Kochab as a reference point for building the pyramids.
Alongside Polaris, Kochab helps form the distinctive shape of the Little Dipper.
On a clear night, you can easily spot Kochab shining brightly in the northern sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Polaris - the brightest star in Ursa Minor, also known as the North Star
