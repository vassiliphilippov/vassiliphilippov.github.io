denature

verb
make nuclear material unsuitable for bombs
Pronunciation: / dɪˈneɪtʃər / (AmE), / dɪˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
to add nonfissionable material to fissionable material, making it unsuitable for use in an atomic bomb

Examples

To denature plutonium
Nuclear engineers often denature plutonium to prevent its use in weapons.
The process will denature uranium, making it unsuitable for bomb production.
To enhance security, facilities routinely denature fissionable materials with nonfissionable isotopes.
