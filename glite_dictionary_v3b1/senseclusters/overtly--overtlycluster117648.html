overtly

adverb
openly; not secretly
3.7 4.33/m
Pronunciation: / o äÀàv…ùtli / (AmE), / …ô äÀàv…úÀêtli / (BrE)
In a way that is open and not secret or hidden; openly or visibly.

Examples

To overtly show feelings
She overtly challenged the company's decision in a public letter.
The politician stated his position overtly during the debate, leaving no room for doubt.
Their marketing campaign was overtly aimed at young adults.

Synonyms

  • openly
    1.6 23/m
    openly means showing something without hiding it, while overtly stresses that it is clearly visible and not hidden.
  • in the open
    2.5 3.11/m
    in the open is used for things done or shown in public, while overtly focuses on being plainly seen or not hidden.
  • outright
    4.3 2.55/m
    outright is direct and complete, used without doubt or reserve, while overtly emphasizes being openly shown and not hidden.
  • directly
    1.4 5.93/m
    directly means doing or saying something in a straightforward way, while overtly highlights being open and not hidden.
  • visibly
    1.6 5.54/m
    visibly can be seen by the eyes, while overtly can mean openly shown or clearly noticed, not only physically seen.
