tin plague

noun
transformation of tin at low temperatures
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɪn pleɪɡ / (AmE), / tɪn pleɪɡ / (BrE)
A phenomenon in which white tin turns into brittle, powdery grey tin at very low temperatures.

Examples

Tin plague effect
At low temperatures, antique tinware may suffer from tin plague, causing it to crumble.
Scientists studied the effects of tin plague on electronics stored in extreme cold environments.
The brittle powder produced by tin plague poses problems for preserving old tin artifacts.
