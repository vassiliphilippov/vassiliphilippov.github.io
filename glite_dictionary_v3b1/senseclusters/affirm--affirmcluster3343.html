affirm

verb
to state or confirm positively
4.1 14/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàf…úrm /; / …ôÀàf…ùÀêm / (AmE), / …ôÀàf…úÀêm / (BrE)
to state or declare that something is true, or to show support for something

Examples

To affirm a statement
He repeatedly affirms his commitment to honesty in all his work.
She affirmed her support for the new policy during the meeting.
The witness chose to affirm the statement under oath.
Recent research affirms the effectiveness of the proposed treatment.
The new documents affirm his claims about the meeting.
The witness's testimony affirmed that the suspect was at the scene.
He affirmed under oath that the evidence was genuine.
She affirmed her commitment to upholding the company's values.
The witness affirmed that he had seen the accident occur.

Synonyms

    7.9 0.30/m

  • assert
    3.9 28/m

  • maintain
    4.4 18/m
    maintain states a view firmly over time, often against disagreement, while affirm covers stating truth and showing support.
  • avow
    7.6 0.60/m
    avow openly declares or admits something publicly and often boldly, while affirm means to state truth or to express support.
  • endorse
    4.2 12/m
    endorse gives public approval or backing to a person or idea, while affirm can mean to state truth or to show support.
  • confirm
    1.8 62/m
    confirm proves or makes something certain with facts or evidence, while affirm is more about stating or showing support.
  • attest
    6.6 6.89/m
    attest officially confirms or gives witness to facts in formal or legal settings, while affirm means to state or show support.
