entwine

verb
twist or wind things together
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtwaɪn /; / ɛnˈtwaɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtwaɪn / (BrE)
to twist or wind things together, or to become twisted together (literally or figuratively)

Examples

To entwine fingers
His fingers gently entwined with hers as they walked.
Ivy was entwining up the columns, giving the building a wild, overgrown look.
She watched the vines entwine themselves around the old fence.
Legends and history often entwine in ancient cultures.
The vines entwine around the old wooden fence in the garden.
Their fingers entwined as they walked down the path together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intertwine - to twist or be twisted together
  • antonym: untangle - to separate things that are twisted or knotted together
