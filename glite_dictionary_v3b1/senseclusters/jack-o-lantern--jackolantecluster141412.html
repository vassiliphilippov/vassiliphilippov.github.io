jack-o-lantern

noun
poisonous orange mushroom
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæk.oʊˌlæn.tɚn / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.əʊˌlæn.tən / (BrE)
A large poisonous mushroom with orange caps, narrow clustered stalks, and luminescent gills (Omphalotus illudens).

Examples

A jack-o-lantern mushroom
At night, the jack-o-lantern glows faintly, making the forest floor appear enchanted.
Researchers study the chemical compounds that cause the jack-o-lantern mushroom to emit light.
The toxic jack-o-lantern should not be confused with the edible chanterelle, despite their similar color.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: chanterelle - an edible yellow or orange mushroom similar in appearance, but not poisonous
  • degree higher: mushroom - an edible or poisonous fungus with a stalk and cap
