fangy

adjective
shaped like a fang
Pronunciation: / ˈfæŋi / (AmE), / ˈfæŋi / (BrE)
having fangs or looking like a fang (long, narrow, and pointed)

Examples

A fangy blade
The ancient dagger had a fangy blade that could pierce thick leather easily.
She admired the fangy leaves of the rare plant in the greenhouse.
The artist designed a fangy necklace inspired by wolf teeth.
The wolf bared its fangy grin as it approached the campsite.
Some species of bats have especially fangy mouths for catching insects.
The artist drew a fangy monster for the cover of the comic book.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pointed - having a sharp or tapered end
  • synonym: toothed - having teeth, especially noticeable ones
  • antonym: toothless - lacking teeth
