underemployment

noun
insufficient job utilization
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (BrE)
a situation where people have jobs that do not fully use their skills, education, or available working hours

Examples

High underemployment rate
Many recent graduates face underemployment when they can only find part-time work.
The government is concerned about high levels of underemployment among skilled workers.
Addressing underemployment is important for improving the overall economy.
After graduating, she faced underemployment by working part-time in a job unrelated to her degree.
Many skilled workers experience underemployment when they cannot find positions that match their qualifications.
The government is trying to reduce underemployment by creating more specialized job opportunities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overemployment - a situation where people work more hours than they want or is healthy
  • antonym: unemployment - the state of not having a job
