xerophytic

adjective
adapted to dry environments
9.9 9.2
Pronunciation: /zɪə.rə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ /; / /ziː.rə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ (AmE), /zɪə.rə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ /; / /ziː.rə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a plant or organism) adapted to live in a dry or desert environment, typically with features that minimize water loss.

Examples

xerophytic plants
Cacti are classic examples of xerophytic species thriving in arid regions.
Desert environments are dominated by xerophytic vegetation that can survive long periods without rain.
Many xerophytic plants have thick, fleshy leaves to store water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrophytic - adapted to growing in water or very wet conditions
  • antonym: mesophytic - adapted to moderate or neither dry nor wet conditions
