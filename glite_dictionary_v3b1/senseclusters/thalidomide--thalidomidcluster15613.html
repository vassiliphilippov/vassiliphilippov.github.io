thalidomide

noun
drug withdrawn for causing birth defects
9.3
Pronunciation: /θə.ˈlɪ.də.maɪd/ (AmE), /θə.ˈlɪ.də.maɪd/ (BrE)
a sedative and hypnotic drug formerly prescribed for morning sickness, later withdrawn from the market after it was found to cause severe birth defects.

Examples

Thalidomide drug
Doctors now prescribe thalidomide for certain cancers despite its controversial history.
Many countries have strict guidelines for the use of thalidomide due to its past effects on pregnancy.
The use of thalidomide in the 1950s led to a tragedy involving thousands of children born with abnormalities.
