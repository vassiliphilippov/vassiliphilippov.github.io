polytomy

noun
multiple lineage split
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪtəmi / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtəmi /; / pɒˈlɪtəmi / (BrE)
a point in a tree diagram (especially a phylogenetic tree) where more than two branches come from a single ancestor, often showing uncertainty about relationships

Examples

A polytomy in trees
The evolutionary tree displayed a polytomy where several species diverged from a common ancestor.
Scientists debated whether the polytomy in the diagram represented rapid evolution or lack of data.
A polytomy can indicate uncertainty about the exact relationships among descendant groups.
The presence of a polytomy in the diagram suggests unresolved evolutionary relationships among the species.
Researchers debated whether the polytomy was due to rapid diversification or lack of data.
A large polytomy can make it difficult to interpret the evolutionary history of a group.
In the evolutionary tree, a polytomy indicates uncertainty about the relationships among several species.
The diagram showed a polytomy where three branches split from a single ancestor.
Resolving a polytomy can help clarify the order of evolutionary events.
