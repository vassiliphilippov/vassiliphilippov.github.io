polytomy

noun
multiple lineage split
9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈlɪ.tə.miː/ (AmE), /pə.ˈlɪ.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a point in a tree diagram (especially a phylogenetic tree) where more than two branches come from a single ancestor, often showing uncertainty about relationships

A polytomy can indicate uncertainty about the exact relationships among descendant groups.
The presence of a polytomy in the diagram suggests unresolved evolutionary relationships among the species.
Researchers debated whether the polytomy was due to rapid diversification or lack of data.
