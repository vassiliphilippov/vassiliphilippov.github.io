janissary

noun
loyal follower or supporter
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒænəˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒænɪsəri / (BrE)
A loyal follower or supporter, especially one who unquestioningly serves a leader or cause.

Examples

A janissary follower
Every movement has its janissary—someone who will champion the cause no matter what.
She surrounded herself with loyal janissaries who helped promote her ideas within the company.
The senator's janissaries defended every decision he made, regardless of public opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devotee - someone deeply dedicated to a person or cause
  • synonym: follower - one who supports and admires a leader or cause
  • antonym: opponent - a person who opposes or competes with another
