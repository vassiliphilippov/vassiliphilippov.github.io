a line, curve, or surface on a phase diagram indicating the boundary where a homogeneous phase becomes unstable and spontaneous separation begins

Examples

Spinodal curve
The spinodal marks the limit where a mixture becomes unstable and separates into different phases.
Scientists use the spinodal to predict when spontaneous phase separation will occur in alloys.
On the phase diagram, the spinodal helps identify regions of instability in the material.
