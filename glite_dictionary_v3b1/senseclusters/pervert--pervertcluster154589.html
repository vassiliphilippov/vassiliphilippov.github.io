pervert

verb
to distort from original purpose or meaning
7.5
Pronunciation: / pərˈvɝt /; / pərˈvɜrt / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to distort, twist, or corrupt the original meaning, purpose, or facts of something

Examples

To pervert the truth
He accused the officials of trying to pervert the original goals of the organization.
Some technology can be perverted from its intended use to cause harm.
When laws are perverted for personal gain, justice is undermined.
He tried to pervert the facts to protect himself from blame.
Please don't pervert what I said to fit your own agenda.
Some politicians will pervert the truth to sway public opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abuse - to use something for the wrong purpose
  • antonym: clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand
  • synonym: distort - to change something so that it is no longer true or accurate
  • synonym: falsify - to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • synonym: misuse - to use something incorrectly or wrongly
  • antonym: preserve - to maintain something in its original or existing state
