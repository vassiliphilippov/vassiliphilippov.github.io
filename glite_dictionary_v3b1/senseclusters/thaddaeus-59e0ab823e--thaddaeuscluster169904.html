Thaddaeus

proper noun
one of the Twelve Apostles in the New Testament
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈθæd.ɪ.əs/ /; / /θæ.ˈdeɪ.əs/ (AmE), /ˈθæd.ɪ.əs/ /; / /θæ.ˈdeɪ.əs/ (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the Twelve Apostles, sometimes identified as the brother of St. James and invoked as the patron saint of desperate or lost causes.

Examples

Thaddaeus the Apostle
