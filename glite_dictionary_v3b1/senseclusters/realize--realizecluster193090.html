realize

verb
convert assets into cash
1.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.ə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈrɪə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to convert goods, property, or assets into cash; to obtain money or profit from something

Examples

Realize a profit
It took months to realize the value of the artwork at auction.
Once we realize the property, we'll distribute the proceeds among the shareholders.
The company plans to realize its assets to pay off outstanding debts.
After selling her shares, she managed to realize over $10,000 in profit.
He was able to realize his earnings from the investment within a year.
The company hopes to realize a significant profit from its latest product launch.
She hopes to realise a substantial sum from the sale of her late uncle's property.
The company decided to realise some of its assets to raise funds.
The paintings were realised at auction for more than expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquire - to buy or obtain an asset or property
  • synonym: earn - to receive payment for work or services
  • synonym: liquidate - to convert assets into cash, especially to pay debts
  • antonym: lose - to fail to keep or gain money in a transaction
  • synonym: make - to earn or receive as income
