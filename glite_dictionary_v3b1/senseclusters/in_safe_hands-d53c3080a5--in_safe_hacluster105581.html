in safe hands

preposition
under competent, trustworthy care
Pronunciation: / ɪn seɪf hændz / (AmE), / ɪn seɪf hændz / (BrE)
in the care of someone who is competent and trustworthy, so that there is no reason to worry

Examples

In safe hands with experts
I felt reassured knowing that my application was in safe hands.
Our company's finances are in safe hands with the new accountant.
You can relax now; the children are in safe hands with their grandmother.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in bad hands - in the care of someone unreliable or untrustworthy
  • synonym: in good hands - being cared for or managed by someone competent
