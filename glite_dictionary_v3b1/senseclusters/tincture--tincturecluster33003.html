tincture

verb
fill or permeate with a quality
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪŋktʃər /; / ˈtɪŋktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋktʃə / (BrE)
to fill or subtly affect something with a quality, feeling, or color

Examples

Memories tinctured with joy
A subtle melancholy tinctured his memories of childhood.
Her voice was tinctured with regret as she spoke of the past.
The evening sky was tinctured with shades of orange and pink.
His memories of the event were tinctured with sadness.
The artist tinctured the canvas with muted shades of blue.
The sunset tinctured the clouds with a warm golden hue.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dye - to color something deeply or permanently
  • synonym: imbue - to fill or permeate with a feeling or quality
  • synonym: permeate - to spread or diffuse through
  • synonym: tinge - to color slightly; to tint
