adopt

verb
take on a form, quality, or attitude

to take on or begin to have a new role, responsibility, quality, or attitude

Examples

To adopt a new attitude
After much consideration, she adopted a more professional tone in her emails.
The actor adopted an air of confidence for the challenging role.
When living abroad, he adopted several customs unique to the local culture.
After the CEO retired, Mark adopted the responsibilities without hesitation.
She was chosen to adopt the role of team leader during the transition.
The company will adopt new safety regulations next year.

Synonyms

  • assume
    3.7 10/m
    assume often means formally taking a role or duty, while adopt also covers choosing a new attitude or quality.
  • take on
    2.8 22/m
    take on is to agree to handle a task or role, while adopt adds the idea of choosing a new attitude.
  • take on
    3.6 13/m
    take on can mean beginning to show a new quality or form, while adopt means actively choose and keep that quality.
  • take
    1.0 192/m
    take can simply mean occupying a position or state, while adopt stresses choosing and beginning a new role or attitude.
