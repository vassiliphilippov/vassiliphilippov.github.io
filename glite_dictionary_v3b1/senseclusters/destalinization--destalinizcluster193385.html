destalinization

noun
removal of Stalin's influence
Pronunciation: / dɛˌstɑːlɪnəˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌstɑlɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɛˌstɑːlɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌstælɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of removing the influence of Joseph Stalin from government, society, and culture, often by changing policies, removing monuments, and renaming locations.

Examples

Destalinization process
During the 1960s, destalinization prompted leaders to change place names associated with Stalin.
Many public buildings were renamed as part of the destalinization effort.
The policy of destalinization led to the removal of Stalin's statues across the country.
Monuments dedicated to Stalin were removed during the wave of destalinisation across Eastern Europe.
New policies were introduced as part of destalinisation to move away from authoritarian rule.
The country underwent destalinisation after a period of political reform in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: de-Stalinization - the American spelling of 'destalinisation'
  • synonym: de-Stalinize - to remove or reduce the influence of Joseph Stalin from an organization or society
