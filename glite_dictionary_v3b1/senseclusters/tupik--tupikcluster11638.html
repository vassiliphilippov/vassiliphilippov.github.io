tupik

noun
traditional Inuit summer tent
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː.pɪk / (AmE), / ˈtuː.pɪk / (BrE)
A traditional tent made of animal skins, used as a summer dwelling by Inuit people.

Examples

A tupik tent
In the museum, you can see a reconstructed tupik complete with caribou skin covering.
She described how her grandparents would set up a tupik along the river for fishing and camping.
The archaeologists uncovered the remains of a tupik used by Inuit families during the summer months.
