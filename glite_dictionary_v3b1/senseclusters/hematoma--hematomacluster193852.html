hematoma

noun
swelling of clotted blood under tissue
7.5 0.65/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåhiÀêm…ôÀàto äm…ô / (AmE), / ÀåhiÀêm…ôÀàt…ô äm…ô / (BrE)
A localized collection of blood outside blood vessels in tissue, often forming a swelling; the blood may be liquid or clotted.

Examples

A hematoma swelling
After the fall, a large hematoma developed near his elbow, causing discomfort.
It can take several weeks for a hematoma to completely disappear without medical intervention.
The doctor examined the hematoma on her leg and recommended rest.
A large haematoma developed after she bumped her head on the door.
The surgeon drained the haematoma to relieve pressure on the patient's leg.
Untreated, a haematoma can cause further complications and may require medical intervention.
