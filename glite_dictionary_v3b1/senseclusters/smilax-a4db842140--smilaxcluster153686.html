Smilax

noun
genus of woody climbing plants
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsmaɪlæks / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɪlæks / (BrE)
A genus of climbing shrubs and woody vines, many of which have prickly stems and are sometimes called greenbriers or catbriers.

Examples

Smilax climbing shrubs
Many species of Smilax have thorny stems that make them difficult to handle.
Researchers identified the plant as a member of the Smilax genus.
The woods were thick with Smilax, making it hard to find a clear path.
