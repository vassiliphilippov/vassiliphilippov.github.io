enclose

verb
surround completely
1.2 3.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkloʊz / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊz / (BrE)
to surround or cover something completely, often with a wall, fence, or barrier

Examples

To enclose with fence
A layer of mist enclosed the valley at dawn.
The garden was enclosed by a tall wooden fence.
Thick stone walls enclose the ancient city.
A stone wall encloses the ancient garden, keeping it private and serene.
Once the capsule doors closed, the astronauts were enclosed in the small cabin for the duration of the mission.
The dense mist began to enclose the bridge as night fell.
A thick fog began to enclose the city, making it difficult to see.
As the sun set, darkness enclosed the valley in shadows.
The garden is enclosed by a tall wooden fence for privacy.

  • antonym: expose - to leave uncovered or unprotected
  • antonym: open - not closed or surrounded on all sides
  • synonym: surround - to be all around; encircle
