domicile

verb
to make one's home in a place
5.8 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑː.mə.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.saɪl / (BrE)
to establish or make one's home in a particular place or community, often as a legal residence

Examples

To domicile in a city
After many years abroad, she decided to domicile in New York.
Corporations can domicile themselves in countries with more favorable tax laws.
Under the current law, you must domicile in the state for at least six months to qualify as a resident.
