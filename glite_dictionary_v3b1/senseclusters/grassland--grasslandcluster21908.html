grassland

noun
land dominated by grass vegetation
1.5 2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræsˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːsˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of land where grass is the main type of vegetation, often forming a natural habitat for wildlife or used for grazing animals

Examples

A grassland area
Bison once roamed freely across the North American grassland.
Farmers use the grassland for grazing their cattle in the summer months.
The grassland is home to many unique species of birds and mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prairie - a large open area of grassland, especially in North America
  • synonym: savanna - a grassy plain in tropical and subtropical regions, with few trees
