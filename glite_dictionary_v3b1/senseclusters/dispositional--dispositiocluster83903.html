dispositional

adjective
about personality traits
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to a person's or thing's usual mood, character, or tendency to behave in a certain way

Examples

Dispositional traits
His dispositional kindness made him popular among his classmates.
Researchers studied the dispositional factors that influence people's reactions to stress.
A dispositional tendency toward optimism can improve overall well-being.
Her dispositional optimism helped her stay positive during difficult times.
The psychologist examined the dispositional traits that influence decision-making.
Some materials have dispositional properties that make them resistant to heat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired - developed or learned; not innate
  • synonym: innate - existing from birth; inherent
  • antonym: situational - relating to a specific situation rather than a person's character
  • synonym: temperamental - relating to a person's usual mood or temperament
