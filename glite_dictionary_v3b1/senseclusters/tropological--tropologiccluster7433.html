tropological

adjective
moral interpretation
Pronunciation: / ˌtroʊpəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌtrɒpəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the moral or spiritual interpretation of texts, especially the moral sense of scripture in theology

Examples

Tropological interpretation of scripture
The scholar offered a tropological reading of the ancient text, focusing on its lessons for personal conduct.
In medieval theology, a tropological approach often emphasized the ethical teachings found in scripture.
Her essay explored the tropological significance of the parable, highlighting its message about compassion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegorical - relating to symbolic or metaphorical interpretation
  • antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
