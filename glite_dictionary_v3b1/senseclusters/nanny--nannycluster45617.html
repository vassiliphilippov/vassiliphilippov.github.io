nanny

noun
woman employed to care for children
Pronunciation: / ˈnæni / (AmE), / ˈnæni / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to take care of children in a family, usually in the family's home

Examples

Live-in nanny
After hiring a nanny, they found it much easier to balance work and family life.
The nanny reads stories to the children every afternoon before nap time.
When their son was a baby, a live-in nanny helped the parents with daily routines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babysitter - person who looks after children, usually temporarily
  • synonym: nursemaid - young woman employed to look after children
