shade

noun
a slight degree of difference
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪd / (AmE), / ʃeɪd / (BrE)
a slight or subtle difference in degree, meaning, feeling, or color

Examples

A shade of meaning
She looked a shade happier after hearing the news.
The weather was a shade cooler today than yesterday.
This sauce has a shade more spice than usual.
After the promotion, she found herself no longer living in the shade of her more famous colleague.
Despite her efforts, she remained in her sister's shade at work.
His groundbreaking discovery put all previous research deeply in the shade.
Her analysis captured every shade of emotion in the novel.
Political disagreements often arise from subtle shades of opinion.
The translator struggled with the shades of meaning between the two languages.
