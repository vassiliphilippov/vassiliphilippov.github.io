unsubstituted

adjective
no substituents attached
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnsəbˈstɪtjutɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnsəbˈstɪtjuːtɪd / (BrE)
not having any parts, atoms, or groups replaced or added, especially in a chemical compound

Examples

An unsubstituted compound

An unsubstituted aromatic ring often serves as a reference in chemical studies.
Researchers observed different reactivity in the unsubstituted molecule compared to its substituted forms.
The unsubstituted molecule served as a control in the chemical reaction.
Researchers compared the effects of substituted and unsubstituted compounds in the experiment.
An unsubstituted benzene ring contains only hydrogen atoms attached to the carbon atoms.
