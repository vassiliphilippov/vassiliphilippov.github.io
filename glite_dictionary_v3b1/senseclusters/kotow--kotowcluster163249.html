kotow

noun
traditional Chinese act of respect or submission
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈtaʊ / (AmE), / kəʊˈtaʊ / (BrE)
an act of deep respect or submission, traditionally shown in former Chinese custom by kneeling and touching the ground with the forehead

Examples

The kotow ceremony
He refused to do the kotow, insisting on maintaining his dignity.
Historical accounts describe the kotow as a symbol of absolute submission.
The ambassador was required to perform the kotow before the emperor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kowtow - to act in an excessively deferential manner; also the act itself
