keep a close watch

verb
monitor or observe carefully
3.0 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / kip …ô klo äs w…ët É / (AmE), / kiÀêp …ô kl…ô äs w…ít É / (BrE)
to monitor or observe someone or something very carefully, especially to notice any changes or problems

Examples

To keep a close watch on health
After the surgery, doctors had to keep a close watch on the patient's recovery.
Parents should keep a close watch on their children at the swimming pool.
The security team will keep a close watch on the entrance throughout the event.
