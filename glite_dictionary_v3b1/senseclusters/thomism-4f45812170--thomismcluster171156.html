Thomism

noun
doctrine developed by Saint Thomas Aquinas
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtoʊ.mɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈtɑː.mɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.mɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the system of theology and philosophy developed by Saint Thomas Aquinas, emphasizing reason, natural law, and the compatibility of faith and rational thought; still influential in Roman Catholic doctrine, especially among Dominicans

Examples

Thomism philosophy system
Her dissertation explored the influence of Thomism on modern legal theory.
Many Catholic universities continue to teach Thomism as a foundational approach to theology and philosophy.
The Dominicans remain strong advocates of Thomism, emphasizing its role in interpreting Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Augustinianism - theological doctrine based on Augustine, often contrasted with Thomism
  • synonym: Scholasticism - medieval philosophical and theological system that Thomism is part of
