voluntaryism

noun
belief in voluntary association
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːlənˌtɛriˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɒlənˌtɛəriˌɪzəm /; / ˈvɒlənˌtɛriˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or practice that people should act or associate voluntarily, without force or compulsion

Examples

Voluntaryism belief system

Many supporters of voluntaryism believe that charity should be given freely, not through government mandate.
His political philosophy shifted toward voluntaryism after reading several influential essays.
The charity was founded on the idea of voluntaryism, encouraging people to help without obligation.
Many political movements promote voluntaryism as an alternative to government intervention.
She admired the voluntaryism shown by community members who organized the event on their own.
The organization is based on voluntaryism, relying entirely on donations and unpaid workers.
Many supporters of voluntaryism believe that communities function best without government intervention.
The festival was organized through voluntaryism, with all services provided by local volunteers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anarchism - a political theory favoring the abolition of government and all forms of involuntary authority
  • antonym: authoritarianism - the enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • antonym: compulsion - the state of being forced to do something
  • antonym: compulsory - required by law or rule; not optional
  • synonym: libertarianism - a political philosophy advocating minimal state intervention in the lives of citizens
  • synonym: voluntarism - the principle of relying on voluntary action
  • synonym: volunteerism - the use or involvement of volunteers, especially in community services
