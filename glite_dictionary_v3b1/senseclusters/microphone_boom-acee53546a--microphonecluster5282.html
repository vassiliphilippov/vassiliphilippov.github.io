microphone boom

noun
pole holding overhead microphone on set
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkrəˌfoʊn buːm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəfəʊn buːm / (BrE)
a pole used to hold a microphone above a film or television set so it remains out of the camera's view

Examples

A microphone boom pole
A technician had to duck every time the microphone boom swept over the set.
The sound engineer quickly adjusted the microphone boom to capture the actor's voice clearly.
They rented a lightweight microphone boom for the documentary's outdoor scenes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boom - a long pole used to support equipment, such as a microphone or camera
