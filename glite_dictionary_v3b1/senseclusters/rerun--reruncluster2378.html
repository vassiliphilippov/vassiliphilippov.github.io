rerun

verb
to broadcast again (TV, film)
2.3 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀàr ån / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàr ån / (BrE)
to broadcast, show, or perform something (such as a TV program, film, or play) again after its original presentation

Examples

To rerun a TV show
Many channels rerun holiday movies every December.
The network decided to rerun the popular sitcom over the summer.
They reran the documentary last night due to high demand.
After its initial success, the theater decided to rerun the play for two more weeks.
The popular musical will rerun in the summer due to high demand.
They reran last year's festival performance for audiences who missed it.

Synonyms

  • rebroadcast
    4.0 0.22/m
    rebroadcast means sending a program again over the air or network, while rerun refers to showing that program after its first broadcast.
  • reshow
    3.4 0.01/m
    reshow means showing a film, program, or object again for viewers, while rerun focuses on repeating a prior broadcast performance.
  • rescreen
    4.2 0.00/m
    rescreen means to show a film or program again, often in cinemas, while rerun means broadcasting it again on TV.
  • repeat
    1.0 104/m
    repeat means to do or perform something again in many situations, while rerun specifically means showing a program after its first presentation.
  • replay
    2.1 1.76/m
    replay means to play a recording or event again for instant viewing, while rerun means broadcasting the program again later.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebroadcast - to broadcast again
  • synonym: repeat - to do or perform again
  • synonym: replay - to play again, especially in relation to broadcasts
