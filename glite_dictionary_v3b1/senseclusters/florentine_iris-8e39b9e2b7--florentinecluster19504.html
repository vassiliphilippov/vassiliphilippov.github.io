Florentine iris

noun
species of iris with white-lavender flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈflɔrəntiːn ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈflɒrəntiːn ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
A species of iris (Iris florentina) with large white flowers, lavender-tinged falls, and a fragrant rhizome, native to southern Europe.

Examples

The Florentine iris flower
Botanists found several Florentine irises blooming in the ancient gardens of Tuscany.
She planted a Florentine iris in her garden, attracted by its striking white and lavender flowers.
The Florentine iris is prized for its fragrant rhizomes, which are used in perfumes.
