Yalta Conference

noun
1945 WWII meeting of Allied leaders
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑltə ˈkɑnfərəns / (AmE), / ˈjæltə ˈkɒnfərəns / (BrE)
A conference held in Yalta in February 1945, where Roosevelt, Stalin, and Churchill planned the final stages of World War II and agreed on the territorial division of Europe.

Examples

Yalta Conference 1945 meeting
Many historians debate the decisions made during the Yalta Conference and their consequences.
The Yalta Conference brought together the leaders of the United States, the United Kingdom, and the Soviet Union.
The agreements reached at the Yalta Conference had a significant impact on post-war Europe.
