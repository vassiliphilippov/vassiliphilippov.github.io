underbake

verb
to do insufficiently
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈbeɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbeɪk / (BrE)
to prepare, plan, or develop something without enough time, detail, or thoroughness, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

An underbaked idea
The proposal was underbaked and lacked the necessary research to convince the investors.
If you underbake your ideas, the final project may not meet expectations.
Critics argued that the new policy was underbaked and needed more careful planning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neglect - to fail to give proper attention or care
  • antonym: overbake - to prepare or develop something excessively or with too much detail
  • synonym: rush - to do something too quickly without enough care
