pericardiectomy

noun
removal of pericardium
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˌkɑːrdiˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˌkɑːdiˈɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove part or all of the pericardium, the membrane surrounding the heart

Examples

A pericardiectomy surgery
After years of chronic pericarditis, the doctor recommended a pericardiectomy to improve the patient's quality of life.
The surgeon performed a successful pericardiectomy to relieve the pressure on the heart.
Recovery from a pericardiectomy can take several weeks, depending on the patient's overall health.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cardiectomy - surgical removal of the heart
  • synonym: pericardiotomy - a surgical incision into the pericardium
