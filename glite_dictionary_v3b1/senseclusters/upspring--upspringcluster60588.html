upspring

verb
to arise suddenly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
to arise, appear, or come into being suddenly, especially of ideas, feelings, or situations; used in archaic or literary contexts

Examples

New ideas upspring
New hopes upspring in the hearts of the people after the announcement.
In the quiet of the night, strange thoughts would often upspring in her mind.
Doubts upsprang as soon as the unexpected news reached the village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emerge - to come into view or existence
  • antonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly
