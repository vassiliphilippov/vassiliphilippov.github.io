unbreathing

adjective
completely still
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbriːðɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈbriːðɪŋ / (BrE)
not breathing; completely still, silent, or lifeless (literally or figuratively)

Examples

The unbreathing forest
The forest was unbreathing in the early morning, not a leaf stirring in the air.
She stood unbreathing at the window, watching the snowfall in perfect silence.
For a moment, the entire audience was unbreathing, waiting for the verdict to be announced.
The unbreathing statue stood silently in the center of the garden.
Rescuers rushed to help the unbreathing victim pulled from the water.
In the eerie stillness, the forest seemed unbreathing and empty.
