peril

verb
to put in danger
8.8 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈper.əl /; / ˈpɛr əl / (AmE), / ˈper.ɪl /; / ˈpɛr əl / (BrE)
to put someone or something in danger or at risk of harm

Examples

To peril lives
Ignoring safety regulations may peril the workers in the factory.
The reckless plan could peril the lives of everyone involved.
They knew that crossing the border at night would peril their mission.
Careless handling of chemicals may peril the entire laboratory staff.
His risky investment decisions could peril the family's financial stability.
The company refused to peril its reputation by cutting safety corners.
