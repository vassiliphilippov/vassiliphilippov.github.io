copyhold

noun
historical English land tenure system
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêpiÀåho äld / (AmE), / Ààk…ípih…ô äld / (BrE)
a former form of land tenure in England, under which land was held from the lord of the manor by a tenant according to the custom of the manor and was evidenced by a copy of the manor court roll

Examples

A copyhold estate
In medieval England, a copyhold provided security for tenants who farmed the land.
The system of copyhold tenure was finally abolished by law in the 20th century.
Under copyhold, peasants received land in exchange for services to the lord of the manor.
