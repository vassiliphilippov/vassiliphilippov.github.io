save

verb
deliver from sin (religious)
Pronunciation: / seɪv / (AmE), / seɪv / (BrE)
(in religion) to deliver someone from sin or its consequences; to grant salvation.

Examples

To save a soul
Many people pray, hoping that their souls will be saved.
She believed that faith in God would save her from eternal damnation.
The preacher spoke of how anyone could be saved by accepting forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redeem - to save someone from sin, error, or evil
