absolute

adjective
not capable of being violated or infringed
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæb.sə.luːt/ (AmE), /ˈæb.sə.luːt/ /; / /ˈæb.səl.juːt/ (BrE)
not limited, restricted, or qualified in any way; unconditional or having total control

Examples

Absolute power
An absolute ban on discrimination is necessary to ensure equality.
Some argue that human dignity is an absolute right and should never be compromised.
The constitution guarantees certain absolute freedoms for all citizens.
He demanded an absolute promise before agreeing to the deal.
The court issued an absolute order that could not be appealed.
They gave me an absolute assurance that the work would be finished on time.
In an absolute dictatorship, the leader's power is unchecked.
The country was ruled by an absolute monarch who faced no legal constraints.
The king claimed absolute authority over his subjects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autocratic - ruling with absolute power or authority
  • antonym: conditional - subject to conditions or requirements; not absolute
  • synonym: inviolable - never to be broken, infringed, or dishonored
  • antonym: limited - (of a monarch or government) subject to restrictions by law or constitution
