Alnus viridis crispa

noun
North American subspecies of green alder shrub
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈælnəs vəˈraɪdɪs ˈkrɪspə / (AmE), / ˈælnəs vɪˈrɪdɪs ˈkrɪspə / (BrE)
A North American subspecies of green alder, a shrub with light green leaves and winged nuts.

Examples

Alnus viridis crispa shrub
Alnus viridis crispa is commonly found along stream banks in northeastern Canada.
Many birds and animals feed on the winged nuts of Alnus viridis crispa during the autumn months.
The leaves of Alnus viridis crispa are often used by botanists to distinguish it from other alders.
