Waldorf

proper noun
creative education method
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔldɔrf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːldɔːf / (BrE)
a method of education that emphasizes creativity, imagination, and hands-on learning, often in schools following Rudolf Steiner's philosophy

Examples

Waldorf school education
Many parents choose Waldorf schools for their focus on the arts and holistic development.
The Waldorf approach encourages children to learn through creative play and practical activities.
Teachers trained in the Waldorf method often use storytelling and art in their lessons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Montessori - an educational method focusing on independence and self-directed activity
