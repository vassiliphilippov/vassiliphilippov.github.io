Caucasian

noun
person of European or light-skinned descent
Pronunciation: / kɔˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / kɔˈkeɪziən / (BrE)
a person belonging to a race traditionally identified as having light or pale skin, originally from Europe, North Africa, or parts of Western Asia

Examples

A Caucasian person
She is a Caucasian who grew up in Eastern Europe.
The film featured a diverse cast, including both Caucasian and non-Caucasian actors.
The survey showed that most participants identified as Caucasian.

Related Terms

  • synonym: White - a person with light skin associated with European ancestry
