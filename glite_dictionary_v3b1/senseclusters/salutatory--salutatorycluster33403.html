salutatory

noun
a welcoming speech at a ceremony
5.2
Pronunciation: / səˈluːtəˌtɔri / (AmE), / səˈljuːtətəri / (BrE)
an opening or welcoming statement or address, especially one given at a graduation ceremony

Examples

The salutatory address
Her salutatory welcomed faculty, students, and families to the graduation event.
The class president delivered the salutatory at the beginning of the commencement ceremony.
Traditionally, the student with the second-highest rank presents the salutatory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: valedictory - a farewell statement, especially at a graduation ceremony
