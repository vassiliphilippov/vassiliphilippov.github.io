negate

verb
make ineffective or contradict
Pronunciation: / nɪˈɡeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪt / (BrE)
to make something ineffective, invalid, or to deny its truth or existence

Examples

To negate the effect
His actions serve to negate his earlier promises of reform.
If one fact negates another, they cannot both be true at the same time.
The results of the experiment negate the theory proposed by the researchers.
He attempted to negate the allegations made against him during the interview.
Her recent actions did nothing to negate the perception that she was dishonest.
You cannot simply negate established facts because they are inconvenient.
If the new data is accurate, it could negate the need for further testing.
One strong counterexample was enough to negate his argument.
The scientist worked to negate the previous theory with new experimental evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: affirm - to state something positively and with certainty
  • synonym: disprove - to show that something is not true
  • synonym: invalidate - make an argument, statement, or theory unsound or erroneous
  • synonym: nullify - make legally null and void; invalidate
