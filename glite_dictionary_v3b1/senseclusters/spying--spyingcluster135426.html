spying

noun
act of secretly observing for information
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of secretly watching people or things to get information

Examples

Secret spying activity
He was caught spying on the military facility late at night.
Movies about spying often feature secret gadgets and elaborate disguises.
The government accused her of spying for a foreign agency.
Her spying was eventually discovered by the security staff.
The agency was accused of spying on foreign diplomats during the negotiations.
There are strict laws to prevent industrial spying between competing companies.
