cardioid

noun
heart-shaped mathematical curve
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑr.diˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.di.ɔɪd / (BrE)
a heart-shaped curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle that rolls around another circle of the same radius

Examples

A cardioid curve
In mathematics class, we studied the properties of the cardioid and its applications.
The cardioid can be created by tracing a point on a rolling circle with the same radius as the fixed circle.
The microphone has a cardioid pickup pattern, making it ideal for recording vocals.
