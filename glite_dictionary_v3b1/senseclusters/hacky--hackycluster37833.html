hacky

adjective
unoriginal or clichéd
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhæki / (AmE), / ˈhæki / (BrE)
trite, unoriginal, or full of clichés; lacking freshness or originality, especially in jokes, stories, or remarks

Examples

Hacky jokes
The comedian's routine was criticized for being too hacky and relying on old jokes.
Many viewers found the movie's plot twists hacky and predictable.
Writers are encouraged to avoid hacky dialogue that feels overused or stale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - new or different; not stale or clichéd
  • antonym: original - new and different from anything else
  • synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness
