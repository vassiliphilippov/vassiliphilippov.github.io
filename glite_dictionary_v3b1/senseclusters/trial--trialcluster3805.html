trial

noun
court examination of guilt or innocence
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl / (BrE)
a formal examination of evidence in a court of law to determine a person's guilt or innocence

Examples

A court trial
After several days in court, the trial finally concluded with a verdict.
Her lawyer advised her to prepare thoroughly before the trial began.
The defendant faced a lengthy trial that attracted national attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquittal - a legal judgment of not guilty
  • antonym: conviction - a legal judgment of guilty
  • synonym: hearing - a legal session for presenting evidence and arguments
