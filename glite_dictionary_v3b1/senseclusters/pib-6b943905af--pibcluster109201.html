PiB

noun
unit of digital information (2^50 bytes)
Pronunciation: / piː aɪ ˈbiː / (AmE), / piː aɪ ˈbiː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 tebibytes or 2^50 bytes

Examples

1 PiB of data
Modern data centers often manage databases that exceed 1 PiB in size.
Storing all that high-resolution video would require several PiB of disk space.
The backup server has a total storage capacity of 3 PiB.
