zest

verb
to remove citrus peel
4.1
Pronunciation: / zɛst / (AmE), / zɛst / (BrE)
to scrape off the outer colored skin of a citrus fruit (like a lemon or orange) to use for flavoring food or drinks

Examples

To zest a lemon
She carefully zested an orange to add a fresh flavor to the salad.
The recipe says to zest a lemon before squeezing the juice.
He zests limes for the pie filling to give it a tangy taste.
She was zesting a lemon to add flavor to the cake.
He carefully zested the orange, making sure not to include the bitter white part.
Before serving, the chef likes to zest a lime over the dish for extra aroma.

  • synonym: peel - to remove the outer skin of a fruit or vegetable
