sleepily

adverb
in a tired or drowsy manner
1.0 1.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsliÀêp…™li / (AmE), / ÀàsliÀêp…™li / (BrE)
In a manner that shows one is tired or ready for sleep.

Examples

To sleepily yawn
He yawned sleepily and stretched his arms above his head.
She answered the phone sleepily, barely awake from her nap.
The cat blinked sleepily as it settled into its bed.

Synonyms

  • drowsily
    2.4 0.27/m
    drowsily means slow and heavy in movement and speech, while sleepily means showing tiredness or readiness for sleep.
  • dozily
    6.2 0.01/m
    dozily describes being half asleep and unfocused, whereas sleepily simply shows tiredness and a wish to sleep.
  • somnolently
    8.1 0.01/m
    somnolently sounds formal and literary, often used in writing to show deep drowsiness, while sleepily is everyday.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alertly - in a manner that shows wakefulness and attentiveness
  • synonym: sleepy - feeling or showing a need for sleep
