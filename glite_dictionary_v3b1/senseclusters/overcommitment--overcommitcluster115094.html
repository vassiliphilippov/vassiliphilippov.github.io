overcommitment

noun
excessive obligation
5.0
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ (BrE)
the act or state of promising or taking on more work, tasks, or responsibilities than you can manage

Examples

Work overcommitment
The manager's overcommitment to multiple projects led to missed deadlines and stressed employees.
Financial overcommitment can put a company at risk of defaulting on its obligations.
Her overcommitment to volunteer work left her with little time for her family.
His constant overcommitment to work and social events left him feeling exhausted.
The manager warned the team about the dangers of overcommitment during busy periods.
She realized her overcommitment was affecting her health and decided to cut back on activities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: balance - a state where different elements are in the correct proportions
  • synonym: burnout - a state of physical or mental exhaustion caused by excessive work or stress
  • synonym: overextension - the act of stretching resources or commitments beyond safe limits
  • synonym: overload - a situation where someone has too much to do or handle
  • antonym: prudence - careful management of resources and commitments
