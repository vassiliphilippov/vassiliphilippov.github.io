iniquitous

adjective
extremely immoral or wicked
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɪkwɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪkwɪtəs / (BrE)
Extremely immoral, wicked, or unfair.

Examples

An iniquitous act
Many believed the new tax to be iniquitous and deeply unfair.
The judge condemned the iniquitous system that allowed injustice to persist.
They protested against the iniquitous laws imposed by the regime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: righteous - Morally right or justifiable
  • synonym: wicked - Morally wrong or evil
