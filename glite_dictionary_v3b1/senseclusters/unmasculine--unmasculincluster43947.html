unmasculine

adjective
not masculine gender
7.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmæskjəlɪn / (AmE), / ʌnˈmæskjʊlɪn / (BrE)
not masculine in grammatical gender; belonging to a gender category other than masculine, such as feminine or neuter

Examples

Unmasculine noun
In some languages, the word for 'table' is considered unmasculine and takes different endings.
The adjective must agree with the unmasculine noun in both gender and number.
Students learned to identify which nouns are unmasculine during their grammar lessons.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: feminine - having grammatical gender typically associated with female
  • antonym: masculine - having grammatical gender typically associated with male
  • degree higher: neuter - having grammatical gender that is neither masculine nor feminine
