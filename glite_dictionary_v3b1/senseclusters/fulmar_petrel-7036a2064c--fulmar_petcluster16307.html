fulmar petrel

noun
polar region seabird
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlmɑr ˈpɛtrəl / (AmE), / ˈfʊlmɑː ˈpɛtrəl / (BrE)
a large, heavy, short-tailed seabird found in polar regions, known for gliding over oceans and closely related to albatrosses and petrels

Examples

A fulmar petrel bird
A fulmar petrel can often be seen gliding low over the waves with stiff wings.
The fulmar petrel is well adapted to life over the open seas of the Arctic and Antarctic.
Unlike many other seabirds, the fulmar petrel rarely ventures far from its cold oceanic habitat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fulmar - a seabird of the family Procellariidae resembling a gull
  • synonym: petrel - a small or medium-sized seabird in the order Procellariiformes
