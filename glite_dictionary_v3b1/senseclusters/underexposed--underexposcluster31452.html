underexposed

adjective
too dark (photo)
6.3
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ɪk.ˈspoʊzd/ /; / /ən.dər.ɪk.ˈspoʊzd/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.rɪk.ˈspəʊzd/ /; / /ən.də.rɪk.ˈspəʊzd/ (BrE)
too dark because not enough light reached the camera sensor or film during exposure

Examples

Underexposed photo
The entire roll of film was underexposed, making the images difficult to see.
If your photo looks underexposed, try increasing the exposure time next time.
Many beginners accidentally take underexposed pictures when shooting indoors without a flash.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dark - having little or no light
  • antonym: overexposed - too bright because too much light reached the camera sensor or film
