chromesthesia

noun
a type of synesthesia involving color sensations
7.2 9.9
Pronunciation: /kroʊ.mes.ˈθiː.ə/ (AmE), /krəʊ.mes.ˈθiː.ə/ (BrE)
a form of synesthesia in which sounds or other nonvisual stimuli evoke the perception of colors

Examples

Chromesthesia in music
For some people with chromesthesia, every musical note appears as a different color.
Scientists have developed tests to measure the consistency of color associations in chromesthesia.
The artist described how her chromesthesia influences her choice of colors when painting.
Her chromaesthesia made conversations a uniquely colorful experience, as every voice produced a different hue.
Research into chromaesthesia helps scientists understand the connections between sensory pathways in the brain.
Some musicians with chromaesthesia see vivid colors whenever they hear certain notes or chords.
