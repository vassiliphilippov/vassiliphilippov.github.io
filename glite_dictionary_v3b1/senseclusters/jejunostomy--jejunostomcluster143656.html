jejunostomy

noun
surgical connection of the jejunum to the abdominal wall
9.9
Pronunciation: /dʒə.dʒuː.ˈnɑːs.tə.miː/ /; / /dʒə.dʒuː.ˈnɔːs.tə.miː/ (AmE), /dʒe.dʒuː.ˈnɒs.tə.miː/ /; / /dʒə.dʒuː.ˈnɒs.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure to create an opening from the jejunum to the abdominal wall, typically to provide a route for artificial feeding

Examples

A jejunostomy surgery
A surgeon may recommend a jejunostomy if the stomach or upper intestine cannot be used for feeding.
After her operation, a jejunostomy was used to deliver medication directly to her intestine.
The patient required a jejunostomy for long-term nutritional support.
