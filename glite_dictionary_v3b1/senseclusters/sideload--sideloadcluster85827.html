sideload

verb
to load from the side
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.loʊd/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.ləʊd/ (BrE)
to load or unload cargo or goods onto or from a vehicle or ship from the side rather than from the top or rear

Examples

To sideload cargo
The workers used a special ramp to sideload the heavy machinery onto the truck.
It is safer to sideload fragile items to avoid stacking them on top of each other.
The crew had to sideload the supplies because the rear entrance was blocked.

Related Terms

  • synonym: load - to put goods or cargo onto a vehicle or ship
  • synonym: unload - to remove goods or cargo from a vehicle or ship
