whizz along

verb
move along very quickly
Pronunciation: / wɪz əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / wɪz əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to move along very quickly, making a light rushing sound or giving a sense of speed

Examples

To whizz along quickly
Cyclists whizz along the river path during the morning rush hour.
The sports car whizzed along the empty highway at dawn.
We watched the train whizzing along the tracks in the distance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crawl - to move very slowly
  • synonym: speed - to move quickly
  • synonym: zoom - to move quickly with a humming or buzzing sound
