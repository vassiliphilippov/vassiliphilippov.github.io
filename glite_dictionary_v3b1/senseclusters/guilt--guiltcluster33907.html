guilt

verb
to make feel guilty
Pronunciation: / ɡɪlt / (AmE), / ɡɪlt / (BrE)
to make someone feel responsible or ashamed for something, often to persuade them to act

Examples

To guilt a friend
She guilted him into staying late at work by reminding him of his promises.
He felt he was being guilted into donating more money than he could afford.
They guilt their children into visiting more often by talking about how lonely they are.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give someone support or confidence
  • synonym: shame - to make someone feel ashamed
