systemic

adjective
affecting an entire system
3.4
Pronunciation: / sɪˈstɛmɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈstɛmɪk / (BrE)
affecting or relating to the whole of a system, especially the body rather than a particular part or organ

Examples

Systemic effects
Exposure to the chemical led to systemic symptoms rather than just local irritation.
The financial crisis revealed deep systemic issues in the economy.
The new medication can cause systemic side effects throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: localized - confined to a particular part or area
