great

adjective
large in size, number, or extent
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt / (BrE)
very large in size, number, or extent; larger than others of the same kind

Examples

Great success
After a great delay, the concert finally began.
The great oak tree towered over all the others in the forest.
The museum houses a great collection of ancient artifacts.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: huge - extremely large in size
  • synonym: large - of considerable or relatively great size
  • antonym: small - of a size less than normal or usual
