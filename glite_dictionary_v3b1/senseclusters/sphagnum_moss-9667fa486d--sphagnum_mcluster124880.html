sphagnum moss

noun
a type of peat-forming moss
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsfæɡnəm mɑs / (AmE), / ˈsfæɡnəm mɒs / (BrE)
a pale or ashy moss of the genus Sphagnum, whose decomposed remains form peat and is often found in wetlands

Examples

Sphagnum moss in wetlands
Gardeners often use sphagnum moss to retain moisture in soil mixes.
Peat bogs are largely made up of decomposed sphagnum moss over thousands of years.
The wet, spongy ground was covered in a thick layer of sphagnum moss.
