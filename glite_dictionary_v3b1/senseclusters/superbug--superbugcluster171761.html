superbug

noun
antibiotic-resistant bacterium
3.4 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.pər.bʌg/ (AmE), /ˈsuː.pə.bʌg/ (BrE)
a strain of bacteria that has developed resistance to most or all available antibiotics, making it difficult to treat with conventional drugs

Examples

A superbug strain
Doctors warned that overuse of antibiotics can lead to the rise of dangerous superbugs.
Hospitals are increasingly concerned about the spread of superbugs among patients.
Researchers are developing new treatments to fight superbugs that no longer respond to existing drugs.
