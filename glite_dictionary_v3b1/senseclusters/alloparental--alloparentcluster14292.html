alloparental

adjective
non-parental caregiving
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæloʊpəˈrɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌaləʊpəˈrɛntəl /; / ˌæləʊpəˈrɛntəl / (BrE)
describing care or behavior toward young provided by individuals other than the biological parents

Examples

Alloparental care behavior
Some primates display alloparental care, with older siblings helping to look after the young.
Researchers observed alloparental behavior in meerkats, where non-parents assist in feeding pups.
In certain bird species, alloparental support increases the survival rate of chicks.
In some animal groups, alloparental care increases the survival rate of the young.
Researchers observed alloparental behavior among older siblings helping to feed the infants.
The concept of alloparental support is important in understanding social structures in primates.
