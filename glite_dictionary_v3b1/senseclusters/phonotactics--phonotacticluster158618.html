phonotactics

noun
rules for sound combinations
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊnəˈtæktɪks / (AmE), / ˌfəʊnəˈtæktɪks / (BrE)
the rules in a language about which sound combinations are allowed and how sounds can be arranged

Examples

English phonotactics rules
English phonotactics do not allow words to begin with the sound combination 'ng'.
She learned about phonotactics to understand why some invented words sound unnatural.
Different languages have unique phonotactics that affect how foreign words are adapted.
The phonotactics of Japanese do not allow certain consonant clusters found in English.
Linguists study phonotactics to understand why some words are easier to pronounce than others.
Children learn the phonotactics of their native language as they acquire speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phonology - the study of the sound system of languages
  • antonym: syntax - the set of rules for arranging words in sentences
