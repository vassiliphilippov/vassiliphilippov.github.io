schismatic

noun
person causing division
Pronunciation: / skɪzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / skɪzˈmætɪk / (BrE)
a person who causes or is involved in division or separation within an organization, especially a religious group

Examples

A schismatic leader
The church expelled the schismatic after he led a group to form a new congregation.
During the heated debate, one member was labeled a schismatic for challenging the group's traditions.
History books often mention the schismatics who split from the main religious order.
The church leaders labeled him a schismatic for refusing to accept their authority.
During the council, several schismatics argued for a complete break from tradition.
History books often discuss the impact of schismatics on religious movements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformist - a person who follows accepted rules or customs
  • synonym: dissenter - a person who disagrees with the majority or official opinion
  • synonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to the official doctrine of a religion
  • antonym: unifier - a person who brings people together
