portacaval shunt

noun
surgical connection between portal vein and vena cava
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɔrtəˈkeɪvəl ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtəkəˈvɑːl ʃʌnt / (BrE)
a surgical passage created between the portal vein and the inferior vena cava to allow blood from the abdominal organs to bypass the liver

Examples

A portacaval shunt surgery
A portacaval shunt is sometimes used when liver transplantation is not an option.
Complications can arise after a portacaval shunt, so patients require careful monitoring.
The surgeon performed a portacaval shunt to alleviate the patient's portal hypertension.
