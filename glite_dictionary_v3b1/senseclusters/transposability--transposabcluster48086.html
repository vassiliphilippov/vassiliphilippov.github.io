transposability

noun
ability to change order or arrangement
6.4
Pronunciation: / trænˌspoʊzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / trænˌspəʊzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or capability of being able to change the order or arrangement of parts or elements.

Examples

The transposability of parts
The transposability of data structures allows for greater flexibility in programming.
The transposability of genetic elements is a key factor in evolution.
This musical piece demonstrates the transposability of the main theme to different keys.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immutability - quality of being unchangeable
  • synonym: permutability - ability to be changed in order or arrangement
