take a breather

verb
take a short rest or break
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə ˈbriðər /; / teɪk ə ˈbriðɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈbriːðə / (BrE)
to take a short break from an activity to rest or recover energy

Examples

To take a breather now
After running for ten miles, I had to take a breather before continuing.
Let’s take a breather and grab some coffee before our next meeting.
You’ve been working for hours—why don’t you take a breather?
After hiking for an hour, we decided to take a breather at the top of the hill.
Once you finish that chapter, let's take a breather and grab some coffee.
She stepped outside to take a breather before returning to the noisy party.

  • synonym: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax, sleep, or recover strength
  • synonym: take a break - pause activity to rest
