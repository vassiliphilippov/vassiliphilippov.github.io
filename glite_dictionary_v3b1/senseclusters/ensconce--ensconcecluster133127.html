ensconce

verb
settle securely; establish firmly
9.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈskɑːns/ /; / /en.ˈskɑːns/ (AmE), /en.ˈskɒns/ /; / /ɪn.ˈskɒns/ (BrE)
to settle or place someone or something comfortably, securely, or safely in a position

Examples

To ensconce comfortably
After a long day, she ensconced herself in her favorite armchair with a good book.
He ensconced the valuable documents in a hidden safe behind the painting.
The cat ensconced itself under the warm blanket to avoid the cold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: install - to place or fix in position for use
  • antonym: unsettle - to move from a settled or secure position
