reconvergence

noun
restoring network routes
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈvɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the process by which a network restores consistent and stable routes after a change or failure in topology

Examples

Network reconvergence
After the outage, rapid reconvergence ensured that data could flow through the network again.
Network engineers monitor reconvergence times to minimize disruptions during maintenance.
Efficient reconvergence is critical for maintaining reliable communication in large computer networks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convergence - the process of reaching a stable state in a network
