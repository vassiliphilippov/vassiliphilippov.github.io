Raffia taedigera

noun
a tall Brazilian palm with large leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈræfiə taɪˈdɛdʒərə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə tɪˈdiːdʒərə / (BrE)
A tall palm tree native to Brazil with large, leathery, feather-like leaves, whose strong stems are used for structural purposes.

Examples

Tall Raffia taedigera palm
Local builders sometimes use the strong stems of Raffia taedigera for constructing frameworks.
Researchers studied the habitat of Raffia taedigera along the riverbanks in Brazil.
The leaves of the Raffia taedigera are often used to make baskets and mats.
