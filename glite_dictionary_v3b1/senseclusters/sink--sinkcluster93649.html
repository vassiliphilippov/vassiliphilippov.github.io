sink

verb
to cause to go under water
4.5
Pronunciation: / sɪŋk / (AmE), / sɪŋk / (BrE)
to cause something, especially a ship, to go below the surface of water and become submerged; to destroy by causing to submerge

Examples

To sink a ship
Rising water threatened to sink the entire village if the dam failed.
The submarine managed to sink several enemy ships during the war.
They tried to sink the old boat as part of an artificial reef project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: float - to rest or move on the surface of a liquid without sinking
