hunt

verb
pursue relentlessly
Pronunciation: / hʌnt / (AmE), / hʌnt / (BrE)
to pursue or chase someone or something relentlessly, often in order to capture or catch

Examples

To hunt a celebrity
Journalists hunted the celebrity for days, hoping for an exclusive interview.
She vowed to hunt down the truth no matter how long it took.
The detectives continued to hunt the fugitive across several states.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • synonym: pursue - to follow or chase in order to catch or attack
  • synonym: track - to follow the trail or movements of someone or something
