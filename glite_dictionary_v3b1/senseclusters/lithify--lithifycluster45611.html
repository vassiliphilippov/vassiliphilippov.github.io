lithify

verb
to become stone
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪθəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪθɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to turn something into stone or solid rock, especially through natural geological processes

Examples

To lithify into rock
Over millions of years, sediment can lithify and form solid rock layers.
The process of minerals filling spaces in wood can eventually lithify the entire tree trunk.
Scientists study how volcanic ash deposits lithify into new types of rock.
Over millions of years, mud can lithify into shale under pressure.
Geologists study how sediments lithify to better understand the formation of rocks.
The process by which volcanic ash lithifies is important in the creation of tuff.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erode - to wear away or break down by natural forces
  • synonym: petrify - to change organic matter into stone
  • synonym: solidify - to make or become hard or solid
