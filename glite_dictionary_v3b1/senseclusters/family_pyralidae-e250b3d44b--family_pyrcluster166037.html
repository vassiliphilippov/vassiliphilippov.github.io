family Pyralidae

noun
a family of moths including bee and flour moths
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli paɪˈrælɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli paɪˈrælɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of moths that includes several species of bee moths, corn borers, and flour moths, many of which are agricultural pests.

Examples

Family Pyralidae moths
Many pests affecting flour mills belong to the family Pyralidae.
Researchers recently discovered a new species within the family Pyralidae in South America.
The family Pyralidae contains several species known for damaging stored grains and crops.
