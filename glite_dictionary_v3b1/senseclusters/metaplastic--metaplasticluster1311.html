metaplastic

adjective
abnormally changed tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈplæstɪk / (BrE)
describing tissue that has changed from one type to another, especially in an abnormal or disease-related way

Examples

Metaplastic tissue change
The pathologist noted several metaplastic areas in the tissue sample.
Doctors are concerned when they observe metaplastic changes in the lining of the esophagus.
A metaplastic process can sometimes be reversed if the underlying cause is treated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dysplastic - showing abnormal growth or development of cells or tissue
  • antonym: normal - not showing abnormal or disease-related changes
