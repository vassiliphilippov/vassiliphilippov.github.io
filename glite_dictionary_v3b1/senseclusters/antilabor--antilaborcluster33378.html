antilabor

adjective
opposed to unions
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈleɪbər / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈleɪbə / (BrE)
opposed to labor unions, workers' rights, or policies that support workers

Examples

Antilabor laws
The company adopted several antilabor policies that made it harder for workers to organize.
Union members protested against the antilabor legislation passed by the government.
Critics argue that the reforms are antilabor and undermine workers' rights.
The new law was criticized as antilabor by several workers' organizations.
His antilabor stance made him unpopular among union members.
Many rural voters remained antilabor and campaigned for the conservative candidate in the election.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiunion - opposed to labor unions
  • antonym: prolabor - supporting labor unions or workers' interests
