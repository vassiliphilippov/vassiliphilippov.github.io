pack

verb
appoint supporters to influence outcome
5.8
Pronunciation: / pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
to fill (a committee, jury, or other body) with people who support a particular person or cause, especially to influence the outcome in one's favor

Examples

To pack a jury
He attempted to pack the board of directors so his proposals would pass easily.
Some critics claim the government is trying to pack the committee with its allies.
The defense lawyer argued that the prosecution had packed the jury to ensure a conviction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stack - to arrange (a group, outcome, etc.) unfairly in one's favor
