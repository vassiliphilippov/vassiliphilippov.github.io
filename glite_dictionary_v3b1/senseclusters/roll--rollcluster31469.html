roll

noun
deep, prolonged sound (thunder, bells)
Pronunciation: / roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)
a deep, continuous, or rapid sound, especially from thunder or drums

Examples

A roll of thunder
A sudden roll of drums signaled the start of the ceremony.
The roll of the church bells echoed through the town.
We heard the distant roll of thunder approaching across the valley.
His fingers blurred as he executed a perfect roll on the snare drum.
She signaled the start of the show with a dramatic drum roll.
The snare drummer played a steady roll as the parade marched by.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drumroll - a short, continuous drumbeat used to build suspense
  • synonym: rumble - a continuous deep, resonant sound
