Catch-22

noun
a no-win, contradictory dilemma
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkætʃ twɛnˈti tu / (AmE), / ˌkætʃ twɛnˈti tjuː / (BrE)
a difficult situation in which there is no escape because of mutually conflicting or dependent conditions; a dilemma with no possible solution

Examples

A Catch-22 situation
Applying for a loan without credit history is a Catch-22 situation.
Quitting his job without another offer would be a Catch-22 for him.
She faced a Catch-22 trying to get experience when all jobs required prior experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dilemma - a situation requiring a difficult choice between equally undesirable alternatives
