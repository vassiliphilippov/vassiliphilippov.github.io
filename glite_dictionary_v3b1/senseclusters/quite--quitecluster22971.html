quite

adverb
to a significant or extreme degree
Pronunciation: / kwaɪt / (AmE), / kwaɪt / (BrE)
to a significant, noticeable, or fairly high degree; really, rather, or exceptionally

Examples

Quite good
Her decision took me quite by surprise; I never thought she would leave so suddenly.
That was quite a performance—everyone in the audience was blown away.
The results were quite surprising, exceeding even our most optimistic expectations.
She gave quite a performance at the concert last night.
That was quite an adventure you had on your trip to Peru.
The view from the mountaintop was quite something to behold.
It's quite late, so we should probably head home soon.
She recovered quite quickly after her illness.
The soup was quite tasty, with a subtle blend of spices.
