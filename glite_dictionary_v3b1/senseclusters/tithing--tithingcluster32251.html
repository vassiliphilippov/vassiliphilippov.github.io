tithing

noun
giving portion of income
9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtaɪ.ðɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtaɪ.ðɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of giving a fixed portion (often a tenth) of one's income or produce, especially for religious or charitable purposes

Examples

Religious tithing practice
Many families practice tithing as part of their religious beliefs.
The church encourages tithing to support its community programs and outreach.
Some people set aside a percentage of their salary for tithing each month.
Many members of the church practice tithing as part of their faith.
The community relied on tithing to fund repairs for the old chapel.
Farmers in the region have a long tradition of tithing a portion of their crops each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charity - the voluntary giving of help, typically in the form of money
  • synonym: donation - something given to a person or organization, often for charity
  • synonym: tithe - a tenth part of income given as a contribution or tax
  • antonym: withholding - the act of keeping back or refusing to give something
