gray-brown

adjective
brown mixed with grey
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈbraʊn /; / ˌɡreɪˈbraʊn / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈbraʊn /; / ˌɡreɪˈbraʊn / (BrE)
Of a brown color mixed with or tinged by grey.

Examples

A gray-brown color
A gray-brown bird perched quietly on the stone wall, blending into its surroundings.
She wore a gray-brown woolen scarf during the chilly winter morning.
The old leather chair had a gray-brown hue that showed its age.
She chose a grey-brown paint for the kitchen walls to create a warm, neutral atmosphere.
The grey-brown coat of the rabbit helped it blend into the forest floor.
The grey-brown feathers of the sparrow make it difficult to spot among the tree branches.
