postsynaptic

adjective
receiving nerve signal
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstsaɪˈnæptɪk / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstsaɪˈnæptɪk / (BrE)
relating to the part of a nerve cell that receives a signal across a synapse

Examples

Postsynaptic membrane
The postsynaptic membrane contains receptors that bind to neurotransmitters released by the presynaptic neuron.
Changes in postsynaptic response can affect how signals are transmitted in the nervous system.
Certain drugs can alter postsynaptic activity, influencing communication between nerve cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: presynaptic - relating to the part of a nerve cell that sends a signal across a synapse
