patination

noun
surface layer formation
Pronunciation: / ˌpætəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpætɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming a thin, often colored layer on the surface of metal, usually for protection or decoration

Examples

Chemical patination
The artist used chemicals to speed up the patination of the copper sculpture.
Natural patination can give old coins a unique and valuable appearance.
Over time, exposure to air and moisture led to the patination of the bronze doors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrosion - the gradual destruction of materials by chemical reaction
  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction where a material combines with oxygen
