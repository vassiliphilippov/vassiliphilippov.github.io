collateral damage

noun
unintentional civilian harm in military operations
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl ˈdæm.ɪdʒ /; / kəˈlæt̬.ə.rəl ˈdæm.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əl ˈdæm.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
unintended harm, injury, or destruction caused to people or things that are not the main target, especially during a military action

Examples

Minimize collateral damage
Efforts to minimize collateral damage are a major concern in modern warfare.
Government officials expressed regret over the collateral damage caused during the military operation.
The airstrike resulted in significant collateral damage, despite efforts to avoid civilian areas.
Civilian casualties are often referred to as collateral damage in official reports.
Environmental concerns center on the collateral damage of industrial waste to nearby ecosystems.
The company restructured to cut costs, but many long-time employees became collateral damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: civilian casualties - deaths or injuries of non-combatants in war
  • synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs value, usefulness, or normal function
  • degree higher: destruction - action of causing so much damage that something no longer exists or cannot be repaired
