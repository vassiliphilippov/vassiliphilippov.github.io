seemingly

adverb
appearing to be true; by appearances only
3.7 27/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêm…™≈ãli / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêm…™≈ãli / (BrE)
appearing to be true based on what is seen or known, though not necessarily so; as far as one can tell from appearances alone

Examples

She was seemingly calm
She was seemingly calm during the interview, though her hands were shaking.
The house is seemingly abandoned, although lights sometimes appear in the windows.
The project is seemingly simple, but there are many hidden challenges.

Synonyms

  • apparently
    2.8 99/m
    apparently is used when people report or notice something as true, while seemingly focuses on appearance without proof.
  • on the face of it
    5.5 0.86/m
    on the face of it describes a quick view of facts, while seemingly describes a general impression from appearances alone.
  • ostensibly
    7.5 5.84/m
    ostensibly says how something is claimed or presented, while seemingly just notes how things look without confirming truth.
