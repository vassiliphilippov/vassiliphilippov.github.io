fusion

noun
the act of joining or merging
4.1 4.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfjuÀê í…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàfjuÀê í…ôn /; / Ààfj ä í…ôn /; / ÀàfjuÀê í(…ô)n / (BrE)
the process or result of joining or combining two or more things into one (such as in music, cuisine, or general use)

Examples

Fusion cuisine
Our new menu is a fusion of Asian and Western cuisines.
The artist's work is a fusion of classical and modern styles.
The team's success was due to a fusion of talent and hard work.
Scientists studied the fusion of two atomic nuclei in the experiment.
The festival celebrated cultural fusion through art, food, and dance.
Their music is a fusion of jazz and rock, creating a unique sound.
Her music is a fusion of jazz and classical styles.
Scientists observed the fusion of nuclei inside the laboratory reactor.
The fusion of two metals requires extremely high temperatures.

Synonyms

  • blend
    2.3 20/m
    a blend brings flavors or styles smoothly together, while fusion joins elements into a single new form across music, food, or culture.
  • mix
    1.0 24/m
    a mix means putting different things together in no set order, while fusion makes them join into one new combined form.
  • combination
    2.3 53/m
    a combination is joining items into a set or result, while fusion suggests a deeper merging into a single new entity.
  • mixture
    1.2 12/m
    a mixture keeps different parts together in one mass, while fusion usually makes those parts form a single new whole.
  • amalgamation
    7.5 0.75/m
    an amalgamation is a formal joining often used for companies, while fusion is a broader joining in art, food, or style.
  • coalescence
    7.8 0.28/m
    coalescence is parts coming together slowly to form one whole, while fusion often means a more active, creative joining of styles or things.
  • unification
    4.3 4.51/m
    unification is bringing groups or states into one political unit, while fusion is a broader idea of joining people, styles, or things.
