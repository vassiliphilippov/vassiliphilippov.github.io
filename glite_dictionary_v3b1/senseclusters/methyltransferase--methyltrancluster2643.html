methyltransferase

noun
enzyme transferring methyl group
9.1
Pronunciation: /meθ.əl.træns.fə.ˈreɪs/ (AmE), /meθ.əl.træns.fə.ˈreɪs/ (BrE)
an enzyme that transfers a methyl group from one molecule to another, often affecting biological processes

Examples

A methyltransferase enzyme
A methyltransferase can regulate gene expression by adding methyl groups to DNA.
Scientists discovered a new methyltransferase involved in plant growth.
The activity of the methyltransferase enzyme is crucial for normal cell function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that speeds up chemical reactions in living things
