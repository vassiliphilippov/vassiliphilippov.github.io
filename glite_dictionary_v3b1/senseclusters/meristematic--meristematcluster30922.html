meristematic

adjective
plant growth tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛrɪstəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛrɪstəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to plant tissue where active growth and cell division take place

Examples

Meristematic plant tissue
The tips of roots and shoots contain meristematic tissue that allows the plant to grow taller and longer.
Scientists study meristematic cells to understand how plants repair themselves after injury.
Because of its meristematic regions, the tree can continue to produce new leaves each season.
