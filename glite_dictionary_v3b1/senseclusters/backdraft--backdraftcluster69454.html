backdraft

noun
fire explosion
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.dræft/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.drɑːft/ (BrE)
a sudden explosion that occurs when air enters a space filled with hot gases from a fire

Examples

Fire backdraft
Firefighters are trained to recognize the signs of a backdraft before entering a burning building.
A sudden backdraft can be deadly if proper precautions are not taken during firefighting operations.
The explosion was caused by a backdraft when fresh air rushed into the smoke-filled room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flashover - a rapid spread of fire caused by ignition of all combustible materials in a room
