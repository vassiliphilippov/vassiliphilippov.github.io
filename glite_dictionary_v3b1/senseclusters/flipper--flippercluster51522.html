flipper

noun
flat swimming limb of aquatic animals
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪpə / (BrE)
A broad, flat limb or appendage of an aquatic animal, such as a whale, seal, or turtle, adapted for swimming.

Examples

Seal flippers
A turtle propels itself through the water with strong flippers.
The dolphin injured its left flipper on a piece of sharp coral.
The seal used its flipper to wave at the crowd during the show.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fin - a thin, flat body part or attachment that helps fish and some animals to swim
