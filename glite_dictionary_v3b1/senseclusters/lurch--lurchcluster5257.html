lurch

verb
move or walk unsteadily
5.6 4.74/m
Pronunciation: / l…ùt É /; / l…ùÀêt É / (AmE), / l…úÀêt É / (BrE)
to move suddenly, unsteadily, or with abrupt movements, especially forward or sideways

Examples

To lurch forward
He began to lurch across the road after twisting his ankle.
She lurched out of bed, dizzy from the medication.
The old bus lurched forward when the driver braked suddenly.
After slipping on the ice, he lurched forward and caught himself just in time.
As the ship was hit by a wave, it suddenly lurched to one side.
The old bus lurched around the corner, making everyone grab their seats.
After the earthquake, the building lurched dangerously but remained standing.
He lurched to the side to avoid the cyclist coming towards him.
The bus lurched forward as the driver hit the brakes suddenly.

Synonyms

  • jerk
    2.0 15/m
    jerk is a sudden, sharp movement like a quick twitch, while lurch is a larger, unsteady sudden motion.
  • jolt
    3.7 1.70/m
    jolt is a sudden, rough shock that throws things, while lurch is an unsteady forward or sideways movement.
  • pitch
    5.1 4.07/m
    pitch is a sudden tilt, often in a vehicle, while lurch is a quick, unsteady move forward or to the side.
  • slew
    7.6 1.18/m
    slew is a sudden slide or turn, usually of a vehicle, while lurch is a quick unsteady movement.
  • careen
    7.7 1.89/m
    careen moves quickly in an uncontrolled way, often side to side, while lurch is a sudden unsteady shift.
  • reel
    4.6 1.67/m
    reel means to stagger or sway as if dizzy, while lurch is a sudden, sharp unsteady movement forward or sideways.
  • stagger
    4.0 6.84/m
    stagger is to walk unsteadily because of weakness or balance, while lurch is a sudden, abrupt unsteady movement.
