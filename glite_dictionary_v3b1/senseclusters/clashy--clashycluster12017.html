clashy

adjective
harshly dissonant
Pronunciation: / ˈklæʃi / (AmE), / ˈklæʃi / (BrE)
having a harsh, unpleasant, or mismatched quality when combined, especially in sound, appearance, style, color, or design

Examples

Clashy colors
The colors in her outfit were a bit too clashy for the formal event.
The two songs played together sounded oddly clashy and unpleasant.
Some people enjoy bold, clashy patterns in their home decor.
The bright red shoes looked a bit clashy with her green dress.
He avoided wearing clashy patterns to keep his outfit simple.
The living room felt clashy because of the mix of bold colors and styles.

  • synonym: discordant - harsh or jarring in sound
