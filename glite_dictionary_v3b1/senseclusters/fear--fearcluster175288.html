fear

verb
to politely introduce unpleasant news or regret
7.4 12/m

(formal, chiefly in expressions like 'I fear') to say or think that something unpleasant is likely or true, often as a polite way to introduce bad news.

Examples

I fear bad news
Fear I may have some bad news to share with you today.
I fear we have lost all hope of reaching a decision by tonight.
She said, 'I fear there is nothing more we can do.'

  • synonym: regret - to feel sorry or sad about something
