confide

verb
share a secret trustingly
Pronunciation: / kənˈfaɪd / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪd / (BrE)
to tell someone a secret or private matter, trusting them not to tell others

Examples

To confide a secret
After much hesitation, he finally confided his secret to his brother.
Many employees confide in their managers when they experience workplace difficulties.
She felt she could confide her worries to her best friend without fear of judgment.
I decided to confide in my best friend about my fears.
She would never confide such sensitive information to a stranger.
Sometimes it's a relief to confide your worries to someone you trust.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
  • synonym: disclose - make secret or new information known
  • synonym: entrust - to give someone responsibility for something important or valuable
  • synonym: reveal - to make known, especially something secret or hidden
