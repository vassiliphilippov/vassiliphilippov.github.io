secret

adjective
kept hidden; not openly disclosed
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː.krət /; / ˈsiːkrət / (AmE), / ˈsiː.krɪt /; / ˈsiːkrɪt / (BrE)
kept hidden or not meant to be known or seen by others; done or made in a way that is meant to be hidden

Examples

Secret plan
She kept her ambitions secret from her colleagues.
The two were married in a secret ceremony that their friends knew nothing about.
They held a secret meeting to discuss their plans.
He revealed a secret plan to his trusted colleagues only.
Only authorized personnel have access to the secret documents.
They held a secret meeting late at night to discuss the proposal.
The chef guards his secret recipe very carefully to keep it unique.
The organization uses a secret code to communicate confidential information.
They held a secret meeting to discuss the surprise party plans.
