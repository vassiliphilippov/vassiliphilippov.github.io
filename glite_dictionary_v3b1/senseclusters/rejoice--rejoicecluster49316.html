rejoice

verb
feel or express great joy
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɔɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪs / (BrE)
to feel or show great joy or delight; to be extremely happy

Examples

To rejoice loudly
Fans rejoiced when their team finally won the championship after years of disappointment.
She rejoiced at the news of her friend's recovery.
The community rejoiced together during the festival.
Many nations rejoiced in the end of the conflict.
The crowd began to rejoice as the team scored the winning goal.
We all rejoiced at the news of her recovery.
Many people rejoiced when peace was declared after years of conflict.
She could not help but rejoice at the good news.
The crowd rejoiced in the team's unexpected victory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lament - to express sadness or grief
  • antonym: mourn - to feel or express deep sorrow or regret
