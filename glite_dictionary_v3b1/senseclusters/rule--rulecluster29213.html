rule

noun
regulations for religious orders
Pronunciation: / ruːl / (AmE), / ruːl / (BrE)
an official instruction, law, principle, or standard that guides or controls behavior, action, or how something works (including in games, mathematics, language, etc.)

Examples

A rule in football
Each religious order often has its own distinct rule that members must obey.
The rule of St. Francis emphasizes poverty and humility among its followers.

Euclid's rule is still used as a foundation in geometry today.
The Pythagorean rule allows you to find the length of any side in a right triangle.
The teacher explained a simple rule for multiplying two negatives.
Newton's first rule states that an object will remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a force.
The conservation of energy is a fundamental rule in thermodynamics.
The right-hand rule is used to determine the direction of the magnetic field in physics.
