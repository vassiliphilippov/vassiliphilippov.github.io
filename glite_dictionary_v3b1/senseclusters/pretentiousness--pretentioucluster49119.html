pretentiousness

noun
quality of being pretentious
Pronunciation: / prɪˈtɛn(t)ʃəsnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛn(t)ʃəsnəs /; / prɪˈtɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of trying to impress others by pretending to be more important, cultured, or sophisticated than one really is

Examples

His pretentiousness shows
Despite his pretentiousness, he lacked true sophistication or depth.
Her pretentiousness was obvious in the way she talked about her so-called exclusive travels.
The novel's pretentiousness made it difficult for readers to connect with the characters.
Her pretentiousness was obvious at the dinner party, as she discussed only topics she thought others would find impressive.
People often mistake confidence for pretentiousness when someone shares their achievements.
The novel was criticized for its pretentiousness and overly complex language that added little substance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest; lack of pretentiousness
  • synonym: pretension - a claim or assertion; also the quality of being pretentious
