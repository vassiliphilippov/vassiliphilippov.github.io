pericardial vein

noun
vein draining the heart’s pericardium
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈkɑrdiəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkɑːdɪəl veɪn / (BrE)
any of several small veins that drain blood from the pericardium, the membrane surrounding the heart

Examples

Pericardial vein drains blood
Damage to a pericardial vein during surgery can cause complications.
The pericardial vein is important in draining blood from the tissues surrounding the heart.
The anatomy class examined the path of each pericardial vein closely.
