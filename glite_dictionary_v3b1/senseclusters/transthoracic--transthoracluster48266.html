transthoracic

adjective
across the chest
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænθɔːˈræsɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrænθɔːˈræsɪk / (BrE)
passing through or performed across the chest wall

Examples

A transthoracic approach
The surgeon used a transthoracic approach to access the patient's lungs.
A transthoracic biopsy was necessary to obtain a tissue sample from the chest.
Doctors often perform a transthoracic echocardiogram to evaluate heart function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intrathoracic - located or occurring within the chest cavity
