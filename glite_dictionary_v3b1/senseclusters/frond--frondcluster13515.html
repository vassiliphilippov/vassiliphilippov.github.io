frond

verb
to grow fronds
9.1 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / frÉ‘nd / (AmE), / frÉ’nd / (BrE)
to produce or develop fronds, especially referring to plants such as ferns or palms.

Examples

Ferns frond in spring
In early spring, the ferns in the garden frond quickly after the first rain.
The palm trees fronded beautifully, adding lush greenery to the landscape.
After a period of dormancy, the plant began to frond again, showing new growth.

Synonyms

  • foliate
    7.1 0.04/m
    foliate means producing general leaves on a plant, while frond means producing the divided leaf of ferns or palms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leaf - a flat, green part of a plant that grows from the stem or branch
  • antonym: wither - to dry up and shrink, especially referring to plant parts
