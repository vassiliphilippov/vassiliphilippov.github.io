East India Company

proper noun
historic British trading company
Pronunciation: / ist ˈɪndiə ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / iːst ˈɪndiə ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a British company established in 1600 to conduct trade with India and Southeast Asia, later exercising political and administrative control in parts of India until 1858

Examples

The East India Company trade
Many historical buildings in Kolkata were once owned by the East India Company.
The East India Company played a key role in the expansion of the British Empire.
The British government dissolved the East India Company following the Indian Rebellion of 1857.
