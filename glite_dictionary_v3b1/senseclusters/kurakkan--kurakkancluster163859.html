An East Indian cereal grass (Eleusine coracana), whose seeds produce a slightly bitter flour used as a staple food in South Asia.

Examples

Kurakkan flour
Kurakkan is often ground into flour for making traditional Sri Lankan flatbreads.
Due to its resilience, kurakkan is widely cultivated in drought-prone regions.
In many rural communities, kurakkan remains a vital source of nutrition.
