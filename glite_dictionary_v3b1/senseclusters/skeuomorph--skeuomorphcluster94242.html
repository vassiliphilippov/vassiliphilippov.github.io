skeuomorph

noun
decorative imitation
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈskjuː.ə.mɑːrf/ /; / /ˈskjuː.ə.mɔːrf/ (AmE), /ˈskjuː.ə.mɔːf/ (BrE)
a design feature or object that imitates the appearance or function of an earlier object or material, even though it is no longer needed

Examples

A digital skeuomorph
The digital notepad app uses a paper texture as a skeuomorph to make it feel familiar.
Leather stitching on plastic phone cases is a common skeuomorph in modern design.
The shutter sound on smartphone cameras is a skeuomorph from older film cameras.
The save icon on many computers is a skeuomorph based on the image of a floppy disk.
Designers often use a skeuomorph to make new technology feel more familiar to users.
The wooden texture on the plastic dashboard is a classic example of a skeuomorph in car design.
