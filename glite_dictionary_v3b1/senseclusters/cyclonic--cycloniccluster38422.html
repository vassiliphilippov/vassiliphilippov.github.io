cyclonic

adjective
relating to a cyclone or low-pressure system
Pronunciation: / saɪˈklɑnɪk / (AmE), / saɪˈklɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to a cyclone or the weather conditions typical of cyclones, such as rotating winds and strong storms

Examples

Cyclonic storm winds
Satellite images revealed a distinctive cyclonic swirl over the ocean.
The region often experiences cyclonic storms during the summer months.
The weather forecaster explained that the cyclonic conditions would bring heavy rain and strong winds to the area.
Hurricanes are one of the most dangerous cyclonic phenomena in the world.
Meteorologists warned of a possible cyclonic disturbance approaching the coastline.
The area suffered cyclonic winds that toppled trees and damaged houses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticyclonic - relating to the atmosphere around a high-pressure center, opposite of cyclonic
  • synonym: cyclone - a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure
