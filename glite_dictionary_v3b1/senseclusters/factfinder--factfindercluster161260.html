factfinder

noun
neutral dispute investigator
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfækt.faɪn.dər/ (AmE), /ˈfækt.faɪn.də/ (BrE)
a person or group who investigates, collects, or determines the facts in a case, dispute, or subject

Examples

The factfinder reviews
The company and the union agreed to let a factfinder review the case before making a final decision.
A factfinder was appointed to investigate the causes of the industrial dispute.
Both parties respected the factfinder's recommendations and moved forward with negotiations.
In a bench trial, the factfinder is the judge rather than a jury.
The factfinder must carefully consider all the evidence before reaching a decision.
Both parties presented their arguments to the factfinder during the hearing.
The court relied on the factfinder to determine what really happened during the incident.
As a skilled factfinder, she uncovered important details that others had missed.
Every good journalist must be an excellent factfinder to ensure accurate reporting.
