Plains Indian

noun
member of a Great Plains Native American tribe
Pronunciation: / pleɪnz ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / pleɪnz ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
a member of any of the indigenous peoples of the Great Plains of North America, traditionally living a nomadic life following the buffalo

Examples

A Plains Indian tepee
A Plains Indian tepee was specially designed to be easily taken down and moved.
Many Plains Indian tribes were skilled horse riders and hunters.
The traditional lifestyle of the Plains Indian revolved around following herds of buffalo.
