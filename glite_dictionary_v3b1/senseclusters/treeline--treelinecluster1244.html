treeline

noun
row of trees
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtriːˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtriːˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line or row of trees that forms a boundary or marks the edge of an area such as a field or property

Examples

A treeline border
A narrow treeline separated the farm from the busy highway.
Birds gathered along the treeline at the edge of the meadow.
The old house was hidden behind a thick treeline bordering the property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fence - a structure serving as a boundary or enclosure
  • synonym: hedgerow - a line of bushes or small trees forming a boundary
