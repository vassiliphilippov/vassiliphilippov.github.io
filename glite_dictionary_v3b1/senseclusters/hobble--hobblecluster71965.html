hobble

verb
impede; restrict progress
6.1 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑː.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈhɒb.əl/ (BrE)
to limit or restrict the action, progress, or effectiveness of someone or something

Examples

To hobble progress
Efforts to reform the system were hobbled by bureaucratic resistance.
Severe budget cuts can hobble a company's growth for years.
She refused to let her injury hobble her career ambitions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: facilitate - to make (an action or process) easy or easier
  • synonym: hamper - to make movement or progress difficult
  • synonym: impede - to slow or prevent the progress of
  • synonym: restrain - to prevent from doing something; to hold back
