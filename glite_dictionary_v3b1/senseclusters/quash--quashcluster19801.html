quash

verb
formally declare invalid
Pronunciation: / kwɑʃ / (AmE), / kwɒʃ / (BrE)
to officially reject or declare something, especially a decision or ruling, as invalid

Examples

To quash a decision
The committee voted to quash the proposal, citing serious procedural flaws.
The defense team filed a motion to quash the subpoena.
The judge decided to quash the previous conviction due to new evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annul - to make a legal agreement invalid
  • antonym: uphold - to confirm or support a decision or law
