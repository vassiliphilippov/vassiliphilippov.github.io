dissipative

adjective
wasteful or indulgent
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsɪˌpeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪpətɪv / (BrE)
characterized by wasteful spending, indulgence, or excessive pursuit of pleasure.

Examples

Dissipative lifestyle
His dissipative habits quickly drained his inheritance and left him in debt.
A dissipative lifestyle can lead to financial and personal difficulties over time.
She regretted her dissipative choices after realizing how much she had wasted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extravagant - spending more than is necessary or reasonable
  • antonym: frugal - careful with money and resources; not wasteful
  • synonym: self-indulgent - allowing oneself to enjoy too much pleasure or luxury
