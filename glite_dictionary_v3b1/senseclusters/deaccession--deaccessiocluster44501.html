deaccession

verb
officially remove from a collection
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /diː.æk.ˈseʃ.ən/ /; / /də.æk.ˈseʃ.ən/ (AmE), /diː.æk.ˈseʃ.ən/ /; / /dɪ.æk.ˈseʃ.ən/ (BrE)
to officially remove an item, especially a work of art, from a museum or library collection, often by selling it to raise funds for other acquisitions

Examples

To deaccession museum items
After much debate, the committee deaccessioned part of the sculpture collection.
Some institutions have strict policies restricting what can be deaccessioned.
The museum decided to deaccession several old paintings to fund new acquisitions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accession - to add an item to a museum or library collection
