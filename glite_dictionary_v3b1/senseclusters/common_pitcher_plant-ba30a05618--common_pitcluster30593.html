common pitcher plant

noun
a bog plant with rosettes of pitcher-shaped leaves
7.7 0.00/m

a perennial bog plant of northeastern North America, with dark red flowers and broad, decumbent, winged, tube-like leaves (pitchers) that form a rosette, known for trapping insects; also naturalized in Europe, especially Ireland.

Examples

The common pitcher plant traps insects
Botanists studied the common pitcher plant to learn how it traps insects for nutrition.
The common pitcher plant has become naturalized in northern Europe, especially parts of Ireland.
The common pitcher plant thrives in wet, acidic bogs where few other plants can survive.
