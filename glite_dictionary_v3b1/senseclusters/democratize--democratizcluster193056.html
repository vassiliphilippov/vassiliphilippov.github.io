democratize

verb
make widely accessible
5.1 0.41/m
Pronunciation: / d…™Ààm…ëkr…ôta…™z /; / d…™Ààm…ëÀê.kr…ô.ta…™z / (AmE), / d…™Ààm…íkr…ôta…™z /; / d…™Ààm…í.kr…ô.ta…™z / (BrE)
to make something (for example information, technology, or services) widely accessible and usable by many people, especially by removing barriers so it is not limited to experts or elites.

Examples

To democratize technology
Open-source projects aim to democratize software development by lowering barriers for contributors worldwide.
The nonprofit plans to democratize access to scientific data through free, online databases for researchers.
They democratized the design tool so small businesses could create their own marketing materials.
The startup aims to democratise machine learning tools so small businesses can use them easily.
By providing user guides and APIs, they democratized access to satellite imagery for researchers worldwide.
Open-source projects are democratizing scientific software, allowing non-experts to run complex analyses at home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liberalize - to make rules, services, or systems less restrictive and more widely available
  • synonym: open up - to make something available to more people
  • antonym: restrict - to limit access or availability; opposite of making widely accessible
