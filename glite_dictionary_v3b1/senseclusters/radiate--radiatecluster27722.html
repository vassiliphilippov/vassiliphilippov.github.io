radiate

verb
send out rays or express feelings strongly
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪ.di.eɪt /; / ˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.dɪ.eɪt /; / ˈreɪdɪeɪt /; / ˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to clearly show or express a strong feeling or quality through appearance or behavior

Examples

Radiate happiness
After hearing the good news, he began to radiate pure joy.
She managed to radiate confidence even in the most stressful situations.
The fireplace seemed to radiate warmth throughout the entire room.
After hours in the cold, his face finally radiated with warmth.
Her cheeks radiated with happiness after hearing the good news.
She often radiates a healthy pink glow after her morning run.
After receiving good news, he radiated happiness everywhere he went.
She radiated confidence during the important presentation.
The children radiated excitement on the morning of their school trip.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up energy or a quality
  • synonym: beam - to smile with obvious pleasure; to radiate happiness
  • synonym: emit - to send out something such as light, heat, sound, etc.
  • synonym: exude - to make a quality or feeling apparent by one's mood or behavior
  • synonym: glow - to show a warm, healthy color in the face
