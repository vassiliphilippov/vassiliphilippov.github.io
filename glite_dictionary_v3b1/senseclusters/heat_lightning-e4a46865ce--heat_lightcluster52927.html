heat lightning

noun
distant lightning flashes seen without thunder
Pronunciation: / hit ˈlaɪt.nɪŋ / (AmE), / hiːt ˈlaɪt.nɪŋ / (BrE)
Flashes of lightning seen near the horizon at night without audible thunder, usually on hot evenings, believed to be reflections of distant lightning by clouds.

Examples

Heat lightning at night
Despite its name, heat lightning is simply regular lightning from distant storms.
Many people mistake heat lightning for storm warnings, but it rarely brings rain nearby.
On warm summer nights, you can sometimes watch heat lightning flicker on the horizon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lightning - a flash of light in the sky caused by electricity during a storm
