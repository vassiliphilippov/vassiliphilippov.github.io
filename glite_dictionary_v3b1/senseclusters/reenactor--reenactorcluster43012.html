reenactor

noun
person who acts out historical events
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈnæktər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈnæktə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in the recreation of historical events by acting out roles from the past

Examples

A reenactor in uniform
As a Civil War reenactor, he spends weekends dressed in period uniforms.
The film hired several reenactors to accurately reproduce scenes from the past.
Thousands of reenactors participated in the anniversary of the historic battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: re-enact - to act out (a past event) again
