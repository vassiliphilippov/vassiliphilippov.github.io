Euterpe oleracea

noun
Brazilian palm yielding açaí berries and edible buds
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈtɜːrpi ˌoʊləˈreɪsiə / (AmE), / juːˈtɜːpi ˌɒləˈreɪsɪə / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree whose young leaf buds are eaten like cabbage and which produces the açaí berry.

Examples

Euterpe oleracea palm tree
Farmers in Brazil cultivate Euterpe oleracea both for its edible shoots and its popular açaí berries.
Many rainforest communities rely on Euterpe oleracea as a staple crop and economic resource.
The young leaf buds of Euterpe oleracea are harvested and cooked much like cabbage.
