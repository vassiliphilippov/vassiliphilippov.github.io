backwater

noun
unprogressive or stagnant place
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈbækwɔːtə / (BrE)
a place, situation, or area in which no significant progress or development is taking place

Examples

A cultural backwater
After years of neglect, the once-thriving port had become a cultural backwater.
The reforms aimed to revitalize what was long considered an educational backwater.
The small town was seen as a backwater, untouched by modern industry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backwoods - an area far from cities, often considered undeveloped
  • antonym: center - a place where important development or activity happens
  • synonym: outpost - a remote part of a country or empire
