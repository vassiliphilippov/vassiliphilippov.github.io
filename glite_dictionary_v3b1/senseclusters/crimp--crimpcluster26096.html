crimp

noun
fold or bend made by pressing
9.9 6.1
Pronunciation: / krɪmp / (AmE), / krɪmp / (BrE)
A small fold, ridge, or bend formed by pressing, folding, or pinching a material, especially to create a decorative or functional edge.

Examples

A metal crimp
The metal wire holds better after a crimp has been applied.
The tailor added a crimp to the edge of the dress for extra detail.
You can see a slight crimp where the paper was folded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crease - a line or ridge produced on paper or cloth by folding, pressing, or crushing
  • synonym: fold - a line or shape made by doubling something over on itself
