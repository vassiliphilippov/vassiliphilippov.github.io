presence of mind

noun
ability to stay calm and act quickly in a crisis
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛzəns əv ˈmaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈprɛzəns əv ˈmaɪnd / (BrE)
the ability to remain calm and take quick, sensible action, especially in a sudden or unexpected situation

Examples

Presence of mind in danger
Thanks to her presence of mind, she managed to call for help immediately after the accident.
The pilot showed remarkable presence of mind during the emergency landing.
Without his presence of mind, the situation could have ended much worse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: composure - calmness or self-control, especially in a difficult situation
  • antonym: panic - a sudden overwhelming fear, often resulting in irrational behavior
