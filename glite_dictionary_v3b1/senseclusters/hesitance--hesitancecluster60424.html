hesitance

noun
uncertainty or reluctance to act or decide
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛzɪtəns / (AmE), / ˈhɛzɪtəns / (BrE)
the state or quality of feeling uncertain or indecisive about doing something; reluctance to act or speak promptly

Examples

Show hesitance to act
Despite his hesitance, he finally decided to sign the contract.
He noticed the slight hesitance before she agreed to the proposal.
There was a clear hesitance in her voice when she answered the question.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confidence - certainty and self-assurance in decision or action
  • synonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or feeling reluctant before doing something
