James Polk

proper noun
11th U.S. president, led territorial expansion
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪmz poʊk / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz pəʊk / (BrE)
(1795–1849) the 11th President of the United States (1845–1849), known for his policies of territorial expansion that led to the Mexican-American War and the annexation of California and much of the southwestern United States.

Examples

James Polk presidency
James Polk's presidency was marked by significant territorial expansion in the United States.
Many historians credit James Polk with achieving most of his administration's major goals.
The annexation of Texas took place under James Polk's leadership.
