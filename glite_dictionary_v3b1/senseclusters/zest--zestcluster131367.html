zest

noun
a sharp or spicy taste or quality
Pronunciation: / zɛst / (AmE), / zɛst / (BrE)
great enthusiasm, energy, or a lively, exciting quality (sometimes a pleasantly sharp taste)

Examples

Zest for life
Her writing had a zest that made even ordinary topics exciting.
The chef added orange zest to give the dessert an extra layer of flavor.
The lemonade had a refreshing zest that made it perfect for a summer afternoon.
After her vacation, she returned to work full of zest for her job.
His stories were told with such zest that everyone was captivated.
She tackled every new challenge with remarkable zest and determination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • antonym: blandness - lacking strong features or characteristics; dullness of taste
  • synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • synonym: piquancy - pleasantly sharp or appetizing flavor
