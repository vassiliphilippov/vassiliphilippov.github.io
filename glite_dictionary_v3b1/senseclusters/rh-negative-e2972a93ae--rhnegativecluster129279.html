Rh-negative

adjective
lacking the Rh blood factor
3.4
Pronunciation: / ɑr eɪ ˈnɛɡətɪv / (AmE), / ɑː ɑː ˈnɛɡətɪv / (BrE)
(of a person or their blood) lacking the Rh factor in the red blood cells.

Examples

Rh-negative blood type
Doctors routinely check if patients are Rh-negative before certain medical procedures.
If an Rh-negative person receives Rh-positive blood, complications can occur.
Many women who are Rh-negative require special care during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Rh-positive - having the Rh factor in the blood
