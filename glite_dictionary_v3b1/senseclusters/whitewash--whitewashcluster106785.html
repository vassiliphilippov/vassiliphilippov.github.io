whitewash

noun
an attempt to cover up faults or failures
5.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪ.twɑːʃ/ /; / /ˈhwaɪ.twɑːʃ/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪ.twɒʃ/ /; / /ˈhwaɪ.twɒʃ/ (BrE)
an attempt to conceal faults, errors, or unpleasant facts, often in a misleading or deceptive way

Examples

A political whitewash
Many felt the investigation was just a whitewash to protect those in power.
The official report was criticized as a whitewash that ignored the real problems.
The scandal was soon forgotten after a media whitewash downplayed key evidence.
