flock to

verb
go somewhere in large numbers
Pronunciation: / flɑk tu / (AmE), / flɒk tʊ / (BrE)
(of people) to go to a place in large numbers, often because of interest or attraction.

Examples

People flock to events
During the festival, thousands of people flock to the city square for the celebrations.
Every spring, birdwatchers flock to the wetlands to spot rare species.
Shoppers flock to the mall on Black Friday in search of the best deals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gather - to come together in one place
