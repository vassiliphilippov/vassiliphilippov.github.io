argumentativeness

noun
tendency to argue
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrɡjəˈmɛntətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡjʊˈmɛntətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or tendency of often arguing or disagreeing with others

Examples

His argumentativeness in discussions
His argumentativeness made group discussions difficult, as he challenged every point.
Teachers sometimes struggle with students whose argumentativeness disrupts the class.
Her argumentativeness can be useful in debates but tiring in casual conversations.
Her argumentativeness often led to heated debates during meetings.
Some people appreciate argumentativeness as a sign of critical thinking.
His argumentativeness sometimes made it hard to reach a consensus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreeableness - the quality of being pleasant and willing to agree
  • synonym: contentiousness - the tendency to cause or engage in arguments
