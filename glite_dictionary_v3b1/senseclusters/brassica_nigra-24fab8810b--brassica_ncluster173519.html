Brassica nigra

noun
plant producing seeds for mustard
7.5
Pronunciation: / brəˈsɪkə ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈnɪɡrə / (BrE)
a Eurasian annual plant, commonly known as black mustard, cultivated for its pungent seeds used in making table mustard

Examples

Brassica nigra seeds
Farmers in the region often plant Brassica nigra as a crop for its valuable seeds.
The seeds of Brassica nigra are ground to make traditional table mustard.
Wild patches of Brassica nigra can sometimes be found along country roads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: black mustard - common name for Brassica nigra, the plant producing pungent seeds used in mustard
  • degree lower: white mustard - related species producing milder seeds for table mustard
