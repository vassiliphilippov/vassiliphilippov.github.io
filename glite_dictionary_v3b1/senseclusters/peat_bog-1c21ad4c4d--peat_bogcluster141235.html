peat bog

noun
wetland with layers of peat
6.3 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / pit b…ë…° / (AmE), / piÀêt b…í…° / (BrE)
A type of wet, spongy ground consisting largely of accumulated, partially decomposed plant material (peat), often used as a source of fuel.

Examples

A peat bog area
Dried peat bog is sometimes used as a traditional fuel source in rural areas.
Much of the region is covered by peat bog and marshland.
The team collected samples from a remote peat bog in Scotland.
