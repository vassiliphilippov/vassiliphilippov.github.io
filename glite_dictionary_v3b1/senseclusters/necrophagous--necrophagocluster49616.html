necrophagous

adjective
eating dead animals
9.9
Pronunciation: / nəˈkrɑːfəɡəs / (AmE), / nɪˈkrɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
feeding on dead or decaying animal flesh

Examples

Necrophagous birds
Vultures are well-known necrophagous birds that help clean up animal remains.
Some beetles are necrophagous, playing an important role in decomposition.
The necrophagous habits of certain animals are essential for maintaining ecosystem balance.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: carnivorous - feeding on other animals
  • synonym: scavenging - feeding on dead or decaying matter
