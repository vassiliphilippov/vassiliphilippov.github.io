new math

noun
method of teaching math with modern abstract concepts
1.6
Pronunciation: / nu mæθ / (AmE), / njuː mæθ / (BrE)
a method of teaching mathematics introduced in the mid-20th century that emphasized abstract concepts such as set theory and structure, often seen as confusing by those used to traditional methods

Examples

New math method
Many students struggled when the school introduced new math into the curriculum.
Parents often found new math homework difficult to help with due to its unfamiliar methods.
The new math movement aimed to teach students the underlying concepts of mathematics, not just procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: traditional math - conventional methods of teaching mathematics focusing on arithmetic and basic principles
