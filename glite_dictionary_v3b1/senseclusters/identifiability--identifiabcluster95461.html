identifiability

noun
uniqueness of model parameters
8.6
Pronunciation: /aɪ.den.tɪ.fɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ /; / /ɪ.den.tɪ.fɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /aɪ.den.tɪ.fɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ /; / /ɪ.den.tɪ.fɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a statistical or mathematical model that allows its parameters or structure to be uniquely determined from observed data

Examples

Identifiability of parameters
The identifiability of the model ensures that each parameter can be estimated without confusion.
Researchers discussed the identifiability of their statistical approach before analyzing the data.
Without identifiability, the results of the experiment could be misleading or unclear.

  • synonym: identifiable - able to be recognized or distinguished as separate
