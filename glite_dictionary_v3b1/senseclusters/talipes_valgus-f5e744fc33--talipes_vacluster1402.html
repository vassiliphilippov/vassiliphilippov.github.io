talipes valgus

noun
foot deformity with foot twisted outward
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtæ.ləˌpiz ˈvæl.ɡəs / (AmE), / ˈtæ.lɪˌpiːz ˈval.ɡəs / (BrE)
A deformity of the foot in which the foot is twisted outward, so that the sole faces away from the body's midline.

Examples

Talipes valgus deformity
Corrective footwear can help manage the symptoms of talipes valgus in young patients.
Radiographs confirmed the presence of talipes valgus affecting both feet.
The child was diagnosed with talipes valgus and referred to an orthopedic specialist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: talipes varus - foot deformity with inward twist (sole faces midline)
