ridden

adjective
dominated by something negative
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪdən / (AmE), / ˈrɪdən / (BrE)
affected or dominated by something undesirable or negative, often used after a noun to show what is present in excess.

Examples

Ridden with corruption
The country was ridden with corruption, making progress difficult.
After years of being debt-ridden, the company finally recovered.
The old house was ridden with mold and needed major repairs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not affected or dominated by something negative
  • synonym: plagued - troubled or affected persistently by something bad
