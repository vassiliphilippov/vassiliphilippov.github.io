synthetic

adjective
characterized by combining elements into single words (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a language) characterized by the systematic combining of root and modifying elements into single words, often resulting in words that express several grammatical meanings at once.

Examples

Synthetic language
Finnish is considered a highly synthetic language due to its complex word formation.
In linguistic typology, synthetic languages contrast with analytic languages like Chinese.
Many indigenous American languages are more synthetic than English, expressing more information within single words.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analytic - (of a language) tending to use separate words rather than inflection
