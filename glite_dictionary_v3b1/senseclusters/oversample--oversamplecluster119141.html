oversample

verb
to sample excessively
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsæmpəl / (BrE)
to collect more data than necessary, either to improve accuracy or to better represent a group

Examples

To oversample a group
To ensure enough data for analysis, the researchers decided to oversample participants from smaller groups.
Audio engineers sometimes oversample signals to improve sound quality during processing.
If you oversample certain categories, you may need to adjust your results to avoid bias.
To reduce noise, engineers decided to oversample the digital signal during processing.
The research team chose to oversample minority groups to ensure accurate representation in the survey.
Audio quality improved significantly after they began to oversample the recordings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resample - to take a new sample from data, often at a different rate or method
  • synonym: sample - to take measurements or data points from a signal or population
  • antonym: undersample - to sample data at a lower rate or to reduce the representation of a group in a dataset
