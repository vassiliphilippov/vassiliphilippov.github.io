pupillary sphincter

noun
muscle that constricts the pupil
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpjupəˌlɛri ˈsfɪŋktər / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpɪləri ˈsfɪŋktə / (BrE)
a ring of smooth muscle in the iris of the eye that contracts to constrict the pupil

Examples

The pupillary sphincter muscle
Damage to the pupillary sphincter may result in abnormal pupil reactions to light.
Doctors examined the patient's pupillary sphincter function during the eye exam.
The pupillary sphincter controls how much light enters the eye by adjusting the size of the pupil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: iris - the colored part of the eye surrounding the pupil
