Pisonia aculeata

noun
a spiny West Indian tree species
Pronunciation: / pɪˈsoʊniə ˌækjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / pɪˈsəʊniə ˌækjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a small spiny tree native to the West Indies and tropical regions, known for its sticky fruits that cling to animal fur and feathers

The spiny Pisonia aculeata tree
