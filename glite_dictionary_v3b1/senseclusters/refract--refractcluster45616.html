refract

verb
change direction when passing into a different medium
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrakt /; / rɪˈfrɑːkt / (BrE)
to change direction when passing from one material to another, as light or other waves do (such as when light passes through water or glass)

Examples

Refract light
A prism can refract white light into the colors of the spectrum.
The atmosphere can refract starlight, making stars appear to twinkle.
Waves will refract when they enter shallow water at an angle.
A prism refracts light into the colors of the spectrum.
As light passes from air into water, it refracts and changes direction.
When sunlight enters a glass of water, the refracted rays can create colorful patterns.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: diffuse - (of light) to spread out in many directions
  • antonym: reflect - to bounce back from a surface rather than passing through
  • degree higher: scatter - (of light) to be dispersed or spread irregularly
