rethread

verb
to restore threads
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈθrɛd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈθrɛd / (BrE)
to cut, restore, or replace the spiral grooves (threads) on a screw, bolt, nut, or pipe so it can fasten or join properly

Examples

To rethread a bolt
The mechanic had to rethread the damaged bolt before reassembling the engine to ensure a tight fit.
If the threads on a pipe are worn out, you may need to rethread it to prevent leaks.
After removing the old nut, she carefully rethreaded the screw to make it usable again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repair - to fix or restore something to good condition
  • synonym: replace - to put something new in the place of something old or damaged
