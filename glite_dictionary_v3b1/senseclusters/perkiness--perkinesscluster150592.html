perkiness

noun
lively, cheerful quality
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːk.i.nəs /; / ˈpɝːki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːk.i.nəs /; / ˈpɜː.ki.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being lively, cheerful, and full of energy (sometimes in a way that seems overly playful or inappropriate)

Examples

His perkiness brightens days
Despite the rainy weather, his perkiness never seemed to fade.
Her perkiness in the morning always cheers up the whole office.
The host's perkiness made the television show more enjoyable for viewers.
Her perkiness in the early morning meetings always lifted everyone's spirits.
Some found his constant perkiness endearing, while others thought it was a bit much.
The actor brought a youthful perkiness to the role that made the character memorable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gloominess - the quality of being sad or depressed
  • antonym: solemnity - the state of being serious and dignified
  • synonym: vivacity - the quality of being lively and animated
