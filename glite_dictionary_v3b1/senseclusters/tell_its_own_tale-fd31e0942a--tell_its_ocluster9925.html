tell its own tale

verb
to convey a story by itself
2.4
Pronunciation: / tɛl ɪts oʊn teɪl / (AmE), / tɛl ɪts əʊn teɪl / (BrE)
(of an object, fact, or feature) to reveal or convey its own story or meaning without explicit explanation.

Examples

Tell its own tale clearly
Each wrinkle on his face seemed to tell its own tale about a life fully lived.
The crumbling walls tell their own tale of years of neglect and weathering.
The faded photograph can tell its own tale to anyone who looks closely enough.

Related Terms

  • synonym: speak for itself - to be so clear or self-evident that no further explanation is needed
