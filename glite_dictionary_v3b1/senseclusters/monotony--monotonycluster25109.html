monotony

noun
lack of variety; tedious sameness
5.5 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / m…ôÀàn…ëÀêt…ôni / (AmE), / m…ôÀàn…ít…ôni / (BrE)
the quality of being boring or dull because of repetition or lack of variety (in activities, experiences, or sound)

Examples

The monotony of work
After a few weeks of traveling, the monotony of hotel rooms started to bother him.
He tries to break the monotony of his daily routine by taking a new route to work.
The teacher introduced games and activities to prevent monotony in the classroom.
She tried to add excitement to her performance to avoid the monotony of singing in the same tone.
The monotony in his voice suggested he was not interested in the conversation.
The professor's monotony made it difficult for students to stay engaged during the lecture.
