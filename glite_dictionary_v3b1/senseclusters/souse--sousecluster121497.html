souse

noun
pickled, jellied pork trimmings
Pronunciation: / saʊs / (AmE), / saʊs / (BrE)
pork trimmings that are chopped, pickled in vinegar, and set in a jelly; a type of head cheese.

Examples

Pickled souse meat
He prepared homemade souse using pork, vinegar, and spices for the holiday feast.
Some people enjoy souse on crackers as a savory snack.
The traditional Southern dish souse is often served cold with sliced onions.
