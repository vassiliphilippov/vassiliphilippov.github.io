Haeckel

proper noun
German biologist and philosopher (1834–1919)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛkəl / (BrE)
(Ernst) Haeckel (1834–1919), a German biologist and philosopher known for advocating Darwinism and formulating the theory of recapitulation.

Examples

Haeckel theory
Haeckel was instrumental in popularizing Darwin's theories in Germany during the late 19th century.
Many scientific illustrations created by Haeckel are admired for their artistic as well as biological detail.
The concept of recapitulation, proposed by Haeckel, influenced generations of evolutionary biologists.
