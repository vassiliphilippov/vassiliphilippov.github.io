Confucius

proper noun
Chinese philosopher, founder of Confucianism
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuːʃəs / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʃəs / (BrE)
(c. 551–478 BC) A Chinese philosopher whose teachings and sayings became the foundation of Confucianism.

Examples

Confucius teachings
Confucius is often quoted for his wisdom about human relationships and society.
Many of Confucius' teachings emphasize the importance of respect and morality.
The ideas of Confucius still influence Chinese culture today.
