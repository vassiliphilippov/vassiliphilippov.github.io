sectarian

adjective
relating to a sect or sects
Pronunciation: / sɛkˈtɛriən / (AmE), / sɛkˈtɛːrɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a sect, especially a religious or political group with particular beliefs that distinguish it from others.

Examples

Sectarian conflict
He was criticized for making sectarian remarks during the heated debate.
Laws were introduced to reduce sectarian discrimination in education and employment.
The country has experienced decades of sectarian conflict between rival groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonsectarian - not associated with any sect or group
  • synonym: religious - relating to religion
