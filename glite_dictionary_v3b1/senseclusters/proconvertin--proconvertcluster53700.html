proconvertin

noun
blood coagulation factor, also called factor VII
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / pro äk…ônÀàv…úrt…™n / (AmE), / pr…ô äk…ônÀàv…úÀêt…™n / (BrE)
a blood coagulation factor (factor VII) produced mainly in the liver under the influence of vitamin K, essential for normal blood clotting

Examples

Proconvertin blood factor
A deficiency of proconvertin can lead to abnormal blood clotting and excessive bleeding.
The doctor ordered tests to measure proconvertin levels in the patient's blood.
Vitamin K is necessary for the synthesis of proconvertin in the liver.

Synonyms

  • factor VII
    5.9 0.00/m
    factor VII is a blood protein described as made in the kidneys with vitamin K, while proconvertin is said to be made mainly in the liver.
  • stable factor
    9.4 0.00/m
    stable factor is given as another name for the clotting protein made in kidneys, while proconvertin is described as made mainly in the liver.
