woozy

adjective
feeling weak or dizzy
5.7 1.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàwuÀêzi / (AmE), / ÀàwuÀêzi / (BrE)
feeling weak, dizzy, or unsteady, as if about to lose balance or consciousness.

Examples

Feeling woozy and dizzy
After standing up too quickly, I felt woozy and had to sit back down.
He was still woozy from the effects of the anesthesia.
The medication made her feel a little woozy during the afternoon.

Synonyms

  • lightheaded
    4.4 0.52/m
    lightheaded feels faint and dizzy in the head, while woozy adds a weak, sickly feeling and loss of balance.
  • dizzy
    1.0 11/m
    dizzy makes you feel a spinning or whirling sensation, while woozy also suggests weakness and a near loss of balance.
  • giddy
    4.9 0.53/m
    giddy feels light and playful or has a whirling head, while woozy feels weak and unsteady, often close to fainting.
  • faint
    2.7 2.05/m
    faint describes being very weak and near unconsciousness, while woozy is a less certain, mixed feeling of dizziness and unsteadiness.
  • groggy
    5.6 1.37/m
    groggy feels slow, confused, and unsteady from sleep, illness, or alcohol, while woozy is mainly dizzy and weak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you might lose your balance or faint
  • synonym: giddy - feeling light-headed and likely to fall
  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed or supported; not shaking or likely to fall
