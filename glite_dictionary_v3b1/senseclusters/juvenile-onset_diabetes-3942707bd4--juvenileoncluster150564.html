juvenile-onset diabetes

noun
early-onset form of diabetes mellitus

A form of diabetes mellitus occurring in children or young people, characterized by early onset, the need for insulin treatment, and symptoms such as excessive thirst and urination.

Examples

Juvenile-onset diabetes in children
Doctors diagnosed her with juvenile-onset diabetes when she was only eight years old.
Many families learn to recognize the early symptoms of juvenile-onset diabetes in children.
Regular insulin injections are necessary to manage juvenile-onset diabetes effectively.

Synonyms

  • juvenile diabetes
    3.2 0.13/m

  • type I diabetes
    3.5 0.09/m
    type I diabetes starts when the pancreas makes little or no insulin, while juvenile-onset diabetes focuses on early appearance in children.
  • insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus
    4.9 0.04/m
    insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus needs regular insulin injections to control blood sugar, while juvenile-onset diabetes highlights its early start in young people.
  • autoimmune diabetes
    4.5 0.00/m
    autoimmune diabetes is caused by the immune system attacking insulin cells, while juvenile-onset diabetes stresses early onset and insulin treatment.
