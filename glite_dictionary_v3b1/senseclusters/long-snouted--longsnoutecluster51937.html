long-snouted

adjective
having a longer-than-average snout
5.6
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈsnaʊtɪd / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈsnaʊtɪd / (BrE)
Having a snout that is longer than average, especially in comparison to other animals of the same type.

Examples

A long-snouted fish
Many species of long-snouted fish use their extended jaws to probe for food in crevices.
The long-snouted crocodile is well-adapted to catching fish in rivers.
The scientist studied a rare long-snouted lizard found only in this region.
