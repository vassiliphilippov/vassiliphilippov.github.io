uncultivable

adjective
not able to be cultivated (land)
6.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌltɪˌveɪbəl /; / ʌnˈkʌltɪvəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltɪvəbl / (BrE)
not able to be cultivated or made to produce crops or organisms, especially due to unsuitable conditions

Examples

Uncultivable soil

Some bacteria remain uncultivable in laboratory conditions despite many attempts.
The land was declared uncultivable after years of drought and erosion.
