uncultivable

adjective
not able to be cultivated (land)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌltɪˌveɪbəl /; / ʌnˈkʌltɪvəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltɪvəbl / (BrE)
not able to be cultivated or made to produce crops or organisms, especially due to unsuitable conditions

Examples

Uncultivable soil
Government initiatives have failed to make the uncultivable soil productive for farmers.
He purchased a large tract of rocky, uncultivable land outside the city.
Much of the desert region is considered uncultivable due to lack of water.
Much of the rocky hillside is uncultivable and cannot support any crops.
Some bacteria remain uncultivable in laboratory conditions despite many attempts.
The land was declared uncultivable after years of drought and erosion.
