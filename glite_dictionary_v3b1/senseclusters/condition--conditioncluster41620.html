condition

noun
an experimental treatment or arrangement
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
in an experiment, a particular situation or treatment that is deliberately varied to observe its effect compared to a control situation

Examples

Test condition
Each group was tested under a different condition to measure the effects of temperature on performance.
Participants in the control condition did not receive any form of intervention.
The experiment included a placebo condition and two treatment conditions for comparison.

Related Terms

  • antonym: control - an unvaried standard in an experiment for comparison with tested conditions
