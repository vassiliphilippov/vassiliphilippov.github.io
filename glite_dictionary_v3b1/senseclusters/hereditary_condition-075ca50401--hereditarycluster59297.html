hereditary condition

noun
disease passed genetically
Pronunciation: / həˈrɛdəˌtɛri kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛd.ɪ.tər.i kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a disease or disorder that is passed from parent to child through genes

Examples

A hereditary condition disease
Doctors can now screen for many hereditary conditions before birth.
He was concerned about the possibility of passing a hereditary condition to his children.
Sickle cell anemia is a hereditary condition that affects red blood cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genetic disorder - a disease caused by abnormalities in genes or chromosomes
