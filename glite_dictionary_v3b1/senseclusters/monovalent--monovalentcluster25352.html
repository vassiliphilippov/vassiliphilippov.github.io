monovalent

adjective
having a single antibody type or specificity
6.2 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of an antibody or vaccine) effective against or containing only one specific antigen or pathogen; having a single valence.

Examples

Monovalent vaccine
A monovalent serum may be less effective if multiple pathogens are present.
Researchers developed a monovalent antibody to fight the specific infection.
The monovalent vaccine targets only one strain of the influenza virus.
