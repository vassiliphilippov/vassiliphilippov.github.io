plucky

adjective
courageous and determined
Pronunciation: / ˈplʌki / (AmE), / ˈplʌki / (BrE)
having or showing courage and determination, especially in difficult situations

Examples

A plucky young woman
Despite the odds, the plucky young woman completed the marathon.
Reporters admired the plucky rescue workers who entered the burning building.
The plucky child stood up for his friend when he was being bullied.
Despite her small size, the plucky dog chased away the much larger animal.
He made a plucky attempt to climb the mountain, despite the bad weather.
The young scientist’s plucky attitude inspired the rest of the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brave - showing courage and not being afraid in difficult situations
  • antonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened
  • antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence; easily frightened
