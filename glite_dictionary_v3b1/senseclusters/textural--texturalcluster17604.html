textural

adjective
relating to texture
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛkstʃərəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to the structure, arrangement, or feel of a material or surface, especially how it is experienced by touch or sight

Examples

Textural quality of fabric
The textural differences between the two fabrics made one feel much softer than the other.
Artists often experiment with textural elements to add depth and interest to their paintings.
The bread's unique textural quality comes from the way it is baked.
The textural differences between the fabrics made each one unique to the touch.
Chefs often consider the textural qualities of ingredients when designing a dish.
The painting's textural richness draws viewers in for a closer look.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: rough - having an uneven or irregular surface
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface
  • synonym: structural - relating to the arrangement or organization of parts
  • synonym: tactile - relating to the sense of touch
