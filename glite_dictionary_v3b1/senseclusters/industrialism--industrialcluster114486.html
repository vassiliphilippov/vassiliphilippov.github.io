industrialism

noun
system based on large industries
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌstriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
an economic and social system based on the development of large-scale industries, rather than agriculture or craftsmanship

Examples

The rise of industrialism
Historians often debate the impact of industrialism on workers and traditional communities.
Many rural areas struggled to adapt to the challenges posed by rapid industrialism.
The rise of industrialism in the 19th century transformed societies around the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agriculturalism - an economic system based on agriculture
  • synonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership of capital
