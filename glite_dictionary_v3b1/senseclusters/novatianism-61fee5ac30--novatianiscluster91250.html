Novatianism

noun
strict refusal to forgive
9.9
Pronunciation: /noʊ.veɪ.ˈʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /noʊ.ˈveɪ.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /nəʊ.veɪ.ˈʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /nəʊ.ˈveɪ.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a strict policy or attitude of refusing forgiveness or reconciliation, especially in matters of church discipline for those who have lapsed

Examples

Strict Novatianism policy

Some historians argue that Novatianism influenced later debates about forgiveness and discipline.
Her approach to the rules was marked by a kind of Novatianism that left little room for compassion.
