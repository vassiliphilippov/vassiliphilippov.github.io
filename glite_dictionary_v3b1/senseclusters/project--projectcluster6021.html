project

verb
to throw or send forward
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to throw, send, or cast something forward; to propel something outward

Examples

To project water
Engineers designed a new system to project water farther from the fountain.
The catapult was used to project large stones over the castle walls.
The device can project small objects into the air at high speed.
During the storm, the lighthouse projected a powerful beam across the sea.
The lamp projects a soft, golden light onto the table.
The old movie projector projected images onto the blank wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cast - to throw or direct something, such as light or a shadow, in a particular direction
  • synonym: emit - to send out (light, heat, sound, etc.)
  • synonym: launch - to send something out with force, especially into the air or into space
  • synonym: propel - to drive or push something forwards
