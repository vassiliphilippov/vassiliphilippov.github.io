abiogenist

noun
person who believes in abiogenesis
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.baɪˈɑː.dʒə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.bʌɪˈɒ.dʒə.nɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or advocates the theory of abiogenesis (the origin of living organisms from non-living matter)

Examples

An abiogenist scientist
Few modern biologists identify as abiogenists, but the historical controversy was intense.
In the nineteenth century, being an abiogenist often put one at odds with the supporters of biogenesis.
The prominent scientist was known as a staunch abiogenist during debates on the origin of life.
