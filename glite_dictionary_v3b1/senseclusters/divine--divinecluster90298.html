divine

verb
search using a divining rod
9.9
Pronunciation: / dəˈvaɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪn / (BrE)
to discover or perceive something by supernatural means, intuition, or dowsing

Examples

To divine the future
Some farmers still use a willow branch to divine the location of underground streams.
The old prospector claimed he could divine gold deposits with nothing but a rod.
They hired a dowser to divine for water before drilling a new well.
He could divine trouble coming before anyone else sensed it.
Many ancient peoples tried to divine the future by observing the stars.
She seemed to divine his feelings without him saying a word.
