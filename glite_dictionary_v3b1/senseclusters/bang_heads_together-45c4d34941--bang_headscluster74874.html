bang heads together

verb
force people to cooperate or agree
3.9
Pronunciation: / bæŋ hɛdz təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / bæŋ hɛdz təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to take forceful or decisive action to make people who are disagreeing or not cooperating start working together or reach agreement

Examples

To bang heads together firmly
If you two don't stop arguing, I'll have to bang heads together.
The editor decided to bang heads together to meet the publishing deadline.
When the team couldn't agree, the coach had to bang heads together to get them focused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knock heads together - to force people to cooperate or reconcile their differences
