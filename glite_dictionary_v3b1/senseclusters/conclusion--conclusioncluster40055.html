conclusion

noun
statement reached by reasoning
Pronunciation: / kənˈkluʒən / (AmE), / kənˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
a statement or proposition reached as a result of reasoning, especially as inferred from premises in logic

Examples

Logical conclusion
Based on the premises, the only reasonable conclusion was that the suspect had an alibi.
In formal logic, a syllogism contains two premises and a conclusion.
The debater struggled to defend the conclusion drawn from the arguments presented.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inference - a conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning
  • antonym: premise - a previous statement from which another is inferred or follows as a conclusion
