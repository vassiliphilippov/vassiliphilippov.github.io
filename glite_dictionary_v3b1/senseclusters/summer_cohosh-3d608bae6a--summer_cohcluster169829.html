summer cohosh

noun
a plant with white racemes, also bugbane
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌmɚ ˈkoʊhɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə ˈkəʊhɒʃ / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the eastern United States, known for its tall, upright clusters (racemes) of white flowers; also called bugbane.

Examples

Summer cohosh plant
Many gardeners plant summer cohosh to attract pollinators with its showy flowers.
Native Americans historically used the root of summer cohosh for medicinal purposes.
The shady woodlands are home to the elegant summer cohosh in full bloom each June.
