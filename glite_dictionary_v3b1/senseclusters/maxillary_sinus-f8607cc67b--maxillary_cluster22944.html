maxillary sinus

noun
air cavity in the upper jaw bone
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmæksəˌlɛri ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈmæksɪləri ˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
either of the pair of air-filled cavities located in the maxilla (upper jaw bone) on each side of the nose.

Examples

The maxillary sinus cavity
A CT scan showed a blockage in her left maxillary sinus.
Sinusitis often affects the maxillary sinus after a severe cold.
The dentist discovered that the infection had spread into the maxillary sinus.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: paranasal sinus - any of the air-filled spaces around the nasal cavity
