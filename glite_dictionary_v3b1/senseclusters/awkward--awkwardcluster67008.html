awkward

adjective
causing inconvenience or difficulty
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɔk.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kwəd /; / ˈɔːk.wəd / (BrE)
causing difficulty, inconvenience, or embarrassment; not easy or comfortable to deal with

Examples

An awkward situation
Having the plumber over during dinner was a bit awkward.
It was an awkward location for a meeting, far from public transport.
They arrived at an awkward time, just as we were leaving for the airport.
Asking about her previous job loss made the conversation awkward.
He found himself in an awkward position during the heated argument.
There was an awkward silence after she made the unexpected announcement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfortable - free from stress, embarrassment, or difficulty
  • antonym: convenient - fitting well with needs or plans
  • synonym: difficult - not easy or hard to do, deal with, or understand
  • synonym: embarrassing - making someone feel awkward or self-conscious; causing embarrassment
  • synonym: inconvenient - causing trouble or difficulty
