diving duck

noun
duck that feeds by diving underwater
8.1 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.vɪŋ dʌk/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.vɪŋ dʌk/ (BrE)
any of various ducks, especially those found in bays and estuaries, that feed by diving underwater for food rather than dabbling at the surface.

Examples

A diving duck species
Many birdwatchers are fascinated by the agile swimming abilities of the diving duck.
The diving duck disappeared beneath the surface of the lake in search of food.
Unlike dabbling ducks, a diving duck feeds by submerging completely underwater.
