Pronunciation: /jʊ.rə.ˈpiː.ən ˈhɑːrn.biːm/ /; / /jʊə.rə.ˈpiː.ən ˈhɔːrn.biːm/ (AmE), /jʊə.rə.ˈpiː.ən ˈhɔːn.biːm/ /; / /jʊə.rə.ˈpɪ.ən ˈhɔːn.biːm/ (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, with smooth grey bark and leaves resembling those of a beech, which turn yellow-orange in autumn.

Examples

The European hornbeam tree
In autumn, the leaves of the European hornbeam turn a striking yellow-orange color.
The European hornbeam is often planted as a hedge or ornamental tree in parks and gardens.
The smooth grey bark of the European hornbeam makes it easy to identify in the wild.
