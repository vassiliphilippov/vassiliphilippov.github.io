reinvade

verb
to enter again
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈveɪd / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈveɪd / (BrE)
to enter or occupy an area again, especially after having left or been removed.

Examples

Ants reinvade the kitchen
After the pesticide wore off, ants began to reinvade the kitchen in search of food.
Scientists observed that the species would reinvade the cleared area within a few months.
If the barriers are not maintained, pests may reinvade the property repeatedly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invade - to enter a place in large numbers, often causing harm or disruption
  • antonym: withdraw - to leave or remove oneself from a place
