pillow lava

noun
rounded lava masses from underwater eruptions
6.7 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪl.oʊ ˈlæ.və / (AmE), / ˈpɪl.əʊ ˈlæ.və / (BrE)
A type of lava that hardens in rounded, pillow-shaped masses, typically formed by underwater volcanic eruptions.

Examples

Pillow lava formation
Pillow lava is often evidence that volcanic activity once occurred underwater.
Geologists discovered vast fields of pillow lava on the ocean floor.
The rounded shapes of pillow lava help scientists identify past submarine eruptions.
