preclinical test

noun
laboratory test before human trials
3.8 4.5
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈklɪn.ɪ.kəl test/ (AmE), /priː.ˈklɪn.ɪ.kəl test/ (BrE)
a laboratory test conducted on animal subjects to evaluate a new drug or medical device before it is tested in humans

Examples

A preclinical test on animals
A preclinical test on laboratory mice provided promising data supporting further development of the vaccine.
Before progressing to human studies, the researchers conducted a series of preclinical tests to assess the drug's safety.
The results from the preclinical test suggested that the new implant would not cause significant side effects in humans.
