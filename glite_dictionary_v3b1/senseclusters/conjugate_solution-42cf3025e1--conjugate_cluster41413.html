conjugate solution

noun
one of two phases from partially mixed liquids
9.9 0.00/m

each of the two separate liquid phases that form when two partially miscible liquids are mixed, with one phase rich in one component and the other rich in the second component

Examples

Two conjugate solutions
At a certain temperature, the system forms two conjugate solutions that separate into distinct layers.
Scientists use conjugate solutions to study the solubility limits of immiscible liquid pairs.
The chemical analysis revealed one conjugate solution was rich in ethanol, while the other was rich in water.
