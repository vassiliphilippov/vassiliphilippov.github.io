phalanstery

noun
communal living community
Pronunciation: / fəˈlænstəˌri / (AmE), / fəˈlænstəˌri / (BrE)
a community or building designed for communal living, especially as envisioned in utopian socialist theories

Examples

A phalanstery community
The reformers dreamed of building a phalanstery based on shared labor and resources.
Fourier's idea of a phalanstery inspired several experimental communities in the nineteenth century.
Residents of the phalanstery worked together to maintain the gardens and common spaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commune - a group of people living together and sharing possessions and responsibilities
  • synonym: monastery - a building where monks live and work together
