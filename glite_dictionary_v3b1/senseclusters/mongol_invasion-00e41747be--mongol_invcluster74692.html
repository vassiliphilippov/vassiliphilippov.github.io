Mongol invasion

noun
military campaigns by Mongol forces (13th–14th centuries)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑŋɡəl ɪnˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈmɒŋɡɒl ɪnˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
a series of military campaigns by Mongol forces during the 13th and 14th centuries, resulting in the conquest of vast territories across Eurasia

Examples

The Mongol invasion of Eurasia
Historians still study the effects of the Mongol invasion on different cultures.
Many cities were destroyed during the Mongol invasion of the thirteenth century.
The Mongol invasion changed the course of history in much of Asia and Eastern Europe.
