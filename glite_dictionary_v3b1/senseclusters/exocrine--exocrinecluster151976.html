exocrine

noun
gland secreting through a duct
9.7 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈek.soʊ.kraɪn/ (AmE), /ˈek.səʊ.kraɪn/ (BrE)
a gland that releases its secretions externally through a duct, rather than into the bloodstream

Examples

An exocrine gland
Damage to an exocrine can affect the secretion of essential fluids.
Sweat glands are an example of an exocrine in the human body.
The pancreas acts as an exocrine, producing digestive enzymes released into the intestine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endocrine - a gland that secretes hormones directly into the bloodstream
