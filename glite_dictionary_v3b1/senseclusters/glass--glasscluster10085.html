glass

noun
hard, transparent material
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a hard, brittle, usually transparent or translucent material made by fusing sand with soda, lime, and sometimes other ingredients, and cooling rapidly, used in windows, bottles, and many other products

Examples

Glass window
Scientists studied the properties of glass to design better smartphone screens.
She accidentally dropped the vase, and it shattered into glass fragments.
The window was made of thick glass to withstand strong winds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crystal - solid material with a regular atomic structure, often transparent
