wingbeat

noun
movement of a wing
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪŋ.biːt/ (AmE), /ˈwɪŋ.biːt/ (BrE)
the movement or rhythm of a bird's or insect's wing during flight

Examples

Strong wingbeat
The eagle's powerful wingbeat carried it high above the valley.
Scientists measured the frequency of the butterfly's wingbeat in the laboratory.
With each rapid wingbeat, the dragonfly hovered steadily over the pond.
The steady wingbeat of the hummingbird was barely visible to the naked eye.
She listened to the soft wingbeat of an owl passing overhead in the night.
Scientists measured the wingbeat frequency of different bird species for their study.
The hummingbird's rapid wingbeat allows it to hover in place while feeding on nectar.

The slow, powerful wingbeat of the eagle was visible as it soared above the valley.
