Toxotes jaculatrix

noun
species of archerfish known for shooting insects with water
Pronunciation: / toʊˈxoʊtiːz ˌdʒækjʊˈleɪtrɪks / (AmE), / tɒkˈsəʊtiːz ˌdʒækjʊˈleɪtrɪks / (BrE)
a species of small freshwater fish, also known as the banded archerfish, that catches insects by squirting water at them to knock them into the water; native to Southeast Asia and Australia
