feyness

noun
sense of doom
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfeɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈfeɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or state of seeming fated, doomed, or marked by an otherworldly sense, especially of impending death

Examples

A feyness before death
There was a strange feyness in his eyes, as if he knew his fate was sealed.
The old stories often describe heroes with a sudden feyness before their final battle.
After the ominous dream, she carried an air of feyness that unsettled her friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doom - a sense of unavoidable destruction or fate
  • synonym: fatalism - the belief that all events are predetermined and inevitable
  • antonym: hopefulness - a feeling of optimism or expectation of positive outcomes
