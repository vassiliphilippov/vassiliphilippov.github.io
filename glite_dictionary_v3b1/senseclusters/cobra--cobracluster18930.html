cobra

noun
venomous hooded snake of Asia and Africa
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊbrə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊbrə / (BrE)
A large, highly venomous snake found in Asia and Africa, known for expanding the skin of its neck into a hood when threatened.

Examples

A cobra snake
A single bite from a cobra can deliver enough venom to be fatal to humans.
Several species of cobra inhabit the forests and grasslands of India.
The cobra raised its hood menacingly as it faced the snake charmer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: python - a large non-venomous constricting snake
  • synonym: viper - a venomous snake with long hinged fangs
