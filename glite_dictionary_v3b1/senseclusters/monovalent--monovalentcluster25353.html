monovalent

adjective
having a valence of one (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊ.nəˈveɪ.lənt / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əˈveɪ.lənt / (BrE)
(of a chemical element or group) having a valence of one, able to combine with or replace one atom of hydrogen.

Examples

Monovalent ion
Hydrogen chloride is formed when a monovalent chlorine atom combines with hydrogen.
The chemist explained that sodium is a monovalent element because it forms only one bond.
This vaccine contains a monovalent antibody targeting a single strain of the virus.
