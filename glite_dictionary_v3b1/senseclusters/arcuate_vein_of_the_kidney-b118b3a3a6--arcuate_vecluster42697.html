arcuate vein of the kidney

noun
a kidney vein between cortex and medulla
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrkjuˌeɪt veɪn əv ðə ˈkɪdni / (AmE), / ˈɑːkjʊeɪt veɪn ɒv ðə ˈkɪdni / (BrE)
a vein in the kidney that receives blood from the interlobular veins and drains into the interlobar veins

Examples

The arcuate vein of the kidney
A blockage in the arcuate vein of the kidney can affect overall kidney function.
Blood from the interlobular veins drains into the arcuate vein of the kidney before reaching the renal vein.
During the dissection, the arcuate vein of the kidney could be clearly seen at the boundary between the cortex and medulla.
