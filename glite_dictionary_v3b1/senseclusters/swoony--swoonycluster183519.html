swoony

adjective
prone to faintness
8.0 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàswuÀêni / (AmE), / ÀàswuÀêni / (BrE)
feeling faint, light-headed, or dreamy, especially because of strong emotion or romance

Examples

A swoony feeling
She felt swoony after hearing the romantic song at the concert.
The hot weather made him a bit swoony during the afternoon walk.
Fans grew swoony when the celebrity waved at the crowd.
The romantic music made her feel completely swoony during the dance.
He gave her a swoony smile that made her heart skip a beat.
After the thrilling news, everyone in the room was a little swoony with excitement.

Synonyms

  • moony
    7.3 0.05/m
    moony is dreamy and absent-minded in a slow way, while swoony is dreamy with a strong romantic, faint feeling.
  • dreamy
    3.4 1.39/m
    dreamy has a soft, unreal, romantic quality, while swoony is dreamy plus light-headed faintness from strong emotion.
  • woozy
    5.7 1.02/m
    woozy feels physically dizzy and unsteady, while swoony often combines light-headed faintness with romantic, dreamy feelings.
  • faint
    2.7 2.05/m
    faint means very close to losing consciousness, while swoony adds a romantic, dreamy cause to that light-headed feeling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you might fall or lose balance
  • degree higher: ecstatic - feeling or showing overwhelming happiness or joy
  • synonym: faint - feeling weak or likely to lose consciousness
  • antonym: steady - not easily affected by emotion or physical weakness
  • antonym: unmoved - not affected by emotion or excitement
