even so

adverb
nevertheless; in spite of that
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌivən ˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌiːvən ˈsəʊ / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has just been said; nevertheless.

Examples

Even so he tries
He may not have much experience, but even so, he's a quick learner.
She knew it would be risky, but even so, she decided to proceed.
The weather was terrible; even so, the match continued as planned.
