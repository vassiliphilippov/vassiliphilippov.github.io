vintage recording

noun
modern recording made to sound old
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ rɪ.ˈkɔːr.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ rə.ˈkɔːr.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ rɪ.ˈkɔː.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ rə.ˈkɔː.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in music production) a recording made in the present that is intentionally produced to sound like recordings from an earlier era, often using analog equipment, tape saturation, or vinyl-like artifacts.

Examples

Modern vintage recording
The producer wanted a warm, nostalgic sound, so he recorded the album as a vintage recording.
Many listeners assumed the tracks were old, but they were actually modern vintage recordings produced on tape.
To capture the era's charm, the band used analog consoles when making the vintage recording.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authentic vintage recording - a recording actually made in an earlier historical era (not just produced in a vintage style)
  • synonym: retro recording - a recording made to imitate the style or sound of an earlier period
