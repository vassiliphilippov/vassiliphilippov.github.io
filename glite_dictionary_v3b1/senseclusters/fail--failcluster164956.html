fail

verb
stop working or operating (machine, system)
2.2 19/m
Pronunciation: / feÉªl / (AmE), / feÉªl / (BrE)
(of a machine, system, or part) to stop working or operating properly.

Examples

Fail suddenly
If the brakes fail while driving, it can be extremely dangerous.
My refrigerator failed last night and ruined all the food inside.
The old laptop finally failed during the presentation, leaving us without our slides.

Synonyms

  • malfunction
    4.2 1.99/m
    malfunction is a technical verb for a machine not working correctly, while fail is the general word for stopping operation.
  • crash
    3.2 5.49/m
    crash happens when a computer or system suddenly stops working, while fail can be any stop or malfunction.
  • break down
    3.2 8.68/m
    break down describes a machine or system that stops working, while fail is a broader verb for stopping operation.
  • give out
    4.1 1.96/m
    give out means a part or machine becomes worn or used up and stops working, while fail is more general.
  • cut out
    4.1 1.69/m
    cut out is when an engine or device suddenly stops working and may restart, while fail simply means stop operating.
  • die
    4.6 15/m
    die means a machine stops working completely, often permanently, while fail can mean a temporary or partial stop.
  • go down
    3.4 6.18/m
    go down is used for services or systems that stop, often temporarily, while fail covers many kinds of stopping.
  • go bad
    3.5 1.13/m
    go bad usually describes food or goods becoming spoiled, while fail refers to a machine or system stopping working.
