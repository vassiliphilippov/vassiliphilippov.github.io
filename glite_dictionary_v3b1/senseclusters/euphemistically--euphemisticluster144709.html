euphemistically

adverb
using mild or indirect language
9.2
Pronunciation: / juːfəˈmɪstɪkli / (AmE), / juːfəˈmɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that uses or expresses something in a mild or indirect term instead of one that is harsh or blunt.

Examples

Used euphemistically
She euphemistically called her strict diet a 'lifestyle change.'
The company euphemistically described the job cuts as a reorganization.
Their disagreement was euphemistically referred to as a 'difference of opinion.'

Related Terms

  • antonym: bluntly - in a direct and straightforward way, without softening
