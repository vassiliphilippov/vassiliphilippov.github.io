duress

noun
threat or force to compel action
9.4 6.3
Pronunciation: /dʊ.ˈres/ /; / /də.ˈres/ (AmE), /djʊ.ˈres/ /; / /dʒʊ.ˈres/ (BrE)
threats, violence, or other action brought to bear on someone to do something against their will or better judgment

Examples

Under severe duress
He claimed he only agreed to the terms under severe duress.
It was later revealed that his confession was made under duress and could not be considered voluntary.
The contract was signed while she was acting under duress from her employer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats
