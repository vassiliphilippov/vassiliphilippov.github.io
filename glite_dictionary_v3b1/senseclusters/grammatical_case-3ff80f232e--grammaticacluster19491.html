grammatical case

noun
form showing grammatical relationship
3.8
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈmætɪkəl keɪs / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmætɪkəl keɪs / (BrE)
A category in grammar in which nouns, pronouns, or adjectives exhibit different forms to show their relationship to other words in a sentence, often marked by inflection.

Examples

Grammatical case system
English retains some traces of grammatical case in its pronoun system, such as 'he' and 'him'.
Many languages, such as Russian and Latin, use grammatical case to show the role of nouns in sentences.
The concept of grammatical case can be challenging for learners whose native language does not use cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: case - one of the forms a noun, pronoun, or adjective takes to show its role in a sentence
