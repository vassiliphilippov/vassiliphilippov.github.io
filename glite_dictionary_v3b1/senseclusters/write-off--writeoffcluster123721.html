write-off

noun
cancellation of a bad debt or worthless asset
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ɒf / (BrE)
the act or result of removing the value of an asset or debt from accounts because it is no longer valuable or collectible

Examples

A write-off asset
Following the accident, the damaged vehicle was considered a total write-off by the insurance company.
Last year, the bank announced a write-off of its unpaid credit card balances.
The company recorded a large write-off after several customers defaulted on their loans.
After the audit, a large write-off was taken due to uncollectible debts.
The accountant explained the impact of the write-off on the financial statements.
The company recorded a write-off of several million dollars for obsolete inventory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bad debt - a debt unlikely to be collected
  • degree lower: write down - reduce the book value of an asset less severely than a write-off
