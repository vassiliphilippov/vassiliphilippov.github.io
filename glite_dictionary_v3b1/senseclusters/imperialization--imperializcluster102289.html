imperialization

noun
adoption of imperial traits
9.6
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpɪriəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which something adopts the authority, style, or symbols of an empire, often without territorial expansion

Examples

Imperialization of culture
The imperialization of local traditions was evident in the new government ceremonies.
Historians debate the long-term effects of imperialization on cultural identity.
Some critics argue that imperialization can occur even without direct political control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act of establishing control over foreign lands and people
  • antonym: decolonization - the process of a colony gaining independence from imperial control
