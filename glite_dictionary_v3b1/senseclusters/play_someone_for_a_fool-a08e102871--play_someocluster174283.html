play (someone) for a fool

verb
attempt to deceive someone as if they are foolish
Pronunciation: / pleɪ (ˈsʌmwʌn) fɚ ə ful / (AmE), / pleɪ (ˈsʌmwʌn) fə ə fuːl / (BrE)
to try to deceive or take advantage of someone by assuming they are naive or unintelligent

Examples

To play me for a fool
Don’t play me for a fool; I know exactly what’s going on.
He was angry when he realized his friends tried to play him for a fool.
She refused to let her colleagues play her for a fool during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to intentionally make someone believe something that is not true
  • synonym: outsmart - to defeat or trick someone by being more clever
