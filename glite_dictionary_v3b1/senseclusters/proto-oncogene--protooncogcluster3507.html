proto-oncogene

noun
a gene with the potential to cause cancer if altered
7.2 7.7
Pronunciation: /proʊ.tə.ˈɑːŋ.koʊ.dʒiː/ /; / /proʊ.tə.ˈɑːn.koʊ.dʒiː/ /; / /ˈproʊ.tə.ɑːŋ.koʊ.dʒiː/ (AmE), /prəʊ.tə.ˈɒŋ.kəʊ.dʒiː/ /; / /prəʊ.tə.ˈɒn.kəʊ.dʒiː/ /; / /ˈprəʊ.tə.ɒŋ.kəʊ.dʒiː/ (BrE)
a normal gene that can become an oncogene, potentially leading to the development of cancer if mutated or expressed at high levels

Examples

A proto-oncogene mutation
Many cancers are linked to changes in a proto-oncogene within the body's cells.
Researchers study the proto-oncogene to understand how gene mutations can lead to cancer.
When a proto-oncogene becomes mutated, it may contribute to uncontrolled cell growth.
