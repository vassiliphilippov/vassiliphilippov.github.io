hanging wall

noun
upper side of an inclined fault (geology)
Pronunciation: / ˈhæŋɪŋ wɔl / (AmE), / ˈhæŋɪŋ wɔːl / (BrE)
the upper side of an inclined geological fault, lying above the fault plane

Examples

The hanging wall side
Geologists studied the minerals found above the fault's hanging wall.
Movement of the hanging wall relative to the footwall can help identify the type of fault.
The gold vein was located within the hanging wall of the fault.

Related Terms

  • antonym: footwall - the lower side of an inclined geological fault
