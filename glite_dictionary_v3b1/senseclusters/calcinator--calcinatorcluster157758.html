calcinator

noun
device for calcination
Pronunciation: / ˈkælsɪˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkælsɪneɪtə / (BrE)
a furnace or device used to heat substances to high temperatures for calcination

Examples

Laboratory calcinator
The laboratory used a calcinator to process mineral samples.
A calcinator is essential for producing quicklime from limestone in industrial settings.
Technicians monitored the temperature inside the calcinator to ensure proper calcination.
The calcinator ensured the minerals were heated evenly throughout the process.
A skilled calcinator is essential for producing high-quality lime in industrial settings.
Modern factories often use an automated calcinator to improve efficiency and safety.
