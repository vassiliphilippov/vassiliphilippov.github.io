centration

noun
focus on one aspect
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛnˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of focusing attention on a single aspect or point, often ignoring other relevant features

Examples

Childhood centration
In early childhood, centration can cause children to overlook important details in a problem.
The psychologist explained how centration affects decision-making in young children.
Because of centration, the child focused only on the height of the glass, not its width.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distraction - lack of concentration or focus
  • synonym: focus - concentration of attention or effort on something
