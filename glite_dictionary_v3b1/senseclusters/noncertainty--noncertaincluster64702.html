noncertainty

noun
lack of certainty
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɜːrtənti / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɜːtənti / (BrE)
the state of not being certain; a lack of confidence or assurance about something

Examples

The noncertainty of results
There is always some noncertainty when making predictions about the future.
The scientist acknowledged the noncertainty in her experimental results.
Legal decisions often involve a degree of noncertainty due to complex evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - the state of being sure or confident about something
  • synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not certain
