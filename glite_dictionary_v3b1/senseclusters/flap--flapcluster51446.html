flap

verb
move or flutter noisily (esp. in wind)
Pronunciation: / flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
to move or cause to move up and down or back and forth quickly, like wings, arms, or flags

Examples

To flap wings
Several banners flapped loudly in the gusty winds above the stadium.
Sheets of plastic flapped against the fence during the storm.
The sails flapped restlessly as the boat drifted with the current.
Birds often flap their wings vigorously before taking flight.
The flag continued to flap on the pole despite the gentle breeze.
The loose sheet began to flap in the wind as the storm picked up.
Excited, the little boy flapped his arms, pretending to be a plane.
The bird flapped its wings furiously as it tried to take off.
The flag flapped wildly in the strong wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat - strike or move repeatedly, especially wings
  • synonym: flutter - to move quickly and lightly through the air
  • synonym: undulate - move with a smooth, wave-like motion
  • synonym: wave - move back and forth in a swinging motion, e.g. a flag
