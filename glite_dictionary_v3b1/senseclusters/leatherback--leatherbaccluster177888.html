leatherback

noun
large marine turtle with leathery shell
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛðɚˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌbæk / (BrE)
A very large sea turtle with a flexible, leathery shell, found in numerous oceans and recognized as the largest living turtle.

Examples

A leatherback turtle
A mature leatherback can weigh as much as 700 kilograms.
Scientists track leatherback migrations across entire ocean basins.
The leatherback is the only sea turtle without a hard, bony shell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leatherback turtle - the same species as 'leatherback', referring to the turtle Dermochelys coriacea
