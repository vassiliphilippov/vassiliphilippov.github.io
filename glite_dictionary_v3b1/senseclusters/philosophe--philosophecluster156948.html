philosophe

noun
French Enlightenment thinker
9.9
Pronunciation: / fiˈlɑːsəf / (AmE), / fiˈlɒsɒf / (BrE)
a French Enlightenment thinker who advocated reason, science, and progress in society

Examples

The philosophe of France
Voltaire was a leading philosophe of the eighteenth century.
The philosophes believed that reason could improve society and human life.
Many philosophes wrote essays challenging traditional authority and promoting new ideas.
The philosophe Voltaire wrote many works criticizing intolerance and superstition.
During the Enlightenment, a philosophe might gather in Parisian salons to discuss new ideas.
Many philosophes believed that education could improve society for everyone.
