discalced

adjective
barefoot or wearing sandals (religious orders)
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkælst / (AmE), / dɪsˈkælst / (BrE)
(of members of certain religious orders) barefoot or wearing only sandals as a sign of humility or austerity.

Examples

Discalced friars
Only the discalced friars were permitted to enter the chapel barefoot.
The discalced monks lived a life of humility and simplicity.
Their order became known as the discalced Carmelites due to their distinctive lack of footwear.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shod - wearing shoes
