Stachys

noun
genus of woolly or hairy herbs and shrubs
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪkɪs / (AmE), / ˈstakɪs / (BrE)
A large genus of mostly woolly or hairy herbs, subshrubs, or shrubs found in temperate regions of the Eastern Hemisphere and tropical Australasia, including species such as woundwort and lamb's ear.

Examples

Stachys plant species
Botanists discovered a new variety of Stachys in the temperate forests of eastern Asia.
Many gardeners grow Stachys for its attractive, velvety leaves and easy maintenance.
The species Stachys byzantina is widely known as lamb's ear in ornamental gardens.
