guess

noun
an opinion based on little evidence
2.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
an opinion or answer made without enough information or certainty

Examples

A wild guess
If I had to make a guess, I'd say he's around fifty years old.
My guess is that they'll arrive late due to the weather.
She took a wild guess at the answer, hoping she was right.
His guess was surprisingly close to the correct answer.
Let's take a guess at how much the repairs will cost.
Without any evidence, her guess was as good as mine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - something that is definitely true or known
  • synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information
  • synonym: estimate - an approximate calculation or assessment
