non-language

noun
not a true language
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a way of communicating or content that does not use spoken or written language, such as gestures, facial expressions, images, or sounds

Examples

Non-language communication
Facial expressions are often considered a form of non-language communication.
Some researchers study animal calls as examples of non-language systems.
Gestures and body language can be powerful non-language ways to convey meaning.
The presentation included non-language elements like charts and music to support the main points.
Researchers analyzed both non-language cues, such as gestures, and spoken responses in the study.
The museum exhibit features non-language materials, including photographs and sound recordings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: language - a system of communication using words or symbols
  • synonym: nonverbal - not involving or using words or speech
  • synonym: signal - a gesture, action, or sound used to convey information
