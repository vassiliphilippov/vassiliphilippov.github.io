center

verb
direct attention to something
1.5 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.tər/ (AmE), /ˈsen.tə/ (BrE)
to focus attention or activity on something; to make something the main subject; or to place something in the middle

Examples

To center your thoughts
It's important to center your energy when preparing for an important performance.
Let's center our discussion on the most pressing problems for the community.
She tried to center her thoughts on the task ahead instead of worrying too much.
Her research has centered upon climate change and its effects.
Much of the discussion centered on ways to improve education.
The meeting centered around proposals for the new park.
He centred himself on the mat before beginning his meditation.
Please centre the logo on the page before printing.
The microphone should be centred between the two speakers for optimal sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: center - move or place in the center (American spelling)
  • synonym: focus - to direct attention or effort at something in particular
  • synonym: revolve around - to have something as a main subject or purpose
