cryopreservation

noun
freezing for preservation
8.4
Pronunciation: /kraɪ.oʊ.prez.ər.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kraɪ.oʊ.prɪ.zɜːr.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kraɪ.əʊ.prez.ə.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kraɪ.əʊ.prɪ.zə.ˈveɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of preserving cells, tissues, organs, or other biological material by cooling them to very low temperatures to prevent decay

Examples

Cryopreservation of cells
Recent advances in cryopreservation have improved the success rates of organ transplants.
Scientists use cryopreservation to store rare plant seeds for future research.
The fertility clinic offers cryopreservation services for patients undergoing cancer treatment.
Scientists use cryopreservation to store embryos for future fertility treatments.
Advances in cryopreservation have made it possible to preserve organs for transplantation.
Some people choose cryopreservation after death, hoping for revival in the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thawing - the process of warming something that was frozen so it becomes unfrozen
