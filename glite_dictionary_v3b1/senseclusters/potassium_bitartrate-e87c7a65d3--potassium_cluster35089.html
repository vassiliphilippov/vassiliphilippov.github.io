potassium bitartrate

noun
salt used in baking (cream of tartar)
9.7 4.2
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈtæ.siː.əm baɪ.ˈtɑːr.treɪt/ (AmE), /pə.ˈtæ.sɪ.əm baɪ.ˈtɑː.treɪt/ (BrE)
a white, crystalline salt used especially as an ingredient in baking powder and for stabilizing egg whites, also known as cream of tartar

Examples

Potassium bitartrate in baking
Many recipes call for potassium bitartrate to stabilize egg whites in meringue.
The addition of potassium bitartrate to the dough improves the texture of baked goods.
You can find potassium bitartrate listed as an ingredient on containers of baking powder.
