adaptedness

noun
degree of suitability
Pronunciation: / əˈdæptɪdnəs / (AmE), / əˈdæptɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or degree of being well suited or adjusted to a particular environment or purpose

Examples

The adaptedness of species
The adaptedness of a species can determine its long-term survival in changing environments.
Researchers measured the adaptedness of plants growing in the desert compared to those in wetter regions.
High adaptedness allows certain animals to thrive despite harsh climate conditions.
The adaptedness of desert plants allows them to survive with very little water.
Scientists study the adaptedness of animals to understand how they thrive in extreme climates.
High adaptedness increases a species' chances of survival when the environment changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fitness - the ability of an organism to survive and reproduce
  • antonym: maladaptation - a trait that is more harmful than helpful
