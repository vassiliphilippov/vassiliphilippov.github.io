RF

noun
hormone-releasing substance (biology)
Pronunciation: / ɑr ɛf / (AmE), / ɑː ɛf / (BrE)
A substance produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of a specific hormone by the anterior pituitary gland; also called releasing factor.

Examples

RF levels in blood
Researchers measured the levels of RF in the subjects' blood samples.
The discovery of RF revolutionized our understanding of hormonal regulation in the body.
The hypothalamus secretes RF to trigger the release of certain pituitary hormones.
