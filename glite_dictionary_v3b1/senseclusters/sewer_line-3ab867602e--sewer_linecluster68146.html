sewer line

noun
main pipe in a sewage system
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsuː.ɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈsjuː.ə laɪn / (BrE)
a main pipe that carries sewage from buildings to a treatment facility or disposal area

Examples

A broken sewer line
A collapsed sewer line can lead to sewage flooding the street.
The city replaced the aging sewer line to prevent leaks and contamination.
Tree roots can sometimes block a sewer line, causing backups in homes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sewer - an underground conduit for carrying off drainage water and waste matter
