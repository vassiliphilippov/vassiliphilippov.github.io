domo

adverb
Japanese intensifier
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊməʊ / (BrE)
a Japanese adverb used as a polite intensifier meaning 'very' or 'much', often before expressions of thanks or apology

Examples

Domo arigato
He greeted his host with a polite domo before entering the house.
When receiving a gift in Japan, people often say domo to show extra gratitude.
She added domo to her apology to make it sound more sincere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slightly - to a small degree; a little
  • synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely
