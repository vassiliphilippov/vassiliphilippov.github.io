quartz

noun
colorless glass of pure silica
5.3 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / kw…îrts / (AmE), / kw…îÀêts / (BrE)
A colorless glass made of almost pure silica, valued for its clarity and resistance to heat and chemicals.

Examples

Quartz glass
Laboratory equipment often uses quartz because of its high resistance to heat.
Some watches have faces protected by quartz to prevent scratches and damage.
The optical properties of quartz make it ideal for precise scientific instruments.

Synonyms

  • quartz glass
    6.8 0.02/m
    quartz glass is a clear, high-heat resistant material used in labs and industry, while quartz is the general name for that pure silica glass.
  • vitreous silica
    9.2 0.00/m
    vitreous silica is the technical name used in science and industry for high-purity silica glass, while quartz is the more general, common name.

Related Terms
