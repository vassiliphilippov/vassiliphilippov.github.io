blech

verb
to express disgust
9.9
Pronunciation: / blɛk / (AmE), / blɛk / (BrE)
to express disgust or strong dislike by making a sound or exclamation, often 'blech', especially in informal speech.

Examples

To blech at taste
She couldn't help but blech when she tasted the spoiled milk.
Whenever he sees bugs in his food, he loudly blechs in disgust.
The children all bleched at the idea of eating broccoli for dinner.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admire - to regard with respect or approval
  • synonym: retch - to make a sound as if vomiting, often from disgust
