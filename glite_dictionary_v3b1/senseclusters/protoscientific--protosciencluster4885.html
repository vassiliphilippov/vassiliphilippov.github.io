protoscientific

adjective
not fully scientific
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtoʊsaɪənˈtɪfɪk /; / ˌproʊtoʊˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊsaɪənˈtɪfɪk /; / ˌprəʊtəʊˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk / (BrE)
relating to ideas, methods, or practices that existed before modern science or are not fully scientific

Examples

Protoscientific ideas
The ancient theory was considered protoscientific because it lacked experimental evidence.
Many protoscientific ideas paved the way for later scientific discoveries.
His explanation sounded protoscientific, but it was not supported by reliable data.
Alchemy is often considered a protoscientific practice that led to the development of modern chemistry.
The researchers used protoscientific methods before formal experiments were established.
Many ancient observations of the stars were protoscientific and lacked systematic measurement.
Many protoscientific theories laid the groundwork for later scientific discoveries.
Astrology was once regarded as a protoscientific attempt to explain natural phenomena.
The museum displayed artifacts from protoscientific experiments in early chemistry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preliminary - coming before the main or final part
  • synonym: pseudoscientific - falsely presented as scientific but lacking scientific basis
  • antonym: scientific - based on or using the methods of science
