variation

noun
astronomical orbital perturbation
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveəriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Astronomy) A perturbation of the mean motion or orbit of a planet or satellite, especially a disturbance of the Moon's orbit.

Examples

Lunar variation
Astronomers analyzed the variation in the Moon's orbit to predict eclipses more accurately.
Mathematical models account for every known variation affecting satellite trajectories.
The gravitational pull of other planets can cause a variation in Earth's orbital path.
