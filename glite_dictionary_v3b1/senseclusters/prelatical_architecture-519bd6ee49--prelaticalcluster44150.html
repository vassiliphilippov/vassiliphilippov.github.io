prelatical architecture

noun
architecture relating to bishops or church leaders
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛˈlætɪkəl ˈɑrkɪˌtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌprɛˈlætɪkl ˈɑːkɪˌtɛktʃə / (BrE)
architecture that is associated with or characteristic of bishops or church leaders, especially in ecclesiastical buildings

Examples

Prelatical architecture style
Many historians trace the development of prelatical architecture to the influence of powerful bishops in medieval Europe.
She studied the symbolic designs often found in prelatical architecture during her university course.
The old abbey displayed remarkable prelatical architecture with its elaborate arches and grand towers.
