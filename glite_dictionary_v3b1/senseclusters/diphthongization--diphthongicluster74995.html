diphthongization

noun
vowel becoming diphthong
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪf.θɑːŋ.dʒə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪf.θɒŋ.dʒə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a single vowel sound changes into a diphthong in speech

Examples

Diphthongization of vowels
Linguists study diphthongization to understand how vowel sounds change over time.
The diphthongization of certain vowels is common in English dialects.
Some regional accents show more diphthongization than others, especially in stressed syllables.
