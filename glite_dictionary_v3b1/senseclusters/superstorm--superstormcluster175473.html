superstorm

noun
very intense storm
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌstɔːm / (BrE)
an extremely large and powerful storm that causes widespread destruction, or a situation with an overwhelming combination of problems

Examples

A superstorm hits
The superstorm left thousands of people without electricity for days.
Scientists warned that the approaching superstorm could break rainfall records.
After the superstorm, emergency crews worked around the clock to clear debris.
The coastal city was devastated by a superstorm that caused massive flooding and power outages.
After the merger, the company faced a superstorm of legal and financial challenges.
Scientists warn that climate change could make superstorms more frequent in the future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hurricane - a powerful tropical storm with strong winds and heavy rain
  • degree lower: storm - a period of violent weather with wind, rain, or snow
