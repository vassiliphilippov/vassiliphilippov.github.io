central body

noun
region in a cell containing centrioles
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəl ˈbɑːdi / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
A small region of cytoplasm near the nucleus that contains the centrioles and organizes microtubules in a cell.

Examples

The central body in cells
Damage to the central body may disrupt normal cell functions involving the centrioles.
The central body plays a crucial role in cell division by organizing microtubules.
Under the microscope, you can see the central body adjacent to the nucleus in animal cells.
