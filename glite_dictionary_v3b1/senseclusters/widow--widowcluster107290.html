widow

verb
to cause someone to lose a spouse by death
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊ / (BrE)
to make someone a widow or widower by causing the death of their spouse

Examples

The war widowed her
Many communities were devastated as the epidemic widowed numerous women.
The civil war widowed thousands of families within a few years.
The sudden accident widowed her at only twenty-eight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orphan - to make someone an orphan by causing the death of their parents
