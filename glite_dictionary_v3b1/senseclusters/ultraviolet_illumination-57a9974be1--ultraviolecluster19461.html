ultraviolet illumination

noun
ultraviolet-range radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˌɪl.jəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˌɪl.jʊˈmɪəʃ.ən / (BrE)
Radiation in the ultraviolet range, with wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays.

Examples

Ultraviolet illumination light
Museum displays often use ultraviolet illumination to reveal details not seen in normal light.
Plants can suffer damage if exposed to excessive ultraviolet illumination for long periods.
The lab used ultraviolet illumination to detect invisible ink on the documents.

  • degree lower: visible light - electromagnetic radiation visible to the human eye
