deflate

verb
let air or gas out and make collapse
2.1 2.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfleɪt/ /; / /diː.ˈfleɪt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfleɪt/ /; / /diː.ˈfleɪt/ (BrE)
to let air or gas out of something so that it becomes smaller or loses its shape

Examples

To deflate a balloon
After the party, they began to deflate all the decorations.
He had to deflate the tire before repairing the puncture.
When you deflate a balloon, it shrinks and loses its shape.
He accidentally deflated his bike tire while checking the pressure.
Please deflate the air mattress before we pack it away.
The children enjoyed popping and deflating all the balloons after the party.
If you puncture the tire, it will deflate quickly and become unusable.
The party was over, so all the balloons slowly began to deflate.
You should deflate the air mattress before packing it away.
