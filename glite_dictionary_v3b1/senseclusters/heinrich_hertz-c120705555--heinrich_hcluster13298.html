Heinrich Hertz

proper noun
German physicist; pioneer in electromagnetic waves
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪnˌrɪk ˈhɜrts / (AmE), / ˈhaɪnʁɪç ˈhɛəts / (BrE)
(1857–1894) German physicist who was the first to produce and detect electromagnetic waves artificially, confirming the theories of James Clerk Maxwell.
