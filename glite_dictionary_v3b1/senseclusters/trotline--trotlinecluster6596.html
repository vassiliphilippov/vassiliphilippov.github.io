trotline

noun
long fishing line with multiple baited hooks
6.7 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɑːt.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈtrɒt.laɪn/ (BrE)
a long fishing line stretched horizontally, with many shorter lines and baited hooks attached, typically suspended between buoys

Examples

A trotline with hooks
He baited each hook on the trotline before lowering it into the river.
The fishermen checked the trotline early every morning for catfish.
Using a trotline can catch many fish at once in slow-moving waters.
