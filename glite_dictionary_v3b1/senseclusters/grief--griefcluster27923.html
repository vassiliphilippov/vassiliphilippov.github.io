grief

noun
something causing great sadness
5.1
Pronunciation: / ɡriːf / (AmE), / ɡriːf / (BrE)
deep and intense sadness, especially after a loss, or something that causes this feeling

Examples

Deep grief
Losing his childhood home became a lifelong grief for him.
The accident was a lasting grief to the whole family.
The sudden end of her career was a source of deep grief.
Coping with grief can take a long time and requires support.
He was overcome with grief after the passing of his grandmother.
The community shared a profound sense of grief at the loss of their leader.

Related Terms

  • antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness
  • synonym: sorrow - deep distress or sadness caused by loss or disappointment
