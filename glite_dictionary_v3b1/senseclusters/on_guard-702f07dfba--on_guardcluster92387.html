on guard

adjective
vigilant, watchful
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɑn ˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒn ˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
watchful and ready to deal with possible danger, problems, or surprises

Examples

On guard all night
The security team remained on guard all night to ensure our safety.
Travelers should always stay on guard against pickpockets in crowded areas.
We were on guard throughout the meeting in case any problems arose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to danger or problems
  • antonym: off guard - not prepared to deal with something unexpected
