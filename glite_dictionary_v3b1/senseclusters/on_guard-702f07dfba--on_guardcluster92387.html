on guard

adjective
vigilant, watchful
3.5 1.21/m
Pronunciation: / …ën Àà…°…ërd / (AmE), / …ín Àà…°…ëÀêd / (BrE)
watchful and ready to deal with possible danger, problems, or surprises

Examples

On guard all night
The security team remained on guard all night to ensure our safety.
Travelers should always stay on guard against pickpockets in crowded areas.
We were on guard throughout the meeting in case any problems arose.

Synonyms

  • on (one's) guard
    4.6 0.00/m
    on (one's) guard is a fuller idiom often used to stress caution, while on guard is the basic phrase for similar readiness.
  • vigilant
    6.0 4.15/m
    vigilant describes steady, careful watching, while on guard focuses on being ready to act against sudden danger or problems.
  • alert
    3.5 4.72/m
    alert describes a quick state of readiness and attention, while on guard implies a continuous watch and readiness to deal with danger.
  • watchful
    3.2 3.30/m
    watchful means quietly observing for signs of trouble, while on guard adds the idea of preparing to act against danger.
  • on (one's) toes
    6.3 0.00/m
    on (one's) toes is an informal phrase for staying lively and ready for anything, while on guard feels more cautious and protective.
