crusty

adjective
gruff or surly in manner
7.3 0.71/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkrÊŒsti / (AmE), / ËˆkrÊŒsti / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) brusque, surly, or forbidding; easily irritated or gruff.

Examples

Crusty old man
Despite his crusty demeanor, he had a kind heart underneath.
Her crusty remarks sometimes made people uncomfortable, but she was honest.
The crusty old professor snapped at his students for talking during class.

Synonyms

  • gruff
    5.7 1.36/m
    gruff is rough and direct in voice and manner, while crusty is surly and easily irritated or forbidding.
  • ill-humored
    7.0 0.01/m
    ill-humored often shows quick irritation and an unfriendly manner, while crusty is brusque and forbidding in everyday behavior.
  • vinegary
    7.6 0.01/m
    vinegary has a sour, sharp way of speaking and acting, while crusty is more brusque, surly, and easily irritated.
  • crabbed
    8.6 0.01/m
    crabbed means annoyed and bad-tempered in manner, while crusty adds a brusque, forbidding tone that keeps people away.
  • crabby
    5.4 0.64/m
    crabby is quickly in a bad mood and shows small complaints, while crusty is colder and openly surly in manner.
  • cranky
    4.2 3.86/m
    cranky gets easily upset and complains, while crusty sounds rough and forbidding rather than merely moody.
  • crotchety
    8.8 0.33/m
    crotchety is irritable and oddly stubborn in small ways, while crusty is blunt, surly, and more openly forbidding.
  • crankish
    8.3 0.00/m
    crankish shows odd, ill-tempered behavior and strange complaints, while crusty is more simply brusque and surly in manner.

  • antonym: pleasant - friendly and agreeable in manner
