Ramses

proper noun
ancient Egyptian royal name, esp. Ramses II
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈræmˌsiːz / (AmE), / ˈræmˌsiːz / (BrE)
the name of any of eleven kings of ancient Egypt who ruled between about 1315 and 1090 BC, especially Ramses II, one of Egypt's most famous pharaohs

Examples

Ramses the Great
Many historians regard Ramses as one of Egypt's greatest and most powerful pharaohs.
The temples built under Ramses are famous for their grand scale and intricate carvings.
The tomb of Ramses II is considered one of the most impressive in the Valley of the Kings.
