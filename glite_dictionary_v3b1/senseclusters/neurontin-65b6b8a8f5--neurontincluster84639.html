Neurontin

noun
anticonvulsant drug; trade name
8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈnuː.rɑːn.tɪn/ /; / /ˈnjuː.rɑːn.tɪn/ (AmE), /ˈnjuː.rɒn.tɪn/ /; / /ˈnjʊə.rɒn.tɪn/ (BrE)
A trademarked anticonvulsant drug used to control certain types of seizures in epilepsy and to manage nerve pain caused by shingles.

Examples

Neurontin for pain
After her shingles diagnosis, she was given Neurontin for the nerve pain.
My neurologist prescribed Neurontin to help reduce the frequency of my seizures.
Some patients report mild dizziness as a side effect of Neurontin.
