claypan

noun
dense clay soil layer
Pronunciation: / ˈkleɪˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈkleɪˌpæn / (BrE)
a hard, dense layer of soil rich in clay that restricts water movement and root growth

Examples

Soil claypan layer
Farmers often struggle to grow crops in fields with a thick claypan just below the surface.
The construction crew had to break through the claypan before laying the foundation.
Because of the claypan, rainwater pooled on the ground instead of soaking in.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardpan - a hard, compacted layer of soil that is difficult for roots and water to penetrate
  • antonym: topsoil - the upper, fertile layer of soil where most plant roots grow
