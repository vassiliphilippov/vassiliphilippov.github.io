Jules Verne

proper noun
French science fiction writer (1828–1905)
Pronunciation: / ʒuːl vɜrn / (AmE), / ʒuːl vɜːn / (BrE)
(1828–1905) a French writer known as the father of science fiction, whose works include 'Journey to the Center of the Earth' and 'Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas'.

Examples

Jules Verne books
Jules Verne's imaginative journeys inspired generations of explorers and inventors.

Many consider Jules Verne to be the father of science fiction for his innovative adventure novels.
