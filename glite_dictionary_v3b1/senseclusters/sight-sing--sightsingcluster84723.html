sight-sing

verb
sing music at first sight
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪt sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪt sɪŋ / (BrE)
to sing a piece of written music by reading it for the first time, without prior rehearsal

Examples

To sight-sing music
Many music students practice daily to improve their ability to sight-sing.
She can sight-sing even the most complex choral music without preparation.
The choir was asked to sight-sing a new hymn during rehearsal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sight-read - to play or sing music by reading the score for the first time
