oral personality

noun
psychoanalytic personality type from oral stage fixation
6.8 7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəl pɚˌsæn.əˈlɪ.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈɔː.rəl pəˌsæn.əˈlɪ.ti / (BrE)
(In psychoanalysis) a personality type marked by either generous optimism or aggressive selfishness, believed to develop from fixation during the oral stage of childhood development.

Examples

An oral personality type
In psychoanalytic theory, an oral personality develops from unresolved conflicts during infancy.
People with an oral personality may be prone to dependency or excessive optimism.
The therapist suggested that his generosity might be a result of an oral personality.
