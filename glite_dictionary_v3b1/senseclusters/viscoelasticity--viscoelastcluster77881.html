viscoelasticity

noun
material property
9.9
Pronunciation: /vɪs.koʊ.ɪ.læs.ˈtɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /vɪs.kəʊ.ɪ.læs.ˈtɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the property of a material that shows both viscous and elastic behavior when deformed

Examples

The viscoelasticity property
The viscoelasticity of the material allows it to absorb shocks efficiently.
Engineers study viscoelasticity to design better protective gear and building materials.
Rubber exhibits viscoelasticity, making it useful for products that need to stretch and return to shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elasticity - the ability of a material to return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed
  • synonym: viscosity - the thickness or resistance to flow in a liquid or material
