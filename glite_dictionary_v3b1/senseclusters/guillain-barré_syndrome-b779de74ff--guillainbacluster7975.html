Guillain-Barré syndrome

noun
rare nerve disorder causing weakness or paralysis
8.5
Pronunciation: / ɡiːˌjæn bɑːˈreɪ ˌsɪnˈdroʊm / (AmE), / ɡiːˌjæn bærˈeɪ ˌsɪnˈdrəʊm / (BrE)
A rare medical condition in which the body's immune system attacks the peripheral nerves, causing pain, muscle weakness, and sometimes paralysis of the limbs.

Examples

Guillain-Barré syndrome symptoms
Early diagnosis of Guillain-Barré syndrome is crucial for effective treatment and recovery.
He experienced numbness and weakness in his legs, which were later identified as symptoms of Guillain-Barré syndrome.
The patient developed Guillain-Barré syndrome following a mild respiratory infection.
