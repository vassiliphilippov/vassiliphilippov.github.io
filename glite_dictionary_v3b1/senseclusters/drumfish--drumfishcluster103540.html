drumfish

noun
bottom-dwelling fish that makes a drumming noise
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌmˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized fish found in shallow coastal and fresh waters, known for making a drumming noise.

Examples

A drumfish in water
Fishermen often seek drumfish in shallow coastal waters for both food and sport.
Several drumfish were spotted among the rocks at low tide, vibrating the water with their unique drumming noise.
The drumfish is easily recognized by the distinctive sound it produces while swimming near the shore.
