white matter

noun
pale brain/spinal tissue of nerve fibers
9.5 4.1
Pronunciation: /waɪt ˈmæt.ər/ /; / /hwaɪt ˈmæt.ər/ (AmE), /waɪt ˈmæt.ə/ /; / /hwaɪt ˈmæt.ə/ (BrE)
The pale-colored tissue of the central nervous system, consisting mainly of nerve fibers covered in myelin sheaths, responsible for transmitting signals between different areas of the brain and spinal cord.

Examples

The white matter tissue
Damage to the white matter can disrupt communication between different parts of the brain.
Magnetic resonance imaging revealed abnormalities in the patient's white matter.
Scientists are studying how aging affects white matter in the human brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gray matter - the darker tissue of the brain and spinal cord, consisting mainly of nerve cell bodies
