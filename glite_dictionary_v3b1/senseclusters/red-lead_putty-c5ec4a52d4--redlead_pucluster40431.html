red-lead putty

noun
sealing compound for pipe fittings
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛdˌlɛd ˈpʌti / (AmE), / ˌrɛdˌlɛd ˈpʌti / (BrE)
A cement-like substance made by mixing white and red lead with boiled linseed oil, used as a sealing compound on pipe fittings.

Examples

Red-lead putty sealing compound
Because of its composition, red-lead putty is particularly effective on threaded pipe connections.
Plumbers often use red-lead putty to ensure a watertight seal on metal pipes.
The old joints were sealed with red-lead putty to prevent leakage.
