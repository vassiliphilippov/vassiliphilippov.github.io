ecotypic

adjective
of an ecotype
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkoʊˈtɪpɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːkəʊˈtɪpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an ecotype, a genetically distinct population within a species adapted to specific environmental conditions

Examples

Ecotypic variation
The ecotypic variation in these fish allows them to survive in different water temperatures.
Researchers studied the ecotypic traits that help certain plants thrive in arid regions.
Understanding ecotypic differences is important for conserving endangered species.
