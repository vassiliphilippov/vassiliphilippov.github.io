mandamus

noun
court order requiring a duty
6.2 9.9
Pronunciation: /mæn.ˈdeɪ.məs/ (AmE), /mæn.ˈdeɪ.məs/ (BrE)
A legal order from a court commanding a public official or government body to perform a specific duty that is required by law.

Examples

A mandamus order
A mandamus can force officials to fulfill duties required by law when they refuse to act.
After exhausting all other options, the lawyer filed for a writ of mandamus against the agency.
The judge issued a mandamus compelling the city council to hold the election.
