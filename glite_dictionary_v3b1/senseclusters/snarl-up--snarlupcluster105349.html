snarl-up

noun
traffic jam or severe blockage
7.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / sn…ërl  åp / (AmE), / sn…ëÀêl  åp / (BrE)
A situation in which traffic is prevented from moving normally, usually because of vehicles blocking each other; a traffic jam.

Examples

A snarl-up on road
A serious snarl-up delayed our journey by more than an hour.
Rush hour often causes a snarl-up near the city center.
There was a massive snarl-up on the motorway after the accident.

Synonyms

  • traffic jam
    1.0 1.93/m
    a traffic jam is many vehicles stuck or moving slowly on a crowded road, while a snarl-up is when vehicles block each other and stop traffic.
  • congestion
    3.4 3.17/m
    congestion means overcrowding that slows movement in traffic or places, while snarl-up is a pile of blocked vehicles that stops traffic.
