allotropism

noun
existence of an element in different forms
Pronunciation: / əˈlɑːtrəˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈlɒtrəˌpɪzəm / (BrE)
the phenomenon of a chemical element existing in two or more different physical forms, with distinct structures and properties

Examples

Allotropism of carbon
Allotropism explains why carbon can exist as both graphite and diamond.
Modern chemistry textbooks often discuss allotropism when teaching about elements like sulfur and oxygen.
Researchers study allotropism to understand the different properties of materials formed by the same element.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allotropy - the property of an element to exist in more than one form
