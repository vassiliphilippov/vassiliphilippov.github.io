veined

adjective
marked with lines like veins
4.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / veÉªnd / (AmE), / veÉªnd / (BrE)
having lines, streaks, or markings that resemble veins, often used to describe leaves, marble, or similar surfaces

Examples

Veined marble surface
His hands were thin and veined after years of hard work.
She admired the veined leaves, their patterns glowing in the sunlight.
The marble countertop was beautifully veined with grey and gold.

Synonyms

  • veinlike
    6.8 0.01/m
    veinlike describes markings that look like narrow veins across a surface, while veined is a general adjective for such streaks.
  • veinous
    8.2 0.00/m
    veinous describes something having many veins or relating to veins in plants, while veined focuses on visible vein-like streaks.
  • venose
    9.9 0.00/m
    venose means marked with vein-like lines often on leaves or wings, while veined is a more general term for streaked surfaces.
  • veinal
    9.9 0.00/m
    veinal refers to plant veins or their pattern in tissues, while veined is used for any surface with vein-like streaks.
  • veiny
    4.3 0.01/m
    veiny looks informal and often means having thick or visible veins, while veined is neutral and describes any vein-like markings.
  • marbled
    4.4 0.02/m
    marbled shows streaks or swirls like marble stone, while veined simply means having narrow streaks that resemble veins.
