arrange

verb
set printed text into a format
1.8 1.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈreɪndʒ/ (AmE), /ə.ˈreɪndʒ/ (BrE)
to put things, ideas, or information in a particular order or position, often to make them neat, attractive, or ready for presentation

Examples

To arrange the books
Can you arrange this report so it fits on two pages?
She needs to arrange the text for printing by tomorrow.
The editor will arrange the chapters for the book layout.
She carefully arranged the flowers in the vase to make a beautiful centerpiece.
The desserts were arranged on the tray to look especially tempting.
The stylist arranged his hair for the photo shoot.
I need to arrange my notes before the meeting starts.
She arranged her thoughts before giving the presentation.
We are arranging our schedule for next week's conference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disorder - to throw into confusion or lack of order
  • antonym: disorganize - to throw into disorder or confusion
  • synonym: format - to arrange or organize written text or data into a particular layout
  • synonym: organize - to arrange systematically; to put in order
  • antonym: scatter - to throw or spread randomly
