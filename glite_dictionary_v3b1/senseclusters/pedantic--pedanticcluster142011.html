pedantic

adjective
overly concerned with minor details or rules
8.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈdæntɪk / (AmE), / pɪˈdantɪk / (BrE)
Excessively concerned with minor details or rules, or displaying learning in a showy or overly scholarly way.

Examples

A pedantic person
He can sometimes seem pedantic when he insists on correcting every small factual error.
Her pedantic approach to grammar annoyed her classmates during the writing workshop.
The manual was filled with pedantic explanations that made it difficult to read.

Related Terms

  • synonym: didactic - intended to teach, especially in a patronizing way
