humble

verb
make someone feel less important or proud
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌmbəl / (BrE)
to make someone feel less important or proud; to lower someone's dignity or pride

Examples

To humble an opponent
Being defeated in the chess tournament really humbled him.
She was humbled by the generous praise she received from her mentors.
Sometimes life has a way of humbling those who become too proud.
His achievements failed to humble his pride until he faced true adversity.
The loss was enough to humble even the most confident athlete.
Traveling in such poor conditions can really humble a person.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embarrass - to make someone feel awkward or self-conscious
  • antonym: exalt - to lift up in rank, power, or character; to praise highly
  • synonym: humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed or foolish by injuring their dignity
