virtuous

adjective
morally good or righteous
4.3 4.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ùÀêt Éu…ôs / (AmE), / Ààv…úÀêt Éu…ôs / (BrE)
having or showing high moral standards; morally good or righteous.

Examples

Virtuous leader
He was respected as a virtuous leader who always tried to do what was right.
It is not always easy to remain virtuous when faced with difficult choices.
The novel's virtuous heroine sacrifices her happiness for the good of others.

Synonyms

  • good
    1.0 173/m
    good is a broad everyday word for being kind or right in many ways, while virtuous stresses strong moral standards.
  • honorable
    3.7 11/m
    honorable is seen as deserving respect for fair actions in public life, while virtuous means having high personal moral standards.
  • righteous
    4.7 11/m
    righteous follows strict moral rules and can sound judgmental, while virtuous is a general state of moral goodness and right conduct.
  • noble
    3.6 11/m
    noble shows high moral qualities with dignity and public respect, while virtuous focuses on everyday moral goodness and right actions.
  • saintly
    4.2 1.13/m
    saintly seems almost perfect in goodness and often has a religious feel, while virtuous means being morally good in life.
  • moral
    3.7 82/m
    moral describes principles of right and wrong or a lesson, while virtuous describes a person who acts with high moral standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: righteous - morally right or justifiable
  • antonym: wicked - morally bad or evil
