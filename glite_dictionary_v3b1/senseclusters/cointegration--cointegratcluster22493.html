cointegration

noun
long-term statistical link
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊɪntɪɡˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊɪntɪɡˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical property where two or more time series show a long-term relationship, moving together over time despite short-term differences

Examples

Strong cointegration relationship
Researchers found strong cointegration between the prices of oil and natural gas over several decades.
The concept of cointegration is important in analyzing economic time series data.
Without cointegration, the relationship between the two variables would not persist in the long run.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correlation - a statistical relationship between two variables
  • antonym: independence - the state of variables not affecting each other
