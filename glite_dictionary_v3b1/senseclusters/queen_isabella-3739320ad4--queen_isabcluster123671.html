Queen Isabella

proper noun
queen of Castile (1451–1504), spouse of Ferdinand II
6.2
Pronunciation: /kwiːn ɪ.zə.ˈbel.ə/ (AmE), /kwiːn ɪ.zə.ˈbel.ə/ (BrE)
(1451–1504) The queen of Castile whose marriage to Ferdinand of Aragon began the unification of Spain; known for instituting the Spanish Inquisition and sponsoring Columbus's voyages.

Examples

Queen Isabella of Castile
Queen Isabella and Ferdinand are known as the Catholic Monarchs for their role in Spanish history.
It was Queen Isabella who ultimately agreed to finance Columbus's famous voyage in 1492.
Many historians credit Queen Isabella for initiating major reforms in Castile during her reign.

  • synonym: Ferdinand II of Aragon - King of Aragon and husband of Queen Isabella
  • synonym: Spanish Inquisition - Judicial institution established by Isabella and Ferdinand
