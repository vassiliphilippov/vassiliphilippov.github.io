deleveraging

noun
reducing debt
Pronunciation: / diːˈlɛvərɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈlɛvərɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of reducing debt by paying off loans or selling assets to improve financial stability

Examples

Corporate deleveraging
After the economic downturn, many companies focused on deleveraging to strengthen their balance sheets.
The bank's deleveraging strategy involved selling off non-core assets to reduce its liabilities.
Households began deleveraging by paying down credit card debt and avoiding new loans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: borrowing - the act of taking money with the intention to pay it back later
  • synonym: repayment - the act of paying back money borrowed
