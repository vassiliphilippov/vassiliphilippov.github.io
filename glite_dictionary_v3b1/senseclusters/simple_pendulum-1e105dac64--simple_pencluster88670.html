simple pendulum

noun
idealized pendulum in physics
5.0 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪm.pəl ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ /; / /ˈsɪm.pəl ˈpɪn.dʒə.ləm/ (AmE), /ˈsɪm.pəl ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ /; / /ˈsɪm.pəl ˈpɪn.dʒə.ləm/ (BrE)
an idealized pendulum consisting of a point mass suspended by a weightless, frictionless thread of constant length, used in scientific models and experiments

Examples

A simple pendulum model
A simple pendulum is an effective way to demonstrate basic principles of harmonic motion.
Students set up a simple pendulum in the lab to study oscillatory motion.
The period of a simple pendulum depends on its length and gravity, not its mass.
