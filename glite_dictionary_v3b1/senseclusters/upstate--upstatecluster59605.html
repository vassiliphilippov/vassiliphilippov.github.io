upstate

adverb
in or toward the northern parts of a state
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ʌpˈsteɪt / (BrE)
in or toward the northern or inland parts of a state, especially as contrasted with the more urban, southern, or coastal areas.

Examples

Move upstate for work
After college, she decided to move upstate in search of new opportunities.
Many families spend their summers upstate to enjoy the countryside and lakes.
Traffic is always heavy on weekends when people head upstate for vacations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downstate - in or toward the southern or main metropolitan parts of a state
