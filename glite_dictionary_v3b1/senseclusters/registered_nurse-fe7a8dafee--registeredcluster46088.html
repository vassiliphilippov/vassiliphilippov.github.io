registered nurse

noun
nurse licensed and registered to practice
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdʒɪstərd nɜrs / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪstəd nɜːs / (BrE)
a nurse who has completed the required training and passed licensing exams to be officially registered and authorized to practice

Examples

Registered nurse license
After graduating and passing her board exams, she became a registered nurse at the local hospital.
Many registered nurses work in specialized departments such as emergency or pediatrics.
The job posting requires applicants to be a licensed registered nurse with at least two years of experience.
