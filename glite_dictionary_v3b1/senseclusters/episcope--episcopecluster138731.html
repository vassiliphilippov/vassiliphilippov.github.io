episcope

noun
opaque object projector
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪskəʊp / (BrE)
a device that uses reflected light to project or display images of opaque objects, often for viewing details on a screen or through a microscope

Examples

An episcope device
The science teacher used an episcope to show the details of a coin to the whole class.
Before digital projectors, an episcope was often used to display book pages to audiences.
The museum demonstrated how an episcope could project artwork onto a large screen for study.
The scientist used an episcope to examine the surface details of the mineral sample.
With the help of an episcope, students could observe the texture of coins and leaves clearly.
The museum technician relied on an episcope to study the paint layers on the old artifact.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microscope - an instrument used to view very small objects
  • synonym: overhead projector - a device that projects images of transparencies onto a screen
  • antonym: projector - a device that projects images onto a surface
  • synonym: slide projector - a device that projects photographic slides onto a screen
