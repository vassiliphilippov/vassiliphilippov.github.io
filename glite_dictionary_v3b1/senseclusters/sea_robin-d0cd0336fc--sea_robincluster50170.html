sea robin

noun
a marine fish with winglike fins
8.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ëˆsi ËŒrÉ‘b.Éªn / (AmE), / Ëˆsi ËŒrÉ’b.Éªn / (BrE)
A bottom-dwelling marine fish of the family Triglidae, especially found in bays and estuaries, known for its bony head and winglike pectoral fins.

Examples

A sea robin fish
A sea robin can often be seen gliding along the sandy bottoms near the shore.
Some anglers release sea robins because of their firm, bony bodies and unusual appearance.
The fisherman was surprised to find a sea robin in his net along with flounder and crabs.
