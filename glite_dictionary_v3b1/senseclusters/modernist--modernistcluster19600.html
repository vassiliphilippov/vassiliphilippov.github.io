modernist

noun
artist or thinker who rejects traditional styles
4.4 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑːr.də.nɪst/ (AmE), /ˈmɒd.ə.nɪst/ (BrE)
an artist, writer, or thinker who intentionally rejects traditional styles or ideas, especially associated with the Modernism movement in the arts and literature of the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

A famous modernist artist
As a devoted modernist, she experimented boldly with new materials and forms in her sculptures.
Being a modernist, he rejected the classical techniques in favor of abstraction and innovation.
The gallery is featuring works by several leading modernists of the early twentieth century.

Related Terms
