baffler

noun
one who confuses
Pronunciation: / ˈbæflər / (AmE), / ˈbæflə / (BrE)
a person or thing that confuses or is very difficult to understand

Examples

A real baffler
The magician was a true baffler, leaving the audience puzzled after every trick.
Her explanation was such a baffler that no one understood what she meant.
The new software update is a real baffler for many users.
The final question on the exam was a real baffler for most students.
That riddle is such a baffler that nobody in the group could solve it.
The instructions for the new device turned out to be a complete baffler.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarifier - someone or something that makes things clear
  • synonym: confuser - someone or something that causes confusion
  • synonym: enigma - a person or thing that is mysterious or puzzling
  • synonym: mystery - something that is difficult to understand or explain
  • antonym: solution - an answer to a problem or puzzle
