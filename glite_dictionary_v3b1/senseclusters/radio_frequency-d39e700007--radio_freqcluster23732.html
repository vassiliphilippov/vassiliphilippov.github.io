radio frequency

noun
the range of electromagnetic wave frequencies used for radio transmission
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
an electromagnetic wave frequency between the audio and infrared ranges, typically used for radio communication.

Examples

Radio frequency waves
Certain cordless phones can interfere with other devices operating on the same radio frequency.
Modern communication systems rely heavily on radio frequency technology.
The radio frequency spectrum is regulated by government agencies in many countries.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: audio frequency - the frequency range audible to the human ear
  • degree higher: infrared - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light but shorter than microwave radiation
