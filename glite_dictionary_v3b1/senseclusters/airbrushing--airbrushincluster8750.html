airbrushing

noun
deliberate alteration
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌbrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeəˌbrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of altering or enhancing images (especially photographs) to improve appearance or remove unwanted details

Examples

Photo airbrushing
The magazine was criticized for its excessive airbrushing of celebrity photos.
Some historians argue that airbrushing uncomfortable facts from the record distorts our understanding of the past.
The politician denied any airbrushing of his official biography.
The magazine was criticized for excessive airbrushing of celebrities on its cover.
With modern software, airbrushing imperfections from photos has become much easier.
Some artists use airbrushing to create smooth gradients and realistic effects in their paintings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: censorship - the suppression or removal of information considered objectionable
  • synonym: editing - the process of making changes to text, images, or recordings
  • synonym: retouching - the act of improving or repairing a photo or image
  • antonym: revealing - making something known or visible
