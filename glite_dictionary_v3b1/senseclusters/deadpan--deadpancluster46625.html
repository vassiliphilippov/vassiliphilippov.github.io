deadpan

verb
to joke seriously
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈded.pæn/ (AmE), /ˈded.pæn/ (BrE)
to say or do something in a deliberately expressionless or emotionless way, especially to create humor

Examples

To deadpan a joke
He managed to deadpan a hilarious comment without even cracking a smile.
During the interview, she deadpanned a joke that caught everyone off guard.
The comedian often deadpans his punchlines, making them even funnier.
He deadpanned the punchline so well that it took a moment for the audience to laugh.
She deadpanned her response, making it hard to tell if she was joking.
During the meeting, he deadpanned a comment that left everyone confused.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emote - to show emotion while speaking
  • antonym: smirk - to smile in a self-satisfied or knowing way
