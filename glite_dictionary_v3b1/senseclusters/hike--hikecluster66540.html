hike

verb
increase sharply (esp. price, rate)
Pronunciation: / haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
to increase something, especially by a large or sudden amount

Examples

To hike prices
Protests erupted when the government hiked fuel prices overnight.
The city decided to hike parking fees by 20% next year.
Utility companies sometimes hike their rates during the winter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cut - to reduce or decrease something
  • synonym: raise - to cause to increase in amount, level, or strength
