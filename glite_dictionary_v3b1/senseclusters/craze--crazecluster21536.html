craze

verb
develop fine cracks (esp. ceramics)
Pronunciation: / kreɪz / (AmE), / kreɪz / (BrE)
to develop a fine network of cracks on the surface of a material, especially ceramics, glazes, paints, or plastics

Examples

Glaze crazes after firing
Improper cooling can cause a ceramic glaze to craze unexpectedly.
Some collectors value pottery that has started to craze because of its unique appearance.
The antique vase began to craze after years of display in the sunlight.
The old ceramic vase started crazing after years of exposure to sunlight.
If the glaze is not applied correctly, the surface may craze and lose its smooth finish.
The paint on the antique sign has crazed, giving it a distinctive aged look.
