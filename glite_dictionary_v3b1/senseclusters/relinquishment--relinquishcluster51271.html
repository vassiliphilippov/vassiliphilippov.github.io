relinquishment

noun
formal act of giving up a claim or right
3.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪŋkwɪʃmənt /; / rəˈlɪŋkwɪʃmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪŋkwɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving up, surrendering, or abandoning a claim, right, position, or responsibility

Examples

The relinquishment of rights
After hours of negotiation, she finally signed the relinquishment of her rights to the property.
His public relinquishment of the throne shocked the nation.
The company's relinquishment of its patent allowed competitors to enter the market.
Her relinquishment of control allowed others to step up and lead the project.
Negotiations focused on the relinquishment of nuclear weapons by the two countries.
The relinquishment of his claim to the land was signed before witnesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abdication - the act of formally renouncing a throne or power
  • antonym: claim - a demand for something as rightful or due
  • synonym: renunciation - the formal rejection of something, typically a belief, claim, or course of action
  • antonym: retention - the continued possession or control of something
