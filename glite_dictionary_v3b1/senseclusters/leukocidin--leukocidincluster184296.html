leukocidin

noun
bacterial toxin
9.9
Pronunciation: / luːˈkɑːsɪdɪn / (AmE), / luːˈkəʊsɪdɪn / (BrE)
a toxin produced by certain bacteria that destroys white blood cells

Examples

Leukocidin toxin
Some strains of Staphylococcus bacteria produce leukocidin, making infections more dangerous.
Researchers studied how leukocidin attacks and destroys white blood cells in the body.
The presence of leukocidin can lead to more severe symptoms in bacterial infections.

  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms
