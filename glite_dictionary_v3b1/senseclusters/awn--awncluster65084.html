awn

noun
bristlelike appendage on grasses
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɔn / (AmE), / ɔːn / (BrE)
a slender, bristlelike extension growing from the bracts of grasses and some cereal plants

Examples

A long awn
The barley plant has a long awn extending from each seed.
Wheat varieties without a prominent awn are easier to handle during harvest.
You can often see a fine awn protruding from the heads of wild grasses.
