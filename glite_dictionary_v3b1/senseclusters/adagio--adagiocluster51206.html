adagio

noun
slow, skillful ballet section
Pronunciation: / əˈdɑdʒioʊ / (AmE), / əˈdɑːdʒiəʊ / (BrE)
a slow section of a pas de deux in ballet that requires great skill and strength from the dancers

Examples

Ballet adagio section
During the adagio, the male dancer demonstrated incredible strength as he lifted his partner effortlessly.
Her favorite part of the ballet was the adagio, where the music slowed and the emotion became more intense.
The dancers performed the adagio with remarkable grace and perfect synchronization.

  • antonym: allegro - a fast section or movement in music or dance
