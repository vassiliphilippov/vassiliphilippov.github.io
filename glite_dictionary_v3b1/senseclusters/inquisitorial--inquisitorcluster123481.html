inquisitorial

adjective
relating to searching or prying questioning
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkwɪzəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
having a tendency to question closely or harshly, especially in a manner resembling an inquisitor; characterized by prying or searching investigation.

Examples

Inquisitorial questioning
Her inquisitorial questions made it clear she doubted his explanation.
The committee conducted an inquisitorial review of the company’s finances.
The reporter’s inquisitorial tone made the politician visibly uncomfortable during the interview.

  • synonym: interrogative - having the quality of questioning or investigating
