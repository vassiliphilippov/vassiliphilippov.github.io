contemnor

noun
person defying court
9.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈtem.nər/ (AmE), /kən.ˈtem.nə/ /; / /kɒn.ˈtem.nə/ (BrE)
a person who is found guilty of disobeying or showing disrespect to a court or its orders

Examples

A contemnor in court
The judge warned that anyone who disrupted the proceedings could be named a contemnor.
After refusing to comply with the court's order, she was declared a contemnor.
The lawyer explained the consequences of being labeled a contemnor to his client.
The judge declared him a contemnor for refusing to comply with the court's order.
In old legal documents, a contemnor is someone who disrespects the authority of the court.
He was labeled a contemnor after openly mocking the council's decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defendant - a person accused or sued in a court of law
  • synonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act
  • antonym: respecter - a person who shows respect
  • synonym: scorner - a person who mocks or shows contempt
