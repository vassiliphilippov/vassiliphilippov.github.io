Jacobian

noun
matrix determinant
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈkoʊbiən / (AmE), / dʒəˈkəʊbiən / (BrE)
the determinant of the Jacobian matrix, used to measure how a transformation changes area or volume in mathematics

Examples

The Jacobian determinant
To change variables in the integral, you need to calculate the Jacobian of the transformation.
The Jacobian helps determine how much an area stretches or shrinks under a mapping.
In multivariable calculus, the Jacobian is essential for understanding coordinate transformations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Jacobian matrix - a matrix of all first-order partial derivatives of a vector-valued function
  • synonym: determinant - a scalar value calculated from a square matrix
