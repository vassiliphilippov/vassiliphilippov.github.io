preconfirmation

noun
provisional approval
7.3
Pronunciation: /priː.kɑːn.fər.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.kɑːn.fər.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /priː.kən.fər.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.kɒn.fə.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.kɒn.fə.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /priː.kən.fə.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a message or action that confirms something in advance, before it is officially approved or finalized

Examples

A preconfirmation message
The company sent a preconfirmation email to let the applicant know their documents were received.
A preconfirmation does not guarantee final approval, but it is a positive sign.
Before the official contract, the client received a preconfirmation of their reservation.
We received a preconfirmation email with the hotel details before the official booking was finalized.
The travel agency sent a preconfirmation to let us know our reservation was being processed.
A preconfirmation is often used to reassure customers before the final agreement is made.
The airline requires preconfirmation of your flight details at least 24 hours before departure.
Without preconfirmation, your appointment may be given to another client.
Please send a preconfirmation email to secure your spot in the workshop.
