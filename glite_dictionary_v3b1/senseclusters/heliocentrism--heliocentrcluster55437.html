heliocentrism

noun
Sun-centered theory
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːliəˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːliəˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that the Sun is at the center of the solar system and the planets revolve around it

Examples

Heliocentrism theory
The acceptance of heliocentrism marked a major turning point in the history of science.
Galileo supported heliocentrism despite strong opposition from religious authorities.
Many textbooks now explain the basics of heliocentrism in their astronomy chapters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Copernican theory - the model proposing the Sun at the center of the solar system
  • antonym: geocentrism - the theory that the Earth is at the center of the universe
