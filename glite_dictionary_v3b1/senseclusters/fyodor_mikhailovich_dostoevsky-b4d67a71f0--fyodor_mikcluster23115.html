Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky

proper noun
Russian novelist and psychologist of human nature
Pronunciation: / ˈfjɔdər mɪˌxaɪləˌvɪtʃ ˌdɑstoʊˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˈfjɔːdɔː mɪˌxajləˌvɪtʃ ˌdɒstəˈjɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) Russian novelist known for exploring human psychology and suffering in works such as 'Crime and Punishment' and 'The Brothers Karamazov'.
