split ticket

noun
ballot selecting candidates from different parties
7.1 4.7
Pronunciation: /splɪt ˈtɪk.ət/ /; / /splɪt ˈtɪk.ɪt/ (AmE), /splɪt ˈtɪk.ɪt/ (BrE)
A ballot cast by a voter who selects candidates from more than one political party in the same election.

Examples

Cast a split ticket
He decided to cast a split ticket in the election, supporting both Republican and Democratic candidates.
In recent years, the number of people casting a split ticket has decreased.
Voters who choose a split ticket may want to emphasize individual qualities over party loyalty.
