sclerose

verb
to harden abnormally
9.9
Pronunciation: / skləˈroʊs / (AmE), / skləˈrəʊz / (BrE)
to become or make tissue or an organ hard or stiff, often because of disease or abnormal changes

Examples

Tissue scleroses
If left untreated, the tissue may sclerose and lose its normal function.
Doctors monitor patients for signs that the arteries could sclerose over time.
Certain diseases can cause organs to sclerose, leading to serious health problems.
Repeated injury can sclerose the affected tissue, making movement more difficult.
Doctors warned that the disease might sclerose the patient's arteries over time.
If left untreated, chronic inflammation could sclerose the organ and impair its function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harden - to become or make hard or harder
  • antonym: soften - to become or make less hard or firm
