jook house

noun
Southern U.S. roadside venue with food, drinks, and music
6.4
Pronunciation: / dʒʊk haʊs / (AmE), / dʒʊk haʊs / (BrE)
A small roadside establishment in the southeastern United States where food, drinks, and dancing to jukebox music are offered, especially in African-American communities.

Examples

Old jook house
Local musicians often performed live at the old jook house before it closed down.
The documentary explores the cultural history of the Southern jook house scene.
They spent the evening dancing at the jook house on the edge of town.
