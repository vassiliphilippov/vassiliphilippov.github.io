hydrothermal

adjective
using hot water
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊˈθɜrməl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈθɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to the use of hot water, especially under pressure, to synthesize or process materials such as crystals.

Examples

Hydrothermal process
The hydrothermal process is commonly used to grow synthetic quartz crystals for electronic devices.
Scientists studied hydrothermal vents on the ocean floor to understand unique ecosystems.
A hydrothermal method can produce high-purity materials for use in advanced batteries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geothermal - relating to the heat produced inside the earth
