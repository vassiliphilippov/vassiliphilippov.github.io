detune

verb
to make out of tune
6.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈtuːn/ /; / /dɪ.ˈtjuːn/ /; / /diː.ˈtuːn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈtjuːn/ /; / /diː.ˈtjuːn/ (BrE)
to change the pitch or tuning of a musical instrument so that it is not in standard tuning

Examples

To detune a guitar
The band decided to detune their guitars for a heavier sound.
If you detune the piano, it will sound strange and unpleasant.

The band decided to detune their guitars to create a heavier sound for the new song.
Some musicians detune their instruments to experiment with unique tones and effects.
He had to detune the piano slightly to match the old recording's pitch.
