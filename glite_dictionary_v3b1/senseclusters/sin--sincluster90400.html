sin

noun
trigonometric function (opposite/hypotenuse)
Pronunciation: / sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
(in trigonometry) the ratio of the length of the side opposite a given angle to the length of the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle.

Examples

Sin of angle
He explained how the sin function relates angles to ratios in a right-angled triangle.
In mathematics, sin 90° is equal to 1.
To find the height of the tree, use the sin of the angle and the distance from the base.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cosine - trigonometric function: ratio of adjacent side to hypotenuse
  • synonym: tangent - trigonometric function: ratio of opposite side to adjacent side
