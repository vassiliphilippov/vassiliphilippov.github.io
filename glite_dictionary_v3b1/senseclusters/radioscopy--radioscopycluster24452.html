radioscopy

noun
examination with X-rays or penetrating radiation
3.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /reɪ.dɪ.ˈɑː.skə.piː/ (AmE), /reɪ.dɪ.ˈɒ.skə.piː/ (BrE)
examination of the internal structure of opaque objects using X-rays or other penetrating radiation, especially for medical diagnosis

Examples

Radioscopy with X-rays
Advancements in radioscopy have greatly improved the detection of internal injuries.
Safety protocols are strictly followed during radioscopy to minimize radiation exposure.
The doctor used radioscopy to examine the patient's lungs for any signs of abnormality.
