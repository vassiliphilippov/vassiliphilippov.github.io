introjected

adjective
unconsciously incorporated into the psyche
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈdʒɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdʒɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of an idea, attitude, or quality) unconsciously incorporated into one's own mind or psyche, especially through internalization of external influences.

Examples

An introjected feeling
His sense of guilt was an introjected response to his parents' strict moral code.
She realized that much of her anxiety came from introjected expectations learned in childhood.
Therapists help clients examine introjected beliefs that might be holding them back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internalized - (of beliefs, norms, etc.) made part of one's own internal understanding or psyche
