soliton wave

noun
self-reinforcing solitary wave in nonlinear systems
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊlɪˌtɑn weɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪtɒn weɪv / (BrE)
A self-reinforcing, solitary wave packet that maintains its shape while it travels at constant speed, typically found in nonlinear systems.

Examples

A soliton wave packet
A soliton wave does not disperse or lose energy as it moves through a nonlinear medium.
Researchers observed a soliton wave traveling undisturbed through the optical fiber.
The concept of a soliton wave is important in fields like fluid dynamics and quantum physics.
