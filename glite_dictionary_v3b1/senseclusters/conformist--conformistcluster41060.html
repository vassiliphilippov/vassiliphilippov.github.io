conformist

noun
person who conforms to accepted norms
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
a person who conforms to accepted rules, standards, or customs, especially in matters of conduct or religious practices

Examples

A conformist person
He was considered a conformist who rarely questioned authority.
In religious communities, a conformist typically follows established rituals and beliefs.
Most members of the group were conformists unwilling to challenge social norms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonconformist - a person who does not conform to accepted standards or customs
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports or upholds tradition
