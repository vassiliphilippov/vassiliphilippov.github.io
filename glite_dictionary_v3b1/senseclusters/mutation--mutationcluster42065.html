mutation

noun
change in genetic material
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /mjuː.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mjuː.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a permanent change in the genetic material (DNA) of an organism, often resulting in new traits or characteristics

Examples

A mutation in DNA
Radiation can cause a mutation in an organism’s DNA, leading to unexpected traits.
Some genetic disorders are the result of a single mutation in the genome.
The scientist studied the mutation that made the bacteria resistant to antibiotics.
Some genetic diseases arise from a single mutation in the DNA sequence.
The scientist observed a mutation in the bacteria after exposing them to radiation.
The sudden mutation of traditional customs shocked the older generation.
