globalism

noun
worldwide integration policy
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈgloʊ.bəl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈgləʊ.bəl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief in or support for increasing worldwide connections, integration, and interdependence among countries, especially in trade, culture, and politics

Examples

Globalism in politics
Many politicians debate the benefits and drawbacks of globalism in today's interconnected world.
Critics of globalism argue that it can harm local industries and cultures.
Supporters believe that globalism leads to greater cooperation and economic growth among nations.
The rise of globalism has led to increased cultural exchange between countries.
Some politicians argue that globalism threatens local industries and traditions.
Advocates of globalism believe that international cooperation benefits everyone.
Some politicians criticize globalism for threatening local jobs and industries.
The debate over globalism often centers on the balance between international cooperation and national sovereignty.
Supporters of globalism argue that it leads to economic growth and cultural exchange.

Related Terms

  • synonym: globalization - the process of increasing worldwide integration and interdependence
  • antonym: nationalism - the belief in promoting the interests of one's own nation over others
