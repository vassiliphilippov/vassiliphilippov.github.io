praelector

noun
degree-presenting official
Pronunciation: / prəˈlɛktər / (AmE), / prəˈlɛktə / (BrE)
an official at a university or college who presents or announces candidates and reads or recites during ceremonies

Examples

The praelector announces graduates
The praelector called each graduate's name during the commencement ceremony.
As praelector, she had the honor of presenting the candidates to the university president.
Traditionally, the praelector wears academic robes while performing official duties.
The praelector announced the names of the graduates during the ceremony.
At the university's annual event, the praelector read the official proclamation.
Each college appoints a praelector to perform ceremonial duties at formal gatherings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chancellor - the head of a university, often presiding over graduation ceremonies
  • synonym: lecturer - a person who gives lectures, especially at a university
  • synonym: presenter - a person who formally introduces or presents someone or something
  • synonym: reader - a person who reads aloud or a university academic rank
