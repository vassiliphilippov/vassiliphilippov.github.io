faintness

noun
quality of being barely perceptible
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪntnəs /; / ˈfeɪnt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪntnɪs /; / ˈfeɪnt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being barely perceptible, dim, or weak, especially in sound, light, or appearance

Examples

Faintness of the sound
He was startled by the faintness of the knock at the door.
The faintness of the distant music made it sound almost like a dream.
There was a faintness in her voice that made it hard to hear her words.
Artists sometimes use faintness to create a sense of mystery or distance in their work.
The faintness of the sound made it hard to tell where it was coming from.
There was a certain faintness to the light in the old attic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brightness - the quality of being bright or giving out or reflecting much light
  • synonym: dullness - the quality of being lacking in liveliness, vividness, or sharpness
  • antonym: loudness - the quality of being loud
