accessory fruit

noun
fruit with extra fleshy tissue beyond ovary
5.4
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛsəri frut / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri ˈfruːt / (BrE)
A fruit in which much of the fleshy part is derived from tissues other than the ripened ovary, as in apples or strawberries.

Examples

An accessory fruit example
Strawberries are often discussed in botany classes as a classic accessory fruit.
The apple is an example of an accessory fruit because its flesh develops from the receptacle, not just the ovary.
Unlike a true berry, an accessory fruit includes tissues derived from flower parts other than the ovary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: true fruit - fruit in which the edible part comes only from the ovary
