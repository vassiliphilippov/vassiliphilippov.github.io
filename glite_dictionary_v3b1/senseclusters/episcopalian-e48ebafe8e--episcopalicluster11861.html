Episcopalian

noun
member of the Episcopal Church
Pronunciation: / ɪˌpɪskəˈpeɪliən / (AmE), / ɪˌpɪskəˈpeɪliən / (BrE)
A member of the Episcopal Church, especially in the United States, or more generally someone who adheres to episcopal forms of church government.

Examples

An Episcopalian believer
As an Episcopalian, he valued tradition and inclusivity within the church.
Her grandfather was a devout Episcopalian who attended services every Sunday.
The Episcopalian community organized a charity event for local families.
