endopod

noun
inner limb branch
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndoʊˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌpɒd / (BrE)
the inner branch of the limb of a crustacean or similar arthropod

Examples

The endopod branch
The endopod of the crab's leg is adapted for swimming.
Scientists examined the endopod to understand how the shrimp moves in water.
In some species, the endopod is longer than the exopod and helps with walking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exopod - the outer branch of the limb of a crustacean or similar arthropod
