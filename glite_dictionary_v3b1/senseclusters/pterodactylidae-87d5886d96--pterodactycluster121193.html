Pterodactylidae

noun
family of extinct flying reptiles (pterosaurs)
9.9
Pronunciation: /te.rə.dæk.ˈtɪl.ɪ.diː/ /; / /te.rə.dæk.ˈtɪl.aɪ.diː/ (AmE), /te.rə.dæk.ˈtɪl.ɪ.diː/ /; / /te.rə.dæk.ˈtɪl.aɪ.diː/ (BrE)
A family of extinct flying reptiles (pterosaurs), typically characterized by long wings and often referred to as pterodactyls, that lived during the Late Jurassic and Cretaceous periods.

Examples

The Pterodactylidae family
Fossils belonging to the Pterodactylidae family have been discovered on several continents.
Scientists debated whether new finds should be classified within Pterodactylidae or a separate family.
The Pterodactylidae are known for their long, slender wings and toothed beaks.
