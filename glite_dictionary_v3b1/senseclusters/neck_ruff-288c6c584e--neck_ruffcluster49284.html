neck ruff

noun
high, stiff, circular collar
8.3
Pronunciation: / nɛk rʌf / (AmE), / nɛk rʌf / (BrE)
A high, stiff, and often elaborate circular collar, typically made of starched linen or lace, worn around the neck, especially in the 16th and 17th centuries.

Examples

An ornate neck ruff
He recognized the costume instantly by the ornate neck ruff characteristic of that era.
In the portrait, the noblewoman is depicted wearing an elaborate neck ruff around her collar.
The neck ruff became popular fashion among European aristocrats during the Renaissance.
