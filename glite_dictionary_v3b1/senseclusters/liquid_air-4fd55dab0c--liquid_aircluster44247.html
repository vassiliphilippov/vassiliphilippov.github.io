liquid air

noun

(in energy technology) a method or system of storing energy by liquefying air and later regasifying it to produce electricity.

Examples

Liquid air energy storage
The pilot plant uses liquid air to store excess wind energy for later electricity generation.
Engineers are developing large-scale liquid air systems to balance variable renewable power on the grid.
When demand rises, the plant warms liquid air and uses the expanding gas to spin turbines.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: energy storage - the broader category of technologies and methods used to store electrical energy for later use.
  • synonym: liquid-air energy storage - the specific technology (often abbreviated LAES) of storing energy by liquefying and later regasifying air.
