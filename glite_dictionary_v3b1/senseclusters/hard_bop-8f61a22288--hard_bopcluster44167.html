hard bop

noun
a jazz subgenre with blues and soulful influences
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /hɑːrd bɑːp/ /; / /hɑːrd bɔːp/ (AmE), /hɑːd bɒp/ (BrE)
a style of jazz music that emerged in the 1950s, characterized by a strong blues influence and a more earthy, soulful approach compared to bebop
