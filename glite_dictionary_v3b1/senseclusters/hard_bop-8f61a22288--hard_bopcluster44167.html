hard bop

noun
a jazz subgenre with blues and soulful influences
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɑrd bɑp / (AmE), / hɑːd bɒp / (BrE)
a style of jazz music that emerged in the 1950s, characterized by a strong blues influence and a more earthy, soulful approach compared to bebop

Examples

Hard bop music
Art Blakey is known as one of the pioneers of hard bop music.
Many jazz musicians turned to hard bop for its expressive and improvisational qualities.
The band's new album is heavily influenced by hard bop traditions of the late 1950s.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bebop - a fast, complex style of jazz from the 1940s
  • degree higher: modal jazz - a style of jazz that uses musical modes rather than chord progressions as a harmonic framework
