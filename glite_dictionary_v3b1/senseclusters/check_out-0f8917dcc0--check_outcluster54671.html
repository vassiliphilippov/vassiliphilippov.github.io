check out

verb
find out someone's opinions or intentions
1.0
Pronunciation: / tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to examine, inspect, or investigate something or someone to find out more, check its quality, or see if it is true

Examples

To check out a problem
He tried to check out the team's attitude during the meeting.
I want to check out what she really thinks before making a decision.
We're going to check out how the new manager feels about the changes.
Her story didn't check out when we looked at the evidence.
If these numbers check out, the company is in serious trouble.
We'll have to see if his alibi checks out with the security footage.
I want to check out that restaurant everyone keeps talking about.
Please check out the new software updates before installing them.
The mechanic will check out your car to see what’s wrong with it.
