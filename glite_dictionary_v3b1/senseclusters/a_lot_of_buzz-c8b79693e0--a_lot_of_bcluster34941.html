a lot of buzz

noun
widespread excitement or interest
3.1
Pronunciation: / ə ˈlɑt əv ˈbʌz / (AmE), / ə ˈlɒt əv ˈbʌz / (BrE)
widespread excitement or interest about something, often attracting public attention or media coverage

Examples

A lot of buzz about
Critics agreed that the movie lived up to a lot of buzz it had received.
The new smartphone generated a lot of buzz before its official launch.
There was a lot of buzz surrounding the candidate's announcement.
