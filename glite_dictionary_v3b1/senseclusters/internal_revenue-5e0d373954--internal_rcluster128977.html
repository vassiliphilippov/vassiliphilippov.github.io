internal revenue

noun
domestic government revenue (not customs)
1.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɝnəl ˈrɛvəˌnu / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˈrɛvənjuː / (BrE)
government revenue collected from domestic sources, such as income and sales taxes, but not including customs duties

Examples

Internal revenue collection
A sharp increase in internal revenue was reported after the implementation of new tax policies.
The government relies heavily on internal revenue to fund public services and infrastructure.
Unlike customs duties, internal revenue is derived from sources within the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: customs - taxes collected on imports and exports
  • degree higher: revenue - income, especially that of a government or organization
