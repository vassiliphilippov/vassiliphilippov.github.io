canyon oak

noun
an evergreen oak tree of the southwestern US and NW Mexico
Pronunciation: / ˈkænjən oʊk / (AmE), / ˈkænjən əʊk / (BrE)
a medium-sized evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, with oblong leathery leaves often having spiny edges

Examples

The canyon oak tree
Hikers admired the glossy, spiny-edged leaves of the canyon oak along the trail.
The steep slopes were dotted with canyon oak and other native plants.
Wildlife often rely on the acorns produced by canyon oak trees for food.
