fritillary

noun
butterfly with brown-and-silver wings
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪtəˌlɛri / (AmE), / frɪˈtɪləri / (BrE)
A butterfly of the family Nymphalidae, typically having brownish wings marked with black and silver spots.

Examples

A fritillary butterfly
Several fritillaries can often be seen near streams during summer.
The wings of the fritillary are beautifully patterned with silver and black spots.
We watched a fritillary fluttering among the wildflowers in the meadow.
