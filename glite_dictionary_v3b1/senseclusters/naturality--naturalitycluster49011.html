naturality

noun
mathematical consistency
Pronunciation: / ˌnætʃəˈræləti / (AmE), / ˌnatʃəˈralɪti / (BrE)
the property of a mathematical construction or transformation behaving consistently with structure-preserving maps, especially in category theory

Examples

Naturality condition
The professor emphasized the importance of naturality when defining new mathematical transformations.
In category theory, naturality ensures that diagrams commute under certain mappings.
The proof relied on the naturality of the constructed transformation to guarantee consistency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: functoriality - the property of a mapping between categories preserving structure
