rip off

verb
steal; take without consent
6.3 1.75/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªp É”f / (AmE), / rÉªp É’f / (BrE)
to steal something, especially by taking it quickly or without the owner's knowledge

Examples

To rip off a wallet
He was caught ripping off expensive electronics from the store.
Someone tried to rip off my bag while I was shopping in the market.
The thieves ripped off several laptops from the office last night.

Synonyms

  • swipe
    5.5 1.34/m
    swipe is a quick, casual taking of small items by chance, while rip off implies stealing quickly without the owner's knowledge.
  • knock off
    8.4 0.12/m
    knock off is informal slang for stealing and can also mean copying goods, while rip off focuses on stealing quickly.
  • walk off
    7.5 0.14/m
    walk off is to take something by walking away with it, often in plain view, while rip off stresses secret quick theft.
  • steal
    1.0 96/m
    steal is the general, neutral verb for taking without permission and can be formal, while rip off is more informal and sudden.
