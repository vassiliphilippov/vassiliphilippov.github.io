minuscule

adjective
medieval small cursive script
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnəˌskjul / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪskjuːl / (BrE)
(of writing or script) relating to a small cursive script developed from uncial, used from the 7th to 9th centuries.

Examples

Minuscule handwriting style
Scholars can identify the transition from uncial to minuscule writing styles in medieval texts.
The ancient manuscript was written in a distinctive minuscule script typical of the Carolingian era.
The paleographer specialized in decoding minuscule hands from the ninth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uncial - a script characterized by rounded, separated letters used in Latin and Greek manuscripts
