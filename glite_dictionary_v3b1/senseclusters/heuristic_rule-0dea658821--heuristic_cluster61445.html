heuristic rule

noun
problem-solving guideline based on experience
2.5
Pronunciation: / hjuˈrɪstɪk ruːl / (AmE), / hjʊəˈrɪstɪk ruːl / (BrE)
A practical guideline or principle based on experience or common sense, used to increase the chance of solving a problem effectively.

Examples

A heuristic rule used
Engineers rely on heuristic rules to quickly estimate design parameters before detailed analysis.
The chess computer evaluates positions using several heuristic rules programmed by experts.
When faced with a complex decision, using a heuristic rule can often save time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rule of thumb - a general principle based on experience rather than exact calculation
