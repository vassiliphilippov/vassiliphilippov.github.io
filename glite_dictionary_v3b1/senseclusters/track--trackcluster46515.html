track

noun
endless belt for tracked vehicles
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: / træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
a path, course, or mark made by people, animals, vehicles, or objects moving over a surface (including roads, railway lines, racecourses, or marks in snow or dirt)

Examples

Railway track
Engineers inspected the tracks of the bulldozer before beginning the demolition work.
Snowmobiles with tracks can travel over deep snow that would stop other vehicles.
The tank's track was damaged by the rocky terrain, leaving it stranded.
A narrow dirt track led from the village to the top of the hill.
Recent rains had made the track almost impassable by vehicles.
The hikers followed the track through the dense forest until they reached the river.
She stood well back from the track as the express train approached.
The train rattled along the track as it sped through the countryside.
Workers were repairing the damaged track after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: course - the route or direction followed by something or someone
  • antonym: field - an area where sporting events are held (contrasts in athletics: field events vs track events)
  • synonym: path - a way or track laid down for walking or made by continual treading
  • synonym: racecourse - a track specially prepared for racing, especially for horses
  • synonym: rail - a steel bar forming part of the track for trains
  • degree higher: road - a wide way leading from one place to another
  • synonym: trail - a path, track, or unpaved lane or road
  • synonym: tread - the part of a track or tire that touches the ground
