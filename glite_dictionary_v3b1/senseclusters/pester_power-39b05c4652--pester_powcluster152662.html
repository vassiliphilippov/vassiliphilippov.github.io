pester power

noun
persistent persuasion to influence decisions
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛstər ˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈpɛstə ˌpaʊə / (BrE)
the ability to influence someone's decisions, especially those of parents, by persistently asking for something, often used in reference to children.

Examples

Pester power of children
Advertisers often target children because they are aware of pester power.
Shops display sweets at the checkout to take advantage of children's pester power.
Their parents finally gave in to the pester power of the twins.
