prenup

verb
to sign prenuptial agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːnʌp / (AmE), / ˈpriːnʌp / (BrE)
to make or enter into a prenuptial agreement before getting married, specifying property or financial arrangements in case of divorce.

Examples

To prenup before marriage
They decided to prenup before their wedding to clarify their financial responsibilities.
Some couples choose to prenup to avoid disputes if their marriage ends.
After discussing their assets, they agreed to prenup for peace of mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divorce - to legally end a marriage
  • synonym: marry - to become legally joined in marriage
