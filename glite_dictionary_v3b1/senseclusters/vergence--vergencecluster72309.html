vergence

noun
convergence of light rays
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈvɜːr.dʒəns/ (AmE), /ˈvɜː.dʒəns/ (BrE)
the degree to which a bundle of light rays is converging or diverging, especially in optics and lens design

Examples

Optical vergence
The optometrist measured the vergence of the patient's eyes to assess their focusing ability.
Adjusting the lens changes the vergence of the light entering the camera.
Engineers calculated the vergence required to direct the laser beam precisely.
