patent troll

noun
litigious patent holder not producing inventions
6.3 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpæt.ənt troʊl/ /; / /ˈpeɪ.tənt troʊl/ /; / /ˈpæt.ənt trɑːl/ (AmE), /ˈpæt.ənt trəʊl/ /; / /ˈpeɪ.tənt trəʊl/ /; / /ˈpæt.ənt trɒl/ (BrE)
a person or company that acquires and enforces patent rights primarily to profit from litigation or licensing, rather than to produce or market new inventions, often by exploiting minor or questionable patents

Examples

A patent troll sues
After acquiring the rights, the patent troll filed lawsuits against multiple established companies.
Some legislators propose new laws to address the problem of patent trolls in the software industry.
The small tech company was sued by a patent troll that owned thousands of vague patents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: non-practicing entity - person or company owning patents but not manufacturing products
