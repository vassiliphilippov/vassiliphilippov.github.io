reinhabit

verb
to live somewhere again
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
to live in or occupy a place again after having left or been away

Examples

To reinhabit a place
After decades of neglect, conservationists worked to reinhabit the old forest village.
Wildlife began to reinhabit the area once the pollution was cleaned up.
The family decided to reinhabit their ancestral home after many years abroad.
After the renovations, the family decided to reinhabit their old house.
The actor was excited to reinhabit the character for the new season.
Scientists hope that native species will eventually reinhabit the restored wetland.
