coagulopathy

noun
blood clotting disorder
Pronunciation: / koʊˌæɡjəˈlɑːpəθi / (AmE), / kəʊˌæɡjʊˈlɒpəθi / (BrE)
a medical condition where the blood does not clot normally, causing problems like too much bleeding or sometimes too much clotting

Examples

Blood coagulopathy
The patient developed a coagulopathy after receiving certain medications during surgery.
Doctors monitored her closely for signs of coagulopathy following the severe infection.
A rare coagulopathy can cause either dangerous bleeding or unexpected blood clots.
The doctor suspected coagulopathy when the patient developed unexplained bruises and prolonged bleeding.
Certain medications can increase the risk of coagulopathy in vulnerable individuals.
After surgery, the medical team monitored her closely for signs of coagulopathy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemophilia - a genetic disorder causing difficulty in blood clotting
  • synonym: thrombosis - the formation of a blood clot inside a blood vessel
