monist

noun
believer in unity
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊnɪst / (AmE), / ˈməʊnɪst /; / ˈmɒnɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that everything is made of or explained by a single substance or principle

Examples

A monist philosopher
The monist argued that mind and body are not truly separate.
As a monist, she believed that everything in the universe is connected by one principle.
Many philosophers have debated with the monist about the nature of reality.
The monist maintained that mind and matter are not separate but aspects of one reality.
As a monist, she rejected the idea of a dualistic universe.
Many philosophers have debated with the monist about the nature of existence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dualist - a person who believes that reality consists of two fundamental substances or principles
  • synonym: materialist - a person who believes that only physical matter exists
