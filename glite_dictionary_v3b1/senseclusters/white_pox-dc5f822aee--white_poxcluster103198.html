white pox

noun
mild form of smallpox
3.0
Pronunciation: / waɪt pɑks / (AmE), / waɪt pɒks / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent strain of the virus.

Examples

Mild white pox disease
During the 18th century, some people contracted white pox and survived with only minor scarring.
Historians believe that white pox spread more slowly than the more deadly forms of smallpox.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with white pox due to the milder symptoms and faster recovery.
