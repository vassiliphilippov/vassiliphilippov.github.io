file

verb
move in line, one after another
Pronunciation: / faɪl / (AmE), / faɪl / (BrE)
(of people) to move in a line, one after another, as in a procession or orderly manner.

Examples

People file out
After the concert ended, the crowd filed slowly towards the exits.
Guests file into the hall as the wedding music plays.
The soldiers filed out of the barracks in perfect order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: march - walk in a steady, regular way, often in a group
  • synonym: stream - move in a continuous flow (of people)
