forcedly

adverb
unnaturally, under compulsion
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔːrst.liː/ (AmE), /ˈfɔːst.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that is done with force or not naturally, often because of compulsion or pressure

Examples

Forcedly smiled
She laughed forcedly at the boss's joke, trying to hide her discomfort.
His apology sounded forcedly sincere, but everyone could tell he did not mean it.
The children smiled forcedly for the family photo, wishing they could go play instead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naturally - in a way that is normal or not forced
