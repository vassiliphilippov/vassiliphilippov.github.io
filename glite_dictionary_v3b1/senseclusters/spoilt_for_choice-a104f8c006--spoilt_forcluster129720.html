spoilt for choice

adjective
having too many good options to choose from
Pronunciation: / spɔɪlt fɔr tʃɔɪs / (AmE), / spɔɪlt fə tʃɔɪs / (BrE)
having so many good options available that it is difficult to choose

Examples

To be spoilt for choice
Living in a big city, you are often spoilt for choice when searching for entertainment.
The new restaurant menu left us spoilt for choice when it came to appetizers.
With dozens of brands available, customers can feel spoilt for choice at the electronics store.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lack of choice - having very few or no options to choose from
  • synonym: overwhelmed - feeling as if too much is happening or too many choices are present
