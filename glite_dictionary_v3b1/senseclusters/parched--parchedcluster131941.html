parched

adjective
dried out by heat or sun
1.9 3.8
Pronunciation: / pɑrtʃt / (AmE), / pɑːtʃt / (BrE)
(of land, plants, or substances) dried out with heat; very dry, especially as a result of prolonged exposure to the sun.

Examples

Parched earth
After weeks without rain, the fields were completely parched.
Her lips felt parched after hours in the hot wind.
The hikers searched for water under the parched desert sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baked - hardened or dried by heat
  • synonym: scorched - burned or dried out by intense heat
  • antonym: soaked - thoroughly wet; saturated with liquid
