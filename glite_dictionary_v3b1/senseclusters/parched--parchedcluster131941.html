parched

adjective
dried out by heat or sun
4.7 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / p…ërt Ét / (AmE), / p…ëÀêt Ét / (BrE)
(of land, plants, or substances) dried out with heat; very dry, especially as a result of prolonged exposure to the sun.

Examples

Parched earth
After weeks without rain, the fields were completely parched.
Her lips felt parched after hours in the hot wind.
The hikers searched for water under the parched desert sun.

Synonyms

  • sunbaked
    4.8 0.10/m
    sunbaked is dried and hardened by long strong sunlight, while parched means very dry from prolonged heat or sun.
  • baked
    1.0 0.00/m
    baked is dried and sometimes hardened by heat or sun, while parched describes land or plants left very dry by long sun exposure.
  • dried-out
    2.8 0.49/m

  • desiccated
