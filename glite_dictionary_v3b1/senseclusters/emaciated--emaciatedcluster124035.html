emaciated

adjective
abnormally thin or wasted in appearance
5.6 6.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃiː.eɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ɪ.ˈmeɪ.si.eɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃiː.eɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ə.ˈmeɪ.ʃiː.eɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
extremely thin, especially because of disease, hunger, or lack of food

Examples

An emaciated body
After months of illness, the cat became so emaciated that it weighed barely two kilograms.
Doctors treated emaciated children suffering from severe malnutrition at the emergency clinic.
The refugee appeared emaciated, with his bones clearly visible beneath his skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skeletal - very thin, like a skeleton
