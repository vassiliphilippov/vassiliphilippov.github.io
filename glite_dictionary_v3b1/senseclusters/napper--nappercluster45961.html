napper

noun
cloth finishing tool
Pronunciation: / ˈnæpər / (AmE), / ˈnæpə / (BrE)
a person or machine that raises the surface fibers of cloth to create a soft, fuzzy texture called a nap

Examples

Fabric napper
The textile mill invested in a new napper to improve the softness of their fabrics.
A skilled napper can give woolen cloth a luxurious, velvety feel.
Modern factories often use an automated napper to finish large rolls of material efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: finisher - a person or machine that completes the final surface of a product
  • synonym: shearer - a machine or person that trims the surface of cloth
