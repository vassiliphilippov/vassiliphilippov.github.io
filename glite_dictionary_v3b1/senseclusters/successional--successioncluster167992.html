successional

adjective
in sequence; ecological change
6.7
Pronunciation: / səkˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / səkˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring in a sequence, especially in the process of ecological development or change

Examples

Successional changes in nature
The successional changes in the grassland led to the growth of new plant species over time.
Ecologists study successional patterns to understand how ecosystems recover after disturbances.
Each successional stage in a forest brings different animals and plants to the area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: random - happening without a specific pattern or order
  • synonym: sequential - arranged or happening in a particular order or sequence
