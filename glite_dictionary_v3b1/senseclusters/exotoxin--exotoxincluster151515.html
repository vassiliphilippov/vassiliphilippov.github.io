exotoxin

noun
toxin secreted by microorganisms
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.soʊˈtɑk.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səʊˈtɒk.sɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous substance produced and released by certain microorganisms, especially bacteria, into the surrounding environment

Examples

Bacterial exotoxins
Researchers are developing new vaccines to neutralize bacterial exotoxins effectively.
Some bacteria produce dangerous exotoxins that can cause severe illness in humans.
The laboratory detected the presence of an exotoxin in the contaminated water sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endotoxin - a toxin that is present inside bacterial cells and released when they disintegrate
