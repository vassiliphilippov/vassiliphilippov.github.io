murrelet

noun
small Pacific seabird
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜːrlɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌrɪlɪt / (BrE)
a small seabird of northern coastal regions, related to the auk, known for nesting near the sea

Examples

The murrelet seabird
The marbled murrelet is known for nesting high in old-growth trees near the coast.
Researchers observed a flock of murrelets diving for fish in the chilly Pacific waters.
Conservation efforts aim to protect the habitat of the endangered murrelet.
The murrelet is often seen flying low over the waves near rocky shores.
Scientists study the nesting habits of the murrelet to help protect its habitat.
A murrelet can dive underwater to catch small fish and crustaceans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auk - a seabird of the family Alcidae, related to puffins and murres
  • synonym: puffin - a seabird with a colorful beak, related to auks and murrelets
