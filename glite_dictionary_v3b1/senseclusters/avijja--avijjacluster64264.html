avijja

noun
Buddhist ignorance
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈvɪdʒ.ə/ (AmE), /ə.ˈvɪdʒ.ə/ (BrE)
ignorance or lack of true understanding, especially as a fundamental cause of suffering in Buddhist philosophy

Examples

The avijja of ignorance
According to Buddhist teachings, avijja is the main reason beings remain trapped in the cycle of rebirth.
Meditation practices are designed to help overcome avijja and achieve enlightenment.
The concept of avijja highlights the importance of understanding reality in Buddhist philosophy.
