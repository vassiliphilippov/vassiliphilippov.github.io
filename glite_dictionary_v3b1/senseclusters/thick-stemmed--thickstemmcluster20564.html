thick-stemmed

adjective
having a thick stem (botany)
3.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈθɪk.stemd/ (AmE), /ˈθɪk.stemd/ (BrE)
(of a plant) having a stem that is thick in proportion to its height or typical size.

Examples

Thick-stemmed plants
Gardeners often prefer thick-stemmed tomatoes because they are more resilient to wind damage.
Some thick-stemmed plants provide better support for heavy blossoms.
The thick-stemmed cactus can store water for long periods during drought.
