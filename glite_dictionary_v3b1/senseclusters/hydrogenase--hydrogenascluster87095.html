hydrogenase

noun
enzyme for hydrogen
8.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑdʒəˌneɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒdʒəˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that enables living organisms to produce or use hydrogen gas in biochemical reactions

Examples

hydrogenase enzyme
Some bacteria contain hydrogenase to help them use hydrogen as an energy source.
Researchers study hydrogenase to develop new ways of producing clean energy.
The activity of hydrogenase is essential in certain microbial metabolic processes.
