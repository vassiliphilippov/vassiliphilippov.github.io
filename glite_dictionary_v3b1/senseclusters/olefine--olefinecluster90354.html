olefine

noun
unsaturated aliphatic hydrocarbon
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊlɪfiːn / (AmE), / ˈəʊlɪfiːn / (BrE)
An unsaturated aliphatic hydrocarbon containing at least one double bond between carbon atoms; also known as an olefin.

Examples

An olefine compound
Ethylene is the simplest example of an olefine.
In organic chemistry, an olefine is a hydrocarbon that contains at least one double bond.
Industrial production of plastics often starts with basic olefines like propylene and ethylene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkene - hydrocarbon containing a carbon-carbon double bond
  • synonym: olefin - unsaturated hydrocarbon with double bond (standard spelling)
