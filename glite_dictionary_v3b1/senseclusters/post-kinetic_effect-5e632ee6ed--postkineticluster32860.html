post-kinetic effect

noun
effect occurring after motion or activity
Pronunciation: / poʊst kɪˈnɛt̬ɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / pəʊst kɪˈnɛtɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
An effect or phenomenon that occurs after a period of motion or physical activity.

Examples

The post-kinetic effect
Researchers are investigating the post-kinetic effect to better understand fatigue and recovery in athletes.
The study highlighted several post-kinetic effects observed in muscle tissue following exercise.
Unexpected post-kinetic effects were noted after the abrupt cessation of activity during the experiment.
