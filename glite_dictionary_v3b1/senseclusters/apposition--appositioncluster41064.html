apposition

noun
the act of positioning side by side
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of placing things close together or side by side, especially for comparison or contrast

Examples

Apposition of colors
In art, the apposition of light and dark colors can produce dramatic effects.
The designer used apposition of different textures to create visual interest in the room.
Their arguments gained strength through the apposition of opposing viewpoints.
