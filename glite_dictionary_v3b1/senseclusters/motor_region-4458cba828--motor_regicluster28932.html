motor region

noun
area of brain controlling movement
5.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààmo ät…ö ÀàriÀêd í…ôn / (AmE), / Ààm…ô ät…ô ÀàriÀêd í…ôn / (BrE)
a region of the cerebral cortex responsible for controlling voluntary muscle movements

Examples

The motor region of brain
Damage to the motor region can lead to loss of voluntary movement on one side of the body.
Researchers studied the motor region to understand how the brain controls fine motor skills.
The surgeon carefully avoided the motor region during the procedure to prevent impairing the patient's movement.

Synonyms

  • motor area
    5.1 0.07/m
    motor area is a simple, common name for the brain zone that controls voluntary muscles, while motor region is a more neutral term.
  • Rolando's area
    9.6 0.00/m
    Rolando's area is an anatomical name used in textbooks for the motor zone, while motor region is a wider, plain term.
  • motor cortex
    5.5 0.23/m
    motor cortex is the formal brain term that stresses planning and doing voluntary movements, while motor region focuses on muscle control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: motor cortex - the part of the cerebral cortex involved in planning, control, and execution of voluntary movements
