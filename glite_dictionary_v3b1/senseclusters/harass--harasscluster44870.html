harass

verb
annoy or trouble persistently
Pronunciation: / həˈræs / (AmE), / həˈrɑːs / (BrE)
to annoy or trouble someone repeatedly or persistently, often making them feel worried or uncomfortable

Examples

To harass a neighbor
She threatened to call the police if her neighbor continued to harass her.
Some people use social media to harass others anonymously.
The manager was accused of trying to harass employees who disagreed with company policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - to ease someone's distress or suffering
  • synonym: pester - to annoy someone repeatedly with requests or interruptions
  • synonym: torment - to cause severe mental or physical suffering
