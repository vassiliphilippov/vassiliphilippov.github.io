channel

verb
direct or guide something towards a goal
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃænəl / (BrE)
to direct, guide, or transmit something towards a particular end, place, or purpose

Examples

To channel energy
He tried to channel his anger into creative work.
The teacher channels her students' energy into productive activities.
They channeled funds to support local charities.
He channels his energy into helping others whenever possible.
The organization channeled relief funds directly to the affected communities.
They decided to channel all requests through the customer service department.
The artist's message is channeled through his music to reach a wide audience.
The cable will channel the signal across long distances.
Water pipes are used to channel hot water throughout the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carry - to support and move (something) from one place to another; also to transmit sound, signals, etc.
  • synonym: conduct - to transmit (heat, electricity, sound, etc.)
  • synonym: direct - to cause to move towards a particular destination or aim
  • synonym: divert - to cause something to change its direction or use
  • synonym: transmit - to send or convey from one person or place to another
