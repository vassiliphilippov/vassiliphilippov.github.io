living off-grid

verb
living independently of public utilities
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪvɪŋ ɑf ˈɡrɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ ɒf ˈɡrɪd / (BrE)
Living in a way that is not connected to or reliant on public utilities such as electricity, water, or sewage systems.

Examples

To live off-grid in mountains
After leaving the city, she spent five years living off-grid in the mountains.
He found living off-grid challenging but ultimately rewarding.
More families are choosing living off-grid to reduce their environmental impact.
