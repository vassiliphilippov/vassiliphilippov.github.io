label

noun
radioactive tracer isotope
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪbəl / (BrE)
A radioactive isotope introduced into a compound to trace or study the mechanism of a chemical reaction.

Examples

A radioactive label
By following the label, scientists identified each step of the reaction mechanism.
Researchers tracked the movement of glucose in the body using a carbon-14 label.
The chemist added a radioactive label to the molecule to study its pathway.
