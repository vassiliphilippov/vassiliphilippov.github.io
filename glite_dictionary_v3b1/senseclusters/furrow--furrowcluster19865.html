furrow

verb
make a groove or trench
5.9 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfɜː.r.oʊ/ /; / /ˈfʌ.r.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈfɜː.rəʊ/ /; / /ˈfʌ.rəʊ/ (BrE)
to make a long, narrow groove or trench in a surface, especially in the ground or in an object

Examples

To furrow the soil
Earthworms naturally furrow the ground as they move through it.
Heavy rain can furrow the hillside, creating small channels in the soil.
The farmer used a plow to furrow the field before planting seeds.
Decades of weather had furrowed the stone pillars of the temple.
Skilled artisans would furrow designs into the marble base by hand.
The sculptor carefully furrowed each column to enhance its classical look.
