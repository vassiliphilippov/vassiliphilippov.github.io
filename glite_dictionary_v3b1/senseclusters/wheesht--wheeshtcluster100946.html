wheesht

interjection
be quiet
Pronunciation: / wiːʃt / (AmE), / wiːʃt / (BrE)
used to tell someone to be quiet or stop talking, especially in Scottish English

Examples

Wheesht now
The teacher said wheesht when the class became too noisy.
If you want to hear the announcement, you need to wheesht for a moment.
She whispered wheesht as they crept past the sleeping baby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hush - used to tell someone to be quiet
  • synonym: silence - the absence of sound or telling someone to be quiet
