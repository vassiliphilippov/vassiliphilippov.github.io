scroop

noun
rustling fabric sound
Pronunciation: / skruːp / (AmE), / skruːp / (BrE)
a crisp, rustling sound produced by certain fabrics, especially silk, when rubbed or moved

Examples

The scroop of silk
The scroop of her silk skirt was audible as she entered the quiet room.
He loved the distinctive scroop that fine taffeta made when he handled it.
The tailor recognized the quality of the fabric by its unique scroop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rustle - a soft, muffled crackling sound like that made by dry leaves or paper
