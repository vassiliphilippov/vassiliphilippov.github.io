mill wheel

noun
water wheel used to drive a mill
2.9 3.7
Pronunciation: /mɪl wiːl/ /; / /mɪl hwiːl/ (AmE), /mɪl wiːl/ /; / /mɪl hwiːl/ (BrE)
A water wheel, typically large and vertical, used for driving machinery in a mill by converting the energy of flowing water.

Examples

A mill wheel by river
The old stone factory still has a functioning mill wheel by the river.
The turning mill wheel powered the machinery that ground the grain into flour.
They restored the mill wheel as part of a historical preservation project.
