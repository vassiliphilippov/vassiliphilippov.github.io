invidia

noun
spiteful envy; deadly sin (historical/theological)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɪdiə / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪdiə / (BrE)
Spiteful envy or resentment at the success of others, personified as one of the deadly sins in classical and medieval thought.

Examples

Invidia the sin
Her downfall was attributed to invidia, the green-eyed monster that plagued her every thought.
In classical art, invidia is often depicted as a woman devouring her own heart.
The medieval church preached that invidia could corrupt even the purest souls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: envy - the feeling of discontent or resentful longing aroused by someone else's possessions, qualities, or luck
