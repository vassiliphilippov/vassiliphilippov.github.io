outwalk

verb
to walk farther
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈwɔk / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈwɔːk / (BrE)
to walk farther or faster than another person

Examples

To outwalk competitors
During the marathon, she managed to outwalk most of her competitors in the final stretch.
He tried to outwalk his brother on the long trail, but soon grew tired.
Despite the steep hill, Maria could easily outwalk her friends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lag - to fall behind in movement or progress
  • synonym: outpace - to move faster than someone else
