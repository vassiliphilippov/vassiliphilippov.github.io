sequoia

noun
large Californian coniferous tree
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: / sɪˈk(w)ɔɪ.ə / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
A very large evergreen coniferous tree native to California, known for reaching great heights and for its massive size, including both the coast redwood and giant sequoia species.

Examples

Giant sequoia tree
Scientists study the growth rings of sequoia trees to learn about historical climate patterns.
The ancient sequoia towers above the forest, its trunk thick and reddish-brown.
We visited a national park to see the massive sequoias that are hundreds of years old.

Related Terms

  • synonym: giant sequoia - largest sequoia species; found in the Sierra Nevada
  • degree lower: pine - another type of coniferous tree
  • synonym: redwood - large North American coniferous tree related to the sequoia
