like the sound of one's own voice

verb
enjoy listening to oneself speak (idiom)
Pronunciation: / laɪk ðə saʊnd əv wʌnz oʊn vɔɪs / (AmE), / laɪk ðə saʊnd əv wʌnz əʊn vɔɪs / (BrE)
to enjoy hearing oneself speak, often implying that someone talks excessively or self-importantly without considering others' interest

Examples

She likes the sound of her own voice
Don't mind her—she just likes the sound of her own voice and loves to lecture everyone.
He always dominates meetings because he likes the sound of his own voice.
Some people talk at length not to contribute but because they like the sound of their own voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modest - not talking about oneself excessively
  • synonym: talkative - tending to talk a lot
