cape

verb
to skin trophy animal
Pronunciation: / keɪp / (AmE), / keɪp / (BrE)
to remove the skin from the head, neck, or shoulders of a hunted animal for use as a trophy or for mounting

Examples

To cape a deer
The guide demonstrated how to cape a deer for a proper shoulder mount.
Hunters often cape their trophies to preserve the animal's head and neck for display.
He learned to cape animals carefully to avoid damaging the fur around the shoulders.
