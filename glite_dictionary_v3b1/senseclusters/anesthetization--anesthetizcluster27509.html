anesthetization

noun
process of anesthesia
7.1
Pronunciation: / əˌnɛsθətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌniːsθətaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / əˌniːsθɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of making a person or animal unable to feel pain, usually by giving anesthesia before a medical procedure

Examples

Patient anesthetization
The anesthetization of the patient was completed before the operation began.
Proper anesthetization is essential to ensure the patient does not feel pain during surgery.
Advances in anesthetization have made many complex surgeries much safer and more comfortable.
The anesthetization of the patient was carefully monitored throughout the surgery.
Modern anesthetization techniques have greatly reduced the risks of surgical procedures.
After anesthetization, she felt no pain during the dental treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anesthesia - loss of sensation, especially as induced for medical purposes
  • antonym: awakening - the process of regaining consciousness or sensation
  • antonym: consciousness - the state of being awake and aware
  • synonym: numbness - lack of physical sensation
  • synonym: sedation - the act of calming or making someone sleep with drugs
