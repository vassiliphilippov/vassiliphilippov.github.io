workhorse

noun
dependable machine under heavy use
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːkˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌhɔːs / (BrE)
a person, machine, or thing that does a lot of hard or routine work reliably and steadily

Examples

Office workhorse
For decades, the Boeing 747 was the workhorse of international air travel.
The diesel generator has become a workhorse for many remote construction sites.
This server is the workhorse of our computer lab, running day and night without failure.
This old truck is a real workhorse on the farm.
The new software has become the workhorse of our office, handling most daily tasks.
She is the workhorse of the team, always taking on extra projects without complaint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drudge - a person who does hard, boring work
  • synonym: mainstay - a chief support or main element
  • antonym: showpiece - a person or thing that is mainly for display, not hard work
  • synonym: workhorse - a person who works hard and reliably
