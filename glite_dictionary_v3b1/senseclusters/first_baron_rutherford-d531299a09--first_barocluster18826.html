First Baron Rutherford

(1871–1937) British physicist, born in New Zealand, who discovered the atomic nucleus and proposed the nuclear model of the atom.

Examples

First Baron Rutherford Nobel Prize
First Baron Rutherford was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1908 for his work on radioactivity.
In 1911, First Baron Rutherford proposed the revolutionary nuclear model of the atom.
Many textbooks credit First Baron Rutherford with establishing the modern understanding of atomic structure.
