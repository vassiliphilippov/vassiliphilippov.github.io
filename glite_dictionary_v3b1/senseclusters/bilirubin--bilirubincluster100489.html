bilirubin

noun
pigment in bile formed from hemoglobin breakdown
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪləˈrubɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɪlɪˈruːbɪn / (BrE)
an orange-yellow pigment formed in the liver by the breakdown of hemoglobin, present in bile and excreted in urine and feces; excess in the blood causes jaundice

Examples

High bilirubin level
Elevated bilirubin can indicate liver dysfunction or disease.
The doctor ordered a blood test to measure bilirubin levels in the newborn.
When the body can’t process bilirubin properly, jaundice may develop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jaundice - yellowing of the skin and eyes caused by increased bilirubin
