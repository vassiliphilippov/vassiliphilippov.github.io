intonation

noun
variation in pitch during speech
7.5 3.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the rise and fall in the pitch of the voice when speaking, especially as it affects meaning or expression

Examples

Rising intonation
English questions often have a rising intonation at the end of the sentence.
She practiced her speech, paying careful attention to intonation to keep the audience interested.
You can change the meaning of a sentence by simply altering your intonation.
