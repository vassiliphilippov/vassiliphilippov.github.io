bibliolatry

noun
excessive reverence for the Bible
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪbliˈɑlətri / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliˈɒlətri / (BrE)
Excessive reverence or devotion to the Bible, often to the extent of treating it as an object of worship rather than a source of guidance.

Examples

Strict bibliolatry
Her strict adherence to the scriptures bordered on bibliolatry, leaving little room for interpretation.
Many scholars have argued that bibliolatry can obscure the true message of religious teachings.
Some critics accuse certain religious groups of practicing bibliolatry rather than genuine faith.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idolatry - worship of idols or excessive devotion to something
