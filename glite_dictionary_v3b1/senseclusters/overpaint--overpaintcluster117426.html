overpaint

noun
later paint layer
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.peɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.peɪnt/ (BrE)
a layer of paint applied over an earlier layer, often not part of the original artwork or surface

Examples

A layer of overpaint
The conservator carefully removed the overpaint to restore the original colors of the mural.
Analysis showed that the overpaint was added many years after the painting was completed.
Experts debated whether to leave the overpaint or reveal the artist's first version beneath.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underpainting - an initial layer of paint applied to a surface before the final layers
