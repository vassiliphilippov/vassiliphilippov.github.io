sixpence

noun
old British coin worth six pence
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪksˌpɛns / (AmE), / ˈsɪkspəns / (BrE)
a former British coin worth six old pence, used before decimalization and no longer in circulation since 1970

Examples

A sixpence coin
Children used to tuck a sixpence into their Christmas puddings for good luck.
She found an old sixpence in the attic while cleaning yesterday.
The shopkeeper gave me a sixpence as part of my change in the 1960s.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: penny - the basic unit coin of the UK before decimalization
  • degree higher: shilling - a former British coin worth twelve old pence
