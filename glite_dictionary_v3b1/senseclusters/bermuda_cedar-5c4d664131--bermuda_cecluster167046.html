Bermuda cedar

noun
evergreen tree native to Bermuda
7.4
Pronunciation: / bɚˌmjuːdə ˈsiːdɚ / (AmE), / bəˌmjuːdə ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
an evergreen coniferous tree (Juniperus bermudiana) native to Bermuda, with dense, pyramidal foliage, often grown ornamentally

Examples

Bermuda cedar tree
Gardeners often choose Bermuda cedar for its attractive foliage and natural resilience.
The Bermuda cedar once dominated the Bermudian landscape before many were destroyed by blight.
The wood of the Bermuda cedar is prized for its aromatic scent and durability.
