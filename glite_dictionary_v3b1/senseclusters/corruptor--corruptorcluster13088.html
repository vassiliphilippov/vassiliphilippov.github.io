corruptor

noun
one who corrupts
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌptər / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes others to become morally bad or dishonest

Examples

A corruptor of youth
The judge called the defendant a corruptor of public trust.
Many believed the new policy would make the government a corruptor rather than a protector.
The story warned of the dangers posed by a corruptor in a position of power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debaucher - a person who leads others into immoral behavior
  • antonym: reformer - a person who works to improve moral standards
