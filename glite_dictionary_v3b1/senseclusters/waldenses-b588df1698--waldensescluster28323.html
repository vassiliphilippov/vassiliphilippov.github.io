Waldenses

proper noun
medieval Christian group
Pronunciation: / wɔlˈdɛnsiz / (AmE), / wɔlˈdɛnsiz / (BrE)
members of a Christian movement founded in the 12th century in Europe, known for advocating poverty and lay preaching

Examples

Waldenses preach poverty
The Waldenses were among the earliest groups to challenge the authority of the medieval church.
Historians study the beliefs and practices of the Waldenses to understand religious reform in Europe.
Many Waldenses sought refuge in remote mountain valleys to escape persecution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cathars - members of a medieval Christian sect in southern Europe
  • synonym: Protestants - members of Christian churches that separated from the Roman Catholic Church
