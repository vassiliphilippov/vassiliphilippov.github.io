disarticulation

noun
joint separation
9.5
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ɑːr.tɪ.kjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪs.ər.tɪ.kjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪs.ɑː.tɪ.kjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the separation of two bones at their joint, usually by surgery or injury

Examples

Finger disarticulation
The surgeon performed a disarticulation of the patient's finger after the severe injury.
Accidental disarticulation of the shoulder can occur during high-impact sports.
Successful disarticulation requires careful separation of the bones at the joint.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reduction - the process of restoring a dislocated joint to its normal position
