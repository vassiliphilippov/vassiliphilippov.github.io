vulnificus

noun
marine pathogenic bacterium
9.9
Pronunciation: / vʌlˈnɪfɪkəs / (AmE), / vʌlˈnɪfɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of Gram-negative marine bacterium (Vibrio vulnificus) that can cause serious infections in humans, especially from seafood or wounds

Examples

Vulnificus bacteria
Doctors warned that vulnificus infections can be life-threatening if not treated quickly.
Eating raw oysters contaminated with vulnificus can lead to severe illness.
Researchers studied how vulnificus spreads in warm coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Salmonella - a genus of bacteria that can cause foodborne illness
  • synonym: Vibrio - a genus of bacteria found in marine environments
