reverse

verb
cancel or annul a decision officially
2.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrs / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs / (BrE)
to officially cancel or overturn a previous decision, especially in a legal context

Examples

To reverse a decision
If the appeal is successful, the court will reverse the conviction.
The government may reverse its policy on export restrictions if the economy weakens.
The judge decided to reverse the earlier ruling after reviewing new evidence.
A higher authority can reverse the judgement if new evidence emerges.
If the appeal is successful, the board may reverse its earlier decision.
The Supreme Court decided to reverse the lower court's ruling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: affirm - to uphold or confirm a previous decision
  • synonym: overturn - to invalidate or reverse a legal decision
  • synonym: revoke - to officially cancel something, such as a law or order
  • antonym: uphold - to confirm or support a previous decision, especially in law
