countersue

verb
to sue in return
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌsuː / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsjuː / (BrE)
to sue someone in response to their lawsuit against you

Examples

To countersue a company
The company decided to countersue after being accused of breach of contract.
If you feel wronged by the original lawsuit, you may choose to countersue.
Their lawyer advised them to countersue for damages caused by the false claims.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sue - to start a legal case against someone in court
