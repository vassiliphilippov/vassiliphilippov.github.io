ferule

noun
stick or paddle used for punishment
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛr.juːl / (BrE)
a flat piece of wood, stick, or cane, especially one used in the past for punishing children by hitting them

Examples

A ferule stick
In Victorian times, a ferule was often kept in classrooms as a means of discipline.
The old ferule hangs on display as a reminder of past educational practices.
The teacher struck the desk with the ferule to get the students' attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cane - a slender stick used as a walking aid or for punishment
  • synonym: rod - a thin straight stick, often used for punishment
