keeled

adjective
having a ridge like a keel
9.9
Pronunciation: / kild / (AmE), / kiːld / (BrE)
having a prominent ridge or shaped like the keel of a ship, especially used to describe parts of plants or animals

Examples

Keeled breastbone
Several species of birds have keeled breastbones to support flight muscles.
The keeled leaves help the plant channel rainwater toward its roots.
The back of the lizard is distinctly keeled, giving it a rough texture.
