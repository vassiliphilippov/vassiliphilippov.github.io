lymph cell

noun
type of white blood cell involved in immunity
7.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /lɪmf sel/ /; / /lɪmpf sel/ (AmE), /lɪmf sel/ /; / /lɪmpf sel/ (BrE)
A type of white blood cell (leukocyte) without granules in its cytoplasm, playing an essential role in the immune system and typically constituting about a quarter of the total white blood cell count, with its number increasing during infection.

Examples

A lymph cell count
A sudden rise in lymph cell count can indicate that the body is fighting an infection.
Doctors measured the patient's lymph cell levels to monitor his immune response after surgery.
Low lymph cell numbers may make a person more susceptible to certain diseases.
