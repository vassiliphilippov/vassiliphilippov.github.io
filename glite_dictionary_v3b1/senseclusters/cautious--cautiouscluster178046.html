cautious

adjective
avoiding excess or risk; restrained
2.9 14/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…î É…ôs / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀê É…ôs / (BrE)
being careful to avoid risk, danger, or mistakes; acting in a careful and deliberate way

Examples

A cautious estimate
He gave a cautious estimate of the project's cost to avoid any surprises later.
Her cautious approach made sure the team never overpromised what they could deliver.
The scientist used cautious language when presenting the uncertain results.
His cautious approach helped the company avoid costly mistakes.
Investors remain cautious about putting money into unfamiliar markets.
She was always cautious when crossing the busy street.

Synonyms

  • circumspect
    7.9 1.18/m
    circumspect is careful to think about risks and consequences before acting, while cautious means acting carefully to avoid danger.
  • prudent
    6.2 7.02/m
    prudent plans and acts with long-term sense and care, while cautious focuses on avoiding immediate risks and mistakes.
  • wary
    3.6 10/m
    wary stays on guard because of distrust or threat, while cautious describes careful, deliberate behavior to avoid risk.
  • watchful
    3.2 3.30/m
    watchful keeps close attention to signs of trouble, while cautious means acting carefully and deliberately to prevent harm.
  • vigilant
    6.0 4.15/m
    vigilant stays constantly alert and ready to spot danger, while cautious is more about careful, deliberate action to avoid risk.
