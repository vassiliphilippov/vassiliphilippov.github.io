myrrh tree

noun
tree that produces myrrh resin
Pronunciation: / mɜr tri / (AmE), / mɜː triː / (BrE)
A small tree native to eastern Africa and Asia that produces myrrh, an aromatic resin used in perfumes and incense.

Examples

A myrrh tree plant
A myrrh tree can thrive in arid regions with little rainfall.
The ancient Egyptians valued the myrrh tree for its fragrant resin.
Traders once traveled great distances to harvest resin from the myrrh tree.
