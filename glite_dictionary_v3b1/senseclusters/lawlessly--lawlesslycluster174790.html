lawlessly

adverb
in a lawless or illegal manner
7.2 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…îl…ôsli / (AmE), / Ààl…îÀêl…ôsli / (BrE)
In a manner that disregards or violates the law; without regard for legal rules.

Examples

Act lawlessly
He was arrested for crossing the border lawlessly in the middle of the night.
The mob acted lawlessly, ignoring all police warnings.
Wild animals roamed the territory, and people often behaved lawlessly before order was established.

Synonyms

  • illegally
    2.1 9.33/m
    illegally describes doing something not allowed by law, while lawlessly suggests acting with no regard for legal rules or order.
  • unlawfully
    4.3 0.73/m
    unlawfully means acting against the law in a legal sense, while lawlessly implies open disregard or violation of legal rules.
  • illicitly
    6.8 0.18/m
    illicitly often describes secret or morally forbidden acts, while lawlessly focuses on openly breaking or ignoring legal rules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illegally - in a way that is against the law
  • antonym: legally - in accordance with the law
