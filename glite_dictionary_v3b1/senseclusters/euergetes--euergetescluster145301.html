euergetes

proper noun
Hellenistic honorific title
Pronunciation: / juːˈɜrdʒəˌtiːz / (AmE), / juːˈɜːdʒɪtiːz / (BrE)
a regnal title or honorific used by Hellenistic rulers meaning 'benefactor', often added to a ruler's name

Examples

Euergetes the king
Ptolemy III was known as Euergetes for his generous contributions to temples and cities.
The title Euergetes was adopted by several rulers to highlight their role as public benefactors.
Historians often refer to him as Euergetes in recognition of his charitable acts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or money to others
