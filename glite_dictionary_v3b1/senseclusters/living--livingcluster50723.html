living

adjective
(of stone) in its natural, unmined state
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of minerals, rock, or stone) in its natural state and location; not quarried or removed from its original site.

Examples

Living stone
Engineers tunneled through the living stone to create an underground passage.
Statues emerged gracefully from the living cliff face, still part of the hillside itself.
The temple was carved directly into the living rock of the mountain.
