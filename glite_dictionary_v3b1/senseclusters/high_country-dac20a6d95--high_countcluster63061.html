high country

noun
elevated land above foothills, below timberline
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈkʌntri / (AmE), / haɪ ˈkʌntri / (BrE)
an area of land located above the foothills or piedmont but below the timberline, often characterized by higher elevation and specific vegetation or climate

Examples

The high country area
Many hikers come to the high country for its breathtaking views and wildflowers.
Snow persists in the high country long after it has melted in the valleys below.
They spent the summer exploring the rugged beauty of the high country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low country - an area of land at low elevation, often coastal or near sea level
