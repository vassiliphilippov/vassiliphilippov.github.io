compound pistil

noun
pistil with fused carpels (botany)
9.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.paʊnd ˈpɪs.təl/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.paʊnd ˈpɪs.təl/ (BrE)
In botany, a pistil that consists of two or more fused carpels forming a single organ.

Examples

A compound pistil in flowers
Scientists observed the formation of seeds within the compound pistil during the experiment.
The flower was identified by its distinctive compound pistil composed of three fused carpels.
Unlike plants with a single carpel, species with a compound pistil often have more complex fruit structures.
