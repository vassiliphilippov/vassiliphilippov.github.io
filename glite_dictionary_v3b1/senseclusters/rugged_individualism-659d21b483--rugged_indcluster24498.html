rugged individualism

noun
belief in self-reliance and free competition
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌɡɪd ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪd ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that individuals should be self-reliant and free to compete with minimal interference from government or society, especially in social and economic matters

Examples

Rugged individualism belief
His political philosophy was shaped by a strong belief in rugged individualism and limited government.
Many early American pioneers embraced rugged individualism as they built new lives on the frontier.
The concept of rugged individualism was often invoked during debates over welfare policies.
