Synonyms

  • below
    1.0 39/m
    below is used for lower places, levels, or numbers, while under often describes being directly beneath or covered by something.
  • underneath
    1.0 7.55/m
    underneath often means directly under or on the underside and can suggest hiding, while under is more general for lower position.
  • beneath
    1.0 2.18/m
    beneath sounds more formal and fits rank or feelings of being unworthy, while under is the common word for physical lower position.
