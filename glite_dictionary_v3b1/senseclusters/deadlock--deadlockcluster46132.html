deadlock

noun
computing process standstill
6.6 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÉ›dËŒlÉ‘k / (AmE), / ËˆdÉ›dËŒlÉ’k / (BrE)
a situation in computing where two or more processes cannot proceed because each is waiting for the other to release a resource

Examples

System deadlock
The system experienced a deadlock when both programs waited for each other to finish.
Developers must design software carefully to avoid deadlock situations in multi-threaded applications.
A deadlock can cause the entire computer to freeze until the issue is resolved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: livelock - a computing situation where processes continually change state but cannot make progress
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation where progress is impossible
