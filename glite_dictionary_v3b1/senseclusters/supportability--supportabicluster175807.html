supportability

noun
ability to be supported
7.2
Pronunciation: / səˌpɔrtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / səˌpɔːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which something can be supported by evidence, facts, or logical reasoning

Examples

Supportability of an argument
The supportability of his argument depended on the quality of the data he presented.
Before publishing the article, the editor questioned the supportability of several claims.
The supportability of the proposal was challenged during the meeting due to insufficient evidence.

  • synonym: plausibility - the quality of seeming reasonable or probable
  • antonym: unreliability - the quality of not being trustworthy or dependable
