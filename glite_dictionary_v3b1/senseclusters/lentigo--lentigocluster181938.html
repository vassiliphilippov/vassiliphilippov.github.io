lentigo

noun
small brownish skin spot
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / l…õnÀàta…™go ä / (AmE), / l…õnÀàtiÀêg…ô ä / (BrE)
a small, sharply circumscribed brownish spot on the skin caused by an increased amount of melanin, often occurring as a result of sun exposure or aging

Examples

A lentigo spot
After years of sun exposure, several lentigines appeared on her hands.
Age-related lentigines are common among older adults, especially on the face and arms.
The dermatologist examined the lentigo to rule out any malignancy.

Synonyms

    1.0 3.07/m
