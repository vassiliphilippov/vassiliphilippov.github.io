neurofibril

noun
microscopic fiber in nerve cells
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈfaɪbrəl / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈfaɪbrɪl / (BrE)
Any of the long, thin microscopic fibers found within nerve cells, providing structural support and extending into the axon and dendrites.

Examples

A neurofibril fiber
Damage to a neurofibril can affect the function of nerve cells.
Scientists observed the neurofibril under a high-powered microscope during the experiment.
The neurofibril is essential for maintaining the structure of the neuron.
