trichuriasis

noun
disease from whipworm infestation

Pronunciation: /trɪ.kjʊr.ˈaɪ.sɪs/ /; / /trɪ.kjuːr.ˈaɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /trɪ.kjʊə.ˈraɪ.sɪs/ /; / /trɪ.kjuː.ˈraɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation with whipworm (Trichuris trichiura), common in tropical regions with poor sanitation.

Examples

Trichuriasis infection

Public health campaigns in rural areas focus on preventing trichuriasis through improved sanitation and hygiene education.
