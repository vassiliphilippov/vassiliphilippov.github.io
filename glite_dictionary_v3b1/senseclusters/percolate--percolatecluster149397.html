percolate

verb
cause a liquid to filter through a substance
9.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpɜːr.kə.leɪt/ /; / /ˈpɜːr.kjə.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈpɜː.kə.leɪt/ /; / /ˈpɜː.kjə.leɪt/ (BrE)
to cause a liquid or gas to move slowly through a porous material or substance

Examples

Water percolates through soil
Hot water is percolated through ground coffee to make an aromatic drink.
The chemist allowed the solution to percolate through the charcoal to purify it.
To extract the active ingredient, they percolate alcohol through the dried roots.
After the rainstorm, water began to percolate through the soil into the underground reservoirs.
Chemicals from the landfill may percolate into the groundwater, causing environmental concerns.
It can take hours for the coffee to percolate through the filter, creating a rich and strong brew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filter - to pass a liquid or gas through a device to remove unwanted substances
  • synonym: seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material or small holes
