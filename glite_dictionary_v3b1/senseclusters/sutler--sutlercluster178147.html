sutler

noun
military camp provision seller (historical)
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒt.lÉš / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒt.lÉ™ / (BrE)
a person who followed armies and sold provisions to soldiers in camp or in the field, especially during historical wars

Examples

The sutler sold goods
During the Civil War, the sutler set up his tent near the regiment to provide goods to the soldiers.
Many soldiers relied on the sutler for tobacco, writing paper, and other necessities not supplied by the army.
The arrival of a sutler was often met with excitement by troops stationed far from towns.

Synonyms

  • victualer
    9.9 0.00/m
    a victualer supplies food to ships or armies and often works as a trader, while a sutler follows armies and sells to soldiers in camp.
  • provisioner
    7.9 0.06/m
    a provisioner supplies food on a large or official scale, while a sutler is a small merchant who follows armies and sells to soldiers.
