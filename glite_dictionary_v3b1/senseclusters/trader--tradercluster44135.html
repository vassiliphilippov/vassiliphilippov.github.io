trader

noun
person who buys and sells goods
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪ.dɚ /; / ˈtreɪdər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪ.də /; / ˈtreɪdə / (BrE)
a person or company that buys and sells goods or assets for profit

Examples

A trader buys goods
After years as a small-time trader, she opened her own retail store downtown.
Many street markets are filled with independent traders selling a variety of products.
The trader stocked his shelves with imported spices and silks.
The trader made a profit by buying stocks at a low price and selling them later.
Local traders gathered at the market every morning to sell their fresh produce.
As a successful trader, she traveled to many countries to find new products.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: buyer - someone who purchases goods
  • synonym: dealer - a person or business that buys and sells a particular product
  • synonym: merchant - a person who buys and sells goods, especially in large quantities
  • synonym: retailer - a person or business selling goods to the public in relatively small quantities
  • degree higher: wholesaler - person or company selling goods in large quantities, often to retailers
