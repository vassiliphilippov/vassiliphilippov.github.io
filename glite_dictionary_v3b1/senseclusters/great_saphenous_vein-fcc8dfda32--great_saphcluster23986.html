great saphenous vein

noun
longest vein in the leg
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt səˈfinəs veɪn / (AmE), / ɡreɪt səˈfiːnəs veɪn / (BrE)
the longest vein in the human body, running from the foot up the leg to join the femoral vein in the groin

Examples

The great saphenous vein in leg
A blood clot in the great saphenous vein can lead to serious complications if not treated promptly.
The great saphenous vein is often used for coronary artery bypass grafting in heart surgery.
Ultrasound imaging revealed an abnormal enlargement of the patient's great saphenous vein.
