malignant tumor

noun
cancerous tumor that can spread
1.5 4.1
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈlɪg.nənt ˈtuː.mər/ (AmE), /mə.ˈlɪg.nənt ˈtjuː.mə/ /; / /mə.ˈlɪg.nənt ˈtuː.mə/ (BrE)
A mass of abnormal cells that is cancerous and tends to spread to other parts of the body.

Examples

A malignant tumor
Doctors explained that a malignant tumor can invade surrounding tissues and spread through the body.
Early detection of a malignant tumor can greatly improve survival rates.
The biopsy confirmed that the mass was a malignant tumor requiring urgent treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benign tumor - a tumor that is not cancerous and does not spread
