art deco

noun
decorative style of the 1920s-30s, geometric and ornate
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɑrt ˈdeɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ɑːt ˈdɛkəʊ / (BrE)
a style of visual arts, architecture, and design that was popular in the 1920s and 1930s, characterized by bold geometric shapes, rich colors, and lavish ornamentation

Examples

Art deco style
Many Miami Beach hotels are famous for their art deco architecture.
She collects jewelry that exemplifies the art deco style of the early twentieth century.
The lobby featured classic art deco elements such as zigzag motifs and sleek metallic finishes.
