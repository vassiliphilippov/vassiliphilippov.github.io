void

noun
a completely empty space
1.0
Pronunciation: / vɔɪd / (AmE), / vɔɪd / (BrE)
a completely empty space or a feeling of emptiness, especially one that is hard to fill

Examples

A void in life
After her departure, there was a void in his life that nothing could fill.
He gazed into the black void of space with a sense of awe and fear.
The new skyscraper rose from the void left by the demolished factory.
After her departure, he felt as though a void had opened in his life.
He tried to fill the emotional void with work, but nothing seemed to help.
The spacecraft drifted silently through the vast void of outer space.
After the house was emptied, a strange void echoed through the once lively rooms.
Art helped him cope with the void left by his best friend's departure.
She tried to fill the void in her heart with new hobbies and friendships.
