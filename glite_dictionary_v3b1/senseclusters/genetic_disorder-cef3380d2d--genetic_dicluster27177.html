genetic disorder

noun
disease caused by inherited gene abnormality
1.9
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a disease or condition caused by abnormalities in an individual's genes or chromosomes, inherited from one or both parents

Examples

A genetic disorder disease
Cystic fibrosis is a well-known genetic disorder that affects the lungs and digestive system.
Early diagnosis can help manage many genetic disorders more effectively.
Parents worried about passing on a genetic disorder may seek genetic counseling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congenital disorder - a condition present from birth, sometimes genetic
  • synonym: hereditary disease - a disease inherited from one's parents
