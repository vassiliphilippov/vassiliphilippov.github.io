capacitance

noun
ability to store electric charge
2.8 6.6
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈpæs.ə.təns/ /; / /kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.təns/ (AmE), /kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.təns/ /; / /kə.ˈpæs.ə.təns/ (BrE)
the ability of a system or component (especially in electronics) to store electric charge

Examples

Capacitance in electronics
He measured the capacitance using a specialized meter in the electronics lab.
Materials with high capacitance are useful in making efficient energy storage devices.
The capacitance of the circuit was increased by adding another capacitor.
An oscilloscope was used to measure the capacitance in the circuit.
The capacitance of the capacitor determines how much charge it can store.
When connecting components in parallel, the total capacitance increases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capacitator - device for storing electric charge, also known as a capacitor
  • synonym: capacity - the maximum amount that something can contain; ability to hold or store
  • antonym: inductance - property of an electrical circuit to oppose changes in current
