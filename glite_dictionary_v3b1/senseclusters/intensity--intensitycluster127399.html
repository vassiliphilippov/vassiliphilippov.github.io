intensity

noun
strength of transmitted energy
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛnsəti /; / ɪnˈtɛnsɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtensəti /; / ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (BrE)
the strength or amount of energy in something such as sound, light, or electromagnetic waves

Examples

Intensity of light
By increasing the intensity of the laser, they were able to cut through tougher materials.
Scientists measured the intensity of the radio signal from the satellite.
The intensity of the sunlight made it difficult to see without sunglasses.
She adjusted the speaker volume to reduce the intensity of the background noise.
The intensity of the music made conversation almost impossible in the crowded club.
When measuring noise pollution, scientists record the intensity of sound at various distances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loudness - the quality of being loud, perceived strength of sound
  • synonym: strength - the quality or state of being strong; degree of force or power
