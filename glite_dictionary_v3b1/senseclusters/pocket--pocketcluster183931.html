pocket

noun
opening on billiard table for balls
8.9 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.kət/ /; / /ˈpɑː.kɪt/ (AmE), /ˈpɒk.ɪt/ (BrE)
a special area or opening in a game or sport, such as the hole in a billiard table or the space between pins in bowling

Examples

Billiard pocket

The black ball hovered near the side pocket throughout the game.

Skilled bowlers aim for the pocket to increase their chances of scoring a strike.
The ball landed perfectly in the pocket, knocking down all ten pins.
