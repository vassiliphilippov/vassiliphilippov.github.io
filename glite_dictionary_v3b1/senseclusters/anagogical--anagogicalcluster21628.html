anagogical

adjective
relating to mystical or spiritual interpretation
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈɡɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌænəˈɡɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by anagoge; interpreting events or writings in a spiritual or mystical sense, especially in scriptural exegesis.

Examples

An anagogical meaning
Her interpretation of the poem was deeply anagogical, focusing on underlying religious symbolism.
Some medieval scholars preferred anagogical analyses to uncover hidden truths within scripture.
The text is open to an anagogical reading, revealing layers of spiritual meaning beyond the literal.
