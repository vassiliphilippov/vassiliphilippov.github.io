quoter

noun
one who cites or repeats quotations
Pronunciation: / ˈkwoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊtə / (BrE)
a person who repeats or cites words from someone else, especially as a habit or in writing or speech

Examples

A quoter of Shakespeare
As a habitual quoter of Shakespeare, she often impressed her audience.
He became known as the quoter in meetings, always referencing famous speeches.
The article’s author is a frequent quoter of classic literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quote - to repeat or copy out a group of words from a text or speech
