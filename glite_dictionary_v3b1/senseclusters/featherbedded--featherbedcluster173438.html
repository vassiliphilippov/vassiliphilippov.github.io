featherbedded

adjective
overprotected; cushioned
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛðərˌbɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəbɛdɪd /; / ˈfɛðəˌbɛdɪd / (BrE)
kept comfortable or protected from hardship, especially by special treatment or by having more workers than necessary (often in a job or business context)

Examples

A featherbedded job
The company remained featherbedded thanks to generous government subsidies.
Some critics argue that the industry is featherbedded and resists necessary reforms.
Employees in the department enjoyed a featherbedded environment with little real competition.
The company struggled financially because of several featherbedded departments that were not efficient.
Critics argued that the new policy created featherbedded jobs that were unnecessary.
Some workers enjoyed the security of featherbedded positions, even if their roles were redundant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coddled - treated with excessive care or protection
  • antonym: exposed - left unprotected or open to difficulty
  • synonym: overstaffed - having more employees than needed
  • synonym: pampered - given too much care or comfort
  • antonym: streamlined - made more efficient by reducing unnecessary workers or processes
