intemperateness

noun
lack of moderation, especially in drinking
6.1 8.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtem.pər.ə.nəs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtem.pə.rə.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of lacking moderation or self-control, especially by overindulging in things like alcohol, food, or passions

Examples

His intemperateness in drinking
His intemperateness led to many problems in both his personal and professional life.
She was concerned about his growing intemperateness and frequent visits to the tavern.
The novel explores the consequences of intemperateness among the upper classes.
His intemperateness often led him to say things he later regretted.
She was criticized for the intemperateness of her remarks during the debate.
The intemperateness of the festival crowd caused problems for local authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intemperance - lack of moderation or self-control, especially regarding alcohol
  • antonym: moderation - the quality of being moderate or restrained
  • antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober; moderation or abstinence from alcohol
