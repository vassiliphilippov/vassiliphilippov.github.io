Cercopithecus talapoin

noun
the smallest guenon monkey from central and west Africa
Pronunciation: / sɜːrkoʊˈpɪθɪkəs ˌtæləˈpɔɪn / (AmE), / sɜːkəˈpɪθɪkəs ˌtæləˈpɔɪn / (BrE)
A small species of guenon monkey native to swampy forests of central and western Africa, recognized as the smallest member of its genus.

Examples

The Cercopithecus talapoin monkey
Researchers observed a group of Cercopithecus talapoin in the swampy forests of Cameroon.
The Cercopithecus talapoin is distinguished by its small size and agile movements among the trees.
Unlike larger guenons, the Cercopithecus talapoin prefers dense, watery habitats near rivers.
