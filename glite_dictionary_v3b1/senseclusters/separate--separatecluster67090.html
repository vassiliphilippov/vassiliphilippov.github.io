separate

verb
treat people differently by group
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛpəreɪt / (BrE)
to treat people differently based on qualities such as sex or race, often by setting them apart from others

Examples

To separate by race
For years, the law separated people of different races in schools and public places.
Some companies are accused of separating workers by ethnicity, leading to unfair treatment.
The policy was criticized because it separates students based on gender.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discriminate - treat a person or group differently, especially unfairly
  • antonym: integrate - bring people or groups together as equals
