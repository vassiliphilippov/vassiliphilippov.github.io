irredentist

noun
an advocate of restoring lost territory
Pronunciation: / ˌɪr.ɪˈdɛn.tɪst /; / ˌɪrəˈdɛntɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈdɛn.tɪst /; / ˌɪrɛˈdɛntɪst / (BrE)
a person who wants their country to take back land that it used to own

Examples

An irredentist movement
She published a paper analyzing irredentist rhetoric in early twentieth-century European politics.
The politician was labeled an irredentist after calling for the annexation of neighboring territories.
Throughout history, irredentist movements have occasionally led to conflicts between countries.
Despite his reputation as an irredentist, he eventually accepted the existing international boundaries.
Many historians argue that nationalist and irredentist movements contributed to the start of the conflict.
The new leader is considered an outspoken irredentist seeking the return of disputed border regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irredentism - the political principle or policy of advocating restoration of lost territories to their former country
  • synonym: nationalist - a person who strongly identifies with their own nation and vigorously supports its interests
