irrevocability

noun
the state of being impossible to reverse
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɛvəˌkəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛvəkəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to change, reverse, or retract; finality.

Examples

The irrevocability of contracts
In many legal systems, the irrevocability of certain contracts ensures finality in business dealings.
The irrevocability of her decision weighed heavily on her mind.
They discussed the irrevocability of changes made to the environment.
