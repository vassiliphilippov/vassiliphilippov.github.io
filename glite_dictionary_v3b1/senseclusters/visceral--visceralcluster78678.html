visceral

adjective
relating to internal organs (medical)
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪsərəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl / (BrE)
relating to the internal organs, especially those within the chest or abdomen, such as the heart, liver, and intestines.

Examples

Visceral organs
Visceral fat accumulation is linked to higher risks of cardiovascular disease.
Her condition required careful monitoring of visceral functions such as digestion and respiration.
The patient exhibited signs of visceral pain after surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: somatic - relating to the body, especially as distinct from the viscera
