refounding

noun
establishing again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of establishing something again after it has ended, failed, or been changed

Examples

The refounding of orchestra
The refounding of the orchestra attracted many new talented musicians.
After years of inactivity, the refounding of the festival was celebrated by the whole town.
The refounding of the company marked a new era of innovation and growth.
The refounding of the university brought in new leadership and a modern curriculum.
After years of decline, the club underwent a complete refounding to attract new members.
The refounding of the movement focused on inclusivity and updated goals.

  • synonym: reestablishment - the act of setting up something again
  • synonym: reorganization - the process of arranging something in a new way
