stem

verb
originate from; arise from
Pronunciation: / stɛm / (AmE), / stɛm / (BrE)
to arise or originate from a particular cause or situation

Examples

Problems stem from stress
Her anxiety stemmed from a difficult childhood.
Many English words stem from Latin or Greek roots.
Our challenges often stem from poor communication within the team.
The problems were stemming from a lack of clear communication between departments.
Many health issues can be stemmed from poor nutrition and lack of exercise.
Her anxiety stems from a difficult experience she had as a child.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arise - to come into being or originate
  • antonym: cause - to make something happen
  • synonym: derive - to come from or be developed from something
  • synonym: originate - to have a specified beginning; arise
  • synonym: result - to happen as a consequence of something
