timespan

noun
duration of time
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪmˌspæn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌspæn / (BrE)
a period or length of time between two points or during which something happens or exists

Examples

A short timespan between updates can improve the user experience.
The timespan of the project was extended due to unexpected delays.
Over a timespan of ten years, the city changed dramatically.
Scientists measured the timespan between the two major earthquakes.
