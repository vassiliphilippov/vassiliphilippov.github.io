grandmaster

noun
world-class chess or bridge player
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrændˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːnˌmɑːstə /; / ˈɡrændˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who has achieved the highest official title or rank in chess, bridge, or certain martial arts

Examples

Chess grandmaster
Many aspire to earn the title of grandmaster in the competitive world of chess.
She became a chess grandmaster at the age of fifteen, breaking several records.
The bridge grandmaster is known for his brilliant strategies and calm demeanor under pressure.
The youngest grandmaster in chess history broke the previous record by several months.
She trained for years before finally earning the title of grandmaster in taekwondo.
Many aspiring players dream of becoming a grandmaster one day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: champion - a person who has won a competition or holds a top title
  • degree lower: international master - a chess title ranking below grandmaster
  • degree lower: master - a skilled player, but lower than grandmaster in chess
  • antonym: novice - a person new to a field or activity
