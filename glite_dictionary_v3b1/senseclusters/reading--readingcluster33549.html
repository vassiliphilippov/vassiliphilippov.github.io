reading

noun
interpretation or performance (of text, music, law, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
a particular interpretation or performance of a text, law, piece of music, or artistic work; or a way of understanding the meaning or significance of something

Examples

A reading of the law
Critics praised his unique reading of Beethoven’s Ninth Symphony.
Her reading of the poem revealed new layers of meaning to the audience.
The court's reading of the law was different from what most people expected.
Her reading of the novel emphasized its feminist themes.
The professor offered a new reading of Shakespeare's Hamlet.
There are several possible readings of his ambiguous statement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or interpreting something
  • antonym: misreading - an incorrect or mistaken interpretation
