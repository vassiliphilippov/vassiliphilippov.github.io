cop

verb
steal; take unlawfully (slang)
6.9
Pronunciation: / kɑp / (AmE), / kɒp / (BrE)
to take, steal, or obtain something, especially in a casual or slang way

Examples

To cop a phone
He got caught trying to cop a phone from the electronics store.
Someone tried to cop my bag while I was distracted by the street performer.
Thieves frequently cop wallets in crowded markets.
He managed to cop a pair of limited-edition sneakers before they sold out.
She was copping tickets for her friends at the box office.
If you want to cop a good deal, you have to act fast.

  • synonym: get - to obtain or receive something
  • antonym: lose - to no longer have something
  • antonym: return - to give back something that was taken or used
  • synonym: rob - to take property illegally from a person or place
  • synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right
