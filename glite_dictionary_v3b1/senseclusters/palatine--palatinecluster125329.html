palatine

adjective
relating to a count palatine's rights or authority
Pronunciation: / ˈpæl.ə.taɪn /; / ˈpæləˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.tɪn /; / ˈpalaˌtiːn / (BrE)
relating to a count palatine, royal privileges, or someone with royal authority (historical/governmental)

Examples

Palatine rights
During the Middle Ages, certain regions enjoyed palatine status, allowing their rulers special privileges.
The palatine authority granted him judicial independence from the monarch.
The duke held palatine powers within his territory, ruling almost like a king.
A palatine official managed the affairs of the magnificent estate.
The palatine gardens were reserved for the enjoyment of the royal family.
The palace's palatine splendor impressed visitors from across the land.
