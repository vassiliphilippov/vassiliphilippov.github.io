circumscribe

verb
to restrict within limits
6.3 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.kəm.skraɪb/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.kəm.skraɪb/ (BrE)
to limit or restrict something within certain boundaries

Examples

To circumscribe freedom
His creativity was circumscribed by a lack of resources and support.
Strict policies circumscribe what students can and cannot do on campus.
These new laws will inevitably circumscribe the powers of local government.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • antonym: free - not limited or restricted
  • synonym: restrict - to put a limit on; keep under control
