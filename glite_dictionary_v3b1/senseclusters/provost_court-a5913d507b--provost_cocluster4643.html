provost court

noun
military court for minor offenses in occupied area
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌvoʊst kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈprɒvəʊst kɔːt / (BrE)
a military court established to try individuals charged with minor offenses in an occupied area

Examples

Provost court trial
During the occupation, any disputes involving soldiers and locals were brought before a provost court.
He was sentenced to a week of hard labor by the provost court after being found guilty of disorderly conduct.
The military commander established a provost court to handle cases of petty theft among civilians.
