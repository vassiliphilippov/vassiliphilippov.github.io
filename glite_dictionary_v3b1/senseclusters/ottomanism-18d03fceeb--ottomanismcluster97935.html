Ottomanism

noun
Ottoman unity ideology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑtəmənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒtəmənˌɪzəm / (BrE)
a political ideology advocating unity and equal citizenship for all peoples within the Ottoman Empire

Examples

Ottomanism as an ideology
In the late nineteenth century, Ottomanism was promoted to strengthen the empire's internal cohesion.
Supporters of Ottomanism believed that shared citizenship could overcome ethnic divisions.
The decline of Ottomanism led to the rise of competing national identities within the empire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalism - a political ideology focused on promoting the interests of a particular nation
  • synonym: pan-Islamism - a political movement aiming for unity among Muslims
