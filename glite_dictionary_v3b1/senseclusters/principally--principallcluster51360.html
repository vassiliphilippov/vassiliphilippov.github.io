Synonyms

  • primarily
    2.4 20/m
    primarily focuses on the main reason or purpose and appears in formal contexts, while principally is more general.
  • mainly
    1.0 19/m
    mainly means for the most part in everyday use and is very common, while principally is slightly more formal.
  • chiefly
    3.0 1/m
    chiefly stresses the main part or cause and sounds a bit old-fashioned, while principally is more neutral.
  • predominantly
    3.7 5/m
    predominantly implies a large majority or strong dominance in situations, while principally is less forceful.
  • mostly
    1.0 70/m
    mostly is common and informal for saying something happens in most cases, while principally is slightly more formal.
  • largely
    2.4 30/m
    largely points to a big part or amount and is used for extent, while principally focuses on main cause.
  • for the most part
    3.2 12/m
    for the most part says something happens in most situations but leaves exceptions, while principally feels more formal and concise.
  • in the main
    3.7 0.4/m
    in the main is a British, somewhat formal phrase meaning generally, while principally is used more broadly in both styles.
