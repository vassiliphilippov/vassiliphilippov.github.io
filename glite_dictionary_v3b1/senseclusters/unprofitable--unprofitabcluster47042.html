unprofitable

adjective
not making a profit
4.9 1.17/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàpr…ëÀêf…™t…ôb…ôl / (AmE), /  ånÀàpr…íf…™t…ôb…ôl / (BrE)
not making or likely to make a profit; not giving financial gain.

Examples

Unprofitable business
Investing in that business turned out to be unprofitable for everyone involved.
The company closed several unprofitable stores last year to cut costs.
They decided to discontinue the unprofitable product line after several years of losses.

Synonyms

  • unremunerative
    9.3 0.01/m
    unremunerative describes work that gives little or no pay, while unprofitable means not making financial gain or likely to lose money.
  • profitless
    5.5 0.06/m
    profitless describes something that brings no profit and seems pointless, while unprofitable simply means it does not make financial gain.
  • uneconomic
    6.7 0.20/m
    uneconomic means not cost effective or not worth the expense, while unprofitable means it fails to bring financial gain.
