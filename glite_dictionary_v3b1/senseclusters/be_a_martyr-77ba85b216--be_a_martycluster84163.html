be a martyr

verb
sacrifice oneself for others, often self-pitying
2.4 6.6
Pronunciation: /biː ə ˈmɑːr.tər/ /; / /bɪ ə ˈmɑːr.tər/ (AmE), /biː ə ˈmɑː.tə/ /; / /bɪ ə ˈmɑː.tə/ (BrE)
to suffer or sacrifice oneself, often unnecessarily or to gain sympathy, especially for the benefit of others

Examples

To be a martyr unnecessarily

He refused to be a martyr at work by always working overtime without complaint.
She often chooses to be a martyr by taking on everyone else's problems.
