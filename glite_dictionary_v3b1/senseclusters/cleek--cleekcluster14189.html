cleek

verb
to hook or snatch
Pronunciation: / kliːk / (AmE), / kliːk / (BrE)
to seize, catch, or pull something toward oneself with a hook or similar tool, especially in Scottish or archaic usage

Examples

To cleek a fish
The fisherman tried to cleek the salmon from the river with his long pole.
In old tales, thieves would cleek valuables through open windows using a hooked stick.
She managed to cleek the basket from the high shelf with a metal hook.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grab - to take hold of something suddenly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something held
  • synonym: snatch - to quickly seize something
