Pegasus

proper noun
winged horse in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛɡəsəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛɡəsəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, an immortal winged horse that sprang from the blood of Medusa and is a symbol of poetic or creative inspiration.

Examples

The winged Pegasus
A statue of Pegasus stands proudly at the entrance to the museum.
In the ancient myth, Pegasus helps Bellerophon defeat the monstrous Chimera.
Poets often use Pegasus as a symbol of inspiration and creativity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bellerophon - hero who tamed Pegasus in Greek mythology
  • synonym: Medusa - Gorgon slain whose blood gave birth to Pegasus
