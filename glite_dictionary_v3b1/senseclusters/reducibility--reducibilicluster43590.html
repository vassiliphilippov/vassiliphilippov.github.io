reducibility

noun
ability to be reduced
8.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˌduːsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / rɪˌdjuːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a chemical substance to undergo reduction by gaining electrons in a reaction

Examples

Reducibility of a metal
The reducibility of the compound was tested in various chemical environments.
Scientists are interested in the reducibility of metals for use in batteries.
Understanding the reducibility of minerals helps in designing efficient industrial processes.
