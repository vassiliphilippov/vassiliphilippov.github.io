nasal septum

noun
partition dividing the nasal cavity
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪzəl ˈsɛptəm / (AmE), / ˈneɪzl ˈsɛptəm / (BrE)
the partition of bone and cartilage that divides the nasal cavity into two nostrils

Examples

The nasal septum bone
A deviated nasal septum can cause breathing difficulties and frequent nosebleeds.
Swelling of the nasal septum may result from an infection or trauma.
The surgeon repaired the patient's nasal septum after a sports injury.
