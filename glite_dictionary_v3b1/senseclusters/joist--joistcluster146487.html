joist

noun
a supporting beam for floors or ceilings
4.8 6.0
Pronunciation: /dʒɔɪst/ (AmE), /dʒɔɪst/ (BrE)
A long, strong piece of wood, metal, or concrete placed horizontally in a building, used to support a floor or ceiling.

Examples

Wooden joists support
During renovation, the crew replaced several rotten joists in the ceiling.
The carpenter installed each joist carefully to ensure a level floor.
Water damage weakened the wooden joists beneath the kitchen.
