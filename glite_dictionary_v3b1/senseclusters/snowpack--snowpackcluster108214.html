snowpack

noun
layer of accumulated snow
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌpæk / (BrE)
a thick layer of snow that builds up on the ground, especially in mountains, and lasts through the winter

Examples

Mountain snowpack
The mountain snowpack provides essential water for rivers during the summer months.
Scientists measure the snowpack each year to predict possible droughts.
A deep snowpack can increase the risk of avalanches in the spring.
The snowpack in the mountains is deeper than usual this year.
Scientists measure the snowpack each spring to predict water supplies for the summer.
A thin snowpack can increase the risk of avalanches in steep areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drift - a mass of snow piled up by the wind
  • degree lower: frost - a thin layer of ice crystals on surfaces from freezing temperatures
  • degree higher: glacier - a large, slow-moving mass of ice formed from compacted snow
  • degree higher: ice - frozen water, often forming a solid layer
