diwan

noun
Muslim council of state or ministry
Pronunciation: / diˈwɑːn / (AmE), / diˈwɑːn / (BrE)
A council of state or government ministry in some Muslim countries, especially in the context of historical Islamic administration.

Examples

The diwan of finance
A new diwan was established to oversee financial reforms in the sultanate.
Historians often study the archives of the diwan to understand governance in medieval Islamic states.
The Ottoman Empire administered its provinces through a diwan that made key policy decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divan - an alternative or obsolete spelling of diwan, also referring to council or government body
