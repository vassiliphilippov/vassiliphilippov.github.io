spoil

noun
goods taken by force, especially in war
Pronunciation: / spɔɪl / (AmE), / spɔɪl / (BrE)
items of value taken by force, especially in war; loot or plunder

Examples

War spoils
For centuries, conquerors claimed the spoils as their reward.
The soldiers returned home with the spoils of war.
The victors divided the spoils among themselves after the battle.
The pirates divided the spoil among themselves after the raid.
The tomb had long ago been emptied of its spoil by grave robbers.
Victorious soldiers returned home laden with the spoil of battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: booty - valuables seized during war or piratical activity
  • synonym: loot - goods stolen or taken by force, especially in war
  • synonym: plunder - goods taken by force or theft
