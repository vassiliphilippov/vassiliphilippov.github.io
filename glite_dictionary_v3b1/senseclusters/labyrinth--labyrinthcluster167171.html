labyrinth

noun
maze of complex paths or tunnels
Pronunciation: / ˈlæb.əˌrɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈlæb.ər.ɪnθ / (BrE)
A complex structure of interconnected passages or paths, often difficult to navigate or escape from.

Examples

A labyrinth of tunnels
Archaeologists explored the ancient labyrinth beneath the city, searching for hidden chambers.
I got lost in the labyrinth of narrow alleys in the old quarter.
The children played hide and seek in the labyrinth of hedges behind the old mansion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maze - a network of paths or hedges designed as a puzzle
