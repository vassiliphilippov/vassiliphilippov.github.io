low-density

adjective
having a low concentration
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˈdɛnsəti /; / ˌloʊˈdɛn.sə.ti / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈdɛnsəti /; / ˌləʊˈdɛn.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
having a small amount of people, things, or material in a given space; not dense

Examples

Low-density foam
The company developed a low-density foam for lightweight packaging solutions.
These apartments are located in a low-density residential neighborhood with plenty of open space.
Urban planners aim to preserve green spaces by supporting low-density housing developments.
Helium is a low-density gas compared to air, which is why balloons filled with it float.
The engineer recommended using low-density foam for better insulation.
They built new homes in a low-density residential area to reduce crowding.
