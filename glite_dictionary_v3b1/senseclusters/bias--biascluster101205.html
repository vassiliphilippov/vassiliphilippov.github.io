bias

noun
systematic unfair skew in algorithms/data
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.əs/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.əs/ (BrE)
(in computing, data science, and AI) a systematic skew, error, or prejudice in data, models, or algorithms that leads to unfair or discriminatory outcomes for particular individuals or groups.

Examples

Bias in data
Investigators found that the hiring algorithm produced clear bias against candidates from certain schools.
Researchers measure bias in training data to prevent models from learning unfair stereotypes.
After complaints, the company audited its system and fixed several sources of bias in recommendations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discrimination - the unjust or prejudicial treatment of different categories of people, often an outcome of biased systems
  • synonym: prejudice - a preconceived opinion or unfair feeling for or against a group or person
