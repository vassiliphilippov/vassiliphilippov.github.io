error

noun
misconception from incorrect information
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrɚ /; / ˈɛr.ɚ /; / ˈɛrər / (AmE), / ˈɛrə /; / ˈɛr.ər / (BrE)
a mistake or something that is not correct (such as in a belief, calculation, action, or decision)

Examples

Make an error
He developed an error after reading an outdated textbook.
Her argument was based on an error about the causes of climate change.
Many people act on error because they trusted unreliable sources.
An error in calculation led to the wrong answer.
She discovered a spelling error in the document before printing.
The report contained an error that affected the final results.
She later realized that her biggest error was trusting him too easily.
Simple errors in calculation can lead to incorrect results.
The pilot admitted that a technical error caused the delay in landing.
