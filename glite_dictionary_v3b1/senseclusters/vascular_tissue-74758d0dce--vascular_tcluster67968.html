vascular tissue

noun
tissue transporting water and nutrients in plants
Pronunciation: / ˈvæskjələr ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈvaskjʊlə ˈtɪsjuː / (BrE)
the tissue in higher plants that transports water and nutrients throughout the body of the plant

Examples

Plant vascular tissue
Botanists study vascular tissue to better understand how plants adapt to different environments.
Damage to the vascular tissue can seriously hinder a plant's growth and survival.
Most higher plants rely on vascular tissue to transport water and nutrients from the soil to their leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phloem - tissue that transports nutrients in plants
  • synonym: xylem - tissue that carries water from roots to leaves in plants
