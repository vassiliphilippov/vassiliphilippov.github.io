sputum

noun
saliva and mucus from the respiratory tract
Pronunciation: / ˈspjuːtəm / (AmE), / ˈspjuːtəm / (BrE)
Saliva and mucus coughed up from the respiratory tract, often used for medical analysis.

Examples

A sputum sample
The doctor requested a sputum sample to test for respiratory infections.
The nurse carefully collected the patient's sputum in a sterile container.
Thick, yellow sputum can be a sign of bacterial bronchitis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phlegm - thick mucus produced in the respiratory passages
