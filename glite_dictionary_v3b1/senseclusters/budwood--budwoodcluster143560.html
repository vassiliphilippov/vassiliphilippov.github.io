budwood

noun
plant stem for grafting
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbʌdˌwʊd / (BrE)
a piece of plant stem containing buds, used for grafting onto another plant

Examples

Fresh budwood for grafting
The nursery ordered fresh budwood to propagate new varieties of apple trees.
Careful selection of budwood ensures healthy growth after grafting.
Farmers often store budwood in cool conditions before using it in the spring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rootstock - the root part of a plant onto which budwood is grafted
  • synonym: scion - a young shoot or twig used for grafting
