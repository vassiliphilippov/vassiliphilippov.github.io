parallelepiped

noun
solid with parallelogram faces
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.əˌlɛ.ləˈpaɪ.pɛd / (AmE), / ˌpar.ə.lɛ.lɪˈpiː.pɛd / (BrE)
A solid figure in geometry with six faces, each of which is a parallelogram.

Examples

A parallelepiped shape
A cube is a special case of a parallelepiped where all faces are squares.
In geometry class, we learned how to draw a parallelepiped accurately.
The volume of a parallelepiped can be calculated using the scalar triple product.
