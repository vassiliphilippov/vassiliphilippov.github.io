abolishable

adjective
able to be abolished
Pronunciation: / əˈbɑlɪʃəbl̩ / (AmE), / əˈbɒlɪʃəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be abolished; capable of being done away with or eliminated

Examples

An abolishable law
Many outdated laws are still abolishable through legislative action.
The death penalty is abolishable in some countries but permanent in others.
They argued that certain practices are not only unethical but also abolishable with will and effort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abolish - to formally put an end to a system, practice, or institution
  • antonym: irremovable - impossible to abolish or remove
