cherry-pick data

verb
select only favorable data
9.6 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛri pɪk ˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri pɪk ˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
to selectively choose only information or data that supports a particular argument or outcome, while ignoring data that may contradict it

Examples

To cherry-pick data carefully
If you cherry-pick data, your conclusions may be misleading or biased.
Researchers should never cherry-pick data to make their results look better than they are.
The politician was accused of cherry-picking data to support her policy proposals.

  • synonym: cherry-pick - to selectively choose the best or most desirable items
