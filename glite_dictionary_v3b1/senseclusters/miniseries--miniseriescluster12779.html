miniseries

noun
short limited series
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪniˌsɪriz / (AmE), / ˈmɪniˌsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a short series in television, radio, books, or other media that tells a complete story in a limited number of parts or episodes

Examples

A miniseries on TV
The author released a miniseries of graphic novels that concluded after just five issues.
Fans eagerly awaited each episode of the new podcast miniseries about unsolved mysteries.
The radio miniseries told a complete story over six nightly broadcasts.
The new crime miniseries was a hit with viewers.
She watched the entire miniseries over the weekend because she could not wait to see the ending.
The historical miniseries received several awards for its writing and performances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anthology - a collection of separate stories or episodes, often with different characters
  • synonym: serial - a story or program released in regular parts or episodes
  • synonym: series - a set of television episodes with a continuing story or theme
