co-chair

verb
to lead jointly
4.1
Pronunciation: /koʊ tʃer/ (AmE), /kəʊ tʃeə/ (BrE)
to serve as a joint chairperson of a meeting, committee, or event with another person

Examples

To co-chair a meeting
She co-chaired the annual conference with her colleague last year.
Two professors will co-chair the committee to ensure balanced leadership.
He has co-chaired several important meetings for the organization.
