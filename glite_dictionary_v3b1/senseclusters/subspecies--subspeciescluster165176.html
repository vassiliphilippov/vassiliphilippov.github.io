subspecies

noun
a division of a species in taxonomy
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌspiːʃiz / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌspiːʃiːz / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic group below species, typically formed by populations of a species that are geographically isolated and show distinct characteristics.

Examples

A subspecies group
Scientists debate whether these populations represent a new subspecies or simply variation within the species.
The Galápagos tortoise has several recognized subspecies adapted to different islands.
This bird’s coloring is unique to its subspecies, which lives only in southern regions.
