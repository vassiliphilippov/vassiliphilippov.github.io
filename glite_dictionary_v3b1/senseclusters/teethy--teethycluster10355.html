teethy

adjective
with toothlike edges
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːθi / (AmE), / ˈtiːθi / (BrE)
having toothlike projections or a jagged, serrated edge resembling teeth

Examples

Teethy blade
The old pocketknife had a teethy blade that made it ideal for cutting rope.
The leaf's teethy margin helped distinguish it from other species in the garden.
She admired the teethy design of the saw, which made woodworking much easier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: serrated - having a notched or sawlike edge
  • antonym: smooth - having an even, flat surface without projections
