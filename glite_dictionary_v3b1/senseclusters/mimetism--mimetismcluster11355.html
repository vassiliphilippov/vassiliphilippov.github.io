mimetism

noun
imitation in society
9.9
Pronunciation: /mɪ.ˈme.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /maɪ.ˈmiː.tɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /mɪ.ˈme.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /maɪ.ˈmiː.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the tendency or practice of imitating the behavior, customs, or expressions of others, especially in social situations

Examples

Social mimetism
The artist's mimetism was evident in how closely his paintings resembled those of his mentor.
Social mimetism can influence the way people dress and speak in different groups.
Her mimetism made her quickly adapt to the customs of every new place she visited.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitation - the act of copying someone or something
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being new and different from others
