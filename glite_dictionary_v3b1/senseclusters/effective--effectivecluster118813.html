effective

adjective
producing a desired result
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
producing the intended or desired result; successful in achieving a purpose or having a strong impact

Examples

An effective solution
Her speech was so effective that it changed many people's minds.
The new marketing strategy proved to be very effective in increasing sales.
This medicine is especially effective against coughs caused by allergies.
Group work can be more effective than working alone for some tasks.
That advertisement was surprisingly effective in attracting new customers.
The new medicine proved to be very effective in treating the illness.
She made an effective presentation that convinced the entire board.
These new policies have proved to be very effective in reducing waste.
This antibiotic is not effective against viral infections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ineffective - not producing the desired result
