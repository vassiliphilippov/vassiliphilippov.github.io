(of a verb form) not limited by tense, person, or number, as in infinitives, participles, or gerunds.

Examples

Non-finite verb form
Gerunds and infinitives are both examples of non-finite verb forms in English.
In the sentence 'She wants to leave', 'to leave' is a non-finite verb form.
Participle clauses often use non-finite verbs to convey additional information.
