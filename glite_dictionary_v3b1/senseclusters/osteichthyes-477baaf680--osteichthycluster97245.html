Osteichthyes

noun
class of bony fish
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːstiˈɪkθiˌiz / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˈɪkθiːz /; / ˌɒstiˈɪkθiˌiːz / (BrE)
a class of fish with skeletons made mainly of bone (bony fish), including most modern fish

Examples

Osteichthyes bony fish
Scientists study the evolution of Osteichthyes to understand how modern bony fishes developed.
The Osteichthyes represent the largest class of vertebrates in the animal kingdom.
Unlike sharks, which have cartilage skeletons, members of the Osteichthyes have skeletons made mostly of bone.
Salmon, trout, and tuna are all classified within the Osteichthyes.
The Osteichthyes include the vast majority of modern fish species.
Unlike sharks, which have cartilage skeletons, the Osteichthyes possess true bone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Chondrichthyes - class of cartilaginous fishes (sharks and rays)
