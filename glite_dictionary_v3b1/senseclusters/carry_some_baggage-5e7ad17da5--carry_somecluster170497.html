carry some baggage

verb
have unresolved emotional issues
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri səm ˈbæɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkari səm ˈbaɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
To have unresolved emotional issues or past experiences that continue to affect one's behavior or attitudes.

Examples

Many people carry some baggage into new jobs after difficult experiences at their previous workplaces.
She tries to hide it, but she clearly carries some baggage from her last relationship.
