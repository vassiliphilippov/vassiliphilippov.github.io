sneak

verb
steal or take secretly
2.8 2.4
Pronunciation: /sniːk/ (AmE), /sniːk/ (BrE)
to move, take, or give something in a secret or stealthy way so that others do not notice

Examples

To sneak a snack
A thief managed to sneak her phone out of her bag while she was distracted.
He tried to sneak a few extra cookies from the kitchen without anyone noticing.
The children would sometimes sneak candy from the store when the clerk wasn't looking.
He tried to sneak his phone into the classroom without the teacher noticing.
Security guards caught him attempting to sneak backstage at the concert.
She would often sneak a snack late at night when everyone else was asleep.
He would often sneak into the kitchen late at night for a snack.
She tried to sneak past the sleeping dog without waking it.
The children sneaked out of the house to play in the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully in order to avoid being heard or noticed
  • antonym: hand over - to give (something) to someone openly
  • synonym: slip - to give or hand over (something) discreetly or quietly
  • synonym: smuggle - to move (goods) illegally into or out of a country, often hidden
  • synonym: steal - to take (someone else's property) without permission or legal right
  • antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps in a specified direction (contrasts with 'sneak')
  • synonym: tiptoe - to walk quietly and carefully with your heels raised
