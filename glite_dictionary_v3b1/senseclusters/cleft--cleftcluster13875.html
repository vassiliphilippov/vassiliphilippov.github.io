cleft

verb
to split or divide
6.3
Pronunciation: / klɛft / (AmE), / klɛft / (BrE)
to split or divide something by a crack or fissure.

Examples

Tree cleft by lightning
The ancient tree was cleft by a bolt of lightning during the storm.
Over time, the glacier cleft the mountain into two distinct peaks.
The log was cleft in half with a single swing of the axe.
