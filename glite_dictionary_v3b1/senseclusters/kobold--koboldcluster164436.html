kobold

noun
German folklore sprite
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌboʊld /; / ˈkoʊˌbɔld / (AmE), / ˈkəʊbɒld /; / ˈkəʊˌbɒld / (BrE)
a mythical, often mischievous, sprite or goblin from German folklore, believed to live in houses or mines

Examples

A mischievous kobold
According to legend, a kobold would play tricks on miners who disrespected the earth.
The children listened in awe to stories about the mischievous kobold living in the cellar.
Some believed that leaving bread out at night would keep the household kobold happy.
In the old story, a kobold would hide tools from the miners as a prank.
Legends say that a kobold can bring good luck or cause trouble in the home.
The children dressed as a kobold for the village festival celebrating local myths.

Related Terms

  • synonym: goblin - a mischievous, ugly creature from folklore
  • synonym: sprite - a small, supernatural being or elf
