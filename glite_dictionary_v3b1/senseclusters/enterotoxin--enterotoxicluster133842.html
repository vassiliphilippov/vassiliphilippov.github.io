enterotoxin

noun
toxin affecting intestinal lining
5.2 7.1
Pronunciation: /en.tə.rə.ˈtɑːk.sɪn/ /; / /en.tə.roʊ.ˈtɑːk.sɪn/ (AmE), /en.tə.rə.ˈtɒk.sɪn/ /; / /en.tə.rəʊ.ˈtɒk.sɪn/ (BrE)
a toxin produced by certain bacteria that specifically targets the cells lining the intestines, often causing gastrointestinal symptoms

Examples

Bacterial enterotoxin effect
Cholera bacteria release an enterotoxin that causes severe diarrhea and dehydration.
Scientists developed a vaccine targeting the enterotoxin of E. coli to prevent foodborne illness.
The patient's symptoms were traced to an enterotoxin produced by contaminated shellfish.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cytotoxin - a toxin that is toxic to cells in general
  • synonym: exotoxin - a toxin released by bacteria into surrounding tissue
  • degree higher: neurotoxin - a toxin that targets the nervous system
