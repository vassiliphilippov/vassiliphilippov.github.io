shake up

verb
make changes in arrangement or organization
2.6
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to change or reorganize the structure, arrangement, or management of something, often to improve it

Examples

Shake up the company
After the merger, the new CEO began to shake up the entire organization.
Sometimes you need to shake up your daily routine to keep things interesting.
The company plans to shake up its management team to improve performance.
A new manager has been hired to shake up the sales department.
After years of decline, the company decided it was time to shake up its business model.
The recent scandals forced the board to shake up its leadership structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reorganize - to arrange something or a system in a new way
  • antonym: shake down - search thoroughly; also to extort money (unrelated meaning)
