Examples

Shake up the company

Sometimes you need to shake up your daily routine to keep things interesting.

Synonyms

  • reorganize
    3.4 4.04/m
    reorganize is a neutral term for arranging parts or people in a new way, while shake up suggests sudden, disruptive change.
  • restructure
    4.4 4.92/m
    restructure describes changing the basic structure of an organization to adapt, while shake up sounds more informal and faster.
  • reshuffle
    5.3 0.12/m
    reshuffle means moving people or roles around, often without big change, while shake up implies bold, wide-reaching reorganization.
  • retool
    5.9 0.66/m
    retool means changing tools, methods, or systems to update or improve them, while shake up focuses on reorganizing people or management.
  • reorchestrate
    7.5 0.00/m
    reorchestrate means rearranging how tasks and people work together for smoother operation, while shake up implies sudden structural change.
