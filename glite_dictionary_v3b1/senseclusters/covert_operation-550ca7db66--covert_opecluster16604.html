covert operation

noun
secret government action that can be denied
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊvərt ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən /; / ˈkoʊvərt ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkəʊvɜːt ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən /; / ˈkəʊvət ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a secret or undercover action, especially by a government, military, or intelligence agency, planned so that it is not detected or can be denied

Examples

A covert operation
He was recruited to take part in a covert operation during the early years of the conflict.
Many covert operations are designed to ensure plausible deniability for the sponsoring government.
The agency launched a covert operation to gather intelligence in the foreign country.
A covert operation was launched to rescue the hostages without alerting the kidnappers.
She was involved in several covert operations during her military service.
The agency planned a covert operation to infiltrate the enemy's network.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clandestine operation - a secret operation intended to conceal its existence or execution
  • antonym: overt operation - an open and publicly known intelligence or military action
