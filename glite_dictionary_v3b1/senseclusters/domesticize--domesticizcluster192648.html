domesticize

verb
make something docile or tame
3.9
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɛstɪˌsaɪz /; / dəˈmɛstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an animal, plant, or person) docile, tractable, or suitable for domestic life; to tame; to adapt for human use or companionship

Examples

To domesticize wild animals
It took several generations to domesticize the once-feral horses on the island.
Researchers are studying ways to domesticize certain wild plants for agriculture.
The zookeeper managed to domesticize the wild fox over time.
Attempts to domesticise the stubborn animal were only partly successful.
Early humans began to domesticise wolves, leading to the dogs we know today.
They worked hard to domesticise the wild horses on the farm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: domesticate - to adapt (an animal or plant) for domestic use or life
  • synonym: tame - to make an animal less wild; to domesticate
