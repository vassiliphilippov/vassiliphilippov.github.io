antinomian

noun
a follower of the doctrine that faith alone is necessary for salvation

A person who believes that, under the gospel dispensation of grace, the moral law is of no use or obligation because faith alone is necessary to salvation.

Examples

An antinomian preacher
During the Reformation, some antinomians argued that faith alone guaranteed salvation, dismissing traditional rules.
He denied that his teachings were antinomian, insisting he still valued moral conduct.
The theologian was accused of being an antinomian after questioning the ongoing role of moral law in Christian life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antinomianism - the doctrine that Christians are released by grace from the obligation of obeying the moral law
  • antonym: legalist - a person who adheres strictly to the law or to a particular set of rules
