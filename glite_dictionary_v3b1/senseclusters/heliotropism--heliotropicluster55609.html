heliotropism

noun
growth/movement toward sunlight
9.6 7.9
Pronunciation: /hiː.lɪ.ə.ˈtroʊ.pɪ.zəm/ /; / /hɪ.lɪ.ə.ˈtroʊ.pɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /hiː.lɪ.ə.ˈtrɒp.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /hɪ.lɪ.ə.ˈtrɒp.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the directional growth or movement of an organism, especially a plant, in response to sunlight

Examples

heliotropism in plants

Researchers studied the heliotropism of young seedlings to understand how they respond to sunlight.
The sunflower is well known for its heliotropism, turning to follow the sun throughout the day.
