twirly

adjective
spiral or spinning
4.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtw…úÀêrli / (AmE), / Ààtw…úÀêli / (BrE)
having twists, curls, or a tendency to spin or move in circles

Examples

Twirly dress spins
She wore a twirly dress that spun out when she danced.
The child drew a twirly pattern with her finger in the sand.
His twirly mustache made him look like an old-fashioned movie villain.

Synonyms

  • swirly
    3.9 0.08/m
    swirly shows curved or spiral patterns you can see, while twirly suggests physical twists or a tendency to spin in circles.
  • whirly
    7.1 0.00/m
    whirly means spinning quickly or making fast turns, while twirly suggests gentler twists, curls, or a tendency to move in circles.
  • spinny
    6.7 0.03/m
    spinny means turning very fast and often causing dizziness, while twirly focuses on twists, curls, or a general circular motion.
