spell

noun
state of enchantment or trance
8.2 17/m

a series of words or magical influence that is believed to have special power; a state of enchantment or trance

Examples

A magic spell
As soon as the music began, the audience fell into a spell of silence and wonder.
He seemed to be in a spell, staring out the window without moving for minutes.
The witch placed him under a powerful spell, causing him to forget his past.
He tried to break the spell by repeating a counter-charm.
The old book contains ancient spells written in a forgotten language.

Synonyms

  • incantation
    8.8 1.46/m
    incantation is a chanted series of words used in rituals, while spell is a more general word for magical words or effect.
  • charm
    8.2 2.68/m
    charm is a small word or object meant to protect or attract, while spell refers to words or an enchanted state.
  • enchantment
    7.7 0.88/m
    enchantment is the lasting magic placed on someone or something, while spell names the words or brief influence that cause it.
