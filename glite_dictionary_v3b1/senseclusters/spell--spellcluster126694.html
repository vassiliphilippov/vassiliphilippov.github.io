spell

noun
state of enchantment or trance
1.0
Pronunciation: / spɛl / (AmE), / spɛl / (BrE)
a series of words or magical influence that is believed to have special power; a state of enchantment or trance

Examples

A magic spell
As soon as the music began, the audience fell into a spell of silence and wonder.
He seemed to be in a spell, staring out the window without moving for minutes.
The witch placed him under a powerful spell, causing him to forget his past.
He tried to break the spell by repeating a counter-charm.
The old book contains ancient spells written in a forgotten language.
The witch cast a powerful spell that turned the prince into a frog.

Related Terms

  • antonym: awakening - The cessation of a trance or magical state; returning to normal consciousness
  • antonym: blessing - a prayer asking for divine favor and protection
  • antonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke a supernatural power to inflict harm
  • synonym: enchantment - A feeling of great pleasure; the state of being under a magical spell
  • synonym: incantation - a series of words said as a magic spell or charm
  • synonym: trance - A half-conscious state, often of heightened suggestibility, similar to an enchanted spell
