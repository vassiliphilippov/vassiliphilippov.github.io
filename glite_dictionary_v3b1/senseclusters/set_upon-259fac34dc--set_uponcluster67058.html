set upon

verb
attack suddenly from all sides
Pronunciation: / sɛt əˈpɑn / (AmE), / sɛt əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to attack someone or something suddenly and from all sides

Examples

To set upon an enemy
He was set upon by three masked men late at night.
The hikers were set upon by a swarm of angry bees as they passed the hive.
Wild dogs set upon the injured animal before help could arrive.
