to feel or make someone feel stifled, restricted, or unable to develop because of limiting circumstances

Examples

To suffocate ideas
Creativity often suffocates under rigid and uninspired leadership.
He feared that living in such a small town would suffocate his ambitions.
She felt she would suffocate if she stayed in that unfulfilling job any longer.
He feared that routine would suffocate his ambition if he stayed in the same job.
Rigid social expectations can suffocate individuality over time.
The constant micromanagement at work began to suffocate her creativity.
