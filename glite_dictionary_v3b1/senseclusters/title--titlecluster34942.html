title

noun
statutory or legislative heading
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtəl /; / ˈtaɪtɫ̩ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtəl / (BrE)
the name or heading of a book, chapter, film, artwork, or other creative work; or written material shown on screen in films or TV

Examples

Book title
Title IX of the Education Amendments prohibits sex discrimination in schools.
Congress passed several reforms under Title II of the Social Security Act.
The senator argued for changes to Title 21, which regulates controlled substances.
Each chapter begins with a title that summarizes its content.
She spent hours deciding on the perfect title for her essay.
The textbook uses bold titles to organize its main sections.
He couldn't remember the exact title of the song but hummed the tune.
Many paintings in the gallery didn't have a title displayed.
The title of her favorite novel is 'To Kill a Mockingbird'.
