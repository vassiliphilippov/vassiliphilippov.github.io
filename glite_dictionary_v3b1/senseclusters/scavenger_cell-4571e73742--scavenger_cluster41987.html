scavenger cell

noun
immune system cell that digests debris
Pronunciation: / ˈskævəndʒɚ sɛl / (AmE), / ˈskævɪndʒə sɛl / (BrE)
a cell in the body that engulfs and digests cellular debris and invading microorganisms, especially as part of the immune system

Examples

Scavenger cell activity
Scavenger cell activity increases during infection to help protect the body.
Scientists studied how scavenger cells respond to different bacteria in the lab.
The immune system relies on scavenger cells to remove pathogens and dead tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phagocyte - a type of cell that engulfs and absorbs waste material and harmful organisms
