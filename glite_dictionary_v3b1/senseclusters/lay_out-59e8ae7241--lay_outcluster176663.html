lay out

verb
arrange or prepare items for use
Pronunciation: / leɪ aʊt / (AmE), / leɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange, prepare, or display items in an orderly or visible way

Examples

To lay out the tools
Before the meeting, he laid out the documents on the conference table.
She laid out the ingredients on the counter before cooking dinner.
The teacher laid out the materials for the art lesson.
Before packing, she carefully laid out her clothes on the bed.
He laid out the tools on the workbench so he could reach them easily.
The report lays out the company's strategy for the next five years.
He laid out all the documents on the table for everyone to see.
The chef laid out the ingredients before starting to cook.
We laid out our plans in detail during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: display - to put something in a place where people can see it
  • antonym: scatter - to throw loosely about; distribute irregularly
  • synonym: set out - to arrange or display something for use or notice
