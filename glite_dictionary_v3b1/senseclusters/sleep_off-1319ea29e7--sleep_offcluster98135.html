sleep off

verb
recover by sleeping
Pronunciation: / sliːp ɔf /; / slip ɔf / (AmE), / sliːp ɒf / (BrE)
to recover from the effects of something unpleasant (such as illness, tiredness, or alcohol) by sleeping

Examples

To sleep off a hangover
After the party, he needed a full day to sleep off his hangover.
He tried to sleep off his headache after a long night out.
She hopes to sleep off her cold and feel better in the morning.
After the long flight, he needed to sleep off his exhaustion before going out to explore the city.
If you drink too much coffee at night, it can be hard to sleep off the jitters.
She decided to sleep off her headache instead of taking more medicine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sleep - rest by closing eyes and becoming unconscious for a period; natural periodic state of rest
  • synonym: sleep away - to spend or pass time by sleeping, often to make something pass
