expungement

noun
removal of legal record
8.7
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspʌndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌndʒmənt / (BrE)
the legal process of removing a record, especially a criminal record, from official files so it is treated as if it never existed

Examples

Criminal expungement
After years of good behavior, he was granted expungement of his criminal record.
The lawyer explained how expungement could help clients start fresh without past mistakes holding them back.
Many people seek expungement to improve their chances of finding employment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erasure - the act of removing or deleting something completely
  • antonym: record - an official document of something, especially a person's history
