tack

noun
a course or direction in sailing
8.7 6.1
Pronunciation: /tæk/ (AmE), /tæk/ (BrE)
(in sailing) the direction or course of a sailing boat, or the act of changing direction by turning the bow through the wind

Examples

A sailing tack
He learned to sail upwind by steadily shifting tack across the bay.
On each tack, the captain gave clear instructions to the crew.
The yacht changed tack as the wind shifted direction.
After the wind shifted, the captain ordered the crew to change the tack of the ship.
Sailors must be skilled at choosing the right tack to maintain speed in a race.
The yacht is on a starboard tack as it heads toward the harbor.
After each tack, the crew adjusted the sails for maximum speed.
Changing tack in strong winds requires skill and coordination from the sailors.
The yacht made a quick tack to avoid the rocky shoreline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat - to sail against the wind in zigzags
  • synonym: gybe - to change course in sailing by moving the stern through the wind
  • antonym: jibe - (nautical) turning a sailing boat's stern through the wind
  • antonym: tack (attach) - to fasten or fix in place with tacks or nails
