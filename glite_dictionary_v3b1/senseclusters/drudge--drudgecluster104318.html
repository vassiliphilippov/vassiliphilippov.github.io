drudge

noun
person who does dull, hard work
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: / drʌdʒ / (AmE), / drʌdʒ / (BrE)
a person who does hard, dull, or menial work, especially work that is boring or repetitive

Examples

Office drudge
He spent years as a drudge in the office, performing the most tedious tasks.
In the past, many factory workers were regarded as little more than drudges.
Janet refused to be the household drudge, always picking up after everyone else.
After years as an office drudge, she longed for a more creative career.
He felt like a drudge spending all day filing paperwork.
The factory drudges were often overlooked despite their hard work.
Although he was new at the company, he wasn't content to be just a drudge.
She refused to become a drudge and looked for more meaningful work.
Working as a drudge in the office left him feeling unfulfilled and tired.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laborer - a person doing unskilled manual work
  • synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to work
  • synonym: toiler - a person who works hard for long hours, often at unloved tasks
