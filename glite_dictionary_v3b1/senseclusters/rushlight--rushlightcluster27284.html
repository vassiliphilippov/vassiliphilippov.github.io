rushlight

noun
candle made from rush pith and tallow
5.5 9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌʃ.laɪt/ (AmE), /ˈrʌʃ.laɪt/ (BrE)
a slender candle made by soaking the dried pith of a rush plant in tallow, used especially in the past for lighting

Examples

A rushlight candle
By the dim glow of a rushlight, she read her letter late into the night.
He recalled evenings spent working by rushlight before the advent of gas lamps.
The old cottage was lit only by a single rushlight on the table.
