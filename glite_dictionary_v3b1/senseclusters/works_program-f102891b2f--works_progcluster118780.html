works program

noun
government job-creating public works plan
4.9
Pronunciation: / wɝːks ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / wɜːks ˈprəʊɡram / (BrE)
A government-funded initiative that provides jobs through the construction or maintenance of public works, such as roads, buildings, or infrastructure.

Examples

A works program project
Many highways and bridges were built under the New Deal's works program.
She found steady employment through a federally funded works program in her city.
The government launched a works program to reduce unemployment during the recession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: public works - projects such as highways or parks funded and built by the government for public use
  • synonym: relief program - government plan providing assistance or employment in economic crisis
