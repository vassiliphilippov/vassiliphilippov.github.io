homesteading

noun
claiming public land
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊmˌstɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌstɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of claiming, settling, and farming land to gain ownership, especially as part of a government program

Examples

Frontier homesteading
In the 1800s, many families moved west in search of opportunities for homesteading.
The government encouraged homesteading to help settle and develop remote regions.
After years of hard work, their homesteading efforts finally resulted in legal ownership of the land.
The government encouraged homesteading to help populate the western territories.
Many families faced harsh conditions during homesteading in the nineteenth century.
Photographs from the era show the daily life of people involved in homesteading.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act of establishing control over new lands and settling them
  • synonym: settlement - the act of establishing a community in a new place
  • antonym: squatting - occupying land or property without legal right
