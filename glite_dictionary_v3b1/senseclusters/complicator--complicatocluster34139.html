complicator

noun
one who complicates
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmplɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes a situation more difficult or complex

Examples

A complicator in problems
The sudden change in weather became a major complicator for the rescue team.
He is often seen as a complicator because he adds unnecessary steps to simple tasks.
New regulations can act as a complicator in the approval process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: facilitator - someone or something that makes a process easier
  • synonym: obstacle - something that makes progress more difficult
