blossomy

adjective
full of blossoms
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈblɑːsəmi / (AmE), / ˈblɒsəmi / (BrE)
covered with or resembling blossoms; full of flowers or having the scent or appearance of blossoms

Examples

Blossomy garden
The blossomy garden attracted many butterflies and bees in early summer.
We walked through a blossomy meadow filled with the scent of spring flowers.
Her dress had a blossomy pattern that reminded me of cherry trees in bloom.
The garden was especially blossomy in the spring, filling the air with sweet scents.
She wore a blossomy perfume that reminded him of a summer meadow.
After the rain, the entire park smelled blossomy and fresh.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bare - without any covering, especially flowers or leaves
  • synonym: blooming - producing or covered with flowers
  • synonym: floral - having a scent or appearance like flowers
  • synonym: flowery - full of or covered with flowers
  • synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • antonym: musty - having a stale, unpleasant smell
