intertidal

adjective
relating to the shore area between tides
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tərˈtaɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈtaɪ.dl̩ / (BrE)
of or relating to the area of the shore that is exposed to the air at low tide and submerged at high tide.

Examples

Intertidal zone
Children explored the intertidal pools, searching for shells and small sea creatures.
Many unique species thrive in the intertidal zone, adapting to both wet and dry conditions.
The intertidal region is rich in marine life, especially during low tide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subtidal - relating to the area always underwater below the low-tide mark
