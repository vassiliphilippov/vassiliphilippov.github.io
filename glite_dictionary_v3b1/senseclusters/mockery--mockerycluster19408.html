mockery

noun
ridicule, scornful imitation or derision
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑkəri /; / ˈmɑːkəri / (AmE), / ˈmɒkəri / (BrE)
the act of making fun of someone or something, especially by imitating them in a way that is intended to ridicule or satirize

His speech was met with mockery from the audience, who laughed at every mistake.
The cruel mockery in her voice made him blush with embarrassment.

Critics viewed the film as a mockery of the original novel, ridiculing its style and themes.
His poem was a brilliant mockery of the poet laureate's sentimental verses.
The play was a clever mockery of classical tragedy, filled with exaggerated characters and absurd dialogue.

The comedian's mockery of politicians drew both laughter and criticism.
Their mockery of celebrity culture was both amusing and insightful.
