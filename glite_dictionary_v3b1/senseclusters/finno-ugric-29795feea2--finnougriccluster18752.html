Finno-Ugric

noun
Uralic language family branch
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪnoʊ ˈjuːɡrɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪnəʊ ˈjuːɡrɪk / (BrE)
the branch of the Uralic language family that includes Finnish, Estonian, Hungarian, and related languages of northern Eurasia

Examples

Finno-Ugric language group
Estonian is a member of the Finno-Ugric language group, along with Finnish and Hungarian.
Scholars have long studied the origins and migration of the Finno-Ugric peoples.
The Finno-Ugric languages share many features despite the vast distances between their speakers.
