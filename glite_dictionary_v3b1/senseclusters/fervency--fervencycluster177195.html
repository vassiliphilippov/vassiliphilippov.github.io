fervency

noun
intense passion or enthusiasm
8.0 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…úÀêrv…ônsi / (AmE), / Ààf…úÀêv…ônsi / (BrE)
Intense and passionate feeling; great warmth or enthusiasm.

Examples

With fervency and passion
He delivered his speech with a fervency that moved the audience to tears.
Her fervency for social justice inspired everyone around her.
You could feel the fervency in her voice as she described her dream.

Synonyms

  • fervor
    6.3 3.26/m
    fervor is the common noun for intense enthusiasm in speech and writing, while fervency is a rarer variant.
  • fervidness
    9.0 0.00/m
    fervidness is a formal word for intense zeal and heat of feeling, while fervency is a less usual variant.
  • ardor
    7.5 1.05/m
    ardor is warm, personal passion often for people or causes, while fervency stresses intense general enthusiasm.
  • passion
    2.5 20/m
    passion is a powerful, sometimes sudden emotion or desire, while fervency points to steady, burning enthusiasm.
