Escherichia

noun
genus of intestinal bacteria
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛʃəˈrɪkiə / (AmE), / ˌɛʃəˈrɪkɪə /; / ˌɛʃəˈrɪkiə / (BrE)
a genus of rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria found in the intestines of humans and animals, including species such as Escherichia coli

Examples

Escherichia coli bacteria
Escherichia is a genus that includes important bacteria like E. coli.
Researchers studied various strains of Escherichia to understand antibiotic resistance.
The gut microbiome often contains species of Escherichia.
Escherichia is a genus of bacteria commonly found in the lower intestine of warm-blooded organisms.
Many strains of Escherichia are harmless, but some can cause foodborne illnesses.
The most well-known species in the genus Escherichia is Escherichia coli, often abbreviated as E. coli.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Escherichia coli - a species of bacterium commonly found in the intestines, sometimes causing food poisoning
