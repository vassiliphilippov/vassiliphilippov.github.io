Examples

Incapacitating effects

The patient suffered an incapacitating stroke and required full-time care.

Synonyms

  • disabling
    5.6 0.01/m
    disabling makes a person or machine unable to do certain tasks after injury or illness, while incapacitating broadly prevents normal functioning.
  • crippling
    5.8 0.39/m
    crippling causes severe long-term damage that stops normal use, while incapacitating makes someone or something unable to function.
  • paralyze
    5.3 12/m
    paralyze stops movement or action suddenly or permanently, while incapacitating means a loss of normal function more broadly.
  • immobilize
    6.0 1.81/m
    immobilize prevents movement or escape for a time, while incapacitating makes the whole person or thing unable to function normally.
