Aureomycin

noun
antibiotic drug (trade name)
Pronunciation: / ɔːrioʊˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ɔːriəʊˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
A yellow crystalline antibiotic used to treat various bacterial and rickettsial infections, known by the trade name Aureomycin.

Examples

Aureomycin antibiotic
Researchers studied the effectiveness of Aureomycin in treating typhus fever.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to Aureomycin over time.
The doctor prescribed Aureomycin to help control the infection.

Related Terms
