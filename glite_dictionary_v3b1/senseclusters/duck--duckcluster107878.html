duck

verb
lower quickly to avoid something
Pronunciation: / dʌk / (AmE), / dʌk / (BrE)
to quickly lower your head or body to avoid being hit, seen, or to get out of the way

Examples

To duck behind cover
He ducked behind a wall to avoid being seen.
She had to duck as the ball flew over her head.
When the branch swung toward him, he ducked instinctively.
As the camera flashed, I saw him duck out of the spotlight.
He managed to duck just in time, avoiding the branch.
She had to duck when the ball came flying towards her head.
