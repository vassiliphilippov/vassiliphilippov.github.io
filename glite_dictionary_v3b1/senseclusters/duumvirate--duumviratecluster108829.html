duumvirate

noun
joint rule by two
9.9
Pronunciation: / duːˈʌmvɪˌreɪt / (AmE), / djuːˈʌmvɪˌreɪt / (BrE)
a government, office, or position held jointly by two people; or the pair of officials who hold it

Examples

A duumvirate government
The ancient Roman republic was sometimes governed by a duumvirate instead of a single consul.
A duumvirate led the committee, making all major decisions together.
The company was managed by a duumvirate of co-founders who shared equal authority.
The ancient city was led by a duumvirate that balanced power between two officials.
During the crisis, a temporary duumvirate was established to ensure fair decision-making.
Historians studied the duumvirate as a unique form of shared leadership in early governments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monarchy - a government ruled by a single person, such as a king or queen
  • degree higher: triumvirate - a group of three people holding power or office together
