Heterosomata

noun
an order of flatfishes (halibut, flounder, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəroʊˈsoʊmətə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈsɒmətə / (BrE)
an order of flatfishes, including halibut, sole, flounder, plaice, turbot, and tonguefishes, characterized by having both eyes on one side of the head.

Examples

Heterosomata flatfishes
Halibut and flounder, both part of Heterosomata, are popular choices in seafood cuisine.
Scientists study the evolutionary adaptations of Heterosomata to understand their unusual body structure.
The Heterosomata include some of the world's most commercially valuable fish species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flatfish - a fish with a flattened body and both eyes on one side
