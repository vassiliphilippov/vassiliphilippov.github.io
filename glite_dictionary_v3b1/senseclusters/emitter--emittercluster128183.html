emitter

noun
electrode in a transistor
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪtə / (BrE)
the electrode in a transistor from which charge carriers enter the external circuit

Examples

Emitter voltage
By increasing the voltage at the emitter, you can control the transistor's operation.
Technicians tested the emitter for continuity during the circuit analysis.
The current flows from the emitter to the collector in an NPN transistor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: base - the electrode in a transistor that controls the flow of carriers
  • antonym: collector - the electrode in a transistor that collects charge carriers
