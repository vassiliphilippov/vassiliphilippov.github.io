sternpost

noun
vertical timber at a ship's stern
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstɜrnˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈstɜːnˌpəʊst / (BrE)
(nautical) the principal vertical timber or metal piece at the stern of a vessel, to which the transom and the ends of the side planks or plates are attached.

Examples

The sternpost of ship
A metal sternpost replaced the original wooden one to better withstand the harsh conditions at sea.
Old sailing vessels relied on a sturdy sternpost to maintain the shape of the hull at the rear.
The ship's sternpost was damaged during the storm and had to be repaired in dry dock.
