placer mining

noun
extracting minerals from alluvial deposits
4.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpleɪ.sər ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ˈplæ.sər ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpleɪ.sə ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ˈplæ.sə ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of extracting valuable minerals from alluvial deposits by washing, dredging, or similar methods

Examples

Placer mining method
Placer mining was a common method during the California Gold Rush, especially along riverbeds.
Modern placer mining uses mechanical equipment to separate gold from gravel and sand.
The regulations for placer mining differ significantly from those for hard rock mining.
