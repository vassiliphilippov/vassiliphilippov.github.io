iterable

noun
object for looping
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɪt̬ərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɪtərəbl / (BrE)
an object or collection, especially in programming, that can be looped over to access its elements one at a time

Examples

A Python iterable
In Python, any iterable can be used in a for loop to process its elements.
The function expects an iterable as input, such as a list or a string.
You can convert a dictionary into an iterable of its keys or values.
