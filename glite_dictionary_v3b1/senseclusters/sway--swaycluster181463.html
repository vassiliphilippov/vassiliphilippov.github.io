sway

verb
influence someone's opinion or decision
7.2 3.0
Pronunciation: /sweɪ/ (AmE), /sweɪ/ (BrE)
to influence someone so that they change their opinion or decision

Examples

To sway a decision
Despite his efforts, she was not easily swayed by peer pressure.
Her heartfelt apology finally swayed his opinion on the matter.
The advertisement was designed to sway undecided voters before the election.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influence - to affect someone's opinion or behavior
  • synonym: persuade - to make someone do or believe something by giving them a good reason
