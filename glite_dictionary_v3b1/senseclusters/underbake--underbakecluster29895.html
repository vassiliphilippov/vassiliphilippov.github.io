underbake

verb
to bake insufficiently
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈbeɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbeɪk / (BrE)
to bake something for less time than needed so it is not fully cooked inside

Examples

To underbake a cake
If you underbake the bread, the center will remain doughy and uncooked.
She accidentally underbaked the cookies, so they were too soft to hold their shape.
Be careful not to underbake the quiche or the filling will be runny.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bake - to cook food using dry heat in an oven
  • antonym: overbake - to bake something for too long so it becomes too dry or hard
