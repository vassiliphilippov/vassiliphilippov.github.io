Lower Peninsula of Michigan

proper noun
southern peninsula of Michigan, USA
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ɚ pəˈnɪn.sjə.lə əv mɪˈɡæn.ɪkən / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə pəˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə əv mɪˈɡæn.ɪkən / (BrE)
the part of the U.S. state of Michigan that lies between Lake Michigan and Lake Huron, forming the larger, southern portion of the state.

Examples

The Lower Peninsula of Michigan
Detroit and Lansing are both located in the Lower Peninsula of Michigan.
Many people from the Lower Peninsula of Michigan travel north for vacations in the summer.
Winter storms can be severe in the Lower Peninsula of Michigan due to its proximity to the Great Lakes.
