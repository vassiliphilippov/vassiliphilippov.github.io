incoherence

noun
lack of clarity or organization
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkoʊˈhɪrəns /; / ˌɪn.koʊˈhɪr.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəʊˈhɪərəns /; / ˌɪn.kəʊˈhɪə.rəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unclear, disorganized, or lacking logical connection; speech or writing that is difficult to understand

Examples

Speech incoherence
She apologized for the incoherence in her presentation, blaming lack of preparation.
The incoherence of his argument made it difficult to follow his logic.
The report was rejected due to its incoherence and poor structure.
After the accident, his speech was marked by incoherence and scattered thoughts.
Her letter was so full of incoherence that nobody could tell what she meant.
The manager's explanation descended into incoherence, leaving everyone more confused than before.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coherence - the quality of being logical and consistent
  • synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that have no meaning or make no sense
