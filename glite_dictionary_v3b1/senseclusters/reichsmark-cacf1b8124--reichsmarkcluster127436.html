Reichsmark

noun
German currency 1924–1948
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪksˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈraɪksˌmɑːk / (BrE)
the official currency of Germany from 1924 to 1948

Examples

One Reichsmark coin
During the 1930s, a loaf of bread cost two Reichsmark in Berlin.
Collectors today sometimes find old Reichsmark coins at flea markets.
After World War II, the Reichsmark was replaced by a new currency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Deutsche Mark - the currency that replaced the Reichsmark in West Germany after 1948
  • synonym: mark - a former currency unit in Germany
