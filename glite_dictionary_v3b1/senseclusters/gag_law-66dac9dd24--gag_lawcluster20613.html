gag law

noun
law restricting debate in a legislative body
4.2
Pronunciation: / ɡæɡ lɔ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ lɔː / (BrE)
A rule or law that limits or ends debate or discussion in a legislative or deliberative body.

Examples

Gag law in parliament
Opponents argue that the gag law restricts free speech and open discussion among lawmakers.
The parliament enacted a gag law to curb lengthy debates on controversial issues.
The senator criticized the introduction of a gag law as undermining democratic processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: closure - ending a debate in a legislative assembly by calling for a vote
  • antonym: filibuster - prolonged speech or series of speeches to delay action in a legislative assembly
