metallic element

noun
a chemical element with metallic properties
2.7 3.4
Pronunciation: / məˈtælɪk ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / mɪˈtælɪk ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
any chemical element that is typically a shiny solid, conducts heat or electricity, and can be shaped into sheets or wires; a metal.

Examples

A metallic element like gold
Gold, a valuable metallic element, has been used as currency for centuries.
Iron is a metallic element commonly used in construction due to its strength.
Scientists discovered a new metallic element with unique magnetic properties.
