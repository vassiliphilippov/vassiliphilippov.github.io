interphase

noun
cell growth stage
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌfeɪz / (BrE)
the stage in a cell's life cycle when it grows, performs normal functions, and prepares for division

Examples

Cell interphase stage
Most of a cell's life is spent in interphase, carrying out its normal activities.
During interphase, the cell copies its DNA in preparation for division.
Scientists observed that the nucleus remains intact throughout interphase.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mitosis - the process of cell division
  • degree higher: prophase - the first stage of mitosis, following interphase
