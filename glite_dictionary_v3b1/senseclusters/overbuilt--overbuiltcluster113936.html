overbuilt

adjective
too many buildings
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbɪlt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɪlt / (BrE)
having more buildings or structures than are needed or suitable for an area

Examples

Overbuilt city
The downtown area is now overbuilt, with empty office towers lining the streets.
Some experts warn that the resort town has become overbuilt and may face economic problems.
Developers realized too late that the neighborhood was already overbuilt for its population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crowded - filled with too many people or things
  • antonym: underdeveloped - lacking sufficient buildings or infrastructure
