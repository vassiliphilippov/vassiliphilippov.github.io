fabricate

verb
to invent or falsify
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to invent false information in order to deceive someone

Examples

To fabricate a story
He was accused of trying to fabricate evidence to avoid jail.
If you fabricate stories, people will stop trusting you.
The journalist lost her job after it was discovered she had fabricated interviews.

Related Terms

  • synonym: falsify - to make false by altering evidence or information
  • synonym: invent - to create something that is not true; to make up
  • antonym: tell the truth - to say what is true, not lie
