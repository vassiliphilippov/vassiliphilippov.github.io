bonanza

noun
a sudden source of wealth or success
Pronunciation: / bəˈnænzə / (AmE), / bəˈnænzə / (BrE)
a situation, event, or place that brings a large and unexpected amount of wealth, success, or something valuable (originally a rich source of ore)

Examples

A bonanza season
Holiday shopping season is a bonanza for retailers seeking to boost their profits.
The discovery of oil in the region turned out to be an economic bonanza for the small town.
The tech industry experienced a bonanza when demand for smartphones skyrocketed.
Prospectors came from miles around, hoping to find their own bonanza in the hills.
The discovery of a bonanza transformed the remote town into a thriving community.
The miners celebrated when they struck a bonanza deep within the mountains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth or sudden success
  • antonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or loss
  • antonym: drought - a prolonged period of scarcity or shortage
  • synonym: windfall - an unexpected gain or piece of good fortune
