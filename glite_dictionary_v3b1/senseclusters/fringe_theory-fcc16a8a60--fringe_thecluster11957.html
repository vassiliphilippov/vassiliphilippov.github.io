fringe theory

noun
unconventional or marginal idea in a field
3.4
Pronunciation: / frɪndʒ ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / frɪndʒ ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
An idea or set of ideas that differs significantly from those generally accepted by experts in a particular field, often lacking substantial evidence or support.

Examples

A fringe theory idea
Despite being a fringe theory, it attracted attention from curious thinkers.
Some scientists dismissed his research as a fringe theory lacking credible evidence.
The documentary critically examined several popular fringe theories about ancient civilizations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream theory - an idea generally accepted by most experts in a field
  • synonym: pseudoscience - a belief or practice incorrectly presented as scientific
