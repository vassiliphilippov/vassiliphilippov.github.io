proud flesh

noun
swollen tissue around healing wound
9.9
Pronunciation: / praʊd flɛʃ / (AmE), / praʊd flɛʃ / (BrE)
swollen, excess tissue that forms around a healing wound or ulcer, often impeding the healing process

Examples

Proud flesh on wound
If left untreated, proud flesh may delay the proper closure of the wound.
New methods have been introduced to prevent the growth of proud flesh during the healing process.
The veterinarian treated the horse’s leg to remove the proud flesh that had developed around the cut.
