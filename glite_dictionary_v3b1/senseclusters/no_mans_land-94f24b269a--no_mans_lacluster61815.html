no man's land

noun
unowned or uninhabited land; disputed area
5.5 5.8
Pronunciation: /noʊ mænz lænd/ (AmE), /nəʊ mænz lænd/ (BrE)
an area of land that is unowned, uninhabited, or disputed, especially the land between opposing armies' front lines

Examples

War no man's land
After the factory closed, the old industrial district became a sort of no man's land.
During the war, soldiers avoided crossing the no man's land between the trenches.
The area between the two borders was a no man's land filled with debris and abandoned buildings.
After the ceasefire, the border became a no man's land where neither side dared to enter.
During the First World War, soldiers rarely dared to cross no man's land under the threat of enemy fire.
The area between the two fences was treated as no man's land and was heavily mined.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battlefield - an area where a battle is fought between armies
  • synonym: wasteland - an area that is unused, barren, or ruined
