to control, direct, or strongly influence the way that someone behaves or how something happens

Examples

Values govern choices
His dedication to fairness governs his decisions at work.
Principles of honesty and respect govern her daily interactions with others.
Religious beliefs often govern the conduct of communities around the world.

Synonyms

  • control
    2.4 93/m
    control means holding power to direct or manage people or things, while govern means to officially guide or shape how they act.
  • rule
    1.0 26/m
    rule is to govern or have authority over a country or group in a formal way, while govern describes guiding behavior or events more generally.
  • dictate
    5.0 12/m
    dictate means to order or command with strong authority, while govern can mean to guide, influence, or manage more broadly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over; dominate
  • antonym: follow - to act according to rules or advice (as an antonym in some contexts, when a person is not governed by something)
  • synonym: regulate - to control or maintain the rate or speed of a process
