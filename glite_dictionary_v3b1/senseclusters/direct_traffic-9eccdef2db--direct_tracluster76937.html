direct traffic

noun
website visits arriving directly
3.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈrekt ˈtræf.ɪk/ /; / /də.ˈrekt ˈtræf.ɪk/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt ˈtræf.ɪk/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈrekt ˈtræf.ɪk/ /; / /də.ˈrekt ˈtræf.ɪk/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt ˈtræf.ɪk/ (BrE)
website visits that arrive at a site without a referring source recorded (for example via a typed URL, bookmark, or when referral data is missing), as measured in web analytics.

Examples

Website direct traffic
The analytics dashboard shows a surge in direct traffic after the new site launch.
We noticed most of our holiday sales came from direct traffic rather than search or ads.
To increase direct traffic, the marketing team encouraged customers to bookmark the homepage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: direct visit - a single instance of a user arriving at a website directly (often used interchangeably with 'direct traffic' at the visit level)
  • antonym: referral traffic - website visits that arrive via links from other websites or sources, often contrasted with direct traffic
