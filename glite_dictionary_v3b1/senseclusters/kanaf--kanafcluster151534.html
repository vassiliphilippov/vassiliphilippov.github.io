kanaf

noun
fiber plant of the East Indies
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɑf / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːf / (BrE)
A valuable fiber plant of the East Indies, now widely cultivated for its use in making ropes and textiles.

Examples

Strong kanaf fibers
Farmers across Asia are increasingly cultivating kanaf for its strong natural fibers.
Research into sustainable materials has highlighted the environmental benefits of using kanaf in textiles.
The rope was made from the fibers of kanaf plants harvested in Indonesia.
