over-the-top

adjective
delivered directly over the internet (OTT)
5.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ðəˈtɑːp/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ðəˈtɔːp/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ðiːˈtɑːp/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ðəˈtɒp/ /; / /əʊ.və.ðiːˈtɒp/ (BrE)
(in media and telecommunications) delivered over the public internet directly to users, bypassing traditional distribution channels such as cable, satellite, or telecom operator services.

Examples

Over-the-top streaming
Many viewers prefer over-the-top streaming services to traditional cable plans for on-demand viewing.
The company launched an over-the-top app that delivers movies directly without needing a set-top box.
Regulators are debating rules for over-the-top services that compete with traditional telecommunications providers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: OTT - common abbreviation for 'over-the-top' referring to internet-delivered media services
  • antonym: cable - traditional subscription television distribution delivered via cable networks; contrasted with OTT
