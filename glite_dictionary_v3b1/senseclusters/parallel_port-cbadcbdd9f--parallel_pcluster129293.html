parallel port

noun
computer interface for simultaneous data transfer
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl pɔrt / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛl pɔːt / (BrE)
a socket on a computer used for connecting external devices, especially printers, allowing data to be transmitted simultaneously across multiple wires

Examples

A parallel port socket
He needed an adapter to connect his old scanner to the computer's parallel port.
Most modern laptops have replaced the parallel port with USB interfaces.
The early personal computer used a parallel port to connect to printers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: serial port - computer interface for sequential data transfer
