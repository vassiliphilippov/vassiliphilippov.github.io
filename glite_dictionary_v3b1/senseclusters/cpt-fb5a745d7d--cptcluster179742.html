CPT

noun
physics symmetry principle
Pronunciation: / si pi ti / (AmE), / siː piː tiː / (BrE)
a principle in physics stating that the laws of nature remain unchanged under the combined operations of charge conjugation, parity transformation, and time reversal

Examples

CPT theorem
The CPT theorem is a cornerstone of modern particle physics.
Researchers are conducting experiments to test the limits of CPT symmetry.
A violation of CPT would have profound implications for our understanding of the universe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parity - a symmetry relating to spatial inversion in physics
  • synonym: symmetry - a property where something remains unchanged under certain transformations
