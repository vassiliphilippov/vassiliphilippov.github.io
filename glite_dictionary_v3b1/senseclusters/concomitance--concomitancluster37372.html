concomitance

noun
the simultaneous occurrence of things
6.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈkɑː.mɪ.təns / (AmE), / kənˈkɒm.ɪ.təns / (BrE)
The state or fact of two or more things occurring or existing together.

Examples

The concomitance of events
In statistics, researchers often note the concomitance of multiple variables to establish correlation.
The concomitance of poverty and poor health is well documented in many studies.
The concomitance of thunder and lightning highlights their common origin in the same weather phenomenon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coincidence - a remarkable concurrence of events without apparent causal connection
  • synonym: concurrence - the fact of two or more events or circumstances happening or existing at the same time
