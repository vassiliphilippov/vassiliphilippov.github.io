syncarpous

adjective
(of ovary) with fused carpels in plants
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / sɪnˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
(of the ovary of a flowering plant) having carpels that are united or fused together.

Examples

A syncarpous ovary
A syncarpous ovary can affect the development of the plant's fruit.
Botanists study whether a plant's ovary is syncarpous or apocarpous to classify its family.
In lilies, the ovary is described as syncarpous because its carpels are fused.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apocarpous - (of a flower's ovary) with separate carpels
