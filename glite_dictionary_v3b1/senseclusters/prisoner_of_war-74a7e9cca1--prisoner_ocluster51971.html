prisoner of war

noun
captured person in war
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzənər əv wɔr / (AmE), / ˈprɪzənə əv wɔː / (BrE)
a person, especially a member of the armed forces, who is captured and held by the enemy during war

Examples

A prisoner of war camp
After months in captivity, the prisoner of war was finally released.
During World War II, thousands of soldiers became prisoners of war in enemy camps.
The Red Cross ensured humane treatment of the prisoners of war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: POW - abbreviation of 'prisoner of war'
  • degree lower: civilian internee - a civilian captured and held during war
  • antonym: escapee - a prisoner of war who has escaped
