broadbill

noun
North American diving duck
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔdˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌbɪl / (BrE)
a type of duck with a broad, flat bill, found in North America and the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

North American broadbill
Hunters commonly refer to the lesser scaup as a broadbill in North America.
The broadbill is often seen in large flocks on northern lakes in early autumn.
The distinct bluish-grey bill helps to identify the male broadbill during the breeding season.
A flock of broadbills was spotted resting on the lake during migration.
Hunters are only allowed to take a limited number of broadbill ducks each season.
The distinctive flat bill helps the broadbill feed efficiently in shallow water.
