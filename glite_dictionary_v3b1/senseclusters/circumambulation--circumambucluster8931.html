circumambulation

noun
walking around
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜrkəmˌæmbjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˌæmbjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of walking or moving all the way around something, especially in a circle or as part of a ritual

Examples

Ritual circumambulation
The festival included a circumambulation of the city square by all the participants.
During their morning walk, they completed a full circumambulation of the lake.
The circumambulation of the ancient monument was part of the guided tour.
The monks began their circumambulation of the temple at sunrise, chanting prayers as they walked.
During the festival, thousands of pilgrims take part in the circumambulation of the holy site.
The circumambulation of the ancient monument is believed to bring good fortune to participants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encirclement - the act of surrounding or going around something
  • synonym: perambulation - the act of walking through or around a place, especially for inspection
  • synonym: procession - a group of people moving forward in an orderly way, often as part of a ceremony
