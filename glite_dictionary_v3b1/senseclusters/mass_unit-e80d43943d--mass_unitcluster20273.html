mass unit

noun
unit for measuring mass
Pronunciation: / mæs ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / mɑːs ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement used to quantify mass, such as the gram or kilogram

Examples

A mass unit kilogram
In the laboratory, we measured the sample using a precise mass unit called the milligram.
Scientists often convert between different mass units depending on the scale of measurement needed.
The kilogram is the standard mass unit in the International System of Units.
