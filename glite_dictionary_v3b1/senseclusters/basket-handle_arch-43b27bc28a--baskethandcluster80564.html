basket-handle arch

noun
a type of round, wide arch in architecture
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbæskɪt ˌhændl ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt ˌhændl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch with an inner curve formed by connecting arcs from three circles, resulting in a shape wider and flatter than a semicircle

Examples

A basket-handle arch bridge
Architects often choose the basket-handle arch for bridges needing both strength and aesthetic appeal.
The cathedral’s nave is supported by a series of basket-handle arches that create a vast, open space.
Unlike a traditional semicircular arch, the basket-handle arch offers greater width and elegance to the structure.

  • synonym: elliptical arch - an arch shaped like part of an ellipse
