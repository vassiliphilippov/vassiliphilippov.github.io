Lord Rayleigh

proper noun
English physicist; discoverer of argon
8.5
Pronunciation: / lɔrd ˈreɪli / (AmE), / lɔːd ˈreɪli / (BrE)
(1842–1919) English physicist known for studies of the density of gases, the discovery of argon, and major contributions to acoustic theory.

Examples

Lord Rayleigh Nobel Prize
Lord Rayleigh shared the 1904 Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of argon.
In his research, Lord Rayleigh precisely measured the densities of different gases.
The work of Lord Rayleigh fundamentally influenced the field of acoustics.
