loosen

verb
become less strict or severe
3.3 4/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀês…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàluÀês…ôn / (BrE)
to make rules, restrictions, or controls less strict or severe, or to become less strict

Examples

To loosen strict rules
After the exam period, the university decided to loosen its library rules.
If we loosen the dress code a little, everyone might feel more comfortable.
Some governments may loosen restrictions as the crisis improves.
Authorities are considering loosening restrictions as infection rates continue to decline.
In recent years, some countries have loosened their immigration policies to attract more skilled workers.
The company decided to loosen its dress code to allow employees more comfort.

Synonyms

    relax makes rules or limits less strict to allow more freedom, while loosen is a general word for reducing strictness.
  • ease up
    3.7 1/m
    ease up reduces pressure or strictness slowly and often in speech, while loosen is a more neutral, general term.
  • liberalize
    6.3 3/m
    liberalize makes laws or systems more open and free in formal political settings, while loosen is less formal and more general.
  • lessen
    2.8 4/m
    lessen means to make something smaller in amount or degree, while loosen focuses on reducing rules or restrictions.
