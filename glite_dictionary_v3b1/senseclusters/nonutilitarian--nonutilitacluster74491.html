nonutilitarian

adjective
not utilitarian
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌjuːtɪlɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌjuːtɪlɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
not intended for practical use or utility; valued for other reasons such as beauty or meaning

Examples

Nonutilitarian art
The artist preferred a nonutilitarian approach, valuing beauty over practical function.
Some laws are created for nonutilitarian reasons, such as tradition or moral beliefs.
Her nonutilitarian philosophy emphasized individual rights rather than the greatest good.
The museum displayed several nonutilitarian objects created purely for artistic expression.
Her collection includes many nonutilitarian items that serve no practical purpose but are beautiful to look at.
Some architecture is intentionally nonutilitarian, focusing on form rather than function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decorative - serving only to make something look more attractive
  • antonym: utilitarian - focused on usefulness or the greatest good for the most people
