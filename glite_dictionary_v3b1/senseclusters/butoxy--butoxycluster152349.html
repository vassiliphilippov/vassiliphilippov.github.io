butoxy

noun
alkoxy group
Pronunciation: / ˈbjuːtəksi / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtəksi / (BrE)
the alkoxy group or radical with the structure −O−C4H9, consisting of an oxygen atom bonded to a butyl group

Examples

Butoxy group
The chemist introduced a butoxy group to modify the compound's solubility.
Replacing the butoxy substituent with a methoxy group changed the reaction outcome.
A butoxy group is often found in certain pharmaceutical molecules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkoxy - a group consisting of an alkyl group bonded to oxygen, general formula −O−R
  • degree lower: ethoxy - the alkoxy group −O−C2H5, consisting of an oxygen atom bonded to an ethyl group
  • degree lower: methoxy - the alkoxy group −O−CH3, consisting of an oxygen atom bonded to a methyl group
