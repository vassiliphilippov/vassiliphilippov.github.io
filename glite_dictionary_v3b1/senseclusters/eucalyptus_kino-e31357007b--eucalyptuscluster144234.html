eucalyptus kino

noun
reddish resin from Eucalyptus trees
7.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈkɑlɪptəs ˈkiːnoʊ / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs ˈkiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a reddish-brown dried gum or resin exuded from certain Eucalyptus trees, particularly Eucalyptus camaldulensis, used in tanning and traditional medicine

Examples

Eucalyptus kino gum
Tanners once relied on the astringent quality of eucalyptus kino to treat leather.
The bark of the tree oozes eucalyptus kino, which is then collected and dried.
Traditional healers have long used eucalyptus kino for its medicinal properties.
