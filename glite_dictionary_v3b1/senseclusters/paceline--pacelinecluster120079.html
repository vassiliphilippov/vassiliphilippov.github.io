paceline

noun
cyclists in a line
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpeɪ.slaɪn/ (AmE), /ˈpeɪ.slaɪn/ (BrE)
a line of cyclists riding closely together to reduce wind resistance and increase speed

Examples

A paceline of cyclists
The cyclists formed a paceline to conserve energy during the long race.
Riding in a paceline allows each cyclist to take turns facing the wind.
She learned how to join a paceline during her first group ride.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breakaway - a group of cyclists that has separated from the main group
  • synonym: drafting - riding closely behind another cyclist to reduce wind resistance
