give someone enough rope

verb
allowing freedom that leads to self-destruction
4.2 6.8
Pronunciation: /gɪv ˈsʌm.wʌn ɪ.ˈnʌf roʊp/ (AmE), /ɡɪv ˈsʌm.wʌn ɪ.ˈnʌf rəʊp/ (BrE)
to allow someone enough freedom or opportunities that they may cause their own failure or downfall

Examples

To give someone enough rope
She thought it wise to give her enough rope, hoping her rival would make a fatal error.
Sometimes it's better to give someone enough rope rather than confront them directly.
The manager decided to give him enough rope and see if he would finally admit his mistakes.

Related Terms
