underground

adjective, adverb
secret; hidden from authorities
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəɡraʊnd /; / ˌʌndəˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
secret or hidden; done in a way to avoid being discovered, especially by authorities

Examples

Underground music scene
Many artists were involved in the underground music scene during the 1970s.
She was a member of the underground resistance during the occupation.
The group operated an underground newspaper to spread dissenting views.
After the revolution began, many activists went underground to avoid arrest.
He published his controversial writings underground to escape censorship.
The opposition party operated underground during the military regime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively, especially for illicit purposes
  • synonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed; secret
  • antonym: open - not secret or hidden; done publicly
  • antonym: openly - in a way that is not hidden or secret
  • synonym: secretly - without others knowing; in secret
