English oak

noun
a European oak tree, Quercus robur
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ oʊk / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous European oak tree (Quercus robur) with smooth leaves and rounded lobes, known for its hard, strong, light-colored wood

Examples

The English oak tree
An ancient English oak stood in the center of the meadow, its branches stretching wide.
Carpenters prize English oak for its durability and attractive appearance.
The furniture was made from solid English oak and had a beautiful grain.
