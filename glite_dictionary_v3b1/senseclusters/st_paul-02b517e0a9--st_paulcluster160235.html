St. Paul

proper noun
Christian missionary and apostle
5.7
Pronunciation: /seɪnt pɔːl/ /; / /seɪnt pɑːl/ (AmE), /seɪnt pɔːl/ (BrE)
A Christian missionary and apostle, originally known as Saul, who played a major role in spreading Christianity and authored several New Testament Epistles.

Examples

Apostle St. Paul
St. Paul traveled extensively to spread the teachings of Christianity across the Roman Empire.
Before converting, St. Paul was known as Saul and persecuted early Christians.
Many of the Epistles in the New Testament are attributed to St. Paul.
