Saint Peter

proper noun
leading Apostle and first Pope in Christianity
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈpiːtər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
The leading disciple of Jesus Christ, regarded as the first Pope and a principal figure in Christianity.

Examples

Saint Peter the Apostle
Catholics regard Saint Peter as the first Bishop of Rome.
Many Christians believe that Saint Peter guards the gates of heaven.
The Basilica of Saint Peter is one of the holiest sites in Rome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Peter - the Apostle Peter; original disciple of Jesus; also known as Simon Peter
