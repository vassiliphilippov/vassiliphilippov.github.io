McCarthyism

noun
practice of making unfair political accusations
8.5 0.81/m
Pronunciation: / m…ôÀàk…ër.Œ∏iÀå…™z.…ôm / (AmE), / m…ôÀàk…ëÀê.Œ∏iÀå…™z.…ôm / (BrE)
The practice of making unfair or unsubstantiated accusations, especially of disloyalty or subversion, as was done during the period of Senator McCarthy's anti-Communist activities in the United States in the 1950s.

Examples

McCarthyism in 1950s
Many people's lives were ruined during the era of McCarthyism in the 1950s.
Some critics argue that modern political debate sometimes resembles McCarthyism in its eagerness to accuse without evidence.
The professor warned about the dangers of McCarthyism and the suppression of dissent in a free society.

Synonyms

  • red-baiting
    9.5 0.03/m
    red-baiting accuses people of being communists to harm their reputation, while McCarthyism refers to a wider campaign of unfair anti-communist accusations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fairness - impartial and just treatment without favoritism or discrimination
  • synonym: witch-hunt - an attempt to find and punish people whose opinions are unpopular and who are said to be a danger to society
