renegade

adjective
having deserted a cause or principle
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛnəˌɡeɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛnɪɡeɪd / (BrE)
having deserted or betrayed a cause, organization, or principle; disloyal.

Examples

Renegade group
A renegade faction has withdrawn its support for the coalition government.
He was seen as a renegade officer for abandoning his post during the crisis.
Several renegade members of the party broke ranks and voted against the bill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyal - faithful to a person, cause, or organization
  • synonym: recreant - having deserted a cause; cowardly or disloyal
