tepal

noun
undifferentiated perianth segment in flowers
9.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtiËpÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆtiËpÉ™l / (BrE)
a segment of the outer part of a flower (perianth) that is not clearly differentiated into sepals or petals, as seen in lilies and tulips

Examples

A tepal segment
In some plant species, each tepal is similar in color and texture, making them hard to distinguish.
The flower of the lily has six prominent tepals arranged in two whorls.
Unlike roses, tulips have tepals that are not clearly classified as petals or sepals.
