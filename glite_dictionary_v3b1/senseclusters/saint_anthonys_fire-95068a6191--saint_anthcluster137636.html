Saint Anthony's fire

noun
historical severe skin inflammation or disease
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈænθəniːz faɪr / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈænθəniːz faɪə / (BrE)
a historical term for various severe inflammatory or gangrenous skin diseases, such as ergotism, erysipelas, or shingles

Examples

Saint Anthony's fire disease
Historical records describe Saint Anthony's fire as a mysterious and terrifying skin disease.
In medieval Europe, Saint Anthony's fire caused widespread suffering due to contaminated rye.
The villagers feared Saint Anthony's fire whenever crops were tainted by ergot fungus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ergotism - poisoning caused by eating food affected by ergot fungus, historically known as Saint Anthony's fire
  • synonym: erysipelas - an acute streptococcal infection of the skin and subcutaneous tissues
  • synonym: shingles - a painful skin rash caused by the varicella-zoster virus, sometimes referred to historically as Saint Anthony's fire
