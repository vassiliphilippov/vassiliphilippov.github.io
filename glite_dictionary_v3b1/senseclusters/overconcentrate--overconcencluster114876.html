overconcentrate

verb
to make too strong
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkɑːnsənˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkɒnˈsɛntreɪt / (BrE)
to make something too concentrated, especially by adding too much of a substance or reducing volume excessively

Examples

To overconcentrate a solution
If you overconcentrate the juice, it will taste too strong and may be unpleasant to drink.
Be careful not to overconcentrate the cleaning solution, as it could damage surfaces.
The chemist warned that overconcentrating the sample could affect the accuracy of the results.

Related Terms
