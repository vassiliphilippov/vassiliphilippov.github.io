amphetamine

noun
a synthetic stimulant drug
7.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /æm.ˈfet.ə.miːn/ /; / /æm.ˈfet.ə.mɪn/ (AmE), /æm.ˈfet.ə.miːn/ /; / /æm.ˈfet.ə.mɪn/ (BrE)
a synthetic stimulant drug that increases activity in the central nervous system, used medically to treat certain conditions such as ADHD and narcolepsy, and sometimes misused illicitly

Examples

Amphetamine medication
Amphetamine is sometimes used to treat attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).
Abuse of amphetamine can lead to serious health risks, including addiction.
The doctor prescribed amphetamine to help manage her narcolepsy symptoms.
