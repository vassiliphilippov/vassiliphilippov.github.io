tabloid

noun
small-format sensational newspaper
Pronunciation: / ˈtæb.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtæb.lɔɪd / (BrE)
a newspaper or magazine that is smaller than usual and focuses on sensational news, gossip, or entertainment

Examples

A tabloid newspaper
Many people read a tabloid for its gossip and entertainment news.
The tabloids were full of sensational headlines during the election.
The celebrity scandal was splashed across the front page of the tabloid.
Many people read the tabloid for its celebrity gossip and eye-catching headlines.
She doesn't trust news reported in a tabloid because of its sensational style.
The front page of the tabloid featured another shocking story about a famous actor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broadsheet - a newspaper with large pages considered more serious or authoritative
