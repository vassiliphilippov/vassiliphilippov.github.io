pitch

verb
lead a card to establish trump suit (cards)
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɪtʃ / (BrE)
to throw, hit, or toss something, especially in sports like baseball or golf, or in card games

Examples

To pitch a ball
He decided to pitch his highest card to try to set the trump suit early.
If you pitch a spade, then spades will become trump for this round.
Players must carefully choose which card to pitch when trying to control the trump suit.
He pitched a no-hitter for his team last night.
She loves to pitch in the neighborhood softball league every summer.
The coach taught him how to pitch accurately against left-handed batters.
Could you pitch me that marker from across the table?
He pitches small rocks into the pond for fun.
She pitched the keys gently onto the sofa as she walked in.

Related Terms

  • synonym: throw - propel something with force through the air by a movement of the arm and hand
  • synonym: toss - to throw something lightly or carelessly
