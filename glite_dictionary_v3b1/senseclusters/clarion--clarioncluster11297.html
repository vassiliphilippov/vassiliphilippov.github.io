clarion

noun
clear call or sound
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈklæriən / (AmE), / ˈklæriən / (BrE)
a clear and loud sound, or a strong and clear call to action or attention

Examples

A clarion of hope
The general's speech was a clarion that inspired the troops to action.
A clarion rang out across the valley, signaling the start of the festival.
Her message served as a clarion for everyone to join the cause.

Related Terms

  • synonym: call - a loud cry or appeal to action
  • synonym: signal - a sound or gesture that gives a message or warning
