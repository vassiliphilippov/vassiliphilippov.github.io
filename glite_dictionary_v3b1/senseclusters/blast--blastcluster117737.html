blast

noun
a strong current of air
Pronunciation: / blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
a sudden, strong movement of air or energy

Examples

A blast of wind
A sudden blast of wind knocked over the outdoor chairs.
The blast from the explosion shattered windows blocks away.
We had to brace ourselves against the icy blast coming off the lake.
A cold blast of wind hit us as we opened the door.
The news sent a blast of excitement through the crowd.
He was caught in a blast of energy as the music started.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement
