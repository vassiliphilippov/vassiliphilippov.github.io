repopulate

verb
to fill again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈpɑːpjəˌleɪt /; / ˌriːˈpɑpjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpɒpjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to fill a place again with people, animals, or plants after they have disappeared, declined, or been removed

Examples

To repopulate the forest
After the forest fire, conservationists worked hard to repopulate the area with native species.
The government launched a program to repopulate the abandoned villages in the countryside.
Scientists hope to repopulate the lake with fish after the pollution is cleaned up.
After the wildfire, efforts began to repopulate the area with native plant species.
The government launched a program to repopulate the abandoned villages.
Scientists hope to repopulate the lake with fish that once thrived there.
After the war, the government worked to repopulate the abandoned villages.

The city was slowly repopulated as families returned to rebuild their homes.

  • antonym: evacuate - to remove people or animals from a place
  • synonym: populate - to fill an area with people or animals
