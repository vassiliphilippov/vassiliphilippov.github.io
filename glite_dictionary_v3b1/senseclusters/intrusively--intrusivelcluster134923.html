intrusively

adverb
by forceful insertion
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruːsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːsɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that involves forcing or inserting something into another thing, especially in geology when rock forms within existing rock.

Examples

Magma cools intrusively
The magma cooled intrusively beneath the Earth's surface, forming large granite bodies.
Geologists study how minerals are deposited intrusively within older layers of rock.
The new material was added intrusively, changing the structure of the original formation.

  • synonym: forcibly - in a way that uses force
