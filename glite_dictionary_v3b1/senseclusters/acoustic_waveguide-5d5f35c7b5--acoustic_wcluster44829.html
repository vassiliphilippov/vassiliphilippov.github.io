acoustic waveguide

noun
structure for guiding sound waves
1.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkuː.stɪk ˈweɪv.ɡaɪd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkuː.stɪk ˈweɪv.ɡaɪd/ (BrE)
A physical structure or device designed to direct or confine sound waves along a defined path.

Examples

An acoustic waveguide device
An acoustic waveguide can be used to direct ultrasonic signals in medical devices.
Researchers tested several types of acoustic waveguide to optimize their speaker system.
The design of the acoustic waveguide affects the quality of sound transmission.
