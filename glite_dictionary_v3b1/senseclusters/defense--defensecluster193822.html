defense

noun
the act of protecting from harm or attack
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
the act, system, or means of protecting someone or something from attack, danger, or harm

Examples

Defense against disease
Good nutrition is an important defense against illness.
He always took pride in his ability to provide defense for his younger brother.
The city organized a strong defense against the invading army.
As a member of the defense, she helped organize strategic plans to repel invaders.
The city relied on its strong defense during the siege.
The government increased funding for the defense to prepare for possible threats.
During the siege, the city's defenses were repeatedly tested by invaders.
The ancient stone defense has stood for centuries, protecting the town from attacks.
The army constructed new defenses along the river to stop the advancing enemy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attack - an aggressive action against someone or something
  • synonym: barricade - a temporary structure for blocking passage or defending an area
  • synonym: defense - military action or resources protecting a country (US spelling)
  • synonym: fortification - a defensive wall or other reinforcement built to strengthen a place against attack
  • antonym: offense - the action or group attacking or taking aggressive action
  • synonym: protection - the act of keeping someone or something safe from harm
