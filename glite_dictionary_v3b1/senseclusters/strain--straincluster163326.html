strain

noun
subgroup of organisms within a species
Pronunciation: / streɪn / (AmE), / streɪn / (BrE)
a type or variety of an animal, plant, or microorganism within a species, often with specific characteristics

Examples

A new strain of virus
Each strain of yeast can produce unique flavors in beer brewing.
Researchers discovered a strain of bacteria that is resistant to antibiotics.
This year's flu vaccine targets the most common strains of the virus.
Farmers often crossbreed cattle to develop a superior strain for milk production.
The scientist discovered a new strain of apple that is resistant to disease.
This strain of chicken grows faster than traditional breeds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breed - a specific group of domestic animals or plants with homogeneous appearance and behavior
