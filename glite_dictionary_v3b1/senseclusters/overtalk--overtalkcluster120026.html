overtalk

verb
to interrupt by talking
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtɔk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtɔːk / (BrE)
to speak at the same time as someone else, making it difficult for them to be heard or interrupting them.

Examples

To overtalk another person
During the heated debate, several participants tried to overtalk each other to make their points.
It is considered rude to overtalk someone when they are sharing their opinion.
She felt frustrated when her ideas were ignored because others would overtalk her in meetings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking by speaking yourself
  • antonym: listen - to pay attention to what someone is saying
