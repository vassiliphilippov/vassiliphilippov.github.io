false buckthorn

noun
deciduous tree of southeastern US and Mexico
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈbʌkˌθɔn / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈbʌkˌθɔːn / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States and Mexico, not a true buckthorn but resembling it in appearance.

Examples

The false buckthorn tree
Botanists are studying the distribution of false buckthorn in the southeastern United States.
Many birds are attracted to the berries of the false buckthorn tree.
The leaves of the false buckthorn turn a bright yellow in autumn.
