catch

verb
become popular or widespread
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / kætʃ /; / katʃ / (BrE)
to become affected by or come under the influence of something such as an illness, fire, emotion, or trend

Examples

To catch a cold
It was a good idea, but it just didn't catch with the public.
Sometimes a new slang word will catch and quickly spread among teenagers.
The trend took a while to catch, but now everyone is doing it.
After a few sparks, the roof finally caught.
Don't let that curtain catch from the candle flame!
If the wood is dry, it will catch quickly.
I hope I don't catch the flu this winter.
It's easy to catch an infection when traveling abroad.
She often catches colds during the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contract - to catch or develop (a disease or illness)
  • antonym: extinguish - to put out a fire; stop it burning
  • synonym: ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
  • antonym: resist - to withstand or not be affected by something
  • synonym: take off - to become popular or successful quickly
