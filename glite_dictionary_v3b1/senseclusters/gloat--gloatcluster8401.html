gloat

verb
to show smug satisfaction
5.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /gloʊt/ /; / /gləʊt/ (AmE), /gləʊt/ (BrE)
to show or feel great pleasure or satisfaction, especially in a way that is smug or makes others feel bad, often about your own success or someone else's failure

Examples

To gloat over victory
He couldn't resist the urge to gloat about his team's victory.
It's not polite to gloat when others make mistakes.
She stood there gloating over her competitor's defeat.
After winning the game, he couldn't help but gloat over his rival's defeat.
She tried not to gloat, but her satisfaction was obvious.
The press was accused of gloating about the scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exult - to show or feel elation, often after a success
  • antonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret, often for the misfortune of others
  • synonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
  • synonym: relish - to enjoy or take pleasure in something
  • antonym: sympathize - to share the feelings of another, typically feeling pity or compassion
