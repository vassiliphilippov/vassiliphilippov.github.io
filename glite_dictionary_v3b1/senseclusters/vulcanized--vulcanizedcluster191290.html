vulcanized

adjective
treated rubber made stronger and more elastic
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlkəˌnaɪzd /; / ˈvʌl.kəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.kə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of rubber) treated with sulfur or another agent under heat in a chemical process to make it harder, stronger, more elastic, and less affected by odors.

Examples

Vulcanized rubber
Charles Goodyear revolutionized industry with his invention of vulcanized rubber.
The laboratory compared the properties of natural rubber to those of vulcanized samples.
Tires are made from vulcanized rubber to ensure durability and flexibility.
After the chemical process, the vulcanised material became much more elastic and robust.
The tires are made from vulcanised rubber to ensure durability and safety.
This factory specializes in producing vulcanised hoses for the automotive industry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unvulcanized - (of rubber) not subjected to the vulcanization process; lacking added strength and elasticity
  • synonym: vulcanized - alternative spelling (American English) of 'vulcanised'
