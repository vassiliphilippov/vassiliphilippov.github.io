Titanosaurus

noun
genus of Cretaceous herbivorous dinosaurs
9.9
Pronunciation: /taɪ.tə.ˈnoʊ.sɑː.rəs/ /; / /taɪ.tə.ˈnoʊ.sɔː.rəs/ (AmE), /taɪ.tə.ˈnəʊ.sɔː.rəs/ (BrE)
A genus of large herbivorous sauropod dinosaurs that lived during the Cretaceous period in South America.

Examples

A Titanosaurus fossil

The Titanosaurus was one of the giant sauropods that grazed on prehistoric vegetation.

Fossils of Titanosaurus have been found in various regions of South America.
Scientists believe Titanosaurus could reach lengths of up to 12 meters.
