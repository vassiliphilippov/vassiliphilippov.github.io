humpf

noun
sound of annoyance
9.9
Pronunciation: / hʌmpf / (AmE), / hʌmpf / (BrE)
a short, uttered sound expressing annoyance, doubt, or contempt, similar to a snort or grunt

Examples

A loud humpf
She let out a loud humpf when she saw the long line at the bank.
With a quiet humpf, he turned away from the argument, clearly unimpressed.
The teacher responded to the silly question with a dismissive humpf.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grunt - a low, short sound made to show displeasure
  • synonym: hmph - a sound expressing annoyance or disbelief
