forstall

verb
to monopolize supply
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈstɔl / (AmE), / fɔːˈstɔːl / (BrE)
to buy up goods or supplies in order to create a shortage and raise prices by controlling the market supply

Examples

To forstall the market
The merchants attempted to forstall the market by purchasing all available wheat.
Authorities introduced new laws to prevent companies from trying to forstall essential medical supplies.
By secretly buying up the stock, he hoped to forstall his competitors and control the prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distribute - to give out or deliver goods to people or places
  • synonym: hoard - to collect and keep large amounts of something, often secretly
  • synonym: monopolize - to take complete control of something, especially a market
