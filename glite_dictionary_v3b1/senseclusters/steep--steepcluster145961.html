steep

noun
a steep slope or place
3.1 0.70/m
Pronunciation: / stip / (AmE), / stiÀêp / (BrE)
A steep place, such as the side or slope of a hill or cliff.

Examples

A rocky steep
From our window, we could see the green steeps rising behind the village.
Goats grazed on the rocky steeps above the valley.
The hikers climbed carefully up the steep before reaching the summit.

Synonyms

  • cliff
    1.0 30/m
    a cliff is a high, often vertical rock face at the sea or river, while a steep is any sharp slope on a hill.
  • bluff
    7.4 0.00/m
    a bluff is a broad, steep bank by a river or sea, while a steep is any sharp slope on a hill.
  • precipice
    7.4 1.52/m
    a precipice is a very high, dangerous sheer drop, while a steep can mean a less extreme slope on a hillside.
  • drop-off
    5.5 0.77/m
    a drop-off is an abrupt, often sudden fall in ground level, while a steep is any sloping side of a hill.
