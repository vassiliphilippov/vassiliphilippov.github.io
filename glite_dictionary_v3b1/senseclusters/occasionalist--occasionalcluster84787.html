occasionalist

noun
supporter of occasionalism
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈkeɪʒənəlɪst / (AmE), / əˈkeɪʒənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports the philosophical theory that God is the only true cause of all events and actions

Examples

A famous occasionalist
The occasionalist argued that only God can truly cause anything to happen in the world.
As an occasionalist, she believed that human actions are not the real source of change.
Many philosophers debated with the occasionalist about the nature of causation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: causal determinist - a person who believes all events are determined by prior causes
  • synonym: occasionalism - the philosophical theory that God is the only true cause of events
