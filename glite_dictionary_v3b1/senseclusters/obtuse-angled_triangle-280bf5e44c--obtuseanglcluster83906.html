obtuse-angled triangle

noun
triangle with an obtuse angle
Pronunciation: / əbˈtuːs ˌæŋɡəld ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs ˌæŋɡəld ˈtraɪæŋɡl / (BrE)
a triangle that has one interior angle greater than 90 degrees

Examples

An obtuse-angled triangle
The largest angle in an obtuse-angled triangle is always greater than 90 degrees.
The teacher drew an obtuse-angled triangle on the board to explain the concept.
You can find the length of the sides in an obtuse-angled triangle using the Law of Sines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acute-angled triangle - a triangle with all interior angles less than 90 degrees
  • antonym: right-angled triangle - a triangle with one right angle (exactly 90 degrees)
