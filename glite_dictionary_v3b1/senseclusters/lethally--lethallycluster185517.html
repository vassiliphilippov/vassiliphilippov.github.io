lethally

adverb
extremely; intensely
Pronunciation: / ˈliːθəli / (AmE), / ˈliːθəli / (BrE)
to an extremely great or harmful degree, often used figuratively to emphasize intensity.

Examples

Lethally spicy food
The soup was lethally spicy, making it impossible for most people to eat.
He was lethally bored during the long meeting and struggled to stay awake.
Her jokes were lethally funny, leaving the entire audience in stitches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deadly - extremely or intensely, often in a harmful way
  • antonym: mildly - to a small or gentle degree
