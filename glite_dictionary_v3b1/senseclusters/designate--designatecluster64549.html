designate

verb
to assign a person to a post or task
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛzɪɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛzɪɡneɪt / (BrE)
to officially choose or appoint someone for a job, role, or purpose

Examples

To designate a leader
She was designated as the official spokesperson for the event.
The manager will designate a team leader for the new project.
You must designate someone to handle the logistics during your absence.
She was designated as his successor early in her career.
The committee will designate a spokesperson at tomorrow’s meeting.
The scholarship is designated for students from rural communities.

  • antonym: dismiss - to remove someone from a job or position
