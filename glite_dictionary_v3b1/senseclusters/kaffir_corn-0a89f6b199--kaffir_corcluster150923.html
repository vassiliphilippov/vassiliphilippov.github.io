kaffir corn

noun
a type of grain sorghum
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæfɚ kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈkæfə kɔːn / (BrE)
a type of sorghum grown for food and animal feed, with stems and leaves resembling Indian corn but with sawtooth-edged leaves

Examples

A kaffir corn plant
Farmers in the region prefer to plant kaffir corn because it thrives in dry conditions.
Livestock are often fed kaffir corn due to its high nutritional value.
The long, sawtooth leaves distinguish kaffir corn from other cereal crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sorghum - a genus of grasses cultivated for grain, especially Sorghum bicolor
