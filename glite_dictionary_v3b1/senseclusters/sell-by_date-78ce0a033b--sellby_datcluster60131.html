sell-by date

noun
date marking latest sale of perishable goods
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛl baɪ ˌdeɪt /; / ˈsɛl baɪ deɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl baɪ ˌdeɪt /; / ˈsɛl baɪ deɪt / (BrE)
the date printed or marked on food or other perishable goods showing the last day the product should be sold to ensure it is still fresh and good quality

Examples

The sell-by date
Check the sell-by date on the milk to make sure it's still fresh.
Shops often reduce prices on items close to their sell-by date.
This yogurt passed its sell-by date last week, but it might still be safe to eat.
He threw away the yogurt because it was two days past its sell-by date.
It's important to check the sell-by date before buying milk or cheese.
Supermarkets are required to remove products past their sell-by date from shelves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: best-before date - date indicating when a product is at its best quality
  • synonym: expiration date - the last date after which a product should not be used or consumed
