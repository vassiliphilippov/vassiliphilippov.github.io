hit out at

verb
criticize or attack verbally
Pronunciation: / hɪt aʊt æt / (AmE), / hɪt aʊt ət / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something strongly and publicly

Examples

To hit out at critics
During the interview, the politician hit out at her opponents' policies.
She hit out at the unfair treatment she received at work.
The athlete hit out at his coaches after the disappointing result.

Related Terms
