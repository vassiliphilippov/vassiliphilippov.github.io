Trichoderma

noun
plant-protecting fungus
Pronunciation: / ˌtraɪkoʊˈdɜːrmə / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi used in agriculture to protect plants from disease and promote growth

Examples

Trichoderma fungus in soil
Farmers often add Trichoderma to the soil to help protect crops from harmful fungi.
The use of Trichoderma has increased as growers seek natural ways to improve plant health.
Researchers discovered that Trichoderma can boost root development in young plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biocontrol - the use of living organisms to control pests or diseases
  • antonym: fungicide - a chemical substance used to kill fungi
