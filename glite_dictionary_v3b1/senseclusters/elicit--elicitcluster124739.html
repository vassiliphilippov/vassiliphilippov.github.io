elicit

verb
draw out a response or reaction
4.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlɪ.sɪt/ /; / /ə.ˈlɪ.sɪt/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlɪ.sɪt/ /; / /ə.ˈlɪ.sɪt/ (BrE)
to get or bring out a reaction, answer, information, or feeling from someone, often by asking or reasoning

Examples

To elicit a response
His speech was designed to elicit emotion from the audience.
The experiment elicited strong reactions from participants.
The teacher's question managed to elicit a thoughtful response from the quiet student.
Careful reasoning can sometimes elicit the correct solution to a complex problem.
Her questions were designed to elicit honest answers from the participants.
The investigator tried to elicit details about the incident from the witnesses.
By analyzing the teacher's questions, we can elicit her intended lesson objective.
From the incomplete data, the scientist managed to elicit the law governing the phenomenon.
The critic attempted to elicit a deeper meaning from the writer's ambiguous passage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: construe - to interpret the meaning of something
  • synonym: deduce - to reach a conclusion by reasoning
  • synonym: evoke - to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
  • synonym: extract - to remove or take out, often with force or effort
  • synonym: infer - to deduce or conclude from evidence and reasoning
  • antonym: suppress - to prevent the development, action, or expression of something (especially an emotion)
