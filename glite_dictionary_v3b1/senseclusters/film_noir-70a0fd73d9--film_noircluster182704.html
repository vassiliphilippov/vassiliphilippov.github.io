film noir

noun
pessimistic crime or thriller film style
5.1
Pronunciation: / fɪlm nwɑr / (AmE), / fɪlm nwɑː / (BrE)
A style of crime film or thriller, especially from the 1940s and 1950s, characterized by pessimism, fatalism, and a sense of menace, often featuring cynical characters.

Examples

Film noir style
Critics praised her latest movie as a modern take on film noir.
Many classic film noir movies feature hard-boiled detectives and femme fatales.
The detective story was shot in the style of film noir, with shadowy lighting and morally ambiguous characters.
