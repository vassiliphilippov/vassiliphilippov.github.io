cut

adjective
with page edges trimmed (of books)
Pronunciation: / kʌt / (AmE), / kʌt / (BrE)
(of the pages of a book) having the folds of the leaves trimmed or slit open.

Examples

Cut leaves
Collectors often prefer books with cut pages for practical use.
Early printed books are sometimes found with uncut or cut pages.
The rare edition has cut pages, making it easier to read.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uncut - having pages with folds untrimmed or unopened (of books)
