ragi

noun
type of cereal grass; staple grain
7.2 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈrɑː.giː/ /; / /ˈrɑː.gɪ/ (AmE), /ˈrɑː.giː/ /; / /ˈrɑː.gɪ/ (BrE)
An East Indian cereal grass (Eleusine coracana) whose seeds yield a nutritious but slightly bitter flour used as a staple food in parts of Asia and Africa.

Examples

A ragi plant
Farmers often rotate crops between rice and ragi to improve soil fertility.
In southern India, ragi flour is commonly used to prepare nutritious porridge for breakfast.
The health benefits of ragi make it popular among people seeking gluten-free alternatives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: finger millet - another name for ragi, a type of small-grained cereal
