weakening

adjective
causing a loss of strength or vitality
Pronunciation: / ˈwikənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone or something to become less strong, healthy, or effective; leading to a loss of strength or vitality.

Examples

Weakening effect
After weeks without proper nutrition, he was in a weakening condition.
The harsh winter had a weakening impact on the herd's health.
The virus had a weakening effect on the patient's immune system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strengthening - making something stronger or more powerful
