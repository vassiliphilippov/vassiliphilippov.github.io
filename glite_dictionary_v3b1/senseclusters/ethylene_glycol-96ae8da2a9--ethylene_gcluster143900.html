ethylene glycol

noun
poisonous liquid used as antifreeze
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθəˌlin ɡlaɪˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪliːn ˈɡlaɪkɒl / (BrE)
a sweet, colorless, and poisonous syrupy liquid used as an antifreeze and solvent

Examples

Sweet ethylene glycol
Accidental ingestion of ethylene glycol can lead to severe poisoning and requires immediate medical attention.
Automotive coolant typically contains ethylene glycol to prevent engines from freezing in cold temperatures.
The laboratory used ethylene glycol as a solvent in several organic chemistry experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antifreeze - a substance added to a liquid to lower its freezing point
