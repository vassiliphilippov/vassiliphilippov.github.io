glib

adjective
fluent but insincere or superficial
6.4 6.3
Pronunciation: /glɪb/ (AmE), /glɪb/ (BrE)
(of words or a person speaking) fluent and persuasive, but insincere or superficial; lacking real sincerity or depth

Examples

A glib apology
He offered a glib apology that seemed rehearsed rather than heartfelt.
Her glib remarks failed to impress the interview panel.
Politicians sometimes give glib answers to difficult questions.
He made a glib apology, but I doubted he really meant it.
Her glib remarks did not convince anyone in the meeting.
Politicians are often criticized for their glib answers during debates.
His glib charm made him popular, but many doubted his sincerity.
She was wary of his glib promises and chose not to trust him.
The politician gave a glib response to the tough question, avoiding the real issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sincere - genuine; free from pretense or deceit
  • synonym: slick - persuasive but insincere or superficial
  • synonym: smooth-talking - skilled at speaking easily and confidently, especially in a way that is not sincere
  • synonym: superficial - not thorough, deep, or complete; lacking depth
  • antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration or deep thought
