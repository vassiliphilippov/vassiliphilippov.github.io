embryoma of the kidney

noun
malignant kidney tumor in children
9.3
Pronunciation: /em.bri.ˈoʊ.mə əv ðə ˈkɪd.niː/ /; / /ɪm.bri.ˈoʊ.mə əv ðə ˈkɪd.niː/ (AmE), /em.bri.ˈəʊ.mə əv ðə ˈkɪd.niː/ /; / /ɪm.bri.ˈəʊ.mə əv ðə ˈkɪd.niː/ (BrE)
a malignant tumor of the kidney that occurs in young children, typically marked by high blood pressure, blood in the urine, and a palpable abdominal mass

Examples

Malignant embryoma of the kidney
Early diagnosis of embryoma of the kidney can significantly improve a patient's prognosis.
The doctor suspected an embryoma of the kidney when the child presented with hypertension and a palpable mass.
Treatment options for embryoma of the kidney often involve surgery followed by chemotherapy.
