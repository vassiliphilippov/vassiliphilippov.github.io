plug and pray

verb
connect a device, hope it works
5.6 6.8
Pronunciation: /plʌg ən preɪ/ /; / /plʌg ænd preɪ/ (AmE), /plʌg ən preɪ/ /; / /plʌg ænd preɪ/ (BrE)
To connect or set up an electronic device and hope it functions correctly, often with little confidence that it will work as intended.

Examples

To plug and pray device
After installing the new driver, all I could do was plug and pray that my printer would work.
Sometimes setting up a network at home is just a matter of plug and pray.
The technician laughed and said, "Just plug and pray—maybe this time it will start."
