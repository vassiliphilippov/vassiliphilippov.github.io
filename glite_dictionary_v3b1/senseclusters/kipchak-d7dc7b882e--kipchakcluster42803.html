Kipchak

noun
Turkic language branch
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪptʃæk / (AmE), / ˈkɪptʃæk / (BrE)
a branch of the Turkic languages spoken across the Eurasian steppe, including languages such as Kazakh and Tatar

Examples

Kipchak languages
Kazakh and Tatar are both part of the Kipchak branch of Turkic languages.
The Kipchak languages were historically spoken by nomadic peoples across the Eurasian steppe.
Linguists study the Kipchak group to understand the development of Central Asian languages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Oghuz - a branch of the Turkic languages including Turkish and Azerbaijani
  • synonym: Turkic - a family of languages spoken in Central Asia and Eastern Europe
