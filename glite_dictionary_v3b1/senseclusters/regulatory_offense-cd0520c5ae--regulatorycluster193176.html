regulatory offense

noun
statutory crime, not from common law
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtɔri əˈfɛns /; / ˈrɛgjəˌleɪtɔri əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlətəri əˈfɛns /; / ˈrɛgjʊlət(ə)ri əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A crime defined and created by statute rather than by common law, typically involving the violation of regulations rather than inherently wrongful acts.

Examples

A regulatory offense charge
Breaking health and safety rules in the workplace can result in a regulatory offense charge.
Most regulatory offenses do not require proof of criminal intent, unlike traditional crimes.
The company was fined for committing a regulatory offense by failing to comply with environmental standards.
Many environmental violations are classified as regulatory offences rather than criminal offences.
The company paid a fine for a regulatory offence related to workplace safety laws.
Unlike other crimes, a regulatory offence may not require proof of intent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: common law offence - an offence established by common law rather than statute
  • synonym: statutory offence - an offence created by statute law
  • synonym: statutory offense - an offense created by statute
