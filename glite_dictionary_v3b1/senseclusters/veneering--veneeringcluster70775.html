veneering

noun
process of applying a veneer layer
Pronunciation: / vəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / vɪˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of applying a thin layer of material, such as fine wood, to the surface of an object to improve its appearance or finish

Examples

Veneering a table
Modern veneering can give inexpensive materials the appearance of luxury.
The cabinetmaker demonstrated veneering techniques to the students in the workshop.
Victorian furniture often features intricate veneering with rare wood species.
