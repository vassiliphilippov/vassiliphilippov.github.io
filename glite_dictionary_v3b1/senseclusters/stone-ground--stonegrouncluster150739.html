stone-ground

adjective
ground with stone mills
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊnˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
describing food, especially flour, that has been ground using traditional stone mills rather than modern steel rollers

Examples

Stone-ground flour
Many bakers believe that stone-ground flour gives bread a richer flavor and texture.
The market sells a variety of stone-ground cornmeal for traditional recipes.
She always chooses stone-ground products for their natural taste and nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: refined - processed to remove impurities or coarse parts
  • synonym: wholemeal - made from the entire wheat grain
