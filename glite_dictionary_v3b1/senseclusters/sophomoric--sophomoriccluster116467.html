sophomoric

adjective
immature or overconfident
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːfəˈmɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɒfəˈmɒrɪk / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is immature, foolish, or lacking in good judgment, especially in a way that shows overconfidence

Examples

Sophomoric humor
The comedian's sophomoric jokes failed to impress the more serious members of the audience.
Her sophomoric attitude made it difficult for the team to take her ideas seriously.
Critics described the film as sophomoric because of its silly humor and lack of depth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: juvenile - childish or immature
  • antonym: mature - showing good judgment and adult behavior
