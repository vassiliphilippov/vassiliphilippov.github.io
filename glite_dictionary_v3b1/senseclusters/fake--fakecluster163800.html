fake

noun
a counterfeit or imitation object
3.5 2.37/m
Pronunciation: / feÉªk / (AmE), / feÉªk / (BrE)
an object made to look real or genuine in order to deceive people; a counterfeit or imitation

Examples

A fake painting
Experts quickly identified the painting as a fake during the auction.
Many tourists unknowingly purchase designer handbags that are actually fakes.
The wristwatch he bought turned out to be a fake and stopped working after a week.

Synonyms

  • counterfeit
    6.7 2.88/m
    counterfeit is made to look exactly like a real item to deceive buyers, while fake is a general imitation used to trick people.
  • forgery
    6.0 1.47/m
    forgery is a copied document or artwork created to pass as original and fool experts, while fake is any deceptive imitation.
  • knockoff
    6.0 1.29/m
    knockoff is an unauthorized, cheaper copy of a designer product sold to imitate style, while fake is any item made to deceive.
  • imitation
    3.7 0.00/m
    imitation is made to look like something else, often without intent to trick buyers, while fake implies deliberate deception.
  • sham
    6.1 4.37/m
    sham is a thing or person that pretends to be real to mislead others, while fake usually names a false object.
