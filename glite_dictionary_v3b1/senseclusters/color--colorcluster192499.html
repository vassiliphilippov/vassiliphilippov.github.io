color

verb
change color, often undesirably
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚ /; / ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to add color to something or to change its color, especially by painting, dyeing, or exposure

Examples

To color a picture
Exposure to the sun caused the curtains to color over the years.
If the wallpaper begins to color, it may be time to redecorate.
The cheap fabric tends to color after only a few washes.
Children love to color pictures in their books with crayons and markers.
She decided to color the living room walls a light blue.
The designer will color the fabric to match the curtains.
Over time, the sun colored the old photographs, giving them a faded look.
She carefully colors each illustration in the book with bright markers.
The children excitedly colored pictures of animals during art class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bleach - to remove color from something
  • synonym: color - add color to something (American spelling)
  • synonym: discolor - to change to a different, usually less attractive color
  • synonym: discolour - to change colour in a way that makes something less attractive
  • synonym: dye - to color something by soaking it in a coloring substance
  • synonym: paint - to cover with paint
