firman

noun
Ottoman official decree
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɪrˈmɑːn /; / ˈfɜːrmən / (AmE), / fɪəˈmɑːn /; / ˈfɜːmən / (BrE)
an official order or decree issued by a ruler, especially in the Ottoman Empire

Examples

An official firman
The sultan issued a firman granting new rights to the local community.
Without a firman, merchants were not allowed to enter the city walls.
The museum displays a historic firman written in elegant calligraphy.
The merchant presented the firman to prove his right to trade in the city.
A firman from the sultan allowed the construction of the new bridge.
Without a firman, foreign traders were not permitted to enter the port.
