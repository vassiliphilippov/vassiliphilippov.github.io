jurisdictionally

adverb
in terms of legal authority
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɝːɪsˈdɪkʃənəli / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərɪsˈdɪkʃənəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the authority or power to make legal decisions or judgments

Examples

Decide jurisdictionally
The judge determined jurisdictionally whether the case could be heard in that court.
The dispute was resolved jurisdictionally before any evidence was presented.
Lawyers argued jurisdictionally about which country had the right to try the case.
