extratropical

adjective
outside the tropics
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstrəˈtrɑːpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
describing or relating to weather systems or phenomena that occur outside the tropical regions, especially in cooler or mid-latitude areas

Examples

After losing its warm core, the hurricane was classified as extratropical.
The extratropical cyclone caused strong winds and heavy rainfall across the coast.
Meteorologists tracked the movement of an extratropical storm approaching the continent.
Unlike hurricanes, extratropical systems often form in cooler regions far from the equator.
The extratropical storm brought heavy rain and strong winds to the coastal city.
Scientists are studying how extratropical cyclones affect weather patterns in the northern hemisphere.
After moving north, the hurricane became an extratropical system with different characteristics.
