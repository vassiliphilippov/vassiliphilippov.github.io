value judgment

noun
an opinion based on personal values
Pronunciation: / ˈvælju ˈdʒʌdʒmənt /; / ˈvælju ˌdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈvæljuː ˈdʒʌdʒmənt /; / ˈvalju ˌdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
an opinion or assessment based on personal values or standards rather than objective facts

Examples

A value judgment opinion
Her criticism was more of a value judgment than an objective analysis.
The professor reminded students to avoid making value judgments in their research papers.
Try to separate facts from value judgments when making decisions.
Her comments about the new policy were clearly a value judgement.
Making a value judgement about art is always influenced by personal taste.
Try to avoid value judgements when analyzing historical events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fact - something that is known or proven to be true
  • synonym: judgment - an opinion or decision about something
  • synonym: opinion - a belief or view about something, not necessarily based on fact
