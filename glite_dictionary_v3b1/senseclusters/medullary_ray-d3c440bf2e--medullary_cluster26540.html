medullary ray

noun
sheet of plant tissue separating vascular bundles
Pronunciation: / məˈdʌləri reɪ / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌləri reɪ / (BrE)
a sheet of tissue consisting of parenchyma cells that extends from the pith to the cortex in plant stems and roots, separating vascular bundles and facilitating radial transport.

Examples

The medullary ray tissue
In many plants, the medullary ray divides the vascular bundles and aids in water movement.
Scientists study the medullary rays to better understand nutrient transport in trees.
The medullary ray can easily be seen in a cross-section of a woody stem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vascular bundle - a strand of conducting vessels in the stem or leaves of a plant
