kinesthetic

adjective
relating to bodily movement sensation
Pronunciation: / ˌkɪnəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the perception or sensation of body movement, position, or muscular tension; connected with activities, learning, and awareness that involve physical movement

Examples

Kinesthetic learning style
Dancers rely heavily on their kinesthetic awareness to coordinate complex movements.
Some students are kinesthetic learners who understand concepts better through physical activities.
The kinesthetic sense allows you to touch your nose even with your eyes closed.
The teacher used kinesthetic activities to help students understand the science lesson.
Some children have a strong kinesthetic learning style and prefer hands-on experiences.
Dancers develop excellent kinesthetic awareness through years of practice and movement.
Children with a kinaesthetic learning style learn best through hands-on activities.
Many physical therapists use kinaesthetic feedback to guide patients during rehabilitation.
The dance instructor emphasized kinaesthetic awareness to help students control their movements.
