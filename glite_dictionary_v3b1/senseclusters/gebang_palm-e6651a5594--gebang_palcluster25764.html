gebang palm

noun
tropical palm tree with large, useful leaves
5.1
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈbɑŋ pɑm / (AmE), / ɡəˈbaŋ pɑːm / (BrE)
a large palm tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia, with wide leaves used for thatching or weaving containers

Examples

A gebang palm tree
A majestic gebang palm stood at the edge of the rice field, its fronds swaying in the wind.
Farmers often use gebang palm leaves to thatch roofs in rural villages.
The leaves of the gebang palm are commonly woven into baskets and mats in Indonesia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fan palm - any palm with fan-shaped leaves
