precast

adjective
cast in final shape before installation
Pronunciation: / ˌpriˈkæst / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkɑːst / (BrE)
(of building materials, especially concrete) cast in its final shape before being transported to the site for installation.

Examples

Precast concrete sections
Engineers inspected the precast sections before they were delivered to the construction area.
Many modern apartment buildings feature precast wall panels to speed up construction.
The new bridge uses precast concrete beams for faster assembly on site.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cast-in-place - (of concrete) poured and formed at the construction site, not precast
