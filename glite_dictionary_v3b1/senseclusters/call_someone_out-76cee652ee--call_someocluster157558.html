call (someone) out

verb
challenge or criticize someone publicly
Pronunciation: / kɔl ˈmaɪ aʊt / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈmaɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to challenge or criticize someone, especially publicly, about something they have done wrong or a mistake they have made

Examples

To call someone out
He didn't hesitate to call his coworker out when she made a mistake during the presentation.
People often appreciate it when someone calls out injustice instead of staying silent.
When I forgot my promise, my friend called me out in front of everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: call (someone) on (something) - to confront someone about their behavior or words
  • synonym: challenge - confront someone in order to dispute or correct them
