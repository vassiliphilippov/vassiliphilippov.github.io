anthropophagic

adjective
culturally absorbing
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpəˈfædʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈfædʒɪk / (BrE)
absorbing, transforming, and reworking elements from other cultures, especially in art or cultural criticism, like cultural cannibalism

Examples

Anthropophagic art style
The artist's anthropophagic style blends indigenous traditions with modern European influences.
Critics praised the film's anthropophagic use of global pop culture to create something original.
Her writing is known for its anthropophagic approach, transforming borrowed stories into new narratives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appropriative - taking elements from another culture, often without permission
  • synonym: syncretic - combining different beliefs or practices
