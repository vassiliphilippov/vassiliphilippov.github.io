fixed charge

noun
regular cost not changing with activity
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪkst tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɪkst tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a regular payment or cost that does not change with the level of activity or business volume, such as rent, insurance, or mortgage payments

Examples

A fixed charge payment
Insurance premiums are often considered a fixed charge in a company's annual budget.
Rent is a fixed charge that businesses must pay each month, regardless of their sales.
The company's high fixed charges made it harder to adjust to changes in revenue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: variable charge - a cost that changes according to business volume or activity
