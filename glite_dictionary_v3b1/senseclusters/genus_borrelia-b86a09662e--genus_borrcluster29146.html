genus Borrelia

noun
genus of spiral parasitic bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs bɔːrˈiː.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bɒˈrɛl.i.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, flexible parasitic spirochete bacteria characterized by three to five wavy spirals, some species of which cause Lyme disease and relapsing fever.

Examples

Genus Borrelia bacteria
Scientists are studying the genus Borrelia to better understand its transmission by ticks.
Symptoms of relapsing fever are caused by spirochetes in the genus Borrelia.
The genus Borrelia includes bacteria responsible for Lyme disease and other infections.
