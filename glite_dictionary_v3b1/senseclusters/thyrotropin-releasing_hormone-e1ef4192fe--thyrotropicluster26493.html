thyrotropin-releasing hormone

noun
hormone controlling thyroid hormone release
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪroʊˈtroʊpɪn rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪrəʊˈtrəʊpɪn rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) from the anterior pituitary gland

Examples

Thyrotropin-releasing hormone production
A disorder in the hypothalamus can affect the production of thyrotropin-releasing hormone.
Researchers measured thyrotropin-releasing hormone levels to investigate thyroid function in patients.
The level of thyrotropin-releasing hormone increases when the body needs more thyroid hormones.
