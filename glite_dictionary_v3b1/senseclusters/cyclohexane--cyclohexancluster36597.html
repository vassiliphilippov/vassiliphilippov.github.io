cyclohexane

noun
liquid hydrocarbon solvent
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkloʊˈhɛkseɪn / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkləʊˈhɛkseɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid hydrocarbon used as a solvent and in chemical manufacturing

Examples

Pure cyclohexane liquid
The laboratory used cyclohexane as a solvent for the chemical reaction.
Because cyclohexane is flammable, it must be handled with care in the lab.
Many industrial processes rely on cyclohexane for the production of nylon and other materials.
