sangha

noun
Buddhist community
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈsʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a community of Buddhist monks, nuns, and followers who practice and support Buddhism together

Examples

The sangha community
The sangha plays an important role in supporting each member's spiritual growth.
She joined the local sangha to learn more about Buddhist teachings and meditation.
During the festival, the entire sangha gathered to celebrate and share a meal.
The local sangha gathers every week to meditate and discuss Buddhist teachings.
She found a sense of belonging in her new sangha after moving to the city.
Many people join a sangha to deepen their spiritual practice and connect with others.
Many Buddhists take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the sangha.
The sangha gathers daily to chant and meditate together in the temple.
Joining the sangha is considered a significant step on the Buddhist path.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congregation - a group of people assembled for religious worship
  • antonym: laity - ordinary people as distinct from religious professionals
  • synonym: monastery - a building or community where monks or nuns live and practice their religion
  • synonym: monastic community - a group of monks or nuns living together for religious purposes
