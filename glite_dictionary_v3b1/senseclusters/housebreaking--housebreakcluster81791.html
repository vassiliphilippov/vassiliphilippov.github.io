housebreaking

noun
pet toilet training
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of training a pet, especially a dog, to urinate or defecate outside or in a specific place

Examples

Puppy housebreaking
Successful housebreaking depends on a consistent routine and positive reinforcement.
Many new dog owners struggle with housebreaking their puppies during the first few weeks.
The trainer offered helpful tips for housebreaking a rescue dog in a new home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obedience training - teaching a pet to follow commands and behave properly
  • synonym: potty training - teaching a young child or pet to use a toilet or designated area
