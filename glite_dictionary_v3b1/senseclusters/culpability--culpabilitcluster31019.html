responsibility or blame for a fault or wrongdoing; the state of being at fault or deserving blame

Examples

Culpability for fault
He expressed remorse but denied any culpability for the accident.
Legal proceedings must establish the culpability of the person accused before passing judgment.
The investigation focused on determining the culpability of each suspect in the crime.
The court debated whether the autonomous vehicle's software bore culpability for the pedestrian's death.
Scholars argue society must assign culpability to algorithms that systematically discriminate in hiring decisions.
Activists demanded clear rules so companies cannot shift culpability for harmful AI decisions onto users.

Synonyms

  • culpa
    9.9 0.66/m
    culpa is a legal and formal word for blame in a court or report, while culpability names being at fault.
  • blameworthiness
    7.6 0.03/m
    blameworthiness means deserving blame because of a wrong action, while culpability is the condition of being blameworthy.
  • guilt
    2.7 9.89/m
    guilt is the state of having done a wrong or feeling remorse, while culpability refers to legal or moral blame.
  • blame
    1.0 19/m
    blame is a common word for fault or criticism given to someone, while culpability is the formal idea of being at fault.
  • fault
    1.8 59/m
    fault is often a simple mistake or cause of a problem, while culpability is formal blame for wrongdoing.
