wryness

noun
dry or ironic humor
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
a quality of expressing dry, mocking, or ironic humor

Examples

Wryness in her jokes
Her wryness made even serious topics seem amusing during the meeting.
He responded to the criticism with a touch of wryness in his voice.
The author's wryness is evident throughout the novel, adding a unique charm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irony - the use of words to express something different from their literal meaning
  • synonym: sarcasm - the use of sharp or cutting remarks, often for humor
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest and straightforward
