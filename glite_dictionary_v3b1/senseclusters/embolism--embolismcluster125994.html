embolism

noun
blockage of a blood vessel by an embolus
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛmbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the sudden blockage of a blood vessel by an embolus, such as a blood clot, air bubble, or other particle carried in the bloodstream

Examples

Blood embolism
Air bubbles entering the bloodstream during surgery may cause an embolism.
Doctors explained that an embolism can quickly become life-threatening if not treated promptly.
The patient suffered an embolism that blocked the artery in his leg.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embolus - a mass, such as a clot or air bubble, that causes an embolism
  • degree lower: ischemia - insufficient blood supply to an organ or part of the body
  • synonym: thrombosis - formation of a blood clot inside a blood vessel
