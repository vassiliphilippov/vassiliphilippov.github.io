numb

verb
make unable to feel; deaden
4.2 3.70/m
Pronunciation: / nÊŒm / (AmE), / nÊŒm / (BrE)
to make a part of the body or the mind unable to feel sensation or emotion, typically due to shock, cold, or a drug

Examples

To numb the pain
The dentist injected a local anesthetic to numb the area before starting the procedure.
The icy wind quickly numbed my fingers as I waited for the bus.
Years of disappointment had numbed his enthusiasm for the sport.

Synonyms

  • benumb
    8.5 0.07/m
    benumb makes a body or mind lose feeling or sensitivity, while numb often describes loss of feeling from shock, cold, or drugs.
  • freeze
    1.0 7.21/m
    freeze causes extreme cold that makes a body part feel numb, while numb can result from shock or drugs as well.
  • dull
    3.4 2.43/m
    dull makes pain, sound, or colour less sharp or intense, while numb means the feeling or sensation is largely or fully lost.
  • blunt
    4.2 1.94/m
    blunt makes something less sharp, strong, or effective, while numb describes a loss of physical or emotional feeling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deaden - to make less sensitive or less intense; to dull
  • antonym: stimulate - to make more sensitive or responsive
