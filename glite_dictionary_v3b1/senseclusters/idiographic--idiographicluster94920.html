idiographic

adjective
relating to individual cases or events
Pronunciation: / ˌɪdiəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the study or description of individual cases or events, especially in contrast to general laws.

Examples

Idiographic study methods
Her research used idiographic methods to analyze the personal experiences of participants.
The idiographic approach in psychology focuses on understanding the unique characteristics of each individual.
Unlike nomothetic studies, idiographic research emphasizes in-depth case analysis over broad generalizations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nomothetic - relating to the discovery of general laws or principles
