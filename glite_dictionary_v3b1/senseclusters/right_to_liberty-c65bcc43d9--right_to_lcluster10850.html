right to liberty

noun
legal or moral entitlement to freedom
Pronunciation: / raɪt tuː ˈlɪbɚti / (AmE), / raɪt tʊ ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
the legal or moral entitlement to personal freedom and independence, free from arbitrary restraint or interference

Examples

The right to liberty protection
Activists argue that detaining suspects without trial violates their fundamental right to liberty.
The constitution guarantees every citizen the right to liberty and security of person.
Throughout history, people have fought for their right to liberty against oppressive regimes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants
