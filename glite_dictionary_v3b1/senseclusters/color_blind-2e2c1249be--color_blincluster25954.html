color blind

adjective
claiming not to consider race
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌl.ər blaɪnd/ /; / /ˈkɔːl.ər blaɪnd/ (AmE), /ˈkʌl.ə blaɪnd/ (BrE)
(of an attitude, policy, or person) claiming or attempting not to consider a person's race when treating or judging them; often criticized for overlooking systemic or institutional racial inequalities.

Examples

Color blind policy
Many companies claim a color blind hiring policy, but disparities persist in their workforce.
She argued that a color-blind approach to education ignores structural inequalities affecting minority students.
Critics say being color blind can prevent people from recognizing and addressing racial injustice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: race-blind - claiming not to take race into account when making decisions or judgments
  • antonym: race-conscious - aware of race and its social effects; deliberately considers race in decisions or policies
