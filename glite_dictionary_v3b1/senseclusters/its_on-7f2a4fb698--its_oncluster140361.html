it's on

x
confirmation something is happening or starting
Pronunciation: / ɪts ɑn / (AmE), / ɪts ɒn / (BrE)
Used to say that something previously discussed, planned, or considered is definitely going to happen or has started.

Examples

It's on now
After months of delays, the merger it's on and will begin next week.
The concert was uncertain, but now it's on and tickets are selling fast.
When I challenged him to a chess match, he replied, 'it's on.'

Related Terms

  • antonym: it's off - used to indicate something will not happen as planned
