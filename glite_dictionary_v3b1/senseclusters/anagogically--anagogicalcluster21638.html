anagogically

adverb
spiritually interpretively
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈɡɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌænəˈɡɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to spiritual or mystical interpretation, especially of texts or symbols

Examples

Explain anagogically the story
The medieval scholar explained the story anagogically, focusing on its spiritual significance.
Some readers prefer to interpret ancient myths anagogically rather than literally.
The priest encouraged the congregation to reflect anagogically on the meaning of the parable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegorically - in a way that uses symbols or stories to represent ideas
  • antonym: literally - in a way that uses the exact meaning of words
