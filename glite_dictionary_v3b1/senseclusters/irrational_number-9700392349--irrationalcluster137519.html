irrational number

noun
number that can't be written as a fraction
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɪˈræʃənl ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənəl ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a real number that cannot be expressed as a ratio of two integers, such as √2 or π.

Examples

The irrational number pi
Pi is perhaps the most famous irrational number in mathematics.
Some decimals, like 0.333..., are rational, but numbers like e and π are always irrational numbers.
The square root of 2 is an irrational number that cannot be written as a simple fraction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rational number - a number that can be expressed as the quotient of two integers
