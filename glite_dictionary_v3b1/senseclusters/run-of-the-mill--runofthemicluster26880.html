run-of-the-mill

adjective
ordinary; not special
Pronunciation: / ˌrʌn əv ðə ˈmɪl / (AmE), / ˌrʌn əv ðə ˈmɪl / (BrE)
ordinary and not special in any way; not remarkable

Examples

A run-of-the-mill product
His performance was entirely run-of-the-mill, with nothing to set it apart from the rest.
It was just another run-of-the-mill day at the office.
The restaurant's food was run-of-the-mill, nothing to get excited about.
He wore a run-of-the-mill grey suit to the meeting, blending in with everyone else.
Most of the suggestions were run-of-the-mill and did not inspire any excitement in the team.
The film was entertaining but ultimately run-of-the-mill, with a predictable plot and characters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: average - typical; not outstanding
  • antonym: exceptional - unusually good; outstanding
  • synonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features
  • antonym: remarkable - worthy of attention; striking, unusual
