Shigella dysenteriae

noun
bacterium causing dysentery
Pronunciation: / ʃɪˈɡɛl.ə daɪˌsɛnˈtɪər.i / (AmE), / ʃɪˈɡɛl.ə dɪs.ɛnˈtɪə.ri / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes dysentery in humans

Examples

Shigella dysenteriae bacterium
Shigella dysenteriae is commonly found in regions with poor sanitation and can spread through contaminated water.
Doctors confirmed that the outbreak was due to an infection with Shigella dysenteriae.
Proper hand washing can help prevent the transmission of Shigella dysenteriae among children.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Escherichia coli - a related genus of bacteria, some types of which can cause illness in humans
  • synonym: dysentery - an infectious disease causing severe diarrhea
