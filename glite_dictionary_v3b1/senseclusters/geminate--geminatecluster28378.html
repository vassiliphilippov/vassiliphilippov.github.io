geminate

noun
doubled or long consonant
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛm.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛm.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
a consonant that is pronounced for a longer duration or appears as a doubled letter in speech or writing

Examples

A geminate consonant
In Italian, the word 'fatto' contains a geminate 't' sound.
Some languages use geminate consonants to distinguish word meanings.
The double 'l' in 'illegal' is a geminate consonant.
