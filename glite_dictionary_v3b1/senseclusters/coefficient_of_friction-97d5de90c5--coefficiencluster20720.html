coefficient of friction

noun
physics ratio for frictional force
4.0 6.4
Pronunciation: / koʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv ˈfrɪkʃən / (BrE)
the numerical ratio describing the force that resists motion between two contacting surfaces, equal to the force maintaining contact divided by the force necessary to move the object

Examples

The coefficient of friction value
Different surfaces have a different coefficient of friction, affecting how objects slide across them.
The engineer calculated the coefficient of friction to select a safe tire material.
Waxing the floor lowers the coefficient of friction, making it easier for objects to move.

Related Terms

  • synonym: friction - the resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another
