quivery

adjective
slightly trembling
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈkwɪvəri / (BrE)
shaking or trembling slightly, often because of emotion or weakness

Examples

A quivery voice
Her hands felt quivery as she tried to unlock the door after hearing the strange noise.
He gave a quivery smile when he stood up to speak in front of the class.
The old dog’s legs were quivery after the long walk in the park.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - firm and not shaking or moving
  • synonym: tremulous - shaking or quivering slightly
