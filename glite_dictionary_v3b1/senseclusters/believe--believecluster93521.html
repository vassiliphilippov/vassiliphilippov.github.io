believe

verb
accept as true; trust in truth
Pronunciation: / bəˈliv /; / bɪˈliv / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to accept or think that something is true, to trust or have confidence in something or someone

Examples

I believe the story
Do you believe everything you read online?
Many people believe that honesty is the best policy.
She couldn't believe her eyes when she saw the surprise.
He believed every word she said, despite the evidence to the contrary.
Many people believe that climate change is a serious global issue.
She couldn't believe her eyes when she saw the new car in the driveway.
Do you believe he will keep his promise this time?
I can't believe she finished the project all by herself.
Most people believe that exercise is good for your health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about; to disbelieve
  • synonym: suppose - to assume something to be true without proof
  • antonym: suspect - to have an idea or impression but without certain proof
  • synonym: think - to form an opinion about something or someone
  • synonym: trust - to place confidence in or rely on someone or something
