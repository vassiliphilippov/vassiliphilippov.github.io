Scottish maple

noun
a Eurasian maple tree with peeling bark
Pronunciation: / ˈskɑːtɪʃ ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A Eurasian maple tree (Acer pseudoplatanus) with pale grey bark that peels in flakes and leaves with five ovate lobes that turn yellow in autumn.

Examples

A Scottish maple tree
A Scottish maple can often be identified by its flaky, pale grey bark.
Many gardens in Europe feature the Scottish maple for its ornamental value.
The leaves of the Scottish maple turn a brilliant yellow during autumn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sycamore maple - another common name for Acer pseudoplatanus
