summer solstice

noun
longest day, sun's northernmost point
4.2 0.36/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒmÉš ËˆsÉ‘l.stÉªs / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒmÉ™ ËˆsÉ’l.stÉªs / (BrE)
The time or date, usually June 21 in the northern hemisphere, when the sun reaches its most northerly point, marking the longest day of the year.

Examples

The summer solstice day
Celebrations and festivals are held around the world to mark the summer solstice.
On the summer solstice, the sun sets at its latest time of the year.
The ancient monument at Stonehenge is aligned with the rising sun on the summer solstice.

Synonyms

  • midsummer
    3.7 1.40/m
    midsummer is the local, festival season around June 21 with long daylight, while summer solstice is the single astronomical day.
  • June 21
    1.0 0.32/m
    June 21 is the calendar date people put on plans, while summer solstice is the astronomical event that may fall on nearby dates.
