legginess

noun
long legs or stems
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛɡinəs / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡinəs / (BrE)
the quality of having unusually long legs or, in plants, long and thin stems due to insufficient light

Examples

The legginess of plants
The model's legginess made her stand out on the runway.
Gardeners try to prevent legginess in seedlings by providing enough sunlight.
His legginess gave him an advantage in the high jump competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spindliness - the quality of being long, thin, and weak-looking
  • antonym: sturdiness - the quality of being strong and solid
