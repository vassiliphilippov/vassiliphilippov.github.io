tremendismo

noun
Spanish literary style marked by violence and grotesque imagery
Pronunciation: / trɛˌmɛnˈdizmoʊ / (AmE), / trɛˌmɛnˈdɪzməʊ / (BrE)
A post-war Spanish literary style characterized by the use of violence and grotesque imagery, often emphasizing the ugly and irrational aspects of life.

Examples

Tremendismo literary style
Her writing style was often compared to tremendismo because of its focus on bleak and violent themes.
Scholars have debated whether the author's work truly fits within the tradition of tremendismo.
The influence of tremendismo can be seen in many Spanish novels of the mid-20th century.
