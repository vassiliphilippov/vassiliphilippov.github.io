reduce

verb
to make smaller or less; to lessen
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdus / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to make something smaller, less in amount, size, number, or degree; to decrease or cut down

Examples

To reduce costs
Due to the recession, the family had to reduce their spending.
Rising costs forced them to reduce the size of their workforce.
You should reduce your daily sugar intake if you want to improve your health.
After the surgeon reduced the dislocated shoulder, the patient felt relief.
The broken bone was carefully reduced and secured with pins.
The doctor needed to reduce the fracture immediately to prevent complications.
I need to reduce the image before uploading it to the website.
The company decided to reduce the size of its workforce due to budget cuts.
You can reduce the amount of sugar in this recipe if you'd like.
