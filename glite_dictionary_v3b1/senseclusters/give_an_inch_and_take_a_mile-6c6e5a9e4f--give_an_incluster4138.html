give an inch and take a mile

x
exploit a small concession to gain more
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ən ɪntʃ ənd teɪk ə maɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪv ən ɪntʃ ənd teɪk ə maɪl / (BrE)
if you allow someone a small amount of freedom or make a small concession, they will try to take much more than you intended

Examples

Give an inch and take a mile
If you give an inch and take a mile with your boss, you might soon find yourself in trouble.
Parents often complain that if they give an inch and take a mile, their children will keep demanding more.
She warned him not to give an inch and take a mile when negotiating the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overstep the mark - to go beyond what is acceptable or allowed
  • synonym: push one's luck - to take risks expecting continued success
