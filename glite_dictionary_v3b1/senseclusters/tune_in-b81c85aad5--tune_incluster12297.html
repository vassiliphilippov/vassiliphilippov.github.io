tune in

verb
adjust device to receive a station
1.7 2.7
Pronunciation: /tuːn ɪn/ /; / /tjuːn ɪn/ (AmE), /tjuːn ɪn/ /; / /tʃuːn ɪn/ (BrE)
to set a radio or television to a particular station or program, or to watch or listen to a broadcast

Examples

To tune in a radio
If you tune in at 8 p.m., you'll hear the live concert broadcast.
Millions of Americans tune in to their favorite shows each week.
She quickly tuned in the radio to catch the morning news.
I always tune in to the local news at six every evening.
Millions of viewers tuned in to watch the championship game.
You can tune in online if you can't reach a TV.

Related Terms

  • synonym: listen - to give attention with the ear; to hear attentively
  • antonym: tune out - to stop listening to or ignore; opposite action of paying attention or adjusting to receive
  • synonym: watch - to look at or observe attentively
