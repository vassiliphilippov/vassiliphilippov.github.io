proteasome

noun
cellular protein complex
9.9
Pronunciation: /proʊ.ˈtiː.ə.soʊm/ /; / /ˈproʊ.tiː.ə.soʊm/ (AmE), /prəʊ.ˈtiː.ə.səʊm/ /; / /ˈprəʊ.tiː.ə.səʊm/ (BrE)
a large protein complex in cells that breaks down unneeded or damaged proteins into smaller peptides

Examples

The proteasome complex
The proteasome plays a key role in controlling the quality of proteins inside the cell.
Scientists study the proteasome to understand how cells remove damaged or misfolded proteins.
Certain drugs can block the proteasome, which may help treat some types of cancer.
