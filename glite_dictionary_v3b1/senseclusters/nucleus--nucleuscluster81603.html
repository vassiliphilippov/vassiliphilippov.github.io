nucleus

noun
central part of the lens in the eye
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
the central structure of the lens in the eye, surrounded by the cortex

Examples

Lens nucleus
During the operation, the nucleus was differentiated from the surrounding cortex.
The density of the nucleus affects the type of lens replacement required.
The surgeon carefully removed the nucleus of the lens to treat the cataract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cortex - the outer layer of the lens of the eye
