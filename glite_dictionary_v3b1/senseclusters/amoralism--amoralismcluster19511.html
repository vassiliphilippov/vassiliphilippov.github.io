amoralism

noun
doctrine rejecting moral distinctions
Pronunciation: / əˈmɔr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / əˈmɔː.rəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that moral distinctions or principles are invalid or irrelevant

Examples

Amoralism belief
He regarded amoralism as a serious threat to societal values and order.
Some argue that amoralism undermines the very foundation of social cooperation.
The philosopher's writings explore amoralism and its implications for ethics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amorality - the state or quality of being without moral standards or principles
  • antonym: moralism - the practice of moralizing, especially showing a tendency to make judgments about others' morality
