Westinghouse

proper noun
surname; engineer
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstɪŋˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwɛstɪŋˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a family name, especially referring to George Westinghouse (1846–1914), an American engineer and industrialist

Examples

Westinghouse invented the brake
George Westinghouse was a key figure in the development of the American electrical industry.
The Westinghouse family contributed significantly to engineering and innovation in the 19th century.
Many inventions are credited to Westinghouse, including improvements to railway safety.
