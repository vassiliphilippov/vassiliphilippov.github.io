quartz sand

noun
sand composed mainly of quartz grains
Pronunciation: / kwɔrts sænd / (AmE), / kwɔːts sand / (BrE)
Sand that consists mainly of quartz grains, commonly used as a raw material in the production of glass and other industrial applications.

Examples

Layer of quartz sand
A layer of quartz sand covers the surface of the site, providing stability for construction.
Engineers tested different grades of quartz sand for use in water filtration systems.
The factory imports quartz sand to manufacture high-quality glass products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: silica sand - sand rich in silica, often used interchangeably with quartz sand in technical contexts
