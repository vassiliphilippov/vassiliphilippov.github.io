arteriosclerosis obliterans

noun
arterial disease causing vessel closure
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌtɪri.oʊskləˈroʊsɪs ɒbˌlɪtəˈrænz / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəri.əʊskləˈrəʊsɪs ɒbˌlɪtəˈrænz / (BrE)
A severe stage of arteriosclerosis characterized by the closure or obstruction of blood vessels, especially arteries, due to their thickening and hardening.

Examples

Arteriosclerosis obliterans disease
Early diagnosis of arteriosclerosis obliterans is crucial to prevent limb loss.
Peripheral blood flow can be severely restricted in patients with arteriosclerosis obliterans.
The surgeon explained that arteriosclerosis obliterans was the cause of the patient's chronic leg pain.
