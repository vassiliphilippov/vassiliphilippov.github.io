ripcord

noun
cord for releasing a parachute
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪpˌkɔrd /; / ˈrɪpkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈrɪpˌkɔːd /; / ˈrɪpkɔːd / (BrE)
a cord that you pull to release a parachute or to let gas out of a balloon, usually to help someone or something descend safely

Examples

Pull the ripcord
Always check your ripcord before a jump to ensure it functions properly.
In an emergency, failing to find the ripcord could be life-threatening.
The skydiver pulled the ripcord and the parachute deployed smoothly.
In case of an emergency, you must know the location of the ripcord.
The pilot showed us how to use the ripcord before we took off in the hot air balloon.
When the balloon began to descend, he reached for the ripcord and pulled it quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parachute - a device used to slow the motion of a person or object through an atmosphere by creating drag
