risky

adjective
involving danger or hazard
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪski / (AmE), / ˈrɪski / (BrE)
involving the possibility of loss, harm, or danger; hazardous (including financial or other risks)

Examples

A risky choice
Climbing alone in bad weather can be extremely risky.
He avoided taking on any risky projects at work so close to his retirement.
Investing in that company was a risky decision, but it paid off in the end.
He lost most of his money on a risky real estate deal.
Investing in that startup would be a risky move for our company.
Some people enjoy making risky bets on the stock market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hazardous - dangerous, involving risk or danger
  • antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm or risk; secure
  • synonym: speculative - involving a high degree of risk, especially financially
