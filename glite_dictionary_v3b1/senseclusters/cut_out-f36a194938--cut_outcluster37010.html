cut out

verb
to block or interrupt someone’s progress
Pronunciation: / kʌt aʊt / (AmE), / kʌt aʊt / (BrE)
to block, intercept, or prevent someone or something from continuing, especially by moving in front or in sports

Examples

To cut out a player
During the race, a competitor cut out the runner, forcing her to slow down.
She was about to make a turn when another car cut out in front of her.
The van suddenly cut out the cyclist at the intersection.
He cut out their attack and quickly started a counteroffensive.
She cuts out potential threats with her quick reactions during the game.
The defender cut out the pass just before it reached the striker.
