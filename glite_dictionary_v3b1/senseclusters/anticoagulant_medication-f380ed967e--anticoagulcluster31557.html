anticoagulant medication

noun
medicine that prevents blood from clotting
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌkoʊˈæɡjələnt ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈkəʊˌæɡjʊlənt ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a medicine that prevents or slows the clotting of blood, often used to reduce the risk of blood clots

Examples

Anticoagulant medication use
Patients on anticoagulant medication require regular blood tests to ensure safe dosage levels.
Taking anticoagulant medication can increase the chance of bruising or bleeding.
The doctor prescribed an anticoagulant medication to lower the risk of stroke after the surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blood thinner - medicine that reduces the risk of blood clotting
