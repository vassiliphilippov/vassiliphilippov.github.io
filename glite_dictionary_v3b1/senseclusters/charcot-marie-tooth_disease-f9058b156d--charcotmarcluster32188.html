Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease

noun
hereditary nerve disorder causing muscle weakness
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑrkoʊ ˈmæri tuːθ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːkəʊ ˈmæri tuːθ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a hereditary neurological disorder that causes progressive weakness and wasting of the muscles in the hands and lower legs, often starting in childhood or young adulthood

Examples

Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease symptoms
Her father and grandfather both had Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease, so she decided to get tested.
Researchers are looking for new treatments for Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease to slow its progression.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease after observing muscle weakness in his legs.
