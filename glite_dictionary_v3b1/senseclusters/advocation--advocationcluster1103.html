advocation

noun
legal advocacy
Pronunciation: / ˌædvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌædvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of pleading or speaking in support of someone, especially in a legal context

Examples

Legal advocation
The lawyer's advocation before the judge convinced the jury to reconsider the verdict.
Her strong advocation for her client helped win the case.
Without proper advocation, many people struggle to defend their rights in court.
