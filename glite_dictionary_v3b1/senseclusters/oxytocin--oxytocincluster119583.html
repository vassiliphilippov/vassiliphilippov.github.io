oxytocin

noun
hormone stimulating labor and milk ejection
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksɪˈtoʊsɪn / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈtəʊsɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the pituitary gland that stimulates uterine contractions during childbirth and the ejection of milk during breastfeeding

Examples

Oxytocin hormone release
Breastfeeding releases oxytocin, which strengthens the bond between mother and baby.
Researchers are studying how oxytocin may influence social behavior.
The doctor administered oxytocin to help induce labor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pitocin - trade name for synthetic oxytocin used to induce labor
