ground floor

noun
initial stage of an enterprise
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd flɔr / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd flɔː / (BrE)
the initial stage or starting point of a business or venture, usually seen as offering an advantage to those involved at this early stage

Examples

Ground floor opportunity
Being part of the ground floor team gave him influence over key decisions.
Investors who joined at the ground floor reaped the biggest rewards when the company grew.
She was lucky enough to get in on the ground floor of a successful tech startup.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts
  • antonym: end - the final stage or conclusion of something
