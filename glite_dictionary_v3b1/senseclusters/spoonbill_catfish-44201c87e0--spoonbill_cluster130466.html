spoonbill catfish

noun
large US freshwater catfish with a spoon-shaped snout
Pronunciation: / ˈspuːnˌbɪl ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspuːnbɪl ˈkatfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large freshwater catfish of the central United States, notable for its flattened head and long, spoon-shaped snout

Examples

A spoonbill catfish fish
Fishermen are often excited to catch a spoonbill catfish because of its unusual appearance.
The spoonbill catfish can be found in the Mississippi River and its tributaries.
The long, paddle-like snout helps the spoonbill catfish sense prey in murky waters.

  • synonym: paddlefish - another name for the same species
