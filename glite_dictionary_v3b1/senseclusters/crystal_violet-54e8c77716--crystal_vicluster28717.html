crystal violet

noun
synthetic dye and antiseptic
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstl ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
a synthetic green crystal that turns violet in water, used as a dye, stain in microscopy, and as an antiseptic and antifungal agent

Examples

Crystal violet dye
Some antifungal creams contain crystal violet as their active ingredient.
The laboratory technician used crystal violet to stain the bacterial sample for microscopic analysis.
The solution turned a deep purple after adding crystal violet to the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gentian violet - another name for crystal violet, commonly used in medicine
