rule in favor of

verb
decide to support one party (legal)
3.7
Pronunciation: / ruːl ɪn ˈfeɪvɚ ʌv / (AmE), / ruːl ɪn ˈfeɪvər ɒv / (BrE)
(of a court or judge) to make a decision that supports one party over the other, especially in a legal dispute.

Examples

To rule in favor of a party
A lower court ruled in favor of the company, but the decision was later appealed.
In previous cases, the Supreme Court has often ruled in favor of similar claims.
The judge decided to rule in favor of the defendant after reviewing all the evidence.

Related Terms
