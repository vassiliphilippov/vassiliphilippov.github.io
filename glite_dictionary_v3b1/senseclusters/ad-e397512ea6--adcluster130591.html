A.D.

adverb
used before years in the Christian era

used before a year to indicate a date in the Christian era, counting from the supposed year of the birth of Jesus Christ

Examples

A.D. 100
The Roman Empire was at its peak around A.D. 100.
The manuscript dates back to A.D. 780, making it over a thousand years old.
The temple was constructed in A.D. 350 by the local king.

Synonyms

  • AD
    1.0 0.00/m
    AD is written without dots and is common in modern texts, while A.D. has dots and feels older or more formal.
  • Anno Domini
    8.2 0.04/m
    Anno Domini is the full Latin phrase used in formal dates, while A.D. is the short written abbreviation with dots.
  • year of our Lord
    8.2 0.27/m
    year of our Lord is a formal English phrase used in legal or historical records, while A.D. is its brief punctuation form.
  • Common Era
    4.6 0.03/m
    Common Era is a neutral, secular term used in modern writing, while A.D. is a religious abbreviation showing Christian dating.
