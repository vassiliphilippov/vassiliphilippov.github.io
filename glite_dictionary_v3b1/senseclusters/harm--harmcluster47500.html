harm

verb
to cause injury or damage
Pronunciation: / hɑrm / (AmE), / hɑːm / (BrE)
to cause physical or mental injury or damage to someone or something

Examples

To harm a person
Loud noises can harm a baby's hearing permanently.
She promised that nothing would harm her children while she was around.
You must be careful not to harm the fragile equipment during transport.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damage - to cause physical harm or destruction to something
  • synonym: hurt - to cause pain, injury, or discomfort
  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
