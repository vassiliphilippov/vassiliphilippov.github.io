retighten

verb
to make strict again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtaɪtən / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtaɪtən / (BrE)
to make something stricter or more secure again, especially after it has been loosened or relaxed.

Examples

To retighten the rules
After the initial relaxation, the committee decided to retighten the safety regulations.
Officials may retighten restrictions if infection rates begin to rise again.
The mechanic had to retighten the bolts after noticing they had come loose.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loosen - to make something less strict or less secure
  • synonym: tighten - to make something more strict or secure
