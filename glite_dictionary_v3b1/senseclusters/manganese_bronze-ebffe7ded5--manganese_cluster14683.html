manganese bronze

noun
hard brass alloy with manganese
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋɡəniːz ˈbrɑnz / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəniːz ˈbrɒnz / (BrE)
A type of brass alloy containing 1–4% manganese, which improves its strength and hardness.

Examples

manganese bronze alloy
Engineers chose manganese bronze because of its superior resistance to corrosion.
The propeller was made from manganese bronze for increased durability.
You can identify manganese bronze by its distinct golden color and toughness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brass - a yellow alloy of copper and zinc
  • synonym: bronze - an alloy of copper and tin, often with other elements
