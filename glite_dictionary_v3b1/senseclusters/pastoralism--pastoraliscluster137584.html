pastoralism

noun
herding-based lifestyle
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpæstərəlɪzəm /; / ˈpæstərəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a way of life or economic system based on raising and herding domesticated animals, often involving moving them to different grazing areas

Examples

Pastoralism way of life
Pastoralism has shaped the culture and economy of many communities in East Africa.
In areas with little rainfall, pastoralism is often more sustainable than farming.
The spread of pastoralism led to the domestication of sheep, goats, and cattle.
Traditional pastoralism often involves moving sheep or cattle between summer and winter pastures.
In some regions, pastoralism is the main source of income for rural families.
Climate change is affecting the patterns of pastoralism in many parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agriculture - the practice of cultivating the land and growing crops
  • synonym: nomadism - a lifestyle of moving from place to place, often for grazing animals
