trample

verb
tread heavily or roughly
4.1 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈtræm.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈtræm.pəl/ (BrE)
to step or stomp heavily on something, often causing damage, injury, or showing disregard

Examples

To trample the flowers
Please do not trample on the newly planted grass.
The crowd began to trample the flowers in their excitement.
The protesters trampled over the barriers set up by the police.
Cattle sometimes trample crops when moving through the fields.
The child was nearly trampled by the crowd at the festival.
The tourists panicked and accidentally trampled several people at the entrance.
Crowds of tourists sometimes trample over sensitive habitats, causing damage.
Please be careful not to trample the wildflowers along the trail.
The parade trampled the fresh grass, leaving muddy footprints everywhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or is damaged
  • synonym: flatten - to make or become level or smooth
  • synonym: stamp - bring down one’s foot forcefully, often as an expression of anger
  • synonym: tread - to step or walk on or over
