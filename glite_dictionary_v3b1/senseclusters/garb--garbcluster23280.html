garb

verb
to dress; to clothe someone
7.9 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / …°…ërb / (AmE), / …°…ëÀêb / (BrE)
to dress or provide someone with clothing, especially in a particular style or type of clothes

Examples

To garb in costume
She was garbed in a flowing white gown for the ceremony.
The actors were garbed in elaborate costumes for the historical drama.
Volunteers garbed the children in warm coats before sending them outside.

Synonyms

  • dress
    1.0 74/m
    dress is to put clothes on for a style or event, while garb means to clothe someone in a particular type of clothing.
  • outfit
    3.7 0.96/m
    outfit means to provide clothes and gear for a special purpose, while garb focuses on dressing someone in a specific style.
  • apparel
    7.2 0.02/m
    apparel is a formal word for providing clothes to someone, while garb often stresses dressing in a particular style.
  • clothe
    3.2 0.95/m
    clothe means to put clothes on someone in a general way, while garb implies dressing them in a chosen style.
  • fit out
    4.2 0.07/m
    fit out means to equip with clothes or tools for a purpose, while garb specifically means dressing in a particular style.
