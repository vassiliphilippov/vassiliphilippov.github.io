kestrel

noun
small falcon species
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛstrəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛstrəl / (BrE)
a small falcon (bird of prey) with long wings and tail, known for hovering in the air while hunting

Examples

A kestrel bird
Many birdwatchers are excited to see a kestrel during their hikes.
The kestrel is known for its ability to hover in mid-air while hunting.
We spotted a kestrel hovering above the meadow, looking for small animals to catch.
A kestrel hovered motionless above the field, searching for its next meal.
We spotted a kestrel perched on the fence post early in the morning.
With sharp eyesight, the kestrel can spot even the smallest rodent from high above.
