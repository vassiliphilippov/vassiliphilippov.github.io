misbegotten

adjective
(archaic) born out of wedlock
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪs.bɪˈɡɑː.tən / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.bɪˈɡɒt.ən / (BrE)
(archaic) Illegitimate; born to parents not married to each other.

Examples

Misbegotten child
In the medieval period, misbegotten children had no right to inherit titles or property.
Rumors claimed that Prince John was misbegotten, which fueled the court's gossip.
She was shunned by the community as a misbegotten child, despite her innocence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legitimate - born to parents married to each other
