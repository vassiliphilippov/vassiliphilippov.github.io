requisition

noun
the seizing of property to pay a demand
3.6 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of seizing property that belongs to someone else and holding it until profits pay the demand for which it was seized, usually by legal or governmental authority

Examples

Requisition of property
During the war, the government ordered the requisition of several factories to produce military supplies.
Revolutionary governments sometimes resort to the requisition of essential goods in times of crisis.
The requisition of private land was challenged in court by the owners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confiscation - the action of taking someone's property with authority
