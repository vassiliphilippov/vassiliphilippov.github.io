burned-out

adjective
no longer working due to heat or friction
Pronunciation: / bɝnd aʊt /; / bɝnt aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnd aʊt /; / bɜːnt aʊt / (BrE)
(of a device or component) no longer functioning because excessive heat or friction has caused damage.

Examples

Burned-out bulb
After years of use, the television ended up with a burned-out picture tube.
She replaced the burned-out bulb in the living room lamp.
The burned-out motor made it impossible to start the machine.
After years of use, the burnt-out motor failed to start.
The burnt-out fuse needed to be replaced before the lamp would work again.
We had to remove the burnt-out bulb and put in a new one.

  • synonym: burned-out - inoperative as a result of heat or friction; alternative spelling
