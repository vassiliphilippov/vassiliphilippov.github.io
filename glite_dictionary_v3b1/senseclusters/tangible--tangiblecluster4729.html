tangible

adjective
able to be touched or perceived
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒəbəl /; / ˈtændʒəbl / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪbəl /; / ˈtan.dʒɪ.bl̩ / (BrE)
real and able to be touched, felt, or clearly shown; not abstract or imaginary

Examples

Tangible evidence
Her fear was almost tangible, as if you could reach out and feel it.
The museum displayed several tangible artifacts that visitors could touch.
There was a tangible sense of excitement in the air before the concert began.
Only tangible property can be insured against fire or theft.
The company owns several tangible assets, including buildings and equipment.
Their tangible resources give them a significant advantage over rival firms.
Despite his promises, there was no tangible improvement in their living conditions.
She needed a tangible goal to stay motivated in her studies.
The evidence provided by the witness was tangible and crucial to the case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intangible - unable to be touched or grasped; not having a physical presence
  • synonym: palpable - able to be touched or felt; easily perceived
