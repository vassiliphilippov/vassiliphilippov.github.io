dissect

verb
cut open for scientific study
3.3
Pronunciation: / daɪˈsɛkt, dɪˈsɛkt / (AmE), / daɪˈsɛkt, dɪˈsɛkt / (BrE)
to cut open or apart (especially a dead body or plant) in order to study its internal parts

Examples

To dissect a frog
In biology class, we had to dissect a frog to study its anatomy.
She watched closely as the professor began to dissect the plant under the microscope.
The scientists dissected the specimen carefully to examine the disease's effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analyze - to examine in detail, especially to break down into components
