procurator

noun
Roman official managing finance and taxes
Pronunciation: / proʊˈkjʊrˌeɪtər / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊərətə / (BrE)
an official in ancient Rome appointed by the Emperor to manage financial affairs and collect taxes in a province or district

Examples

Roman procurator
Archaeologists discovered a document bearing the name of a procurator from Judaea.
During the Roman Empire, the procurator acted as the emperor's financial agent.
The procurator was responsible for gathering taxes in the province.
