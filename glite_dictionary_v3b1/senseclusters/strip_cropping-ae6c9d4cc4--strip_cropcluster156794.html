strip cropping

noun
growing crops in strips to prevent erosion
Pronunciation: / strɪp ˈkrɑpɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪp ˈkrɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of growing crops in narrow strips along the contours of the land to reduce soil erosion

Examples

Strip cropping method
By alternating strips of corn and soybeans, strip cropping reduces the chances of soil loss during heavy rains.
Many farmers adopt strip cropping to help preserve their topsoil and protect the environment.
The agricultural extension agent recommended strip cropping to prevent erosion on sloped fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contour plowing - plowing along the contours of the land to reduce erosion
