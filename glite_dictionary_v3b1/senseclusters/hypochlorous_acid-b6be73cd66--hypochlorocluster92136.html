hypochlorous acid

noun
antimicrobial oxidant produced by white blood cells
5.5
Pronunciation: / \u02ccha\u026apo\u028a\u02c8kl\u0254r\u0259s \u02c8\u00e6s\u026ad / (AmE), / \u02cch\u028c\u026ap\u0259\u02c8kl\u0254\u02d0r\u0259s \u02c8asid / (BrE)
(in biology and medicine) an antimicrobial oxidizing agent (HOCl) produced by neutrophils and other phagocytes during the immune response to kill or inactivate bacteria and other microbes.

Examples

Hypochlorous acid kills bacteria
During inflammation, hypochlorous acid is produced by neutrophils to destroy invading bacteria.
Researchers found that low concentrations of hypochlorous acid help neutrophils kill pathogens effectively.
Topical formulations containing hypochlorous acid are used to reduce infection and promote wound healing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antimicrobial agent - a substance that kills or inhibits the growth of microorganisms
  • synonym: oxidant - a chemical that oxidizes other substances; HOCl acts as an oxidant in immune cells
