porosity

noun
ability to absorb fluids through pores
7.7 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôÀàr…ëÀês…ôti / (AmE), / p…ôÀàr…ís…ôti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being porous; the extent to which a material can absorb fluids by allowing them to pass through its pores.

Examples

Material porosity level
Due to its low porosity, the container keeps liquids from leaking out.
Geologists study the porosity of rocks to assess their potential as water reservoirs.
The porosity of the sponge allows it to soak up water quickly.

Synonyms

  • porousness
    7.0 0.05/m
    porousness describes how easily fluids or air pass through a material in everyday talk, while porosity is the measured technical property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permeability - the ability of a material to allow fluids to pass through it
