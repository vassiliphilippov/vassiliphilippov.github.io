greenfield

adjective
not previously developed
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌfiːld / (BrE)
describing land or a project that has not been previously developed or used

Examples

Greenfield site development
The company chose a greenfield site for its new headquarters to avoid demolition costs.
Developers often prefer greenfield projects because they offer more flexibility in design.
Building on greenfield land can impact local wildlife and natural habitats.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brownfield - previously developed land, often requiring cleanup
  • synonym: undeveloped - not built on or used for development
