silcrete

noun
silica-cemented rock
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlˌkriːt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlˌkriːt / (BrE)
a hard, silica-cemented rock formed by the accumulation of silica in soil or sediment

Examples

Hard silcrete rock
Archaeologists discovered ancient tools made from silcrete at the excavation site.
The durability of silcrete made it a valuable material for early human toolmakers.
In some regions, roads are built using crushed silcrete as a base layer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quartzite - a hard metamorphic rock consisting mainly of quartz
  • degree lower: sandstone - a sedimentary rock composed mainly of sand-sized minerals
