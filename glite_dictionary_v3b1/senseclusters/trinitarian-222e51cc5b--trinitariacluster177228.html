Trinitarian

adjective
about the Trinity
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɪnɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌtrɪnɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
relating to the Christian doctrine of the Trinity, which teaches that God exists as Father, Son, and Holy Spirit

Examples

Trinitarian doctrine
The church follows a strictly Trinitarian doctrine in its teachings.
Many Christian denominations hold Trinitarian beliefs about the nature of God.
A Trinitarian view is central to most mainstream Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Unitarian - relating to the belief that God is a single entity, not a Trinity
