keep out

verb
prevent entry; shut out
1.3 6.90/m
Pronunciation: / kip a ät / (AmE), / kiÀêp a ät / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from entering a place; to shut out

Examples

To keep out animals
Please keep out of the construction area for your safety.
Strict laws are used to keep out invasive species from the country.
The thick curtains kept out most of the noise from the street.

Synonyms

  • shut out
    3.8 3.89/m
    shut out stops someone from entering or being included, while keep out instructs or prevents entry in general.
  • lock out
    3.8 1.99/m
    lock out uses a lock or denies access to systems or places, while keep out is a general prevention of entry.
  • close off
    3.9 1.01/m
    close off blocks an area or passage with barriers, while keep out simply means preventing people or things from entering.
  • seal off
    3.9 1.16/m
    seal off closes an area tightly, often by officials, while keep out is a general warning to stop entry.
  • turn away
    2.8 4.22/m
    turn away refuses a person at the door or membership, while keep out means to stop entry or access more generally.
  • exclude
    3.0 34/m
    exclude deliberately leaves someone out of a group or idea, while keep out focuses on stopping physical entry or access.
