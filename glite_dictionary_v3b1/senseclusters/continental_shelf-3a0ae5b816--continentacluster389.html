continental shelf

noun
shallow seabed around a continent
7.3 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.tɪˈnen.t̬əl ʃɛlf / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnen.tl ʃɛlf / (BrE)
the area of seabed around a continent, typically up to about 200 meters deep, which is relatively shallow compared to the open ocean and extends outward from the coast before dropping steeply to the ocean floor

Examples

The continental shelf area
Fishing boats operate in the continental shelf because the waters are rich in marine life.
Oil reserves are often found on the continental shelf off the coast of various countries.
The continental shelf marks the boundary between the shallow sea and the deep ocean.
