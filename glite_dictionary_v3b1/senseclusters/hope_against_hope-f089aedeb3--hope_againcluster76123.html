hope against hope

verb
to persist in hope despite great odds
5.0
Pronunciation: / hoʊp əˈgɛnst hoʊp / (AmE), / həʊp əˈɡɛnst həʊp / (BrE)
to continue to hope even when it seems very unlikely that something will happen

Examples

To hope against hope for safety
Even after months without news, she continued to hope against hope for her brother's safe return.
He sat by the phone, hoping against hope that she would call him.
They hoped against hope that the missing hikers would be found alive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: despair - to lose all hope
  • synonym: hold out hope - to continue to believe that something might happen
