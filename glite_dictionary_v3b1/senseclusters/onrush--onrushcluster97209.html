onrush

noun
sudden and forceful attack
Pronunciation: / ˈɑnˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, powerful forward movement or attack

Examples

An onrush of people
The enemy was caught off guard by the sudden onrush of troops at dawn.
The general prepared his forces to withstand the onrush from the north.
Their lines crumbled under the onrush of artillery and infantry.
He was caught off guard by the onrush of memories triggered by the song.
The onrush of fans into the stadium was difficult to control.
The river's onrush after the storm swept away the old bridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assault - a violent attack
  • antonym: defense - the act of resisting an attack
  • synonym: rush - a sudden movement forward; surge
