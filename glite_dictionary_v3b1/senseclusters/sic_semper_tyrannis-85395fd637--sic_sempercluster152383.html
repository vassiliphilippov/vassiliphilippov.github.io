Sic semper tyrannis

proper noun
opposition to tyranny
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪk ˈsɛmpər tɪˈrænɪs / (AmE), / sɪk ˈsɛmpə tɪˈranɪs / (BrE)
a Latin phrase meaning 'thus always to tyrants', used to express opposition to tyranny or the downfall of tyrants

Examples

Sic semper tyrannis motto
The state seal of Virginia features the motto Sic semper tyrannis as a symbol of liberty.
After the revolution, the crowd shouted Sic semper tyrannis to celebrate the end of the dictator's rule.
The phrase Sic semper tyrannis is often used to express resistance against oppressive leaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: down with tyranny - a call to oppose or overthrow tyrannical rule
  • antonym: long live the king - a phrase expressing support for a monarch, opposite in sentiment
