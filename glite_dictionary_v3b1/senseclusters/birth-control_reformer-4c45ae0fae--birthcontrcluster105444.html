birth-control reformer

noun
advocate for birth control reform
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrθ kənˌtroʊl rɪˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθ kənˈtrəʊl rɪˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a social reformer who advocates for the use and acceptance of birth control and family planning.

Examples

Birth-control reformer advocates
As a birth-control reformer, she worked tirelessly to improve access to family planning information.
Many birth-control reformers faced strong opposition from religious groups during the early twentieth century.
Margaret Sanger is often recognized as a pioneering birth-control reformer in the United States.
