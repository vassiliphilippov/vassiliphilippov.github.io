factor III

noun
blood-clotting enzyme from platelets
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tɚ θri / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə θriː / (BrE)
an enzyme released from blood platelets that initiates the conversion of prothrombin to thrombin during blood clotting

Examples

Factor III enzyme
A deficiency in factor III can lead to problems with excessive bleeding.
During surgery, the presence of factor III is critical for proper blood clotting.
Researchers are studying how factor III interacts with other proteins in the clotting cascade.

Related Terms
