Sir Francis Bacon

proper noun
English philosopher and statesman (1561–1626)
Pronunciation: / sɚ ˈfrænsɪs ˈbeɪkən / (AmE), / sə ˈfrænsɪs ˈbeɪkən / (BrE)
(1561–1626) English statesman and philosopher, recognized as a precursor of British empiricism and noted for advocating inductive reasoning.

Examples

Sir Francis Bacon philosopher
Sir Francis Bacon was instrumental in developing the scientific method based on empirical observation.
Many historians view Sir Francis Bacon as a key figure in the transition to modern science.
The essays of Sir Francis Bacon continue to be widely studied in philosophy and literature courses.
