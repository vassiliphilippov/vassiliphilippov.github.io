iliopsoas

noun
hip flexor muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪlioʊˈsoʊəs / (AmE), / ˌɪliəʊˈsəʊəs / (BrE)
a large muscle in the pelvis that flexes the hip joint and helps lift the thigh toward the body

Examples

The iliopsoas muscle
The iliopsoas plays a crucial role in walking and running by lifting the leg.
Physical therapists often target the iliopsoas when treating lower back pain.
Tightness in the iliopsoas can limit hip mobility and cause discomfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: psoas - a muscle running from the lower spine to the femur, part of the iliopsoas group
  • antonym: quadriceps - a large muscle group at the front of the thigh
