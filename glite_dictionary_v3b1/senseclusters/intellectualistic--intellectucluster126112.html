intellectualistic

adjective
overly theoretical
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˌlɛktʃuəˈlɪstɪk /; / ɪnˌtɛlɪktʃuəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪntəlɛk.tʃuˈæl.ɪ.stɪk /; / ɪnˌtɛlɪktjʊəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
focused too much on intellectual or theoretical ideas, often ignoring practical or emotional aspects

Examples

Too intellectualistic ideas
Some critics found the philosopher's arguments too intellectualistic and disconnected from real-world issues.
The discussion became intellectualistic, focusing on abstract theories rather than practical solutions.
Her intellectualistic approach made it difficult for her to relate to her colleagues' everyday concerns.
Her intellectualistic approach to art made her work difficult for some people to understand.
The discussion became overly intellectualistic, losing touch with real-world concerns.
Critics argued that the policy was too intellectualistic and ignored practical solutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intellectual - relating to the intellect or mental capacity
  • antonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically
  • synonym: theoretical - concerned with ideas and principles rather than practical application
