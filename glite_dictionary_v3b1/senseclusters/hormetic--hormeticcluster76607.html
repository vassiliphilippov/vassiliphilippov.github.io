hormetic

adjective
beneficial at low dose
9.9
Pronunciation: /hɔːr.ˈmet.ɪk/ /; / /hɔːr.ˈmiː.tɪk/ (AmE), /hɔː.ˈmet.ɪk/ /; / /hɔː.ˈmiː.tɪk/ (BrE)
describing a beneficial effect produced by a low dose of something that is harmful at higher doses

Examples

A hormetic effect
Some scientists believe that a hormetic response explains why small amounts of stress can be good for health.
The hormetic effect of certain chemicals means they are only beneficial in tiny quantities.
Research suggests that fasting may have hormetic benefits by triggering protective processes in the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: toxic - harmful or poisonous, especially at higher doses
