break

verb
voice becoming lower and uneven at puberty
1.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /breɪk/ (AmE), /breɪk/ (BrE)
to change suddenly in tone or pitch (of a voice), especially during puberty or strong emotion

Examples

His voice breaks
Don't be embarrassed if your voice breaks while you're speaking; it's a normal part of growing up.

When his voice broke, he was told to stop singing in the children's choir.
His voice broke as he tried not to cry during the speech.
She started singing, but her voice suddenly broke on the high notes.
When he mentioned his late father, his voice broke with emotion.
