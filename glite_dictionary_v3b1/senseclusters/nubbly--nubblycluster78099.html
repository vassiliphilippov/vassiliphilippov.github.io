nubbly

adjective
having a rough, knobby textile surface
8.0 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆnÊŒbli / (AmE), / ËˆnÊŒbli / (BrE)
(of textiles) having a rough or uneven surface, often with small lumps or knots.

Examples

nubbly fabric texture
He admired the nubbly homespun fabric used in the handmade cushions.
She wore a nubbly scarf that felt warm against her neck.
The nubbly texture of the rug gave the room a cozy feel.

Synonyms

  • nubby
    7.5 0.23/m
    nubby is a fabric texture with tiny tufts and visible knots, while nubbly refers to a similarly rough, slightly lumpy textile surface.
  • nobby
    8.4 0.00/m
    nobby has noticeable knobs or rounded bumps on a surface, while nubbly describes smaller, softer lumps in textiles.
  • knobby
    5.8 0.29/m
    knobby is marked by firm rounded protuberances often on wood or bones, while nubbly is softer and used mainly for fabrics.
  • pebbled
    4.6 0.12/m
    pebbled has a regular pattern of small, rounded bumps like tiny stones, while nubbly suggests irregular, softer textile lumps.
