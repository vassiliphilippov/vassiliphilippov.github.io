sweetbriar

noun
wild rose with fragrant leaves and pink flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈswitˌbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈswiːtbraɪə / (BrE)
A Eurasian wild rose with prickly stems, fragrant leaves, bright pink flowers, and scarlet hips, often grown as an ornamental shrub.

Examples

A sweetbriar bush
A sweetbriar bush grew along the old stone wall, filling the air with a delicate scent.
She picked a few sweetbriar hips to make a fragrant herbal tea.
The garden was famous for its ancient sweetbriars that bloomed each spring.
