tangible

noun
physical object
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtændʒəbəl / (BrE)
a physical object that can be touched or seen, as opposed to something abstract or intangible

Examples

A valuable tangible
The museum displayed several rare tangibles from ancient civilizations.
Investors often prefer tangibles like real estate or gold over stocks.
She packed all her important tangibles before moving to a new city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intangible - something that cannot be touched or physically measured
  • synonym: item - a single thing, especially one of a list or collection
  • synonym: object - a material thing that can be seen and touched
