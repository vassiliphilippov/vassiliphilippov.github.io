multipolar

adjective
several centers of power
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
having or involving several centers of power, influence, or activity

Examples

Multipolar world order
The international system is becoming increasingly multipolar as new economic powers emerge.
A multipolar world can lead to more balanced global decision-making.
Historians debate whether the 21st century will remain multipolar or shift toward a new order.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bipolar - having two centers of power or influence
  • antonym: unipolar - having a single center of power or influence
