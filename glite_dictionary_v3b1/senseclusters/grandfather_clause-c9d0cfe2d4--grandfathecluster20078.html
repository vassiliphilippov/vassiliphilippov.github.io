grandfather clause

noun
legal exemption for pre-existing situations
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræn(d)ˌfɑðɚ klɔz / (AmE), / ˈɡræn(d)ˌfɑːðə klɑːz / (BrE)
a provision in a law or regulation that exempts certain people or things from the requirements of the law based on pre-existing circumstances, especially one allowing old rules to apply to existing situations when new rules are introduced

Examples

A grandfather clause in law
His eligibility was preserved by a grandfather clause despite the change in company policy.
Many older buildings are not required to install new elevators because of a grandfather clause in the regulation.
The city council included a grandfather clause so that current vendors could keep their licenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exemption - the process of being free from an obligation or liability imposed on others
