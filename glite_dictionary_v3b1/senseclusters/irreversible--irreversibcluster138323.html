irreversible

adjective
not able to be reversed or undone
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈvɜrsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈvɜːsəbl / (BrE)
not able to be undone or changed back to a previous state or condition.

Examples

Irreversible damage
After the surgery, he suffered irreversible nerve damage in his left arm.
The company's decision to close all stores is irreversible and will affect hundreds of employees.
The damage to the environment may be irreversible if immediate action is not taken.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reversible - able to be reversed or changed back
