Kendall test

noun
nonparametric correlation test
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛn.dəl tɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɛn.dl̩ tɛst / (BrE)
a statistical test using nonparametric measures of correlation, applied when assumptions for standard correlation analysis are not met

Examples

Perform a Kendall test
Before drawing conclusions, the analyst performed a Kendall test on the collected samples.
Researchers applied the Kendall test to evaluate the association between the two rankings.
The Kendall test is useful when data do not meet the requirements for a Pearson correlation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Spearman rank correlation - a nonparametric test measuring the strength and direction of association between two ranked variables
