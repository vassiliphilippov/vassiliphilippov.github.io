fat

adjective
lucrative or well-paid (especially a job or contract)
Pronunciation: / fæt / (AmE), / fæt / (BrE)
(of a sum of money, contract, or job) large and lucrative; offering considerable financial reward.

Examples

Fat bonus
He's hoping for a fat bonus at the end of the year after exceeding his targets.
It was a fat job offer, promising both a high salary and excellent benefits.
She landed a fat contract with the new client that doubled her earnings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lucrative - producing a great deal of profit
  • antonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality; not generous or sufficient
