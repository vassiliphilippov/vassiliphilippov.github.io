coturnix

noun
Old World quail genus
Pronunciation: / koʊˈtɜrnɪks / (AmE), / kəˈtɜːnɪks / (BrE)
A member of the genus of Old World quails, commonly found in Europe, Asia, and Africa.

Examples

A coturnix quail
A flock of coturnix was spotted in the grasslands near the river.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the coturnix across southern Europe.
The common coturnix is widely farmed for its eggs and meat.
