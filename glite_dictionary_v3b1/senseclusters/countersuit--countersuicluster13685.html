countersuit

noun
a lawsuit filed in response to another
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌsuːt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsuːt / (BrE)
A lawsuit filed in response to another lawsuit brought by an opposing party.

Examples

A countersuit filed
After being sued for breach of contract, the defendant responded with a countersuit.
Both parties are now embroiled in lawsuits and a lengthy countersuit.
The company filed a countersuit claiming that the original accusation was baseless.
