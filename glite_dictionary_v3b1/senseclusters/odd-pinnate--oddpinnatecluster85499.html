odd-pinnate

adjective
pinnate leaf with one terminal leaflet
Pronunciation: / ɑdˈpɪneɪt / (AmE), / ɒdˈpɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) pinnate with a single leaflet at the tip, resulting in an odd total number of leaflets.

Examples

Odd-pinnate leaf
Many legumes have odd-pinnate compounds leaves on their stems.
The rowan tree displays odd-pinnate leaves, each ending with a single leaflet at the tip.
To identify an odd-pinnate leaf, count the leaflets and look for a lone terminal one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: even-pinnate - (of a leaf) pinnate with a pair of leaflets at the tip, not a single terminal leaflet
