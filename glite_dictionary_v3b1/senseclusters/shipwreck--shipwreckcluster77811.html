shipwreck

verb
to suffer or cause a shipwreck
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪpˌrɛk / (AmE), / ˈʃɪprɛk /; / ˈʃɪp.rɛk / (BrE)
to be destroyed, lost, or stranded at sea, or to cause a ship to be destroyed or stranded

Examples

To shipwreck at sea
After the boat capsized, the family was shipwrecked and stranded for several days.
Many sailors have been shipwrecked on this treacherous coastline over the centuries.
They feared they would shipwreck before reaching the island in the storm.
A reef off the coast has shipwrecked several vessels over the years.
After the fierce storm, the crew realized their boat would soon be shipwrecked.
Many explorers have been shipwrecked while trying to find new lands.
