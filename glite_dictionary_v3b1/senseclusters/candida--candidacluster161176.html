candida

noun
genus of yeastlike fungi; can cause infection
Pronunciation: / ˈkændɪdə / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdə / (BrE)
a genus of yeastlike fungi, some species of which can cause infections in humans, especially in moist areas of the body

Examples

Candida infection
Oral thrush is usually a result of candida overgrowth in the mouth.
Some antifungal medications specifically target candida species in the body.
The doctor explained that the infection was caused by candida, a common type of yeast.
