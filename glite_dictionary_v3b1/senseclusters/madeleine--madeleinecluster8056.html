madeleine

noun
memory trigger
Pronunciation: / ˈmædələn / (AmE), / ˈmadlɪn / (BrE)
something, especially a sensory cue, that unexpectedly brings back a vivid memory of the past

Examples

A madeleine moment
The old song was a madeleine that instantly took him back to his childhood.
For her, the scent of jasmine served as a madeleine, recalling warm evenings in her hometown.
Tasting the soup became a madeleine, filling him with memories of family dinners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reminder - something that helps you remember something
  • synonym: trigger - something that causes a reaction or memory
