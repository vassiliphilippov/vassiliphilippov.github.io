circus

noun
chaotic, noisy situation
4.4 4.74/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…úÀêr.k…ôs / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀê.k…ôs / (BrE)
A situation characterized by chaos, disorganization, or frenetic activity, often with an element of comedy or spectacle.

Examples

Office circus chaos
After the announcement, the office was a complete circus with people running everywhere.
The press conference turned into a circus when everyone started shouting at once.
What was meant to be a simple handover became a circus of confusion and laughter.

Synonyms

  • three-ring circus
    5.4 0.04/m
    three-ring circus has many things happening at once and feels hectic, while circus means chaotic spectacle with noisy disorganization.
  • clown show
    7.0 0.04/m
    clown show is a farcical display that suggests poor management or mockery, while circus is broader chaotic spectacle with fun.
  • carnival
    3.2 1.20/m
    carnival is wild and noisy with a playful festival feel, while circus implies chaotic, disorganized spectacle often with performers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carnival - lively and noisy festival or event
  • synonym: commotion - noisy, confused disturbance
  • antonym: order - state of neatness or organization
