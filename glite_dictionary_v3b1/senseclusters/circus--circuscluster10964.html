circus

noun
chaotic, noisy situation
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜːr.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəs / (BrE)
A situation characterized by chaos, disorganization, or frenetic activity, often with an element of comedy or spectacle.

Examples

Office circus chaos
After the announcement, the office was a complete circus with people running everywhere.
The press conference turned into a circus when everyone started shouting at once.
What was meant to be a simple handover became a circus of confusion and laughter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carnival - lively and noisy festival or event
  • synonym: commotion - noisy, confused disturbance
  • antonym: order - state of neatness or organization
