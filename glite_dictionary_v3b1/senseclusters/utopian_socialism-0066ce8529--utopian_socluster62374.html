utopian socialism

noun
idealistic form of socialism advocating voluntary cooperative communities
Pronunciation: / juˈtoʊpiən ˈsoʊʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / juːˈtəʊpɪən ˈsəʊʃəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a form of socialism based on the belief that social and economic equality can be achieved through voluntary cooperation and the creation of ideal communities, rather than through political revolution

Examples

Utopian socialism ideals
Some 19th-century reformers advocated utopian socialism as a peaceful alternative to revolution.
The principles of utopian socialism inspired several experimental communities.
Unlike Marxist theories, utopian socialism focused on ideal societies rather than class struggle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: scientific socialism - the Marxist theory of socialism based on scientific analysis of historical development
