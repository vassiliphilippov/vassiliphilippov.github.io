comparatist

noun
scholar of comparisons
8.5
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpærətɪst / (AmE), / kəmˈpærətɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies and compares literature, languages, or cultures, often across different countries or periods

Examples

A comparatist scholar
The comparatist delivered a lecture on similarities between Eastern and Western poetry.
As a comparatist, she often travels to study different cultures and their literature.
Many universities offer programs for aspiring comparatists interested in global literary studies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comparative literature - the study of literature across cultures and languages
  • synonym: linguist - a person who studies languages
