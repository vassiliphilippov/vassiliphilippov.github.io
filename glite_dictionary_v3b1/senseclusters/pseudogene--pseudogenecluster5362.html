pseudogene

noun
nonfunctional gene sequence
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.doʊ.dʒiːn/ /; / /ˈsjuː.doʊ.dʒiːn/ (AmE), /ˈsjuː.dəʊ.dʒiːn/ /; / /ˈsuː.dəʊ.dʒiːn/ (BrE)
a section of DNA that resembles a gene but does not produce a functional product

Examples

A pseudogene section
Researchers discovered a pseudogene that may have once played a role in immune response.
The presence of a pseudogene can help scientists trace the evolutionary history of a species.
Unlike a normal gene, a pseudogene does not produce a functional protein.
