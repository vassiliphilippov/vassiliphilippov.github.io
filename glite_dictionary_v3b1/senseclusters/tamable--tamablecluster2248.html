tamable

adjective
able to be tamed
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪ.mə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.mə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being tamed or trained to be less wild or more obedient

Examples

A tamable animal
Researchers hope the new breed will be more tamable than its aggressive ancestors.
Some wild animals are not tamable, no matter how much you train them.
The horses on the island were surprisingly tamable after just a few weeks of gentle handling.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: tame - to domesticate or make less wild
  • antonym: untamable - not capable of being tamed
