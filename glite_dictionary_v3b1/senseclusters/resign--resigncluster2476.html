resign

verb
voluntarily leave a position or job
1.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪn / (BrE)
to officially or formally leave a job, position, or office by giving notice

Examples

To resign from work
After the allegations came to light, the minister decided to resign from his post.
If you are unhappy in your job, you always have the option to resign.
The CEO resigned following the company's disappointing annual results.
After much consideration, she decided to resign from her position at the company.
He officially resigned last week and sent a letter to the entire department.
The manager will resign if the board does not accept his proposal.
After months of controversy, the minister resigned from office yesterday.
She resigned her position as CEO to spend more time with her family.
The coach resigned following the team's disappointing season.

  • synonym: retire - to leave one's job, especially when reaching an age or after long service
