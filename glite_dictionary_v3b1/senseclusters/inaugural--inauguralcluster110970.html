inaugural

adjective
marking a beginning; first in a series
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɔɡjərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjʊərəl / (BrE)
marking or relating to the beginning of something, especially an institution, activity, or official event

Examples

Inaugural event
The company held an inaugural ceremony to launch its latest product line.
The new president delivered his inaugural address to a large audience.
They published the inaugural issue of the science journal last month.
Guests dressed in formal attire for the inaugural ball at the city hall.
Her inaugural lecture as professor of physics attracted students from across the university.
The president delivered his inaugural speech to a packed audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceremonial - relating to or used for formal events or ceremonies
  • antonym: final - coming at the end; last
  • synonym: first - coming before all others in time or order
  • synonym: initial - existing or occurring at the beginning
