Synonyms

  • passionate
    2.8 20/m
    passionate feels deep, often romantic or emotional warmth, while ardent means showing strong enthusiasm or intense feeling.
  • fervent
    6.1 2.25/m
    fervent shows strong, sincere feeling used in formal speech, while ardent is broader and used in many contexts.
  • fervid
    8.4 0.18/m
    fervid is intense and flame-like in tone and often literary, while ardent is common everyday word for strong feeling.
  • impassioned
    5.8 2.10/m
    impassioned describes emotional speech or writing that strongly convinces, while ardent covers strong enthusiasm or deep feeling more generally.
  • zealous
    6.8 1.90/m
    zealous is active and eager in pursuing a cause or goal, while ardent stresses strong feeling or passion.
  • enthusiastic
    2.7 13/m
    enthusiastic shows keen interest and lively excitement about something, while ardent suggests deeper, more lasting passion or intensity.
  • avid
    3.9 5.44/m
    avid means eager and keen about an activity or hobby, while ardent means having strong emotional passion rather than just interest.
  • fiery
    3.5 4.49/m
    fiery is quick to show strong emotion or temper, while ardent is steady, warm passion rather than sudden anger.
  • vehement
    7.7 1.07/m
    vehement is forceful, often angry or opposing, while ardent is warm, passionate, and less about force or conflict.
  • perfervid
    9.9 0.04/m
    perfervid is a very rare, highly intense literary word, while ardent is normal modern English for strong feeling.
