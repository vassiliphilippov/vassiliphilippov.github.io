semidarkness

noun
partial darkness; dimness
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈdɑrknəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈdɑːknɪs / (BrE)
A state or condition where there is only partial darkness; dimly lit.

Examples

The semidarkness of evening
As evening fell, the forest was shrouded in a mysterious semidarkness.
He waited in semidarkness for the lights to come back on.
The semidarkness of the old hallway made it difficult to see the pictures on the walls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brightness - the quality or state of giving out or reflecting light
  • degree higher: darkness - the state of being without light or with very little light
  • synonym: dimness - a state of only limited or reduced light
