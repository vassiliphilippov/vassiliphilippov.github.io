unworld

verb
to remove from world
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɜrld / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːld / (BrE)
to remove someone or something from worldly concerns or deprive of worldly things; now archaic

Examples

To unworld oneself
The monk tried to unworld himself by giving up all his possessions.
In ancient times, some people would unworld themselves to seek spiritual enlightenment.
She wished to unworld her mind from the distractions of daily life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: involve - to include or engage in worldly matters
  • synonym: isolate - to separate from others or from society
