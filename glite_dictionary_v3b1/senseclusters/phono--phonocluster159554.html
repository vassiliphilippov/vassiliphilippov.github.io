phono

noun
loudness unit; record player
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəʊ / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the perceived loudness of sounds, or informally, a phonograph or record player

Examples

Phono unit measurement
The technician measured the sound in phono units to assess its loudness.
He set up his old phono to play classic vinyl records in the living room.
A reading of 40 phono indicates a moderate level of perceived loudness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: silence - the absence of sound
  • synonym: sone - a unit of perceived loudness based on subjective hearing
