PPR

noun
viral disease in goats
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / pi pi É‘r / (AmE), / piË piË É‘Ë / (BrE)
a highly contagious viral disease affecting small ruminants, such as sheep and goats, causing fever, sores, and often high mortality

Examples

PPR virus in goats
The veterinarian confirmed that the goats were suffering from PPR after several showed signs of fever and mouth sores.
Farmers are advised to vaccinate their herds to prevent the spread of PPR among sheep and goats.
An outbreak of PPR can cause significant losses in regions where small ruminants are a major source of income.
