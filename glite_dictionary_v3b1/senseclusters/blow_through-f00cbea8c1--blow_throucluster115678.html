blow through

verb
(of wind) move through something
1.0
Pronunciation: / bloʊ θruː / (AmE), / bləʊ θruː / (BrE)
(of wind or air) to move or pass quickly through a space or opening.

Examples

Wind blows through windows
A cold draft blows through the old house every night in winter.
Fresh air blows through the hall whenever someone opens the door.
She shivered as the icy wind blew through the cracked window frame.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blow in - (of wind) to move into a place with force
