monometallism

noun
use of one metal as currency standard
7.2 9.6
Pronunciation: /mə.nə.mɪ.ˈtæl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /moʊ.noʊ.mɪ.ˈtæl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /mɒn.ə.mɪ.ˈtæl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /məʊ.nə.mɪ.ˈtæl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The use of a single metal, such as gold or silver, as the standard for a country's currency or monetary value.

Examples

The monometallism system
Economists debated the merits of monometallism versus bimetallism in the nineteenth century.
Supporters of monometallism argued that a single-metal standard would bring greater monetary stability.
The country adopted monometallism, using only gold as its currency standard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bimetallism - the use of two metals, usually gold and silver, as currency standards
