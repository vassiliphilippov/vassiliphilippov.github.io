tract

noun
bundle of nerve fibers in the brain
Pronunciation: / trækt / (AmE), / trakt / (BrE)
a bundle of nerve fibers in the brain or spinal cord that follow a specific path and function together

Examples

Nerve tract
Damage to the spinal tract can result in loss of sensation.
Researchers studied the role of the corticospinal tract in movement control.
The optic tract carries visual information from the eyes to the brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nerve - a bundle of fibers transmitting impulses in the body
  • synonym: pathway - a route of nerve fibers transmitting signals
