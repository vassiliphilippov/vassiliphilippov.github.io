18-karat gold

noun
gold alloy with 75% gold
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtiːn ˈkærət ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˌeɪtiːn ˈkærət ɡəʊld / (BrE)
An alloy that contains 75 percent pure gold, commonly used in jewelry.

Examples

18-karat gold jewelry
Many wedding rings are crafted from 18-karat gold for a balance of strength and purity.
The jeweler confirmed that the bracelet was solid 18-karat gold.
This necklace is made of 18-karat gold, giving it a warm, rich color.
