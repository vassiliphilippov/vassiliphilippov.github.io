censorship

noun
suppression of information or content
3.4 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.sər.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ˈsen.sə.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
the practice or system of removing, suppressing, or prohibiting parts of books, films, news, or other information that are considered objectionable, harmful, or sensitive

Examples

Internet censorship
Debates around internet censorship have intensified due to concerns over free speech.
During wartime, government censorship was used to prevent sensitive information from reaching the enemy.
The book was banned by censorship authorities because of its controversial ideas.
Government censorship of the media was a major issue in the election.
Many artists protest against censorship that limits creative freedom.
The novel faced heavy censorship before it could be published in some countries.
