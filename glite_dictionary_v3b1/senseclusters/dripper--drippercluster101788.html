dripper

noun
source of drips
3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdrɪp.ər/ (AmE), /ˈdrɪp.ə/ (BrE)
an object or device from which liquid falls in drops, often unintentionally or as a result of leakage

Examples

Leaky dripper
They placed a bucket beneath the dripper to catch the steady drops from the broken gutter.
A slow dripper in the ceiling left a small puddle on the floor overnight.
The mechanic fixed the pipe to stop it from becoming a constant dripper in the workshop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leak - an unintended hole or crack through which liquid escapes
  • antonym: spout - a tube or lip for pouring liquid
