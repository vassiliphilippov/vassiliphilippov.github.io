sterilizer

noun
device for sterilizing by heating
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛr.əˌlaɪ.zɚ /; / ˈstɛr.əˌlaɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈstɛr.ɪ.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
a device that uses heat or chemicals to destroy microorganisms on equipment or substances, commonly used in medical or laboratory settings, for example to clean surgical instruments

Examples

Medical sterilizer device
After every procedure, the dentist puts his instruments in the sterilizer.
The hospital purchased a new sterilizer to ensure all surgical tools are free of bacteria.
You must run the equipment through the sterilizer before using it on patients.
Laboratory staff regularly service the steriliser to ensure proper sterilization of equipment.
The surgical instruments were placed in the steriliser before the operation.
When the steriliser malfunctioned, the whole batch of tools had to be reprocessed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autoclave - a strong heated container used for chemical reactions and sterilization
