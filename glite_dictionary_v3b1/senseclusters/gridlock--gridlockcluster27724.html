gridlock

noun
political stalemate
5.5 3.03/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8\u0261r\u026ad\u02ccl\u0251k / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0261r\u026adl\u0252k / (BrE)
A situation in politics or government in which opposing parties or factions prevent decisions or legislation from being passed, causing a stalemate.

Examples

Political gridlock
After weeks of negotiation, the parliament fell into gridlock, delaying the budget and public services.
Voters frustrated by gridlock blamed both parties for failing to pass meaningful reforms.
The mayor warned that federal-state gridlock could prevent essential disaster-relief funds from reaching towns.

Synonyms

  • deadlock
    5.8 1.26/m
    deadlock is a situation where two sides cannot move, while gridlock is a political stalemate blocking government decisions.
  • impasse
    8.1 2.56/m
    impasse is a breakdown in talks where no agreement is reached, while gridlock is a political stalemate that blocks laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deadlock - a situation in which opposing parties cannot reach agreement, stopping progress
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation in which no progress can be made or no advancement is possible
