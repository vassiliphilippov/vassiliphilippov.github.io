tailwater

noun
water below a dam
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪlˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
the water or section of a river immediately downstream from a dam or other hydraulic structure

Examples

Cold tailwater below dam
The cold tailwater below the dam provides an excellent habitat for trout.
Engineers measured the flow rate of the tailwater to monitor the dam's performance.
After heavy rain, the level of the tailwater rose quickly, affecting nearby fishing spots.
The tailwater from the hydroelectric plant was carefully measured to assess its temperature.
Fish populations often thrive in the tailwater areas below large dams.
Environmental scientists studied the tailwater to monitor changes in water quality downstream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: headwater - the source or upper part of a river or stream
  • synonym: outflow - water flowing out of a place
  • synonym: reservoir - an artificial lake used to store water, often formed by a dam
  • antonym: upstream - in the direction opposite to the flow of a river; before a dam
