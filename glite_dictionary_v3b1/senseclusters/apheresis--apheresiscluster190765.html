apheresis

noun
omission of sounds at word's start
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /ə.fə.ˈriː.sɪs/ /; / /æ.fə.ˈriː.sɪs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈfɪə.rɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
(linguistics) The omission of one or more sounds or letters from the beginning of a word, as in 'coon' from 'raccoon' or 'till' from 'until'.

Examples

Apheresis in linguistics
Examples of apheresis include 'special' shortened to ''special' in speech.
Linguists use the term apheresis to describe the loss of sounds at the beginning of words.
The word 'squire' originated through apheresis from 'esquire'.

Linguists study aphaeresis to understand historical changes in word forms.
The contraction of 'until' to till is an example of aphaeresis.
