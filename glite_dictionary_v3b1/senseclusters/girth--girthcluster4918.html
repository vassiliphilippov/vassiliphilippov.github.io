girth

noun
strap holding a horse's saddle
Pronunciation: / ɡɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɜːθ / (BrE)
a strap that passes under a horse's belly, fastening a saddle or pack in place

Examples

Horse girth
Before the ride, she checked that the girth was tight enough to keep the saddle secure.
It's important to clean the girth regularly to prevent chafing on the horse's skin.
The leather girth broke during the competition, causing the saddle to slip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cinch - a strap used for the same purpose, especially in Western riding
