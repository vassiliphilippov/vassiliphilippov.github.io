decorticate

verb
remove the outer layer
8.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkɔrtɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkɔːtɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to remove the outer layer or covering from something, especially bark from a tree or outer tissue from an organ

Examples

To decorticate a branch
Foresters often decorticate branches before using them in craftwork.
In surgery, doctors can decorticate an organ to remove diseased tissue.
To make the wood usable, craftsmen must first decorticate the logs.
