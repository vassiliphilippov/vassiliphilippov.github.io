arpeggio

noun
musical chord played in succession
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈpɛdʒioʊ / (AmE), / ɑːˈpɛdʒiəʊ / (BrE)
A chord whose notes are played in rapid succession rather than at the same time.

Examples

A arpeggio chord
The guitar solo featured a rapid arpeggio across the strings.
The pianist played a sweeping arpeggio to open the piece.
You should practice arpeggios daily to improve your finger strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chord - A group of (typically three or more) musical notes sounded together
