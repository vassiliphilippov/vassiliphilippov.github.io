triple entendre

noun
phrase with three meanings
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪpəl ɑnˈtɑndrə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpəl ɒnˈtɒndrə / (BrE)
a phrase or expression that has three distinct meanings or interpretations, often deliberately used for effect in literature, jokes, or wordplay

Examples

A triple entendre phrase
Critics praised the novel for its clever use of triple entendre throughout the dialogue.
Her song uses a subtle triple entendre to comment on politics, romance, and freedom all at once.
The comedian's joke was a masterful triple entendre that left the audience laughing and thinking.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: double entendre - phrase with two meanings, often with one risqué meaning
