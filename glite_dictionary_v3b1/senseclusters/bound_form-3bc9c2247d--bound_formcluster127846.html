bound form

noun
morpheme that cannot stand alone
9.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ba änd f…îrm / (AmE), / ba änd f…îÀêm / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A morpheme that appears only as part of a larger word or construction and cannot stand alone, such as the '-s' in 'cats'.

Examples

A bound form morpheme
In English, plural '-s' is considered a bound form attached to many nouns.
Linguists distinguish a bound form from a word that can occur independently.
The '-ed' in 'walked' is a bound form indicating past tense.

Synonyms

  • bound morpheme
    8.6 0.00/m
    bound morpheme is the usual linguistic term for a morpheme that cannot stand alone, while bound form is an alternative label.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bound morpheme - a morpheme that cannot occur independently and must attach to another morpheme
  • antonym: free form - a morpheme that can stand alone as a word
