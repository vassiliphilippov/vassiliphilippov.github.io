nitrobacteria

noun
soil bacteria converting nitrites to nitrates
Pronunciation: / naɪtroʊbækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / naɪtrəʊbækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Bacteria found in soil that convert nitrites into nitrates as part of the nitrogen cycle.

Examples

Nitrobacteria in soil
Nitrobacteria play a key role in making soil nutrients available to plants.
The presence of nitrobacteria accelerates the conversion of nitrites to nitrates in agricultural fields.
Without nitrobacteria, the nitrogen cycle in the ecosystem would be incomplete.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denitrifying bacteria - bacteria that convert nitrates into nitrogen gas
  • synonym: nitrifying bacteria - bacteria that convert ammonia into nitrites or nitrates
