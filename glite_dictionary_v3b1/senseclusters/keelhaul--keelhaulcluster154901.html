keelhaul

noun
severe punishment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːlˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈkiːlˌhɔːl / (BrE)
a severe punishment or harsh reprimand, originally referring to dragging someone under a ship's keel as punishment.

Examples

A public keelhaul
After the error, the manager gave him a public keelhaul in front of the entire team.
The politician faced a media keelhaul for his controversial remarks.
In the past, sailors feared the brutal keelhaul as the worst form of discipline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: punishment - the act of penalizing someone for wrongdoing
  • synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval or criticism
