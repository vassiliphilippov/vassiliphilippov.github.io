photoelectric cell

noun
device detecting/measuring light with electricity
4.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒfoÊŠtoÊŠÉªËˆlÉ›ktrÉªk sÉ›l / (AmE), / ËŒfÉ™ÊŠtÉ™ÊŠÉªËˆlÉ›ktrÉªk sÉ›l / (BrE)
A device that detects and measures light or other electromagnetic radiation by converting it into electrical energy.

Examples

A photoelectric cell device
A photoelectric cell is often found in automatic lighting systems that respond to changes in daylight.
Scientists measured the intensity of the laser beam with a sensitive photoelectric cell.
The security system uses a photoelectric cell to detect any movement in the hallway.

Synonyms

  • photocell
    6.2 0.15/m
    photocell is the everyday name for sensors that turn light into an electrical signal, while photoelectric cell is the formal technical name.
  • photodetector
    7.2 0.19/m
    photodetector senses light and gives an electronic signal for instruments, while photoelectric cell refers to the device that converts light into electricity.
  • electric eye
    5.3 0.03/m
    electric eye detects light for automatic control or security, while photoelectric cell names the sensor that converts light into electrical energy.
