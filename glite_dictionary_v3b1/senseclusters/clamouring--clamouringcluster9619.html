A loud and persistent outcry or demand from many people, often in protest or excitement.

Examples

The clamouring crowd
Amidst the clamouring for change, the government was forced to respond.
The clamouring of the protesters could be heard outside the gates all night.
The judge struggled to speak over the clamouring in the packed courtroom.

Synonyms

  • clamor
    6.0 1.85/m
    clamor is a loud, steady public outcry used to press for change, while clamouring describes that noisy, ongoing demand.
  • outcry
    4.6 3.42/m
    outcry is a strong, public shout of protest or anger, while clamouring suggests many people loudly demanding or calling.
  • hue and cry
    7.7 0.18/m
    hue and cry is an idiom for a loud public alarm or demand for action, while clamouring is general noisy protest.
