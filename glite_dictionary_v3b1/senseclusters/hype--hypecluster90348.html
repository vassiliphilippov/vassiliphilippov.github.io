hype

verb
promote in an exaggerated way
3.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /haɪp/ (AmE), /haɪp/ (BrE)
to promote or publicize something in an exaggerated or intensive way to attract attention

Examples

To hype a product
Critics accused the studio of hyping the movie far beyond its actual quality.
He hyped his skills during the interview, which made him seem more qualified than he really was.
The company decided to hype the new smartphone with flashy ads and celebrity endorsements.
The movie studio hyped the new film with flashy trailers and celebrity interviews.
Critics argued that the product was hyped far beyond its actual value.
She hyped her upcoming book on social media to generate excitement among readers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advertise - to make something known to the public, especially to sell it
  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
  • synonym: promote - encourage or support the publicity of something
