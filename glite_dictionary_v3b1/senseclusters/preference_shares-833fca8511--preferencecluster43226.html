preference shares

noun
shares with priority dividends, no voting rights
2.5 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpref.ər.əns ʃerz/ (AmE), /ˈpref.ə.rəns ʃeəz/ (BrE)
shares that entitle the holder to a fixed dividend, whose payment takes priority over ordinary shares, but usually without voting rights

Examples

Preference shares fixed dividend
Holders of preference shares receive dividends before ordinary shareholders in the event of profit distributions.
The company issued preference shares to raise capital without diluting control of the business.
Unlike ordinary shares, preference shares do not carry voting rights at annual general meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ordinary shares - shares that have voting rights and receive dividends after preference shares
