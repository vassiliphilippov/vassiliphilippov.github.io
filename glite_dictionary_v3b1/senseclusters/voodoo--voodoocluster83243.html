voodoo

noun
magical charm
Pronunciation: / ˈvuːduː / (AmE), / ˈvuːduː / (BrE)
A charm or object believed, especially superstitiously, to possess magical powers.

Examples

Voodoo charm
She carried a small voodoo in her pocket, hoping it would bring her good luck.
Some believed the mysterious illness was caused by a voodoo placed on the family.
The merchant sold handmade voodoo charms to curious tourists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amulet - an ornament or small piece of jewelry believed to protect against evil
  • synonym: talisman - an object thought to have magical powers and bring good luck
