omnivorism

noun
wide-ranging tastes
Pronunciation: / ɑmˈnɪvərɪzəm / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪvərɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency or practice of enjoying or engaging with a wide variety of cultural products, genres, or ideas

Examples

Cultural omnivorism
Her omnivorism in literature meant she read everything from classic novels to modern graphic novels.
The festival celebrated cultural omnivorism by featuring music, food, and art from around the world.
His omnivorism was evident in his willingness to try new experiences and ideas without hesitation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eclecticism - the practice of selecting from various sources or styles
  • antonym: specialization - the practice of focusing on a narrow range of interests
