philistinism

noun
materialistic attitude; disregard for culture or values
Pronunciation: / fɪˈlɪstɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪstɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
A focus on material wealth and possessions with little regard for cultural, ethical, or spiritual values.

Examples

Philistinism and money
He was accused of philistinism because he cared only about profits, not the arts or literature.
Her commitment to poetry was seen as a rejection of the prevailing philistinism of her peers.
Many critics regard the commercialization of art as a symptom of modern philistinism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cultured - showing refinement and good taste
  • synonym: materialism - the belief that physical possessions are more important than spiritual values
