logographer

noun
historical writer
9.9
Pronunciation: / loʊˈɡɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / ləʊˈɡɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who writes historical accounts or records important events, especially in ancient times

Examples

Ancient logographer
The ancient logographer provided valuable insights into the customs of early civilizations.
As a logographer, she dedicated her life to documenting the history of her people.
Many details about the war were preserved thanks to the diligent work of a single logographer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronicler - a person who records events in chronological order
  • synonym: historian - a person who studies or writes about history
