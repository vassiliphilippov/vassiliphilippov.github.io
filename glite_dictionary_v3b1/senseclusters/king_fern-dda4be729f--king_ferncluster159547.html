king fern

noun
large widely distributed fern with tufted fronds
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɪŋ fɜrn /; / kɪŋ fɝn / (AmE), / kɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
a large fern species with deep roots and upright, tufted fronds, found in rainforests in Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, and other regions

Examples

The king fern plant

The king fern can grow particularly large in rainforests, reaching impressive heights.
Botanists consider the king fern to be one of the oldest surviving plant species.
The king fern can grow fronds that reach up to five meters in length.
We admired a massive king fern while hiking through the New Zealand rainforest.
