superabundance

noun
an excessive amount; surplus
7.9 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåsuÀêp…ôr…ôÀàb ånd…ôns / (AmE), / ÀåsuÀêp…ôr…ôÀàb ånd…ôns / (BrE)
an extremely large or excessive quantity of something; more than is needed or appropriate

Examples

A superabundance of food
The garden produced a superabundance of tomatoes this year, far more than the family could eat.
The report criticized the superabundance of unnecessary details in the proposal.
There was a superabundance of decorations, making the room look cluttered rather than festive.

Synonyms

  • overabundance
    5.7 0.45/m
    overabundance is an almost identical term for a very large excess, while superabundance stresses extreme or overflowing amounts.
  • plethora
    7.7 3.30/m
    plethora suggests a much greater amount that cannot be managed, while superabundance simply means an extreme excess.
  • superfluity
    8.7 0.12/m
    superfluity is a formal word for something unnecessary and extra, while superabundance highlights sheer quantity over need.
  • excess
    3.3 13/m
    excess means more than allowed or wanted in general situations, while superabundance suggests a very large, often extreme, surplus.
  • surplusage
    9.9 0.06/m
    surplusage is an old or legal term for extra quantity, while superabundance is the common word for a huge, overwhelming excess.
