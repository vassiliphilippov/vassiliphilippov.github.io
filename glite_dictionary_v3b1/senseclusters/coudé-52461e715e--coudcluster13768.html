coudé

noun
bent telescope focus
9.9
Pronunciation: / kuːˈdeɪ / (AmE), / kuːˈdeɪ / (BrE)
an arrangement in a telescope where light is bent to a fixed point, allowing instruments to remain stationary during observation

Examples

Coudé focus telescope
The observatory upgraded its telescope to include a coudé focus for advanced spectrographic studies.
By using the coudé arrangement, astronomers could attach heavy instruments without moving the telescope.
The coudé allowed for more precise measurements by keeping the equipment stationary during long exposures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nasmyth focus - a telescope focus arrangement where light is directed to the side of the telescope
  • antonym: prime focus - the point where light is focused directly by the main mirror or lens of a telescope
