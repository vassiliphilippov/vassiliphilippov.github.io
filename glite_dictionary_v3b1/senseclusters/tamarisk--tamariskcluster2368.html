tamarisk

noun
shrub or small tree of the genus Tamarix
9.6 8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtæmərɪsk / (AmE), / ˈtamərɪsk / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Tamarix, typically with small scale-like or needle-shaped leaves and feathery clusters of small white or pink flowers, often found in coastal or salty soils.

Examples

A tamarisk tree
A row of tamarisk trees lined the edge of the dry, sandy plain.
People have planted tamarisks to help prevent erosion near rivers and lakes.
The tamarisk is well adapted to grow in salty coastal soils.
