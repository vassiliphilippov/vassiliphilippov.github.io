gray fox

noun
North American fox species with gray fur
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /greɪ fɑːks/ (AmE), /greɪ fɒks/ (BrE)
A medium-sized fox (Urocyon cinereoargenteus) native to North and Central America, including the southern United States, with gray or dark grey fur; known for its ability to climb trees.

Examples

A gray fox climbing
A gray fox was spotted climbing a tree in the national park.
The gray fox is found throughout the southern United States and into Central America.
Unlike the red fox, the gray fox prefers wooded areas and can climb trees to escape predators.
Researchers spotted a family of grey foxes near the edge of the forest.
The grey fox has a coat that helps it blend into rocky habitats.
The grey fox is known for its ability to climb trees unlike most other foxes.
