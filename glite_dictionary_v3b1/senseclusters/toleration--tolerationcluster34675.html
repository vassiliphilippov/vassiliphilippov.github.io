toleration

noun
willingness to allow different beliefs or practices
6.6 4.1
Pronunciation: /tɑːr.lə.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /tɒ.lə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or practice of allowing or accepting people, beliefs, or practices that are different from your own, either personally or officially

Examples

Religious toleration
Her toleration of different lifestyles set an example for everyone in the community.
Promoting toleration of diverse religious beliefs is essential in multicultural societies.
The school's policy encourages toleration rather than discrimination.
Debates about toleration shaped early modern European politics.
Religious toleration allowed people of different faiths to live together peacefully.
The principle of toleration was fundamental to the new constitution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intolerance - unwillingness to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own
  • synonym: tolerance - the ability or willingness to tolerate the existence of opinions or behavior that one dislikes or disagrees with
