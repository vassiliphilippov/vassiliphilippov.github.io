alkalemia

noun
elevated blood pH condition
9.9
Pronunciation: /æl.kə.ˈliː.miː.ə/ /; / /əl.kə.ˈliː.miː.ə/ (AmE), /æl.kə.ˈliː.miː.ə/ /; / /əl.kə.ˈliː.miː.ə/ (BrE)
A condition in which the pH of the blood is higher than normal, indicating a lower concentration of hydrogen ions (above 7.45).

Examples

Alkalemia in blood
Respiratory alkalemia can occur due to rapid breathing and loss of carbon dioxide.
The laboratory results confirmed a case of alkalemia after the patient's persistent vomiting.
Treatment was required to correct the metabolic alkalemia observed in the ICU.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acidemia - condition with unusually low blood pH
