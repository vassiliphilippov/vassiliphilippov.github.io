extubation

noun
removal of a tube
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstuˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstjuːˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing a tube, especially from a person's airway, after it is no longer needed

Examples

Extubation procedure
The doctor performed extubation after confirming the patient could breathe independently.
Successful extubation is an important step in a patient's recovery from surgery.
Nurses monitored the patient closely following extubation to ensure there were no complications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intubation - the insertion of a tube into a person's airway
  • synonym: removal - the act of taking something away
