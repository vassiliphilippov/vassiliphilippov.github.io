DNA

noun
digital data stored in synthetic DNA
6.3 1.91/m
Pronunciation: / di 5bn e6a / (AmE), / did0 5bn e6a / (BrE)
(in computing and data archiving) synthetic DNA used as a medium for storing digital information by encoding binary data as sequences of nucleotides.

Examples

DNA data storage
Researchers encoded a small file into DNA to test its potential as a long-term storage medium.
A startup stores archival photographs by synthesizing DNA strands that represent binary data and reading them later.
Advances in chemistry have made writing and reading DNA for digital backups more accurate and affordable.

Synonyms
