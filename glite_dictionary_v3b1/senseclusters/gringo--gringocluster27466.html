gringo

noun
Latin American term for foreigners, esp. Americans/English
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɪŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
(usually disparaging) a term used in Latin America for a foreigner, especially an American or English person.

Examples

A gringo visitor
He was the only gringo in the small mountain village.
Some find the term gringo offensive, while others use it more playfully.
The locals referred to the new arrivals as gringos because of their accent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreigner - a person from another country
  • antonym: local - a person who belongs to a place (antonym: foreigner)
