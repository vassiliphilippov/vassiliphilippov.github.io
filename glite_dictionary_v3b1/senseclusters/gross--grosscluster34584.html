gross

verb
earn before deductions
7.8 3.6
Pronunciation: /groʊs/ (AmE), /grəʊs/ (BrE)
(of a business, film, etc.) to earn a particular amount of money before taxes, expenses, or other deductions.

Examples

To gross a million
Despite high production costs, the play grossed a substantial amount in ticket sales.
Our company grosses nearly a million dollars annually before expenses are deducted.
The movie grossed over $200 million worldwide during its opening weekend.
