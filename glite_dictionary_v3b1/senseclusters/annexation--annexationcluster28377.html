annexation

noun
the act of joining or adding territory
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌænəkˈseɪʃən /; / ˌænɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænɪkˈseɪʃən /; / ˌanɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of adding or taking over territory or property, especially by a country or state

Examples

The annexation of land
After years of dispute, the council voted in favor of the annexation of the adjacent land.
The country's sudden annexation of its neighbor's territory was widely condemned internationally.
The history textbook detailed the annexation of the region in the nineteenth century.
Debates in the United Nations centered on the legality of the annexation following the conflict.
During the 19th century, the annexation of new colonies was a common practice among European powers.
The country's government condemned the annexation of its northern region by military force.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquisition - the act of acquiring something
  • antonym: cession - the formal giving up of rights, property, or territory
  • synonym: conquest - the act of conquering a place or people
  • antonym: secession - the action of withdrawing formally from a federation or body
