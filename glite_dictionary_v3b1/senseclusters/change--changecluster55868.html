change

noun
an event or process of becoming different
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
the act, process, or result of becoming different; a difference or transformation

Examples

A big change
Many people find it difficult to adapt to change, even when it is positive.
The company announced a major change in its leadership structure last week.
There has been a noticeable change in the weather since this morning.
Moving to a new city brought about a welcome change in her life.
The company went through a significant change after the new CEO arrived.
We need to adapt when there is a change in circumstances.
After years of routine, the new manager brought a welcome change to the office atmosphere.
Going somewhere different for vacation this year will be a refreshing change.
It's nice to have a change from eating the same food every day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alteration - the process of making something different without changing it completely
  • antonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or unwavering
  • synonym: modification - a small change or adjustment to something
  • synonym: novelty - the quality of being new, original, or unusual
  • antonym: stability - the state of being unchanging or not likely to change
  • synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse
