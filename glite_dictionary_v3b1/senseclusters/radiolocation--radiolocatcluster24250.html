radiolocation

noun
locating objects using radio waves
2.3 6.7
Pronunciation: /reɪ.di.oʊ.loʊ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /reɪ.dɪ.əʊ.ləʊ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The use of radio waves, particularly microwaves, to detect and determine the location of distant objects by analyzing reflected signals.

Examples

Radiolocation of objects
During the experiment, scientists used radiolocation to map the positions of migrating birds at night.
Modern airports rely on radiolocation systems to safely guide aircraft during takeoff and landing.
New advances in radiolocation have improved the accuracy of maritime navigation.
