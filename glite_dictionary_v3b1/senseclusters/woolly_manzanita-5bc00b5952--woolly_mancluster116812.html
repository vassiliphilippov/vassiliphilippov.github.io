woolly manzanita

noun
a woolly-twigged Californian shrub
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli mænzəˈniːtə / (AmE), / ˈwʊli mænzəˈniːtə / (BrE)
an upright, openly branched Californian shrub (Arctostaphylos tomentosa) whose young twigs are covered with woolly hairs

Examples

The woolly manzanita shrub
The woolly manzanita thrives in the dry hills of California.
The young branches of woolly manzanita are covered in a dense, fuzzy coating.
We saw several woolly manzanitas along the hiking trail.
