exit poll

noun
survey of voters leaving polls
6.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈeg.zɪt poʊl/ /; / /ˈek.sət poʊl/ /; / /ˈeg.zɪt pɑːl/ (AmE), /ˈek.sɪt pəʊl/ /; / /ˈeg.zɪt pəʊl/ /; / /ˈek.sɪt pɒl/ (BrE)
a survey of voters taken immediately after they have exited the polling stations, used especially to predict the outcome of an election

Examples

An exit poll survey
Analysts warn that an exit poll may not always accurately reflect the final vote count.
Many voters were surprised to see reporters conducting an exit poll outside the polling station.
The news channel released the results of the exit poll moments after the polls closed.
