peel off

verb
leave a group or formation and move away
4.1 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / pil …îf / (AmE), / piÀêl …íf / (BrE)
(of a person or vehicle) to leave a group or formation and move away in a different direction.

Examples

Jets peel off formation
At the signal, the lead jet peeled off from the formation and flew west.
During the marathon, one runner peeled off to take a different route.
Several cars peeled off from the convoy as we entered the city.

Synonyms

  • pull away
    3.5 6.95/m
    pull away means starting to move and create distance from others, while peel off means leaving a formation and going another way.
  • break away
    3.6 0.24/m
    break away means to leave suddenly or escape from control or a group, while peel off means a vehicle or person leaving a formation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break away - to leave or escape from a group or formation
  • antonym: join up - to become part of a group or formation
