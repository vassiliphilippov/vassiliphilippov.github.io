US spelling

noun
American system of English spelling
Pronunciation: / ˌju ˈɛs ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˈɛs ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The system of English spelling used in the United States, differing in some conventions from British spelling (e.g., 'color' vs. 'colour').

Examples

US spelling rules
He switched to US spelling when writing for an American publication.
Many words in American textbooks use US spelling instead of British spelling.
Teachers often highlight the differences between US spelling and the spelling used in other countries.
