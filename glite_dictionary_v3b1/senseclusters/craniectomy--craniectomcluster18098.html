craniectomy

noun
skull removal surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: / kræniˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / kræniˈɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a portion of the skull is removed to access or relieve pressure on the brain

Examples

A craniectomy surgery
After the accident, the doctors performed a craniectomy to reduce swelling in the patient's brain.
A craniectomy may be necessary when other treatments cannot relieve intracranial pressure.
The neurosurgeon explained the risks and benefits of a craniectomy to the patient's family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: craniotomy - a surgical operation in which a bone flap is temporarily removed from the skull
  • synonym: trepanation - the act of drilling or scraping a hole into the skull
