cultic

adjective
relating to a cult
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌltɪk / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪk / (BrE)
relating to the practices, rituals, or beliefs of a cult or religious worship, especially in historical or academic contexts

Examples

Cultic rituals
The group followed cultic rituals that were unfamiliar to outsiders.
Some music genres have developed a cultic following over the years.
The author’s cultic status grew as more fans became devoted to his work.
The temple was decorated with cultic symbols used during ancient ceremonies.
Researchers studied the cultic practices of early civilizations to understand their beliefs.
Artifacts with cultic significance were discovered at the archaeological site.
The ancient temple was the site of many cultic rituals performed in secrecy.
Researchers studied the cultic symbols found on the walls of the underground chamber.
Some communities still observe cultic traditions passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - belonging to widely accepted ideas or practices
  • synonym: ritual - a religious or solemn ceremony with a series of actions
  • synonym: sectarian - relating to a sect or group with strong devotion
  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
