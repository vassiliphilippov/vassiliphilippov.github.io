managerialism

noun
focus on management control
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmænɪˈdʒɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˈdʒɪəriəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or practice that management and managers should have a central, controlling role in organizations or society, often emphasizing rules, procedures, and business techniques

Examples

Managerialism in organizations
Some critics argue that managerialism can stifle innovation within large organizations.
The rise of managerialism has led to more standardized procedures in many companies.
Employees sometimes feel frustrated by the strict rules imposed by managerialism.
Critics argue that managerialism can lead to decisions that prioritize efficiency over employee well-being.
The rise of managerialism has changed how universities are run, focusing more on measurable outcomes.
Some believe that managerialism reduces creativity by emphasizing strict control and procedures.
The rise of managerialism has changed how many public institutions are run.
Some teachers feel that managerialism in schools reduces their professional autonomy.
Debates about managerialism often focus on its impact on public service values.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autonomy - independence or freedom in decision-making
  • synonym: bureaucracy - a system of administration marked by strict rules and hierarchy
  • antonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual
  • antonym: democracy - a system of government where power is held by the people
  • synonym: privatization - the transfer of public services or assets to private ownership or control
