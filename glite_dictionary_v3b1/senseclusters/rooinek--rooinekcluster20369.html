rooinek

noun
British newcomer (slang)
Pronunciation: / ˈruːiˌnɛk /; / ˈrɔɪˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈruːiˌnɛk /; / ˈrɔɪˌnɛk / (BrE)
a South African slang or informal term for a British person or an English-speaking white South African of British descent, often used insultingly or for newcomers

Examples

A new rooinek in town
The locals laughed as the new rooinek tried to understand South African slang.
He was called a rooinek because he had just arrived from England and didn't know the area.
Some South Africans use the word rooinek to refer to British tourists visiting the country.
He was called a rooinek by his classmates because he spoke English at home.
Some people use the term rooinek in a joking way, but it can be offensive.
During the rugby match, the crowd shouted at the visiting rooinek supporters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Brit - informal term for a British person
