sleep out

verb
work in a house without living there
7.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / slip a ät / (AmE), / sliÀêp a ät / (BrE)
(of an employee, especially domestic staff) to work in a household but not reside there overnight.

Examples

To sleep out at work
Due to her short commute, she is able to sleep out rather than stay overnight.
The family's nanny prefers to sleep out and return home every evening.
They asked the new housekeeper if she would be willing to sleep out or live in.

Synonyms

  • live out
    3.9 0.21/m
    live out means working in someone's house while living elsewhere, while sleep out means working there but not staying overnight.

Related Terms
