uncut

adjective
not trimmed or shortened
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌt / (BrE)
not trimmed, shortened, or cut; left in its original or natural state (such as hair, grass, objects, etc.)

Examples

Uncut hair
He wore his hair uncut for several months, letting it grow long and wild.
She admired the natural look of uncut hedges along the old country road.
The grass in the field remained uncut throughout autumn, swaying in the breeze.
She preferred to walk through the uncut meadow, enjoying the wildflowers.
The field was covered with uncut grass after several weeks of rain.
The gardener left one section of the lawn uncut for butterflies and bees.
Her hair was long and completely uncut since childhood.
The diamond was still uncut and had not yet been shaped by a jeweler.
They released the uncut version of the film, which included several previously unseen scenes.
