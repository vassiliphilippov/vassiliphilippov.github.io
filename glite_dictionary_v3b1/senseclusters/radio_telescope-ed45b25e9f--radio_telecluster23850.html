radio telescope

noun
astronomical instrument for detecting radio waves
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈtelɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A large instrument used to detect radio waves from space for the purpose of astronomical observation.

Examples

A radio telescope dish
A radio telescope can detect radio waves that are invisible to the human eye.
Construction of the new radio telescope was completed in the desert to minimize interference.
The scientists used a radio telescope to listen for signals from deep space.
