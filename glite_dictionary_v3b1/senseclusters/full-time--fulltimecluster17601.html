full-time

adjective, adverb
for the whole standard period (esp. work, study)
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.taɪm /; / ˈfʊlˌtaɪm / (BrE)
for or involving the whole normal or standard period of time for a job, study, or activity (e.g. a full-time job, working full-time)

Examples

A full-time job
After graduation, she started her first full-time job at a marketing firm.
He switched from a full-time to a part-time position to spend more time with his family.
Many students work full-time during the summer to save money for college.
After college, she hopes to work full-time in her chosen career.
He studied part-time last year but now attends classes full-time.
Since the promotion, Alex manages the store full-time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: part-time - working or involving less than the normal or full amount of time
