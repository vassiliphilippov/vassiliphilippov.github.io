concentrated

adjective
light coming from a focused source
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of light) transmitted directly from a single, focused source, resulting in increased intensity.

Examples

Concentrated light
A concentrated spotlight illuminated the central performer on stage.
Plants placed under concentrated sunlight often grow faster.
The surgeon used a concentrated beam of light during the delicate operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diffuse - (of light) spread out and not concentrated in one direction
