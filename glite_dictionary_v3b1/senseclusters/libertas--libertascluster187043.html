libertas

noun
Latin for freedom
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbərtɑs / (AmE), / ˈlɪbətɑːs / (BrE)
the Latin word for freedom or liberty, often used to symbolize the concept of personal or political freedom

Examples

Roman libertas ideal
The ancient Romans valued libertas as a fundamental principle of their society.
The inscription on the monument read libertas, reminding citizens of their rights.
Many organizations use the word libertas in their mottos to represent freedom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freedom - the state of being free from restrictions
  • synonym: liberty - the condition of being free within society
  • antonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control
