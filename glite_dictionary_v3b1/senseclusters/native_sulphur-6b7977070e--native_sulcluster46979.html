native sulphur

noun
naturally occurring elemental sulfur
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪ.tɪv ˈsʌl.fər / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv ˈsʌl.fə / (BrE)
Sulfur occurring in its natural, uncombined state, especially as found in geological formations; an old term for elemental sulfur.

Examples

Bright native sulphur
In the laboratory, native sulphur samples are prized for their purity and bright yellow color.
Miners extracted native sulphur directly from volcanic deposits.
The crystals in the cave turned out to be native sulphur formed over thousands of years.
