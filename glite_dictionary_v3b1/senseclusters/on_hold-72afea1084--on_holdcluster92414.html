on hold

adjective
temporarily paused or delayed
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɑn hoʊld / (AmE), / ɒn həʊld / (BrE)
temporarily stopped, delayed, or paused until further notice or action

Examples

On hold status
My doctor's appointment is on hold until the clinic reopens.
Plans for the music festival are still on hold because of the weather forecast.
The launch of the new product has been on hold since last month due to technical issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cancelled - called off or discontinued permanently
  • synonym: postponed - delayed until a later time
  • synonym: suspended - temporarily stopped or halted
