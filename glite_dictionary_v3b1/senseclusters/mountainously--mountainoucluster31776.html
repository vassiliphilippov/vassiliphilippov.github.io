mountainously

adverb
in a huge way
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊntənəsli / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪnəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is like a mountain, especially being very large, impressive, steep, or rugged

Examples

Rises mountainously
The clouds rose mountainously above the distant hills, casting long shadows over the valley.
Stacks of paperwork grew mountainously on his desk as the week went on.
The city skyline appeared mountainously tall against the evening sky.
The road wound mountainously through the highlands, making the drive both challenging and scenic.
Clouds gathered mountainously on the horizon, hinting at an approaching storm.
The landscape rose mountainously ahead, promising a difficult but rewarding hike.

  • antonym: gently - in a mild or gradual way
  • antonym: minutely - in a very small or tiny way
