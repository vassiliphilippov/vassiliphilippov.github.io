Synonyms

  • unify
    3.7 6.61/m
    unify means bring separate parts into one formal whole for a system, while unite means join people or things.
  • merge
    3.2 16/m
    merge combines two or more items into a single entity, while unite often stresses people or groups joining for a purpose.
  • coalesce
    7.4 1.54/m
    coalesce often describes parts coming together naturally over time, while unite usually implies a deliberate joining for a shared aim.
  • amalgamate
    7.4 0.34/m
    amalgamate means legally or formally combine organizations or substances, while unite is more about people or things joining as one.
  • join
    1.0 59/m
    join means connect or become part of something, often physically or socially, while unite stresses forming one single group.
  • consolidate
    4.7 4.36/m
    consolidate brings several parts into a stronger, single whole for efficiency, while unite focuses on joining people or groups.
  • conjoin
    5.9 1.47/m
    conjoin means join together, often in a formal or technical way, while unite is common for people or groups forming one.
