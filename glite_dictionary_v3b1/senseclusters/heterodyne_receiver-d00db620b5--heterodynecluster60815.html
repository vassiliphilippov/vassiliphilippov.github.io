heterodyne receiver

noun
radio receiver using frequency mixing
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛt̬.ɚˌdaɪn rɪˈsiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛt.ər.daɪn rɪˈsiː.və / (BrE)
a type of radio receiver that combines a locally generated frequency with the incoming carrier frequency to produce an intermediate frequency signal for easier demodulation and amplification.

Examples

A heterodyne receiver circuit
A heterodyne receiver can filter out unwanted frequencies for a cleaner audio output.
Many early aviation radios used a heterodyne receiver for reliable communication.
The engineer calibrated the heterodyne receiver to improve signal clarity during the experiment.
