on the whole

adverb
in general; considering everything
5.4 2.8
Pronunciation: /ɑːn ðə hoʊl/ /; / /ən ðə hoʊl/ /; / /ɑːn ðiː hoʊl/ (AmE), /ɒn ðə həʊl/ /; / /ən ðə həʊl/ /; / /ɒn ðiː həʊl/ (BrE)
used to say that something is generally true or to give a summary after considering everything

Examples

On the whole generally true
On the whole, I find this book to be informative and well-written.
On the whole, people in the city seem friendly and welcoming.
On the whole, the event was a great success despite a few minor issues.
On the whole, people seem satisfied with the new policy.
On the whole, the team performed very well this season.
There were a few minor issues, but on the whole it was a smooth event.
