dominantly

adverb
by dominant gene
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.mɪ.nənt.liː/ /; / /ˈdɑː.mə.nənt.liː/ (AmE), /ˈdɒ.mɪ.nənt.liː/ /; / /ˈdɒ.mə.nənt.liː/ (BrE)
in a manner determined by a dominant gene, especially in reference to genetic inheritance

Examples

Dominantly inherited trait
The disease is dominantly inherited, so only one parent needs to carry the gene.
If a trait is passed dominantly, it will appear even if only one copy of the gene is present.
Some genetic disorders are transmitted dominantly rather than recessively.
