oviparity

noun
egg-laying reproduction
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvəˈpærəti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvɪˈpærɪti / (BrE)
the reproductive process in which animals lay eggs that develop and hatch outside the mother's body

Examples

Oviparity in animals
Many reptiles and all birds reproduce through oviparity, laying eggs that hatch outside the body.
The study compared oviparity in amphibians with other reproductive strategies.
Insects often display oviparity, depositing eggs in safe environments for their young to develop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ovoviviparity - producing eggs that hatch inside the mother's body
  • antonym: viviparity - giving birth to live young rather than laying eggs
