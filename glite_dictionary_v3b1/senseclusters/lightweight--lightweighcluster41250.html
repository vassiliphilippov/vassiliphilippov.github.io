lightweight

noun
wrestler or boxer in 139–154 lb weight class
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌweɪt /; / ˈlaɪtweɪt / (BrE)
a person who competes in the lightweight class in sports like boxing or wrestling

Examples

A lightweight boxer
After months of training, he finally qualified as a lightweight for the national championships.
The reigning lightweight was known for his speed and agility in the ring.
There is fierce competition among lightweights for the world title this year.
As a lightweight, he had to maintain a strict diet to stay within the weight limit.
He became the national lightweight champion after a series of impressive matches.
The coach specializes in training promising lightweights for international competition.
At the weigh-in, he was just under the lightweight limit.
Many legendary boxers have competed in the lightweight division.
She became the national lightweight champion after winning the final match.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: featherweight - a boxer or wrestler in the next lighter weight class below lightweight
  • degree higher: middleweight - a boxer or wrestler in the next heavier weight class above lightweight
  • degree higher: welterweight - a boxer who weighs more than a lightweight but less than a middleweight
