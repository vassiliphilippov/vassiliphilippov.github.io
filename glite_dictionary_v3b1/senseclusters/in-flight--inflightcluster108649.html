in-flight

adjective
during a flight
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌflaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌflaɪt / (BrE)
happening or provided while an aircraft is flying (such as meals, entertainment, or services during a flight)

Examples

In-flight meal
Passengers enjoyed the in-flight meal service during the long journey.
The airline upgraded its in-flight entertainment system to include more movies and games.
She always brings headphones to use with the in-flight audio programs.
The airline offers in-flight entertainment to make long journeys more enjoyable.
The missile was destroyed by defense systems while still in-flight.
Birds can sleep in-flight during long migrations across the ocean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airborne - carried or transported through the air
  • antonym: grounded - not flying; on the ground
  • synonym: onboard - available or occurring on a vehicle, especially an aircraft or ship
