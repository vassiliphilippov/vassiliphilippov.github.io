coccolith

noun
algal calcium plate
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑkəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəlɪθ / (BrE)
a tiny, round plate of calcium carbonate produced by certain algae, forming part of their outer covering

Examples

A coccolith plate
Scientists studied the coccolith layers in the sediment to learn about ancient ocean conditions.
Each coccolith is produced by a single-celled alga called a coccolithophore.
The white cliffs are largely composed of fossilized coccoliths from millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coccolithophore - a type of algae that produces coccoliths
  • synonym: fossil - the preserved remains or impression of a prehistoric organism
