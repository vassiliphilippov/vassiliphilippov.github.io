sortie

verb
to attack from defense
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrti / (AmE), / ˈsɔːti / (BrE)
to make a sudden attack or movement out from a defensive position, especially by troops inside a fortification

Examples

To sortie from cover
The soldiers sortied from the castle to surprise the enemy at dawn.
After days of waiting, the defenders finally sortied to break the siege.
The commander ordered his troops to sortie and disrupt the attackers' lines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retreat - to withdraw from a position or attack
  • synonym: sally - to rush out suddenly from a defensive position to attack
