credit line

noun
maximum amount of money a customer can borrow
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (BrE)
an amount of money that a bank or financial institution agrees to lend to a customer, up to a stated maximum, to be used when needed

Examples

A credit line increase
Before applying for a loan, check if your credit line is sufficient for your needs.
Having a higher credit line can improve your financial flexibility.
The bank increased my credit line after reviewing my payment history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credit limit - the maximum amount of credit available to a customer
