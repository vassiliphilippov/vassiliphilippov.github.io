crucifier

noun
person who crucifies
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person who crucifies someone or something, especially as a form of punishment or execution

Examples

Roman crucifier
The ancient text described the crucifier as a figure feared by many.
In the play, the crucifier showed no remorse for his actions.
The artist painted the crucifier standing over the cross in a dramatic scene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: executioner - a person who carries out a death sentence
