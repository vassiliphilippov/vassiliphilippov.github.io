showboating

verb
to attract attention
6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈʃoʊ.boʊ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʃəʊ.bəʊ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
to behave in a flashy or exaggerated way to attract attention, especially in sports or performance

Examples

To showboat after scoring
The player was criticized for showboating after scoring a goal instead of celebrating with his team.
Some fans enjoy when athletes start showboating during important matches.
Instead of showboating, she focused on helping her teammates win the game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grandstanding - to act in a way intended to impress others or attract attention
  • antonym: modesty - the quality of not drawing attention to oneself
