owling

noun
wool smuggling crime
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the illegal act of smuggling wool or sheep out of England, especially in the past

Examples

Historic owling crime
In the seventeenth century, owling was considered a serious offense by the English authorities.
Laws were enacted to prevent owling and protect the English wool trade from illegal export.
Historical records show that many were prosecuted for owling during the medieval period.
