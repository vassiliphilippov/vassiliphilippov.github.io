board

verb
live and take meals at a place
Pronunciation: / bɔrd / (AmE), / bɔːd / (BrE)
to live at a place where you are given meals, or to provide someone with meals and lodging, usually for payment

She boarded several young artists in her large country house.
The family used to board university students during the summer holidays.

He will board at the farmhouse while he completes his research project.
