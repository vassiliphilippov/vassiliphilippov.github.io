tutelage

noun
individual teaching or supervision
6.8 0.73/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀêt…™l…™d í / (AmE), / ÀàtjuÀêt…™l…™d í / (BrE)
the act of teaching or instructing, especially when done individually by a tutor; guidance or supervision in study or learning

Examples

Private tutelage lessons
During my summer holidays, I learned French under private tutelage at home.
She owes her impressive academic progress to the personal tutelage she received.
Under the tutelage of his mentor, he quickly mastered the basics of chess.

Synonyms

  • tuition
    3.6 0.26/m
    tuition is private one-to-one teaching often paid for by students, while tutelage adds ongoing guidance and supervision in learning.
  • tutoring
    2.2 4.51/m
    tutoring is the act of giving extra one-to-one lessons to help a student, while tutelage means broad guidance in their learning.
  • tutorship
    7.0 0.01/m
    tutorship is the formal role or job of a private tutor working with pupils, while tutelage stresses the learner's guidance and supervision.
  • teaching
    1.0 130/m
    teaching is the general process or profession of instructing many learners, while tutelage focuses on close, personal guidance for one student.
  • instruction
    1.9 56/m
    instruction is formal classroom-based delivery of skills or knowledge to groups, while tutelage implies private, ongoing supervision of a learner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guidance - advice or instruction aimed at resolving a problem or difficulty, especially as given by someone in authority
  • synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge
  • synonym: tuition - private teaching or instruction, often for individuals or small groups
