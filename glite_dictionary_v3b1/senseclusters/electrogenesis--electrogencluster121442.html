electrogenesis

noun
generation of electricity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the production or generation of electricity, especially by living organisms such as certain fish

Examples

Fish electrogenesis
Some fish use electrogenesis to navigate and communicate in murky waters.
Researchers study electrogenesis in electric eels to understand how they generate high voltages.
The phenomenon of electrogenesis is rare among animals but crucial for certain aquatic species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioelectricity - electricity produced by living organisms
  • antonym: electrolysis - chemical decomposition produced by passing an electric current through a liquid
