colectomy

noun
colon removal surgery
Pronunciation: / koʊˈlɛktəmi / (AmE), / kəʊˈlɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove all or part of the colon

Examples

A colectomy operation
After the colectomy, the patient needed several weeks to recover fully.
Doctors recommended a colectomy to treat the severe colon disease.
Advances in surgical techniques have made colectomy safer and less invasive.
After being diagnosed with colon cancer, she underwent a colectomy to remove the affected section.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of a colectomy before the procedure.
Recovery from a colectomy can take several weeks, depending on the patient's health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appendectomy - surgical removal of the appendix
  • synonym: colon resection - surgical removal of part of the colon
  • synonym: colostomy - a surgical procedure creating an opening from the colon to the abdominal wall
