pepo

noun
fruit with hard rind
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpiː.poʊ/ (AmE), /ˈpiː.pəʊ/ (BrE)
a type of fleshy fruit with a hard outer rind and no internal divisions, such as a melon, squash, or cucumber

Examples

A pepo fruit
A pumpkin is a well-known example of a pepo in the plant world.
Botanists classify the cucumber as a pepo because of its hard outer rind.
The thick skin of a watermelon makes it a classic pepo among fruits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drupe - a fleshy fruit with a single hard stone inside
