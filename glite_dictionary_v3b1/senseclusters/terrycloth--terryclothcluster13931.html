terrycloth

noun
absorbent fabric with loops, used for towels
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛriˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈtɛriˌklɒθ / (BrE)
A thick, absorbent cotton fabric with uncut loops on both sides, commonly used for towels and bathrobes.

Examples

A terrycloth towel
Drying off is easy with a terrycloth bath towel because it absorbs water quickly.
He wrapped himself in a soft terrycloth robe after his shower.
The hotel provides thick terrycloth towels in every room.
