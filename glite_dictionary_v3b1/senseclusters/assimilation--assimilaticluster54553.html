assimilation

noun
integration into a larger group or culture
7.6
Pronunciation: / əˌsɪməˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsɪmɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which people or groups become integrated into a larger group or culture, often by adopting its customs and values

Examples

Cultural assimilation
Language classes play an important role in the assimilation of newcomers.
Some groups resist assimilation in order to preserve their cultural heritage.
The assimilation of immigrants into the local community can take several generations.
Some fear that rapid assimilation could erase traditional cultural practices.
The government's policy promoted the assimilation of immigrants into mainstream society.
The school programs were designed to assist with the assimilation of refugee children.
The assimilation of immigrants into the local culture can take several generations.
Language learners often struggle with the assimilation of new sounds.
The assimilation of new information is essential for academic success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to new conditions
  • synonym: integration - the process of combining into a whole, especially with respect to people in society
  • antonym: segregation - the act of separating people of different races, religions, etc., and treating them differently
  • antonym: separation - the act of keeping things apart or dividing them
