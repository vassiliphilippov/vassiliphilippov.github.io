kaki

noun
blackish New Zealand stilt (bird)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːki / (AmE), / ˈkɑːki / (BrE)
a blackish stilt (Himantopus novaezelandiae) native to New Zealand, sometimes regarded as a color phase of the white-headed stilt

Examples

A wild kaki bird
Seeing a kaki in the wild is considered a memorable experience for birdwatchers.
The population of the kaki has declined sharply due to habitat loss and introduced predators.
Wildlife rangers track the movements of the rare kaki in New Zealand wetlands.
