pass

verb
to die (euphemistically)
Pronunciation: / pæs / (AmE), / pɑːs / (BrE)
(of a person) to die; to stop living, especially used as a polite or indirect way of referring to death.

Examples

He passed peacefully
He passed peacefully, surrounded by his family.
Many believe that when loved ones pass, they continue to watch over us.
Sadly, her grandfather passed last winter after a long illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist (physically)
  • antonym: live - to remain alive; to continue to exist
  • synonym: perish - to die, especially in a violent or sudden way
