raise hell

verb
cause trouble or commotion
3.7
Pronunciation: / reɪz hɛl / (AmE), / reɪz hɛl / (BrE)
to behave in a loud, disruptive, or angry way, often to cause trouble or protest

Examples

To raise hell loudly
Local fans began raising hell outside the stadium after their team lost.
She threatened to raise hell if her complaint wasn’t taken seriously.
The teenagers were raising hell in the empty park until the police arrived.
Neighbors started to raise hell about the noise from the club.
She promised to raise hell at the next meeting if nothing changed.
The workers threatened to raise hell if their demands were not met.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cause a stir - to create a commotion or draw a lot of attention
  • antonym: keep the peace - to maintain order and avoid causing trouble
  • synonym: kick up a fuss - to protest or complain loudly
  • synonym: make a scene - to behave in a loud or noticeable way in public, especially to attract attention
