blindish

adjective
somewhat blind
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪndɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat blind or having qualities of being partially unable to see clearly

Examples

A blindish look
Without his glasses, he feels a bit blindish in the classroom.
After the surgery, her vision was still blindish for a few days.
He described the foggy morning as making everything look blindish and unclear.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blind - unable to see
  • synonym: partially sighted - having limited vision but not completely blind
