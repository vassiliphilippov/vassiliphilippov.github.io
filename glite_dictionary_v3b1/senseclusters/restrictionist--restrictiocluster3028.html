restrictionist

adjective
favoring restrictions
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstrɪkʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃənɪst / (BrE)
favoring or supporting the imposition of restrictions or limits, especially on immigration, trade, or other activities

Examples

Restrictionist policy
The government adopted a restrictionist approach to immigration, tightening entry requirements for foreign workers.
Many voters supported the candidate's restrictionist policies on international trade.
A restrictionist attitude can lead to stricter laws and regulations in various sectors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberal - favoring fewer restrictions or more openness, especially in policy
  • synonym: protectionist - favoring the protection of domestic industries by restricting imports
