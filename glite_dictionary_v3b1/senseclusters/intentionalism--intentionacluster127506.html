intentionalism

noun
philosophy of mind theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈten.ʃə.nəl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈten.ʃə.nəl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the philosophical view that the qualitative character of conscious experience is determined by its representational or intentional content

Examples

Intentionalism in philosophy
Many philosophers debate whether intentionalism can fully explain the nature of conscious experience.
Her thesis provided a detailed defense of intentionalism in the philosophy of mind.
Critics of intentionalism argue that it overlooks the importance of subjective qualities in experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: qualia theory - the view that conscious experience is defined by intrinsic qualities, not just representational content
  • synonym: representationalism - the theory that mental states are defined by their representational content
