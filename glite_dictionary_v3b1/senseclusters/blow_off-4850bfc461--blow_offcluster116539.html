(of an object) to become detached and carried away by an explosion or other powerful force.

Examples

Blow off the lid

The lid of the container was blown off in the accident.

Synonyms

  • come off
    3.5 4.69/m
    come off is used when something comes loose or falls away, while blow off means it is detached and blown away by force.
  • break off
    2.8 1.72/m
    break off describes a part that separates by breaking, while blow off implies it is torn away and carried off by a strong force.
  • break away
    3.5 0.18/m
    break away often means a piece separates from the whole by force, while blow off means it is carried away by force.
  • detach
    4.0 1.47/m
    detach is a formal verb for coming apart, while blow off describes being ripped free and swept away by a strong blast or wind.
