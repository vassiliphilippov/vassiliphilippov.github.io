anaplasmosis

noun
tick-borne disease affecting cattle
7.1 8.3
Pronunciation: /æn.ə.plæz.ˈmoʊ.sɪs/ /; / /ə.nə.plæz.ˈmoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /æn.ə.plæz.ˈməʊ.sɪs/ /; / /ə.nə.plæz.ˈməʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a disease caused by Anaplasma bacteria, spread by ticks, affecting animals (such as cattle) and humans

Examples

Anaplasmosis disease in cattle
Effective tick control can help prevent anaplasmosis outbreaks on livestock farms.
The spread of anaplasmosis is closely linked to the presence of cattle ticks in the area.
Veterinarians diagnosed anaplasmosis in several cows after they showed signs of severe anemia.
Public health alerts advised residents after several anaplasmosis cases were reported in humans.
Doctors recommended antibiotics as soon as they suspected anaplasmosis following the tick bite.
Because symptoms can be vague, people with fever after hiking were tested for anaplasmosis.
