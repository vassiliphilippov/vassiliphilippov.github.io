viral hemorrhagic fever

noun
serious illness involving viral infection and bleeding
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪɹəl ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.vɚ /; / ˈvaɪɹəl ˌhiːməˈɹædʒɪk ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪə.rəl ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və /; / ˈvaɪərəl ˌhiːməˈrædʒɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
Any of a group of severe illnesses caused by viral infections, characterized by fever, bleeding, and damage to blood vessels, often found in specific geographic areas.

Examples

Viral hemorrhagic fever symptoms
Doctors suspected the patient had viral hemorrhagic fever after he began to bleed from his gums.
Ebola is a type of viral hemorrhagic fever that can be fatal if not treated promptly.
Outbreaks of viral hemorrhagic fever are often difficult to control in remote areas.
Doctors are trained to recognize the symptoms of viral haemorrhagic fever in travelers from affected regions.
Several outbreaks of viral haemorrhagic fever were reported in West Africa during the last decade.
The patient was diagnosed with a viral haemorrhagic fever after returning from overseas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ebola - a severe viral hemorrhagic fever caused by ebolavirus
  • synonym: dengue fever - a mosquito-borne viral hemorrhagic illness
