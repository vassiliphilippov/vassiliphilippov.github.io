catcall

noun
loud disapproving cry or whistle
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌkɑl / (AmE), / ˈkatkɔːl / (BrE)
a loud, harsh, or shrill call or whistle expressing disapproval, annoyance, or aggression, often made at public performances or in the street

Examples

A loud catcall
During the play, several catcalls erupted when the actor forgot his lines.
Ignoring the catcall, the politician continued his speech without hesitation.
The comedian was interrupted by a loud catcall from the back of the audience.

  • synonym: boo - a sound made to show disapproval
