soling

noun
foundation layer material
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer of stones, gravel, or broken material used as a foundation or bedding for a road, floor, or drain

Examples

Road soling layer
The workers spread a thick soling before constructing the new road.
Proper soling is essential to ensure the stability of the drainage system.
Engineers inspected the soling to confirm it was evenly distributed under the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building or structure
  • synonym: subbase - a layer of material laid beneath a road or pavement to provide support
