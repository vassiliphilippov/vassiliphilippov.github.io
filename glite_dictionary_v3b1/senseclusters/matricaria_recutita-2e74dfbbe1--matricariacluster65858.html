Matricaria recutita

noun
species of herb similar to chamomile
9.6
Pronunciation: /mæ.trɪ.ˈkeə.ri.ə rɪ.ˈkjuː.tɪ.tə/ (AmE), /mə.trɪ.ˈkeə.ri.ə rɪ.ˈkjuː.tɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an annual Eurasian herb, also known as German chamomile, similar in fragrance and medicinal uses to chamomile but with a more bitter taste and generally considered to have a milder effect

Examples

Matricaria recutita plant
Gardeners often plant Matricaria recutita for its medicinal uses and its delicate, fragrant flowers.
Some herbal teas are made from Matricaria recutita to promote relaxation and ease digestive discomfort.
Unlike true chamomile, Matricaria recutita has a slightly more bitter flavor and is sometimes blended with other herbs.
