self-concern

noun
concern for one's own interests
5.3 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Àås…õlf.k…ônÀàs…úrn / (AmE), / Àås…õlf.k…ônÀàs…úÀên / (BrE)
Concern for one's own interests and welfare.

Examples

Self-concern for welfare
A healthy degree of self-concern is important for maintaining personal boundaries.
His self-concern sometimes made him seem indifferent to others' problems.
Therapists encourage clients to balance self-concern with empathy for others.

Synonyms

  • egoism
    7.2 0.35/m
    egoism treats acting for your own benefit as a guiding rule, while self-concern means simply caring for your own welfare.
  • self-interest
    4.0 5.54/m
    self-interest means choosing what helps you most in a situation, while self-concern is a general care for your own welfare.
  • self-centeredness
    5.8 0.25/m
    self-centeredness focuses on your wants and ignores others' needs, while self-concern simply names caring about your welfare.
  • selfishness
    3.0 3.85/m
    selfishness puts your own advantage first and may harm others, while self-concern is a neutral care for yourself.
  • egocentrism
    8.8 0.26/m
    egocentrism sees your view as central and can miss others' perspectives, while self-concern simply means caring for yourself.
