get the drift

verb
understand the main idea
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ðə drɪft / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə drɪft / (BrE)
to understand the general meaning or main point of something that is said or written, even if not every detail is clear

Examples

To get the drift of talk
After listening for a few minutes, he finally got the drift of the conversation.
I didn't catch every detail of his explanation, but I got the drift.
You don't have to explain it all—she'll get the drift soon enough.
