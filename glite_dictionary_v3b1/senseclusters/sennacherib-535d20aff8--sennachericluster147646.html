Sennacherib

proper noun
king of Assyria (died 681 BC)
Pronunciation: / səˈnækərɪb / (AmE), / səˈnækərɪb / (BrE)
(died 681 BC) King of Assyria; known for his military campaigns against Judea and Babylon and for rebuilding Nineveh.

Examples

Sennacherib king of Assyria
Sennacherib led a powerful army against the kingdom of Judah in the late 8th century BC.
Many scholars have studied Sennacherib's reign and his inscriptions for insights into Assyrian history.
The ancient Assyrian capital of Nineveh was rebuilt by Sennacherib after its destruction.
