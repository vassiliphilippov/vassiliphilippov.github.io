renga

noun
Japanese linked poetry
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈrɛŋɡə / (BrE)
a form of Japanese collaborative poetry in which multiple poets compose linked verses together

Examples

A renga poem
During the festival, poets gathered to compose a renga that celebrated the changing seasons.
The workshop introduced students to the art of renga and encouraged them to write collaboratively.
Each participant added a verse to the renga, creating a unique poem together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haiku - a short Japanese poem with three lines and a 5-7-5 syllable pattern
  • synonym: tanka - a traditional Japanese poem with five lines and a 5-7-5-7-7 syllable pattern
