plaster cast

noun
hard covering for broken bones
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstɚ kæst / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə kɑːst / (BrE)
a hard protective covering made of plaster of Paris, used to immobilize broken bones while they heal

Examples

A plaster cast on leg
After breaking his leg, he wore a plaster cast for six weeks.
She signed her friend's plaster cast with a colorful marker.
The doctor removed the plaster cast once the bone had healed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: splint - a rigid device used to support and immobilize a broken bone
