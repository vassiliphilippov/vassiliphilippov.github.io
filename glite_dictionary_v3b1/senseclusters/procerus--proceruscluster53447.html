procerus

noun
facial muscle of nose
Pronunciation: / proʊˈsɪrəs / (AmE), / prəʊˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
a small muscle at the top of the nose that helps create horizontal wrinkles on the bridge of the nose

Examples

The procerus muscle
The procerus muscle contracts when you frown or squint in bright sunlight.
Botox injections can relax the procerus to reduce wrinkles between the eyebrows.
Anatomy students studied the procerus to understand its role in facial expressions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrugator supercilii - a facial muscle that draws the eyebrows downward and inward
