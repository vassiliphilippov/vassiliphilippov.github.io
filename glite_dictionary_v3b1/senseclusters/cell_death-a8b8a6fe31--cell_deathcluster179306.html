cell death

noun
degeneration and death of living cells
2.7
Pronunciation: / sɛl dɛθ / (AmE), / sɛl dɛθ / (BrE)
the process by which living cells undergo degeneration and die, often as a normal part of growth and development in an organism

Examples

Programmed cell death
Programmed cell death plays a crucial role in shaping tissues during embryonic development.
Researchers studied the mechanisms that trigger cell death in cancer cells.
Some diseases are caused by either too much or too little cell death in certain tissues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apoptosis - the programmed death of cells as part of normal growth or maintenance
