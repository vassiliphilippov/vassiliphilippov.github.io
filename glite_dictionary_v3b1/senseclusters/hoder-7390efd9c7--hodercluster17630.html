Hoder

proper noun
blind god in Norse mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊ.dər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊ.də / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, a blind god who, deceived by Loki, killed his brother Balder with a shaft of mistletoe.

Examples

Hoder in Norse myths
Because Hoder was blind, he was easily manipulated by Loki's cunning plan.
In the ancient legend, Hoder tragically killed Balder with a mistletoe spear.
Many Norse myths recount the fateful actions of Hoder during the gods' downfall.
