pozzolanic

adjective
reactive with lime
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːtsəˈlænɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒtsəˈlænɪk / (BrE)
relating to materials that react with lime to form cement-like compounds, often used in construction

Examples

Pozzolanic materials
The addition of pozzolanic materials improved the durability of the concrete structure.
Engineers often select pozzolanic additives to enhance the strength of building materials.
Ancient Romans used pozzolanic ash in their concrete to build lasting monuments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydraulic - able to harden under water, as in hydraulic cement
