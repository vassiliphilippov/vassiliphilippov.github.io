break in

verb
train to make obedient or accustomed
Pronunciation: / breɪk ɪn / (AmE), / breɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to train or accustom someone or something (such as an animal, person, or new shoes) to become adapted or comfortable in a particular role or environment

Examples

Break in new shoes
It took me weeks to break in the new shoes until they were comfortable.
It took patience and care to break in the wild colt.
The manager had to break in several new employees this month.
After several weeks, the wild mare was finally broken in and ready to ride.
It takes patience and experience to break in a colt safely.
The new trainer was skilled at breaking in young horses for the ranch.
