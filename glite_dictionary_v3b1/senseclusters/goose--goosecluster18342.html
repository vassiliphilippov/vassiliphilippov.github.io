goose

verb
prod or spur into action
Pronunciation: / ɡus / (AmE), / ɡuːs / (BrE)
to prompt or prod someone or something into action, often by encouraging or stimulating them suddenly

Examples

To goose sales
Rising costs goosed the company to improve its efficiency.
She needed a little extra motivation, so her friend goosed her into applying for the job.
The coach tried to goose the team into playing harder in the second half.
