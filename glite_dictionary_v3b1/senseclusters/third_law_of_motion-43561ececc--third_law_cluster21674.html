third law of motion

noun
Newton's law: action equals reaction
Pronunciation: / θɝd lɔ əv ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / θɜːd lɔː ɒv ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
Newton's law stating that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Examples

Newton's third law of motion
According to the third law of motion, when you push on a wall, the wall pushes back with equal force.
Physics students learn the third law of motion to understand why rockets launch upward.
The concept of recoil in firearms is a practical demonstration of the third law of motion.
