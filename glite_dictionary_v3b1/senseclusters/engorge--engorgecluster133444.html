engorge

verb
to swell with fluid
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to become or make swollen with blood or another fluid

Examples

Tissue engorges with blood

The mosquito will engorge itself on its host's blood before flying away.
During the allergic reaction, his lips started to engorge and become painful.
After the mosquito bites, its abdomen will engorge with blood.
The river began to engorge after days of heavy rain.
During allergic reactions, tissues may engorge and cause discomfort.
