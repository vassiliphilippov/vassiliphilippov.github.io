unappealable

adjective
not subject to appeal
8.8 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Àå ån…ôÀàpiÀêl…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / Àå ån…ôÀàpiÀêl…ôb…ôl / (BrE)
(of a decision or ruling) not able to be challenged or changed by an appeal to a higher court or authority.

Examples

Unappealable court decision
An order designated as unappealable cannot be challenged in any court.
Because the contract stated the arbitration was final and unappealable, neither party could contest the outcome.
The decision of the disciplinary committee was unappealable under the association's rules.

Synonyms

  • unreviewable
    9.4 0.06/m
    unreviewable means a decision cannot be examined or reconsidered by a higher court, while unappealable means it cannot be changed by appeal.
  • indefeasible
    9.9 0.05/m
    indefeasible describes a right or decision that cannot be annulled or voided, while unappealable refers to rulings not open to appeal.
  • incontestible
    8.4 0.00/m
    incontestible means something cannot be disputed or questioned by people, while unappealable means it cannot be changed by court appeal.
  • unassailable
    8.0 0.94/m
    unassailable describes a position or claim that cannot be attacked or defeated, while unappealable refers to decisions not subject to appeal.
