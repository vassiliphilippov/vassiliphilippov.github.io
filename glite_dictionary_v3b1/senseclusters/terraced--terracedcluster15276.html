terraced

adjective
in rows or levels
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛrəst / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəst / (BrE)
arranged in rows or a series of flat or raised levels, especially for houses, land, gardens, or seating

Examples

Terraced houses
They live in a terraced house on a quiet street in London.
The hillside was terraced to create flat areas for farming.
Many British cities have rows of terraced homes built in the 19th century.
The terraced gardens made the hillside look beautiful and organized.
They bought a terraced house in a row of similar homes on the street.
Spectators enjoyed a clear view from the terraced seating in the stadium.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detached - not joined to another building
  • antonym: flat - having a level surface without raised areas
  • synonym: row house - a house that is one of a row of similar houses joined together by their side walls
  • synonym: stepped - having a series of steps or levels
