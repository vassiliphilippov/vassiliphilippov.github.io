forbearer

noun
predecessor or forerunner
8.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrÀàb…õr…ôr / (AmE), / f…îÀêÀàb…õ…ôr…ô / (BrE)
a person or thing that came before another, especially as a predecessor or forerunner in a role, idea, or institution

Examples

A forbearer of democracy
The ancient assembly served as a forbearer to our modern parliamentary system, influencing its procedures.
Many scientists consider Newton a forbearer of modern physics.
The traditions of the village are rooted in the customs of its forbearers.

Synonyms

  • predecessor
    4.9 1.64/m
    a predecessor is someone who held a role or office immediately before another, while a forbearer refers to any earlier person or thing in that lineage.
  • forerunner
    6.2 1.60/m
    a forerunner shows or signals a coming change or leader, while a forbearer simply names an earlier person or thing in the same role or idea.
  • precursor
    7.2 3.78/m
    a precursor appears earlier and hints at what will develop, while a forbearer is an earlier person or thing in a role or institution.
  • foreparent
    9.5 0.00/m
    a foreparent is a founder or early originator of a group, while a forbearer is any earlier person or thing in a role or tradition.
