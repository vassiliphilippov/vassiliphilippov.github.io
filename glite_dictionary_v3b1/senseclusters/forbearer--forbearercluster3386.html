forbearer

noun
predecessor or forerunner
8.1
Pronunciation: /fər.ˈber.ər/ /; / /fɔːr.ˈber.ər/ (AmE), /fɔː.ˈbeə.rə/ /; / /fə.ˈbeə.rə/ (BrE)
a person or thing that came before another, especially as a predecessor or forerunner in a role, idea, or institution

Examples

A forbearer of democracy
The ancient assembly served as a forbearer to our modern parliamentary system, influencing its procedures.
Many scientists consider Newton a forbearer of modern physics.
The traditions of the village are rooted in the customs of its forbearers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: successor - a person who comes after another in a role or position
