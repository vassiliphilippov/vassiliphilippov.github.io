bearish

adjective
expecting prices to fall (finance)
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbeərɪʃ / (BrE)
Expecting prices, especially of stocks or other securities, to fall; pessimistic about market prospects.

Examples

A bearish market
Her bearish predictions prompted several clients to sell their shares early.
Many investors took a bearish view of the market after the disappointing earnings report.
While some analysts are bearish, others expect the stock to rebound soon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bullish - expecting prices to rise (finance)
