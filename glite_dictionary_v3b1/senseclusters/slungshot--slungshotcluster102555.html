slungshot

noun
weighted rope tool
Pronunciation: / ˈslʌŋˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈslʌŋˌʃɒt / (BrE)
a small weight attached to a cord or rope, used to throw a line between ships or from ship to shore

Examples

Sailor's slungshot line
The sailor prepared the slungshot before tossing the line to the other ship.
A slungshot can help connect ropes between a dock and a boat quickly.
He swung the slungshot overhead and released it to send the line across the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heaving line - a rope with a weighted end used for throwing
