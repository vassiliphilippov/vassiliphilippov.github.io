steady state theory

noun
cosmological theory of continuous creation
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛdi steɪt ˈθiəri / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi steɪt ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
A cosmological theory proposing that the universe maintains a constant average density, with new matter continuously created to compensate for galaxies moving apart, so the universe has no beginning or end in time.

Examples

The steady state theory
Few scientists still support the steady state theory as new observations have contradicted its predictions.
Fred Hoyle was one of the main proponents of the steady state theory in the mid-20th century.
The steady state theory was once a major alternative to the Big Bang in cosmology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: big bang theory - the prevailing cosmological model that the universe originated from a singularity and has been expanding
