tube

noun
anatomical hollow cylindrical body structure
Pronunciation: / tuːb / (AmE), / tjuːb / (BrE)
a long, hollow, cylindrical object used as a container or passage for liquids, gases, or other materials (including in the body, plants, or as packaging)

Examples

A plastic tube
Doctors inserted a small tube into the patient's windpipe to help him breathe.
Many plants have a vascular tube system for transporting water and nutrients.
The fallopian tube plays a key role in human reproduction.
A cardboard tube was used to mail the posters.
He inserted the plastic tube into the tank to siphon out the water.
The laboratory uses a glass tube to transfer chemicals safely.
He squeezed the paint out of the tube onto the palette.
The tunnel resembled a giant tube burrowing beneath the city.
They inserted a flexible tube to drain the excess fluid from her chest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cylinder - a solid or hollow object in the shape of a circular tube or pipe
  • synonym: duct - a tube or vessel in the body that conveys a substance
  • synonym: pipe - a tube through which fluids can be conveyed
