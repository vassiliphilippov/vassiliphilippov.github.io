recursion

The process of defining or calculating something in terms of itself, often by repeating a procedure or operation; especially, a technique in mathematics and computing in which a function calls itself as part of its execution.

Examples

Recursion in math
Mathematicians often use recursion to define sequences, such as the Fibonacci numbers.
The concept of recursion is fundamental in computer programming, where functions may call themselves to solve complex problems.
Understanding recursion requires seeing how a process can continue to build on itself.
