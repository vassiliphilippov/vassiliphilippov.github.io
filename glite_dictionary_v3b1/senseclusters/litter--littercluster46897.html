litter

verb
strew objects or trash over a surface
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtər /; / ˈlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
to make a place untidy by leaving rubbish or many things scattered around

Examples

To litter the ground
After the parade, confetti littered the streets, making cleanup a challenge.
Broken glass and wrappers littered the playground after the festival ended.
The wind littered leaves across the front porch overnight.
After the festival, people began to litter the streets with flyers and plastic cups.
Please do not litter the park with empty bottles and wrappers.
Tourists sometimes litter the beach despite the warning signs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pollute - to make air, water, or land dirty or harmful
  • synonym: scatter - to throw or spread things over a wide area
