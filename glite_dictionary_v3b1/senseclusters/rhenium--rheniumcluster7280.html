rhenium

noun
a rare metallic chemical element
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈriːniəm / (AmE), / ˈriːniəm / (BrE)
a rare, heavy, silvery-white metallic element used in high-temperature alloys and obtained mainly as a by-product in refining molybdenum

Examples

Rare rhenium metal
Due to its rarity, rhenium is among the most expensive metals in the world.
Scientists discovered rhenium as a by-product in the mining of molybdenum.
The jet engine relies on rhenium alloys to withstand extremely high temperatures.
