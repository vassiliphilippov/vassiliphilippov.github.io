scheme

verb
devise a secret or elaborate plan
1.8
Pronunciation: / skiːm / (AmE), / skiːm / (BrE)
to make secret or dishonest plans to achieve something

Examples

To scheme against others
She would often scheme to get out of doing her chores.
The committee began to scheme ways to increase membership.
They are scheming to take control of the company.
She began to scheme against her business partner when profits started to drop.
They schemed for months to win the contract unfairly.
You can see him scheming in the background, waiting for his chance to take control.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: collaborate - to work together, especially on something secret (sometimes neutral)
  • synonym: conspire - to secretly plan with others to do something illegal or harmful
  • synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
  • synonym: plot - to secretly make plans to achieve something, especially something harmful or illegal
