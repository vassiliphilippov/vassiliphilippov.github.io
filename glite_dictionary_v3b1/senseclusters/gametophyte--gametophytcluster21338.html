gametophyte

noun
gamete-producing phase in plant life cycle
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈmiːtəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ɡəˈmiːtəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
The stage in the life cycle of a plant or alga that produces gametes and has a single set of chromosomes, typically alternating with a sporophyte stage.

Examples

A gametophyte stage
Ferns have a small, heart-shaped gametophyte that is often overlooked in their life cycle.
Sperm and egg cells are produced on the surface of the gametophyte.
The moss gametophyte is the most familiar stage you see covering rocks and soil.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sporophyte - the phase in the plant life cycle that produces spores, alternating with gametophyte
