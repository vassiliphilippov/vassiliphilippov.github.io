disposition

noun
habitual tendency or inclination
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a person's usual mood, temperament, or natural tendency to act or think in a particular way

Examples

Friendly disposition
Her cheerful disposition made her popular among her colleagues.
Some dogs have a nervous disposition and require gentle training.
This fabric has a disposition to shrink when washed in hot water.
Despite the challenges, his positive disposition never faded.
She has a cheerful disposition that puts everyone at ease.
Their son inherited his father's calm and patient disposition.
Despite the stressful environment, she maintained a calm disposition.
He shows a disposition to help others whenever he can.
Her cheerful disposition made her a pleasure to work with every day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • synonym: inclination - a tendency to do something
  • synonym: personality - the combination of characteristics forming an individual's distinctive character
  • synonym: propensity - an inclination or natural tendency to behave in a particular way
  • synonym: temperament - a person's or animal's nature as it affects their behavior
  • synonym: tendency - an inclination toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior
