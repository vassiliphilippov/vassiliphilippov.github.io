easily

adverb
without difficulty or effort
1.0 95/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêz…ôli / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêz…™li / (BrE)
without difficulty or effort; with ease.

Examples

Do it easily
She learned to play the piano easily compared to her classmates.
The vase was so fragile that it broke easily when it fell.
This book can be easily understood, even by beginners.

Synonyms

    3.1 3.44/m
    effortlessly shows no visible strain or work, while easily is more general and can allow some small effort.
  • handily
    3.0 1.65/m
    handily is used to say something is done without trouble and often conveniently, while easily means with little effort.
  • readily
    2.3 27/m
    readily often adds quickness or willingness to act, while easily focuses mainly on how little effort something needs.
  • in a breeze
    3.0 0.03/m
    in a breeze paints a picture of something happening very smoothly and quickly, while easily is more neutral.
  • in a snap
    3.0 0.18/m
    in a snap suggests something happens instantly or quickly with little effort, while easily only stresses low difficulty.
  • easy as pie
    1.7 0.20/m
    easy as pie is a playful phrase for something extremely simple or fun, while easily is plain and neutral.
