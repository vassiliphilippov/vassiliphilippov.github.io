Romanism

noun
the Roman Catholic Church's beliefs and practices
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmənɪzəm / (BrE)
The beliefs, practices, and system of the Roman Catholic Church, often used by opponents and sometimes considered offensive or pejorative.

Examples

Romanism beliefs
Accusations of Romanism were common in American religious debates of the early 20th century.
During the 19th century, some writers in Britain harshly criticized Romanism in political pamphlets.
The term Romanism is now rarely used except in historical contexts.
