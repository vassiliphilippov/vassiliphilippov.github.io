continuative

noun
a function word linking clauses or sentences
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪnjuˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjʊətɪv / (BrE)
A word, typically a conjunction or particle, used to link words, phrases, clauses, or sentences without being inflected.

Examples

A continuative word
In some languages, a continuative links ideas smoothly without changing form.
She explained that 'but' can serve as a continuative in English grammar.
The word 'and' functions as a continuative in this sentence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conjunction - a word used to connect clauses or sentences
