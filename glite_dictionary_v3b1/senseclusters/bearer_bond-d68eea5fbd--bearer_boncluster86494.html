bearer bond

noun
bond payable to holder, not registered owner
9.0 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÉ›rÉš bÉ‘nd / (AmE), / ËˆbeÉ™rÉ™ bÉ’nd / (BrE)
A bond that is not registered in the owner's name and is payable to whoever holds the physical certificate, often with detachable coupons for interest payments.

Examples

A bearer bond certificate
Because bearer bonds are not registered to an owner, they can be easily transferred or lost.
Interest payments on a bearer bond are collected by presenting its coupons to the issuer.
The thief stole a suitcase full of bearer bonds from the vault.
