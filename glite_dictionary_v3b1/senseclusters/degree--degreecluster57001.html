degree

noun
highest power in an algebraic term or polynomial
3.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈgriː/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈgriː/ (BrE)
the highest power to which the variable or term in an algebraic expression or polynomial is raised

Examples

Polynomial degree four
In the polynomial 3x^4 - 2x + 7, the degree is four.
The degree of a constant term is considered to be zero.
To solve this equation, first determine the degree of the variable.
