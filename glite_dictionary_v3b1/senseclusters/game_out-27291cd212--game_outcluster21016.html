game out

verb
plan or strategize by considering scenarios
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪm aʊt / (AmE), / ɡeɪm aʊt / (BrE)
to plan or strategize by considering possible scenarios or outcomes, often through detailed discussion or simulation

Examples

To game out scenarios
Before the presentation, the team decided to game out possible questions from the audience.
The consultants gamed out several strategies before making their recommendation.
We should game out different responses in case the situation changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: improvise - to perform without preparation or planning
  • synonym: plan - to make arrangements for something
