proportionalism

noun
ethical decision theory
9.5
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɔrʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the principle or theory that actions, decisions, or penalties should be balanced and appropriate to their circumstances or consequences

Examples

Proportionalism in ethics
Some philosophers argue that proportionalism offers a more flexible approach to moral dilemmas.
The professor explained how proportionalism differs from strict rule-based ethics.
Debates about proportionalism often arise in discussions of medical ethics.
The judge applied proportionalism to ensure the punishment fit the crime.
Many legal systems value proportionalism when determining appropriate sentences.
Debates about proportionalism often arise in discussions of public policy and justice.
