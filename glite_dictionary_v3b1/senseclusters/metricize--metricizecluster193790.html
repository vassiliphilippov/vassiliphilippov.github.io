metricize

verb
introduce performance metrics
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈme.trɪ.saɪz/ /; / /mə.ˈtrɪ.saɪz/ (AmE), /ˈme.trɪ.saɪz/ /; / /mə.ˈtrɪ.saɪz/ (BrE)
to introduce or apply quantitative metrics to measure, evaluate, or track the performance, progress, or quality of a process, team, or activity.

Examples

To metricize performance
The project team decided to metricize the workflow by tracking KPIs and delivery times weekly.
To improve quality, managers metricized customer support, measuring response times and satisfaction scores monthly.
They began metricizing the hiring process to monitor diversity, time-to-hire and candidate experience.
The city began to metricise its public services, tracking response times and satisfaction scores.
After the audit, the department metricised every workflow to measure efficiency and reduce delays.
Teams are metricising their campaigns now, using clear KPIs to report weekly progress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: measure - to determine the size, amount, or degree of something, often by using numbers or instruments
  • synonym: metricize - apply measurable metrics (American spelling)
  • synonym: quantify - to express or measure the quantity of something, especially in numerical terms
