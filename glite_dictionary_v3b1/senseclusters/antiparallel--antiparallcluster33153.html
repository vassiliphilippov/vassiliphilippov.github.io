antiparallel

adjective
parallel but in opposite directions
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈpær.əˌlɛl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈpær.ə.lɛl / (BrE)
(of two or more vectors or lines) parallel to each other but oriented in opposite directions.

Examples

Antiparallel vectors
He explained that the force vectors are antiparallel, pointing in opposite directions.
In DNA, the two strands run in an antiparallel configuration.
The two magnetic field lines in the diagram are antiparallel to each other.
