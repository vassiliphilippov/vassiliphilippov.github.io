satisfiability

noun
logical truth possibility
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsætɪsfaɪəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌsætɪsfaɪəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the property of a logical formula or statement that can be made true by assigning appropriate values to its variables

Examples

Satisfiability of a formula
The satisfiability of complex logical formulas is a key topic in computer science.
Researchers developed new algorithms to test the satisfiability of mathematical statements.
Understanding satisfiability helps in solving many problems in artificial intelligence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unsatisfiability - the property of a logical formula that cannot be made true by any assignment of values
  • synonym: validity - the quality of being logically or factually sound
