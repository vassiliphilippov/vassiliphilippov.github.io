Aeolus

proper noun
god of the winds in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈiːələs / (AmE), / ˈiːələs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the winds, who was said to control the storms and breezes.

Examples

Aeolus god of winds
Artistic depictions often show Aeolus surrounded by swirling gusts of wind.
In Homer’s Odyssey, Aeolus gives Odysseus a bag containing all the winds except the west wind.
The ancient Greeks believed that Aeolus could unleash storms at sea.
