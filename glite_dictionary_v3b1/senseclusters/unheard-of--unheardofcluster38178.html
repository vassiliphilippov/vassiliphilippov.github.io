unheard-of

adjective
previously unknown; unprecedented
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhɝd ˌɑv / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɜːd ˌɒv / (BrE)
Previously unknown or not known to have happened before; unprecedented.

Examples

An unheard-of event
It would be unheard-of for him to miss a meeting without calling ahead.
Such generosity was almost unheard-of in their small town.
The chef created an unheard-of combination of flavors for the new dish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unprecedented - never done or known before
