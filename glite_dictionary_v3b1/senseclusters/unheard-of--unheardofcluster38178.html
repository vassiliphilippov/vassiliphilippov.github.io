unheard-of

adjective
previously unknown; unprecedented
6.7 2.58/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàh…ùd Àå…ëv / (AmE), /  ånÀàh…úÀêd Àå…ív / (BrE)
Previously unknown or not known to have happened before; unprecedented.

Examples

An unheard-of event
It would be unheard-of for him to miss a meeting without calling ahead.
Such generosity was almost unheard-of in their small town.
The chef created an unheard-of combination of flavors for the new dish.

Synonyms

    6.3 19/m

  • undreamed of
    7.5 0.06/m

  • unimagined
    7.0 0.17/m

  • unthought-of
    7.9 0.01/m

  • novel
    5.1 12/m
    novel means new and different in a useful way, while unheard-of means something never known or seen before.
