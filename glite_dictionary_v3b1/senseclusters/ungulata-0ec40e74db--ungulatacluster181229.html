Ungulata

noun
former order of hoofed mammals
9.9
Pronunciation: /ʌŋ.ˈguː.lə.tə/ /; / /ʌŋ.ˈgjuː.lə.tə/ (AmE), /ʌŋ.ˈguː.lə.tə/ /; / /ʌŋ.ˈgjuː.lə.tə/ (BrE)
(in former classifications) a major division of mammals comprising all hoofed mammals, now divided into Perissodactyla and Artiodactyla.

Examples

The Ungulata group
In the 19th century, many textbooks listed all hoofed animals as members of the Ungulata.
Modern zoologists divide former Ungulata into two separate orders: Perissodactyla and Artiodactyla.
The classification of Ungulata has changed as scientific understanding has advanced.
