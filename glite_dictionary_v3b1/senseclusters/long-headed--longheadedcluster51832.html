long-headed

adjective
having a long, narrow head
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
having a head that is longer than it is wide; dolichocephalic.

Examples

A long-headed shape
In both humans and animals, being long-headed can have significance in genetic studies.
Some ancient European populations were predominantly long-headed according to anthropological classifications.
The long-headed skull shape is contrasted with round-headed or brachycephalic types.

  • synonym: dolichocephalic - having a relatively long head
