nonvascular

adjective
without transport tissues
7.9
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈvæskjələr / (AmE), / nɒnˈvæskjʊlə / (BrE)
describing plants that lack specialized tissues for transporting water and nutrients

Examples

Nonvascular plants
Mosses and liverworts are common examples of nonvascular plants found in damp environments.
Unlike ferns, nonvascular plants do not have true roots, stems, or leaves.
Scientists study nonvascular species to understand the early evolution of land plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vascular - having specialized tissues for transporting water and nutrients
