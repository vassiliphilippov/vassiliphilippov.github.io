clear out

verb
empty completely
1.0
Pronunciation: / klɪr aʊt / (AmE), / klɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to remove everything from a place so that it is empty, or to leave a place and take all your belongings

Examples

To clear out a room
She cleared out her old files to make space on her computer.
The landlord asked the tenants to clear out by the end of the month.
We need to clear out the garage before we move.
After the tenants cleared out, the apartment was left completely empty.
She cleared out her office when she decided to quit her job.
We're planning to clear out by the end of the week and hand over the keys.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clean out - to remove all contents from a place
  • synonym: move out - to leave one's place of residence and take possessions
