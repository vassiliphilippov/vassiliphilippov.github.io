red oak

noun
a group of American oak trees with bristled leaves and red wood
Pronunciation: / rɛd oʊk / (AmE), / rɛd əʊk / (BrE)
any of several North American oak trees with pointed or bristled leaf lobes, acorns that take two years to mature, and reddish wood, including species such as Quercus rubra.

Examples

Red oak wood
Carpenters often use red oak wood for making high-quality furniture.
Several red oak trees lined the path in the city park.
The red oak is known for its beautiful autumn foliage and strong timber.

Related Terms

  • antonym: white oak - a group of oak trees with rounded lobes and acorns maturing in one year
