conceitedly

adverb
in a conceited or proud manner
5.7 5.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈsitɪdli / (AmE), / kənˈsiːtɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows excessive pride in oneself or one's abilities; in a conceited manner.

Examples

To act conceitedly
He walked conceitedly into the room, expecting everyone to notice him.
She spoke conceitedly about her latest achievements, unaware of how she came across.
The actor conceitedly dismissed the criticism as jealousy from less talented people.

  • antonym: modestly - in a modest or humble manner
