postdate

verb
to date later than actual
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to write a date on a document (such as a check or contract) that is later than the actual date of writing

Examples

To postdate a check
He decided to postdate the contract so it would take effect next month.
It is illegal in some places to postdate a check to delay payment.
The accountant advised her not to postdate any official documents.
She decided to postdate the contract so it would take effect next month.
The bank will not process a postdated check until the written date arrives.
If you postdate the letter, it may cause confusion about when it was actually sent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antedate - to put an earlier date on a document than the actual date
