indistinctly

adverb
in a vague or unclear way
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktli / (BrE)
in a way that is not clear or easy to see, hear, or understand; vaguely or unclearly.

Examples

To see indistinctly
She could only remember indistinctly what had happened that night.
The speaker's words were heard indistinctly at the back of the hall.
Through the heavy fog, the outline of the house appeared indistinctly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to perceive or understand
  • synonym: vaguely - in a way that is uncertain, indefinite, or unclear
