siren

noun
mythical creature luring sailors
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪrən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪərən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a sea nymph with the head and upper body of a woman and the lower body of a bird, who lured sailors to shipwreck with her enchanting music and singing.

Examples

Mythical siren song
Ancient myths often described sirens as beautiful yet deadly, tempting men toward dangerous rocks.
In Homer’s Odyssey, Odysseus bravely resisted the call of the siren by having himself tied to the mast.
The tale warned sailors of the siren’s seductive song echoing over the waves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mermaid - a mythical sea creature with the head and upper body of a woman and the tail of a fish
