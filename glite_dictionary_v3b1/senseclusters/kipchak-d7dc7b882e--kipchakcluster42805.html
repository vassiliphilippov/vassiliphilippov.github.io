Kipchak

proper noun
Turkic people or language
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪptʃæk / (AmE), / ˈkɪptʃæk / (BrE)
a member of a medieval Turkic people of the Eurasian steppes, or the language they spoke

Examples

A Kipchak warrior
The Kipchak confederation played a major role in Eurasian history.
Many modern Turkic languages have roots in the Kipchak language.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts belonging to the ancient Kipchak people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cuman - a member of a related Turkic people in Eastern Europe
  • synonym: Turkic - relating to the family of peoples and languages including Kipchak
