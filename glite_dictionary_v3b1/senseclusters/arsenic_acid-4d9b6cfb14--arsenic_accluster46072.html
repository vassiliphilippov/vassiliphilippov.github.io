arsenic acid

noun
a water-soluble acid derived from arsenic
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrsənɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈɑːsənɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline, water-soluble acid, H₃AsO₄, formed by dissolving arsenic pentoxide in water, used in chemical manufacturing and as a reagent

Examples

Arsenic acid solution
Arsenic acid is used as a reagent in some chemical synthesizing processes.
Exposure to arsenic acid can pose serious health risks if proper safety measures are not followed.
The laboratory handled arsenic acid with extreme caution due to its toxicity.
