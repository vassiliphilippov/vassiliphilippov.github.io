purse seine

noun
large net for encircling fish
9.3 8.8
Pronunciation: / pɝs seɪn / (AmE), / pɜːs seɪn / (BrE)
a large fishing net that is set around a school of fish by two boats and closed at the bottom with a line to encircle the catch

Examples

A purse seine net
A purse seine can be several hundred meters long, encircling large groups of fish at once.
Modern fisheries often rely on the purse seine for efficient mass catches.
The crew launched the purse seine to capture the entire school of tuna.
