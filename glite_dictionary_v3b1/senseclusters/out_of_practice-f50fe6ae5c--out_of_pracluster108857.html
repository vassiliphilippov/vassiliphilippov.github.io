out of practice

adjective
having lost skill due to lack of use
3.6
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ˈpræktɪs / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈpræktɪs / (BrE)
Having lost proficiency in a skill due to lack of recent use or exercise.

Examples

Out of practice at piano
After years away from the piano, she found herself out of practice.
I'm a bit out of practice with my Spanish, so please speak slowly.
The team is clearly out of practice after the off-season break.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practiced - skilled due to practice or experience
  • synonym: rusty - lacking skill due to disuse
