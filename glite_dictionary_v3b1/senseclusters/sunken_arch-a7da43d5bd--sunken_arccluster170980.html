sunken arch

noun
flattened foot arch; flatfoot
8.5 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌŋkən ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌŋkən ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
A condition in which the arch of the foot is flattened, causing the entire sole to touch the ground; also known as flatfoot.

Examples

A sunken arch foot
After years of standing at work, he developed a sunken arch in his left foot.
People with a sunken arch may experience pain in their feet and ankles.
Supportive insoles are often recommended to help those with a sunken arch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flatfoot - a condition in which the arch of the foot collapses; the entire sole touches the ground
