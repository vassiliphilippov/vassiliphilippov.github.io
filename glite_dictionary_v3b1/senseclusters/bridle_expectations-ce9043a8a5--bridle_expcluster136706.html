bridle expectations

verb
control or restrain expectations
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪdəl ˌɛkspɛkˈteɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdəl ˌɛkspɛkˈteɪʃənz / (BrE)
to control or restrain one's expectations to avoid disappointment or unrealistic hopes

Examples

To bridle expectations carefully
He learned to bridle expectations after several of his projects fell through.
Investors need to bridle expectations in a volatile market.
You should bridle your expectations before you get too excited about the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manage expectations - to ensure expectations are realistic and achievable
  • antonym: raise expectations - to make expectations higher or greater
