tut

verb
make a disapproving clicking sound
7.1
Pronunciation: / tʌt / (AmE), / tʌt / (BrE)
to make a short, disapproving sound with your tongue to show annoyance or disapproval

Examples

To tut in annoyance
He walked past the broken vase and couldn't help but tut in annoyance.
She tutted at the mess on the kitchen table, clearly displeased.
The teacher tutted when the class was noisy.
The teacher began tutting when the students whispered during the exam.
He tutted at the mess in the kitchen but cleaned it up anyway.
She couldn't help tutting every time someone was late to the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approve - to show agreement or positive regard
  • synonym: cluck - to make a short clicking sound with the tongue, often to express annoyance
  • synonym: tsk - to make a similar sound of disapproval with the tongue
