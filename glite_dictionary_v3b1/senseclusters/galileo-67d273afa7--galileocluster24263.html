Galileo

proper noun
Italian astronomer and physicist (1564–1642)
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæləˈleɪoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈleɪəʊ / (BrE)
(1564–1642) Italian astronomer, physicist, and mathematician who pioneered the use of the telescope in astronomy and made major scientific discoveries.

Examples

Galileo Galilei
Galileo was placed under house arrest for supporting the heliocentric theory.
Many scientists view Galileo as the father of modern observational science.
The invention of the telescope by Galileo revolutionized astronomy.
