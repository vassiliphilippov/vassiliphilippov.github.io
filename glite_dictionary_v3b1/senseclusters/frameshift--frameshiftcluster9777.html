frameshift

noun
genetic mutation type
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfreɪmˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈfreɪmˌʃɪft / (BrE)
a genetic mutation caused by the insertion or deletion of DNA bases that alters the reading frame of a gene

Examples

A frameshift mutation
A single frameshift in the DNA sequence can disrupt the entire protein structure.
Scientists discovered a frameshift mutation responsible for the rare genetic disorder.
The insertion of an extra base caused a frameshift that led to a nonfunctional enzyme.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutation - a change in the DNA sequence of a gene
  • antonym: point mutation - a genetic mutation affecting a single nucleotide
