stomach

noun
appetite for food
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
the desire, appetite, or courage to do or deal with something difficult or unpleasant

Examples

No stomach for danger
After a long hike, she had a strong stomach for supper.
Children often have a bigger stomach for sweets than adults.
He found his stomach was gone after hearing the bad news.
Despite the harsh criticism, she showed plenty of stomach for defending her work in public.
He found he had no stomach for office politics and avoided confrontations whenever possible.
The coach questioned whether the player had the stomach to endure a tough season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appetite - a natural desire to satisfy a bodily need, especially for food
  • antonym: aversion - strong dislike or unwillingness
