whish

verb
move quickly with a soft rush sound
Pronunciation: / wɪʃ / (AmE), / wɪʃ / (BrE)
to move quickly, making a soft, sweeping or rushing sound

Examples

Birds whish past
A flock of birds whished overhead as we walked by the river.
The arrow whished past the target, missing it by inches.
The train whished through the tunnel, leaving only a faint echo.
A gust of wind whished through the trees at night, stirring the branches.
She listened as the silk curtain whished softly against the window frame.
The arrow whished past my ear, barely missing me.

  • synonym: whoosh - move quickly with a rushing sound
