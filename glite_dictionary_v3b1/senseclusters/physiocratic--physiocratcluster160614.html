physiocratic

adjective
about Physiocrat theory
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪzi.oʊˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪzi.əˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
relating to the economic theories of the Physiocrats, who believed agriculture is the main source of a nation's wealth

Examples

Physiocratic economic theory
The professor explained the physiocratic belief that only agriculture produces true wealth.
Her research focused on physiocratic influences in eighteenth-century French economic policy.
A physiocratic approach would prioritize farming over manufacturing in national planning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mercantilist - relating to the economic theory that trade and accumulation of wealth are central
  • synonym: physiocracy - an economic theory emphasizing agriculture as the source of wealth
