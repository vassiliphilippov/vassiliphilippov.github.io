patter

noun
quick, light, repeated sound
Pronunciation: / ˈpætər / (AmE), / ˈpætə / (BrE)
a quick series of light, rapid sounds, often made by small objects or animals moving or hitting a surface

Examples

The patter of rain
Rain began to patter against the rooftop as the storm rolled in.
She heard the patter of her dog's paws on the wooden floor.
The quiet patter of leaves in the wind soothed him to sleep.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tap - a light, quick sound or touch
  • antonym: thud - a dull, heavy sound made by an object falling or hitting something
