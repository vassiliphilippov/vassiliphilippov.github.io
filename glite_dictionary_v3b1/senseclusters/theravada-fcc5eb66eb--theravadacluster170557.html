Theravada

noun
major conservative school of Buddhism
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrəˈvɑdə / (AmE), / ˌtɛːrəˈvɑːdə / (BrE)
the more conservative of the two main schools of Buddhism, emphasizing personal salvation through individual effort, strict adherence to the Pali Canon, and the attainment of nirvana; predominant in countries such as Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, Laos, and Cambodia

Examples

Theravada Buddhism
Theravada Buddhism is practiced by the majority of people in Thailand.
He became interested in Theravada meditation techniques after his trip to Myanmar.
Scholars often compare the teachings of Theravada and Mahayana traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Mahayana - the other main school of Buddhism, generally considered more liberal and inclusive
