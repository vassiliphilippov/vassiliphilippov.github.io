critical angle

noun
the smallest angle for total reflection of light
3.0 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɪt.ɪ.kəl ˈæŋ.gəl/ /; / /ˈkrɪt.ɪ.kəl ˈeɪŋ.gəl/ (AmE), /ˈkrɪt.ɪ.kəl ˈæŋ.gəl/ /; / /ˈkrɪt.ɪ.kəl ˈeɪŋ.gəl/ (BrE)
the smallest angle of incidence at which light is completely reflected at the boundary between two media, causing total internal reflection

Examples

The critical angle of light
Beyond the critical angle, no refracted ray emerges into the second medium.
Scientists calculated the critical angle for the interface between water and air.
The critical angle determines when light will be totally reflected inside the glass.
