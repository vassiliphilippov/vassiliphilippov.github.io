Dawson City

proper noun
historic town in Yukon, Canada
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔsən ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈdɔːsən ˈsɪti / (BrE)
A town in northwestern Yukon, Canada, known for its central role in the Klondike Gold Rush of the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

Dawson City gold rush
During the Klondike Gold Rush, Dawson City grew rapidly as prospectors flocked to the area.
Many tourists visit Dawson City each year to learn about its gold rush heritage.
The historic buildings of Dawson City have been carefully preserved as a reminder of its colorful past.
