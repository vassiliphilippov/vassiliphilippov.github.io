anglaise

noun
egg and breadcrumb coating
Pronunciation: / ɑ̃ˈɡleɪz / (AmE), / ɑ̃ˈɡleɪz / (BrE)
a cooking method in which food is coated in egg and breadcrumbs before frying

Examples

Anglaise coating method
The chef used the anglaise method to prepare the crispy chicken cutlets.
Before frying, the vegetables were coated using the anglaise technique for extra crunch.
Many classic dishes call for the anglaise process to achieve a golden, crisp exterior.
