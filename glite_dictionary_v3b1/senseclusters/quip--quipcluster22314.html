quip

verb
make a witty or clever remark
Pronunciation: / kwɪp / (AmE), / kwɪp / (BrE)
to say something witty or clever, often as a joke; to make a witty remark

Examples

To quip about work
"At least my coffee's still hot," she quipped after the power went out.
She couldn't help but quip about the weather during the dull meeting.
Whenever the conversation grew tense, Mark would quip to lighten the mood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jest - to speak in a joking manner
  • synonym: joke - to say something amusing or humorous
