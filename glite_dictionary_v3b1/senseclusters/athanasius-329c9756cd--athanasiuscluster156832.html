Athanasius

proper noun
patriarch of Alexandria, early Christian theologian
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈneɪziəs / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈneɪziəs / (BrE)
(c. 293–373) Greek patriarch of Alexandria, prominent Christian theologian, and influential defender of Christian orthodoxy against Arianism; recognized as a church father and saint in the Roman Catholic Church.

Examples

Athanasius of Alexandria
Athanasius played a pivotal role in establishing Christian orthodoxy during the early Church councils.
Many churches commemorate Saint Athanasius for his defense against Arianism.
The teachings of Athanasius continue to influence Christian theology today.
