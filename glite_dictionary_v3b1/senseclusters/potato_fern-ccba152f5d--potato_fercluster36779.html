potato fern

noun
South American epiphytic fern with tuberous rhizomes
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪtoʊ fɜrn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ fɜːn / (BrE)
a small epiphytic fern native to South America, characterized by tuberous swellings along its rhizomes

Examples

Tuberous potato fern
Gardeners prize the potato fern for its unique tuberous rhizomes, which resemble small potatoes.
Researchers studied the potato fern to understand how its tubers help it survive in challenging environments.
The potato fern is commonly found growing on tree branches in South American rainforests.
