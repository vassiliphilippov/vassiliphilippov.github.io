liquidus

noun
phase diagram line
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdəs / (BrE)
the temperature or line on a phase diagram showing when a substance is completely liquid

Examples

Liquidus temperature
The liquidus curve helps metallurgists determine the melting behavior of alloys.
At temperatures above the liquidus, the material is fully molten and can be cast into molds.
Understanding the liquidus is essential for controlling the solidification process in manufacturing.
The liquidus of this metal alloy is higher than its solidus, indicating a range of partial melting.
Engineers must know the liquidus to ensure the material is fully melted during processing.
At the liquidus temperature, the last solid particle dissolves and the substance becomes entirely liquid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melting point - the temperature at which a solid becomes a liquid
  • antonym: solidus - the line on a phase diagram below which a substance is completely solid
