electron beam

noun
focused stream of electrons
4.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛk.trɑn bim / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn biːm / (BrE)
A stream of electrons moving together in a focused, nearly parallel group, used in devices like cathode ray tubes and electron microscopes.

Examples

A focused electron beam
A focused electron beam is essential for operating electron microscopes effectively.
Scientists adjusted the intensity of the electron beam for more precise imaging.
The electron beam was directed at the material to study its internal structure.
