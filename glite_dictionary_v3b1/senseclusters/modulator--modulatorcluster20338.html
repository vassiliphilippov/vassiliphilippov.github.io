modulator

noun
signal control device
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdʒəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a device or person that changes or controls signals, especially in communication systems

Examples

Signal modulator
The technician installed a new modulator to improve the quality of the television signal.
A modulator is essential in radio broadcasting to combine audio with carrier waves.
Without a functioning modulator, the communication system could not transmit data properly.
The technician adjusted the modulator to ensure the broadcast signal was clear.
A digital modulator is essential in modern communication systems.
She learned how a modulator can affect the quality of audio transmissions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demodulator - a device that extracts information from a modulated signal
  • synonym: transmitter - a device that sends out signals or data
