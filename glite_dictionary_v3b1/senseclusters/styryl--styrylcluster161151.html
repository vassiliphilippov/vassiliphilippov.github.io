styryl

noun
styrene-derived group
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪrɪl / (AmE), / ˈstaɪrɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group or radical derived from styrene, consisting of a phenyl group attached to a vinyl group (C6H5CH=CH–)

Examples

Styryl group
The styryl group is important in the synthesis of certain dyes and pharmaceuticals.
Researchers studied the reactivity of the styryl radical under different laboratory conditions.
Adding a styryl substituent can change the properties of the original compound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vinyl - a chemical group derived from ethylene (–CH=CH2)
