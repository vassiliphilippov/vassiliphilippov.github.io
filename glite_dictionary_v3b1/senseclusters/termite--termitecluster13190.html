termite

noun
wood-eating social insect
1.6 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtɜːr.maɪt/ (AmE), /ˈtɜː.maɪt/ (BrE)
a small, pale, soft-bodied social insect that feeds on wood and is known for causing damage to wooden structures

Examples

A termite mound
Termite mounds in Africa can reach impressive heights and house millions of insects.
A colony of termites can cause severe damage to a wooden house if left unchecked.
The wooden beams were infested with termites, so the owner called pest control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ant - a social insect, often confused with termites, but belonging to a different family
  • degree higher: cockroach - another insect related to termites but usually not wood-eating
