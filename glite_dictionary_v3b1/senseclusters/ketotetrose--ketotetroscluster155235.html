ketotetrose

noun
a four-carbon ketone sugar
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːtoʊˈtɛtroʊs / (AmE), / ˌkɛtəʊˈtɛtrəʊz / (BrE)
A monosaccharide sugar containing a ketone group and four carbon atoms.

Examples

A ketotetrose sugar
Researchers often study the structure and function of ketotetroses in carbohydrate chemistry.
The simplest ketotetrose found in nature is erythrulose.
Unlike an aldotetrose, a ketotetrose contains its carbonyl group at the second carbon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aldotetrose - a four-carbon sugar containing an aldehyde group
  • degree higher: ketopentose - a five-carbon monosaccharide with a ketone group
