necrotizing enteritis

noun
severe intestinal inflammation causing tissue death
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛkrəˌtaɪzɪŋ ˌɛntəˈraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛkrəʊˌtaɪzɪŋ ˌɛntəˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A severe inflammation of the intestine marked by tissue death, bloody diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain.

Examples

Severe necrotizing enteritis
Early intervention is crucial for treating necrotizing enteritis to prevent serious complications or death.
Outbreaks of necrotizing enteritis have been associated with contaminated food and poor sanitation.
The patient was diagnosed with necrotizing enteritis after experiencing severe abdominal pain and bloody diarrhea.
