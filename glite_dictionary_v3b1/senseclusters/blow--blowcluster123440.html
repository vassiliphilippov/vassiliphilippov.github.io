blow

verb
spend money extravagantly
Pronunciation: / bloʊ / (AmE), / bləʊ / (BrE)
to spend or waste money, opportunities, or chances carelessly

Examples

To blow your money
He blew all his savings on a luxury car he hardly drives.
It's easy to blow your budget if you don't keep track of your spending.
Whenever she wins money, she tends to blow it on expensive clothes and gadgets.
Don't blow your chance to make a good impression at the meeting.
He was hoping for a promotion, but he blew his interview by arriving late.
She blew the exam by forgetting to answer several questions.
He accidentally blew their cover by talking too much at the party.
Her careless remark blew the secret operation.
Once the information was leaked, the entire plan was blown.

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
  • synonym: expose - to make something visible, typically hidden or secret
  • antonym: fix - to repair or make right, opposite of spoil
  • synonym: screw up - to make a serious mistake or mess up a situation (informal)
  • synonym: squander - to waste money, opportunities, or resources recklessly
  • synonym: suck - to be extremely bad or unpleasant (slang)
  • synonym: waste - to use or expend carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose
