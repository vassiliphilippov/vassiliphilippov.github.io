watering hole

noun
compromised website used to target users
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər.ɪŋ hoʊl/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər.ɪŋ hoʊl/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə.rɪŋ həʊl/ (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) a website that has been compromised by attackers in order to target a particular group of users, often to deliver malware or steal credentials.

Examples

Watering hole attack
Security researchers warned of a watering hole attack that infected dozens of employee laptops last month.
They discovered a watering hole on a popular forum where the attackers planted a malicious download.
After the incident, several watering holes frequented by financial journalists were taken offline for investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malicious website - a website created or altered to distribute malware, phish credentials, or otherwise harm visitors
  • synonym: watering hole attack - a cyberattack in which attackers compromise websites to infect or monitor targeted visitors
