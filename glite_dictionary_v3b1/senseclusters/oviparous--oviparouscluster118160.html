oviparous

adjective
egg-laying (biology)
6.3 9.6
Pronunciation: / oʊˈvɪpərəs / (AmE), / əʊˈvɪpərəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) producing young by laying eggs, which then hatch outside the body.

Examples

Oviparous animals lay eggs
Birds are a classic example of oviparous animals.
Most reptiles are oviparous and lay their eggs on land.
The oviparous frog deposits its eggs in water, where they develop into tadpoles.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ovoviviparous - producing eggs that hatch within the body, so young are born live
  • antonym: viviparous - giving birth to live young instead of laying eggs
