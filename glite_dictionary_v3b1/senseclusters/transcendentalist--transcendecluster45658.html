transcendentalist

noun
advocate of transcendentalism
8.3
Pronunciation: / trænˌsɛnˈdɛntəlɪst / (AmE), / trænˌsɛnˈdɛntəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or advocates the philosophy of transcendentalism, especially as developed in 19th-century New England

Examples

A transcendentalist thinker
As a devoted transcendentalist, she rejected materialism in favor of spiritual values.
Ralph Waldo Emerson is widely regarded as a leading transcendentalist of his time.
The lecture explored the influence of transcendentalist ideas on American literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transcendentalism - the philosophical movement that emphasizes the spiritual over the material
