superintend

verb
to oversee or direct; manage
5.7 5.6
Pronunciation: /suː.pə.rɪn.ˈtend/ (AmE), /suː.pə.rɪn.ˈtend/ (BrE)
to be in charge of and direct the work or activities of others; to oversee and manage a process or group

Examples

To superintend the work
It is his job to superintend the budget and ensure everything runs smoothly.
She was hired to superintend the construction of the new building.
The principal will superintend the final exams this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oversee - to watch over and direct a process or activity
  • synonym: supervise - to direct and closely monitor the work of others
