conglutinate

verb
to cause to stick together
6.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈgluːtɪˌneɪt /; / kənˈɡluːtɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / kɒnˈgluːtɪˌneɪt /; / kɒnˈɡluːtɪneɪt / (BrE)
to stick things together or to become stuck together, especially by something sticky

Examples

To conglutinate cells
Certain proteins can conglutinate cells, forming larger clumps.
The surgeon skillfully caused the tissues to conglutinate after the operation.
When dried, the sap helps to conglutinate the bark pieces together.
After the surgery, the doctor expected the wound edges to conglutinate over time.
Certain substances make the small particles conglutinate into larger clusters.
When exposed to heat, the plastic sheets began to conglutinate and form a single layer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adhere - to stick fast or cleave; to attach firmly to something else
  • antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to stop being joined together
