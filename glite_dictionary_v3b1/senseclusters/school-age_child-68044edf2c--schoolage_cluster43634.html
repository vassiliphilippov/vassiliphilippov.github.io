school-age child

noun
child old enough for school
1.3 0.84/m
Pronunciation: / skuÀêl e…™d í t Éa…™ld / (AmE), / skuÀêl e…™d í t Éa…™ld / (BrE)
a child of an age at which children generally attend school, typically between early childhood and adolescence

Examples

A school-age child
After moving to a new city, their school-age child adjusted quickly to the local elementary school.
The government provides free lunches to every school-age child in public schools.
Vaccinations are recommended for every school-age child before the start of the academic year.

Synonyms

  • schoolchild
    1.0 3.04/m
    schoolchild is a child who attends primary or secondary school, while school-age child refers to any child of school-going age.
  • pupil
    1.0 9.34/m
    pupil is a student often used in school settings, while school-age child emphasizes the child's age whether they attend or not.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: toddler - a young child who is just beginning to walk
