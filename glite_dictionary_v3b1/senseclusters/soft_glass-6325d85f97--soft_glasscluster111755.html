soft glass

noun
soft, amorphous material with glass-like rheology
Pronunciation: / sɑft ɡlæs / (AmE), / sɒft ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
(in physics and materials science) an amorphous soft material that behaves like a glassy solid, showing slow relaxation, aging, and yielding; examples include dense colloidal suspensions, concentrated emulsions, and some foams.

Examples

Soft glass in physics
Physicists model how soft glass flows under stress in colloidal suspensions.
Some concentrated emulsions behave like a soft glass and slowly relax after deformation.
The team observed aging in a sample of soft glass after repeated shear cycles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colloidal glass - a dense suspension of particles that becomes kinetically arrested and behaves like a glass
  • synonym: soft glassy material - a technical term for soft matter systems that exhibit glass-like rheological behaviour
