xenolith

noun
rock fragment embedded in igneous rock
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈzɛnəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈzɛnəlɪθ / (BrE)
(Geology) a fragment of rock that is distinct in origin and composition from the igneous rock in which it is embedded.

Examples

A xenolith fragment
A basalt flow can often enclose xenoliths from the surrounding bedrock.
Geologists studied the xenoliths to understand the region's geologic history.
The granite outcrop contained a xenolith that was easily identifiable due to its darker color.
