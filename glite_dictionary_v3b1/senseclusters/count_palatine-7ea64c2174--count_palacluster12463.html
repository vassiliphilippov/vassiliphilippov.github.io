count palatine

noun
nobleman with royal powers in his region
Pronunciation: / kaʊnt ˌpæl.əˈtaɪn / (AmE), / kaʊnt ˌpæ.ləˈteɪn / (BrE)
A nobleman who exercised royal authority in his own domain, often as a ruler of a palatinate in medieval Europe.

Examples

A count palatine ruler
Several counts palatine played significant roles in the politics of medieval Germany.
The count palatine could exercise judicial powers independently of the king.
The count palatine wielded considerable authority over his principality.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: duke - a nobleman of high rank below a monarch
  • synonym: palatine - a nobleman entrusted with royal powers in his territory
