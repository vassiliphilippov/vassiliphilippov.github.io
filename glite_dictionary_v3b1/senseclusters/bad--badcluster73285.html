bad

adjective
serious or severe (situation, condition)
1.0
Pronunciation: / bæd / (AmE), / bad /; / bæd / (BrE)
of poor quality, low standard, or not good; undesirable or below what is expected

Examples

A bad accident
He was in a bad car accident and needed surgery.
She suffered a bad headache that lasted all day.
The city was hit by a bad storm last night.
It was a bad decision to travel during the storm.
She felt bad about forgetting her friend's birthday.
The meal at the restaurant was bad and did not meet our expectations.
He is known as a bad driver because he often makes careless mistakes on the road.
She received a bad grade on her math test because she didn't study enough.
The restaurant had a bad reputation for its slow service and bland food.

Related Terms

  • antonym: good - of high quality; desirable or positive
  • degree lower: mild - not severe or intense (often used with discomfort or phenomena)
  • synonym: poor - of low quality or standard
  • degree higher: terrible - extremely bad or unpleasant
