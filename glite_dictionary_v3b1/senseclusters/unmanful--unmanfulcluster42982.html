unmanful

adjective
lacking traditionally manly qualities
5.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmænfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmanfʊl / (BrE)
Not possessing the qualities traditionally regarded as befitting a man, such as courage or strength.

Examples

An unmanful act
His unmanful response to the crisis surprised his colleagues.
Some cultures discourage expressions that are considered unmanful in men.
The novel critiques characters whose actions are seen as unmanful by society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manful - having qualities befitting a man, such as bravery or strength
