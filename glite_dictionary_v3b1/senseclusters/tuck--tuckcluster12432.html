tuck

noun
a straight, narrow-bladed sword
5.4
Pronunciation: / tʌk / (AmE), / tʌk / (BrE)
a straight sword with a narrow, double-edged blade used primarily in the 16th and 17th centuries.

Examples

A tuck sword
Collectors prize the tuck for its elegant, narrow blade and historical significance.

The knight drew his tuck and prepared for battle.
