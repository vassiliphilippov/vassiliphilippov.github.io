kitschy

adjective
excessively sentimental or gaudy
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkɪt.ʃiː/ (AmE), /ˈkɪt.ʃiː/ (BrE)
Excessively sentimental or gaudy, in a way that is considered to be in poor taste or lacking in subtlety.

Examples

A kitschy movie
Critics described the movie as kitschy but oddly charming.
She collects kitschy souvenirs from every country she visits, much to her friends' amusement.
The decor was so kitschy that it felt like stepping into a theme park from the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tacky - showing poor taste and quality
  • antonym: tasteful - showing good aesthetic judgment
