short-fetched

adjective
improbable, implausible
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrt ˌfɛtʃt / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt ˌfɛtʃt / (BrE)
(of an idea or explanation) improbable and unlikely to be true; implausible.

Examples

A short-fetched idea
Her explanation was a bit short-fetched for my liking.
It's a short-fetched claim that lacks supporting evidence.
The committee rejected the proposal as too short-fetched to consider seriously.

Related Terms

  • synonym: far-fetched - improbable or unlikely to happen
