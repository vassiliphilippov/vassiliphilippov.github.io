chondrichthian

noun
cartilaginous fish (sharks and rays)
9.6 9.9
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.ˈdrɪk.θiː.ən/ (AmE), /kɒn.ˈdrɪk.θiː.ən/ (BrE)
A member of a class of fishes whose skeletons are made of cartilage rather than bone, including sharks, rays, and skates.

Examples

A chondrichthian fish
Most sharks are classified as chondrichthians because their skeletons are made of cartilage.
Scientists study fossilized teeth to learn about ancient chondrichthians.
Unlike bony fish, chondrichthians do not have true bone in their bodies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: osteichthyan - a bony fish (opposite of chondrichthian)
