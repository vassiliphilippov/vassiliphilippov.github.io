covenant

noun
formal written agreement; treaty

a formal written agreement between two or more parties, especially nations, to do or refrain from doing something

Examples

A legal covenant
Breaking a covenant can have serious legal and diplomatic consequences.
The leaders negotiated every term before finally agreeing to the covenant.
The two countries signed a covenant pledging mutual cooperation and support.

Synonyms

  • treaty
    4.1 31/m
    a treaty is a formal written agreement between countries about peace or trade, while a covenant emphasizes mutual promises to act or refrain.
  • pact
    3.4 8.34/m
    a pact is a usually short agreement between states or leaders for a common goal, while a covenant is more formal and often legal.
  • compact
    6.5 1.72/m
    a compact is a signed agreement often used between states or groups, while a covenant stresses solemn promises and duties.
  • accord
    6.0 4.36/m
    an accord is an agreement reached by negotiation and can be less binding, while a covenant usually carries stronger formal obligations.
  • convention
    4.1 13/m
    a convention is an agreement between many countries that sets general rules, while a covenant often names specific promises between parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement between parties
  • synonym: treaty - a formally concluded and ratified agreement between countries
