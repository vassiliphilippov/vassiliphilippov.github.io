sickle-cell anemia

noun
genetic disorder causing crescent-shaped red blood cells
6.6 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪk.əl sɛl ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /ˈsɪk.əl sɛl ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
A genetic blood disorder, chiefly affecting people of African descent, in which red blood cells become crescent-shaped, causing anemia and other health problems.

Examples

sickle-cell anemia disease
Children with sickle-cell anemia require regular medical check-ups.
Many people with sickle-cell anemia experience episodes of pain known as crises.
Researchers are working on new treatments for sickle-cell anemia.
New treatments have improved the life expectancy of children born with sickle-cell anaemia.
People living with sickle-cell anaemia frequently experience episodes of pain known as sickle cell crises.
She was diagnosed with sickle-cell anaemia after a series of unexplained illnesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anemia - A condition in which the blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • synonym: sickle cell disease - collective term for disorders involving sickle-shaped red blood cells
