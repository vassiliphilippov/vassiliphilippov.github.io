gauge

noun
diameter of tube or barrel
3.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /geɪdʒ/ (AmE), /geɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a standard measurement of size, thickness, or distance, such as the diameter of wire, tube, or the distance between rails

Examples

Wire gauge
Pipes are often classified according to their gauge.
The gauge of the shotgun determines what size ammunition it can use.
The wire's gauge affects its current-carrying capacity.
Differences in gauge can prevent trains from traveling on certain networks.
The museum displays a locomotive with a very narrow gauge.
The railway uses a broad gauge, making it incompatible with some other lines.
The electrician checked the gauge of the wire before installing it.
Thicker wire will have a lower gauge number according to standard measurements.
You need the correct gauge when working with electrical circuits to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caliber - internal diameter of a gun barrel
  • synonym: track gauge - the standard distance between the inner sides of the two parallel rails that make up a railway track
