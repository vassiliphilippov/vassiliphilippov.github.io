rhyolite

noun
acid volcanic rock
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪ.əˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.əˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a light-coloured volcanic rock with a high silica content, typically formed from lava rich in silica and often exhibiting a fine-grained structure

Examples

Layer of rhyolite
Geologists discovered a layer of rhyolite beneath the mountain's summit.
The thin lava flows in the canyon were determined to be composed of rhyolite.
Under the microscope, rhyolite reveals its characteristic fine-grained texture.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: andesite - intermediate volcanic rock
  • degree lower: basalt - basic volcanic rock with lower silica content
  • synonym: granite - coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock similar in composition to rhyolite
