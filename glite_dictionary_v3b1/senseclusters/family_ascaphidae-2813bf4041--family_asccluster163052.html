family Ascaphidae

noun
family of tailed frogs (amphibians)
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli æˌskæfəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli aˌskafɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of amphibians consisting of a single species of frog known as the tailed frog, found in North America.

Examples

The family Ascaphidae
Biologists study the family Ascaphidae to understand the evolution of amphibians.
Only one species belongs to the family Ascaphidae, making it unique among frogs.
The family Ascaphidae contains some of the most primitive frogs alive today.
