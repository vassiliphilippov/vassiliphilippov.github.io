sugar apple

noun
sweet tropical fruit with scaly rind
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʊɡər ˈæpəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ˈapəl / (BrE)
A sweet tropical fruit with a thick, scaly rind and soft, pulpy flesh surrounding shiny black seeds; native to the Americas and widely cultivated in tropical regions.

Examples

Fresh sugar apples
She bought a basket of fresh sugar apples at the market.
The gardener showed us how to separate the seeds from a sugar apple before eating it.
The ripe sugar apple fell from the tree and split open to reveal its sweet flesh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cherimoya - a related tropical fruit with creamy flesh, often confused with sugar apple
  • synonym: custard apple - another name for several similar tropical fruits, sometimes used interchangeably with 'sugar apple'
