monetize

verb
make money from (content, data, attention)
5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.nə.taɪz/ /; / /ˈmʌ.nə.taɪz/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.nə.taɪz/ /; / /ˈmʌ.nə.taɪz/ (BrE)
to earn money from something (such as a website, app, data, or content) by charging, showing ads, selling access, or otherwise converting it into revenue.

Examples

To monetize a website
Many websites monetize their content by showing ads and offering paid subscriptions to users.
After years of free access, the app was monetized and now charges for premium features.
Independent creators are increasingly focused on monetizing their videos through sponsorships and memberships.
Small creators often monetise their videos through ads and paid memberships.
The startup quickly monetised its free app by adding a subscription tier for premium features.
They discussed options for monetising user data while ensuring compliance with privacy laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercialise - to manage or exploit in a way designed to make a profit; similar in meaning when applied to products or services.
  • synonym: commercialize - to organize or manage something so that it makes money
  • synonym: monetize - US spelling variant meaning the same: to generate revenue from something.
  • synonym: profit - to gain money from an activity or resource
