Synonyms

  • soak
    1.0 7.38/m
    soak means to make something very wet by leaving it in water or pouring liquid over it, while drench means covering completely with liquid.
  • douse
    4.4 0.99/m
    douse means to pour liquid quickly over someone or something, while drench suggests making the whole thing completely wet.
  • saturate
    5.2 6.20/m
    saturate means to fill or soak something until it cannot absorb more, while drench focuses on making it completely wet.
  • immerse
    4.2 1.15/m
    immerse means to put or dip something fully into liquid, while drench often means soaking by pouring or heavy rain.
  • souse
    8.8 0.01/m
    souse means to soak or plunge something in liquid, often in a rough or informal way, while drench is more general.
  • sop
    8.3 0.07/m
    sop means to soak or wet something thoroughly, often by letting liquid soak into it, while drench means cover with lots of liquid.
  • waterlog
    3.7 0.01/m
    waterlog means to fill or damage something with water until it cannot absorb more, while drench means make it completely wet.
