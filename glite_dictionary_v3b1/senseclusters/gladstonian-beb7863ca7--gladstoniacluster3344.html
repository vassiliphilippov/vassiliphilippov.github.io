Gladstonian

adjective
of Gladstone's policies
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡlædˈstoʊniən / (AmE), / ɡlædˈstəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the policies, principles, or style of British statesman William Gladstone, especially his emphasis on moral government and liberal reforms

Examples

Gladstonian policies
The new prime minister adopted a Gladstonian approach to social reform and fiscal responsibility.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of Gladstonian policies on British society.
Her speech reflected a Gladstonian belief in moral leadership and public service.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; opposed to change or innovation
  • synonym: liberal - favoring political and social reform, often associated with progressive policies
