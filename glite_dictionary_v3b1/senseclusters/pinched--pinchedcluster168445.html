pinched

adjective
thin or drawn in appearance (face)
Pronunciation: / pɪn(t)ʃt / (AmE), / pɪn(t)ʃt / (BrE)
(of a person's face or features) thin and drawn, often as a result of illness, hunger, or cold.

Examples

Pinched face
Her pinched cheeks spoke of many days without enough to eat.
In the harsh winter, many villagers had pinched faces from the cold.
The child's pinched features worried the doctor during the examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: full-faced - having a face that is broad or rounded
  • synonym: gaunt - very thin and bony, especially from hunger or illness
  • synonym: haggard - looking exhausted and unwell, especially from fatigue or suffering
