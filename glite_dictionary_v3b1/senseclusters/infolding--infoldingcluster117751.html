infolding

noun
the process of folding inward
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of folding inward, especially of an outer layer so as to form a pocket or cavity in a surface

Examples

Infolding of tissue
During embryonic development, infolding of tissue layers can lead to the formation of complex organs.
The infolding of the cell membrane is essential during endocytosis.
This process involves the infolding of the surface to create a protective pouch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invagination - the folding in of a membrane or layer to form a cavity or pouch
