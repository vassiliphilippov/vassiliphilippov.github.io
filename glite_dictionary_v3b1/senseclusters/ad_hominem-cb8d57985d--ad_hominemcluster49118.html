ad hominem

noun
personal attack fallacy
7.4
Pronunciation: /æd ˈhɑː.mɪ.nəm/ /; / /əd ˈhɑː.mɪ.nəm/ (AmE), /æd ˈhɒm.ɪ.nəm/ /; / /əd ˈhɒm.ɪ.nəm/ (BrE)
an argument or attack that targets a person instead of the issue; a logical fallacy where someone attacks the person making an argument rather than the argument itself

Examples

An ad hominem argument
The politician resorted to an ad hominem instead of answering the question directly.
Using an ad hominem weakens your argument and distracts from the real issue.
In debates, it is important to avoid ad hominem attacks and focus on the facts.
Instead of responding to the issue, he made an ad hominem against his opponent.
The debate was filled with ad hominem rather than logical arguments.
She recognized the ad hominem and refused to take the insult personally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appeal to reason - an argument based on logic and evidence
  • synonym: argumentum ad hominem - the logical fallacy of attacking the person instead of the argument
  • synonym: personal attack - an argument that targets a person instead of the issue
  • antonym: rebuttal - a response that addresses the argument directly
