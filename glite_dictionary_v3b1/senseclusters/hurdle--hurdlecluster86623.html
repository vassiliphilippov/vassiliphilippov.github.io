hurdle

noun
obstacle to overcome
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜrdl̩ /; / ˈhɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdl̩ /; / ˈhɜːdəl / (BrE)
a barrier that must be jumped over in a race, or a difficulty or problem that must be overcome

Examples

She overcame every hurdle on her way to starting her own business.
He knocked down one hurdle but still managed to finish in first place.
In the 400-meter race, clearing every hurdle smoothly is crucial for success.
The athletes lined up at the starting blocks, ready to leap over each hurdle on the track.
The athlete cleared every hurdle on the track with ease.
Learning a new language can be a difficult hurdle for many people.
They faced several hurdles before launching their new business.
