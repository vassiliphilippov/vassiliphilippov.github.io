hoped-for

adjective
wanted or expected with hope
3.5 1.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààho äpt f…îr / (AmE), / Ààh…ô äpt f…îÀê / (BrE)
that is wanted or expected, especially with hope or desire for a positive result

Examples

The hoped-for result
After years of hard work, she finally received the hoped-for promotion.
The rain that the farmers had hoped-for arrived just in time to save the crops.
Their hoped-for victory eluded them despite their best efforts.

Synonyms

  • wished-for
    4.2 0.29/m
    wished-for often describes a result people imagine happily, while hoped-for focuses on expecting a positive future outcome.
  • desired
    2.5 0.00/m
    desired expresses strong want or choice used in formal or neutral speech, while hoped-for highlights expectation and hopeful waiting.
  • longed-for
    5.5 0.50/m
    longed-for shows deep, often long desire and emotion, while hoped-for suggests expecting a good result without long suffering.
  • yearned-for
    6.0 1.20/m
    yearned-for feels poetic and shows a strong, aching desire over time, while hoped-for is more about expecting a good outcome.
  • wanted
    1.0 0.00/m
    wanted is a general, neutral word for needing or wishing, while hoped-for adds expectation and a wish for a positive result.

  • synonym: anticipated - regarded as likely; expected
  • antonym: unexpected - not expected or anticipated
