angular artery

noun
artery branching from the facial artery
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡjələr ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡjʊlə ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
the terminal branch of the facial artery, supplying blood to regions near the side of the nose and the lower eyelid

Examples

The angular artery
A surgeon may need to locate the angular artery during facial procedures.
Injury to the angular artery can cause significant bleeding near the nose.
The angular artery supplies blood to the side of the nose and the lower eyelid.
