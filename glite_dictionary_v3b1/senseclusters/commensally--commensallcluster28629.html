commensally

adverb
in a commensal manner (biology)
7.8 7.1
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmen.sə.lɪ/ (AmE), /kə.ˈmen.sə.lɪ/ /; / /kɒ.ˈmen.sə.lɪ/ (BrE)
In a commensal manner; relating to or involving a relationship in which one organism benefits while the other is neither helped nor harmed.

Examples

Live commensally on whales
Barnacles often live commensally on whales, gaining transportation but not harming the host.
Some bacteria dwell commensally in the human gut without causing harm.
The two species interacted commensally, with one receiving shelter and the other unaffected.
