peplos

noun
ancient Greek women's garment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛp.ləs / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.lɒs / (BrE)
a body-length garment worn by women in ancient Greece, fastened at the shoulders and usually belted at the waist

Examples

The peplos garment
In ancient Athens, the peplos was a symbol of femininity and modesty.
Museums often display the peplos in exhibits on classical Greek clothing.
The statue depicts a goddess wearing a traditional peplos draped from her shoulders.
