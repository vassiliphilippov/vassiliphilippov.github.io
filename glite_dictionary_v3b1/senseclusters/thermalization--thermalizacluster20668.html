thermalization

noun
reaching thermal equilibrium
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌθɜːrmələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌθɜːmələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which something reaches thermal equilibrium or a stable temperature, usually by exchanging energy until temperatures are equal

Examples

Thermalization process
After several collisions, the particles undergo thermalization and reach the same temperature.
The thermalization of the gas occurs as energy is exchanged between its molecules.
Scientists study thermalization to understand how systems reach equilibrium in physics experiments.
After entering the dense cloud, the cosmic ray underwent thermalization and lost most of its energy.
The thermalization of particles ensures that the system eventually reaches a uniform temperature.
In nuclear reactors, thermalization is important for slowing down neutrons to sustain the reaction.
The laboratory equipment required thermalization before any precise measurements could be taken.
After several minutes, the device completed thermalization and was ready for use.
Proper thermalization is essential for sensors to provide reliable temperature data.
