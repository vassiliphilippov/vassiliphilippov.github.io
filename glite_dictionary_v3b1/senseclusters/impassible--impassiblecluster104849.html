impassible

adjective
incapable of suffering
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpæsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːsɪbəl / (BrE)
incapable of suffering, feeling pain, or being affected by emotion or change, especially used of a deity.

Examples

Impassible god
Many believers describe the deity as impassible, unchanged by human sorrow or joy.
The philosopher argued that a truly impassible being could not be affected by earthly events.
Some religious traditions debate whether God is impassible or capable of experiencing emotion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impassive - showing no emotion or feeling
  • antonym: passible - capable of feeling or suffering
