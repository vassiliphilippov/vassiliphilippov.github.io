corroborative

adjective
serving to support or confirm
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑː.bəˌreɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrɒb.əˌreɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
serving to support or confirm a statement, theory, or finding with additional evidence or authority

Examples

Corroborative evidence
The study presented corroborative evidence supporting the hypothesis.
The witness provided corroborative details that matched the initial report.
We need corroborative documents to back up your claims.
