viscosity

noun
resistance of a fluid to flow
4.6
Pronunciation: / vɪˈskɑːsəti / (AmE), / vɪsˈkɒsɪti / (BrE)
The property of a liquid that resists the force causing it to flow; thickness or stickiness of a fluid.

Examples

Liquid viscosity level
As temperature increases, the viscosity of engine oil decreases.
Honey has a much higher viscosity than water, which is why it pours more slowly.
Scientists measured the viscosity of the solution to determine how easily it would flow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fluidity - ease with which a substance flows
