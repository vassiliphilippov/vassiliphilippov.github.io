greedy

adjective
intensely desirous; eager for more
1.0 1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡridi / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdi / (BrE)
having or showing an excessive desire to have more of something, especially food, money, or possessions, than is needed or fair

Examples

Greedy ambition
His greedy ambition pushed him to work day and night.
She was greedy for knowledge and read every book she could find.
The investor became greedy for more profit, ignoring all risks.
His greedy behavior alienated him from his friends.
She accused the company of being greedy for laying off workers to increase profits.
The greedy child tried to grab more than his share of the candy.
Children can become greedy when there are lots of sweets on the table.
Don't be greedy with the cake—leave some for everyone else.
He was so greedy at dinner that he ate three helpings before anyone had seconds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avaricious - having or showing an extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • synonym: avid - having or showing a keen interest or enthusiasm for something
  • antonym: content - satisfied; not desiring more
  • synonym: gluttonous - excessively eating or drinking; synonym for greedy in this sense
  • degree higher: insatiable - impossible to satisfy
  • antonym: selfless - concerned more with others' needs than one's own; unselfish
