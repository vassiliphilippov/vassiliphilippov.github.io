leaf spring

noun
layered metal spring for vehicle suspension
8.1
Pronunciation: / lif sprɪŋ / (AmE), / liːf sprɪŋ / (BrE)
a long, narrow spring made up of several layers of metal strips, used in the suspension of vehicles to absorb shock

Examples

A leaf spring layer
Engineers replaced the worn leaf spring to improve the ride quality of the carriage.
Many heavy-duty vehicles use a leaf spring system for load-bearing and shock absorption.
The old truck still had its original leaf spring in the rear suspension.

Related Terms
