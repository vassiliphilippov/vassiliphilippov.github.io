  • all of a sudden

    all of a sudden feels like an informal, quick surprise used in speech, while abruptly looks more formal and sharp in writing.
  • of a sudden

  • all at once
    3.0 1.60/m

  • out of the blue
    3.4 3.35/m
    out of the blue feels informal and surprising like a sudden gift, while abruptly is neutral and fits many formal contexts.
  • in a flash
    2.5 2.22/m
    in a flash means very quickly like lightning, while abruptly stresses the suddenness of a change rather than speed alone.
  • in the blink of an eye
    3.3 1.80/m
    in the blink of an eye means extremely quickly like a flash in front of your eyes, while abruptly focuses on a sudden, unexpected stop.
  • on the spur of the moment
