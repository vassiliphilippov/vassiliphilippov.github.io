virion

noun
complete infectious virus particle
6.7 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪ.ri.ən/ /; / /ˈvaɪ.ri.ən/ (AmE), /ˈvɪ.ri.ən/ /; / /ˈvaɪ.ri.ən/ /; / /ˈvɪ.ri.ɒn/ (BrE)
the complete, infectious form of a virus outside a host cell, consisting of the viral genome enclosed in a protein coat and, in some cases, a lipid envelope

Examples

A virion particle
A virion is inactive until it infects a suitable host cell.
The lipid envelope surrounding the virion helps it enter host cells.
Under the microscope, the virion was seen as a small, spherical particle with a distinct protein shell.
