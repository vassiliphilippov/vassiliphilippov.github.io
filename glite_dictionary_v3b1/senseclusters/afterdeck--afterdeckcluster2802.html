afterdeck

noun
stern deck section of a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈæftɚˌdɛk / (AmE), / ˈɑːftədɛk / (BrE)
the part of a ship's deck situated toward the stern, aft of the midships section

Examples

The afterdeck area
Crew members gathered on the afterdeck to watch the sunset over the ocean.
The captain stood on the afterdeck as the ship sailed into open waters.
The damaged cargo had to be moved from the afterdeck to ensure the ship's balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foredeck - the part of a ship's deck near the bow
