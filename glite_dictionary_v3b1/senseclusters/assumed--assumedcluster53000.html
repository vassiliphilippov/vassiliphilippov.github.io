assumed

adjective
adopted in order to deceive
1.9 7.4
Pronunciation: / əˈsumd / (AmE), / əˈsjuːmd / (BrE)
(of a name, character, quality, etc.) adopted for a particular purpose, often in order to deceive or conceal one's identity.

Examples

An assumed name
She greeted us with an assumed cheerfulness that barely hid her true feelings.
The company was registered at an assumed address to keep its location secret.
The spy traveled under an assumed name to avoid detection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fictitious - not real or true; imaginary or fabricated
  • antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • synonym: pretended - not real; made to seem true for deceptive purposes
