heat

verb
arouse or excite feelings
3.3
Pronunciation: / hit / (AmE), / hiːt / (BrE)
to arouse or excite strong feelings or passions, especially in a person or group

Examples

To heat a debate
Controversial topics can heat a debate quickly, leading to strong reactions.
Old arguments sometimes heat old emotions, even after many years.
The leader’s speech managed to heat the crowd, making them shout in excitement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inflame - to provoke or intensify strong feelings
  • antonym: quell - to suppress or calm strong feelings
