evaporated milk

noun
milk concentrated by evaporation
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvæpəˌreɪtɪd mɪlk / (AmE), / ɪˈvapəˌreɪtɪd mɪlk / (BrE)
milk that has been thickened by removing about 60% of its water content through evaporation, usually then canned and used in cooking or as a milk substitute

Examples

Evaporated milk can
Evaporated milk is often used as a substitute for fresh milk in baking recipes.
Add one can of evaporated milk to make the soup creamier.
The recipe called for evaporated milk, sugar, and eggs to make the custard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condensed milk - milk thickened by evaporation and sweetened
