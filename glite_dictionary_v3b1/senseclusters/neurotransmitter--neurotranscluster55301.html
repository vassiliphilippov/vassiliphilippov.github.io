neurotransmitter

noun
chemical transmitting signals between nerve cells
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /nʊr.oʊ.træns.ˈmɪt.ər/ /; / /njʊr.oʊ.træns.ˈmɪt.ər/ (AmE), /nʊə.træns.ˈmɪt.ə/ /; / /njʊə.træns.ˈmɪt.ə/ (BrE)
a chemical substance that transmits signals across a synapse from one nerve cell to another

Examples

A neurotransmitter chemical
Serotonin is a well-known neurotransmitter involved in regulating mood and behavior.
Some drugs work by altering neurotransmitter levels in the brain.
When a neurotransmitter is released, it binds to receptors on the next neuron.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hormone - a chemical messenger, often contrasted with neurotransmitters, that is released into the bloodstream
  • synonym: neuropeptide - a type of neurotransmitter consisting of short chains of amino acids
