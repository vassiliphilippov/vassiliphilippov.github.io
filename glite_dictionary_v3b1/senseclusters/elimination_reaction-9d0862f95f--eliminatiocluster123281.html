elimination reaction

noun
chemical reaction yielding two products
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɪməˈneɪʃən riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪmɪˈneɪʃən rɪˈakʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which a single molecule breaks down to form two or more different molecules, typically by removing atoms or groups of atoms.

Examples

An elimination reaction produces alkene
An elimination reaction often produces an alkene and a small molecule such as water or hydrogen halide.
Chemists used an elimination reaction to convert the alcohol into an alkene.
Understanding the mechanism of an elimination reaction is crucial in organic synthesis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: addition reaction - a chemical reaction in which atoms are added to a molecule
