ethyl chloride

noun
a flammable gas used as a local anesthetic
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈiːθɪl ˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable gas used as a local anesthetic and in chemical manufacturing

Examples

Ethyl chloride spray
Ethyl chloride is commonly sprayed on the skin to numb small areas before minor medical procedures.
Because ethyl chloride evaporates quickly, it provides instant cooling and localized anesthesia.
The nurse applied ethyl chloride to the patient's arm before inserting the needle.
