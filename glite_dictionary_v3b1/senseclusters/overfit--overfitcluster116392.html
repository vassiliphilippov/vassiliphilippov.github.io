overfit

adjective
too closely fitted
7.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈfɪt/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.fɪt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈfɪt/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.fɪt/ (BrE)
fitted too closely to a specific dataset, causing poor performance on new or unseen data

Examples

An overfit model
The overfit model performed well on training data but failed on real-world examples.
If your algorithm is overfit, it may not handle new situations effectively.
Researchers noticed the results were unreliable because the system was overfit to the sample data.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generalizable - able to perform well on new or unseen data
  • antonym: underfit - not fitted closely enough to the data, missing important patterns
