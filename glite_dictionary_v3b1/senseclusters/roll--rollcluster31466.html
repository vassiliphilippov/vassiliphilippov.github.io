roll

verb
move in a wavy, rising-and-falling pattern
Pronunciation: / roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)
to move or turn over and over, or to move smoothly in a circular or swaying way (as with objects, people, vehicles, waves, or clouds)

Examples

To roll down a hill
Dark storm clouds began to roll across the sky.
Heavy waves rolled toward the rocky shoreline during the storm.
Mist rolled over the fields as the sun rose.
News coverage continued to roll throughout the night as events unfolded.
The moment the director shouted 'Action!' the cameras began to roll.
The printing presses will roll as soon as the latest edition is ready.
As the plane hit turbulence, it started to roll unexpectedly.
Lying in the hammock, I could feel it gently roll from side to side.
The old ferry began to roll violently as the storm intensified.
