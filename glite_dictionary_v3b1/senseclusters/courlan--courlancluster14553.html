courlan

noun
Central/South American wading bird
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr·læn / (AmE), / ˈkɔː·læn / (BrE)
A large, long-legged wading bird of Central and South America, known for its loud, piercing call.

Examples

A courlan bird
Birdwatchers in the tropics often hope to spot a courlan wading along riverbanks.
The courlan is known for its eerie, wailing cries echoing across marshlands at dusk.
While hiking through the wetlands, we encountered a flock of courlans feeding in the shallow water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limpkin - another name for the courlan, a wading bird found in the Americas
