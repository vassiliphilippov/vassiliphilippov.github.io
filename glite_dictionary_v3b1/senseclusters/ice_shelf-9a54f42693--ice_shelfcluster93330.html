ice shelf

noun
floating platform of land-attached ice
Pronunciation: / aɪs ʃɛlf / (AmE), / aɪs ʃɛlf / (BrE)
a thick, floating platform of ice that forms where a glacier or ice sheet flows down to a coastline and extends over the sea

Examples

A large ice shelf
A large section of the ice shelf broke off and drifted into the ocean.
Scientists have discovered deep cracks in the Antarctic ice shelf.
The melting of the ice shelf could contribute significantly to rising sea levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glacier - a large mass of moving ice
  • degree lower: iceberg - a large floating mass of detached ice
