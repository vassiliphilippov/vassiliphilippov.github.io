sphere of influence

noun

A country or region in which another country has significant cultural, economic, military, or political influence, though it does not have formal authority over it.

Examples

China's sphere of influence
China has been extending its sphere of influence in Africa through investment and diplomacy.
During the Cold War, Eastern Europe was considered the Soviet Union's sphere of influence.
The country's leaders were determined to resist being drawn into any foreign sphere of influence.
