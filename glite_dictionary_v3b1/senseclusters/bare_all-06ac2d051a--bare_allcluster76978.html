bare all

verb
reveal everything; be completely open
Pronunciation: / bɛr ɔl / (AmE), / beə ɔːl / (BrE)
to reveal everything about oneself, especially personal or private information; to be completely open or honest

Examples

To bare all feelings
After years of silence, he decided to bare all in his new memoir.
Sometimes it takes courage to bare all and tell the truth.
The actress chose to bare all during the emotional interview.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - keep something secret; not reveal information
  • synonym: confess - admit or disclose something private, often wrongdoing
