COVID-19

noun
infectious disease caused by coronavirus SARS-CoV-2
2.8
Pronunciation: / koʊˌvɪd naɪnˈtiːn / (AmE), / kəʊˌvɪd naɪnˈtiːn / (BrE)
a highly infectious disease caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2, first identified in 2019, which led to a global pandemic

Examples

COVID-19 pandemic
Many countries imposed travel restrictions to try to contain the spread of COVID-19.
Researchers are working to develop effective vaccines against COVID-19.
The symptoms of COVID-19 can range from mild to severe, including fever and difficulty breathing.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: SARS - severe acute respiratory syndrome, another disease caused by a coronavirus
  • synonym: coronavirus - a type of virus that causes respiratory infections, including COVID-19
