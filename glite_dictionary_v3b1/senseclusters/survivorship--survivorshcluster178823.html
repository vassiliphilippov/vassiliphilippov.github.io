survivorship

noun
right of surviving owner
Pronunciation: / sərˈvaɪvərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvəʃɪp / (BrE)
the legal right or condition by which a surviving joint owner or beneficiary continues to hold property or receive benefits after another's death

Examples

Survivorship rights
Because the property was held with right of survivorship, the house passed immediately to his sister.
Joint bank accounts often include survivorship so the surviving owner can access the funds.
The lawyer explained how survivorship affects the transfer of assets after a co-owner's death.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inheritance - the act of receiving property or money after someone's death
  • synonym: joint tenancy - a form of property ownership by two or more people with rights of survivorship
  • antonym: tenancy in common - a form of property ownership without rights of survivorship
