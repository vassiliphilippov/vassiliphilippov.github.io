lipochondrodystrophy

noun
rare inherited disorder affecting bone and cartilage
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪ.poʊˌkɑn.droʊ.dɪsˈtrəʊ.fi / (AmE), / ˌlɪ.pəʊˌkɒn.drəʊ.dɪsˈtrɒf.i / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder (autosomal recessive) involving an error in mucopolysaccharide metabolism, leading to severe skeletal, cartilage, bone abnormalities, and developmental delay.

Examples

Rare lipochondrodystrophy disorder
Children with lipochondrodystrophy often present with distinctive skeletal features at an early age.
Due to its rarity, research on lipochondrodystrophy remains limited and highly specialized.
The diagnosis of lipochondrodystrophy was confirmed after genetic testing and clinical evaluation.
