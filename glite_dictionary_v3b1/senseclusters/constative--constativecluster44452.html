constative

noun
descriptive statement
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnstətɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstətɪv / (BrE)
a statement or utterance that describes a situation and can be judged as true or false

Examples

A constative in linguistics
In linguistics, a constative is a statement that can be evaluated as true or false.
The sentence 'It is raining' is an example of a constative.
He explained the difference between a constative and a performative in his lecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: declarative - a sentence that makes a statement or declaration
  • antonym: performative - an utterance that performs an action rather than describing it
