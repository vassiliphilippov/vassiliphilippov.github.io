intracellular fluid

noun
liquid inside cells
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.trəˈsɛl.jə.lɚ ˈfluː.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈsɛl.jʊ.lə ˈfluː.ɪd / (BrE)
the liquid found inside the cell membranes, typically containing dissolved substances essential for cellular processes

Examples

Intracellular fluid volume
Certain medications can affect the volume of intracellular fluid in the body.
Most of the water in our bodies is found as intracellular fluid within the cells.
The balance between intracellular fluid and extracellular fluid is vital for proper cell function.
