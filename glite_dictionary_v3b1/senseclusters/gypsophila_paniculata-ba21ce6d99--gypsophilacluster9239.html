Gypsophila paniculata

noun
a tall plant with tiny white or pink flowers
Pronunciation: / dʒɪpˈsɑfɪlə ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / dʒɪpˈsɒfɪlə ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of numerous tiny white or pink flowers, commonly used in floral arrangements.

Examples

Dried Gypsophila paniculata
Dried Gypsophila paniculata can last for months and is popular in wreaths.
Many gardeners grow Gypsophila paniculata for its airy clusters of flowers.
The florist added sprigs of Gypsophila paniculata to the bouquet for a delicate touch.
