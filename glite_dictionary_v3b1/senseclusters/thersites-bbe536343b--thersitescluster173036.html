Thersites

proper noun
insolent Greek myth figure
Pronunciation: / θərˈsaɪtiːz / (AmE), / θəˈsaɪtiːz / (BrE)
a character in Greek mythology and literature known for his ugliness and insolence, especially in Homer's Iliad

Examples

Thersites in the Iliad
In the epic, Thersites is mocked for his boldness and lack of respect for authority.
The critic called the rude protester a modern Thersites for his constant insults.
She compared her classmate to Thersites because of his sarcastic and disrespectful comments.
