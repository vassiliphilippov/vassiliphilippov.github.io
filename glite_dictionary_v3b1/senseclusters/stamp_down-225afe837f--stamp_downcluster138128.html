stamp down

verb
bring under control by force or authority
Pronunciation: / stæmp daʊn / (AmE), / stæmp daʊn / (BrE)
to put an end to something or bring it under control by the use of force or authority

Examples

To stamp down illegal parking
Strict rules were introduced to stamp down illegal parking in the city center.
The government vowed to stamp down on corruption within the police force.
The principal tried to stamp down bullying in the school with new policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to something by force
