Dostoevsky

proper noun
Russian novelist (1821–1881)
Pronunciation: / ˌdɑstəˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˌdɒstɔɪˈɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) A Russian novelist noted for exploring human suffering, morality, and psychology, author of works such as 'Crime and Punishment' and 'The Brothers Karamazov'.
