mortifying

adjective
causing embarrassment or shame
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtəˌfaɪɪŋ /; / ˈmɔːrtɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone to feel very embarrassed, ashamed, or humiliated

Examples

A mortifying mistake
It was mortifying to trip on stage in front of so many people.
She found it mortifying when her mistake was announced in the meeting.
The mortifying experience haunted him for weeks.
It was mortifying to forget my lines in front of the whole class.
Missing the open goal was a mortifying moment for the young player.
She found the experience of being corrected in public deeply mortifying.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embarrassing - causing embarrassment, awkward or uncomfortable
  • antonym: exalting - raising in rank, power, or character; opposite meaning
  • synonym: humiliating - making someone feel ashamed and foolish
