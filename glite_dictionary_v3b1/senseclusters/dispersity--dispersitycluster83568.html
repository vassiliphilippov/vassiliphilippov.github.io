dispersity

noun
polymer weight distribution
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɜːrsəti / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which things (such as molecules or particles) in a sample differ in size, weight, or composition, especially in scientific contexts

Examples

Dispersity of molecules
The dispersity of the polymer sample was higher than expected, indicating a wide range of molecular weights.
Researchers aim for low dispersity to produce materials with more consistent properties.
A dispersity value close to 1 suggests the polymer chains are nearly all the same length.
The dispersity of the polymer sample affected its physical properties.
Scientists measured the dispersity to determine how evenly the particles were distributed in the solution.
A low dispersity indicates that most particles are similar in size.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heterogeneity - the quality of consisting of diverse elements
  • synonym: polydispersity - the degree of non-uniformity in the size or molecular weight of particles in a mixture
  • antonym: uniformity - the quality of being the same or consistent throughout
