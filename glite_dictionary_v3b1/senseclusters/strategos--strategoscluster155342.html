strategos

noun
Byzantine military governor
Pronunciation: / strəˈtiːɡoʊs / (AmE), / strəˈtiːɡɒs / (BrE)
a military governor in the Byzantine Empire who held both civil and military authority over a region called a theme

Examples

Byzantine strategos rules
The strategos was responsible for defending the theme against invasions.
During the rebellion, the strategos coordinated both the army and local administration.
Historians study the role of the strategos in maintaining order in the Byzantine Empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: governor - an official in charge of a region or state
