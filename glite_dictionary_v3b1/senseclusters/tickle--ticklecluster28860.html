tickle

verb
touch or stroke lightly, often causing laughter
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪk.əl/ (AmE), /ˈtɪk.əl/ (BrE)
to touch someone lightly to cause a tingling feeling or laughter

Examples

To tickle someone's feet
A feather can easily tickle your nose and make you sneeze.
If you tickle his feet, he'll burst out laughing.
The long grass began to tickle her ankles as she walked through the field.
He loves to tickle his dog behind the ears.
If you tickle a baby gently, it might start giggling.
She tried not to laugh when her friend began to tickle her feet.

  • synonym: tease - to touch or handle lightly or playfully
