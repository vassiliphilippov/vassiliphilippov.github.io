mount

verb
get up on to ride (e.g. horse, bike)
3.0 5.47/m
Pronunciation: / maÊŠnt / (AmE), / maÊŠnt / (BrE)
to get up on the back of something such as a horse or bicycle to ride it

Examples

To mount a horse
Before the race, the cyclist quickly mounted his bike and fastened his helmet.
She skillfully mounted the horse and adjusted her reins.
The actress nervously mounted the elephant for the parade scene.

Synonyms

  • get on
    1.0 1.11/m
    get on is used for climbing onto a horse, bike, or bus in everyday speech, while mount is the standard riding verb.
  • mount up
    4.0 0.27/m
    mount up is a phrase riders or soldiers use to tell people to get on quickly, while mount is the neutral riding verb.
  • hop on
    2.7 1.61/m
    hop on is casual and suggests a quick, light movement onto a bike or horse, while mount describes the act of riding.
  • jump on
    1.8 0.86/m
    jump on is sudden and fast, often used when getting up quickly, while mount focuses on mounting to ride calmly.
  • climb on
    2.0 1.48/m
    climb on means using hands and steps to get onto something, while mount usually means getting on to ride.
  • bestride
    8.8 0.11/m
    bestride shows sitting or standing with a leg on each side and sounds formal or old, while mount is the normal riding term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dismount - to get down from something one has mounted, such as a horse or bicycle
