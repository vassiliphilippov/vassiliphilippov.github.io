discriminator

noun
GAN model that tells real from fake
8.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈskrɪm.ɪ.neɪ.tər/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskrɪm.ə.neɪ.tər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈskrɪm.ɪ.neɪ.tə/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskrɪm.ə.neɪ.tə/ (BrE)
(in machine learning, especially in Generative Adversarial Networks) a model or neural network that learns to distinguish real data from generated (synthetic) data, often by classifying inputs as real or fake.

Examples

GAN discriminator model
The discriminator in the GAN quickly learned to reject images produced by the generator.
During training, their discriminator became very good at spotting tiny visual artifacts.
They adjusted the discriminators to improve accuracy on both real and synthetic samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classifier - a model that assigns labels or categories to data; similar to a discriminator in deciding whether inputs belong to a class.
