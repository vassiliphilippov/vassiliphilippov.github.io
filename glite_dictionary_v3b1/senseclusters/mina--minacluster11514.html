mina

noun
ancient weight or money
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnə /; / ˈmiːnə / (AmE), / ˈmiːnə / (BrE)
an ancient unit of weight or money used in the Middle East, especially in Mesopotamia and Greece

Examples

A mina of silver
The merchant paid a mina of silver for the goods.
In ancient Babylon, a mina was a standard measure for trade and taxation.
Historians estimate that one mina could equal about 500 grams, depending on the region.
In ancient Babylon, a mina was used to measure both silver and grain.
The inscription listed the tribute as one mina of gold from each city.
Historians debate the exact weight of a mina in different periods.
