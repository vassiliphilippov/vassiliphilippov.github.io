rail

noun
horizontal barrier supported by posts
Pronunciation: / reɪl / (AmE), / reɪl / (BrE)
a horizontal bar or barrier, often used as a fence, handhold, or as part of a railway track

Examples

Hand rail
A wooden rail separated the garden from the sidewalk.
She grabbed the rail for support as she climbed the stairs.
The balcony has a sturdy metal rail for safety.
Please hold onto the rail when using the stairs for safety.
The hand rail was painted white to match the rest of the fence.
We installed a wooden rail along the porch to prevent anyone from falling.
A loose rail can be a serious safety hazard on busy train lines.
Steel rails are laid on wooden or concrete sleepers to form a railway track.
The damaged rail had to be replaced before trains could resume service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bar - a long rigid piece of material, often used as a support or barrier
  • synonym: fence - a structure serving as an enclosure, barrier, or boundary
  • synonym: railroad tie - a beam supporting and fixing the rails on a railway track
  • synonym: track - the pair of rails forming a railway line
