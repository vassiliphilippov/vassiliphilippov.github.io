meadow pea

noun
a wild Eurasian pea grown for forage
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdoʊ pi / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ piː / (BrE)
a scrambling perennial Eurasian wild pea (Lathyrus pratensis) with yellowish flowers and compressed seed pods, cultivated for forage

Examples

The meadow pea plant
Bright yellow flowers covered the meadow pea growing along the hedgerow.
Livestock often graze on fields of meadow pea during the spring.
The farmer planted meadow pea as part of his crop rotation to improve the soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vetch - climbing legumes in the genus Vicia, similar forage peas
