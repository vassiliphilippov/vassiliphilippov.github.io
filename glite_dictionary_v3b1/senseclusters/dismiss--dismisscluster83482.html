dismiss

verb
remove from employment
2.8
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪs / (BrE)
to remove someone from their job; to terminate the employment of someone

Examples

Company dismisses an employee
After repeated warnings, the company decided to dismiss the employee for poor performance.
If you violate company policy, they may dismiss you immediately.
The manager had to dismiss several staff members due to budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from their job, especially suddenly
  • antonym: hire - to employ someone for a job
  • synonym: lay off - to end the employment of workers for economic reasons
