trepan

noun
surgical tool for cutting bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / trɪˈpæn /; / ˈtrɛpæn / (AmE), / trɪˈpan /; / ˈtrɛpan / (BrE)
a tool or surgical instrument with a circular blade used for cutting holes, especially in bone (such as the skull) or in materials like stone

Examples

In archaeology, a trepan may be used to take core samples from stone.
The mechanic selected a trepan to drill a perfect circle in the metal plate.
The surgeon used a trepan to remove a small disc of bone from the patient's skull.
