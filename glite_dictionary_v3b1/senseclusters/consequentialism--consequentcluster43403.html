consequentialism

noun
ethics by outcomes
9.9
Pronunciation: /kən.sɪ.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /kən.sə.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /kɒn.sɪ.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /kɒn.sə.ˈkwen.ʃəl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the ethical theory that judges whether actions are right or wrong based on their outcomes or consequences, rather than on rules or intentions

Examples

Consequentialism ethical theory
Many philosophers debate whether consequentialism provides a fair way to judge moral actions.
The manager's approach was based on consequentialism, always considering the results of each decision.
Some critics argue that consequentialism ignores important moral principles in favor of outcomes.
Philosophers often debate whether consequentialism provides a fair way to judge moral actions.
Some people criticize consequentialism for ignoring the importance of moral rules.
In ethics class, we discussed how consequentialism differs from other moral theories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deontology - the ethical theory that judges actions by rules or duties rather than outcomes
  • synonym: utilitarianism - the ethical theory that actions are right if they benefit the majority
