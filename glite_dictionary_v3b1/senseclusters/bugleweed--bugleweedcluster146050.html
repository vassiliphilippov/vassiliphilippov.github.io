bugleweed

noun
a low-growing evergreen herb (Ajuga) used as ground cover
Pronunciation: / ˈbjuːɡəlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbjuːɡlˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several low-growing evergreen herbs of the genus Ajuga, native to Eurasia, often used as ground cover in gardens.

Examples

Garden bugleweed
Bugleweed can spread quickly and fill in bare spots in shady areas.
Some varieties of bugleweed produce small, attractive blue or purple flowers in spring.
The gardener planted bugleweed along the edges of the pathway to control weeds.
