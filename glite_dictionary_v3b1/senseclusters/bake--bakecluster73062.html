bake

verb
precompute graphics data (lighting/textures)
7.1
Pronunciation: /beɪk/ (AmE), /beɪk/ (BrE)
(in computer graphics) to precompute rendering results such as lighting, shadows, or texture information and store them in image maps or files so they can be used at runtime to improve performance.

Examples

To bake textures
To bake lighting into textures is a common optimization in modern game engines.
The artists spent the afternoon baking ambient occlusion maps to reduce real-time lighting costs.
After adjustments, the team baked the final textures and saw a notable performance improvement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lightmap - an image that stores precomputed lighting information for use in real-time rendering
  • synonym: precompute - to compute something in advance so results are ready for later use
