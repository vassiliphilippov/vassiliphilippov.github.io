cabbage tree

noun
tropical tree with toxic bark used medicinally
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæbɪdʒ tri / (AmE), / ˈkabɪdʒ triː / (BrE)
a tropical tree with rough, unpleasant-smelling, toxic bark and strong, durable wood; its bark and seeds are used as a purgative, vermifuge, and narcotic

Examples

Toxic cabbage tree
Farmers warned that the cabbage tree is toxic to livestock if consumed.
Many indigenous communities have long valued the cabbage tree for its durable wood and medicinal properties.
The bark of the cabbage tree was once used to prepare traditional medicines.
