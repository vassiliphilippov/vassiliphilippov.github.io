stop

noun
obstruction in a pipe or tube
Pronunciation: / stɑp / (AmE), / stɒp / (BrE)
a device or object used to block, control, or prevent movement, or to regulate something (such as in a camera)

Examples

A stop valve
If a stop forms in the water line, the pressure may drop suddenly.
The buildup of debris created a stop in the irrigation pipe, blocking the flow.
The plumber discovered a stop in the kitchen sink drain and quickly removed it.
Adjusting the stop on your camera allows you to control the exposure of your photos.
Higher-quality lenses often feature a precisely engineered stop to ensure sharp images.
When shooting in bright sunlight, using a smaller stop can prevent your images from becoming overexposed.
She placed a heavy rock as a stop behind the car's wheel.
The door wouldn't stay open without a stop to hold it in place.
This bottle has a rubber stop to keep the liquid from spilling out.
