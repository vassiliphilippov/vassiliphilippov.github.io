swallow

noun
an act of swallowing
1.0 12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsw…ëÀêlo ä /; / Ààsw…ëlo ä / (AmE), / Ààsw…íl…ô ä / (BrE)
the act of swallowing or the amount of food or drink taken down the throat at one time

Examples

A big swallow
After each swallow, she felt her sore throat ache.
He took a quick swallow of water before answering the question.
The medicine had a bitter taste on the first swallow.
A sudden swallow of coffee burned his tongue.

Synonyms

  • throatful
    8.0 0.01/m
    throatful means a quantity that fills the throat in one gulp, while swallow names the act or single amount taken.
  • deglutition
    9.9 0.02/m
    deglutition is the medical term for the swallowing process used in science, while swallow is common and used in everyday speech.
  • swig
    6.5 1.71/m
    swig is a large, hurried drink often with alcohol, while swallow is a neutral word for the act or small amount taken.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mouthful - a quantity of food or drink that fills the mouth
  • degree lower: sip - a small mouthful of a drink
