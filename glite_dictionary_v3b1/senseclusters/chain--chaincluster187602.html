chain

noun
series of hills or mountains
7.0 1.34/m

a series of hills or mountains connected together to form a line or range

Examples

A mountain chain
A snow-capped chain rose in the distance, marking the national border.
The Andes form the longest chain of mountains in the world.
They hiked across a rugged chain separating the two valleys.

Synonyms

  • chain of mountains
    3.0 0.04/m
    chain of mountains is the full phrase for a linked series of peaks, while chain is a shorter, more general term.
  • mountain chain
    2.9 0.13/m
    mountain chain describes connected hills or peaks in plain language, while chain is more general and may name other linked things.
  • mountain range
    1.8 2.06/m
    mountain range is the common, neutral term for a long line of mountains, while chain sounds more visual or poetic.
  • range of mountains
    2.3 0.13/m
    range of mountains means a group of connected peaks seen as one unit, while chain is shorter and more informal.
  • range
    2.5 7.63/m
    range can mean any line of hills or mountains in general speech, while chain refers specifically to a linked series.
  • cordillera
    7.9 0.08/m
    cordillera names a large system of mountain ranges, especially in the Americas, while chain means a single connected line.
