Italophile

adjective
admiring Italy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæləˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈtæləˌfaɪl / (BrE)
having or showing a strong admiration for Italy, its people, language, or culture

Examples

Italophile attitude
As an Italophile traveler, she visited Rome every summer to explore its art and cuisine.
His Italophile tastes were clear from the Italian music and films he enjoyed at home.
The Italophile professor taught Italian literature with great enthusiasm and passion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Francophile - admiring France or French culture
  • antonym: Italophobe - having a dislike or fear of Italy or Italian culture
