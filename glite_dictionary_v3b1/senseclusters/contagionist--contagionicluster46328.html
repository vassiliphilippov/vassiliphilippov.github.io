contagionist

noun
believer in social contagion
Pronunciation: / kənˈteɪdʒənɪst / (AmE), / kənˈteɪdʒənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that diseases, behaviors, emotions, or ideas can spread from person to person like an infection

Examples

The contagionist theory
The contagionist presented evidence that fear can spread quickly through a community.
As a contagionist, she researched how rumors move from person to person.
Some economists are contagionists who believe financial crises can spread between countries.
In the nineteenth century, a contagionist would support strict quarantine measures during epidemics.
The debate between the contagionist and anticontagionist theories shaped public health policies.
As a leading contagionist, she advocated for isolating patients to prevent the spread of disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticontagionist - a person who denies that diseases are spread by contagion
  • synonym: epidemiologist - a person who studies the spread of diseases
  • synonym: infectionist - a person who believes diseases are caused by infection
  • antonym: skeptic - a person who doubts or questions accepted ideas
