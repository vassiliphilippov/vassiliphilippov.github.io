rack

noun
a rapid horse gait
Pronunciation: / ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
a rapid gait of a horse in which each foot strikes the ground separately

Examples

Horse rack
During the show, the horse transitioned from a trot to a swift rack.
Riders admire the rack for its speed and comfort compared to other gaits.
The Tennessee Walking Horse is famous for its smooth rack gait.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gallop - the fastest gait of a horse, with all feet off the ground at once during each stride
  • degree lower: trot - a moderate-speed gait of a horse where diagonal legs move together
