Gypsy

noun
a member of the Romani people; often offensive
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪpsi /; / ˈdʒɪp.si / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪpsi /; / ˈdʒɪp.si / (BrE)
a member of a traditionally itinerant people, especially of Romani origin, living chiefly in Europe and North America; now often considered offensive when referring to Romani people

Examples

A Gypsy family
She learned traditional dances from a Gypsy family.
The novel tells the story of a young Gypsy traveling across Eastern Europe.
Use of the term Gypsy can be considered disrespectful by many Romani people.
Many Gipsy artists have contributed to traditional music across Europe.
She was fascinated by the colorful wagons of the Gipsy community at the fair.
The old story tells of a mysterious Gipsy who could predict the future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Romani - a member of the Romani people; preferred, neutral term
  • synonym: traveler - a person who travels or moves from place to place, sometimes used specifically for itinerant groups
