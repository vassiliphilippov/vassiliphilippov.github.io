shoreface

noun
beach zone near waves
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːˌfeɪs / (BrE)
the part of a beach between the low-tide line and the area where waves begin to break at low tide

Examples

The shoreface zone
Marine biologists collected samples from the shoreface to study coastal erosion.
The shoreface is often affected by strong currents and shifting sands.
After the storm, the shoreface showed clear signs of sediment movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreshore - the part of the shore between high and low water marks
  • antonym: offshore - the area of sea away from the shore
