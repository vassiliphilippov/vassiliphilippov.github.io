prelude

verb
serve as an introduction or opening to
1.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈprel.juːd/ /; / /ˈpriːl.juːd/ /; / /ˈpreɪ.luːd/ (AmE), /ˈprel.juːd/ /; / /ˈpriːl.juːd/ (BrE)
to introduce or come before something, often marking the beginning

Examples

Storms prelude rain
A sudden storm preluded the evening's festivities, setting a dramatic tone.
Soft music preluded the speaker's entrance, creating a calm atmosphere.
The short poem preludes the main story, giving readers a taste of what's to come.
A soft melody preluded the main performance, setting a gentle mood for the audience.
As the pianist preluded, the guests settled into their seats, awaiting the concert.
The ceremony preluded a series of important announcements from the organizers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: introduce - to present or bring into use; to begin
