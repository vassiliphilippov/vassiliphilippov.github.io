curve

verb
to bend in a smooth shape
2.8 1.66/m
Pronunciation: / k…ùv /; / k…úrv / (AmE), / k…úÀêv / (BrE)
to bend or form a smooth, rounded shape or path; to turn smoothly or sharply

Examples

To curve a line
She watched the ribbon curve gracefully through the air.
The road begins to curve just beyond the old oak tree.
You must curve the metal strip to fit the shape of the lamp.
As he kicked, the soccer ball curved beautifully into the net.
She curved the ribbon into a perfect bow for the gift box.
The road curves gently around the lake, following the shoreline.

The narrow country lane began to curve sharply as it approached the river.
The path curved gently around the hillside, offering a beautiful view of the valley.

Synonyms

  • arc
    4.4 0.28/m
    an arc moves or forms in a smooth rounded path, while curve is a general verb for bending or turning.
  • arch
    3.7 2.07/m
    an arch forms a strong, rounded shape like a bridge, while curve is the general action of bending or turning.
  • wind
    2.9 14/m
    wind moves along a twisting path with several turns, while curve usually describes a single smooth bend or turn.
