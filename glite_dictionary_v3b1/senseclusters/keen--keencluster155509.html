keen

verb
wail or lament in grief
5.7 7.7
Pronunciation: /kiːn/ (AmE), /kiːn/ (BrE)
to wail or lament loudly in grief, especially for someone who has died

Examples

To keen for the dead
Mourners keened at the funeral, their cries echoing through the hall.
She keened for her lost child, her sorrow heard by everyone nearby.
The women gathered together to keen out their grief late into the night.
The women began keening loudly as the funeral procession passed through the village.
In some cultures, it is traditional to keen for the dead as a sign of respect.
She could hear her neighbor keening late into the night after the tragic news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: celebrate - to show happiness or joy, often for a special event
  • synonym: lament - to express grief or sorrow
  • synonym: mourn - to feel or show deep sorrow for someone who has died
