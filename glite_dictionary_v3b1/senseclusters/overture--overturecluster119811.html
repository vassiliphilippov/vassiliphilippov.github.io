overture

noun
orchestral introduction to an opera or oratorio
8.6 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.vɚ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.və.tjʊə / (BrE)
a piece of orchestral music played at the beginning of an opera, oratorio, or other extended musical work

Examples

Opera overture
Each act of the oratorio began with a brief overture setting the mood.
Tchaikovsky's 1812 Overture is famous for its dramatic cannon fire and bells.
The orchestra performed the stirring overture before the curtain rose on the opera.
