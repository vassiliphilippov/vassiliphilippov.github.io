precipitation

noun
water falling from the atmosphere (rain, snow, etc.)
7.8 2.9
Pronunciation: / prəˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən /; / prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
water (such as rain, snow, or hail) that falls from the sky, or the amount of it that falls in a certain period

Examples

Heavy precipitation
Farmers closely monitor precipitation levels to predict crop yields each year.
Heavy precipitation caused flooding in several areas throughout the state last night.
Meteorologists forecast a week of scattered precipitation across the region.
Annual precipitation in this region averages around 45 inches.
Low precipitation this year has affected the local crops.
Meteorologists are forecasting heavy precipitation over the next 24 hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drought - a long period with very little or no precipitation
  • synonym: rain - water that falls from clouds in droplets
  • synonym: rainfall - the amount of rain that falls in a particular period, measured in quantity
  • synonym: snow - frozen water vapor falling in light white flakes
