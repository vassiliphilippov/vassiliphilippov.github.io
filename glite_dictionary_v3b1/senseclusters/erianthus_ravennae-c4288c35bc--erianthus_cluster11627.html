Erianthus ravennae

noun
ornamental grass with large plumes
Pronunciation: / ɛˈraɪænθəs rəˈvæni / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪænθəs rəˈvæniː / (BrE)
A species of tall grass often grown as an ornamental plant for its long white-ribbed leaves and large plumes that resemble those of pampas grass.

Examples

Erianthus ravennae grass
Gardeners often plant Erianthus ravennae to add height and movement to the landscape.
The striking plumes of Erianthus ravennae make it a popular choice for ornamental borders.
Unlike pampas grass, Erianthus ravennae is less invasive and easier to manage in temperate gardens.
