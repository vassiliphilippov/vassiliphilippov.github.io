sotto

adverb
softly; quietly
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɒtəʊ / (BrE)
in a soft or quiet voice; softly or in an undertone

Examples

Speak sotto voce
He spoke sotto so that only his friend could hear him.
During the meeting, she made a comment sotto to avoid drawing attention.
The actor delivered his lines sotto to create a sense of intimacy on stage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loudly - in a strong or high volume voice
  • synonym: softly - in a quiet or gentle voice
