theropod

noun
carnivorous bipedal dinosaur group
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈθɛr.əˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈθɛrəˌpɒd / (BrE)
any of a group of mostly carnivorous dinosaurs with short forelimbs and strong hind legs, including the Tyrannosaurus and Velociraptor, that lived from the Triassic to the Cretaceous period.

Examples

A theropod dinosaur
Paleontologists believe that modern birds are descended from small feathered theropods.
The discovery of a new Jurassic theropod provided valuable insight into dinosaur evolution.
The towering theropod skeleton dominated the center of the museum exhibit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dinosaur - any extinct reptile belonging to the Dinosauria, typically large, prehistoric, and mostly terrestrial
