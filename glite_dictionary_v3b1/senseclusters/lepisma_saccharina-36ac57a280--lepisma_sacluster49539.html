Lepisma saccharina

noun
a household insect, also called silverfish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ləˈpɪzmə ˌsækəˈraɪnə / (AmE), / lɛˈpɪzmə ˌsakəˈrʌɪnə / (BrE)
A small, silver-grey, wingless insect often found in houses, known for feeding on book bindings and starchy materials; also called silverfish.

Examples

The Lepisma saccharina insect
Old libraries often struggle with damage caused by Lepisma saccharina, which feeds on the glue in book bindings.
The presence of Lepisma saccharina can be a sign that there is too much moisture in your home.
We discovered a Lepisma saccharina scurrying across the bathroom floor late at night.
