overwind

verb
to wind too tightly
8.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈwaɪnd/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ˈwɪnd/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈwaɪnd/ /; / /əʊ.və.ˈwɪnd/ (BrE)
to wind something, such as a spring, clock, or mechanism, too tightly or beyond its safe limit

Examples

To overwind a clock
If you overwind the clock, it may stop working properly.
He accidentally overwound his old music box and broke the spring inside.
Watchmakers warn customers not to overwind delicate mechanical watches.
If you overwind the music box, the spring inside could snap.
He accidentally overwound his old watch and it stopped working.
Make sure not to overwind the toy car or it might break.
