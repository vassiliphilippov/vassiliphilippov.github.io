folly

noun
a foolish mistake or idea
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑli /; / ˈfɑːli / (AmE), / ˈfɒli / (BrE)
foolishness or a foolish action; lack of good sense or judgment

Examples

A folly decision
His financial folly left the company in serious trouble.
It would be utter folly to try climbing that mountain without any experience.
She soon realized the folly of ignoring the weather warnings.
His decision to invest everything in the risky scheme was an act of pure folly.
Looking back, she regretted the folly of ignoring her friends' warnings.
The plan was abandoned after its folly became clear to everyone involved.
Building the house on a floodplain was a true folly that cost them dearly.
He realized his folly only after it was too late to change his mind.
Some old follies are hard to forget, no matter how much time has passed.
