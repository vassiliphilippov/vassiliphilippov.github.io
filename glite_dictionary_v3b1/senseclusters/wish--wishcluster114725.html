wish

verb
express hope or desire for the future
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /wɪʃ/ (AmE), /wɪʃ/ (BrE)
to want, hope for, or express a desire that something will happen, especially when it is unlikely or imaginary, or to make a polite request

Examples

To wish for more time
I wish you all the best in your new job.
She wished for a peaceful resolution to the conflict.
They always wish each other good luck before an exam.
He didn't wish to discuss the matter any further.
I wish I could travel more often, but work keeps me busy.
She wished for a peaceful evening by the fire.
If you wish to speak with the manager, please let us know.
She wished him a safe journey before he left.
You may leave early if you wish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dread - to anticipate something with fear, opposite of wish
  • synonym: hope - to want something to happen or be true
  • synonym: request - to ask for something politely or formally
  • synonym: want - to desire something
