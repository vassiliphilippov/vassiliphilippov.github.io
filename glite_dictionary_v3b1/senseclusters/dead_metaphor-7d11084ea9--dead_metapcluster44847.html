dead metaphor

noun
metaphor with lost imagery due to overuse
7.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / d…õd Ààm…õt…ôf…îr / (AmE), / d…õd Ààm…õt…ôf…îÀê / (BrE)
a metaphor that has lost its original imagery or imaginative force through frequent use and has become understood as a literal expression

Examples

A dead metaphor
Linguists study how a dead metaphor loses its imaginative impact over time.
Many common phrases, like 'falling in love,' originated as a dead metaphor but now feel literal.
The expression 'the leg of a table' is considered a dead metaphor because we no longer picture an actual leg.
