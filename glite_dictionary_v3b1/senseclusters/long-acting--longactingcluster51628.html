long-acting

adjective
remaining active for a long period
4.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /lɔːŋ ˈæk.tɪŋ/ /; / /lɑːŋ ˈæk.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /lɒŋ ˈæk.tɪŋ/ /; / /lʊŋ ˈæk.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
(of a drug or medical treatment) active over a prolonged period of time, requiring less frequent administration than standard alternatives.

Examples

Long-acting medicine
Some allergy medications are available in long-acting forms to provide relief for 24 hours.
The doctor prescribed a long-acting insulin to help control her blood sugar levels.
This long-acting painkiller allows patients to take fewer doses each day.
