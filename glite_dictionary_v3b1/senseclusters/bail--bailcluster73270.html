bail

noun
money security to ensure court appearance
2.7 4.3
Pronunciation: / beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
money or property given as security to ensure someone returns for their trial after being released from jail

Examples

Released on bail
He was released on bail while awaiting trial.
If you cannot afford to pay bail, you may have to stay in jail until your court date.
The judge set bail at $10,000 for the defendant.
After his arrest, the judge set bail at $10,000.
If he skips his trial, he will forfeit the bail.
She was released on bail while awaiting her court date.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bond - a written agreement to pay a sum if a legal obligation is not met
  • degree lower: parole - the release of a prisoner temporarily or permanently before the sentence is finished, on promise of good behavior
