reflection

noun
geometric transformation flipping across an axis
8.6 5.1
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈflek.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ˈflek.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈflek.ʃən/ /; / /rə.ˈflek.ʃən/ (BrE)
(mathematics) A transformation in which a figure is flipped across a line or plane, reversing the direction along one axis to produce a mirror image.

Examples

A math reflection
A reflection in mathematics results in a shape with reversed orientation.
If you apply a reflection followed by another reflection across the same line, the figure returns to its original position.
The triangle underwent a reflection over the y-axis, producing a mirror image.
