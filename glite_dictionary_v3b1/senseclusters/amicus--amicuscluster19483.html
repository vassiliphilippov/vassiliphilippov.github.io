amicus

noun
formal friend
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈmiːkəs / (AmE), / əˈmiːkəs / (BrE)
a friend, especially used in formal or literary contexts.

Examples

Loyal amicus
He trusted his old amicus with secrets he told no one else.
In the letter, she addressed him as her dearest amicus.
The poet wrote about the loyalty of a true amicus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile
  • synonym: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection
