order Opiliones

noun
a taxonomic order of arachnids (harvestmen)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər ˌoʊpɪˈlaɪəniːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌəʊpɪˈlaɪəniːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of arachnids commonly known as harvestmen, distinguished by their small body and very long, thin legs.

Examples

The order Opiliones species
Scientists discovered a new species within the order Opiliones during their expedition in South America.
The order Opiliones includes over 6,000 species of harvestmen found throughout the world.
Unlike spiders, members of the order Opiliones have a fused body segment and lack venom glands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spider - arachnid of the order Araneae, often confused with harvestmen
