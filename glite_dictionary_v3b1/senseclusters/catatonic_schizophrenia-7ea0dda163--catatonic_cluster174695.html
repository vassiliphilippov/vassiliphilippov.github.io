catatonic schizophrenia

noun
a form of schizophrenia with motor immobility and stupor
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈtɑnɪk ˌskɪtsəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈtɒnɪk ˌskɪtsəˈfriːnɪə / (BrE)
a subtype of schizophrenia marked by extreme motor immobility, periods of stupor, and sometimes brief episodes of excitement or agitation

Examples

Catatonic schizophrenia symptoms
Advances in treatment have improved outcomes for people with catatonic schizophrenia.
Her symptoms of catatonic schizophrenia included both stupor and episodes of agitation.
The patient was diagnosed with catatonic schizophrenia after displaying prolonged periods of immobility.

  • synonym: paranoid schizophrenia - a subtype of schizophrenia characterized by paranoia and delusions
