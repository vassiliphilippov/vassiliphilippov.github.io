ideograph

noun
written symbol representing an idea
7.1 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.dɪ.ə.græf/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.dɪ.ə.græf/ (BrE)
a written symbol that represents an idea or object directly rather than a particular word or sound

Examples

Ancient ideographs symbols
Ancient Egyptian script contains many ideographs that represent concepts rather than sounds.
In Mandarin, each ideograph corresponds to an idea or object, not a specific word.
The museum displayed several clay tablets featuring early ideographs from Sumerian civilization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ideogram - a written character symbolizing the idea of a thing without indicating the sounds used to say it
  • antonym: phonogram - a written character representing a spoken sound
