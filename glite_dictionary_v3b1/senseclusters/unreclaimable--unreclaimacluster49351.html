unreclaimable

adjective
cannot be restored
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈkleɪməbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈkleɪməbəl / (BrE)
impossible to recover, restore, or bring back to a usable or original state (such as land, objects, or documents)

Examples

Unreclaimable land
The polluted land was declared unreclaimable after years of toxic waste dumping.
Some memories are lost forever and are simply unreclaimable.
The damage to the ancient painting was so severe that it became unreclaimable.
After the fire, many important documents were deemed unreclaimable by the authorities.
The company considered the lost shipment unreclaimable after months of searching.
Some environmental damage is sadly unreclaimable despite our best efforts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irretrievable - impossible to recover or get back
  • synonym: irreversible - impossible to change back to a previous state
  • antonym: recoverable - able to be recovered or restored
