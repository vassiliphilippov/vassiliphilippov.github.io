raven

verb
to feed greedily or voraciously
8.1 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.vən/ /; / /ˈræ.vən/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.vən/ /; / /ˈræ.vən/ (BrE)
to eat food greedily or devour hungrily

Examples

To raven food
In the old tale, monsters raven the bodies of the fallen.
Starving, the wolves ravened their prey within minutes.
The lions ravened the fresh carcasses after the hunt.
Having fasted all day, he ravened the bread within minutes.
The children began to raven the moment dinner was served.
They ravened on the fruit, hardly stopping to breathe.
