master

noun
original copy used for duplication
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə / (BrE)
an original version of a recording, film, or other creative work from which copies are made

Examples

Master copy
After losing the master tape, the band had to re-record the entire album.
The studio kept the master in a secure vault to prevent unauthorized duplication.
They pressed copies of the vinyl record directly from the original master.

Related Terms

  • antonym: copy - a reproduction of an original item
  • degree higher: prototype - the first or preliminary version of a device or product
