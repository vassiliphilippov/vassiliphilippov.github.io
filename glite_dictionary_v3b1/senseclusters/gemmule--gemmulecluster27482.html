gemmule

noun
reproductive bud
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛmjul / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛmjuːl / (BrE)
a small reproductive structure or bud, especially in sponges or plants, that can develop into a new individual

Examples

Sponge gemmule formation
Some freshwater sponges survive winter by forming gemmules that remain dormant until conditions improve.
The biologist explained how a gemmule can give rise to a new sponge under the right conditions.
In certain plants, a gemmule acts as a means of asexual reproduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seed - the reproductive structure in plants that can grow into a new plant
  • synonym: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual
