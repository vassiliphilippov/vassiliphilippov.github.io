machzor

noun
Jewish holiday prayer book
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑxzɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɑːxzɔː / (BrE)
a Jewish prayer book used for special holidays such as Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur

Examples

A machzor prayer book
Each year, he purchases a new machzor for the High Holy Days.
The rabbi explained the special prayers found only in the machzor.
She carefully packed her machzor before leaving for synagogue on Yom Kippur.

Related Terms

  • antonym: siddur - a Jewish prayer book for regular daily and Sabbath services
