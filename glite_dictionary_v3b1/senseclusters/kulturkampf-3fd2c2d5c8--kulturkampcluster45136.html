Kulturkampf

noun
cultural conflict
Pronunciation: / ˈkʊltʊrˌkɑmpf /; / kʊlˈtuːrˌkɑmpf / (AmE), / ˈkʊltʊəˌkampf /; / kʊlˈtʊəˌkampf / (BrE)
a conflict or struggle between groups with different cultural or ideological values, especially between secular and religious groups or authorities

Examples

Modern Kulturkampf debate
The debate over education policy became a modern Kulturkampf.
Many historians study the Kulturkampf to understand the clash between church and state in Germany.
Social media has intensified the ongoing Kulturkampf between different political groups.
The government’s new laws sparked a modern Kulturkampf between secular and religious groups.
Historians often refer to the 19th-century German Kulturkampf as a turning point in church-state relations.
Debates over religious symbols in schools can sometimes resemble a Kulturkampf in society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culture war - a conflict between groups with different cultural values
  • antonym: harmony - a state of agreement or peaceful coexistence
  • antonym: religious tolerance - acceptance of different religious beliefs and practices
  • synonym: secularization - the process of removing religious influence from public life
