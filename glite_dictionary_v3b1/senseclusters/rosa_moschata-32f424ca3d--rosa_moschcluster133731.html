Rosa moschata

noun
species of musky-scented Mediterranean rose
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊzə mɒsˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə mɒsˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of rose native to the Mediterranean region, noted for its curved or climbing branches and loose clusters of musky-scented white flowers.
