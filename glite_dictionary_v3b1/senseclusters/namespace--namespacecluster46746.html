namespace

verb
to organize identifiers
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪmˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈneɪmˌspeɪs / (BrE)
to organize or qualify names or identifiers so they do not conflict with those in other contexts, typically by placing them in a namespace

Examples

To namespace code
To prevent naming conflicts, developers often namespace their code modules.
You should namespace your variables to avoid accidental overwriting in large projects.
The team decided to namespace all new functions for better organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: group - to put things together based on shared characteristics
  • synonym: qualify - to add information to make something more specific
