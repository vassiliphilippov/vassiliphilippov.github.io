river limpet

noun
freshwater mollusk resembling a limpet
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvər ˈlɪmpɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈlɪmpɪt / (BrE)
A small, conical freshwater gastropod mollusk that superficially resembles a limpet and lives on freshwater plants.

Examples

Small river limpets
Children collected river limpets during their field trip to the freshwater pond.
Scientists observed a colony of river limpets feeding on submerged aquatic plants.
The river limpet clings to the underside of rocks in fast-flowing streams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limpet - marine mollusk with a conical shell
