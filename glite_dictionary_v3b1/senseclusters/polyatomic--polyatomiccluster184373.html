polyatomic

adjective
consisting of more than two atoms (chemistry)
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliəˈtɑːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒliəˈtɒmɪk / (BrE)
(of a molecule) consisting of more than two atoms.

Examples

Polyatomic molecule
Many common ions, such as sulfate and nitrate, are polyatomic.
Ozone is a polyatomic molecule, because it contains three oxygen atoms.
The polyatomic nature of the compound determines its unique physical properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diatomic - consisting of two atoms
  • antonym: monatomic - consisting of a single atom
