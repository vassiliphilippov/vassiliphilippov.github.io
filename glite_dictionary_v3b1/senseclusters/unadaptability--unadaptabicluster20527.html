unadaptability

noun
inability to adapt to change
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.əˌdæp.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˌdæp.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being unable to adjust to new conditions or to be modified for different situations

Examples

His unadaptability to cultures
His unadaptability to different cultures made it hard for him to travel abroad.
Technological advances often expose the unadaptability of outdated systems.
The unadaptability of the company led to its downfall in a rapidly changing market.
