hermaphrodite

noun
organism with both male and female sexual traits
7.7
Pronunciation: / hərˈmæfɹəˌdaɪt / (AmE), / hɜːˈmæf.rə.daɪt / (BrE)
a person, animal, or plant that has both male and female reproductive organs or sexual characteristics

Examples

A hermaphrodite animal
Historically, some animals were mistakenly classified as hermaphrodites due to ambiguous physical traits.
In literature, the hermaphrodite often symbolizes a union of opposites.
The rare flower is a hermaphrodite with both stamens and pistils in the same blossom.
