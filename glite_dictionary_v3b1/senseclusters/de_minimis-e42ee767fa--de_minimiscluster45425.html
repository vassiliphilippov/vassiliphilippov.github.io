de minimis

noun
trivial legal matters
9.9
Pronunciation: /diː ˈmɪn.ɪ.mɪs/ /; / /də ˈmɪn.ɪ.mɪs/ /; / /deɪ ˈmɪn.ɪ.mɪs/ (AmE), /diː ˈmɪn.ɪ.mɪs/ /; / /də ˈmɪn.ɪ.mɪs/ /; / /deɪ ˈmɪn.ɪ.mɪs/ (BrE)
a legal principle stating that trivial matters are too minor to be considered by the law

Examples

De minimis rule
The court ruled that the error was de minimis and did not affect the outcome of the case.
Because the amount in question was de minimis, the lawsuit was quickly dismissed.
Lawyers argued that the alleged violation was so minor it should be considered de minimis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth
  • synonym: triviality - something of little importance or value
