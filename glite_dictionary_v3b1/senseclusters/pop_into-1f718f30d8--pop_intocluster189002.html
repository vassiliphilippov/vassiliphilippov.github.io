pop into

verb
occur suddenly in the mind
3.9 0.83/m

(of a thought, idea, or memory) to occur to someone suddenly and briefly; to come into someone's mind without deliberate effort.

Examples

An idea pops into mind
Suddenly a useful recipe idea popped into my head while I was washing the dishes.
Every time I try to concentrate, random memories pop into my mind and distract me.
An image of their old house popped into her thoughts during the quiet meeting.

Synonyms

  • come to mind
    3.0 8.00/m
    come to mind means you suddenly think of something, while pop into is more informal and suggests a very quick, brief idea.
  • spring to mind
    3.4 0.46/m
    spring to mind often comes quickly in speech as a clear example, while pop into suggests a sudden, brief thought.
  • occur
    2.4 20/m
    occur is used in formal or written language for an unexpected idea, while pop into suits casual speech for a quick, sudden thought.
  • pop up
    3.5 8.36/m
    pop up often refers to things, events, or windows that appear suddenly, while pop into refers only to sudden thoughts.
