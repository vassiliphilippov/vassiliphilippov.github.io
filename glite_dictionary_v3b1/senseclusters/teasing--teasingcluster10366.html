teasing

noun
making fun of or provoking someone
2.5 2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtizɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making fun of or provoking someone in a playful or malicious way, often with persistent jokes or ridicule

Examples

Constant teasing
His teasing sometimes crossed the line from playful to hurtful.
She enjoyed lighthearted teasing with her friends after school.
The constant teasing from his classmates made him feel uncomfortable.
Her constant teasing made him laugh, even though he pretended to be annoyed.
Some people don't realize when their teasing becomes hurtful rather than funny.
The teasing among friends was harmless and brought them closer together.
