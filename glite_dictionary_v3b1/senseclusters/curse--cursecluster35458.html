curse

noun
source of misery or harm
Pronunciation: / kɝs / (AmE), / kɜːs / (BrE)
a person, thing, or situation that causes great harm, trouble, or suffering

Examples

A curse of poverty
Malaria was once considered a curse in tropical regions.
Some say money can be a curse as well as a blessing.
Traffic is the curse of city life for many commuters.
For generations, the disease acted as a curse on the small village.
Some consider fame to be a curse rather than a blessing because of the loss of privacy.
The drought has been a curse on the region for years, devastating crops and livelihoods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bane - a cause of great distress or annoyance
  • antonym: blessing - a thing conducive to happiness or welfare
