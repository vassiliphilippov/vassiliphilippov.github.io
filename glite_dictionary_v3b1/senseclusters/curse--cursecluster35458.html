curse

noun
source of misery or harm
4.9 4.56/m
Pronunciation: / k…ùs / (AmE), / k…úÀês / (BrE)
a person, thing, or situation that causes great harm, trouble, or suffering

Examples

A curse of poverty
Malaria was once considered a curse in tropical regions.
Some say money can be a curse as well as a blessing.
Traffic is the curse of city life for many commuters.
For generations, the disease acted as a curse on the small village.
Some consider fame to be a curse rather than a blessing because of the loss of privacy.
The drought has been a curse on the region for years, devastating crops and livelihoods.

Synonyms

  • scourge
    8.2 1.61/m
    a scourge is a severe, spreading problem that harms many people and places, while a curse is any cause of great suffering.
  • bane
    7.8 1.86/m
    a bane is a single cause that ruins or weakens someone over time, while a curse can mean any source of great harm.
  • affliction
    6.7 3.48/m
    an affliction is a lasting illness or trouble that causes suffering, while a curse is a general cause of harm or trouble.
  • torment
    5.6 4.52/m
    torment is severe physical or mental pain someone feels, whereas a curse is a person or thing that causes great suffering.
