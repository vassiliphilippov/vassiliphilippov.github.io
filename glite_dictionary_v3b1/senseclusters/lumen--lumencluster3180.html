lumen

noun
unit of luminous flux (light intensity)
7.5 0.4/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀê.m…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàluÀê.m…ôn / (BrE)
a unit of luminous flux in the International System of Units, equal to the amount of light emitted in a solid angle of one steradian by a point source of one candela

Examples

A 100 lumen bulb
For energy efficiency, compare the lumen output rather than the wattage of light sources.
The projector's brightness is rated at 2,500 lumens, ensuring clear images even in daylight.
This new light bulb produces 800 lumens, making it suitable for reading.
