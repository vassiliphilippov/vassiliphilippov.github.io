collectivity

noun
group action principle
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːlɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌkɒlɛkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality or principle of acting as a group, emphasizing shared responsibility or common ownership

Examples

Collectivity in action
The collectivity of the team allowed them to achieve more than any individual could alone.
In some cultures, collectivity is valued over personal ambition or competition.
The success of the project depended on the collectivity of all its members working together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over individuals
  • antonym: individualism - the belief in the importance of individual rights and independence
