nonconsumptive

adjective
not involving consumption
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈsʌmptɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈsʌmptɪv / (BrE)
describing effects on organisms caused by predators or competitors that do not involve direct killing or eating

Examples

Nonconsumptive predator effects
The presence of predators can have significant nonconsumptive effects on prey populations.
Scientists measured the nonconsumptive impact of competition on plant growth in the ecosystem.
Many ecological studies now consider both consumptive and nonconsumptive influences on animal behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consumptive - involving the consumption or direct use of something
