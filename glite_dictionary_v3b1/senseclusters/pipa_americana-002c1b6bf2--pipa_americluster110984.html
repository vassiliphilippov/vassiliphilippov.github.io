Pipa americana

noun
South American toad species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːpə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈpiːpə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of South American toad that incubates its young in pits on the skin of its back.

Examples

Pipa americana toad
Herpetologists studied the unusual reproductive habits of Pipa americana in the Amazon rainforest.
The female Pipa americana carries her eggs in honeycomb-like pits on her back until they hatch.
Unlike many amphibians, Pipa americana provides a remarkable form of parental care for its young.
