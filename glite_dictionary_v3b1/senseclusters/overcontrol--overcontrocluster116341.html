overcontrol

noun
excessive regulation
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
excessive regulation or management that restricts freedom or flexibility, often causing negative consequences

Examples

Strict overcontrol
The team's overcontrol of the project led to frustration among creative members.
Parents should avoid overcontrol to help their children develop independence.
In some organizations, overcontrol can prevent employees from taking initiative.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think without restraint
  • synonym: micromanagement - controlling every small detail of a task or project
