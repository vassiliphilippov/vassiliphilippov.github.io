Eratosthenes

proper noun
Greek mathematician and astronomer (c. 276–194 BC)
9.9
Pronunciation: /er.ə.ˈtɑːs.θə.niːz/ (AmE), /er.ə.ˈtɒs.θə.niːz/ (BrE)
(c. 276–194 BC) A Greek mathematician, geographer, and astronomer, best known for calculating the Earth's circumference and measuring the distances to the Moon and Sun.

Examples

Eratosthenes measured Earth
Eratosthenes accurately estimated the circumference of the Earth over two thousand years ago.
Many historians regard Eratosthenes as one of the most important figures in ancient science.
The sieve of Eratosthenes is a classical algorithm for finding prime numbers.
