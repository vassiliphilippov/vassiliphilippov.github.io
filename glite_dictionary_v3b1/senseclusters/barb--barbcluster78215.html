barb

noun
filament projecting from a feather shaft
Pronunciation: / bɑrb / (AmE), / bɑːb / (BrE)
one of the slender, parallel filaments that project from the main shaft of a feather and interlock to form the feather's vane

Examples

Feather barb
Each feather consists of a central shaft with hundreds of barbs on either side.
The barbs of a bird's feather help hook together, giving the feather its smooth surface.
When examined under a microscope, the tiny barbs are clearly visible along the feather's shaft.
