iliac vein

noun
vein draining the pelvic region
Pronunciation: / ˈɪliˌæk veɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪliːæk veɪn / (BrE)
any of three large veins in the pelvic region that drain blood from the lower limbs and pelvis into the inferior vena cava.

Examples

The iliac vein drains
Blood from the lower limbs collects in the iliac vein before entering the inferior vena cava.
Deep vein thrombosis can sometimes occur in the iliac vein, causing swelling and pain in the leg.
The surgeon carefully identified the iliac vein during the procedure to prevent accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: femoral vein - large vein in the thigh that becomes the external iliac vein
  • degree higher: inferior vena cava - large vein receiving blood from the iliac veins and other lower body veins
