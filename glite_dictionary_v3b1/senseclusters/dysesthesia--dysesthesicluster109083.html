dysesthesia

noun
abnormal unpleasant sensation
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɛsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɛsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
an abnormal and unpleasant sensation, such as burning, tingling, or pain, often caused by nerve damage

Examples

Burning dysesthesia
The patient described a burning dysesthesia in her feet after the nerve injury.
After chemotherapy, some people experience dysesthesia that makes even light touch uncomfortable.
Doctors often ask about dysesthesia when diagnosing nerve disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anesthesia - loss of sensation in a part of the body
  • synonym: paresthesia - an abnormal sensation such as tingling or prickling, usually not painful
