SOE

noun
WWII British agency
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs oʊ ˈi / (AmE), / ˌɛs əʊ ˈiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'Special Operations Executive', a British World War II organization responsible for espionage and supporting resistance movements

Examples

British SOE agents
The SOE was created to conduct espionage and sabotage behind enemy lines during World War II.
Many brave agents served in the SOE, risking their lives to support European resistance groups.
Historians continue to study the impact of the SOE on the outcome of the war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: OSS - the American Office of Strategic Services during World War II
