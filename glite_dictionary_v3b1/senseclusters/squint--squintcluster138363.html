squint

noun
the act of looking with eyes partly closed
4.5 4.3
Pronunciation: / skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
an act of looking at something with your eyes partly closed, usually in order to see more clearly or because of bright light

Examples

A quick squint
A prolonged squint can leave your eyes feeling tired.
He took a squint at his watch in the bright sunlight.
She gave the screen a quick squint before deciding to call for help.

  • synonym: glance - a quick look at something
  • degree higher: stare - a long and fixed look
