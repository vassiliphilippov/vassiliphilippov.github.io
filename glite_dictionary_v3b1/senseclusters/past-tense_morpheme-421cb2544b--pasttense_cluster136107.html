past-tense morpheme

noun
morpheme marking past tense
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpæst ˌtɛns ˈmɔːrfiːm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːst ˌtɛns ˈmɔːfiːm / (BrE)
A morpheme that indicates that a verb is in the past tense.

Examples

The past-tense morpheme -ed
In English, the '-ed' ending in 'walked' is a past-tense morpheme.
Some languages use an internal vowel change as a past-tense morpheme.
The word 'talked' contains the past-tense morpheme '-ed'.
