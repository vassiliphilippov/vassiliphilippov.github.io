bicoastal

noun
person living on both coasts
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪˈkoʊstl̩ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈkəʊstl̩ / (BrE)
a person who lives or works on both the east and west coasts of a country, especially the United States

Examples

A bicoastal travels
As a bicoastal, she split her time between New York and Los Angeles for her job.
Many bicoastals find it challenging to maintain homes on both coasts.
He became a bicoastal after his company opened a second office in San Francisco.
