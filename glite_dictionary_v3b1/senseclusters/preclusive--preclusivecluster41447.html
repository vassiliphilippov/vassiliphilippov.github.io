preclusive

adjective
preventing something from happening
Pronunciation: / prɪˈkluːsɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈkluːsɪv / (BrE)
preventing the possibility of something; making something impossible.

Examples

Preclusive effect
Her absence was preclusive of any chance for reconciliation.
Strict rules can be preclusive to innovation in the workplace.
The new law has a preclusive effect on future claims.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preventive - intended to stop something from happening
