fascistization

noun
adoption of fascism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfæʃɪstəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæʃɪstaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of a society, organization, or government adopting or being controlled by fascist principles, policies, or practices

Examples

The fascistization of the government led to strict control over the media and public life.
Many historians have studied the fascistization of institutions during the early twentieth century.
Activists warned against the slow fascistization of political discourse in recent years.
The fascistization of the media led to strict censorship and loss of independent reporting.
Many citizens protested against the gradual fascistization of their country's institutions.
Historians debate the causes behind the fascistization of the government during that era.
