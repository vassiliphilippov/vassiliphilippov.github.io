diaphragmatic pleurisy

noun
acute chest pain due to diaphragm infection
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.ə.frəɡˈmæt̬.ɪk ˈplʊr.ə.si / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.ə.frəɡˈmat.ɪk ˈplʊə.rə.si / (BrE)
An acute inflammation of the diaphragm's covering membrane (pleura), characterized by sudden, severe chest pain, usually due to infection.

Examples

Severe diaphragmatic pleurisy pain
Doctors treated her diaphragmatic pleurisy promptly to reduce the risk of further complications.
Inflammation from diaphragmatic pleurisy can make deep breathing extremely painful.
The patient was diagnosed with diaphragmatic pleurisy after experiencing abrupt, intense chest pain.
