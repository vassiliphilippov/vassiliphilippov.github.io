Huntington's chorea

noun
hereditary neurological disorder causing dementia
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌntɪŋtənz kəˈriə / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋtənz kəˈriːə / (BrE)
A hereditary neurological disorder in adults characterized by involuntary movements and progressive mental deterioration, often leading to dementia.

Examples

Huntington's chorea disease
Her father was diagnosed with Huntington's chorea, and genetic testing revealed she had inherited the condition.
Research into Huntington's chorea has advanced understanding of other neurodegenerative diseases as well.
The onset of Huntington's chorea usually occurs in mid-adulthood, gradually affecting both movement and cognition.
