synophthalmia

noun
fused eyes defect
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪnɑfˈθælmiə / (AmE), / ˌsɪnɒfˈθælmiə / (BrE)
a rare congenital condition in which the eyes are partially or completely fused together

Examples

Synophthalmia condition
Doctors identified synophthalmia in the ultrasound images before the baby was born.
Cases of synophthalmia are extremely rare and often associated with other severe abnormalities.
The textbook included a detailed illustration of synophthalmia for medical students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclopia - a birth defect where a single eye is present in the center of the forehead
