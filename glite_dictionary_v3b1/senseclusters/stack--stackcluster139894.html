stack

verb
arrange unfairly to one's advantage
8.2
Pronunciation: / stæk / (AmE), / stak / (BrE)
to arrange the order of something, especially so as to unfairly increase one's advantage or chance of success

Examples

To stack the deck
He was caught attempting to stack the board with his own supporters.
She accused the organizers of stacking the deck against her from the start.
There are claims that the company tried to stack the hiring process in favor of internal candidates.
