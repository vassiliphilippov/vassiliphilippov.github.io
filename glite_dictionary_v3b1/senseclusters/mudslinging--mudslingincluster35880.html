mudslinging

adjective
unfairly accusatory
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌslɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌslɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
characterized by making unfair or malicious accusations, especially to damage someone's reputation.

Examples

Mudslinging debate
The debate quickly turned mudslinging as both candidates began making personal attacks.
Voters grew tired of the mudslinging advertisements that dominated the election season.
She refused to participate in the mudslinging campaign against her opponent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defamatory - intended to harm someone's reputation with false statements
  • antonym: praiseworthy - deserving approval or admiration
  • synonym: slanderous - containing false and damaging statements about someone
