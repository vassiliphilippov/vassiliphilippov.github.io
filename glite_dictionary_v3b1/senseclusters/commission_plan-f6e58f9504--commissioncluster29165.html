commission plan

noun
system of city government by commission
6.9 7.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪʃ.ən plæn / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ.ən plan / (BrE)
A system of municipal government in which legislative and executive powers are vested in a small commission, with each member responsible for a specific department.

Examples

The commission plan government
Many American cities experimented with the commission plan in the early twentieth century.
The city adopted the commission plan to improve the efficiency of its administration.
Under the commission plan, each commissioner oversees a different department, such as public safety or finance.
