ill

adjective
not well; suffering from a disease
1.0 30/m
Pronunciation: / Éªl / (AmE), / Éªl / (BrE)
suffering from a disease or not in good health; affected by an impairment of normal physical or mental function.

Examples

Feeling ill
He felt ill after eating food from the street vendor.
Many employees called in ill after the flu swept through the office.
She has been ill for several days and needs to see a doctor.

Synonyms

  • sick
    1.0 60/m
    sick means affected by illness in everyday speech, while ill is a more neutral medical word for poor health.
  • unwell
    1.0 0.7/m
    unwell means not feeling well or comfortable, while ill suggests a medical condition or more serious poor health.
  • poorly
    2.8 0.00/m
    poorly is a common British word for feeling unwell, while ill is standard and used in both British and other English settings.
  • ailing
    5.6 0.3/m
    ailing describes someone in long-term or weak health, while ill covers any sickness short or long.
  • under the weather
    5.3 0.4/m
    under the weather is an informal phrase for feeling slightly unwell, while ill is a direct term for poor health.
  • indisposed
    6.9 0.00/m
    indisposed often means slightly ill or unwilling to act, while ill clearly means being medically unwell.
