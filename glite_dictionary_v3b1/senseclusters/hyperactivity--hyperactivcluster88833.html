hyperactivity

noun
excessive activity and restlessness
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərækˈtɪvɪti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərəˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
a condition marked by abnormally high levels of activity, restlessness, or impulsiveness, especially in children

Examples

Child hyperactivity
Children with hyperactivity may find it difficult to sit still or concentrate in class.
Excessive sugar intake is sometimes wrongly blamed for causing hyperactivity in children.
The doctor explained that hyperactivity can be a symptom of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypoactivity - a condition marked by low levels of activity or movement
