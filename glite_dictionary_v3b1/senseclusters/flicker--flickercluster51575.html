flicker

noun
momentary flash of light
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪkər /; / ˈflɪk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪkə /; / ˈflɪk.ə / (BrE)
a brief or unsteady flash of light, movement, or feeling

Examples

A flicker of light
A sudden flicker of lightning illuminated the dark sky.
The television emitted a brief flicker before turning off completely.
There was a flicker from the candle before it went out.
She noticed a flicker of hesitation before he answered.
The candle gave off only a faint flicker in the dark room.
There was a flicker of movement in the shadows behind the curtain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flash - a sudden brief burst of bright light
  • synonym: flutter - a quick, light movement or fluttering
