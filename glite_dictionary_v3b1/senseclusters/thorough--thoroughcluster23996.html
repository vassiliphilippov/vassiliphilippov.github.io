thorough

adjective
used informally to intensify a negative quality
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈθʌrə / (BrE)
(informal, often pejorative) Used to emphasize the extent or intensity of a quality, especially something regarded as bad or undesirable.

Examples

Thorough nuisance
He's a thorough nuisance who causes problems wherever he goes.
I left the meeting feeling like a thorough fool for forgetting my notes.
The report described the project as a thorough failure from start to finish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complete - full; with all parts or elements
  • antonym: partial - not complete; only in part
  • synonym: utter - complete; absolute (especially used to intensify negatives)
