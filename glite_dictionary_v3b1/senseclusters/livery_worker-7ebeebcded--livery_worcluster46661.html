livery worker

noun
worker at a livery stable
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪvəri ˈwɝːkər / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri ˈwɜːkə / (BrE)
A person employed to look after horses and stables at a livery stable.

Examples

livery worker at stable
In the 19th century, every town needed at least one livery worker to care for travelers' horses.
She took a summer job as a livery worker at the local stables.
The livery worker carefully groomed each horse before the day's first ride.
