Zoonomia

proper noun
1794 book by Darwin
Pronunciation: / zoʊəˈnoʊmiə / (AmE), / zəʊəˈnəʊmiə / (BrE)
the title of a 1794 book by Erasmus Darwin discussing theories about the laws and functions of animal life and physiology

Examples

Zoonomia by Darwin
Erasmus Darwin's Zoonomia was influential in early discussions about animal physiology.
Many historians study Zoonomia to understand the development of evolutionary ideas before Charles Darwin.
The theories presented in Zoonomia sparked debate among scientists in the late eighteenth century.
