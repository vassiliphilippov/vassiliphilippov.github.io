pull the trigger

verb
make a final decision; take decisive action
Pronunciation: / pʊl ðə ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / pʊl ðə ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
to make a final decision or take decisive action, especially after some hesitation

Examples

To pull the trigger finally
After months of consideration, they finally pulled the trigger on opening their own restaurant.
He hesitated for weeks before he pulled the trigger and accepted the job offer.
If you're serious about moving, it's time to pull the trigger and contact a real estate agent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hesitate - to pause or delay before deciding
  • synonym: make a decision - to decide; to reach a conclusion
