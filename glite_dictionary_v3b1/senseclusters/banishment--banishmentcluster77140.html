banishment

noun
the act of sending someone away as punishment
8.5 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbæn.ɪʃ.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈbæn.ɪʃ.mənt/ (BrE)
the act or state of being officially forced to leave a place, country, or community, usually as a punishment

Examples

Official banishment from country
After years of banishment, she was finally allowed to return home.
Political banishment was a common punishment in ancient times.
The king ordered the banishment of the traitor from the kingdom.
His banishment from the country lasted several years.
Social banishment can be just as painful as physical exile.
The law once allowed for the banishment of criminals to distant colonies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave a place
  • antonym: inclusion - the action or state of including or being included
  • synonym: ostracism - exclusion from a society or group
