melanoderma

noun
abnormally dark skin condition
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛl.əˈnɒd.ər.mə / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.əˈnɒd.ə.mə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by abnormally dark skin due to increased deposits of melanin.

Examples

Dark melanoderma patches
After years of medication, the patient developed melanoderma on her arms.
Sun exposure can worsen melanoderma in susceptible individuals.
The dermatologist diagnosed him with melanoderma after noticing pronounced skin darkening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hyperpigmentation - excessive pigmentation of the skin
