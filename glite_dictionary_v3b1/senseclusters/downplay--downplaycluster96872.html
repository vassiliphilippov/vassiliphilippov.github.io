downplay

verb
make seem less important
9.2 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.pleɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.pleɪ/ (BrE)
to make something seem less important or less serious than it really is

Examples

To downplay the problem
During the interview, he tried to downplay his involvement in the scandal.
She always downplays her accomplishments, preferring to let her work speak for itself.
The company spokesperson attempted to downplay the risks associated with the new product.
Officials attempted to downplay the seriousness of the situation to avoid public panic.
She tends to downplay her achievements, even though she's very successful.
The company tried to downplay the impact of the scandal in its official statement.
