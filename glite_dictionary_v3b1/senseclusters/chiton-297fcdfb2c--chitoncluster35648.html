Chiton

noun
a genus of marine mollusks (chitons)
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈkʌɪtən / (BrE)
A genus of marine mollusks in the class Polyplacophora, commonly known as chitons, characterized by their eight-plated shells.

Examples

Chiton species
Many Chiton species inhabit tidal zones where they cling to rocks.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Chiton genus along the rocky shore.
The Chiton genus is easily identified by its distinctive overlapping shell plates.
