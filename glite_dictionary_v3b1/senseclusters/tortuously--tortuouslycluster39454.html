tortuously

adverb
in a winding or complex manner
9.1 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îr.t Éu.…ôs.li /; / Ààt…îrt Éu…ôsli / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀê.tj ä.…ôs.li /; / Ààt…îÀêtjuÀê…ôsli / (BrE)
in a complicated, winding, or overly complex way; with many twists and turns

Examples

To tortuously explain a story
She tortuously explained her point, making it difficult for others to follow her logic.
The river wound tortuously through the dense forest before reaching the sea.
Their negotiations proceeded tortuously, taking months to reach a final agreement.
He explained the plan tortuously, confusing everyone with unnecessary complexities and digressions.
The path tortuously ascended the hillside, doubling back on itself several times.
The river winds tortuously through the mountains, making navigation difficult.
