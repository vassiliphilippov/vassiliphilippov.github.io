do someone any favors

verb
help or benefit someone
5.1 1.20/m
Pronunciation: / du Ààs åmw ån Àà…õni Ààfe…™v…öz / (AmE), / duÀê Ààs åmw ån Ààeni Ààfe…™v…ôz / (BrE)
to help or benefit someone; to provide a service or act that assists another person, often with the implication that such help may not be as beneficial as it seems.

Examples

To do someone any favors by helping
He didn't do her any favors by covering up the mistake.
Quitting now won't do the team any favors before the big game.
You aren't doing yourself any favors by skipping breakfast every morning.

Synonyms

  • help
    1.0 151/m
    help is general support or aid, while do someone any favors suggests offering a small or possibly misleading benefit.
  • assist
    1.0 55/m
    assist is a more formal, task-focused way to help, while do someone any favors is more casual and may imply limited benefit.
  • give a hand
    1.8 9.74/m
    give a hand is an informal phrase for helping or applause, while do someone any favors focuses on giving help that may not help much.
  • oblige
    4.1 4.55/m
    oblige means do something to please or meet a request, while do someone any favors often carries expectation or polite pressure.
