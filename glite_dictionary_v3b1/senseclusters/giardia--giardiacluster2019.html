giardia

noun
intestinal parasite causing diarrhea
9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /dʒiː.ˈɑːr.diː.ə/ /; / /dʒɪ.ˈɑːr.diː.ə/ (AmE), /dʒiː.ˈɑː.dɪ.ə/ /; / /dʒɪ.ˈɑː.dɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A microscopic parasite that infects the intestines and is a common cause of diarrhea in humans and animals.

Examples

The giardia parasite
Drinking untreated water can expose you to giardia, which may cause severe diarrhea.
Proper handwashing can help prevent the spread of giardia in childcare settings.
The doctor diagnosed her with an infection caused by giardia after her camping trip.
