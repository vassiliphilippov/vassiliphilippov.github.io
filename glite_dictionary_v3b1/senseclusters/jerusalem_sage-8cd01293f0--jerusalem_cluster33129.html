Jerusalem sage

noun
Mediterranean subshrub with purple or yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈruːsələm seɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒəˈruːsələm seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a spreading subshrub native to Mediterranean regions, cultivated for its dense whorls of purple or yellow flowers

Examples

Jerusalem sage plant
He chose Jerusalem sage for its drought-resistant qualities and attractive flowers.
The garden’s border was filled with blooming Jerusalem sage in early summer.
Yellow-flowered Jerusalem sage added a splash of color to the rockery.
