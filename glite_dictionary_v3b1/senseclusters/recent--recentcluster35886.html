recent

adjective
new; not long ago
Pronunciation: / ˈriːsənt / (AmE), / ˈriːsənt / (BrE)
having happened or started not long ago; new or fresh

Examples

A recent event
A recent storm caused significant damage in several neighborhoods.
She displayed the recent paintings she had completed over the summer.
The company is proud of its recent achievements in renewable energy.
Her promotion to manager is a fairly recent development in the company.
I've read about this breakthrough in a recent scientific journal.
The earthquake was a recent event that affected the whole community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: old - having lived or existed for a long time; not new
