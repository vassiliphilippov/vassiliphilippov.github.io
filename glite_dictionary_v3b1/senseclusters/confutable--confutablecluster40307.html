confutable

adjective
able to be refuted
8.4 8.7
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈfjuː.tə.bəl/ /; / /kən.ˈfuː.tə.bəl/ (AmE), /kən.ˈfjuː.tə.bəl/ /; / /kɒn.ˈfjuː.tə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of an argument or claim) able to be proved wrong or refuted.

Examples

A confutable argument
Any argument is confutable if new facts can be presented against it.
Her explanation was confutable, as several witnesses contradicted her story.
The theory was considered easily confutable due to a lack of supporting evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrefutable - impossible to refute; indisputable
  • synonym: refutable - able to be proved false or wrong
