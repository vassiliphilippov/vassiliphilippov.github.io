quantize

verb
approximate by discrete values (in signals)
9.8 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkw…ëÀên.ta…™z /; / Ààkw…ënÀåta…™z / (AmE), / Ààkw…ín.ta…™z /; / Ààkw…ín.t å…™z / (BrE)
to change something that varies continuously (like a signal or value) into a set of separate, fixed steps or amounts; in digital/audio contexts, to approximate a continuously varying signal by restricting its amplitude to a set of discrete values.

Examples

To quantize a signal
Audio engineers often quantize signals during digital recording to reduce file size.
If you quantize the input, small variations in signal strength are ignored.
Modern image processing algorithms quantize colors to simplify complex images.
If you quantise the data too coarsely, quality will suffer.
Modern audio systems quantise the analog signal before storage.
The software can automatically quantise notes entered by the user.
In digital audio, engineers quantise the signal to convert it into discrete values.
Physicists have long tried to quantise gravity, but it remains an unsolved problem.
The researchers aimed to quantise the electromagnetic field in their experiment.
