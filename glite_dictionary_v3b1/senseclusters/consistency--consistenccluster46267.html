consistency

noun
absence of contradictions in a logical system
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɪstənsi / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being logical, coherent, or free from contradiction

Examples

Logical consistency
A mathematical system is valued for its consistency, ensuring no theorems contradict one another.
Before accepting new axioms, logicians test their consistency with existing mathematical frameworks.
Gödel’s incompleteness theorem explores the limits of consistency within formal mathematical systems.
Her report was praised for its consistency and attention to detail.
The witness's statements lacked consistency with the evidence presented.
Without consistency in your story, people will question your credibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coherence - the quality of being logical, consistent, and forming a unified whole
  • antonym: contradiction - a combination of ideas or statements that are opposed to one another
  • antonym: inconsistency - the state of containing contradictions
