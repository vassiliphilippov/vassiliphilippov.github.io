rollover

noun
unused plan allowance carried forward
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.loʊ.vər/ /; / /ˈroʊ.lə.vər/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.ləʊ.və/ /; / /ˈrəʊ.lə.və/ (BrE)
An amount of a mobile or subscription plan (for example, data, minutes, or messages) that was unused in one billing period and is permitted to be used in a later billing period.

Examples

My plan gives 5GB and any unused 1GB rollover automatically appears next month.
They advertised free international minutes and a two-month rollover for unused call time.
Consumers complained when the provider removed the rollover feature from its cheapest plan.
