carpetbagger

noun
outsider seeking personal gain, esp. in politics
5.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːr.pət.bæɡ.ər/ /; / /ˈkɑːr.pɪt.bæɡ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.pɪt.bæɡ.ə/ /; / /ˈkɑː.pət.bæɡ.ə/ (BrE)
A person who moves to a place to exploit opportunities, particularly for personal or political gain, often viewed as an outsider with little real connection to the place.

Examples

A carpetbagger politician
Many locals resented the arrival of the carpetbagger who wanted to run for mayor.
Some politicians are accused of being carpetbaggers when they move districts just to run for office.
The company's new CEO was criticized as a carpetbagger with no commitment to the community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insider - a person who belongs to a group, organization, or place and has special knowledge or influence
  • synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities with little regard for principles
