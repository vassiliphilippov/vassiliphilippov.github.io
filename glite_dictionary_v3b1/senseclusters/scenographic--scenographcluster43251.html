scenographic

adjective
relating to visual staging
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsiːnəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiːnəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the visual design or arrangement of scenes or spaces to create a dramatic effect, especially in theater, exhibitions, film, or photography

Examples

Scenographic design
The exhibition featured a scenographic layout that made visitors feel like they were part of a story.
Designers used scenographic techniques to transform the lobby into an immersive experience.
Her approach to museum curation is highly scenographic, focusing on dramatic lighting and spatial effects.
The director praised the scenographic choices that brought the story to life.
Her scenographic skills transformed the stage into a magical forest.
A strong scenographic approach can greatly enhance the audience's experience.
The film's scenographic elements created a unique atmosphere that drew viewers into the story.
Her scenographic skills were evident in the way she arranged the lighting and props for the photo shoot.
Critics praised the scenographic choices that made each scene visually striking and memorable.
