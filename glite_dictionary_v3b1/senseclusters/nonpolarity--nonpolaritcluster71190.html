nonpolarity

noun
diffused global power
9.5
Pronunciation: / nɑnpoʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / nɒnpəˈlarəti / (BrE)
a situation in international relations where power is distributed among many actors, with no single state or group dominating

Examples

Global nonpolarity
The rise of new economic powers contributed to global nonpolarity in the twenty-first century.
Some analysts believe nonpolarity makes international cooperation more challenging.
In a world of nonpolarity, no single country can easily set the global agenda.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bipolarity - a system dominated by two major powers or blocs
  • synonym: multipolarity - a system where several states or groups hold significant power
  • antonym: unipolarity - a system dominated by a single powerful state
