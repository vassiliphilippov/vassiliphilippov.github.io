dredge

verb
remove material from water's bottom
8.0
Pronunciation: / drɛdʒ / (AmE), / drɛdʒ / (BrE)
to remove mud, sand, or other material from the bottom of water, or to search the bottom of water for something, usually using special equipment

Examples

To dredge a river
After years of neglect, it took weeks to dredge the accumulated silt from the canal.
Authorities plan to dredge the harbor next spring to allow larger ships to dock.
The crew used a barge to dredge the riverbed and deepen the channel.
Divers were called in to dredge the lake for evidence after the accident.
Rescuers had to dredge the harbor floor to locate the sunken boat.
They used a large net to dredge the riverbed for missing jewelry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drag - pull something with force, often along the ground or through water
  • synonym: excavate - to dig out and remove earth, especially from the ground
