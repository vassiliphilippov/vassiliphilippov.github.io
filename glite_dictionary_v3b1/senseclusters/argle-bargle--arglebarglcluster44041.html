argle-bargle

noun
meaningless talk; wrangling.
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrɡl ˌbɑrɡl /; / ˈɑːrɡəl ˌbɑːrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡl ˌbɑːɡl / (BrE)
noisy, silly, or meaningless talk; pointless argument or chatter

Examples

Pointless argle-bargle
After hours of argle-bargle, the committee still couldn’t reach a decision.
She grew tired of all the argle-bargle and left the room in frustration.
Their meeting was filled with pointless argle-bargle instead of productive discussion.
After two hours of argle-bargle, the meeting ended with no decisions made.
He dismissed the rumors as mere argle-bargle and refused to comment further.
The politician's speech was nothing but argle-bargle designed to confuse the audience.
