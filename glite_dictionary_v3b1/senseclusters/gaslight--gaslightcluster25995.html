gaslight

verb
to manipulate reality
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈgæs.laɪt/ (AmE), /ˈgæs.laɪt/ (BrE)
to manipulate someone so that they begin to doubt their own perception, memory, or sanity

Examples

To gaslight a friend
She realized he was trying to gaslight her by denying things he had clearly said.
In the movie, the villain gaslights his victim to make her question her own memory.
Friends warned him that his partner might be gaslighting him after he started doubting his own experiences.
He tried to gaslight her by insisting she was imagining things that had really happened.
Victims may not realize they are being gaslighted until their confidence is deeply shaken.
It is wrong to gaslight someone just to avoid taking responsibility for your actions.
She realized he was trying to gaslight her by constantly denying things he had done.
After months of being gaslighted, he began to question his own memory.
