Pan

proper noun
Greek god of nature and shepherds
7.7
Pronunciation: /pæn/ (AmE), /pæn/ (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the fields, woods, shepherds, and flocks, often depicted as a man with the legs, horns, and ears of a goat; identified with the Roman gods Sylvanus and Faunus.

Examples

Greek god Pan
In myths, Pan is known for his wild nature and musical abilities.
Many ancient statues depict Pan playing his pipes in the forests.
The Romans often identified their woodland god Faunus with the Greek Pan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Faunus - Roman god of the countryside, similar to Pan
  • synonym: Sylvanus - Roman woodland deity, associated with the Greek Pan
