imperii

noun
Latin genitive form
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛri.aɪ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛri.aɪ / (BrE)
the Latin genitive form of 'imperium', meaning 'of the empire' or 'of power', used in legal or historical contexts

Examples

The imperii power
The phrase 'maiestas imperii' was important in Roman law.
Historians often refer to 'finis imperii' when discussing the boundaries of the Roman Empire.
In ancient texts, 'auctoritas imperii' described the authority of the emperor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imperium - Latin noun meaning command, power, or empire
