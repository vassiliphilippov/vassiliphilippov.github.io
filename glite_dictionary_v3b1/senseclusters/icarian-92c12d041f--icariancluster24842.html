Icarian

adjective
recklessly ambitious
9.9
Pronunciation: / aɪˈkɛriən / (AmE), / aɪˈkeəriən / (BrE)
showing reckless ambition or overconfidence, especially in a way that leads to failure, like Icarus in Greek mythology

Examples

Icarian ambition
His Icarian dreams of instant success ended in disappointment.
The company's Icarian expansion into new markets proved unsustainable.
She warned him not to pursue such an Icarian course without considering the risks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overambitious - having excessive ambition or goals
  • antonym: prudent - acting with care and thought for the future
