Lysippus

proper noun
Greek sculptor of the 4th century BC
Pronunciation: / laɪˈsɪpəs / (AmE), / laɪˈsɪpəs / (BrE)
An ancient Greek sculptor of the 4th century BC, renowned for his innovations in the proportions of the human figure.

Examples

Lysippus the sculptor
Lysippus was said to be the favored sculptor of Alexander the Great.
Many art historians admire Lysippus for his influence on later sculpture in the Hellenistic period.
The statue attributed to Lysippus displays a remarkable sense of realism and movement.
