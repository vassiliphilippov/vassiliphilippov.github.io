BPD

noun
lung disease in infants
Pronunciation: / bi pi di / (AmE), / biː piː diː / (BrE)
a chronic lung disease in premature infants, often caused by prolonged oxygen therapy or mechanical ventilation

Examples

Infant BPD diagnosis
Doctors diagnosed the premature baby with BPD after several weeks of respiratory support.
Infants with BPD often require ongoing medical care after leaving the hospital.
The risk of developing BPD increases with the length of time a newborn needs ventilation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bronchopulmonary dysplasia - a chronic lung disorder in newborns, especially premature infants
  • synonym: respiratory distress syndrome - a breathing disorder in newborns, especially those born prematurely
