ectopic

adjective
occurring in an abnormal position
4.9
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈtɑpɪk / (AmE), / ɛkˈtɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of a body part or process) occurring in an abnormal position or place; displaced from its usual location.

Examples

An ectopic heartbeat
An ectopic heartbeat can sometimes cause palpitations or irregular heart rhythms.
Doctors identified ectopic tissue growth outside the intended organ.
The patient was diagnosed with an ectopic pregnancy, which required immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal - occurring in the correct or usual position
