Proserpine

proper noun
Roman goddess of the underworld
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːsərˌpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɒsəˌpiːn / (BrE)
the Roman goddess of the underworld, regarded as the counterpart of the Greek Persephone.

Examples

Proserpine goddess underworld
In ancient Roman mythology, Proserpine was abducted by Pluto and taken to the underworld.
Many Renaissance artists depicted Proserpine in their paintings, emphasizing her dual role as goddess of death and rebirth.
The story of Proserpine symbolizes the changing seasons and the cycle of life and death.
