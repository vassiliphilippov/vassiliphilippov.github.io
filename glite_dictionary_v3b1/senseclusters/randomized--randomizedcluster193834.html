randomized

adjective
chosen or arranged at random
3.2 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈræn.də.maɪzd/ (AmE), /ˈræn.də.maɪzd/ (BrE)
set up, chosen, or distributed in a deliberately random manner, especially to avoid bias or increase fairness

Examples

A randomized order
Contestants were placed in randomized order before being called up to the stage.
The patients were assigned to randomized groups to ensure unbiased results.
This study uses a randomized control trial design to test the new drug.
A randomised sample was selected to avoid selection bias.
Patients were randomised into two groups to ensure fairness in the experiment.
The study was a randomised controlled trial involving 200 participants.
