classically

adverb
in the style of ancient Greece and Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪkli / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is traditional or characteristic of classical style, especially in art, music, or ancient Greek and Roman culture

Examples

To be classically styled
She posed for a portrait classically draped, reminiscent of ancient statues.
The building is classically designed, featuring columns and marble facades.
This sculpture is classically inspired, echoing the art of ancient Rome.
She was classically trained in ballet from a young age.
The building is classically designed, with columns and marble floors.
He approaches problems classically, relying on established theories and methods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernly - in a way that is modern or contemporary
  • synonym: traditionally - in a traditional manner
