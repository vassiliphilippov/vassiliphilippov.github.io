unindustrialized

adjective
not developed through industry
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.ɪnˈdʌstri.əˌlaɪzd /; / ˌʌn.ɪnˈdʌstr.i.ə.laɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪnˈdʌstri.ə.laɪzd /; / ˌʌn.ɪnˈdʌs.tri.ə.laɪzd / (BrE)
(of a country, region, etc.) not having undergone industrial development; lacking industry or large-scale manufacturing.

Examples

An unindustrialized country
Many rural areas remain unindustrialized, relying primarily on agriculture for income.
Several unindustrialized countries face challenges in improving infrastructure and living standards.
The government hopes to attract factories to unindustrialized regions to boost employment.
Many rural regions remain unindustrialised due to limited access to infrastructure and investment.
The government aims to bring economic growth to unindustrialised areas.
Wildlife is more abundant in unindustrialised landscapes untouched by factories and urban expansion.
