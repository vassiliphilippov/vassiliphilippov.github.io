abstrusely

adverb
in a difficult-to-understand manner
9.2
Pronunciation: / əbˈstruːsli / (AmE), / əbˈstruːsli / (BrE)
In a way that is difficult to understand; obscurely or complexly.

Examples

To speak abstrusely
She spoke abstrusely about the subject, leaving most of the audience confused.
The instructions were written abstrusely, making it hard for anyone to assemble the furniture correctly.
The philosopher explained his ideas so abstrusely that only a few students could follow his reasoning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • synonym: obscurely - in a way that is not clear or easy to understand
