preconscious

noun
accessible mental state
8.6
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈkɑːn.ʃəs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈkɑːn.ʃəs/ (AmE), /priː.ˈkɒn.ʃəs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈkɒn.ʃəs/ (BrE)
the part of the mind that contains thoughts and memories not currently in conscious awareness but easily accessible to consciousness

Examples

The preconscious mind
Some memories stored in the preconscious can be recalled when triggered by a specific event.
Therapists often help patients access thoughts from the preconscious during counseling sessions.
Freud believed the preconscious acts as a bridge between the unconscious and conscious mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conscious - the part of the mind that is aware of thoughts and surroundings
  • synonym: subconscious - the part of the mind below conscious awareness
