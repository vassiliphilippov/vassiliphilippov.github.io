FEC

noun
error correction method
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɛf i si / (AmE), / ɛf iː siː / (BrE)
a method of adding extra data to a signal so that receivers can detect and correct errors during transmission

Examples

FEC error correction
The engineers implemented FEC to improve the reliability of the communication system.
Without FEC, the video stream would suffer from frequent interruptions due to errors.
Modern satellite links often rely on FEC to maintain data integrity over long distances.
