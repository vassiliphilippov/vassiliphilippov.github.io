come to

verb
to occur or be realized suddenly
Pronunciation: / kʌm tu / (AmE), / kʌm tʊ / (BrE)
(of an idea, thought, or realization) to be suddenly experienced or occur to someone.

Examples

An idea comes to her
A brilliant solution came to her just as she was about to give up.
As I was walking down the street, an old memory suddenly came to me.
The answer had not come to him until he saw the diagram.
