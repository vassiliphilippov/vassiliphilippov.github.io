mythicize

verb
make into a myth
5.0 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪ.θɪ.saɪz/ (AmE), /ˈmɪ.θɪ.saɪz/ (BrE)
to make something into a myth or treat it as a myth; to give something mythical qualities or interpret it in a mythical way

Examples

To mythicize a story
Over time, cultures often mythicize their national heroes.
Some writers mythicize historical events to capture the imagination of readers.
The media tend to mythicize celebrities and their personal stories.
If we mythicize the founding of the nation, we may ignore important facts.
Many historians have tried to mythicize the lives of ancient heroes.
The film tends to mythicize the struggles of its main character, turning reality into legend.
Some historians attempt to mythicise certain events to emphasize their cultural significance.
The director chose to mythicise the simple tale, giving it an epic, timeless quality.
Writers often mythicise the deeds of national heroes in their stories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mythicize - to interpret as a myth or in mythical terms
  • synonym: mythologize - to create or promote a myth about something
