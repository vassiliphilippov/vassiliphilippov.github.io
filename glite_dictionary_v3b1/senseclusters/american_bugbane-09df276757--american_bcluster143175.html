American bugbane

noun
North American plant with white flower racemes
8.8
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbʌɡˌbeɪn / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbʌɡˌbeɪn / (BrE)
A plant native to eastern North America, with erect clusters of small white flowers, also known as black cohosh or Cimicifuga americana.

Examples

American bugbane plant
Many gardeners grow American bugbane for its tall, showy racemes of white flowers.
The roots of American bugbane have long been used in traditional remedies.
You can identify American bugbane by its large leaves and clusters of white blooms in the spring.
