principle

noun
moral rule or belief influencing conduct
6.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ /; / /ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ (BrE)
a moral rule or standard that guides your behavior and helps you know what is right and wrong

Examples

Strong principle
He refused to compromise his principle even when it cost him his job.
Honesty is a core principle in her life.
They admired her for acting on principle rather than out of convenience.
Despite the temptation, he refused to compromise his principles.
Good leaders are often guided by a clear set of principles.
She is a woman of strong principle who always stands up for what is right.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior
  • synonym: ethic - a set of moral principles or a code of behavior
  • antonym: expediency - doing what is convenient rather than what is morally right
  • synonym: scruple - a feeling of doubt or hesitation due to moral considerations
  • synonym: value - a person's principles or standards of behavior; one's judgment of what is important in life
