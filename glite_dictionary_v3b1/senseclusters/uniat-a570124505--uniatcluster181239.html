Uniat

noun
member of an Eastern Church in union with Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːniˌæt / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪˌat / (BrE)
A member of a Christian Eastern Church that acknowledges the authority of the Pope in Rome while retaining its own liturgy and traditions.

Examples

A Uniat priest
A local Uniat priest explained the significance of the feast day to visitors.
Several Uniats attended the service celebrating the Church's reunion with Rome.
The traditions of the Uniat communities differ from those of the Roman Catholics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Eastern Catholic - member of an Eastern Church in communion with Rome
