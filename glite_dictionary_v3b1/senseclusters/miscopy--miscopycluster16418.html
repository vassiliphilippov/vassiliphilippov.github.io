miscopy

verb
to copy incorrectly
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈkɑːpi / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkɒpi / (BrE)
to copy something incorrectly or with mistakes

Examples

To miscopy a sentence
He accidentally miscopied the address, so the letter was returned.
If you miscopy the data, the results will be inaccurate.
The teacher noticed that several students had miscopied the instructions on their papers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: copy - to make a duplicate of something
  • antonym: transcribe - to write down or record something exactly as heard or seen
