chlorhexidine

noun
long-lasting medical antiseptic
9.3
Pronunciation: / klɔrˈhɛksəˌdin / (AmE), / klɔːˈhɛksɪˌdiːn / (BrE)
a long-lasting antiseptic used for skin disinfection and handwashing before surgery, and in other medical and dental settings

Examples

Chlorhexidine skin disinfectant
If you are allergic to chlorhexidine, inform the nurse before any procedure.
Surgeons routinely use chlorhexidine to scrub their hands before entering the operating room.
The dentist recommended rinsing with chlorhexidine mouthwash to reduce oral bacteria.
