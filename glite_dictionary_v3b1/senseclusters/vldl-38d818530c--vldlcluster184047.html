VLDL

noun
very low-density lipoprotein (blood lipid carrier)
8.2 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / vi …õl Ààdi …õl / (AmE), / viÀê …õl ÀàdiÀê …õl / (BrE)
a type of lipoprotein in the blood that is rich in triglycerides and delivers them to body tissues, eventually being converted to LDL

Examples

High VLDL level
A high VLDL concentration can contribute to the build-up of cholesterol in the arteries.
Doctors measure VLDL levels to assess a patient's risk of heart disease.
The laboratory report showed her VLDL value was higher than normal.

Synonyms

  • very low density lipoprotein
    5.4 0.01/m
    very low density lipoprotein is the full scientific name used in papers and reports, while VLDL is the short abbreviation used in labs.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: HDL - high-density lipoprotein, a different type of lipoprotein involved in cholesterol transport
  • degree higher: LDL - low-density lipoprotein, another type of blood lipid carrier
  • synonym: lipoprotein - molecules made of protein and fat that transport lipids in the blood
