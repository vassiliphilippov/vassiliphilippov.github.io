domicile

noun
a person's permanent legal home
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑː.mə.saɪl /; / ˈdɑː.məˌsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.saɪl / (BrE)
a person's official or permanent home or residence, especially in legal or formal contexts

Examples

Legal domicile
A person's domicile is crucial in deciding their tax obligations.
She changed her domicile to France after living there for several years.
The court had to determine his domicile before proceeding with the case.
After retiring, she established her permanent domicile in the countryside.
He claimed France as his legal domicile for tax purposes.
The university requires students to provide proof of domicile when applying for housing.
