hypernym

noun
a more general word; e.g. 'animal' for 'dog'
6.8 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ.pər.nɪm / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪ.pə.nɪm / (BrE)
A word that is more general or inclusive in meaning than another; for example, 'flower' is a hypernym of 'rose'.

Examples

A hypernym word
In linguistics, a hypernym helps organize words into a hierarchy.
In the classification of living things, 'animal' is a hypernym of 'dog'.
