magick

noun
occult spiritual practice
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmædʒɪk / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪk / (BrE)
a spiritual or occult practice involving rituals and supernatural powers to create change or influence events

Examples

Ceremonial magick
He devoted years to studying magick in order to transform his spiritual life.
The rituals of magick often involve symbols, chants, and focused intention.
Many people explore magick as a way to seek personal growth and understanding.
Some people study magick as part of their spiritual or occult beliefs.
The ancient text describes rituals of magick used to protect against evil.
He claimed that his success was due to the practice of magick rather than luck.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magic - the use of supernatural powers to influence events
  • antonym: science - the systematic study of the natural world through observation and experiment
  • synonym: sorcery - the use of spells or supernatural powers, especially for evil purposes
