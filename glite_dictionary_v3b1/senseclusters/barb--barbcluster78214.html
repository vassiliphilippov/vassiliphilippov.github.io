barb

noun
sharp backward point on arrow or spear
Pronunciation: / bɑrb / (AmE), / bɑːb / (BrE)
a sharp, backward-facing point or projection, such as on an arrowhead, hook, or barbed wire

Examples

Fish hook barb
Once the fish was hooked, the barb made it nearly impossible to free it easily.
Removing a barb from the wound required great care to avoid further injury.
The ancient hunters designed their arrows with a barb to ensure the prey could not escape.
Each barb was sharp enough to deter animals from crossing the boundary.
Make sure you avoid the barb when handling barbed wire to prevent injury.
The barb on the wire tore his sleeve as he climbed over the fence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prong - a pointed projection, similar in shape to a barb
  • synonym: spike - a sharp pointed object or projection
