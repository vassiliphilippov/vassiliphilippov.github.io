exteroceptor

noun
receptor responding to external stimuli
4.4 8.4
Pronunciation: /ek.stə.rə.ˈsep.tər/ /; / /ɪk.stə.rə.ˈsep.tər/ (AmE), /ek.stə.rə.ˈsep.tə/ /; / /ɪk.stə.rə.ˈsep.tə/ (BrE)
a sensory receptor that responds to stimuli originating outside the body, such as light, sound, or touch

Examples

Exteroceptors detect stimuli
Photoreceptors in the eyes are specialized exteroceptors for detecting light.
Skin contains many exteroceptors that help us sense touch, pressure, and temperature.
When you hear a loud noise, it is your exteroceptors in the ears that respond.
