take one's sweet time

verb
do something very slowly; with no hurry
2.6 6.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk wʌnz swiːt taɪm / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz swiːt taɪm / (BrE)
to do something deliberately slowly, taking much longer than necessary, often implying a lack of haste or urgency

Examples

She always takes her sweet time when choosing what to wear.
You really take your sweet time getting ready in the morning.
