watchdog

noun
guardian against illegal practices or waste
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑtʃˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌdɒɡ / (BrE)
a person or organization that monitors and acts as a guardian against theft, illegal activity, or waste, especially by keeping an eye on others' conduct

Examples

A watchdog group
Many believe the free press should serve as a watchdog over public officials.
The consumer rights group acts as a watchdog to prevent companies from exploiting customers.
The government appointed an independent watchdog to ensure transparency in financial transactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act
  • synonym: overseer - someone who supervises others
