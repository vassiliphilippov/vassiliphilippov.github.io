cultish

adjective
like a cult
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌltɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪʃ / (BrE)
having qualities or characteristics similar to a cult, such as being strange, exclusive, controlling, or having a small, devoted group of followers

Examples

Cultish behavior
The leader's cultish behavior made many members uncomfortable.
Some fans displayed a cultish devotion to the celebrity, following every move.
The company's cultish culture discouraged employees from questioning management.
The director's cultish films attracted a small but passionate audience.
Her cultish style of dress set her apart from her classmates.
The show developed a cultish following among science fiction fans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cult-like - resembling or characteristic of a cult or its followers
  • antonym: mainstream - typical or widely accepted by most people
  • synonym: sectarian - relating to or characteristic of a sect or group with extreme beliefs
