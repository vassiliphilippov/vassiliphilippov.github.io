mortifyingly

adverb
with severe self-denial
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔːr.tɪ.faɪ.ɪŋ.lɪ/ /; / /ˈmɔːr.tə.faɪ.ɪŋ.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈmɔː.tɪ.faɪ.ɪŋ.lɪ/ /; / /ˈmɔː.tə.faɪ.ɪŋ.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that involves severe self-discipline or self-denial, especially for religious or moral reasons

Examples

Mortifyingly strict life
He lived mortifyingly, giving up all comforts for his beliefs.
The rules at the monastery were mortifyingly strict, requiring daily fasting and silence.
She described her childhood as mortifyingly disciplined, with little room for fun or relaxation.

  • antonym: indulgently - in a way that allows oneself to enjoy pleasures or luxuries
