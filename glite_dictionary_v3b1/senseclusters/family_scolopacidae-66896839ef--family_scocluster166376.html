family Scolopacidae

noun
a family of wading birds (sandpipers, snipes, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˌskɑːləˈpeɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌskɒləˈpæsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a large family of wading birds including sandpipers, woodcocks, snipes, tattlers, curlews, godwits, and dowitchers

Examples

Family Scolopacidae birds
Many shorebirds in the family Scolopacidae migrate long distances every year.
Ornithologists study the family Scolopacidae to understand the evolution of wading birds.
The family Scolopacidae includes sandpipers, godwits, and curlews found across the world.
