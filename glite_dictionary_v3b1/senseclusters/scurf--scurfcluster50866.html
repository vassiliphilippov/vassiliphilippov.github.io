scurf

noun
scaly covering on plant parts (botany)
Pronunciation: / skɝf / (AmE), / skɜːf / (BrE)
(Botany) A thin, scaly or branny covering that resembles scales or bran and often covers certain plant parts.

Examples

Plant scurf
Careful examination revealed a fine scurf encasing the buds.
Some desert plants are covered with a whitish scurf to reflect sunlight.
The scurf on the leaf surface protected it from excessive moisture loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scale - a thin, flat, dry covering found on certain animals or plants
