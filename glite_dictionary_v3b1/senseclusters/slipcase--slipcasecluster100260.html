slipcase

verb
to put in slipcase
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪpˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈslɪpˌkeɪs / (BrE)
to place an item, such as a book or media, into a protective slipcase for storage or display

Examples

To slipcase a book
The publisher will slipcase the collector's edition books before sending them to stores.
After restoring the old album, she carefully slipcased it to keep it safe.
They always slipcase rare DVDs to protect them from dust and damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: box - to put something into a box for protection or storage
  • antonym: unwrap - to remove a covering or packaging from something
