mutagenic

adjective
able to cause genetic mutation
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːtəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
Able to cause genetic mutation, especially referring to substances or factors from outside an organism.

Examples

A mutagenic substance
Certain pesticides have been classified as potentially mutagenic by regulatory agencies.
Prolonged exposure to mutagenic chemicals can increase the risk of cancer.
Scientists tested the mutagenic effects of radiation on mouse cells.
