Baptistic doctrine

noun
doctrine related to Anabaptist or Baptist beliefs
9.5
Pronunciation: / bæpˈtɪstɪk ˈdɑktrɪn / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪstɪk ˈdɒktrɪn / (BrE)
any of various religious doctrines or teachings closely related to those of Anabaptists, especially emphasizing believer's baptism and church membership by voluntary profession of faith

Examples

Baptistic doctrine teachings
Many religious scholars have studied the influence of Baptistic doctrine on American Protestantism.
Some congregations follow a Baptistic doctrine, requiring members to make a personal confession of faith.
The church still adheres to a Baptistic doctrine that emphasizes adult baptism.
