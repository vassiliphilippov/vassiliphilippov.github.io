seasoned

adjective
aged or processed for use
4.1 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêz…ônd / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêz…ônd / (BrE)
(of food, materials, etc.) having been aged or processed, often to improve suitability or quality, especially by being exposed to the elements for a period.

Examples

Seasoned wood
For the best flavor, the recipe calls for seasoned firewood that has been dried for at least a year.
Only seasoned timber should be used for building furniture to ensure durability.
The carpenter prefers to work with seasoned wood because it is less likely to warp.

Synonyms

  • aged
    4.1 2.04/m
    aged means kept to mature for flavor or quality, while seasoned means treated or dried to suit use or resist the elements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unseasoned - not having been aged or processed; raw or fresh
