eonni

noun
older sister (used by females in Korean)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.niː/ /; / /ˈoʊn.niː/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.niː/ /; / /ˈəʊn.niː/ (BrE)
In Korean culture, a term used by a younger female to address an older sister or older woman, especially one with whom she has a close relationship.

Examples

To call eonni
In many K-dramas, a younger girl will refer to an older girl as eonni.
Lisa called her friend eonni because she looked up to her as an older sister.
She smiled and said, 'Thank you, eonni,' after receiving advice from her older colleague.
