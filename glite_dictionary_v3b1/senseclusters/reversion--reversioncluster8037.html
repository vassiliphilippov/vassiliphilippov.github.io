reversion

noun
turning back or returning to previous direction
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɝːʒən /; / ɹɪˈvɝːʒən /; / rɪˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃən /; / ɹɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of returning to a previous state, condition, or way of being

Examples

Reversion to tradition
His ideas showed a reversion to old-fashioned thinking rather than innovation.
Scientists noted a surprising reversion to earlier methods after new techniques failed.
The sudden reversion of the car's direction startled the passengers.
After several years of change, the policy underwent a reversion to its original form.
Her sudden reversion to old habits surprised her colleagues.
Some environmentalists argue that the area needs a reversion to its natural wilderness.
After years of innovation, the company faced a reversion to its outdated practices.
Social unrest can sometimes cause a reversion to previous, less egalitarian social structures.
Their victory was followed by a sudden reversion to old rivalries and divisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progression - the process of developing or moving forward
  • synonym: regression - a return to an earlier or less advanced state
  • synonym: reversal - the action of making something the opposite of what it was
