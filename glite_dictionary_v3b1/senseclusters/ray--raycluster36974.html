ray

noun
narrow beam or line of light
Pronunciation: / reɪ / (AmE), / reɪ / (BrE)
a narrow beam or line of light or energy, such as sunlight or a laser

Examples

A ray of light
A single ray of sunlight filtered through the clouds, lighting up the field.
She saw a ray of light coming from the lighthouse on the distant shore.
The dark room was illuminated by a ray from the streetlamp outside.
Scientists study the properties of a laser ray by observing how it travels through various materials.
Sunlight consists of multiple rays that enter Earth's atmosphere and scatter in different directions.
The X-ray machine emits a powerful ray to capture images of your bones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beam - a shaft or stream of light
  • synonym: shaft - a long narrow streak of light
  • degree lower: wave - a vibration that transfers energy through space, as in electromagnetic waves
