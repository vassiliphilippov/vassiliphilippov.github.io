protective coloration

noun
camouflage coloring for animal protection
5.8 3.6
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈtekt.ɪv kʌ.lə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /proʊ.ˈtekt.ɪv kʌ.lə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /prə.ˈtekt.ɪv kə.lə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prə.ˈtekt.ɪv kɒ.lə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
coloring or markings on an animal that help it avoid predators by making it less visible or more difficult to detect

Examples

Protective coloration on animals
Many insects rely on protective coloration to avoid being eaten by birds.
The arctic fox's protective coloration makes it nearly invisible against the snow.
The butterfly's wings exhibit protective coloration that mimics the appearance of leaves.
