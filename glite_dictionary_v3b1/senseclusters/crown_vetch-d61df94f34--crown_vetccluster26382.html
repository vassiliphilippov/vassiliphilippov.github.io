crown vetch

noun
a plant with pink-and-white flower clusters, used for ground cover
9.9
Pronunciation: / kraʊn vɛtʃ / (AmE), / kraʊn vɛtʃ / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with pink-and-white clustered flowers and sharply angled seed pods, commonly used for ground cover and erosion control.

Examples

Crown vetch plant
Crown vetch spreads quickly, making it effective for stabilizing embankments along roads.
Gardeners value crown vetch for its attractive pink-and-white blooms and easy maintenance.
The hillside was planted with crown vetch to help prevent soil erosion.
