millibar

noun
unit of atmospheric pressure
5.6 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌbɑː / (BrE)
a unit of atmospheric pressure equal to one thousandth of a bar, commonly used in meteorology

Examples

One thousand millibars
At sea level, the standard atmospheric pressure is about 1013 millibars.
Scientists often record air pressure in millibars when studying weather patterns.
The weather report mentioned a drop in pressure to 990 millibars before the storm arrived.
