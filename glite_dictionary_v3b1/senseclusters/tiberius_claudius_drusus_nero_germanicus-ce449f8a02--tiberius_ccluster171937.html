Roman Emperor (AD 41–54) who consolidated the Roman Empire and oversaw the conquest of southern Britain. He was allegedly poisoned by his fourth wife, Agrippina.

Examples

Tiberius Claudius Drusus Nero Germanicus emperor
Tiberius Claudius Drusus Nero Germanicus became emperor after the assassination of Caligula.
Tiberius Claudius Drusus Nero Germanicus is credited with beginning the Roman conquest of Britain.
Historians debate whether Tiberius Claudius Drusus Nero Germanicus was truly poisoned by his wife Agrippina.
