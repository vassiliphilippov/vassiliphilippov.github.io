to recognize or show the difference between things or people; to distinguish or make something different from others

Examples

To differentiate two objects
Experts can easily differentiate various species of birds by their calls.
It can be difficult to differentiate between authentic and fake paintings.
The company needed a way to differentiate its products from competitors'.
A unique aroma differentiates this coffee from its competitors.
Her creativity differentiates her from the rest of the design team.
Quality customer service can differentiate a business in a crowded market.

Synonyms

  • distinguish
    3.1 22/m
    distinguish means to notice or show clear differences, while differentiate often focuses on showing small or detailed differences between similar things.
  • tell apart
    3.0 1.56/m
    tell apart is a casual phrase for recognizing two items by sight or detail, while differentiate is more formal and often shows precise differences.
  • discriminate
    4.2 4.28/m
    discriminate often means to treat a group unfairly or make legal distinctions, while differentiate stays neutral and means show differences.
