major

noun
main field of academic study
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər /; / ˈmeɪdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
the main subject that a student studies at a college or university; a student who specializes in a subject

Examples

A major in biology
His major is computer science, and he plans to work in technology after graduation.
Many students declare their major by the end of their sophomore year.
She decided to change her major from biology to psychology after her first year.
As a major in economics, he often attended additional seminars.
Many English majors enjoy analyzing literature and writing essays.
She is a biology major at Stanford University.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minor - a student specializing in a subordinate subject
