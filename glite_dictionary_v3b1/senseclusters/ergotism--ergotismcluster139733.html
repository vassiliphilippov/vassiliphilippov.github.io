ergotism

noun
poisoning by ergot-infected grain
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɜːr.gə.tɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈɜː.gə.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A condition caused by ingesting ergot-infected grain, resulting in symptoms such as nausea, cramping, vomiting, and, in severe cases, seizures and gangrene.

Examples

Ergotism symptoms
Historic outbreaks of ergotism devastated entire communities in medieval Europe.
The doctor quickly diagnosed the patient's strange symptoms as a rare case of ergotism.
Victims of ergotism often suffered from severe muscle cramps and hallucinations.
