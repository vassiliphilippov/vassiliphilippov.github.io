quadrivalent

noun
four-strain vaccine
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑːdrɪˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌkwɒdrɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
a vaccine or preparation that provides protection against four different strains or types of a disease

Examples

A quadrivalent shot
The doctor recommended the quadrivalent to ensure protection against multiple flu strains.
Many countries now use a quadrivalent for their annual influenza vaccination programs.
Receiving a quadrivalent can help reduce the risk of infection from several types of the virus.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bivalent - a vaccine that protects against two strains or types
  • degree lower: trivalent - a vaccine that protects against three strains or types
