rough out

verb
make a preliminary or rough version
Pronunciation: / rʌf aʊt / (AmE), / rʌf aʊt / (BrE)
to prepare or make the first, basic version of something, often in a sketchy or unfinished form

Examples

To rough out a plan
Before sculpting, he likes to rough out the basic shape in clay.
She quickly roughed out the outline of her proposal on a napkin.
The architect will rough out the design before making the detailed plans.
