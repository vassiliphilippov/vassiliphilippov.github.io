traipse around

verb
walk or wander aimlessly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪps əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtreɪps əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to walk or move about casually, often without a clear purpose or direction

Examples

To traipse around the park
He traipsed around the park for hours but never found his keys.
They enjoy traipsing around art galleries on rainy weekends.
We spent the afternoon traipsing around the city, exploring its winding streets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stride - to walk with long, purposeful steps
  • synonym: wander - to move about without a fixed course or purpose
