blabber

verb
talk rapidly about trivial things
4.4 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈblæb.ər/ (AmE), /ˈblæb.ə/ (BrE)
to talk too much, especially about unimportant things or by revealing secrets carelessly

Examples

To blabber secrets
He was blabbering so much that I could barely concentrate on my work.
Please don't blabber all the details of our conversation to everyone at work.
She tends to blabber on the phone for hours about nothing in particular.
Please don't blabber about our plans to everyone at the office.
He tends to blabber when he gets nervous and can't keep a secret.
She accidentally blabbered the surprise party details to her friend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk quickly in a way that is difficult to understand, often about unimportant things
  • synonym: chatter - to talk quickly about unimportant things
  • antonym: clam up - to suddenly stop talking, especially when nervous or unwilling
  • synonym: gossip - to talk about other people's private lives
  • antonym: keep quiet - to not say anything or reveal information
