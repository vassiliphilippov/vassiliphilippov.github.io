refold

verb
to restore molecular shape
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfoʊld / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfəʊld / (BrE)
to cause a protein or other molecule to return to its correct three-dimensional shape after being unfolded or denatured

Examples

To refold a protein
Scientists worked to refold the protein after it had been denatured during the experiment.
Proper conditions are needed to refold enzymes so they regain their biological activity.
After purification, the team attempted to refold the molecule into its functional structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denature - to alter the natural structure of a protein or molecule
  • synonym: fold - to form the correct three-dimensional structure of a protein or molecule
