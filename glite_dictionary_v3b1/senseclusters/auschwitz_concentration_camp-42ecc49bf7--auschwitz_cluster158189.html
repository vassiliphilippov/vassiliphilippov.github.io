Auschwitz concentration camp

noun
Nazi WWII death camp in Poland
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊʃvɪts ˌkɑnsənˈtreɪʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˈaʊʃvɪts ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən kamp / (BrE)
A Nazi concentration and extermination camp for Jews and others in southwestern Poland during World War II, infamous for mass murder.

Examples

The Auschwitz concentration camp site
She visited the Auschwitz concentration camp to learn more about the atrocities committed during the Holocaust.
Survivors shared their harrowing experiences of life in the Auschwitz concentration camp at last night's memorial event.
The liberation of the Auschwitz concentration camp marked a turning point in world recognition of Nazi war crimes.
