biosimilar

noun
similar biological drug
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈsɪmələr / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈsɪmɪlə / (BrE)
a biological drug that is highly similar to an already approved biological medicine and has no clinically meaningful differences

Examples

A biosimilar drug
The hospital began using a biosimilar to reduce treatment costs.
Doctors may prescribe a biosimilar when the original biological drug is too expensive.
Approval of a new biosimilar can increase patient access to important treatments.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: biologic - a drug made from living organisms or their products
  • synonym: generic - a drug that is equivalent to a brand-name drug in dosage and effect
