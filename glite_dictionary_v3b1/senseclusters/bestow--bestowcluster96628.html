bestow

verb
to give as an honor or gift
3.9
Pronunciation: / bɪˈstoʊ / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊ / (BrE)
to give or present something to someone, especially as an honor or gift

Examples

To bestow an award
He was honored when the university chose to bestow an honorary degree on him.
The charity plans to bestow awards to those who have made significant contributions to the community.
The queen decided to bestow the title of knight upon the young hero.
He felt honored when the king chose to bestow a knighthood on him.
Scholarships are bestowed each year on students who show exceptional promise.
The committee decided to bestow the award upon her for her outstanding service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: award - to give something as an official payment, honor, or recognition
  • synonym: confer - to grant or bestow (a title, degree, benefit, or right)
  • synonym: grant - to give or allow someone something, usually in an official way
  • antonym: withdraw - to take back or remove something that was given
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something that is expected or due
