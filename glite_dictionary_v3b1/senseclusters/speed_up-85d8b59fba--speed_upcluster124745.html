speed up

verb
to move or occur faster
2.5 8.21/m
Pronunciation: / spiÀêd  åp / (AmE), / spiÀêd  åp / (BrE)
to move or make something move or happen faster; to increase speed

Examples

To speed up the car
If you don't speed up, we'll miss the train.
The production line was sped up to meet the deadline.
Traffic always speeds up after the construction zone ends.
Can you speed up the process so we finish earlier?
Production will speed up once the new machines arrive.
The driver tried to speed up to catch the green light.

Synonyms

  • accelerate
    3.7 28/m
    accelerate is a formal verb often used for engines or processes to increase speed quickly, while speed up is more general.
  • speed
    1.0 10/m
    speed often means causing something to move faster directly, while speed up sounds more phrasal and used in everyday speech.
  • pick up the pace
    4.0 0.65/m
    pick up the pace is a casual phrase for going faster in activity or work, while speed up is a neutral phrasal verb.
  • gain speed
    2.5 0.36/m
    gain speed means begin to move faster over time, while speed up can mean make something happen faster immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accelerate - to begin to move more quickly
  • antonym: slow down - to reduce speed or make something go slower
