skeezy

adjective
unpleasant or sleazy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskiːzi / (AmE), / ˈskiːzi / (BrE)
unpleasant, sleazy, or giving a feeling of being untrustworthy or dirty

Examples

A skeezy bar
The bar on the corner looked too skeezy for us to go inside.
He avoided the skeezy part of town after dark.
That website seems a bit skeezy, so I wouldn't enter my information there.

Related Terms

  • antonym: respectable - considered to be good, proper, or correct
  • synonym: shady - suspicious or of questionable honesty
  • synonym: sleazy - morally low or dirty in appearance
