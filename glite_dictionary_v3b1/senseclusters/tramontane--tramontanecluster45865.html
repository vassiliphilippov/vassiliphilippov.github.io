tramontane

noun
cold wind from mountains in Italy/Mediterranean
4.3
Pronunciation: / trəˈmɑnˌteɪn / (AmE), / trəˈmɒn.tən / (BrE)
A cold, dry wind that blows south from the mountains into Italy and the western Mediterranean region.

Examples

A tramontane wind
Fishermen stayed ashore, wary of the strong tramontane sweeping down from the Alps.
The tramontane is known for its dry chill, characteristic of winters in the western Mediterranean.
The sudden arrival of the tramontane dropped temperatures and cleared the skies over Genoa.
