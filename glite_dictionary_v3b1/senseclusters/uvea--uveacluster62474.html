uvea

noun
middle layer of the eye
Pronunciation: / ˈjuː.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈjuː.vi.ə / (BrE)
the pigmented middle layer of the eye, consisting of the iris, ciliary body, and choroid

Examples

The uvea layer
Doctors examined the patient's uvea for signs of infection.
Inflammation of the uvea is known as uveitis and can threaten vision if untreated.
The uvea includes the iris, which gives the eye its color.
