inclination of an orbit

noun
angle between an orbit's plane and the ecliptic
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌklɪneɪʃən əv ən ˈɔrbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˌklɪneɪʃən əv ən ˈɔːbɪt / (BrE)
(astronomy) the angle between the plane of an object's orbit and the plane of the ecliptic, measured in degrees.

Examples

Inclination of an orbit angle
Different moons have different inclinations of their orbits around their planets.
Scientists measured the inclination of an orbit to predict when the satellite would cross the equator.
The inclination of an orbit determines how tilted a planet's path is relative to the ecliptic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orbital inclination - the angle between an orbit's plane and a reference plane
