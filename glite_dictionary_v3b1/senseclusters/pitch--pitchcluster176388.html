pitch

noun
a vendor's selling spot or stand
6.8 3.4
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɪtʃ / (BrE)
a place or spot used by a vendor to sell goods, or a presentation to persuade others to buy or support something

Examples

A sales pitch

Street performers compete for the best pitch on the popular promenade.
The flower seller set up her pitch near the busy station every morning.
After hearing the marketing team's pitch, the investors decided to fund the project.
He practiced his elevator pitch before meeting with potential clients.
Her sales pitch was so convincing that everyone wanted to buy the product.
