two-year-old horse

noun
horse aged two years, often raced
Pronunciation: / tuː ˌjɪr oʊld hɔrs / (AmE), / tuː ˌjɪə əʊld hɔːs / (BrE)
A horse that is two years old, especially one that is entered in races restricted to horses of that age.

Examples

A two-year-old horse race
Her colt is a promising two-year-old horse ready for its first race season.
Many famous races are open only to two-year-old horses.
Owners invest heavily in training two-year-old horses for competitive events.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: three-year-old horse - a horse that is three years old, often raced in classic events
  • degree lower: yearling - a horse that is between one and two years old
