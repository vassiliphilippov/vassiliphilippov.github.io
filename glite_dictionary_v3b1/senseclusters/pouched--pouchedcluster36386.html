pouched

adjective
having a pouch or sac
Pronunciation: / paʊtʃt / (AmE), / paʊtʃt / (BrE)
having a pouch or sac, especially as a part of the body.

Examples

Pouched frog species
Some frogs are pouched, carrying their offspring in skin sacs for protection.
The pouched rat uses its cheek pouches to carry food back to its burrow.
The kangaroo is a well-known pouched mammal native to Australia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pouchless - lacking a pouch
