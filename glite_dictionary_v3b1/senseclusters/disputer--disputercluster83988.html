disputer

noun
person who argues
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspjuːtər / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːtə / (BrE)
a person who argues, debates, or challenges something

Examples

A disputer in debate
The disputer challenged every point made during the discussion.
As a skilled disputer, she often found herself in heated debates.
The meeting was delayed because one disputer refused to compromise.
The disputer presented new evidence to strengthen her case in court.
As the main disputer, he argued against the proposed changes during the hearing.
Each disputer was given time to explain their position before the judge.
The disputer presented strong evidence to support her position during the academic conference.
As a skilled disputer, he was often chosen to represent his university in formal debates.
Each disputer was given five minutes to respond to the opposing argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arguer - a person who argues or presents reasons for or against something
  • synonym: challenger - a person who disputes or opposes something
  • synonym: debater - a person who takes part in a formal argument or discussion
  • synonym: litigant - a person involved in a lawsuit
  • synonym: opponent - a person who disagrees or competes against another in a debate
  • antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to create harmony and resolve conflict
  • antonym: supporter - a person who supports or defends a claim or right
