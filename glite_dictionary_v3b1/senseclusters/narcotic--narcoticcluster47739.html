narcotic

adjective
relating to narcotic drugs or their effects
3.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /nɑːr.ˈkɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /nɑː.ˈkɒt.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to drugs or substances that dull the senses, relieve pain, or cause sleep or stupor (often with potential for abuse or addiction)

Examples

Narcotic substances
Prolonged narcotic use can lead to serious health complications.

The doctor prescribed a narcotic analgesic to relieve the patient's severe pain.
The drug has a narcotic effect, making users feel sleepy or sedated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases physiological or nervous activity
