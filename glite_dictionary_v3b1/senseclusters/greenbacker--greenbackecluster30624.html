greenbacker

noun
supporter of paper money
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːnˌbækər / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌbækə / (BrE)
a person, especially in 19th-century US history, who supported the use of paper money not backed by gold

Examples

The greenbacker party
The greenbacker party gained influence during the economic struggles after the Civil War.
As a proud greenbacker, he campaigned for currency reform across the Midwest.
Historians often debate the impact of the greenbacker movement on American financial policy.
In the late 1800s, a greenbacker would argue for more government-issued paper currency.
The politician was called a greenbacker because of his opposition to the gold standard.
Many farmers became greenbackers to support easier access to money during hard times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: goldbug - a person who supports currency backed by gold
  • synonym: inflationist - a person who supports increasing the money supply
  • synonym: populist - a supporter of the rights and power of ordinary people
