Sinapis alba

noun
species of mustard plant
Pronunciation: / sɪˈneɪpɪs ˈælbə / (AmE), / sɪˈneɪpɪs ˈalba / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant species, also known as white or yellow mustard, cultivated for its pungent seeds that are used to make mustard and mustard oil.

Examples

Sinapis alba seeds
Sinapis alba is widely grown in Europe for its flavorful seeds.
Farmers harvest Sinapis alba mainly in late summer.
The seeds of Sinapis alba are used to produce table mustard.
