spectrometry

noun
measurement and analysis of spectra
4.2 6.0
Pronunciation: /spek.ˈtrɑː.mə.triː/ (AmE), /spek.ˈtrɒm.ə.triː/ (BrE)
The scientific technique of measuring and analyzing spectra, especially using a spectroscope, to identify the properties of substances.

Examples

Spectrometry analysis
Spectrometry allows scientists to identify the chemical composition of unknown substances.
Advances in spectrometry have improved our understanding of molecular structures.
Modern laboratories rely on mass spectrometry to detect trace amounts of drugs in samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spectroscopy - the study of spectra produced when matter interacts with or emits electromagnetic radiation
