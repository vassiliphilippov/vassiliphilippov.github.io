semelparous

adjective
reproducing once
Pronunciation: / səˈmɛlˌpæɹəs / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛlpərəs / (BrE)
describing an organism that reproduces only once in its lifetime before dying

Examples

Semelparous organism
The mayfly is a classic example of a semelparous insect, living only long enough to reproduce once.
Many species of bamboo are semelparous, flowering and dying after a single reproductive event.
Scientists study semelparous animals to understand the evolutionary reasons for single-time reproduction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: iteroparous - describing an organism that can reproduce multiple times during its life
