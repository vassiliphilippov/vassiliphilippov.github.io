hole

noun
an opening or gap
Pronunciation: / hoʊl / (AmE), / həʊl / (BrE)
an opening, gap, or hollow space in or through something (can be made or natural)

Examples

A hole in the wall
A mouse made a hole in the wall and used it as a nest.
She dug a small hole in the garden to plant a flower.
The dog escaped through a hole in the fence.
She punched a hole in her ticket to validate it.
The plumber drilled a hole in the wall to pass the pipe through.
They cut a hole in the ice so they could go fishing.
He accidentally made a hole in the wall while moving the furniture.
Raindrops collected in every small hole in the pavement after the storm.
The rabbit dug a hole in the garden to hide its food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aperture - an opening, hole, or gap
  • synonym: cavity - an empty space within a solid object
  • synonym: gap - a break or opening in a surface
  • antonym: mound - a raised area of earth or solid matter
  • antonym: plug - something that blocks a hole
