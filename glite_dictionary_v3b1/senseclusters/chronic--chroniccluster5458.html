chronic

adjective
consistent or habitual (often negative)
5.4 1.73/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr…ëÀê.n…™k / (AmE), / Ààkr…ín.…™k / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) having a particular bad habit or characteristic for a long time and difficult to change.

Examples

Chronic liar
He's a chronic liar and no one trusts what he says anymore.
She's a chronic worrier, always expecting the worst.
The company has a chronic habit of missing deadlines.

Synonyms

  • inveterate
    8.4 0.58/m
    inveterate describes a long-established habit that is unlikely to change, while chronic refers to a long-term bad trait hard to change.
  • habitual
    4.0 3.06/m
    habitual describes something done regularly as a custom, while chronic describes a long-term bad habit that is hard to change.
  • persistent
    4.9 9.71/m
    persistent describes something that continues or repeats without stopping, while chronic describes a long-term bad trait hard to change.
