ganoine

noun
shiny substance on scales of some fishes
Pronunciation: / ˈɡænoʊˌiːn / (AmE), / ˈɡænɔɪn / (BrE)
a shiny, enamel-like substance secreted by the corium of certain fishes, especially ganoid fishes, forming the outer layer of their scales

Examples

Fish ganoine layer
Researchers studied the composition of ganoine to understand its protective properties in ancient fish.
The scales of gars are covered with a layer of ganoine, making them exceptionally hard and shiny.
Unlike most modern fish, sturgeons retain a surface coating of ganoine on their bony plates.
