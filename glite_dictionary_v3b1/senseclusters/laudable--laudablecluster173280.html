laudable

adjective
worthy of praise
7.2 1.18/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…îd…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / Ààl…îÀêd…ôb…ôl / (BrE)
Deserving praise and commendation.

Examples

Laudable efforts
Her laudable efforts to help the community did not go unnoticed.
His passion for teaching is truly laudable and inspires his students.
It is laudable that the company recycles all of its waste materials.

Synonyms

  • meritorious
    7.5 0.71/m
    meritorious often appears in formal writing to praise a specific achievement, while laudable is general praise for good action.
  • praiseworthy
    4.5 0.75/m
    praiseworthy is used to show clear approval and admiration for actions, while laudable is a more general term of praise.
  • commendable
    4.6 1.52/m
    commendable is common in formal or public comments to approve someone's act, while laudable means deserving praise.
  • applaudable
    6.1 0.00/m
    applaudable suggests praise that people will openly show, while laudable is a broader word for deserving praise.
  • admirable
    3.3 5.32/m
    admirable often implies respect or admiration for qualities, while laudable focuses mainly on actions deserving praise.
  • creditable
    6.2 0.23/m
    creditable means acceptable and worthy of some praise though not outstanding, while laudable simply means deserving praise.
  • meritable
    7.7 0.00/m
    meritable means deserving reward or praise but sounds rare or formal, while laudable is more common in everyday use.
  • worthy
    2.8 28/m
    worthy can mean deserving respect, attention, or choice in many ways, while laudable specifically means deserving praise.
