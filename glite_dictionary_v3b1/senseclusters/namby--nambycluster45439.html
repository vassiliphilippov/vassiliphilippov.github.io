namby

noun
timid or weak person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnæmbi / (AmE), / ˈnæmbi / (BrE)
an informal and often derogatory term for a timid, weak, or ineffectual person who lacks strength or resolve

Examples

He is a namby
He was called a namby for refusing to stand up to the bully.
No one expected the namby in the group to take charge during the crisis.
She dismissed him as a namby who never voiced his own opinions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage to face danger or difficulty
  • antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or strength
  • synonym: wimp - a person who is weak or lacks courage
