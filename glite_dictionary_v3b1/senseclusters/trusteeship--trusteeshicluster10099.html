trusteeship

noun
country or territory under UN-administered government
8.3 6.7
Pronunciation: /trʌ.ˈstiː.ʃɪp/ /; / /trə.ˈstiː.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /trʌ.ˈstiː.ʃɪp/ /; / /trə.ˈstiː.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
A country or territory that is governed by another country under the supervision of the United Nations, as part of an arrangement to prepare it for self-government or independence.

Examples

UN trusteeship territory
After World War II, several territories were placed under trusteeship to help them transition toward independence.
The end of the trusteeship marked a crucial step in the country's path to self-governance.
The island nation only achieved full sovereignty after decades of trusteeship under the United Nations.
