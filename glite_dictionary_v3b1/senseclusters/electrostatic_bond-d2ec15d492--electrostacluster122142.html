electrostatic bond

noun
chemical bond formed by ionic attraction
8.4 5.4
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.trəʊ.ˈstæt.ɪk bɑːnd/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trəʊ.ˈstæt.ɪk bɒnd/ (BrE)
a chemical bond formed between two atoms when one loses an electron to become a positive ion and the other gains an electron to become a negative ion, resulting in attraction between their opposite charges

Examples

An electrostatic bond in salt
Table salt is held together by an electrostatic bond between sodium and chloride ions.
The strength of an electrostatic bond depends on the charges and distances between the ions.
When magnesium reacts with oxygen, an electrostatic bond is formed, creating magnesium oxide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ionic bond - a chemical bond formed by the attraction between oppositely charged ions
