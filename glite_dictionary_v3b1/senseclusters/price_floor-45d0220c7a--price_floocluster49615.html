price floor

noun
official minimum allowable price
5.1 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / pra…™s fl…îr / (AmE), / pra…™s fl…îÀê / (BrE)
an official minimum price set by a government or authority, below which a good or service cannot be sold

Examples

A price floor on wages
A price floor on wages can help protect workers from extremely low pay.
If the market price falls below the price floor, suppliers may end up with surplus goods.
The government introduced a price floor to support local farmers during difficult seasons.
