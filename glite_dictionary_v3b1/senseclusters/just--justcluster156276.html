Synonyms

  • just now
    1.0 9.16/m
    just now refers to a very short moment in the past, while just covers very recent time in many contexts.
  • newly
    1.0 38/m
    newly describes something made or started a short time ago, while just indicates a recent time more flexibly in speech.
  • freshly
    1.0 16/m
    freshly means recently done and often feels new or still warm, while just is more neutral about time.
  • recently
    1.0 173/m
    recently refers to a not-long-ago period, while just means a very short, immediate time before now.
