Epicurean

noun
follower of pleasure philosophy
7.9
Pronunciation: /e.pə.kjʊ.ˈriː.ən/ /; / /e.pɪ.kjʊ.ˈriː.ən/ (AmE), /e.pɪ.kjʊə.ˈriː.ən/ /; / /e.pə.kjʊə.ˈriː.ən/ (BrE)
a person who follows the philosophy that values pleasure, especially simple and moderate pleasures, as the highest good

Examples

An Epicurean enjoys life
As an Epicurean, he believed in enjoying good food and peaceful company.
The ancient Epicureans taught that happiness comes from simple, moderate pleasures.
She read about Epicureans and their ideas on living a balanced and joyful life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hedonist - a person who believes that pleasure is the most important thing in life
  • antonym: stoic - a person who practices self-control and does not show emotion or seek pleasure
