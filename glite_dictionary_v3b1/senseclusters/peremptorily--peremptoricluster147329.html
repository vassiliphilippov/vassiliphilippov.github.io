peremptorily

adverb
imperatively; commandingly
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛmptərəli / (AmE), / pəˈrɛmptərɪli / (BrE)
In a way that leaves no opportunity for denial or refusal; in an imperative and commanding manner.

Examples

To peremptorily order
He peremptorily ordered everyone out of the room.
She peremptorily dismissed the proposal, ending the conversation abruptly.
The judge spoke peremptorily, leaving no room for further objections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritatively - in a manner that shows confidence and authority
