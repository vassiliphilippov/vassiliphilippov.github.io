protoxide

noun
oxide with least oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊtɑˌksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which an element is combined with the smallest possible amount of oxygen in a series of oxides

Examples

Protoxide compound
Iron protoxide is also known as ferrous oxide and has the formula FeO.
The chemist explained that a protoxide contains the lowest amount of oxygen possible for that element.
Copper protoxide is different from cupric oxide because it has less oxygen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxide - a compound of oxygen with another element
