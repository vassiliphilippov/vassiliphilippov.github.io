polecat

noun
Eurasian weasel-like mammal with strong odor
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊlˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlˌkæt /; / ˈpəʊlkæt / (BrE)
a small, weasel-like mammal that releases a strong, unpleasant smell when threatened

Examples

A polecat animal
Farmers often blamed the polecat for disturbances in their chicken coops.
The polecat is rarely seen during the day because it is mostly nocturnal.
When threatened, a polecat can emit a pungent smell to deter predators.
Many people mistake a polecat for other small carnivorous mammals in North America.
The smell of polecat spray can linger for days after it has been released.
When threatened, a polecat can spray a noxious fluid to ward off predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skunk - American mammal known for its foul-smelling defensive spray
