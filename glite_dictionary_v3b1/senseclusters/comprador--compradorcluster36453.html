comprador

noun
local collaborator
9.9
Pronunciation: /kəm.prə.ˈdɑːr/ /; / /kəm.prə.ˈdɔːr/ (AmE), /kəm.prə.ˈdɔː/ /; / /kɒm.prə.ˈdɔː/ (BrE)
a local agent or member of an elite who collaborates with foreign economic or political interests, often at the expense of local interests

Examples

Elite comprador
The foreign company relied on a trusted comprador to manage its local operations.
Some viewed the politician as a comprador who put foreign interests above his own people.
During the colonial era, a comprador often acted as a middleman between local workers and foreign bosses.
