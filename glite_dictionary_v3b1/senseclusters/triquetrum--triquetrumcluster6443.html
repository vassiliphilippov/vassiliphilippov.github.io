triquetrum

noun
ancient measuring instrument
Pronunciation: / traɪˈkwiːtrəm / (AmE), / trɪˈkwiːtrəm / (BrE)
an ancient astronomical instrument with three arms used to measure angles or the altitude of celestial bodies

Examples

Ancient triquetrum instrument
The museum displayed a rare triquetrum used by astronomers in the Middle Ages.
With the help of a triquetrum, ancient scientists could measure the altitude of stars.
He explained how the triquetrum was an important tool before the invention of the telescope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: astrolabe - an ancient instrument used to make astronomical measurements
  • synonym: sextant - a navigational instrument for measuring angles between objects
