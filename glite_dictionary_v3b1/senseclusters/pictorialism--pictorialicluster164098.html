pictorialism

noun
artistic photography style
Pronunciation: / pɪkˈtɔːriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / pɪkˈtɔːrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a style of photography that emphasizes artistic composition and visual beauty, often resembling paintings

Examples

Pictorialism in photography
The museum's new exhibit explores the influence of pictorialism on early twentieth-century photography.
Many photographers adopted pictorialism to give their images a more artistic and painterly feel.
She studied pictorialism to better understand how composition affects the mood of a photograph.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impressionism - an art style focusing on light and impression rather than detail
  • antonym: photorealism - a style of art or photography aiming for lifelike accuracy
