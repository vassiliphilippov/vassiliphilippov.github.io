circumflex scapular artery

noun
artery serving shoulder and scapula muscles
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrkəmˌflɛks ˈskæpjələr ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌfleks ˈskæpjʊlə ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a branch of the subscapular artery that supplies blood to muscles around the scapula and shoulder region

Examples

The circumflex scapular artery
An injury to the circumflex scapular artery can affect blood flow to the shoulder region.
Surgeons carefully avoided the circumflex scapular artery during the procedure to prevent excessive bleeding.
The circumflex scapular artery passes through the triangular space to supply nearby muscles.
