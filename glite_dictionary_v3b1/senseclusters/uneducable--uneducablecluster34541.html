uneducable

adjective
unable to learn
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɛdʒəkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɛdjuːkəbəl /; / ʌnˈɛdjʊkəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be taught or educated, either because of unwillingness, inability, or severe limitations

Examples

Completely uneducable student
Despite many attempts, the teacher found the student completely uneducable in the subject.
Some people considered the new employee uneducable because he refused to follow any advice.
Her stubborn attitude made her seem uneducable to her mentors.
Some believed the criminal was uneducable and would never change his ways.
The teacher found the group of students challenging but refused to label any as uneducable.
Despite many interventions, his behavior seemed almost uneducable over the years.
Some believed the child was uneducable, but with support, he made great progress.
The outdated system labeled many students as uneducable without considering their individual needs.
It is important not to assume anyone is uneducable before exploring all possible teaching methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incorrigible - impossible to correct or reform
  • synonym: ineducable - unable to be educated or taught
  • antonym: reformable - able to be improved or changed for the better
  • antonym: teachable - willing and able to learn
