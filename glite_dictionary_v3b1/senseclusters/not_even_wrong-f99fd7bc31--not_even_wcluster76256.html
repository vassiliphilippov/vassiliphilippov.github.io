not even wrong

adjective
completely meaningless or invalid (argument, statement)
5.5
Pronunciation: / nɑt ˈivɪn rɔŋ / (AmE), / nɒt ˈiːvən rɒŋ / (BrE)
so fundamentally flawed or meaningless that it cannot be considered correct or incorrect; lacking validity to the extent that it lies outside the scope of reasoned discussion

Examples

Not even wrong idea
Calling the fake news article 'incorrect' gives it too much credit; it's just not even wrong.
Her criticism of the research was so misguided that it was not even wrong.
The professor dismissed the hypothesis as not even wrong, saying it could not be tested or disproven.
