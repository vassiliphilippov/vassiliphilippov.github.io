conditional

adjective
subject to conditions or reservations
1.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃ.ə.nəl /; / kənˈdɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ə.nəl /; / kənˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
depending on one or more conditions; not definite or absolute

Examples

Conditional approval
Her acceptance of the job offer was conditional on receiving a higher salary.
The ceasefire is only conditional; it will remain in place as long as both sides follow the agreement.
You may go to the party, but my permission is conditional upon your finishing your homework first.
His offer of help was conditional on receiving an apology.
The contract is only conditional upon successful completion of the trial period.
Their support for the proposal was strictly conditional.

Related Terms

  • synonym: qualified - limited or restricted in some way
  • antonym: unconditional - not subject to any conditions
