melanize

verb
convert or infiltrate with melanin
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmiːləˌnaɪz /; / ˈmɛlənaɪz /; / ˈmiːlənaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become black or dark by the formation or addition of melanin (a natural pigment in living things)

Examples

Certain fungi can melanize their cell walls in response to environmental stress.
The skin may melanize after prolonged exposure to sunlight.

Some fungi melanise when exposed to ultraviolet light as a protective response.
The cuticle of the insect melanises rapidly following its final molt.

  • antonym: depigment - to remove pigment from, especially melanin
