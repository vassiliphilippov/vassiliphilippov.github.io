tactical warning

noun
military warning after hostile act
Pronunciation: / ˈtæk.tɪ.kəl ˈwɔr.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tɪ.kəl ˈwɔː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
A military warning issued after a hostile act has occurred, alerting forces to immediate threats.

Examples

A tactical warning issued
A tactical warning is critical for quick response when an enemy attack is already underway.
After receiving a tactical warning, the troops immediately moved to defensive positions.
The commander issued a tactical warning to all units following the surprise missile attack.
