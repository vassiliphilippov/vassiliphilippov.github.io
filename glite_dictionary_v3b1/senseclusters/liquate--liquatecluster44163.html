liquate

verb
to melt and separate
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkweɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkweɪt / (BrE)
to melt or separate a substance by heating it until it becomes liquid, often to separate its components

Examples

To liquate an alloy
The metallurgist will liquate the alloy to separate its components.
To purify the metal, they must first liquate it at a high temperature.
Certain ores are easier to process if you liquate them before extraction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid by heating
  • antonym: solidify - to make or become solid
