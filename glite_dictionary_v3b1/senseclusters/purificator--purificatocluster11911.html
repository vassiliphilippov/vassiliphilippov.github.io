purificator

noun
white linen cloth for wiping the chalice
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpjʊrɪfɪˈkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˌpjʊərɪfɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a white linen cloth used in Christian liturgy to wipe the chalice after each communicant has partaken of the wine

Examples

A purificator cloth
During Mass, a clean purificator is placed on the altar alongside the chalice.
Sarah laundered the purificators used at Sunday’s service before returning them to the sacristy.
The priest carefully wiped the chalice with a purificator after each communicant received the wine.
