chain of command

noun
system of hierarchical authority
2.9 3.0
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn əv kəˈmænd / (AmE), / tʃeɪn əv kəˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
the system within an organization by which instructions are passed from people at the top of the hierarchy to those lower down

Examples

The chain of command

Every employee is expected to follow the chain of command when raising concerns.
The new policy was communicated through the chain of command before it reached all staff.
