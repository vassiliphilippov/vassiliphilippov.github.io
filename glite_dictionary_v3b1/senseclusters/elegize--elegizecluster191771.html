elegize

verb
compose an elegy; mourn in verse
8.7 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈe.lə.dʒaɪz/ /; / /ˈe.lɪ.dʒaɪz/ (AmE), /ˈe.lə.dʒaɪz/ /; / /ˈe.lɪ.dʒaɪz/ (BrE)
to compose or write an elegy, especially as a tribute to someone who has died or as an act of mourning or lamentation.

Examples

To elegize a friend
He chose to elegize his late friend in a moving poem published in the local paper.
Instead of celebrating the heroes, the speech seemed to elegize their sacrifices with solemn words.
The poet would often elegize lost loved ones through her evocative verses.
After her friend's passing, she began to elegise his memory in her journals.
He was commissioned to elegise the victims of the tragedy for the memorial service.
The poet chose to elegise the fallen leader in a moving verse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deplore - to express strong disapproval or sorrow, especially publicly
  • synonym: elegize - compose or write an elegy (American spelling)
  • synonym: eulogize - to praise highly in speech or writing, especially after death
  • synonym: lament - to express grief or sorrow, especially in words or song
