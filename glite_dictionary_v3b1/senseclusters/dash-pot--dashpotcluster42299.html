dash-pot

noun
mechanical vibration damper
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdæʃˌpɑt / (AmE), / ˈdæʃˌpɒt / (BrE)
a mechanical device used to dampen vibration, in which a piston moves through a chamber filled with liquid or air to resist motion

Examples

A dash-pot device
If the dash-pot fails, the door may slam shut instead of closing gently.
Shock absorbers in vehicles often use a dash-pot to ensure a smooth ride over bumps.
The engineer added a dash-pot to the mechanism to minimize vibrations during operation.
