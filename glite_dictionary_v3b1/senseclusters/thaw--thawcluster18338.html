thaw

verb
become unfrozen; melt
1.1 5.58/m
Pronunciation: / Œ∏…î / (AmE), / Œ∏…îÀê / (BrE)
to become soft or liquid after being frozen, or to cause something to do this

Examples

To thaw frozen food
As spring arrived, the river began to thaw and flow freely again.
He left the frozen vegetables out to thaw for dinner.
You'll need to thaw the chicken before you cook it.

Synonyms

  • unfreeze
    3.5 0.10/m
    unfreeze means to make something that was frozen soft or liquid, while thaw is the common everyday word for that change.
  • defrost
    1.7 0.89/m
    defrost removes frost or ice from food or a surface, while thaw refers to frozen items becoming soft or liquid naturally.
  • melt
    1.0 21/m
    melt means changing a solid into a liquid usually by heat, while thaw means frozen things soften or become liquid, often slowly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeze - to become hard or solid due to extreme cold
  • synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid, especially by heating
