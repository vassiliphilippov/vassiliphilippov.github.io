Zeno of Citium

proper noun
Greek founder of Stoic philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˈziːnoʊ əv ˈsɪtiəm / (AmE), / ˈziːnəʊ ɒv ˈsɪtiəm / (BrE)
(c. 335–263 BC) An ancient Greek philosopher who founded the Stoic school of philosophy in Athens.

Examples

Zeno of Citium philosopher
Zeno of Citium emphasized the importance of rationality and self-control in human life.
Zeno of Citium established the foundations of Stoic philosophy in Athens.
Many of Zeno of Citium's teachings have influenced Western philosophical thought.
