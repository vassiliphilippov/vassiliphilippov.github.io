Nilo-Saharan

noun
East African language family
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnaɪ.loʊ sə.ˈhær.ən/ (AmE), /ˈnaɪ.ləʊ sə.ˈhɑː.rən/ (BrE)
A family of East African languages spoken by Nilotic peoples from the Sahara south to Kenya and Tanzania.

Examples

Nilo-Saharan language family
Linguists continue to debate whether Nilo-Saharan truly forms a single language family.
Many people in South Sudan and northern Kenya speak languages belonging to the Nilo-Saharan family.
The distribution of Nilo-Saharan languages stretches from the Sahara Desert to the Great Lakes Region.
