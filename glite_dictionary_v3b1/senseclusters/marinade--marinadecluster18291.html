marinade

noun
flavored liquid for soaking food before cooking
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmærɪˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈneɪd / (BrE)
A mixture, usually of vinegar or wine, oil, and spices, in which food is soaked to give it flavor before cooking.

Examples

A spicy marinade
Before grilling, he soaked the vegetables in a spicy marinade.
Let the beef sit in the marinade overnight to absorb all the flavors.
She prepared a tangy marinade with garlic and lemon for the chicken.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marinate - to soak food in a marinade before cooking
