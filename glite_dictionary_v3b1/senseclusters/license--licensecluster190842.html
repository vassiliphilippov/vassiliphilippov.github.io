license

verb
give official permission
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsəns /; / ˈlaɪ.səns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsəns /; / ˈlaɪ.səns / (BrE)
to give official permission or authorization to do something, especially after verifying qualifications or standards or by granting a license

Examples

To license a company
After passing several tests, the company was finally licensed to operate.
She is licensed to teach in three different countries.
The state licenses doctors to ensure they meet strict professional standards.
After passing the exam, she was licenced as a pharmacist.
He is licenced to drive heavy vehicles in the UK.
The government must licence all operators before they can provide service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: license - to officially give permission; American spelling
  • synonym: permit - to give formal consent to do something
  • antonym: prohibit - to formally forbid something
