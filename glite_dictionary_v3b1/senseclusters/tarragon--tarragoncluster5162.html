tarragon

noun
aromatic culinary herb
Pronunciation: / ˈtærəɡən /; / ˈtærəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈtarəɡən /; / ˈtærəɡən / (BrE)
an aromatic herb with narrow leaves, used to flavor food, especially in cooking

Examples

Fresh tarragon leaves
She added fresh tarragon to the vinaigrette for a distinctive flavor.
The chicken was seasoned with tarragon and lemon juice.
You can grow tarragon in your herb garden if the climate is mild.
Pickled tarragon gives the vinaigrette a distinctive taste.
She added a handful of tarragon to the sauce for extra flavor.
The chef recommends using fresh tarragon leaves in this chicken recipe.
