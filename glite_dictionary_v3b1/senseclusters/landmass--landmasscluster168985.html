landmass

noun
large continuous area of land
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlændˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈlændˌmæs / (BrE)
a large continuous area of land, especially one forming a distinct part of the earth's surface

Examples

A large landmass
Australia is considered the smallest landmass among the continents.
Melting glaciers are causing the landmass in some regions to rise.
The Eurasian landmass contains both Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continent - one of the main large landmasses of the earth
