megagametophyte

noun
female gametophyte from a megaspore in plants
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡəɡəˈmiːtəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmiːɡəɡəˈmiːtəfaɪt / (BrE)
the female gametophyte that develops from a megaspore in plants that produce both microspores and megaspores, such as seed plants and some ferns

Examples

The megagametophyte tissue
After fertilization, the megagametophyte becomes part of the seed's nutritive tissue.
Scientists studied the nutritional content of the megagametophyte in several conifer species.
The megagametophyte develops inside the ovule and gives rise to the egg cell.
