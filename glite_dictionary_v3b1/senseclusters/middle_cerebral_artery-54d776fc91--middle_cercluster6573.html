middle cerebral artery

noun
major artery in the brain
3.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪd.əl sə.ˈriː.brəl ˈɑːr.tə.riː/ /; / /ˈmɪd.əl sə.ˈrɪ.brəl ˈɑːr.tə.riː/ (AmE), /ˈmɪd.əl sə.ˈriː.brəl ˈɑː.tə.ri/ /; / /ˈmɪd.əl sə.ˈrɪ.brəl ˈɑː.tə.ri/ (BrE)
A major artery in the brain that is a branch of the internal carotid artery, supplying blood to large areas of the cerebral cortex.

Examples

The middle cerebral artery branch
A blockage in the middle cerebral artery can lead to a stroke affecting movement and speech.
Damage to the middle cerebral artery may result in loss of sensation on one side of the body.
Doctors used imaging techniques to examine the patient's middle cerebral artery for signs of narrowing.
