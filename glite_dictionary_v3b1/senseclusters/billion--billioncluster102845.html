billion

adjective
one million million; former British usage
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪljən / (AmE), / ˈbɪljən / (BrE)
(formerly in Britain) denoting a quantity consisting of one million million (1,000,000,000,000) items or units.

Examples

Billion in old Britain
Britain once used billion to represent a figure with twelve zeros, not just nine.
Historical documents may use billion in the older British sense, which can cause confusion today.
In reports from the early 20th century, a billion pounds meant one million million pounds in the UK.
