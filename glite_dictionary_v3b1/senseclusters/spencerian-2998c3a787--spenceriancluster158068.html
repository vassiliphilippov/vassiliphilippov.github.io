Spencerian

adjective
of Herbert Spencer
Pronunciation: / spɛnˈsɪriən / (AmE), / spɛnˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas, writings, or theories of Herbert Spencer, especially his views on society and politics

Examples

Spencerian theory
The sociologist presented a Spencerian analysis of how competition shapes social structures.
Many 19th-century thinkers were influenced by Spencerian ideas about progress and evolution.
Critics argue that Spencerian philosophy supports a hands-off approach to government intervention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Darwinian - relating to the theories of Charles Darwin, especially evolution by natural selection
