tread

verb
put down or press the foot
6.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /tred/ (AmE), /tred/ (BrE)
to step, walk, or press with the foot, sometimes heavily or to crush something

Examples

To tread on flowers
Be careful not to tread on the flowers in the garden.
She trod softly across the hallway to avoid waking the baby.
The ancient floor creaked loudly whenever someone tread on it.
During the parade, the horses tread through the city streets, echoing with every step.
The children tread heavily on the wooden floorboards, making them creak loudly.
You must be careful not to tread on the fragile plants in the garden.
They tread grapes every autumn to make traditional wine.
Villagers used to tread olives in large stone vats.
With careful steps, he began to tread the clay into shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compress - to press or squeeze something together
  • synonym: crush - to press something so hard it is damaged or destroyed
  • synonym: step - to move by lifting the foot and setting it down in a new position
  • synonym: trample - to walk heavily on something, damaging or crushing it
