amen cadence

noun
church music cadence using subdominant–tonic
5.8 8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.mən ˈkeɪ.dəns / (AmE), / ˈɑː.men ˈkeɪ.dəns / (BrE)
a type of cadence in church music where the subdominant chord is followed by the tonic chord, often used at the end of hymns

Examples

The amen cadence in hymns
Composers often use the amen cadence to bring a peaceful close to liturgical music.
Many traditional church anthems end with an amen cadence to give a sense of finality.
The choir concluded the hymn with a beautiful amen cadence that lingered in the sanctuary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plagal cadence - a cadence in which the subdominant precedes the tonic, also known as the Amen cadence
