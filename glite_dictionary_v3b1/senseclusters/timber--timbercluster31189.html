timber

noun
a large piece of wood for building
1.8 1.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪm.bər/ (AmE), /ˈtɪm.bə/ (BrE)
wood that has been cut and prepared for use in building, especially as beams or posts

Examples

Timber beams
Rain had rotted the old timber at the corner of the shed.
The barn was supported by thick timbers that have lasted for centuries.
Workers lifted the heavy timber into place for the new fence.
Each ceiling timber was carefully measured and fitted into place.
The old barn was supported by large timber beams, some of which were over a hundred years old.
They replaced the rotten timber in the bridge to ensure its safety.
Much of the region's economy depends on timber production and export.
The house was built from sturdy timber sourced from the nearby forest.
They inspected the quality of the timber before starting construction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used to support weight
  • synonym: lumber - wood that has been processed for building; US equivalent of 'timber'
  • synonym: post - an upright supporting member in a structure, typically made of wood
