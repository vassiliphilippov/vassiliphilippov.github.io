obscuration

noun
something that blocks view
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɑbskjʊˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒbskjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of something being hidden, blocked, or made hard to see or understand

Examples

Technical jargon can lead to the obscuration of important information in scientific articles.
During the eclipse, the obscuration of the sun was nearly complete.

Clouds caused the obscuration of the moon, making it difficult to see the lunar event.
