equerry

noun
royal household attendant, British context
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkwɛri / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɛri / (BrE)
an officer or official in a royal or noble household, originally responsible for horses but now usually serving as a personal attendant to a member of the royal family

Examples

An equerry to queen
After his years of military service, he was appointed as an equerry to the queen.
During the ceremony, the equerry stood nearby, ready to assist any member of the royal family.
The equerry organized the royal schedule and accompanied the prince to official events.
As an equerry, he oversaw the care and training of the royal horses.
Historically, an equerry played an important role in the daily affairs of the palace.
The equerry accompanied the prince to all official functions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groom - a person responsible for the care of horses
