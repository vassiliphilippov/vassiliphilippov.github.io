post hoc

noun
retrospective, unplanned analysis
Pronunciation: / po8ast h51k / (AmE), / p598ast h52k / (BrE)
a secondary, retrospective analysis carried out after a study to explore unplanned results or relationships; usually exploratory and not a definitive test of a preplanned hypothesis.

Examples

Post hoc analysis
After the trial ended, the statisticians ran a post hoc analysis to investigate unexpected subgroup effects.
The authors cautioned that their post hoc findings require confirmation in a new, preplanned study.
Journal reviewers criticized the team for presenting a post hoc result as if it were a confirmed hypothesis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: a priori analysis - an analysis or hypothesis specified before data collection and testing
  • synonym: exploratory analysis - an analysis used to find patterns or relationships in data without prior hypotheses
