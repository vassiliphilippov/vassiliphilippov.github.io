non-denial denial

noun
an ambiguous or evasive denial
Pronunciation: / nɑn dəˈnaɪəl dɪˈnaɪəl / (AmE), / nɒn dɪˈnaɪəl dɪˈnaɪəl / (BrE)
a statement or response that appears to deny an allegation but actually avoids directly confirming or denying it

Examples

A non-denial denial statement
Her non-denial denial did little to reassure the public that nothing was wrong.
The senator’s non-denial denial fueled further speculation about the scandal.
When asked directly about the company’s practices, the spokesperson offered a non-denial denial that left reporters unsatisfied.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admission - a statement acknowledging the truth of something
  • degree lower: denial - the act of stating something is not true
  • synonym: equivocation - the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself
