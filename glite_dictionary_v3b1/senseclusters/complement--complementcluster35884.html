complement

verb
to add to and complete
1.9 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪˌmɛnt / (BrE)
to add to something in a way that enhances or completes it

Examples

To complement an outfit
Her scarf complements her dress and adds a touch of color to her outfit.
The team hired a designer whose skills complemented those of the engineers.
This wine complements the meal perfectly, bringing out the flavors of the food.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contrast - to be strikingly different from something else; sometimes an antonym in context (when something contrasts rather than complements)
  • synonym: supplement - to add something to improve or complete something else
