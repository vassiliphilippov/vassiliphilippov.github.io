wide-angle lens

noun
camera lens with wide field of view
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /waɪd ˈæŋ.gəl lenz/ /; / /waɪd ˈeɪŋ.gəl lenz/ (AmE), /waɪd ˈæŋ.gəl lenz/ (BrE)
a camera lens that has a wider field of view than a standard lens, typically with a short focal length, causing the subject to appear foreshortened and objects at the edge to be distorted

Examples

A wide-angle lens for photos
A wide-angle lens can make small rooms appear much larger in real estate photos.
Because of the distortion at the edges, you should compose your shot carefully when using a wide-angle lens.
The landscape photographer used a wide-angle lens to capture the entire mountain range in one shot.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: fisheye lens - an ultra wide-angle lens with strong visual distortion
  • degree lower: standard lens - a camera lens with a field of view similar to that of the human eye
  • antonym: telephoto lens - a camera lens with a longer focal length than standard, giving a narrow field of view and magnified image
