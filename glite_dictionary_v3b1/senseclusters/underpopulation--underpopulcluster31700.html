underpopulation

noun
too few people
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˌpɑpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a situation where there are not enough people in a place, group, or organization for it to function well or use resources fully

Examples

Rural underpopulation
Rural areas in the country are facing underpopulation as young people move to cities.
The government launched incentives to address underpopulation in remote regions.
Because of underpopulation, many schools in the village have closed due to lack of students.
The rural village suffered from underpopulation after many young people moved to the city.
Due to underpopulation, the committee struggled to organize the annual festival.
Some schools are closing because of underpopulation in the surrounding neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depopulation - a reduction in the number of people living in an area
  • antonym: overpopulation - a situation where there are more people than the environment can support
