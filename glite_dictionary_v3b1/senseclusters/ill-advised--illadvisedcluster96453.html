ill-advised

adjective
not wise; lacking careful judgment
5.4 1.17/m

(of an action or decision) not showing good judgment or careful consideration; likely to result in negative consequences.

Examples

An ill-advised decision
Ignoring expert advice proved to be ill-advised in the end.
It would be ill-advised to make major investments without proper research.
The company took an ill-advised risk by expanding too quickly.

Synonyms

  • ill-judged
    6.3 0.11/m
    ill-judged describes a decision made without careful judgment, while ill-advised highlights that it will likely cause bad results.
  • ill-considered
    6.8 0.38/m
    ill-considered refers to actions not well thought through, while ill-advised points to poor judgment with likely harm.
  • inadvisable
    5.5 0.30/m
    inadvisable means not recommended in general, while ill-advised describes a choice showing poor judgment and risk.
  • unadvisable
    6.3 0.01/m
    unadvisable is a less common form meaning not wise to do, while ill-advised focuses on poor judgment leading to consequences.
  • unwise
    3.5 3.07/m
    unwise is a broad, everyday word for poor choices, while ill-advised implies a decision likely to cause real harm.
  • imprudent
    7.6 0.66/m
    imprudent describes a lack of caution or foresight, while ill-advised signals a specific decision made without good judgment.
  • ill-conceived
    6.1 0.65/m
    ill-conceived refers to plans or ideas that are badly planned, while ill-advised stresses poor judgment and likely harmful results.
