metacyclic

adjective
with cyclic subgroups
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
in group theory, having a cyclic normal subgroup such that the quotient group is also cyclic

Examples

Metacyclic group structure
A metacyclic group can be described as an extension of two cyclic groups.
The mathematician proved that every finite metacyclic group is solvable.
Understanding metacyclic structures is important in advanced algebra courses.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cyclic - having a structure that repeats in cycles; in group theory, generated by a single element
