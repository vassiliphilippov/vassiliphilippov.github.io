coproduce

verb
to produce jointly
4.6 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / Àåko äpr…ôÀàdus / (AmE), / Àåk…ô äpr…ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to produce something together with one or more other people or organizations

Examples

To coproduce a film
The two companies agreed to coproduce a new line of electric vehicles.
Several countries will coproduce the documentary to share costs and expertise.
She was invited to coproduce the film after her previous success in the industry.

Synonyms

  • co-create
    4.0 0.00/m
    co-create means people work together to imagine and design ideas, while coproduce means organisations join to make and deliver a concrete product.
