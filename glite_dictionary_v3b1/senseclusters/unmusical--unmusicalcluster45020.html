unmusical

adjective
lacking musical quality or ability
6.0 0.05/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàmjuÀêz…™k…ôl / (AmE), /  ånÀàmjuÀêz…™k…ôl / (BrE)
not having a pleasant, harmonious, or musical sound; lacking melody or musical quality

Examples

Unmusical noise
He found the constant banging of the radiator rather unmusical and annoying.
Her performance was criticized as being flat and unmusical compared to her peers.
The singer's voice was surprisingly unmusical despite her years of training.
Despite his efforts, the melody remained strangely unmusical and discordant.
Her unmusical voice made the song difficult to enjoy.
The bird's unmusical cry startled everyone in the garden.

Synonyms

  • untuneful
    5.7 0.00/m
    untuneful is used for sounds that lack a pleasing tune or melody, while unmusical describes a general lack of musical quality.
  • tuneless
    5.3 0.19/m
    tuneless means having no clear tune and sounding odd, while unmusical covers all cases of lacking musical quality.
  • unmelodic
    6.5 0.01/m
    unmelodic lacks a melody or pleasant tune, while unmusical refers more generally to anything without musical quality.
  • unmelodious
    8.0 0.03/m
    unmelodious sounds harsh or discordant to the ear, while unmusical simply means not having musical quality or melody.
  • nonmusical
    3.8 0.10/m
    nonmusical is not related to making or having music and can be neutral, while unmusical often means sounding bad or lacking melody.

Related Terms
