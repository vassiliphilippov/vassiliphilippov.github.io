seminal

adjective
influential; formative of developments
8.0 3.72/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…õm.…ô.n…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀê.m…™.n…ôl / (BrE)
(of a work, idea, or discovery) strongly influencing later developments; highly original or formative.

Examples

Seminal work
Her seminal research changed the direction of neuroscience for decades to come.
Many scientists drew inspiration from his seminal theories.
The book is regarded as a seminal text in the field of environmental studies.

Synonyms

  • germinal
    8.1 0.08/m
    germinal denotes an early idea that starts later growth, while seminal describes a highly original work that shapes future developments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: derivative - (of a work or idea) imitative of another, and therefore less original
  • synonym: formative - serving to form something, especially having a profound and lasting influence
  • synonym: influential - having great influence on someone or something
