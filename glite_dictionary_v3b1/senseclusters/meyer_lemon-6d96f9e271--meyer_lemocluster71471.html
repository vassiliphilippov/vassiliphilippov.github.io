Meyer lemon

noun
small citrus tree
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪər ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / ˈmaɪə ˈlɛmən / (BrE)
a type of lemon that is rounder, thinner-skinned, and sweeter than common lemons; also, the small citrus tree that produces this fruit

Examples

The Meyer lemon tree
The Meyer lemon tree in her garden produced fruit with a unique, sweet flavor.
He added slices of Meyer lemon to the salad for a fresh, citrusy taste.
Many people grow a Meyer lemon indoors because it thrives in pots and has fragrant blossoms.
The recipe calls for the juice of one Meyer lemon to add a subtle sweetness.
She decorated the cake with thin slices of Meyer lemon for a fresh look.
Farmers markets often sell Meyer lemons during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lime - a small green citrus fruit with sour juice
