transceiver

noun
two-way communication device
5.6
Pronunciation: / trænˈsiːvər / (AmE), / trænˈsiːvə / (BrE)
a device that can both send and receive signals, especially for communication (such as a radio)

Examples

A transceiver device
The pilot used the aircraft's transceiver to communicate with air traffic control.
Modern smartphones contain a powerful transceiver for wireless data transmission.
The technician repaired the faulty transceiver in the radio station's equipment.
The radio transceiver allowed the team to communicate over long distances in the field.
Modern smartphones contain a transceiver for both sending and receiving wireless signals.
Each walkie-talkie contains a transceiver for two-way communication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: receiver - a device that receives radio signals
  • antonym: transmitter - a device that sends out radio signals
  • synonym: walkie-talkie - a portable two-way radio
