wind-up

noun
pre-throw movement
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
the preparatory movement or motion made before throwing or striking, especially in sports like baseball.

Examples

Pitcher's wind-up
The pitcher's wind-up was slow and deliberate before he threw the fastball.
Coaches often teach young players to improve their wind-up for better accuracy.
Her unique wind-up made it difficult for batters to predict the pitch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follow-through - the movement after a throw or strike
  • synonym: preparation - the act of getting ready for an action
