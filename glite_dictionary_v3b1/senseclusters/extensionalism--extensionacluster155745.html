extensionalism

noun
doctrine of reference
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical doctrine that the meaning of expressions is determined by their actual references or extensions

Examples

Extensionalism in language
According to extensionalism, the meaning of a term depends on the actual objects it refers to.
Many analytic philosophers have debated the merits of extensionalism versus intensionalism.
The theory of extensionalism plays a key role in discussions about language and logic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intensionalism - the doctrine that meaning is determined by sense or intention rather than reference
