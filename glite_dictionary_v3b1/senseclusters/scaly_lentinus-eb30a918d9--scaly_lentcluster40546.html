scaly lentinus

noun
a fungus with a scaly cap and licorice-like odor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪli lɛnˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈskeɪli lɛnˈtiːnəs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a scaly cap, white flesh, and a ring on the stalk, with an odor reminiscent of licorice.

Examples

The scaly lentinus mushroom
Mycologists collected several specimens of scaly lentinus from the forest floor.
The scaly lentinus is recognized by its distinctive scaly cap and ringed stalk.
The odor of the scaly lentinus is often compared to that of licorice.
