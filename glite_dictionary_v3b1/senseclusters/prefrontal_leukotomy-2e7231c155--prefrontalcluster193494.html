prefrontal leukotomy

noun
surgical brain procedure for mental illness
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈfrʌntəl luːˈkɑːtəmi /; / priˈfrʌntəl luˈkɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈfrʌntəl luːˈkɒtəmi /; / priˈfrʌntəl ljuːˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure that involves severing nerve pathways in the frontal lobe of the brain, formerly used to treat certain mental illnesses but often resulting in significant cognitive and personality changes

Examples

A prefrontal leukotomy operation

The controversial practice of prefrontal leukotomy was widely abandoned after the development of psychiatric medications.
Advances in psychiatric medicine have made prefrontal leucotomy largely obsolete today.
After the prefrontal leucotomy, the patient experienced significant personality changes.
The controversial practice of prefrontal leucotomy was once used to treat severe mental illnesses.
