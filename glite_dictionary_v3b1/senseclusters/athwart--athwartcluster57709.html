athwart

preposition
across; literary
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈθwɔrt / (AmE), / əˈθwɔːt / (BrE)
across or from one side to the other, often used in literary or poetic contexts

Examples

A shadow falls athwart the street
A long shadow fell athwart the quiet street at dusk.
The fallen tree lay athwart the narrow path, blocking their way.
Moonlight streamed athwart the old wooden floor, creating patterns in the darkness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: across - from one side to the other side of something
  • antonym: along - moving in a line parallel to something
