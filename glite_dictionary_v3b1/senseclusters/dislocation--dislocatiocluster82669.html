dislocation

noun
disruption of an established order

a disturbance or interruption of the usual arrangement or functioning of something, especially a system or society.

Examples

Economic dislocation
Rapid technological changes can lead to dislocation in traditional industries.
The abrupt closure of the factory created social dislocation in the community.
The war caused a major dislocation in the nation's economic system.

Synonyms

  • disruption
    4.1 11/m
    disruption is a sudden interruption of normal flow or order, while dislocation disturbs the usual arrangement or functioning.
  • upheaval
    6.9 5.62/m
    upheaval is a sudden, often violent social change that causes turmoil, while dislocation means a disturbance of normal functioning.
  • disarrangement
    7.6 0.02/m
    disarrangement is a messy or changed order of things, while dislocation refers to a broader interruption of systems or functioning.
  • breakdown
    4.4 6.30/m
    breakdown is a complete failure when something stops working, while dislocation is a disturbance that may not fully stop functioning.
