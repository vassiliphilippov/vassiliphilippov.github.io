mainline

noun
principal route
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪnˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the main or principal route, line, or channel in a system, such as transportation, utilities, or networks

Examples

The mainline train
The express train travels only on the mainline to reduce travel time between cities.
Engineers inspected the mainline to ensure the water supply remained uninterrupted.
After the accident, all traffic was diverted from the mainline to a temporary route.
The mainline water supply serves the entire neighborhood.
Trains on the mainline run more frequently than those on the branch lines.
Engineers inspected the mainline to ensure there were no leaks in the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artery - a main route or channel for transport or communication
  • antonym: branch - a secondary route or offshoot from a main system
  • antonym: branch line - a secondary or less important route, especially in railways
  • synonym: trunk line - a main route or major supply line in a network
