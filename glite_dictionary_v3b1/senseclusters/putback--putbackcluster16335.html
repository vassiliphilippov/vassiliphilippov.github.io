putback

noun
repurchase obligation
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊtˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpʊtˌbæk / (BrE)
a legal or contractual requirement for a seller or originator to repurchase or accept the return of defective goods, loans, or securities

Examples

Putback clause
The contract included a putback clause in case the goods were found to be defective.
After discovering errors in the loan documents, the investor initiated a putback process.
The bank faced several putbacks due to misrepresented mortgage information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buyback - the act of purchasing something previously sold
  • synonym: repurchase - the act of buying something back
