nonphagocytic

adjective
not engulfing particles
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnfæɡəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnfæɡəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
not capable of engulfing and digesting particles, cells, or other substances

Examples

Nonphagocytic cells
Unlike white blood cells, red blood cells are nonphagocytic and do not ingest bacteria.
The nonphagocytic cells play a different role in the immune response.
Some bacteria can evade the immune system by hiding inside nonphagocytic cells.
