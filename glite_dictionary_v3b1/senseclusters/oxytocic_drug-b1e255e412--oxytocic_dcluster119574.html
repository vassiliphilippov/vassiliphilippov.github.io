oxytocic drug

noun
drug that induces labor
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksɪˈtɑsɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈtɒsɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that induces labor by stimulating contractions of the muscles of the uterus

Examples

An oxytocic drug injection
Administration of an oxytocic drug is common practice during difficult childbirths.
After receiving the oxytocic drug, the woman began experiencing regular uterine contractions.
The doctor prescribed an oxytocic drug to help induce labor in the patient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uterotonic - a drug that increases the tone of uterine muscle
