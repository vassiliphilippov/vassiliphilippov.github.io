softly

adverb
in a quiet or low voice
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔftli / (AmE), / ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
in a quiet or low voice, or at a low volume (including in music or speech)

Examples

Speak softly
He asked his question softly, barely above a whisper.
She closed the door softly so as not to wake anyone.
The music played softly in the background during dinner.
She asked the pianist to begin the song softly and gradually grow louder.
The instructions said to play the passage softly throughout the movement.
When indicated, the musicians transitioned softly into the next section.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loudly - with a great deal of volume
  • synonym: piano - (in music) played or sung softly or quietly
  • synonym: quietly - in a quiet manner; with little noise
