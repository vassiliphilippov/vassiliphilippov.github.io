cedar tree

noun
large evergreen tree (genus Cedrus)
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsidɚ tri /; / ˈsiːdər triː / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə triː /; / ˈsiːdə ˌtriː / (BrE)
a large evergreen coniferous tree with fragrant, durable wood, including both true cedars (genus Cedrus) and other similar trees commonly called cedars

Examples

A large cedar tree

After the storm, branches from the old cedar tree were scattered across the lawn.
Cedar chests are often made from the wood of the cedar tree because of its pleasant scent.
The cedar tree in their backyard provided shade throughout the summer.
