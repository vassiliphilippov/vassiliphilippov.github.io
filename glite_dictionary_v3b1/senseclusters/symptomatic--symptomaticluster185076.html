symptomatic

adjective
relating to symptoms or their indication
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
showing or indicating symptoms of a disease or condition; serving as a sign of a particular problem

Examples

Symptomatic of disease
A fever may be symptomatic of a more serious underlying condition.
The doctor prescribed medication for symptomatic relief of the patient's cough.
The study focused on symptomatic patients rather than those without any signs.
Rising temperatures and headaches may be symptomatic of dehydration.
The manager said the delays were symptomatic of deeper issues within the company.
The patient's persistent cough was symptomatic of a serious underlying illness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asymptomatic - showing no symptoms of disease
  • synonym: indicative - serving as a sign or indication of something
