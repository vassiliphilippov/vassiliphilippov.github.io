vulcanized fiber

noun
tough, paper-based synthetic material
3.2 8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlkəˌnaɪzd ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈvʌlkənaɪzd ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A tough, leatherlike material made by compressing layers of paper or cloth and treating them with chemicals, often used for electrical insulation and mechanical parts.

Examples

Durable vulcanized fiber
Electrical panels often use vulcanized fiber as an insulating material because of its durability.
He studied a sample of vulcanized fiber under the microscope to observe its layered structure.
The gears in early machinery were sometimes made of vulcanized fiber for their strength and light weight.
