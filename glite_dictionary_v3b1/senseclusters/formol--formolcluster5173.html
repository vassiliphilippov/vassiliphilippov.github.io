formol

noun
10% formaldehyde solution for preservation/disinfection
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmɑl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmɒl / (BrE)
A 10% solution of formaldehyde in water, used as a disinfectant or to preserve biological specimens.

Examples

A formol solution
Before dissection, all specimens were fixed in formol to preserve their structure.
Handle formol with care, as it contains formaldehyde, which can be hazardous.
The laboratory technician stored the tissue sample in formol to prevent decomposition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formaldehyde - a colorless, pungent gas used in solution as a preservative
