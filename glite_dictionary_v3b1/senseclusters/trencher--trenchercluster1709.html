trencher

noun
wooden serving or carving board
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛntʃə / (BrE)
a wooden board or platter used for serving or carving food, especially in medieval times

Examples

Medieval trencher plate
In medieval banquets, meat was often served on a trencher instead of a plate.
The museum displayed a well-preserved trencher used during the Middle Ages.
They carved the roasted pig directly on the wooden trencher at the feast.
