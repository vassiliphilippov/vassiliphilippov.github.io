sweating sickness

noun
disease of cattle, causes sweating and fever
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈswɛtɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈswetɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (BrE)
A disease affecting cattle, especially calves, characterized by fever, sweating, and skin lesions.

Examples

Cattle sweating sickness
Early diagnosis of sweating sickness is crucial to prevent further losses in the herd.
Farmers became worried when several calves developed sweating sickness during the summer.
The veterinarian explained that sweating sickness is often caused by toxins from certain ticks.
