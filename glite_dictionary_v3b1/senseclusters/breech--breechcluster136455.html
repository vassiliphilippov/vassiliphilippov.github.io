breech

noun
rear opening of a gun barrel
9.6 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / briÀêt É / (AmE), / briÀêt É / (BrE)
the rear part of the barrel of a gun or firearm, through which bullets or other projectiles are loaded

Examples

Breech of a gun
He inspected the breech to ensure there was no obstruction before firing.
Modern artillery often uses a breech-loading mechanism for faster firing.
The soldier loaded the rifle by opening the breech and inserting a cartridge.

Synonyms

  • gun chamber
    5.5 0.00/m
    a gun chamber is the internal space that holds the cartridge for firing, while a breech is the rear barrel opening used to load.
  • firing chamber
    7.2 0.01/m
    a firing chamber is the part where the cartridge sits when fired, while a breech is specifically the rear barrel opening used to load ammunition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: muzzle - the open end of the barrel of a firearm
