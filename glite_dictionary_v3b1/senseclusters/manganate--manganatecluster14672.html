manganate

noun
a salt of manganic acid (MnO4^2−)
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋɡəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡəneɪt / (BrE)
a salt of manganic acid, containing the manganate ion (MnO4^2−), in which manganese is present as part of the anion

Examples

A manganate salt
Chemists studied the reactions between manganate and various acids in the laboratory.
Potassium manganate is a green compound used in chemical analysis.
The production of manganate involves the oxidation of manganese dioxide with potassium hydroxide.
