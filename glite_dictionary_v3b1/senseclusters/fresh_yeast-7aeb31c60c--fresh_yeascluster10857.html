fresh yeast

noun
moist, living yeast for baking
2.5
Pronunciation: / frɛʃ jiːst / (AmE), / frɛʃ jiːst / (BrE)
yeast in moist, living form used as a leavening agent in baking

Examples

Fresh yeast dough
For the best results, dissolve fresh yeast in lukewarm water before adding it to the dough.
Some traditional recipes specify fresh yeast instead of dry yeast for a richer taste.
The bakery uses fresh yeast to make its breads especially soft and flavorful.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry yeast - dehydrated form of yeast used in baking
  • synonym: yeast - microscopic fungus used as a leavening agent in baking and brewing
