Leo X

proper noun
Renaissance pope; son of Lorenzo de' Medici
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌliː.oʊ ˈtɛn / (AmE), / ˌliː.əʊ ˈtɛn / (BrE)
(1475–1521) Pope from 1513 to 1521, son of Lorenzo de' Medici, notable for excommunicating Martin Luther and granting Henry VIII the title 'Defender of the Faith'.

Examples

Leo X pope
Leo X excommunicated Martin Luther after Luther refused to recant his teachings.
As pope, Leo X played a significant role in the early years of the Protestant Reformation.
The Medici family produced several influential figures, including Leo X.
