tithe

verb
pay or collect a tenth part (esp. as tax)
7.8 7.3
Pronunciation: /taɪð/ (AmE), /taɪð/ (BrE)
to pay, give, or collect a tenth part of income or produce, especially for religious or charitable purposes

Examples

To tithe income
During the medieval period, churches would tithe the crops harvested by local farmers.
Some religious sects still tithe their members as part of their faith practice.
The villagers were tithed a portion of their annual earnings.
After receiving his paycheck, he set aside a portion to tithe to his local church.
Even though she had moved away, she continued to tithe out of gratitude for her upbringing.
Many members of the congregation continue to tithe regularly despite economic hardships.
Certain crops were tithed annually to support the local parish.
In the medieval period, farmers were often required to tithe a portion of their harvest to the church.
The landowner tithes the wheat before distributing the rest to the tenants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: donate - to give money or goods for a good cause
  • antonym: exempt - to free from an obligation or liability
  • synonym: tax - to impose a compulsory financial charge
