contraflow

noun
reverse traffic system
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːntrəˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrəˌfləʊ / (BrE)
a temporary traffic arrangement where vehicles travel in the opposite direction to the usual flow, often due to roadworks or closures

Examples

Temporary contraflow
A contraflow was introduced on the highway to allow repairs on the main carriageway.
Drivers were confused by the sudden contraflow system set up during the festival.
The city used a contraflow to keep traffic moving while the bridge was closed for maintenance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detour - a different route taken to avoid a blocked road
  • antonym: one-way - a street or road where traffic moves in only one direction
